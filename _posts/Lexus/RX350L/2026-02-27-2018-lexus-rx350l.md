# 2018 Lexus RX350L Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2018 Lexus RX 350L, buyers can generally expect the hallmark luxury and comfort associated with the brand, backed by a strong reputation for overall durability. However, owner reports submitted to federal safety regulators highlight a few specific areas worth inspecting during a pre-purchase evaluation. While major mechanical failures are relatively uncommon, paying attention to potential quirks in the braking system, interior hardware, and electronics can save you from unexpected post-purchase headaches.

---

## Engine

### 3.5L V6 (2GR-FKS)

The 2018 Lexus RX 350L is powered by a 3.5-liter V6 engine producing approximately 290 horsepower and 267 lb-ft of torque. This powerplant is generally robust and smooth, though isolated owner complaints note occasional misfires or fuel system sensor glitches.

**Engine Misfire and Fuel Sensor Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Illuminated check engine lights, rough idling, or potential fuel pump sensor electrical shorting that could lead to unexpected stalling.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified technician scan for diagnostic trouble codes and inspect the fuel suction tube and pump assembly if drivability concerns arise.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

Paired with the V6 engine, an 8-speed automatic transmission delivers power smoothly to the front wheels or available all-wheel-drive system. Overall, this transmission option maintains a solid reputation for long-term mechanical reliability.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

Brake system feedback centers around unusual noises and potential actuator component wear reported by some owners.

**Brake Squawk Noise and Actuator Concerns**
- **Symptoms:** An audible squawking or barking noise when applying the brake pedal, accompanied by owner concerns regarding brake actuator performance.
- **Solution:** Inspect the brake assembly and consult a dealer or specialist to determine if the brake actuator requires service or replacement.
- **Cost:** Repair costs vary depending on whether components are replaced under goodwill or out of pocket.

## Steering

### Steering and Suspension Joints

Steering and front suspension components generally hold up well, though early wear on rubber boots and joints has been noted in select vehicles.

**Premature Ball Joint and Sway Bar Link Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Leaking grease from front ball joints or sway bar links, potentially accompanied by loose steering feel or loss of control confidence.
- **Solution:** Lift the vehicle during a pre-purchase inspection to check steering and suspension boots for grease leaks or excessive play.

## Exterior

### Doors and Sunroof

Body hardware requires attention to mechanical latches and trim pieces, which can occasionally bind or fail prematurely.

**Interior Door Handle and Sunshade Jams**
- **Symptoms:** Interior door handles failing to unlatch doors from the inside, or panoramic sunroof sunshades bunching up and failing to retract fully.
- **Solution:** Inspect all interior door handles for normal operation and test the panoramic sunshade mechanism to ensure smooth travel.

## Interior

### Climate Control Electronics

Electrical accessories are generally dependable, though cabin climate actuators can occasionally fail over time.

**HVAC Servo Motor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Malfunctioning automatic climate control airflow, failure to direct air properly, or strange clicking noises behind the dashboard.
- **Solution:** Test all HVAC modes, temperature zones, and fan speeds during a test drive; replace failed servo motors as necessary.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V682000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lexus&model=RX350L&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** If the fuel pump fails, the engine can stall while driving, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Toyota will notify owners, and dealers will replace the fuel pump assembly with an improved one, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2018 Lexus RX 350L remains a compelling choice for luxury crossover shoppers, offering refined performance, a spacious three-row cabin, and strong underlying engineering. While owner complaints point to specific items worth checking—such as brake actuator noises, interior door handle operation, and climate control actuators—none overshadow the vehicle's overall dependability. Thoroughly inspect mechanical and electrical functions during a test drive to ensure a trouble-free ownership experience.
