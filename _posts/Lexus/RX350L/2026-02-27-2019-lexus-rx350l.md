# 2019 Lexus RX350L Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used luxury midsize crossover, the 2019 Lexus RX350L generally upholds the brand's strong reputation for refinement and comfort. However, owner reports and technical service insights highlight a few recurring problem areas that prospective buyers should inspect closely. While its powertrain options offer smooth everyday performance, certain mechanical and safety components require attention during a pre-purchase inspection.

---

## Engine

### 3.5L V6 Engine

The 3.5-liter V6 engine produces approximately 290 horsepower and provides smooth, linear acceleration well-suited for a luxury crossover. Overall, the powerplant is historically robust, though occasional fuel pump and electronic management complaints have surfaced.

**Engine Stalling and Power Loss**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have reported sudden dashboard warning lights followed by a drop into reduced engine power mode or unexpected engine stalling during operation.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified technician diagnose electronic throttle body or fuel delivery codes to determine if a sensor or pump replacement is required.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 8-speed automatic transmission is paired with front-wheel drive or all-wheel drive, designed to maximize highway efficiency and smooth power delivery. While most units operate reliably, isolated shifting complaints warrant a thorough test drive.

**Harsh Shifting and Engagement Delay**
- **Symptoms:** Occasional hesitation when moving out of lower gears, accompanied by sudden vehicle jerking and a faint burning smell.
- **Solution:** Check transmission fluid condition and software calibration levels; consult a dealer for potential control module updates.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes and Actuator

The braking system provides confident stopping power for a three-row luxury vehicle, but the hydraulic brake actuator is a known point of wear and noise concern.

**Brake Actuator Noise and Stiff Pedal Feel**
- **Symptoms:** A noticeable squishing, squeaking, or honking noise when applying pressure to the brake pedal, often accompanied by a stiff pedal feel.
- **Solution:** Inspect the brake hydraulic actuator assembly and refer to known technical service bulletins regarding brake squawk noises for proper component replacement.

## Safety

### Forward Collision Avoidance

Advanced safety features enhance driver awareness, though sensor calibration and intermittent collision-warning faults require careful verification.

**Radar Sensor and CMBS Faults**
- **Symptoms:** Unexpected collision avoidance warnings, sudden activation of braking assist, or failure of the system to engage during low-speed frontal scenarios.
- **Solution:** Clean sensor areas and have a dealership recalibrate the millimeter-wave radar and camera systems if warning lights persist.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V682000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lexus&model=RX350L&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** If the fuel pump fails, the engine can stall while driving, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Toyota will notify owners, and dealers will replace the fuel pump assembly with an improved one, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2019 Lexus RX350L combines family-friendly three-row versatility with strong luxury appeal, but used car buyers should remain vigilant. Pay close attention to brake actuator noises during a test drive and ensure all safety sensors and engine electronics are functioning correctly. A comprehensive pre-purchase inspection by a qualified specialist will help uncover any lingering mechanical or electrical issues before purchase.
