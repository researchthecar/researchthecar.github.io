# 2000 Lexus SC400

### Overview
The 2000 Lexus SC400 is a luxury grand tourer known for its robust build quality and performance. As a product of Toyota's luxury division, it generally exhibits high levels of reliability, benefiting from proven engineering and meticulous assembly. Owners typically report a satisfying ownership experience with fewer unexpected repairs compared to many contemporaries. While no vehicle is entirely immune to issues, the SC400's design prioritizes durability and longevity, making it a solid choice for those seeking a blend of performance and dependability. Regular maintenance is key to preserving its excellent condition.

## Electrical

### General Electrical System
**Window Regulator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Power windows may become slow, inoperative, or make grinding noises.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the window regulator assembly.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600

**Headlight Switch Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Headlights may flicker, not turn on, or turn off unexpectedly.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the headlight switch.
- **Cost:** $150 - $300


## Exterior

### Body and Paint
**Clear Coat Peeling**
- **Symptoms:** The clear coat on the paint may start to peel or fade, particularly on horizontal surfaces exposed to sun.
- **Solution:** Repainting affected panels.
- **Cost:** $500 - $2000+ per panel


## Interior

### Leather Upholstery
**Seat Leather Cracking/Wear**
- **Symptoms:** The leather on seats, especially the driver's seat, may show signs of cracking, drying, or excessive wear over time.
- **Solution:** Leather conditioning, repair kits, or reupholstery.
- **Cost:** $100 - $1500+


### Climate Control Unit
**Pixelated Display**
- **Symptoms:** The LCD display on the climate control unit may become partially or fully unreadable due to dead pixels.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of the climate control unit.
- **Cost:** $300 - $700

### Summary Verdict
The 2000 Lexus SC400 stands out as a highly reliable luxury coupe. Its powertrain, including the legendary 4.0L V8 engine and 5-speed automatic transmission, is exceptionally durable with no widespread issues reported. The suspension system also generally holds up well. Common, though not universal, issues tend to be related to age and wear on specific electrical components like window regulators and headlight switches, as well as cosmetic concerns such as paint clear coat degradation and interior leather wear. These are typical for a vehicle of this age and do not detract significantly from its overall excellent reliability record. With proper care and maintenance, the SC400 remains a dependable and enjoyable classic.
