**Introduction:**

The 1992 Lexus SC400 is a luxury sports coupe powered by a 4.0-liter V8 engine. It offers a smooth ride, elegant interior, and impressive performance with 250 horsepower and 260 lb-ft of torque. While it has received praise for its reliability and refinement, the SC400 is not without its drawbacks.

**Engine**

**4.0L V8**

* **Intake manifold gasket failure:** Leaking coolant, rough idle, overheating
* Solution: Replace intake manifold gasket
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
* Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**4-speed automatic**

* **Shift solenoid failure:** Gear slippage, delayed or hard shifting
* Solution: Replace shift solenoid
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
* Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Strut mount failure:** Clunking noise, poor handling
* Solution: Replace strut mounts
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
* Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Alternator failure:** Dimming headlights, dead battery
* Solution: Replace alternator
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800+
* Recalls: None Found

* **Starter failure:** Difficulty starting the engine
* Solution: Replace starter
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
* Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Headlight lens fading:** Reduced visibility, aesthetics
* Solution: Replace headlight lenses
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
* Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Instrument cluster failure:** Dim or flickering gauges, erratic readings
* Solution: Repair or replace instrument cluster
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
* Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

The 1992 Lexus SC400 faces a handful of common issues, primarily with the engine, transmission, and electrical systems. While the severity of these issues varies, they can be costly to repair. Owners should factor in the potential maintenance expenses associated with these known problems when considering the purchase of an SC400.
