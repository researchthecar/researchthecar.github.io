**Introduction:**

The 2016 Lexus CT200h is a hybrid luxury compact car available with a 1.8L 4-cylinder engine paired with a CVT. Known for its fuel efficiency and comfort, it also has some reported issues.

**Engine:**

* **1.8L 4-Cylinder**
    * **Piston Ring Failure:** Excessive oil consumption, blue smoke from exhaust; Replacement of piston rings; $2,000 - $4,000+
    * **Water Pump Failure:** Coolant leaks, overheating; Replacement of water pump; $500 - $1,000+

**Transmission:**

* **CVT (Continuously Variable Transmission)**
    * **Shuddering or Slipping:** Difficulty shifting, reduced acceleration; Repair or replacement of CVT; $3,000 - $5,000+
    * **Overheating:** Transmission warning light, loss of power; Repair of cooling system; $500 - $1,500+

**Suspension:**

* **No Issues Found**

**Electrical:**

* **Battery Failure:** Premature discharge, difficulty starting; Replacement of battery; $200 - $500+
* **Electrical Short Circuit:** Dashboard malfunctions, loss of power; Repair or replacement of electrical components; $500 - $2,000+

**Exterior:**

* **Tailgate Corrosion:** Rusting around tailgate; Replacement of tailgate; $1,000 - $2,000+

**Interior:**

* **Infotainment System Malfunction:** Freezing, unresponsive touch screen; Software update or replacement of infotainment unit; $500 - $2,000+
* **Air Conditioning Compressor Failure:** Reduced cooling, insufficient air flow; Replacement of compressor; $1,000 - $2,000+

**Summary:**

The 2016 Lexus CT200h faces some reliability issues, particularly with the engine and transmission. The cost of repairs can be significant, potentially reducing the car's value and overall satisfaction. Potential buyers should consider these issues and factor in the potential repair costs before purchasing.
