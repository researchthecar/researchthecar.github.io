**Introduction**

The 2013 Lexus CT200h is a hybrid luxury compact car known for its fuel efficiency, comfortable ride, and advanced safety features. It comes with a 1.8-liter 4-cylinder engine paired with an electric motor, producing a combined 134 horsepower and 151 lb-ft of torque. The sole transmission option is a continuously variable transmission (CVT).

**Engine**

No Issues Found

**Transmission**

**CVT**

**Premature Failure**
Description/Symptoms: Transmission slipping, shuddering, or refusing to engage gears.
Solution: Replace entire transmission assembly.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $3,000 - $5,000+
Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

**Strut Mount Bearing Failure**
Description/Symptoms: Clunking or grinding noise from front suspension, especially over bumps.
Solution: Replace strut mount bearings.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500 per strut
Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

**Battery Failure**
Description/Symptoms: Vehicle fails to start or experiences electrical malfunctions.
Solution: Replace hybrid battery.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+
Recalls: NHTSA Recall Number: 18V-185, 19V-034

**Alternator Failure**
Description/Symptoms: Dimming headlights, loss of power steering, or difficulty starting the vehicle.
Solution: Replace alternator.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

**Sunroof Leakage**
Description/Symptoms: Water leaking into the cabin through the sunroof.
Solution: Realign sunroof or replace seals.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

**Navigation System Malfunction**
Description/Symptoms: Navigation system freezes, gives incorrect directions, or does not respond.
Solution: Update or replace navigation software.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 - $1,000+
Recalls: None Found

**Rear Seat Folding Mechanism Failure**
Description/Symptoms: Rear seats are unable to fold down or fold up.
Solution: Replace rear seat folding mechanism or repair cables.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+
Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

While the 2013 Lexus CT200h offers many desirable features, it has experienced several significant issues, including CVT transmission failure, strut mount bearing failure, hybrid battery failure, and electrical malfunctions. The severity and cost of these issues can be concerning, especially for potential buyers considering a used model.
