# 2018 Lexus RC F Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2018 Lexus RC F is a high-performance luxury coupe known for its naturally aspirated V8 powerplant and robust luxury build quality. While overall reliability tends to be strong compared to German rivals, buyers should still review standard mechanical and electrical systems before purchasing a used model.

---

## Engine

### 5.0L V8

Powered by a 5.0-liter naturally aspirated V8 engine producing roughly 467 horsepower and 389 lb-ft of torque. This performance engine generally offers strong durability for daily driving and track use.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Sport Direct-Shift Automatic

An 8-speed automatic transmission featuring manual paddle shifters, routing power to the rear wheels. It delivers quick shifts and solid long-term mechanical reliability.

---

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V107000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lexus&model=RC+F&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** A cracked cover may leak fuel, which, in the presence of an ignition source, can increase the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Toyota will notify owners, and dealers will replace the high pressure fuel pumps, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2018 Lexus RC F combines executive coupe comfort with a high-revving V8 engine and commendable build quality. Prospective buyers should perform a thorough pre-purchase inspection to verify that all powertrain and electrical features operate correctly.
