# 2021 Lexus RC F

### Overview
The 2021 Lexus RC F is a high-performance luxury coupe that generally exhibits strong reliability, a hallmark of the Lexus brand. Built upon a robust platform and powered by a potent naturally aspirated V8 engine, it aims to balance exhilarating driving dynamics with dependable daily usability. While its performance focus might suggest potential for increased wear, Lexus's engineering and quality control typically mitigate common issues. This analysis focuses on documented technical reliability concerns beyond standard maintenance, examining key systems like the engine, transmission, suspension, electrical components, and interior/exterior features to provide a comprehensive picture of its long-term dependability.

### Summary Verdict
The 2021 Lexus RC F demonstrates excellent mechanical reliability, particularly with its renowned 5.0L V8 engine and 8-speed automatic transmission, which show no significant widespread issues. The suspension and exterior components are also generally robust. The primary areas where minor concerns have been noted are within the electrical system, specifically occasional infotainment glitches that can usually be resolved with software updates, and a possibility of premature battery failure, which is not uncommon in performance vehicles. Interior components and upholstery have proven durable. Overall, the RC F offers a highly reliable ownership experience, consistent with Lexus's reputation for quality and longevity, with most potential issues being minor and manageable.
