The 2019 Lexus RC F is a high-performance coupe that offers a thrilling driving experience. It features a naturally aspirated 5.0-liter V8 engine that produces 472 horsepower and 395 lb-ft of torque and is paired with an 8-speed automatic transmission. The RC F also comes standard with a Torsen limited-slip differential and adaptive dampers.

While the RC F is a well-rounded sports car, it has not been immune to issues. Here is a list of the specific problems that have been reported by owners:

**Engine**

* **5.0-Liter V8**

**Intake Valve Carbon Buildup**
* Description/Symptoms: Over time, carbon deposits can build up on the intake valves, which can lead to reduced engine performance, increased fuel consumption, and rough idling.
* Solution: The intake valves need to be cleaned.
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000

**Transmission**

* **8-Speed Automatic**

**Shifting Problems**
* Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported experiencing rough shifting or delayed shifts.
* Solution: The transmission may need to be serviced or reprogrammed. If you continue to experience shifting issues, the transmission may need to be replaced.
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $3,000

**Suspension**

* **Adaptive Dampers**

**Malfunctioning Adaptive Dampers**
* Description/Symptoms: The adaptive dampers may malfunction, leading to a rough ride or poor handling.
* Solution: The adaptive dampers need to be replaced.
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000

**Electrical**

* **Infotainment System**

**Infotainment System Crashing**
* Description/Symptoms: The infotainment system may randomly crash or freeze. The system might also have issues with Bluetooth connectivity.
* Solution: The infotainment system may need to be updated or replaced.
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500

**Exterior**

* **Headlights**

**LED Headlights Malfunctioning**
* Description/Symptoms: The LED headlights may malfunction or flicker.
* Solution: The LED headlights need to be replaced.
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000

**Interior**

* **Rear Seat Space**

**Limited Rear Seat Space**
* Description/Symptoms: The rear seats are cramped and uncomfortable for adults.
* Solution: Unfortunately, there is no solution for this issue.
* Estimated Cost to Fix: None

**Summary**

Overall, the Lexus RC F is a reliable and well-built sports car, but it has not been immune to issues like any vehicle. The most common problems reported by owners include intake valve carbon buildup, shifting problems, and malfunctioning adaptive dampers. While these issues can be costly to fix, they are relatively rare.
