# 2020 Lexus RC F Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2020 Lexus RC F blends high-performance V8 heritage with everyday luxury coupe comfort. While owner-reported complaints for this model year are exceptionally sparse, buyers exploring this high-performance luxury coupe should still review typical V8 running gear, electronics, and standard pre-purchase inspection protocols.

---

## Engine

### 5.0L V8 Engine

The 5.0L naturally aspirated V8 produces around 472 horsepower and 395 lb-ft of torque. It is generally praised for robust mechanical durability and high-revving performance, though routine premium fuel and maintenance are required.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Sport Direct-Shift Automatic Transmission

This traditional 8-speed automatic transmission features manual paddle shifters and handles high-revving power delivery smoothly. It maintains a strong reputation for long-term reliability under normal driving conditions.

---

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V615000: Tires](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lexus&model=RC+F&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 110, "Tire Selection and Rims." The vehicle can be overloaded without the weight capacity information, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the tire information label, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2020 Lexus RC F maintains an outstanding reputation for build quality and powertrain longevity, backed by very few owner complaints. Prospective buyers should conduct a standard pre-purchase inspection focusing on consumables like tires and brakes, but can otherwise approach this high-performance coupe with confidence.
