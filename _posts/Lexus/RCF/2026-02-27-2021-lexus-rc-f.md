# 2021 Lexus RC F Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2021 Lexus RC F is a high-performance luxury coupe powered by a naturally aspirated V8 engine. Known for Lexus's strong reputation for overall build quality and luxury execution, it offers a compelling alternative to traditional German rivals. Prospective buyers should still review standard mechanical components and inspect any pre-owned model thoroughly.

---

## Engine

### 5.0L V8 Engine

The 5.0-liter naturally aspirated V8 produces 472 horsepower and 395 lb-ft of torque, delivering strong high-rpm performance. This engine generally enjoys a solid reputation for mechanical durability in everyday driving conditions.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Sport Direct-Shift Automatic Transmission

The 8-speed automatic transmission features manual paddle shifters and manages power delivery to the rear wheels. It is widely regarded as robust under normal operating conditions, provided routine fluid maintenance is observed.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V615000: Tires](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lexus&model=RC+F&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 110, "Tire Selection and Rims." The vehicle can be overloaded without the weight capacity information, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the tire information label, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2021 Lexus RC F combines high-output V8 performance with the brand's traditional reputation for dependable craftsmanship. Buyers evaluating a used model should perform a standard pre-purchase inspection to verify overall mechanical and cosmetic health.
