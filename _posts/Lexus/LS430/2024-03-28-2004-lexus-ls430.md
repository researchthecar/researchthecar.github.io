**2004 Lexus LS430**

The 2004 Lexus LS430 was a luxury sedan known for its comfort, reliability, and performance. It was available with a 4.3-liter V8 engine mated to a 6-speed automatic transmission.

The LS430 was praised for its spacious cabin, smooth ride, and powerful engine. However, it also had some drawbacks, such as its high price and limited maneuverability.

**Engine**

**4.3L V8**

* **Intake manifold gasket failure:** Symptoms include coolant leaks, overheating, and poor engine performance. Solution: Replace intake manifold gaskets. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1200.
* **VVT-i system issues:** Symptoms include rough idle, lack of power, and increased fuel consumption. Solution: Replace VVT-i solenoid or oil control valve. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800.

**Transmission**

**6-Speed Automatic**

* **Transmission slipping:** Symptoms include delayed engagement, difficulty shifting, and loss of power. Solution: Overhaul or replace transmission. Estimated Cost to Fix: $2000 - $4000.
* **Torque converter shudder:** Symptoms include vibration during acceleration. Solution: Replace torque converter. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1500 - $2500.

**Suspension**

* **Front lower control arm bushings worn:** Symptoms include clunking noises, vibration, and reduced stability. Solution: Replace control arm bushings. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800.
* **Rear air suspension leaks:** Symptoms include sagging rear end, rough ride, and warning lights. Solution: Replace air suspension components. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1000 - $2000.

**Electrical**

* **Battery drain:** Symptoms include slow cranking, failure to start, and warning lights. Solution: Test and replace battery or electrical components. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500.
* **Alternator failure:** Symptoms include battery warning light, dim lights, and electrical problems. Solution: Replace alternator. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800.

**Exterior**

* **Rear bumper cover detachment:** Symptoms include loose or rattling rear bumper cover. Solution: Reinstall or replace bumper cover clips. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400.
* **Headlight condensation:** Symptoms include moisture inside headlight lenses. Solution: Replace headlight assemblies with updated seals. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1000.

**Interior**

* **Power seat memory loss:** Symptoms include inability to store or recall seat positions. Solution: Reset or replace seat control module. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500.
* **Navigation system freezing:** Symptoms include unresponsive touchscreen or system glitches. Solution: Update software or replace navigation unit. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1200.

**Summary**

Overall, the 2004 Lexus LS430 is a reliable and comfortable luxury sedan. However, it is not without its issues. Engine and transmission problems can be costly to repair, while suspension and electrical issues can affect the driving experience. Interior issues are relatively minor, but can be annoying to owners.
