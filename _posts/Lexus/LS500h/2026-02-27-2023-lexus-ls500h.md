# 2023 Lexus LS500h Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2023 Lexus LS500h represents the pinnacle of luxury, combining a sophisticated hybrid powertrain with world-class craftsmanship and comfort. As a high-end luxury flagship, it appeals to buyers looking for serene ride quality, cutting-edge technology, and exceptional long-term dependability. While owner-reported complaints are sparse for this model year, prospective buyers should always evaluate the complex electronics and hybrid systems during a pre-purchase inspection.

---

## Engine

### 3.5L V6 Multi Stage Hybrid

The 3.5-liter V6 multi-stage hybrid system pairs a naturally aspirated gasoline engine with advanced electric motors to produce approximately 354 total system horsepower. This powertrain delivers seamless acceleration and impressive fuel efficiency for a large luxury sedan, maintaining Lexus's reputation for robust engineering and longevity.

## Transmission

### Multi-Stage Continuously Variable Transmission

The multi-stage hybrid transmission uses a specialized continuously variable design combined with a 4-speed automatic gear device to simulate stepped gear changes. It provides smooth power delivery and highway efficiency, generally proving to be durable under normal driving conditions.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V124000: Back Over Prevention:Display Function](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lexus&model=LS&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** Due to incorrect programming of the parking-assist electronic control unit (ECU) software, the rearview camera image may not immediately display within a couple of seconds after the shifter is placed into reverse. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 111, "Rear Visibility." A rearview.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the parking assist ECU software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V744000: Back Over Prevention:Display Function](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lexus&model=LS&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** A software error may cause the rearview camera to freeze or display a blank screen when the vehicle is in reverse. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 111, "Rear Visibility." A rearview camera that fails to display an image can reduce the driver's view behind the vehicle.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the parking assist software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.
