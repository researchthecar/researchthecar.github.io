# 2021 Lexus LS500h Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2021 Lexus LS500h, buyers can generally expect the high level of luxury, comfort, and advanced engineering typical of Lexus's flagship sedan. While owner reports to federal safety regulators are relatively scarce for this hybrid model, reviewing specific component feedback can help you perform a thorough pre-purchase inspection.

---

## Engine

### 3.5L V6 MultiStage Hybrid

This 3.5-liter V6 engine paired with Lexus's MultiStage Hybrid System delivers a smooth and efficient driving experience with robust overall output. It is generally regarded as a sophisticated and reliable powertrain option for buyers seeking luxury combined with improved fuel economy.

## Transmission

### MultiStage Hybrid Continuously Variable Transmission

The MultiStage Hybrid transmission combines a planetary gear CVT with a four-stage automatic device to simulate traditional gear shifts. It offers seamless power delivery and solid long-term durability for daily driving.

## Suspension

### Tires And Wheels

Suspension-related complaints are minor, typically focusing on individual tire wear and pressure maintenance rather than systemic mechanical failures.

**Tire Pressure and Wear Concerns**
- **Symptoms:** Some owners have reported tires failing to hold recommended air pressure over several weeks or experiencing premature inner tread wear.
- **Solution:** Inspect tires thoroughly for uneven wear patterns and monitor tire pressure regularly; replace tires as needed.

## Exterior

### Headlight Assemblies

Electrical and lighting feedback is limited, though buyers should visually inspect exterior lighting fixtures for cosmetic or sealing imperfections.

**Lens Delamination and Condensation**
- **Symptoms:** Headlight lenses may exhibit external delamination or visible moisture condensation trapped inside the sealed housing.
- **Solution:** Inspect headlight assemblies for clarity and moisture buildup during a pre-purchase test drive.

### Sunroof

Exterior body components are generally durable, though isolated incidents involving glass integrity have been noted by owners.

**Glass Shattering**
- **Symptoms:** Rare reports indicate the sunroof glass may spontaneously shatter while driving, producing a loud noise and scattering glass fragments.
- **Solution:** Examine the sunroof glass for any pre-existing stress cracks or chips before finalizing purchase.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V275000: Air Bags:Sensor:Occupant Classification:Front Passenger](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lexus&model=LS&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 208, "Occupant Crash Protection." Incorrect detection of an occupant may result in improper air bag deployment during a crash, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and repair the front passenger seat, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2021 Lexus LS500h remains a highly refined and sophisticated flagship luxury sedan. Because owner-reported issues are few, buyers should focus on standard pre-purchase checks—including inspecting the tires, headlights, and glass panels—to ensure the vehicle has been well-maintained.
