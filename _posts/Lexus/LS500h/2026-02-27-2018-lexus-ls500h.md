# 2018 Lexus LS500h Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a pre-owned 2018 Lexus LS 500h, buyers can generally expect the high level of luxury and craftsmanship associated with Lexus's flagship sedan. While overall owner satisfaction remains high, reviewing real-world owner feedback highlights a few electrical, suspension, and interior complaints worth noting during a pre-purchase inspection.

---

## Engine

### 3.5L Twin-Turbo V6 (LS 500)

The 3.5-liter twin-turbocharged V6 engine produces approximately 416 horsepower and 442 lb-ft of torque, delivering robust acceleration for the flagship sedan. It is generally regarded as a durable power plant, though early model years have occasionally attracted owner notes regarding auxiliary electrical components.

### 3.5L V6 Hybrid (LS 500h)

The 3.5-liter V6 multi-stage hybrid powertrain generates a combined 354 horsepower, pairing electric motors with a traditional V6 for exceptional efficiency and smooth power delivery. This advanced hybrid system benefits from Lexus's long-standing electrification expertise and typically demonstrates strong long-term reliability.

## Transmission

### 10-Speed Automatic (LS 500)

The 10-speed direct-shift automatic transmission is paired with the standard twin-turbo V6 to provide seamless gear changes and optimized highway efficiency. While generally smooth, occasional owner reports note unexpected shifting behavior that warrants a thorough test drive.

**Unexpected Downshifting**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have reported instances where the vehicle abruptly downshifted from high gears to lower gears at highway speeds, causing unexpected engine revving.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified technician inspect the transmission control module and check for software updates or stored diagnostic trouble codes.

### Multi-Stage Hybrid Transmission (LS 500h)

The multi-stage hybrid transmission combines an electronically controlled continuously variable transmission with a four-speed automatic gear device to simulate stepped gear changes. It offers refined operation tailored specifically to the hybrid luxury driving experience.

## Suspension

### Tires and Alignment

The sophisticated suspension setup is designed to isolate passengers from road imperfections, though proper alignment is critical to prevent premature tire wear.

**Inner Tire Tread Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have observed premature or uneven inner shoulder wear on all four tires, potentially shortening tire lifespan and affecting traction.
- **Solution:** Verify suspension alignment settings and inspect tire wear patterns during routine maintenance intervals.

## Electrical

### Battery and Charging

Modern luxury sedans rely heavily on complex electrical architectures, making battery health checks an essential part of routine upkeep.

**Premature Battery Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Some drivers experienced sudden battery draining or failure, resulting in the vehicle failing to start without prior dashboard warnings.
- **Solution:** Test the battery health and charging system output, and ensure all software modules are updated to prevent parasitic drains.

## Interior

### Driver Seat

The cabin offers extensive adjustments and luxury finishes, but buyers should personally evaluate seat comfort during a test drive.

**Seat Cushion Irregularity**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have reported abnormal lumps or protruding internal bolsters within the lower seat cushion, leading to discomfort or leg numbness on longer drives.
- **Solution:** Inspect the physical padding and internal frame of the driver seat during inspection to ensure proper cushion contouring.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V134000: Tires:Sidewall](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lexus&model=LS&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** If the tire tread layer separates from the vehicle, vehicle handling and stability may be affected, increasing the risk of crash.
- **What to do:** Toyota will notify owners, and Lexus dealers will replace the affected tires, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V275000: Air Bags:Sensor:Occupant Classification:Front Passenger](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lexus&model=LS&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 208, "Occupant Crash Protection." Incorrect detection of an occupant may result in improper air bag deployment during a crash, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and repair the front passenger seat, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2018 Lexus LS 500h delivers the tranquil ride, upscale cabin, and advanced engineering expected of a flagship luxury sedan. While owner complaints point to occasional electrical quirks, suspension alignment sensitivities, and localized seat discomfort, these do not overshadow the vehicle's overall build quality. Prospective buyers should conduct a thorough pre-purchase inspection focusing on electronics, tire wear, and powertrain operation to ensure a worry-free ownership experience.
