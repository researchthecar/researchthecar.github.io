Alright, here's a comprehensive breakdown of common issues and complaints for the 2019 Lexus GS 350, formatted for your website and incorporating the requested level of detail.

The 2019 Lexus GS 350 is a mid-size luxury sedan known for its comfortable ride, elegant interior, and reliability. It comes standard with a 3.5-liter V6 engine producing 311 horsepower and 280 lb-ft of torque, paired with an 8-speed automatic transmission. All-wheel drive (AWD) is available as an option. People generally appreciate the GS 350 for its smooth and quiet ride, user-friendly technology, and Lexus's reputation for dependability. Common complaints often revolve around the somewhat dated infotainment system, the relatively small rear seat, and the lack of some of the more cutting-edge features found in newer competitors.

**Engine**

**3.5L V6 (2GR-FKS)**

*   **Fuel Injector Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rough idling, misfires, check engine light illuminated, decreased fuel economy. This is sometimes attributed to issues with direct injection systems causing carbon buildup.
    *   Solution: Replacement of faulty fuel injectors and potentially cleaning of carbon deposits on intake valves.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1500+ depending on the number of injectors replaced and labor rates.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**8-Speed Automatic (AA80E)**

*   **Harsh Shifting**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Noticeable hard shifts, especially between lower gears. Some owners report the issue becoming more prevalent as the vehicle ages and mileage increases.
    *   Solution: Transmission fluid flush (using the correct Lexus-specified fluid), software update (if available), or in more severe cases, valve body replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Creaking Noise (Front)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Creaking or squeaking noises emanating from the front suspension, particularly when going over bumps or turning at low speeds. This can often be attributed to worn-out sway bar bushings or ball joints.
    *   Solution: Inspection and replacement of worn sway bar bushings and/or ball joints.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Navigation/Infotainment System Freezing/Lag**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The infotainment system (including navigation, audio, and climate controls) may freeze, become unresponsive, or exhibit noticeable lag.
    *   Solution: Software update (if available), or in some cases, replacement of the infotainment system unit.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 - $200+ (for software updates), $1000+ (for system replacement).
    *   Recalls: None Found
*   **Battery Drain**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The vehicle's battery drains unexpectedly, especially if the car sits for a few days without being driven. This can be caused by a parasitic drain from a faulty module or accessory.
    *   Solution: Diagnostic testing to identify the source of the parasitic drain and repair or replace the affected component.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+ depending on the source of the drain.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Automatic high beam malfunction**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Automatic high beams can turn on even in broad daylight.
    *   Solution: The sensor on the windshield might be dirty or obstructed. Clean it with a soft cloth. If the problem persists, the high beam system sensor can be reset by a Lexus mechanic.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0-200+ depending on whether the sensor needs to be reset by the dealership.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Driver's Seat Squeaking**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Squeaking or creaking noise coming from the driver's seat, often when adjusting the seat or during driving.
    *   Solution: Lubricating the seat rails and joints or tightening loose bolts. In some cases, the seat frame may need repair or replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2019 Lexus GS 350 is a generally reliable vehicle. The most common issues reported are related to the infotainment system, engine fuel injectors, and some suspension components. The severity of these issues varies, with some being relatively minor annoyances and others potentially requiring more costly repairs. While the GS 350 maintains a strong reputation for reliability, potential buyers should be aware of these potential problems and factor them into their purchasing decision.
