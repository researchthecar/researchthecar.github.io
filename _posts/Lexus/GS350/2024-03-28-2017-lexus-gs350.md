**Introduction**

The 2017 Lexus GS350 is a luxury sedan powered by a 3.5-liter V6 engine that produces 311 horsepower and 280 lb-ft of torque. It comes with an eight-speed automatic transmission and rear-wheel drive. The GS350 is praised for its refined interior, comfortable ride, and strong performance. However, it has also been criticized for its high price and lack of driver engagement.

**Engine**

* **Rear Main Seal Leak**
    * Description/Symptoms: Oil leaking from the rear of the engine
    * Solution: Replace the rear main seal
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

* **None Found**

**Suspension**

* **Front Suspension Noise**
    * Description/Symptoms: Clunking or rattling noise from the front suspension
    * Solution: Replace the front sway bar bushings
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain**
    * Description/Symptoms: Battery draining quickly even when the car is off
    * Solution: Replace the battery
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Headlight Condensation**
    * Description/Symptoms: Condensation inside the headlights
    * Solution: Replace the headlights
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Climate Control Malfunction**
    * Description/Symptoms: Climate control system not working properly
    * Solution: Replace the climate control module
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2017 Lexus GS350 is a reliable vehicle with a few minor issues. The most common issues are oil leaks, suspension noise, and battery drain. The estimated cost to fix these issues ranges from $200 to $1,000. Overall, the GS350 is a good choice for those looking for a luxury sedan with a comfortable ride and strong performance.
