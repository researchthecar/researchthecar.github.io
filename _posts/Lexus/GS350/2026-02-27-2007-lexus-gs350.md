# 2007 Lexus GS350

### Overview
The 2007 Lexus GS350, equipped with a potent 3.5L V6 engine and a smooth 6-speed automatic transmission, is generally regarded as a reliable luxury sedan. It offers a blend of performance, comfort, and advanced features for its era. While most owners report a positive ownership experience with minimal unexpected repairs, like any vehicle, there are a few areas that have seen occasional reports of issues. This analysis focuses on documented technical problems beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential areas of concern for prospective buyers or current owners.

---


## Engine

### 3.5L V6 (2GR-FSE)
**Oil Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Leaking oil from valve covers or oil pan.
- **Solution:** Replacement of gaskets or seals.
- **Cost:** $300 - $700

**Carbon Buildup**
- **Symptoms:** Rough idle, reduced power, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Intake manifold cleaning or walnut blasting.
- **Cost:** $400 - $800

### Summary Verdict
The 2007 Lexus GS350 stands out as a highly reliable luxury sedan, with its powertrain components demonstrating remarkable durability. The primary areas that may require attention are minor oil leaks from the engine and potential carbon buildup, both of which are manageable with timely maintenance. Electrical issues, particularly with the infotainment system, are not uncommon for vehicles of this age and can range from software glitches to hardware failures. Overall, the GS350 offers a strong value proposition for those seeking a dependable and comfortable vehicle, provided that potential buyers are aware of and prepared for the possibility of minor electrical repairs.
