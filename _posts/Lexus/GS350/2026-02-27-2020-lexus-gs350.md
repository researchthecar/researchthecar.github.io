# 2020 Lexus GS350 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2020 Lexus GS350 represents the final year of Lexus's midsize luxury sport sedan, offering a refined blend of comfort and a naturally aspirated V6 engine. As a mature luxury vehicle, it generally benefits from strong build quality and straightforward mechanical systems. Prospective buyers examining this model should still review vehicle history records and inspect key components during a pre-purchase evaluation.

---

## Engine

### 3.5L V6 (2GR-FKS)

This 3.5-liter naturally aspirated V6 produces approximately 311 horsepower and 280 lb-ft of torque. It is widely regarded as a durable, highly reliable engine option for daily driving.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Sport Direct Shift Automatic

This 8-speed automatic transmission pairs with rear-wheel drive models to deliver smooth shifts and responsive gear selection. It maintains a solid reputation for long-term mechanical dependability.

### 6-Speed Automatic

This traditional 6-speed automatic transmission is utilized in all-wheel-drive configurations. It offers proven mechanical durability and predictable shift patterns for everyday use.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V222000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lexus&model=GS&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** Fuel pump failure can cause an engine stall, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the fuel pump assembly, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2020 Lexus GS350 remains a compelling choice for used-car buyers seeking a comfortable, well-built luxury sedan with a robust powertrain lineup. While owner reports to regulatory bodies are minimal, buyers should always conduct a thorough test drive and mechanical inspection to verify overall condition.
