**2016 Lexus GS350**

The 2016 Lexus GS350 is a luxury sedan offering a combination of comfort, performance, and reliability. It was available with a 3.5L V6 engine paired with an 8-speed automatic transmission, producing 311 horsepower and 280 lb-ft of torque.

The GS350 has received positive reviews for its spacious interior, impressive performance, and comfortable ride. However, like any vehicle, it has had its fair share of reported issues.

**Engine**

* **3.5L V6 Engine**

    * **Carbon Build-up on Intake Valves**
        * Description/Symptoms: The accumulation of carbon deposits on the intake valves can lead to reduced engine performance, rough idling, and potentially more serious issues if not addressed.
        * Solution: The carbon deposits can be cleaned through a process called walnut blasting or by manually removing them.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
        * Recalls: NHTSA Campaign Number 19V067000

**Transmission**

* **8-Speed Automatic Transmission**

    * **Hesitation or Jerking During Shifting**
        * Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported experiencing hesitation or jerking during gear changes, especially in low-speed situations.
        * Solution: Updating the transmission software and/or replacing the transmission valve body assembly can resolve the issue.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **No Issues Found**

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain**
    * Description/Symptoms: Various owners have reported issues with excessive battery drain, resulting in difficulty starting the vehicle or a dead battery.
    * Solution: Identifying and fixing the source of the drain, which can include faulty electrical components, wiring issues, or software glitches.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
        * Recalls: NHTSA Campaign Number 18V671000

**Exterior**

* **Tail Light Condensation**
    * Description/Symptoms: Moisture can accumulate inside the tail light assemblies, causing condensation and potentially affecting their functionality.
    * Solution: Replacing the affected tail light assembly.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
        * Recalls: NHTSA Campaign Number 17V219000

**Interior**

* **Climate Control Malfunction**
    * Description/Symptoms: Issues with the climate control system can include inoperative buttons, incorrect temperature regulation, or excessive noise.
    * Solution: Repairing or replacing faulty climate control components, such as the control panel, actuators, or sensors.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+

**Summary**

Overall, the 2016 Lexus GS350 has received positive reviews and is generally considered a reliable vehicle. However, like any other car, it has had its share of reported issues, including carbon build-up on intake valves, transmission hesitation or jerking, battery drain, tail light condensation, and climate control malfunctions. The severity and cost of these issues vary, but most can be addressed with professional repairs.
