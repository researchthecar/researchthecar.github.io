### Overview
The 2016 Lexus GS F is a high-performance luxury sedan known for its potent V8 engine and engaging driving dynamics. As a performance-oriented model, it generally upholds Lexus's reputation for reliability, though specific components may experience issues under demanding use. This analysis focuses on documented technical reliability concerns beyond routine maintenance, examining key areas such as the powertrain, suspension, electrical systems, and interior/exterior components. While the GS F is built with quality in mind, understanding potential weak points can aid owners in proactive maintenance and informed ownership.

### Summary Verdict
The 2016 Lexus GS F demonstrates strong overall reliability, consistent with the brand's reputation. The powerful 5.0L V8 engine and 8-speed automatic transmission are robust and rarely present significant issues. Potential concerns are primarily concentrated in the electrical system, with occasional infotainment glitches and rare instances of battery drain requiring diagnosis. The adaptive suspension can also be a source of minor issues, typically related to sensors. Interior components like seat actuators may require attention over time. Despite these minor points, the GS F remains a dependable performance sedan, with most owners experiencing trouble-free operation.
