**Introduction:**

The 2019 Lexus GS F is a high-performance luxury sedan powered by a naturally aspirated 5.0L V8 engine, delivering 467 horsepower and 389 lb-ft of torque. Paired exclusively with an 8-speed automatic transmission, it offers a thrilling driving experience with its rear-wheel-drive configuration. The GS F is praised for its agile handling, impressive acceleration, and comfortable interior. However, like any vehicle, it has its share of reported issues.

**Engine**

* **5.0L V8**
    * **Excessive Oil Consumption**
        * Description/Symptoms: Some owners have experienced higher-than-normal oil consumption, requiring frequent top-ups.
        * Solution: Replace piston rings, oil control valve, and other affected components.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $3,000 - $6,000+
        * Recalls: None Found
    * **Misfiring**
        * Description/Symptoms: Engine misfires can occur, causing rough idling, power loss, and check engine light illumination.
        * Solution: Replace spark plugs, ignition coils, or fuel injectors as needed.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

* **8-Speed Automatic**
    * **Harsh Shifting**
        * Description/Symptoms: Rough and delayed gear changes are reported by some owners.
        * Solution: Update transmission software, flush transmission fluid, or rebuild transmission.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $3,000+
        * Recalls: None Found
    * **Loss of Power**
        * Description/Symptoms: Sudden loss of power while driving can occur due to transmission issues.
        * Solution: Replace transmission control module or torque converter.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $6,000+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Adaptive Suspension Malfunction**
        * Description/Symptoms: The adaptive suspension system may fail, leading to a poor ride quality and handling problems.
        * Solution: Replace suspension components, struts, or control module.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $4,000+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain**
        * Description/Symptoms: Battery may drain quickly even when the vehicle is not in use.
        * Solution: Replace battery, alternator, or check for faulty electrical components.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+
        * Recalls: None Found
* **ECU Issues**
        * Description/Symptoms: Electronic control unit (ECU) problems can lead to various electrical malfunctions.
        * Solution: Replace ECU or reprogram software.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $3,000+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **No Issues Found**

**Interior**

* **Radio Malfunctions**
        * Description/Symptoms: Radio may experience intermittent power loss, distorted sound, or unresponsive touch controls.
        * Solution: Update software or replace radio unit.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

The 2019 Lexus GS F is a generally reliable vehicle, but it has faced some reported issues, particularly concerning the engine, transmission, and electrical systems. The severity of these issues can vary, with some being minor inconveniences and others requiring substantial repairs. The cost to fix these issues can be significant, but it is important to note that not all GS F owners will experience these problems.
