# 2012 Lexus HS250h

### Overview
The 2012 Lexus HS250h, a compact luxury hybrid sedan, aimed to blend fuel efficiency with Lexus's renowned refinement. It shares its powertrain with the Toyota Prius, featuring a 2.4-liter four-cylinder engine paired with an electric motor. While generally considered reliable, as is typical for Lexus vehicles, some specific areas have seen more attention from owners and technicians. This analysis focuses on documented technical issues beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential concerns for owners and prospective buyers.

---


## Engine

### 2.4L Hybrid Synergy Drive (2AZ-FXE)
**Hybrid Battery Degradation**
- **Symptoms:** Reduced fuel economy, loss of electric-only driving capability, dashboard warning lights.
- **Solution:** Hybrid battery pack replacement.
- **Cost:** $2,500 - $4,000

**Inverter Cooling Pump Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Overheating of the hybrid system, dashboard warning lights related to hybrid system faults.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the inverter cooling pump.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600

### Summary Verdict
The 2012 Lexus HS250h generally exhibits strong reliability, consistent with the brand's reputation. The primary concerns revolve around the hybrid system's longevity, specifically the hybrid battery pack and the inverter cooling pump, which can incur significant repair costs. Electrical issues, particularly with the 12V auxiliary battery and occasional infotainment system glitches, are also noted. However, the core powertrain and mechanical components, including the transmission and suspension, are largely trouble-free. Owners should be aware of potential hybrid battery replacement costs as the vehicle ages.
