## 2012 Lexus HS250h

The 2012 Lexus HS250h is a luxury hybrid sedan with an impressive 35 mpg combined fuel economy. It features a 2.4-liter Atkinson-cycle gasoline engine paired with an electric motor, producing 187 horsepower and 182 lb-ft of torque. The HS250h is known for its quiet and comfortable interior, as well as its advanced safety features. However, it has received some criticism for its sluggish acceleration and lack of handling prowess.

**Engine**

**2.4L Atkinson-Cycle**

* **Oil Leak from PCV Valve:** The PCV valve may fail and cause an oil leak, leading to a loss of engine oil and potential engine damage. The solution is to replace the faulty PCV valve. Estimated cost to fix: $200 - $400.
* **Intake Manifold Gasket Failure:** The intake manifold gasket may fail, causing a vacuum leak and rough engine idle. The solution is to replace the faulty gasket. Estimated cost to fix: $300 - $500.

**Transmission**

**Suspension**

* **Strut Assembly Failure:** The strut assembly may fail, causing a clunking noise or uneven tire wear. The solution is to replace the faulty strut assembly. Estimated cost to fix: $600 - $1,000 per strut.

**Electrical**

* **Battery Failure:** The 12-volt battery may fail prematurely, causing electrical system malfunctions and difficulty starting the vehicle. The solution is to replace the faulty battery. Estimated cost to fix: $150 - $300.
* **Alternator Failure:** The alternator may fail, causing the battery to not charge and the vehicle to stall. The solution is to replace the faulty alternator. Estimated cost to fix: $500 - $800.

**Exterior**

* **Headlight Condensation:** Headlights may experience condensation build-up, impairing visibility. The solution is to replace the affected headlight assembly. Estimated cost to fix: $400 - $600 per headlight.

**Interior**

* **Climate Control Issue:** The climate control system may experience issues, resulting in insufficient heating or cooling. The solution varies depending on the specific issue and may involve replacing faulty sensors or actuators. Estimated cost to fix: $200 - $600.
* **Navigation System Malfunction:** The navigation system may malfunction, displaying incorrect information or freezing. The solution is to update the navigation software or replace the faulty navigation unit. Estimated cost to fix: $200 - $1,000.

**Summary**

Overall, the 2012 Lexus HS250h has a relatively low number of reported issues. Most problems are relatively minor and inexpensive to fix. However, some issues, such as strut assembly failure and navigation system malfunctions, can be more severe and costly to repair.
