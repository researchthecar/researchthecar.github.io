# 2018 Lexus NX300h Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used luxury compact crossover, looking closely at owner experiences helps highlight potential trouble spots. While luxury models generally offer refined driving dynamics and upscale cabins, checking specific powertrain specs and historical owner complaints can guide your purchase decision. NHTSA owner reports provide insight into areas like engine performance and braking feel that are worth verifying during a pre-purchase test drive.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4

The 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine generates approximately 235 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque, providing standard motivation for non-hybrid models. It balances everyday responsiveness with acceptable efficiency, though buyers should verify proper maintenance history.

### 2.5L Hybrid Inline-4

The 2.5-liter naturally aspirated four-cylinder paired with an electric hybrid system produces a net 194 horsepower. It offers excellent fuel economy for city driving, though some owners have reported intermittent fuel-related stalling concerns.

**Engine Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have reported unexpected engine stalling while driving at various speeds accompanied by warning messages.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified technician inspect the fuel system and check for applicable manufacturer recalls or software updates.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

The conventional 6-speed automatic transmission works alongside the turbocharged engine to deliver power to the front or all four wheels. It generally operates smoothly, with few systemic complaints reported by owners.

### Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)

The electronic continuously variable transmission handles power delivery seamlessly in hybrid variants. It prioritizes fuel efficiency and smooth transitions during acceleration.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

Brake system complaints focus primarily on rare instances of pedal feel inconsistencies and stopping performance concerns.

**Brake Pedal Travel and Stopping Concerns**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have occasionally noted unusual brake pedal softness, extended stopping distances, or unexpected vehicle acceleration behavior.
- **Solution:** Inspect the brake pads, rotors, master cylinder, and electronic brake booster components during a pre-purchase evaluation.

## Electrical

### Electrical System and Accessories

Electrical complaints are infrequent but cover miscellaneous cabin accessories and auxiliary systems.

**Accessory and Power Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Isolated reports mention unexpected mirror malfunctions or electrical accessory faults.
- **Solution:** Test all power mirrors, windows, lighting fixtures, and dashboard displays before finalizing your purchase.

## Summary Verdict

Overall, this vehicle offers a comfortable ride and upscale features, but careful inspection is advised. Pay close attention to engine performance, hybrid propulsion behavior, and brake responsiveness during a test drive to ensure everything operates correctly.
