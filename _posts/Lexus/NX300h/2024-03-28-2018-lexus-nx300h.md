**Introduction**

The 2018 Lexus NX300h is a luxury compact SUV known for its stylish design, comfortable interior, and fuel-efficient hybrid powertrain. It comes with a single engine option and two transmission options: a continuously variable transmission (CVT) and a six-speed automatic transmission. The engine produces 194 horsepower and 184 lb-ft of torque, providing a smooth and efficient driving experience. Overall, owners appreciate the Lexus NX300h's reliability, safety features, and spacious cabin, but some have reported issues with the vehicle's electrical system and interior.

**Electrical**

* **Malfunctioning Infotainment System**
  - Description/Symptoms: The infotainment system may freeze, reboot, or become unresponsive.
  - Solution: Replace the infotainment system unit.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+

**Exterior**

**No Issues Found**

**Interior**

* **Squeaky Sunroof**
  - Description/Symptoms: The sunroof may produce a squeaking noise when opening or closing.
  - Solution: Replace the sunroof seal or lubricate the tracks.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500

**Other**

* **Low Fuel Economy**
  - Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported lower fuel economy than expected.
  - Solution: Ensure proper tire pressure, replace the air filter, or have the vehicle inspected for any mechanical issues.
- Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the underlying cause

**Summary**

Overall, the 2018 Lexus NX300h has a relatively low number of reported issues, and most of them are minor. The electrical system and interior may require some attention, but the vehicle's reliability and performance are generally well-rated. Maintenance costs can vary depending on the severity of the issue but are generally within the range of other vehicles in its class.
