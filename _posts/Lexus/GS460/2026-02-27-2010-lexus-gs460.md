## Electrical

### General Electrical System
**Infotainment System Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Occasional freezing or unresponsiveness of the touchscreen, navigation system errors, or Bluetooth connectivity issues.
- **Solution:** Software updates for the infotainment system, or in some cases, replacement of the head unit.
- **Cost:** $100 - $1500 (depending on software update vs. unit replacement)

**Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Premature battery failure or unexplained battery drain, potentially due to parasitic draw from modules.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis to identify the source of the parasitic draw, which may involve testing modules or wiring harnesses. Battery replacement may be necessary.
- **Cost:** $200 - $600

### Summary Verdict
The 2010 Lexus GS460 demonstrates a high level of reliability, consistent with Lexus's reputation. The powertrain, including the 4.6L V8 engine and 6-speed automatic transmission, is exceptionally robust with no significant widespread issues reported. Similarly, the suspension, exterior, and interior components are generally durable. The primary areas where minor concerns have been noted are within the electrical system, specifically occasional infotainment glitches and potential battery drain issues. These are typically manageable with software updates or targeted diagnostics and repairs. Overall, the GS460 is a dependable luxury sedan with a strong track record.
