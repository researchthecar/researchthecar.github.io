**2014 Lexus RX450h**

The 2014 Lexus RX450h is a hybrid luxury SUV that combines a 3.5-liter V6 engine with an electric motor for a combined output of 295 horsepower and 268 lb-ft of torque. It comes with a standard continuously variable transmission (CVT). The RX450h is praised for its comfortable ride, spacious interior, and impressive fuel economy, but it has also been criticized for its lack of driving excitement and high price tag.

**Engine**

* 3.5L V6

* **Noise from engine bay**
  * Description/Symptoms: A rattling or knocking noise from the engine bay, particularly at idle or low RPMs.
  * Solution: Replacing the camshaft adjuster.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $1,500+
  * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

* Continuously variable transmission (CVT)

* **Hesitation or shudder during acceleration**
  * Description/Symptoms: The transmission hesitates or shudders when accelerating, especially from a stop or at low speeds.
  * Solution: Updating the transmission control module software.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 - $100
  * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* No Issues Found

**Electrical**

* **Malfunctioning backup camera**
  * Description/Symptoms: The backup camera fails to display an image or displays a distorted or blurry image.
  * Solution: Replacing the backup camera.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $700+
  * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Defective wiper motors**
  * Description/Symptoms: The wiper motors fail prematurely, causing the wipers to stop working or work intermittently.
  * Solution: Replacing the wiper motors.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
  * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Squeaky dashboard**
  * Description/Symptoms: The dashboard makes a squeaking noise when driving over bumps or rough roads.
  * Solution: Replacing the dashboard panel or applying a lubricant.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
  * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

Overall, the 2014 Lexus RX450h has a relatively low number of reported issues, with most being minor and relatively inexpensive to fix. However, some issues, such as the camshaft adjuster noise and backup camera malfunction, can be more severe and costly to resolve.
