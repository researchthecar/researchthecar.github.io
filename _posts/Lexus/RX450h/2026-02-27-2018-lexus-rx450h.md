# 2018 Lexus RX450h Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used luxury hybrid crossover, the Lexus RX 450h generally commands strong respect for its plush ride, upscale cabin, and overall mechanical pedigree. While the brand is heavily praised for dependability, owner reports submitted to safety authorities highlight a handful of recurring mechanical, electrical, and structural grievances that prospective buyers should keep on their radar during a pre-purchase inspection.

---

## Engine

### 3.5L V6 Hybrid (2RE/2GR-FXS)

The 3.5-liter V6 hybrid engine pairs with electric motors to deliver around 308 net horsepower, providing smooth acceleration and strong fuel economy. This hybrid powertrain is generally robust, though minor sensor and misfire complaints occasionally surface.

**Cylinder Misfire**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may experience a check engine light illumination along with engine hesitation or cylinder #1 misfires.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified technician diagnose the ignition coils, spark plugs, or fuel delivery components to isolate the misfiring cylinder.

**Fuel Pump Sensor Short**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have reported electrical shorting within the fuel pump sensor assembly, potentially causing unexpected vehicle stalling.
- **Solution:** Replace the faulty fuel suction tube and pump assembly to restore accurate fuel readings and reliable engine operation.

## Transmission

### Electronically Controlled Continuously Variable Transmission (ECVT)

The electronic continuously variable transmission distributes power seamlessly between the V6 engine and electric drive system. It operates quietly and reliably, with very few mechanical transmission-specific complaints reported by owners.

**Transmission Parking Behavior**
- **Symptoms:** Some operators note that the vehicle may not automatically shift into park if the driver shuts down the engine while in gear.
- **Solution:** Always ensure the vehicle is manually engaged in Park before exiting to prevent unexpected movement.

## Suspension

### Ball Joints and Suspension Linkage

The steering and suspension components maintain a comfortable ride quality, though early wear on rubber boots and joints can occasionally require component replacement.

**Leaking Ball Joints and Sway Bar Links**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may notice vague steering control or loose handling characteristics, while inspections reveal grease leaking from the ball joints.
- **Solution:** Inspect steering and suspension linkages regularly, and replace worn ball joints or sway bar links as necessary.

## Brakes

### Brake Actuator and Service Brakes

The regenerative braking system blends electric motor resistance with traditional friction brakes. While functional, the hydraulic brake actuator is known to generate unwelcome noises in some vehicles.

**Brake Squawk Noise**
- **Symptoms:** Applying the brake pedal can produce an irritating squawking or barking noise.
- **Solution:** Replacing the brake actuator has been attempted by dealers, though some owners report the noise can eventually return.
- **Cost:** Repair costs vary depending on warranty coverage and dealer participation.

## Exterior

### Door Handles and Sunroof

Exterior body hardware is generally durable, but owners have reported occasional mechanical snags with door release handles and panoramic sunshades.

**Interior Door Handle Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Pulling the interior door handle fails unlatching the door, requiring occupants to roll down the window to open it from the outside.
- **Solution:** Inspect and replace the broken interior door handle mechanism.

**Sunshade Bunching**
- **Symptoms:** The panoramic sunroof sunshade may bunch up or fail to retract fully when the switch is engaged.
- **Solution:** Inspect the sunshade track and motor assembly, and replace jammed or misaligned fabric components.

## Interior

### HVAC System

Electrical systems on modern luxury hybrids manage numerous comfort and convenience features, with occasional reports of cabin climate actuators acting up.

**HVAC Servo Motor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Climate control air distribution or temperature mixing may fail to function correctly due to a stuck or dead servo motor.
- **Solution:** Diagnose and replace the failed climate control servo motor behind the dashboard.

## Summary Verdict

The Lexus RX 450h remains a highly competent and generally reliable hybrid luxury crossover choice for used-car buyers. While owner complaints point to specific concerns such as brake actuator noises, interior door handle durability, and occasional electronic or fuel component hiccups, a thorough pre-purchase inspection can help verify that these systems are operating properly before you buy.
