The 2013 Lexus RX 450h is a luxury hybrid SUV known for its comfortable ride, excellent fuel economy, and reliability. It features a 3.5-liter V6 engine paired with an electric motor, producing a combined 295 horsepower and 247 lb-ft of torque. There were no other engine options, but it did come in FWD or AWD configurations. Owners often praise the RX 450h for its quiet cabin, smooth acceleration, and Lexus's reputation for quality. Common complaints, however, sometimes involve the infotainment system's complexity and the somewhat uninspiring driving dynamics.

**Electric Motor**

*   **Hybrid Battery Degradation**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Reduced fuel economy, decreased electric-only driving range, "Check Hybrid System" warning light. The battery's capacity diminishes over time, impacting its ability to store and deliver energy.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the hybrid battery pack.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $3,000 - $7,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Inverter Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, inability to start, hybrid system warning light, overheating.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the inverter assembly.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $3,000 - $5,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

*   **CVT Transmission Jerking/Hesitation**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Jerky acceleration, hesitation when accelerating from a stop or at low speeds, unusual noises from the transmission.
    *   Solution: Software update for the transmission control module (TCM) or replacement of the CVT transmission.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500 for software update; $4,000 - $7,000+ for transmission replacement
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Air Suspension Compressor Failure (If Equipped)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Uneven ride height, sagging suspension, inability to adjust ride height, "Check Air Suspension" warning light.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the air suspension compressor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Rear Air Shock Leaks (If Equipped)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Leaking air causing the vehicle to sag.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the rear air shocks.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800-$1,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Navigation/Infotainment System Freezing/Crashing**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The navigation or infotainment screen freezes, becomes unresponsive, or crashes and restarts intermittently.
    *   Solution: Software update to the navigation/infotainment system or replacement of the head unit.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300 for software update; $1,000 - $3,000+ for head unit replacement
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Power Door Lock Actuator Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Door locks not working, making clicking noises.
    *   Solution: Replacing the actuator.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200-$500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Automatic Liftgate Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Liftgate not opening or closing properly.
    *   Solution: Replacing the liftgate motor or struts.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $700+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Dashboard Cracking**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Dashboard developing cracks over time.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the dashboard, or use of dashboard covers.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+ for dashboard replacement
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Navigation Screen Delamination**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Bubbling or separation of the navigation screen layers, making it difficult to see the display.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the navigation screen.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $3,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2013 Lexus RX 450h generally enjoys a reputation for reliability, but owners should be aware of potential issues, particularly related to the hybrid battery, inverter, CVT transmission, and electrical components. While some repairs, like software updates, are relatively inexpensive, others, such as hybrid battery or transmission replacements, can be quite costly. Potential buyers should consider these potential expenses when evaluating the vehicle.
