Here's a comprehensive breakdown of common issues and complaints associated with the 2012 Lexus LS600h, formatted for your website, aiming for accuracy and consistency.

The 2012 Lexus LS600h is a luxury hybrid sedan known for its smooth ride, opulent interior, and advanced technology. It's powered by a 5.0-liter V8 engine paired with an electric motor, producing a combined 438 horsepower and delivering responsive acceleration. Drivers often praise its quiet cabin, comfortable seats, and comprehensive suite of features. Some common complaints, though, revolve around its complex hybrid system, potential for costly repairs, and somewhat limited trunk space due to the battery pack. A non-hybrid version, the LS460, was also available with a 4.6-liter V8, and neither version came with different transmissions.

**Electric Motor**

* **Hybrid Battery Degradation/Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Reduced fuel economy, decreased electric motor assistance, error messages on the dashboard (e.g., "Check Hybrid System"), and in severe cases, the car may refuse to start or drive.
    * Solution: Replacement of the hybrid battery pack. Some owners opt for refurbished batteries, while others choose new ones.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $3,000 - $8,000+ depending on new, refurbished, or DIY installation
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

* **CVT Transmission Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: Shuddering during acceleration, hesitation, and error codes related to the CVT system.
    * Solution: Repair or replacement of the CVT.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $4,000 - $7,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Air Suspension Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The vehicle sits unevenly, rides harshly, or the air suspension compressor runs constantly. Error messages may appear on the dashboard related to the suspension system.
    * Solution: Replacement of failed air struts, air compressor, or height sensors. Addressing leaks in the air lines.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $3,000+ per strut, $500 - $1,500+ for the compressor, depending on the parts and labor.
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Navigation System/Display Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: Freezing, slow response times, or complete failure of the navigation screen and associated infotainment functions.
    * Solution: Software update, replacement of the navigation unit, or repair of damaged wiring.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+ depending on the issue and solution.
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Power Window/Door Lock Malfunctions**
    * Description/Symptoms: Intermittent or complete failure of power windows or door locks.
    * Solution: Replacement of faulty window motors, door lock actuators, or wiring repair.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+ per window/door.
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Parking Assist Sensor Malfunction**
    * Description/Symptoms: Erroneous beeping or failure to detect obstacles when parking.
    * Solution: Replacement of faulty parking sensors or wiring repair.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Dashboard Cracking**
    * Description/Symptoms: Cracking or splitting of the dashboard material, especially in areas exposed to direct sunlight.
    * Solution: Dashboard replacement or repair using specialized materials.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $2,000+ depending on the repair method.
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Automatic Trunk Lid Malfunction**
    * Description/Symptoms: Failure of the automatic trunk lid to open or close properly.
    * Solution: Replacement of the trunk lid motor, struts, or wiring repair.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2012 Lexus LS600h, while a luxurious and comfortable vehicle, can present owners with some potentially costly issues. The most significant concerns revolve around the hybrid battery and air suspension system, which can lead to expensive repairs. Electrical gremlins and interior issues like dashboard cracking are also common. The cost of these repairs can be significant, so potential buyers should carefully inspect the vehicle and consider a pre-purchase inspection by a qualified mechanic.
