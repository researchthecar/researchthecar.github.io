**2011 Lexus LS600h**

The 2011 Lexus LS600h is a luxury sedan that combines elegance and high performance. It is powered by a 5.0-liter V8 engine paired with an electric motor, producing 438 horsepower and 385 lb-ft of torque. The LS600h is available with either rear-wheel drive or all-wheel drive and comes with a variety of features, including a navigation system, a premium audio system, and leather upholstery.

While the 2011 Lexus LS600h is a well-respected vehicle, it is not without its problems. Here is a comprehensive list of the specific issues people are having with the 2011 Lexus LS600h, categorized by major components:

**Engine**

* **5.0-liter V8 engine**
    * **Intake manifold gasket failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaks from the intake manifold, causing overheating and loss of power.
        * Solution: Replace the intake manifold gasket.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,000+
        * Recalls: None Found
* **Water pump failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: The water pump fails, causing the engine to overheat.
        * Solution: Replace the water pump.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $600 - $800+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

* **Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)**
    * **Hesitation or shuddering during acceleration**
        * Description/Symptoms: The transmission hesitates or shudders when accelerating from a stop or at low speeds.
        * Solution: Reprogram the transmission control module or replace the transmission.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $3,000+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Air suspension failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: The air suspension system fails, causing the vehicle to sag or ride rough.
        * Solution: Replace the air suspension compressor or struts.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Battery drain**
        * Description/Symptoms: The battery drains prematurely, even when the vehicle is not in use.
        * Solution: Test and replace the battery or alternator.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+
        * Recalls: None Found
* **Headlight failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: One or both headlights fail to illuminate.
        * Solution: Replace the headlight bulb or ballast.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Sunroof leaks**
        * Description/Symptoms: Water leaks into the cabin from the sunroof.
        * Solution: Replace the sunroof seals or adjust the sunroof alignment.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Navigation system glitches**
        * Description/Symptoms: The navigation system freezes, crashes, or displays incorrect information.
        * Solution: Update the navigation system software or replace the navigation unit.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
        * Recalls: None Found
* **Climate control issues**
        * Description/Symptoms: The climate control system does not blow cold or hot air, or the fan does not work.
        * Solution: Repair or replace the climate control actuator or blower motor.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2011 Lexus LS600h is a well-built and luxurious vehicle, but it is not without its problems. The most common issues reported by owners include intake manifold gasket failure, water pump failure, transmission hesitation, air suspension failure, battery drain, and navigation system glitches. These issues can be expensive to fix, but fortunately, most are covered under warranty. Overall, the 2011 Lexus LS600h is a reliable and enjoyable vehicle, but it is important to be aware of the potential issues that can arise.
