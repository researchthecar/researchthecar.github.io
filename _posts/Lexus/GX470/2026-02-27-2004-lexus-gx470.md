# 2004 Lexus GX470

### Overview
The 2004 Lexus GX470 is a luxury mid-size SUV known for its robust construction and Toyota's legendary reliability. Built on a truck chassis, it offers a comfortable ride and capable off-road performance, largely due to its standard Kinetic Dynamic Suspension System (KDSS). The GX470 is powered by a potent V8 engine, which, combined with its solid build, contributes to its strong reputation. While generally very dependable, like any vehicle, it has a few specific areas that owners have reported issues with over time, primarily related to age and mileage.

---


## Engine

### 4.7L V8 (2UZ-FE)
**Timing Belt Replacement**
- **Symptoms:** While not a failure, this is a critical maintenance item. Neglecting it can lead to catastrophic engine damage.
- **Solution:** Scheduled replacement at recommended intervals (typically 90,000 miles).
- **Cost:** $600 - $1000

**Leaking Valve Cover Gaskets**
- **Symptoms:** Oil smell, visible oil leaks around the top of the engine, potential for oil to drip onto exhaust manifolds.
- **Solution:** Replacement of valve cover gaskets.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600

**Cracked Exhaust Manifolds**
- **Symptoms:** Exhaust ticking noise, especially on cold starts, which may quiet down as the engine warms up. Can sometimes lead to exhaust leaks.
- **Solution:** Replacement of cracked exhaust manifolds, often with aftermarket stainless steel units.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1200

## Suspension

### Kinetic Dynamic Suspension System (KDSS)
**Leaking KDSS Lines/Actuators**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle leaning to one side, loss of hydraulic pressure, potential for warning lights.
- **Solution:** Replacement of leaking hydraulic lines or actuators.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1500


### Adjustable Air Suspension (Rear)
**Air Spring Leaks/Compressor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Rear end sagging, compressor running excessively, vehicle not maintaining ride height.
- **Solution:** Replacement of air springs or compressor unit.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1200


## Electrical

### Body Control Module (BCM)
**Intermittent Electrical Gremlins**
- **Symptoms:** Various electrical components malfunctioning intermittently (e.g., power windows, interior lights, dashboard warnings).
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and potential reprogramming or replacement of the BCM.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1000


### Navigation System (if equipped)
**Screen Malfunctions/Freezing**
- **Symptoms:** Navigation screen becomes unresponsive, freezes, or displays errors.
- **Solution:** Software update or replacement of the navigation unit.
- **Cost:** $400 - $1000

### Headlight Lenses
**Yellowing/Clouding**
- **Symptoms:** Reduced headlight visibility due to oxidation and UV damage.
- **Solution:** Restoration kits or replacement of headlight assemblies.
- **Cost:** $100 - $400


## Interior

### Leather Upholstery
**Wear on Driver's Seat Bolster**
- **Symptoms:** Cracking or wear on the outer edge of the driver's seat due to ingress/egress.
- **Solution:** Professional repair or replacement of seat cover.
- **Cost:** $200 - $600


### Headliner Fabric
**Saggy Headliner**
- **Symptoms:** Fabric detaching from the headliner board, especially around the sunroof.
- **Solution:** Re-gluing or replacement of the headliner.
- **Cost:** $300 - $700

### Summary Verdict
The 2004 Lexus GX470 is a highly reliable vehicle, benefiting from Toyota's robust engineering and quality control. The 4.7L V8 engine is a strong point, though routine maintenance like timing belt replacement is crucial. Potential issues tend to be age-related, such as minor fluid leaks (valve covers), exhaust manifold cracks, and wear on suspension components like the KDSS or air springs. Electrical gremlins and interior wear are also noted but are not typically widespread failures. Overall, with proper maintenance, the GX470 is expected to provide many years of dependable service, making it a solid used SUV choice.
