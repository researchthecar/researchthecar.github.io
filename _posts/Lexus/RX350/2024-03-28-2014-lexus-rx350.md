**Introduction**

The 2014 Lexus RX350 is a midsize luxury SUV known for its reliability, comfort, and high-end features. It comes standard with a 3.5L V6 engine paired with an 8-speed automatic transmission, delivering 270 horsepower and 247 lb-ft of torque. Other engine and transmission options are not available.

**Engine**

**3.5L V6**
- **Engine Stalling**
  - Description/Symptoms: The engine suddenly stalls while driving or idling, resulting in a loss of power.
  - Solution: Replacing the faulty fuel pump or fuel injectors.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,000+
- **Intake Manifold Valve Problems**
  - Description/Symptoms: Improper functioning of intake manifold valves can cause rough idling, engine hesitation, and decreased fuel efficiency.
  - Solution: Cleaning or replacing the intake manifold valves.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+
- **Recalls:** NHTSA Campaign ID: 17V311000 (Fuel Pump Failure), NHTSA Campaign ID: 20V722000 (Airbag Deployment Failure)

**Transmission**

**8-Speed Automatic**
- **Hesitation and lurching**
  - Description/Symptoms: The transmission hesitates or lurches when shifting gears, causing an uncomfortable driving experience.
  - Solution: Reprogramming the transmission control module (TCM) or replacing the transmission solenoid valve.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,500+
- **No issues found**

**Suspension**
- **Front Control Arm Bushings Wear**
  - Description/Symptoms: Worn control arm bushings can result in excessive vibrations, clunking noises, and poor handling.
  - Solution: Replacing the faulty control arm bushings.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
- **Rear Stabilizer Bar Links**
  - Description/Symptoms: Faulty stabilizer bar links can cause clunking noises and poor stability when cornering.
  - Solution: Replacing the stabilizer bar links.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $700+
- **No issues found:**

**Electrical**
- **Faulty Ignition Switch**
  - Description/Symptoms: The ignition switch may fail to turn the engine on or off, causing intermittent starting problems.
  - Solution: Replacing the ignition switch assembly.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $250 - $500+
- **Battery Drain**
  - Description/Symptoms: Excessive battery drain can lead to a dead battery, causing the vehicle to fail to start.
  - Solution: Identifying and fixing the source of the battery drain, which could be a faulty alternator, parasitic draw, or battery issues.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+
- **No issues found:**

**Exterior**

**No issues found:**

**Interior**
- **Instrument Cluster Failure**
  - Description/Symptoms: The instrument cluster may fail to display information or work intermittently, affecting visibility and vehicle control.
  - Solution: Replacing the instrument cluster.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
- **Center Console Lid Hinge Failure**
  - Description/Symptoms: The center console lid hinge may break or become loose, making it difficult to open or close the lid.
  - Solution: Replacing the center console lid hinge assembly.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
- **Climate Control Malfunction**
  - Description/Symptoms: The climate control system may not blow air, or blow air at an incorrect temperature, resulting in discomfort.
  - Solution: Diagnosis and repair of the faulty climate control components (e.g., actuator, blower motor, or control module).
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,200+

**Summary**

Overall, the 2014 Lexus RX350 is a reliable and comfortable vehicle, but it has some common issues that can affect its performance and overall driving experience. The issues range in severity and cost, with some requiring immediate attention and others being more minor inconveniences. It's important to note that not all vehicles will experience these issues, and individual experiences may vary.
