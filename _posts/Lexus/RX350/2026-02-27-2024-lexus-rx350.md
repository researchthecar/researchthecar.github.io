# 2024 Lexus RX350 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2024 Lexus RX 350 continues the luxury brand's tradition of offering a refined, comfortable midsize crossover experience. For used-car shoppers, understanding the available powertrain options and monitoring early owner feedback helps ensure a confident purchase. While luxury vehicles feature advanced electronics and complex assemblies, keeping an eye on powertrain performance and cabin technology remains wise during a pre-purchase inspection.

---

## Engine

### 2.4L Turbocharged Inline-4

This 2.4-liter turbocharged inline-4 engine produces approximately 275 horsepower and 317 lb-ft of torque, providing responsive daily acceleration. As a relatively recent powerplant addition for this model generation, it balances modern efficiency with traditional Lexus engineering standards, though buyers should verify proper maintenance history.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 8-speed automatic transmission is paired with front-wheel drive or all-wheel drive configurations, offering smooth gear shifts optimized for fuel economy. It generally operates seamlessly, contributing to the vehicle's refined highway cruising manners.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V482000: Seats:Front Assembly:Head Restraint](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lexus&model=RX&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** The head restraints on the driver and front passenger seats may be inadvertently removed without pressing the lock release button. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) Number 202, "Head Restraints." A head restraint that is inadvertently removed and not reinstalled can increase the.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the driver and front passenger seat head rest assemblies, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V059000: Seat Belts:Rear/Other:Webbing](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lexus&model=RX&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 209, "Seat Belt Assemblies." A damaged seat belt may not adequately restrain an occupant, increasing the risk of injury during a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the seat belt webbing and replace the seat belt assembly if necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V744000: Back Over Prevention:Display Function](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lexus&model=RX&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** A software error may cause the rearview camera to freeze or display a blank screen when the vehicle is in reverse. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 111, "Rear Visibility." A rearview camera that fails to display an image can reduce the driver's view behind the vehicle.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the parking assist software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V162000: Back Over Prevention:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lexus&model=RX&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** The rearview camera image may not display when the vehicle is placed in reverse. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 111, "Rear Visibility." A rearview image that does not display reduces the driver's view behind the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the rearview camera software or replace the rearview camera, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.
