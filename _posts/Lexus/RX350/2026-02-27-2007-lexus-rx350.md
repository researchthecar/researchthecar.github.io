# 2007 Lexus RX350

### Overview
The 2007 Lexus RX350 is generally regarded as a reliable luxury SUV, known for its comfortable ride, upscale interior, and robust powertrain. Built on Toyota's reputation for dependability, this generation of the RX typically avoids major widespread mechanical failures. Owners often praise its smooth performance and long-term durability. While no vehicle is entirely immune to issues, the RX350 from this model year tends to exhibit fewer significant problems compared to many of its competitors. Routine maintenance is key to preserving its longevity, but significant component failures are not commonly reported across the board. This makes it a solid choice for those seeking a dependable used SUV.

## Electrical

### Standard Electrical System
**Occasional HVAC Blower Motor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of airflow from vents, intermittent fan operation, or fan only working at certain speeds.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the HVAC blower motor resistor or the blower motor itself.
- **Cost:** $150 - $350

**Power Window Motor Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Power windows failing to operate, moving slowly, or getting stuck.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the power window motor and regulator assembly.
- **Cost:** $200 - $500 per window

### Summary Verdict
The 2007 Lexus RX350 stands out for its strong reliability, with its 3.5L V6 engine and 5-speed automatic transmission being particularly robust and rarely reporting significant issues. The chassis and suspension components also hold up well over time. The most commonly cited, though not widespread, electrical issues involve the HVAC blower motor and power window motors, which are generally manageable repairs. Exterior and interior components are typically durable. Overall, the RX350 from this year is a dependable choice, with most potential problems being minor and related to specific electrical components rather than systemic failures. Regular maintenance is the best way to ensure continued reliability.
