The 2014 Lexus RX 350 is a popular luxury crossover known for its comfortable ride, reliability, and upscale interior. The RX 350 came standard with a 3.5-liter V6 engine producing 270 horsepower and 248 lb-ft of torque, paired with a 6-speed automatic transmission. Owners generally appreciate the RX 350's smooth ride, quiet cabin, and strong resale value. Common dislikes include the somewhat dated infotainment system and the lack of a sporty driving experience.

**Engine**

**3.5L 2GR-FE V6**

*   **Oil Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks can develop from various areas, including the valve cover gaskets, timing cover, and oil pan gasket. Symptoms include visible oil leaks under the vehicle, burning oil smell, and low oil levels.
    *   Solution: Replacing the leaking gaskets or seals.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1000+, depending on the location and complexity of the repair.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Water Pump Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The water pump can fail, leading to overheating. Symptoms include a coolant leak, engine overheating, and a whining noise from the engine bay.
    *   Solution: Replacing the water pump and potentially the thermostat.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**Aisin U660E 6-Speed Automatic**

*   **Torque Converter Shudder**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported a shuddering or vibration during light acceleration, particularly at lower speeds. This can be due to torque converter issues.
    *   Solution: Replacing or rebuilding the torque converter, or performing a transmission fluid flush.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $3000+ depending on complexity
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Strut/Shock Absorber Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Worn struts or shock absorbers can cause a bouncy ride, poor handling, and increased stopping distances.
    *   Solution: Replacing the struts and/or shock absorbers.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $1200+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Navigation Screen Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The navigation screen can fail or become unresponsive.
    *   Solution: Replacing the navigation screen unit.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $2000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Battery Drain**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported experiencing issues with the vehicle battery draining unexpectedly, especially after the car has been sitting for extended periods.
    *   Solution: Getting the electrical system tested to determine which components or modules are causing the battery drain. Possible causes include a faulty alternator, parasitic drain from electrical components, a failing battery, or issues with the vehicle's computer system.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $1000+ depending on the cause of the drain and the components replaced.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Automatic Tailgate Malfunction**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The power tailgate may fail to open or close correctly, or may make unusual noises during operation.
    *   Solution: Diagnosing and repairing or replacing the tailgate motor, struts, or sensors.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Sunroof Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The sunroof may stick, fail to open or close completely, or leak.
    *   Solution: Cleaning and lubricating the sunroof tracks, repairing or replacing the sunroof motor, or replacing damaged seals.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2014 Lexus RX 350 is generally a reliable vehicle, but owners may encounter issues such as oil leaks, water pump failure, torque converter shudder, and electrical problems. The severity and cost of these issues can vary, but most are manageable with regular maintenance and prompt repairs. Addressing potential problems early can help prevent more significant and costly repairs down the line.
