# 2018 Lexus GX460 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used luxury SUV, the Lexus GX460 stands out for its rugged body-on-frame build and traditional V8 capability. While generally celebrated for long-term durability, prospective buyers should review owner-reported grievances regarding minor suspension quirks and drivetrain sensations to ensure a confident purchase.

---

## Engine

### 4.6L V8 Engine

This vehicle features a 4.6-liter naturally aspirated V8 engine producing approximately 301 horsepower and 329 lb-ft of torque. It is widely regarded as a robust and reliable powerplant capable of high mileage when properly maintained.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

Power is routed through a traditional 6-speed automatic transmission paired with a full-time four-wheel-drive system. This unit is generally dependable, offering smooth gear shifts and proven mechanical longevity.

## Suspension

### Rear Air Suspension

Suspension complaints primarily involve air-spring variations and ride-height discrepancies reported by owners.

**Vehicle Leaning and Height Inconsistency**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may sit visibly lower on one side during driving or while parked, occasionally accompanied by a hissing noise from the rear after shutting off the engine.
- **Solution:** Have the air suspension airbags, height sensors, and compressor inspected for leaks or electronic faults.

## Interior

### Sunroof Drains

Electrical and body integrity concerns occasionally highlight fluid management issues around the sunroof infrastructure.

**Sunroof Drain Clogs and Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Water may infiltrate the cabin due to clogged, disconnected, or deteriorated sunroof drain lines.
- **Solution:** Inspect and clear the sunroof drain tubes periodically to ensure proper water evacuation away from the vehicle interior.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V682000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lexus&model=GX460&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** If the fuel pump fails, the engine can stall while driving, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Toyota will notify owners, and dealers will replace the fuel pump assembly with an improved one, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V831000: Equipment:Other:Labels](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lexus&model=GX460&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 110, "Tire Selection and Rims." An incorrect load information label can result in the operator overloading the vehicle and increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The Lexus GX460 maintains a solid reputation for mechanical durability, anchored by its durable V8 engine and traditional truck-based architecture. Used car shoppers should inspect the rear suspension for any uneven ride height and check for proper drainage around the sunroof to ensure a trouble-free ownership experience.
