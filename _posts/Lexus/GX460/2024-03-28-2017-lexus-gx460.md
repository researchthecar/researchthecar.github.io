**Introduction:**

The 2017 Lexus GX460 is a mid-size luxury SUV known for its rugged off-road capabilities and sophisticated interior. It features a 4.6-liter V8 engine producing 301 horsepower and 329 lb-ft of torque, paired with a 6-speed automatic transmission. Owners appreciate the GX460's reliability, off-road prowess, and luxurious amenities. However, a few common issues have been reported.

### **Engine**

**4.6L V8**

- **Intake manifold gasket leak:** Description/Symptoms: Coolant may leak from the intake manifold gasket, causing a burning smell in the cabin, reduced engine performance, and potential overheating. Solution: Replace the faulty gasket. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000. Recalls: None Found
- **Timing chain rattle:** Description/Symptoms: A rattling noise may be heard from the engine, particularly on startup or acceleration. Solution: Replace the timing chain and related components. Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,000. Recalls: None Found

### **Transmission**

**6-Speed Automatic**

- **Transmission shudder:** Description/Symptoms: The transmission may exhibit shuddering or jerking during gear changes. Solution: Service or replace the transmission fluid and/or torque converter. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500. Recalls: None Found
- **Slipping gears:** Description/Symptoms: The transmission may fail to engage gears properly, resulting in a loss of power or vehicle movement. Solution: Repair or replace the transmission. Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000. Recalls: None Found

### **Suspension**

- **Front suspension clunking:** Description/Symptoms: A clunking noise may be heard from the front suspension, particularly over bumps. Solution: Replace worn or damaged suspension components, such as control arms or ball joints. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000. Recalls: None Found
- **Rear axle seal leak:** Description/Symptoms: Oil may leak from the rear axle seals, causing a low fluid level and potential damage to the axle components. Solution: Replace the faulty axle seals. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500. Recalls: None Found

### **Electrical**

- **Alternator failure:** Description/Symptoms: The alternator may fail, causing a loss of power to electrical components and potential battery drainage. Solution: Replace the alternator. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000. Recalls: None Found
- **Battery drain:** Description/Symptoms: The battery may drain prematurely, even with the vehicle parked and unattended. Solution: Diagnose and repair electrical system components, such as faulty wiring or modules. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500. Recalls: None Found

### **Exterior**

- **Headlight condensation:** Description/Symptoms: Condensation may accumulate inside the headlights after rain or car washes. Solution: Replace the headlight lens or assemble. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000. Recalls: None Found

### **Interior**

- **Panoramic moonroof rattling:** Description/Symptoms: The panoramic moonroof may rattle or squeak when closed. Solution: Adjust or tighten the mounting bolts. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500. Recalls: None Found
- **Climate control fan failure:** Description/Symptoms: The climate control fan may fail to function, resulting in poor ventilation or heating/cooling. Solution: Replace the fan motor or related components. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000. Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

The 2017 Lexus GX460 is a reliable and capable vehicle, but it has some common issues that can be frustrating for owners. While some issues, such as headlight condensation, are relatively minor, others, like transmission shuddering or timing chain rattle, can be more costly and impactful. It's important to address these issues promptly to maintain the vehicle's performance and value.
