The 2015 Lexus GX460 is a luxury SUV known for its off-road capability, comfortable ride, and reputation for reliability. It came with a single engine option, the 4.6L V8 producing 301 horsepower and 329 lb-ft of torque, paired with a 6-speed automatic transmission. Owners generally appreciate its build quality, comfortable interior, and off-road prowess. Common dislikes include its relatively poor fuel economy, outdated infotainment system, and the side-hinged rear door.

**Engine**

**4.6L V8 (1UR-FE)**

*   **Valve Cover Gasket Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks from the valve cover gasket onto the exhaust manifold, causing a burning smell. Visible oil around the valve covers.
    *   Solution: Replace the valve cover gaskets.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Water Pump Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaks, overheating, and potential engine damage. Noise from the water pump area.
    *   Solution: Replace the water pump and coolant.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**A760F 6-Speed Automatic**

*   **Torque Converter Shudder**
    *   Description/Symptoms: A noticeable shudder or vibration, particularly at lower speeds (30-45 mph) during light acceleration.
    *   Solution: A transmission fluid flush with Toyota WS fluid *might* alleviate the issue. If the shudder persists, the torque converter might need replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: Fluid Flush: $200 - $400+, Torque Converter Replacement: $1500 - $3000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Air Suspension Compressor Failure (If Equipped)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The vehicle sits unevenly, the rear suspension doesn't inflate/deflate properly, warning lights illuminate on the dashboard.
    *   Solution: Replace the air suspension compressor. Address any leaks in the air lines or air struts if applicable.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Air Strut Leaks (If Equipped)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The vehicle slowly sinks overnight or over a period of days, particularly in cold weather. Hissing sound near the air struts.
    *   Solution: Replace the leaking air struts.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1500+ per strut (depending on whether OEM or aftermarket parts are used).
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Navigation System/Infotainment Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Slow response times, freezing, or complete failure of the navigation system or infotainment screen. Bluetooth connectivity issues.
    *   Solution: Software updates can sometimes resolve the issue. In some cases, the entire head unit might need replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: Software Update: $0 - $200, Head Unit Replacement: $1000 - $3000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

* **Parking Sensor Malfunction**
    * Description/Symptoms: The parking sensors will not work and display a warning.
    * Solution: Clean sensors, replace broken or damaged sensors, or replace the entire parking sensor module
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: Clean sensors: $0 - $100, Replace Sensors: $200 - $800, Replace Module: $500 - $1000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Cracked Side Mirrors**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The side mirrors are prone to cracking at the base.
    *   Solution: Replace the entire side mirror assembly.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300-$800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Dashboard Cracking**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Cracking of the dashboard material, especially in hot climates with sun exposure.
    *   Solution: Dashboard replacement or the use of a dashboard cover.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: Dashboard Cover: $50 - $200, Dashboard Replacement: $1500 - $3000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Sunroof Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Sunroof may get stuck, fail to close, or make excessive noise when opening or closing.
    *   Solution: Clean and lubricate sunroof tracks, replace sunroof motor if necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: Cleaning/Lubrication: $50 - $200, Sunroof Motor Replacement: $300 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2015 Lexus GX460 is generally a reliable vehicle, but some common issues include valve cover gasket leaks, water pump failures, potential torque converter shudder, air suspension problems (if equipped), and some electrical/interior annoyances. While the engine and transmission issues can be costly to repair, they are not universally experienced by all owners. The severity of these issues can range from minor inconvenience to significant expense. The cracked dashboard is also a frequently mentioned cosmetic concern. Prospective buyers should have these potential problems in mind during pre-purchase inspections.
