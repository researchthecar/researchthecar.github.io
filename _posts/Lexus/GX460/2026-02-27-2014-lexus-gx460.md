# 2014 Lexus GX460

### Overview
The 2014 Lexus GX460 is a body-on-frame SUV known for its robust construction and off-road capability. Built on a proven platform, it generally exhibits strong reliability, reflecting Lexus's reputation for quality. The powertrain, particularly the V8 engine and automatic transmission, is a well-established unit designed for durability. While most owners report a positive ownership experience with minimal unexpected repairs, like any vehicle, certain components can experience issues over time. This analysis focuses on documented, non-routine problems that may arise, excluding standard maintenance and wear-and-tear.

### Summary Verdict
The 2014 Lexus GX460 demonstrates excellent overall reliability, consistent with the brand's reputation. The powertrain is particularly robust. The most commonly cited non-routine issues tend to be related to the advanced Kinetic Dynamic Suspension System (KDSS), which can develop hydraulic leaks, and occasional electrical gremlins such as infotainment system glitches or intermittent battery drain. These issues, while not pervasive, are the primary areas where owners might encounter repair costs beyond standard maintenance. For prospective buyers, a thorough inspection of the KDSS and electrical systems is advisable.
