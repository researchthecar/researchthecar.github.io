## 2010 Lexus GX460

The 2010 Lexus GX460 is a luxury SUV that offers a combination of off-road capability and on-road refinement. It comes with a single engine and transmission option:

* **Engine:** 4.6L V8, 301 horsepower, 329 lb-ft torque
* **Transmission:** 6-speed automatic

### Engine

**No Issues Found**

### Transmission

**Delayed or Hesitant Shifts**
* Description/Symptoms: Transmission hesitates or delays when shifting gears, especially from 1st to 2nd or 2nd to 3rd
* Solution: Replace valve body or transmission control module
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500
* Recalls: None Found

### Suspension

**Leaking Rear Shocks**
* Description/Symptoms: Rear shocks leak fluid, causing a loss of damping and a rough ride
* Solution: Replace rear shocks
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800 per shock
* Recalls: None Found

### Electrical

**Battery Drain**
* Description/Symptoms: Battery drains prematurely, even when the vehicle is turned off
* Solution: Diagnose and repair electrical system, may involve replacing a faulty module or component
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
* Recalls: None Found

### Exterior

**Door Handle Recall**
* Description/Symptoms: Exterior door handles may become loose or detached
* Solution: Replace door handles at dealership
* Estimated Cost to Fix: Free (recall)
* Recalls: NHTSA Campaign Number: 10V325000

### Interior

**Steering Wheel Squeak**
* Description/Symptoms: Steering wheel squeaks when turned
* Solution: Replace steering column or lubricate steering components
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
* Recalls: None Found

**Climate Control Malfunction**
* Description/Symptoms: Climate control system may malfunction, causing irregular temperature or airflow
* Solution: Replace climate control module or diagnose and repair electrical system
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
* Recalls: None Found

## Summary

The 2010 Lexus GX460 has a few reported issues, but overall it is a reliable and well-built vehicle. The most common issues are related to the transmission, which may experience delayed or hesitant shifts. Suspension issues, such as leaking rear shocks, are also relatively common. Electrical problems can sometimes occur, including battery drain or climate control malfunctions. Interior issues are generally minor, such as a steering wheel squeak. Overall, the cost of repairs for the GX460 can range from relatively inexpensive to quite expensive, depending on the severity of the issue.
