# 2018 Lexus LC500h Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2018 Lexus LC 500h is a striking luxury grand tourer that pairs flagship styling with an advanced multi-stage hybrid system. Because it is a low-volume flagship model with an intricate powertrain, general complaint volume is very light, giving buyers a relatively clean initial outlook on everyday durability.

---

## Engine

### 3.5L V6 Hybrid

This 3.5-liter V6 petrol engine pairs with Lexus's Multi-Stage Hybrid system to deliver a combined 354 horsepower. It is generally regarded as exceptionally durable, benefiting from Toyota's long-standing hybrid engineering expertise.

## Transmission

### Multi-Stage Hybrid Transmission

The unique multi-stage hybrid transmission combines a continuously variable transmission with a four-speed automatic gear set to simulate direct steps. It provides smooth power delivery and maintains strong reliability standards typical of the brand.

## Exterior

### Door Latch Actuators

Exterior body structures are generally well-assembled, though occasional door mechanism complaints have been noted by early owners.

**Door Actuator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have reported that interior or exterior door handles fail to open the door due to internal actuator malfunction, often suspected to be aggravated by moisture exposure.
- **Solution:** The affected door lock actuator motor must be replaced by a qualified technician.

## Summary Verdict

The 2018 Lexus LC 500h offers exotic styling backed by robust hybrid mechanicals, making it a solid choice for used luxury buyers seeking uniqueness without typical high-end reliability anxiety. Prospective buyers should thoroughly check all power door functions and verify full hybrid system health during a pre-purchase inspection.
