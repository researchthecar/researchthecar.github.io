# 2024 Lexus LC500h Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2024 Lexus LC 500h represents the pinnacle of luxury grand touring, blending striking styling with an advanced hybrid powertrain designed for effortless cruising and impressive efficiency. As a low-volume flagship coupe, it benefits from Lexus's renowned build quality and meticulous engineering. Prospective buyers exploring the used market can generally expect a refined, highly reliable ownership experience, though verifying service history and inspecting complex flagship systems remains standard best practice.

---

## Engine

### 3.5L V6 Hybrid

The 3.5-liter naturally aspirated V6 paired with Lexus's Multi-Stage Hybrid System produces around 354 total system horsepower. It delivers smooth, instantaneous power delivery and exemplary mechanical durability typical of Toyota and Lexus hybrid engineering.

## Transmission

### Multi-Stage Hybrid Transmission

This unique transmission combines a planetary gear-type continuously variable transmission with a four-speed automatic gear set to mimic the feel of a traditional stepped-ratio gearbox. It offers seamless shifts and robust long-term reliability under normal touring conditions.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V124000: Back Over Prevention:Display Function](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lexus&model=LC&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** Due to incorrect programming of the parking-assist electronic control unit (ECU) software, the rearview camera image may not immediately display within a couple of seconds after the shifter is placed into reverse. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 111, "Rear Visibility." A rearview.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the parking assist ECU software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V744000: Back Over Prevention:Display Function](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lexus&model=LC&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** A software error may cause the rearview camera to freeze or display a blank screen when the vehicle is in reverse. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 111, "Rear Visibility." A rearview camera that fails to display an image can reduce the driver's view behind the vehicle.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the parking assist software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.
