# 2009 Lexus IS F

### Overview
The 2009 Lexus IS F, a high-performance variant of the IS sedan, is renowned for its potent V8 engine and track-inspired handling. As a performance model, it generally exhibits strong reliability, reflecting Lexus's commitment to quality. However, like any vehicle, especially one designed for spirited driving, certain components may experience issues over time. This analysis focuses on documented, non-wear-and-tear related problems that owners have reported, providing insights into potential areas of concern and their associated repair costs. The IS F's engineering prioritizes performance, which can sometimes translate to more specialized maintenance needs for its unique components.

---


## Engine

### 5.0L V8 (2UR-GSE)
**Oil Consumption**
- **Symptoms:** Excessive oil level drop between changes, potentially leading to low oil warnings.
- **Solution:** Monitor oil levels frequently. In some cases, piston ring replacement or valve stem seal replacement may be necessary.
- **Cost:** $1,500 - $3,000

**Carbon Buildup**
- **Symptoms:** Reduced engine performance, rough idling, potential misfires.
- **Solution:** Intake manifold cleaning, walnut blasting of intake valves.
- **Cost:** $300 - $700

### Summary Verdict
The 2009 Lexus IS F is a remarkably reliable performance sedan, with its core mechanical components, including the powerful 5.0L V8 engine and 8-speed automatic transmission, generally holding up well. The most commonly cited non-wear-and-tear issues relate to potential oil consumption in the V8 engine and carbon buildup, both of which are manageable with regular monitoring and appropriate maintenance. Other systems such as suspension, electrical, exterior, and interior components show a high degree of reliability, consistent with Lexus's reputation. While the IS F offers exhilarating performance, owners should be aware of the specific needs of its high-output engine to ensure long-term durability.
