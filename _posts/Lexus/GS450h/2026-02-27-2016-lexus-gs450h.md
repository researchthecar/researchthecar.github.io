# 2016 Lexus GS450h Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2016 Lexus GS 450h, buyers can generally expect the exceptional luxury and refined engineering associated with the brand. While owner feedback is sparse, a few localized owner reports point to minor mechanical and cosmetic areas that warrant inspection during a pre-purchase evaluation.

---

## Engine

### 3.5L V6 Hybrid

The 3.5-liter V6 hybrid powertrain pairs a gasoline engine with an electric motor to deliver smooth power and strong fuel efficiency. Overall, this powertrain option maintains a strong reputation for long-term durability.

**Low Pressure Fuel Pump Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may unexpectedly cut off while driving at highway speeds and emit a humming noise from underneath the rear seats, requiring a 12-volt battery reset.
- **Solution:** Inspect and replace the faulty low-pressure fuel pump.

**Vacuum Pump Noise**
- **Symptoms:** The engine may produce a persistent knocking or rattling sound upon startup that continues even after the engine has warmed up.
- **Solution:** Have the vacuum pump inspected by a qualified technician and replaced if necessary.

## Transmission

### Electronically Controlled Continuously Variable Transmission (ECVT)

This planetary-gear-based continuously variable transmission routes power seamlessly to the rear wheels in hybrid configurations. It is widely regarded as a highly durable and refined component of the hybrid system.

## Steering

### Electric Power Steering

Steering mechanism complaints are uncommon, though individual vehicles may exhibit localized mechanical feedback issues.

**Sticky or Notchy Steering Feel**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may notice a persistent "sticky" or "notchy" sensation around the center position when driving at highway speeds of 60 mph and above.
- **Solution:** Inspect the steering column and electronic power steering assist assembly for binding or calibration issues.

## Exterior

### Dashboard and Exterior Trim

Exterior finish and interior dash materials generally hold up well, though vehicles exposed to intense sunlight may show localized cosmetic wear.

**Melting Dashboard**
- **Symptoms:** The dashboard material can develop a sticky texture and degrade visually over time, particularly in hot and sunny climates.
- **Solution:** Consult a dealership or professional trim specialist regarding potential material replacement or interior protective treatments.

**Paint and Clear Coat Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have observed clear coat peeling on roof edges, discoloration on door handles and pillar covers, and fading on headlight covers.
- **Solution:** Inspect exterior paint condition and apply protective clear coats or professional detailing if early degradation is present.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V222000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lexus&model=GS&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** Fuel pump failure can cause an engine stall, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the fuel pump assembly, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2016 Lexus GS 450h remains a sophisticated and efficient luxury hybrid option for used car buyers. While isolated owner complaints highlight potential concerns like fuel pump issues, steering feel, and cosmetic trim wear, the vehicle's core mechanical platform is generally robust. A thorough pre-purchase inspection focusing on the hybrid systems, steering responsiveness, and exterior/interior trim condition is recommended.
