# 2007 Lexus GS450h

### Overview
The 2007 Lexus GS450h is a luxury hybrid sedan that aimed to blend performance with fuel efficiency. As a hybrid, it features a sophisticated powertrain combining a gasoline engine with electric motors. This generation of the GS450h is generally known for its strong build quality and reliability, typical of the Lexus brand. However, like any complex vehicle, especially one with hybrid technology, certain areas may experience issues over time. This analysis focuses on documented technical reliability concerns beyond standard maintenance, providing insights into potential problem areas and their associated costs for owners.

---


## Engine

### 3.5L V6 Hybrid
**Hybrid Battery Degradation**
- **Symptoms:** Reduced fuel economy, loss of electric-only driving capability, dashboard warning lights.
- **Solution:** Hybrid battery pack replacement.
- **Cost:** $3,000 - $6,000

**Inverter Cooling Pump Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Overheating of the hybrid system, dashboard warning lights, potential loss of power.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the inverter cooling pump.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600

### Summary Verdict
The 2007 Lexus GS450h demonstrates strong overall reliability, characteristic of the Lexus brand. The primary concerns revolve around the hybrid system's components, specifically the hybrid battery pack and the inverter cooling pump, which can incur significant repair costs. Electrical issues, such as auxiliary battery failure and hybrid system warning lights, are also noted. Interior electronics, particularly the navigation system, may experience occasional glitches. Despite these potential issues, the GS450h remains a dependable luxury sedan, with most problems being manageable and repairable, though often at a premium due to the hybrid technology involved.
