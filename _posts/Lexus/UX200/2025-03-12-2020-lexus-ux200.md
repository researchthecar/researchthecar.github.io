The 2020 Lexus UX 200 is a subcompact luxury crossover known for its comfortable ride, fuel efficiency, and stylish interior. It primarily came with a 2.0-liter inline-4 engine producing 169 horsepower and 151 lb-ft of torque paired with a Direct-Shift CVT (Continuously Variable Transmission). Some of the things owners like about the vehicle are the comfortable ride, interior design, and fuel economy. Some of the things owners dislike are the underpowered engine and small back seat.

**Engine**

**2.0L Inline-4 (M20A-FKS)**

* **Excessive Oil Consumption**
    * Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported excessive oil consumption, requiring more frequent oil additions between scheduled oil changes. Symptoms include the low oil level warning light illuminating, and the need to add oil every few hundred miles.
    * Solution: In some cases, Lexus dealerships have performed oil consumption tests and replaced piston rings or the entire engine under warranty.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: If out of warranty, piston ring replacement can cost $2,000 - $4,000+. Engine replacement can be significantly more, $6,000+.
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Engine Misfires**
    * Description/Symptoms: Rough idling, hesitation during acceleration, and the check engine light illuminating. This can be caused by faulty spark plugs or ignition coils.
    * Solution: Replacing the faulty spark plugs or ignition coils.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: Spark plug replacement $100 - $300, ignition coil replacement $150 - $400+.
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**Direct-Shift CVT**

* **Hesitation During Acceleration**
    * Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported a hesitation or delay in acceleration, particularly when trying to accelerate quickly from a stop or at low speeds. This is a common complaint with many CVT transmissions.
    * Solution: There is no known permanent fix for this issue. Some owners have reported that a software update can improve the response of the transmission.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: Software update $100 - $200
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Jerky Shifts/Engagement**
    * Description/Symptoms: Reports of rough or jerky shifting, especially at low speeds or when the transmission is cold.
    * Solution: A transmission fluid flush and software update might improve the shifting, but a complete transmission replacement might be needed in more severe cases.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: Transmission fluid flush and software update $200 - $400, Transmission Replacement $4,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

No Issues Found

**Electrical**

* **Infotainment System Freezing/Crashing**
    * Description/Symptoms: The infotainment system can freeze, become unresponsive, or crash, requiring a restart to regain functionality. This can affect navigation, audio, and other vehicle functions.
    * Solution: Software updates from Lexus can sometimes resolve these issues. In some cases, the infotainment unit may need to be replaced.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: Software Update $0 - $200, Infotainment Unit Replacement $1,000 - $2,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Backup Camera Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: The backup camera may display a black screen, flicker, or show distorted images.
    * Solution: Check camera connections. If connections are fine, the camera unit or wiring harness may need replacing.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Automatic High Beam Malfunction**
    * Description/Symptoms: The automatic high beam system might not function correctly, failing to switch between high and low beams appropriately.
    * Solution: Recalibration of the system is required. If that fails, replacing the sensor can fix the issue.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: Recalibration $100 - $200, Sensor Replacement $300 - $600+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Rattling Noises**
    * Description/Symptoms: Owners have reported rattling noises coming from various locations within the cabin, such as the dashboard, door panels, or rear cargo area.
    * Solution: Identifying the source of the rattle and applying felt tape or other sound-deadening material to eliminate the noise.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies widely depending on the source and complexity of the repair. $50 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2020 Lexus UX 200 has some issues that owners have reported. The most concerning issues are related to the engine and transmission. The potential for high repair costs associated with the engine and transmission should be a key consideration for prospective buyers. Electrical issues such as infotainment problems can also be annoying but are generally less expensive to fix.
