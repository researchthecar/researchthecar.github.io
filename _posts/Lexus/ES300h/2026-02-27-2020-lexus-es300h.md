# 2020 Lexus ES300h Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2020 Lexus ES 300h, buyers generally find a luxurious, highly efficient hybrid sedan known for impressive fuel economy and solid build quality. While owner-reported complaints are relatively few, prospective buyers should still inspect specific areas such as the electrical system, brakes, and engine components for potential wear or manufacturer-noted service needs.

---

## Engine

### 2.5L 4-Cylinder Hybrid (A25A-FXS)

The 2.5-liter four-cylinder hybrid engine pairs with an electric motor to generate a net 215 horsepower, delivering exceptional fuel efficiency. It is generally robust, though occasional fluid leaks can develop around high-mileage seals.

**Timing Chain Cover Leak**
- **Symptoms:** Oil leaks originating from the front timing chain cover have been reported around moderate mileage.
- **Solution:** Inspect the front timing chain cover during a pre-purchase inspection and reseal if active leaking is found.

## Transmission

### Electronically Controlled Continuously Variable Transmission (ECVT)

The planetary-type electronic continuously variable transmission channels power smoothly to the front wheels. It is widely considered a highly dependable unit with minimal reported drivetrain failures.

## Brakes

### Parking Brake

The regenerative braking system blends seamlessly with traditional friction brakes, though mechanical parking brake components should be verified during an inspection.

**Parking Brake Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The parking brake may exhibit mechanical inconsistency or failure to securely hold the vehicle on inclines.
- **Solution:** Have a mechanic inspect the electronic parking brake actuator and cables for proper engagement.

## Electrical

### Battery System

Electrical architecture is modern and feature-rich, though the 12V auxiliary battery may require periodic monitoring or replacement.

**Frequent 12V Battery Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Some owners report needing 12V auxiliary battery replacements more frequently than expected within a few years of ownership.
- **Solution:** Test the 12V auxiliary battery's state of health and charging system output during routine maintenance.

## Safety

### Adaptive Cruise and Forward Collision

Advanced safety suites provide robust driver aids, but sensors and cameras must remain properly calibrated for optimal performance.

**Cruise Control Deceleration Delay**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have occasionally noted instances where adaptive cruise control or automatic emergency braking did not engage as expected when approaching slowing traffic.
- **Solution:** Ensure sensor lenses and windshield cameras are clean and calibrate driver-assistance software if issues persist.

---

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V064000: Engine And Engine Cooling](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lexus&model=ES300h&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** A coolant leak can cause the engine to overheat and cause a vehicle stall in conventional gasoline vehicles, increasing the risk of a crash. Additionally, for all involved vehicles, the engine may get damaged mechanically, possibly resulting in an engine oil leak, increasing the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Toyota will notify owners, and dealers will inspect and replace the engine including the engine block, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2020 Lexus ES 300h remains a strong and efficient choice for a used luxury hybrid sedan, boasting high overall reliability and comfort. Buyers should perform a thorough pre-purchase inspection focusing on the engine seals, 12V electrical health, and parking brake functionality to ensure a trouble-free ownership experience.
