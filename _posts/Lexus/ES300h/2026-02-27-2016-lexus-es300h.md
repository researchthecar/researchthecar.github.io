# 2016 Lexus ES300h Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2016 Lexus ES 300h, buyers can generally expect the refined luxury and hybrid efficiency the brand is known for. While owner feedback is largely positive, prospective purchasers should remain aware of reported concerns regarding hybrid system behavior, brake components, and interior controls based on driver experiences.

---

## Engine

### 2.5L 4-Cylinder Hybrid (2AR-FXE)

This 2.5-liter Atkinson-cycle four-cylinder engine is paired with an electric motor to produce a net 200 horsepower. It provides smooth power delivery and strong fuel efficiency, making it a reliable and economical choice for daily commuting.

**Fuel Economy Variance**
- **Symptoms:** Some owners report fuel economy significantly lower than the EPA estimates, staying in the upper 20s to low 30s MPG range.
- **Solution:** Verify driving habits, tire pressures, and hybrid battery performance during a pre-purchase inspection.

**Cruise Control / Speed Control Behavior**
- **Symptoms:** Unexpected deceleration or radar cruise control behavior when following slower traffic at highway speeds.
- **Solution:** Have the radar sensor and adaptive cruise system calibrated and inspected if erratic speed management is experienced.

## Transmission

### Electronic Continuously Variable Transmission (e-CVT)

The planetary-type electronic continuously variable transmission seamlessly blends power from the gasoline engine and electric motor. It is generally very robust with few recurring mechanical complaints reported by owners.

## Brakes

### Brake Booster and Actuator

Issues related to the brake booster and electronic pump can cause unusual noises or pedal feel, requiring careful inspection during a pre-purchase evaluation.

**Vacuum Leak and Continuous Pump Running**
- **Symptoms:** The brake booster pump may run continually every few seconds, potentially signaling a loss of vacuum pressure and heightened risk of brake failure.
- **Solution:** Inspect the brake booster assembly, master cylinder, and electronic brake pump; replace malfunctioning components immediately.

**Pedal Slip and Delayed Response**
- **Symptoms:** A mechanical delay, slippage feeling, or unusual noise originating from inside the pedal assembly or electronic brake pump during hard stops.
- **Solution:** Inspect the brake pedal mechanism, booster, and electronic pump assembly to ensure proper pressure and mechanical integrity.

## Interior

### Shift Lever Assembly

Cabin controls are generally durable, though minor trim and hardware wear like a loose shift knob can occasionally occur.

**Spinning Shift Knob**
- **Symptoms:** The top of the shift knob begins to spin freely in place, causing the driver to lose grip when attempting to shift into Park or other gears.
- **Solution:** Tighten or replace the shift knob assembly if the internal threading fails.

## Summary Verdict

The 2016 Lexus ES 300h offers a compelling mix of luxury, comfort, and high fuel economy. While isolated owner reports highlight potential concerns with the braking system components and hybrid cruise control operation, a thorough pre-purchase inspection focusing on the brakes and electronics will help ensure a confident buying experience.
