# 2017 Lexus ES300h Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2017 Lexus ES 300h continues the brand's long-standing reputation for comfort, exceptional fuel economy, and overall dependability. Buyers exploring the used market will find that complaints for this model year are very sparse, reflecting the robust nature of its hybrid powertrain and luxury appointments. However, as with any modern luxury vehicle, checking electronic features and reviewing specific owner-reported quirks before purchasing remains a smart strategy.

---

## Engine

### 2.5L 4-Cylinder Hybrid (2AR-FXE)

The 2.5-liter four-cylinder hybrid engine pairs with an electric motor to produce a net 200 horsepower, prioritizing efficiency and smooth operation. This engine is widely regarded as exceptionally reliable with a long service lifespan.

## Transmission

### Electronic Continuously Variable Transmission (ECVT)

The electronic continuously variable transmission distributes power seamlessly between the gasoline engine and electric motor for front-wheel-drive models. It generally provides trouble-free operation, though rare electronic shift communication glitches have been reported by owners.

**Shift Failure to Engage**
- **Symptoms:** When attempting to shift from park into reverse after starting the stationary vehicle, the car stayed in neutral and rolled as if no gear engaged, displaying an error message on the screen.
- **Solution:** Owners experiencing selector or sensor errors should have the vehicle inspected by a qualified technician to diagnose shifter module or transmission control unit response.

## Summary Verdict

The 2017 Lexus ES 300h is an exceptionally solid choice for used car buyers seeking high fuel efficiency and luxury comfort.
