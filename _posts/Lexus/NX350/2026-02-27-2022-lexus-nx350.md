# 2022 Lexus NX350 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a pre-owned 2022 Lexus NX 350, buyers can generally expect the refined luxury and build quality associated with the brand, though owner-reported feedback highlights a few notable areas of concern. While powertrain reliability tends to be solid, early production models have drawn complaints regarding exterior mirrors, electrical software glitches, and isolated mechanical issues. As with any modern luxury vehicle, a thorough pre-purchase inspection and test drive focusing on electronics and body hardware are recommended.

---

## Engine

### 2.4L Turbocharged Inline-4

The 2.4-liter turbocharged inline-4 engine produces approximately 275 horsepower and 317 lb-ft of torque, providing brisk acceleration for daily driving. This modern powerplant generally maintains strong reliability expectations, though owners should monitor routine maintenance and fluid levels.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 8-speed automatic transmission delivers power smoothly across available front-wheel and all-wheel-drive configurations. It generally operates unobtrusively with no widespread mechanical complaint patterns reported by owners.

## Electrical

### Infotainment and Display System

Cabin electronics manage infotainment, climate controls, and connectivity, though software bugs and screen freezes have been noted by some owners.

**Infotainment Connectivity and Software Freezes**
- **Symptoms:** The touchscreen interface may fail to connect to smartphones or CarPlay consistently, or a software update prompt can cover vehicle information screens.
- **Solution:** Restarting the screen or cycling the vehicle ignition may temporarily resolve connection glitches, while software updates address persistent bugs.

## Exterior

### Door Locking and Starting Electronics

Power-operated locking and starting mechanisms rely heavily on integrated electronic modules that can occasionally experience operational glitches.

**Electronic Door Lock and Ignition Failures**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have reported instances where the push-button start failed or electronic door locks behaved unexpectedly following collisions or system faults.
- **Solution:** Have the vehicle's 12-volt battery, door modules, and electronic control units inspected by a qualified technician.

### Side Mirrors

Exterior mirrors provide vital rear visibility, though excessive glass vibration has been a recurring complaint among drivers.

**Excessive Mirror Vibration**
- **Symptoms:** The driver-side mirror shakes or vibrates excessively during driving, causing blurred reflections and reduced visibility, particularly at night.
- **Solution:** Inspect the mirror assembly mountings and have the dealership or technician secure or replace unstable mirror components.

## Safety

### Wheel Assemblies

Wheel hubs and fastening hardware are critical safety components that require careful inspection during routine maintenance intervals.

**Wheel Separation**
- **Symptoms:** Isolated reports indicate that a wheel and tire assembly may detach unexpectedly while the vehicle is in motion.
- **Solution:** Ensure all lug nuts are properly torqued to manufacturer specifications and have wheel hub assemblies inspected during routine service.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V238000: Structure:Body](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lexus&model=NX350&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** Missing spot-welds between the body panels and the front shock absorber mounting areas may cause the front shock absorber to separate from the mounting area. A separated shock absorber can cause a loss of vehicle stability, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will add welds to body panels or replace the body panels where welds have failed, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V661000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lexus&model=NX350&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 135, "Light Vehicle Brake Systems." An electronic parking brake that cannot be engaged while in "park," may result in a vehicle rollaway, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will reprogram the Skid Control ECU, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2022 Lexus NX 350 offers strong performance and upscale features, but used car shoppers should pay close attention to potential pain points. Reviewers and owners have highlighted specific concerns regarding infotainment software behavior, exterior mirror vibration, and isolated electronic or mechanical glitches. Prospective buyers should verify that all software updates are current, test all cabin electronics thoroughly, and ensure a comprehensive pre-purchase inspection is performed.
