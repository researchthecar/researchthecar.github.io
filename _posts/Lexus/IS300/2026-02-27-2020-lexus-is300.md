# 2020 Lexus IS300 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2020 Lexus IS300, buyers generally expect the refined build quality and dependability associated with the brand. While the powertrain options provide a smooth and responsive drive, owners have reported a few isolated concerns regarding engine misfires and steering behavior. Reviewing vehicle history and performing a thorough pre-purchase inspection can help ensure a trouble-free ownership experience.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4 (8AR-FTS)

This 2.0-liter turbocharged inline-4 engine produces around 241 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque, powering rear-wheel drive models. It delivers spirited performance, though buyers should verify proper maintenance history.

**Intermittent Engine Misfires**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have reported intermittent engine misfires accompanied by a flashing malfunction indicator lamp, reduced engine power, sudden loss of acceleration, and noticeable engine shaking.
- **Solution:** Requires professional diagnosis of the ignition, fuel, or engine management systems to isolate the root cause of the misfire events.

### 3.5L V6 (2GR-FKS)

The 3.5-liter V6 engine generates approximately 260 horsepower in all-wheel-drive configurations, offering a smooth power delivery and robust long-term reliability. It is a solid choice for buyers seeking traditional naturally aspirated performance.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Sport Direct-Shift Automatic

This 8-speed automatic transmission is paired with rear-wheel-drive models, offering quick gear changes and highway efficiency. It generally maintains a strong reputation for durability.

### 6-Speed Electronically Controlled Automatic

Utilized in all-wheel-drive variants, this 6-speed automatic transmission delivers steady shifts tailored for varying weather conditions. It is known for straightforward mechanical dependability.

## Suspension

### Front Suspension Shocks

The suspension balances comfort and agility, but owners should monitor shock absorbers and tire wear during routine inspections.

**Uneven Wear and Handling Instability**
- **Symptoms:** Front shock absorbers may exhibit uneven wear, resulting in a bouncy ride, steering wheel vibrations around highway speeds, and unexpected swerving during cornering.
- **Solution:** Inspect shock absorbers, alignment, and suspension bushings, replacing worn components as necessary.

## Steering

### Electronic Power Steering

Steering components generally provide responsive handling, though occasional driver reports highlight electronic feedback anomalies.

**Steering Wheel Sticking at Highway Speeds**
- **Symptoms:** The steering wheel may feel slightly stuck or resistant when driving at highway speeds around 70 mph or greater.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified technician inspect the electronic power steering module, steering rack, and related electrical connections.

## Summary Verdict

The 2020 Lexus IS300 maintains a strong reputation for luxury and overall mechanical durability. While isolated owner complaints indicate potential issues with engine misfires, steering feel, and suspension wear, these do not represent widespread systemic failures. Prospective buyers should prioritize a comprehensive test drive and pre-purchase inspection to ensure all systems are operating correctly.
