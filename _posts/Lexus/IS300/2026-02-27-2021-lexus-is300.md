# 2021 Lexus IS300 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2021 Lexus IS300 brings sharp styling, luxury comfort, and the renowned reliability associated with the brand. For used-car shoppers looking at this model year, owner reports are sparse, though buyers should still evaluate condition and functionality thoroughly during a pre-purchase inspection.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4

This 2.0-liter turbocharged inline-4 engine produces approximately 241 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque, powering rear-wheel-drive models. It provides a smooth balance of everyday efficiency and responsive acceleration for daily driving.

### 3.5L V6

The 3.5-liter V6 engine delivers around 260 horsepower and 236 lb-ft of torque in all-wheel-drive configurations. Known for its refined power delivery and robust long-term durability, it is a favored choice for buyers seeking traditional naturally aspirated performance.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Sport Direct-Shift Automatic

Paired with rear-wheel-drive models, this 8-speed automatic transmission offers quick gear changes and helps optimize highway fuel economy. It maintains a strong reputation for smooth operation and dependability.

### 6-Speed Electronically Controlled Automatic

Utilized in all-wheel-drive configurations, this 6-speed automatic transmission delivers steady and predictable power delivery. It is a proven unit known for solid mechanical longevity.

## Exterior

### Sunroof

Exterior body components are generally durable, but rare glass-related incidents have been documented by vehicle owners.

**Sunroof Glass Shattering**
- **Symptoms:** An owner experienced an unexpected and loud shattering of the sunroof glass while driving at freeway speeds without any apparent impact from outside objects.
- **Solution:** Inspect the sunroof glass for any pre-existing stress cracks or structural flaws prior to purchase.

## Safety

### Horn

Vehicle safety systems generally perform well, though isolated owner feedback highlights specific audible warning concerns.

**Low Horn Volume**
- **Symptoms:** An owner reported that the factory horn lacked sufficient volume to alert an oncoming driver during an impending collision scenario.
- **Solution:** Verify horn functionality during inspection and consider an aftermarket upgrade if a louder alert is desired.
