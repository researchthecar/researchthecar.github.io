**Introduction**

The 2018 Lexus IS300 is a luxury sports sedan that offers a comfortable and engaging driving experience. It is powered by a 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine that produces 241 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque. The engine is paired with an eight-speed automatic transmission. The IS300 is available in both rear-wheel drive and all-wheel drive configurations.

**Engine Option**

* 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine

**Engine Issues**

* **Engine misfire**
    * Description/Symptoms: The engine may misfire, causing rough idling, hesitation, and reduced power.
    * Solution: Replace the spark plugs or ignition coils.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Turbocharger failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The turbocharger may fail, resulting in a loss of power, excessive smoke, and oil leaks.
    * Solution: Replace the turbocharger.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission Option**

* Eight-speed automatic transmission

**Transmission Issues**

* **Transmission slipping**
    * Description/Symptoms: The transmission may slip gears, causing a loss of power and difficulty accelerating.
    * Solution: Repair or replace the transmission.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension Issues**

* **Front suspension noise**
    * Description/Symptoms: The front suspension may make a clunking or rattling noise when driving over bumps or rough roads.
    * Solution: Replace the front suspension bushings.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Rear suspension sag**
    * Description/Symptoms: The rear suspension may sag, causing the vehicle to ride low in the back.
    * Solution: Replace the rear suspension springs or shocks.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical Issues**

* **Battery drain**
    * Description/Symptoms: The battery may drain quickly, making it difficult to start the vehicle.
    * Solution: Replace the battery or alternator.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Electrical short**
    * Description/Symptoms: An electrical short may cause various electrical system malfunctions, such as flickering lights, power window issues, or dashboard malfunctions.
    * Solution: Repair or replace the affected electrical components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior Issues**

* **Headlight condensation**
    * Description/Symptoms: Moisture may accumulate inside the headlights, causing them to fog up.
    * Solution: Replace the headlights.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800 per headlight
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior Issues**

* **Infotainment screen freezes**
    * Description/Symptoms: The infotainment screen may freeze or become unresponsive.
    * Solution: Reset the infotainment system or update the software.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: Free
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Power seat failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The power seats may fail to move or adjust properly.
    * Solution: Repair or replace the power seat motor or control module.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2018 Lexus IS300 is a reliable and enjoyable vehicle. However, like all cars, it has some potential issues. The most common problems include engine misfires, transmission slipping, front suspension noise, and electrical shorts. The severity and cost of these issues can vary depending on the specific problem and the extent of the damage. Overall, the IS300 is a good choice for luxury sports sedan buyers who are looking for a comfortable and engaging driving experience.
