# 2019 Lexus IS300 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2019 Lexus IS300, buyers generally expect the hallmark luxury and dependability associated with the brand. While owner reports to federal safety regulators are relatively sparse for this model year, keeping an eye on specific system histories and minor accessory components during a pre-purchase inspection can help ensure a smooth ownership experience.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4

This 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine produces approximately 241 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque, powering rear-wheel-drive models. It provides a balance of efficiency and responsive daily performance for everyday driving.

### 3.5L Naturally Aspirated V6

The 3.5-liter V6 engine delivers around 260 horsepower and 236 lb-ft of torque in all-wheel-drive configurations, or higher output in rear-wheel-drive IS 350 variants. It is known for smooth power delivery and generally robust mechanical longevity.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Sport Direct-Shift Automatic

This 8-speed automatic transmission pairs exclusively with rear-wheel-drive models to offer quick gear shifts and highway cruising efficiency. It is generally dependable when maintained with regular fluid changes.

### 6-Speed Electronically Controlled Automatic

This conventional 6-speed automatic transmission is coupled with all-wheel-drive configurations. It provides smooth engagement and steady performance across varying weather conditions.

## Suspension

### Tires and TPMS

The suspension and wheel assemblies absorb road imperfections well, though routine tire wear and sensor checks remain standard maintenance items.

**Low Tire Pressure Warning**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may experience unexpected illumination of the low tire pressure warning light, occasionally tied to factory-equipped tire performance.
- **Solution:** Check tire pressures manually, inspect tires for slow leaks or punctures, and reset or service the tire pressure monitoring system sensors if required.

## Interior

### Climate Control and Accessories

Electrical and accessory complaints are minimal, though minor interior comfort features should be thoroughly tested during a test drive.

**Air Conditioning Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have reported instances of the air conditioning system failing to blow cold air due to component sealing issues.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified technician inspect the AC system lines and replace damaged O-rings or seals as necessary.
