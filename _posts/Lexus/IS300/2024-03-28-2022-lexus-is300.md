**Introduction**

The 2022 Lexus IS300, a compact sport sedan, is powered by a 3.5-liter V6 engine that produces 260 horsepower and 236 pound-feet of torque. It comes standard with an 8-speed automatic transmission. The IS300 offers a responsive and engaging driving experience, a well-equipped interior with premium materials, and a suite of advanced safety features. However, it has faced a few reported issues.

**Engine**

* **3.5-liter V6 Engine**

  * **Oil Leaks:** Oil leaks can occur from the oil filter adapter or valve cover gaskets. Symptoms include a burning oil smell, smoke from the engine, and low oil levels. Solution involves replacing the faulty gaskets. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200-$500+
  * **Timing Chain Tensioner Failure:** The timing chain tensioner may fail, causing the timing chain to skip or break. Symptoms include rattling noises, rough idle, and reduced engine performance. Solution requires replacing the timing chain tensioner and, in some cases, the timing chain itself. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000-$2,000+
  * **Fuel Injector Issues:** Faulty fuel injectors can lead to rough idling, hesitation during acceleration, and reduced fuel efficiency. Solution involves replacing the affected fuel injectors. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200-$400 per injector

**Transmission**

* **8-Speed Automatic Transmission**

  * **Shifting Delays or Hard Shifts:** Some owners have reported delayed shifting or hard shifts. Solution often involves updating the transmission control software. Estimated Cost to Fix: Often covered under warranty
  * **Transmission Fluid Leaks:** Leaks can occur from the transmission pan or seals. Symptoms include low transmission fluid levels, slipping gears, and difficulty shifting. Solution requires replacing the faulty seals or pan. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300-$600+

**Suspension**

* **No Issues Found**

**Electrical**

* **Alternator Failure:** The alternator may fail, leading to electrical system issues, battery drain, and difficulty starting the vehicle. Solution requires replacing the alternator. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500-$800+
* **Battery Drain:** Battery drain can occur due to faulty door switches, interior lights staying on, or parasitic draw from electronics. Solution involves identifying and fixing the source of the drain. Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the issue

**Exterior**

* **Cracked Windshield:** Some owners have reported cracked windshields, particularly on vehicles equipped with the panoramic sunroof. Solution involves replacing the windshield. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500-$1,000+

**Interior**

* **Dashboard Trim Rattle:** Loose or vibrating dashboard trim pieces can cause rattling noises. Solution involves tightening or replacing the affected trim pieces. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100-$300
* **Infotainment System Glitches:** Occasional glitches or errors with the infotainment system have been reported. Solution typically involves updating the software or resetting the system. Estimated Cost to Fix: Often covered under warranty
* **Steering Wheel Buttons Sticking:** Some users have experienced sticky or unresponsive steering wheel buttons. Solution involves cleaning or replacing the affected buttons. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100-$200

**Summary**

The 2022 Lexus IS300 has received positive reviews for its performance, luxury, and safety features. However, it has experienced some reported issues, primarily related to the engine, transmission, electrical system, and interior components. The severity and cost of these issues vary, with some being covered under warranty. Overall, the IS300 remains a solid choice for those seeking a well-rounded compact sport sedan.
