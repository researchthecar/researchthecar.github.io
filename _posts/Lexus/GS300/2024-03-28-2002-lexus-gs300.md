**Overview:**

The 2002 Lexus GS300 is a mid-size luxury sedan powered by a 3.0L V6 engine. It offers a smooth and refined ride, along with a spacious and well-appointed interior. However, like any vehicle, it has its fair share of issues.

**Engine**

**3.0L V6**

* **Excessive Oil Consumption:** Ingestion of oil through the intake system, leading to blue smoke from the exhaust and potential engine damage.
    * Solution: Replace piston rings and valve stem seals
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $3,000 - $5,000+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Carbon Buildup on Intake Valves:** Accumulation of carbon on intake valves, reducing engine performance and fuel efficiency.
    * Solution: Chemical cleaning or walnut blasting of intake valves
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**5-Speed Automatic**

* **Transmission Fluid Leaks:** Leaks from seals or gaskets within the transmission, leading to fluid loss and potential transmission failure.
    * Solution: Replace leaking seals or gaskets
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Premature Wear on Lower Control Arm Bushings:** Wear and tear on the lower control arm bushings, resulting in a rattling noise and poor handling.
    * Solution: Replace worn bushings
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Power Window Malfunction:** Failure of power window motors or switches, preventing windows from opening or closing properly.
    * Solution: Replace faulty motors or switches
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Alternator Failure:** Electrical system issues caused by a failing alternator, leading to battery discharge and potential vehicle stalling.
    * Solution: Replace alternator
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Sunroof Drain Clogs:** Blockage of sunroof drains, causing water to leak into the cabin or electronics.
    * Solution: Clear clogged drains and inspect for damage
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $200
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Trunk Lid Misalignment:** Misalignment of the trunk lid, affecting its opening and closing.
    * Solution: Adjust hinges or replace latch mechanism
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Heater Core Failure:** Blockage or failure of the heater core, preventing the vehicle from heating properly.
    * Solution: Replace heater core
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,200
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Door Lock Actuator Malfunction:** Failure of door lock actuators, causing difficulty locking or unlocking the doors.
    * Solution: Replace faulty actuators
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

The 2002 Lexus GS300 offers a solid and reliable driving experience, but it is not without its issues. Issues with excessive oil consumption, transmission leaks, and sunroof drain clogs are relatively common and can be expensive to resolve. However, some issues, such as heater core failure, are less frequent and generally less costly to fix. Overall, maintenance and repair costs for the GS300 are typically in line with other luxury sedans of its era.
