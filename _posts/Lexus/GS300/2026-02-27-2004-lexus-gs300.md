# 2004 Lexus GS300

### Overview
The 2004 Lexus GS300, part of the third generation of the GS line, is generally regarded as a reliable luxury sedan. It is equipped with a robust 3.0L inline-six engine known for its smooth power delivery and durability. While the vehicle offers a comfortable and refined driving experience, like any car, it can encounter specific issues over time. This analysis focuses on documented technical reliability concerns beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential problem areas for owners and prospective buyers.

## Suspension

### Standard Chassis
**Front Control Arm Bushings**
- **Symptoms:** Clunking noises over bumps, vague steering feel, uneven tire wear.
- **Solution:** Replacement of worn control arm bushings or the entire control arm assembly.
- **Cost:** $300 - $700


## Electrical

### Body Control Module (BCM)
**Intermittent Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** Issues with power windows, door locks, interior lights, or dashboard warning lights.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and potential reprogramming or replacement of the BCM.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1200


### Alternator
**Premature Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Dimming headlights, battery warning light, difficulty starting the engine.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the alternator.
- **Cost:** $400 - $800


## Exterior

### Headlight Lenses
**Oxidation/Yellowing**
- **Symptoms:** Cloudy or yellowed headlight lenses, reduced light output.
- **Solution:** Restoration of lenses using a polishing kit or replacement of headlight assemblies.
- **Cost:** $50 - $300


## Interior

### Driver's Seat Actuator
**Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Power driver's seat not moving forward, backward, or reclining.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the seat adjustment motor/actuator.
- **Cost:** $200 - $500

### Summary Verdict
The 2004 Lexus GS300 is a highly reliable vehicle, with its powertrain being a particular strong point. Most reported issues are relatively minor and often related to age and mileage rather than inherent design flaws. Common concerns include suspension components like control arm bushings, some electrical gremlins such as BCM malfunctions or alternator failures, and cosmetic issues like headlight oxidation. Interior components like seat actuators can also fail. Overall, with proactive maintenance and addressing these specific potential issues, the GS300 remains a dependable luxury sedan.
