# 2022 Lexus IS350 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2022 Lexus IS350 continues the brand's tradition of offering a luxury sport sedan with a strong focus on refinement and everyday usability. Powered by a potent V6 engine paired with responsive automatic transmissions, it bridges the gap between comfort and performance. As you shop for a used model, reviewing known powertrain specifications and keeping an eye out for any emerging owner-reported quirks will help ensure a satisfying purchase.

---

## Engine

### 3.5L V6 (311 hp / 280 lb-ft)

This naturally aspirated 3.5-liter V6 engine delivers smooth power delivery and robust performance with approximately 311 horsepower. It is widely regarded as a highly durable and dependable option for everyday driving.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Sport Direct-Shift Automatic

The 8-speed automatic transmission handles power for rear-wheel-drive models, offering responsive gear shifts and smooth highway cruising. Overall, it exhibits strong reliability characteristics in daily use.

### 6-Speed Electronically Controlled Automatic

Paired exclusively with all-wheel-drive configurations, this 6-speed automatic transmission provides dependable power distribution across various weather conditions. It is a proven, mechanical unit known for long-term durability.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V222000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lexus&model=IS&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** Fuel pump failure can cause an engine stall, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the fuel pump assembly, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2022 Lexus IS350 is a solid choice for used-car buyers seeking a blend of luxury, dynamic styling, and dependable engineering. Thanks to its robust powertrain options, it avoids major mechanical pitfalls. Prospective buyers should still conduct a standard pre-purchase inspection to verify overall vehicle condition before finalizing the deal.
