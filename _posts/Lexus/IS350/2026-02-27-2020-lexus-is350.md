# 2020 Lexus IS350 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2020 Lexus IS350, buyers generally expect the refined performance and high build quality associated with the brand. While owner reports to federal safety regulators remain relatively scarce for this model year, prospective buyers should review available feedback regarding powertrain operation and steering feel. Evaluating these systems during a thorough test drive helps ensure a confident purchase.

---

## Engine

### 3.5L V6 (2GR-FKS)

The 3.5-liter V6 engine produces approximately 311 horsepower and 280 lb-ft of torque, delivering smooth power delivery and a strong reputation for long-term durability. It serves as the primary powerplant for the IS350 trim, balancing sporty responsiveness with everyday reliability.

### 2.0L Turbocharged I4 (8AR-FTS)

The 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine generates around 241 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque, powering the IS300 rear-wheel-drive variants. While generally efficient, some owners have reported intermittent drivability concerns requiring thorough professional diagnosis.

**Intermittent Engine Misfires**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may experience sudden engine shaking, a flashing malfunction indicator lamp, reduced engine power, and a loss of acceleration.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified technician diagnose the ignition system, fuel components, and engine management computer to isolate the root cause of the misfire.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Sport Direct-Shift Automatic

The 8-speed automatic transmission is paired with rear-wheel-drive models, offering responsive gear changes and seamless highway cruising. It is generally regarded as a robust unit requiring routine fluid maintenance.

### 6-Speed Electronic Automatic

The 6-speed automatic transmission is utilized in all-wheel-drive configurations, providing steady power distribution across varying road conditions. It maintains a solid track record for mechanical durability.

## Suspension

### Front Suspension

The independent front suspension is tuned for a balance of agile handling and ride comfort, though high-mileage or rough-road exposure can lead to premature wear on dampening components.

**Uneven Shock Absorber Wear and Vibration**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may notice a bouncy ride, uneven tire wear, steering wheel vibration around corners, and unexpected vehicle wandering during turns.
- **Solution:** Inspect shock absorbers, alignment settings, and suspension bushings, replacing worn components as necessary.

## Steering

### Electric Power Steering System

The electric power steering system provides light maneuverability at low speeds and firmer feedback on the highway, though rare owner complaints suggest occasional high-speed stiffness.

**Highway Steering Resistance**
- **Symptoms:** The steering wheel may feel slightly stuck or resistant at sustained highway speeds above 70 mph.
- **Solution:** Inspect the electronic power steering module, steering rack, and related wiring connectors for faults, noting that intermittent issues can sometimes be difficult to replicate.

## Summary Verdict

The 2020 Lexus IS350 largely maintains the brand's strong reputation for dependability and comfort. While isolated owner reports note potential concerns regarding intermittent engine misfires on four-cylinder variants and minor steering or suspension quirks, the core V6 platform remains a solid used-car choice. Prospective buyers should conduct a thorough test drive covering both highway speeds and uneven road surfaces to ensure all systems operate properly.
