**Introduction**

The 2023 Lexus IS350 is an entry-level luxury sedan known for its sporty handling, stylish exterior, and comfortable interior. It comes with a powerful 3.5-liter V6 engine that produces 311 horsepower and 280 lb-ft of torque. The engine is paired with an 8-speed automatic transmission. The IS350 also offers all-wheel drive as an option. Despite its many strengths, the IS350 has also been plagued by several issues reported by owners.

**Engine**

**3.5-Liter V6**

* **Valve Spring Issue:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Premature wear of valve springs can cause a misfire, decreased engine power, and possible engine damage.
    * Solution: Replacement of affected valve springs and retainers.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1500 - $3500+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Fuel Injector Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Malfunctioning fuel injectors can lead to difficulty starting, rough idling, and reduced engine performance.
    * Solution: Replacement of failed fuel injectors.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1200+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

* **8-Speed Automatic**
    * **Shifting Hesitation:**
        * Description/Symptoms: Delays or hesitation when shifting gears, especially during acceleration or deceleration.
        * Solution: Software updates, solenoid replacement, or transmission fluid flush.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
        * Recalls: None Found
    * **Transmission Fluid Leakage:**
        * Description/Symptoms: Fluid leaks from the transmission, potentially leading to reduced lubrication and transmission damage.
        * Solution: Repair or replacement of leaking seals or gaskets.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1500+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Front Suspension Noise:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Clunking or rattling noises from the front suspension, especially over bumps or uneven surfaces.
    * Solution: Inspection and replacement of worn or damaged suspension components, such as struts or control arms.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1500+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Rear Suspension Sagging:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Premature sagging or uneven settling of the rear suspension, affecting vehicle stability and handling.
    * Solution: Replacement of worn or damaged suspension components, such as springs or shock absorbers.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $700 - $2000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain Issue:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Rapid battery discharge, even when the vehicle is not in use, leading to starting difficulties and potential battery failure.
    * Solution: Diagnosis and repair of parasitic electrical drain, such as malfunctioning modules or wiring issues.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Infotainment System Glitches:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Malfunctions or glitches in the infotainment system, including freezing, slow response, or connectivity issues.
    * Solution: Software updates or module replacements.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+
    * Recalls: None Found
