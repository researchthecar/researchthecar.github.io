**2008 Lexus IS350: Common Problems and Fix Costs**

The 2008 Lexus IS350 is a luxury sport sedan praised for its refined handling, comfortable interior, and reliable performance. However, like any vehicle, it has its fair share of reported issues.

**Engine**

* **2GR-FSE 3.5L V6 Engine**
    * **Carbon Buildup on Intake Valves:** Buildup of carbon on the intake valves over time can cause rough idling, decreased power, and reduced fuel efficiency.
    * Solution: Manual cleaning of the intake valves.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: NHTSA ID 19V493000

**Transmission**

* **6-Speed Automatic Transmission (A760E)**
    * **Shifting Issues:** Hard or delayed shifting, especially when downshifting.
    * Solution: Replacement of the transmission solenoids.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500
    * Recalls: NHTSA ID 22E059000

**Suspension**

* **No Issues Found**

**Electrical**

* **Alternator Failure:** Early models of the IS350 may experience alternator problems, leading to battery issues or electrical system malfunctions.
* Solution: Replacement of the alternator.
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
* Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Weatherstripping Deterioration:** The weatherstripping around windows and doors may deteriorate over time, allowing water or wind to enter the cabin.
* Solution: Replacement of the weatherstripping.
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500

**Interior**

* **Creaking Center Console:** A common problem in the IS350 is a creaking noise from the center console.
* Solution: Lubrication or replacement of the console bushings.
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300

**Summary**

While most owners report a positive experience with the 2008 Lexus IS350, the aforementioned issues are worth noting. The severity and cost of the repairs can vary depending on the extent of the problem and the labor rates in your area. Regular maintenance and prompt attention to any issues should help minimize the likelihood of major repairs.
