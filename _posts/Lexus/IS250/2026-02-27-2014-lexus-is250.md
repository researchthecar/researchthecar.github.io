# 2014 Lexus IS250

### Overview
The 2014 Lexus IS250 is generally regarded as a reliable vehicle, reflecting Lexus's commitment to quality and durability. This model year benefits from a refined platform and proven powertrain components. While most owners report a positive ownership experience with minimal unexpected repairs, like any vehicle, there are specific areas that have seen occasional reports of issues. This analysis focuses on documented technical reliability concerns beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential problem areas and their associated costs for the IS250.

---


## Engine

### 2.5L V6 (2GR-FSE)
**Carbon Buildup**
- **Symptoms:** Rough idle, reduced power, potential misfires.
- **Solution:** Direct injection cleaning service, intake manifold cleaning.
- **Cost:** $300 - $700

**Oil Leaks (Valve Cover Gaskets)**
- **Symptoms:** Visible oil on engine components, burning oil smell.
- **Solution:** Replacement of valve cover gaskets.
- **Cost:** $400 - $800

### Summary Verdict
The 2014 Lexus IS250 is a solid choice for those seeking a reliable luxury sedan. The powertrain and core mechanical components demonstrate strong durability. The most commonly cited issues are related to engine carbon buildup, a known characteristic of direct-injection engines, and occasional oil leaks from valve cover gaskets. Electrical gremlins, particularly with the infotainment system and potential battery drain, are also noted but not pervasive. Overall, the IS250 offers a dependable ownership experience with manageable repair costs for the few documented non-wear-and-tear issues.
