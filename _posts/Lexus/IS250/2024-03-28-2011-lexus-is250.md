**Introduction:**

The 2011 Lexus IS250 is a compact luxury sedan that offers exceptional driving dynamics and a premium interior. It is powered by a 2.5-liter V6 engine rated at 204 horsepower and 185 lb-ft of torque, paired with a six-speed automatic transmission. Other engine and transmission options include a 3.5-liter V6 with a six-speed automatic or eight-speed automatic transmission. Owners appreciate the IS250's stylish design, comfortable cabin, and impressive performance, but some have encountered issues with various components.

**Engine**

* **2.5-liter V6**
    * **Intake manifold gasket leak:**
        * **Description/Symptoms:** Engine oil or coolant leaks at the intake manifold gasket, leading to overheating or decreased performance.
        * **Solution:** Replace the intake manifold gasket.
        * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $500 - $1,000+
        * **Recalls:** None Found
* **3.5-liter V6**
    * **Variable valve timing (VVT) system failure:**
        * **Description/Symptoms:** Engine performance issues, such as decreased power or fuel economy, accompanied by engine codes or illumination of the check engine light.
        * **Solution:** Replace the VVT solenoid or timing chain.
        * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $1,500 - $3,000+
        * **Recalls:** None Found

**Transmission**

* **Six-speed automatic transmission**
    * **Slipping gears:**
        * **Description/Symptoms:** Transmission slips out of gear, especially when under load, causing a loss of power.
        * **Solution:** Repair or replace the transmission.
        * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $2,000 - $4,000+
        * **Recalls:** None Found
* **Eight-speed automatic transmission**
    * **No major issues found**

**Suspension**

* **Front suspension:**
    * **Strut bearing failure:**
        * **Description/Symptoms:** Unusual noises or clunking sounds from the front suspension, especially during turns or over bumps.
        * **Solution:** Replace the strut bearing.
        * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $300 - $600+
        * **Recalls:** None Found

**Electrical**

* **Battery drain:**
    * **Description/Symptoms:** Vehicle battery drains prematurely, even when parked.
    * **Solution:** Diagnose and repair the source of the electrical draw.
        * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $100 - $500+
        * **Recalls:** None Found
* **Navigation system:**
    * **GPS signal loss:**
        * **Description/Symptoms:** Navigation system fails to acquire or maintain a GPS signal, affecting accurate navigation.
        * **Solution:** Update the navigation software or replace the GPS receiver.
        * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $500 - $1,500+
        * **Recalls:** None Found

**Exterior**

* **Headlight condensation:**
    * **Description/Symptoms:** Condensation forms inside the headlights, affecting visibility at night.
    * **Solution:** Inspect and repair the headlight seals.
    * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $300 - $600+
        * **Recalls:** None Found

**Interior**

* **Sunroof drain tube clogging:**
    * **Description/Symptoms:** Water leaks from the sunroof into the cabin when it rains.
    * **Solution:** Clean or replace the sunroof drain tubes.
    * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $200 - $400+
        * **Recalls:** None Found
* **Rear seat belt buckle:**
    * **Description/Symptoms:** Rear seat belt buckle malfunctions, failing to engage or release.
    * **Solution:** Replace the rear seat belt buckle.
    * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $100 - $200+
        * **Recalls:** None Found

**Summary:**

Overall, the 2011 Lexus IS250 is a reliable vehicle with some minor issues that are common in this year and model. The severity of the issues varies, but most are moderate and can be resolved with regular maintenance and repairs. The estimated cost to fix these issues ranges from $200 to $4,000+ depending on the severity and complexity of the problem.
