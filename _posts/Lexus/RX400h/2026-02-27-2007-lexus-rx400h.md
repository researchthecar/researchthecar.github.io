# 2007 Lexus RX400h

### Overview
The 2007 Lexus RX400h is a luxury hybrid SUV known for its blend of performance, fuel efficiency, and comfort. As a hybrid, it combines a gasoline engine with electric motors, offering a smooth and quiet driving experience. Reliability is generally a strong suit for Lexus vehicles, and the RX400h is no exception, often praised for its durable powertrain and well-built interior. However, like any vehicle, it has specific areas that owners and technicians have noted over time. This report details common technical reliability points for the 2007 RX400h, focusing on potential issues beyond routine maintenance.

---


## Engine

### 3.3L V6 Hybrid (3MZ-FE)
**Inverter Cooling Pump Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Overheating warnings, reduced hybrid system performance, dashboard warning lights.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the inverter cooling pump.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600

**Hybrid Battery Degradation**
- **Symptoms:** Reduced electric-only range, decreased fuel economy, loss of acceleration power.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the hybrid battery pack.
- **Cost:** $3,000 - $5,000

### Infotainment System
**Navigation System Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Screen freezing, inaccurate GPS location, unresponsiveness.

### Summary Verdict
The 2007 Lexus RX400h is a highly reliable hybrid SUV, with most owners reporting satisfaction. The primary concerns tend to revolve around the hybrid system's inverter cooling pump and the eventual degradation of the hybrid battery, which are common for hybrid vehicles of this era. Electrical issues, particularly with the navigation system, and some interior wear on the driver's seat are also noted. However, the core powertrain and mechanical components are generally robust. With proactive maintenance and addressing potential hybrid system issues promptly, the RX400h can continue to provide dependable service.
