# 2006 Lexus RX400h

### Overview
The 2006 Lexus RX400h is a luxury hybrid SUV known for its blend of performance, fuel efficiency, and Lexus's hallmark reliability. This generation, part of the RX series, introduced a sophisticated hybrid powertrain that generally proved robust. Owners often praise its comfortable ride, quiet cabin, and advanced features for its time. While generally a dependable vehicle, like any car, it has specific areas that have seen more attention from owners and mechanics over the years. This analysis focuses on documented technical issues beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential concerns for prospective buyers or current owners.

---


## Engine

### 3.3L V6 Hybrid (3MZ-FE)
**Inverter Cooling Pump Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Overheating of the hybrid system, dashboard warning lights, reduced hybrid system performance.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the inverter cooling pump.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600

**Hybrid Battery Degradation**
- **Symptoms:** Reduced fuel economy, loss of electric-only driving capability, check hybrid system warning light.
- **Solution:** Hybrid battery pack replacement.
- **Cost:** $3,000 - $5,000

### Summary Verdict
The 2006 Lexus RX400h is a highly reliable hybrid SUV, consistent with Lexus's reputation. The primary concerns revolve around the hybrid system's inverter cooling pump and the eventual degradation of the hybrid battery pack, which are common to many hybrid vehicles of this era. Electrical issues are generally minimal, with occasional infotainment glitches. Exterior and interior components are typically durable, though headlight condensation can occur. Overall, the RX400h offers a dependable ownership experience, with potential repair costs mainly concentrated on the hybrid battery and related cooling components if they arise.
