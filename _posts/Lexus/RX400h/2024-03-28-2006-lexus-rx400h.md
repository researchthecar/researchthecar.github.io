The 2006 Lexus RX400h is a mid-size luxury hybrid SUV that offers a comfortable and refined driving experience. It is powered by a 3.3-liter V6 engine that produces 268 horsepower and 240 lb-ft of torque, paired with a continuously variable transmission (CVT). The RX400h also features a spacious cabin, a user-friendly infotainment system, and a suite of advanced safety features. Despite its overall reliability, the 2006 Lexus RX400h has been reported to have some common issues, which are detailed below:

**Engine**

* **3.3L V6**
    * **Intake Manifold Gasket Failure:** Symptoms include rough idling, poor acceleration, and increased oil consumption. Solution: Replace the intake manifold gasket. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800+

**Transmission**

* **Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)**
    * **Shifting Problems:** Symptoms include slipping, hesitation, or jerking during gear changes. Solution: Reinitialize transmission control module or replace CVT if necessary. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $3,000+
    * **Coolant Line Leaks:** Symptoms include overheating and coolant loss. Solution: Replace the defective coolant line. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+

**Suspension**

* **Front Lower Control Arm Bushing Wear:** Symptoms include clunking or rattling noises from the front suspension, especially on uneven roads. Solution: Replace the worn control arm bushings. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+

**Electrical**

* **Smart Key System Malfunction:** Symptoms include difficulty starting the vehicle or unlocking the doors. Solution: Reprogram or replace the smart key system. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
* **Battery Drain:** Symptoms include a dead battery after sitting for a period of time. Solution: Diagnose and repair the source of the battery drain, which could be a faulty electrical component or a parasitic drain. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+

**Exterior**

* **Roof Rack Crossbars:** Symptoms include rattling or wind noise. Solution: Tighten or replace the crossbars. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+

**Interior**

* **Navigation System Software Issues:** Symptoms include freezing, slow performance, or incorrect directions. Solution: Update the navigation system software. Estimated Cost to Fix: Free if under warranty, otherwise $100 - $200+
* **Power Seat Failure:** Symptoms include difficulty adjusting the seat or seat malfunctioning. Solution: Repair or replace the power seat motor or switch. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+

**Other**

* **Hybrid Battery Failure:** Symptoms include decreased fuel efficiency, reduced power, or check engine light. Solution: Replace the hybrid battery. Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+

**Summary**

Overall, the 2006 Lexus RX400h is a reliable vehicle with a comfortable and luxurious driving experience. However, it has been reported to have some common issues, including engine intake manifold gasket failure, transmission shifting problems, suspension control arm bushing wear, and electrical issues. The severity of these issues can vary, and the cost to fix them can range from minor to major repairs. If you are considering purchasing a 2006 Lexus RX400h, it is important to be aware of these potential issues and to have the vehicle inspected by a qualified mechanic before making a purchase decision.
