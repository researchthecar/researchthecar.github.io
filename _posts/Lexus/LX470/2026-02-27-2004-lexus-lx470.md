# 2004 Lexus LX470

### Overview
The 2004 Lexus LX470, a luxury full-size SUV, is renowned for its robust construction and legendary Toyota reliability. Based on the Land Cruiser platform, it offers a blend of off-road capability and on-road comfort. While generally very dependable, like any vehicle, it can experience specific issues as it ages. This analysis focuses on documented common problems beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential areas of concern for owners and prospective buyers. The LX470's strong build quality contributes significantly to its long-term durability, making it a solid choice for those seeking a capable and reliable luxury SUV.

---


## Engine

### 4.7L V8 (2UZ-FE)
**Timing Belt Replacement**
- **Symptoms:** While not a failure, this is a critical maintenance item. Neglecting it can lead to catastrophic engine damage.
- **Solution:** Scheduled replacement at recommended intervals (typically 90,000 miles).
- **Cost:** $700 - $1200

**Oil Leaks (Valve Covers/Rear Main Seal)**
- **Symptoms:** Visible oil drips under the vehicle, oil smell, low oil level.
- **Solution:** Replacement of gaskets or seals.
- **Cost:** $300 - $800 (Valve Covers), $800 - $1500 (Rear Main Seal)

**Coolant Leaks (Water Pump/Hoses)**
- **Symptoms:** Puddles of coolant under the vehicle, overheating, sweet smell.
- **Solution:** Replacement of water pump, hoses, or thermostat.
- **Cost:** $400 - $700

## Suspension

### Hydraulic Height Control (HHC)
**Leaking Hoses/Accumulators**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle sagging, loss of ride height adjustment, 'suspension fault' warning light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of leaking hydraulic lines, accumulators, or pump.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1500 (depending on component)

**Pump Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Complete loss of ride height control, loud whining noise from pump.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the hydraulic pump.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1500

## Electrical

### Body Control Module (BCM)
**Intermittent Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** Various electrical gremlins, such as power window issues, door lock problems, or dashboard warning lights.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and potential reprogramming or replacement of the BCM.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1200


### Navigation System
**Outdated Maps/Screen Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Navigation system not functioning correctly, screen discoloration or unresponsiveness.
- **Solution:** Software update or replacement of the navigation unit.
- **Cost:** $300 - $1000


## Exterior

### Paint/Clear Coat
**Clear Coat Peeling**
- **Symptoms:** Peeling or delaminating clear coat, especially on horizontal surfaces like the roof and hood.
- **Solution:** Repainting affected areas.
- **Cost:** $500 - $2000+ (depending on extent)


### Headlight Lenses
**Clouding/Yellowing**
- **Symptoms:** Reduced headlight output, hazy appearance of headlight lenses.
- **Solution:** Restoration kits or replacement of headlight assemblies.
- **Cost:** $50 - $300 (restoration), $400 - $800 (replacement)


## Interior

### Leather Upholstery
**Cracking/Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Cracking, tearing, or excessive wear on leather seats, especially on driver's side.
- **Solution:** Leather repair kits or professional reupholstering.
- **Cost:** $100 - $500 (repair kit), $1000 - $3000+ (reupholstering)


### Heated Seat Elements
**Malfunctioning Elements**
- **Symptoms:** Heated seats not working or working intermittently.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the heating elements or switches.
- **Cost:** $200 - $500

### Summary Verdict
The 2004 Lexus LX470 is a highly reliable vehicle, consistently praised for its durability. The 4.7L V8 engine is robust, though routine maintenance like timing belt replacement is crucial. The transmission is generally trouble-free. The most common issues tend to arise from the advanced Hydraulic Height Control suspension system, which can develop leaks or pump failures over time. Electrical gremlins related to the BCM or navigation system can occur, and exterior cosmetic issues like clear coat peeling and headlight hazing are common with age. Interior wear on leather is also typical. Despite these potential age-related concerns, the LX470's overall reliability remains exceptionally high, making it a sound investment for those seeking a long-lasting luxury SUV.
