# 2007 Lexus LX470

### Overview
The 2007 Lexus LX470, a luxury full-size SUV, is renowned for its robust construction and legendary Toyota reliability. Based on the Land Cruiser platform, it offers exceptional off-road capability paired with a comfortable and well-appointed interior. This generation, particularly the 2007 model year, benefits from years of refinement, making it a strong contender for those seeking a durable and capable vehicle. While generally very reliable, like any vehicle, it can experience specific issues as it ages, primarily related to its advanced systems and components. This analysis focuses on documented technical reliability concerns beyond routine maintenance.

## Suspension

### Adaptive Variable Suspension (AVS) with Automatic Height Control (AHC)
**Hydraulic Leaks/Pump Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle sagging, suspension warning lights, uneven ride height, pump noise.
- **Solution:** Repair or replace leaking lines, seals, or the AHC pump. Replacement of the entire AHC system may be necessary in severe cases.
- **Cost:** $1,500 - $4,000

**Actuator/Sensor Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** Erratic ride height adjustments, suspension warning lights, stiff or overly soft ride.
- **Solution:** Diagnose and replace faulty actuators or sensors.
- **Cost:** $300 - $800 per component


## Electrical

### Body Control Module (BCM)
**Intermittent Electrical Gremlins**
- **Symptoms:** Various non-critical electrical issues such as power window malfunctions, interior light problems, or dashboard warning lights appearing intermittently.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and potential reprogramming or replacement of the BCM.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1,200


### Navigation System
**Screen/Unit Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Blank screen, frozen display, unresponsiveness, or failure to read discs.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of the navigation unit.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1,500

### Summary Verdict
The 2007 Lexus LX470 is a highly reliable vehicle, largely due to its robust Toyota underpinnings and proven powertrain. The primary areas where owners might encounter significant repair costs are related to the sophisticated Adaptive Variable Suspension (AVS) and Automatic Height Control (AHC) system, which can develop leaks or component failures over time. Electrical issues, particularly with the Body Control Module or the integrated navigation system, are also noted but are less common. Overall, the LX470's mechanical components, including the engine and transmission, are exceptionally durable. Regular maintenance is key, but beyond that, the vehicle offers a strong track record for dependability, making it a sound investment for those seeking a long-lasting luxury SUV.
