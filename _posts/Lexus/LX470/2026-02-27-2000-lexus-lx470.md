# 2000 Lexus LX470

### Overview
The 2000 Lexus LX470 is a highly regarded full-size luxury SUV known for its robust construction and legendary Toyota reliability. Built on the Land Cruiser platform, it offers exceptional durability and off-road capability. While generally very dependable, like any vehicle of this age, certain components may require attention. This analysis focuses on documented common issues beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential areas for owners to be aware of. The LX470's strong engineering and premium materials contribute significantly to its long-term ownership experience, making it a solid choice for those seeking a capable and enduring luxury SUV.

---


## Engine

### 4.7L V8 (2UZ-FE)
**Timing Belt Replacement**
- **Symptoms:** While not a failure, this is a critical maintenance item. Failure to replace can lead to catastrophic engine damage.
- **Solution:** Scheduled replacement every 90,000 miles.
- **Cost:** $700 - $1200

**Head Gasket Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Coolant loss, white smoke from exhaust, engine overheating, milky oil.
- **Solution:** Replacement of head gaskets and associated seals.
- **Cost:** $1500 - $2500

**Oil Leaks (Rear Main Seal, Valve Covers)**
- **Symptoms:** Visible oil drips under the vehicle, burning oil smell.
- **Solution:** Replacement of seals and gaskets.
- **Cost:** $400 - $800

## Suspension

### Adjustable Height Control (AHC)
**Hydraulic Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle sagging, loss of adjustable height function, 'system error' warning light.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of hydraulic lines, pumps, or accumulators.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1500

**Pump Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Inability to raise or lower the vehicle, pump noise.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the AHC pump.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1500

## Electrical

### Body Control Module (BCM)
**Intermittent Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** Gauges acting erratically, power window issues, door lock problems, warning lights.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and potential reprogramming or replacement of the BCM.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1000


### Alternator
**Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Dimming headlights, battery warning light, starting issues.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the alternator.
- **Cost:** $400 - $700

## Interior

### Leather Upholstery
**Cracking/Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Visible cracks and wear on seats, especially driver's side.
- **Solution:** Professional cleaning, conditioning, or repair/replacement of panels.
- **Cost:** $200 - $800


### Navigation System (if equipped)
**Outdated Maps/Functionality**
- **Symptoms:** Inability to find newer roads, slow performance.
- **Solution:** Replacement with modern aftermarket unit or use of external GPS.
- **Cost:** $300 - $1000+

### Summary Verdict
The 2000 Lexus LX470 is a remarkably reliable vehicle, especially considering its age and capability. The 4.7L V8 engine is a strong point, though routine maintenance like timing belt replacement is crucial. The primary areas that may require attention are the sophisticated Adjustable Height Control (AHC) suspension system, which can develop leaks or pump issues, and some electrical components like the BCM or alternator, which can fail over time. Interior wear on leather is common but cosmetic. Overall, with proper maintenance and addressing potential AHC and electrical concerns, the LX470 offers excellent long-term durability and value.
