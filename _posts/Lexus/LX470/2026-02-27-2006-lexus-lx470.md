# 2006 Lexus LX470

### Overview
The 2006 Lexus LX470, a luxury full-size SUV, is renowned for its robust build quality and legendary Toyota reliability. Based on the Land Cruiser platform, it offers exceptional durability and off-road capability. While generally a very dependable vehicle, like any complex machine, it can experience specific issues as it ages. This analysis focuses on documented technical reliability concerns beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential problem areas for owners and prospective buyers. The LX470's strong engineering heritage means that most issues, when they arise, are often related to age and mileage rather than inherent design flaws.

---


## Engine

### 4.7L V8 (2UZ-FE)
**Timing Belt Replacement**
- **Symptoms:** While not a failure, this is a critical maintenance item. Failure to replace can lead to catastrophic engine damage.
- **Solution:** Scheduled replacement every 90,000 miles.
- **Cost:** $700 - $1200

**Head Gasket Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Coolant loss, white smoke from exhaust, overheating, milky oil.
- **Solution:** Replacement of head gaskets and associated components.
- **Cost:** $2000 - $3500

**Oil Leaks (Valve Covers, Oil Pan)**
- **Symptoms:** Visible oil drips, burning oil smell, low oil level.
- **Solution:** Replacement of gaskets or seals.
- **Cost:** $300 - $700

## Suspension

### Adaptive Variable Suspension (AVS) / Automatic Height Control (AHC)
**Hydraulic Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle sagging, loss of ride height adjustment, warning lights.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of hydraulic lines, accumulators, or pump.
- **Cost:** $500 - $2000+

**Pump Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Inability to adjust ride height, constant running of pump, suspension warning lights.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the AHC pump.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1500

### Summary Verdict
The 2006 Lexus LX470 is a highly reliable vehicle, benefiting from Toyota's robust engineering and proven components. The 4.7L V8 engine is generally bulletproof, with the primary concern being the scheduled timing belt replacement. The transmission is also very durable. The most common non-routine issues tend to arise from the advanced Adaptive Variable Suspension (AVS) and Automatic Height Control (AHC) system, which can develop leaks or pump failures due to age and complexity. Electrical gremlins, often related to the Body Control Module or navigation screen, can occur. Overall, the LX470 offers excellent long-term reliability, and most potential issues are well-documented and manageable with proper maintenance and timely repairs.
