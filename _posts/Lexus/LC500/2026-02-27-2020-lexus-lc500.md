# 2020 Lexus LC500 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2020 Lexus LC 500 stands out as a flagship luxury grand tourer, celebrated for its striking design, high-end interior craftsmanship, and robust naturally aspirated V8 performance. When shopping for a used LC 500, buyers generally find a vehicle built to high luxury standards with very few widespread mechanical complaints reported by early owners.

---

## Engine

### 5.0L V8 (2UR-GSE)

This 5.0-liter naturally aspirated V8 produces approximately 471 horsepower and 398 lb-ft of torque, sending power to the rear wheels. It is widely regarded as exceptionally reliable, featuring a high-revving character and robust mechanical architecture.

## Transmission

### Direct-Shift 10-Speed Automatic

A 10-speed Sport Direct Shift automatic transmission manages gear changes for the rear-wheel-drive platform. It offers crisp shifting and smooth highway cruising, with few powertrain complaints noted from owners.

---

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V682000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lexus&model=LC500&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** If the fuel pump fails, the engine can stall while driving, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Toyota will notify owners, and dealers will replace the fuel pump assembly with an improved one, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2020 Lexus LC 500 offers exceptional engineering and very strong reliability credentials for a high-performance grand tourer. Prospective buyers should still conduct a standard pre-purchase inspection and verify service history, but the vehicle generally presents a dependable ownership experience.
