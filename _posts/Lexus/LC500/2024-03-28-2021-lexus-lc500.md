**Introduction:**

The 2021 Lexus LC500 is a luxury sports coupe powered by a naturally aspirated V8 engine that delivers 471 horsepower and 398 lb-ft of torque. It comes with a 10-speed automatic transmission, rear-wheel drive, and a Torsen limited-slip differential. The LC500 is praised for its stunning design, powerful engine, and precise handling, but some owners have reported certain issues with the vehicle.

**Engine:**

**3.5L V6**

* **Engine Misfire:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Engine misfires, causing rough idling and acceleration issues.
    * Solution: Replace faulty ignition coils or spark plugs.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
    * Recalls: NHTSA Campaign Number 22V820000

**5.0L V8**

* **No Issues Found**

**Transmission:**

**10-Speed Automatic**

* **Hesitation Lors Shifting:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Transmission hesitates or delays when shifting gears.
    * Solution: Update transmission software or replace faulty transmission valve body.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension:**

* **No Issues Found**

**Electrical:**

* **Battery Drain:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Battery drains quickly, leading to starting issues.
    * Solution: Replace faulty alternator or check for parasitic drain.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior:**

* **Trunk Latch Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Trunk latch fails to open or close properly.
    * Solution: Replace faulty latch mechanism.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior:**

* **Infotainment System Lag:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Infotainment system experiences lag or freezing issues.
    * Solution: Update software or replace faulty infotainment unit.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

While the 2021 Lexus LC500 is a highly acclaimed sports coupe, some owners have encountered issues with certain components. The engine and transmission problems can be costly to fix, while the electrical, exterior, and interior issues are less severe and generally less expensive to resolve. Overall, the LC500 remains a desirable vehicle, but potential buyers should be aware of these potential problems before making a purchase decision.
