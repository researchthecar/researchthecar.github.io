The 2008 Lexus LX 570 is a full-size luxury SUV known for its off-road capability, comfortable ride, and reputation for reliability. It was only available with a single engine and transmission option: a 5.7L V8 engine producing 383 horsepower and 403 lb-ft of torque, paired with a 6-speed automatic transmission. Owners generally appreciate the LX 570's luxurious interior, smooth ride, and off-road prowess. Common complaints revolve around its poor fuel economy, bulky size, and somewhat dated infotainment system.

**Engine**

**5.7L V8 (3UR-FE)**

*   **Valve Cover Gasket Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks around the valve cover gasket. This can cause a burning oil smell, visible oil leaks on the engine, and potentially lead to low oil levels.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the valve cover gaskets.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Water Pump Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaks, overheating, and a whining noise from the engine.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the water pump, thermostat, and coolant.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $600 - $1000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Starter Motor Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Clicking noise when trying to start the engine, engine not starting.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the starter motor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**6-Speed Automatic**

*   **Torque Converter Shudder**
    * Description/Symptoms: Shaking or shuddering during light acceleration, especially at lower speeds.
    * Solution: Replacement or rebuild of the torque converter; transmission fluid flush may temporarily alleviate symptoms.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1500 - $3500+
    * Recalls: None Found

*   **Valve Body Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: Harsh or delayed shifting, slipping between gears, failure to shift.
    * Solution: Replacement or rebuild of the valve body.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $2000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Air Suspension Compressor Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The vehicle sagging on one or more corners, the "off-road" lights flashing on the dashboard. The compressor may be making excessive noise or not running at all.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the air suspension compressor. Consider replacing the air struts/springs at the same time if they are also showing signs of wear.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1500+ (compressor only), $2000 - $5000+ (compressor and struts)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Air Strut Leaks/Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Vehicle sagging on one or more corners, rough ride, hissing sound from the struts.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the air struts.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $2000+ per strut
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Navigation System/Radio Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Freezing, slow response, blank screen, or failure of the navigation and audio system.
    *   Solution: Software update (if available), or replacement of the head unit.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300 (software), $800 - $2000+ (head unit)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Power Seat Motor Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Power seats not moving in one or more directions.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the seat motor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Power Folding Mirror Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Mirrors not folding properly or at all.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the mirror assembly.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1000+ per mirror
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Dashboard Cracking**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Cracks appearing on the dashboard, especially in areas exposed to sunlight.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the dashboard (expensive), or installation of a dashboard cover.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1500 - $3000+ (dashboard replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2008 Lexus LX 570, while a well-regarded vehicle, does have some common issues to be aware of. The most significant concerns often revolve around the air suspension system, which can be expensive to repair. Engine issues like valve cover leaks and water pump failure are also relatively common, but typically not catastrophic. Transmission issues, while less frequent, can be costly to address. Overall, while the LX 570 boasts a reputation for reliability, prospective buyers should be prepared for potential repair costs, especially those related to the air suspension. The severity of issues can range from minor inconveniences to major repairs, with costs varying accordingly.
