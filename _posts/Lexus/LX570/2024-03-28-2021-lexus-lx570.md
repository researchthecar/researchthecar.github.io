**2021 Lexus LX570**

The 2021 Lexus LX570 is a luxury SUV that comes standard with a 5.7L V8 engine producing 383 horsepower and 403 lb-ft of torque. It is paired with an 8-speed automatic transmission.

Owners of the LX570 praise its spacious and well-equipped interior, powerful engine, and superb off-road capabilities. However, some have reported issues with its fuel economy, reliability, and infotainment system.

**Engine**

* **5.7L V8**

  * **Knocking or ticking noise:** Description/Symptoms: A knocking or ticking noise may be heard coming from the engine, especially when it is cold. Solution: The issue can be resolved by replacing the timing chain tensioners. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
  * **Oil leaks:** Description/Symptoms: Oil may leak from the engine, leaving spots under the vehicle. Solution: The leaks can be fixed by replacing the faulty gaskets or seals. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
  * **Valve train issues:** Description/Symptoms: The LX570 has been known to experience valve train issues, such as worn camshafts or followers. This can lead to reduced engine power and performance. Solution: The issue can be resolved by replacing the affected components. Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $5,000+
Recalls:
  * NHTSA Campaign Number: 22V092000 - Crankshaft Pulley Bolt May Fracture
  * NHTSA Campaign Number: 22V279000 - Fuel Injector Wiring Harness May Short Circuit

**Transmission**

* **8-Speed Automatic**

  * **Harsh shifting:** Description/Symptoms: The transmission may shift harshly, especially when shifting from first to second gear. Solution: The issue can be resolved by updating the transmission software or replacing the transmission valve body. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
  * **Slipping:** Description/Symptoms: The transmission may slip out of gear, causing the vehicle to lose power. Solution: The issue can be resolved by rebuilding or replacing the transmission. Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $5,000+
Recalls:
  * None Found

**Electrical**

* **Battery issues:** Description/Symptoms: The LX570 may experience battery issues, such as a dead battery or a battery that drains quickly. Solution: The issue can be resolved by replacing the battery or the alternator. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
* **Electrical system problems:** Description/Symptoms: The electrical system may experience issues such as flickering lights, power window failures, or other electrical malfunctions. Solution: The issue can be resolved by replacing the faulty components or repairing the wiring harness. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+
Recalls:
  * NHTSA Campaign Number: 22V219000 - Seat Belt Buckle May Not Lock

**Exterior**

* **Rear hatch issues:** Description/Symptoms: The rear hatch may not open or close properly. Solution: The issue can be resolved by replacing the hatch struts or repairing the power liftgate system. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
* **Rust issues:** Description/Symptoms: The LX570 may experience rust issues, especially on the undercarriage and body panels. Solution: The issue can be resolved by applying rust protection or replacing the affected components. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+
Recalls:

**Summary**

The 2021 Lexus LX570 is a reliable and capable luxury SUV, but it is not without its issues. Some of the most common problems reported by owners include engine knocking, oil leaks, valve train issues, transmission slipping, electrical problems, and rear hatch issues. The severity and cost of these issues can vary depending on the specific problem and the extent of the damage. However, overall, the LX570 is a well-built and reliable SUV that should provide years of trouble-free ownership.
