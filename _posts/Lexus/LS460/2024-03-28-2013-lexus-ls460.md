**Introduction**

The 2013 Lexus LS460 is a full-size luxury sedan powered by a 4.6L V8 engine mated to an 8-speed automatic transmission. It boasts a spacious and luxurious interior, advanced safety features, and a powerful engine, but it also has some reported issues.

**Engine**

* **4.6L V8 Engine**

    * **Intake Manifold Gasket Failure:** Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaking at the intake manifold gasket, causing engine overheating and coolant loss. Solution: Replace intake manifold gasket. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+

**Transmission**

* **8-Speed Automatic Transmission**

    * **Torque Converter Problem:** Description/Symptoms: Transmission shuddering, hesitation, or slipping. Solution: Replace torque converter. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500

**Suspension**

* **No Issues Found**

**Electrical**

* **HID Headlight Failure:** Description/Symptoms: One or both HID headlights malfunctioning or flickering. Solution: Replace ballast or HID bulb. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600
* **Radio Malfunction:** Description/Symptoms: Radio screen unresponsive, erratic behavior, or loss of sound. Solution: Replace or repair radio module. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,200

**Exterior**

* **Door Lock Malfunction:** Description/Symptoms: Doors locking or unlocking erratically, not responding to key fob or interior buttons. Solution: Replace door lock actuator. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500

**Interior**

* **Dashboard Trim Squeaking:** Description/Symptoms: Annoying squeaking noise coming from the dashboard trim. Solution: Tighten or replace loose trim clips. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200
* **Navigation System Freezing:** Description/Symptoms: Navigation system touchscreen unresponsive or freezing. Solution: Update software or replace navigation module. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800

**Summary**

The 2013 Lexus LS460 is a luxurious and powerful sedan with some notable issues. The intake manifold gasket failure and torque converter problem are the most severe and expensive issues. While electrical and interior issues are generally less severe and costly, they can still be annoying and detract from the overall experience. Potential buyers should be aware of these issues and factor them into their decision-making process.
