The 2017 Lexus LS 460 is a luxury sedan known for its refined ride, comfortable interior, and reputation for reliability. The LS 460 came standard with a 4.6-liter V8 engine producing 386 horsepower and 367 lb-ft of torque. All LS 460 models were equipped with an eight-speed automatic transmission. Owners often praise the car's smooth ride quality, quiet cabin, and comfortable seats. However, some common complaints include the somewhat dated infotainment system and less engaging driving dynamics compared to some competitors. This information will provide a comprehensive overview of the most common issues and complaints associated with the 2017 Lexus LS 460.

**Engine**

**4.6L V8 (1UR-FSE)**

*   **Oil Leak from Valve Cover Gaskets**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks around the valve cover area, leading to a burning smell and potential oil consumption. Can also cause oil to drip onto exhaust components.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the valve cover gaskets.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Water Pump Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Coolant leak, overheating, temperature gauge rising, and/or warning lights illuminating.
    * Solution: Replacement of the water pump and potentially the thermostat.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,200+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**A760E 8-Speed Automatic Transmission**

*   **Rough Shifting**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Harsh or delayed shifts between gears, particularly during acceleration or deceleration. May be more noticeable in lower gears.
    *   Solution: Transmission fluid flush or replacement. In more severe cases, valve body repair or replacement may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 (fluid flush) - $2,000+ (valve body)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Air Suspension Compressor Failure (if equipped)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Vehicle sits unevenly, particularly after being parked. Air suspension warning lights may illuminate. Compressor may be excessively noisy or fail to activate.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the air suspension compressor. Air struts may also require replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $2,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Air Suspension Strut Leaks (if equipped)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The air strut bladders will leak. The vehicle will sit unevenly and the air compressor will run continuously trying to keep the air struts inflated.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the leaky air struts.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $2,000+ per strut
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Navigation System Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The navigation screen may freeze, fail to update, or become unresponsive. The GPS may lose signal intermittently.
    *   Solution: Software update or replacement of the navigation unit.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 (Software update) - $1,500+ (replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Battery Drain**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The vehicle's battery drains quickly, especially when the car is not in use. This can lead to difficulty starting the vehicle.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis of the parasitic drain, often caused by faulty modules or accessories.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+ (diagnosis and repair)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Automatic Trunk Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The automatic trunk may not open or close properly, or it may become stuck. Can be caused by sensor or motor failure.
    *   Solution: Repair or replacement of the trunk motor, sensors, or struts.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Dashboard Cracking**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Cracking or splitting of the dashboard material, particularly in hot climates.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the dashboard or use of a dashboard cover.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $2,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2017 Lexus LS 460 is generally a reliable vehicle, but some common issues include oil leaks, water pump failures, rough transmission shifting, air suspension problems, and electrical glitches. The severity and cost of these issues can vary significantly. While some problems like oil leaks and rough shifting can be relatively inexpensive to address, air suspension and electrical issues can be more costly to repair. Buyers should carefully inspect the vehicle, especially the air suspension system (if equipped), and address any warning signs promptly to prevent further damage.
