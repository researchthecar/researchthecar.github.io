Here's a comprehensive breakdown of common issues and complaints for the 2016 Lexus LS460, formatted for your website, and tailored to the specific engine and transmission options.

The 2016 Lexus LS460 is a full-size luxury sedan known for its comfortable ride, quiet interior, and reputation for reliability. It comes standard with a 4.6-liter V8 engine producing 386 horsepower and 367 lb-ft of torque, paired with an 8-speed automatic transmission. Some owners appreciate the smooth and powerful engine, the luxurious interior, and the advanced technology features. Some of the common complaints include the somewhat dated infotainment system, the conservative styling, and the lack of sporty handling compared to some competitors.

**Engine**

**4.6L V8 (1UR-FSE)**

*   **Oil Leak from Valve Cover Gaskets**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Oil leaking from the valve cover gaskets, leading to a burning smell, visible oil on the engine, and potentially low oil levels.
    *   Solution: Replacing the valve cover gaskets.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Water Pump Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Overheating, coolant leaks, and a whining noise coming from the front of the engine. Can cause engine damage if ignored.
    *   Solution: Replacing the water pump and possibly the thermostat.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Alternator Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Battery light illumination, dimming headlights, difficulty starting, and eventual loss of electrical power.
    *   Solution: Replacing the alternator.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $600 - $1200+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**8-Speed Automatic (AA80E)**

*   **Harsh Shifting**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Jerky or abrupt shifts, particularly between lower gears. Can be more noticeable when the transmission is cold.
    *   Solution: Performing a transmission fluid flush, and checking valve bodies for wear. In some cases, valve body replacement may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $2000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Air Suspension Failure (if equipped)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Vehicle sagging on one or more corners, "Air Suspension" warning light, compressor running constantly or not at all, and a rough ride.
    *   Solution: Diagnosing the leak (air struts, compressor, lines, or valve block) and replacing the faulty components.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $3000+ per strut. Compressor $600 - $1500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Control Arm Bushing Wear**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Clunking noises from the suspension, especially when going over bumps, and vague steering feel.
    *   Solution: Replacing the worn control arm bushings. In some cases, it may be more cost-effective to replace the entire control arm.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1000+ per arm.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Navigation/Infotainment System Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Freezing, slow response times, map errors, and inability to connect to Bluetooth.
    *   Solution: Updating the software (if available), replacing the head unit.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+ for software update. $500 - $2000+ for head unit replacement.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Parking Sensor Malfunction**
    *   Description/Symptoms: False alarms, no alarms when an object is present, and error messages on the dashboard.
    *   Solution: Cleaning or replacing the faulty parking sensor(s).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+ per sensor.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Automatic High Beam Malfunction**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Automatic high beams not engaging or disengaging properly, or not working at all.
    *   Solution: Checking the sensor on the windshield and ensuring it is clean and unobstructed. Replacing the sensor if faulty.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Sunroof Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Sunroof jamming, not opening or closing properly, or making excessive noise.
    *   Solution: Cleaning and lubricating the sunroof tracks. Repairing or replacing the sunroof motor or mechanism.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $1000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2016 Lexus LS460 is generally considered a reliable vehicle, but owners may encounter some issues. Engine issues, such as oil leaks and water pump failures, can be relatively expensive to repair. The air suspension (if equipped) can also be a source of significant repair costs. While the infotainment system can be a minor annoyance, more serious problems can arise with the transmission and electrical systems. On the whole, these problems do not occur frequently enough to consider the car unreliable. With proper maintenance, the 2016 LS460 can provide years of comfortable and dependable service.
