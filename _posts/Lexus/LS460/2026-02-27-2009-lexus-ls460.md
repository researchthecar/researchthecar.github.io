# 2009 Lexus LS460

### Overview
The 2009 Lexus LS460 is a luxury sedan renowned for its refined driving experience and high-quality interior. As a flagship model, it generally exhibits strong reliability, reflecting Lexus's commitment to engineering excellence. While most owners report a trouble-free ownership experience, like any vehicle, certain components can occasionally present issues. This analysis focuses on documented, non-wear-and-tear related problems that may arise, providing insights into potential repair areas and their associated costs. The LS460's robust construction and advanced technology contribute to its overall dependable nature, making it a solid choice for those seeking a premium used sedan.

---


## Engine

### 4.6L V8
**Carbon Buildup**
- **Symptoms:** Rough idle, reduced power, decreased fuel efficiency.
- **Solution:** Professional intake manifold cleaning and walnut blasting.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600

**Oil Leaks (Valve Covers)**
- **Symptoms:** Visible oil on engine components, burning oil smell.
- **Solution:** Replacement of valve cover gaskets.
- **Cost:** $400 - $800

### Summary Verdict
The 2009 Lexus LS460 demonstrates excellent overall reliability, with the 4.6L V8 engine and 8-speed automatic transmission being particularly robust. Potential issues are infrequent and often related to specific advanced systems like the Adaptive Variable Suspension (air spring leaks) or the infotainment system (navigation glitches). Electrical components, such as the keyless entry system, can occasionally require attention. Exterior and interior components generally hold up well, with no widespread common failures reported. For owners, addressing potential carbon buildup on the engine or minor oil leaks from valve covers are the most likely non-wear-and-tear repairs. Despite these minor points, the LS460 remains a highly dependable luxury vehicle.
