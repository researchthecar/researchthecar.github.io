**2014 Lexus LS460**

The 2014 Lexus LS460 is a luxury sedan that is known for its smooth ride, quiet cabin, and comfortable interior. It is powered by a 4.6-liter V8 engine that produces 386 horsepower and 367 lb-ft of torque. The LS460 is available with either rear-wheel drive or all-wheel drive and comes standard with an eight-speed automatic transmission.

People who like the LS460 praise its luxurious interior, smooth ride, and quiet cabin. However, some people have complained about its lack of sporty handling and its high price.

**Engine**
* **4.6L V8**
    * **Timing chain rattle:** Symptoms include a rattling noise coming from the engine when it is running and the issue typically occurs around 70,000 miles. Solution: Timing chain replacement. Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,000+
    * **Valve cover leaks:** Symptoms include oil leaking from the valve covers and it typically occurs around 60,000 miles. Solution: Replacing the valve cover gaskets is sufficient to resolve the leak. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
    * **Intake manifold runner motor issues:** Symptoms include the engine stalling or running rough. Solution: Replacing the intake manifold runner motor. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500
* **No other issues found.**

**Transmission**
* **8-speed automatic**
    * **Transmission slipping:** Symptoms include the transmission slipping or shuddering when shifting gears. Solution: Replacing the transmission fluid and filter may fix the issue, however, a transmission rebuild or replacement may also be necessary. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $4,000+
    * **Torque converter shudder:** Symptoms include a shuddering or vibration felt when accelerating or decelerating. Solution: Replacing the torque converter. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000
* **No other issues found.**

**Suspension**
* **Air suspension failure:** Symptoms include the suspension sagging or becoming unresponsive. Solution: Replacing the air suspension system. Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+
* **No other issues found.**

**Electrical**
* **Battery drain:** Symptoms include the battery draining quickly or the car not starting. Solution: Replacing the battery or repairing the charging system. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
* **Navigation system failure:** Symptoms include the navigation system not working or freezing. Solution: Updating the software or replacing the navigation system. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
* **No other issues found.**

**Exterior**
* **Headlight condensation:** Symptoms include condensation forming inside the headlights. Solution: Replacing the headlight assembly. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
* **No other issues found.**

**Interior**
* **Door panel stitching coming loose:** Symptoms include the stitching on the door panels coming loose and separating. Solution: Replacing the door panel. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
* **Center console trim peeling:** Symptoms include the trim on the center console peeling off or becoming discolored. Solution: Replacing the center console trim. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
* **No other issues found.**

**Summary**
Overall, the 2014 Lexus LS460 is a reliable and luxurious sedan that has been praised for its smooth ride, quiet cabin, and comfortable interior. However, there are some potential issues that owners should be aware of, including timing chain rattle, valve cover leaks, intake manifold runner motor issues, transmission slipping, torque converter shudder, suspension failure, and battery drain. The cost of these repairs can vary, but they can be expensive. If you are considering purchasing a 2014 Lexus LS460, be sure to get a thorough inspection and consider purchasing an extended warranty.
