**Introduction:**

The 2007 Lexus LS460 is a luxurious full-size sedan renowned for its smooth ride, refined interior, and impressive performance. It is offered with a 4.6-liter V8 engine paired with an 8-speed automatic transmission, delivering 380 horsepower and 367 lb-ft of torque. While the LS460 is generally well-regarded, it has experienced some issues over the years.

**Engine**

**4.6L V8**

* **Variable Valve Timing Oil Control Valve Issue:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The oil control valve can fail, leading to misfires, rough idling, and reduced engine power.
    * Solution: Replace the oil control valve.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Water Pump Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The water pump can fail, resulting in overheating and potential engine damage.
    * Solution: Replace the water pump.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**8-Speed Automatic**

* **Shifting Problems:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The transmission may experience delayed or rough shifting, especially during acceleration.
    * Solution: Reprogram the transmission control module or replace the transmission fluid.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Air Suspension Compressor Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The compressor for the air suspension system can fail, causing the vehicle to sag or handle poorly.
    * Solution: Replace the air suspension compressor.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $1,500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Alternator Regulator Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The alternator regulator can malfunction, leading to undercharging or overcharging of the battery.
    * Solution: Replace the alternator regulator.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Power Window Motor Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The power window motor can fail, causing one or more windows to become inoperable.
    * Solution: Replace the power window motor.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Trunk Lid Misalignment:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The trunk lid may not align properly, causing it to stick or not close completely.
    * Solution: Adjust or replace the trunk lid hinges.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Navigation Screen Malfunction:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The navigation screen may malfunction, becoming unresponsive or displaying corrupted information.
    * Solution: Replace the navigation screen.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Radio Display Fading:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The display on the radio may fade or become difficult to read.
    * Solution: Replace the radio display or the entire radio unit.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

The 2007 Lexus LS460 is a well-made luxury sedan, but it has experienced some issues related to its engine, transmission, suspension, and electrical systems. While some of these issues are relatively minor and inexpensive to fix, others can be more serious and costly. Overall, the LS460 is a reliable vehicle, but potential buyers should be aware of these potential problems before purchasing one.
