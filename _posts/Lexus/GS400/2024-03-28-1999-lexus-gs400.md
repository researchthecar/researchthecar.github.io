**Introduction:**

The 1999 Lexus GS400 is a mid-size luxury sedan known for its refinement, comfort, and performance. It comes equipped with a powerful V8 engine and a smooth-shifting automatic transmission. Despite its overall reliability, the GS400 has faced some common issues over the years.

**Engine**

* **1UZ-FE V8**
    * **Oil Consumption:** Excessive oil consumption, resulting in low oil levels and potential engine damage.
    Description/Symptoms: Burning of oil, blue smoke from exhaust, low oil levels on dipstick.
    Solution: Replacing piston rings and valve seals.
    Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,000+
    Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

* **A750E Automatic**
    * **Transmission Fluid Leaks:** Leaks from the transmission pan, seals, or lines, causing low transmission fluid levels.
    Description/Symptoms: Transmission slipping or jerking, difficulty shifting gears, whining or grinding noises.
    Solution: Replacing leaking components and topping up transmission fluid.
    Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
    Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Front Suspension Struts:** Premature wear of the front suspension struts, leading to a bumpy ride and reduced handling.
    Description/Symptoms: Noise over bumps, uneven tire wear, reduced shock absorption.
    Solution: Replacing the front suspension struts.
    Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800+
    Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Alternator Failure:** Premature failure of the alternator, resulting in electrical issues and potential battery drain.
    Description/Symptoms: Dimming headlights, difficulty starting the car, electrical component malfunctions.
    Solution: Replacing the alternator.
    Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Headlight Lens Yellowing:** Yellowing or clouding of the headlight lenses, reducing visibility at night.
    Description/Symptoms: Cloudy or yellow headlights.
    Solution: Replacing the headlight lenses or restoring them with a headlight restoration kit.
    Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
    Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Climate Control Malfunction:** Failure of the climate control system components, resulting in inconsistent temperature control.
    Description/Symptoms: Blowing cold or hot air, difficulty adjusting temperature, fan not working.
    Solution: Replacing or repairing failed climate control components.
    Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
    Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

Overall, the 1999 Lexus GS400 is a reliable vehicle, but it has faced some common issues that owners should be aware of. The most significant concerns relate to the engine's oil consumption and the potential for transmission fluid leaks. The cost of repairs for these issues can range from moderate to expensive, so it's important to factor them into the cost of ownership. Despite these issues, the GS400 remains a popular choice for those seeking a luxurious and comfortable driving experience.
