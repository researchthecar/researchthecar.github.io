# 2021 Lexus LS500 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used luxury sedan, the 2021 Lexus LS500 offers a commanding presence and an exceptionally plush cabin. While flagship luxury cars often harbor complex mechanical issues, owner feedback for this model year remains relatively sparse. Prospective buyers should still review powertrain options and check for isolated reports regarding suspension wear, body glass, and lighting components.

---

## Engine

### 3.5L Twin-Turbocharged V6

This 3.5-liter twin-turbocharged V6 produces around 416 horsepower and 442 lb-ft of torque, delivering smooth and effortless acceleration. It serves as the standard and only gasoline engine option for the flagship sedan, generally known for robust long-term durability despite rare isolated owner complaints.

## Transmission

### 10-Speed Direct-Shift Automatic

The 10-speed automatic transmission pairs with rear-wheel drive or all-wheel drive to keep engine revs low and fuel economy optimized on the highway. It provides seamless gear shifts suited to a luxury grand tourer, with very few reliability concerns reported by owners.

## Suspension

### Factory Tires and Wheels

Owner reports highlight occasional tire pressure inconsistencies and premature inner tread wear on factory-equipped tires.

**Slow Air Pressure Loss**
- **Symptoms:** Tires fail to retain recommended air pressure for more than a few weeks, requiring frequent top-offs.
- **Solution:** Have a tire specialist inspect the bead seal, valve stems, and run-flat sidewalls for hidden leaks.

**Inner Tread Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Premature wear occurs specifically along the inner edge of factory-installed tires, reducing grip.
- **Solution:** Check vehicle alignment and suspension geometry, and replace worn tires as necessary.

## Exterior

### Headlight Assemblies

Isolated complaints note exterior lens degradation and internal moisture buildup within the headlight housings.

**Lens Delamination and Condensation**
- **Symptoms:** External lens surfaces show peeling or delamination, accompanied by visible moisture accumulation inside the sealed housing.
- **Solution:** Inspect headlight assemblies for seal integrity and replace affected units if visibility or appearance is compromised.

### Sunroof Glass

An isolated owner reported a rare incident of spontaneous sunroof glass breakage during normal vehicle operation.

**Spontaneous Sunroof Shattering**
- **Symptoms:** The sunroof glass suddenly shatters outward or inward while driving, accompanied by a loud pop and falling glass.
- **Solution:** Clear broken glass from the interior cabin and replace the sunroof glass assembly.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V275000: Air Bags:Sensor:Occupant Classification:Front Passenger](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lexus&model=LS&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 208, "Occupant Crash Protection." Incorrect detection of an occupant may result in improper air bag deployment during a crash, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and repair the front passenger seat, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2021 Lexus LS500 maintains a solid reputation for luxury, comfort, and core mechanical dependability. While a small number of owners have flagged isolated concerns regarding tires, headlights, and sunroof glass, these issues do not indicate widespread systemic failure. Used car buyers should perform a thorough pre-purchase inspection focusing on tire wear, glass condition, and electronics to ensure a trouble-free ownership experience.
