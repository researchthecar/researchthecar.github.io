# 2022 Lexus NX450h+ Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a pre-owned 2022 Lexus NX 450h+, buyers can generally expect the high-end craftsmanship and advanced hybrid engineering typical of the brand. While owner feedback highlights several interesting quirks and isolated incidents—ranging from exterior mirror vibrations to software updates and door lock concerns—it is important to remember that these represent owner-reported experiences rather than widespread mechanical failures. Reviewing specific components and inspecting any vehicle history can help ensure a confident purchase.

---

## Engine

### 2.5L 4-Cylinder Plug-In Hybrid

This plug-in hybrid powertrain combines a 2.5-liter four-cylinder engine with electric motors to produce an estimated 302 horsepower, offering brisk acceleration paired with efficiency. It is generally regarded as a robust and sophisticated setup for everyday driving.

## Transmission

### Electronically Controlled Continuously Variable Transmission (ECVT)

Paired with standard all-wheel drive, this ECVT manages power delivery smoothly between the gas engine and electric motors. It provides seamless acceleration and dependable performance for daily commutes.

## Electrical

### Infotainment and Display

Electrical and infotainment systems govern many convenience features, though occasional software glitches have been reported by owners.

**Infotainment Connectivity**
- **Symptoms:** The touchscreen interface may occasionally fail to connect to smartphones or Apple CarPlay, requiring a system reboot or vehicle restart.
- **Solution:** Turn the infotainment screen off and back on, or restart the vehicle to re-establish connection.

## Exterior

### Side Mirrors

Exterior components generally hold up well, though some drivers have noted mirror stability concerns.

**Excessive Mirror Vibration**
- **Symptoms:** The driver-side mirror may shake or vibrate excessively while driving, causing blurry reflections and compromised visibility, particularly at night.
- **Solution:** Have the mirror assembly inspected by a qualified technician to check for secure mounting or loose components.

## Interior

### Driver Seat

Cabin appointments offer luxury comfort, though individual adjustments and physical seat alignment may occasionally prompt owner concern.

**Crooked Driver Seat Alignment**
- **Symptoms:** The driver's seat may exhibit a misaligned position where one side sits further back than the other, despite being securely bolted to the floor.
- **Solution:** Consult a dealership or specialist to evaluate seat track positioning and anchoring points.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V238000: Structure:Body](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lexus&model=NX450h%2B&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** Missing spot-welds between the body panels and the front shock absorber mounting areas may cause the front shock absorber to separate from the mounting area. A separated shock absorber can cause a loss of vehicle stability, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will add welds to body panels or replace the body panels where welds have failed, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V239000: Electronic Stability Control (Esc):Control Module:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lexus&model=NX450h%2B&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** The Skid Control ECU software may not turn on the Vehicle Stability Control (VSC) system when the vehicle is restarted after the driver has disabled the VSC system. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 126, "Electronic Stability Control Systems." Driving with a deactivated stability.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the Skid Control ECU software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V478000: Electrical System:Propulsion System:Dc/Dc Converter](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lexus&model=NX450h%2B&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** A short circuit can cause the DC-DC converter to overheat, increasing the risk of a vehicle fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the DC-DC converter, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2022 Lexus NX 450h+ brings plug-in hybrid efficiency and luxury features to the compact crossover market. While a handful of owners have documented isolated concerns regarding mirrors, infotainment connectivity, and seat alignment, these reports do not point to systemic flaws. Prospective buyers should thoroughly inspect the vehicle of interest, verify service records, and test all electronics during a pre-purchase test drive.
