# 2023 Lexus RZ450e

### Overview
The 2023 Lexus RZ450e represents Lexus's foray into the all-electric SUV market, built on the e-TNGA platform shared with Toyota's bZ4X. As a premium offering, it aims to deliver the refined driving experience and build quality expected from the Lexus brand. Early data suggests a strong focus on reliability, a hallmark of Lexus vehicles. The RZ450e utilizes a dual-motor all-wheel-drive system, providing consistent power delivery. While electric vehicles generally have fewer moving parts than their internal combustion engine counterparts, potential areas of concern often lie within the battery system, charging components, and advanced electronic features. This analysis focuses on documented technical issues beyond standard maintenance.

### Summary Verdict
The 2023 Lexus RZ450e demonstrates a strong initial reliability profile, consistent with Lexus's reputation. The electric powertrain and core chassis components show no widespread issues. The primary areas of concern, based on early data, are related to the electrical system, specifically intermittent charging port functionality and potential software glitches within the infotainment system. The 12V auxiliary battery has also seen some reports of premature failure, a common point for some EVs. These issues are generally repairable and do not indicate systemic flaws in the vehicle's fundamental design. Overall, the RZ450e appears to be a reliable electric vehicle, with most potential problems being manageable and within the scope of typical EV ownership concerns.
