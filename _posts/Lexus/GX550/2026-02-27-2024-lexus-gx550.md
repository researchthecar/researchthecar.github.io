# 2024 Lexus GX550 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2024 Lexus GX 550 introduces a completely redesigned, rugged luxury SUV platform powered by a twin-turbocharged V6 engine paired with a 10-speed automatic transmission. Because this is the debut year for a brand-new generation, buyers should weigh the appeal of modern styling and advanced capability against the typical growing pains associated with a first-year vehicle release.

---

## Engine

### 3.4L Twin-Turbocharged V6

The 3.4-liter twin-turbocharged V6 engine produces approximately 349 horsepower and 479 lb-ft of torque, providing robust acceleration and towing capability. As a relatively new powerplant shared across select modern Toyota and Lexus applications, buyers should monitor early long-term reliability reports.

## Transmission

### 10-Speed Electronic Automatic Transmission

This 10-speed automatic transmission features a wide gear range designed to optimize both highway cruising efficiency and low-speed off-road crawling. It operates smoothly in standard driving conditions, though first-year models warrant a thorough test drive to check for shift quality.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V744000: Back Over Prevention:Display Function](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lexus&model=GX&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** A software error may cause the rearview camera to freeze or display a blank screen when the vehicle is in reverse. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 111, "Rear Visibility." A rearview camera that fails to display an image can reduce the driver's view behind the vehicle.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the parking assist software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V767000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Engine:Hard Parts Internal/Mechanical](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lexus&model=GX&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** A loss of drive power increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will perform an inspection on the #1 main bearing using vehicle inspection software and drive data, and if necessary, replace the engine. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V179000: Equipment:Other:Labels](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lexus&model=GX&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 110, "Tire Selection and Rims." A vehicle with an incorrect maximum capacity weight value may be overloaded, which can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the load carrying capacity modification label, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V341000: Electrical System: Instrument Cluster/Panel](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lexus&model=GX&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 101, "Control and Displays" and in addition, for the subject Mirai vehicles, FMVSS 305, "Electric-powered vehicles: Electrolyte Spillage and Electrical Shock Protection." An instrument panel display that fails to show critical safety.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the combination meter software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

As a first-year redesign, the 2024 Lexus GX 550 brings impressive capability, fresh styling, and modern luxury to the table. While early owner feedback and data show few documented mechanical failures outside of standard recall campaigns, used-car buyers looking for absolute bulletproof reliability may want to give the platform a couple of years to mature.
