**Introduction**

The 2001 Lexus GS430 is a luxury sports sedan known for its smooth ride, powerful engine, and luxurious interior. It is offered with a single engine option: a 4.3-liter V8 producing 300 horsepower and 325 lb-ft of torque, paired with a 5-speed automatic transmission. Despite its well-rounded performance, the GS430 has faced some specific issues.

**Engine**

**4.3L V8**

* **Intake Manifold Gasket Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaks, overheating, rough idle
    * Solution: Replace intake manifold gaskets
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
    * Recalls: NHTSA Campaign ID Number: 12V401000

**Transmission**

**5-Speed Automatic**

* **No Issues Found**

**Suspension**

* **No Issues Found**

**Electrical**

* **Alternator Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Electrical system malfunctions, battery discharge
    * Solution: Replace alternator
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,200
* **Ignition Coil Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Engine misfires, hard starting
    * Solution: Replace ignition coils
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $600

**Exterior**

* **No Issues Found**

**Interior**

* **Climate Control System Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Air conditioning or heating malfunction
    * Solution: Repair or replace climate control system components
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500
* **Power Window Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Windows stuck or malfunctioning
    * Solution: Repair or replace window motors or switches
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500

**Summary**

While the Lexus GS430 is generally a reliable vehicle, it has faced some specific issues. Engine intake manifold gasket failures and electrical problems, such as alternator and ignition coil failures, have been reported. Interior issues include climate control system failures and power window malfunctions. Although the severity of these issues can vary, they can be costly to fix, ranging from a few hundred dollars to over a thousand dollars.
