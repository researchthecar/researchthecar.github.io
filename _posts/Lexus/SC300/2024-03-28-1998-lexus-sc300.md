## 1998 Lexus SC300

The 1998 Lexus SC300 is a luxury sports coupe that was produced by Lexus, the luxury division of Toyota. The SC300 was powered by a 3.0-liter V6 engine that produced 225 horsepower and 210 lb-ft of torque. The engine was paired with a four-speed automatic transmission.

The SC300 was praised for its smooth ride, comfortable interior, and powerful engine. However, it was also criticized for its high price and lack of features.

**Engine**

**3.0L V6**

* **Timing Belt Tensioner Failure:** The timing belt tensioner can fail, causing the timing belt to slip and potentially damage the engine. Symptoms include a rattling noise from the engine, difficulty starting the engine, and a loss of power. The solution is to replace the timing belt tensioner. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
* **Intake Manifold Gasket Leak:** The intake manifold gasket can leak, causing a loss of power and a decrease in fuel economy. Symptoms include a rough idle, a decrease in power, and a check engine light. The solution is to replace the intake manifold gasket. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500
* **Ignition Coil Failure:** The ignition coils can fail, causing a loss of spark to the spark plugs. Symptoms include a misfire, a rough idle, and a decrease in power. The solution is to replace the ignition coils. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200 per coil
* **Catalytic Converter Failure:** The catalytic converter can fail, causing a loss of power and a decrease in fuel economy. Symptoms include a rattling noise from the exhaust system, a decrease in power, and a check engine light. The solution is to replace the catalytic converter. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000

**Transmission**

**4-Speed Automatic**

* **Transmission Fluid Leak:** The transmission fluid can leak from the transmission pan, causing a loss of fluid and potential damage to the transmission. Symptoms include a slipping transmission, a rough shifting transmission, and a check engine light. The solution is to replace the transmission pan gasket. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
* **Torque Converter Failure:** The torque converter can fail, causing a loss of power and a decrease in fuel economy. Symptoms include a slipping transmission, a rough shifting transmission, and a check engine light. The solution is to replace the torque converter. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000

**Suspension**

* **Strut Mount Failure:** The strut mounts can fail, causing a clunking noise from the suspension and a decrease in ride quality. Symptoms include a clunking noise from the suspension, a decrease in ride quality, and a misalignment of the wheels. The solution is to replace the strut mounts. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500
* **Sway Bar Link Failure:** The sway bar links can fail, causing a clunking noise from the suspension and a decrease in handling. Symptoms include a clunking noise from the suspension, a decrease in handling, and a misalignment of the wheels. The solution is to replace the sway bar links. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
* **Ball Joint Failure:** The ball joints can fail, causing a clunking noise from the suspension and a decrease in handling. Symptoms include a clunking noise from the suspension, a decrease in handling, and a misalignment of the wheels. The solution is to replace the ball joints. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400

**Electrical**

* **Alternator Failure:** The alternator can fail, causing a loss of power and a decrease in battery life. Symptoms include a dim or flickering headlights, a decrease in battery life, and a check engine light. The solution is to replace the alternator. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
* **Starter Failure:** The starter can fail, causing the engine to not start. Symptoms include a clicking noise when trying to start the engine, a decrease in battery life, and a check engine light. The solution is to replace the starter. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500
* **Electrical Wiring Harness Failure:** The electrical wiring harness can fail, causing a loss of power and a decrease in battery life. Symptoms include a dim or flickering headlights, a decrease in battery life, and a check engine light. The solution is to replace the electrical wiring harness. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000

**Exterior**

* **Sunroof Weatherstripping Failure:** The sunroof weatherstripping can fail, causing water to leak into the cabin. Symptoms include water leaking into the cabin, a musty odor in the cabin, and a decrease in visibility. The solution is to replace the sunroof weatherstripping. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
* **Headlight Lens Oxidation:** The headlight lenses can oxidize over time, causing a decrease in visibility. Symptoms include a decrease in visibility, a decrease in headlight brightness, and a yellowing of the headlight lenses. The solution is to replace the headlight lenses. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400

**Interior**

* **Dashboard Cracking:** The dashboard can crack over time, causing a decrease in the appearance of the interior. Symptoms include cracks in the dashboard, a decrease in the appearance of the interior, and a decrease in the value of the vehicle. The solution is to replace the dashboard. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
* **Climate Control Failure:** The climate control system can fail, causing a decrease in the comfort of the interior. Symptoms include a decrease in the comfort of the interior, a decrease in the temperature of the cabin, and a check engine light. The solution is to replace the climate control system. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000

**Summary**

The 1998 Lexus SC300 is a well-built and reliable vehicle. However, it can experience some common issues, including timing belt tensioner failure, intake manifold gasket leak, ignition coil failure, and catalytic converter failure. These issues can be expensive to fix, so it is important to factor them into the cost of ownership.
