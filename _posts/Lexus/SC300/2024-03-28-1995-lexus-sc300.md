**Introduction:**

The 1995 Lexus SC300 is a luxury coupe powered by a 3.0-liter inline-six engine. It offers a smooth and comfortable ride, along with impressive performance. However, like any vehicle, it has its share of reported issues that owners should be aware of before purchasing.

**Engine**

**3.0L 2JZ-GE Inline-Six**

* **Oil Leak from Valve Cover Gaskets:** Leaking gaskets can cause oil to seep into other engine components, leading to performance issues and potential damage. Solution: Replace the valve cover gaskets. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
* **Intake Manifold Gasket Failure:** A failing intake manifold gasket can cause air leaks, rough idling, and loss of power. Solution: Replace the intake manifold gasket. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
* **Timing Belt Failure:** Neglecting to replace the timing belt at regular intervals can lead to a catastrophic failure, potentially requiring a complete engine rebuild. Solution: Replace the timing belt every 60,000 miles. Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,200+

**Recalls:**

* None Found

**Transmission**

**4-Speed Automatic (A343E)**

* **Transmission Slipping:** Worn clutches or seals can cause the transmission to slip or hesitate during gear changes. Solution: Rebuild or replace the transmission. Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,000+
* **Failed Torque Converter:** A faulty torque converter can cause shuddering, vibration, or loss of power. Solution: Replace the torque converter. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,200 - $2,000+

**5-Speed Manual (W58)**

* **Shifting Problems:** Worn synchros or linkage can make it difficult to shift gears smoothly. Solution: Repair or replace the affected components. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+

**Recalls:**

* None Found

**Suspension**

* **Front Strut Top Mounts:** Worn strut top mounts can lead to noise, vibration, and reduced handling performance. Solution: Replace the strut top mounts. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+
* **Rear Shock Absorbers:** Failed rear shock absorbers can result in a rough ride and poor handling. Solution: Replace the rear shock absorbers. Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $600+

**Electrical**

* **Alternator Failure:** A failing alternator can prevent the vehicle from charging its battery properly. Solution: Replace the alternator. Estimated Cost to Fix: $600 - $1,000+
* **Battery Drain:** A parasitic battery drain can discharge the battery overnight or after prolonged parking. Solution: Identify and fix the source of the drain. Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the cause.
* **Cruise Control Failure:** Malfunctioning cruise control components can prevent the system from engaging or maintaining speed. Solution: Replace the affected components. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+

**Exterior**

* **Door Handle Failure:** Broken door handles can make it difficult to open the doors. Solution: Replace the door handles. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+ per handle
* **Tail Light Electrical Issues:** Faulty wiring or connections can cause the tail lights to malfunction. Solution: Repair or replace the affected wiring or connectors. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+

**Interior**

* **Dashboard Cracking:** Prolonged sun exposure can cause the dashboard to crack. Solution: Repair or replace the dashboard. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
* **Air Conditioning Blower Failure:** A failed blower motor can cause the air conditioning or heater to stop working. Solution: Replace the blower motor. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+

**Summary:**

The 1995 Lexus SC300 is a well-built and reliable vehicle, but it has some common issues that owners should be aware of, particularly with the engine, transmission, and suspension. While the severity of these issues varies, some can be costly to repair. It's recommended to have the vehicle thoroughly inspected by a qualified mechanic before purchasing to identify any potential problems.
