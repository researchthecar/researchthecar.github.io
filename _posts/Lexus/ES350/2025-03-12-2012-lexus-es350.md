The 2012 Lexus ES 350 is a mid-size luxury sedan known for its comfortable ride, quiet cabin, and reliability. It came standard with a 3.5-liter V6 engine producing 268 horsepower and 248 lb-ft of torque, paired with a 6-speed automatic transmission. Owners generally appreciate the car's smooth ride, spacious interior, and Lexus' reputation for dependability. Criticisms often center around the somewhat conservative styling and a less engaging driving experience compared to some competitors.

**Engine**

**3.5L 2GR-FE V6**

*   **Oil Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks can occur from various locations, including the valve cover gaskets and timing cover. Symptoms include visible oil leaks on the engine, a burning oil smell, and low oil levels.
    *   Solution: Replacing the leaking gaskets or seals.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+ depending on the location and complexity of the repair.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**6-Speed Automatic (U660E)**

*   **Torque Converter Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners report issues with the torque converter, leading to shuddering or hesitation during acceleration, particularly at lower speeds.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the torque converter.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **No Issues Found**

**Electrical**

*   **Navigation System Failures**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The navigation system can experience failures, including screen freezes, inaccurate GPS data, or complete system shutdowns.
    *   Solution: Software updates or replacement of the navigation unit.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100-$1000+ depending on if the unit is repaired or needs to be replaced
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Bluetooth Connectivity Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: Problems pairing devices, dropped connections, and inability to transfer phonebook contacts.
    * Solution: Software updates, resetting the system, or replacing the Bluetooth module.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 - $500+ depending on what needs to be replaced
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **No Issues Found**

**Interior**

*   **Dashboard Cracking**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The dashboard can develop cracks, particularly in warmer climates or areas with prolonged sun exposure.
    *   Solution: Replacing the dashboard or using a dashboard cover.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $2,000+ to replace the dashboard; dashboard covers are significantly cheaper.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Center Console Peeling/Cracking**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The material on the center console, particularly around the gear selector and cup holders, can peel or crack.
    *   Solution: Replacing the affected trim pieces or the entire center console.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+ depending on the severity and the parts required.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2012 Lexus ES 350 is generally a reliable vehicle, but certain issues can arise. Oil leaks and torque converter problems are the most significant mechanical concerns, potentially leading to costly repairs. Electrical issues with the navigation and bluetooth can be a minor annoyance. Interior problems like dashboard and center console cracking can affect the vehicle's appearance.
