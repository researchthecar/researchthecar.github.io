**Introduction:**
The 2013 Lexus ES350 is a midsize luxury sedan powered by a 3.5-liter V6 engine mated to a 6-speed automatic transmission. It delivers 268 horsepower and 248 lb-ft of torque. The ES350 is praised for its comfortable ride, luxurious interior, and advanced safety features. However, it is critiqued for its uninspiring handling and lack of engaging driving dynamics.

**Engine: 3.5-Liter V6**
- **No Issues Found**

**Transmission: 6-Speed Automatic**

**Suspension:**
- **Loose Rear Suspension Bushing:** The rear suspension bushings can loosen, causing a clunking noise and decreased handling stability.
    - Solution: Replace the rear suspension bushings.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $600+
- **Premature Rear Shock Absorber Wear:** The rear shock absorbers can wear out prematurely, leading to a rough ride and decreased suspension performance.
    - Solution: Replace the rear shock absorbers.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $700+
- **Rear Subframe Bending:** In rare cases, the rear subframe can bend under stress, causing suspension misalignment and reduced handling.
    - Solution: Reinforce or replace the rear subframe.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+

**Electrical:**
- **Battery Drain:** The battery can drain prematurely if the vehicle is left unused for an extended period due to a parasitic electrical draw.
    - Solution: Identify and fix the source of the parasitic draw.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
- **Faulty Alternator:** The alternator can fail, resulting in insufficient power generation and potential electrical problems.
    - Solution: Replace the alternator.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $700+
- **Faulty Ignition Switch:** The ignition switch can fail, preventing the vehicle from starting.
    - Solution: Replace the ignition switch.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+

**Exterior:**
- **Loose Body Panel Clips:** The body panel clips can become loose, causing rattling or squeaking noises.
    - Solution: Replace or tighten the loose clips.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $200+
- **Fog Light Condensation:** The fog lights can accumulate condensation, reducing their effectiveness.
    - Solution: Replace the fog light assembly.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+

**Interior:**
- **Cracked Dashboard:** The dashboard can develop cracks over time, especially in warmer climates.
    - Solution: Replace the dashboard.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
- **Faulty Power Window Switches:** The power window switches can fail, causing the windows to become inoperable.
    - Solution: Replace the faulty switches.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+
- **Rear Seat Belt Release Problem:** The rear seat belt release mechanism can malfunction, making it difficult to release the seat belt.
    - Solution: Replace the rear seat belt release mechanism.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+

**Summary:**
The 2013 Lexus ES350 generally has few significant issues. The most common problems reported are related to suspension and electrical components. The cost of repairs can range from minor to moderate, but most issues can be resolved without major expense. Overall, the ES350 remains a reliable and comfortable luxury sedan with low maintenance costs.
