# 2016 Jeep Patriot Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2016 Jeep Patriot offers a budget-friendly entry point into SUV ownership with traditional Jeep styling, but used-car buyers should approach with caution. Widely reported owner complaints point to recurring mechanical and electrical issues, particularly concerning unexpected engine stalling and transmission performance. Reviewing available service history and conducting a thorough pre-purchase inspection is strongly recommended.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L 4-Cylinder Engine

The base 2.0-liter naturally aspirated four-cylinder engine produces approximately 158 horsepower and 141 lb-ft of torque, paired typically with a manual or continuously variable transmission. While adequate for basic commuting, this engine option is frequently associated with owner complaints regarding unexpected stalling and electrical warning lights.

**Engine Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle randomly stalls while driving at various speeds or idling, often accompanied by dashboard warning lights before shutting down completely.
- **Solution:** Diagnose sensor functionality, inspect the crankshaft or camshaft position sensors, and replace faulty components as necessary.

**Loss of Motive Power**
- **Symptoms:** Sudden reduction in engine power or failure to accelerate properly while depressing the accelerator pedal.
- **Solution:** Inspect the throttle body, powertrain control module, and related electrical sensors for faults.

### 2.4L 4-Cylinder Engine

The larger 2.4-liter four-cylinder engine generates around 172 horsepower and 165 lb-ft of torque, providing slightly better power for highway merging and all-wheel-drive configurations. Like the base engine, it shares vulnerability to sensor-related stalling and electrical quirks reported by owners.

**Electrical Warning Lights and Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** Random illumination of dashboard warning indicators followed by engine hesitation or sudden stalling during operation.
- **Solution:** Check battery health, wiring harnesses, and replace failing engine management sensors.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

The conventional 6-speed automatic transmission offers relatively conventional shifting dynamics compared to older CVT setups, providing a more traditional driving feel for daily motorists.

### Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT) / 5-Speed Manual

Depending on the drivetrain configuration, these older transmission options can exhibit hesitation, gear-engagement lag, and premature mechanical wear that require careful verification during a test drive.

**Transmission Slipping and Harsh Shifting**
- **Symptoms:** Jerking during gear changes, hesitation when engaging gears, or a noticeable slipping sensation under acceleration.
- **Solution:** Check transmission fluid condition and level, update control software, or rebuild/replace worn internal components.

## Brakes

### Service Brake System

Braking components require regular maintenance, with wheel speed sensor and caliper issues occasionally triggering stability warning lights.

**ABS and Traction Control Faults**
- **Symptoms:** Anti-lock brake (ABS) and traction control warning lights illuminate intermittently, sometimes accompanied by spongy pedal feel or sticking calipers.
- **Solution:** Inspect wheel speed sensors, tone rings, brake calipers, and ABS module connections.

## Electrical

### Totally Integrated Power Module (TIPM)

The electrical architecture is prone to component failures, most notably within the TIPM, which can lead to frustrating intermittent accessory and lighting issues.

**Electrical System Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** Erratic behavior from exterior lights, horn failures, and random warning lights caused by internal electrical distribution faults.
- **Solution:** Inspect, repair, or replace the Totally Integrated Power Module (TIPM) assembly.
- **Cost:** $1,000+

## Safety

### Active Headrest

Cabin comfort components are generally durable, though the safety-related active headrest mechanism has a documented history of accidental deployment.

**Inadvertent Headrest Deployment**
- **Symptoms:** The driver or passenger active head restraint deploys violently forward without a rear-end collision, striking occupants in the head.
- **Solution:** Replace the damaged internal mechanical retention clips or the entire headrest assembly.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V907000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Engine](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Jeep&model=Patriot&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** If the engine stalls, there is an increased the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will replace the crankshaft or camshaft sensor connector, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2016 Jeep Patriot can serve as an affordable commuter or light utility vehicle, but prospective buyers should proceed with caution due to frequent owner complaints regarding unexpected engine stalling, transmission hesitation, and electrical faults. A thorough mechanical inspection focusing on the powertrain and electrical systems is essential before purchase.
