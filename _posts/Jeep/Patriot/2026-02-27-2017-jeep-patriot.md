# 2017 Jeep Patriot Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2017 Jeep Patriot represents the final model year of a long-running, boxy compact SUV that borrowed heavily from traditional Jeep styling cues. Beneath its rugged exterior, however, the Patriot is widely known for aging mechanical components, particularly its available continuously variable transmission and dated interior build quality. Used car buyers looking at this model should approach with careful inspection, as mechanical and electrical issues can turn an affordable purchase into a costly project.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L 4-Cylinder Engine

This entry-level 2.0-liter naturally aspirated four-cylinder produces approximately 158 horsepower and 141 lb-ft of torque. It offers basic, utilitarian performance for daily commuting but can feel underpowered when passing on highways.

### 2.4L 4-Cylinder Engine

The larger 2.4-liter naturally aspirated four-cylinder pushes out roughly 172 horsepower and 165 lb-ft of torque, providing slightly better grunt for highway driving and towing. While mechanically straightforward, owners occasionally report exhaust and timing wear as mileage climbs.

## Transmission

### 5-Speed Manual Transmission

A traditional 5-speed manual transmission paired with front-wheel drive configurations on base trims. It is generally more mechanically resilient than its automatic counterparts, though clutch wear depends heavily on prior driver habits.

### Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)

The available continuously variable automatic transmission has proven to be a frequent trouble spot for long-term reliability. Buyers should carefully evaluate shifting smoothness during a test drive.

**CVT Hesitation and Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers experience severe bucking, jerking during acceleration, or sudden transmission failure leaving the vehicle immobilized.
- **Solution:** Requires professional diagnosis; advanced wear typically demands a complete transmission rebuild or replacement.

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

A conventional 6-speed automatic transmission that replaced the CVT on many four-wheel-drive variants. It generally provides a more traditional driving feel and improved long-term durability.

## Exterior

### Security and Body Control Modules

Electrical architecture can exhibit quirks as vehicles age, with security and accessory modules occasionally requiring professional intervention.

**Security Module Shuts Down Vehicle**
- **Symptoms:** The security system malfunctions, unexpectedly shutting down the engine and preventing the car from starting or running reliably.
- **Solution:** Reset or replace the security module at a certified dealership or specialized repair facility.

## Safety

### Active Head Restraints

Interior cabin components are basic, but the active headrest safety system has generated notable owner complaints regarding accidental deployment.

**Spontaneous Headrest Deployment**
- **Symptoms:** The active head restraints pop open unexpectedly while driving without any collision, striking occupants in the head and neck.
- **Solution:** Replace the damaged headrest assembly and internal triggering mechanism.

## Summary Verdict

The 2017 Jeep Patriot remains an affordable entry point into utility-style driving, but prospective buyers should proceed with caution. Critical pain points include potential CVT transmission failure and unexpected active headrest deployments. Thoroughly inspect service history, test all electrical features, and prioritize models equipped with the manual or conventional 6-speed automatic transmission to minimize long-term headaches.
