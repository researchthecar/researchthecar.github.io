# 2022 Jeep Wagoneer Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2022 Jeep Wagoneer marks the return of an iconic nameplate, offering robust towing capability, spacious three-row seating, and upscale luxury features. However, as a first-model-year vehicle, it has faced notable owner complaints regarding complex electrical glitches, unexpected engine stalling, and front-end handling vibrations. Used-car shoppers should carefully review service histories and prioritize models where early teething issues have been thoroughly addressed.

---

## Engine

### 3.0L Hurricane Twin-Turbo I6

A potent inline-six engine producing robust horsepower and torque, offering strong towing performance for the large SUV.

### 5.7L HEMI V8 with eTorque

A traditional V8 engine paired with a mild-hybrid eTorque system, providing strong low-end grunt, though early units have logged complaints concerning electrical coordination and stalling.

**Engine Stalling and Auto Start-Stop Failures**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may stall unexpectedly when stopping at traffic lights or during low-speed driving, sometimes engaging the emergency parking brake automatically.
- **Solution:** Dealerships may need to reprogram software modules, update start-stop systems, or inspect electrical and fuel pump components.

## Transmission

### TorqueFlite 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

A smooth-shifting 8-speed automatic transmission paired with rear-wheel drive or various four-wheel-drive systems, generally praised for responsive gear selection.

## Brakes

### Brake System

Provides strong stopping power for a heavy SUV, though integration with electronic parking brakes can occasionally trigger false warnings.

**Brake Pedal Stiffness and Electronic Braking Interferences**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers report hard brake pedal feel or unexpected activation of the emergency parking brake during electrical glitches or start-stop malfunctions.
- **Solution:** Inspect electrical systems, auxiliary batteries, and brake booster connections for fault codes and software updates.

## Steering

### Front Suspension & Steering Alignment

Equipped with independent front and rear setups or air suspension options, but front-end vibration and alignment sensitivity require close inspection.

**Highway Vibration and Rapid Tire Wear**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle develops a noticeable shake or wobble at highway speeds (70-80 mph), often accompanied by premature balding on the outside edges of front tires.
- **Solution:** Perform a thorough front-end alignment and inspect steering and suspension components for premature wear or geometry issues.

## Electrical

### Electrical and Charging System

The complex electronics suite manages extensive cabin displays and safety features, making reliable alternator and battery health critical.

**Battery Drain and Power Loss**
- **Symptoms:** Sudden electrical failures, low battery warnings, or alternator faults resulting in a total loss of power steering and braking assist.
- **Solution:** Test the primary and auxiliary batteries, check alternator output, and install updated charging software if necessary.

## Exterior

### Door Latches and Seating

Cabin features premium amenities, though mechanical seat adjustments and door latch components merit a physical check during a pre-purchase inspection.

**Rear Door Latch and Second-Row Seat Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** Rear doors unlocking or opening unexpectedly from the inside, or second-row bench seats sticking in fixed positions.
- **Solution:** Inspect door latch mechanisms and seat tracks; lubricate or replace faulty components as needed.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V919000: Seats](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Jeep&model=Wagoneer&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 207, "Seating Systems." A seat back that is not locked into position can increase the risk of injury in a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace as necessary, the rear center-seat recliner strap, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V873000: Air Bags: Air Bag/Restraint Control Module:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Jeep&model=Wagoneer&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** Disabled air bags can increase the risk of injury to occupants in a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will reprogram the ORC, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V00A000: Back Over Prevention:Display Function](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Jeep&model=Wagoneer&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 111, "Rear Visibility." A rearview camera that does not display an image reduces the driver's rear view, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will reprogram the radio software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V545000: Structure:Body:Roof And Pillars](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Jeep&model=Wagoneer&modelYear=2022)**

## Summary Verdict

The 2022 Jeep Wagoneer delivers impressive luxury, utility, and capability, but its status as a launch-year model means buyers should proceed with caution. Pre-owned shoppers should verify that all software updates have been applied, inspect the front tires for abnormal wear patterns, and ensure the electrical and charging systems are fully healthy. A comprehensive pre-purchase inspection is highly recommended before finalizing any purchase.
