# 2023 Jeep Wagoneer Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2023 Jeep Wagoneer enters the used market as a full-size, three-row luxury SUV loaded with advanced technology and robust towing capabilities. However, prospective buyers should review owner-reported feedback carefully, as early-production models have exhibited notable electrical, powertrain, and sudden-stalling concerns. While it offers spacious comfort and strong performance, verifying service records and remaining recall statuses is essential before purchasing.

---

## Engine

### 3.0L Twin-Turbo Inline-6 (Hurricane)

The 3.0-liter twin-turbocharged inline-6 engine produces approximately 420 horsepower and 468 lb-ft of torque, delivering strong acceleration and towing capacity. Buyers should inspect for any historical electrical or stalling complaints tied to early powertrain software iterations.

**Engine Stalling and Power Loss**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle suddenly shuts down or loses motive power while driving at low speeds, idling, or cruising in traffic.
- **Solution:** Diagnostic evaluation by a certified dealer to check software modules, sensors, and electrical connections.

### 5.7L V8 with eTorque

The 5.7-liter V8 engine pairs with a mild-hybrid eTorque system to generate 392 horsepower and 404 lb-ft of torque, offering traditional V8 capability. It provides smooth power delivery, though used buyers should verify battery and starter-generator health on hybrid-equipped models.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 8-speed automatic transmission comes paired with rear-wheel or four-wheel drive systems, providing smooth gear changes under normal operation. Reliability is generally acceptable, but rare control module glitches have led to severe shifting and sudden parking engagement incidents.

**Transmission Control Module and Shifter Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle fails to shift out of first gear, experiences unexpected parking brake engagement, or abruptly shifts into park while in motion.
- **Solution:** Reprogramming or replacement of the transmission control module and inspection of gear selector linkages.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

Braking performance is normally secure for a heavy SUV, though rare electrical faults have been reported to coincide with reduced braking responsiveness.

**Brake Pedal Travel and Response Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Brake pedal travels all the way to the floor with diminished stopping responsiveness during unexpected vehicle power downs.
- **Solution:** Thorough inspection of brake fluid pressure, electronic brake boosters, and related control modules.

## Steering

### Power Steering and Steering Column

Steering assists rely heavily on electronic feedback modules, and rare communication failures can temporarily restrict steering maneuverability.

**Power Steering Loss**
- **Symptoms:** Power steering system unexpectedly loses communication, causing the steering wheel to stiffen or lock up completely while driving.
- **Solution:** Inspect power steering control modules, wiring harnesses, and software calibration.

## Electrical

### Body Electrical and Infotainment

The extensive electrical architecture supports numerous screens and driver-assist features, but complex software integration has resulted in intermittent glitches and warning lights.

**Instrument Cluster and Control Panel Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Gauge cluster, heads-up display, or control panels fail to activate or display error prompts such as pushing the start button while slowing down.
- **Solution:** Software updates or module replacements performed by an authorized dealership.

## Exterior

### Glass and Sunroof

Exterior panels and trim elements generally fit well, though specific recall campaigns highlight potential vulnerabilities with exterior window trim attachment.

**Panoramic Sunroof and Trim Detachment**
- **Symptoms:** Panoramic glass panels shattering unexpectedly while parked or exterior trim and quarter window components loosening.
- **Solution:** Inspection and repair through applicable manufacturer recall campaigns and glass replacement.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V545000: Structure:Body:Roof And Pillars](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Jeep&model=Wagoneer&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** The upper B-pillar interior trim may not be fully seated and could interfere with the side curtain air bag (SABIC) deployment. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 226, "Ejection Mitigation." A compromised deployment of the SABIC can reduce occupant protection in the event of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and reseat or replace the trim pieces as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V577000: Back Over Prevention:Display Function](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Jeep&model=Wagoneer&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 111, "Rear Visibility." A rearview image that does not display while in reverse decreases the driver's visibility of the rear view, increasing the risk of injury or crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the Central Vision Park Assist Module software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V716000: Seat Belts: Rear/Other:Buckle Assembly](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Jeep&model=Wagoneer&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** The third-row seat belts may not allow occupants to properly access or fasten the seat belt to the buckle. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard numbers 208, "Occupant Crash Protection" and 209, "Seat Belt Assemblies." An unfastened seat belt may increase the risk of injury to occupants during.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the third-row seat belt buckle, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V199000: Air Bags:Frontal:Driver Clockspring/Spiral Cassette](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Jeep&model=Wagoneer&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** The steering column control module may not be welded properly, which can prevent the driver's air bag from deploying in a crash. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 208, "Occupant Crash Protection." An air bag that does not deploy in a crash can increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the steering column control module, as necessary free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V436000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Jeep&model=Wagoneer&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 111, "Rear Visibility." A rearview camera image that does not display decreases the driver's visibility, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the radio software or the software will be OTA, "Over-The-Air," free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V593000: Structure:Exterior Trim](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Jeep&model=Wagoneer&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** The trim on the driver and passenger doors may not have been installed properly, which can allow it to detach. Detached trim pieces can create a road hazard for other vehicles, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the door trim, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V642000: Structure:Exterior Trim](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Jeep&model=Wagoneer&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** The quarter trim on the driver and passenger windows may not have been properly secured, which can allow it to detach. Detached trim pieces can create a road hazard for other vehicles, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the quarter window glass assembly, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V413000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission:Gear Position Indication (Prndl)](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Jeep&model=Wagoneer&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** A software error may cause the transmission gear selection indicator not to display. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 102, "Transmission shift lever sequence, starter interlock, and transmission braking effect." A missing gear selection indicator may lead to unintended vehicle.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the shifter module software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2023 Jeep Wagoneer presents an impressive mix of luxury, passenger space, and capability, but used buyers should proceed with caution. While many owners enjoy trouble-free driving, a cluster of complex electrical, stalling, and steering complaints warrants a thorough pre-purchase inspection. Always verify that all open safety recalls have been fully addressed before finalizing a purchase.
