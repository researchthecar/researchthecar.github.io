The 2024 Jeep Wagoneer is a full-size SUV offering a blend of luxury, capability, and spaciousness. Engine options include a 3.0L Hurricane Twin Turbo I6 engine that produces 420 horsepower and 486 lb-ft of torque, and a 3.0L Hurricane Twin Turbo 510 I6 engine that produces 510 horsepower and 500 lb-ft of torque. Both engines are paired with an 8-speed automatic transmission. Owners often praise the Wagoneer for its comfortable ride, ample passenger and cargo space, and its imposing presence. Criticisms tend to focus on its large size making it hard to maneuver, fuel economy, and some reported electronic glitches.

**3.0L Hurricane Twin Turbo I6 (510 HP)**

**Transmission**

**8-Speed Automatic**

*   **Harsh or Erratic Shifting**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported experiencing rough or unpredictable shifting patterns, particularly when the transmission is cold or during low-speed maneuvers. This can manifest as noticeable clunking or hesitation between gears.
    *   Solution: Dealership inspection, software updates for the transmission control module (TCM), or in some cases, replacement of the transmission.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100-$200+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Air Suspension Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Vehicles equipped with the optional air suspension system may experience issues with the system failing to maintain the correct ride height, causing the vehicle to sag or lean to one side. Error messages related to the air suspension system may also appear on the dashboard.
    *   Solution: Inspect the air compressor, air struts, and air lines for leaks or damage. Repair or replace faulty components. Recalibrate the air suspension system.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Uconnect System Glitches**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The Uconnect infotainment system may experience intermittent freezing, lag, or unresponsive touch screen inputs. Connectivity issues with Apple CarPlay or Android Auto have also been reported.
    *   Solution: Software updates from the dealer. In some cases, a full system reset or replacement of the Uconnect module may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 (software update) - $1,000+ (module replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found
*   **Sensor Malfunctions**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Issues with the various sensors in the vehicle such as the blind spot monitoring system, parking sensors, or lane keep assist system can occur. This can lead to false alerts or the systems failing to function properly.
    *   Solution: Diagnostic testing to identify the faulty sensor. Replacement of the malfunctioning sensor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **No Issues Found**

**Interior**

*   **Rattling Noises**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported experiencing rattling or squeaking noises emanating from the dashboard, door panels, or rear cargo area. These noises can be distracting and difficult to pinpoint.
    *   Solution: Tighten loose trim panels and fasteners. Apply felt tape or foam padding to areas where components may be rubbing against each other.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2024 Jeep Wagoneer, while generally well-received, has some common issues. The most frequently reported issues involve the Uconnect infotainment system and the air suspension. Electrical glitches and interior rattles are also reported. While software updates can address some electrical issues, more serious problems may require component replacement, potentially leading to higher repair costs. Owners should pay close attention to the performance of the infotainment system and air suspension during ownership.
