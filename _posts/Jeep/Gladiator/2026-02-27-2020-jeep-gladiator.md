# 2020 Jeep Gladiator Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2020 Jeep Gladiator brings midsize truck utility with open-air Wrangler capability, but used buyers should review its mechanical and electrical history carefully. While many owners enjoy its distinct design and off-road prowess, early model-year production models have accumulated notable owner complaints concerning electrical glitches, steering concerns, and drivetrain behavior.

---

## Engine

### 3.6L Pentastar V6

The 3.6-liter Pentastar V6 produces 285 horsepower and 260 lb-ft of torque, serving as the standard gasoline engine for this model year. It generally provides dependable daily driving power, though isolated owner reports note misfires and electrical sensor faults.

**Engine Misfire and Check Engine Lights**
- **Symptoms:** Illuminated check engine lights accompanied by engine misfires and rough running conditions.
- **Solution:** Diagnose cylinder fault codes, inspect spark plugs, ignition coils, and valvetrain components, and replace failed parts as necessary.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

The ZF-sourced 8-speed automatic transmission offers smooth shifts and well-matched gear ratios for towing and off-roading. However, some owners have experienced electronic engagement anomalies and harsh shifting.

**Shifting Delays and Power Train Faults**
- **Symptoms:** Start-stop system unavailability, followed by check engine lights and erratic gear selection where the transmission may stay locked in lower gears.
- **Solution:** Perform a diagnostic scan for transmission control module codes, update software where applicable, or repair internal hydraulic components.

### 6-Speed Manual Transmission

The 6-speed manual transmission gives drivers direct control but has been associated with specific clutch and hydraulic component durability complaints.

**Clutch System and Master Cylinder Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Clutch slippage, soft pedal feel, or complete inability to disengage gears due to hydraulic or mechanical failure.
- **Solution:** Inspect the clutch master cylinder, slave cylinder, and clutch disc assembly, replacing defective components as required.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

The braking system generally provides adequate stopping power for a midsize truck, though sensor calibration and hardware mounting issues have caused isolated incidents.

**Brake Caliper or Sensor Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** Unintended brake engagement triggered by collision avoidance sensors, or unusual scraping sounds from loose brake hardware.
- **Solution:** Inspect wheel speed sensors, brake pad mounting brackets, and caliper assemblies, securing or replacing damaged hardware.

## Steering

### Steering Gear Box and Linkage

Solid front axle steering setups require precise alignment and healthy components to track straight, and early models can develop loose steering response over time.

**Excessive Play and Highway Wander**
- **Symptoms:** Loose steering feel requiring frequent steering wheel corrections at highway speeds, commonly referred to by owners as 'wandering' or 'death wobble'.
- **Solution:** Inspect the steering box, track bar, and ball joints for excessive play; adjust or replace worn steering components.

## Electrical

### Instrument Cluster and Infotainment

Electrical architecture controls most cabin and gauge functions, with intermittent glitching and screen failures appearing as notable owner pain points.

**Blank Dashboard or Glitching Instrument Panel**
- **Symptoms:** Digital dash screens or instrument clusters turning completely blank while driving, causing a loss of speed, fuel level, and warning displays.
- **Solution:** Inspect cluster wiring, check for CAN bus communication errors, and replace faulty instrument cluster modules.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V636000: Power Train:Driveline:Driveshaft](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Jeep&model=Gladiator&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** If the driveshaft completely separates from the vehicle it may become a road hazard. Either of these scenarios can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** FCA US will notify owners, and dealers will replace the driveshaft, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V580000: Seat Belts](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Jeep&model=Gladiator&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** In the event of a crash, a front seat belt retractor may not properly slow the occupant's forward movement after locking. In the event of a crash, if the front seat belts fail to lock, there is an increased risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will replace the front seat belt retractors, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

When shopping for a used model, verify that all open safety recalls have been addressed and thoroughly test drive the vehicle to check for steering play or electrical screen glitches. A pre-purchase inspection by a knowledgeable mechanic is strongly recommended to assess the condition of the steering linkage, transmission, and electrical systems.
