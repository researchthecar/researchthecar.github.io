# 2007 Jeep Commander

### Overview
The 2007 Jeep Commander, a mid-size SUV, offered a blend of rugged styling and practicality. However, like many vehicles of its era, it has specific areas where reliability can be a concern. Owners have reported issues primarily related to the powertrain, particularly with certain engine and transmission configurations. Electrical systems and interior components also present some common complaints that can affect the overall ownership experience. While the Commander can be a capable vehicle, potential buyers should be aware of these recurring problems and factor them into their purchasing decisions and ongoing maintenance plans.

---


## Engine

### 3.7L V6
**Intake Manifold Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Coolant leaks, overheating, rough idle, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the intake manifold.
- **Cost:** $400 - $700

**Oil Cooler Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Coolant in oil, milky oil, overheating.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the oil cooler and potentially flushing the cooling system.
- **Cost:** $500 - $900


### 4.7L V8
**Head Gasket Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Overheating, coolant loss, white smoke from exhaust, milky oil.
- **Solution:** Replacement of head gaskets and associated components.
- **Cost:** $1500 - $2500

**Camshaft Position Sensor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Stalling, rough idle, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the camshaft position sensor.
- **Cost:** $150 - $300


### 5.7L V8 HEMI
**Cylinder Deactivation (MDS) Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Rough idle, misfires, check engine light, potential for valve spring issues.
- **Solution:** Can range from software updates to replacement of valve springs or MDS solenoids.
- **Cost:** $300 - $1200


## Transmission

### 5-Speed Automatic (W5A580)
**Harsh Shifting / Slipping**
- **Symptoms:** Delayed engagement, rough shifts between gears, slipping.
- **Solution:** Transmission fluid and filter change, solenoid replacement, or full transmission rebuild.
- **Cost:** $300 - $2500

**Torque Converter Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Shuddering at low speeds, slipping, noise.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the torque converter.
- **Cost:** $700 - $1500


## Suspension

### Standard Chassis
**Worn Ball Joints and Control Arms**
- **Symptoms:** Clunking noises over bumps, uneven tire wear, steering wander.
- **Solution:** Replacement of ball joints and control arms.
- **Cost:** $400 - $800


## Electrical

### Body Control Module (BCM)
**Intermittent Electrical Failures**
- **Symptoms:** Various warning lights, power window issues, radio problems, keyless entry malfunctions.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and potential reprogramming or replacement of the BCM.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1000


### Alternator
**Premature Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Dimming lights, battery warning light, dead battery.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the alternator.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600


## Exterior

### Body Panels
**Rust on Fenders and Rocker Panels**
- **Symptoms:** Visible rust bubbles or holes, particularly in areas with harsh winters.
- **Solution:** Rust repair and repainting, or replacement of body panels.
- **Cost:** $300 - $1000+


### Liftgate Struts
**Weak or Failed Liftgate Struts**
- **Symptoms:** Liftgate not staying open, difficulty lifting.
- **Solution:** Replacement of liftgate struts.
- **Cost:** $100 - $250


## Interior

### HVAC Blower Motor
**Blower Motor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** No air coming from vents or intermittent airflow.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the HVAC blower motor.
- **Cost:** $200 - $400


### Seat Heaters
**Malfunctioning Seat Heaters**
- **Symptoms:** One or both seat heaters not working.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of heating elements or switches.
- **Cost:** $200 - $500


--- 
### Summary Verdict
The 2007 Jeep Commander presents a mixed reliability profile. While its robust design and available V8 engines offer capability, potential buyers should be prepared for common issues, particularly with the 4.7L V8 head gaskets and various transmission problems. Electrical gremlins related to the BCM and premature alternator failure are also notable. Exterior rust can be a concern depending on the climate. Despite these drawbacks, addressing these known issues can lead to a more dependable ownership experience. It is recommended to have a pre-purchase inspection focusing on these specific problem areas.
