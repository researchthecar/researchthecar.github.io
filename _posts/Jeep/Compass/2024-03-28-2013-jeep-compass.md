**Introduction to the 2013 Jeep Compass**

The 2013 Jeep Compass is a compact crossover SUV that offers a blend of ruggedness and comfort. It is available with three engine options: a 2.0L World gasoline engine, a 2.4L Tigershark MultiAir2 gasoline engine, and a 2.0L VW TDI diesel engine. Transmission options include a 5-speed manual, a 6-speed automatic, and a CVT. The Compass received mixed reviews at the time of its release, with critics praising its off-road capabilities but criticizing some of its interior features and fuel economy.

**Engine**

**2.0L World Gasoline Engine**
* **Intake Manifold Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: The intake manifold can crack, causing a loss of engine power and increased exhaust emissions.
    * Solution: Replace the intake manifold.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Fuel Injector Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The fuel injectors can fail, causing a loss of engine power and increased fuel consumption.
    * Solution: Replace the fuel injectors.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+ per injector
    * Recalls: None Found

**2.4L Tigershark MultiAir2 Gasoline Engine**
* **Timing Chain Tensioner Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The timing chain tensioner can fail, causing a loss of engine power and a rattling noise.
    * Solution: Replace the timing chain tensioner.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,200+
    * Recalls: None Found

**2.0L VW TDI Diesel Engine**
* **Exhaust Gas Recirculation (EGR) Valve Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The EGR valve can fail, causing a loss of engine power and increased emissions.
    * Solution: Replace the EGR valve.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $700+
    * Recalls: N/A

**Transmission**

**5-Speed Manual**
* **Synchronizer Wear**
    * Description/Symptoms: The synchronizers in the transmission can wear out, making it difficult to shift gears.
    * Solution: Replace the synchronizers.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $600 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**6-Speed Automatic**
* **Torque Converter Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The torque converter can fail, causing a loss of power and transmission slipping.
    * Solution: Replace the torque converter.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,200 - $1,800+
    * Recalls: None Found

**CVT**
* **Belt Wear**
    * Description/Symptoms: The belt in the CVT can wear out, causing transmission slipping and loss of power.
    * Solution: Replace the belt.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,200+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Strut Bearing Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The strut bearings can fail, causing a rattling noise and reduced handling.
    * Solution: Replace the strut bearings.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400 per strut
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain**
    * Description/Symptoms: The battery can drain quickly, even when the vehicle is turned off.
    * Solution: Check for parasitic electrical draws and repair/replace faulty components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the cause
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Plastic Trim Fading**
    * Description/Symptoms: The plastic trim on the exterior of the vehicle can fade over time due to UV exposure.
    * Solution: Replace or repaint the faded trim.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $400+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **HVAC System Malfunction**
    * Description/Symptoms: The HVAC system can malfunction, leading to problems with heating, cooling, or ventilation.
    * Solution: Diagnose and repair the specific HVAC component that is causing the issue.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Plastic Interior Parts Squeaking**
    * Description/Symptoms: Various plastic interior parts, such as the dashboard or door panels, can develop squeaks or rattles over time.
    * Solution: Identify the source of the noise and apply lubrication or replace the affected parts.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2013 Jeep Compass has a number of potential issues that can affect its engine, transmission, suspension, electrical, and interior systems. The severity and cost of these issues can vary depending on the specific problem and the affected component. Overall, the Compass is a vehicle with a mixed reputation, with some owners experiencing significant issues while others report a positive ownership experience.
