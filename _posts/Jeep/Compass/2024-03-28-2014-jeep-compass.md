## 2014 Jeep Compass

The 2014 Jeep Compass is a compact SUV that offers a combination of ruggedness and refinement. It is powered by a 2.0-liter or 2.4-liter gasoline engine and comes with either a six-speed manual or a six-speed automatic transmission. The Compass is available in both front-wheel drive and all-wheel drive configurations.

Despite its rugged exterior, the Compass offers a comfortable and well-equipped interior. Some of the key features include a spacious cabin, a large cargo area, and a user-friendly infotainment system. However, the Compass has received some criticism for its lack of power and its poor fuel economy.

**Engine**

**2.0-liter Engine**
* **Timing Chain Failure:** The timing chain may stretch or break, causing the engine to lose power or stall. The symptoms include a rattling noise from the engine, a loss of power, and an illuminated check engine light. The solution is to replace the timing chain and related components. The estimated cost to fix is $1,500 - $2,500+
* **Intake Manifold Valve Issues:** The intake manifold valves may become stuck or clogged, causing the engine to run rough or hesitate. The symptoms include a loss of power, a rough idle, and an illuminated check engine light. The solution is to clean or replace the intake manifold valves. The estimated cost to fix is $500 - $1,000.
* **Oil Consumption:** The 2.0-liter engine may consume excessive amounts of oil. The symptoms include a low oil level on the dipstick and an illuminated low oil pressure warning light. The solution is to replace the piston rings and valve seals. The estimated cost to fix is $1,000 - $2,000+

* **No Issues Found**

**Transmission**

**Six-Speed Manual Transmission**
* **Clutch Failure:** The clutch may wear out prematurely or fail completely. The symptoms include difficulty shifting gears, a slipping clutch, and an inability to engage gears. The solution is to replace the clutch and related components. The estimated cost to fix is $1,000 - $2,000+
* **Transmission Noise:** The transmission may make noises such as whining, grinding, or growling. The symptoms include noise coming from the transmission, a difficulty shifting gears, and an illuminated check engine light. The solution is to replace the transmission. The estimated cost to fix is $2,500 - $4,000+
* **Recalls:** NHTSA Campaign Number: 19V236000

**Six-Speed Automatic Transmission**
* **Shifting Problems:** The transmission may shift gears roughly or hesitate to shift. The symptoms include a delayed or jerky shift, a loss of power, and an illuminated check engine light. The solution is to replace the transmission solenoids or the transmission control module. The estimated cost to fix is $500 - $1,500.
* **Transmission Failure:** The transmission may fail completely. The symptoms include a loss of power, a refusal to shift gears, and an illuminated check engine light. The solution is to replace the transmission. The estimated cost to fix is $2,500 - $4,000+

**Suspension**
* **Front Strut Failure:** The front struts may fail, causing the vehicle to ride rough and handle poorly. The symptoms include a clunking noise from the front of the vehicle, a loss of stability, and an inability to align the vehicle. The solution is to replace the front struts. The estimated cost to fix is $500 - $1,000.
* **Rear Leaf Spring Failure:** The rear leaf springs may break or sag, causing the vehicle to ride low in the rear and handle poorly. The symptoms include a sagging rear end, a loss of stability, and an inability to align the vehicle. The solution is to replace the rear leaf springs. The estimated cost to fix is $500 - $1,000.

**Electrical**
* **Battery Drain:** The battery may drain prematurely. The symptoms include a dead battery, a slow engine crank, and an illuminated check engine light. The solution is to identify and fix the source of the battery drain. The estimated cost to fix varies depending on the source of the drain.
* **Electrical System Failure:** The electrical system may fail, causing the vehicle to lose power or experience other electrical problems. The symptoms include a loss of power, a refusal to start, and an illuminated check engine light. The solution is to identify and fix the source of the electrical system failure. The estimated cost to fix varies depending on the source of the failure.

**Exterior**
* **Sunroof Leaks:** The sunroof may leak water into the cabin. The symptoms include water dripping from the sunroof, a musty smell in the cabin, and an illuminated check engine light. The solution is to replace the sunroof seal or the sunroof drain tubes. The estimated cost to fix is $500 - $1,000.
* **Liftgate Failure:** The liftgate may fail to open or close properly. The symptoms include a difficulty opening or closing the liftgate, a rattling noise from the liftgate, and an illuminated check engine light. The solution is to replace the liftgate struts or the liftgate latch. The estimated cost to fix is $500 - $1,000.

**Interior**
* **HVAC System Failure:** The HVAC system may fail to heat or cool the cabin. The symptoms include a lack of heat or air conditioning, a noisy HVAC system, and an illuminated check engine light. The solution is to replace the HVAC blower motor or the HVAC control module. The estimated cost to fix is $500 - $1,000.
* **Dashboard Rattle:** The dashboard may rattle or make noise. The symptoms include a rattling or buzzing noise from the dashboard, a loose dashboard, and an illuminated check engine light. The solution is to identify and fix the source of the dashboard rattle. The estimated cost to fix varies depending on the source of the rattle.

**Summary**

The 2014 Jeep Compass is a reliable and capable SUV that offers a lot of value for the money. However, it is not without its problems. The most common issues include timing chain failure, intake manifold valve issues, and transmission problems. The average cost to fix these issues is between $1,000 and $2,000. Overall, the Compass is a good choice for buyers who are looking for a rugged and affordable SUV that can handle both on- and off-road driving.
