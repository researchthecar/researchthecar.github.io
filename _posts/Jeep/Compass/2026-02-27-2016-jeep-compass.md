# 2016 Jeep Compass Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2016 Jeep Compass is a compact crossover SUV that offers an accessible entry point into the brand's rugged styling, but it unfortunately carries a history of notable mechanical and electrical complaints. Buyers looking at the used market should approach this model year with caution, as numerous owner reports highlight recurring powertrain stumbles and unexpected component failures.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L 4-Cylinder Engine

The base 2.0-liter four-cylinder engine produces 158 horsepower and 141 lb-ft of torque, typically paired with front-wheel drive. While adequate for basic daily commuting, this engine option is frequently tied to owner reports of sudden stalling and electrical sensor issues.

**Engine Stalling and Crankshaft Sensor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicles experience unexpected engine stalling while driving or coming to a stop, often accompanied by dashboard warning lights or a failure to turn over due to faulty crankshaft position sensors.
- **Solution:** Diagnose fault codes, replace failing crankshaft or camshaft position sensors, and inspect related electrical wiring harnesses.

### 2.4L 4-Cylinder Engine

The optional 2.4-liter four-cylinder engine generates 172 horsepower and 165 lb-ft of torque, providing slightly better pep for highway driving and compatibility with four-wheel drive systems. Like the smaller engine, it is susceptible to complaints involving unexpected power loss.

**Loss of Motive Power**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may abruptly lose motive power or shut down completely while traveling at various speeds, requiring the driver to pull over and restart after a cooling or waiting period.
- **Solution:** Inspect the powertrain control module, throttle body, and associated electrical relays or sensors.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

A conventional 6-speed automatic transmission is paired with select configurations, offering a more traditional shifting feel compared to older continuously variable setups. It generally receives fewer severe mechanical complaints than earlier CVT units, though proper maintenance history is essential.

### CVT / Power Train

Some variants utilize a continuously variable transmission (CVT) or general power train configurations that have accumulated owner complaints regarding premature failure, slipping, and sudden power loss.

**Transmission Failure and Power Loss**
- **Symptoms:** Unusual noises, severe hesitation, overheating during summer driving, or complete loss of drive capability leading to a stall.
- **Solution:** Thoroughly inspect transmission fluid condition, check for internal mechanical wear, and replace or rebuild the transmission if internal failure occurs.

## Interior

### Active Headrests

The cabin features standard comfort amenities, but active safety components like the reactive headrests have drawn complaints for deploying spontaneously without a rear-end collision.

**Spontaneous Headrest Deployment**
- **Symptoms:** The front passenger or driver headrest unexpectedly pops open or deploys forward with a loud noise, potentially startling the driver or striking occupants in the head.
- **Solution:** Replace the damaged active headrest assembly and internal trigger mechanism.

## Safety

### Seat Belts

Occupant protection systems are vital, making reported seat belt latch failures a critical area to verify during a pre-purchase inspection.

**Random Seat Belt Disengagement**
- **Symptoms:** Front driver or passenger seat belts unlatch or disconnect on their own during normal driving without the release button being pressed.
- **Solution:** Inspect the seat belt buckle assembly and replace faulty latch mechanisms.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V907000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Engine](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Jeep&model=Compass&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** If the engine stalls, there is an increased the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will replace the crankshaft or camshaft sensor connector, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

While the 2016 Jeep Compass presents an affordable option for buyers seeking utility and high seating positions, its elevated volume of complaints regarding engine stalling, transmission concerns, and unexpected component issues calls for thorough vetting. Prospective buyers should insist on a comprehensive mechanical inspection, verify that all safety recalls have been addressed, and test-drive the vehicle carefully to check for smooth power delivery and secure seat belt operation.
