**Introduction to the 2021 Jeep Compass**

The 2021 Jeep Compass is a compact crossover SUV known for its off-road capabilities and versatile interior. It offers a range of engine and transmission options, including the 2.4L Tigershark engine with a 6-speed automatic transmission, a 1.3L turbocharged engine with a 9-speed automatic transmission, and a 2.0L turbocharged engine with a 9-speed automatic transmission. The Compass has received positive reviews for its spacious interior, off-road performance, and value for money. However, some owners have reported various issues with the vehicle.

**Engine**

**2.4L Tigershark Engine**

* **Premature Cylinder Head Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Excessive oil consumption, loss of coolant, overheating, and engine knocking.
    * Solution: Engine rebuild or replacement.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $5,000 - $10,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**1.3L Turbocharged Engine**

* **Turbocharger Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Reduced engine performance, increased oil consumption, and turbocharger noises.
    * Solution: Turbocharger replacement.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Fuel Injector Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Engine misfires, rough idling, and decreased fuel efficiency.
    * Solution: Fuel injector replacement.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600
    * Recalls: None Found

**2.0L Turbocharged Engine**

* **Timing Chain Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Engine noise, loss of power, and engine failure.
    * Solution: Timing chain replacement.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,000
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**6-Speed Automatic Transmission**

* **Torque Converter Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Shuddering, slipping, and transmission overheating.
    * Solution: Torque converter replacement.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000
    * Recalls: None Found

**9-Speed Automatic Transmission**

* **Transmission Fluid Leak:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Fluid leaking from the transmission pan or lines.
    * Solution: Repair or replacement of leaking parts.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Strut Assembly Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Excessive noise, vibration, and loss of handling.
    * Solution: Strut assembly replacement.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Sway Bar Link Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Clunking noises, especially when turning.
    * Solution: Sway bar link replacement.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Dead battery after short periods of inactivity.
    * Solution: Battery replacement or diagnosis of any parasitic draws.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Electrical Harness Issues:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Malfunctioning electrical components, such as lights, gauges, or sensors.
    * Solution: Wiring harness repair or replacement.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,000
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Cracked or Leaking Taillights:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Moisture or water entering the taillight assembly.
    * Solution: Taillight replacement.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Loose or Rattling Side Mirrors:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Excessive vibration or noise from the side mirrors.
    * Solution: Tightening or replacing the side mirrors.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Sunroof Issues:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Creaking, rattling, or leaking sunroof.
    * Solution: Repair or replacement of sunroof components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Dashboard Rattle:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Excessive noise coming from the dashboard area.
    * Solution: Identifying and fixing the source of the rattle.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2021 Jeep Compass has received mixed reviews from owners. While it offers a spacious interior, impressive off-road capabilities, and a wide range of engine options, it has also been plagued by various issues. Major engine and transmission problems, such as premature cylinder head failure, turbocharger failure, and torque converter failure, are among the most significant concerns. Other issues include suspension, electrical, exterior, and interior problems, ranging from strut assembly failure to dashboard rattles.

While some issues can be relatively minor and inexpensive to fix, others can be serious and costly to repair. It is important for potential buyers to be aware of these potential problems and consider the cost of ownership before purchasing a 2021 Jeep Compass.
