# 2017 Jeep Compass Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used compact SUV, the Jeep Compass offers an appealing combination of iconic styling and accessible pricing. However, prospective buyers should review common owner-reported complaints closely. While many owners enjoy daily driving, specific recurring issues involving engine stalling, electrical glitches, and erratic parking brake engagement warrant careful inspection during a pre-purchase test drive.

---

## Engine

### 2.4L Tigershark 4-Cylinder

The 2.4-liter Tigershark four-cylinder engine produces approximately 180 horsepower and 175 lb-ft of torque, providing adequate power for daily commuting. Buyers should verify maintenance records and pay close attention to idle smoothness during a test drive.

**Engine Stalling and Power Loss**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicles may experience unexpected engine stalling while driving or stopping, frequently accompanied by loss of motive power and illumination of dashboard warning lights.
- **Solution:** Diagnostic scanning by a qualified mechanic is required to check for fault codes, software updates, or failing sensors.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

The conventional six-speed automatic transmission is paired with front-wheel drive or all-wheel drive configurations on select trims. It generally offers smooth gear changes, though proper fluid maintenance is critical for long-term durability.

### 9-Speed Automatic Transmission

The nine-speed automatic transmission aims to optimize highway fuel efficiency on select models. Buyers should thoroughly test acceleration response across various speed ranges to ensure smooth operation.

**Transmission Shifting and Acceleration Irregularities**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may notice hesitation, refusal to accelerate at highway speeds, or rough shifting behavior.
- **Solution:** Have a professional technician inspect transmission fluid condition, update powertrain control modules, or perform adaptation resets.

## Brakes

### Electronic Parking Brake

The braking system provides reliable stopping power under normal conditions, though electronic parking brake anomalies require immediate professional attention if experienced.

**Unintended Parking Brake Engagement**
- **Symptoms:** The emergency parking brake may engage unexpectedly while driving, causing sudden vehicle deceleration and potential loss of momentum.
- **Solution:** Inspect parking brake switches, wiring harnesses, and control modules for faults or software glitches.

## Electrical

### Uconnect Infotainment System

Electrical components govern key convenience features, including infotainment and instrument cluster displays. Checking all interior electronics during an inspection helps ensure seamless operation.

**Screen Delamination and Ghost Presses**
- **Symptoms:** Bubbles forming beneath the touchscreen can cause unprompted inputs, randomly turning on climate controls, heated seats, or placing unintended phone calls.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the touchscreen display assembly is typically required to resolve persistent delamination.

## Safety

### Seat Belts and Active Headrests

Safety restraint systems are vital for occupant protection. Ensuring that seat belts latch securely and headrests show no signs of prior accidental deployment is an important step when evaluating a used model.

**Unprompted Deployment or Unlatching**
- **Symptoms:** Front passenger active headrests may deploy unexpectedly while parked, or driver-side seat belts may unlclip during operation.
- **Solution:** Have a certified repair facility inspect seat belt buckles and active headrest mechanisms for internal component failures.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V740000: Electrical System: Instrument Cluster/Panel](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Jeep&model=Compass&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 101, "Controls and Displays." An instrument cluster that is too bright, and cannot be dimmed, can reduce the driver's ability to see at night, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will update IPC software, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V824000: Equipment](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Jeep&model=Compass&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** Chrysler (FCA US LLC) is recalling various Dodge, Chrysler, and RAM vehicles equipped with Kidde Plastic-Handle or Push Button 'Pindicator' Fire Extinguishers. These extinguishers may become clogged, preventing the extinguisher from discharging as expected or requiring excessive force to activate the extinguisher.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 17V301000: Power Train:Axle Assembly:Axle Shaft](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Jeep&model=Compass&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** The left front halfshaft may not be properly seated, and, as a result, it may disengage from the transmission and/or break. Additionally, if the vehicle is parked without using the parking brake, the vehicle may roll, despite being in 'Park.' Either condition may increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, dealers will inspect the left front halfshaft for engagement and replace any that are not properly engaged in the transmission, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V433000: Equipment:Other:Owners/Service/Other Manual](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Jeep&model=Compass&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** If a customer follows the User's Guide, they may hook up the battery jumper cables incorrectly, increasing their risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners and will replace the User's Guides with a corrected version, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

When evaluating a used model, conducting a thorough pre-purchase inspection focused on engine idle quality, transmission shift responsiveness, and electronics functionality is strongly advised. While NHTSA complaints highlight potential areas of concern such as unexpected stalling and touchscreen glitches, a well-maintained example can still serve as a practical daily driver.
