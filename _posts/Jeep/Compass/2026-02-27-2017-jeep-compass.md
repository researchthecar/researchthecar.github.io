# 2017 Jeep Compass

### Overview
The 2017 Jeep Compass, while offering a capable platform for its class, exhibits a mixed reliability profile. Owners have reported a range of issues, particularly concerning the powertrain and certain electrical components. The vehicle's design, which predates some of the more modern FCA platforms, may contribute to some of these recurring problems. While many owners experience satisfactory performance, potential buyers should be aware of common complaints to make an informed decision. Maintenance and timely repairs are crucial for mitigating some of the less severe issues and ensuring longevity.

---


## Engine

### 2.0L Tigershark (MultiAir) I4
**Oil Consumption**
- **Symptoms:** Excessive oil burning, requiring frequent top-offs. May be accompanied by blue smoke from the exhaust.
- **Solution:** Engine internal inspection, potential piston ring replacement or valve seal repair.
- **Cost:** $1,200 - $2,500

**Stalling/Rough Idling**
- **Symptoms:** Engine may stall unexpectedly or run roughly at idle, often related to MultiAir system issues.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and potential replacement of MultiAir solenoid, oil filter, or related sensors.
- **Cost:** $300 - $800


### 2.4L Tigershark (MultiAir) I4
**Oil Consumption**
- **Symptoms:** Similar to the 2.0L, this engine can suffer from excessive oil consumption.

- **Cost:** $1,200 - $2,500

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic (6F24)
**Harsh Shifting/Hesitation**
- **Symptoms:** Transmission may exhibit rough shifts, delayed engagement, or hesitation during acceleration.
- **Solution:** Transmission fluid flush and filter replacement, potential software update or internal transmission repair.
- **Cost:** $400 - $1,500

**Overheating**
- **Symptoms:** Transmission fluid temperature can rise excessively, leading to performance issues or failure.
- **Solution:** Inspection of transmission cooler, fluid levels, and potential repair or replacement of cooling system components.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1,200


### CVT (Continuously Variable Transmission)
**Jerking/Shuddering**
- **Symptoms:** The CVT can experience jerky movements or shuddering, especially at lower speeds.
- **Solution:** CVT fluid change, software update, or potential replacement of CVT unit.
- **Cost:** $1,500 - $4,000

**Failure to Engage**
- **Symptoms:** Transmission may fail to engage gears properly or slip.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and potential replacement of CVT unit.
- **Cost:** $3,000 - $5,000

## Electrical

### Infotainment System
**Freezing/Unresponsiveness**
- **Symptoms:** Touchscreen may freeze, become unresponsive, or reboot randomly.
- **Solution:** Software update, or replacement of the infotainment unit.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1,200


### Battery Drain
**Premature Battery Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Battery may drain quickly, leading to starting issues. Can be caused by parasitic draw.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis of parasitic draw, repair of faulty modules or wiring, or battery replacement.
- **Cost:** $200 - $600

### Summary Verdict
The 2017 Jeep Compass presents a mixed reliability picture. The most frequently cited concerns revolve around the Tigershark engines' tendency for oil consumption and potential stalling issues, as well as problems with both the automatic and CVT transmissions, including harsh shifting and overheating. Electrical gremlins, particularly with the infotainment system and battery drain, are also noted. While the suspension, exterior, and interior components generally fare better, the powertrain and electrical systems warrant careful consideration. Prospective buyers should prioritize vehicles with a well-documented maintenance history and be prepared for potential repairs related to these common issues.
