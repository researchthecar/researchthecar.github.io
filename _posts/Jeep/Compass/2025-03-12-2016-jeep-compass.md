The 2016 Jeep Compass is a compact SUV that offered a mix of affordability and off-road capability, appealing to budget-conscious buyers and those wanting a Jeep. It came standard with a 2.0-liter inline-4 engine producing 158 horsepower and 141 lb-ft of torque. An optional 2.4-liter inline-4 engine, generating 172 horsepower and 165 lb-ft of torque, was also available. Transmission options included a 5-speed manual, a 6-speed automatic (available only with the 2.4L engine), and a continuously variable transmission (CVT). Owners often praised its fuel efficiency and maneuverability, while common complaints included lackluster performance, a dated interior, and reliability concerns.

**Engine**

**2.0L Inline-4**

*   **Engine Misfires**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rough idling, hesitation during acceleration, check engine light illuminated. Often caused by faulty ignition coils or spark plugs.
    *   Solution: Replacing the faulty ignition coils and/or spark plugs.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $400+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Catalytic Converter Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Check engine light, reduced fuel economy, poor acceleration, rotten egg smell.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the catalytic converter.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**2.4L Inline-4**

*   **Excessive Oil Consumption**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Low oil level, blue smoke from exhaust, engine ticking or knocking sounds.
    *   Solution: In severe cases, engine rebuild or replacement might be necessary. Other solutions would include piston ring replacement or valve stem seal replacement
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $5000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Purge Valve Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Check engine light, difficulty starting, rough idle, poor fuel economy.
    *   Solution: Replacing the purge valve.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)**

*   **Slipping or Jerking**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Hesitation during acceleration, jerky shifting, transmission slipping, difficulty maintaining speed, especially on inclines.
    *   Solution: Transmission fluid flush and replacement, valve body repair or replacement, or in severe cases, complete CVT replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $4000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Overheating**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Warning light, transmission slipping, erratic shifting, complete transmission failure.
    *   Solution: Check and repair/replace the transmission cooler lines or cooler, replace transmission fluid, or replace transmission.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $4000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**6-Speed Automatic Transmission**

*   **Rough Shifting**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Harsh or delayed shifting, particularly between gears.
    *   Solution: Transmission fluid flush and replacement or valve body repair/replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Premature Wear of Suspension Components**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Clunking noises from suspension, uneven tire wear, poor handling.
    *   Solution: Replacement of worn ball joints, tie rod ends, struts/shocks, or control arm bushings.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1000+ per component.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **TIPM (Totally Integrated Power Module) Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Intermittent starting problems, stalling, malfunctioning fuel pump, erratic electrical behavior (lights, wipers, etc.).
    *   Solution: Replacement of the TIPM or repair if possible.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Power Window Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Windows not operating, intermittent operation, grinding noises.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the power window motor or regulator.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Rust Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rust forming along rocker panels, wheel wells, and other body panels, especially in areas with road salt exposure.
    *   Solution: Rust repair, body panel replacement, and rust prevention treatments.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $2000+ depending on the severity.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Sun Visor Sagging/Breaking**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Sun visor falling down, difficult to keep in position, visor breaking at the hinge.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the sun visor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $200+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2016 Jeep Compass has a number of known issues, affecting the engine, transmission, suspension, electrical system, exterior, and interior. Engine issues such as excessive oil consumption and catalytic converter failure can be costly to repair, while the CVT transmission is prone to problems that may lead to expensive repairs or replacements. TIPM failures can cause various electrical problems that may result in significant costs. Although some repairs are relatively inexpensive, others may be quite costly, making it crucial to carefully inspect a used 2016 Jeep Compass and address any issues.
