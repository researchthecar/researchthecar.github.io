# 2020 Jeep Compass Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2020 Jeep Compass offers distinct styling and capable handling, but prospective buyers should approach with caution due to notable owner-reported reliability trends. Common problem areas include powertrain electrical interactions, engine stalling, and infotainment glitches. Reviewing these recurring patterns helps ensure you choose a well-maintained example or budget for potential post-purchase repairs.

---

## Engine

### 2.4L Tigershark 4-Cylinder

The 2.4-liter naturally aspirated 4-cylinder engine produces 180 horsepower and 175 lb-ft of torque, providing adequate power for daily commuting. While generally serviceable, this engine option is linked in owner complaints to sporadic stalling incidents and head gasket concerns.

**Engine Stalling and Coolant Warning**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicles may experience sudden engine shut-offs or power loss while driving, frequently accompanied by a 'Coolant Temperature Too High' warning on the dashboard even when normal operating temperatures are observed.
- **Solution:** Requires professional diagnostic testing of the cooling system, sensors, and Powertrain Control Module (PCM) to isolate thermal or electrical faults.

**Head Gasket Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Coolant contamination or leakage around the cylinder head gasket, potentially leading to overheating and engine performance issues.
- **Solution:** Inspection and potential replacement of the head gasket and associated cylinder components by a qualified technician.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Manual / 9-Speed Automatic

Depending on the drivetrain configuration, the Compass utilizes either a 6-speed manual or a 9-speed automatic transmission. Owners have reported occasional shifting roughness and cooling system vulnerabilities that warrant a thorough pre-purchase inspection.

**Transmission Cooling and Powertrain Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Dash lights for service engine and service transmission illuminate, potentially followed by sudden transmission cooling system failure and complete mechanical breakdown.
- **Solution:** Inspection of transmission fluid lines, cooling components, and potential mechanical overhaul or replacement.

**Shifting Jerks and Park Engagement Defects**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle jolts forward or backward while shifting during driving, or displays warnings indicating the vehicle is not properly secured in Park after the gear selector is moved.
- **Solution:** Diagnostic inspection of the gear shifter assembly, actuator, and transmission control module software updates or hardware replacement.

## Electrical

### Auto Start-Stop and Battery System

The vehicle's complex electrical architecture supports numerous convenience features, though battery management and start-stop integration are frequent sources of owner complaints.

**Stop/Start System Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Dashboard displays 'Stop/Start Unavailable Service Stop/Start System' warnings, frequently accompanied by premature failure of the main and auxiliary batteries.
- **Solution:** Testing and replacement of both primary and auxiliary batteries, along with diagnostic resets of the start-stop charging system.

### Infotainment and Backup Camera

Safety and driver-aid electronics heavily rely on dashboard display functionality, making screen blackouts or camera distortions a notable inconvenience.

**Distorted Display and Backup Camera Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The infotainment and dashboard screen may freeze, go completely black, or display pixilated and distorted images, causing the rearview backup camera to fail when reversing.
- **Solution:** Software flashing, screen module reset, or replacement of the infotainment display unit and camera wiring harness.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

- **Issue:** Reduced visibility increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will tighten the wiper nuts, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2020 Jeep Compass presents a mixed reliability picture for used car shoppers. While it offers appealing utility, buyers should carefully evaluate vehicle history reports for prior engine stalling, transmission concerns, and electrical battery draws. Securing an extended warranty or obtaining a comprehensive pre-purchase inspection from an independent mechanic is strongly advised before finalizing a purchase.
