# 2021 Jeep Compass

### Overview
The 2021 Jeep Compass, while offering a capable and stylish package, presents a mixed bag in terms of long-term reliability. Owners have reported a number of recurring issues, particularly concerning the powertrain and electrical systems. While the compact SUV generally performs well in everyday driving, specific components have shown a propensity for failure, leading to unexpected repair costs and inconvenience. This analysis focuses on documented technical issues beyond routine maintenance, aiming to provide a clear picture of potential reliability concerns for prospective and current owners of the 2021 model year.

---


## Engine

### 2.4L Tigershark (MultiAir) I4
**Oil Consumption**
- **Symptoms:** Excessive oil burning, requiring frequent top-offs. Can lead to low oil levels and potential engine damage if not monitored.
- **Solution:** Software updates, piston ring replacement, or in severe cases, engine replacement.
- **Cost:** $500 - $2,500

**Cooling System Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Coolant leaks from hoses, water pump, or radiator. Overheating may occur.
- **Solution:** Replacement of leaking components (hoses, water pump, radiator).
- **Cost:** $300 - $800


## Transmission

### 9-Speed Automatic (948TE)
**Harsh Shifting / Jerking**
- **Symptoms:** Erratic gear changes, clunking noises, or hesitation during acceleration and deceleration.
- **Solution:** Transmission fluid flush and refill, software recalibration, or transmission control module replacement.
- **Cost:** $200 - $1,200

**Transmission Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Complete loss of power, inability to engage gears, or grinding noises.
- **Solution:** Transmission rebuild or replacement.
- **Cost:** $2,500 - $5,000+

## Electrical

### Infotainment System
**System Freezing / Lagging**
- **Symptoms:** Touchscreen becomes unresponsive, slow to react, or freezes entirely. Bluetooth connectivity issues.
- **Solution:** Software updates, infotainment unit reset, or replacement of the unit.
- **Cost:** $100 - $1,000


### Battery Drain
**Premature Battery Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Battery drains quickly, leading to starting problems. Can be related to parasitic draw from modules.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis of parasitic draw, replacement of faulty modules or battery.
- **Cost:** $200 - $600

### Summary Verdict
The 2021 Jeep Compass exhibits some notable reliability concerns, primarily centered around its 2.4L Tigershark engine's oil consumption and the 9-speed automatic transmission's shifting behavior and potential for failure. Electrical issues, particularly with the infotainment system and battery drain, are also reported. While the chassis, exterior, and interior components appear to be relatively robust, the powertrain and electrical systems warrant careful consideration. Prospective buyers should be aware of these potential issues and factor in possible repair costs, especially for vehicles outside of warranty. Regular maintenance and prompt attention to any warning signs are crucial for mitigating these risks.
