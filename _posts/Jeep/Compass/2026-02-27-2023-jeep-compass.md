# 2023 Jeep Compass Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2023 Jeep Compass introduces a refreshed interior, updated technology, and a standard turbocharged 2.0-liter four-cylinder engine paired with an 8-speed automatic transmission and all-wheel drive. While the updated powertrain brings modern performance and refinement compared to older model years, used-car buyers should review owner-reported concerns closely. Early owner feedback highlights potential issues related to unexpected electronic braking, steering components, and electrical glitches.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbo Inline-4

The 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine produces approximately 200 horsepower and 221 lb-ft of torque, providing standard all-wheel drive performance across the lineup. This modern powerplant offers improved responsiveness over past iterations, though buyers should verify regular maintenance history.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

An 8-speed automatic transmission comes standard, routing power to all four wheels. It generally delivers smooth gear changes and helps optimize highway efficiency, though periodic software updates may be necessary to maintain optimal shift quality.

## Steering

### Steering Rack and Column

The electric power steering system provides light effort at low speeds and stable feedback on the highway, but isolated failures in the steering rack have been noted by owners.

**Steering Rack Failure and Noise**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have reported experiencing loud clunking noises when turning the steering wheel, along with play or stiffness in the steering mechanism.
- **Solution:** Have a certified mechanic inspect the steering gear assembly; replacement of the steering rack may be necessary if internal wear is detected.

## Safety

### Forward Collision Avoidance

Advanced safety features enhance everyday protection, though sensor calibration sensitivity can occasionally lead to erratic warning alerts or unexpected braking events.

**Unexpected Emergency Braking**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle's advanced safety systems may occasionally trigger unprompted emergency braking or issue false collision warnings while driving.
- **Solution:** Check for dealer software updates related to the collision avoidance module and recalibrate sensor systems if false activations persist.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V576000: Electrical System: Instrument Cluster/Panel](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Jeep&model=Compass&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** The brightness level of the instrument panel cluster (IPC) may not be adjustable due to incorrect software in the body control module (BCM). As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 101, "Control and Displays." An IPC display that is not bright enough may not clearly show critical safety.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the body control module (BCM) software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V436000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Jeep&model=Compass&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 111, "Rear Visibility." A rearview camera image that does not display decreases the driver's visibility, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the radio software or the software will be OTA, "Over-The-Air," free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2023 Jeep Compass benefits from a more powerful standard turbo engine and a smoother automatic transmission than its predecessors. Prospective buyers should perform a comprehensive pre-purchase inspection focusing on the steering system and advanced driver-assistance electronics to ensure trouble-free ownership.
