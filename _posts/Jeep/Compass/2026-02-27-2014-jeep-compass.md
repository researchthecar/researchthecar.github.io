# 2014 Jeep Compass

### Overview
The 2014 Jeep Compass, a compact SUV, offers a blend of practicality and off-road capability, particularly with its Freedom Drive II 4x4 system. It was available with two main engine options: a 2.0L inline-4 and a more powerful 2.4L inline-4. Transmission choices typically included a 5-speed manual or a Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT). While generally considered a decent option for its segment, some model years have reported specific areas of concern that potential buyers should be aware of. This analysis focuses on common technical reliability issues reported for the 2014 model year, excluding routine maintenance and general wear and tear.

---


## Engine

### 2.0L Inline-4
**Oil Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Visible oil spots under the vehicle, low oil level warnings, burning oil smell.
- **Solution:** Replacement of leaking seals or gaskets (e.g., valve cover gasket, oil pan gasket).
- **Cost:** $200 - $600


### 2.4L Inline-4
**Oil Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Visible oil spots under the vehicle, low oil level warnings, burning oil smell.
- **Solution:** Replacement of leaking seals or gaskets (e.g., valve cover gasket, oil pan gasket).
- **Cost:** $200 - $600

**Variable Valve Timing (VVT) Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Rough idle, engine knocking or ticking noises, check engine light illumination.
- **Solution:** Replacement of VVT solenoid or phaser.
- **Cost:** $300 - $700


## Transmission

### 5-Speed Manual
**Clutch Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Slipping clutch, difficulty shifting gears, burning smell.
- **Solution:** Clutch replacement (clutch kit).
- **Cost:** $700 - $1200


### CVT (Continuously Variable Transmission)
**Hesitation or Jerking**
- **Symptoms:** Sudden acceleration or deceleration, rough shifting feel, transmission warning light.
- **Solution:** Transmission fluid flush and filter replacement, or in severe cases, transmission rebuild or replacement.
- **Cost:** $500 - $2500+


## Suspension

### Standard Chassis
**Strut Mounts**
- **Symptoms:** Clunking or popping noises when turning or going over bumps, uneven tire wear.
- **Solution:** Replacement of front strut mounts.
- **Cost:** $250 - $500


## Electrical

### Body Control Module (BCM)
**Intermittent Electrical Failures**
- **Symptoms:** Malfunctioning power windows, door locks, interior lights, or dashboard warning lights.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and potential reprogramming or replacement of the BCM.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1000


### Alternator
**Premature Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Dimming headlights, battery warning light, difficulty starting the engine.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the alternator.
- **Cost:** $400 - $700


## Exterior

### Body Panels
**Rusting**
- **Symptoms:** Visible rust formation, particularly on wheel wells, rocker panels, and door bottoms, especially in areas with road salt.
- **Solution:** Rust repair and repainting, or replacement of affected panels.
- **Cost:** $300 - $1500+


## Interior

### HVAC System
**Blend Door Actuator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Inability to control temperature (stuck on hot or cold), air only coming from certain vents.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the blend door actuator.
- **Cost:** $200 - $400


--- 
### Summary Verdict
The 2014 Jeep Compass presents a mixed reliability profile. Owners should be particularly attentive to potential oil leaks from both engine options and issues with the CVT transmission, which can be costly to repair. Electrical gremlins, such as BCM malfunctions and alternator failures, are also noted concerns. While suspension components like strut mounts can require attention, and exterior rust can be an issue in certain climates, the interior HVAC system's blend door actuator is a more common, albeit less severe, problem. Overall, while not plagued by widespread catastrophic failures, the Compass requires diligent maintenance and awareness of these specific potential issues to ensure long-term reliability.
