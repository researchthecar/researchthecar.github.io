**2015 Jeep Compass**

The 2015 Jeep Compass is a compact SUV available with a variety of engine and transmission options. It features a spacious interior and a rugged exterior, making it popular for both urban and off-road driving. The Compass comes standard with a 2.4-liter four-cylinder engine that produces 172 horsepower and 165 lb-ft of torque. It can be paired with either a six-speed manual transmission or a six-speed automatic transmission. Optional engine choices include a 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine with 270 horsepower and 295 lb-ft of torque and a 3.6-liter V6 engine with 295 horsepower and 260 lb-ft of torque. The 2.0-liter engine is offered with a nine-speed automatic transmission, while the 3.6-liter engine comes with a six-speed automatic transmission.

**Engine**

**2.4-liter Four-Cylinder Engine**

* **Intake Valve Deposits:** Carbon deposits can build up on the intake valves, reducing engine performance and fuel economy. Symptoms include rough idling, hesitation, and decreased power. The solution involves cleaning the intake valves and replacing the intake manifold gasket. Estimated cost to fix: $500 - $800+
* **Timing Chain Tensioner Failure:** The timing chain tensioner can fail, causing the timing chain to skip, which can lead to engine damage. Symptoms include a rattling noise from the engine and a loss of power. The solution involves replacing the timing chain tensioner. Estimated cost to fix: $800 - $1,200+
* **PCV Valve Failure:** The PCV valve can fail, leading to increased oil consumption and emissions. Symptoms include blue smoke from the exhaust, rough idling, and increased oil consumption. The solution involves replacing the PCV valve. Estimated cost to fix: $100 - $200+
* **Recalls: R13, R07, R06, R05**

**2.0-liter Turbocharged Four-Cylinder Engine**

* **Turbocharger Failure:** The turbocharger can fail, causing a loss of power and increased exhaust smoke. Symptoms include a whistling noise from the engine, blue smoke from the exhaust, and a decrease in acceleration. The solution involves replacing the turbocharger. Estimated cost to fix: $1,500 - $2,000+
* **Fuel Injector Failure:** The fuel injectors can fail, causing misfires and a loss of power. Symptoms include rough idling, hesitation, and decreased fuel economy. The solution involves replacing the fuel injectors. Estimated cost to fix: $400 - $600+
* **Intake Valve Deposits:** Carbon deposits can build up on the intake valves, reducing engine performance and fuel economy. Symptoms include rough idling, hesitation, and decreased power. The solution involves cleaning the intake valves and replacing the intake manifold gasket. Estimated cost to fix: $500 - $800+

**3.6-liter V6 Engine**

* **Valve Spring Failure:** The valve springs can fail, causing a loss of compression and power. Symptoms include a tapping noise from the engine, decreased performance, and increased oil consumption. The solution involves replacing the valve springs. Estimated cost to fix: $1,200 - $1,600+
* **Timing Chain Failure:** The timing chain can fail, causing the engine to lose power and run rough. Symptoms include a rattling noise from the engine, a loss of power, and a check engine light. The solution involves replacing the timing chain. Estimated cost to fix: $1,000 - $1,500+
* **Water Pump Failure:** The water pump can fail, causing the engine to overheat. Symptoms include a coolant leak, a high coolant temperature gauge, and overheating. The solution involves replacing the water pump. Estimated cost to fix: $300 - $500+

**Transmission**

**Six-Speed Manual Transmission**

* **Synchro Ring Failure:** The synchro rings can wear out, causing difficulty shifting gears. Symptoms include grinding noises when shifting, especially between 2nd and 3rd gear. The solution involves replacing the synchro rings. Estimated cost to fix: $600 - $900+
* **Clutch Failure:** The clutch can wear out, causing slippage and difficulty engaging gears. Symptoms include a burning smell, difficulty shifting gears, and a loss of power. The solution involves replacing the clutch. Estimated cost to fix: $800 - $1,200+

**Six-Speed Automatic Transmission**

* **Transmission Control Module Failure:** The transmission control module (TCM) can fail, causing the transmission to shift erratically or not shift at all. Symptoms include harsh shifts, slipping gears, and a check engine light. The solution involves replacing the TCM. Estimated cost to fix: $500 - $700+
* **Valve Body Failure:** The valve body can fail, causing the transmission to slip or shift erratically. Symptoms include slipping gears, harsh shifts, and a check engine light. The solution involves replacing the valve body. Estimated cost to fix: $800 - $1,200+
* **Torque Converter Failure:** The torque converter can fail, causing the transmission to slip or shudder. Symptoms include slipping gears, shuddering on acceleration, and a check engine light. The solution involves replacing the torque converter. Estimated cost to fix: $1,000 - $1,500+
* **Recalls: R08, R05**

**Nine-Speed Automatic Transmission**

* **Shift Solenoid Failure:** The shift solenoids can fail, causing the transmission to shift erratically or not shift at all. Symptoms include harsh shifts, slipping gears, and a check engine light. The solution involves replacing the shift solenoids. Estimated cost to fix: $500 - $700+
* **Mechatronic Unit Failure:** The mechatronic unit can fail, causing the transmission to shift erratically or not shift at all. Symptoms include harsh shifts, slipping gears, and a check engine light. The solution involves replacing the mechatronic unit. Estimated cost to fix: $2,000 - $2,500+

**Suspension**

* **Strut Failure:** The struts can wear out, causing a loss of ride quality and handling. Symptoms include a bumpy ride, excessive body roll, and uneven tire wear. The solution involves replacing the struts. Estimated cost to fix: $600 - $900+ per axle
* **Ball Joint Failure:** The ball joints can wear out, causing the wheels to lose alignment and the suspension to become unstable. Symptoms include a clunking noise when driving over bumps, uneven tire wear, and a loss of steering control. The solution involves replacing the ball joints. Estimated cost to fix: $400 - $600+ per side

**Electrical**

* **Battery Failure:** The battery can fail, causing the vehicle to lose power. Symptoms include difficulty starting the vehicle, a dim display, and a clicking noise when trying to start the engine. The solution involves replacing the battery. Estimated cost to fix: $150 - $250+
* **Alternator Failure:** The alternator can fail, causing the vehicle to lose power. Symptoms include a dim display, flickering lights, and a loss of power. The solution involves replacing the alternator. Estimated cost to fix: $500 - $700+

**Exterior**

* **Rear Bumper Sagging:** The rear bumper can sag or detach due to wear and tear. Symptoms include a loose or drooping rear bumper. The solution involves tightening or replacing the bumper mounts. Estimated cost to fix: $200 - $300+

**Interior**

* **Climate Control Malfunction:** The climate control system can malfunction, causing the air conditioning or heating to not work properly. Symptoms may include erratic temperature control, a lack of airflow, or a malfunctioning display. The solution may involve replacing or repairing various components, such as the blower motor, blend door actuator, or climate control module. Estimated cost to fix: $200 - $500+
* **Seat Adjustability Issues:** The power seat adjusters can fail, causing the seats to become stuck or difficult to adjust. Symptoms include inoperable seat controls, reduced seat movement range, or a lack of support. The solution may involve replacing the seat adjusters or repairing electrical connections. Estimated cost to fix: $300 - $600+
* **Door Lock Malfunction:** The power door locks can malfunction, preventing the doors from locking or unlocking properly. Symptoms include unresponsive door lock buttons, doors that remain ajar, or a lack of remote locking functionality. The solution may involve replacing door lock actuators, repairing wiring issues, or reprogramming the vehicle's body control module. Estimated cost to fix: $200 - $400+

**Summary**

The 2015 Jeep Compass faces a range of issues across its various engine and transmission options. Engine problems can include intake valve deposits, timing chain failures, and valve spring failures, with repair costs ranging from $500 to $2,000+ depending on the severity of the issue. Transmission issues are also common, with failures affecting the six-speed manual, six-speed automatic, and nine-speed automatic transmissions.
