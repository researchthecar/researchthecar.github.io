# 2022 Jeep Compass

### Overview
The 2022 Jeep Compass, while offering a capable and stylish package, presents a mixed bag in terms of long-term reliability. Owners have reported a few recurring issues, particularly concerning the powertrain and some electrical components. While many drivers experience a trouble-free ownership, potential buyers should be aware of these documented concerns. The vehicle's design and features are generally well-received, but the reliability data suggests that proactive maintenance and awareness of common problems are advisable for this model year. This analysis focuses on documented technical issues beyond standard wear and tear.

---


## Engine

### 1.3L Turbocharged I4 (T270)
**Turbocharger Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of power, whistling noises from the engine bay, increased oil consumption, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the turbocharger unit.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1500

**Cooling System Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Overheating, visible coolant leaks under the vehicle, sweet smell from the engine bay.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of leaking hoses, radiator, or water pump.
- **Cost:** $300 - $700


## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic (FWD)
**Harsh Shifting / Hesitation**
- **Symptoms:** Jerky gear changes, delayed engagement, occasional shuddering during acceleration.
- **Solution:** Software update for transmission control module, or internal transmission repair/rebuild.
- **Cost:** $500 - $2000


### 9-Speed Automatic (AWD)
**Transmission Slipping**
- **Symptoms:** Engine revs increase without corresponding vehicle speed increase, difficulty engaging gears.
- **Solution:** Transmission fluid flush and filter replacement, or potential transmission rebuild.
- **Cost:** $600 - $2500

### Summary Verdict
The 2022 Jeep Compass demonstrates average reliability, with the most frequently cited concerns revolving around its automatic transmissions (particularly the 9-speed) and occasional electrical glitches with the infotainment system. Engine-related issues, such as turbocharger problems and cooling system leaks, have also been noted, though not universally. The suspension and exterior components appear to be relatively robust. While not plagued by widespread critical failures, these issues can lead to unexpected repair costs and inconvenience. Prospective buyers should consider these potential points of failure and factor in the possibility of maintenance beyond routine servicing.
