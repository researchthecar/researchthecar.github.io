# 2022 Jeep Compass Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2022 Jeep Compass offers buyers a refreshed exterior, a modernized interior with upgraded tech, and a standard 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine paired with an eight-speed automatic transmission and all-wheel drive. While the updated powertrain provides a more refined driving experience compared to older model years, prospective buyers should review owner-reported issues concerning electrical glitches, electronic safety systems, and unexpected engine stalling linked to the automatic start-stop system.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged I4

The 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine produces approximately 200 horsepower and 221 lb-ft of torque, providing adequate power for daily commuting and highway driving. It is designed to offer a balance of efficiency and capability when paired with standard all-wheel drive.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

The eight-speed automatic transmission delivers smooth gear shifts across various driving conditions and helps optimize fuel economy. Overall durability appears solid, though some owners have experienced electronic integration quirks related to park positioning.

## Steering

### Steering Rack and Column

Steering complaints are relatively uncommon but can involve mechanical noises or stiffness requiring professional inspection of the steering gear.

**Steering Column Clunking and Play**
- **Symptoms:** Owners report a noticeable clunking noise or play when turning the steering wheel at low speeds or while idling.
- **Solution:** Inspection of the steering intermediate shaft and suspension components may lead to component tightening or replacement of worn half-axles.

## Electrical

### Infotainment and Instrument Cluster

Electrical and infotainment complaints center around intermittent screen blackouts, flickering warning lights, and software glitches that occasionally require system resets.

**Infotainment Reboot and Screen Blanking**
- **Symptoms:** The center infotainment screen or digital instrument cluster may intermittently freeze, go blank, or spontaneously reboot while driving.
- **Solution:** Software updates performed by a dealership can often resolve display glitches, though some vehicles may require module resets or replacements.

## Safety

### Advanced Driver Assistance Systems (ADAS)

Safety technology features advanced driver-assistance systems that occasionally trigger false warnings or unexpected braking interventions due to sensor calibration sensitivity.

**Unexpected Braking and ADAS Warnings**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers experience unexpected emergency braking events or intermittent warnings indicating that forward collision avoidance and blind-spot monitoring are temporarily unavailable.
- **Solution:** Reprogramming or recalibrating the front sensors and cameras at a service center can help restore proper functionality.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V248000: Seats:Front Assembly:Head Restraint](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Jeep&model=Compass&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 202, "Head Restraints." Improperly welded head restraints can result in an increased risk of injury to seat occupants during a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace both front seat head restraints, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V058000: Electrical System: Instrument Cluster/Panel](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Jeep&model=Compass&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** The instrument panel center cluster display may be insufficiently illuminated and may not appear visible during daytime driving conditions. An instrument panel display that is not visible to the driver may result in the.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the windshield and headlamp switch, add a light sensor module, and add a wiring jumper harness to allow the instrument panel center cluster display backlighting to function correctly, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V576000: Electrical System: Instrument Cluster/Panel](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Jeep&model=Compass&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** The brightness level of the instrument panel cluster (IPC) may not be adjustable due to incorrect software in the body control module (BCM). As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 101, "Control and Displays." An IPC display that is not bright enough may not clearly show critical safety.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the body control module (BCM) software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V436000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Jeep&model=Compass&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 111, "Rear Visibility." A rearview camera image that does not display decreases the driver's visibility, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the radio software or the software will be OTA, "Over-The-Air," free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

When shopping for a used 2022 Jeep Compass, buyers should thoroughly test all electronics, infotainment features, and advanced safety systems during a test drive. Pay close attention to the behavior of the automatic start-stop system and verify that all software updates have been applied to minimize the risk of unexpected stalling or dashboard display failures. A comprehensive pre-purchase inspection by a qualified mechanic is recommended.
