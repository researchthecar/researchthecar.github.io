# 2017 Jeep Wrangler Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2017 Jeep Wrangler offers legendary off-road capability and iconic styling, but prospective buyers should carefully weigh its mechanical and electrical quirks. While owners appreciate its rugged charm and open-air versatility, this model year attracts notable complaints regarding braking components, engine oil leaks, and steering stability. Reviewing maintenance history and checking for prior component replacements can help buyers find a dependable rig.

---

## Engine

### 3.6L Pentastar V6

The standard 3.6-liter Pentastar V6 engine produces 285 horsepower and 260 lb-ft of torque, offering solid everyday driveability and trail performance. While generally durable, it is known for oil filter housing leaks and occasional cooling system issues.

**Oil Filter Housing Cracking and Leaking**
- **Symptoms:** Oil leaks or coolant mixing under the hood, often caused by the cracking of the composite plastic oil filter housing during heat cycles.
- **Solution:** Replace the failure-prone plastic housing assembly, preferably with an updated or aftermarket aluminum replacement unit.

## Transmission

### 5-Speed Automatic Transmission

The available 5-speed automatic transmission pairs adequately with the V6 engine for daily driving and crawling, though older units warrant a check for fluid leaks.

**Transmission Pan Gasket Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Fluid pooling under the vehicle or burning smells originating from transmission fluid dripping onto hot exhaust components.
- **Solution:** Inspect the transmission pan gasket, replace worn seals, and torque fasteners to manufacturer specifications.

### 6-Speed Manual Transmission

The 6-speed manual transmission gives drivers direct control over gear selection, but internal component wear can lead to shifting complaints over time.

**Synchronizer and Shifter Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Difficulty shifting gears, grinding noises, or the transmission popping out of reverse while parking or maneuvering.
- **Solution:** Inspect transmission fluid for metallic debris, and rebuild or replace damaged internal synchronizers and shift components.

## Brakes

### Brake System & ABS

Brake system concerns range from sticking calipers to electronic ABS module failures that disable stability features.

**ABS Control Module Failure**
- **Symptoms:** ABS, traction control, and brake warning lights illuminating on the dashboard with a loss of advanced braking safety features.
- **Solution:** Diagnose the ABS module and replace the faulty unit, keeping in mind potential parts backorders.

**Caliper Binding and Mounting Bolt Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Pulling to one side, burning smells, scraping noises, or loose brake calipers coming into contact with wheel components.
- **Solution:** Inspect brake calipers, replace stuck slide pins or worn hoses, and secure or replace missing caliper mounting bolts.

## Steering

### Steering and Front Suspension

Front-end stability is critical on solid-axle Jeeps, and worn steering linkage or suspension geometry can trigger severe steering vibrations.

**Death Wobble**
- **Symptoms:** Violent shaking and vibration of the steering wheel and front end after hitting a bump or groove at highway speeds.
- **Solution:** Inspect front suspension joints, track bars, ball joints, and steering stabilizers for wear, and replace compromised components.

## Electrical

### Electrical and Infotainment

electrical issues in this generation frequently involve auxiliary features like backup cameras and basic battery connection stability.

**Backup Camera and Battery Terminal Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Intermittent failure or complete blanking of the rearview camera screen, or loose battery terminals causing sudden stalling.
- **Solution:** Inspect and clean battery terminals to ensure secure clamping, or diagnose and replace malfunctioning camera wiring harnesses.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V680000: Seat Belts:Front:Anchorage](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Jeep&model=Wrangler&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** A separated or severed seat belt buckle strap will result in an inoperable seat belt, increasing the risk of injury in the event of a crash.
- **What to do:** FCA US will notify owners, and dealers will replace the driver's seat belt buckle, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V524000: Electrical System:Wiring:Front Underhood](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Jeep&model=Wrangler&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** A vehicle stall can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will replace the powertrain control module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V849000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Storage:Tank Assembly](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Jeep&model=Wrangler&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** A fuel leak in the presence of an ignition source can increase the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and Jeep dealers will inspect the fuel tank and replace fuel tank and sending unit, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V734000: Air Bags:Frontal:Sensor/Control Module-Inactive](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Jeep&model=Wrangler&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** In certain crash conditions, the front impact sensor wiring may be pulled until it detaches before a signal can be received by the Occupant Restraint Controller (ORC). If the ORC module does not receive a signal from the front impact sensor, both frontal air bags and the seat belt pretensioners will not deploy in the event of a crash, increasing the risk of.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will reroute the front impact sensor wiring and relocate the impact sensor branch, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V098000: Exterior Lighting:Brake Lights:Switch](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Jeep&model=Wrangler&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** Having the brake lamps continuously illuminated or being able to shift the vehicle out of PARK when the brake pedal is not pressed (and the ignition is in the RUN mode) can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will replace the brake switches, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2017 Jeep Wrangler is a capable, highly customizable vehicle, but buyers should perform a thorough mechanical inspection before purchasing. Pay close attention to the engine oil filter housing, front brake components, steering linkage condition, and transmission service history. Selecting a well-maintained example will greatly reduce the likelihood of encountering expensive surprise repairs.
