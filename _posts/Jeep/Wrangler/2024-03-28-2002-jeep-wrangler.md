**Introduction**

The 2002 Jeep Wrangler is a popular SUV known for its off-road capabilities and rugged design. It comes with two engine options: a 2.5L inline-4 and a 4.0L inline-6, paired with either a 5-speed manual or 4-speed automatic transmission. Despite its popularity, the Wrangler has some common issues that potential buyers should be aware of.

**Engine**

**2.5L Inline-4**
- **Intake Manifold Cracking:** Description/Symptoms: Cracks develop in the plastic intake manifold, causing vacuum leaks and reduced engine performance. Solution: Replace the intake manifold. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+
- **Cylinder Head Warping:** Description/Symptoms: Warping occurs in the cylinder head, leading to coolant and oil leaks, and overheating. Solution: Replace the cylinder head. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,000+

**4.0L Inline-6**
- **Throttle Position Sensor Failure:** Description/Symptoms: The throttle position sensor fails, causing erratic engine behavior, stalls, and decreased fuel efficiency. Solution: Replace the throttle position sensor. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
- **Camshaft Position Sensor Failure:** Description/Symptoms: The camshaft position sensor fails, causing hard starting, engine misfires, and reduced power. Solution: Replace the camshaft position sensor. Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $250+

**Transmission**

**5-Speed Manual**
- **Clutch Failure:** Description/Symptoms: The clutch wears prematurely, leading to slipping and difficulty engaging gears. Solution: Replace the clutch assembly. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800+

**4-Speed Automatic**
- **Transmission Fluid Leaks:** Description/Symptoms: Leaks develop in the transmission pan or lines, causing fluid loss and transmission damage. Solution: Repair or replace the leaking components. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
- **Solenoid Failure:** Description/Symptoms: Solenoids inside the transmission fail, resulting in erratic shifting, delayed engagement, and slipping. Solution: Replace the faulty solenoid. Estimated Cost to Fix: $250 - $400+

**Suspension**

- **Leaf Spring Breakage:** Description/Symptoms: Leaf springs break under load, causing a sagging suspension, reduced stability, and rough ride. Solution: Replace the broken leaf spring. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+ per spring

**Electrical**

- **Alternator Failure:** Description/Symptoms: The alternator fails, resulting in battery drain and electrical system problems. Solution: Replace the alternator. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+
- **Battery Drain:** Description/Symptoms: Battery drains quickly due to parasitic power draw. Solution: Diagnose and fix the source of the drain (e.g., alternator diode, faulty wiring). Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300+

**Exterior**

- **Plastic Trim Cracking:** Description/Symptoms: Plastic trim pieces, such as door handles and mirror covers, crack and break. Solution: Replace the affected trim pieces. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+ per piece
- **Rust:** Description/Symptoms: Rust develops on the frame, body panels, and exhaust system. Solution: Sand, treat, and repaint affected areas. Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the extent of rust

**Interior**

- **Plastic Dash Panel Warping:** Description/Symptoms: The plastic dash panel warps in hot weather, causing gaps and creaking noises. Solution: Replace the dash panel. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $700+

**Summary**

While the 2002 Jeep Wrangler is a capable off-roader, it has several common issues that potential buyers should consider. Engine problems, such as intake manifold cracking and camshaft position sensor failure, can be costly to repair. Transmission issues, including clutch failure and solenoid problems, can also lead to significant expenses. The Wrangler is also prone to suspension problems, such as leaf spring breakage. Electrical problems and interior issues are less severe but still require attention. Rust is a common problem for older Wranglers, and extensive rust repair can be time-consuming and expensive. Overall, the 2002 Jeep Wrangler's issues are of moderate severity, but the cost of repairs can vary depending on the specific problem.
