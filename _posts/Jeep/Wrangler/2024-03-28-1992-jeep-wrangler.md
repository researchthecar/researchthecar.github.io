**Introduction:**
The 1992 Jeep Wrangler is a rugged and versatile off-road SUV known for its legendary capabilities. It is powered by a 2.5-liter inline-four engine, producing 120 horsepower and 140 lb-ft of torque, paired with a standard five-speed manual or optional three-speed automatic transmission. The Wrangler's appeal lies in its unmatched off-road performance, but it also comes with its fair share of issues.

**Engine**
* **2.5-liter inline-four**
    * **Intake manifold gasket failure:** Symptoms include coolant leaks, overheating, and rough idle.
    * Solution: Replace intake manifold gasket.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200-$400
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**
* **AX-5 five-speed manual**
    * **Grinding gears, especially in second:** Caused by worn synchronizers or damaged gear teeth.
    * Solution: Repair or replace synchronizers and gears.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500-$1000+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **TF999 three-speed automatic**
    * **Slipping gears:** Transmission fluid may be low or contaminated, solenoid failure, or internal damage.
    * Solution: Check and replace fluid if necessary, replace solenoid, or rebuild/replace transmission.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300-$2500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**
* **Leaf springs:**
    * **Sagging or breakage:** Overloading or excessive use can weaken springs.
    * Solution: Replace leaf springs.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300-$600
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Shocks and struts:**
    * **Leaking or worn:** Loss of damping ability reduces ride quality and handling.
    * Solution: Replace shocks and struts.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200-$500
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**
* **Alternator:**
    * **Failure:** Electrical problems, dim headlights, dashboard warning lights.
    * Solution: Replace alternator.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400-$600
    * Recalls: 97V101000
* **Battery:**
    * **Premature discharge:** Short circuits or faulty charging system can drain battery.
    * Solution: Inspect charging system, replace battery if necessary.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100-$200+
    * Recalls: 95V052000

**Exterior**
* **Door hinges:**
    * **Rust and corrosion:** Water and salt damage can lead to hinge failure.
    * Solution: Clean and lubricate hinges, replace if necessary.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $150-$500
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Tailgate:**
    * **Latch failure:** Latch may not engage or release properly.
    * Solution: Adjust or replace latch mechanism.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100-$200
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**
* **Dashboard and center console:**
    * **Cracked plastic or discoloration:** UV damage or harsh cleaning chemicals.
    * Solution: Replace dashboard or console.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500-$1000
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Seats fabric:**
    * **Tearing:** Excessive wear and tear or poor quality material.
    * Solution: Repair or replace seat fabric.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200-$500
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**
The 1992 Jeep Wrangler is a capable off-roader with a loyal following, but it is not without its issues. The most common problems relate to the engine intake manifold gaskets, manual transmission, and leaf spring suspension. Electrical and interior issues are also prevalent, but generally less severe. The cost of repairs can vary depending on the specific issue and the chosen mechanic. Overall, the Wrangler's reliability can be a concern, especially for owners who plan on frequent off-road use.
