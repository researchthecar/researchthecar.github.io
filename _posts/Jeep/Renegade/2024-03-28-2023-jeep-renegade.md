## 2023 Jeep Renegade

The 2023 Jeep Renegade is a compact SUV offered with a choice of two engine options: a 1.3L turbocharged inline-4 engine (175 horsepower, 200 lb-ft of torque) paired with a 9-speed automatic transmission and a 2.4L naturally aspirated inline-4 engine (180 horsepower, 175 lb-ft of torque) paired with either a 6-speed manual transmission or a 9-speed automatic transmission.

The Renegade is known for its rugged exterior, off-road capabilities, and spacious interior. However, it has also been reported to have a few common issues.

### Engine

**1.3L Turbocharged Inline-4 Engine**

* **Timing Chain Tensioner Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Loss of engine power, rattling noise, or check engine light illumination.
    * Solution: Replace the timing chain tensioner.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Oil Consumption:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Excessive oil consumption, causing low oil levels and potential damage to the engine.
    * Solution: Inspect and repair or replace any leaking gaskets or seals.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
    * Recalls: None Found

### Transmission

**9-Speed Automatic Transmission**

* **Shifting Problems:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears or hard shifts, causing delayed or lurching acceleration.
    * Solution: Reprogram the transmission control module or replace the transmission.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $4,000+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Fluid Leaks:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Visible leaks of transmission fluid, causing fluid loss and potential transmission failure.
    * Solution: Replace the leaking component, such as gaskets or seals.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**6-Speed Manual Transmission**

* **No Issues Found**

### Suspension

* **Strut Mount Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Excessive noise or vibration while driving, uneven tire wear.
    * Solution: Replace the strut mounts.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,200+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Ball Joint Wear:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Loose or worn ball joints can cause clunking or knocking noises, as well as reduced stability.
    * Solution: Replace the ball joints.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

### Electrical

* **Electrical Harness Problems:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Various electrical issues, such as intermittent power loss, flickering lights, or malfunctioning components.
    * Solution: Diagnose and repair or replace the faulty wiring harness.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Battery Drain:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Battery draining quickly, preventing the vehicle from starting.
    * Solution: Diagnose and repair the cause of the drain, such as a faulty parasitic draw.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+
    * Recalls: None Found

### Exterior

* **Door Handle Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Handles may break or become loose, making it difficult to open or close the doors.
    * Solution: Replace the door handles.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Rear Window Glass Shattering:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Spontaneous shattering of the rear window glass, potentially due to thermal stress.
    * Solution: Replace the rear window glass.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,200+
    * Recalls: None Found

### Interior

* **Infotainment System Lag or Freezing:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Slow response or freezing of the Uconnect infotainment system.
    * Solution: Software updates or hardware replacement.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 - $1,000+ (if hardware replacement is required)
    * Recalls: NHTSA Campaign ID: 23V282000
* **Headliner Sagging:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Looseness or sagging of the headliner material, causing an unsightly appearance.
    * Solution: Reattach or replace the headliner.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+
    * Recalls: None Found

### Summary

The 2023 Jeep Renegade has been reported to have several issues, including timing chain tensioner failure, oil consumption, and transmission problems. While some of these issues can be costly to fix, it's important to note that these issues may not affect all vehicles. The overall severity and cost of the issues will vary depending on the specific problem and the individual vehicle.
