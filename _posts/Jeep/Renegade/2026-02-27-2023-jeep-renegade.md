# 2023 Jeep Renegade Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2023 Jeep Renegade brings distinct styling and standard four-wheel drive to the subcompact SUV segment, but prospective used-car buyers should review owner-reported feedback carefully. While many owners enjoy its compact footprint and capability, public safety and driver assistance complaints highlight intermittent electronic alerts and mechanical concerns. Before purchasing a used model, a thorough inspection and test drive focusing on electronics, active safety systems, and powertrain engagement are strongly recommended.

---

## Engine

### 1.3L Turbocharged 4-Cylinder

The 1.3-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine produces approximately 177 horsepower and 210 lb-ft of torque, providing adequate power for daily commuting and light utility. As owner reports reflect occasional electronic and powertrain management complaints, routine maintenance records should be closely checked.

## Transmission

### 9-Speed Automatic Transmission

Paired exclusively with standard four-wheel drive, this 9-speed automatic transmission aims to balance highway efficiency with off-road capability. Buyers should evaluate shift quality and engagement responsiveness during a test drive to ensure smooth operation.

## Steering

### Steering Assembly

Steering complaints are infrequent but involve directional tracking inconsistencies.

**Steering Pull**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may exhibit a noticeable pull to one side while driving at varying speeds.
- **Solution:** Inspect tire pressures, check wheel alignment, and verify electric power steering calibration.

## Electrical

### Battery and Infotainment Display

Electrical system concerns center on auxiliary power draws and infotainment screen behavior.

**Battery Drain and Screen Image Retention**
- **Symptoms:** Some owners experience premature battery failure requiring frequent replacements, as well as temporary image ghosting or burn-in on the main infotainment display.
- **Solution:** Test the charging system and battery health, and consult a dealer regarding potential software patches for infotainment display anomalies.

## Safety

### Air Bag and Seat Belts

Issues related to supplemental restraint warnings and seat belt indicators have been noted in owner feedback.

**Air Bag Warning and Seat Belt Alerts**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have reported unexpected air bag warning lights illuminating on the instrument cluster, as well as recurring seat belt or warning chime activations.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified technician scan the occupant restraint controller for diagnostic trouble codes and inspect seat belt sensors or wiring harnesses.

### Forward Collision Avoidance

Advanced driver assistance features can occasionally misinterpret normal traffic environments.

**Unintended Forward Collision Activation**
- **Symptoms:** The forward collision warning or active braking system may occasionally trigger unexpectedly when approaching stationary objects or vehicles in adjacent turning lanes.
- **Solution:** Check for available software updates to advanced driver assistance modules at an authorized dealership.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V510000: Air Bags:Frontal](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Jeep&model=Renegade&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** A seat belt buckle switch sensor may be improperly connected, preventing the front seat air bag from deploying as intended. A front seat air bag that does not deploy as intended can increase the risk of injury in a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will repair the connection, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V918000: Exterior Lighting:Back Up Lights](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Jeep&model=Renegade&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 108, "Lamps, Reflective Devices, and Associated Equipment." The incorrect reverse lights may have reduced visibility, and not alert pedestrians and other drivers that the vehicle is backing up, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the bumper reverse light assembly, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2023 Jeep Renegade offers distinctive character and standard four-wheel drive capability, but used-car shoppers should approach with attentive inspection habits. Owner complaints point to occasional electrical anomalies, sensitive driver-assist features, and restraint system warning lights. Verifying maintenance history, checking for outstanding recalls, and performing a detailed test of all onboard electronics will help ensure a dependable purchase.
