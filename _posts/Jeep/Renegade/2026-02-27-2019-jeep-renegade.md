# 2019 Jeep Renegade Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2019 Jeep Renegade offers distinctive styling and urban-friendly dimensions, but prospective buyers should approach with a measure of caution regarding its mechanical and electronic longevity. Owner feedback submitted to safety regulators highlights several recurring problem areas, most notably concerning infotainment stability, powertrain behavior, and minor fluid leaks. While many drivers enjoy its personality and capability, being aware of these common trouble spots can help you spot potential issues during a test drive and negotiate a fair price.

---

## Engine

### 1.3L Turbo Inline-4

The 1.3-liter turbocharged inline-4 engine produces approximately 177 horsepower and 210 lb-ft of torque, providing punchy acceleration for daily driving. It serves as a modern replacement for older powerplants, though buyers should regularly check fluid levels to ensure long-term reliability.

**Coolant Leaks and Engine Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have reported unexpected coolant loss, fluid pooling near the front of the engine, or performance degradation linked to cooling system integrity.
- **Solution:** Inspect hoses, connections, and the water pump assembly for leaks; replace damaged components as necessary.

### 2.4L Tigershark Inline-4

The naturally aspirated 2.4-liter Tigershark engine delivers 180 horsepower and 175 lb-ft of torque, offering a traditional driving feel. It is widely found across various trim levels, though routine oil level monitoring is recommended due to known manufacturer-noted consumption tendencies in similar FCA applications.

## Transmission

### 9-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 9-speed automatic transmission pairs with both engine choices and features an expansive gear range designed for highway efficiency. While generally capable, early model software calibration can sometimes lead to rough shift quality and hesitation.

**Rough Shifting and Drivetrain Hesitation**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may experience lurching, harsh gear engagement, delayed acceleration response, or unexpected entry into limp mode.
- **Solution:** Check for available powertrain control module software updates or consult a specialist to diagnose internal valve body and sensor concerns.

## Suspension

### Tires and Alignment

The suspension setup balances compact maneuverability with light off-road stance capability, though keeping up with wheel alignments is crucial to prevent premature tire wear.

**Accelerated Outer Tread Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Front or rear tires exhibit uneven wear patterns, sometimes wearing down to the steel belts prematurely.
- **Solution:** Check suspension geometry, ensure proper wheel alignment, and rotate tires at recommended intervals.

## Brakes

### Brake Rotors and Pads

The braking system provides adequate stopping power for daily commuting, but some owners report accelerated wear and warping on factory or replacement rotors.

**Premature Rotor Warping**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers notice steering wheel vibrations or pulsing sensations when applying the brakes, often occurring shortly after component replacement.
- **Solution:** Replace warped brake rotors and inspect brake calipers for proper sliding movement and release.

## Electrical

### Uconnect Infotainment Screen

The vehicle's electrical architecture heavily integrates climate, audio, and safety display features into the central Uconnect touchscreen. Screen failures can be both frustrating and safety-impairing since it houses the rearview camera feed.

**Screen Blanking and Backup Camera Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The infotainment display may freeze, develop lines, turn completely black, or fail to show the rearview camera image while the vehicle is shifted into reverse.
- **Solution:** Perform a system reset, update the software firmware, or replace the faulty display unit if internal hardware has failed.
- **Cost:** $1600

## Safety

### Seat Belts

Passive safety systems include standard airbags and seat belt pretensioners, though occasional latch or sensor glitches have been noted by vehicle owners.

**Seat Belt Latching and Retractor Concerns**
- **Symptoms:** Occupants may experience difficulty with seat belts locking, retracting smoothly, or triggering unexpected warning chimes.
- **Solution:** Inspect seat belt assemblies, buckles, and sensors; replace malfunctioning mechanisms under safety warranty guidelines when applicable.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V191000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Jeep&model=Renegade&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 111, "Rearview Mirrors." The lingering rearview image can distract the driver, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Chrysler has notified owners, and dealers will update the radio display software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V287000: Air Bags: Air Bag/Restraint Control Module](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Jeep&model=Renegade&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** Incorrect software may cause the occupant restraint controller (ORC) module to disable certain passive safety features. Deactivated passive safety features can increase the risk of injury in the event of a crash.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will update the ORC module software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V438000: Air Bags: Air Bag/Restraint Control Module](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Jeep&model=Renegade&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** The miscalibrated ORC may incorrectly deploy the air bags during a frontal crash, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will replace the ORC. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V510000: Air Bags:Frontal](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Jeep&model=Renegade&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** A seat belt buckle switch sensor may be improperly connected, preventing the front seat air bag from deploying as intended. A front seat air bag that does not deploy as intended can increase the risk of injury in a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will repair the connection, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

When shopping for a used 2019 Jeep Renegade, conduct a thorough test drive to verify smooth transmission shifting and check that all infotainment features, climate controls, and the backup camera operate reliably. Ensuring the cooling system and brakes are free of premature wear will help you avoid unexpected out-of-pocket expenses down the road. With proper inspection and maintenance, it can remain a fun and functional daily driver.
