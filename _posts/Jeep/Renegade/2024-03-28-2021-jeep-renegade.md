**Introduction:**

The 2021 Jeep Renegade is a compact SUV that offers a rugged and adventurous driving experience. It's powered by a 1.3L turbocharged inline-4 engine that produces 177 horsepower and 210 lb-ft of torque, paired with a 9-speed automatic transmission. The Renegade also comes with an optional 2.4L naturally aspirated inline-4 engine that produces 180 horsepower and 175 lb-ft of torque. AWD is standard on all models.

Despite its popularity, the Renegade has been met with its fair share of issues.

**Engine**
* **1.3L Inline-4 Engine**
    * **Excessive Oil Consumption:** Blown piston rings allow oil to enter the combustion chamber. Symptoms include decreased oil levels, increased exhaust smoke, and poor engine performance. Solution involves replacing the piston rings. Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,000+
    * **Timing Chain Failure:** Premature wear of the timing chain can cause it to skip or break, resulting in catastrophic engine damage. Symptoms include rattling noises, engine misfires, and loss of power. Solution involves replacing the timing chain and associated components. Estimated Cost to Fix: $3,000 - $5,000+
* **2.4L Inline-4 Engine**
    * **No Issues Found**

**Transmission**
* **9-Speed Automatic Transmission**
    * **Delayed Shift Engagements:** Hesitation or delay when shifting gears, especially from a standstill. Solution involves updating the transmission control module software. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
    * **Harsh Shifts:** Abrupt or jerky gear changes, particularly when downshifting. Solution may involve replacing the torque converter or transmission fluid. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+

**Suspension**
* **No Issues Found**

**Electrical**
* **Battery Drain:** Premature battery discharge due to a faulty alternator or parasitic draw. Symptoms include difficulty starting the vehicle and dead batteries. Solution involves replacing the alternator or identifying and устранение any current draws. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+
* **Electrical Short:** Malfunctioning wiring or components can cause electrical shorts, leading to various issues such as blown fuses, flickering lights, or even fires. Solution involves diagnosing and replacing faulty components. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+

**Exterior**
* **Windshield Cracking:** Premature cracking of the windshield, especially in cold temperatures. Solution involves replacing the windshield. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+

**Interior**
* **Air Conditioning Malfunction:** Failure of the air conditioning system to properly cool or heat the cabin. Solution may involve replacing the compressor, evaporator, or other components. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+
* **Sunroof Leakage:** Water leaks through the sunroof during rain or car washes. Solution involves inspecting and resealing the sunroof components. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
* **Dashboard Rattle:** Noises or vibrations coming from the dashboard, especially on rough roads. Solution involves identifying and tightening any loose components or applying sound dampening materials. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+

**Summary:**

While the Jeep Renegade offers a fun and capable driving experience, it is not without its problems. The engine, transmission, electrical, and interior components have all been reported to have issues that can be costly to repair. Potential buyers should be aware of these potential problems before making a purchase decision.
