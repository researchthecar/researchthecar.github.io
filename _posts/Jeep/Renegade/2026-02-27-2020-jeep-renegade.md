# 2020 Jeep Renegade Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2020 Jeep Renegade offers distinctive styling and compact maneuverability, but prospective buyers should carefully review owner-reported reliability trends. While many owners enjoy its daily drive and capability, recurring complaints highlight specific concerns regarding electrical system glitches, powertrain hesitation, and oil consumption. Taking time to thoroughly inspect these key areas during a test drive can help you avoid unexpected repair bills.

---

## Engine

### 1.3L Turbo 4-Cylinder

The 1.3-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine produces 177 horsepower and 210 lb-ft of torque, providing punchy low-end power for city driving. Buyers should monitor oil levels closely, as some owners have reported oil consumption and gasket leaks.

**Excessive Oil Consumption and Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Engine burns a high volume of oil between changes or develops leaks from gaskets and vacuum pumps, potentially risking component damage or stalling.
- **Solution:** Check oil levels regularly and inspect the vacuum pump and valve cover gaskets for leaks; replace failed seals as needed.

**Engine Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle unexpectedly loses power or stalls while driving, accompanied by warning lights on the dashboard.
- **Solution:** Diagnose underlying electrical or fuel delivery faults and update powertrain control modules if applicable.

### 2.4L Naturally Aspirated 4-Cylinder

The 2.4-liter Tigershark four-cylinder engine generates 180 horsepower and 175 lb-ft of torque, offering straightforward naturally aspirated power. While generally dependable, routine fluid level checks are advised.

## Transmission

### 9-Speed Automatic Transmission

The ZF 9-speed automatic transmission pairs with various trims and drivetrains to optimize highway efficiency. Some drivers report rough shifting or slipping, so a thorough test drive through all gears is recommended.

**Transmission Slippage and Jerking**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle experiences sudden jerking, hesitation, or slipping while accelerating at cruising speeds.
- **Solution:** Check transmission fluid condition, apply software updates, or consult a specialist for mechanical inspection and potential component replacement.
- **Cost:** $1,000

## Electrical

### Uconnect Infotainment Screen

The Uconnect infotainment system controls crucial vehicle functions, climate settings, and the backup camera. Screen blackouts and freezing are frequent complaints among owners.

**Screen Glitching and Backup Camera Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The touchscreen goes completely black, displays white lines, or freezes, rendering the backup camera and radio controls inoperable.
- **Solution:** Reset the infotainment system or replace the display unit/module if hardware failure persists.
- **Cost:** $1,000

## Safety

### Seat Belts and Airbags

Safety systems are critical for occupant protection, though a number of owners have filed complaints regarding seat belt latching failures and restraint performance during impacts.

**Seat Belt and Restraint Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** Driver or passenger seat belts fail to lock properly during sudden stops or fail to latch securely into place.
- **Solution:** Inspect seat belt assemblies and buckle mechanisms, replacing faulty retractors or sensors as needed.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V909000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Foundation Components:Disc:Caliper](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Jeep&model=Renegade&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** The right rear brake caliper could have been fractured during the casting process. A cracked right rear brake caliper can fail, reducing braking performance and increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will inspect and replace the right rear brake calipers, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V191000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Jeep&model=Renegade&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 111, "Rearview Mirrors." The lingering rearview image can distract the driver, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Chrysler has notified owners, and dealers will update the radio display software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V510000: Air Bags:Frontal](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Jeep&model=Renegade&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** A seat belt buckle switch sensor may be improperly connected, preventing the front seat air bag from deploying as intended. A front seat air bag that does not deploy as intended can increase the risk of injury in a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will repair the connection, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

When shopping for a used 2020 Jeep Renegade, pay close attention to the service history, specifically checking for documented oil consumption fixes, transmission smoothness, and properly functioning electronics. While many vehicles provide trouble-free service, verifying that the infotainment screen and powertrain operate without hesitation will help ensure a more confident purchase.
