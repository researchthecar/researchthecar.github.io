**Introduction to the 2022 Jeep Renegade**

The 2022 Jeep Renegade is a subcompact SUV known for its off-road capabilities and rugged design. It comes with four trim levels: Sport, Latitude, Altitude, and Trailhawk. The Renegade offers two engine options: a 1.3-liter turbocharged inline-four engine with 177 horsepower and 210 lb-ft of torque, and a 2.4-liter naturally aspirated inline-four engine with 180 horsepower and 175 lb-ft of torque. Both engines can be paired with either a six-speed manual transmission or a nine-speed automatic transmission.

Owners appreciate the Renegade's compact size, impressive off-road performance, and aggressive styling. However, some have reported issues with certain components of the vehicle.

**Engine**

* **1.3-liter Turbocharged Inline-Four Engine**
    * **Excessive Oil Consumption**
        * Description/Symptoms: Excessive oil consumption, leading to low oil levels and potential engine damage.
        * Solution: Reimbursement for oil consumption and potential engine replacement.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: Covered under warranty, typically no cost to the owner.
        * Recalls: N/A
* **2.4-liter Naturally Aspirated Inline-Four Engine**
    * **No Issues Found**

**Transmission**

* **Six-Speed Manual Transmission**
    * **Difficulty Shifting Gears**
        * Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears, especially from second to third gear.
        * Solution: Replacement of the transmission fluid and/or synchronizers.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $600 - $1,200
        * Recalls: None Found
* **Nine-Speed Automatic Transmission**
    * **Slipping Gears**
        * Description/Symptoms: Transmission slipping, causing delayed or hesitant shifting.
        * Solution: Software updates and/or transmission replacement.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000
        * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Front Suspension Noise**
    * Description/Symptoms: Excessive noise from the front suspension, especially when turning or going over bumps.
    * Solution: Replacement of the strut assemblies.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,200 - $1,800
        * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain**
    * Description/Symptoms: Battery drains prematurely, requiring frequent recharging or jump-starting.
    * Solution: Diagnosis and repair of the faulty electrical components, such as the alternator or battery.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
        * Recalls: None Found
* **Headlight Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: Headlights flickering, dimming, or failing to turn on.
    * Solution: Replacement of the headlight assemblies or electrical connectors.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
        * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Rust**
    * Description/Symptoms: Excessive rusting on the underbody, especially in areas exposed to moisture and salt.
    * Solution: Rust treatment and repairs, or replacement of rusted components.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the extent of the rust damage.
        * Recalls: None Found
* **Loose Trim**
    * Description/Symptoms: Trim pieces, such as the roof rails or body cladding, becoming loose or detached.
    * Solution: Re-attaching or replacing the trim pieces.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
        * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Climate Control Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: Malfunctioning climate system, leading to inadequate heating or cooling.
    * Solution: Diagnosis and repair of the faulty components, such as the blower motor or thermostat.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
        * Recalls: None Found
* **Infotainment System Glitches**
    * Description/Symptoms: Freezing, lagging, or unresponsive infotainment system.
    * Solution: Software updates or replacement of the infotainment unit.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,200
        * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2022 Jeep Renegade offers a balance of off-road capability and daily practicality. However, some owners have reported issues with certain components, including oil consumption, transmission problems, suspension noise, and electrical issues. The severity and cost of these issues can vary depending on the specific issue and how it is repaired.
