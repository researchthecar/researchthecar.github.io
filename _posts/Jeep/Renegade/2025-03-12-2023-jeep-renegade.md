The 2023 Jeep Renegade is a subcompact SUV known for its quirky styling and off-road capabilities, particularly in the Trailhawk trim. Engine options include a 1.3-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine producing 177 horsepower and 210 lb-ft of torque, paired with a nine-speed automatic transmission. Owners often appreciate the Renegade's distinctive look and its ability to handle light off-roading. However, some common criticisms include its relatively small cargo space, fuel economy, and the performance of the nine-speed automatic transmission.

**Engine**

**1.3L Turbocharged Four-Cylinder**

*   **Excessive Oil Consumption**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Owners have reported the 1.3L engine consuming excessive amounts of oil, requiring frequent top-offs between oil changes. Symptoms include low oil level warnings, blue exhaust smoke, and potential engine damage if the oil level is not maintained.
    *   Solution: The fix varies from replacing PCV valve to performing an engine rebuild with updated piston rings if significant wear is present.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200-$4,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**Nine-Speed Automatic**

*   **Rough or Delayed Shifting**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported experiencing rough or delayed shifting with the nine-speed automatic transmission. This can manifest as harsh gear changes, hesitation when accelerating, and difficulty finding the correct gear.
    *   Solution: Software updates or transmission replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $5,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

No Issues Found

**Electrical**

*   **Uconnect System Freezing/Malfunctioning**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Owners have reported issues with the Uconnect infotainment system, including freezing, slow response times, and occasional malfunctions. This can affect features like navigation, audio, and climate control.
    *   Solution: Software updates or replacement of the Uconnect module.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 - $1,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Sunroof Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some Renegade owners have experienced water leaks originating from the sunroof area, particularly during heavy rain. This can lead to water damage to the interior.
    *   Solution: Cleaning and resealing sunroof drains, or replacing the sunroof seal.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Rattling/Buzzing Sounds from Dashboard or Trim**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners report experiencing rattling or buzzing sounds coming from the dashboard or other interior trim pieces. This can be distracting and difficult to pinpoint.
    *   Solution: Identifying and securing loose trim panels or components.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $200+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2023 Jeep Renegade has some common issues, with the most concerning relating to the engine's oil consumption and the transmission's shifting performance. Electrical issues with the Uconnect system and sunroof leaks are also reported. The severity and cost of these issues can vary greatly, with engine-related problems potentially leading to significant repair expenses. It's advisable to thoroughly inspect the vehicle, check for any signs of these issues during a test drive, and review the vehicle's maintenance history before purchasing.
