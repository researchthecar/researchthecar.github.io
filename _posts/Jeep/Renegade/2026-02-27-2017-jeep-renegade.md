# 2017 Jeep Renegade Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2017 Jeep Renegade offers distinctive styling and compact dimensions, but prospective buyers should approach with caution due to a notable volume of owner-reported complaints. Powertrain hesitation, electrical glitches, and unexpected engine stalling represent the primary areas of concern documented by drivers. Reviewing service histories and conducting a thorough pre-purchase inspection can help uncover lingering issues before you buy.

---

## Engine

### 1.4L Turbo Inline-4

A 1.4-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine producing around 160 horsepower, paired typically with a manual transmission. It offers a punchy drive for city commuting, though routine maintenance is essential for longevity.

### 2.4L Tigershark Inline-4

A naturally aspirated 2.4-liter four-cylinder engine making roughly 180 horsepower, usually mated to an automatic transmission. It serves as the volume engine for most trims but has attracted complaints regarding stalling and fuel delivery issues.

**Engine Stalling and Loss of Motive Power**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicles may unexpectedly stall while driving, accompanied by dashboard warning lights and prompts to shift to park.
- **Solution:** Diagnostic testing by a qualified technician to check fuel delivery systems, software updates, or sensors.

**Fuel Pump Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle fails to start or abruptly shuts down during operation due to faulty fuel delivery.
- **Solution:** Replace the defective fuel pump assembly.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Manual Transmission

A traditional six-speed manual gearbox available on select lower trims paired with the 1.4L turbo engine, offering straightforward mechanical operation.

### 9-Speed Automatic Transmission

A nine-speed automatic transmission paired predominantly with the 2.4L engine and available all-wheel-drive systems. Some owners report rough shifts, hesitation, and occasional electronic engagement faults.

**Transmission Slipping and Harsh Shifting**
- **Symptoms:** Sudden jerking, hesitation when accelerating, or dropping into 'limp mode' with a service transmission warning light.
- **Solution:** Reprogramming the powertrain control module or performing mechanical repairs depending on diagnostic results.

## Suspension

### Wheels and Hardware

Standard suspension and wheel components generally hold up well, though roadside maintenance can be complicated by factory wheel hardware defects.

**Swollen Lug Nuts**
- **Symptoms:** Metal caps on lug nuts expand over time, making it impossible to fit a standard socket over them during a flat tire change.
- **Solution:** Replace factory lug nuts with solid aftermarket alternatives.

## Steering

### Electric Power Steering

Provides light and adjustable steering feel for daily driving, though electrical issues can occasionally trigger power steering assistance failures.

**Power Steering Loss**
- **Symptoms:** Steering wheel becomes difficult to turn or locks up while driving, often accompanied by stability control error messages.
- **Solution:** Inspect the electric power steering module, wiring, and vehicle charging system.

## Electrical

### Electrical System

Governs interior electronics, exterior lighting, and various sensor suites. Owners occasionally experience ghost warning messages and accessory glitches.

**Intermittent Warning Lights and Failures**
- **Symptoms:** Random dashboard warning lights, malfunctioning exterior lights, and erratic behavior from infotainment or backup camera systems.
- **Solution:** Inspect wiring harnesses, test battery health, and reset or update software modules.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V731000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Jeep&model=Renegade&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** A loss of fuel pressure can cause a stall, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will replace the fuel pump, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V485000: Electrical System: Instrument Cluster/Panel](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Jeep&model=Renegade&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 101, "Control and Displays." If the malfunction indicator lamps illuminate at incorrect times, the driver may be unaware when a true malfunction occurs, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will update the Instrument Panel Control software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V131000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Engine](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Jeep&model=Renegade&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** An engine stall can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the oil pump for its production information, and replace the BSM as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2017 Jeep Renegade has personality and capability, but reliability concerns require due diligence from prospective buyers. Pay close attention during test drives for any signs of transmission hesitation, rough shifting, or electrical anomalies. Securing a comprehensive pre-purchase inspection from an independent mechanic is strongly advised before finalizing a purchase.
