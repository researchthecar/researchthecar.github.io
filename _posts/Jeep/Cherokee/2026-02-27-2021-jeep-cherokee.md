# 2021 Jeep Cherokee Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2021 Jeep Cherokee offers distinctive styling, respectable off-road capability in Trailhawk trim, and a comfortable ride. However, used car buyers should approach with caution due to notable owner complaints concerning powertrain components, particularly involving the Power Transfer Unit (PTU) and rear differential. While many owners enjoy daily driving, mechanical vigilance and a thorough pre-purchase inspection are strongly advised.

---

## Engine

### 2.4L Tigershark 4-Cylinder

The base 2.4-liter Tigershark four-cylinder engine produces 180 horsepower and 171 lb-ft of torque. It provides adequate power for daily commuting, though it can feel strained under heavy acceleration.

**Engine Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle may abruptly lose power or stall while driving, accompanied by warning lights.
- **Solution:** Have a certified mechanic diagnose fuel delivery, sensors, or powertrain control modules.

### 3.2L Pentastar V6

The available 3.2-liter Pentastar V6 engine generates 271 horsepower and 239 lb-ft of torque, offering smoother power delivery and a higher towing capacity. It is generally regarded as a robust upgrade for highway driving and towing.

### 2.0L Turbocharged 4-Cylinder

The turbocharged 2.0-liter four-cylinder engine puts out 270 horsepower and 295 lb-ft of torque, combining strong low-end acceleration with respectable efficiency. It is a popular option for buyers seeking pep without stepping up to the V6.

## Transmission

### 9-Speed Automatic Transmission

Paired across all engine options, the 9-speed automatic transmission manages gear ratios to optimize fuel economy. Owners have reported occasional hesitation, rough shifting, and significant mechanical failures related to the all-wheel-drive system's transfer case.

**Power Transfer Unit (PTU) and Drivetrain Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Sudden loss of motive power, transmission slipping, or display of a 'Service 4WD' warning message.
- **Solution:** Inspect the PTU for leaks or internal failure; replacement or rebuild may be necessary.
- **Cost:** $3,186.02

### Rear Differential

The rear differential transfers power to the rear wheels in 4WD models. Owner complaints indicate susceptibility to physical cracking and mechanical binding during tight low-speed maneuvers.

**Rear Differential Binding and Cracking**
- **Symptoms:** Grinding, binding, wheel hop, or jerky movement during low-speed turns from a stop.
- **Solution:** Inspect differential fluid and housing for physical cracks or internal wear; replace components as needed.

## Suspension

### Control Arms

The front suspension relies on lower control arms to maintain wheel alignment and ride quality. Premature wear can affect handling stability.

**Lower Control Arm Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Premature wear or clunking noises from the front suspension during steering or over bumps.
- **Solution:** Inspect control arm bushings and ball joints, and replace worn suspension assemblies.

## Electrical

### Infotainment and Electronics

The vehicle's electrical architecture controls vital convenience and safety features, including the Uconnect infotainment system and driver assistance modules.

**Screen Blanking and Camera Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The central display goes black, freezes, or becomes pixelated, impacting the backup camera and climate controls.
- **Solution:** Reset the infotainment system or update software; persistent hardware failures may require screen replacement.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V310000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission:Cooler/Radiator:Hoses And Fittings](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Jeep&model=Cherokee&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** A transmission fluid leak increases the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the hose, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V657000: Suspension:Front:Control Arm:Lower Arm](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Jeep&model=Cherokee&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** A front lower control arm fracture while driving may result in a loss of steering control, increasing the risk of a crash without prior warning.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the front lower control arms and replace them as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V516000: Air Bags:Side/Window:Curtain](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Jeep&model=Cherokee&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** An improperly secured connector for the right-side curtain air bag may prevent the air bag from deploying as intended. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard numbers 214, "Side Impact Protection" and 226, "Ejection Mitigation." - **What to do:** Dealers will secure the connector, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V290000: Power Train](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Jeep&model=Cherokee&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** A failed PTU may result in a loss of drive power or vehicle rollaway, increasing the risk of a crash or injury.

## Summary Verdict

The 2021 Jeep Cherokee brings versatile capability and a comfortable interior to the compact SUV market, but prospective buyers should proceed with care. Due to documented owner reports involving Power Transfer Unit failures, rear differential binding, and electrical glitches, a comprehensive pre-purchase inspection by an independent mechanic is essential. Verifying service records and checking for completed powertrain recalls will help mitigate unexpected repair expenses.
