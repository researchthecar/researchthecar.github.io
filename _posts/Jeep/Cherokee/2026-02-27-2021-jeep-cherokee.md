# 2021 Jeep Cherokee

### Overview
The 2021 Jeep Cherokee, while offering a range of powertrain options and a capable platform, presents a mixed reliability picture based on available data. Owners generally appreciate its off-road prowess and comfortable interior. However, certain powertrain configurations and specific electronic components have been noted for potential issues. This analysis focuses on documented technical problems beyond routine maintenance, aiming to provide a clear understanding of potential reliability concerns for prospective buyers and current owners. The data reflects common complaints and repair trends observed in the automotive repair industry for this model year.

---


## Engine

### 2.0L Turbo I4
**Turbocharger Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of power, whistling noises, increased oil consumption.
- **Solution:** Turbocharger replacement or repair.
- **Cost:** $1,200 - $2,500

**Coolant Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Overheating, visible coolant puddles under the vehicle, sweet smell.
- **Solution:** Repair or replace leaking hoses, gaskets, or water pump.
- **Cost:** $300 - $800


### 3.2L V6 Pentastar
**Oil Cooler Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Coolant loss, oil in coolant, overheating.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the oil cooler assembly.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1,000

**Carbon Buildup**
- **Symptoms:** Rough idle, reduced engine performance, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Engine intake cleaning service.
- **Cost:** $200 - $400


## Transmission

### 9-Speed Automatic (948TE)
**Harsh Shifting / Jerking**
- **Symptoms:** Erratic gear changes, clunking noises, hesitation.
- **Solution:** Software update, transmission fluid flush and fill, or internal transmission repair.
- **Cost:** $300 - $1,500+

**Transmission Slipping**
- **Symptoms:** Engine revs but vehicle doesn't accelerate, delayed engagement.
- **Solution:** Transmission repair or replacement.
- **Cost:** $2,000 - $4,000+

### Infotainment System
**Screen Freezing / Unresponsiveness**

### Battery Drain
**Intermittent Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Dead battery after sitting, warning lights on dashboard.

### Summary Verdict
The 2021 Jeep Cherokee exhibits some common concerns, particularly with its 9-speed automatic transmission, which can experience shifting issues and, in some cases, slipping. Engine-wise, both the 2.0L Turbo and 3.2L V6 have reported issues like turbocharger problems and coolant leaks, respectively. Electrical gremlins, such as infotainment system unresponsiveness and intermittent battery drain, are also noted. While the suspension, exterior, and interior generally hold up well, potential buyers should be aware of the powertrain and electrical system vulnerabilities. Regular maintenance and prompt attention to early symptoms are crucial for mitigating these potential problems and ensuring long-term reliability.
