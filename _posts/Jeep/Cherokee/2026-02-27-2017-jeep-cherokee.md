# 2017 Jeep Cherokee Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2017 Jeep Cherokee offers a comfortable ride, decent off-road capabilities in higher trims, and a practical interior, making it an appealing option for compact SUV shoppers. However, prospective buyers should approach with caution due to a significant volume of owner complaints centered around electrical glitches, shifter behavior, and powertrain reliability. Reviewing service records and confirming that safety updates have been performed is essential before purchasing.

---

## Engine

### 2.4L Tigershark 4-Cylinder Engine

The base 2.4-liter Tigershark four-cylinder engine produces approximately 184 horsepower and 171 lb-ft of torque, providing adequate power for daily commuting. While generally fuel-efficient, owners have reported occasional hesitation and unexpected stalling incidents.

**Engine Stalling and Loss of Power**
- **Symptoms:** The engine may unexpectedly stall or shut down while driving at various speeds, occasionally accompanied by warning lights or a failure to respond to the accelerator.
- **Solution:** Requires professional diagnosis, which may involve checking fuel delivery, throttle body operation, or software calibrations.

### 3.2L Pentastar V6 Engine

The optional 3.2-liter Pentastar V6 engine delivers a smoother and more robust 271 horsepower and 239 lb-ft of torque, making it the preferred choice for towing and highway passing. It offers solid overall mechanical durability when properly maintained.

## Transmission

### 9-Speed Automatic Transmission

Equipped with a 9-speed automatic transmission across all trims, the Cherokee aims to balance highway efficiency with responsive gearing. However, the associated all-wheel-drive and Power Transfer Unit systems have been a frequent source of owner complaints regarding mechanical failure and loss of drive power.

**Power Transfer Unit (PTU) and Drivetrain Disengagement**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may experience sudden loss of propulsion, loud grinding noises, severe shaking, or an inability to engage park or drive due to internal component wear.
- **Solution:** Inspection and potential replacement or software updates for the Power Transfer Unit (PTU) as specified by manufacturer service campaigns.

### Shifter and Indicator Module

The electrical system controls critical cabin convenience features as well as electronic gear selection. Intermittent communication faults within the shifter module are a well-documented nuisance for owners.

**Service Shifter Warning and Illuminated Display**
- **Symptoms:** The dashboard displays a 'Service Shifter' warning message while all the indicator lights on the gear selector simultaneously illuminate red, potentially restricting normal instrument cluster displays.
- **Solution:** Inspect the wiring harness, shifter module, and related electrical connections for faults, often requiring component replacement or software updates.

## Brakes

### Electronic Parking Brake

Safety and driver-assistance features help protect occupants, though electronic parking brake malfunctions have occasionally caused unexpected driving interruptions.

**Unintended Parking Brake Engagement**
- **Symptoms:** The electronic emergency parking brake may unexpectedly engage while the vehicle is in motion or upon shutting off the vehicle, leaving the driver temporarily stranded.
- **Solution:** Inspect the parking brake module, wheel speed sensors, and actuator switches for electrical glitches or diagnostic trouble codes.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V343000: Power Train:Driveline:Differential Unit](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Jeep&model=Cherokee&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** Relative movement in the Power Transfer Unit (PTU) between the differential input splines and the transmission output shaft may cause some input spline teeth to wear off, which may eventually cause a loss of engagement between the transmission and the differential inside the PTU. A loss of drive can cause a vehicle crash without prior warning.
- **What to do:** FCA US LLC will notify owners, and dealers will perform a software update that will maintain vehicle propulsion by engaging rear wheel drive and prevent rolling in Park by activating the electronic parking brake if a failed input spline. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V332000: Electrical System:Wiring](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Jeep&model=Cherokee&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** These vehicles are being recalled to address a defect that could prevent the cruise control system from disengaging. If, when using cruise control, there is a short circuit within the vehicle's wiring, the driver may not be able to shut off the cruise control either by depressing the brake pedal or manually turning the system off once it has been engaged.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the software, and perform a software flash on the engine or powertrain control module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V543000: Equipment:Other:Labels](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Jeep&model=Cherokee&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 110, "Tire Selection and Rims." The missing tire and rim designation information may lead to an incorrect combination being installed on the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 16V799000: Air Bags:Knee Bolster](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Jeep&model=Cherokee&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** If the driver's knee air bag does not properly inflate, there is an increased risk of injury in the event of a crash.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will replace the driver's knee air bag module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V302000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Jeep&model=Cherokee&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** The power transfer unit (PTU) may become damaged and disengage the transmission and differential, resulting in a loss of drive power or loss of park function. A loss of drive power can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** FCA US LLC will notify owners, and dealers will perform a software update, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V011000: Power Train](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Jeep&model=Cherokee&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** The power transfer unit (PTU) may become damaged and disengage the transmission and differential, resulting in a loss of drive power and/or loss of park function. A loss of drive power can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the drivetrain control module and PTU software and replace the PTU, as necessary free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2017 Jeep Cherokee presents a mixed proposition for used-car buyers, combining daily versatility with notable mechanical and electrical pain points. While the V6 engine and comfortable ride quality draw positive marks, prospective owners should thoroughly verify the service history, check for outstanding safety campaigns regarding the powertrain, and test all electrical and shifter functions prior to purchase.
