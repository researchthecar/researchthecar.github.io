# 2016 Jeep Cherokee Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2016 Jeep Cherokee, buyers will find a compact SUV that offers distinct styling and capable off-road performance. However, owners have reported various mechanical and electrical concerns that prospective buyers should keep in mind. Paying close attention to the powertrain, transmission behavior, and electrical systems during a test drive can help identify potential problem areas before making a purchase.

---

## Engine

### 2.4L Tigershark 4-Cylinder

The 2.4-liter Tigershark four-cylinder engine produces approximately 184 horsepower and 171 lb-ft of torque, serving as the standard powerplant for base trims. While adequate for daily commuting, it has been associated with owner complaints concerning stalling and electrical warning lights.

**Engine Stalling and Loss of Power**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers experience unexpected engine stalling while driving, accompanied by warning lights and a sudden loss of motive power.
- **Solution:** Diagnose fault codes, inspect electronic throttle bodies, and update powertrain control modules as needed.

### 3.2L Pentastar V6

The 3.2-liter Pentastar V6 engine generates 271 horsepower and 239 lb-ft of torque, providing stronger acceleration and a higher towing capacity. It is generally paired with upper trim levels and four-wheel-drive systems.

## Transmission

### 9-Speed Automatic Transmission

The nine-speed automatic transmission aims to optimize highway fuel efficiency but has drawn criticism from owners regarding rough engagement and PTU durability.

**Shifting Delays and Power Transfer Unit (PTU) Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers note harsh shifting, hesitation during acceleration, grinding noises from the rear, and illumination of transmission warning lights.
- **Solution:** Inspect the Power Transfer Unit (PTU) for fluid leaks or internal wear, and perform software recalibrations or transmission fluid services.

### Shifter and Warning Indicators

Electrical architecture governs numerous convenience and safety features, with electronic shifter integration being a frequent source of owner grievances.

**Service Shifter and Park Defects**
- **Symptoms:** Messages like 'Service Shifter' appear on the instrument cluster, and the vehicle may occasionally fail to recognize when it is placed in park.
- **Solution:** Inspect shifter module wiring harnesses and replace faulty electronic gear selector assemblies.

## Brakes

### Electronic Parking Brake

Braking components provide stopping power, though electronic parking brake malfunctions have occasionally compromised vehicle control.

**Inadvertent Parking Brake Engagement**
- **Symptoms:** The electronic parking brake engages unexpectedly while driving or prevents the vehicle from moving after being parked.
- **Solution:** Check wheel speed sensors, update parking brake module software, and inspect brake actuator assemblies.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V332000: Electrical System:Wiring](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Jeep&model=Cherokee&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** These vehicles are being recalled to address a defect that could prevent the cruise control system from disengaging. If, when using cruise control, there is a short circuit within the vehicle's wiring, the driver may not be able to shut off the cruise control either by depressing the brake pedal or manually turning the system off once it has been engaged.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the software, and perform a software flash on the engine or powertrain control module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 15V826000: Electrical System:Wiring: Rear Compartment/Trunk](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Jeep&model=Cherokee&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** In the affected vehicles, water may leak into the Power Liftgate Control Module and result in a high resistance short circuit. A short circuit in the module increases the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the module and connectors for corrosion, replacing the damaged components as necessary. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V824000: Equipment](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Jeep&model=Cherokee&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** Chrysler (FCA US LLC) is recalling various Dodge, Chrysler, and RAM vehicles equipped with Kidde Plastic-Handle or Push Button 'Pindicator' Fire Extinguishers. These extinguishers may become clogged, preventing the extinguisher from discharging as expected or requiring excessive force to activate the extinguisher.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 16V590000: Seats](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Jeep&model=Cherokee&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** The seat fasteners on the first or second row seats may not have been tightened to the proper torque specification. Loose fasteners may allow the seats to move, increasing the risk of injury to the seat occupants in the event of a crash.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will replace the seats or seat sets, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V287000: Power Train:Axle Assembly:Axle Shaft](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Jeep&model=Cherokee&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** A fractured halfshaft may disconnect from the drive axle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will replace the left halfshaft, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V284000: Power Train:Axle Assembly:Axle Shaft](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Jeep&model=Cherokee&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** A fractured halfshaft may disconnect from the drive axle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will replace the right halfshaft, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V343000: Power Train:Driveline:Differential Unit](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Jeep&model=Cherokee&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** Relative movement in the Power Transfer Unit (PTU) between the differential input splines and the transmission output shaft may cause some input spline teeth to wear off, which may eventually cause a loss of engagement between the transmission and the differential inside the PTU. A loss of drive can cause a vehicle crash without prior warning.
- **What to do:** FCA US LLC will notify owners, and dealers will perform a software update that will maintain vehicle propulsion by engaging rear wheel drive and prevent rolling in Park by activating the electronic parking brake if a failed input spline. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V302000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Jeep&model=Cherokee&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** The power transfer unit (PTU) may become damaged and disengage the transmission and differential, resulting in a loss of drive power or loss of park function. A loss of drive power can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** FCA US LLC will notify owners, and dealers will perform a software update, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2016 Jeep Cherokee offers versatile utility and comfortable ride dynamics, but used car buyers should approach with careful inspection. Potential purchasers should thoroughly evaluate transmission shift quality, test all electronic features including the shifter and parking brake, and review service history records for prior powertrain repairs.
