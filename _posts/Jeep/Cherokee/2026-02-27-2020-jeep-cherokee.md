# 2020 Jeep Cherokee Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2020 Jeep Cherokee offers a comfortable ride and capable off-road performance, making it an appealing compact SUV for daily driving and weekend adventures. However, prospective buyers should thoroughly evaluate its mechanical and electrical history. Owner reports to federal safety regulators highlight recurring concerns regarding drivetrain components, steering assistance, and electrical systems that warrant a careful pre-purchase inspection.

---

## Engine

### 2.4L Tigershark 4-Cylinder

The base 2.4-liter Tigershark four-cylinder engine produces 180 horsepower and 171 lb-ft of torque, providing adequate power for routine commuting. While generally dependable, high-mileage examples require monitoring for oil consumption and internal wear.

**Engine Misfire and Low Compression**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle runs rough, fails to idle cleanly, and can exhibit low compression in specific cylinders.
- **Solution:** Diagnose cylinder health, inspect spark plugs and coils, and perform engine repairs or rebuilds as necessary.

### 3.2L Pentastar V6

The available 3.2-liter Pentastar V6 engine delivers 271 horsepower and 239 lb-ft of torque, offering stronger acceleration and a higher towing capacity. It is a proven powerplant within the Jeep lineup, though regular maintenance is essential for longevity.

### 2.0L Turbocharged 4-Cylinder

The 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine generates 270 horsepower and 295 lb-ft of torque, combining brisk acceleration with competitive low-end pulling power. Buyers should ensure proper turbo cool-down habits and consistent oil change intervals have been maintained.

## Transmission

### 9-Speed Automatic Transmission

Paired across all engine options, the nine-speed automatic transmission manages power delivery to either front-wheel drive or four-wheel drive systems. While later iterations are refined, some owners report shift delays, rough engagement, or unusual noise over time.

**Rough Shifting and Gear Slippage**
- **Symptoms:** Transmission upshifts roughly during acceleration, jerks forward abruptly, or slips between intermediate gears like 3rd and 4th.
- **Solution:** Update transmission control modules, check fluid condition, or repair internal components if mechanical wear is present.

### Power Transfer Unit (PTU) and Drivetrain

The active drive systems and power transfer units route torque to the rear wheels when traction slips, but internal mechanical failures can occur and require costly component replacement.

**Power Transfer Unit Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Illumination of the 'Service 4WD' warning light, accompanied by grinding noises from the drivetrain and loss of four-wheel drive capability.
- **Solution:** Replace the failing power transfer unit assembly.

## Brakes

### Electronic Parking Brake

The electronic parking brake system secures the vehicle on inclines automatically, but software glitches or sensor errors can trigger unexpected activations while driving.

**Inadvertent Parking Brake Engagement**
- **Symptoms:** The electronic parking brake randomly engages while the vehicle is in motion, causing sudden deceleration or stalling.
- **Solution:** Inspect brake actuators, software modules, and wiring harnesses for faults.

## Steering

### Power Steering and Column Module

The electric power steering system provides light, adjustable feedback for parking and highway driving, though occasional electronic or mechanical assist failures have been reported by owners.

**Loss of Power Steering Assist**
- **Symptoms:** Sudden loss of power steering functionality while maneuvering at low speeds, requiring heavy steering effort.
- **Solution:** Replace the power steering rack and pinion assembly along with associated clock springs or control modules.

## Electrical

### Electrical System and Battery

Integrated electronics and stop-start battery configurations manage multiple vehicle accessories, though electrical issues and premature auxiliary battery wear can lead to unexpected stalling.

**Engine Shut-Off and Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle stalls or shuts off independently while driving, accompanied by blank instrument clusters and auxiliary warning lights.
- **Solution:** Test the primary and auxiliary batteries, check bus connections, and replace failing power supply components.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V191000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Jeep&model=Cherokee&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 111, "Rearview Mirrors." The lingering rearview image can distract the driver, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Chrysler has notified owners, and dealers will update the radio display software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V637000: Steering:Rack And Pinion:Rack](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Jeep&model=Cherokee&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** A loss of steering control increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will replace the steering gear, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V290000: Power Train](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Jeep&model=Cherokee&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** A failed PTU may result in a loss of drive power or vehicle rollaway, increasing the risk of a crash or injury.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

When shopping for a used 2020 Jeep Cherokee, buyers should prioritize a comprehensive test drive and a pre-purchase inspection that specifically evaluates the transmission operation, power transfer unit condition, and electronic systems. While many vehicles provide dependable service, verifying that all four-wheel-drive functions and safety features operate correctly will help avoid costly out-of-pocket repairs after purchase.
