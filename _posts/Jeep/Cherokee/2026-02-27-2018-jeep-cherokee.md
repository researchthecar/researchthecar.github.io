# 2018 Jeep Cherokee Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2018 Jeep Cherokee offers distinctive styling and capable off-road trims, making it a tempting choice for compact SUV shoppers. However, prospective buyers should approach with caution due to a notable history of powertrain, electrical, and shifter-related complaints reported to safety authorities.

---

## Engine

### 2.4L Tigershark 4-Cylinder

The base 2.4-liter Tigershark four-cylinder engine produces 180 horsepower and 171 lb-ft of torque, offering adequate power for daily commuting but modest acceleration.

### 3.2L Pentastar V6

The available 3.2-liter Pentastar V6 engine generates 271 horsepower and 239 lb-ft of torque, providing stronger towing capability and smoother highway passing performance.

**Engine Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may suddenly shut off while driving at various speeds accompanied by dashboard warning lights, with subsequent restarts failing or requiring multiple attempts.
- **Solution:** Requires diagnostic testing of electrical and fuel delivery systems, software updates, or replacement of failing engine management sensors.

## Transmission

### 9-Speed Automatic Transmission

The ZF-sourced nine-speed automatic transmission is paired with front-wheel drive or four-wheel drive systems, though early production versions have faced durability concerns.

**Power Transfer Unit (PTU) Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers experience a loss of four-wheel drive capability, intermittent check engine lights, failure to shift properly, and grinding noises from the drivetrain.
- **Solution:** Inspection and potential replacement of the Power Transfer Unit (PTU) assembly or internal components.

### Shifter Assembly and Electronics

Cabin electronics and shifter interfaces manage essential gear selection and driver displays, but sensor and wiring glitches can prompt persistent warning messages.

**Shifter or Park Defect**
- **Symptoms:** Illumination of all gear lights on the shifter bezel, warning messages on the instrument cluster, and intermittent failure to properly register park mode.
- **Solution:** Inspect wiring harnesses, update transmission control modules, or replace faulty shifter bezels and internal wiring components.

## Brakes

### Electronic Parking Brake

The braking system utilizes hydraulic service brakes paired with an electronic parking brake actuator, which occasionally experiences locking or failure issues.

**Parking Brake Binding**
- **Symptoms:** The electronic parking brake unexpectedly engages or freezes while driving or parked, illuminating service brake messages and preventing vehicle movement.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis of the electronic brake control module, brake actuators, and rear caliper motors.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V282000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Hoses, Lines/Piping, And Fittings](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Jeep&model=Cherokee&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** A fuel leak in the presence of the ignition source can increase the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the fuel tube, replacing it as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V494000: Power Train:Axle Assembly:Axle Shaft](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Jeep&model=Cherokee&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** The bearing cage for the right front halfshaft assembly may not have been properly heat treated, possibly resulting in the bearing cage breaking and a potential halfshaft assembly failure. If the halfshaft bearing cage breaks, the halfshaft may not be able to transmit engine power, causing a loss of drive or it can allow the vehicle to move while in the.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will replace the front right halfshaft assemblies, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V332000: Electrical System:Wiring](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Jeep&model=Cherokee&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** These vehicles are being recalled to address a defect that could prevent the cruise control system from disengaging. If, when using cruise control, there is a short circuit within the vehicle's wiring, the driver may not be able to shut off the cruise control either by depressing the brake pedal or manually turning the system off once it has been engaged.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the software, and perform a software flash on the engine or powertrain control module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V543000: Equipment:Other:Labels](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Jeep&model=Cherokee&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 110, "Tire Selection and Rims." The missing tire and rim designation information may lead to an incorrect combination being installed on the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 18V524000: Electrical System:Wiring:Front Underhood](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Jeep&model=Cherokee&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** A vehicle stall can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will replace the powertrain control module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V670000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Engine](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Jeep&model=Cherokee&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** If the oil pump fails, the engine will stall, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the oil pump, replacing it as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V011000: Power Train](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Jeep&model=Cherokee&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** The power transfer unit (PTU) may become damaged and disengage the transmission and differential, resulting in a loss of drive power and/or loss of park function. A loss of drive power can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the drivetrain control module and PTU software and replace the PTU, as necessary free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

While the 2018 Jeep Cherokee provides comfort and versatile capability, shoppers should carefully inspect the vehicle history for unresolved Power Transfer Unit (PTU) or electrical complaints. A thorough pre-purchase inspection focusing on the transmission, PTU, and electronic shifter operation is strongly recommended before buying.
