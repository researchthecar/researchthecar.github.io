# 2018 Jeep Cherokee

### Overview
The 2018 Jeep Cherokee, while offering a range of powertrain options and a capable chassis, presents a mixed reliability profile. Owners have reported a number of recurring issues, particularly concerning the powertrain and electrical systems. The available engines, including the 2.4L Tigershark and the 3.2L Pentastar V6, have seen some specific problems, as have the transmissions, especially the 9-speed automatic. While the suspension and exterior generally hold up well, electrical gremlins and interior component wear can detract from the ownership experience. This analysis focuses on documented widespread issues beyond routine maintenance.

---


## Engine

### 2.4L Tigershark MultiAir2
**Excessive Oil Consumption**
- **Symptoms:** Low oil level warnings, oil burning smell, visible smoke from exhaust.
- **Solution:** Engine oil consumption test, potential piston ring replacement or engine rebuild.
- **Cost:** $1,500 - $3,000

**Coolant Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Overheating, visible coolant puddles under the vehicle, sweet smell.
- **Solution:** Inspection and replacement of leaking hoses, water pump, or radiator.
- **Cost:** $300 - $800


### 3.2L Pentastar V6
**Oil Leaks (Valve Cover Gaskets)**
- **Symptoms:** Oil smell, visible oil on engine components, potential for oil to drip onto exhaust manifold.
- **Solution:** Replacement of valve cover gaskets.
- **Cost:** $400 - $700


## Transmission

### 948TE 9-Speed Automatic
**Harsh Shifting / Jerking**
- **Symptoms:** Sudden jolts or rough gear changes, hesitation during acceleration or deceleration.
- **Solution:** Software updates, transmission fluid flush and refill, potential internal component repair.
- **Cost:** $500 - $2,500

**Transmission Slipping**
- **Symptoms:** Engine revs up but vehicle speed does not increase proportionally, difficulty engaging gears.
- **Solution:** Transmission fluid check and top-up, potential internal transmission repair or replacement.
- **Cost:** $1,000 - $4,000

## Electrical

### Infotainment System
**Uconnect System Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Screen freezing, unresponsive touch controls, Bluetooth connectivity issues, audio problems.
- **Solution:** Software updates, system reset, potential replacement of head unit.
- **Cost:** $100 - $1,200


### Battery Drain
**Premature Battery Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle fails to start, battery warning light, electrical components malfunctioning.
- **Solution:** Diagnostic testing to identify parasitic draw, battery replacement.
- **Cost:** $200 - $500

### Summary Verdict
The 2018 Jeep Cherokee exhibits moderate reliability concerns, with the powertrain and electrical systems being the primary areas of reported issues. The 2.4L engine's oil consumption and the 9-speed automatic transmission's shifting problems are notable. Electrical issues, particularly with the Uconnect system and battery drain, also require attention. While the suspension, exterior, and interior components are generally less problematic, potential buyers should be aware of these common faults. Regular maintenance and prompt attention to early warning signs are crucial for mitigating repair costs and ensuring a more dependable ownership experience.
