# 2019 Jeep Cherokee Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2019 Jeep Cherokee, buyers will find a stylish and capable compact SUV that offers impressive off-road prowess, especially in Trailhawk trims. However, ownership reports indicate that potential buyers should exercise caution. A high volume of owner complaints highlights recurring mechanical and electrical concerns—most notably involving engine stalling, unexpected loss of motive power, and power transfer unit (PTU) failures. Thoroughly inspecting the vehicle's maintenance history and drivetrain functionality is strongly recommended before purchasing.

---

## Engine

### 2.4L Tigershark 4-Cylinder Engine

The base 2.4-liter Tigershark four-cylinder engine produces approximately 180 horsepower and 171 lb-ft of torque. It provides adequate power for daily commuting, though owners have reported occasional reliability hiccups related to stalling and electrical warnings.

**Engine Stalling and Loss of Power**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle may suddenly stall while driving, at a complete stop, or when utilizing the automatic start-stop system, accompanied by various warning lights on the dashboard.
- **Solution:** Diagnostic testing by a qualified technician to check for software updates, sensor faults, or fuel delivery issues.

### 3.2L Pentastar V6 Engine

The available 3.2-liter Pentastar V6 engine generates 271 horsepower and 239 lb-ft of torque, offering stronger acceleration and a higher towing capacity. While generally robust, some owners have experienced major drivetrain and stalling incidents.

**Engine Failure and Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** Sudden loss of motive power while underway, complete engine shutdown, or inability to restart after stopping.
- **Solution:** Professional mechanical inspection; severe cases may require extensive engine repair or total replacement.

### 2.0L Turbocharged 4-Cylinder Engine

The 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine outputs 270 horsepower and 295 lb-ft of torque, combining brisk acceleration with efficient cruising. It represents a punchy upgrade for drivers seeking stronger low-end torque.

## Transmission

### 9-Speed Automatic Transmission

Paired across most trims, the 9-speed automatic transmission aims to balance highway efficiency with responsive gearing. Some drivers report rough shift quality and hesitation as the transmission hunts for the correct gear.

**Harsh Shifting and Drivability Concerns**
- **Symptoms:** Transmission may shift abruptly or lurch violently around highway or city speeds, sometimes feeling like a rear-end collision.
- **Solution:** Reprogramming or updating the powertrain control module, or inspecting internal transmission components for wear.

### Power Transfer Unit (PTU) and Four-Wheel Drive

The Jeep Cherokee's available four-wheel-drive systems rely on a Power Transfer Unit to distribute torque to the rear wheels. PTU wear and internal component failure represent one of the most frequently cited mechanical complaints for this model year.

**Power Transfer Unit Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Illumination of the 'Service 4WD' warning light, grinding or slipping noises during acceleration, and total loss of motive power.
- **Solution:** Inspection and replacement of the failing Power Transfer Unit (PTU) or associated drivetrain components.
- **Cost:** $3,000 to $4,600

## Brakes

### Brake System and Electronic Parking Brake

The service brakes and electronic parking brake systems generally provide reliable stopping power, though occasional electrical glitches can trigger unwanted emergency braking events.

**Unintended Parking Brake Engagement**
- **Symptoms:** Emergency brake system unexpectedly engages while driving, causing the vehicle to brake abruptly or stall in traffic.
- **Solution:** Inspect electronic brake actuators, wheel speed sensors, and related control modules.

## Electrical

### Infotainment and Accessory Electronics

The interior electronics manage cabin climate, audio, and safety display features. Owners occasionally report stubborn infotainment glitches and camera malfunctions that require software intervention.

**Infotainment Malfunction and Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Infotainment display screen freezing, failure of the backup camera display, and parasitic battery drain.
- **Solution:** Software updates for the Uconnect system or replacement of faulty display modules.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V917000: Electrical System: Instrument Cluster/Panel](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Jeep&model=Cherokee&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** Without the activation of warning chimes, messages, and gauges to provide vehicle information to the driver, there would be an increased risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Chrysler has notified owners, and dealers will inspect and, as necessary, replace the instrument cluster, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V492000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Jeep&model=Cherokee&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** A component in the transmission may not have been welded properly, possibly causing the transmission to not transmit engine power to the wheels. If the transmission weld fails, the vehicle will stop moving, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will replace the transmission, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V739000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Jeep&model=Cherokee&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** A vehicle stall can increase the risk of crash.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will re-flash the transmission controller with a new calibration, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V449000: Air Bags](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Jeep&model=Cherokee&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** The impact and acceleration sensors may fail and reduce the air bag system's ability to properly detect certain crash events. In the event of a crash, the inability to properly detect a crash may result in improper air bag deployment, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will replace the affected sensors, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V637000: Steering:Rack And Pinion:Rack](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Jeep&model=Cherokee&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** A loss of steering control increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will replace the steering gear, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V344000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Engine](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Jeep&model=Cherokee&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** Cylinder damage or camshaft failure can cause the engine to stall, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will inspect and repair or replace the affected engine components, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V248000: Seats](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Jeep&model=Cherokee&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** In the event of a crash, the seat may move, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Chrysler has notified owners, and dealers will tighten the mounting bolts, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V524000: Electrical System:Wiring:Front Underhood](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Jeep&model=Cherokee&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** A vehicle stall can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will replace the powertrain control module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2019 Jeep Cherokee is an appealing choice for buyers seeking daily comfort combined with genuine off-road capability. However, used car shoppers should proceed with caution and prioritize models with verified service records. Pay close attention to how the transmission shifts during a test drive, verify the operation of the four-wheel-drive system, and check whether relevant powertrain and PTU updates or repairs have been properly performed.
