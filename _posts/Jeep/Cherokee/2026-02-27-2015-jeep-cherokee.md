# 2015 Jeep Cherokee

### Overview
The 2015 Jeep Cherokee, while offering capable off-road performance and a comfortable ride, presented a mixed bag in terms of long-term reliability. Owners reported a range of issues, particularly concerning the powertrain and some electrical components. The available engine options, including the 2.4L Tigershark and the 3.2L Pentastar V6, each had their own set of potential problems. Similarly, the 9-speed automatic transmission, a significant departure from traditional automatics, was a frequent source of complaint. While the chassis and suspension generally held up well, attention to detail in other areas would have significantly improved the overall ownership experience for many.

---


## Engine

### 2.4L Tigershark MultiAir I4
**Oil Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Visible oil stains under the vehicle, low oil level warnings.
- **Solution:** Replacement of seals or gaskets, such as the valve cover gasket or oil pan gasket.
- **Cost:** $200 - $500

**Stalling/Rough Idling**
- **Symptoms:** Engine stalls unexpectedly, particularly at low speeds or idle; rough running.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and potential replacement of fuel injectors, spark plugs, or the MultiAir solenoid.
- **Cost:** $300 - $800


### 3.2L Pentastar V6
**Oil Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Visible oil stains under the vehicle, low oil level warnings.
- **Solution:** Replacement of seals or gaskets, such as the valve cover gasket or oil pan gasket.
- **Cost:** $200 - $500

**Coolant Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Low coolant levels, overheating, visible coolant puddles.
- **Solution:** Replacement of coolant hoses, water pump, or thermostat housing.
- **Cost:** $300 - $700


## Transmission

### 948TE 9-Speed Automatic
**Shuddering/Jerking**
- **Symptoms:** Rough shifting, hesitation, shuddering during acceleration or gear changes.
- **Solution:** Software updates, transmission fluid flush and fill, or internal transmission component repair/replacement.
- **Cost:** $500 - $2500

**Hesitation/Failure to Engage**
- **Symptoms:** Delay in gear engagement, transmission not engaging properly.
- **Solution:** Transmission control module (TCM) diagnosis and potential replacement, or internal transmission repair.
- **Cost:** $600 - $2000

## Electrical

### Body Control Module (BCM)
**Intermittent Electrical Failures**
- **Symptoms:** Various warning lights illuminating, power windows malfunctioning, infotainment system glitches.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and potential reprogramming or replacement of the BCM.
- **Cost:** $400 - $900


### Infotainment System
**Screen Freezing/Unresponsiveness**
- **Symptoms:** Touchscreen becomes unresponsive, system freezes or reboots randomly.
- **Solution:** Software updates or replacement of the infotainment unit.
- **Cost:** $300 - $1000

### Summary Verdict
The 2015 Jeep Cherokee is a vehicle that requires careful consideration regarding its reliability. While the core mechanical components like the suspension and exterior generally perform as expected, the powertrain, particularly the 9-speed automatic transmission and some engine-specific issues like oil leaks, are recurring concerns. Electrical gremlins, especially with the BCM and infotainment system, can also lead to frustrating and costly repairs. Potential buyers should be aware of these common problems and factor in potential maintenance costs beyond routine servicing. Thorough pre-purchase inspections are highly recommended.
