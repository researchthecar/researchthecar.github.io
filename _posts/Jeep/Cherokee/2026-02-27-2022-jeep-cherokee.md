# 2022 Jeep Cherokee

### Overview
The 2022 Jeep Cherokee presents a mixed bag in terms of technical reliability. While it offers capable performance and a comfortable ride, certain powertrain configurations and electronic systems have shown a propensity for issues. Owners have reported concerns primarily related to the engine and transmission, particularly with specific engine variants. The electrical system also appears to be a point of attention, with some users experiencing glitches. However, the exterior and interior components generally hold up well, with no widespread structural or material defects noted. Overall, the Cherokee's reliability is dependent on the specific configuration chosen, with some powertrains requiring more vigilance than others.

---


## Engine

### 2.0L Turbo I4
**Turbocharger Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of power, whistling noises, increased oil consumption.
- **Solution:** Turbocharger replacement.
- **Cost:** $1,200 - $2,500

**Coolant Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Overheating, visible coolant puddles, sweet smell.
- **Solution:** Repair or replace leaking hoses, gaskets, or water pump.
- **Cost:** $300 - $800


### 3.2L Pentastar V6
**Oil Cooler Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Coolant in oil, oil in coolant, overheating.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the oil cooler assembly.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1,000

**Intake Manifold Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Rough idle, check engine light, vacuum leaks.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the intake manifold.
- **Cost:** $400 - $700


## Transmission

### 9-Speed Automatic (948TE)
**Harsh Shifting / Jerking**
- **Symptoms:** Sudden jolts or rough gear changes, especially at lower speeds.
- **Solution:** Software update for transmission control module, or internal transmission repair.
- **Cost:** $150 - $1,500

**Transmission Slipping**
- **Symptoms:** Engine revs without acceleration, delayed engagement.
- **Solution:** Transmission fluid flush and filter replacement, or internal transmission rebuild/replacement.
- **Cost:** $500 - $3,500

## Electrical

### Infotainment System
**Screen Freezing / Unresponsiveness**
- **Symptoms:** Touchscreen becomes unresponsive, system freezes or reboots.
- **Solution:** Software update, or replacement of the infotainment unit.
- **Cost:** $200 - $1,200


### Battery Drain
**Intermittent Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Dead battery after sitting, warning lights on dashboard.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis of parasitic draw, repair or replacement of faulty module.
- **Cost:** $200 - $600

### Summary Verdict
The 2022 Jeep Cherokee offers a compelling package for many buyers, but potential owners should be aware of the reported reliability concerns, particularly with the 9-speed automatic transmission and certain engine options like the 2.0L Turbo. While the suspension, exterior, and interior components generally perform well, electrical gremlins, especially within the infotainment system, can be a source of frustration. Regular maintenance and prompt attention to any warning signs are crucial for mitigating potential repair costs and ensuring a more positive ownership experience. It is advisable to research specific trim levels and powertrain combinations for the most accurate reliability expectations.
