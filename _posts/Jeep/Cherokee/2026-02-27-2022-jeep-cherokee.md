# 2022 Jeep Cherokee Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2022 Jeep Cherokee offers a capable and comfortable ride with modern technology, but used-car buyers should carefully research reported issues before purchasing. While it provides solid utility and classic Jeep styling, owner feedback highlights specific mechanical concerns—particularly involving the powertrain and all-wheel-drive systems. Conducting a thorough pre-purchase inspection can help uncover any lingering electrical or mechanical quirks.

---

## Engine

### 2.4L Tigershark 4-Cylinder

The standard 2.4-liter naturally aspirated 4-cylinder engine produces around 180 horsepower and 171 lb-ft of torque. It provides adequate power for daily commuting, though overall performance is modest for the vehicle's weight.

### 3.2L Pentastar V6

The available 3.2-liter V6 engine generates 271 horsepower and 239 lb-ft of torque, offering stronger towing capability and smoother acceleration. It is generally robust, but buyers should monitor fluid levels and check for minor gasket leaks.

**Coolant Odor and Consumption**
- **Symptoms:** An occasional sweet smell of antifreeze inside or outside the vehicle, accompanied by low coolant levels requiring occasional topping off.
- **Solution:** Have a mechanic inspect oil cooler gaskets, hoses, and the intake manifold for leaks or hairline cracks.

## Transmission

### 9-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 9-speed automatic transmission routes power through front- or all-wheel-drive configurations. While generally smooth once moving, some owners have experienced mechanical issues tied to the all-wheel-drive Power Transfer Unit (PTU).

**Power Transfer Unit (PTU) Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Illuminated 4WD service lights, grinding or thumping noises from the front end, loss of 4WD capability, or complete loss of motive power.
- **Solution:** Take the vehicle to a qualified technician to diagnose internal PTU failure; replacement of the unit is often necessary if mechanical blockages occur.
- **Cost:** $5000

**Unexpected Shifting to Park**
- **Symptoms:** While coasting or lifting off the accelerator, the transmission may unexpectedly shift out of gear and display a 'Shift to Park' message on the dashboard.
- **Solution:** Bring the vehicle to a dealer or specialist to check for transmission control module software updates or mechanical selector issues.

## Suspension

### Front Struts

The suspension system delivers a comfortable, compliant ride quality suited for both highway cruising and light trail work. Front strut wear should be checked during a pre-purchase inspection.

**Premature Strut Failure**
- **Symptoms:** A noticeable rattling or knocking noise coming from the front suspension area when driving over rough roads or bumps.
- **Solution:** Inspect front struts for damage or fluid leaks, and replace faulty components under warranty or with new OEM assemblies.

## Electrical

### Charging System and Battery

Modern electronics and convenience features place a heavy demand on the electrical architecture. Battery health and charging performance should be verified on used models.

**Premature Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle's battery goes completely dead after being parked for a few days, sometimes preceded by intermittent charging faults.
- **Solution:** Test the charging system and replace the battery with a high-quality OEM unit if internal cell failure is detected.

## Interior

### Sun Visors

Cabin materials are generally comfortable and user-friendly, though occasional trim or hardware durability complaints can arise.

**Visor Mounting Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The passenger-side sun visor detaches or falls out of its mounting hole due to a weak retaining clip.
- **Solution:** Replace the broken visor clip assembly to restore secure operation.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V239000: Exterior Lighting:Turn Signal](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Jeep&model=Cherokee&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** The turn signal self-canceling feature may not function properly. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 108, "Lamps, Reflective Devices, and Associated Equipment." Turn signals that do not function properly may fail to indicate the driver's intention to change vehicle direction.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the steering column control module as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

- **Issue:** An improperly secured connector for the right-side curtain air bag may prevent the air bag from deploying as intended. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard numbers 214, "Side Impact Protection" and 226, "Ejection Mitigation." An air bag that does not deploy as intended can increase the risk of.
- **What to do:** Dealers will secure the connector, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V290000: Power Train](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Jeep&model=Cherokee&modelYear=2022)**

- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2022 Jeep Cherokee presents a mix of everyday practicality and potential mechanical considerations. Prospective buyers should pay close attention to the operation of the all-wheel-drive system and transmission during a test drive, and verify that all electronic features and suspension components function properly. A comprehensive pre-purchase inspection by a knowledgeable technician is strongly recommended.
