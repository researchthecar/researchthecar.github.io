# 2016 Jeep Grand Cherokee Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2016 Jeep Grand Cherokee offers a rugged design, comfortable ride, and impressive off-road capability, but prospective used-car buyers should review its reliability record closely. Owner feedback and federal safety data highlight several recurring mechanical and electrical concerns that require careful inspection before purchase.

---

## Engine

### 3.6L V6 (Pentastar)

The 3.6-liter Pentastar V6 produces around 295 horsepower and 260 lb-ft of torque, serving as the standard engine choice. It provides balanced daily performance and towing capability, though buyers should monitor for occasional valve train wear and misfire complaints.

**Engine Misfire**
- **Symptoms:** Illuminated check engine light, rough idle, and cylinder misfire codes such as P0302.
- **Solution:** Inspect ignition coils, spark plugs, and compression; replace faulty components or repair internal valve train wear as necessary.

**Engine Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** Unexpected engine shut-off while driving or idling, accompanied by warning lights and difficult restarts.
- **Solution:** Diagnose fuel delivery and electrical systems; replace faulty sensors or fuel pump components if found defective.

### 5.7L V8 (HEMI)

The 5.7-liter HEMI V8 generates 360 horsepower and 390 lb-ft of torque, offering robust towing capacity and strong acceleration. It is a popular choice for heavier hauling, though fuel economy is significantly lower than the V6.

### 3.0L V6 Turbo Diesel

The 3.0-liter EcoDiesel V6 delivers high torque and exceptional highway cruising range, though it requires diligent maintenance and can involve expensive specialized repairs out of warranty.

## Transmission

### TorqueFlite 8-Speed Automatic

The ZF-sourced 8-speed automatic transmission delivers smooth gear changes and optimizes fuel efficiency across all engine options. While generally robust, some owners report electronic shifter display issues and erratic engagement symptoms.

**Shifting and Parking Defects**
- **Symptoms:** Transmission stuck in gear, unexpected hard engagement or bucking when slowing down, or failure to register when shifted into Park.
- **Solution:** Update transmission control modules, inspect shifter linkages, or repair internal valve body components.

## Brakes

### Brake System

Brake performance is generally strong when maintained, but owners have reported occasional caliper seizing and overheating concerns that demand routine checks.

**Brake Dragging and Seizing**
- **Symptoms:** Brakes seizing repetitively, causing wheels to overheat, vehicle shaking during deceleration, and smoke from the wheel wells.
- **Solution:** Inspect brake calipers, slide pins, and the ABS module; replace seized calipers or hydraulic components.

## Steering

### Steering Gear and EPS

The steering system provides light and responsive handling for daily driving, though sudden electronic power steering failures have been documented by some drivers.

**Power Steering Loss**
- **Symptoms:** Power steering warning light illuminates and the steering wheel becomes excessively stiff and difficult to turn.
- **Solution:** Diagnose the electric power steering (EPS) module and steering gear rack; replace faulty rack assemblies if required.

## Exterior

### Exterior Lighting

electrical issues can affect lighting and accessory systems, requiring careful verification of all switches and fuses during a pre-purchase inspection.

**Headlight Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Headlights completely shutting off intermittently at night while high beams or fog lamps continue to function.
- **Solution:** Inspect headlight wiring harnesses, switches, and control modules; replace failed assemblies as needed.

## Interior

### Seats

Cabin features and ergonomics are generally praised, though the sporadic deployment of active head restraints remains a notable safety complaint among owners.

**Inadvertent Active Headrest Deployment**
- **Symptoms:** Front seat head restraints deploying unexpectedly while driving or sitting idle, potentially causing driver injury.
- **Solution:** Inspect and replace deployed headrest actuator assemblies.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V475000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Engine:Diesel](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Jeep&model=Grand+Cherokee&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** An engine stall can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** FCA US will notify owners, and dealers will update the powertrain control module software to maintain vehicle propulsion by reading the camshaft position signal in the event that the crankshaft position signal is lost, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V332000: Electrical System:Wiring](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Jeep&model=Grand+Cherokee&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** These vehicles are being recalled to address a defect that could prevent the cruise control system from disengaging. If, when using cruise control, there is a short circuit within the vehicle's wiring, the driver may not be able to shut off the cruise control either by depressing the brake pedal or manually turning the system off once it has been engaged.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the software, and perform a software flash on the engine or powertrain control module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V699000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Exhaust System:Emission Control:Gas Recirculation Valve (Egr Valve)](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Jeep&model=Grand+Cherokee&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** Combustion in the intake manifold can increase the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** FCA US LLC will notify owners, and dealers will replace the EGR cooler, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17E061000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Engine:Gasoline:Turbo/Supercharger](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Jeep&model=Grand+Cherokee&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** A brake fluid leak may lengthen the distance needed to stop the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** ATI will notify owners and will replace the supercharger brackets, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V814000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Hoses, Lines/Piping, And Fittings](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Jeep&model=Grand+Cherokee&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** A fuel leak in the presence of an ignition source, can increase the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the engine assembly for damage to the fuel rail crossover tube, replacing it as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V168000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Foundation Components:Disc:Caliper](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Jeep&model=Grand+Cherokee&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** A cracked brake caliper may lengthen the distance needed to stop the vehicle and increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the front left brake caliper and depending on its casting date, replace it, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V249000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission:Park/Neutral Start Interlock Switch](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Jeep&model=Grand+Cherokee&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** The brake transmission shift interlock solenoid may have a loose electrical connection which could result in a loss of solenoid function. If the solenoid loses function, the transmission may lock in the Park or Neutral position when the vehicle comes to a stop, possibly rendering the vehicle disabled in traffic, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will replace the shifter assembly, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V406000: Fuel System, Diesel:Delivery:Fuel Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Jeep&model=Grand+Cherokee&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** Fuel starvation may result in an unexpected loss of drive power, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the HPFP, and inspect and replace, if necessary, additional fuel system components, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2016 Jeep Grand Cherokee is a capable and comfortable SUV with appealing engine and transmission choices, but buyers should proceed with a thorough mechanical inspection. Pay close attention to potential electrical glitches, brake seizing, and transmission shifter operation to avoid unexpected repair costs.
