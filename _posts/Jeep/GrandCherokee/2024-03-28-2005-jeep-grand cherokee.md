**Introduction**

The 2005 Jeep Grand Cherokee is a midsize SUV known for its off-road capabilities and rugged exterior. It is available with a variety of engine and transmission options, including:

* 3.7L V6 engine (210 hp, 235 lb-ft torque)
* 4.7L V8 engine (235 hp, 305 lb-ft torque)
* 5.7L Hemi V8 engine (330 hp, 370 lb-ft torque)
* 4-speed automatic transmission
* 5-speed automatic transmission

Owners appreciate the Cherokee's powerful engines and comfortable interior, while some criticize its poor fuel economy and limited rear legroom.

**Engine**

**3.7L V6**
* **Oil consumption:** The engine may consume excessive amounts of oil, leading to low oil levels and potential engine damage.
    * Solution: Replace or repair the piston rings or valve seals.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1500 - $2500+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Timing chain noise:** A ticking or rattling noise may be heard from the engine, indicating a worn or damaged timing chain.
    * Solution: Replace the timing chain and tensioner.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800+
    * Recalls: None Found

**4.7L V8**
* **Manifold bolts:** The intake manifold bolts may break or loosen, causing a vacuum leak and reduced engine performance.
    * Solution: Replace the manifold bolts with updated versions.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
    * Recalls: None Found

**5.7L Hemi V8**
* **Valve seat failure:** The valve seats can become damaged or dislodge, leading to valve failure and catastrophic engine damage.
    * Solution: Replace the cylinder heads.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2500 - $4000+
    * Recalls: 05V377000

**Transmission**

**4-speed Automatic**
* **Shifting problems:** The transmission may fail to shift properly, causing rough shifts or slipping gears.
    * Solution: Replace the transmission solenoid pack or valve body.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**5-speed Automatic**
* **Torque converter shudder:** The torque converter may shudder or vibrate, especially during acceleration.
    * Solution: Replace the torque converter.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1000 - $1500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Sway bar links:** The sway bar links may become worn or damaged, causing clunking noises over bumps.
    * Solution: Replace the sway bar links.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Strut mount bearing failure:** The strut mount bearings may wear out prematurely, leading to excessive noise and handling issues.
    * Solution: Replace the strut mount bearings.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Power window failure:** The power window motors or switches may fail, causing the windows to become inoperable.
    * Solution: Replace the faulty motors or switches.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Ignition switch failure:** The ignition switch may fail, preventing the engine from starting.
    * Solution: Replace the ignition switch.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+
    * Recalls: 14V518000

**Exterior**

* **Headlight condensation:** Moisture can build up inside the headlight assemblies, causing condensation and reduced visibility.
    * Solution: Replace the headlight assemblies.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $600+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Climate control issues:** The climate control system may fail to distribute air evenly, resulting in uneven heating or cooling.
    * Solution: Inspect and repair or replace the blend door actuator.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Dashboard rattles:** The dashboard may develop rattles or squeaks due to loose components.
    * Solution: Disassemble and reassemble the dashboard to locate and tighten any loose parts.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2005 Jeep Grand Cherokee has some common issues that can range in severity and cost. The most serious issue is the valve seat failure on the 5.7L Hemi V8 engine, which can result in catastrophic engine damage. Other issues, such as oil consumption and electrical problems, are less severe but can still be costly to repair. Overall, the Cherokee is a reliable SUV, but potential buyers should be aware of these potential issues before making a purchase.
