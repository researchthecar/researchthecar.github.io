**2021 Jeep Grand Cherokee**

The 2021 Jeep Grand Cherokee is a midsize SUV that offers a variety of engine and transmission options. It is known for its off-road capabilities, spacious interior, and comfortable ride. However, like any vehicle, it has some common issues that owners have reported.

**Engine**

* **3.6L Pentastar V6**
    * **Intake manifold failure:** The intake manifold can crack, causing a loss of power and decreased fuel efficiency.
    * **Solution:** Replace intake manifold.
    * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $1,500 - $2,000+
* **5.7L HEMI V8**
    * **Cylinder head failure:** The cylinder heads can crack, causing coolant leaks and overheating.
    * **Solution:** Replace cylinder heads.
    * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $5,000 - $8,000+
    * **Recalls:** None Found

**Transmission**

* **8-speed automatic**
    * **Torque converter failure:** The torque converter can fail, causing loss of power and transmission slipping.
    * **Solution:** Replace torque converter.
    * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $2,000 - $3,000+
    * **Recalls:** NHTSA Campaign Numbers: 21V-874, 21V-884
* **10-speed automatic**
    * **Transmission control module failure:** The TCM can fail, causing shifting issues and transmission slipping.
    * **Solution:** Replace TCM.
    * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $1,000 - $2,000+
    * **Recalls:** None Found

**Suspension**

* **Front strut failure:** The front struts can leak or fail, causing a loss of stability and handling.
* **Solution:** Replace struts.
* **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $500 - $1,000+

**Electrical**

* **Body control module failure:** The BCM can fail, causing various electrical issues, such as loss of power windows, lights, and climate control.
* **Solution:** Replace BCM.
* **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $1,000 - $2,000+
* **Recalls:** NHTSA Campaign Number: 21V-031

**Exterior**

* **Tailgate handle failure:** The tailgate handle can break, making it difficult to open the tailgate.
* **Solution:** Replace tailgate handle.
* **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $100 - $200+

**Interior**

* **Headliner sagging:** The headliner can sag and detach from the roof.
* **Solution:** Reattach or replace headliner.
* **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $400 - $800+

**Summary**

The 2021 Jeep Grand Cherokee is a popular SUV with some common issues that owners have reported. The severity and cost of these issues can vary depending on the specific problem and the age and mileage of the vehicle. It is important to have regular maintenance and inspections performed to address any potential issues early on.
