# 2018 Jeep Grand Cherokee Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used midsize SUV, the model offers a compelling mix of rugged capability and daily comfort, but buyers should approach with a clear understanding of potential mechanical and electrical hiccups. While many owners enjoy its styling and performance, owner reports highlight specific problem areas ranging from infotainment glitches to engine and active headrest concerns. Thoroughly inspecting service records and testing all electronic and mechanical functions during a pre-purchase test drive will help ensure a reliable ownership experience.

---

## Engine

### 3.6L V6

The standard 3.6-liter Pentastar V6 engine produces approximately 295 horsepower and 260 lb-ft of torque, offering a balanced blend of everyday performance and towing capability. It is generally considered the most straightforward and dependable engine choice for daily commuting.

### 5.7L HEMI V8

The optional 5.7-liter HEMI V8 delivers robust towing power with 360 horsepower and 390 lb-ft of torque, though owners should monitor for cooling and internal engine concerns.

**Engine Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may unexpectedly stall while driving or fail to maintain proper operation, often accompanied by warning lights.
- **Solution:** Diagnose the root cause, which may involve powertrain control module updates, fuel delivery components, or sensor replacements.

**Coolant Leaks and Head Gasket Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Unexplained loss of coolant, engine overheating, or white smoke from the exhaust due to compromised head gaskets.
- **Solution:** Replace faulty gaskets, hoses, or damaged cooling components as diagnosed by a qualified mechanic.

### 3.0L Turbo Diesel V6

The EcoDiesel V6 provides high torque and strong fuel economy for long-distance driving, though high-pressure fuel system maintenance demands careful attention.

**Fuel Pump Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Difficulty starting, engine sputtering, or sudden fuel delivery failure leaving the vehicle stranded.
- **Solution:** Inspect and replace failing high-pressure fuel pumps and associated fuel system components.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic

This smooth-shifting automatic transmission pairs across most engine options to maximize efficiency and responsiveness. It generally proves durable when routine fluid maintenance is observed.

## Electrical

### Infotainment System

Cabin electronics provide intuitive smartphone integration and vehicle controls, but aging display panels are prone to cosmetic and functional touch-sensitivity faults.

**Screen Freezing and Delamination**
- **Symptoms:** The touchscreen displays bubbles, suffers from 'ghost touching' where commands activate randomly, or freezes entirely.
- **Solution:** Replace the touchscreen display unit or infotainment assembly.

## Interior

### Seat

Cabin seating offers solid comfort on long trips, but owners have reported rare, unexpected failures within the active safety headrest mechanisms.

**Spontaneous Active Headrest Deployment**
- **Symptoms:** The active head restraint deploys violently forward without any preceding collision or impact, startling occupants.
- **Solution:** Inspect the internal deployment mechanism and replace the affected headrest assembly.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V280000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Jeep&model=Grand+Cherokee&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** If the incorrect park lock rod is installed, the transmission may not shift into 'PARK' and keep the vehicle from moving, increasing the risk of unintended vehicle movement and the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will install the correct park lock rod, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V332000: Electrical System:Wiring](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Jeep&model=Grand+Cherokee&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** These vehicles are being recalled to address a defect that could prevent the cruise control system from disengaging. If, when using cruise control, there is a short circuit within the vehicle's wiring, the driver may not be able to shut off the cruise control either by depressing the brake pedal or manually turning the system off once it has been engaged.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the software, and perform a software flash on the engine or powertrain control module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V759000: Equipment:Accessory:Floor Mat](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Jeep&model=Grand+Cherokee&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** If the floor mat prevents the accelerator pedal from returning to idle, there would be an increased risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will replace any of the affected floor mats, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18E097000: Equipment:Accessory:Floor Mat](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Jeep&model=Grand+Cherokee&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** If the floor mat prevents the accelerator pedal from returning to idle, there would be an increased risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will replace any of the affected floor mats, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V699000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Exhaust System:Emission Control:Gas Recirculation Valve (Egr Valve)](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Jeep&model=Grand+Cherokee&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** Combustion in the intake manifold can increase the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** FCA US LLC will notify owners, and dealers will replace the EGR cooler, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V524000: Electrical System:Wiring:Front Underhood](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Jeep&model=Grand+Cherokee&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** A vehicle stall can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will replace the powertrain control module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V048000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Hoses, Lines/Piping, And Fittings](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Jeep&model=Grand+Cherokee&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** If the fuel line separates, the engine may stall, increasing the risk of a crash. In addition, a fuel line separation increases the risk of an engine compartment fire and injury.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will inspect and, as necessary, replace the fuel line, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V406000: Fuel System, Diesel:Delivery:Fuel Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Jeep&model=Grand+Cherokee&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** Fuel starvation may result in an unexpected loss of drive power, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the HPFP, and inspect and replace, if necessary, additional fuel system components, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

Overall, this model provides an appealing combination of capability and everyday drivability, making it a popular choice on the pre-owned market. Buyers should pay close attention during a test drive to verify proper infotainment operation, smooth engine performance, and flawless electronic systems. Securing a comprehensive pre-purchase inspection can help catch any hidden wear items before finalizing your purchase.
