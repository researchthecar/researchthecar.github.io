**Introduction:**

The 1998 Jeep Grand Cherokee is a mid-size SUV known for its off-road capability and rugged exterior. It features a 4.0L inline-6 engine or a 5.2L V8 engine, paired with a 4-speed automatic transmission. The vehicle offers a comfortable interior with ample space and a variety of features. However, like all vehicles, the 1998 Jeep Grand Cherokee has experienced various issues.

**Engine**

**4.0L Inline-6**

* **Intake Manifold Gasket Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Leaks oil and coolant, causing overheating and engine performance issues.
    * Solution: Replace intake manifold gasket.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Crankshaft Position Sensor Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Causes difficulty starting, stalling, and erratic engine performance.
    * Solution: Replace crankshaft position sensor.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $250+
    * Recalls: None Found

**5.2L V8**

* **Distributor Cap and Rotor Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Leads to misfiring, poor engine performance, and difficulty starting.
    * Solution: Replace distributor cap and rotor.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $150+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Oxygen Sensor Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Can cause decreased fuel efficiency, engine performance issues, or emissions problems.
    * Solution: Replace oxygen sensor.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $300+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**4-Speed Automatic**

* **Transmission Slippage:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears, grinding noises, or loss of power.
    * Solution: Transmission rebuild or replacement.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Solenoid Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Causes shifting problems, such as delayed or harsh shifts.
    * Solution: Replace solenoid pack.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Front Upper Control Arm Bushing Wear:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Causes excessive vibrations, poor handling, and uneven tire wear.
    * Solution: Replace upper control arm bushings.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $300+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Rear Leaf Spring Sagging:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Leads to decreased ground clearance, premature tire wear, and handling issues.
    * Solution: Replace leaf springs.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $600+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Alternator Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Causes charging system problems, battery drain, and difficulty starting.
    * Solution: Replace alternator.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Starter Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Prevents engine from starting or causes intermittent starting issues.
    * Solution: Replace starter.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Rear Window Seal Leakage:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Allows water to enter into the vehicle through the rear window.
    * Solution: Replace rear window seal.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $250+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Headlight Switch Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Causes headlights to malfunction or flicker.
    * Solution: Replace headlight switch.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Dashboard Cracking:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Develops cracks on the dashboard due to sun exposure or temperature changes.
    * Solution: Replace or repair dashboard.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Center Console Lid Hinge Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Hinge becomes loose or breaks, causing the lid to fall off.
    * Solution: Replace center console lid hinge.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $150+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 1998 Jeep Grand Cherokee has a variety of issues that owners may encounter, including engine problems, transmission issues, and electrical malfunctions. The severity and cost of these issues vary, with some being minor and inexpensive to fix, while others can be more significant and costly to repair. Overall, the Grand Cherokee is a capable vehicle but is prone to certain problems that potential buyers should be aware of.
