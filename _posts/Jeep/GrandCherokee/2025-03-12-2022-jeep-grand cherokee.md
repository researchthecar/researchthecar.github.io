The 2022 Jeep Grand Cherokee marked a significant redesign for the popular SUV, offering a blend of luxury, capability, and technology. Available powertrains included a 3.6L Pentastar V6 (293 hp, 260 lb-ft of torque) paired with an 8-speed automatic transmission, a 5.7L Hemi V8 (357 hp, 390 lb-ft of torque) also with an 8-speed automatic, and a 2.0L turbocharged I4 PHEV (total system output of 375 hp, 470 lb-ft of torque) with a specialized transmission. Owners appreciate the Grand Cherokee's updated styling, comfortable ride, and off-road prowess, but some have expressed concerns about electronic glitches, build quality issues, and the complexity of the new technology.

**Engine**

**3.6L Pentastar V6**

*   **Engine Tick/Knock**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported a ticking or knocking sound emanating from the engine, particularly at idle or low speeds. This can sometimes be attributed to normal valvetrain noise, but in some instances, it may indicate a more serious issue.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis by a qualified technician is crucial to determine the source of the noise. Potential fixes range from oil changes and valve adjustments to more extensive engine repairs if a mechanical problem is identified.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 (Oil Change) - $3000+ (Engine Repair)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**5.7L Hemi V8**

*   **No Issues Found**

**Electric Motor**

**2.0L Turbocharged I4 PHEV**

*   **Check Engine Light/Reduced Power**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners of the 4xe PHEV models have reported experiencing a check engine light accompanied by a reduction in power or inability to operate in electric mode. This can often be related to software or hardware issues with the hybrid system.
    *   Solution: Dealers may need to reflash the vehicle's software or replace faulty components within the hybrid system.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $1000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**8-Speed Automatic Transmission (All Engines)**

*   **Harsh Shifting/Hesitation**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported experiencing harsh or jerky shifts, particularly during low-speed maneuvers or when transitioning between gears. Others have described hesitation or delays in gear engagement.
    *   Solution: The issue may be addressable through a software update to the transmission control module (TCM). In some cases, transmission replacement may be necessary if internal damage has occurred.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 (Software Update) - $5000+ (Transmission Replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Air Suspension Problems (if equipped)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some models equipped with the optional air suspension system have experienced issues such as the vehicle not maintaining the correct ride height, compressor failure, or error messages related to the system.
    *   Solution: Faulty components, such as the air compressor, air struts, or height sensors, may need to be replaced. Software updates to the suspension control module can also address some issues.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Uconnect System Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Problems with the Uconnect infotainment system are frequently reported, including screen freezing, unresponsive touch controls, Bluetooth connectivity problems, and navigation malfunctions.
    *   Solution: Software updates often resolve minor glitches. In some cases, the Uconnect module may need to be replaced.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 (Software Update) - $1500+ (Module Replacement)
    *   Recalls: 23V-175 - Affects radio software
*   **Sensor Malfunctions**
    * Description/Symptoms: Issues with the parking sensors, blind spot monitoring, adaptive cruise control, and lane keep assist can arise, resulting in false alerts or system failures.
    * Solution: Faulty sensors may need to be replaced, or the system may require recalibration.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+
    * Recalls: None Found
*   **Automatic High Beam Malfunction**
    * Description/Symptoms: Some owners report that the automatic high beam feature does not function as intended, either not activating when needed or failing to dim when oncoming traffic is detected.
    * Solution: The headlight assembly may need to be replaced, or the system may require recalibration.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **No Issues Found**

**Interior**

*   **Sunroof Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported water leaks around the sunroof, particularly during heavy rain.
    *   Solution: The sunroof seals may need to be replaced, or the drain tubes may need to be cleared.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found
*   **Seat Problems**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported the seats either are not heating up or have been staying on indefinitely.
    *   Solution: The issue may be addressable through a software update to the seat control module. In some cases, the seat heating element may need to be replaced.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 (Software Update) - $1000+ (Heater Replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2022 Jeep Grand Cherokee, while praised for its redesign and features, has exhibited some common issues, particularly within its electrical systems and transmission. Engine issues are less common but can still occur. The severity and cost of these issues can vary widely, from minor software glitches to more substantial mechanical repairs. Potential buyers should be aware of these potential problems and consider a thorough inspection before purchase.
