# 2023 Jeep Grand Cherokee Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2023 Jeep Grand Cherokee brings modern styling, advanced tech, and versatile powertrain options to the midsize SUV segment. However, used-car buyers should review owner complaints and active service alerts carefully, as early production models have faced notable electrical, suspension, and hybrid-system concerns.

---

## Engine

### 3.6L V6 Engine

The standard 3.6-liter Pentastar V6 engine produces 293 horsepower and 260 lb-ft of torque, offering familiar, capable performance for daily driving and light towing.

**Engine Stalling and Loss of Power**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers report unexpected engine stalling while driving or sudden loss of motive power accompanied by dashboard warnings.
- **Solution:** Requires diagnostic testing by a dealership or certified mechanic to isolate software faults, fuel delivery issues, or electrical issues.

### 2.0L Turbocharged 4-Cylinder Plug-In Hybrid (4xe)

The 4xe plug-in hybrid powertrain combines a 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine with electric motors to deliver 375 horsepower and 470 lb-ft of torque, providing strong acceleration and electric-only capability.

**Hybrid System Shutdown**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicles may display electrical warning lights, shift unexpectedly into park, or experience a complete loss of propulsion.
- **Solution:** Check service history for completed recall updates or take the vehicle to a certified dealer for hybrid control module and high-voltage battery diagnostics.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

The TorqueFlite 8-speed automatic transmission delivers smooth gear shifts across all powertrain trims, though electronic shifter integration requires close inspection.

**Shifter or Park Defect**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may fail to register park status correctly or can unexpectedly shift into park while in motion.
- **Solution:** Inspect software calibration and electronic shifter modules; perform manufacturer-recommended updates.

## Safety

### Rear Coil Springs and Sway Bars

The suspension system provides a comfortable, compliant ride quality, but owners have reported premature wear and mechanical failures involving sway bars and rear springs.

**Rear Coil Spring Separation**
- **Symptoms:** A loud thud or clunk noise followed by sudden vehicle tilting or structural dropping of the rear passenger corner.
- **Solution:** Inspect rear suspension components and ensure all safety recall repairs for coil spring retention have been performed.

### Forward Collision Avoidance

Advanced driver assistance features enhance security on the road, though sensitive collision sensors can occasionally cause phantom braking events.

**Unexpected Automatic Braking**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle's safety system triggers false collision warnings and abruptly applies the brakes without obstacles ahead.
- **Solution:** Clean radar and camera sensors, and check for software updates at a dealership.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V336000: Equipment:Other:Labels](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Jeep&model=Grand+Cherokee&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirement Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 208, "Occupant Crash Protection." Without a label that shows the proper restraint usage guidelines, the occupants may have an increased risk of injury in the event of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect unsold vehicles and install the required label, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V352000: Steering:Column](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Jeep&model=Grand+Cherokee&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** A loss of steering control increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and, if necessary, replace the intermediate shaft and assembly parts, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V413000: Suspension:Rear:Springs:Coil Springs](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Jeep&model=Grand+Cherokee&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** Rear coil springs that detach from the vehicle while driving increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and if necessary, repair the rear coil spring assemblies, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V577000: Back Over Prevention:Display Function](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Jeep&model=Grand+Cherokee&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 111, "Rear Visibility." A rearview image that does not display while in reverse decreases the driver's visibility of the rear view, increasing the risk of injury or crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the Central Vision Park Assist Module software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 103, "Windshield Defrosting and Defogging Systems." **[Recall 24V132000: Suspension:Front:Control Arm:Upper Ball Joint](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Jeep&model=Grand+Cherokee&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** A loss of vehicle control increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the upper control arm pinch bolts, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V130000: Exterior Lighting:Headlights](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Jeep&model=Grand+Cherokee&modelYear=2023)**

- **What to do:** Dealers will reconfigure the vehicle high beam parameters, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V199000: Air Bags:Frontal:Driver Clockspring/Spiral Cassette](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Jeep&model=Grand+Cherokee&modelYear=2023)**

As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 208, "Occupant Crash Protection." An air bag that does not deploy in a crash can increase the risk of injury.

## Summary Verdict

The 2023 Jeep Grand Cherokee is a feature-rich and capable SUV, but used-car shoppers should thoroughly inspect vehicle history reports. Ensure that all outstanding safety recalls—particularly those involving electrical systems and rear suspension components—have been completed by an authorized dealer prior to purchase.
