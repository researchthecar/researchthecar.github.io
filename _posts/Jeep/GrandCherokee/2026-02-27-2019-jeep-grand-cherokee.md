# 2019 Jeep Grand Cherokee Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2019 Jeep Grand Cherokee, buyers find a capable and comfortable midsize SUV with a strong lineup of engine and transmission choices. However, prospective owners should review common owner-reported concerns ranging from electrical and braking system issues to interior and seat belt hardware complaints. Thoroughly inspecting service records and verifying that past recall work has been completed can help ensure a more reliable ownership experience.

---

## Engine

### 3.6L V6

The 3.6-liter Pentastar V6 produces around 295 horsepower and 260 lb-ft of torque, offering balanced performance and everyday driveability for daily commuting.

**Head Gasket Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers report engine overheating, loss of coolant, and illuminated warning lights on the dashboard.
- **Solution:** Replace the damaged head gasket and inspect the cylinder head for warping.

### 5.7L HEMI V8

The 5.7-liter HEMI V8 delivers approximately 360 horsepower and 390 lb-ft of torque, providing robust towing capability and strong acceleration.

### 6.4L HEMI V8

The 6.4-liter SRT V8 generates 475 horsepower, powering performance-oriented trims with aggressive acceleration and sport-tuned dynamics.

### 6.2L Supercharged HEMI V8

The 6.2-liter Trackhawk supercharged V8 pushes 707 horsepower for high-performance track-ready capability, though it requires meticulous maintenance.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic

The ZF 8-speed automatic transmission is paired across most powertrain options, delivering smooth, responsive gear shifts and contributing to overall highway fuel efficiency.

## Brakes

### Hydraulic Brakes

The service brake system provides adequate stopping power for this midsize SUV, though some owners have experienced electronic or hydraulic module symptoms.

**Brake Warning and Hydraulic Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Illuminated brake and traction control warning lights, abnormal squealing noises, or spongy pedal feel during operation.
- **Solution:** Inspect the hydraulic braking system, replace worn components, and ensure all relevant anti-lock brake module campaigns are addressed.

## Electrical

### Infotainment and Electrical System

Electrical accessories govern major cabin features, including the Uconnect infotainment system and charging modules, which occasionally exhibit hardware wear over time.

**Screen Delamination and Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** The touchscreen displays bubbles or separation at the edges, impacting navigation and hands-free safety controls, sometimes accompanied by battery drain after service visits.
- **Solution:** Replace the Uconnect touchscreen display unit or address underlying electrical draws as needed.

## Interior

### Seats and Headrests

Interior seating features active head restraints designed to reduce whiplash in rear-end collisions, though mechanical clips can occasionally fail.

**Unexpected Headrest Deployment**
- **Symptoms:** Active headrests disengage or deploy unexpectedly while the vehicle is parked or occupied, occasionally fracturing internal clips.
- **Solution:** Inspect headrest release mechanisms and replace damaged internal retainer clips or units.

## Safety

### Seat Belts

Cabin safety restraints include standard front and side airbags along with adjustable seat belts designed to protect occupants during a collision.

**Seatbelt Latch Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Front seatbelt buckles fail to engage properly when inserted, leaving the restraint unusable and posing a safety hazard.
- **Solution:** Inspect and replace faulty seatbelt buckle assemblies or retractor mechanisms.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V191000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Jeep&model=Grand+Cherokee&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 111, "Rearview Mirrors." The lingering rearview image can distract the driver, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Chrysler has notified owners, and dealers will update the radio display software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V699000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Exhaust System:Emission Control:Gas Recirculation Valve (Egr Valve)](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Jeep&model=Grand+Cherokee&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** Combustion in the intake manifold can increase the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** FCA US LLC will notify owners, and dealers will replace the EGR cooler, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V183000: Power Train:Driveline:Differential Unit](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Jeep&model=Grand+Cherokee&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** A sudden loss of power while driving or loss of the transmission PARK function can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will replace the front differential, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V406000: Fuel System, Diesel:Delivery:Fuel Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Jeep&model=Grand+Cherokee&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** Fuel starvation may result in an unexpected loss of drive power, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the HPFP, and inspect and replace, if necessary, additional fuel system components, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V426000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Antilock/Traction Control/Electronic Limited Slip:Control Unit/Module](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Jeep&model=Grand+Cherokee&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** A vehicle that starts and shifts out of Park without applying the brake pedal can result in vehicle rollaway, increasing the risk of a crash without prior warning and/or injury to others outside of the vehicle.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 23V411000: Engine And Engine Cooling](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Jeep&model=Grand+Cherokee&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** An engine stall can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the powertrain control module software to maintain vehicle propulsion, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V838000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Antilock/Traction Control/Electronic Limited Slip:Control Unit/Module](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Jeep&model=Grand+Cherokee&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** Disabled ABS and ESC systems can reduce vehicle handling and control in certain driving situations. Any of these scenarios can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the integrated pressure transducer and update the ABS module software, as necessary free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2019 Jeep Grand Cherokee delivers impressive styling, diverse engine choices, and strong towing capabilities, making it an appealing option on the used market. Buyers should pay close attention during test drives to verify proper transmission operation, inspect the condition of the infotainment touchscreen, and check that all safety recalls and brake system campaigns have been successfully performed.
