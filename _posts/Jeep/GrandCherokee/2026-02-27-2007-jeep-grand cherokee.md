# 2007 Jeep Grand Cherokee

### Overview
The 2007 Jeep Grand Cherokee, while offering a capable off-road experience and a comfortable ride, presented a mixed bag in terms of long-term reliability. Owners reported a range of issues, particularly concerning the powertrain and electrical systems. The available engine options, including the 3.7L V6, 4.7L V8, and the more powerful 5.7L HEMI V8, each had their own set of potential problems, with some experiencing premature wear or specific component failures. Similarly, the transmission options, primarily the 5-speed automatic, could be prone to issues if not properly maintained. While the chassis and exterior generally held up well, the interior and electrical components were areas where owners frequently encountered problems, impacting the overall ownership experience.

---


## Engine

### 3.7L V6
**Crankshaft Position Sensor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Stalling, rough idle, check engine light, difficulty starting.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the crankshaft position sensor.
- **Cost:** $150 - $300

**Intake Manifold Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Vacuum leaks, rough idle, loss of power, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the intake manifold gasket or the entire manifold.
- **Cost:** $400 - $700


### 4.7L V8
**Head Gasket Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Overheating, coolant loss, white smoke from exhaust, milky oil.
- **Solution:** Replacement of head gaskets and potentially other related components.
- **Cost:** $1200 - $2500

**Camshaft and Lifter Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Ticking noise from the engine, rough idle, loss of power.
- **Solution:** Replacement of camshaft and lifters.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1500


### 5.7L HEMI V8
**Cylinder Deactivation System (MDS) Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Rough idle, misfires, check engine light, potential for valve spring issues.
- **Solution:** Repair or disable of the MDS system, potential valve spring replacement.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1500

**Oil Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Visible oil leaks from valve covers or oil pan.
- **Solution:** Replacement of gaskets and seals.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600


## Transmission

### 5-Speed Automatic (W5A580)
**Torque Converter Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Slipping gears, shuddering, delayed engagement, fluid leaks.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the torque converter.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1500

**Transmission Control Module (TCM) Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Erratic shifting, transmission warning light, limp mode.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the TCM.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1000


## Suspension

### Standard Chassis
**Worn Ball Joints and Tie Rod Ends**
- **Symptoms:** Clunking noises over bumps, loose steering, uneven tire wear.
- **Solution:** Replacement of worn ball joints and tie rod ends.
- **Cost:** $400 - $800

**Leaking Shock Absorbers/Struts**
- **Symptoms:** Bouncy ride, poor handling, visible fluid leaks.
- **Solution:** Replacement of shock absorbers or struts.
- **Cost:** $300 - $700


## Electrical

### General Electrical System
**TIPM (Totally Integrated Power Module) Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Intermittent electrical issues, non-functioning accessories, blown fuses without cause, starting problems.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the TIPM.
- **Cost:** $600 - $1200

**Window Regulator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Power windows not working, windows falling down into the door.
- **Solution:** Replacement of window regulators.
- **Cost:** $200 - $400

**Heater Blower Motor Resistor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Heater/AC only works on highest setting or not at all.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the blower motor resistor.
- **Cost:** $100 - $250

### Summary Verdict
The 2007 Jeep Grand Cherokee exhibits moderate reliability concerns, with the powertrain and electrical systems being the most frequent sources of problems. While the engines, particularly the HEMI, can be robust, they are not immune to issues like head gasket failures (on the 4.7L) or specific component wear. The automatic transmission also requires attention to avoid costly repairs. Electrical gremlins, especially related to the TIPM and window regulators, are common complaints. Suspension components can wear out, but are generally manageable. Owners should be prepared for potential repairs, particularly in the aforementioned areas, and prioritize regular maintenance to mitigate risks.
