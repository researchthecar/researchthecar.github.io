# 2021 Jeep Grand Cherokee Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2021 Jeep Grand Cherokee, buyers will find a capable midsize SUV that offers strong utility and recognizable styling. However, reviewing owner feedback highlights several mechanical and electrical areas that warrant close inspection before purchase. While many owners enjoy their time behind the wheel, being aware of reported powertrain glitches and safety system quirks can help you make a more informed buying decision.

---

## Engine

### 3.6L V6

The 3.6-liter Pentastar V6 produces around 295 horsepower and 260 lb-ft of torque, providing balanced everyday performance. It is a widely used powerplant in the lineup, though some owners have experienced fuel delivery and electrical warning light glitches.

**Fuel Pump Failure and Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers report unexpected engine stalling while driving or difficulty starting the vehicle due to premature fuel pump failure.
- **Solution:** Replace the faulty in-tank electric fuel pump assembly.

### 5.7L HEMI V8

The 5.7-liter HEMI V8 generates roughly 360 horsepower and 390 lb-ft of torque, offering robust towing capacity and strong acceleration. It provides traditional V8 capability for buyers needing heavier utility.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic

The ZF 8-speed automatic transmission is paired with various trims and drivetrain configurations, generally delivering smooth gear changes. While typically robust, a subset of drivers have encountered premature shifting malfunctions and warning alerts.

**Transmission Shifting and Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have reported erratic shifting, sudden entry into limp mode, illuminated service warnings, or complete transmission failure at relatively low mileage.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified technician diagnose transmission control modules or perform mechanical repairs/replacement as needed.

## Steering

### Steering System

The power steering setup provides manageable low-speed maneuverability and highway stability, though intermittent electronic assist complaints have been documented.

**Steering Assist Failure and Erratic Movement**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers note erratic steering feel where the vehicle may drift or pull unexpectedly, or steering assist may temporarily feel compromised.
- **Solution:** Inspect steering linkage, electronic power assist components, and alignment settings.

## Exterior

### Exterior Lighting

Exterior lighting systems provide solid nighttime visibility, though automatic headlight switch and high-beam mechanism concerns have surfaced in owner feedback.

**Headlight and High Beam Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** Headlights may flicker, change color temperature, or auto high-beam switches may fail to engage or remain locked in position.
- **Solution:** Inspect headlight switch assemblies, wiring harnesses, and replace faulty bulb or control modules.

## Safety

### Seat Belts

Standard safety equipment includes modern airbag and restraint systems, but physical retractor wear can occasionally hinder proper belt retraction.

**Seat Belt Retractor Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Driver or passenger seat belts fail to retract smoothly, leaving slack and hanging up during use.
- **Solution:** Inspect the seat belt retractor mechanism and replace the assembly if internal spring tension fails.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V797000: Exterior Lighting:Headlights](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Jeep&model=Grand+Cherokee&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 108, "Lamps, Reflective Devices, and Associated Equipment." Difficulty activating the headlight high beams may result in reduced visibility, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will reprogram the vehicles, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V950000: Exterior Lighting:Lighting Control Module:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Jeep&model=Grand+Cherokee&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** An incomplete SLM software update may cause one or both headlights to become inoperable. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 108, "Lamps, Reflective Devices, and Associated Equipment." Inoperable headlights can reduce visibility for the driver and oncoming traffic, increasing the risk.
- **What to do:** Dealers will reprogram the SLM software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V729000: Visibility:Rearview Mirrors/Devices](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Jeep&model=Grand+Cherokee&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 111, "Rear Visibility." A rearview camera that does not display an image reduces the driver's rear view, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** FCA US LLC will update the radio software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V873000: Air Bags: Air Bag/Restraint Control Module:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Jeep&model=Grand+Cherokee&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** Disabled air bags can increase the risk of injury to occupants in a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will reprogram the ORC, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V180000: Child Seat:Vehicle Tether Anchor](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Jeep&model=Grand+Cherokee&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 225, "Child Restraint Anchorage Systems." A missing weld may allow the child seat tether to separate from the seat frame during a crash, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and, if necessary, replace the second-row seat back frame, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V284000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Exhaust System:Emission Control:Gas Recirculation Valve (Egr Valve)](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Jeep&model=Grand+Cherokee&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** An unexpected loss of drive power increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the EGR valve assembly, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V544000: Exterior Lighting:Tail Lights](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Jeep&model=Grand+Cherokee&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 108, "Lamp, Reflective Devices, and Associated Equipment." A tail light side marker that does not illuminate can reduce vehicle visibility to other drivers, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace both body-mounted tail light assemblies, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V352000: Steering:Column](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Jeep&model=Grand+Cherokee&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** A loss of steering control increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and, if necessary, replace the intermediate shaft and assembly parts, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2021 Jeep Grand Cherokee remains an appealing option for used car buyers seeking versatility, comfort, and respectable towing capabilities. However, prospective buyers should perform a thorough pre-purchase inspection paying close attention to the powertrain, steering responsiveness, and electrical accessories. Ensuring all previous maintenance has been addressed will help secure a more reliable ownership experience.
