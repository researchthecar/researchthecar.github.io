# 2023 Jeep Grand Cherokee L Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2023 Jeep Grand Cherokee L offers spacious three-row seating, upscale interior amenities, and strong utility for families. However, prospective used-car buyers should review owner complaints regarding various electrical glitches, suspension components, and occasional powertrain behavior. Careful pre-purchase inspections can help uncover potential underlying issues before finalizing a purchase.

---

## Engine

### 3.6L V6 Engine

The standard 3.6-liter Pentastar V6 engine produces approximately 293 horsepower and 260 lb-ft of torque. It provides adequate everyday performance and towing capability for most family hauling needs, though buyers should verify regular maintenance history.

### 5.7L HEMI V8 Engine

The optional 5.7-liter HEMI V8 engine generates around 357 horsepower and 390 lb-ft of torque, significantly increasing towing capacity. While robust and eager, it consumes more fuel than the base V6 and requires attentive fluid maintenance.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

Paired with both engine choices, the TorqueFlite 8-speed automatic transmission generally delivers smooth shifts and efficient highway cruising. Owners should thoroughly test-shift the vehicle to confirm seamless gear engagement.

**Shifter and Gear Engagement Warnings**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may experience sudden 'Service Shifter' warnings accompanied by the transmission unexpectedly dropping from Drive into Neutral while the vehicle is in motion.
- **Solution:** Have a certified technician inspect the shifter assembly module, transmission control module (TCM), and related software calibration for updates or hardware replacement.

## Suspension

### Rear Coil Springs and Control Links

The multi-link independent suspension delivers a plush ride quality, but certain components like rear springs and control arms warrant careful lift inspection.

**Rear Coil Spring Displacement**
- **Symptoms:** Unusual popping noises or sudden suspension drops can occur if a rear coil spring unseats or dislodges from its mounting position while driving.
- **Solution:** Inspect rear coil springs, retaining components, and mounting perches for wear, and replace any damaged or improperly seated hardware.

## Electrical

### Infotainment and Audio Systems

Advanced cabin electronics offer great connectivity features but can occasionally suffer from software glitches or digital display bugs.

**Loud Audio Booming and Screen Freezes**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have reported sudden, excessively loud booming noises emitting from the vehicle speakers upon startup, alongside pixelated or freezing infotainment screens.
- **Solution:** Check for manufacturer software updates for the Uconnect system and have audio amplifier modules tested or replaced if audio distortion persists.

## Interior

### Seats and Adjustments

Cabin creature comforts are extensive, though power-operated interior adjustments should be thoroughly tested during a pre-purchase test drive.

**Seat Lumbar and Folding Failures**
- **Symptoms:** Power lumbar support airbags can overinflate autonomously, altering seating posture, or third-row seats may occasionally fail to return to their upright position.
- **Solution:** Inspect seat control switches, pneumatic lines, and folding release motors; faulty switches or motors will require replacement.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V897000: Air Bags:Side/Window](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Jeep&model=Grand+Cherokee+L&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** An improperly secured connector for the front seat side air bags (SAB) may prevent the air bags from deploying. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 214, "Side Impact Protection." An air bag that does not deploy during a crash increases the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 25V430000: Visibility:Defroster/Defogger/Hvac System:Fan/Motor](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Jeep&model=Grand+Cherokee+L&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 103, "Windshield Defrosting and Defogging Systems." An inoperative windshield defrosting and defogging system may decrease the driver's visibility, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the blower motor, as necessary free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V472000: Seats:Mid/Rear Assembly:Head Restraint](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Jeep&model=Grand+Cherokee+L&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** The second-row seat head restraints may not lock in the upright position. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 202, "Head Restraints." Head restraints that do not lock can result in an increased risk of injury to seat occupants during a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace both second-row seat head restraints, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V051000: Suspension:Rear:Springs:Coil Springs](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Jeep&model=Grand+Cherokee+L&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** Rear coil springs that detach from the vehicle while driving increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and repair the rear coil spring assembly, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V328000: Air Bags: Air Bag/Restraint Control Module:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Jeep&model=Grand+Cherokee+L&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** A software error in the occupant restraint controller module may cause the delayed deployment of the side air bags during a crash. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 214, "Side Impact Protection." Delayed air bag deployment during a crash increases the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the occupant restraint controller module software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2023 Jeep Grand Cherokee L is a versatile and feature-packed SUV, but buyers should conduct a thorough inspection paying close attention to suspension components, electronic displays, and transmission shift behavior. Ensuring all software updates and recalls have been addressed will lead to a more dependable ownership experience.
