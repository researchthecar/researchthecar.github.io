# 2021 Jeep Grand Cherokee L Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2021 Jeep Grand Cherokee L introduces a three-row layout to the iconic SUV lineup, offering spacious accommodations and modern tech. However, as an introductory model year, it experiences a higher-than-average volume of early-adopter complaints. Buyers should thoroughly inspect the vehicle's complex electronics, steering hardware, and powertrain operation during a pre-purchase test drive.

---

## Engine

### 3.6L Pentastar V6

A 3.6-liter naturally aspirated V6 producing 290 horsepower and 257 lb-ft of torque, serving as the standard and most common powerplant. It provides adequate everyday performance and generally dependable operation, though minor accessory and electrical integration complaints have been reported.

### 5.7L Hemi V8

An available 5.7-liter V8 engine generating 357 horsepower and 390 lb-ft of torque, designed for buyers needing higher towing capacities. It delivers robust acceleration but consumes substantially more fuel than the standard V6.

## Transmission

### TorqueFlite 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

An eight-speed automatic transmission paired across all trims and drivetrains. While generally smooth when functioning correctly, early models have exhibited occasional shifting anomalies and mechanical engagement concerns.

**Transmission Shifting and Engagement Failures**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers report sudden hesitation, stuttering during acceleration, failure to shift gears properly, or the transmission becoming completely stuck in gear.
- **Solution:** Have a certified technician inspect transmission software, fluid condition, and internal components; software flashes or mechanical repairs may be required.

## Steering

### Steering Rack and Column

The electronic power steering system provides comfortable handling, though isolated failures of the steering rack and control modules have raised safety concerns for some owners.

**Power Steering Loss and Rack Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Sudden heavy steering effort accompanied by warning lights on the dashboard, making the vehicle extremely difficult to maneuver.
- **Solution:** Replace the faulty steering rack and pinion assembly or faulty steering control modules.

## Interior

### Power Seats and Lumbar Support

Cabin appointments are generally upscale and feature-rich, though power-adjustable seat components have experienced reliability issues in early builds.

**Random Seat Lumbar Inflation**
- **Symptoms:** Front seat lumbar support bladders inflate independently while driving and refuse to deflate, causing discomfort and structural strain on the seatback.
- **Solution:** Inspect and replace malfunctioning seat lumbar switches, pumps, or control modules.

## Safety

### Advanced Driver Assistance Systems

Advanced safety features add modern protection but rely heavily on sensitive sensors and software that can occasionally misbehave.

**Forward Collision Avoidance Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** Adaptive cruise control or collision warning systems improperly braking or dragging the vehicle unexpectedly during normal traffic flow.
- **Solution:** Have dealership technicians check for software updates or sensor recalibrations for the driver assistance suite.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V897000: Air Bags:Side/Window](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Jeep&model=Grand+Cherokee+L&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** An improperly secured connector for the front seat side air bags (SAB) may prevent the air bags from deploying. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 214, "Side Impact Protection." An air bag that does not deploy during a crash increases the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 25V083000: Equipment:Electrical:Infotainment:Video (Touch)Screen/Monitor/Unit](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Jeep&model=Grand+Cherokee+L&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 111, "Rear Visibility." A rearview image that does not display reduces the driver's visibility, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 26V051000: Suspension:Rear:Springs:Coil Springs](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Jeep&model=Grand+Cherokee+L&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** Rear coil springs that detach from the vehicle while driving increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and repair the rear coil spring assembly, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2021 Jeep Grand Cherokee L is a compelling choice for families seeking three rows of seating and strong capability, but as an initial model year, it warrants careful inspection. Prospective buyers should verify that all electronic features, steering components, and transmission operations are functioning properly before purchasing.
