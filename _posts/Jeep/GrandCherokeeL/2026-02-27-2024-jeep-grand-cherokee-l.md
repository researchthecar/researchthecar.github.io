# 2024 Jeep Grand Cherokee L Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2024 Jeep Grand Cherokee L offers a spacious three-row cabin, sophisticated tech, and solid capability. However, used car buyers should note that owner reports filed with safety authorities point toward some recurring early-mileage electrical glitches, powertrain sensor concerns, and automated driver assistance quirks.

---

## Engine

### 3.6L V6 Engine

The standard 3.6-liter Pentastar V6 produces approximately 293 horsepower and 260 lb-ft of torque, providing adequate power for daily commuting and towing. It is widely used across the Jeep lineup and generally offers straightforward mechanical reliability.

### 2.0L Turbocharged I4 Plug-In Hybrid (4xe)

The 4xe plug-in hybrid powertrain pairs a 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine with an electric motor system to deliver around 375 horsepower and 470 lb-ft of torque. It offers strong acceleration and improved efficiency, though its complex electrical architecture requires careful inspection.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

The TorqueFlite 8-speed automatic transmission delivers smooth and responsive shifts across various driving conditions. It pairs with both engine options and is generally regarded as a durable unit, though proper software calibration is vital.

## Electrical

### Electrical System & Battery

The electrical system handles extensive infotainment and driver-assistance features, but owners have flagged parasitic drains and display issues.

**Parasitic Battery Drain and Infotainment Freezes**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have reported dead batteries caused by modules failing to shut down properly, as well as intermittent infotainment screen flickering or freezing.
- **Solution:** Have a dealer check for software updates or faulty modules such as the Telematics Box Module that may cause excessive power draw.

## Safety

### Forward Collision & Brakes

Advanced safety and driver-assist technologies add peace of mind, though sensor misinterpretations can occasionally trigger false warnings or unexpected braking.

**Unexpected Automatic Emergency Braking**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may incorrectly interpret traffic situations while turning or navigating intersections, leading to sudden, unintended braking.
- **Solution:** Ensure sensor calibrations and ADAS software are up to date through authorized service channels.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V872000: Electrical System:Instrument Panel:Speedometer/Odometer](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Jeep&model=Grand+Cherokee+L&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** A software error in the instrument panel cluster may cause the speedometer to display the incorrect vehicle speed. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 101, "Displays and Controls." Driving without knowing the vehicle's actual speed increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and update the instrument panel cluster software as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V897000: Air Bags:Side/Window](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Jeep&model=Grand+Cherokee+L&modelYear=2024)**

As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 214, "Side Impact Protection." An air bag that does not deploy during a crash increases the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

When shopping for a used 2024 Jeep Grand Cherokee L, prioritize vehicles with verified maintenance records and ensure all open software updates have been performed. While comfort and powertrain performance are strong suits, checking the electrical and driver-assistance systems during a pre-purchase inspection can help avoid frustrating early ownership issues.
