**Introduction**

The 2023 Jeep Grand Cherokee L is a three-row SUV that seats up to eight passengers. It is available with a variety of engine and transmission options, including:

- 3.6L V6 engine with 293 horsepower and 260 lb-ft of torque
- 5.7L V8 engine with 357 horsepower and 390 lb-ft of torque
- 3.0L EcoDiesel V6 engine with 260 horsepower and 480 lb-ft of torque
- 4xe plug-in hybrid system with 2.0L turbocharged four-cylinder engine and two electric motors, producing a combined 375 horsepower and 470 lb-ft of torque

The Grand Cherokee L also features a spacious interior, a long list of standard features, and impressive off-road capabilities.

However, like any vehicle, the Grand Cherokee L has its share of problems. Here is a comprehensive list of the specific issues people are having with the vehicles, separated into categories of the major components:

**Engine**

**3.6L V6 Engine**

- **Cylinder head issues**: Some owners have reported problems with the cylinder heads, which can lead to coolant leaks, overheating, and engine failure. The solution is to replace the cylinder heads, which can cost between $3,000 and $5,000.
- **Intake manifold issues**: Another problem that has been reported is a faulty intake manifold, which can cause a loss of power, rough idling, and decreased fuel economy. The solution is to replace the intake manifold, which can cost between $500 and $1,000.

**5.7L V8 Engine**

- **Timing chain issues**: Some owners have reported problems with the timing chain, which can lead to a loss of power, rough idling, and increased fuel consumption. The solution is to replace the timing chain, which can cost between $1,500 and $2,500.
- **Valve seat issues**: Another problem that has been reported is a faulty valve seat, which can lead to a loss of compression and engine damage. The solution is to replace the valve seat, which can cost between $1,000 and $2,000.

**3.0L EcoDiesel V6 Engine**

- **Diesel particulate filter (DPF) issues**: The DPF is a component of the exhaust system that traps soot and other pollutants. Some owners have reported problems with the DPF, which can lead to a loss of power, increased fuel consumption, and a warning light on the dashboard. The solution is to clean or replace the DPF, which can cost between $500 and $1,500.
- **Exhaust gas recirculation (EGR) valve issues**: The EGR valve is responsible for reducing emissions. Some owners have reported problems with the EGR valve, which can lead to a loss of power, rough idling, and increased fuel consumption. The solution is to replace the EGR valve, which can cost between $300 and $600.

**Transmission**

**8-Speed Automatic Transmission**

- **Shifting problems**: Some owners have reported problems with the transmission, which can lead to rough shifting, delayed shifts, and a loss of power. The solution is to reprogram the transmission or replace it, which can cost between $1,500 and $3,000.
- **Torque converter issues**: Another problem that has been reported is a faulty torque converter, which can lead to a loss of power, shuddering, and whining noises. The solution is to replace the torque converter, which can cost between $1,000 and $2,000.

**Suspension**

**Front Suspension**

- **Strut issues**: Some owners have reported problems with the front struts, which can lead to a rough ride, noise, and difficulty steering. The solution is to replace the struts, which can cost between $600 and $1,200.
- **Ball joint issues**: Another problem that has been reported is a faulty ball joint, which can lead to a loss of control and a loud clunking noise. The solution is to replace the ball joint, which can cost between $200 and $400.

**Rear Suspension**

- **Shock absorber issues**: Some owners have reported problems with the rear shock absorbers, which can lead to a rough ride, noise, and difficulty steering. The solution is to replace the shock absorbers, which can cost between $400 and $800.
- **Spring issues**: Another problem that has been reported is a faulty spring, which can lead to a loss of control and a loud clunking noise. The solution is to replace the spring, which can cost between $200 and $400.

**Electrical**

- **Battery issues**: Some owners have reported problems with the battery, which can lead to a loss of power, difficulty starting the vehicle, and a warning light on the dashboard. The solution is to replace the battery, which can cost between $100 and $200.
- **Starter issues**: Another problem that has been reported is a faulty starter, which can lead to a loss of power and the inability to start the vehicle. The solution is to replace the starter, which can cost between $300 and $600.
- **Alternator issues**: Another problem that has been reported is a faulty alternator, which can lead to a loss of power, flickering lights, and a warning light on the dashboard. The solution is to replace the alternator, which can cost between $500 and $1,000.

**Exterior**

- **Headlight issues**: Some owners have reported problems with the headlights, which can lead to reduced visibility and a warning light on the dashboard. The solution is to replace the headlights, which can cost between $200 and $400.
- **Taillight issues**: Another problem that has been reported is a faulty taillight, which can lead to reduced visibility and a warning light on the dashboard. The solution is to replace the taillight, which can cost between $100 and $200.

**Interior**

- **Infotainment system issues**: Some owners have reported problems with the infotainment system, which can lead to a loss of functionality, freezing, and a blank screen. The solution is to update the software or replace the infotainment system, which can cost between $500 and $1,000.
- **Climate control issues**: Another problem that has been reported is a faulty climate control system, which can lead to a loss of functionality, inconsistent temperatures, and a warning light on the dashboard. The solution is to replace the climate control system, which can cost between $300 and $600.
- **Steering wheel issues**: Another problem that has been reported is a faulty steering wheel, which can lead to a loss of control and a warning light on the dashboard. The solution is to replace the steering wheel, which can cost between $400 and $800.

**Summary**

The 2023 Jeep Grand Cherokee L is a capable SUV with a lot to offer. However, it is important to be aware of the potential problems that can occur with this vehicle. The most severe problems are related to the engine, transmission, and suspension. These problems can be expensive to fix, so it is important to factor in the cost of potential repairs when considering this vehicle.
