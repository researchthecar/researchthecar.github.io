# 2023 Jeep Grand Wagoneer Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2023 Jeep Grand Wagoneer is a full-size luxury SUV offering spacious accommodations, high-end technology, and robust towing capabilities. However, early owner reports to safety authorities highlight several recurring issues, particularly involving electrical glitches, unexpected engine stalling, and complex accessory malfunctions that warrant close inspection before purchasing a used model.

---

## Engine

### 3.0L Hurricane Twin-Turbo Inline-6

The 3.0-liter Hurricane twin-turbo inline-6 engine delivers strong horsepower and torque figures, serving as the modern high-output powerhouse for the lineup. Buyers should verify service records and expect complex turbocharged plumbing.

### 6.4L HEMI V8

The naturally aspirated 6.4-liter HEMI V8 provides traditional power and robust towing capability for this large platform. While generally stout, some owners have reported unexpected powertrain shutdowns.

**Engine Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may unexpectedly stall or shut off completely while operating at low speeds or coming to a stop, occasionally shifting into park automatically.
- **Solution:** Diagnostic testing by a qualified technician to check for software glitches, thermostat faults, or sensor failures.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

Paired across the lineup, the 8-speed automatic transmission delivers smooth shifting characteristics tailored for heavy towing and highway cruising. Overall mechanical reliability has been relatively stable, though proper fluid maintenance is critical.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

Braking performance is vital for a vehicle of this size, though rare electronic brake booster faults can cause severe pedal travel issues.

**Electronic Brake Booster Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The brake pedal travels straight to the floor with a near-complete loss of hydraulic braking assistance, triggering diagnostic trouble codes such as C0021.
- **Solution:** Inspect the electronic brake control module (BCSM) and replace the electronic brake booster assembly.

## Steering

### Power Steering

Electronic power steering systems provide light maneuverability at low speeds, but intermittent electrical glitches can cause unexpected loss of assist.

**Sudden Power Steering Loss**
- **Symptoms:** Power steering assistance fails completely without prior warning, making the steering heavy and difficult to control.
- **Solution:** Have the vehicle towed for dealership diagnosis, which may require steering rack or electrical module servicing.

## Electrical

### Electrical System & Warning Lights

The heavy reliance on complex electronic architectures across multiple digital screens and driver aids can occasionally lead to erratic warning lights and sporadic system faults.

**Electrical Warning Lights and System Faults**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers experience unexpected dashboard warning lights, lane-keeping system errors, and intermittent brake warnings while underway.
- **Solution:** Inspect wiring harnesses, module software levels, and battery health; software updates or module replacements may be required.

## Interior

### Seat Comfort Control Module

Power adjustable seating features integrated comfort control systems that can experience electronic malfunctions, creating potential driver distraction and safety concerns.

**Seat Airbag and Lumbar Overinflation**
- **Symptoms:** Front seat lumbar or massage air bladders automatically overinflate beyond maximum limits without user input, pushing the occupant forward into the steering wheel.
- **Solution:** Replace the Comfort Control Module (CCM) or faulty seat wiring harness.
- **Cost:** Covered under warranty when applicable

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V545000: Structure:Body:Roof And Pillars](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Jeep&model=Grand+Wagoneer&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** The upper B-pillar interior trim may not be fully seated and could interfere with the side curtain air bag (SABIC) deployment. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 226, "Ejection Mitigation." A compromised deployment of the SABIC can reduce occupant protection in the event of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and reseat or replace the trim pieces as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V577000: Back Over Prevention:Display Function](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Jeep&model=Grand+Wagoneer&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 111, "Rear Visibility." A rearview image that does not display while in reverse decreases the driver's visibility of the rear view, increasing the risk of injury or crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the Central Vision Park Assist Module software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V716000: Seat Belts: Rear/Other:Buckle Assembly](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Jeep&model=Grand+Wagoneer&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** The third-row seat belts may not allow occupants to properly access or fasten the seat belt to the buckle. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard numbers 208, "Occupant Crash Protection" and 209, "Seat Belt Assemblies." An unfastened seat belt may increase the risk of injury to occupants during.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the third-row seat belt buckle, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V199000: Air Bags:Frontal:Driver Clockspring/Spiral Cassette](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Jeep&model=Grand+Wagoneer&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** The steering column control module may not be welded properly, which can prevent the driver's air bag from deploying in a crash. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 208, "Occupant Crash Protection." An air bag that does not deploy in a crash can increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the steering column control module, as necessary free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V436000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Jeep&model=Grand+Wagoneer&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 111, "Rear Visibility." A rearview camera image that does not display decreases the driver's visibility, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the radio software or the software will be OTA, "Over-The-Air," free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V593000: Structure:Exterior Trim](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Jeep&model=Grand+Wagoneer&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** The trim on the driver and passenger doors may not have been installed properly, which can allow it to detach. Detached trim pieces can create a road hazard for other vehicles, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the door trim, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V642000: Structure:Exterior Trim](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Jeep&model=Grand+Wagoneer&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** The quarter trim on the driver and passenger windows may not have been properly secured, which can allow it to detach. Detached trim pieces can create a road hazard for other vehicles, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the quarter window glass assembly, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

When shopping for a used 2023 Jeep Grand Wagoneer, thoroughly test all electronic accessories, seating adjustments, and braking systems to ensure proper function. Given the complexity of its luxury features and powertrain options, a comprehensive pre-purchase inspection and verification of remaining warranty coverage are strongly recommended.
