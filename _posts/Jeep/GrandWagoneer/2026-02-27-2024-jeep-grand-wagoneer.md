# 2024 Jeep Grand Wagoneer Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2024 Jeep Grand Wagoneer is a massive, high-end luxury SUV loaded with advanced technology, powerful engine options, and ample towing capabilities. However, prospective buyers should approach the used market with a degree of caution. Early owner reports highlight a series of frustrating electrical issues, battery drain issues, and complex powertrain or safety sensor warnings that require close inspection before purchasing.

---

## Engine

### 3.0L Hurricane Twin-Turbo Inline-6

The 3.0-liter Hurricane twin-turbo inline-6 produces around 510 horsepower and 500 lb-ft of torque, providing robust acceleration and towing. While powerful and relatively modern, owners have reported occasional unexpected engine shutdowns and electronic management faults.

**Sudden Engine Shutdown and Loss of Motive Power**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may unexpectedly shut down or enter limp mode while driving at highway speeds, accompanied by flickering dashboard lights and a loss of throttle response.
- **Solution:** Requires professional diagnostic evaluation by a certified dealership to inspect powertrain control modules, wiring harnesses, and software calibrations.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

Paired standard with the vehicle's high-output engines, this 8-speed automatic transmission delivers smooth and seamless gear shifts across varying driving modes. It generally operates reliably, with few widespread transmission-specific mechanical failures reported in early owner data.

## Steering

### Power Steering and Sensors

Steering performance relies heavily on electronic assist motors and sensors that integrate closely with the vehicle's overall electrical architecture.

**Power Steering Warning Lights After Stall**
- **Symptoms:** Following an unexpected engine stall or after driving over rough surfaces like railroad tracks, warning lights for the power steering and stability systems illuminate, complicating vehicle control.
- **Solution:** Have the electronic power steering modules, steering angle sensors, and associated wiring checked for fault codes and proper calibration.

## Electrical

### Charging and Battery System

The extensive electronics suite powering the cabin and driver assists can occasionally introduce parasitic draws or communication errors.

**Persistent Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicles may experience dead batteries and fail to start intermittently, requiring frequent jump starts or prolonged stays at the dealership for electrical diagnostics.
- **Solution:** Inspect parasitic draws, battery health, and charging system software; multiple battery replacements may be necessary if underlying electrical modules fail to go into deep sleep.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V577000: Back Over Prevention:Display Function](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Jeep&model=Grand+Wagoneer&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 111, "Rear Visibility." A rearview image that does not display while in reverse decreases the driver's visibility of the rear view, increasing the risk of injury or crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the Central Vision Park Assist Module software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V716000: Seat Belts: Rear/Other:Buckle Assembly](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Jeep&model=Grand+Wagoneer&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** The third-row seat belts may not allow occupants to properly access or fasten the seat belt to the buckle. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard numbers 208, "Occupant Crash Protection" and 209, "Seat Belt Assemblies." An unfastened seat belt may increase the risk of injury to occupants during.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the third-row seat belt buckle, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V199000: Air Bags:Frontal:Driver Clockspring/Spiral Cassette](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Jeep&model=Grand+Wagoneer&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** The steering column control module may not be welded properly, which can prevent the driver's air bag from deploying in a crash. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 208, "Occupant Crash Protection." An air bag that does not deploy in a crash can increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the steering column control module, as necessary free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V944000: Back Over Prevention:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Jeep&model=Grand+Wagoneer&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** Incorrect software in the rearview camera module may cause the rearview camera image not to display. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 111, "Rear Visibility." A rearview camera image that does not display decreases the driver's visibility, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the rearview camera module software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V593000: Structure:Exterior Trim](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Jeep&model=Grand+Wagoneer&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** The trim on the driver and passenger doors may not have been installed properly, which can allow it to detach. Detached trim pieces can create a road hazard for other vehicles, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the door trim, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V642000: Structure:Exterior Trim](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Jeep&model=Grand+Wagoneer&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** The quarter trim on the driver and passenger windows may not have been properly secured, which can allow it to detach. Detached trim pieces can create a road hazard for other vehicles, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the quarter window glass assembly, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2024 Jeep Grand Wagoneer offers immense luxury, comfort, and performance, but its complex web of electronics and high-output powertrain options can introduce ownership hurdles. Used-car shoppers should thoroughly inspect the vehicle's service history, verify that all software updates and battery recalls have been addressed, and ensure a comprehensive pre-purchase inspection is performed.
