# 2022 Jeep Grand Wagoneer Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2022 Jeep Grand Wagoneer enters the luxury full-size SUV market with impressive towing capability, high-end appointments, and advanced technology. However, early-model growing pains are evident, particularly regarding complex electrical architecture, powertrain stalls, and auxiliary cabin component issues. Prospective buyers should carefully evaluate maintenance records and verify that all software updates and technical bulletins have been addressed.

---

## Engine

### 6.4L V8 Engine

The 6.4-liter naturally aspirated V8 engine produces roughly 471 horsepower and 455 lb-ft of torque, providing strong traditional performance for this heavy SUV. It serves as the standard powerplant for the lineup, though buyers should watch for occasional accessory and electrical integration glitches.

### 3.0L Twin-Turbocharged Inline-6 Engine

The 3.0-liter twin-turbocharged inline-6 engine (Hurricane) delivers high-output performance with strong torque ratings, though its complex forced-induction systems require careful monitoring. Reliability reports indicate occasional electronic and powertrain management hiccups in early production models.

**Turbocharger and Engine Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have reported sudden engine warning lights, severe loss of power, and unexpected vehicle stalling while at highway speeds, accompanied by 'Shift to Park and Restart' messages.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified technician inspect the turbocharger assemblies, powertrain control module, and related wiring harnesses for faults or software updates.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 8-speed automatic transmission pairs with four-wheel-drive systems to deliver smooth, responsive gear changes across various driving conditions. Overall mechanical durability is generally sound, though occasional shifting hesitation tied to broader electrical glitches has been reported.

## Suspension

### Air Suspension

The adaptive air suspension provides a plush ride quality and adjustable ground clearance, though its complex electronic sensors can occasionally cause leveling faults.

**Suspension Leveling Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** The electronic air suspension can occasionally become stuck in specialized modes, such as tire jack mode, leaving the vehicle riding at an uneven height.
- **Solution:** Calibrate the suspension control module and inspect height sensors or air compressor lines for leaks or faults.

## Steering

### Steering Assembly

Steering feel is generally well-regarded for a large SUV, though electronic steering assist dependency leaves it vulnerable to broader electrical system faults.

**Loss of Power Assist During Electrical Failures**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers note that when major electrical faults trigger 'Systems Disabled' warnings, the power steering can suddenly become heavy or fail entirely.
- **Solution:** Address underlying electrical system root causes, as power steering functionality is closely tied to the vehicle's electronic architecture.

## Electrical

### Electrical System

The extensive electronics suite powering the infotainment, driver aids, and comfort features has been a primary source of owner complaints, ranging from parasitic battery drains to catastrophic system resets.

**Premature Battery Depletion and Complete System Shutdown**
- **Symptoms:** Owners experience repeated battery drainage, leaving the vehicle entirely dead, alongside sudden total electrical failures that knock out steering assist, braking displays, and engine power while driving.
- **Solution:** Inspect the charging system, battery draw sources, and main power distribution modules for parasitic drains or faulty software logic.

## Exterior

### Windshield and Trim

Exterior trim pieces and glass components require close inspection for alignment issues and secure fastening on early production models.

**Windshield Distortion and Loose Trim**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have observed visual distortion in factory windshields or exterior window trim pieces detaching unexpectedly at highway speeds.
- **Solution:** Have exterior trim re-secured or replaced under warranty, and inspect windshields for optical clarity or stress cracks.

## Interior

### Seats

Cabin appointments are exceptionally luxurious, but automated features like power-adjusting seats can experience pneumatic or electronic control faults.

**Uncontrolled Lumbar Inflation**
- **Symptoms:** The front passenger seat lumbar or massage system unexpectedly self-inflates to maximum pressure without user input, creating a hard, uncomfortable bulge in the seatback.
- **Solution:** Dealers typically need to replace or reprogram the seat control module and pneumatic bladders to restore normal seat function.

## Recall Check

**[Recall 21V919000: Seats](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Jeep&model=Grand+Wagoneer&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 207, "Seating Systems." A seat back that is not locked into position can increase the risk of injury in a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace as necessary, the rear center-seat recliner strap, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V873000: Air Bags: Air Bag/Restraint Control Module:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Jeep&model=Grand+Wagoneer&modelYear=2022)**

- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 111, "Rear Visibility." A rearview camera that does not display an image reduces the driver's rear view, increasing the risk of a crash.

## Summary Verdict

The 2022 Jeep Grand Wagoneer offers immense luxury, capability, and passenger space, but its first-model-year status brings notable reliability risks. Prospective buyers should prioritize vehicles with comprehensive service histories, verify that all electrical and powertrain updates have been completed, and strongly consider an extended warranty to mitigate potential out-of-pocket repair costs.
