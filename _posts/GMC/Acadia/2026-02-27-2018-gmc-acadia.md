# 2018 GMC Acadia Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2018 GMC Acadia is a midsize three-row crossover offering comfortable passenger accommodations and modern tech, making it a popular choice for families. However, prospective buyers should carefully review owner-reported complaint trends, particularly regarding persistent electrical and drivetrain quirks. While many owners enjoy its ride quality and utility, checking service records for common recurring issues can save buyers from unexpected headaches down the road.

---

## Engine

### 2.5L 4-Cylinder

A naturally aspirated 2.5-liter four-cylinder engine producing around 193 horsepower, prioritizing fuel economy for everyday commuting.

### 3.6L V6

A robust 3.6-liter V6 engine delivering approximately 310 horsepower, providing strong acceleration and towing capability for the midsize crossover.

**Thermostat and Temperature Sensor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The temperature gauge drops to cold unexpectedly, the cooling fan runs loudly, and the vehicle may display a 'Steer assist reduced' warning alongside a P0128 diagnostic code.
- **Solution:** Replace the faulty thermostat and/or temperature sensor.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

A conventional 6-speed automatic transmission paired typically with the base four-cylinder engine.

### 9-Speed Automatic Transmission

A 9-speed automatic transmission paired with V6 models, offering smooth highway cruising and optimized efficiency.

**Transmission Slippage and Power Loss**
- **Symptoms:** The transmission slips while driving, causing the vehicle to progressively lose power until it can no longer move.
- **Solution:** Professional diagnostic inspection; severe cases may require extensive transmission and driveshaft rebuilding or replacement.
- **Cost:** $7,000

### Shifter Microswitch / Park Detection

Electrical sensors and cabin electronics that govern gear position sensing, accessory power, and ignition control.

**Shift to Park Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Despite placing the gear selector into park, the dashboard repeatedly displays a 'Shift to Park' warning with continuous audible chimes, preventing the vehicle from shutting off or locking properly and potentially draining the battery.
- **Solution:** Repair or replace the shifter assembly or internal park switch mechanism.
- **Cost:** $1,000

## Steering

### Electronic Power Steering

The power steering system relies on electronic assistance to provide smooth maneuvering at low speeds and steady feedback on the highway.

**Steering Assist Reduction**
- **Symptoms:** The dashboard displays 'Steer assist reduced. Drive with care' and steering effort increases significantly while driving.
- **Solution:** Inspect electrical connections, software modules, or replace failing steering components.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V358000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=GMC&model=Acadia&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** A damaged fuel line can create a fuel leak, increasing the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will replace the high pressure fuel pump, and high pressure fuel pipe, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V516000: Power Train:Driveline:Driveshaft](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=GMC&model=Acadia&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** If the front right intermediate drive shaft assembly separates while driving, the vehicle will lose propulsion, increasing the risk of a crash. If the assembly separates while parked on a grade, without the park brake engaged, the vehicle may move unintentionally, increasing the risk of a vehicle roll-away.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will replace the right front intermediate drive shaft assembly, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2018 GMC Acadia presents a mixed picture for used car buyers. While it offers versatile seating and capable engine options, it is plagued by a prominent, widely reported 'Shift to Park' electrical defect that can prevent vehicles from shutting off and drain batteries. Additionally, buyers should inspect for cooling system thermostat issues and potential transmission concerns. Thoroughly testing all electronics and reviewing service history prior to purchase is strongly recommended.
