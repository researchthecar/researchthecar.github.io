# 2019 GMC Acadia Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2019 GMC Acadia, buyers will find a comfortable, feature-packed midsize crossover that offers decent utility. However, a review of owner complaints and reliability data highlights a few stubborn trouble spots that deserve careful attention during a pre-purchase inspection. While many owners enjoy daily driving, recurring electrical and shifter bugs can turn a simple trip into a frustrating experience.

---

## Engine

### 2.5L 4-Cylinder

The naturally aspirated 2.5-liter four-cylinder engine produces approximately 193 horsepower, serving as the base power plant focused primarily on fuel economy for daily commuting.

### 3.6L V6

The 3.6-liter V6 engine delivers a stronger 310 horsepower for more confident passing and towing, though buyers should watch closely for thermostat and cooling system complaints.

**Thermostat and Cooling Failures**
- **Symptoms:** Illuminated check engine light accompanied by diagnostic trouble codes (such as P0128), and occasional auxiliary system disruptions.
- **Solution:** Replace the faulty thermostat and flush the engine coolant.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic

Paired with the base four-cylinder engine, this 6-speed automatic transmission provides routine shifting characteristics for everyday driving conditions.

### 9-Speed Automatic

The 9-speed automatic transmission is paired with the V6 engine to optimize highway efficiency, though specific internal line leaks have been reported by some owners.

**Transmission Fluid Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Harsh shifting or internal transmission damage stemming from fluid loss at the accumulator feed pipe.
- **Solution:** Inspect accumulator feed pipes for leaks and replace damaged transmission seals or components as necessary.

## Steering

### Electronic Steering Assist

The power steering assist framework utilizes electronic modules that occasionally throw reduction warnings during cold weather.

**Steering Assist Reduction Warning**
- **Symptoms:** Display of a "Steering Assist is Reduced. Drive with Care" warning message, sometimes tied to ambient temperature drops or sensor malfunctions.
- **Solution:** Check diagnostic trouble codes, test electrical connections, and replace faulty temperature sensors or steering control modules.

## Electrical

### Shifter Park Sensor and Console Electronics

Center console electrical components and gear position sensors are prone to recurring communication failures.

**"Shift to Park" Message and Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle fails to recognize it is in park, displaying persistent warning messages, sounding chimes, preventing the engine from shutting off, and draining the battery.
- **Solution:** Diagnose the gearshift microswitch assembly and replace faulty shifter components.
- **Cost:** $1,000

## Safety

### Advanced Driver Assistance Sensors

Active safety features provide helpful protection when functioning, but sensor longevity can be inconsistent.

**Blind Spot and Collision Warning Failures**
- **Symptoms:** Blind spot indicators or forward collision avoidance sensors stop working intermittently without an active recall.
- **Solution:** Inspect radar modules and wiring harnesses for moisture intrusion or failure.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V115000: Tires](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=GMC&model=Acadia&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** Over cured tires may develop a break in the sidewall resulting in sudden air loss or a belt edge separation which could lead to a tread/belt loss. Either condition can cause a loss of vehicle control, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the tires, replacing them as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V668000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=GMC&model=Acadia&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** Missing bolts on the start-stop accumulator endcap could result in a transmission oil leak and may progress to a loss of propulsion, increasing the risk of a crash. A transmission fluid leak in the presence of an ignition source may increase the risk of fire.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the start-stop transmission accumulator and replace it if any bolts are missing, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2019 GMC Acadia offers a comfortable ride and versatile cabin space, making it a tempting choice on the used market. However, prospective buyers should thoroughly test the gear shifter to ensure it reliably registers the park position, as the notorious "Shift to Park" defect can drain batteries and prevent ignition shutdown. A comprehensive pre-purchase inspection checking for coolant leaks, thermostat operation, and transmission fluid integrity will go a long way toward ensuring a worry-free ownership experience.
