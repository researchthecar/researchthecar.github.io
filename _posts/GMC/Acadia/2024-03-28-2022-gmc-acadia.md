**Introduction**

The 2022 GMC Acadia is a mid-size SUV that offers a spacious and comfortable interior, along with a range of engine and transmission options. It is powered by a standard 2.5-liter four-cylinder engine, with a 3.6-liter V6 and a 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine available as options. Transmission options include a nine-speed automatic transmission and a six-speed automatic transmission for the base engine.

While the Acadia has received praise for its comfortable ride and spacious interior, it has also been criticized for its lackluster fuel economy and compromised handling.

**Engine**

**2.5-liter Four-Cylinder Engine (193 horsepower, 188 lb-ft of torque)**

* **Engine Oil Consumption Issue:** Excessive oil consumption leading to low oil levels and potential engine damage.
    * Solution: Engine replacement
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $4,000 - $6,000+
    * Recalls: NHTSA Campaign Number 22V-319

* **Timing Chain Tensioner Failure:** Premature wear of the timing chain tensioner, causing a rattling noise and potential engine damage.
    * Solution: Timing chain and tensioner replacement
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,000

**3.6-liter V6 Engine (310 horsepower, 271 lb-ft of torque)**

* **Air Intake Manifold Failure:** Malfunctioning air intake manifold, resulting in rough idling, poor acceleration, and potential engine damage.
    * Solution: Air intake manifold replacement
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000

* **Valve Spring Failure:** Weak valve springs, causing excessive valve noise and reduced engine performance.
    * Solution: Valve spring replacement
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $600

**2.0-liter Turbocharged Four-Cylinder Engine (230 horsepower, 258 lb-ft of torque)**

* **Turbocharger Failure:** Premature turbocharger failure, leading to reduced engine power and potential damage to other engine components.
    * Solution: Turbocharger replacement
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,000

* **Fuel Injector Issue:** Leaking fuel injectors, causing rough idling, poor fuel economy, and potential engine damage.
    * Solution: Fuel injector replacement
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500

**Transmission**

**Nine-Speed Automatic Transmission (available with all engines)**

* **Shifting Problems:** Erratic shifting, delayed shifts, or slipping gears.
    * Solution: Transmission fluid change or replacement
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600

* **Torque Converter Failure:** Premature failure of the torque converter, causing loss of power or slipping gears.
    * Solution: Torque converter replacement
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500

**Six-Speed Automatic Transmission (base engine only)**

**Suspension**

* **Strut Mount Failure:** Worn-out strut mounts, leading to clunking or rattling noises.
    * Solution: Strut mount replacement
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400

* **Rear Suspension Squeak:** Squeaking noise from the rear suspension, particularly noticeable over bumps or uneven surfaces.
    * Solution: Lubrication or replacement of suspension components
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain Issue:** Excessive battery drain, leading to premature battery failure.
    * Solution: Battery inspection and replacement if necessary
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300

* **Headlight Malfunction:** Headlights flickering or turning off intermittently.
    * Solution: Headlight bulb or wiring replacement
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500

**Exterior**

**Body Panel Rust:** Premature rust formation on body panels, particularly around the wheel wells and lower doors.
    * Solution: Rust repair or panel replacement
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+

**Liftgate Struts:** Weak or failing liftgate struts, causing the liftgate to open slowly or fall suddenly.
    * Solution: Liftgate strut replacement
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200

**Interior**

* **Air Conditioning Vents Rattling:** Loose or broken air conditioning vents, causing a rattling noise.
    * Solution: Vent repair or replacement
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200

* **Infotainment System Malfunction:** Software glitches, display issues, or unresponsive touch screen.
    * Solution: Software update or infotainment system replacement
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500

**Summary**

The 2022 GMC Acadia has been plagued by a range of issues affecting its engine, transmission, suspension, and other components. While some issues are relatively minor and inexpensive to fix, others can be more severe and costly. The engine oil consumption issue, in particular, has been a major concern for owners, leading to numerous recalls and potentially catastrophic engine damage. Overall, the Acadia's reliability has been below average, with some owners experiencing multiple issues within a short period of ownership.
