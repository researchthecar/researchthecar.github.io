# 2016 GMC Acadia Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2016 GMC Acadia, prospective buyers should carefully weigh its spacious interior and family-friendly utility against a collection of known mechanical and electrical complaints. While it offers comfortable seating and decent performance, owner reports filed with safety agencies highlight recurring trouble spots ranging from transmission hesitation and shuddering to sudden steering and electrical glitches. Thoroughly inspecting the powertrain, steering rack, and service history can help you avoid costly surprises down the road.

---

## Engine

### 3.6L V6 Engine

Equipped with a 3.6-liter V6 producing around 281 to 288 horsepower, this engine provides strong acceleration suitable for hauling families and cargo. Overall mechanical reliability is generally decent, though owners occasionally report accessory drive or sensor issues.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

Features a 6-speed automatic transmission paired with front-wheel or all-wheel drive. This unit is a frequent subject of owner complaints regarding rough shifting, shuddering, and premature wear.

**Transmission Shudder and Hesitation**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle shudders or hesitates during acceleration, particularly around mid-range gears or when passing, sometimes accompanied by a rumble feeling.
- **Solution:** Diagnose transmission internal wear or torque converter function; fluid flushes or component rebuilding/replacement may be required.

## Steering

### Steering Rack and Power Assist

The steering system relies on hydraulic or electronic assist to maneuver the heavy crossover. Failures in the rack and pinion or power assist pumps can create severe driving hazards.

**Power Steering Failure and Rack Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Sudden loss of power steering assist making the wheel extremely difficult to turn, or sudden rack and pinion failure while driving.
- **Solution:** Inspect the steering rack, pinion, and power steering pump; replace failed components as necessary.

## Safety

### Seat Belts

Occupant safety restraints are critical, though isolated complaints point to physical failures in seat belt anchoring hardware.

**Seat Belt Latch or Cable Breakage**
- **Symptoms:** The mounting cable or latch mechanism for the front seat belts breaks unexpectedly during normal buckling.
- **Solution:** Replace the damaged seat belt assembly and anchor cable.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 15V609000: Visibility:Windshield Wiper/Washer:Motor](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=GMC&model=Acadia&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** A wiper motor that overheats increases the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** GM has notified owners, and dealers will replace the windshield wiper motor cover assembly, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 15V833000: Seats](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=GMC&model=Acadia&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** If the seat frame is not welded in the correct places, the seat may not perform as intended, and in the event of a crash, the seat occupant could be at an increased risk of injury.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the third row left side lower seat frame, replacing it as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V246000: Air Bags:Critical Fasteners](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=GMC&model=Acadia&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** The side curtain air bags may not be properly attached. Unsecured side curtain air bags may not perform as intended, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will inspect and, if necessary, realign or replace the weld nuts, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V334000: Air Bags:Frontal:Driver Side:Inflator Module](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=GMC&model=Acadia&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** The driver's air bag inflator may explode during deployment, due to a manufacturing defect. An inflator explosion may result in sharp metal fragments striking the driver or other occupants, resulting in serious injury or death.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the driver's air bag module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2016 GMC Acadia delivers versatile three-row passenger space and everyday utility, but used car buyers must approach with eyes wide open regarding potential mechanical liabilities. Transmission shudder, torque converter concerns, and occasional steering rack failures represent the most significant risk areas. Prioritize models with a comprehensive, documented service history, and ensure a pre-purchase inspection is performed focusing heavily on the transmission and steering components.
