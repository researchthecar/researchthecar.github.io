# 2021 GMC Acadia Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2021 GMC Acadia offers a comfortable midsize SUV experience with versatile seating and modern tech, but potential buyers should approach with awareness of certain reported mechanical concerns. Owner feedback submitted to safety regulators highlights notable problem areas, particularly concerning powertrain reliability, fuel delivery, and steering system glitches. Reviewing these recurring patterns can help prospective buyers inspect vehicles thoroughly and budget for potential pre-owned maintenance.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged I4

A 2.0-liter turbocharged inline-four engine producing around 228 horsepower, providing an efficient base option for daily commuting. Buyers should verify regular oil maintenance and check for proper turbocharger operation.

### 3.6L V6

A 3.6-liter V6 engine generating approximately 310 horsepower, offering stronger towing capability and highway acceleration. This standard-setting GM powerplant requires routine servicing to maintain long-term reliability.

### Fuel Pump & Control Module

Components responsible for delivering pressurized fuel from the tank to the engine, which occasionally encounter electrical or module-level failures.

**Fuel Pump Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle fails to start or cranks continuously without firing, often leaving drivers stranded and requiring a tow.
- **Solution:** Diagnose the fuel control module and fuel pump assembly, and replace faulty components as necessary.

## Transmission

### 9-Speed Automatic Transmission

A nine-speed automatic transmission paired with front-wheel or all-wheel drive. While designed for smooth cruising and efficiency, some owners have reported slipping and harsh engagement symptoms.

**Transmission Slipping and Jerky Shifting**
- **Symptoms:** Transmission slips during acceleration, accompanied by harsh or jerky shifts, particularly when moving from a stop.
- **Solution:** Inspect transmission fluid condition, update powertrain control modules, or repair/replace internal components such as the torque converter.
- **Cost:** Over $7,000 for major torque converter and transmission repairs

## Suspension

### Rear Suspension Links

Suspension geometry and linkage components that maintain stable handling, wheel alignment, and ride comfort.

**Rear Toe Link Wear and Misalignment**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle unintendedly drifts to one side while driving, or rear suspension components are found to be loose prematurely.
- **Solution:** Inspect rear suspension links for wear, replace worn toe links, and perform a complete four-wheel alignment.

## Brakes

### Brake Booster and Lines

Hydraulic and vacuum-assisted braking components designed to bring the SUV to a safe stop under various driving conditions.

**Brake Booster Failure and Pedal Stiffening**
- **Symptoms:** Brake pedal suddenly becomes extremely stiff and inoperable, resulting in severely reduced stopping power and potential engine damage from vacuum pump debris.
- **Solution:** Inspect the vacuum pump and brake booster assembly, replacing failed components and clearing any metal debris from related systems.

## Steering

### Electronic Power Steering System

Electric power-assist steering system that relies on vehicle sensor inputs to provide smooth maneuverability at all speeds.

**Steering Assist Reduction**
- **Symptoms:** Driver receives a 'Steering Assist Reduced Drive with Care' warning message on the dash, accompanied by a stiff steering wheel, dropping temperature gauge, and continuously running cooling fans.
- **Solution:** Inspect the thermostat and engine cooling sensors, as temperature sensor failures can trigger electronic power steering fault modes.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V422000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Hoses, Lines/Piping, And Fittings](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=GMC&model=Acadia&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** Improperly attached and seated fuel supply lines may loosen or separate, causing a fuel leak and increasing the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace fuel supply lines as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V709000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=GMC&model=Acadia&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** An inoperative rearview camera can reduce the driver's rear visibility, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the coaxial cables, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2021 GMC Acadia presents a blend of practical utility and family-friendly features, but used-car buyers should perform a diligent inspection before purchasing. Pay close attention to transmission shift quality, verify proper operation of the steering and cooling systems, and ensure the braking components have no history of vacuum pump or booster issues. A thorough pre-purchase inspection by an independent mechanic is highly recommended to catch potential underlying mechanical or electrical concerns.
