**Introduction:**

The 2021 GMC Acadia is a midsize SUV offered with two engine options: a 2.5L 4-cylinder and a 3.6L V6. Both engines are paired with a 9-speed automatic transmission. The Acadia is known for its spacious interior, comfortable ride, and user-friendly infotainment system. However, it has also received some criticism for its poor fuel economy and lackluster performance.

**Engine**

* **2.5L 4-cylinder**
    * **Engine Misfire**
        * Description/Symptoms: Rough idle, lack of power, increased emissions
        * Solution: Replace spark plugs and ignition coils
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
        * Recalls: None Found
* **3.6L V6**
    * **Timing Chain Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: Engine rattling, loss of power, engine failure
        * Solution: Replace timing chain and guides
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

* **9-speed Automatic**
    * **Shift Shudder**
        * Description/Symptoms: Vibration when shifting gears
        * Solution: Update transmission software
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: Free if under warranty, $100 - $200+ if out of warranty
        * Recalls: None Found
    * **Transmission Slipping**
        * Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears, loss of power
        * Solution: Rebuild or replace transmission
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+
        * Recalls: None Found
    * **Transmission Fluid Leak**
        * Description/Symptoms: Transmission fluid leaking from the transmission pan or lines
        * Solution: Replace transmission pan gasket or lines
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **No Issues Found**

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain**
        * Description/Symptoms: Battery dies prematurely
        * Solution: Replace battery or repair electrical system
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
        * Recalls: None Found
* **Headlight Malfunction**
        * Description/Symptoms: Headlights flickering or not working
        * Solution: Replace headlight bulbs or repair wiring
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Tailgate Latch Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: Tailgate not closing or latching properly
        * Solution: Replace tailgate latch
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Climate Control Malfunction**
        * Description/Symptoms: A/C or heater not working
        * Solution: Replace climate control module or repair wiring
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
        * Recalls: None Found
* **Infotainment System Glitch**
        * Description/Symptoms: Screen freezing, unresponsive touch inputs, audio cutting out
        * Solution: Update software or replace infotainment unit
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: Free if under warranty, $500 - $1,000+ if out of warranty
        * Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

Overall, the 2021 GMC Acadia has received some reported issues with its engine, transmission, and electrical systems. The severity of these issues can range from minor inconveniences to major repairs. It's important to note that not all Acadias will experience these issues, and the cost to fix can vary depending on the specific issue and the location of the repair. If you are considering purchasing a 2021 GMC Acadia, it is recommended to research specific issues and potential recalls before making a decision.
