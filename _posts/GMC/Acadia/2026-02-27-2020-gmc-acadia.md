# 2020 GMC Acadia Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used midsize SUV, the 2020 GMC Acadia presents a versatile cabin and modern technology package that appeals to families. However, prospective buyers should carefully review historical owner feedback regarding mechanical and electrical quirks. While many drivers enjoy its comfortable ride and drivetrain options, addressing recurring patterns in engine temperature monitoring and electronic accessories can sometimes involve unexpected service visits.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbo I4

This turbocharged 4-cylinder engine generates approximately 228 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque, providing a balance of efficiency and daily driveability for the Acadia.

### 3.6L V6

The naturally aspirated V6 engine pushes around 310 horsepower and 271 lb-ft of torque, offering robust acceleration and towing capability, though it is tied to notable reports of sensor-related thermostat glitches.

**Thermostat and Steering Assist Warnings**
- **Symptoms:** A faulty engine thermostat and coolant sensor can trigger dashboard messages such as 'Steering Assist Reduced, Drive With Care' alongside erratic temperature gauge behavior.
- **Solution:** Replace the failed thermostat and coolant sensor assembly to restore normal engine temperature signals and eliminate incorrect steering warnings.
- **Cost:** $1000+

## Transmission

### 9-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 9-speed automatic transmission pairs with front-wheel or all-wheel drive configurations, delivering generally smooth highway cruising while occasionally exhibiting torque converter durability concerns.

**Torque Converter Shudder and Rough Shifting**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may experience a noticeable vibration or shudder during acceleration, hesitation when leaving a stoplight, or rough gear changes.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified technician inspect the transmission fluid condition and torque converter; fluid flushes or component replacements may be required.

## Brakes

### Electronic Parking Brake

The service brake and electronic parking brake systems provide solid stopping power, though occasional electronic software triggers can cause unexpected brake actuation.

**Unintended Parking Brake Engagement**
- **Symptoms:** The electronic parking brake may unexpectedly engage or activate automatically while driving at low speeds or parking on inclines without driver input.
- **Solution:** Have a dealership inspect the body control module and parking brake software logic for potential resets or updates.

## Electrical

### Infotainment and Backup Camera

Electrical and infotainment accessories offer great convenience features when operating properly, though owners occasionally report display blanks and rear-view camera glitches.

**Backup Camera and Screen Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The rearview camera display may intermittently show a black screen, blue screen, or error icon, while the infotainment display develops lines or restarts spontaneously.
- **Solution:** Inspect software calibrations, wiring harnesses, or replace the faulty camera module and display unit as necessary.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V115000: Tires](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=GMC&model=Acadia&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** Over cured tires may develop a break in the sidewall resulting in sudden air loss or a belt edge separation which could lead to a tread/belt loss. Either condition can cause a loss of vehicle control, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the tires, replacing them as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V639000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=GMC&model=Acadia&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** Insufficient fuel supply can result in an engine stall without warning, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will replace the fuel pump module. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V684000: Tires](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=GMC&model=Acadia&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** Overcured tires may experience a carcass break in the sidewall resulting in a sudden air loss or could develop a belt edge separation which could lead to partial or full tread/belt loss. Either condition increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will inspect vehicles and replace tires that have DOT number and production mold number combinations identified by Continental, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V446000: Air Bags:Side/Window:Curtain:Inflator](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=GMC&model=Acadia&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** The diffuser component of the Roof-Rail Air Bag (RRAB) inflator may not have been properly crimped to the inflator and could separate from the inflator during air bag deployment. If the diffuser separates from the inflator during deployment, RRAB performance may be degraded, increasing the risk of injury in a crash.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will replace the suspect RRAB modules, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V668000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=GMC&model=Acadia&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** Missing bolts on the start-stop accumulator endcap could result in a transmission oil leak and may progress to a loss of propulsion, increasing the risk of a crash. A transmission fluid leak in the presence of an ignition source may increase the risk of fire.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the start-stop transmission accumulator and replace it if any bolts are missing, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V709000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=GMC&model=Acadia&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** An inoperative rearview camera can reduce the driver's rear visibility, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the coaxial cables, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2020 GMC Acadia is an appealing midsize SUV offering spacious seating and strong engine choices, but buyers should perform a thorough pre-purchase inspection. Pay close attention to cooling system sensor behavior, transmission shift smoothness, and the functionality of electronics like the backup camera. Verifying that past recalls and common thermostat issues have been addressed will help ensure a more reliable ownership experience.
