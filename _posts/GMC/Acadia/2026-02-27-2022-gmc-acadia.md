# 2022 GMC Acadia Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used GMC Acadia, understanding its reliability profile can help you spot potential trouble spots before making a purchase. While owner reports to federal safety regulators highlight several recurring glitches—particularly involving cooling systems, electronics, and steering warnings—many drivers continue to enjoy the model's comfortable ride and family-friendly packaging. Reviewing these common pain points will help you navigate your test drive and inspection with confidence.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged I4

The 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine produces approximately 228 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque. It offers a balanced blend of efficiency and everyday drivability for base and mid-tier trims, though buyers should watch for related cooling and thermostat sensors.

### 3.6L V6

The 3.6-liter V6 engine pushes out 310 horsepower and 271 lb-ft of torque, providing robust acceleration for highway passing and towing. It is a popular option for larger families, but owners have occasionally reported thermostat and temperature sensor glitches.

**Thermostat and Cooling System Faults**
- **Symptoms:** Illuminated check engine lights, a drop in the temperature gauge, cooling fans running loudly for extended periods after shutdown, and associated 'steering assist reduced' warnings.
- **Solution:** Diagnose the cooling system and replace faulty thermostats or temperature sensors as needed.

## Transmission

### 9-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 9-speed automatic transmission pairs with both engine choices to optimize fuel economy and smooth power delivery across various driving conditions. Overall mechanical durability is generally sound, though proper fluid maintenance remains essential.

## Steering

### Electric Power Steering

Steering assist features rely heavily on vehicle sensor inputs to adjust effort dynamically, making proper diagnostic scans vital if warnings appear.

**Steering Assist Reduced Warning**
- **Symptoms:** A warning message reading 'steering assist reduced, drive with care' appears on the driver display, sometimes linked to cooling system temperature anomalies.
- **Solution:** Inspect thermostat and temperature sensors, as engine cooling faults can trigger cascading electronic warnings.

## Electrical

### Infotainment and Dashboard Screens

The electrical architecture governs modern cabin tech, safety camera systems, and convenience features, though intermittent display glitches have been reported by some owners.

**Intermittent Screen Blackout**
- **Symptoms:** The entire dashboard instrument panel and infotainment entertainment screens occasionally go completely black while driving.
- **Solution:** Have a dealership inspect software versions or module connections; software updates may resolve intermittent blank displays.

## Safety

### Seat Belts

Occupant safety systems include standard belt pretensioners and airbags, with rear-row belt mechanics warranting close inspection during a used vehicle test drive.

**Rear Seat Belt Binding**
- **Symptoms:** Rear passenger side or driver-side rear seat belts fail to extend or retract smoothly when passengers attempt to buckle up or secure a child safety seat.
- **Solution:** Inspect seat belt retractors and anchor points for binding or mechanical wear, replacing components if they fail to operate smoothly.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V702000: Air Bags](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=GMC&model=Acadia&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 208, "Occupant Crash Protection." The air bags will not deploy with an unactivated SDM, increasing the risk of injury in a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 22V427000: Suspension:Rear](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=GMC&model=Acadia&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** The left-rear suspension toe link may have been tightened improperly, which could result in a loose or separated toe link. A loose toe link can separate and cause a loss of vehicle control, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the left-rear suspension and replace the fastener if it is loose or missing, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V446000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Storage:Tank Assembly](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=GMC&model=Acadia&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** The fuel tank rollover valve may not provide a proper seal in the event of a rollover crash. A fuel tank rollover valve that does not properly seal can result in a fuel leak during a rollover crash, increasing the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the fuel tank and replace the fuel tank shell if the rollover valve is found to be dislodged or missing, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

When evaluating a used model, conducting a thorough pre-purchase inspection can save you from unexpected headaches. Pay special attention to cooling system behavior, dashboard electronics, and seat belt operation during your test drive. While many owners experience trouble-free motoring, verifying that all technical bulletins and sensor recalls have been addressed will help ensure a reliable ownership experience.
