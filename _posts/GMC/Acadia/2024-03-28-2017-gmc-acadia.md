**Introduction:**

The 2017 GMC Acadia is a mid-size SUV that offers a spacious interior, comfortable ride, and a range of engine and transmission options. It comes with a standard 3.6L V6 engine that produces 310 horsepower and 271 lb-ft of torque. Other engine options include a 2.5L 4-cylinder engine with 193 horsepower and 188 lb-ft of torque, and a 2.8L Duramax turbo-diesel engine with 181 horsepower and 369 lb-ft of torque. Transmission options include a 6-speed automatic, an 8-speed automatic, and a 9-speed automatic.

Despite its many positive attributes, the 2017 GMC Acadia has several issues that owners have reported.

**Engine**

**3.6L V6 Engine**

* **Intake Manifold Gasket Failure:** The intake manifold gasket may fail, causing coolant and oil leaks. Symptoms include a rough idle, loss of power, and overheating. The solution is to replace the gasket. The estimated cost to fix is $300 - $500.
* **Timing Chain Guide Failure:** The timing chain guide may fail, causing the timing chain to jump or break. Symptoms include a rattling noise from the engine, loss of power, and engine failure. The solution is to replace the timing chain guide and the timing chain. The estimated cost to fix is $1,000 - $2,000.

**2.5L 4-Cylinder Engine**

* **Intake Valve Issue:** There is an issue with the intake valves that can cause them to stick open. Symptoms include a rough idle, loss of power, and engine failure. The solution is to replace the intake valves. The estimated cost to fix is $1,000 - $2,000.

**Transmission**

**6-Speed Automatic Transmission:**

* **Shifting Issues:** Some owners have reported problems with the transmission shifting smoothly. Symptoms include delayed shifts, hard shifts, and slipping gears. The solution may involve updating the transmission software or replacing the transmission. The estimated cost to fix is $500 - $1,500.

**8-Speed Automatic Transmission:**

* **No Issues Found**

**9-Speed Automatic Transmission:**

* **Shifting Issues:** As with the 6-speed automatic, some owners have reported issues with the transmission shifting smoothly. Symptoms include delayed shifts, hard shifts, and slipping gears. The solution may involve updating the transmission software or replacing the transmission. The estimated cost to fix is $500 - $1,500.

**Suspension**

* **Strut Wear:** The front struts may wear out prematurely. Symptoms include a bouncy ride, poor handling, and excessive noise. The solution is to replace the struts. The estimated cost to fix is $500 - $1,000 per strut.

**Electrical**

* **Battery Issues:** Some owners have reported problems with the battery draining prematurely. Symptoms include difficulty starting the vehicle, dim headlights, and flickering interior lights. The solution may involve replacing the battery or inspecting the electrical system for a parasitic drain. The estimated cost to fix is $100 - $500.

**Exterior**

* **Headlight Moisture:** Some owners have reported that moisture can accumulate inside the headlights. Symptoms include condensation or fogging inside the headlights. The solution is to replace the headlights. The estimated cost to fix is $500 - $1,000 per headlight.

**Interior**

* **Dashboard Squeaking:** Some owners have reported that the dashboard may squeak or rattle. Symptoms include a creaking or rattling noise coming from the dashboard. The solution may involve tightening screws or adding padding to the dashboard. The estimated cost to fix is $100 - $500.

**Summary**

While the 2017 GMC Acadia has many positive attributes, it has several issues that owners have reported. The severity and cost of these issues can vary. Some issues, such as intake manifold gasket failure, can be relatively minor and inexpensive to fix. However, other issues, such as timing chain guide failure, can be more serious and expensive to repair. If you are considering purchasing a 2017 GMC Acadia, be sure to do your research and be aware of the potential issues that you may encounter.
