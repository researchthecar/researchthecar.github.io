# 2023 GMC Acadia Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2023 GMC Acadia is a midsize three-row crossover offering a comfortable ride, intuitive technology, and multiple engine configurations. While most owners enjoy its versatility, used car buyers should review owner-reported complaints regarding electrical glitches, powertrain behavior, and occasional accessory failures before purchasing.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbo 4-Cylinder Engine

This 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine produces approximately 228 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque, offering a balanced mix of efficiency and everyday drivability for the Acadia.

### 3.6L V6 Engine

The 3.6-liter V6 engine delivers 310 horsepower and 271 lb-ft of torque, providing robust acceleration and towing capability for highway driving and family hauling.

**Engine Stalling and Power Loss**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers report unexpected engine stalling while driving or sudden loss of motive power, sometimes requiring a vehicle restart.
- **Solution:** Have a certified mechanic or dealer inspect the fuel system, control modules, and sensor connections for faults.

## Transmission

### 9-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 9-speed automatic transmission pairs with both front-wheel drive and all-wheel drive configurations, designed to optimize fuel economy while maintaining smooth power delivery.

**Shifting Noise and Drivetrain Shudder**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may experience a clunking noise during low-speed gear changes, occasional vibrations between 45-60 mph, or momentary shuddering during acceleration.
- **Solution:** Consult a dealership to check for powertrain software updates or mechanical wear within the transmission and control modules.

## Suspension

### Rear Suspension

The multi-link rear suspension setup helps maintain stable handling and a composed ride across varied road conditions.

**Suspension Toe Link Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Routine maintenance inspections may reveal premature wear or damage to the rear suspension toe links, requiring component replacement.
- **Solution:** Inspect rear suspension components during routine tire rotations and replace worn toe links as needed.

## Electrical

### Electrical and Infotainment System

Electrical architecture supports advanced driver aids, infotainment, and vehicle telematics, though some owners have experienced component freezes and backordered replacement parts.

**Module Failures and Screen Freezing**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have reported issues such as telematic control module failures, backup camera dropouts, and infotainment screens freezing or disconnecting from smartphone pairing.
- **Solution:** Software resets or replacing affected control modules by a dealer can resolve persistent connectivity and camera malfunctions.

## Safety

### Forward Collision Avoidance

Advanced safety features are designed to protect occupants, but sensor calibration issues can occasionally result in false-positive collision alerts or braking events.

**Spontaneous Emergency Braking**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may experience sudden, unexpected deceleration as the automated emergency braking system triggers without an actual obstruction ahead.
- **Solution:** Have the forward-facing camera and radar sensors inspected, cleaned, and recalibrated by a qualified service center.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V708000: Equipment:Other:Labels](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=GMC&model=Acadia&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 110, "Tire Selection and Rims." Illegible tire size information may result in the wrong tires being installed, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the vehicle certification label, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V043000: Power Train:Driveline:Driveshaft](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=GMC&model=Acadia&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** Either of these scenarios can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the right-front half-shaft assemblies, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V172000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=GMC&model=Acadia&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** Either of these scenarios can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the sun gears, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V681000: Visibility:Windshield](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=GMC&model=Acadia&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** A windshield that is not properly bonded may detach during a crash and increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the windshield and apply primer and urethane sealant. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2023 GMC Acadia is a spacious and capable family crossover, but prospective buyers should perform a thorough pre-purchase inspection. Pay close attention to electrical functions, transmission shift quality, and any history of powertrain or sensor complaints to ensure a reliable ownership experience.
