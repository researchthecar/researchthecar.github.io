# 2017 GMC Acadia Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2017 GMC Acadia is a midsize crossover offering spacious three-row seating and modern technology features, making it a popular choice for families. However, used car buyers should carefully inspect prospective vehicles for recurring electrical issues and powertrain quirks. While it provides a comfortable ride and decent fuel economy, historical owner complaints highlight specific trouble spots that require attention during a pre-purchase inspection.

---

## Engine

### 2.5L 4-Cylinder

The base 2.5-liter four-cylinder engine produces approximately 193 horsepower and 188 lb-ft of torque. It offers adequate efficiency for daily commuting, though it can feel underpowered when the vehicle is fully loaded with passengers and cargo.

### 3.6L V6

The available 3.6-liter V6 engine generates around 310 horsepower and 271 lb-ft of torque, providing strong acceleration and a higher towing capacity. It is generally robust, though minor cooling and sensor issues can occasionally arise.

**Thermostat Failure Leading to Steering Assist Warnings**
- **Symptoms:** A thermostat sticking open can trigger dashboard messages warning of reduced steering assist and increased steering effort.
- **Solution:** Replace the faulty thermostat and clear electronic control module codes.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

Paired primarily with the base engine, the 6-speed automatic transmission delivers smooth daily shifts but has received occasional owner reports concerning shudder and rough engagement.

**Transmission Shudder and Harsh Shifting**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may experience abnormal vehicle vibration, shuddering during acceleration, or delayed engagement when shifting gears.
- **Solution:** Inspect the transmission assembly and torque converter; fluid flushes or component replacements may be required.

### Hydra-Matic 9-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 9-speed automatic transmission offers wider gear ratios for improved highway efficiency and power delivery when paired with applicable engine options.

## Electrical

### Ignition and Shifter Microswitch

Electrical complaints center heavily around shifter sensor glitches and auxiliary battery drain issues that can leave drivers stranded.

**Shift to Park Warning Message**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers receive a persistent "Shift to Park" message on the instrument cluster even when the vehicle is securely placed in park, preventing the engine from shutting off and draining the battery.
- **Solution:** Repair or replace the shifter assembly, wiring harness jumper, or internal microswitches.

## Safety

### Seat Belts

Safety equipment generally performs well in collisions, but periodic physical inspection of seat belt tethers and latch mechanisms is recommended.

**Lower Seat Belt Cable Fatigue**
- **Symptoms:** The flexible braided steel cable attached to the lower seat belt anchor can experience fatigue failure over time without warning.
- **Solution:** Inspect lower seat belt anchorages and replace any damaged seat belt assembly components.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V516000: Power Train:Driveline:Driveshaft](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=GMC&model=Acadia&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** If the front right intermediate drive shaft assembly separates while driving, the vehicle will lose propulsion, increasing the risk of a crash. If the assembly separates while parked on a grade, without the park brake engaged, the vehicle may move unintentionally, increasing the risk of a vehicle roll-away.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will replace the right front intermediate drive shaft assembly, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V358000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=GMC&model=Acadia&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** A damaged fuel line can create a fuel leak, increasing the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will replace the high pressure fuel pump, and high pressure fuel pipe, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V802000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Foundation Components:Disc:Caliper](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=GMC&model=Acadia&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** If there is a brake fluid leak, braking performance may be reduced, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the front brake calipers, replacing the brake caliper assemblies, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V334000: Air Bags:Frontal:Driver Side:Inflator Module](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=GMC&model=Acadia&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** The driver's air bag inflator may explode during deployment, due to a manufacturing defect. An inflator explosion may result in sharp metal fragments striking the driver or other occupants, resulting in serious injury or death.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the driver's air bag module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2017 GMC Acadia is an appealing midsize SUV with flexible seating and strong V6 performance, but prospective buyers should approach with informed caution. The most prominent issue involves the "Shift to Park" sensor defect, which can prevent the vehicle from turning off and drain the battery. Buyers should thoroughly test the shifter mechanism, electronics, and transmission operation during a pre-purchase inspection to avoid unexpected repair bills.
