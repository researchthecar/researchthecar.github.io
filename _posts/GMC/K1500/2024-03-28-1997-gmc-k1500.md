The 1997 GMC K1500 is a full-size pickup truck that was available with a variety of engine and transmission options. The standard engine was a 5.7L V8 that produced 250 horsepower and 330 lb-ft of torque. Other engine options included a 5.0L V8, a 6.5L diesel V8, and a 7.4L V8. The K1500 was also available with a variety of transmission options, including a 4-speed automatic, a 5-speed manual, and a 6-speed manual.

People who like the 1997 GMC K1500 often praise its powerful engines, spacious interior, and towing capacity. However, the K1500 also has some common problems.

**Engine**

**5.7L V8**
* **Intake manifold gasket failure**
Description/Symptoms: Coolant and oil leaks, engine overheating, rough idle
Solution: Replace intake manifold gasket
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
Recalls: None Found

* **Vortec 5700 engine lifter failure**
Description/Symptoms: Tapping or ticking noise from the engine, loss of power, engine damage
Solution: Replace lifters
Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+
Recalls: None Found

**5.0L V8**
* **Intake manifold gasket failure**
Description/Symptoms: Coolant and oil leaks, engine overheating, rough idle
Solution: Replace intake manifold gasket
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
Recalls: None Found

**6.5L diesel V8**
* **Head gasket failure**
Description/Symptoms: Coolant and oil leaks, engine overheating, white smoke from exhaust
Solution: Replace head gasket
Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+
Recalls: None Found

* **Injection pump failure**
Description/Symptoms: Engine stalling, loss of power, black smoke from exhaust
Solution: Replace injection pump
Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
Recalls: None Found

**7.4L V8**
* **Intake manifold gasket failure**
Description/Symptoms: Coolant and oil leaks, engine overheating, rough idle
Solution: Replace intake manifold gasket
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**4-speed automatic**
* **Transmission slippage**
Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears, loss of power, slipping gears
Solution: Replace transmission
Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,500 - $4,000+
Recalls: None Found

* **Torque converter failure**
Description/Symptoms: Shuddering or vibration during acceleration, loss of power, transmission overheating
Solution: Replace torque converter
Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
Recalls: None Found

**5-speed manual**
* **Clutch failure**
Description/Symptoms: Grinding or slipping noise when shifting gears, difficulty shifting gears, loss of power
Solution: Replace clutch
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
Recalls: None Found

**6-speed manual**
* **Transmission noise**
Description/Symptoms: Whining or grinding noise from transmission, difficulty shifting gears
Solution: Replace transmission
Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,500 - $4,000+
Recalls: None Found

* **Front suspension wear and tear**
Description/Symptoms: Loose or worn ball joints, tie rods, or bushings, excessive play in steering, uneven tire wear
Solution: Replace worn or damaged suspension components
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
Recalls: None Found

* **Rear suspension sagging**
Description/Symptoms: Sagging or drooping rear end, uneven tire wear
Solution: Replace rear springs or shocks
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 1997 GMC K1500 is a reliable and capable pickup truck with a number of engine and transmission options to choose from. However, it does have some common problems, including engine intake manifold gasket failure, transmission slippage, and suspension wear and tear. The severity and cost of these issues can vary depending on the specific engine and transmission option, as well as the age and condition of the vehicle. Overall, the 1997 GMC K1500 is a good choice for drivers who need a powerful and versatile pickup truck.
