**Introduction:**

The 1996 GMC K1500 is a full-size pickup truck that offers a combination of power and durability. It's available with several engine and transmission options, including a 5.7L V8 paired with a 4-speed automatic or manual transmission. The K1500 is praised for its spacious cabin, comfortable ride, and strong towing capabilities. However, it also has some known issues that potential buyers should be aware of.

**Engine**

* **5.7L V8**

  * **Intake Manifold Gasket Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Coolant and oil leaks, overheating
    * Solution: Replace intake manifold gaskets
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

* **4-Speed Automatic**

  * **Transmission Slippage:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears, delayed engagement, slipping gears
    * Solution: Replace transmission or rebuild
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Leaf Springs:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Sagging suspension, reduced ride height, rough ride
    * Solution: Replace leaf springs
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Alternator Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Dimming headlights, battery not charging, engine stalling
    * Solution: Replace or rebuild alternator
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $700+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Door Handle Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Difficulty opening or closing door, broken or loose handles
    * Solution: Replace door handles
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Window Regulator Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Difficulty raising or lowering windows, stuck windows
    * Solution: Replace window regulator
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

The 1996 GMC K1500 has some potential issues to consider, particularly with the transmission and suspension. These issues can be expensive to repair, so potential buyers should factor in these costs when purchasing the vehicle. However, the K1500 still remains a popular choice for those looking for a durable and capable pickup truck.
