**Introduction:** The 1997 GMC Sonoma was a compact pickup truck with various engine and transmission options. It was known for its utility and affordability but faced some common issues.

**Engine**

**2.2L Inline-4**

* **Intake Manifold Gasket Failure:** Leaking intake manifold gaskets cause a loss of power, rough idling, and increased fuel consumption. Requires replacement of gaskets. $200 - $400+
* **Distributor Pickup Coil Failure:** Symptoms include hard starting, no starting, or engine stalling. Replacement of the distributor pickup coil is needed. $100 - $200+

**4.3L V6**

* **Timing Chain Tensioner Failure:** A faulty timing chain tensioner can cause a rattling or grinding noise from the engine. Requires repair or replacement of the timing chain tensioner. $500 - $700+

**Transmission**

**4-Speed Automatic 4L60-E**

* **3-4 Shift Solenoid Failure:** Transmission may not shift properly, especially between 3rd and 4th gears. Requires replacement of the 3-4 shift solenoid. $200 - $400+
* **Reverse Gear Loss:** Failure of the reverse gear band can cause loss of reverse gear. Repair or replacement of the transmission is necessary. $1,000 - $2,000+

**5-Speed Manual NV3500**

* **Transmission Fluid Leaks:** Leaking output shaft seals or input shaft seals can cause fluid loss. Replacement of seals is required. $200 - $300+

**Suspension**

* **Leaf Spring Failure:** Worn or broken leaf springs can cause a sagging or rough ride. Replacement of leaf springs is needed. $400 - $600+
* **Front Control Arm Bushing Failure:** Deteriorated bushings can cause excessive play in the front suspension. Replacement of bushings is required. $100 - $200+

**Electrical**

* **Alternator Failure:** A faulty alternator can cause battery discharge or electrical system problems. Requires replacement of the alternator. $300 - $500+
* **Starter Failure:** A failing starter can prevent engine starting. Requires replacement of the starter. $200 - $400+

**Exterior**

* **Rust on Body Panels:** Rust can occur on body panels, especially on lower sections. Rust removal and protective coating are required. $500 - $1,000+

**Interior**

* **Dashboard Cracking:** The dashboard can develop cracks over time due to sun exposure. Replacement of the dashboard is needed. $600 - $1,000+
* **Seat Fabric Tearing:** The seat fabric can tear or become damaged, especially in high-use areas. Repair or replacement of seat covers is required. $200 - $400+

**Summary:** The 1997 GMC Sonoma had various issues, ranging from minor to severe. The cost of repairs can vary widely depending on the specific issue and labor costs. Some issues, such as intake manifold gasket failure or transmission problems, can be relatively expensive to fix, while others, like bushing replacements, are more affordable. It's important to factor in the potential costs of repairs when considering a used 1997 Sonoma.
