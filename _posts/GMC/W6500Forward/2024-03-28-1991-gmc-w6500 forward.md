**Introduction:**

The 1991 GMC W6500 Forward is a heavy-duty commercial truck renowned for its durability and versatility. It features a powerful 8.2L V8 engine, paired with a Allison MD3060 6-speed automatic transmission, delivering 230 horsepower and 475 lb-ft of torque. Despite its robust construction, the W6500 has been plagued by various issues affecting its performance, reliability, and comfort.

**Engine**

**8.2L V8**
- **Engine Overheating:**
  - Description/Symptoms: The engine experiences excessive heat and coolant temperature rises abnormally.
  - Solution: Replace the thermostat or radiator.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600+
- **Low Oil Pressure:**
  - Description/Symptoms: Insufficient oil pressure in the engine, indicated by the oil pressure warning light.
  - Solution: Replace the oil pump or engine bearings.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,000+

**Transmission**

**Allison MD3060 6-Speed Automatic**
- **Gear Slipping:**
  - Description/Symptoms: The transmission fails to engage or hold gears correctly, causing rough shifts or loss of power.
  - Solution: Replace the transmission fluid, filter, or solenoid pack.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+
- **Delayed Engagement:**
  - Description/Symptoms: The transmission hesitates or delays engaging gears when shifting.
  - Solution: Replace the transmission control module or electrical wiring.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+

**Suspension**

**No Issues Found**

**Electrical**

**Alternator Failure:**
- Description/Symptoms: The alternator malfunctions, causing insufficient electrical power and battery discharge.
- Solution: Replace the alternator.
- Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800+
**Starter Failure:**
- Description/Symptoms: The starter motor doesn't engage or turns over slowly, making it difficult to start the vehicle.
- Solution: Replace the starter motor or solenoid.
- Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+

**Exterior**

**Rust on Body Panels:**
- Description/Symptoms: Corrosion appears on the body panels, fenders, and doors.
- Solution: Repair or replace affected panels.
- Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $3,000+

**Interior**

**Climate Control Malfunction:**
- Description/Symptoms: The heating, ventilation, and air conditioning (HVAC) system malfunctions, failing to provide heat or cool air.
- Solution: Replace the HVAC blower motor or control module.
- Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600+

**Summary:**

The 1991 GMC W6500 Forward has been known to experience a range of issues, from engine overheating and transmission problems to electrical failures and interior malfunctions. While it offers reliable performance when well-maintained, the severity of some issues can be significant and increase repair costs. Regular inspections, preventative maintenance, and timely repairs are crucial to mitigate these potential problems and ensure the long-term reliability of the W6500 Forward.
