## 2005 GMC Envoy XUV

With a standard 4.2L I6 engine that delivers 275 horsepower and 275 lb-ft of torque, the 2005 GMC Envoy XUV is a versatile SUV known for its spacious interior, off-road capabilities, and powerful engine. However, like all vehicles, it has reported issues that owners should be aware of.

**Engine**

* **4.2L I6**
    * **Intake Manifold Gasket Failure:**
        * Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaking from the intake manifold, overheating, decreased engine performance
        * Solution: Replace intake manifold gasket
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600
        * Recalls: None Found
    * **Oil Pressure Sensor Failure:**
        * Description/Symptoms: Low oil pressure warning light illuminated, engine damage in severe cases
        * Solution: Replace oil pressure sensor
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $250
        * Recalls: None Found
    * **Timing Chain Guide Failure:**
        * Description/Symptoms: Engine rattling noise, reduced engine performance, possible catastrophic engine failure
        * Solution: Replace timing chain and guides
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500
        * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

* **4-Speed Automatic**
    * **Transmission Solenoid Failure:**
        * Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears, erratic shifting, slipping gears
        * Solution: Replace faulty solenoid
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600
        * Recalls: None Found
    * **Torque Converter Shudder:**
        * Description/Symptoms: Vibration while accelerating, especially at low speeds
        * Solution: Replace torque converter
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $1,500
        * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Front Control Arm Bushing Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Clunking or rattling noises when driving over bumps, excessive tire wear
    * Solution: Replace control arm bushings
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Rear Shock Absorber Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Poor ride quality, bottoming out over bumps, uneven tire wear
    * Solution: Replace rear shock absorbers
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Battery losing charge prematurely, difficulty starting
    * Solution: Diagnose and fix electrical system issue (e.g., faulty alternator, parasitic draw)
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the issue
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Headlight Switch Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Headlights not working, intermittent headlight operation
    * Solution: Replace headlight switch
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $350
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Side Mirror Adjustment Motor Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Side mirrors not adjusting properly
    * Solution: Replace side mirror adjustment motor
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $150
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Sunroof Drain Plugs Clogging:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Water accumulating in sunroof area, potential for leaks
    * Solution: Clean or replace sunroof drain plugs
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 - $100
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **HVAC Blower Motor Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: No air flow from vents, excessive noise
    * Solution: Replace blower motor
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Instrument Cluster Dimming:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Instrument cluster lights fading or not working properly
    * Solution: Replace instrument cluster
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

While the 2005 GMC Envoy XUV is a capable and versatile vehicle, it has a few common issues that owners should be aware of. The most severe and costly issue is the potential timing chain guide failure, which can lead to catastrophic engine damage if not addressed promptly. Other issues, such as transmission solenoid failures and suspension component wear, are less severe but can still be costly to repair. Overall, the 2005 GMC Envoy XUV has a fair reliability rating, but owners should budget for potential repairs, especially if the vehicle has higher mileage.
