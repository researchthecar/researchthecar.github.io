## Engine

### 7.8L Duramax Diesel (LG4)
**Fuel Injector Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Rough idling, loss of power, increased exhaust smoke, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of faulty fuel injectors.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1500 per injector

**EGR System Clogging**
- **Symptoms:** Reduced engine performance, increased emissions, potential engine shutdown.
- **Solution:** Cleaning or replacement of EGR valve and cooler.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1200

## Electrical

### Standard Electrical System
**Alternator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Dimming lights, battery warning light, inability to start the vehicle.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the alternator.
- **Cost:** $400 - $800

**Wiring Harness Corrosion**
- **Symptoms:** Intermittent electrical faults, non-functioning accessories, starting issues.
- **Solution:** Inspection, cleaning, and repair or replacement of corroded sections.
- **Cost:** $300 - $900


## Exterior

### Body Panels and Frame
**Frame Rust (in corrosive environments)**
- **Symptoms:** Visible rust on the frame rails, potential structural weakening.
- **Solution:** Rust treatment, undercoating, or frame repair/replacement in severe cases.
- **Cost:** $200 - $2000+

**Door Latch/Lock Actuator Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Doors not locking or unlocking properly, difficulty opening/closing.
- **Solution:** Replacement of faulty latch or actuator.
- **Cost:** $150 - $350


## Interior

### Cabin Components
**HVAC Blower Motor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** No airflow from vents or intermittent airflow.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the blower motor.
- **Cost:** $200 - $450

**Seat Upholstery Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Tearing or excessive wear on driver's seat.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of seat cover.
- **Cost:** $100 - $300

### Summary Verdict
The 2006 GMC W5500 Forward is generally considered a reliable commercial vehicle, particularly for its intended vocational use. The Duramax diesel engine, while powerful, can experience issues with fuel injectors and the EGR system, which are common for diesel engines of this era. The Allison automatic transmission is known for its durability. Electrical components like the alternator and wiring can be points of failure, especially in harsh conditions or with age. Exterior issues like frame rust are environment-dependent, while interior wear is typical for a work vehicle. Overall, with proper maintenance, the W5500 Forward offers dependable service, with most reported issues being repairable and not indicative of systemic design flaws.
