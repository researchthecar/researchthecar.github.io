## Electrical

### Wiring Harness
**Corrosion/Abrasion**
- **Symptoms:** Intermittent electrical faults, warning lights, non-functional accessories.
- **Solution:** Inspection, cleaning, repair, or replacement of affected sections.
- **Cost:** $150 - $500


### Alternator
**Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Battery warning light, dimming lights, engine stalling due to lack of power.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the alternator.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600


## Exterior

### Body Panels
**Rust/Corrosion**
- **Symptoms:** Visible rust, particularly on lower body panels, wheel arches, and frame.
- **Solution:** Rust treatment, panel repair, or replacement.
- **Cost:** $200 - $1500+


### Headlights/Taillights
**Condensation/Clouding**
- **Symptoms:** Fogging inside lenses, reduced light output, potential electrical issues.
- **Solution:** Sealing repair or replacement of light assemblies.
- **Cost:** $100 - $400 per assembly


## Interior

### HVAC System
**Blower Motor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** No airflow from vents, or intermittent airflow.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the blower motor or resistor.
- **Cost:** $200 - $500


### Seat Upholstery
**Wear and Tear**
- **Symptoms:** Rips, tears, and thinning of fabric or vinyl, especially on driver's seat.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of seat covers or entire seat.
- **Cost:** $150 - $600

### Summary Verdict
The 2001 GMC W5500 Forward demonstrates a generally reliable profile for its intended commercial use. The powertrain options, including the Vortec V6 and V8 engines paired with either automatic or manual transmissions, are known for their durability and lack of widespread critical failures. Common issues tend to be related to the electrical system, such as wiring harness corrosion, and exterior components like rust on body panels, which are typical for vehicles of this age and class. Interior components like the HVAC blower motor can also experience failures. Overall, with consistent maintenance, the W5500 Forward is a dependable workhorse.
