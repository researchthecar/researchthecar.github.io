# 2019 GMC Terrain

### Overview
The 2019 GMC Terrain, a compact SUV, offers a blend of comfort, technology, and practicality. It's available with a choice of turbocharged gasoline engines, aiming for a balance between performance and fuel efficiency. The interior is generally well-appointed for its class, featuring modern infotainment options and available driver-assistance features. Reliability for the 2019 model year is generally considered average, with some specific areas that have seen more attention from owners and technicians. While many owners report a positive experience, potential buyers should be aware of common concerns that have been documented.

---


## Engine

### 1.5L Turbocharged I4 (L3B)
**Oil Dilution**
- **Symptoms:** Engine oil level appears to rise, potential for rough idle or reduced performance.
- **Solution:** Software updates to engine control module, potential for revised PCV system components.
- **Cost:** $100 - $300

**Turbocharger Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of power, whistling noises, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Inspection and potential replacement of turbocharger assembly or related hoses.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1500

### Summary Verdict
The 2019 GMC Terrain presents a generally reliable package, particularly with the more powerful 2.0L engine option. The primary concerns revolve around the 1.5L engine's tendency for oil dilution and potential turbocharger issues, as well as some transmission hesitation with the 9-speed automatic. Electrical gremlins, specifically with the infotainment system and occasional battery drain, are also noted. While not deal-breakers for many, these issues suggest that regular maintenance and prompt attention to early symptoms are advisable for owners of the 1.5L models and those experiencing transmission quirks.
