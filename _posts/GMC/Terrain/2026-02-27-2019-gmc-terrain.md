# 2019 GMC Terrain Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2019 GMC Terrain offers a comfortable ride, spacious cabin, and efficient powertrain choices, making it a popular choice in the compact SUV segment. However, prospective buyers should carefully review potential trouble spots, particularly concerning brake vacuum pump failures and occasional electrical or visibility glitches. Being aware of these patterns can help you spot a well-maintained model and avoid unexpected repair bills.

---

## Engine

### 1.5L Turbo I4

The standard 1.5-liter turbocharged 4-cylinder engine produces around 170 horsepower paired with a 9-speed automatic transmission, offering adequate daily efficiency for commuters.

### 2.0L Turbo I4

The available 2.0-liter turbocharged 4-cylinder engine pushes output to roughly 252 horsepower, providing much stronger acceleration and towing capacity when properly equipped.

### 1.6L Turbo-Diesel I4

The rare 1.6-liter turbodiesel engine focuses on high highway fuel economy and strong low-end torque, though models are less common on the used market.

## Transmission

### 9-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 9-speed automatic transmission handles power delivery across most trims. While generally smooth, some owners have experienced mechanical slipping or electronic gear selection faults.

**Transmission Slipping and Shifting Delays**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may notice rough shifting, momentary hesitation upon acceleration, or unexpected drops in gear engagement.
- **Solution:** Diagnostic inspection by a qualified transmission specialist; fluid flush, software update, or mechanical repair may be required.

**Unintended Neutral Shift or Park Defect**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle may display gear indicator errors, fail to shift into park properly, or unexpectedly drop into neutral.
- **Solution:** Inspect and replace faulty transmission sensors, shift interlock components, or wiring harnesses.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes & Vacuum Pump

Brake system reliability is paramount, and a notable subset of owners have reported sudden brake vacuum pump degradation leading to hard pedal feel.

**Vacuum Pump Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The brake pedal suddenly becomes extremely hard to depress, significantly reducing stopping power and requiring excessive force.
- **Solution:** Replace the failed vacuum pump and inspect related brake booster components.
- **Cost:** $1000

## Electrical

### Back Over Prevention Camera

The backup camera and parking aid systems are important safety features, though intermittent screen blanking or blue-screen errors have been documented by several drivers.

**Rearview Camera Display Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The backup camera display turns solid blue or goes completely blank, often accompanied by a 'Service Rear Vision System' warning message.
- **Solution:** Inspect the camera wiring harness, software modules, or replace the camera assembly if hardware has failed.
- **Cost:** $1000

## Exterior

### Sunroof

Exterior glass integrity is generally dependable, but panoramic sunroof models carry a small risk of spontaneous glass breakage.

**Panoramic Sunroof Shattering**
- **Symptoms:** The glass panel of the panoramic sunroof may spontaneously fracture or implode under normal driving conditions.
- **Solution:** Replace the damaged glass panel and clear any remaining debris from the tracks.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V576000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Foundation Components:Disc:Caliper](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=GMC&model=Terrain&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** A reduction of braking performance can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will bleed the vehicle's brake system, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V668000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=GMC&model=Terrain&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** Missing bolts on the start-stop accumulator endcap could result in a transmission oil leak and may progress to a loss of propulsion, increasing the risk of a crash. A transmission fluid leak in the presence of an ignition source may increase the risk of fire.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the start-stop transmission accumulator and replace it if any bolts are missing, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V184000: Seats](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=GMC&model=Terrain&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** In the event of a crash, the insufficient welds may result in an increased risk of injury for an occupant seated in the rear-right position.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will replace the rear-right seatback frame, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2019 GMC Terrain is a versatile and feature-packed compact SUV, but buyers should perform thorough pre-purchase inspections. Pay close attention to the braking system for any signs of vacuum pump fatigue and verify that all electronic displays and the backup camera operate correctly. Consulting service records for past transmission and brake repairs will provide added peace of mind.
