# 2023 GMC Terrain Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2023 GMC Terrain, prospective buyers will find a compact crossover designed primarily for daily commuting and family road trips. While owner feedback highlights solid overall utility and style, various driver reports submitted to federal safety regulators point to potential mechanical and electrical hiccups. Reviewing these known trouble spots can help shoppers perform targeted pre-purchase inspections.

---

## Engine

### 1.5L Turbocharged I4

The standard 1.5-liter turbocharged inline-4 engine produces around 175 horsepower and 203 lb-ft of torque, paired with front-wheel or all-wheel drive. It offers adequate power for daily driving with a focus on fuel efficiency, though some owners have reported unexpected powertrain hesitations.

### Fuel System & Engine Stalling

Engine and fuel delivery components manage the combustion process and fuel supply. Driver complaints indicate occasional unexpected stalls and fuel pump related power losses requiring careful diagnostic checks.

**Unexpected Engine Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** Engine shuts off abruptly while driving or attempting to accelerate, often illuminating the check engine light and causing a loss of power.
- **Solution:** Diagnostic scans by a professional mechanic are necessary to inspect fuel pump operation, fuel delivery modules, and associated electrical sensors.

**Reduced Engine Power**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle suddenly displays a reduced power warning on the dash panel and decelerates or restricts acceleration on highways.
- **Solution:** Inspect throttle body components, fuel pressure levels, and engine control unit (ECU) error codes.

## Transmission

### 9-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 9-speed automatic transmission works alongside the standard turbo engine to optimize highway cruising and city efficiency. While generally smooth, occasional reports of rough shifting and hesitation have been documented by drivers.

**Transmission Slipping and Jerking**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle slips and jerks out of gear during acceleration, causing abnormal driving behavior.
- **Solution:** Have a certified technician inspect the transmission module; software updates may temporarily resolve shifting anomalies, though mechanical checks could be required.

## Electrical

### Infotainment System

The electrical system powers all comfort, safety, and entertainment features. Infotainment screen blanks are among the notable electronic complaints logged for this model year.

**Black Screen Infotainment Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The central infotainment display screen goes completely blank or unresponsive, disabling access to audio and vehicle settings.
- **Solution:** Perform a system reset, check fuse integrity, or visit a dealership for software reflashing and potential hardware replacement if the unit fails entirely.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V929000: Air Bags:Sensor:Occupant Classification:Front Passenger](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=GMC&model=Terrain&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** The Occupant Classification System may not properly disable the front passenger air bag when there is a small child or child restraint detected in the seat. In the event of a crash, a front passenger air bag that deploys with a child or infant in the front passenger seat can increase their risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the passenger presence system, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V339000: Child Seat:Vehicle Latch Anchor](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=GMC&model=Terrain&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** The four rear-seat lower child seat LATCH anchorage bars may have excessive powder coating, which could prevent a child seat from being installed with the anchorage bars. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 225, "Child Restraint Anchorage Systems." The inability to install a child seat.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the latch anchorage bars, and remove and replace the finish, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2023 GMC Terrain offers a comfortable ride and versatile cabin space, making it an appealing option on the used market. However, buyers should remain vigilant during test drives, paying close attention to transmission shifting smoothness, dashboard warning lights, and infotainment responsiveness. Conducting a thorough pre-purchase inspection with an independent mechanic can help ensure any underlying fuel system or electrical glitches are identified before purchase.
