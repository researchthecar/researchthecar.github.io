# 2022 GMC Terrain Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2022 GMC Terrain, prospective buyers should weigh its modern styling and feature set against a notable cluster of owner complaints regarding fuel system issues, electrical glitches, and sporadic powertrain concerns. While many drivers enjoy its comfortable ride and cabin layout, reviewing specific component feedback can help you spot potential trouble spots during a test drive or pre-purchase inspection.

---

## Engine

### 1.5L Turbocharged 4-Cylinder

The 2022 GMC Terrain is standardly equipped with a 1.5-liter turbocharged inline-4 engine producing approximately 170 horsepower and 203 lb-ft of torque. It offers adequate efficiency for daily commuting, though buyers should verify smooth idle and fuel system integrity.

**Fuel Pump and Stalling Failures**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have reported sudden engine stalling while driving at various speeds, accompanied by check engine lights, loss of power, and an inability to restart the vehicle.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified mechanic inspect the fuel pump module and diagnostic trouble codes (such as P129F) to determine if fuel pump or control module replacement is necessary.

## Transmission

### 9-Speed Automatic Transmission

Paired with the standard engine, the 9-speed automatic transmission delivers smooth gear ratios across front-wheel and all-wheel drive configurations, though electronic control faults can occasionally trigger safety concerns.

**Transmission Warning and Unexpected Shutoff**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have experienced sudden 'service transmission' warning messages followed by unexpected vehicle shutoffs or shifting anomalies while at highway speeds.
- **Solution:** Scan the powertrain control module for stored diagnostic trouble codes and perform a comprehensive transmission and software inspection.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

The braking system must be rigorously tested for proper pedal feel and stopping responsiveness during a pre-purchase inspection.

**Stiff Pedal and Burning Odor**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have noted exceptionally hard brake pedals with long response times, dragging brakes, or abnormal burning odors during operation.
- **Solution:** Check the brake booster, hydraulic lines, calipers, and brake pads for wear, sticking components, or mechanical failure.

## Steering

### Steering Assembly

Power steering responsiveness is critical for vehicle maneuverability; any binding or warning lights during low-speed turns require immediate professional attention.

**Steering Wheel Lock and ESC Warning**
- **Symptoms:** The steering wheel may unexpectedly stiffen or lock during turns, accompanied by illumination of the Electronic Stability Control and traction control lights.
- **Solution:** Inspect steering sensors, electronic power steering components, and related control modules for fault codes.

## Electrical

### Infotainment and OnStar Module

Electrical architecture governs key cabin amenities and safety alerts, making a thorough electronics check vital during used car evaluation.

**Black Screen and Module Failures**
- **Symptoms:** The infotainment display screen suddenly goes completely black, disabling backup camera functionality, turn signal audio cues, and connected OnStar features.
- **Solution:** Inspect software firmware versions or replace faulty control modules and display hardware as needed.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V724000: Seats](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=GMC&model=Terrain&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** A seat with a missing or loose bolt may not properly restrain the occupant in a crash, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the driver's seat and replace the seat cushion frame, if necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V339000: Child Seat:Vehicle Latch Anchor](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=GMC&model=Terrain&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** The four rear-seat lower child seat LATCH anchorage bars may have excessive powder coating, which could prevent a child seat from being installed with the anchorage bars. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 225, "Child Restraint Anchorage Systems." The inability to install a child seat.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the latch anchorage bars, and remove and replace the finish, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2022 GMC Terrain provides a comfortable ride and versatile interior space, but potential buyers should exercise caution regarding reported electrical glitches, fuel system failures, and occasional powertrain hiccups. Conducting a thorough pre-purchase inspection that prioritizes the electronics, fuel pump operation, and steering components will help ensure a dependable ownership experience.
