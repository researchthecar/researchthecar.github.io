# 2018 GMC Terrain Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2018 GMC Terrain brings a modern exterior, spacious cabin, and a choice of turbocharged engines and automatic transmissions to the compact SUV market. However, prospective buyers should review ownership feedback closely, as this model year has accumulated notable complaints regarding braking assistance, electrical glitches, and powertrain performance.

---

## Engine

### 1.5L Turbo I4

A 1.5-liter turbocharged 4-cylinder engine producing around 170 horsepower paired with a 9-speed automatic transmission. It offers efficient daily driving dynamics but is occasionally associated with owner reports of stalling and mechanical noises.

### 2.0L Turbo I4

A 2.0-liter turbocharged 4-cylinder engine delivering roughly 252 horsepower mated to a 9-speed automatic transmission. This option provides stronger acceleration and towing capability for highway merging and hauling.

### 1.6L Turbo-Diesel I4

A 1.6-liter turbocharged diesel 4-cylinder engine producing about 137 horsepower paired with a 6-speed automatic transmission. It prioritizes fuel economy over swift acceleration, appealing to long-distance commuters.

### Engine Performance and Stalling

Owner reports highlight occasional engine stalling and mechanical noise issues that require professional diagnosis.

**Engine Stalling and Loss of Motive Power**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers experience unexpected engine stalls while driving or sudden loss of motive power, often accompanied by warning lights.
- **Solution:** Diagnostic scanning by a qualified technician to identify faulty sensors, fuel delivery components, or powertrain control module software updates.

## Transmission

### 9-Speed Automatic

A 9-speed automatic transmission coupled with the gasoline turbocharged engines. It aims to optimize fuel efficiency and smooth power delivery.

### 6-Speed Automatic

A 6-speed automatic transmission paired specifically with the diesel engine option.

### Shifter and Parking Mechanism

Some owners have encountered electronic or mechanical shifting faults preventing proper gear selection.

**Transmission Shifter and Park Defects**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle displays 'Transmission Unable to Shift' messages, refuses to shift into reverse or park properly, or experiences unexpected gear engagement issues.
- **Solution:** Inspect electronic shifter modules, linkage, and transmission control software; replace faulty components as necessary.

## Brakes

### Brake Vacuum Pump and Booster

The braking system is prone to a notable safety concern involving sudden vacuum pump failure, making the brake pedal difficult to push.

**Brake Vacuum Pump Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The brake pedal suddenly becomes extremely hard to depress, resulting in drastically reduced braking power and stopping capability.
- **Solution:** Replace the faulty brake vacuum pump and inspect the brake booster assembly.

## Electrical

### Infotainment System

Electrical complaints center primarily around unstable touchscreen behavior and infotainment reboots.

**Infotainment Freeze and Reboot**
- **Symptoms:** Touchscreen goes haywire, randomly changes radio stations, freezes, or reboots independently while driving.
- **Solution:** Update the infotainment system software or replace the radio control module if hardware failure is diagnosed.

## Safety

### Air Bag and Restraints

Reports indicate intermittent warning lights associated with the supplemental restraint system.

**Airbag Warning Light**
- **Symptoms:** Service safety restraint system or airbag warning lights illuminate unexpectedly on the instrument cluster.
- **Solution:** Have a dealer scan the airbag control module for diagnostic trouble codes and inspect seatbelt tensioners or wiring harnesses.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V340000: Air Bags: Air Bag/Restraint Control Module](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=GMC&model=Terrain&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** The sensing diagnostic module (SDM) that senses a crash and deploys the necessary air bags may not power down correctly when the vehicle is shut off, causing it to be inoperative when the vehicle is restarted. If the SDM becomes inoperative, it will not detect a crash or command the necessary air bag deployment, increasing the risk of injury in the event of.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will reprogram the SDM with updated software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V576000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Foundation Components:Disc:Caliper](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=GMC&model=Terrain&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** A reduction of braking performance can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will bleed the vehicle's brake system, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V358000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=GMC&model=Terrain&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** A damaged fuel line can create a fuel leak, increasing the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will replace the high pressure fuel pump, and high pressure fuel pipe, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V668000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=GMC&model=Terrain&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** Missing bolts on the start-stop accumulator endcap could result in a transmission oil leak and may progress to a loss of propulsion, increasing the risk of a crash. A transmission fluid leak in the presence of an ignition source may increase the risk of fire.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the start-stop transmission accumulator and replace it if any bolts are missing, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V516000: Power Train:Driveline:Driveshaft](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=GMC&model=Terrain&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** If the front right intermediate drive shaft assembly separates while driving, the vehicle will lose propulsion, increasing the risk of a crash. If the assembly separates while parked on a grade, without the park brake engaged, the vehicle may move unintentionally, increasing the risk of a vehicle roll-away.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will replace the right front intermediate drive shaft assembly, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

When shopping for a used 2018 GMC Terrain, verify that all safety recalls and service history are up to date, paying close attention to the functionality of the brakes and electronic systems. A thorough pre-purchase inspection by an independent mechanic is strongly advised to catch potential vacuum pump or electrical issues before purchase.
