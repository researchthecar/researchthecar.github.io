# 2016 GMC Terrain Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2016 GMC Terrain, buyers will find a spacious and comfortable crossover SUV, but one that requires careful mechanical scrutiny. Several recurring owner complaints highlight potential trouble spots, particularly regarding engine operation, exterior visibility components, and electronics. Prioritizing a thorough pre-purchase inspection can help prospective owners avoid costly surprises.

---

## Engine

### 2.4L Ecotec 4-Cylinder

The 2.4-liter Ecotec four-cylinder engine produces approximately 182 horsepower and 172 lb-ft of torque, offering adequate efficiency for daily commuting. However, buyers should be mindful of reported tendencies for higher oil consumption and related internal wear.

**Engine Stalling and Oil Consumption**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicles may experience unexpected engine stalling while idling or slowing down, often accompanied by loud grinding noises, timing chain wear, or excessive oil consumption.
- **Solution:** Inspect timing chain components, monitor oil levels frequently, and replace worn seals or timing components as diagnosed by a qualified mechanic.

### 3.6L V6

The available 3.6-liter V6 engine delivers a robust 301 horsepower and 272 lb-ft of torque, providing much stronger acceleration and towing capability for the Terrain. It is generally paired with a smooth-shifting automatic transmission.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

The six-speed automatic transmission comes paired with both front-wheel and all-wheel drive configurations. While generally dependable, proper fluid maintenance is critical to avoid shifting irregularities.

**Transmission Fault Codes and Shifting Concerns**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may experience unexpected shifts in driving behavior, illuminated dashboard warning lights, and diagnostic trouble codes such as P0700 indicating transmission control module faults.
- **Solution:** Have a professional transmission specialist scan for diagnostic trouble codes and inspect internal components or control modules for wear.

## Brakes

### Brake System

The braking system provides adequate stopping power under normal conditions, though owners have noted instances of pedal softness and premature component wear.

**Brake Pedal Softness and Delayed Stopping**
- **Symptoms:** Brake pedals may feel soft or spongy, requiring longer distances to bring the vehicle to a complete stop and increasing the risk of rear-end collisions.
- **Solution:** Inspect brake pads, rotors, master cylinders, and brake lines for leaks, wear, or hydraulic pressure loss.

## Electrical

### Infotainment System

Cabin electronics manage infotainment, navigation, and camera feeds, with occasional software glitches noted by owners.

**Screen Freezing and Infotainment Reboots**
- **Symptoms:** The dashboard display screen may go completely black, freeze, or constantly reboot, occasionally causing the backup camera and parking assistance to shut off.
- **Solution:** Check for software updates at a dealership or replace faulty infotainment control units if hardware failure is confirmed.

## Exterior

### Windshield Wiper Mechanism

The exterior visibility systems include standard wiper linkages that have shown susceptibility to mechanical failure under heavy weather loads.

**Wipper Linkage and Motor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Front windshield wipers suddenly stop working or become completely inoperable during heavy rainstorms, severely reducing driver visibility.
- **Solution:** Inspect the wiper motor and linkage assembly for stripped gears or broken components, and replace the failing parts.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V449000: Equipment:Other:Labels](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=GMC&model=Terrain&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 110, "Tire Selection and Rims." If the information on the certification labels is incorrect, the operator may install incorrectly sized tires or rims or may improperly inflate the tires, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will inspect and replace the certification label, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V137000: Exterior Lighting:Headlights](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=GMC&model=Terrain&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 108, "Lamps, Reflective Devices, and Associated Equipment." The bright reflection may cause a glare for other drivers, reducing their visibility and increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will install headlamp appliques, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2016 GMC Terrain presents a mixed picture for used car buyers, balancing passenger comfort and utility against reported mechanical concerns. Prospective buyers should prioritize a comprehensive mechanical inspection focusing closely on the 2.4L engine's oil levels and timing components, the stability of the windshield wiper linkages, and proper operation of all electronics.
