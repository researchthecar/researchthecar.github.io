# 2020 GMC Terrain Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2020 GMC Terrain presents a modern compact crossover package with distinct styling and comfortable daily drivability, but prospective buyers should carefully review known mechanical pain points. Owner reports highlight several recurring system concerns, particularly involving brake assist function and electrical accessories. Conducting a thorough pre-purchase inspection on potential used models can help identify these issues before they turn into unexpected repair bills.

---

## Engine

### 1.5L Turbocharged Inline-4

The standard 1.5L turbocharged 4-cylinder engine produces approximately 170 horsepower paired with a 9-speed automatic transmission, offering adequate efficiency for daily commuting while requiring routine turbo maintenance.

### 2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4

The available 2.0L turbocharged engine increases output to 252 horsepower for stronger acceleration and towing capability, though it demands careful adherence to oil change intervals.

### Fuel System

Fuel delivery components occasionally experience module or pump failures leading to starting difficulties.

**Fuel Pump and Module Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle fails to start or cranks continuously without catching, occasionally stalling shortly after refueling.
- **Solution:** Diagnose the fuel pump control module and replace faulty fuel pump components as necessary.

## Transmission

### 9-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 9-speed automatic transmission manages power delivery across front-wheel and all-wheel-drive configurations, generally operating smoothly when properly maintained.

## Brakes

### Vacuum Pump and Brake Assist

Brake system complaints center heavily on vacuum pump failures that reduce power assist and compromise stopping ability.

**Brake Assist Loss and Hard Pedal**
- **Symptoms:** Brake pedal suddenly becomes extremely hard to depress, accompanied by a service brake assist warning message and greatly increased stopping distances.
- **Solution:** Inspect and replace the vacuum pump and associated brake booster components.

## Electrical

### Infotainment and Backup Camera

Cabin electronics generally function well, though backup camera screens and infotainment displays can occasionally glitch.

**Backup Camera Display Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Rearview camera displays a blue screen or reads 'service rear camera system' when shifting into reverse.
- **Solution:** Replace the faulty backup camera unit or inspect wiring harnesses and software modules.

## Safety

### Airbag System

Safety systems require prompt attention if warning indicators appear to ensure proper occupant protection.

**Airbag Warning Light and Diagnostic Codes**
- **Symptoms:** Airbag warning light illuminates intermittently on the dashboard, with diagnostic trouble codes indicating potential deployment faults.
- **Solution:** Have a dealership inspect the SRS wiring and connectors, replacing faulty sensors or modules as indicated by diagnostic trouble codes.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V668000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=GMC&model=Terrain&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** Missing bolts on the start-stop accumulator endcap could result in a transmission oil leak and may progress to a loss of propulsion, increasing the risk of a crash. A transmission fluid leak in the presence of an ignition source may increase the risk of fire.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the start-stop transmission accumulator and replace it if any bolts are missing, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V339000: Child Seat:Vehicle Latch Anchor](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=GMC&model=Terrain&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** The four rear-seat lower child seat LATCH anchorage bars may have excessive powder coating, which could prevent a child seat from being installed with the anchorage bars. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 225, "Child Restraint Anchorage Systems." The inability to install a child seat.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the latch anchorage bars, and remove and replace the finish, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2020 GMC Terrain is a versatile option for used compact SUV buyers, but it demands careful inspection of key problem areas. Pay close attention to brake pedal feel and vacuum pump operation during a test drive, and verify that all electronic features like the backup camera function reliably. Addressing these potential weak points upfront will help ensure a safer and more dependable ownership experience.
