# 2017 GMC Terrain Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2017 GMC Terrain, understanding its underlying powertrain options and common owner-reported trouble spots can help you make an informed decision. While this compact SUV offers a spacious cabin and comfortable ride, prospective buyers should review specific mechanical areas—particularly engine performance, timing components, and electrical accessories—before finalizing a purchase.

---

## Engine

### 2.4L 4-Cylinder Engine

The 2.4-liter four-cylinder engine produces approximately 182 horsepower and 172 lb-ft of torque, providing adequate daily driving performance. Buyers should monitor oil levels closely and check service history for recurring maintenance items.

**Engine Stalling and Loss of Power**
- **Symptoms:** The engine may unexpectedly stall or display reduced power warnings on the dashboard, occasionally accompanied by erratic idling or difficulty accelerating from a stop.
- **Solution:** Diagnose fault codes, inspect variable valve timing (VVT) solenoids, and check for carbon buildup or sensor failures.

**Rear Main Seal and Oil Consumption**
- **Symptoms:** Excessive oil consumption can lead to oil leaks around the rear main seal, often exacerbated by internal engine pressure or carbon accumulation.
- **Solution:** Inspect the rear main seal for active leaks and address underlying causes of excessive oil consumption or crankcase pressure.

### 3.6L V6 Engine

The robust 3.6-liter V6 engine generates 301 horsepower and 272 lb-ft of torque, offering stronger towing and acceleration capabilities. While generally powerful, buyers should listen closely for rattling noises during cold starts that could indicate timing chain wear.

**Timing Chain Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Engine may fail to start or run properly due to premature timing chain stretch, often triggering check engine lights and mechanical noise.
- **Solution:** Replace the timing chain assembly and guides if excessive wear or slack is detected by a qualified mechanic.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 6-speed automatic transmission is paired with front-wheel or all-wheel drive configurations. Smooth operation is essential during a test drive, as shifting hesitation can point to expensive repairs.

**Shifting Delays and Power Loss**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may hesitate, fail to shift gears correctly, or experience sudden loss of acceleration while driving at highway speeds.
- **Solution:** Inspect transmission fluid condition, check electronic control modules, and service internal components or replace the transmission if mechanical failure is present.

## Exterior

### Exterior Lighting

Electrical accessories and exterior lighting systems require thorough testing to ensure all headlamps, brake lights, and dash modules function reliably.

**Headlight and Taillight Moisture Ingress**
- **Symptoms:** Headlights may experience operational failures, and taillight housings can fill with water, leading to electrical wire corrosion.
- **Solution:** Inspect lamp housings for proper seals, clear out accumulated moisture, and replace damaged sockets or wiring harnesses.

## Safety

### Seat Belts and Airbag Sensors

Safety restraint systems are critical; any intermittent locking of seat belts or persistent airbag warning lights should be professionally inspected before purchase.

**Seat Belt Locking and Pretensioner Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Passenger seat belts may lock up repeatedly and fail to release normally, or seat belt pretensioners may deploy unexpectedly accompanied by airbag warning messages.
- **Solution:** Inspect seat belt assemblies, locking mechanisms, and SRS sensors, replacing faulty retractors or modules as needed.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V137000: Exterior Lighting:Headlights](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=GMC&model=Terrain&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 108, "Lamps, Reflective Devices, and Associated Equipment." The bright reflection may cause a glare for other drivers, reducing their visibility and increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will install headlamp appliques, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2017 GMC Terrain is a versatile crossover offering flexible cargo space and a comfortable ride, but it demands careful inspection from used-car shoppers. Pay close attention to engine oil levels, cold-start timing chain noises, and transmission shifting behavior during your test drive to avoid unexpected repair bills.
