# 2024 GMC Sierra EV Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2024 GMC Sierra EV represents a cutting-edge entry into the electric truck market, bringing massive power, innovative features like CrabWalk, and Ultium platform technology to buyers. Because this is an initial model year, early owners have reported a scattering of pioneering electrical and structural glitches. Prospective buyers should inspect software updates, accessory attachments, and high-voltage systems carefully during a test drive.

---

## Engine

### Ultium Dual-Motor EV Powertrain

This fully electric dual-motor setup delivers robust horsepower and instant torque through an all-wheel-drive configuration. As a primary EV powertrain option, it provides strong hauling capability though early examples warrant careful review of battery and high-voltage health.

## Transmission

### Single-Speed Direct-Drive Transmission

A specialized single-speed direct-drive transmission manages power delivery seamlessly across all driving modes. It simplifies maintenance by eliminating traditional multi-speed gear shifting.

## Brakes

### Integrated Trailer Brake Module

Braking systems manage heavy towing loads, with software-based displays requiring attention after updates.

**Blank Trailer Brake Display**
- **Symptoms:** Following software updates, the integrated trailer brake module display may remain blank while towing, making it difficult to monitor applied power visually.
- **Solution:** Verify trailer brake functionality and request a software update check from an authorized dealer to restore proper display telemetry.

## Steering

### Infotainment and Steering Integration

Advanced steering and display features rely heavily on stable software integration.

**Head-Up Display and CrabWalk Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may experience missing head-up display projections on the windshield or find that the CrabWalk feature is unavailable in the infotainment system.
- **Solution:** Perform an infotainment system reboot or visit a dealer to reset module software controlling advanced chassis features.

## Electrical

### High-Voltage Battery and Electrical System

Electrical architecture controls major vehicle systems, though initial owner reports indicate isolated high-voltage warnings.

**High-Voltage Battery and Electrical Odors**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may notice a strong electrical burning smell accompanied by high-voltage battery warning messages and multiple dashboard warning lights.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified dealership inspect the high-voltage battery pack and electrical architecture immediately upon experiencing warning lights or unusual odors.

### Front Trunk and Latches

Latch and lock mechanisms secure access points, though early software integration can lead to false triggers.

**Erroneous Front Trunk Opening**
- **Symptoms:** The front trunk may open unexpectedly while operating the vehicle, triggering interlocks that prevent shifting out of park.
- **Solution:** Check latch alignment and consult a dealer for potential software calibrations governing frunk security sensors.

## Exterior

### Tonneau Cover and Bed Accessories

Exterior bed accessories require secure attachment points to prevent detachment during high-speed driving.

**Loose Tonneau Cover**
- **Symptoms:** Factory-installed soft tonneau covers can detach from their forward anchor points at highway speeds, flapping loudly and posing a hazard to surrounding traffic.
- **Solution:** Secure or adjust anchor channels, and consider aftermarket retention straps if factory fitment appears loose.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V320000: Electrical System:Wiring](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=GMC&model=Sierra+EV&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** A loss of drive power increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the rear drive unit, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V755000: Exterior Lighting:Lighting Control Module](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=GMC&model=Sierra+EV&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 108, "Lamps, Reflective Devices, and Associated Equipment." Front turn signals that are difficult to see may not clearly indicate the driver's intentions to other road users, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the headlamp multipurpose LED driver (MLD) module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V015000: Seat Belts:Rear/Other:Anchorage](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=GMC&model=Sierra+EV&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** The bolt that secures the rear center seat belt assembly to the seat structure may not have been properly tightened. An improperly secured seat belt assembly may not restrain an occupant as intended, increasing the risk of injury in a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2024 GMC Sierra EV is an impressive technological showcase, but as an early-production electric vehicle, buyers should anticipate minor software bugs and accessory adjustments. Ensure all dealer updates have been applied and verify the condition of bed covers and electronic features before finalizing a purchase.
