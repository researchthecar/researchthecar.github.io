### 3.0L Duramax Diesel (4JJ1-TC)
**Glow Plug Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Hard starting in cold weather, check engine light illumination.
- **Solution:** Replacement of faulty glow plugs and potentially the glow plug control module.
- **Cost:** $300 - $700

**EGR Valve Clogging**
- **Symptoms:** Reduced engine power, rough idling, increased exhaust smoke.
- **Solution:** Cleaning or replacement of the Exhaust Gas Recirculation (EGR) valve.
- **Cost:** $400 - $800

**Alternator Failure**

### Summary Verdict
The 2006 GMC W3500 Forward demonstrates a generally reliable profile, particularly with its gasoline V8 engine options. The diesel engine, while offering better fuel economy for commercial use, shows a few more documented potential issues such as glow plug and EGR valve problems, which are not uncommon for diesel engines of this era. Transmission and suspension systems appear to be robust. Electrical system concerns, like alternator failure and wiring harness issues, are present but not overwhelmingly prevalent. Exterior and interior components are typically durable for commercial applications. Overall, with diligent maintenance, especially for the diesel variant, the W3500 should provide dependable service for its intended purpose.
