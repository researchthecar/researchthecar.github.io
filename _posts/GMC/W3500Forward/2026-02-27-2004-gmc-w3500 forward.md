## Electrical

### Wiring Harness
**Corrosion/Abrasion**
- **Symptoms:** Intermittent electrical faults, warning lights, non-starting.
- **Solution:** Inspect and repair/replace damaged sections of the wiring harness. Ensure proper sealing and protection.
- **Cost:** $300 - $800


### Alternator
**Premature Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Battery warning light, dimming lights, difficulty starting.
- **Solution:** Replace the alternator. Ensure proper belt tension.
- **Cost:** $400 - $700


## Exterior

### Body Panels
**Rust/Corrosion**
- **Symptoms:** Visible rust, particularly on lower body panels, wheel arches, and frame.
- **Solution:** Rust treatment and repair, repainting, or replacement of affected panels.
- **Cost:** $200 - $1500+


### Headlights
**Clouding/Yellowing**
- **Symptoms:** Reduced light output, poor visibility at night.
- **Solution:** Headlight restoration kit or replacement of headlight assemblies.
- **Cost:** $100 - $400


## Interior

### HVAC System
**Blower Motor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** No airflow from vents, or intermittent airflow.
- **Solution:** Replace the blower motor and resistor if necessary.
- **Cost:** $250 - $500


### Seat Upholstery
**Wear and Tear**
- **Symptoms:** Rips, tears, and thinning of fabric or vinyl, especially on driver's seat.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of seat covers or the entire seat.
- **Cost:** $150 - $600

### Summary Verdict
The 2004 GMC W3500 Forward is generally considered a reliable workhorse, particularly in its powertrain components. The gasoline V6 and V8 engines, along with the available transmissions, have a solid reputation for durability when properly maintained. The primary areas that may require attention are related to the vehicle's age and exposure to commercial use, such as potential electrical harness issues due to corrosion or abrasion, and common exterior wear like rust. Interior components like the HVAC blower motor and seat upholstery can also show signs of wear. Proactive inspection and maintenance, especially for electrical connections and body integrity, will contribute significantly to the longevity and operational efficiency of this commercial vehicle.
