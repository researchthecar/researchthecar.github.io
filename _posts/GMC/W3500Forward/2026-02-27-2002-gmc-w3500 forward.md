## Electrical

### Wiring Harness
**Corrosion**
- **Symptoms:** Intermittent electrical failures, warning lights, starting issues.
- **Solution:** Inspect and clean corroded connections, replace damaged sections of the harness.
- **Cost:** $150 - $500


### Alternator
**Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Battery not charging, dimming lights, engine stalling.
- **Solution:** Replace alternator.
- **Cost:** $300 - $700


## Exterior

### Body Panels
**Rust**
- **Symptoms:** Corrosion on wheel arches, door sills, and frame components, especially in rust-prone climates.
- **Solution:** Rust treatment, panel replacement, or repair.
- **Cost:** $200 - $1500+


### Headlights
**Clouding/Yellowing**
- **Symptoms:** Reduced light output, poor visibility at night.
- **Solution:** Headlight restoration kit or replacement.
- **Cost:** $50 - $200


## Interior

### HVAC System
**Blower Motor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** No air coming from vents or intermittent airflow.
- **Solution:** Replace blower motor or resistor.
- **Cost:** $150 - $400


### Dashboard Gauges
**Malfunctioning Gauges**
- **Symptoms:** Speedometer, fuel gauge, or other indicators not working correctly.
- **Solution:** Repair or replace instrument cluster.
- **Cost:** $300 - $800

### Summary Verdict
The 2002 GMC W3500 Forward is generally considered a reliable workhorse, particularly with its range of gasoline and diesel engine options. Most reported issues are not widespread and tend to be isolated incidents or related to age and mileage. Common concerns include potential electrical issues like alternator failure or wiring harness corrosion, and exterior rust in certain climates. Interior components like the HVAC blower motor can also require attention. With diligent maintenance and prompt repair of minor issues, the W3500 Forward can continue to provide dependable service for commercial applications.
