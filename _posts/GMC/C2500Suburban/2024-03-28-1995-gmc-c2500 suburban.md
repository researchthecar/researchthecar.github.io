**Introduction**

The 1995 GMC C2500 Suburban is a full-size SUV known for its spacious interior, powerful engine, and towing capabilities. It features a 5.7L V8 engine paired with a 4-speed automatic transmission.

**Engine (5.7L V8)**

* **Intake Manifold Gasket Failure**
  * Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaks, loss of coolant, overheating
  * Solution: Replace intake manifold gasket
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
  * Recalls: None Found
* **Oil Pressure Sensor Failure**
  * Description/Symptoms: Faulty oil pressure readings, engine noises, engine damage
  * Solution: Replace oil pressure sensor
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300
  * Recalls: None Found
* **Distributor Cap and Rotor Failure**
  * Description/Symptoms: Ignition misfires, poor engine performance
  * Solution: Replace distributor cap and rotor
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $150
  * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission (4-Speed Automatic)**

* **Solenoid Pack Failure**
  * Description/Symptoms: Transmission slipping, harsh shifting, loss of gears
  * Solution: Replace solenoid pack
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500
  * Recalls: None Found
* **Torque Converter Failure**
  * Description/Symptoms: Transmission overheating, loss of power, shuddering
  * Solution: Replace torque converter
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,500
  * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Front Ball Joint Failure**
  * Description/Symptoms: Clunking noises, uneven tire wear, difficulty steering
  * Solution: Replace ball joints
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $250 - $500
  * Recalls: None Found
* **Rear Leaf Spring Failure**
  * Description/Symptoms: Sagging suspension, poor ride quality, reduced load capacity
  * Solution: Replace leaf springs
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,200
  * Recalls: None Found
* **Steering Gear Failure**
  * Description/Symptoms: Difficulty steering, loose steering, excessive play
  * Solution: Replace steering gear
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000
  * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Alternator Failure**
  * Description/Symptoms: Battery discharge, dim headlights, electrical system malfunctions
  * Solution: Replace alternator
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800
  * Recalls: None Found
* **Starter Failure**
  * Description/Symptoms: Difficulty starting, grinding noises, smoke or burning
  * Solution: Replace starter
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $250 - $500
  * Recalls: None Found
* **Body Control Module (BCM) Failure**
  * Description/Symptoms: Electrical system malfunctions, loss of power windows and locks
  * Solution: Replace BCM
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
  * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Door Lock Actuator Failure**
  * Description/Symptoms: Difficulty locking or unlocking doors
  * Solution: Replace door lock actuator
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300
  * Recalls: None Found
* **Power Window Motor Failure**
  * Description/Symptoms: Difficulty raising or lowering windows
  * Solution: Replace power window motor
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
  * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Instrument Cluster Failure**
  * Description/Symptoms: Inaccurate readings, dim displays, loss of functionality
  * Solution: Replace instrument cluster
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
  * Recalls: None Found
* **HVAC Blower Motor Failure**
  * Description/Symptoms: No air flow from vents, poor heating or cooling
  * Solution: Replace blower motor
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
  * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 1995 GMC C2500 Suburban has a reputation for being a reliable vehicle, but it is not without its potential issues. The most common engine problems include intake manifold gasket failure and oil pressure sensor failure. The transmission can experience solenoid pack and torque converter issues. Suspension components such as ball joints, leaf springs, and steering gear can also fail over time. Electrical problems include alternator failure, starter failure, and BCM failure. Finally, exterior and interior components like door lock actuators, power window motors, instrument clusters, and HVAC blower motors can encounter issues. The severity and cost of these issues vary depending on the specific part and the extent of the damage.
