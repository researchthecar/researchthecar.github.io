## 1991 GMC V3500 with 7.4L V8 Engine and 4-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 1991 GMC V3500 is a heavy-duty pickup truck known for its powerful performance and durability. It boasts a 7.4L V8 engine that delivers 230 horsepower and 380 lb-ft of torque, paired with a 4-speed automatic transmission. Despite its strengths, the V3500 has faced several common issues over the years:

### Engine

- **Intake Manifold Gasket Failure:**
  - Description/Symptoms: Coolant or oil leaks, engine overheating, rough idle
  - Solution: Replace intake manifold gaskets
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
  - Recalls: None Found

- **Fuel Pump Failure:**
  - Description/Symptoms: Engine stalling, difficult starting, loss of power
  - Solution: Replace fuel pump
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
  - Recalls: None Found

### Transmission

- **Torque Converter Failure:**
  - Description/Symptoms: Transmission slipping, shuddering, or overheating
  - Solution: Replace torque converter
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1500+
  - Recalls: None Found

### Suspension

- **Leaf Spring Breakage:**
  - Description/Symptoms: Rear of the vehicle sagging, rough ride quality
  - Solution: Replace leaf springs
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1000+
  - Recalls: None Found

### Electrical

- **Alternator Failure:**
  - Description/Symptoms: Dimming headlights, difficulty starting, electrical system issues
  - Solution: Replace alternator
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
  - Recalls: None Found

- **Battery Drain:**
  - Description/Symptoms: Battery dies quickly, electrical accessories don't work
  - Solution: Inspect electrical system for parasitic drain and fix
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+
  - Recalls: None Found

### Exterior

- **Door Lock Actuator Failure:**
  - Description/Symptoms: Doors won't lock or unlock electronically
  - Solution: Replace door lock actuators
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
  - Recalls: None Found

- **Power Window Failure:**
  - Description/Symptoms: Windows won't go up or down electronically
  - Solution: Replace power window motors or switches
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800+
  - Recalls: None Found

### Interior

- **HVAC Blower Motor Failure:**
  - Description/Symptoms: No air flow from vents
  - Solution: Replace HVAC blower motor
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
  - Recalls: None Found

- **Cruise Control Failure:**
  - Description/Symptoms: Cruise control won't engage or hold speed
  - Solution: Inspect and replace cruise control switch or module
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+
  - Recalls: None Found

### Summary

Overall, the 1991 GMC V3500 with the 7.4L V8 engine and 4-speed automatic transmission faces several issues that range in severity and cost. While some problems, such as intake manifold gasket failure and fuel pump failure, can be relatively minor and affordable to fix, others like torque converter failure can be more costly. It is important for owners to be aware of these potential issues and to address them promptly to maintain the reliability and safety of their vehicle.
