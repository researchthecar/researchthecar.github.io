# 2020 GMC Savana 3500

### Overview
The 2020 GMC Savana 3500 is a heavy-duty full-size van designed for commercial and utilitarian purposes. ## Electrical

### General Electrical System
**Intermittent Power Loss**
- **Symptoms:** Dashboard lights flickering, loss of power to accessories, engine stalling.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and repair of faulty wiring harnesses, relays, or control modules.
- **Cost:** $300 - $800


## Exterior

### Body Panels and Doors
**Door Latch Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Doors not opening or closing properly, difficulty engaging or disengaging latches.
- **Solution:** Replacement of faulty door latch mechanisms.
- **Cost:** $250 - $500


## Interior

### HVAC System
**Blower Motor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** No airflow from vents, inconsistent fan speeds, unusual noises from the dashboard.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the blower motor or blower motor resistor.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600

### Summary Verdict
The 2020 GMC Savana 3500 demonstrates a generally solid reliability record, particularly concerning its core powertrain components. The V6 and V8 engines, along with the 6-speed automatic transmission, are robust and typically do not present widespread issues. However, like many vehicles in this class, some owners have reported isolated incidents related to the electrical system, specifically intermittent power loss, and exterior components like door latches. The interior HVAC system's blower motor can also be a point of failure. These issues are not pervasive but represent the most commonly cited technical concerns. Overall, the Savana 3500 is a dependable choice for demanding applications, with most potential problems being repairable and not indicative of systemic design flaws.
