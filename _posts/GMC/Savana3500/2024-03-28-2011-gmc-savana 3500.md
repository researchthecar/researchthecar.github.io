**Introduction:**

The 2011 GMC Savana 3500 is a full-size van offered with various engine and transmission options. Its 6.0L V8 engine produces 397 horsepower and 401 pound-feet of torque, paired with a 6-speed automatic transmission. Another engine option is the 6.6L V8 with 352 horsepower and 525 pound-feet of torque, mated to a 6-speed automatic transmission. The Savana is known for its spacious interior, towing capacity, and versatility. However, it also experiences certain issues that owners have reported.

**Engine**

* **6.0L V8**
    * **Intake Manifold Gasket Failure:**
        * Description/Symptoms: Leaking coolant, overheating, reduced engine performance
        * Solution: Replace intake manifold gasket
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
        * Recalls: None Found
    * **Oil Pressure Sensor Failure:**
        * Description/Symptoms: Illuminated check engine light, low oil pressure warning, engine damage if not addressed
        * Solution: Replace oil pressure sensor
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
        * Recalls: None Found
* **6.6L V8**
    * **Exhaust Gas Recirculation (EGR) Valve Failure:**
        * Description/Symptoms: Poor engine performance, increased emissions, reduced fuel economy
        * Solution: Replace EGR valve
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
        * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

* **6-Speed Automatic**
    * **Transmission Slipping:**
        * Description/Symptoms: Hesitation during acceleration, delayed gear shifts, loss of power
        * Solution: Transmission fluid flush, replacement of clutches or other internal components
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,200 - $3,000+
        * Recalls: None Found
    * **Torque Converter Shudder:**
        * Description/Symptoms: Vibrations during acceleration or gear changes
        * Solution: Replacement of torque converter
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,500
        * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Front Struts Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Worn or leaking struts, reduced ride quality, uneven tire wear
    * Solution: Replace front struts
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+ per pair
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Ball Joint Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Clunking noises, vibration during driving, steering instability
    * Solution: Replace ball joints
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600 per joint
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Alternator Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Battery not charging properly, dimming lights, electrical system failure
    * Solution: Replace alternator
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Battery Drain:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Rapid battery discharge, difficulty starting the engine
    * Solution: Identify and fix electrical components causing drain
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Door Handle Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Broken or loose door handles, difficulty opening doors
    * Solution: Replace door handles
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Tailgate Hinge Corrosion:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Corrosion and weakening of tailgate hinges, difficulty opening or closing tailgate
    * Solution: Replace tailgate hinges
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Dash Panel Cracking:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Cracks developing in dash panel, affecting appearance and potential durability
    * Solution: Replace dash panel
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Air Conditioning Compressor Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Reduced or no cooling from air conditioner, high-pitched noise when turned on
    * Solution: Replace air conditioning compressor
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,500
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

Overall, the 2011 GMC Savana 3500 experiences a range of issues that may require repairs, from minor fixes to more costly replacements. While some issues are common to vehicles, others are specific to this model. The severity and cost of the issues vary, and some may be more inconvenient than others. However, it is important for owners to be aware of potential problems and have them addressed promptly to maintain the safety and reliability of the vehicle.
