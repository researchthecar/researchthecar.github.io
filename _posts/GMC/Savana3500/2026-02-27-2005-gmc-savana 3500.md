# 2005 GMC Savana 3500

### Overview
The 2005 GMC Savana 3500 is a full-size van known for its utility and cargo capacity, often used for commercial purposes or as a passenger shuttle. Reliability for this model year is generally considered average to good, with most owners reporting satisfaction with its durability. However, like any vehicle, it has specific areas that may require attention over time. This analysis focuses on common reported issues beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential mechanical and electrical concerns that owners might encounter. Understanding these potential weak points can help in proactive maintenance and cost management for Savana 3500 owners.

---


## Engine

### 4.3L V6 Vortec
**Intake Manifold Gasket Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Coolant leaks, overheating, rough idle, sweet smell from exhaust.
- **Solution:** Replacement of intake manifold gaskets.
- **Cost:** $400 - $700

**Oil Cooler Lines Leaking**
- **Symptoms:** Visible fluid leaks under the vehicle, low oil pressure warning.
- **Solution:** Replacement of leaking oil cooler lines.
- **Cost:** $300 - $500


### 5.3L V8 Vortec
**Active Fuel Management (AFM) Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Engine misfires, rough idle, increased oil consumption, lifter noise.
- **Solution:** Deactivation of AFM system or replacement of lifters and camshaft.
- **Cost:** $800 - $2000+

**Intake Manifold Gasket Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Coolant leaks, overheating, rough idle, sweet smell from exhaust.
- **Solution:** Replacement of intake manifold gaskets.
- **Cost:** $400 - $700


### 6.0L V8 Vortec
**Intake Manifold Gasket Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Coolant leaks, overheating, rough idle, sweet smell from exhaust.
- **Solution:** Replacement of intake manifold gaskets.
- **Cost:** $400 - $700

**Oil Cooler Lines Leaking**
- **Symptoms:** Visible fluid leaks under the vehicle, low oil pressure warning.
- **Solution:** Replacement of leaking oil cooler lines.

## Transmission

### 4L60-E (4-Speed Automatic)
**Torque Converter Clutch (TCC) Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Slipping, shuddering, inability to engage overdrive, P0740 code.
- **Solution:** Rebuild or replacement of torque converter and transmission.
- **Cost:** $1500 - $3000+

**Solenoid Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Harsh shifting, delayed engagement, transmission warning light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of transmission solenoids.
- **Cost:** $400 - $800


### 4L80-E (4-Speed Automatic)
**No widespread issues reported.**
- **Symptoms:** No widespread issues reported.

- **Cost:** No widespread issues reported.


## Suspension

### Standard Chassis
**Ball Joint Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Clunking noises over bumps, uneven tire wear, steering wander.
- **Solution:** Replacement of worn ball joints.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600

**Leaf Spring Sagging**
- **Symptoms:** Rear end sag, reduced load capacity, rougher ride.
- **Solution:** Replacement of sagging leaf springs.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1000


## Electrical

### Body Control Module (BCM)
**Intermittent Power Failures**
- **Symptoms:** Random loss of power to various accessories (lights, windows, radio), warning lights.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and potential reprogramming or replacement of BCM.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1000


### Alternator
**Premature Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Dimming headlights, battery warning light, starting issues.
- **Solution:** Replacement of alternator.
- **Cost:** $400 - $700


## Exterior

### Body Panels
**Rusting**
- **Symptoms:** Visible rust, particularly on wheel arches, rocker panels, and door bottoms, especially in rust-belt regions.
- **Solution:** Rust repair and repainting, or replacement of affected panels.
- **Cost:** $200 - $1500+


### Door Latches
**Sticking or Failing**
- **Symptoms:** Doors difficult to open or close, failure to latch securely.
- **Solution:** Lubrication or replacement of door latch mechanisms.
- **Cost:** $150 - $400


## Interior

### HVAC System
**Blower Motor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** No airflow from vents, or intermittent airflow.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the blower motor or resistor.
- **Cost:** $200 - $500


### Seat Upholstery
**Wear and Tear**
- **Symptoms:** Tearing or excessive wear on driver's seat, common in high-use vehicles.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of seat covers/cushions.
- **Cost:** $100 - $400


--- 
### Summary Verdict
The 2005 GMC Savana 3500 is a robust workhorse with a generally reliable powertrain, especially the 4L80-E transmission. Common issues tend to be related to age and mileage, such as intake manifold gasket leaks on V8 engines and potential wear on suspension components like ball joints. Electrical gremlins, particularly with the BCM, can arise. Rust is a concern in certain climates. While not immune to problems, most issues are repairable and not indicative of fundamental design flaws. Regular maintenance and addressing minor issues promptly can ensure the longevity of the Savana 3500.
