## Introduction to the 2020 GMC Savana 3500

The 2020 GMC Savana 3500 is a versatile full-size van designed for commercial and personal use. It is available with various engine and transmission options, providing businesses and families with a customizable transportation solution. Despite its ruggedness, the Savana 3500 offers comfortable features and ample cargo space, making it an ideal choice for a wide range of applications.

The Savana 3500 is equipped with either a 6.0-liter V8 gasoline engine producing 341 horsepower and 373 lb-ft of torque or a 6.6-liter Duramax V8 turbo-diesel engine generating 260 horsepower and 525 lb-ft of torque. Transmission options include a 6-speed automatic and a 10-speed automatic.

Owners appreciate the Savana 3500's powerful engines, ample cargo space, and comfortable interior. However, some have reported various issues with the vehicle's components.

## Engine

### 6.0-liter V8 Gasoline Engine

**Oil Leaks**
Description/Symptoms: Leaking oil from the engine, particularly around the oil pan and valve cover gaskets
Solution: Replace the faulty gaskets
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
Recalls: None Found

**Spark Plug Failure**
Description/Symptoms: Premature failure of spark plugs, causing misfires and reduced engine performance
Solution: Replace the spark plugs
Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200
Recalls: None Found

### 6.6-liter Duramax V8 Turbo-Diesel Engine

**Fuel Injector Failure**
Description/Symptoms: Failure of one or more fuel injectors, resulting in rough idling, black smoke from the exhaust, and reduced power
Solution: Replace the faulty fuel injectors
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000 per injector
Recalls: None Found

**EGR Valve Malfunction**
Description/Symptoms: Malfunctioning exhaust gas recirculation (EGR) valve, causing increased emissions and reduced engine efficiency
Solution: Replace the EGR valve
Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $700
Recalls: None Found

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

**Gear Slipping**
Description/Symptoms: Transmission gears slipping, leading to rough shifting and loss of power
Solution: Repair or replace the transmission
Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000
Recalls: None Found

### 10-Speed Automatic Transmission

**Delayed Shifting**
Description/Symptoms: Transmission hesitating or delaying when shifting gears
Solution: Update the transmission software or replace the transmission valve body
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500
Recalls: 20V604 for transmission control module software update

## Suspension

### Front Suspension

**Strut Failure**
Description/Symptoms: Premature failure of front struts, leading to a bumpy ride and reduced handling
Solution: Replace the struts and mounts
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000 per side
Recalls: None Found

### Rear Suspension

**Leaf Spring Breakage**
Description/Symptoms: Breaking or sagging of leaf springs in the rear suspension, causing the vehicle to lean and reduce load capacity
Solution: Replace the leaf springs and U-bolts
Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,500 per side
Recalls: None Found

## Electrical

### Battery Issues

**Premature Battery Failure**
Description/Symptoms: Battery draining quickly or failing prematurely
Solution: Replace the battery and check alternator output
Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300
Recalls: None Found

### Wiring Harness Faults

**Electrical Shorts**
Description/Symptoms: Faulty wiring harness causing electrical shorts, resulting in blown fuses or intermittent electrical issues
Solution: Repair or replace the affected wiring harness
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
Recalls: None Found

## Exterior

### Body Panels

**Rust Issues**
Description/Symptoms: Premature rusting on body panels, particularly around wheel arches and undercarriage
Solution: Replace the rusted panels or apply anti-rust treatment
Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the extent of the damage
Recalls: None Found

### Headlights

**Headlight Malfunction**
Description/Symptoms: Headlights failing or experiencing condensation inside the housings
Solution: Replace the headlights or repair the housings
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500 per light
Recalls: None Found

## Interior

### Climate Control

**Air Conditioning Malfunction**
Description/Symptoms: Air conditioning system failing to cool properly
Solution: Inspect and repair the system components, including the compressor, condenser, and expansion valve
Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the specific issue
Recalls: None Found

### Instrument Cluster

**Gauge Failure**
Description/Symptoms: Gauges on the instrument cluster failing or malfunctioning
Solution: Replace or repair the affected gauges
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500 per gauge
Recalls: None Found

## Summary

Overall, the 2020 GMC Savana 3500 is a capable workhorse with powerful engines and ample cargo space. However, it has been plagued by various issues, including engine leaks, transmission problems, suspension failures, electrical faults, and interior malfunctions. While some issues are minor and relatively inexpensive to fix, others can be more severe and costly to repair. It is recommended for potential buyers to thoroughly inspect the vehicle and consider its reliability records before making a purchase decision.
