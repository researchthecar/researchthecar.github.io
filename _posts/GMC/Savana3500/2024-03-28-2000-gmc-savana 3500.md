**Introduction**
The 2000 GMC Savana 3500 is a full-size van that was available with multiple engine and transmission options. It was praised for its spacious interior, powerful engines, and towing capabilities. However, owners have reported a number of issues with the vehicle, including engine problems, transmission issues, and electrical problems.

**Engine**

**6.0L Vortec V8**
* **Intake manifold gasket failure:** The intake manifold gasket can fail, causing coolant and oil to leak. Symptoms include overheating, coolant leaks, and loss of power. The solution is to replace the intake manifold gasket. Estimated cost to fix: $500 - $1,000.
* **Vortec lifter failure:** The lifters in the Vortec engine can fail, causing a loss of power, a rough idle, and a knocking noise. The solution is to replace the lifters. Estimated cost to fix: $1,000 - $2,000.

**8.1L Vortec V8**
* **No known issues.**

**Transmission**

**4L80E Automatic Transmission**
* **Transmission slipping:** The transmission may slip or hesitate when shifting, especially when the vehicle is towing or under heavy load. The solution is to rebuild or replace the transmission. Estimated cost to fix: $2,000 - $3,000.
* **Torque converter failure:** The torque converter may fail, causing the transmission to shudder or lose power. The solution is to replace the torque converter. Estimated cost to fix: $1,000 - $2,000.

**Suspension**

**Front suspension:**
* **No known issues.**

**Rear suspension:**
* **Leaf spring failure:** The leaf springs can fail, causing the rear of the vehicle to sag or bounce excessively. The solution is to replace the leaf springs. Estimated cost to fix: $500 - $1,000.

**Electrical**

**Battery:**
* **Battery failure:** The battery may fail prematurely, leaving the vehicle stranded. The solution is to replace the battery. Estimated cost to fix: $100 - $200.

**Alternator:**
* **Alternator failure:** The alternator may fail, preventing the battery from charging. Symptoms include dim lights, flickering headlights, and a dead battery. The solution is to replace the alternator. Estimated cost to fix: $300 - $500.

**Exterior**

**No known issues.**

**Interior**

* **HVAC issues:** The HVAC system may fail, causing poor airflow or insufficient heating or cooling. The solution is to diagnose and repair the HVAC system. Estimated cost to fix: $500 - $1,000.

**Other**

* **Brakes:** The brakes may fail, causing the vehicle to lose braking power. The solution is to diagnose and repair the brake system. Estimated cost to fix: $500 - $1,500.

**Summary**

The 2000 GMC Savana 3500 is a capable van that has been praised for its performance and reliability. However, like all vehicles, it has some known issues. The most common issues include engine problems, transmission issues, and electrical problems. The severity of these issues varies, and the cost to fix them can be significant. Overall, the Savana 3500 is a good choice for those who need a spacious and powerful van, but it is important to be aware of the potential issues before buying one.
