# 2009 GMC Savana 3500

### Overview
The 2009 GMC Savana 3500 is a full-size van designed for commercial and heavy-duty passenger transport. Known for its robust construction and utilitarian design, it offers a range of powertrain options to suit various needs. Reliability for this model year is generally considered good, particularly for its core mechanical components. However, like any vehicle, specific issues can arise, often related to the powertrain or electrical systems, especially with higher mileage. Owners should be aware of potential problems that may require attention to maintain optimal performance and longevity. This analysis focuses on documented common issues beyond routine maintenance.

---


## Engine

### 4.3L V6 Vortec
**Intake Manifold Gasket Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Coolant leaks, engine misfires, rough idle, overheating.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the intake manifold gaskets.
- **Cost:** $400 - $700

**Oil Cooler Line Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Low oil pressure, oil leaks under the vehicle, potential engine damage if not addressed.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the leaking oil cooler lines.
- **Cost:** $300 - $500


### 5.3L V8 Vortec
**Active Fuel Management (AFM) System Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Engine knocking or ticking noise, oil consumption, reduced fuel economy, potential lifter failure.
- **Solution:** Deactivation of AFM system or replacement of lifters and related components.
- **Cost:** $800 - $2000+

**Intake Manifold Gasket Leaks**

## Transmission

### 4-Speed Automatic (4L60-E)
**Torque Converter Clutch (TCC) Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Shuddering during acceleration, slipping, failure to engage or disengage.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the torque converter.
- **Cost:** $600 - $1200

**Solenoid Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Harsh shifting, delayed engagement, transmission warning light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of faulty shift solenoids.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600

## Suspension

### Standard Chassis
**Ball Joint Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Clunking noises over bumps, uneven tire wear, steering wander.
- **Solution:** Replacement of worn ball joints.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600

**Leaf Spring Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Sagging rear end, reduced load capacity, squeaking noises.
- **Solution:** Replacement of broken or sagging leaf springs.
- **Cost:** $400 - $800

### Summary Verdict
The 2009 GMC Savana 3500 demonstrates a generally solid reliability record, especially concerning its core powertrain and chassis components. The most frequently reported issues tend to be related to the engine, particularly intake manifold gasket leaks on V8 models and potential Active Fuel Management (AFM) system problems on the 5.3L V8. Transmission issues, while not pervasive, can include torque converter clutch problems with the 4L60-E. Electrical components like the alternator and power window motors are also noted as potential failure points. Suspension components such as ball joints and leaf springs may require attention with age and mileage. Overall, the Savana 3500 is a dependable workhorse, but owners should be mindful of these specific areas for proactive maintenance and potential repairs to ensure continued service.
