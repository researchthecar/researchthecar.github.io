Here's a breakdown of common issues and complaints reported for the 2023 GMC Savana 3500, categorized by component, adhering to your specified format. The 2023 GMC Savana 3500 is a full-size cargo van known for its utilitarian nature and robust capabilities. It's available with several engine and transmission options, including a 6.6L V8 gasoline engine producing 401 horsepower and 464 lb-ft of torque, paired with a 6-speed automatic transmission. Other engine options include a 2.8L Duramax Diesel 4-cylinder engine, however it has been reported that the engine option is not available on the Savana. Owners often appreciate its spacious cargo area and towing capacity but may find its dated design, fuel economy, and basic interior lacking compared to more modern vans.

**Engine**

**6.6L V8 Gasoline**

*   **Engine Misfires**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Engine misfires can cause rough idling, reduced power, and the check engine light to illuminate.
    *   Solution: Diagnosing and replacing faulty spark plugs, ignition coils, or fuel injectors.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Oil Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks can occur from various locations such as the valve cover gaskets or oil pan.
    *   Solution: Replacing the leaking gasket or seal.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**6-Speed Automatic**

*   **Harsh Shifting**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Drivers may experience rough or jerky shifts between gears.
    *   Solution: Transmission fluid flush, software update, or valve body replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,200+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Transmission Slipping**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The transmission hesitates or fails to engage gears properly.
    *   Solution: Inspection and potential replacement of damaged clutch packs or torque converter.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $4,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Premature Bushing Wear**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Worn suspension bushings can lead to clunking noises, loose steering, and uneven tire wear.
    *   Solution: Replacing the worn bushings in the control arms, sway bar links, or other suspension components.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Rear Leaf Spring Sag**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The rear leaf springs can sag over time, especially when the van is frequently loaded with heavy cargo, causing a reduction in ride height.
    *   Solution: Replacing the leaf springs.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Battery Drain**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The battery drains overnight or within a short period, leading to difficulty starting the van.
    *   Solution: Identifying and repairing parasitic draws or replacing a faulty battery or alternator.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Infotainment System Glitches**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Intermittent issues with the infotainment system, such as freezing, crashing, or malfunctioning touch screen.
    *   Solution: Software updates or replacement of the infotainment unit.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $1000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Sliding Door Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The sliding door can become difficult to open or close, or it may get stuck due to faulty rollers or latch mechanisms.
    *   Solution: Repair or replacement of the door rollers, latch, or cables.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $600+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Seatbelt Retractor Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Seatbelts failing to retract properly.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the seatbelt retractor mechanism.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $400+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Air Conditioning Malfunction**
    *Description/Symptoms: Inconsistent cold air production.
    *Solution: Repair or replacement of the AC compressor, condenser, or hoses.
    *Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1000+
    *Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2023 GMC Savana 3500 has issues ranging from relatively minor electrical glitches and suspension component wear to more significant engine and transmission problems. The severity and cost of repairs can vary significantly depending on the specific issue. Addressing these potential problems proactively can help to extend the lifespan and reliability of the vehicle.
