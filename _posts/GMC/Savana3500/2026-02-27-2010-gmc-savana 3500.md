## Electrical

### Standard Electrical System
**Alternator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Battery warning light illuminated, dimming headlights, difficulty starting the engine, or complete loss of electrical power.
- **Solution:** Replace the alternator.
- **Cost:** $400 - $700

**Power Window Motor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Power windows not operating up or down, slow operation, or unusual noises when attempting to use them.
- **Solution:** Replace the power window motor and regulator assembly.
- **Cost:** $250 - $500

## Interior

### Cabin Components
**HVAC Blower Motor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** No air coming from vents, or air only coming from certain vents regardless of setting. Intermittent operation.
- **Solution:** Replace the HVAC blower motor and resistor.
- **Cost:** $200 - $450

### Summary Verdict
The 2010 GMC Savana 3500 demonstrates solid reliability, particularly in its powertrain. The V8 engine options and automatic transmissions are generally robust and designed for heavy-duty use. The primary areas where owners might encounter issues are within the electrical system, specifically alternator failures and power window motor malfunctions. The interior can also see issues with the HVAC blower motor. These are not uncommon for vehicles of this age and type, and often stem from consistent use. Proactive checks of the electrical system and addressing any HVAC quirks promptly can ensure the Savana 3500 continues its dependable service.
