## Electrical

### General Electrical System
**Intermittent Power Loss**
- **Symptoms:** Dashboard lights flicker, accessories fail intermittently, engine may stall.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis of wiring harness issues, faulty grounds, or a failing alternator. Replacement of affected components.
- **Cost:** $300 - $800

**Faulty Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Battery dies unexpectedly, vehicle may not start.
- **Solution:** Identify parasitic draw from modules, aftermarket accessories, or faulty components. Repair or replace faulty items.
- **Cost:** $150 - $500

### Summary Verdict
The 2016 GMC Savana 3500 demonstrates strong overall reliability, particularly with its core powertrain and chassis components, which are known for their durability in demanding applications. The available V8 engines and automatic transmissions generally perform without significant widespread issues. However, potential owners should be aware of some electrical gremlins, such as intermittent power loss and battery drain, which can require diagnostic attention. Minor issues like sticking door latches and HVAC blower motor failures are also noted but are typically repairable at a moderate cost. For a heavy-duty van, the Savana 3500 offers a dependable platform, with most reported issues being manageable and not indicative of systemic design flaws.
