The 2022 GMC Savana 3500 is a full-size van geared towards commercial use, offering a robust platform for cargo and passenger transport. It is offered with a standard 4.3L V6 engine that produces 276 hp and 298 lb-ft of torque, a 6.6L V8 engine that produces 401 hp and 464 lb-ft of torque, and a 2.8L Duramax Turbo-Diesel engine that produces 181 hp and 369 lb-ft of torque. The 4.3L V6 is paired with an 8-speed automatic transmission while the 6.6L V8 and 2.8L Duramax Turbo-Diesel engines are paired with a 6-speed automatic transmission. Owners often praise the Savana's spacious interior and towing capability, but some find the fuel economy and outdated design to be drawbacks.

**Electrical**

*   **Battery Drain**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The battery drains unexpectedly overnight or within a short period, preventing the vehicle from starting. This can be caused by a parasitic draw from an electrical component that is not shutting off properly, an issue with the alternator, or an old/damaged battery.
    *   Solution: Thoroughly inspect the electrical system to identify the source of the parasitic draw. Replace the faulty component and/or replace the battery.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Infotainment System Freezing/Crashing**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The infotainment screen freezes or crashes intermittently while in operation, rendering navigation, audio, and other features inoperable.
    *   Solution: Perform a system software update.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 - $150+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Side Door Roller Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Difficulty opening or closing the side door due to worn or broken rollers. This can cause the door to stick or require excessive force to operate.
    *   Solution: Replace the side door rollers.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **HVAC Blower Motor Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The HVAC blower motor stops working, resulting in no airflow from the vents for heating or air conditioning.
    *   Solution: Replace the HVAC blower motor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $400+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2022 GMC Savana 3500, while appreciated for its utility, appears to exhibit some electrical and interior issues, potentially causing minor inconveniences and moderate repair costs. While there were no widespread or high-cost issues reported for the engine and transmission options, problems like battery drain, infotainment system freezes, and HVAC blower motor failures are worth considering. The side door roller failure is also another concern to look out for. Overall, the vehicle seems relatively reliable, but potential buyers should be aware of these possible issues to make an informed decision.
