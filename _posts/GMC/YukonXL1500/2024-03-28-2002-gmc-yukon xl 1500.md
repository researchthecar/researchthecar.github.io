**2002 GMC Yukon XL 1500**

The 2002 GMC Yukon XL 1500 is a full-size SUV that comes standard with a 5.3L V8 engine and a 4-speed automatic transmission. It is also available with a 6.0L V8 engine and a 5-speed automatic transmission. The Yukon XL 1500 has a towing capacity of up to 8,100 pounds and a seating capacity of up to 9 passengers.

People like the Yukon XL 1500 for its spacious interior, powerful engine, and towing capacity. However, some people have complained about its fuel economy and handling.

**Engine**

**5.3L V8**

* **Intake manifold gasket failure:** The intake manifold gasket can fail, causing coolant and oil to leak. Symptoms include a loss of coolant, oil leaks, and a rough idle. The solution is to replace the intake manifold gasket. Estimated cost to fix: $300 - $600+
* **Spark plug failure:** The spark plugs can fail, causing a misfire. Symptoms include a rough idle, loss of power, and poor fuel economy. The solution is to replace the spark plugs. Estimated cost to fix: $100 - $200+
* **Valve cover gasket failure:** The valve cover gasket can fail, causing oil to leak. Symptoms include an oil leak from the top of the engine and a burning oil smell. The solution is to replace the valve cover gasket. Estimated cost to fix: $200 - $400+

**6.0L V8**

* **Head gasket failure:** The head gasket can fail, causing coolant and oil to leak. Symptoms include a loss of coolant, oil leaks, and a rough idle. The solution is to replace the head gasket. Estimated cost to fix: $1,000 - $2,000+

**Transmission**

**4-speed automatic**

* **Torque converter failure:** The torque converter can fail, causing the transmission to slip or shudder. Symptoms include a slipping transmission, shuddering, and a loss of power. The solution is to replace the torque converter. Estimated cost to fix: $1,000 - $2,000+

**5-speed automatic**

* **Transmission control module failure:** The transmission control module can fail, causing the transmission to shift erratically or not at all. Symptoms include a transmission that shifts erratically, a loss of power, and a check engine light. The solution is to replace the transmission control module. Estimated cost to fix: $500 - $1,000+

**Suspension**

* **Front suspension strut failure:** The front suspension struts can fail, causing a loss of control and a rough ride. Symptoms include a clunking noise, a loss of control, and a rough ride. The solution is to replace the front suspension struts. Estimated cost to fix: $500 - $1,000+
* **Rear suspension spring failure:** The rear suspension springs can fail, causing the vehicle to sag and lose stability. Symptoms include a sagging rear end and a loss of stability. The solution is to replace the rear suspension springs. Estimated cost to fix: $300 - $600+

**Electrical**

* **Battery drain:** The battery can drain, causing the vehicle to not start. Symptoms include a dead battery and a lack of power. The solution is to diagnose and repair the cause of the battery drain. Estimated cost to fix: $100 - $500+
* **Alternator failure:** The alternator can fail, causing the battery to not charge. Symptoms include a dead battery and a lack of power. The solution is to replace the alternator. Estimated cost to fix: $300 - $600+
* **Starter failure:** The starter can fail, causing the vehicle to not start. Symptoms include a clicking noise when trying to start the vehicle and a lack of power. The solution is to replace the starter. Estimated cost to fix: $200 - $400+

**Exterior**

* **Rust:** The body of the Yukon XL 1500 can rust, especially in areas where salt is used on the roads. Symptoms include rust spots on the body of the vehicle. The solution is to prevent rust by applying rust-proofing and repairing any rust spots that do appear. Estimated cost to fix: $500 - $2,000+

**Interior**

* **Power window failure:** The power window motors or switches can fail, causing the windows to not work. Symptoms include windows that do not go up or down, or windows that go up or down slowly. The solution is to replace the failed motors or switches. Estimated cost to fix: $200 - $500+
* **Climate control failure:** The climate control system can fail, causing the heat or air conditioning to not work. Symptoms include a lack of heat or air conditioning, or the climate control system not working properly. The solution is to diagnose and repair the failed component. Estimated cost to fix: $200 - $1,000+

**Summary**

The 2002 GMC Yukon XL 1500 is a spacious and powerful SUV. However, it has some potential issues, including engine problems, transmission problems, suspension problems, electrical problems, and interior problems. The severity and cost of these issues can vary depending on the specific problem.
