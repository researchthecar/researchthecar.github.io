The 2001 GMC Yukon XL 1500 is a full-size SUV that was available with a range of engine and transmission options. It was praised for its spacious interior, powerful engine, and towing capabilities. However, it also had some common issues that owners should be aware of.

**Engine**

* **5.3L V8**
    * **Intake manifold gasket failure:** The intake manifold gasket can fail, causing coolant and oil leaks. Symptoms include overheating, low coolant levels, and a rough idle. The fix involves replacing the intake manifold gasket. Estimated cost: $500 - $800+
    * **Valve cover gasket leaks:** The valve cover gaskets can leak, causing oil to leak onto the engine. Symptoms include oil leaks, a burning oil smell, and low oil levels. The fix involves replacing the valve cover gaskets. Estimated cost: $200 - $400+
* **6.0L V8**
    * **Piston ring failure:** The piston rings can fail, causing excessive oil consumption and blue smoke from the exhaust. Symptoms include low oil levels, a knocking noise from the engine, and decreased performance. The fix involves replacing the piston rings. Estimated cost: $2,000 - $3,000+
    * **Vortec heads cracking:** The Vortec heads can crack, causing coolant and oil leaks. Symptoms include overheating, low coolant levels, and a rough idle. The fix involves replacing the Vortec heads. Estimated cost: $1,500 - $2,000+

**Transmission**

* **4L60E automatic transmission**
    * **Shifting problems:** The transmission may have difficulty shifting gears, or it may slip or shudder during shifts. Symptoms include delayed shifts, harsh shifts, and loss of power. The fix usually involves replacing the transmission fluid and filter, or rebuilding the transmission. Estimated cost: $500 - $2,000+
    * **Torque converter failure:** The torque converter can fail, causing the transmission to slip or shudder. Symptoms include loss of power, overheating, and a burning smell. The fix involves replacing the torque converter. Estimated cost: $1,000 - $1,500+
* **4L80E automatic transmission**
    * **Overdrive failure:** The overdrive unit can fail, causing the transmission to lose power in overdrive. Symptoms include a lack of power at highway speeds, and a Check Engine Light. The fix involves replacing the overdrive unit. Estimated cost: $500 - $1,000+

**Suspension**

* **Front suspension:** The front suspension components, such as the ball joints, control arms, and tie rods, can wear out over time. Symptoms include clunking or rattling noises from the front end, uneven tire wear, and difficulty steering. The fix involves replacing the worn components. Estimated cost: $500 - $1,000+
* **Rear suspension:** The rear suspension components, such as the leaf springs and shocks, can also wear out over time. Symptoms include a rough ride, sagging in the rear end, and difficulty handling. The fix involves replacing the worn components. Estimated cost: $500 - $1,000+

**Electrical**

* **Alternator failure:** The alternator can fail, causing the battery to drain and the vehicle to lose power. Symptoms include dimming headlights, a dead battery, and a Check Engine Light. The fix involves replacing the alternator. Estimated cost: $500 - $800+
* **Starter failure:** The starter can fail, preventing the engine from starting. Symptoms include a clicking noise when trying to start the vehicle, and a dead battery. The fix involves replacing the starter. Estimated cost: $300 - $600+

**Exterior**

* **Rust:** The body of the Yukon XL 1500 can be prone to rust, especially in areas with harsh winters. Symptoms include bubbling or flaking paint, and rust spots on the body. The fix involves repairing or replacing the rusted areas. Estimated cost: $500 - $2,000+

**Interior**

* **Dashboard cracking:** The dashboard can crack over time, especially in areas exposed to sunlight. Symptoms include visible cracks in the dashboard. The fix involves replacing the dashboard. Estimated cost: $500 - $1,000+

**Summary**

The 2001 GMC Yukon XL 1500 is a capable SUV with a spacious interior and powerful engine. However, it has some common issues that owners should be aware of, including engine gasket failures, transmission problems, and suspension wear. The severity of the issues varies, but some can be costly to repair.
