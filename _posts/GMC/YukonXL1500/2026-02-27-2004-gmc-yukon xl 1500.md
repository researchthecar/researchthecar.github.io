# 2004 GMC Yukon XL 1500

### Overview
The 2004 GMC Yukon XL 1500, a full-size SUV known for its spacious interior and towing capability, generally presents a solid reliability record for its era. While it shares many components with its Chevrolet Tahoe and Suburban counterparts, specific attention should be paid to its powertrain and electrical systems, which can occasionally exhibit issues. Owners often praise its comfortable ride and robust build, making it a popular choice for families and those needing significant cargo or towing capacity. However, like any vehicle of this age, proactive maintenance and awareness of common problem areas are key to ensuring long-term dependability and avoiding costly repairs.

---


## Engine

### 5.3L Vortec V8 (LM7)
**Intake Manifold Gasket Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Coolant leaks, rough idle, potential engine overheating.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the intake manifold gaskets.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600

**Active Fuel Management (AFM) Lifter Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Engine misfires, ticking noise from the engine, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of lifters, camshaft, and potentially AFM delete.
- **Cost:** $1,500 - $3,000+

## Transmission

### 4L60-E (4-Speed Automatic)
**Torque Converter Clutch (TCC) Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Slipping, shuddering, failure to engage or disengage.
- **Solution:** Rebuild or replacement of the torque converter and transmission.
- **Cost:** $1,200 - $2,500

**Overheating**
- **Symptoms:** Delayed shifting, slipping, burning smell.
- **Solution:** Transmission fluid and filter change, installation of an auxiliary transmission cooler.
- **Cost:** $200 - $500

## Suspension

### Standard Chassis
**Ball Joint Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Clunking noises, uneven tire wear, steering instability.
- **Solution:** Replacement of worn ball joints.
- **Cost:** $400 - $800

**Shock/Strut Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Bouncy ride, poor handling, visible leaks.
- **Solution:** Replacement of shocks or struts.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600


## Electrical

### Body Control Module (BCM)
**Intermittent Electrical Failures**
- **Symptoms:** Malfunctioning power windows, locks, lights, or dashboard indicators.
- **Solution:** Reprogramming or replacement of the BCM.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1,000


### Alternator
**Premature Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Dimming lights, battery warning light, starting issues.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the alternator.
- **Cost:** $400 - $700

### Summary Verdict
The 2004 GMC Yukon XL 1500 is a generally reliable full-size SUV, particularly with the 5.3L and 6.0L Vortec V8 engines. Common issues tend to be related to the 4L60-E transmission's torque converter and potential intake manifold leaks on the 5.3L. Electrical gremlins, often stemming from the BCM or alternator, can also arise. Suspension components like ball joints and shocks are typical wear items for a vehicle of this size and age. While not immune to problems, addressing these known areas with proper maintenance can ensure the Yukon XL 1500 remains a dependable vehicle for years to come.
