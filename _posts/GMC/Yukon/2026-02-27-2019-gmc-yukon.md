# 2019 GMC Yukon Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2019 GMC Yukon is a full-size SUV offering impressive hauling capabilities, spacious three-row seating, and robust V8 power. However, prospective buyers should review recurring owner complaints regarding powertrain performance, electrical glitches, and steering assist failures before purchasing.

---

## Engine

### 5.3L V8 Engine

The standard 5.3-liter V8 engine produces 355 horsepower and 383 lb-ft of torque, providing capable daily towing and acceleration. While generally robust, owners have occasionally reported unexpected stalling and valvetrain ticking noises.

**Unexpected Engine Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle shuts down completely while driving or immediately after remote/standard starting and idling.
- **Solution:** Diagnose electrical or fuel delivery components; inspect throttle body and sensors for faults.

**Valve Ticking Noise**
- **Symptoms:** Audible ticking sound originating from the valvetrain during engine operation.
- **Solution:** Inspect lifters and valvetrain components for wear; service or replace affected parts.

### 6.2L V8 Engine

The larger 6.2-liter V8 engine generates 420 horsepower and 460 lb-ft of torque, paired with a smooth-shifting transmission. It offers enhanced towing performance and brisk acceleration for the heavy SUV.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 6-speed automatic transmission comes standard on base trims and handles everyday driving duties. Some owners have experienced premature internal wear and sudden slippage.

**Transmission Slipping and Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Sudden mechanical failure, severe slipping, or dropping out of gear, leaving the vehicle stranded in traffic.
- **Solution:** Rebuild or replace the transmission unit depending on internal component damage.

### 10-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 10-speed automatic transmission delivers power smoothly across a wide gear range on higher trims. While efficient, a few drivers note erratic shifting behavior or gear lag.

**Harsh Shifting and Gear Lag**
- **Symptoms:** Transmission gets stuck in low gears, refuses to shift properly at low speeds, or produces vibrations during high-gear transitions.
- **Solution:** Reprogram the transmission control module (TCM) or inspect internal valve body components.

## Brakes

### Trailer Brake System

The integrated trailer brake controller aids in safe towing operations. A recurring electronic glitch can trigger persistent false warning chimes.

**Constant Trailer Brake Warnings**
- **Symptoms:** Persistent beeping and dashboard alerts stating 'Service Trailer Brake System' even when no trailer is attached.
- **Solution:** Inspect trailer brake control module and related wiring harnesses for corrosion or faults.

## Steering

### Power Steering System

The electronic power steering system provides light maneuverability for a large vehicle, though unexpected assist failures require immediate diagnostic attention.

**Loss of Power Steering Assist**
- **Symptoms:** Service power steering warning message appears, followed by a sudden loss of steering assist and heavy steering resistance.
- **Solution:** Inspect the electronic power steering gear assembly and wiring; replace faulty components or steering gear units as needed.

## Electrical

### Interior and Exterior Electrical

The Yukon relies heavily on complex cabin electronics for seating, lighting, and infotainment. Owners have occasionally noted malfunctions in convenience features like power folding seats.

**Power Seat Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Electrically controlled third-row seats fail to slide forward or backward.
- **Solution:** Inspect seat adjustment switches, motors, and track wiring for power supply issues.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V761000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Antilock/Traction Control/Electronic Limited Slip:Wheel Speed Sensor/Tone Ring](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=GMC&model=Yukon&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** Unintended activation of the driveline-protection system will cause unintended braking on the wheel on the opposite side of the failed sensor, causing the vehicle to pull to one side unexpectedly, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will reprogram the EBCM, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V289000: Power Train:Transfer Case (4-Wheel Drive)](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=GMC&model=Yukon&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** A component missing from the drivetrain transfer case may cause the front and/or rear wheels to lock up without warning to the driver. Front or rear wheel lockup increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and, if necessary, replace the transfer case assembly, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2019 GMC Yukon is a capable and comfortable family cruiser, but used-car buyers should thoroughly inspect the powertrain and electrical accessories. Pay close attention to transmission shift quality during a test drive and verify that all electronic features, steering assists, and trailer brake systems operate without warning lights.
