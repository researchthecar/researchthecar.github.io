# 2023 GMC Yukon Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2023 GMC Yukon offers a spacious and commanding presence with multiple powerful engine choices and a modern cabin. However, prospective buyers should review reported owner complaints carefully, particularly regarding powertrain behavior, electrical warning lights, and occasional accessory or braking concerns.

---

## Engine

### 5.3L V8 Engine

The standard 5.3-liter V8 engine produces 355 horsepower and 383 lb-ft of torque, providing capable performance for everyday towing and driving needs.

### 6.2L V8 Engine

The available 6.2-liter V8 engine delivers robust performance with 420 horsepower and 460 lb-ft of torque, though owners have reported occasional severe internal issues.

**Catastrophic Engine Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Sudden engine shutdown and mechanical failure while driving at highway speeds.
- **Solution:** Requires professional inspection and potential engine replacement by an authorized service center.

**Engine Stalling and Knocking**
- **Symptoms:** Unusual knocking noises under the hood followed by unexpected engine shutdown and dashboard warning lights.
- **Solution:** Have the vehicle towed for diagnostic testing of internal engine components or replacement.

### 3.0L Duramax Turbo-Diesel I6

The 3.0-liter Duramax turbo-diesel inline-six generates 277 horsepower and 460 lb-ft of torque, offering strong low-end pulling power and high fuel efficiency.

## Transmission

### 10-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 10-speed automatic transmission is paired across the lineup to optimize fuel economy and power delivery, but select owners have experienced valve body failures.

**Valve Body and Shifting Failures**
- **Symptoms:** Random downshifting, sudden stops, or a 'Service Transmission' computer notification.
- **Solution:** Inspect and replace the faulty transmission control valve body or update control modules.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes and Control Module

Brake system reliability is critical, and a subset of owners have experienced electronic control module failures affecting braking performance.

**Brake System and ABS Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Sudden loss of normal braking capability accompanied by dashboard warnings for ABS and Electronic Stability Control.
- **Solution:** Replace the faulty brake system control module and verify proper hydraulic function.

## Electrical

### Infotainment and Instrument Cluster

The electronic displays and cabin technology are feature-rich, though screen flashing and module glitches have been noted by some drivers.

**Screen Blinking and Display Failures**
- **Symptoms:** Front dashboard and infotainment screen flicker or fail, obscuring speed and odometer details.
- **Solution:** Diagnose software glitches or replace faulty display modules.

## Exterior

### Windshield Wipers

Exterior visibility features include rain-sensing wipers, which can occasionally experience intermittent electronic or mechanical outages.

**Rain-Sensing Wiper Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Windshield wipers randomly stop working during rain and refuse to reactivate for 10-15 minutes.
- **Solution:** Inspect wiper motor, relays, and rain sensor components for faults.

## Safety

### Seat Belts

Safety restraints generally function as expected, but isolated incidents of spontaneous unlatching have been reported.

**Unintended Seatbelt Release**
- **Symptoms:** Driver's seatbelt unlatches spontaneously while driving without any pressure on the release button.
- **Solution:** Inspect and replace the seatbelt buckle assembly if defective.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V903000: Exterior Lighting:Lighting Control Module:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=GMC&model=Yukon&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** The daytime running lights (DRLs) may not deactivate when the headlights are on. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 108, "Lamps, Reflective Devices, and Associated Equipment." DRLs that do not deactivate as intended may result in a glare, reducing visibility, and increasing the risk.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 23V642000: Suspension:Rear](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=GMC&model=Yukon&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** The rear suspension outer control arm bolts may not have been properly heat-treated, which can cause the bolts to break. A broken suspension bolt can result in misalignment of the rear wheel, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace both rear suspension outer control arm bolts, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V674000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Brake Fluid Low Warning: Lamp](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=GMC&model=Yukon&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Vehicle Safety Standard number 135, "Light Vehicle Brake Systems." Without a warning light, the vehicle may be driven with low brake fluid, which can reduce braking performance and increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 25V274000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Engine:Hard Parts Internal/Mechanical](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=GMC&model=Yukon&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** Engine failure increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the engine, and as necessary, repair or replace the engine. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

When shopping for a used 2023 GMC Yukon, buyers should conduct a thorough pre-purchase inspection focusing on the transmission shift behavior, braking electronics, and engine health. While many vehicles provide dependable service, verifying that open recalls and control module updates have been completed is recommended.
