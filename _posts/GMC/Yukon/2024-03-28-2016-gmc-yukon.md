**Introduction:**

The 2016 GMC Yukon is a full-size SUV known for its spacious interior, powerful engines, and rugged capabilities. It is offered with a choice of two engine options: a 5.3L V8 and a 6.2L V8. Both engines are paired with an 8-speed automatic transmission. The Yukon comes in various trim levels, offering different features and configurations. Despite its strengths, some owners have reported specific issues with the vehicle.

**Engine**

**5.3L V8**

- **Intake Manifold Gasket Failure:**
  - Description/Symptoms: Loss of coolant, rough idling, reduced engine power
  - Solution: Replace intake manifold gasket
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
  - Recalls: None Found

- **Oil Leak from Oil Pan:**
  - Description/Symptoms: Oil leak from under the vehicle
  - Solution: Replace oil pan gasket
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500
  - Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**8-Speed Automatic**

- **Shifting Issues:**
  - Description/Symptoms: Slipping gears, delayed shifts, rough shifting
  - Solution: Transmission software update or replacement
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500
  - Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

**Front Suspension:**

- **Strut Mount Failure:**
  - Description/Symptoms: Clunking or rattling noises, reduced ride comfort
  - Solution: Replace strut mount
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
  - Recalls: None Found

- **Sway Bar Link Failure:**
  - Description/Symptoms: Knocking or rattling noises, reduced handling stability
  - Solution: Replace sway bar link
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300
  - Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

- **Battery Drain Issue:**
  - Description/Symptoms: Difficulty starting, battery drains quickly
  - Solution: Replace faulty component causing drain (e.g., alternator, starter)
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
  - Recalls: None Found

- **Headlight Issues:**
  - Description/Symptoms: Dim or flickering headlights
  - Solution: Replace headlight assembly or wiring harness
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
  - Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

- **Rust on Exhaust Tips:**
  - Description/Symptoms: Rust on chrome exhaust tips
  - Solution: Replace exhaust tips
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200
  - Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

- **Navigation System Issues:**
  - Description/Symptoms: Navigation freezing or not functioning properly
  - Solution: Software update or replacement
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500
  - Recalls: None Found

- **Climate Control Issues:**
  - Description/Symptoms: Malfunctioning controls, temperature issues
  - Solution: Replace climate control module
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $700
  - Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

Overall, the 2016 GMC Yukon exhibits a range of issues, some of which can be costly to repair. The engine and transmission issues are relatively minor, while suspension and electrical problems can be more severe and require significant expenses. The interior and exterior issues are mostly cosmetic or inconvenience-related. The severity of these issues varies, and not all Yukons experience them. However, it is essential for potential buyers to be aware of these potential problems before purchasing a 2016 GMC Yukon.
