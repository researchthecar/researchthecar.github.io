# 2024 GMC Yukon Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2024 GMC Yukon brings bold styling, cavernous interior space, and powerful engine options to the full-size SUV segment. However, prospective buyers should review owner-reported complaints carefully, particularly regarding powertrain behavior, sudden engine stalling, and complex electrical glitches. While it remains a popular choice for families and heavy towing duties, staying informed on these potential problem areas can help you navigate a used vehicle purchase successfully.

---

## Engine

### 5.3L V8

The standard 5.3-liter V8 engine produces 355 horsepower and 383 lb-ft of torque, offering solid everyday performance and towing capability for most family needs.

### 6.2L V8

The larger 6.2-liter V8 generates 420 horsepower and 460 lb-ft of torque, providing robust acceleration and effortless hauling, though it requires attentive maintenance.

**Loss of Motive Power and Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have reported sudden engine shutdowns or loss of propulsion while driving at highway speeds, accompanied by check engine lights.
- **Solution:** Take the vehicle to an authorized dealership to check for open manufacturer recalls and software updates or hardware repairs.

### 3.0L Duramax Turbo-Diesel I6

The 3.0-liter turbo-diesel inline-six delivers 277 horsepower and an impressive 460 lb-ft of torque, maximizing fuel economy and low-end pulling power.

## Transmission

### 10-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 10-speed automatic transmission pairs with all engine choices to optimize efficiency and smooth power delivery, though some owners report hydraulic control issues.

**Valve Body and Shifting Defects**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have experienced harsh lunging, shuddering, failure to shift out of lower gears, or sudden shifts into neutral.
- **Solution:** Requires diagnostic testing at a dealer; faulty valve bodies often need replacement.

## Electrical

### Electrical and Infotainment System

The extensive electronics suite handles safety features, cabin displays, and cameras, but intermittent software glitches can frustrate drivers.

**Camera and Screen Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** Backup and surround-view cameras randomly go blank or become inoperative, requiring repeated software flashes or module replacements.
- **Solution:** Dealerships perform software updates or replace failing camera modules and wiring harnesses.

## Exterior

### Windshield Wipers

Exterior accessories and wiper systems generally function well, though occasional electrical issues affect wiper operation.

**Wiper Motor and Switch Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Windshield wipers may continue running continuously even when the vehicle is turned off, or they can fail completely during heavy rain.
- **Solution:** Inspect wiper control modules and switches; faulty components require replacement.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V674000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Brake Fluid Low Warning: Lamp](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=GMC&model=Yukon&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Vehicle Safety Standard number 135, "Light Vehicle Brake Systems." Without a warning light, the vehicle may be driven with low brake fluid, which can reduce braking performance and increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 25V274000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Engine:Hard Parts Internal/Mechanical](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=GMC&model=Yukon&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** Engine failure increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the engine, and as necessary, repair or replace the engine. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V619000: Fuel System, Diesel:Storage:Tank Assembly](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=GMC&model=Yukon&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** The original fuel tank may have been replaced with an incorrect fuel tank, which can allow fuel to leak in a rollover crash. A fuel leak in the presence of an ignition source increases the risk of a vehicle fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect for the correct fuel tank and replace it, if necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2024 GMC Yukon is a highly capable and luxurious full-size SUV that appeals to buyers needing serious passenger room and towing capacity. However, prospective second-hand owners should thoroughly verify that all recall work has been performed and check for any history of transmission valve body or engine drivability issues. Pre-purchase inspections are strongly recommended to ensure all electronic features and powertrain components are operating as expected.
