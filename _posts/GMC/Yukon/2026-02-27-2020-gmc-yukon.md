# 2020 GMC Yukon Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2020 GMC Yukon, buyers will find a traditional, full-size SUV offering robust towing capabilities and generous passenger space. While these vehicles generally deliver strong performance, owner reports highlight a few mechanical areas that warrant careful pre-purchase inspection, particularly regarding the transmission and powertrain systems.

---

## Engine

### 5.3L V8 Engine

The 5.3L V8 engine produces 355 horsepower and 383 lb-ft of torque, providing reliable everyday hauling and towing performance. Buyers should routinely check for fluid leaks and ensure proper maintenance history.

**Engine Coolant Leak and Hesitation**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have reported illuminated check engine warning lights, abnormal popping noises from the engine compartment, and hesitation during acceleration alongside coolant loss.
- **Solution:** Inspect cooling components and engine seals for leaks; repair or replace failing gaskets or hoses as needed.

### 6.2L V8 Engine

The high-output 6.2L V8 engine delivers 420 horsepower and 460 lb-ft of torque paired with smooth power delivery. It offers brisk acceleration and strong towing capacity for demanding drivers.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

The traditional 6-speed automatic transmission comes paired with the standard V8 engine setup. It offers dependable shifting characteristics for daily driving and routine towing duties.

### 10-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 10-speed automatic transmission provides optimized gear ratios for efficiency and towing performance. Prospective buyers should thoroughly test-drive the vehicle to check for any shuddering or harsh shifting.

**Transmission Shudder and Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Owners note severe transmission shuddering, sudden slipping, unexpected locking of wheels, and complete transmission failure while driving.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified mechanic inspect the transmission fluid condition and valve operation; severe cases may require a complete transmission overhaul or replacement.

## Brakes

### Brake System and Trailer Brakes

The braking system provides stopping power suited for a heavy full-size SUV, though auxiliary towing brake modules and vacuum assists should be verified during inspection.

**Trailer Brake Warnings and Loss of Assist**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers experience persistent trailer brake warning messages on the dash causing driver distraction, as well as temporary loss of brake power assist during extended trips.
- **Solution:** Inspect the trailer brake wiring harness, vacuum lines, and brake booster components for proper operation.

## Safety

### Collision Avoidance and Cameras

Advanced safety features enhance driver awareness, but owners should verify that parking sensors, backup cameras, and collision warnings operate without interference.

**Forward Collision Alert and Camera Obstruction**
- **Symptoms:** Reports indicate unexpected forward collision alerts coinciding with a momentary drop in brake pedal pressure, alongside backup camera image obstructions.
- **Solution:** Clean camera lenses and sensor areas; have dealer software updates applied if electronic safety systems misbehave.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V115000: Tires](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=GMC&model=Yukon&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** Over cured tires may develop a break in the sidewall resulting in sudden air loss or a belt edge separation which could lead to a tread/belt loss. Either condition can cause a loss of vehicle control, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the tires, replacing them as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V837000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=GMC&model=Yukon&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** Over-pressurization can crack the fuel pump and cause leaks, increasing the risk of a fire in the presence of an ignition source.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will replace the fuel pump, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V761000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Antilock/Traction Control/Electronic Limited Slip:Wheel Speed Sensor/Tone Ring](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=GMC&model=Yukon&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** Unintended activation of the driveline-protection system will cause unintended braking on the wheel on the opposite side of the failed sensor, causing the vehicle to pull to one side unexpectedly, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will reprogram the EBCM, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V289000: Power Train:Transfer Case (4-Wheel Drive)](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=GMC&model=Yukon&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** A component missing from the drivetrain transfer case may cause the front and/or rear wheels to lock up without warning to the driver. Front or rear wheel lockup increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and, if necessary, replace the transfer case assembly, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2020 GMC Yukon is a capable and spacious full-size SUV that serves families and towers well, but used-car buyers should proceed with diligence. Pay close attention to transmission shift quality and test for any shuddering during a pre-purchase inspection to help avoid unexpected repair costs.
