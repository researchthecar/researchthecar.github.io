# 2017 GMC Yukon Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2017 GMC Yukon, buyers will find a spacious and capable full-size SUV equipped with robust V8 engine options. However, ownership history reveals several notable mechanical and electrical pain points that require careful inspection before purchase. Prospective owners should pay close attention to transmission shift quality, brake performance, and suspension components to avoid unexpected repair bills.

---

## Engine

### 5.3L V8 (EcoTec3)

The standard 5.3-liter EcoTec3 V8 produces 355 horsepower and 383 lb-ft of torque, providing strong towing capacity and adequate daily drivability. While generally durable, it is susceptible to active fuel management (AFM) lifter issues that can lead to expensive internal repairs.

**Lifter Failure and Engine Knock**
- **Symptoms:** Sudden ticking or knocking noises from the engine bay, accompanied by a loss of power or engine misfires.
- **Solution:** Inspect and replace failed valve lifters or damaged camshaft components.

### 6.2L V8 (EcoTec3)

Available on Denali trims, the larger 6.2-liter V8 generates 420 horsepower and 460 lb-ft of torque, paired with an 8-speed automatic transmission. It offers brisk acceleration and superior towing capability, though buyers should still listen closely for valvetrain noise during a test drive.

## Transmission

### Hydra-Matic 8-Speed Automatic

The 8-speed automatic transmission found in many models provides smooth highway cruising when operating properly. However, it is prone to well-documented shudder, harsh engagement, and premature wear issues that may require major servicing.

**Transmission Shudder and Slipping**
- **Symptoms:** Rough shifting, hesitation when accelerating from a stop, slipping gears, or a noticeable vibration and shudder at cruising speeds.
- **Solution:** Flush and replace transmission fluid with updated spec fluid, or repair/replace internal transmission components and torque converters.

## Brakes

### Brake Vacuum Pump and Pedal

The braking system provides firm stopping power under normal conditions but can suffer from vacuum pump degradation. Buyers should test the brake pedal feel thoroughly, especially at low speeds.

**Brake Pedal Hardening and Reduced Assist**
- **Symptoms:** Brake pedal suddenly feels extremely hard to push, resulting in significantly increased stopping distances and reduced power assist.
- **Solution:** Inspect the brake vacuum pump and associated lines, and replace failed components according to recall or warranty guidelines.

## Steering

### Magnetic Ride Control Shocks and Struts

The available Magnetic Ride Control suspension delivers an exceptionally smooth ride when functioning correctly. Over time, however, the specialized shocks can leak and fail, leading to a jarring and unstable ride that is expensive to fix.

**Leaking Struts and Rough Ride**
- **Symptoms:** Excessive vehicle bouncing, rough or bumpy ride quality, fluid leaks visible on shock absorbers, and a shaky steering feel.
- **Solution:** Replace worn or leaking front and rear struts/shock absorbers.

## Exterior

### Exterior Tail Lights

Lighting systems are generally reliable except for the rear LED assemblies, which are prone to premature internal electrical failures. Replacements can be costly out of pocket.

**LED Circuit Board Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Rear brake lights or taillights intermittently or permanently fail to illuminate due to cracked internal circuit board solder joints.
- **Solution:** Replace the entire taillight assembly unit.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V645000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Power Assist:Vacuum](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=GMC&model=Yukon&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** As the vacuum level drops, the brake assist decreases, increasing braking effort, extending the distance required to stop the vehicle, thereby increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will reprogram the Electronic Brake Control Module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V761000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Antilock/Traction Control/Electronic Limited Slip:Wheel Speed Sensor/Tone Ring](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=GMC&model=Yukon&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** Unintended activation of the driveline-protection system will cause unintended braking on the wheel on the opposite side of the failed sensor, causing the vehicle to pull to one side unexpectedly, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will reprogram the EBCM, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V651000: Air Bags:Frontal](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=GMC&model=Yukon&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** In the affected vehicles, certain driving conditions may cause the air bag sensing and diagnostic module (SDM) software to activate a diagnostic test. During this test, deployment of the frontal air bags and the seat belt pretensioners would not occur in the event of a crash.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will reflash the SDM software. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2017 GMC Yukon offers undeniable utility, luxury, and towing prowess, but potential buyers must proceed with caution. A thorough pre-purchase inspection focusing on the transmission shift quality, suspension condition, and brake responsiveness is essential. Securing a vehicle with a documented service history or an extended warranty can help mitigate the risk of expensive powertrain and electrical repairs.
