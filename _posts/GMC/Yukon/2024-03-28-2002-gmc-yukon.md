**Introduction**

The 2002 GMC Yukon is a versatile and capable SUV that has earned a loyal following among drivers. It offers a spacious cabin, powerful engines, and a variety of features that make it suitable for both family use and off-road adventures. However, like any vehicle, the Yukon is not without its share of problems.

**Engine**

**5.3L V8**

* **Intake Manifold Gasket Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Leaking coolant, reduced engine power, misfires
    * Solution: Replace the intake manifold gasket
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Valve Seat Recession**
    * Description/Symptoms: Loss of compression, rough idle, reduced engine power
    * Solution: Replace the cylinder heads
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**6.0L V8**

* **Hydraulic Roller Lifter Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Tapping or ticking noise from the engine, reduced engine power
    * Solution: Replace the hydraulic roller lifters
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Head Gasket Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Leaking coolant, overheating, white smoke from the exhaust
    * Solution: Replace the head gaskets
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**4L60E Automatic Transmission**

* **Torque Converter Lock-Up Clutch Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Slipping or shuddering during shifts, loss of power
    * Solution: Replace the torque converter lock-up clutch
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,200 - $1,800+
    * Recalls: 02V296000

* **Sun Gear Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Noisy transmission, difficulty shifting, loss of power
    * Solution: Replace the sun gear
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $1,500+
    * Recalls: 02V048000

**Suspension**

* **Ball Joint Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Loose steering, popping or clunking noises
    * Solution: Replace the ball joints
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $250 - $450+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Rear Differential Bearing Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Loud growling noise from the rear end
    * Solution: Replace the differential bearings
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain**
    * Description/Symptoms: Battery dies quickly when not in use
    * Solution: Identify and fix the source of the drain
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the issue
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Alternator Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Noisy alternator, dimming lights, battery warning light
    * Solution: Replace the alternator
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $250 - $400+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Rust Prone**
    * Description/Symptoms: Rust developing on the frame, body panels, and exhaust system
    * Solution: Preventative measures include regular washing and using rust-inhibiting products
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the extent of the damage
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Headlight Lens Cloudiness**
    * Description/Symptoms: Headlight lenses becoming cloudy or yellowed
    * Solution: Restore the lenses using a headlight restoration kit or replace them
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $200+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Heater Core Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: No heat coming from the vents
    * Solution: Replace the heater core
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Bluetooth Module Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Bluetooth connectivity issues, no sound from the radio
    * Solution: Replace the Bluetooth module
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $350+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2002 GMC Yukon is a reliable and versatile SUV, but it is not immune to its fair share of issues. The most common problems involve the engine, transmission, suspension, and electrical systems. While some repairs can be expensive, they are generally not catastrophic and can be avoided with regular maintenance and inspections. The Yukon's strengths, such as its spacious interior and powerful engines, outweigh its weaknesses, making it a popular choice among drivers despite its potential for repairs.
