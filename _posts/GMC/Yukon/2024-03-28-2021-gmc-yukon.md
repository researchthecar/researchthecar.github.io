**Introduction:**

The 2021 GMC Yukon is a full-size SUV that offers potent powertrains and a spacious interior. Engine options include a 5.3L V8 with 355 horsepower and 383 lb-ft of torque, a 6.2L V8 with 420 horsepower and 460 lb-ft of torque, and a 3.0L Duramax Turbo-Diesel V6 with 277 horsepower and 460 lb-ft of torque. Transmission options include a 10-speed automatic or a 10-speed heavy-duty automatic for the diesel engine. The Yukon is praised for its robust performance, comfortable cabin, and advanced safety features. However, it has faced criticism for its occasionally sluggish transmission and lackluster fuel economy.

**Engine**

**5.3L V8**
* **Intake Valve Deposits:** Carbon buildup on intake valves can cause knocking, reduced performance, and increased fuel consumption. Solution: Cleaning or replacing affected valves. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
* **Piston Ring Failure:** Premature wear or breakage of piston rings can lead to excessive oil consumption and exhaust emissions. Solution: Engine rebuild or replacement. Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,500 - $5,000+
* **Recalls:**
    * NHTSA Campaign Number: 21V561000 - Fuel Line Issue

**6.2L V8**
* **Fuel Injector Failure:** Malfunctioning fuel injectors can cause engine misfires, reduced power, and increased emissions. Solution: Replacement of affected injectors. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500 per injector
* **Timing Chain Tensioner Failure:** A faulty tensioner can lead to chain slack, engine damage, and catastrophic failure. Solution: Replacement of timing chain and tensioner. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
* **Recalls:** None Found

**3.0L Duramax Turbo-Diesel V6:**
* **Emissions System Malfunction:** Issues with the diesel particulate filter or exhaust gas recirculation system can result in increased emissions and engine performance issues. Solution: Diagnosis and repair of affected components. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+
* **Fuel Filter Clogging:** Excessive dirt or water in the fuel can clog the fuel filter, restricting fuel flow and potentially damaging the engine. Solution: Replacement of fuel filter. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200

**Transmission**

**10-Speed Automatic**
* **Delayed Shifting:** Transmission hesitation or delay in shifting gears can lead to reduced performance and increased fuel consumption. Solution: Software updates or transmission repairs. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
* **Transmission Fluid Leaks:** A leaking transmission can cause reduced fluid levels and potential transmission damage. Solution: Repair or replacement of affected components. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+

**10-Speed Heavy-Duty Automatic**
* **Overheating:** Excessive towing or extreme conditions can overheat the transmission, causing performance issues or failure. Solution: Cooling system enhancements or transmission rebuild. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,500+
* **Recalls:** None Found

**Suspension**

* **Rear Suspension Sagging:** Over time, rear suspension components can wear or weaken, leading to a sagging appearance and reduced handling. Solution: Replacement of affected components. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
* **Front Strut Failure:** Premature wear or damage to front struts can affect ride quality and handling. Solution: Replacement of affected struts. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600 per strut

**Electrical**

* **Electrical System Malfunctions:** Occasional issues with electrical components such as lights, windows, or infotainment system have been reported. Solution: Diagnosis and repair of affected modules or wiring. Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the issue

**Exterior**

* **Side Mirror Noise:** Under certain conditions, side mirrors may produce a rattling or vibrating noise. Solution: Adjustment or replacement of mirror housing. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500

**Interior**
* **Climate Control Malfunction:** Issues with the heating or air conditioning system can affect cabin comfort and temperature. Solution: Diagnosis and repair of affected components. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+
* **Infotainment System Bugs:** Software glitches or compatibility issues with the infotainment system can occur. Solution: Software updates or system resets. Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the issue

**Summary**

The 2021 GMC Yukon is a powerful and well-equipped SUV with some potential issues. Engine concerns primarily involve fuel system and valve train problems. The transmission may experience occasional delays or leaks. Suspension components can deteriorate over time, while electrical gremlins and interior electronics may occasionally malfunction. While these issues can be costly to fix, the overall severity of the problems is moderate. Proper maintenance and addressing issues promptly should ensure a reliable and enjoyable ownership experience.
