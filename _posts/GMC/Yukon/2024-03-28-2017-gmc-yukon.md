**Introduction:**

The 2017 GMC Yukon is a full-size SUV that offers a range of engine and transmission options. The 5.3L V8 engine produces 355 horsepower and 383 lb-ft of torque, while the 6.2L V8 engine produces 420 horsepower and 460 lb-ft of torque. The Yukon is available with a 6-speed automatic transmission, an 8-speed automatic transmission, or a 10-speed automatic transmission. It is known for its spacious interior, powerful towing capacity, and comfortable ride. However, it has also been reported to have certain issues.

**Engine**

* **5.3L V8 Engine**
 - **Intake Manifold Gasket Failure:** Description/Symptoms: Leaking oil or coolant from the intake manifold area, accompanied by rough idling or loss of power. Solution: Replace the intake manifold gasket. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
 - **Active Fuel Management System Issues:** Description/Symptoms: Engine misfires, rough idling, or reduced fuel economy. Solution: Repair or replace the AFM lifters or camshaft. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000+
* **6.2L V8 Engine**
 - **Valve Spring Failure:** Description/Symptoms: Broken or weak valve springs, leading to engine ticking, reduced power, or misfires. Solution: Replace the valve springs. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500+

**Transmission**

* **6-Speed Automatic Transmission**
 - **Torque Converter Shudder:** Description/Symptoms: Vibrations or shuddering felt during acceleration or gear changes. Solution: Replace the torque converter. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
 - **Transmission Fluid Leaks:** Description/Symptoms: Leaking transmission fluid from the transmission pan or seals. Solution: Repair or replace the leaking component. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+
* **8-Speed Automatic Transmission**
 - **Shifting Issues:** Description/Symptoms: Hesitation or rough shifting, delayed engagement of gears. Solution: Update the transmission software or replace faulty components. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+
* **10-Speed Automatic Transmission**
 - **No Issues Found**

**Suspension**

* **Front Lower Control Arm Bushing Failure:** Description/Symptoms: Clunking or rattling noises from the front suspension, especially over bumps. Solution: Replace the lower control arm bushings. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain Issues:** Description/Symptoms: Battery discharge while the vehicle is parked, leading to no-starts. Solution: Diagnose and repair electrical system components causing the drain. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+
* **Headlight Flickering:** Description/Symptoms: Headlights flickering or dimming intermittently. Solution: Replace faulty headlight bulbs or repair electrical connections. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
* **Infotainment System Malfunctions:** Description/Symptoms: Touchscreen freezing, navigation issues, or audio system problems. Solution: Update the software or replace the infotainment module. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+

**Exterior**

* **Rear Liftgate Latch Failure:** Description/Symptoms: Difficulty opening or closing the rear liftgate. Solution: Replace the liftgate latch or actuator. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
* **Running Board Corrosion:** Description/Symptoms: Corrosion or rust on the running boards. Solution: Replace or repair the running boards. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+

**Interior**

* **Center Console Lid Latch Failure:** Description/Symptoms: Center console lid not closing properly or latch breaking. Solution: Replace the center console lid latch. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+
* **Climate Control Issues:** Description/Symptoms: Malfunctioning climate control, uneven temperature distribution, or fan noises. Solution: Repair or replace faulty climate control components. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,000+
* **Seat Adjustment Issues:** Description/Symptoms: Difficulty adjusting the seats, or seats moving unintentionally. Solution: Repair or replace faulty seat adjustment mechanisms. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+

**Summary:**

The 2017 GMC Yukon has various reported issues, ranging from engine and transmission problems to suspension and electrical malfunctions. While some issues are minor and relatively inexpensive to fix, others can be severe and costly. Owners should be aware of these potential issues and budget accordingly for repairs.
