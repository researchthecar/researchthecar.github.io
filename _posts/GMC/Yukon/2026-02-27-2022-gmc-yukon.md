# 2022 GMC Yukon Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2022 GMC Yukon is a spacious and capable full-size SUV that offers strong utility, modern technology, and multiple robust engine options. However, prospective buyers should review recurring owner complaints regarding electrical glitches, transmission control valve failures, and certain engine-related concerns. Careful pre-purchase inspections and verification of completed recalls are recommended.

---

## Engine

### 5.3L EcoTec3 V8

The 5.3L EcoTec3 V8 produces approximately 355 horsepower and 383 lb-ft of torque, providing standard power for most trims. It pairs with a 10-speed automatic transmission and generally offers a balance of utility and everyday drivability.

### 6.2L EcoTec3 V8

The 6.2L V8 delivers 420 horsepower and 460 lb-ft of torque, offering brisk acceleration and high towing capacity for upper trims like the Denali and AT4. While potent, certain units have experienced owner complaints regarding propulsion loss and internal wear.

**Loss of Motive Power and Engine Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have reported unexpected loss of motive power, sudden engine stalling, and abnormal knocking sounds while driving at highway speeds.
- **Solution:** Have a certified mechanic inspect the engine and verify that all applicable manufacturer recalls and software updates have been performed.

### 3.0L Duramax Turbo-Diesel I6

The 3.0L Duramax turbo-diesel engine generates 277 horsepower and 460 lb-ft of torque, prized for its high torque output and impressive fuel economy on long trips. It mates to a 10-speed automatic transmission.

## Transmission

### 10-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 10-speed automatic transmission is paired across all engine choices, providing smooth shifting and optimized highway efficiency. However, some owners have reported control valve failures leading to unexpected loss of gears.

**Transmission Control Valve Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle enters limp mode, loses reverse gear, or experiences restricted speed capabilities due to an internal solenoid or valve body fault.
- **Solution:** Replace the faulty transmission control valve or valve body assembly.

## Suspension

### Rear Shocks and Air Suspension

Advanced suspension setups provide a smooth ride and off-road capability, but mechanical wear on shocks and sensors can require service.

**Shock Leaks and Height Adjustment Errors**
- **Symptoms:** Rear shocks begin leaking or dashboard displays a 'Vehicle too high, drive with caution' error preventing manual height adjustments.
- **Solution:** Inspect and replace leaking shocks or faulty suspension control components.

## Safety

### OnStar and Telematics Module

Electrical and telematics systems manage many of the vehicle's convenience and safety communications, though module failures have been noted by owners.

**Telematics Module Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of OnStar connectivity, disabling safety features, emergency calling, remote door locking, and built-in Wi-Fi apps.
- **Solution:** Replace the failed OnStar telematics module.
- **Cost:** $1100

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V617000: Seat Belts:Critical Fasteners](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=GMC&model=Yukon&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** A seat belt with a deformed rivet may not properly restrain an occupant, increasing the risk of injury during a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the rivet head formation on both the left and right side third-row seat belt buckle assemblies and replace seat belt buckle assemblies, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V903000: Exterior Lighting:Lighting Control Module:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=GMC&model=Yukon&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** The daytime running lights (DRLs) may not deactivate when the headlights are on. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 108, "Lamps, Reflective Devices, and Associated Equipment." DRLs that do not deactivate as intended may result in a glare, reducing visibility, and increasing the risk.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 25V274000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Engine:Hard Parts Internal/Mechanical](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=GMC&model=Yukon&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** Engine failure increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the engine, and as necessary, repair or replace the engine. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V619000: Fuel System, Diesel:Storage:Tank Assembly](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=GMC&model=Yukon&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** The original fuel tank may have been replaced with an incorrect fuel tank, which can allow fuel to leak in a rollover crash. A fuel leak in the presence of an ignition source increases the risk of a vehicle fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect for the correct fuel tank and replace it, if necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V085000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission:Control Module:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=GMC&model=Yukon&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** Rear wheels that lock up while driving increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will install new transmission control module (TCM) software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2022 GMC Yukon is a feature-rich, highly capable full-size SUV, but prospective buyers should carefully inspect the vehicle's history. Pay close attention to transmission operation, verify that engine recalls and updates have been completed, and test all electrical and telematics features before purchasing.
