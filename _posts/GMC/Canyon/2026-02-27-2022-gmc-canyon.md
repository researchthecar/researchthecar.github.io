# 2022 GMC Canyon Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2022 GMC Canyon is a capable midsize pickup offering versatile powertrain choices and utility for everyday driving and towing. While most owners enjoy their trucks, used-car buyers should review certain recurring owner-reported glitches, particularly involving ignition key extraction and transmission behavior in specific configurations.

---

## Engine

### 2.5L I4 Engine

The 2.5-liter naturally aspirated four-cylinder engine produces 200 horsepower and 191 lb-ft of torque, serving as the base powerplant for lower trims. It provides adequate power for basic commuting and light-duty tasks, generally maintaining a straightforward mechanical reputation.

### 3.6L V6 Engine

The 3.6-liter V6 engine generates 308 horsepower and 275 lb-ft of torque, offering stronger acceleration and robust towing capability. This widely chosen powerplant is generally reliable, though buyers should verify proper maintenance history during a pre-purchase inspection.

### 2.8L Duramax Turbo-Diesel I4

The 2.8-liter Duramax turbo-diesel four-cylinder delivers 181 horsepower and an impressive 369 lb-ft of torque for maximum towing and low-end grunt. It is a sought-after option for efficiency and pulling power, though complex emissions equipment requires attentive upkeep.

### Ignition Switch and Key Cylinder

Owner reports indicate occasional electrical and switch-related anomalies that can affect convenience features and cabin electronics.

**Ignition Key Stuck in Cylinder**
- **Symptoms:** The ignition key occasionally becomes physically locked in the cylinder after the vehicle is placed in park and shut off, preventing removal.
- **Solution:** Drivers sometimes need to restart the vehicle, shift through gears, or wiggle the steering wheel; persistent issues may require ignition cylinder or switch replacement.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 6-speed automatic transmission pairs with the base four-cylinder engine to deliver predictable gear changes. It offers a mechanically conventional design that tends to be straightforward to service.

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 8-speed automatic transmission is paired with V6 and diesel models to optimize highway efficiency and power delivery. Some owners have reported shift quality concerns, so a thorough test drive is recommended.

**First Gear Slippage**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers experience momentary slipping or hesitation when pulling away from a stop in first gear.
- **Solution:** Consult a qualified technician for diagnostic evaluation, as software updates or internal component service may be required.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V375000: Seats:Critical Fasteners](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=GMC&model=Canyon&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** An improperly welded seat frame may not properly restrain the seat occupant, increasing the risk of injury in a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the front passenger seat and replace the cushion frame, if necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2022 GMC Canyon presents a solid option for midsize truck buyers needing everyday utility and diverse engine configurations. While most systems operate reliably, prospective buyers should carefully check for ignition key retention issues and ensure the transmission shifts smoothly during a test drive.
