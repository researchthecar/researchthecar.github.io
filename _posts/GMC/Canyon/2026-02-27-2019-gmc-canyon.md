# 2019 GMC Canyon Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2019 GMC Canyon, understanding its powertrain options and potential owner-reported trouble spots can help you make an informed decision. While this midsize truck offers capable utility and diverse engine choices, owner complaints submitted to safety agencies highlight certain issues concerning powertrain shifting, electrical components, and steering functionality that prospective buyers should inspect closely.

---

## Engine

### 2.5L I4

The 2.5-liter four-cylinder engine produces 200 horsepower and 191 lb-ft of torque, serving as the base powerplant for light-duty daily driving and fleet applications.

### 3.6L V6

The 3.6-liter V6 engine delivers 308 horsepower and 275 lb-ft of torque paired with smooth daily performance, though select owners have noted isolated cooling and stalling incidents.

**Engine Cooling and Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have reported coolant temperature sensor failures illuminating the check engine light, disabling the air conditioning, and occasional unexpected engine stalling while driving at highway speeds.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified mechanic inspect the cooling sensors, wiring harnesses, and diagnostic trouble codes to determine if sensor replacement or further engine diagnostics are necessary.

### 2.8L Duramax Turbo-Diesel I4

The 2.8-liter turbo-diesel engine generates 181 horsepower and a robust 369 lb-ft of torque, providing strong low-end pulling power and improved fuel economy for towing enthusiasts.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Manual Transmission

The 6-speed manual transmission is paired exclusively with the base four-cylinder engine, offering a traditional shifting feel for base configuration models.

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 6-speed automatic transmission works alongside select engines to deliver conventional gear selection, though buyers should verify smooth operation during test drives.

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 8-speed automatic transmission provides optimized gearing for performance and fuel efficiency, but owner reports indicate potential rough shifting and slippage on inclines.

**Transmission Slipping and Harsh Shifting**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers note transmission slipping on inclines, slow or harsh morning engagement where engine revs high before lurching, and constant rough shifting at lower speeds.
- **Solution:** Inspect transmission fluid condition, check for updated powertrain control module calibrations, and have a transmission specialist evaluate internal clutch operation if slipping persists.

## Steering

### Steering Column

Steering system operation is generally responsive, but buyers should test lock-to-lock maneuverability for any unusual resistance.

**Steering Wheel Binding**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have reported instances where the steering wheel temporarily seizes or binds while executing turns at low speeds.
- **Solution:** Have the electronic power steering system, steering shaft joints, and related sensors thoroughly inspected by a qualified technician.

## Electrical

### Ignition and Climate Sensors

Electrical complaints center primarily around accessory sensors and ignition switch operation affecting key removal.

**Ignition Key Sticking and Sensor Faults**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle keys can become stuck in the ignition switch or fail to turn, preventing the vehicle from starting or shutting off properly, alongside occasional ambient temperature sensor malfunctions.
- **Solution:** Inspect the ignition cylinder and switch assembly for mechanical wear or electrical binding, and replace faulty temperature sensors as needed.

## Summary Verdict

The 2019 GMC Canyon is a capable midsize pickup truck with versatile powertrain choices, but used-car buyers should perform a thorough mechanical and electrical inspection before purchasing. Pay close attention to how the transmission shifts during a test drive—especially on inclines—and verify that all ignition functions, sensors, and steering components operate without hesitation.
