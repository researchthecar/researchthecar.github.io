# 2021 GMC Canyon Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2021 GMC Canyon offers midsize truck capability with a mix of available engine choices, but prospective buyers should review owner-reported issues carefully. While many owners enjoy its everyday utility, complaints lodged with safety authorities highlight potential concerns ranging from electrical malfunctions to drivetrain vibrations. Independent pre-purchase inspections are recommended to catch any hidden wear or intermittent glitches before finalizing a purchase.

---

## Engine

### 2.5L I4

The 2.5-liter four-cylinder engine produces approximately 200 horsepower and 191 lb-ft of torque, serving as the base power plant for light-duty tasks. It provides adequate efficiency for daily commuting, though it can feel underpowered when hauling heavy loads.

### 3.6L V6

The 3.6-liter V6 engine generates around 308 horsepower and 275 lb-ft of torque, paired typically with an 8-speed automatic transmission. It offers a strong balance of everyday drivability and towing capability, making it a popular choice among midsize truck buyers.

### 2.8L Duramax Turbo-Diesel I4

The 2.8-liter turbo-diesel engine delivers 181 horsepower and a robust 369 lb-ft of torque, mated to a 6-speed automatic transmission. It is prized for its low-end pulling power and fuel economy, though buyers should ensure proper maintenance history.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 8-speed automatic transmission pairs with gasoline engines to optimize power delivery and efficiency. Some owners have reported shift quality concerns and vibrations, making a test drive essential.

**Drivetrain Vibration and Shudder**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have reported abnormal vibrations and shuddering sensations while cruising at highway speeds, often without warning lights illuminating.
- **Solution:** Consult a qualified technician to inspect transmission fluid quality and drivetrain components for wear or misalignment.

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 6-speed automatic transmission is paired with the diesel engine option to handle high torque output smoothly. It generally offers straightforward operation, though routine fluid changes remain critical for longevity.

## Steering

### Electric Power Steering

The electric power steering system provides adjustable assist for parking and highway driving, but rare electronic failures can pose serious handling hazards.

**Loss of Power Steering Assistance**
- **Symptoms:** The electric power steering motor may unexpectedly stop working while driving, making the vehicle extremely difficult to control.
- **Solution:** Have the steering motor, wiring harness, and related electrical modules inspected immediately if steering stiffness occurs.

## Electrical

### Backup Camera System

Driver assistance and visibility systems help maneuver safely, though software glitches can occasionally disrupt camera feeds.

**Blank or Distorted Reverse Image**
- **Symptoms:** The infotainment display may show a solid blue screen or fail to display the rearview camera image when shifting into reverse.
- **Solution:** Check camera wiring connections, software firmware updates, or replace the backup camera module if hardware failure is confirmed.

## Exterior

### Exterior Lighting

Exterior lighting components are essential for visibility, and intermittent bulb or circuit failures should be checked promptly.

**Headlight Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The driver's side low beam headlight may intermittently fail to illuminate during operation without triggering a dashboard warning light.
- **Solution:** Inspect the headlight bulb, wiring connections, and fuses, and replace faulty components as necessary.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V811000: Seat Belts](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=GMC&model=Canyon&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** If an incorrect bolt was used, the seat belt may not be properly attached to the vehicle. If a seat belt assembly is not properly attached to the vehicle, the seat belt may not properly restrain an occupant in the event of a crash, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will replace the suspect bolts, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V497000: Equipment:Other:Labels](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=GMC&model=Canyon&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 110, "Tire Selection and Rims." An incorrect label may lead to unintentionally overloading the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2021 GMC Canyon is a versatile midsize truck with capable powertrain choices, but used-car buyers should perform a thorough evaluation. Pay close attention to transmission shift smoothness, electrical accessories, and steering response during a test drive. Checking service records and ensuring all past safety recalls have been addressed will help secure a more dependable ownership experience.
