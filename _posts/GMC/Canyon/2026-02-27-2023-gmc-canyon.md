# 2023 GMC Canyon Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2023 GMC Canyon enters a modern generation with a rugged stance, upscale interior technology, and a standard high-output turbocharged engine. While buyers appreciate its capability and refreshed design, early production models have drawn owner complaints concerning electrical glitches, advanced driver assistance system behaviors, and powertrain hiccups. Used-car shoppers should thoroughly inspect vehicle history records and test drive all electronic and safety systems before finalizing a purchase.

---

## Engine

### 2.7L Turbo High-Output I4

The 2.7-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine produces approximately 310 horsepower and 430 lb-ft of torque, providing strong towing capability and brisk acceleration. It serves as the sole engine option for this generation of the midsize truck, blending V6-like power with four-cylinder efficiency.

**Coolant Leak at Radiator Hose Fitting**
- **Symptoms:** A coolant leak may develop near the upper radiator hose quick-connect fitting, risking engine overheating if fluid levels drop unmonitored.
- **Solution:** Inspect the upper radiator hose connection and replace faulty seals or hoses as required.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 8-speed automatic transmission is paired exclusively with the 2.7L turbo engine across all trim levels. While generally responsive, some owners report refinement issues during low-speed city driving.

**Rough Shifting and Lurching**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may experience harsh downshifts when coming to a stop, particularly when transitioning from second to first gear, or a sharp lurch during low-speed acceleration.
- **Solution:** Have a certified technician check for updated powertrain control module calibrations or inspect internal transmission components if harsh engagement persists.

## Electrical

### Infotainment and Instrument Cluster

**Screen Freezing and Blackout**
- **Symptoms:** The infotainment display screen and digital instrument cluster may freeze, duplicate images, or turn completely black, temporarily disabling the radio, backup camera, and climate settings.
- **Solution:** Reboot the system or visit a dealership to check for software patches and module updates.

## Exterior

### Windshield Defroster and Wipers

**Ineffective Defrosting Operation**
- **Symptoms:** During cold weather conditions, the front window defroster may fail to adequately clear frost or ice from the windshield, limiting driver visibility.
- **Solution:** Check climate control blend doors and ensure software operating parameters are up to date.

## Safety

### Forward Collision Avoidance System

**Unexpected Automatic Braking**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may occasionally apply the brakes unexpectedly or trigger false forward collision alerts while driving under normal conditions.
- **Solution:** Ensure sensor lenses and cameras are clean and consult a dealership for potential software recalibrations or updates.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V133000: Electrical System:Adas:Driver Monitoring:Camera/Sensor](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=GMC&model=Canyon&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** The front camera module (FCM) system may falsely detect an obstacle, causing the automatic emergency braking (AEB) to engage unexpectedly. Unexpected automatic emergency braking can cause the vehicle to abruptly stop or slow down, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the front camera module (FCM) software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V237000: Wheels:Lugs/Nuts/Bolts/Studs](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=GMC&model=Canyon&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** Damaged bolts may loosen or break, which can result in a loss of vehicle control and increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the left and right front wheel hub bolts, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V703000: Seat Belts:Critical Fasteners](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=GMC&model=Canyon&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** A loose seat belt may not properly restrain an occupant in a crash, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will tighten the left and right front seat belt buckle attachment bolts, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2023 GMC Canyon brings impressive capability and a modern tech suite to the midsize truck segment, but as a redesign launch year, it carries some notable early-adopter quirks. Buyers should prioritize checking for software updates regarding infotainment stability and advanced safety features, while also verifying that cooling system hoses are secure. A thorough pre-purchase inspection can help ensure a trouble-free ownership experience.
