# 2024 GMC Canyon Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2024 GMC Canyon is a midsize truck that brings aggressive styling, an upgraded interior, and a standard turbocharged engine. However, early adopters have reported several recurring issues, particularly involving engine cooling, electrical systems, and braking performance. Prospective buyers should review service history and verify that all software updates and recalls have been handled.

---

## Engine

### 2.7L Turbocharged I4

Powered by a 2.7-displacement turbocharged inline-4 engine delivering solid horsepower and high torque, providing strong capability for towing and off-roading. Overall performance is praised, though early models suffer from cooling system vulnerability.

**Coolant Leaks and Overheating**
- **Symptoms:** Sweet smell of coolant, coolant pooling on the driveway, check engine light illumination, and potential engine overheating due to upper radiator hose failures.
- **Solution:** Inspect the upper radiator hose assembly for leaks or structural defects and replace faulty hoses or fittings.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

An 8-speed automatic transmission paired with the turbocharged engine, designed for smooth gear changes and optimized highway efficiency. While generally robust, some owners have reported component availability delays for internal valve body repairs.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

Brake components provide strong stopping power when functioning properly, but reports of premature rotor warping and electronic module warnings warrant a thorough pre-purchase inspection.

**Brake Pulsing and Rotor Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Brake squealing, heavy pedal pulsing at speed, and premature or uneven wear on front brake pads and rotors.
- **Solution:** Inspect brake pads and rotors for warping or uneven wear, then resurface or replace damaged components as necessary.

**Brake System Failure Warning**
- **Symptoms:** Sudden dashboard warning lights indicating brake failure and restricted vehicle speed functionality.
- **Solution:** Have the dealer diagnose the brake control module and replace faulty electronic modules or sensors.

## Electrical

### Infotainment System

The central infotainment screen handles numerous vehicle controls and media functions, though software instability can cause intermittent audio dropouts.

**Infotainment Audio Loss and Reboots**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of sound across radio, satellite, Bluetooth, and navigation sources, requiring system resets.
- **Solution:** Perform software updates or reset the infotainment module; persistent failures may require head unit replacement.

## Exterior

### Exterior Lighting & Controls

Exterior lighting systems rely heavily on digital interfaces and automated controls, which have proven prone to intermittent flickering complaints.

**Headlight Flickering and Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Headlights randomly flickering, flashing, or failing completely during night driving.
- **Solution:** Inspect headlight wiring harnesses and software controls, and apply required dealer updates or component replacements.

### Trim and Glass

Exterior and cabin exterior-adjacent elements show occasional susceptibility to environmental reflections and glass stress.

**Dashboard Trim Glare**
- **Symptoms:** Bright sunlight reflecting off interior chrome dashboard trim into the driver's eyes or side mirrors.
- **Solution:** Apply matte anti-glare films or replace offending brightwork with non-reflective accessories.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V491000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Storage:Tank Assembly](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=GMC&model=Canyon&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** The lock ring that secures the fuel pump to the fuel tank assembly may not have been fully locked during assembly, which can result in a fuel leak during a crash. A fuel leak in the presence of an ignition source increases the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and fully lock the fuel tank lock ring as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V673000: Exterior Lighting:Headlights](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=GMC&model=Canyon&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Safety Standard number 108, "Lamps, Reflective Devices, and Associated Equipment." Headlights flickering can reduce visibility, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace a module in the headlight, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V856000: Air Bags:Frontal:Passenger Side:Inflator Module](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=GMC&model=Canyon&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** Incorrect sensing and diagnostic module (SDM) software may cause a second stage passenger air bag deployment when only a first stage is intended. As such, these vehicles fail to conform to Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 208, “Occupant Crash Protection." An air bag that deploys improperly increases the risk of injury in a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the SDM software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2024 GMC Canyon is an appealing midsize truck with impressive capability, but early production models display notable quirks. Buyers should prioritize checking the cooling system for hose leaks, testing all electrical and lighting functions, and ensuring the brakes operate smoothly without pulsation. A comprehensive pre-purchase inspection can help avoid unexpected out-of-pocket repair costs.
