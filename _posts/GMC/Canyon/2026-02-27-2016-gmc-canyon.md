# 2016 GMC Canyon Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2016 GMC Canyon offers midsize truck buyers strong towing capabilities and versatile engine options, but it also carries notable reliability concerns. Owners have frequently reported critical electronic power steering failures and transmission drivability issues that require careful attention during a used vehicle inspection.

---

## Engine

### 2.5L 4-Cylinder

A naturally aspirated 4-cylinder engine producing around 200 horsepower, well-suited for light-duty commuting and basic utility tasks while offering moderate fuel economy.

### 3.6L V6

A powerful V6 engine rated at approximately 305 horsepower, providing robust acceleration and higher towing capacity for everyday work and recreational hauling.

**Loss of Motive Power and Warning Lights**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may experience an unexpected loss of engine power accompanied by various warning lights on the dashboard while attempting to accelerate or drive.
- **Solution:** Diagnostic scanning is required to identify faulty sensors or electrical faults triggering limp mode or power cutoffs.

### 2.8L Duramax Turbo-Diesel 4-Cylinder

A torque-rich diesel engine delivering around 181 horsepower and 369 lb-ft of torque, favored by buyers seeking maximum towing capability and superior fuel efficiency.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

A conventional 6-speed automatic transmission paired with select engine configurations, offering familiar shifting behavior though prone to shudder issues over time.

**Transmission Shudder and Bucking**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers often notice a noticeable shudder, vibration, or bucking sensation when slowing down, accelerating from a stop, or cruising at highway speeds.
- **Solution:** Professional diagnosis is required; fluid flushes or torque converter replacement may be necessary depending on internal wear.

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

An optional multi-speed automatic transmission designed to optimize gear selection for improved fuel efficiency and smooth power delivery.

### 6-Speed Manual Transmission

A traditional 6-speed manual gearbox available on base configurations, offering direct driver engagement for those preferring clutch control.

## Suspension

### Rear Leaf Springs

The traditional rear leaf spring suspension supports heavy payloads and towing duties, though older units are prone to annoying squeaks and binding noises.

**Leaf Pack Squeaking and Binding**
- **Symptoms:** Loud squeaking, rattling, or chirping noises emanate from the rear suspension when driving over uneven terrain or bumps.
- **Solution:** Lubricate leaf spring pads or replace worn suspension bushings and leaf pack assemblies.

## Brakes

### Brake Booster and Hydraulic System

The hydraulic braking system relies on a brake booster to amplify pedal force, though a subset of owners have experienced serious booster and pedal firmness failures.

**Stiff Brake Pedal and Lockup**
- **Symptoms:** The brake pedal can become extremely hard to depress, or brakes may intermittently lock up unexpectedly during normal driving conditions.
- **Solution:** Inspect brake booster operation and system software; faulty boosters or hydraulic components may need replacement.

## Steering

### Electric Power Steering System

The electric power steering system provides adjustable assist for low-speed maneuvering and highway cruising, but failures can result in sudden loss of assist.

**Steering Assist Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The power steering suddenly cuts out while driving, making the steering wheel extremely heavy and difficult to turn, frequently accompanied by Stabilitrak warnings.
- **Solution:** Inspect steering rack wiring, fuses, and modules; replacement of the electric power steering assembly may be required if internal faults persist.

## Electrical

### Infotainment and Ignition Switches

Cabin electronics manage infotainment, navigation, and key-release functions, with occasional reports of screen blackouts and ignition key retention.

**Screen Freezes and Stuck Ignition Keys**
- **Symptoms:** The infotainment screen and backup camera display may become completely inoperable, or the ignition switch may fail to release the key after shutting off the engine.
- **Solution:** Perform software updates for the infotainment system or replace faulty ignition switch mechanisms as needed.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V621000: Air Bags:Frontal](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=GMC&model=Canyon&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** The driver's frontal air bag may improperly inflate during second-stage deployment in the event of a high speed crash. An improperly inflated air bag increases the risk of injury in a crash.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will replace the driver's frontal air bag module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V358000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=GMC&model=Canyon&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** A damaged fuel line can create a fuel leak, increasing the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will replace the high pressure fuel pump, and high pressure fuel pipe, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 15V807000: Latches/Locks/Linkages](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=GMC&model=Canyon&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** The affected vehicles have a console compartment lid latch assembly that may fail to keep the compartment door closed in the event of a crash. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of the Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 201, "Occupant Protection in Interior Impact." If the console compartment door does not remain.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will replace the console compartment door latch assembly, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V125000: Air Bags:Frontal](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=GMC&model=Canyon&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** The driver frontal air bag may improperly inflate during second-stage deployment in the event of a high speed crash. An improperly inflated air bag increases the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will replace the driver side frontal air bag module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

When shopping for a used 2016 GMC Canyon, prioritize a thorough pre-purchase inspection that specifically checks the operation of the electric power steering and the responsiveness of the automatic transmission. While the truck delivers strong utility and diverse powertrain choices, verifying that steering assist and transmission shudder issues have been addressed will help ensure a much safer and more reliable ownership experience.
