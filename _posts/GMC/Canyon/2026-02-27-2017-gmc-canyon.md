# 2017 GMC Canyon Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2017 GMC Canyon brings midsize truck versatility with a choice of capable engine and transmission configurations. While it offers respectable utility and comfortable daily driving manners, used car buyers should pay close attention to potential powertrain quirks, electrical glitches, and steering assist concerns reported by owners.

---

## Engine

### 2.5L 4-Cylinder

The base 2.5-liter four-cylinder engine produces approximately 200 horsepower and 191 lb-ft of torque, offering adequate efficiency for light daily use.

**Engine Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may unexpectedly stall while executing turns or during regular driving routes, increasing the risk of accidents.
- **Solution:** Diagnostic inspection of fuel delivery, sensors, and throttle body components by a qualified technician.

### 3.6L V6

The 3.6-liter V6 engine delivers around 308 horsepower and 275 lb-ft of torque, providing strong towing capability and confident highway acceleration.

### 2.8L Duramax Turbo-Diesel

The 2.8-liter Duramax turbo-diesel four-cylinder generates 181 horsepower and a robust 369 lb-ft of torque, tailored for maximum towing pull and fuel economy.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

Paired with select engines, this 6-speed automatic transmission delivers traditional shifting characteristics for daily work and commuting.

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 8-speed automatic transmission provides optimized gearing for efficiency, though certain units have exhibited notable driveline shudder issues.

**Transmission Shudder and Bucking**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may experience a noticeable shudder, vibration, or bucking sensation during acceleration, often linked to torque converter operation.
- **Solution:** Professional inspection; specialized fluid flushes or torque converter service may be required.

## Brakes

### Braking System

Standard hydraulic disc brakes provide solid stopping power, though occasional sensor warnings can trigger driver concern.

**Trailer Brake and ABS Warnings**
- **Symptoms:** Unprompted warning messages regarding trailer brakes or ABS engagement illuminate on the instrument cluster.
- **Solution:** Check brake control modules, wiring harnesses, and wheel speed sensors for corrosion or faults.

## Steering

### Power Steering Assist

Electric power steering systems provide light effort at low speeds, but unexpected loss of assist has been reported by some owners.

**Steering Assist Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The steering wheel suddenly becomes heavy and difficult to maneuver while driving, accompanied by warning lights on the dashboard.
- **Solution:** Inspect power steering electrical connections, sensors, and steering module for faults.

## Electrical

### Ignition and Infotainment

Onboard electronics and convenience features generally function well, though intermittent cabin electrical anomalies have been noted.

**Stuck Ignition Key**
- **Symptoms:** The key becomes trapped in the ignition cylinder and cannot be removed, or the vehicle fails to shut down electrically.
- **Solution:** Inspect the shifter microswitches and ignition lock cylinder assembly for wear or electrical faults.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V358000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=GMC&model=Canyon&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** A damaged fuel line can create a fuel leak, increasing the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will replace the high pressure fuel pump, and high pressure fuel pipe, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2017 GMC Canyon remains a compelling choice for a used midsize truck thanks to its comfortable ride and versatile engine lineup. Prospective buyers should thoroughly test drive the vehicle to check for any transmission shudder symptoms, and ensure that steering and electrical systems are fully operational before finalizing a purchase.
