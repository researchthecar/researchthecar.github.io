# 2019 GMC Sierra 1500 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2019 GMC Sierra 1500 offers strong capability and a comfortable ride, but used-car buyers should approach it with a level of caution. A significant portion of owner complaints center around powertrain issues, specifically concerning transmission performance and engine valvetrain components. Thoroughly reviewing maintenance records and inspecting critical mechanical areas can help prospective buyers avoid costly surprises.

---

## Engine

### 2.7L Turbo I4

A turbocharged 4-cylinder engine producing around 310 horsepower and 348 lb-ft of torque, providing capable daily driving performance.

### 5.3L EcoTec3 V8

A traditional pushrod V8 engine making approximately 355 horsepower and 383 lb-ft of torque, popular for towing but prone to occasional valvetrain and DFM system concerns.

**Engine Lifter Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Owners report sudden engine misfires, tapping noises, or loss of power caused by failing Dynamic Fuel Management (DFM) lifters.
- **Solution:** Requires diagnosis by a qualified technician and replacement of the affected lifters and potentially the camshaft.

**Engine Stalling and Loss of Power**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle unexpectedly loses motive power or stalls while driving, often accompanied by dashboard warning lights.
- **Solution:** Have the powertrain control module, fuel system, and electrical components inspected for fault codes.

### 6.2L EcoTec3 V8

A high-output V8 engine generating around 420 horsepower and 460 lb-ft of torque, paired with high towing capacities and premium performance.

### 3.0L Duramax Turbo-Diesel I6

A diesel powerplant focused on high torque output and impressive fuel economy for long-distance hauling.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

Multi-speed automatic transmission option that offers optimized gear ratios for efficiency, but has drawn owner complaints regarding rough shifting behavior and torque converter longevity.

**Transmission Shudder and Hard Shifting**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers experience a shudder or vibration during acceleration, harsh gear engagement, or hesitation before slamming into gear.
- **Solution:** Flush and replace transmission fluid with updated fluid specifications or service the torque converter and valve body as needed.

**Transmission Slipping and Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Transmission slips out of gear, fails to engage drive or reverse, or experiences internal pump and torque converter failure.
- **Solution:** Depending on the extent of internal wear, transmission repair or complete replacement may be required.

### 10-Speed Automatic Transmission

A multi-speed automatic transmission designed to keep the engine in its optimal power band and improve overall towing efficiency.

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

A traditional automatic transmission known for simpler mechanical design, paired with select powertrain configurations.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

Hydraulic brake systems designed to handle truck payloads, though electronic module failures and warning lights require prompt attention.

**Brake System Warning and Pedal Drop**
- **Symptoms:** Brake system and ABS warning lights illuminate unexpectedly, and the brake pedal may depress completely to the floorboard.
- **Solution:** Inspect the electronic brake control module (EBCM), hydraulic lines, and brake booster for faults or fluid leaks.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V115000: Tires](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=GMC&model=Sierra+1500&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** Over cured tires may develop a break in the sidewall resulting in sudden air loss or a belt edge separation which could lead to a tread/belt loss. Either condition can cause a loss of vehicle control, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the tires, replacing them as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V889000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Antilock/Traction Control/Electronic Limited Slip:Abs Warning Light](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=GMC&model=Sierra+1500&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** Due to an Electronic Brake Control Module (EBCM) software error, the vehicle's Electronic Stability Control (ESC) and Antilock Brake System (ABS) may become disabled. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 126, "Electronic Stability Control" and 135, "Light Vehicle Brake Systems.".
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will reprogram the EBCM, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V888000: Electrical System:12V/24V/48V Battery:Cables](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=GMC&model=Sierra+1500&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** An intermittent electrical connection between the B+ battery cable and the alternator could cause the vehicle to stall, increasing the risk of a crash. The intermittent connection could also lead to electrical arcing, which could generate sufficient heat to damage surrounding material, increasing the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will inspect for the presence of glue or other contamination at the B+ cable attachment to the alternator and clean the connection and reattach the B+ cable connection, as necessary. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V792000: Seat Belts](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=GMC&model=Sierra+1500&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** The seat belt brackets may not be secured to the seat frame in the front-row center seating position. If a seat-belt bracket is not secured, the seat belt may not properly restrain an occupant in the front-row center seat position, increasing the risk of injury in a crash.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will inspect left-side and right-side front-row center seat-belt bracket attachments and reassemble it correctly as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V055000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Power Assist](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=GMC&model=Sierra+1500&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** This software has an error, and as a result, the vehicle's electronic brake assist may be disabled. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 126, "Electronic Stability Control" and 135, "Light Vehicle Brake Systems." A loss of electronic brake assist can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will reprogram the EBCM, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V814000: Seat Belts:Pretensioner](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=GMC&model=Sierra+1500&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** When the front seat belt pretensioners deploy, hot gas may vent through an opening in the pretensioner bracket, possibly igniting the carpet. A vehicle fire could result if materials ignite inside the vehicle.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will close off the opening in the pretensioner bracket, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V423000: Tires](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=GMC&model=Sierra+1500&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** Over cured tires may develop a break in the sidewall resulting in sudden air loss or a belt edge separation which could lead to a tread/belt loss. Either condition can cause a loss of vehicle control, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 21V985000: Tires:Temporary/Emergency Spare Tire](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=GMC&model=Sierra+1500&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** An ABS system that does not function properly can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will provide a new 17" spare tire and wheel assembly that is compatible with the accessory wheels, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

When shopping for a used 2019 GMC Sierra 1500, a pre-purchase inspection by a trusted mechanic is essential. Pay close attention to how the transmission shifts during a test drive and check for any history of engine valvetrain repairs. Selecting a well-maintained vehicle with documented service history will greatly reduce the likelihood of running into unexpected mechanical headaches.
