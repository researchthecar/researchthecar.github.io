# 2017 GMC Sierra 1500 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2017 GMC Sierra 1500 offers strong capability and a comfortable ride, but buyers looking at the used market should carefully evaluate potential mechanical and electrical hiccups. While its robust engine lineup and versatile configurations make it a popular half-ton truck, owner complaints highlight recurring issues ranging from transmission behavior to intermittent braking and electrical glitches. Thoroughly inspecting service records and performing a comprehensive test drive will help ensure any prospective vehicle is free of these common faults.

---

## Engine

### 4.3L V6 EcoTec3

The base 4.3-liter V6 engine delivers dependable utility with respectable torque for everyday driving and light work. It represents a simpler entry point into the Sierra lineup, generally avoiding the active fuel management complications seen on larger V8s.

### 5.3L V8 EcoTec3

The 5.3-liter V8 engine provides around 355 horsepower and balanced towing capacity, making it the most common choice for buyers. However, owners should watch out for valvetrain and lifter concerns tied to cylinder deactivation systems.

**Active Fuel Management Lifter Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Ticking noises from the engine bay, sudden loss of motive power, or illumination of the check engine warning light.
- **Solution:** Inspect valvetrain components and replace failed or collapsed lifters as necessary.

### 6.2L V8 EcoTec3

The high-output 6.2-liter V8 pushes 420 horsepower and provides stout towing performance paired with premium trims. It offers brisk acceleration but demands careful maintenance and quality fuel.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

The standard 6-speed automatic transmission handles daily driving duties well, though some owners report rough engagement and hesitation over time.

**Transmission Shudder and Hard Shifting**
- **Symptoms:** Noticeable vibrations or shuddering during acceleration, harsh shifts between lower gears, or delayed engagement into drive and reverse.
- **Solution:** Perform a transmission fluid flush and update control module calibration, or repair internal components if mechanical wear is present.

**Transmission Slipping**
- **Symptoms:** Unexpected loss of vehicle speed, engine revving without matching acceleration, or sudden drops into limp mode.
- **Solution:** Diagnose hydraulic pressure losses and replace worn clutch packs or torque converters.

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

The optional 8-speed automatic transmission aims to optimize fuel economy and power delivery, but it faces similar owner complaints regarding shift quality and mechanical shudder.

**Power Train Shudder and Engagement Delays**
- **Symptoms:** Rough shifting, shuddering sensations at highway speeds, and delayed engagement when shifting selectors.
- **Solution:** Service transmission fluid and inspect internal valving or torque converter operation.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

The braking system generally provides confident stopping power, but intermittent loss of vacuum assist can lead to a hard pedal feel and decreased responsiveness.

**Stiff Pedal and Reduced Braking Power**
- **Symptoms:** Brake pedal becoming exceptionally hard to depress at low speeds, resulting in significantly increased stopping distances.
- **Solution:** Inspect the brake vacuum pump and assist systems for pressure loss or mechanical failure.

## Steering

### Electric Power Steering

The electric power steering system provides light and accurate maneuverability at parking speeds, but intermittent electrical drops can temporarily reduce assist.

**Intermittent Steering Loss**
- **Symptoms:** Sudden loss of power steering assist during low-speed turns, accompanied by flickering instrument clusters or warning messages.
- **Solution:** Check electrical connections, ground points, and steering control modules for faults.

## Electrical

### Electrical System

Electrical accessories generally function well, though specific wiring harnesses and rear glass defroster circuits have caused isolated safety concerns.

**Defroster and Window Wiring Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Unexplained burning smells, smoke, or unexpected shattering of the rear window glass during remote start or cold weather operation.
- **Solution:** Inspect rear window grid wiring harnesses and replace damaged glass assemblies.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V645000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Power Assist:Vacuum](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=GMC&model=Sierra+1500&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** As the vacuum level drops, the brake assist decreases, increasing braking effort, extending the distance required to stop the vehicle, thereby increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will reprogram the Electronic Brake Control Module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V761000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Antilock/Traction Control/Electronic Limited Slip:Wheel Speed Sensor/Tone Ring](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=GMC&model=Sierra+1500&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** Unintended activation of the driveline-protection system will cause unintended braking on the wheel on the opposite side of the failed sensor, causing the vehicle to pull to one side unexpectedly, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will reprogram the EBCM, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V651000: Air Bags:Frontal](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=GMC&model=Sierra+1500&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** In the affected vehicles, certain driving conditions may cause the air bag sensing and diagnostic module (SDM) software to activate a diagnostic test. During this test, deployment of the frontal air bags and the seat belt pretensioners would not occur in the event of a crash.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will reflash the SDM software. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V487000: Child Seat:Tether: Connector/Clip](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=GMC&model=Sierra+1500&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 225, "Child Restraint Anchorage Systems." If the instructions are missing from the owner's manual, the child seat may be installed incorrectly and not be properly secured, increasing the risk of injury in a crash.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will provide a corrected insert for the owners manual, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V437000: Air Bags:Frontal](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=GMC&model=Sierra+1500&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** While being previously remedied for recall 16V-651, the affected vehicles may not have received the complete software update necessary to remedy the recall condition. Without the update, certain driving conditions may cause the air bag sensing and diagnostic module (SDM) software to activate a diagnostic test.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will reflash the SDM software. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2017 GMC Sierra 1500 is a capable and comfortable full-size truck, but prospective buyers should approach with an informed eye. Pay close attention during the test drive for any transmission shudder, rough shifting, or unusual engine ticking that might indicate lifter wear. Ensuring that the brakes operate smoothly and checking the electrical and steering systems will help you secure a reliable, hard-working truck.
