Here's a compilation of common issues and complaints reported for the 2024 GMC Sierra 1500, categorized by major component and specific engine/transmission options. It's important to note that as a new model year, widespread, long-term data is still emerging. This information is based on initial owner reports and available service information.

The 2024 GMC Sierra 1500 is a full-size pickup truck known for its rugged design, comfortable interior, and a range of engine options. Engine choices include a 2.7L Turbo High-Output (310 hp, 430 lb-ft of torque), a 5.3L EcoTec3 V8 (355 hp, 383 lb-ft of torque), a 6.2L EcoTec3 V8 (420 hp, 460 lb-ft of torque), and a 3.0L Duramax Turbo-Diesel (305 hp, 495 lb-ft of torque). Many appreciate its advanced technology features and towing capabilities. Common dislikes often revolve around the price point and certain interior material choices in lower trim levels.

**Engine**

**2.7L Turbo High-Output**

*   **Turbocharger Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, check engine light, whistling or whining noise from the engine, potential for excessive oil consumption, damage to other engine components.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the turbocharger. Inspection of related components (oil lines, intercooler) for damage.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**5.3L EcoTec3 V8**

*   **Active Fuel Management (AFM) Lifter Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Misfires, rough idling, check engine light, ticking or knocking noise from the engine, potential for camshaft damage.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the failed lifters, potentially including the camshaft. AFM disable kits are a popular aftermarket solution to prevent recurrence.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $3,000 - $6,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Oil Consumption**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Low oil level warnings, noticeable decrease in oil level between oil changes, blue smoke from the exhaust.
    *   Solution: Possible solutions include replacing the PCV valve, or piston rings.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50-$100+, $4,000+ (for piston ring replacement).
    *   Recalls: None Found

**6.2L EcoTec3 V8**

*   **Active Fuel Management (AFM) Lifter Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Identical to the 5.3L EcoTec3 V8 AFM lifter failure symptoms. Misfires, rough idling, check engine light, ticking or knocking noise from the engine, potential for camshaft damage.
    *   Solution: Identical to the 5.3L EcoTec3 V8 AFM lifter failure solution. Replacement of the failed lifters, potentially including the camshaft. AFM disable kits are a popular aftermarket solution to prevent recurrence.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $3,000 - $6,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**3.0L Duramax Turbo-Diesel**

*   **Fuel Injector Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rough idle, hard starting, excessive black smoke from exhaust, reduced power, potential for check engine light.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis to identify the faulty injector(s). Replacement of the affected fuel injector(s).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $2,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**10-Speed Automatic (All Engines Except Base 2.7L)**

*   **Harsh Shifting/Clunking**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Abrupt or jerky shifts, particularly between certain gears (e.g., 1-3, 5-7), a noticeable clunking sound during shifts.
    *   Solution: Software reflash of the transmission control module (TCM). Replacement of valve body components if issues persist.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+ (reflash), $800 - $2,500+ (valve body)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**8-Speed Automatic (Base 2.7L)**

*No Issues Found*

**Suspension**

*   **Premature Wear of Upper Control Arm Bushings**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Squeaking or creaking noises from the front suspension, loose or wandering steering feel, uneven tire wear.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the upper control arm bushings.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Freezing/Lagging**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The infotainment screen freezes, is unresponsive to touch, or experiences significant lag when switching between functions.
    *   Solution: Software updates from the dealer. If problems persist, the head unit might need replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: Free (software update), $500 - $1500+ (head unit replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Sensor Malfunctions (e.g., Blind Spot Monitoring, Parking Sensors)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Intermittent or complete failure of safety system features. False warnings or failure to detect objects.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis to identify the faulty sensor(s). Replacement of the affected sensor(s).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+ per sensor
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Automatic Running Board Malfunction**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Running boards fail to extend or retract properly. Can become stuck in either position.
    *   Solution: Lubrication of joints and hinges. Replacement of the motor or entire running board assembly if necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $200+ (lubrication/minor repair), $500 - $1,500+ (replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Rattling/Buzzing Noises from Dashboard or Door Panels**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Annoying rattles or buzzing sounds originating from various interior panels, particularly when driving on rough roads.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis to pinpoint the source of the noise. Tightening of fasteners, addition of sound dampening material.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $300+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary:** The 2024 GMC Sierra 1500, while offering many appealing features, has already shown some common issues. Engine problems, particularly AFM lifter failure in the V8 engines and potential turbocharger issues in the 2.7L turbo, are potentially expensive and should be carefully considered. Electrical gremlins, like infotainment glitches and sensor malfunctions, are also relatively common. While some issues can be resolved with software updates or minor repairs, others may require significant investment. Prospective buyers should be aware of these potential problems and factor them into their purchasing decision. Monitor reliability trends as more data becomes available throughout the model year.
