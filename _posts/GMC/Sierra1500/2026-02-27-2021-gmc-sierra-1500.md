# 2021 GMC Sierra 1500 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2021 GMC Sierra 1500 offers a commanding presence, robust towing capabilities, and a range of powertrain choices suited for various hauling needs. However, prospective buyers should review recurring owner complaints regarding engine operation, transmission performance, and electrical issues.

---

## Engine

### 2.7L Turbocharged I4

The 2.7-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine produces 310 horsepower and 348 lb-ft of torque, serving as the capable base engine for everyday driving and light utility.

### 5.3L EcoTec3 V8

The popular 5.3-liter V8 delivers 355 horsepower and 383 lb-ft of torque, balancing traditional truck performance with widespread availability across trims.

### 6.2L EcoTec3 V8

The high-output 6.2-liter V8 pumps out 420 horsepower and 460 lb-ft of torque for maximum towing, though it requires premium fuel and warrants careful inspection for internal mechanical issues.

**Engine Knock and Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Engine starts shaking, develops a loud knocking noise, and may experience total loss of motive power requiring replacement.
- **Solution:** Have the engine inspected by a certified technician; severely damaged engines will require a complete motor replacement.

### 3.0L Duramax Turbo-Diesel I6

The 3.0-liter inline-six turbo-diesel generates 277 horsepower and 460 lb-ft of torque, offering excellent fuel economy and strong low-end pulling power for long-distance drivers.

### Engine Cooling & Control

Monitors and regulates engine operating temperatures to prevent overheating and ensure efficient thermal management.

**Coolant Control Valve Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Illuminated check engine light accompanied by a diagnostic trouble code (such as P1098) indicating a stuck or unresponsive coolant control valve.
- **Solution:** Replace the faulty engine coolant control valve assembly.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 8-speed automatic transmission pairs with select engine options to deliver smooth gear selection and optimized highway efficiency.

### 10-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 10-speed automatic transmission provides close gear ratios for heavy towing, though some owners report rough shifting behavior and drivetrain shudder over time.

**Transmission Shudder and Harsh Shifting**
- **Symptoms:** Noticeable vibration, shuddering between 25-65 mph under light throttle, harsh or delayed gear changes, and abnormal mechanical noises.
- **Solution:** Perform a professional diagnostic evaluation; fluid flushes or torque converter service may be required depending on wear.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes & Electronic Parking Brake

Hydraulic service brakes and electronic parking mechanisms designed to bring the heavy truck to a safe stop under various loads.

**Electronic Parking Brake and Stopping Concerns**
- **Symptoms:** Electronic parking brake fails to release properly, or brake pedal feels unresponsive resulting in increased stopping distances.
- **Solution:** Inspect rear brake actuators, pads, and rotors, and recalibrate or replace faulty electronic parking brake components.

## Electrical

### Shift-to-Park and Infotainment Controls

Manages cabin electronics, infotainment connectivity, and gear selector sensor feedback.

**Shift-to-Park Warning and Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Continuous dashboard alerts stating 'Shift to Park' even when the vehicle is in park, preventing the vehicle from shutting off properly and draining the battery.
- **Solution:** Inspect and replace the faulty shifter microswitch or gear selector assembly.

## Exterior

### Tailgate Latch Assembly

Cabin and exterior access controls, including door latches and power-locking tailgate mechanisms.

**Power Tailgate Unintended Opening**
- **Symptoms:** Tailgate unlatches and drops on its own after exposure to heavy rain or car washes due to water intrusion into the electronic release switch.
- **Solution:** Inspect the tailgate exterior release switch assembly and replace or seal the unit to prevent water short circuits.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V792000: Seat Belts](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=GMC&model=Sierra+1500&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The seat belt brackets may not be secured to the seat frame in the front-row center seating position. If a seat-belt bracket is not secured, the seat belt may not properly restrain an occupant in the front-row center seat position, increasing the risk of injury in a crash.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will inspect left-side and right-side front-row center seat-belt bracket attachments and reassemble it correctly as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V811000: Seat Belts](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=GMC&model=Sierra+1500&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** If an incorrect bolt was used, the seat belt may not be properly attached to the vehicle. If a seat belt assembly is not properly attached to the vehicle, the seat belt may not properly restrain an occupant in the event of a crash, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will replace the suspect bolts, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V684000: Tires](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=GMC&model=Sierra+1500&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** Overcured tires may experience a carcass break in the sidewall resulting in a sudden air loss or could develop a belt edge separation which could lead to partial or full tread/belt loss. Either condition increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will inspect vehicles and replace tires that have DOT number and production mold number combinations identified by Continental, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V985000: Tires:Temporary/Emergency Spare Tire](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=GMC&model=Sierra+1500&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** An ABS system that does not function properly can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will provide a new 17" spare tire and wheel assembly that is compatible with the accessory wheels, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V797000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission:Control Module:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=GMC&model=Sierra+1500&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** Rear wheel lock-up can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will install new transmission control module software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V274000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Engine:Hard Parts Internal/Mechanical](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=GMC&model=Sierra+1500&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** Engine failure increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the engine, and as necessary, repair or replace the engine. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V083000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission:Control Module:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=GMC&model=Sierra+1500&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** Rear wheel lock up increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the vehicles and update the transmission control software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2021 GMC Sierra 1500 is a capable and feature-rich full-size truck, but buyers should perform a thorough pre-purchase inspection. Pay close attention to transmission shift quality, test for potential coolant control valve issues, and verify that all electronic features—particularly the shifter and tailgate mechanisms—operate correctly.
