# 2016 GMC Sierra 1500 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2016 GMC Sierra 1500 offers a blend of capability, comfort, and advanced features, but prospective buyers should carefully review its mechanical track record. While it remains a popular choice for truck enthusiasts, certain powertrains and systems have documented owner complaints regarding braking feel, transmission behavior, and intermittent electrical or structural concerns. A thorough pre-purchase inspection is strongly recommended.

---

## Engine

### 4.3L V6 EcoTec3

This 4.3-liter V6 engine generates approximately 285 horsepower and 305 lb-ft of torque, providing capable entry-level performance for daily driving and light utility. It generally offers solid reliability when properly maintained.

### 5.3L V8 EcoTec3

The 5.3-liter V8 pushes out around 355 horsepower and 383 lb-ft of torque, serving as the most common and versatile choice for towing and hauling. While mostly robust, isolated owner reports note severe internal failures.

**Catastrophic Engine Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Loud thud followed by a flashing check engine light, grinding sounds, and sudden loss of engine operation.
- **Solution:** Requires professional diagnosis; severe cases may necessitate engine replacement or rebuilding of internal components.

### 6.2L V8 EcoTec3

Delivering an impressive 420 horsepower and 460 lb-ft of torque, this 6.2-liter V8 provides high-end towing capability and brisk acceleration. It pairs with higher trims and demands attentive maintenance.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

This traditional six-speed automatic is paired with select engine configurations, known for offering predictable shifting behavior and simpler long-term maintenance compared to its eight-speed counterpart.

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

The eight-speed automatic provides wider gear ratios for optimized efficiency, but it has generated frequent owner reports regarding harsh shifting and erratic behavior.

**Rough Shifting and Transmission Slippage**
- **Symptoms:** Hard shifting between gears, hesitation, bucking, and momentary slipping with engine RPM flare before engaging.
- **Solution:** Software updates or valve body service may improve shift quality, though mechanical wear can require component replacement.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

The braking system generally provides confident stopping power, though some drivers report intermittent firmness or loss of assist at low speeds.

**Hard Brake Pedal and Loss of Assist**
- **Symptoms:** Brake pedal suddenly becomes stiff or travels directly to the floor with a noticeable reduction in braking force, increasing stopping distance.
- **Solution:** Inspect the vacuum pump, brake booster, and hydraulic lines for pressure loss or mechanical failure.

## Steering

### Steering Assembly

Steering response is typically direct for a full-size pickup, but high-mileage examples may develop looseness in the linkage or steering box.

**Excessive Steering Play**
- **Symptoms:** Noticeable looseness or free play in the steering wheel, requiring constant minor corrections while maintaining direction.
- **Solution:** Inspect steering linkage, lash adjustments, and the steering box for wear.

## Electrical

### Rear Window Defroster

Electrical accessories and wiring harnesses generally function well, though isolated incidents involving high-draw accessories like the rear defroster have been reported.

**Rear Window Shattering**
- **Symptoms:** Smoke near the rear window followed by spontaneous shattering when the rear defroster is activated.
- **Solution:** Replace the rear glass assembly and inspect the electrical grid connections for shorts.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V645000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Power Assist:Vacuum](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=GMC&model=Sierra+1500&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** As the vacuum level drops, the brake assist decreases, increasing braking effort, extending the distance required to stop the vehicle, thereby increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will reprogram the Electronic Brake Control Module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V761000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Antilock/Traction Control/Electronic Limited Slip:Wheel Speed Sensor/Tone Ring](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=GMC&model=Sierra+1500&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** Unintended activation of the driveline-protection system will cause unintended braking on the wheel on the opposite side of the failed sensor, causing the vehicle to pull to one side unexpectedly, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will reprogram the EBCM, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V651000: Air Bags:Frontal](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=GMC&model=Sierra+1500&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** In the affected vehicles, certain driving conditions may cause the air bag sensing and diagnostic module (SDM) software to activate a diagnostic test. During this test, deployment of the frontal air bags and the seat belt pretensioners would not occur in the event of a crash.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will reflash the SDM software. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V256000: Suspension:Front:Control Arm](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=GMC&model=Sierra+1500&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** Inadequate welds may allow the control arm to separate from the bushing, compromising steering and increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will replace the left and right front upper control arms and realign the vehicle, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V437000: Air Bags:Frontal](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=GMC&model=Sierra+1500&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** While being previously remedied for recall 16V-651, the affected vehicles may not have received the complete software update necessary to remedy the recall condition. Without the update, certain driving conditions may cause the air bag sensing and diagnostic module (SDM) software to activate a diagnostic test.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will reflash the SDM software. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V504000: Air Bags:Side/Window:Curtain:Inflator](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=GMC&model=Sierra+1500&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** A separated inflator end cap or inflator rupture can allow compressed gas to escape, resulting in the end cap or other components being propelled into the vehicle, increasing the risk of injury or crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the left and right side RRAB modules, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V736000: Air Bags:Side/Window:Curtain:Inflator](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=GMC&model=Sierra+1500&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** If the end cap separates from the inflator, the compressed gas will escape and the end cap can be propelled into the vehicle, increasing the risk of injury or a crash.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will replace the RRAB modules on the left and/or right side, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2016 GMC Sierra 1500 is a capable and comfortable truck, but buyers should approach it with an informed eye. Pay close attention to the transmission shift quality during a test drive, and verify that the brakes and steering feel responsive and firm. A comprehensive pre-purchase inspection by a qualified mechanic will help uncover any lingering mechanical or electrical issues before purchase.
