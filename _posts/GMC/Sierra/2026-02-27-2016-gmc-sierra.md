# 2016 GMC Sierra Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2016 GMC Sierra offers robust capabilities, comfortable ride quality, and strong towing performance, making it a popular choice in the full-size truck market. However, prospective buyers should carefully evaluate powertrain options and common owner-reported trouble spots before purchasing a used model. While many trucks operate reliably, certain mechanical and electrical issues frequently surface in owner complaints.

---

## Engine

### 4.3L EcoTec3 V6

The base 4.3L V6 engine delivers 285 horsepower and 305 lb-ft of torque, offering reliable daily driving capability and respectable utility for light-duty tasks.

### 5.3L EcoTec3 V8

The 5.3L V8 provides a strong balance of capability with 355 horsepower and 383 lb-ft of torque, serving as the most common and versatile engine choice for Sierra buyers.

**Fuel Pump and Engine Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers report unexpected engine stalling while driving, loss of engine power, and failure to start due to fuel pump failure [nhtsa-complaint-11493553, nhtsa-complaint-11660921].
- **Solution:** Diagnose the fuel pressure and electrical supply; replace faulty fuel pump assemblies or relays as necessary.

### 6.2L EcoTec3 V8

The high-output 6.2L V8 generates 420 horsepower and 460 lb-ft of torque, providing exceptional towing performance and brisk acceleration for demanding truck owners.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

The traditional 6-speed automatic transmission pairs with base engines, offering straightforward shifting mechanics and generally dependable long-term operation.

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 8-speed automatic transmission improves fuel economy and gear selection on select trims, though some models experience noticeable shifting complaints.

**Harsh Shifting and Slippage**
- **Symptoms:** Owners report rough gear engagement, bucking, hesitation during acceleration, and sudden RPM flares when shifting between gears [nhtsa-complaint-11729705, nhtsa-complaint-11118643].
- **Solution:** Update transmission control module software, flush fluid, or repair internal wiring harnesses and valve bodies per technical service guidelines.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

Brake system complaints center on occasional loss of braking assistance, requiring attentive inspection during pre-purchase evaluations.

**Hard Brake Pedal and Loss of Assist**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers experience a stiff brake pedal that suddenly requires excessive effort to depress or travels to the floor with reduced stopping power at low speeds [nhtsa-complaint-11710305, nhtsa-complaint-11706495].
- **Solution:** Inspect vacuum pump operation, brake booster lines, and hydraulic components; replace failed brake boosters or vacuum pumps.

## Steering

### Steering Assembly

Steering complaints indicate that certain trucks develop noticeable play over time, impacting directional stability.

**Excessive Steering Play**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers notice looseness, slop, or free play in the steering wheel, causing the truck to wander or feel unstable at highway speeds [nhtsa-complaint-11194964, nhtsa-complaint-11165611].
- **Solution:** Inspect steering linkage, intermediate shafts, and gear boxes; adjust or replace worn steering components as needed.

## Exterior

### Exterior Lighting

Lighting system concerns primarily involve premature failure of LED elements in premium taillight housings.

**LED Taillight and Headlight Failure**
- **Symptoms:** LED strips in tail lamps or headlights begin to flicker, dim partially, or fail completely in brake and turn signal functions [nhtsa-complaint-11297821].
- **Solution:** Replace the faulty light assembly unit as individual internal LEDs are typically non-serviceable.

## Safety

### Seat Belts

Safety equipment generally performs well, though rear seat belt retraction mechanisms can occasionally stick.

**Seat Belt Retraction Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Rear seat belts fail to retract smoothly after unbuckling, leaving webbing hanging loose and susceptible to door damage [nhtsa-complaint-11351566].
- **Solution:** Inspect seat belt retractors and replace faulty assemblies under warranty or service guidelines.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V084000: Electrical System:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=GMC&model=Sierra&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standards (FMVSS) number 114, "Theft Protection", and/or 208, "Occupant Crash Protection." An unbelted driver is at a greater risk of injury in a crash.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will update the radio software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 15V830000: Air Bags:Frontal:Sensor/Control Module-Inactive](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=GMC&model=Sierra&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** In the affected vehicles, the mounting stud used to secure the air bag sensing and diagnostic Module (SDM) may be incorrectly positioned, and as a result, the SDM housing could fracture. If the SDM housing fractures, water may get into SDM causing it to malfunction and hinder the proper deployment of the air bags in the event of a crash, thereby increasing.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will have the mounting stud repositioned and the SDM replaced, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2016 GMC Sierra remains a capable and comfortable full-size truck option, but buyers should conduct a thorough inspection before purchasing. Pay close attention to 8-speed transmission shift quality, brake pedal feel, steering responsiveness, and the functionality of fuel pumps and electrical accessories. Selecting a well-maintained model with a documented service history helps mitigate potential ownership surprises.
