**Introduction:**

The 1996 GMC C1500 Suburban is a full-size SUV powered by a 5.7L V8 engine that produces 250 horsepower and 345 lb-ft of torque. It's paired with a 4-speed automatic transmission. The Suburban is known for its spacious interior, comfortable ride, and strong towing capacity. However, it has several reported issues that owners have encountered.

**Engine**

* **5.7L V8**
    * **Intake manifold gasket failure:** Leaking coolant from the intake manifold, overheating, and loss of power. Solution: Replace the intake manifold gasket. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800+
    * **Vortec valve cover gasket leak:** Oil leaking from the valve covers, causing a burning oil smell and potential engine damage. Solution: Replace the valve cover gaskets. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
    * **Cylinder head gasket failure:** Loss of coolant, overheating, and white or blue smoke from the exhaust. Solution: Replace the cylinder head gaskets. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+

**Transmission**

* **4-speed automatic**
    * **Delayed shifting and slipping:** Transmission slipping, delayed engagement, and shuddering. Solution: Flush and replace the transmission fluid, inspect and repair or replace internal components as needed. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+
    * **Output speed sensor failure:** Transmission shifting issues, check engine light, and inaccurate speedometer readings. Solution: Replace the output speed sensor. Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300+

**Suspension**

* **Leaf spring failure:** Breaking or bending of the rear leaf springs, causing sagging and decreased stability. Solution: Replace the leaf springs. Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800+ per side
* **Front ball joint failure:** Wear and tear on the ball joints, resulting in loose steering, clunking noises, and potential loss of control. Solution: Replace the ball joints. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+ per side

**Electrical**

* **Battery drain:** Battery draining quickly, even when the vehicle is turned off. Solution: Inspect electrical components for shorts or parasitic drains. Repair or replace faulty components. Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the cause
* **Alternator failure:** Loss of power, dim or flickering headlights, and charging system warning light. Solution: Replace the alternator. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+

**Exterior**

* **Rust on the frame and undercarriage:** Corrosion and deterioration of the frame and undercarriage components. Solution: Rust prevention and repair. Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the severity

**Interior**

* **Dashboard cracking:** Cracks and warping of the dashboard, especially in areas exposed to sunlight. Solution: Replace or repair the dashboard. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
* **Power window failure:** Malfunctioning or inoperable power windows. Solution: Repair or replace the window motors, switches, or wiring. Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies

**Summary:**

The 1996 GMC C1500 Suburban has a number of reported issues, including some that can be severe and expensive to repair. The most common issues affect the engine, transmission, and suspension. While these issues may not affect all Suburbans, they are important to be aware of when considering purchasing or owning one of these vehicles.
