**Introduction:**

The 2003 GMC Yukon XL 2500 is a full-size SUV known for its spacious interior, powerful engine options, and rugged capabilities. It is available with two engine options: a 6.0L V8 and an 8.1L V8, both mated to a 4-speed or 6-speed automatic transmission. The Yukon XL 2500 is appreciated for its comfortable ride, towing capacity, and off-road performance. However, it has some issues that owners should be aware of.

**Engine**

**6.0L V8**

- **Excessive Oil Consumption:** Oil may be consumed at a high rate, leading to low oil levels and potential engine damage.
  - Solution: Engine rebuild or replacement
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $3,000 - $6,000+
  - Recalls: None Found

- **Head Gasket Failure:** The head gaskets can fail, causing coolant and oil leaks.
  - Solution: Head gasket replacement
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500
  - Recalls: None Found

**8.1L V8**

No issues found.

**Transmission**

**4-Speed Automatic**

- **Transmission Slipping:** Gears may slip or fail to engage, resulting in difficulty driving.
  - Solution: Transmission rebuild or replacement
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,500
  - Recalls: None Found

**6-Speed Automatic**

No issues found.

**Suspension**

- **Rear Coil Spring Breakage:** The rear coil springs may break, causing a loss of ride height and handling issues.
  - Solution: Coil spring replacement
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800
  - Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

- **Alternator Failure:** The alternator may fail, leading to a loss of electrical power and engine stalling.
  - Solution: Alternator replacement
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $600 - $1,000
  - Recalls: None Found

- **Battery Drain:** The battery may drain excessively, even when the vehicle is not in use.
  - Solution: Diagnose and repair electrical drain source
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the cause
  - Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

No issues found.

**Interior**

- **Climate Control Malfunction:** The climate control system may malfunction, causing it to fail to heat or cool the cabin.
  - Solution: Climate control module replacement
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,200
  - Recalls: None Found

- **Power Seat Failure:** The power seats may fail to adjust or move, making it difficult to find a comfortable driving position.
  - Solution: Power seat motor or switch replacement
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
  - Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

The 2003 GMC Yukon XL 2500 is a capable and spacious SUV, but it is not without its issues. The most severe problems are related to the engine, particularly oil consumption and head gasket failure, which can lead to costly repairs. The transmission may also experience slipping issues, and the suspension and electrical systems have their share of problems. However, many of these issues can be resolved with timely repairs and maintenance. The estimated cost to fix these problems can range from a few hundred dollars to several thousand dollars, depending on the severity of the issue and the specific parts that need to be replaced.
