**Introduction**

The 2004 GMC Yukon XL 2500 is a full-size SUV with seating for up to eight passengers. It is available with three engine options: a 5.3L V8, a 6.0L V8, and a 6.6L V8. The 5.3L V8 produces 295 horsepower and 335 lb-ft of torque, while the 6.0L V8 produces 320 horsepower and 360 lb-ft of torque. The 6.6L V8 produces 335 horsepower and 500 lb-ft of torque. All three engines are mated to a 4-speed automatic transmission.

The Yukon XL 2500 has a spacious interior and a comfortable ride. It is a popular choice for families and those who need a vehicle with plenty of cargo space. However, there are some common issues that owners have reported with the vehicle.

**Engine**

* **5.3L V8**
    * **Intake manifold gasket failure:** This can cause a loss of coolant and overheating. The solution is to replace the intake manifold gasket. The estimated cost to fix is $500 - $1000.
    * **Valve cover gasket leak:** This can cause an oil leak. The solution is to replace the valve cover gaskets. The estimated cost to fix is $200 - $500.
* **6.0L V8**
    * **Vortec engine lifter failure:** This can cause a loud ticking noise from the engine. The solution is to replace the lifters. The estimated cost to fix is $1000 - $2000.
    * **Oil pump failure:** This can cause a loss of oil pressure and engine damage. The solution is to replace the oil pump. The estimated cost to fix is $500 - $1000.
* **6.6L V8**
    * **No issues found**

**Transmission**

* **4-speed automatic transmission**
    * **Shifting problems:** This can cause the transmission to slip or jerk when shifting. The solution is to rebuild or replace the transmission. The estimated cost to fix is $2000 - $3000.
    * **Torque converter failure:** This can cause the transmission to slip or shudder. The solution is to replace the torque converter. The estimated cost to fix is $1000 - $2000.

**Suspension**

* **Front suspension:**
    * **Control arm failure:** This can cause the front end of the vehicle to shake or vibrate. The solution is to replace the control arms. The estimated cost to fix is $500 - $1000.
* **Rear suspension:**
    * **Leaf spring failure:** This can cause the rear end of the vehicle to sag. The solution is to replace the leaf springs. The estimated cost to fix is $400 - $800.

**Electrical**

* **Battery drain:** This can cause the battery to die prematurely. The solution is to identify and fix the source of the drain. The estimated cost to fix is $200 - $500.
* **Alternator failure:** This can cause the vehicle to lose power while driving. The solution is to replace the alternator. The estimated cost to fix is $500 - $1000.
* **Starter failure:** This can prevent the vehicle from starting. The solution is to replace the starter. The estimated cost to fix is $300 - $600.

**Exterior**

* **Tailgate latch failure:** This can cause the tailgate to open unexpectedly. The solution is to replace the tailgate latch. The estimated cost to fix is $200 - $400.
* **Headlight switch failure:** This can cause the headlights to not work. The solution is to replace the headlight switch. The estimated cost to fix is $100 - $200.
* **Door lock actuator failure:** This can prevent the doors from locking or unlocking. The solution is to replace the door lock actuator. The estimated cost to fix is $200 - $400.

**Interior**

* **Instrument cluster failure:** This can cause the gauges to not work. The solution is to replace the instrument cluster. The estimated cost to fix is $500 - $1000.
* **Climate control system failure:** This can cause the heat or air conditioning to not work. The solution is to repair or replace the climate control system. The estimated cost to fix is $200 - $500.
* **Cruise control failure:** This can prevent the cruise control from working. The solution is to replace the cruise control switch. The estimated cost to fix is $100 - $200.

**Summary**

The 2004 GMC Yukon XL 2500 is a popular SUV with a spacious interior and a comfortable ride. However, there are some common issues that owners have reported with the vehicle. The most severe and expensive issues include intake manifold gasket failure, Vortec engine lifter failure, and transmission shifting problems. Other issues, such as battery drain and alternator failure, are less severe and less expensive to fix. Overall, the Yukon XL 2500 is a reliable vehicle, but it is important to be aware of the potential issues that can occur.
