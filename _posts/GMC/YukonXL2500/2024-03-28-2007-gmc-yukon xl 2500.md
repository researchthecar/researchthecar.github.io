**Introduction:**
The 2007 GMC Yukon XL 2500 is a full-size SUV equipped with a 6.0L V8 engine (320 hp and 360 lb-ft of torque) mated to a 4-speed automatic transmission (4L60E). It provides a spacious and comfortable interior with plenty of cargo space.

**Engine**
- **6.0L V8**
    - **Active Fuel Management Failure:**
        - Description/Symptoms: The cylinders fail to deactivate while cruising, leading to poor fuel economy.
        - Solution: Replacement of the AFM lifters and camshaft
        - Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
        - Recalls: None Found
    - **Intake Manifold Gasket Leak:**
        - Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaks from the intake manifold gaskets, causing overheating and loss of power.
        - Solution: Replacing the intake manifold gaskets
        - Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,200 - $2,000+
        - Recalls: None Found
    - **Engine Knock:**
        - Description/Symptoms: Metallic knocking sound while accelerating or under load
        - Solution: Replacement of the piston rings, bearings, or camshaft
        - Estimated Cost to Fix: $5,000 - $8,000+
        - Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**
- **4-speed Automatic (4L60E)**
    - **Slippage:**
        - Description/Symptoms: Transmission fails to engage or slips gears, especially under load.
        - Solution: Overhaul or rebuild of the transmission
        - Estimated Cost to Fix: $3,000 - $5,000+
        - Recalls: None Found
    - **Overheating:**
        - Description/Symptoms: Transmission overheats and goes into limp mode.
        - Solution: Replacement of the transmission cooler or fluid flush
        - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
        - Recalls: None Found
    - **Shift Solenoid Failure:**
        - Description/Symptoms: Transmission fails to shift smoothly or stays in one gear.
        - Solution: Replacement of the shift solenoids
        - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,200+
        - Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**
- **Ball Joint Failure:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Clunking or grinding noise while turning or hitting bumps.
    - Solution: Replacement of the ball joints
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400 per joint
    - Recalls: None Found
- **Control Arm Bushing Wear:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Loose or sloppy steering, uneven tire wear.
    - Solution: Replacement of the control arm bushings
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300 per side
    - Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**
- **Alternator Failure:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Dim headlights, flickering gauges, battery drain.
    - Solution: Replacement of the alternator
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800+
    - Recalls: None Found
- **Battery Drain:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Battery goes dead prematurely.
    - Solution: Diagnostics to identify parasitic draw and repair/replacement of affected components.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    - Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**
- **Tail Light Circuit Board Failure:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Tail lights, brake lights, or turn signals not working properly.
    - Solution: Replacement of the tail light circuit board or wiring harness
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
    - Recalls: None Found
- **Grille Chrome Peeling:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Chrome plating on the grille peels or fades over time.
    - Solution: No permanent fix, can be repainted or replaced.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+ for replacement
    - Recalls: None Found

**Interior**
- **Navigation System Failure:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Navigation system malfunctions, displays incorrect information, or does not function at all.
    - Solution: Update or replacement of the navigation system module
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,200+
    - Recalls: None Found
- **Instrument Cluster Malfunction:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Gauges or warning lights don't work properly or display inaccurate information.
    - Solution: Repair or replacement of the instrument cluster
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600+
    - Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**
The 2007 GMC Yukon XL 2500 has potential issues with the engine, transmission, suspension, electrical, and interior components. Engine issues, such as AFM failure, can be costly to repair, while transmission problems can significantly impact drivability. Suspension issues can compromise handling and safety. Electrical and interior issues can be annoying but generally less expensive to fix.
