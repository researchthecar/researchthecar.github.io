**2010 GMC Yukon XL 2500**

The 2010 GMC Yukon XL 2500 comes standard with a 6.0L V8 engine producing 360 horsepower and 380 lb-ft of torque. It is also available with an optional 6.2L V8 engine producing 403 horsepower and 417 lb-ft of torque. Transmission options include a six-speed automatic and a four-speed automatic.

Overall, the GMC Yukon XL 2500 is a popular choice for those seeking a full-size SUV with ample power and towing capabilities. However, owners have reported various issues with this vehicle.

**Engine**
- 6.0L V8
  - **Excessive Oil Consumption**
    - Description/Symptoms: Engine burns excessive oil, resulting in low oil levels and potential engine damage.
    - Solution: Replace piston rings or entire engine.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $3,000 - $8,000+
    - Recalls: None Found

- 6.2L V8
  - **No Issues Found**

**Transmission**
- Six-Speed Automatic
  - **Shifting Hesitations and Delays**
    - Description/Symptoms: Transmission hesitates or delays when shifting, causing sluggish performance.
    - Solution: Update transmission control module (TCM) software.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $250+
    - Recalls: None Found

- Four-Speed Automatic
  - **Premature Failure**
    - Description/Symptoms: Transmission may fail prematurely, requiring a complete replacement.
    - Solution: Replace transmission.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $3,500 - $5,000+
    - Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**
  - **Leaking Shock Absorbers**
    - Description/Symptoms: Shock absorbers leak fluid, causing a loss of damping and potential instability while driving.
    - Solution: Replace shock absorbers.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    - Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**
  - **Battery Drain**
    - Description/Symptoms: Battery drains prematurely, leaving the vehicle unable to start.
    - Solution: Diagnose and repair or replace faulty electrical components.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    - Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**
  - **Door Handle Malfunction**
    - Description/Symptoms: Door handle fails to open the door properly.
    - Solution: Replace door handle assembly.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $250+
    - Recalls: None Found

**Interior**
  - **Rear Seat Belt Locks**
    - Description/Symptoms: Rear seat belt buckles may not latch properly, posing a safety hazard.
    - Solution: Replace faulty seat belt buckles.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
    - Recalls: NHTSA Recall Number 13V303000

**Summary**

The 2010 GMC Yukon XL 2500 has several reported issues, including engine oil consumption, transmission problems, leaking shock absorbers, electrical issues, door handle malfunctions, and rear seat belt lock failures. The severity of these issues ranges from minor annoyances to potential safety hazards. The costs to fix these issues vary depending on the nature of the problem and the required parts or labor.
