**Introduction:**
The 2019 GMC Savana 2500 is a full-size van offered with various engine and transmission options. The base engine is a 4.8L V8 that produces 296 horsepower and 308 lb-ft of torque, paired with a 6-speed automatic transmission. Higher trim levels offer a 6.0L V8 with 341 horsepower and 373 lb-ft of torque, mated to an 8-speed automatic transmission. The Savana is known for its spacious cargo area, strong towing capacity, and customizable options. However, it is also susceptible to various issues affecting its different components.

**Engine**
- **4.8L V8**
  - **Intake Manifold Failure:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Cracks in the intake manifold can lead to coolant leaks, engine overheating, and rough idle.
    - Solution: Replacement of the intake manifold.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
    - Recalls: NHTSA Campaign ID: 20V509000
  - **Knock Sensor Failure:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Faulty knock sensors can cause the engine to run poorly, knocking or pinging noises, and decreased performance.
    - Solution: Replacement of the knock sensor.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
    - Recalls: None Found

- **6.0L V8**
  - **Valve Train Noise:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Excessive noise, ticking or clattering sounds from the engine, particularly when cold.
    - Solution: Inspection and adjustment of the valve train components.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    - Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**
- **6-Speed Automatic**
  - **Rough Shifting:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Jerking or hesitation during gear changes, delayed or missed shifts.
    - Solution: Transmission fluid flush and filter replacement or repair of internal transmission components.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,500+
    - Recalls: None Found
- **8-Speed Automatic**
  - **Fluid Leaks:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Transmission fluid leaks can cause a loss of fluid, slipping gears, or complete transmission failure.
    - Solution: Repair or replacement of leaking components.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+
    - Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**
- **Rear Leaf Spring Failure:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Broken or cracked leaf springs can cause sagging or uneven suspension, reduced stability, and poor handling.
    - Solution: Replacement of the leaf springs.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800
    - Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**
- **Battery Drain:**
    - Description/Symptoms: The battery drains quickly, even when the vehicle is off.
    - Solution: Inspection and repair or replacement of faulty electrical components.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    - Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**
- **Sliding Door Malfunction:**
    - Description/Symptoms: The sliding door does not open or close properly, malfunctions, or becomes jammed.
    - Solution: Inspection and repair or replacement of door components.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
    - Recalls: None Found

**Interior**
- **Climate Control Failure:**
    - Description/Symptoms: The heating or air conditioning system does not work properly, blows hot or cold air, or makes excessive noise.
    - Solution: Inspection and repair or replacement of climate control components.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800
    - Recalls: None Found
- **Radio Malfunction:**
    - Description/Symptoms: The radio does not turn on, glitches, or displays error messages.
    - Solution: Inspection and repair or replacement of radio components.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
    - Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**
The 2019 GMC Savana 2500 is a versatile vehicle with a range of engine and transmission options. However, it is not immune to certain issues affecting its engine, transmission, suspension, electrical, exterior, and interior components. The severity of these issues varies, with some minor repairs requiring a few hundred dollars to others that could cost over $2,000. Notably, the 4.8L V8 engine has had issues with intake manifold failure and knock sensor failure, while the 8-speed automatic transmission has been reported to have fluid leaks. The Savana has also experienced problems with rear leaf spring failure, sliding door malfunctions, climate control failure, and radio malfunctions. Potential buyers and owners should be aware of these issues and consider the potential costs of repairs before purchasing or maintaining the vehicle.
