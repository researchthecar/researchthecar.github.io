**Introduction:**

The 2021 GMC Savana 2500 is a reliable, capable full-size van with a wide range of configurations. It's powered by a standard 6.6L V8 engine producing 401 horsepower and 464 lb-ft of torque, paired with a 6-speed automatic transmission. Optional engine choices include a 6.0L V8 and a 2.8L Duramax turbo-diesel engine. Overall, the Savana 2500 is known for its spacious interior, powerful engine, and comfortable ride quality. However, it has faced some issues like other vehicles.

**Engine**

* **6.6L V8 Engine**
   * **Fuel Injector Failure:**
     * Description/Symptoms: Engine misfires, loss of power, rough idle.
     * Solution: Replace faulty fuel injectors and reprogram engine control module.
     * Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,200+
     * Recalls: NHTSA Campaign Number 21V407000
   * **Intake Manifold Gasket Leak:**
     * Description/Symptoms: Coolant leak, overheating engine, decreased engine performance.
     * Solution: Replace intake manifold gasket and tighten bolts.
     * Estimated Cost to Fix: $600 - $900+
     * Recalls: None Found
* **6.0L V8 Engine**
   * **No Major Issues Found**
* **2.8L Duramax Turbo-Diesel Engine**
   * **Turbocharger Failure:**
     * Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, increased engine noise, excessive smoke.
     * Solution: Replace turbocharger and associated components.
     * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,500 - $4,000+
     * Recalls: NHTSA Campaign Number 21V356000
   * **Fuel Filter Clogging:**
     * Description/Symptoms: Engine hesitation, loss of power, increased fuel consumption.
     * Solution: Replace clogged fuel filter and bleed fuel system.
     * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
     * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

* **6-Speed Automatic Transmission**
   * **Shifting Problems:**
     * Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears, delayed gear engagement, harsh shifting.
     * Solution: Reset transmission control module, replace solenoid pack, or overhaul transmission.
     * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $3,000+
     * Recalls: None Found
* **10-Speed Automatic Transmission**
   * **No Major Issues Found**

**Suspension**

* **Front Suspension:**
   * **Control Arm Bushing Failure:**
     * Description/Symptoms: Clunking or squeaking noise when turning, uneven tire wear.
     * Solution: Replace worn control arm bushings.
     * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
     * Recalls: None Found
* **Rear Suspension:**
   * **Leaf Spring Breakage:**
     * Description/Symptoms: Sagging or tilting, rough ride, loss of stability.
     * Solution: Replace broken leaf springs.
     * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800+
     * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Battery Discharge:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Vehicle battery drains quickly, difficulty starting the engine.
    * Solution: Test and replace a faulty battery or alternator.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Electrical Short:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Blown fuses, flickering lights, malfunctioning electronics.
    * Solution: Identify and repair the source of the electrical short.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Rust:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Corrosion on body panels, frame, or undercarriage.
    * Solution: Apply rust-resistant coatings, clean and protect affected areas regularly.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the extent of damage
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Paint Peeling:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Areas of paint peeling or flaking off the body panels.
    * Solution: Sand and repaint affected areas.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Climate Control Malfunction:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Air conditioning or heating system not working properly.
    * Solution: Diagnose and repair faulty components, such as compressor, condenser, or actuators.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Seatbelt Tensioner Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Seatbelt fails to retract or lock properly.
    * Solution: Replace faulty seatbelt tensioner assembly.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
    * Recalls: NHTSA Campaign Number 22V119000

**Summary:**

The 2021 GMC Savana 2500 faces some issues, including fuel injector failure, intake manifold gasket leak, turbocharger problems, transmission shifting issues, suspension component failures, electrical problems, rust, paint peeling, climate control malfunction, and seatbelt tensioner failure. The severity and cost of these issues vary widely. Some repairs may be relatively inexpensive, while others can be costly. Owners should be aware of these potential issues and take appropriate steps to address them as needed.
