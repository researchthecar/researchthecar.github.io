**Introduction**

The 2014 GMC Savana 2500 is a full-size van offered with various engine and transmission options. It is known for its spacious interior, ample cargo capacity, and powerful engines. However, like any vehicle, it has its fair share of reported issues.

**Engine**

**6.0L V8**

* **Excessive oil consumption:** Piston rings may become worn or damaged, leading to excessive oil consumption. Symptoms include low oil level warnings, increased oil usage, and visible smoke from the exhaust. Solution: Replacing the piston rings. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
* **Engine hesitation or misfiring:** Ignition coils or spark plugs may malfunction, causing the engine to hesitate or misfire. Symptoms include rough idle, decreased performance, and check engine light illumination. Solution: Replacing the faulty ignition coils or spark plugs. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
* **Leaking oil pan gasket:** The oil pan gasket may fail, resulting in oil leaks. Symptoms include oil drips under the vehicle and low oil level warnings. Solution: Replacing the oil pan gasket. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+

**Transmission**

**6-speed Automatic**

* **Shifting problems:** The transmission may exhibit delayed or harsh shifting, slipping gears, or refusing to shift properly. Symptoms include difficulty changing gears, erratic gear engagement, and check engine light illumination. Solution: Repairing or replacing the transmission. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $4,000+

**Suspension**

* **Rear suspension creaking:** The rear suspension components, such as leaf springs or bushings, may wear out or become damaged, causing creaking noises when driving over uneven surfaces. Solution: Replacing the affected suspension components. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+

**Electrical**

* **Door lock module failure:** The door lock module may malfunction, preventing the doors from locking or unlocking properly. Symptoms include intermittent or complete door lock failure and check engine light illumination. Solution: Replacing the door lock module. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
* **Alternator failure:** The alternator may fail to provide enough power to the vehicle's electrical system. Symptoms include dimming headlights, flickering dashboard lights, and difficulty starting the vehicle. Solution: Replacing the alternator. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+

**Exterior**

* **Rear door hinge corrosion:** The rear door hinges may corrode over time, causing the doors to sag or become difficult to open or close. Solution: Replacing the corroded hinges. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+

**Interior**

* **Dashboard trim peeling:** The dashboard trim may start to peel or detach from the underlying surface. Symptoms include visible peeling or loose trim pieces. Solution: Replacing the damaged trim. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+

**Summary**

Overall, the 2014 GMC Savana 2500 has a mixed bag of issues. While some components like the engine and suspension may experience serious problems that require costly repairs, other issues like interior trim peeling or door hinge corrosion are more minor and less expensive to fix. However, it's important to note that these issues may vary depending on maintenance and driving habits.
