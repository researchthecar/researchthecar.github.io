**Introduction:**

The 1996 GMC Savana 2500 is a full-size van offered with a range of engine and transmission options. The 5.7L V8 engine produces 255 horsepower and 330 lb-ft of torque, while the 6.0L V8 generates 290 horsepower and 360 lb-ft of torque. Transmission options include a 4-speed automatic and a 5-speed manual. Owners praise the Savana 2500 for its spacious interior, powerful engine, and comfortable ride. However, some issues have been reported with the vehicle.

**Engine**

**5.7L V8**

* **Intake Manifold Gasket Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Coolant or oil leaking from the intake manifold, causing performance issues and overheating.
    * Solution: Replace the intake manifold gasket.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Head Gasket Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: White smoke from the exhaust, coolant consumption, and overheating.
    * Solution: Replace the head gaskets.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**6.0L V8**

* **Valve Guide Seal Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Excessive oil consumption, blue smoke from the exhaust, and engine knocking.
    * Solution: Replace the valve guide seals.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**4-Speed Automatic**

* **Transmission Slippage:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears, hesitation during acceleration, and shuddering.
    * Solution: Replace the transmission filter and fluid, or rebuild the transmission if necessary.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $3,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**5-Speed Manual**

* **Clutch Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears, grinding noises, and slipping.
    * Solution: Replace the clutch assembly.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Front Suspension Strut Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Noisy suspension, clunking sounds, and reduced stability.
    * Solution: Replace the front suspension struts.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Rear Suspension Leaf Spring Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Sagging rear end, uneven tire wear, and reduced handling.
    * Solution: Replace the rear suspension leaf springs.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Alternator Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Dimming lights, difficulty starting, and battery discharge.
    * Solution: Replace the alternator.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Battery Drain:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Dead battery after sitting for a short period of time.
    * Solution: Inspect the electrical system for a parasitic drain and resolve the issue.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the cause of the drain
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Rusting Body Panels:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Rust forming on the body, particularly on the doors, fenders, and rocker panels.
    * Solution: Repair or replace the affected body panels.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the severity of the rust
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Dashboard Cracking:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Cracks developing on the dashboard, especially on the passenger side.
    * Solution: Repair or replace the dashboard.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Seat Belt Buckle Jamming:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Seat belt buckle gets stuck in the locked position, preventing passengers from unbuckling.
    * Solution: Clean and lubricate the buckle or replace it if necessary.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $200+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

The 1996 GMC Savana 2500 has some reported issues that can affect its performance, reliability, and comfort. The severity and cost of the issues vary, but some, such as engine and transmission failures, can be expensive to repair. Rusting body panels and interior components may also require attention. Overall, owners should be aware of these potential issues and factor them into their ownership decision.
