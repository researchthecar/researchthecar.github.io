## Electrical

### General Electrical System
**Intermittent Power Loss**
- **Symptoms:** Dashboard lights flickering, accessories not working intermittently.
- **Solution:** Check battery terminals, wiring harness connections, and fuses. May require diagnosis of the Body Control Module (BCM).
- **Cost:** $150 - $500

**Faulty Alternator**
- **Symptoms:** Battery warning light illuminated, dimming headlights, difficulty starting.
- **Solution:** Replace the alternator.
- **Cost:** $400 - $800


## Exterior

### Body Panels and Trim
**Door Latch Actuator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Doors not locking or unlocking properly, power locks not functioning.
- **Solution:** Replace the faulty door latch actuator.
- **Cost:** $200 - $400


## Interior

### HVAC System
**Blower Motor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** No air coming from vents, or air only coming from certain vents regardless of setting.
- **Solution:** Replace the HVAC blower motor and resistor.
- **Cost:** $250 - $500


### Infotainment System (if equipped)
**Screen Freezing or Unresponsive**
- **Symptoms:** Touchscreen becomes unresponsive, system freezes during operation.
- **Solution:** Software update or replacement of the infotainment unit.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1500

### Summary Verdict
The 2018 GMC Savana 2500 demonstrates strong reliability in its core powertrain and chassis components, with no widespread issues reported for its V8 engines or automatic transmissions. The primary areas where owners might encounter technical problems are within the electrical system, specifically related to intermittent power issues or alternator failures. Exterior concerns are minimal, with occasional door latch actuator issues being noted. The interior can sometimes present HVAC blower motor problems or, if equipped, infotainment system glitches. Overall, the Savana 2500 is a dependable vehicle for its intended purpose, with most potential issues being repairable and not indicative of systemic design flaws.
