The GMC Savana 2500 is a full-size van offered in a variety of configurations, including cutaway, cargo, and passenger versions. It is powered by a choice of 4.3L V6, 6.0L V8, and 3.6L V6 engines, paired with either a 6-speed or 10-speed automatic transmission. The Savana 2500 is known for its spacious interior, powerful engines, and towing and payload capacities. However, it has also been plagued by several issues.

**Engine**

**4.3L V6**
* **Intake manifold gasket failure:** Description/Symptoms: Coolant and/or oil leak from the intake manifold. Causes overheating and engine damage. Solution: Replace intake manifold gasket. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
* **Valve train noise:** Description/Symptoms: Tapping or ticking noise from the engine. Caused by worn cam lobes or lifters. Solution: Replace camshaft and/or lifters. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,000+
* **Crankshaft position sensor failure:** Description/Symptoms: Engine stalling, difficulty starting, or reduced performance. Caused by a faulty crankshaft position sensor. Solution: Replace crankshaft position sensor. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
* **Recalls:** NHTSA Campaign Number: 21V742
    * **Issue:** Engine compartment fire due to fuel line contact with engine heat shield.
    * **Solution:** Install protective heat shield over fuel line.

**6.0L V8**
* **Oil pump failure:** Description/Symptoms: Low oil pressure, engine knocking, or engine seizure. Caused by a failed oil pump. Solution: Replace oil pump. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $1,500+
* **Water pump failure:** Description/Symptoms: Coolant leak, overheating, or engine damage. Caused by a failed water pump. Solution: Replace water pump. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
* **Fuel injector failure:** Description/Symptoms: Engine misfires, rough idle, or stalling. Caused by a clogged or faulty fuel injector. Solution: Replace fuel injector. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500 per injector
* **Recalls:** None Found

**Transmission**

**6-speed Automatic**
* **Torque converter failure:** Description/Symptoms: Transmission slipping, shuddering, or noise. Caused by a worn or failed torque converter. Solution: Replace torque converter. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
* **Transmission control module (TCM) failure:** Description/Symptoms: Erratic shifting, transmission not shifting, or check engine light. Caused by a faulty TCM. Solution: Replace TCM. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000

**10-speed Automatic**
* **Shift solenoid failure:** Description/Symptoms: Transmission not shifting properly, slipping, or shuddering. Caused by a faulty shift solenoid. Solution: Replace shift solenoid. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
* **Recalls:** None Found

**Suspension**
* **Front ball joint failure:** Description/Symptoms: Noise, vibration, or steering instability. Caused by a worn or failed ball joint. Solution: Replace ball joint. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
* **Rear leaf spring failure:** Description/Symptoms: Sagging rear end, uneven tire wear, or handling problems. Caused by a broken or weakened leaf spring. Solution: Replace leaf spring. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
* **Shock absorber failure:** Description/Symptoms: Rough ride, poor handling, or excessive bounce. Caused by worn or damaged shock absorbers. Solution: Replace shock absorbers. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+

**Electrical**
* **Battery drain:** Description/Symptoms: Battery discharging quickly, even when vehicle is off. Caused by a parasitic draw from electrical components. Solution: Identify and fix the source of the drain. Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the issue
* **Alternator failure:** Description/Symptoms: Dimming headlights, difficulty starting, or battery warning light on. Caused by a failed alternator. Solution: Replace alternator. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
* **Starter failure:** Description/Symptoms: Engine not cranking or difficult starting. Caused by a failed starter. Solution: Replace starter. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800+
* **Recalls:** None Found

**Exterior**
* **Door handle failure:** Description/Symptoms: Door handle breaks or falls off. Caused by a faulty or weak handle. Solution: Replace door handle. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
* **Window regulator failure:** Description/Symptoms: Window does not open or close properly. Caused by a broken or damaged window regulator. Solution: Replace window regulator. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+

**Interior**
* **Dashboard cracking:** Description/Symptoms: Cracks or warping of the dashboard. Caused by exposure to heat or UV rays. Solution: Replace dashboard. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
* **Seat belt retractor failure:** Description/Symptoms: Seat belt does not retract or lock properly. Caused by a faulty retractor. Solution: Replace seat belt retractor. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
* **Air conditioning failure:** Description/Symptoms: A/C not blowing cold air. Caused by a refrigerant leak, compressor failure, or other issue. Solution: Diagnose and repair the A/C system. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+

**Summary**

The GMC Savana 2500 has a number of reported issues across various components, including the engine, transmission, suspension, electrical system, and interior. While some of the issues are minor and relatively inexpensive to fix, others can be more severe and costly. It is important for owners to be aware of these potential problems and to address them promptly to avoid further damage or safety concerns.
