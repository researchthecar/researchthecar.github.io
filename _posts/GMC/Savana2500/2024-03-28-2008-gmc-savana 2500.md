**Introduction:**

The 2008 GMC Savana 2500 is a full-size van available with a choice of two gasoline engines and a 6-speed automatic transmission. The 4.8L V8 produces 285 horsepower and 290 lb-ft of torque, while the 6.0L V8 generates 323 horsepower and 373 lb-ft of torque. The Savana is known for its spacious cabin, versatile cargo area, and strong towing capabilities. However, certain issues have been reported with the vehicle.

**Engine**

**4.8L V8**

* **Intake Manifold Gasket Failure:** Description/Symptoms: Coolant or oil leaking from the intake manifold, rough idle, decreased power. Solution: Replace the intake manifold gasket. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
* **Valve Cover Bolt Thread Stripping:** Description/Symptoms: Oil leaking from the valve covers, ticking noise. Solution: Retap or replace the valve cover bolts. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
* **Timing Chain Guide Failure:** Description/Symptoms: Noisy engine, loss of power. Solution: Replace the timing chain and guides. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,000+

**6.0L V8**

* **Spark Plug Replacement Difficulty:** Description/Symptoms: Difficult access to the spark plugs due to tight engine bay. Solution: Use specialized tools or engine removal. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
* **Oil Consumption:** Description/Symptoms: Excessive oil consumption, requiring frequent top-ups. Solution: Replace piston rings or valve seals. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+

**Transmission**

* **6-Speed Automatic:** Description/Symptoms: Hesitation shifting, slipping, shuddering. Solution: Repair or rebuild the transmission. Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,000+

**Suspension**

* **Front Suspension Strut Failure:** Description/Symptoms: Abnormal noises, poor ride quality, reduced handling. Solution: Replace the struts. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
* **Rear Leaf Spring Axle Wrap:** Description/Symptoms: Binding and squeaking from the rear suspension. Solution: Install axle wrap eliminators or replace the leaf springs. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+

**Electrical**

* **Alternator Failure:** Description/Symptoms: Dim lights, electrical system malfunctions. Solution: Replace the alternator. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
* **Battery Drain:** Description/Symptoms: Battery dies prematurely. Solution: Inspect and repair electrical system components or replace the battery. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+

**Exterior**

* **Door Handle Breakage:** Description/Symptoms: Door handles break off, rendering doors inoperable. Solution: Replace the door handles. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+

**Interior**

* **Dashboard Cracking:** Description/Symptoms: Cracks develop on the dashboard. Solution: Replacement of the dashboard. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
* **HVAC Controls Malfunction:** Description/Symptoms: Heating, ventilation, or air conditioning controls stop functioning. Solution: Replace the climate control module or individual components. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+

**Summary:**

Overall, the 2008 GMC Savana 2500 has a number of potential issues to be aware of, particularly with the engine and transmission. The cost of repairs can be significant, with some issues exceeding $2,000 to fix. While the Savana offers spaciousness and versatility, these issues may be a concern for potential buyers.
