**Introduction:**

The 2003 GMC Savana 2500 is a full-size van with a powerful 6.0L V8 engine that produces 300 horsepower and 360 lb-ft of torque. It comes standard with a 4-speed automatic transmission, but a 6-speed manual transmission is also available. The Savana is praised for its spacious interior, strong performance, and towing capabilities. However, it has received criticism for its fuel inefficiency and stiff ride.

**Engine**

**6.0L V8 Engine**

* **Intake manifold gasket leaks:** This can cause coolant leaks and engine overheating. The intake manifold gaskets must be replaced.
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000

**Transmission**

**4-speed Automatic Transmission**

* **Shifting problems:** The transmission may have difficulty shifting gears or may slip out of gear. The transmission may need to be rebuilt or replaced.
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000

**Suspension**

* **Front suspension noise:** The front suspension may make noise over bumps or rough roads. The suspension components may need to be inspected and replaced if necessary.
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500

**Electrical**

* **Battery drain:** The battery may drain quickly, especially when the vehicle is parked. The electrical system may need to be inspected for parasitic draw.
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500

**Exterior**

* **Rust on body panels:** The body panels may develop rust, especially around the wheel wells and rocker panels. The affected areas may need to be repaired or replaced.
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+

**Interior**

* **HVAC problems:** The heating, ventilation, and air conditioning system may not work properly. The HVAC components may need to be inspected and replaced if necessary.
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500

**Summary:**

Overall, the 2003 GMC Savana 2500 has received mixed reviews. While it offers a spacious interior, strong performance, and towing capabilities, it is also known for its fuel inefficiency, stiff ride, and some mechanical issues. Some of the more serious issues, such as intake manifold gasket leaks and transmission problems, can be costly to repair. However, many of the other issues are relatively minor and can be addressed with routine maintenance and repairs.
