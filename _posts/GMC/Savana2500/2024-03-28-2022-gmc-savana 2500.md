**Introduction**
The 2022 GMC Savana 2500 is a versatile full-size van that offers a spacious interior, robust performance, and a variety of customizable options. It is available with two engine and transmission options: a 6.6L V8 gasoline engine paired with a 6-speed automatic transmission and a 2.8L Duramax turbo-diesel engine paired with a 10-speed automatic transmission. The Savana 2500 has received praise for its towing and cargo capabilities, as well as its optional all-wheel drive system. However, like any vehicle, it has its share of reported issues.

**Engine**

**6.6L V8 Gasoline Engine**
* **Intake manifold gasket failure:** The intake manifold gasket may fail, causing coolant or oil leaks and a drop in engine performance. The gasket will need to be replaced. $500 - $1000
* **Fuel injector failure:** The fuel injectors may fail, causing difficulty starting the engine, rough idle, and reduced power. The fuel injectors will need to be replaced. $200 - $500 per injector
* **Knock sensor failure:** The knock sensor may fail, causing the engine to run poorly and potentially damage the engine. The knock sensor will need to be replaced. $200 - $500

**2.8L Duramax Turbo-Diesel Engine**
* **Turbocharger failure:** The turbocharger may fail, causing a loss of power and decreased fuel efficiency. The turbocharger will need to be replaced. $2000 - $3000
* **Fuel injector failure:** Similar to the gasoline engine, the fuel injectors may fail, causing difficulty starting the engine, rough idle, and reduced power. The fuel injectors will need to be replaced. $200 - $500 per injector

**Transmission**

**6-speed Automatic Transmission**
* **Transmission slipping:** The transmission may slip while shifting gears, causing a loss of power and difficulty accelerating. The transmission may need to be rebuilt or replaced. $1500 - $3000

**10-speed Automatic Transmission**
* **No issues found**

**Suspension**

* **Front suspension strut failure:** The front suspension struts may fail, causing a rough ride, uneven tire wear, and difficulty steering. The struts will need to be replaced. $500 - $1000 per strut
* **Rear leaf spring breakage:** The rear leaf springs may break, causing the vehicle to sag in the rear and affect handling. The leaf springs will need to be replaced. $500 - $1000 per leaf spring

**Electrical**

* **Battery drain:** The battery may drain quickly, even when the vehicle is not in use. The electrical system will need to be inspected for any parasitic draws. $100 - $500
* **Alternator failure:** The alternator may fail, causing the battery to not charge and the vehicle to lose power. The alternator will need to be replaced. $500 - $1000
* **Starter failure:** The starter may fail, preventing the engine from starting. The starter will need to be replaced. $300 - $600

**Exterior**

* **Rear door latch failure:** The rear door latch may fail, causing the door to open unexpectedly or not open at all. The latch will need to be replaced. $200 - $500

**Interior**

* **Heater core failure:** The heater core may fail, causing a lack of heat in the cabin. The heater core will need to be replaced. $500 - $1000
* **Air conditioning compressor failure:** The air conditioning compressor may fail, causing a lack of cold air in the cabin. The compressor will need to be replaced. $1000 - $1500

**Summary**
The 2022 GMC Savana 2500 is a reliable and versatile vehicle, but it is not without its reported issues. Engine issues, such as intake manifold gasket failure and fuel injector failure, are the most commonly reported concerns. Transmission issues, while present, are less frequent. Suspension and electrical problems can also occur, although their severity varies. Interior issues are primarily related to HVAC components. The estimated cost to fix the issues can range from a few hundred dollars to several thousand, depending on the severity of the problem. Overall, while the Savana 2500 is a capable vehicle, it is important to be aware of these potential issues before making a purchase decision.
