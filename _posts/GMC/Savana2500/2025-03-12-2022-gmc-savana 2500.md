The 2022 GMC Savana 2500 is a full-size van known for its utilitarian nature and suitability for commercial use. Engine options include a 4.3L V6 producing 276 horsepower and 298 lb-ft of torque and a 6.6L V8 producing 401 horsepower and 464 lb-ft of torque. Both engines are paired with a heavy-duty 6-speed automatic transmission. Owners generally appreciate the Savana's robust build and cargo capacity, while common dislikes include its outdated design, fuel economy, and relatively basic interior features.

**Transmission**

**6-Speed Automatic**

*   **Torque Converter Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Shuddering or vibrations, especially during light acceleration or when maintaining a constant speed. Can also present as rough shifting or difficulty engaging gears.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the torque converter with an updated design may resolve the issue. Additionally, performing a transmission flush using the correct fluid type can sometimes alleviate symptoms.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Leaf Spring Squeaking**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Noticeable squeaking or groaning noises emanating from the rear suspension, especially when driving over bumps or during turning maneuvers. This is often caused by friction between the leaf springs.
    *   Solution: Applying a lithium-based grease or specialized leaf spring lubricant between the leaf springs can temporarily eliminate the noise. In some cases, replacing the leaf spring bushings can provide a more permanent solution.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $200+ for lubrication; $300 - $600+ for bushing replacement.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Battery Drain**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Premature battery failure or difficulty starting the vehicle, particularly after it has been sitting for an extended period. This can be caused by parasitic draw from faulty electrical components or accessories.
    *   Solution: Diagnose the source of the parasitic draw using a multimeter and electrical system testing procedures. Repair or replace the faulty component causing the drain.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+ depending on the source of the drain.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Infotainment System Freezing/Crashing**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The infotainment screen freezes, becomes unresponsive, or restarts unexpectedly. This can disrupt navigation, audio, and other vehicle functions.
    *   Solution: Attempt a system reset by holding the power button. If the issue persists, software updates from the dealership may be required. In severe cases, the infotainment unit may need to be replaced.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 - $50 for a software update; $500 - $1500+ for replacement.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Side Door Sticking/Difficult to Open**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Difficulty opening or closing the side cargo doors. This can be due to misaligned hinges, worn rollers, or damaged latches.
    *   Solution: Adjust or replace the door hinges and rollers. Lubricate the latching mechanism. In some cases, the door may need to be realigned professionally.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $300+ depending on the extent of the repairs.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **No Issues Found**

**Summary**

The 2022 GMC Savana 2500 generally experiences a mix of minor and moderate issues. The torque converter and electrical issues can be more costly to address. The leaf spring squeaking and door issues tend to be less severe and less expensive to fix. While the Savana 2500 is a solid work van, potential buyers should be aware of these common problems and inspect the vehicle carefully before purchasing.
