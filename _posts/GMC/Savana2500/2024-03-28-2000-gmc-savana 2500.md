**Introduction:**

The 2000 GMC Savana 2500 is a full-size van available with several engine and transmission options. The 5.7-liter V8 engine produces 255 horsepower and 330 lb-ft of torque. The 6.0-liter V8 engine generates 300 horsepower and 355 lb-ft of torque. Both engines come with a 4-speed automatic transmission. The Savana is praised for its spacious cabin, strong engines, and versatility. However, it is criticized for its dated interior, poor fuel economy, and cumbersome handling.

**Engine**

**5.7-Liter V8**
- **Valve Cover Gasket Leak:** Oil leaks from the valve cover gaskets, causing a burning oil smell and possible engine damage.
    - Solution: Replace valve cover gaskets.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400

**6.0-Liter V8**
- **Intake Manifold Leak:** The intake manifold gasket fails, leading to vacuum leaks and reduced engine performance.
    - Solution: Replace intake manifold gasket.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500

**Transmission**
- **Automatic Transmission Slipping:** The transmission slips or hesitates when shifting, causing rough gear changes.
    - Solution: Replace or rebuild transmission.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500

**Suspension**
- **Front Struts Wear Out Prematurely:** The front struts lose their damping ability prematurely, resulting in a bouncy ride and reduced handling.
    - Solution: Replace front struts.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $600 - $800

**Electrical**
- **Alternator Failure:** The alternator fails, leading to loss of electrical power and possible battery damage.
    - Solution: Replace alternator.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500
- **Starter Motor Failure:** The starter motor fails, preventing the engine from starting.
    - Solution: Replace starter motor.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $300

**Exterior**
- **Tailgate Corrosion:** The tailgate rusts prematurely, affecting its appearance and functionality.
    - Solution: Replace tailgate or have it repaired.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000

**Interior**
- **Dashboard Cracking:** The dashboard can develop cracks over time, degrading its appearance.
    - Solution: Replace or reupholster dashboard.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
- **Cup Holder Breakage:** The cup holders in the center console can break easily, rendering them unusable.
    - Solution: Replace cup holders.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $100

**Summary**

The 2000 GMC Savana 2500 has a range of issues, including engine leaks, transmission problems, suspension wear, and electrical failures. While some issues are minor and inexpensive to fix, others are more severe and costly. The overall severity and cost of the issues vary depending on the specific problem.
