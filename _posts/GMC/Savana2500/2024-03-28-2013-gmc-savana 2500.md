**Introduction:**

The 2013 GMC Savana 2500 is a full-size van that offers a range of powertrain options. The base engine is a 4.8L V8 with 280 horsepower and 296 lb-ft of torque, while a 6.6L gasoline V8 making 403 horsepower and 460 lb-ft of torque is available as an upgrade. For diesel enthusiasts, a 6.6L Duramax turbo-diesel V8 delivering 397 horsepower and 765 lb-ft of torque is also available. Transmission options include a 6-speed automatic or a 6-speed manual. The Savana 2500 is known for its spacious cargo and passenger capacity, as well as its robust towing and hauling capabilities. However, it has also been plagued by several issues.

**Engine**

**4.8L V8**

* **Intake Manifold Gasket Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaks, rough idling, decreased engine power.
    * Solution: Replace intake manifold gasket.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+.
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Timing Chain Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Rattling noises during engine startup, inconsistent engine performance.
    * Solution: Replace timing chain and related components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+.
    * Recalls: None Found

**6.6L Gasoline V8**

* **Fuel Injector Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Engine misfires, reduced fuel economy, rough idling.
    * Solution: Replace fuel injectors.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+ per injector.
    * Recalls: 13V219000, 14V324000

* **Piston Ring Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Excessive oil consumption, blue smoke from exhaust, loss of engine power.
    * Solution: Engine rebuild or replacement.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $4,000 - $8,000+.
    * Recalls: None Found

**6.6L Duramax Turbo-Diesel V8**

* **EGR Valve Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Reduced engine power, increased emissions, rough idling.
    * Solution: Replace EGR valve.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+.
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Injection Pump Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Engine stalls, difficulty starting, loss of power.
    * Solution: Replace injection pump.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+.
    * Recalls: 14V325000

**Transmission**

**6-Speed Automatic**

* **Torque Converter Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Shuddering during acceleration, delayed engagement, slipping gears.
    * Solution: Replace torque converter.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+.
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Transmission Control Module Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Erratic shifting, transmission slipping out of gear, refusal to shift.
    * Solution: Replace transmission control module.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+.
    * Recalls: None Found

**6-Speed Manual**

* **Clutch Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Difficulty engaging gears, slipping gears, grinding noises.
    * Solution: Replace clutch assembly.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+.
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Transmission Mount Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Excessive vibration, noise from transmission area.
    * Solution: Replace transmission mounts.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+.
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Front Suspension Control Arm Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Noises from suspension, premature tire wear, misalignment.
    * Solution: Replace control arms.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+.
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Rear Suspension Leaf Spring Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Sagging rear end, rough ride, decreased load capacity.
    * Solution: Replace leaf springs.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+.
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Battery loses charge quickly, vehicle fails to start.
    * Solution: Identify and repair electrical component causing drain.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on cause.
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Alternator Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Dimming lights, battery warning light, difficulty starting.
    * Solution: Replace alternator.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+.
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Tailgate Latch Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Tailgate will not close or open properly.
    * Solution: Replace tailgate latch mechanism.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+.
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Side Door Hinge Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Side door will not open or close smoothly, door sags.
    * Solution: Replace side door hinges.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+.
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Instrument Cluster Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Malfunctioning gauges, warning lights not functioning.
    * Solution: Replace instrument cluster.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+.
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Climate Control System Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Inconsistent temperature control, blower motor not working.
    * Solution: Diagnose and repair climate control system components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the issue.
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

The 2013 GMC Savana 2500 is a versatile vehicle with impressive towing and hauling capabilities. However, it is plagued by a range of issues that can be costly to fix. Engine problems, such as intake manifold gasket failure and timing chain failure, are particularly concerning. Transmission issues, including torque converter failure and transmission control module failure, can also be debilitating. Suspension components, such as control arms and leaf springs, are prone to failure, leading to poor handling and decreased load capacity. While electrical and exterior issues are generally less severe, they can still be inconvenient and costly to resolve. Overall, the 2013 GMC Savana 2500 has a mix of major and minor issues that can significantly impact its reliability and value.
