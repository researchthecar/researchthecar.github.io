**Introduction to the 1998 GMC Savana 2500:**

The 1998 GMC Savana 2500 is a full-size van offered in various configurations, including cargo, passenger, and cutaway. It came with a range of engine and transmission options, including:

* 4.3L V6 with 190 horsepower and 250 lb-ft of torque
* 5.0L V8 with 230 horsepower and 285 lb-ft of torque
* 5.7L V8 with 255 horsepower and 330 lb-ft of torque
* 6.0L V8 with 300 horsepower and 360 lb-ft of torque
* 4-speed automatic transmission
* 5-speed automatic transmission

The Savana 2500 was well-regarded for its spacious interior, powerful engines, and cargo-carrying capacity. However, it also had its share of reported issues.

**Engine**

**4.3L V6**

* **Intake manifold gasket failure:** This can cause coolant and oil leaks, as well as overheating. Solution: Replace intake manifold gasket. Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800+
* **Cylinder head gasket failure:** This can lead to coolant and oil mixing, resulting in engine damage. Solution: Replace cylinder head gasket. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+

**5.0L V8**

* **Intake manifold gasket failure:** This can cause vacuum leaks and performance issues. Solution: Replace intake manifold gasket. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
* **Camshaft position sensor failure:** This can cause engine stalling and rough idling. Solution: Replace camshaft position sensor. Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300+

**5.7L V8**

* **Valve lifter failure:** This can cause noisy valve operation and reduced engine performance. Solution: Replace valve lifters. Estimated Cost to Fix: $600 - $1,200+
* **Spark plug blowout:** This can result in engine misfires and decreased power. Solution: Replace spark plugs and repair or replace cylinder head. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,000+

**6.0L V8**

* **No significant issues found**

**Transmission**

**4-speed Automatic**

* **Transmission seal failure:** This can cause fluid leaks and transmission issues. Solution: Replace transmission seals. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
* **Valve body failure:** This can cause shifting problems and transmission slipping. Solution: Replace valve body. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,200+

**5-speed Automatic**

* **No significant issues found**

**Suspension**

* **Ball joint failure:** This can cause excessive play in the steering and suspension, as well as premature tire wear. Solution: Replace ball joints. Estimated Cost to Fix: $250 - $500+ per wheel
* **Tie rod end failure:** This can cause steering wander and instability. Solution: Replace tie rod ends. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $250+ per wheel

**Electrical**

* **Alternator failure:** This can cause a loss of charging power and battery drain. Solution: Replace alternator. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
* **Starter failure:** This can prevent the engine from starting. Solution: Replace starter motor. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+

**Exterior**

* **Rust on undercarriage:** This can be a concern in areas with harsh winter conditions. Solution: Rust treatment or undercoating. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
* **Door lock actuator failure:** This can make it difficult to lock or unlock the doors. Solution: Replace door lock actuator. Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300+ per door

**Interior**

* **Dashboard cracking:** This is a common issue caused by sun exposure. Solution: Replace dashboard. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
* **HVAC blower motor failure:** This can cause a loss of heating or air conditioning. Solution: Replace blower motor. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
* **Seat adjustment failure:** This can make it difficult to adjust the seats. Solution: Repair or replace seat adjustment mechanism. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+

**Summary:**

The 1998 GMC Savana 2500 had a range of reported issues, with some being more severe and costly than others. The most common issues include intake manifold gasket failure, transmission seal failure, and ball joint failure. The severity of these issues and the estimated cost to fix them can vary depending on the specific part and labor costs in your area. Overall, the Savana 2500 could be a reliable workhorse with proper maintenance and timely repairs.
