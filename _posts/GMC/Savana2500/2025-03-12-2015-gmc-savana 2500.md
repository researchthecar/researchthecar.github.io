The 2015 GMC Savana 2500 is a full-size cargo van known for its ruggedness and utility. It was often used for commercial purposes, offering ample cargo space and towing capacity. Engine options include a 4.8L V8 (280 hp, 295 lb-ft of torque), a 6.0L V8 (342 hp, 373 lb-ft of torque), and a 6.6L Duramax Diesel V8 (260 hp, 525 lb-ft of torque). Transmission options were generally a 6-speed automatic. Owners appreciated the van's reliability and spaciousness, but often cited its outdated design and poor fuel economy as drawbacks.

**Engine**

**4.8L V8**
* **No Issues Found**

**6.0L V8**
* **Excessive Oil Consumption**
    * Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported excessive oil consumption, requiring frequent oil top-offs between scheduled oil changes. Symptoms include the need to add oil more frequently and potential blue smoke from the exhaust.
    * Solution: The repair could be a valve cover replacement, valve seal replacement, or piston ring replacement.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $3000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**6.6L Duramax Diesel V8**
* **Injector Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Fuel injector failure, causing rough running, misfires, and potentially black smoke from the exhaust. Can also cause decreased fuel economy.
    * Solution: Replacement of faulty fuel injector(s). Proper diagnosis to identify the specific injector(s) is crucial.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1000+ per injector
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**6-Speed Automatic (All Engines)**
* **Harsh Shifting/Slipping**
    * Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported harsh shifting, slipping between gears, or delayed engagement. This can manifest as clunking noises during gear changes.
    * Solution: The repair is a full transmission rebuild or replacement.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $3000 - $5000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Leaf Spring Sagging/Breaking**
    * Description/Symptoms: Rear leaf springs sagging prematurely, leading to reduced load-carrying capacity and a "bottomed-out" feeling when loaded. Some springs may even break, especially under heavy use.
    * Solution: Replacement of the leaf spring assembly. Consider upgrading to heavier-duty springs if the vehicle is frequently used for hauling heavy loads.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1000+ per side
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: Premature battery drain, especially if the vehicle is not driven frequently. This can be caused by parasitic draws from various electrical components.
    * Solution: Diagnosing the source of the parasitic draw and repairing or replacing the faulty component. This can involve testing various circuits and modules.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Power Door Lock Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Intermittent or complete failure of the power door locks. This is often due to faulty actuators or wiring issues.
    * Solution: Replacing the faulty door lock actuator or repairing the wiring harness.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300+ per door
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Sliding Door Latch Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The sliding door latch fails, making it difficult or impossible to open or close the sliding door properly.
    * Solution: Replacing the faulty sliding door latch.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **HVAC Blower Motor Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The HVAC blower motor stops working, resulting in no airflow from the vents. This can affect both heating and air conditioning.
    * Solution: Replacing the faulty blower motor.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

The 2015 GMC Savana 2500 can be a reliable workhorse, but potential buyers should be aware of some common issues. Engine problems like excessive oil consumption in the 6.0L V8 and injector issues in the 6.6L Duramax Diesel V8 can be costly to repair. The transmission can also be a weak point, with reports of harsh shifting. Suspension and electrical issues also surface. The severity and cost of these issues vary, but being proactive with maintenance and inspection can help mitigate potential problems.
