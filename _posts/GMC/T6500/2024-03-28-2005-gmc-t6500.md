**Introduction**

The 2005 GMC T6500 is a heavy-duty commercial truck offered with multiple engine and transmission options. It is powered by a 350-hp, 6.6-liter Duramax diesel engine and a 6-speed Allison automatic transmission. The T6500 is known for its durability, payload capacity, and towing capabilities. However, like any vehicle, it has its share of issues.

**Engine**

**6.6L Duramax Diesel**

* **Injector Failure:** Premature failure of the fuel injectors can lead to engine misfires, rough idling, and a loss of power. **Solution:** Replace the faulty injectors. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $1,000 - $2,000+**
* **Turbocharger Failure:** The turbocharger can fail due to oil starvation or excessive wear. Symptoms include a loss of power, black smoke from the exhaust, and a whining noise. **Solution:** Replace the turbocharger. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $2,000 - $3,000+**

**Transmission**

**6-Speed Allison Automatic**

* **Transmission Slippage:** The transmission may slip or shudder when shifting, especially under heavy loads. **Solution:** Repair or replace the transmission. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $1,500 - $3,000+**
* **Torque Converter Failure:** The torque converter can fail, causing a loss of power and overheating. **Solution:** Replace the torque converter. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $1,000 - $2,000+**

**Suspension**

* **Rear Axle Bearing Failure:** The rear axle bearings can fail prematurely, leading to a grinding noise, vibration, and a loss of control. **Solution:** Replace the faulty bearings. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $500 - $1,000+**
* **Leaf Spring Breakage:** The leaf springs can break, causing a sagging ride height and a decrease in handling. **Solution:** Replace the broken leaf springs. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $200 - $500+**

**Electrical**

* **Alternator Failure:** The alternator can fail, causing the battery to discharge and the vehicle to lose power. **Solution:** Replace the alternator. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $300 - $500**
* **Battery Drain:** The battery can drain prematurely due to a faulty alternator or a parasitic drain. **Solution:** Diagnose and repair the underlying cause of the drain. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $100 - $500+**

**Exterior**

* **Tailgate Latch Failure:** The tailgate latch can fail, allowing the tailgate to open unexpectedly. **Solution:** Replace the faulty latch. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $200 - $400**

**Interior**

* **Dashboard Noise:** The dashboard can rattle or creak, especially over rough terrain. **Solution:** Apply sound-dampening material or tighten loose components. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $100 - $300**
* **HVAC System Malfunction:** The heating, ventilation, and air conditioning (HVAC) system may fail to heat or cool properly. **Solution:** Diagnose and repair the faulty component, such as the blower motor or compressor. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $500 - $1,500+**

**Summary**

Overall, the 2005 GMC T6500 has a mixed reputation for reliability. While it is a durable and capable truck, it is not immune to issues. The most common problems involve the engine, transmission, and electrical system. The severity of these issues can vary, with some being relatively minor and inexpensive to fix, while others can be major and require a significant investment. It is important to factor in the potential costs of maintenance and repairs when considering the purchase of a used T6500.
