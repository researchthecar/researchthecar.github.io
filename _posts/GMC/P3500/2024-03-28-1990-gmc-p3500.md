**Introduction:**

The 1990 GMC P3500 is a heavy-duty pickup truck that offers both power and versatility. It is powered by a 7.4L V8 gasoline engine, producing 230 horsepower and 360 lb-ft of torque. Paired with this engine is a 4-speed automatic transmission. The P3500 boasts a payload capacity of over 6,000 pounds and a towing capacity of up to 12,000 pounds. Owners appreciate its rugged construction, reliable performance, and spacious interior. However, like any vehicle, the P3500 has encountered some reported issues over the years.

**Engine**
7.4L V8 Gasoline Engine

**Intake Manifold Gasket Failure**
Description/Symptoms: Leaking coolant, overheating engine
Solution: Replace intake manifold gasket
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**
4-Speed Automatic Transmission

**Slipping Gears**
Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting, transmission slipping out of gear
Solution: Overhaul or replace transmission
Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

**Front Ball Joint Wear**
Description/Symptoms: Clunking noises, excessive tire wear
Solution: Replace ball joints
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400 per side
Recalls: None Found

**Leaf Spring Fatigue**
Description/Symptoms: Sagging rear end, rough ride
Solution: Replace leaf springs
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000 per axle
Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

**Alternator Failure**
Description/Symptoms: Difficulty starting, dim headlights
Solution: Replace alternator
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

**Tailgate Hinge Rust**
Description/Symptoms: Rusted hinges, difficulty opening tailgate
Solution: Replace hinges
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

**Cracked Dashboard**
Description/Symptoms: Cracks on dashboard surface
Solution: Replace dashboard
Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800+
Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

The 1990 GMC P3500 is a generally reliable truck, but it has experienced some issues that can be costly to repair. The most common issues affect the engine, transmission, and suspension. The average cost of repairs can range from $500 to $3,000 or more, depending on the severity of the issue. It is important to note that these issues may not affect all P3500s, and many owners have reported years of trouble-free ownership.
