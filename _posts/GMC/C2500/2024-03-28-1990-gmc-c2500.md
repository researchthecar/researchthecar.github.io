**Introduction:**

The 1990 GMC C2500 is a heavy-duty pickup truck that was available with a variety of engine and transmission options. Popular engine options included the 5.7L V8, 7.4L V8, and 8.1L V8 engines. Transmission options included a 4-speed automatic transmission and a 5-speed manual transmission. The C2500 was known for its durability and towing capacity, but it also had its fair share of issues.

**Engine**

**5.7L V8 Engine**

* **Intake Manifold Gasket Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: A leak in the intake manifold gasket can cause a rough idle, loss of power, and increased emissions.
    * Solution: Replace the intake manifold gasket.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Distributor Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: A failing distributor can cause a loss of spark, which will result in the engine not starting.
    * Solution: Replace the distributor.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
    * Recalls: None Found

**7.4L V8 Engine**

* **Oil Pan Gasket Leak:**
    * Description/Symptoms: A leaking oil pan gasket can cause a loss of oil, which can damage the engine if not addressed.
    * Solution: Replace the oil pan gasket.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Water Pump Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: A failing water pump can cause the engine to overheat.
    * Solution: Replace the water pump.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $250 - $400+
    * Recalls: None Found

**8.1L V8 Engine**

* **Head Gasket Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: A blown head gasket can cause a loss of coolant, overheating, and a rough idle.
    * Solution: Replace the head gasket.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Injector Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: A failing injector can cause a loss of power, rough idle, and increased emissions.
    * Solution: Replace the injector.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $250+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**4-Speed Automatic Transmission**

* **Slipping Transmission:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The transmission may slip or shudder when shifting gears.
    * Solution: Repair or replace the transmission.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**5-Speed Manual Transmission**

* **Grinding Gears:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The transmission may grind when shifting gears.
    * Solution: Replace the clutch or transmission.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Front End Shimmy:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The front end of the truck may shimmy at highway speeds.
    * Solution: Inspect and replace worn or damaged suspension components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Rear Axle Seal Leak:**
    * Description/Symptoms: A leaking rear axle seal can cause a loss of differential fluid.
    * Solution: Replace the rear axle seal.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Alternator Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: A failing alternator will not charge the battery, which can cause the vehicle to stall.
    * Solution: Replace the alternator.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Headlight Switch Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The headlight switch may not turn on or off the headlights.
    * Solution: Replace the headlight switch.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **HVAC System Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The HVAC system may not blow air or heat.
    * Solution: Inspect and repair the HVAC system.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Bedside Rust:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The bedsides of the truck may rust.
    * Solution: Inspect and repair or replace the affected panels.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

The 1990 GMC C2500 is a durable and capable pickup truck, but it is not without its issues. Some of the more common problems include intake manifold gasket failure, water pump failure, and head gasket failure. These issues can be costly to fix, so it is important to have the vehicle inspected regularly and to address any problems as soon as they arise.
