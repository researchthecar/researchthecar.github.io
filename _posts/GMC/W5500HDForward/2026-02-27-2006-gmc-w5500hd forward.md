### Duramax 6.6L V8 Turbo Diesel (LLY)
**Injector Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Rough idle, misfires, reduced power, black smoke.
- **Solution:** Replacement of faulty fuel injectors.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1500 per injector, labor extra.

**EGR Cooler Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Coolant loss, white smoke from exhaust, overheating.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the EGR cooler assembly.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1000

### Standard Electrical System
**Alternator Failures**
- **Symptoms:** Battery warning light, dimming lights, starting problems.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the alternator.

### Summary Verdict
The 2006 GMC W5500HD Forward generally exhibits good reliability, particularly with its gasoline V8 engine options and robust transmission choices like the Allison automatics. The Duramax diesel engine, while powerful, has a few documented issues such as injector problems and EGR cooler leaks that require attention and can be costly to repair. The chassis and suspension are built for heavy-duty use and typically hold up well, though rust can be a concern in certain environments. Electrical system issues, like alternator failures and wiring corrosion, are not uncommon for a vehicle of this age and usage. Overall, the W5500HD is a capable workhorse, but potential buyers should be aware of the specific potential issues, especially with the diesel powertrain and age-related electrical and body concerns.
