**Introduction:**

The 2022 GMC Yukon XL is a full-size SUV that offers plenty of space and capability. It comes with a choice of two engines: a 5.3L V8 with 355 horsepower and 383 lb-ft of torque, or a 6.2L V8 with 420 horsepower and 460 lb-ft of torque. Transmission options include a 10-speed automatic with rear-wheel drive or all-wheel drive. The Yukon XL is praised for its spacious interior, comfortable ride, and strong performance. However, some owners have reported issues with various components of the vehicle.

**Engine**

- **5.3L V8**
 - **Intake Manifold Gasket Failure**
  - Description/Symptoms: Coolant leak, overheating, loss of power
  - Solution: Replace intake manifold gaskets
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800+
  - Recalls: None Found
- **6.2L V8**
 - **No Issues Found**

**Transmission**

- **10-Speed Automatic**
 - **Shifting Problems**
  - Description/Symptoms: Hard shifting, delayed shifts, slipping gears
  - Solution: Replace or repair transmission components
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $4,000+
  - Recalls: NHTSA ID Number: 22V-862

**Suspension**

- **Air Suspension**
 - **Air Compressor Failure**
  - Description/Symptoms: Suspension sags, difficulty adjusting ride height, warning messages on the dash
  - Solution: Replace air compressor
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
  - Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

- **Battery Discharge**
 - Description/Symptoms: Battery drains while parked, difficulty starting the vehicle
 - Solution: Replace or repair faulty electrical components
 - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
 - Recalls: None Found
- **Headlight Malfunction**
 - Description/Symptoms: Dim or flickering headlights, warning messages on the dash
 - Solution: Replace headlight assemblies or electrical wiring
 - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600+
 - Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

- **Rear Hatch Seal**
 - Description/Symptoms: Water leaks into the cargo area, trunk seal tearing
 - Solution: Replace or repair rear hatch seal
 - Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+
 - Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

- **Climate Control Malfunction**
 - Description/Symptoms: AC or heat not functioning properly, temperature control issues
 - Solution: Replace or repair climate control components
 - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+
 - Recalls: None Found
- **Infotainment Screen Issues**
 - Description/Symptoms: Touchscreen freezes, glitches, or goes blank
 - Solution: Update or replace infotainment software or screen
 - Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,000+
 - Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

Overall, the 2022 GMC Yukon XL is a well-rounded SUV, but it has had some reported issues with the engine, transmission, suspension, electrical system, and interior. While the severity of these issues can vary, some can be costly to repair. It's important to research potential issues and consider the cost of repairs before purchasing a used Yukon XL.
