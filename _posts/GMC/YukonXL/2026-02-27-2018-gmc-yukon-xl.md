# 2018 GMC Yukon XL Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2018 GMC Yukon XL offers spacious three-row seating, robust towing capabilities, and a choice of powerful V8 engines, making it a popular choice for large families and road-trip enthusiasts. While it provides high-end comfort and utility, prospective buyers should review known mechanical considerations and potential electrical or powertrain concerns reported by owners to ensure a reliable long-term ownership experience.

---

## Engine

### 5.3L V8 EcoTec3

A 5.3-liter V8 engine producing around 355 horsepower and 383 lb-ft of torque, paired with active fuel management technology. It provides a balanced blend of everyday capability and efficiency for full-size SUV towing.

### 6.2L V8 EcoTec3

A high-output 6.2-liter V8 engine delivering approximately 420 horsepower and 460 lb-ft of torque. It offers strong acceleration and superior towing performance for demanding drivers.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

A traditional 6-speed automatic transmission paired with the standard 5.3L engine option, known for straightforward gear ratios and dependable highway cruising.

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

An 8-speed automatic transmission offering closer gear spacing for improved power delivery and efficiency. Buyers should inspect shift quality during a test drive.

### 10-Speed Automatic Transmission

A modern 10-speed automatic transmission providing smooth shifting and optimized rpm ranges, commonly associated with higher trim levels and the 6.2L engine.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V673000: Seat Belts:Rear/Other](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=GMC&model=Yukon+XL&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** Certain second-row or third-row rear seatbelts retractor assemblies may not automatically lock when the seatbelt is fully pulled out of the retractor, possibly preventing a child seat from being properly secured. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 208, "Occupant Crash.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the rear seatbelt retractors and replace them if necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V289000: Power Train:Transfer Case (4-Wheel Drive)](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=GMC&model=Yukon+XL&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** A component missing from the drivetrain transfer case may cause the front and/or rear wheels to lock up without warning to the driver. Front or rear wheel lockup increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and, if necessary, replace the transfer case assembly, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2018 GMC Yukon XL remains a capable and versatile full-size SUV with strong engine options and immense utility. While major mechanical failures are not overwhelmingly prevalent in owner feedback, a thorough pre-purchase inspection focusing on the powertrain, electronics, and overall mechanical condition is recommended to ensure a trouble-free purchase.
