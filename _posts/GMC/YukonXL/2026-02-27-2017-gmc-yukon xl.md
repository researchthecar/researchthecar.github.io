# 2017 GMC Yukon XL

### Overview
The 2017 GMC Yukon XL is a full-size SUV known for its spacious interior and towing capabilities. Generally, it offers a solid ownership experience with a few areas that have seen more attention from owners and technicians. Reliability for the powertrain is typically strong, especially with the larger V8 engines. However, like many vehicles in this class, some owners have reported issues related to the infotainment system and certain suspension components. Regular maintenance is key to ensuring the longevity of this robust vehicle, but specific recurring problems are not widespread across all units, indicating a generally well-built platform for its model year.

## Engine

### 5.3L V8 EcoTec3
**Active Fuel Management (AFM) Lifter Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Engine misfires, rough idle, check engine light, potential engine noise.
- **Solution:** Replacement of lifters, camshaft, and potentially torque converter. Some owners opt for AFM disablers.
- **Cost:** $1,500 - $3,000

### Summary Verdict
The 2017 GMC Yukon XL demonstrates good overall reliability, particularly with its V8 powertrains. The most commonly cited issues revolve around the Active Fuel Management system in the 5.3L engine and occasional failures of the rear air suspension compressor. Electrical gremlins, primarily with the infotainment system, have also been noted. While these issues can be costly to repair, they do not affect the majority of vehicles. With diligent maintenance and addressing potential AFM concerns proactively, the Yukon XL should provide dependable service for its owners.
