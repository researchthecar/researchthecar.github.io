# 2022 GMC Yukon XL Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2022 GMC Yukon XL is a full-size, three-row SUV known for its cavernous interior, smooth highway ride, and strong towing capabilities. However, owner feedback collected by federal safety agencies points to several recurring powertrain, electrical, and mechanical concerns that potential buyers should evaluate carefully before purchasing a used model.

---

## Engine

### 5.3L EcoTec3 V8

The base 5.3-liter V8 engine produces 355 horsepower and 383 lb-ft of torque, offering solid everyday performance and towing capability for most families.

### 6.2L EcoTec3 V8

The available 6.2-liter V8 engine generates 420 horsepower and 460 lb-ft of torque, providing robust acceleration but carrying notable owner complaints regarding sudden power loss.

**Loss of Motive Power**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers report that the vehicle can suddenly lose power, stall, or display electrical warnings while traveling at highway speeds.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis by a qualified technician is required; repairs may involve software updates, electronic throttle body replacement, or addressing internal mechanical components.

### 3.0L Duramax Turbo-Diesel I6

The 3.0-liter turbo-diesel inline-six delivers 277 horsepower and 460 lb-ft of torque, pairing excellent highway fuel economy with strong low-end pulling power.

## Transmission

### 10-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 10-speed automatic transmission is standard across all engine options, delivering broad gear coverage for efficiency and towing, though some owners report shifting irregularities.

**Harsh Shifting and Slippage**
- **Symptoms:** The transmission may slip, experience hard aggressive shifts, or cause unexpected jerking and high RPMs while driving.
- **Solution:** Requires professional inspection, potential valve body replacement, or a complete transmission control module reprogramming.

### Drivetrain and Differential

The four-wheel-drive and rear axle systems provide excellent traction but require careful inspection for early signs of mechanical binding or failure.

**Transfer Case and Differential Seizing**
- **Symptoms:** Unusual noises or sudden locking up of the rear differential and transfer case while driving at highway speeds.
- **Solution:** Have the differential and transfer case inspected by a certified mechanic; damaged internal components will need to be repaired or replaced.

## Electrical

### Infotainment and OnStar System

The vehicle relies heavily on advanced electronics for connectivity, climate control, and driver assistance, which occasionally exhibit software glitches.

**Module Failures and Screen Freezing**
- **Symptoms:** The infotainment display may go completely black, freeze intermittently, or the OnStar safety module may stop working unexpectedly.
- **Solution:** Dealership software updates or module replacements are typically required to restore full functionality.

## Safety

### Air Bag and Restraint Systems

Passive safety systems manage multiple airbags and occupant sensors, though electrical faults can trigger diagnostic warning lights.

**Overhead Console and Warning Lights**
- **Symptoms:** Illuminated airbag warning lights on the dashboard accompanied by non-functional overhead console controls.
- **Solution:** Dealer diagnostics are required to clear trouble codes, repair damaged wiring harnesses, or replace faulty console modules.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V617000: Seat Belts:Critical Fasteners](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=GMC&model=Yukon+XL&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** A seat belt with a deformed rivet may not properly restrain an occupant, increasing the risk of injury during a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the rivet head formation on both the left and right side third-row seat belt buckle assemblies and replace seat belt buckle assemblies, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V903000: Exterior Lighting:Lighting Control Module:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=GMC&model=Yukon+XL&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** The daytime running lights (DRLs) may not deactivate when the headlights are on. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 108, "Lamps, Reflective Devices, and Associated Equipment." DRLs that do not deactivate as intended may result in a glare, reducing visibility, and increasing the risk.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 25V274000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Engine:Hard Parts Internal/Mechanical](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=GMC&model=Yukon+XL&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** Engine failure increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the engine, and as necessary, repair or replace the engine. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V085000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission:Control Module:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=GMC&model=Yukon+XL&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** Rear wheels that lock up while driving increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will install new transmission control module (TCM) software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2022 GMC Yukon XL remains a top contender for buyers needing immense passenger room and cargo capacity. However, prospective buyers should thoroughly inspect the service history, check for outstanding recall completions, and ensure the powertrain and electronics operate without hesitation during a test drive.
