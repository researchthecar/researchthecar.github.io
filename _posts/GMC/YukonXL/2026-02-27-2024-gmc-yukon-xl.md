# 2024 GMC Yukon XL Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2024 GMC Yukon XL, buyers will find a spacious and capable full-size SUV offering a mix of powerful V8 and efficient diesel engine options. While most examples provide smooth highway cruising and strong utility, owner reports suggest keeping an eye on powertrain stability, electrical modules, and transmission behavior. Reviewing service records and verifying whether any open manufacturer campaigns have been performed is recommended before purchasing.

---

## Engine

### 5.3L V8 Engine

The standard 5.3-liter V8 engine produces 355 horsepower and 383 lb-ft of torque, providing reliable baseline performance for daily driving and light towing. Buyers should ensure proper maintenance intervals have been followed to keep this traditional pushrod V8 running smoothly.

### 6.2L V8 Engine

The larger 6.2-liter V8 generates 420 horsepower and 460 lb-ft of torque, delivering robust acceleration for heavy loads and highway passing. While powerful, some owner complaints highlight unexpected driveability concerns that require careful pre-purchase inspection.

**Engine Stalling and Loss of Power**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have reported sudden engine stalling or unexpected loss of motive power while driving at highway speeds, accompanied by warning lights or failure to restart.
- **Solution:** Requires professional diagnosis, which may involve software updates, filter replacements, or component repairs depending on the root cause.

### 3.0L Duramax Turbo-Diesel I6

The 3.0-liter turbo-diesel inline-six offers 277 horsepower and 460 lb-ft of torque, combining high torque output with excellent fuel economy for long-distance cruising. It is a popular choice for buyers looking to maximize driving range and towing efficiency.

## Transmission

### 10-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 10-speed automatic transmission pairs with all available engine options to optimize fuel delivery and highway cruising. While generally smooth, some owners have experienced shifting irregularities and valve body concerns.

**Valve Body and Shifting Defects**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may experience harsh shifting, transmission shuddering, failure to shift out of lower gears, or unintended neutral shifts.
- **Solution:** May require dealership inspection and replacement of the valve body or associated transmission control components.

## Electrical

### Infotainment and Camera System

The electrical architecture drives numerous convenience and safety displays, though certain software glitches can cause intermittent accessory failures.

**Intermittent Camera and Display Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Backup and surround-view cameras may intermittently go completely black or become completely inoperative during operation.
- **Solution:** Reprogramming software modules or replacing affected wiring cables and control units.

## Exterior

### Sunroof

Interior comfort features are generally well-appointed, though isolated structural glass incidents have been noted by owners.

**Glass Shattering**
- **Symptoms:** An abnormal loud bang followed by the sudden shattering of the panoramic sunroof glass while driving at highway speeds.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the sunroof glass assembly and clearing of any glass debris.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V674000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Brake Fluid Low Warning: Lamp](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=GMC&model=Yukon+XL&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Vehicle Safety Standard number 135, "Light Vehicle Brake Systems." Without a warning light, the vehicle may be driven with low brake fluid, which can reduce braking performance and increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 25V274000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Engine:Hard Parts Internal/Mechanical](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=GMC&model=Yukon+XL&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** Engine failure increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the engine, and as necessary, repair or replace the engine. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2024 GMC Yukon XL remains a dominant choice for families needing expansive passenger and cargo room alongside strong towing capability. While most drivers enjoy trouble-free operation, prospective buyers should thoroughly test drive the vehicle to check for transmission shifting smoothness, inspect all electrical accessories and cameras, and review vehicle service histories to address potential engine or powertrain concerns.
