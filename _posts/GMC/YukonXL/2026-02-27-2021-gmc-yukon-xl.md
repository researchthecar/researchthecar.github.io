# 2021 GMC Yukon XL Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2021 GMC Yukon XL offers generous passenger space, modern technology, and a commanding road presence, making it a popular choice for large families and road-trippers. However, early production models of this generation exhibit notable reliability pain points. Prospective buyers should pay close attention to potential powertrain hiccups, electronic glitches, and advanced driver-assistance system quirks that can sour ownership if not thoroughly inspected.

---

## Engine

### 5.3L V8 L84

The 5.3-liter V8 engine produces 355 horsepower and 383 lb-ft of torque, serving as the standard workhorse for the Yukon XL. While generally capable and well-suited for towing, it shares vulnerability with other GM small-block V8s regarding active fuel management lifter failures.

**Lifter Collapse**
- **Symptoms:** Illuminated check engine light, ticking noises from the engine bay, shaking, and potential cylinder wall scoring.
- **Solution:** Requires professional diagnosis and replacement of failed valve lifters and inspection of cylinder walls for damage.

**Engine Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** Unexpected loss of power while driving at highway speeds, accompanied by warning lights and difficulty restarting.
- **Solution:** Diagnose fuel delivery or powertrain control module issues; replace faulty components as necessary.

### 6.2L V8 L87

The larger 6.2-liter V8 churns out 420 horsepower and 460 lb-ft of torque, delivering brisk acceleration and stronger towing capability for higher trims like the Denali. It provides robust performance but demands attentive maintenance and premium fuel.

### 3.0L Duramax Turbo-Diesel I6

The 3.0-liter Duramax turbo-diesel inline-six generates 277 horsepower and 460 lb-ft of torque, pairing strong low-end pulling power with exceptional highway fuel economy. It is a sought-after option for long-distance drivers, though emissions equipment requires proper operating cycles.

## Transmission

### 10-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 10-speed automatic transmission is paired across all engine options in the Yukon XL, aiming to optimize both performance and highway efficiency. While generally smooth when functioning correctly, early units have experienced notable mechanical failures involving torque converters and valve bodies.

**Torque Converter and Internal Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Sudden loss of motive power, harsh shifting, failure to engage reverse, and metal debris circulating through the transmission fluid.
- **Solution:** May require complete transmission overhaul or replacement depending on internal wear.

**Valve Body Assembly Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle enters limp mode, displays 'Reduce Acceleration' warnings, and skips gears or locks up.
- **Solution:** Replace the valve body control assembly and flush transmission fluid.

## Suspension

### Air Ride Leveling Suspension

Advanced adaptive and air suspension setups deliver a plush highway ride and adjustable ground clearance, though they add long-term mechanical complexity compared to traditional steel springs.

**Adaptive Suspension Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Recurring ride height errors, uneven vehicle stance, rough ride quality, and warning messages on the dashboard.
- **Solution:** Inspect air struts, compressors, and electronic control modules; replace failing suspension components.

## Electrical

### Electrical System

**Key Fob Recognition and Accessory Failures**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle fails to detect a functioning key fob for starting, while power windows or other accessories malfunction.
- **Solution:** Reprogram key fobs or inspect body control modules for software glitches.

### Backup Camera

The complex electronic architecture powering infotainment screens, driver displays, and safety sensors can occasionally present frustrating software glitches and sensor dropouts.

**Intermittent Display Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Rear view camera fails to project an image onto the infotainment screen when shifting into reverse.
- **Solution:** Inspect camera wiring harnesses and update or replace infotainment display modules as needed.

## Safety

### Forward Collision Avoidance

Advanced driver-assistance features provide helpful protection on long trips but can occasionally generate false alerts or unexpected braking interventions.

**Phantom Braking**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle unexpectedly applies the brakes while driving in normal traffic without an actual obstruction.
- **Solution:** Have dealer check for software updates or sensor calibration issues, noting that diagnosing intermittent faults can be challenging.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V455000: Steering:Steering Control Module](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=GMC&model=Yukon+XL&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** A software issue may cause a loss of power steering assist at startup or while driving. Increased steering effort, particularly at lower speeds, increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 22V036000: Power Train:Driveline:Driveshaft](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=GMC&model=Yukon+XL&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** Driveshaft failure can cause a sudden loss of drive power, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the left and/or right rear driveshaft assemblies, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V792000: Seat Belts](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=GMC&model=Yukon+XL&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The seat belt brackets may not be secured to the seat frame in the front-row center seating position. If a seat-belt bracket is not secured, the seat belt may not properly restrain an occupant in the front-row center seat position, increasing the risk of injury in a crash.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will inspect left-side and right-side front-row center seat-belt bracket attachments and reassemble it correctly as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V811000: Seat Belts](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=GMC&model=Yukon+XL&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** If an incorrect bolt was used, the seat belt may not be properly attached to the vehicle. If a seat belt assembly is not properly attached to the vehicle, the seat belt may not properly restrain an occupant in the event of a crash, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will replace the suspect bolts, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V735000: Seats](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=GMC&model=Yukon+XL&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The attachment bolts for the front seats may not have been properly reinstalled after the repair was completed. If an occupied front seat has loose or missing attachment bolts, it may move during a crash, increasing the risk of injury to the seated occupant.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the seat attachments for both front seats and install attachment bolts as needed, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V739000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump:Control/Drive Module](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=GMC&model=Yukon+XL&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The fuel pump power control module may cause the fuel pump to fail or operate intermittently, which can interrupt fuel flow to the engine, and cause an unexpected engine stall. An engine stall increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the fuel pump power control module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V421000: Air Bags:Srs Malfunction Warning Lamp/Light](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=GMC&model=Yukon+XL&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The air bag malfunction indicator light may fail to warn the driver of a problem with the air bag, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 21V190000: Seat Belts](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=GMC&model=Yukon+XL&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The third-row outboard seat belts may have been entrapped or misrouted behind the outboard seat-folding mechanism, which could result in damaged seat belts. A damaged seat belt can break during a crash, increasing the risk of injury to the occupant.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the third-row outboard seat belts, replacing any damaged seat belts, and rerouting them if necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2021 GMC Yukon XL is a formidable full-size SUV that excels in passenger room, cargo flexibility, and towing capability. However, used-car buyers should approach early models from this production year with caution due to reported issues involving transmission durability, active fuel management lifters, and occasional electronic or safety system glitches. Securing a comprehensive pre-purchase inspection and verifying service history or remaining powertrain warranty coverage is strongly recommended.
