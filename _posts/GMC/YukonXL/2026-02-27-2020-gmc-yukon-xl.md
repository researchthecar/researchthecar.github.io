# 2020 GMC Yukon XL Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used full-size SUV, reviewing owner experiences is an essential part of the evaluation process. This report highlights key areas of concern reported by drivers, offering practical insights to help you make an informed decision.

---

## Engine

### 5.3L V8

The 5.3L V8 engine produces 355 horsepower and 383 lb-ft of torque, providing reliable towing capability and everyday performance. While generally robust, some owners have experienced valvetrain and internal component concerns requiring careful inspection.

**Engine Knock and Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The check engine light flashes accompanied by loud knocking noises and severe engine malfunction while driving.
- **Solution:** Have the engine inspected by a professional technician; major internal repairs or a complete engine rebuild may be required.

**Lifter Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Owners report sudden loud ticking noises, engine misfires, and a complete loss of motive power due to stuck or failed valve lifters.
- **Solution:** Replace the faulty valve lifters and inspect the valvetrain for associated damage.

### 6.2L V8

The high-output 6.2L V8 engine delivers 420 horsepower and 460 lb-ft of torque for robust acceleration and towing capacity. Buyers should verify proper maintenance history during pre-purchase inspections.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

The traditional 6-speed automatic transmission is paired with standard configurations to deliver smooth, predictable gear changes. Regular fluid maintenance is recommended to ensure long-term durability.

### 10-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 10-speed automatic transmission offers refined highway cruising and optimized power delivery across multiple gears. Owners have occasionally reported unexpected mechanical failures and loud noises requiring extensive service.

**Transmission Failure and Whining Noise**
- **Symptoms:** A sudden loud whining noise on startup or complete mechanical failure resulting in the vehicle losing all motive power.
- **Solution:** Inspect the transmission assembly; component replacement or a complete transmission unit swap may be necessary.

## Suspension

### Front Suspension Struts

Suspension components play a critical role in maintaining ride quality and stability over uneven road surfaces.

**Premature Strut Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Front suspension becomes excessively stiff, causing harsh impacts and a rough ride when encountering road bumps.
- **Solution:** Replace the worn front struts and related suspension dampers to restore proper ride comfort.

## Steering

### Steering System

Issues related to steering assist and alignment can impact overall vehicle handling and driver confidence.

**Loss of Steering Control**
- **Symptoms:** The steering warning light illuminates, followed by a sudden and complete loss of steering assist or excessive wandering while driving.
- **Solution:** Have the electronic steering system and related components thoroughly inspected and repaired or replaced by a qualified technician.

## Electrical

### Lighting and Infotainment

Electrical concerns generally involve cabin electronics and exterior lighting fixtures that may require localized diagnosis.

**Exterior Light and Infotainment Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** Headlights or marker lights fail due to moisture corrosion in the housing seals, while infotainment screens freeze, flicker, or go completely blank.
- **Solution:** Inspect light seals for moisture damage, replace affected assemblies, and check infotainment software or hardware connections.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V115000: Tires](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=GMC&model=Yukon+XL&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** Over cured tires may develop a break in the sidewall resulting in sudden air loss or a belt edge separation which could lead to a tread/belt loss. Either condition can cause a loss of vehicle control, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the tires, replacing them as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V837000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=GMC&model=Yukon+XL&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** Over-pressurization can crack the fuel pump and cause leaks, increasing the risk of a fire in the presence of an ignition source.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will replace the fuel pump, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

When evaluating this vehicle, pay close attention to powertrain operation, especially regarding transmission performance and valvetrain sound during a pre-purchase inspection. A thorough evaluation by a trusted mechanic can help identify potential issues before finalizing your purchase.
