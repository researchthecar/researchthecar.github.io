# 2016 GMC Yukon XL Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2016 GMC Yukon XL offers generous cabin space, robust towing capabilities, and powerful V8 engine options, making it a popular choice for large families and road trips. However, prospective buyers should carefully evaluate common mechanical and electrical pain points reported by owners. Notable trouble spots include failing LED taillight circuit boards, transmission shifting shudders on automatic models, and occasional issues with the active fuel management systems and brake boosters.

---

## Engine

### 5.3L V8

The 5.3L V8 engine produces approximately 355 horsepower and 383 lb-ft of torque, providing strong utility and towing performance. While generally durable, it is susceptible to complaints regarding the cylinder-deactivation system.

**Active Fuel Management (AFM) System Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Loud tapping sounds, engine skipping, sudden loss of power, and potential engine failure.
- **Solution:** Inspect the lifters and AFM components; severely worn systems may require extensive top-end engine repairs or complete engine replacement.

### 6.2L V8

The larger 6.2L V8 delivers an impressive 420 horsepower and 460 lb-ft of torque paired with smooth acceleration. Buyers should listen closely for valvetrain ticking during a pre-purchase inspection.

**Dynamic/Active Fuel Management Tapping and Stall**
- **Symptoms:** Loud ticking noises from the valvetrain, engine misfires, rough running conditions, and sudden power loss.
- **Solution:** Diagnose valvetrain wear and replace failed lifters or malfunctioning fuel management components as needed.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic

The standard 6-speed automatic transmission provides dependable shifting for daily driving and towing duties. It generally exhibits fewer widespread mechanical complaints than its multi-speed counterparts.

### 8-Speed Automatic

The 8-speed automatic transmission improves highway cruising efficiency but is prone to torque converter shudder and rough engagement. Regular fluid maintenance is critical for longevity.

**Transmission Shudder and Harsh Shifting**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle jolts or shudders during acceleration, rough gear transitions, and hesitation when attempting to accelerate from a stop.
- **Solution:** Flush and replace transmission fluid using updated low-viscosity formulations, or service the torque converter if internal wear is present.

## Brakes

### Brake Assist and Vacuum Pump

The braking system provides adequate stopping power for a large SUV, though owners have documented intermittent failures related to vacuum assist loss.

**Brake Pedal Hardness and Delayed Stopping**
- **Symptoms:** Brake pedal suddenly becomes stiff or hard to press down, leading to extended stopping distances or a temporary loss of brake assist.
- **Solution:** Inspect the brake vacuum pump, lines, and brake booster assembly, and replace failing components.

## Exterior

### Exterior Lighting Circuit Boards

Electrical accessories and exterior lighting require careful testing, particularly the rear LED housings which are known for premature internal circuit board degradation.

**LED Taillight Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Rear brake lights and taillights fail intermittently or stop working entirely due to cracked internal circuit connections.
- **Solution:** Replace the faulty taillight assembly or repair the cracked internal circuit board traces.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V651000: Air Bags:Frontal](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=GMC&model=Yukon+XL&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** In the affected vehicles, certain driving conditions may cause the air bag sensing and diagnostic module (SDM) software to activate a diagnostic test. During this test, deployment of the frontal air bags and the seat belt pretensioners would not occur in the event of a crash.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will reflash the SDM software. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V437000: Air Bags:Frontal](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=GMC&model=Yukon+XL&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** While being previously remedied for recall 16V-651, the affected vehicles may not have received the complete software update necessary to remedy the recall condition. Without the update, certain driving conditions may cause the air bag sensing and diagnostic module (SDM) software to activate a diagnostic test.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will reflash the SDM software. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V256000: Suspension:Front:Control Arm](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=GMC&model=Yukon+XL&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** Inadequate welds may allow the control arm to separate from the bushing, compromising steering and increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will replace the left and right front upper control arms and realign the vehicle, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2016 GMC Yukon XL remains a capable and comfortable full-size transport vehicle, but potential buyers should thoroughly inspect key trouble areas before purchasing. Pay close attention to the transmission for signs of shuddering, listen for any valvetrain noise indicative of cylinder-deactivation wear, and verify that all exterior LED taillights and brake assist features function properly.
