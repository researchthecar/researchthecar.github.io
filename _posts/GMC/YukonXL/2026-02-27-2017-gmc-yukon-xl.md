# 2017 GMC Yukon XL Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used full-size SUV, the Yukon XL offers expansive passenger and cargo space alongside strong towing capabilities. However, prospective buyers should carefully evaluate common mechanical and electrical pain points reported by owners, particularly concerning powertrain performance, exterior lighting, and brake reliability. Being aware of these areas can help you spot potential issues during a pre-purchase inspection and negotiate a fair price.

---

## Engine

### 5.3L V8

The standard 5.3-liter V8 engine delivers ample horsepower and torque for everyday driving and heavy towing. While generally robust, buyers should monitor for valvetrain and fuel system wear.

**Fuel Injector Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Engine misfires, rough running, illuminated check engine light, and potential reduced power mode.
- **Solution:** Diagnose faulty injectors and replace the affected components.

**Active Fuel Management (AFM) Lifter Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Violent engine shaking, severe knocking noises, loss of power, and stuck lifters causing internal valvetrain damage.
- **Solution:** Inspect the valvetrain and replace damaged lifters and camshafts as necessary.
- **Cost:** $6,000.00

### 6.2L V8

The optional 6.2-liter V8 engine provides high output and strong towing capacity for demanding utility needs.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic

The 6-speed automatic transmission handles standard duty cycles, though some owners experience shifting roughness and torque converter shudder over time.

**Transmission Shudder and Hard Shifting**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle jerks during acceleration, experiences harsh upshifts or downshifts, and develops a noticeable shudder, often linked to torque converter operation.
- **Solution:** Service the transmission, update software, or replace failing torque converters and solenoids.

### 8-Speed Automatic

The 8-speed automatic transmission provides smooth power delivery across a wide gear range, though proper fluid maintenance remains vital.

## Brakes

### Brake System and Exterior Lighting

Braking and exterior lighting systems are critical for safety, though owners have reported intermittent tail lamp failures and occasional brake assist firmness.

**Brake Light Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** Rear brake lamps or tail lights fail intermittently or stop working entirely, sometimes without illuminating dashboard warning indicators.
- **Solution:** Inspect bulb sockets, wiring harnesses, and tail light assemblies for defects and replace failing components.

**Brake Pedal Stiffness**
- **Symptoms:** Brake pedal suddenly feels hard to depress when navigating slow traffic or low-speed turns.
- **Solution:** Check vacuum assist hoses, brake booster operation, and hydraulic pressure.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V651000: Air Bags:Frontal](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=GMC&model=Yukon+XL&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** In the affected vehicles, certain driving conditions may cause the air bag sensing and diagnostic module (SDM) software to activate a diagnostic test. During this test, deployment of the frontal air bags and the seat belt pretensioners would not occur in the event of a crash.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will reflash the SDM software. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The Yukon XL is a capable and spacious full-size SUV that serves families and towers well, but buyers must perform a thorough inspection. Pay close attention to engine idle quality and valvetrain health, test-drive carefully to check for transmission shudder, and verify that all exterior lighting and brake functions operate properly.
