# 2019 GMC Yukon XL Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2019 GMC Yukon XL is a spacious and capable full-size SUV offering robust towing capacity and comfortable three-row seating. For used-car buyers, understanding its powertrain options and potential minor electrical or interior quirks helps ensure a confident purchase.

---

## Engine

### 5.3L V8 EcoTec3

A 5.3-liter V8 engine producing approximately 355 horsepower and 383 lb-ft of torque, paired with Active Fuel Management. It provides dependable everyday performance for hauling and highway cruising.

### 6.2L V8 EcoTec3

A 6.2-liter V8 engine delivering roughly 420 horsepower and 460 lb-ft of torque, offering stronger acceleration and higher towing capabilities. It is generally robust when properly maintained.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

A traditional 6-speed automatic transmission typically mated to the 5.3L engine option. It delivers smooth gear shifts suited for daily driving.

### 10-Speed Automatic Transmission

A modern 10-speed automatic transmission usually paired with the 6.2L V8 engine. It optimizes power delivery and highway efficiency.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V673000: Seat Belts:Rear/Other](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=GMC&model=Yukon+XL&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** Certain second-row or third-row rear seatbelts retractor assemblies may not automatically lock when the seatbelt is fully pulled out of the retractor, possibly preventing a child seat from being properly secured. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 208, "Occupant Crash.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the rear seatbelt retractors and replace them if necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V289000: Power Train:Transfer Case (4-Wheel Drive)](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=GMC&model=Yukon+XL&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** A component missing from the drivetrain transfer case may cause the front and/or rear wheels to lock up without warning to the driver. Front or rear wheel lockup increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and, if necessary, replace the transfer case assembly, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2019 GMC Yukon XL is a strong contender in the full-size SUV market, offering substantial passenger room and towing strength. Buyers should inspect the vehicle thoroughly, paying close attention to general wear, but can generally expect a capable and versatile long-distance cruiser.
