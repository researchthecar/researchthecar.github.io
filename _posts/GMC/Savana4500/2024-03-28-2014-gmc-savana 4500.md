**Introduction**

The 2014 GMC Savana 4500 is a full-size van offered with various engine and transmission options. It is known for its spacious interior, powerful engines, and capable towing and hauling capabilities. However, like any vehicle, it has its share of issues reported by owners.

**Engine**

**6.0L V8 Gasoline**
* **Intake Manifold Failure:**
    * **Description/Symptoms:** Cracking or warping of the intake manifold can lead to vacuum leaks, engine misfires, and a loss of power.
    * **Solution:** Replacement of the intake manifold.
    * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $800 - $1,500+
* **Head Gasket Failure:**
    * **Description/Symptoms:** Leaking head gasket can cause coolant leaks, overheating, and engine damage.
    * **Solution:** Replacement of the head gasket.
    * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $2,000 - $3,000+
* **Piston Ring Failure:**
    * **Description/Symptoms:** Worn or broken piston rings can lead to excessive oil consumption, reduced engine performance, and blue smoke from the exhaust.
    * **Solution:** Replacement of the piston rings.
    * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $2,500 - $4,000+
* **Oil Pressure Sensor Failure:**
    * **Description/Symptoms:** Malfunctioning oil pressure sensor can provide inaccurate oil pressure readings, triggering false alarms or engine damage.
    * **Solution:** Replacement of the oil pressure sensor.
    * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $100 - $200+
* **Valvetrain Noise:**
    * **Description/Symptoms:** Excessive noise from the valve train, such as ticking or clattering, can indicate worn or damaged valve lifters or camshaft lobes.
    * **Solution:** Adjustment or replacement of valve lifters or camshaft.
    * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $600 - $1,200+

**6.6L Duramax V8 Diesel**
* **Injector Failure:**
    * **Description/Symptoms:** Faulty fuel injectors can cause rough idling, poor fuel economy, and a lack of power.
    * **Solution:** Replacement of the fuel injectors.
    * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $500 - $800 per injector
* **EGR Valve Failure:**
    * **Description/Symptoms:** Malfunctioning exhaust gas recirculation (EGR) valve can lead to increased emissions, reduced performance, and black smoke from the exhaust.
    * **Solution:** Replacement of the EGR valve.
    * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $300 - $500+
* **Turbocharger Failure:**
    * **Description/Symptoms:** Worn or damaged turbocharger can result in reduced boost pressure, loss of power, and increased fuel consumption.
    * **Solution:** Replacement of the turbocharger.
    * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $1,500 - $3,000+

**Transmission**

**6-Speed Automatic**
* **Clutch Pack Failure:**
    * **Description/Symptoms:** Slipping or shuddering during gear changes, delayed engagement, or complete loss of drive.
    * **Solution:** Replacement of the clutch pack.
    * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $1,500 - $2,500+
* **Torque Converter Failure:**
    * **Description/Symptoms:** Loss of fluid pressure within the torque converter can lead to slippage, overheating, and a lack of power.
    * **Solution:** Replacement of the torque converter.
    * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $1,000 - $2,000+
* **Transmission Control Module (TCM) Failure:**
    * **Description/Symptoms:** Faulty TCM can cause erratic gear shifts, transmission shuddering, or a complete loss of function.
    * **Solution:** Replacement of the TCM.
    * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $500 - $1,000+

**Suspension**

* **Front Stabilizer Bar Links:**
    * **Description/Symptoms:** Worn or damaged stabilizer bar links can cause excessive body roll, poor handling, and clunking noises during cornering or over bumps.
    * **Solution:** Replacement of the stabilizer bar links.
    * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $200 - $400+
* **Rear Leaf Spring Failure:**
    * **Description/Symptoms:** Broken or sagging leaf springs can result in a lowered ride height, poor handling, and excessive bouncing.
    * **Solution:** Replacement of the leaf springs.
    * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $600 - $1,200+

**Electrical**

* **Alternator Failure:**
    * **Description/Symptoms:** Malfunctioning alternator can lead to dim headlights, battery drain, and electrical system problems.
    * **Solution:** Replacement of the alternator.
    * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $400 - $600+
* **Starter Failure:**
    * **Description/Symptoms:** Worn or damaged starter can cause difficulty starting the engine, grinding noises, or a complete failure to crank.
    * **Solution:** Replacement of the starter.
    * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $200 - $400+

**Exterior**

* **Rust on Undercarriage:**
    * **Description/Symptoms:** Exposure to road salts, moisture, and chemicals can lead to rust formation on the undercarriage, potentially affecting the structural integrity of the vehicle.
    * **Solution:** Rust treatment or replacement of affected components.
    * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** Varies depending on the extent of rust damage.

**Interior**

* **Dashboard Panel Gaps:**
    * **Description/Symptoms:** Misaligned or loose dashboard panels can create gaps and rattles, detracting from the interior aesthetics.
    * **Solution:** Adjustment or replacement of the dashboard panels.
    * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $200 - $500+
* **Seat Adjustment Failure:**
    * **Description/Symptoms:** Malfunctioning seat motors or switches can prevent proper seat adjustment, leading to discomfort or safety concerns.
    * **Solution:** Replacement of the seat motors or switches.
    * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $300 - $600+
* **Climate Control Malfunction:**
    * **Description/Symptoms:** Faulty climate control system can result in poor heating or cooling performance, uneven air distribution, or fan noise.
    * **Solution:** Repair or replacement of the climate control module or related components.
    * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $400 - $800+

**Summary**

The 2014 GMC Savana 4500 has been reported to have various issues across its components, including engine, transmission, suspension, electrical, and interior systems. Some of the more severe and costly issues to watch out for include intake manifold failure, head gasket failure, clutch pack failure, and turbocharger failure. It is recommended to have the vehicle inspected by a qualified mechanic who is familiar with the specific issues associated with this model.
