## Electrical

### Standard Electrical System
**Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Premature battery discharge, vehicle won't start.
- **Solution:** Diagnose and repair parasitic draw, replace faulty battery if necessary.
- **Cost:** $150 - $500

**Alternator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Battery warning light illuminated, dimming headlights, engine stalling.
- **Solution:** Replace the alternator.
- **Cost:** $400 - $800


## Exterior

### Body Panels and Trim
**Door Latch Actuator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Doors not locking or unlocking electronically, manual operation may be difficult.
- **Solution:** Replace the door latch actuator assembly.
- **Cost:** $200 - $500


## Interior

### HVAC System
**Blower Motor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** No air coming from vents, or air only comes out at certain speeds.
- **Solution:** Replace the HVAC blower motor and resistor.
- **Cost:** $250 - $600

### Summary Verdict
The 2018 GMC Savana 4500 demonstrates strong reliability, particularly in its core powertrain components, which are built for demanding commercial use. The available V8 engines and automatic transmissions are generally robust and do not present widespread systemic failures. The most commonly reported non-wear-and-tear issues tend to be in the electrical system, such as battery drain and alternator problems, and some specific exterior and interior components like door latches and HVAC blower motors. These issues are typically repairable with moderate cost. Overall, the Savana 4500 is a dependable choice for commercial applications, with most potential problems being manageable.
