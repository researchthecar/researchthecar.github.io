**Introduction:**

The 2012 GMC Savana 1500 is a full-sized van offered with a choice of either a 4.8L V8 or 6.0L V8 engine, paired with a 4-speed automatic transmission. It is known for its spacious interior, powerful engine options, and reliable performance. However, like any vehicle, it has some reported issues that potential buyers should be aware of.

**Engine**
- **4.8L V8**
  - **Intake manifold gasket failure**
    - Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaks, overheating, misfires
    - Solution: Replace intake manifold gasket
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800
  - **Timing chain failure**
    - Description/Symptoms: Engine rattling, loss of power
    - Solution: Replace timing chain
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,200
- **6.0L V8**
  - **Active fuel management (AFM) system deactivation**
    - Description/Symptoms: Reduced fuel efficiency, engine performance issues
    - Solution: Disable AFM system or replace lifters
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
  - **Fuel injector failure**
    - Description/Symptoms: Misfires, rough idle
    - Solution: Replace fuel injectors
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $600 - $1,200

**Transmission**
- **4-speed automatic**
  - **Slipping gears**
    - Description/Symptoms: Vehicle may not engage gears properly, loss of power
    - Solution: Rebuild or replace transmission
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,000

**Suspension**
- **Front ball joint failure**
    - Description/Symptoms: Clicking or popping sounds, steering instability
    - Solution: Replace ball joints
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800
- **Rear leaf spring breakage**
    - Description/Symptoms: Sagging rear end, uneven tire wear
    - Solution: Replace leaf springs
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $600 - $1,000

**Electrical**
- **Alternator failure**
    - Description/Symptoms: Battery not charging, loss of power
    - Solution: Replace alternator
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800
- **Battery drain**
    - Description/Symptoms: Battery dies after sitting for a period of time
    - Solution: Identify and fix parasitic draw
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the issue

**Exterior**
- **Side door lock failure**
    - Description/Symptoms: Side doors may not lock or unlock properly
    - Solution: Replace door lock actuators
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400

**Interior**
- **Dashboard cracking**
    - Description/Symptoms: Cracks may appear on the dashboard
    - Solution: Replace dashboard
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000

**Recalls:**
- NHTSA Campaign Number: 12V249000 - Side air bag inflator may rupture
- NHTSA Campaign Number: 13V189000 - Steering shaft may seize
- NHTSA Campaign Number: 14V787000 - Brake line may corrode and leak

**Summary:**

The 2012 GMC Savana 1500 is a generally reliable vehicle with a spacious interior and powerful engine options. However, it does have some reported issues that potential buyers should be aware of, such as intake manifold gasket failure, timing chain failure, alternator failure, and battery drain. The severity of these issues varies, and the cost to fix them can range from a few hundred dollars to over $1,000. Overall, the vehicle is a good choice for those who need a large and reliable van, but it is important to be aware of the potential issues before making a purchasing decision.
