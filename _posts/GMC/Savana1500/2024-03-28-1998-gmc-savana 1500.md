**Introduction:**
The 1998 GMC Savana 1500 is a full-size van that was available with two engine options: a 5.0L V8 and a 5.7L V8. The 5.0L V8 produced 200 horsepower and 280 lb-ft of torque, while the 5.7L V8 produced 255 horsepower and 330 lb-ft of torque. Both engines were paired with a 4-speed automatic transmission. The Savana 1500 was praised for its spacious interior and powerful engine options, but it also received criticism for its poor fuel economy and uncomfortable ride quality.

**Engine**

**5.0L V8**
* **Intake Manifold Gasket Failure:** This issue occurs when the intake manifold gasket fails, causing coolant and oil to leak. Symptoms include a decrease in engine performance, overheating, and a coolant or oil leak. The solution is to replace the intake manifold gasket. Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800+
* **Head Gasket Failure:** This issue occurs when the head gasket fails, causing coolant and oil to leak into the cylinders. Symptoms include a decrease in engine performance, overheating, and a coolant or oil leak. The solution is to replace the head gasket. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
* **Valve Cover Gasket Leak:** This issue occurs when the valve cover gasket fails, causing oil to leak from the top of the engine. Symptoms include an oil leak and a burning oil smell. The solution is to replace the valve cover gasket. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
* **EGR Valve Failure:** This issue occurs when the EGR valve fails, causing the engine to run rough and stall. Symptoms include a decrease in engine performance, a rough idle, and stalling. The solution is to replace the EGR valve. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+

**5.7L V8**
* **Intake Manifold Gasket Failure:** (See 5.0L V8)
* **Head Gasket Failure:** (See 5.0L V8)
* **Valve Cover Gasket Leak:** (See 5.0L V8)
* **EGR Valve Failure:** (See 5.0L V8)
* **Timing Chain Failure:** This issue occurs when the timing chain fails, causing the engine to jump time and potentially causing catastrophic damage. Symptoms include a rattling noise from the engine, a decrease in engine performance, and a check engine light. The solution is to replace the timing chain and any damaged components. Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,000+

**Transmission**
**4-Speed Automatic Transmission**
* **Transmission Slipping:** This issue occurs when the transmission slips out of gear, causing the vehicle to lose power. Symptoms include a loss of power, a delayed response from the transmission, and a check engine light. The solution is to rebuild or replace the transmission. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
* **Torque Converter Failure:** This issue occurs when the torque converter fails, causing the transmission to overheat and slip. Symptoms include a loss of power, a burning smell, and a check engine light. The solution is to replace the torque converter. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
* **Solenoid Failure:** This issue occurs when a solenoid in the transmission fails, causing the transmission to not shift correctly. Symptoms include a rough shift, a delayed response from the transmission, and a check engine light. The solution is to replace the failed solenoid. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+

**Suspension**
* **Front Suspension Bushing Wear:** This issue occurs when the front suspension bushings wear out, causing the vehicle to handle poorly and make noise. Symptoms include a rattling noise from the front end, a decrease in handling performance, and uneven tire wear. The solution is to replace the worn bushings. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
* **Rear Suspension Leaf Spring Breakage:** This issue occurs when the rear suspension leaf springs break, causing the vehicle to sag in the rear and handle poorly. Symptoms include a sagging rear end, a decrease in handling performance, and a rough ride. The solution is to replace the broken leaf springs. Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800+

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain:** This issue occurs when the battery drains prematurely, causing the vehicle to not start. Symptoms include a dead battery, a slow crank, and a check engine light. The solution is to diagnose and fix the source of the battery drain, which could be a faulty alternator, starter, or parasitic draw. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+
* **Alternator Failure:** This issue occurs when the alternator fails, causing the battery to not charge and the vehicle to not start. Symptoms include a dead battery, a slow crank, and a check engine light. The solution is to replace the alternator. Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800+
* **Starter Failure:** This issue occurs when the starter fails, causing the vehicle to not start. Symptoms include a clicking noise when trying to start the vehicle, a no-crank condition, and a check engine light. The solution is to replace the starter. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+

**Exterior**
* **Rust:** This issue occurs when rust develops on the body of the vehicle, causing the metal to deteriorate and weaken. Symptoms include visible rust spots, bubbling paint, and holes in the body panels. The solution is to prevent rust by regularly washing and waxing the vehicle and repairing any damage as soon as possible. Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the severity of the rust
* **Body Panel Damage:** This issue occurs when the body panels of the vehicle are damaged, such as in a collision or from vandalism. Symptoms include dents, scratches, and broken body panels. The solution is to repair or replace the damaged body panels. Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the severity of the damage

**Interior**
* **Cracked Dashboard:** This issue occurs when the dashboard cracks, often due to exposure to sunlight or extreme temperatures. Symptoms include visible cracks in the dashboard. The solution is to replace the dashboard. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
* **Seat Upholstery Wear:** This issue occurs when the seat upholstery wears out or tears, often due to age or excessive use. Symptoms include worn or torn seat upholstery. The solution is to repair or replace the seat upholstery. Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the material and type of repair
* **Climate Control Issues:** This issue occurs when the climate control system fails, causing the vehicle to not heat or cool properly. Symptoms include a lack of heat or air conditioning, a noisy blower motor, and a check engine light. The solution is to diagnose and repair the faulty component, such as the blend door actuator, blower motor, or refrigerant leak. Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800+

**Summary**
The 1998 GMC Savana 1500 is a reliable van that is known for its spacious interior and powerful engine options. However, it has some common issues that can be expensive to repair, such as a timing chain failure on the 5.7L V8 engine and a transmission failure. It is important to regularly maintain this vehicle and address any issues promptly to avoid costly repairs.
