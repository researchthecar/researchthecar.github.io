**Introduction:**

The 1991 GMC K3500 is a heavy-duty pickup truck known for its durability and towing capacity. It is powered by a 7.4L V8 engine paired with a 4-speed automatic transmission. Other engine and transmission options include:

- 5.7L V8 with 4-speed automatic or 5-speed manual
- 6.5L diesel V8 with 4-speed automatic

The K3500 is popular among towing and hauling enthusiasts due to its reliable performance and strong build quality. However, it does have some known issues that potential buyers should be aware of.

**Engine**

**7.4L V8**

- **Intake Manifold Gasket Failure:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Coolant or oil leaks from the intake manifold, causing overheating or engine damage.
    - Solution: Replace the intake manifold gasket.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500
    - Recalls: None Found

- **Timing Chain Failure:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Rattle or grinding noises from the engine, loss of power, and engine misfires.
    - Solution: Replace the timing chain and gears.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
    - Recalls: None Found

**5.7L V8**

- **Oil Pump Failure:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Loss of oil pressure, engine knocking, and eventual engine seizure.
    - Solution: Replace the oil pump and related components.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
    - Recalls: None Found

**6.5L Diesel V8**

- **Injector Failure:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Engine misfires, decreased power, and black smoke from the exhaust.
    - Solution: Replace the faulty injector(s).
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500 per injector
    - Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**4-Speed Automatic**

- **Torque Converter Failure:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Transmission slipping, shuddering, or overheating.
    - Solution: Replace the torque converter.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,200 - $2,000
    - Recalls: None Found

**4-Speed Automatic (Diesel)**

- **Transmission Failure:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Transmission slipping, shuddering, or refusing to engage in gear.
    - Solution: Rebuild or replace the transmission.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+
    - Recalls: None Found

**5-Speed Manual**

- **Clutch Failure:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears, grinding noises, and slipping clutch.
    - Solution: Replace the clutch and related components.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
    - Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

- **Ball Joint Failure:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Clunking noises, steering instability, and premature tire wear.
    - Solution: Replace the ball joints.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500 per ball joint
    - Recalls: None Found

- **Leaf Spring Failure:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Sagging rear end, poor handling, and reduced payload capacity.
    - Solution: Replace the leaf springs.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $600 - $1,000
    - Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

- **Alternator Failure:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Dimming lights, battery discharge, and electrical system malfunctions.
    - Solution: Replace the alternator.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600
    - Recalls: None Found

- **Starter Failure:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Engine cranks slowly or fails to start.
    - Solution: Replace the starter or starter solenoid.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
    - Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

- **Rust:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Rust and corrosion on body panels, frame, and suspension components.
    - Solution: Anti-rust treatment and repair or replacement of affected components.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the extent of damage
    - Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

- **Dashboard Cracking:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Cracks or fading on the dashboard.
    - Solution: Replace the dashboard or use a dashboard cover.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500
    - Recalls: None Found

- **HVAC Malfunction:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Air conditioner or heater not working properly, blower motor not functioning.
    - Solution: Diagnose and repair or replace the faulty components.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
    - Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

The 1991 GMC K3500 is a reliable and capable truck but has some potential issues that owners should be aware of. The severity and cost of the issues vary, with some engine and transmission problems being more expensive to fix. Regular maintenance and proper repairs can help extend the life of the vehicle and minimize the likelihood of major breakdowns.
