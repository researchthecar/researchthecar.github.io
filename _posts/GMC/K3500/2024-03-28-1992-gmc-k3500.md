**1992 GMC K3500**

The 1992 GMC K3500 is a heavy-duty pickup truck that is available with a variety of engine and transmission options. The base engine is a 5.7L V8 that produces 210 horsepower and 300 lb-ft of torque. Other engine options include a 7.4L V8 and a 6.5L turbo-diesel V8. Transmission options include a 4-speed automatic and a 5-speed manual.

The GMC K3500 is known for its durability and towing capacity, but it is not without its problems. Some of the most common issues with the 1992 GMC K3500 include:

**Engine**

* **5.7L V8**
    * **Intake manifold gasket failure:** This is a common problem on the 5.7L V8 engine. The intake manifold gasket can fail, which can cause coolant and oil to leak. Symptoms of intake manifold gasket failure include:
        * Coolant leak
        * Oil leak
        * Engine overheating
        * Rough idle
        * Poor performance
    * **Solution:** The intake manifold gasket will need to be replaced. The cost of this repair will vary depending on the mechanic, but it is typically around $500 - $1000.
    * **Recalls:** None Found
* **7.4L V8**
    * **No major issues found**
* **6.5L turbo-diesel V8**
    * **Injection pump failure:** The injection pump can fail on the 6.5L turbo-diesel V8 engine. This can cause the engine to lose power or stall. Symptoms of injection pump failure include:
        * Loss of power
        * Stalling
        * Hard starting
        * Smoke from the exhaust
    * **Solution:** The injection pump will need to be replaced. The cost of this repair will vary depending on the mechanic, but it is typically around $2000 - $3000.
    * **Recalls:** None Found

**Transmission**

* **4-speed automatic**
    * **No major issues found**
* **5-speed manual**
    * **Clutch failure:** The clutch can fail on the 5-speed manual transmission. This can cause the transmission to slip or not engage properly. Symptoms of clutch failure include:
        * Slipping transmission
        * Difficulty shifting gears
        * Grinding noise when shifting
    * **Solution:** The clutch will need to be replaced. The cost of this repair will vary depending on the mechanic, but it is typically around $500 - $1000.
    * **Recalls:** None Found

**Suspension**

* **Front suspension:**
    * **Ball joint failure:** The ball joints can fail on the front suspension. This can cause the front end of the truck to shake or wander. Symptoms of ball joint failure include:
        * Shaking or wandering front end
        * Clicking or popping noise when turning
        * Uneven tire wear
    * **Solution:** The ball joints will need to be replaced. The cost of this repair will vary depending on the mechanic, but it is typically around $200 - $400 per side.
    * **Recalls:** None Found
* **Rear suspension:**
    * **Leaf spring failure:** The leaf springs can fail on the rear suspension. This can cause the rear end of the truck to sag or bounce. Symptoms of leaf spring failure include:
        * Sagging or bouncing rear end
        * Difficulty steering
        * Uneven tire wear
    * **Solution:** The leaf springs will need to be replaced. The cost of this repair will vary depending on the mechanic, but it is typically around $500 - $1000 per side.
    * **Recalls:** None Found

**Electrical**

* **No major issues found**
