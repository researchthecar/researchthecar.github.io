**Introduction**

The 1997 GMC K3500 is a heavy-duty pickup truck known for its power and durability. It comes equipped with a Vortec 7400 V8 engine that produces 300 horsepower and 452 lb-ft of torque, paired with a 4L80E four-speed automatic transmission. Other transmission options include a manual transmission and a truck-specific Allison transmission. People appreciate the K3500's towing and payload capacity, but common complaints include poor fuel economy and a stiff suspension.

**Engine**

**Vortec 7400 V8**

* **Intake Manifold Gasket Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Engine overheating, coolant leaks, loss of power
    * Solution: Replace intake manifold gasket
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Intake Valve Sticking**
    * Description/Symptoms: Rough idle, loss of power, engine misfires
    * Solution: Clean or replace intake valves
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**4L80E Four-Speed Automatic**

* **Torque Converter Shudder (pre-1999 models only)**
    * Description/Symptoms: Vibrations from the drivetrain when accelerating or under load
    * Solution: Replace torque converter
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,200
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Slipping Gears**
    * Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears, slipping gears under load, delayed engagement
    * Solution: Repair or rebuild transmission
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,200 - $2,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Leaf Spring Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Sagging or broken leaf springs, reduced ride height, rough ride
    * Solution: Replace leaf springs
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Shock Absorber Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Excessive bouncing, poor handling, nosedive while braking, squat while accelerating
    * Solution: Replace shock absorbers
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Alternator Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Electrical system malfunction, battery not charging, flickering lights
    * Solution: Replace alternator
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $600+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Battery Drain**
    * Description/Symptoms: Battery discharges quickly, difficulty starting, electrical system failure
    * Solution: Test and repair electrical system components, replace battery if necessary
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Taillight Lens Cracking**
    * Description/Symptoms: Cracked or damaged taillight lenses, reduced visibility
    * Solution: Replace taillight lenses
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Dashboard Cracking**
    * Description/Symptoms: Cracks or fading on the dashboard, reduced aesthetic appeal
    * Solution: Repair or replace dashboard
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Window Regulator Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Difficulty rolling up or down windows, stuck windows
    * Solution: Replace window regulator
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 1997 GMC K3500 is a durable workhorse with a powerful engine and capable suspension system. However, it is not without its issues. Potential buyers should be aware of the common problems associated with the engine, transmission, suspension, electrical, and interior components. The severity and cost of these issues vary, so it's important to consider them carefully before purchasing a used K3500.
