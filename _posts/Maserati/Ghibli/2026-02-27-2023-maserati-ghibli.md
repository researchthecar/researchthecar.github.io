# 2023 Maserati Ghibli Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2023 Maserati Ghibli continues to offer Italian flair, striking exterior design, and powerful luxury performance for used car shoppers. As an exotic luxury sedan, prospective buyers should balance its engaging driving dynamics and upscale cabin against the complexities of maintaining a high-performance European vehicle. Reviewing the available powertrain options and understanding potential luxury ownership costs will help ensure a satisfying purchase.

---

## Engine

### 3.0L Twin-Turbo V6

This 3.0-liter twin-turbocharged V6 engine produces around 345 horsepower and 369 lb-ft of torque in base trims, offering brisk acceleration. It generally provides strong performance, though buyers should budget for specialized maintenance typical of high-end Italian powertrains.

### 3.0L Twin-Turbo V6 (Modena)

Upgraded for the Modena trim, this variant of the 3.0-liter twin-turbo V6 pushes output to approximately 424 horsepower for a more spirited drive. It delivers robust power delivery, though proper routine servicing is essential to maintain long-term reliability.

### 3.8L Twin-Turbo V8

Exclusive to the high-performance Trofeo trim, the 3.8-liter twin-turbocharged V8 churns out an impressive 580 horsepower. It provides supercar-level straight-line speed, but demands meticulous upkeep and higher running costs.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed ZF Automatic Transmission

The ZF 8-speed automatic transmission is paired across all Ghibli engines, delivering quick and smooth gear changes via rear-wheel drive or available all-wheel drive. It is widely regarded as a responsive and robust unit in modern luxury applications.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V098000: Back Over Prevention:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Maserati&model=Ghibli&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 111, "Rear Visibility." A rearview camera image that does not display decreases the driver's visibility, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2023 Maserati Ghibli remains an alluring choice for buyers seeking distinctive luxury and brisk performance away from the mainstream German competition. While formal owner complaints are minimal, prospective owners must be prepared for the higher fuel, insurance, and specialized maintenance expenses associated with an Italian luxury sedan. A thorough pre-purchase inspection by a knowledgeable specialist is strongly recommended.
