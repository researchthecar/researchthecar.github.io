**Introduction**

The 2020 Maserati Ghibli is a midsize luxury sports sedan. It is available with three engine options: a 3.0L V6 Twin Turbo with 345 horsepower and 369 lb-ft of torque, a 3.8L V8 Twin Turbo with 580 horsepower and 538 lb-ft of torque, and a 2.0L Inline-4 Turbocharged Hybrid with 330 horsepower and 369 lb-ft of torque. The V6 engine comes with an 8-speed automatic transmission, while the V8 engine comes with an 8-speed automatic transmission or a 6-speed manual transmission. The hybrid engine comes with an 8-speed automatic transmission.

The Ghibli is a popular choice for drivers who want a luxurious and sporty sedan. It has a stylish design, a spacious interior, and a powerful engine. However, like all cars, the Ghibli has some issues that owners should be aware of.

**Engine**

**3.0L V6 Twin Turbo**

* **Fuel injector failure:** The fuel injectors can fail, causing the engine to run poorly or stall. The solution is to replace the fuel injectors. The estimated cost to fix is $1,000 - $2,000.
* **Turbocharger failure:** The turbochargers can fail, causing a loss of power. The solution is to replace the turbochargers. The estimated cost to fix is $2,000 - $4,000.
* **Timing chain tensioner failure:** The timing chain tensioner can fail, causing the timing chain to skip. This can lead to engine damage. The solution is to replace the timing chain tensioner. The estimated cost to fix is $1,500 - $2,500.

**3.8L V8 Twin Turbo**

* **Engine overheating:** The engine can overheat due to a faulty thermostat or water pump. The solution is to replace the thermostat or water pump. The estimated cost to fix is $500 - $1,000.
* **Oil leaks:** The engine can develop oil leaks from the oil pan gasket or valve cover gaskets. The solution is to replace the leaking gaskets. The estimated cost to fix is $200 - $500.
* **Emissions system issues:** The emissions system can develop problems, causing the car to fail emissions tests. The solution is to repair or replace the emissions system components. The estimated cost to fix is $500 - $1,500.

**Transmission**

**8-Speed Automatic Transmission**

* **Transmission slipping:** The transmission can slip, causing the car to hesitate or lose power. The solution is to replace the transmission. The estimated cost to fix is $3,000 - $5,000.
* **Transmission fluid leaks:** The transmission can develop fluid leaks from the pan gasket or seals. The solution is to replace the leaking gaskets or seals. The estimated cost to fix is $200 - $500.
* **Shifting problems:** The transmission can have problems shifting gears, causing the car to jerk or hesitate. The solution is to replace the transmission control module or the transmission solenoid pack. The estimated cost to fix is $500 - $1,500.

**6-Speed Manual Transmission**

* **Clutch failure:** The clutch can fail, causing the car to be unable to move. The solution is to replace the clutch. The estimated cost to fix is $1,000 - $2,000.
* **Transmission noise:** The transmission can make noise when shifting gears. The solution is to replace the transmission bearings or gears. The estimated cost to fix is $1,500 - $2,500.
* **Shifting problems:** The transmission can have problems shifting gears, causing the car to jerk or hesitate. The solution is to adjust the shift linkage or replace the transmission synchronizers. The estimated cost to fix is $200 - $500.

**Suspension**

* **Front suspension noise:** The front suspension can make noise when driving over bumps or rough roads. The solution is to replace the front suspension struts or control arms. The estimated cost to fix is $500 - $1,000.
* **Rear suspension noise:** The rear suspension can make noise when driving over bumps or rough roads. The solution is to replace the rear suspension shocks or springs. The estimated cost to fix is $500 - $1,000.
* **Alignment issues:** The car can develop alignment issues, causing the tires to wear unevenly. The solution is to get the car aligned. The estimated cost to fix is $100 - $200.

**Electrical**

* **Battery drain:** The battery can drain quickly, even when the car is not being used. The solution is to replace the battery or find the electrical drain and fix it. The estimated cost to fix is $200 - $500.
* **Electrical glitches:** The car can experience electrical glitches, such as flickering lights or power windows that do not work properly. The solution is to diagnose the electrical system and repair or replace the faulty components. The estimated cost to fix is $200 - $1,000.
* **Infotainment system issues:** The infotainment system can have problems, such as freezing or not responding to inputs. The solution is to update the software or replace the infotainment system. The estimated cost to fix is $500 - $1,500.

**Exterior**

* **Roof leaks:** The roof can leak, causing water to enter the cabin. The solution is to seal the roof or replace the sunroof. The estimated cost to fix is $500 - $1,000.
* **Door handle issues:** The door handles can become loose or break. The solution is to replace the door handles. The estimated cost to fix is $200 - $500.

**Interior**

**Summary**

The Maserati Ghibli is a stylish and powerful luxury sports sedan. However, it has some issues that owners should be aware of. If you are considering buying a Maserati Ghibli, be sure to research the common issues and factor in the potential cost of repairs.
