The 2015 Maserati Ghibli is a luxury sedan that offers a blend of Italian styling and performance. Engine options include a 3.0-liter twin-turbocharged V6 producing 345 horsepower and 369 lb-ft of torque (base model), and a more powerful version of the same engine producing 404 horsepower and 406 lb-ft of torque (Ghibli S and SQ4). Both engines are paired with an 8-speed automatic transmission. People often praise the Ghibli for its distinctive design, sporty handling, and luxurious interior. Common criticisms include its reliability issues, higher maintenance costs compared to competitors, and less refined interior materials in certain areas.

**Engine**

**3.0L Twin-Turbo V6 (345 hp & 404 hp)**

*   **Oil Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks can occur from various points, including the valve cover gaskets and oil pan gasket. Symptoms include visible oil leaks under the car, the smell of burning oil, and low oil levels.
    *   Solution: Replacing the leaking gaskets or seals is the typical solution.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,000+ depending on the source of the leak and labor costs.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Turbocharger Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported premature turbocharger failure, leading to reduced engine power, unusual noises (whining, screeching), and potentially engine damage.
    *   Solution: Turbocharger replacement is necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,500 - $5,000+ depending on labor and parts costs.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**ZF 8-Speed Automatic**

*   **Rough Shifting**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Harsh or jerky shifting, especially between certain gears, is a recurring complaint.
    *   Solution: In some cases, a transmission fluid flush and software update can improve the shifting. In more severe cases, valve body replacement or transmission rebuild may be needed.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 (fluid flush and software update) to $3,000+ (valve body or rebuild).
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Hesitation/Delay in Engagement**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Delay when shifting from park to drive or reverse. Hesitation when accelerating from a stop.
    *   Solution: Software updates or transmission repair/replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100-$4,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Premature Bushing Wear**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Owners have reported premature wear of suspension bushings, leading to clunking noises, imprecise handling, and uneven tire wear.
    *   Solution: Replacing the worn bushings is required.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+ per corner, depending on the specific bushings and labor costs.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Electronic Damper Issues (if equipped)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some models are equipped with electronically adjustable dampers. Failures can lead to a harsh or bouncy ride, along with warning lights on the dashboard.
    *   Solution: Replacing the faulty damper is necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+ per damper, including labor.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The infotainment system can be prone to glitches, including freezing, slow response times, and connectivity problems with Bluetooth and other devices.
    *   Solution: Software updates may address some issues. In more severe cases, replacing the head unit may be required.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 (software update) to $1,500+ (head unit replacement).
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Sensor Malfunctions**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Various sensors (e.g., tire pressure, oxygen, mass airflow) can fail, triggering warning lights and potentially affecting engine performance.
    *   Solution: Replacing the faulty sensor is necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+ per sensor, including diagnosis and labor.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Battery Drain**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners experience unexplained battery drain, especially if the vehicle sits for extended periods.
    *   Solution: Thoroughly inspect the electrical system for parasitic drains. Replacing the battery may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 (battery replacement) to $500+ (diagnosing and fixing parasitic drains).
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **No Issues Found**

**Interior**

*   **Sticky Buttons/Trim**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some interior trim pieces and buttons can develop a sticky or tacky surface over time, especially in warmer climates.
    *   Solution: Professional cleaning or replacement of affected parts.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+ depending on the extent of the issue.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2015 Maserati Ghibli presents a mixed bag for potential buyers. While it offers distinctive styling and a sporty driving experience, reliability concerns are significant. Engine and transmission issues can be costly to repair, and electrical glitches are a recurring annoyance. Suspension component wear and interior trim issues also contribute to the overall ownership experience. The severity of issues ranges from minor annoyances to potentially major mechanical failures, with repair costs varying widely depending on the problem. Potential buyers should carefully inspect any used Ghibli and consider a pre-purchase inspection by a qualified mechanic familiar with Maserati vehicles.
