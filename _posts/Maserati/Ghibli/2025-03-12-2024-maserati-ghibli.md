The 2024 Maserati Ghibli is a luxury sedan known for its distinctive Italian styling, powerful engine options, and sporty driving dynamics. Engine options include a 3.0-liter twin-turbocharged V6 producing 345 horsepower and 369 lb-ft of torque in the base model and 424 horsepower and 428 lb-ft of torque in the Modena and Modena Q4 models. A more powerful Trofeo model boasts a 3.8-liter twin-turbocharged V8 generating 572 horsepower and 538 lb-ft of torque. All Ghibli models use an 8-speed automatic transmission. Many owners appreciate the Ghibli for its attractive design and exhilarating performance, while some criticize its reliability, outdated interior technology, and relatively high price point compared to competitors.

**Engine**

**3.0L Twin-Turbocharged V6 (345 hp / 424 hp)**

*   **Oil Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks can occur from various engine seals and gaskets, resulting in oil spots under the vehicle, low oil level warnings, and a burning oil smell.
    *   Solution: Replacing the affected seals or gaskets.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1000+, depending on the location and severity of the leak.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Turbocharger Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Premature turbocharger failure can lead to a loss of power, excessive smoke from the exhaust, and unusual noises from the engine.
    *   Solution: Replacing the faulty turbocharger(s).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,500 - $5,000+ (including labor).
    *   Recalls: None Found

**3.8L Twin-Turbocharged V8 (572 hp)**

*   **Engine Misfires**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Engine misfires can occur due to faulty ignition coils or spark plugs, resulting in rough idling, reduced power, and the check engine light illuminating.
    *   Solution: Replacing the faulty ignition coils or spark plugs.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**8-Speed Automatic Transmission**

*   **Rough Shifting**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported rough or delayed shifting, particularly during low-speed maneuvers.
    *   Solution: Software updates, transmission fluid flush, or valve body replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $2000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The infotainment system may experience glitches, freezes, or connectivity problems (e.g., Bluetooth, Apple CarPlay/Android Auto).
    *   Solution: Software updates, system reset, or component replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 (software update) - $1,500+ (component replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Sensor Malfunctions**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Malfunctioning sensors (e.g., parking sensors, blind-spot monitoring sensors) can lead to false alerts or non-functionality.
    *   Solution: Replacing the faulty sensor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+ per sensor.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Sunroof/Moonroof Problems**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Issues such as sticking, failure to open or close properly, or water leaks around the sunroof/moonroof seal.
    *   Solution: Cleaning and lubricating the tracks, seal replacement, or sunroof motor replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $1,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

While the 2024 Maserati Ghibli offers an attractive design and impressive performance, potential buyers should be aware of potential reliability issues. Engine oil leaks, turbocharger problems, and rough shifting are among the more significant concerns. Electrical glitches and interior issues are also possibilities. The cost of repairs can vary widely depending on the specific issue, ranging from a few hundred dollars for minor fixes to several thousand for more extensive repairs like turbocharger replacement. Due to the complexity of these vehicles, and the limited data on the new 2024 model, it's essential to consider a pre-purchase inspection by a qualified mechanic familiar with Maserati vehicles.
