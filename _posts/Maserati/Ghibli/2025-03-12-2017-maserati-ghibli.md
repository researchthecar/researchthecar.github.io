The 2017 Maserati Ghibli is a luxury sedan that blends Italian style with performance. It was offered with two engine options: a 3.0-liter twin-turbocharged V6 producing either 345 horsepower and 369 lb-ft of torque or 404 horsepower and 406 lb-ft of torque and a 3.0-liter twin-turbocharged V6 producing 404 horsepower and 406 lb-ft of torque. All engines are paired with an 8-speed automatic transmission. Owners often praise the Ghibli's distinctive styling, luxurious interior, and sporty handling. However, some common complaints include reliability issues, particularly with electronics, and higher maintenance costs compared to other luxury sedans.

**Engine**

**3.0L Twin-Turbocharged V6 (345 hp/369 lb-ft & 404 hp/406 lb-ft)**

*   **Oil Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks, often originating from the valve cover gaskets or oil cooler lines. Noticeable oil spots under the vehicle or a burning oil smell may indicate this issue.
    *   Solution: Replacing the leaking gaskets or lines.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,500+ depending on the source and labor rates.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Coolant Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaks may occur from the water pump, thermostat housing, or radiator. Overheating and low coolant levels are common symptoms.
    *   Solution: Repairing or replacing the faulty parts.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+ depending on the source and labor rates.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**8-Speed Automatic**

*   **Rough Shifting/Hesitation**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Harsh or delayed shifting, particularly between certain gears. Some owners have reported hesitation during acceleration.
    *   Solution: A software update for the transmission control module (TCM) might resolve the issue. In some cases, replacing the valve body or rebuilding the transmission may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 (Software Update) to $4,000+ (Valve Body/Rebuild)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **No Issues Found**

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The infotainment system can be prone to glitches, including freezing, lagging, or failure to respond to inputs. Connectivity issues with Bluetooth and Apple CarPlay/Android Auto are also reported.
    *   Solution: Software updates may resolve some issues. In more severe cases, the entire infotainment unit may need replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 (Software Update) to $2,000+ (Unit Replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Sensor Failures**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Various sensor failures, including those related to the tire pressure monitoring system (TPMS), parking sensors, and other driver-assistance features. Warning lights on the dashboard indicate these issues.
    *   Solution: Replacing the faulty sensor(s).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+ per sensor, depending on the specific sensor and labor rates.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Battery Drain**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Premature battery drain is a common complaint. The car may struggle to start or require frequent jump-starts.
    *   Solution: Replacing the battery with a higher-quality aftermarket battery. Also, ensure all electrical systems are shutting down properly when the car is off.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Door Handle Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Door handles may fail to operate properly, getting stuck or requiring multiple attempts to open the door.
    *   Solution: Replacing the door handle assembly.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Sunroof Malfunction**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The sunroof may get stuck, make unusual noises when opening or closing, or fail to seal properly, leading to water leaks.
    *   Solution: Repairing or replacing the sunroof mechanism.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Power Window Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The power windows may operate slowly, get stuck, or fail to operate at all.
    *   Solution: Replacing the window motor or regulator.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $700+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2017 Maserati Ghibli, while offering a luxurious driving experience and distinctive Italian styling, does suffer from reliability concerns. Electrical issues, particularly with the infotainment system and sensors, are common. Engine oil leaks and transmission problems can also be costly to repair. Overall, potential buyers should be aware of these issues and factor in higher maintenance costs when considering purchasing a 2017 Ghibli. While minor sensor issues can be resolved for a few hundred dollars, significant problems like transmission rebuilds or infotainment system replacements can easily exceed several thousand dollars.
