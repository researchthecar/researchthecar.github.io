# 2017 Maserati Ghibli Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used luxury sport sedan, researching owner-reported feedback helps highlight potential mechanical and electrical quirks to look out for during a test drive. While these vehicles offer striking Italian styling and strong performance, prospective owners should pay close attention to powertrain behavior, brake responsiveness, and electrical accessories.

---

## Engine

### 3.0L Twin-Turbo V6

Equipped with a Ferrari-derived 3.0-liter twin-turbocharged V6 offering robust horsepower ratings, this engine delivers brisk performance. Buyers should ensure routine maintenance was strictly followed to preserve longevity and proper turbo operation.

**Acceleration Hesitation and Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers report that the vehicle may hesitate, nearly stall, or experience delayed acceleration when attempting to pull away from a stop sign or red light.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified technician inspect the throttle body, fuel delivery system, and engine control module for software updates or failing components.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

Utilizing a smooth-shifting 8-speed automatic paired with rear-wheel or all-wheel drive, this transmission generally delivers refined gear changes. Owners should verify smooth shifting behavior across all modes during a pre-purchase inspection.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

The braking system provides strong stopping power when operating correctly, but potential buyers should thoroughly check pedal feel and vacuum pump history.

**Intermittent Hard Brake Pedal**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers experience an unexpectedly hard brake pedal and temporary inability to stop the vehicle safely, often linked to vacuum booster pump performance.
- **Solution:** Inspect the brake vacuum pump and associated lines, replacing worn components as necessary.

## Electrical

### Infotainment and Convenience Electronics

Cabin electronics feature advanced infotainment interfaces, though cabin and accessory wiring warrants careful testing to prevent annoying glitches.

**Intermittent Audio Loss**
- **Symptoms:** Owners note that sound from the speakers may cut out completely despite the infotainment screen and GPS functioning normally, sometimes occurring after rainy weather.
- **Solution:** Check amplifier connections and software firmware for potential glitches or moisture intrusion.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V173000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Engine](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Maserati&model=Ghibli&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** Due to an engine software problem, at slow speeds such as stop-and-go traffic, the transmission may unexpectedly shift into neutral or park, or the engine may shut off. If the transmission shifts into neutral or park unexpectedly, or if the engine shuts down without warning, it can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Maserati will notify owners, and dealers will re-flash the software for the engine control module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V856000: Power Train:Driveline:Differential Unit](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Maserati&model=Ghibli&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** The rear differential unit pinion nut may not have been properly tightened, allowing the pinion nut to loosen. A loose pinion gear may bind, possibly resulting in the rear wheels locking up and increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Maserati will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the rear differential unit pinion nut torque, and, as necessary, replace the rear differential unit, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V840000: Tires:Sidewall](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Maserati&model=Ghibli&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** A loss of air may result in tire failure or a loss of vehicle control, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Maserati will notify owners, and dealers will replace the affected tires, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V839000: Back Over Prevention: Camera System](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Maserati&model=Ghibli&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** If the back-up camera is inoperable, it may be more difficult for the operator to see people or objects behind the vehicle, increasing the risk of injury or crash.
- **What to do:** Maserati will notify owners, and dealers will update the ETM software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V046000: Seats:Front Assembly:Power Adjust](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Maserati&model=Ghibli&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** Adjusting the front seats may cause the seat wiring harness to rub, possibly resulting in an electrical short. An electrical short can increase the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Maserati will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the vehicles, replacing the seat wiring harness, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

Overall, evaluating a pre-owned model requires careful inspection of service records, particularly checking for smooth engine acceleration, proper brake booster function, and fully operational electronics. Conducting a thorough pre-purchase inspection with a marque specialist will help uncover hidden issues and ensure a more confident ownership experience.
