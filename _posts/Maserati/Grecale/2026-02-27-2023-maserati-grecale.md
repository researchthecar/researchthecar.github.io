# 2023 Maserati Grecale Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2023 Maserati Grecale brings Italian flair and luxury performance to the compact luxury SUV segment, sharing its underlying architecture with Alfa Romeo. As a relatively new luxury model, it offers engaging dynamics and high-end tech, but prospective used-car buyers should navigate initial model-year complexities carefully. While owner reports to safety regulators are limited, early adopters have highlighted a few electronic and mechanical quirks worth monitoring during a pre-purchase inspection.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4 Mild Hybrid

This 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine utilizes mild-hybrid technology, producing around 296 horsepower in GT trim or 325 horsepower in Modena trim. It provides responsive acceleration for daily driving while incorporating modern electrification to optimize efficiency. Buyers should ensure all software updates and hybrid system modules are fully up to date.

### 3.0L Twin-Turbo V6 (Trofeo)

Derived from the MC20's Nettuno engine, this high-performance 3.0-liter twin-turbocharged V6 pumps out an impressive 523 horsepower. It transforms the Grecale into a high-performance machine with track-ready capabilities. Maintenance on this complex powerplant can be demanding, so a thorough professional inspection is strongly recommended.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed ZF Automatic Transmission

The Grecale uses a smooth-shifting 8-speed ZF automatic transmission paired with standard all-wheel drive across all trims. It delivers quick gear changes and highway cruising efficiency. Overall, this gearbox has a strong reputation for durability in various luxury applications.

### Gear Selector and Park Mechanism

Electrical and powertrain control modules manage critical functions like gear selection, vehicle speed sensing, and auxiliary luxury features.

**Failure to Engage Park**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may fail to shift into Park, displaying dashboard warnings indicating the car is moving too fast even when completely stopped.
- **Solution:** Have a dealership inspect the electronic gear selector module and software logic for potential updates or component replacement.

### Engine Cooling and Powertrain Management

Engine management and cooling subsystems monitor thermal regulation and power delivery to prevent overheating and performance drop-offs.

**Motive Power Loss and Coolant Warning**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may suddenly lose driving power while underway, accompanied by an illuminated coolant warning light on the instrument cluster.
- **Solution:** Have a certified technician diagnose the cooling system, sensors, and powertrain control unit for fault codes or component failure.

## Exterior

### Door Locking System

The body control and keyless entry systems oversee automated locks and security features.

**Unintentional Auto-Locking**
- **Symptoms:** The doors may automatically lock by themselves while keys are left inside the cabin or while loading cargo and passengers.
- **Solution:** Consult dealership service bulletins and software calibrations to address keyless entry and body control module anomalies.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V892000: Back Over Prevention:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Maserati&model=Grecale&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 111, "Rear Visibility." An inoperative rearview camera display reduces the driver's rear visibility, increasing the risk of injury or crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the CVPAM software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V098000: Back Over Prevention:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Maserati&model=Grecale&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 111, "Rear Visibility." A rearview camera image that does not display decreases the driver's visibility, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 25V722000: Exterior Lighting:Headlights](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Maserati&model=Grecale&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 108, "Lamp, Reflective Devices and Associated Equipment." Improperly aimed headlights can result in insufficient illumination of the road, reducing the driver's visibility, and increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Maserati North America will inspect and, if necessary, install one or two caps to cover the horizontal headlight aim adjustment mechanism, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2023 Maserati Grecale is an enticing pre-owned luxury SUV that combines striking styling with strong performance options. A comprehensive pre-purchase inspection focusing on electronics, software calibrations, and powertrain health will help ensure a satisfying ownership experience.
