# 2024 Maserati Grecale Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2024 Maserati Grecale enters the luxury compact SUV market with striking Italian styling and spirited performance options. As a relatively new model in the luxury segment, prospective used-car buyers should weigh its dynamic driving characteristics against the typical complexities associated with modern high-end European vehicles.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4 Mild Hybrid

This 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine utilizes mild-hybrid technology to produce approximately 296 hp (GT) or 325 hp (Modena). It offers a balance of efficiency and punchy performance suited for daily luxury driving.

### 3.0L Twin-Turbocharged V6

Borrowed from the MC20 supercar, the Nettuno 3.0-liter twin-turbo V6 in the Trofeo trim pumps out around 523 horsepower. It delivers thrilling acceleration and a distinct exhaust note for enthusiasts seeking high performance.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed ZF Automatic Transmission

This ZF-sourced eight-speed automatic transmission routes power through an all-wheel-drive system across all trims. It provides quick, smooth gear changes whether cruising comfortably or driving aggressively.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V892000: Back Over Prevention:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Maserati&model=Grecale&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 111, "Rear Visibility." An inoperative rearview camera display reduces the driver's rear visibility, increasing the risk of injury or crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the CVPAM software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V098000: Back Over Prevention:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Maserati&model=Grecale&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 111, "Rear Visibility." A rearview camera image that does not display decreases the driver's visibility, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 25V722000: Exterior Lighting:Headlights](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Maserati&model=Grecale&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 108, "Lamp, Reflective Devices and Associated Equipment." Improperly aimed headlights can result in insufficient illumination of the road, reducing the driver's visibility, and increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Maserati North America will inspect and, if necessary, install one or two caps to cover the horizontal headlight aim adjustment mechanism, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.
