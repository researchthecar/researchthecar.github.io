# 2021 Maserati Quattroporte Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2021 Maserati Quattroporte, buyers are stepping into a world of Italian luxury, striking exhaust notes, and executive styling. However, luxury Italian sedans demand diligent maintenance and a careful pre-purchase inspection to ensure all onboard systems and interior materials are holding up well over time. Reviewing owner experiences can help highlight specific trouble spots to watch for during your test drive.

---

## Engine

### 3.0L Twin-Turbo V6

The base 3.0-liter twin-turbocharged V6 engine develops around 345 to 424 horsepower depending on the trim level, delivering brisk acceleration and a characteristic Italian exhaust note. Proper maintenance and timely fluid changes are critical to ensuring long-term reliability for this performance powerplant.

### 3.8L Twin-Turbo V8

The available 3.8-liter twin-turbocharged V8 engine generates approximately 580 horsepower in Trofeo specification, offering immense performance and effortless highway passing power. Buyers considering this high-output engine should budget for premium maintenance costs and verify service records.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed ZF Automatic Transmission

An 8-speed ZF automatic transmission routes power smoothly to either the rear wheels or the Q4 intelligent all-wheel-drive system. This gearbox is widely regarded as responsive and durable when properly maintained.

## Interior

### Dashboard Trim

The cabin features high-end materials, premium leather upholstery, and advanced electronics typical of a flagship luxury sedan.

**Leather Dashboard Separation**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have reported that the leather covering on the dashboard can begin to separate and lift upward near the windshield.
- **Solution:** Have the dashboard inspected for adhesive failure or warping; repair or replacement of the dash assembly may be required by a specialized upholsterer or dealer.

## Safety

### Rear Seat Belts

Advanced safety features and passive restraint systems are standard, though individual comfort and fit should be verified during inspection.

**Rear Seat Belt Positioning**
- **Symptoms:** Rear seat occupants have noted that the shoulder belt sits too high or awkwardly behind the shoulder, potentially resting against the neck.
- **Solution:** Test the rear seating position during a pre-purchase inspection to ensure passengers can wear the seat belt comfortably and safely without improper neck contact.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V872000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Hoses, Lines/Piping, And Fittings](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Maserati&model=Quattroporte&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** A fuel leak may cause an engine stall, increasing the risk of a crash. A fuel leak also increases the risk of fire in the presence of an ignition source.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the fuel line sensor housing, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V703000: Seat Belts](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Maserati&model=Quattroporte&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 208, "Occupant Crash Protection." An unsecured child restraint system can increase the risk of injury during a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the seat belt assemblies, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V613000: Tires](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Maserati&model=Quattroporte&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 110, "Tire Selection and Rims." Tires that are inflated to the incorrect pressure could lead to a loss of vehicle stability and control, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the placard and update the tire pressure monitoring system, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V269000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Hoses, Lines/Piping, And Fittings](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Maserati&model=Quattroporte&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** A fuel leak may cause an engine stall, increasing the risk of a crash. A fuel leak also increases the risk of fire in the presence of an ignition source.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the fuel line sensor housing, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V098000: Back Over Prevention:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Maserati&model=Quattroporte&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 111, "Rear Visibility." A rearview camera image that does not display decreases the driver's visibility, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2021 Maserati Quattroporte remains a distinct and stylish choice for luxury sedan buyers, offering powerful engine options and an opulent cabin. While reported owner complaints are relatively sparse, prospective buyers should thoroughly inspect interior trim elements like the dashboard and verify rear passenger comfort. A comprehensive pre-purchase inspection by a knowledgeable technician is strongly recommended before finalizing your purchase.
