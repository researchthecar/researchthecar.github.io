# 2022 Maserati Quattroporte Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2022 Maserati Quattroporte brings unmistakable Italian styling and thrilling performance to the luxury flagship sedan segment. Buyers looking at a used model will find a vehicle defined by its exotic character and powerful engine lineup, though prospective owners should always budget for the higher maintenance costs typical of high-end European luxury cars.

---

## Engine

### 3.0L Twin-Turbo V6

This 3.0-liter twin-turbocharged V6 produces approximately 345 to 424 horsepower, offering robust acceleration and a stirring exhaust note. Overall, it serves as a sophisticated powerplant requiring diligent adherence to scheduled maintenance.

### 3.8L Twin-Turbo V8

The high-performance 3.8-liter twin-turbocharged V8 pumps out around 580 horsepower in Trofeo specification, delivering blistering straight-line speed. It is an exotic engine that rewards enthusiastic driving while demanding meticulous upkeep.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed ZF Automatic

This smooth-shifting 8-speed automatic transmission routes power efficiently to either the rear wheels or the intelligent Q4 all-wheel-drive system. It is widely regarded as a responsive and mechanically robust unit.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V269000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Hoses, Lines/Piping, And Fittings](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Maserati&model=Quattroporte&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** A fuel leak may cause an engine stall, increasing the risk of a crash. A fuel leak also increases the risk of fire in the presence of an ignition source.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the fuel line sensor housing, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V098000: Back Over Prevention:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Maserati&model=Quattroporte&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 111, "Rear Visibility." A rearview camera image that does not display decreases the driver's visibility, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.
