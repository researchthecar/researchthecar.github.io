**Introduction to the 2017 Maserati GranTurismo**
The 2017 Maserati GranTurismo is a high-performance luxury coupe known for its sleek styling, powerful engine, and comfortable interior. It is available with a choice of two V8 engine options, and a 6-speed automatic transmission. The 4.2L V8 produces 444 horsepower and 376 lb-ft of torque, while the 4.7L V8 produces 454 horsepower and 384 lb-ft of torque. The GranTurismo has received praise for its handling, performance, and luxurious interior. However, there have been some reported issues with the vehicle that owners and potential buyers should be aware of.

**Engine**
- **4.2L V8**
    - **Timing chain tensioner failure:** Symptoms include a rattling noise from the engine, especially at idle. The tensioner can fail, causing the timing chain to skip, which can lead to catastrophic engine damage. 
    Solution: Replace the timing chain tensioner. 
    Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,000+
    Recalls: None Found

- **4.7L V8**
    - **Intake manifold failure:** Symptoms include a loss of power, rough idling, and poor fuel economy. The intake manifold can crack or warp, causing vacuum leaks and other issues. 
    Solution: Replace the intake manifold. 
    Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
    Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**
- **6-speed automatic:**
    - **Torque converter failure:** Symptoms include shuddering or slipping during gear changes, and difficulty engaging gears. The torque converter can fail due to excessive heat or wear. 
    Solution: Replace the torque converter. 
    Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,500 - $4,000+
    Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**
- **Front suspension:**
    - **Strut mount bearing failure:** Symptoms include clunking or rattling noises from the front suspension, especially over bumps or uneven surfaces. The strut mount bearing can wear out or fail, causing the strut to move excessively. 
    Solution: Replace the strut mount bearing. 
    Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**
- **Battery drain:** Symptoms include difficulty starting the car, or a dead battery after sitting for a period of time. The battery can drain due to a parasitic draw from various electrical components. 
    Solution: Diagnose and repair the source of the parasitic draw. 
    Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**
- **Headlight condensation:** Symptoms include moisture or condensation inside the headlight assemblies. This can occur due to a faulty seal or ventilation system. 
    Solution: Replace the headlight assembly or repair the seal. 
    Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
    Recalls: None Found

**Interior**
- **Infotainment system:** Symptoms include freezing or unresponsive touch screen, software glitches, and connectivity issues. The infotainment system can malfunction due to software bugs or hardware failures. 
    Solution: Update the software or replace the infotainment unit. 
    Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+
    Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2017 Maserati GranTurismo is a desirable luxury coupe, but potential buyers should be aware of some common issues that have been reported by owners. The engine, transmission, suspension, electrical, and interior components can all experience problems that can be costly to repair. It is important to factor these potential issues into the decision-making process when considering the purchase of a GranTurismo.
