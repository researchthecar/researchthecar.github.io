# 2018 Maserati GranTurismo Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2018 Maserati GranTurismo delivers exotic Italian styling and a sonorous naturally aspirated V8 engine experience for used luxury car buyers. As a high-performance grand tourer, prospective owners should carefully weigh its evocative character against the specialized maintenance and upkeep inherent to exotic ownership.

---

## Engine

### 4.7L V8

Equipped with a Ferrari-derived 4.7-liter naturally aspirated V8 producing approximately 454 horsepower and 384 lb-ft of torque. This Ferrari-built powerplant offers thrilling exhaust notes and robust high-rpm performance, though routine servicing demands specialized technician expertise.

## Transmission

### 6-speed ZF Automatic Transmission

Features a conventional 6-speed ZF torque-converter automatic transmission paired with rear-wheel drive. This unit provides smooth everyday shifting dynamics and generally avoids the higher clutch-wear tendencies of older automated manual setups.

---

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V461000: Air Bags](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Maserati&model=GranTurismo&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** The air bag Electronic Control Unit (ECU) may have a defective power supply capacitor that can cause a deactivation or inadvertent deployment of the air bag. A deactivated air bag increases the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Maserati will notify owners, and dealers will replace the air bag ECU, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2018 Maserati GranTurismo remains a compelling choice for enthusiasts seeking dramatic grand touring flair paired with a charismatic V8 engine. Buyers should prioritize thorough pre-purchase inspections by marque specialists and budget adequately for premium maintenance.
