# 2019 Maserati Levante

### Overview
The 2019 Maserati Levante, a luxury SUV, offers a blend of performance and Italian styling. While generally appreciated for its driving dynamics and premium interior, like many high-performance vehicles, it can present specific reliability concerns. This analysis focuses on documented technical issues beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential problem areas for owners and prospective buyers. We will examine the engine, transmission, suspension, electrical systems, exterior, and interior components to offer a comprehensive reliability picture.

---


## Engine

### 3.0L Twin-Turbocharged V6 (Ghibli/Quattroporte derived)
**Oil Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Visible oil spots under the vehicle, low oil level warnings, burning oil smell.
- **Solution:** Replacement of seals, gaskets, or oil pan.
- **Cost:** $300 - $700

**Turbocharger Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of power, whistling noises from the engine bay, increased exhaust smoke.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of turbocharger unit(s).
- **Cost:** $1500 - $3000

## Transmission

### 8-Speed ZF Automatic
**Harsh Shifting / Jerking**
- **Symptoms:** Noticeable clunks or hesitations during gear changes, especially at lower speeds.
- **Solution:** Software updates, transmission fluid flush and fill, or internal component repair.
- **Cost:** $400 - $1200

**Transmission Fluid Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Reddish fluid under the vehicle, slipping gears, warning lights.
- **Solution:** Replacement of seals, transmission pan, or cooler lines.
- **Cost:** $300 - $800


## Suspension

### Air Suspension System
**Air Spring Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle sagging on one or more corners, compressor running excessively, uneven ride height.
- **Solution:** Replacement of leaking air springs or seals.
- **Cost:** $600 - $1500 per corner

**Compressor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Inability to adjust ride height, constant compressor noise, suspension warning lights.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the air suspension compressor.
- **Cost:** $700 - $1300


## Electrical

### Infotainment System
**Screen Freezing / Unresponsiveness**
- **Symptoms:** Touchscreen becomes unresponsive, system reboots randomly, features not working.
- **Solution:** Software updates, system reset, or replacement of the head unit.
- **Cost:** $500 - $2000


### Battery Drain
**Intermittent Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Dead battery after short periods of inactivity, electrical components malfunctioning.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis of parasitic draw, replacement of faulty modules or battery.
- **Cost:** $200 - $800


## Exterior

### Door Handles
**Sticking or Malfunctioning Pop-Out Handles**
- **Symptoms:** Handles fail to deploy or retract, difficulty opening doors.
- **Solution:** Lubrication, adjustment, or replacement of the handle mechanism.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600

## Interior

### Leather Upholstery
**Premature Wear / Cracking**
- **Symptoms:** Visible cracking or excessive wear on seats, especially driver's seat, despite moderate use.
- **Solution:** Professional cleaning and conditioning, or replacement of affected panels.
- **Cost:** $200 - $800

### Summary Verdict
The 2019 Maserati Levante, while offering a compelling driving experience, exhibits some common luxury SUV reliability concerns. The powertrain, particularly the V6 engine and automatic transmission, can experience issues like oil leaks, turbo problems, and transmission shifting anomalies. The air suspension system is a notable area for potential expense, with leaks and compressor failures being reported. Electrical gremlins, especially with the infotainment system and intermittent battery drain, also require attention. Exterior and interior components, while generally well-built, can show wear, with door handles and leather upholstery being occasional points of concern. Regular maintenance and prompt attention to warning signs are crucial for mitigating potential repair costs.
