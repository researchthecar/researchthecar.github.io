# 2017 Maserati Levante Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used luxury performance SUV like the Maserati Levante, buyers are naturally drawn to its striking Italian styling and engaging driving dynamics. However, ownership requires careful inspection of the vehicle's powertrain and electronic systems. While owner-submitted complaints to safety agencies highlight certain recurring mechanical and electrical quirks, understanding these potential pain points helps prospective buyers navigate negotiations and pre-purchase inspections more effectively.

---

## Engine

### 3.0L Twin-Turbo V6 (Base & S)

The 3.0-liter twin-turbocharged V6 engine delivers robust performance ranging from 345 to 424 horsepower, offering strong acceleration. Buyers should evaluate engine smoothness and service history carefully during a pre-purchase inspection.

**Unintended Neutral Shift**
- **Symptoms:** While slowing down, coasting, or accelerating slowly at low speeds, the vehicle may unexpectedly shift into neutral, creating a sudden loss of drive control.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified technician inspect software calibrations and transmission control modules for updates or fault codes.

**Stalling or Shifter Defect**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have reported instances where the vehicle's RPM fluctuates unexpectedly when coming to a stop, occasionally resulting in the engine dying.
- **Solution:** Inspect throttle bodies, idle air control systems, and related electrical sensors, and check for applicable manufacturer service campaigns.

## Transmission

### ZF 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

Paired with standard all-wheel drive, the ZF 8-speed automatic transmission provides quick and smooth gear changes. It is generally robust, though proper fluid maintenance is essential for long-term reliability.

## Suspension

### Axle and Suspension Components

**Axle Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Unusual loud noises from the undercarriage followed by a sudden loss of vehicle control and drivetrain failure.
- **Solution:** Inspect half-shafts, CV joints, and suspension mounting points thoroughly during every routine service interval.

## Brakes

### Parking Brake

**Random Parking Brake Engagement**
- **Symptoms:** The electronic parking brake may unexpectedly engage while coming to a stop or driving at very low speeds.
- **Solution:** Check electronic parking brake actuators, switches, and associated control modules for faults or calibration errors.

### Service Brakes

**Brake Responsiveness Delay**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have noted instances of delayed brake pedal response or unusual noise when attempting to slow down at low speeds.
- **Solution:** Inspect brake pads, rotors, hydraulic lines, and the anti-lock braking system (ABS) module for wear or hydraulic pressure issues.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V161000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Engine](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Maserati&model=Levante&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** Due to an engine software problem, at slow speeds such as stop-and-go traffic, the transmission may unexpectedly shift into neutral or the engine may shut off. If the transmission shifts into neutral unexpectedly or if the engine shuts down without warning, it can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Maserati will notify owners, and dealers will update the engine control module software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V839000: Back Over Prevention: Camera System](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Maserati&model=Levante&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** If the back-up camera is inoperable, it may be more difficult for the operator to see people or objects behind the vehicle, increasing the risk of injury or crash.
- **What to do:** Maserati will notify owners, and dealers will update the ETM software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V046000: Seats:Front Assembly:Power Adjust](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Maserati&model=Levante&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** Adjusting the front seats may cause the seat wiring harness to rub, possibly resulting in an electrical short. An electrical short can increase the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Maserati will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the vehicles, replacing the seat wiring harness, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.
