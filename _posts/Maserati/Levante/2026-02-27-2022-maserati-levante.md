# 2022 Maserati Levante Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2022 Maserati Levante delivers Italian styling and thrilling performance to the luxury midsize SUV segment, backed by a range of potent turbocharged engines. While owner feedback is generally limited, prospective buyers should carefully evaluate powertrain responsiveness and electronics during a test drive. As with any high-end European luxury vehicle, thorough maintenance history and a pre-purchase inspection are highly recommended before buying.

---

## Engine

### 3.0L Twin-Turbo V6 (GT / Modena)

This 3.0-liter twin-turbocharged V6 produces around 345 hp in the GT and 424 hp in the Modena, offering spirited acceleration for everyday driving. It provides a balanced blend of performance and luxury refinement, though routine maintenance is vital for long-term reliability.

**Throttle Hesitation and Jerking**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may hesitate and jerk momentarily when depressing the accelerator pedal after coming to a complete stop, without illuminating any warning lights.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified technician inspect the throttle body, fuel system, and engine control module for software updates or faults.

### 3.8L Twin-Turbo V8 (Modena S / Trofeo)

Powered by a formidable 3.8-liter twin-turbo V8 pushing up to 580 horsepower, this option transforms the Levante into a high-performance super-SUV. It delivers blistering straight-line speed accompanied by an aggressive exhaust note, requiring attentive ownership and meticulous upkeep.

## Transmission

### ZF 8-Speed Automatic

This widely praised 8-speed automatic transmission routes power smoothly through the standard all-wheel-drive system across all trims. It offers rapid, responsive shifts that complement the Levante's sporty driving dynamics.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V872000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Hoses, Lines/Piping, And Fittings](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Maserati&model=Levante&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** A fuel leak may cause an engine stall, increasing the risk of a crash. A fuel leak also increases the risk of fire in the presence of an ignition source.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the fuel line sensor housing, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V098000: Back Over Prevention:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Maserati&model=Levante&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 111, "Rear Visibility." A rearview camera image that does not display decreases the driver's visibility, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.
