Here's a breakdown of common issues and complaints reported by owners of the 2021 Maserati Levante, categorized by major components.

The 2021 Maserati Levante is a luxury SUV that blends Italian styling with performance and practicality. Engine options include a 3.0-liter twin-turbo V6 producing either 345 or 424 horsepower and 369 or 428 lb-ft of torque, respectively, and a 3.8-liter twin-turbo V8, delivering either 550 or 590 horsepower and 538 or 538 lb-ft of torque, respectively. All engines are paired with an 8-speed automatic transmission. People often praise the Levante for its distinctive design, powerful engine options, and engaging driving dynamics. Criticisms frequently revolve around reliability issues, higher maintenance costs compared to competitors, and infotainment system usability.

**Engine**

**3.0L Twin-Turbo V6**

*   **Oil Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks are reported from various locations on the engine, including valve cover gaskets and oil pan gasket. Symptoms can include visible oil spots under the vehicle, the smell of burning oil, and low oil levels.
    *   Solution: Replacing the leaking gaskets or seals.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Engine Misfires**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Engine misfires can occur due to faulty ignition coils or spark plugs. Symptoms may include rough idling, reduced power, and the check engine light illuminating.
    *   Solution: Replacing the faulty ignition coils or spark plugs.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**3.8L Twin-Turbo V8**

*   **Turbocharger Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported issues with the turbochargers, including reduced boost pressure and potential failure. Symptoms can include a loss of power, unusual noises from the engine bay, and the check engine light turning on.
    *   Solution: Replacing or rebuilding the turbochargers.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $3,000 - $6,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Coolant Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaks are noted, sometimes from hoses or connections near the turbos due to high heat. Visible coolant under the vehicle, low coolant levels, and overheating may occur.
    *   Solution: Replacing the leaking hoses or components.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**8-Speed Automatic (ZF 8HP)**

*   **Harsh Shifting**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported harsh or erratic shifting, particularly at lower speeds.
    *   Solution: Software updates to the transmission control module (TCM) or, in more severe cases, replacement of valve body components.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 (software) - $2,000+ (valve body)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Delayed Engagement**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Delays when shifting into Drive or Reverse, particularly when cold.
    *   Solution:  Fluid flush and software updates, but sometimes requires valve body replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 (fluid) - $2,000+ (valve body)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Air Suspension Problems**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Issues with the air suspension system, including uneven ride height, compressor failures, and leaks in the air struts. This leads to the vehicle not maintaining proper ride height or becoming stuck in a lowered position.
    *   Solution: Replacing the air struts, compressor, or addressing leaks in the air lines.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $3,000+ (per component)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Sensor Malfunctions**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Ride height sensors can malfunction, providing incorrect data to the suspension control module and resulting in an unstable ride.
    *   Solution: Replacing the malfunctioning sensor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $700+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Freezing/Crashing**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The infotainment system can be prone to freezing, crashing, and exhibiting slow response times. The screen may become unresponsive, or the system may reboot unexpectedly.
    *   Solution: Software updates or replacement of the infotainment unit.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $1,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Sensor Failures**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Multiple sensors can fail, including those for the TPMS, blind-spot monitoring system, and parking sensors.
    *   Solution: Replacing the faulty sensors.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+ (per sensor)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Battery Drain**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported excessive battery drain, leading to difficulty starting the vehicle or a completely dead battery.
    *   Solution: Identifying and repairing the parasitic drain, which could be caused by a faulty module or improperly installed aftermarket accessories.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Door Handle Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The electronic door handles can sometimes malfunction, failing to unlock or open the doors.
    *   Solution: Replacing the faulty door handle mechanism.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+ (per handle)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Sunroof Malfunctions**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The sunroof may experience issues with opening or closing, or may leak water.
    *   Solution: Repair or replacement of the sunroof motor or sealing components.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,200+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Dashboard Creaks and Rattles**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Various creaks and rattles emanating from the dashboard and trim pieces, especially on uneven road surfaces.
    *   Solution: Tightening loose components or applying sound-deadening materials. This is often a difficult and time-consuming process.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Power Seat Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Problems with the power seat adjustments, including motors failing or becoming stuck.
    *   Solution: Replacing the faulty seat motor or switch.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+ (per seat)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2021 Maserati Levante, while offering a luxurious driving experience, is not without its issues. Engine issues like oil leaks and turbocharger problems can be costly, especially on the V8 models. Transmission harshness is a common complaint, and electrical issues such as infotainment glitches and sensor failures can be frustrating. The air suspension also appears to be a potential source of expensive repairs. Overall, while some issues are minor, others can be quite severe and costly to address, making it important for potential buyers to thoroughly inspect the vehicle and consider an extended warranty.
