# 2022 Maserati MC20 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2022 Maserati MC20 marks a bold, exotic new chapter for the Italian brand, introducing a mid-engine carbon-fiber supercar powered by an innovative twin-turbo V6. Because this is an exotic, low-volume performance machine, prospective buyers should approach the used market with standard supercar expectations regarding specialized maintenance and complex electronics. While owner feedback in broader public logs remains sparse due to exclusive production numbers, understanding the powertrain options and potential ancillary quirks is essential for any exotic car buyer.

---

## Engine

### 3.0L Nettuno Twin-Turbo V6

The MC20 is powered by a 3.0-liter twin-turbocharged 'Nettuno' V6 engine utilizing F1-derived pre-chamber combustion technology, producing approximately 621 horsepower and 538 lb-ft of torque. This sophisticated powerplant delivers blistering performance, though its exotic engineering requires meticulous dealer maintenance.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Dual-Clutch Automatic

Power is sent to the rear wheels via an 8-speed dual-clutch automatic transmission that offers lightning-fast gearchanges suited for both track and street driving. The setup is generally robust, though dual-clutch systems demand careful driving habits in heavy traffic to minimize clutch wear.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V872000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Hoses, Lines/Piping, And Fittings](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Maserati&model=MC20&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** A fuel leak may cause an engine stall, increasing the risk of a crash. A fuel leak also increases the risk of fire in the presence of an ignition source.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the fuel line sensor housing, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V265000: Exterior Lighting:Tail Lights](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Maserati&model=MC20&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 108, "Lamps, Reflective Devices, and Associated Equipment." Flickering brake lights may confuse other drivers, which can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the tail lights, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V537000: Equipment:Other:Labels](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Maserati&model=MC20&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 110, "Tire Selection and Rims." A vehicle with an incorrect maximum capacity weight value may be overloaded, which can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will install corrected labels, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V098000: Back Over Prevention:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Maserati&model=MC20&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 111, "Rear Visibility." A rearview camera image that does not display decreases the driver's visibility, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2022 Maserati MC20 is an exhilarating exotic performer that trades mainstream predictability for high-end Italian flair and track-ready dynamics. Because comprehensive reliability data for ultra-luxury mid-engine sports cars is limited, buyers should prioritize examples with verified service histories and remaining factory coverage. Thorough pre-purchase inspections by a qualified exotic specialist are strongly advised before finalizing any purchase.
