# 2023 Cadillac LYRIQ Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used Cadillac Lyriq, buyers should weigh its striking design and modern EV capabilities against early-model electrical and software nuances. As an all-electric luxury SUV built on GM's Ultium platform, early models have seen owner reports centered around infotainment glitches, lighting behavior, and sensor integration.

---

## Engine

### Ultium Electric Drive (Single Motor RWD)

The single-motor rear-wheel-drive powertrain produces approximately 340 horsepower and 325 lb-ft of torque, offering responsive acceleration and strong efficiency. It serves as the base propulsion option for early Lyriq models with reliable daily performance.

### Ultium Electric Drive (Dual Motor AWD)

The dual-motor all-wheel-drive setup boosts output to around 500 horsepower, delivering enhanced traction and brisk acceleration. This configuration adds robust performance capability while maintaining smooth Ultium electric delivery.

## Transmission

### Single-Speed Direct-Drive Transmission

The single-speed direct-drive transmission routes electric motor power seamlessly to the wheels without traditional gear shifts. It operates quietly and reliably with minimal mechanical complexity.

## Brakes

### Regenerative and Hydraulic Brakes

Braking complaints focus on software-driven transitions between regenerative and friction brakes rather than mechanical brake wear.

**Regenerative Braking Reset**
- **Symptoms:** One-pedal driving modes or regenerative braking settings may unexpectedly reset or turn off, altering stopping behavior.
- **Solution:** Consult a dealer to inspect software calibrations and ensure brake module firmware is up to date.

## Exterior

### Exterior Lighting and Dashboard Dimmability

Owner feedback highlights occasional sensitivity with automatic lighting transitions and dashboard display brightness during twilight hours.

**Premature Instrument Panel Dimming**
- **Symptoms:** The dashboard and gauge cluster screens dim aggressively during late afternoon, dusk, or dawn, making speedometers and indicators difficult to read.
- **Solution:** Check for dealership software updates that recalibrate ambient light sensor thresholds.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V710000: Electrical System: Instrument Cluster/Panel](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Cadillac&model=LYRIQ&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** A software error in the driver video display control module may cause the instrument panel to go blank. A blank instrument panel will not show critical information, such as vehicle speed and warning lights, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** GM will update the video display control module software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V367000: Electrical System:Propulsion System:Traction Battery](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Cadillac&model=LYRIQ&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** A loss of drive power can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and, if necessary, replace the high-voltage battery pack, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V682000: Electrical System:Body Control Module:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Cadillac&model=LYRIQ&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** Without external warning sounds, pedestrians may be unaware of an approaching vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will reprogram the BCM, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V785000: Seat Belts:Rear/Other:Anchorage](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Cadillac&model=LYRIQ&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** An improperly welded seat belt bracket may not restrain an occupant as intended, increasing their risk of injury during a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the rear seat cushion frame, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V589000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Antilock/Traction Control/Electronic Limited Slip:Control Unit/Module](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Cadillac&model=LYRIQ&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** The anti-lock brake system (ABS) may activate unexpectedly and release brake pressure in the vehicle's service brake system. A release of brake pressure can cause a loss of braking ability, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 25V232000: Suspension:Front:Stabilizer Bar](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Cadillac&model=LYRIQ&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** Damaged cables or coolant lines can result in a vehicle shutdown, causing a loss of drive power and increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and tighten the left and/or right stabilizer bar bracket bolts, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V356000: Equipment:Electrical:Infotainment:Video (Touch)Screen/Monitor/Unit](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Cadillac&model=LYRIQ&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** A blank video display can result in a loss of critical safety information, such as the speedometer, warning lights, and rearview camera image, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The Cadillac Lyriq offers an appealing blend of luxury and electric performance, but early model years require careful inspection of software and electrical systems. Buyers should verify that all open recalls and infotainment updates have been completed by a certified dealer prior to purchase.
