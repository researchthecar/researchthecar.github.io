# 2024 Cadillac LYRIQ Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2024 Cadillac Lyriq marks Cadillac's bold entry into the all-electric luxury SUV market, featuring striking styling and an advanced Ultium-based powertrain. As an early model year for this platform, prospective buyers should approach with cautious optimism, keeping in mind that owners have reported a variety of initial software and electrical glitches. While it delivers impressive comfort and performance, thorough pre-purchase checks and verifying that all manufacturer recalls and updates have been completed are strongly advised.

---

## Engine

### Rear-Wheel Drive (RWD) Electric Drive Unit

The single permanent magnet synchronous motor powers the rear wheels, producing approximately 340 horsepower and 325 lb-ft of torque. This configuration provides smooth, efficient daily acceleration, though some early owners have experienced electrical and regenerative braking integration complaints.

### All-Wheel Drive (AWD) Dual-Motor Electric Drive Unit

The dual-motor setup adds a front induction motor to deliver all-wheel drive traction and bumps total output to around 500 horsepower. It offers brisk acceleration and strong passing power, representing a sophisticated powertrain that relies heavily on stable vehicle software.

## Transmission

### Single-Speed Direct-Drive Transmission

The vehicle utilizes a fixed-ratio, single-speed direct-drive gearbox tailored to each motor configuration. It delivers seamless, gearless power delivery typical of modern electric vehicles without traditional multi-speed shifting maintenance concerns.

## Brakes

### Anti-Lock Brakes and Regenerative System

The braking system blends regenerative braking with traditional friction brakes, though early software integration has occasionally led to owner complaints regarding transition smoothness and pedal feel.

**Brake Failure and Pedal Transition Concerns**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers report unexpected firmness, loss of regenerative braking feel, or inconsistent anti-lock braking performance.
- **Solution:** Have a certified dealer inspect the brake control module, ABS sensors, and software calibrations.

## Electrical

### Electrical System and Battery Management

The vehicle relies heavily on complex computerized architectures and high-voltage battery modules, making stable software updates crucial for everyday reliability.

**12-Volt Battery and Software Failures**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have documented total electrical loss, blank infotainment displays, charging failures, or high-voltage battery diagnostic codes.
- **Solution:** Ensure all manufacturer software updates are applied, and have the 12-volt battery and charging module tested by a certified technician.

## Exterior

### Exterior Lighting and Body

Exterior lighting assemblies integrate distinctive styling with advanced LED technology, though a small number of owners have encountered alignment or operational anomalies.

**Headlight Aiming and Functionality**
- **Symptoms:** Headlights may point upward incorrectly or experience performance issues during night driving.
- **Solution:** Have the dealership inspect headlight leveling sensors, alignment, and assembly wiring.

## Safety

### Forward Collision Avoidance

Advanced safety features like forward collision avoidance and automatic emergency braking offer high-tech protection, but can occasionally trigger false positives or unexpected activation.

**Unexpected Automatic Braking**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may experience sudden, unwarranted phantom braking events while driving at moderate speeds without obstacles present.
- **Solution:** Check for software updates or recall remedies related to the forward collision avoidance and automated emergency braking systems.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V785000: Seat Belts:Rear/Other:Anchorage](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Cadillac&model=LYRIQ&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** An improperly welded seat belt bracket may not restrain an occupant as intended, increasing their risk of injury during a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the rear seat cushion frame, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V320000: Electrical System:Wiring](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Cadillac&model=LYRIQ&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** A loss of drive power increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the rear drive unit, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V589000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Antilock/Traction Control/Electronic Limited Slip:Control Unit/Module](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Cadillac&model=LYRIQ&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** The anti-lock brake system (ABS) may activate unexpectedly and release brake pressure in the vehicle's service brake system. A release of brake pressure can cause a loss of braking ability, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 25V232000: Suspension:Front:Stabilizer Bar](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Cadillac&model=LYRIQ&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** Damaged cables or coolant lines can result in a vehicle shutdown, causing a loss of drive power and increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and tighten the left and/or right stabilizer bar bracket bolts, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V356000: Equipment:Electrical:Infotainment:Video (Touch)Screen/Monitor/Unit](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Cadillac&model=LYRIQ&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** A blank video display can result in a loss of critical safety information, such as the speedometer, warning lights, and rearview camera image, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 26V082000: Back Over Prevention:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Cadillac&model=LYRIQ&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** An inoperative rearview camera can reduce the driver's rear visibility, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2024 Cadillac Lyriq presents an attractive proposition for used EV buyers seeking luxury, advanced driver-assistance features, and bold design. However, as an introductory model year, it exhibits common early-production teething issues centered around software stability, electrical modules, and sensor-driven safety features like automatic emergency braking. Buyers should verify that all open campaigns and software patches have been performed at a certified dealership and perform a comprehensive test drive of all electronic systems before finalizing a purchase.
