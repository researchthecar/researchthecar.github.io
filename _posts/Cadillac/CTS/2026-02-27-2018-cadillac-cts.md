# 2018 Cadillac CTS Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2018 Cadillac CTS, buyers will find a luxury sport sedan offering sharp handling and powerful engine choices. While generally well-regarded for performance and styling, prospective owners should review available owner-reported feedback regarding electronics, powertrain operation, and certain accessory components.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4

The 2.0L turbocharged inline-4 engine produces approximately 268 horsepower and 295 lb-ft of torque, providing brisk acceleration for daily driving. It serves as the standard powerplant for the lineup, balancing efficiency with responsive turbocharged performance.

### 3.6L V6

The naturally aspirated 3.6L V6 engine delivers around 335 horsepower and 285 lb-ft of torque, offering smooth and linear power delivery. It is a popular upgrade for buyers seeking stronger mid-range performance.

**Engine Shaking and Vibration**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may experience sudden shaking, vibration, or potential misfire sensations when lightly applying the gas pedal at cruising speeds.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified mechanic inspect the ignition system, fuel injectors, and powertrain control module for fault codes or component failure.

### 3.6L Twin-Turbocharged V6 (V-Sport)

Exclusive to the high-performance V-Sport trim, the 3.6L twin-turbo V6 pushes out roughly 420 horsepower and 430 lb-ft of torque. This potent engine transforms the sedan into a serious performance machine, though it demands diligent maintenance.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 8-speed automatic transmission pairs with most engine options to deliver quick ratios and optimized fuel economy. Buyers should thoroughly test-drive this unit to ensure smooth gear shifts.

**Transmission Shudder and Rough Shifting**
- **Symptoms:** The transmission may exhibit an intermittent shudder, rough or jerky gear changes, and unpredictable behavior without advance warning.
- **Solution:** Diagnostic evaluation by a professional is recommended to check transmission fluid condition, software calibration, and internal mechanical components.

### Rear Differential

The rear-wheel-drive and available all-wheel-drive hardware provide engaging driving dynamics, though rear differential components should be inspected for leaks or abnormal noise during a pre-purchase check.

**Rear Differential Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may notice loud noises from the rear of the vehicle accompanied by fluid leaks, which can potentially lead to mechanical lockup.
- **Solution:** Inspection and replacement of the rear differential unit by a certified service center.

## Electrical

### CUE Infotainment and Display Panel

Cabin electronics and infotainment features offer modern connectivity but can occasionally suffer from display panel blemishes or accessory feature glitches.

**Screen Delamination and Cracking**
- **Symptoms:** The CUE display panel or backup display screen may develop strange web-like interior defects or crack spontaneously.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the infotainment screen assembly is typically required to resolve display distortion or physical panel failure.

**Auto Start/Stop System Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** The automatic engine start/stop feature may fail repeatedly during vehicle operation.
- **Solution:** Have the starting and charging system, battery sensors, and control modules tested by a technician.

## Safety

### Air Bag System

Standard safety equipment is designed to protect occupants, though isolated owner reports indicate potential system faults during severe accidents.

**Failure to Deploy**
- **Symptoms:** Air bags may fail to deploy during significant frontal impact collisions.
- **Solution:** Professional diagnostic inspection of the airbag sensors, wiring harnesses, and Restraints Control Module (RCM).

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V086000: Steering:Electric Power Assist System](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Cadillac&model=CTS&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** Loss of power steering assist would require a higher steering effort, especially at lower speeds, which may increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will replace the power steering gear assembly, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V175000: Steering:Electric Power Assist System](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Cadillac&model=CTS&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** A loss of power steering assist can require greater steering effort, especially at low speeds, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the power steering gear assembly, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2018 Cadillac CTS is an appealing choice for used-car shoppers desiring distinctive luxury styling and robust performance options. While major mechanical failures are relatively infrequent, potential buyers should pay close attention to transmission shift quality, infotainment screen condition, and rear differential health during a pre-purchase inspection.
