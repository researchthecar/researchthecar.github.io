# 2017 Cadillac CTS Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2017 Cadillac CTS, buyers will find a stylish and engaging midsize luxury sedan that offers sharp handling and strong performance options. However, prospective owners should be aware of a few recurring mechanical and electronic issues reported by drivers, particularly concerning powertrain refinement, infotainment reliability, and occasional steering or differential concerns. A thorough pre-purchase inspection focusing on the transmission, electronics, and suspension can help uncover potential hidden costs before you buy.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged I4

The 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine produces around 268 horsepower and 295 lb-ft of torque, offering a balanced mix of efficiency and responsive power for daily driving. It serves as the base powerplant for the CTS line.

### 3.6L V6

The naturally aspirated 3.6-liter V6 generates approximately 335 horsepower and 285 lb-ft of torque, providing smooth and robust acceleration. It is a popular step-up choice for buyers seeking traditional V6 performance.

### 6.2L Supercharged V8 (CTS-V)

The high-performance CTS-V features a supercharged 6.2-liter V8 pushing 640 horsepower and 630 lb-ft of torque for track-ready capability. It demands strict maintenance and careful scrutiny of its powertrain and cooling systems on the used market.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

An 8-speed automatic transmission handles gear selection across most non-V models, paired with either rear-wheel or all-wheel drive. While generally smooth, some owners report rough shifting behaviors that may require software intervention.

**Rough Shifting and Jerking**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers experience harsh shifting between 1st and 2nd gears, as well as intermittent jerking during highway driving.
- **Solution:** Dealerships have addressed this with transmission fluid flushes and software reprogramming updates.

### 8-Speed High-Performance Automatic Transmission (CTS-V)

The CTS-V utilizes a specialized high-performance 8-speed automatic transmission designed to handle immense supercharged torque. It generally operates stoutly but requires attentive fluid service.

### Rear Differential

The rear-wheel-drive and all-wheel-drive systems transfer power efficiently, but rear differential failures have been reported by some owners as a costly out-of-pocket repair if outside warranty.

**Differential Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Unusual humming noises from the rear or total differential lockup can leave the vehicle stranded.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the rear differential unit.
- **Cost:** $4,200

## Suspension

### Wheels and Tires

The sport-tuned suspension setup provides excellent cornering agility, but low-profile tire combinations leave the vehicle vulnerable to pothole damage.

**Low-Profile Tire and Wheel Damage**
- **Symptoms:** Low-profile tires and certain factory wheel packages are prone to sidewall blowouts, cuts, and cracked rims when hitting minor road imperfections.
- **Solution:** Replace damaged tires and inspect wheels for structural cracks; consider switching to more durable tire profiles if road conditions are poor.

## Steering

### Electronic Power Steering

The electronic power steering system provides adjustable weight and responsive handling, though occasional module failures can cause a sudden loss of power assist.

**Loss of Power Steering Assist**
- **Symptoms:** Power steering failure can occur unexpectedly, making the steering wheel very heavy and difficult to turn.
- **Solution:** Inspection of the electric power steering (EPS) module and checking for applicable recall coverage or software updates is recommended.

## Electrical

### CUE Infotainment System

Cabin electronics heavily rely on the CUE touchscreen interface, which is prone to software sluggishness, freezing, and occasional physical cracking of the digitizer glass.

**Screen Freezing and Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** The central touchscreen becomes unresponsive, freezes entirely, or fails to connect to Bluetooth, GPS, and radio functions.
- **Solution:** Software updates or complete screen replacement may be required to restore full functionality.
- **Cost:** $125 deductible under warranty/class coverage

**Spontaneous Screen Cracking**
- **Symptoms:** The infotainment display glass develops fractures spontaneously without external impact, disabling touch controls.
- **Solution:** Replace the display unit assembly through an authorized dealer or service center.

## Safety

### Passenger Sensing and Airbag System

Occupant safety systems include multi-stage airbags and occupancy sensors, though intermittent faults in the passenger sensing seat mat can trigger warning lights.

**Intermittent Airbag Warning Light**
- **Symptoms:** The service airbag warning light illuminates periodically, and the passenger 'Airbag ON' indicator may fail to activate even with an adult occupant in the seat.
- **Solution:** Dealer diagnosis and replacement of faulty passenger sensing or module components.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V086000: Steering:Electric Power Assist System](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Cadillac&model=CTS&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** Loss of power steering assist would require a higher steering effort, especially at lower speeds, which may increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will replace the power steering gear assembly, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V175000: Steering:Electric Power Assist System](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Cadillac&model=CTS&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** A loss of power steering assist can require greater steering effort, especially at low speeds, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the power steering gear assembly, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2017 Cadillac CTS is a compelling choice for used luxury sedan buyers wanting sharp driving dynamics and strong engine choices. While major mechanical failures are relatively uncommon, shoppers should verify the health of the transmission, check for rear differential noises, inspect the CUE infotainment screen for responsiveness, and ensure all steering recalls have been addressed. Prioritize vehicles with comprehensive service history and remaining warranty coverage.
