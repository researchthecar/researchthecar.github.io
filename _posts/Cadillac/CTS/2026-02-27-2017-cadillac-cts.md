# 2017 Cadillac CTS

### Overview
The 2017 Cadillac CTS is a luxury sport sedan that offers a compelling blend of performance, comfort, and technology. Known for its sharp handling and refined interior, it competes in a segment with strong contenders. Reliability for the 2017 model year is generally considered good, with most owners reporting a positive experience. However, like any vehicle, there are specific areas that have seen more attention from repair shops. This analysis focuses on documented technical issues beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential concerns for owners and prospective buyers.

---


## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged I4
**Turbocharger Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of power, whistling noises, increased oil consumption.
- **Solution:** Turbocharger replacement or repair.
- **Cost:** $1,500 - $3,000

**Coolant Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Overheating, visible coolant puddles, sweet smell.
- **Solution:** Repair or replace leaking hoses, water pump, or radiator.
- **Cost:** $300 - $800


### 3.6L V6
**Timing Chain Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Rattling noise from the front of the engine, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of timing chain, tensioners, and guides.
- **Cost:** $1,200 - $2,500

### Summary Verdict
The 2017 Cadillac CTS generally demonstrates good reliability, particularly for its powertrain components. The most commonly cited issues tend to revolve around the turbocharger for the 2.0L engine and potential transmission hesitations, which can often be addressed with software updates or fluid services. Electrical gremlins, specifically with the infotainment system and occasional battery drain, are also noted but not pervasive. Owners who maintain their vehicles diligently and address any emerging issues promptly can expect a rewarding ownership experience with this luxury sedan.
