# 2016 Cadillac CTS Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2016 Cadillac CTS offers a sharp, engaging drive and a striking cabin, but prospective buyers should inspect it closely for specific mechanical and electronic quirks. While many owners enjoy its athletic handling and luxury features, owner feedback highlights recurring trouble spots that can require costly attention. Taking the time to thoroughly test drive and verify the condition of key subsystems can save you from unexpected headaches down the road.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged I4

This 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine produces approximately 268 horsepower and 295 lb-ft of torque, offering brisk acceleration for daily driving. It provides a balanced blend of efficiency and power, though regular maintenance is key to keeping the turbocharger and fueling systems healthy.

### 3.6L V6

The naturally aspirated 3.6-liter V6 engine delivers around 335 horsepower and 285 lb-ft of torque, providing smooth, linear power delivery. It is a popular choice for buyers seeking effortless passing power, though routine fluid checks are essential for long-term reliability.

### 3.6L Twin-Turbo V6

Reserved for high-performance variants, the 3.6-liter twin-turbo V6 pumps out an impressive 420 horsepower and 430 lb-ft of torque. While it turns the CTS into a true sport sedan, maintenance costs and potential component wear tend to run higher than the standard engines.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 8-speed automatic transmission handles power delivery across most trims and rear-wheel or all-wheel-drive configurations. While it helps optimize fuel economy, some versions are prone to rough shifting behaviors that require careful evaluation during a test drive.

**Rough Shifting and Torque Converter Shudder**
- **Symptoms:** Owners report harsh engagement when shifting into lower gears, along with a noticeable shudder or violent bucking and surging during low-speed driving.
- **Solution:** Check for available manufacturer service bulletins or software updates with a dealer, as fluid flushes or reprogramming may be required.

## Steering

### Steering Rack and Pinion

The electronic steering system provides responsive handling, though mechanical wear in the steering rack can occasionally lead to a complete loss of power assist.

**Loss of Power Steering Assist**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers experience a sudden loss of power steering functionality while operating the vehicle at various speeds.
- **Solution:** Inspect the steering assembly and replace the rack and pinion unit if mechanical failure is diagnosed.

## Electrical

### CUE Infotainment Touchscreen

The Cadillac User Experience (CUE) system controls critical audio, navigation, and climate functions, but early iterations are prone to display failures.

**Screen Delamination and Cracking**
- **Symptoms:** The infotainment display develops spiderweb cracks or internal delamination, rendering the touch screen unresponsive and obscuring the backup camera view.
- **Solution:** Replace the faulty display assembly, which may involve out-of-pocket costs if the vehicle is outside of warranty coverage.
- **Cost:** $1700.00

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V595000: Seats:Front Assembly:Seat Heater/Cooler](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Cadillac&model=CTS&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** If a damaged seat heater is turned on while no one is inside the vehicle, the damaged seat heater can overheat, increasing the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will recalibrate the body control module, disabling the automatic activation of the heated seat when the vehicle is remotely started, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V086000: Steering:Electric Power Assist System](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Cadillac&model=CTS&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** Loss of power steering assist would require a higher steering effort, especially at lower speeds, which may increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will replace the power steering gear assembly, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V175000: Steering:Electric Power Assist System](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Cadillac&model=CTS&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** A loss of power steering assist can require greater steering effort, especially at low speeds, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the power steering gear assembly, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V329000: Air Bags:Frontal:Driver Side:Inflator Module](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Cadillac&model=CTS&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** The front-driver air bag inflator may rupture during deployment. An inflator rupture may result in sharp metal fragments striking the driver or other occupants, resulting in serious injury or death.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the front-driver air bag module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2016 Cadillac CTS is an appealing luxury sedan with strong engine options and sharp handling, but shoppers should perform a rigorous pre-purchase inspection. Pay close attention to the behavior of the 8-speed automatic transmission during low-speed maneuvers and verify that the CUE infotainment touchscreen responds correctly without internal cracking. Addressing these potential electronic and powertrain items early can help ensure a much more satisfying ownership experience.
