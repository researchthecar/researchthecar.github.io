**Introduction:**

The 2003 Cadillac CTS is a luxury sports sedan available with two engine options: a 220 horsepower 3.2L V6 and a 320 horsepower 3.2L V8. Both engines are paired with a standard 5-speed automatic transmission. The CTS is known for its sharp handling, refined interior, and powerful engines. However, it has also been plagued by a number of issues over the years.

**Engine**

**3.2L V6**

* **Intake manifold gasket failure:** The intake manifold gasket can fail, causing coolant and oil leaks. Symptoms include overheating, white smoke from the exhaust, and loss of coolant. The solution is to replace the intake manifold gasket. Estimated cost to fix: $500 - $800+.

**3.2L V8**

* **Valve cover gasket leaks:** The valve cover gaskets can leak oil, causing a burning smell and oil consumption. Symptoms include a visible oil leak from the valve covers. The solution is to replace the valve cover gaskets. Estimated cost to fix: $300 - $500.
* **Fuel injector failure:** The fuel injectors can fail, causing a loss of power and engine misfires. Symptoms include rough idling, hesitation during acceleration, and a check engine light. The solution is to replace the failed fuel injectors. Estimated cost to fix: $200 - $400 per injector.

**Transmission**

**5-Speed Automatic Transmission**

* **Transmission shudder:** The transmission can shudder during shifts, especially when shifting from 1st to 2nd gear. Symptoms include a vibration or shaking sensation during acceleration. The solution is to replace the transmission fluid and filter. Estimated cost to fix: $150 - $300.
* **Transmission failure:** In some cases, the transmission can fail completely. Symptoms include a lack of power, grinding noises, and a check engine light. The solution is to rebuild or replace the transmission. Estimated cost to fix: $1,500 - $3,000+.

**Suspension**

* **Lower control arm bushings:** The lower control arm bushings can wear out, causing a clunking noise when driving over bumps. Symptoms include a noticeable clunking sound when turning or driving over rough roads. The solution is to replace the lower control arm bushings. Estimated cost to fix: $400 - $600.
* **Rear suspension links:** The rear suspension links can fail, causing the rear wheels to become misaligned. Symptoms include uneven tire wear and a pulling sensation when driving. The solution is to replace the failed suspension links. Estimated cost to fix: $600 - $800.

**Electrical**

* **Alternator failure:** The alternator can fail, causing the battery to lose power. Symptoms include a flickering dashboard, dim headlights, and a dead battery. The solution is to replace the alternator. Estimated cost to fix: $500 - $800.
* **Starter failure:** The starter can fail, preventing the engine from starting. Symptoms include a clicking noise when trying to start the engine and no cranking. The solution is to replace the starter. Estimated cost to fix: $400 - $600.

**Exterior**

* **Headlight condensation:** The headlights can develop condensation inside the lens, causing poor visibility at night. Symptoms include foggy or cloudy headlights. The solution is to seal the headlights or replace them if they are damaged. Estimated cost to fix: $200 - $400 per headlight.
* **Wheel bearing failure:** The wheel bearings can fail, causing a grinding or humming noise during driving. Symptoms include a loud noise that changes with speed. The solution is to replace the failed wheel bearings. Estimated cost to fix: $300 - $500 per wheel.

**Interior**

* **Center console rattling:** The center console can rattle, causing a distracting noise while driving. Symptoms include a loose or vibrating center console. The solution is to tighten or replace the center console mounts. Estimated cost to fix: $100 - $200.
* **Power window failure:** The power windows can fail, preventing the windows from opening or closing. Symptoms include a non-functioning power window or a window that closes slowly. The solution is to replace the window motor or switch. Estimated cost to fix: $300 - $500 per window.

**Summary:**

The 2003 Cadillac CTS is a vehicle with some serious flaws. The engine and transmission can develop significant problems, which can be expensive to fix. The suspension, electrical system, and interior also have their share of issues. While the CTS is praised for its handling and performance, its reliability is a major concern.
