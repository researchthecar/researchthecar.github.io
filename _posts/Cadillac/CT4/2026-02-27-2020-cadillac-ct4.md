# 2020 Cadillac CT4 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2020 Cadillac CT4, buyers will find an engaging compact luxury sedan with sharp handling and modern tech. However, owner reports highlight a few mechanical and electrical areas worth monitoring, particularly concerning powertrain performance and electronic brake controls during a test drive.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged I4

The base 2.0L turbocharged inline-four engine produces approximately 237 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque, offering a balanced mix of efficiency and pep for daily driving. It is generally reliable, though owners have flagged isolated incidents of hesitation and stalling.

**Engine Stalling and Hesitation**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have reported instances where the vehicle hesitates, fails to accelerate properly at highway speeds, and occasionally stalls out of nowhere.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis by a qualified technician is required to check fuel delivery, powertrain control modules, and throttle body operation.

### 2.7L Turbocharged I4

The upgraded 2.7L turbocharged inline-four delivers stronger performance, pushing around 310 horsepower and 350 lb-ft of torque for a more athletic driving dynamic. Buyers should verify smooth operation under hard acceleration during a test drive.

**Power Loss and Shuddering**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may experience sudden shudders and a loss of power during rapid acceleration, potentially linked to cylinder deactivation systems.
- **Solution:** Inspect cylinder deactivation components and software calibration with the help of a dealership or specialist.

### Brake Control Module and Master Cylinder

The braking hardware is generally robust, but electronic module failures can pose serious safety concerns requiring immediate professional attention.

**Brake System Failure and Warning Lights**
- **Symptoms:** Multiple warning lights illuminate on the dashboard accompanied by a severe reduction in braking power while coming to a stop.
- **Solution:** Have the electronic brake control module, master cylinder, and brake retainers thoroughly inspected and replaced if worn or malfunctioning.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

Paired with the base 2.0L engine, this 8-speed automatic provides responsive gear shifts tailored for efficiency and spirited driving. No widespread recurring complaints have heavily impacted this specific transmission option.

### 10-Speed Automatic Transmission

Working alongside the 2.7L engine, the 10-speed automatic offers closely spaced ratios to keep the engine in its optimal power band. It generally delivers smooth operation under normal driving conditions.

## Brakes

### Tail Lamp Assemblies

Exterior lighting housings should be checked for interior condensation or moisture damage, which can compromise visibility.

**Moisture Intrusion and Light Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Moisture builds up inside the tail light assembly, leading to corrosion and causing the rear brake lights to stop working.
- **Solution:** Inspect tail light seals for leaks and replace the corroded bulb sockets or entire tail light assembly as necessary.

## Safety

### Air Bag and Restraints System

Safety restraint sensors and wiring require careful inspection to ensure airbag readiness in the event of a collision.

**Service Safety Restraint System Warning**
- **Symptoms:** An error message appears on the instrument cluster indicating a fault within the supplemental restraint system.
- **Solution:** Take the vehicle to a dealer or qualified repair shop to scan diagnostic trouble codes and replace faulty airbag sensors or modules.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V611000: Air Bags:Side/Window:Curtain](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Cadillac&model=CT4&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** The roof rail side-curtain air bags may not have been installed correctly, which could result in an improper air bag deployment during a crash. Air bags that do not deploy properly can increase the risk of injury in a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect both left and right-side roof rail air bags and reinstall the air bags, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V588000: Service Brakes](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Cadillac&model=CT4&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** Sensor connection contamination in the electronic brake boost system may cause an interruption of communication between the sensor and the brake boost system. If communication with the sensor is interrupted, the electronic brake boost assist could be lost.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will replace the electronic brake boost module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V903000: Exterior Lighting:Lighting Control Module:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Cadillac&model=CT4&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** The daytime running lights (DRLs) may not deactivate when the headlights are on. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 108, "Lamps, Reflective Devices, and Associated Equipment." DRLs that do not deactivate as intended may result in a glare, reducing visibility, and increasing the risk.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 25V148000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Cadillac&model=CT4&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** The transmission may become damaged internally and cause the wheels to lock-up. Wheel lock-up while driving increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will install transmission control module monitoring software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2020 Cadillac CT4 is an appealing choice for used luxury car buyers seeking sharp dynamics and modern styling. While major drivetrain architectures are generally solid, prospective owners should carefully check for smooth engine acceleration, proper functioning of the electronic brake systems, and dry tail light housings before finalizing a purchase.
