# 2021 Cadillac CT4 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2021 Cadillac CT4 brings compact luxury sport sedan styling and engaging rear-wheel-drive dynamics to the market. While generally well-regarded for its handling and feature set, used car shoppers should review potential owner-reported concerns regarding powertrain hesitation and electrical integration before purchasing.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged I4

The 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine produces approximately 237 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque, serving as the standard powerplant for base trims. Owner reports indicate occasional diagnostic trouble codes related to evaporative emissions components, but the engine is generally straightforward for daily driving.

### 2.7L Turbocharged I4

The larger 2.7-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine generates around 310 horsepower and 350 lb-ft of torque in standard form, or up to 325 horsepower in the CT4-V. Buyers looking for stronger acceleration will appreciate this option, though keeping an eye on overall powertrain health during a pre-purchase inspection is advised.

### 3.6L Twin-Turbo V6

Exclusive to the high-performance CT4-V Blackwing variant, the twin-turbocharged V6 delivers robust track-ready power. Performance models require careful verification of maintenance history and proper diagnostic checks.

**Sudden Loss of Propulsion**
- **Symptoms:** While driving at highway speeds, the vehicle may suddenly lose propulsion and drop out of gear as if shifted into neutral.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified technician inspect the transmission and powertrain control modules for underlying faults.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 8-speed automatic transmission pairs with the standard 2.0L engine to deliver smooth daily shifts. Buyers should test-drive thoroughly to ensure crisp engagement across all gears.

### 10-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 10-speed automatic transmission handles higher-output engines and provides quick gear changes. Owners have reported intermittent valve body and control valve concerns that may require professional service.

**Transmission Control Valve and Shifting Hesitation**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may experience hesitation, hard shifting, or jerking behavior during acceleration due to a sticking or worn control valve.
- **Solution:** Consult a dealership for diagnostic evaluation and potential replacement of affected valve body components.

## Safety

### Electrical Integration and Safety Systems

Complex onboard computer networks manage numerous driver-assistance features, which can occasionally trigger cascading warning indicators if voltage drops occur.

**Multiple Warning Lights and Power Steering Loss**
- **Symptoms:** Simultaneous illumination of warning lights for stability control, collision avoidance, airbags, and a sudden loss of power steering assist.
- **Solution:** Have the vehicle's electrical charging system, battery, and body control modules diagnosed for communication errors.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V611000: Air Bags:Side/Window:Curtain](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Cadillac&model=CT4&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The roof rail side-curtain air bags may not have been installed correctly, which could result in an improper air bag deployment during a crash. Air bags that do not deploy properly can increase the risk of injury in a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect both left and right-side roof rail air bags and reinstall the air bags, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V421000: Air Bags:Srs Malfunction Warning Lamp/Light](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Cadillac&model=CT4&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The air bag malfunction indicator light may fail to warn the driver of a problem with the air bag, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 22V903000: Exterior Lighting:Lighting Control Module:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Cadillac&model=CT4&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The daytime running lights (DRLs) may not deactivate when the headlights are on. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 108, "Lamps, Reflective Devices, and Associated Equipment." DRLs that do not deactivate as intended may result in a glare, reducing visibility, and increasing the risk.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 25V148000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Cadillac&model=CT4&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The transmission may become damaged internally and cause the wheels to lock-up. Wheel lock-up while driving increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will install transmission control module monitoring software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2021 Cadillac CT4 offers an appealing blend of luxury and sporty driving dynamics, but potential buyers should conduct a thorough test drive to check for transmission hesitation or shifting roughness. Ensuring that all relevant powertrain updates and diagnostic checks are up to date will help secure a more dependable ownership experience.
