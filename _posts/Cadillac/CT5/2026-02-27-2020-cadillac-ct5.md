# 2020 Cadillac CT5 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2020 Cadillac CT5 offers a blend of luxury and engaging rear-wheel-drive dynamics, but prospective buyers should review recurring owner complaints carefully. While major mechanical failures are relatively uncommon compared to cosmetic or convenience issues, several reported patterns involving the powertrain and exterior door handles warrant a thorough pre-purchase inspection.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbo I4

The base 2.0-liter turbocharged inline-four engine produces 237 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque, delivering adequate everyday power and efficiency. It serves as the standard powerplant for lower trims and generally offers straightforward, conventional reliability.

### 3.0L Twin-Turbo V6

The available 3.0-liter twin-turbocharged V6 generates 335 horsepower (or up to 360 hp in V-Series models) for strong, effortless acceleration. Buyers should routinely monitor oil levels and inspect for minor leaks during regular maintenance intervals.

**Check Engine and Oil Level Alerts**
- **Symptoms:** Illuminated check engine warning lights accompanied by unexpectedly low engine oil levels and potential oil leaks.
- **Solution:** Inspect engine gaskets and seals for leaks, top off oil levels, and have a qualified technician diagnose any faulty sensors.

## Transmission

### 10-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 10-speed automatic transmission is paired with both engine options to maximize highway efficiency and keep power delivery smooth. While generally responsive, some owners have reported intermittent harsh shifting and unexpected power loss requiring professional diagnosis.

**Loss of Motive Power and Harsh Shifting**
- **Symptoms:** Sudden loss of motive power at highway speeds, unexpected downshifts into park or neutral, and intermittent vehicle shuddering.
- **Solution:** Consult a certified dealer for transmission control module updates, valve replacement, or warranty evaluations.

## Electrical

### Infotainment and Instrument Cluster

Cabin electronics manage vital driver gauges and infotainment features, though occasional software glitches and blank screens have been reported by early owners.

**Display Screen Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** The digital driver speedometer screen temporarily disappears, or the backup camera display shows a blue error screen.
- **Solution:** Perform software resets or updates at a dealership; replace faulty control modules if flickering or blank screens persist.

## Exterior

### Door Handles and Latches

The exterior door mechanisms utilize electronic touch sensors that can occasionally fail, leading to locking or unlatching difficulties from the outside.

**Exterior Door Handle Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Exterior door handles fail to unlatch doors from the outside, leaving passengers unable to open doors even when the key fob is present.
- **Solution:** Inspect the electronic microswitches and internal linkage mechanisms; replace faulty door handle assemblies as needed.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V611000: Air Bags:Side/Window:Curtain](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Cadillac&model=CT5&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** The roof rail side-curtain air bags may not have been installed correctly, which could result in an improper air bag deployment during a crash. Air bags that do not deploy properly can increase the risk of injury in a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect both left and right-side roof rail air bags and reinstall the air bags, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V588000: Service Brakes](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Cadillac&model=CT5&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** Sensor connection contamination in the electronic brake boost system may cause an interruption of communication between the sensor and the brake boost system. If communication with the sensor is interrupted, the electronic brake boost assist could be lost.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will replace the electronic brake boost module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V903000: Exterior Lighting:Lighting Control Module:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Cadillac&model=CT5&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** The daytime running lights (DRLs) may not deactivate when the headlights are on. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 108, "Lamps, Reflective Devices, and Associated Equipment." DRLs that do not deactivate as intended may result in a glare, reducing visibility, and increasing the risk.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 25V148000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Cadillac&model=CT5&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** The transmission may become damaged internally and cause the wheels to lock-up. Wheel lock-up while driving increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will install transmission control module monitoring software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

When shopping for a used Cadillac CT5, pay close attention to transmission shift quality and ensure all exterior door handles and electronic displays function properly. A comprehensive pre-purchase inspection by a knowledgeable mechanic can help verify that powertrain components and door latches are free of known owner-reported issues.
