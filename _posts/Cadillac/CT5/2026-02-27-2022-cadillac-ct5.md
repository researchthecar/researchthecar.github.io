# 2022 Cadillac CT5 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2022 Cadillac CT5, buyers will find a stylish and engaging luxury sedan with modern tech and strong performance options. However, owner reports highlight a few recurring electrical and safety concerns—particularly regarding exterior door handles and restraint systems—that deserve a careful inspection before purchase.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4

This base 2.0-liter turbocharged engine produces 237 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque, offering a balanced mix of efficiency and everyday drivability. It is widely used across Cadillac's lineup and generally provides dependable operation for daily commuters.

### 3.0L Twin-Turbocharged V6

The 3.0-liter twin-turbo V6 delivers robust performance with 335 horsepower (or up to 360 hp in V-Series models), providing strong acceleration and passing power. It is a solid choice for enthusiasts seeking a more dynamic luxury driving experience.

### 6.2L Supercharged V8

Reserved exclusively for the high-performance CT5-V Blackwing, this hand-built supercharged V8 pumps out an impressive 668 horsepower. It transforms the sedan into a track-capable muscle car while maintaining a high-performance reputation.

## Transmission

### 10-Speed Automatic Transmission

Paired with most engine options, this 10-speed automatic transmission delivers smooth and rapid gear changes while optimizing fuel economy on the highway. It is generally regarded as a durable unit in modern GM luxury applications.

### 6-Speed Manual Transmission

Offered exclusively on the CT5-V Blackwing for purists who prefer three pedals, this 6-speed manual transmission provides crisp engagement and direct control. It remains a rare and highly sought-after option for driving enthusiasts.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

Braking components provide strong stopping power, though unusual noises should be inspected by a professional technician.

**Scraping Noise and Stuttering**
- **Symptoms:** Brakes may emit loud scraping noises or feel as though they are stuttering and locking up when coming to a complete stop.
- **Solution:** Inspect brake pads, rotors, and ABS hydraulic modules for wear or premature glazing.

## Exterior

### Door Handle Pressure Pads

Door electrical switches and touch pads are prone to premature failure, requiring careful testing of all entry points prior to purchase.

**Driver Door Fails to Open**
- **Symptoms:** The exterior door handle pressure pad becomes unresponsive, making it impossible to open the driver's door from the outside.
- **Solution:** Replace the faulty door handle assembly and integrated pressure pad mechanism.

## Safety

### Air Bag Sensors

Airbag and restraint monitoring systems require immediate attention if warning lights appear to ensure occupant protection features function correctly.

**Service Restraint System Warning**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may experience a dashboard warning message indicating a fault in the safety restraint system, often linked to faulty airbag sensors.
- **Solution:** Have the diagnostic trouble codes read by a dealership or qualified technician and replace the affected airbag sensor as needed.

### Forward Collision Avoidance

Advanced driver assistance features add convenience and safety, but sensor calibrations should be verified during pre-purchase inspections.

**Unintended Wheel Lockup**
- **Symptoms:** Front wheels may occasionally lock up or engage emergency braking briefly when hitting bumps or unexpectedly while driving.
- **Solution:** Inspect collision avoidance sensor alignment, check for software updates, and have the automatic braking system evaluated by a certified specialist.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V611000: Air Bags:Side/Window:Curtain](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Cadillac&model=CT5&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** The roof rail side-curtain air bags may not have been installed correctly, which could result in an improper air bag deployment during a crash. Air bags that do not deploy properly can increase the risk of injury in a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect both left and right-side roof rail air bags and reinstall the air bags, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V903000: Exterior Lighting:Lighting Control Module:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Cadillac&model=CT5&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** The daytime running lights (DRLs) may not deactivate when the headlights are on. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 108, "Lamps, Reflective Devices, and Associated Equipment." DRLs that do not deactivate as intended may result in a glare, reducing visibility, and increasing the risk.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2022 Cadillac CT5 is a compelling luxury sport sedan offering a blend of sharp handling, comfortable styling, and powerful engine options ranging from efficient turbos to blistering V8s. While it generally presents well as a used vehicle purchase, buyers should thoroughly test electronic door handles, safety restraint sensors, and driver-assistance features to ensure everything operates reliably.
