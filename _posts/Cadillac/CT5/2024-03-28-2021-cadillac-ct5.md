**Introduction:**

The 2021 Cadillac CT5 is a luxury sedan that offers a combination of performance and comfort. It is available with a range of engine and transmission options, including a turbocharged 2.0-liter inline-four, a 3.0-liter twin-turbocharged V6, and a 4.2-liter Blackwing naturally aspirated V8. The CT5 is praised for its elegant design, spacious interior, and engaging driving dynamics. However, some aspects of the vehicle have faced criticism, such as its infotainment system and its occasionally firm ride.

**Engine**

**2.0L Turbocharged Inline-Four**

* **Hesitation on acceleration**
  * Description/Symptoms: A brief hesitation or lag when accelerating from a stop or when pressing the gas pedal.
  * Solution: Reflashing the engine control module (ECM).
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300+
  * Recalls: None Found
* **Timing chain noise**
  * Description/Symptoms: A rattling or knocking noise coming from the engine, especially during startup or acceleration.
  * Solution: Replacing the timing chain and related components.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,000+
  * Recalls: None Found

**3.0L Twin-Turbocharged V6**

* **Turbocharger failure**
  * Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, blue or black smoke from the exhaust, whining or squealing noises.
  * Solution: Replacing the affected turbocharger(s).
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,500 - $3,500+ per turbocharger
  * Recalls: None Found

**4.2L Blackwing Naturally Aspirated V8**

* **No major issues reported.**

**Transmission**

**10-Speed Automatic**

* **Delayed gear shifts**
  * Description/Symptoms: Slow or hesitation when shifting gears, especially during acceleration or deceleration.
  * Solution: Reprogramming the transmission control module (TCM).
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
  * Recalls: None Found

**Manual**

* **Grinding noises when shifting**
  * Description/Symptoms: A grinding or crunching sound when shifting into certain gears, especially when the car is cold.
  * Solution: Adjusting or replacing the clutch hydraulics.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
  * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Excessive road noise**
  * Description/Symptoms: A noticeable amount of road noise and vibrations transmitted into the cabin.
  * Solution: Adding additional sound-deadening material or adjusting suspension components.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
  * Recalls: None Found
* **Harsh ride over rough roads**
  * Description/Symptoms: A firm and uncomfortable ride when driving over uneven surfaces.
  * Solution: Upgrading the suspension system with softer springs or adaptive dampers.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,500+
  * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Malfunctioning blind-spot monitoring system**
  * Description/Symptoms: The system does not detect vehicles in blind spots or provides false alerts.
  * Solution: Replacing the radar sensors or reprogramming the system.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
  * Recalls: None Found
* **Defective infotainment system**
  * Description/Symptoms: The infotainment display freezes, glitches, or does not respond to inputs.
  * Solution: Software updates or replacement of the entire unit.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
  * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **No major issues reported.**

**Interior**

* **Dashboard creaking or rattling**
  * Description/Symptoms: Noises coming from the dashboard when driving over rough roads or when applying pressure to it.
  * Solution: Using anti-rattle clips or foam tape to secure loose components.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+
  * Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

The 2021 Cadillac CT5 has a moderate number of reported issues, with some being more severe and expensive to fix than others. The engine and transmission issues are generally repairable for a reasonable cost, while suspension and electrical problems can be more costly. Overall, the vehicle offers a solid balance of performance and luxury but may require some attention to address certain issues after purchase.
