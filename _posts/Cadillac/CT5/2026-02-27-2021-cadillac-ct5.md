# 2021 Cadillac CT5 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2021 Cadillac CT5 delivers a compelling mix of athletic rear-wheel-drive handling, bold styling, and modern tech, making it an appealing choice for used luxury sedan buyers. However, prospective buyers should inspect specific trouble spots, particularly with exterior door handle mechanisms and intermittent transmission or electrical hiccups reported by owners. Being aware of these recurring owner complaints can help you navigate a test drive and secure a reliable pre-owned example.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged I4

The base 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine produces 237 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque, offering efficient daily performance. It is generally regarded as a dependable powertrain option for everyday commuting.

### 3.0L Twin-Turbocharged V6

The 3.0-liter twin-turbo V6 pushes out 335 horsepower (or up to 360 hp in the V-Series), delivering strong acceleration and effortless passing power. While robust, buyers should keep an eye out for minor accessory fuel system leaks as these vehicles age.

**Fuel Odor and Hose Leak**
- **Symptoms:** A noticeable gasoline odor inside the cabin upon starting the vehicle, which tends to clear up once driving.
- **Solution:** Inspect the fuel line connections under the hood and replace any leaking fuel hoses or clamps.

## Transmission

### 10-Speed Automatic Transmission

Paired across most trims, the 10-speed automatic transmission provides smooth shifts and optimizes highway fuel economy. Most owners experience trouble-free operation, though occasional shift control module faults have been documented.

**Shift Control Fault Code P17A5**
- **Symptoms:** A 'Service Transmission' message appears on the dashboard, accompanied by harsh shifting or a risk of getting stuck in gear.
- **Solution:** Take the vehicle to a qualified service center for diagnostic testing; faulty gear shift assemblies may need to be replaced.

## Exterior

### Exterior Door Handles

The exterior door handles and associated electronic switches are prone to intermittent failure, which can lock occupants out from the outside.

**Inoperative Door Handles**
- **Symptoms:** Doors fail to open from the outside using the exterior handle, forcing occupants to enter through another door or open from the inside.
- **Solution:** Have the faulty door handle switch or entire handle assembly replaced by a certified technician.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V611000: Air Bags:Side/Window:Curtain](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Cadillac&model=CT5&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The roof rail side-curtain air bags may not have been installed correctly, which could result in an improper air bag deployment during a crash. Air bags that do not deploy properly can increase the risk of injury in a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect both left and right-side roof rail air bags and reinstall the air bags, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V421000: Air Bags:Srs Malfunction Warning Lamp/Light](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Cadillac&model=CT5&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The air bag malfunction indicator light may fail to warn the driver of a problem with the air bag, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 22V903000: Exterior Lighting:Lighting Control Module:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Cadillac&model=CT5&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The daytime running lights (DRLs) may not deactivate when the headlights are on. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 108, "Lamps, Reflective Devices, and Associated Equipment." DRLs that do not deactivate as intended may result in a glare, reducing visibility, and increasing the risk.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 25V148000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Cadillac&model=CT5&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The transmission may become damaged internally and cause the wheels to lock-up. Wheel lock-up while driving increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will install transmission control module monitoring software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2021 Cadillac CT5 is a striking and capable luxury sport sedan that offers plenty of comfort and performance on the used market. While major mechanical failures are relatively infrequent, prospective buyers should thoroughly test all exterior door handles and ensure the transmission shifts smoothly during a pre-purchase inspection. Addressing these localized issues early will help ensure a satisfying and dependable ownership experience.
