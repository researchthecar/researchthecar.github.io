# 2023 Cadillac CT5 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2023 Cadillac CT5 delivers a compelling mix of athletic handling, modern technology, and luxury styling. However, prospective buyers should review available owner reports concerning early powertrain performance and minor electrical or hardware glitches. Evaluating service histories and ensuring all software updates are applied can help secure a dependable ownership experience.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged I4

The base 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine produces 237 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque, offering efficient daily driving. It provides standard power for lower trims while maintaining balanced overall reliability.

### 3.0L Twin-Turbocharged V6

The available 3.0-liter twin-turbo V6 delivers 335 horsepower in standard form or up to 360 horsepower in higher performance trims, providing robust acceleration. This engine option is generally well-regarded mechanically, though complex forced-induction systems require diligent maintenance.

### 6.2L Supercharged V8

Reserved for the high-performance CT5-V Blackwing, the 6.2-liter supercharged V8 pushes out an impressive 668 horsepower. It is a powerful enthusiast powerplant, though driven examples should be carefully inspected for wear.

## Transmission

### 10-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 10-speed automatic transmission pairs with the various engine options to optimize efficiency and power delivery across trims. While generally smooth, some owners have experienced valve body or sudden power reduction issues requiring professional evaluation.

**Powertrain and Valve Body Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have reported unexpected drops into reduced power mode, rough shifting, and illuminated check engine lights while driving at highway speeds.
- **Solution:** Take the vehicle to a certified dealer to diagnose transmission fault codes and inspect the valve body or internal hydraulic components.

### 6-Speed Manual Transmission

A 6-speed manual transmission is exclusively offered on the CT5-V Blackwing for enthusiast drivers seeking direct engagement. It is a robust unit built to handle high torque outputs.

## Steering

### Electronic Steering Assist

The electronic power steering system provides adjustable feedback depending on drive modes, though sporadic module warnings have been noted by some drivers.

**Steering Assistance Reduced Warning**
- **Symptoms:** Operators may see warning messages on the instrument cluster such as "Steering assistance reduced, drive with care" accompanied by power steering faults.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified technician scan the steering control modules and electrical connections for diagnostic trouble codes.

## Electrical

### Factory Tires and TPMS

The suspension setup balances ride comfort and athletic handling, though specific original equipment tires and sensor hardware require routine monitoring.

**Tire Structural Failure and Sensor Faults**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have experienced unexpected internal tire carcass damage during normal road conditions alongside inconsistent tire pressure monitoring sensor readings.
- **Solution:** Inspect tire treads and sidewalls regularly, and have dealership technicians recalibrate or replace faulty TPMS sensors.

## Exterior

### Door Handles and Latch Mechanisms

Exterior styling incorporates modern flush door handles and sensors, which can occasionally experience latch or release mechanism sticking.

**Driver Door Latch Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The driver-side door fails to open electronically, requiring the manual key blade or rolling down windows with the key fob to gain entry.
- **Solution:** Inspect the electronic door release actuator and latch assembly, replacing faulty mechanical or electrical components as needed.

## Interior

### Wiring Harness and Dashboard Electronics

Advanced electrical architectures power the vehicle's infotainment and safety suites, but harness integrity should be checked during pre-purchase inspections.

**Multiple Warning Lights and Wiring Damage**
- **Symptoms:** Illumination of all dashboard safety and electrical warning lights due to localized wiring insulation damage.
- **Solution:** Inspect main wiring harnesses for environmental or rodent damage, and repair or replace affected wiring sections.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V903000: Exterior Lighting:Lighting Control Module:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Cadillac&model=CT5&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** The daytime running lights (DRLs) may not deactivate when the headlights are on. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 108, "Lamps, Reflective Devices, and Associated Equipment." DRLs that do not deactivate as intended may result in a glare, reducing visibility, and increasing the risk.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2023 Cadillac CT5 remains a stylish and engaging option in the luxury sedan market, balancing comfortable cruising with strong performance variants. While most systems operate reliably, buyers should carefully check transmission behavior, steering warnings, and electrical components during a test drive. Verifying service history and confirming any prior warranty repairs will help ensure a confident purchase.
