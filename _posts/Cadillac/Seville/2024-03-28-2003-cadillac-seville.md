**Introduction**

The 2003 Cadillac Seville is a luxurious sedan offering a smooth ride and well-equipped interior. It features a 4.6L Northstar V8 engine paired with a 4-speed automatic transmission, producing 300 horsepower and 295 lb-ft of torque. Positive aspects of the vehicle include its spacious cabin, powerful engine, and elegant exterior styling. Common complaints include its complex infotainment system and relatively poor fuel economy.

**Engine**

* **4.6L Northstar V8**

  * **Timing Chain Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The timing chain can stretch or snap, leading to catastrophic engine damage and loss of power. Symptoms include excessive noise from the engine, difficulty starting, and reduced engine performance.
    * Solution: Replace the timing chain and tensioners.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

  * **Intake Manifold Gasket Leak**
    * Description/Symptoms: A gasket between the intake manifold and engine can fail, causing coolant and oil leaks. Symptoms include overheating, coolant loss, and oily residue in the engine bay.
    * Solution: Replace the intake manifold gasket.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,200
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

* **4-Speed Automatic**

  * **Transmission Slipping**
    * Description/Symptoms: The transmission may slip or refuse to shift gears, causing difficulty driving and potential damage to the transmission. Symptoms include a lack of acceleration, rough shifting, and a burning smell.
    * Solution: Service or rebuild the transmission, potentially replacing various components such as seals, clutches, or fluid.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **No Issues Found**

**Electrical**

* **Battery Terminal Corrosion**
    * Description/Symptoms: Corrosion on the battery terminals can result in difficulty starting the vehicle. Symptoms include dim lighting, slow cranking, or a no-start condition.
    * Solution: Clean the battery terminals and replace them if necessary.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $200
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Power Window Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The power window motors or switches can fail, causing the window to become inoperable. Symptoms include the window not rolling up or down, or slow and erratic operation.
    * Solution: Replace the faulty window motor or switch.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Headlight Lens Fogging**
    * Description/Symptoms: The headlight lenses may become foggy or cloudy, reducing visibility at night.
    * Solution: Replace the headlight lenses or polish them if possible.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $400
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Trunk Lid Sticking**
    * Description/Symptoms: The trunk lid may become difficult to open or close, potentially due to a faulty latch or adjustment.
    * Solution: Adjust or replace the trunk latch assembly.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Climate Control Malfunction**
    * Description/Symptoms: The climate control system may not regulate temperature properly, resulting in discomfort in the cabin. Symptoms include incorrect air temperature, fan speed issues, or lack of air flow.
    * Solution: Diagnose and repair the climate control module or various components such as sensors, actuators, or blowers.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Seat Heater Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The seat heaters may not work properly, providing insufficient warmth. Symptoms include lack of heat, intermittent operation, or overheating.
    * Solution: Replace the faulty heating element or wiring.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2003 Cadillac Seville has several potential issues that can affect its reliability and comfort. The most severe and expensive issue is the timing chain failure, which can lead to catastrophic engine damage. Other common issues include intake manifold gasket leaks, transmission slipping, battery terminal corrosion, and headlight lens fogging. While these issues can be costly to repair, the Seville generally offers a pleasant driving experience with its luxurious interior and powerful engine.
