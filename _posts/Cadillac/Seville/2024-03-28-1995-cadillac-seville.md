**Introduction**
The 1995 Cadillac Seville is a mid-size luxury sedan that offers a smooth and comfortable ride. It comes standard with a 4.6L Northstar V8 engine, which produces 270 horsepower and 295 lb-ft of torque. Other engine options include a 4.9L Northstar V8 and a 5.7L LT1 V8. Transmission options include a 4-speed automatic and a 6-speed manual. Despite its luxurious amenities, owners have reported several issues with the vehicle.

**Engine**

**4.6L Northstar V8**
- **Intake Manifold Gasket Failure:** This is a common problem that can cause coolant leaks and overheating. The solution is to replace the intake manifold gaskets, which typically costs around $800-$1,200.
- **Head Gasket Failure:** This is a more serious problem that can lead to engine failure. Symptoms include overheating, coolant leaks, and white smoke from the exhaust. The solution is to replace the head gaskets, which can cost upwards of $2,000.

**4.9L Northstar V8**
- **Piston Ring Failure:** This can cause excessive oil consumption and blue smoke from the exhaust. The solution is to replace the piston rings, which typically costs around $1,500-$2,000.
- **Valve Guide Wear:** This can lead to excessive oil consumption and a loss of power. The solution is to replace the valve guides, which typically costs around $1,000-$1,500.

**5.7L LT1 V8**
- **Oil Pump Failure:** This can lead to a loss of oil pressure and engine damage. Symptoms include a ticking noise from the engine and a drop in oil pressure. The solution is to replace the oil pump, which typically costs around $500-$700.
- **Water Pump Failure:** This can lead to overheating and engine damage. Symptoms include a leak from the water pump and a loss of coolant. The solution is to replace the water pump, which typically costs around $300-$500.

**Transmission**

**4-Speed Automatic**
- **Transmission Slipping:** This can be caused by worn clutch plates or a faulty torque converter. Symptoms include difficulty shifting gears and a loss of power. The solution is to rebuild or replace the transmission, which typically costs around $2,000-$3,000.
- **Overheating:** This can be caused by a clogged transmission cooler or a faulty transmission fluid pump. Symptoms include a burning smell and a loss of power. The solution is to flush the transmission cooler and replace the transmission fluid pump, which typically costs around $500-$700.

**6-Speed Manual**
- **Synchronizer Failure:** This can make it difficult to shift gears smoothly. Symptoms include grinding and difficulty shifting gears. The solution is to replace the synchronizers, which typically costs around $1,000-$1,500.
- **Clutch Failure:** This can lead to a loss of power and difficulty driving. Symptoms include a burning smell and a slipping clutch. The solution is to replace the clutch, which typically costs around $500-$700.

**Suspension**
- **Struts/Shock Failure:** This can lead to a rough ride and poor handling. Symptoms include a bouncing or swaying motion when driving over bumps. The solution is to replace the struts or shocks, which typically costs around $600-$1,200.
- **Control Arm Bushing Failure:** This can lead to a loss of alignment and poor handling. Symptoms include a clunking noise when driving over bumps and a wandering steering wheel. The solution is to replace the control arm bushings, which typically costs around $200-$400.

**Electrical**
- **Alternator Failure:** This can lead to a loss of power and a dead battery. Symptoms include a dim or flickering headlights and a growling noise from the alternator. The solution is to replace the alternator, which typically costs around $400-$600.
- **Battery Discharge:** This can be caused by a faulty battery or a parasitic drain. Symptoms include a dead battery and a difficulty starting the vehicle. The solution is to test the battery and charging system, and repair any electrical problems, which typically costs around $100-$500.

**Exterior**
- **Door Handle Malfunction:** This can make it difficult to open the doors. Symptoms include a loose or broken door handle. The solution is to replace the door handle, which typically costs around $200-$400.
- **Power Window Failure:** This can prevent the windows from opening or closing. Symptoms include a buzzing noise from the window motor and a difficulty moving the window. The solution is to replace the window motor or switch, which typically costs around $200-$600.

**Interior**
- **Dashboard Cracking:** This can be caused by excessive sun exposure or extreme temperature changes. The solution is to replace the dashboard, which typically costs around $1,000-$2,000.
- **Climate Control Malfunction:** This can lead to uncomfortable temperatures in the cabin. Symptoms include a lack of heat or air conditioning and a noisy blower motor. The solution is to diagnose and repair the problem, which can vary in cost depending on the specific issue.

**Summary**
The 1995 Cadillac Seville is a luxurious sedan that has been plagued by numerous issues. The most common problems include engine gasket failures, transmission slipping, and electrical malfunctions. These issues can be costly to repair, and some of them can even lead to engine or transmission failure. While the Seville is a comfortable and stylish car, it is important to be aware of the potential problems before purchasing one.
