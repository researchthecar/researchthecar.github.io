# 2018 Cadillac ATS Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used luxury sport sedan like the Cadillac ATS, understanding its common mechanical and electrical quirks is essential. While it offers sharp handling and engaging performance, buyers should pay close attention to potential issues involving the drivetrain, transmission, and interior electronics during a pre-purchase inspection.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged 4-Cylinder

The base 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine produces approximately 272 horsepower and 295 lb-ft of torque, offering lively acceleration. Regular maintenance and attentive monitoring of the fuel system are recommended for long-term reliability.

**Fuel Pump Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Strong fuel odors around the vehicle and unexpected engine stalling while driving.
- **Solution:** Inspect the fuel pump assembly and replace loose or failing pump components.

### 3.6L V6

The available 3.6-liter V6 engine delivers a smooth 335 horsepower and 285 lb-ft of torque, providing robust passing power. It generally proves dependable, though routine servicing of ignition and fluid components remains vital.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

Paired with the 2.0L turbo engine, the 8-speed automatic transmission delivers quick shifts. Buyers should test-drive thoroughly within the mid-speed range to check for any signs of transmission shudder.

**Transmission Shudder**
- **Symptoms:** A noticeable shaking or shuddering sensation, particularly between 42 and 48 mph, accompanied by delayed acceleration or fluid issues.
- **Solution:** Flush and replace the transmission fluid with the updated specification fluid to resolve shuddering behavior.

### Rear Differential

Rear-wheel drive and all-wheel drive configurations require periodic inspection of differential fluids and bearings to prevent premature wear and noise.

**Differential Bearing Wear**
- **Symptoms:** An abnormal growling or whining noise coming from the rear of the vehicle while driving.
- **Solution:** Inspect and replace damaged rear differential bearings or rebuild the differential assembly.

## Steering

### Power Steering System

Electronic power steering components should be thoroughly checked for error codes and system stability during a pre-purchase evaluation.

**Intermittent Power Steering Loss**
- **Symptoms:** Power assist suddenly cuts out while driving, displaying a service warning message. Restarting the vehicle temporarily restores function.
- **Solution:** Diagnose the electronic power steering module and electrical connections, replacing faulty components as needed.

## Electrical

### CUE Infotainment Screen

Electrical accessories and infotainment displays require careful verification during a test drive to ensure all features function properly.

**Internal Screen Cracking**
- **Symptoms:** The touchscreen develops spider-web cracks from the inside out, rendering touch controls unresponsive or difficult to use.
- **Solution:** Replace the infotainment display unit.
- **Cost:** $1500

## Exterior

### Side Mirrors

Exterior body hardware and trim pieces should be inspected for structural security, especially in vehicles exposed to harsh winter climates.

**Internal Mirror Corrosion**
- **Symptoms:** Internal mirror brackets corrode over time, causing the mirror housing to loosen or detach completely from the vehicle.
- **Solution:** Inspect mirror mounts for stability and replace corroded mirror assemblies.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V358000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Cadillac&model=ATS&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** A damaged fuel line can create a fuel leak, increasing the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will replace the high pressure fuel pump, and high pressure fuel pipe, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V175000: Steering:Electric Power Assist System](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Cadillac&model=ATS&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** A loss of power steering assist can require greater steering effort, especially at low speeds, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the power steering gear assembly, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The vehicle offers dynamic handling and strong powertrain choices, but potential buyers should conduct a thorough inspection. Pay specific attention to transmission operation for shudder symptoms, verify that the infotainment touchscreen and power steering are functioning correctly, and listen for any unusual rear differential noises during a test drive.
