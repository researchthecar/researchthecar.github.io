# 2014 Cadillac ATS

### Overview
The 2014 Cadillac ATS, a compact luxury sport sedan, aimed to compete with established German rivals. It was praised for its sharp handling, upscale interior, and available potent powertrains. However, like many vehicles in its class, it has specific areas where owners have reported issues. This analysis focuses on common technical reliability concerns beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential problems across its various engine and transmission configurations, as well as other key vehicle systems. Understanding these potential issues can help prospective buyers and current owners make informed decisions regarding maintenance and repairs.

---


## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged I4
**Turbocharger Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of power, whistling noises, increased oil consumption.
- **Solution:** Turbocharger replacement or repair.
- **Cost:** $1,200 - $2,500

**PCV Valve Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Rough idle, engine stalling, oil leaks, check engine light.
- **Solution:** PCV valve replacement.
- **Cost:** $150 - $300

### 3.6L Naturally Aspirated V6
**Timing Chain Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Rattling noise from the front of the engine, check engine light, rough running.
- **Solution:** Timing chain and tensioner replacement.
- **Cost:** $1,500 - $3,000


## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic (M30/M31)
**Harsh Shifting / Hesitation**
- **Symptoms:** Jerky gear changes, delayed engagement, transmission warning light.
- **Solution:** Transmission fluid flush, solenoid replacement, or transmission rebuild.
- **Cost:** $500 - $3,000


### 8-Speed Automatic (8L45/8L90)
**Torque Converter Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Shuddering at low speeds, slipping, rough engagement.
- **Solution:** Torque converter replacement.
- **Cost:** $1,000 - $2,000

### Summary Verdict
The 2014 Cadillac ATS offers a compelling driving experience, but potential buyers should be aware of specific reliability concerns. The 2.0L Turbo engine can experience turbocharger and PCV valve issues, while the 3.6L V6 may develop timing chain problems. Transmission issues, particularly with the automatic gearboxes, such as harsh shifting and torque converter problems, are also noted. The CUE infotainment system is a common source of electrical complaints, with touchscreen malfunctions being prevalent. While suspension, exterior, and interior components generally hold up well, addressing these engine, transmission, and electrical issues proactively can contribute to a more reliable ownership experience for the 2014 ATS.
