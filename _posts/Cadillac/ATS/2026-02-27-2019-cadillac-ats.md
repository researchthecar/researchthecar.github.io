# 2019 Cadillac ATS Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2019 Cadillac ATS represents the final model year for this luxury sport sedan, offering sharp handling and powerful engine options. While overall owner feedback is limited, prospective buyers should review available complaint reports regarding potential electronic and powertrain behavior.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged I4

A 2.0-liter turbocharged inline-four engine producing around 272 horsepower and 295 lb-ft of torque. It offers brisk acceleration, though owners have reported occasional stalling issues.

**Unexpected Engine Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** The engine may unexpectedly shut off when the vehicle comes to a complete stop, requiring a waiting period before it can be restarted.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified technician diagnose electronic throttle body, fuel delivery, or ignition systems for faults.

### 3.6L V6

A naturally aspirated 3.6-liter V6 engine delivering approximately 335 horsepower and 285 lb-ft of torque. It provides smooth, linear power delivery for a more traditional luxury performance feel.

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission (V6)

An 8-speed automatic transmission paired with the 3.6L V6 engine, designed to handle higher power outputs. Proper fluid maintenance is key to long-term reliability.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

An 8-speed automatic transmission paired with the 2.0L turbo engine, offering responsive gear shifts. Buyers should test drive thoroughly to ensure smooth operation across all gears.

## Steering

### Power Steering System

Steering components generally provide precise handling, but electronic assist systems warrant careful inspection during a pre-purchase evaluation.

**Intermittent Steering and Power Loss**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may experience intermittent loss of power steering assistance, making the vehicle difficult to maneuver alongside warning lights.
- **Solution:** Inspect electric power steering modules, wiring harnesses, and battery health for voltage drops.
