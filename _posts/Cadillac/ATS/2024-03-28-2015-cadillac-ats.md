**Introduction**

The 2015 Cadillac ATS is a compact luxury sport sedan that offers a range of engine and transmission options. The base engine is a 2.5-liter four-cylinder that produces 202 horsepower and 191 lb-ft of torque. A 3.6-liter V6 is available as an upgrade, producing 321 horsepower and 275 lb-ft of torque. Both engines are mated to a six-speed automatic transmission, with a rear-wheel drive or all-wheel drive configuration.

The ATS is known for its sporty handling, comfortable interior, and advanced technology features. However, there have been some reported issues with the vehicle, as outlined below.

**Engine**

**2.5-Liter Four-Cylinder**

* **Timing Chain Failure:** Premature wear or failure of the timing chain can lead to catastrophic engine damage.
    * Solution: Replacement of the timing chain and related components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,500 - $4,000+
    * Recalls: NHTSA Campaign ID 15V566000, GM Ref 15141

**3.6-Liter V6**

* **Valve Cover Gasket Leak:** A faulty valve cover gasket can leak oil, resulting in decreased engine performance.
    * Solution: Replacement of the valve cover gasket.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $700
* **Intake Manifold Gasket Failure:** A defective intake manifold gasket can cause air leaks, leading to rough idling and reduced engine power.
    * Solution: Replacement of the intake manifold gasket.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500

**Transmission**

**Six-Speed Automatic**

* **Harsh Shifting:** The transmission may shift abruptly or hesitate between gears.
    * Solution: Resetting the transmission control module or replacing faulty solenoid valves.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $500
* **Transmission Fluid Leak:** A leak in the transmission case or lines can cause low fluid levels, leading to transmission failure.
    * Solution: Repair or replacement of the leaking components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000

**Suspension**

* **Strut Mount Bearing Failure:** A worn or defective strut mount bearing can cause a clunking or knocking noise when turning or driving over bumps.
    * Solution: Replacement of the strut mounts.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $600
* **Rear Suspension Bushing Wear:** Premature wear of the rear suspension bushings can lead to excessive movement and a loss of stability.
    * Solution: Replacement of the worn bushings.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain:** A parasitic drain from an electrical component can lead to a dead battery.
    * Solution: Diagnosing and repairing the source of the drain.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300
* **Headlight Malfunction:** Faulty headlight bulbs or wiring can cause unreliable or dim lighting.
    * Solution: Replacing the affected components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400

**Exterior**

* **Roof Panel Alignment Issues:** Misaligned panels on the roof of the vehicle can cause wind noise and water leaks.
    * Solution: Adjustment or replacement of the affected panels.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000

**Interior**

* **Infotainment System Glitches:** The infotainment system may experience bugs, freezes, or connectivity issues.
    * Solution: Software updates or component replacement.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $700

**Summary**

The 2015 Cadillac ATS is a well-rounded luxury sport sedan with a comfortable interior and excellent handling. However, there are some known issues with the vehicle, particularly with the engine, transmission, and electrical components. The severity of these issues can range from minor annoyances to potentially major repairs, so it's important to research and address any potential problems before purchasing or owning an ATS. Repair costs can vary depending on the specific issue and the location of repairs.
