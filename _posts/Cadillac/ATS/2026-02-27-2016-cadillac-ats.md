# 2016 Cadillac ATS Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2016 Cadillac ATS, buyers will find a sporty and engaging compact luxury car offering sharp handling and strong engine choices. However, prospective owners should review its common trouble spots, particularly regarding transmission performance, infotainment electronics, and suspension durability. Paying close attention to how the vehicle shifts and verifying the condition of the electronics during a test drive can help buyers avoid unexpected repair bills.

---

## Engine

### 2.5L I4

The base 2.5-liter naturally aspirated inline-four engine produces approximately 202 horsepower and 191 lb-ft of torque. It offers basic daily efficiency, though drivers seeking livelier acceleration often prefer the turbocharged alternatives.

### 2.0L Turbo I4

The 2.0-liter turbocharged inline-four engine generates roughly 272 horsepower and 295 lb-ft of torque. It delivers robust acceleration and strong passing power, making it one of the more popular choices for enthusiasts.

### 3.6L V6

The 3.6-liter naturally aspirated V6 engine pushes out about 333 horsepower and 285 lb-ft of torque. It provides smooth, linear power delivery and pairs well with the ATS's nimble chassis.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 8-speed automatic transmission delivers power to the rear wheels or available all-wheel-drive system. While it offers quick ratios, some owners report drivability quirks that require professional attention.

**Transmission Shudder and Hard Shifting**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may notice a shudder or vibration during acceleration, particularly when going up hills, along with hard downshifting or rough engagement from a stop.
- **Solution:** Inspection by a qualified technician is necessary; repairing or replacing components like the torque converter or updating transmission software can resolve the harsh shifting behavior.

### 6-Speed Manual Transmission

The 6-speed manual transmission is paired exclusively with the 2.0L turbocharged engine for a more direct driver engagement. It is a rarer find on the used market and generally praised for mechanical simplicity.

## Suspension

### Wheels and Rims

The factory wheel and low-profile tire setup provides responsive handling but proves vulnerable to rough road impacts.

**Factory Wheel Cracking**
- **Symptoms:** Factory rims can develop structural cracks when encountering normal road seams and potholes, leading to slow air leaks in the tires.
- **Solution:** Damaged wheels must be professionally welded if repairable or replaced entirely with aftermarket or updated factory replacements.

## Electrical

### CUE Infotainment Screen

The CUE infotainment system controls climate, audio, and vehicle settings. Early iterations are prone to display panel failures.

**Cracking and Unresponsive Touchscreen**
- **Symptoms:** The infotainment display may develop internal spiderweb cracks, become unresponsive to touch, or register ghost inputs that cause menus to wander independently.
- **Solution:** The touchscreen digitizer or entire display assembly typically needs to be replaced to restore normal operation.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 15V463000: Visibility:Sun/Moon Roof Assembly](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Cadillac&model=ATS&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** 118, "Power-Operated Window, Partition, and Roof Panel Systems." Because the switch is not recessed, the roof panel switch may inadvertently be pressed resulting in unintended auto-closure of the roof panel, increasing the risk of personal injury.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners and dealers will replace the roof console accessory switch trim plate, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 15V558000: Visibility:Defroster/Defogger/Hvac System](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Cadillac&model=ATS&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** If the coil antenna generates excessive heat, there is an increased risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will update the Electronic Climate Control module to remove the automatic rear defogger "on" function, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V329000: Air Bags:Frontal:Driver Side:Inflator Module](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Cadillac&model=ATS&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** The front-driver air bag inflator may rupture during deployment. An inflator rupture may result in sharp metal fragments striking the driver or other occupants, resulting in serious injury or death.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the front-driver air bag module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2016 Cadillac ATS is an engaging and capable sport sedan, but used car buyers should perform targeted checks before purchasing. Ensure the transmission shifts smoothly without hesitation or shudder, test all infotainment touchscreen functions thoroughly, and inspect the alloy wheels for any signs of cracking. Addressing these key areas during a pre-purchase inspection will help ensure a more reliable ownership experience.
