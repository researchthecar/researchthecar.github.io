**Introduction**

The 2013 Cadillac ATS is a compact luxury sedan that offers impressive performance and handling. It is available with a variety of engine and transmission options, including a 2.5-liter naturally aspirated inline-four, a 2.0-liter turbocharged inline-four, and a 3.6-liter naturally aspirated V6. The ATS is praised for its sharp styling, luxurious interior, and responsive handling. However, it has also been criticized for its firm ride quality and limited rear seat space.

**Engine**

**2.5L Naturally Aspirated Inline-Four**

* **Intake Valve Failure:**
    * Intake valves may stick open or closed due to carbon buildup or manufacturing defects.
    * Symptoms include rough idling, misfires, and poor fuel economy.
    * Solution: Replace the faulty intake valves and clean the intake manifold.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Timing Chain Tensioner Failure:**
    * Timing chain tensioner may fail, resulting in a noisy engine and potential timing chain damage.
    * Symptoms include rattling or knocking sounds from the engine.
    * Solution: Replace the faulty timing chain tensioner.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
    * Recalls: None Found

**2.0L Turbocharged Inline-Four**

* **No Issues Found**

**3.6L Naturally Aspirated V6**

* **Oil Leaks:**
    * Oil may leak from the camshaft cover gasket or oil pan due to faulty seals.
    * Symptoms include low oil levels and oil stains under the vehicle.
    * Solution: Replace the faulty gaskets or oil pan.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**6-Speed Manual**

* **Clutch Failure:**
    * Clutch disc or pressure plate may wear out prematurely due to aggressive driving or manufacturing defects.
    * Symptoms include slipping, shuddering, or difficulty engaging gears.
    * Solution: Replace the faulty clutch components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,200 - $2,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**6-Speed Automatic**

* **Torque Converter Shudder:**
    * Torque converter may develop a shudder during acceleration or gear changes.
    * Symptoms include a vibration or shudder felt through the steering wheel or vehicle.
    * Solution: Replace the torque converter.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Front Strut Mounts:**
    * Strut mounts may wear out prematurely, causing noise and a rough ride.
    * Symptoms include clunking or rattling noises from the front of the vehicle.
    * Solution: Replace the faulty strut mounts.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Rear Shock Absorbers:**
    * Rear shock absorbers may fail, resulting in a harsh ride and reduced handling.
    * Symptoms include poor ride quality and instability over bumps.
    * Solution: Replace the faulty shock absorbers.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Infotainment System Failure:**
    * Infotainment system may experience software glitches, freezing, or slow response.
    * Symptoms include unresponsive touchscreens, audio cutouts, and system crashes.
    * Solution: Perform a software update or replace the infotainment module.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Headlight Condensation:**
    * Headlights may accumulate condensation inside the housing due to poor sealing.
    * Symptoms include fogged or hazy headlights.
    * Solution: Replace the affected headlight assembly.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Roof Antenna Water Leak:**
    * Roof antenna may allow water to leak into the cabin during heavy rain.
    * Symptoms include water stains on the headliner or dashboard.
    * Solution: Replace the faulty antenna assembly.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Windshield Wiper Motor Failure:**
    * Windshield wiper motor may fail, causing the wipers to stop working or move slowly.
    * Symptoms include intermittent or non-functioning wipers.
    * Solution: Replace the faulty wiper motor.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Air Conditioner Malfunction:**
    * Air conditioner may fail to blow cold air or malfunction due to refrigerant leaks, compressor issues, or electrical problems.
    * Symptoms include warm air blowing from the vents or reduced cooling performance.
    * Solution: Diagnose and repair the faulty components, such as the compressor, refrigerant lines, or electrical system.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Steering Wheel Controls:**
    * Steering wheel buttons may become unresponsive or malfunction due to electrical issues.
    * Symptoms include intermittent or non-functioning buttons.
    * Solution: Replace the faulty steering wheel controls.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2013 Cadillac ATS is a well-rounded luxury sedan with impressive performance and handling. However, it has some notable issues that can be costly to repair, particularly with the engine intake valves and transmission torque converter. The severity and cost of the issues vary depending on the specific components affected. It is important for potential owners to be aware of these issues and factor the potential repair costs into their decision-making.
