**Introduction**

The 2013 Cadillac Escalade EXT is a luxury pickup truck that offers a spacious cabin, powerful performance, and advanced technology features. It comes standard with a 6.2L V8 engine and a 6-speed automatic transmission, producing 403 horsepower and 417 lb-ft of torque. Other engine options include a 6.0L V8 and a 6.6L V8, with horsepower and torque ranging from 360 to 420 and 380 to 460, respectively. The Escalade EXT is praised for its comfortable ride, impressive towing and hauling capabilities, and upscale interior. However, it also has some common issues that potential owners should be aware of.

**Engine**

**6.2L V8**

- **Engine Stalling:** The engine may stall while idling or under acceleration due to a faulty throttle position sensor.
  - Solution: Replace the throttle position sensor.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
  - Recalls: N/A

- **Coolant Leak:** The coolant reservoir or hoses may develop leaks, causing coolant to leak out and potentially overheat the engine.
  - Solution: Replace the faulty reservoir or hoses.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+
  - Recalls: N/A

**6.0L V8**

- **Intake Manifold Gaskets:** The intake manifold gaskets may fail, leading to vacuum leaks and performance issues.
  - Solution: Replace the intake manifold gaskets.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+
  - Recalls: N/A

**Transmission**

**6-Speed Automatic Transmission**

- **Transmission Slipping:** The transmission may slip or hesitate during gear changes due to worn clutch packs or a faulty solenoid.
  - Solution: Rebuild or replace the transmission.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
  - Recalls: N/A

- **Reverse Gear Failure:** The transmission may fail to engage reverse gear due to a valve body malfunction or a faulty solenoid.
  - Solution: Replace the valve body or solenoid.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
  - Recalls: N/A

**Suspension**

**Front Suspension**

- **Strut Wear:** The front struts may wear out prematurely, causing a rough ride and reduced handling.
  - Solution: Replace the struts.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800+
  - Recalls: N/A

- **Control Arm Bushings:** The control arm bushings may fail, leading to loose and noisy suspension components.
  - Solution: Replace the control arm bushings.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
  - Recalls: N/A

**Electrical**

- **Battery Drain:** The vehicle may experience battery drain due to a faulty alternator or parasitic drain.
  - Solution: Diagnose and repair the underlying electrical issue.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
  - Recalls: N/A

- **HID Headlight Malfunction:** The HID headlights may fail to illuminate or flicker due to a faulty ballast or igniter.
  - Solution: Replace the faulty ballast or igniter.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
  - Recalls: N/A

**Exterior**

- **Exterior Trim Corrosion:** The exterior trim pieces, such as the grille and bumpers, may corrode over time.
  - Solution: Replace the corroded trim pieces.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
  - Recalls: N/A

- **Paint Peeling:** The paint may peel or chip in certain areas, especially on the hood and roof.
  - Solution: Repaint the affected areas.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
  - Recalls: N/A

**Interior**

- **Touchscreen Lag:** The touchscreen display may experience lag or become unresponsive due to software glitches or hardware issues.
  - Solution: Update the software or replace the touchscreen unit.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+
  - Recalls: N/A

- **Climate Control Malfunction:** The climate control system may fail to regulate temperature or airflow due to a faulty blend door actuator or a leak in the refrigerant system.
  - Solution: Replace the blend door actuator or repair the refrigerant leak.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600+
  - Recalls: N/A

**Summary**

The 2013 Cadillac Escalade EXT has a number of common issues that potential owners should be aware of before purchasing. While some issues are relatively minor and inexpensive to fix, others can be more severe and costly to repair. Overall, the severity and cost of these issues can vary greatly depending on the specific problem and the condition of the vehicle.
