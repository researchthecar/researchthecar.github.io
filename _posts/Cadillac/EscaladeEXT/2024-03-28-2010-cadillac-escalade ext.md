**Introduction:**

The 2010 Cadillac Escalade EXT is a full-size luxury pickup truck that offers a powerful V8 engine, spacious interior, and advanced technology. It comes equipped with a 6.2L V8 engine paired with a 6-speed automatic transmission. The engine produces 403 horsepower and 417 lb-ft of torque. Drivers appreciate the Escalade EXT's comfortable ride, impressive towing and hauling capabilities, and high-end features. However, it has received some criticism for its fuel economy, large size, and expensive maintenance costs.

**Engine**

- **6.2L V8**
  - **Intake Manifold Failure:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Malfunctioning intake manifold causing reduced engine power, rough idling, and Check Engine Light illumination
    - Solution: Replace intake manifold
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,500+
    - Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

- **6-Speed Automatic**
  - **Torque Converter Shudder:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Vibration felt during acceleration or deceleration, especially at low speeds
    - Solution: Replace torque converter
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
    - Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

- **Air Suspension Leaks:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Air suspension system leaks, causing the vehicle to sag on one or more corners.
    - Solution: Repair or replace air suspension components
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500+
    - Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

- **Battery Drain:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Battery drains prematurely, even when the vehicle is not in use.
    - Solution: Diagnose and repair electrical system, including alternator, battery, or parasitic draw
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600
    - Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

- **Headlight Condensation:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Condensation or moisture buildup inside headlight assemblies
    - Solution: Replace headlights or vent assemblies
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800
    - Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

- **Climate Control Malfunction:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Climate control system malfunctions, causing inconsistent or weak airflow, or temperature issues.
    - Solution: Diagnose and repair climate control components, including actuators, sensors, or control module
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    - Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

The 2010 Cadillac Escalade EXT has some notable issues, particularly with the intake manifold and torque converter. These issues can be costly to fix, and there are a fair number of vehicles affected by them. Other issues, such as air suspension leaks, headlight condensation, and climate control malfunctions, are less severe and expensive to repair. Overall, the Escalade EXT is a reliable vehicle, but potential buyers should be aware of these common issues before making a purchase.
