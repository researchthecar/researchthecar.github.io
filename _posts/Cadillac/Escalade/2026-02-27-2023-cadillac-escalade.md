# 2023 Cadillac Escalade Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2023 Cadillac Escalade continues to offer high-end luxury, massive passenger space, and robust powertrains. However, buyers should review owner-reported complaints carefully, as issues regarding electrical warnings, unexpected shifting behavior, and powertrain performance have surfaced in early ownership.

---

## Engine

### 6.2L V8 Engine (L87)

The standard 6.2-liter V8 engine produces approximately 420 horsepower and 460 lb-ft of torque, delivering strong acceleration and towing capability for this full-size luxury SUV.

**Catastrophic Engine Failure and Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers report sudden loss of propulsion, engine stalling on highways, and unexpected electrical warning lights followed by complete engine failure.
- **Solution:** Requires professional inspection, potential component replacement, or adherence to manufacturer recall directives.

### 3.0L Duramax Turbo-Diesel Inline-6 (LZ0)

The available 3.0-liter turbo-diesel engine emphasizes low-end torque and enhanced fuel economy, making it a smooth option for long-distance cruising.

## Transmission

### 10-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 10-speed automatic transmission is paired with both engine options and rear-wheel or four-wheel drive, designed to optimize fuel efficiency and power delivery.

**Unintended Neutral Shifts and Shifting Faults**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicles may unexpectedly shift into neutral while driving or display messages indicating conditions are incorrect for a shift, resulting in a temporary loss of motive power.
- **Solution:** Have a certified dealer inspect transmission control modules, software calibrations, and linkage components.

## Suspension

### Control Arms and Suspension

The suspension system utilizes advanced adaptive damping and available air ride adaptive suspension to deliver a plush, controlled ride across varied road surfaces.

**Upper Control Arm Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have experienced sudden snapping of front control components while in motion, leading to wheel detachment and severe safety hazards.
- **Solution:** Inspect front suspension components thoroughly for structural defects or wear, and replace damaged parts immediately.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V903000: Exterior Lighting:Lighting Control Module:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Cadillac&model=Escalade&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** The daytime running lights (DRLs) may not deactivate when the headlights are on. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 108, "Lamps, Reflective Devices, and Associated Equipment." DRLs that do not deactivate as intended may result in a glare, reducing visibility, and increasing the risk.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 23V642000: Suspension:Rear](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Cadillac&model=Escalade&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** The rear suspension outer control arm bolts may not have been properly heat-treated, which can cause the bolts to break. A broken suspension bolt can result in misalignment of the rear wheel, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace both rear suspension outer control arm bolts, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V674000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Brake Fluid Low Warning: Lamp](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Cadillac&model=Escalade&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Vehicle Safety Standard number 135, "Light Vehicle Brake Systems." Without a warning light, the vehicle may be driven with low brake fluid, which can reduce braking performance and increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 25V274000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Engine:Hard Parts Internal/Mechanical](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Cadillac&model=Escalade&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** Engine failure increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the engine, and as necessary, repair or replace the engine. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2023 Cadillac Escalade remains an impressive flagship SUV with commanding presence and powerful engine choices. Prospective buyers should perform a rigorous pre-purchase inspection focusing on the powertrain, transmission shifting behavior, and suspension components to ensure safety and reliability.
