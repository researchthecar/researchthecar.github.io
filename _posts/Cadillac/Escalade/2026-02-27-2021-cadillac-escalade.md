# 2021 Cadillac Escalade Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2021 Cadillac Escalade marks the debut of a fully redesigned generation for GM's flagship full-size luxury SUV. It introduced striking modern styling, a massive curved OLED dashboard display, independent rear suspension, and advanced driver assistance tech. While buyers praise its commanding presence, immense interior space, and refined ride quality, used car shoppers should approach early models from this generation with care due to reported powertrain electronic glitches, intermittent accessory failures, and notable electrical complaints.

---

## Engine

### 6.2L V8 (L87)

The standard 6.2-liter V8 engine produces 420 horsepower and 460 lb-ft of torque, providing robust acceleration and towing capability for the heavy luxury SUV. While generally strong, early production models occasionally exhibit reported valvetrain noises, misfires, or unexpected stalling incidents.

**Engine Stalling and Power Loss**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle may suddenly lose propulsion, illuminate warning lights on the dashboard, and stall while driving or slowing down.
- **Solution:** Diagnostic scanning required at a dealership or qualified repair shop to isolate failing sensors, fuel system components, or software glitches.

**Engine Knocking and Mechanical Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Loud knocking noises coming from the engine bay, accompanied by a sudden drop in acceleration or complete mechanical breakdown.
- **Solution:** Professional mechanical inspection is required; severe cases may demand major engine repair or replacement.

### 3.0L Duramax Turbo-Diesel Inline-6 (LM2)

The available 3.0-liter turbo-diesel inline-6 generates 277 horsepower and 460 lb-ft of torque, offering exceptional fuel economy and strong low-end torque for highway cruising and towing. It is a smooth, efficient alternative to the V8, though modern diesel emissions equipment requires regular maintenance.

## Transmission

### 10-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 10-speed automatic transmission is paired with both engine options and features a rotary or electronic shift interface designed for smooth gear changes and optimized highway fuel efficiency. Overall, major mechanical failures are relatively uncommon, though electronic control module updates are occasionally needed.

**Transmission Warning and Shifting Errors**
- **Symptoms:** Messages such as 'Service Transmission Now' appear on the instrument cluster, accompanied by temporary inability to shift properly.
- **Solution:** Have transmission control modules inspected for software updates or mechanical wear by a certified technician.

## Electrical

### Infotainment and Backup Camera

The high-tech cabin features a curved OLED display suite integrating navigation, cameras, and connectivity. Software glitches can occasionally cause backup camera delays or temporary infotainment resets.

**Intermittent Backup Camera Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The infotainment screen displays a blue hourglass or fails to show a rearview image when shifting into reverse.
- **Solution:** Software reflashing or camera module replacement by a dealership may be necessary to resolve intermittent display blackouts.

## Exterior

### Door Latches and Locks

Cabin convenience features are generally luxurious and feature-rich, but power latch mechanisms and electronic door locks have drawn owner complaints regarding occasional mechanical binding or electrical unresponsiveness.

**Door Latch Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Doors fail to unlock or open properly from either the inside or outside, occasionally trapping occupants inside the vehicle.
- **Solution:** Replace faulty power door latch assemblies and inspect electrical wiring harnesses within the door panels.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V739000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump:Control/Drive Module](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Cadillac&model=Escalade&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The fuel pump power control module may cause the fuel pump to fail or operate intermittently, which can interrupt fuel flow to the engine, and cause an unexpected engine stall. An engine stall increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the fuel pump power control module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V036000: Power Train:Driveline:Driveshaft](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Cadillac&model=Escalade&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** Driveshaft failure can cause a sudden loss of drive power, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the left and/or right rear driveshaft assemblies, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V421000: Air Bags:Srs Malfunction Warning Lamp/Light](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Cadillac&model=Escalade&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The air bag malfunction indicator light may fail to warn the driver of a problem with the air bag, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 21V190000: Seat Belts](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Cadillac&model=Escalade&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The third-row outboard seat belts may have been entrapped or misrouted behind the outboard seat-folding mechanism, which could result in damaged seat belts. A damaged seat belt can break during a crash, increasing the risk of injury to the occupant.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the third-row outboard seat belts, replacing any damaged seat belts, and rerouting them if necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V617000: Seat Belts:Critical Fasteners](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Cadillac&model=Escalade&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** A seat belt with a deformed rivet may not properly restrain an occupant, increasing the risk of injury during a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the rivet head formation on both the left and right side third-row seat belt buckle assemblies and replace seat belt buckle assemblies, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V827000: Exterior Lighting:Lighting Control Module:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Cadillac&model=Escalade&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The daytime running lights (DRLs) may not deactivate when the headlights are on. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 108, "Lamps, Reflective Devices, and Associated Equipment." Daytime Running Lights that do not deactivate as intended may cause a glare, reducing visibility and.
- **What to do:** Dealers will provide an over-the-air (OTA) update for the Body Control Module software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V797000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission:Control Module:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Cadillac&model=Escalade&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** Rear wheel lock-up can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will install new transmission control module software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V274000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Engine:Hard Parts Internal/Mechanical](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Cadillac&model=Escalade&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** Engine failure increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the engine, and as necessary, repair or replace the engine. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2021 Cadillac Escalade delivers top-tier luxury, massive cargo space, and impressive road presence, making it a compelling choice on the used market. However, because it was the launch year for a complete redesign, buyers should expect a higher propensity for electronic issues, software updates, and occasional powertrain complaints. When shopping for a used model, secure a comprehensive pre-purchase inspection, verify that all open recalls have been completed, and check service records closely.
