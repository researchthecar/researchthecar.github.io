**2007 Cadillac Escalade**

The 2007 Cadillac Escalade is a full-size luxury SUV offered with a 6.2L V8 engine and a 6-speed automatic transmission. It features a spacious cabin, powerful engine, and advanced technology. However, it has also been plagued by a variety of issues across different components.

**Engine**

* **6.2L V8**
    * **Intake Manifold Runner Control Valve Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: Faulty intake manifold runner control valve can lead to rough idling, engine misfires, and reduced fuel efficiency.
        * Solution: Replace intake manifold runner control valve
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $600+
        * Recalls: None Found
    * **Timing Chain Tensioner Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: Worn or damaged timing chain tensioner can cause rattling noises, reduced engine performance, and potential engine failure.
        * Solution: Replace timing chain tensioner and related components
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,200 - $1,800+
        * Recalls: NHTSA Campaign Number: 14V526000

**Transmission**

**6-Speed Automatic Transmission**
    * **Torque Converter Shudder**
        * Description/Symptoms: Vibrations or shuddering felt during acceleration or gear changes.
        * Solution: Replace torque converter
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,000+
        * Recalls: None Found
    * **Transmission Fluid Leaks**
        * Description/Symptoms: Leaking transmission fluid can lead to reduced transmission performance and potential failure.
        * Solution: Repair or replace leaking components, such as gaskets, seals, or hoses
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Front Control Arm Bushings**
    * Description/Symptoms: Worn or damaged front control arm bushings can cause vibrations, noise, and reduced handling stability.
    * Solution: Replace front control arm bushings
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $600+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Rear Air Suspension Compressor Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Faulty rear air suspension compressor can lead to uneven ride height, reduced suspension performance, and potential airbag malfunction.
    * Solution: Replace rear air suspension compressor
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $1,500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Alternator Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Failed alternator can prevent the vehicle from starting or powering electrical systems.
    * Solution: Replace alternator
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $700+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Battery Discharge**
    * Description/Symptoms: Vehicle battery can discharge quickly or fail to hold a charge.
    * Solution: Inspect and replace battery, check electrical system for parasitic drains
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Tailgate Handle Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Broken or loose tailgate handle can prevent the tailgate from opening properly.
    * Solution: Replace tailgate handle
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $300+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Headlight Condensation**
    * Description/Symptoms: Moisture buildup inside headlight housing can affect visibility and damage electrical components.
    * Solution: Replace headlight housing or reseal existing housing
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Navigation System Malfunction**
    * Description/Symptoms: GPS system may malfunction, lose signal, or display incorrect navigation information.
    * Solution: Update navigation software, replace GPS antenna, or repair faulty navigation control module
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Power Seat Control Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Faulty power seat control module can cause seats to move erratically or become inoperable.
    * Solution: Replace power seat control module
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2007 Cadillac Escalade has encountered various issues, ranging from minor inconveniences to major mechanical failures. Engine and transmission problems are common, with potential high repair costs. Suspension and electrical issues can also be costly to fix. The interior and exterior issues are generally less severe. Overall, the vehicle's reliability and repair costs can be a significant concern for owners.
