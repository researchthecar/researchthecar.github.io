# 2022 Cadillac Escalade Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2022 Cadillac Escalade is a full-size luxury SUV offering impressive road presence, cutting-edge technology, and robust powertrain options. However, owner reports submitted to the NHTSA highlight several notable mechanical and electrical concerns that prospective buyers should keep in mind. While many owners enjoy its comfort and capability, reviewing potential drivetrain and electronic glitches is crucial before making a purchase.

---

## Engine

### 6.2L V8 L87 Engine

The 6.2-liter V8 L87 engine produces approximately 420 horsepower and 460 lb-ft of torque, providing strong acceleration and towing capability for the heavy SUV. While generally robust, a subset of owners have experienced unexpected stalling and mechanical failures.

**Engine Stalling and Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Owners report unexpected engine shutdown while driving, severe stalling, and in some cases complete engine seizure.
- **Solution:** Requires professional diagnostic evaluation by a certified technician. Repairs may involve component replacements or major engine service depending on internal wear.

### 3.0L Duramax Turbo-Diesel I6 Engine

The 3.0-liter Duramax turbo-diesel inline-six produces 277 horsepower and 460 lb-ft of torque, offering exceptional highway fuel economy and low-end pulling power for the Escalade. It provides a smooth, torque-rich driving experience suited for long-distance travel.

## Transmission

### 10-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 10-speed automatic transmission delivers power smoothly across all driving conditions and helps optimize fuel efficiency. Although generally well-regarded, some owners have noted shifting irregularities and internal hydraulic valve concerns.

**Unintended Neutral Shift and Valve Body Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers experience unexpected shifts into neutral while traveling at highway speeds, accompanied by warning lights and potential loss of drive power.
- **Solution:** Consult a dealership or transmission specialist to inspect internal components, such as the valve body and control modules, for sticking or binding.

## Electrical

### Electrical Warning Lights and Camera System

The Escalade features advanced digital cockpits and extensive electronic architectures that manage driver aids and infotainment. Intermittent software glitches and camera malfunctions represent common minor grievances among owners.

**Dashboard Warnings and Backup Camera Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers notice intermittent electrical warning lights, sudden loss of vehicle power features, and progressive failure of the backup camera display.
- **Solution:** Have the vehicle's electrical system scanned for diagnostic trouble codes and update or replace faulty control modules and camera hardware as needed.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V617000: Seat Belts:Critical Fasteners](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Cadillac&model=Escalade&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** A seat belt with a deformed rivet may not properly restrain an occupant, increasing the risk of injury during a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the rivet head formation on both the left and right side third-row seat belt buckle assemblies and replace seat belt buckle assemblies, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V903000: Exterior Lighting:Lighting Control Module:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Cadillac&model=Escalade&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** The daytime running lights (DRLs) may not deactivate when the headlights are on. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 108, "Lamps, Reflective Devices, and Associated Equipment." DRLs that do not deactivate as intended may result in a glare, reducing visibility, and increasing the risk.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 25V274000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Engine:Hard Parts Internal/Mechanical](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Cadillac&model=Escalade&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** Engine failure increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the engine, and as necessary, repair or replace the engine. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V085000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission:Control Module:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Cadillac&model=Escalade&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** Rear wheels that lock up while driving increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will install new transmission control module (TCM) software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2022 Cadillac Escalade remains a luxurious and highly capable full-size SUV, but prospective buyers should perform a thorough pre-purchase inspection. Pay close attention to powertrain operation, looking specifically for any signs of hesitation, unexpected shifts into neutral, or electrical warnings. Securing a comprehensive warranty can offer additional peace of mind against potential repair costs.
