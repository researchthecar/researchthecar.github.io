# 2017 Cadillac Escalade Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2017 Cadillac Escalade offers a commanding luxury experience paired with robust V8 power and generous towing capacity. However, prospective buyers should approach the used market with awareness of certain mechanical and electrical pain points reported by owners. While its traditional body-on-frame architecture and plush interior make it a popular cruiser, staying informed on potential engine management and transmission quirks is essential for a satisfying ownership experience.

---

## Engine

### 6.2L V8 Engine

Powered by a 6.2-liter V8 engine generating approximately 420 horsepower and 460 lb-ft of torque, this powerplant delivers swift acceleration and strong towing capability. It features Active Fuel Management (AFM) cylinder deactivation to improve highway efficiency, though owners should monitor for potential valvetrain wear.

**Active Fuel Management (AFM) Lifter Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may experience sudden stuttering, violent shaking, engine misfires, and loud ticking noises from the valvetrain while driving.
- **Solution:** Replace the failed AFM/DOD lifters and inspect the camshaft for scoring or damage, often requiring a complete valvetrain service.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

Equipped with an 8-speed automatic transmission paired with standard rear-wheel drive or optional four-wheel drive, providing smooth power delivery across a wide gear range. While generally capable, some owners report shifting harshness and driveline vibrations over time.

**Rough Shifting and Driveline Clunking**
- **Symptoms:** Harsh shifts between lower gears, sudden lurching when engaging forward or reverse, and abnormal vibrations during acceleration.
- **Solution:** Reprogram the transmission control module, update software, or inspect internal components for wear.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

Issues related to premature wear or brake assist alerts can compromise stopping confidence if left unaddressed.

**Brake System Malfunction and Hard Pedal**
- **Symptoms:** Sudden reduction in braking effectiveness, requiring excessive pedal effort to stop, often accompanied by warnings to service the brake assist.
- **Solution:** Inspect brake booster, calipers, lines, and pads for abnormal wear or component failure.

## Steering

### Power Steering and Column

Steering responsiveness is normally light and precise, though unexpected power steering loss poses significant safety risks.

**Electric Power Steering Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Power steering assist cuts out unexpectedly while driving, causing the steering wheel to become stiff and difficult to turn.
- **Solution:** Inspect the electric power steering (EPS) module, wiring connections, and steering rack.

## Electrical

### Infotainment and Mirrors

Cabin electronics provide high-tech convenience features, though occasional glitches in the infotainment and mirror memory systems have been documented.

**CUE Infotainment Erratic Behavior**
- **Symptoms:** The touchscreen becomes unresponsive or randomly scrolls through screens and features, preventing access to backup camera views.
- **Solution:** Reset or replace the CUE display module as recommended by diagnostic procedures.

**Randomly Tilting Side Mirrors**
- **Symptoms:** Side view mirrors spontaneously adjust upward toward the sky or downward toward the ground.
- **Solution:** Replace faulty seat memory modules, battery cables, or mirror assembly components.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V645000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Power Assist:Vacuum](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Cadillac&model=Escalade&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** As the vacuum level drops, the brake assist decreases, increasing braking effort, extending the distance required to stop the vehicle, thereby increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will reprogram the Electronic Brake Control Module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V651000: Air Bags:Frontal](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Cadillac&model=Escalade&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** In the affected vehicles, certain driving conditions may cause the air bag sensing and diagnostic module (SDM) software to activate a diagnostic test. During this test, deployment of the frontal air bags and the seat belt pretensioners would not occur in the event of a crash.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will reflash the SDM software. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2017 Cadillac Escalade remains a compelling choice for buyers seeking full-size luxury, spacious seating, and strong towing capabilities. However, potential owners should thoroughly inspect the vehicle for valvetrain health, transmission shift quality, and proper operation of electronics. A pre-purchase inspection by a knowledgeable mechanic is strongly recommended to catch any hidden issues before finalizing a purchase.
