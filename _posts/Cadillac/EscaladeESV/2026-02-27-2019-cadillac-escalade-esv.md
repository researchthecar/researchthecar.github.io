# 2019 Cadillac Escalade ESV Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used full-size luxury SUV, the 2019 Cadillac Escalade ESV offers imposing style, extensive cargo capacity, and strong standard V8 performance. While many owners enjoy comfortable family hauling and towing capabilities, prospective buyers should review reported owner complaints regarding powertrain performance, electrical accessories, and complex mechanical features. Keeping a close eye on service records and conducting a thorough pre-purchase inspection can help mitigate potential ownership surprises.

---

## Engine

### 6.2L V8 L86

The 6.2-liter V8 L86 engine produces 420 horsepower and 460 lb-ft of torque, providing robust acceleration and towing capacity for this large SUV. Overall performance is generally strong, though some owners have reported valve train component issues.

**Lifter Failure and Loss of Power**
- **Symptoms:** Rough idle, ticking or knocking noises, blinking check engine light, and sudden loss of engine propulsion while driving at highway speeds.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified mechanic inspect the valve train, replace failed lifters and damaged pushrods, or perform comprehensive engine diagnostics.

## Transmission

### 10-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 10-speed automatic transmission works in conjunction with rear-wheel drive or four-wheel drive to optimize highway fuel economy and power delivery. While shifts are generally smooth, prospective buyers should test-drive thoroughly for any harsh engagement or locking symptoms.

**Transmission Lockup and Drivetrain Binding**
- **Symptoms:** Intermittent transmission binding, rough running conditions, and sudden wheel lockups causing a loss of vehicle control.
- **Solution:** Inspect transmission fluid condition, check for internal mechanical wear, and consult a transmission specialist for diagnostic evaluation.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

The braking system provides stopping power suited for a heavy full-size luxury SUV, supported by various electronic assists. Routine inspection of pads, rotors, and ABS sensors ensures consistent stopping performance.

**Forward Collision Mitigation Delay**
- **Symptoms:** Electronic safety systems fail to automatically slow or stop the vehicle in time during sudden traffic stopping situations despite driver input.
- **Solution:** Have dealership technicians recalibrate forward-facing sensors, check brake pad life, and inspect the collision avoidance system modules.

## Electrical

### Power Running Boards

The electrical architecture controls numerous convenience features, displays, and sensors throughout the cabin and exterior. Cabin electronics are generally feature-rich, though motorized accessories require careful verification during a test drive.

**Retractable Running Board Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Power running boards fail to deploy or retract properly, creating potential tripping hazards when entering or exiting the vehicle.
- **Solution:** Check electrical connections, clear debris from hinges, and service or replace the running board motors and sensors as needed.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V673000: Seat Belts:Rear/Other](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Cadillac&model=Escalade+ESV&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** Certain second-row or third-row rear seatbelts retractor assemblies may not automatically lock when the seatbelt is fully pulled out of the retractor, possibly preventing a child seat from being properly secured. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 208, "Occupant Crash.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the rear seatbelt retractors and replace them if necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2019 Cadillac Escalade ESV remains an attractive choice for buyers seeking luxury, space, and towing utility, but it demands careful evaluation before purchase. While major mechanical failures are not universal, reviewing service histories for engine valve train health and transmission operation is essential. Buyers should thoroughly test all power accessories, safety sensors, and drivetrain functions to ensure everything operates correctly.
