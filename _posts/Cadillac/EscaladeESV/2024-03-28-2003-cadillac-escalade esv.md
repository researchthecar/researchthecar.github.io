### 2003 Cadillac Escalade ESV Overview

The 2003 Cadillac Escalade ESV is a full-size SUV that offers spacious seating for up to eight passengers and ample cargo room. It comes with a powerful 6.0L V8 engine that produces 345 horsepower and 380 lb-ft of torque, paired with a 4-speed automatic transmission. The Escalade ESV is known for its luxurious interior, comfortable ride, and strong performance. 

However, like any vehicle, the 2003 Escalade ESV has its fair share of issues. Here's a comprehensive list of problems reported by owners:

**Engine**

* **Engine Knock**
    * Description/Symptoms: Knocking noise coming from the engine, particularly when the engine is cold.
    * Solution: Replace engine knocking sensor.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $250
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Intake Manifold Gasket Leak**
    * Description/Symptoms: Leak in the intake manifold gasket, causing a rough idle, decreased performance, and possible misfires.
    * Solution: Replace intake manifold gasket.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

* **4-Speed Automatic Transmission Slipping**
    * Description/Symptoms: Transmission slipping between gears, causing rough shifting and decreased performance.
    * Solution: Rebuild or replace transmission.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Rear Air Suspension Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Rear suspension sagging, causing an uneven ride and reduced handling.
    * Solution: Replace air suspension system.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $1,500
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Front Suspension Bushing Wear**
    * Description/Symptoms: Worn suspension bushings, leading to a loose and rattling suspension.
    * Solution: Replace suspension bushings.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $300
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain**
    * Description/Symptoms: Battery drains quickly, especially when parked.
    * Solution: Diagnose and replace faulty electrical components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Alternator Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Alternator stops charging the battery, causing electrical problems and eventually a dead battery.
    * Solution: Replace alternator.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $600
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Body Panel Rust**
    * Description/Symptoms: Rust on body panels, particularly around wheel wells and lower panels.
    * Solution: Repair or replace rusted panels.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Headlamp Condensation**
    * Description/Symptoms: Condensation buildup inside the headlamps, reducing visibility.
    * Solution: Replace headlamps or install a vent kit.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Sunroof Leaks**
    * Description/Symptoms: Leak through the sunroof, causing water to enter the cabin.
    * Solution: Replace sunroof seals or drain tubes.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Power Window Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Power windows fail to operate, either intermittently or permanently.
    * Solution: Replace power window motor or switch.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

Overall, the 2003 Cadillac Escalade ESV is a reliable and enjoyable vehicle. However, it has its fair share of issues, especially with the transmission and suspension. The severity of the issues varies from minor annoyances to major repairs, with costs ranging from a few hundred dollars to over a thousand dollars. Potential buyers should be aware of these potential problems before making a purchase decision.
