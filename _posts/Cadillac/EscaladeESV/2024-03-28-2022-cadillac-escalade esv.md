**Introduction**
The 2022 Cadillac Escalade ESV is a full-size luxury SUV that offers a powerful engine, a spacious interior, and a range of advanced features. Other engine and transmission options include a 3.0L Duramax turbocharged diesel V6 engine paired with a 10-speed automatic transmission, a 6.2L V8 engine paired with a 10-speed automatic transmission, and a 420-horsepower 6.2L V8 engine paired with the 10-speed automatic transmission.

Despite its impressive features, the Escalade ESV has received mixed reviews regarding its reliability.

**Engine**
- **6.2L V8 Engine**
    - **Intake Valve Deposits**
        - Description/Symptoms: Intake valves can accumulate carbon deposits over time, leading to reduced engine performance, rough idling, and poor fuel efficiency.
        - Solution: Removing the intake manifold and cleaning the intake valves
        - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
        - Recalls: None Found
- **3.0L Duramax Turbocharged Diesel V6 Engine**
    - **Diesel Engine Fuel Filter Issues:**
        - Description/Symptoms: The fuel filter may become clogged or damaged, restricting fuel flow and causing engine power loss or stalling.
        - Solution: Replacing the fuel filter
        - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
        - Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**
- **10-Speed Automatic Transmission**
    - **Transmission Fluid Leaks**
         - Description/Symptoms: Leaks can occur from the transmission pan gasket, output shaft seal, or other components, leading to low transmission fluid levels and potential damage.
         - Solution: Replacing the leaking component and resealing the transmission
         - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+
         - Recalls: None Found
    - **Shifting Issues**
        - Description/Symptoms: The transmission may exhibit rough or delayed shifts, slipping, or a lack of response.
        - Solution: Diagnosing the issue and repairing or replacing the affected components, such as solenoids, sensors, or the transmission control module
        - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $3,000+
        - Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**
- **Magnetic Ride Control System**
    - **Strut Failure**
        - Description/Symptoms: The struts may fail due to worn or damaged components, leading to reduced ride quality, handling issues, and uneven tire wear.
        - Solution: Replacing the affected strut(s)
        - Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+ per strut
        - Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**
- **Battery Issues**
    - **Premature Battery Failure**
        - Description/Symptoms: The battery may fail prematurely due to manufacturing defects, overcharging, or other factors, resulting in starting problems or electrical system malfunctions.
        - Solution: Replacing the battery
        - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
        - Recalls: None Found
- **Infotainment System**
    - **Infotainment Screen Blackouts**
        - Description/Symptoms: The infotainment system screen may experience intermittent blackouts, rendering the system unusable.
        - Solution: Updating the software or replacing the infotainment unit
        - Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $1,000+
        - Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**
- **Exterior Trim Issues**
    - **Chrome Trim Peeling**
        - Description/Symptoms: The chrome trim on the vehicle's exterior may peel or flake off, resulting in a degraded appearance.
        - Solution: Replacing the affected trim pieces
        - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+
        - Recalls: None Found

**Interior**
- **Interior Trim Rattling**
    - **Dashboard Rattle**
         - Description/Symptoms: The dashboard or other interior trim pieces may rattle or vibrate during driving, creating unwanted noise.
         - Solution: Identifying the source of the rattle and securing or replacing the affected components
         - Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500
         - Recalls: None Found

**Summary**
The 2022 Cadillac Escalade ESV offers impressive features and capabilities, but it has also received reports of various issues that can impact its reliability and ownership experience. The engine, transmission, and suspension components have been the subject of some concerns, along with electrical and exterior issues. While some issues may be minor and relatively inexpensive to fix, others can be more severe and costly. It's important for potential buyers to consider these potential issues and thoroughly research the vehicle's reliability before making a purchase decision.
