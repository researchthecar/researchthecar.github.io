**Introduction:**

The 2012 Cadillac Escalade ESV is a full-size luxury SUV offered with a variety of engine and transmission options. It has a powerful V8 engine, spacious interior, and advanced technology features. However, like most vehicles, it has experienced some issues that owners should be aware of.

**Engine**

**6.2L V8 with 6-Speed Automatic Transmission**

**Cylinder Deactivation Failure**
Description/Symptoms: The engine may hesitate or stall due to a failure in the cylinder deactivation system.
Solution: Replacement of the cylinder deactivation solenoids or valve lifters.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,500+
Recalls: NHTSA Campaign Number 13V118000

**Transmission**

**6-Speed Automatic Transmission**

**Slipping Gears**
Description/Symptoms: The transmission may slip out of gear, causing a loss of power.
Solution: Replacement or rebuilding of the transmission.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $3,000 - $5,000+
Recalls: NHTSA Campaign Number 13V304000

**Suspension**

**No Issues Found**

**Electrical**

**Alternator Failure**
Description/Symptoms: The battery may not charge properly due to a faulty alternator.
Solution: Replacement of the alternator.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

**No Issues Found**

**Interior**

**Cruise Control Switch Failure**
Description/Symptoms: The cruise control switch may become inoperable or operate intermittently.
Solution: Replacement of the cruise control switch assembly.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $250
Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

The 2012 Cadillac Escalade ESV has experienced some issues, primarily related to the engine, transmission, and electrical system. The cost of repairs can be significant, especially for major components like the transmission. However, the vehicle also offers excellent performance and features, making it a desirable option for those seeking a full-size luxury SUV.
