The 2014 Cadillac Escalade ESV is a full-size luxury SUV known for its spaciousness, powerful engine, and upscale features. It's a popular choice for families needing ample cargo and passenger space, as well as those who appreciate a refined driving experience. While many owners enjoy the Escalade ESV's comfort and capabilities, some common complaints have surfaced over the years. These include issues with the automatic transmission, electrical glitches, and problems with the suspension system. The 2014 Escalade ESV came standard with a 6.2L V8 engine producing 420 horsepower and 460 lb-ft of torque, paired with a 6-speed automatic transmission.

**Engine**

**6.2L V8**

*   **Active Fuel Management (AFM) Lifter Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The AFM system, designed to improve fuel economy by deactivating cylinders, can lead to premature lifter failure. Symptoms include a ticking or knocking noise from the engine, misfires, rough idling, and a check engine light.
    *   Solution: Replacing the affected lifters and potentially the camshaft is often required. Some owners choose to disable the AFM system to prevent future issues.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,500 - $5,000+

*   **Oil Consumption**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners report excessive oil consumption, requiring frequent top-offs. This can be caused by worn piston rings, valve seals, or issues with the AFM system.
    *   Solution: Depending on the cause, repairs may include replacing piston rings, valve seals, or addressing AFM-related issues.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $6,000+

**Transmission**

**6L80 6-Speed Automatic**

*   **Harsh Shifting/Slipping**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The 6L80 transmission can exhibit harsh or erratic shifting, particularly between certain gears. Slipping during acceleration is also a common complaint.
    *   Solution: A transmission flush and fluid change may alleviate minor issues. More severe cases may require valve body replacement or a complete transmission rebuild.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 (fluid change) - $4,000+ (rebuild)
Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Air Suspension Compressor Failure (if equipped)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Escalade ESV models equipped with air suspension can experience compressor failure, leading to the vehicle sitting lower than normal or failing to level properly. A warning light on the dashboard may also illuminate.
    *   Solution: Replacing the air suspension compressor is the typical fix.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,500+

*   **Magnetic Ride Control (MRC) Shock Absorber Failure (if equipped)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The MRC system, designed to provide a smoother ride, can suffer from shock absorber failure. Symptoms include a bouncy or unstable ride, particularly over bumps. Leaking fluid from the shock absorbers may also be visible.
    *   Solution: Replacing the failed MRC shock absorbers is necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $600 - $1,200+ per shock

**Electrical**

*   **Battery Drain**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners report unexplained battery drain, even when the vehicle is not in use. This can lead to difficulty starting the car.
    *   Solution: Identifying the source of the parasitic draw is essential. Common culprits include faulty modules, aftermarket accessories, or a failing alternator.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+

*   **Navigation/Infotainment System Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Problems with the navigation and infotainment system can include freezing, slow response times, and display malfunctions.
    *   Solution: Software updates may resolve some issues. In other cases, replacing the head unit may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $1,500+

**Exterior**

*   **Liftgate Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The power liftgate can malfunction, failing to open or close properly. This can be caused by a faulty motor, struts, or latch mechanism.
    *   Solution: Replacing the defective component is the typical fix.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+

**Interior**

*   **Heated/Cooled Seat Malfunction**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The heated and cooled seats can stop working, providing no heat or cooling. This can be due to a faulty heating element, blower motor, or control module.
    *   Solution: Replacing the defective component is necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+

**Summary**

The 2014 Cadillac Escalade ESV, while a luxurious and capable SUV, is prone to certain issues. Engine problems related to the AFM system and oil consumption can be costly to address. Transmission issues, electrical glitches, and suspension problems also contribute to the ownership experience. The severity of these issues ranges from minor inconveniences to major repairs, with costs varying accordingly. Potential buyers should be aware of these common complaints and have the vehicle inspected by a qualified mechanic before purchase.
