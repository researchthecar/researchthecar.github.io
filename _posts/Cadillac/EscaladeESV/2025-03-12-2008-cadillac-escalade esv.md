The 2008 Cadillac Escalade ESV is a full-size luxury SUV known for its spaciousness, powerful engine, and upscale features. While appreciated for its comfortable ride and towing capacity, it's also faced criticism for its fuel economy and relatively high maintenance costs. Available engine options include the 6.2L V8 engine which produces 403 horsepower and 417 lb-ft of torque, paired with a 6-speed automatic transmission. Common likes include the ESV's ample passenger and cargo space, while common dislikes often revolve around fuel consumption and certain reliability concerns.

**Engine**

**6.2L V8 (L92)**

*   **Oil Consumption**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Excessive oil consumption, requiring frequent oil additions between changes. Can lead to fouled spark plugs, catalytic converter damage, and eventually engine damage.
    *   Solution: Monitoring oil levels frequently, using higher viscosity oil, or potentially requiring piston ring replacement or engine rebuild in severe cases.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $3,000 - $7,000+ (for engine rebuild or replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Active Fuel Management (AFM) Lifter Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Ticking noise from the engine, misfires, and potential engine damage. Caused by the AFM lifters collapsing, preventing cylinders from firing properly.
    *   Solution: Replacing the failed lifters, potentially disabling AFM system with aftermarket parts/tuning.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**6L80 6-Speed Automatic Transmission**

*   **Torque Converter Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Shuddering at highway speeds, harsh shifting, or complete loss of drive. Often accompanied by a diagnostic trouble code (DTC) related to the torque converter.
    *   Solution: Replacing the torque converter, flushing the transmission fluid, and inspecting for further damage.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Hard Shifting/Slipping**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Abrupt or delayed shifts, transmission slipping between gears, or difficulty engaging reverse.
    *   Solution: Transmission fluid flush and filter change, valve body repair or replacement, or transmission rebuild/replacement in severe cases.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 (fluid/filter) - $4,000+ (rebuild/replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Air Suspension Compressor Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The rear of the vehicle sagging, the air compressor running constantly, or a "Service Ride Control" message displayed on the dashboard.
    *   Solution: Replacing the air suspension compressor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Air Suspension Strut/Shock Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Sagging suspension, bouncy ride, hissing sound from the strut/shock, or "Service Ride Control" message.
    *   Solution: Replacing the leaking air strut/shock.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+ (per strut/shock)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Battery Drain**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Dead battery, requiring frequent jumps or replacement. Can be caused by parasitic draws from various electrical components.
    *   Solution: Identifying and repairing the source of the parasitic draw (e.g., faulty door actuator, OnStar module, etc.).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+ (depending on the source)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Instrument Panel Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Gauges malfunctioning, dim or flickering display, or complete failure of the instrument panel.
    *   Solution: Repairing or replacing the instrument panel.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Liftgate Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Liftgate failing to open or close properly, requiring manual assistance.
    *   Solution: Replacing the liftgate actuator, motor, or hinges.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Navigation/Infotainment System Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: System freezing, unresponsive touchscreen, or complete failure.
    *   Solution: Repairing or replacing the navigation/infotainment unit.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,200+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Heated/Cooled Seat Malfunction**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Seats not heating or cooling properly, or the system failing entirely.
    *   Solution: Replacing the heating/cooling element or control module.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $700+ (per seat)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2008 Cadillac Escalade ESV can present owners with a range of issues, some of which can be quite costly to repair. Engine-related problems, particularly oil consumption and AFM lifter failure, can be significant expenses. Suspension issues, especially those related to the air suspension system, are also common. Electrical and interior problems, while less severe, can still be frustrating. Potential buyers should carefully inspect the vehicle's maintenance history and be prepared for potential repairs, particularly concerning the engine and suspension.
