# 2021 Cadillac Escalade ESV Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2021 Cadillac Escalade ESV, buyers will find a luxurious, spacious full-size SUV equipped with modern technology and powerful engine choices. While owners generally praise its comfort and road presence, real-world complaints highlight potential mechanical and electrical hiccups. Prospective buyers should review vehicle service histories carefully and perform a thorough pre-purchase inspection to catch any underlying drivetrain or electrical concerns.

---

## Engine

### 6.2L V8 Engine

The 6.2-liter V8 engine produces 420 horsepower and 460 lb-ft of torque, providing strong acceleration for this heavy SUV. While generally robust, some owners have reported drivability issues and stalling.

**Engine Stalling and Loss of Motive Power**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle may experience unexpected engine stalling at stops or while driving, accompanied by a loss of motive power and dashboard warning lights.
- **Solution:** Have a certified mechanic or dealership diagnose the electrical or fuel delivery systems and check for applicable software updates.

**Abnormal Engine Noises and Misfires**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may notice unusual rattling sounds, rough idling, or misfires, sometimes accompanied by blue or white exhaust smoke and a check engine light.
- **Solution:** Inspect ignition components, fuel injectors, and internal engine components for wear or failure.

### 3.0L Duramax Turbo-Diesel Inline-6

The 3.0-liter Duramax turbo-diesel engine generates 277 horsepower and 460 lb-ft of torque, offering excellent low-end grunt and superior fuel economy for long-distance cruising.

## Transmission

### 10-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 10-speed automatic transmission is paired with both engine options, delivering smooth gear changes and optimized highway efficiency. Routine monitoring is recommended as some owners report electronic shift behavior concerns.

**Unintended Neutral Shift and Shifting Irregularities**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers report unexpected shifts into neutral, gear dragging, or warning messages such as 'Service Transmission Now - Unable to Shift Soon.'
- **Solution:** Check transmission fluid levels, inspect valve bodies, and consult a dealer for potential control module reprogramming.

## Interior

### Cabin Electronics and Climate Controls

electrical issues in convenience features and sensors can occasionally cause minor frustrations for passengers.

**Malfunctioning Rear Climate and Door Latches**
- **Symptoms:** Rear passenger vents may occasionally blow unexpectedly hot air, and rear doors may fail to open from the inside or outside.
- **Solution:** Inspect door latch mechanisms, child safety lock actuators, and climate control blend doors for electronic or mechanical failure.

## Safety

### Seat Belts

Safety equipment generally performs well, though recall compliance is critical for proper operation.

**Seat Belt Retractor Concerns**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have experienced delays in obtaining replacement parts for safety recalls related to seat belt assemblies.
- **Solution:** Verify through a dealership whether all safety recall repairs have been properly completed.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V455000: Steering:Steering Control Module](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Cadillac&model=Escalade+ESV&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** A software issue may cause a loss of power steering assist at startup or while driving. Increased steering effort, particularly at lower speeds, increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 22V036000: Power Train:Driveline:Driveshaft](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Cadillac&model=Escalade+ESV&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** Driveshaft failure can cause a sudden loss of drive power, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the left and/or right rear driveshaft assemblies, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V811000: Seat Belts](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Cadillac&model=Escalade+ESV&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** If an incorrect bolt was used, the seat belt may not be properly attached to the vehicle. If a seat belt assembly is not properly attached to the vehicle, the seat belt may not properly restrain an occupant in the event of a crash, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will replace the suspect bolts, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V739000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump:Control/Drive Module](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Cadillac&model=Escalade+ESV&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The fuel pump power control module may cause the fuel pump to fail or operate intermittently, which can interrupt fuel flow to the engine, and cause an unexpected engine stall. An engine stall increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the fuel pump power control module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V421000: Air Bags:Srs Malfunction Warning Lamp/Light](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Cadillac&model=Escalade+ESV&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The air bag malfunction indicator light may fail to warn the driver of a problem with the air bag, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 21V190000: Seat Belts](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Cadillac&model=Escalade+ESV&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The third-row outboard seat belts may have been entrapped or misrouted behind the outboard seat-folding mechanism, which could result in damaged seat belts. A damaged seat belt can break during a crash, increasing the risk of injury to the occupant.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the third-row outboard seat belts, replacing any damaged seat belts, and rerouting them if necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V617000: Seat Belts:Critical Fasteners](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Cadillac&model=Escalade+ESV&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** A seat belt with a deformed rivet may not properly restrain an occupant, increasing the risk of injury during a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the rivet head formation on both the left and right side third-row seat belt buckle assemblies and replace seat belt buckle assemblies, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V827000: Exterior Lighting:Lighting Control Module:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Cadillac&model=Escalade+ESV&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The daytime running lights (DRLs) may not deactivate when the headlights are on. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 108, "Lamps, Reflective Devices, and Associated Equipment." Daytime Running Lights that do not deactivate as intended may cause a glare, reducing visibility and.
- **What to do:** Dealers will provide an over-the-air (OTA) update for the Body Control Module software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2021 Cadillac Escalade ESV offers unmatched luxury, massive cargo space, and impressive towing capability. However, used-car buyers should remain vigilant regarding potential electrical glitches, transmission shifting quirks, and engine drivability complaints reported by some owners. Ensuring all past recalls have been serviced and obtaining a comprehensive pre-purchase inspection will help secure a reliable ownership experience.
