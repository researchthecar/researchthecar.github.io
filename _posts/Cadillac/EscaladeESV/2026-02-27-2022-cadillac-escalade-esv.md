# 2022 Cadillac Escalade ESV Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2022 Cadillac Escalade ESV, buyers will find a luxurious and spacious full-size SUV equipped with powerful engine options. However, owner reports submitted to safety regulators highlight several potential powertrain and electrical concerns that warrant a careful inspection before purchase. Prospective buyers should thoroughly test drive the vehicle and review maintenance records to ensure all major systems operate smoothly.

---

## Engine

### 6.2L V8 Engine

The 6.2-liter V8 engine produces 420 horsepower and 460 lb-ft of torque, offering robust towing and acceleration capabilities. While generally powerful and refined, this powerplant has been associated in some owner reports with unexpected stalling and internal mechanical failures.

**Engine Stalling and Loss of Power**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have reported sudden engine failure or loss of motive power while driving at highway speeds, accompanied by check engine warning lights.
- **Solution:** Requires professional diagnostic inspection of the fuel, ignition, and electrical systems to identify the root cause of the stall.

**Internal Engine Damage**
- **Symptoms:** Severe mechanical failure involving internal components such as the crankshaft and bottom-end seals, necessitating major engine repairs or replacement.
- **Solution:** Inspection by a qualified mechanic to assess internal wear; severe cases require a complete engine replacement.

### 3.0L Duramax Turbo-Diesel I6 Engine

The 3.0-liter Duramax turbo-diesel inline-six generates 277 horsepower and 460 lb-ft of torque, prioritizing strong low-end pulling power and high fuel efficiency for long-distance cruising. It is a popular option for buyers seeking improved highway range.

## Transmission

### 10-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 10-speed automatic transmission is paired with both engine options and helps optimize fuel economy and power delivery. Owners have occasionally reported unexpected shifting behavior, making a comprehensive pre-purchase test drive essential.

**Unintended Neutral Shift**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may unexpectedly shift into neutral while driving at highway speeds or when slowing down, posing a significant safety hazard.
- **Solution:** Diagnostic evaluation of the transmission control module and hydraulic systems; software updates or component repairs may be required.

## Steering

### Steering Gear

The steering system provides responsive handling for a large SUV, though isolated component wear has been documented early in the vehicle's lifecycle.

**Premature Steering Gear Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Unusual noises emanating from the steering wheel area during operation, leading to dealership inspection and required component replacement.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the defective steering gear assembly under warranty or through professional service.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V617000: Seat Belts:Critical Fasteners](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Cadillac&model=Escalade+ESV&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** A seat belt with a deformed rivet may not properly restrain an occupant, increasing the risk of injury during a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the rivet head formation on both the left and right side third-row seat belt buckle assemblies and replace seat belt buckle assemblies, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V903000: Exterior Lighting:Lighting Control Module:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Cadillac&model=Escalade+ESV&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** The daytime running lights (DRLs) may not deactivate when the headlights are on. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 108, "Lamps, Reflective Devices, and Associated Equipment." DRLs that do not deactivate as intended may result in a glare, reducing visibility, and increasing the risk.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 25V274000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Engine:Hard Parts Internal/Mechanical](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Cadillac&model=Escalade+ESV&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** Engine failure increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the engine, and as necessary, repair or replace the engine. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V085000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission:Control Module:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Cadillac&model=Escalade+ESV&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** Rear wheels that lock up while driving increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will install new transmission control module (TCM) software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2022 Cadillac Escalade ESV delivers exceptional luxury, cabin space, and towing performance. However, because owner complaints highlight potential risks involving sudden engine stalls, unintended neutral shifts, and electrical glitches, buyers should exercise caution. Always obtain a detailed vehicle history report, verify that all applicable updates or service campaigns have been completed, and perform an extensive pre-purchase inspection.
