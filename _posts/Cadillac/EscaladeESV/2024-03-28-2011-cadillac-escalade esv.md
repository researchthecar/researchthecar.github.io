**2011 Cadillac Escalade ESV**

The 2011 Cadillac Escalade ESV is a full-size luxury SUV offered with a choice of two engines and two transmissions. The 6.2L V8 engine produces 403 horsepower and 417 lb-ft of torque, while the 6.6L V8 engine produces 353 horsepower and 460 lb-ft of torque. Both engines are paired with a 6-speed automatic transmission.

Owners generally praise the Escalade ESV for its spacious interior, comfortable ride, and powerful engines. However, some have reported issues with the vehicle's reliability.

**Engine**

**6.2L V8**

* **Intake Manifold Gasket Failure:** The intake manifold gasket may fail, causing coolant and oil leaks. Symptoms include overheating, coolant loss, and reduced engine power. The solution is to replace the intake manifold gasket. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
* **Fuel Injector Failure:** The fuel injectors may fail, causing the engine to run poorly or stall. Symptoms include rough idling, reduced engine power, and increased fuel consumption. The solution is to replace the failed fuel injectors. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600
* **Valve Cover Gasket Leak:** The valve cover gaskets may leak, causing oil leaks. Symptoms include oil leaks under the valve covers and reduced engine power. The solution is to replace the valve cover gaskets. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400

**6.6L V8**

* **EGR Cooler Failure:** The EGR cooler may fail, causing the engine to overheat. Symptoms include overheating, reduced engine power, and increased emissions. The solution is to replace the EGR cooler. Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,200

**Transmission**

**6-Speed Automatic**

* **Transmission Slipping:** The transmission may slip, causing the vehicle to hesitate or fail to shift smoothly. Symptoms include slipping gears, loss of power, and increased fuel consumption. The solution is to rebuild or replace the transmission. Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000
* **Torque Converter Failure:** The torque converter may fail, causing the transmission to overheat or fail to engage. Symptoms include slipping gears, shuddering, and increased fuel consumption. The solution is to replace the torque converter. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000

**Suspension**

* **Front Suspension Sagging:** The front suspension may sag, causing the vehicle to ride lower in the front. Symptoms include a noticeable drop in ride height and reduced handling. The solution is to replace the front suspension components. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000

**Electrical**

* **Electrical Harness Faults:** The electrical harness may develop faults, causing various electrical problems. Symptoms may include flickering lights, inoperative accessories, and battery drain. The solution is to repair or replace the faulty wiring. Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the extent of the damage

**Exterior**

* **Tailgate Latch Failure:** The tailgate latch may fail, preventing the tailgate from opening or closing properly. Symptoms include difficulty opening or closing the tailgate and potential safety hazards. The solution is to replace the tailgate latch. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400

**Interior**

* **Climate Control Malfunction:** The climate control system may malfunction, causing the vehicle to heat or cool improperly. Symptoms may include erratic temperature control, air conditioning failure, and excessive noise. The solution is to diagnose and repair the malfunctioning component, which may include the compressor, condenser, or control module. Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the specific issue

**Recalls**

* NHTSA Campaign ID Number: 17V196000 - Driver Airbag Module Replacement
* NHTSA Campaign ID Number: 17V828000 - Backup Camera Display Failure
* NHTSA Campaign ID Number: 18V018000 - Brake Boost Vacuum Hose Replacement

**Summary**

The 2011 Cadillac Escalade ESV is a spacious and powerful SUV that has received generally positive feedback. However, it has been reported to have some reliability issues, particularly with the engine, transmission, and electrical system. The severity and cost of these issues can vary depending on the specific problem and the extent of the damage.
