# 2020 Cadillac CT6 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2020 Cadillac CT6 represents the final bow for Cadillac's flagship luxury sedan in North America, offering sophisticated styling, cutting-edge tech like Super Cruise, and a roomy, premium cabin. When shopping for a used model, buyers should carefully review service histories and ensure any applicable powertrain safety campaigns have been properly addressed by an authorized dealer. While overall owner satisfaction can be high, paying close attention to transmission operation and electronic driver aids during a test drive will help ensure a trouble-free ownership experience.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged I4

The 2.0-liter turbocharged inline-four engine produces around 235 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque, serving as the entry-level powerplant for the CT6 in certain markets. It offers respectable efficiency for a large luxury sedan, though drivers seeking effortless passing power may prefer the larger multi-cylinder options.

### 3.6L V6

The naturally aspirated 3.6-liter V6 delivers 335 horsepower and 284 lb-ft of torque, providing smooth and balanced acceleration suited for daily cruising. This engine is a common find on the used market and generally offers a dependable balance of performance and refinement.

### 3.0L Twin-Turbo V6

The high-performance 3.0-liter twin-turbocharged V6 churns out 404 horsepower and 400 lb-ft of torque, paired standard with all-wheel drive for brisk acceleration. It transforms the large sedan into a remarkably swift cruiser, though prospective buyers should inspect the turbochargers and cooling plumbing thoroughly.

### 4.2L Twin-Turbo V8 (Blackwing)

Exclusive to top-tier trims, the hand-built 4.2-liter twin-turbo V8 (Blackwing) generates up to 500 or 550 horsepower depending on the variant. It provides immense power and exclusivity for enthusiasts, though specialized maintenance can be more demanding than standard V6 models.

## Transmission

### 10-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 10-speed automatic transmission routes power smoothly across various driving conditions, though some owners have experienced serious drivetrain incidents that warrant a close mechanical inspection before purchase.

**Transmission Seizure and Harsh Shifting**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have reported unexpected transmission seizing while driving at highway speeds, as well as harsh shifting or temporary wheel lock-up.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified technician inspect the transmission control module and mechanical components, and verify that all manufacturer powertrain recalls have been completed.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

Brake component complaints primarily revolve around intrusive noises during normal stopping, highlighting the importance of inspecting pad life and rotor condition during a pre-purchase evaluation.

**Brake Screeching and Scraping Noises**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have noted loud screeching or scraping noises coming from the front wheels while applying the brake pedal.
- **Solution:** Inspect brake pads, rotors, and calipers for premature wear or debris accumulation, and replace worn friction components as necessary.
- **Cost:** $150 - $450

## Electrical

### Super Cruise & Driver Assistance

Advanced electrical systems and driver assistance features add great convenience but can occasionally require specialized diagnostic software resets if faults arise.

**Super Cruise Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** The advanced hands-free driving feature may fail to engage, accompanied by continuous error messages flashing on the dashboard instrument cluster.
- **Solution:** Have the dealer or a specialized technician check for software updates, sensor alignment issues, or faulty control modules.

## Exterior

### Tailgate and Liftgate

Exterior body hardware is generally robust, though sporadic glitches with automated power latches can lead to unexpected inconveniences.

**Unintended Trunk/Hatch Opening**
- **Symptoms:** The rear hatch or trunk lid may unlatch and open spontaneously without driver input.
- **Solution:** Inspect the electronic release switch, key fob proximity sensors, and latch wiring harness for shorts or moisture intrusion.
- **Cost:** $300 - $7,800

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V731000: Tires:Pressure Monitoring And Regulating Systems](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Cadillac&model=CT6&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 138, "Tire Pressure Monitoring Systems." Drivers may drive the vehicle with low tire pressure without a low tire pressure warning, negatively impacting the vehicle handling and increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will replace all four TPMS sensors with the correct sensors, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V148000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Cadillac&model=CT6&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** The transmission may become damaged internally and cause the wheels to lock-up. Wheel lock-up while driving increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will install transmission control module monitoring software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2020 Cadillac CT6 remains a compelling choice for luxury car buyers wanting executive-level comfort and advanced technology at a fraction of its original price. While most mechanical systems prove reliable, prospective buyers should prioritize a comprehensive pre-purchase inspection—focusing specifically on transmission operation, brake pad wear, and the functionality of electronic driver aids like Super Cruise. Ensuring all open manufacturer campaigns are completed will help secure a more confident and worry-free ownership experience.
