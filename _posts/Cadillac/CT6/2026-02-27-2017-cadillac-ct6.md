# 2017 Cadillac CT6 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used luxury sedan, understanding potential mechanical hurdles can save you time and money. While this vehicle offers sophisticated styling and impressive road manners, owner reports submitted to safety authorities highlight a few recurring trouble spots—most notably concerning transmission behavior and minor electrical or structural glitches. Prospective buyers should carefully evaluate these areas during a test drive to ensure a smooth ownership experience.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4

This base 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine produces around 265 horsepower and is paired with rear-wheel drive. It offers adequate efficiency for daily commuting, though shoppers should inspect the engine bay for minor fluid or vacuum line leaks.

### 3.6L V6

The naturally aspirated 3.6-liter V6 generates roughly 335 horsepower with all-wheel drive, offering a balanced blend of power and refinement. Overall engine architecture is generally robust, but peripheral emission lines warrant routine checks.

**Evaporative Emissions Leak**
- **Symptoms:** Illuminated check engine light caused by a broken or damaged EVAP line.
- **Solution:** Inspect and replace the damaged EVAP line or affected emission components.

### 3.0L Twin-Turbocharged V6

At the top of the standard gasoline lineup, this twin-turbo V6 pushes out 404 horsepower for brisk acceleration. Maintenance can be more complex and costly due to the twin-turbo configuration, so detailed service history is recommended.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

An 8-speed automatic transmission manages power delivery across multiple engine variants. Owners have reported intermittent shifting anomalies, making a thorough test drive focused on gear changes essential before purchasing.

**Transmission Shudder and Hesitation**
- **Symptoms:** Noticeable jerking, rough gear engagement, or a shuddering sensation during normal acceleration and cruising.
- **Solution:** Consult a qualified technician for diagnostic evaluation, potential software updates, or fluid flushes as recommended by service guidelines.

**Transmission Slipping and Lurching**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle slips out of gear, fails to engage drive properly, or lurches abruptly when accelerating from a stop.
- **Solution:** Perform a comprehensive transmission diagnostic check to inspect internal components, hydraulic pressure, and control modules.

## Steering

### Steering Rack and Power Assist

Steering response is typically precise and weighted well for a large luxury car, though rare mechanical binding incidents underscore the need for a pre-purchase steering check.

**Steering Binding and Assist Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Steering wheel binds up or becomes extremely heavy, making directional control difficult when power assist fails.
- **Solution:** Inspect the steering rack, electronic power assist motor, and associated wiring connections for faults.

## Electrical

### Audio and Climate Control Electronics

Advanced cabin tech and driver aids generally perform well, though minor wiring vulnerabilities or moisture intrusion points require vigilance.

**Trunk Water Intrusion Affecting Audio/AC**
- **Symptoms:** Water accumulation in the trunk area disrupts the amplifier, causing the radio and climate control interface to malfunction.
- **Solution:** Check trunk seals for leaks, dry out affected modules, and seal any entry points to protect onboard electronics.

## Exterior

### Glass and Sunroof

Exterior panels and trim maintain a sleek aesthetic, but owners should carefully inspect glass components for any signs of factory stress or weather wear.

**Sunroof Glass Fracture**
- **Symptoms:** The sunroof panel shatters or explodes unexpectedly while driving at highway speeds without external impact.
- **Solution:** Inspect the sunroof glass and frame assembly for stress points, and replace the shattered panel with OEM glass if damaged.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V437000: Seats:Mid/Rear Assembly](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Cadillac&model=CT6&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 225, "Child Restraint Anchorage Systems." If the child seat cannot be attached correctly to the anchorage bar, there is an increased the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will inspect, remove any excess adhesive, and apply anti-corrosion primer to the inboard child seat anchorage bars, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V759000: Exterior Lighting](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Cadillac&model=CT6&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 108, "Lamps, Reflective Devices, and Associated Equipment." The excessive brightness may affect the vision of other drivers, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will install right and left side in-line headlight jumper harnesses, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

This vehicle delivers a compelling mix of luxury, advanced features, and confident road performance. However, because owner complaints frequently center on transmission hesitation, shifting shudders, and occasional electrical quirks, buyers should prioritize a detailed pre-purchase inspection. Test driving the car through various stop-and-go and highway conditions will help verify that the transmission and electronics operate smoothly.
