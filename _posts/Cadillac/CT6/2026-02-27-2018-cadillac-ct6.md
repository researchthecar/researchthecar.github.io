# 2018 Cadillac CT6 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2018 Cadillac CT6 is a sophisticated full-size luxury sedan that offers advanced technology, spacious comfort, and strong performance options. While owners generally praise its highway cruising capabilities and features like Super Cruise, prospective buyers should review available owner feedback regarding occasional powertrain concerns, electrical glitches, and differential issues.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4

This 2.0-liter turbocharged inline-4 engine produces around 265 horsepower, offering an efficient entry-level option for the CT6. It provides adequate daily power, though buyers should verify regular maintenance history.

### 3.6L V6

The 3.6-liter V6 engine generates approximately 335 horsepower and provides a balanced blend of refinement and responsiveness for everyday driving. It is generally reliable, though minor cooling system components like thermostats may require attention.

**Stuck Thermostat**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle fails to reach proper operating temperature in a timely manner.
- **Solution:** Replace the faulty thermostat with an updated unit to restore proper engine temperature regulation.

### 3.0L Twin-Turbo V6

This high-output 3.0-liter twin-turbo V6 delivers 404 horsepower and standard all-wheel drive for effortless acceleration. It offers brisk performance but demands careful inspection of its complex electronic and powertrain systems.

**Sudden Power Loss**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may unexpectedly lose all power to the wheels while driving at highway speeds, creating a dangerous situation.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified technician diagnose the powertrain control modules and related fuel or electrical systems for faults.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 8-speed automatic transmission pairs with select engines to deliver smooth gear changes and optimized highway efficiency. Some drivers report firm shift quality that may benefit from software calibration.

**Hard Shifting**
- **Symptoms:** The transmission may shift too aggressively or abruptly during acceleration.
- **Solution:** Check transmission fluid levels and consult a dealer for potential software updates or control module reprogramming.

### Hydra-Matic 8L45 / 8L90 Automatic

These multi-speed automatic transmissions are designed to optimize power delivery across various driving conditions. Regular fluid maintenance is recommended to maintain smooth operation over time.

### Differential Case

The all-wheel-drive drivetrain distributes power to all four wheels for enhanced traction in adverse weather. Owners should remain alert to any unusual noises or vibrations originating from the undercarriage.

**Differential Housing Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The front differential case may crack or separate abruptly, resulting in sudden vehicle stoppage and potential drivetrain damage.
- **Solution:** Inspect the front differential housing and driveshaft tunnel for structural integrity or signs of stress.

## Steering

### Super Cruise and Driver Assistance

Advanced safety and semi-autonomous driving features provide added convenience on supported highways. Drivers must always remain attentive and ready to take control.

**Unintended Steering Movement**
- **Symptoms:** While engaged in Super Cruise mode, the vehicle may occasionally jerk violently to one side.
- **Solution:** Ensure camera and sensor lenses are clean, and check for available driver-assistance software updates at a dealership.

## Electrical

### Infotainment and Navigation Screen

The CT6 cabin features extensive digital displays and advanced electronic interfaces. Software updates can often resolve minor screen stability issues.

**Screen Flickering and System Shutdown**
- **Symptoms:** The navigation display may flicker continuously and the vehicle can occasionally experience unexpected electrical shutdowns while in drive mode.
- **Solution:** Have the dealer inspect infotainment software, wiring harnesses, and battery connections per manufacturer service guidelines.

## Exterior

### LED Headlights

Exterior lighting relies heavily on modern LED technology for enhanced visibility and styling. Component failures, while uncommon, can be costly due to integrated assembly designs.

**Premature Headlight Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Portions of the driver-side LED headlight assembly may stop working prematurely.
- **Solution:** Replace the affected headlight assembly as required.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V673000: Seat Belts:Rear/Other](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Cadillac&model=CT6&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** Certain second-row or third-row rear seatbelts retractor assemblies may not automatically lock when the seatbelt is fully pulled out of the retractor, possibly preventing a child seat from being properly secured. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 208, "Occupant Crash.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the rear seatbelt retractors and replace them if necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V437000: Seats:Mid/Rear Assembly](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Cadillac&model=CT6&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 225, "Child Restraint Anchorage Systems." If the child seat cannot be attached correctly to the anchorage bar, there is an increased the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will inspect, remove any excess adhesive, and apply anti-corrosion primer to the inboard child seat anchorage bars, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2018 Cadillac CT6 delivers an impressive luxury sedan experience with advanced features and confident road manners. While most owners enjoy trouble-free driving, prospective buyers should thoroughly inspect the vehicle's electrical systems, drivetrain components, and advanced driver aids during a pre-purchase inspection.
