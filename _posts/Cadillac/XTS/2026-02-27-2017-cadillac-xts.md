# 2017 Cadillac XTS Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2017 Cadillac XTS offers a spacious luxury sedan experience packed with advanced comfort and technology features. Prospective buyers should review owner reports regarding occasional electrical glitches and suspension component wear, ensuring a thorough pre-purchase inspection before buying.

---

## Engine

### 3.6L V6 (LGX)

The standard 3.6-liter naturally aspirated V6 engine produces approximately 304 horsepower and 264 lb-ft of torque, delivering smooth power for daily driving. It is generally reliable, though routine maintenance is essential to ensure long-term mechanical health.

### 3.6L Twin-Turbo V6 (LF3)

Exclusive to the high-performance V-Sport trim, the 3.6-liter twin-turbocharged V6 generates an impressive 410 horsepower and 369 lb-ft of torque. It provides robust acceleration paired with all-wheel drive, though buyers should verify service records for forced-induction upkeep.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 6-speed automatic transmission pairs with standard front-wheel drive models to deliver predictable gear shifts. Regular fluid changes help maintain smooth operation and prevent premature internal wear.

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 8-speed automatic transmission is utilized alongside specific engine configurations to optimize highway cruising efficiency. Buyers should test drive thoroughly to ensure crisp, hesitation-free shifting.

## Suspension

### Rear Air Suspension

The rear air suspension system maintains the vehicle's ride height and leveling, though rear air spring components can occasionally experience leaks or compressor failures over time.

**Air Bag Failure and Rear Sagging**
- **Symptoms:** The rear suspension air bags may suddenly deflate while driving or parked, causing the rear end to drop and resulting in a rough, bouncy ride.
- **Solution:** Inspect the air suspension compressor, lines, and air springs for leaks or mechanical failure, and replace worn components as necessary.

## Electrical

### CUE Infotainment System

Electrical issues primarily center around infotainment software performance and display responsiveness.

**Screen Freezing or Glitching**
- **Symptoms:** The touchscreen display may freeze, become completely unresponsive, or exhibit spider-web cracks and display failure.
- **Solution:** Have a dealership recalibrate or inspect the infotainment module; damaged touchscreens may require full assembly replacement.
