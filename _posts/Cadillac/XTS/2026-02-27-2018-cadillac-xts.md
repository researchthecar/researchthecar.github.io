# 2018 Cadillac XTS Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2018 Cadillac XTS is a spacious and luxurious full-size sedan offering comfortable cruising and a well-appointed cabin. For used-car buyers, understanding its powertrain options and typical owner-reported experiences can help ensure a satisfying purchase.

---

## Engine

### 3.6L V6 (304 hp)

The standard naturally aspirated 3.6-liter V6 produces 304 horsepower and 264 lb-ft of torque, providing smooth acceleration for daily driving. It is generally a dependable powerplant when maintained with regular oil changes.

### 3.6L Twin-Turbo V6 (410 hp)

The available twin-turbocharged 3.6-liter V6 generates 410 horsepower and 369 lb-ft of torque, exclusive to high-performance V-Sport trims. Buyers should expect higher maintenance complexity and running costs compared to the base engine.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

A 6-speed automatic transmission pairs with the standard V6 engine, delivering smooth shifts. It is a proven unit in the lineup, though routine fluid service is recommended.

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

An 8-speed automatic transmission is coupled with the twin-turbo V6 engine for quicker response and efficiency. Drivers should test drive to ensure smooth operation across all gears.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V576000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Foundation Components:Disc:Caliper](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Cadillac&model=XTS&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** A reduction of braking performance can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will bleed the vehicle's brake system, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2018 Cadillac XTS delivers a comfortable, premium driving experience with distinct powertrain choices ranging from a relaxed base V6 to a potent twin-turbo setup. Prospective buyers should thoroughly test drive and inspect all mechanical and electronic systems to ensure a reliable ownership experience.
