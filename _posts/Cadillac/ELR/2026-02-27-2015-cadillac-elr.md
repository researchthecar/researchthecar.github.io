# 2015 Cadillac ELR

### Overview
The 2015 Cadillac ELR, a luxury extended-range electric vehicle, aimed to blend electric driving with the convenience of a gasoline generator. While its innovative powertrain offered a unique driving experience, like many first-generation advanced vehicles, it encountered some specific reliability concerns. Owners reported issues primarily related to the complex electrical system and the integration of the gasoline range extender. The build quality and interior components generally held up well, but the sophisticated technology presented a steeper learning curve for maintenance and repair, sometimes leading to higher-than-average service costs for specific components. Overall, the ELR's reliability is a mixed bag, with its advanced nature being both its strength and a potential source of concern.

## Engine

### 1.4L 4-Cylinder Gasoline Range Extender
**Engine Mount Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Excessive vibration felt in the cabin, especially when the range extender is active.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the engine mounts.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600

**Coolant Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Low coolant levels, overheating warnings, visible coolant puddles under the vehicle.
- **Solution:** Inspection and repair/replacement of hoses, seals, or radiator.
- **Cost:** $200 - $500

## Electrical

### Battery Pack and Charging System
**Charging System Faults**
- **Symptoms:** Inability to charge the battery, charging interrupted, error messages on the dashboard related to charging.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and repair of charging port, onboard charger, or battery management system.
- **Cost:** $500 - $2000+

**Infotainment System Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Touchscreen unresponsiveness, freezing, audio issues, navigation problems.
- **Solution:** Software updates, module reset, or replacement of infotainment unit.
- **Cost:** $300 - $1500


### Hybrid Control Module
**Module Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** Erratic hybrid system behavior, loss of power, warning lights related to hybrid system.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and potential replacement of the hybrid control module.
- **Cost:** $800 - $2500+

### Summary Verdict
The 2015 Cadillac ELR, while technologically advanced, exhibits a reliability profile that leans towards moderate concern, particularly in its electrical and range-extender systems. The complexity of its hybrid powertrain means that issues, when they arise, can be costly to diagnose and repair. Owners should be prepared for potential expenses related to charging system faults and the gasoline engine's ancillary components. While the core structure and interior are generally robust, the specialized nature of the ELR's technology requires diligent maintenance and potentially higher repair budgets compared to conventional vehicles. It is recommended to seek out technicians with specific experience in extended-range electric vehicles for servicing.
