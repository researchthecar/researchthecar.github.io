# 2018 Ferrari 812 Superfast

### Overview
The 2018 Ferrari 812 Superfast represents the pinnacle of front-engine V12 grand tourers, combining blistering performance with luxurious comfort. Engineered for enthusiasts and collectors, its reliability is generally high, reflecting Ferrari's commitment to quality and advanced manufacturing. However, as with any high-performance exotic vehicle, potential issues can arise, particularly concerning complex electronic systems and specialized components. This analysis focuses on documented technical reliability concerns beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into common areas of attention for owners and prospective buyers. The focus is on specific component failures rather than general wear and tear, offering a clear picture of the 812 Superfast's technical robustness.

### Summary Verdict
The 2018 Ferrari 812 Superfast demonstrates a high level of technical reliability, particularly in its core powertrain components, the 6.5L V12 engine and 7-speed dual-clutch transmission. These systems are exceptionally robust. Areas that may require attention are primarily within the complex electrical systems, such as occasional infotainment glitches and potential sensor malfunctions for ABS and traction control. The active aerodynamics, while advanced, can also be a source of repair. Interior and exterior components, barring external damage, are generally well-built. Owners should be aware that repairs on such a specialized vehicle can be costly, emphasizing the importance of diligent maintenance and prompt attention to any warning lights or system anomalies.
