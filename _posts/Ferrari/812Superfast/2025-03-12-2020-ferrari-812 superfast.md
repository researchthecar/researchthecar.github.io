The 2020 Ferrari 812 Superfast is a grand tourer known for its naturally aspirated 6.5L V12 engine, producing a staggering 789 horsepower and 530 lb-ft of torque. This powerful engine is mated to a 7-speed dual-clutch transmission. While the 812 Superfast is celebrated for its exhilarating performance, stunning design, and luxurious interior, some owners have reported specific issues. Common praises include the car's raw power, evocative sound, and engaging driving experience. Criticisms often revolve around its high price point, relatively low fuel economy, and occasional electronic gremlins.

**Engine**

**6.5L V12**

*   **Oil Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks from the engine, often noticed as drips under the car or a burning oil smell. May lead to low oil levels and potential engine damage.
    *   Solution: Identify and replace the faulty gasket or seal.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $3000+

*   **Exhaust Manifold Cracking**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Cracks developing in the exhaust manifold, resulting in a metallic rattling sound or a noticeable exhaust leak. May cause reduced engine performance.
    *   Solution: Replace the cracked exhaust manifold.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $4000 - $8000+

**Transmission**

**7-Speed Dual-Clutch**

*   **Clutch Slippage**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The dual-clutch transmission may exhibit slippage during gear changes, especially under hard acceleration. This can feel like a delay or hesitation in power delivery.
    *   Solution: Inspection and potential replacement of the clutch pack.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $8000 - $15000+

*   **Harsh Shifting**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported instances of harsh or jerky shifting, particularly at lower speeds. This can diminish the smoothness and refinement expected from a Ferrari.
    *   Solution: Transmission software update or replacement of faulty solenoids within the transmission.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $5000+

**Suspension**

*   **Magneride Damper Failures**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The adaptive Magneride dampers may fail, leading to a loss of damping control, a bouncy ride, and potential warning lights on the dashboard.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the faulty Magneride damper(s).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $3000 - $6000+

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Freezing/Crashing**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The infotainment system may freeze, crash, or become unresponsive. This can affect navigation, audio, and other vehicle functions.
    *   Solution: Software update or replacement of the infotainment system unit.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $3000+

*   **Sensor Malfunctions**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Various sensors, such as those related to the tire pressure monitoring system (TPMS) or parking sensors, may malfunction, triggering warning lights.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis and replacement of the faulty sensor(s).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1000+

**Exterior**

*   **No Issues Found**

**Interior**

*   **Adhesive Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Adhesive failure can occur in various areas of the interior, such as trim pieces or dashboard components detaching or becoming loose.
    *   Solution: Reapplying adhesive or replacing the affected trim pieces.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $1000+

**Recalls:** None Found

**Summary:** The 2020 Ferrari 812 Superfast is a high-performance vehicle that offers an exhilarating driving experience. However, potential owners should be aware of potential issues such as oil leaks, exhaust manifold issues, transmission problems, suspension failures, and electrical glitches. While some issues are relatively minor and inexpensive to fix, others can be more significant and costly. A pre-purchase inspection by a qualified Ferrari technician is highly recommended to identify any existing or potential problems.
