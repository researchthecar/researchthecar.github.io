The 2022 Ferrari 812 Superfast is a grand tourer known for its naturally aspirated 6.5-liter V12 engine that produces a staggering 789 horsepower and 530 lb-ft of torque. This rear-wheel-drive beast is paired with a 7-speed dual-clutch transmission. Drivers love the 812 Superfast for its exhilarating performance, iconic Ferrari styling, and the visceral experience of a high-revving V12. Some criticisms include its high price tag, limited practicality, and the firm ride quality that can be uncomfortable on long journeys. There is no other engine or transmission option offered in the 812 Superfast.

**Engine**

**6.5L V12**

*   **Oil Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks are a reoccuring issue. These leaks can originate from various seals and gaskets around the engine, leading to oil consumption and potential engine damage if left unaddressed. Owners report seeing oil spots under the car after it's been parked, or noticing a burning smell.
    *   Solution: Locating the exact source of the leak and replacing the affected seals/gaskets is required. This may involve removing engine components for access.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $5000+ (depending on the leak location and parts involved)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**7-Speed Dual-Clutch Transmission**

*   **Jerky Shifting at Low Speeds**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported experiencing jerky or rough shifting, particularly at low speeds or during initial acceleration. This can manifest as hesitation or a noticeable "kick" when the transmission engages.
    *   Solution: Software recalibration of the TCU (Transmission Control Unit) may resolve the issue. In some cases, clutch replacement is needed if the issue persists.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 (for software) - $8,000+ (for clutch replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Freezing/Crashing**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The infotainment system may occasionally freeze, crash, or become unresponsive. This can disrupt navigation, audio, and other functions controlled through the system.
    *   Solution: A software update may resolve the issue. If not, the infotainment unit may need to be replaced.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 (for software update) - $3,000+ (for unit replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Carbon Fiber Cracking**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported cracking in visible carbon fiber trim, particularly around areas exposed to heat or stress. This can compromise the aesthetic appearance and potentially the structural integrity of the components.
    *   Solution: Affected carbon fiber pieces need to be repaired or replaced.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $10,000+ (depending on the part and repair method)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Dashboard Leather Shrinking/Warping**
    *   Description/Symptoms: In hot climates, some owners have experienced the leather on the dashboard shrinking or warping, creating unsightly gaps and uneven surfaces.
    *   Solution: Replacing the affected dashboard leather panel is typically the only remedy.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $10,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

No Issues Found

**Summary**

The 2022 Ferrari 812 Superfast, while an exceptional performance car, appears to have some potential issues that owners should be aware of. The reported problems include oil leaks, transmission jerkiness, infotainment glitches, carbon fiber cracking, and interior leather warping. While some issues are relatively minor and can be resolved with software updates, others, such as oil leaks or clutch replacement, can be more costly to repair. The severity of these issues varies, but the potential costs associated with repairs can be significant, making pre-purchase inspections and a thorough understanding of the car's history crucial.
