The 2022 Ferrari SF90 Stradale is a high-performance plug-in hybrid sports car that combines blistering speed with advanced technology. It features a 4.0-liter twin-turbocharged V8 engine paired with three electric motors, producing a combined output of 986 horsepower and 590 lb-ft of torque. It is only offered with an 8-speed dual-clutch transmission. People are drawn to the SF90 Stradale for its incredible acceleration, advanced hybrid technology, and stunning design. However, potential drawbacks include the complexity of the hybrid system, which can lead to potential issues, and the high cost of ownership.

**Electric Motor**

**Electric Motor**
*   **High Voltage Battery Malfunction**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The high voltage battery system fails, causing a warning light to illuminate on the dashboard. This can result in reduced power output, the inability to drive in electric-only mode, or complete immobilization of the vehicle.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis by a certified Ferrari technician is required to determine the cause of the battery malfunction. Replacement of faulty battery cells or modules is typically necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $20,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Electric Motor Overheating**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The electric motors overheat during sustained high-performance driving or repeated acceleration. This can lead to a temporary reduction in power or complete shutdown of the electric drive system. Warning messages may appear on the dashboard.
    *   Solution: The electric motor cooling system needs to be inspected and flushed. Faulty cooling system components (such as pumps or fans) may need to be replaced.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $5,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Engine**

**4.0L Twin-Turbocharged V8 (F154CD)**

*   **Oil Leaks from Turbocharger Lines**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks are observed near the turbocharger lines or fittings. This can result in a burning oil smell and potential engine damage if oil levels are not maintained.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the leaking turbocharger oil lines and fittings with updated parts.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $3,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Check Engine Light - Misfire**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The check engine light illuminates and the engine misfires, particularly under heavy acceleration. Diagnostic codes may indicate a problem with the ignition coils or spark plugs.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis by a qualified technician to determine the cause of the misfire. Replacement of faulty ignition coils or spark plugs may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**8-Speed Dual-Clutch Transmission**

*   **Transmission Hesitation or Jerking**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The transmission hesitates during gear changes, particularly between lower gears. Jerking or rough shifting may also occur.
    *   Solution: Software update, or in some cases, replacement of the Mechatronic unit within the transmission may be required.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $10,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Clutch Slippage**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The clutch slips during acceleration, causing a delay in power delivery or a noticeable reduction in performance.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the clutch pack is required.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $8,000 - $15,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Magneride Damper Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The Magneride dampers (adaptive suspension) fail, resulting in a noticeable degradation in ride quality and handling. Warning lights may illuminate on the dashboard.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the faulty Magneride damper(s).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $5,000 - $10,000+ per damper
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Freezing/Crashing**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The infotainment system freezes, crashes, or becomes unresponsive. This can affect navigation, audio, and other vehicle functions.
    *   Solution: A software update can resolve this. In cases of hardware failure, the head unit may need to be replaced.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $5,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Sensor Malfunctions (e.g., Parking Sensors, TPMS)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Parking sensors or the tire pressure monitoring system (TPMS) malfunction, providing false readings or failing to operate altogether.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis to identify the faulty sensor(s). Replacement of the defective sensor(s).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+ per sensor
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Carbon Fiber Trim Delamination**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The clear coat on the carbon fiber exterior trim starts to peel or delaminate, resulting in a cloudy or uneven appearance.
    *   Solution: The affected carbon fiber trim piece needs to be refinished or replaced.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $10,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Stitching Coming Undone**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Stitching on the dashboard, seats, or door panels comes loose or unravels, detracting from the vehicle's premium appearance.
    *   Solution: Professional upholstery repair to restitch the affected areas.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $2,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2022 Ferrari SF90 Stradale is a complex and technologically advanced vehicle, and as such, owners may encounter a range of issues. While some problems, such as infotainment glitches, are relatively minor and inexpensive to fix, others, such as high voltage battery failure or transmission issues, can be quite severe and costly to repair. The most expensive repairs include powertrain and suspension components. Careful inspection and preventative maintenance are crucial for maintaining the SF90 Stradale and minimizing potential problems.
