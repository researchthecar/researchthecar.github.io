The 2018 Ferrari 488 GTB is a mid-engine sports car known for its stunning performance and striking design. It is powered by a 3.9-liter twin-turbocharged V8 engine producing 661 horsepower and 561 lb-ft of torque. The only transmission option available is a 7-speed dual-clutch automatic transmission. Owners often praise the car's exhilarating acceleration, responsive handling, and distinctive Ferrari exhaust note. Some criticisms include the high running costs and potential for certain electronic glitches.

**Engine**

**3.9L Twin-Turbocharged V8**

*   **Turbocharger Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Whining or screeching noise from the engine bay, loss of power, check engine light.
    *   Solution: Replace the failed turbocharger.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $5,000 - $8,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Oil Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Visible oil leaks on the engine, burning oil smell, low oil level.
    *   Solution: Replace faulty gaskets or seals causing the leaks.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**7-Speed Dual-Clutch**

*   **Clutch Wear/Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Slipping during gear changes, jerky shifting, difficulty engaging gears, transmission warning light. This issue is more common with aggressive driving habits or track use.
    *   Solution: Replace the clutch pack.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $8,000 - $12,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Mechatronic Unit Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Erratic shifting, inability to select gears, transmission warning lights.
    *   Solution: Replace the mechatronic unit.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $6,000 - $10,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Suspension Lift Malfunction**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Front suspension lift fails to raise or lower the vehicle, error message on the dashboard.
    *   Solution: Diagnose and repair the hydraulic system, potentially replace the pump or actuators.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $5,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **ECU Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Check engine light illumination, performance issues, warning lights that can't be resolved after clearing the codes, car not starting.
    *   Solution: Replace the faulty ECU.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $3,000 - $6,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Sensor Malfunctions**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Various warning lights, including TPMS, ABS, or engine management lights. Can lead to drivability issues.
    *   Solution: Diagnose the faulty sensor and replace it.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+ (depending on the sensor)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **No Issues Found**

**Interior**

*   **Infotainment System Freezing/Crashing**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Infotainment screen freezes, reboots unexpectedly, unresponsive to touch inputs.
    *   Solution: Software update or replacement of the infotainment unit.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $4,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2018 Ferrari 488 GTB, while an exceptional performance car, is not without its potential issues. The most concerning problems relate to the engine's turbochargers, the transmission's clutch and mechatronic unit, and electrical gremlins. These repairs can be expensive, potentially costing thousands of dollars. While not all 488 GTBs will experience these problems, prospective buyers should be aware of the potential risks and factor in maintenance costs accordingly.
