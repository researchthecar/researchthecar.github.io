# 2016 Ferrari FF

### Overview
The 2016 Ferrari FF, a unique shooting brake grand tourer, combines blistering performance with surprising practicality. Powered by a potent V12 engine and featuring an innovative all-wheel-drive system, the FF offers a compelling blend of supercar dynamics and everyday usability. Reliability for such a high-performance, low-volume vehicle is generally strong, with owners reporting satisfaction. However, as with any exotic car, maintenance and potential repairs can be costly, and specific components may require attention over time. This analysis focuses on documented technical issues beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential areas of concern for prospective or current owners.

### Battery Drain
**Premature Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Difficulty starting after periods of inactivity, warning lights.
- **Solution:** Check for parasitic draws, ensure battery is healthy, or replace battery.
- **Cost:** $300 - $800

### Summary Verdict
The 2016 Ferrari FF demonstrates commendable reliability for a vehicle of its caliber. The core powertrain, including the V12 engine and dual-clutch transmission, is robust. The innovative 4RM-S all-wheel-drive system also appears to be well-engineered. The most commonly cited potential issues are related to the electrical system, specifically minor glitches with the infotainment unit and occasional battery drain, which are not uncommon in modern, complex vehicles. Owners should be prepared for the high cost of specialized maintenance and potential repairs, but major mechanical failures are not a widespread concern. Regular servicing by qualified technicians is crucial for maintaining its performance and longevity.
