The 2023 Ferrari SF90 Spider is a high-performance plug-in hybrid supercar that blends blistering speed with open-top driving pleasure. It features a 4.0-liter twin-turbocharged V8 engine paired with three electric motors, producing a combined output of 986 horsepower and 590 lb-ft of torque. It comes exclusively with an 8-speed dual-clutch transmission. Owners typically praise the SF90 Spider for its incredible acceleration, advanced technology, and stunning design. Conversely, some common gripes include the high price tag, complex electronics that can be prone to glitches, and the limited range in electric-only mode.

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Freezing/Crashing**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The infotainment screen occasionally freezes, becomes unresponsive, or crashes entirely, requiring a system reboot. Sometimes the navigation and climate control is affected.
    *   Solution: Software updates from the dealer or complete replacement of the head unit.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: Warranty coverage or $2,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Hybrid System Faults**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Error messages related to the hybrid system appear on the dashboard, potentially leading to reduced power or limited functionality. Diagnostic codes reveal issues with the battery management system or electric motor control.
    *   Solution: Dealer diagnostic and repair, which may involve replacing faulty sensors, wiring harnesses, or even hybrid system components.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: Warranty coverage or $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Loose Interior Trim Panels**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rattling or vibration noises emanating from loose trim panels within the cabin, particularly around the dashboard, door panels, or center console.
    *   Solution: Re-securing loose panels with appropriate fasteners or replacing damaged clips.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: Warranty coverage or $50 - $500
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**
The 2023 Ferrari SF90 Spider, while an exceptional vehicle, has shown some issues, particularly within the electrical system. While the issues are not widespread, they can range from minor inconveniences to more complex problems, depending on the component that is failing. Most issues are covered under the new car warranty if reported early, but can become costly outside of the warranty period.
