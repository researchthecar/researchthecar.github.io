**Introduction**

The 2021 Ferrari Portofino M is a high-performance convertible that combines sleek design with exhilarating performance. It features a twin-turbocharged 3.9L V8 engine that produces 612 horsepower and 560 lb-ft of torque. Paired with an 8-speed dual-clutch transmission, the Portofino M can accelerate from 0-60 mph in just 3.45 seconds. Despite its impressive specs, certain aspects of the vehicle have been met with criticism.

**Engine**

**3.9L Twin-Turbocharged V8**

**Turbocharger Lag**
Description/Symptoms: Delayed throttle response, particularly at low RPMs
Solution: ECU update
Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 (covered under warranty)
Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**8-Speed Dual-Clutch Transmission**

**Shift Hesitation**
Description/Symptoms: Occasional delayed shifts during spirited driving
Solution: Transmission fluid flush or replacement
Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600
Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

**Adaptive Magnetic Ride Control**

**No Issues Found**

**Electrical**

**Infotainment System**

**Bluetooth Connectivity Issues**
Description/Symptoms: Difficulty pairing or maintaining a Bluetooth connection
Solution: Software update
Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 (covered under warranty)
Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

**No Issues Found**

**Interior**

**Steering Wheel Controls**

**Buttons Become Sticky**
Description/Symptoms: Buttons on the steering wheel becoming difficult to press or sticking
Solution: Button replacement
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

Overall, the 2021 Ferrari Portofino M is a highly capable sports car that offers impressive performance and style. However, some minor issues with the engine and transmission, as well as a few interior concerns, have been reported by owners. While the cost of repairs can be substantial, most of the issues are covered under warranty.
