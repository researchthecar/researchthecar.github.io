# 2006 Ferrari 360

### Overview
The 2006 Ferrari 360, a mid-engine V8-powered sports car, is celebrated for its exhilarating performance and iconic design. While generally considered a robust machine, like any high-performance vehicle, it can experience specific issues that owners should be aware of. This analysis focuses on documented technical reliability concerns beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential problem areas for the 360 Modena and Spider variants. Understanding these potential pitfalls can aid prospective buyers and current owners in maintaining the car's exceptional driving experience and value.

---


## Engine

### 3.6L V8 (F131)
**Sticky Valves**
- **Symptoms:** Rough idle, misfires, loss of power.
- **Solution:** Valve cleaning or replacement, often requiring engine disassembly.
- **Cost:** $2,000 - $5,000+

**Cam Belt Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Sudden engine stoppage, catastrophic internal engine damage.
- **Solution:** Complete engine rebuild or replacement.
- **Cost:** $8,000 - $15,000+

**Oil Leaks (Rear Main Seal)**
- **Symptoms:** Visible oil drips, burning oil smell.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the rear main seal, often requiring transmission removal.
- **Cost:** $1,500 - $3,000


## Transmission

### F1 (Automated Manual)
**Hydraulic Pump Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Difficulty shifting gears, 'check transmission' warning light, inability to select gears.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the hydraulic pump and fluid.
- **Cost:** $1,000 - $2,500

**Clutch Actuator Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Jerky gear changes, clutch slippage, transmission warning lights.
- **Solution:** Actuator adjustment, repair, or replacement.
- **Cost:** $1,500 - $3,000

## Electrical

### General Electrical System
**Window Regulator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Power windows not operating, slow operation.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the window regulator mechanism.
- **Cost:** $300 - $700

**Dashboard Warning Lights**
- **Symptoms:** Intermittent or persistent illumination of various warning lights (ABS, Airbag, Check Engine).
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and repair of underlying sensor or module faults.
- **Cost:** $500 - $2,000+

### Trim & Upholstery
**Leather Creasing/Wear**

### Summary Verdict
The 2006 Ferrari 360 is a desirable supercar with a generally solid mechanical foundation. However, potential owners and current custodians should be particularly mindful of the engine's cam belt service intervals and the potential for sticky valves. The F1 transmission, while offering a unique driving experience, can be prone to hydraulic system issues. Electrical gremlins, though not pervasive, can also surface. Regular, specialized maintenance is paramount to mitigating these risks and ensuring the 360 remains a thrilling and reliable performer.
