The 2019 Ferrari GTC4Lusso T is a high-performance luxury grand tourer powered by a 3.9-liter twin-turbocharged V8 engine. It produces 602 horsepower and 561 lb-ft of torque and is paired with a 7-speed dual-clutch transmission.

The GTC4Lusso T is praised for its thrilling performance, luxurious interior, and spacious cargo capacity. However, it has also been criticized for its high price, limited rear-seat legroom, and firm suspension. Other engine and transmission options for the GTC4Lusso include a 6.3-liter naturally aspirated V12 with a 7-speed dual-clutch transmission (GTC4Lusso) and a 3.9-liter twin-turbocharged V8 with a 7-speed dual-clutch transmission (GTC4Lusso T HS).

**Engine**
**3.9-liter Twin-Turbocharged V8**

* **Turbocharger Failure:** Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, smoke from the engine bay, unusual noises. Solution: Replacement of turbocharger. Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $5,000+
    * **Recalls:** NHTSA Recall Number 22V900000 (Turbocharger Impeller)

* **Camshaft Position Sensor Failure:** Description/Symptoms: Engine misfires, check engine light illumination. Solution: Replacement of camshaft position sensor. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000

**Transmission**

**7-Speed Dual-Clutch Transmission**

* **Clutch Pack Failure:** Description/Symptoms: Slipping gears, difficulty shifting, check engine light illumination. Solution: Replacement of clutch pack. Estimated Cost to Fix: $3,000 - $5,000+

* **Shifter Cable Failure:** Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears, transmission stuck in one gear. Solution: Replacement of shifter cable. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000

**Suspension**

* **Front Suspension Strut Failure:** Description/Symptoms: Knocking or clunking noises from the front suspension, poor handling. Solution: Replacement of front suspension struts. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500

* **Rear Suspension Spring Failure:** Description/Symptoms: Sagging rear end, poor handling. Solution: Replacement of rear suspension springs. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain:** Description/Symptoms: Battery constantly dying, difficulty starting the vehicle. Solution: Inspection and repair or replacement of electrical components causing the drain. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500

* **Infotainment System Failure:** Description/Symptoms: Black screen, unresponsive touchscreen, software glitches. Solution: Software update or replacement of infotainment system components. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,500

**Exterior**

* **Headlight Condensation:** Description/Symptoms: Moisture accumulation inside the headlights. Solution: Inspection and repair of headlight assembly. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000

* **Windshield Wiper Motor Failure:** Description/Symptoms: Wipers not working or working intermittently. Solution: Replacement of windshield wiper motor. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000

**Interior**

* **Climate Control Malfunction:** Description/Symptoms: AC or heater not working properly, temperature fluctuations. Solution: Inspection and repair or replacement of climate control components. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500

* **Power Window Failure:** Description/Symptoms: Power windows not functioning or working intermittently. Solution: Inspection and repair or replacement of power window motor or switch. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000

**Summary**
Overall, the 2019 Ferrari GTC4Lusso T is a capable and luxurious vehicle. However, it is not without its issues. The most severe and costly problems reported by owners include turbocharger failure, clutch pack failure, and suspension strut failure. These issues can potentially lead to expensive repairs. Prospective buyers should be aware of these issues and factor them into their purchasing decision.
