The 2024 Ferrari Roma is a grand touring sports car that blends classic Ferrari styling with modern technology. It is powered by a 3.9-liter twin-turbocharged V8 engine producing 612 horsepower and 561 lb-ft of torque. All Romas are equipped with an 8-speed dual-clutch transmission. People generally praise the Roma for its elegant design, powerful engine, and comfortable ride. Common complaints involve the infotainment system's complexity and the lack of rear seat space. As the 2024 Roma is a relatively new model, widespread, well-documented issues are still emerging. The information below is based on currently available data and may evolve over time.

**Engine**

**3.9L Twin-Turbocharged V8**

*   **Oil Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks may occur from various points in the engine, potentially leading to low oil levels and engine damage if left unaddressed. Symptoms can include visible oil under the car, the smell of burning oil, and low oil pressure warning lights.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis by a qualified mechanic is necessary to pinpoint the leak's source. Repairs may involve replacing gaskets, seals, or potentially more extensive work depending on the leak's location.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $5000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Turbocharger Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Potential issues with the turbochargers such as failure of the wastegate actuator or the turbocharger itself. This may result in a loss of power, unusual noises (whining, screeching), or error codes on the dashboard.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis of the turbocharger is required to pinpoint the exact problem. Repairs can include replacing faulty actuators, rebuilding the turbo, or complete turbocharger replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $3,000 - $10,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**8-Speed Dual-Clutch Transmission**

*   **Rough Shifting/Hesitation**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported instances of rough or jerky shifting, particularly at low speeds, or hesitation when accelerating. This can be more noticeable in automatic mode.
    *   Solution: The transmission control module (TCM) may need recalibration or software updates. In more severe cases, clutch pack replacement or valve body repair might be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $8,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

No Issues Found

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Glitches**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Reports of the infotainment system freezing, crashing, or exhibiting lag. Connectivity issues with Apple CarPlay or Android Auto may also occur.
    *   Solution: Software updates from Ferrari may resolve some glitches. In more severe cases, the infotainment unit might need replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 (Software Update) - $5,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Sensor Malfunctions**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Issues with various sensors, such as parking sensors, tire pressure sensors, or oxygen sensors, can occur. These malfunctions can trigger warning lights on the dashboard and affect vehicle performance.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis using an OBD-II scanner to identify the faulty sensor. Replacement of the defective sensor is typically required.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Automatic Trunk Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Malfunctions with the automatic trunk opening/closing mechanism. Could include the trunk not opening, not closing, or getting stuck.
    *   Solution: Diagnose the specific component (actuator, sensor, etc.) that is malfunctioning and replace it.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Creaking/Rattling Noises**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported creaking or rattling noises emanating from the dashboard, center console, or other interior trim pieces. These noises can be exacerbated by temperature changes or road conditions.
    *   Solution: Identifying the source of the noise and tightening or replacing the affected trim pieces or fasteners. Applying felt tape or other sound-dampening materials can also help.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2024 Ferrari Roma, while a desirable and high-performing vehicle, has some reported issues. Engine oil leaks and turbocharger problems can be costly to repair, and electrical glitches with the infotainment system can be frustrating. Rough shifting in the transmission and interior noises are also concerns. The severity of these issues can range from minor inconveniences to significant repairs, with potential costs ranging from a few hundred dollars to upwards of $10,000. As a relatively new model, long-term reliability data is still emerging.
