The 2023 Ferrari Roma is a stunning grand tourer that blends classic Ferrari styling with modern technology. Powered by a 3.9-liter twin-turbocharged V8 engine producing 612 horsepower and 561 lb-ft of torque, it delivers exhilarating performance. The Roma is widely praised for its elegant design, comfortable interior, and engaging driving experience. However, some owners have reported issues with certain aspects of the car. The only available transmission for the 2023 Ferrari Roma is an 8-speed dual-clutch automatic. Some owners dislike the limited rear passenger space and the infotainment system's user interface, while others appreciate the car's performance and stunning design.

**Engine**

**3.9L Twin-Turbocharged V8**

*   **Oil Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported oil leaks originating from various points on the engine, including around the valve covers and oil pan. Symptoms can include visible oil drips under the car, a burning oil smell, and low oil levels.
    *   Solution: The solution typically involves replacing the leaking gaskets and seals. In more severe cases, the oil pan or valve covers themselves may need to be replaced.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $3,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Turbocharger Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Reports have surfaced of turbocharger failures, manifesting as a loss of power, unusual noises from the engine bay (whining or screeching), and potentially check engine lights related to boost pressure.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the faulty turbocharger unit is usually required.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $5,000 - $10,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**8-Speed Dual-Clutch Automatic**

*   **Harsh Shifting/Jerking**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have experienced rough or jerky shifting, particularly at lower speeds. This can be accompanied by a clunking noise during gear changes.
    *   Solution: The issue may be addressed by a software update to the transmission control module (TCM). In more severe cases, replacement of the clutch packs or valve body within the transmission may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 (software update) - $8,000+ (major repair)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Freezing/Crashing**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The infotainment system can occasionally freeze, crash, or become unresponsive. This may require a reboot of the system to restore functionality.
    *   Solution: A software update may resolve the issue. If the problem persists, the infotainment unit itself may need to be replaced.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 (software update) - $3,000+ (unit replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Sensor Malfunctions**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Various sensors, such as those related to the parking assist system, tire pressure monitoring system (TPMS), or adaptive cruise control, have been reported to malfunction. This can trigger warning lights on the dashboard and affect the functionality of these systems.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the faulty sensor is typically required.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+ per sensor
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Convertible Top Malfunctions (if applicable)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: If equipped with the convertible top, some owners have encountered issues with the top failing to open or close properly. This could be due to problems with the hydraulic system, sensors, or mechanical linkages.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis and repair of the faulty components within the convertible top mechanism are required.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $5,000+ depending on the complexity of the repair
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Air Conditioning Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: Some owners have experienced issues with the air conditioning not blowing cold air.
    * Solution: The solution typically involves replacing the failing AC compressor, condenser, or refilling refrigerant.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500-$3,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2023 Ferrari Roma, while a desirable and high-performance vehicle, has some reported issues across various categories. Engine and transmission problems, although not widespread, can be costly to address. Electrical glitches and sensor malfunctions also occur. The severity of these issues ranges from minor inconveniences to more significant mechanical failures. The cost of repairs can vary widely depending on the specific problem, potentially reaching several thousand dollars, especially for engine and transmission work. Prospective buyers should be aware of these potential issues and consider obtaining a thorough pre-purchase inspection.
