**Introduction**
The Ferrari F12 Berlinetta, introduced in 2012, is a high-performance sports car that boasts a 6.3-liter V12 engine and a 7-speed dual-clutch transmission. Critics praised the F12 for its thrilling performance, sophisticated handling, and captivating design. However, despite its accolades, the F12 has not been immune to issues.

**Engine**

**6.3-Liter V12 Engine**

**Coolant Leaks**
Description/Symptoms: Leakage of coolant from various engine components, such as gaskets or hoses, causing overheating and performance issues.
Solution: Replacement of faulty gaskets or hoses.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+
Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**7-Speed Dual-Clutch Transmission**

**Clutch Wear**
Description/Symptoms: Premature wear of the clutch plates, resulting in slipping, shuddering, or difficulty shifting gears.
Solution: Replacement of worn clutch plates.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $3,000 - $6,000+
Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

**Front Suspension**

**Lower Control Arm Bushings Failure**
Description/Symptoms: Worn or damaged lower control arm bushings, leading to excessive play, clunking noises, or impaired handling.
Solution: Replacement of affected bushings.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,200
Recalls: None Found

**Rear Suspension**

**Adaptive Dampers Malfunction**
Description/Symptoms: Failure of the adaptive dampers, resulting in a compromised ride quality or impaired handling.
Solution: Replacement of faulty dampers.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,500 - $4,000+
Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

**Battery Drain**
Description/Symptoms: Excessive battery drain, leading to difficulty starting or dead batteries.
Solution: Diagnosis of electrical system components and repairs as needed.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+
Recalls: None Found

**Summary**
While the Ferrari F12 Berlinetta remains a formidable sports car, it is not without its potential issues. The most prevalent concerns are related to the engine's coolant leaks, transmission clutch wear, and suspension bushings. The cost of repairs for these issues can be substantial, ranging from a few hundred dollars to several thousand dollars. However, it is important to note that not all F12s will experience these problems, and the vehicle's overall reliability has been generally positive.
