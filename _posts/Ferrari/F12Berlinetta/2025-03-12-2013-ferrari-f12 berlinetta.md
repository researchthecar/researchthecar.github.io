The 2013 Ferrari F12 Berlinetta is a grand tourer known for its powerful naturally aspirated V12 engine and stunning design. The only engine option available for this model year was the 6.3L F140FC V12, producing 730 horsepower and 509 lb-ft of torque. It was exclusively paired with a 7-speed dual-clutch transmission. Owners generally praise the car's exhilarating performance, responsive handling, and luxurious interior. However, some common complaints revolve around the high running costs, limited practicality, and potential for expensive repairs.

**Engine**

**6.3L F140FC V12**

*   **Oil Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks can develop from various engine seals and gaskets, leading to visible drips, low oil levels, and a burning oil smell.
    *   Solution: Replacing the affected seals and gaskets.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $5,000+, depending on the location and complexity of the repair.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Catalytic Converter Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The catalytic converters can fail prematurely, triggering a check engine light and reduced performance.
    *   Solution: Replacing the faulty catalytic converters.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $5,000 - $10,000+ per converter, depending on location
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**7-Speed Dual-Clutch Transmission**

*   **Clutch Wear**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Due to the F12 Berlinetta's high performance, the clutches in the dual-clutch transmission can experience premature wear, leading to slipping, jerky shifts, and difficulty engaging gears.
    *   Solution: Replacing the clutch pack.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $5,000 - $10,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Transmission Control Unit (TCU) Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The TCU can experience failures leading to erratic shifting, limp mode, or complete transmission failure.
    *   Solution: Replacing or reprogramming the TCU.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $5,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Magneride Damper Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The Magneride adaptive dampers can leak fluid or fail electronically, resulting in a harsh ride, poor handling, and error messages on the dashboard.
    *   Solution: Replacing the faulty dampers.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $5,000+ per damper
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Battery Drain**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The car can experience rapid battery drain, especially when left unattended for extended periods. This can result in difficulty starting the engine or complete battery failure.
    *   Solution: Diagnosing and addressing the source of the parasitic drain, which could be a faulty module, aftermarket accessory, or wiring issue. Replacing the battery may also be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Sensor Failures**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Various sensors throughout the car (e.g., oxygen sensors, mass airflow sensors, wheel speed sensors) can fail, leading to check engine lights, reduced performance, and safety system malfunctions.
    *   Solution: Replacing the faulty sensors.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+ per sensor, depending on location and type.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Headlight Condensation**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Moisture can accumulate inside the headlight housings, leading to condensation and reduced visibility.
    *   Solution: Resealing or replacing the headlight housings.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $5,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Sticky Buttons**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Over time, the soft-touch coating on some interior buttons and trim pieces can become sticky and unpleasant to the touch.
    *   Solution: Refinishing or replacing the affected parts.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+ depending on the extent and the area to be replaced
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Infotainment System Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The infotainment system can experience glitches, freezes, and connectivity problems.
    *   Solution: Updating or replacing the infotainment system software or hardware.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $3,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2013 Ferrari F12 Berlinetta is a high-performance machine that demands diligent maintenance and care. The most common issues include oil leaks, catalytic converter failures, clutch wear, electrical problems, and interior cosmetic issues. While some of these issues may be relatively minor, others can be quite expensive to repair, particularly those related to the engine, transmission, and suspension. Potential buyers should be aware of these potential pitfalls and budget accordingly or look for a well-maintained example with a comprehensive service history. Overall, owning an F12 Berlinetta can be a thrilling experience, but it requires a significant financial commitment.
