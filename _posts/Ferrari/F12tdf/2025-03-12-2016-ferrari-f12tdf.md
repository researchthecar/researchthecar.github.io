The 2016 Ferrari F12tdf is a high-performance, track-focused variant of the F12berlinetta. It boasts a naturally aspirated 6.3-liter V12 engine producing 769 horsepower and 520 lb-ft of torque. This engine is exclusively paired with a 7-speed dual-clutch transmission. Known for its exceptional power, razor-sharp handling, and distinctive styling, the F12tdf offers an exhilarating driving experience. However, some owners have reported specific issues, primarily related to its complex systems. While celebrated for its performance, the car's limited production and high cost of ownership make addressing these issues potentially expensive.

**Engine**

**6.3L Naturally Aspirated V12**

*   **Oil Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks can develop from various engine seals and gaskets, including the valve cover gaskets and the rear main seal. Symptoms include visible oil drips under the car, a burning oil smell, and low oil levels.
    *   Solution: Replacing the affected seals and gaskets.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $5,000+ depending on the location and extent of the leak.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Exhaust Manifold Cracks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The exhaust manifolds can be susceptible to cracking due to thermal stress. Symptoms include an exhaust leak sound, reduced performance, and potential check engine light activation.
    *   Solution: Replacing the cracked exhaust manifold.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $5,000 - $10,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**7-Speed Dual-Clutch Transmission**

*   **Clutch Wear**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Premature clutch wear can occur, especially with aggressive driving or track use. Symptoms include clutch slippage, difficulty engaging gears, and jerky shifting.
    *   Solution: Replacing the clutch assembly.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $8,000 - $15,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Software Glitches**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported occasional software glitches in the transmission control unit (TCU). Symptoms include erratic shifting behavior, difficulty selecting gears, and temporary loss of drive.
    *   Solution: Reflashing or updating the TCU software. In more severe cases, replacement of the TCU might be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $5,000+ depending on the solution.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Magnetorheological Damper Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The F12tdf utilizes magnetorheological dampers for adaptive suspension. These dampers can sometimes develop leaks or experience internal failures. Symptoms include uneven handling, a bouncy ride, and suspension warning lights.
    *   Solution: Replacing the faulty damper(s).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $3,000 - $6,000+ per damper.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Sensor Failures**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Various sensors throughout the car, such as those related to the engine, transmission, and ABS system, can fail. Symptoms include warning lights, reduced performance, and diagnostic trouble codes.
    *   Solution: Diagnosing and replacing the faulty sensor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+ depending on the sensor.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Battery Drain**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The car can experience excessive battery drain, especially when left unused for extended periods. This can lead to difficulty starting the car or a completely dead battery.
    *   Solution: Replacing the battery, checking for parasitic drain, and using a battery tender when the car is not in use.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **No Issues Found**

**Interior**

*   **Sticky Interior Trim**
    * Description/Symptoms: Some Ferrari models from this era are known to have issues with the interior trim becoming sticky or tacky to the touch, due to the material breaking down over time.
    * Solution: Cleaning the affected surfaces with a specialized cleaner or replacing the affected trim pieces.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $5,000+ depending on the severity and chosen repair method.
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2016 Ferrari F12tdf, while an exceptional performance car, can suffer from issues common to high-performance vehicles with complex systems. Issues range in severity from minor electrical glitches to more significant concerns like engine oil leaks and transmission problems. The cost of repair can be substantial, particularly for engine, transmission, and suspension components. Prospective buyers should carefully inspect the car's maintenance history and consider a pre-purchase inspection by a qualified Ferrari technician.
