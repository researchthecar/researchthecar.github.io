# 2016 Ferrari California T

### Overview
The 2016 Ferrari California T, a grand tourer convertible, is celebrated for its potent twin-turbocharged V8 engine and engaging driving dynamics. While Ferraris are exotic performance machines, the California T generally exhibits strong build quality and reliability for its class. Owners typically report satisfaction with its powertrain and overall construction. However, as with any high-performance vehicle, potential owners should be aware of specific areas that may require attention, particularly concerning the advanced technology and specialized components that contribute to its exhilarating performance. Regular maintenance by qualified technicians is crucial for preserving its peak condition and ensuring long-term dependability.

---


## Engine

### 3.9L Twin-Turbocharged V8
**Turbocharger Actuator Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Reduced engine power, boost pressure warning lights, inconsistent turbo spool.
- **Solution:** Replacement or recalibration of turbocharger wastegate actuators.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1,500

**Oil Leaks (Rear Main Seal)**
- **Symptoms:** Visible oil spots under the vehicle, burning oil smell, low oil level.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the rear main crankshaft seal.
- **Cost:** $1,200 - $2,500


## Transmission

### 7-Speed Dual-Clutch Automatic
**Transmission Control Module (TCM) Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Erratic shifting, delayed gear engagement, 'transmission failure' warnings.
- **Solution:** Software update or replacement of the TCM.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1,200


## Suspension

### Magnetorheological Dampers (Optional MagneRide)
**Damper Leaks/Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Harsh ride, clunking noises over bumps, 'suspension failure' warning light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of faulty dampers.
- **Cost:** $1,000 - $2,000 per damper

## Electrical

### Infotainment System
**Screen Freezing/Unresponsiveness**
- **Symptoms:** Infotainment screen becomes unresponsive or freezes intermittently.
- **Solution:** Software update or reset of the infotainment unit.
- **Cost:** $200 - $500


### Battery Drain
**Parasitic Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Battery dies after short periods of inactivity, difficulty starting.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and repair of faulty modules or circuits causing drain.
- **Cost:** $300 - $800


## Exterior

### Retractable Hardtop Mechanism
**Top Operation Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Slow or jerky operation of the retractable roof, failure to fully open or close, water leaks.
- **Solution:** Lubrication, adjustment, or replacement of hydraulic components or seals.
- **Cost:** $800 - $2,000

### Summary Verdict
The 2016 Ferrari California T generally presents a reliable profile for a luxury exotic sports car. The primary areas of concern revolve around the sophisticated twin-turbo V8 engine's turbocharger components and potential oil leaks, as well as the dual-clutch transmission's control module. The complex retractable hardtop mechanism can also be a source of issues. While electrical gremlins and suspension component failures (especially with optional MagneRide) are possible, they are not overwhelmingly prevalent. With diligent maintenance and prompt attention to any warning signs, the California T can offer a rewarding ownership experience, balancing exhilarating performance with a commendable level of dependability for its segment.
