The 2021 Ferrari 812 GTS is a front-engine, rear-wheel-drive grand tourer convertible. It boasts a naturally aspirated 6.5-liter V12 engine that produces a staggering 789 horsepower and 530 lb-ft of torque. This engine is paired exclusively with a 7-speed dual-clutch transmission. Praised for its exhilarating performance, iconic Ferrari styling, and the unmatched sound of its V12, the 812 GTS also faces criticism for its high price tag, limited practicality, and occasionally reported electronic glitches. This report compiles common issues reported by owners, categorized for easy reference.

**Engine**

**6.5L V12**

*   **Excessive Oil Consumption**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported higher than expected oil consumption, requiring more frequent top-offs between scheduled services. The rate of consumption varies.
    *   Solution: Monitoring oil levels and topping off as needed. In some cases, a dealer inspection may be warranted to check for leaks or other underlying issues.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: Dependent on the cause; inspection may be covered under warranty. More extensive repairs could range from $500 to $5000+ depending on the severity.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Engine Misfires**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Reports of occasional engine misfires, sometimes accompanied by error codes. These misfires can lead to rough idling and reduced performance.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis by a Ferrari technician, potentially involving spark plug replacement, coil pack replacement, or fuel injector cleaning/replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300-$1000+ depending on the cause and extent of the repair.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**7-Speed Dual-Clutch Transmission**

*   **Harsh Shifting**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners experience harsh or jerky shifting, particularly at low speeds or during downshifts.
    *   Solution: Transmission software reflash or recalibration by a Ferrari technician. In some cases, clutch pack replacement may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: Software reflashing $200 - $500. Clutch Pack Replacement $5000+.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Transmission Slippage**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The vehicle will sometimes have sudden slippage while trying to accelerate.
    *   Solution: Full transmission replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $15,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

No Issues Found

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Freezing/Crashing**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The infotainment system occasionally freezes, becomes unresponsive, or crashes, requiring a restart.
    *   Solution: Software update by a Ferrari technician. In some cases, replacement of the infotainment unit may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: Software update may be covered under warranty; replacement of the unit could be $1000+.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Sensor Malfunctions**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Issues with various sensors, such as tire pressure monitoring system (TPMS) sensors or parking sensors, leading to inaccurate readings or error messages.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis and replacement of the faulty sensor(s).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200-$500 per sensor, plus labor.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Retractable Hardtop Mechanism Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Occasional issues with the retractable hardtop mechanism, including slow operation, uneven closing, or error messages.
    *   Solution: Inspection and adjustment of the hardtop mechanism by a Ferrari technician. Lubrication of the moving parts.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2000+ depending on the complexity of the repair.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Stitching Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: Some owners report issues with stitching coming loose or unraveling on seats, dashboard, or door panels.
    * Solution: Repair or replacement of the affected panels by a qualified upholsterer.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1000+ depending on the extent of the damage and materials.
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2021 Ferrari 812 GTS, while an exceptional performance vehicle, is not without its potential issues. The most common complaints revolve around the engine's oil consumption, harsh shifting from the transmission, electrical glitches within the infotainment system, and potential issues with the retractable hardtop mechanism. The severity of these issues ranges from minor inconveniences requiring software updates to more costly repairs like transmission or sensor replacements. While most of these issues are not widespread, prospective buyers should be aware of them and carefully inspect any potential purchase, or have the vehicle inspected by a professional.
