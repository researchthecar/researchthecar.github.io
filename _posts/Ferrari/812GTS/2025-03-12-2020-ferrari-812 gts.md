The 2020 Ferrari 812 GTS is a stunning convertible grand tourer, celebrated for its naturally aspirated V12 engine and exhilarating performance. This model boasts a 6.5-liter V12 engine that delivers a staggering 789 horsepower and 530 lb-ft of torque. The only transmission option available is a 7-speed dual-clutch transmission. Owners often praise the car's incredible engine sound, responsive handling, and luxurious interior. Some common criticisms include its relatively high running costs, firm ride (especially on rough roads), and limited cargo space.

**Engine**

**6.5L V12**

*   **Oil Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks are reported around the valve covers and oil pan gaskets. Owners may notice oil spots on the garage floor or a burning smell.
    *   Solution: Replacing the valve cover gaskets and/or oil pan gasket. Ensure the surfaces are properly cleaned and torqued to specification during reassembly.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Erratic Idle**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported an erratic idle, especially when the engine is cold. This can manifest as fluctuating RPMs or even occasional stalling.
    *   Solution: Checking and cleaning the fuel injectors, throttle body, and idle air control valve (if applicable). A software update might also be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**7-Speed Dual-Clutch Transmission**

*   **Harsh Shifting**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have complained about harsh or jerky shifting, particularly at lower speeds. This can feel like a sudden jolt or hesitation during gear changes.
    *   Solution: A transmission software update might resolve the issue. If the problem persists, a more thorough inspection of the transmission's internal components (e.g., clutch packs, solenoids) may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $5,000+ (depending on severity and required repairs)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **No Issues Found**

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Freezing/Crashing**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The infotainment system may occasionally freeze, crash, or become unresponsive. This can affect navigation, audio, and other vehicle functions.
    *   Solution: Restarting the system by turning the vehicle off and on again. A software update might be available to address the issue.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 (software update) - $1,000+ (if hardware replacement is needed)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Battery Drain**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported issues with excessive battery drain, especially if the car is left unused for an extended period.
    *   Solution: A parasitic draw test can identify the source of the battery drain. Disconnecting the battery when storing the car for long periods can help prevent the issue.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+ (depending on the source of the drain)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Retractable Hardtop Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Reports of retractable hardtop malfunctioning. This could be difficulty opening/closing, unusual noises during operation, or error messages on the dashboard.
    *   Solution: Inspecting the hydraulic system, sensors, and alignment of the roof mechanism. Lubricating the moving parts and replacing faulty components.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $5,000+ (depending on the complexity of the repair)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Leather Shrinkage/Separation**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Reports of the leather shrinking or separating from the dashboard or door panels, particularly in areas exposed to direct sunlight.
    *   Solution: Re-wrapping the affected areas with new leather. Applying a leather conditioner regularly can help prevent the issue.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2020 Ferrari 812 GTS is a remarkable vehicle, but like any high-performance car, it is not without its potential issues. The engine and transmission issues, while not widespread, can be expensive to address. Electrical glitches and hardtop malfunctions are also potential concerns. While the severity and cost of these issues vary, prospective buyers should be aware of them and factor them into their purchasing decision.
