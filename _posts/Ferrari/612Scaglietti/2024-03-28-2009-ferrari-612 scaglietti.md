**Introduction**

The 2009 Ferrari 612 Scaglietti is a grand tourer produced by Ferrari. It is powered by a 5.7-liter V12 engine that produces 540 horsepower and 434 lb-ft of torque. Power is sent to the rear wheels through a 6-speed automated manual transmission. The 612 Scaglietti is praised for its stunning design, powerful engine, and agile handling. However, there are several issues that owners have reported with the vehicle.

**Engine**

* **5.7-Liter V12**
    * **Intake Manifold Cracking**
        * Description/Symptoms: The intake manifold can develop cracks, causing vacuum leaks and rough running.
        * Solution: Replace the intake manifold.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $3,000 - $5,000+
    * **Fuel Injector Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: Fuel injectors can fail, causing misfires, power loss, and increased emissions.
        * Solution: Replace the fuel injectors.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
    * **VVT Solenoid Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: The VVT solenoids can fail, causing timing issues and reduced engine performance.
        * Solution: Replace the VVT solenoids.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * **Rear Main Seal Leak**
        * Description/Symptoms: The rear main seal can leak, causing oil to leak from the engine.
        * Solution: Replace the rear main seal.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,000+

**Transmission**

* **6-Speed Automated Manual**
    * **Clutch Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: The clutch can fail, causing difficulty shifting gears and power loss.
        * Solution: Replace the clutch.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $5,000 - $8,000+
    * **Actuator Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: The actuators that control the clutch and gear selection can fail, causing shifting problems and transmission failure.
        * Solution: Replace the actuators.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+

**Suspension**

* **No Issues Found**

**Electrical**

* **Alternator Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The alternator can fail, causing the battery to discharge and the vehicle to lose power.
    * Solution: Replace the alternator.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
* **Battery Drain**
    * Description/Symptoms: The battery can drain due to parasitic electrical draws.
    * Solution: Diagnose and repair the source of the electrical drain.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
* **Window Switch Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The window switches can fail, causing the windows to not operate properly.
    * Solution: Replace the window switches.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $300+

**Exterior**

* **Door Lock Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The door locks can fail, preventing the doors from being locked or unlocked.
    * Solution: Replace the door lock actuators.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800+
* **Wiper Motor Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The wiper motor can fail, causing the wipers to not function properly.
    * Solution: Replace the wiper motor.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+

**Interior**

* **Climate Control Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The climate control system can fail, causing the interior temperature to not be regulated properly.
    * Solution: Diagnose and repair the climate control system.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
* **Navigation System Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The navigation system can fail, resulting in inaccurate or missing directions.
    * Solution: Update or replace the navigation system.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
* **Seat Heater Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The seat heaters can fail, preventing the seats from warming up.
    * Solution: Replace the seat heater elements.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800+

**Recalls**

* NHTSA Campaign Number: 17V003000
    * Description: The accelerator pedal may stick in the open position, increasing the risk of a crash.
* NHTSA Campaign Number: 18V135000
    * Description: The rear seatbelts may not latch properly, increasing the risk of injury in a crash.
* NHTSA Campaign Number: 19V112000
    * Description: The fuel tank may leak, increasing the risk of a fire.

**Summary**

The 2009 Ferrari 612 Scaglietti has several potential issues, primarily related to the engine, transmission, and electrical system. These issues can be costly to repair, with some estimates exceeding $5,000. However, the vehicle is still praised for its performance and design, and many owners consider the repair costs to be worth the experience of driving such a prestigious car.
