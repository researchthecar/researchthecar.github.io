# 2006 Ferrari 612 Scaglietti

### Overview
The 2006 Ferrari 612 Scaglietti, a grand tourer blending performance with practicality, is a complex machine with a V12 engine and a rear-wheel-drive platform. While generally well-regarded for its driving dynamics and luxurious appointments, like any high-performance exotic, it can present specific reliability concerns. This analysis focuses on documented issues beyond routine maintenance, highlighting areas that owners and prospective buyers should be aware of. The 612 Scaglietti utilizes advanced engineering, which, while contributing to its exceptional performance, can also lead to more intricate and potentially costly repairs when issues arise. Understanding these potential pitfalls is crucial for maintaining this exclusive vehicle.

---


## Engine

### 5.7L V12 (F133)
**Ignition Coil Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Misfires, rough idling, check engine light, reduced power.
- **Solution:** Replacement of faulty ignition coil(s).
- **Cost:** $300 - $600 per coil

**Coolant Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Overheating, visible coolant on the ground, sweet smell.
- **Solution:** Inspection and repair/replacement of hoses, water pump, or radiator.
- **Cost:** $800 - $2500+

**Camshaft/Timing Belt Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Engine noise, loss of power, potential catastrophic engine damage if belt breaks.
- **Solution:** Scheduled replacement of timing belts and associated components (tensioners, idlers).
- **Cost:** $2500 - $5000+


## Transmission

### F1 Automated Manual (Single Clutch)
**Clutch Actuator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Difficulty shifting gears, 'gearbox fault' warning light, inability to engage gears.
- **Solution:** Replacement or repair of the clutch actuator system.
- **Cost:** $3000 - $7000+

**Hydraulic System Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Slow or jerky gear changes, fluid leaks from the transmission area, warning lights.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of hydraulic lines, pumps, or seals.
- **Cost:** $1500 - $4000+

## Suspension

### Adaptive Dampers
**Electronic Damper Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Harsh ride, clunking noises, warning lights related to suspension.
- **Solution:** Replacement of faulty adaptive dampers.
- **Cost:** $1000 - $2500+ per corner


## Electrical

### General Electrical System
**Window Regulator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Power windows not operating, slow operation, unusual noises.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the window regulator mechanism.
- **Cost:** $400 - $800

**Infotainment System Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Screen freezing, non-responsive controls, audio issues.
- **Solution:** Software updates or replacement of head unit components.
- **Cost:** $500 - $2000+

### Summary Verdict
The 2006 Ferrari 612 Scaglietti, while a magnificent V12 grand tourer, requires diligent maintenance and awareness of its specific potential issues. The powertrain, particularly the F1 transmission's automated manual system, can be a source of significant expense, with clutch actuators and hydraulic systems being common failure points. The V12 engine itself is robust but requires adherence to its strict maintenance schedule, especially regarding timing belts and coolant systems. Electrical gremlins, such as window regulators and infotainment glitches, are not uncommon in vehicles of this era and complexity. Suspension components, especially the adaptive dampers, can also be costly to repair. Despite these potential challenges, many owners find the 612 Scaglietti to be a rewarding and reliable exotic when properly cared for by specialists.
