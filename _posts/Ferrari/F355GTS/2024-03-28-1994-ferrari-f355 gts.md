**Introduction:**

The 1994 Ferrari F355 GTS is a mid-engine, rear-wheel-drive sports car that replaced the 348. It was offered with a 3.5-liter V8 engine producing 380 horsepower and 268 lb-ft of torque, mated to a 6-speed manual transmission. The F355 GTS was praised for its performance, handling, and exotic styling, but it also had some reported issues.

**Engine**

* **V8 Engine:**
    * **Cylinder Head Gasket Failure:**
        * Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaks, overheating, and loss of power.
        * Solution: Replace cylinder heads and gaskets.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $5,000 - $10,000+
        * Recalls: None Found
    * **Intake Manifold Gasket Failure:**
        * Description/Symptoms: Vacuum leaks, loss of power, and rough idle.
        * Solution: Replace intake manifold gaskets.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

* **6-Speed Manual Transmission:**
    * **Synchros Wear:**
        * Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting into gears, especially when downshifting.
        * Solution: Replace worn synchros.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Front Control Arms:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Clunking or knocking noises when turning or over bumps.
    * Solution: Replace worn control arms.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000 per arm
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Alternator Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Dimming headlights, electrical malfunctions, and a dead battery.
    * Solution: Replace alternator.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,500+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Battery Drain:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Dead battery after a period of inactivity.
    * Solution: Diagnose and repair electrical leak.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **No Major Issues Found**

**Interior**

* **Power Window Switch Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Inoperative power windows.
    * Solution: Replace faulty switch.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500 per switch
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

The 1994 Ferrari F355 GTS is a performance-oriented sports car with some potential issues, particularly with its engine and transmission. These issues can be costly to fix, but they are generally not widespread and can be addressed if caught early on. Despite these issues, the F355 GTS remains a highly desirable and iconic classic Ferrari.
