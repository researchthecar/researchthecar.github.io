# 2023 Ferrari 296 GTB

### Overview
The 2023 Ferrari 296 GTB represents a significant leap in Ferrari's hybrid supercar technology, combining a potent twin-turbocharged V6 engine with an electric motor. This powertrain architecture, while advanced, introduces new complexities. Early data suggests a high level of engineering and build quality, typical of Ferrari's flagship models. However, as with any high-performance, low-volume vehicle, specific component reliability can be subject to individual usage, maintenance, and the inherent nature of cutting-edge automotive technology. This analysis focuses on documented or commonly anticipated technical concerns beyond routine maintenance, aiming to provide a clear picture of potential reliability aspects.

---


## Engine

### 3.0L Twin-Turbocharged V6 Hybrid
**Hybrid System Integration**
- **Symptoms:** Intermittent power delivery fluctuations, warning lights related to electric motor or battery management.
- **Solution:** Software updates, diagnostic checks of electric motor and battery control modules, potential replacement of hybrid components.
- **Cost:** $1,500 - $5,000+

**Turbocharger Actuator Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Reduced engine power, turbo lag, wastegate rattle.
- **Solution:** Replacement of faulty turbocharger actuator or wastegate assembly.
- **Cost:** $800 - $2,000

### Summary Verdict
The 2023 Ferrari 296 GTB, with its advanced hybrid powertrain, exhibits a strong reliability profile for a vehicle of its complexity and performance class. The primary areas of potential concern revolve around the integration of the hybrid system, including its battery management and electric motor components, and potential software-related glitches within the infotainment system. While the V6 engine and dual-clutch transmission are robust, the hybrid elements introduce new variables. Owners should adhere strictly to Ferrari's recommended maintenance schedule and be aware that repairs involving hybrid components can be costly. Overall, for a supercar, the 296 GTB is engineered to high standards, but the cutting-edge technology necessitates diligent care and awareness of potential, albeit infrequent, issues.
