The 2024 Ferrari 296 GTB is a mid-engine, rear-wheel-drive plug-in hybrid sports car. Its primary powertrain consists of a 3.0-liter twin-turbocharged V6 engine paired with an electric motor, producing a combined output of 818 horsepower and 546 lb-ft of torque. The sole transmission option is an 8-speed dual-clutch automatic. Owners typically rave about the 296 GTB's exhilarating performance, sharp handling, and distinctive V6 sound. Some criticisms focus on the complexity of the hybrid system and the potential for increased maintenance costs associated with it.

**Electric Motor**

No Issues Found

**Engine**

**3.0L Twin-Turbocharged V6 Hybrid**
* **High Voltage Battery Faults**
    * Description/Symptoms: Warning light illuminates on the dash. Reduced power output and inability to utilize electric mode. Diagnostic codes indicate an issue within the high-voltage battery pack itself or its management system.
    * Solution: Diagnosis by a Ferrari technician to pinpoint the faulty battery module or control unit. Replacement of the defective component. Software updates for the battery management system.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $15,000 - $30,000+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Turbocharger Oil Leaks**
    * Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks detected around the turbochargers. Visible smoke from the exhaust. Reduced engine performance.
    * Solution: Replacement of the leaking turbocharger oil lines, gaskets, or seals. If the turbocharger itself is damaged, replacement of the entire unit.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $5,000 - $10,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**8-Speed Dual-Clutch Automatic**
* **Clutch Slippage/Harsh Shifting**
    * Description/Symptoms: Noticeable slippage during gear changes, especially under hard acceleration. Jerky or harsh shifting between gears. Diagnostic trouble codes related to clutch performance.
    * Solution: Inspection of the clutch pack for wear or damage. Replacement of the clutch pack. Software recalibration of the transmission control unit (TCU).
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $8,000 - $15,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Magneride Damper Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Clunking noises from the suspension. Reduced ride quality. Warning lights on the dash related to the suspension system. Uneven tire wear.
    * Solution: Diagnosis to identify the faulty damper. Replacement of the affected Magneride damper unit.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $4,000 - $8,000+ (per damper)
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Infotainment System Freezing/Crashing**
    * Description/Symptoms: The infotainment screen freezes, becomes unresponsive, or restarts unexpectedly. Loss of functionality for navigation, audio, and climate control.
    * Solution: Software update for the infotainment system. In some cases, replacement of the infotainment unit may be necessary.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $5,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Headlight Condensation**
    * Description/Symptoms: Moisture or condensation build-up inside the headlight housings. Reduced headlight output.
    * Solution: Replacement of the headlight housings. Ensuring proper sealing of the headlight assembly.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $3,000 - $6,000+ (per headlight)
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Dashboard/Trim Rattles**
    * Description/Symptoms: Annoying rattling noises emanating from the dashboard or interior trim panels, especially when driving on rough roads.
    * Solution: Identification of the source of the rattle. Tightening or replacing loose fasteners. Installation of sound-deadening material to eliminate vibrations.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+

    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2024 Ferrari 296 GTB, while a stunning performance car, is exhibiting some common issues. The most serious are related to the high-voltage battery and the potential for turbocharger or transmission clutch failure, which could result in significant repair costs. Electrical issues such as infotainment system malfunctions and rattles in the interior trim also appear to be present but are less impactful financially. Potential buyers should be aware of these potential problems and factor them into their ownership considerations.
