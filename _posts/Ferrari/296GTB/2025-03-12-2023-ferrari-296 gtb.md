Here's a breakdown of common issues and complaints reported for the 2023 Ferrari 296 GTB, categorized by major components. The 2023 Ferrari 296 GTB is a high-performance plug-in hybrid sports car featuring a mid-mounted 3.0-liter twin-turbocharged V6 engine paired with an electric motor, delivering a combined output of 818 horsepower and 546 lb-ft of torque. All models use an 8-speed dual-clutch transmission. People admire the 296 GTB for its exhilarating performance, cutting-edge technology, and striking design. Criticisms often revolve around its high price, complex hybrid system (potentially leading to reliability concerns), and limited practicality as a daily driver.

**Electric Motor**

**Electric Motor**
*   **High Voltage Battery Fault**
    *   Description/Symptoms: "Hybrid System Fault" warning on the dashboard, reduced power output, inability to charge the battery, or complete failure of the electric motor.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis by a certified Ferrari technician to determine the cause of the fault (e.g., faulty battery cell, BMS issue). Replacement of the faulty battery module or the entire high-voltage battery pack. Software update.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $15,000 - $40,000+ (depending on the extent of the damage and whether individual modules or the entire pack needs replacement).
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Electric Motor Cooling System Leak**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Coolant leak near the electric motor, overheating warning, reduced electric motor performance.
    *   Solution: Inspection and repair or replacement of leaking coolant lines, hoses, or the electric motor cooling pump.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $5,000
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Engine**

**3.0L Twin-Turbocharged V6**
*   **Oil Leaks from Turbocharger Lines**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Oil drips under the car, burning oil smell, visible oil residue around the turbochargers or their feed/drain lines. Can lead to low oil levels and potential engine damage if not addressed.
    *   Solution: Replacement of faulty turbocharger oil lines and gaskets. Thorough cleaning of affected areas.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $6,000+ (depending on accessibility and if other components need removal).
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Fuel Injector Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Engine misfires, rough idling, check engine light illumination, reduced power, poor fuel economy.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis to identify the faulty fuel injector(s). Replacement of the failed injector(s).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $4,000+ (depending on the number of injectors replaced and labor).
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**8-Speed Dual-Clutch Transmission**
*   **Hesitation/Jerking During Low-Speed Shifts**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Noticeable hesitation or jerking during shifting, especially at low speeds or in automatic mode. Can feel like a "clunky" shift.
    *   Solution: Software update to transmission control module (TCM). In some cases, clutch recalibration or replacement may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 (software update) to $10,000+ (clutch replacement).
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Transmission Overheating**
    * Description/Symptoms: Warning light on the dashboard, rough or improper gear changes, loss of power
    * Solution: Replacing or repairing the transmission
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $10,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Adaptive Damper Malfunction**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Uneven ride quality, clunking noises from the suspension, "Suspension Fault" warning on the dashboard. Can affect handling and stability.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis to identify the faulty damper. Replacement of the affected adaptive damper.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $3,000 - $8,000+ (depending on the location of the damper and labor).
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Freezing/Crashing**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Infotainment screen freezing, system crashes, unresponsive touch controls, loss of navigation or audio functions.
    *   Solution: Software update to the infotainment system. In some cases, replacement of the infotainment unit may be required.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 (software update) to $5,000+ (infotainment unit replacement).
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Sensor Failures**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Various warning lights and/or messages on the dashboard. May affect driving experience if it affects the safety/driving system.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis of the sensors at fault. Replacement of faulty sensor(s).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $3,000+ (depending on the number and type of sensors, and if a wiring harness needs to be fixed or replaced).
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Panel Gaps/Alignment Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Inconsistent panel gaps between body panels, misaligned doors or trunk lid. Primarily a cosmetic issue.
    *   Solution: Adjustment of body panels by a skilled technician to improve alignment.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $3,000+ (depending on the complexity of the adjustments).
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Loose Interior Trim Pieces**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rattling noises from interior trim panels (e.g., dashboard, door panels). Loose or detached trim pieces.
    *   Solution: Reattachment or replacement of loose trim clips or panels.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,500+ (depending on the extent of the damage and the location of the trim).
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2023 Ferrari 296 GTB, while a stunning and high-performing vehicle, appears to have potential issues ranging from minor cosmetic concerns to more significant mechanical and electrical problems. The hybrid system, in particular, seems to be a potential source of expensive repairs. While not all owners will experience these issues, potential buyers should be aware of the possibility of costly repairs, especially related to the high-voltage battery, transmission, and adaptive suspension. Thorough pre-purchase inspections are recommended. The cost to fix these issues can range from a few hundred dollars for minor trim repairs to tens of thousands for major component replacements.
