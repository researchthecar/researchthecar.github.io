# 2019 Ferrari Portofino Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2019 Ferrari Portofino is a high-performance retractable hardtop grand tourer designed to offer both everyday usability and supercar capability. As a complex luxury exotic, prospective buyers should approach ownership with an understanding that maintenance and specialized repairs demand a significant financial commitment. While formal owner complaint filings are sparse, monitoring powertrain health and electronics remains vital when shopping in the pre-owned market.

---

## Engine

### 3.9L Twin-Turbocharged V8

This 3.9-liter twin-turbocharged V8 produces approximately 591 horsepower and 561 lb-ft of torque. It delivers exhilarating acceleration while serving as a sophisticated powerhouse requiring meticulous maintenance.

## Transmission

### 7-Speed Dual-Clutch Automatic

The 7-speed dual-clutch transmission provides lightning-fast gear changes via steering-wheel-mounted paddle shifters. Proper clutch calibration and fluid maintenance are essential for long-term operational smoothness.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V536000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ferrari&model=Portofino&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** The brake fluid reservoir cap may not vent properly, creating a vacuum inside the brake fluid reservoir, and resulting in a brake fluid leak that may lead to a partial or total loss of brake function. A loss of brake function can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the brake fluid reservoir cap and update the software to provide a new warning message for low brake fluid, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.
