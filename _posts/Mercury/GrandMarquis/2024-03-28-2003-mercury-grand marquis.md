**Introduction:**

The 2003 Mercury Grand Marquis is a full-size luxury sedan praised for its spacious interior, comfortable ride, and powerful V8 engine. It is powered by a standard 4.6L Modular V8 engine paired with a 4-speed automatic transmission.

**Engine**

* **4.6L Modular V8**
    * **Valve Seat Failure:** Premature wear and failure of valve seats can cause loss of compression, reduced power, and misfiring.
        * Solution: Replace the affected valve seats.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
        * Recalls: NHTSA Campaign Number 03V401000
    * **Intake Manifold Gasket Failure:** A faulty intake manifold gasket can cause coolant and/or oil leaks, leading to overheating or engine damage.
        * Solution: Replace the intake manifold gasket.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

* **4-Speed Automatic Transmission (4R75W)**
    * **Torque Converter Lock-up Solenoid Failure:** A malfunctioning torque converter lock-up solenoid can prevent the transmission from shifting smoothly, causing slipping or shuddering.
        * Solution: Replace the torque converter lock-up solenoid.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Strut Mount Failure:** Worn or damaged strut mounts can lead to a clunking or rattling noise when driving over bumps.
        * Solution: Replace the strut mounts.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Battery Discharge:** A parasitic drain on the battery can result in premature battery failure, especially during cold weather.
        * Solution: Identify and fix the source of the parasitic drain.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the source of the drain
        * Recalls: None Found
* **Alternator Failure:** A faulty alternator can lead to a loss of electrical power, causing the vehicle to stall or run poorly.
        * Solution: Replace the alternator.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $600 - $900+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Door Handle Breakage:** The plastic door handles can become brittle and break, making it difficult to open the doors.
        * Solution: Replace the affected door handles.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Dashboard Cracking:** The dashboard can develop cracks over time, especially in areas exposed to sunlight.
        * Solution: Replace or repair the dashboard.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $600 - $1,200+
        * Recalls: None Found
* **Power Window Motor Failure:** The power window motors can fail, causing the windows to become inoperable.
        * Solution: Replace the affected power window motors.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+ per window
        * Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

The 2003 Mercury Grand Marquis generally has a moderate to high severity of issues, with estimated repair costs ranging widely depending on the problem. Engine and transmission concerns are the most prevalent and potentially expensive to address. The electrical, exterior, and interior issues are less severe but can still be costly to resolve.
