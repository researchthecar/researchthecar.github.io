The 2007 Mercury Grand Marquis is a full-size sedan known for its comfortable ride and traditional body-on-frame construction. It came standard with a 4.6L Modular V8 engine, producing 224 horsepower and 272 lb-ft of torque. This engine was paired exclusively with a 4-speed automatic transmission. People generally appreciate the Grand Marquis for its spaciousness, reliability, and smooth ride. Common dislikes include its somewhat dated styling and lower fuel economy compared to newer vehicles.

**Engine**

**4.6L Modular V8**

*   **Spark Plug Thread Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Spark plugs may eject from the cylinder head due to stripped threads, resulting in a loud popping noise and engine misfire. This is more common on early production 4.6L 2V engines.
    *   Solution: Repair the cylinder head with thread repair inserts, or replace the cylinder head.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2000+ depending on the repair method and whether the head is replaced.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Intake Manifold Cracks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Cracks in the plastic intake manifold can develop, leading to coolant leaks, overheating, and potential engine damage.
    *   Solution: Replace the intake manifold with an updated version (if available) or a new manifold.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Oil Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks can occur from various locations, including the valve cover gaskets, oil pan gasket, and rear main seal. Symptoms include visible oil drips, burning oil smell, and low oil level.
    *   Solution: Replace the leaking gaskets or seals.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+ depending on the location and severity of the leak.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**4-Speed Automatic (4R70W/4R75W)**

*   **Harsh or Delayed Shifting**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The transmission may exhibit harsh or delayed shifts, particularly between 1st and 2nd gear or when shifting into overdrive.
    *   Solution: Fluid flush/change, Valve body replacement, or full transmission rebuild/replacement
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150-$3000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Torque Converter Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Shuddering at highway speeds and rough shifts.
    *   Solution: Replace torque converter, flush transmission cooler lines
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $600 - $1200+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Air Suspension Failure (if equipped)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The rear of the vehicle may sag or sit unevenly due to a failing air compressor, air springs (bags), or height sensors.
    *   Solution: Replace the faulty air compressor, air springs, and/or height sensors. Many owners convert to coil springs.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1500+ depending on the component replaced and whether converting to coil springs.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Ball Joint Wear**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Front suspension ball joints are prone to wear, leading to clunking noises, loose steering, and uneven tire wear.
    *   Solution: Replace the worn ball joints.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500 per side.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Blend Door Actuator Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Improper climate control (e.g., only hot or cold air blowing), clicking noises from behind the dashboard.
    *   Solution: Replace the faulty blend door actuator.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $400
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Instrument Cluster Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Intermittent or complete failure of the instrument cluster, including gauges not working or flickering.
    *   Solution: Repair or replace the instrument cluster.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Rust on Lower Body Panels**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rust develops on the lower rocker panels, wheel wells, and door bottoms, especially in areas with road salt usage.
    *   Solution: Rust repair, panel replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1000+ per panel depending on the severity of the rust and the repair method.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **No Issues Found**

**Summary**

The 2007 Mercury Grand Marquis is a generally reliable vehicle, but owners should be aware of common issues such as spark plug thread failures, intake manifold cracks, air suspension problems (if equipped), and blend door actuator failures. The severity and cost of these repairs can vary, but addressing them promptly can help prevent further damage and maintain the vehicle's overall condition. The most expensive repairs typically relate to the engine or air suspension systems.
