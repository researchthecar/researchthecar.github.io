**Introduction:**

The 1998 Mercury Grand Marquis is a full-size luxury sedan known for its spacious interior, comfortable ride, and powerful engine. It comes with a choice of a 4.6L V8 engine paired with a four-speed automatic transmission or a 5.4L V8 engine paired with a five-speed automatic transmission.

**Engine**

**4.6L V8**

- **Intake manifold gasket failure:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Coolant or oil leaks, overheating, rough idle
    - Solution: Replace intake manifold gasket
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
    - Recalls: None Found

**5.4L V8**

- **Spark plug blowout:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Engine misfires, rough idle, loss of power
    - Solution: Replace spark plugs and inspect for head damage
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    - Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**Four-Speed Automatic**

- **TCC solenoid failure:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Harsh shifts, slipping gears, delayed engagement
    - Solution: Replace TCC solenoid
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
    - Recalls: None Found

**Five-Speed Automatic**

- **Shift solenoid failure:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears, transmission stuck in specific gear
    - Solution: Replace shift solenoid
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $250 - $500+
    - Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

- **Strut mount failure:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Knocking or clunking noises, rough ride, misalignment
    - Solution: Replace strut mounts
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
    - Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

- **Battery discharge:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Battery drains quickly even when the car is off
    - Solution: Inspect electrical system for parasitic draw
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+
    - Recalls: None Found

- **Headlight switch failure:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Headlights not working, intermittent operation
    - Solution: Replace headlight switch
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $250+
    - Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

- **Bumper cover fading:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Protective layer on bumper cover becomes dull or peels
    - Solution: Paint or replace bumper cover
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
    - Recalls: None Found

- **Weatherstripping deterioration:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Leaks, wind noise, poor insulation
    - Solution: Replace weatherstripping
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $250+
    - Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

- **Dashboard rattling:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Annoying rattling noises coming from dashboard
    - Solution: Tighten loose screws or apply foam padding
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $150
    - Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

The 1998 Mercury Grand Marquis is generally a reliable vehicle, but it can experience some common issues. The engine and transmission problems can be serious and expensive to fix. Suspension and electrical issues are typically less severe and less costly. The exterior and interior issues tend to be aesthetic or minor inconveniences. Overall, the Grand Marquis offers a comfortable and powerful ride, but it is important to be aware of potential issues before making a purchase decision.
