## Suspension

### Standard Chassis
**Strut Mounts**
- **Symptoms:** Clunking or popping noises from the front suspension, especially when turning or going over bumps.
- **Solution:** Replacement of worn strut mounts.
- **Cost:** $200 - $500


## Electrical

### Body Control Module (BCM)
**Intermittent Electrical Failures**
- **Symptoms:** Various electrical components may malfunction or stop working intermittently, including power windows, door locks, and interior lights.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and potential reprogramming or replacement of the BCM.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1000


### Alternator
**Premature Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Battery warning light illuminated, dimming headlights, difficulty starting the engine, or complete loss of electrical power.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the alternator.
- **Cost:** $400 - $700


## Exterior

### Paint and Body
**Clear Coat Peeling**
- **Symptoms:** The clear coat on the exterior paint may start to peel or flake, particularly on horizontal surfaces like the roof and hood.
- **Solution:** Repainting affected areas.
- **Cost:** $500 - $2000+


## Interior

### HVAC System
**Blend Door Actuator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Inability to control temperature or direct airflow (e.g., air only blows out of defroster vents).
- **Solution:** Replacement of the faulty blend door actuator.
- **Cost:** $200 - $400

### Summary Verdict
The 2009 Mercury Milan is a generally dependable vehicle with a few common areas of concern. The powertrain (engine and transmission) appears robust with no widespread issues reported. However, owners should be aware of potential problems with suspension components like strut mounts, electrical systems including the Body Control Module and alternator, exterior paint durability, and interior HVAC blend door actuators. Addressing these issues proactively can help maintain the Milan's overall reliability and comfort. Costs for repairs vary, but the provided estimates offer a general guideline for budgeting.
