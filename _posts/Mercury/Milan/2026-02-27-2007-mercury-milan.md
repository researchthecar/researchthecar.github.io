# 2007 Mercury Milan

### Overview
The 2007 Mercury Milan, a mid-size sedan, offered a blend of comfort and practicality. It shared its platform with the Ford Fusion and Lincoln MKZ, benefiting from Ford's engineering. Reliability for the Milan generally aligns with its Ford counterparts, with some specific areas to note. While many owners report a positive ownership experience, certain components have shown a propensity for issues over time, particularly concerning the powertrain and some electrical systems. Regular maintenance is key to ensuring the longevity of this vehicle.

---


## Engine

### 2.3L I4
**Cooling Fan Motor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Overheating, AC not blowing cold air.
- **Solution:** Replace cooling fan motor assembly.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600

**Camshaft Position Sensor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Check engine light, rough idle, stalling.
- **Solution:** Replace camshaft position sensor.
- **Cost:** $150 - $300


### 3.0L V6
**Intake Manifold Gasket Leak**
- **Symptoms:** Vacuum leaks, rough idle, coolant loss.
- **Solution:** Replace intake manifold gaskets.
- **Cost:** $400 - $800

**Variable Valve Timing (VVT) Solenoid Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Check engine light, rough idle, reduced engine power.
- **Solution:** Replace VVT solenoids.
- **Cost:** $250 - $500


## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic (6F50)
**Transmission Fluid Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Leaking fluid, slipping gears, harsh shifting.
- **Solution:** Inspect and repair or replace seals and gaskets; flush and refill transmission fluid.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1500

**Torque Converter Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Shuddering, slipping, delayed engagement.
- **Solution:** Replace torque converter.
- **Cost:** $800 - $2000


## Suspension

### Standard Chassis
**Worn Ball Joints/Tie Rod Ends**
- **Symptoms:** Clunking noises over bumps, loose steering, uneven tire wear.
- **Solution:** Replace worn ball joints and/or tie rod ends.
- **Cost:** $300 - $700

**Strut Mount Bearing Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Popping or creaking noise when turning the steering wheel.
- **Solution:** Replace strut mount bearings.
- **Cost:** $200 - $400


## Electrical

### General Electrical System
**Window Regulator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Power windows not operating, window stuck in position.
- **Solution:** Replace window regulator assembly.
- **Cost:** $200 - $400

**Instrument Cluster Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** Gauges not working, warning lights staying on, dimming display.
- **Solution:** Repair or replace instrument cluster.
- **Cost:** $400 - $900


## Exterior

### Body and Trim
**No Major Issues**
- **Symptoms:** No widespread issues reported.
- **Solution:** No widespread issues reported.
- **Cost:** N/A


## Interior

### Cabin Components
**HVAC Blower Motor Resistor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** HVAC fan only works on highest setting or not at all.
- **Solution:** Replace HVAC blower motor resistor.
- **Cost:** $100 - $250

**Door Lock Actuator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Power door locks not working, manual operation difficult.
- **Solution:** Replace door lock actuator.
- **Cost:** $150 - $300


--- 
### Summary Verdict
The 2007 Mercury Milan is a generally reliable vehicle, especially when equipped with the 2.3L I4 engine. The 3.0L V6, while offering more power, has a slightly higher potential for issues like intake manifold leaks. Automatic transmission issues, particularly fluid leaks and torque converter problems, are the most significant concerns. Suspension components like ball joints and tie rods can wear out, and electrical gremlins such as window regulators and instrument cluster problems are not uncommon. Overall, with diligent maintenance and addressing specific known issues, the Milan can be a dependable sedan.
