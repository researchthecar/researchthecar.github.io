**Introduction:**

The 2004 Pontiac Vibe is a compact SUV with two engine options: a 1.8-liter 4-cylinder and a 2.4-liter 4-cylinder. Both engines are paired with either a 5-speed manual transmission or a 4-speed automatic transmission. The Vibe is known for its versatility, fuel efficiency, and affordability. However, it does have some common issues that owners should be aware of.

**Engine**

**1.8-Liter 4-Cylinder**

* **Intake manifold gasket failure:** Symptoms include coolant leaks, overheating, and a loss of power. Solution: Replace the intake manifold gasket. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
* **Valve cover gasket leak:** Symptoms include oil leaks and a burning oil smell. Solution: Replace the valve cover gasket. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
* **Timing chain tensioner failure:** Symptoms include a rattling noise from the engine and a loss of power. Solution: Replace the timing chain tensioner. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+

**2.4-Liter 4-Cylinder**

* **Intake manifold gasket failure:** Symptoms include coolant leaks, overheating, and a loss of power. Solution: Replace the intake manifold gasket. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
* **Valve cover gasket leak:** Symptoms include oil leaks and a burning oil smell. Solution: Replace the valve cover gasket. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+

**Transmission**

**5-Speed Manual Transmission**

* **Clutch failure:** Symptoms include difficulty shifting gears and a burning clutch smell. Solution: Replace the clutch. Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800+
* **Transmission noise:** Symptoms include a whining or grinding noise from the transmission. Solution: Rebuild or replace the transmission. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+

**4-Speed Automatic Transmission**

* **Transmission slipping:** Symptoms include a loss of power and hesitation when shifting gears. Solution: Rebuild or replace the transmission. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
* **Transmission fluid leak:** Symptoms include a leak under the transmission and a low transmission fluid level. Solution: Replace the transmission fluid pan gasket or other seals. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+

**Suspension**

**Front Suspension**

* **Strut mount failure:** Symptoms include a clunking noise from the front of the vehicle and uneven tire wear. Solution: Replace the strut mounts. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+

**Rear Suspension**

* **Shock absorber failure:** Symptoms include a rough ride and poor handling. Solution: Replace the shock absorbers. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+

**Electrical**

* **Battery drain:** Symptoms include a dead battery and difficulty starting the vehicle. Solution: Test the battery and electrical system to identify the source of the drain. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
* **Alternator failure:** Symptoms include a loss of power and a dim charging light on the dashboard. Solution: Replace the alternator. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+

**Exterior**

* **Headlight bulb failure:** Symptoms include a dim or non-functioning headlight. Solution: Replace the headlight bulb. Estimated Cost to Fix: $10 - $50+

**Interior**

* **Dashboard rattle:** Symptoms include a rattling noise from the dashboard. Solution: Locate and tighten any loose screws or panels. Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 - $100+
* **Climate control issues:** Symptoms include a malfunctioning air conditioner or heater. Solution: Diagnose and repair the climate control system. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+

**Summary**

The 2004 Pontiac Vibe is a generally reliable vehicle, but it does have some common issues that owners should be aware of. The severity of these issues varies, but some, such as intake manifold gasket failure and transmission problems, can be costly to repair. Potential buyers should carefully consider these issues before purchasing a used Vibe.
