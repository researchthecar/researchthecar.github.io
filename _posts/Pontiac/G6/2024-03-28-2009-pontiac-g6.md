The 2009 Pontiac G6 is a mid-size sedan that was offered with a variety of engine and transmission options. The base engine was a 2.4-liter inline-4 that produced 169 horsepower and 160 lb-ft of torque. This engine was available with either a 4-speed automatic transmission or a 5-speed manual transmission. The optional engine was a 3.5-liter V6 that produced 215 horsepower and 223 lb-ft of torque. This engine was available with either a 5-speed automatic transmission or a 6-speed manual transmission.

Owners of the 2009 Pontiac G6 have reported a number of issues with their vehicles. These issues include:

**Engine**

**2.4-liter inline-4**

* **Intake manifold gasket failure:** The intake manifold gasket can fail, causing a vacuum leak. This can lead to a loss of power and fuel economy. The solution is to replace the intake manifold gasket. The estimated cost to fix this issue is $300 - $500.
* **Timing chain failure:** The timing chain can fail, causing the engine to lose power and eventually stall. The solution is to replace the timing chain. The estimated cost to fix this issue is $1,000 - $2,000.

**3.5-liter V6**

* **Valve cover gasket leak:** The valve cover gasket can leak, causing oil to leak onto the engine. This can lead to a loss of oil and engine damage. The solution is to replace the valve cover gasket. The estimated cost to fix this issue is $200 - $400.
* **Water pump failure:** The water pump can fail, causing the engine to overheat. The solution is to replace the water pump. The estimated cost to fix this issue is $300 - $500.

**Transmission**

**4-speed automatic transmission**

* **Transmission slipping:** The transmission may slip, causing the vehicle to lose power and acceleration. The solution is to rebuild or replace the transmission. The estimated cost to fix this issue is $2,000 - $3,000.
* **Torque converter failure:** The torque converter may fail, causing the transmission to lose power and acceleration. The solution is to replace the torque converter. The estimated cost to fix this issue is $1,000 - $2,000.

**5-speed automatic transmission**

* **Shift solenoid failure:** The shift solenoid may fail, causing the transmission to shift erratically. The solution is to replace the shift solenoid. The estimated cost to fix this issue is $200 - $400.
* **Transmission cooler line leak:** The transmission cooler line may leak, causing the transmission fluid to leak out. This can lead to transmission damage. The solution is to replace the transmission cooler line. The estimated cost to fix this issue is $200 - $400.

**Suspension**

* **Strut failure:** The struts can fail, causing the vehicle to ride rough and handle poorly. The solution is to replace the struts. The estimated cost to fix this issue is $500 - $1,000 per strut.
* **Shock failure:** The shocks can fail, causing the vehicle to ride rough and handle poorly. The solution is to replace the shocks. The estimated cost to fix this issue is $300 - $600 per shock.

**Electrical**

* **Battery drain:** The battery may drain, causing the vehicle to not start. The solution is to test the battery and electrical system to find the source of the drain. The estimated cost to fix this issue is $100 - $300.
* **Alternator failure:** The alternator may fail, causing the battery to not charge. The solution is to replace the alternator. The estimated cost to fix this issue is $400 - $600.

**Exterior**

* **Headlight failure:** The headlights may fail, making it difficult to see at night. The solution is to replace the headlight bulbs. The estimated cost to fix this issue is $50 - $100 per bulb.
* **Tail light failure:** The tail lights may fail, making it difficult for other drivers to see the vehicle at night. The solution is to replace the tail light bulbs. The estimated cost to fix this issue is $50 - $100 per bulb.

**Interior**

* **Climate control failure:** The climate control system may fail, causing the vehicle to be too hot or too cold. The solution is to diagnose and repair the climate control system. The estimated cost to fix this issue is $500 - $1,000.
* **Power window failure:** The power windows may fail, making it difficult to open and close the windows. The solution is to replace the power window motor or switch. The estimated cost to fix this issue is $200 - $400 per window.

**Summary**

The 2009 Pontiac G6 is a reliable vehicle that offers good performance and fuel economy. However, there are a number of potential issues that owners should be aware of. These issues can be expensive to fix, so it is important to factor them into the cost of ownership.
