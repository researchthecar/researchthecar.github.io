**Introduction**

The 2004 Pontiac Aztek was a mid-size SUV that offered a unique blend of style and functionality. It was available with two engine options: a 3.4L V6 and a 3.8L V6. The 3.4L V6 produced 185 horsepower and 210 lb-ft of torque, while the 3.8L V6 generated 200 horsepower and 225 lb-ft of torque. Both engines were paired with a 4-speed automatic transmission.

While the Aztek was praised for its versatility and practicality, it also received criticism for its unconventional design and lackluster performance.

**Engine**

**3.4L V6**

* **Intake Manifold Gasket Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Leaking coolant and oil, engine overheating
    * Solution: Replace intake manifold gasket
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $600+
* **Valve Cover Gasket Leak**
    * Description/Symptoms: Oil leak from under the valve covers
    * Solution: Replace valve cover gaskets
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $300+

**3.8L V6**

* **No Significant Issues Found**

**Transmission**

**4-Speed Automatic**

* **Transmission Fluid Leak**
    * Description/Symptoms: Leaking transmission fluid from the pan or lines
    * Solution: Replace leaking components
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+

**Suspension**

* **Front Suspension Strut Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Clunking noises, vibration, uneven tire wear
    * Solution: Replace front suspension struts
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $600+ per side
* **Rear Suspension Spring Sag**
    * Description/Symptoms: Vehicle sagging in the rear, poor handling
    * Solution: Replace rear suspension springs
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $300+ per side

**Electrical**

* **Alternator Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Dimming lights, difficulty starting the vehicle
    * Solution: Replace alternator
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $600+
* **Battery Drain**
    * Description/Symptoms: Dead battery after sitting for a period of time
    * Solution: Test electrical system for parasitic draw
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+

**Exterior**

* **Door Handle Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Door handle breaks or becomes loose
    * Solution: Replace door handle assembly
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $300+ per door
* **Rear Bumper Sag**
    * Description/Symptoms: Rear bumper sags or becomes loose
    * Solution: Realign or replace rear bumper
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $300+

**Interior**

* **Climate Control Malfunction**
    * Description/Symptoms: Heater or AC not working, temperature not regulating
    * Solution: Diagnose and replace faulty climate control components
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
* **Power Window Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Power windows not working or moving slowly
    * Solution: Replace power window motor or switch
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+ per window

**Summary**

Overall, the 2004 Pontiac Aztek has several common issues that can be costly to repair. These issues range from minor leaks to major component failures, so it's important to be aware of them before purchasing a used Aztek. The severity of these issues can vary depending on the specific year and mileage of the vehicle.
