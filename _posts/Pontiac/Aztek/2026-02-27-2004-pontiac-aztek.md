# 2004 Pontiac Aztek

### Overview
The 2004 Pontiac Aztek, a distinctive crossover SUV, presented a unique design that polarized opinions. From a reliability standpoint, it exhibited a mixed bag of performance. While some owners reported generally trouble-free operation, others encountered specific issues, particularly with certain powertrain components and electrical systems. The Aztek's modular design, while offering versatility, also meant a complex assembly that could lead to isolated problems. Understanding these common failure points is crucial for potential buyers and current owners looking to maintain their vehicle effectively. This analysis focuses on documented technical issues beyond routine maintenance.

---


## Engine

### 3.4L V6 (LA1)
**Intake Manifold Gasket Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Coolant leaks, overheating, sweet smell from engine bay, white smoke from exhaust.
- **Solution:** Replacement of intake manifold gaskets.
- **Cost:** $400 - $700

**Oil Cooler Line Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Oil leaks under the vehicle, low oil pressure warning light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of leaking oil cooler lines.
- **Cost:** $300 - $500


## Transmission

### 4-Speed Automatic (4T65-E)
**Torque Converter Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Slipping, shuddering, delayed engagement, transmission fluid leaks.
- **Solution:** Rebuild or replacement of the torque converter.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1500

**Transmission Fluid Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Leaking fluid from transmission pan or cooler lines, low fluid level.
- **Solution:** Tightening or replacement of seals, gaskets, or lines.
- **Cost:** $200 - $500


## Suspension

### Standard Chassis
**Strut Mount Bearing Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Clunking or popping noises when turning, especially at low speeds.
- **Solution:** Replacement of strut mount bearings.
- **Cost:** $250 - $450


## Electrical

### Body Control Module (BCM)
**BCM Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** Intermittent power windows, door locks, interior lights, or dashboard warning lights.
- **Solution:** Reprogramming or replacement of the BCM.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1000


### Alternator
**Alternator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Dimming headlights, battery warning light, dead battery.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the alternator.
- **Cost:** $400 - $600


## Exterior

### Body Panels
**Plastic Panel Fitment**
- **Symptoms:** Gaps or misalignment in exterior plastic trim pieces.
- **Solution:** Adjustment or replacement of clips and fasteners.
- **Cost:** $100 - $300


## Interior

### HVAC System
**Blend Door Actuator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Inability to control temperature or direct airflow to specific vents.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the blend door actuator.
- **Cost:** $200 - $400


--- 
### Summary Verdict
The 2004 Pontiac Aztek is known for its polarizing design and a reliability record that is average for its era. Key areas of concern often involve the 3.4L V6 engine, particularly with intake manifold gasket and oil cooler line leaks. The 4-speed automatic transmission can experience torque converter issues and fluid leaks. Electrically, the Body Control Module (BCM) and alternator are common failure points. While the exterior and interior have fewer widespread mechanical issues, minor problems like plastic panel fitment and HVAC blend door actuators can arise. Regular maintenance and prompt attention to early symptoms are recommended for owners.
