**Introduction:**

The 1990 Pontiac Firebird was a muscle car that offered thrilling performance and stylish looks. It was available with a range of engine and transmission options, including a 5.0L V8 with 225 horsepower and a 5.7L V8 with 255 horsepower. While the Firebird was praised for its power and handling, it also faced various issues that impacted its overall reliability.

**Engine**

**5.0L V8**
- **Intake Manifold Gasket Failure:** A faulty intake manifold gasket can cause coolant and oil leaks, resulting in overheating and engine damage.
  - Solution: Replace intake manifold gasket.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+
- **Valve Cover Gasket Leaks:** Worn valve cover gaskets can lead to oil leaks, causing smoke and potential engine damage.
  - Solution: Replace valve cover gaskets.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300+

**5.7L V8**
- **Head Gasket Failure:** A blown head gasket can result in coolant and oil mixing, leading to engine overheating and potential damage.
  - Solution: Replace head gasket.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $1000 - $1500+
- **Cracked Exhaust Manifolds:** Cracked exhaust manifolds can cause exhaust leaks and affect engine performance.
  - Solution: Replace exhaust manifolds.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800+

**Transmission**

**700R4 Automatic Transmission**
- **Overdrive Solenoid Failure:** A faulty overdrive solenoid can prevent the transmission from shifting into overdrive gear, affecting fuel economy.
  - Solution: Replace overdrive solenoid.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+

**Suspension**

- **Rear Leaf Spring Squeak:** The leaf springs can develop a squeak over time, especially when carrying heavy loads.
  - Solution: Lubricate or replace leaf springs.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+

**Electrical**

- **Alternator Failure:** A faulty alternator can lead to electrical problems, including battery discharge and loss of power.
  - Solution: Replace alternator.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+
- **Power Window Motors Failure:** The power window motors can fail, leading to inoperable windows.
  - Solution: Replace power window motors.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+ per window

**Exterior**

- **Plastic Bumper Fade:** The plastic bumpers can fade and discolor over time, affecting the vehicle's appearance.
  - Solution: Paint or replace bumpers.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1000+

**Interior**

- **Dash Panel Cracks:** The dash panel can develop cracks, especially in hot climates.
  - Solution: Replace dash panel.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1000+
- **Center Console Lid Breakage:** The center console lid can break or become loose, affecting its function.
  - Solution: Repair or replace center console lid.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+

**Summary:**

The 1990 Pontiac Firebird offered a combination of power and style. However, it faced several issues that ranged from minor annoyances to potentially severe mechanical problems. Some of the most common issues included intake manifold gasket failure, head gasket failure, and rear leaf spring squeak. The severity and cost of these issues could vary depending on the affected component and the extent of the damage.
