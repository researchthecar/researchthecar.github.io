**Introduction to the 1991 Pontiac Firebird:**

The 1991 Pontiac Firebird was a stylish and sporty muscle car available in hardtop, convertible, and Trans Am performance-tuned versions. It featured a potent 5.0L V8 engine producing 225 horsepower and 330 lb-ft of torque. Other engine options included a 3.1L V6, 5.7L V8, and a high-performance 350 cubic inch V8. Transmission options included a 4-speed automatic and a 5-speed manual. The Firebird was known for its sleek design, nimble handling, and spirited performance. However, like any vehicle, it had its fair share of reported issues.

**Engine**

**5.0L V8**

* **Intake Manifold Gasket Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaks, overheating, reduced engine power
    * Solution: Replace intake manifold gasket
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $250 - $400
* **Valve Cover Gasket Leak:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks, engine noise
    * Solution: Replace valve cover gasket
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200

**Transmission**

**4-Speed Automatic**

* **Slipping Gears:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, difficulty shifting, grinding noises
    * Solution: Replace transmission components or rebuild transmission
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+

**5-Speed Manual**

* **Shift Linkage Problems:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears, loose or sloppy shifter
    * Solution: Adjust or replace shift linkage
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200

**Suspension**

* **Strut Mount Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Excessive vibration, clunking noises, poor handling
    * Solution: Replace strut mounts
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400

**Electrical**

* **Alternator Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Dimming lights, electrical system failure
    * Solution: Replace alternator
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500

* **Ignition Switch Problems:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Difficulty starting the vehicle, intermittent electrical issues
    * Solution: Replace ignition switch
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $250

* **Headlight Switch Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Headlights flickering or not working
    * Solution: Replace headlight switch
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $150

**Exterior**

* **Door Handle Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Difficulty opening doors
    * Solution: Replace door handle
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $100

**Interior**

* **Power Window Motor Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Windows not working or operating slowly
    * Solution: Replace power window motor
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $300

* **Instrument Cluster Malfunction:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Gauges not working or inaccurate
    * Solution: Replace instrument cluster
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $600

**Summary:**

The 1991 Pontiac Firebird is a powerful and stylish muscle car, but it has its fair share of reported issues. The most significant problems are transmission issues in the 4-speed automatic, which can be costly to repair. Other issues, such as engine gasket failures and electrical problems, are more manageable and less expensive to fix. Overall, the Firebird is a reliable vehicle, but it's essential to be aware of these potential issues before making a purchase.
