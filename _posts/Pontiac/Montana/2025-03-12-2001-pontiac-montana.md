The 2001 Pontiac Montana is a minivan known for its versatility and spacious interior, making it a popular choice for families. The 2001 Montana came standard with a 3.4L LA1 V6 engine, producing 185 horsepower and 210 lb-ft of torque. Some owners appreciated the vehicle's comfortable ride and ample cargo space, while others found the overall build quality and reliability to be lacking. Common complaints include issues with the electrical system, particularly power windows and door locks, as well as problems with the transmission and engine.

**Engine**

**3.4L LA1 V6**

*   **Intake Manifold Gasket Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Coolant leak, overheating, white smoke from the exhaust, rough idle, and potentially coolant mixing with the oil.
    *   Solution: Replacing the intake manifold gasket.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Lower Engine Oil Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Oil drips from the bottom of the engine, visible oil on the oil pan, and a burning oil smell.
    *   Solution: Replacing the oil pan gasket.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $400+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

*No Issues Found*

**Suspension**

*   **Rear Air Suspension Problems**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The rear of the vehicle sags, the air compressor runs constantly or not at all, and a "Service Ride Control" message may appear on the dash.
    *   Solution: Replacing the air springs, air compressor, or ride height sensors.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1200+ depending on the component(s) that require replacement.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Power Window Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The power windows stop working, either intermittently or completely. Often affects the driver's side window first.
    *   Solution: Replacing the window motor, window regulator, or repairing wiring issues in the door harness.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $400+ per window.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Power Door Lock Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The power door locks stop working, either intermittently or completely. One or more doors may be affected.
    *   Solution: Replacing the door lock actuator or repairing wiring issues in the door.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+ per door.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Instrument Panel Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Gauges stop working or give inaccurate readings, the instrument panel lights flicker or go out, or the entire cluster malfunctions.
    *   Solution: Replacing the instrument panel cluster or repairing wiring connections.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Sliding Door Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Difficulty opening or closing the sliding doors, doors becoming stuck, or power sliding door malfunctions.
    *   Solution: Adjusting the door alignment, replacing the door rollers, or repairing the power sliding door mechanism.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Dashboard Cracking**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Cracks appearing on the dashboard, especially in areas exposed to sunlight.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the dashboard or using a dashboard cover.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1000+ (dashboard replacement) or $20 - $100 (dashboard cover).
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**
The 2001 Pontiac Montana faces a mix of mechanical and electrical issues. Engine oil leaks, and intake manifold leaks are common. Suspension issues related to the rear air suspension can also be expensive to repair. Electrical problems with power windows and door locks are frequently reported. Interior issues such as dashboard cracking detract from the vehicle's aesthetics. While some fixes are relatively inexpensive, others can be more costly, making it important for potential buyers to carefully inspect the vehicle and be aware of these potential problems.
