**Introduction:**
The 2001 Pontiac Montana is a popular minivan that offers a spacious interior, comfortable ride, and a variety of engine and transmission options. It is powered by a standard 3.4L V6 engine that produces 185 horsepower and 215 lb-ft of torque, paired with a 4-speed automatic transmission. Other engine and transmission options include a 3.8L V6 engine with a 5-speed automatic transmission and a 3.9L V6 engine with a 4-speed automatic transmission.

Owners appreciate the Montana's roomy interior, its smooth handling, and its ample cargo space. However, it has also been criticized for its lack of power, poor fuel economy, and unreliability.

**Engine**
* **3.4L V6**
    * **Intake manifold gasket failure:** Causes coolant and oil leaks, overheating, and engine damage.
        * Solution: Replace intake manifold gasket.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1000
    * **Head gasket failure:** Causes coolant and oil leaks, overheating, and engine damage.
        * Solution: Replace head gasket.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1000 - $2000
* **3.8L V6**
    * **Timing chain failure:** Causes engine noise, loss of power, and potential engine damage.
        * Solution: Replace timing chain and tensioners.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1500 - $2500
    * **Intake valve carbon buildup:** Causes engine misfires, rough idling, and decreased fuel economy.
        * Solution: Clean intake valves.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600
* **3.9L V6**
    * **Crankshaft position sensor failure:** Causes engine no-start or stalling.
        * Solution: Replace crankshaft position sensor.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
    * **Oil pressure sensor failure:** Causes inaccurate oil pressure readings, potential engine damage.
        * Solution: Replace oil pressure sensor.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200

**Transmission**
* **4-speed automatic transmission**
    * **Slipping or shuddering:** Difficulty engaging gears, loss of power.
        * Solution: Overhaul or replace transmission.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1500 - $3000
    * **Torque converter failure:** Causes transmission slipping, overheating, and loss of power.
        * Solution: Replace torque converter.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1000 - $1500
* **5-speed automatic transmission**
    * **Shift solenoid failure:** Causes delayed or hard shifting.
        * Solution: Replace shift solenoid.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800

**Suspension**
* **Front strut failure:** Causes excessive noise, vibration, and decreased handling.
        * Solution: Replace front struts.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800
* **Rear shock absorber failure:** Causes excessive bouncing and decreased handling.
        * Solution: Replace rear shock absorbers.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600

**Electrical**
* **Alternator failure:** Causes battery drain, no-start conditions.
        * Solution: Replace alternator.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800
* **Battery failure:** Causes no-start conditions, electrical problems.
        * Solution: Replace battery.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200
* **Starter failure:** Causes no-start conditions.
        * Solution: Replace starter.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600

**Exterior**
* **Rust:** Affects body panels, especially around wheel wells and undercarriage.
        * Solution: Rust repair or replacement of affected panels.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies greatly depending on severity

**Interior**
* **HVAC system failure:** Causes malfunctioning of heating, ventilation, and air conditioning.
        * Solution: Diagnose and repair or replace faulty components.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1000
* **Power window failure:** Causes inoperable windows.
        * Solution: Replace power window motor or switch.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500

**Summary:**

The 2001 Pontiac Montana has a history of significant issues with its engine, transmission, and electrical systems. These issues can be costly to repair, and can affect the reliability and overall value of the vehicle. Potential owners should be aware of these issues before purchasing a used Montana.
