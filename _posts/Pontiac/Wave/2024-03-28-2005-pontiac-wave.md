**Introduction**
The 2005 Pontiac Wave is a compact sedan powered by a 2.2-liter Ecotec inline-4 engine rated at 145 horsepower and 152 lb-ft of torque. The Wave is available with two transmission options: a 5-speed manual transmission or a 4-speed automatic transmission. It has a spacious interior and a comfortable ride, making it a popular choice for budget-minded consumers. However, the Wave also has some notable drawbacks, including a lack of power and outdated technology.

**Engine**
* **2.2L Ecotec I4**
  * **Intake manifold gasket failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Leaking oil, coolant, or both from the intake manifold gasket
    * Solution: Replace the intake manifold gasket
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Timing chain tensioner failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Noisy timing chain, loss of engine power, and possible engine failure
    * Solution: Replace the timing chain tensioner
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1200+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**
* **5-speed Manual Transmission**
  * **Synchronizer wear**
    * Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears, particularly into second or third gear
    * Solution: Replace the worn synchronizers
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **4-speed Automatic Transmission**
  * **Solenoid failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Erratic shifting, delayed engagement, or complete transmission failure
    * Solution: Replace the faulty solenoid
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1000+
    * Recalls: None Found
  * **Transmission fluid leaks**
    * Description/Symptoms: Leaking transmission fluid from the transmission pan, seals, or lines
    * Solution: Replace or repair the leaking component
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**
* **Front strut mount failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Noises from the front suspension, reduced handling and stability
    * Solution: Replace the strut mounts
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**
* **Alternator failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Dimming lights, difficulty starting the engine, or battery draining
    * Solution: Replace the alternator
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1000+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Starter failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Difficulty starting the engine or failure to start
    * Solution: Replace the starter motor
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**
* **Door handle failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Door handle becomes loose or breaks off
    * Solution: Replace the door handle
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**
* **Climate control failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Malfunctioning climate control system, including air conditioning, heating, or ventilation
    * Solution: Repair or replace the climate control components
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Window regulator failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Power windows become inoperable or move slowly
    * Solution: Replace the window regulator motor or other faulty components
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**
The 2005 Pontiac Wave is a reliable and affordable compact sedan that offers a comfortable ride and a spacious interior. However, it is plagued by several common issues, particularly with its engine, transmission, and electrical system. The severity of these issues can range from minor inconveniences to major repairs, with estimated costs varying greatly depending on the specific problem. These issues may deter some potential buyers and should be taken into consideration when weighing the pros and cons of owning a 2005 Pontiac Wave.
