## 2004 Pontiac Grand Am

The 2004 Pontiac Grand Am is a popular midsize sedan that is known for its sporty styling, comfortable interior, and affordable price. It is powered by a 3.4L V6 engine that produces 175 horsepower and 210 lb-ft of torque, which is paired with a 4-speed automatic transmission. Other engine options include a 2.2L inline-4 and a 3.8L V6, while transmission options include a 5-speed manual and a 4-speed automatic. The Grand Am has received generally positive reviews, with its strong points being its performance and handling, while its drawbacks include its cramped rear seat and dated interior.

### Engine
- **3.4L V6**

  - **Intake Manifold Gasket Failure**
    - Description/Symptoms: Loss of coolant, engine overheating, rough idle
    - Solution: Replace intake manifold gasket
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800+
    - Recalls: None Found

  - **Valve Cover Gasket Leak**
    - Description/Symptoms: Oil leak from valve cover area
    - Solution: Replace valve cover gasket
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
    - Recalls: None Found

### Transmission
- **4-speed Automatic**

  - **Transmission Solenoid Issues**
    - Description/Symptoms: Hard shifting, slipping gears, no engagement
    - Solution: Replace transmission solenoid
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+
    - Recalls: None Found

### Suspension

  - **No Issues Found**

### Electrical

  - **Starter Failure**
    - Description/Symptoms: Engine does not crank
    - Solution: Replace starter
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
    - Recalls: None Found

### Exterior

  - **Taillight Assembly Failure**
    - Description/Symptoms: Taillights do not work
    - Solution: Replace taillight assembly
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
    - Recalls: None Found

### Interior

  - **Power Window Regulator Failure**
    - Description/Symptoms: Window does not go up or down
    - Solution: Replace power window regulator
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+
    - Recalls: None Found

## Summary

The 2004 Pontiac Grand Am has a few common issues to watch out for, including intake manifold gasket failure, valve cover gasket leak, transmission solenoid issues, starter failure, taillight assembly failure, and power window regulator failure. While these issues can be annoying and costly to fix, they are not particularly severe and are typically covered under warranty. Overall, the Grand Am is a reliable and affordable vehicle that offers good performance and handling for its price.
