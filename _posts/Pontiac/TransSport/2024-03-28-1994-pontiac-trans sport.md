**Introduction**

The 1994 Pontiac Trans Sport is a versatile minivan that offers a spacious interior, comfortable ride, and a wide range of features. It is powered by a standard 3.8L V6 engine paired with a 4-speed automatic transmission. However, several issues have been reported with the vehicle, affecting its reliability and performance.

**Engine**

**3.8L V6**

* **Intake Manifold Gasket Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Leaking coolant, overheating, engine misfires
    * Solution: Replace intake manifold gasket
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500
    * Recalls: 03V287000
* **Valve Cover Gasket Leaks:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks from valve covers, visible oil residue
    * Solution: Replace valve cover gaskets
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300
* **Spark Plug Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Engine misfires, rough idle, loss of power
    * Solution: Replace spark plugs
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $200

**Transmission**

**4-Speed Automatic**

* **Transmission Slipping:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Transmission slips gears, loss of power, delayed shifts
    * Solution: Replace or rebuild transmission
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $3,000
* **Torque Converter Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Transmission shudder, loss of power, overheated transmission fluid
    * Solution: Replace torque converter
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $600 - $1,200

**Suspension**

* **Front Suspension Struts:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Loose handling, excessive body roll, uneven tire wear
    * Solution: Replace front struts
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800
* **Rear Suspension Shocks:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Rough ride, bouncy handling, worn-out shocks
    * Solution: Replace rear shocks
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Battery dies prematurely, electrical components not functioning
    * Solution: Check for parasitic draw, repair/replace faulty components
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500
* **Alternator Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: No charging of battery, dashboard warning lights, dim headlights
    * Solution: Replace alternator
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600
* **Starter Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Engine won't crank, clicking sound from starter
    * Solution: Replace starter
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400

**Exterior**

* **Rust:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Rust formation on body panels, especially around wheel wells
    * Solution: Sand and repaint affected areas
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500+
* **Plastic Trim Fading:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Faded or discolored plastic trim
    * Solution: Replace trim or use touch-up paint
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $200+

**Interior**

* **Dashboard Cracks:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Cracks on dashboard material, especially in sun-exposed areas
    * Solution: Repair cracks with epoxy or vinyl repair kit
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $200
* **Power Window Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Power windows do not operate, switches not functioning
    * Solution: Replace window motor or switch
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400

**Summary**

The 1994 Pontiac Trans Sport has several common issues that affect its reliability and performance. The most severe and costly issues include transmission problems, intake manifold gasket failure, and suspension issues. While the vehicle offers a comfortable and spacious interior, the numerous issues can make it a less desirable choice for those seeking a reliable and hassle-free vehicle.
