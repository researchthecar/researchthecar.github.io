**Introduction:**
The 2009 Pontiac G5, a compact sedan, was offered in two engine options: a 2.2L inline-four rated at 148 horsepower and 152 lb-ft of torque, and a 2.4L inline-four rated at 169 horsepower and 160 lb-ft of torque. Both engines could be paired with either a five-speed manual transmission or a four-speed automatic transmission. The G5 received generally favorable reviews, praised for its handling and responsive steering, but criticized for its cramped interior and lack of features.

**Engine**
- **2.2L Inline-Four**
    **Cylinder Head Gasket Failure**
    Description/Symptoms: Premature failure of the cylinder head gasket, leading to coolant and oil leaks, overheating, and engine misfiring.
    Solution: Replacing the cylinder head gasket and resurfacing the cylinder head.
    Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $1,500+
    Recalls: None Found
- **2.4L Inline-Four**
    **Piston Ring Failure**
    Description/Symptoms: Faulty piston rings can cause excessive oil consumption, leading to blue or black smoke from the exhaust, rough idle, and reduced engine performance.
    Solution: Replacing the piston rings and honing the cylinder walls.
    Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $2,500+
    Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**
- **Five-Speed Manual**
    **Synchronizer Failure**
    Description/Symptoms: Worn synchronizers make gear shifting difficult, causing grinding noises and difficulty engaging gears.
    Solution: Replacing the synchronizers and rebuilding the transmission.
    Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,200 - $1,700+
    Recalls: None Found
- **Four-Speed Automatic**
    **Torque Converter Clutch Failure**
    Description/Symptoms: The torque converter clutch may slip or shudder during gear changes, leading to rough shifting, loss of power, and increased fuel consumption.
    Solution: Replacing the torque converter clutch and rebuilding the transmission.
    Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,000+
    Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**
- **Front Control Arm Bushing Failure**
    Description/Symptoms: Premature failure of the front control arm bushings can cause a rattling noise, excessive vibration, and uneven tire wear.
    Solution: Replacing the worn bushings.
    Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+
    Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**
- **Battery Drain**
    Description/Symptoms: A parasitic drain on the battery can lead to premature battery failure and difficulty starting the vehicle.
    Solution: Identifying the source of the drain and repairing the faulty component.
    Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the source of the drain
    Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**
- **Sunroof Leakage**
    Description/Symptoms: Faulty seals around the sunroof can cause water leaks into the cabin, leading to water damage and musty odors.
    Solution: Replacing the sunroof seals.
    Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    Recalls: None Found

**Interior**
- **Power Window Failure**
    Description/Symptoms: Power window motors or switches can fail, causing the windows to become inoperable.
    Solution: Replacing the faulty motors or switches.
    Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
    Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**
Overall, the 2009 Pontiac G5 has some potential reliability issues, particularly with its engine and transmission. The cost of repairs can be significant, so it's important to factor in maintenance costs when considering this vehicle. However, the G5 remains a popular choice for those seeking a sporty and fuel-efficient sedan.
