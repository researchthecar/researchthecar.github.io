**Introduction:**

The 2002 Pontiac Bonneville is a mid-size sedan known for its spacious interior, comfortable ride, and powerful V6 engine. It came with a standard 3.8L V6 engine that produced 205 horsepower and 230 lb-ft of torque. Optional engine options included a 3.4L V6 with 185 horsepower and 215 lb-ft of torque, and a 4.6L V8 with 225 horsepower and 260 lb-ft of torque. Transmission options included a 4-speed automatic for the 3.4L V6, a 5-speed manual or 4-speed automatic for the 3.8L V6, and a 4-speed automatic for the 4.6L V8.

**Engine**

* **3.8L V6**
    * **Intake Manifold Gasket Leak:**
        * Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaks around the intake manifold, leading to overheating and possible engine damage.
        * Solution: Replace the intake manifold gasket.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1000+
        * Recalls: None Found
    * **Valve Cover Gasket Leak:**
        * Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks from the valve cover, causing a burning oil smell and potential engine damage.
        * Solution: Replace the valve cover gasket.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
        * Recalls: None Found

* **3.4L V6**
    * **No Issues Found**

* **4.6L V8**
    * **Timing Chain Tensioner Failure:**
        * Description/Symptoms: The timing chain tensioner fails, causing the timing chain to skip or break, leading to catastrophic engine damage.
        * Solution: Replace the timing chain and tensioner.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1500 - $2500+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

* **4-Speed Automatic**
    * **Slipping/Hard Shifting:**
        * Description/Symptoms: The transmission slips or shifts harshly, making it difficult to drive.
        * Solution: Replace the transmission solenoid or other internal components.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1500+
        * Recalls: None Found
* **5-Speed Manual**
    * **Grinding Gears:**
        * Description/Symptoms: The transmission grinds when shifting gears, especially when cold.
        * Solution: Replace the clutch or other internal components.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1000+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Front Control Arm Bushings Worn:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The control arm bushings wear out, causing clunking and vibration during driving.
    * Solution: Replace the control arm bushings.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Rear Suspension Springs Sagging:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The rear suspension springs sag, causing the car to sit low and handle poorly.
    * Solution: Replace the rear suspension springs.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Battery Draining:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The battery drains quickly, requiring frequent jumpstarts.
    * Solution: Check for parasitic draw and/or replace the battery.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $300+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Headlight Switch Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The headlight switch fails, causing the headlights to malfunction.
    * Solution: Replace the headlight switch.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $250+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Door Lock Actuator Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The door lock actuator fails, making it difficult to open or close the doors.
    * Solution: Replace the door lock actuator.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Trunk Lid Rust:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The trunk lid develops rust around the edges, leading to corrosion and potential failure.
    * Solution: Repair or replace the trunk lid.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Power Window Regulator Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The power window regulator fails, causing the power windows to malfunction.
    * Solution: Replace the power window regulator.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Dashboard Rattle:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The dashboard rattles while driving, especially on rough roads.
    * Solution: Locate and tighten or replace the loose components causing the rattle.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $200+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

The 2002 Pontiac Bonneville has a number of potential issues, ranging from minor electrical glitches to serious engine and transmission failures. The intake manifold gasket leak and timing chain tensioner failure are two of the most severe problems, requiring costly repairs. Overall, the cost of repairs for the Bonneville can vary widely depending on the issue and the severity of the damage.
