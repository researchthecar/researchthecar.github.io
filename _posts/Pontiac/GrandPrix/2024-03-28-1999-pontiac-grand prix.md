**Introduction:** The 1999 Pontiac Grand Prix is a mid-size coupe or sedan with a 3.8L V6 engine and a 4-speed automatic transmission as standard. The GT model features a more powerful 3.8L supercharged V6 engine and other performance enhancements. Owners praise the Grand Prix for its comfortable ride, spacious interior, and strong performance, but some have experienced issues with various components.

## **Engine**
- **3.8L V6**
 -- **Intake manifold gasket failure**
     - **Description/Symptoms:** Leaking coolant or oil, poor engine performance
     - **Solution:** Replace intake manifold gasket
     - **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $200 - $400+
     - **Recalls:** None Found
 -- **Valve cover gasket leaks**
     - **Description/Symptoms:** Leaking oil, visible oil stains on valve covers
     - **Solution:** Replace valve cover gaskets
     - **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $100 - $200+
     - **Recalls:** None Found
- **3.8L Supercharged V6 (GT)**
 -- **Supercharger oil leaks**
     - **Description/Symptoms:** Leaking oil from the supercharger, loss of power or acceleration
     - **Solution:** Replace supercharger oil seals or gasket
     - **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $500 - $800+
     - **Recalls:** None Found

## **Transmission**
- **4-Speed Automatic**
 -- **Transmission slipping or shuddering**
    - **Description/Symptoms:** Difficulty shifting gears, slipping or shuddering during acceleration
    - **Solution:** Replace or rebuild transmission
    - **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $1,000 - $3,000+
    - **Recalls:** None Found
 -- **Transmission fluid leaks**
    - **Description/Symptoms:** Leaking transmission fluid, low fluid levels, shifting problems
    - **Solution:** Replace transmission fluid pan gasket or seals
    - **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $100 - $300+
    - **Recalls:** None Found

## **Suspension**
- **No Issues Found**

## **Electrical**
- **Faulty ignition coils**
    - **Description/Symptoms:** Engine misfires, difficulty starting, decreased performance
    - **Solution:** Replace ignition coils
    - **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $100 - $200+ per coil
    - **Recalls:** None Found
- **Alternator failure**
    - **Description/Symptoms:** Dimming headlights, loss of electrical power, dead battery
    - **Solution:** Replace alternator
    - **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $500 - $800+
    - **Recalls:** None Found

## **Exterior**
- **Door lock actuator failure**
    - **Description/Symptoms:** Doors won't lock or unlock, intermittent door locking problems
    - **Solution:** Replace door lock actuators
    - **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $200 - $400+ per door
    - **Recalls:** None Found

## **Interior**
- **Heater core leaks**
    - **Description/Symptoms:** Leaking coolant into the passenger compartment, foggy windows, coolant smell
    - **Solution:** Replace heater core
    - **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $400 - $600+
    - **Recalls:** None Found
- **Dashboard lights failure**
    - **Description/Symptoms:** Dim or burnt-out dashboard lights, difficulty reading gauges at night
    - **Solution:** Replace dashboard bulbs or wiring
    - **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $50 - $150+
    - **Recalls:** None Found

**Summary:** The 1999 Pontiac Grand Prix has some potential issues, particularly with the engine, transmission, and electrical system. The severity and cost of these issues can vary, with some being relatively minor and others requiring significant repairs. However, the Grand Prix also offers a comfortable ride, spacious interior, and strong performance, making it a popular choice for many drivers despite its potential drawbacks.
