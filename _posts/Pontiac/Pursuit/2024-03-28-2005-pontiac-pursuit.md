**Introduction:**

The 2005 Pontiac Pursuit is a compact sedan that offers a combination of style and practicality. It comes standard with a 2.2L Ecotec I4 engine producing 144 horsepower and 155 lb-ft of torque, paired with a 4-speed automatic transmission. The Pursuit is praised for its comfortable ride, spacious interior, and reliable performance, but it does have some notable issues.

**Engine**

**2.2L Ecotec I4**

* **Intake manifold gasket failure:** Causes coolant and/or oil leaks, overheating, and rough idling.
    * Solution: Replace the intake manifold gasket.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600
    * Recalls: N/A
* **Water pump failure:** Premature failure can lead to coolant leaks, overheating, and engine damage.
    * Solution: Replace the water pump.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
    * Recalls: N/A
* **Timing chain tensioner failure:** Can cause a rattling noise from the engine, reduced power, and eventually timing belt failure.
    * Solution: Replace the timing chain tensioner.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: N/A

**Transmission**

**4-Speed Automatic**

* **Transmission slipping:** May experience hesitation, delayed shifting, or slipping gears.
    * Solution: Repair or replace the transmission.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
    * Recalls: N/A

**Suspension**

* **Strut mounts failure:** Can cause noise, vibration, and reduced handling.
    * Solution: Replace the strut mounts.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
    * Recalls: N/A
* **Lower control arm bushings failure:** Premature failure can lead to alignment issues, excessive tire wear, and poor handling.
    * Solution: Replace the lower control arm bushings.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600
    * Recalls: N/A

**Electrical**

* **Battery drain:** May be caused by a faulty alternator, starter, or electrical components.
    * Solution: Diagnose and repair the source of the drain.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600+
    * Recalls: N/A
* **Ignition switch failure:** Can cause the engine to stall or not start.
    * Solution: Replace the ignition switch.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
    * Recalls: N/A

**Exterior**

* **Power window failure:** May experience windows that are slow, stuck, or inoperable.
    * Solution: Diagnose and repair the faulty window motor or switch.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
    * Recalls: N/A

**Interior**

* **Dashboard rattle:** May experience a rattling noise coming from the dashboard.
    * Solution: Diagnose and secure any loose components in the dashboard.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300
    * Recalls: N/A
* **Heater core failure:** Can cause a loss of heat or a musty odor in the cabin.
    * Solution: Replace the heater core.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800+
    * Recalls: N/A

**Summary:**

The 2005 Pontiac Pursuit is generally a reliable and practical vehicle, but it does have some known issues that can range in severity and cost. Engine issues, such as intake manifold gasket failure and water pump failure, are relatively common, while transmission issues can be more costly to repair. Suspension and electrical issues are also present, but the extent of the impact depends on the severity of the failure. Interior and exterior issues are generally less significant and less expensive to address. Overall, the Pursuit is a decent option for those seeking a compact sedan, but it's important to be aware of its potential issues and factor them into the purchase decision.
