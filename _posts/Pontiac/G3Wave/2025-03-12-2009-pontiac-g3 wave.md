The 2009 Pontiac G3 Wave, also sold as the Aveo in other markets, is a subcompact car known for its fuel efficiency and affordability. The engine options for the 2009 model year included a 1.6-liter inline-4 engine producing around 103 horsepower and 107 lb-ft of torque. It was available with either a 5-speed manual or a 4-speed automatic transmission. Owners generally appreciate the G3 Wave for its low price point and decent gas mileage, however, they often criticize the vehicle for its lackluster performance, basic interior, and potential reliability issues.

**Engine**

**1.6L Inline-4 Engine**

*   **Check Engine Light and Misfires**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The check engine light illuminates, often accompanied by engine misfires, rough idling, and reduced power. This can be caused by faulty ignition coils or spark plugs.
    *   Solution: Replace the faulty ignition coil(s) and/or spark plugs.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Evaporative Emission Control System (EVAP) Problems**
    * Description/Symptoms: Check engine light is on, and the code points to an EVAP system leak. Common culprits include a faulty gas cap, purge valve, or vent valve.
    * Solution: Replace the faulty gas cap, purge valve, or vent valve as needed.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $200+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**4-Speed Automatic Transmission**

*   **Rough Shifting/Delayed Engagement**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The transmission may exhibit harsh or delayed shifting between gears, particularly when cold. It might also hesitate before engaging into drive or reverse.
    *   Solution: A transmission fluid flush *might* help in mild cases. More severe issues may require valve body repair or replacement, or even a full transmission rebuild or replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 (fluid flush) - $2000+ (rebuild/replacement)
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Transmission Slipping**
    * Description/Symptoms: The engine revs up without a corresponding increase in vehicle speed. This often occurs during acceleration or when climbing hills.
    * Solution: Typically requires a transmission rebuild or replacement.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1500 - $3000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Worn Suspension Components**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Clunking or rattling noises from the suspension, especially when driving over bumps. Uneven tire wear and poor handling can also result. Common culprits include worn struts/shocks, ball joints, tie rod ends, and sway bar links.
    *   Solution: Replace the worn suspension components.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1000+ (depending on the components needing replacement)
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Power Window Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Intermittent or complete failure of power windows. This can be caused by a faulty window motor, regulator, or switch.
    *   Solution: Replace the faulty window motor, regulator, or switch.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+
    * Recalls: None Found
*   **Door Lock Actuator Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The doors won't lock/unlock with the key fob or the interior switch.
    *   Solution: Replace the faulty door lock actuator
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $250+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Rust**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rust forming on the rocker panels, wheel wells, and undercarriage.
    *   Solution: Repair or replace rusted panels. Treat remaining rust.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Broken Door Handles**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Interior door handles break easily.
    *   Solution: Replace the broken door handle.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $150+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2009 Pontiac G3 Wave, while economical, is prone to various issues affecting the engine, transmission, suspension, electrical, and interior components. Engine issues primarily revolve around ignition and EVAP system problems, while the automatic transmission can suffer from rough shifting and slipping. Suspension wear is common, and electrical problems can manifest in power window and door lock failures. Rust can be a significant concern in regions with harsh climates. The severity and cost of these repairs vary, but potential buyers should be aware of these common issues and factor them into their purchase considerations. The engine and transmission issues can potentially be quite expensive to remedy, so pre-purchase inspections are highly recommended.
