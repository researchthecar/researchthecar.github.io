**Introduction:**

The 1995 Pontiac Sunrunner is a compact SUV that was available with a choice of two engine options: a 2.0L 4-cylinder engine producing 115 horsepower and 130 lb-ft of torque, and a 3.0L V6 engine producing 155 horsepower and 180 lb-ft of torque. Both engines were mated to a 5-speed manual transmission or a 4-speed automatic transmission. The Sunrunner was known for its affordability, spacious interior, and off-road capabilities, but it was also plagued by several common issues.

**Engine**

**2.0L 4-Cylinder**

* **Intake manifold gasket failure:** Causes coolant and/or oil leaks, overheating, and reduced engine performance.
    * Solution: Replace intake manifold gasket.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500
* **Head gasket failure:** Causes coolant and/or oil leaks, overheating, and white smoke from the exhaust.
    * Solution: Replace head gasket.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,200
* **EGR valve failure:** Causes rough idling, increased emissions, and poor fuel economy.
    * Solution: Replace EGR valve.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300

**3.0L V6**

* **Valve cover gasket leak:** Causes oil leaks and a burning oil smell.
    * Solution: Replace valve cover gasket.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200
* **Power steering pump failure:** Causes loss of power steering and a whining noise.
    * Solution: Replace power steering pump.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500
* **Transmission cooler line leak:** Causes transmission fluid leaks and overheating.
    * Solution: Replace transmission cooler line.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $250

**Transmission**

**5-Speed Manual**

* **Clutch failure:** Causes difficulty shifting gears and slipping.
    * Solution: Replace clutch.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $600 - $1,000
* **Synchro failure:** Causes grinding noises when shifting gears.
    * Solution: Replace synchro.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800

**4-Speed Automatic**

* **Transmission slipping:** Causes a loss of power and difficulty accelerating.
    * Solution: Rebuild or replace transmission.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,000
* **Torque converter failure:** Causes shuddering and a loss of power.
    * Solution: Replace torque converter.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000

**Suspension**

* **Front strut failure:** Causes clunking noises, excessive vibration, and reduced handling.
    * Solution: Replace front struts.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800
* **Rear shock absorber failure:** Causes a rough ride and reduced handling.
    * Solution: Replace rear shock absorbers.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400

**Electrical**

* **Alternator failure:** Causes battery drain and low electrical power.
    * Solution: Replace alternator.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500
* **Starter failure:** Causes difficulty starting the engine or a no-start condition.
    * Solution: Replace starter.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $250 - $400
* **Ignition switch failure:** Causes difficulty starting the engine or a no-start condition.
    * Solution: Replace ignition switch.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300

**Exterior**

* **Rust:** Affects various areas of the body, including the wheel arches, quarter panels, and roof.
    * Solution: Rust repair or replacement of affected panels.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the extent of rust damage
* **Sunroof leaks:** Causes water to leak into the interior.
    * Solution: Replace sunroof seal or adjust sunroof alignment.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
* **Tailgate rust:** Affects the bottom edge of the tailgate.
    * Solution: Rust repair or tailgate replacement.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the extent of rust damage

**Interior**

* **HVAC blower motor failure:** Causes lack of heat or air conditioning.
    * Solution: Replace blower motor.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
* **Dashboard cracking:** Affects the upper dashboard.
    * Solution: Replace dashboard or repair cracks.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600

**Summary:**

The 1995 Pontiac Sunrunner was a relatively affordable and versatile SUV, but it was plagued by various issues that could be costly to repair. Engine problems, transmission failures, and suspension issues were particularly common. The severity of the issues and the associated repair costs could vary depending on the specific part that failed and the extent of the damage. Before purchasing a used Sunrunner, it is important to have the vehicle thoroughly inspected by a qualified mechanic to assess potential issues and estimate the cost of any necessary repairs.
