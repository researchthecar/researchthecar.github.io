**Introduction**

The 1994 Pontiac Sunrunner was a compact SUV that was offered with a choice of two engines and two transmissions. The standard engine was a 2.3L inline-4 that produced 150 horsepower and 160 lb-ft of torque. The optional engine was a 3.4L V6 that produced 185 horsepower and 215 lb-ft of torque. Both engines were available with either a 5-speed manual transmission or a 4-speed automatic transmission.

People who liked the Sunrunner praised its affordability, versatility, and ruggedness. However, some people complained about its lack of power and poor fuel economy.

**Engine**

**2.3L Inline-4**

* **Engine Valve Cover Gasket Leak:** Oil leaks from the valve cover gasket, causing a burning oil smell and reduced oil levels. The solution is to replace the valve cover gasket. The estimated cost to fix this issue is $100-$200.
* **Intake Manifold Gasket Leak:** Coolant leaks from the intake manifold gasket, causing overheating and reduced engine performance. The solution is to replace the intake manifold gasket. The estimated cost to fix this issue is $200-$300.

**3.4L V6**

* **Head Gasket Failure:** Coolant and oil mix, leading to overheating, white smoke from the exhaust, and reduced engine performance. The solution is to replace the head gasket. The estimated cost to fix this issue is $1000-$1500.
* **Timing Chain Tensioner Failure:** The timing chain tensioner fails, causing the timing chain to skip and the engine to lose power. The solution is to replace the timing chain tensioner. The estimated cost to fix this issue is $500-$700.

**Transmission**

**5-Speed Manual Transmission**

* **Clutch Failure:** The clutch wears out prematurely, causing difficulty shifting gears and slipping. The solution is to replace the clutch. The estimated cost to fix this issue is $500-$700.
* **Transmission Input Shaft Bearing Failure:** The input shaft bearing fails, causing a whining noise and difficulty shifting gears. The solution is to replace the input shaft bearing. The estimated cost to fix this issue is $300-$500.

**4-Speed Automatic Transmission**

* **Transmission Solenoid Failure:** The transmission solenoid fails, causing erratic shifting and transmission slipping. The solution is to replace the transmission solenoid. The estimated cost to fix this issue is $200-$300.
* **Torque Converter Failure:** The torque converter fails, causing overheating, shuddering, and transmission slipping. The solution is to replace the torque converter. The estimated cost to fix this issue is $1000-$1500.

**Suspension**

* **Front Suspension Struts:** The front suspension struts wear out prematurely, causing a rough ride and poor handling. The solution is to replace the front suspension struts. The estimated cost to fix this issue is $400-$600.
* **Rear Suspension Shocks:** The rear suspension shocks wear out prematurely, causing a rough ride and poor handling. The solution is to replace the rear suspension shocks. The estimated cost to fix this issue is $300-$500.

**Electrical**

* **Alternator Failure:** The alternator fails, causing electrical problems and a dead battery. The solution is to replace the alternator. The estimated cost to fix this issue is $300-$500.
* **Starter Failure:** The starter fails, preventing the engine from starting. The solution is to replace the starter. The estimated cost to fix this issue is $200-$400.

**Exterior**

* **Rust:** The body panels are prone to rust, especially in areas like the wheel wells and rocker panels. The solution is to prevent or repair the rust. The estimated cost to fix this issue varies widely depending on the severity of the rust.

**Interior**

* **Dashboard Cracking or Warping:** The dashboard can crack or warp due to sun exposure. The solution is to replace the dashboard. The estimated cost to fix this issue is $500-$700.
* **Climate Control Malfunction:** The climate control system may malfunction, causing problems with heating and cooling. The solution is to diagnose and repair the climate control system. The estimated cost to fix this issue varies depending on the specific problem.

**Summary**

The 1994 Pontiac Sunrunner is a generally reliable vehicle. However, it is important to be aware of the potential issues that can occur, especially with the engine and transmission. The cost of repairs can vary significantly depending on the severity of the issue.
