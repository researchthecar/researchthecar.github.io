**Introduction**

The 1990 Pontiac Firefly was a subcompact hatchback that was produced and sold in Canada from 1985 to 1991. It was powered by a single 1.0L engine, mated to a 5-speed manual transmission. The Firefly was not well-received by critics, who cited its weak engine, poor handling, and cramped interior.

**Engine**

* **1.0L**

**Issues:**

* **Accelerated Oil Consumption**
Description/Symptoms: The Firefly's 1.0L engine burns excessive oil, leading to low oil levels and potential engine damage. Symptoms include a drop in oil level between oil changes, blue smoke from the exhaust, and engine knocking.
Solution: The engine may need to be rebuilt or replaced.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+
Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

* **5-speed Manual**

**Issues:**

* **Difficult Gear Shifting**
Description/Symptoms: The transmission struggles to engage gears, particularly when shifting from first to second gear. Symptoms include not being able to shift into gear, grinding noises when shifting, and popping out of gear.
Solution: The transmission may need to be rebuilt or replaced.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Front Struts**
Description/Symptoms: The front struts may fail prematurely, leading to a rough ride and poor handling. Symptoms include excessive bouncing, difficulty in controlling the vehicle, and uneven tire wear.
Solution: Replacement of the front struts.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $600+
Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Alternator Failure**
Description/Symptoms: The alternator may fail prematurely, causing the battery to drain and the vehicle to lose power. Symptoms include flickering lights, difficulty starting the engine, and the battery warning light illuminating.
Solution: Replacement of the alternator.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+
Recalls: None Found

* **Ignition Coil Failure**
Description/Symptoms: The ignition coil may fail, causing the engine to misfire and run poorly. Symptoms include a rough idle, loss of power, and difficulty starting the engine.
Solution: Replacement of the ignition coil.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Rust**
Description/Symptoms: The Firefly is prone to rust, particularly on the body panels and undercarriage. Symptoms include visible rust patches, bubbling paint, and holes in the metal.
Solution: Repair of rust damage or replacement of affected panels.
Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the extent of damage
Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Dashboard Cracking**
Description/Symptoms: The dashboard may develop cracks over time, particularly in areas exposed to sunlight. Symptoms include visible cracks and a wrinkled appearance.
Solution: Replacement of the dashboard.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $600+
Recalls: None Found

**Overall Summary**

The 1990 Pontiac Firefly is a vehicle with numerous issues affecting its engine, transmission, suspension, electrical system, and other components. These issues can result in expensive repairs and decreased reliability. The severity and cost of the repairs vary depending on the specific issue. As such, it is not a recommended vehicle for purchase.
