# 2017 Smart Forfour

### Overview
The 2017 Smart Forfour, a unique city car, offers a compact and agile driving experience. Designed for urban environments, it prioritizes maneuverability and efficient use of space. Reliability data for the 2017 model year indicates a generally solid performance, particularly in its core mechanical components. However, like many vehicles in its class, some owners have reported specific issues, primarily related to the electrical system and certain transmission aspects, especially in higher mileage examples. The focus on its niche market means extensive long-term reliability data can be less common than for mainstream models, but the available information suggests a vehicle that, with proper maintenance, can serve its purpose effectively.

## Electrical

### General Electrical System
**Infotainment System Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** The central infotainment screen may freeze, become unresponsive, or experience random reboots. Bluetooth connectivity issues have also been noted.
- **Solution:** Software updates for the infotainment system, or potential replacement of the unit if persistent.
- **Cost:** $300 - $800

**Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Premature battery drain or failure, potentially linked to parasitic draw from various electronic modules.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis to identify the source of the drain, followed by repair or replacement of faulty modules or wiring.
- **Cost:** $200 - $600

### Summary Verdict
The 2017 Smart Forfour demonstrates reasonable reliability for its segment, with its engines and suspension generally performing well. The primary areas of concern are the 6-speed dual-clutch automatic transmission, which can exhibit shifting issues, and the electrical system, with infotainment glitches and battery drain being the most common complaints. These issues are not universally experienced but are noted by a segment of owners. For potential buyers, a thorough inspection of the transmission's behavior and the electrical system's functionality is recommended. Overall, the Forfour is a competent city car, but awareness of these specific potential issues can help in making an informed decision.
