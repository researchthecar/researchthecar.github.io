**Introduction:**

The 2005 Smart Forfour is a four-door subcompact hatchback known for its small size, maneuverability, and fuel efficiency. It was offered with a choice of two engines: a 1.1-liter inline-three and a 1.5-liter inline-four. Transmission options included a five-speed manual and a six-speed semi-automatic transmission.

Despite its compact size, the Smart Forfour offers a surprisingly spacious interior and a host of standard features. However, it has also been known to experience reliability issues, which we will discuss in detail below.

**Engine**

**1.1-liter Inline-Three**

* **Intake Manifold Gasket Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Engine coolant leaks, overheating, and engine misfires.
    * Solution: Replace the intake manifold gasket.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $600
* **Timing Chain Tensioner Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Rattling noise from the engine, especially during startup.
    * Solution: Replace the timing chain tensioner.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $700+

**1.5-liter Inline-Four**

* **No Issues Found**

**Transmission**

**Five-Speed Manual**

* **Shift Linkage Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears, grinding noises, and popping out of gear.
    * Solution: Replace the shift linkage components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500

**Six-Speed Semi-Automatic**

* **Actuator Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Transmission slips or hesitates during gear changes.
    * Solution: Replace the faulty actuator.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $1,500+

**Suspension**

* **Rear Axle Beam Cracking:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Cracking or popping noises from the rear suspension.
    * Solution: Reinforce or replace the rear axle beam.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
* **Rear Shock Absorber Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Bouncing or sagging rear end, poor handling.
    * Solution: Replace the rear shock absorbers.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $600

**Electrical**

* **Battery Discharge:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Battery dies prematurely, electrical system malfunctions.
    * Solution: Replace the defective battery or alternator.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
* **Starter Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Difficulty starting the engine, grinding noises during startup.
    * Solution: Replace the starter motor.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $600

**Exterior**

* **No Issues Found**

**Interior**

* **Climate Control Panel Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Climate control system malfunctions, buttons stop working.
    * Solution: Replace the climate control panel.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500
* **Power Window Regulator Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Difficulty rolling up or down the windows, rattling noises.
    * Solution: Replace the power window regulator.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400

**Summary:**

While the 2005 Smart Forfour offers compact size and fuel efficiency, it is not without its drawbacks. The vehicle has been plagued by a number of reliability issues, including engine, transmission, suspension, and electrical problems. Some of these issues, such as the rear axle beam cracking, are particularly severe and can result in costly repairs. It is important for potential buyers to be aware of these issues before purchasing a used Smart Forfour.
