# 2017 Smart Fortwo Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When evaluating a used 2017 Smart Fortwo, buyers will encounter a compact city car that relies on lightweight packaging and nimble maneuverability. Available in both gasoline and fully electric powertrains, it offers unique advantages for tight urban parking alongside distinct mechanical quirks. Prospective owners should review owner-reported concerns carefully during a pre-purchase inspection to ensure long-term reliability.

---

## Engine

### 0.9L Turbocharged 3-Cylinder (Gasoline)

This 0.9-liter turbocharged three-cylinder gasoline engine produces approximately 89 horsepower and 100 lb-ft of torque. It provides adequate pep for city driving while maintaining modest fuel efficiency, though routine maintenance is essential for longevity.

### Electric Drive Motor (EV)

The electric drive variant pairs an electric motor with a high-voltage battery system, delivering instantaneous torque tailored for urban commutes. Buyers should verify battery health and charging capabilities, as electrical control units can occasionally fail.

**Power Electronics and Control Unit Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may become completely inoperable due to failures in the Power Electronics Control Unit or DC-to-DC converter, lacking any backup propulsion system.
- **Solution:** Requires professional diagnosis and replacement of the failed control unit or DC-to-DC converter components.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Dual-Clutch Automatic Transmission

This six-speed dual-clutch automatic transmission delivers prompt gear changes suited to the Fortwo's city-car mission. While generally functional, intermittent selector glitches can occasionally disrupt engagement.

**Intermittent Reverse Shifting Failure**
- **Symptoms:** When shifting into reverse, the gear indicator may show blank on the information center accompanied by a high-pitched beeping sound.
- **Solution:** Inspect the transmission linkage, software calibration, and related selector sensors for faults.

### 5-Speed Manual Transmission

The traditional five-speed manual transmission gives drivers direct control over the engine's power band. It offers a simpler mechanical layout compared to the automatic options, though clutch wear depends heavily on prior driving habits.

## Steering

### Steering Rack and Front Suspension

Provides direct and lightweight steering feedback for easy parking, though mechanical components can occasionally produce noises under specific environmental conditions.

**Grinding Noises During Turns**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may hear a loud grinding or rubbing sound originating from the driver-side front wheel area or steering rack during low-speed turns, particularly in hot and humid weather.
- **Solution:** Inspect the steering rack, suspension joints, and front wheel assemblies for friction or wear.

## Electrical

### High-Voltage Charging System

Monitors and manages the vehicle's electrical storage and charging functions, though select owners report cold-weather range drops and charging restrictions.

**Charging and Battery Range Limitations**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may fail to charge past low percentages due to a suspected faulty high-voltage control box, or experience reduced mileage ranges in cold weather.
- **Solution:** Have a certified technician inspect the high-voltage control module (such as the N83 box) and battery management sensors.

## Safety

### Seat Belts

Occupant restraint systems are critical for safety, making it important to verify that all seat belt retractors and latches operate smoothly.

**Failure to Retract or Secure**
- **Symptoms:** Seat belts may fail to retract smoothly to their home position when not in use, or fail to engage securely and hold occupants back during sudden braking maneuvers.
- **Solution:** Inspect seat belt retractors, buckles, and latch mechanisms, replacing faulty assemblies as necessary.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V447000: Steering](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Smart&model=Fortwo&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** Failure of the left front axle steering knuckle could affect vehicle control, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** MBUSA will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the left front axle steering knuckle, replacing any from the affected batch, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2017 Smart Fortwo presents a distinct set of pros and cons for used car shoppers, excelling in tight urban spaces while demanding careful attention to electrical and mechanical components. Prospective buyers should perform a comprehensive inspection—paying close attention to charging functionality in EV models, steering noises, and seat belt operation—to secure a reliable vehicle.
