# 2017 Smart Fortwo

### Overview
The 2017 Smart Fortwo is a subcompact city car known for its maneuverability and compact size, making it ideal for urban environments. Reliability for this model year is generally considered good, with owners reporting fewer significant mechanical problems compared to some competitors. However, like any vehicle, there are specific areas that have seen more attention from repair shops. The focus for the Fortwo is typically on its unique powertrain and compact electrical systems. This analysis breaks down common issues by category, providing insights into potential repair needs and associated costs for owners.

### 0.9L Turbocharged
**Turbocharger Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Reduced engine power, whistling noises, increased oil consumption.
- **Solution:** Turbocharger replacement.
- **Cost:** $1,200 - $2,500


## Transmission

### 5-Speed Automated Manual
**Clutch Actuator Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Jerky gear changes, difficulty shifting, warning lights on the dashboard.
- **Solution:** Clutch actuator adjustment or replacement.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1,000

**Transmission Fluid Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Leaking fluid, potential for gear slippage or failure if not addressed.
- **Solution:** Seal replacement and fluid top-up.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600

## Electrical

### Body Control Module (BCM)
**BCM Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Intermittent issues with lights, power windows, or central locking; dashboard warning lights.
- **Solution:** BCM reprogramming or replacement.
- **Cost:** $400 - $800


### Battery Drain
**Parasitic Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Battery dies frequently, car won't start.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and repair of faulty electrical component causing drain.
- **Cost:** $200 - $500

### Summary Verdict
The 2017 Smart Fortwo generally exhibits good reliability, particularly with its naturally aspirated engine. Owners should be aware of potential issues with the turbocharged engine's turbocharger and the automated manual transmission's clutch actuator, which can lead to costly repairs. Electrical gremlins, such as BCM glitches and parasitic battery drain, are also noted concerns. While interior components like the infotainment screen and HVAC blower motor can occasionally fail, these are typically less critical than powertrain issues. Overall, for its intended urban use, the Fortwo offers a reasonably dependable ownership experience, provided routine maintenance is followed and potential problem areas are addressed promptly.
