**2014 Smart Fortwo**

The 2014 Smart Fortwo is a compact city car known for its fuel efficiency and affordability. It is offered with a 1.0-liter gasoline engine mated to a 5-speed manual or 6-speed automatic transmission, producing 70 horsepower and 68 lb-ft of torque.

The Fortwo is generally praised for its maneuverability, fuel efficiency, and low cost of ownership. However, it has also been criticized for its cramped interior, limited storage space, and lack of power.

**Engine**

**1.0-Liter Gasoline Engine**

* **Turbocharger failure:** The turbocharger can fail, causing a loss of power and reduced fuel efficiency. Solution: Replace the turbocharger. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
* **Fuel injector failure:** The fuel injectors can become clogged or fail, leading to engine misfiring and poor performance. Solution: Replace the fuel injectors. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+

**Transmission**

**5-Speed Manual Transmission**

* **Clutch failure:** The clutch can become worn or damaged, causing difficulty shifting gears or a loss of power. Solution: Replace the clutch. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+

**6-Speed Automatic Transmission**

* **Transmission control module (TCM) failure:** The TCM can fail, causing transmission shifting problems or a loss of power. Solution: Replace the TCM. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
* **Torque converter failure:** The torque converter can fail, causing transmission slipping or a loss of power. Solution: Replace the torque converter. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+

**Suspension**

* **Front suspension strut failure:** The front suspension struts can fail, causing a loss of ride comfort and handling. Solution: Replace the front suspension struts. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
* **Rear suspension bushing failure:** The rear suspension bushings can fail, causing a loss of handling and stability. Solution: Replace the rear suspension bushings. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+

**Electrical**

* **Battery failure:** The battery can fail, causing a loss of power or difficulty starting the vehicle. Solution: Replace the battery. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
* **Starter motor failure:** The starter motor can fail, preventing the engine from starting. Solution: Replace the starter motor. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+

**Exterior**

* **Windshield wiper motor failure:** The windshield wiper motor can fail, preventing the wipers from working. Solution: Replace the windshield wiper motor. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
* **Headlight bulb failure:** The headlight bulbs can fail, reducing visibility at night. Solution: Replace the headlight bulbs. Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $100+

**Interior**

* **Climate control system failure:** The climate control system can fail, causing the air conditioning or heating system to malfunction. Solution: Repair or replace the climate control system. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
* **Door lock actuator failure:** The door lock actuator can fail, preventing the door from locking or unlocking. Solution: Replace the door lock actuator. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+

**Summary**

The 2014 Smart Fortwo has a number of potential issues, including engine, transmission, suspension, electrical, exterior, and interior problems. The severity and cost of these issues can vary, but some of the more expensive repairs can be costly. Overall, the Fortwo is a reliable and fuel-efficient vehicle, but owners should be aware of the potential issues before purchasing.
