**2018 Smart Fortwo**

The 2018 Smart Fortwo is a compact, two-seater city car available in both coupe and convertible body styles. It is powered by a turbocharged 0.9-liter three-cylinder engine that produces 89 horsepower and 100 lb-ft of torque. The engine is paired with a standard six-speed dual-clutch transmission.

Owners appreciate the Fortwo's small size, fuel efficiency, and maneuverability. However, some have reported issues with its engine, transmission, and other components.

**Engine**

* **0.9-Liter Three-Cylinder Engine**
    * **Turbocharger Failure:** The turbocharger may fail, leading to reduced engine performance and power loss. **Solution:** Replace the turbocharger. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $1,000 - $1,500+
    * **Timing Chain Failure:** The timing chain may stretch or break, causing the engine to lose timing. **Solution:** Replace the timing chain. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $1,500 - $2,000+
* **Recalls:**
    * N/A

**Transmission**

* **Six-Speed Dual-Clutch Transmission**
    * **Clutch Pack Failure:** The clutch pack may wear prematurely, causing the transmission to slip or jerk. **Solution:** Replace the clutch pack. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $500 - $1,000+
    * **Mechatronic Unit Failure:** The mechatronic unit is responsible for controlling the transmission. It may fail, leading to shifting issues and other transmission problems. **Solution:** Replace the mechatronic unit. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $1,500 - $2,500+
* **Recalls:**
    * N/A

**Suspension**

* **No Issues Found**

**Electrical**

* **Battery Failure:** The battery may fail prematurely, causing the vehicle to lose power. **Solution:** Replace the battery. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $200 - $500+
* **Electrical Wiring Issues:** Electrical wiring may become damaged, causing electrical system malfunctions. **Solution:** Repair or replace the damaged wiring. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $200 - $1,000+
* **Recalls:**
    * None Found

**Exterior**

* **Door Handle Failure:** The door handles may break or become misaligned, making it difficult to open the doors. **Solution:** Replace the door handles. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $200 - $500+
* **Wiper Motor Failure:** The wiper motor may fail, causing the wipers to stop working. **Solution:** Replace the wiper motor. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $200 - $500+
* **Recalls:**
    * None Found

**Interior**

* **Center Console Trim Loose:** The center console trim may become loose, rattling when driving. **Solution:** Reattach the trim or replace it if necessary. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $50 - $200+
* **Cruise Control Switch Failure:** The cruise control switch may fail, preventing the cruise control from working. **Solution:** Replace the cruise control switch. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $100 - $300+
* **Recalls:**
    * None Found

**Summary**

The 2018 Smart Fortwo has a few potential issues that owners should be aware of. These issues range in severity from minor cosmetic concerns to more serious mechanical problems. The estimated cost to fix these issues varies widely, depending on the nature of the problem. Overall, the Fortwo is a reliable and fuel-efficient vehicle, but it is important to be aware of its potential issues before purchasing one.
