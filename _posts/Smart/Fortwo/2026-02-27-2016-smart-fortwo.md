# 2016 Smart Fortwo Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2016 Smart Fortwo brings a compact footprint and distinctive styling to city driving, but prospective buyers should carefully weigh its unique powertrain characteristics and historical owner feedback before purchasing.

---

## Engine

### 0.9L Turbocharged 3-Cylinder

This 0.9-liter turbocharged three-cylinder engine produces approximately 89 horsepower and 100 lb-ft of torque. It provides adequate pep for urban commuting, though buyers should monitor routine maintenance closely.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Dual-Clutch Automatic

The 6-speed dual-clutch automatic transmission delivers power to the rear wheels, offering a smoother shift quality compared to older automated manual generations found in earlier Fortwo models.

### 5-Speed Manual

The 5-speed manual transmission pairs directly with the 0.9L turbo engine, giving drivers traditional gear selection and a more direct mechanical connection.

---

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V448000: Parking Brake](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Smart&model=Fortwo&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** As the parking brake lever travel increases, the holding force may decrease to a point where the vehicle is no longer safely secured in all parking situations, allowing the vehicle to unexpectedly move when parked on a slope, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** MBUSA will notify owners, and dealers will replace the parking brake cable lock nut, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

Overall, the 2016 Smart Fortwo serves as an ultra-compact city car with straightforward mechanical options. Buyers are encouraged to thoroughly test drive both transmission types and inspect the vehicle's maintenance history to ensure a reliable ownership experience.
