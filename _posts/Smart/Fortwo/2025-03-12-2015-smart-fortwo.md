The 2015 Smart Fortwo is a quirky, subcompact car known for its exceptional maneuverability and fuel efficiency, particularly in urban environments. It is available as a coupe or convertible. The 2015 Fortwo is powered by a 1.0-liter three-cylinder engine producing 70 horsepower and 68 lb-ft of torque. A 5-speed automated manual transmission is standard. Owners often praise its ease of parking and fuel economy, while common complaints include the jerky transmission, limited cargo space, and overall lack of power.

**Engine**

**1.0L Three-Cylinder Engine**
*   **Oil Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks can develop from various seals and gaskets, leading to low oil levels and potential engine damage. Visible oil spots under the car, burning oil smell, and low oil pressure warning lights are common symptoms.
    *   Solution: Replacing the affected gaskets and seals is necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Failed Catalytic Converter**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Check engine light, reduced engine performance, poor fuel economy, and failing an emissions test are common symptoms.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the catalytic converter.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $700 - $1200+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**5-Speed Automated Manual Transmission**
*   **Jerky Shifting**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The automated manual transmission is known for its rough and jerky shifts, especially in stop-and-go traffic. The transmission may also hesitate or fail to engage smoothly.
    *   Solution: Software updates to the transmission control module (TCM) might improve shifting quality, but sometimes replacement of the clutch actuator is required.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200 (software update), $500 - $1000+ (clutch actuator)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Clutch Actuator Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears, grinding noises during gear changes, or complete failure to shift. The car might get stuck in gear or refuse to shift at all.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the clutch actuator.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Worn Suspension Bushings**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Clunking or rattling noises from the suspension, especially when going over bumps. Poor handling and uneven tire wear can also result.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the worn bushings.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Faulty Window Switch**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Intermittent or complete failure of the power windows.
    *   Solution: Replacing the faulty window switch.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $200
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Failed Key Fob**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Key fob stops working, making it difficult to lock/unlock doors or start the car.
    *   Solution: Replace key fob battery, or reprogram/replace the key fob.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $5 - $20 (battery), $100 - $300 (reprogram/replace)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Cracked Tail Light Lens**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Cracks in the tail light lens, allowing moisture to enter.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the tail light assembly.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Air Conditioning Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Weak or no cold air from the AC system.
    *   Solution: Recharge AC system, or repair/replace faulty compressor, condenser, or other components.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $200+ (recharge), $300 - $1000+ (component replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Dashboard Cracking**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Cracking or warping of the dashboard, especially in hot climates.
    *   Solution: Replacement of dashboard, or installation of dash cover.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $200+ (dash cover), $500 - $1500+ (replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2015 Smart Fortwo has a few common issues that prospective buyers should be aware of. The automated manual transmission can be a significant source of frustration due to its jerky shifting and potential clutch actuator failures. Engine oil leaks are another potential problem. While some issues are relatively inexpensive to fix, others, like catalytic converter failure, can be costly. Overall, carefully inspect the transmission and engine for any signs of trouble before purchasing a used 2015 Smart Fortwo.
