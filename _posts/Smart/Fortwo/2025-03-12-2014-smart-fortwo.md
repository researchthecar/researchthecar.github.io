The 2014 Smart Fortwo is a small, rear-engine, rear-wheel-drive city car known for its fuel efficiency and maneuverability in urban environments. It was available with a 1.0-liter three-cylinder gasoline engine producing 70 horsepower and 68 lb-ft of torque, paired with a 5-speed automated manual transmission. Some appreciate its compact size for easy parking, while others dislike its unconventional transmission and limited cargo space. A fully electric version was also available.

**Engine**

**1.0L 3-Cylinder Gasoline Engine**
* **Oil Consumption**
    * Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported excessive oil consumption, requiring frequent oil top-offs between scheduled oil changes. This can lead to engine damage if not monitored closely.
    * Solution: Monitoring oil levels frequently and using the correct grade of oil. If consumption is excessive, a mechanic should inspect for leaks, worn piston rings, or valve stem seals.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies greatly depending on the cause, from $50 for an oil change to $1,000+ for internal engine repairs.
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Spark Plugs**
    * Description/Symptoms: Rough idling, misfires, and decreased fuel efficiency can be caused by the spark plugs failing or becoming worn.
    * Solution: Replacement of spark plugs.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $150
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**5-Speed Automated Manual Transmission**
* **Hesitation and Jerky Shifting**
    * Description/Symptoms: The 5-speed automated manual transmission is known for its slow and jerky shifts, particularly at low speeds. Many owners report hesitation between gears.
    * Solution: Unfortunately, there is no definitive fix for this issue, as it is inherent in the design of the transmission. Some have had success with software updates or transmission adaptations to improve shifting smoothness. Regular maintenance, including fluid changes, is recommended.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300 for software updates or adaptations, or potentially thousands for transmission replacement.
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Transmission Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Complete transmission failure, rendering the vehicle undrivable. Symptoms include loud noises, inability to shift, or complete loss of power.
    * Solution: Transmission replacement or rebuild.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electric Motor**
No Issues Found

**Suspension**

* **Worn Shock Absorbers**
    * Description/Symptoms: Bouncy ride, poor handling, and increased stopping distance.
    * Solution: Replacement of shock absorbers.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+ for parts and labor.
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Battery Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: Rapid battery drain, difficulty starting, or complete battery failure, especially in cold weather. This can be exacerbated by the vehicle's limited charging capacity.
    * Solution: Replacement of the battery. Ensuring all electrical connections are clean and tight. Having the charging system tested.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300 for a new battery.
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Lighting Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: Headlights or taillights failing prematurely.
    * Solution: Bulb replacement. Check wiring connections for corrosion.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $20 - $100+ depending on the bulb type and labor.
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Door Handle Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Door handles breaking or becoming difficult to operate.
    * Solution: Replacement of the door handle assembly.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Air Conditioning Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: Air conditioning system blowing warm air or failing to cool effectively.
    * Solution: Recharge the AC system and inspect for leaks. Replacement of the compressor or other components may be necessary.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $800+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2014 Smart Fortwo has a few common issues. The most significant are related to the automated manual transmission's jerky shifting and potential for failure, which can be costly to repair. Other issues include oil consumption, electrical problems, and air conditioning malfunctions. While some of these issues are relatively minor, the transmission problems can be severe and expensive. Potential buyers should be aware of these common issues and factor them into their purchasing decision.
