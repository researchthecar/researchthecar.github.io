**2002 Saleen S7**

The Saleen S7 is an American supercar manufactured by Saleen Automotive from 2000 to 2009. It is the first supercar to be made in the United States since the Ford GT40 in 1966. The S7 is powered by a 7.0-liter naturally aspirated V8 engine producing 550 horsepower and 500 lb-ft of torque. It is mated to a 6-speed manual transmission. The S7 can accelerate from 0 to 60 mph in 3.3 seconds and has a top speed of 248 mph. Despite its impressive performance, the S7 has faced several issues over the years.

**Engine**

* **V8 Engine**
    * **Valve Seat Failure:** The valve seats can wear out prematurely, leading to loss of compression and engine damage.
    * **Oil Consumption:** The engine can consume excessive amounts of oil, requiring frequent top-ups.
    * **Timing Chain Failure:** The timing chain can stretch or break, causing the engine to lose timing and potentially damage the valves.
    * **Intake Manifold Gasket Failure:** The intake manifold gasket can fail, leading to vacuum leaks and engine performance issues.
* **Symptoms:** Rough idle, loss of power, excessive oil consumption, engine noises.
* **Solution:** Replace the valve seats, piston rings, or timing chain as needed. Repair or replace the intake manifold gasket.
* **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $2,000 - $10,000+
* **Recalls:** None Found

**Transmission**

* **6-Speed Manual Transmission**
    * **Gear grinding:** The transmission may grind gears, especially when shifting into lower gears.
    * **Clutch Failure:** The clutch can wear out prematurely, leading to slipping and difficulty shifting gears.
    * **Synchro Failure:** The synchronizers can fail, causing the transmission to pop out of gear.
* **Symptoms:** Gear grinding, slipping gears, difficulty shifting gears, popping out of gear.
* **Solution:** Replace the clutch, gears, or synchronizers as needed.
* **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $1,500 - $5,000+
* **Recalls:** None Found

**Suspension**

* **No Issues Found**

**Electrical**

* **Electrical Harness Problems:** The electrical harness can develop loose connections or shorts, leading to electrical issues with various components.
* **Alternator Failure:** The alternator can fail, resulting in a loss of electrical power and potentially stranding the vehicle.
* **Battery Drain:** The battery can drain quickly, requiring frequent charging or replacement.
* **Symptoms:** Electrical component malfunctions, flickering lights, dead battery.
* **Solution:** Repair or replace the electrical harness, alternator, or battery as needed.
* **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $200 - $2,000+
* **Recalls:** None Found

**Exterior**

* **No Issues Found**

**Interior**

* **Panel Gaps:** Some vehicles may have uneven panel gaps, affecting the car's appearance.
* **Infotainment System Malfunctions:** The infotainment system can experience problems such as freezing, loss of sound, or navigation errors.
* **Symptoms:** Off-center panels, frozen screen, no sound, navigation errors.
* **Solution:** Adjust or replace body panels as needed. Update or replace the infotainment system.
* **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $100 - $1,500+
* **Recalls:** None Found

**Summary**

The Saleen S7 is a high-performance supercar with impressive capabilities. However, it has been plagued by various issues over the years, including engine problems, transmission issues, electrical problems, and interior malfunctions. The severity of these issues can range from minor annoyances to potentially catastrophic failures. The cost to fix these issues can be significant, especially for major engine or transmission repairs.
