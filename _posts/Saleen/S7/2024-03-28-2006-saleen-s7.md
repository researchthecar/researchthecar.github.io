**Introduction**

The 2006 Saleen S7 is a supercar hand-built by Saleen Automotive in Irvine, California. It is powered by a 7.0L Powerhouse V8 engine, producing 550 horsepower and 500 lb-ft of torque. The S7 was available with a 6-speed manual transmission.

Critics praised the S7 for its blistering performance, sharp handling, and exotic styling. However, its high price and limited production numbers made it an exclusive offering.

**Engine**

**7.0L Powerhouse V8**

* **Valve Spring Failure:** Premature failure of valve springs leads to engine misfires, reduced power, and increased oil consumption. Symptoms include a rough idle, hesitation during acceleration, and a loss of power. Replacement of the valve springs is required. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $2,000 - $3,000+
* **Oil Pump Failure:** A faulty oil pump can lead to inadequate lubrication and engine damage. Symptoms include reduced oil pressure, knocking sounds, and engine overheating. Replacement of the oil pump and affected components is necessary. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $3,000 - $5,000+

**Transmission**

**6-speed Manual**

* **Clutch Slippage:** Worn clutch plates can cause slippage, resulting in difficulty engaging gears, loss of power, and a burning smell. Replacement of the clutch is required. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $1,500 - $2,500+

**Suspension**

* **Front Suspension Noise:** A worn or damaged front suspension component, such as a ball joint, bushing, or shock absorber, can produce noises while driving. Symptoms include clunking, rattling, or squeaking sounds. Replacement of the affected component is necessary. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $500 - $1,500+
* **Rear Suspension Sagging:** Weak or broken rear suspension springs can cause the rear of the vehicle to sag. Symptoms include uneven ride height, reduced handling stability, and excessive tire wear. Replacement of the suspension springs is required. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $400 - $800+

**Electrical**

* **Alternator Failure:** A faulty alternator can prevent the battery from charging properly. Symptoms include dim headlights, difficulty starting the engine, and electrical system malfunctions. Replacement of the alternator is required. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $500 - $1,000+
* **Battery Draining:** A parasitic drain on the battery can lead to a dead battery. Symptoms include difficulty starting the engine, flickering lights, and reduced electrical system functionality. Diagnostic testing is required to identify and correct the source of the drain. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $200 - $600+

**Exterior**

* **Rear Wing Vibration:** The carbon fiber rear wing can vibrate at high speeds, creating noise and potential damage. Strengthening or replacing the wing is required. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $1,500 - $3,000+

**Interior**

* **HVAC Malfunction:** A faulty heating, ventilation, and air conditioning (HVAC) system can result in improper temperature control. Symptoms include ineffective heating or cooling, airflow problems, and electrical system issues. Repair or replacement of the HVAC components is required. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $500 - $1,500+
* **Dashboard Cracking:** The dashboard can develop cracks over time due to exposure to UV radiation or extreme temperatures. Replacement of the dashboard is required. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $1,000 - $2,000+

**Summary**

The 2006 Saleen S7 is a high-performance supercar, but it has been known to experience certain mechanical issues. Engine problems, particularly valve spring and oil pump failures, can be serious and expensive to repair. Suspension issues and electrical malfunctions can also occur. The high cost of parts and labor for this exclusive vehicle should be considered before purchasing.
