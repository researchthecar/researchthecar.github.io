**Introduction**

The 2013 Nissan NV2500 is a full-size cargo van that offers a spacious cargo area and a powerful V6 engine. It is available with a choice of two engine options: a 4.0L V6 with 261 horsepower and 281 lb-ft of torque, and a 5.6L V8 with 317 horsepower and 385 lb-ft of torque. Both engines are mated to a 5-speed automatic transmission. The NV2500 comes in two trim levels: S and SV.

**Engine**

**4.0L V6**

* **Oil consumption:** Excessive oil consumption has been reported by some owners. This issue can be caused by a faulty valve cover gasket. Symptoms include low oil levels, engine knocking, and blue smoke from the exhaust. The fix involves replacing the valve cover gasket. Estimated cost to fix: $200 - $400+
* **Camshaft position sensor failure:** The camshaft position sensor may fail, causing the engine to run poorly or stall. Symptoms include a check engine light, rough idling, and poor acceleration. The fix involves replacing the camshaft position sensor. Estimated cost to fix: $100 - $200+

**5.6L V8**

* **No Issues Found**

**Transmission**

**5-Speed Automatic**

* **Transmission slipping:** Some owners have reported that their transmissions are slipping, causing the vehicle to hesitate or jerk during acceleration. This issue may be caused by a faulty torque converter or transmission seals. The fix involves repairing or replacing the transmission. Estimated cost to fix: $1,000 - $2,500+
* **Transmission fluid leak:** Transmission fluid leaks have been reported by some owners. This issue can be caused by a faulty transmission pan gasket or seal. The fix involves replacing the gasket or seal. Estimated cost to fix: $100 - $300+

**Suspension**

* **Leaking shock absorbers:** Some owners have reported that their shock absorbers are leaking. This issue can cause a loss of ride control and uneven tire wear. The fix involves replacing the shock absorbers. Estimated cost to fix: $200 - $400+ per shock

**Electrical**

* **Battery drain:** Some owners have reported that their batteries are draining quickly. This issue can be caused by a faulty alternator or starter. The fix involves replacing the alternator or starter. Estimated cost to fix: $500 - $1,000+
* **Headlight switch failure:** The headlight switch may fail, causing the headlights to not turn on or stay on. The fix involves replacing the headlight switch. Estimated cost to fix: $100 - $200+

**Exterior**

* **Body panel corrosion:** Some owners have reported that their body panels are corroding prematurely. This issue is most common on vehicles that are driven in salty or humid environments. The fix involves repairing or replacing the corroded body panels. Estimated cost to fix: $500 - $2,000+

**Interior**

* **HVAC system failure:** The HVAC system may fail, causing the air conditioning or heating to not work properly. The fix involves repairing or replacing the HVAC system. Estimated cost to fix: $500 - $1,500+

**Summary**

The 2013 Nissan NV2500 is a spacious and powerful cargo van that offers a good value for the price. However, it has been plagued by several issues, including oil consumption, transmission problems, and electrical problems. The severity of these issues varies, but some of them can be expensive to fix. Overall, the NV2500 is a decent choice for those who need a full-size cargo van, but it is important to be aware of the potential issues that it may have.
