# 2017 Nissan NV2500 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2017 Nissan NV2500 is a heavy-duty commercial cargo and passenger van designed for commercial work fleets and utility applications. While owner reports to regulatory bodies are minimal, buyers should carefully evaluate powertrain performance and overall mechanical condition during a pre-purchase inspection.

---

## Engine

### 4.0L V6

The 4.0-liter V6 engine produces around 261 horsepower and 281 lb-ft of torque, providing standard pulling power for daily commercial duties. Buyers should test drive the vehicle thoroughly to verify smooth power delivery and responsive acceleration.

**Acceleration Hesitation**
- **Symptoms:** The engine may rev high without an immediate response in vehicle movement during acceleration.
- **Solution:** Inspect throttle body operation, transmission linkage, and powertrain control modules for proper function.

### 5.6L V8

The 5.6-liter V8 engine offers higher horsepower and torque outputs for heavier towing and hauling requirements. It is a robust option, though fuel economy is lower compared to the V6.

## Transmission

### 5-Speed Automatic

The 5-speed automatic transmission pairs with the V6 engine to handle commercial duty cycles. Regular fluid maintenance is recommended to maintain shift quality over high mileage.

**Sluggish Shifting**
- **Symptoms:** The transmission may feel delayed or hesitant to engage gears when stepping on the accelerator.
- **Solution:** Check transmission fluid levels, condition, and software calibration to ensure proper shifting behavior.

### 7-Speed Automatic

The 7-speed automatic transmission is paired with the V8 engine, offering broader gear ratios for improved acceleration and highway efficiency. Ensure smooth engagement during a test drive.

---

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V551000: Electrical System:Ignition](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=NV2500&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** In these models, over time, the weight on the key ring and road conditions or some other jarring event may cause the ignition switch to move out of the run position to the accessory position, turning off the engine. If the vehicle stalls, it can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Nissan will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the production information on the ignition switch, and replace it as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

When shopping for a used 2017 Nissan NV2500, focus on a comprehensive pre-purchase inspection of the powertrain and service history. Verifying proper transmission and engine responsiveness will help ensure dependable commercial service.
