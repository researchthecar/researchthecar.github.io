## Electrical

### Standard Electrical System
**Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Premature battery failure, vehicle not starting.
- **Solution:** Identify and repair parasitic draw or replace faulty battery.
- **Cost:** $150 - $500

**Alternator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Dimming lights, battery warning light, engine stalling.
- **Solution:** Replace the alternator.
- **Cost:** $400 - $800


## Exterior

### Body Panels and Doors
**Door Latch/Lock Actuator Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Doors not locking or unlocking properly, difficulty opening from inside or outside.
- **Solution:** Replace faulty door latch or lock actuator.
- **Cost:** $200 - $500


## Interior

### HVAC System
**Blower Motor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** No air coming from vents or intermittent airflow, regardless of fan speed setting.
- **Solution:** Replace the blower motor or blower motor resistor.
- **Cost:** $250 - $600

### Summary Verdict
The 2017 Nissan NV2500 demonstrates solid reliability, particularly in its powertrain. The 4.0L V6 engine and 5-speed automatic transmission are generally robust and have few documented widespread issues. The chassis and suspension also hold up well under typical commercial use. Areas that have seen some reports of problems include the electrical system, with occasional battery drain and alternator failures, and some interior components like the HVAC blower motor. Exterior issues are less common but can involve door latch mechanisms. Overall, the NV2500 is a dependable work vehicle, with most potential issues being repairable and not indicative of systemic design flaws.
