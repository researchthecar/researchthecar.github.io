# 2012 Nissan NV2500

### Overview
The 2012 Nissan NV2500 is a commercial van designed for utility and durability, often used for business purposes. It typically comes with a robust V6 engine and a reliable transmission, built to handle heavy loads and frequent use. While generally considered dependable, like any vehicle, it can experience specific issues over time. This analysis focuses on documented technical reliability concerns beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential problem areas for owners and fleet managers.

---


## Engine

### 4.0L V6 (VQ40DE)
**Oil Cooler Gasket Leak**
- **Symptoms:** Leaking oil, potential coolant contamination, overheating.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the oil cooler gasket.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600

**Intake Manifold Gasket Leak**
- **Symptoms:** Vacuum leaks, rough idle, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the intake manifold gaskets.
- **Cost:** $400 - $700


## Transmission

### 5-Speed Automatic (RE5R05A)
**Harsh Shifting / Delayed Engagement**
- **Symptoms:** Jerky gear changes, difficulty engaging gears, transmission warning light.
- **Solution:** Transmission fluid and filter change, solenoid replacement, or transmission rebuild.
- **Cost:** $500 - $2500+

### Summary Verdict
The 2012 Nissan NV2500 demonstrates good overall reliability, particularly in its powertrain components when properly maintained. The 4.0L V6 engine is generally robust, though potential leaks from the oil cooler and intake manifold gaskets are noted. The 5-speed automatic transmission, while functional, can sometimes exhibit issues with harsh shifting or delayed engagement, often addressable with maintenance or component replacement. Other systems like suspension, electrical, exterior, and interior components show no widespread significant reliability concerns, indicating a solid build for commercial use. Regular maintenance is key to mitigating potential issues and ensuring longevity.
