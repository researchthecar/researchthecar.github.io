The 2019 Nissan NV2500 is a full-size cargo van known for its spacious interior, durability, and practicality. It offers a comfortable ride and ample cargo space, making it a popular choice for businesses and individuals needing a reliable work van. However, some common complaints revolve around its fuel economy, basic interior features, and handling compared to some competitors. The 2019 NV2500 is equipped with a 4.0L V6 engine producing 261 horsepower and 281 lb-ft of torque, or a 5.6L V8 engine producing 375 horsepower and 387 lb-ft of torque. Both engines are paired with a 7-speed automatic transmission.

**Engine**

**4.0L V6**

*   **No Issues Found**

**5.6L V8**

*   **Excessive Oil Consumption**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Engine consumes an unusually high amount of oil between oil changes. Symptoms include needing to add oil frequently, blue exhaust smoke, and potential engine damage if oil levels are not maintained.
    *   Solution: Possible solutions include replacing valve stem seals, piston rings, or in severe cases, a complete engine overhaul or replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $5000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**7-Speed Automatic**

*   **Harsh Shifting/Jerking**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Transmission exhibits harsh or jerky shifting between gears, especially during acceleration or deceleration. This can also manifest as a noticeable "clunk" during gear changes.
    *   Solution: The issue can stem from the valve body or TCM needing to be reprogrammed. In other cases, a full transmission replacement may be required.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $5000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Premature Wear of Front Suspension Components**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Front suspension components, such as ball joints and tie rod ends, may experience premature wear, leading to loose steering, vibrations, and unusual noises.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the worn suspension components is the common fix. Regular inspections and lubrication can help prevent premature wear.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Dead Battery/Starting Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Battery drains prematurely, leading to starting issues or a completely dead battery, even when the vehicle is relatively new. This can be intermittent.
    *   Solution: Check for parasitic draws, replace the battery, or replace the alternator if it is not charging properly.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Malfunctioning Door Lock Actuators**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Door lock actuators may fail, causing doors to not lock or unlock properly with the key fob or interior switch.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the faulty door lock actuator.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $400 per door
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Sliding Door Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Difficulties opening or closing the sliding side door, including sticking, binding, or requiring excessive force.
    *   Solution: Lubricate the door tracks and rollers. If the problem persists, the rollers or the entire sliding door mechanism might need to be replaced.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **No Issues Found**

**Summary**
The 2019 Nissan NV2500, while generally reliable, can experience issues. The most concerning issue is excessive oil consumption in the 5.6L V8 engine, which can lead to significant engine damage if not addressed promptly. Transmission problems (harsh shifting) can also be a source of frustration. The severity of these issues ranges from minor inconveniences to costly repairs, with potential repair costs ranging from a few hundred to several thousand dollars depending on the problem. While the NV2500 provides a solid work platform, being aware of these potential problems can help owners proactively maintain their vehicle and minimize potential repair costs.
