**Introduction of the 2014 Nissan NV2500**

The 2014 Nissan NV2500 is a full-size van that is available in three trim levels: S, SV, and SL. It is powered by a 4.0-liter V6 engine that produces 261 horsepower and 281 lb-ft of torque. The engine is mated to a five-speed automatic transmission. The NV2500 is known for its spacious interior, comfortable ride, and strong towing capacity. However, it has also been reported to have some issues.

**Engine**

**4.0-liter V6**

* **Oil Leaks**
    * Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks may occur from the valve covers, oil pan, or rear main seal. Symptoms include oil spots under the vehicle and low oil levels.
    * Solution: Replace the leaking gaskets or seals.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**Five-Speed Automatic**

* **Transmission Slipping**
    * Description/Symptoms: The transmission may slip or hesitate when shifting gears. Symptoms include rough shifting, loss of power, and increased fuel consumption.
    * Solution: Replace or rebuild the transmission.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **No Issues Found**

**Electrical**

* **Faulty Alternator**
    * Description/Symptoms: The alternator may fail to charge the battery, leading to electrical problems such as dim headlights, slow engine cranking, and battery failure.
    * Solution: Replace the alternator.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Rust**
    * Description/Symptoms: Rust may develop on the body panels, frame, and suspension components. Symptoms include peeling paint, bubbling, and holes.
    * Solution: Repair or replace the affected parts.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Faulty Door Locks**
    * Description/Symptoms: The door locks may fail to lock or unlock electronically. Symptoms include difficulty opening or closing the doors, and the door ajar warning light remaining on.
    * Solution: Replace the door lock actuator.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2014 Nissan NV2500 is a reliable full-size van with a spacious interior and strong towing capacity. However, it has been reported to have some issues, particularly with the engine and transmission. The severity of the issues and cost to fix them can range from minor to major. It is important to weigh the potential costs of repairs against the benefits of the vehicle before making a purchase decision.
