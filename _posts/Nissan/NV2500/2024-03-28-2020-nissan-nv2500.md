**Introduction:**
The 2020 Nissan NV2500 is a full-size commercial van. It is available with a 4.0-liter V6 engine mated to a 5-speed automatic transmission. Other engine and transmission options are not available. The V6 engine generates 261 horsepower and 281 lb-ft of torque. The NV2500 is praised for its spacious cargo area, powerful engine, and comfortable interior. However, it is criticized for its lack of advanced safety features and poor fuel economy.

**Engine**
* **4.0-liter V6 Engine**
    * **Valve Cover Gasket Leak:** Oil may leak from the valve cover gaskets, causing a burning oil smell and reduced engine performance. Solution: Replace the valve cover gaskets. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800+
    * **Timing Chain Tensioner Failure:** The timing chain tensioner may fail, causing a rattling noise from the engine. Solution: Replace the timing chain tensioner. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $1,500+
    * **Oil Pump Failure:** The oil pump may fail, resulting in reduced oil pressure and potential engine damage. Solution: Replace the oil pump. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,200 - $1,800+
    * **Recalls:**
        * NHTSA Campaign Number: 20V417000 - Crankshaft Position Sensor May Fail
        * NHTSA Campaign Number: 20V356000 - Rear Axle May Leak

**Transmission**
* **5-Speed Automatic Transmission:**
    * **Shift Solenoid Failure:** The shift solenoids may fail, causing delayed or erratic shifting. Solution: Replace the shift solenoids. Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $600+
    * **Transmission Control Module Failure:** The transmission control module may fail, leading to transmission problems and warning lights. Solution: Replace the transmission control module. Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,200+
    * **Torque Converter Failure:** The torque converter may fail, causing shuddering, slipping, or loss of power. Solution: Replace the torque converter. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,000+
