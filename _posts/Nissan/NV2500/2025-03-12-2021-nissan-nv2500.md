The 2021 Nissan NV2500 is a full-size cargo van known for its robust capabilities and spacious interior. It offers a practical solution for businesses and individuals needing to transport goods and equipment. The NV2500 comes standard with a 4.0L V6 engine producing 261 horsepower and 281 lb-ft of torque. An optional 5.6L V8 engine is also available, boasting 375 horsepower and 387 lb-ft of torque. Both engines are paired with a heavy-duty 7-speed automatic transmission. Owners often praise the NV2500 for its ample cargo space, comfortable seating, and powerful engine options. However, some criticize its fuel economy and handling compared to other vans in its class.

**Engine**

**4.0L V6**

*   **No Issues Found**

**5.6L V8**

*   **Excessive Oil Consumption**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported excessive oil consumption, requiring frequent oil top-offs between scheduled oil changes. This can be indicated by the low oil pressure light illuminating or a noticeable drop in oil level on the dipstick.
    *   Solution: Determining the cause of the oil consumption requires a thorough inspection, potentially including a compression test, leak-down test, and visual inspection of the engine for leaks. Repairs could range from replacing valve stem seals to piston rings or, in severe cases, engine replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $5,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**7-Speed Automatic Transmission**

*   **Harsh Shifting/Hesitation**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported experiencing harsh or erratic shifting, particularly when shifting between gears. Hesitation or delays when accelerating may also be present.
    *   Solution: Diagnosing the cause of this issue may require a transmission fluid check, software update, or replacement of faulty solenoids or valve body.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $3,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Premature Wear of Front Suspension Components**
    * Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported the front suspension components, such as ball joints, tie rod ends, and sway bar links, wearing out faster than expected. This can be indicated by clunking noises, uneven tire wear, or loose steering.
    * Solution: Replacement of worn-out front suspension components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Battery Drain**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have experienced issues with the battery draining prematurely, especially when the vehicle is not driven for extended periods. This can result in difficulty starting the vehicle or requiring frequent jump-starts.
    *   Solution: Diagnosing a parasitic drain may require testing individual circuits to isolate the source of the drain. Potential causes include faulty relays, modules, or aftermarket accessories.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Sliding Door Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: Problems reported with the sliding door include difficulty opening/closing, getting stuck, or making unusual noises.
    * Solution: Inspection and adjustment of the door mechanism, lubrication of moving parts, or replacement of faulty components such as rollers or latches.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $800+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **HVAC Blower Motor Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The HVAC blower motor may fail, resulting in a lack of airflow from the vents. This can be indicated by no air blowing out, even when the fan is set to high.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the faulty blower motor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2021 Nissan NV2500 has some reported issues that potential buyers should be aware of. Excessive oil consumption on the 5.6L V8 engine can be a costly repair. Potential electrical issues like battery drain could be annoying but inexpensive. Premature wear on front suspension components may occur and sliding door issues are commonly reported. While not all NV2500s will experience these problems, it's wise to be vigilant during pre-purchase inspections and address any concerns promptly.
