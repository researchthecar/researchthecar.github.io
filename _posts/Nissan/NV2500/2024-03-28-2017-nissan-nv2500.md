**Introduction**

The 2017 Nissan NV2500 is a versatile commercial van designed for various businesses and work operations. It boasts a powerful 4.0L V6 engine producing 261 horsepower and 281 lb-ft of torque, paired with a standard 5-speed automatic transmission. The NV2500 is known for its spacious interior, sturdy build, and reliable performance. However, like any vehicle, it has experienced some specific issues that owners have reported.

**Engine**

**4.0L V6**

* **Carbon buildup on intake valves**
> Description/Symptoms: Reduced engine power, rough idle, and potential engine damage
> Solution: Intake valve cleaning or replacement
> Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1200+
> Recalls: NHTSA Campaign Number 17V578000

* **Oil consumption issues**
> Description/Symptoms: Excessive oil consumption requiring frequent top-ups between oil changes
> Solution: Piston ring and valve seal replacement
> Estimated Cost to Fix: $1500 - $2500+
> Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**5-speed Automatic**

* **Shifting delays and rough shifting**
> Description/Symptoms: Hesitation or jerkiness during gear changes
> Solution: Transmission fluid replacement or transmission rebuild
> Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $2000+
> Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Leaf spring failure**
> Description/Symptoms: Broken or sagging leaf springs, resulting in decreased handling and ride comfort
> Solution: Leaf spring replacement
> Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1000+ per spring
> Recalls: None Found

* **Rear axle seal leakage**
> Description/Symptoms: Oil leakage from the rear axle, causing reduced lubrication and potential damage
> Solution: Seal replacement
> Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
> Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Battery drain issues**
> Description/Symptoms: Battery losing charge prematurely, requiring frequent recharging or replacement
> Solution: Electrical system diagnosis to identify the cause of drain
> Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
> Recalls: None Found

* **Alternator failure**
> Description/Symptoms: Poor battery charging, dimming headlights, and other electrical issues
> Solution: Alternator replacement
> Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1000+
> Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Door latch failure**
> Description/Symptoms: Doors not latching properly, posing a safety hazard
> Solution: Door latch replacement
> Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
> Recalls: NHTSA Campaign Number 19V660000

* **Rust issues**
> Description/Symptoms: Corrosion on body panels, especially around wheel wells and undercarriage
> Solution: Rust repair or prevention treatment
> Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the extent of damage
> Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Rear seat belt buckle failure**
> Description/Symptoms: Seat belts not releasing properly, posing a safety hazard
> Solution: Seat belt buckle replacement
> Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200
> Recalls: NHTSA Campaign Number 19V417000

* **Dash panel cracking**
> Description/Symptoms: Cracks developing on the dashboard, affecting aesthetics and potentially interfering with controls
> Solution: Dash panel replacement
> Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1000+
> Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2017 Nissan NV2500 has had a range of issues reported by owners, including engine carbon buildup, oil consumption, transmission shifting problems, suspension issues, electrical problems, and various interior and exterior concerns. The severity of these issues varies, with some being relatively minor and inexpensive to fix, while others can be more significant and require expensive repairs. It's important to note that not all vehicles will experience these problems, and individual experiences may vary.
