The 2014 Nissan NV2500 is a commercial van designed for utility and durability, often used for cargo transport and trades. ## Electrical

### General Electrical System
**Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Intermittent starting issues, dimming lights.
- **Solution:** Diagnose and replace faulty parasitic draw component (e.g., module, accessory).
- **Cost:** $150 - $500

**Alternator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Battery warning light illuminated, dimming headlights, engine stalling.
- **Solution:** Replace alternator.
- **Cost:** $400 - $800


## Exterior

### Body Panels and Doors
**Door Latch/Lock Actuator Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Doors not locking or unlocking properly, difficulty opening from inside or outside.
- **Solution:** Replace faulty door latch or lock actuator.
- **Cost:** $200 - $500


### Lighting
**Headlight Bulb Failure**
- **Symptoms:** One or both headlights not illuminating.
- **Solution:** Replace headlight bulbs.
- **Cost:** $50 - $150


## Interior

### HVAC System
**Blower Motor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** No air coming from vents, or air only coming from certain vents.
- **Solution:** Replace blower motor or blower motor resistor.
- **Cost:** $200 - $500

### Summary Verdict
The 2014 Nissan NV2500 demonstrates solid reliability, particularly in its powertrain. The V6 and V8 engines, along with the 5-speed automatic transmission, are generally robust and do not present widespread common failures. The chassis and suspension also hold up well under typical commercial use. Electrical issues, such as battery drain and alternator failure, are the most frequently cited technical concerns, though these are not unique to this model and can often be addressed with component replacement. Minor issues with door latches and HVAC blower motors are also noted but are typically repairable. Overall, the NV2500 is a dependable work vehicle with a good track record for durability.
