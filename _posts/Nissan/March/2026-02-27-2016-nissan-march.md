# 2016 Nissan March

### Overview
The 2016 Nissan March, also known as the Micra in many markets, is a subcompact car generally praised for its fuel efficiency and maneuverability in urban environments. As a budget-friendly option, its reliability is a key consideration for potential buyers. While it offers a generally dependable ownership experience, like any vehicle, it has specific areas where owners have reported issues. This analysis focuses on documented technical reliability concerns, excluding routine maintenance and standard wear and tear, to provide a clear picture of potential problem areas for the 2016 model year across its various configurations.

### CVT (Continuously Variable Transmission)
**Jerking or Hesitation**
- **Symptoms:** Sudden jolts, delayed acceleration, or a feeling of the transmission slipping.
- **Solution:** Software update, transmission fluid flush, or in severe cases, transmission replacement.
- **Cost:** $300 - $1500

**Overheating**
- **Symptoms:** Transmission warning light illuminated, reduced performance, burning smell.
- **Solution:** Check and top up transmission fluid, inspect cooling system, or replace transmission cooler.
- **Cost:** $200 - $800

### Summary Verdict
The 2016 Nissan March demonstrates a generally solid reliability record, particularly with its manual transmission and standard engine options. The most frequently cited technical concerns revolve around the CVT transmission, with reports of jerking, hesitation, and occasional overheating, which can lead to costly repairs. Electrical issues, such as battery drain and minor infotainment glitches, are also noted but are typically less severe. For buyers prioritizing long-term dependability, opting for a manual transmission variant is advisable. Regular maintenance, especially for the CVT fluid, is crucial to mitigate potential problems and ensure a smoother ownership experience with this economical subcompact.
