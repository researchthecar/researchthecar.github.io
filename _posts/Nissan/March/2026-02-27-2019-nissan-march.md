# 2019 Nissan March

### Overview
The 2019 Nissan March, a subcompact hatchback known for its urban maneuverability and fuel efficiency, generally presents a solid reliability record. Owners often praise its straightforward mechanics and low running costs. While no vehicle is entirely immune to issues, the March typically avoids widespread, costly problems. Common areas of attention, if any arise, tend to be minor and related to specific components rather than systemic failures. This analysis focuses on documented technical reliability, excluding routine maintenance and expected wear and tear, to provide a clear picture for potential buyers and current owners.

### Summary Verdict
The 2019 Nissan March demonstrates good overall reliability, making it a sensible choice for budget-conscious urban drivers. The powertrain options, particularly the manual transmission and the smaller engines, are generally robust. The CVT, while efficient, has a minor reported issue of occasional hesitation, which is often rectifiable with maintenance. Other systems like suspension, electrical, exterior, and interior components show no significant widespread problems, indicating solid build quality for its class. Owners can expect a dependable vehicle with minimal unexpected repair costs, provided routine maintenance is followed.
