Common areas of attention, if any arise, tend to be minor and related to specific components rather than systemic failures. This analysis focuses on documented technical reliability, excluding routine maintenance and expected wear and tear, to provide a clear picture of potential mechanical and electrical concerns for the 2021 model year across its available configurations.

### Summary Verdict
The 2021 Nissan March demonstrates good overall reliability, making it a sensible choice for budget-conscious buyers and city dwellers. The powertrain, particularly the engines, shows no significant recurring problems. The CVT transmission is the most notable area for potential minor issues, with some reports of hesitation, though these are generally resolvable with software updates or maintenance. Electrical systems are largely stable, with only occasional infotainment glitches noted. The exterior and interior components are also holding up well, with no widespread complaints. For its segment, the March offers a dependable ownership experience, provided routine maintenance is performed.
