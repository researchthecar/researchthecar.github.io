**Introduction:**

The 2009 Nissan Urvan is a full-size van offered in a variety of configurations. It is powered by a 4.0L V6 engine paired with a 5-speed automatic transmission, producing 250 horsepower and 279 lb-ft of torque. The Urvan has a spacious and versatile interior, making it a popular choice for families and businesses. However, it has received some negative reviews due to its lack of modern features and poor fuel economy.

**Engine**

**4.0L V6**

* **Oil Consumption:** Excessive oil consumption is a common problem, causing engines to run low on oil and potentially leading to engine damage.
    * Solution: Replace piston rings and valve seals.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Coolant Leak:** Leaks from the coolant system, particularly from the radiator and hoses, are known to occur.
    * Solution: Replace leaking components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,200
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**5-Speed Automatic**

* **Shifting Issues:** Rough or delayed shifting, especially when shifting into higher gears, is a reported issue.
    * Solution: Replace the transmission fluid and filter. More extensive repairs may be necessary in severe cases.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $3,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Rear Suspension Sagging:** The rear suspension can become overstressed and sag, particularly when carrying heavy loads.
    * Solution: Replace or reinforce the rear leaf springs.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Alternator Failure:** Premature alternator failure can lead to electrical system issues and battery problems.
    * Solution: Replace the alternator.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Starting Issues:** Difficulty starting the engine, particularly in cold weather, has been reported.
    * Solution: Replace the starter solenoid or starter motor.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Door Handle Breakage:** The exterior door handles are prone to breaking, making it difficult to open the doors.
    * Solution: Replace the door handles.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Dashboard Cracking:** The dashboard can crack over time, especially in areas exposed to direct sunlight.
    * Solution: Replace the dashboard or use a dashboard cover.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
    * Recalls: None Found

* **HVAC System Issues:** Malfunctioning air conditioning, heating, or ventilation is a common complaint.
    * Solution: Diagnose and repair the specific component that has failed.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2009 Nissan Urvan has several reported issues that can impact its reliability and functionality. **Engine oil consumption and coolant leaks** are serious concerns that can lead to costly repairs. **Transmission shifting problems and suspension sagging** can compromise the driving experience and safety. The **electrical system** has also shown signs of weakness, with **alternator failures and starting issues**. **Exterior and interior issues** are generally less severe but can still be frustrating. The cost to fix these issues can vary significantly depending on the severity of the problem and the labor rates in your area. It is important to have these issues addressed promptly to prevent further damage and maintain the safety and reliability of your Urvan.
