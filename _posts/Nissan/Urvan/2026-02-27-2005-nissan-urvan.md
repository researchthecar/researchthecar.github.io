# 2005 Nissan Urvan

### Overview
The 2005 Nissan Urvan, a robust commercial van, is generally known for its utilitarian design and dependable performance, particularly in its core function as a people or cargo mover. While specific model year data can be scarce, general trends for this generation suggest a focus on mechanical durability. Owners often praise its straightforward engineering, which contributes to easier maintenance and repair. However, like any vehicle of this age, certain components may exhibit wear over time. This analysis focuses on documented common issues beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential areas of concern for prospective buyers or current owners.

---


## Engine

### 2.7L TD27 Diesel
**Glow Plug Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Difficulty starting in cold weather, rough idling.
- **Solution:** Replacement of faulty glow plugs.
- **Cost:** $150 - $300

**Head Gasket Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Overheating, white smoke from exhaust, coolant loss, milky oil.
- **Solution:** Replacement of head gasket and associated seals.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1500


### 3.0L QD32 Diesel
**Injector Pump Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Poor engine performance, rough idling, black smoke, starting problems.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of the fuel injection pump.
- **Cost:** $600 - $1200

**Turbocharger Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of power, excessive smoke, whining or whistling noises from the engine bay.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the turbocharger unit.
- **Cost:** $1000 - $2000


## Transmission

### 5-Speed Manual Transmission
**Clutch Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Slipping clutch, difficulty shifting gears, burning smell.
- **Solution:** Replacement of clutch kit (clutch disc, pressure plate, release bearing).
- **Cost:** $500 - $900

**Synchronizer Ring Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Grinding noise when shifting gears, difficulty engaging gears.
- **Solution:** Internal transmission repair or rebuild, replacement of synchronizer rings.
- **Cost:** $700 - $1400


### 4-Speed Automatic Transmission
**Torque Converter Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Slipping, shuddering, delayed engagement, overheating.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of the torque converter.
- **Cost:** $600 - $1100

**Transmission Fluid Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Leaking fluid, rough shifting, overheating.
- **Solution:** Inspection and repair of seals, gaskets, or cooler lines.
- **Cost:** $200 - $500


## Suspension

### Standard Chassis
**Leaf Spring Sagging**
- **Symptoms:** Rear end sag, reduced load-carrying capacity, uneven ride height.
- **Solution:** Replacement of worn or broken leaf springs.
- **Cost:** $400 - $800

**Shock Absorber Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Bouncy ride, poor handling, visible leaks from shocks.
- **Solution:** Replacement of worn shock absorbers.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600


## Electrical

### General Electrical System
**Alternator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Dimming headlights, battery warning light illuminated, weak battery.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the alternator.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600

**Wiring Harness Corrosion**
- **Symptoms:** Intermittent electrical faults, non-functioning lights or accessories.
- **Solution:** Inspection and repair or replacement of corroded wiring sections.
- **Cost:** $200 - $500


## Exterior

### Body Panels and Seals
**Rust and Corrosion**
- **Symptoms:** Visible rust on body panels, wheel arches, and undercarriage, especially in humid or coastal areas.
- **Solution:** Rust treatment, panel repair, or replacement.
- **Cost:** $300 - $1500+

**Door Seal Degradation**
- **Symptoms:** Water leaks into the cabin, wind noise.
- **Solution:** Replacement of worn or damaged door seals.
- **Cost:** $100 - $300


## Interior

### Cabin Components
**HVAC Blower Motor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** No or weak airflow from vents, unusual noises from the dashboard.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the blower motor.
- **Cost:** $150 - $350

**Seat Upholstery Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Torn or worn seat fabric/vinyl, especially on driver's seat.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of seat covers or foam padding.
- **Cost:** $100 - $400


--- 
### Summary Verdict
The 2005 Nissan Urvan is a workhorse vehicle that, while not without its potential issues, generally offers good mechanical reliability for its class and age. The diesel engines, particularly the TD27, are known for their longevity if maintained. Common areas to inspect include the fuel injection system on the QD32, potential head gasket issues on older diesel engines, and standard wear items like clutch components and suspension parts. Electrical gremlins and rust are also factors to consider, especially on higher-mileage examples or those used in harsh environments. Overall, with diligent maintenance and addressing specific known issues, the Urvan can remain a cost-effective and dependable option.
