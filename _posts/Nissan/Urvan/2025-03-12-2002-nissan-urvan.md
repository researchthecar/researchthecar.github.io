The 2002 Nissan Urvan, also known as the Nissan Caravan in some markets, is a light commercial van known for its reliability and practicality. Engine options for this model year typically include a range of gasoline and diesel engines depending on the region. These often include the KA24DE 2.4L gasoline engine, the QD32 3.2L diesel engine, and potentially others depending on the market. Power output varies, but the KA24DE typically produces around 143 horsepower and 154 lb-ft of torque. People generally appreciate the Urvan for its spacious cargo area, dependability, and ease of maintenance. Common dislikes include its basic interior, relatively weak engine performance compared to modern vans, and limited availability of advanced safety features.

**Engine**

**KA24DE 2.4L Gasoline Engine**

*   **Distributor Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Engine misfires, rough idling, difficulty starting, or complete engine failure. Can also cause stalling and poor fuel economy.
    *   Solution: Replace the faulty distributor with a new or refurbished unit.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Knocking Noise**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The engine may produce knocking or pinging sounds, especially under load. This is often caused by using low octane fuel or carbon buildup.
    *   Solution: Use the recommended octane fuel, perform a carbon cleaning service, and check the ignition timing.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $200+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**QD32 3.2L Diesel Engine**

*   **Fuel Injector Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Poor fuel economy, black smoke from the exhaust, rough idling, and difficulty starting the engine.
    *   Solution: Replace or rebuild faulty fuel injectors. A fuel system cleaning may also be beneficial.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Glow Plug Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Hard starting in cold weather, excessive white smoke during start-up, and rough idling.
    *   Solution: Replace the faulty glow plugs. It's often recommended to replace all glow plugs at the same time.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**No Issues Found**

**Suspension**

*   **Worn Leaf Springs**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Sagging rear end, reduced load carrying capacity, and a bumpy ride.
    *   Solution: Replace the worn leaf springs with new or heavy-duty leaf springs.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Worn Shock Absorbers**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Bouncy ride, poor handling, excessive body roll, and increased stopping distance.
    *   Solution: Replace the worn shock absorbers with new ones.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Alternator Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Dimming headlights, warning lights on the dashboard, difficulty starting the engine, and a dead battery.
    *   Solution: Replace the faulty alternator with a new or refurbished unit.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $350+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Wiring Harness Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Intermittent electrical problems, such as malfunctioning lights, sensors, or other electrical components.
    *   Solution: Inspect and repair the damaged wiring harness. In some cases, the entire wiring harness may need to be replaced.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Rust (Especially in Coastal Areas)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rust forming on the body panels, undercarriage, and around window seals.
    *   Solution: Repair or replace the affected body panels, treat the rust, and apply rust preventative measures.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $1000+ (depending on severity)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Sliding Door Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Difficulty opening or closing the sliding door, sticking, or excessive noise.
    *   Solution: Lubricate the door tracks and rollers. Adjust or replace worn rollers or hinges.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $300+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Dashboard Cracking/Fading**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Cracks or discoloration of the dashboard plastic due to sun exposure.
    *   Solution: Replace the dashboard or install a dashboard cover.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **HVAC Control Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Malfunctioning heating or air conditioning system, such as blowing hot air when cold is selected or vice versa.
    *   Solution: Diagnose and repair the HVAC system, which may involve replacing the HVAC control unit, blend door actuator, or other components.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $400+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2002 Nissan Urvan is generally a reliable van, but owners should be aware of common issues such as distributor or fuel injector failures, worn suspension components, electrical problems, and rust. The severity and cost of these issues can vary widely, but addressing them promptly can help prevent further damage and maintain the van's overall performance and reliability. Rust is a significant concern, especially in coastal regions. While some repairs can be relatively inexpensive, others, such as engine or transmission work, can be more costly.
