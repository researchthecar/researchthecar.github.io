**Introduction**

The 2000 Nissan Urvan is a versatile passenger van known for its spacious interior, reliable engine, and comfortable ride. It is powered by a 2.4L TD Bluebird diesel engine that produces 125 horsepower and 228 lb-ft of torque and is paired with a 5-speed automatic transmission. While the Urvan has received praise for its practicality and durability, it has also faced its fair share of issues that owners have encountered.

**Engine**

* **2.4L TD Bluebird Diesel Engine**
    * **Fuel Injectors: Leaking fuel**
        Description/Symptoms: Fuel leakage from the fuel injectors can lead to poor engine performance, increased fuel consumption, and potentially a fire hazard.
        Solution: Replace the faulty fuel injectors.
        Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
    * **Intake Manifold: Cracks**
        Description/Symptoms: Cracks in the intake manifold can cause reduced engine power, rough idling, and increased exhaust emissions.
        Solution: Replace the cracked intake manifold.
        Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500

**Transmission**

* **5-Speed Automatic Transmission**
    * **Torque Converter: Failure**
        Description/Symptoms: A failing torque converter can lead to slipping gears, hesitation during acceleration, and overheating.
        Solution: Replace the torque converter.
        Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,500

**Suspension**

* **Front Suspension: Worn Out Bushings**
        Description/Symptoms: Worn-out bushings in the front suspension can cause knocking or clunking noises, reduced handling, and premature tire wear.
        Solution: Replace the worn-out bushings.
        Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300
* **Rear Suspension: Broken Leaf Springs**
        Description/Symptoms: Broken leaf springs can cause the rear of the vehicle to sag, reduce load-bearing capacity, and result in a rough ride.
        Solution: Replace the broken leaf springs.
        Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800

**Electrical**

* **Alternator: Failure**
        Description/Symptoms: A failing alternator can lead to difficulty starting the vehicle, battery drainage, and electrical system malfunctions.
        Solution: Replace the faulty alternator.
        Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500
* **Starter: Solenoid Failure**
        Description/Symptoms: A failing solenoid in the starter can prevent the vehicle from starting, causing a clicking or grinding noise when attempting to start.
        Solution: Replace the starter solenoid.
        Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $250

**Exterior**

* **Front Bumper: Loose or Damaged**
        Description/Symptoms: A loose or damaged front bumper can result from minor collisions, impact damage, or improper installation.
        Solution: Repair or replace the damaged bumper.
        Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $500
* **Rear Taillights: Moisture Buildup**
        Description/Symptoms: Moisture buildup in the rear taillights can lead to reduced visibility and corrosion.
        Solution: Seal any gaps or cracks around the taillight housing and install new gaskets.
        Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $250

**Interior**

* **Dashboard: Cracks**
        Description/Symptoms: Cracks in the dashboard can occur due to exposure to sunlight, extreme temperatures, or rough handling.
        Solution: Repair or replace the cracked dashboard.
        Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $1,000
* **Headliner: Sagging**
        Description/Symptoms: A sagging headliner can occur due to aging, heat exposure, or improper installation.
        Solution: Re-attach the headliner using adhesive or replace the entire headliner.
        Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500

**Summary**

While the Nissan Urvan is generally a reliable vehicle, it is not immune to specific issues. The most common issues reported by owners involve the fuel injectors, intake manifold, torque converter, and suspension components. These issues can range from minor inconveniences to more severe problems that may require costly repairs. Potential buyers should be aware of these potential issues and budget accordingly for repairs or maintenance.
