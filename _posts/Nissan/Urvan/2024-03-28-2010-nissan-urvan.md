**Introduction:**

The 2010 Nissan Urvan is a versatile and spacious van offered with a sole 3.5L V6 engine paired with a 5-speed automatic transmission. Known for its ample cargo space, comfortable interior, and optional 4WD capabilities, it has been appreciated by owners for its practicality and reliability. However, like any vehicle, the Urvan has encountered some issues over the years, which are detailed below.

**Engine**

* **3.5L V6**

  * **Oil leaks:** Rear main seal failure is a common issue, leading to oil leaks.
    * Description/Symptoms: Oil drips or puddles under the vehicle.
    * Solution: Replacing the rear main seal.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,200+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

* **5-Speed Automatic**

  * **Delayed shifting:** Transmission may hesitate or delay when shifting gears.
    * Description/Symptoms: Rough or hesitant gear changes, especially when cold or during acceleration.
    * Solution: Flushing and replacing transmission fluid, or potentially transmission rebuild or replacement.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $4,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Leaking shocks:** Worn or damaged shock absorbers can cause leaks.
    * Description/Symptoms: Clunking or rattling noises, reduced ride height, or uneven tire wear.
    * Solution: Replacing the shock absorbers.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Battery drain:** Electrical components may drain the battery prematurely.
    * Description/Symptoms: Difficulty starting the vehicle, flickering lights, or dead battery.
    * Solution: Diagnosing and repairing electrical faults, or replacing the battery.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $800+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Rust on rear bumper:** Rear bumper may develop rust spots.
    * Description/Symptoms: Visible rust or corrosion on the bumper.
    * Solution: Replacing the bumper or rust repair.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Dashboard rattles:** Dashboard components may make rattling noises.
    * Description/Symptoms: Squeaks or rattles coming from the dashboard.
    * Solution: Identifying and tightening loose parts or replacing defective panels.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

While the 2010 Nissan Urvan is generally a reliable vehicle, it has faced some issues over time. The most significant concerns are the potential oil leaks from the engine, delayed shifting in the transmission, and premature electrical component failures. These issues can be costly to repair, but most are not widespread or severe.
