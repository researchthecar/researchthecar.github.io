**Introduction:**
The 2011 Nissan Urvan is a popular commercial van that offers a spacious interior and reliable performance. It is available with a 2.5-liter diesel engine or a 3.5-liter gasoline engine, paired with either a 5-speed manual or 5-speed automatic transmission. The Urvan is known for its durability, fuel efficiency, and ease of maintenance. However, it has also been plagued by several common issues that owners have reported.

**Engine**

**2.5-Liter Diesel Engine**
* **Injector Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Leaking or sticking injectors cause engine misfires, loss of power, and increased emissions.
    * Solution: Replace faulty injectors.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Turbocharger Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Worn or damaged turbocharger bearings lead to reduced engine power, noisy operation, and black smoke from the exhaust.
    * Solution: Replace the turbocharger.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $1,500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**3.5-Liter Gasoline Engine**
* **Ignition Coil Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Misfiring, rough idling, and reduced engine performance.
    * Solution: Replace the faulty ignition coil.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**5-Speed Automatic Transmission**
* **Shift Solenoid Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Harsh shifting, delayed engagement, or failure to shift gears.
    * Solution: Replace the faulty shift solenoid.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Torque Converter Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Shuddering or slipping during acceleration, loss of power, and abnormal transmission fluid leaks.
    * Solution: Replace the torque converter.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,200 - $1,800+
    * Recalls: None Found

**5-Speed Manual Transmission**
* **Clutch Disc Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears, slipping clutch, and grinding noises.
    * Solution: Replace the clutch disc.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $600 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Rear Leaf Spring Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Sagging or broken leaf springs cause reduced handling, uneven tire wear, and excessive body roll.
    * Solution: Replace the rear leaf springs.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Front Strut Wear:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Worn or damaged struts cause a rough ride, reduced handling, and uneven tire wear.
    * Solution: Replace the front struts.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $600+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Battery discharges quickly, even when the vehicle is not in use.
    * Solution: Identify and replace the faulty electrical component causing the drain.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the specific cause
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Alternator Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Low or no charging output, battery warning light illuminated, and electrical system malfunctions.
    * Solution: Replace the alternator.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $600+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Rust on Body Panels:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Surface rust or corrosion on body panels, particularly around the lower edges.
    * Solution: Repair rust-affected areas and apply anti-corrosion treatment.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the extent of damage
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Instrument Cluster Malfunction:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Inaccurate or intermittent readings, flickering gauges, or total failure of the cluster.
    * Solution: Replace the defective instrument cluster.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Climate Control System Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Malfunctioningエアコン, heating, or ventilation system, resulting in poor temperature control or air flow.
    * Solution: Diagnose and repair the faulty component within the climate control system.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the specific cause
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**
The 2011 Nissan Urvan is a reliable and practical van, but it has been plagued by several common issues. Engine problems, such as injector failure and turbocharger failure, can be costly to repair. Transmission issues, including shift solenoid failure and torque converter failure, can also cause drivability problems. Additionally, suspension and electrical components may experience premature wear or failure. Owners should be aware of these potential issues and budget accordingly for repairs.
