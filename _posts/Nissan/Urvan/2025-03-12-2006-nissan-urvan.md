The 2006 Nissan Urvan (also known as the Nissan Caravan in some markets) is a light commercial van known for its reliability and practicality. Common engine options include the 2.0L petrol engine and the 2.5L diesel engine. The 2.5L diesel engine produces around 87 horsepower and 133 lb-ft of torque. Owners often appreciate its cargo capacity and durability, while some dislike its basic features and relatively low power output. Transmission options include 5-speed manual and 4-speed automatic.

**Engine**

**2.5L Diesel**

*   **Fuel Injector Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rough idling, black smoke from exhaust, decreased fuel efficiency, engine misfires.
    *   Solution: Replace faulty fuel injectors.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Glow Plug Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Hard starting, especially in cold weather, white smoke upon starting, rough engine running when cold.
    *   Solution: Replace faulty glow plugs.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**4-Speed Automatic**

*   **Slipping or Delayed Shifting**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The transmission may hesitate or slip when shifting between gears. Gear changes may be harsh or delayed.
    *   Solution: A full transmission flush and filter replacement may help in early stages. If the problem persists, a transmission rebuild or replacement may be required.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 (flush) to $1500 - $3000+ (rebuild/replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Worn Out Bushings**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Clunking noises from the suspension, especially when going over bumps. Poor handling and instability.
    *   Solution: Replace worn suspension bushings (control arm bushings, sway bar bushings).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Alternator Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Battery light on the dashboard, dimming headlights, difficulty starting the engine, eventually leading to a dead battery.
    *   Solution: Replace the faulty alternator.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Faulty Starter Motor**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Clicking sound when trying to start the engine, engine not cranking, intermittent starting issues.
    *   Solution: Replace the faulty starter motor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Rust**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rust forming on the body panels, especially around wheel wells, door seams, and undercarriage.
    *   Solution: Repair or replace rusted body panels. Apply rust prevention treatments.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $1000+ (depending on severity)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Air Conditioning Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Air conditioning not blowing cold air, weak airflow, strange noises from the A/C compressor.
    *   Solution: Recharge refrigerant, repair or replace A/C compressor, check for leaks in the system.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 (recharge) to $400 - $800+ (compressor replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2006 Nissan Urvan is generally a reliable vehicle, but common issues include fuel injector issues in the diesel engine, transmission slipping, suspension wear, electrical component failures, rust, and air conditioning problems. The severity and cost of these issues can vary depending on the vehicle's condition and maintenance history. Engine and transmission repairs can be some of the more expensive fixes, while suspension and electrical repairs are typically more moderate. Rust can become a significant issue over time and lead to expensive bodywork.
