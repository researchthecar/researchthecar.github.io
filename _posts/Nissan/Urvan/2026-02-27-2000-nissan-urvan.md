# 2000 Nissan Urvan

### Overview
The 2000 Nissan Urvan, a versatile commercial van, is generally known for its robust build and utilitarian design, making it a popular choice for businesses and transport services. While it prioritizes durability and functionality over luxury, its mechanical components have demonstrated a good level of reliability for its intended purpose. Owners often praise its straightforward mechanics, which can contribute to easier and more cost-effective maintenance. However, like any vehicle of this age and type, certain areas may require attention over time, particularly those subjected to heavy use. This analysis focuses on common reported issues beyond standard wear and tear, providing insights into potential reliability concerns for the 2000 model year.

---


## Engine

### 2.7L TD27 Diesel
**Head Gasket Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Overheating, coolant loss, white smoke from exhaust, milky oil.
- **Solution:** Replacement of head gasket, potential machining of cylinder head.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1500

**Glow Plug Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Difficulty starting in cold weather, rough idling.
- **Solution:** Replacement of faulty glow plugs.
- **Cost:** $150 - $300


### 3.2L QD32 Diesel
**Injector Pump Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Rough idling, loss of power, black smoke, starting problems.
- **Solution:** Rebuilding or replacement of the injection pump.
- **Cost:** $700 - $1200

**Turbocharger Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of power, excessive smoke, whining noises from the engine bay.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the turbocharger unit.
- **Cost:** $1000 - $2000


## Transmission

### 5-Speed Manual Transmission
**Clutch Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Slipping clutch, difficulty shifting gears, burning smell.
- **Solution:** Replacement of clutch kit (clutch disc, pressure plate, release bearing).
- **Cost:** $500 - $900

**Synchronizer Ring Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Grinding noise when shifting gears, difficulty engaging gears.
- **Solution:** Internal transmission repair, replacement of synchronizer rings.
- **Cost:** $600 - $1100


### 4-Speed Automatic Transmission
**Torque Converter Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Slipping, shuddering, delayed engagement, fluid leaks.
- **Solution:** Rebuilding or replacement of the torque converter.
- **Cost:** $700 - $1300

**Transmission Fluid Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Puddles under the vehicle, low transmission fluid level, harsh shifting.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of seals and gaskets.
- **Cost:** $200 - $500


## Suspension

### Standard Chassis
**Leaf Spring Fatigue**
- **Symptoms:** Sagging rear end, harsh ride, clunking noises over bumps.
- **Solution:** Replacement of worn or broken leaf springs.
- **Cost:** $400 - $800

**Shock Absorber Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Bouncy ride, poor handling, excessive body roll.
- **Solution:** Replacement of shock absorbers.
- **Cost:** $200 - $400


## Electrical

### General Electrical System
**Alternator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Battery warning light illuminated, dimming headlights, electrical system malfunctions.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the alternator.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600

**Wiring Harness Corrosion**
- **Symptoms:** Intermittent electrical faults, non-functioning lights or accessories.
- **Solution:** Inspection and repair/replacement of corroded wiring sections.
- **Cost:** $200 - $500


## Exterior

### Body Panels and Trim
**Rust and Corrosion**
- **Symptoms:** Visible rust spots, particularly on wheel arches, sills, and door bottoms; perforation of metal.
- **Solution:** Rust treatment, panel repair, or replacement.
- **Cost:** $300 - $1500+

**Door Lock Actuator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Central locking system not working, doors not locking or unlocking remotely.
- **Solution:** Replacement of faulty door lock actuators.
- **Cost:** $150 - $300 per door


## Interior

### Seating and Upholstery
**Seat Frame Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Wobbly seats, torn upholstery, broken adjustment mechanisms.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of seat frames and mechanisms.
- **Cost:** $100 - $400 per seat


### HVAC System
**Blower Motor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** No airflow from vents, inconsistent fan speeds.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the blower motor.
- **Cost:** $200 - $400

### Summary Verdict
The 2000 Nissan Urvan is a workhorse vehicle that generally offers good reliability, especially concerning its core mechanicals. The diesel engines, while capable, can be prone to specific issues like head gasket problems or injector pump wear, particularly with high mileage or less-than-ideal maintenance. Transmission issues, both manual and automatic, often relate to clutch wear or torque converter problems, which are not uncommon for commercial vehicles. Suspension components like leaf springs and shocks are susceptible to wear due to load-carrying duties. Electrical gremlins and rust are also potential concerns typical for vehicles of this age and intended use. Overall, with diligent maintenance and prompt attention to reported issues, the Urvan can remain a dependable option.
