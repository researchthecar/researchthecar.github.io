# 2005 Nissan Micra

### Overview
The 2005 Nissan Micra, a popular subcompact car, generally offers a good level of reliability for its class. It's known for its affordability and practicality, making it a common choice for city driving and first-time car owners. While no vehicle is entirely without potential issues, the Micra from this era has a reputation for being relatively robust. Owners typically report satisfaction with its fuel efficiency and ease of use. However, as with any vehicle of this age, certain components may show wear, and specific model years or engine/transmission combinations can have unique points of attention. This analysis focuses on documented common faults beyond standard maintenance.

### 1.5L dCi (Diesel)
**Injector Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Rough idling, loss of power, increased fuel consumption, difficulty starting.
- **Solution:** Cleaning or replacement of faulty injectors.
- **Cost:** $300 - $800 per injector

**Turbocharger Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of power, whistling noises from the engine, black smoke from exhaust.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of the turbocharger.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1500

### 4-Speed Automatic
**Slipping Gears**
- **Symptoms:** Delayed engagement, engine revving without acceleration, harsh shifting.
- **Solution:** Transmission fluid flush and filter change, or transmission rebuild/replacement.
- **Cost:** $200 - $400 (fluid service), $1500 - $3000 (rebuild/replacement)


## Suspension

### Standard Independent Front / Torsion Beam Rear
**Worn Bushings and Ball Joints**
- **Symptoms:** Clunking noises over bumps, vague steering, uneven tire wear.
- **Solution:** Replacement of worn bushings and ball joints.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600


## Electrical

### General Electrical System
**Window Regulator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Power windows not operating, slow or jerky window movement.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the window regulator mechanism.
- **Cost:** $150 - $300 per window

**Alternator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Battery warning light illuminated, dimming headlights, starting problems.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the alternator.
- **Cost:** $400 - $700


## Exterior

### Body Panels and Trim
**Rust on Wheel Arches and Sills**
- **Symptoms:** Visible corrosion, bubbling paintwork, especially in areas with harsh winters.
- **Solution:** Rust treatment and repair, potentially panel replacement.
- **Cost:** $200 - $1000+


## Interior

### Cabin Components
**HVAC Fan Motor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** No airflow from vents, or intermittent fan operation.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the blower motor.
- **Cost:** $150 - $300

### Summary Verdict
The 2005 Nissan Micra is generally considered a reliable small car, particularly with its petrol engines. The 1.5L dCi diesel engine, while offering better fuel economy, is more prone to issues like injector and turbocharger problems. Automatic transmissions can sometimes develop slipping issues. Common wear items like suspension bushings and exterior rust are to be expected on a vehicle of this age, especially if used in challenging climates. Electrical components like window regulators and alternators can also fail. Overall, for its segment and age, the Micra offers a reasonable ownership experience, provided potential diesel-specific issues and general wear are addressed.
