**2015 Nissan Micra**

The 2015 Nissan Micra is a subcompact hatchback that was offered with two engine options: a 1.2-liter three-cylinder and a 1.6-liter four-cylinder. Both engines could be paired with either a five-speed manual transmission or a continuously variable transmission (CVT). The Micra was praised for its fuel efficiency, affordability, and maneuverability, but criticized for its lack of power and poor sound insulation.

**Engine**

* **1.2-liter Three-Cylinder**

    * **Head Gasket Failure:**
        * Description/Symptoms: Premature failure of the cylinder head gasket, leading to coolant leaks, overheating, and engine damage.
        * Solution: Replacement of the cylinder head gasket.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
    * **Intake Manifold Gasket Failure:**
        * Description/Symptoms: Loss of engine performance due to a failed gasket between the intake manifold and the engine block.
        * Solution: Replacement of the intake manifold gasket.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500

* **1.6-liter Four-Cylinder**

    * **Valve Cover Gasket Leak:**
        * Description/Symptoms: Oil leakage from the valve cover gasket, causing visible oil stains.
        * Solution: Replacement of the valve cover gasket.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300
    * **Timing Chain Tensioner Failure:**
        * Description/Symptoms: Noise from the engine at start-up or under load, indicating a faulty timing chain tensioner.
        * Solution: Replacement of the timing chain tensioner.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,200

**Transmission**

* **Five-Speed Manual Transmission**

    * **Difficulty Shifting Gears:**
        * Description/Symptoms: Difficulty engaging or disengaging gears, grinding noises.
        * Solution: Inspection and repair of the transmission.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+

* **Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)**

    * **Slipping:**
        * Description/Symptoms: Loss of power or acceleration due to slipping of the CVT belt.
        * Solution: Replacement or repair of the CVT belt.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,000+
    * **Noise:**
        * Description/Symptoms: Abnormal noises from the CVT, such as whining or grinding.
        * Solution: Inspection and repair of the CVT, potentially involving replacement of parts.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+

**Suspension**

* **Front MacPherson Struts:**
    * **Premature Strut Failure:**
        * Description/Symptoms: Clunking or rattling noises, reduced handling and stability.
        * Solution: Replacement of the front MacPherson struts.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800 per strut

**Electrical**

* **Rearview Camera Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Inoperable or blurry rearview camera display.
    * Solution: Replacement of the rearview camera assembly.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500
* **Battery Drain:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Rapid battery discharge, leading to difficulty starting the vehicle.
    * Solution: Inspection and repair of the electrical system to identify and eliminate battery drain.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+

**Exterior**

* **Wheel Bearing Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Noise from the wheels, especially when turning or accelerating.
    * Solution: Replacement of the affected wheel bearing.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $250 - $500 per bearing
    * **Recalls:** NHTSA Recall Number 15V-197; 16V-024
* **Body Panel Rust:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Development of rust spots on body panels, especially in areas with high moisture or salt exposure.
    * Solution: Repair or replacement of the affected body panels.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the extent of the damage

**Interior**

* **Steering Wheel Squeak:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Audible squeaking or creaking noise when turning the steering wheel.
    * Solution: Lubrication or replacement of the steering wheel clock spring.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300

**Summary**

The 2015 Nissan Micra has been reported to have some reliability issues, particularly with the engine, transmission, and wheel bearings. While most of these issues are not severe and can be fixed at a reasonable cost, the potential for major repairs such as head gasket failure or CVT replacement can be a concern. Overall, the Micra offers good fuel efficiency and affordability, but potential buyers should be aware of the potential repair costs associated with this vehicle.
