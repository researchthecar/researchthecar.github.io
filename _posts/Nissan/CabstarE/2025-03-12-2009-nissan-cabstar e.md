The 2009 Nissan Cabstar E is a light commercial vehicle known for its maneuverability and versatility in urban environments. Engine options typically include various diesel engines, such as the YD25DDTi 2.5-liter diesel, known for its reliability and fuel efficiency. Power output for the YD25DDTi ranges around 110-130 horsepower and 184-221 lb-ft of torque, depending on the specific tuning. Common transmission options are a 5-speed manual and a 5-speed automated manual (AMT). Owners often appreciate its compact size and payload capacity, but some dislike the relatively basic interior and potential for rust in certain climates.

**Engine**

**YD25DDTi 2.5L Diesel**

*   **Injector Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Poor starting, rough running, excessive smoke, and reduced power. Can lead to engine misfires and potential damage to the catalytic converter.
    *   Solution: Replace faulty injectors with new or refurbished units. It's advisable to replace all injectors at once for optimal performance.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $1200+ depending on the number of injectors and labor costs.

*   **Turbocharger Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, excessive black smoke, and a whining noise from the turbocharger. Can also trigger the engine management light.
    *   Solution: Replace the turbocharger with a new or refurbished unit. Inspect oil lines to the turbocharger to ensure proper lubrication.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $2000+ depending on the source of the turbocharger and labor costs.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**5-Speed Manual Transmission**

*   **Synchro Wear**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting into gears, especially 2nd and 3rd. Grinding noise when shifting.
    *   Solution: Rebuild the transmission with new synchros.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1500+ depending on the extent of the damage and labor costs.

*   **Clutch Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Slipping clutch, difficulty engaging gears, and a high clutch pedal.
    *   Solution: Replace the clutch disc, pressure plate, and release bearing. It is also recommended to resurface the flywheel.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1000+ depending on the parts used and labor costs.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**5-Speed Automated Manual Transmission (AMT)**

*   **AMT Actuator Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Failure to shift gears, error codes related to the AMT system, and potential immobilization of the vehicle.
    *   Solution: Replace or repair the AMT actuator. Ensure proper calibration of the system after replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1000 - $2500+ depending on the source of the actuator and labor costs.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Clutch Actuator Malfunction**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Jerky gear changes, clutch slipping, and error codes related to the AMT system.
    *   Solution: Replace or repair the clutch actuator. Check and adjust clutch parameters after replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $2000+ depending on the specific issue and labor costs.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Leaf Spring Sagging/Breakage**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Reduced ride height, poor handling, and potential cracking or breakage of the leaf springs. Common when heavily loaded.
    *   Solution: Replace the leaf springs with new or reinforced units. Consider upgrading the spring rate if the vehicle is frequently used for heavy loads.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1200+ depending on the number of springs replaced and labor costs.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Worn Shock Absorbers**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Bouncy ride, poor handling, and excessive body roll.
    *   Solution: Replace the shock absorbers. It is recommended to replace them in pairs.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600+ depending on the brand of shock absorbers and labor costs.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Wiring Harness Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Intermittent electrical problems, such as lights flickering, sensors malfunctioning, and difficulty starting. Often caused by corrosion or damage to the wiring harness.
    *   Solution: Inspect and repair or replace the damaged sections of the wiring harness. Use dielectric grease on electrical connectors to prevent corrosion.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1000+ depending on the extent of the damage and labor costs.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Alternator Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Battery not charging, dim headlights, and the battery warning light illuminating.
    *   Solution: Replace the alternator. Check the condition of the battery and drive belt.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+ depending on the brand of alternator and labor costs.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Rust**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rust forming around wheel arches, sills, and other areas prone to water and salt exposure.
    *   Solution: Sand down the affected areas, treat the rust, and repaint. Apply rust-proofing measures.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $2000+ depending on the extent of the rust and labor costs.

**Interior**

*   **Heater Blower Motor Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Heater only works on certain settings or not at all.
    *   Solution: Replace the heater blower motor resistor or the blower motor itself.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+ depending on the parts needed and labor costs.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2009 Nissan Cabstar E, while generally reliable, exhibits some common issues. Engine and transmission problems, particularly with the injectors and AMT system, can be costly. Suspension issues, especially with leaf springs, are also prevalent. Electrical and rust issues can further add to the maintenance costs. Overall, potential buyers should carefully inspect these areas and be prepared for potential repairs. The severity of issues can range from minor inconveniences to major repairs costing several thousand dollars.
