# 2021 Nissan 370Z

### Overview
The 2021 Nissan 370Z, a sports car known for its performance and engaging driving dynamics, generally exhibits good reliability. As one of the final model years for this generation, many of the initial kinks have been worked out. Owners typically report satisfaction with its mechanical robustness, particularly the powertrain. However, like any vehicle, there are specific areas where minor concerns have been noted, primarily related to the infotainment system and some interior components. The overall build quality is solid, contributing to a positive ownership experience for enthusiasts who appreciate its blend of power and handling.

## Interior

### Seat Upholstery
**Wear on Bolsters**
- **Symptoms:** Visible wear and tear on the driver's seat bolsters, especially on leather seats, due to ingress/egress.
- **Solution:** Professional cleaning, conditioning, or seat cover installation.
- **Cost:** $50 - $300


### Trim Pieces
**Minor Rattles**
- **Symptoms:** Occasional interior trim rattles, particularly around the dashboard or door panels.
- **Solution:** Locating and securing loose trim pieces, using sound-deadening material.
- **Cost:** $20 - $150

### Summary Verdict
The 2021 Nissan 370Z is a mechanically sound sports car with a reliable powertrain. The V6 engine and available transmissions are generally trouble-free. Most reported issues are minor and related to the infotainment system's occasional glitches or typical interior wear on high-contact areas like seat bolsters. Electrical components like the battery can be a point of concern, but this is not uncommon for vehicles of this age. Overall, the 370Z offers a dependable platform for driving enthusiasts, with most potential issues being manageable and not indicative of systemic failures.
