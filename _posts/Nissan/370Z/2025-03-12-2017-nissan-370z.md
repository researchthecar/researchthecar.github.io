The 2017 Nissan 370Z is a two-door sports car known for its sharp handling, potent V6 engine, and iconic design. It came standard with a 3.7-liter VQ37VHR V6 engine producing 332 horsepower and 270 lb-ft of torque (350 hp and 276 lb-ft in the NISMO trim). It was available with either a 6-speed manual transmission or a 7-speed automatic transmission. Owners appreciate its affordability, performance, and engaging driving experience, while some common criticisms include its dated interior, road noise, and relatively poor fuel economy.

**Engine**

**3.7L VQ37VHR V6**

*   **Oil Consumption**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported excessive oil consumption, requiring them to add oil between scheduled oil changes. Symptoms include low oil level warning lights, visible exhaust smoke (especially during startup), and the need to frequently top off the oil.
    *   Solution: The fix can range from replacing the PCV valve to more extensive engine work such as piston ring replacement, depending on the severity.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 (PCV valve) - $3,000+ (piston ring replacement).
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Timing Chain Tensioner Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Reports of premature timing chain tensioner failure. Symptoms include a rattling noise on startup, especially when the engine is cold. Can lead to more serious engine damage if left unattended.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the timing chain tensioner, guides, and potentially the chain itself.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**6-Speed Manual**

*   **Clutch Master Cylinder Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have experienced clutch master cylinder failure, leading to difficulty shifting gears or a soft/spongy clutch pedal feel. In some cases, the clutch may not disengage completely.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the clutch master cylinder and potentially the slave cylinder.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600
    *   Recalls: None Found

**7-Speed Automatic**

*   **Valve Body Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners report harsh or erratic shifting, slipping between gears, or delayed engagement. These issues can often be traced to problems within the valve body.
    *   Solution: Replacement or rebuild of the valve body. In some cases, a software update may resolve the issue.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $2,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **No Issues Found**

**Electrical**

*   **Battery Drain**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported issues with the battery draining quickly, especially if the car is not driven frequently. This can be due to a parasitic drain from an electrical component.
    *   Solution: Identifying and addressing the source of the parasitic drain, which could involve testing various electrical components.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+ depending on the complexity of the problem.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **TPMS Sensor Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Failure of the tire pressure monitoring system (TPMS) sensors. Symptoms include a TPMS warning light on the dashboard.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the faulty TPMS sensor(s).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $200 per sensor.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Door Lock Actuator Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The door lock actuator fails, preventing the door from locking or unlocking properly using the remote or interior switch.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the faulty door lock actuator.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $400
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Navigation/Infotainment System Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported issues with the factory navigation/infotainment system, including freezing, slow response times, or failure to boot up.
    *   Solution: In some cases, a software update may resolve the issue. More severe problems may require replacement of the unit.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 (software update) - $1,000+ (replacement unit)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2017 Nissan 370Z, while generally reliable, can experience some notable issues. Engine oil consumption and timing chain tensioner problems on the V6 can be costly if left unchecked. Transmission issues with both the manual and automatic options can affect the driving experience. Electrical glitches like battery drain and TPMS failures are fairly common and relatively inexpensive to fix. The severity and cost of these issues vary, but potential buyers should be aware of these common complaints and factor them into their purchasing decision.
