The 2013 Nissan 370Z is a popular sports car known for its performance and sharp handling. It's powered by a 3.7-liter V6 engine producing 332 horsepower and 270 lb-ft of torque in the base model and 350 horsepower in the Nismo trim. Transmission options include a 6-speed manual with SynchroRev Match and a 7-speed automatic. People love the Z for its engaging driving experience, powerful engine, and iconic styling. Common dislikes include a cramped interior, road noise, and some outdated interior features.

**Engine**

**3.7L VQ37VHR V6**
*   **Oil Consumption**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported excessive oil consumption, requiring frequent top-ups between oil changes. Symptoms include low oil levels, the need to add oil frequently, and potentially blue smoke from the exhaust.
    *   Solution: The underlying cause can vary, sometimes involving worn piston rings or valve stem seals. A compression test and leak-down test can help diagnose the source. Repairs could range from replacing valve stem seals to a full engine rebuild.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $5,000+ depending on the cause and extent of the repair.

*   **Camshaft Position Sensors Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Malfunction Indicator Lamp (MIL) illumination. Engine may run rough.
    *   Solution: Replace the faulty camshaft position sensor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $400
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**6-Speed Manual**
*   **SynchroRev Match Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported issues with the SynchroRev Match system, which can cause jerky downshifts or failure to engage.
    *   Solution: Recalibration or replacement of the SynchroRev Match system components may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300-$1,000+ depending on the specific components and labor.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**7-Speed Automatic**
*   **Valve Body Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Erratic shifting, slipping between gears, or harsh gear changes.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the valve body.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,200 - $2,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Rear Differential Bushings**
    * Description/Symptoms: Clunking noise from the rear end, especially when shifting gears or accelerating. Can also lead to vibrations at higher speeds.
    * Solution: Replacement of the worn differential bushings. Aftermarket bushings are available and can offer increased durability.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800

**Electrical**

*   **Steering Lock Malfunction**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Steering wheel lock malfunctions, preventing the car from starting. The steering wheel may become locked, and the ignition won't turn.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the steering lock actuator.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,500+ depending on dealer vs. independent shop.
    *   Recalls: There was a recall for Steering Lock, 11V516000.

*   **Fuel Level Sensor**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Inaccurate fuel gauge readings. The fuel gauge may display incorrect fuel levels or fluctuate erratically.
    *   Solution: Replacing the fuel level sensor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Door Handles**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The exterior door handles can become brittle and break, especially in colder climates.
    *   Solution: Replacing the door handle.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150-$300

**Interior**

*   **Navigation System Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The navigation system screen may go black or become unresponsive.
    *   Solution: Software update or replacement of the navigation unit.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200-$1,000+ depending on the solution.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Bluetooth Connectivity Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Problems connecting to Bluetooth devices or maintaining a stable connection.
    *   Solution: Software update or replacement of the Bluetooth module.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+ depending on the solution.

**Summary:**

The 2013 Nissan 370Z, while a sporty and fun-to-drive car, has some potential issues to be aware of. Engine oil consumption, steering lock malfunction, and transmission problems are some of the more significant concerns. The steering lock issue is a safety concern that may prevent the car from operating. The cost to address these issues can vary widely, but some, like engine work or transmission replacement, can be expensive. A thorough inspection before purchase is highly recommended.
