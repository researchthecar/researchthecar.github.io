# 2018 Nissan 370Z Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2018 Nissan 370Z carries forward the brand's iconic rear-wheel-drive sports car formula, powered by a spirited naturally aspirated V6. While owner reports to safety agencies are sparse for this model year, prospective buyers should still carefully inspect core mechanical systems and driver-assistance sensors during a pre-purchase inspection.

---

## Engine

### 3.7L V6 (VQ37VHR)

This 3.7-liter V6 engine produces approximately 332 horsepower and 270 lb-ft of torque, offering strong acceleration and a high-revving character. Overall mechanical durability is generally solid, though track use or deferred maintenance can occasionally lead to premature wear.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Manual Transmission

The traditional 6-speed manual features Nissan's SynchroRev Match technology for smoother downshifts. It provides an engaging driver experience, though clutch lifespan heavily depends on driving habits.

### 7-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 7-speed automatic transmission includes manual shift modes and paddle shifters for responsive cruising. It is a proven unit, but routine fluid changes are recommended to ensure long-term shifting smoothness.

## Electrical

### Front Radar Sensor

Electrical and sensor complaints are minimal, though advanced driver-assistance features can occasionally register false obstruction warnings.

**Front Radar Obstruction Warning**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may experience warning messages indicating that the front radar is unavailable or obstructed while operating on highways or bridges.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified technician inspect the radar sensor alignment, mounting brackets, and wiring harnesses for intermittent faults.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V153000: Air Bags:Side/Window](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=370Z&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** The driver's side curtain air bag may have been installed incorrectly during production, possibly affecting its deployment. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 214, "Side Impact Protection," and 226, "Ejection Mitigation." The incorrect curtain air bag installation can prevent.
- **What to do:** Nissan will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the curtain air bag, correcting its installation as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2018 Nissan 370Z is a straightforward, performance-focused sports car with very few documented owner complaints for this model year. Buyers should perform a thorough test drive, verify proper sensor operation, and check for any signs of heavy track use or neglect.
