# 2018 Nissan 370Z

### Overview
The 2018 Nissan 370Z, a sports coupe known for its performance-oriented design and V6 engine, generally presents a solid reliability record. As a model with a long-standing heritage, many of its core components have been refined over time. However, like any performance vehicle, specific areas can experience issues, particularly those related to its potent powertrain and sporty suspension. Owners should be aware of potential concerns, though widespread critical failures are not a dominant theme for this model year. This analysis focuses on documented technical issues beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential repair areas and their associated costs.

---


## Engine

### 3.7L V6 (VQ37VHR)
**Oil Consumption**
- **Symptoms:** Increased oil level checks required, potential for low oil warning lights.
- **Solution:** Monitor oil levels regularly, top up as needed. In severe cases, internal engine inspection may be required.
- **Cost:** $50 - $200 (for oil and filter)

**Variable Valve Event and Lift (VVEL) Actuator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Check engine light, rough idle, loss of power, potential stalling.
- **Solution:** Replacement of VVEL actuators and associated solenoids.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1500


## Transmission

### 7-Speed Automatic
**Hesitation or Jerking**
- **Symptoms:** Noticeable delays in gear changes, rough shifting, especially at lower speeds.
- **Solution:** Transmission fluid flush and replacement, software update, or internal transmission repair.
- **Cost:** $300 - $1000


### 6-Speed Manual
**Clutch Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Slipping clutch, difficulty engaging gears, burning smell.
- **Solution:** Clutch replacement (clutch disc, pressure plate, release bearing).
- **Cost:** $1000 - $2000


## Suspension

### Standard Sports Suspension
**Worn Sway Bar Links**
- **Symptoms:** Clunking or rattling noises over bumps, especially at lower speeds.
- **Solution:** Replacement of front or rear sway bar links.
- **Cost:** $150 - $300

### Summary Verdict
The 2018 Nissan 370Z offers a generally reliable experience for a sports car, with its robust 3.7L V6 engine being a strong point. The most commonly cited issues revolve around the VVEL system, which can trigger the check engine light and affect performance, and potential oil consumption that requires regular monitoring. Both the automatic and manual transmissions are generally dependable, though the manual clutch is a wear item that will eventually need replacement. Suspension components like sway bar links can wear out, leading to noise. Electrical, exterior, and interior components show good reliability with no widespread problems reported. Regular maintenance and prompt attention to any warning lights are key to maintaining the 370Z's performance and longevity.
