# 2017 Nissan 370Z Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2017 Nissan 370Z delivers classic rear-wheel-drive sports car performance with a naturally aspirated V6 engine. While driver reports indicate overall mechanical soundness, used car buyers should inspect specific mechanical subsystems like the manual clutch hydraulics and the convertible top mechanism prior to purchase.

---

## Engine

### 3.7L V6 (VQ37VHR)

The 3.7-liter V6 engine produces approximately 332 horsepower and 270 lb-ft of torque, offering brisk acceleration. It is generally regarded as a durable powerplant, though routine maintenance of cooling and ignition systems is key.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Manual

The 6-speed manual transmission features SynchroRev Match technology for smoother downshifts. Buyers should check for proper clutch pedal feel and hydraulic integrity.

**Clutch Slave Cylinder Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The hydraulic system becomes contaminated due to seal failure, preventing the driver from disengaging the clutch and shifting gears.
- **Solution:** Replace the failed slave cylinder and flush the hydraulic fluid system.

### 7-Speed Automatic

The 7-speed automatic transmission includes manual shift modes and paddle shifters. It provides smooth everyday operation when properly maintained.

## Exterior

### Convertible Top

Exterior components are generally robust, but convertible roadster models require careful inspection of the soft top fabric and frame mechanism.

**Convertible Top Bow and Bungee Cord Wear**
- **Symptoms:** The 5th bow bungee cords can fail, causing the convertible top to rub against the vehicle structure and fail to operate correctly.
- **Solution:** Inspect and replace worn bungee cords or alignment components to ensure proper clearance.

## Summary Verdict

The 2017 Nissan 370Z is an engaging sports car choice for enthusiasts, provided it has been properly maintained. Prospective buyers should thoroughly test manual transmission engagement and inspect convertible mechanisms on roadster trims to avoid unexpected out-of-pocket repairs.
