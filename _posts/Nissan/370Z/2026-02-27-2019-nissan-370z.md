# 2019 Nissan 370Z Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

Shopping for a used 2019 Nissan 370Z means looking into a classic sports car known for its engaging rear-wheel-drive dynamics and muscular V6 performance. While overall owner complaint volume is very low, prospective buyers should still carefully evaluate the vehicle's powertrain health and inspect for isolated driveability symptoms during a pre-purchase test drive.

---

## Engine

### 3.7L V6 (VQ37VHR)

The 3.7-liter V6 engine (VQ37VHR) produces approximately 332 horsepower and 270 lb-ft of torque, delivering robust acceleration and a high-revving sports car character. It is generally robust, though buyers should verify smooth cold-start behavior during an inspection.

**Cold Soak Acceleration Limitation**
- **Symptoms:** After sitting for hours or days, the vehicle may experience a severe lack of power upon startup, with the accelerator limited to roughly 5 mph and 1500 to 2000 RPM.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis often aligns with specific manufacturer technical service guidelines addressing electronic throttle control or engine control module calibration.

## Transmission

### 7-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 7-speed automatic transmission features manual shift modes and available paddle shifters for a responsive driving experience. Regular fluid maintenance helps ensure long-term durability.

### 6-Speed Manual Transmission

The traditional 6-speed manual transmission features Nissan's SynchroRev Match technology for smoother downshifts. It remains a favorite for enthusiast buyers seeking direct mechanical engagement.
