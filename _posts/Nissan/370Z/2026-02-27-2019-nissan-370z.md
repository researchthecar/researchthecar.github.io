# 2019 Nissan 370Z

### Overview
The 2019 Nissan 370Z, a sports car known for its performance and engaging driving dynamics, generally exhibits good reliability. As a model in its later production years, many of the initial kinks have been worked out. The V6 engine is a robust powerplant, and the manual transmission is a highlight for enthusiasts. However, like any vehicle, there are specific areas where owners have reported concerns. This analysis focuses on common mechanical and electrical issues, excluding routine maintenance and standard wear and tear, to provide a clear picture of the 370Z's long-term dependability.

---


## Engine

### 3.7L V6 (VQ37VHR)
**Oil Consumption**
- **Symptoms:** Higher than expected oil levels dropping between changes, especially under spirited driving.
- **Solution:** Regular oil level checks and top-offs. In severe cases, internal engine inspection may be required.
- **Cost:** $0 - $200 (for oil and checks)

**Exhaust Manifold Cracks**
- **Symptoms:** Exhaust leaks, ticking noises from the engine bay, particularly when cold.
- **Solution:** Replacement of cracked exhaust manifold(s).
- **Cost:** $400 - $1000


## Transmission

### 6-Speed Manual
**Clutch Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Slipping clutch, difficulty shifting gears, burning smell.
- **Solution:** Clutch replacement (clutch kit, flywheel, pressure plate).
- **Cost:** $800 - $1800

**Synchro Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Grinding when shifting into certain gears, difficulty engaging gears.
- **Solution:** Transmission internal repair or rebuild, focusing on worn synchronizers.
- **Cost:** $1500 - $3000

## Electrical

### Standard System
**Infotainment System Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Screen freezing, unresponsive touch controls, Bluetooth connectivity issues.
- **Solution:** Software updates, system reset, or potential head unit replacement.
- **Cost:** $100 - $800

**Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Difficulty starting, dead battery after short periods.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis of parasitic draw, replacement of faulty modules or battery.
- **Cost:** $200 - $600

### Summary Verdict
The 2019 Nissan 370Z is a generally reliable sports car, particularly for its powertrain. The 3.7L V6 engine is stout, though some owners report higher oil consumption under hard use and occasional exhaust manifold cracks. The manual transmission, while a joy to use, can experience clutch wear and synchro issues over time, which are common for performance-oriented manual gearboxes. The automatic transmission is typically trouble-free. Electrical gremlins, such as infotainment glitches and battery drain, are the most frequently cited non-wear-related issues. Overall, with diligent maintenance and awareness of potential minor concerns, the 370Z should provide a rewarding ownership experience for enthusiasts.
