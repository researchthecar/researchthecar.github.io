# 2013 Nissan 370Z

### Overview
The 2013 Nissan 370Z is a performance-oriented sports coupe known for its engaging driving dynamics and powerful V6 engine. As a model in its generation, it generally exhibits good reliability, particularly in its core mechanical components. However, like any vehicle, specific areas can experience issues over time. This analysis focuses on documented technical problems beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential concerns for owners and prospective buyers. The 370Z's enthusiast appeal often means it's driven enthusiastically, which can sometimes highlight inherent weaknesses if they exist.

---


## Engine

### 3.7L V6 (VQ37VHR)
**Oil Consumption**
- **Symptoms:** Higher than expected oil levels dropping between changes, potentially leading to low oil warnings.
- **Solution:** Regularly check oil levels and top up as needed. In some cases, internal engine modifications or addressing PCV system issues might be considered.
- **Cost:** $50 - $500 (for top-up or minor repairs)

**Variable Valve Event and Lift (VVEL) Actuator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Check Engine Light illumination, rough idle, loss of power, and potential stalling.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the VVEL actuator or associated components. This can be a labor-intensive repair.
- **Cost:** $800 - $2000

### Summary Verdict
The 2013 Nissan 370Z is generally a reliable sports car, with its VQ37VHR engine and transmission options proving robust for the most part. The primary documented concerns revolve around potential oil consumption and issues with the VVEL system, which can manifest as check engine lights and performance degradation. These are not universal but are noted enough to warrant attention. Other systems like suspension, electrical, exterior, and interior components show a good track record with no widespread critical failures reported, indicating solid build quality in these areas. Regular maintenance and prompt attention to any oil level drops or engine performance anomalies are key to ensuring long-term reliability.
