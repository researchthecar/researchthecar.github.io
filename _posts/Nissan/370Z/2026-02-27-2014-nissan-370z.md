# 2014 Nissan 370Z

### Overview
The 2014 Nissan 370Z is a performance-oriented sports coupe known for its engaging driving dynamics and powerful V6 engine. As a model in its generation, it generally exhibits good mechanical reliability, particularly in its core powertrain components. Owners often praise its sporty handling and the responsiveness of its engine. However, like any vehicle, certain areas can experience issues over time. This analysis focuses on documented technical reliability concerns beyond standard maintenance, providing insights into potential problem areas for prospective and current owners.

---


## Engine

### 3.7L V6 (VQ37VHR)
**Oil Consumption**
- **Symptoms:** Increased oil level checks required, potential blue smoke from exhaust under certain conditions.
- **Solution:** Monitor oil levels regularly. In severe cases, engine rebuild or replacement may be necessary.
- **Cost:** $1,500 - $4,000+

**Variable Valve Event and Lift (VVEL) Actuator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Check Engine Light illumination, rough idle, loss of power, potential stalling.
- **Solution:** Replacement of VVEL actuators and associated sensors.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1,800


## Transmission

### 7-Speed Automatic
**Hesitation/Jerking**
- **Symptoms:** Slight delay or abruptness during gear changes, particularly at lower speeds.
- **Solution:** Transmission fluid flush and filter replacement, software update, or internal transmission repair.
- **Cost:** $300 - $1,200

## Electrical

### Standard System
**Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Difficulty starting the vehicle, dead battery after sitting for a short period.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis of parasitic draw, replacement of faulty modules or components causing the drain.
- **Cost:** $200 - $600

**Infotainment System Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Screen freezing, unresponsive touch controls, Bluetooth connectivity issues.
- **Solution:** Software updates, system reset, or replacement of the head unit.
- **Cost:** $400 - $1,000

### Summary Verdict
The 2014 Nissan 370Z is generally a reliable sports car, with its VQ37VHR engine and manual transmission being particularly robust. The most commonly cited issues revolve around the engine's VVEL system and potential oil consumption, which require diligent monitoring. Automatic transmission models may experience some shifting anomalies. Electrical gremlins, such as battery drain and infotainment glitches, are also noted but are not typically catastrophic. Overall, with regular maintenance and attention to potential VVEL and oil level concerns, the 370Z should provide an enjoyable and dependable driving experience for enthusiasts.
