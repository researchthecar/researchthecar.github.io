**Introduction**
The 2015 Nissan 370Z is a two-door sports car available with either a 3.7L V6 engine producing 332 horsepower and 270 lb-ft of torque or a 3.7L V6 engine producing 350 horsepower and 276 lb-ft of torque paired with a 7-speed automatic transmission with Downshift Rev Matching or a 6-speed manual transmission with SynchroRev Match. The 370Z is praised for its agile handling, powerful engine, and sleek design, but some owners have reported various issues with the vehicle.

**Engine**
- **3.7L V6 Engine (332 hp)**
  - **Oil Leak from Oil Filter Adapter Gasket:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Owners may experience an oil leak from the oil filter adapter gasket, resulting in a noticeable loss of oil and potential engine damage.
    - Solution: Replacing the oil filter adapter gasket is necessary.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $300+
    - Recalls: None Found
  - **Timing Chain Stretch:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported issues with timing chain stretch, leading to rattling noises, engine performance issues, and even engine failure.
    - Solution: Replacing the timing chain and guides is essential to rectify the problem.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
    - Recalls: None Found
- **3.7L V6 Engine (350 hp)**
  - **No Issues Found**

**Transmission**
- **7-Speed Automatic Transmission with Downshift Rev Matching**
  - **Shifting Issues:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Owners may encounter rough or delayed shifting, especially when downshifting.
    - Solution: Updating the transmission software or replacing the transmission fluid and filter can often resolve the issue.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    - Recalls: None Found
- **6-Speed Manual Transmission with SynchroRev Match**
  - **Difficulty Shifting into Gear:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported issues with shifting gears, particularly into first or second gear.
    - Solution: Adjusting the clutch linkage or replacing the clutch components may fix the problem.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+
    - Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**
- **Front Suspension:**
  - **Strut Mount Bearing Failure:**
    - Description/Symptoms: The strut mount bearings can fail prematurely, leading to knocking or clunking noises when turning or hitting bumps.
    - Solution: Replacing the strut mount bearing assemblies is necessary.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+
    - Recalls: None Found
- **Rear Suspension:**
  - **No Issues Found**

**Electrical**
- **Battery:**
  - **Premature Battery Failure:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Some owners have experienced premature battery failure, causing difficulty starting the vehicle or complete electrical failure.
    - Solution: Replacing the battery is the recommended solution.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
    - Recalls: None Found
- **Alternator:**
  - **Alternator Failure:**
    - Description/Symptoms: A faulty alternator can fail to charge the battery, leading to electrical problems and potential breakdowns.
    - Solution: Replacing the alternator is necessary.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    - Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**
- **Body Panels:**
  - **Door Alignment Issues:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported issues with misaligned doors, resulting in difficulty closing or uneven gaps.
    - Solution: Adjusting the door hinges or door latch mechanisms may resolve the issue.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    - Recalls: None Found
- **Paint:**
  - **Paint Chipping or Fading:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Paint damage, chipping, or fading can occur due to various factors, including environmental conditions and improper maintenance.
    - Solution: Professional paint repair or repainting is necessary.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the severity of the damage
    - Recalls: None Found

**Interior**
- **Dashboard:**
  - **Rattling Dashboard:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported a rattling noise coming from the dashboard.
    - Solution: Identifying and securing any loose components or cushioning the dashboard may eliminate the noise.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+
    - Recalls: None Found
- **Seats:**
  - **Worn Seat Bolsters:**
    - Description/Symptoms: The side bolsters of the seats can show signs of wear or tear over time.
    - Solution: Repairing or replacing the seat covers or foam is usually necessary.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    - Recalls: None Found

**Overall Summary**
While the 2015 Nissan 370Z is a sought-after sports car, it has some reported issues that owners should be aware of. The engine concerns, such as oil leaks and timing chain issues, can be costly to repair. The transmission problems can also lead to inconvenience and increased maintenance. The electrical issues, suspension concerns, and interior issues are typically less severe but should still be considered. Despite these issues, many owners remain satisfied with the 370Z's performance and driving experience. It's important to weigh the potential costs and severity of these issues against the vehicle's overall desirability before making a purchase decision.
