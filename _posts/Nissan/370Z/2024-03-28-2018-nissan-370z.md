**Introduction:**
The 2018 Nissan 370Z is a two-door sports car with a front-engine, rear-wheel-drive layout. It's offered with two engine options, a 3.7-liter V6 and a 3.0-liter twin-turbocharged V6. The 3.7-liter engine produces 332 horsepower and 276 lb-ft of torque, while the 3.0-liter twin-turbocharged engine produces 400 horsepower and 350 lb-ft of torque. Both engines are paired with a 6-speed manual transmission or a 7-speed automatic transmission. The 370Z is praised for its handling, performance, and styling, but it's criticized for its cramped interior and lack of modern technology.

**Engine**
**3.7-liter V6**

* **Valve Cover Gasket Leak:** The valve cover gasket can leak oil, causing engine performance issues. Replacing the valve cover gasket is the solution, with an estimated cost of $200-$300.
* **Timing Chain Tensioner Failure:** In some cases, the timing chain tensioner can fail, leading to engine damage. Replacing the timing chain tensioner and timing chain is the solution, with an estimated cost of $1000-$1500.

* **Recalls:**
    * NHTSA Campaign Number: 20V395000 - Timing Chain Failure

**3.0-liter Twin-Turbocharged V6**

* **Turbocharger Wastegate Failure:** The turbocharger wastegate can fail, causing decreased engine performance. Replacing the turbocharger wastegate actuator is the solution, with an estimated cost of $500-$700.
* **Oil Leak:** Some vehicles may experience an oil leak from the oil pan gasket. Replacing the oil pan gasket is the solution, with an estimated cost of $200-$300.

* **Recalls:**
    * None Found

**Transmission**
**6-Speed Manual Transmission**

* **Shifting Issues:** Some vehicles may experience difficulty shifting gears or grinding noises when shifting. Replacing the transmission or transmission components may be necessary, with an estimated cost of $1500-$2500.

* **Recalls:**
    * None Found

**7-Speed Automatic Transmission**

* **Transmission Slipping:** Some vehicles may experience transmission slipping or shuddering during gear changes. Flushing the transmission fluid and replacing the transmission filter is often the solution, with an estimated cost of $200-$300.
* **Transmission Control Module Failure:** In some cases, the transmission control module may fail, causing transmission problems. Replacing the transmission control module is the solution, with an estimated cost of $500-$800.

* **Recalls:**
    * None Found

**Suspension**
* **Strut Mount Wear:** The strut mounts can wear out over time, causing noise and vibration. Replacing the strut mounts is the solution, with an estimated cost of $300-$500.

* **Recalls:**
    * None Found

**Electrical**
* **Battery Drain:** Some vehicles may experience a battery drain, especially when the car is parked. Identifying and fixing the source of the battery drain is the solution, with an estimated cost ranging from $100-$500 depending on the cause.

* **Recalls:**
    * None Found

**Exterior**
* **Weatherstripping Issues:** The weatherstripping around the doors and windows may become damaged, allowing water or wind to enter the vehicle. Replacing the weatherstripping is the solution, with an estimated cost of $100-$200.

* **Recalls:**
    * None Found

**Interior**
* **Climate Control Malfunction:** The climate control system may malfunction, causing the air conditioning or heating to not work properly. Replacing the climate control unit or components may be necessary, with an estimated cost of $500-$1000.

* **Recalls:**
    * None Found

**Summary:**
Overall, the 2018 Nissan 370Z has some common issues to watch out for, particularly with the engine, transmission, and electrical systems. The severity of these issues varies, with some being relatively minor and inexpensive to fix, while others can be more serious and costly. Potential buyers and owners should be aware of these potential issues and factor them into their decision-making process.
