**Introduction:**

The 2012 Nissan 370Z is a stylish and sporty coupe that offers a thrilling driving experience. It is powered by a 3.7L V6 engine that produces 332 horsepower and 270 lb-ft of torque. The engine is mated to a 6-speed manual transmission or a 7-speed automatic transmission. The 370Z is known for its sharp handling, responsive steering, and powerful engine. However, like any vehicle, it is not without its issues.

**Engine**

**3.7L V6**

* **Oil Consumption:** The 3.7L V6 engine is known to consume excessive amounts of oil, especially in vehicles with high mileage. Symptoms include low oil levels, blue smoke from the exhaust, and a ticking noise from the engine. The solution is to have the engine inspected and repaired as needed. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+
* **Timing Chain Rattle:** Some 370Zs with the 3.7L V6 engine experience a rattling noise from the timing chain. This can be caused by a stretched or worn timing chain or a faulty timing chain tensioner. The solution is to replace the affected parts. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
* **Throttle Position Sensor Failure:** The throttle position sensor is responsible for measuring the position of the throttle valve. A faulty sensor can cause the engine to run erratically or even stall. The solution is to replace the throttle position sensor. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+

**Transmission**

**6-Speed Manual**

* **Transmission Noise:** Some 370Zs with the 6-speed manual transmission experience a grinding or whining noise when shifting gears. This can be caused by worn or damaged gears or bearings. The solution is to have the transmission inspected and repaired as needed. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+
* **Clutch Slippage:** The clutch can slip or shudder when engaging, especially in vehicles with high mileage. This can be caused by a worn clutch disc or pressure plate. The solution is to replace the clutch. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+

**7-Speed Automatic**

* **Transmission Hesitation:** Some 370Zs with the 7-speed automatic transmission experience hesitation or jerking when shifting gears. This can be caused by a faulty transmission control module or a malfunctioning valve body. The solution is to have the transmission inspected and repaired as needed. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+
* **Transmission Fluid Leak:** Some 370Zs with the 7-speed automatic transmission experience a leak from the transmission pan gasket. This can cause the transmission to lose fluid and overheat. The solution is to replace the transmission pan gasket. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+

**Suspension**

* **Rear Suspension Noise:** Some 370Zs experience a clunking or rattling noise from the rear suspension. This can be caused by worn or damaged sway bar links or bushings. The solution is to replace the affected parts. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
* **Front Suspension Squeak:** Some 370Zs experience a squeaking noise from the front suspension when driving over bumps. This can be caused by worn or damaged control arm bushings. The solution is to replace the affected bushings. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain:** Some 370Zs experience a battery drain that can cause the vehicle to not start. This can be caused by a faulty alternator, a parasitic draw on the electrical system, or a failing battery. The solution is to have the electrical system inspected and repaired as needed. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+
* **Radio Malfunction:** Some 370Zs experience issues with the radio, such as loss of sound or display errors. This can be caused by a faulty radio unit or a malfunctioning amplifier. The solution is to replace the affected components. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+

**Exterior**

* **Hood Rattle:** Some 370Zs experience a rattling noise from the hood when driving over rough roads. This can be caused by a loose hood latch or misaligned hood hinges. The solution is to adjust the hood latch or hood hinges as needed. Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 - $200+
* **Antenna Trim Damage:** The antenna trim on some 370Zs is prone to damage from car washes or other sources. This can cause the antenna to fail or become inoperable. The solution is to replace the antenna trim. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+

**Interior**

* **Sun Visor Breakage:** The sun visors on some 370Zs are prone to breakage, especially at the hinge point. This can be caused by excessive force or a faulty design. The solution is to replace the affected sun visor. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $300+
* **Door Panel Squeak:** Some 370Zs experience a squeaking noise from the door panels when driving over rough roads. This can be caused by loose or worn door panel clips. The solution is to tighten or replace the affected clips. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+

**Summary:**

Overall, the 2012 Nissan 370Z is a reliable and enjoyable sports car. However, like any vehicle, it is not without its issues. The most common issues include oil consumption, transmission noise, and suspension noise. The severity of the issues can vary depending on the specific problem and the age and mileage of the vehicle. The cost of repairs can also vary significantly. Some issues are relatively inexpensive to fix, while others can be more costly.
