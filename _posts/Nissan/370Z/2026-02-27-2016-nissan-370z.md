# 2016 Nissan 370Z Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

Shopping for a used sports car like the Nissan 370Z offers an engaging rear-wheel-drive experience, but buyers should carefully evaluate powertrain durability and mechanical upkeep. While owner-reported complaints are relatively limited, specific areas like the hydraulic clutch system warrant a thorough pre-purchase inspection to avoid unexpected roadside surprises.

---

## Engine

### 3.7L V6 (VQ37VHR)

This naturally aspirated 3.7-liter V6 engine produces approximately 332 horsepower and 270 lb-ft of torque, delivering strong acceleration and classic sports car performance. It is generally robust, though regular maintenance of fluid levels and cooling systems is essential for long-term reliability.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Manual Transmission

The traditional six-speed manual gearbox features an available synchrorev match system for smoother downshifts. Buyers should inspect the clutch hydraulics closely for leaks or loss of pedal pressure during a test drive.

**Clutch Pedal Loss of Pressure**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may find the clutch pedal sinking completely to the floor with no pressure, often accompanied by a dry clutch fluid reservoir.
- **Solution:** Inspect the master and slave cylinders for leaks, refill and bleed the hydraulic fluid system, and replace failing hydraulic components as needed.

### 7-Speed Automatic Transmission

The conventional seven-speed automatic transmission includes manual shift modes and paddle shifters for a sportier feel. It generally operates reliably when fluid changes are performed at recommended intervals.
