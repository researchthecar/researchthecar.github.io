The 2015 Nissan 370Z is a two-door sports car known for its performance and sharp handling. It's powered by a 3.7-liter V6 engine producing 332 horsepower and 270 lb-ft of torque (or 350 horsepower and 276 lb-ft of torque in the NISMO variant). Transmission options include a 6-speed manual and a 7-speed automatic. Enthusiasts appreciate its engaging driving experience, powerful engine, and relatively affordable price point. Common dislikes include a cramped interior, dated infotainment system, and somewhat harsh ride quality.

**Engine**

**3.7L VQ37VHR V6**

*   **Oil Consumption**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported excessive oil consumption, requiring frequent top-offs between oil changes. The oil light may illuminate prematurely.
    *   Solution: In some cases, a PCV valve replacement helps. More severe cases may require piston ring replacement or engine rebuild.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $150 (PCV valve), $3,000 - $7,000+ (Engine Rebuild).
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Camshaft Position Sensor Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The car may experience rough idling, stalling, reduced engine power, and the check engine light will illuminate. Diagnostic codes may indicate a camshaft position sensor issue.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the faulty camshaft position sensor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $350
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**6-Speed Manual**

*   **Clutch Master Cylinder Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting, clutch pedal sticking to the floor, or a spongy clutch pedal feel.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the clutch master cylinder.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
    *   Recalls: None Found

**7-Speed Automatic**

*   **Valve Body Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Harsh or erratic shifting, slipping between gears, or delayed engagement.
    *   Solution: Valve body replacement or rebuild. In some cases, a software update may improve shifting behavior.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $2,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Rear Differential Bushing Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Clunking noise from the rear end, especially during acceleration or deceleration. The car may feel loose or unstable, particularly during cornering.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the rear differential bushings.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **IPDM (Intelligent Power Distribution Module) Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Intermittent electrical issues, such as the car not starting, lights flickering, or various sensors malfunctioning.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the IPDM.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $700
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **TPMS Sensor Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The TPMS (Tire Pressure Monitoring System) light illuminates on the dashboard, even when tire pressures are within the correct range.
    * Solution: Replacement of the faulty TPMS sensor(s).
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $200 per sensor
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Door Handle Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The door handle may become loose, break, or fail to open the door.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the faulty door handle assembly.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Navigation System Failure (If Equipped)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The navigation screen may freeze, become unresponsive, or display incorrect information.
    *   Solution: Software update (if available) or replacement of the navigation unit.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200 (Software update), $500 - $1500+ (Replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Bluetooth Connectivity Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: Difficulty pairing devices, frequent disconnections, or poor audio quality during phone calls.
    * Solution: Software update (if available), or replacement of the head unit.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 (Software update), $300-$1000+ (Replacement Head Unit)
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

The 2015 Nissan 370Z, while a fun and sporty vehicle, does have some common issues. Engine oil consumption and clutch master cylinder failure appear fairly frequently. Electrical issues with the IPDM and TPMS sensors are also noted. Suspension issues, such as the differential bushings, can also be a concern. While some fixes are relatively inexpensive, engine repairs can be costly. Potential buyers should be aware of these issues and carefully inspect the vehicle's maintenance history before purchasing.
