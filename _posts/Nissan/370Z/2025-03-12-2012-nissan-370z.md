The 2012 Nissan 370Z is a popular sports car known for its performance and aggressive styling. It came standard with a 3.7-liter V6 engine (VQ37VHR) producing 332 horsepower and 270 lb-ft of torque, or 350 horsepower and 276 lb-ft of torque in the NISMO trim. It was offered with either a 6-speed manual transmission with SynchroRev Match or a 7-speed automatic transmission. People generally like the 370Z for its engaging driving experience, powerful engine, and relatively affordable price point for a sports car. Some common dislikes include the road noise, limited cargo space, and dated interior.

**Engine**

**3.7L VQ37VHR V6**

*   **Oil Consumption**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported excessive oil consumption, requiring frequent top-offs between oil changes. This can manifest as the oil level dropping significantly over a few thousand miles, potentially leading to engine damage if not monitored.
    *   Solution: Monitoring oil levels frequently and addressing potential leaks. Switching to a different oil viscosity might help in some cases. In severe instances, engine repair or replacement may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $7,000+ depending on severity.

**Transmission**

**6-Speed Manual**
*   **SynchroRev Match Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported the SynchroRev Match system malfunctioning, leading to jerky downshifts or failure to engage properly.
    *   Solution: Calibration or replacement of the SynchroRev Match system components.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,000+

**7-Speed Automatic**
*   **Valve Body Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners report issues with the transmission valve body, leading to harsh shifting, slipping, or failure to shift properly.
    *   Solution: Replacement or rebuild of the transmission valve body.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $2,000+

**Suspension**

*   **Rear Differential Bushings**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Degradation of the rear differential bushings can lead to clunking noises from the rear of the vehicle, especially during acceleration or deceleration.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the rear differential bushings.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500

**Electrical**

*   **Door Lock Actuators Failing**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The door lock actuators can fail, resulting in doors that won't lock or unlock with the key fob or interior switch.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the faulty door lock actuator.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $400 per door

**Interior**

*   **Dashboard Cracking/Melting**
    *   Description/Symptoms: In hot climates, some owners have reported the dashboard developing cracks or becoming sticky/melted.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the dashboard (expensive) or use of a dashboard cover.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,500+

**Summary**

The 2012 Nissan 370Z has some common issues, with the most concerning being potential engine oil consumption and automatic transmission valve body problems. The dashboard issue, while not impacting performance, is a common cosmetic complaint. The severity of these issues can range from minor inconveniences to major repairs. Addressing these potential problems proactively can help ensure the long-term reliability of the vehicle.
