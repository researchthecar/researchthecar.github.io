The 2019 Nissan 370Z is a rear-wheel-drive sports car known for its sharp handling and powerful engine. It came standard with a 3.7-liter V6 engine producing 332 horsepower and 270 lb-ft of torque, or 350 horsepower and 276 lb-ft of torque in the Nismo trim. Transmission options included a 6-speed manual and a 7-speed automatic. Owners generally like the 370Z's performance and affordability, but some dislike its dated interior and limited practicality.

**Engine**

**3.7L V6**

* **Oil Consumption**
    * Description/Symptoms: Some owners report excessive oil consumption, requiring frequent top-offs between oil changes. This can lead to low oil levels and potential engine damage if not monitored.
    * Solution: Monitor oil levels regularly. Consider using a thicker oil or having the engine inspected for leaks or internal wear. More drastic measures could include piston ring replacement if the consumption is severe and linked to piston ring issues.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies greatly depending on the cause. From $100 for an oil change to $3,000+ for engine repairs.
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Camshaft Position Sensor Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The camshaft position sensors can sometimes fail, leading to a check engine light and rough running, stalling, or difficulty starting the engine.
    * Solution: Replace the faulty camshaft position sensor(s).
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $150-$400
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**6-Speed Manual**

* **Clutch Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Some owners have experienced premature clutch failure, especially with aggressive driving. Symptoms include slipping, difficulty shifting, and unusual noises.
    * Solution: Replace the clutch assembly.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**7-Speed Automatic**

* **Valve Body Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: Some owners report hard shifting, delayed engagement, or slipping, especially in lower gears. These issues can be caused by a faulty valve body.
    * Solution: Replace or rebuild the valve body.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $2,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **No Issues Found**

**Electrical**

* **Door Lock Actuator Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Door lock actuators can fail, causing the doors to not lock or unlock properly with the key fob or interior switch.
    * Solution: Replace the faulty door lock actuator.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200-$400 per door.
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Paint Peeling (Rare)**
    * Description/Symptoms: Though less common on the 2019 model, some owners have reported premature paint peeling, especially on the roof or hood.
    * Solution: Repainting the affected areas.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500+ depending on the area being repainted.
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Navigation System Issues (If Equipped)**
    * Description/Symptoms: Navigation system can freeze, become unresponsive, or display inaccurate information.
    * Solution: Software update, or replacement of the navigation unit if necessary.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 (Software Update) - $1000+ (Replacement)
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2019 Nissan 370Z is a sporty car that provides great performance for a reasonable cost. The most common issues revolve around potential oil consumption in the engine and possible valve body issues in the 7-speed automatic transmission. Clutch failure in the 6-speed manual is also something to be aware of, particularly if purchasing a used vehicle where the previous owner may have driven the vehicle aggressively. Electrical issues like door lock actuator failures can also occur. While some paint issues have been reported, this seems to be a less prevalent problem on the 2019 model year. The severity and cost of repairs can vary greatly depending on the specific issue and the chosen repair method.
