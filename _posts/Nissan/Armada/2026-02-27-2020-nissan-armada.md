# 2020 Nissan Armada Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2020 Nissan Armada, buyers get a traditional, rugged full-size SUV powered by a robust V8 engine. While overall owner satisfaction leans toward its towing capacity and spacious cabin, prospective buyers should review reported electrical anomalies and driver-assist system quirks before purchasing.

---

## Engine

### 5.6L V8 Endurance Engine

The 5.6-liter Endurance V8 engine produces 390 horsepower and 394 lb-ft of torque, providing strong acceleration and towing capability. It is generally regarded as a durable powerplant, though some owners have noted isolated electrical and starting concerns.

## Transmission

### 7-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 7-speed automatic transmission delivers power smoothly to either the rear wheels or the available four-wheel-drive system. It operates reliably under normal driving conditions without widespread mechanical failure patterns.

## Brakes

### Brake Light Switch

Brake system feedback is generally minimal, though isolated component switches may require replacement over time.

**Faulty Brake Light Switch**
- **Symptoms:** Brake lights may fail to illuminate properly or cause recurring dashboard warnings.
- **Solution:** Replace the faulty brake light switch unit.

## Electrical

### Battery and Charging System

Electrical complaints center primarily around unexpected battery drain and starting difficulties reported by some owners.

**Unexplained Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may fail to start unexpectedly due to premature battery depletion, sometimes accompanied by warning lights.
- **Solution:** Have the battery, alternator, and parasitic draw tested by a qualified technician, and replace components as necessary.

## Safety

### Forward Collision Avoidance and BCI

Safety and driver-assist complaints occasionally note erratic behavior from collision avoidance and blind-spot intervention systems.

**Blind Collision Intervention (BCI) Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may experience unexpected warnings, flashing dashboard alerts, or sudden brake engagement.
- **Solution:** Inspect sensor alignment and software calibration at a certified dealership or service center.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V373000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump:Control/Drive Module](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=Armada&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** Fuel pump failure can cause an engine stall, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the fuel pump module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V455000: Equipment:Other:Labels](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=Armada&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 110, "Tire Selection and Rims." An overloaded vehicle can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will install new labels, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2020 Nissan Armada offers traditional full-size SUV capability with a powerful V8 engine, but buyers should thoroughly inspect the electrical charging system and test drive to ensure driver-assist safety features operate smoothly.
