# 2019 Nissan Armada Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2019 Nissan Armada is a full-size, truck-based SUV offering robust towing capabilities and a spacious cabin. While it delivers strong performance from its standard V8 engine, prospective buyers should review recurring owner complaints regarding electrical system glitches, battery drain issues, and safety sensor warnings.

---

## Engine

### 5.6L V8 Engine

Equipped with a 5.6-liter V8 engine producing around 390 horsepower, this powertrain provides strong acceleration and towing capacity. While mechanically robust in most units, isolated reports indicate potential internal wear or ticking noises that require careful pre-purchase inspection.

**Engine Ticking and Internal Damage**
- **Symptoms:** Ticking noise emanating from the engine bay, accompanied by metal debris in oil inspections and potential cylinder damage.
- **Solution:** Requires professional inspection; severe cases may necessitate engine replacement or cylinder head repair.

## Transmission

### 7-Speed Automatic Transmission

Paired with a 7-speed automatic transmission available in rear-wheel or four-wheel drive, the drivetrain generally operates smoothly. Owners occasionally report shifter noises, but major mechanical failures are infrequent.

## Suspension

### Suspension & Shocks

Suspension components provide a comfortable ride when functioning properly, but rear suspension sagging and bouncy handling have been reported by owners.

**Rear Air Suspension Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Rear end fails to raise to the proper ride height, leading to excessive bounciness, poor handling, and unstable vehicle control on wet roads.
- **Solution:** Inspect factory shocks, air compressors, and suspension components for leaks or mechanical failure, replacing worn parts as necessary.

## Brakes

### Driver Assistance & BCI Sensors

Advanced safety features and collision intervention systems can occasionally trigger false warnings or sensor faults, impacting drivability.

**Backup Collision Intervention (BCI) Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Intermittent dashboard warning lights for BCI and automatic emergency braking, sometimes placing the vehicle in limp mode or engaging brakes unexpectedly.
- **Solution:** Have the dealer or a qualified technician diagnose sensor modules, brake switches, and body control modules (BCM).

## Electrical

### Electrical System & Battery

The electrical system is a common focal point for owner complaints, particularly concerning unexplained battery drains and charging anomalies.

**Battery Drain and Starting Failures**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle fails to start intermittently, battery drains completely while parked, and dashboard displays power issues despite a healthy battery test.
- **Solution:** Check charging systems, replace faulty batteries, and ensure any related engine control module (ECM) updates have been applied.
- **Cost:** $200

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V654000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=Armada&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 111, "Rear Visibility." The lack of an image in the back-up camera display increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2019 Nissan Armada is a capable and powerful full-size SUV that appeals to buyers needing substantial towing capacity and passenger space. However, used-car shoppers should thoroughly inspect the vehicle's electrical system for battery drain issues, verify that all safety sensors and driver-assistance features operate correctly, and check the rear suspension for signs of excessive wear or failure.
