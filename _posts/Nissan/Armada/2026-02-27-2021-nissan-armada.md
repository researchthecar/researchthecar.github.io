# 2021 Nissan Armada Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2021 Nissan Armada, buyers will find a robust full-size SUV powered by a strong V8 engine. While most owners enjoy its spacious interior and towing capability, owner reports highlight a few potential trouble spots—ranging from electrical and battery concerns to occasional engine stalling and transmission complaints. Prospective buyers should thoroughly inspect the electrical system and service history before purchasing.

---

## Engine

### 5.6L V8 Engine

The 2021 Nissan Armada comes equipped with a 5.6-liter Endurance V8 engine producing around 400 horsepower and 413 lb-ft of torque. It provides stout towing capacity and solid performance, though some owners have reported isolated incidents of unexpected stalling and electrical charging concerns.

**Engine Stalling and Starting Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicles may experience unexpected engine stalling while driving or bucking and hesitation after refueling, sometimes accompanied by low-voltage battery warnings.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified mechanic inspect the charging system, battery health, and fuel delivery components to isolate the root electrical or fuel pump cause.

## Transmission

### 7-Speed Automatic Transmission

Paired exclusively with the V8 engine, the 7-speed automatic transmission routes power smoothly to either the rear wheels or the intelligent four-wheel-drive system. While generally durable, a small number of high-mileage owners have reported severe internal transmission failures.

**Transmission Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may notice sudden powertrain malfunctions, slipping, or complete transmission failure requiring a costly replacement at higher mileages.
- **Solution:** Verify regular transmission fluid maintenance services have been performed and test-drive the vehicle carefully to check for smooth shifting behavior.

## Electrical

### Battery and Charging System

The electrical architecture supports numerous advanced convenience and safety features, but owner reports indicate that battery longevity and sporadic electrical issues can occasionally cause frustration.

**Premature Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have experienced dead batteries leaving them stranded, requiring multiple battery replacements within a short ownership period.
- **Solution:** Test the alternator output and check for parasitic draw on the electrical system if recurring battery drain occurs.

## Safety

### Forward Collision Avoidance

Advanced safety systems are designed to protect occupants, though sensor calibration sensitivities can occasionally lead to unexpected driver assistance interventions.

**Unintended Emergency Braking**
- **Symptoms:** The automated emergency braking system may occasionally trigger false positives, causing the vehicle to brake abruptly while driving with no obstructions nearby.
- **Solution:** Ensure camera and radar sensors are clean and free of debris, and consult a dealer for any applicable software updates or sensor recalibrations.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V373000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump:Control/Drive Module](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=Armada&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** Fuel pump failure can cause an engine stall, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the fuel pump module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V455000: Equipment:Other:Labels](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=Armada&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 110, "Tire Selection and Rims." An overloaded vehicle can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will install new labels, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2021 Nissan Armada remains an appealing choice for buyers needing a capable, three-row family SUV with traditional V8 performance. While most vehicles operate reliably, used car shoppers should pay close attention to battery health, service records, and transmission shifting behavior during a pre-purchase inspection to avoid potential costly surprises.
