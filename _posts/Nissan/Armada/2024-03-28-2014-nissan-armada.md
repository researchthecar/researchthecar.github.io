**2014 Nissan Armada**

The 2014 Nissan Armada is a full-size SUV that offers a combination of power and space. It comes with a single engine option, a 5.6L V8 that produces 317 horsepower and 385 lb-ft of torque, paired with a 7-speed automatic transmission. The Armada is known for its spacious interior, comfortable ride, and towing capabilities. However, like any vehicle, it has its share of issues.

**Engine**

* **5.6L V8**

**Timing Chain Tensioner Failure**
Description/Symptoms: The timing chain tensioner may fail, causing the timing chain to skip or break. Symptoms include a rattling or whining noise from the engine, loss of power, and difficulty starting.
Solution: Replace the timing chain tensioner and timing chain.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+
Recalls: None Found

**Intake Manifold Gasket Leak**
Description/Symptoms: The intake manifold gasket may leak, causing a loss of coolant. Symptoms include a low coolant level, overheating, and reduced engine performance.
Solution: Replace the intake manifold gasket.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

* **7-Speed Automatic**

**Torque Converter Shudder**
Description/Symptoms: The torque converter may shudder when shifting gears. This can be felt as a vibration or shaking sensation.
Solution: Replace the torque converter.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500
Recalls: None Found

**Valve Body Failure**
Description/Symptoms: The valve body, which controls the flow of transmission fluid, may fail. Symptoms include delayed or harsh shifting, slipping gears, and loss of power.
Solution: Replace the valve body.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000
Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **No Issues Found**

**Electrical**

* **Alternator Failure**
Description/Symptoms: The alternator may fail, causing the battery to not charge properly. Symptoms include dim headlights, a flickering dashboard, and difficulty starting the vehicle.
Solution: Replace the alternator.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **No Issues Found**

**Interior**

* **Climate Control Malfunction**
Description/Symptoms: The climate control system may malfunction, causing the temperature to not adjust properly. Symptoms include cold air blowing when heat is desired or vice versa.
Solution: Replace the climate control module.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2014 Nissan Armada has several common issues, including timing chain tensioner failure, intake manifold gasket leak, torque converter shudder, valve body failure, and alternator failure. While some of these issues are relatively minor and inexpensive to fix, others can be more severe and costly. Overall, the Armada is a reliable vehicle, but it is important to be aware of potential issues before purchasing one.
