**Introduction**

The 2012 Nissan Armada is a full-size SUV that offers a comfortable ride and ample cabin space. It is powered by a 5.6-liter V8 engine that produces 317 horsepower and 385 lb-ft of torque, paired with a 5-speed automatic transmission. Other engine and transmission options are not available for the 2012 model year.

Owners praise the Armada for its spacious interior, strong engine performance, and excellent towing capacity. However, some complaints have been reported regarding certain components.

**Engine**

**5.6L V8**

* **Oil Consumption:**
Description/Symptoms: Excessive oil consumption, leading to low oil levels and potential engine damage.
Solution: Replace piston rings and valve stem seals.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**5-Speed Automatic**

* **Slipping Gears:**
Description/Symptoms: Transmission slipping out of gear during acceleration or shifting.
Solution: Overhaul or replace transmission.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+
Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Rear Suspension Sagging:**
Description/Symptoms: Sagging or uneven ride height at the rear of the vehicle.
Solution: Replace rear suspension springs.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain:**
Description/Symptoms: Battery losing power prematurely, leading to starting issues.
Solution: Replace battery or identify and fix parasitic drain.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
Recalls: None Found

* **Faulty Ignition Coils:**
Description/Symptoms: Engine misfiring, loss of power, and check engine light illumination.
Solution: Replace ignition coils.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Rust on Frame:**
Description/Symptoms: Premature rusting of the vehicle's frame.
Solution: Affected vehicles are eligible for a frame replacement program.
Estimated Cost to Fix: Covered by Nissan under warranty
Recalls: NHTSA Campaign ID: 17V-368

* **Condensation in Taillights:**
Description/Symptoms: Moisture buildup inside the taillights.
Solution: Replace taillights.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Faulty Climate Control:**
Description/Symptoms: Climate control system malfunctioning, resulting in incorrect temperature regulation.
Solution: Replace climate control module.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2012 Nissan Armada has received mixed reviews, with some owners experiencing issues such as oil consumption, transmission slipping, and faulty ignition coils. However, the severity of these issues varies, and some vehicles may not be affected at all. The estimated cost to fix the reported issues ranges from $100 to $4,000+, depending on the specific problem and the repair method used. It is advisable for owners to regularly maintain their Armada and address any issues promptly to minimize potential expenses and ensure a safe and reliable driving experience.
