# 2017 Nissan Armada

### Overview
The 2017 Nissan Armada is a full-size SUV known for its robust towing capacity and spacious interior. Powered by a potent V8 engine, it offers strong performance for its class. Reliability for the 2017 model year is generally considered good, with owners reporting satisfaction with its durability and capability. While no vehicle is entirely without potential issues, the Armada tends to hold up well under typical use. Common areas of concern, though not widespread, can include certain electronic features and occasional transmission quirks, but overall, it presents a solid option for those seeking a capable and dependable large SUV.

## Electrical

### Infotainment System
**Screen Freezing or Unresponsiveness**
- **Symptoms:** The infotainment screen may occasionally freeze, become unresponsive to touch, or reboot unexpectedly.
- **Solution:** Software update for the infotainment system, or a hard reset of the unit.
- **Cost:** $100 - $250


### Battery Drain
**Intermittent Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Some owners have reported intermittent battery drain issues, leading to a dead battery.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis to identify parasitic draw from specific modules or accessories, followed by repair or replacement of faulty components.
- **Cost:** $200 - $600

### Summary Verdict
The 2017 Nissan Armada demonstrates solid overall reliability, particularly with its powerful 5.6L V8 engine and robust chassis. The primary areas where minor issues have been noted are the transmission, with occasional reports of hesitation, and the electrical system, specifically concerning the infotainment screen and potential battery drain. These issues are not pervasive and often addressable with software updates or standard diagnostic procedures. For a large, capable SUV, the Armada offers a dependable ownership experience with a few predictable, albeit minor, potential concerns.
