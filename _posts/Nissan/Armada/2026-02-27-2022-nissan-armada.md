# 2022 Nissan Armada Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2022 Nissan Armada, buyers generally find a robust full-size SUV powered by a strong V8 engine. While most owners enjoy its spacious interior and towing capability, owner reports to federal safety regulators highlight a few recurring electrical, safety, and cooling concerns that are worth noting during your pre-purchase inspection.

---

## Engine

### 5.6L Endurance V8

The 5.6-liter Endurance V8 engine produces approximately 400 horsepower and 413 lb-ft of torque, providing strong acceleration and towing capacity. This powerplant is generally dependable, though isolated owner reports mention parasitic draws and sensor-related electrical integration issues.

## Transmission

### Radiator Fan Clutch

Engine cooling relies on a mechanical fan clutch setup that occasionally experiences premature wear or sticking.

**Engaged Fan Clutch**
- **Symptoms:** The radiator fan remains constantly engaged, which can sluggishly reduce acceleration responsiveness and alter vehicle performance.
- **Solution:** Inspect and replace a faulty fan clutch assembly as necessary.

### 7-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 7-speed automatic transmission routes power smoothly to either the rear wheels or the intelligent four-wheel-drive system. It generally operates reliably with no widespread complaint patterns documented for this model year.

## Electrical

### Battery and Charging System

Electrical system complaints center around parasitic drains that prematurely exhaust replacement batteries.

**Unexplained Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle experiences repeated battery drains, resulting in a hard-start or no-start condition even after replacing the battery.
- **Solution:** Perform a parasitic draw test to isolate electrical modules or components keeping the system awake while parked.

### Infotainment and Instrument Cluster

Digital displays and interior electronics can occasionally suffer from intermittent blackouts or communication errors.

**Dashboard and Screen Blanking**
- **Symptoms:** The dashboard, infotainment screen, and climate controls suddenly go blank or trigger erratic warning lights and buzzing.
- **Solution:** Check for software updates, module glitches, or loose wiring harness connections at the dealership.

## Safety

### Forward Collision Avoidance

Driver-assist features add helpful protection but can occasionally exhibit calibration sensitivity leading to false triggers or delayed reactions.

**Phantom Braking or Failure to Alert**
- **Symptoms:** The automated emergency braking system may either fail to activate during an obstruction or unexpectedly slam on the brakes without an immediate hazard.
- **Solution:** Have the radar sensors, forward-facing camera alignment, and related collision modules inspected and recalibrated.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V455000: Equipment:Other:Labels](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=Armada&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 110, "Tire Selection and Rims." An overloaded vehicle can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will install new labels, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2022 Nissan Armada is a capable and comfortable full-size SUV, but prospective buyers should thoroughly test all electrical systems and driver-assist safety tech during a test drive. Verifying battery health, checking for software updates, and ensuring the pre-purchase inspection covers potential parasitic draws will help secure a reliable ownership experience.
