**Introduction:**

The 2015 Nissan Armada is a full-size SUV that offers a spacious interior and a powerful engine. The Armada is available with a choice of a 5.6-liter V8 engine mated to a 7-speed automatic transmission or a 4.0-liter V6 engine paired with a 5-speed automatic transmission. Owners appreciate the Armada's comfortable ride, ample cargo space, and towing capabilities. However, some have also reported issues with the vehicle.

**Engine**
- **5.6L V8**
    - **Intake Manifold Gasket Leak:**
        Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaking from the intake manifold, causing overheating and reduced engine performance.
        Solution: Replace the intake manifold gasket.
        Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800+
    - **Cam Position Sensor Failure:**
        Description/Symptoms: The cam position sensor fails, leading to engine stalling, misfires, and reduced power.
        Solution: Replace the cam position sensor.
        Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $300+
- **4.0L V6**
    - **No Issues Found**

**Transmission**
- **7-Speed Automatic**
    - **Shifting Problems:**
        Description/Symptoms: The transmission hesitates or slips when shifting gears.
        Solution: Replace the transmission solenoid pack.
        Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1000+
    - **Torque Converter Lockup Failure:**
        Description/Symptoms: The torque converter fails to lock up, causing shuddering or slipping during acceleration.
        Solution: Replace the torque converter.
        Estimated Cost to Fix: $1000 - $1500+
- **5-Speed Automatic**
    - **No Issues Found**

**Suspension**
- **Front Suspension Lower Control Arm Bushing Wear:**
    Description/Symptoms: The lower control arm bushings wear out prematurely, causing clunking noises and poor handling.
    Solution: Replace the lower control arm bushings.
    Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $300+ per side
- **Rear Suspension Shock Absorber Failure:**
    Description/Symptoms: The rear shock absorbers fail, causing excessive bounce and poor ride quality.
    Solution: Replace the rear shock absorbers.
    Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $400+ per pair

**Electrical**
- **Battery Drain:**
    Description/Symptoms: The battery drains quickly, even when the vehicle is not in use.
    Solution: Diagnose and repair the cause of the battery drain, which can be a faulty alternator, parasitic drain, or other electrical issue.
    Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the cause

**Exterior**
- **Headlamp Condensation:**
    Description/Symptoms: Moisture accumulates inside the headlamps, causing reduced visibility.
    Solution: Replace the headlamps with sealed units.
    Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1000+ per pair
- **Power Liftgate Failure:**
    Description/Symptoms: The power liftgate fails to open or close properly.
    Solution: Replace the liftgate motor or switch.
    Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800+

**Interior**
- **Climate Control Malfunction:**
    Description/Symptoms: The climate control system fails to blow hot or cold air, or the controls do not function properly.
    Solution: Diagnose and repair the faulty component, which can be a blend door actuator, temperature sensor, or other electrical issue.
    Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the component

**Overall Summary:**

While the 2015 Nissan Armada has some notable issues, the majority of problems are related to the engine and transmission. The electrical, exterior, and interior issues are generally less severe and less costly to repair. Overall, the Armada offers a good value for its price point but may require some maintenance and repairs over time.
