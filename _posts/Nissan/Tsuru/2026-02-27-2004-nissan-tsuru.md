# 2004 Nissan Tsuru

### Overview
The 2004 Nissan Tsuru, a popular compact sedan in many markets, is generally recognized for its robust and straightforward mechanical design. Built with simplicity and durability in mind, it often appeals to budget-conscious buyers and those prioritizing low running costs. While not a vehicle known for cutting-edge technology or luxury features, its core engineering emphasizes reliability and ease of maintenance. This focus has contributed to its reputation for longevity, with many Tsuru models continuing to serve their owners well into higher mileage. However, like any vehicle, specific components can experience issues over time, particularly those subject to wear and tear or environmental factors.

## Suspension

### Standard Independent Front/Rear
**Worn Bushings/Ball Joints**
- **Symptoms:** Clunking noises over bumps, imprecise steering, uneven tire wear.
- **Solution:** Replacement of worn bushings and ball joints.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600


## Electrical

### Standard Electrical System
**Alternator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Dimming headlights, battery warning light illuminated, difficulty starting.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the alternator.
- **Cost:** $400 - $700

**Starter Motor Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Engine cranks slowly or not at all, clicking sound when turning the key.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the starter motor.
- **Cost:** $350 - $600


## Exterior

### Body Panels and Trim
**Rust/Corrosion**
- **Symptoms:** Visible rust on body panels, wheel arches, and undercarriage, especially in humid or salted environments.
- **Solution:** Rust treatment and repair, or panel replacement.
- **Cost:** $200 - $1000+


## Interior

### Cabin Components
**HVAC Fan Motor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** No airflow from vents, or intermittent fan operation.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the blower motor.
- **Cost:** $200 - $400

### Summary Verdict
The 2004 Nissan Tsuru is a fundamentally reliable vehicle, particularly praised for its durable powertrain. The 1.6L engine and its associated manual and automatic transmissions are known for their longevity with proper maintenance. Common issues tend to be related to wear and tear on suspension components like bushings and ball joints, and electrical system parts such as the alternator and starter motor, which are typical for vehicles of this age. Exterior rust can be a concern depending on climate. Interior issues are generally minor, with the HVAC blower motor being a notable component that may require replacement. Overall, the Tsuru offers good value for its reliability, with most repairs being relatively affordable.
