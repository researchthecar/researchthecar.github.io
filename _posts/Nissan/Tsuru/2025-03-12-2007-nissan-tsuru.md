The 2007 Nissan Tsuru is a popular sedan, particularly in Mexico, known for its simplicity, reliability, and affordability. It typically comes with a 1.6-liter inline-4 engine, producing around 105 horsepower and 102 lb-ft of torque, paired with a 5-speed manual transmission. Some enjoy the Tsuru for its fuel efficiency and ease of maintenance, while others dislike its basic features and lack of modern safety equipment.

**Engine**

**1.6L Inline-4**

*   **Excessive Oil Consumption**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners report higher than normal oil consumption, requiring frequent top-ups. Symptoms include low oil level warning light, blue smoke from the exhaust, and a burning oil smell.
    *   Solution: Monitoring oil levels and topping off as needed is a temporary solution. A more permanent solution involves replacing the piston rings, valve stem seals, or, in severe cases, a complete engine rebuild.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+

*   **Idle Air Control Valve Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rough idling, stalling, or difficulty starting the engine. This is often due to a malfunctioning Idle Air Control (IAC) valve.
    *   Solution: Replacing the Idle Air Control Valve.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+

**Transmission**

**5-Speed Manual**

*   **Worn Clutch**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Clutch slippage, difficulty shifting gears, or a high engagement point.
    *   Solution: Replacing the clutch disc, pressure plate, and throw-out bearing.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600+

**Suspension**

*   **Worn Shock Absorbers and Struts**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Bouncy ride, poor handling, excessive body roll during cornering, and longer stopping distances.
    *   Solution: Replacing the worn shock absorbers and struts.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+

*   **Worn Suspension Bushings**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Clunking or rattling noises from the suspension, especially when going over bumps. Poor handling and steering response may also be noticeable.
    *   Solution: Replacing the worn suspension bushings (e.g., control arm bushings, sway bar bushings).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $400+

**Electrical**

*   **Faulty Alternator**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Dimming headlights, warning lights on the dashboard (e.g., battery light), difficulty starting the engine, or a completely dead battery.
    *   Solution: Replacing the alternator.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $400+

*   **Battery Problems**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Difficulty starting the engine, especially in cold weather. The battery may not hold a charge or may drain quickly.
    *   Solution: Replacing the battery. Checking the charging system for any issues that may be draining the battery.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $75 - $200+

**Exterior**

*   **Rust**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rust formation, especially in areas prone to moisture and salt exposure, such as wheel wells, rocker panels, and undercarriage.
    *   Solution: Treating and repairing rust spots. Applying rust inhibitors to prevent future corrosion. In severe cases, panel replacement may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $1,000+ (depending on severity)

**Interior**

*   **Broken Interior Trim Pieces**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Cracking or breaking of plastic trim pieces, such as dashboard components, door panels, or center console parts.
    *   Solution: Replacing the damaged trim pieces.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $20 - $200+ (depending on the part)

**Summary**

The 2007 Nissan Tsuru is a generally reliable vehicle, but owners may encounter issues related to oil consumption, suspension wear, electrical components, rust, and interior trim. The severity and cost of these issues vary, but addressing them promptly can help maintain the vehicle's overall reliability and longevity.
