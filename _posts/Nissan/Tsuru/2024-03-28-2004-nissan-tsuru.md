**2004 Nissan Tsuru**

The 2004 Nissan Tsuru is a compact sedan powered by a 1.6-liter engine paired with a 5-speed manual or 4-speed automatic transmission. Known for its affordability, reliability, and spacious cabin, it also has some potential issues that owners should be aware of.

**Engine**

* **1.6-liter GA16DE Engine**

 - **Valve Cover Gasket Leak:** Oil leaking from the valve cover gasket. Signs include oil stains on the engine and a burning oil smell. Replace the valve cover gasket. $100 - $200+
 - **Crankshaft Position Sensor Failure:** Difficulty starting or sudden engine stalling. Replace the crankshaft position sensor. $100 - $200+

**Transmission**

* **5-Speed Manual Transmission:**

 - **Grinding Noises During Shifting:** Difficulty shifting or grinding noises. May require transmission fluid replacement or synchronizer repair. $200 - $500+

* **4-Speed Automatic Transmission:**

 - **Transmission Slipping or Shuddering:** Hesitation or slipping during gear changes. May require transmission fluid flush or replacement of transmission components. $500 - $1500+

**Suspension**

* **Front Suspension Strut Mount Failure:** Clunking or banging noises when driving over bumps. Replace the strut mounts. $150 - $300+

**Electrical**

* **Faulty Power Window Motors:** Power windows not working or moving slowly. Replace the power window motors. $200 - $400+
* **Alternator Failure:** Difficulty starting or electrical system not functioning properly. Replace the alternator. $400 - $700+

**Exterior**

* **Door Lock Actuators Failure:** Doors not locking or unlocking properly. Replace the door lock actuators. $100 - $200+

**Interior**

* **Cracked Dashboard:** Cracks or blemishes on the dashboard. May require dashboard replacement. $500 - $1000+

**Summary**

While the 2004 Nissan Tsuru offers affordability and reliability, it does have potential issues that can be costly to repair. Engine problems, transmission issues, and electrical failures are among the most common, with costs ranging from a few hundred dollars to over $1000+. However, regular maintenance and addressing issues promptly can help minimize the risk and severity of potential problems.
