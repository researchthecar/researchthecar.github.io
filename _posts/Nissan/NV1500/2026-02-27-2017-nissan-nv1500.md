# 2017 Nissan NV1500 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2017 Nissan NV1500 is a heavy-duty commercial cargo van built for tradespeople and fleets needing robust workspace capability. Shared with Nissan's full-size truck platform, it offers conventional body-on-frame toughness and practical interior proportions. Used-car buyers looking at this model should evaluate powertrain conditions and service history carefully, as commercial vehicles often undergo rigorous daily use.

---

## Engine

### 4.0L V6

The 4.0-liter V6 engine produces approximately 261 horsepower and 281 lb-ft of torque, providing standard power for hauling gear and cargo. It is generally regarded as a durable workhorse, though routine maintenance is vital for longevity.

### 5.6L V8

The optional 5.6-liter V8 engine delivers around 317 horsepower and 385 lb-ft of torque, making it well-suited for heavy payloads and towing duties. It offers strong acceleration under load with traditional V8 reliability.

## Transmission

### 5-Speed Automatic

This conventional 5-speed automatic transmission pairs with the V6 engine to handle commercial duty cycles. It utilizes traditional gear sets rather than a continuously variable design, offering straightforward mechanical serviceability.

### 7-Speed Automatic

This 7-speed automatic transmission is paired with the V8 engine, providing closer gear ratios for improved highway efficiency and power delivery. It is built to withstand commercial work environments when properly maintained.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V551000: Electrical System:Ignition](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=NV1500&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** In these models, over time, the weight on the key ring and road conditions or some other jarring event may cause the ignition switch to move out of the run position to the accessory position, turning off the engine. If the vehicle stalls, it can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Nissan will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the production information on the ignition switch, and replace it as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2017 Nissan NV1500 provides a solid, utilitarian platform for commercial buyers prioritizing cargo space and traditional truck-based mechanics. While current owner reports do not highlight widespread recurring defects in this specific dataset, prospective buyers should thoroughly inspect the powertrain, suspension, and service records for signs of heavy commercial wear before purchasing.
