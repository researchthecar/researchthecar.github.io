**2021 Nissan NV1500**

The 2021 Nissan NV1500 is a full-size van that offers a robust 4.0L V6 engine paired with a 5-speed automatic transmission. It boasts 261 horsepower and 281 lb-ft of torque, providing ample power for daily tasks and hauling. Owners generally appreciate the vehicle's spacious cabin, comfortable ride, and reliable performance. However, certain issues have been reported by users, as detailed below:

**Engine**

* **4.0L V6 Engine**
    * **Oil Pressure Sensor Failure:** Malfunctioning sensor triggers false oil pressure warnings; replacement of sensor required.
    * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $150 - $300
    * **Recalls:** None Found

**Transmission**

* **5-Speed Automatic Transmission**
    * **Transmission Fluid Leaks:** Faulty seals or gaskets may cause fluid leaks, compromising transmission operation; replacement of seals or gaskets necessary.
    * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $300 - $600
    * **Recalls:** None Found

**Suspension**

* **No Issues Found**

**Electrical**

* **Alternator Failure:** Premature alternator failure has been reported, causing charging issues; replacement of alternator required.
* **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $500 - $800
* **Recalls:** None Found

**Exterior**

* **Tailgate Hinge Rust:** Exposed hinges may rust and deteriorate over time; replacement of hinges recommended.
* **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $200 - $400
* **Recalls:** None Found

**Interior**

* **Dashboard Squeaks and Rattles:** Loose or misaligned dashboard components may cause annoying squeaks and rattles; tightening or replacement of components needed.
* **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $100 - $200
* **Recalls:** None Found

**Summary**

Overall, the 2021 Nissan NV1500 is a generally reliable vehicle with a few reported issues. The engine and transmission problems seem to be relatively minor and affordable to fix. However, the electrical issues, particularly alternator failure, can be more costly to address. Owners may also experience minor annoyances like dashboard squeaks and rattles.
