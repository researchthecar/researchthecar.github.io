**Introduction:**
The 2017 Nissan Titan is a full-size pickup truck that offers a variety of engine and transmission options. The base engine is a 5.6-liter V8 with 390 horsepower and 394 lb-ft of torque. A 5.0-liter V8 with 310 horsepower and 385 lb-ft of torque is also available. Both engines can be paired with either a 7-speed automatic transmission or a 6-speed manual transmission. The Titan has a spacious cabin and a comfortable ride, but it also has some notable drawbacks.

**Engine**
- **5.6L V8**
  - **Valve Body Failure**
    - Description/Symptoms: The valve body, which controls the flow of fluid in the transmission, can fail, leading to problems shifting gears and a loss of power.
    - Solution: The valve body must be replaced.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
    - Recalls: None Found
- **5.0L V8**
  - No Issues Found

**Transmission**
- **7-speed Automatic Transmission**
  - **Torque Converter Failure**
    - Description/Symptoms: The torque converter, which transfers power from the engine to the transmission, can fail, leading to a loss of power and a shuddering sensation when shifting gears.
    - Solution: The torque converter must be replaced.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
    - Recalls: None Found
- **6-speed Manual Transmission**
  - **Clutch Failure**
    - Description/Symptoms: The clutch, which engages and disengages the transmission, can wear out over time, leading to difficulty shifting gears and a loss of power.
    - Solution: The clutch must be replaced.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    - Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**
- **Front Suspension**
  - **Strut Failure**
    - Description/Symptoms: The struts, which support the front of the vehicle and provide damping, can fail, leading to a rough ride and poor handling.
    - Solution: The struts must be replaced.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    - Recalls: None Found
- **Rear Suspension**
  - **Leaf Spring Failure**
    - Description/Symptoms: The leaf springs, which support the rear of the vehicle and provide damping, can fail, leading to a saggy ride and poor handling.
    - Solution: The leaf springs must be replaced.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    - Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**
- **Alternator Failure**
  - Description/Symptoms: The alternator, which charges the battery, can fail, leading to a loss of power and a dead battery.
  - Solution: The alternator must be replaced.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
  - Recalls: None Found
- **Battery Failure**
  - Description/Symptoms: The battery, which provides power to the electrical system, can fail, leading to a loss of power and a dead battery.
  - Solution: The battery must be replaced.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
  - Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**
- **No Issues Found**

**Interior**
- **Dashboard Cracking**
  - Description/Symptoms: The dashboard can crack, leading to an unsightly appearance.
  - Solution: The dashboard must be replaced.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
  - Recalls: None Found
- **Center Console Cracking**
  - Description/Symptoms: The center console can crack, leading to an unsightly appearance and potential problems with the electrical system.
  - Solution: The center console must be replaced.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
  - Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**
The 2017 Nissan Titan has some notable issues, particularly with the engine, transmission, and suspension. These issues can be expensive to fix, so it is important to be aware of them before purchasing a used Titan. Overall, the Titan is a reliable vehicle, but it is important to do your research before buying one.
