# 2019 Nissan Titan Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2019 Nissan Titan offers full-size truck capability backed by a standard V8 engine. While it provides strong utility and comfort, used car shoppers should review reported electrical glitches, powertrain quirks, and minor safety system complaints before making a purchase.

---

## Engine

### 5.6L Endurance V8

The 5.6-liter Endurance V8 engine produces 390 horsepower and 394 lb-ft of torque, providing robust towing capacity and reliable daily performance when properly maintained.

**Engine Electrical Warning Lights and Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have reported unexpected check engine lights, electrical warning prompts, and occasional engine stalling linked to accessory loads or sudden power drops.
- **Solution:** Diagnostic scanning for fault codes, updating engine control modules, or inspecting related electrical connections.

**Loss of Motive Power**
- **Symptoms:** Owners note instances where the vehicle illuminated a check engine light and experienced a sudden loss of motive power while driving.
- **Solution:** Professional diagnostic inspection of fuel, ignition, and emissions systems.

### 5.0L Cummins Turbo Diesel V8

The 5.0-liter Cummins turbo diesel V8 delivers strong low-end torque (555 lb-ft) and 310 horsepower, designed for heavy-duty towing applications.

**Check Engine Warning and Emissions Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Early warning lights and emissions-related fault codes can appear, occasionally requiring dealership diagnosis.
- **Solution:** Inspect emissions components, sensors, and update software as needed.

## Transmission

### 7-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 7-speed automatic transmission pairs smoothly with the standard V8 engine to optimize highway cruising and power delivery.

### 6-Speed Heavy-Duty Automatic Transmission

The 6-speed heavy-duty automatic transmission is designed to handle the high torque output of the Cummins diesel engine.

**Acceleration Lag and Flutter**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers note a fluttering feel or hesitation during acceleration, leading to delayed response when merging into traffic.
- **Solution:** Check transmission fluid levels, inspect for software updates, or consult a certified technician.

## Electrical

### Infotainment and Accessories

Electrical accessories and infotainment controls are generally functional, though software glitches and screen freezes are common owner complaints.

**Infotainment System Reboots and Freezing**
- **Symptoms:** The touchscreen display or infotainment system may randomly crash, freeze, or reboot, particularly when the backup camera is engaged.
- **Solution:** Perform a system software update or replace the head unit if hardware failure is confirmed.

### Backup Camera System

Standard safety features include rearview cameras and driver aids, though camera clarity and display reliability have drawn owner complaints.

**Blurry Display or Camera Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The rearview camera image may appear blurry, delayed, or fail to display entirely when shifting into reverse.
- **Solution:** Inspect camera wiring connections, clean the lens, or replace the camera module.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V654000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=Titan&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 111, "Rear Visibility." The lack of an image in the back-up camera display increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 19V495000: Electrical System:Wiring](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=Titan&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** An electrical short may cause an engine stall, increasing the risk of a crash. In addition, it increases the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Nissan will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the alternator harness for the proper routing and any damage. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V169000: Tires](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=Titan&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** Over cured tires may develop a break in the sidewall, resulting in sudden air loss or belt edge separation which could lead to tread/belt loss. Either condition can cause a loss of vehicle control, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Nissan will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the tires, and replace them as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V556000: Exterior Lighting:Headlights](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=Titan&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 108, "Lamps, Reflective Devices, and Associated Equipment." The incorrect marks may cause the headlights to be aimed incorrectly during servicing, resulting in insufficient illumination of the road while driving at night, thereby increasing the risk.
- **What to do:** Nissan will notify owners, and dealers will inspect and, as necessary, replace the headlight assemblies, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2019 Nissan Titan is a capable full-size truck with a strong V8 engine lineup, but buyers should thoroughly inspect electrical systems, infotainment operation, and powertrain responsiveness during a test drive. Verifying service records and checking for completed recall work will help ensure a dependable ownership experience.
