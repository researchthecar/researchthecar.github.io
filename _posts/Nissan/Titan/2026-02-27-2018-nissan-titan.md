# 2018 Nissan Titan Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used full-size pickup truck, the 2018 Nissan Titan presents an intriguing alternative to traditional American mainstays. Equipped with a muscular V8 engine, it offers plenty of muscle for towing and hauling. However, prospective buyers should review owner-reported complaints regarding engine performance, electrical glitches, and transmission behavior before finalizing a purchase.

---

## Engine

### 5.6L Endurance V8

The 5.6-liter Endurance V8 engine produces 390 horsepower and 394 lb-ft of torque, mated to a 7-speed automatic transmission. It delivers strong acceleration and towing capability, though buyers should verify proper maintenance history.

**Fuel Pump Failure and Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers report unexpected engine stalling while driving, loss of motive power, and fuel pump failure sometimes accompanied by metal debris or starting difficulties.
- **Solution:** Requires diagnosis by a qualified mechanic and potential replacement of the fuel pump or affected internal engine components.

## Transmission

### 7-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 7-speed automatic transmission works in tandem with the V8 powerplant to manage power delivery across rear-wheel and four-wheel-drive configurations. While generally durable, intermittent shifting complaints have been noted by some owners.

**Erratic Shifting and Slipping**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have experienced transmission slipping, high revving without immediate acceleration, and delayed or harsh downshifting during highway passing.
- **Solution:** Have the transmission fluid condition checked and consult a transmission specialist for diagnostic scanning and software updates if applicable.

## Steering

### Steering Rack and Column

The hydraulic-assisted steering system provides predictable handling for a large truck, but periodic inspections of the rack and pinion are recommended.

**Steering Rack Leaks and Vibration**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have noted steering wheel vibration accompanied by abnormal metal-on-metal noises or power steering fluid leaks from the rack and pinion.
- **Solution:** Inspect the steering rack and power steering hoses for leaks or wear, replacing damaged steering components as necessary.

## Electrical

### Electrical and Infotainment System

The electrical architecture handles modern cabin electronics and driver aids, though minor software and battery drain anomalies have been reported.

**Battery Drain and Starting Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicles may experience intermittent starting failures, weak batteries without clear warning, and unresponsive hands-free or emergency communication microphones.
- **Solution:** Test the battery, alternator, and charging system draw, and check software modules for communication faults.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V240000: Equipment:Other:Labels](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=Titan&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 110, "Tire Selection and Rims." An overloaded vehicle can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 19V654000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=Titan&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 111, "Rear Visibility." The lack of an image in the back-up camera display increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 19V495000: Electrical System:Wiring](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=Titan&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** An electrical short may cause an engine stall, increasing the risk of a crash. In addition, it increases the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Nissan will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the alternator harness for the proper routing and any damage. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V169000: Tires](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=Titan&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** Over cured tires may develop a break in the sidewall, resulting in sudden air loss or belt edge separation which could lead to tread/belt loss. Either condition can cause a loss of vehicle control, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Nissan will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the tires, and replace them as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2018 Nissan Titan is a capable and comfortable full-size truck with a robust V8 engine, making it a compelling value choice on the used market. However, because some owners have reported powertrain, steering, and electrical concerns, a comprehensive pre-purchase inspection and a detailed test drive are strongly recommended before buying.
