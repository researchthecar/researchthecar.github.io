**Introduction:**

The 2021 Nissan Titan is a full-size pickup truck available with a 5.6L V8 engine mated to a 9-speed automatic transmission, delivering 400 horsepower and 413 lb-ft of torque. It is known for its spacious cabin, impressive towing and hauling capabilities, and off-road prowess. However, like any vehicle, it is not without its share of issues.

**Engine**

* **5.6L V8**
    * **No Issues Found**

**Transmission**

* **9-Speed Automatic**
    * **No Issues Found**

**Suspension**

* **Rear Leaf Springs**
    * **Sagging or Breaking:** Over time, the rear leaf springs can sag or break under heavy loads, affecting handling and stability.
        * Solution: Replace the leaf springs.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * **Squeaking:** Some owners have reported a squeaking or rattling noise coming from the rear suspension, particularly when driving over uneven surfaces.
        * Solution: Lubricate the leaf spring bushings or replace the bushings if they are worn.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300
* **Front Struts**
    * **Leaking:** A small number of owners have reported leaks from the front struts, leading to a loss of damping ability.
        * Solution: Replace the leaking struts.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800+ per strut

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain:** Some owners have experienced a parasitic battery drain, often attributed to faulty aftermarket accessories or electrical components.
        * Solution: Diagnose and eliminate the source of the drain.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the issue
* **Glitching Instrument Panel:** A handful of owners have reported glitches or freezes on the instrument panel display.
        * Solution: Software updates, instrument cluster replacement if necessary.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $1,000+

**Exterior**

* **Tailgate Handle Failure:** Some owners have experienced issues with the tailgate handle, making it difficult to open or close the tailgate.
        * Solution: Replace the tailgate handle.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200
* **Bedliner Cracking:** Some owners have reported cracking or warping of the spray-on bedliner.
        * Solution: Repair or replace the bedliner.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
* **Door Lock Malfunctions:** Occasionally, owners may encounter issues with the door locks not functioning correctly or becoming stuck.
        * Solution: Diagnose and repair the faulty lock mechanism or replace the door lock actuator.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $500+

**Interior**

* **Center Console Creaking:** Some owners have reported a creaking noise coming from the center console area, particularly when driving on rough roads.
        * Solution: Tighten or replace the fasteners holding the console in place.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $200
* **Climate Control Issues:** A small number of owners have experienced problems with the climate control system, such as intermittent airflow or temperature fluctuations.
        * Solution: Diagnose and repair the faulty component or replace the climate control module.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
* **Seat Belt Pretensioner Malfunction:** Some owners have reported issues with the seat belt pretensioners not functioning during sudden stops.
        * Recall: NHTSA Campaign Number 21V863000
        * Solution: Replace the defective seat belt pretensioners.

**Summary:**

Overall, the 2021 Nissan Titan has received generally positive reviews for its capabilities and features. However, it does have some reported issues that owners should be aware of, primarily related to suspension components, electrical glitches, and exterior components. While some issues are minor and relatively inexpensive to fix, others can be more severe and costly. It is important for prospective buyers to do their research and consider the potential maintenance costs before making a purchase decision.
