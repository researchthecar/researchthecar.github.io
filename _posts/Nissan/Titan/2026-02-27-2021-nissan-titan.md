# 2021 Nissan Titan Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2021 Nissan Titan brings a strong standard V8 engine and competitive capability to the full-size truck market, but buyers should carefully review potential powertrain and electrical concerns. While many owners enjoy its comfortable cabin and robust utility, owners have reported specific transmission and unexpected braking behaviors that merit a thorough pre-purchase inspection.

---

## Engine

### 5.6L Endurance V8

The 5.6-liter Endurance V8 produces 400 horsepower and 413 lb-ft of torque, offering brisk acceleration and solid towing capability. It is generally robust, though occasional electrical harness and ignition quirks have been noted by owners.

**Electrical Harness and Engine Failures**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle unexpectedly enters limp mode, displays warning messages, or experiences severe electrical faults.
- **Solution:** Inspect engine harness integrity and consult a qualified technician for diagnostic trouble codes.

## Transmission

### 9-Speed Automatic

The 9-speed automatic transmission is paired with the V8 engine to improve highway efficiency and power delivery. While modern, some owners experience rough downshifts and shifting software misbehaviors.

**Harsh Shifting and Sudden Lurching**
- **Symptoms:** Transmission jerks, downshifts abruptly into lower gears, or causes the vehicle to lurch forward unexpectedly during braking and deceleration.
- **Solution:** Check for pending transmission software updates or reprogramming bulletins, and inspect transmission control modules.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

**Brake Pedal Pulsation**
- **Symptoms:** Abnormal pulsation felt through the brake pedal when attempting to slow down from highway speeds.
- **Solution:** Inspect brake pads and rotors for uneven wear or warping, and service or replace components as needed.

## Electrical

### Infotainment and Keyless Systems

**Screen Glare and Key System Errors**
- **Symptoms:** Infotainment screen suffers from heavy sun glare making backup camera visibility difficult, and key system error warnings appear intermittently.
- **Solution:** Inspect software version and consider anti-glare screen protectors or dealer diagnostic checks for keyless modules.

## Safety

### Forward Collision System

**Unexpected Emergency Braking**
- **Symptoms:** The automatic emergency braking system activates unexpectedly when passing under bridges or encountering shadows on the road.
- **Solution:** Ensure sensor lenses and windshield camera areas are clean and check with a dealer for any calibration updates.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V169000: Tires](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=Titan&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** Over cured tires may develop a break in the sidewall, resulting in sudden air loss or belt edge separation which could lead to tread/belt loss. Either condition can cause a loss of vehicle control, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Nissan will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the tires, and replace them as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V471000: Exterior Lighting:Turn Signal:Bulbs](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=Titan&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** Inoperable front turn signals cannot alert on-coming drivers and/or pedestrians that the vehicle will be turning, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the bulbs as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V457000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=Titan&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The transmission parking pawl may not engage when the vehicle is shifted into park, which can result in a vehicle rollaway. A vehicle rollaway increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the transmission parking pawl pin, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V671000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=Titan&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The transmission parking pawl may not engage when the vehicle is shifted into park, which can result in a vehicle rollaway. A vehicle rollaway increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will perform the applicable repairs below, free of charge:  Model Year 2020-2022 Frontier vehicles: reprogram Transmission Control Module (TCM)  Model Year 2020-2022 Titan vehicles: reprogram TCM and Engine Control Module (ECM) . Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V580000: Air Bags: Air Bag/Restraint Control Module](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=Titan&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** An unsecured ACU can prevent or delay air bags from deploying as intended, increasing the risk of injury in a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the ACU, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2021 Nissan Titan offers strong V8 performance and daily utility, but prospective buyers should pay close attention to transmission shift quality and electrical system behavior during a test drive. Verifying that all powertrain software updates have been properly applied will help ensure a more reliable ownership experience.
