# 2022 Nissan Titan Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2022 Nissan Titan offers robust full-size truck performance backed by a powerful standard V8 engine. While many owners appreciate its capability and towing strength, prospective buyers should review certain owner-reported issues concerning powertrain response, advanced driver assistance systems, and electrical components. Thoroughly inspecting the vehicle's service history and performing a pre-purchase test drive will help ensure a reliable ownership experience.

---

## Engine

### 5.6L Endurance V8

The 5.6-liter Endurance V8 engine produces 400 horsepower and 413 lb-ft of torque, providing strong towing and acceleration. It serves as the standard powerplant for the Titan lineup, balancing heavy-duty capability with everyday driveability.

**Acceleration Hesitation and Speed Limitation**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may experience sudden acceleration hesitation or find that the vehicle struggles to pick up speed and refuses to exceed 40 mph, even after previous manufacturer service updates.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified technician inspect powertrain control modules, throttle position sensors, and software calibrations for updates or faults.

## Transmission

### 9-Speed Automatic

The 9-speed automatic transmission is paired with the 5.6L V8, designed to optimize fuel efficiency and deliver smooth gear transitions across various driving loads. It generally operates reliably when properly maintained.

## Steering

### Steering System

The steering setup provides direct feedback typical of a full-size pickup, though mechanical bindings should be checked during a pre-purchase inspection.

**Steering Binding or Stiffness**
- **Symptoms:** Operators have reported instances where the steering wheel becomes exceptionally difficult to turn or experiences stiffness while navigating turns.
- **Solution:** Have the steering column, linkage, and power steering components thoroughly inspected by a professional mechanic.

## Safety

### Forward Collision Avoidance

Driver assistance features enhance situational awareness, though occasional sensor false positives require attentive maintenance and calibration.

**Inadvertent Emergency Braking**
- **Symptoms:** Sensors may occasionally misinterpret surroundings, causing the emergency braking system or collision warning to activate unexpectedly while driving at highway speeds.
- **Solution:** Inspect front-facing radar and camera sensors for alignment, cleanliness, or software calibration issues at a certified service center.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V457000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=Titan&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** The transmission parking pawl may not engage when the vehicle is shifted into park, which can result in a vehicle rollaway. A vehicle rollaway increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the transmission parking pawl pin, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2022 Nissan Titan is a capable full-size truck with a strong V8 engine and competitive towing specs. Buyers should pay close attention during a test drive to ensure smooth acceleration and proper operation of driver-assistance electronics. Verifying that all open service campaigns have been completed will further protect your investment.
