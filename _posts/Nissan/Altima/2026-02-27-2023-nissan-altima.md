# 2023 Nissan Altima Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2023 Nissan Altima, buyers will find a modern midsize sedan that offers comfortable seating, solid fuel economy, and updated styling. However, reviewing owner complaints highlights certain areas that require closer inspection during a pre-purchase test drive. Potential shoppers should pay careful attention to powertrain performance, braking feel, and electronic features.

---

## Engine

### 2.5L 4-Cylinder Engine

The standard 2.5-liter naturally aspirated four-cylinder engine produces approximately 188 horsepower, providing adequate daily driving performance and strong fuel economy. While generally reliable, individual owner reports note occasional high-pressure fuel pump and performance management concerns.

**High-Pressure Fuel Pump Issues**
- **Symptoms:** The engine-side mounting threads can fail, causing the high-pressure fuel pump to become loose and unseated, resulting in fuel or oil leaks and sudden power loss.
- **Solution:** Inspect the fuel pump mounting area and replace damaged components or hardware as required.

**Engine Power Reduction and Shaking**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may display an engine malfunction warning, shake, lose power while driving at highway speeds, and emit smoke from under the hood.
- **Solution:** Diagnostic scanning is required to identify faulty sensors, fuel delivery components, or mechanical failures.

### 2.0L Variable Compression Turbo 4-Cylinder Engine

The available 2.0-liter VC-Turbo four-cylinder engine utilizes variable compression technology to deliver up to 248 horsepower, offering stronger acceleration while maintaining efficiency. This advanced powertrain requires diligent maintenance and regular oil service intervals.

## Transmission

### Xtronic Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)

The Xtronic Continuously Variable Transmission is paired with front-wheel drive or intelligent all-wheel drive configurations, prioritizing smooth power delivery and fuel efficiency. Buyers should thoroughly test-drive the vehicle across different speeds to check for smooth operation and responsive acceleration.

**Unexpected Acceleration Loss**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may suddenly begin slowing down at highway speeds where pressing the accelerator increases engine RPM without corresponding vehicle acceleration.
- **Solution:** Have the transmission system inspected by a qualified technician for internal mechanical or control module faults.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

Braking components provide standard stopping power, but owners have reported occasional hydraulic feel concerns and unexpected anti-lock braking interventions.

**Spongy Pedal and Premature Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have reported persistent spongy brake pedal feel, darkened brake fluid, and premature rotor wear or metal-on-metal scraping noises.
- **Solution:** Inspect brake pads, rotors, and the hydraulic fluid system, and bleed or replace components as necessary.

**Erratic ABS Activation**
- **Symptoms:** The anti-lock braking system may independently engage on slippery surfaces, causing the vehicle to jerk or veer off course unexpectedly.
- **Solution:** Inspect ABS wheel speed sensors, wiring harnesses, and the brake control module.

## Electrical

### Electrical System & Backup Camera

Electrical accessories generally function well, though multiple owners have experienced intermittent backup camera glitches and premature charging component wear.

**Backup Camera Display Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The backup camera display turns completely white or grey while reversing, impairing visibility behind the vehicle.
- **Solution:** Inspect the camera unit, wiring connections, and display module, and replace faulty parts if required.

**Charging System Component Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Premature failure of electrical components such as the battery and alternator, requiring early replacement.
- **Solution:** Test the charging system output and replace failing batteries or alternators.

## Exterior

### Seats and Door Latches

Interior cabin amenities offer good comfort, though isolated structural hardware issues regarding seat mountings and door latches have been reported.

**Front Seat Detachment**
- **Symptoms:** A popping sound may occur from the front driver seat, followed by the seat unhinging or detaching while driving.
- **Solution:** Inspect seat mounting tracks and hardware, and replace damaged assembly components.

**Driver Door Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The driver door may fail to open from both the inside and outside after turning off the ignition.
- **Solution:** Inspect the door latch mechanism, lock actuator, and associated control wiring.

## Safety

### Forward Collision Avoidance

Driver assistance features enhance daily safety, but sensor sensitivity and warning light illuminations have been noted by some operators.

**Automatic Emergency Braking Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** The automatic emergency braking system warning light may illuminate repeatedly, or the system may fail to react promptly in traffic scenarios.
- **Solution:** Have the radar and camera sensors recalibrated or inspected by a certified dealer or technician.

## Summary Verdict

The 2023 Nissan Altima is an appealing midsize sedan option for used car buyers seeking modern tech, comfort, and good fuel economy. While many owners enjoy trouble-free driving, prospective buyers should carefully check the operation of the transmission, brakes, backup camera, and driver-assistance sensors during a comprehensive pre-purchase inspection.
