**Introduction to 2012 Nissan Altima**

The 2012 Nissan Altima is a midsize sedan known for its spacious interior, comfortable ride, and fuel-efficient engine options. It comes with a 2.5-liter 4-cylinder engine (175 hp, 180 lb-ft torque) paired with a CVT transmission, or a 3.5-liter V6 engine (270 hp, 258 lb-ft torque) available with a CVT or 6-speed manual transmission.

**Engine**
**2.5-liter 4-cylinder**

**Excessive Oil Consumption**
Description/Symptoms: Excessive oil consumption resulting in low oil levels and potential engine damage
Solution: Piston ring or valve stem seal replacement
Estimated Cost to Fix: $1500 - $3000+
**Recalls:** NHTSA Campaign Number: 19V484000

**Intake Manifold Airflow Sensor Failure**
Description/Symptoms: Faulty sensor resulting in reduced engine performance, hesitation, and illuminated check engine light
Solution: Replacement of intake manifold airflow sensor
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+

**3.5-liter V6**

**No Issues Found**

**Transmission**
**CVT**

**Shifting Issues**
Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting, slipping gears, and transmission shuddering
Solution: Transmission fluid replacement, filter change, or solenoid replacement
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $2500+
Recalls: NHTSA Campaign Numbers: 19V190000, 20V570000, 22V044000

**6-Speed Manual**

**Clutch Issues**
Description/Symptoms: Worn clutch disc or pressure plate, resulting in difficulty engaging gears, slipping, or shuddering
Solution: Clutch replacement
Estimated Cost to Fix: $1200 - $2000+

**Suspension**

**Strut Mount Bearing Failure**
Description/Symptoms: Worn bearings in the strut mounts causing clunking noises, vibration, and decreased handling
Solution: Replacement of strut mounts
Estimated Cost to Fix: $600 - $1200+

**No Issues Found**

**Electrical**

**Battery Discharge**
Description/Symptoms: Battery drains quickly, resulting in difficulty starting the car
Solution: Battery replacement or alternator repair
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1000+

**Window Regulator Failure**
Description/Symptoms: Inoperative window, rattling noises, or slow/intermittent operation
Solution: Window regulator replacement
Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $700+

**Exterior**

**Condensation in Tail Lights**
Description/Symptoms: Moisture accumulation in tail lights, potentially affecting visibility
Solution: Resealing or replacement of tail lights
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600+

**Interior**

**Dashboard Rattle**
Description/Symptoms: Loose or rattling components in the dashboard, creating unwanted noises
Solution: Tightening or replacing loose components
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600+

**Center Console Squeak**
Description/Symptoms: Squeaking noises coming from the center console area
Solution: Adjustment or lubrication of console components
Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+

**Summary**

The 2012 Nissan Altima has some reported issues, including excessive oil consumption, transmission problems, and various electrical and interior concerns. While some issues can be costly to repair, others are more minor and affordable. It's important to note that the severity and cost of these issues can vary depending on the specific vehicle, its maintenance history, and the mechanic performing the repairs. Overall, the 2012 Altima is known for its comfort and efficiency, but potential buyers should be aware of the potential issues before purchasing.
