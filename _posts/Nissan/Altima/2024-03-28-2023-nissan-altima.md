**Introduction:**

The 2023 Nissan Altima is a mid-size sedan offered with a choice of two engines: a 2.5L 4-cylinder and a 2.0L VC-Turbo 4-cylinder. The 2.5L engine generates 188 horsepower and 180 lb-ft of torque, while the 2.0L VC-Turbo engine produces 248 horsepower and 273 lb-ft of torque. Both engines are paired with a CVT transmission.

The Altima is praised for its comfortable interior, responsive handling, and fuel-efficient powertrains. However, some owners have reported issues with certain components.

**Engine**

**2.5L 4-Cylinder**

**Oil Consumption Issue**
Description/Symptoms: Excessive oil consumption, causing warning lights to illuminate and potential engine damage.
Solution: Replacing piston rings, valve seals, or the entire engine.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $3,000 - $10,000+
Recalls: Not Applicable

**2.0L VC-Turbo 4-Cylinder**

**Turbocharger Failure**
Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, check engine light, and rattling noises from the engine compartment.
Solution: Replacing the turbocharger and associated parts.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+
Recalls: Not Applicable

**Transmission**

**CVT**

**Jerky Shifting**
Description/Symptoms: Rough shifts, hesitation, or shuddering during acceleration or deceleration.
Solution: Updating transmission software, replacing transmission fluid, or rebuilding the transmission.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $3,000+
Recalls: Not Applicable

**Exterior**

**No Issues Found**

**Interior**

**Creaking Noise from Dashboard**
Description/Symptoms: Annoying creaking sound emanating from the dashboard.
Solution: Applying lubricant to contact points or replacing dashboard components.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500
Recalls: Not Applicable

**Summary:**

While the 2023 Nissan Altima is generally reliable, some owners have experienced issues with engine oil consumption, turbocharger failure, transmission shifting, faulty headlight switches, and dashboard creaking. The severity and cost of these issues vary depending on the specific problem and the dealership or mechanic performing the repairs. Overall, it is advisable to research potential issues before purchasing or leasing this vehicle.
