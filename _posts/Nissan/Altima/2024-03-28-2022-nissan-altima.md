**Introduction:**

The 2022 Nissan Altima is a midsize sedan known for its spacious interior, advanced safety features, and sporty handling. It is available with three engine options: a 2.0L 4-cylinder, a 2.5L 4-cylinder, and a 2.0L turbocharged 4-cylinder. Transmission options include a CVT, a 6-speed manual, and an 8-speed automatic. Owners appreciate the Altima's comfortable ride, fuel efficiency, and engaging driving experience, while some find the base engine underpowered and the interior materials lacking.

**Engine:**

**2.0L 4-cylinder**
- **Excessive Oil Consumption:**
 - Description/Symptoms: Burning excessive oil, leading to low oil levels and engine damage
 - Solution: Replacing piston rings and valve seals
 - Estimated Cost to Fix: $1500 - $3000+
 - Recalls: NTB21-068
- **Timing Chain Failure:**
 - Description/Symptoms: Premature failure of the timing chain, resulting in engine damage
 - Solution: Replacing the timing chain and components
 - Estimated Cost to Fix: $1000 - $2500
 - Recalls: None Found

**2.5L 4-cylinder**
- **No Major Issues Found**

**2.0L Turbocharged 4-cylinder**
- **High-Pressure Fuel Pump Failure:**
 - Description/Symptoms: Failure of the high-pressure fuel pump, causing engine stalling or loss of power
 - Solution: Replacing the high-pressure fuel pump
 - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1000
 - Recalls: None Found

**Transmission:**

**CVT**
- **Premature Failure:**
 - Description/Symptoms: Transmission slipping, hesitation, or failure
 - Solution: Replacing the CVT transmission
 - Estimated Cost to Fix: $3000 - $5000+
 - Recalls: None Found
- **Shuddering:**
 - Description/Symptoms: Transmission shuddering or vibrating during acceleration
 - Solution: Updating transmission software or replacing CVT components
 - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1500
 - Recalls: None Found

**6-speed Manual**
- **No Major Issues Found**

**8-speed Automatic**
- **Shifting Issues:**
 - Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears, delayed shifts, or rough shifts
 - Solution: Repairing or replacing transmission components
 - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2000
 - Recalls: None Found

**Suspension:**

- **Front Strut Failure:**
 - Description/Symptoms: Noises, clunking, or vibration from the front suspension
 - Solution: Replacing faulty struts
 - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1000 per strut
 - Recalls: None Found

**Electrical:**

- **Battery Drain:**
 - Description/Symptoms: Battery draining prematurely, leading to difficulty starting the vehicle
 - Solution: Diagnosing and fixing the source of the battery drain
 - Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500
 - Recalls: None Found

**Exterior:**

- **Headlight Condensation:**
 - Description/Symptoms: Moisture accumulation inside the headlights
 - Solution: Replacing the headlights
 - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1000 per headlight
 - Recalls: None Found

**Interior:**

- **Dashboard Cracking:**
 - Description/Symptoms: Cracking on the dashboard or instrument cluster
 - Solution: Replacing the dashboard or instrument cluster
 - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1500
 - Recalls: None Found
- **Infotainment System Malfunctions:**
 - Description/Symptoms: Touchscreen not responding, infotainment system freezing or crashing
 - Solution: Updating software or replacing infotainment components
 - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1000
 - Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

Overall, while the 2022 Nissan Altima is generally a reliable vehicle, it has experienced various issues, ranging from minor inconveniences to more serious problems. The most significant issues include excessive oil consumption in the 2.0L engine, premature CVT transmission failure, and suspension issues. The estimated cost to repair these issues can be substantial. However, it's important to note that not all Altima vehicles will encounter these problems, and many owners report positive experiences with the vehicle.
