**2010 Nissan Altima**

The 2010 Nissan Altima is a midsize sedan known for its spacious interior and fuel efficiency. It is powered by a 2.5-liter four-cylinder engine producing 175 horsepower and 180 lb-ft of torque, paired with a six-speed manual or a continuously variable transmission (CVT). Other engine options include a 3.5-liter V6 with 270 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque, paired with a six-speed automatic transmission.

Some owners appreciate the Altima's comfortable ride, roomy cabin, and impressive fuel economy. However, complaints have also been raised regarding its lackluster performance, inconsistent transmission behavior, and certain reliability concerns.

**Engine**

**2.5-liter Four-Cylinder Engine**

* **Oil Consumption:** Excessive oil consumption may occur, resulting in low oil levels and potential engine damage.
  * Solution: Repair or replacement of piston rings, oil seals, or other engine components.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $3,000+
  * Recalls: NHTSA Campaign Number 15V193000
* **Timing Chain Noise:** A rattling or ticking sound may be heard from the engine, caused by a faulty timing chain.
  * Solution: Replacement of timing chain and associated components.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,200+
  * Recalls: None Found

**3.5-liter V6 Engine**

* **No Issues Found**

**Transmission**

**Six-Speed Manual Transmission**

* **Difficult Shifting:** Difficulty shifting gears may be experienced due to worn synchronizers or other transmission components.
  * Solution: Repair or replacement of affected components within the transmission.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
  * Recalls: None Found

**Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)**

* **Lagging Response:** The transmission may respond slowly or erratically, leading to delayed acceleration or rough shifting.
  * Solution: Software updates or reprogramming of transmission control module.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
  * Recalls: None Found
* **Premature Failure:** Complete CVT failure may occur, resulting in loss of drive.
  * Solution: Replacement of the entire CVT unit.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $4,000 - $6,000+
  * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **No Issues Found**

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain:** Excessive battery drain may occur, resulting in difficulty starting the vehicle.
  * Solution: Replacement of battery or repair of faulty electrical components.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
  * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Trunk Latch Failure:** The trunk latch may fail to engage or release properly.
  * Solution: Replacement of trunk latch.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
  * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Cruise Control Inoperability:** The cruise control may become inoperable due to a faulty switch or wiring harness.
  * Solution: Replacement of cruise control switch or repair of wiring harness.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
  * Recalls: None Found
* **Heater/Air Conditioner Malfunction:** The heater or air conditioner may not function properly due to faulty blend door actuators or other climate control components.
  * Solution: Repair or replacement of blend door actuators or other affected components.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,200+
  * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2010 Nissan Altima has received mixed reviews regarding its reliability. While some owners have reported relatively few issues, others have encountered significant problems with the engine, transmission, and other components. The severity and cost of these issues can vary widely, but some of the more common and expensive repairs could potentially result in significant financial burdens.
