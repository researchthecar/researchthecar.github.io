# 2016 Nissan Altima

### Overview
The 2016 Nissan Altima is a mid-size sedan known for its comfortable ride and fuel efficiency, particularly with its standard 2.5L engine. While generally considered a reliable vehicle, like any car, it has specific areas that have seen more attention from owners regarding potential issues. This analysis focuses on documented technical problems beyond routine maintenance, examining the engine, transmission, suspension, electrical systems, exterior, and interior components. Understanding these potential weak points can help owners anticipate and address issues proactively, ensuring the longevity and optimal performance of their Altima.

---


## Engine

### 2.5L 4-Cylinder (QR25DE)
**Oil Consumption**
- **Symptoms:** Excessive oil burning, potentially leading to low oil levels and engine damage if not monitored.
- **Solution:** Engine internal inspection and repair, potentially piston ring replacement. Regular oil level checks are crucial.
- **Cost:** $1,500 - $3,000

**CVT Transmission Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Hesitation, slipping, shuddering, or complete failure of the Continuously Variable Transmission.
- **Solution:** Transmission fluid flush and replacement, or complete CVT unit replacement.
- **Cost:** $2,500 - $5,000

## Transmission

### CVT (Continuously Variable Transmission)
**Overheating and Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Transmission slipping, jerky acceleration, warning lights, and eventual failure, often exacerbated by heavy use or towing.
- **Solution:** Transmission fluid cooler upgrade, regular fluid changes, or complete transmission replacement.
- **Cost:** $2,500 - $5,000

### Summary Verdict
The 2016 Nissan Altima, particularly with the 2.5L engine, presents a generally reliable profile. The most frequently cited technical concern revolves around the CVT transmission, which can experience overheating and failure, especially under demanding conditions. Some owners have also reported oil consumption issues with the 2.5L engine, necessitating vigilant monitoring. The 3.5L V6 engine and other systems like suspension, electrical, exterior, and interior components have not shown widespread or significant reliability concerns. Proactive maintenance, particularly for the CVT fluid and oil levels, is recommended to mitigate potential issues and ensure a positive ownership experience.
