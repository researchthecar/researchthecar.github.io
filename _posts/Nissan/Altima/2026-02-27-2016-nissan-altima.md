# 2016 Nissan Altima Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2016 Nissan Altima is a popular midsize sedan appreciated by used-car buyers for its comfortable interior, fuel-efficient engine options, and smooth ride. However, owner complaints filed with safety regulators indicate that this model year has several notable reliability challenges that prospective buyers must evaluate carefully. Most prominent among these are reports involving the transmission, engine drivability, and electrical accessories. Understanding these potential issues is key to selecting a dependable example.

---

## Engine

### 2.5L 4-Cylinder (QR25DE)

The 2.5-liter four-cylinder is the standard engine for most Altima trims, producing 182 horsepower and 180 lb-ft of torque. It prioritizes fuel economy for daily driving and highway commutes. While mechanically straightforward, it is frequently paired with a transmission that requires vigilant maintenance.

### 3.5L V6 (VQ35DE)

The 3.5-liter V6 engine boosts performance to 270 horsepower and 251 lb-ft of torque, offering a more responsive driving experience. It has a strong reputation for durability, though it shares the same transmission and electronic throttle control components as the four-cylinder.

## Transmission

### Xtronic Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)

The Xtronic CVT is standard across both engine configurations. While designed to optimize engine RPMs for maximum fuel efficiency, it has generated a high volume of complaints regarding durability and drivability.

**CVT Slipping, Shuddering, and Premature Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Owners frequently describe symptoms such as transmission slipping, severe shuddering or jerking under acceleration, high RPM fluctuations accompanied by loss of speed, whining noises, or a sudden and complete loss of propulsion in traffic.
- **Solution:** In most cases of severe slipping or mechanical failure, the entire CVT unit must be replaced and reprogrammed by a qualified technician.
- **Cost:** Complete transmission replacement and programming costs generally range between $5,000 and $7,000.

**Gear Shifter Lock**
- **Symptoms:** The gear selector lever can become physically locked in the 'Park' position, requiring drivers to manually press the shift lock override with a tool or screwdriver to shift into gear.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the shift lever/shifter assembly.
- **Cost:** Replacing the shifter assembly at an independent repair shop is reported to cost approximately $1,158.

## Brakes

### General / multiple configurations

**Engine Hesitation and Sudden Lurching**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers report that the vehicle may fail to respond immediately when the accelerator pedal is pressed, especially after coming to a complete stop or when the engine is warm. Following a delay of several seconds, the engine may suddenly rev to high RPMs and lurch forward. Engine stalling at intersections or rough idling have also been reported.
- **Solution:** Diagnostic checks are required, which often pinpoint issues with oxygen sensors, the mass air flow sensor, or the throttle body assembly. In some instances, replacing the brake light switches has been attempted.
- **Cost:** Diagnostic scans typically run between $150 and $600. Comprehensive sensor and parts replacements have been reported to cost up to $1,500.

**Fuel Gauge Inaccuracy**
- **Symptoms:** The fuel gauge may falsely indicate that the fuel tank is completely empty even immediately after refueling, and the check engine light may illuminate.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the faulty fuel level sensor within the fuel tank.

## Electrical

### Power and Starting System

**Frequent Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** The 12-volt starting battery fails prematurely or dies repeatedly, with some owners reporting needing multiple replacements within short spans of time.
- **Solution:** Technicians must perform parasitic draw testing to isolate and repair electrical components that fail to go into sleep mode when the vehicle is turned off.

## Exterior

### Exterior Lighting

**Dim Low-Beam Headlights**
- **Symptoms:** The low-beam headlights become extremely dim over time, making night driving hazardous. Replacing the headlight bulbs with new ones does not improve visibility.
- **Solution:** The issue is typically caused by internal deterioration of the headlight housing reflectors. Replacing the entire headlight assembly units is required to restore normal light output.

### Hood Latch and Cabin Controls

**Hood Latch Assembly Failures**
- **Symptoms:** The hood latch mechanism may fail to hold the hood down securely, leading to instances where the hood flies open while driving, cracking the windshield and obstructing driver visibility. Alternatively, the latch can become seized and stuck, preventing the hood from being opened at all.
- **Solution:** Replacing or thoroughly lubricating the latch mechanism. Many complaints indicate that owners continue to experience latch failures even after seeking repairs associated with the manufacturer's safety campaigns.

## Safety

### Air Bag System

**Airbag Warning Light and Sensor Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** The SRS airbag warning light illuminates continuously on the instrument cluster, which typically indicates that the airbag safety system is deactivated. Diagnostic codes such as B0020 or B0020-13 are frequently triggered.
- **Solution:** The issue is often caused by fragile seat wiring harnesses or a failing passenger occupant classification sensor. Repairs require replacing the internal seat sensors or the seat-back assembly.
- **Cost:** Simple occupant sensor diagnostic resets cost around $310, while replacing the seat-back assembly or occupant sensor ranges from $1,728 to $3,500 at authorized dealerships.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V915000: Structure:Emergency:Escape/Egress/Exit](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=Altima&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** The door latch-lock cable may not have been routed properly in the rear door when the remedy was applied. As a result, the cable may interfere with the window regulator causing the rear passenger door to unlatch and inadvertently open without warning when the window is lowered.
- **What to do:** Nissan will notify owners, and dealers will secure the latch-lock cables in the correct position using the new remedy procedure, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V040000: Latches/Locks/Linkages:Doors:Latch](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=Altima&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** As a result, lowering a rear window may inadvertently cause that door to unlatch and open. If the rear passenger doors inadvertently open while the vehicle is in motion, it may increase the risk of injury to the rear passengers.
- **What to do:** Nissan will notify owners, and dealers will correct the rear door latch/lock cable routing, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V244000: Air Bags:Sensor:Occupant Classification](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=Altima&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** As a result, the passenger frontal air bag may be turned off and not deploy in the event of a crash. If the passenger frontal air bag does not deploy as intended in the event of a crash, the passenger is at an increased risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will reprogram the Air Bag Control Unit (ACU) and OCS Electronic Control Unit (ECU) in Altima, Maxima, Murano, Rogue, and Sentra vehicles, and replace the OCS ECU in LEAF, NV200, NV200 Taxi, Pathfinder, Infiniti Q50, JX35, and QX60. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V315000: Latches/Locks/Linkages:Hood:Latch](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=Altima&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** If the primary hood latch is inadvertently released, there is an increased likelihood that the secondary hood latch may corrode over time. Corrosion to the secondary latch may cause it to bind and remain in the unlatched position when the hood is closed.
- **What to do:** Nissan will notify owners, and dealers will install a stronger release spring in the hood release lever, a warning label near the secondary hood latch, and an Owners Manual addendum card with instructions on how to lubricate and maintain. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2016 Nissan Altima is a comfortable and highly efficient midsize sedan, but it is accompanied by notable long-term reliability warnings. Because transmission replacements and airbag sensor failures can be exceptionally expensive to address out-of-pocket, any used Altima under consideration must be vetted carefully. Prioritize vehicles with a thoroughly documented CVT fluid service history or those that have already had the transmission replaced. During the test drive, pay close attention to any hesitations, shuddering, or delayed acceleration responses. We highly recommend having a trusted mechanic perform a pre-purchase inspection to scan for transmission codes like P17F0 and safety codes like B0020, and to inspect the headlight housings and hood latch for proper operation.
