**Introduction to the 2019 Nissan Altima**

The 2019 Nissan Altima is a midsize sedan that offers a comfortable and spacious interior with a modern design. It is powered by a standard 2.5-liter four-cylinder engine that produces 188 horsepower and 180 lb-ft of torque, or an optional 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine that produces 248 horsepower and 273 lb-ft of torque. Both engines are mated to a continuously variable transmission (CVT).

Owners of the 2019 Altima have reported generally positive experiences with the vehicle. They praise its comfortable interior, smooth ride, and fuel efficiency. However, there have been some reported issues with the vehicle, which we will explore in detail below.

**Engine**

**2.5-liter Four-Cylinder Engine**

* **Issue: Excessive Oil Consumption**
  * Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported that their 2.5-liter engines are consuming excessive oil, leading to low oil levels. Symptoms include illuminated oil pressure warning lights and engine knocking.
  * Solution: Nissan has issued a Technical Service Bulletin (NTB18-071) to address this issue. The solution involves replacing the piston rings and cleaning the oil control valve.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,000+
  * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)**

* **Issue: Shuddering or Vibration**
  * Description/Symptoms: Some owners have experienced shuddering or vibration during acceleration or at low speeds. This is likely due to a faulty valve body.
  * Solution: Replacing the valve body has been reported to resolve the issue.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500
  * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Issue: Loose or Worn Suspension Components**
  * Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported loose or worn suspension components, such as control arms or ball joints. This can lead to clunking or rattling noises, as well as poor handling.
  * Solution: Replacing the affected components is necessary to resolve the issue.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500 per component
  * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Issue: Electrical System Malfunctions**
  * Description/Symptoms: Some owners have experienced electrical system malfunctions, such as sudden loss of power or inoperative lights.
  * Solution: The cause of the issue can vary, but often requires diagnosis by a qualified mechanic.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the repair needed
  * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Issue: Body Panel Alignment Issues**
  * Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported misaligned body panels, such as gaps between doors or hoods.
  * Solution: Adjusting or replacing the affected panels can resolve the issue.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500
  * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Issue: Sunroof Rattling**
  * Description/Symptoms: Some owners have experienced rattling noises coming from the sunroof.
  * Solution: Adjusting or replacing the sunroof components can resolve the issue.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
  * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2019 Nissan Altima has received mixed reviews from owners. While some have reported positive experiences, others have encountered issues with the engine, transmission, suspension, electrical system, exterior, and interior. The severity and cost of these issues vary depending on the specific problem and its solution. It is important for potential buyers to research and be aware of the potential problems associated with this vehicle before making a purchase.
