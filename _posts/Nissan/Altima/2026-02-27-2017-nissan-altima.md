# 2017 Nissan Altima Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2017 Nissan Altima, buyers will find a comfortable, efficient midsize sedan that offers decent fuel economy and a smooth ride. However, prospective owners need to exercise caution, as this model year carries notable owner complaints regarding powertrain reliability and electrical anomalies. Reviewing service history and checking for completed recalls is vital before making a purchase.

---

## Engine

### 2.5L 4-Cylinder

The 2.5-liter naturally aspirated four-cylinder engine produces approximately 179 horsepower and 177 lb-ft of torque. It provides adequate daily driving performance and strong fuel economy, though occasional reports of engine stalling and sensor issues have been noted by owners.

### 3.5L V6

The 3.5-liter V6 engine delivers a more robust 270 horsepower and 251 lb-ft of torque for drivers seeking stronger acceleration. It generally runs smoothly but shares the same underlying platform vulnerability concerns as the rest of the lineup.

## Transmission

### Xtronic Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)

The Xtronic Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT) is paired with front-wheel drive across the Altima lineup. While designed to maximize fuel efficiency, this transmission is a frequent subject of owner complaints regarding shuddering, slipping, and premature failure.

**Transmission Shudder and Slippage**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers experience sudden jerking, severe shuddering during acceleration, loss of motive power, or a complete failure to engage gears while driving.
- **Solution:** May require professional diagnostic testing, fluid flushing, valve body replacement, or a complete transmission unit replacement.

## Brakes

### Brake Pedal Switch

The braking system provides adequate stopping power, though switch failures can occasionally strand drivers by disabling push-button starting.

**Brake Light Switch Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle fails to recognize when the brake pedal is depressed, preventing the push-button ignition from starting the engine.
- **Solution:** Inspect and replace the faulty brake light switch.

## Exterior

### Exterior Lighting

Electrical system concerns on this model range from exterior lighting degradation to battery drain issues.

**Headlight Dimming and Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Front headlights become excessively dim or stop working entirely during nighttime driving, creating visibility hazards.
- **Solution:** Inspect headlight assemblies, wiring harnesses, and bulbs, replacing faulty components as necessary.

## Safety

### Air Bag System

Safety equipment generally functions as intended, though occupant classification sensor faults can trigger persistent warning lights.

**Airbag Warning Light and Sensor Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** The airbag warning light illuminates unexpectedly on the dashboard, often pointing to faults within the passenger seat occupant detection sensor.
- **Solution:** Have a dealer or qualified mechanic scan diagnostic trouble codes and replace faulty seat sensor pads or wiring modules.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V915000: Structure:Emergency:Escape/Egress/Exit](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=Altima&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** The door latch-lock cable may not have been routed properly in the rear door when the remedy was applied. As a result, the cable may interfere with the window regulator causing the rear passenger door to unlatch and inadvertently open without warning when the window is lowered.
- **What to do:** Nissan will notify owners, and dealers will secure the latch-lock cables in the correct position using the new remedy procedure, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V040000: Latches/Locks/Linkages:Doors:Latch](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=Altima&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** As a result, lowering a rear window may inadvertently cause that door to unlatch and open. If the rear passenger doors inadvertently open while the vehicle is in motion, it may increase the risk of injury to the rear passengers.
- **What to do:** Nissan will notify owners, and dealers will correct the rear door latch/lock cable routing, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V315000: Latches/Locks/Linkages:Hood:Latch](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=Altima&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** If the primary hood latch is inadvertently released, there is an increased likelihood that the secondary hood latch may corrode over time. Corrosion to the secondary latch may cause it to bind and remain in the unlatched position when the hood is closed.
- **What to do:** Nissan will notify owners, and dealers will install a stronger release spring in the hood release lever, a warning label near the secondary hood latch, and an Owners Manual addendum card with instructions on how to lubricate and maintain. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2017 Nissan Altima can be an affordable and comfortable commuter car, but buyers must proceed with caution. CVT transmission durability is a primary concern, so any used model should undergo a rigorous pre-purchase inspection focusing on transmission behavior, electrical functions, and safety sensor operation. Verify that all safety recalls have been addressed before finalizing a purchase.
