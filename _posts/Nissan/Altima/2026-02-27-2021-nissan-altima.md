# 2021 Nissan Altima Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2021 Nissan Altima, buyers will find a popular midsize sedan that offers comfortable seating, modern tech, and decent fuel economy. However, ownership reports suggest paying close attention to specific problem areas, most notably surrounding electrical door locks and driver-assistance or braking electronics. While many drivers enjoy a smooth daily commute, reviewing service history and testing all electronic and mechanical features thoroughly prior to purchase is strongly recommended.

---

## Engine

### 2.5L 4-Cylinder Engine

The standard 2.5-liter naturally aspirated 4-cylinder engine produces approximately 188 horsepower, providing adequate power for everyday driving while delivering strong fuel economy. Overall durability is generally acceptable, though isolated owner reports highlight severe internal failures and power loss issues.

**Engine Power Loss and Metal Contamination**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have reported sudden engine power loss while driving, stuttering, and in severe cases, internal engine failure due to metal contamination found in the oil pan.
- **Solution:** Requires professional mechanical diagnosis; severe cases may necessitate engine replacement.

### 2.0L Variable Compression Turbo (VC-Turbo) 4-Cylinder Engine

The available 2.0-liter turbocharged 4-cylinder engine uses variable compression technology to dynamically adjust performance and efficiency, generating up to 248 horsepower. It delivers a much punchier driving experience than the base engine, though prospective buyers should ensure diligent oil-change history.

## Transmission

### Xtronic Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)

Equipped with an Xtronic Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT) paired with either front-wheel drive or intelligent all-wheel drive, this setup prioritizes fuel efficiency. CVT longevity can vary, making a thorough pre-purchase transmission inspection essential.

**Transmission Fluid Leaks and Power Loss**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have experienced unexpected power loss while driving or noticed front seal fluid leaks from the transmission assembly.
- **Solution:** Inspect the transmission housing and seals for leaks; damaged seals must be replaced, and transmission performance must be professionally evaluated.

## Exterior

### Door Lock Actuators

Electrical accessories generally function well, though recurring issues with power door lock actuators can leave occupants unable to unlock or open doors automatically.

**Door Lock Actuator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Power door locks fail to respond to key fobs or interior door buttons, often failing sequentially across multiple doors.
- **Solution:** Replace the faulty door lock actuators.

### Latches and Locks

Cabin materials and layout are generally comfortable, though interior hardware complaints frequently center on stubborn door latches and locking mechanisms.

**Rear Door Latch Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** Rear doors fail to latch or unlatch properly using electronic controls, forcing occupants to rely strictly on manual operation or experience difficulty exiting.
- **Solution:** Inspect and replace defective door latches or internal linkage components.

## Safety

### Forward Collision Avoidance

Advanced safety features aim to protect occupants, but sensitivity in the automatic emergency braking and collision detection systems can lead to phantom braking events.

**Unexpected Automatic Braking**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle's automatic emergency braking system may inadvertently trigger, causing unexpected deceleration or stopping without an immediate obstruction.
- **Solution:** Check for software updates or sensor recalibrations at a qualified service center.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V138000: Steering:Linkages:Tie Rod Assembly](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=Altima&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The tie rod ball joint fastener was not tightened properly, which could result in the tie rod ball joint detaching from the steering knuckle. A detached tie rod ball joint can cause a loss of steering control, and increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Nissan will notify owners, and dealers will tighten the left and right-side tie rod ball joint fasteners, or install new fasteners as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V628000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=Altima&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 111, "Rear Visibility." A rearview camera that does not properly display an image can reduce the driver's rear view, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the rearview camera and harness for damage and replace them as necessary. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2021 Nissan Altima is a comfortable and efficient commuter option, but prospective buyers should approach with a healthy dose of caution. Key areas to inspect during a pre-purchase test drive include the operation of all power door locks, the responsiveness of the CVT transmission, and the behavior of driver-assistance sensors. Verifying a complete service record will significantly minimize the risk of inheriting unresolved mechanical or electrical issues.
