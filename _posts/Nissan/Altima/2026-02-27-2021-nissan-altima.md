# 2021 Nissan Altima

### Overview
The 2021 Nissan Altima is a mid-size sedan that generally offers a good balance of comfort, features, and fuel efficiency. It comes with two engine options: a standard 2.5-liter four-cylinder and an optional 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder for sportier performance. Both are paired with a Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT). Reliability for the 2021 model year is considered average to good, with most owners reporting satisfaction. However, like any vehicle, there are specific areas that have seen more attention than others. This analysis focuses on documented technical issues beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential concerns for owners and prospective buyers.

## Transmission

### Xtronic CVT (Continuously Variable Transmission)
**Hesitation or Jerking**
- **Symptoms:** Some drivers have reported occasional hesitation or slight jerking during acceleration or low-speed maneuvers.
- **Solution:** Software updates for the CVT control module may resolve minor drivability issues. In more severe cases, internal transmission inspection and repair might be necessary.
- **Cost:** $100 - $150 for software update; $1,000 - $3,000 for internal repairs.

## Electrical

### Infotainment System
**Screen Freezing or Unresponsiveness**
- **Symptoms:** Occasional reports of the infotainment screen freezing, becoming unresponsive, or requiring a reset.
- **Solution:** Software updates for the infotainment system are often available and can resolve these glitches. A hard reset of the system may also be effective.
- **Cost:** $50 - $150 for software update or diagnostic.


### 12V Battery
**Premature Failure**
- **Symptoms:** A small number of owners have reported the 12V battery failing sooner than expected, leading to starting issues.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the 12V battery.
- **Cost:** $150 - $300

### Summary Verdict
The 2021 Nissan Altima demonstrates solid reliability overall, with its standard 2.5L engine and chassis components performing commendably. The optional 2.0L VC-Turbo engine also appears to be robust. The most frequently noted technical concerns, though not widespread, revolve around the Xtronic CVT exhibiting occasional hesitation and the infotainment system experiencing minor glitches like screen unresponsiveness. A few reports of premature 12V battery failure have also surfaced. These issues are generally manageable with software updates or component replacement, and the associated repair costs are typically within the expected range for a vehicle of this class. For the most part, the Altima is a dependable choice, but potential buyers should be aware of the minor CVT and electrical quirks.
