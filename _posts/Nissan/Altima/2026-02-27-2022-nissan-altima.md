# 2022 Nissan Altima

### Overview
The 2022 Nissan Altima is a mid-size sedan that generally offers a solid ownership experience with a focus on comfort and practicality. It comes equipped with either a naturally aspirated 2.5-liter four-cylinder engine or a more potent 2.0-liter variable compression turbo (VC-Turbo) engine, both paired with a continuously variable transmission (CVT). Reliability data for the 2022 model year indicates a good overall standing, with most reported issues being minor and not indicative of widespread systemic failures. Owners generally appreciate the Altima's fuel efficiency and comfortable ride, making it a competitive choice in its segment. However, as with any vehicle, specific components can occasionally experience issues.

## Transmission

### CVT (Continuously Variable Transmission)
**Hesitation or Jerking**
- **Symptoms:** Occasional hesitation during acceleration or a slight jerk when shifting between drive and reverse.
- **Solution:** Software update for the transmission control module, or in severe cases, transmission fluid flush and replacement.
- **Cost:** $150 - $350

## Electrical

### Infotainment System
**Screen Freezing or Unresponsiveness**
- **Symptoms:** The infotainment screen may occasionally freeze or become unresponsive to touch inputs.
- **Solution:** Software update for the infotainment system, or a hard reset of the unit.
- **Cost:** $100 - $250


### Battery
**Premature Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Reports of the battery draining faster than expected, potentially leading to starting issues.
- **Solution:** Check for parasitic draws, ensure all accessories are off when the vehicle is parked, or battery replacement.
- **Cost:** $200 - $400

### Summary Verdict
The 2022 Nissan Altima demonstrates good overall reliability, with the majority of its systems performing as expected. The primary areas where minor issues have been noted include the CVT transmission, which can occasionally exhibit hesitation, and the electrical system, with some reports of infotainment screen glitches and battery drain. These issues are generally not indicative of major design flaws and are often resolvable with software updates or standard maintenance. The engines, suspension, exterior, and interior components are largely free from widespread complaints, contributing to a positive reliability outlook for this model year. Owners should be aware of potential CVT behavior and ensure regular battery checks.
