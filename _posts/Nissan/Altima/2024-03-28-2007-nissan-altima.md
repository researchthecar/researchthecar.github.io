**Introduction:**

The 2007 Nissan Altima is a midsize sedan available with a choice of two engines: a 2.5L four-cylinder producing 175 horsepower and 180 lb-ft of torque, or a 3.5L V6 producing 270 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque. Both engines can be paired with either a six-speed manual or a five-speed automatic transmission.

Drivers appreciate the Altima's comfortable ride, spacious interior, and sporty handling. However, some have reported various issues with the vehicle.

**Engine**

**2.5L Four-Cylinder**

* **Premature Timing Chain Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Noisy engine, reduced power, illuminated check engine light
    * Solution: Replace timing chain and related components
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Oil Consumption:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Excessive oil consumption, blue smoke from exhaust
    * Solution: Replace piston rings and valve seals
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500

**3.5L V6**

* **Fuel Injector Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Rough idle, hesitation during acceleration, poor gas mileage
    * Solution: Replace fuel injectors
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600

* **Valve Cover Gasket Leak:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Oil leak from valve cover area
    * Solution: Replace valve cover gasket
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400

**Transmission**

**Six-Speed Manual**

* **Shift Fork Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears, grinding noises
    * Solution: Replace shift forks and related components
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $1,500

* **Clutch Slave Cylinder Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Leaking fluid, difficulty shifting gears
    * Solution: Replace clutch slave cylinder
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400

**Five-Speed Automatic**

* **Transmission Slippage:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Jerking or slipping during gear changes
    * Solution: Replace or rebuild transmission
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,500 - $4,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Strut Mount Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Noises while turning or driving over bumps
    * Solution: Replace strut mounts
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600

* **Sway Bar Link Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Clunking noises while turning or driving over bumps
    * Solution: Replace sway bar links
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400

**Electrical**

* **Alternator Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Dimming lights, electrical system malfunctions
    * Solution: Replace alternator
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $600
    * Recalls: NHTSA 09V273000

* **Battery Drain:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Battery drains quickly, especially after sitting
    * Solution: Diagnose and repair electrical system
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+

**Exterior**

* **Plastic Panel Breakage:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Broken or cracked plastic panels on the exterior
    * Solution: Replace plastic panels
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300

* **Rear Bumper Sagging:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Rear bumper sagging or misaligned
    * Solution: Adjust or replace bumper brackets
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400

**Interior**

* **Headliner Sagging:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Loose or sagging headliner material
    * Solution: Replace or repair headliner
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500

* **Shift Knob Vibration:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Vibrating shift knob while driving
    * Solution: Replace shift knob or related components
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $150

**Summary:**

The 2007 Nissan Altima has several reported issues, including timing chain failures, oil consumption, and transmission slipping. The severity and cost of repairs vary, but some issues can be expensive to fix. Potential buyers should be aware of these issues and factor them into their purchase decision.
