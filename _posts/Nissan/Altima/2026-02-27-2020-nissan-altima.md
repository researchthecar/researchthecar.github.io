# 2020 Nissan Altima Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2020 Nissan Altima, buyers will find a stylish, comfortable midsize sedan that offers competitive fuel economy and an available Intelligent All-Wheel Drive system. However, like any modern vehicle, owner reports highlight a few recurring mechanical and electrical issues that warrant a careful inspection before purchasing. Paying close attention to powertrain responsiveness and door lock functionality during a test drive can help ensure you select a reliable example.

---

## Engine

### 2.5L 4-Cylinder Engine

The standard 2.5-liter naturally aspirated four-cylinder engine produces approximately 188 horsepower and 180 lb-ft of torque, providing adequate everyday acceleration and strong fuel efficiency. Overall, this engine is mechanically straightforward, though occasional stalling and driveability complaints have been reported by owners.

**Engine Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may experience unexpected engine stalling, hesitation, and jerking while driving.
- **Solution:** Diagnose throttle body, fuel delivery, or sensor faults; replace faulty components as necessary.

### 2.0L Turbocharged 4-Cylinder Engine

The available 2.0-liter variable-compression turbocharged (VC-Turbo) four-cylinder engine generates up to 248 horsepower and 273 lb-ft of torque, offering significantly punchier performance. It pairs exclusively with front-wheel drive and adds a sportier dynamic for enthusiastic drivers.

## Transmission

### Xtronic Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)

Equipped across all trims, the Xtronic Continuously Variable Transmission prioritizes fuel economy and smooth cruising. While generally efficient, used buyers should closely check CVT responsiveness, as some owners have experienced premature wear or hesitation.

**Transmission Delay and Stuttering**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may notice delayed acceleration, stuttering, or surging behavior during normal driving conditions.
- **Solution:** Check transmission fluid condition, update powertrain control modules, or consult a specialist for mechanical inspection.

## Steering

### Power Steering and Front Suspension

Steering components generally perform well, though rare reports indicate intermittent electronic power steering interruptions that require professional diagnosis.

**Intermittent Power Steering Loss**
- **Symptoms:** Power steering assistance may cut out temporarily while driving, accompanied by dashboard warning lights.
- **Solution:** Inspect electrical connections, steering control modules, and related diagnostic trouble codes.

## Exterior

### Door Lock Actuators

Electrical complaints frequently center around power door lock actuators failing prematurely across multiple doors, which can hinder convenient access.

**Actuator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle doors fail to lock or unlock via the interior power switches or the remote key fob.
- **Solution:** Replace the malfunctioning door lock actuator assemblies.

## Safety

### Forward Collision Avoidance

Safety systems such as forward collision warning and automatic emergency braking enhance protection but can occasionally generate false alerts or unexpected braking activations.

**Phantom Braking and Warning Activation**
- **Symptoms:** The automated emergency braking system may unexpectedly trigger or illuminate warning lights without an actual obstacle present.
- **Solution:** Inspect collision sensors, clean windshield camera areas, and check for relevant software updates.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V138000: Steering:Linkages:Tie Rod Assembly](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=Altima&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** The tie rod ball joint fastener was not tightened properly, which could result in the tie rod ball joint detaching from the steering knuckle. A detached tie rod ball joint can cause a loss of steering control, and increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Nissan will notify owners, and dealers will tighten the left and right-side tie rod ball joint fasteners, or install new fasteners as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V169000: Tires](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=Altima&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** Over cured tires may develop a break in the sidewall, resulting in sudden air loss or belt edge separation which could lead to tread/belt loss. Either condition can cause a loss of vehicle control, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Nissan will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the tires, and replace them as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V570000: Visibility:Glass, Side/Rear](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=Altima&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** The rear window glass may not remain properly secured to the vehicle. Rear window glass that separates from the vehicle can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Nissan will notify owners, and dealers will replace the back window glass, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V685000: Tires](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=Altima&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** Both conditions increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Nissan will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the vehicle's tires to see if they are affected, replacing them as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V628000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=Altima&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 111, "Rear Visibility." A rearview camera that does not properly display an image can reduce the driver's rear view, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the rearview camera and harness for damage and replace them as necessary. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V437000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Engine:Hard Parts Internal/Mechanical](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=Altima&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** Engine failure increases the risk of a crash. A bearing failure may cause a breach in the engine block, allowing hot oil to be discharged, increasing the risk of an engine fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will reprogram the engine control module (ECM) software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2020 Nissan Altima is a practical and comfortable sedan choice, but prospective buyers should perform a thorough pre-purchase inspection. Pay close attention to the operation of all door locks, test the transmission for smooth and prompt engagement, and verify that all driver-assist safety features are functioning normally to avoid unexpected repair bills.
