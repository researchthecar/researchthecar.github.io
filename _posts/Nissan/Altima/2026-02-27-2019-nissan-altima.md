# 2019 Nissan Altima Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2019 Nissan Altima, prospective buyers will find a modern midsize sedan that brought standard all-wheel-drive capability to certain trims and a heavily revised powertrain lineup. While many drivers appreciate its comfortable interior, smooth highway ride, and updated styling, ownership experiences can vary. Reviewing owner feedback reveals a handful of recurring mechanical and electrical pain points that buyers should inspect closely before finalizing a purchase.

---

## Engine

### 2.5L 4-Cylinder Engine

The standard 2.5-liter naturally aspirated four-cylinder engine produces around 188 horsepower, offering adequate daily commuting power and competitive fuel economy for used car buyers.

**Engine Stalling and Coolant Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Some owners have reported unexpected engine stalling, activation of warning lights, or coolant leaks associated with the cooling system and EGR components.
- **Solution:** Inspect the cooling system hoses, thermostat, and EGR components for leaks or malfunctions, and repair or replace faulty parts as necessary.

### 2.0L Turbocharged 4-Cylinder Engine

The available 2.0-liter variable-compression turbocharged (VC-Turbo) four-cylinder engine delivers higher performance with approximately 248 horsepower, though buyers should verify complete maintenance histories on advanced turbo powerplants.

## Transmission

### Xtronic Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)

Paired with front-wheel drive or the intelligent all-wheel-drive system, the Xtronic CVT prioritizes fuel efficiency. Buyers should thoroughly test-drive the transmission to ensure smooth operation without hesitation, slipping, or unusual noise.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

The braking system provides adequate stopping power under normal conditions, but used vehicle shoppers should check brake pad life and rotor condition closely.

**Brake Responsiveness and Shaking**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have reported vibration during braking, burning odors, or instances where the vehicle required longer stopping distances than anticipated.
- **Solution:** Inspect brake pads, rotors, calipers, and anti-lock braking sensors for premature wear or failure.

## Exterior

### Door Lock Actuators

Cabin electronics and convenience features generally function well, though door lock actuators are a known trouble spot requiring attention upon inspection.

**Inoperative Door Locks**
- **Symptoms:** Owners note that one or more doors fail to lock or unlock using the key fob or interior master switch, leaving passenger doors stuck or insecure.
- **Solution:** Replace the faulty door lock actuators within the affected door assemblies.

## Safety

### Forward Collision Avoidance

Advanced safety features enhance protection on the road, but sensitive sensors can occasionally generate false activations or require recalibration.

**Unexpected Automatic Braking and Sensor Faults**
- **Symptoms:** The automated emergency braking or driver assistance systems can occasionally trigger false warnings or unexpected sudden braking events.
- **Solution:** Check collision sensors and camera modules for proper calibration, and update vehicle software if dealer bulletins apply.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V169000: Tires](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=Altima&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** Over cured tires may develop a break in the sidewall, resulting in sudden air loss or belt edge separation which could lead to tread/belt loss. Either condition can cause a loss of vehicle control, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Nissan will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the tires, and replace them as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V316000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=Altima&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** The fuel pump lock ring may not be fully engaged, possibly causing fuel to leak. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 301, "Fuel System Integrity." A fuel leak in the presence of an ignition source increases the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Nissan will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the fuel tank assembly. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V922000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=Altima&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** The retainer clip connecting the low pressure fuel tube to the high pressure fuel pump may not have been locked into position. If the retainer clip is unlocked, the fuel tube may shift and cause a fuel leak, increasing the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Nissan has notified owners, and dealers will route the fuel hose into the holding clip and lock the retainer clip into place, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V235000: Exterior Lighting:Brake Lights:Switch](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=Altima&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** The brake switch bracket may be in the incorrect location, which may cause the brake switch to remain on. Additionally, the transmission shift lever may be moved out of the Park position without the brake pedal being pressed, allowing the vehicle to roll unexpectedly.
- **What to do:** Nissan will notify owners, and dealers will inspect and install a new brake pedal as needed, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V654000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=Altima&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 111, "Rear Visibility." The lack of an image in the back-up camera display increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 23V628000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=Altima&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 111, "Rear Visibility." A rearview camera that does not properly display an image can reduce the driver's rear view, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the rearview camera and harness for damage and replace them as necessary. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V437000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Engine:Hard Parts Internal/Mechanical](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=Altima&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** Engine failure increases the risk of a crash. A bearing failure may cause a breach in the engine block, allowing hot oil to be discharged, increasing the risk of an engine fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will reprogram the engine control module (ECM) software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2019 Nissan Altima presents a compelling mix of modern styling, comfortable seating, and available all-wheel drive, making it an attractive option on the used market. However, prospective owners should perform a comprehensive inspection checking the door lock actuators, cooling system integrity, and brake responsiveness. Taking the time to verify these components will help ensure a reliable and satisfying ownership experience.
