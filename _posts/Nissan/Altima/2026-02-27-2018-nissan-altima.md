# 2018 Nissan Altima Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2018 Nissan Altima, buyers will find a comfortable commuter sedan that offers decent fuel economy and a smooth highway ride. However, prospective owners should review owner-reported reliability trends carefully, particularly regarding powertrain durability and advanced driver assistance systems. While many drivers enjoy trouble-free daily commuting, certain recurring mechanical and electrical pain points require attention during a pre-purchase inspection.

---

## Engine

### 2.5L 4-Cylinder Engine

The standard 2.5-liter four-cylinder engine produces approximately 179 horsepower and is paired exclusively with the front-wheel-drive system. It provides adequate power for daily driving and generally reliable operation, though owners have reported occasional auxiliary component concerns.

## Transmission

### Xtronic Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)

The continuously variable transmission maximizes fuel efficiency across trim levels. Buyers must verify service history and transmission health, as CVT longevity is a known focal point for this model year.

**CVT Failure and Hesitation**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers experience sudden loss of power on the highway, unexpected drops in vehicle speed, and diagnostic trouble codes indicating transmission failure.
- **Solution:** Requires professional diagnosis and potentially a complete transmission replacement if internal wear exceeds service limits.

## Brakes

### Brake Pedal Switch and Sensors

The braking system relies on electronic switches that govern both stopping functionality and vehicle ignition interlocks.

**Brake Light Switch Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Intermittent braking behavior, failure to start using the push-button ignition, and brake lights failing to illuminate properly.
- **Solution:** Replace the faulty brake light switch and inspect associated electrical connectors.

## Exterior

### Hood Latch Mechanism

Interior and body access latches require periodic inspection to ensure secure operation.

**Hood Latch Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The hood may unlatch unexpectedly while driving at highway speeds, potentially lifting up and obstructing the driver's forward visibility.
- **Solution:** Inspect the hood latch mechanism for corrosion or binding, and ensure any related manufacturer service campaigns or replacement parts have been properly installed.

### Exterior Headlights

Electrical lighting components should be thoroughly tested during nighttime test drives to ensure adequate road illumination.

**Dim Headlight Output**
- **Symptoms:** Low-beam halogen headlights provide insufficient illumination during night driving, reducing roadway visibility.
- **Solution:** Inspect headlight assemblies and bulbs for degradation or consider verified aftermarket lighting upgrades if permitted.

## Safety

### Air Bag System

Occupant safety systems rely on sensitive wiring and sensors that can trigger persistent warning lights if faults occur.

**Air Bag Warning Light**
- **Symptoms:** The supplemental restraint system warning light remains illuminated on the instrument cluster, often pointing to side airbag sensor or module faults.
- **Solution:** Have a certified technician diagnose the diagnostic trouble codes and replace faulty airbag components or wiring harnesses as needed.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V315000: Latches/Locks/Linkages:Hood:Latch](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=Altima&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** If the primary hood latch is inadvertently released, there is an increased likelihood that the secondary hood latch may corrode over time. Corrosion to the secondary latch may cause it to bind and remain in the unlatched position when the hood is closed.
- **What to do:** Nissan will notify owners, and dealers will install a stronger release spring in the hood release lever, a warning label near the secondary hood latch, and an Owners Manual addendum card with instructions on how to lubricate and maintain. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V654000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=Altima&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 111, "Rear Visibility." The lack of an image in the back-up camera display increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2018 Nissan Altima is an affordable and comfortable midsize sedan, but used-car buyers should proceed with diligence. Pay close attention to transmission operation during your test drive, verify that all safety recalls and service campaigns have been addressed, and ensure the electrical and braking systems function flawlessly before finalizing your purchase.
