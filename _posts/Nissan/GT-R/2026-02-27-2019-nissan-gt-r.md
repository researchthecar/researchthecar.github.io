# 2019 Nissan GT-R Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2019 Nissan GT-R is a high-performance supercar known for its formidable twin-turbocharged V6 engine and advanced all-wheel-drive system, offering thrilling acceleration and track-ready capabilities. For prospective used-car buyers, it represents an exotic ownership experience with unique maintenance demands and high running costs. While powertrain options are robustly engineered, shoppers should thoroughly inspect any pre-owned model for proper mechanical upkeep and signs of aggressive driving.

---

## Engine

### 3.8L Twin-Turbocharged V6 (VR38DETT)

The standard 3.8-liter twin-turbo V6 produces 565 horsepower and 467 lb-ft of torque, delivering blistering performance. Premium and Track Edition variants push output even higher, while requiring meticulous maintenance to ensure long-term durability.

### 3.8L Twin-Turbocharged V6 (NISMO)

Exclusive to the NISMO trim, this hand-built variant increases output to 600 horsepower and 481 lb-ft of torque via upgraded turbochargers. It is built for high-performance track use and demands strict adherence to service intervals.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Dual-Clutch Transmission

A rear-mounted 6-speed dual-clutch automatic transmission handles the immense power output while providing lightning-quick shifts. Buyers should note that normal operating mechanical clunks and whines are characteristic of this performance-focused transaxle.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V654000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=GT-R&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 111, "Rear Visibility." The lack of an image in the back-up camera display increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2019 Nissan GT-R remains an awe-inspiring performance machine, but prospective buyers must approach the used market with diligence. Because these vehicles are frequently driven hard, a comprehensive pre-purchase inspection by a specialist is essential to verify the condition of the high-output engine and complex dual-clutch transmission.
