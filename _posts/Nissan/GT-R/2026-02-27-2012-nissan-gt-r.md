# 2012 Nissan GT-R

### Overview
The 2012 Nissan GT-R, codenamed R35, is a high-performance sports car renowned for its advanced technology and exhilarating driving dynamics. While generally considered reliable for its class, like any complex performance vehicle, it has specific areas that have seen more attention from owners and technicians. This data focuses on documented, non-wear-and-tear related issues that have been reported by owners and repair shops, excluding routine maintenance. The GT-R's sophisticated twin-turbocharged V6 engine and advanced all-wheel-drive transmission system are the heart of its performance, and thus, are key areas to examine for potential concerns.

---


## Engine

### 3.8L Twin-Turbo V6 (VR38DETT)
**Turbocharger Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of power, excessive smoke, whistling noises, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of affected turbocharger(s).
- **Cost:** $2,500 - $5,000 per turbocharger

**Fuel Injector Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Rough idling, misfires, poor acceleration, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of faulty fuel injectors.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1,500 for a set

**Coolant Leaks (Intercooler/Radiator)**
- **Symptoms:** Overheating, visible coolant leaks, sweet smell.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of intercooler or radiator.
- **Cost:** $1,000 - $2,500


## Transmission

### 6-Speed Dual-Clutch Transmission (GR6)
**Transmission Grinding/Harsh Shifting**
- **Symptoms:** Clunking noises during gear changes, jerky acceleration, transmission warning light.
- **Solution:** Transmission fluid flush and filter replacement, potential rebuild or replacement of transmission components (clutches, actuators).
- **Cost:** $1,500 - $5,000 (fluid/filter), $8,000 - $15,000+ (rebuild/replacement)

**Transmission Overheating**
- **Symptoms:** Transmission warning light, reduced engine power, inability to shift gears.
- **Solution:** Inspection and potential repair/replacement of transmission cooler and related lines.
- **Cost:** $800 - $2,000

**Infotainment System Glitches**

**Battery Drain Issues**

### Summary Verdict
The 2012 Nissan GT-R is a highly capable and generally reliable supercar, especially considering its performance output. The most common areas requiring attention tend to be related to its complex powertrain, specifically the twin-turbo V6 engine and the GR6 dual-clutch transmission. Issues like turbocharger wear, fuel injector problems, and transmission shifting anomalies are not uncommon for high-mileage or heavily used examples. Electrical gremlins, particularly with the infotainment system, can also occur. However, with proper maintenance and addressing issues promptly, the GT-R remains a formidable and enjoyable performance vehicle. Owners should be prepared for higher maintenance and repair costs associated with a vehicle of this caliber.
