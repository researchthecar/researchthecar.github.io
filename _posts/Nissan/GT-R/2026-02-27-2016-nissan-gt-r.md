# 2016 Nissan GT-R

### Overview
The 2016 Nissan GT-R, often referred to as 'Godzilla,' is a high-performance supercar renowned for its exhilarating speed and advanced engineering. While its twin-turbocharged V6 engine and sophisticated all-wheel-drive system are generally robust, like any complex performance vehicle, certain areas have seen more attention from owners and technicians. This analysis focuses on documented technical reliability concerns beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential weak points and their associated repair considerations for the 2016 model year. Owners can expect a thrilling driving experience, but awareness of these specific components can aid in proactive maintenance and informed repairs.

---


## Engine

### 3.8L Twin-Turbo V6 (VR38DETT)
**Turbocharger Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of power, whistling noises, increased exhaust smoke, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of turbocharger unit(s).
- **Cost:** $3,000 - $6,000

**PCV System Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Oil leaks, increased oil consumption, rough idle, potential for engine sludge.
- **Solution:** Replacement of PCV valve and potentially related hoses.
- **Cost:** $200 - $500


## Transmission

### 6-Speed Dual-Clutch Automatic (GR6)
**Clutch Pack Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Rough shifting, hesitation during gear changes, grinding noises, transmission warning light.
- **Solution:** Rebuilding or replacement of clutch packs.
- **Cost:** $4,000 - $8,000

**Transmission Fluid Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Visible fluid leaks, low transmission fluid level, potential for overheating and shifting issues.
- **Solution:** Inspection and replacement of seals, gaskets, or transmission lines.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1,500


## Suspension

### Adaptive Dampers
**Electronic Damper Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Harsh ride, excessive body roll, suspension warning light, inconsistent damping.
- **Solution:** Replacement of faulty adaptive dampers.
- **Cost:** $1,000 - $2,500 per corner


## Electrical

### Infotainment System
**Screen Glitches/Freezing**
- **Symptoms:** Touchscreen unresponsive, display errors, system reboots.
- **Solution:** Software update or replacement of infotainment unit.
- **Cost:** $500 - $2,000


### Battery Drain
**Parasitic Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Dead battery after short periods of inactivity, electrical components malfunctioning.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and repair of faulty modules or circuits causing drain.
- **Cost:** $300 - $1,000

### Summary Verdict
The 2016 Nissan GT-R is a highly capable supercar with a generally strong reliability record for its performance class. The primary areas of concern tend to be related to the high-stress components of its powertrain, specifically the turbochargers and the dual-clutch transmission's clutch packs, which can require significant investment to repair or replace if issues arise. The adaptive suspension and certain electrical components, like the infotainment system and potential battery drain issues, are also noted. However, for a vehicle of this caliber, these issues are not uncommon and often stem from aggressive driving or high mileage. Regular, diligent maintenance is crucial for mitigating these potential problems and ensuring the longevity of this exceptional machine.
