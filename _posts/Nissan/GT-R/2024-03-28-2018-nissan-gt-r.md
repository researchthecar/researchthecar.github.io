**2018 Nissan GT-R**

The 2018 Nissan GT-R is a high-performance sports car that is available with a single engine and transmission option. The 3.8L twin-turbocharged V6 engine produces 565 horsepower and 467 lb-ft of torque. It is paired with a 6-speed dual-clutch automatic transmission. The GT-R is known for its impressive acceleration, handling, and braking performance. However, it is also known for its high price and limited practicality.

**Engine**

**3.8L Twin-Turbocharged V6**

* **Turbocharger Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, excessive smoke from exhaust, loud noises from engine
    * Solution: Replace turbochargers
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $5,000 - $7,000+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Engine Knock:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Knocking or rattling sound from engine, reduced power
    * Solution: Replace engine components (pistons, rods, bearings)
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $10,000 - $15,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**6-Speed Dual-Clutch Automatic**

* **Clutch Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Slipping or shuddering during gear changes, loss of power
    * Solution: Replace clutch pack
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $3,000 - $5,000
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **No Issues Found**

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Battery dies prematurely, electrical components malfunction
    * Solution: Diagnose and repair electrical system issues
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **No Issues Found**

**Interior**

* **Infotainment System Malfunction:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Touchscreen unresponsive, system freezes or glitches
    * Solution: Software update or replacement of infotainment unit
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2018 Nissan GT-R is a powerful and capable sports car, but it is also prone to some serious and expensive issues. Engine and transmission problems can be particularly costly to repair. Electrical and infotainment system issues are also common. Overall, the GT-R is a high-risk vehicle that should be approached with caution.
