# 2021 Nissan Titan XD Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2021 Nissan Titan XD offers a heavy-duty alternative to traditional light-duty full-size trucks, anchored by its standard V8 engine and standard four-wheel drive. For used-car shoppers, understanding how the powertrain and supporting systems perform in everyday driving is essential to making a confident purchase.

---

## Engine

### 5.6L V8 Endurance Engine

The 5.6-liter naturally aspirated V8 gasoline engine produces around 400 horsepower and 413 lb-ft of torque, providing strong towing capabilities. It serves as the primary power plant for the Titan XD lineup with a conventional, durable design.

## Transmission

### 9-Speed Automatic Transmission

A 9-speed automatic transmission handles gear selection, pairing smoothly with the V8 engine and standard four-wheel-drive system across all trims. It offers responsive shifting tailored for heavy hauling and highway cruising.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V471000: Exterior Lighting:Turn Signal:Bulbs](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=Titan+XD&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** Inoperable front turn signals cannot alert on-coming drivers and/or pedestrians that the vehicle will be turning, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the bulbs as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2021 Nissan Titan XD presents a straightforward mechanical profile with its standard V8 powertrain. Buyers should thoroughly inspect the vehicle's service history and conduct a comprehensive pre-purchase inspection to ensure all mechanical and electrical functions operate as expected.
