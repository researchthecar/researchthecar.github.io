**Introduction:**

The 2009 Nissan Pickup, also known as the Nissan Frontier, is a mid-size pickup truck that offers a combination of versatility, ruggedness, and affordability. It comes with various engine and transmission options:

* 2.5L 4-cylinder engine with a 5-speed manual transmission or a 5-speed automatic transmission
* 4.0L V6 engine with a 6-speed manual transmission or a 5-speed automatic transmission

Owners of the 2009 Nissan Pickup appreciate its reliability, fuel efficiency, and off-road capabilities. However, like any vehicle, it has some known issues that potential buyers should be aware of.

**Engine**

**2.5L 4-Cylinder Engine**

* **Leaking Valve Cover Gasket:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Oil leak from the engine valve cover, leading to low oil levels and potential engine damage.
    * Solution: Replace the valve cover gasket.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Timing Chain Tensioner Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Noisy engine, rough idling, and reduced engine power.
    * Solution: Replace the timing chain tensioner.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $600 - $1000
    * Recalls: None Found

**4.0L V6 Engine**

* **Intake Manifold Gasket Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Coolant leak, causing overheating and engine misfires.
    * Solution: Replace the intake manifold gasket.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $700
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Rear Main Seal Leak:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Oil leak from the rear of the engine, leading to low oil levels and potential engine damage.
    * Solution: Replace the rear main seal.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**5-Speed Manual Transmission**

* **Shifting Difficulty:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Hard or impossible to shift gears, especially when cold.
    * Solution: Replace the clutch master or slave cylinder.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Grinding Noises:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Grinding noises when shifting gears, indicating worn gears or synchronizers.
    * Solution: Rebuild or replace the transmission.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1500 - $2500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**5-Speed Automatic Transmission**

* **Transmission Slipping:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Loss of power during acceleration, indicating worn clutches or bands.
    * Solution: Rebuild or replace the transmission.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1800 - $3000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**6-Speed Manual Transmission**

* **No Known Issues Found**

**Suspension**

* **Leaf Spring Squeaking:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Squeaking or creaking noises from the rear suspension, especially over bumps.
    * Solution: Lubricate or replace the leaf springs.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Shock Absorber Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Rough or bouncy ride, reduced handling and braking performance.
    * Solution: Replace the shock absorbers.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $600
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Dead battery after sitting for a short period, indicating a parasitic drain.
    * Solution: Identify and fix the source of the drain, such as a faulty alternator or parasitic electrical device.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: Depends on the source of the drain
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Headlight Bulbs Burning Out Prematurely:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Frequent replacement of headlight bulbs due to premature burnout.
    * Solution: Replace the headlight bulbs with higher-quality ones and check the electrical system for any overvoltage issues.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $200
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Hood Flutter:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Excessive shaking of the hood at highway speeds.
    * Solution: Adjust or replace the hood hinges and latches.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Bed Liner Damage:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Cracking or peeling of the factory-installed bed liner.
    * Solution: Replace the bed liner or apply a protective coating.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1000
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Dashboard Rattling:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Noises and vibrations coming from the dashboard area, especially over rough roads.
    * Solution: Tighten or replace loose trim pieces or components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Air Conditioner Malfunction:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Inconsistent or weak air conditioning performance, indicating refrigerant leaks or compressor issues.
    * Solution: Repair or replace the air conditioning system components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2009 Nissan Pickup is a generally reliable vehicle, but it has some known issues that potential buyers should be aware of. The engine and transmission problems are the most severe and expensive to fix, while the suspension and electrical issues are relatively minor. The interior and exterior issues are mostly cosmetic and do not affect the performance of the vehicle. Overall, the 2009 Nissan Pickup is a good choice for those seeking a versatile and affordable mid-size truck, but potential buyers should factor in the potential costs of repairs before making a decision.
