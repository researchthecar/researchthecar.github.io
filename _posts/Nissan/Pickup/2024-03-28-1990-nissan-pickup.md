**Introduction:**

The 1990 Nissan Pickup, also known as the D21, was a compact pickup truck produced by Nissan. It was available with a variety of engine and transmission options and offered a combination of reliability, versatility, and off-road capability. However, like any vehicle, it has experienced some issues over the years.

**Engine:**

* **2.4L I4**
    * **Intake manifold gasket failure:** Description: The intake manifold gasket may fail, causing engine oil or coolant leaks. Symptoms: Leaking fluids, rough idle, engine misfires. Solution: Replace the intake manifold gasket and bolts. Estimated Cost to Fix: $250 - $400+
    * **Exhaust manifold cracking:** Description: The exhaust manifold may crack due to high heat stress. Symptoms: Loud exhaust noise, reduced engine power. Solution: Replace the exhaust manifold. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+
* **3.0L V6**
    * **Oil pump failure:** Description: The oil pump may fail, resulting in loss of oil pressure. Symptoms: Illuminated oil light, engine knocking, engine seizure. Solution: Replace the oil pump and related components. Estimated Cost to Fix: $700 - $1,200+
    * **Valve cover gasket leaks:** Description: The valve cover gaskets may develop leaks, causing oil to seep out. Symptoms: Oil leaks visible on the engine, oil burning smell. Solution: Replace the valve cover gaskets. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $250+

**Transmission:**

* **5-speed manual:**
    * **Clutch failure:** Description: The clutch may wear out or fail, causing difficulty shifting gears. Symptoms: Slipping clutch, difficulty engaging gears. Solution: Replace the clutch. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $900+
* **4-speed automatic:**
    * **Transmission leaks:** Description: The transmission may develop leaks from seals or gaskets. Symptoms: Transmission fluid leaks, transmission slipping. Solution: Replace the faulty seals or gaskets. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    * **Shift solenoids failure:** Description: The shift solenoids may fail, causing delayed or hard shifts. Symptoms: Transmission shifting problems, check engine light. Solution: Replace the shift solenoids. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+

**Suspension:**

* **Leaf springs failure:** Description: The leaf springs may sag or break under heavy loads. Symptoms: Reduced ride height, poor handling. Solution: Replace the leaf springs. Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800+
* **Shock absorbers failure:** Description: The shock absorbers may wear out, leading to reduced ride comfort and handling. Symptoms: Bouncy ride, reduced shock absorption. Solution: Replace the shock absorbers. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+

**Electrical:**

* **Alternator failure:** Description: The alternator may fail, causing loss of power or electrical problems. Symptoms: Dimming headlights, battery not charging. Solution: Replace the alternator. Estimated Cost to Fix: $250 - $500+
* **Starter motor failure:** Description: The starter motor may fail, preventing the engine from starting. Symptoms: No engine crank, grinding noise. Solution: Replace the starter motor. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+

**Exterior:**

* **Rust:** Description: The body and frame may be prone to rust, especially in areas with high humidity or road salt. Symptoms: Rust spots, holes, structural weakening. Solution: Rust prevention measures (e.g., washing, waxing, undercoating) and rust repair. Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on severity
* **Bedside dents:** Description: The aluminum bed can be easily dented or scratched. Symptoms: Visible dents or scratches. Solution: Bedside replacement or repair. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+

**Interior:**

* **Plastic trim peeling:** Description: The plastic trim on the dashboard and interior may peel or crack over time. Symptoms: Loose or peeling trim. Solution: Replace or repair the trim. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+
* **Seat wear:** Description: The fabric or vinyl seats may show signs of wear, especially on the driver's seat. Symptoms: Worn or torn seat material. Solution: Seat repair or replacement. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+

**Summary:**

The 1990 Nissan Pickup is generally a reliable vehicle, but it has been known to experience some common issues. These issues can affect the engine, transmission, suspension, electrical system, exterior, and interior. While some issues like rust or interior wear are not severe, others like engine or transmission failure can be costly to repair. Potential buyers should be aware of these potential issues before purchasing a used 1990 Nissan Pickup.
