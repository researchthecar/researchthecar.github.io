# 2022 Nissan Pathfinder Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2022 Nissan Pathfinder brings a rugged new design and a modernized cabin to the midsize three-row SUV segment, moving away from older CVT transmissions in favor of a responsive conventional automatic. While initial owner satisfaction leans positive regarding styling and family utility, used-car buyers should review specific recurring complaints. Notable areas of attention include premature rear brake wear, spontaneous panoramic sunroof glass breakage, water leaks, and occasional electronic or powertrain glitches.

---

## Engine

### 3.5L V6 Engine

Powered by a 3.5-liter V6 engine producing 284 horsepower and 259 lb-ft of torque, this model provides solid towing capacity and confident acceleration for daily driving and family road trips.

**Unintended Neutral Shift**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers report that the vehicle may unexpectedly shift into neutral while driving, causing a sudden loss of acceleration and potential safety hazards in traffic.
- **Solution:** Have the powertrain control module (PCM) inspected and updated by a certified technician or dealership.

## Transmission

### 9-Speed Automatic Transmission

Equipped with a 9-speed automatic transmission paired with either front-wheel drive or intelligent four-wheel drive, offering smoother power delivery compared to legacy CVTs.

**Shifter or Park Defect**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers experience unusual noises, shifting delays, or difficulty engaging park properly, sometimes triggering warning lights or requiring towing.
- **Solution:** Inspect transmission control modules, linkage, and software calibration; update or replace faulty transmission components as needed.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

Braking components provide adequate stopping power for this three-row SUV, though rear pads may require more frequent attention than expected.

**Premature Brake Wear and Failure Symptoms**
- **Symptoms:** Owners report unusually rapid wear on the rear brake pads and rotors, sometimes accompanied by a feeling that the brakes are not fully releasing or causing vibration.
- **Solution:** Inspect brake pads, rotors, and calipers regularly; replace worn components and ensure slide pins are properly lubricated.

## Exterior

### Panoramic Sunroof

Exterior panels and lighting generally hold up well, though the panoramic sunroof option has been a point of concern for unexpected glass failure.

**Spontaneous Glass Shattering**
- **Symptoms:** The panoramic sunroof glass may unexpectedly explode outward or shatter while driving at moderate to highway speeds, creating a loud noise and raining glass shards.
- **Solution:** Inspect the sunroof assembly for stress points and replace shattered glass panels under comprehensive insurance or warranty if applicable.

## Interior

### Seats and Cabin

The interior offers flexible passenger and cargo space, though specific complaints focus on seat positioning and occasional second-row latch movement.

**Front Passenger Seat Ergonomics and Stability**
- **Symptoms:** Front passengers report that seats feel too low or uncomfortable, and second-row seats may unexpectedly shift or unlatch when encountering bumps.
- **Solution:** Check seat anchor mechanisms and track adjustments; consult a dealership for any available seating hardware updates.

## Safety

### Driver Assistance and Restraints

Advanced safety suites provide helpful driver aids, but sensor and camera calibration issues can temporarily disable key assist features.

**Steering Assist and Forward Collision Sensor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Lane departure steering assist or forward collision sensors may fail randomly, disabling adaptive cruise control or automatic emergency braking features.
- **Solution:** Have radar and camera sensors recalibrated or replaced by a certified service center.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V742000: Seats:Mid/Rear Assembly:Head Restraint](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=Pathfinder&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 202, "Head Restraints." Headrests that move or detach may not adequately restrain occupants in a crash, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace all three third-row seat headrests, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V743000: Seats:Critical Fasteners](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=Pathfinder&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** The second-row seat back frames may have insufficient welds that could separate. Seat backs with separated welds may fail to adequately restrain occupants during a crash, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replaced the seat assemblies as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V706000: Latches/Locks/Linkages:Hood:Latch](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=Pathfinder&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 113, "Hood Latch System." A detached secondary hood latch may allow the hood to open if the primary hood latch is not engaged, obstructing the driver's view and increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the secondary hood latch bracket as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V111000: Seats:Critical Fasteners](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=Pathfinder&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 210, "Seat Belt Assembly Anchorages." A missing mounting bolt may allow the seat track to move during a crash, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the second-row seat track and install a new mounting bolt as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V527000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=Pathfinder&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** When the Sirius XM subscription is set to "Not Subscribed," it may cause the infotainment head unit to reboot, resulting in an inoperative rearview camera. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 111, "Rear Visibility." An inoperative rearview camera display reduces the driver's rear.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2022 Nissan Pathfinder is a capable and family-friendly three-row SUV with strong overall performance, but prospective buyers should perform a thorough pre-purchase inspection. Pay close attention to the service history for rear brake wear, verify that all driver-assistance sensors are operating properly, and check the cabin and roof lining for any signs of water intrusion or sunroof glass damage.
