The 2019 Nissan Pathfinder is a mid-size SUV known for its spacious interior, comfortable ride, and user-friendly technology. All 2019 Pathfinders come equipped with a 3.5-liter V6 engine producing 284 horsepower and 259 lb-ft of torque, paired with a continuously variable transmission (CVT). Owners often praise the Pathfinder for its ample passenger and cargo space, making it a good choice for families. However, some common complaints include CVT transmission issues, electrical problems, and concerns about overall reliability.

**Engine**

**3.5L V6**

*   **Excessive Oil Consumption**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported higher than expected oil consumption, requiring frequent oil top-offs between scheduled oil changes. Symptoms include low oil level warning lights and visible exhaust smoke in severe cases.
    *   Solution: The solution varies depending on the severity. It may involve replacing valve stem seals or piston rings. In some extreme cases, engine replacement may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $5,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**CVT (Continuously Variable Transmission)**

*   **Juddering/Shaking/Hesitation**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Owners have reported experiencing juddering, shaking, or hesitation during acceleration, particularly at lower speeds. This can feel like the transmission is slipping or struggling to find the right gear.
    *   Solution: In some cases, a software update to the transmission control module (TCM) can alleviate the issue. In other cases, a CVT fluid change may help. More severe cases may require complete CVT replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $5,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Transmission Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported complete transmission failure, resulting in the vehicle being undrivable. This may be preceded by loud noises, slipping, or a complete loss of power transfer.
    *   Solution: Transmission replacement is typically required.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $4,000 - $6,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

No Issues Found

**Electrical**

*   **Battery Drain**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have experienced issues with the battery draining unexpectedly, particularly when the vehicle is left sitting for extended periods. This can result in the vehicle being unable to start.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis involves checking for parasitic draws in the electrical system. The faulty component causing the drain needs to be identified and replaced. Potential culprits include faulty door lock actuators, infotainment systems, or other electrical modules.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Infotainment System Malfunctions**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Intermittent issues with the infotainment system, including freezing, crashing, unresponsive touch screen, and Bluetooth connectivity problems.
    *   Solution: Start with a system reset. Software updates from the dealer are also a possibility. If those don't work, replacing the infotainment system may be needed.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 - $1,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Automatic Liftgate Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The automatic liftgate may fail to open or close properly, or may become unresponsive.
    *   Solution: Check the liftgate struts for wear or damage. Also, inspect the liftgate motor and sensors. Replacement of the struts, motor, or sensors may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Air Conditioning Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: The air conditioning system not blowing cold air or only blowing warm air. This issue can occur intermittently or constantly.
    * Solution: Repair any leaks to the A/C system. Replace the A/C compressor, condenser, or other components if damaged. Recharge the A/C system.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2019 Nissan Pathfinder presents a mixed bag for potential buyers. While it excels in space and comfort, the reported CVT transmission issues are a significant concern due to their potential severity and cost. Electrical problems and potential oil consumption further contribute to potential repair bills. While some issues are minor and easily addressed, others could lead to major expenses. Due diligence, a thorough pre-purchase inspection, and a Carfax report are highly recommended.
