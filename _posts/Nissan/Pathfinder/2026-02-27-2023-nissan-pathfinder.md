# 2023 Nissan Pathfinder Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2023 Nissan Pathfinder is a capable and spacious three-row midsize SUV offering modern styling, comfortable seating, and a standard 9-speed automatic transmission that replaced older CVT setups. While many owners enjoy its comfort and utility, NHTSA owner complaints highlight recurring areas of concern, notably electronic camera system failures, premature brake wear, and occasional powertrain hiccups.

---

## Engine

### 3.5L V6 Engine

The Pathfinder comes standard with a 3.5-liter V6 engine producing approximately 284 horsepower and 259 lb-ft of torque. It provides smooth acceleration and adequate towing capability for daily family duties.

**Engine Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicles may unexpectedly lose power or stall while driving at highway speeds or in traffic.
- **Solution:** Diagnose fuel delivery and powertrain management systems; faulty components such as fuel pumps may require replacement.

## Transmission

### 9-Speed Automatic Transmission

Paired with the V6 engine, the 9-speed automatic transmission delivers crisp shifts and improves drivability compared to previous generations. Proper software calibration is essential for optimal performance.

**Unintended Neutral Shift**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may unexpectedly shift into neutral while driving, triggering warning lights and loss of propulsion.
- **Solution:** Check for available transmission control module software updates or consult a dealership for diagnostic evaluation.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

The braking system provides confident stopping power, though some owners report premature wear on rear brake components.

**Premature Rear Brake Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Rear brake pads and rotors wear out prematurely at relatively low mileage, sometimes accompanied by vibration or noise.
- **Solution:** Inspect brake pads and rotors regularly, and replace worn components as necessary.
- **Cost:** Varies by repair scope

## Electrical

### Camera and Infotainment Electronics

Electrical systems power advanced driver assistance and infotainment features, though camera control modules have proven prone to premature failure.

**Around View Monitor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The 360-degree backup and surround-view camera system fails completely, rendering display feeds inoperable.
- **Solution:** Inspect camera control units and wiring harnesses; faulty control modules may need to be replaced.

## Safety

### Seat Belts

Safety equipment includes comprehensive airbag and seatbelt systems designed to protect occupants, though mechanical latch failures have been reported by some owners.

**Seatbelt Locking Mechanism Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Second-row seatbelt buckles fail to lock securely in place or refuse to release properly.
- **Solution:** Have malfunctioning seatbelt assemblies inspected and replaced by an authorized dealer.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V268000: Seats:Critical Fasteners](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=Pathfinder&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** The driver's powered seat may not be fully secured to the seat frame, causing the seat frame welds to detach from the rear inboard seat bracket. An unsecured seat with broken welds may not properly restrain an occupant during a crash, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the driver's seat rail clearance, and if necessary, replace the driver's seat cushion frame, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2023 Nissan Pathfinder is a comfortable and well-equipped family SUV, but prospective buyers should inspect the vehicle thoroughly. Pay close attention to brake pad wear, test all camera and driver-assistance electronics, and verify that the powertrain shifts smoothly without hesitation or unintended neutral drops during a test drive.
