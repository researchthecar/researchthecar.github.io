**1990 Nissan Pathfinder**

The 1990 Nissan Pathfinder was a popular SUV that was known for its ruggedness and off-road capabilities. It was available with a 3.0-liter V6 engine that produced 143 horsepower and 169 lb-ft of torque. The Pathfinder was also available with a 5-speed manual or 4-speed automatic transmission.

People liked the Pathfinder for its spacious interior, comfortable ride, and reliable performance. However, there were some common issues that people experienced with the vehicle.

**Engine**

* **3.0-liter V6**
    * **Head gasket failure:** This was a common issue on the 3.0-liter V6 engine. Symptoms included a loss of coolant, white smoke from the exhaust, and overheating. The solution was to replace the head gasket. Estimated cost to fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
    * **Timing chain failure:** This was another common issue on the 3.0-liter V6 engine. Symptoms included a rattling noise from the engine, loss of power, and eventually engine failure. The solution was to replace the timing chain. Estimated cost to fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
    * **Intake manifold gasket failure:** This was a less common issue, but it could cause a loss of power and fuel economy. The solution was to replace the intake manifold gasket. Estimated cost to fix: $500 - $1,000+

**Transmission**

* **5-speed manual:**
    * **Transmission noise:** This was a common issue on the 5-speed manual transmission. Symptoms included a grinding noise when shifting gears. The solution was to replace the transmission. Estimated cost to fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
* **4-speed automatic:**
    * **Transmission slipping:** This was a common issue on the 4-speed automatic transmission. Symptoms included a loss of power and eventually transmission failure. The solution was to rebuild the transmission. Estimated cost to fix: $2,000 - $3,000+

**Suspension**

* **Front suspension:**
    * **Strut failure:** This was a common issue on the Pathfinder. Symptoms included a clunking noise when driving over bumps and a loss of handling. The solution was to replace the struts. Estimated cost to fix: $500 - $1,000+
* **Rear suspension:**
    * **Leaf spring failure:** This was a less common issue, but it could cause a loss of handling and a rough ride. The solution was to replace the leaf springs. Estimated cost to fix: $500 - $1,000+

**Electrical**

* **Alternator failure:** This was a common issue on the Pathfinder. Symptoms included a loss of power, dimming lights, and a dead battery. The solution was to replace the alternator. Estimated cost to fix: $500 - $1,000+
* **Starter failure:** This was a less common issue, but it could cause the engine to not start. The solution was to replace the starter. Estimated cost to fix: $300 - $500+

**Exterior**

* **Rust:** This was a common issue on the Pathfinder, especially in areas where there was a lot of salt on the roads. Symptoms included rust on the body panels, frame, and suspension components. The solution was to repair or replace the rusted parts. Estimated cost to fix: $500 - $2,000+

**Interior**

* **Door lock failure:** This was a common issue on the Pathfinder. Symptoms included a door that would not lock or unlock. The solution was to replace the door lock actuator. Estimated cost to fix: $300 - $500+
* **Window regulator failure:** This was a less common issue, but it could cause a window to become stuck in the up or down position. The solution was to replace the window regulator. Estimated cost to fix: $500 - $1,000+

**Summary**

The 1990 Nissan Pathfinder was a popular SUV that was known for its ruggedness and off-road capabilities. However, there were some common issues that people experienced with the vehicle, including head gasket failure, timing chain failure, and transmission problems. The cost of these issues could range from $500 to over $2,500.
