# 2018 Nissan Pathfinder Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2018 Nissan Pathfinder is a three-row midsize SUV offering spacious seating and decent towing capabilities. However, prospective buyers should approach with caution due to recurring owner complaints regarding transmission durability, electronic sensor glitches, and infotainment blackouts. Thoroughly inspecting the powertrain and electronic features during a test drive is strongly advised.

---

## Engine

### 3.5L V6

The 3.5-liter V6 engine produces around 284 horsepower and 259 lb-ft of torque, providing capable performance for daily driving and towing. While generally powerful, some owners have reported premature knocking and hesitation issues that require close inspection.

**Engine Knocking and Hesitation**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers report sudden acceleration hesitation accompanied by abnormal knocking noises from the engine bay, potentially indicating premature internal wear.
- **Solution:** Requires professional mechanical diagnosis; severely worn engines may require component overhaul or replacement.

## Transmission

### Xtronic Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)

Equipped with an Xtronic Continuously Variable Transmission paired with front-wheel drive or optional four-wheel drive, this setup prioritizes fuel economy. Buyers should pay close attention to CVT operation, as transmission longevity is a known concern for this model year.

**Transmission Slipping and Whining**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle experiences sudden slipping while driving at highway speeds, severe shaking, or develops a noticeable whining noise from the transmission housing.
- **Solution:** Often requires professional transmission fluid service, valve body replacement, or complete CVT unit replacement.

## Brakes

### Anti-Lock Brake System and Sensors

**ABS Warning Lights and Switch Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** ABS warning lights illuminate repeatedly shortly after service, or switch contamination prevents proper vehicle starting and gear shifting.
- **Solution:** Inspect stop lamp switches and anti-lock brake actuators; clean or replace contaminated electrical components.

## Electrical

### Infotainment and Display Control Unit

**Blank Display Screen**
- **Symptoms:** The infotainment display and backup camera screen go completely blank or stop functioning, disabling climate controls and audio systems.
- **Solution:** May require a software update, head unit reset, or complete replacement of the Display Control Unit (DCU).

## Safety

### Collision Avoidance and Radar Sensors

**Front Radar Sensor Unavailable**
- **Symptoms:** A persistent warning message appears indicating the front radar or automatic emergency braking (AEB) sensor is unavailable due to an obstruction, even when clear.
- **Solution:** Clean sensor areas or have a dealership recalibrate or replace faulty radar sensor modules.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V807000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Antilock/Traction Control/Electronic Limited Slip](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=Pathfinder&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** A brake fluid leak on the circuit board may result in an electrical short, increasing the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Nissan will notify owners, and dealers will replace the ABS actuator, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V601000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Antilock/Traction Control/Electronic Limited Slip](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=Pathfinder&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** A brake fluid leak on the circuit board may result in an electrical short, increasing the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Nissan will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the serial number on the ABS actuator, replacing it as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V654000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=Pathfinder&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 111, "Rear Visibility." The lack of an image in the back-up camera display increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2018 Nissan Pathfinder offers great utility and family-friendly features, but potential buyers must proceed with caution. Significant owner complaints regarding CVT transmission reliability, electrical display failures, and safety sensor errors mean a pre-purchase inspection by a qualified mechanic is essential before buying.
