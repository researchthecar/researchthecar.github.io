# 2019 Nissan Pathfinder Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2019 Nissan Pathfinder, buyers will find a spacious three-row crossover equipped with a standard V6 engine and a continuously variable transmission. While it offers comfortable everyday driving and useful family features, prospective owners should review owner-reported feedback regarding specific mechanical and electrical components. Thoroughly inspecting the vehicle's maintenance history, powertrain performance, and electrical accessories during a test drive helps ensure a more confident purchase.

---

## Engine

### 3.5L V6 Engine

Powered by a 3.5-liter V6 engine producing around 284 horsepower, this model provides solid acceleration for highway cruising and towing. Routine maintenance, particularly timely oil changes, is essential to keep the engine operating smoothly and prevent premature wear.

## Transmission

### Xtronic CVT

The vehicle utilizes an Xtronic continuously variable transmission paired with front-wheel drive or intelligent four-wheel drive. Buyers should pay close attention to smooth operation and fluid condition during a test drive, as proper upkeep is vital for longevity.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

The braking system provides adequate stopping power under normal conditions, but proper hydraulic and switch maintenance is critical for safety.

**Brake Pedal Softness and Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The brake pedal travels all the way to the floor with reduced or complete loss of braking action, sometimes accompanied by warning lights.
- **Solution:** Inspect the master cylinder, brake lines, and stop lamp switch, and replace faulty components as necessary.

## Exterior

### Door Lock Actuator

Electrical accessories generally function well, though individual door lock actuators can wear out over time.

**Power Door Lock Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Specific doors fail to lock or unlock using the power switch or key fob, often affecting individual side passenger doors.
- **Solution:** Inspect and replace the malfunctioning door lock actuator mechanism.

## Safety

### Forward Collision Avoidance

Driver assistance features enhance everyday safety, but sensor calibration issues can occasionally trigger unintended warnings.

**Forward Collision System Warnings**
- **Symptoms:** The forward collision avoidance warning light illuminates unexpectedly, accompanied by audible alerts or erratic system behavior while driving.
- **Solution:** Have the sensor alignment and system software inspected by a qualified repair facility.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V654000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=Pathfinder&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 111, "Rear Visibility." The lack of an image in the back-up camera display increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 19V807000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Antilock/Traction Control/Electronic Limited Slip](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=Pathfinder&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** A brake fluid leak on the circuit board may result in an electrical short, increasing the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Nissan will notify owners, and dealers will replace the ABS actuator, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2019 Nissan Pathfinder presents a practical choice for families seeking three-row capability and a comfortable ride. While most systems operate reliably, buyers should carefully evaluate the service history, check for proper electrical and brake function, and ensure that all safety features operate as intended before finalizing a purchase.
