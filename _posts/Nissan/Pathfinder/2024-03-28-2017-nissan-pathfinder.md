**2017 Nissan Pathfinder**

The 2017 Nissan Pathfinder is a popular three-row SUV that offers a spacious interior, a user-friendly infotainment system, and good fuel economy. It comes with a 3.5-liter V6 engine that produces 284 horsepower and 259 lb-ft of torque, paired with a continuously variable transmission (CVT).

Despite its strengths, the 2017 Pathfinder has received numerous complaints from owners regarding various issues.

**Engine**

* **3.5L V6**
    * **Intake Valve Deposits**
        * Description/Symptoms: Buildup of carbon deposits on the intake valves can cause reduced engine performance, misfires, and increased fuel consumption.
        * Solution: Cleaning or replacing the intake valves
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
        * Recalls: None Found
    * **Timing Chain Tensioner Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: A faulty timing chain tensioner can lead to excessive noise, engine stalling, and potential catastrophic engine damage.
        * Solution: Replacing the timing chain tensioner and associated components
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

* **Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)**
    * **Jerking or Hesitation During Acceleration**
        * Description/Symptoms: Rough or hesitant gear changes, especially when accelerating from a stop or low speeds.
        * Solution: Updating the transmission software or, in severe cases, replacing the CVT unit.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $1,000+ depending on the severity of the issue
        * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Rear Suspension Sagging**
    * Description/Symptoms: Premature sagging or droopiness in the rear suspension, resulting in uneven ride height and potential handling issues.
    * Solution: Replacing the rear suspension components (e.g., coils, springs)
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
        * Recalls: None Found
* **Rear Shock Absorber Leaks**
    * Description/Symptoms: Leaking shock absorbers can cause reduced ride quality, uneven handling, and potential failure of the suspension system.
    * Solution: Replacing the faulty shock absorbers
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+ per shock
        * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain**
    * Description/Symptoms: Excessive parasitic battery drain, leading to a dead or depleted battery.
    * Solution: Identifying and repairing the source of the drain, such as faulty components or software issues.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
        * Recalls: None Found
* **Infotainment Screen Glitches**
    * Description/Symptoms: Occasional freezing, lagging, or unresponsive touchscreen on the infotainment system.
    * Solution: Updating the software or replacing the infotainment unit.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $1,000+ depending on the severity of the issue
        * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Loose Exterior Trim**
    * Description/Symptoms: Various exterior trim pieces, such as running boards, door handles, and mirrors, may become loose or detach.
    * Solution: Tightening or replacing the affected trim pieces.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Panoramic Sunroof Leaks**
    * Description/Symptoms: Water leaking from the panoramic sunroof during heavy rain or car washes.
    * Solution: Inspecting and resealing the sunroof seals.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2017 Nissan Pathfinder has a number of common issues that can range in severity and cost to fix. The most significant issues include timing chain tensioner failure, CVT problems, and electrical issues. While some issues are relatively minor, others can be costly to repair. Potential buyers should be aware of these issues and factor them into their decision-making process.
