The 2016 Nissan Pathfinder is a mid-size SUV that seats seven passengers, known for its comfortable ride and spacious interior. The sole engine option for the 2016 model year is a 3.5-liter V6 engine producing 260 horsepower and 240 lb-ft of torque, paired with a continuously variable transmission (CVT). Many owners appreciate the Pathfinder's smooth ride and ample cargo space, while some have expressed concerns about the reliability of the CVT and some electrical components.

**Engine**

**3.5L V6**

*   **Timing Chain Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Engine rattle or knocking noise, especially on startup. Check engine light may illuminate. Can lead to engine damage if left unresolved.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the timing chain, tensioners, and guides.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Oil Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Oil spots on the ground under the engine, burning oil smell, low oil level. Leaks can occur from valve cover gaskets, oil pan gasket, or front timing cover.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the leaking gasket or seal.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,200+ (depending on the location of the leak)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**CVT (Continuously Variable Transmission)**

*   **Juddering/Shaking**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Noticeable shaking or juddering during acceleration, especially at lower speeds. May be more pronounced when the transmission is cold.
    *   Solution: Software update of the TCM (Transmission Control Module) or CVT replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 (software update) to $3,500 - $5,000+ (CVT replacement).
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Transmission Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Complete loss of power, inability to shift, loud whining or grinding noises.
    *   Solution: CVT replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $3,500 - $5,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Rear Suspension Sagging**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rear of the vehicle sitting lower than normal, especially when loaded with passengers or cargo. Can lead to a bouncy ride.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the rear shocks or springs.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Backup Camera Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Backup camera displaying a blank screen, distorted image, or not functioning at all.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the backup camera or repair of the wiring.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600+
    *   Recalls: 17V093000

*   **Battery Drain**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Dead battery after sitting for a short period of time.
    *   Solution: Diagnose parasitic draw on the electrical system and repair the faulty component.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Power Liftgate Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Power liftgate not opening or closing properly, getting stuck, or making unusual noises.
    *   Solution: Repair or replacement of the liftgate motor, struts, or sensors.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Air Conditioning Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Inadequate cooling, especially on the driver's side. Can be caused by a refrigerant leak, compressor failure, or blend door actuator issue.
    *   Solution: Recharge the refrigerant, replace the compressor, or replace the blend door actuator.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $1,200+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Navigation/Infotainment System Freezing or Crashing**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The infotainment screen freezes, becomes unresponsive, or crashes frequently.
    *   Solution: Software update or replacement of the infotainment system unit.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $1,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2016 Nissan Pathfinder, while offering a comfortable ride and spacious interior, has some notable reliability concerns. The most common issues are related to the CVT transmission, which can experience juddering, shaking, or even complete failure. Engine-related problems, such as timing chain issues and oil leaks, are also prevalent. Electrical problems, such as backup camera failure and battery drain, add to the list of potential headaches. While some issues can be resolved with relatively inexpensive fixes, others, such as CVT replacement, can be quite costly. Potential buyers should be aware of these issues and factor them into their purchase decision.
