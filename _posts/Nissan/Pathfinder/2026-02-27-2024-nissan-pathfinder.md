# 2024 Nissan Pathfinder Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2024 Nissan Pathfinder is a capable and comfortable three-row midsize SUV offering solid family utility. While many owners enjoy its spacious interior and smooth ride, used car buyers should note a few recurring pattern complaints reported to safety regulators, particularly regarding premature brake wear and minor powertrain or electrical quirks.

---

## Engine

### 3.5L V6 Engine

Equipped with a naturally aspirated 3.5-liter V6 producing around 284 horsepower and 259 lb-ft of torque, providing strong daily drivability. This conventional V6 generally offers familiar mechanics, though occasional sensor and fuel delivery complaints have been noted by owners.

**Engine Stalling and Drivability**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicles may experience unexpected warning lights, sensor faults, or stalling behavior.
- **Solution:** Diagnostic scans by a qualified technician are necessary to isolate faulty sensors or software calibration issues.

**Fuel Pump Failures**
- **Symptoms:** Engine may fail to start or experience check-engine lights accompanied by fuel delivery issues.
- **Solution:** Inspect fuel pressure and replace the failing fuel pump module if necessary.

## Transmission

### 9-Speed Automatic Transmission

Features a 9-speed conventional automatic transmission replacing older CVTs, paired with front-wheel drive or intelligent four-wheel drive. While generally smoother and more responsive than past generations, some drivers have reported erratic shifting behavior and unexpected neutral drops.

**Unintended Neutral Shift and Shifting Hesitation**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may unexpectedly drop out of gear, coast, or whine loudly when attempting to climb hills or shift under load.
- **Solution:** Check for powertrain control module software updates or have a professional inspect internal transmission components.

## Brakes

### Brake Pads and Rotors

The braking system provides adequate stopping power for a heavy three-row SUV, but multiple owner reports highlight an abnormally high rate of premature rear brake wear.

**Premature Rear Brake Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Rear brake pads and rotors wear down rapidly, sometimes requiring replacement well before 35,000 miles accompanied by squealing or scraping noises.
- **Solution:** Inspect brake pad thickness and rotor condition regularly; replace worn friction materials and service brake calipers if sticking.

## Electrical

### Battery and Wiring

Modern electrical architectures rely heavily on sensors, displays, and networked modules that require a healthy battery and intact wiring harnesses.

**Battery Drain and Wiring Damage**
- **Symptoms:** Frequent battery failures or electrical glitches due to rodent damage on soy-coated wiring insulation.
- **Solution:** Inspect wiring harnesses for rodent nesting or damage and replace compromised sections as needed.

## Exterior

### Sunroof and Glass

Exterior glass and trim components generally hold up well, though isolated reports of spontaneous sunroof glass failure warrant inspection during a used vehicle purchase.

**Sunroof Shattering**
- **Symptoms:** Panoramic sunroof glass may spontaneously crack or shatter while driving without external impact.
- **Solution:** Replace the damaged glass panel and inspect the frame for alignment issues or stress points.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V154000: Air Bags:Frontal:Driver Side:Inflator Module](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=Pathfinder&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** Due to a manufacturing issue, the driver's air bag inflator may absorb moisture, and cause the air bag to deploy improperly. An air bag that deploys improperly may not protect the driver as intended during a crash, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the front driver's air bag assembly, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V176000: Seat Belts:Pretensioner](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=Pathfinder&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard numbers 208 "Occupant Crash Protection," 209 "Seat Belt Assemblies," and 210 "Seat Belt Assembly Anchorages." An unsecured lap belt pretensioner will not properly restrain an occupant during a crash, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the left-side front lap seat belt pretensioner assemblies as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2024 Nissan Pathfinder is a practical and comfortable choice for families seeking three-row capability and a traditional V6 with a 9-speed automatic. Prospective buyers should carefully inspect the condition of the rear brakes and verify proper transmission operation during a test drive, as these represent the most frequently cited areas of concern in owner complaints.
