# 2004 Nissan Pathfinder

### Overview
The 2004 Nissan Pathfinder, a capable mid-size SUV, offered a blend of off-road prowess and on-road comfort. It was available with a robust V6 engine and a choice of automatic or manual transmissions, catering to different driver preferences. While generally considered a reliable vehicle for its era, like any automotive model, it has certain areas that have seen more frequent reports of issues. This analysis focuses on common mechanical and electrical concerns reported by owners and technicians, excluding routine maintenance and standard wear and tear, to provide a clear picture of its long-term reliability.

---


## Engine

### 3.5L V6 (VQ35DE)
**Timing Chain Cover Gasket Leak**
- **Symptoms:** Oil leaks detected around the front of the engine, particularly near the timing chain cover.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the timing chain cover gasket.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600

**Intake Manifold Gasket Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Coolant leaks, engine misfires, rough idle, and potential overheating.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the intake manifold gaskets.
- **Cost:** $400 - $800


## Transmission

### 5-Speed Automatic (RE5R05A)
**Harsh Shifting / Slipping**
- **Symptoms:** Delayed engagement, harsh shifts between gears, or slipping sensation.
- **Solution:** Transmission fluid and filter change, or internal transmission repair/rebuild.
- **Cost:** $300 - $3000+


### 6-Speed Manual
**No widespread issues reported.**
- **Symptoms:** N/A
- **Solution:** N/A
- **Cost:** Varies


## Suspension

### Standard Independent Front Suspension / Solid Rear Axle
**Worn Ball Joints and Tie Rod Ends**
- **Symptoms:** Clunking noises over bumps, vague steering, uneven tire wear.
- **Solution:** Replacement of worn ball joints and tie rod ends.
- **Cost:** $300 - $700

**Leaking Struts/Shocks**
- **Symptoms:** Bouncy ride, visible fluid leaks from shock absorbers or struts.
- **Solution:** Replacement of worn struts or shocks.
- **Cost:** $400 - $800


## Electrical

### General Electrical System
**Instrument Cluster Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** Intermittent or complete failure of gauges, warning lights staying on or not illuminating.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of the instrument cluster.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1000

**Window Motor Failures**
- **Symptoms:** Power windows not operating, slow operation, or unusual noises.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the power window motor and regulator.
- **Cost:** $200 - $400


## Exterior

### Body and Trim
**Rear Hatch Strut Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Rear hatch not staying open on its own.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the rear hatch support struts.
- **Cost:** $100 - $200

**Paint Fading/Peeling**
- **Symptoms:** Visible degradation of the paint finish, especially on horizontal surfaces.
- **Solution:** Repainting affected areas or full vehicle repaint.
- **Cost:** $500 - $3000+


## Interior

### Cabin Components
**HVAC Blower Motor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** No airflow from vents, or intermittent fan operation.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the HVAC blower motor.
- **Cost:** $200 - $400

**Seat Heater Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Heated seats not working or working intermittently.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of seat heating elements or switches.
- **Cost:** $200 - $500


--- 
### Summary Verdict
The 2004 Nissan Pathfinder is a generally dependable SUV, particularly known for its capable V6 engine and robust drivetrain. The most commonly reported issues revolve around the automatic transmission, which can develop shifting problems over time, and some engine-related gasket leaks (timing chain cover, intake manifold). Suspension components like ball joints and tie rods are also areas to monitor for wear. Electrical gremlins, such as instrument cluster issues and window motors, can occur. Exterior paint and interior comfort features like seat heaters and HVAC blowers are also noted for occasional failures. Despite these points, with proper maintenance and addressing these common issues, the 2004 Pathfinder can remain a reliable vehicle.
