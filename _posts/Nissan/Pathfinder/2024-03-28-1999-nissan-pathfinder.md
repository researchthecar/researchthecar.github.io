**Introduction:**

The 1999 Nissan Pathfinder is a mid-size SUV that offers a reliable and spacious driving experience. It comes equipped with a choice of two engine options: a 3.3-liter V6 with 170 horsepower and 202 lb-ft of torque, or a 2.4-liter 4-cylinder with 143 horsepower and 154 lb-ft of torque. Both engines are paired with either a 5-speed manual or a 4-speed automatic transmission. Praised for its comfortable ride and well-equipped interior, the Pathfinder also has some reported issues that may require attention.

**Engine**

* **3.3-liter V6**
    * **Valve Cover Gasket Leak:** Loose valve cover bolts can cause an oil leak from the valve cover gasket. This can lead to a noticeable drop in oil levels.
        * **Solution:** Replace the valve cover gasket and tighten the bolts to the proper torque specifications.
        * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $200 - $400+
        * **Recalls:** None Found
* **2.4-liter 4-cylinder**
    * **No Issues Found**

**Transmission**

* **5-speed Manual**
    * **Synchro Mesh Issues:** Worn synchronizers can cause difficulty shifting gears, especially from 2nd to 3rd gear.
        * **Solution:** Replace the faulty synchronizers and gears.
        * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $1,000 - $2,000+
        * **Recalls:** None Found
* **4-speed Automatic**
    * **Overdrive Clutch Failure:** The overdrive clutch can wear out prematurely, causing issues with holding the overdrive gear.
        * **Solution:** Replace the failed overdrive clutch and related components.
        * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $800 - $1,200+
        * **Recalls:** None Found

**Suspension**

* **Front Struts:** Worn struts can lead to a rough and bumpy ride.
    * **Solution:** Replace the worn struts with new ones.
    * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $400 - $800+
    * **Recalls:** None Found
* **Rear Shock Absorbers:** Leaking or damaged shock absorbers can affect ride quality and handling.
    * **Solution:** Replace the faulty shock absorbers.
    * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $200 - $400+
    * **Recalls:** None Found

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain:** A faulty alternator or weak battery can cause the battery to drain quickly.
    * **Solution:** Replace the faulty alternator or battery and test the electrical system.
    * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $200 - $500+
    * **Recalls:** None Found
* **Power Window Failure:** The power window motors or switches can fail, causing windows to get stuck.
    * **Solution:** Replace the failed motor or switch.
    * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $200 - $500+
    * **Recalls:** None Found

**Interior**

* **Headliner Sagging:** The headliner fabric can sag and detach from the roof.
    * **Solution:** Reattach the headliner using adhesive or replace it with a new one.
    * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $200 - $400+
    * **Recalls:** None Found
* **Seatbelt Retractor Failure:** The seatbelt retractor can fail to retract the belt properly.
    * **Solution:** Replace the faulty retractor.
    * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $200 - $400+
    * **Recalls:** NHTSA Recall Campaign ID: 11E014000

**Exterior**

* **Rust on Frame and Body:** The frame and body of the Pathfinder can be prone to rust, especially in areas exposed to salt or moisture.
    * **Solution:** Apply rust-resistant coatings and address any rust spots promptly.
    * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** Varies depending on the severity of the rust.
    * **Recalls:** None Found

**Summary:**

Overall, the 1999 Nissan Pathfinder offers a comfortable and reliable driving experience. However, it is important to be aware of the potential issues that may arise, particularly with the transmission and suspension. While the cost of repairs can vary, they can range from minor expenses to more significant costs. Regular maintenance and addressing issues promptly can help prolong the life of the vehicle and minimize costly repairs.
