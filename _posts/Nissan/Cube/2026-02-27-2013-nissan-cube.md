# 2013 Nissan Cube

### Overview
The 2013 Nissan Cube is a subcompact hatchback known for its distinctive, boxy styling and practical interior. Reliability for this model year is generally considered good, with owners reporting fewer significant mechanical issues compared to some competitors. The focus of common concerns tends to be on less critical areas, such as minor electrical glitches or interior wear, rather than major powertrain failures. Routine maintenance is key to ensuring its longevity, but for the most part, the Cube offers a dependable ownership experience for its target demographic.

### CVT (Continuously Variable Transmission)
**Hesitation or Jerking**
- **Symptoms:** Occasional hesitation during acceleration or a slight jerking sensation.
- **Solution:** Transmission fluid flush and filter replacement; potential software update for the transmission control module.

## Electrical

### General Electrical System
**Power Window Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** Power windows may operate slowly, intermittently, or stop working altogether.
- **Solution:** Lubrication of window tracks, replacement of window regulator or motor.
- **Cost:** $200 - $500

**Key Fob Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Key fob may become unresponsive or require multiple attempts to unlock/lock doors.
- **Solution:** Battery replacement in the key fob; reprogramming of the fob.
- **Cost:** $50 - $150

## Interior

### Cabin Materials
**Seat Fabric Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Premature wear or pilling on seat fabric, especially on driver's side.
- **Solution:** Seat covers or professional cleaning/repair.
- **Cost:** $50 - $200

**Center Console Durability**
- **Symptoms:** Scratches or wear on the plastic surfaces of the center console.
- **Solution:** Protective films or replacement of console trim pieces.
- **Cost:** $100 - $300

### Summary Verdict
The 2013 Nissan Cube presents itself as a reliable choice in the subcompact segment. Its 1.8L engine and standard suspension systems are generally trouble-free. The CVT transmission, while generally robust, can occasionally exhibit minor issues like hesitation, which are typically addressable with maintenance. Electrical components, particularly power windows and key fobs, are the most common areas for minor complaints, along with some interior wear on high-contact surfaces. Overall, the Cube is a practical and dependable vehicle with a good track record for avoiding major mechanical failures.
