## Transmission

### CVT (Continuously Variable Transmission)
**Hesitation or Jerking**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle may hesitate during acceleration or exhibit jerky movements, particularly at lower speeds. Some owners report a feeling of the transmission slipping.
- **Solution:** Software updates for the CVT control module, or in more severe cases, transmission fluid flush and replacement, or complete transmission replacement.
- **Cost:** $300 - $800 (for fluid service/software), $3,000 - $6,000 (for replacement)

## Electrical

### Infotainment System
**Screen Freezing or Unresponsiveness**
- **Symptoms:** The infotainment touchscreen may freeze, become unresponsive to touch inputs, or reboot unexpectedly.
- **Solution:** Software updates for the infotainment system, or a reset of the unit. In rare cases, the unit may need replacement.
- **Cost:** $100 - $300 (for software/reset), $500 - $1,500 (for replacement)


### Battery
**Premature Battery Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Difficulty starting the vehicle, dimming headlights, or electrical components malfunctioning due to low voltage.
- **Solution:** Battery testing and replacement.
- **Cost:** $150 - $300

### Summary Verdict
The 2020 Nissan Rogue Sport generally presents a good reliability record, with the powertrain (engine and suspension) being particularly robust. The primary areas of concern are the Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT), which can exhibit hesitation or jerking, and some electrical components, notably the infotainment system's occasional unresponsiveness and the possibility of premature battery failure. While these issues are not universal, they are the most frequently reported technical problems. Owners should be mindful of CVT maintenance and potential software glitches in the infotainment. Overall, the Rogue Sport is a dependable choice, but awareness of these specific potential issues is advisable.
