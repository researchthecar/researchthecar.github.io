# 2018 Nissan Rogue Sport Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2018 Nissan Rogue Sport offers a compact footprint and practical cargo space, making it a popular choice for urban commuters and small families. However, prospective buyers should review its mechanical and safety records closely. While it provides decent everyday usability, owners have flagged notable issues concerning powertrain durability, forward collision controls, and cooling components.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L 4-Cylinder Engine

The 2018 Rogue Sport is powered by a 2.0-liter 4-cylinder engine producing around 141 horsepower, designed primarily for fuel efficiency. Buyers should inspect cooling system health carefully, as radiator fan failures have been reported to cause severe overheating.

**Radiator Fan and Overheating Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The radiator cooling fan can fail unexpectedly, causing the vehicle to overheat and smoke while idling, slowing down, or driving on the highway.
- **Solution:** Replace the failed radiator cooling fan assembly and replenish coolant.
- **Cost:** $1000-1500

**Head Gasket Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Engine loses coolant and blows smoke from under the hood, indicating potential head gasket compromise.
- **Solution:** Diagnose engine block and cylinder head integrity; replace head gasket or engine assembly as required.

## Transmission

### Xtronic Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)

Equipped with an Xtronic CVT paired with front-wheel drive or all-wheel drive, this transmission maximizes efficiency but requires thorough service history checks. Premature wear and failure symptoms are among the more serious out-of-pocket risks for used buyers.

**CVT Failure and Power Loss**
- **Symptoms:** The transmission stutters, hesitates, or completely loses the ability to accelerate while driving, often accompanied by warning lights.
- **Solution:** Inspect transmission fluid condition and internal components; rebuild or replace the CVT assembly.

### Automatic Emergency Braking

Braking system complaints center largely around sensitive or erratic automatic emergency braking activations that can startle drivers or cause unexpected slowdowns.

**Unexpected Automatic Braking**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle triggers dashboard warnings and aggressively engages the emergency brakes on clear roads without obstacles present.
- **Solution:** Have a dealer check for sensor recalibrations, software updates, or radar alignment issues.

## Safety

### Air Bag System

Safety equipment generally protects occupants well, though isolated complaints highlight unexpected sensor behaviors and warning lights.

**Inadvertent Air Bag Deployment**
- **Symptoms:** Air bags deploy independently while driving at highway speeds accompanied by loud noises, impairing visibility and vehicle control.
- **Solution:** Inspect impact sensors and the supplemental restraint system (SRS) control module for faults.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V654000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=Rogue+Sport&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 111, "Rear Visibility." The lack of an image in the back-up camera display increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 23V093000: Electrical System:Ignition:Anti-Theft:Immobilizer/Proximity:Key/Sender](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=Rogue+Sport&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** Driver contact with a key in the collapsed position could inadvertently shut off the vehicle while driving, increasing the risk of a crash. Additionally, if the vehicle shuts off while driving, the air bags may not deploy in a crash as intended, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will insert a spacer into the key slot of the jackknife key, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

When shopping for a used 2018 Nissan Rogue Sport, pay close attention to the service history of the CVT transmission and the cooling fan system. While it offers a comfortable ride and practical daily utility, checking for prior mechanical repairs or unresolved warning lights can save you from costly unexpected visits to the mechanic.
