# 2020 Nissan Rogue Sport Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2020 Nissan Rogue Sport, buyers should carefully evaluate powertrain durability, electronics, and advanced driver assistance systems. While this compact crossover offers nimble handling and efficient daily commuting, owner feedback highlights several recurring mechanical and safety-related complaints that require attention during a pre-purchase inspection.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L 4-Cylinder Engine

The 2020 Nissan Rogue Sport is powered by a 2.0-liter 4-cylinder engine producing 141 horsepower, paired with front-wheel or all-wheel drive. While generally adequate for daily driving, buyers should watch for reported cooling fan and oil leak issues.

**Cooling Fan and Overheating Concerns**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have reported loud shaking or rattling noises when shutting off the vehicle, faulty radiator cooling fans, and unexpected engine overheating without prior warning lights.
- **Solution:** Inspect the radiator cooling fan assembly and coolant levels. Replace faulty fan units or relays as necessary to prevent engine damage.

**Engine Oil Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Oil leaking directly into the engine compartment, potentially leading to major mechanical failure.
- **Solution:** Perform a thorough under-hood inspection for oil seepage or leaks, and replace failed gaskets or components as diagnosed.

## Transmission

### Xtronic Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)

The vehicle utilizes an Xtronic Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT) designed to optimize fuel economy. CVT reliability varies, making a professional transmission evaluation essential before purchase.

**Transmission Slippage**
- **Symptoms:** Unresponsive acceleration and mechanical slipping sensations while driving, sometimes occurring without warning lights on the dashboard.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified mechanic inspect CVT fluid condition and internal pressures. Severe slipping may require transmission replacement or rebuild.

## Safety

### Forward Collision Avoidance

Advanced safety features enhance protection but can experience calibration or sensor ghosting issues that trigger false braking events.

**Unexpected Emergency Braking**
- **Symptoms:** The automatic emergency braking system activates unexpectedly while driving in normal traffic conditions with clear roads ahead, creating potential rear-end collision hazards.
- **Solution:** Check for software updates related to the collision avoidance system and ensure radar and camera sensors are properly calibrated and free of obstructions.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V093000: Electrical System:Ignition:Anti-Theft:Immobilizer/Proximity:Key/Sender](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=Rogue+Sport&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** Driver contact with a key in the collapsed position could inadvertently shut off the vehicle while driving, increasing the risk of a crash. Additionally, if the vehicle shuts off while driving, the air bags may not deploy in a crash as intended, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will insert a spacer into the key slot of the jackknife key, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2020 Nissan Rogue Sport presents a mixed reliability picture for used car buyers. While it provides comfortable daily transportation, prospective owners should prioritize a comprehensive pre-purchase inspection focusing on the cooling system, CVT transmission performance, and the proper operation of driver-assist technologies.
