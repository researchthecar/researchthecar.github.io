# 2017 Nissan Rogue Sport

### Overview
The 2017 Nissan Rogue Sport is a compact crossover known for its practicality and fuel efficiency. As a relatively new model in its first generation, its long-term reliability is still being established. However, initial data suggests a generally solid performance, with most owners reporting satisfaction. Common areas of concern, while not widespread, tend to be minor and related to specific components rather than systemic failures. The vehicle's design prioritizes everyday usability, making it a sensible choice for urban commuting and light family duties. Maintenance is typically straightforward, contributing to its overall appeal.

## Electrical

### Infotainment System
**Screen Freezing or Unresponsiveness**
- **Symptoms:** The infotainment screen may occasionally freeze, become unresponsive to touch, or reboot unexpectedly.
- **Solution:** Software updates for the infotainment system. Battery terminal cleaning or replacement if voltage issues are suspected.
- **Cost:** $100 - $500


### Battery
**Premature Battery Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Difficulty starting the engine, dimming headlights, or electrical components malfunctioning.
- **Solution:** Battery testing and replacement.

### Summary Verdict
The 2017 Nissan Rogue Sport generally presents a reliable profile for its model year. The powertrain, consisting of the 2.0L engine and CVT, is mostly dependable, though some owners have noted occasional transmission hesitation. Electrical gremlins, particularly with the infotainment system and battery life, are the most frequently cited minor issues. However, these are typically resolved with software updates or component replacement and do not indicate widespread systemic flaws. Exterior and interior components have shown good durability. Overall, the Rogue Sport is a sensible choice for those seeking a practical and reasonably trouble-free compact SUV, provided routine maintenance is kept up.
