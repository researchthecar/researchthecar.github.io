# 2017 Nissan NP300 Frontier

### Overview
The 2017 Nissan NP300 Frontier, a robust mid-size pickup truck, is generally recognized for its durability and workhorse capabilities. Designed for demanding tasks, it often features a body-on-frame construction and capable powertrains. While specific widespread issues are not extensively documented for this model year, like any vehicle, certain components may experience wear or require attention over time. This analysis focuses on documented technical reliability concerns beyond routine maintenance, aiming to provide a clear picture for owners and potential buyers.

## Engine

### 2.3L YS23 DD4X Turbo Diesel
**Glow Plug Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Difficulty starting in cold weather, rough idling, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of faulty glow plugs.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600

**EGR Valve Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Reduced engine power, increased fuel consumption, black smoke from exhaust, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Cleaning or replacement of the EGR valve.
- **Cost:** $400 - $800

### Summary Verdict
The 2017 Nissan NP300 Frontier demonstrates a generally strong reliability record, particularly for its robust mechanical components. The diesel engine variants have seen some reports of issues related to the EGR valve and glow plugs, which are common for diesel powertrains and typically manageable with maintenance. The automatic transmission can occasionally exhibit harsh shifting, often resolvable with fluid services. Other systems like suspension, electrical, exterior, and interior components appear to be largely free from widespread, significant defects. Overall, the NP300 Frontier is a dependable choice for those seeking a capable and resilient pickup truck, provided regular maintenance is performed.
