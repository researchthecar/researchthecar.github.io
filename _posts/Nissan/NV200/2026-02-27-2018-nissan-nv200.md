# 2018 Nissan NV200 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2018 Nissan NV200 is a compact cargo van designed primarily for urban commercial use, offering agile handling and high fuel efficiency for delivery fleets and small businesses. While it generally serves as a practical workhorse, prospective buyers should review owner-reported complaints regarding certain electrical, body, and powertrain concerns to ensure long-term dependability.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L 4-Cylinder Engine

Equipped with a 2.0-liter naturally aspirated four-cylinder engine producing around 131 horsepower and 139 lb-ft of torque, paired with front-wheel drive. This powerplant is generally straightforward and adequate for light city deliveries, though owners should inspect the engine bay for any rare reports of abnormal noises or fuel system discrepancies.

## Transmission

### Xtronic Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)

Uses a Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT) routing power to the front wheels. While efficient for stop-and-go driving, buyers should thoroughly test-shift and check for any responsiveness delays or communication trouble codes during a pre-purchase inspection.

**ECM and Transmission Communication Faults**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may experience fault codes involving a loss of communication between the engine control module (ECM/PCM) and transmission control systems, potentially impacting vehicle drivability.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified technician diagnose electrical communication codes and inspect control module wiring harnesses or software calibration.

## Electrical

### Visibility

Visibility systems assist drivers maneuvering larger cargo vehicles in tight commercial spaces.

**Backup Camera Display Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The rearview camera image may blink on and off or fail entirely while the vehicle is in reverse, reducing rear visibility.
- **Solution:** Check the camera wiring harness, display connections, and power supply for intermittent faults, replacing the camera unit or display module if required.

## Exterior

### Sliding Side Doors

Exterior body components on commercial vans endure heavy daily use, occasionally leading to mechanical wear on sliding doors and weather stripping.

**Stuck Sliding Doors**
- **Symptoms:** The sliding side doors may continually get stuck and fail to open properly from either the inside or the outside.
- **Solution:** Inspect and lubricate the door tracks, rollers, and latch mechanisms; replace worn latch assemblies or alignment components if necessary.

### Windows and Glass

Glass and weatherstripping integrity is vital for keeping cargo and cabins dry during inclement weather.

**Side Window Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Water may leak through the side window seals during rain or washing, potentially leading to interior moisture issues.
- **Solution:** Inspect window seals and weather stripping for proper seating or damage, and replace faulty window glass or gaskets as needed.

## Safety

### Air Bags

Safety restraint systems rely on accurate electronic sensor inputs to ensure proper airbag deployment readiness.

**Passenger Seat Weight Sensor Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** The airbag warning light may flash intermittently due to a failure in the passenger seat weight sensor failing to transmit proper signals.
- **Solution:** Have the occupant classification system and seat sensor wiring diagnosed with a specialized scan tool, and replace faulty sensor components if indicated.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V654000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=NV200&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 111, "Rear Visibility." The lack of an image in the back-up camera display increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 25V676000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump:Control/Drive Module](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=NV200&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** The Fuel Tank Temperature (FTT) sensor harness may have been incorrectly routed, damaging wires and causing a short circuit in the fuel pump fuse. A blown fuel pump fuse can cause the engine to stall, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and reroute the FTT sensor harness or replace the fuel pump assembly as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2018 Nissan NV200 is a dedicated urban delivery van that can serve small business needs well, provided it has been properly maintained. Buyers should carefully evaluate sliding door operation, check for any electronic or backup camera glitches, and perform a thorough pre-purchase inspection of the powertrain and electrical systems.
