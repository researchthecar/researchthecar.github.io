**Introduction:** The 2018 Nissan NV200 is a compact cargo van known for its spacious interior, fuel efficiency, and maneuverability. It comes equipped with a 2.0L four-cylinder engine paired with either a 5-speed manual or 4-speed automatic transmission. Common praises include its value for the price, versatile cargo space, and city-friendly driving dynamics. However, it has also been criticized for its lack of power, low-quality interior materials, and limited safety features.

**Engine**
- **2.0L Four-Cylinder Engine**
    - **Intake Valve Carbon Buildup**
       - Description/Symptoms: Reduced engine power, increased fuel consumption
       - Solution: Cleaning or replacing intake valves
       - Estimated Cost to Fix: $600 - $1,200+
       - Recalls: None Found
    - **Timing Chain Failure**
       - Description/Symptoms: Engine rattling, misfiring, or complete engine failure
       - Solution: Replacing the timing chain and related components
       - Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+
       - Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**
- **5-Speed Manual Transmission**
    - **Clutch Failure**
       - Description/Symptoms: Slipping or difficulty engaging gears, burning smell
       - Solution: Replacing the clutch assembly
       - Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000
       - Recalls: None Found
- **4-Speed Automatic Transmission**
    - **Shift Solenoid Malfunction**
       - Description/Symptoms: Delayed or rough shifting, transmission slipping
       - Solution: Replacing the shift solenoids
       - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,200
       - Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**
- **Front Struts**
    - **Premature Wear**
       - Description/Symptoms: Noise, vibration, reduced handling, uneven tire wear
       - Solution: Replacing the front struts
       - Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600 per strut
       - Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**
- **Electrical Harness**
    - **Grounding Issues**
       - Description/Symptoms: Electrical system malfunctions, starting problems
       - Solution: Inspecting and repairing the electrical harness
       - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+
       - Recalls: None Found
- **Battery**
    - **Weak Battery**
       - Description/Symptoms: Difficult starting, reduced electrical system performance
       - Solution: Replacing the battery
       - Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $250
       - Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**
- **Rear Door Latch**
    - **Corrosion**
       - Description/Symptoms: Difficulty opening or closing the rear door
       - Solution: Replacing the rear door latch assembly
       - Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600
       - Recalls: None Found

**Interior**
- **Dashboard**
    - **Creaking or Rattles**
       - Description/Symptoms: Unwanted noises while driving
       - Solution: Tightening or replacing dashboard components
       - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
       - Recalls: None Found
- **Seat Adjustment**
    - **Malfunctioning Seat Adjuster**
       - Description/Symptoms: Difficulty adjusting seat position
       - Solution: Replacing the seat adjuster
       - Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800
       - Recalls: None Found

**Summary:** The 2018 Nissan NV200 is generally a reliable compact cargo van, but it is not without its issues. Engine and transmission problems can be significant, especially if not addressed promptly. The suspension, electrical, exterior, and interior components have also been known to fail prematurely. While some of these repairs can be relatively inexpensive, major issues can be costly to rectify.
