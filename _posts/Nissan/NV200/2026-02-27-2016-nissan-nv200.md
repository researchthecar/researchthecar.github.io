# 2016 Nissan NV200 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2016 Nissan NV200, buyers will find a compact cargo van designed primarily for urban delivery and commercial utility. While generally serving fleet and small business owners well with its practical packaging and maneuverability, prospective buyers should review owner-reported complaints regarding electrical glitches, safety systems, and interior comfort before making a purchase.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L 4-Cylinder (MR20DE)

The NV200 is powered by a 2.0-liter naturally aspirated 4-cylinder engine producing approximately 131 horsepower and 139 lb-ft of torque. It is built for efficiency and light-duty commercial tasks rather than rapid acceleration, though routine maintenance is essential to avoid sporadic driveability complaints.

**Engine Stalling and Power Loss**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have reported instances where the vehicle unexpectedly stalls while idling or driving, followed by a failure to accelerate past low speeds.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified mechanic inspect the electronic throttle body, fuel delivery system, and powertrain control module for faults.

## Transmission

### Xtronic Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)

Power is sent to the front wheels via a Continuously Variable Transmission designed to maximize fuel economy in stop-and-go city driving. Buyers should thoroughly test drive the vehicle to ensure smooth operation, as CVTs require diligent fluid maintenance.

## Interior

### Climate Control and Ventilation

The cabin is tailored for cargo utility with durable materials, though rear-area climate control performance can be a weak point in hotter climates.

**Insufficient Rear Air Conditioning Flow**
- **Symptoms:** Rear passengers may experience very low air flow from the rear vents, causing the cargo or rear seating area to become excessively warm.
- **Solution:** Check the rear blower motor, fuses, and cabin air filters for blockages or component failure.

## Safety

### Air Bag System

Safety equipment includes standard front and seat-mounted side airbags, alongside roof-mounted curtain airbags. Owners should verify that all dash warning lights cycle properly upon startup.

**Passenger Air Bag Warning Light**
- **Symptoms:** The passenger seat air bag warning light may illuminate or stay on continuously, indicating that the occupant detection system thinks the airbag is inactive even when occupied.
- **Solution:** Take the vehicle to a dealer or specialist to diagnose the occupant classification sensor and wiring harnesses.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V244000: Air Bags:Sensor:Occupant Classification](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=NV200&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** As a result, the passenger frontal air bag may be turned off and not deploy in the event of a crash. If the passenger frontal air bag does not deploy as intended in the event of a crash, the passenger is at an increased risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will reprogram the Air Bag Control Unit (ACU) and OCS Electronic Control Unit (ECU) in Altima, Maxima, Murano, Rogue, and Sentra vehicles, and replace the OCS ECU in LEAF, NV200, NV200 Taxi, Pathfinder, Infiniti Q50, JX35, and QX60. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V676000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump:Control/Drive Module](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=NV200&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** The Fuel Tank Temperature (FTT) sensor harness may have been incorrectly routed, damaging wires and causing a short circuit in the fuel pump fuse. A blown fuel pump fuse can cause the engine to stall, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and reroute the FTT sensor harness or replace the fuel pump assembly as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2016 Nissan NV200 remains a practical option for urban delivery and commercial use, provided you inspect it thoroughly before purchase. Pay close attention to powertrain driveability, ensure the safety warning lights operate normally, and test the climate control system to confirm rear airflow meets your needs.
