## 2016 Nissan NV200

The 2016 Nissan NV200 is a compact cargo van that offers a spacious cargo area and versatile seating configurations. It comes equipped with a 2.0-liter four-cylinder engine that produces 131 horsepower and 159 lb-ft of torque, paired with a five-speed manual transmission or an optional four-speed automatic transmission.

## Engine
- **2.0L Four-Cylinder**
 **Intake Valve Deposits**
  - Description/Symptoms: Reduced engine power, poor fuel economy, rough idling, and engine knocking
  - Solution: Cleaning or replacing the intake valves
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $400-$800+
  - Recalls: None Found

## Transmission
- **5-Speed Manual**
 **Shift Linkage Issues**
  - Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears, grinding noises, and popping out of gear
  - Solution: Replacing the shift linkage
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200-$400+
  - Recalls: None Found
- **4-Speed Automatic**
 **Transmission Slipping**
  - Description/Symptoms: Inability to engage gears, delayed gear engagement, and loss of power
  - Solution: Rebuilding or replacing the transmission
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000-$3,000+
  - Recalls: None Found

## Suspension
- **No Issues Found**

## Electrical
- **Battery Drain**
  - Description/Symptoms: Battery quickly drains, even when the vehicle is parked
  - Solution: Replacing the faulty battery or electrical components
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $150-$500+
  - Recalls: None Found

## Exterior
- **No Issues Found**

## Interior
- **Faulty Climate Control System**
  - Description/Symptoms: Blower motor failure, temperature control issues, and defroster problems
  - Solution: Replacing the climate control module or specific components
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $300-$800+
  - Recalls: None Found

## Summary
The 2016 Nissan NV200 generally receives positive feedback for its spacious cargo area, fuel efficiency, and maneuverability. However, it has some reported issues, such as engine intake valve deposits, transmission shift linkage problems, and transmission slipping. Electrical issues with battery drain and interior problems with the climate control system have also been reported. The severity and cost of these issues vary depending on the specific problem and the required repairs.
