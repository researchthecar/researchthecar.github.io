# 2017 Nissan NV200

### Overview
The 2017 Nissan NV200 is a compact commercial van designed for urban delivery and cargo transport. It is known for its fuel efficiency and maneuverability, making it a practical choice for businesses. Reliability is generally considered good, with owners reporting fewer significant mechanical issues compared to some competitors. The NV200 typically comes with a straightforward powertrain focused on durability and low running costs. While it's a workhorse vehicle, proactive maintenance is still key to ensuring its longevity and optimal performance in demanding commercial applications. This analysis focuses on common reported issues beyond standard wear and tear.

## Electrical

### Standard Electrical System
**Intermittent Power Loss to Accessories**
- **Symptoms:** Radio, power windows, or other accessories may intermittently stop working.
- **Solution:** Check for loose wiring connections, faulty fuses, or a failing body control module (BCM).
- **Cost:** $150 - $500


## Exterior

### Body Panels and Doors
**Door Latch/Lock Actuator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Doors may not lock or unlock properly, or may not open from the inside or outside.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the faulty door latch or lock actuator.
- **Cost:** $200 - $450


## Interior

### Cabin Components
**HVAC Blower Motor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** No air or reduced airflow from the vents, regardless of fan speed setting.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the HVAC blower motor and potentially the blower motor resistor.
- **Cost:** $250 - $600

### Summary Verdict
The 2017 Nissan NV200 demonstrates solid reliability, particularly in its powertrain. The 2.0L engine and CVT transmission are generally robust and rarely present significant issues beyond expected maintenance. The most commonly reported non-wear-and-tear problems tend to be in the electrical and exterior components, such as intermittent accessory power or door latch malfunctions. Interior issues like HVAC blower motor failure are also noted but are not considered widespread. Overall, the NV200 is a dependable choice for commercial use, with most potential problems being relatively minor and manageable.
