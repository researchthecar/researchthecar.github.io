# 2017 Nissan NV200 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used compact cargo van, understanding common owner complaints can help you spot potential trouble areas before making a purchase. While these vehicles are designed primarily for commercial utility, used car buyers should inspect specific mechanical and electrical systems carefully. Reviewing owner reports provides useful insight into what might need attention during a pre-purchase inspection.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Naturally Aspirated I4

This 2.0-liter four-cylinder engine produces around 131 horsepower and 139 lb-ft of torque, providing adequate power for urban deliveries. It is generally regarded as a durable workhorse, though buyers should verify regular maintenance history.

## Transmission

### Xtronic Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)

Paired with front-wheel drive, this continuously variable transmission prioritizes fuel efficiency for city driving. Buyers should test drive thoroughly to ensure smooth operation and check for any signs of hesitation or shuddering.

## Electrical

### Ignition Switch

Electrical and starting accessories generally perform reliably, though mechanical wear in the ignition switch can occasionally occur.

**Ignition Switch Binding**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may experience difficulty turning the key in the ignition cylinder, requiring them to wiggle or juggle the key to reach the start position.
- **Solution:** Inspect the ignition cylinder and switch assembly, and replace worn internal components or the entire switch assembly if binding persists.

## Safety

### Air Bag System

Owner reports highlight occasional malfunctions within the occupant classification and airbag sensor network.

**Passenger Air Bag Sensor and Deployment Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have reported unexpected airbag warning lights, error messages indicating the passenger airbag is disabled, and in rare instances, improper deployment when shutting the door.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified technician inspect the occupant detection sensors, wiring harnesses, and control modules, replacing faulty components as necessary.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V551000: Electrical System:Ignition](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=NV200&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** In these models, over time, the weight on the key ring and road conditions or some other jarring event may cause the ignition switch to move out of the run position to the accessory position, turning off the engine. If the vehicle stalls, it can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Nissan will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the production information on the ignition switch, and replace it as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V676000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump:Control/Drive Module](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=NV200&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** The Fuel Tank Temperature (FTT) sensor harness may have been incorrectly routed, damaging wires and causing a short circuit in the fuel pump fuse. A blown fuel pump fuse can cause the engine to stall, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and reroute the FTT sensor harness or replace the fuel pump assembly as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

Overall, evaluating a used model involves checking key convenience and safety systems rather than major powertrain failures. Buyers should perform a thorough test drive, verify that all safety electronics and airbag indicators function correctly, and ensure the vehicle receives a comprehensive pre-purchase inspection.
