# 2020 Nissan NV200 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

Navigating the used compact cargo van market can be tricky, but models like the Nissan NV200 offer practical utility for urban businesses and delivery drivers. When shopping for a pre-owned 2020 model, evaluating how the powertrain and supporting systems have held up over time is essential for keeping operating costs low and avoiding unexpected downtime.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L 4-Cylinder

The 2.0-liter naturally aspirated four-cylinder engine produces around 131 horsepower and 139 lb-ft of torque, paired with front-wheel drive. It is designed primarily for light-duty city delivery work, prioritizing basic fuel efficiency over brisk acceleration.

## Transmission

### Xtronic Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)

Power is routed to the front wheels via a continuously variable transmission tailored for urban stop-and-go driving. Prospective buyers should inspect the CVT operation thoroughly during a test drive to ensure smooth engagement without hesitation or slipping.

---

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V676000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump:Control/Drive Module](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=NV200&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** The Fuel Tank Temperature (FTT) sensor harness may have been incorrectly routed, damaging wires and causing a short circuit in the fuel pump fuse. A blown fuel pump fuse can cause the engine to stall, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and reroute the FTT sensor harness or replace the fuel pump assembly as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2020 Nissan NV200 serves as a functional, purpose-built cargo van for everyday commercial needs. Prospective buyers should perform a thorough mechanical inspection of the powertrain and electronics before purchase to ensure long-term reliability.
