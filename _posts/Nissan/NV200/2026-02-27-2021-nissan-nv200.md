# 2021 Nissan NV200 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2021 Nissan NV200 is a compact cargo van built for urban delivery and commercial utility, offering nimble handling and efficient performance. When shopping for a used model, buyers should carefully review service records and inspect powertrain components, as owner reports point to potential areas of mechanical wear.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L 4-Cylinder Engine

Equipped with a 2.0-liter 4-cylinder engine producing around 131 horsepower and 139 lb-ft of torque. This powerplant is designed for daily commercial duties, balancing modest fuel consumption with reliable everyday operation.

## Transmission

### Xtronic Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)

Features a continuously variable automatic transmission paired with front-wheel drive. Buyers should evaluate shifting smoothness and responsiveness during a test drive to ensure long-term durability.

---

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V676000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump:Control/Drive Module](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=NV200&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The Fuel Tank Temperature (FTT) sensor harness may have been incorrectly routed, damaging wires and causing a short circuit in the fuel pump fuse. A blown fuel pump fuse can cause the engine to stall, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and reroute the FTT sensor harness or replace the fuel pump assembly as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2021 Nissan NV200 remains a practical option for small business owners and delivery fleets needing compact cargo space. Prospective buyers should perform a thorough pre-purchase inspection focusing on the engine and transmission to ensure a trouble-free commercial ownership experience.
