# 2019 Nissan NV200 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2019 Nissan NV200, buyers will find a compact cargo van designed primarily for urban delivery and light commercial duties. Equipped with a standard four-cylinder engine and a continuously variable transmission, it offers practical fuel efficiency and nimble handling for city streets. While owner reports to federal safety regulators generally point to typical mechanical wear, prospective buyers should review available maintenance records and inspect the powertrain closely during a test drive.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L 4-Cylinder Engine

The 2019 Nissan NV200 is powered by a 2.0-liter four-cylinder engine producing approximately 131 horsepower and 139 lb-ft of torque. This engine is tailored for basic urban utility rather than heavy towing or high-speed hauling.

**Catalytic Converter Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may hear a sudden ticking noise from the engine and experience a severe loss of power, limiting vehicle speed.
- **Solution:** Inspect and replace damaged catalytic converters and check the exhaust system for internal debris.

## Transmission

### Xtronic Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)

Power is routed to the front wheels through an Xtronic continuously variable transmission. Buyers should pay close attention to transmission responsiveness and smoothness during a pre-purchase test drive.

**Transmission Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may experience sudden drivability failures or complete loss of forward propulsion while traveling at highway speeds.
- **Solution:** Have the transmission thoroughly inspected by a qualified specialist; rebuild or replacement may be required in cases of severe internal component failure.

## Steering

### Steering System

Safety components in commercial vans see heavy use, making thorough checks of steering and stability systems essential before purchasing.

**Steering Wheel Lockup**
- **Symptoms:** Under certain slippery driving conditions, the steering wheel may abruptly lock up, making the vehicle unresponsive to steering inputs.
- **Solution:** Inspect the steering column and intermediate shaft for mechanical binding or electronic assist failures.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V654000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=NV200&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 111, "Rear Visibility." The lack of an image in the back-up camera display increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 25V676000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump:Control/Drive Module](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=NV200&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** The Fuel Tank Temperature (FTT) sensor harness may have been incorrectly routed, damaging wires and causing a short circuit in the fuel pump fuse. A blown fuel pump fuse can cause the engine to stall, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and reroute the FTT sensor harness or replace the fuel pump assembly as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2019 Nissan NV200 serves as a functional, space-efficient choice for small business owners and delivery drivers. While most examples perform routine work reliably, isolated reports involving major powertrain components and steering behavior emphasize the importance of a thorough pre-purchase inspection. Prospective buyers should verify service history, test the transmission thoroughly, and check for completed safety recalls prior to purchase.
