The 2024 Nissan Z is a sports car that pays homage to its predecessors while offering modern performance and technology. It comes standard with a 3.0-liter twin-turbo V6 engine producing 400 horsepower and 350 lb-ft of torque. Transmission options include a 6-speed manual and a 9-speed automatic. Owners often praise the Z's retro styling, powerful engine, and engaging driving dynamics. However, some criticisms include limited interior space and concerns about long-term reliability.

**Engine**

**3.0L Twin-Turbo V6**

*   **Turbocharger Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Whining noise from the engine, reduced power, and potential oil leaks from the turbocharger seals.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the faulty turbocharger.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Fuel Injector Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rough idling, misfires, and potential engine stalling. Could trigger a check engine light.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis of the faulty injector(s) and replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+ per injector
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**9-Speed Automatic**

*   **Rough Shifting/Hesitation**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Jerky or harsh shifts between gears, particularly at lower speeds. Hesitation when accelerating from a stop.
    *   Solution: Software reflash of the transmission control module (TCM). If the problem persists, valve body replacement or complete transmission replacement may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 (reflash) to $3,000 - $7,000+ (replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**6-Speed Manual**

*No Issues Found*

**Suspension**

*   **Rear Differential Bushing Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Clunking noise from the rear of the vehicle, especially when accelerating or decelerating. Imprecise handling and increased vibration.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the faulty rear differential bushings.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Freezing/Crashing**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The infotainment screen freezes, becomes unresponsive, or crashes unexpectedly.
    *   Solution: Software update of the infotainment system. If the problem persists, replacement of the head unit may be required.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 (software update) to $1,000+ (head unit replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Sensor Malfunctions (e.g., Blind Spot Monitoring, Lane Departure Warning)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Warning lights illuminating on the dashboard, indicating a failure of the sensor system. Inaccurate or erratic readings from the affected system.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis of the faulty sensor and replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+ per sensor.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Poor Panel Alignment**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Gaps between body panels are inconsistent or unusually large, especially around the doors, hood, and trunk.
    *   Solution: Adjustment of the body panels to improve alignment. In severe cases, bodywork may be required.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+ depending on severity.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Rattling Noises**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rattling or squeaking noises emanating from the dashboard, door panels, or rear hatch area.
    *   Solution: Identify the source of the rattle and apply sound deadening material or tighten loose components.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+ (depending on complexity)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2024 Nissan Z, while lauded for its performance and design, presents some common issues. Engine issues, particularly with the turbochargers and fuel injectors, can be costly to repair. The 9-speed automatic transmission may exhibit rough shifting, potentially requiring expensive repairs. Electrical glitches and interior rattles can also detract from the ownership experience. Overall, while not all vehicles will experience these issues, prospective buyers should be aware of these potential problems.
