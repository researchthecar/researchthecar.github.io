# 2023 Nissan Z Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2023 Nissan Z, buyers will find a compelling modern sports car that revives a legendary nameplate with twin-turbocharged performance. Because this model year is still relatively fresh on the pre-owned market, long-term reliability trends are still emerging from early owner experiences. Reviewing available owner reports highlights a couple of isolated mechanical and body latch concerns worth keeping in mind during a pre-purchase inspection.

---

## Engine

### 3.0L Twin-Turbo V6 (400 hp)

This 3.0-liter twin-turbocharged V6 generates approximately 400 horsepower and 350 lb-ft of torque, providing strong acceleration for the Z. It serves as the primary powerplant for the lineup, combining traditional sports car performance with modern turbo engineering.

**Fuel System Leak**
- **Symptoms:** Owners may notice a strong fuel smell inside the garage or around the vehicle, indicating a potential leak within the engine bay.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified mechanic inspect the engine compartment fuel lines and connections, replacing any faulty components as necessary.

## Transmission

### 9-Speed Automatic Transmission

The available 9-speed automatic transmission offers quick shifts and smooth power delivery during everyday driving. It pairs well with the twin-turbo V6, though prospective buyers should test drive it thoroughly to ensure proper operation.

### 6-Speed Manual Transmission

The traditional 6-speed manual transmission caters to enthusiast drivers seeking a more engaging, hands-on shifting experience. It features rev-matching capabilities on select trims and represents a solid choice for purists.

## Exterior

### Hood Latch

Exterior body components and latches generally hold up well, though proper adjustment of the hood mechanism is critical to prevent unexpected openings.

**Unexpected Hood Release**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle's hood can suddenly pop open while driving, potentially obstructing the driver's forward visibility.
- **Solution:** Inspect the primary and secondary hood latch mechanisms for proper engagement, alignment, or mechanical failure, and adjust or replace parts if needed.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V725000: Structure:Body:Bumpers](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=Z&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** A pop-up engine hood that does not activate and raise the rear end of the hood in a frontal pedestrian crash, can increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will tighten the fasteners, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V798000: Structure:Body:Bumpers](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=Z&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** A pop-up engine hood that does not activate in a pedestrian crash can increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the front bumper harness, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2023 Nissan Z is an exciting performance machine, but prospective buyers should conduct a thorough pre-purchase inspection given its early-production status. While major mechanical failures are rare, checking for fuel system integrity and verifying that exterior latches operate securely will help ensure a worry-free ownership experience.
