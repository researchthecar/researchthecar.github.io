# 2024 Nissan Z Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2024 Nissan Z is a sports car that revives the iconic nameplate with a twin-turbo V6 and modern styling. As a relatively new model on the market, long-term reliability data is still developing, though owner feedback provides early insights into build quality and component performance.

---

## Engine

### 3.0L Twin-Turbo V6 (400 hp)

The standard twin-turbocharged 3.0-liter V6 produces approximately 400 horsepower and 350 lb-ft of torque, delivering strong acceleration. This engine serves as the sole powerplant for the lineup and generally offers solid mechanical performance in early owner reports.

## Transmission

### 9-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 9-speed automatic transmission pairs with the twin-turbo V6 to offer smooth power delivery during daily driving and aggressive shifting under acceleration.

### 6-Speed Manual Transmission

The traditional 6-speed manual transmission caters to enthusiast drivers seeking a more connected shifting experience with the twin-turbo V6 engine.

## Exterior

### Rear Hatchback Window

Early reports indicate isolated incidents regarding exterior glass integrity.

**Spontaneous Window Shattering**
- **Symptoms:** The rear hatchback window can shatter unexpectedly while the vehicle is stopped or in operation, scattering glass shards.
- **Solution:** Replace the shattered rear hatchback glass with a new factory window unit.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V725000: Structure:Body:Bumpers](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=Z&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** A pop-up engine hood that does not activate and raise the rear end of the hood in a frontal pedestrian crash, can increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will tighten the fasteners, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V798000: Structure:Body:Bumpers](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=Z&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** A pop-up engine hood that does not activate in a pedestrian crash can increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the front bumper harness, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2024 Nissan Z is an exciting modern sports car with potent engine options, though buyers should inspect overall build quality and glass integrity during a pre-purchase evaluation.
