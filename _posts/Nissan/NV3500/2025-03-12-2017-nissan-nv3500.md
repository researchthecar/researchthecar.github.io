The 2017 Nissan NV3500 is a full-size cargo van known for its robust build and practicality. It's available with two engine options: a 4.0L V6 producing 261 horsepower and 281 lb-ft of torque and a 5.6L V8 generating 375 horsepower and 387 lb-ft of torque. Both engines are paired with a 5-speed automatic transmission. Owners appreciate the NV3500's spacious cargo area and towing capacity. However, some common complaints include fuel economy and the van's overall driving experience.

**Engine**

**5.6L V8**

* **Excessive Oil Consumption**
    * Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported higher than normal oil consumption, requiring frequent top-offs between oil changes. Symptoms include the low oil pressure warning light illuminating, visible exhaust smoke, and needing to add oil every few thousand miles.
    * Solution: The solution varies depending on the cause. Could require valve stem seal replacement, piston ring replacement, or engine replacement if the issue is severe.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $5,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Failed Catalytic Converters**
    * Description/Symptoms: The catalytic converters can fail prematurely. Symptoms include the check engine light illuminating, a sulfur or rotten egg smell from the exhaust, decreased engine performance, and potential failure to pass emissions testing.
    * Solution: Replacement of the failed catalytic converter(s).
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $2,000+ per converter
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Premature Ball Joint Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Owners have reported ball joint failure at relatively low mileages. Symptoms include clunking noises from the front suspension, uneven tire wear, and loose or wandering steering.
    * Solution: Replacement of the worn ball joints, along with wheel alignment.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+ per side
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: Some owners have experienced issues with the battery draining overnight or after short periods of inactivity. This can lead to difficulty starting the vehicle.
    * Solution: Thoroughly inspect the electrical system for parasitic draws. Check for aftermarket accessories that may be drawing power when the vehicle is off. Repair or replace faulty components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Sliding Door Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: Problems with the sliding door mechanism, including difficulty opening or closing, sticking, or unusual noises.
    * Solution: Adjusting, lubricating, or replacing the sliding door rollers, hinges, or cables.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Dashboard Cracking**
    * Description/Symptoms: Cracking or warping of the dashboard, particularly in areas exposed to direct sunlight.
    * Solution: Dashboard replacement or using a dashboard cover to protect it from further damage.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2017 Nissan NV3500 is a reliable work van, but owners should be aware of potential issues such as excessive oil consumption in the V8 engine, premature ball joint failure, and electrical issues. The cost of these repairs can range from a few hundred to several thousand dollars, depending on the severity and location of the problem. Being proactive with maintenance and addressing potential problems early can help mitigate these issues.
