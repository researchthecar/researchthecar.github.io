**Introduction:**

The 2020 Nissan NV3500 is a full-size passenger van offered with a single engine and transmission option. It is powered by a 5.6-liter V8 engine producing 375 horsepower and 387 lb-ft of torque, paired with a 7-speed automatic transmission. The NV3500 is praised for its spacious interior, versatility, and towing capacity. However, it is criticized for its outdated design, poor fuel economy, and lack of advanced safety features.

**Engine**

**5.6L V8**

**Oil consumption:**
Description/Symptoms: Excessive oil consumption, causing low oil levels and potential engine damage.
Solution: Piston ring replacement or engine rebuild.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $5,000+
Recalls: NTSB campaign 22V-422

**Transmission**

**7-Speed Automatic**

**Transmission shudder:**
Description/Symptoms: Vibration or shuddering during acceleration or shifting gears.
Solution: Transmission fluid replacement or transmission rebuild.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $4,000+
Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

**Dashboard rattling:**
Description/Symptoms: Dashboard components rattle or vibrate, especially on rough roads.
Solution: Dashboard reinforcement or replacement.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
Recalls: None Found

**Seatbelt buckle issues:**
Description/Symptoms: Seatbelt buckles may become difficult to latch or may not hold securely.
Solution: Seatbelt buckle replacement.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $250
Recalls: NTSB campaign 21V-790

**Summary:**

The 2020 Nissan NV3500 has several known issues, including excessive oil consumption, transmission shudder, and dashboard rattling. However, the severity and cost of these issues vary, and the NV3500 remains a reliable and practical choice for those requiring a spacious and versatile van.
