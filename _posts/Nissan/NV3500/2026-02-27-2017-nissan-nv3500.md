# 2017 Nissan NV3500 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used full-size commercial van like the Nissan NV3500, understanding its mechanical baseline and known owner-reported quirks is vital for a smooth ownership experience. While these large-capacity passenger and cargo workhorses are built for heavy-duty commercial deployment, prospective buyers should review available powertrain options and typical owner feedback before purchasing.

---

## Engine

### 4.0L V6

The 4.0-liter V6 engine produces around 261 horsepower and 281 lb-ft of torque, providing baseline utility for lighter commercial transport. It is generally a durable workhorse, though buyers should verify regular maintenance history.

### 5.6L V8

The 5.6-liter V8 engine pushes approximately 375 horsepower and 387 lb-ft of torque, offering robust towing and hauling capability for heavy commercial payloads. It serves as the primary high-capacity power plant for the lineup.

**Engine Revving and Loss of Power**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have reported unexpected loud engine revving upon startup or while driving, accompanied by intermittent hesitation and a temporary loss of acceleration power.
- **Solution:** Diagnostic scanning for throttle body or sensor faults is recommended, as these owner reports reflect unverified driver complaints rather than a universal recall.

**Engine Noise and Metal Friction Sounds**
- **Symptoms:** Some drivers noted a metallic rubbing noise from the engine bay, occasionally accompanied by an illuminated check engine light following fuel fill-ups or temperature shifts.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified mechanic inspect the belt drive accessories, pulleys, and timing components for premature wear or alignment issues.

## Transmission

### 7-Speed Automatic

The 7-speed automatic transmission is paired with larger engine configurations to optimize highway cruising and load management. Routine fluid changes are essential to maintain smooth shifting over high mileage.

## Interior

### Rear Air Conditioning

Cabin comfort systems in large passenger configurations require thorough pre-purchase checks, particularly regarding climate control distribution across multiple rows.

**Insufficient Rear Airflow**
- **Symptoms:** Occupants in the rear rows may experience inadequate climate control airflow, causing cabin temperatures to remain uncomfortably warm during hot weather.
- **Solution:** Inspect the rear blower motor, ductwork integrity, and cabin air filters to ensure proper air distribution throughout the passenger compartment.

---

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V551000: Electrical System:Ignition](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=NV3500&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** In these models, over time, the weight on the key ring and road conditions or some other jarring event may cause the ignition switch to move out of the run position to the accessory position, turning off the engine. If the vehicle stalls, it can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Nissan will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the production information on the ignition switch, and replace it as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The Nissan NV3500 stands as a heavy-duty contender for buyers needing serious passenger or cargo capacity. While mechanical durability is generally aligned with commercial expectations, prospective owners should thoroughly test drive the vehicle to check for any engine hesitation or climate control issues in the rear seating rows.
