The 2021 Nissan NV3500 is a full-size van primarily used for commercial purposes. It offers significant cargo space and towing capacity. Engine options include a 4.0L V6 (261 hp, 281 lb-ft of torque) and a 5.6L V8 (375 hp, 387 lb-ft of torque), both paired with a 7-speed automatic transmission. Some owners appreciate its utilitarian design, payload capacity, and relatively lower price point compared to competitors. Common complaints include subpar fuel economy, a dated interior, and handling characteristics typical of a large van.

**Electrical**
*   **Dead Battery**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Battery drains quickly, especially when the vehicle is not driven regularly. The van may be difficult to start or require jump-starting. This can be caused by a parasitic draw from the electrical system.
    *   Solution: Diagnose and eliminate any parasitic draws. Replace the battery with a new, high-quality battery.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**
*   **Sliding Door Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The sliding door can be difficult to open or close, requiring excessive force. The door may bind or make unusual noises.
    *   Solution: Inspect and lubricate the sliding door tracks and rollers. Adjust or replace the rollers if worn or damaged.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $300+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2021 Nissan NV3500 appears to have relatively few widespread, significant issues. Overall, the severity of reported issues is relatively low, and the estimated repair costs are moderate.
