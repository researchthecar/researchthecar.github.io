The 2019 Nissan NV3500 is a full-size cargo van known for its robust build, practicality, and ample interior space. Available engine options include a 4.0L V6 engine (261 hp, 281 lb-ft of torque) and a 5.6L V8 engine (375 hp, 387 lb-ft of torque), both paired with a 5-speed automatic transmission. Owners often appreciate the van's reliability, payload capacity, and customizable interior. Common dislikes include its fuel economy and basic interior features.

**Engine**

**4.0L V6**

*   **Timing Chain Rattle**
    *   Description/Symptoms: A rattling noise coming from the front of the engine, especially upon startup. This indicates wear or stretching of the timing chain or failure of the timing chain tensioners.
    *   Solution: Replacing the timing chain, tensioners, and guides.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1500 - $2500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**5.6L V8**

*   **Oil Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Oil leaking from various locations on the engine, often from the valve cover gaskets or the front timing cover. Visible oil puddles under the vehicle or oil consumption may also be observed.
    *   Solution: Replacing the leaking gaskets or seals.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1000+ (depending on the leak location)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**Both Engines (5-Speed Automatic)**

*   **Rough Shifting/Slipping**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Harsh or delayed shifts between gears, slipping during acceleration, or difficulty engaging certain gears.
    *   Solution: This can be caused by low transmission fluid, a faulty valve body, or worn clutch packs. A full transmission rebuild or replacement may be required.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 (fluid change) - $4000+ (rebuild/replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Premature Ball Joint Wear**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Clicking or squeaking noises coming from the front suspension, loose steering, or uneven tire wear.
    *   Solution: Replacing the worn ball joints.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+ per side.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Battery Drain**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The battery drains quickly, especially when the vehicle is not in use for extended periods. This can lead to difficulty starting the van.
    *   Solution: Diagnosing the parasitic draw by systematically testing fuses and circuits. Possible culprits include faulty relays, aftermarket accessories, or a failing alternator.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+ (depending on the cause)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Sliding Door Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Difficulty opening or closing the sliding side door, sticking, or requiring excessive force. The rollers or tracks may be worn or misaligned.
    *   Solution: Adjusting or replacing the rollers, tracks, or door latch mechanism.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **HVAC Blower Motor Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The HVAC blower motor stops working or only works intermittently, resulting in no airflow from the vents.
    *   Solution: Replacing the blower motor or blower motor resistor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $400+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2019 Nissan NV3500 is a capable work van, but like any vehicle, it has its share of potential issues. Engine oil leaks, timing chain issues on the V6, transmission problems, and suspension wear are the most significant concerns. Electrical issues such as battery drains and interior problems like HVAC failures are also possible. Repair costs can range from a few hundred dollars for minor fixes to several thousand for major engine or transmission work. Prospective buyers should thoroughly inspect the vehicle and address any identified issues to ensure long-term reliability.
