The 2007 Nissan Platina, also known in some markets as the Renault Symbol, was a subcompact sedan primarily sold in Mexico and South America. It was based on the Renault Clio platform. The Platina was equipped with a 1.6-liter 16-valve inline-4 engine. This engine produced around 110 horsepower and 110 lb-ft of torque. The vehicle was favored for its fuel efficiency and affordability. However, some owners reported concerns regarding its reliability and build quality.

**Engine**

**1.6L 16-Valve Inline-4**

*   **Excessive Oil Consumption**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported the engine consuming more oil than usual, requiring frequent top-ups. Blue smoke may be visible from the exhaust.
    *   Solution: The solution can range from replacing valve stem seals to piston rings, depending on the severity. In some cases, an engine rebuild or replacement might be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $3000+

*   **Rough Idling/Misfires**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The engine may idle roughly or experience misfires, leading to reduced performance and potential stalling.
    *   Solution: Possible solutions include replacing spark plugs, ignition coils, or cleaning/replacing fuel injectors.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+

**Transmission**

**4-Speed Automatic Transmission**

*   **Slipping/Delayed Shifting**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The transmission may exhibit slipping between gears, delayed shifts, or harsh shifting.
    *   Solution: Could be caused by low transmission fluid, a faulty solenoid, or worn clutch packs. A transmission flush, solenoid replacement, or complete rebuild/replacement may be required.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $2500+

**Suspension**

*   **Worn Suspension Components**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Clunking or rattling noises from the suspension, especially over bumps. Vehicle may exhibit poor handling and excessive body roll.
    *   Solution: Replacing worn shocks, struts, bushings, or ball joints.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1000+

**Electrical**

*   **Faulty Window Regulators**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Power windows may fail to operate or operate intermittently.
    *   Solution: Replacing the window regulator assembly.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $400+

*   **Instrument Cluster Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Gauges may malfunction or display incorrect readings. The entire cluster may fail.
    *   Solution: Repair or replacement of the instrument cluster.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600+

**Exterior**

*   **Rusting**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rust may appear on the body panels, especially in areas prone to moisture and salt exposure.
    *   Solution: Rust repair, including sanding, patching, and repainting.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $1000+ (depending on severity)

**Interior**

*   **Loose Trim Pieces**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Interior trim pieces, such as door panels or dashboard components, may become loose or detached.
    *   Solution: Reattaching or replacing the loose trim pieces.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $20 - $200+

**Summary**
The 2007 Nissan Platina, while generally affordable and economical, can suffer from a range of issues as it ages. Engine problems like excessive oil consumption and rough idling can be moderate to severe, with repair costs ranging widely. Transmission issues, such as slipping, can also be costly to fix. Other common issues include worn suspension components and electrical problems that can impact the overall reliability and enjoyment of the vehicle. Potential buyers should thoroughly inspect these areas and factor in potential repair costs when considering purchasing this model.
