## Introduction
The 2007 Nissan Platina is a compact sedan that was available with two engine options: a 1.6-liter inline-4 and a 1.8-liter inline-4. Both engines were paired with a 4-speed automatic transmission. The Platina was praised for its affordable price, fuel efficiency, and spacious interior, but it was criticized for its lack of power and dated styling.

**Engine**
* 1.6-liter inline-4 (107 hp, 112 lb-ft)
* 1.8-liter inline-4 (122 hp, 127 lb-ft)

**Transmission**
* 4-speed automatic

## Issues

**Engine**

**1.6-liter inline-4**
* **Oil Consumption:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Excessive oil consumption, leading to low oil levels and potential engine damage.
    * Solution: Replace piston rings and/or oil seals.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,500+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Timing Chain Tensioner Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Worn or damaged timing chain tensioner, leading to timing chain skipping and potential engine damage.
    * Solution: Replace timing chain tensioner and related components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**1.8-liter inline-4**
* **Intake Manifold Gasket Leak:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Leaking intake manifold gasket, leading to vacuum leaks and reduced engine performance.
    * Solution: Replace intake manifold gasket.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Thermostat Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Malfunctioning thermostat, leading to overheating or undercooling of the engine.
    * Solution: Replace thermostat.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

* **4-speed Automatic**
* **Clunking or Jerking When Shifting:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Transmission clunks or jerks during shifts, indicating worn or damaged transmission components.
    * Solution: Overhaul or replace transmission.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Transmission Fluid Leak:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Leaking transmission fluid, leading to low fluid levels and potential transmission damage.
    * Solution: Replace leaking seals or gaskets.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Rear Suspension Sagging:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Sagging rear suspension, indicating worn or damaged rear springs or shocks.
    * Solution: Replace rear springs or shocks.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Power Window Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Malfunctioning power window motors or switches, leading to inoperable windows.
    * Solution: Replace power window motor(s) or switch(es).
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300 per window
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Alternator Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Malfunctioning alternator, leading to electrical system problems and eventually a no-start condition.
    * Solution: Replace alternator.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Headlight Condensation:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Moisture buildup inside headlight assemblies, impairing visibility.
    * Solution: Replace headlight assemblies or seal leaks.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500 per headlight
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Climate Control Malfunction:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Malfunctioning heater or air conditioner, leading to inadequate temperature control.
    * Solution: Repair or replace climate control components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

## Summary
Overall, the 2007 Nissan Platina has several potential issues that can be costly to repair. The most severe and expensive issues include engine oil consumption, timing chain tensioner failure, and transmission failure. The cost of repairs can vary significantly depending on the severity of the problem and the location of the repair. It's important to research potential issues before purchasing a Platina or to have it inspected by a qualified mechanic before making a decision.
