**Introduction:**

The 2014 Nissan Xterra is a compact SUV powered by a 4.0-liter V6 engine paired with a 5-speed automatic transmission. It delivers 261 horsepower and 281 lb-ft of torque. The Xterra is known for its rugged capabilities and off-road performance, but it has received some criticism for its outdated interior and limited safety features.

**Transmission**

**5-speed Automatic Transmission**

* **Transmission Slippage**
    * Description/Symptoms: Gears slipping or jerking during acceleration or shifting
    * Solution: Repair or replace transmission
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **HVAC Fan Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Blower motor stops working, causing lack of air conditioning or heating
    * Solution: Replace blower motor
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Radio Display Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: LCD screen on radio becomes blank or distorted
    * Solution: Replace radio unit
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

The 2014 Nissan Xterra generally has a good reliability record, with few major issues reported. The transmission slippage and HVAC fan failure are relatively common, but they can be costly to fix. Overall, the Xterra is a rugged and capable SUV, but it may not be the best choice for those who prioritize interior comfort and advanced safety features.
