**Introduction:**
The 2009 Nissan Aprio is a small, budget-friendly sedan that offers a practical and affordable option for drivers. It comes with a 1.6-liter engine and a 4-speed automatic transmission. The Aprio has received positive reviews for its comfortable ride, spacious interior, and fuel efficiency. However, it has also been criticized for its lackluster performance and outdated design.

### **Engine**

**1.6-Liter Engine (110 hp, 112 lb-ft)**

* **Timing Chain Tensioner Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The timing chain tensioner can fail, causing the timing chain to slip and potentially damage the engine. Symptoms include rattling or grinding noises from the engine, decreased power, and difficulty starting.
    * Solution: Replace the timing chain tensioner.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800+
    * Recalls: None Found

### **Transmission**

**4-Speed Automatic Transmission**

* **Transmission Fluid Leak:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The transmission fluid may leak from the transmission pan or other seals. Symptoms include a low transmission fluid level, slipping gears, and difficulty shifting.
    * Solution: Repair or replace the leaking component.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

### **Suspension**

* **Front Suspension Strut Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The front suspension struts can fail, causing a loss of stability, uneven tire wear, and a rough ride. Symptoms include excessive bouncing, swaying, or pulling to one side while driving.
    * Solution: Replace the failed struts.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+ (for both struts)
    * Recalls: None Found

### **Electrical**

* **Battery Discharge:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The battery may discharge prematurely, causing difficulty starting the vehicle. Symptoms include dim headlights, slow cranking, and a need to jump-start the car frequently.
    * Solution: Diagnose the electrical system to find the source of the discharge and repair or replace the faulty component.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+
    * Recalls: None Found

### **Exterior**

* **Door Lock Solenoid Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The door lock solenoids may fail, causing the doors to become difficult or impossible to lock or unlock. Symptoms include clicking or buzzing noises from the doors and the inability to activate the door locks with the key fob or interior controls.
    * Solution: Replace the failed solenoid.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300+ (for one door)
    * Recalls: None Found

### **Interior**

* **Headliner Sagging:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The headliner fabric may begin to sag or detach from the roof. Symptoms include a drooping headliner that obscures the rearview mirror and creates an unsightly appearance.
    * Solution: Replace the headliner.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
    * Recalls: None Found

### **Summary**

Overall, the 2009 Nissan Aprio is a reliable and affordable car. However, it does have some potential issues that can be costly to repair. The most severe issues include timing chain tensioner failure, transmission fluid leaks, and door lock solenoid failure. It is important to consider these potential issues before purchasing an Aprio or budgeting for future repairs.
