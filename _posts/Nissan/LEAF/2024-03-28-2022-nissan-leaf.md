**Introduction:**
The 2022 Nissan LEAF is an electric vehicle with a single-speed transmission. It offers a 147-horsepower electric motor and 236 lb-ft of torque and has a range of up to 215 miles on a full charge. Drivers appreciate its affordability, fuel efficiency, and roomy interior, but some common issues have been reported.

**Electric Motor**
* **No Issues Found**

**Transmission**
* **No Issues Found**

**Suspension**
* **No Issues Found**

**Electrical**
* **Battery Drain:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The battery drains quickly, even when the vehicle is parked and turned off.
    * Solution: Nissan has issued a recall (#NTSC2201060660) to replace the battery relay assembly.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: Covered under warranty
* **Charging Port Malfunction:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The charging port may not work properly, causing the vehicle to not charge or to charge slowly.
    * Solution: Replace the charging port.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+

**Exterior**
* **Headlight Malfunction:**
    * Description/Symptoms: One or both headlights may not work properly, or may flicker excessively.
    * Solution: Replace the headlight assembly.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
* **Door Handle Malfunction:**
    * Description/Symptoms: One or more door handles may become stiff or difficult to open.
    * Solution: Replace the door handle assembly.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+

**Interior**
* **Climate Control Malfunction:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The climate control system may not work properly, resulting in inconsistent or ineffective heating or cooling.
    * Solution: Replace the climate control module.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
* **Infotainment System Crashing:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The infotainment system may crash or freeze, losing functionality and making navigation or audio controls unavailable.
    * Solution: Update the infotainment system software.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: Free

**Summary:**
The 2022 Nissan LEAF has a few common issues, though most are relatively minor and covered under warranty. The electrical issues, particularly the battery drain and charging port malfunction, are more significant concerns but have been addressed by Nissan with recalls. Overall, the LEAF is a generally reliable and affordable electric vehicle, but it is important to be aware of these potential issues.
