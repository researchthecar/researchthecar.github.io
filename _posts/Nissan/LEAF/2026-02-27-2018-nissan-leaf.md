# 2018 Nissan LEAF Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2018 Nissan LEAF is a popular fully electric hatchback offering a practical driving range and smooth daily commuting experience. While owners generally praise its efficiency and technology, used car buyers should evaluate potential electrical and driver-assist system quirks. Reviewing maintenance records and testing all electronic features prior to purchase is recommended.

---

## Engine

### 40 kWh Electric Motor

The standard electric motor produces approximately 147 horsepower and 236 lb-ft of torque, providing brisk acceleration for city driving. This powertrain option is generally reliable, though drivers should monitor thermal management during extended high-speed or rapid-charging sessions.

## Transmission

### Single-Speed Fixed Gear Direct Drive

The single-speed automatic reduction gear delivers power seamlessly to the front wheels without traditional gear shifts. This direct-drive transmission setup is mechanically simple and rarely presents major reliability concerns.

## Steering

### Steering Wheel Trim

Steering mechanics are robust, though cabin touchpoints like the steering wheel trim may exhibit premature wear.

**Steering Wheel Material Breakdown**
- **Symptoms:** Plastic or metallic components on the steering wheel can detach, warp, or experience paint chipping, potentially causing minor injuries or lacerations.
- **Solution:** Inspect the steering wheel condition closely and replace or repair damaged trim pieces if necessary.

## Electrical

### High-Voltage Battery and Power System

The electrical architecture manages high-voltage power distribution and battery charging. While most systems perform reliably, high-mileage examples can experience high-voltage battery degradation or power distribution module failures.

**Traction Battery and Power Module Failures**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may experience sudden loss of motive power, erratic battery state-of-charge displays, or warning messages such as 'Service EV System, No Power'.
- **Solution:** Have the high-voltage battery health and power distribution modules professionally tested by an authorized service center.

## Safety

### Forward Collision Avoidance

Advanced safety features aim to protect occupants, though sensor sensitivity can occasionally cause false activation of safety systems.

**Unexpected Automatic Braking**
- **Symptoms:** The automatic emergency braking system may sporadically engage or slam on the brakes without an actual obstruction in the path of travel.
- **Solution:** Ensure vehicle software and sensor calibrations are updated to the latest manufacturer specifications.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V654000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=LEAF&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 111, "Rear Visibility." The lack of an image in the back-up camera display increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 23V048000: Equipment:Other:Owners/Service/Other Manual](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=LEAF&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 103, "Windshield Defrosting and Defogging Systems." Reduced defroster performance can limit visibility out of the windshield, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 23V494000: Vehicle Speed Control](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=LEAF&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** Unintentional acceleration can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will reprogram the vehicle control module (VCM), free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V071000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=LEAF&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 111, "Rear Visibility." A rearview camera that does not properly display an image can reduce the driver's rear view, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the rearview camera and harness for damage and replace them as necessary. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2018 Nissan LEAF offers an affordable gateway into electric vehicle ownership with solid everyday performance. Prospective buyers should thoroughly check battery health, test drive the vehicle to verify driver-assist sensor behavior, and ensure all recall services have been properly completed.
