# 2021 Nissan LEAF Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2021 Nissan Leaf is a popular, all-electric hatchback known for its practical range, comfortable ride, and zero-emission daily commuting capability. However, prospective buyers should review specific electrical and visibility concerns that have emerged from owner reports and safety notices.

---

## Engine

### 40 kWh Electric Motor (Standard)

The standard 40 kWh electric motor delivers approximately 147 horsepower and 236 lb-ft of torque, providing responsive acceleration for city and suburban driving. This powertrain option generally offers dependable everyday performance, though owners should verify any open high-voltage battery service history.

### 62 kWh Electric Motor (PLUS)

The larger 62 kWh electric motor found in PLUS models generates about 214 horsepower and 250 lb-ft of torque, increasing overall driving range and highway passing power. It shares the same basic architecture as the standard motor, though battery thermal management and charging limitations remain key points to check.

## Transmission

### Single-Speed Direct Drive Transmission

A single-speed fixed gear direct-drive transmission manages power delivery to the front wheels, offering seamless acceleration without traditional gear shifts. This system has proven mechanically robust and requires very little maintenance over the life of the vehicle.

## Electrical

### Visibility

Exterior visibility systems are generally well-designed, but rear camera anomalies can temporarily impair parking safety.

**Rear View Camera Display Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The backup camera display may intermittently show a blank, grey, distorted, or pink screen, particularly during colder temperatures or after previous recall service.
- **Solution:** Inspect camera wiring harnesses and software updates; in some cases, opening and closing the tailgate temporarily restores the display, but permanent repair may require component replacement.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V048000: Equipment:Other:Owners/Service/Other Manual](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=LEAF&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 103, "Windshield Defrosting and Defogging Systems." Reduced defroster performance can limit visibility out of the windshield, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 23V494000: Vehicle Speed Control](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=LEAF&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** Unintentional acceleration can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will reprogram the vehicle control module (VCM), free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V071000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=LEAF&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 111, "Rear Visibility." A rearview camera that does not properly display an image can reduce the driver's rear view, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the rearview camera and harness for damage and replace them as necessary. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V655000: Electrical System:Propulsion System:Traction Battery](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=LEAF&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** A quick charging battery that overheats increases the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the battery software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2021 Nissan Leaf remains an appealing and affordable entry point into electric vehicle ownership, provided shoppers thoroughly check for open safety campaigns and charging system updates. Prioritize models where all recall remedies have been completed and ensure the electronics and charging ports function correctly.
