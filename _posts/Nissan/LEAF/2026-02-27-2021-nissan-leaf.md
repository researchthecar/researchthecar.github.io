# 2021 Nissan LEAF

### Overview
The 2021 Nissan LEAF, an all-electric compact car, is generally recognized for its solid reliability, particularly in its powertrain and core electrical systems. As a mature EV platform, many potential early-life issues have been addressed through design refinements. Owners typically report a positive ownership experience with fewer mechanical concerns compared to traditional internal combustion engine vehicles. The LEAF's focus on simplicity in its electric drivetrain contributes to its dependable nature, making it a practical choice for daily commuting and urban driving. However, like any vehicle, specific components can experience issues, though widespread problems are uncommon for this model year.

### Summary Verdict
The 2021 Nissan LEAF demonstrates strong reliability, especially concerning its electric powertrain and fundamental components. The primary concerns revolve around the long-term health of the high-voltage battery, with degradation being a possibility over time, though this is common for all EVs and often covered by warranty for a period. Minor electrical gremlins, such as 12V battery failures or infotainment glitches, are also reported but are generally not systemic. Overall, the LEAF is a dependable electric vehicle with a low incidence of major mechanical failures, making it a cost-effective and reliable choice for daily transportation.
