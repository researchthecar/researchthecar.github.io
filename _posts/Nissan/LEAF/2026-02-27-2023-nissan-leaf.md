# 2023 Nissan LEAF Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2023 Nissan LEAF continues to offer an accessible entry into the all-electric vehicle market, appealing to daily commuters and budget-conscious buyers alike. Because owner-submitted complaint data for this model year is currently sparse, prospective buyers can generally look forward to a reliable ownership experience free of widespread mechanical trends. As with any modern EV, checking charging equipment compatibility and inspecting high-voltage battery health remain essential steps during a pre-purchase inspection.

---

## Engine

### 40 kWh Electric Motor

Features a 110 kW AC synchronous electric motor producing 147 horsepower and 236 lb-ft of torque, paired with a 40 kWh lithium-ion battery pack. This standard-range powertrain delivers smooth, responsive city performance with minimal mechanical complexity for everyday reliability.

### 60 kWh Electric Motor

Features a 160 kW AC synchronous electric motor producing 214 horsepower and 250 lb-ft of torque, paired with a larger 60 kWh lithium-ion battery pack (PLUS models). This extended-range powertrain provides stronger acceleration while maintaining straightforward electric drive reliability.

## Transmission

### Single-Speed Fixed Gear Direct Drive

Utilizes a single-speed reduction gear transmission paired with front-wheel drive across all trim levels. This direct-drive system lacks multi-speed shifting mechanisms, resulting in exceptional mechanical durability and minimal maintenance requirements.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V048000: Equipment:Other:Owners/Service/Other Manual](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=LEAF&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 103, "Windshield Defrosting and Defogging Systems." Reduced defroster performance can limit visibility out of the windshield, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 23V362000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Foundation Components:Hoses, Lines/Piping, And Fittings](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=LEAF&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** The right-hand brake tubes may have been damaged during production and could corrode, possibly causing a hole in the brake line and a brake fluid leak. A brake fluid leak can reduce braking performance and extend the distance required to stop, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the right-hand brake tube, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V494000: Vehicle Speed Control](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=LEAF&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** Unintentional acceleration can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will reprogram the vehicle control module (VCM), free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2023 Nissan LEAF presents a solid, dependable option for used electric vehicle shoppers, backed by a simple powertrain and very few early owner complaints. While major mechanical red flags are absent in current owner reports, buyers should still conduct a thorough inspection of the charging hardware and battery capacity to ensure long-term satisfaction.
