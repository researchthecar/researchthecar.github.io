# 2019 Nissan LEAF Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2019 Nissan Leaf, buyers are looking at a popular all-electric hatchback known for offering practical daily driving range and smooth electric performance. However, ownership experiences highlight several noteworthy electronic, propulsion, and convenience concerns that deserve close inspection before purchasing. Paying attention to battery health, open safety campaigns, and onboard accessory functionality will help ensure a more reliable driving experience.

---

## Engine

### 40 kWh Electric Motor

The standard 40 kWh electric motor generates approximately 147 horsepower and 236 lb-ft of torque, providing responsive acceleration well-suited for city commuting. Buyers should verify battery state-of-health and review vehicle service histories for any history of propulsion warnings.

**EV System Power Failure and Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may display a 'Service EV system no power' warning message accompanied by a sudden loss of acceleration and vehicle deceleration while driving.
- **Solution:** Requires professional diagnostic evaluation by a certified technician to inspect the inverter, battery management system, or related electrical components.

### 62 kWh Electric Motor (PLUS)

The high-capacity 62 kWh electric motor ups output to roughly 214 horsepower and 250 lb-ft of torque, significantly increasing driving range for longer trips. This powertrain option is generally reliable, though prospective buyers should still confirm that all applicable high-voltage battery recalls have been addressed.

## Transmission

### Single-Speed Direct Drive Transmission

The single-speed direct drive automatic transmission provides seamless, gearless power delivery to the front wheels. This simple electric drive unit lacks traditional gears and clutches, resulting in minimal mechanical trouble spots under normal operation.

## Electrical

### Electrical System & Door Locks

Electrical architecture supports vehicle accessory systems and high-voltage charging; owner reports point toward occasional accessory lock failures and charging notices.

**Door Lock Actuator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Power door locks may fail to lock or unlock properly on specific doors, requiring manual operation.
- **Solution:** Replace the faulty door lock actuator assembly within the affected door panel.

**Battery Charging and Warning Lights**
- **Symptoms:** Illumination of electrical warning lights or dashboard alerts related to battery charging functions and fast-charging limitations.
- **Solution:** Have the vehicle inspected for open battery software updates and ensure the charging port is evaluated for hardware faults.

## Safety

### Seat Belts

Occupant safety systems generally perform well, though minor sensor and chime complaints have been reported by some owners.

**Seat Belt Reminder and Audible Chime Issues**
- **Symptoms:** The audible seat belt reminder chime or dashboard indicator may malfunction, failing to alert occupants properly when unbuckled.
- **Solution:** Inspect buckle sensors and wiring harnesses, and replace faulty buckle assemblies as needed.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V654000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=LEAF&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 111, "Rear Visibility." The lack of an image in the back-up camera display increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 23V048000: Equipment:Other:Owners/Service/Other Manual](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=LEAF&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 103, "Windshield Defrosting and Defogging Systems." Reduced defroster performance can limit visibility out of the windshield, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 23V494000: Vehicle Speed Control](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=LEAF&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** Unintentional acceleration can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will reprogram the vehicle control module (VCM), free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V071000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=LEAF&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 111, "Rear Visibility." A rearview camera that does not properly display an image can reduce the driver's rear view, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the rearview camera and harness for damage and replace them as necessary. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V700000: Electrical System:Propulsion System:Traction Battery](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=LEAF&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** A quick charging battery that overheats increases the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the battery software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2019 Nissan Leaf is an appealing option for budget-conscious used electric vehicle buyers, offering smooth acceleration and practical daily utility. While its mechanical EV components are fundamentally sound, prospective owners must carefully verify that all open high-voltage battery and charging recalls have been officially resolved. Inspecting electronics, door locks, and battery health prior to purchase will help secure a dependable commuter car.
