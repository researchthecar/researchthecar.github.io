# 2022 Nissan LEAF Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2022 Nissan LEAF represents a mature generation of this popular electric vehicle, offering two distinct battery and motor configurations suited for daily commuting and local driving. As a fully electric vehicle, it bypasses traditional internal combustion and multi-speed transmission concerns, though buyers should still evaluate overall build quality and electrical accessories. Reviewing owner reports helps prospective buyers understand potential real-world ownership quirks.

---

## Engine

### 40 kWh Electric Motor

This standard electric motor pairs with a 40 kWh battery pack, generating approximately 147 horsepower and 236 lb-ft of torque. It provides brisk low-end acceleration well-suited for urban and suburban driving.

### 62 kWh Electric Motor

The larger battery option in LEAF PLUS trims pairs with a more potent motor producing 214 horsepower and 250 lb-ft of torque. It offers extended driving range and stronger highway passing power.

## Transmission

### Single-Speed Direct Drive Transmission

A single-speed fixed-gear automatic transmission channels power directly to the front wheels across all trims. It offers seamless power delivery without traditional gear shifts and requires minimal maintenance.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V048000: Equipment:Other:Owners/Service/Other Manual](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=LEAF&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 103, "Windshield Defrosting and Defogging Systems." Reduced defroster performance can limit visibility out of the windshield, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 23V494000: Vehicle Speed Control](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=LEAF&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** Unintentional acceleration can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will reprogram the vehicle control module (VCM), free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V071000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=LEAF&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 111, "Rear Visibility." A rearview camera that does not properly display an image can reduce the driver's rear view, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the rearview camera and harness for damage and replace them as necessary. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V655000: Electrical System:Propulsion System:Traction Battery](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=LEAF&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** A quick charging battery that overheats increases the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the battery software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2022 Nissan LEAF provides straightforward electric mobility with minimal mechanical complexity in its powertrain. Prospective buyers should inspect all standard vehicle systems normally, keeping in mind that owner feedback for this model year remains sparse.
