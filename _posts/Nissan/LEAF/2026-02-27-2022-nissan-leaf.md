# 2022 Nissan LEAF Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2022 Nissan LEAF is a practical and popular all-electric hatchback offering a smooth, quiet ride and a choice of battery pack sizes suited for daily commuting. While owners generally appreciate its everyday usability, secondhand buyers should carefully research battery management status and charging capabilities. Certain high-voltage battery concerns have surfaced around quick-charging limitations and potential thermal anomalies, making a thorough pre-purchase inspection essential.

---

## Engine

### Electric Drive Motor

The standard electric drive motor delivers responsive torque and emission-free daily driving performance. It provides reliable everyday propulsion, though high-mileage or heavily cycled battery packs require careful verification.

**Loss of Motive Power**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers report unexpected deceleration, entering "turtle mode," or sudden power reduction while driving at highway speeds.
- **Solution:** Consult a certified dealer for battery management software diagnostics and updates.

## Transmission

### Single-Speed Direct Drive

The single-speed direct-drive transmission provides seamless power delivery without traditional gear shifts. It features a simple mechanical layout with a strong reputation for long-term durability.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes and e-Pedal

The braking setup combines traditional friction brakes with regenerative braking and the one-pedal driving feature to maximize energy recovery.

**Brake Responsiveness Inconsistency**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers note momentary reduction in braking effectiveness or unexpected roll while using the e-Pedal feature on inclines or rough surfaces.
- **Solution:** Have the regenerative braking system and physical brake components inspected by a qualified technician.

## Electrical

### High-Voltage Battery System

Electrical and battery management systems govern power flow, charging routines, and accessory integration across the vehicle.

**Charging Restrictions and Power Limits**
- **Symptoms:** Owners experience limitations on Level 3 DC fast charging, warning messages regarding battery state, or failure to restart after charging.
- **Solution:** Verify if open battery management recalls have been successfully remedied by an authorized dealer.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V048000: Equipment:Other:Owners/Service/Other Manual](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=LEAF&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 103, "Windshield Defrosting and Defogging Systems." Reduced defroster performance can limit visibility out of the windshield, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 23V494000: Vehicle Speed Control](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=LEAF&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** Unintentional acceleration can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will reprogram the vehicle control module (VCM), free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V071000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=LEAF&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 111, "Rear Visibility." A rearview camera that does not properly display an image can reduce the driver's rear view, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the rearview camera and harness for damage and replace them as necessary. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V655000: Electrical System:Propulsion System:Traction Battery](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=LEAF&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** A quick charging battery that overheats increases the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the battery software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2022 Nissan LEAF remains an attractive option for budget-conscious electric vehicle shoppers, provided its battery health and recall histories are fully verified. Buyers should inspect the high-voltage battery state of health, confirm that all software updates and safety campaigns have been completed, and test all charging methods before finalizing a purchase.
