### Overview
The 2017 Nissan LEAF is a popular all-electric compact car known for its urban maneuverability and zero-emission operation. As an early adopter of mainstream EV technology, its reliability is a key consideration for potential buyers. This analysis focuses on documented technical issues beyond standard maintenance, examining common problem areas reported by owners and technicians. While generally considered reliable for an early-generation EV, specific components and systems have shown tendencies for certain issues that can impact long-term ownership experience and cost.

## Electrical

### Battery Pack
**Battery Degradation**
- **Symptoms:** Reduced driving range, slower charging times, battery warning lights.
- **Solution:** Battery replacement (often out of warranty), software updates may offer minor improvements.
- **Cost:** $3,000 - $7,000+


### 12V Auxiliary Battery
**Premature Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Various electrical systems malfunctioning (lights, infotainment, starting issues), dashboard warning lights.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the 12V battery.
- **Cost:** $150 - $300


### On-Board Charger
**Charging System Faults**
- **Symptoms:** Inability to charge, charging interrupted, error messages related to charging.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of the on-board charger unit.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1,500

## Interior

### HVAC System
**Heater Core Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Lack of heat, coolant leaks in the cabin, fogging windows.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the heater core.
- **Cost:** $400 - $800


### Infotainment System
**Screen Glitches/Freezing**
- **Symptoms:** Touchscreen unresponsive, system freezing, display errors.
- **Solution:** Software updates, system reset, or replacement of the infotainment unit.
- **Cost:** $300 - $1,000

### Summary Verdict
The 2017 Nissan LEAF generally offers a reliable ownership experience, particularly concerning its electric powertrain and basic chassis components. However, potential buyers should be aware of common issues such as battery degradation, which is a significant concern for any EV of this age, and potential failures of the 12V auxiliary battery and on-board charger. Interior components like the HVAC system and infotainment can also present issues. While not as prevalent as in some internal combustion engine vehicles, these electrical and climate control concerns are the primary areas to monitor for maintenance and potential repair costs.
