# 2017 Nissan LEAF Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used Nissan Leaf, buyers generally find a practical and efficient daily electric hatchback. While the electric powertrain offers low running costs and smooth local commuting, prospective owners should review known driver-reported feedback regarding occupancy sensor glitches and rare propulsion warnings before purchasing.

---

## Engine

### Electric Drive Motor

The electric drive motor paired with available battery packs delivers quiet, instant torque suited for urban driving. These electric systems generally require minimal routine maintenance compared to internal combustion engines.

**Sudden Loss of Propulsion**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle unexpectedly loses acceleration capability and drops to a reduced creep speed, accompanied by a turtle warning light on the dashboard.
- **Solution:** Diagnostic inspection by an EV-certified technician is required to check battery management and inverter software codes.

## Transmission

### Single-Speed Direct Drive

The single-speed direct drive transmission delivers power seamlessly to the wheels without traditional gear shifts. It is mechanically simple and known to be durable over long-term ownership.

## Safety

### Air Bag

Safety equipment generally functions reliably, though some owners have reported front passenger occupancy sensor faults that require professional service.

**SRS Airbag Warning Light Flashing**
- **Symptoms:** The airbag warning light flashes continuously on the dashboard, often accompanied by diagnostic trouble code B00A0 indicating an occupancy classification sensor fault.
- **Solution:** Replace the faulty occupant classification sensors and/or the SRS control unit as recommended by diagnostic testing.
- **Cost:** $1,000

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V253000: Air Bags](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=LEAF&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** Due to a manufacturing error within the air bag inflators, the passenger frontal air bag may not properly deploy in the event of a crash. In the event of a crash, if the front passenger air bag doesn't deploy properly, it can increase the risk of injury to passengers.
- **What to do:** Nissan will notify owners, and dealers will replace the front passenger air bag inflator, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V296000: Equipment:Other:Owners/Service/Other Manual](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=LEAF&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 103, "Windshield Defrosting and Defogging Systems." Reduced defroster performance can limit visibility out of the windshield, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The Nissan Leaf remains a solid choice for budget-conscious electric vehicle shoppers looking for an affordable commuter car. Buyers should thoroughly inspect the vehicle's diagnostic history for any history of SRS airbag warning lights or propulsion alerts to ensure a trouble-free ownership experience.
