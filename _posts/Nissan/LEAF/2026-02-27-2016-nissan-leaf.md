# 2016 Nissan LEAF Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used all-electric vehicle like this model year, buyers often seek affordable, zero-emission daily transportation. While ownership costs can be very low, prospective buyers should review driver-submitted complaints regarding brake pedal feel and unexpected powertrain behavior. These reports, sourced from federal vehicle safety databases, highlight areas to inspect closely during a test drive.

---

## Engine

### 80 kW AC Synchronous Electric Motor (24 kWh / 30 kWh Battery)

Powered by an 80 kW AC synchronous electric motor paired with either a 24 kWh or an upgraded 30 kWh lithium-ion battery pack, producing 107 horsepower and 187 lb-ft of torque. This powertrain delivers brisk low-end acceleration suitable for urban commuting, though overall driving range varies depending on battery health and degradation.

**Unintended Neutral Shift or Power Loss**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have reported that the vehicle unexpectedly shifts into neutral or loses propulsion power while driving on highways or inclines.
- **Solution:** Diagnostic scanning by a qualified EV technician is required to check for inverter or software fault codes.

## Transmission

### Single-Speed Fixed Gear Direct Drive

Uses a single-speed direct-drive transmission that routes motor power directly to the front wheels. This simple gearbox generally requires very little maintenance and lacks traditional multi-speed shifting complexities.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

Components related to regenerative and hydraulic braking operations.

**Brake Pedal Travel and Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have experienced a hard or unresponsive brake pedal requiring excessive force or travel to the floor to bring the vehicle to a stop.
- **Solution:** Inspect the regenerative braking system, brake fluid, master cylinder, and ABS module for faults or software updates.

## Steering

### Steering Column and Mechanism

Provides electronic power steering assist for maneuverability.

**Steering Bind and Noise**
- **Symptoms:** A plastic sticking feeling and audible noise occur when turning the wheel, causing momentary steering bind during gentle curves.
- **Solution:** Inspect the steering intermediate shaft and internal column bushings for wear or binding.

## Electrical

### Electrical System

Manages high-voltage battery communications, vehicle accessory power, and charging systems.

**Main Battery and Powertrain Shutdown**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle displays warning messages, experiences sudden deceleration, or completely shuts down while driving at high speeds.
- **Solution:** Have the high-voltage battery health, onboard charger, and main contactors inspected by a certified EV service center.

## Exterior

### Door Handles

Exterior trim elements including door handles and body structure.

**Exterior Door Handle Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Exterior door handles can break or snap, preventing doors from opening from the outside.
- **Solution:** Replace the broken door handle assembly with updated replacement parts.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V436000: Air Bags:Frontal:Passenger Side:Inflator Module](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=LEAF&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** If the wiring harness disconnects, the passenger air bag may not to deploy during a crash, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Nissan will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the wiring harness connector for proper engagement and, if necessary, replace the passenger air bag module and main body harness, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V253000: Air Bags](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=LEAF&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** Due to a manufacturing error within the air bag inflators, the passenger frontal air bag may not properly deploy in the event of a crash. In the event of a crash, if the front passenger air bag doesn't deploy properly, it can increase the risk of injury to passengers.
- **What to do:** Nissan will notify owners, and dealers will replace the front passenger air bag inflator, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V244000: Air Bags:Sensor:Occupant Classification](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=LEAF&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** As a result, the passenger frontal air bag may be turned off and not deploy in the event of a crash. If the passenger frontal air bag does not deploy as intended in the event of a crash, the passenger is at an increased risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will reprogram the Air Bag Control Unit (ACU) and OCS Electronic Control Unit (ECU) in Altima, Maxima, Murano, Rogue, and Sentra vehicles, and replace the OCS ECU in LEAF, NV200, NV200 Taxi, Pathfinder, Infiniti Q50, JX35, and QX60. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V296000: Equipment:Other:Owners/Service/Other Manual](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=LEAF&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 103, "Windshield Defrosting and Defogging Systems." Reduced defroster performance can limit visibility out of the windshield, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

This vehicle can serve as an economical commuter choice if the battery health has been verified and all electrical and brake systems operate correctly. Buyers should thoroughly test the brakes, verify state-of-health reports for the high-voltage battery, and check for smooth steering response before purchasing.
