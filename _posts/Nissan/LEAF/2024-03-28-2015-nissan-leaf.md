**Introduction:**

The 2015 Nissan LEAF is an all-electric compact hatchback that offers a quiet and fuel-efficient driving experience. It is powered by an electric motor that produces 107 horsepower and 187 lb-ft of torque. The LEAF comes with a single-speed transmission and is available in two trim levels: S and SV. It is known for its spacious interior, advanced technology features, and low operating costs. However, like any vehicle, it has had some reported issues.

**Electrical**

**Battery**

**Premature Battery Degradation:**

Description/Symptoms: Some LEAF owners have reported that their vehicle's battery experiences premature degradation, resulting in reduced range and power loss.
Solution: Battery replacement or reconditioning.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $5,000 - $10,000+
Recalls: None Found

**Charging Port**

**Charging Issue:**

Description/Symptoms: Some LEAF owners have encountered issues with the charging port, including difficulty connecting or maintaining a charge.
Solution: Cleaning or replacing the charging port.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500
Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

**Paint Defects:**

Description/Symptoms: Some LEAF owners have reported paint defects, such as bubbling or peeling, on their vehicles.
Solution: Paint repairs or repainting.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,500+
Recalls: None Found

**Windshield Wiper Motor**

**Wiper Motor Failure:**

Description/Symptoms: Some LEAF owners have experienced failure of the windshield wiper motor, resulting in the inability to use the wipers.
Solution: Replacement of the wiper motor.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $700 - $1,200
Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

**Dashboard Squeaks:**

Description/Symptoms: Some LEAF owners have reported annoying squeaks or rattles coming from the dashboard area.
Solution: Inspection and tightening of loose panels or components.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $250
Recalls: None Found

**Door Panel Defects:**

Description/Symptoms: Some LEAF owners have encountered issues with loose or damaged door panels, resulting in rattling or other noises.
Solution: Replacement or repair of the door panels.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

Overall, the 2015 Nissan LEAF has received positive feedback for its performance, efficiency, and technology features. However, it has had some issues with battery degradation, charging port malfunctions, paint defects, and interior squeaks. The severity and cost of these issues vary depending on the specific problem and the extent of the repairs required. It's important for LEAF owners to address any issues promptly to maintain the vehicle's performance and safety.
