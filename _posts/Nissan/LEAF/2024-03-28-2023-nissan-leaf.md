**2023 Nissan LEAF**

The 2023 Nissan LEAF is an all-electric hatchback that offers a single electric motor with 214 horsepower and 250 lb-ft of torque. It has a range of up to 215 miles on a single charge. The LEAF is praised for its comfortable interior, advanced safety features, and affordability. However, it has also been criticized for its limited range and long charging times.

**Suspension**

**Front and Rear Suspension**

* **Faulty Rear Sway Bar End Links:** The rear sway bar end links may wear out prematurely and begin to rattle or squeak when driving over bumps or uneven surfaces.
* **Description/Symptoms:** Excessive noise coming from the rear suspension when driving over bumpy roads or making turns.
* **Solution:** Replacing the faulty end links with new ones.
* **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $300 - $500+
* **Recalls:** None Found

**Electrical**

**Battery**

* **Battery Degradation:** The battery may experience degradation over time, which can reduce its capacity and range.
* **Description/Symptoms:** Reduced driving range, difficulty charging the battery, or premature battery failure.
* **Solution:** Replacing the battery with a new one.
* **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $5,000 - $10,000+
* **Recalls:** None Found

**Other Electrical**

* **Faulty Charging Port:** The charging port may fail to properly connect or charge the battery.
* **Description/Symptoms:** Difficulty charging the vehicle, intermittent charging, or error messages related to the charging port.
* **Solution:** Replacing the charging port assembly.
* **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $500 - $1,000+
* **Recalls:** None Found

**Exterior**

**No Issues Found**

**Interior**

* **Loose Interior Trim:** Some interior trim pieces, such as door panels, may become loose or rattle over time.
* **Description/Symptoms:** Excessive noise or vibration coming from the interior when driving over rough roads or music is playing.
* **Solution:** Tightening or replacing the loose trim pieces.
* **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $100 - $300+
* **Recalls:** None Found

**Summary**

The 2023 Nissan LEAF is a generally reliable vehicle. However, it is not without its issues. The most common issues reported by owners are related to the battery, suspension, and charging port. While these issues can be significant and costly to fix, they are not widespread. Overall, the LEAF offers a decent balance of value, comfort, and performance.
