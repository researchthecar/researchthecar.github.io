# 2005 Nissan Almera

### Overview
The 2005 Nissan Almera, a compact car, generally presented a mixed bag of reliability. While many owners reported satisfactory performance, certain areas showed a propensity for issues. The vehicle's design, aimed at affordability and practicality, sometimes meant compromises in long-term durability for specific components. Understanding these potential weak points can be crucial for prospective buyers or current owners looking to maintain their Almera. This analysis focuses on common reported technical issues, excluding routine maintenance and standard wear and tear, to provide a clear picture of its reliability profile.

### 2.2L dCi Diesel (YD22DDTi)
**Turbocharger Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of power, whistling noise from engine bay, black smoke from exhaust.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the turbocharger unit.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1500

**Injector Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Rough idling, poor fuel economy, engine misfires, difficulty starting.
- **Solution:** Cleaning or replacement of fuel injectors.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600 per injector

### 4-Speed Automatic
**Slipping Gears**
- **Symptoms:** Transmission hesitates to shift, engine revs increase without corresponding acceleration, 'clunking' noises.
- **Solution:** Transmission fluid flush and filter replacement, or transmission rebuild/replacement.
- **Cost:** $250 - $500 (fluid service), $1500 - $3000 (rebuild/replacement)


## Suspension

### Standard Independent Front/Rear
**Worn Bushings**
- **Symptoms:** Clunking or rattling noises over bumps, vague steering feel, uneven tire wear.
- **Solution:** Replacement of worn suspension bushings.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600

**Strut Mount Bearing Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Popping or creaking noise when turning the steering wheel, especially at low speeds.
- **Solution:** Replacement of strut mount bearings.
- **Cost:** $200 - $400


## Electrical

### General Electrical System
**Alternator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Dimming headlights, battery warning light illuminated, electrical components malfunctioning, engine stalling.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the alternator.
- **Cost:** $400 - $700

**Window Regulator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Power windows not operating, slow or jerky window movement, clicking noise when operating.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the window regulator mechanism.
- **Cost:** $200 - $400 per window

## Interior

### Cabin Components
**HVAC Blower Motor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** No air coming from vents, or air only comes out at certain speeds.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the HVAC blower motor.
- **Cost:** $250 - $500

**Dashboard Warning Lights**
- **Symptoms:** Intermittent illumination of various warning lights without apparent cause.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and repair of faulty sensors or wiring harness.
- **Cost:** $150 - $400

### Summary Verdict
The 2005 Nissan Almera offers a generally acceptable level of reliability, particularly with its petrol engines and manual transmissions. However, owners should be aware of potential issues with the diesel engine's turbocharger and injectors, as well as the automatic transmission's tendency for slipping gears. Suspension components like bushings and strut mounts can also require attention over time. Electrical gremlins, such as alternator failure and window regulator issues, are not uncommon. While not plagued by widespread critical failures, addressing these specific points can contribute to a more dependable ownership experience for this generation of Almera.
