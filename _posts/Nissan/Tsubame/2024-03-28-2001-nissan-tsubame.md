**Introduction:**

The 2001 Nissan Tsubame is a mid-size sedan that was only sold in the Japanese market. It was powered by a 2.0-liter inline-4 engine mated to a 4-speed automatic transmission. The Tsubame was known for its comfortable ride, spacious interior, and fuel efficiency. However, there were some issues reported by owners.

**Engine**

* **2.0-liter Inline-4**
    * **Excessive Oil Consumption:** Burning oil at an excessive rate, resulting in low oil levels and potential engine damage.
        * Solution: Engine rebuild or replacement of piston rings, valve seals, or other components.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $5,000+
    * **Timing Chain Guide Failure:** Premature failure of the timing chain guide, causing the timing chain to skip and potentially leading to engine damage.
        * Solution: Replacement of the timing chain guide and related components.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000
    * **Crankshaft Position Sensor Failure:** Malfunctioning crankshaft position sensor, resulting in starting problems or engine stalling.
        * Solution: Replacement of the crankshaft position sensor.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
        * Recalls: 01V141000

**Transmission**

* **4-speed Automatic**
    * **Torque Converter Shudder:** Vibration or shudder during acceleration or gear changes.
        * Solution: Replacement of the torque converter.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500
    * **Transmission Control Module Failure:** Malfunctioning transmission control module, causing erratic shifting or transmission failure.
        * Solution: Replacement of the transmission control module.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000

**Suspension**

* **No Issues Found**

**Electrical**

* **Alternator Failure:** Premature failure of the alternator, leading to charging system problems and battery drain.
        * Solution: Replacement of the alternator.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800
    * **Battery Drain:** Excessive battery drain, resulting in starting problems or complete battery failure.
        * Solution: Diagnosis and repair of electrical components causing the drain.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    * **Headlight Switch Failure:** Malfunctioning headlight switch, leading to headlight failure or intermittent operation.
        * Solution: Replacement of the headlight switch.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $300

**Exterior**

    * **No Issues Found**

**Interior**

    * **HVAC Control Panel Malfunction:** Faulty HVAC control panel, resulting in malfunctioning climate controls or complete failure.
        * Solution: Replacement of the HVAC control panel.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800
    * **Cruise Control Switch Failure:** Sticking or unresponsive cruise control switch.
        * Solution: Replacement of the cruise control switch.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $300

**Summary:**

The 2001 Nissan Tsubame has some potential issues, particularly with the engine and electrical system. The cost of repairs can be significant, especially for major engine or transmission problems. However, the Tsubame is still a comfortable and fuel-efficient car, and many owners report having a positive experience with it.
