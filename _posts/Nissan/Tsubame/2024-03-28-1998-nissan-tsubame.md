## Introduction

The 1998 Nissan Tsubame was a compact sedan with a distinctive design. It was powered by a 1.8-liter inline-four engine producing 125 horsepower and 120 lb-ft of torque. The Tsubame came with a standard five-speed manual transmission or an optional four-speed automatic transmission.

People who liked the Tsubame praised its affordability, sporty handling, and comfortable interior. However, it was also criticized for its lack of power and limited rear-seat space.

## Engine

- 1.8-liter inline-four engine

  - **Excessive oil consumption**
    - Description/Symptoms: Burning of oil, leading to low oil levels and potential engine damage.
    - Solution: Replacing piston rings or valve seals.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
    - Recalls: None Found
  
## Transmission

- Five-speed manual transmission

  - **Difficulty shifting into gear**
    - Description/Symptoms: Hard or impossible to engage gears, especially when the transmission is cold.
    - Solution: Replacing or rebuilding the transmission.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,500+
    - Recalls: None Found

- Four-speed automatic transmission

  - **Slipping gears**
    - Description/Symptoms: Transmission failing to engage gears properly, resulting in loss of power or difficulty accelerating.
    - Solution: Replacing or rebuilding the transmission.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
    - Recalls: None Found

## Electrical

- **Faulty alternator**
  - Description/Symptoms: Undercharging battery, leading to electrical problems or a dead battery.
  - Solution: Replacing the alternator.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $250 - $500
  - Recalls: None Found

## Interior

- **Sticky dashboard**
  - Description/Symptoms: Dashboard material becoming tacky or sticky, leaving a residue on items it touches.
  - Solution: Replacing the dashboard.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
  - Recalls: None Found

## Summary

Overall, the 1998 Nissan Tsubame is a budget-friendly sedan with some minor issues to be aware of. The most prevalent problem is excessive oil consumption in the 1.8-liter engine, which can be expensive to fix. The manual transmission can also experience shifting difficulties, and the automatic transmission may slip gears. Other issues, such as a faulty alternator and a sticky dashboard, are less common. With proper maintenance and repairs, the Tsubame can provide a reliable and enjoyable driving experience.
