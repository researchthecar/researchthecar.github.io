**Introduction**

The 2017 Nissan Juke is a subcompact crossover SUV that offers a unique and sporty design. It is available with two engine options: a 1.6-liter naturally aspirated four-cylinder engine and a 1.6-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine. Both engines are paired with a continuously variable transmission (CVT). The Juke has received praise for its distinctive styling, responsive handling, and comfortable interior. However, it has also been criticized for its limited cargo space and lack of rear-seat legroom.

**Engine**

**1.6-liter Naturally Aspirated Four-Cylinder Engine with CVT**

**Timing Chain Tensioner Failure**
Description/Symptoms: Engine rattling or knocking noises, decreased engine performance
Solution: Replace timing chain tensioner
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
Recalls: None Found

**Valve Cover Gasket Leak**
Description/Symptoms: Oil leaking from the valve cover area
Solution: Replace valve cover gasket
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
Recalls: None Found

**1.6-liter Turbocharged Four-Cylinder Engine with CVT**

**Turbocharger Failure**
Description/Symptoms: Decreased engine power, whistling or squealing noises from the turbocharger
Solution: Replace turbocharger
Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**CVT (Standard)**

**CVT Slipping or Hesitation**
Description/Symptoms: Transmission slipping or hesitating during acceleration or gear changes
Solution: Replace CVT fluid or rebuild CVT
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $3,000+
Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

**Front Strut Assembly Failure**
Description/Symptoms: Clunking or rattling noises from the front suspension, uneven tire wear
Solution: Replace front strut assembly
Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $600+
Recalls: None Found

**Rear Shock Absorber Failure**
Description/Symptoms: Leaking rear shock absorbers, reduced suspension performance
Solution: Replace rear shock absorbers
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

**Battery Drain**
Description/Symptoms: Battery draining quickly, electrical accessories not working properly
Solution: Diagnose and repair electrical system (may involve replacing battery, alternator, or other components)
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+
Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

**Sunroof Leaking**
Description/Symptoms: Water leaking into the cabin from the sunroof
Solution: Replace sunroof seal or repair sunroof mechanism
Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $700+
Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

**Dashboard Rattle**
Description/Symptoms: Rattling or buzzing noise coming from the dashboard
Solution: Tighten or replace loose parts in the dashboard
Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+
Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2017 Nissan Juke has several potential issues that can affect its engine, transmission, suspension, electrical system, exterior, and interior. The severity and cost of these issues vary, with some being relatively minor and inexpensive to fix, while others can be more serious and costly. Overall, the Juke has received mixed reviews in terms of reliability, with some owners reporting significant problems, while others have had positive experiences. It is important for potential buyers to be aware of these issues before purchasing a Juke and to factor the potential cost of repairs into their decision-making process.
