# 2020 Nissan Maxima Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2020 Nissan Maxima, buyers are generally drawn to its sharp styling, comfortable cabin, and robust V6 performance. However, evaluating owner complaints reveals a few recurring trouble spots that prospective owners should inspect closely during a test drive. While many drivers enjoy trouble-free operation, paying attention to potential electronic safety sensor glitches and powertrain reports can help you secure a reliable example.

---

## Engine

### 3.5L V6 Engine

The 2020 Nissan Maxima is powered by a 3.5-liter V6 engine producing approximately 300 horsepower and 261 lb-ft of torque. This naturally aspirated powerplant provides strong, smooth acceleration and generally dependable mechanical performance.

## Transmission

### Xtronic Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)

Power is sent to the front wheels via an Xtronic Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT). While engineered for fuel efficiency, prospective buyers should carefully check this unit for any signs of hesitation, slipping, or unusual noises during a test drive.

**Transmission Failure / Drivability Concerns**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have reported unexpected jerking, abnormal noises, sudden stalling, or complete transmission failure while driving.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified mechanic inspect the transmission operation and fluid condition, which may require professional repair or CVT replacement if internal wear is present.

### Automatic Emergency Braking System

**Unintended Autonomous Braking**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may forcefully brake on its own or trigger emergency braking, traction control, and slip indicator warning lights while traveling at highway speeds.
- **Solution:** Inspect forward-facing radar and camera sensors for alignment, cleanliness, or software calibration issues at a dealership or specialized service center.

**Parking Brake Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** The electronic emergency brake or parking brake may engage or illuminate warning indicators spontaneously, particularly after heavy rain.
- **Solution:** Check the parking brake actuator, switches, and wiring harnesses for moisture intrusion or electrical faults.

## Exterior

### Glass and Trim

**Sunroof and Windshield Stress Fractures**
- **Symptoms:** The panoramic sunroof glass may shatter unexpectedly while driving, or windshields can develop sudden cracks and stress fractures without apparent impact.
- **Solution:** Carefully inspect the roof glass and windshield for any preexisting chips or frame stress, and replace damaged glass through comprehensive insurance coverage if necessary.

## Safety

### Forward Collision Avoidance

**Sensor and Warning Light Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** Forward collision warning lights or driver assistance error messages illuminate intermittently or remain constantly active shortly after starting the vehicle.
- **Solution:** Clean sensor areas and have the diagnostic trouble codes scanned to determine if a radar sensor or camera module requires reprogramming or replacement.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V570000: Visibility:Glass, Side/Rear](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=Maxima&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** The rear window glass may not remain properly secured to the vehicle. Rear window glass that separates from the vehicle can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Nissan will notify owners, and dealers will replace the back window glass, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2020 Nissan Maxima offers an engaging driving experience and a high-end feel, but used car buyers should perform targeted checks before purchasing. Pay close attention to how the CVT operates during acceleration, test all driver-assistance and forward-collision safety systems, and thoroughly inspect the glass and exterior trim for any signs of stress fractures or loose body molding. NHTSA complaints indicate that electronic sensor quirks and occasional powertrain concerns are worth investigating prior to finalizing a deal.
