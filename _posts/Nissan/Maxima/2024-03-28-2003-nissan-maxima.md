### 2003 Nissan Maxima

The 2003 Nissan Maxima is a mid-size sedan praised for its comfortable and spacious interior, powerful engine, and sporty handling. The VQ35DE 3.5-liter V6 engine provides responsive performance with 255 horsepower and 246 lb-ft of torque. The vehicle is available with either a 5-speed automatic transmission or a 6-speed manual transmission.

Despite its positive attributes, the 2003 Maxima has experienced several issues reported by owners.

**Engine**

**VQ35DE 3.5-Liter V6**

* **Intake Manifold Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Cracks in the intake manifold can cause vacuum leaks, resulting in rough idling, poor performance, and illuminated check engine light.
    * Solution: Replacement of the intake manifold.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,200+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Oil Consumption:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Excessive oil consumption, requiring frequent top-ups.
    * Solution: Piston ring replacement or engine rebuild.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $5,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

* **5-Speed Automatic Transmission**
    * **Slipping Gears:**
        * Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears, transmission slipping out of gear while driving.
        * Solution: Transmission rebuild or replacement.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,500+
        * Recalls: None Found
* **6-Speed Manual Transmission**
    * **No Issues Found**

**Suspension**

* **Rear Subframe Cracking:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Cracks in the rear subframe can lead to instability and difficulty steering.
    * Solution: Reinforcement of the subframe or replacement.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Alternator Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Charging system malfunctions, causing battery drain and electrical issues.
    * Solution: Replacement of the alternator.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Rear Spoiler Cracking:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Cracks in the rear spoiler, compromising aesthetics.
    * Solution: Replacement of the rear spoiler.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Climate Control Malfunction:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Inoperative climate control system, leading to discomfort and inability to regulate cabin temperature.
    * Solution: Repair or replacement of the climate control module or associated components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

While the 2003 Nissan Maxima offers a compelling combination of performance and comfort, it is not without its potential issues. Several engine and transmission problems can be costly to resolve. Suspension, electrical, and interior issues are also prevalent. However, the severity and cost of these issues vary, and some may not affect all vehicles. It is important for potential buyers to be aware of these potential problems before purchasing a used 2003 Maxima.
