**Introduction:**

The 2021 Nissan Maxima is a full-size sedan that offers a powerful engine and a comfortable interior. It is available with a single engine option, a 3.5-liter V6 that produces 300 horsepower and 261 lb-ft of torque. The Maxima comes standard with a CVT and offers an optional 9-speed automatic transmission.

People like the Maxima for its spacious interior, comfortable seats, and powerful engine. However, some people have reported issues with the vehicle's engine, transmission, and other components.

**Engine**

* **3.5-liter V6**
    * **Intake valve deposits:** Intake valves can accumulate carbon deposits over time, leading to reduced engine performance and fuel economy.
        * Solution: Cleaning the intake valves.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * **Timing chain stretch:** The timing chain can stretch over time, leading to engine noise and reduced power.
        * Solution: Replacing the timing chain and related components.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
    * **Oil consumption:** Some Maxima owners have reported excessive oil consumption, which can lead to engine damage if not addressed.
        * Solution: Replacing the piston rings and other engine components.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $5,000+
* **Recalls:**
    * NHTSA Campaign Number: 21V522000
        * Issue: Fuel pump failure
        * Resolution: Replacing the fuel pump
    * NHTSA Campaign Number: 21V517000
        * Issue: Fuel injector failure
        * Resolution: Replacing the fuel injectors

**Transmission**

* **CVT**
    * **Hesitation and shuddering:** Some Maxima owners have reported hesitation and shuddering during acceleration, which can be caused by a faulty torque converter or transmission fluid.
        * Solution: Replacing the torque converter or performing a transmission fluid flush.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $3,000+
    * **Premature failure:** In some cases, the CVT transmission can fail prematurely, resulting in costly repairs or replacement.
        * Solution: Replacing the transmission.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $4,000 - $7,000+
* **9-speed automatic**
    * **Shifting problems:** Some Maxima owners with the 9-speed automatic transmission have reported shifting problems, such as delayed or hard shifts.
        * Solution: Reprogramming the transmission control module or replacing transmission components.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+
* **Recalls:** None Found

* **No Issues Found**

**Electrical**

* **Battery drain:** Some Maxima owners have reported excessive battery drain, which can lead to a dead battery and difficulty starting the vehicle.
        * Solution: Identifying and repairing the source of the battery drain, such as a faulty alternator or electrical component.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+
* **Electrical short:** Electrical shorts can occur in the Maxima's electrical system, causing various electrical issues, such as flickering lights or malfunctioning components.
        * Solution: Identifying and repairing the source of the electrical short.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+
* **Recalls:** None Found

**Exterior**

* **Headlight condensation:** Headlight condensation can occur in the Maxima's headlights, which can impair visibility and affect the lifespan of the headlights.
        * Solution: Replacing the headlights.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
* **Door handle failure:** The door handles on the Maxima can fail, making it difficult to open the doors.
        * Solution: Replacing the door handles.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
* **Recalls:** None Found

**Interior**

* **Navigation system issues:** Some Maxima owners have reported issues with the navigation system, such as freezing or inaccurate information.
        * Solution: Updating the navigation system software or replacing the navigation unit.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+
* **Climate control problems:** Some Maxima owners have reported problems with the climate control system, such as malfunctioning controls or weak airflow.
        * Solution: Repairing or replacing climate control components.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+
* **Recalls:** None Found

**Summary**

The 2021 Nissan Maxima has a number of reported issues, including problems with the engine, transmission, electrical system, and interior components. While some of these issues are relatively minor and inexpensive to fix, others can be costly and affect the vehicle's reliability. Overall, the severity and cost of these issues can vary depending on the specific problem and the extent of the repairs required.
