# 2004 Nissan Maxima

### Overview
The 2004 Nissan Maxima, often praised for its sporty driving dynamics and comfortable interior, generally presents a solid reliability record for its model year. While no vehicle is entirely without potential issues, the Maxima from this era tends to avoid widespread, critical failures. Owners can expect a dependable experience, though like any car, attention to regular maintenance is key. This analysis focuses on common reported technical issues beyond routine wear and tear, providing insights into potential areas of concern for prospective buyers or current owners.

---


## Engine

### 3.5L V6 (VQ35DE)
**Oil Leaks (Valve Covers/Camshaft Seals)**
- **Symptoms:** Visible oil on engine components, burning oil smell, low oil level.
- **Solution:** Replacement of valve cover gaskets or camshaft seals.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600

**Coolant Leaks (Intake Manifold Gasket)**
- **Symptoms:** Sweet smell of coolant, visible coolant residue, engine overheating, milky oil.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the intake manifold gasket.
- **Cost:** $400 - $700


## Transmission

### 5-Speed Automatic (RE5R05A)
**Harsh Shifting/Delayed Engagement**
- **Symptoms:** Jerky gear changes, hesitation when shifting into Drive or Reverse.
- **Solution:** Transmission fluid and filter change, solenoid replacement, or transmission rebuild.
- **Cost:** $500 - $2500+


## Suspension

### Standard Chassis
**Worn Control Arm Bushings**
- **Symptoms:** Clunking noises over bumps, vague steering feel, uneven tire wear.
- **Solution:** Replacement of control arm bushings or entire control arms.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600


## Electrical

### Standard System
**Window Motor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Power windows not operating, slow window operation.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the window motor and regulator assembly.
- **Cost:** $200 - $400

**Instrument Cluster Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Flickering gauges, dead pixels on displays, intermittent power loss to cluster.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of the instrument cluster.
- **Cost:** $400 - $800


## Exterior

### Standard Body
**Headlight Haze/Yellowing**
- **Symptoms:** Reduced headlight visibility, yellowed or cloudy plastic lenses.
- **Solution:** Headlight restoration kit or replacement of headlight assemblies.
- **Cost:** $50 - $300


## Interior

### Standard Cabin
**HVAC Blower Motor Resistor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** HVAC fan only works on highest setting or not at all.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the blower motor resistor.
- **Cost:** $100 - $250


--- 
### Summary Verdict
The 2004 Nissan Maxima offers a generally reliable ownership experience, particularly concerning its powertrain. The robust 3.5L V6 engine is known for its performance, though potential oil and coolant leaks are the most common engine-related concerns, typically manageable with timely repairs. The automatic transmission can sometimes exhibit shifting issues, often addressable with maintenance or component replacement. Suspension components like bushings can wear over time, leading to noise and handling degradation. Electrical gremlins, such as window motors and instrument cluster problems, are not uncommon for a vehicle of this age. Exterior and interior issues are generally minor and cosmetic or related to specific components like HVAC. Overall, the Maxima is a decent choice for reliability, provided it has been maintained well.
