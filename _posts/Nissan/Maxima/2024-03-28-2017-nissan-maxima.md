**Introduction:**

The 2017 Nissan Maxima is a sporty midsize sedan that offers a thrilling driving experience. With its aggressive styling, powerful engine, and spacious interior, it has been well-received by drivers. The Maxima comes equipped with a standard 3.5-liter V6 engine that produces 300 horsepower and 261 lb-ft of torque, paired with a CVT transmission.

**Engine**

**3.5L V6**

**Intake Valve Deposits**
Description/Symptoms: Carbon buildup forms on intake valves, which reduces airflow and engine performance. This can lead to rough idling, hesitation during acceleration, and decreased fuel efficiency.
Solution: Intake valve cleaning
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800+
Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**CVT**

**Hesitation and Shuddering**
Description/Symptoms: The transmission may hesitate or shudder when shifting gears. This can be caused by worn or slipping transmission belts or faulty sensors.
Solution: Transmission fluid flush, belt replacement, or sensor repair
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+
Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

**No Issues Found**

**Electrical**

**Battery Drain**
Description/Symptoms: The battery may discharge prematurely, leaving the vehicle unable to start. This can be caused by a faulty alternator, parasitic drain, or damaged battery.
Solution: Battery replacement, alternator repair, or parasitic drain diagnostic
Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

**Sunroof Leaks**
Description/Symptoms: The sunroof may leak water into the cabin, particularly during heavy rain. This can be caused by clogged sunroof drains or faulty seals.
Solution: Clean sunroof drains or replace sunroof seals
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

**Navigation System Issues**
Description/Symptoms: The navigation system may freeze, become unresponsive, or display incorrect directions. This can be caused by software glitches or faulty hardware.
Solution: Software update or hardware replacement
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $700+
Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

The 2017 Nissan Maxima is generally a reliable vehicle with few reported issues. However, some potential problems include intake valve deposits, transmission hesitation, sunroof leaks, and navigation system glitches. The severity and cost of these issues vary, but most can be addressed with proper maintenance and repairs.
