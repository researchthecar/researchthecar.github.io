## 2022 Nissan Maxima

The 2022 Nissan Maxima is a full-size sedan that offers a comfortable and stylish driving experience. It is powered by a 3.5L V6 engine that produces 300 horsepower and 261 lb-ft of torque, paired with a CVT transmission. The Maxima is available in four trim levels: S, SV, SR, and Platinum.

People who like the 2022 Nissan Maxima praise its powerful engine, comfortable interior, and stylish exterior. However, some drivers have reported experiencing various issues with the vehicle.

### Engine

- No Issues Found

### Transmission

- **CVT Transmission**
  - **Hesitation or Jerking During Acceleration**
    - Description/Symptoms: The transmission may hesitate or jerk when accelerating, especially from a stop.
    - Solution: Reprogramming the transmission control module (TCM) or replacing the valve body
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
    - Recalls: None Found
  - **Premature Failure**
    - Description/Symptoms: The transmission may fail prematurely, causing loss of power or difficulty shifting gears.
    - Solution: Replacing the transmission
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $3,000 - $5,000+
    - Recalls: None Found

### Suspension

- **Front Lower Control Arms**
  - **Premature Wear**
    - Description/Symptoms: The front lower control arms may wear prematurely, causing clunking noises and reduced handling.
    - Solution: Replacing the control arms
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800+
    - Recalls: None Found

### Electrical

- **Battery Drain**
  - Description/Symptoms: The battery may drain prematurely, especially when the vehicle is parked.
    - Solution: Replacing the battery or diagnosing and repairing electrical system issues
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    - Recalls: None Found
- **Infotainment System Issues**
  - Description/Symptoms: The infotainment system may experience glitches, freezing, or connectivity problems.
    - Solution: Updating the software or replacing the infotainment unit
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+
    - Recalls: None Found

### Exterior

- **Sunroof Shattering**
  - Description/Symptoms: The sunroof may spontaneously shatter, showering the interior of the vehicle with glass.
    - Solution: Replacing the sunroof assembly
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
    - Recalls: None Found

### Interior

- **Interior Trim Peeling**
  - Description/Symptoms: The interior trim, such as the dashboard or door panels, may peel or crack prematurely.
    - Solution: Replacing the affected trim pieces
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    - Recalls: None Found

### Summary

Overall, the 2022 Nissan Maxima is a reliable vehicle, but some owners have reported experiencing specific issues. The most common problems are related to the CVT transmission, suspension, and electrical system. While some of these issues can be costly to fix, most are not severe and can be easily resolved.
