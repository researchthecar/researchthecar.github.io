# 2022 Nissan Maxima

### Overview
The 2022 Nissan Maxima, a full-size sedan, generally presents a strong profile in terms of reliability, building on Nissan's established reputation. It is equipped with a potent V6 engine and a continuously variable transmission (CVT) designed for a balance of performance and efficiency. While the Maxima is often praised for its comfortable ride, upscale interior, and engaging driving dynamics, like any vehicle, it can experience specific issues. This analysis focuses on documented technical problems beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential areas of concern for owners and prospective buyers. The data aims to offer a clear picture of the Maxima's technical robustness.

### Summary Verdict
The 2022 Nissan Maxima demonstrates a generally solid reliability record, with its core powertrain components (3.5L V6 engine and Xtronic CVT) showing no widespread reported issues. The primary areas where owners have reported concerns are within the electrical system, specifically related to infotainment system glitches and, less commonly, premature 12V battery failure. These issues, while not critical to the vehicle's operation, can impact the user experience. Other categories like suspension, exterior, and interior components are considered reliable with no significant widespread problems documented. Overall, the Maxima offers a dependable ownership experience, with potential electrical quirks being the most notable, albeit manageable, concerns.
