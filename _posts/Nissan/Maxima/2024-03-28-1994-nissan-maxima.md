**1994 Nissan Maxima**

The 1994 Nissan Maxima is a mid-size sedan that was offered with a choice of two engines and two transmissions. The standard engine was a 3.0-liter V6 that produced 190 horsepower and 190 lb-ft of torque. The optional engine was a 3.5-liter V6 that produced 210 horsepower and 222 lb-ft of torque. Both engines were available with a five-speed manual transmission or a four-speed automatic transmission.

The Maxima was praised for its stylish design, comfortable interior, and powerful engine. However, it was criticized for its poor fuel economy and lack of some features that were standard on other cars in its class.

**Engine**

* **3.0-liter V6**
    * **Cylinder head gasket failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaks from the engine, overheating, and loss of engine power.
        * Solution: Replace the cylinder head gasket.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
    * **Intake manifold gasket failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: Vacuum leaks, rough idling, and decreased engine power.
        * Solution: Replace the intake manifold gasket.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500
* **3.5-liter V6**
    * **Timing chain tensioner failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: Engine rattling noise, loss of engine power, and possible engine failure.
        * Solution: Replace the timing chain tensioner.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000

**Transmission**

* **Five-speed manual transmission**
    * **Synchronizer wear**
        * Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears, especially into second gear.
        * Solution: Replace the synchronizers.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,200
* **Four-speed automatic transmission**
    * **Torque converter failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: Shuddering, slipping, and loss of transmission power.
        * Solution: Replace the torque converter.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,200 - $1,800+

**Suspension**

* **Front suspension**
    * **Strut mount bearing failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: Clunking noise when driving over bumps, excessive vibration, and reduced steering response.
        * Solution: Replace the strut mount bearing.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500 per side
* **Rear suspension**
    * **Sway bar link failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: Clunking noise when driving over bumps, reduced handling, and increased body roll.
        * Solution: Replace the sway bar link.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200 per side

**Electrical**

* **Alternator failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Dimming headlights, flickering dashboard lights, and difficulty starting the engine.
    * Solution: Replace the alternator.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800
* **Battery failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Difficulty starting the engine, dead battery, and reduced electrical power.
    * Solution: Replace the battery.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200

**Exterior**

* **Door lock actuator failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Difficulty locking or unlocking the doors, door locks not responding, and stuck door locks.
    * Solution: Replace the door lock actuator.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400 per door

**Interior**

* **Dashboard cracking**
    * Description/Symptoms: Cracks or splits in the dashboard material, especially around the passenger airbag area.
    * Solution: Replace the dashboard or repair the cracks.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+

**Recalls**

* NHTSA Campaign Number: 95V112000
    * Description: Rear seatbelts may not be properly anchored, increasing the risk of injury in a crash.
* NHTSA Campaign Number: 08V063000
    * Description: Passenger airbag may not deploy properly, increasing the risk of injury in a crash.

**Summary**

The 1994 Nissan Maxima is a reliable and comfortable car. However, it is not without its problems. The most common issues reported by owners include engine gasket failures, transmission problems, suspension issues, electrical problems, and interior problems. The severity of these issues varies, but some can be costly to repair. Overall, the Maxima is a good value for the price, but potential buyers should be aware of the potential problems that can occur.
