**Introduction:**

The 2009 Nissan Maxima is a full-size sedan known for its performance, style, and advanced features. It's powered by a 3.5L V6 engine producing 290 horsepower and 261 lb-ft of torque. The Maxima is available with a 5-speed automatic transmission or a 6-speed manual transmission.

**Engine**

**3.5L V6**

* **Valve cover leaks:** Oil leaks from the valve cover gaskets, leading to engine oil loss. Solution: Replace the valve cover gaskets. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+

**Transmission**

**5-speed Automatic**

* **Shift solenoid issues:** The shift solenoids may fail, causing transmission shifting problems or stalling. Solution: Replace the faulty shift solenoids. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+

**6-speed Manual**

* **Clutch wear:** The clutch disc can wear prematurely, causing slipping or difficulty shifting. Solution: Replace the clutch assembly. Estimated Cost to Fix: $600 - $1200+

**Suspension**

* **Rear suspension bushings:** The rear suspension bushings may deteriorate, causing increased noise and a rough ride. Solution: Replace the rear suspension bushings. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $350+

**Electrical**

* **Battery drain:** The battery may drain prematurely due to a faulty alternator or parasitic drain. Solution: Determine the source of the drain and replace or repair the faulty component. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $600+
* **Headlight issues:** The headlights may flicker or fail due to faulty wiring or a blown bulb. Solution: Inspect the wiring and replace the faulty components. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+

**Exterior**

* **Sunroof motor failure:** The sunroof motor may fail, preventing the sunroof from opening or closing. Solution: Replace the sunroof motor. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1000+

**Interior**

* **Navigation system glitches:** The navigation system may experience occasional glitches or malfunctions. Solution: Update the software or replace the navigation unit. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1200+
* **Rear seat heating element failure:** The rear seat heating element may fail, preventing the rear seats from heating. Solution: Replace the heating element. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+

**Summary:**

Overall, the 2009 Nissan Maxima has several known issues that can vary in severity and cost to fix. The engine and transmission issues are more prevalent, while the other issues are less common. Owners should be aware of these potential problems and budget accordingly for repairs.
