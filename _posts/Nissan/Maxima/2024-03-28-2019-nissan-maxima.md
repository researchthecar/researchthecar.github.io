**Introduction to the 2019 Nissan Maxima:**

The 2019 Nissan Maxima, the latest generation of Nissan's flagship sedan, offers a well-appointed interior, powerful engine options, and a smooth ride. It comes with a standard 3.5-liter V6 engine that produces 300 horsepower and 261 lb-ft of torque, paired with a continuously variable transmission (CVT). Another engine option is a 3.0-liter twin-turbocharged V6 that generates 400 horsepower and 350 lb-ft of torque, coupled with a 7-speed automatic transmission.

The Maxima has received praise for its responsive handling, comfortable seating, and impressive performance. However, some owners have reported various issues with the vehicle.

**Engine**

**3.5L V6** (CVT)

* **Oil Consumption:** Excessive oil consumption, leading to low oil levels and potential engine damage.
    * Solution: Engine rebuild or replacement with updated piston rings.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $5,000 - $10,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**3.0L Twin-Turbo V6** (7-Speed Automatic)

* No Issues Found

**Transmission**

**CVT** (3.5L V6)

* **Premature Failure:** Transmissions failing prematurely, causing loss of power and drivability.
    * Solution: Transmission replacement.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $5,000 - $7,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**7-Speed Automatic** (3.0L Twin-Turbo V6)

* No Issues Found

**Suspension**

* **Rear Suspension Sag:** Premature sagging of the rear suspension, resulting in uneven tire wear and handling issues.
    * Solution: Replacement of rear suspension components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain:** Battery draining prematurely due to faulty electrical components or parasitic battery drain.
    * Solution: Diagnosis and replacement of faulty components or installation of a battery isolator.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Fog Light Condensation:** Fog lights developing condensation and becoming inoperable.
    * Solution: Replacement of fog light assemblies with improved sealing.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Touchscreen Malfunction:** Touchscreen becoming unresponsive or displaying errors.
    * Solution: Software update or touchscreen replacement.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Climate Control Inoperability:** Climate control system failing to function properly.
    * Solution: Diagnosis and repair or replacement of faulty components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2019 Nissan Maxima offers a wide range of features and performance capabilities. However, some owners have experienced significant issues with their vehicles, including engine and transmission problems, suspension issues, and electrical malfunctions. These issues can be costly and time-consuming to repair, potentially impacting the overall ownership experience.
