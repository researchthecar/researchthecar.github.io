**1995 Nissan Maxima**

The 1995 Nissan Maxima is a midsize sedan renowned for its sporty handling, luxurious interior, and powerful performance. It came equipped with a standard 3.0L V6 engine producing 190 horsepower and 208 lb-ft of torque. Optional powertrain options included a 2.4L 4-cylinder engine with 155 horsepower and 163 lb-ft of torque, paired with a 5-speed manual or 4-speed automatic transmission.

While the Maxima offers a compelling package, it is not immune to certain mechanical issues. The following is a comprehensive list of specific problems reported by owners:

**Engine**

**3.0L V6**

* **Intake manifold gasket failure:** Symptoms include excessive oil consumption, smoke from the engine bay, and rough idling. Solution involves replacing the intake manifold gasket. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1000
* **Crankshaft position sensor failure:** Symptoms include stalling, difficulty starting, and loss of power. Solution involves replacing the crankshaft position sensor. Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300
* **Knock sensor failure:** Symptoms include engine knocking, decreased performance, and increased fuel consumption. Solution involves replacing the knock sensor. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
* **Oxygen sensor failure:** Symptoms include increased fuel consumption, poor engine performance, and emission issues. Solution involves replacing the faulty oxygen sensor. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $250

**2.4L 4-Cylinder**

* **Timing belt failure:** Symptoms include a sudden loss of power, a rattling noise from the engine, and difficulty starting. Solution involves replacing the timing belt. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800+
* **Intake camshaft failure:** Symptoms include a ticking noise from the engine, reduced performance, and poor fuel economy. Solution involves replacing the intake camshaft. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1000 - $1500

**Transmission**

**5-Speed Manual**

* **Clutch failure:** Symptoms include difficulty shifting, slipping gears, and a burning smell. Solution involves replacing the clutch disc, pressure plate, and throw-out bearing. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1000

**4-Speed Automatic**

* **Transmission fluid leak:** Symptoms include slipping gears, delayed shifting, and grinding noises. Solution involves repairing or replacing the source of the leak. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
* **Torque converter failure:** Symptoms include slipping gears, shuddering, and overheating. Solution involves replacing the torque converter. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1000 - $2000

**Suspension**

* **Strut mount failure:** Symptoms include a clunking noise from the suspension, poor handling, and premature tire wear. Solution involves replacing the strut mounts. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
* **Control arm bushing failure:** Symptoms include excessive vibration, uneven tire wear, and a loose steering feel. Solution involves replacing the control arm bushings. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500

**Electrical**

* **Alternator failure:** Symptoms include dimming headlights, difficulty starting, and a battery that won't hold a charge. Solution involves replacing the alternator. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600
* **Starter failure:** Symptoms include clicking noises when trying to start the engine, no cranking, or intermittent starting issues. Solution involves replacing the starter. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400

**Exterior**

* **Sunroof leak:** Symptoms include water leaking into the cabin when it rains. Solution involves repairing or replacing the sunroof seals. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500

**Interior**

* **Dash panel cracking:** Symptoms include cracks developing on the dashboard or instrument panel. Solution involves replacing the dashboard or repairing the cracks. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1000
* **Power seat failure:** Symptoms include difficulty adjusting the seat, intermittent movement, or a completely unresponsive seat. Solution involves repairing or replacing the power seat motor or wiring. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500

**Summary**

The 1995 Nissan Maxima offers a combination of performance and luxury, but it is prone to certain mechanical issues, particularly with its engine and transmission. While the severity and cost of the repairs can vary, some of the more significant issues can result in expensive repairs. Potential buyers should be aware of these potential problems before purchasing a used Maxima and budget accordingly for potential maintenance costs.
