# 2019 Nissan Maxima Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2019 Nissan Maxima offers a blend of bold styling and a robust V6 engine, making it an appealing option for used car buyers seeking a sporty full-size sedan. While it generally provides a comfortable ride, prospective buyers should review a few reported electrical, sunroof, and automated braking quirks that have surfaced in owner feedback.

---

## Engine

### 3.5L V6 Engine

Equipped with a 3.5-liter V6 engine producing around 300 horsepower and 261 lb-ft of torque, providing brisk acceleration. This engine is generally robust, though individual electrical sensors or accessories require routine maintenance.

**Engine Stalling and Noise**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have reported unexpected vehicle stalling or unusual noises coming from under the front hood.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified mechanic inspect the engine sensors, fuel delivery, and timing components.

## Transmission

### Xtronic CVT

Utilizes an Xtronic Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT) paired with front-wheel drive. Buyers should carefully evaluate transmission responsiveness during a test drive, as CVT repairs can be costly.

**Transmission Performance Concerns**
- **Symptoms:** Unusual noises or hesitation during acceleration and shifting operation.
- **Solution:** Test drive the vehicle thoroughly to check for smooth operation and verify fluid maintenance records.

## Electrical

### Infotainment and Lighting

electrical issues involving the entertainment screen and exterior lighting can occasionally frustrate owners.

**Radio and Headlight Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** The radio head unit cuts out randomly, volume surges on its own, or headlight system error messages appear intermittently.
- **Solution:** Check for software updates for the infotainment system and inspect headlight wiring harnesses.

## Exterior

### Sunroof and Visibility

Exterior glass components, particularly large panoramic sunroof panels, require careful inspection for stress or defects.

**Panoramic Sunroof Shattering**
- **Symptoms:** The panoramic glass roof may spontaneously fracture or shatter while driving, accompanied by a loud pop.
- **Solution:** Inspect the sunroof glass for any stress fractures and verify if replacement is covered by insurance or warranty.

## Safety

### Service Brakes

The braking system incorporates modern safety assists, but sensor calibration issues can occasionally trigger false warnings or automated stops.

**Unexpected Automatic Braking**
- **Symptoms:** The dashboard lights up and the automated emergency braking system unexpectedly activates, causing an abrupt stop.
- **Solution:** Inspect collision avoidance sensors, radar units, and software calibration at a qualified service center.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V654000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=Maxima&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 111, "Rear Visibility." The lack of an image in the back-up camera display increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2019 Nissan Maxima offers strong V6 performance and sharp styling, but used car shoppers should perform a thorough inspection. Pay close attention to the transmission's operation during a test drive, verify that all infotainment and safety sensors function correctly, and inspect the panoramic sunroof and brakes for any reported anomalies.
