# 2017 Nissan Maxima Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2017 Nissan Maxima offers sporty styling and V6 performance, making it an engaging midsize sedan to drive. However, used car buyers should carefully review owner-reported issues concerning safety electronics, suspension components, and occasional electrical anomalies.

---

## Engine

### 3.5L V6 Engine

Equipped with a 3.5-liter V6 engine producing 300 horsepower and 261 lb-ft of torque, providing strong acceleration. The engine is generally robust, though rare electrical and starting concerns have been logged by owners.

## Transmission

### Xtronic Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)

Features a sport-tuned Xtronic Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT) powering the front wheels. Buyers should test-drive thoroughly to check for smooth operation and responsiveness.

## Suspension

### Rear Control Arms

Rear suspension durability can be affected by corrosion in certain climates, leading to control arm wear or failure.

**Rear Control Arm Corrosion and Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Clunking noises, sudden wheel misalignment, accelerated tire wear, or physical breakage of the rear lower suspension components.
- **Solution:** Inspect rear suspension components for corrosion and replace damaged or compromised control arms.

## Steering

### Charging and Power System

electrical issues involving unexpected stalling and headlight condensation have been reported by a subset of owners.

**Unexpected Engine Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle suddenly turns off while driving, accompanied by a loss of power steering assist.
- **Solution:** Diagnose the charging system, alternator output, and electronic control relays to identify the source of power loss.

## Safety

### Air Bag System

Owner reports indicate occasional intermittent warnings and sensor discrepancies within the Supplemental Restraint System.

**Air Bag Warning Light**
- **Symptoms:** The airbag warning light remains illuminated on the dashboard, or passenger seat occupancy sensors incorrectly indicate that the airbag is deactivated.
- **Solution:** Have the occupant classification sensor and airbag wiring harness inspected and repaired or replaced by a qualified technician.

### Forward Collision / AEB

Forward collision-avoidance radar systems can occasionally trigger false positives or sensor obstruction warnings.

**Radar Sensor / CMBS Fault**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers experience unexpected activation of the Automatic Emergency Braking (AEB) system or fault messages regarding radar sensor obstructions.
- **Solution:** Clean the front bumper radar sensor area, check sensor alignment, and update control modules at a dealership if necessary.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V601000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Antilock/Traction Control/Electronic Limited Slip](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=Maxima&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** A brake fluid leak on the circuit board may result in an electrical short, increasing the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Nissan will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the serial number on the ABS actuator, replacing it as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V636000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Antilock/Traction Control/Electronic Limited Slip](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=Maxima&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** A brake fluid leak onto the circuit board may result in an electrical short, increasing the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Nissan will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the ABS Actuator, replacing it as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V244000: Air Bags:Sensor:Occupant Classification](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=Maxima&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** As a result, the passenger frontal air bag may be turned off and not deploy in the event of a crash. If the passenger frontal air bag does not deploy as intended in the event of a crash, the passenger is at an increased risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will reprogram the Air Bag Control Unit (ACU) and OCS Electronic Control Unit (ECU) in Altima, Maxima, Murano, Rogue, and Sentra vehicles, and replace the OCS ECU in LEAF, NV200, NV200 Taxi, Pathfinder, Infiniti Q50, JX35, and QX60. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V807000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Antilock/Traction Control/Electronic Limited Slip](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=Maxima&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** A brake fluid leak on the circuit board may result in an electrical short, increasing the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Nissan will notify owners, and dealers will replace the ABS actuator, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2017 Nissan Maxima is a stylish and capable sedan, but prospective buyers should perform a thorough pre-purchase inspection. Pay special attention to the operation of driver-assistance radar sensors, the condition of rear suspension components for corrosion, and the functionality of interior safety electronics.
