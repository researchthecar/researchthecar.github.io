# 2016 Nissan Maxima Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2016 Nissan Maxima delivers bold styling and a sporty driving dynamic, but used-car buyers should approach with caution due to notable powertrain and electrical complaints. While owners generally praise its performance and interior comfort, reports of transmission failure and sudden engine stalling require thorough pre-purchase inspections. Reviewing maintenance records and checking for prior component replacements can significantly reduce the risk of unexpected repair bills.

---

## Engine

### 3.5L V6

Powered by a 3.5-liter V6 engine producing approximately 300 horsepower, this powertrain offers strong acceleration and a responsive driving experience. While generally robust mechanically, it has experienced occasional owner reports concerning sudden power loss and stalling.

**Engine Stalling and Power Loss**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicles may unexpectedly lose acceleration power while driving or stall completely, often accompanied by warning lights.
- **Solution:** Diagnose electrical or fuel delivery faults, which may require replacing failing components such as the alternator or fuel system parts.

## Transmission

### Xtronic CVT (Continuously Variable Transmission)

Equipped with an Xtronic Continuously Variable Transmission paired with front-wheel drive, the Maxima prioritizes fuel efficiency alongside its sporty character. However, this transmission system represents a primary area of concern for long-term reliability and high-cost repairs.

**CVT Failure and Hesitation**
- **Symptoms:** The transmission may slip, hesitate, cause sudden vehicle slowdowns, or experience complete internal failure requiring costly replacement.
- **Solution:** Inspect transmission fluid condition, check for diagnostic trouble codes, and replace the transmission unit if internal mechanical failure is confirmed.
- **Cost:** $8,000+

## Electrical

### Charging and Electrical System

The electrical architecture supports advanced cabin tech and driver aids, though owners have reported charging system faults and unexpected battery drains.

**Alternator and Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Battery and brake warning lights illuminate on the dashboard, followed by electrical system failure or vehicle stalling.
- **Solution:** Test the charging system output and replace faulty alternators or batteries as needed.

## Exterior

### Sunroof

Exterior body panels and lighting are generally durable, though panoramic glass components warrant careful inspection for stress fractures.

**Sunroof Shattering**
- **Symptoms:** The glass sunroof panel may fracture or explode spontaneously while driving, producing loud noises and showering the cabin with glass fragments.
- **Solution:** Replace the damaged glass assembly and inspect the sunroof frame for alignment issues.

## Safety

### Air Bag

Safety systems generally function as expected, but intermittent airbag warning lights require professional diagnosis to ensure proper occupant protection.

**Air Bag Warning Light**
- **Symptoms:** The supplemental restraint system warning light remains illuminated on the instrument cluster.
- **Solution:** Scan the airbag control module for diagnostic codes and repair faulty sensors, wiring, or modules.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V601000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Antilock/Traction Control/Electronic Limited Slip](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=Maxima&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** A brake fluid leak on the circuit board may result in an electrical short, increasing the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Nissan will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the serial number on the ABS actuator, replacing it as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V911000: Air Bags:Sensor:Occupant Classification](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=Maxima&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** If the front passenger seat occupant is misclassified, the air bag may deploy incorrectly in the event of a crash, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Nissan will notify owners, and dealers will replace the ECU and update the OCS software free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V636000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Antilock/Traction Control/Electronic Limited Slip](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=Maxima&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** A brake fluid leak onto the circuit board may result in an electrical short, increasing the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Nissan will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the ABS Actuator, replacing it as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V193000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Antilock/Traction Control/Electronic Limited Slip](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=Maxima&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** Insufficient brake fluid pressure may lengthen the distance needed to stop the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Nissan will notify owners, and dealers will inspect and replace the ABS actuator, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 15V733000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Foundation Components:Disc:Caliper](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=Maxima&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** The affected vehicles have front brake calipers whose mounting bolts may not be properly tightened. If the bolts were not properly tightened, the caliper may detach and reduce braking ability, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Nissan will notify owners, and dealers will check the brake caliper mounting bolts, tightening them as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 15V486000: Fuel System, Gasoline](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=Maxima&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** In the affected vehicles, due to an improperly installed o-ring that may not have been seated correctly at the time of assembly, fuel may leak during a crash from the area where the fuel sending unit attaches to the fuel tank. If fuel leaks from between the fuel sending unit and the tank, it may increase the risk of a fire in the event of a crash.
- **What to do:** Nissan will notify owners, and dealers will install a retainer ring between the fuel tank and the fuel sending unit. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V244000: Air Bags:Sensor:Occupant Classification](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=Maxima&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** As a result, the passenger frontal air bag may be turned off and not deploy in the event of a crash. If the passenger frontal air bag does not deploy as intended in the event of a crash, the passenger is at an increased risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will reprogram the Air Bag Control Unit (ACU) and OCS Electronic Control Unit (ECU) in Altima, Maxima, Murano, Rogue, and Sentra vehicles, and replace the OCS ECU in LEAF, NV200, NV200 Taxi, Pathfinder, Infiniti Q50, JX35, and QX60. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V807000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Antilock/Traction Control/Electronic Limited Slip](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=Maxima&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** A brake fluid leak on the circuit board may result in an electrical short, increasing the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Nissan will notify owners, and dealers will replace the ABS actuator, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2016 Nissan Maxima offers an engaging drive and plenty of features, but potential buyers must proceed with diligence. Due to documented complaints regarding CVT transmission longevity and electrical charging issues, a comprehensive pre-purchase inspection by a qualified mechanic is strongly advised. Verify that all service intervals have been respected to minimize the risk of expensive component failures.
