# 2016 Nissan Maxima

### Overview
The 2016 Nissan Maxima, often referred to as the '4-Door Sports Car,' aimed to blend performance with luxury. This generation (A35) was in its final years before a redesign. Generally, the Maxima is known for its strong V6 engine and engaging driving dynamics. Reliability for this model year is typically good, with owners reporting satisfaction with its powertrain and overall build quality. However, like any vehicle, specific components can experience issues over time. This analysis focuses on documented, non-wear-and-tear related problems reported by owners and automotive repair databases, providing insights into potential areas of concern and their associated repair costs.

---


## Engine

### 3.5L V6 (VQ35DE)
**Oil Leaks (Valve Covers/Gaskets)**
- **Symptoms:** Visible oil on engine components, burning oil smell, low oil level.
- **Solution:** Replacement of valve cover gaskets or other seals.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600

**Coolant Leaks (Intake Manifold Gaskets)**
- **Symptoms:** Sweet smell from engine bay, coolant visible around intake manifold, overheating.
- **Solution:** Replacement of intake manifold gaskets.
- **Cost:** $400 - $700


## Transmission

### CVT (Continuously Variable Transmission)
**Hesitation/Jerking**
- **Symptoms:** Transmission feels rough, hesitates during acceleration, or jerks unexpectedly.
- **Solution:** Software update, fluid flush, or transmission replacement.
- **Cost:** $1,500 - $4,000

**Overheating**
- **Symptoms:** Transmission warning light, reduced performance, burning smell.
- **Solution:** Transmission fluid cooler flush or replacement, transmission repair/replacement.
- **Cost:** $500 - $3,500

## Electrical

### General Electrical System
**Infotainment System Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Screen freezing, unresponsiveness, Bluetooth connectivity issues.
- **Solution:** Software update, system reset, or component replacement.
- **Cost:** $200 - $800

**Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Car won't start, battery dies prematurely.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis of parasitic draw, replacement of faulty module or battery.
- **Cost:** $150 - $500

### Summary Verdict
The 2016 Nissan Maxima presents a generally reliable profile, particularly with its robust 3.5L V6 engine. The primary areas of concern tend to be the CVT transmission, which can exhibit hesitation or overheating issues, and some electrical gremlins like infotainment glitches or battery drain. Oil and coolant leaks from the engine are also noted but are often repairable with gasket replacements. While not plagued by widespread critical failures, potential buyers should be aware of the CVT's sensitivity and ensure proper maintenance. Overall, with diligent upkeep, the 2016 Maxima offers a good balance of performance and dependability for its class.
