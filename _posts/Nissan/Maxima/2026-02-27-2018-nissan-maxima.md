# 2018 Nissan Maxima Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2018 Nissan Maxima offers a stylish blend of midsize sedan comfort and V6 performance, but used-car buyers should carefully inspect several recurring owner-reported issues. While it delivers solid power and a well-appointed interior, complaints logged with safety authorities highlight potential electronic issues, forward collision sensor sensitivity, and transmission behavior that warrant a thorough test drive before purchasing.

---

## Engine

### 3.5L V6 Engine

Powered by a 3.5-liter V6 engine producing 300 horsepower and 261 lb-ft of torque, providing brisk acceleration for a front-wheel-drive sedan. The engine itself is generally robust, though sporadic owner reports mention cooling or smoke concerns requiring inspection.

## Transmission

### Xtronic Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)

Equipped with an Xtronic Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT) paired exclusively with the front-wheel-drive layout. Buyers should pay close attention to acceleration smoothness during a test drive, as CVT longevity is a frequent point of discussion for this model.

**Transmission Shaking and Hesitation**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle shakes during acceleration, gauges on the dashboard appear to vibrate, and power delivery lacks a smooth flow.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified mechanic inspect the transmission fluid condition and TCM software; persistent mechanical shuddering may require internal transmission repair or replacement.

## Suspension

### Rear Coil Springs and Tires

Suspension and wheel components are generally sturdy, but isolated failures such as broken coil springs or accelerated tire wear have been documented by owners.

**Premature Suspension or Tire Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Rear coil springs snapping prematurely or tires experiencing rapid, uneven tread wear despite proper alignment checks.
- **Solution:** Inspect rear suspension springs and tire tread depth during a pre-purchase inspection and replace worn components promptly.

## Brakes

### Hydraulic Service Brakes

Brake system complaints include rare reports of fluid leakage near the actuator module, making a pre-purchase under-hood inspection essential.

**Brake Fluid Leaks and Smoke**
- **Symptoms:** Smoke observed coming from underneath the hood, or dashboard alerts regarding potential brake fluid pressure issues.
- **Solution:** Inspect the hydraulic lines and ABS actuator area thoroughly for leaks and ensure all relevant safety recalls have been completed by an authorized dealer.

## Exterior

### Panoramic Sunroof

The exterior glass components, particularly the optional panoramic sunroof, have generated owner grievances regarding sudden shattering under thermal stress.

**Spontaneous Glass Shattering**
- **Symptoms:** The panoramic sunroof glass shatters or explodes loudly while driving at highway speeds without prior impact.
- **Solution:** Inspect the sunroof glass for any pre-existing stress fractures and verify whether any prior warranty claims or insurance coverage apply if replacement is needed.

## Safety

### Forward Collision Avoidance

Driver-assistance and forward collision technologies can occasionally experience false triggers or sensor interference, which owners report as unexpected deceleration.

**Unintended Braking and Radar Obstruction Warnings**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle displays 'Front Radar Obstruction Unavailable' messages and can independently apply the brakes without driver input.
- **Solution:** Clean the front bumper radar sensor area, check for proper sensor alignment, and consult a dealer or technician for software updates or sensor recalibration.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V654000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=Maxima&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 111, "Rear Visibility." The lack of an image in the back-up camera display increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 19V807000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Antilock/Traction Control/Electronic Limited Slip](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=Maxima&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** A brake fluid leak on the circuit board may result in an electrical short, increasing the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Nissan will notify owners, and dealers will replace the ABS actuator, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2018 Nissan Maxima remains an appealing choice for buyers seeking a spacious, powerful sedan with sporty styling. However, prospective owners should conduct a rigorous test drive to check for transmission smoothness, verify that all safety recall campaigns have been performed, and carefully inspect the panoramic sunroof and electronic driver aids for proper operation.
