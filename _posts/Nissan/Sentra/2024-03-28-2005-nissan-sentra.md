**2005 Nissan Sentra**

The 2005 Nissan Sentra is a compact sedan that was offered with a choice of two engines: a 1.8-liter 4-cylinder and a 2.5-liter 4-cylinder. Both engines were available with a 5-speed manual transmission or a 4-speed automatic transmission. The Sentra was praised for its comfortable ride, spacious interior, and fuel economy. However, it was criticized for its lack of power and uninspired handling.

**Engine**

**1.8-liter 4-cylinder**

* **Oil Consumption:** Excessive oil consumption due to premature wear of piston rings. Symptoms include low oil levels, blue smoke from the exhaust, and decreased engine performance. Solution: Engine rebuild or replacement. Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+
* **Timing Chain Tensioner Failure:** The timing chain tensioner can fail, causing the timing chain to jump and damage the engine. Symptoms include rattling noises from the engine, loss of power, and engine stalling. Solution: Replace timing chain tensioner and timing chain. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000

**2.5-liter 4-cylinder**

* **No Issues Found**

**Transmission**

**5-speed Manual Transmission**

* **Gear Grinding:** Difficulty shifting gears or grinding noises when shifting. Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears and grinding noises. Solution: Replace damaged gear synchronizers. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600

**4-speed Automatic Transmission**

* **Transmission Slipping:** The transmission may slip or hesitate to shift gears. Symptoms: Difficulty accelerating, erratic shifting, or a burning smell. Solution: Repair or replace transmission. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $3,000
* **Torque Converter Failure:** The torque converter can fail, causing a loss of power and transmission damage. Symptoms: Inability to accelerate or move, whining or grinding noises, and fluid leaks. Solution: Replace torque converter. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,200 - $2,000

**Suspension**

* **Strut Failure:** The struts can fail, causing a rough ride and poor handling. Symptoms: Excessive bouncing, uneven tire wear, and a knocking noise over bumps. Solution: Replace struts. Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800

**Electrical**

* **Alternator Failure:** The alternator can fail, causing the battery to drain and the vehicle to stall. Symptoms: Dimming headlights, flickering electrical components, and a dead battery. Solution: Replace alternator. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600

**Exterior**

* **No Issues Found**

**Interior**

* **Power Window Failure:** The power window motors can fail, causing window operation issues. Symptoms: Inability to operate windows, intermittent window operation, or a burning smell. Solution: Replace window motor. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
* **Dashboard Rattle:** The dashboard may rattle due to loose components. Symptoms: Rattling noises from the dashboard while driving. Solution: Tighten loose components or replace dashboard. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300

**Summary**

The 2005 Nissan Sentra has a range of potential issues, with the most notable being engine oil consumption, timing chain tensioner failure, transmission slipping, and suspension strut failure. The severity of these issues can vary depending on the severity of the problem and the specific vehicle. Costs to fix these issues can range from a few hundred dollars to several thousand dollars, depending on the issue and the specific vehicle. Overall, the Sentra is a reliable vehicle but is not without its potential problems.
