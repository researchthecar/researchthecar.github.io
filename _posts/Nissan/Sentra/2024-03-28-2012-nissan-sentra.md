**Introduction:**

The 2012 Nissan Sentra is a compact sedan powered by a 1.8-liter four-cylinder engine producing 130 horsepower and 128 lb-ft of torque. It is available with a six-speed manual or a continuously variable transmission (CVT). The Sentra is praised for its fuel efficiency and affordability, but criticized for its lackluster performance and cramped interior.

**Engine**

**1.8L Four-Cylinder Engine**

* **Excessive Oil Consumption:** Oil consumption exceeding 1 quart per 1,000 miles. **Solution:** Engine rebuild or replacement. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $3,000 - $6,000+**Recalls:** N/A
* **Timing Chain Failure:** Premature failure of the timing chain, leading to engine damage. **Solution:** Timing chain and components replacement. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $1,500 - $2,500+**Recalls:** N/A
* **Intake Valve Deposits:** Carbon buildup on intake valves, resulting in reduced engine power and efficiency. **Solution:** Valve cleaning. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $200 - $500+**Recalls:** N/A

**Transmission**

**Six-Speed Manual Transmission**

* **Clutch Failure:** Premature failure of the clutch, causing difficulty shifting gears or loss of power. **Solution:** Clutch replacement. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $1,000 - $1,500+**Recalls:** N/A

**CVT**

**Summary:**

The 2012 Nissan Sentra has several known issues, including excessive oil consumption, timing chain failure, transmission problems, suspension failure, and electrical issues. While these issues can be severe and costly, they are not common to all Sentra models. Some issues have been addressed by Nissan recalls, while others require repairs at the owner's expense.
