# 2016 Nissan Sentra Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used Nissan Sentra, understanding its mechanical tendencies is essential for a confident purchase. While the Sentra offers practical daily driving and decent fuel economy, prospective buyers should pay close attention to powertrain and electrical histories. Reviewing common owner feedback helps ensure you spot potential issues before bringing a pre-owned model home.

---

## Engine

### 1.8L 4-Cylinder Engine

The 1.8-liter naturally aspirated 4-cylinder engine produces around 130 horsepower, providing adequate power for daily commuting. While generally dependable mechanically, it can occasionally experience drivability concerns tied to electrical or fuel management systems.

**Engine Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may unexpectedly stall while driving at various speeds or coming to a stop, often accompanied by warning lights.
- **Solution:** Diagnose fault codes, inspect electronic sensors or fuel delivery components, and repair or replace faulty parts as needed.

## Transmission

### Xtronic Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)

Equipped with an Xtronic CVT designed to maximize fuel efficiency, this transmission setup is central to the Sentra driving experience. Buyers should thoroughly evaluate CVT responsiveness and service history during a test drive.

**Transmission Slipping and Shuddering**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may notice unexpected hesitation, severe shuddering during acceleration, or sudden slips out of gear while driving.
- **Solution:** Inspect transmission fluid condition and internal components; severe internal wear often necessitates professional transmission overhaul or complete replacement.

## Brakes

### Brake System and Switch

The hydraulic brake components provide standard stopping power, though ancillary electrical switches require periodic inspection.

**Brake Light Switch Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Brake lights may fail to illuminate properly, or the vehicle may intermittently refuse to start due to a faulty brake pedal switch signal.
- **Solution:** Inspect the brake light switch assembly and replace the unit if electrical continuity fails.

## Electrical

### Electrical and Starting System

Electrical architecture controls convenience features, keyless ignition, and safety sensors, which occasionally present intermittent faults.

**Key Fob Recognition Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Push-to-start models may display warning messages indicating the vehicle cannot detect the intelligent key fob.
- **Solution:** Replace the key fob battery or reprogram the ignition transceiver module as required.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V253000: Air Bags](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=Sentra&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** Due to a manufacturing error within the air bag inflators, the passenger frontal air bag may not properly deploy in the event of a crash. In the event of a crash, if the front passenger air bag doesn't deploy properly, it can increase the risk of injury to passengers.
- **What to do:** Nissan will notify owners, and dealers will replace the front passenger air bag inflator, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V436000: Air Bags:Frontal:Passenger Side:Inflator Module](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=Sentra&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** If the wiring harness disconnects, the passenger air bag may not to deploy during a crash, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Nissan will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the wiring harness connector for proper engagement and, if necessary, replace the passenger air bag module and main body harness, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V485000: Electrical System:Wiring](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=Sentra&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** If the engine room harness loses connection to the ECU, the vehicle may not start or the engine may stall, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Nissan will notify owners, and dealers will replace the terminal pin on the engine room harness, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V244000: Air Bags:Sensor:Occupant Classification](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=Sentra&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** As a result, the passenger frontal air bag may be turned off and not deploy in the event of a crash. If the passenger frontal air bag does not deploy as intended in the event of a crash, the passenger is at an increased risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will reprogram the Air Bag Control Unit (ACU) and OCS Electronic Control Unit (ECU) in Altima, Maxima, Murano, Rogue, and Sentra vehicles, and replace the OCS ECU in LEAF, NV200, NV200 Taxi, Pathfinder, Infiniti Q50, JX35, and QX60. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V242000: Air Bags:Sensor:Occupant Classification](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=Sentra&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** The front passenger seat belt bracket may become deformed if it is used to secure a Child Restraint System (CRS). The deformed seat bracket may cause the Occupant Classification System (OCS) to incorrectly classify the installed CRS, resulting in the front passenger air bag to not be turned off as designed when a CRS is in the seat.
- **What to do:** Nissan will notify owners, and dealers will reinforce the seat belt bracket as well as reprogram the Air Bag Control Unit (ACU) and OCS Electronic Control Unit (ECU), free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V135000: Exterior Lighting:Brake Lights:Switch](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=Sentra&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** The brake light switch may become contaminated, preventing the circuit from closing and the brake lights from illuminating. The brake lights may not illuminate, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the brake light switch and install a protective grommet, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

When evaluating a pre-owned Nissan Sentra, conducting a meticulous test drive and a professional pre-purchase inspection is highly recommended. Pay close attention to transmission engagement and engine smoothness to ensure long-term reliability on the road.
