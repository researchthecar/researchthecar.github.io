# 2019 Nissan Sentra Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2019 Nissan Sentra is a practical compact sedan that offers comfortable seating and user-friendly features, making it a popular choice on the used market. However, prospective buyers should research the vehicle thoroughly, as owner reports highlight recurring concerns with electrical accessories, safety sensor behavior, and continuously variable transmission performance. Keeping a close eye on maintenance records and conducting a thorough pre-purchase inspection can help avoid unexpected repair bills.

---

## Engine

### 1.8L 4-Cylinder Engine (Base/S)

A 1.8-liter naturally aspirated four-cylinder engine producing around 130 horsepower, designed primarily for fuel efficiency in daily commuting.

### 1.6L Turbocharged 4-Cylinder Engine (SR Turbo/NISMO)

A turbocharged 1.6-liter four-cylinder engine pushing roughly 188 horsepower, offering a more spirited driving experience for sportier trims.

## Transmission

### Xtronic Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)

The automatic Xtronic CVT routes power to the front wheels across all standard trims, prioritizing fuel economy but drawing notable owner complaints regarding long-term durability and slip issues.

**Transmission Slipping and Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Owners report unexpected transmission slipping, hesitation during acceleration, loss of power, and sudden illumination of warning lights.
- **Solution:** Requires professional diagnosis, which may involve fluid service, valve body replacement, or a complete transmission unit replacement.

### 6-Speed Manual Transmission

A traditional 6-speed manual gearbox available on select base trims for drivers who prefer shifting their own gears, generally avoiding CVT-related complaints.

## Brakes

### Brake Pedal Switch

Brake system feedback emphasizes switch-related failures that can prevent the push-to-start ignition from engaging properly.

**Brake Light Switch Failure**
- **Symptoms:** A stiff push-to-start pedal, failure of brake lights to illuminate when pressed, and intermittent vehicle starting issues.
- **Solution:** Replace the faulty brake pedal position switch.

## Electrical

### Battery and Charging System

Electrical system complaints frequently center around parasitic drains that deplete the battery and occasional door lock actuator failures.

**Parasitic Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle's battery repeatedly dies overnight or after sitting for short periods, often linked to infotainment systems or electrical components staying active.
- **Solution:** Diagnose parasitic draws, update software modules, or replace faulty switches and infotainment components.

## Safety

### Forward Collision Avoidance

Driver assistance features enhance safety but can occasionally trigger false collision warnings or phantom braking events.

**Unexpected Automatic Braking**
- **Symptoms:** The automatic emergency braking system engages unexpectedly or triggers loud warning chimes without any obstruction present on the road.
- **Solution:** Inspect and recalibrate radar and camera sensors, or check for applicable software updates at a dealership.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V135000: Exterior Lighting:Brake Lights:Switch](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=Sentra&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** The brake light switch may become contaminated, preventing the circuit from closing and the brake lights from illuminating. The brake lights may not illuminate, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the brake light switch and install a protective grommet, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V654000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=Sentra&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 111, "Rear Visibility." The lack of an image in the back-up camera display increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2019 Nissan Sentra remains an affordable and comfortable commuter car, but potential buyers should approach with eyes open regarding mechanical reliability. Pay close attention to how the CVT behaves during a test drive, and verify that all electrical accessories and safety sensors operate correctly. Securing a comprehensive pre-purchase inspection can provide peace of mind before finalizing a deal.
