# 2017 Nissan Sentra Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used Nissan Sentra, prospective buyers will find a practical and fuel-efficient commuter sedan that offers comfortable seating and user-friendly technology. However, owners and mechanics have frequently reported durability concerns, particularly regarding the continuously variable transmission (CVT) and associated electrical or starting glitches. Reviewing vehicle service records and conducting a thorough pre-purchase inspection can help uncover hidden wear before finalizing a purchase.

---

## Engine

### 1.8L 4-Cylinder Engine

The standard 1.8-liter naturally aspirated 4-cylinder engine produces around 130 horsepower, providing adequate motivation for daily commuting. While generally dependable mechanically, occasional issues with engine electrical warnings and unexpected stalling have been reported by owners.

**Loss of Motive Power**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle suddenly loses acceleration power while driving at highway speeds or under acceleration, accompanied by check engine lights.
- **Solution:** Diagnose fault codes and inspect throttle body, fuel system, or exhaust components; replace failing sensors or assemblies as needed.

**Engine Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** The engine unexpectedly shuts down while idling or slowing down in traffic, making it difficult to maintain vehicle momentum.
- **Solution:** Inspect ignition and fuel delivery systems, check for vacuum leaks, and update or replace faulty engine control modules.

### 1.6L Turbocharged 4-Cylinder Engine

Available on sportier trims like the SR Turbo and NISMO, the 1.6-liter turbocharged engine increases output to 188 horsepower for a more spirited drive. It requires attentive maintenance regarding turbo cooling and oil changes.

## Transmission

### Xtronic CVT (Continuously Variable Transmission)

Paired with front-wheel drive across most trims, the Xtronic continuously variable transmission maximizes fuel economy but is known for long-term durability issues. Buyers should meticulously verify transmission service history and test drive carefully for hesitation or shuddering.

**Transmission Slipping and Shudder**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle hesitates, slips, jerks, or shudders during acceleration, accompanied by a whining noise or a loss of power transfer.
- **Solution:** Perform transmission fluid analysis and pressure tests; severely worn units typically require complete CVT replacement.
- **Cost:** $4,500 to $7,000 for replacement

### 6-Speed Manual Transmission

Offered strictly on select base trims, the 6-speed manual transmission provides a more engaging driving experience and avoids the durability concerns associated with the CVT.

## Brakes

### Brake Pedal and Stop Lamp Switch

The hydraulic brake system offers reliable stopping power, though switch failures can occasionally disrupt starting sequences and traction control indicators.

**Stiff Pedal and Starting Interlock Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The brake pedal feels stiff upon vehicle entry, and a warning message prompts the driver to depress the brake to start, preventing the ignition from cranking.
- **Solution:** Inspect and replace the stop lamp switch or faulty brake pedal sensors.

## Steering

### Electric Power Steering

The electric power steering system delivers light and responsive handling at low speeds, but intermittent warning lights and rare steering stiffness require prompt diagnosis.

**Power Steering Warning and Seizing**
- **Symptoms:** A 'PS' warning light illuminates on the dashboard, accompanied by stiff steering input or temporary loss of power assist.
- **Solution:** Scan power steering control modules for diagnostic trouble codes and inspect electric motor wiring or steering rack assemblies.

## Electrical

### Ignition and Exterior Lighting

Electrical accessories and exterior lighting systems generally function well, though intermittent ignition switch glitches and headlight failures have been documented by owners.

**Intermittent Ignition Switch Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle fails to crank, dashboard lights appear dim, and digital readouts remain dark before eventually starting normally after multiple attempts.
- **Solution:** Test battery health, starter connections, and ignition switch wiring; replace faulty electrical components.

## Safety

### Seat Belts

Standard safety equipment includes multiple airbags and reinforced cabin structures, though physical wear items like seat belt webbing should be inspected closely during evaluation.

**Seat Belt Fraying**
- **Symptoms:** The fabric on the driver-side seat belt frays or splits prematurely along the edges, compromising structural integrity.
- **Solution:** Replace the damaged seat belt assembly.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V551000: Electrical System:Ignition](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=Sentra&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** In these models, over time, the weight on the key ring and road conditions or some other jarring event may cause the ignition switch to move out of the run position to the accessory position, turning off the engine. If the vehicle stalls, it can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Nissan will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the production information on the ignition switch, and replace it as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V253000: Air Bags](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=Sentra&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** Due to a manufacturing error within the air bag inflators, the passenger frontal air bag may not properly deploy in the event of a crash. In the event of a crash, if the front passenger air bag doesn't deploy properly, it can increase the risk of injury to passengers.
- **What to do:** Nissan will notify owners, and dealers will replace the front passenger air bag inflator, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V135000: Exterior Lighting:Brake Lights:Switch](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=Sentra&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** The brake light switch may become contaminated, preventing the circuit from closing and the brake lights from illuminating. The brake lights may not illuminate, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the brake light switch and install a protective grommet, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The Nissan Sentra remains an appealing choice for budget-conscious used car shoppers seeking affordable daily transportation and solid fuel economy. However, buyers must approach models equipped with the continuously variable transmission with caution, ensuring a thorough pre-purchase inspection and verifying past maintenance records. Prioritizing well-maintained examples or opting for a manual transmission model can significantly mitigate ownership risks.
