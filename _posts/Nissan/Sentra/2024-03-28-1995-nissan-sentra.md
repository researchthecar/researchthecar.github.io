**Introduction:**

The 1995 Nissan Sentra is a compact sedan powered by a 1.6-liter, four-cylinder engine that produces 115 horsepower and 101 lb-ft of torque. It comes standard with a five-speed manual transmission, while a four-speed automatic is optional. The Sentra is known for its reliability, fuel efficiency, and roomy interior. However, it has received some complaints regarding specific issues.

**Engine**

- **1.6-liter, Four-Cylinder Engine**
  - **Oil leakage from camshaft seal:** Oil may leak from the camshaft seal, causing an oil leak and potential engine damage. Replacement of the camshaft seal is necessary. **Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $300+**
  - **Timing belt failure:** The timing belt may fail prematurely, leading to engine damage. Timing belt replacement is required every 60,000 miles. **Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $700+**
- **Recalls:**
  - **NHTSA Campaign Number 01V058000:** Fuel pump fuse may blow, stalling the engine.

**Transmission**

- **Five-Speed Manual Transmission:**
  - **Difficult shifting:** Difficulty shifting gears may occur due to worn or damaged shift linkage or synchronizers. Replacement or repair of the affected components is necessary. **Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+**
- **Four-Speed Automatic Transmission:**
  - **Transmission slipping:** The transmission may slip during gear changes, indicating worn or damaged clutch packs or torque converter. Transmission rebuild or replacement is often required. **Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+**

**Suspension**

- **No Issues Found**

**Electrical**

- **Starter failure:** The starter motor may fail to engage the engine, preventing the vehicle from starting. Replacement of the starter is required. **Estimated Cost to Fix: $250 - $400+**
- **Alternator failure:** The alternator may fail to charge the battery, draining the electrical system. Alternator replacement is necessary. **Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+**

**Exterior**

- **Rust on undercarriage:** The undercarriage may develop rust over time, potentially affecting the structural integrity of the vehicle. Rustproofing and repair may be necessary. **Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the extent of rust damage**

**Interior**

- **Seatbelt buckle failure:** The seatbelt buckle may fail to engage properly, posing a safety hazard. Replacement of the seatbelt buckle is required. **Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+**
- **Dashboard cracking:** The dashboard may crack prematurely, potentially obscuring gauges and controls. Dashboard replacement may be necessary. **Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+**

**Summary:**

The 1995 Nissan Sentra is a reliable and fuel-efficient vehicle, but it has some potential issues to consider. While some issues are minor and inexpensive to fix, others can be more severe and costly. Overall, it is advisable to have the vehicle regularly inspected and maintained to prevent or address any issues promptly.
