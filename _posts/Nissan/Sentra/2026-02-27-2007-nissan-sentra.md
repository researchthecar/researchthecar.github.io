# 2007 Nissan Sentra

### Overview
The 2007 Nissan Sentra, a compact sedan, offered a range of engine and transmission choices catering to different driving needs. While generally considered a reliable vehicle for its class, certain model years and specific configurations have reported common issues. This analysis focuses on documented technical reliability concerns beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential problem areas for owners and prospective buyers. Understanding these specific points can help in proactive maintenance and informed purchasing decisions.

## Engine

### 2.0L QR20DE (4-cylinder)
**Oil Consumption**
- **Symptoms:** Excessive oil burning, potentially leading to low oil levels and engine damage if not monitored.
- **Solution:** Engine rebuild or replacement, piston ring replacement.
- **Cost:** $1,500 - $3,000

**Camshaft Position Sensor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Check Engine light illumination, rough idling, stalling, difficulty starting.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the camshaft position sensor.
- **Cost:** $150 - $300

### Summary Verdict
The 2007 Nissan Sentra is generally a dependable compact car, particularly with the manual transmission and the 1.8L or 2.0L MR20DE engines. The primary concerns revolve around potential oil consumption in the QR20DE engine and issues with the CVT transmission, which can be costly to repair. Electrical components like power window motors and key fobs can also experience failures. Owners should be mindful of regular maintenance, especially for the CVT fluid, and monitor oil levels on QR20DE equipped models. Overall, with proper care, the Sentra can offer reliable service.
