**Introduction:**

The 1990 Nissan Sentra is a compact sedan known for its reliability and fuel efficiency. It is available with a 1.6-liter inline-four engine paired with either a 5-speed manual or 4-speed automatic transmission. The Sentra is praised for its spacious interior, comfortable ride, and responsive handling. However, it also has some common issues that owners should be aware of.

**Engine**

**1.6L Inline-Four**

**Stuck Idle Air Control Valve (IACV)**
Description/Symptoms: The IACV controls the idle speed of the engine. When it gets stuck, it can cause the engine to idle too high or too low, resulting in stalling.
Solution: Replace the IACV.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $250+
Recalls: N/A

**Transmission**

**5-Speed Manual**

**Grinding When Shifting**
Description/Symptoms: The transmission may grind when shifting gears, especially when cold.
Solution: Check the transmission fluid level and change it if necessary. If the issue persists, the synchronizers or other internal components may need to be replaced.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $250 - $750+
Recalls: N/A

**4-Speed Automatic**

**Slipping Transmission**
Description/Symptoms: The transmission may slip or hesitate when shifting gears, causing a loss of power.
Solution: Check the transmission fluid level and change it if necessary. If the issue persists, the transmission may need to be rebuilt or replaced.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $3,500+
Recalls: N/A

**Suspension**

**Strut Mount Wear**
Description/Symptoms: The strut mount connects the strut to the body of the vehicle. When it wears out, it can cause a clunking noise when driving over bumps or rough roads.
Solution: Replace the strut mounts.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $350+
Recalls: N/A

**Electrical**

**Alternator Failure**
Description/Symptoms: The alternator charges the battery and provides power to the vehicle's electrical systems. When it fails, it can cause the battery to drain and the vehicle to stall.
Solution: Replace the alternator.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $350 - $600+
Recalls: N/A

**Exterior**

**Rust on Exhaust System**
Description/Symptoms: The exhaust system on the Sentra is susceptible to rust, especially in areas with harsh winters.
Solution: Inspect the exhaust system for rust and replace any rusted components as necessary.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $500+
Recalls: N/A

**Interior**

**Climate Control Issues**
Description/Symptoms: The climate control system may malfunction, causing the air conditioning or heating to not work properly.
Solution: Diagnose and repair the issue with the specific climate control component that is faulty.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $450+
Recalls: N/A

**Summary:**

Overall, the 1990 Nissan Sentra is a reliable vehicle with a few common issues. The severity and cost of these issues vary depending on the specific problem. While some issues are relatively minor and inexpensive to fix, such as the strut mount wear, others can be more serious and costly, such as the transmission slipping. It is important for owners to be aware of these potential issues and to have their vehicles regularly inspected and maintained to prevent them from becoming major problems.
