# 2024 Nissan Sentra Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2024 Nissan Sentra is a stylish and efficient compact sedan that appeals to daily commuters and budget-conscious buyers alike. While it offers a comfortable ride and modern technology features, prospective buyers should review available owner reports concerning powertrain responsiveness and front suspension components. Being aware of these areas can help you conduct a thorough pre-purchase inspection.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L 4-Cylinder Engine

Equipped with a 2.0-liter naturally aspirated four-cylinder engine producing around 149 horsepower and 146 lb-ft of torque. This engine prioritizes fuel economy for daily commuting and generally operates reliably when properly maintained.

## Transmission

### Xtronic Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)

Paired with an Xtronic Continuously Variable Transmission driving the front wheels across all trims. Buyers should closely evaluate transmission responsiveness and service history during a test drive.

**CVT Malfunction and Power Hesitation**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have reported dashboard warnings such as CVT malfunction lights accompanied by hesitation, jerking, or grinding noises during acceleration or shifting.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified technician perform a full diagnostic scan of the transmission control module and inspect the CVT fluid condition.

## Suspension

### Front Axles and Suspension Joints

The front suspension handles daily driving duties well, but recurring reports of axle noise and joint wear warrant a careful professional inspection.

**Axle Clicking and Component Separation**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have experienced loud clicking or popping noises from the front end when turning, and in rare instances, front suspension or axle detachment.
- **Solution:** Inspect front axle shafts, ball joints, control arms, and sway bar links for premature wear, play, or improper securing.

## Brakes

### Brake Pedal Switch and Rear Brakes

The braking system provides adequate stopping power, though switch-related glitches can occasionally interfere with throttle response.

**Acceleration Delay and Brake Switch Faults**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle may fail to accelerate initially after coming to a complete stop due to a faulty brake pedal position switch.
- **Solution:** Replace the malfunctioning brake pedal switch and verify proper brake light and cruise control operation.

## Electrical

### Infotainment and Lighting

electrical issues can occasionally affect cabin accessories and exterior lighting, requiring thorough testing of all onboard electronics.

**Bluetooth Disconnections and Headlight Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have noted intermittent Apple CarPlay or Bluetooth connectivity drops, alongside sudden exterior headlight darkening while driving.
- **Solution:** Check software updates for the infotainment system and inspect headlight wiring harnesses, switches, and fuses.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V154000: Air Bags:Frontal:Driver Side:Inflator Module](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=Sentra&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** Due to a manufacturing issue, the driver's air bag inflator may absorb moisture, and cause the air bag to deploy improperly. An air bag that deploys improperly may not protect the driver as intended during a crash, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the front driver's air bag assembly, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V304000: Power Train:Driveline:Driveshaft](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=Sentra&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** The front left-side driveshaft may not be fully seated in the continuously variable transmission (CVT) assembly, or may have a loose or missing retaining clip, which can cause a transmission fluid leak and loss of drive power. An unexpected loss of drive power can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the front left driveshaft, and if necessary, replace the driveshaft and CVT assembly, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V498000: Visibility:Windshield](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=Sentra&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 205, "Glazing Materials." An owner who cannot see their VIN clearly may not recognize their vehicle is involved in a safety recall, and may not have the recall remedy performed, which could increase the risk of injury, fire, or a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the windshield as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2024 Nissan Sentra presents a mixed reliability picture based on early owner feedback. While its fuel-efficient engine makes it an attractive daily driver, potential buyers should pay close attention to CVT operation, front axle noises, and electrical accessories during a pre-purchase inspection. Checking service records and ensuring all software updates are applied will help secure a dependable ownership experience.
