## 2021 Nissan Sentra

The 2021 Nissan Sentra is a compact sedan that offers a comfortable ride, good fuel economy, and a spacious interior. It is powered by a 2.0-liter four-cylinder engine that produces 149 horsepower and 146 lb-ft of torque, paired with a continuously variable transmission (CVT). Other engine and transmission options are not available for this model year.

People who like the 2021 Nissan Sentra appreciate its affordability, fuel efficiency, and comfortable seats. Common complaints include a lack of power from the engine, a noisy interior, and a lack of advanced safety features.

### Engine

**2.0L Four-Cylinder Engine**

- **Excessive Oil Consumption**
  - Description/Symptoms: The engine may consume excessive amounts of oil, leading to low oil levels and potential engine damage.
  - Solution: Replace piston rings and valve stem seals.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,000+
  - Recalls: None Found

### Transmission

**Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)**

- **Hesitation and Jerking**
  - Description/Symptoms: The transmission may hesitate or jerk while shifting gears, causing an uncomfortable ride.
  - Solution: Update the transmission control module software.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
  - Recalls: None Found

### Suspension

- **No Issues Found**

### Electrical

- **Battery Drain**
  - Description/Symptoms: The battery may drain prematurely, causing difficulty starting the vehicle.
  - Solution: Replace the battery or alternator.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
  - Recalls: None Found

### Exterior

- **Door Handle Issue**
  - Description/Symptoms: The exterior door handles may stick or become difficult to operate.
  - Solution: Replace the door handles.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $300+
  - Recalls: None Found

### Interior

- **Air Conditioner Malfunction**
  - Description/Symptoms: The air conditioner may not blow cold air, leading to discomfort in warm weather.
  - Solution: Replace the compressor or refrigerant lines.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
  - Recalls: None Found

### Summary

Overall, the 2021 Nissan Sentra has a few minor issues that can be costly to fix. The most common problems are related to the engine, transmission, and electrical systems. While these issues can be inconvenient and expensive, they are not widespread and do not affect the overall reliability of the vehicle.
