The 2003 Nissan Sentra is a compact car known for its fuel efficiency and reliability, though it's not without its issues. Engine options included the 1.8L inline-4 (QG18DE) producing around 126 horsepower and 129 lb-ft of torque. Transmission options were a 5-speed manual and a 4-speed automatic. Owners often praise the Sentra for its affordability and simple mechanics. Common complaints include issues related to age, such as rust, and wear-related component failures.

**Engine**

**1.8L Inline-4 (QG18DE)**

*   **Failed Catalytic Converter**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Check engine light illuminates, often with codes P0420 (Catalyst System Efficiency Below Threshold). The vehicle may experience reduced fuel economy and performance.
    *   Solution: Replace the catalytic converter.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Excessive Oil Consumption**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The engine requires frequent oil top-offs. Blue smoke from the exhaust pipe may be observed. Can lead to engine damage if not monitored.
    *   Solution: Possible solutions include replacing valve stem seals, piston rings, or, in severe cases, engine replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $3000+ (depending on the severity and repair chosen)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Crankshaft Position Sensor Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The vehicle may stall intermittently, have difficulty starting, or run rough. Check engine light often comes on with code P0335.
    *   Solution: Replace the crankshaft position sensor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**4-Speed Automatic Transmission**

*   **Transmission Slipping/Erratic Shifting**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The transmission may slip between gears, shift harshly, or fail to shift at all.
    *   Solution: In some cases, a fluid flush and filter change may resolve the issue. However, often requires transmission repair or replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 (fluid change) - $2500+ (replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Worn Struts and Shocks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Bouncy ride, excessive body roll during cornering, and longer stopping distances. Leaking fluid from the struts/shocks.
    *   Solution: Replace the worn struts and shocks.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $1000+ (depending on parts and labor)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Worn Ball Joints and Tie Rod Ends**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Clunking noises from the front end, loose steering, uneven tire wear.
    *   Solution: Replace the worn ball joints and/or tie rod ends.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600+ (per side)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Failed Window Motor/Regulator**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Window will not roll up or down, or it moves very slowly.
    *   Solution: Replace the window motor and/or regulator.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $400+ (per window)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Alternator Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Dimming headlights, battery warning light on, difficulty starting, stalling.
    *   Solution: Replace the alternator.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Rusting**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rust on the body panels, especially around the wheel wells, rocker panels, and undercarriage.
    *   Solution: Rust repair, which can range from patching small areas to replacing entire body panels.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $2000+ (depending on the extent of the rust)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Dashboard Cracking**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Cracks appearing on the dashboard surface due to age and sun exposure.
    *   Solution: Dashboard replacement or applying a dashboard cover.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 (cover) - $1000+ (replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Faulty Door Lock Actuator**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The door lock will not automatically lock or unlock with the key fob or door switch.
    *   Solution: Replace the door lock actuator.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100-$300+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2003 Nissan Sentra, while generally reliable, can suffer from issues related to its age. Engine problems like catalytic converter failure and oil consumption are relatively common and can be costly. Transmission issues can also arise. Suspension components wear out and require replacement, and electrical issues like window motor failure can occur. Rust is a significant concern, especially in regions with harsh winters. Overall, the severity of these issues varies, with potential repair costs ranging from a few hundred dollars to several thousand, depending on the problem.
