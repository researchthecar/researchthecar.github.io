# 2022 Nissan Sentra Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2022 Nissan Sentra is a stylish and fuel-efficient compact sedan that serves as an appealing option for daily commuters and budget-conscious buyers. While generally comfortable and modern, prospective owners should review owner-reported complaints regarding certain electrical glitches, steering concerns, and suspension components. A thorough pre-purchase inspection can help verify that all systems are operating correctly.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L 4-Cylinder Engine

Powered by a 2.0-liter naturally aspirated 4-cylinder engine producing approximately 149 horsepower and 146 lb-ft of torque, providing adequate power for daily commuting. This engine option is generally straightforward and conventional, though owners have occasionally reported ancillary electrical and starting issues.

## Transmission

### Xtronic Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)

Equipped with an Xtronic Continuously Variable Transmission paired with front-wheel drive, designed to maximize fuel economy during highway and city driving. Buyers should test drive the vehicle to ensure smooth acceleration and prompt responsiveness without unusual noises.

## Suspension

### Suspension and Tie Rods

Suspension feedback includes isolated reports of worn tie rods, suspension noises, and wheel bearing wear.

**Popping Noises and Alignment Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Unusual popping noises when turning the steering wheel and frequent out-of-alignment conditions.
- **Solution:** Inspect tie rods, struts, and wheel bearings for premature wear and replace components as necessary.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

Brake system feedback is generally minimal, though occasional parking brake and ABS warning light concerns have been noted.

**Parking Brake Engagement Difficulty**
- **Symptoms:** The parking brake fails to engage properly on the first attempt, leading to high RPM revving as the system struggles to recognize vehicle status.
- **Solution:** Inspect parking brake actuators, switches, and brake system control modules.

## Steering

### Steering Assembly

Steering component feedback highlights minor instances of wheel vibration and stiffness during transit.

**Steering Play and Drift**
- **Symptoms:** The steering wheel may feel loose, exhibit unwanted play, or drift while driving at steady highway speeds.
- **Solution:** Inspect steering linkages, tie rods, and electronic power steering calibration.

## Electrical

### Electrical System & Battery

Electrical complaints center around occasional battery drain, infotainment glitches, and random warning light illumination.

**Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle battery drains unexpectedly overnight, leading to a failure to start and warning messages regarding the electronic key fob.
- **Solution:** Inspect the electrical system for parasitic draws, check door switches, and replace the battery if it fails load testing.

**Dashboard Warning Lights and Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** Random illumination of electrical and engine warning lights accompanied by rough idling, shaking, or engine stalling while driving.
- **Solution:** Scan the powertrain control module for diagnostic trouble codes and inspect wiring harnesses and sensors.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V421000: Structure:Body](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=Sentra&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** Corrosion of the electrical components could result in failure of various electrical systems, such as loss of wiper motor function or exterior lighting, inoperative air bags, or the ability to shift into gear without applying the brake pedal. Any of these scenarios can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and, if necessary, reseal the driver's side cowl. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V581000: Steering:Linkages:Tie Rod Assembly](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=Sentra&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** A bent tie rod can break and cause a loss of steering control, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** As an interim repair, dealers will inspect and replace any bent or broken tie rods, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2022 Nissan Sentra is a practical daily driver with comfortable seating and solid fuel economy, but used-car buyers should perform a detailed inspection. Pay close attention to the electrical systems, battery condition, steering feel, and suspension components during your test drive to ensure everything is in proper working order.
