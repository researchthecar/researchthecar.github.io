# 2014 Nissan Note

### Overview
The 2014 Nissan Note is a subcompact hatchback known for its practicality and fuel efficiency. As a model in its first generation, it generally offers a solid ownership experience with a focus on urban driving. Reliability is a key selling point for many Nissan vehicles, and the Note typically follows this trend. Owners often praise its spacious interior for its size and its user-friendly features. While no car is entirely without potential issues, the 2014 Note has demonstrated a good track record for its age, with most reported problems being minor or related to specific components rather than widespread design flaws. Regular maintenance is crucial for ensuring its continued dependability.

### CVT (Continuously Variable Transmission)
**Hesitation or juddering**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle may hesitate during acceleration or exhibit slight juddering, particularly at lower speeds. Can sometimes be linked to fluid degradation or sensor issues.
- **Solution:** Transmission fluid change and filter replacement. In more severe cases, recalibration or internal component repair may be necessary.
- **Cost:** $150 - $500 (fluid change), $800 - $2500 (repair/replacement)

### Cabin Components
**Minor trim rattles**

### Summary Verdict
The 2014 Nissan Note generally presents itself as a reliable and practical small car. The petrol and diesel engines are robust, and the manual transmission is largely trouble-free. The CVT, while efficient, has a few reported instances of hesitation, which usually require fluid maintenance or more involved repairs. Electrical systems are mostly sound, with only minor sensor issues occasionally surfacing. Interior components are generally durable, though minor rattles can occur. Overall, the 2014 Note is a dependable choice for budget-conscious buyers, provided it has been maintained adequately, particularly the CVT fluid.
