# 2019 Nissan Rogue Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2019 Nissan Rogue is a popular compact crossover praised for its comfortable ride, spacious cabin, and generous suite of standard active safety features. It represents an appealing choice for families and daily commuters seeking utility and ease of use. However, used-car buyers should be aware of several recurring issues reported by owners. These concerns primarily center on the continuously variable transmission, door lock actuators, and some of the advanced driver-assist systems. While these owner reports highlight areas of concern, they do not mean every Rogue will experience these problems, and many run reliably with proper care.

---

## Engine

### 2.5L 4-Cylinder (QR25DE)

The standard 2.5-liter naturally aspirated four-cylinder engine produces approximately 170 horsepower and 175 lb-ft of torque. It offers adequate daily performance and decent efficiency, though it is often considered noisy under hard acceleration. While the engine block itself is mechanically robust, buyers should look out for accessory and electronic sensor issues.

**Mass Air Flow (MAF) Sensor Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Engine hesitation, a noticeable judder, loss of acceleration power, a check engine light displaying code P0101, or sudden engine stalling.
- **Solution:** Replacing the mass air flow sensor and clearing the engine trouble codes.
- **Cost:** $808

**Fuel Temperature Sensor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Faulty sensor readings and diagnostic trouble codes related to the fuel system, which may require replacing the entire fuel pump assembly.
- **Solution:** Replacing the fuel pump assembly to address the integrated faulty sensor.

**Intermittent Engine Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** The engine unexpectedly stalls or shuts down while driving, sometimes accompanied by a malfunction light, followed by starting difficulties.
- **Solution:** Diagnostic scanning, electrical harness inspections, and engine software updates or component replacements as indicated.

### 2.0L 4-Cylinder Hybrid (MR20DD)

The available 2.0-liter hybrid powertrain pairs a gas engine with a compact electric motor for a combined output of 176 horsepower. It delivers improved fuel economy, particularly in city driving, but is much scarcer on the used market. Buyers should verify hybrid battery health and cooling system integrity before purchasing.

## Transmission

### Xtronic CVT

This continuously variable transmission is paired with both front-wheel drive and all-wheel drive configurations. It is designed to optimize fuel efficiency but has historically drawn owner scrutiny regarding long-term durability. Steady maintenance and regular fluid changes are highly recommended to prolong its lifespan.

**CVT Hesitation, Slipping, and Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle jerking (juddering), noticeable hesitation during acceleration, transmission overheating, lack of throttle response, gear slipping, or complete loss of power on the highway.
- **Solution:** Replacing the CVT assembly and updating the transmission control module software.
- **Cost:** $6,500

## Suspension

### Suspension Struts and Bushings

**Strut Bushing and Bearing Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Popping, clicking, or clanking noises from the front suspension when turning the steering wheel or driving over bumps.
- **Solution:** Replacing the upper strut bushings and bearings, followed by a wheel alignment.
- **Cost:** $1,100

## Brakes

### Brake System and Rotors

**Erroneous Automatic Emergency Braking (AEB) Activation**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle abruptly brakes or slows down unexpectedly on highways, roads, or intersections when no obstacles are present.
- **Solution:** Software update (such as TSB NTB19-033G) or front radar sensor recalibration/replacement.

**Rear Brake Grinding Noise**
- **Symptoms:** An intermittent grinding or rubbing noise coming from the rear brakes, occurring while braking or occasionally when rolling.
- **Solution:** Inspecting, cleaning, or replacing worn rear brake pads, rotors, and calipers.

## Steering

### Steering Column and Boot

**Squeaking or Whining Noise When Turning**
- **Symptoms:** An abnormal squeaking, whining, or grinding noise coming from the steering wheel or steering column during turns.
- **Solution:** Greasing the steering column boot or replacing worn steering gear components.

**Steering Stiffening or Temporary Lockup**
- **Symptoms:** The steering wheel stiffens or briefly locks up while the vehicle is in motion, occasionally accompanied by vehicle shut-off.
- **Solution:** Diagnostic scanning, checking electrical power steering connections, and servicing the steering column/boot.

## Exterior

### Door Locks and Fob System

**Door Lock Actuator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** One or more doors fail to lock or unlock when using the key fob, interior power lock switch, or exterior buttons.
- **Solution:** Replacing the malfunctioning door lock actuator.

**Defective Folding Key Fob**
- **Symptoms:** The key fob mechanism breaks, preventing the key from staying in its folded/closed position, or causing vehicle start issues.
- **Solution:** Servicing under NHTSA Campaign 23V093000, which involves a dealer-supplied insert or key fob replacement.

### Panoramic Sunroof and Power Tailgate

**Panoramic Sunroof Spontaneous Shattering**
- **Symptoms:** A sudden, extremely loud popping or banging sound followed by the panoramic sunroof glass shattering without any external impact.
- **Solution:** Replacing the panoramic sunroof glass assembly.

**Power Tailgate Closing Unexpectedly**
- **Symptoms:** The tailgate closes unexpectedly on its own after being opened, sometimes accompanied by warning beeps.
- **Solution:** Inspecting and replacing faulty lift-gate struts, latches, or pinch sensors.

## Interior

### Cabin Seating and Heating

**Loose Driver's Seat Track**
- **Symptoms:** The driver's seat physically slides or shifts back and forth slightly (about half an inch) during acceleration, braking, or turning.
- **Solution:** Replacing the driver's seat track assembly.
- **Cost:** $1,730

**Front Passenger Seat Heater Overheating**
- **Symptoms:** The seat heater on the front passenger seat overheats, producing smoke or burning a hole through the seat upholstery.
- **Solution:** Replacing the malfunctioning seat heating element and repairing the damaged seat upholstery.

## Safety

### Advanced Driver Assistance Systems (ADAS)

**Unexpected Forward Collision Warning and Braking**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle's collision warning lights flash and the automatic emergency braking system abruptly deploys without any forward obstacles.
- **Solution:** Replacing the front radar sensor or updating system software as per dealer guidance.
- **Cost:** $2,300

**Backup Camera Freeze or Blank Screen**
- **Symptoms:** The infotainment screen shows a frozen or entirely blank image when shifting the vehicle into reverse.
- **Solution:** Updating infotainment software, replacing the backup camera module, or completing relevant recall service.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V654000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=Rogue&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 111, "Rear Visibility." The lack of an image in the back-up camera display increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 21V839000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Engine:Engine Control Module (Ecu/Ecm)](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=Rogue&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** A blown fuse may cause an engine stall, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will install a harness protector cover, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V093000: Electrical System:Ignition:Anti-Theft:Immobilizer/Proximity:Key/Sender](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=Rogue&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** Driver contact with a key in the collapsed position could inadvertently shut off the vehicle while driving, increasing the risk of a crash. Additionally, if the vehicle shuts off while driving, the air bags may not deploy in a crash as intended, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will insert a spacer into the key slot of the jackknife key, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2019 Nissan Rogue remains a highly functional and practical compact SUV, making it a solid choice for used-car buyers who prioritize cargo space, passenger comfort, and safety features. However, its long-term appeal depends heavily on vehicle history and meticulous maintenance. Potential buyers should prioritize examples that show consistent transmission fluid services and have undergone a thorough pre-purchase inspection. During a test drive, be sure to pay close attention to any hesitation, jerking, or unusual noises from the transmission, and test the functionality of the power locks, driver's seat stability, and backup camera. Choosing a well-maintained, single-owner vehicle with a clear service history can help avoid the most common headaches associated with this model.
