# 2016 Nissan Rogue Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used compact SUV, the 2016 Nissan Rogue is a common sight on the pre-owned market, offering comfortable seating, competitive cargo flexibility, and attractive fuel economy. However, prospective buyers should approach this model year with eyes wide open regarding its mechanical history. While it provides an agreeable daily driving experience, reports from owners highlight several persistent trouble spots that require careful inspection before purchase.

---

## Engine

### 2.5L 4-Cylinder (QR25DE)

The 2016 Nissan Rogue is powered by a 2.5-liter naturally aspirated 4-cylinder engine producing 170 horsepower and 175 lb-ft of torque. Paired exclusively with front-wheel or all-wheel drive, this engine provides adequate power for daily commuting, though owners have reported occasional hesitation and stalling issues.

**Engine Stalling and Acceleration Hesitation**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers experience unexpected engine stalling, hesitation, or delayed acceleration while trying to increase speed.
- **Solution:** Diagnose the throttle body, fuel system, or engine control module; faulty components may need cleaning or replacement.

## Transmission

### Xtronic Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)

An Xtronic Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT) manages power delivery across all trims of the 2016 Rogue. While engineered to optimize fuel economy, this transmission system has a documented history of premature wear, shuddering, and failure that shoppers must thoroughly evaluate.

**Transmission Shudder and Slipping**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle shudders during acceleration, jerks, hesitates between gear ratios, or experiences complete power loss.
- **Solution:** Inspect transmission fluid condition; advanced mechanical failure typically requires professional transmission overhaul or complete replacement.
- **Cost:** $6,000

## Exterior

### Door Locks and Power Liftgate

The electrical architecture in the Rogue governs numerous convenience features, with body control modules and door hardware showing susceptibility to premature wear over time.

**Door Lock Actuator and Liftgate Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Car doors fail to lock or unlock properly, lock by themselves unexpectedly, or the power liftgate stops functioning.
- **Solution:** Replace faulty door lock actuators or liftgate motor assemblies.

## Safety

### Air Bag and Seat Belts

Occupant safety systems are critical areas of inspection, as sensor faults and mechanical binding can impair proper protection during a collision.

**Passenger Air Bag Warning and Seat Belt Jamming**
- **Symptoms:** Air bag warning light stays illuminated even when the passenger seat is empty, or front passenger seat belts become physically stuck.
- **Solution:** Inspect the occupant classification sensor system and replace jammed seat belt assemblies.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V716000: Seats](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=Rogue&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to meet performance requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 202a, "Head Restraints" and FMVSS 207, "Seating Systems." In the event of a crash, the improperly welded seat back frames may increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Nissan will notify owners, and dealers will replace the front passenger seat back assembly, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V244000: Air Bags:Sensor:Occupant Classification](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=Rogue&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** As a result, the passenger frontal air bag may be turned off and not deploy in the event of a crash. If the passenger frontal air bag does not deploy as intended in the event of a crash, the passenger is at an increased risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will reprogram the Air Bag Control Unit (ACU) and OCS Electronic Control Unit (ECU) in Altima, Maxima, Murano, Rogue, and Sentra vehicles, and replace the OCS ECU in LEAF, NV200, NV200 Taxi, Pathfinder, Infiniti Q50, JX35, and QX60. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V663000: Seats](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=Rogue&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standards (FMVSS) number 207, "Seating Systems," and 210, "Seat Belt Assembly Anchorages." The insufficient welds can increase the risk of injury to the occupants in the event of a crash.
- **What to do:** Nissan will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the seat frames and replace any defective lower seat frames, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V911000: Air Bags:Sensor:Occupant Classification](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=Rogue&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** If the front passenger seat occupant is misclassified, the air bag may deploy incorrectly in the event of a crash, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Nissan will notify owners, and dealers will replace the ECU and update the OCS software free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V219000: Structure:Body:Hatchback/Liftgate:Support Device/Strut](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=Rogue&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** Corrosion of one or both of the rear lift gate support stays could cause a sudden release of pressure. If this occurs, the support stays may break off, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Nissan will notify owners, and dealers will replace the rear tailgate stays, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V024000: Electrical System:Wiring:Interior/Under Dash](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=Rogue&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** Corrosion in the electrical connector can result in power window/seat failure, all-wheel-drive warning light illumination, battery drainage, and electrical connector damage that increases the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will remove the harness tape and harness covering and replace both connectors and apply white lithium grease to the connectors as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V093000: Electrical System:Ignition:Anti-Theft:Immobilizer/Proximity:Key/Sender](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=Rogue&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** Driver contact with a key in the collapsed position could inadvertently shut off the vehicle while driving, increasing the risk of a crash. Additionally, if the vehicle shuts off while driving, the air bags may not deploy in a crash as intended, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will insert a spacer into the key slot of the jackknife key, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2016 Nissan Rogue remains an affordable and comfortable crossover choice, but it demands careful pre-purchase scrutiny. Because CVT transmission reliability is a known vulnerability for this model year, buyers should verify service records, test drive thoroughly for shudders or hesitation, and consider obtaining an independent mechanical inspection before finalizing a purchase.
