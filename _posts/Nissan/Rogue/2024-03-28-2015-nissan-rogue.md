**Introduction:**

The 2015 Nissan Rogue is a compact SUV available with a 2.5L four-cylinder engine and a CVT (continuously variable transmission). It offers 170 horsepower and 175 lb-ft of torque, providing a comfortable and efficient driving experience. While the Rogue has received positive feedback for its spacious interior and user-friendly infotainment system, some owners have encountered issues that can impact the vehicle's overall reliability.

**Engine**

* **2.5L Four-Cylinder:**

    **Intake Valve Carbon Buildup:**
    Description/Symptoms: Engine misfires, rough idle, reduced power
    Solution: Intake valve cleaning or replacement
    Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

* **CVT:**

    **Premature Failure:**
    Description/Symptoms: Slipping, hesitation, complete loss of power
    Solution: CVT replacement
    Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,500 - $4,000+
    Recalls: NHTSA Campaign Number: 18V103000

**Suspension**

* **No Issues Found**

**Electrical**

* **Alternator Failure:**
    Description/Symptoms: Dimming headlights, flickering electronics, battery not charging
    Solution: Alternator replacement
    Estimated Cost to Fix: $700 - $1,000+
    Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Roof Rack Crossbars Loosening:**
    Description/Symptoms: Crossbars become loose or detached from roof rails
    Solution: Crossbar replacement or tightening
    Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200
    Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Steering Wheel Controls Malfunction:**
    Description/Symptoms: Controls (audio, cruise control) become unresponsive or intermittent
    Solution: Steering wheel control module replacement
    Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500
    Recalls: None Found

* **Dashboard Cracking:**
    Description/Symptoms: Cracks appear on the dashboard, particularly around the passenger airbag area
    Solution: Dashboard replacement
    Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

Overall, the 2015 Nissan Rogue has received some notable complaints regarding its engine and transmission. Issues such as intake valve carbon buildup and CVT failures can lead to significant repair costs. Other concerns include premature electrical failures, loose roof rack crossbars, and interior component malfunctions. While these issues can affect driving performance and reliability, it's important to note that not all Rogues experience these problems.
