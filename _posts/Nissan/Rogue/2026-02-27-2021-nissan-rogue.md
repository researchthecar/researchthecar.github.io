# 2021 Nissan Rogue

### Overview
The 2021 Nissan Rogue, a compact SUV, generally presents a solid reliability profile for its model year. It aims to balance comfort, practicality, and modern technology. While most owners report a positive experience, like any vehicle, there are specific areas where potential issues have been noted. This analysis focuses on documented technical reliability concerns, excluding routine maintenance and standard wear and tear, to provide a clear picture for potential buyers and owners. The Rogue's powertrain and electronic systems are key areas to examine for any recurring problems.

## Transmission

### Xtronic CVT (Continuously Variable Transmission)
**Hesitation or Jerking**
- **Symptoms:** Occasional hesitation during acceleration or a slight jerking sensation, particularly at lower speeds or when engaging gears.
- **Solution:** Software updates for the transmission control module, or in more severe cases, transmission fluid flush and replacement. Internal component inspection may be required.
- **Cost:** $150 - $500

## Electrical

### Infotainment System
**Connectivity Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Occasional freezing, unresponsiveness, or Bluetooth connectivity issues with paired devices.
- **Solution:** Software updates for the infotainment system. A system reset may also resolve temporary glitches.

### 12V Battery
**Premature Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Difficulty starting the vehicle, dimming lights, or electrical system malfunctions attributed to a weak or failed battery.
- **Solution:** Battery testing and replacement.
- **Cost:** $150 - $300

### Summary Verdict
The 2021 Nissan Rogue demonstrates good overall reliability, with its primary powertrain components performing commendably. The most frequently cited technical concerns revolve around the Xtronic CVT, which can exhibit minor hesitation or jerking, and occasional glitches within the infotainment system. Electrical issues, such as premature 12V battery failure, have also been noted by some owners. However, these issues are generally not widespread or catastrophic, and often addressable with software updates or standard component replacement. The suspension, exterior, and interior components are largely free from significant reported defects, contributing to a generally positive ownership experience for this model year.
