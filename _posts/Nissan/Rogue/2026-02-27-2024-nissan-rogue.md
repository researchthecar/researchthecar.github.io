# 2024 Nissan Rogue Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2024 Nissan Rogue, prospective buyers will find a modern compact SUV featuring refreshed styling and an updated infotainment suite. While many owners enjoy its comfort and fuel efficiency, owner reports submitted to safety authorities highlight a few notable areas of concern. Particular attention should be paid to reports regarding sudden loss of motive power and unexpected exterior glass breakage.

---

## Engine

### 1.5L VC-Turbo 3-Cylinder Engine

The 2024 Nissan Rogue comes equipped with a 1.5-liter variable-compression turbocharged three-cylinder engine producing approximately 201 horsepower and 225 lb-ft of torque. It provides adequate acceleration for daily commuting, though buyers should verify powertrain health during a test drive.

**Loss of Motive Power and Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers experience a sudden loss of motive power, activation of limp mode, or engine stalling while driving at various speeds.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified technician inspect the throttle control system and engine management software for potential updates or component failure.

## Transmission

### Xtronic Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)

Paired exclusively with an Xtronic continuously variable transmission, the vehicle aims to optimize fuel economy across front-wheel and all-wheel drive configurations. Smooth power delivery is typical, but proper maintenance records are essential.

**Transmission Malfunction Warning**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers report warning messages on the information screen regarding transmission or powertrain malfunction accompanied by illumination of cluster warning lights.
- **Solution:** Diagnostic scanning by a dealership or qualified repair shop is required to check for transmission control module codes and fluid condition.

## Electrical

### Infotainment System

The interior technology suite offers convenient connectivity features, though intermittent software glitches in the touchscreen display have been reported by some drivers.

**Screen Freezes and Audio Loss**
- **Symptoms:** The infotainment touchscreen fails to respond, loses audio, or resets randomly during vehicle operation or startup.
- **Solution:** Update the infotainment system software or consult a dealership for potential module replacement if software updates do not resolve the issue.

## Exterior

### Rear Windshield Glass

Exterior body and glass panels generally hold up well, though spontaneous rear window breakage has been noted by multiple owners.

**Spontaneous Rear Glass Shattering**
- **Symptoms:** The rear window or windshield shatters unexpectedly while the vehicle is parked or being driven, often accompanied by a loud pop.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the shattered glass panel by an auto glass professional.

## Safety

### Seat Belts

Safety equipment generally performs as expected, but physical inspection of seatbelt operation during a pre-purchase examination is recommended.

**Seatbelt Latchplate Binding**
- **Symptoms:** The driver's seatbelt latchplate binds and progressively cinches tighter during normal driving conditions.
- **Solution:** Inspect the seatbelt assembly and replace components if webbing wear or latchplate binding is confirmed.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V748000: Back Over Prevention](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=Rogue&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** A software error may cause the rearview camera to display a blank screen when the vehicle is in reverse. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 111, "Rear Visibility." The loss of the rearview camera image while in reverse can reduce the driver's rear visibility, increasing the risk of a.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 25V437000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Engine:Hard Parts Internal/Mechanical](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=Rogue&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** Engine failure increases the risk of a crash. A bearing failure may cause a breach in the engine block, allowing hot oil to be discharged, increasing the risk of an engine fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will reprogram the engine control module (ECM) software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V080000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Engine:Hard Parts Internal/Mechanical](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=Rogue&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** Increased temperatures can break down the engine oil, which may cause the engine bearings to seize and become damaged, leading to engine failure. Engine failure can cause a loss of drive power, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will reprogram the engine control module (ECM) software, inspect for diagnostic trouble codes, and test drive the vehicle, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V081000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Fuel Injection System:Throttlebody/Manifold](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=Rogue&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** Broken throttle body gears can cause a loss of drive power, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will reprogram the engine control module (ECM) software and inspect and replace the electronic throttle body assembly as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2024 Nissan Rogue offers a comfortable ride, modern technology, and efficient performance, making it an appealing option in the compact SUV segment. Used car buyers should carefully inspect the vehicle's powertrain operation, verify that all software updates have been applied, and check exterior glass and seatbelt function during a thorough test drive.
