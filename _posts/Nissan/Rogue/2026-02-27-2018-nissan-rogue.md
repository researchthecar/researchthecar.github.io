# 2018 Nissan Rogue Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used compact SUV, the 2018 Nissan Rogue often catches buyers' eyes with its spacious interior, comfortable Zero Gravity seats, and generous cargo features. However, prospective owners need to approach this model year with careful consideration of its documented mechanical and electronic tendencies. While it offers solid fuel economy and an easy daily driving experience, reviewing owner feedback reveals notable recurring complaints regarding the continuously variable transmission and driver-assist safety systems. Taking time to inspect service history and thoroughly test-driving prospective vehicles will go a long way in ensuring a dependable ownership experience.

---

## Engine

### 2.5L 4-Cylinder Engine

The 2018 Rogue comes equipped with a 2.5-liter naturally aspirated 4-cylinder engine producing 170 horsepower and 175 lb-ft of torque. It provides adequate power for daily commuting, though some drivers find acceleration modest for highway passing.

**Engine Stalling and Hesitation**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers experience sudden engine stalling or significant lag and sluggish acceleration from a stop, often accompanied by dashboard warning lights.
- **Solution:** Diagnostic scanning is required to check for failing electronic sensors, throttle body issues, or fuel delivery faults.

## Transmission

### Xtronic CVT (Continuously Variable Transmission)

Paired exclusively with front-wheel drive or all-wheel drive, the Xtronic Continuously Variable Transmission prioritizes fuel efficiency. However, its historical reliability concerns make verifying routine fluid maintenance and transmission health critical before purchasing.

**Transmission Failure and Shaking**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers report premature transmission failure, unusual vibrations, shuddering during acceleration, and whining noises.
- **Solution:** Often requires complete transmission replacement or specialized internal component overhaul by a qualified technician.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

The conventional hydraulic braking system provides stopping power, though sensors linked to advanced driver safety features have generated owner complaints regarding phantom activation.

**Vibration and Unexpected Braking**
- **Symptoms:** Owners note steering wheel or brake pedal vibration during stops, alongside instances of the automatic emergency braking system engaging unexpectedly.
- **Solution:** Inspect brake rotors and pads for warping or uneven wear, and check collision avoidance sensor calibration.

## Interior

### Seats and Liftgate

The cabin offers comfortable passenger accommodations and flexible cargo space, though specific mechanical components like power liftgates and seat tracks can experience mechanical wear.

**Driver Seat Track Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The driver's seat frame or track loosens, causing the seat back to shift or break away from the base while driving.
- **Solution:** Requires replacing the affected seat frame, track assembly, or finish components.
- **Cost:** $5,000

## Safety

### Forward Collision Avoidance

Advanced safety features like automatic emergency braking are standard equipment designed to protect occupants, but sensitive radar calibration can occasionally cause erratic behavior.

**False Activation / Unexpected Braking**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may autonomously brake abruptly while driving at highway speeds without an actual obstruction present.
- **Solution:** Software updates or recalibration of the front radar and camera sensors by a dealer may mitigate false triggers.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V654000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=Rogue&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 111, "Rear Visibility." The lack of an image in the back-up camera display increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 21V839000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Engine:Engine Control Module (Ecu/Ecm)](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=Rogue&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** A blown fuse may cause an engine stall, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will install a harness protector cover, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V093000: Electrical System:Ignition:Anti-Theft:Immobilizer/Proximity:Key/Sender](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=Rogue&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** Driver contact with a key in the collapsed position could inadvertently shut off the vehicle while driving, increasing the risk of a crash. Additionally, if the vehicle shuts off while driving, the air bags may not deploy in a crash as intended, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will insert a spacer into the key slot of the jackknife key, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2018 Nissan Rogue offers strong practicality, comfort, and fuel efficiency, making it an appealing budget-friendly option. However, buyers should exercise caution and prioritize vehicles with comprehensive service records. Pay close attention during test drives to transmission smoothness and verify that all safety sensors and mechanical features operate properly to avoid unexpected repair expenses.
