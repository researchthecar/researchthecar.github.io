# 2023 Nissan Rogue Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used compact SUV, the Nissan Rogue is a popular option known for its comfortable cabin, modern tech features, and smooth ride. However, prospective buyers should review owner-reported feedback carefully, as certain recurring mechanical and body concerns have surfaced across various model years. Understanding these common trouble spots can help you evaluate a prospective vehicle's condition and maintenance history more effectively before making a purchase.

---

## Engine

### 1.5L VC-Turbo 3-Cylinder

The 1.5-liter variable-compression turbocharged three-cylinder engine generates approximately 201 horsepower and 225 lb-ft of torque, balancing everyday efficiency with peppy performance. While generally responsive, buyers should monitor for occasional reports of fuel odors and powertrain hesitation.

**Engine Stalling and Loss of Motive Power**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers report unexpected engine stalling, rough idling, entering limp mode, or a sudden loss of motive power while driving.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified mechanic inspect the powertrain control module, throttle body, and associated sensors to diagnose fault codes.

**Fuel Odor and Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** A strong gasoline smell inside the cabin or around the vehicle, particularly after parking in a garage.
- **Solution:** Inspect the fuel lines, injectors, and engine bay connections for leaks or loose fittings.

## Transmission

### Xtronic Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)

Equipped with an Xtronic Continuously Variable Transmission paired with front-wheel or all-wheel drive, this setup prioritizes fuel economy and smooth power delivery. Prospective buyers should test drive thoroughly to ensure seamless engagement and quiet operation.

**Powertrain Stalling and Shifting Irregularities**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may experience hesitation during acceleration, unexpected gear shifts, or occasional stalling.
- **Solution:** Check transmission fluid condition and have a dealer or specialist scan for diagnostic trouble codes.

## Exterior

### Rear Windshield Glass

The exterior styling is sleek and modern, though owners have reported occasional glass durability concerns with the rear hatch window.

**Spontaneous Rear Window Shattering**
- **Symptoms:** The rear hatchback glass suddenly cracks, shatters, or implodes without apparent impact or provocation.
- **Solution:** Replace the damaged rear windshield and verify that the liftgate frame and defroster wiring are undamaged.

## Safety

### Forward Collision and Airbag Systems

Advanced safety features provide helpful driver assistance, but electrical and sensor glitches can occasionally trigger warning lights or require calibration.

**Brake System and Warning Light Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** Illuminated warning lights related to the braking system or forward collision avoidance, accompanied by intermittent brake responsiveness.
- **Solution:** Have the vehicle's electronic sensors, brake modules, and wiring harnesses inspected by a certified technician.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V268000: Seats:Critical Fasteners](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=Rogue&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** The driver's powered seat may not be fully secured to the seat frame, causing the seat frame welds to detach from the rear inboard seat bracket. An unsecured seat with broken welds may not properly restrain an occupant during a crash, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the driver's seat rail clearance, and if necessary, replace the driver's seat cushion frame, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V374000: Seat Belts:Critical Fasteners](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=Rogue&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** The fastener in the rear seat belt anchor attachment points may not have been tightened properly and can loosen over time. A loose rear seat belt anchor fastener may not properly restrain an occupant in the event of a crash, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and tighten the rear seat belt anchor bolts. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V154000: Air Bags:Frontal:Driver Side:Inflator Module](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=Rogue&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** Due to a manufacturing issue, the driver's air bag inflator may absorb moisture, and cause the air bag to deploy improperly. An air bag that deploys improperly may not protect the driver as intended during a crash, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the front driver's air bag assembly, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V156000: Seat Belts:Pretensioner](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=Rogue&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 208, "Occupant Crash Protection," 209, "Seat Belt Assemblies," and 210 "Seat Belt Assembly Anchorages." Improperly restrained occupants have an increased risk of injury during a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the front seat belt assemblies, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V437000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Engine:Hard Parts Internal/Mechanical](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=Rogue&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** Engine failure increases the risk of a crash. A bearing failure may cause a breach in the engine block, allowing hot oil to be discharged, increasing the risk of an engine fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will reprogram the engine control module (ECM) software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V080000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Engine:Hard Parts Internal/Mechanical](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=Rogue&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** Increased temperatures can break down the engine oil, which may cause the engine bearings to seize and become damaged, leading to engine failure. Engine failure can cause a loss of drive power, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will reprogram the engine control module (ECM) software, inspect for diagnostic trouble codes, and test drive the vehicle, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

When shopping for a used model, conducting a comprehensive pre-purchase inspection is essential to verify the condition of the engine, transmission, and electronics. Pay close attention during your test drive for any hesitation, unusual odors, or warning lights. Choosing a vehicle with a documented service history can offer extra reassurance and help ensure reliable ownership.
