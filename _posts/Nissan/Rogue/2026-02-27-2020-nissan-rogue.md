# 2020 Nissan Rogue Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2020 Nissan Rogue, buyers will find a popular compact SUV offering a comfortable cabin and modern safety features. However, owner reports highlight a few notable areas that warrant careful inspection during a test drive. Particular attention should be paid to driver assistance electronics, power door locks, and specific mechanical components to ensure a trouble-free ownership experience.

---

## Engine

### 2.5L 4-Cylinder Engine

The standard 2.5-liter four-cylinder engine produces approximately 181 horsepower and 181 lb-ft of torque, providing adequate power for daily commuting. This powerplant generally offers straightforward reliability, though routine fluid and maintenance checks remain essential.

## Transmission

### Xtronic Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)

Equipped with an Xtronic continuously variable transmission paired with front-wheel drive or all-wheel drive, this setup prioritizes fuel efficiency. Buyers should inspect and test the transmission smoothly through all operations, as proactive fluid maintenance is key to long-term longevity.

## Exterior

### Door Lock Actuators

Electrical complaints center primarily on power door lock actuators failing prematurely, which can cause doors to remain unlocked or locked against the driver's intent.

**Failing Door Lock Actuators**
- **Symptoms:** Driver or passenger side doors intermittently fail to lock or unlock using either the key fob or the interior power button, requiring manual key operation.
- **Solution:** Replace the malfunctioning door lock actuator mechanism inside the affected door panel.

## Safety

### Forward Collision Avoidance

Owner feedback points to occasional glitches in the safety suite, specifically false activations of the forward collision and automatic emergency braking systems.

**Unexpected Automatic Braking**
- **Symptoms:** The automatic emergency braking (AEB) or forward collision avoidance system occasionally activates unexpectedly while driving or backing up, causing sudden deceleration.
- **Solution:** Have a certified technician inspect sensor alignment, software calibration, and check for relevant manufacturer service updates.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V093000: Electrical System:Ignition:Anti-Theft:Immobilizer/Proximity:Key/Sender](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=Rogue&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** Driver contact with a key in the collapsed position could inadvertently shut off the vehicle while driving, increasing the risk of a crash. Additionally, if the vehicle shuts off while driving, the air bags may not deploy in a crash as intended, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will insert a spacer into the key slot of the jackknife key, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2020 Nissan Rogue remains an appealing choice for used compact SUV buyers seeking comfort and efficiency. While most mechanical systems prove dependable, prospective owners should thoroughly verify the functionality of power door locks and test the collision avoidance systems during a pre-purchase inspection to rule out unexpected electronic behavior.
