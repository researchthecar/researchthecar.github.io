The 2022 Nissan Rogue is a compact SUV known for its comfortable ride, spacious interior, and fuel efficiency. It comes standard with a 1.5L VC-Turbo 3-cylinder engine producing 201 horsepower and 225 lb-ft of torque, paired with a CVT (Continuously Variable Transmission). The Rogue is generally liked for its sleek design and user-friendly technology, however some drivers find the engine to be noisy or underpowered at times. Common complaints also revolve around the CVT's responsiveness and reliability.

**Engine**

**1.5L VC-Turbo**
*   **Engine Failure/Knocking Noises**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported instances of engine failure, often preceded by loud knocking or unusual noises from the engine bay.
    *   Solution: Engine replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $6,000 - $8,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Excessive Oil Consumption**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners report the engine consuming excessive amounts of oil, requiring frequent top-ups between oil changes.
    *   Solution: Some owners have had success with replacing the PCV valve. More severe cases may require piston ring replacement or engine replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $100+ for PCV valve, $3,000+ for piston rings, $6,000 - $8,000+ for engine replacement.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Turbocharger Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, whistling noises from the engine, check engine light.
    *   Solution: Turbocharger replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**CVT (Continuously Variable Transmission)**

*   **CVT Failure/Shuddering**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Shuddering during acceleration, especially at lower speeds, slipping between "gears", and complete transmission failure. Some experience "gear hunting" or inconsistent acceleration.
    *   Solution: In some cases, a software update or fluid change may resolve the issue. More severe cases require CVT replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400 for software update/fluid change, $4,000 - $7,000+ for CVT replacement.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Delayed Engagement**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Noticeable delay when shifting from Park to Drive or Reverse.
    *   Solution: Fluid change, valve body replacement, or complete transmission replacement in severe cases.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400 for fluid change, $1,000 - $2,000+ for valve body, $4,000 - $7,000+ for CVT replacement.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Premature Wear of Suspension Components**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Premature wear of shocks, struts, or bushings, leading to a bouncy ride, clunking noises, or uneven tire wear.
    *   Solution: Replacement of worn suspension components.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+ per corner, depending on the components needing replacement.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Freezing/Crashing**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The infotainment system freezes, crashes, or becomes unresponsive.
    *   Solution: Software update or replacement of the infotainment unit.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 - $200 for software update, $500 - $1,500+ for unit replacement.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Sensor Malfunctions (e.g., Blind Spot Monitoring, Lane Departure Warning)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Warning lights illuminate, and the affected safety features malfunction or become disabled.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis and replacement of faulty sensors.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+ per sensor, depending on location and type.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Battery Drain Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The battery drains quickly, especially when the vehicle is not in use, leading to difficulty starting the car.
    *   Solution: Diagnose and address parasitic drain (e.g., faulty module, improperly installed accessory), replace battery.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300 for diagnostic, $100 - $300+ for new battery, variable cost for addressing parasitic drain.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Automatic Emergency Braking (AEB) System Problems**
    *   Description/Symptoms: False activation of the AEB system, causing the vehicle to brake unexpectedly.
    *   Solution: Sensor recalibration or replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Rattling/Squeaking Noises**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rattling or squeaking noises emanating from the dashboard, door panels, or rear cargo area.
    *   Solution: Identifying and securing the source of the noise (e.g., loose trim pieces, improperly installed components).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: Highly variable, depending on the source and severity of the noise. Could range from a simple DIY fix to several hundred dollars for professional repair.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2022 Nissan Rogue has several potential issues owners should be aware of. The most serious concern is potential engine failure, which can be very expensive to repair. CVT issues are also common and can range from annoying shuddering to complete transmission failure. Electrical glitches and interior rattles are also prevalent. While not all Rogues will experience these problems, potential buyers should be aware of these potential issues and have the vehicle thoroughly inspected before purchase. The cost of repairs can be significant, especially for engine and transmission problems.
