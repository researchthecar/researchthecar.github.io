# 2022 Nissan Rogue Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2022 Nissan Rogue marks the continuation of its redesigned generation, featuring a 1.5-liter turbocharged three-cylinder engine paired with an Xtronic continuously variable transmission (CVT). While the vehicle offers modern styling, comfortable seating, and impressive fuel economy, prospective buyers should review recurring owner complaints. Particular attention should be paid to reports regarding powertrain performance, fuel odors, and occasional electrical or body integrity issues.

---

## Engine

### 1.5L VC-Turbo 3-Cylinder

The 1.5-liter variable-compression turbocharged three-cylinder engine produces approximately 201 horsepower and 225 lb-ft of torque. It prioritizes fuel efficiency but has drawn owner reports regarding electrical warning lights, stalling, and fuel odors.

**Loss of Motive Power and Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers experience unexpected engine stalling, activation of warning lights, and sudden loss of motive power while driving.
- **Solution:** Diagnostic evaluation by a certified technician; software updates or hardware replacement depending on the root cause.

**Fuel Odor or Leak**
- **Symptoms:** A strong smell of gasoline or fuel fumes noticeable around the vehicle, particularly after parking or in enclosed garages.
- **Solution:** Inspect fuel lines, connections, and fuel system components for leaks; replace damaged components as necessary.

## Transmission

### Xtronic Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)

Equipped with an Xtronic CVT designed to optimize fuel economy across front-wheel and all-wheel-drive configurations. While generally smooth in operation, owners have reported occasional high-cost transmission failures requiring unit replacement.

**Transmission Failure / Limp Mode**
- **Symptoms:** Illuminated diagnostic trouble codes (such as P187E), entry into failsafe or limp mode limiting speed and acceleration, and eventual transmission failure.
- **Solution:** Professional diagnosis of transmission health; replacement of faulty transmission components or complete unit replacement if internal failure occurs.
- **Cost:** $9,800

## Steering

### Steering Linkage

Responsible for directional control and wheel articulation. Steering component wear or tie rod failure presents significant safety concerns when reported.

**Tie Rod Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Sudden steering loss, wheel lockup, or unexpected detachment of inner/outer tie rods during low-speed maneuvers or highway driving.
- **Solution:** Inspect steering components for wear, damage, or loose fasteners; replace compromised tie rods and alignment check.

## Electrical

### Backup Camera and Display System

Handles onboard electronics, driver assistance features, and infotainment displays. Backup camera glitches remain a notable point of owner frustration.

**Camera Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The backup camera screen fails to load or display an image when the vehicle is shifted into reverse.
- **Solution:** Inspect camera wiring harnesses and software modules; update or replace faulty camera units.

## Exterior

### Panoptic Sunroof / Glass

Includes exterior body panels, trim, and glass features. Spontaneous sunroof shattering has been reported by a subset of owners.

**Sunroof Shattering**
- **Symptoms:** The panoramic sunroof or glass roof panel suddenly shatters or explodes while driving at highway speeds without prior impact.
- **Solution:** Replace the shattered glass panel and inspect the frame for structural stress or alignment issues.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V259000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Storage:Tank Assembly](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=Rogue&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** Road debris may puncture the thin fuel tank wall, causing a gas leak and increasing the risk of a fire. In addition, the fuel tank may not perform as expected during a crash, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the fuel tank assembly and install a new lock ring, gasket, and o-ring seal, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V527000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=Rogue&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** When the Sirius XM subscription is set to "Not Subscribed," it may cause the infotainment head unit to reboot, resulting in an inoperative rearview camera. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 111, "Rear Visibility." An inoperative rearview camera display reduces the driver's rear.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 22V660000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Storage:Tank Assembly](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=Rogue&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** A hole in the fuel tank could leak, increasing the risk of fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the fuel tank as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V666000: Seat Belts: Rear/Other:Buckle Assembly](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=Rogue&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** The rear seat belts for the left-hand and right-hand rear seats may not retract properly. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard numbers 208, "Occupant Crash Protection," and 209, "Seatbelt Assemblies." Seat belts that do not retract properly may be difficult to operate, preventing their usage.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the rear seat belts, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V772000: Back Over Prevention:Display Function](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=Rogue&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 111, "Rear Visibility." An inoperative rearview camera display reduces the driver's rear visibility, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 25V437000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Engine:Hard Parts Internal/Mechanical](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=Rogue&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** Engine failure increases the risk of a crash. A bearing failure may cause a breach in the engine block, allowing hot oil to be discharged, increasing the risk of an engine fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will reprogram the engine control module (ECM) software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2022 Nissan Rogue offers a comfortable ride, modern technology, and strong fuel economy from its three-cylinder engine. However, used-car buyers should perform a thorough pre-purchase inspection focusing on the CVT transmission, steering components, and fuel system integrity. Verifying service history and confirming that all applicable recall and diagnostic updates have been completed is strongly advised.
