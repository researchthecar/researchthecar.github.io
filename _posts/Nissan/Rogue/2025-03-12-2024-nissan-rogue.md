The 2024 Nissan Rogue is a popular compact SUV known for its comfortable ride, spacious interior, and fuel-efficient engine. It comes standard with a 1.5-liter turbocharged three-cylinder engine producing 201 horsepower and 225 lb-ft of torque, paired with a continuously variable transmission (CVT). Some owners appreciate its user-friendly technology and attractive styling, while others find the CVT transmission to be less engaging than traditional automatic transmissions. Potential drawbacks include some reports of lackluster acceleration and relatively uninspired handling compared to rivals.

**Engine**

**1.5L Turbocharged Three-Cylinder**

*   **Turbocharger Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Whining noises from the engine, reduced power, check engine light illumination. Some owners have reported premature turbocharger failure.
    *   Solution: Turbocharger replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Excessive Oil Consumption**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Needing to add oil frequently between oil changes, blue smoke from the exhaust, low oil level warning light.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis to determine the source of oil consumption; Possible piston ring replacement, valve stem seal replacement, or engine replacement in severe cases.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $5,000+ depending on the source of the leak.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**CVT (Continuously Variable Transmission)**

*   **CVT Hesitation and Jerking**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Hesitation upon acceleration, jerky movements, and a feeling of the transmission "slipping" or not engaging smoothly.
    *   Solution: Software update for the CVT control module. In some cases, CVT replacement may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300 for software update. $3,000 - $5,000+ for CVT replacement.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **CVT Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Transmission stops working, loud noises coming from transmission, vehicle unable to accelerate.
    *   Solution: Transmission replacement
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $3,000 - $5,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

No Issues Found

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Freezing/Crashing**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The infotainment screen freezes, becomes unresponsive, or restarts unexpectedly.
    *   Solution: Software update for the infotainment system. Sometimes requires replacement of the infotainment unit.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 (software update) - $1,000+ (unit replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Sensor Malfunctions (Blind Spot Monitoring, Lane Departure Warning)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: False alerts from the safety systems, system not functioning correctly, warning lights displayed on the dashboard.
    *   Solution: Sensor recalibration or replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+ per sensor, depending on location and availability.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Automatic Emergency Braking System (AEB) Malfunctions**
    *   Description/Symptoms: System detects obstacles that aren't there and applies the brakes, or fails to detect an obstacle and does not apply the brakes.
    *   Solution: Radar sensor calibration or replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Rattling/Squeaking Noises**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rattling sounds from the dashboard, door panels, or rear cargo area while driving.
    *   Solution: Locating the source of the rattle and applying felt tape, tightening loose fasteners, or replacing the affected trim pieces.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $500+, depending on the source and complexity of the repair.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**
The 2024 Nissan Rogue has some issues to be aware of. The most concerning problems revolve around the engine and CVT transmission, which can lead to costly repairs if problems develop. Electrical issues with the infotainment system and safety sensors can also be frustrating. Some owners have reported interior rattles and squeaks, which can impact the overall driving experience. While some fixes are relatively inexpensive, major engine and transmission repairs can be significant.
