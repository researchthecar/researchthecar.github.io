# 2014 Nissan Rogue

### Overview
The 2014 Nissan Rogue is a compact crossover SUV that offered a blend of practicality, fuel efficiency, and a comfortable ride. It was available with a continuously variable transmission (CVT) and a 2.5-liter four-cylinder engine, which was the primary powertrain for most trims. The Rogue aimed to appeal to families and individuals seeking a versatile vehicle for daily commuting and light adventuring. While generally considered a reliable vehicle for its class, like any automotive model, it has specific areas where owners have reported issues. This analysis focuses on documented technical reliability concerns beyond routine maintenance.

---


## Engine

### 2.5L QR25DE (4-Cylinder)
**Oil Consumption**
- **Symptoms:** Increased oil level drops between changes, potential for engine damage if not monitored.
- **Solution:** Regular oil level checks and top-offs. In severe cases, piston ring replacement may be necessary.
- **Cost:** $1,200 - $2,500

**CVT Transmission Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Hesitation, shuddering, slipping, or complete failure of the continuously variable transmission.
- **Solution:** CVT fluid changes at recommended intervals. Transmission repair or replacement.
- **Cost:** $2,500 - $5,000


## Transmission

### CVT (Continuously Variable Transmission)
**Overheating and Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Transmission slipping, jerky acceleration, warning lights, and eventual failure, often exacerbated by towing or heavy loads.
- **Solution:** Regular fluid changes and proper cooling system maintenance. Transmission replacement is often the ultimate solution.
- **Cost:** $2,500 - $5,000

## Electrical

### Infotainment System
**Bluetooth Connectivity Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Difficulty pairing devices, dropped connections, or system freezing.
- **Solution:** Software updates for the infotainment system, or potential replacement of the head unit.
- **Cost:** $100 - $800


### Keyless Entry/Ignition
**Fob Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Key fob losing power prematurely, requiring frequent battery replacement.
- **Solution:** Battery replacement, or in some cases, a faulty receiver module.
- **Cost:** $20 - $200

### Summary Verdict
The 2014 Nissan Rogue generally presents a decent reliability record, with its most significant concerns revolving around the Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT) and some instances of engine oil consumption with the 2.5L engine. The CVT, while contributing to fuel efficiency, has been known to require attention, particularly regarding overheating and potential failure if not maintained. Electrical issues, such as with the infotainment system's Bluetooth, are also noted but are typically less severe. Other areas like suspension, exterior, and interior components are largely free of widespread critical issues. Regular maintenance, especially for the CVT fluid, is crucial for maximizing the longevity of this vehicle.
