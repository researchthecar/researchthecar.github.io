**Introduction to the 2023 Nissan Versa**

The 2023 Nissan Versa is a subcompact sedan known for its fuel efficiency and affordable price. It is powered by a 1.6-liter four-cylinder engine paired with either a five-speed manual transmission or a continuously variable transmission (CVT). The engine produces 122 horsepower and 114 lb-ft of torque. Despite its low power output, the Versa is nimble and handles well. However, it has received criticism for its lack of sound insulation and some interior features.

**Engine**

**1.6-liter four-cylinder engine**

- **Engine misfires:**
   - Description/Symptoms: Engine hesitates or stalls, lack of power, rough idle.
   - Solution: Replace spark plugs, ignition coils, or fuel injectors as needed.
   - Estimated Cost to Fix: $250 - $600
   - Recalls: None Found

- **Timing chain noise:**
   - Description/Symptoms: Rattling or ticking noise from the engine, especially at startup or acceleration.
   - Solution: Replace timing chain tensioner.
   - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
   - Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**Five-speed manual transmission**

- **Transmission fluid leaks:**
   - Description/Symptoms: Transmission fluid leaking from the transmission pan or seals.
   - Solution: Replace transmission pan gasket or seals.
   - Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300
   - Recalls: None Found

- **Difficulty shifting gears:**
   - Description/Symptoms: Gears may be hard to shift or may grind.
   - Solution: Replace clutch or clutch master cylinder.
   - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,200
   - Recalls: None Found

**Continuously variable transmission (CVT)**

- **Transmission slipping:**
   - Description/Symptoms: Vehicle accelerates slowly, lack of power, or shuddering during acceleration.
   - Solution: Replace CVT fluid or transmission belt.
   - Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000
   - Recalls: None Found

- **CVT overheating:**
   - Description/Symptoms: Transmission warning light illuminated, overheating smell, transmission shuddering.
   - Solution: Replace CVT fluid and/or CVT cooler.
   - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500
   - Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

- **No Issues Found**

**Electrical**

- **Electrical system failures:**
   - Description/Symptoms: Electrical components may malfunction, such as lights, power windows, or audio system.
   - Solution: Replace faulty electrical components or check for loose connections.
   - Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $500
   - Recalls: None Found

- **Battery drain:**
   - Description/Symptoms: Battery dies prematurely or has difficulty starting.
   - Solution: Replace battery or diagnose and repair electrical system.
   - Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300
   - Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

- **Rear bumper sag:**
   - Description/Symptoms: Rear bumper may sag or detach.
   - Solution: Replace or repair rear bumper.
   - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
   - Recalls: None Found

- **Tail light condensation:**
   - Description/Symptoms: Water droplets forming inside tail light housing.
   - Solution: Replace tail light assembly.
   - Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300
   - Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

- **HVAC system issues:**
   - Description/Symptoms: AC system may not blow cold air or heater may not work properly.
   - Solution: Diagnose and repair AC system or replace heater core.
   - Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $1,200
   - Recalls: None Found

- **Infotainment system glitches:**
   - Description/Symptoms: Infotainment screen may freeze, lag, or display incorrect information.
   - Solution: Update infotainment software or replace system if necessary.
   - Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500
   - Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2023 Nissan Versa has a few reported issues in various components. While most issues are relatively minor and inexpensive to fix, some issues such as timing chain noise and CVT overheating can be more costly to repair. However, overall, the Versa is a reliable and affordable vehicle that meets the needs of many buyers.
