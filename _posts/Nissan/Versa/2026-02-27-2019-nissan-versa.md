# 2019 Nissan Versa

### Overview
The 2019 Nissan Versa is a subcompact car known for its affordability and fuel efficiency. As a budget-friendly option, its reliability is generally considered decent for its class, though not without some common points of concern that owners should be aware of. This analysis focuses on documented technical issues beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential problem areas for the 1.6L engine and its associated CVT and manual transmissions. While many owners report a trouble-free experience, understanding these potential issues can help in proactive maintenance and informed purchasing decisions.

---


## Engine

### 1.6L DOHC 4-Cylinder
**Oil Consumption**
- **Symptoms:** Excessive oil burning, potentially leading to low oil levels and engine damage if not monitored.
- **Solution:** Regular oil level checks and top-offs. In some cases, internal engine repairs or replacement may be necessary.
- **Cost:** $500 - $2000+

**Cooling System Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Coolant leaks from hoses, water pump, or radiator; engine overheating.
- **Solution:** Replacement of leaking components (hoses, water pump, radiator).
- **Cost:** $300 - $800


## Transmission

### Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)
**Hesitation and Jerking**
- **Symptoms:** Transmission may hesitate during acceleration or exhibit jerky movements.
- **Solution:** Software updates, transmission fluid flush and replacement, or in severe cases, transmission replacement.
- **Cost:** $800 - $3500+

**Overheating**
- **Symptoms:** Transmission warning light illuminated, potential loss of power.
- **Solution:** Transmission fluid cooler inspection and repair/replacement, transmission fluid flush.
- **Cost:** $400 - $1200


### 5-Speed Manual Transmission
**Clutch Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Slipping clutch, difficulty shifting gears, unusual noises.
- **Solution:** Clutch replacement.
- **Cost:** $700 - $1500

### Summary Verdict
The 2019 Nissan Versa offers a generally reliable ownership experience, particularly for its price point. The most frequently cited technical concerns revolve around the 1.6L engine's potential for oil consumption and cooling system leaks, and the CVT transmission's susceptibility to hesitation, jerking, and overheating. The manual transmission appears more robust, with clutch wear being the primary concern. Other systems like suspension, electrical, exterior, and interior components are typically free from widespread issues. Regular maintenance, especially monitoring oil levels and CVT fluid, is crucial for maximizing the longevity and reliability of this economical vehicle.
