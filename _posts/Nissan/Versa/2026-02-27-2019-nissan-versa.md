# 2019 Nissan Versa Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2019 Nissan Versa, prospective buyers will find a budget-friendly subcompact car that is popular for daily commuting and basic transportation. While it offers good fuel economy and a straightforward cabin layout, used car shoppers should thoroughly review vehicle history records and perform a comprehensive mechanical inspection. As with any pre-owned vehicle, evaluating previous maintenance, listening for unusual powertrain sounds, and checking for past repairs are essential steps to ensure long-term dependability.

---

## Engine

### 1.6L 4-Cylinder Engine

The 2019 Nissan Versa is powered by a 1.6-liter naturally aspirated four-cylinder engine producing 109 horsepower and 107 lb-ft of torque. This reliable powerplant focuses primarily on fuel efficiency rather than brisk acceleration, making it well-suited for city driving.

## Transmission

### Xtronic Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)

Equipped with Nissan's Xtronic Continuously Variable Transmission paired with front-wheel drive, this setup delivers smooth highway cruising. However, buyers should pay close attention to transmission responsiveness during a test drive.

**Transmission Hesitation and Shuddering**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may experience intermittent hesitation during acceleration, shuddering, rough deceleration, or unexpected loss of the drive indicator.
- **Solution:** Have the transmission thoroughly inspected by a qualified technician to check for internal wear, fluid condition, and diagnostic trouble codes.

### 5-Speed Manual Transmission

The entry-level configuration features a conventional 5-speed manual transmission paired with front-wheel drive, offering a simpler mechanical design that appeals to drivers preferring traditional gear shifting.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

Brake system performance is vital on subcompact vehicles, and any signs of soft pedal response require immediate professional evaluation.

**Brake Pedal Softness**
- **Symptoms:** The brake pedal may depress all the way to the floorboard during normal stopping maneuvers.
- **Solution:** Check the hydraulic brake lines, master cylinder, and brake fluid for leaks or pressure loss immediately.

## Electrical

### Electrical System

Electrical complaints on this model are generally sporadic, though intermittent sensor and power issues have been reported by some owners.

**Unexpected Engine Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may occasionally stall while in drive, causing a loss of power steering and illuminating warning lights.
- **Solution:** Inspect the electrical connections, battery health, and engine management sensors for intermittent failures.

## Interior

### Cabin Sealing

Interior comfort is straightforward, but buyers should inspect the cabin for any musty odors or damp spots indicating past leaks.

**Water Leaks and Moisture Ingress**
- **Symptoms:** Water may enter the vehicle cabin during heavy rains, leading to damp carpeting and persistent mold issues.
- **Solution:** Inspect door seals, window weatherstripping, and sunroof drains (if equipped) for proper sealing and clear blockages.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V654000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=Versa&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 111, "Rear Visibility." The lack of an image in the back-up camera display increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2019 Nissan Versa can serve as an affordable commuter car, but used-car buyers should proceed with careful inspection. Pay close attention to how the CVT operates during a test drive, check for any signs of electrical or brake irregularities, and inspect the cabin for water intrusion. Working with a trusted mechanic for a pre-purchase inspection can help uncover hidden issues before finalizing your purchase.
