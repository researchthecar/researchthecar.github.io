# 2024 Nissan Versa Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2024 Nissan Versa continues to serve as an economical subcompact sedan option, offering budget-conscious buyers a straightforward commuter car. However, early-model owner reports indicate a notable cluster of concerns regarding sudden engine stalling, loss of motive power, and transmission performance. Prospective buyers should carefully review service records and test-drive thoroughly.

---

## Engine

### 1.6L 4-Cylinder Engine

Powered by a 1.6-liter naturally aspirated 4-cylinder engine producing 122 horsepower, it provides basic commuter performance. While generally straightforward mechanically, owners have logged complaints concerning unexpected electronic throttle and stalling glitches.

**Engine Stalling and Limp Mode**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle may suddenly stall while driving or enter limp mode, resulting in a severe loss of power and an inability to accelerate normally.
- **Solution:** Take the vehicle to a dealer for diagnosis; some owners report software coding updates or computer module checks being investigated.

## Transmission

### Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)

Equipped with Nissan's Xtronic CVT, this transmission maximizes fuel efficiency for daily driving. Buyers should pay close attention to CVT responsiveness during test drives, as some early failures have been reported.

**CVT Failure and Slipping**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers experience transmission slipping, juddering during takeoff, or sudden internal failure requiring major component replacement.
- **Solution:** Consult a certified technician or dealership for transmission evaluation. Units experiencing premature mechanical failure typically require repair or replacement under factory warranty.

### 5-Speed Manual Transmission

A traditional 5-speed manual transmission is available on base trims for drivers preferring a stick shift. It offers a simpler mechanical design compared to the automatic CVT option.

## Electrical

### Electrical System & ECU

Manages onboard electronics, sensors, and powertrain communications. Isolated owner complaints point to sudden electrical anomalies affecting instrument clusters and powertrain operation.

**Dashboard Warning Lights and ECU Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** All dashboard warning lights may illuminate simultaneously, the tachometer can drop to zero, and the vehicle may restrict speed.
- **Solution:** Have the Powertrain Control Module (PCM) and electrical charging system scanned for diagnostic trouble codes.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V154000: Air Bags:Frontal:Driver Side:Inflator Module](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=Versa&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** Due to a manufacturing issue, the driver's air bag inflator may absorb moisture, and cause the air bag to deploy improperly. An air bag that deploys improperly may not protect the driver as intended during a crash, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the front driver's air bag assembly, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2024 Nissan Versa remains an attractive option for budget-minded buyers looking for high fuel economy and modern features at a low price point. However, because multiple owners have raised concerns regarding sudden engine stalling, limp mode activation, and CVT transmission issues, a thorough pre-purchase inspection and a test drive are strongly recommended.
