### Overview

## Transmission

### Xtronic CVT (Continuously Variable Transmission)
**Hesitation or Jerking**
- **Symptoms:** Occasional hesitation during acceleration or a slight jerking sensation, particularly at lower speeds or when engaging from a stop.
- **Solution:** Software updates for the transmission control module (TCM) or, in more severe cases, transmission fluid flush and filter replacement. Internal component failure may require transmission replacement.
- **Cost:** $200 - $500 for fluid service, $1500 - $4000 for replacement

### Summary Verdict
The 2024 Nissan Versa generally presents itself as a reliable vehicle within the subcompact segment. The powertrain, particularly the 1.6L engine, shows a strong track record with no widespread significant issues reported. The Xtronic CVT, while generally performing well, has seen some isolated reports of hesitation or minor jerking, often resolvable with software updates or fluid services. The manual transmission option remains a robust choice. Electrical components, such as the infotainment system, may occasionally exhibit minor glitches, but these are typically addressed through software. The chassis, suspension, exterior, and interior components are largely free from common technical defects, aligning with expectations for a vehicle in this price bracket. Overall, the Versa is a sensible choice for budget-conscious buyers prioritizing dependable daily transportation with minimal unexpected repair costs, provided routine maintenance is adhered to.
