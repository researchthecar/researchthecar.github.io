# 2021 Nissan Versa Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2021 Nissan Versa is a budget-friendly subcompact sedan praised for its fuel efficiency, modern safety features, and affordable daily commuting capability. However, used car buyers should approach this model year with care, particularly regarding powertrain durability. Owner complaints frequently highlight significant mechanical failures with the automatic transmission, along with minor concerns involving electrical and exterior components.

---

## Engine

### 1.6L 4-Cylinder Engine

The 2021 Nissan Versa comes equipped with a 1.6-liter naturally aspirated 4-cylinder engine producing approximately 122 horsepower and 114 lb-ft of torque. This engine provides adequate power for basic urban commuting and generally maintains a dependable mechanical reputation when properly maintained.

## Transmission

### Xtronic Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)

The Xtronic CVT pairs with the front-wheel-drive system on all standard Versa trims, prioritizing fuel economy over spirited performance. This transmission has drawn notable complaints from owners regarding premature internal wear and sudden mechanical failure.

**CVT Failure and Slippage**
- **Symptoms:** Owners report sudden loss of forward drive, acceleration failure, severe jerking, whining noises, and illumination of warning lights while driving.
- **Solution:** Requires professional diagnosis and often complete transmission replacement.

### 5-Speed Manual Transmission

An optional 5-speed manual transmission is available on base trims for drivers who prefer a traditional shift mechanism. This manual gearbox is much simpler mechanically and largely avoids the reliability concerns associated with the automatic CVT.

## Electrical

### Battery and Charging System

Electrical complaints are relatively infrequent, though isolated vehicle owners have reported recurring battery power drain issues.

**Premature Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle battery dies repeatedly over short periods despite the alternator and starter remaining in good working condition.
- **Solution:** Check for parasitic electrical draws and replace faulty battery cells if necessary.

## Exterior

### Windshield and Visors

Exterior body components generally hold up well, though occasional owner reports point to fragile interior sun visors and windshield vulnerability.

**Sun Visor Breakage and Glass Cracks**
- **Symptoms:** The driver-side sun visor unexpectedly drops and obstructs driver visibility, while windshields have shown a tendency to crack easily.
- **Solution:** Replace the broken sun visor assembly or damaged windshield glass.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V471000: Exterior Lighting:Turn Signal:Bulbs](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=Versa&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** Inoperable front turn signals cannot alert on-coming drivers and/or pedestrians that the vehicle will be turning, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the bulbs as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V693000: Steering:Electric Power Assist System](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=Versa&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** Loss of power steering assist can increase the steering effort required, especially at low speeds, and increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the steering column torque sensor lot code, and replace the column, if necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V496000: Steering:Electric Power Assist System](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=Versa&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** Loss of power steering assist can increase the steering effort required, especially at low speeds, and increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the steering column torque sensor lot code and replace the steering column assembly, if necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

When shopping for a used 2021 Nissan Versa, a thorough pre-purchase inspection is essential, with particular emphasis on testing the responsiveness and health of the CVT transmission. Buyers seeking maximum reliability may want to seek out manual transmission models or ensure that any automatic option is backed by a verifiable service history and extended warranty coverage.
