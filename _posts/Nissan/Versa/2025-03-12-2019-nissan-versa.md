The 2019 Nissan Versa is a subcompact car known for its affordability and fuel efficiency. It comes equipped with a 1.6-liter inline-4 engine producing 109 horsepower and 107 lb-ft of torque. Transmission options include a 5-speed manual (available only on the base S trim) and a continuously variable transmission (CVT). Owners often appreciate its spacious interior for its class and its ease of driving, but criticize its lack of power and dated features.

**Engine**

**1.6L I4**
*   **Excessive Oil Consumption**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners report the 1.6L engine consuming an unusually high amount of oil, leading to the need for frequent top-offs between oil changes. Symptoms include low oil level warnings, engine knocking or ticking noises, and blue exhaust smoke.
    *   Solution: The repair can range from replacing the PCV valve to piston ring replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 (PCV valve) - $2000+ (piston rings)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Engine Stalling**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners report engine stalling while driving, especially at low speeds or when idling.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis is necessary to determine the root cause, which could be related to the fuel system, ignition system, or sensors. Could be fuel pump failure.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150-$1000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**CVT (Continuously Variable Transmission)**
*   **CVT Juddering/Shaking**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Owners describe experiencing a juddering or shaking sensation, particularly during acceleration or when climbing hills. This is often attributed to issues within the CVT.
    *   Solution: CVT fluid flush, CVT software re-programming, or CVT replacement if the damage is extensive.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 (fluid flush) - $500 (reprogram) - $3000+ (replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found
*   **CVT Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Complete transmission failure, rendering the vehicle undriveable. Symptoms include loud noises, slipping, and an inability to accelerate.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the CVT.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $3000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Premature Strut/Shock Absorber Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Owners report that the struts and shocks wear out faster than expected, leading to a bouncy ride, poor handling, and excessive noise over bumps.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the affected struts/shocks.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600 per strut/shock (installed)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Battery Drain**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Battery drains quickly, especially if the vehicle is not driven for a few days. Can lead to the car not starting.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis of electrical system for parasitic drain. Possible replacement of battery.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100-$300 (battery) - $100+ (diagnosis and parasitic drain repair)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Faulty Airbag Sensor**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Airbag warning light illuminates on the dashboard, indicating a potential issue with the airbag system.
    *   Solution: Diagnostic scan to identify the specific sensor and replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200-$500
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*No Issues Found*

**Interior**

*   **Dashboard Cracking**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The dashboard, particularly in sun-exposed areas, may develop cracks over time.
    *   Solution: Dashboard replacement or use of a dashboard cover to conceal the cracks.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 (cover) - $1000+ (replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Failing Door Lock Actuators**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Door locks may become unresponsive, either failing to lock/unlock with the remote or manually.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the faulty door lock actuator.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150-$300 per door
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2019 Nissan Versa, while affordable and practical, does have some common issues that potential buyers should be aware of. The most concerning issues revolve around the CVT transmission, which can experience juddering, shaking, and in some cases, complete failure. Engine issues such as excessive oil consumption can also be costly to address. Electrical issues and interior wear are also noted. The severity and cost of these repairs can vary widely. Potential buyers should have a pre-purchase inspection performed by a qualified mechanic to assess the vehicle's condition and identify any potential problems before making a purchase.
