# 2016 Nissan Versa

### Overview
The 2016 Nissan Versa is a subcompact car known for its affordability and fuel efficiency. As a budget-friendly option, its reliability generally aligns with expectations for its class. While it offers a straightforward driving experience, certain areas have seen more attention from owners regarding potential issues. This analysis focuses on documented technical reliability concerns, excluding routine maintenance and normal wear and tear, to provide a clear picture of common problems owners might encounter. Understanding these potential issues can help prospective buyers and current owners make informed decisions regarding maintenance and repairs.

## Transmission

### Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)
**Hesitation or Jerking**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle may hesitate during acceleration or exhibit jerky movements, particularly at lower speeds or when shifting between simulated gears.
- **Solution:** Software updates for the transmission control module, or in more severe cases, transmission fluid flush and replacement, or internal component repair/replacement.
- **Cost:** $150 - $1,200

**Overheating**
- **Symptoms:** Transmission warning light may illuminate, accompanied by a burning smell or reduced performance.
- **Solution:** Check and top off transmission fluid, inspect for leaks, and ensure proper cooling system function. In some cases, transmission cooler may need cleaning or replacement.
- **Cost:** $200 - $800

## Electrical

### General Electrical System
**Infotainment System Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Touchscreen may become unresponsive, audio may cut out, or Bluetooth connectivity issues.
- **Solution:** Software updates for the infotainment system, or in some cases, a system reset or replacement of the unit.
- **Cost:** $100 - $700

**Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle may fail to start, or electrical components may malfunction intermittently due to a depleted battery.
- **Solution:** Test battery and charging system, inspect for parasitic draws from accessories or faulty modules.
- **Cost:** $100 - $400

### Summary Verdict
The 2016 Nissan Versa generally presents a reliable profile, particularly with its standard 1.6L engine and manual transmission. The CVT, while common for fuel efficiency, has seen some reports of hesitation and overheating, which can be costly to repair. Electrical gremlins, such as infotainment glitches and battery drain, are also noted but not considered widespread. The suspension, exterior, and interior components appear to be robust for the model year. Overall, the Versa is a dependable choice for budget-conscious buyers, with the CVT being the primary area to monitor for potential issues.
