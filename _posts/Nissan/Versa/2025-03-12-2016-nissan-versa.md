The 2016 Nissan Versa is a subcompact car known for its affordability and fuel efficiency. It typically comes with a 1.6-liter four-cylinder engine producing around 109 horsepower and 107 lb-ft of torque. Transmission options include a 5-speed manual (less common) and a Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT). Owners often appreciate its spacious interior for its class and its low price point. Common dislikes include its underwhelming performance, basic features, and somewhat unrefined driving experience.

**Engine**

**1.6L I4**

*   **Excessive Oil Consumption**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported that their 1.6L engine consumes oil at a higher than expected rate. Symptoms include the need to frequently add oil between scheduled oil changes, blue smoke from the exhaust, and a burning oil smell.
    *   Solution: The solution varies. Initially, some owners tried using thicker oil or more frequent oil changes. More drastic solutions include engine rebuild or replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $5,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Ignition Coil Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Ignition coils can fail, leading to misfires, rough idling, and a check engine light.
    *   Solution: Replacing the faulty ignition coil.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**CVT (Continuously Variable Transmission)**

*   **CVT Failure/Problems**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The most prevalent issue with the 2016 Nissan Versa is the CVT transmission failing or having problems. Symptoms include shuddering during acceleration, slipping, delayed engagement, and a complete loss of forward or reverse movement. A whining noise may also be present.
    *   Solution: In some cases, a software update or fluid change can temporarily alleviate the issue. However, complete CVT replacement is often required.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $3,000 - $5,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Juddering/Jerking**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners experience juddering or jerking, particularly at low speeds or during acceleration.
    *   Solution: A CVT fluid change *may* help temporarily but is not a permanent fix. CVT replacement is likely.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $3,000 - $5,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

No Issues Found

**Electrical**

*   **Battery Drain**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners report issues with the car battery draining quickly, especially if the car sits for a few days without being driven. This can lead to difficulty starting the vehicle.
    *   Solution: Test the battery and charging system. Replace the battery if necessary. Investigate for parasitic draws.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Sensor Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Various sensors such as the mass airflow sensor, oxygen sensors, and ABS sensors can fail.
    *   Solution: Replace the faulty sensor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $400+ per sensor
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

No Issues Found

**Interior**

*   **Air Conditioning Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The air conditioning system may fail to blow cold air, or the blower motor may stop working.
    *   Solution: Diagnose the A/C system to identify the faulty component (compressor, condenser, etc.) and replace it.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Power Window Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Power windows may fail to operate properly, or at all.
    *   Solution: Replacing the power window motor or regulator.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $400+ per window
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2016 Nissan Versa's most significant problem is the CVT transmission, which is prone to failure and can be a costly repair. Other issues include excessive oil consumption and electrical problems. While affordable, potential buyers should be aware of these potential maintenance costs. The severity of these issues range from minor inconvenience to complete vehicle failure, with repair costs ranging from a few hundred dollars to several thousand.
