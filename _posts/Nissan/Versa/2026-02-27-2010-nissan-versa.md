# 2010 Nissan Versa

### Overview
The 2010 Nissan Versa, a subcompact car, generally offers a budget-friendly ownership experience with a focus on practicality. While it's not known for groundbreaking performance or luxury, its reliability is a key selling point for many buyers. This analysis focuses on common mechanical and electrical issues reported by owners and technicians, excluding routine maintenance. The Versa's straightforward design contributes to its overall dependability, though like any vehicle, certain components can experience wear or defects over time. Understanding these potential problem areas can help owners anticipate and address issues proactively, ensuring a smoother ownership experience.

## Electrical

### Standard Electrical System
**Power Window Motor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Power windows operate slowly, intermittently, or stop working altogether.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the power window motor and regulator assembly.
- **Cost:** $250 - $500

**Faulty Ignition Coils**
- **Symptoms:** Check engine light illumination, rough idling, misfires, poor acceleration.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the affected ignition coil(s).
- **Cost:** $200 - $400

### Summary Verdict
The 2010 Nissan Versa is generally considered a reliable vehicle, particularly in its powertrain. The 1.6-liter engine and available transmissions (automatic and manual) are robust and typically do not present significant issues beyond normal wear. The most commonly reported problems tend to be in the electrical system, specifically with power window motors and occasional ignition coil failures, which are relatively common for many vehicles of this age and class. Suspension and exterior/interior components are largely free of widespread defects. Overall, the Versa offers a dependable option for budget-conscious buyers, with most potential issues being manageable and not indicative of systemic design flaws.
