**Introduction**

The 2016 Nissan Versa is a subcompact sedan that offers a fuel-efficient and affordable option for budget-minded drivers. It is available with either a 1.6-liter four-cylinder engine paired with a 5-speed manual or a continuously variable transmission (CVT), or a 1.8-liter four-cylinder engine paired with a CVT. The Versa has received positive reviews for its spacious interior, comfortable ride, and decent fuel economy, but it has also been criticized for its lack of power, poor sound insulation, and cheap interior materials.

**Engine**

* **1.6-Liter Four-Cylinder Engine with 5-Speed Manual Transmission**

  * **Valve Cover Gasket Leak**
    Description/Symptoms: Oil leaking from under the valve cover onto the engine block, causing an oil smell and smoke.
    Solution: Replacing the valve cover gasket.
    Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
    Recalls: None Found
  
  * **Timing Chain Tensioner Failure**
    Description/Symptoms: Abnormal engine noises, such as rattling or whining, especially when cold starting.
    Solution: Replacing the timing chain tensioner.
    Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
    Recalls: None Found

* **1.6-Liter Four-Cylinder Engine with CVT**

  * **Intake Manifold Gasket Leak**
    Description/Symptoms: Leaking coolant from the intake manifold gasket, resulting in overheating and engine performance issues.
    Solution: Replacing the intake manifold gasket.
    Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500
    Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

* **5-Speed Manual Transmission**

  * **Synchro Ring Failure**
    Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears, especially between 2nd and 3rd, and grinding noises during gear changes.
    Solution: Replacing the faulty synchro ring.
    Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800
    Recalls: None Found

* **CVT**

  * **Transmission Control Module (TCM) Failure**
    Description/Symptoms: Hesitation or jerking during acceleration, erratic gear changes, and check engine light illumination.
    Solution: Reprogramming or replacing the TCM.
    Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
    Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Front Strut Bearing Failure**
  Description/Symptoms: Clunking or rattling noise when going over bumps or turning, especially at low speeds.
  Solution: Replacing the front strut bearings.
  Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
  Recalls: None Found

* **Rear Shock Absorber Failure**
  Description/Symptoms: Excessive bouncing or swaying of the rear end, especially over uneven roads.
  Solution: Replacing the rear shock absorbers.
  Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600
  Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain**
  Description/Symptoms: Battery draining quickly, even when the car is not in use.
  Solution: Identifying and fixing the source of the drain.
  Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500
  Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Door Lock Actuator Failure**
  Description/Symptoms: Power door locks not working properly, with doors not locking or unlocking remotely or manually.
  Solution: Replacing the faulty door lock actuator.
  Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
  Recalls: None Found

* **Wiper Motor Failure**
  Description/Symptoms: Windshield wipers not working or working intermittently, leaving a streaky film on the windshield.
  Solution: Replacing the wiper motor.
  Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $300
  Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Dashboard Rattle**
  Description/Symptoms: Excessive rattling or squeaking from the dashboard area, especially over rough roads.
  Solution: Identifying and tightening loose components or applying sound deadening material.
  Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500
  Recalls: None Found

* **Seat Adjuster Failure**
  Description/Symptoms: Difficulty adjusting the driver's seat, especially the height and forward/back positions.
  Solution: Replacing the faulty seat adjuster motor or mechanism.
  Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600
  Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2016 Nissan Versa has several potential issues to watch out for, affecting different components such as the engine, transmission, suspension, electrical system, and interior. Some of these issues, such as valve cover gasket leaks and battery drain, are relatively minor and can be fixed at a reasonable cost. However, others, such as timing chain tensioner failure and CVT transmission problems, can be more serious and expensive to repair. Overall, the severity and cost of the potential issues with the Versa should be considered before purchasing this vehicle.
