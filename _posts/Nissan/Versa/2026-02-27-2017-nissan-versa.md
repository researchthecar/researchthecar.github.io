# 2017 Nissan Versa Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used Nissan Versa, prospective buyers should carefully weigh its budget-friendly appeal against reported mechanical and electrical concerns. While this compact sedan offers economical daily transportation, owner complaints highlight potential trouble spots across the powertrain, door locks, and safety systems that require thorough pre-purchase inspection.

---

## Engine

### 1.6L 4-Cylinder Engine

Powered by a 1.6-liter naturally aspirated 4-cylinder engine producing 109 horsepower, this option prioritizes fuel efficiency over performance. While generally straightforward, owners have reported occasional stalling and sensor-related starting issues.

**Engine Stalling and Starting Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may experience intermittent starting failures, rev up without engaging, or unexpectedly stall while driving.
- **Solution:** Diagnose fault codes, inspect camshaft sensors, and verify the condition of the engine control module (ECM) and wiring harness.

## Transmission

### Xtronic Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)

Paired exclusively with the CVT automatic transmission across standard front-wheel-drive trims, this setup helps maximize fuel economy but requires careful verification of fluid maintenance history to avoid costly failures.

**Transmission Failure and Sudden Deceleration**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may emit vacuum or whining noises, unexpectedly downshift at highway speeds, or experience total transmission failure resulting in sudden deceleration.
- **Solution:** Have the transmission thoroughly inspected by a qualified specialist; severely worn units may require professional overhaul or replacement.

## Exterior

### Door Lock Actuators

electrical issues primarily center around power door lock actuators failing to cycle properly, occasionally trapping occupants inside.

**Inoperable Door Latches**
- **Symptoms:** Door locks fail to respond to the key fob or interior switches, often producing a buzzing sound, and may become impossible to open manually.
- **Solution:** Replace the faulty door lock actuator assembly.

### Hood Latch Assembly

Interior and body hardware components generally hold up well, though hood latch reliability warrants a physical check before driving.

**Unexpected Hood Opening**
- **Symptoms:** The hood latch mechanism may fail while driving at speed, allowing the hood to fly open and obstruct the driver's view.
- **Solution:** Inspect and replace the primary and secondary hood latches if wear or misalignment is detected.

## Safety

### Seat Belts and Airbags

Safety equipment is critical to verify during a pre-purchase inspection, particularly ensuring proper latch engagement and functioning restraint warning lights.

**Seat Belt Latch and Airbag Deployment Concerns**
- **Symptoms:** Seat belt latches may feel loose or damaged, and some owners report airbags failing to deploy during heavy impacts.
- **Solution:** Have seat belt buckles and the supplemental restraint system (SRS) inspected and repaired by a certified technician.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V551000: Electrical System:Ignition](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=Versa&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** In these models, over time, the weight on the key ring and road conditions or some other jarring event may cause the ignition switch to move out of the run position to the accessory position, turning off the engine. If the vehicle stalls, it can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Nissan will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the production information on the ignition switch, and replace it as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V724000: Air Bags:Side/Window](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=Versa&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** A seam in the fabric section of the side curtain air bags may tear during side curtain air bag deployment, potentially affecting the performance of the air bag. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 226, "Ejection Mitigation", and number 214, "Side Impact Protection." In the.
- **What to do:** Nissan will notify owners, and dealers will replace the left and right side curtain air bags, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V275000: Air Bags:Frontal:Passenger Side:Inflator Module](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=Versa&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** The ring plate that secures the air bag cushion to the passenger frontal air bag assembly may fail, possibly causing the air bag to deploy improperly in the event of a crash. In the event of a crash, if the passenger frontal air bag does not deploy as intended, it can increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Nissan will notify owners, and dealers will replace the passenger frontal air bag module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The Nissan Versa can serve as an affordable commuter vehicle, but buyers must proceed with caution. Given potential risks involving CVT longevity, electrical door locks, and safety restraint components, a comprehensive pre-purchase inspection by an independent mechanic is strongly advised.
