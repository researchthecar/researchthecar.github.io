**Introduction:**

The 2019 Nissan Versa is a subcompact sedan that offers a spacious and comfortable interior with ample cargo capacity. It comes with a standard 1.6-liter inline-four engine that produces 122 horsepower and 114 lb-ft of torque, paired with a 5-speed manual transmission or a continuously variable transmission (CVT). The Versa also offers a 109-horsepower 1.6-liter inline-four engine with a CVT. Drivers appreciate the Versa's affordability, fuel efficiency, and practicality, but some have reported issues with its engine, suspension, and electrical system.

**Engine**

* **1.6L Inline-Four (5-Speed Manual)**
    * **Engine Misfires:**
        * Description/Symptoms: Intermittent engine misfires can occur during acceleration or under load.
        * Solution: Replace the spark plugs or ignition coils.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300
        * Recalls: N/A
* **1.6L Inline-Four (CVT)**
    * **Intake Valve Carbon Deposits:**
        * Description/Symptoms: Carbon buildup on the intake valves can cause reduced engine power, rough idle, and increased fuel consumption.
        * Solution: Clean the intake valves and throttle body.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $250 - $400
        * Recalls: N/A

**Transmission**

* **5-Speed Manual**
    * **Gear Grinding:**
        * Description/Symptoms: Grinding noises occur when shifting gears, especially when the transmission is cold.
        * Solution: Replace the synchronizers in the gearbox.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1000
        * Recalls: N/A
* **CVT**
    * **Transmission Shudder:**
        * Description/Symptoms: The transmission may shudder or vibrate during acceleration or deceleration.
        * Solution: Replace the CVT fluid and filter. In severe cases, the entire transmission may need to be replaced.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2000+
        * Recalls: N/A

**Suspension**

* **Front Suspension Strut Noise:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Squeaking or rattling noises from the front suspension when driving over bumps or uneven surfaces.
    * Solution: Replace the worn or damaged suspension struts.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500
    * Recalls: N/A

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The vehicle's battery drains quickly, even when the car is not in use.
    * Solution: Inspect the electrical system for loose connections or faulty components. Replace the battery if necessary.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300
    * Recalls: N/A
* **Alternator Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The alternator fails to charge the battery, causing electrical system problems and engine stalling.
    * Solution: Replace the faulty alternator.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600
    * Recalls: N/A

**Exterior**

* **Tail Light Condensation:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Water condensation forms inside the tail lights.
    * Solution: Replace the cracked or damaged taillight assembly.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200
    * Recalls: N/A

**Interior**

* **Dashboard Noise:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Rattling or squeaking noises from the dashboard when driving over bumps.
    * Solution: Apply foam or felt pads to the vibrating components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: DIY fix

**Summary:**

The 2019 Nissan Versa is a value-packed sedan with some common issues that can affect its engine, transmission, suspension, and electrical system. The severity of the issues varies, with some being minor annoyances and others requiring costly repairs. It is important to note that not all Versas will experience these problems, and the overall reliability of the vehicle is still considered to be average.
