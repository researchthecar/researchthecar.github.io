# 2023 Nissan Versa Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2023 Nissan Versa, prospective buyers will find an affordable and fuel-efficient subcompact sedan. However, owner reports submitted to safety authorities highlight several recurring mechanical and electrical concerns that warrant a careful inspection during a test drive. Reviewing the specific powertrain options and known trouble spots can help ensure a dependable ownership experience.

---

## Engine

### 1.6L 4-Cylinder Engine

The 1.6-liter naturally aspirated 4-cylinder engine produces 122 horsepower and 114 lb-ft of torque, providing adequate efficiency for daily commuting. While generally economical, some owners have reported unexpected stalling and reduced power incidents.

**Engine Stalling and Loss of Motive Power**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers report that the vehicle may suddenly stall when slowing down for a traffic light or experience a sudden loss of motive power where pressing the accelerator yields little to no acceleration. Restarting the vehicle or resetting the computer is sometimes required to restore normal operation.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis by a qualified technician is recommended to check for software updates, throttle body issues, or sensor faults.

## Transmission

### 5-Speed Manual Transmission

The 5-speed manual transmission is paired with the base trim, offering a traditional shifting experience for drivers who prefer three pedals.

**Clutch Stall and Reduced Power Mode**
- **Symptoms:** Owners note that after stalling the manual transmission in traffic or at an intersection, the vehicle can enter a reduced power or limp mode, requiring a full engine restart to clear.
- **Solution:** Practice careful clutch engagement to avoid unexpected stalls, and consult a dealer if persistent computer resets are needed.

### Xtronic Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)

The Xtronic CVT delivers smooth acceleration and maximizes fuel economy across highway and city driving, though buyers should ensure smooth operation during a test drive.

## Electrical

### Electrical Charging System

The electrical system manages modern convenience features and safety assists, though occasional battery drain issues have been noted by owners.

**Premature Battery Drain and Electrical Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Some drivers experience sudden battery failures requiring frequent jump-starts or total electrical power loss even when charging components test normally.
- **Solution:** Have the battery, alternator, and electrical draw tested by a professional mechanic to identify any underlying parasitic drains.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V154000: Air Bags:Frontal:Driver Side:Inflator Module](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=Versa&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** Due to a manufacturing issue, the driver's air bag inflator may absorb moisture, and cause the air bag to deploy improperly. An air bag that deploys improperly may not protect the driver as intended during a crash, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the front driver's air bag assembly, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2023 Nissan Versa remains an attractive, budget-friendly option with strong fuel economy, but potential buyers should thoroughly check for any history of engine stalling, electrical glitches, or transmission limp-mode behavior. Conducting an extended test drive and verifying service records can help mitigate these risks.
