**Introduction:**

The 1990 Nissan 240SX was a popular sports car known for its nimble handling and stylish design. It was available with a range of engine and transmission options, including:

- Engine: 2.4L KA24E inline-4 (140 hp, 152 lb-ft torque)
- Engine: 3.0L VG30E V6 (160 hp, 180 lb-ft torque)
- Transmission: 5-speed manual
- Transmission: 4-speed automatic

Drivers praised the 240SX for its responsive steering, engaging handling, and affordable price point. However, they also noted some common issues with the vehicle.

**Engine**

**2.4L KA24E**

- **Oil leaks:** Oil may leak from the timing chain cover gasket or oil pan gasket.
    - Solution: Replace the leaking gasket.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
    - Recalls: None Found
- **Timing belt failure:** The timing belt may fail prematurely, leading to catastrophic engine damage.
    - Solution: Replace the timing belt, tensioner, and water pump at the recommended intervals.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800+
    - Recalls: None Found
- **Valve cover gasket leak:** Oil may leak from the valve cover gasket.
    - Solution: Replace the valve cover gasket.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
    - Recalls: None Found

**3.0L VG30E**

- **Overheating:** The engine may overheat due to a faulty thermostat or water pump.
    - Solution: Replace the faulty component.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $400+
    - Recalls: None Found
- **Intake manifold gasket leak:** The intake manifold gasket may fail, causing vacuum leaks and rough idling.
    - Solution: Replace the intake manifold gasket.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
    - Recalls: None Found
- **Head gasket failure:** The head gasket may fail, causing coolant and oil leaks.
    - Solution: Replace the head gasket.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $1000 - $2000+
    - Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**5-Speed Manual**

- **Grinding gears:** The transmission may grind gears when shifting, particularly when cold.
    - Solution: Replace the synchronizers in the transmission.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1000+
    - Recalls: None Found
- **Clutch wear:** The clutch may wear prematurely, causing slipping or difficulty shifting.
    - Solution: Replace the clutch disc, pressure plate, and throw-out bearing.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $700+
    - Recalls: None Found

**4-Speed Automatic**

- **Shifting problems:** The transmission may shift erratically or refuse to shift gears.
    - Solution: Replace the transmission control module or other electrical components.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600+
    - Recalls: None Found
- **Transmission fluid leaks:** Transmission fluid may leak from the transmission pan gasket or seals.
    - Solution: Replace the leaking gasket or seal.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+
    - Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

- **Strut mounts:** The strut mounts may wear out, causing clunking or knocking noises when driving over bumps.
    - Solution: Replace the strut mounts.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300+
    - Recalls: None Found
- **Sway bar end links:** The sway bar end links may fail, causing a rattling noise when driving over uneven surfaces.
    - Solution: Replace the sway bar end links.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
    - Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

- **Alternator failure:** The alternator may fail, causing the battery to discharge and the vehicle to stall.
    - Solution: Replace the alternator.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
    - Recalls: None Found
- **Starter failure:** The starter may fail, preventing the engine from starting.
    - Solution: Replace the starter.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
    - Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

- **Rust:** The body of the 240SX may develop rust, particularly in areas exposed to moisture.
    - Solution: Address rust by painting or replacing affected body panels.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the extent of the rust.
    - Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

- **Power window failure:** The power window motors or switches may fail, causing the windows to become inoperable.
    - Solution: Replace the faulty motor or switch.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $400+
    - Recalls: None Found
- **Dashboard cracking:** The dashboard may crack or warp over time due to heat and UV exposure.
    - Solution: Replace the dashboard.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
    - Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

The 1990 Nissan 240SX is a reliable and affordable sports car with a few common issues. Engine problems such as oil leaks and timing belt failure can be costly to repair, but they can be prevented with regular maintenance. Transmission issues are generally less severe and less expensive to fix. The suspension and electrical systems are generally reliable, but they may require occasional repairs. The body of the 240SX can develop rust, so it's important to inspect it regularly and address any rust spots promptly. Overall, the 240SX is a good choice for those looking for a fun and engaging driving experience, but it's important to be aware of its potential issues and be prepared for occasional repairs.
