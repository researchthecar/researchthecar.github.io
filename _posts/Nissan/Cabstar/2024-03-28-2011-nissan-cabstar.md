**Introduction to the 2011 Nissan Cabstar**

The 2011 Nissan Cabstar is a light-duty commercial vehicle available with a range of engine and transmission options. It is powered by a 2.5-liter dCi diesel engine producing 129 horsepower and 210 lb-ft of torque. Transmission options include a 5-speed manual, 6-speed manual, and 4-speed automatic. The Cabstar has a payload capacity of up to 2.2 tons and is well-regarded for its reliability and fuel efficiency. However, it has faced some issues reported by owners.

**Engine**

* **2.5-liter dCi**
    * **Turbocharger failure**
        * Symptoms: Loss of power, increased engine noise, reduced fuel efficiency
        * Solution: Replace turbocharger
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
    * **Fuel injector failure**
        * Symptoms: Hard starting, rough idling, reduced power
        * Solution: Replace fuel injector(s)
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500 per injector
    * **EGR valve failure**
        * Symptoms: Reduced engine power, increased emissions
        * Solution: Clean or replace EGR valve
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500

**Transmission**

* **5-speed Manual**
    * **Clutch failure**
        * Symptoms: Slipping or chattering clutch, difficulty shifting gears
        * Solution: Replace clutch
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800
* **6-speed Manual**
    * **No significant issues found**
* **4-speed Automatic**
    * **Transmission slipping**
        * Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears, loss of power
        * Solution: Rebuild or replace transmission
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,000+

**Suspension**

* **Front suspension**
    * **Ball joint wear**
        * Symptoms: Clunking or popping sounds from front suspension, reduced stability
        * Solution: Replace ball joint(s)
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400 per joint
* **Rear suspension**
    * **Leaf spring breakage**
        * Symptoms: Sagging rear end, increased load capacity
        * Solution: Replace leaf spring(s)
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000 per spring

**Electrical**

* **Alternator failure**
    * Symptoms: Dim headlights, flickering dash lights, difficulty starting
    * Solution: Replace alternator
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
* **Starter motor failure**
    * Symptoms: Difficulty starting or intermittent starting issues
    * Solution: Replace starter motor
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500

**Exterior**

* **Door lock failure**
    * Symptoms: Central locking does not work, door locks do not engage/disengage
    * Solution: Replace door lock actuator(s)
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300 per actuator

**Interior**

* **Dashboard cracking**
    * Symptoms: Cracks or splits on the dashboard
    * Solution: Replace dashboard
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
* **Climate control failure**
    * Symptoms: Blower does not work, temperature or fan speed does not change
    * Solution: Replace climate control module or blower motor
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
* **Seat adjustment failure**
    * Symptoms: Difficulty adjusting seat position
    * Solution: Replace seat adjustment motor or track
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500

**Recalls**

* NHTSA Recall Number: 14V527000: Fuel Pump Failure (2011-2012 Models)
* NHTSA Recall Number: 15V320000: Brake Line Failure (2011-2015 Models)
* NHTSA Recall Number: 16V501000: Airbag Deployment Failure (2011-2014 Models)

**Summary**

The 2011 Nissan Cabstar has faced some issues, particularly with the engine and transmission. However, most of these issues are relatively minor and can be resolved at a reasonable cost. The vehicle is still regarded as reliable and efficient, but potential buyers should be aware of these reported issues to make an informed decision.
