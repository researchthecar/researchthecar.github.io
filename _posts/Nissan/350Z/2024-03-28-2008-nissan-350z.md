**Introduction**

The 2008 Nissan 350Z is a two-seat sports car available with a 3.5-liter V6 engine and a choice of a 6-speed manual or 5-speed automatic transmission. The V6 engine produces 306 horsepower and 268 lb-ft of torque. The 350Z is praised for its nimble handling, powerful engine, and stylish exterior. However, it has received some criticism for its stiff ride, limited interior space, and high fuel consumption.

**Engine**

* **3.5L V6**
    * **Valve Body Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: Transmission may slip, hesitate or refuse to shift properly
        * Solution: Replacement of the valve body
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
        * Recalls: None Found
    * **Timing Chain Tensioner Wear**
        * Description/Symptoms: Engine may make a rattling or grinding noise on startup
        * Solution: Replacement of the timing chain tensioner
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
        * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

* **6-Speed Manual**
    * **Shifter Bushing Wear**
        * Description/Symptoms: Shifter may feel loose and imprecise
        * Solution: Replacement of the shifter bushings
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
        * Recalls: None Found
* **5-Speed Automatic**
    * **Torque Converter Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: Transmission may shudder, slip or overheat
        * Solution: Replacement of the torque converter
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Rear Sway Bar End Links**
    * Description/Symptoms: Clunking or rattling noise from the rear suspension
    * Solution: Replacement of the sway bar end links
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Front Control Arm Bushings**
    * Description/Symptoms: Squealing or popping noise from the front suspension
    * Solution: Replacement of the control arm bushings
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Alternator Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Electrical system may fail, causing the battery to drain
    * Solution: Replacement of the alternator
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Battery Terminal Corrosion**
    * Description/Symptoms: Difficulty starting the vehicle, electrical system may not function properly
    * Solution: Cleaning of the battery terminals
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $100
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Sunroof Panel Malfunction**
    * Description/Symptoms: Sunroof may not open or close properly
    * Solution: Repair or replacement of the sunroof motor or gears
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Speaker Buzzing**
    * Description/Symptoms: Speakers may produce a buzzing or crackling sound
    * Solution: Replacement of the affected speakers
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2008 Nissan 350Z is a generally reliable sports car, but it has some potential issues that owners should be aware of. The most common problems reported are related to the valve body in the automatic transmission, the timing chain tensioner in the engine, and end links in the suspension. While these issues can be costly to repair, they are not overly common. Overall, the 350Z is a well-built and enjoyable car that is sure to provide years of driving pleasure.
