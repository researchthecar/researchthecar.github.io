# 2006 Nissan 350Z

### Overview
The 2006 Nissan 350Z, a popular sports car, is generally known for its robust mechanicals, particularly its V6 engine. However, like any vehicle, it has specific areas that have seen more attention from owners regarding reliability. This analysis focuses on documented common issues beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential concerns for prospective buyers or current owners. The focus is on components that have shown a propensity for failure or require specific attention to maintain optimal performance and longevity.

---


## Engine

### 3.5L VQ35DE V6
**Oil Consumption**
- **Symptoms:** Increased oil level drops between changes, blue smoke from exhaust under acceleration.
- **Solution:** Monitor oil levels regularly, consider engine rebuild or replacement if severe.
- **Cost:** $1,500 - $4,000+

**Crankshaft Position Sensor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Engine stalling, difficulty starting, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Replace crankshaft position sensor.
- **Cost:** $150 - $300

**Variable Valve Timing (VVT) Actuator Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Rattling noise from the front of the engine, especially on startup; check engine light with VVT codes.
- **Solution:** Replace VVT solenoids and potentially actuators.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1,200


## Transmission

### 6-Speed Manual Transmission
**Clutch Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Slipping clutch, difficulty shifting gears, burning smell.
- **Solution:** Replace clutch assembly (disc, pressure plate, release bearing).
- **Cost:** $800 - $1,500

**Synchronizer Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Grinding noise when shifting, difficulty engaging gears.
- **Solution:** Transmission rebuild or replacement.
- **Cost:** $2,000 - $4,000+

## Electrical

### General Electrical System
**Window Motor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Power windows not operating, slow window operation.
- **Solution:** Replace window regulator and motor assembly.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600

**Headlight Condensation**
- **Symptoms:** Moisture or fogging inside headlight assemblies.
- **Solution:** Seal headlight assemblies or replace.
- **Cost:** $200 - $500

### Summary Verdict
The 2006 Nissan 350Z is a mechanically sound sports car, with its VQ35DE V6 engine being a strong point. However, owners should be aware of potential oil consumption issues, especially on higher-mileage examples, and the possibility of crankshaft position sensor or VVT actuator problems. Manual transmissions can experience clutch wear and, less commonly, synchronizer issues. Electrical components like window motors and headlight seals are minor concerns. Overall, with diligent maintenance and awareness of these common points, the 350Z offers a reliable and engaging driving experience.
