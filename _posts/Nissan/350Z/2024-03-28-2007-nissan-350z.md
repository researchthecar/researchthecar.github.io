**Introduction:**

The 2007 Nissan 350Z is a popular sports car known for its sleek design, powerful engine, and agile handling. It is available with two engine and transmission options:

* **3.5L V6 engine** with a 6-speed manual transmission (306 horsepower, 268 lb-ft of torque)
* **3.5L V6 engine** with a 5-speed automatic transmission (293 horsepower, 262 lb-ft of torque)

Owners appreciate the 350Z's thrilling performance, responsive steering, and attractive styling. However, it has also been reported to have some issues that can affect its reliability and functionality.

**Engine**

**3.5L V6 Engine (Manual Transmission)**

* **Valve Cover Gasket Leak:** Leaking valve cover gaskets can cause oil leaks and possible engine damage.
    * Solution: Replace valve cover gaskets.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $600
* **Cam Chain Tensioner Failure:** A faulty cam chain tensioner can lead to premature wear on the cam chain and possible engine failure.
    * Solution: Replace cam chain tensioner.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $1,500

**3.5L V6 Engine (Automatic Transmission)**

* **Transmission Filter Failure:** A faulty transmission filter can lead to premature transmission wear and failure.
    * Solution: Replace transmission filter and fluid.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $300

**Transmission**

**6-Speed Manual Transmission**

* **Clutch Slave Cylinder Failure:** A failing clutch slave cylinder can cause difficulty shifting gears or prevent the clutch from engaging properly.
    * Solution: Replace clutch slave cylinder.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $700

**5-Speed Automatic Transmission**

* **Solenoid Pack Failure:** A faulty solenoid pack can lead to shifting problems, such as delayed or hard shifts.
    * Solution: Replace solenoid pack.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $1,500
* **Torque Converter Shudder:** Torque converter shudder can cause vibrations during acceleration or gear changes.
    * Solution: Replace torque converter.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,000

**Suspension**

* **Rear Suspension Bushing Wear:** Worn rear suspension bushings can cause excessive noise and poor handling.
    * Solution: Replace suspension bushings.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain:** A parasitic battery drain can quickly deplete the battery, especially when the vehicle is parked.
    * Solution: Diagnose and repair the cause of the drain.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the cause of the drain.
* **Alternator Failure:** A failing alternator cannot provide sufficient power to the vehicle's electrical system.
    * Solution: Replace alternator.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800

**Exterior**

* **Door Lock Actuator Failure:** A faulty door lock actuator can prevent the door from locking or unlocking properly.
    * Solution: Replace door lock actuator.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300

**Interior**

* **Climate Control Malfunction:** The climate control system may not function properly due to a faulty blend door actuator or other components.
    * Solution: Diagnose and repair the faulty component.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $1,000

**Summary**

The 2007 Nissan 350Z is generally a reliable sports car, but it has been reported to have some potential issues that can affect its performance and functionality. While the severity of these issues varies, some can be costly to fix. Potential buyers should be aware of these issues and consider having the vehicle inspected by a qualified mechanic before purchasing.
