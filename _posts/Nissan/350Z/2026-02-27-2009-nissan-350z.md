# 2009 Nissan 350Z

### Overview
The 2009 Nissan 350Z, a popular sports car known for its performance and engaging driving dynamics, generally exhibits good reliability for its class. While it's a performance-oriented vehicle, many owners report a solid ownership experience with fewer major mechanical concerns than some competitors. However, like any vehicle, specific components can experience issues over time, particularly those related to its performance-focused nature. This data focuses on documented, non-wear-and-tear related problems that have been reported by owners and technicians, providing a technical overview for potential buyers or current owners.

---


## Engine

### 3.5L V6 (VQ35HR)
**Oil Consumption**
- **Symptoms:** Increased oil level checks required, potential blue smoke from exhaust under certain conditions.
- **Solution:** Monitor oil levels regularly. In severe cases, engine rebuild or replacement may be necessary.
- **Cost:** $1,500 - $4,000+

**Camshaft Position Sensor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Check Engine Light illumination, rough idling, potential stalling.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the faulty camshaft position sensor.
- **Cost:** $150 - $300


## Transmission

### 6-Speed Manual
**Clutch Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Slipping clutch, difficulty shifting gears, burning smell.
- **Solution:** Clutch replacement (clutch kit including pressure plate, disc, and release bearing).
- **Cost:** $800 - $1,500

**Synchronizer Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Grinding noise when shifting, difficulty engaging gears.
- **Solution:** Transmission disassembly and replacement of worn synchronizers.
- **Cost:** $1,500 - $3,000+

## Electrical

### General Electrical System
**Window Motor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Power windows not operating, slow window operation.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the power window motor and regulator assembly.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600

**Headlight Bulb Failure**
- **Symptoms:** One or both headlights not illuminating.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the headlight bulbs.
- **Cost:** $50 - $150

### Summary Verdict
The 2009 Nissan 350Z is a relatively reliable sports car, especially considering its performance focus. The VQ35HR engine is generally robust, though some owners have reported oil consumption and occasional sensor issues. Manual transmissions can experience clutch wear and, less commonly, synchronizer problems, which are typical for high-performance vehicles. Automatic transmissions tend to be more trouble-free. Electrical components like window motors are the most common non-wear-related failures. Overall, with proper maintenance, the 350Z offers a dependable platform for spirited driving, with most potential issues being manageable and not indicative of systemic design flaws.
