# 2021 Nissan Murano

### Overview
The 2021 Nissan Murano is a mid-size SUV known for its comfortable ride, upscale interior, and distinctive styling. Reliability data for the 2021 model year indicates a generally solid performance, with most owners reporting satisfaction. However, like any vehicle, there are specific areas that have seen more attention from repair shops and owner forums. This analysis focuses on documented technical issues beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential concerns for owners and prospective buyers. The Murano's powertrain and electrical systems are the primary focus for potential issues, though many owners experience trouble-free operation.

---


## Engine

### 3.5L V6 (VQ35DD)
**Oil Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Visible oil spots under the vehicle, low oil level warnings, burning oil smell.
- **Solution:** Replacement of leaking gaskets (e.g., valve cover, oil pan) or seals.
- **Cost:** $300 - $700

**Coolant Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Overheating, visible coolant puddles, sweet smell from engine bay, low coolant level.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of hoses, radiator, water pump, or head gasket.
- **Cost:** $400 - $1200


## Transmission

### CVT (Continuously Variable Transmission)
**Hesitation or Jerking**
- **Symptoms:** Transmission feels like it's slipping, jerky acceleration, delayed engagement.
- **Solution:** Software update, transmission fluid flush and refill, or in severe cases, transmission replacement.
- **Cost:** $500 - $3500

**Overheating**
- **Symptoms:** Transmission warning light, reduced engine power, burning smell.
- **Solution:** Transmission fluid cooler inspection/replacement, transmission fluid flush.
- **Cost:** $400 - $1000

## Electrical

### Infotainment System
**Screen Freezing or Unresponsive**
- **Symptoms:** Touchscreen becomes unresponsive, system freezes, glitches in display.
- **Solution:** Software update, infotainment unit reset, or replacement of the unit.
- **Cost:** $300 - $1500


### Battery Drain
**Premature Battery Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle fails to start, battery warning light, electrical components malfunctioning.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis of parasitic draw, replacement of faulty module, or battery replacement.
- **Cost:** $200 - $600

## Interior

### Climate Control System
**AC/Heater Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** Air conditioning not blowing cold, heater not producing heat, unusual noises from vents.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and repair/replacement of blower motor, blend door actuators, or refrigerant recharge.
- **Cost:** $300 - $800


### Seat Actuators
**Power Seat Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Power seats fail to move forward, backward, or adjust height.
- **Solution:** Replacement of seat motor or control module.
- **Cost:** $400 - $900

### Summary Verdict
The 2021 Nissan Murano presents a generally reliable profile, with its 3.5L V6 engine being a robust unit. The primary areas of concern tend to be the CVT transmission, which can exhibit hesitation or overheating issues, and certain electrical components like the infotainment screen and potential battery drain. Interior climate control and power seat functions have also seen some reports of malfunction. While these issues are documented, they do not appear to be universal, and many owners enjoy a trouble-free experience. Regular maintenance, especially for the CVT fluid, is recommended to mitigate potential problems. Overall, the Murano offers a comfortable and stylish package with a moderate level of expected technical upkeep.
