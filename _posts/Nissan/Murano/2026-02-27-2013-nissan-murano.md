# 2013 Nissan Murano

### Overview
The 2013 Nissan Murano is a mid-size crossover SUV known for its comfortable ride and distinctive styling. Generally, it offers a solid ownership experience with a few areas that warrant attention. Reliability is largely dependent on maintenance history and specific component wear. While many owners report satisfaction, some common issues have been noted, particularly concerning the continuously variable transmission (CVT) and certain engine components. This analysis focuses on documented technical issues beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential repair costs and common symptoms to help prospective and current owners.

---


## Engine

### 3.5L V6 (VQ35DE)
**Oil Leaks (Valve Covers/Gaskets)**
- **Symptoms:** Visible oil on engine components, burning oil smell, low oil level.
- **Solution:** Replacement of valve cover gaskets or other related seals.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600

**Coolant Leaks (Intake Manifold Gasket)**
- **Symptoms:** Sweet smell of coolant, white smoke from exhaust, engine overheating, coolant visible in the engine bay.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the intake manifold gasket.
- **Cost:** $400 - $700


## Transmission

### CVT (Continuously Variable Transmission)
**Hesitation/Jerking**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle hesitates during acceleration, jerky movements, delayed engagement.
- **Solution:** Transmission fluid flush and filter replacement, or in severe cases, transmission rebuild or replacement.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1,500 (for service/flush), $3,000 - $6,000 (for replacement)

**Overheating**
- **Symptoms:** Transmission warning light, burning smell, loss of power.
- **Solution:** Check and top off transmission fluid, inspect cooling system, or transmission repair/replacement.
- **Cost:** $200 - $500 (for fluid/cooling check), $3,000 - $6,000 (for replacement)

## Electrical

### Standard System
**Key Fob Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Key fob not recognized, doors not locking/unlocking remotely.
- **Solution:** Battery replacement in key fob, reprogramming, or replacement of key fob.
- **Cost:** $50 - $250

**Infotainment System Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Screen freezing, unresponsive touch controls, audio issues.
- **Solution:** Software update, system reset, or replacement of the infotainment unit.
- **Cost:** $100 - $500 (for software/reset), $500 - $1,500 (for replacement)

### Summary Verdict
The 2013 Nissan Murano presents a generally reliable package, with its robust 3.5L V6 engine being a strong point. However, potential buyers and current owners should be aware of the common concerns surrounding the CVT, which can lead to significant repair costs if issues arise. Electrical gremlins, such as key fob problems and infotainment glitches, are also noted but are typically less severe. Overall, with diligent maintenance, particularly for the transmission, the Murano can be a dependable vehicle. Addressing potential oil and coolant leaks proactively can also prevent more costly engine repairs down the line.
