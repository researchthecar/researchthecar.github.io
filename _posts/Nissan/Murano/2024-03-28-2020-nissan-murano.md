**Introduction:**

The 2020 Nissan Murano is a popular midsize SUV known for its spacious interior, comfortable ride, and safety features. It offers two engine options: a 3.5-liter V6 producing 260 horsepower and 240 lb-ft of torque, and a hybrid powertrain combining a 2.5-liter inline-four engine with an electric motor for a combined 250 horsepower. Transmission options include a continuously variable transmission (CVT) and a 9-speed automatic transmission.

Despite its overall positive reviews, the 2020 Murano has encountered some issues reported by owners.

**Engine**

* **3.5-Liter V6**
    * **Oil Leak:** Leakage from the oil cooler lines or oil filter housing. Symptoms include a burning oil smell and low oil levels. Solution: Replace faulty lines or housing gasket. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * **Timing Chain Noise:** Excessive noise from the timing chain or tensioner. Solution: Replace timing chain and tensioner components. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
* **2.5-Liter Hybrid**
    * **No Issues Found**

**Transmission**

* **CVT**
    * **Hesitation or Jerking:** Transmission slips or hesitates during acceleration or shifting. Solution: Update transmission software or replace valve body. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+
* **9-Speed Automatic**
    * **Shifting Issues:** Transmission has difficulty shifting smoothly or gets stuck in gear. Solution: Replace transmission control module or clutch packs. Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+

**Suspension**

* **No Issues Found**

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain:** Battery discharges quickly even when the vehicle is not in use. Solution: Replace battery or diagnose electrical system for parasitic drain. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+
* **Headlight Condensation:** Moisture accumulates inside the headlight assemblies. Solution: Replace headlight assemblies or reseal existing ones. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+

**Exterior**

* **Sunroof Leakage:** Water leaks into the cabin from the sunroof. Solution: Replace sunroof seal or adjust sunroof alignment. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+
* **Rear Hatch Liftgate Issue:** Liftgate opens or closes erratically or fails to operate entirely. Solution: Replace liftgate struts, hinges, or latch. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+

**Interior**

* **Infotainment System Glitch:** Touchscreen becomes unresponsive or freezes. Solution: Update software or replace infotainment system. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+
* **Door Lock Malfunction:** Door locks fail to unlock or lock properly. Solution: Replace door lock actuators or harnesses. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+
* **Seat Heater Failure:** Seat heaters do not warm up properly or fail to operate. Solution: Replace seat heater elements or wiring harness. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,000+

**Summary:**

The 2020 Nissan Murano has had some issues reported, primarily with the engine, transmission, and electrical systems. Some issues, such as oil leaks and timing chain noise, can be severe and expensive to fix. Others, such as seat heater failure or door lock malfunctions, are less serious but can still be annoying. Overall, the Murano's reliability is average compared to other midsize SUVs.
