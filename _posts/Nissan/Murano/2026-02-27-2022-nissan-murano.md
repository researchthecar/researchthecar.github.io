# 2022 Nissan Murano

### Overview
The 2022 Nissan Murano is a mid-size SUV that generally offers a comfortable ride and a well-appointed interior. Reliability data for the 2022 model year indicates a solid performance, with most reported issues being minor or isolated incidents rather than widespread problems. The powertrain, particularly the V6 engine and CVT transmission, has a history of decent durability, though like many CVTs, long-term maintenance is key. Electrical systems and interior components are largely dependable, with few recurring complaints. Exterior elements also tend to hold up well under normal usage. Overall, the Murano presents itself as a reliable choice in its segment, especially when routine maintenance is followed.

### CVT (Continuously Variable Transmission)
**Hesitation or Jerking**
- **Symptoms:** Slight hesitation during acceleration or occasional jerky shifts, particularly at lower speeds.
- **Solution:** Software updates, transmission fluid flush and replacement, or in severe cases, transmission replacement.
- **Cost:** $200 - $600 for fluid service; $3,000 - $7,000 for replacement.

### Summary Verdict
The 2022 Nissan Murano demonstrates good overall reliability, with its primary powertrain components showing resilience. The most commonly cited potential issue revolves around the CVT transmission, which can occasionally exhibit minor hesitations or jerking, though this is often addressable with maintenance or software updates. Electrical systems, particularly the infotainment, may experience occasional glitches but are generally stable. Other areas like suspension, exterior, and interior components are largely free of widespread problems. With regular maintenance, the 2022 Murano is expected to be a dependable vehicle for its owners.
