# 2016 Nissan Murano Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2016 Nissan Murano offers a stylish exterior, comfortable interior, and a smooth ride, making it a popular midsize crossover choice. However, prospective buyers should review recurring owner complaints regarding mechanical and electrical systems. While many owners enjoy its comfort and efficiency, being aware of known trouble spots can help you inspect a used model more thoroughly before purchasing.

---

## Engine

### 3.5L V6 Engine

Powered by a 3.5-liter V6 engine producing approximately 260 horsepower and 240 lb-ft of torque, providing strong and smooth acceleration. The engine itself is generally robust, though occasional electrical and auxiliary component glitches have been reported by owners.

**Electrical Warning Lights and Drivetrain Interruption**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers report illuminated check engine or warning lights, occasionally accompanied by accessory glitches or sudden power loss.
- **Solution:** Diagnose fault codes and inspect engine sensors, belts, and pulleys for wear.

## Transmission

### Xtronic Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)

Equipped with an Xtronic Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT) paired with front-wheel drive or optional all-wheel drive. Buyers should carefully evaluate transmission responsiveness during a test drive, as CVT durability is a known focal point for this generation.

**CVT Pulley and Belt Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle loses power on the highway or fails to accelerate properly, often accompanied by diagnostic codes indicating internal CVT pulley or belt issues.
- **Solution:** Professional transmission inspection; replacement of the CVT assembly may be required if internal mechanical damage occurs.

## Brakes

### ABS Actuator

Brake system complaints occasionally highlight ABS actuator issues that trigger warning lights and require professional module diagnosis.

**ABS Actuator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The brake and ABS warning lights stay illuminated on the instrument cluster, and anti-lock braking functionality may be compromised during sudden stops.
- **Solution:** Have the ABS control module and actuator inspected and replaced by a qualified technician if internal valve failure is confirmed.

## Exterior

### Sunroof and Glass

Exterior panels and visibility are generally standard, though isolated incidents of spontaneous glass breakage have been reported with the panoramic sunroof.

**Unexpected Sunroof Shattering**
- **Symptoms:** The panoramic sunroof glass shatters spontaneously while driving at highway speeds without apparent external impact.
- **Solution:** Replace the shattered glass panel and inspect the frame alignment and seal integrity.

## Interior

### Heated Seats

The interior features comfortable Zero Gravity seating, though the heated seat control modules have generated owner reports concerning electrical melting and failure.

**Heated Seat Module Overheating**
- **Symptoms:** The driver's seat heater fails to work or, in more severe cases, experiences overheating and shorting that can melt surrounding plastic harness connectors.
- **Solution:** Inspect the seat heater control module and wiring harness underneath the seat; replace damaged switches, modules, or wiring connectors.

## Safety

### Air Bag System

Safety system complaints primarily center around persistent airbag warning indicators and occasional diagnostic fault codes within the restraint electronics.

**Airbag Warning Light and Deployment Concerns**
- **Symptoms:** Airbag warning lights remain illuminated on the dashboard, or owners report failure of airbags to deploy as expected during collisions.
- **Solution:** Scan the Supplemental Restraint System (SRS) module for diagnostic trouble codes and inspect seat belt pretensioners and sensors.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V601000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Antilock/Traction Control/Electronic Limited Slip](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=Murano&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** A brake fluid leak on the circuit board may result in an electrical short, increasing the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Nissan will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the serial number on the ABS actuator, replacing it as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V636000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Antilock/Traction Control/Electronic Limited Slip](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=Murano&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** A brake fluid leak onto the circuit board may result in an electrical short, increasing the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Nissan will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the ABS Actuator, replacing it as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V193000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Antilock/Traction Control/Electronic Limited Slip](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=Murano&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** Insufficient brake fluid pressure may lengthen the distance needed to stop the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Nissan will notify owners, and dealers will inspect and replace the ABS actuator, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V244000: Air Bags:Sensor:Occupant Classification](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=Murano&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** As a result, the passenger frontal air bag may be turned off and not deploy in the event of a crash. If the passenger frontal air bag does not deploy as intended in the event of a crash, the passenger is at an increased risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will reprogram the Air Bag Control Unit (ACU) and OCS Electronic Control Unit (ECU) in Altima, Maxima, Murano, Rogue, and Sentra vehicles, and replace the OCS ECU in LEAF, NV200, NV200 Taxi, Pathfinder, Infiniti Q50, JX35, and QX60. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V807000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Antilock/Traction Control/Electronic Limited Slip](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=Murano&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** A brake fluid leak on the circuit board may result in an electrical short, increasing the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Nissan will notify owners, and dealers will replace the ABS actuator, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

When shopping for a used 2016 Nissan Murano, prioritize a thorough pre-purchase inspection that focuses on the CVT transmission's operation, the ABS braking system, and the electrical connectors under the heated seats. While it offers strong comfort and performance, verifying service records and checking for unresolved warning lights will help ensure a more dependable ownership experience.
