**Introduction**

The 2021 Nissan Murano is a midsize SUV that offers a comfortable and spacious interior, along with a potent engine and a smooth ride. It comes with a 3.5L V6 engine that produces 260 horsepower and 240 lb-ft of torque, paired with a continuously variable transmission (CVT).

Despite its overall reliability, the 2021 Nissan Murano has a few reported issues that drivers should be aware of.

**Engine**

* **3.5L V6 Engine**
    * **Excessive Oil Consumption**
        * Description/Symptoms: Some owners report experiencing excessive oil consumption, requiring frequent top-ups between oil changes.
        * Solution: The issue can be resolved by replacing the piston rings or performing an engine overhaul.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
        * Recalls: None Found
    * **Timing Chain Noise**
        * Description/Symptoms: A rattling or ticking noise may be heard from the engine, particularly during startup or acceleration.
        * Solution: Replacing the timing chain and tensioner is necessary to address the noise.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $1,500+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

* **Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)**
    * **Hesitation and Jerking**
        * Description/Symptoms: Drivers report experiencing hesitation or jerking during acceleration or shifting.
        * Solution: Updating the transmission control module software or replacing the transmission fluid may resolve the issue.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Front Lower Control Arm Bushing Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: The front lower control arm bushings may wear out prematurely, leading to vibrations, noise, and decreased handling.
        * Solution: Replacing the worn bushings is necessary to resolve the issue.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $600+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain**
        * Description/Symptoms: The battery may drain prematurely, causing the vehicle to fail to start.
        * Solution: Identifying and replacing the faulty component causing the drain, such as a parasitic draw from aftermarket accessories or a faulty electrical module.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Rear Bumper Paint Peeling**
        * Description/Symptoms: The paint on the rear bumper may peel or chip, particularly around the edges.
        * Solution: Repainting or replacing the rear bumper is necessary to resolve the issue.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Climate Control Malfunction**
        * Description/Symptoms: The climate control system may malfunction, resulting in inadequate heating or cooling.
        * Solution: Replacing the climate control actuator or other faulty components may be necessary.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
        * Recalls: None Found
* **Infotainment System Freezing**
        * Description/Symptoms: The infotainment system may freeze or become unresponsive.
        * Solution: Updating the software or replacing the infotainment unit may resolve the issue.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

Overall, the 2021 Nissan Murano is a reliable vehicle with a comfortable interior and impressive performance. However, it is not without its issues, with engine oil consumption and transmission problems being the most common. The cost of repairs can vary depending on the severity of the issue, but some can be relatively expensive. It is advisable for owners to be aware of these potential concerns before purchasing or owning a 2021 Nissan Murano.
