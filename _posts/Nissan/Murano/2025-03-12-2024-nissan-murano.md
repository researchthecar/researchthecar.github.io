The 2024 Nissan Murano is a mid-size SUV powered by a 3.5-liter V6 engine producing 260 horsepower and 240 lb-ft of torque, paired with a Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT). It offers a comfortable ride, a stylish interior, and a good amount of standard safety features. However, some common complaints include the CVT transmission's performance and responsiveness, as well as dated infotainment technology compared to newer competitors. People appreciate the Murano's comfortable seats and smooth ride, while some dislike its less engaging driving experience and smaller cargo space.

**Engine**

**3.5L V6**

*   **Oil Consumption**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported excessive oil consumption requiring more frequent oil changes and top-offs between scheduled maintenance.
    *   Solution: Monitor oil levels regularly and address any leaks. In more severe cases, piston ring replacement may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix:
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**CVT (Continuously Variable Transmission)**

*   **Hesitation and Jerking**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Hesitation during acceleration, jerky transitions between speeds, and a general lack of responsiveness.
    *   Solution: Software update for the CVT control module. In severe cases, CVT replacement may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix:
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Premature Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: In some cases, the CVT transmission has been reported to fail prematurely, requiring a complete replacement. This is often preceded by unusual noises and slipping.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the CVT Transmission.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix:
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Freezing/Crashing**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The infotainment screen freezes, becomes unresponsive, or crashes requiring a system reboot.
    *   Solution: Software update for the infotainment system or replacement of the unit if the problem persists.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix:
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Battery Drain**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The battery drains quickly, especially when the car is not driven for a few days. This can lead to difficulty starting the vehicle.
    *   Solution: Check for parasitic draws from aftermarket accessories or faulty electrical components. Replacing the battery or alternator may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Rattling/Squeaking Noises**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rattling or squeaking noises coming from the dashboard or door panels while driving.
    *   Solution: Identify the source of the noise and apply felt pads or other sound-dampening materials to the affected areas.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix:
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Automatic Liftgate Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Automatic liftgate malfunctioning, failing to open or close properly, or getting stuck.
    *   Solution: Check and replace liftgate struts, control module or associated sensors.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix:
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**
The 2024 Nissan Murano has a few recurring issues that owners should be aware of. The most notable concern centers around the CVT transmission and its potential for hesitation, jerky performance, and premature failure. Electrical issues with the infotainment system and battery drain also appear. While not all Murano owners experience these problems, being informed can help prospective buyers make a more educated decision and address any potential issues promptly. The severity and cost of these issues vary, but addressing them promptly can prevent further damage and ensure the longevity of the vehicle.
