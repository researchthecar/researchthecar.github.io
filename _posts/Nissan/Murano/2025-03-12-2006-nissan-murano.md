The 2006 Nissan Murano is a mid-size crossover SUV known for its distinctive styling and comfortable ride. It came standard with a 3.5-liter V6 engine producing 245 horsepower and 246 lb-ft of torque, paired with a Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT). The Murano was praised for its smooth acceleration, spacious interior, and user-friendly technology. However, some owners have expressed concerns about the reliability of the CVT transmission and certain electrical components.

**Engine**

**3.5L VQ35DE V6**
* **Oil Leaks**
    * Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks often develop from the valve cover gaskets or the oil pan gasket. You may notice oil spots under the vehicle, a burning oil smell, or low oil levels.
    * Solution: Replace the valve cover gaskets or oil pan gasket.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+ depending on the location of the leak.

* **Catalytic Converter Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The check engine light comes on (typically codes P0420 or P0430) and the vehicle may experience reduced performance.
    * Solution: Replace the faulty catalytic converter.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1200+

**Transmission**

**CVT (Continuously Variable Transmission)**
* **CVT Failure/Malfunction**
    * Description/Symptoms: The CVT transmission is known for failing. Symptoms include slipping, jerking, hesitation upon acceleration, whining noises, and the transmission entering limp mode (reduced power). Error codes related to the transmission may be present.
    * Solution: Replacement of the CVT transmission is often the only permanent fix. Some repairs may be possible, but they often provide only temporary relief.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $3000 - $5000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Rear Suspension Noise**
    * Description/Symptoms: Clunking or rattling noises from the rear suspension, especially when going over bumps. This is often related to worn sway bar bushings or end links.
    * Solution: Replace the sway bar bushings and/or end links.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain**
    * Description/Symptoms: Premature battery drain, requiring frequent jump starts. Could be a parasitic draw from a faulty component.
    * Solution: Diagnose and repair the source of the parasitic draw. This could involve replacing a faulty relay, sensor, or module.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+ (depending on the source).
* **ABS (Anti-lock Braking System) Sensor Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: ABS light illuminating on the dashboard. Diagnostic trouble codes related to the ABS sensors. Reduced or non-functional ABS system.
    * Solution: Replace the faulty ABS sensor.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $400+ per sensor.

**Exterior**

* **Power Liftgate Issues (if equipped)**
    * Description/Symptoms: The power liftgate may fail to open or close properly, or make unusual noises during operation. This can be due to a faulty motor, struts, or latch mechanism.
    * Solution: Replace the faulty liftgate motor, struts, or latch.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+

**Interior**

* **Air Conditioning Malfunction**
    * Description/Symptoms: Air conditioning blowing warm air or not functioning at all. This can be due to a refrigerant leak, a faulty compressor, or a malfunctioning blower motor.
    * Solution: Diagnose and repair the A/C system. This may involve recharging the refrigerant, replacing the compressor, or replacing the blower motor.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $1000+ depending on the issue.
* **Sunroof Issues (if equipped)**
    * Description/Symptoms: Sunroof may get stuck, make excessive noise, or leak.
    * Solution: Repair or replace sunroof motor, track, or seals.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+

**Summary**

The 2006 Nissan Murano, while stylish and comfortable, has some known reliability issues. The most significant concern is the CVT transmission, which is prone to failure and costly to replace. Other common problems include oil leaks, catalytic converter failure, and electrical issues. The severity of these issues ranges from minor inconveniences to major repairs, with repair costs varying accordingly. Potential buyers should be aware of these potential problems and factor them into their purchasing decision. Pre-purchase inspections are highly recommended.
