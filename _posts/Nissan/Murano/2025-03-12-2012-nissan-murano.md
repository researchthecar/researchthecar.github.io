The 2012 Nissan Murano is a mid-size crossover SUV known for its comfortable ride and distinctive styling. It comes standard with a 3.5-liter V6 engine producing 260 horsepower and 240 lb-ft of torque, paired with a continuously variable transmission (CVT). Owners appreciate the Murano for its spacious interior, smooth ride, and available features. Common complaints include concerns about the CVT's reliability and higher repair costs compared to some competitors.

**Engine**

**3.5L V6 (VQ35DE)**

* **Oil Consumption**
    * Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported excessive oil consumption, requiring frequent oil top-offs between scheduled oil changes. The vehicle may exhibit blue smoke from the exhaust.
    * Solution: In some cases, replacing the piston rings and/or valve stem seals can reduce oil consumption.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**CVT (Continuously Variable Transmission)**

* **Transmission Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The CVT can experience premature failure. Symptoms include slipping, shuddering, hesitation during acceleration, and unusual noises. The "check engine" light may illuminate.
    * Solution: Replacing the CVT is often the only solution.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $3,500 - $5,000+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Torque Converter Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: Issues within the torque converter can cause shuddering or vibrations, particularly at lower speeds.
    * Solution: Replacing the torque converter can resolve this issue.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Strut Mount Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Premature failure of the front strut mounts can cause clunking noises over bumps and uneven tire wear.
    * Solution: Replacing the strut mounts resolves the issue.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain**
    * Description/Symptoms: Some owners report issues with the battery draining quickly, especially if the vehicle sits for an extended period.
    * Solution: Thoroughly test the charging system and check for parasitic draws. Replace the battery if it fails a load test. Repair or replace any components causing a draw.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Liftgate Support Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The liftgate support struts can weaken over time, causing the liftgate to fall closed unexpectedly.
    * Solution: Replace the liftgate support struts.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Navigation System/Radio Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: Some owners experience issues with the navigation system freezing, malfunctioning, or the radio losing functionality.
    * Solution: Software updates, if available, might resolve the issue. In some cases, the entire unit needs to be replaced.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2012 Nissan Murano can present several potential issues for owners. The most significant concern revolves around the CVT transmission, which is prone to failure and expensive to replace. Other issues, such as excessive oil consumption and strut mount failures, can also contribute to higher running costs. Potential buyers should be aware of these common problems and factor them into their purchasing decision. Due to the high cost and severity of the transmission issues, it is recommended to have a pre-purchase inspection performed by a qualified mechanic.
