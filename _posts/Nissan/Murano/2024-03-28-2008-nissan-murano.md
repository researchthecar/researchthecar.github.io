**Introduction to the 2008 Nissan Murano**

The 2008 Nissan Murano is a mid-size crossover SUV known for its spacious interior, comfortable ride, and powerful V6 engine. It comes with two engine options: a 3.5L V6 and a 4.0L V6, paired with a continuously variable transmission (CVT) or a 5-speed automatic transmission.

People appreciate the Murano's stylish design, advanced features, and strong performance. However, it has also faced some common issues, which are detailed below.

**Engine**

**3.5L V6 with CVT**

* **Engine Oil Consumption:** Excessive oil consumption, leading to low engine oil levels and potential engine damage.
  * Solution: Replacing piston rings and engine seals.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+
  * Recalls: None Found
* **Intake Manifold Air Leaks:** Air leaks through the intake manifold gasket, causing a loss of power and poor fuel economy.
  * Solution: Replacing the intake manifold gasket.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500
  * Recalls: None Found

**4.0L V6 with 5-speed Automatic**

* **No Major Issues Found:** This engine and transmission combination has not reported any widespread issues.

**Transmission**

**CVT**

* **Hesitation and Stalling:** Hesitation or stalling upon acceleration, due to worn CVT chains or a faulty CVT control module.
  * Solution: Replacing the CVT chains or control module.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $3,500 - $5,000+
  * Recalls: None Found

**5-speed Automatic**

* **Slipping Gears:** Transmission slipping out of gears, especially when shifting from Park to Drive.
  * Solution: Replacing the transmission solenoid pack or rebuilding the transmission.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
  * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Front Strut Clunking:** Noises or clunking from the front struts, caused by worn strut mounts.
  * Solution: Replacing the strut mounts.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800
  * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain:** Excessive battery drain, leading to difficulty starting the vehicle.
  * Solution: Replacing the battery or testing for parasitic drain.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
  * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Tailgate Release Failure:** Difficulty opening the tailgate, due to a faulty tailgate release switch.
  * Solution: Replacing the tailgate release switch.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200
  * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Navigation System Malfunction:** Navigation system freezing or displaying incorrect information.
  * Solution: Updating the navigation software or replacing the navigation unit.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
  * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2008 Nissan Murano has received mixed reviews regarding reliability. Some owners have experienced significant issues, while others have had positive experiences. The severity and cost of repairs can vary depending on the specific issue and the severity of the problem.

The most common issues affecting the Murano include engine oil consumption, intake manifold air leaks, CVT hesitation, and transmission slipping. These issues can be costly to repair and may require expertise from a qualified mechanic. It's important to consider these potential issues when purchasing or maintaining a 2008 Nissan Murano.
