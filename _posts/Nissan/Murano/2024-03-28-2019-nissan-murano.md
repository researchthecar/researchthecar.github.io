**Introduction**

The 2019 Nissan Murano is a five-seater midsize SUV powered by a 3.5-liter V6 engine mated to a continuously variable transmission (CVT). This powertrain produces 260 horsepower and 240 lb-ft of torque. The Murano is praised for its spacious cabin, comfortable ride, and user-friendly infotainment system. However, it also has some drawbacks, including its unremarkable fuel economy and lack of off-road capabilities.

**Engine**

* **3.5-liter V6**
    * **Intake Valve Deposits**
        * Description/Symptoms: The intake valves can become clogged with carbon deposits over time, which can lead to reduced engine power, increased fuel consumption, and rough idling.
        * Solution: The intake valves need to be cleaned or replaced.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
        * Recalls: None Found
    * **PCV Valve Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: The positive crankcase ventilation (PCV) valve can fail, causing a buildup of pressure in the crankcase. This can lead to oil leaks, engine damage, and increased emissions.
        * Solution: The PCV valve needs to be replaced.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200
        * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

* **Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)**
    * **Hesitation Upon Acceleration**
        * Description/Symptoms: The transmission may hesitate or shudder when accelerating from a stop or at low speeds.
        * Solution: The transmission control module (TCM) may need to be updated.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
        * Recalls: None Found
    * **Belt Slipping**
        * Description/Symptoms: The CVT belt can slip, causing loss of power and difficulty accelerating.
        * Solution: The CVT belt needs to be replaced.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **No Issues Found**

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain**
        * Description/Symptoms: The battery may drain quickly, even when the vehicle is turned off.
        * Solution: The parasitic draw needs to be identified and fixed.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Panoramic Sunroof Leaks**
        * Description/Symptoms: The panoramic sunroof may leak water into the cabin during heavy rain or when washing the vehicle.
        * Solution: The sunroof seals need to be replaced.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
        * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Infotainment System Glitches**
        * Description/Symptoms: The infotainment system may experience glitches, such as freezing, crashing, or losing functionality.
        * Solution: The infotainment system may need to be updated or replaced.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

Overall, the 2019 Nissan Murano is a reliable vehicle with a spacious cabin and comfortable ride. However, it is not without its issues. Some of the most common problems include intake valve deposits, PCV valve failure, CVT hesitation, belt slipping, battery drain, panoramic sunroof leaks, and infotainment system glitches. While the cost to fix these issues can vary, many of them are relatively minor and affordable to resolve.
