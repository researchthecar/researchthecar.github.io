The 2022 Nissan Murano is a mid-size crossover SUV known for its comfortable ride, stylish interior, and user-friendly technology. It comes equipped with a 3.5-liter V6 engine producing 260 horsepower and 240 lb-ft of torque, paired with a Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT). Owners often praise the Murano for its spacious cabin and smooth driving experience, but some common complaints revolve around the CVT performance and reliability, as well as some electrical issues.

**Engine**

**3.5L V6**

*   **Oil Consumption**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported excessive oil consumption, requiring them to add oil between scheduled oil changes. This can manifest as low oil level warnings or visible blue smoke from the exhaust.
    *   Solution: Monitoring oil levels regularly and addressing any leaks promptly. In severe cases, engine repairs or replacement may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $7,000+ (for major repairs or engine replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**CVT (Continuously Variable Transmission)**

*   **Hesitation/Jerking**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have noted hesitation or jerking during acceleration, especially at lower speeds. This can feel like a stutter or lag in power delivery.
    *   Solution: CVT fluid change may improve the issue. In some cases, a CVT replacement is the only solution.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 (fluid change) - $4,000+ (CVT replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Premature Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported the CVT failing prematurely, often requiring a full replacement. Symptoms include slipping, shuddering, and inability to accelerate.
    *   Solution: CVT replacement
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $4,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Freezing/Crashing**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The infotainment system may freeze, crash, or become unresponsive. This can affect navigation, audio, and climate control functions.
    *   Solution: Software updates may resolve the issue. If not, the head unit may need to be replaced.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 (software update) - $1,000+ (head unit replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Sensor Malfunctions**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Issues with various sensors, such as the tire pressure monitoring system (TPMS) or blind spot monitoring system, can cause warning lights to illuminate and the systems to malfunction.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis and replacement of the faulty sensor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+ (depending on the sensor)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Sunroof/Moonroof Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Sunroof may get stuck, make excessive noise, or leak.
    *   Solution: Cleaning and lubricating the sunroof tracks, or replacing the sunroof motor or seals.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **No Issues Found**

**Suspension**

*   **No Issues Found**

**Summary:**

The 2022 Nissan Murano can encounter a range of issues, with the CVT being the most significant concern due to potential hesitation, jerking, and premature failures, which can be costly to repair. Electrical issues with the infotainment system and sensors are also relatively common. While the engine itself is generally reliable, some owners have reported excessive oil consumption. Overall, while not catastrophic, the potential cost of CVT repairs makes it important for prospective buyers to be aware of these potential issues and consider purchasing an extended warranty.
