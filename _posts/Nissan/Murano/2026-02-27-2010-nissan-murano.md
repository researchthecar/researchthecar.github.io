# 2010 Nissan Murano

### Overview
The 2010 Nissan Murano is a mid-size crossover SUV known for its comfortable ride and distinctive styling. It was generally considered a reliable vehicle for its model year, offering a good balance of features and practicality. However, like any vehicle, it has specific areas where owners have reported issues. This analysis focuses on common mechanical and electrical problems, excluding routine maintenance and standard wear and tear, to provide a clear picture of its long-term reliability.

---


## Engine

### 3.5L V6 (VQ35DE)
**Oil Leaks (Valve Covers/Gaskets)**
- **Symptoms:** Visible oil on engine components, burning oil smell, low oil level.
- **Solution:** Replacement of valve cover gaskets or other seals.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600

**Variable Valve Timing (VVT) Actuator Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Rattling noise from the engine, especially on startup; check engine light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of VVT actuators and potentially related timing components.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1500


## Transmission

### CVT (Continuously Variable Transmission)
**Overheating/Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Hesitation, slipping gears, jerky acceleration, transmission warning light, fluid leaks.
- **Solution:** Transmission fluid flush and filter replacement; in severe cases, transmission rebuild or replacement.
- **Cost:** $2000 - $4500

**Valve Body Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Erratic shifting, harsh engagement, delayed response.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of the transmission valve body.
- **Cost:** $700 - $1500


## Suspension

### Standard Suspension
**Strut Mount Bearing Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Clunking or popping noise when turning the steering wheel, especially at low speeds.
- **Solution:** Replacement of strut mount bearings.
- **Cost:** $200 - $400

### Summary Verdict
The 2010 Nissan Murano, particularly with its standard 3.5L V6 engine, offers a generally dependable ownership experience. However, potential buyers and current owners should be aware of common issues, most notably with the CVT transmission, which can be costly to repair if problems arise. Engine oil leaks and VVT actuator issues are also reported, though often less severe. Electrical gremlins related to the BCM and infotainment system can occur. Suspension components like strut mounts may require attention over time. Despite these points, with diligent maintenance and addressing issues promptly, the Murano can remain a reliable vehicle.
