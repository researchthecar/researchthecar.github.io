The 2016 Nissan Murano is a mid-size crossover SUV known for its comfortable ride, stylish interior, and distinctive exterior design. It came standard with a 3.5-liter V6 engine producing 260 horsepower and 240 lb-ft of torque, paired with a continuously variable transmission (CVT). Many owners appreciate the Murano's spacious cabin and upscale feel, but some have reported issues with its reliability, particularly concerning the CVT transmission and some electronic components.

**Engine**

**3.5L V6**

* **Oil Consumption**
    * Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported excessive oil consumption, requiring them to add oil between scheduled oil changes. This may be indicated by the low oil pressure light illuminating.
    * Solution: In some cases, replacing the valve stem seals or piston rings may be necessary.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**CVT (Continuously Variable Transmission)**

* **Transmission Failure/Shuddering**
    * Description/Symptoms: The most common complaint is related to the CVT transmission exhibiting shuddering, slipping, hesitation upon acceleration, or complete failure. This often manifests as a noticeable vibration or shaking during acceleration.
    * Solution: Replacement of the CVT transmission is often required. In some cases, a software update might temporarily alleviate the issue, but it is not a permanent fix.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $3,500 - $5,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Premature Strut Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported that the front struts fail prematurely, leading to a bouncy ride and unusual noises (clunking) from the front suspension.
    * Solution: Replacement of the struts.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Navigation/Infotainment System Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: The infotainment system, including the navigation system and touchscreen display, can experience freezing, slow response times, or complete failure.
    * Solution: Software updates may resolve some issues, but in other cases, the entire head unit may need replacement.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,200+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Blind Spot Monitoring (BSM) Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Intermittent or complete failure of the blind spot monitoring system.
    * Solution: Replacement of the BSM sensors or related wiring.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $700
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Automatic Liftgate Malfunction**
    * Description/Symptoms: The automatic liftgate may fail to open or close properly, or may stop working altogether.
    * Solution: Replacement of the liftgate motor, struts, or control module.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Seat Heater Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The seat heaters may stop working.
    * Solution: Replacement of the heating element or control module.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

The 2016 Nissan Murano presents a mix of potential issues that buyers should be aware of. The most concerning and potentially costly issue is related to the CVT transmission, which can exhibit shuddering, slipping, or even complete failure, potentially costing several thousands of dollars to repair or replace. Other issues, such as infotainment system malfunctions and suspension problems, while less severe, can still lead to unexpected repair bills. Overall, the severity of these issues ranges from moderate to severe, and the costs can vary widely. Potential buyers should carefully inspect the vehicle's service history and consider a pre-purchase inspection by a qualified mechanic to identify any existing or potential problems.
