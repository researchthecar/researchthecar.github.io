# 2021 Nissan Frontier Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2021 Nissan Frontier, buyers are looking at a transitional model year that introduced a brand-new powertrain just before the truck's major redesign. While overall owner feedback is relatively sparse, prospective buyers should review available vehicle history reports and pay attention to electrical and airbag notifications reported by some drivers.

---

## Engine

### 3.8L V6 Direct-Injection Engine

The 3.8-liter direct-injection V6 engine produces 310 horsepower and 281 lb-ft of torque, providing strong acceleration for daily driving and towing. This engine serves as the sole powerplant for the 2021 model year and generally offers solid baseline reliability for buyers.

## Transmission

### 9-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 9-speed automatic transmission is paired exclusively with the V6 engine across all trims and drivetrain configurations. It delivers smooth highway cruising, though some owners have noted hesitation during quick acceleration or low-speed maneuvers.

### Starting and Powertrain Electrical

Electrical and starting feedback highlights occasional intermittent glitches that are typically resolved through diagnostic checks.

**Intermittent Starting and Hesitation Concerns**
- **Symptoms:** Some owners experienced abnormal noises from the engine compartment during startup or temporary hesitation and power lag when attempting to accelerate from a stop.
- **Solution:** Check the starter motor, battery health, and powertrain control module software for any updates or underlying electrical faults.

## Safety

### Air Bag System

Airbag sensor lights require prompt attention to ensure occupant safety systems are fully operational.

**Airbag Warning Light and Sensor Fluctuations**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have reported that the airbag warning light suddenly illuminates, flashes, or that the passenger seat occupant status light fluctuates while occupied.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified service center inspect the seat occupancy sensors, wiring harnesses, and diagnostic trouble codes to ensure proper airbag deployment readiness.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V457000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=Frontier&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The transmission parking pawl may not engage when the vehicle is shifted into park, which can result in a vehicle rollaway. A vehicle rollaway increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the transmission parking pawl pin, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V671000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=Frontier&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The transmission parking pawl may not engage when the vehicle is shifted into park, which can result in a vehicle rollaway. A vehicle rollaway increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will perform the applicable repairs below, free of charge:  Model Year 2020-2022 Frontier vehicles: reprogram Transmission Control Module (TCM)  Model Year 2020-2022 Titan vehicles: reprogram TCM and Engine Control Module (ECM) . Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2021 Nissan Frontier benefits from a robust V6 engine and modern 9-speed transmission combination. While isolated owner complaints mention minor electrical quirks and airbag warning lights, a thorough pre-purchase inspection and a verified service history will help ensure a dependable ownership experience.
