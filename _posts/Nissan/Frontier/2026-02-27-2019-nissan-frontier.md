# 2019 Nissan Frontier Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2019 Nissan Frontier represents the final year of the truck's long-running second generation, making it a rugged and mechanically simple choice for used-truck buyers. While it avoids the complex technology and turbochargers found in modern midsize pickups, owners have reported a variety of localized electrical, brake, and powertrain quirks. Prospective buyers should carefully inspect mechanical components, brakes, and electronics during a test drive to ensure everything operates smoothly.

---

## Engine

### 2.5L 4-Cylinder Engine

The base 2.5-liter four-cylinder engine produces 152 horsepower and 171 lb-ft of torque, offering basic utility and acceptable fuel economy for light-duty work. It is generally robust, though fleet and owner reports note occasional isolated misfires or ignition concerns.

### 4.0L V6 Engine

The 4.0-liter V6 engine pushes out 261 horsepower and 281 lb-ft of torque, providing strong towing capability and confident acceleration. It is widely considered a durable power plant, though isolated valvetrain or misfire issues can occasionally arise.

**Cylinder Misfire**
- **Symptoms:** The engine may shake, idle roughly, and experience a noticeable loss of power while driving due to a valve misfire on specific cylinders.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified mechanic perform a diagnostic test to inspect spark plugs, ignition coils, and cylinder compression, replacing faulty ignition components or repairing valves as needed.

## Transmission

### 5-Speed Manual Transmission

The traditional 5-speed manual transmission is paired exclusively with the base four-cylinder engine and provides a direct, mechanical shifting feel. It has a reputation for simplicity and longevity when the clutch is maintained properly.

### 5-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 5-speed automatic transmission delivers power smoothly to the rear or four wheels, though its older design lacks the gear ratios of modern multi-speed units. Routine fluid changes are recommended to ensure long-term reliability.

### 6-Speed Manual Transmission

The 6-speed manual transmission is mated to the V6 engine for a more engaging driving experience. While generally sturdy, manual-equipped models can develop driveline noises or linkage wear over time.

**Clutter and Clunking Noise**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may hear a distinct clunking noise coming from the transmission area when the clutch pedal is depressed while traveling over bumps at low speeds.
- **Solution:** Inspect transmission mounts, clutch linkage, and the driveshaft for excessive play or wear.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

The braking system provides adequate stopping power under normal conditions, but owners frequently note a soft pedal feel and occasional premature caliper sticking that requires prompt maintenance.

**Prematurely Sticking Calipers**
- **Symptoms:** Front brake calipers may stick and cause a squeaking noise, premature pad wear, and a reduced overall stopping capability.
- **Solution:** Have a mechanic inspect, clean, and rebuild or replace the front brake calipers and install new brake pads and rotors.

**Mushy Brake Pedal Feel**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers often report excessive brake pedal travel with a spongy feel, where pushing the pedal further during motion fails to increase braking force proportionally.
- **Solution:** Bleed the brake lines thoroughly to remove trapped air, and inspect the master cylinder for internal bypass or wear.

## Electrical

### Infotainment and Electrical System

electrical issues in the cabin are generally minor, though infotainment display hiccups and intermittent accessory power drops have been documented by some owners.

**Random Screen Cycling**
- **Symptoms:** The touchscreen radio and display unit may intermittently turn off and on by itself while driving.
- **Solution:** Check for software updates at a dealership or inspect the radio unit wiring harness and fuses for loose connections.

## Safety

### Air Bag System

The supplemental restraint systems are generally reliable, but sensor malfunctions can trigger warning lights that require professional diagnostic tools to resolve.

**Occupant Classification Sensor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The airbag warning light illuminates on the dashboard due to a failure in the passenger-side occupant classification system (OCS) control unit.
- **Solution:** Have a diagnostic scan performed to isolate the sensor fault, and replace the OCS control module or affected seat components as necessary.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V654000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=Frontier&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 111, "Rear Visibility." The lack of an image in the back-up camera display increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2019 Nissan Frontier is a solid, old-school pickup truck that appeals to buyers prioritizing mechanical straightforwardness over high-tech features. While it is largely dependable, used shoppers should pay close attention to the braking system feel, verify that all electrical accessories and safety warning lights function properly, and listen for unusual driveline noises during a test drive.
