**Introduction:**

The 2012 Nissan Frontier is a mid-size pickup truck available with a range of engine and transmission options. It offers a 4.0L V6 engine rated at 261 horsepower and 281 lb-ft of torque. Transmission options include a 5-speed automatic, a 6-speed manual, and a 4-speed automatic for the 4.0L engine. The Frontier is well-known for its towing capacity, off-road capability, and reliability. However, like any vehicle, it has its fair share of issues.

**Engine**

**4.0L V6**

* **Timing chain failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Gradual loss of power, ticking noise from engine, engine stalls
    * Solution: Replacement of timing chain and related components
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Oil leak from valve covers:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Oil leaking from under valve covers, visible drip or puddle under vehicle
    * Solution: Replacement of valve cover gaskets
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**5-Speed Automatic**

* **Slipping gears:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Loss of power during acceleration, difficulty shifting gears
    * Solution: Replacement or rebuild of transmission
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,500 - $3,500+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Transmission fluid leaks:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Red fluid leaking from transmission pan, low transmission fluid levels
    * Solution: Replacement of transmission pan gasket or seal
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
    * Recalls: None Found

**6-Speed Manual**

* **Hard shifting:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears, grinding noises when shifting
    * Solution: Replacement of clutch or other transmission components
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Clutch problems:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Slipping clutch, difficulty engaging gears
    * Solution: Replacement of clutch assembly
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Sagging rear leaf springs:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Rear end of vehicle sits lower than the front, decreased handling and stability
    * Solution: Replacement of leaf springs
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Worn-out shocks:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Rough ride, excessive bouncing after hitting bumps
    * Solution: Replacement of shocks
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Electrical shorts:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Blown fuses, flickering lights, intermittent electrical issues
    * Solution: Identification and repair of short circuit
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Battery drain:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Battery goes dead after sitting overnight or for a few days
    * Solution: Replacement of battery or identification and repair of parasitic drain
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Rust on body panels:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Formation of rust spots on body panels, especially in areas exposed to moisture
    * Solution: Rust repair or replacement of body panels
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Headlight condensation:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Accumulation of moisture inside headlight lens
    * Solution: Replacement of headlight assembly or resealing of lens
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
    * Recalls: NHTSA Campaign Number: 12V446000

**Interior**

* **Dash rattles:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Noises and vibrations coming from dashboard area
    * Solution: Identification and elimination of source of rattle
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Squeaky seats:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Squeaking noises when entering or exiting the vehicle
    * Solution: Lubrication of seat tracks or replacement of seat components
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

Overall, the 2012 Nissan Frontier is a reliable vehicle, but it is not without its issues. The most severe and costly issues are related to engine timing chain failure and transmission problems, which can lead to expensive repairs. However, these issues are not common and many owners report good experiences with the Frontier. The majority of the other issues listed are relatively minor and can be fixed for a reasonable cost.
