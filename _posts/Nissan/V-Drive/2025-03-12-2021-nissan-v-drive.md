The 2021 Nissan Versa, known as the V-Drive in some markets, is a subcompact sedan offering fuel efficiency and affordability. For the 2021 model year, it primarily features a 1.6-liter four-cylinder engine producing around 122 horsepower and 114 lb-ft of torque. Transmission options usually include a 5-speed manual transmission or a Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT). Owners often praise the Versa for its spacious interior relative to its class, good fuel economy, and user-friendly technology. Common complaints include the lack of power for highway passing, noticeable road noise, and basic interior materials.

**Engine**

**1.6L Inline-4**

*   **Excessive Oil Consumption**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners report higher than expected oil consumption, requiring frequent top-offs between oil changes. Symptoms include the low oil pressure light illuminating, engine knocking, and visible blue smoke from the exhaust.
    *   Solution: The solution depends on the severity. It can range from using a higher viscosity oil, replacing the PCV valve, to more extensive repairs like piston ring replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $2,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)**

*   **CVT Hesitation and Jerking**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners report hesitation upon acceleration, particularly from a stop, and occasional jerking or shuddering during low-speed driving.
    *   Solution: In some cases, a CVT fluid flush and reprogramming of the transmission control module (TCM) can alleviate the issue. In more severe cases, CVT replacement may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $4,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found
*   **CVT Whining Noise**
    *   Description/Symptoms: A noticeable whining or high-pitched noise emanating from the transmission, especially during acceleration. This often indicates internal wear within the CVT.
    *   Solution: CVT replacement is typically recommended.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $4,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Freezing/Crashing**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The infotainment screen occasionally freezes, becomes unresponsive, or crashes, requiring a restart of the vehicle to restore functionality.
    *   Solution: Software updates from the dealer may resolve the issue. In some cases, replacement of the head unit may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found
*   **Battery Drain**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Premature battery drain, leading to difficulty starting the vehicle, especially after it has been sitting for a few days.
    *   Solution: Check for parasitic draws from aftermarket accessories or faulty components. Replacement of the battery is a common solution.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Dashboard Rattles**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rattling noises emanating from the dashboard area, particularly on rough roads.
    *   Solution: Identifying and securing the source of the rattle, which may involve adding foam padding or tightening loose screws.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 - $100+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2021 Nissan Versa/V-Drive is generally a reliable vehicle, but some owners have reported issues with the CVT transmission, engine oil consumption, and electrical glitches. The CVT issues and internal engine repairs tend to be the most costly to repair. The electrical and interior issues are relatively minor. Potential buyers should be aware of these common complaints and consider a thorough inspection and test drive before purchasing.
