# 2017 Nissan Kicks

### Overview
The 2017 Nissan Kicks, a subcompact crossover, generally presents a solid reliability record for its model year. It is known for its fuel efficiency and practical urban driving capabilities. While no vehicle is entirely without potential issues, the Kicks has demonstrated a commendable level of dependability, particularly in its powertrain and core mechanical systems. Owners often report satisfaction with its day-to-day performance, with most common concerns revolving around minor electrical glitches or specific component wear rather than widespread systemic failures. This analysis focuses on documented technical reliability, excluding routine maintenance and standard wear and tear, to provide a clear picture of potential concerns for prospective and current owners.

### Xtronic CVT (Continuously Variable Transmission)
**Hesitation or Jerking**
- **Symptoms:** Slight hesitation during acceleration or occasional jerky movements, particularly at lower speeds.
- **Solution:** Software update for the transmission control module, or in more severe cases, transmission fluid flush and replacement.

## Electrical

### Infotainment System
**Bluetooth Connectivity Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Intermittent connection problems with smartphones, audio cutting out, or failure to pair.
- **Solution:** Software update for the infotainment system, or a factory reset.
- **Cost:** $50 - $150


### 12V Battery
**Premature Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Difficulty starting the engine, dimming headlights, or electrical components malfunctioning.
- **Solution:** Battery replacement.

### Summary Verdict
The 2017 Nissan Kicks is generally considered a reliable vehicle, especially for its segment. The 1.6L engine and its associated CVT transmission, while not without occasional minor concerns like hesitation, have proven to be durable for daily driving. The most frequently reported non-wear-and-tear issues tend to be minor electrical glitches, such as Bluetooth connectivity problems, and less commonly, premature battery failure or condensation in lighting units. Suspension and exterior/interior components are largely free from widespread complaints. Overall, the Kicks offers a dependable ownership experience, with potential issues being relatively minor and manageable in terms of cost and complexity.
