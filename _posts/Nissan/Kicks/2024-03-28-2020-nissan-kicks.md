**Introduction**

The 2020 Nissan Kicks is a subcompact SUV that offers a combination of fuel efficiency, versatility, and affordability. It comes standard with a 1.6-liter four-cylinder engine and continuously variable transmission (CVT). The Kicks is known for its spacious interior, comfortable seats, and user-friendly infotainment system. However, it has received some criticism for its lack of power, bland styling, and limited cargo space.

**Engine**

**1.6-Liter Four-Cylinder Engine**

* **Low Power:** Limited acceleration and passing power, especially at highway speeds.
* **Sluggish Response:** CVT transmission can be slow to react to input, resulting in a lack of responsiveness.
* **Engine Stalling:** Some owners have reported instances of the engine stalling without warning.

**Transmission**

**Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)**

* **Hesitation:** Transmission hesitates when accelerating, causing a jerky driving experience.
* **Noise:** CVT can produce a whining or growling noise during operation.
* **Premature Failure:** Some owners have experienced premature transmission failures, requiring costly repairs or replacements.

**Suspension**

**No Issues Found**

**Electrical**

**Battery Drain:** Some owners have reported issues with the battery draining prematurely, potentially leading to a dead battery.
* **Electrical Components Failure:** Occasional issues with electrical components, such as the infotainment system, power windows, or headlights.
* **Keyless Ignition System Faults:** Some owners have experienced problems with the keyless ignition system, leading to difficulty starting the vehicle.

**Exterior**

**No Issues Found**

**Interior**

**Climate Control:** Some owners have reported problems with the climate control system, resulting in inconsistent temperatures or inadequate cooling/heating.
* **Seat Adjustment Malfunction:** Occasional issues with the seat adjustment mechanisms, leading to difficulty adjusting the seats.
* **Poor Visibility:** Thick A-pillars can create blind spots and hinder visibility.

**Recalls**

* NHTSA Recall Number 20V-324: Front seat belt pretensioners may not deploy properly in a crash.
* NHTSA Recall Number 19V-695: Electronic stability control (ESC) may not function properly.

**Summary**

The 2020 Nissan Kicks offers a comfortable and fuel-efficient ride, but it is not without its issues. The engine and transmission can be sluggish and unreliable, while the electrical system and interior have also experienced some problems. The severity of the issues varies, with some being minor annoyances and others potentially requiring major repairs. The cost of repairs can range from a few hundred dollars to several thousand dollars, depending on the issue and the extent of the damage. Overall, the Kicks is a reliable vehicle with a number of advantages, but potential buyers should be aware of the potential issues and factor them into their decision-making process.
