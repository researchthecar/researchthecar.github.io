# 2019 Nissan Kicks Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used compact crossover, evaluating common owner reports can help you spot potential trouble spots before making a purchase. While these vehicles offer nimble handling and efficient daily commuting, prospective buyers should pay close attention to powertrain performance, sensor stability, and braking system behavior during a test drive.

---

## Engine

### 1.6L 4-Cylinder Engine

Powered by a 1.6-liter naturally aspirated 4-cylinder engine generating approximately 122 horsepower and 114 lb-ft of torque, this setup is designed primarily for fuel-efficient urban commuting. While mechanically straightforward, owners have occasionally reported drivability hiccups tied to sensor performance.

**Engine Stalling and RPM Drop**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle experiences sudden drops in RPM while coming to a stop, occasionally resulting in unexpected engine stalling alongside traction control or ABS warning lights.
- **Solution:** Inspect and clean or replace faulty Mass Air Flow (MAF) sensors, and check related throttle body components.

## Transmission

### Xtronic Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)

Equipped with an Xtronic Continuously Variable Transmission routing power to the front wheels, this configuration prioritizes fuel economy over spirited driving dynamics. CVT longevity requires diligent fluid maintenance, as internal wear can lead to noticeable drivability concerns.

**Transmission Slipping, Shuddering, and Hesitation**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may notice intermittent hesitation during acceleration, abnormal jerking, shuddering, or complete loss of motive power while trying to drive.
- **Solution:** Consult a qualified transmission specialist for diagnostic testing; fluid service or component overhaul may be required depending on wear.

## Suspension

### Front Suspension and Struts

**Premature Coil Spring Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Clunking noises or a broken front coil spring can occur prematurely, requiring replacement of the strut and spring assembly.
- **Solution:** Inspect front suspension components for wear and replace worn struts and coil springs in pairs.

## Brakes

### Anti-lock Braking System (ABS) and Actuator

**ABS Warning Illumination and Actuator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The ABS and stability warning lights illuminate unexpectedly on the dashboard accompanied by service messages, sometimes pointing to internal hydraulic actuator or module faults.
- **Solution:** Have the braking system scanned for diagnostic trouble codes and replace faulty ABS modules or actuators as needed.

## Safety

### Forward Collision Avoidance and Sensors

**Unexpected Braking and Sensor Fault Warnings**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers experience sudden, unprompted emergency braking events or intermittent dashboard warning lights for the forward collision and cruise control systems.
- **Solution:** Inspect radar and camera sensors for obstruction or misalignment, and check for software updates at a qualified service center.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V654000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=Kicks&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 111, "Rear Visibility." The lack of an image in the back-up camera display increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

When evaluating a used model, conducting a thorough test drive is essential to verify smooth transmission operation and responsive braking. Pay close attention to any dashboard warning lights, hesitation during acceleration, or unusual noises from the suspension to ensure the vehicle has been well maintained.
