**2013 Nissan NP300**

The Nissan NP300 is a one-ton pickup truck that has been in production since 2008. It is available with a variety of engine and transmission options, including a 2.5-liter turbodiesel engine with a 6-speed manual transmission or a 5-speed automatic transmission, a 3.0-liter turbodiesel engine with a 6-speed manual or automatic transmission, and a 4.0-liter gasoline engine with a 5-speed automatic transmission. The NP300 is known for its ruggedness and off-road capabilities. However, it has also been the subject of numerous complaints about reliability issues.

**Engine**
* **2.5-liter turbodiesel engine with a 6-speed manual transmission**
    * **Turbocharger failure:** The turbocharger can fail prematurely, leading to a loss of power and fuel economy. The solution is to replace the turbocharger. The estimated cost to fix is $1,500 - $2,000+.
    * **Fuel injector failure:** The fuel injectors can fail, causing the engine to run rough and misfire. The solution is to replace the fuel injectors. The estimated cost to fix is $500 - $1,000+.
    * **EGR valve failure:** The EGR valve can fail, causing the engine to run poorly and emit excessive emissions. The solution is to replace the EGR valve. The estimated cost to fix is $300 - $500+.
* **2.5-liter turbodiesel engine with a 5-speed automatic transmission**
    * **Transmission failure:** The transmission can fail prematurely, leading to a loss of power and mobility. The solution is to replace the transmission. The estimated cost to fix is $3,000 - $5,000+.
    * **Torque converter failure:** The torque converter can fail, causing the transmission to slip and shudder. The solution is to replace the torque converter. The estimated cost to fix is $1,000 - $2,000+.
