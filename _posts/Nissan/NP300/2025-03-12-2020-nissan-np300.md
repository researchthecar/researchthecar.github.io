The 2020 Nissan NP300, also known as the Navara in some markets, is a popular pickup truck known for its durability and practicality. It was offered with a range of engines, including a 2.5-liter gasoline engine (QR25DE) and a 2.5-liter diesel engine (YD25DDTi) in varying states of tune. The 2.5L diesel engine is rated at 161-190 horsepower and 297-332 lb-ft of torque, depending on the specific version. Transmission options included a 6-speed manual and a 7-speed automatic. Owners often appreciate the NP300 for its robust build, off-road capability, and value for money. Common dislikes include the ride quality and some interior materials.
**Engine**

**2.5L Diesel (YD25DDTi)**

*   **Turbocharger Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Whining noise from the turbo, loss of power, excessive black smoke, and potential engine damage.
    *   Solution: Replace the turbocharger.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Fuel Injector Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rough idling, misfires, reduced fuel economy, and black smoke.
    *   Solution: Replace faulty fuel injectors.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $1,200+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **EGR Valve Problems**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Reduced engine performance, rough idling, increased emissions, and the engine light coming on.
    *   Solution: Clean or replace the EGR valve.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600
    *   Recalls: None Found

**2.5L Gasoline (QR25DE)**

*   **Catalytic Converter Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Reduced engine performance, engine light is on, rattling noise from the exhaust.
    *   Solution: Replace catalytic converter.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1500
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**7-Speed Automatic**

*   **Rough Shifting/Slipping**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Harsh or delayed shifts between gears, noticeable slipping during acceleration.
    *   Solution: Transmission flush and software update. If the problem persists, valve body replacement or full transmission rebuild/replacement may be needed.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 for flush, $800 - $2,000+ for valve body, $3,000 - $6,000+ for replacement.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**6-Speed Manual**

No Issues Found

**Suspension**

*   **Rear Leaf Spring Sagging**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The rear of the truck sits noticeably lower, reduced load-carrying capacity, and a harsher ride.
    *   Solution: Replace the rear leaf springs.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Battery Drain**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Battery dies overnight or after short periods of inactivity.
    *   Solution: Identify and repair the parasitic drain. This could involve checking the alternator, wiring harnesses, and aftermarket accessories.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Sensor Failures**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Various warning lights illuminated, inaccurate readings from sensors (e.g., ABS, TPMS), and potential performance issues.
    *   Solution: Diagnose and replace the faulty sensor(s).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500 per sensor
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Rust Issues (Depending on Region)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rust forming around wheel wells, seams, and underbody, particularly in areas with high salt usage.
    *   Solution: Repair and repaint affected areas. Apply rust protection.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $2,000+ (depending on severity)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Airbag Warning Light**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Airbag warning light illuminated on the dashboard.
    *   Solution: Diagnose and repair the airbag system. This may involve replacing the clock spring, seatbelt pretensioner, or airbag module.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2020 Nissan NP300, while generally a reliable workhorse, has some known issues. The diesel engine can suffer from turbocharger and fuel injector problems, which can be costly to repair. The automatic transmission might exhibit rough shifting, requiring a fluid change at the minimum, or a full rebuild if the issue is severe. Rust can be an issue, particularly in regions with harsh winters. Repair costs range from a few hundred dollars for minor sensor replacements to several thousand for major engine or transmission overhauls. While some issues are minor inconveniences, others could significantly impact the vehicle's reliability and resale value.
