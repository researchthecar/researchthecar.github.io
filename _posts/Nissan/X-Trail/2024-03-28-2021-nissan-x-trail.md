**Introduction**

The 2021 Nissan X-Trail is a compact SUV that offers a spacious interior, versatile seating arrangements, and a suite of driver-assistance features. It is available with either a 2.5-liter four-cylinder engine paired with a CVT or a 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine mated to a 9-speed automatic transmission.

While the X-Trail has received praise for its practicality and technology, it has also been met with some criticism regarding its lackluster performance and handling.

**Engine**

* **2.5-Liter Four-Cylinder**

> **Oil Leak**
> - **Description/Symptoms:** Oil is leaking from the engine, typically from the oil pan or valve cover gasket.
> - **Solution:** Replace the leaking gasket or seal.
> - **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $200 - $500
> - **Recalls:** None Found

**Transmission**

* **CVT**

> **Slipping**
> - **Description/Symptoms:** The transmission slips or hesitates when shifting gears.
> - **Solution:** Rebuild or replace the transmission.
> - **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $1,500 - $3,000+
> - **Recalls:** None Found

* **9-Speed Automatic**

> **No Issues Found**

**Suspension**

> **Squeaking or Rumbling**
> - **Description/Symptoms:** Squeaking or rumbling noises coming from the suspension when driving over uneven roads.
> - **Solution:** Replace the worn or damaged suspension components, such as bushings or struts.
> - **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $500 - $1,000+
> - **Recalls:** None Found

**Electrical**

> **Electrical System Failure**
> - **Description/Symptoms:** The vehicle's electrical system may fail, causing the engine to stall or the lights to go out.
> - **Solution:** Diagnose the problem and replace the faulty electrical components, such as the battery, alternator, or starter.
> - **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $200 - $1,000+
> - **Recalls:** 19V825000

**Exterior**

> **Rust**
> - **Description/Symptoms:** Rust forming on the body panels or undercarriage.
> - **Solution:** Repair the rust damage or have the vehicle repainted.
> - **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $500 - $2,000+
> - **Recalls:** None Found

**Interior**

> **Climate Control Issues**
> - **Description/Symptoms:** The climate control system may not work properly, blowing cold air when it should be blowing hot or vice versa.
> - **Solution:** Replace the faulty climate control components, such as the blower motor or temperature sensor.
> - **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $200 - $500+
> - **Recalls:** None Found

**Summary**

While the 2021 Nissan X-Trail is a practical and technology-laden SUV, it has experienced some issues, including oil leaks, transmission problems, suspension squeaks, electrical failures, and rust. The severity of these issues varies, and the cost to fix can range from a few hundred dollars to over two thousand dollars. Overall, the X-Trail's reliability record is mixed, and potential buyers should be aware of these potential issues before purchasing one.
