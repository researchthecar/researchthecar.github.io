**Introduction**
The 2009 Nissan X-Trail is a compact SUV that offers versatility and practicality. It comes with two engine options: a 2.5L 4-cylinder engine and a 3.5L V6 engine. The 2.5L engine produces 166 horsepower and 172 lb-ft of torque, while the 3.5L engine produces 265 horsepower and 246 lb-ft of torque. Both engines are paired with a 5-speed automatic transmission. The X-Trail is known for its comfortable ride, spacious interior, and off-road capabilities. However, it has also been reported to have some issues.

**Engine**
**2.5L 4-cylinder engine**
* **Excessive oil consumption**
    * Description/Symptoms: The engine consumes oil at an excessive rate, requiring frequent top-ups.
    * Solution: Replace the piston rings and oil seals.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $1,500+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Intake manifold runner control valve issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: The intake manifold runner control valve fails, causing decreased engine performance and fuel efficiency.
    * Solution: Replace the intake manifold runner control valve.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
    * Recalls: None Found

**3.5L V6 engine**
* **Timing chain tensioner failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The timing chain tensioner fails, causing a rattling noise from the engine and potentially catastrophic engine damage.
    * Solution: Replace the timing chain tensioner and timing chain.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**
**5-speed automatic transmission**
* **Slipping gears**
    * Description/Symptoms: The transmission slips out of gear, especially when shifting from 2nd to 3rd or 3rd to 4th gear.
    * Solution: Replace the clutches and gears inside the transmission.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,800 - $2,500+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Hesitation when shifting**
    * Description/Symptoms: The transmission hesitates when shifting gears, causing a delay in acceleration.
    * Solution: Replace the transmission fluid and filter.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**
* **Strut mount failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The strut mount fails, causing a clunking noise when driving over bumps and decreased stability.
    * Solution: Replace the strut mount.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $300 per strut
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Rear suspension bushings prematurely wearing**
    * Description/Symptoms: The rear suspension bushings wear out prematurely, causing a squeaking noise when driving over bumps and decreased handling.
    * Solution: Replace the rear suspension bushings.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**
* **Starter failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The starter fails, preventing the engine from starting.
    * Solution: Replace the starter.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $600
    * Recalls: Nissan Service Campaign ID: PC533
* **Alternator failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The alternator fails, causing the battery to not charge properly.
    * Solution: Replace the alternator.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $700
    * Recalls: Nissan Service Campaign ID: PC531

**Exterior**
* **Headlight switch failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The headlight switch fails, causing the headlights to not turn on or off properly.
    * Solution: Replace the headlight switch.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $300
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Door lock actuator failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The door lock actuator fails, preventing the doors from locking or unlocking properly.
    * Solution: Replace the door lock actuator.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $250 per door
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**
* **Dashboard rattling**
    * Description/Symptoms: The dashboard rattles when driving over rough roads.
    * Solution: Tighten the dashboard screws or install dashboard damping material.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Climate control fan motor failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The climate control fan motor fails, causing a lack of airflow in the cabin.
    * Solution: Replace the climate control fan motor.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $300
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**
The 2009 Nissan X-Trail is a practical and capable SUV, but it has been reported to have a number of issues. Some of the most common problems include excessive oil consumption, intake manifold runner control valve issues, timing chain tensioner failure, slipping gears, hesitation when shifting, strut mount failure, rear suspension bushings prematurely wearing, starter failure, alternator failure, headlight switch failure, door lock actuator failure, dashboard rattling, and climate control fan motor failure. These issues can range in severity and cost, but some of them can be serious and expensive to fix.
