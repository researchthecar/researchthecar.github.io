# 2016 Nissan Quest Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used minivan, the 2016 Nissan Quest offers a spacious cabin and comfortable ride, but buyers should approach this model year with care. Owner reports submitted to safety authorities highlight significant concerns regarding powertrain reliability, particularly involving the continuously variable transmission. While comfort and utility are strong suits, verifying the transmission's health and maintenance history is critical before making a purchase.

---

## Engine

### 3.5L V6 Engine

The 2016 Nissan Quest is equipped with a 3.5-liter V6 engine producing approximately 260 horsepower and 240 lb-ft of torque, providing adequate power for family hauling. This engine is generally considered mechanically robust, with few direct complaints logged against the motor itself compared to its paired transmission.

## Transmission

### Xtronic CVT (Continuously Variable Transmission)

The vehicle utilizes an Xtronic continuously variable transmission paired with front-wheel drive. This transmission option is a frequent source of owner complaints regarding longevity and mechanical shudder, making a pre-purchase mechanical inspection essential.

**Transmission Failure and Shaking**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers experience sudden loss of acceleration, violent shaking, shuddering (judder code P17F1), or complete transmission failure without prior warning lights.
- **Solution:** Requires professional inspection, valve body service, or a complete transmission replacement depending on internal wear.

**Loss of Forward Drive Gears**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may reverse normally but fail to engage when shifted into drive or low gear.
- **Solution:** Requires transmission overhaul or replacement to repair internal engagement components.

## Suspension

### Control Arms

Front suspension and steering components can wear out, occasionally leading to steering instability if control arms fail.

**Control Arm Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Worn or failing control arms can cause a loss of steering control, resulting in the vehicle pulling or jerking unexpectedly to one side.
- **Solution:** Replace the faulty control arms and perform a subsequent front-end wheel alignment.

## Brakes

### Brake Pedal Switch

electrical issues are usually minor, though the brake switch is a known trouble spot that affects both safety lighting and ignition.

**Faulty Brake Light Switch**
- **Symptoms:** Brake lights fail to illuminate intermittently, and push-button start functions may become disabled due to lack of signal recognition.
- **Solution:** Replace the malfunctioning brake sensor switch.

## Safety

### Occupant Classification System

Safety system sensors require attention if warning lights indicate potential faults in airbag deployment readiness.

**Passenger Airbag Sensor Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** The airbag warning light illuminates intermittently while an adult passenger is seated, and the 'Airbag Off' indicator toggles unexpectedly.
- **Solution:** Have the passenger seat sensor system diagnosed and recalibrated or replaced by a qualified technician.

## Summary Verdict

The 2016 Nissan Quest presents a mixed ownership picture. While its V6 engine and spacious interior appeal to families, widespread owner complaints regarding CVT transmission shudders, shaking, and premature failure demand extreme caution. Prospective buyers should prioritize vehicles with documented transmission service histories and obtain a thorough independent mechanical inspection before finalizing a purchase.
