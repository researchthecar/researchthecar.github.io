# 2017 Nissan Quest

### Overview
The 2017 Nissan Quest, a capable minivan, generally offers a solid ownership experience with a focus on practicality and family-friendly features. While not immune to the occasional hiccup, its overall reliability is considered good for its class and age. Most reported issues are minor and do not significantly impact daily usability. Owners generally appreciate its comfortable ride and spacious interior, making it a viable option for those seeking a used family vehicle. Regular maintenance is key to ensuring its longevity and preventing more significant problems down the line.

## Transmission

### CVT (Continuously Variable Transmission)
**Hesitation or Jerking**
- **Symptoms:** Transmission may hesitate during acceleration or exhibit slight jerking motions.
- **Solution:** Software update or transmission fluid flush and replacement. In severe cases, transmission rebuild or replacement may be necessary.
- **Cost:** $200 - $600 (fluid/software), $2,000 - $5,000 (rebuild/replacement)

## Electrical

### Standard Electrical System
**Infotainment System Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Occasional freezing or unresponsiveness of the infotainment screen.
- **Solution:** Software update for the infotainment system. Battery terminal cleaning or replacement if voltage issues are suspected.
- **Cost:** $100 - $300

**Power Sliding Door Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** Power sliding doors may be slow to open/close or fail to operate intermittently.
- **Solution:** Lubrication of door tracks, adjustment of sensors, or replacement of motor/actuator.
- **Cost:** $150 - $500

### Summary Verdict
The 2017 Nissan Quest demonstrates good overall reliability, with its V6 engine being particularly robust. The primary area of concern is the CVT transmission, which can occasionally exhibit hesitation or jerking, often resolvable with maintenance or software updates, though more serious issues may require costly repairs. Minor electrical gremlins, such as infotainment glitches and power sliding door issues, are also reported but are generally not critical. The suspension, exterior, and interior components are largely free from widespread problems. With diligent maintenance, particularly concerning the transmission, the 2017 Quest should provide dependable service for families.
