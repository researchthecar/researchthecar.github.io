# 2017 Nissan Quest Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

Shopping for a used 2017 Nissan Quest offers families a spacious and comfortable ride with versatile interior packaging. However, buyers should approach with caution due to reported owner concerns regarding powertrain performance and electrical systems. Reviewing specific maintenance histories and conducting a thorough test drive will help ensure any prospective minivan is in sound operating condition.

---

## Engine

### 3.5L V6

Equipped with a 3.5-liter V6 engine producing approximately 260 horsepower, this powertrain provides smooth and capable acceleration for daily driving and family road trips. It is generally considered a durable mechanical unit, though overall vehicle longevity depends heavily on routine maintenance.

## Transmission

### Xtronic Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)

Paired exclusively with the V6 engine, the Xtronic CVT delivers seamless gear ratios optimized for fuel efficiency. Prospective buyers should carefully evaluate transmission responsiveness during a test drive, as CVT repairs can be costly.

**Transmission Shudder and Sudden Loss of Acceleration**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have reported experiencing a shudder or pop when accelerating from a stop, as well as sudden jolts followed by a complete failure to accelerate while driving in traffic.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified technician inspect the transmission fluid condition and internal components; transmission replacement or rebuild may be necessary if internal failure has occurred.

## Electrical

### Push-Start Switch and Ignition Wiring

The electrical system controls vital comfort and starting features, though isolated high-resistance wiring issues require careful inspection.

**Brake Switch and Ignition System Melting**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have experienced internal electrical burning odors and components catching fire near the push-start and brake switch assembly during vehicle startup.
- **Solution:** Inspect the ignition switch, brake light switch, and surrounding wiring harness for signs of thermal damage or shorts, and replace damaged electrical components immediately.
- **Cost:** $2,000

## Exterior

### Paint Finish

Exterior paint durability can vary, and visual inspection during a walk-around will reveal cosmetic wear.

**Premature Paint Peeling**
- **Symptoms:** Owners note severe and premature peeling of the exterior paint, particularly on the hood and rear body panels.
- **Solution:** Consult an automotive body shop for professional repainting or cosmetic touch-ups to prevent further clear coat and paint degradation.

## Safety

### Air Bag System

Restraint systems are critical for occupant protection, and illuminated warning lights should be addressed promptly.

**Air Bag Warning Light and Module Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The SRS airbag warning light illuminates on the dashboard, indicating a fault within the airbag module or associated wiring harness.
- **Solution:** Take the vehicle to a qualified repair facility to diagnose diagnostic trouble codes and replace faulty airbag modules or wiring harnesses as needed.
- **Cost:** $2,000

## Summary Verdict

The 2017 Nissan Quest remains an appealing option for family transport due to its comfortable ride and practical cabin design. However, potential buyers should remain vigilant regarding possible transmission hesitations and electrical concerns. Prioritizing a pre-purchase inspection by a trusted mechanic will help mitigate unexpected repair expenses.
