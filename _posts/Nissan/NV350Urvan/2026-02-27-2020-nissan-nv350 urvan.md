# 2020 Nissan NV350 Urvan

### Overview
The 2020 Nissan NV350 Urvan is a commercial van designed for utility and reliability in demanding work environments. It typically features robust mechanicals aimed at durability and low running costs. While generally considered dependable, like any vehicle, specific components can experience issues over time. This analysis focuses on documented technical reliability concerns beyond routine maintenance, examining common problem areas reported by owners and technicians. The NV350 Urvan's design prioritizes functionality, which often translates to a straightforward and resilient build, though certain systems may require attention as mileage accumulates.

### 2.5L YD25DDTi (Diesel)
**Glow Plug Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Difficulty starting in cold weather, rough idling.
- **Solution:** Replace faulty glow plugs.
- **Cost:** $150 - $300

**Turbocharger Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of power, whistling noises, black smoke from exhaust.
- **Solution:** Inspect and potentially replace turbocharger and related hoses.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1500

### Summary Verdict
The 2020 Nissan NV350 Urvan demonstrates a generally strong reliability record, particularly with its gasoline engine option. The diesel engine, while offering better fuel economy, has a few documented issues such as glow plug and turbocharger problems that require attention. Transmission reliability is good, with the manual clutch being the most common wear item. Suspension components like bushings can wear over time, leading to noise and handling degradation. Electrical issues, primarily battery drain, are also noted. Overall, the NV350 Urvan is a practical and dependable work vehicle, with most potential issues being manageable and not indicative of systemic design flaws.
