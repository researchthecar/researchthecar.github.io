# 2021 Nissan NV350 Urvan

### Overview
The 2021 Nissan NV350 Urvan is a commercial van designed for utility and reliability in demanding work environments. It typically features robust mechanicals aimed at longevity and ease of maintenance. While generally considered dependable, like any vehicle, specific components can experience issues over time. This analysis focuses on documented technical reliability concerns beyond routine maintenance, examining common problem areas reported by owners and technicians. The NV350 Urvan's design prioritizes functionality, which often translates to a solid ownership experience for businesses relying on its cargo and passenger-carrying capabilities.

### 2.5L YD25DDTi (Diesel)
**Turbocharger Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of power, excessive smoke, whining noise from engine bay.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the turbocharger unit.
- **Cost:** $1,200 - $2,500

**Glow Plug Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Difficulty starting, especially in cold weather, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of faulty glow plugs.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600


## Transmission

### 5-Speed Manual
**Clutch Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Slipping clutch, difficulty shifting gears, burning smell.
- **Solution:** Replacement of clutch kit (clutch disc, pressure plate, release bearing).
- **Cost:** $800 - $1,500


### 5-Speed Automatic
**Transmission Fluid Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Leaking fluid, rough shifting, overheating transmission.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of seals and gaskets, or transmission flush.
- **Cost:** $400 - $1,000


## Suspension

### Standard Leaf Spring Rear
**Leaf Spring Sagging/Breakage**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle sits lower in the rear, uneven ride height, clunking noises over bumps.
- **Solution:** Replacement of damaged or worn leaf springs.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1,000


## Electrical

### General Electrical System
**Alternator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Battery warning light illuminated, dimming headlights, electrical components malfunctioning.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the alternator.

### Summary Verdict
The 2021 Nissan NV350 Urvan demonstrates a generally strong reliability record, particularly for its intended commercial use. The gasoline engine option is largely trouble-free. The diesel engine, while more powerful, has a few documented issues such as turbocharger problems and glow plug failures, which are not uncommon for diesel powertrains. Transmission reliability is decent, with clutch wear being the primary concern for manual variants and fluid leaks for automatics. Suspension components, especially the rear leaf springs, can show wear under heavy loads. Electrical issues like alternator failure are present but not pervasive. Overall, the NV350 Urvan is a practical and dependable choice for businesses, provided regular maintenance is performed and potential diesel-specific issues are addressed proactively.
