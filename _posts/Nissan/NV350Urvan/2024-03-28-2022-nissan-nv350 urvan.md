**Introduction:**

The 2022 Nissan NV350 Urvan is a full-size van offered in both passenger and cargo configurations. It is available with a single engine and transmission option: a 3.5L V6 engine paired with a 5-speed automatic transmission. The V6 engine delivers 261 horsepower and 281 lb-ft of torque. The NV350 Urvan is praised for its spacious interior, comfortable ride, and versatile seating options. However, it has received some criticism for its outdated design and lack of modern features.

**Engine**

**3.5L V6 Engine**

* **Oil Leak:** Some owners have reported experiencing oil leaks from the valve cover gasket or oil pan gasket. Symptoms may include a burning smell under the hood, low oil levels on the dipstick, and oil spots under the vehicle. The solution involves replacing the faulty gasket. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600
* **Timing Chain Issues:** There have been isolated reports of timing chain failures on early production models. Symptoms may include a rattling noise from the engine, reduced power, and the check engine light illuminating. The solution involves replacing the timing chain and tensioner. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500
* **Spark Plug Failure:** Premature spark plug failure has been reported, leading to misfires, rough idling, and reduced engine performance. Replacing the faulty spark plugs is the solution. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200

**Transmission**

**5-Speed Automatic Transmission**

* **Shifting Issues:** Some owners have experienced problems with the transmission not shifting smoothly or properly engaging gears. Symptoms may include rough shifting, delayed shifts, or the transmission slipping out of gear. The transmission may need to be rebuilt or replaced to resolve the issue. Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,500 - $4,000+
* **Torque Converter Failure:** There have been reports of torque converter failures, leading to slipping, shuddering, and overheating. Replacing the torque converter is typically required. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000
* **Transmission Fluid Leaks:** Transmission fluid leaks have been reported around the pan, gasket, or cooler lines. Symptoms may include low transmission fluid levels, slipping, and overheating. The source of the leak must be identified and repaired by replacing the faulty component. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600

**Suspension**

* **Rear Suspension Sag:** Some owners have encountered issues with the rear suspension sagging prematurely, leading to poor handling and uneven tire wear. Replacing the rear springs or upgrading to heavy-duty springs can resolve the sagging issue. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain:** There have been reports of parasitic battery drains on certain models. Symptoms may include a dead battery after periods of non-use or difficulty starting the vehicle. Identifying and repairing the source of the drain is necessary. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500
* **Headlight Malfunction:** Some owners have reported issues with the headlights not working or flickering intermittently. Diagnosing the electrical fault and replacing faulty components or wiring is typically the solution. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $400

**Exterior**

* **Windshield Cracked:** While not a direct manufacturing issue, the NV350 Urvan has a large windshield that is prone to cracking due to road debris or temperature changes. Replacing the windshield is the solution. Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800
* **Rust:** There have been isolated reports of premature rust formation on the undercarriage, especially in areas exposed to road salt or moisture. Rust prevention and undercoating can help mitigate the issue. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500

**Interior**

* **HVAC Malfunction:** Some owners have experienced problems with the heating, ventilation, and air conditioning (HVAC) system not working properly. Diagnosing and repairing electrical faults or replacing faulty components is necessary. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800
* **Seat Belt Malfunction:** There have been a few reports of seat belts not locking or retracting properly. Replacing the faulty seat belt assembly is typically the solution. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400

**Summary:**

The 2022 Nissan NV350 Urvan is a reliable full-size van with a spacious interior and proven powertrain. However, it has some known issues that can affect its performance, dependability, and overall value. While the severity of these issues varies, they can be costly to repair. Potential buyers should be aware of these issues and factor them into their purchasing decisions.
