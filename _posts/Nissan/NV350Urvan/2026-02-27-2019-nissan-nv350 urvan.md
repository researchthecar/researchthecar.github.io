# 2019 Nissan NV350 Urvan

### Overview
The 2019 Nissan NV350 Urvan is a commercial van designed for utility and reliability in demanding work environments. It typically features robust mechanical components aimed at longevity and ease of maintenance. While generally considered dependable, like any vehicle, it can encounter specific issues over time, particularly related to its powertrain and auxiliary systems under heavy use. This analysis focuses on documented technical reliability concerns beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential problem areas for owners and fleet managers.

### 2.5L YD25DDTi (Diesel)
**Turbocharger Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of power, excessive smoke, whistling noises from the engine bay.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the turbocharger unit.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1500

**Glow Plug Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Difficulty starting, especially in cold weather, rough idling.
- **Solution:** Replacement of faulty glow plugs.
- **Cost:** $150 - $300


## Transmission

### 5-Speed Manual
**Clutch Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Slipping clutch, difficulty shifting gears, burning smell.
- **Solution:** Replacement of clutch kit (clutch disc, pressure plate, release bearing).
- **Cost:** $500 - $900


### 5-Speed Automatic
**Transmission Fluid Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Leaking fluid, rough shifting, overheating.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of seals and gaskets, fluid flush and refill.
- **Cost:** $300 - $700


## Suspension

### Standard Leaf Spring Rear / Independent Front
**Worn Bushings**
- **Symptoms:** Clunking noises over bumps, vague steering feel, uneven tire wear.
- **Solution:** Replacement of worn suspension bushings.
- **Cost:** $200 - $500


## Electrical

### General Electrical System
**Alternator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Battery warning light illuminated, dimming headlights, electrical components malfunctioning.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the alternator.

### Summary Verdict
The 2019 Nissan NV350 Urvan demonstrates a generally solid reliability record, particularly for its core mechanical components. The petrol engine option is largely trouble-free. The diesel engine, while more powerful, can experience issues like turbocharger wear and glow plug failures, which are not uncommon for this type of powertrain under demanding use. Transmission reliability is good, though clutch wear on manual versions and potential fluid leaks on automatics are noted. Suspension components, especially bushings, may require attention over time due to the van's utility focus. Electrical issues like alternator failure are also documented. Overall, the NV350 Urvan is a dependable workhorse, with most potential issues being manageable and repairable.
