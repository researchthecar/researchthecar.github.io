**Introduction:**

The 2019 Nissan NV350 Urvan is a versatile van that offers ample cargo space and robust performance. It comes equipped with a 2.5L diesel engine paired with a 5-speed manual transmission and boasts 150 horsepower and 310 lb-ft of torque. While the NV350 Urvan serves its purpose well, it has garnered some criticism for its outdated design, lack of modern features, and reported reliability issues.

**Engine**

* **2.5L Diesel**

**Transmission**

* **5-Speed Manual**

**Suspension**

* **No Issues Found**

**Electrical**

* **Alternator Failure**
Description/Symptoms: The alternator fails, resulting in a loss of electrical power to the vehicle. This can cause the battery to drain and the engine to stall.
Solution: Replace the alternator.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800+
Recalls: None Found

* **Battery Drain**
Description/Symptoms: The battery drains prematurely, leaving the vehicle unable to start. This can be caused by a faulty alternator, parasitic draw, or other electrical components.
Solution: Diagnose and repair the underlying cause of the battery drain.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Rust on Body Panels**
Description/Symptoms: Rust forms on the body panels, particularly on the lower sections. This can be caused by exposure to moisture and road salt.
Solution: Repair the rusted areas or replace the affected body panels.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+
Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Noisy Cabin**
Description/Symptoms: The cabin generates excessive noise from wind, road, and engine vibrations. This can be bothersome during long drives.
Solution: Install soundproofing materials or identify and address sources of noise.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
Recalls: None Found

**Overall Summary:**

While the 2019 Nissan NV350 Urvan meets basic transportation needs, its reliability issues and lack of modern features have been a source of concern for owners. The reported electrical problems, such as alternator failures and battery drains, can be significant inconveniences. Corrosion issues on the exterior can also compromise the vehicle's durability. While some interior issues, like the noisy cabin, may not be as severe, they can still affect the overall driving experience. The severity and cost of these issues vary, but they should be taken into consideration when evaluating the vehicle's suitability.
