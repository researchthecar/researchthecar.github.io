The 2015 Nissan Versa Note is a subcompact hatchback known for its affordability, spacious interior, and fuel efficiency. The Versa Note is only available with a 1.6-liter four-cylinder engine producing 109 horsepower and 107 lb-ft of torque. It is available with either a 5-speed manual transmission or a continuously variable transmission (CVT). Owners often praise the car's comfortable ride and ample rear passenger space, while common criticisms include its underwhelming acceleration and basic interior features.

**Engine**

**1.6L I4**

* **Engine Mount Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Excessive engine vibration, especially at idle, knocking or clunking noises from the engine bay during acceleration or deceleration. Visible cracks or tears in the engine mount rubber.
    * Solution: Replacement of the failed engine mount(s).
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Ignition Coil Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Misfires, rough running engine, check engine light illuminated. Diagnostic trouble codes (DTCs) indicating misfires on specific cylinders.
    * Solution: Replacement of the faulty ignition coil(s).
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**CVT (Continuously Variable Transmission)**

* **CVT Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Hesitation during acceleration, jerking or shuddering, especially at low speeds, inability to accelerate, slipping sensation, whining or buzzing noise from the transmission, transmission overheating, check engine light illuminated.
    * Solution: In some cases, a software update or fluid change might temporarily alleviate symptoms. However, complete transmission replacement or rebuild is often necessary.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $3,000 - $5,000+
    * Recalls:
        *   **PC896** (Issued August 2023): Certain Nissan and Infiniti models may have a software issue with the transmission control module (TCM) that can cause the transmission to enter a failsafe mode and lock in a specific gear.
        *   **PC973** (Issued November 2023): Certain Nissan and Infiniti models may have a software issue with the transmission control module (TCM) that can cause the transmission to enter a failsafe mode and lock in a specific gear.

* **Valve Body Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: Erratic shifting, delayed engagement, harsh shifting, slipping, or inability to shift gears. Check engine light may be illuminated.
    * Solution: Replacement of the valve body.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Strut Mount Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Clunking noise coming from the front suspension, especially when going over bumps. Excessive vibration felt through the steering wheel. Poor steering response.
    * Solution: Replacement of the strut mount(s).
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain**
    * Description/Symptoms: Vehicle is difficult to start or will not start. Battery repeatedly dies even after being charged or replaced.
    * Solution: Diagnosis and repair of the parasitic draw. This could involve replacing a faulty module (e.g., Body Control Module (BCM)), repairing wiring issues, or replacing a component that is not shutting off properly.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $1,000+ (depending on the source of the drain)
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Alternator Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Dimming headlights, warning lights on the dashboard (battery light), slow or sluggish starting, stalling, or complete electrical failure.
    * Solution: Replacement of the failed alternator.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **No Issues Found**

**Interior**

* **Air Conditioning Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Air conditioning blows warm air or no air at all. The compressor may not engage.
    * Solution: Diagnosis and repair of the A/C system. This could involve replacing the compressor, condenser, evaporator, or fixing leaks in the refrigerant lines.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1200+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

The 2015 Nissan Versa Note, while economical and spacious, has potential issues that buyers should be aware of. The most significant concern is the reliability of the CVT transmission, which can lead to costly repairs or replacement. Other common problems include engine mount failures and electrical issues. While some repairs may be relatively inexpensive, CVT issues could result in substantial repair bills. Potential buyers should have a pre-purchase inspection performed by a qualified mechanic to assess the condition of the vehicle, especially the CVT transmission, before making a purchase.
