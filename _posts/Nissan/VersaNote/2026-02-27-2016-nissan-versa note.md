# 2016 Nissan Versa Note

### Overview
The 2016 Nissan Versa Note is a subcompact hatchback known for its fuel efficiency and practicality. As a budget-friendly option, its reliability is generally considered decent for its class, though not without some common concerns that can arise with age and mileage. Owners often praise its spacious interior for its size and its maneuverability in urban environments. However, like many vehicles in this segment, some owners have reported specific issues that are worth noting for potential buyers or current owners looking to maintain their vehicle. This data focuses on documented mechanical and electrical concerns beyond routine maintenance.

## Transmission

### CVT (Continuously Variable Transmission)
**Hesitation or Jerking**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle may hesitate during acceleration or exhibit jerky movements, especially at lower speeds or when shifting between 'drive' and 'reverse'.
- **Solution:** Software update for the transmission control module, or in more severe cases, transmission fluid flush and replacement, or potential transmission replacement.
- **Cost:** $150 - $1,200

**Overheating**
- **Symptoms:** Transmission fluid temperature warning light may illuminate, accompanied by a burning smell or reduced performance.
- **Solution:** Check and top off transmission fluid, inspect for leaks, and ensure proper cooling system operation. In some cases, a transmission cooler may be recommended.
- **Cost:** $100 - $500

## Electrical

### General Electrical System
**Infotainment System Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Touchscreen may become unresponsive, freeze, or reboot unexpectedly. Bluetooth connectivity issues can also occur.
- **Solution:** Software update for the infotainment system, or in some cases, a system reset or replacement of the head unit.
- **Cost:** $100 - $800

**Keyless Entry/Start Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** Remote key fob may not be recognized, or the push-button start may fail to engage the engine.
- **Solution:** Replace key fob battery, reprogram key fob, or inspect the vehicle's immobilizer system and related wiring.
- **Cost:** $50 - $300

### Summary Verdict
The 2016 Nissan Versa Note generally offers a reliable ownership experience, particularly with its powertrain, which is known for its longevity when properly maintained. The most frequently reported issues tend to be related to the CVT transmission, which can exhibit hesitation or overheating under certain conditions, and occasional glitches with the infotainment system or keyless entry. These are not typically catastrophic failures but can be inconvenient and require attention. For buyers, a pre-purchase inspection focusing on the transmission and electrical components is advisable. Overall, the Versa Note remains a practical and economical choice, with most potential issues being manageable.
