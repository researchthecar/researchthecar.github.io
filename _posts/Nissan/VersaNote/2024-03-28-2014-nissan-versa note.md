**Introduction:**

The 2014 Nissan Versa Note is a subcompact hatchback offered with three engine options: a 1.6-liter four-cylinder (109 horsepower and 107 lb-ft of torque), a 1.8-liter four-cylinder (131 horsepower and 125 lb-ft of torque), and an HR16DE 1.6-liter four-cylinder (110 horsepower and 106 lb-ft of torque). Transmission options include a five-speed manual, a Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT), and a four-speed automatic.

The Versa Note is praised for its affordability, fuel efficiency, and spacious interior. However, it has received some complaints about its underwhelming performance and handling.

**Engine**

* **1.6-Liter Four-Cylinder (109 Horsepower)**
    * **Spark Plug Fouling:** Fouled spark plugs can cause misfiring and reduced engine performance.
        * Solution: Replace spark plugs.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $150
    * **Intake Valve Deposits:** Carbon buildup on intake valves can lead to rough idling, reduced power, and increased emissions.
        * Solution: Clean intake valves.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
* **1.8-Liter Four-Cylinder (131 Horsepower)**
    * **Timing Chain Tensioner Failure:** A faulty timing chain tensioner can result in engine knocking or rattling, potentially leading to catastrophic engine failure.
        * Solution: Replace timing chain tensioner and related components.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,500
        * Recalls: N/A
* **HR16DE 1.6-Liter Four-Cylinder (110 Horsepower)**
    * **No Issues Found**

**Transmission**

* **Five-Speed Manual**
    * **Difficulty Shifting:** Gears may become difficult to engage or disengage.
        * Solution: Repair or replace shifter linkage or transmission components.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
* **Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)**
    * **Jerking or Shuddering:** The CVT may experience shuddering or jerking during acceleration or deceleration.
        * Solution: Reprogram transmission control module (TCM) or replace CVT.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000
        * Recalls: N/A
* **Four-Speed Automatic**
    * **Transmission Fluid Leak:** A leak in the transmission can lead to low fluid levels and transmission damage.
        * Solution: Repair the leak and replace transmission fluid.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $250 - $600

**Suspension**

* **Rear Suspension:**
    * **Premature Rear Coil Spring Failure:** Rear coil springs may break prematurely, causing a loss of ride height and handling issues.
        * Solution: Replace rear coil springs.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain:** A parasitic draw can drain the battery when the vehicle is turned off.
    * Solution: Diagnose and repair faulty electrical components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $400
* **Alternator Failure:** A faulty alternator can fail to charge the battery, leading to starting problems.
    * Solution: Replace alternator.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800

**Exterior**

* **Plastic Trim Fading:** Black plastic trim pieces may fade or discolor prematurely.
    * Solution: Replace plastic trim pieces.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600

**Interior**

* **Seat Cushion Wear:** The driver's seat cushion may show premature wear.
    * Solution: Repair or replace seat cushion.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
* **Infotainment System Freeze:** The infotainment system may freeze or become unresponsive.
    * Solution: Reprogram or replace infotainment system.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000

**Summary:**

Overall, the 2014 Nissan Versa Note has a mix of minor and moderate issues. The most severe issues include timing chain tensioner failure, CVT shuddering, and rear coil spring breakage. Repair costs can range from as low as $50 for spark plug replacement to over $2,000 for a new CVT. While some issues may require attention, the Versa Note generally offers a reliable and affordable option in its class.
