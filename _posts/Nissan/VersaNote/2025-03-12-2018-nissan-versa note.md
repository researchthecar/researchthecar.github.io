The 2018 Nissan Versa Note is a subcompact hatchback known for its affordability and fuel efficiency. It comes with a 1.6-liter four-cylinder engine producing 109 horsepower and 107 lb-ft of torque, paired exclusively with a Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT). Owners often appreciate its spacious interior for its class and its easy maneuverability. Common complaints include underwhelming acceleration, a basic interior, and road noise.

**Engine**

**1.6L Four-Cylinder Engine**

*   **Ignition Coil Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Misfires, rough idling, check engine light.
    *   Solution: Replace faulty ignition coil(s).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+ per coil (parts and labor)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Catalytic Converter Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Check engine light, reduced engine performance, rattling noise from exhaust.
    *   Solution: Replace the catalytic converter.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1200+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)**

*   **CVT Transmission Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Shuddering, slipping, delayed engagement, or complete transmission failure. This is often preceded by a whining noise that gets progressively worse.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the CVT. In some cases, a software update might temporarily improve performance, but it's usually a sign of imminent failure.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $3000 - $5000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Valve Body Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Erratic shifting, hesitation, jerking during acceleration.
    *   Solution: Replace or rebuild the valve body.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Rear Shock Absorber Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Bouncy ride, excessive body roll, clunking noise from the rear suspension.
    *   Solution: Replace the rear shock absorbers.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+ (for both)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Battery Drain**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Car is difficult to start or won't start, especially after sitting for a short period.
    *   Solution: Identify the source of the parasitic drain (e.g., faulty sensor, aftermarket accessory) and repair or replace it. Alternatively, replace the battery if it's old or faulty.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+ (battery replacement), diagnostic fees may apply for parasitic drain
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Door Lock Actuator Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Doors won't lock or unlock properly.
    *   Solution: Replace faulty actuator.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $350+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Rusting**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rust spots forming on wheel wells and door panels.
    *   Solution: Remove rust and repair
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100-$1000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Air Conditioning Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: A/C blows warm air, A/C compressor not engaging.
    *   Solution: Diagnose and repair the A/C system (e.g., recharge refrigerant, replace compressor, replace condenser).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1200+ (depending on the component)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2018 Nissan Versa Note presents a mix of potential issues. The most concerning are the potential for CVT transmission problems, which can be quite expensive to fix. Electrical issues and suspension problems are also common. Prospective buyers should be aware of these potential issues and have a mechanic inspect the car before purchase.
