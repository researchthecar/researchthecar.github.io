# 2018 Nissan Versa Note Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2018 Nissan Versa Note is an affordable, fuel-efficient subcompact hatchback designed primarily for budget-conscious commuters and city drivers. While overall owner complaint volume is relatively low for this model year, buyers should review powertrain functionality and mechanical history carefully before purchasing a used example.

---

## Engine

### 1.6L 4-Cylinder Engine

Powered by a 1.6-liter naturally aspirated 4-cylinder engine producing 109 horsepower and 107 lb-ft of torque, providing adequate power for daily urban commuting. This engine is generally reliable and paired with front-wheel drive, though it prioritizes fuel economy over spirited acceleration.

## Transmission

### Xtronic Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)

Uses a continuously variable transmission (CVT) sending power to the front wheels, engineered to maximize fuel efficiency. Buyers should thoroughly test-drive the vehicle to check for smooth operation and responsiveness, as CVT units require attentive maintenance.

**Unexpected Gear Shift**
- **Symptoms:** An owner reported that the vehicle shifted into reverse on its own while traveling at low speeds and attempting to turn, resulting in a minor collision.
- **Solution:** Have the transmission control module, shift linkage, and mechanical assembly inspected by a qualified professional to ensure proper operation.

## Brakes

### Brake Pedal and Front Brakes

Standard braking components provide predictable stopping power for the subcompact hatchback class, though rotors and pads should be inspected for wear during a pre-purchase check.

**Brake Pulsation and Thumping Noise**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may experience a pulsating sensation in the brake pedal accompanied by a loud thumping noise from the front left area when slowing down from highway speeds.
- **Solution:** Inspect brake rotors for warping or uneven wear, and check front suspension and brake components for loose or damaged parts.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V551000: Electrical System:Ignition](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=Versa+Note&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** In these models, over time, the weight on the key ring and road conditions or some other jarring event may cause the ignition switch to move out of the run position to the accessory position, turning off the engine. If the vehicle stalls, it can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Nissan will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the production information on the ignition switch, and replace it as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V654000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=Versa+Note&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 111, "Rear Visibility." The lack of an image in the back-up camera display increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 17V837000: Air Bags:Side/Window](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Nissan&model=Versa+Note&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** During deployment of the side curtain air bag, a component of the air bag inflator may detach. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standards (FMVSS) number 214, "Side Impact Protection," and 226, "Ejection Mitigation." If the component detaches during deployment, it may enter the passenger compartment.
- **What to do:** Nissan will notify owners, and dealers will replace the side curtain air bags, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2018 Nissan Versa Note offers straightforward, economical transportation, but used buyers should verify the condition of the CVT transmission and braking system during a test drive. NHTSA owner reports highlight isolated incidents regarding unintended movement and brake pedal pulsation, so a thorough pre-purchase inspection is recommended.
