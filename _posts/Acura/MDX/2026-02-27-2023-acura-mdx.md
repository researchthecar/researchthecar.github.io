# 2023 Acura MDX Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2023 Acura MDX is a sophisticated and well-regarded three-row luxury crossover that offers sharp handling, a refined cabin, and capable performance. When shopping for a used model, it is helpful to look past its stellar styling and review owner-reported feedback. While most drivers enjoy a trouble-free experience, early production models have surfaced a handful of recurring owner complaints regarding electrical glitches, advanced safety system sensitivities, and mechanical wear items.

---

## Engine

### 3.5L V6 Engine

The standard 3.5-liter naturally aspirated V6 engine produces approximately 290 horsepower and 267 lb-ft of torque, delivering smooth power delivery suited for daily family driving. Overall mechanical durability is strong, though isolated owner reports mention low-speed throttle response nuances.

### 3.0L Turbocharged V6 Engine

Exclusive to the high-performance Type S trim, the 3.0-liter turbocharged V6 generates around 355 horsepower and 354 lb-ft of torque, paired with standard Super Handling All-Wheel Drive (SH-AWD). This powertrain offers brisk acceleration and an athletic personality for enthusiast drivers.

## Transmission

### 10-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 10-speed automatic transmission manages power across both engine options, featuring paddle shifters and responsive gear selection. While generally robust, a small number of drivers note occasional low-speed hesitation or rough shifting behavior.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

Brake system complaints focus primarily on intermittent pedal firmness issues and accelerated rear pad wear linked to caliper operation.

**Brake Pedal Stiffness and Response Loss**
- **Symptoms:** The brake pedal can occasionally feel extremely hard to press, resulting in a temporary loss of stopping power or delayed deceleration.
- **Solution:** Have the brake booster, vacuum lines, and hydraulic system inspected immediately by a qualified technician.

**Uneven Rear Brake Pad Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Rear brake pads wear out prematurely on one side due to binding or malfunctioning brake calipers.
- **Solution:** Inspect rear brake calipers for proper sliding function and replace worn pads and damaged calipers as necessary.

## Electrical

### Electrical System

Electrical architecture handles numerous convenience and safety modules, with some owners reporting sensor sensitivity when encountering rough road surfaces.

**Multiple Dashboard Warning Lights and Limp Mode**
- **Symptoms:** Driving over rough road surfaces can trigger a cascade of warning lights on the dashboard, occasionally forcing the vehicle into limp mode.
- **Solution:** Software updates performed by a dealership often resolve false error codes and communication glitches among sensors.

## Safety

### Forward Collision Avoidance

Advanced safety features enhance occupant protection, but overly aggressive collision avoidance sensors can occasionally trigger false braking events.

**Unexpected Automatic Emergency Braking**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may unexpectedly and harshly slam on the brakes while entering parking spaces or navigating low-speed areas.
- **Solution:** Visit a dealership to check for software updates, sensor recalibration, or alignment adjustments for the front camera and radar systems.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V391000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Pedals And Linkages](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Acura&model=MDX&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard numbers 108, "Lamps, Reflective Devices, and Associated Equipment" and 135, "Light Vehicle Brake Systems." A brake pedal that shifts out of position can prevent the driver from applying the brakes as intended, increasing the risk of a crash or injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the brake pedal assembly as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V332000: Air Bags:Sensor:Occupant Classification:Front Passenger](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Acura&model=MDX&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** The front passenger seat weight sensor may crack and short circuit, which can cause the air bags to deploy unintentionally during a crash. Air bags that deploy unintentionally during a crash increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the seat weight sensors, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2023 Acura MDX remains a compelling choice in the luxury midsize SUV segment, offering exceptional comfort, strong engine options, and rich technology features. Used-car buyers should perform a thorough test drive checking for smooth transmission shifts, proper braking responsiveness, and flawless operation of all driver-assistance systems. Ensuring that any applicable software updates have been completed will help secure a dependable ownership experience.
