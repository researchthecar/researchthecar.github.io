# 2019 Acura MDX Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2019 Acura MDX is a popular three-row luxury crossover known for its comfort, feature content, and available Super Handling All-Wheel Drive (SH-AWD). While generally holding a reputation for durability, used-car buyers should review potential trouble spots reported by owners, particularly concerning electrical issues, forward-collision sensor glitches, and sporadic powertrain or engine stalling concerns.

---

## Engine

### 3.5L V6

The standard 3.5-liter V6 engine produces approximately 290 horsepower and 267 lb-ft of torque, offering smooth and reliable power delivery for daily driving.

### 3.0L V6 Hybrid

The Sport Hybrid variant pairs a 3.0-liter V6 with a three-motor electric system for roughly 321 combined horsepower, blending efficiency with responsive all-wheel drive performance.

### Engine Electrical & Stalling

Owners have logged occasional complaints regarding unexpected engine stalls and warning lights related to emissions or electrical systems.

**Engine Stalling and Loss of Motive Power**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers report unexpected engine stalling while idling at stoplights or sudden losses of motive power on the highway accompanied by electrical warning lights.
- **Solution:** Diagnose fault codes, inspect fuel pump operation and electrical connections, and update software or replace failing components as necessary.

## Transmission

### 9-Speed Automatic Transmission

The standard 9-speed automatic transmission pairs with the 3.5L V6, though rare owner reports point to erratic shifting behavior.

**Unintended Neutral Shift**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may unexpectedly shift into neutral or park itself while driving or maneuvering at low speeds, causing a sudden loss of drive power.
- **Solution:** Inspect transmission control modules and software calibration; software updates or hardware repairs may be required.

### 7-Speed Dual-Clutch Transmission

Exclusive to the Sport Hybrid, this 7-speed dual-clutch transmission delivers crisp, quick gear changes integrated with the hybrid electric motor setup.

## Steering

### Steering & Suspension Components

The suspension provides a compliant ride, though high-mileage models can develop wear in steering linkage and tie rods.

**Outer Tie Rod Clunking and Loose Steering**
- **Symptoms:** A noticeable clunking noise or looseness coming from the front suspension and outer tie rod areas during low-speed turns or rough roads.
- **Solution:** Inspect front suspension tie rods and replace worn components per updated service bulletins.

## Exterior

### Exterior Lighting

Electrical accessories function well overall, but moisture accumulation in exterior lamp housings has frustrated some owners.

**Taillight and Headlight Moisture Intrusion**
- **Symptoms:** Condensation build-up inside headlight or taillight housings, leading to premature component failure or non-illuminating assembly lights.
- **Solution:** Inspect light seals for cracks or leaks and replace affected headlight or taillight assemblies.
- **Cost:** $300 for parts only (per taillight)

## Interior

### Seat Mechanism

Cabin materials are generally durable, but front seat track stability can degrade over years of use.

**Loose Driver and Passenger Seats**
- **Symptoms:** The front seats develop physical play, shifting back and forth slightly during acceleration or deceleration while making clicking or loose noises.
- **Solution:** Inspect seat track mechanisms and internal framing; tighten mounting bolts or replace worn seat hardware.

## Safety

### Forward Collision & Camera Systems

Advanced driver-assistance systems provide helpful safety margins, though occasional windshield-mounted camera and sensor errors can temporarily disable features.

**Unexpected Braking and Sensor Failures**
- **Symptoms:** The AcuraWatch safety suite may trigger false collision warnings, unexpected emergency braking, or display sudden errors for LKAS, ACC, and camera systems.
- **Solution:** Check windshield camera alignment, clean radar/camera lenses, and have the dealer inspect for control module or software faults.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V215000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Acura&model=MDX&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** Fuel pump failure can cause an engine stall while driving, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Honda will notify owners, and dealers will replace the fuel pump assembly, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V256000: Exterior Lighting:Tail Lights](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Acura&model=MDX&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** Loss of the taillights reduces the vehicle's visibility, increasing the risk of crash.
- **What to do:** Acura will notify owners, and dealers will modify the tailgate lid lights and install updated gaskets and a wiring sub-harness or will replace both tailgate lid lights. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V298000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Engine:Gasoline:Belts And Associated Pulleys](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Acura&model=MDX&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** Teeth separation from the timing belt may result in an engine stall, increasing the risk of crash.
- **What to do:** Honda will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the vehicle and replace any affected timing belt free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V777000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Foundation Components:Disc:Caliper](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Acura&model=MDX&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 135, "Light Vehicle Brake Systems." A reduction of braking performance increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Owners will be notified, and Honda or Acura dealers will bleed the brakes, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V751000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Engine:Hard Parts Internal/Mechanical](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Acura&model=MDX&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** A damaged engine may run improperly or stall while driving, increasing the risk of a fire, crash, or injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and repair, or replace the engine as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V858000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Acura&model=MDX&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** Fuel pump failure can cause an engine stall while driving, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the fuel pump module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V259000: Exterior Lighting](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Acura&model=MDX&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** Vehicle lights that fail to illuminate can decrease visibility and increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the wiring and install a fuse harness, replace the lid light, and repair the body dust sealer, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V332000: Air Bags:Sensor:Occupant Classification:Front Passenger](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Acura&model=MDX&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** The front passenger seat weight sensor may crack and short circuit, which can cause the air bags to deploy unintentionally during a crash. Air bags that deploy unintentionally during a crash increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the seat weight sensors, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2019 Acura MDX remains a compelling choice for families seeking a luxury three-row SUV with strong performance and safety features. While most owners enjoy trouble-free driving, prospective buyers should thoroughly test drive the vehicle to check for any transmission shifting irregularities, electronic safety system warnings, or suspension noises. A pre-purchase inspection by a qualified mechanic is recommended to verify overall mechanical health.
