The 2006 Acura MDX is a popular mid-size luxury SUV known for its reliability, comfortable ride, and spacious interior. It comes standard with a 3.5L V6 engine producing 253 horsepower and 250 lb-ft of torque. The engine is paired exclusively with a 5-speed automatic transmission. Owners appreciate the MDX for its smooth power delivery, comfortable seating, and well-appointed cabin. Some common complaints include the relatively low fuel economy and issues related to the navigation system.

**Engine**

**3.5L V6**

*   **Oil Consumption**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported excessive oil consumption, requiring frequent topping off of the oil level between scheduled oil changes. This can lead to low oil levels and potential engine damage if not monitored.
    *   Solution: Regular monitoring of oil levels and addressing any leaks. In severe cases, piston ring replacement may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

*   **VTEC Solenoid Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The VTEC (Variable Valve Timing and Lift Electronic Control) solenoid can fail, leading to a loss of power and/or a check engine light.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the VTEC solenoid.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**5-Speed Automatic**

*   **Transmission Slipping/Harsh Shifting**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have experienced transmission slippage, particularly when shifting between gears, and harsh or delayed shifting.
    *   Solution: A transmission fluid change may alleviate the issue. In more severe cases, a transmission rebuild or replacement may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 (fluid change) - $2,500 - $4,500+ (rebuild/replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Torque Converter Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The torque converter can fail, causing shuddering or vibrations at low speeds, especially during acceleration.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the torque converter.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Rear Strut Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The rear struts may leak or fail, leading to a bouncy ride and reduced handling.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the rear struts.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Navigation System Failure/Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The navigation system screen may fail, freeze, or display inaccurate information. The navigation disc drive may also malfunction.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the navigation unit or repair of the disc drive.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+ (repair), $1000+ (replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Power Door Locks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Power door locks failing to actuate.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the door lock actuator
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Sunroof Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The sunroof may leak or get stuck in the open or closed position.
    *   Solution: Cleaning and lubrication of the sunroof tracks and seals. In some cases, motor replacement may be required.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Climate Control Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Climate control system malfunctions, such as failing to blow cold air or inconsistent temperature control.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis and repair of the climate control system, which may involve replacing the compressor, actuator, or other components.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Automatic Liftgate Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The automatic liftgate may fail to open or close properly, or make unusual noises during operation.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis and repair of the liftgate mechanism, which may involve replacing the motor, struts, or sensors.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**
The 2006 Acura MDX, while generally reliable, has some common issues to watch out for. Engine oil consumption and transmission problems, particularly with the 5-speed automatic, can be costly to address. Electrical issues, especially with the navigation system, are also common. The severity and cost of repairs can vary widely, but addressing these issues promptly can help maintain the vehicle's longevity and performance.
