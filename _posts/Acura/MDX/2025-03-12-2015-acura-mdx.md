The 2015 Acura MDX is a popular luxury SUV known for its reliability, comfortable ride, and spacious interior. It came standard with a 3.5-liter V6 engine producing 290 horsepower and 267 lb-ft of torque, paired with a 9-speed automatic transmission. Many owners appreciate the MDX for its fuel efficiency, smooth handling, and family-friendly features. However, some common complaints include issues with the infotainment system, particularly its responsiveness and complexity, as well as some concerns about the transmission's shift quality in earlier model years.

**Engine**

**3.5L V6**

*   **Oil Consumption**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners report excessive oil consumption, requiring them to add oil between scheduled oil changes. This can manifest as the low oil light illuminating or the need to add a quart of oil every few thousand miles.
    *   Solution: In some cases, a faulty Positive Crankcase Ventilation (PCV) valve can contribute to oil consumption and replacing it may help. More severe cases may require engine teardown and repair, potentially involving piston ring replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $200+ (PCV Valve), $2,000 - $5,000+ (Engine Repair)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Variable Timing Control (VTC) Actuator Rattle**
    *   Description/Symptoms: A rattling noise upon cold start-up, usually lasting for a few seconds. This is often caused by the VTC actuator not holding oil pressure properly.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the VTC actuator.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**9-Speed Automatic**

*   **Rough Shifting/Hesitation**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The 9-speed automatic transmission can exhibit rough shifting, particularly between lower gears. Some owners also report hesitation or reluctance to downshift when accelerating.
    *   Solution: A software update (TSB) from Acura may improve the transmission's shift quality. In more severe cases, valve body replacement or transmission replacement may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+ (Software Update), $1,000 - $2,500+ (Valve Body), $3,000 - $6,000+ (Transmission Replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The infotainment system can be slow to respond, freeze, or experience glitches. Issues may include problems with the navigation system, audio controls, and Bluetooth connectivity.
    *   Solution: Software updates can often resolve infotainment system issues. In some cases, replacing the infotainment unit may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 - $200+ (Software Update), $500 - $1,500+ (Infotainment Unit Replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Battery Drain**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Premature battery drain. Often, the battery will be dead in the morning, and needs to be jumped.
    *   Solution: Diagnostic testing to find parasitic draw.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $1,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Sunroof Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: The sunroof may get stuck, squeak loudly, or fail to close completely. This can be caused by a worn motor or a bent track.
    * Solution: The sunroof motor or entire assembly must be replaced
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Automatic Tailgate Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The automatic tailgate may fail to open or close properly, or it may make unusual noises.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the tailgate struts or motor may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2015 Acura MDX is a generally reliable vehicle, but like any car, it has its share of potential issues. Engine issues such as excessive oil consumption can be concerning and potentially costly to repair. The 9-speed transmission's rough shifting is a common complaint, and while software updates may help, more significant repairs could be needed. Electrical problems, particularly with the infotainment system, can be frustrating. Overall, while the MDX is a solid choice, potential buyers should be aware of these common issues and factor in the possibility of repairs when considering a purchase. The severity of the issues varies, with some being minor inconveniences and others potentially leading to significant repair bills.
