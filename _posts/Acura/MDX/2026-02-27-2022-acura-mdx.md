# 2022 Acura MDX Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a pre-owned midsize luxury SUV, the model year offers a refined cabin, impressive technology, and strong overall capability. However, buyers should carefully review specific mechanical and electronic subsystems during a pre-purchase inspection. While many drivers enjoy smooth daily commuting, certain persistent owner concerns have surfaced regarding all-wheel drive integration, advanced driver assistance features, and electrical routing.

---

## Engine

### 3.5L V6

This naturally aspirated 3.5-liter V6 engine produces approximately 290 horsepower and 267 lb-ft of torque, offering responsive power delivery for daily driving. It is generally considered robust and dependable, forming a reliable backbone for the vehicle.

### 3.0L Turbocharged V6

Found in performance-oriented trims, this turbocharged V6 generates around 355 horsepower and 354 lb-ft of torque for a sportier driving dynamic. Buyers should expect higher performance upkeep costs while appreciating the spirited acceleration.

## Transmission

### 10-Speed Automatic

The 10-speed automatic transmission paired with available Super Handling All-Wheel Drive (SH-AWD) provides smooth gear changes and optimized efficiency. While shifts are generally seamless, potential buyers should thoroughly check the health of the transfer case and drivetrain splines.

**Transfer Case and Spline Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers experience a sudden loss of all-wheel drive capability, abnormal drivetrain noises, or unexpected front-wheel-drive-only operation accompanied by warning lights.
- **Solution:** Requires dealership inspection to assess transfer case wear and replace damaged transmission and transfer case components.

**Transmission Slipping and Acceleration Hesitation**
- **Symptoms:** Engine RPMs may increase without a corresponding acceleration in vehicle speed, or drivers may feel a harsh clunk and temporary loss of motive power.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified technician check powertrain control modules, fluid conditions, and internal mechanical components for wear.

## Suspension

### Air Suspension and Stroke Sensors

The available adaptive air suspension system enhances ride comfort and body control on high-end trims. However, complex electronic ride-height sensors and wiring harnesses require careful inspection for wear or moisture exposure over time.

**Adaptive Suspension Warnings and Component Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Dashboard warning messages illuminate indicating an adaptive air suspension problem, sometimes followed by a ruptured air bag or ride height faults.
- **Solution:** Inspect ride height stroke sensors, wiring harnesses, and air springs for physical damage or moisture intrusion, and replace failed units.

## Steering

### Electric Power Steering Column

The electric power steering setup provides precise handling feedback and easy low-speed maneuverability. Maintenance history should be checked for prior steering column replacements or unresolved noises.

**Power Steering Interruption and Grinding Noise**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may encounter sudden losses of power steering assist while driving or hear loud vibrations and grinding noises when the steering column extends or retracts.
- **Solution:** Inspect steering column components, electronic control modules, and related wiring, replacing worn mechanical assemblies as needed.

## Electrical

### Floor Wiring Harness

Onboard electronics govern numerous luxury and safety features, making robust wiring essential. Buyers should verify that interior wire harnesses are free from moisture damage or corrosion-related faults.

**Wiring Harness Moisture Corrosion**
- **Symptoms:** Accumulated moisture near the floor harness causes sensor connector corrosion, triggering multiple electrical fault codes across various vehicle systems.
- **Solution:** Inspect the floor harness and sensor connectors for moisture intrusion and corrosion, and replace compromised wiring sections.
- **Cost:** $8,000

## Safety

### Forward Collision Avoidance

Advanced safety and driver-assistance suites offer valuable collision mitigation and adaptive cruise control capabilities. Ensuring sensor cleanliness and proper calibration is crucial for consistent operation.

**Unexpected Braking and Lane Assist Faults**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may unexpectedly engage emergency braking or trigger stability and lane-keep assist warnings without an immediate road hazard.
- **Solution:** Check camera and radar sensor alignments, clear obstructed lenses, and update software modules at an authorized service center.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V064000: Air Bags:Sensor:Occupant Classification:Front Passenger](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Acura&model=MDX&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** The front passenger seat weight sensor may crack and short circuit, failing to suppress the air bag as intended. An air bag that deploys unintentionally during a crash can increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the seat weight sensors, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V332000: Air Bags:Sensor:Occupant Classification:Front Passenger](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Acura&model=MDX&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** The front passenger seat weight sensor may crack and short circuit, which can cause the air bags to deploy unintentionally during a crash. Air bags that deploy unintentionally during a crash increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the seat weight sensors, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.
