# 2018 Acura MDX Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2018 Acura MDX delivers a blend of luxury, utility, and refined driving dynamics, making it a popular choice in the used midsize three-row crossover segment. While it generally boasts solid engineering and comforting road manners, used car shoppers should review recurring owner complaints concerning certain powertrain hesitation, electrical sensors, and accessory components before making a purchase.

---

## Engine

### 3.5L V6 Engine

The 3.5-liter V6 engine produces approximately 290 horsepower and 267 lb-ft of torque, providing smooth and capable acceleration. Overall, the powerplant is robust, though routine maintenance is essential to avoid potential fuel and electrical sensor glitches.

**Engine Stalling and Power Loss**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have reported unexpected engine stalling, hesitation, and a sudden loss of motive power while operating the vehicle.
- **Solution:** Diagnose fuel delivery and sensor components; inspect and replace faulty parts or update engine management software as needed.

### Sport Hybrid 3.0L V6 Engine

The 3.0-liter V6 Sport Hybrid powertrain pairs the gasoline engine with electric motors to deliver a combined 321 horsepower and standard all-wheel drive. It offers enhanced efficiency and brisk throttle response with very few mechanical owner complaints.

## Transmission

### 9-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 9-speed automatic transmission is found on standard front-wheel-drive and select all-wheel-drive models, designed to optimize fuel economy on the highway. While generally durable, early software calibrations have drawn scrutiny from some drivers regarding shift smoothness.

**Shift Hesitation and Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have experienced rough shifting, hesitation when accelerating from a complete stop, and intermittent transmission noise.
- **Solution:** Check for available transmission software updates or consult a qualified technician for mechanical inspection.

### 7-Speed Dual-Clutch Transmission

The 7-speed dual-clutch transmission comes paired exclusively with the Sport Hybrid variant to provide lightning-fast gear changes. It adds a sporty character to the hybrid model without generating widespread powertrain complaints.

## Exterior

### Exterior Lighting

Exterior lighting systems provide reliable illumination, though rear light housings occasionally suffer from moisture seals degrading.

**Tail Light Water Intrusion**
- **Symptoms:** Moisture accumulates inside the rear taillight assemblies, potentially leading to electrical shorts or premature component failure.
- **Solution:** Inspect housing seals and replace the affected taillight assemblies to prevent water ingress.

### Sunroof

Exterior glass and body panels are generally well-constructed, but isolated reports of spontaneous sunroof glass breakage warrant buyer awareness.

**Unexpected Glass Shattering**
- **Symptoms:** The sunroof glass panel produces a loud bang and shatters spontaneously while driving at highway speeds.
- **Solution:** Replace the damaged sunroof glass panel and check track alignment to ensure proper structural fit.

## Interior

### Driver Seat Assembly

The cabin offers comfortable seating and premium amenities, though mechanical seat mounting hardware requires close inspection during a used vehicle evaluation.

**Seat Base Slippage**
- **Symptoms:** The driver's seat shifts or rocks out of place unexpectedly while the vehicle is in motion.
- **Solution:** Inspect seat base rivets and mounting hardware, and repair or replace components according to dealer specifications.

## Safety

### AcuraWatch Forward Collision and Lane Departure System

Advanced safety features enhance occupant protection, but failures in the optical sensors can temporarily disable assist systems.

**Multipurpose Camera Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Dashboard warning lights illuminate for active safety systems, and features like adaptive cruise control and collision mitigation become unavailable.
- **Solution:** Inspect the windshield-mounted camera assembly and associated diagnostic trouble codes, and replace the unit if faulty.
- **Cost:** $1700

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V060000: Fuel System, Other:Delivery:Fuel Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Acura&model=MDX&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** Reduced fuel pump performance can result in an engine stall, increasing the risk of crash.
- **What to do:** Honda will notify owners, and dealers will update the fuel injection engine control unit (FI-ECU) software and, if necessary, replace the fuel pump, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V256000: Exterior Lighting:Tail Lights](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Acura&model=MDX&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** Loss of the taillights reduces the vehicle's visibility, increasing the risk of crash.
- **What to do:** Acura will notify owners, and dealers will modify the tailgate lid lights and install updated gaskets and a wiring sub-harness or will replace both tailgate lid lights. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V298000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Engine:Gasoline:Belts And Associated Pulleys](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Acura&model=MDX&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** Teeth separation from the timing belt may result in an engine stall, increasing the risk of crash.
- **What to do:** Honda will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the vehicle and replace any affected timing belt free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V777000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Foundation Components:Disc:Caliper](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Acura&model=MDX&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 135, "Light Vehicle Brake Systems." A reduction of braking performance increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Owners will be notified, and Honda or Acura dealers will bleed the brakes, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V221000: Seats](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Acura&model=MDX&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** In the event of a crash, the rivets can break causing the seats not to be secured to the floor. As such, these vehicles may fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 207, "Seating Systems," and 210, "Seat Belt Assembly Anchorages." In the event of a crash, if the seat does not remain secured to the floor.
- **What to do:** Honda will notify owners, and dealers will replace the driver seat frame assembly in Acura MDX vehicles, and replace the driver and front passenger seat slide rail frames in Honda Pilot and Ridgeline vehicles, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V751000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Engine:Hard Parts Internal/Mechanical](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Acura&model=MDX&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** A damaged engine may run improperly or stall while driving, increasing the risk of a fire, crash, or injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and repair, or replace the engine as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V858000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Acura&model=MDX&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** Fuel pump failure can cause an engine stall while driving, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the fuel pump module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V259000: Exterior Lighting](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Acura&model=MDX&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** Vehicle lights that fail to illuminate can decrease visibility and increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the wiring and install a fuse harness, replace the lid light, and repair the body dust sealer, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2018 Acura MDX remains a compelling option for buyers seeking a feature-rich, comfortable luxury crossover. While most owners experience reliable daily operation, prospective buyers should verify that all safety sensor systems, transmission software updates, and seat hardware components have been thoroughly inspected and serviced.
