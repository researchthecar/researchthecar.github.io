# 2024 Acura MDX Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2024 Acura MDX delivers strong performance, a spacious and premium cabin, and sophisticated tech features. However, prospective buyers should review owner-reported concerns regarding powertrain electronic glitches, braking performance, and occasional body or glass defects.

---

## Engine

### 3.5L V6 Direct Injection

This naturally aspirated 3.5-liter V6 engine produces approximately 290 horsepower and 267 lb-ft of torque, providing smooth and reliable everyday performance for this three-row luxury SUV.

**Engine Stalling and Start-Stop Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may unexpectedly stall or fail to restart after utilizing the auto start-stop system, requiring multiple attempts before functioning normally.
- **Solution:** Have a certified technician inspect the start-stop switch, starter module, and Powertrain Control Module (PCM) for software updates or faulty hardware.

### 3.0L Turbo V6 (Type S)

The high-performance Type S trim features a turbocharged 3.0-liter V6 delivering 355 horsepower and 354 lb-ft of torque, paired exclusively with Acura's Super Handling All-Wheel Drive system.

## Transmission

### 10-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 10-speed automatic transmission delivers crisp and efficient shifting across front-wheel drive and all-wheel drive configurations, though electronic controls require proper calibration.

**Shift and Park Sensor Warnings**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may occasionally encounter electronic warnings such as parking sensor failures or transmission/brakes reduced performance alerts.
- **Solution:** Diagnostic scanning is required to clear fault codes and check for software recalibrations or sensor replacements.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

Braking components provide strong stopping power under normal conditions, but sporadic pedal feel anomalies have been reported by some owners.

**Brake Pedal Travel and Sinking**
- **Symptoms:** The brake pedal may intermittently feel soft or sink toward the floor while the vehicle is stopped in traffic.
- **Solution:** Inspect the master cylinder, brake lines, and hydraulic pressure sensors for leaks or internal bypass issues.

## Electrical

### Infotainment and Body Electronics

Advanced electronic features offer great convenience, but complex wiring harnesses and control units demand careful inspection for moisture or software stability.

**Infotainment Blackout and Water Intrusion**
- **Symptoms:** The digital display or infotainment system may fail completely, sometimes associated with water leaks or trunk area moisture.
- **Solution:** Check sunroof drains and trunk seals for blockages, and have the electrical control modules tested for water damage.

## Exterior

### Glass and Sunroof

Exterior styling and visibility glass are generally robust, though a limited number of isolated glass failure incidents have been documented.

**Spontaneous Sunroof Shattering**
- **Symptoms:** The panoramic sunroof glass may unexpectedly fracture or shatter while driving at highway speeds without prior impact.
- **Solution:** Replace the damaged glass assembly and inspect the surrounding frame alignment and mounting tension.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V391000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Pedals And Linkages](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Acura&model=MDX&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard numbers 108, "Lamps, Reflective Devices, and Associated Equipment" and 135, "Light Vehicle Brake Systems." A brake pedal that shifts out of position can prevent the driver from applying the brakes as intended, increasing the risk of a crash or injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the brake pedal assembly as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V332000: Air Bags:Sensor:Occupant Classification:Front Passenger](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Acura&model=MDX&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** The front passenger seat weight sensor may crack and short circuit, which can cause the air bags to deploy unintentionally during a crash. Air bags that deploy unintentionally during a crash increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the seat weight sensors, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2024 Acura MDX remains a compelling choice in the luxury midsize SUV segment, offering a refined driving dynamic and robust engine choices. While most owners enjoy trouble-free operation, used-car buyers should thoroughly check the vehicle's electrical systems, brake responsiveness, and sunroof glass during a pre-purchase inspection.
