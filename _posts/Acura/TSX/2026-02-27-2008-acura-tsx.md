# 2008 Acura TSX

### Overview
The 2008 Acura TSX, a compact executive car, is generally regarded as a reliable vehicle, reflecting Acura's reputation for quality engineering. It typically features a 2.4-liter four-cylinder engine paired with either a five-speed automatic or a six-speed manual transmission. Owners often praise its sporty handling, comfortable interior, and fuel efficiency for its class. While most owners report a positive ownership experience with minimal unexpected repairs, like any vehicle, certain components can experience issues over time. This analysis focuses on documented, non-wear-and-tear related problems that may arise.

---


## Engine

### 2.4L K24A2 I4
**Oil Consumption**
- **Symptoms:** Excessive oil burning, potentially leading to low oil levels and engine damage if not monitored.
- **Solution:** Engine rebuild or replacement of piston rings and seals.
- **Cost:** $1,500 - $3,500

**VTC Actuator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Rattling noise from the engine on startup, especially when cold.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the VTC actuator and potentially the timing chain tensioner.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1,000


## Transmission

### 5-Speed Automatic
**Torque Converter Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Shuddering or slipping during acceleration, harsh shifting.
- **Solution:** Rebuilding or replacement of the torque converter.
- **Cost:** $1,000 - $2,500

## Electrical

### Standard Electrical System
**Power Window Motor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Power windows not operating, slow operation, or unusual noises.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the power window motor and regulator.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600 per window

**HID Headlight Ballast Failure**
- **Symptoms:** One or both HID headlights flickering, dimming, or not turning on.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the headlight ballast.
- **Cost:** $400 - $800 per side

### Summary Verdict
The 2008 Acura TSX is a solid choice for those seeking a reliable and engaging compact luxury sedan. While it generally avoids major mechanical pitfalls, potential owners should be aware of a few specific issues, primarily related to oil consumption and the VTC actuator in the 2.4L engines, and potential torque converter problems in the automatic transmission. Electrical components like power window motors and HID ballasts can also be points of failure. Despite these, the overall reliability remains high, with many owners experiencing trouble-free ownership when these potential issues are addressed proactively.
