# 2011 Acura TSX

### Overview
The 2011 Acura TSX is generally considered a reliable vehicle, building on Acura's reputation for quality engineering and durability. It offers a comfortable ride, a refined interior, and a good balance of performance and fuel efficiency, particularly with its available engines. While most owners report a positive ownership experience with minimal unexpected repairs, like any vehicle, there are a few areas that have seen occasional reports of issues. This data focuses on documented mechanical and electrical concerns beyond routine maintenance, providing insights for potential buyers or current owners.

---


## Engine

### 2.4L K24Z3 I4 (Standard)
**Oil Consumption**
- **Symptoms:** Excessive oil burning, potentially leading to low oil levels and engine damage if not monitored.
- **Solution:** Regular oil level checks and top-offs. In some cases, piston ring replacement may be necessary.
- **Cost:** $1,200 - $2,500

**VTC Actuator Rattle**
- **Symptoms:** A rattling or ticking noise from the engine, especially on startup or at idle.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the VTC actuator.
- **Cost:** $400 - $800

## Electrical

### Standard Electrical System
**Power Window Motor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Power windows not operating, slow operation, or unusual noises.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the power window motor and regulator.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600 per window

**Bluetooth Connectivity Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Difficulty pairing devices, intermittent connection, or no audio playback.
- **Solution:** Software update for the infotainment system or replacement of the Bluetooth module.
- **Cost:** $150 - $400

### Summary Verdict
The 2011 Acura TSX stands out as a dependable choice in the luxury compact sedan segment. Its powertrain, particularly the 2.4-liter engine, is generally robust, though some owners have noted oil consumption and VTC actuator noise as potential concerns requiring attention. The transmissions, both automatic and manual, have proven to be reliable with few reported issues. Electrical components like power window motors and Bluetooth modules can occasionally fail, but these are not widespread problems. Overall, the TSX offers a solid ownership experience with a good track record for reliability, making it a sensible used car purchase if properly maintained.
