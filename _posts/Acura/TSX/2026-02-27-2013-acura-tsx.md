# 2013 Acura TSX

### Overview
The 2013 Acura TSX is generally considered a reliable vehicle, building on Acura's reputation for dependability. It offers a blend of sporty performance and luxury, making it a popular choice in its segment. While most owners report a positive ownership experience with minimal unexpected repairs, like any vehicle, there are a few areas that have seen more attention than others. This analysis focuses on documented technical issues beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential areas of concern for prospective buyers or current owners.

---


## Engine

### 2.4L Inline-4 (K24Z7)
**Oil Consumption**
- **Symptoms:** Some owners have reported higher than expected oil consumption, particularly at higher mileage. This can manifest as the oil level dropping between regular checks.
- **Solution:** Regular oil level checks and top-offs. In some cases, piston ring replacement may be necessary, though this is not a widespread defect.
- **Cost:** $1000 - $2500

**Timing Chain Tensioner**
- **Symptoms:** Rattling noise from the engine, especially on startup. This can indicate wear on the timing chain tensioner.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the timing chain tensioner and potentially the timing chain itself.
- **Cost:** $600 - $1200

### Summary Verdict
The 2013 Acura TSX stands out as a reliable vehicle with a strong track record. The most commonly cited issues, such as minor oil consumption and potential timing chain tensioner wear, are not indicative of systemic failure and often appear at higher mileage. The transmission, suspension, electrical, exterior, and interior components generally perform without significant widespread problems. For owners, proactive maintenance and attention to oil levels are recommended. Overall, the TSX offers a dependable ownership experience, making it a solid choice for those seeking a well-built luxury sedan.
