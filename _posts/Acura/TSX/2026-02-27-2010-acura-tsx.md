# 2010 Acura TSX

### Overview
The 2010 Acura TSX, a compact executive car, is generally regarded as a reliable vehicle, reflecting Acura's reputation for quality engineering. It was offered with a capable 2.4-liter four-cylinder engine, known for its balance of performance and fuel efficiency. The transmission options typically included a smooth-shifting automatic and, in some markets, a manual. While the TSX is built to last, like any vehicle, it can experience specific issues over time. This analysis focuses on documented, non-wear-and-tear related problems that owners might encounter, providing insights into potential repair areas and their associated costs.

---


## Engine

### 2.4L K24Z3 I4
**Oil Leaks (VTEC Solenoid/Valve Cover Gasket)**
- **Symptoms:** Visible oil on the engine block, burning oil smell, low oil level.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the VTEC solenoid gasket or valve cover gasket.
- **Cost:** $150 - $350

**Timing Chain Tensioner Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Rattling noise from the front of the engine, especially on startup.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the timing chain tensioner and potentially the chain and guides.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1000


## Transmission

### 5-Speed Automatic
**Hesitation/Shuddering**
- **Symptoms:** Jerking or shuddering during gear changes, delayed engagement.
- **Solution:** Transmission fluid flush and filter replacement, or potential internal transmission repair.
- **Cost:** $200 - $1500

## Electrical

### Standard System
**Power Window Motor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Window not operating, slow movement, clicking noises.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the power window motor.
- **Cost:** $200 - $400

**HID Headlight Bulb Failure**
- **Symptoms:** One or both HID headlights not illuminating or flickering.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the HID bulb and potentially the ballast.
- **Cost:** $150 - $500

### Summary Verdict
The 2010 Acura TSX is a solid choice for those seeking a reliable luxury sedan. Its powertrain, particularly the 2.4L engine, is generally robust. The most commonly cited issues tend to be minor oil leaks from gaskets or solenoids and potential wear on the timing chain tensioner, which can manifest as an audible rattle. Transmission issues, while not rampant, can occur with the automatic, sometimes presenting as hesitation. Electrical components like power window motors and HID headlight parts are also occasional points of failure. Overall, the TSX offers good long-term reliability, with most potential issues being manageable and not indicative of systemic design flaws.
