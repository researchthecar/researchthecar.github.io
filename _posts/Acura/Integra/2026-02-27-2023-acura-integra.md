# 2023 Acura Integra Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2023 Acura Integra brings back a beloved nameplate as a stylish and sporty liftback, sharing its core platform and powertrains with the Honda Civic. While praised for its engaging driving dynamics, everyday practicality, and comfortable cabin, used buyers should pay close attention to a few documented trouble spots. Most notably, steering system concerns and minor electrical glitches have surfaced in owner complaints, requiring careful inspection during a used car test drive.

---

## Engine

### 1.5L Turbocharged 4-Cylinder

A 1.5-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine producing 200 horsepower and 192 lb-ft of torque, standard on base, A-Spec, and A-Spec Technology trims. This powerplant provides brisk acceleration and solid fuel economy, though routine maintenance of the turbo and ignition systems is key to long-term reliability.

### 2.0L Turbocharged 4-Cylinder

A high-output 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine generating 320 horsepower and 310 lb-ft of torque, exclusive to the high-performance Type S trim. It transforms the vehicle into a track-capable machine while maintaining strong daily drivability.

## Transmission

### Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)

A smooth-shifting CVT paired with the 1.5L engine on standard and A-Spec trims, designed for maximum fuel efficiency and relaxed daily commuting.

### 6-Speed Manual Transmission

A close-ratio 6-speed manual transmission equipped with a helical limited-slip differential, available on select 1.5L trims and standard on the Type S. It offers enthusiast-focused gear throws and engaging pedal feel.

**Stiff Shifter Operation**
- **Symptoms:** The manual gearbox shifter feels very stiff and difficult to engage into gear during low-speed maneuvers like three-point turns.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified technician inspect the shifter linkage, cables, and clutch hydraulics for proper adjustment or wear.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

The disc brake setup provides confident stopping power, though early-model noise and pedal travel concerns have been flagged by some drivers.

**Brake Squealing and Noise**
- **Symptoms:** Brakes generate loud squeaking or squealing noises, particularly when the vehicle is cold or reversing.
- **Solution:** Inspect brake pads, rotors, and the vehicle stability assist (VSA) system for premature wear, glazing, or operational anomalies.

## Steering

### Electric Power Steering Rack

The electronic power steering system provides light effort at low speeds and reassuring weight at highway speeds, but has been the subject of owner complaints regarding sticky feedback.

**Sticky Steering and Loss of Assist**
- **Symptoms:** The steering wheel develops a noticeable sticky or binding feel at highway speeds, making smooth micro-adjustments difficult and occasionally requiring extra force to turn.
- **Solution:** Consult a dealership or repair facility to determine if steering rack components require service or replacement under applicable recall campaigns or service bulletins.

## Electrical

### Audio and Infotainment Amplifier

Cabin electronics include digital instrument clusters and advanced touchscreen infotainment setups, though select owners have reported audio hardware failures.

**Sudden Sound System Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The audio system completely stops producing sound from the speakers while the display screen and radio functions appear to operate normally.
- **Solution:** Diagnose the factory amplifier and infotainment control module; replace faulty hardware if troubleshooting resets fail.
- **Cost:** $2,000

## Safety

### Forward Collision Avoidance

A suite of active driver assistance technologies designed to enhance road safety, though sensor calibration sensitivity can occasionally lead to false positive triggers.

**Unexpected Automatic Braking**
- **Symptoms:** The adaptive cruise control or collision mitigation system unexpectedly engages hard braking at highway speeds when no obstructions are present.
- **Solution:** Check forward-facing camera and radar sensor alignments, and ensure vehicle software is updated to the latest manufacturer calibrations.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V430000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Antilock/Traction Control/Electronic Limited Slip:Control Unit/Module](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Acura&model=Integra&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** Unintended vehicle movement or an unexpected increase in brake pedal travel can increase the risk of a crash or injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the VSA modulator, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V744000: Steering](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Acura&model=Integra&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** Difficulty steering can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the worm gear spring and redistribute or add grease as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2023 Acura Integra is an appealing choice for buyers seeking a blend of practicality, luxury touches, and engaging driving dynamics. While mechanical fundamentals derived from its Honda platform are generally robust, used shoppers should thoroughly test the steering feel to check for any binding or stickiness, verify proper audio system function, and confirm that all safety recalls have been fully addressed by a certified dealer.
