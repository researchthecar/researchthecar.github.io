# 2000 Acura Integra

### Overview
The 2000 Acura Integra, a popular compact sports coupe and sedan, is generally regarded as a reliable vehicle, especially for its era. Built on Honda's reputation for engineering excellence, the Integra offered a blend of performance, fuel efficiency, and durability. Common models include the GS, GS-R, and Type R, each with distinct performance characteristics. While most owners report positive experiences, like any vehicle, certain components can be prone to issues over time, particularly with age and mileage. This analysis focuses on documented common problems beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential areas of concern for prospective buyers or current owners.

---


## Engine

### 1.8L DOHC VTEC (B18C1 - GS-R)
**Oil Leaks (Valve Cover Gasket, Oil Pan Gasket)**
- **Symptoms:** Visible oil under the car, burning oil smell, low oil level.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the affected gasket.
- **Cost:** $150 - $300

**Timing Belt Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Sudden engine shutdown, no start, potential internal engine damage.
- **Solution:** Replacement of timing belt, tensioner, and water pump. Critical to follow maintenance schedule.
- **Cost:** $400 - $700

## Transmission

### 5-Speed Manual Transmission
**Clutch Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Slipping clutch, difficulty shifting gears, burning smell.
- **Solution:** Clutch replacement (clutch disc, pressure plate, release bearing).
- **Cost:** $500 - $900

**Synchronizer Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Grinding noise when shifting, difficulty engaging gears.
- **Solution:** Transmission internal repair or rebuild, focusing on synchronizer rings.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1500


### 4-Speed Automatic Transmission
**Transmission Fluid Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Leaking fluid, rough shifting, overheating.
- **Solution:** Replacement of seals and gaskets, or transmission flush and refill.
- **Cost:** $200 - $500

**Torque Converter Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Shuddering, slipping, delayed engagement.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the torque converter.
- **Cost:** $600 - $1000


## Suspension

### Standard Chassis
**Worn Ball Joints and Bushings**
- **Symptoms:** Clunking noises over bumps, poor handling, uneven tire wear.
- **Solution:** Replacement of worn ball joints and suspension bushings.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600

**Leaking Shock Absorbers/Struts**
- **Symptoms:** Bouncy ride, poor handling, visible fluid leaks on shocks.
- **Solution:** Replacement of shock absorbers or struts.
- **Cost:** $400 - $700


## Electrical

### Standard Electrical System
**Alternator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Dimming headlights, battery warning light, dead battery.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the alternator.
- **Cost:** $300 - $500

**Power Window Motor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Window not operating, slow operation.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the power window motor.
- **Cost:** $200 - $400


## Exterior

### Body Panels and Seals
**Sunroof Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Water ingress into the cabin, damp carpets, musty smell.
- **Solution:** Cleaning and clearing sunroof drains, or replacement of seals.
- **Cost:** $100 - $300

**Rust (Wheel Arches, Rocker Panels)**
- **Symptoms:** Visible corrosion and bubbling paint on lower body panels.
- **Solution:** Rust repair and repainting, or panel replacement.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1500+


## Interior

### Interior Trim and Upholstery
**Dashboard Cracking**
- **Symptoms:** Cracks appearing on the dashboard surface, especially in hot climates.
- **Solution:** Dashboard replacement or repair (often cosmetic).
- **Cost:** $400 - $800

**HVAC Blower Motor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** No air coming from vents, or intermittent airflow.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the HVAC blower motor.
- **Cost:** $200 - $400

### Summary Verdict
The 2000 Acura Integra is a generally reliable car with a strong mechanical foundation, benefiting from Honda's engineering prowess. Common issues tend to be age-related, such as oil leaks from gaskets, wear on suspension components like ball joints and bushings, and potential electrical problems like alternator failure. The timing belt is a critical maintenance item that requires timely replacement to avoid catastrophic engine damage. Both manual and automatic transmissions can experience wear, with clutch issues in manuals and fluid leaks or torque converter problems in automatics. While interior components like dashboards can crack and exterior elements may show rust, these are often cosmetic or repairable. Overall, with proper maintenance and addressing known weak points, the 2000 Integra remains a dependable and enjoyable vehicle.
