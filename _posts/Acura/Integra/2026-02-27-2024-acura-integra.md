# 2024 Acura Integra Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2024 Acura Integra brings a blend of sharp handling, daily practicality, and sporty styling to the compact luxury segment. As shoppers consider this model on the used market, it is helpful to look closely at owner feedback and mechanical patterns. While the majority of drivers enjoy a trouble-free experience, a notable number of owner complaints and reports center around steering system quirks and advanced driver assistance sensitivities. Reviewing these specific areas can help buyers evaluate whether a prospective vehicle has been properly inspected or repaired.

---

## Engine

### 1.5L Turbocharged Inline-4

This 1.5-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine produces 200 horsepower and 192 lb-ft of torque, offering lively acceleration for standard trims. It generally provides dependable daily performance, though buyers should verify routine maintenance records.

### 2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4

Exclusive to the high-performance Type S variant, this robust 2.0-liter turbocharged engine pumps out 320 horsepower and 310 lb-ft of torque. It is built for enthusiast driving and pairs exclusively with a manual transmission.

## Transmission

### Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)

Available on base and A-Spec trims, this CVT features paddle shifters to simulate stepped gear changes. It prioritizes fuel efficiency and smooth power delivery during commuting.

### 6-Speed Manual Transmission

Offered on select performance trims, this short-throw manual gearbox features a helical limited-slip differential. It is widely praised by enthusiasts for its crisp shift action and engaging feel.

## Steering

### Electric Power Steering System

The electric power steering setup provides responsive handling, but certain owners have experienced intermittent stiffness or warning messages.

**Electric Power Steering Resistance and Warning Lights**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have reported a sticky or freezing sensation in the steering wheel at highway speeds, accompanied by dashboard warning lights requiring driver steering intervention.
- **Solution:** Have a certified technician inspect the electric power steering assembly and software calibration, as some vehicles may require component replacement under manufacturer advisories.

## Safety

### Collision Mitigation Braking System

Advanced driver assistance features enhance highway safety, though sensor calibrations can occasionally lead to false collision alerts.

**Unintended Automatic Emergency Braking**
- **Symptoms:** The adaptive cruise control or forward collision avoidance system can occasionally misinterpret overhead road signs or adjacent traffic, triggering unexpected brake activation.
- **Solution:** Ensure that the vehicle's camera and radar sensors are clean and properly calibrated, and check with a dealer for any available software updates.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V744000: Steering](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Acura&model=Integra&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** Difficulty steering can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the worm gear spring and redistribute or add grease as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V859000: Seats:Critical Fasteners](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Acura&model=Integra&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** The driver's seat cushion frame may not have been tightened properly, which can result in an unsecured seat. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 207, "Seating Systems." An unsecured driver's seat may not adequately restrain the driver during a crash, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the driver's seat cushion frame, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V054000: Seats:Critical Fasteners](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Acura&model=Integra&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** The driver's seat cushion frame may not have been tightened properly, which can result in a loose seat. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 207, "Seating Systems." A loose driver's seat may not adequately restrain the driver during a crash, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the driver's seat cushion frame, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2024 Acura Integra is an appealing choice for used car buyers seeking a sporty and well-equipped compact hatchback. While owner complaints highlight occasional concerns with electric power steering feel and forward collision sensor sensitivity, these issues do not overshadow the vehicle's overall engineering quality. Prospective buyers should conduct a thorough test drive focusing on steering responsiveness at highway speeds and ensure all dealer software updates have been applied.
