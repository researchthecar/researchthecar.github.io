# 2016 Acura ILX Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2016 Acura ILX is a compact luxury sport sedan powered by a direct-injected 2.4-liter four-cylinder engine paired with an eight-speed dual-clutch transmission. While it offers engaging performance and strong fuel economy, used-car buyers should review ownership history carefully. Notable areas of owner concern and official attention involve brake system reliability, fuel system performance, and specific powertrain operation.

---

## Engine

### 2.4L 4-Cylinder (Earth Dreams)

A 2.4-liter naturally aspirated four-cylinder engine producing 201 horsepower and 180 lb-ft of torque. It provides brisk acceleration for the class while maintaining reasonable everyday efficiency, though buyers should watch for occasional fuel system complaints.

**Engine Stalling and Fuel Pump Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have reported unexpected engine stalling while driving at various speeds, accompanied by difficulties with fuel delivery and loss of motive power.
- **Solution:** Inspect the fuel system and replace failing fuel pumps or related components as necessary.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Dual-Clutch Transmission (DCT)

An innovative 8-speed dual-clutch transmission featuring a torque converter for smoother low-speed operation. It generally delivers rapid shifts, though minor refinement issues have been noted by some owners.

**Clunking or Shifting Delays**
- **Symptoms:** The transmission may occasionally produce a clunking noise when the accelerator is depressed after slowing down, or feel as though it is holding a higher gear than appropriate at low speeds.
- **Solution:** Check transmission fluid condition, update powertrain control modules if applicable, or have a qualified technician inspect internal clutch actuation.

## Brakes

### Hydraulic Service Brakes

Standard four-wheel disc brake setup equipped with anti-lock braking and electronic brake distribution.

**Brake Pedal Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have experienced situations where the brake pedal depresses all the way to the floorboard, leading to a sudden loss of braking responsiveness or complete brake failure.
- **Solution:** Verify if the vehicle has completed related safety recalls regarding the brake master cylinder and inspect hydraulic lines for leaks or pressure loss.

## Steering

### Electric Power Steering

Vehicle-speed-sensitive electric power steering system designed to offer light effort at low speeds and firmer feedback on the highway.

**Steering Assist and Electrical Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Some drivers noted unexpected steering assist drops or warning lights related to electrical system and battery management issues.
- **Solution:** Test the battery and charging system output, and check for stored diagnostic trouble codes in the power steering module.

## Exterior

### Glass and Roof Structure

Exterior panels and glass features including side windows, windshield surrounds, and available moonroof.

**Spontaneous Moonroof Shattering**
- **Symptoms:** The glass moonroof panel has been reported to shatter unexpectedly while driving at highway speeds.
- **Solution:** Inspect the moonroof assembly for any structural stress or impact damage, and replace the glass panel if compromised.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V052000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Storage:Tank Assembly](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Acura&model=ILX&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** If the fuel gauge displays that the vehicle has more gas than it actually does, the vehicle may run out of gas and stall, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Acura will notify owners, and dealers will inspect and, as necessary, replace the fuel tank, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V182000: Air Bags:Frontal:Driver Side:Inflator Module](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Acura&model=ILX&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** Due to a manufacturing error, in the event of a crash necessitating deployment of the driver frontal air bag, these inflators may explode. An explosion of an inflator within the driver frontal air bag module may result in sharp metal fragments striking the driver, front seat passenger or other occupants resulting in serious injury or death.
- **What to do:** Honda will notify owners, and dealers will replace the driver's air bag inflator with an alternate inflator, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V424000: Power Train:Driveline:Driveshaft](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Acura&model=ILX&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** A separated driveshaft can cause the vehicle to have a loss of drive or allow the vehicle to move even while in Park, increasing the risk of a crash or injury.
- **What to do:** Acura will notify owners, and dealers will inspect, and, as necessary, replace the driveshaft and half shaft, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V061000: Air Bags:Frontal:Driver Side:Inflator Module](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Acura&model=ILX&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** The affected vehicles are equipped with a dual-stage driver frontal air bag that may be susceptible to moisture intrusion which, over time, could cause the inflator to rupture. In the event of a crash necessitating deployment of the driver's frontal air bag, the inflator could rupture with metal fragments striking the driver or other occupants resulting in.
- **What to do:** Honda will notify owners, and dealers will replace the inflator, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V858000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Acura&model=ILX&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** Fuel pump failure can cause an engine stall while driving, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the fuel pump module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V859000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Foundation Components:Master Cylinder](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Acura&model=ILX&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** Contaminated brake fluid may cause a seal inside the brake master cylinder to deform, which can cause an internal brake fluid leak and reduce brake function. Reduced brake function can extend the distance required to stop, increasing the risk of a crash or injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the brake master cylinder, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2016 Acura ILX combines sporty driving dynamics with the everyday practicality of a compact luxury sedan. While many owners enjoy trouble-free operation, prospective buyers should carefully verify that important safety recalls—particularly those involving the braking system and fuel pump—have been fully addressed. A thorough pre-purchase inspection focusing on the brakes, electrical accessories, and powertrain history is strongly recommended.
