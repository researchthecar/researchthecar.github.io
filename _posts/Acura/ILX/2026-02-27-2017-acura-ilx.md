# 2017 Acura ILX Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2017 Acura ILX offers sporty styling and efficient performance, sharing its underpinnings with the Honda Civic. While generally dependable, used-car buyers should review potential concerns related to electrical glitches, transmission behavior, and brake components. Being informed about these reported areas can help ensure a smoother ownership experience.

---

## Engine

### 2.4L Earth Dreams 4-Cylinder

The 2017 Acura ILX comes equipped with a 2.4-liter direct-injected four-cylinder engine producing 201 horsepower and 180 lb-ft of torque. It provides brisk acceleration and strong fuel economy, though occasional fuel or ignition system hiccups have been reported by owners.

**Engine Misfire and Acceleration Hesitation**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle may experience engine misfires, illuminated check engine lights, and a temporary failure to accelerate while depressing the gas pedal.
- **Solution:** Diagnose the fuel system or ignition components and replace faulty parts as needed.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Dual-Clutch Transmission (DCT)

Power is routed through an innovative 8-speed dual-clutch transmission with a torque converter, providing rapid gear changes. Some drivers note occasional rough engagement or clunks during low-speed urban driving.

**Low-Speed Jerking and Shifting Hesitation**
- **Symptoms:** The transmission may jerk, slip, or hesitate when shifting between reverse and drive, or when driving and slowing down at low speeds.
- **Solution:** Check transmission software updates and fluid condition, or inspect internal clutch components if harsh engagement persists.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

The hydraulic brake setup provides confident stopping power, but owners have occasionally noted unusual pedal noises or sought service for specific recall campaigns.

**Brake Pedal Noise and Feel**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may experience abnormal sounds when depressing the brake pedal, or concerns regarding master cylinder function.
- **Solution:** Inspect the braking system components, pads, rotors, and master cylinder for wear or mechanical defects.

## Electrical

### Electrical System

Cabin electronics and keyless access features generally operate smoothly, though intermittent electrical issues involving security alarms and battery drain have been documented.

**Random Alarm Activation and Dashboard Flickering**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle security alarm may trigger spontaneously in the middle of the night, accompanied by flickering dashboard lights and a failure to start.
- **Solution:** Inspect the keyless access system, battery health, and body control module for electrical shorts or software glitches.

## Safety

### Air Bag System

Standard safety equipment includes a comprehensive airbag suite, though sensor failures can occasionally trigger warning lights and deactivate supplemental restraints.

**Passenger Seat Sensor Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** The check airbag system warning light illuminates on the dashboard due to a malfunctioning passenger seat sensor, disabling the airbag system.
- **Solution:** Diagnose the occupant detection sensor in the passenger seat and replace the faulty sensor assembly if necessary.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V052000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Storage:Tank Assembly](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Acura&model=ILX&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** If the fuel gauge displays that the vehicle has more gas than it actually does, the vehicle may run out of gas and stall, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Acura will notify owners, and dealers will inspect and, as necessary, replace the fuel tank, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V424000: Power Train:Driveline:Driveshaft](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Acura&model=ILX&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** A separated driveshaft can cause the vehicle to have a loss of drive or allow the vehicle to move even while in Park, increasing the risk of a crash or injury.
- **What to do:** Acura will notify owners, and dealers will inspect, and, as necessary, replace the driveshaft and half shaft, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V858000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Acura&model=ILX&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** Fuel pump failure can cause an engine stall while driving, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the fuel pump module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V859000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Foundation Components:Master Cylinder](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Acura&model=ILX&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** Contaminated brake fluid may cause a seal inside the brake master cylinder to deform, which can cause an internal brake fluid leak and reduce brake function. Reduced brake function can extend the distance required to stop, increasing the risk of a crash or injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the brake master cylinder, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2017 Acura ILX is a stylish and generally reliable compact luxury sedan, benefiting from proven Honda mechanical components. While most systems prove trouble-free, buyers should thoroughly test drive the dual-clutch transmission for smooth low-speed operation and verify that all electrical features and safety sensors are functioning correctly.
