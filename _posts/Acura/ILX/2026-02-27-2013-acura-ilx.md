# 2013 Acura ILX

### Overview
The 2013 Acura ILX, a compact luxury sedan, aimed to offer a blend of fuel efficiency and premium features. It was available with two distinct powertrain options: a 2.0-liter four-cylinder engine paired with a 5-speed automatic transmission, and a more performance-oriented 2.4-liter four-cylinder engine mated to a 6-speed manual transmission. While generally considered a reliable vehicle, like any car, it has specific areas where owners have reported issues. This analysis focuses on documented technical problems beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential concerns for prospective buyers or current owners.

## Electrical

### Infotainment System
**Bluetooth Connectivity Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Intermittent connection drops, inability to pair devices, audio cutting out.
- **Solution:** Software update for the infotainment system, or in some cases, replacement of the module.
- **Cost:** $100 - $500

**Backup Camera Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Camera not displaying an image, distorted image, or intermittent operation.
- **Solution:** Inspection and potential replacement of the camera unit or wiring harness.
- **Cost:** $200 - $600


### Keyless Access System
**Door Handle Sensor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Inability to unlock or lock doors by touching the handle.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the faulty door handle sensor.
- **Cost:** $150 - $350

### Summary Verdict
The 2013 Acura ILX generally presents a solid reliability record, particularly concerning its powertrain options. The most frequently reported issues tend to be concentrated in the electrical system, specifically with the infotainment unit's Bluetooth connectivity and the backup camera's functionality. Some owners have also noted problems with the keyless entry sensors and, less commonly, the AC compressor. These issues, while not indicative of a systemic failure across the model, are worth considering. Overall, the ILX is a dependable choice, but potential buyers should be aware of these specific electrical and climate control components.
