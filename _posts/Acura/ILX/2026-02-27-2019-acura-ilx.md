# 2019 Acura ILX Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2019 Acura ILX, buyers will find a compact sport sedan sharing much of its mechanical foundation with the dependable Honda Civic. While overall ownership is generally straightforward, prospective buyers should review available owner feedback regarding specific brake and fuel system concerns, alongside active campaigns tracked by safety authorities.

---

## Engine

### 2.4L Naturally Aspirated 4-Cylinder

The 2.4-liter direct-injected four-cylinder engine produces 201 horsepower and 180 lb-ft of torque, offering lively performance and solid longevity. It is generally robust, though owners should verify that required fuel system updates have been completed.

**Fuel Pump Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may experience engine stalling or a failure to start due to potential fuel pump degradation.
- **Solution:** Have an authorized service center inspect the fuel system and replace components as specified under relevant manufacturer safety notices.

### Brake Master Cylinder

The hydraulic brake system provides dependable stopping power under normal conditions, but owners have reported critical master cylinder performance issues requiring careful verification before purchase.

**Brake Pressure Loss**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers report a spongy brake pedal feel, increased stopping distances, or unexpected brake system failure.
- **Solution:** Inspect the brake master cylinder and hydraulic components for internal leaks, and complete any pending manufacturer recall repairs.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Dual-Clutch Transmission

Paired exclusively with the 2.4L engine, this 8-speed dual-clutch automatic transmission features a torque converter for smooth low-speed engagement. Regular fluid changes help maintain crisp and reliable shifting performance.

## Electrical

### Advanced Driver Assistance Camera

The AcuraWatch safety suite includes collision mitigation and lane keeping features, though camera unit electronics can occasionally fail and require replacement.

**ADAS Camera Module Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Multiple driver-assistance features suspend operation unexpectedly, accompanied by internal electronic failure diagnostic trouble codes.
- **Solution:** Replace the faulty multipurpose camera unit and recalibrate the driver assistance system.
- **Cost:** $800

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V215000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Acura&model=ILX&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** Fuel pump failure can cause an engine stall while driving, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Honda will notify owners, and dealers will replace the fuel pump assembly, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V052000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Storage:Tank Assembly](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Acura&model=ILX&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** If the fuel gauge displays that the vehicle has more gas than it actually does, the vehicle may run out of gas and stall, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Acura will notify owners, and dealers will inspect and, as necessary, replace the fuel tank, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V424000: Power Train:Driveline:Driveshaft](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Acura&model=ILX&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** A separated driveshaft can cause the vehicle to have a loss of drive or allow the vehicle to move even while in Park, increasing the risk of a crash or injury.
- **What to do:** Acura will notify owners, and dealers will inspect, and, as necessary, replace the driveshaft and half shaft, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V858000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Acura&model=ILX&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** Fuel pump failure can cause an engine stall while driving, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the fuel pump module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V859000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Foundation Components:Master Cylinder](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Acura&model=ILX&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** Contaminated brake fluid may cause a seal inside the brake master cylinder to deform, which can cause an internal brake fluid leak and reduce brake function. Reduced brake function can extend the distance required to stop, increasing the risk of a crash or injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the brake master cylinder, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2019 Acura ILX offers sporty styling and a reliable powertrain derived from proven Honda engineering. Prospective buyers should thoroughly inspect service records to confirm that brake master cylinder and fuel pump updates have been performed, and verify the proper functioning of all driver-assistance technologies during a test drive.
