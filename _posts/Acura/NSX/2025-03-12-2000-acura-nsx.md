# 2000 Acura NSX Reliability & Common Issues

### Overview
The 2000 Acura NSX represents the apex of the first-generation NSX, boasting a mid-engine, rear-wheel-drive layout designed for exhilarating performance and precise handling. For this model year, the NSX was equipped with a 3.2-liter V6 engine (C32B) paired exclusively with a 6-speed manual transmission. This naturally aspirated powerhouse produced approximately 290 horsepower and 224 lb-ft of torque. Owners frequently praise its exceptional build quality, timeless design, and engaging driving dynamics. However, as with any high-performance exotic, maintenance can be demanding and specialized. Common owner-cited drawbacks include the high cost of specialized parts and labor, and the relatively cramped interior space for taller drivers.

### Engine
#### 3.2L V6 (C32B)
**Timing Belt Replacement Intervals**
- **Description/Symptoms:** The 3.2L V6 engine utilizes a timing belt, which requires periodic replacement to prevent catastrophic engine damage. Acura recommended replacement every 105,000 miles or 7 years, whichever comes first. Failure to adhere to this schedule can result in the belt breaking, causing pistons to collide with valves, leading to severe internal engine damage.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the timing belt, tensioners, idler pulleys, and water pump. It is also highly recommended to replace the camshaft and crankshaft seals during this service.
- **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $1200 - $2500 (This cost can vary significantly based on labor rates and whether the water pump and seals are included.)
- **Recalls:** No specific recalls for timing belt failure on the 2000 model year NSX.

**Oil Leaks (Camshaft Seals)**
- **Description/Symptoms:** Over time, the camshaft seals can degrade and begin to leak oil. This typically manifests as oil seepage around the valve covers and the front/rear of the engine. While usually not an immediate mechanical failure, unchecked leaks can lead to low oil levels and potential engine damage if not addressed.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the camshaft seals. This is often performed concurrently with timing belt service due to the accessibility of the seals during that procedure.
- **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $300 - $800 (If performed as a standalone service. Significantly less if done with timing belt replacement.)
- **Recalls:** No specific recalls for camshaft seal leaks on the 2000 model year NSX.

### Transmission
#### 6-Speed Manual Transmission
**Clutch Wear**
- **Description/Symptoms:** Like any manual transmission vehicle, the clutch in the NSX will wear out over time and with use. Symptoms of a worn clutch include a high engagement point, difficulty shifting gears, slipping (engine RPMs increase without corresponding acceleration), and a burning smell under heavy acceleration.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the clutch disc, pressure plate, and throw-out bearing. The pilot bearing and rear main seal are also typically replaced at the same time.
- **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $1500 - $3000 (This cost can vary based on labor rates and OEM versus aftermarket parts.)
- **Recalls:** No specific recalls for clutch wear on the 2000 model year NSX.

### Electrical
**Headlight Actuator Failure**
- **Description/Symptoms:** The NSX features pop-up headlights, which are operated by electric actuators. These actuators can fail over time, preventing one or both headlights from raising or lowering correctly. This can be a safety concern, especially at night.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the headlight actuator motor.
- **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $300 - $700 (per actuator)
- **Recalls:** No specific recalls for headlight actuator failure on the 2000 model year NSX.

**Dashboard Illumination Issues**
- **Description/Symptoms:** Some owners have reported intermittent or complete failure of the dashboard illumination. This can be caused by faulty dimmer switches, burned-out bulbs, or issues with the wiring harness.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and repair or replacement of faulty components such as the dimmer switch, bulbs, or wiring.
- **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $100 - $500 (depending on the cause)
- **Recalls:** No specific recalls for dashboard illumination issues on the 2000 model year NSX.

### Interior
**HVAC Blend Door Actuator Failure**
- **Description/Symptoms:** The blend door actuators control the flow of air through the HVAC system, directing it to different vents and controlling temperature. Failure of these actuators can result in poor temperature control, with air only blowing from certain vents or an inability to switch between hot and cold.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the faulty blend door actuator(s).
- **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $200 - $600 (per actuator)
- **Recalls:** No specific recalls for HVAC blend door actuator failure on the 2000 model year NSX.

### Summary
The 2000 Acura NSX demonstrates remarkable reliability for a high-performance exotic sports car. The majority of documented issues stem from routine maintenance items that are expected for a vehicle of this age and performance caliber, such as timing belt replacement and clutch wear. While the initial purchase price and specialized maintenance costs can be significant, the intrinsic build quality and robust engineering of the NSX contribute to a generally dependable ownership experience when properly cared for. Long-term cost of ownership is directly tied to adherence to recommended maintenance schedules and the use of quality replacement parts.
