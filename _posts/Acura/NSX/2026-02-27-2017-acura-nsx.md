# 2017 Acura NSX

### Overview
The 2017 Acura NSX represents a pinnacle of hybrid supercar engineering, blending a twin-turbocharged V6 engine with a sophisticated three-electric-motor Sport Hybrid Super Handling All-Wheel Drive (SH-AWD) system. This advanced powertrain is designed for exhilarating performance and remarkable efficiency. As with any high-performance vehicle, particularly one with complex hybrid technology, understanding potential reliability concerns is crucial for owners. This analysis focuses on documented technical issues beyond standard maintenance, providing insights into the long-term durability of key systems.

### Summary Verdict
The 2017 Acura NSX demonstrates strong technical reliability, particularly considering its complex hybrid powertrain and advanced all-wheel-drive system. The core engine and transmission components have shown remarkable durability, with no widespread issues reported. The hybrid battery system and adaptive suspension also appear robust. The most commonly cited, albeit minor, concerns revolve around occasional infotainment system glitches, which are typically resolvable with software updates. Owners should be aware that while major mechanical failures are rare, the specialized nature of the NSX means any repairs, especially those involving the hybrid system, could be costly if outside of warranty. Overall, the NSX is a reliable supercar for its class.
