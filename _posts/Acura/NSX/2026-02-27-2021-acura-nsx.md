# 2021 Acura NSX Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

Shopping for a modern supercar like the Acura NSX involves looking closely at both high-performance capability and long-term mechanical composure. While formal owner complaints and widespread defect patterns remain scarce for this model year, prospective buyers should still carefully evaluate complex hybrid powertrains and exotic vehicle maintenance histories before purchasing.

---

## Engine

### 3.5L Twin-Turbocharged V6 Hybrid

This twin-turbocharged 3.5-liter V6 engine works alongside a three-motor electric hybrid system to produce around 573 horsepower. It offers exhilarating performance paired with sophisticated engineering, though long-term maintenance requires specialized technician oversight.

## Transmission

### 9-Speed Dual-Clutch Automatic

The 9-speed dual-clutch transmission delivers lightning-fast gear changes and transfers power seamlessly across the all-wheel-drive system. It generally proves robust under spirited driving conditions when properly maintained.

## Summary Verdict

The Acura NSX stands out as a highly sophisticated hybrid supercar that combines everyday drivability with track-ready performance. Because official owner complaints are virtually absent for this production year, buyers are encouraged to secure a comprehensive pre-purchase inspection from a certified specialist to verify the health of the complex hybrid powertrain and electronics.
