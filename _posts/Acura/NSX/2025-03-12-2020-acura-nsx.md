Here's a breakdown of common issues and complaints reported by owners of the 2020 Acura NSX, formatted for your website. The 2020 Acura NSX is a mid-engine hybrid sports car boasting a 3.5-liter twin-turbocharged V6 engine paired with a three-motor hybrid system. It produces a combined 573 horsepower and 476 lb-ft of torque, sent to all four wheels through a 9-speed dual-clutch transmission. Owners generally praise the NSX for its exhilarating performance, advanced technology, and comfortable interior for a sports car. Common dislikes include its relatively high price point, complex hybrid system that can sometimes be difficult to diagnose, and a lack of exhaust note compared to competitors.

**Engine**

**3.5L Twin-Turbocharged V6 Hybrid**

*   **Coolant Leak at Turbocharger Fittings**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaks are sometimes reported at the fittings connecting the coolant lines to the turbochargers. This can lead to a noticeable coolant loss, potentially overheating the engine if left unchecked, and a visible coolant smell.
    *   Solution: Replacing the affected coolant lines and fittings with updated parts and ensuring proper torque specifications during installation.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1500+ depending on the extent of the damage and labor costs.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **High Voltage Battery Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Failure of one or more cells within the high voltage battery of the hybrid system can cause a check engine light, error messages related to the hybrid system, reduced performance, and potentially inability to start the vehicle.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the faulty high-voltage battery pack or individual battery modules.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $5,000 - $10,000+ (depending on whether the whole pack or individual modules need replacement).
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**9-Speed Dual-Clutch Transmission**

*   **Harsh Shifting/Jerking**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners report experiencing harsh shifting, especially at low speeds or during downshifts. This can manifest as jerking or clunking noises from the transmission.
    *   Solution: In some cases, a software update to the Transmission Control Module (TCM) can resolve the issue. More severe cases may require valve body replacement or transmission rebuild.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500 for software updates; $2,000 - $5,000+ for valve body replacement or transmission rebuild.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

No Issues Found

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Freezing/Crashing**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The infotainment system, including the navigation, audio, and climate control interfaces, can occasionally freeze, crash, or become unresponsive.
    *   Solution: Performing a system reset or software update. If the problem persists, replacement of the infotainment unit may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 for system reset; $100 - $300 for software update; $1,000 - $3,000+ for unit replacement.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Sensor Malfunctions**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Issues with various sensors, such as the parking sensors, blind spot monitoring sensors, or tire pressure monitoring system (TPMS) sensors can occur, leading to false warnings or system malfunctions.
    *   Solution: Diagnose the faulty sensor and replace it.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+ per sensor, depending on the type and location.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Automatic Rear Wing Malfunction**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The automatic rear wing that deploys at higher speeds may occasionally fail to deploy or retract properly, or make unusual noises during operation.
    *   Solution: Inspect the wing mechanism for obstructions or damage. Replacement of the wing motor or entire wing assembly may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+ depending on the extent of the issue.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Loose Interior Trim Pieces**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Reports of some interior trim pieces, particularly around the dashboard or center console, becoming loose or rattling over time.
    *   Solution: Re-securing the loose trim pieces with appropriate fasteners or adhesives.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $500+ depending on the complexity of the repair.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2020 Acura NSX, while a high-performance and technologically advanced vehicle, has a few potential issues that owners should be aware of. The severity of these issues ranges from minor inconveniences to potentially expensive repairs involving the hybrid system and transmission. The high-voltage battery and transmission repairs represent the most significant potential costs, while infotainment and sensor issues are more common and generally less expensive to resolve. Overall, while the NSX offers an exhilarating driving experience, prospective buyers should be aware of the potential maintenance costs associated with its complex systems.
