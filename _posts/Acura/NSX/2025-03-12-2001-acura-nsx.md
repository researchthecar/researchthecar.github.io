The 2001 Acura NSX is a mid-engine sports car celebrated for its balance of performance and daily usability. It's powered by a 3.2L V6 engine (C32B) producing 290 hp and 224 lb-ft of torque. All 2001 NSX models came standard with a 6-speed manual transmission. The Targa top was standard for this model year. Owners appreciate the NSX for its precise handling, communicative steering, and iconic styling. Common criticisms include dated interior features and relatively high maintenance costs.

**Engine**

**3.2L V6 (C32B)**

*   **Oil Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks can develop from various locations, including the valve cover gaskets, oil pan gasket, and rear main seal. Symptoms include oil spots on the ground, a burning oil smell, and low oil levels.
    *   Solution: Replacing the faulty gasket or seal.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100-$1000+ depending on the location of the leak and labor required.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Distributor Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The distributor is responsible for delivering high voltage to the spark plugs in the correct firing order. Symptoms of a failing distributor can include a loss of power, misfires, rough idle, and difficulty starting the engine.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the distributor assembly.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1000
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**6-Speed Manual**

*   **Clutch Slave Cylinder Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The clutch slave cylinder can leak or fail, leading to difficulty shifting, a soft or spongy clutch pedal, or complete clutch failure.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the clutch slave cylinder.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Damper Wear/Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Over time, the original dampers can wear out, leading to a bouncy ride, poor handling, and potential leaks of hydraulic fluid.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the dampers.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800-$2000+ depending on the brand and whether you replace all four corners.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Pop-up Headlight Motor Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The pop-up headlights on the NSX are prone to motor failure, resulting in one or both headlights failing to rise or retract properly.
    *   Solution: Replacing the headlight motor or rebuilding it with a rebuild kit.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+ per headlight, depending on whether it is rebuilt or replaced.
    *   Recalls: None Found
*   **Electrical grounds corroding**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Many electrical components on the NSX share common ground locations. Over time the grounds corrode which cause components to function intermittently or fail. Symptoms can include headlight failure, turn signal failure, fuel pump failure, etc.
    *   Solution: Locate and clean all electrical grounds on the vehicle.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50-$200 depending on how many grounds are cleaned.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Targa Top Seal Leaks (if applicable)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Targa top seals can degrade over time, leading to water leaks into the cabin during rain or washing.
    *   Solution: Replacing the targa top seals.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200-$800+ depending on the extent of the sealing needed and how many seals are replaced.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Climate Control Display Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The climate control display can suffer from pixelation or complete failure, making it difficult to read and adjust the climate settings.
    *   Solution: Repair or replacement of the climate control unit.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+ depending on whether it is repaired or replaced.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2001 Acura NSX is a generally reliable sports car, but it is subject to certain age-related issues. Engine oil leaks, distributor problems, and clutch issues are the most common, with some electrical and interior issues also cropping up. The severity and cost of these issues vary, with some being relatively minor and inexpensive to fix, while others can be more significant and costly. Preventative maintenance and careful inspection are crucial to keeping a 2001 NSX in good condition.
