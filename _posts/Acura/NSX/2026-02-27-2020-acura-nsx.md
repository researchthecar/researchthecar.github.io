# 2020 Acura NSX Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2020 Acura NSX is a high-performance hybrid supercar featuring advanced engineering and everyday drivability. As a low-volume exotic vehicle, comprehensive owner complaint data is scarce, reflecting its niche status in the marketplace. Prospective buyers should inspect the hybrid powertrain and complex electronic systems carefully.

---

## Engine

### 3.5L Twin-Turbocharged V6 Hybrid

The 2020 Acura NSX features a 3.5-liter twin-turbocharged V6 engine paired with three electric motors, producing a combined 573 horsepower and 476 lb-ft of torque. This sophisticated hybrid powertrain delivers blistering acceleration while maintaining surprising daily usability, though maintenance requires specialized technician knowledge.

## Transmission

### 9-Speed Dual-Clutch Automatic Transmission

Power is sent to all four wheels via a responsive 9-speed dual-clutch automatic transmission with paddle shifters. The gearbox provides lightning-fast gearchanges suited for both aggressive track driving and smooth highway cruising.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V858000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Acura&model=NSX&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** Fuel pump failure can cause an engine stall while driving, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the fuel pump module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2020 Acura NSX remains a technologically advanced and thrilling exotic supercar with a relatively quiet reliability profile due to its low production numbers. Buyers interested in a used model should prioritize a thorough pre-purchase inspection by an authorized specialist to ensure all hybrid systems, turbochargers, and electronics operate as intended.
