# 2021 Acura TLX Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2021 Acura TLX, buyers will find a sporty, tech-forward sedan that delivers sharp handling and a robust appearance. However, owner reports highlight a few notable mechanical and electrical pain points—most notably concerning the all-wheel-drive transfer case, suspension components, and infotainment stability. Keeping these potential issues in mind will help you evaluate prospective vehicles more effectively during a pre-purchase inspection.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged 4-Cylinder

The base 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine produces 272 horsepower and 280 lb-ft of torque, providing brisk daily acceleration and decent fuel economy. It is generally robust, though occasional powertrain management glitches have been reported by owners.

**Malfunction Indicator Light and Loss of Power**
- **Symptoms:** Flashing malfunction indicator light (MIL) accompanied by a sudden loss of engine power during steady acceleration.
- **Solution:** Have a certified mechanic or dealer scan for stored diagnostic trouble codes and inspect the fuel and ignition systems.

### 3.0L Turbocharged V6

Exclusive to the performance-oriented Type S trim, the 3.0-liter turbocharged V6 generates 355 horsepower and 354 lb-ft of torque. It turns the TLX into a serious sport sedan, though buyers should verify complete maintenance histories on higher-mileage examples.

## Transmission

### 10-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 10-speed automatic transmission pairs with both engine options and generally shifts smoothly. However, a recurring pattern of transfer case and spline interface failures can disrupt power delivery to the rear wheels on AWD models.

**Transfer Case and Spline Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Loud clunking or banging noises from underneath the vehicle upon acceleration, followed by a loss of all-wheel-drive capability as power defaults exclusively to the front wheels.
- **Solution:** Inspect the transmission and transfer case spline interface for wear or stripping; replacement of the transfer case or transmission assembly may be necessary.

## Suspension

### Adaptive Damper System and Rear Shocks

The suspension provides a compliant yet engaging ride quality, particularly with the adaptive damper setup on higher trims. Owners have noted potential failure points in rear stroke sensors, wiring harnesses, and dampers triggering dashboard errors.

**Adaptive Damper System Error and Suspension Noise**
- **Symptoms:** Dashboard warning lights for the Adaptive Damper System (ADS), accompanied by fault codes indicating stroke sensor limit abnormalities or squeaking noises from the suspension.
- **Solution:** Inspect rear shocks, solenoids, stroke sensors, and associated wiring harnesses for corrosion or physical wear, and replace faulty components as needed.

## Electrical

### Infotainment and Body Electrical

The electrical architecture supports extensive cabin technology and driver-aids, but early production models are prone to software glitches, screen flickering, and sensitive sensor interlocks.

**Infotainment Screen Glitches and Shifting Interlocks**
- **Symptoms:** Center display screens randomly shorting, turning blank, or going static when driving over bumps, alongside occasional gear selection interlock errors.
- **Solution:** Check software updates for the infotainment system and inspect body control module connections and door latch sensors.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V064000: Air Bags:Sensor:Occupant Classification:Front Passenger](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Acura&model=TLX&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The front passenger seat weight sensor may crack and short circuit, failing to suppress the air bag as intended. An air bag that deploys unintentionally during a crash can increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the seat weight sensors, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V950000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Storage:Tank Assembly](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Acura&model=TLX&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** A fuel leak in the presence of an ignition source increases the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the fuel tank assembly, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V391000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Pedals And Linkages](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Acura&model=TLX&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard numbers 108, "Lamps, Reflective Devices, and Associated Equipment" and 135, "Light Vehicle Brake Systems." A brake pedal that shifts out of position can prevent the driver from applying the brakes as intended, increasing the risk of a crash or injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the brake pedal assembly as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V332000: Air Bags:Sensor:Occupant Classification:Front Passenger](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Acura&model=TLX&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The front passenger seat weight sensor may crack and short circuit, which can cause the air bags to deploy unintentionally during a crash. Air bags that deploy unintentionally during a crash increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the seat weight sensors, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2021 Acura TLX offers compelling luxury, strong performance, and attractive styling, making it an appealing choice on the used market. Prospective buyers should carefully verify the condition of the all-wheel-drive transfer case, test all suspension modes for damper errors, and ensure the infotainment system operates without freezing. A thorough pre-purchase inspection by an experienced technician is strongly recommended before finalizing a purchase.
