# 2023 Acura TLX Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2023 Acura TLX is a stylish and capable sports sedan offering engaging handling and a high-tech cabin. However, used car buyers should pay close attention to drivetrain integrity, particularly regarding the Super Handling All-Wheel Drive (SH-AWD) system, as well as select electrical and braking concerns reported by owners.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged I4

This 2.0-liter turbocharged inline-4 engine produces 272 horsepower and 280 lb-ft of torque, providing brisk acceleration for base and tech trims. It is generally robust, though peripheral drivetrain components linked to it have seen some owner complaints.

### 3.0L Turbocharged V6

Exclusive to the high-performance Type S trim, this 3.0-liter turbo V6 pushes out 355 horsepower and 354 lb-ft of torque. It delivers thrilling power and athletic performance for enthusiast drivers.

## Transmission

### 10-Speed Automatic

Paired across both engine options, this 10-speed automatic transmission delivers smooth and rapid gear changes. While mechanically sound, certain models have experienced issues with the associated all-wheel-drive transfer case components.

**Transfer Case and Spline Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have reported sudden loss of rear-wheel drive capability, grinding noises during acceleration, and transfer case fluid leaks.
- **Solution:** Requires inspection by a qualified technician, with severe cases necessitating replacement of the transmission-to-PTU splines or the entire transfer case assembly.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

The braking system provides strong stopping power suited for a sport sedan, though some drivers have experienced premature wear or unusual pedal feedback.

**Brake Rotor Wear and Inconsistent Pedal Feel**
- **Symptoms:** Unusual grinding noises, vibration during braking, and a sticky or inconsistent brake pedal feel.
- **Solution:** Have brake rotors and pads inspected for premature warping or wear, and replace components as necessary.

## Steering

### Security and Telematics

The electrical architecture supports advanced driver aids and connectivity features, but select owner reports highlight vulnerabilities in anti-theft systems and intermittent sensor glitches.

**Vulnerability to Theft and Tracking Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Factory anti-theft tracking features failing and vehicles being targeted by relay or clone attacks where alarms fail to sound.
- **Solution:** Consult a dealership for available software updates or install aftermarket physical security deterrents like steering wheel locks.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V950000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Storage:Tank Assembly](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Acura&model=TLX&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** A fuel leak in the presence of an ignition source increases the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the fuel tank assembly, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V391000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Pedals And Linkages](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Acura&model=TLX&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard numbers 108, "Lamps, Reflective Devices, and Associated Equipment" and 135, "Light Vehicle Brake Systems." A brake pedal that shifts out of position can prevent the driver from applying the brakes as intended, increasing the risk of a crash or injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the brake pedal assembly as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V332000: Air Bags:Sensor:Occupant Classification:Front Passenger](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Acura&model=TLX&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** The front passenger seat weight sensor may crack and short circuit, which can cause the air bags to deploy unintentionally during a crash. Air bags that deploy unintentionally during a crash increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the seat weight sensors, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

When shopping for a used 2023 Acura TLX, prospective buyers should thoroughly test drive the vehicle to verify smooth power delivery and proper functioning of the SH-AWD system. Checking service records for any past transfer case repairs or electrical updates is strongly recommended to ensure long-term reliability.
