# 2020 Acura TLX Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2020 Acura TLX delivers an appealing blend of luxury, comfort, and standard features, making it an attractive choice on the used-car market. However, prospective buyers should review known owner concerns, particularly surrounding engine performance, powertrain behavior, and advanced safety electronics. Being well-informed on these specific areas will help you inspect a prospective vehicle thoroughly and ensure a confident purchase.

---

## Engine

### 2.4L 4-Cylinder Engine

The base 2.4-liter naturally aspirated four-cylinder engine produces 206 horsepower and pairs with an 8-speed dual-clutch transmission, offering nimble daily driving dynamics and generally solid reliability.

### 3.5L V6 Engine

The 3.5-liter V6 engine produces 290 horsepower and provides strong acceleration for highway cruising, though select owners have flagged rare concerns regarding internal bearing wear and stalling.

**Engine Stalling and Knocking**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have reported unexpected engine stalling in traffic, rough idling, or mechanical knocking noises that can lead to reduced drivability.
- **Solution:** Have a certified mechanic inspect the engine internals and diagnostic codes; severe cases may require professional mechanical teardown or component replacement.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Dual-Clutch Automatic Transmission

This quick-shifting dual-clutch automatic transmission is paired exclusively with the 2.4-liter engine and front-wheel drive, delivering responsive performance.

### 9-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 9-speed automatic transmission is paired with the 3.5L V6 engine and can be equipped with front-wheel drive or Super Handling All-Wheel Drive (SH-AWD), though some owners report occasional shifting irregularities.

**Harsh Engagement and Neutral Shifting**
- **Symptoms:** The transmission may exhibit delayed gear engagement, harsh shifting, or unexpectedly drop into neutral while driving.
- **Solution:** Check for software updates at an authorized service center or inspect transmission control modules and fluid condition.

## Steering

### Precision All-Wheel Steering (P-AWS)

The Precision All-Wheel Steering system improves handling agility on front-wheel-drive V6 models, though electronic anomalies can occasionally trigger warning lights.

**P-AWS Malfunction and Limp Mode**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers experience sudden warnings indicating a Precision All-Wheel Steering malfunction, often accompanied by the vehicle entering a restricted speed or limp mode.
- **Solution:** Scan the vehicle for stored electronic trouble codes relating to the steering control unit and wheel actuators.

## Safety

### Forward Collision and Driver Assist

The suite of active safety and driver-assistance features enhances security on the road, but sensitive sensors can occasionally generate false warnings or unintended braking.

**Unexpected Braking and Sensor Failures**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers note that the automatic emergency braking system may trigger unexpectedly, or front camera and collision mitigation warning lights may illuminate on the dashboard.
- **Solution:** Inspect the windshield-mounted camera and radar sensors for blockages, calibration issues, or software glitches.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V215000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Acura&model=TLX&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** Fuel pump failure can cause an engine stall while driving, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Honda will notify owners, and dealers will replace the fuel pump assembly, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V751000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Engine:Hard Parts Internal/Mechanical](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Acura&model=TLX&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** A damaged engine may run improperly or stall while driving, increasing the risk of a fire, crash, or injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and repair, or replace the engine as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V858000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Acura&model=TLX&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** Fuel pump failure can cause an engine stall while driving, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the fuel pump module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V064000: Air Bags:Sensor:Occupant Classification:Front Passenger](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Acura&model=TLX&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** The front passenger seat weight sensor may crack and short circuit, failing to suppress the air bag as intended. An air bag that deploys unintentionally during a crash can increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the seat weight sensors, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V332000: Air Bags:Sensor:Occupant Classification:Front Passenger](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Acura&model=TLX&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** The front passenger seat weight sensor may crack and short circuit, which can cause the air bags to deploy unintentionally during a crash. Air bags that deploy unintentionally during a crash increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the seat weight sensors, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2020 Acura TLX is a comfortable and well-equipped luxury sedan offering solid value on the used market. While most owners enjoy a trouble-free experience, prospective buyers should verify that all recall work has been completed and carefully evaluate the transmission shifting behavior and advanced safety electronics during a test drive.
