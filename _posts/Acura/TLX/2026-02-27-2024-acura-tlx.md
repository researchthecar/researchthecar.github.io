# 2024 Acura TLX Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

Shopping for a used 2024 Acura TLX means looking at a modern sport sedan known for sharp handling and advanced technology. While generally modern and well-equipped, early owner reports highlight a few electronic, powertrain, and suspension quirks that prospective buyers should keep in mind during a test drive.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4

This 2.0-liter turbocharged inline-4 engine produces approximately 272 horsepower and 280 lb-ft of torque, offering lively daily acceleration. It is paired with front-wheel drive or Super Handling All-Wheel Drive (SH-AWD) and generally provides dependable daily performance.

### 3.0L Turbocharged V6

Exclusive to the Type S, the 3.0-liter turbocharged V6 delivers around 355 horsepower and 354 lb-ft of torque for robust performance. It comes standard with SH-AWD and adds a much sportier edge to the TLX lineup.

## Transmission

### 10-Speed Automatic

The 10-speed automatic transmission routes power smoothly across standard and Type S trims, though buyers should verify the integrity of transfer case seals during a pre-purchase inspection.

**Transmission and Transfer Case Fluid Leak**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have observed active fluid leaks running down the drivetrain case and collecting around the mating area between the transmission and transfer case.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified technician inspect the mating surfaces, seals, and gaskets to determine the source of the leak and replace damaged components.

## Suspension

### Wheels and Front Suspension

The suspension setup delivers a firm and engaging ride quality, though minor wheel or component balance issues can occasionally introduce unwanted highway vibrations.

**Highway Speed Vibration**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may notice an unwanted vibration in the front of the vehicle when traveling at highway speeds.
- **Solution:** Inspect internal front wheel components for broken plastic hardware or balance issues, and replace damaged pieces as necessary.

## Electrical

### Infotainment and Instrument Cluster

The electrical architecture supports extensive cabin displays and safety technology, but a few owners have experienced unexpected screen blackouts and parasitic battery drains.

**Infotainment Shutdown and Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** The infotainment screen and dashboard displays may shut down completely while driving, leaving the vehicle inoperative due to a power drain.
- **Solution:** Consult a dealer or technician to diagnose software glitches, check for pending updates, and test the electrical charging and battery draw systems.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V391000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Pedals And Linkages](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Acura&model=TLX&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard numbers 108, "Lamps, Reflective Devices, and Associated Equipment" and 135, "Light Vehicle Brake Systems." A brake pedal that shifts out of position can prevent the driver from applying the brakes as intended, increasing the risk of a crash or injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the brake pedal assembly as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2024 Acura TLX remains an attractive option for used car buyers seeking a blend of luxury, technology, and performance. While isolated reports point to early electrical glitches and potential fluid leaks, a thorough pre-purchase inspection focusing on the electronics and drivetrain seals can help ensure a worry-free ownership experience.
