# 2019 Acura TLX

### Overview
The 2019 Acura TLX is a mid-size luxury sedan known for its comfortable ride, upscale interior, and strong performance, particularly with its available V6 engine. It generally offers a good balance of sportiness and refinement, making it a competitive option in its segment. Reliability is a key selling point for Acura, and the TLX largely upholds this reputation, with owners reporting satisfaction with its durability and build quality. However, like any vehicle, there are specific areas where potential issues have been noted, primarily related to certain powertrain components and electronic features, though widespread critical failures are uncommon.

### 3.5L V6
**Oil Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Visible oil stains under the vehicle, burning oil smell, low oil level warnings.
- **Solution:** Replacement of seals and gaskets (e.g., valve cover gaskets, oil pan gasket).
- **Cost:** $300 - $700


## Transmission

### 8-Speed DCT (Dual-Clutch Transmission)
**Hesitation/Jerking**
- **Symptoms:** Rough shifting, hesitation during acceleration, jerky movements at low speeds.
- **Solution:** Software updates, transmission fluid flush and replacement, or internal transmission repair/rebuild.
- **Cost:** $500 - $2,500


### 9-Speed Automatic Transmission
**Shifting Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Rough shifts, delayed engagement, occasional shuddering.
- **Solution:** Software updates for transmission control module, transmission fluid service.
- **Cost:** $300 - $1,000

## Electrical

### Infotainment System
**Screen Freezing/Unresponsiveness**
- **Symptoms:** Touchscreen becomes unresponsive, system freezes or restarts randomly.
- **Solution:** Software updates, hard reset of the infotainment unit, or replacement of the unit.
- **Cost:** $400 - $1,500


### Battery Drain
**Premature Battery Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Difficulty starting the vehicle, dead battery after short periods of inactivity.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis of parasitic draw, replacement of battery, or faulty module.
- **Cost:** $200 - $600

### Summary Verdict
The 2019 Acura TLX is generally a reliable vehicle with a strong track record. Most owners experience trouble-free operation, with the 2.4L engine and standard chassis components proving particularly robust. The most frequently reported issues tend to be minor, such as infotainment system glitches or occasional transmission hesitation, especially with the 9-speed automatic. The 3.5L V6 can sometimes develop oil leaks, and premature battery drain has been noted by a small percentage of owners. Overall, the TLX offers good long-term value and dependability, with most potential issues being manageable and not indicative of systemic failure.
