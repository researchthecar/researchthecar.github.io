# 2018 Acura TLX Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2018 Acura TLX offers a blend of luxury, advanced technology, and engaging performance, making it an appealing option on the used car market. Prospective buyers should review owner-reported reliability trends carefully, particularly concerning engine and transmission behaviors, electrical system glitches, and advanced driver assistance sensitivities. While many drivers enjoy its comfort and handling, checking service records and verifying that all safety and fuel-related recalls have been addressed is essential before purchasing.

---

## Engine

### 2.4L 4-Cylinder

A 2.4-liter naturally aspirated 4-cylinder engine producing approximately 206 horsepower and 182 lb-ft of torque, paired with an 8-speed dual-clutch transmission. It provides a balanced combination of efficiency and daily drivability for front-wheel-drive models.

### 3.5L V6

A 3.5-liter V6 engine generating approximately 290 horsepower and 267 lb-ft of torque, available with front-wheel drive or Acura's Super Handling All-Wheel Drive (SH-AWD). It delivers strong acceleration and smooth power delivery.

**Engine Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle may experience unexpected engine stalling, unusual noises, or drivability concerns during operation.
- **Solution:** Diagnose fault codes, inspect electronic engine management components, and repair or replace faulty sensors or wiring.

**Low Fuel Pressure**
- **Symptoms:** Dashboard warning lights illuminate and the vehicle enters 'limp' mode accompanied by diagnostic codes such as P0087.
- **Solution:** Inspect the fuel system and replace failing fuel pumps or pressure regulators.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Dual-Clutch Transmission

An 8-speed dual-clutch automatic transmission featuring a torque converter, paired exclusively with the 2.4-liter 4-cylinder engine for quick and responsive gear changes.

### 9-Speed Automatic Transmission

A 9-speed automatic transmission paired with the 3.5-liter V6 engine across various trims, designed to optimize highway fuel economy and smooth cruising capability.

**Rough Shifting and Vibration**
- **Symptoms:** Transmission may exhibit harsh engagement, such as banging into second gear upon startup, or noticeable vibrations and rough shifting between 30 and 45 mph.
- **Solution:** Perform software updates and specialized transmission fluid services, or consult a dealer if mechanical wear is present.

## Steering

### Steering and Suspension Control

Handles vehicle steering response and wheel alignment; structural component wear should be inspected thoroughly on used models.

**Control Arm Structural Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Clunking noises, steering misalignment, or structural separation discovered in the front lower control arm near the compliance bushing.
- **Solution:** Inspect suspension components during routine maintenance and replace damaged control arms.

## Electrical

### Electrical System

Manages vehicle starting, keyless access, alarms, and interior electronics; occasional glitches with key recognition and alarm triggers have been reported.

**Keyless Remote Recognition Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle fails to recognize the keyless remote despite fresh batteries, leaving the driver stranded and unable to start the car.
- **Solution:** Reprogram or replace faulty key fobs or vehicle receiver modules.

## Safety

### Forward Collision Avoidance

Includes AcuraWatch driver-assist features like collision mitigation and lane keeping; some owners note system sensitivity leading to false activations.

**Unexpected Braking**
- **Symptoms:** The frontal crash mitigation system may trigger unexpectedly, causing the vehicle to brake harshly or come to a sudden stop during normal driving conditions.
- **Solution:** Check sensor alignment, clean camera and radar areas, and have safety systems recalibrated by a qualified technician.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V060000: Fuel System, Other:Delivery:Fuel Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Acura&model=TLX&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** Reduced fuel pump performance can result in an engine stall, increasing the risk of crash.
- **What to do:** Honda will notify owners, and dealers will update the fuel injection engine control unit (FI-ECU) software and, if necessary, replace the fuel pump, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V751000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Engine:Hard Parts Internal/Mechanical](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Acura&model=TLX&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** A damaged engine may run improperly or stall while driving, increasing the risk of a fire, crash, or injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and repair, or replace the engine as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V858000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Acura&model=TLX&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** Fuel pump failure can cause an engine stall while driving, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the fuel pump module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V332000: Air Bags:Sensor:Occupant Classification:Front Passenger](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Acura&model=TLX&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** The front passenger seat weight sensor may crack and short circuit, which can cause the air bags to deploy unintentionally during a crash. Air bags that deploy unintentionally during a crash increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the seat weight sensors, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2018 Acura TLX is generally a comfortable and well-equipped luxury sedan, but potential buyers should perform a thorough pre-purchase inspection. Pay close attention to powertrain operation, specifically checking for smooth transmission engagement on V6 models and verifying that all electrical systems and driver-assistance features function properly. Ensuring that safety recalls have been completed by an authorized dealer will help secure a more reliable ownership experience.
