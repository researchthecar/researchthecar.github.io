# 2022 Acura TLX Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2022 Acura TLX is a stylish and capable sport sedan, but used car buyers should pay close attention to powertrain and suspension longevity. While it delivers engaging driving dynamics and luxury appointments, owner reports indicate a few recurring mechanical concerns that warrant a careful pre-purchase inspection.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged I4

The base 2.0-liter turbocharged inline-four engine produces 272 horsepower and 280 lb-ft of torque, offering brisk acceleration for daily driving. It is generally robust, though buyers should verify the integrity of surrounding drivetrain seals.

**Transfer Case and Transmission Fluid Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have discovered fluid leaks originating around the transmission or transfer case area during routine maintenance, sometimes accompanied by a perceived loss of all-wheel-drive functionality.
- **Solution:** Inspect the underside of the vehicle for wet spots or leaks around the transfer case and transmission housing, and replace compromised seals or gaskets as needed.

## Transmission

### 10-Speed Automatic

The 10-speed automatic transmission pairs smoothly with both front-wheel-drive and Super Handling All-Wheel Drive (SH-AWD) configurations. However, potential buyers should check for mechanical noises or abrupt disengagements in all-wheel-drive models.

**Stripped Transfer Case Splines**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may hear a loud bang or grinding noise during acceleration, followed by an immediate and unannounced loss of all-wheel drive, reverting the vehicle to front-wheel drive.
- **Solution:** Requires dealership inspection of the transmission output shaft and transfer case splines, often necessitating the replacement of both components.

## Suspension

### Control Arms and Ball Joints

The front suspension delivers planted handling, but early component wear such as squeaking ball joints or control arm issues have been reported by some owners.

**Lower Control Arm and Ball Joint Noise**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may notice premature squeaking, popping, or abnormal sounds coming from the front suspension during low-speed turns or over bumps.
- **Solution:** Have a mechanic inspect the lower control arms and ball joints for wear, and replace the affected suspension components if excessive play or noise is identified.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

Brake performance under normal conditions is strong, though intermittent electronic brake assist faults require prompt attention if experienced during a test drive.

**Temporary Loss of Brake Assist**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have reported a sudden drop in braking power requiring heavy pedal effort, accompanied by warning lights on the dashboard.
- **Solution:** Inspect the brake booster and electronic brake actuator systems; cycling the ignition sometimes temporarily restores pressure, but professional diagnosis is required.

## Electrical

### Electrical System

Modern electronics control a wide array of cabin and chassis functions, making thorough software and battery diagnostics essential during a pre-purchase inspection.

**Dashboard Warning Light Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** Multiple dashboard warning lights may illuminate simultaneously, occasionally accompanied by stiff steering or temporary electrical system glitches.
- **Solution:** Check battery health, charging systems, and main wiring harnesses for loose connections or software anomalies.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V950000: Tires:Bead](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Acura&model=TLX&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** Damaged tires can lead to a sudden loss of air pressure and loss of vehicle control, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the tires as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V950000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Storage:Tank Assembly](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Acura&model=TLX&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** A fuel leak in the presence of an ignition source increases the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the fuel tank assembly, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V391000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Pedals And Linkages](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Acura&model=TLX&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard numbers 108, "Lamps, Reflective Devices, and Associated Equipment" and 135, "Light Vehicle Brake Systems." A brake pedal that shifts out of position can prevent the driver from applying the brakes as intended, increasing the risk of a crash or injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the brake pedal assembly as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2022 Acura TLX is an appealing luxury sport sedan with a lot to offer, but prospective buyers should approach with a healthy level of diligence. Be sure to thoroughly check the all-wheel-drive transfer case for any signs of spline wear or fluid leaks, test all electronic systems, and arrange for a comprehensive pre-purchase inspection by a qualified technician.
