# 2022 Acura TLX Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2022 Acura TLX is a stylish and capable sports sedan offering engaging handling and a high-tech cabin. While it generally provides a solid driving experience, prospective buyers should review certain owner-reported mechanical and electrical concerns. Paying close attention to the powertrain, suspension, and electrical systems during a pre-purchase inspection can help ensure a trouble-free ownership experience.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged I4

This 2.0-liter turbocharged inline-four engine produces approximately 272 horsepower and 280 lb-ft of torque, providing lively everyday performance. It is a standard powerplant for lower trims and balances respectable efficiency with strong acceleration.

### 3.0L Turbocharged V6

Exclusive to the high-performance Type S, this 3.0-liter turbocharged V6 pumps out 355 horsepower and 354 lb-ft of torque. It delivers robust power and aggressive acceleration, though buyers should verify maintenance history on performance-oriented models.

## Transmission

### 10-Speed Automatic Transmission

Paired with a 10-speed automatic transmission across all trims, this gearbox channels power to either the front wheels or Acura's Super Handling All-Wheel Drive (SH-AWD) system. It offers crisp gear shifts, though buyers should check for potential transfer case leaks.

**Transfer Case and Spline Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have reported loud grinding noises or a sudden loss of all-wheel-drive functionality, reverting the vehicle to front-wheel drive due to stripped splines between the transmission and transfer case.
- **Solution:** Have a certified technician inspect the transfer case and transmission interface for leaks or spline damage.

## Suspension

### Control Arms and Ball Joints

The front and rear multi-link suspension setup delivers a firm, sporty ride. Routine checks of bushings and ball joints are recommended to maintain optimal handling.

**Suspension Squeaks and Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may hear abnormal squeaking or knocking noises from the front suspension, often traced to wear in the lower control arms or ball joints.
- **Solution:** Inspect front control arms and ball joints for premature wear or lack of lubrication, and replace components as necessary.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

The braking system provides strong stopping power suited for a sport sedan. Regular inspection of pads, rotors, and hydraulic assist components is advised.

**Brake Pedal Softness and Loss of Assist**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have occasionally noted a sudden feeling of a soft brake pedal or reduced braking power, sometimes accompanied by brake warning lights.
- **Solution:** Inspect the brake hydraulic system, booster, and fluid condition, restarting the vehicle or servicing the system if fault codes are present.

## Interior

### Dashboard and Adaptive Damper Electronics

An extensive suite of electronics manages driver aids, infotainment, and chassis dynamics. Ensuring proper battery voltage and wiring integrity helps prevent random electronic glitches.

**Dashboard Warning Lights and System Failures**
- **Symptoms:** Multiple warning lights may flash simultaneously on the dashboard, occasionally accompanied by stiff steering or adaptive damper system error messages.
- **Solution:** Check battery health, wiring harnesses, and control modules for intermittent faults or loose connections.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V950000: Tires:Bead](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Acura&model=TLX&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** Damaged tires can lead to a sudden loss of air pressure and loss of vehicle control, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the tires as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V950000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Storage:Tank Assembly](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Acura&model=TLX&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** A fuel leak in the presence of an ignition source increases the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the fuel tank assembly, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V391000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Pedals And Linkages](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Acura&model=TLX&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard numbers 108, "Lamps, Reflective Devices, and Associated Equipment" and 135, "Light Vehicle Brake Systems." A brake pedal that shifts out of position can prevent the driver from applying the brakes as intended, increasing the risk of a crash or injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the brake pedal assembly as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2022 Acura TLX presents a compelling mix of luxury, performance, and sharp design. While most owners enjoy a trouble-free experience, prospective buyers should carefully evaluate the service history, specifically checking the SH-AWD transfer case for leaks, testing suspension components for noise, and ensuring all electrical systems operate without warning lights.
