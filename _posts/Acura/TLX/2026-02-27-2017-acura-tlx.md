# 2017 Acura TLX Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2017 Acura TLX offers a blend of luxury and sporty performance, but used-car buyers should inspect it carefully. While many owners enjoy its comfort and features, owner reports highlight some notable mechanical and electrical concerns that prospective buyers should keep on their radar.

---

## Engine

### 2.4L 4-Cylinder Engine

A 2.4-liter naturally aspirated four-cylinder engine producing around 206 horsepower paired with an eight-speed dual-clutch transmission. It generally offers punchy performance and solid efficiency for daily driving.

### 3.5L V6 Engine

A 3.5-liter V6 engine generating approximately 290 horsepower and 267 lb-ft of torque, available with front-wheel drive or Super Handling All-Wheel Drive (SH-AWD). It provides brisk acceleration but has accumulated some owner reports regarding unexpected stalling.

**Engine Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may unexpectedly stall while driving or idling at traffic lights, creating a serious safety hazard in traffic.
- **Solution:** Diagnose the fuel system and check for trouble codes such as low fuel rail pressure or faulty sensors; replace failing components as needed.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Dual-Clutch Transmission

An 8-speed dual-clutch automatic transmission mated exclusively to the 2.4L engine, offering quick shifts and responsive power delivery.

### 9-Speed Automatic Transmission

A 9-speed conventional automatic transmission paired with the 3.5L V6 engine. Some owners have reported shifting anomalies and dashboard warning lights requiring diagnostic attention.

**Unexpected Shifting to Neutral**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may abruptly shift into neutral while driving on the highway, failing to engage gears and requiring a restart to restore operation.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified mechanic inspect transmission control modules, software calibrations, and internal hydraulic components for signs of failure.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

The braking system provides adequate stopping power, though some owners report early wear on rear brake components.

**Premature Rotor Warping**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle shakes violently when applying the brakes due to prematurely warped rear rotors or premature caliper wear.
- **Solution:** Inspect brake pads, rotors, and calipers; resurface or replace warped rotors and service stuck calipers.

## Electrical

### Keyless Access and Alarm System

Electrical accessories and convenience features are generally advanced, though intermittent sensor and alarm glitches have been noted by owners.

**Random Alarm Triggering and Electrical Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle alarm may go off randomly, keyless access units may malfunction, and dashboard lights can flicker intermittently.
- **Solution:** Inspect the hood latch sensor, keyless entry modules, and related wiring harnesses for faults or corrosion.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V060000: Fuel System, Other:Delivery:Fuel Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Acura&model=TLX&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** Reduced fuel pump performance can result in an engine stall, increasing the risk of crash.
- **What to do:** Honda will notify owners, and dealers will update the fuel injection engine control unit (FI-ECU) software and, if necessary, replace the fuel pump, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V751000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Engine:Hard Parts Internal/Mechanical](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Acura&model=TLX&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** A damaged engine may run improperly or stall while driving, increasing the risk of a fire, crash, or injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and repair, or replace the engine as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V858000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Acura&model=TLX&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** Fuel pump failure can cause an engine stall while driving, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the fuel pump module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2017 Acura TLX remains an appealing choice for a used luxury sedan, offering strong engine options and a comfortable cabin. However, buyers should thoroughly test drive the vehicle to check for smooth transmission operation and verify that all electrical features and safety systems function properly. A pre-purchase inspection by a qualified mechanic is strongly recommended.
