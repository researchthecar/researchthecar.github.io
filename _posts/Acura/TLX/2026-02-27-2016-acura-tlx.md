# 2016 Acura TLX Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2016 Acura TLX, buyers will find a refined midsize luxury sedan that combines sporty handling with high-end cabin features. However, looking past the attractive styling reveals a few notable reliability concerns shared by owners, particularly surrounding unexpected engine stalling, complex electrical glitches, and transmission behavior. Being aware of these patterns can help prospective buyers perform a more thorough pre-purchase inspection.

---

## Engine

### 2.4L 4-Cylinder

A 2.4-liter naturally aspirated four-cylinder engine producing roughly 206 horsepower, paired with an 8-speed dual-clutch transmission. It offers brisk acceleration and solid fuel economy for daily commuting.

### 3.5L V6

A 3.5-liter V6 engine generating approximately 290 horsepower, mated to a 9-speed automatic transmission. It provides smooth, confident power delivery but requires careful verification of past mechanical maintenance.

**Engine Stalling and Power Loss**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle may completely shut down while driving on the highway or attempting turns at low speeds, causing sudden loss of power and steering control.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified mechanic inspect fuel delivery components and electrical systems, and check vehicle history for completed recall campaigns.

## Transmission

### 9-Speed Automatic Transmission

This 9-speed automatic transmission is paired with the V6 engine configuration. While offering smooth highway cruising, some owners have reported unexpected electronic shifting anomalies.

**Unintended Neutral Shift and Warning Lights**
- **Symptoms:** Transmission warning indicators illuminate on the dashboard, and the vehicle may unexpectedly shift into neutral while driving or idling on an incline.
- **Solution:** Diagnose internal transmission error codes and software calibration updates; severe mechanical faults may require transmission replacement.

## Electrical

### Backup Camera

Safety equipment is robust overall, but visibility aids like rear cameras require verification to ensure clear daily operation.

**Blurry or Water-Infiltrated Camera Image**
- **Symptoms:** The rear-view camera display becomes cloudy, blurry, or completely obscured due to moisture entering the camera housing.
- **Solution:** Replace the backup camera unit and ensure proper sealing against weather and water intrusion.

## Exterior

### Sunroof

Exterior panels and trim generally hold up well, though the panoramic glass components warrant careful inspection for stress fractures.

**Spontaneous Sunroof Shattering**
- **Symptoms:** The glass sunroof panel may shatter unexpectedly while driving at highway speeds without any apparent impact from outside objects.
- **Solution:** Replace the damaged glass panel and inspect the sunroof assembly frame for structural stress or alignment flaws.

## Interior

### Smart Power Control Unit and Electronics

The electrical architecture supports advanced driver aids and convenience features, though occasional glitches in control units and sensors have been reported by owners.

**Random Alarm Activation and Warning Lights**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle alarm may trigger intermittently while parked, or multiple dashboard warning lights may illuminate alongside a temporary drop into limp mode.
- **Solution:** Inspect circuit boards, such as the smart power control unit behind the glove box, for moisture intrusion or protective coating degradation.

## Safety

### Active Headrests

The cabin features comfortable materials and intuitive controls, though mechanical seat components should be checked for proper latching.

**Unintended Active Headrest Deployment**
- **Symptoms:** Front seat headrests forcefully deploy forward without a rear-end collision, potentially causing discomfort or injury to occupants.
- **Solution:** Inspect the internal mechanical release mechanisms within the seatback and replace faulty headrest actuator assemblies.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V060000: Fuel System, Other:Delivery:Fuel Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Acura&model=TLX&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** Reduced fuel pump performance can result in an engine stall, increasing the risk of crash.
- **What to do:** Honda will notify owners, and dealers will update the fuel injection engine control unit (FI-ECU) software and, if necessary, replace the fuel pump, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V751000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Engine:Hard Parts Internal/Mechanical](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Acura&model=TLX&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** A damaged engine may run improperly or stall while driving, increasing the risk of a fire, crash, or injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and repair, or replace the engine as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V858000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Acura&model=TLX&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** Fuel pump failure can cause an engine stall while driving, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the fuel pump module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2016 Acura TLX remains a compelling option for buyers seeking a comfortable, well-appointed luxury sport sedan on a budget. While many owners enjoy trouble-free driving, prospective buyers should carefully review service records to confirm that past engine and transmission complaints have been addressed. Conducting a thorough pre-purchase inspection focusing on electronics, the sunroof, and powertrain operation will help ensure a reliable ownership experience.
