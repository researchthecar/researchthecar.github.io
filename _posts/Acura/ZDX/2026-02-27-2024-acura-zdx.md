# 2024 Acura ZDX Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2024 Acura ZDX marks Acura's entry into the all-electric luxury SUV segment, sharing its Ultium-based platform and electric motor architecture with General Motors. As a first-model-year EV, it brings modern styling and strong performance, but it also exhibits early adopter quirks centered around complex software, driver-assistance features, and electrical system behavior. Buyers should approach used models with an awareness of these electronic issues, many of which can be addressed through dealer software updates.

---

## Engine

### Single-Motor Permanent Magnet Electric Motor (Rear-Wheel Drive)

The single-motor RWD powertrain delivers an estimated 340 horsepower, providing smooth, efficient everyday acceleration. This setup forms the base tier of the ZDX lineup and generally avoids complex mechanical drivetrain complaints.

### Dual-Motor Permanent Magnet Electric Motors (All-Wheel Drive)

The dual-motor AWD and high-performance Type S variants offer enhanced traction and outputs ranging from around 490 to upwards of 500 horsepower. Early reports note occasional cooling system component concerns within the inverter setup.

**Drive Motor Inverter Cooling System Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Fault codes or cooling system alerts related to the drive motor inverter cooler.
- **Solution:** Have a certified service center inspect the inverter cooling system components and replace faulty parts under warranty if applicable.

## Transmission

### Single-Speed Direct-Drive Transmission

Utilizes a fixed-gear, single-speed direct-drive transmission paired with the electric motor options. This configuration dispenses with traditional multi-gear shifting, resulting in seamless power delivery and high mechanical durability.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes and ABS

The regenerative braking system blends friction and electrical resistance to slow the vehicle. Owners have reported intermittent calibration or actuation anomalies in low-speed stopping conditions.

**Unexpected Braking or Jerking**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may jerk unexpectedly, lunge forward briefly, or activate the anti-lock brake system prematurely while slowing down in stop-and-go traffic.
- **Solution:** Check for any applicable manufacturer software updates or have the regenerative and hydraulic brake integration inspected by a dealer.

## Electrical

### Infotainment and Body Control Modules

The ZDX relies heavily on digital screens and connected software for climate, audio, and vehicle controls. Initial software rollouts have experienced occasional glitches and display failures.

**Screen Blanking and Software Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Digital instrument displays or center infotainment screens may freeze, go blank, or fail to respond after updates.
- **Solution:** Perform a system reboot or visit the dealership to reinstall stable software calibrations.

## Safety

### Forward Collision Avoidance and Camera Systems

Advanced driver assistance systems provide collision mitigation and adaptive cruise control. Sensitivity issues can occasionally trigger phantom braking events.

**False Positive Automatic Emergency Braking**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may unexpectedly engage emergency braking or collision warnings when no obstructions are present in front or while reversing out of spaces.
- **Solution:** Keep camera lenses and radar sensors clean; consult a dealer for potential ADAS calibration updates.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V614000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Antilock/Traction Control/Electronic Limited Slip:Control Unit/Module](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Acura&model=ZDX&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** The anti-lock brake system (ABS) may activate unexpectedly and release brake pressure in the vehicle's service brake system. A release of brake pressure can cause a loss of braking ability, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 25V018000: Electrical System:Propulsion System: Cables](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Acura&model=ZDX&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** The front passenger stabilizer bar bracket bolts may become loose and detach from the frame, which can damage the high voltage cables or EV battery coolant lines. Damaged high voltage cables or EV battery coolant lines can cause a loss of drive power, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and tighten the bolts, and repair the stabilizer bar and bracket, high voltage cables, and EV battery coolant lines as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V234000: Suspension:Front:Stabilizer Bar](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Acura&model=ZDX&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** Damaged cables or coolant lines can result in a vehicle shutdown, causing a loss of drive power and increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and tighten the bolts, and repair the stabilizer bar and bracket, high voltage cables, and EV battery coolant lines as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V112000: Back Over Prevention:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Acura&model=ZDX&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** Additionally, a software error may cause the rearview camera to display a blank screen when the vehicle is in reverse. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) numbers 101, "Control and Displays," 111, "Rear Visibility," and 305, "Electric-Powered Vehicles." An instrument panel display.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the vehicle radio control module software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V306000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Acura&model=ZDX&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** A rearview image that does not display correctly reduces the driver's view behind the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash or injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the rearview camera, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2024 Acura ZDX offers compelling electric performance and luxury amenities, but as a first-year EV sharing platform architecture, it displays notable software and electronic growing pains. Prospective buyers should verify that all dealer software campaigns and updates have been completed. Prioritizing a model with remaining factory warranty coverage provides essential peace of mind against early electrical and driver-assist anomalies.
