# 2005 Acura RL

### Overview
The 2005 Acura RL, a luxury sedan, generally presents a solid reliability record, reflecting Acura's reputation for building durable vehicles. While no car is entirely without potential issues, the RL from this model year tends to perform well, especially in its core mechanical systems. Owners often praise its comfortable ride, advanced features for its time, and overall build quality. However, like many vehicles of this era, certain components may require attention as they age. This analysis focuses on documented common problems beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential areas of concern for prospective buyers or current owners.

---


## Engine

### 3.5L V6
**Timing Belt Replacement**
- **Symptoms:** Stretched or worn timing belt can lead to poor engine performance, misfires, or catastrophic engine damage if it breaks.
- **Solution:** Scheduled replacement at recommended intervals (typically around 105,000 miles).
- **Cost:** $600 - $1000

**Variable Valve Timing (VVT) Actuator Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Rattling noise from the engine, especially on startup; check engine light may illuminate.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the VVT actuator and potentially related seals or solenoids.
- **Cost:** $400 - $800


## Transmission

### 5-Speed Automatic
**Transmission Fluid Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Leaking fluid, potential for slipping gears, rough shifting, or transmission overheating.
- **Solution:** Inspection and repair of seals, lines, or transmission pan.
- **Cost:** $200 - $500

**Torque Converter Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Shuddering or slipping during acceleration, delayed engagement, or unusual noises.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the torque converter.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1500


## Suspension

### Standard Chassis
**Front Suspension Bushing Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Clunking or rattling noises over bumps, vague steering feel, uneven tire wear.
- **Solution:** Replacement of worn control arm bushings.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600


## Electrical

### Standard Electrical System
**Alternator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Dimming headlights, battery warning light illuminated, electrical components malfunctioning, difficulty starting.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the alternator.
- **Cost:** $400 - $700

**Power Window Motor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Power windows not operating, slow operation, or unusual noises when trying to move windows.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the power window motor and regulator.
- **Cost:** $250 - $500


## Exterior

### Standard Exterior
**No Major Issues**
- **Symptoms:** No widespread issues reported.
- **Solution:** No widespread issues reported.
- **Cost:** No widespread issues reported.


## Interior

### Standard Interior
**HVAC Blower Motor Resistor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** HVAC fan only works on the highest setting, or not at all.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the blower motor resistor.
- **Cost:** $100 - $250

**Navigation System Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Screen freezing, slow response, or incorrect map data.
- **Solution:** Software update or replacement of the navigation unit.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1500


--- 
### Summary Verdict
The 2005 Acura RL is generally a reliable luxury sedan, with its V6 engine and automatic transmission proving to be robust for the most part. Potential owners should be aware of the scheduled timing belt replacement and possible VVT actuator issues. Transmission fluid leaks and torque converter problems are less common but can occur. On the electrical side, alternators and power window components are the most frequently cited concerns. Interior issues like HVAC resistor failure and navigation system glitches are also noted. Overall, with proper maintenance and attention to these specific potential issues, the 2005 RL can offer a dependable and comfortable ownership experience.
