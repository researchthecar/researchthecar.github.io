# 2020 Acura RDX Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2020 Acura RDX delivers a compelling mix of athletic handling, a well-appointed cabin, and standard turbocharged performance. While generally regarded as a comfortable and stylish compact luxury SUV, used car buyers should pay close attention to a notable pattern of owner complaints concerning exterior glass and powertrain electronics.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4

The RDX features a 2.0-liter turbocharged inline-4 engine producing 272 horsepower and 280 lb-ft of torque, offering brisk acceleration for daily driving. Overall, the powerplant is robust, though select owners have reported drivability glitches related to moisture intrusion under specific weather conditions.

**Limp Mode Due to Intercooler Moisture**
- **Symptoms:** While driving at highway speeds in heavy rain or inclement weather, moisture can enter the intercooler system, causing the vehicle to enter 'Limp Mode' with a flashing check engine light and a sudden loss of motive power.
- **Solution:** Have the vehicle inspected by a qualified technician. Software updates or intercooler modifications may be required depending on manufacturer service bulletins.

## Transmission

### 10-Speed Automatic Transmission

Power is routed through a 10-speed automatic transmission available with either front-wheel drive or Acura's Super Handling All-Wheel Drive (SH-AWD) system. This transmission generally shifts smoothly and efficiently, with very few widespread mechanical complaints reported by owners.

## Electrical

### Infotainment and Cabin Electronics

Cabin electronics provide a wide array of advanced tech features, though some users experience intermittent software glitches with infotainment displays and starting prompts.

**Infotainment Screen Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The central infotainment display may intermittently go completely dark, resulting in a loss of audio and radio functions.
- **Solution:** Perform a system reboot or software update at the dealership; persistent hardware failures may require module replacement.

## Exterior

### Glass and Sunroof

Exterior body panels and lighting are generally durable, but the vehicle exhibits a distinct concentration of complaints regarding fragile rear glass and sunroof panels.

**Spontaneous Rear Windshield and Sunroof Shattering**
- **Symptoms:** The rear tailgate glass or panoramic sunroof may shatter spontaneously while parked, upon closing doors, or while driving at highway speeds, often accompanied by a loud pop.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the affected glass panel. Note that some owners report dealer reluctance to cover out-of-pocket expenses despite known technical service bulletins.
- **Cost:** $1,100

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V215000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Acura&model=RDX&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** Fuel pump failure can cause an engine stall while driving, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Honda will notify owners, and dealers will replace the fuel pump assembly, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V158000: Seat Belts:Front:Buckle Assembly](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Acura&model=RDX&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** A manufacturing issue with the front seat belts may cause the seat belt buckle channel to interfere with the release button, preventing the seat belt buckle from latching. An unlatched seat belt cannot properly restrain the seat occupant during a crash, increasing their risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the driver and front passenger seat belt buckle release buttons or the buckle assemblies as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V858000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Acura&model=RDX&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** Fuel pump failure can cause an engine stall while driving, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the fuel pump module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V064000: Air Bags:Sensor:Occupant Classification:Front Passenger](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Acura&model=RDX&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** The front passenger seat weight sensor may crack and short circuit, failing to suppress the air bag as intended. An air bag that deploys unintentionally during a crash can increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the seat weight sensors, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V332000: Air Bags:Sensor:Occupant Classification:Front Passenger](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Acura&model=RDX&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** The front passenger seat weight sensor may crack and short circuit, which can cause the air bags to deploy unintentionally during a crash. Air bags that deploy unintentionally during a crash increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the seat weight sensors, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2020 Acura RDX is an appealing used compact luxury crossover that offers strong performance and a comfortable ride. Prospective buyers should thoroughly inspect the vehicle history and check for any past glass replacements, as spontaneous rear windshield and sunroof shattering represents the most prominent owner complaint pattern. Ensuring that powertrain software updates have been applied can also help mitigate potential intercooler moisture issues.
