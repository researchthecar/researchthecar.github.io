# 2021 Acura RDX Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2021 Acura RDX delivers a blend of luxury, comfort, and athletic handling, making it a compelling choice in the compact luxury SUV segment. However, prospective buyers should review reported owner complaints regarding select electrical, structural, and powertrain concerns to ensure a well-informed purchase.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4

This 2.0-liter turbocharged inline-4 engine produces 272 horsepower and 280 lb-ft of torque, providing brisk acceleration for daily driving. It generally offers solid performance, though owners have occasionally reported isolated mechanical issues such as coolant leaks.

## Transmission

### 10-Speed Automatic Transmission

Equipped with a 10-speed automatic transmission paired with front-wheel drive or Super Handling All-Wheel Drive (SH-AWD), it delivers smooth and responsive gear shifts. The transmission setup is typically robust, though minor fluid leaks from output shaft seals have been noted in some all-wheel-drive models.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes & Electronic Parking Brake

The braking system provides confident stopping power, but owners have flagged potential rotor judder and parking brake mechanical issues.

**Brake Vibration and Parking Brake Seizure**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may experience a juddering sensation when applying brakes at moderate to higher speeds, or find that the electronic parking brake fails to release.
- **Solution:** Inspect brake rotors and pads for uneven wear or warping, and have the electronic parking brake actuator tested or replaced if binding occurs.

## Electrical

### Infotainment and Lighting Systems

Electrical complaints center primarily around occasional infotainment glitches and automated exterior lighting sensitivity.

**Infotainment Screen Blanking and Auto High-Beam Glitch**
- **Symptoms:** The central infotainment display may intermittently go completely blank while driving, or auto high-beams may activate inappropriately and blind oncoming traffic.
- **Solution:** Check for available software updates at an Acura dealership or inspect lighting control modules.

## Exterior

### Glass and Sunroof Structure

Exterior body complaints are infrequent but include notable reports of stress-related glass breakage involving the sunroof and rear window.

**Panoramic Sunroof and Rear Window Shattering**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have reported instances where the panoramic sunroof or rear tailgate glass shatters unexpectedly, often accompanied by a loud pop.
- **Solution:** Inspect glass panels for stress cracks or frame alignment issues, and replace damaged glass under insurance or warranty if applicable.

## Safety

### Driver Assistance & Safety Systems

Advanced safety features enhance protection, but electronic sensor faults can trigger simultaneous warning lights and temporary system disablement.

**Multiple Driver Assist Warning Lights**
- **Symptoms:** Dashboard error messages may suddenly illuminate for systems like Lane Keeping Assist, Collision Mitigation Braking, and Road Departure Mitigation, sometimes affecting electronic power steering functionality at startup.
- **Solution:** Have wheel speed sensors, battery voltage, and electronic control modules diagnosed by a qualified technician.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V939000: Power Train:Driveline:Driveshaft](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Acura&model=RDX&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** A component in the right or left front driveshaft may have been improperly heat-treated, which could result in driveshaft failure. Either condition can increase the risk of a crash or injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the right or left front driveshaft as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V064000: Air Bags:Sensor:Occupant Classification:Front Passenger](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Acura&model=RDX&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The front passenger seat weight sensor may crack and short circuit, failing to suppress the air bag as intended. An air bag that deploys unintentionally during a crash can increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the seat weight sensors, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V332000: Air Bags:Sensor:Occupant Classification:Front Passenger](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Acura&model=RDX&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The front passenger seat weight sensor may crack and short circuit, which can cause the air bags to deploy unintentionally during a crash. Air bags that deploy unintentionally during a crash increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the seat weight sensors, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2021 Acura RDX is an enjoyable, well-rounded luxury crossover with strong performance credentials. While most owners experience trouble-free driving, used-car buyers should thoroughly inspect the vehicle's electronic systems, brakes, and glass components during a pre-purchase evaluation.
