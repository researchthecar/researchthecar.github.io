The 2022 Acura RDX is a popular compact SUV known for its sporty handling, comfortable interior, and generous features list. It comes standard with a turbocharged 2.0-liter four-cylinder engine producing 272 horsepower and 280 lb-ft of torque, paired with a 10-speed automatic transmission. While praised for its value, performance, and tech features, some owners have reported issues that are important to consider. Common likes include the spacious interior, strong acceleration, and user-friendly infotainment system. Dislikes often center around the sometimes clunky infotainment system and the presence of road noise at highway speeds.

**Engine**

**2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4**

*   **Excessive Oil Consumption**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported higher-than-expected oil consumption, requiring them to add oil between scheduled oil changes. Symptoms include low oil level warnings and the need to frequently check the oil dipstick.
    *   Solution: The severity of oil consumption can vary. In some cases, switching to a different oil viscosity or brand may help. More severe cases may require a compression test to check the health of the engine and potentially lead to piston ring replacement or other engine repairs.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: Oil change ($50-$150). For piston ring replacement or other engine repairs ($2,000+)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**10-Speed Automatic**

*   **Harsh or Jerky Shifting**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported experiencing harsh or jerky shifting, particularly at lower speeds or during downshifts. This can manifest as a noticeable lurch or hesitation when the transmission changes gears.
    *   Solution: A software update to the transmission control module (TCM) may resolve the issue. In more severe cases, transmission replacement may be required.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: Software update ($0 - $200); Transmission replacement ($5,000+)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

No Issues Found

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Freezing/Crashing**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The infotainment system, particularly the touchpad interface, can freeze, crash, or become unresponsive. This can impact navigation, audio controls, and other vehicle functions.
    *   Solution: Performing a system reset (often involving holding down the power button) may provide a temporary fix. A software update to the infotainment system may provide a longer term fix. A faulty head unit may need to be replaced.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: Software Update ($0-$200). Infotainment system replacement ($1,000+)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Backup Camera Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Backup camera intermittently fails to activate or displays a distorted/black screen when the vehicle is shifted into reverse.
    * Solution: Check and replace the backup camera fuse. If the problem persists, inspect the wiring harness for damage or loose connections. If those are in good condition, replace the backup camera unit.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: Fuse replacement ($5-$20), camera unit replacement ($200-$500+)
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Sunroof Rattling/Squeaking**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported rattling or squeaking noises coming from the sunroof area, particularly when driving on uneven surfaces.
    *   Solution: Lubricating the sunroof seals and tracks may alleviate the noise. Adjusting the sunroof mechanism or replacing worn components may be necessary in more severe cases.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: Lubrication ($50-$100); Sunroof adjustment/repair ($200-$500+)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Rattling/Vibrations in Dashboard**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Owners have reported various rattles and vibrations emanating from within the dashboard, especially at certain engine speeds or road conditions. The exact source can be difficult to pinpoint.
    *   Solution: The fix often involves identifying the source of the rattle (e.g., loose wiring harness, trim panel) and securing or padding the component. Dealership technicians or specialized interior repair shops may be best suited for this task.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: ($100-$400+)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

The 2022 Acura RDX, while generally reliable and well-received, has experienced some common issues. The infotainment system glitches and transmission issues can be annoying and costly to repair. Engine oil consumption and sunroof rattles, though less severe, are also worth noting. Potential buyers should be aware of these issues and consider a pre-purchase inspection to identify any potential problems. The severity of these issues ranges from minor inconveniences to more significant repairs, and the cost of fixing these issues can vary from a few hundred to several thousand dollars.
