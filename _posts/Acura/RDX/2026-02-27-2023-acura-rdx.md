# 2023 Acura RDX Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used luxury compact SUV, the Acura RDX often stands out for its engaging driving dynamics, comfortable interior, and strong value proposition. However, buyers reviewing owner experiences should pay close attention to reports regarding electronics, visibility components, and occasional powertrain hiccups. While many owners enjoy trouble-free driving, checking for software updates and inspecting key systems before purchase can help ensure a smoother ownership experience.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4

The RDX comes standard with a turbocharged 2.0-liter inline-4 engine producing 272 horsepower and 280 lb-ft of torque, paired with a 10-speed automatic transmission. This powerplant delivers brisk acceleration and confident highway passing power for daily commuting.

**Engine Power Loss and Limp Mode**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers report that while accelerating or entering highways, the engine may abruptly lose power and enter limp mode with a flashing check engine light.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified technician pull diagnostic trouble codes to diagnose potential turbocharger, fuel delivery, or sensor faults.

## Transmission

### 10-Speed Automatic Transmission

Power is routed through a 10-speed automatic transmission available with either front-wheel drive or Acura's Super Handling All-Wheel Drive (SH-AWD) system. This transmission generally provides smooth shifting and optimized gear ratios, with few widespread mechanical complaints reported by owners.

## Electrical

### Infotainment and Navigation System

The electrical architecture supports advanced infotainment and driver-assistance features, though some owners have experienced software freezes and navigation glitches.

**Apple CarPlay Disconnection and Black Screen**
- **Symptoms:** The infotainment screen may go completely black, disconnect from Apple CarPlay during transit, and experience prolonged delays before reconnecting.
- **Solution:** Check for manufacturer software updates at a dealership or perform a system reset to clear recurring glitches.

**GPS and Clock Errors**
- **Symptoms:** The navigation system GPS may stop working entirely, leading to incorrect clock and compass readouts and disorientation while driving.
- **Solution:** Update the vehicle's navigation firmware or replace malfunctioning antenna modules if hardware failure is detected.

## Exterior

### Windshield and Windows

Exterior glass and trim generally hold up well, though isolated reports indicate occasional issues with rear window durability.

**Spontaneous Rear Glass Shattering**
- **Symptoms:** The rear windshield or liftgate glass can shatter unexpectedly without impact or external provocation.
- **Solution:** Inspect the rear glass for structural stress points and replace the shattered window panel, verifying proper tailgate alignment.

## Safety

### Forward Collision Avoidance

Driver-assistance suites provide comprehensive active safety, but sensitive radar sensors can occasionally trigger false-positive warnings.

**Inadvertent Emergency Braking**
- **Symptoms:** The collision mitigation system may falsely detect obstacles at highway speeds and engage the emergency brakes abruptly without warning.
- **Solution:** Have the dealer calibrate the radar sensors and check for sensor obstructions or software calibration updates.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V430000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Antilock/Traction Control/Electronic Limited Slip:Control Unit/Module](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Acura&model=RDX&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** Unintended vehicle movement or an unexpected increase in brake pedal travel can increase the risk of a crash or injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the VSA modulator, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V332000: Air Bags:Sensor:Occupant Classification:Front Passenger](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Acura&model=RDX&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** The front passenger seat weight sensor may crack and short circuit, which can cause the air bags to deploy unintentionally during a crash. Air bags that deploy unintentionally during a crash increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the seat weight sensors, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The vehicle offers a compelling blend of luxury, performance, and everyday utility, making it an attractive option on the used market. Prospective buyers should thoroughly test all electronics, inspect the exterior glass and lighting, and ensure all safety system recalls or software updates have been completed.
