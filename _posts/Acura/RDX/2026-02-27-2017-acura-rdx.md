# 2017 Acura RDX Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2017 Acura RDX offers a compelling blend of luxury, comfort, and everyday usability, anchored by a robust V6 powertrain. While generally regarded as a dependable pre-owned luxury crossover, prospective buyers should pay close attention to potential electrical issues, transmission torque converter concerns, and brake wear patterns. Reviewing service history for prior warranty or repair work on these specific components can help ensure a worry-free ownership experience.

---

## Engine

### 3.5L V6

Equipped with a 3.5-liter V6 engine producing approximately 279 horsepower and 252 lb-ft of torque, providing smooth and responsive acceleration. This engine has a strong reputation for longevity, though routine maintenance is essential to prevent carbon buildup and ignition component wear.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

Pairs a conventional 6-speed automatic transmission with front-wheel drive or all-wheel drive, offering refined gear changes compared to early multi-speed units. However, owners have occasionally reported torque converter wear requiring major service.

**Torque Converter Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle experiences shuddering, vibration during acceleration, delayed shifting, or unexpected stalling accompanied by a transmission warning light.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified mechanic inspect the transmission control module and replace the torque converter assembly if internal component wear is confirmed.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

Provides reliable stopping power under normal conditions, though some owners report premature brake wear or rotor pulsation requiring early service.

**Brake Pedal Travel and Rotor Pulsation**
- **Symptoms:** Brake pedal travels excessively toward the floor during application, or severe pulsation is felt through the pedal due to warped rotors.
- **Solution:** Inspect brake lines, master cylinder, and brake pads; resurface or replace warped rotors as necessary.

## Exterior

### Keyless Access and Body Control Unit

Handles keyless entry, security systems, and interior accessory electronics, which can occasionally experience intermittent faults.

**Random Alarm Activation and Dashboard Flickering**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle alarm sounds unexpectedly, dashboard lights flicker or tick while the car is turned off, and keyless entry features may intermittently fail.
- **Solution:** Inspect and replace the smart power control unit or keyless access module responsible for managing security and accessory power.
- **Cost:** $800

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V256000: Equipment:Other:Labels](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Acura&model=RDX&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** The Certification Labels on the affected vehicles were printed with ink that may be inadvertently wiped away with an alcohol solvent. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of 49 CFR Part 567, "Certification." If the label information can be wiped away, the operator may not be able to refer to the information, possibly resulting in the.
- **What to do:** Honda and Acura will notify owners, and dealers will replace the Certification Label, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2017 Acura RDX is a solid choice in the used compact luxury crossover segment, backed by a strong V6 engine and comfortable road manners. Buyers should thoroughly inspect the vehicle for proper transmission shifting behavior and verify that all electrical features, keyless entry modules, and safety warning lights function correctly during a test drive.
