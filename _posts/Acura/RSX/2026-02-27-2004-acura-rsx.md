# 2004 Acura RSX

### Overview
The 2004 Acura RSX, a popular compact sports coupe, is generally regarded as a reliable vehicle, especially for its era. It was offered with two distinct engine and transmission configurations, catering to different performance preferences. While the RSX is known for its engaging driving dynamics and sporty appeal, like any vehicle, it has certain areas that owners have reported issues with over time. This analysis focuses on documented mechanical and electrical concerns beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential problem areas for prospective buyers or current owners looking to maintain their RSX.

---


## Engine

### 2.0L K20A3 (Base Model)
**Oil Consumption**
- **Symptoms:** Excessive oil burning, potentially leading to low oil levels and engine damage if not monitored.
- **Solution:** Engine rebuild or replacement; regular oil level checks are crucial.
- **Cost:** $1,500 - $4,000

**VTEC Solenoid Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of power at higher RPMs, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the VTEC solenoid.
- **Cost:** $150 - $300


### 2.0L K20A2 (Type-S Model)
**Oil Consumption**
- **Symptoms:** Excessive oil burning, potentially leading to low oil levels and engine damage if not monitored.
- **Solution:** Engine rebuild or replacement; regular oil level checks are crucial.
- **Cost:** $1,500 - $4,000

**Timing Chain Tensioner Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Rattling noise from the engine, especially on startup; check engine light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the timing chain tensioner and potentially the chain and guides.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1,200

### Summary Verdict
The 2004 Acura RSX is a solid choice for a sporty and reliable daily driver, particularly if well-maintained. The K-series engines are generally robust, though some owners report oil consumption issues, especially on higher-mileage examples, which necessitates diligent monitoring. Manual transmissions are typically durable, with clutch wear being the most common concern. Automatic transmissions are less frequently reported with issues. Electrical components like power window motors and alternators can fail, but these are not uncommon for vehicles of this age. Overall, the RSX offers a good balance of performance and reliability, with most potential issues being manageable with proper care and timely repairs.
