**Introduction**
The 1995 Oldsmobile Aurora is a luxury sedan known for its sleek design and powerful engine options. It comes equipped with a 4.0L V8 engine that produces 250 horsepower and 260 lb-ft of torque. The Aurora also offers a variety of transmission options, including a 4-speed automatic and a 5-speed manual. Despite its popularity, the Aurora has been plagued by several issues over the years.

**Engine**
- **4.0L V8 Engine**
  - **Oil leaks**
    - Description/Symptoms: Visible oil leaks around the engine block, oil pan, or valve covers
    - Solution: Replace leaking gaskets or seals
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600+
  - **Intake manifold gasket failure**
    - Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaks, engine overheating, rough idling
    - Solution: Replace intake manifold gaskets
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800+
  - **Timing chain tensioner failure**
    - Description/Symptoms: Engine rattling or knocking noises, reduced engine power
    - Solution: Replace timing chain tensioner
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,200+
  - **EGR valve failure**
    - Description/Symptoms: Engine misfires, rough idling, decreased fuel economy
    - Solution: Replace EGR valve
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $250 - $400+

**Transmission**
- **4-Speed Automatic Transmission**
  - **Shifting problems**
    - Description/Symptoms: Transmission may slip or hesitate when shifting gears
    - Solution: Overhaul or replace transmission
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
  - **Torque converter shudder**
    - Description/Symptoms: Vibration or shuddering felt during acceleration
    - Solution: Replace torque converter
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $1,500+
- **5-Speed Manual Transmission**
  - **Clutch failure**
    - Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears, slipping clutch
    - Solution: Replace clutch
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800+

**Suspension**
- **Front suspension bushings failure**
  - Description/Symptoms: Clunking or knocking noises, uneven tire wear
  - Solution: Replace worn bushings
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
- **Rear suspension springs failure**
  - Description/Symptoms: Sagging or bouncy rear end, poor handling
  - Solution: Replace rear suspension springs
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+

**Electrical**
- **Battery drain**
  - Description/Symptoms: Battery dies prematurely
  - Solution: Test and replace faulty electrical components
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
- **Alternator failure**
  - Description/Symptoms: Dimming headlights, flickering dashboard lights
  - Solution: Replace alternator
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800+

**Exterior**
- **Door handle breakage**
  - Description/Symptoms: Difficulty opening doors, broken handle
  - Solution: Replace door handle
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+

**Interior**
- **Dashboard cracking**
  - Description/Symptoms: Cracks on the dashboard surface
  - Solution: Replace dashboard
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
- **Climate control failure**
  - Description/Symptoms: Difficulty adjusting temperature, fan not working
  - Solution: Replace faulty climate control module
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+

**Recalls**
- NHTSA Recall Number 01V273000: Power window switch failure
- NHTSA Recall Number 07V089000: Airbag sensor failure
- NHTSA Recall Number 10V232000: Transmission range sensor failure

**Summary**
The 1995 Oldsmobile Aurora is a stylish and powerful sedan, but it has its share of issues. Engine problems, such as oil leaks and intake manifold gasket failure, can be expensive to repair. Transmission problems can also be costly, especially if the transmission needs to be overhauled or replaced. Suspension and electrical issues are less severe and generally cheaper to fix. Interior problems, such as dashboard cracking and climate control failure, can also be annoying and costly to repair. Overall, the 1995 Oldsmobile Aurora has a moderate level of reliability with varying repair costs depending on the severity of the issue.
