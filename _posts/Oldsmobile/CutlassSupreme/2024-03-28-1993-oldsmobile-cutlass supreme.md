**Introduction:**

The 1993 Oldsmobile Cutlass Supreme is a mid-size sedan known for its comfortable ride and spacious interior. It was available with a variety of engine and transmission options, including:

* 3.1L V6 (160 hp / 180 lb-ft torque)
* 3.4L V6 (165 hp / 210 lb-ft torque)
* 3.8L V6 (205 hp / 225 lb-ft torque)

With 6-speed and 10-speed automatic transmission options, the Cutlass Supreme catered to different driving preferences.

While praised for its smooth performance and cabin space, some owners have encountered specific issues with certain components.

**Engine**

* **3.1L V6**
    * **Intake Manifold Gasket Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaks, overheating, rough idling
        * Solution: Replace intake manifold gasket
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
        * Recalls: None Found
    * **Head Gasket Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaks, overheating, white smoke from exhaust
        * Solution: Replace head gasket
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $1,500
        * Recalls: None Found
* **3.4L V6**
    * **No Major Issues Found**
* **3.8L V6**
    * **Fuel Injector Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: Engine misfires, rough idling, decreased fuel economy
        * Solution: Replace fuel injector(s)
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $250 - $400 per injector
        * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

* **6-Speed Automatic**
    * **Torque Converter Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: Slipping gears, shuddering, overheating
        * Solution: Replace torque converter
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,000
        * Recalls: None Found
* **10-Speed Automatic**
    * **No Major Issues Found**

**Suspension**

* **Strut Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Clunking noises, uneven tire wear, poor handling
    * Solution: Replace struts
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $600 per strut
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain**
    * Description/Symptoms: Battery dies prematurely
    * Solution: Diagnose and repair source of battery drain (e.g., parasitic draw, faulty alternator)
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Ignition Coil Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Engine misfires, hard starting
    * Solution: Replace ignition coil(s)
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $250 per coil
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Door Handle Breakage**
    * Description/Symptoms: Exterior door handle breaks off
    * Solution: Replace door handle
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Dashboard Cracking**
    * Description/Symptoms: Cracks develop on the dashboard
    * Solution: Replace or repair dashboard
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Power Window Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Power windows cease to function
    * Solution: Diagnose and repair electrical issue or replace window motor
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

The 1993 Oldsmobile Cutlass Supreme offers a comfortable and spacious ride, but it has experienced various issues with its engine, transmission, suspension, electrical, exterior, and interior components. These issues range from minor annoyances to costly repairs. While the Cutlass Supreme has received positive feedback for its ride quality, it's important to be aware of these potential problems before considering purchasing one.
