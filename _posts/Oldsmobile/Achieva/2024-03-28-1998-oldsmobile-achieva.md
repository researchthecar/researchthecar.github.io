**Introduction to the 1998 Oldsmobile Achieva**

The 1998 Oldsmobile Achieva was a mid-size sedan offered with a variety of engine and transmission options. The base engine was a 2.4-liter inline-4 producing 150 horsepower and 155 lb-ft of torque, while the optional engine was a 3.4-liter V6 producing 185 horsepower and 215 lb-ft of torque. The 2.4-liter engine was paired with a 4-speed automatic transmission, while the 3.4-liter engine was available with either the 4-speed automatic or a 5-speed manual transmission.

The Achieva was praised for its comfortable ride, spacious interior, and fuel efficiency. However, it was also criticized for its bland styling, lack of performance, and poor reliability.

**Engine**

**2.4-liter**

* **Intake manifold gasket failure:** This issue can cause coolant and oil leaks, as well as engine overheating. The solution is to replace the intake manifold gasket. Estimated cost to fix: $300 - $500+
* **Timing chain failure:** This issue can cause the engine to lose power or stall. The solution is to replace the timing chain. Estimated cost to fix: $800 - $1200+

**3.4-liter**

* **Head gasket failure:** This issue can cause coolant and oil leaks, as well as engine overheating. The solution is to replace the head gasket. Estimated cost to fix: $1000 - $1500+
* **Intake manifold runner control (IMRC) failure:** This issue can cause the engine to idle rough and lose power. The solution is to replace the IMRC. Estimated cost to fix: $200 - $400+

**Transmission**

* **Torque converter failure:** This issue can cause the transmission to shudder or vibrate. The solution is to replace the torque converter. Estimated cost to fix: $500 - $1000+

**Summary**

The 1998 Oldsmobile Achieva is a reliable vehicle with a few common issues. The most serious issues include head gasket failure, intake manifold gasket failure, and transmission failure.
