**2004 Oldsmobile Bravada**

The 2004 Oldsmobile Bravada was a mid-size SUV that offered a range of engine and transmission options. It was powered by a standard 4.2L V6 engine with 275 horsepower and 275 lb-ft of torque. For those seeking more power, there was also an optional 5.3L V8 engine that generated 300 horsepower and 330 lb-ft of torque. Transmission choices included a 4-speed automatic transmission and a 5-speed manual transmission.

Despite its popularity, the 2004 Bravada has been known to experience various issues, ranging from engine and transmission problems to suspension, electrical, exterior, and interior concerns.

**Engine**

* **4.2L V6 Engine**
    * **Intake Manifold Gasket Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: As the intake manifold gasket fails, it can lead to engine coolant and oil leaks. Additional symptoms may include overheating, reduced engine performance, and poor fuel economy.
        * Solution: Replacement of the intake manifold gasket is necessary to resolve this issue.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
        * Recalls: 04V108000
    * **Timing Chain Tensioner Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: Failing timing chain tensioners can lead to a rattling or slapping sound coming from the engine. In severe cases, it can result in engine damage if not addressed promptly.
        * Solution: Replacement of the timing chain tensioners is required.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1000 - $1500+
        * Recalls: None Found
* **5.3L V8 Engine**
    * **Piston Ring Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: Piston ring failure can cause excessive oil consumption and reduced engine performance. Other symptoms may include blue or white smoke from the exhaust and a noticeable drop in oil levels.
        * Solution: Repairing this issue typically requires engine disassembly and replacement of the affected piston rings.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2500 - $3500+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

* **4-Speed Automatic Transmission**
    * **Transmission Fluid Leak**
        * Description/Symptoms: A transmission fluid leak can lead to insufficient fluid levels, which can result in transmission slippage, overheating, and premature failure.
        * Solution: The source of the leak needs to be identified and repaired, and the transmission fluid should be replaced.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
        * Recalls: None Found
* **5-Speed Manual Transmission**
    * **Synchros Wear Out**
        * Description/Symptoms: Worn-out synchros can lead to difficulty shifting gears, grinding noises, and premature transmission failure.
        * Solution: Replacement of the affected synchros and any other damaged components is necessary.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1500 - $2500+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Front Control Arm Bushing Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Failing front control arm bushings can cause unwanted vibrations, uneven tire wear, and poor steering response.
    * Solution: Replacement of the worn-out bushings is required.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $600+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Rear Shock Absorber Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Worn-out rear shock absorbers can lead to reduced ride comfort, decreased handling stability, and premature tire wear.
    * Solution: Replacement of the rear shock absorbers is needed.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Alternator Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: A failing alternator may lead to electrical system issues, such as dim headlights, sluggish engine performance, and a loss of power.
    * Solution: Replacement of the faulty alternator is necessary.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Battery Drain**
        * Description/Symptoms: A battery drain can lead to a dead battery and difficulty starting the vehicle.
        * Solution: Troubleshooting is needed to identify the source of the drain and repair any faulty components.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the cause of the drain
        * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Door Lock Actuator Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Failing door lock actuators can prevent the doors from locking or unlocking properly.
    * Solution: Replacement of the faulty door lock actuators is necessary.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Tail Light Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Malfunctioning tail lights can affect visibility and safety.
    * Solution: Replacing the bulbs or repairing any electrical issues is necessary.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Climate Control Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Issues with the climate control system can lead to malfunctioning AC, heating, or ventilation.
    * Solution: Troubleshooting is needed to identify the source of the problem, which may require replacing faulty components or repairing electrical issues.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the specific issue
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Dashboard Lights Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Malfunctioning dashboard lights can affect visibility and the driver's ability to monitor the vehicle's systems.
    * Solution: Replacing the bulbs or repairing any electrical issues is necessary.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2004 Oldsmobile Bravada has a range of issues that can affect different components of the vehicle. Engine and transmission problems are some of the more common and costly concerns. Suspension, electrical, exterior, and interior issues are also known to occur. The severity and cost of these issues can vary, but regular maintenance and repairs are recommended to address them in a timely manner and ensure a safe and reliable driving experience.
