**1992 Oldsmobile 98**

The 1992 Oldsmobile 98 is a full-size luxury sedan that was praised for its spacious and comfortable interior, powerful engine, and smooth ride. However, it also had some issues that owners should be aware of.

The 98 was available with two engine options: a 3.8L V6 with 170 horsepower and 220 lb-ft of torque, and a 5.0L V8 with 185 horsepower and 245 lb-ft of torque. Both engines were paired with a 4-speed automatic transmission.

**Engine**

**3.8L V6**

* **Intake manifold gasket failure:** Symptoms include coolant leaks, overheating, and poor engine performance. Solution: Replace the intake manifold gasket. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+
* **Timing chain tensioner failure:** Symptoms include a rattling noise from the engine, rough idle, and loss of power. Solution: Replace the timing chain tensioner. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+

**5.0L V8**

* **Cylinder head gasket failure:** Symptoms include coolant leaks, overheating, and white smoke from the exhaust. Solution: Replace the cylinder head gaskets. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
* **Oil pan gasket leak:** Symptoms include oil leaks from the bottom of the engine. Solution: Replace the oil pan gasket. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+

**Transmission**

**4-Speed Automatic**

* **Slipping gears:** Symptoms include a loss of power when shifting gears, or the transmission slipping out of gear completely. Solution: Rebuild or replace the transmission. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
* **Leaking transmission fluid:** Symptoms include transmission fluid leaks, low fluid levels, and difficulty shifting gears. Solution: Replace the leaking seals or components. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+

**Suspension**

* **Front strut failure:** Symptoms include a clunking or rattling noise from the suspension, poor handling, and uneven tire wear. Solution: Replace the front struts. Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800+
* **Rear shock absorber failure:** Symptoms include a bouncing or rough ride, poor handling, and premature tire wear. Solution: Replace the rear shock absorbers. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+

**Electrical**

* **Alternator failure:** Symptoms include dim headlights, electrical system problems, and difficulty starting the engine. Solution: Replace the alternator. Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800+
* **Battery failure:** Symptoms include difficulty starting the engine, dead battery, and electrical system problems. Solution: Replace the battery. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+

**Exterior**

* **Door lock actuator failure:** Symptoms include difficulty locking or unlocking the doors, or the doors locking or unlocking on their own. Solution: Replace the door lock actuator. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
* **Power window failure:** Symptoms include difficulty operating the power windows, or the windows not working at all. Solution: Replace the power window motor or switch. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+

**Interior**

* **Climate control issues:** Symptoms include difficulty adjusting the temperature, air not blowing, or the climate control system not working at all. Solution: Diagnose and repair the faulty component. Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the problem
* **Cruise control issues:** Symptoms include difficulty setting or maintaining the cruise control, or the cruise control not working at all. Solution: Diagnose and repair the faulty component. Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the problem

**Summary**

While the 1992 Oldsmobile 98 is a spacious and powerful vehicle, it does have some potential issues that owners need to be aware of. The most common problems include engine gasket failures, transmission issues, and suspension problems. While some of these issues can be costly to repair, others are relatively minor and can be fixed for a reasonable cost. Overall, the 98 is a reliable and comfortable vehicle, but it's important to factor in the potential for repairs when considering this car.
