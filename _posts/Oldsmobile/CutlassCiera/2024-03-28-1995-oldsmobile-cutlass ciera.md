**1995 Oldsmobile Cutlass Ciera**

The Oldsmobile Cutlass Ciera is a mid-size sedan that was produced from 1982 to 1996. It was offered with a variety of engine and transmission options, including:

* 2.5L V6 with 3-speed automatic transmission (160 hp, 185 lb-ft)
* 3.1L V6 with 4-speed automatic transmission (160 hp, 185 lb-ft)
* 3.3L V6 with 4-speed automatic transmission (180 hp, 220 lb-ft)

People who own the Cutlass Ciera often praise its roomy interior, comfortable ride, and reliable performance. However, the vehicle has also been known to experience some common issues.

**Engine**
* **2.5L V6**
    * **Intake Manifold Gasket Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaks, overheating, rough idle, loss of power
        * Solution: Replace intake manifold gasket
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+
    * **Valve Cover Gasket Leak**
        * Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks, burning oil smell
        * Solution: Replace valve cover gasket
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
* **3.1L V6**
    * **Head Gasket Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaks, overheating, white smoke from exhaust
        * Solution: Replace head gasket
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
    * **Intake Manifold Gasket Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaks, overheating, rough idle, loss of power
        * Solution: Replace intake manifold gasket
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+
* **3.3L V6**
    * **Intake Manifold Gasket Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaks, overheating, rough idle, loss of power
        * Solution: Replace intake manifold gasket
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+
    * **Water Pump Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaks, overheating, squealing noise
        * Solution: Replace water pump
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+

**Transmission**
* **3-Speed Automatic**
    * **Shift Solenoid Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: Transmission slipping or not shifting properly
        * Solution: Replace shift solenoid
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
* **4-Speed Automatic**
    * **Transmission Fluid Leak**
        * Description/Symptoms: Transmission fluid leaking from pan or lines
        * Solution: Replace transmission pan gasket or lines
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+
    * **Torque Converter Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: Transmission slipping or shuddering, loss of power
        * Solution: Replace torque converter
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+

**Suspension**
* **Front Strut Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Clunking or rattling noise over bumps, difficulty steering
    * Solution: Replace front struts
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $600+
* **Rear Shock Absorber Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Bumpy ride, poor handling, uneven tire wear
    * Solution: Replace rear shock absorbers
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+

**Electrical**
* **Alternator Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Dim headlights, flickering dashboard lights, battery not charging
    * Solution: Replace alternator
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $600+
* **Starter Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Engine cranks but doesn't start
    * Solution: Replace starter
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
* **Battery Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Engine won't start, weak or dim lights
    * Solution: Replace battery
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+

**Exterior**
* **Headlight Switch Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Headlights not turning on or flickering
    * Solution: Replace headlight switch
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+

**Interior**
* **Dashboard Lights Not Working**
    * Description/Symptoms: Lights on the dashboard not illuminating
    * Solution: Replace light bulbs or repair wiring
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $200+
* **Power Window Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Power windows not working
    * Solution: Replace window motor or repair wiring
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+

**Summary**

The 1995 Oldsmobile Cutlass Ciera is a reliable and comfortable vehicle that has been known to experience some common issues. While some of these issues can be costly to repair, many of them are relatively minor and can be fixed for a reasonable amount of money. Overall, the Cutlass Ciera is a good choice for buyers who are looking for a spacious and reliable sedan.
