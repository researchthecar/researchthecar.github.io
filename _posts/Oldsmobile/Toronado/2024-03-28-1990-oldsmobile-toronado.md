**Introduction:**

The 1990 Oldsmobile Toronado was a luxurious personal luxury coupe that offered a blend of comfort and performance. It came with a standard 3.8L V6 engine paired with a 4-speed automatic transmission. For those seeking more power, a 5.7L V8 engine was available as an option, also mated to a 4-speed automatic transmission. The Toronado was known for its smooth ride, spacious interior, and stylish design, but it was not without its drawbacks.

**Engine**

**3.8L V6**

* **Intake Manifold Gasket Failure:**
    * Symptoms: Engine coolant leaks, overheating, loss of power
    * Solution: Replace intake manifold gasket
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $600+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Head Gasket Failure:**
    * Symptoms: Engine coolant leaks, overheating, white smoke from exhaust
    * Solution: Replace head gasket
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $1,500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**5.7L V8**

* **Oil Pressure Sensor Failure:**
    * Symptoms: Check engine light, low oil pressure warning
    * Solution: Replace oil pressure sensor
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $300
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Fuel Pump Failure:**
    * Symptoms: Engine stalling, difficulty starting
    * Solution: Replace fuel pump
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**4-Speed Automatic**

* **Slipping Transmission:**
    * Symptoms: Delayed or sudden shifts, loss of power during acceleration
    * Solution: Repair or replace transmission
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Torque Converter Failure:**
    * Symptoms: Harsh shifting, shuddering during acceleration
    * Solution: Replace torque converter
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $700 - $1,200
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Front Suspension Struts Failure:**
    * Symptoms: Poor handling, rough ride, excessive vibration
    * Solution: Replace front suspension struts
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $600+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Rear Suspension Shock Absorbers Failure:**
    * Symptoms: Bouncy ride, difficulty controlling the vehicle
    * Solution: Replace rear suspension shock absorbers
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Alternator Failure:**
    * Symptoms: Dimming headlights, electrical system malfunctions
    * Solution: Replace alternator
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Battery Discharge:**
    * Symptoms: Difficulty starting, battery drain
    * Solution: Test and replace battery or electrical components as needed
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    * Recalls: 18V426000

**Exterior**

* **Sunroof Seal Failure:**
    * Symptoms: Water leaks into the interior
    * Solution: Replace sunroof seal
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $300
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Climate Control Failure:**
    * Symptoms: Malfunctioning heater, air conditioning, or ventilation
    * Solution: Repair or replace climate control system
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Power Window Failure:**
    * Symptoms: Difficulty opening or closing windows
    * Solution: Repair or replace power window motor or switch
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

Overall, the 1990 Oldsmobile Toronado has a range of potential issues spanning from minor inconveniences to major repairs. While some issues are relatively inexpensive to fix, others can be quite costly. Common issues include intake manifold gasket failure, head gasket failure, transmission slipping, and suspension strut failure. While the Toronado was a well-regarded vehicle, potential buyers should be aware of these potential issues and factor in the potential cost of repairs when considering a purchase.
