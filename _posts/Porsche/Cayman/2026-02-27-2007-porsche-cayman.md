# 2007 Porsche Cayman

### Overview
The 2007 Porsche Cayman, a mid-engine sports car, is generally regarded as a reliable vehicle, especially considering its performance-oriented nature. Built with Porsche's renowned engineering standards, it offers a thrilling driving experience with a solid mechanical foundation. While no car is entirely immune to issues, the Cayman's design prioritizes durability and driver engagement. Owners typically report satisfaction with its performance and build quality. However, like any enthusiast vehicle, maintenance is key, and specific components can experience wear or require attention over time, particularly those related to its high-performance systems. This analysis focuses on documented common problems beyond routine maintenance.

---


## Engine

### 2.7L Flat-6 (Base)
**Intermediate Shaft (IMS) Bearing Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Engine knocking noises, oil leaks, engine seizure.
- **Solution:** Bearing replacement (often with an upgraded unit) or engine replacement.
- **Cost:** $1,500 - $3,000 (bearing replacement), $7,000+ (engine replacement)

**Oil Cooler Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Visible oil leaks around the oil cooler, potential for oil smell.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the oil cooler and seals.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600


### 3.4L Flat-6 (S)
**Intermediate Shaft (IMS) Bearing Failure**

## Electrical

### Standard Electrical System
**Window Regulator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Power windows not operating, slow window movement.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the window regulator motor and mechanism.
- **Cost:** $300 - $500

**Headlight Condensation**
- **Symptoms:** Moisture or fogging inside headlight assemblies.
- **Solution:** Sealing the headlight assembly or replacement.
- **Cost:** $100 - $300 (sealing), $500+ (replacement)

### Summary Verdict
The 2007 Porsche Cayman is a well-built sports car with a generally strong reliability record. The most significant potential issue across both engine variants is the Intermediate Shaft (IMS) bearing, a known concern for many Porsche models of this era, which can lead to catastrophic engine failure if it fails. Clutch wear is a common maintenance item for manual transmissions, as expected in a performance vehicle. Electrical gremlins are infrequent but can include issues like window regulators and headlight sealing. Overall, with diligent maintenance and addressing potential IMS issues proactively, the 2007 Cayman offers a rewarding and relatively dependable ownership experience for an enthusiast vehicle.
