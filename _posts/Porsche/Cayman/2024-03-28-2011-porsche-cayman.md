**Introduction:**

The 2011 Porsche Cayman is a mid-engine sports car that offers a thrilling driving experience with its precise handling and powerful engines. It is available with two engine options: a 2.9-liter flat-six and a 3.4-liter flat-six. The 2.9-liter engine produces 265 horsepower and 206 lb-ft of torque, while the 3.4-liter engine generates 320 horsepower and 273 lb-ft of torque. Both engines are paired with a 6-speed manual or a 7-speed dual-clutch automatic transmission.

Despite its impressive performance and handling, the 2011 Porsche Cayman is not without its share of issues. Here is a comprehensive list of the specific problems reported by owners:

**Engine**

* **2.9-liter Flat-Six**
    * **Oil Leaks:** Symptoms include visible oil leaks around the engine or on the ground beneath the vehicle. Solution: Replace the leaking seals or gaskets. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
    * **Timing Chain Tensioner Failure:** Symptoms include a rattling or ticking noise from the engine. Solution: Replace the timing chain tensioner. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,000+
* **3.4-liter Flat-Six**
    * **Piston Ring Failure:** Symptoms include excessive oil consumption and blue smoke from the exhaust. Solution: Replace the piston rings and pistons. Estimated Cost to Fix: $3,000 - $6,000+
    * **Bore Scoring:** Symptoms include a loss of compression and reduced engine power. Solution: Rebuild or replace the engine. Estimated Cost to Fix: $10,000+

**Transmission**

* **6-Speed Manual**
    * **Shift Linkage Bushing Wear:** Symptoms include difficulty shifting gears or excessive play in the shifter. Solution: Replace the worn bushings. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * **Clutch Master Cylinder Failure:** Symptoms include a spongy or unresponsive clutch pedal. Solution: Replace the clutch master cylinder. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
* **7-Speed Dual-Clutch Automatic**
    * **Mechatronic Unit Failure:** Symptoms include sudden loss of power or difficulty shifting gears. Solution: Replace the mechatronic unit. Estimated Cost to Fix: $5,000 - $10,000+
    * **Torque Converter Clutch Shudder:** Symptoms include vibrations or shuddering during acceleration. Solution: Replace the torque converter clutch. Estimated Cost to Fix: $3,000 - $6,000+

**Suspension**

* **Front Ball Joint Wear:** Symptoms include clunking or rattling noises from the suspension, especially over bumps. Solution: Replace the worn ball joints. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+ per side
* **Rear Trailing Arm Bushing Wear:** Symptoms include excessive rear wheel camber and premature tire wear. Solution: Replace the worn bushings. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+ per side

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain:** Symptoms include a dead battery or slow cranking. Solution: Inspect and replace the faulty electrical components, such as the alternator, starter, or battery. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
* **Headlight Moisture Buildup:** Symptoms include condensation inside the headlight lenses. Solution: Replace the headlight lenses or install a headlight breather kit. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+ per headlight

**Exterior**

* **Door Lock Actuator Failure:** Symptoms include difficulty locking or unlocking the doors. Solution: Replace the faulty door lock actuators. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+ per door
* **Sunroof Drain Clogging:** Symptoms include water leaking into the interior from the sunroof. Solution: Clean the clogged sunroof drains. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+

**Interior**

* **Power Seat Motor Failure:** Symptoms include the seat not moving or operating erratically. Solution: Replace the faulty seat motor. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+ per seat
* **Air Conditioning Vents Sticking:** Symptoms include difficulty adjusting the air flow from the vents. Solution: Lubricate or replace the faulty vents. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+

**Summary:**

While the 2011 Porsche Cayman offers a thrilling driving experience, it is not immune to its fair share of issues. Some of the more severe problems, such as piston ring failure and bore scoring, can be very expensive to repair. However, many of the other issues, such as oil leaks and transmission bushing wear, are relatively minor and can be fixed for a reasonable cost. Overall, the severity and cost of the issues with the 2011 Porsche Cayman vary widely, and potential buyers should research and consider these potential issues before making a purchase.
