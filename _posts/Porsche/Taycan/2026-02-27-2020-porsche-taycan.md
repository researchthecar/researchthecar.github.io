# 2020 Porsche Taycan Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The Porsche Taycan is a high-performance electric vehicle that delivers sports-car handling and impressive acceleration. However, early production models require careful scrutiny from prospective used-car buyers, particularly concerning complex high-voltage electrical architecture and software systems.

---

## Engine

### Single-Speed Front / Two-Speed Rear Permanent Magnet Synchronous Electric Motors

The Taycan utilizes advanced permanent magnet synchronous electric motors paired with an innovative two-speed rear transmission, delivering robust horsepower and instant torque. While mechanically formidable, the integrated propulsion and power electronics systems on early models have shown susceptibility to software-related disruptions and sudden power loss.

**Loss of Motive Power / Turtle Mode**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle suddenly loses power, drops into limited propulsion mode (turtle mode), or experiences a complete electrical failure while driving.
- **Solution:** Requires diagnostic evaluation by a certified Porsche technician, often involving inverter updates or high-voltage battery module replacements.

**Engine / Propulsion Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** Software glitches cause sudden propulsion interruptions and display error warnings while on the road.
- **Solution:** Perform applicable software updates and check control modules for internal faults.

## Transmission

### 2-Speed Automatic Rear Transmission

The two-speed automatic transmission works in tandem with the rear electric motor to optimize efficiency at cruising speeds and launch performance from a standstill. It generally operates smoothly, with few mechanical complaints reported by owners.

## Brakes

### Porsche Surface Coated Brakes (PSCB)

The regenerative braking system handles a vast majority of daily deceleration, which can occasionally lead to rotor surface wear concerns if mechanical brakes are not utilized regularly.

**Surface Coating Wear and Rotor Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Unusual wear, pitting, or premature degradation of the tungsten carbide coating on brake rotors due to infrequent mechanical brake use.
- **Solution:** Inspect rotor conditions manually and replace pads or rotors if coating failure is detected.

## Electrical

### High-Voltage Battery and Charging System

The complex high-voltage electrical network governs everything from propulsion to auxiliary cabin features. Owners have reported intermittent screen blackouts, charging anomalies, and module-level faults requiring intensive diagnostic and repair procedures.

**Unintended Neutral Shift and Electrical Display Faults**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle displays critical electrical errors, drops into neutral unexpectedly, or blanks out instrument clusters and infotainment screens while driving.
- **Solution:** Inspect high-voltage battery modules, harness connections, and apply dealer-recommended software patches.

## Safety

### Advanced Driver Assistance & Collision Avoidance

Driver-assistance systems provide added security but can occasionally trigger false warnings or unexpected autonomous braking interventions.

**Phantom Braking and ADAS False Triggers**
- **Symptoms:** Automatic emergency braking or lane-keep assistance activates unexpectedly during normal driving, slowing the vehicle down abruptly.
- **Solution:** Recalibrate radar and camera sensors or check for software updates addressing collision avoidance logic.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V697000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Foundation Components](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Porsche&model=Taycan&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** The pressure input rod connection on the brake booster may be loose and could detach. A detached pressure input rod will cause the brake pedal to fail, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will repair or replace the brake booster as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V794000: Exterior Lighting:Hazard Flashing Warning Lights](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Porsche&model=Taycan&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 108, "Lamps, Reflective Devices, and Associated Equipment." An activation of hazard lights in non-emergency situations could confuse other drivers, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will reprogram the hazard lights, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V911000: Suspension:Rear](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Porsche&model=Taycan&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** A loose control arm connection may fail, causing a loss of vehicle stability and increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the screw connections, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V486000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Engine:Engine Control Module (Ecu/Ecm):Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Porsche&model=Taycan&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** A loss of motive power increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will reprogram the power electronics and the engine control unit, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V100000: Seat Belts: Rear/Other:Buckle Assembly](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Porsche&model=Taycan&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 225, "Child Restraint Anchorage Systems." An improperly secured child seat increases the risk of injury during a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will reroute and secure the center rear seat belt buckle wiring harness and, as necessary, replace a damaged buckle, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V278000: Seats](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Porsche&model=Taycan&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** The driver and passenger seat wire harness could become damaged during horizontal seat adjustment. A damaged seat wire harness may deactivate air bags, increasing the risk of injury in a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the seat wire harness for damage, add fabric tape to secure the harness, and repair damaged wires as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V404000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission:Control Module (Tcm/Pcm/Tecm)](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Porsche&model=Taycan&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** A software error could cause the center screen (including rearview camera image) and the touch control panel remain to black directly after the ignition is activated. Also, the Real-Top-View (including rearview camera image) could display a flickering image.
- **What to do:** Dealers will install new software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V347000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Porsche&model=Taycan&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard numbers 135, "Light Vehicle Brake Systems," and 101, "Control and Displays." An incorrect warning indicator may be misinterpreted and result in driving on worn brake pads with reduced brake performance, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the software for the instrument cluster, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

When shopping for a used Porsche Taycan, verify that all open electrical and high-voltage battery campaigns have been completed by an authorized dealer. A comprehensive pre-purchase inspection by a specialist is strongly recommended to confirm optimal battery health and software version status.
