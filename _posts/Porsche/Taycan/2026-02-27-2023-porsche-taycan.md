# 2023 Porsche Taycan Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2023 Porsche Taycan is a high-performance all-electric luxury sedan celebrated for its breathtaking acceleration, precision handling, and cutting-edge EV technology. However, early owner reports submitted to safety agencies highlight certain electronic, braking, and battery-related concerns that prospective buyers should keep in mind. While modern luxury EVs offer exhilarating driving dynamics, verifying software updates and recall completion history is crucial before purchasing.

---

## Engine

### Single Motor / Rear-Wheel Drive

The base rear-wheel drive setup utilizes a permanent magnet synchronous motor delivering entry-level power for the Taycan lineup. This configuration provides brisk acceleration while maintaining balanced everyday efficiency.

### Dual Motor / All-Wheel Drive (4S, Turbo, Turbo S)

High-output dual permanent magnet synchronous motors power the all-wheel-drive 4S, Turbo, and Turbo S trims, producing massive horsepower figures. These advanced electric propulsion units offer blistering performance but rely heavily on complex thermal and electrical management systems.

**Propulsion Disconnection and Charging Smoke**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have reported unexpected motor wire disconnections leading to sudden loss of propulsion, as well as isolated incidents of smoke and burning odors near the charging port during high-voltage sessions.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified Porsche technician inspect high-voltage wiring harnesses, charging inlets, and propulsion software for faults.

## Transmission

### 2-Speed Automatic Transmission

The Taycan utilizes an innovative two-speed automatic transmission on the rear axle—featuring a short-ratio first gear for explosive launch acceleration and a long-ratio second gear for efficient highway cruising. This unique gearbox generally operates smoothly, though prospective buyers should ensure all powertrain control modules are up to date.

### Automatic Door Handles

Exterior convenience features like flush door handles add sleek styling but require functional proximity sensors to prevent pinching.

**Pinch Hazard from Retracting Handles**
- **Symptoms:** Exterior flush automatic door handles can occasionally retract unexpectedly, catching fingers in the mechanism.
- **Solution:** Have the door handle sensors and retraction logic inspected and adjusted by a dealer if sticking occurs.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

Component issues involving brake pedal feel and automated collision braking response have been noted by some owners in stop-and-go traffic.

**Brake Pedal Softness and Reduced Stopping Force**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have experienced instances where the brake pedal travels excessively toward the floor at low speeds, or where collision-avoidance braking fails to apply full stopping force promptly.
- **Solution:** Inspect the brake hydraulic system, brake booster, and regenerative braking software calibration for potential faults.

## Electrical

### High-Voltage Battery and Charging System

Electrical and high-voltage battery management systems require careful verification of service records to ensure all safety bulletins have been addressed.

**High-Voltage Battery Module Concerns**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have received urgent safety recall notices regarding potential battery cell module defects that carry a risk of thermal events or reduced usable charge capacity.
- **Solution:** Verify that all open high-voltage battery recalls and software updates have been fully performed by an authorized dealership.

## Safety

### Forward Collision Avoidance

Advanced safety suites assist with hazard detection, though software recalibration may be necessary if warning responses appear erratic.

**Inconsistent Collision Warning Braking**
- **Symptoms:** When the forward collision warning activates, the braking pedal can occasionally feel unresponsive or slow to engage, increasing stopping distances.
- **Solution:** Check for sensor calibrations and software patches addressing driver-assistance systems.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V176000: Electrical System: Instrument Cluster/Panel](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Porsche&model=Taycan&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 208, "Occupant Crash Protection." A malfunctioning seat belt warning system may not alert the passenger to buckle their seat belt, increasing the risk of injury in the event of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the instrument cluster software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V615000: Electrical System:Propulsion System:Traction Battery](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Porsche&model=Taycan&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** Liquid accumulation in the high voltage battery can cause electrical arcing, increasing the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will test the battery for leakage and replace the battery as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V840000: Electrical System:Propulsion System:Traction Battery](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Porsche&model=Taycan&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** The high-voltage battery may experience a short circuit within the battery modules. A short circuit in the battery increases the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and repair the high-voltage battery by replacing modules as necessary, free of charge Owner notification letters were mailed on June 14, 2024. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V841000: Electrical System:Propulsion System:Charging:Cable/Cord:Onboard](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Porsche&model=Taycan&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** An overheated outlet or charging cable can increase the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will supply a new 220V/240V compact/portable charging cable with an incorporated temperature sensor, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V215000: Electrical System:Propulsion System:Traction Battery](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Porsche&model=Taycan&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** The high-voltage battery may experience a short circuit within the battery modules. A short circuit in the battery increases the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the affected modules in the high-voltage battery, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V217000: Electrical System:Propulsion System:Traction Battery](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Porsche&model=Taycan&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** The high-voltage battery may experience a short circuit within the battery modules. A short circuit in the battery increases the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will analyze the battery data and replace the high-voltage battery modules as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V455000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Foundation Components:Hoses, Lines/Piping, And Fittings](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Porsche&model=Taycan&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** A brake fluid leak can reduce braking performance and extend the distance required to stop, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the front brake hoses, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V731000: Electrical System:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Porsche&model=Taycan&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** The high-voltage battery may experience a short circuit within the battery module. A short circuit in a high-voltage battery increases the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the affected modules in the high-voltage battery, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

Purchasing a used 2023 Porsche Taycan offers world-class electric performance and luxury, but checking vehicle history is essential. Buyers should prioritize models with verified dealer service records confirming that all high-voltage battery recalls and software campaigns have been completed. A comprehensive pre-purchase inspection focusing on the electrical architecture, braking systems, and powertrain modules will help ensure a reliable ownership experience.
