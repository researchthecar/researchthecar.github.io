# 2021 Porsche Taycan Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The Porsche Taycan is a high-performance all-electric luxury vehicle praised for its exceptional driving dynamics, premium interior, and rapid charging capabilities. As an early model year for Porsche's EV lineup, the 2021 Taycan has garnered owner attention regarding electrical system concerns, battery module charging hiccups, and intermittent loss of motive power. Buyers should carefully review service histories and ensure all relevant manufacturer campaigns have been performed.

---

## Engine

### Single Motor Permanent Magnet Synchronous (Rear-Wheel Drive)

The base rear-wheel-drive powertrain utilizes a single permanent magnet synchronous motor, delivering robust acceleration and smooth power delivery typical of luxury electric vehicles.

### Dual Motor Permanent Magnet Synchronous (All-Wheel Drive)

The dual-motor setup powers all four wheels across variants like the 4S, Turbo, and Turbo S, offering immense horsepower and quick torque vectoring with solid long-term mechanical reliability.

**Loss of Motive Power**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers report that the vehicle may suddenly display an "Engine Control Error" or lose motive power entirely while driving, requiring the vehicle to be safely pulled over and restarted.
- **Solution:** Requires diagnostic evaluation by an authorized Porsche service center to identify software glitches or hardware faults in the motor control modules.

### Climate Control and Defrosting Unit

Responsible for cabin climate regulation and window defogging, requiring proper functioning of electric heating elements to maintain clear visibility in cold weather.

**Cabin Heater Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The cabin heating and front windshield defrosting unit may fail during cold ambient temperatures, causing severe window fogging or ice buildup and obstructing visibility.
- **Solution:** Dealer service centers must inspect the heating unit, coolant valves, or associated climate control modules for component failure.

## Transmission

### 2-Speed Automatic Transmission

The innovative two-speed automatic transmission features a short-ratio first gear for rapid launch acceleration and a long-ratio second gear for high-speed efficiency, operating seamlessly in daily driving.

## Brakes

### Regenerative and Hydraulic Brake System

Combines regenerative braking with traditional hydraulic disc brakes to maximize energy recovery while maintaining robust stopping power.

**Brake Transition and Regeneration Feel**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers note unusual braking behavior or extended perceived stopping distances in wet conditions when transitioning between regenerative braking and mechanical disc brakes.
- **Solution:** Inspect brake pads, rotors, and software calibration during routine service to ensure smooth transitions between regenerative and friction braking.

## Electrical

### Battery and Charging System

Manages high-voltage power distribution and charging; while generally advanced, early models occasionally experience battery module anomalies and charging communication errors.

**High-Voltage Battery Charging Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle's high-voltage battery module may become defective, failing to initiate or complete standard charging cycles and refusing to hold a charge.
- **Solution:** Have the high-voltage battery modules inspected and potentially replaced under warranty or manufacturer service campaigns by a certified technician.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V157000: Suspension](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Porsche&model=Taycan&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** Certain suspension components may not have been tightened correctly and can loosen. Loose suspension components may detach, causing a sudden loss of vehicle stability and control, and increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the affected suspension nuts and bolts. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V608000: Seat Belts:Rear/Other:Retractor](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Porsche&model=Taycan&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 208, "Occupant Crash Protection." An unsecured child restraint system can increase the risk of injury during a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the seat belt assemblies, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V697000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Foundation Components](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Porsche&model=Taycan&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The pressure input rod connection on the brake booster may be loose and could detach. A detached pressure input rod will cause the brake pedal to fail, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will repair or replace the brake booster as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V362000: Suspension:Front](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Porsche&model=Taycan&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** A broken front lower trailing arm may cause a loss of vehicle control, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the front lower trailing arms, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V794000: Exterior Lighting:Hazard Flashing Warning Lights](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Porsche&model=Taycan&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 108, "Lamps, Reflective Devices, and Associated Equipment." An activation of hazard lights in non-emergency situations could confuse other drivers, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will reprogram the hazard lights, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V486000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Engine:Engine Control Module (Ecu/Ecm):Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Porsche&model=Taycan&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** A loss of motive power increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will reprogram the power electronics and the engine control unit, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V052000: Suspension](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Porsche&model=Taycan&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** Suspension misalignment may cause premature or uneven tire wear, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the front and rear axles, adjust the alignment as necessary, and replace any prematurely or unevenly worn tires, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V100000: Seat Belts: Rear/Other:Buckle Assembly](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Porsche&model=Taycan&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 225, "Child Restraint Anchorage Systems." An improperly secured child seat increases the risk of injury during a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will reroute and secure the center rear seat belt buckle wiring harness and, as necessary, replace a damaged buckle, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2021 Porsche Taycan is a masterclass in electric performance, but its complexity as an early-generation EV means buyers should proceed with due diligence. Prospective owners should verify that all open recalls and service campaigns—particularly regarding electrical systems and battery modules—have been addressed. A comprehensive pre-purchase inspection by a qualified Porsche specialist is strongly recommended to ensure all comfort and propulsion systems are operating correctly.
