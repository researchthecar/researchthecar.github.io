# 2022 Porsche Taycan Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2022 Porsche Taycan delivers thrilling electric performance and everyday usability, but prospective buyers should approach with careful inspection. As a high-tech electric vehicle, owners have reported complex electrical system errors and drivetrain quirks. While its performance remains world-class, managing potential software glitches and high-voltage component repairs requires a reliable dealership service history.

---

## Engine

### Single Motor Electric Powertrain

The rear-wheel-drive base setup utilizes a permanent magnet synchronous motor producing up to 402 horsepower with the Performance Battery Plus, offering smooth and efficient electric propulsion.

## Transmission

### Dual Motor Electric Powertrain

The dual-motor all-wheel-drive configurations—found on 4S, Turbo, and Turbo S models—churn out immense horsepower ranging from 522 up to 750 hp, delivering breathtaking acceleration alongside complex thermal and electrical demands.

**Electrical System Error and Propulsion Loss**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may experience sudden electrical system error warnings on the dashboard, leading to limp mode or sudden loss of propulsion while driving.
- **Solution:** Requires diagnostic scanning by a certified Porsche technician to update software, check battery health modules, or replace failing high-voltage components.

### 2-Speed Automatic Transmission

The Taycan utilizes an innovative two-speed automatic transmission on the rear axle designed to optimize both launch acceleration and highway efficiency, though rare electronic shifting faults have been noted by owners.

**Unintended Neutral Shift**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may randomly shift into neutral or park on its own while operating in drive or reverse without prior warning.
- **Solution:** Dealership inspection and potential software updates for the electronic gear selector and transmission control unit.

**Sudden Deceleration and Drivetrain Clunk**
- **Symptoms:** Unusual mechanical clunking sounds from the rear followed by sudden deceleration or a drop in vehicle speed while traveling at highway velocities.
- **Solution:** Requires professional inspection of the dual-speed rear gearbox and related electronic actuator components.

## Suspension

### Wheels and Tires

The adaptive air suspension and performance geometry provide exceptional handling dynamics, though the vehicle's heavy weight and alignment profile can consume rear tires quickly.

**Accelerated Inner Tire Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Rear tires experience rapid and uneven wear on the inner tread edges, potentially leading to premature tire failure.
- **Solution:** Regular wheel alignment checks and tire rotation intervals tailored to aggressive rear-wheel alignment specifications.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

The regenerative braking setup blends traditional friction brakes with electric motor resistance, providing strong stopping power that requires precise calibration during routine service.

**Soft Pedal Feel and Electronic Brake Integration**
- **Symptoms:** Brakes may feel excessively soft at low speeds, occasionally requiring the driver to pump the pedal or experiencing delayed deceleration when paired with electrical system faults.
- **Solution:** Have the brake fluid, regenerative braking system, and master cylinder inspected by a specialized technician.

## Interior

### Climate Control and Defrosting

The climate control architecture relies heavily on high-voltage electric heating elements to warm the passenger cabin efficiently without a traditional combustion engine.

**Cabin Heater Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of cabin heat during cold weather, leading to heavily fogged or frosted windows and compromised visibility.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the faulty high-voltage electric in-line cabin heater unit by an authorized dealer.

## Safety

### Mobile Charger and Charging System

The high-voltage charging equipment and onboard power electronics manage rapid replenishment of the battery pack, but early mobile charge cables have faced scrutiny for safety modifications.

**Mobile Charger Overheating or Electric Shock**
- **Symptoms:** Issues involving the mobile charging cable, including potential electric shock risks or hardware failure prompting safety recall notices.
- **Solution:** Verify that safety recalls regarding power cables and mobile chargers have been completed with updated hardware replacements from Porsche.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V404000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission:Control Module (Tcm/Pcm/Tecm)](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Porsche&model=Taycan&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** A software error could cause the center screen (including rearview camera image) and the touch control panel remain to black directly after the ignition is activated. Also, the Real-Top-View (including rearview camera image) could display a flickering image.
- **What to do:** Dealers will install new software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V176000: Electrical System: Instrument Cluster/Panel](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Porsche&model=Taycan&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 208, "Occupant Crash Protection." A malfunctioning seat belt warning system may not alert the passenger to buckle their seat belt, increasing the risk of injury in the event of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the instrument cluster software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V347000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Porsche&model=Taycan&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard numbers 135, "Light Vehicle Brake Systems," and 101, "Control and Displays." An incorrect warning indicator may be misinterpreted and result in driving on worn brake pads with reduced brake performance, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the software for the instrument cluster, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V840000: Electrical System:Propulsion System:Traction Battery](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Porsche&model=Taycan&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** The high-voltage battery may experience a short circuit within the battery modules. A short circuit in the battery increases the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and repair the high-voltage battery by replacing modules as necessary, free of charge Owner notification letters were mailed on June 14, 2024. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V841000: Electrical System:Propulsion System:Charging:Cable/Cord:Onboard](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Porsche&model=Taycan&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** An overheated outlet or charging cable can increase the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will supply a new 220V/240V compact/portable charging cable with an incorporated temperature sensor, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V215000: Electrical System:Propulsion System:Traction Battery](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Porsche&model=Taycan&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** The high-voltage battery may experience a short circuit within the battery modules. A short circuit in the battery increases the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the affected modules in the high-voltage battery, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V217000: Electrical System:Propulsion System:Traction Battery](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Porsche&model=Taycan&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** The high-voltage battery may experience a short circuit within the battery modules. A short circuit in the battery increases the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will analyze the battery data and replace the high-voltage battery modules as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V455000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Foundation Components:Hoses, Lines/Piping, And Fittings](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Porsche&model=Taycan&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** A brake fluid leak can reduce braking performance and extend the distance required to stop, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the front brake hoses, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2022 Porsche Taycan is an engineering masterpiece that brings sports-car handling to the electric vehicle market, but used buyers should proceed with eyes wide open. While most mechanical aspects are robust, electronic issues, occasional transmission shifting anomalies, and cabin heater failures require thorough vetting. Always request a comprehensive pre-purchase inspection and verify that all manufacturer service campaigns and safety recalls have been fully resolved.
