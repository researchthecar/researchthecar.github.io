**1993 Porsche 968**

The 1993 Porsche 968 is a sports car that was produced from 1992 to 1995. It is powered by a 3.0-liter naturally aspirated flat-6 engine that produces 240 horsepower and 225 lb-ft of torque. The 968 is available with a 6-speed manual transmission or a 4-speed automatic transmission. The 968 was praised for its handling, performance, and styling, but it was also criticized for its high price and lack of creature comforts.

**Engine**

* **3.0-Liter Flat-6**

  * **Timing Belt Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The timing belt can fail, causing the engine to lose power and stall.
    * Solution: Replace the timing belt.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800
    * Recalls: None Found
  * **Oil Leaks**
    * Description/Symptoms: The engine can develop oil leaks from the valve covers, oil pan, and other gaskets.
    * Solution: Replace the leaking gaskets.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

* **6-Speed Manual**

  * **Clutch Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The clutch can wear out and slip, making it difficult to shift gears.
    * Solution: Replace the clutch.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $600 - $1,200
    * Recalls: None Found
* **4-Speed Automatic**

  * **Transmission Slipping**
    * Description/Symptoms: The transmission may slip when shifting gears.
    * Solution: Rebuild or replace the transmission.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Weatherstripping Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The weatherstripping around the windows and doors can deteriorate and allow water to leak into the car.
    * Solution: Replace the weatherstripping.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400

**Interior**

    * Description/Symptoms: The dashboard can crack due to sun exposure.
    * Solution: Replace the dashboard.

    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 1993 Porsche 968 is a reliable and well-rounded sports car, but it does have some potential issues to be aware of. The most serious issue is the timing belt failure, which can cause catastrophic engine damage. Other common issues include oil leaks, clutch failure, and transmission slipping. The cost to fix these issues can vary depending on the severity of the problem, but they are generally not prohibitively expensive. Overall, the 968 is a great choice for enthusiasts who want a classic Porsche with modern performance and handling.
