The 2014 Porsche 911, a timeless sports car, offers a thrilling driving experience. It's available in various trims, including the Carrera, Carrera S, Turbo, and GT3. The base Carrera features a 3.4L flat-six engine producing 350 horsepower and 287 lb-ft of torque. The Carrera S boasts a 3.8L flat-six with 400 horsepower and 325 lb-ft of torque. Transmission choices include a 7-speed manual and a 7-speed PDK dual-clutch automatic. Owners praise the 911's precise handling, powerful engines, and iconic design. Common complaints include high maintenance costs and occasional electronic glitches.

**Engine**

**3.4L Flat-Six (Carrera)**
*   **Oil Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks can develop from various seals and gaskets, leading to oil consumption and potential engine damage if left unattended.
    *   Solution: Replacing the leaking seals and gaskets.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+

*   **Water Pump Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Overheating, coolant leaks, and potential engine damage.
    * Solution: Replacing the water pump.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,500+

**3.8L Flat-Six (Carrera S)**
*   **Oil Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Similar to the 3.4L, oil leaks can originate from seals, gaskets, and even the RMS.
    *   Solution: Replacing the leaking components.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+

*   **Scoring on Cylinder Walls**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Bore scoring due to piston slap. Symptoms include engine ticking, increased oil consumption, and reduced performance.
    *   Solution: Engine rebuild or replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $10,000+

**Transmission**

**7-Speed PDK Dual-Clutch Automatic**
*   **PDK Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rough shifting, delayed engagement, or complete transmission failure. Error messages related to the PDK system may appear.
    *   Solution: Repair or replacement of the PDK unit.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $5,000 - $15,000+

**Suspension**

*   **PASM (Porsche Active Suspension Management) Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Warning lights on the dash, uneven ride height, and a noticeable degradation in ride quality and handling. Failure of the shock absorbers or control module.
    *   Solution: Replacing the faulty shock absorbers or control module.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $4,000+ (depending on the extent of the damage and parts needed)

**Electrical**

*   **PCM (Porsche Communication Management) Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Freezing, rebooting, or complete failure of the infotainment system. Navigation, audio, and other features may be affected.
    *   Solution: Software update or replacement of the PCM unit.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $3,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Battery Drain**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The battery drains quickly, especially when the car is not driven for extended periods.
    *   Solution: Diagnose parasitic drain and replace faulty components.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+

**Exterior**

*   **Convertible Top Malfunctions (Cabriolet Models)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The convertible top may become stuck, operate slowly, or fail to seal properly, leading to wind noise and water leaks.
    *   Solution: Repair or replacement of the convertible top mechanism or hydraulic components.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $3,000+

**Interior**

*   **Sunroof Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Sunroof may not open or close properly, or may get stuck.
    *   Solution: Repair or replacement of the sunroof mechanism.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+

**Summary**

The 2014 Porsche 911 is a desirable sports car. Owners should be aware of potential issues like oil leaks in both the 3.4L and 3.8L engines, PDK transmission problems, and electrical glitches within the PCM. Suspension issues with the PASM system can also arise. Repair costs can range from a few hundred dollars for minor fixes to several thousand for more complex problems. Cylinder scoring on the 3.8L is a more serious and costly issue. Regular maintenance and inspections are crucial for preventing and addressing these issues early on.
