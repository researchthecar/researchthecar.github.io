# 2022 Porsche 911 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used Porsche 911, buyers are generally looking at high-performance engineering combined with everyday usability. While these sports cars are built to rigorous standards, owner feedback highlights a few specific areas worth inspecting prior to purchase, particularly concerning fuel delivery lines and intermittent auxiliary systems.

---

## Engine

### 3.0L Twin-Turbocharged Flat-6

This twin-turbocharged flat-6 engine powers standard Carrera models, offering robust horsepower and torque delivery. Proper maintenance and routine inspection of engine bay lines are recommended for long-term reliability.

**Fuel Line Leak**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may notice a strong gasoline smell around the vehicle, and physical inspection can reveal fuel pooling or leaking from hard lines on top of the engine near hot components.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified technician inspect the fuel system fittings and replace faulty hard fuel lines as necessary.

### 4.0L Naturally Aspirated Flat-6

Found in higher-performance GT variants, this naturally aspirated flat-6 delivers track-ready power and a high-revving character. It remains a robust option when properly maintained.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Dual-Clutch Transmission (PDK)

The 8-speed PDK offers lightning-fast gear changes and smooth power delivery in both daily driving and spirited conditions. It is generally considered a highly dependable transmission architecture.

### 7-Speed Manual Transmission

The 7-speed manual option caters to purists seeking a traditional three-pedal driving experience. Routine clutch maintenance is the primary owner consideration.

## Suspension

### Front Suspension

The precision suspension setup provides sharp handling response, though high-stress components should be thoroughly checked during a pre-purchase inspection.

**Control Arm Failure**
- **Symptoms:** An unexpected snap or structural failure in the front control arm during low-speed maneuvers, potentially accompanied by smoke or steering interference.
- **Solution:** Inspect front suspension control arms and bushings for signs of wear or structural fatigue, and replace damaged components immediately.

## Electrical

### Surround View Camera System

Advanced electronics enhance maneuverability and safety, though complex wiring and camera modules can occasionally exhibit intermittent software or hardware glitches.

**Intermittent Camera Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The backup, front, and side surround-view cameras may randomly stop working or function only part of the time.
- **Solution:** Check camera module connections and software updates, or replace faulty camera hardware if issues persist.

### Tail Light Assemblies

Exterior lighting fixtures combine modern LED styling with aerodynamic integration, requiring checks for seal integrity and housing cracks.

**Internal Stress Cracks and Moisture**
- **Symptoms:** Internal stress cracks develop within the rear light housings, leading to visible condensation, moisture accumulation, and dust buildup.
- **Solution:** Replace the affected tail light assembly if moisture intrusion compromises visibility or electrical function.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V897000: Structure:Interior Panels:Dashboard](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Porsche&model=911&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** In the event of a crash with passenger air bag deployment, the dashboard console may break, causing the air bag to deploy improperly. An improper air bag deployment increases the risk of injury in a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the dashboard, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V155000: Visibility:Windshield](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Porsche&model=911&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** The front windshield and rear window may not be properly secured and can detach. During air bag deployment, an unsecured windshield may not support the front air bags as intended, increasing the risk of injury in a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the windshield and rear window as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V809000: Wheels:Lugs/Nuts/Bolts/Studs](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Porsche&model=911&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** Wheel detachment can result in a loss of vehicle control, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will check the center lock wheel fastening system and replace any incorrectly manufactured parts, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V896000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Porsche&model=911&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** The rearview camera image may not display when the vehicle is placed in reverse. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 111, "Rear Visibility." A rearview image that does not display properly reduces the driver's view behind the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the driver assistance software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.
