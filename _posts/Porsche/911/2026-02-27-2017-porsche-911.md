# 2017 Porsche 911 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2017 Porsche 911 represents a high-performance benchmark, blending everyday usability with world-class engineering. While generally praised for its robust build quality and exhilarating driving dynamics, prospective buyers should still carefully review vehicle history and conduct a thorough pre-purchase inspection to catch any lingering mechanical or electrical quirks.

---

## Engine

### 3.0L Twin-Turbocharged Flat-6

This 3.0-liter twin-turbocharged flat-six engine powers Carrera and Carrera S models, producing roughly 370 to 420 horsepower. It offers strong low-end torque and impressive everyday efficiency, though buyers should ensure consistent maintenance history with high-performance fluids.

### 3.0L Twin-Turbocharged Flat-6 (GTS)

Found in GTS variants, this upgraded 3.0-liter twin-turbo flat-six pushes output to 450 horsepower. It delivers blistering performance while maintaining the core durability of the modern 911 turbocharged engine family.

### 3.0L Twin-Turbocharged Flat-6 (Turbo/Turbo S)

Powering the Turbo and Turbo S trims, this high-output 3.0-liter twin-turbo flat-six generates up to 580 horsepower. It pairs immense power delivery with sophisticated cooling systems, though complex turbo plumbing requires diligent upkeep.

## Transmission

### 7-Speed Manual Transmission

This traditional 7-speed manual gearbox offers an engaging driving experience with a dedicated seventh gear for highway cruising. It is a favored choice for purists seeking mechanical connection.

### 7-Speed Doppelkupplung (PDK) Dual-Clutch Automatic

Porsche's lightning-fast 7-speed PDK dual-clutch automatic transmission delivers seamless gear changes and exceptional track performance. Routine fluid changes are vital to maintain its crisp shift quality over high mileage.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V533000: Air Bags: Air Bag/Restraint Control Module](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Porsche&model=911&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** The air bag Electronic Control Unit (ECU) may have a defective power supply capacitor that can result in air bag deactivation or inadvertent deployment of the air bags. Deactivated air bags increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Porsche will notify owners, and dealers will install new software and replace the air bag ECU as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V946000: Air Bags:Side/Window](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Porsche&model=911&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** The seat mounted side air bag inflator initiator may fail to ignite during a crash. If the air bag inflator initiator fails to ignite, the side air bag will not deploy, increasing the risk of injury in the event of a crash.
- **What to do:** Porsche will notify owners, and dealers will replace the affected air bag modules, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V135000: Visibility:Windshield](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Porsche&model=911&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** During manufacturing, the windshield may not have been properly bonded to the vehicle, allowing it to detach in a crash. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 212, "Windshield Mounting." The 2017 Porsche 911 remains a remarkably solid and dependable high-performance sports car option for used car buyers.
