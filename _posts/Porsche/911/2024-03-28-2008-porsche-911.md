**Introduction:**

The 2008 Porsche 911 is a high-performance sports car that is known for its handling, speed, and luxury. It is available with a variety of engine and transmission options, including:

* **Engine:**
    * 3.6-liter flat-six with 325 horsepower and 273 lb-ft of torque
    * 3.8-liter flat-six with 355 horsepower and 295 lb-ft of torque
    * 3.8-liter flat-six with 385 horsepower and 309 lb-ft of torque
* **Transmission:**
    * 6-speed manual
    * 6-speed automatic with Tiptronic
    * 7-speed automatic with Doppelkupplung (PDK)

People who like the 2008 Porsche 911 praise its performance, handling, and style. However, some people have reported issues with the vehicle, including:

**Engine**

**3.6-liter flat-six with 325 horsepower and 273 lb-ft of torque**
* **Failed camshaft adjuster:** The camshaft adjuster can fail, which can cause the engine to run rough and lose power. The issue is caused by a faulty design of the adjuster. The solution is to replace the camshaft adjuster. Estimated cost to fix: $1,500 - $2,500+.

**3.8-liter flat-six with 355 horsepower and 295 lb-ft of torque**
* **Valve seat recession:** The valve seats can recess, which can cause the engine to lose compression and power. The issue is caused by a faulty design of the valve seats. The solution is to replace the valve seats. Estimated cost to fix: $3,000 - $5,000+.

**Transmission**

**6-speed manual**

* **Grinding gears:** The transmission can grind gears when shifting, which can be caused by worn synchronizers. The solution is to replace the synchronizers. Estimated cost to fix: $1,000 - $2,000+.

**6-speed automatic with Tiptronic**

* **Slipping gears:** The transmission can slip gears when shifting, which can be caused by worn clutches. The solution is to replace the clutches. Estimated cost to fix: $2,000 - $3,000+.

**Suspension**

* **Worn control arms:** The control arms can wear out, which can cause the suspension to become loose and unstable. The solution is to replace the control arms. Estimated cost to fix: $500 - $1,000+.

**Electrical**

* **Faulty ignition coils:** The ignition coils can fail, which can cause the engine to misfire and run rough. The solution is to replace the ignition coils. Estimated cost to fix: $500 - $1,000+.

**Exterior**

* **Cracked headlights:** The headlights can crack, which can allow moisture to enter the housing and damage the electrical components. The solution is to replace the headlights. Estimated cost to fix: $500 - $1,000+.

**Interior**

* **Broken door handles:** The door handles can break, which can make it difficult to open the doors. The solution is to replace the door handles. Estimated cost to fix: $200 - $500+.
* **Faulty seat controls:** The seat controls can fail, which can make it difficult to adjust the seats. The solution is to replace the seat controls. Estimated cost to fix: $500 - $1,000+.

**Summary:**

The 2008 Porsche 911 is a high-performance sports car that is known for its handling, speed, and luxury. However, some people have reported issues with the vehicle, including engine problems, transmission problems, suspension problems, electrical problems, exterior problems, and interior problems. The severity of the issues varies, and the cost to fix them can be significant.
