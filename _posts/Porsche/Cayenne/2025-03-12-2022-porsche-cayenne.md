The 2022 Porsche Cayenne is a luxury SUV known for its performance and versatility. Available engine options include a turbocharged 3.0-liter V6 producing 335 horsepower and 332 lb-ft of torque, a twin-turbocharged 2.9-liter V6 with 434 horsepower and 405 lb-ft of torque (Cayenne S), a twin-turbocharged 4.0-liter V8 with 453 horsepower and 453 lb-ft of torque (Cayenne GTS), and a twin-turbocharged 4.0-liter V8 with 541 horsepower and 567 lb-ft of torque (Cayenne Turbo). Additionally, there are E-Hybrid models combining a gasoline engine with an electric motor. All models typically come with an 8-speed automatic transmission. Owners often praise the Cayenne for its engaging driving dynamics, luxurious interior, and comfortable ride. Some criticisms include the high price point, especially when adding options, and the complex infotainment system.

**Engine**

**3.0L Turbocharged V6**

*   **Coolant Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaks are detected near the water pump or thermostat housing, leading to low coolant levels, overheating, and potential engine damage.
    *   Solution: Replace the water pump and/or thermostat housing, including new gaskets and seals.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Twin-Turbocharged 4.0-liter V8**

*   **Oil Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks occur around the turbocharger oil lines or valve cover gaskets. Symptoms include visible oil leaks, burning oil smell, and low oil levels.
    *   Solution: Replace the leaking oil lines and/or valve cover gaskets.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1200+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**8-Speed Automatic Transmission (All Models)**

*   **Harsh Shifting**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Transmission exhibits harsh or jerky shifting, particularly between lower gears. This may be more pronounced when cold.
    *   Solution: In some cases, a software update can improve shifting smoothness. More severe cases may require valve body replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 (software update) - $2500+ (valve body replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Delayed Engagement**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Delay when shifting into drive or reverse.
    *   Solution: Replacement of transmission.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $8,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Air Suspension Failure (If Equipped)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Air suspension system malfunctions, leading to the vehicle sitting unevenly or failing to maintain the correct ride height. The air compressor might be noisy or fail completely.
    *   Solution: Diagnose and replace the faulty components, such as air struts, air compressor, or height sensors.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1000 - $3000+ per strut, $800 - $1500+ for compressor.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Freezing/Crashing**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The infotainment system freezes, crashes, or becomes unresponsive. This can affect navigation, audio, and other vehicle functions.
    *   Solution: Software updates are often released to address these issues. In some cases, the infotainment unit may need to be replaced.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 (software update) - $1500+ (unit replacement).
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Battery Drain**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The car's battery drains quickly, especially when the vehicle is not in use. This can be caused by a parasitic drain from an electrical component.
    *   Solution: Diagnose and repair the source of the parasitic drain. This may involve replacing a faulty module or repairing wiring.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Sunroof Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Sunroof rattles, fails to open or close properly, or leaks water.
    *   Solution: Repair or replace sunroof components such as tracks, seals, or the motor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **No Issues Found**

**Summary**

The 2022 Porsche Cayenne, while a desirable luxury SUV, can experience several issues. Engine oil and coolant leaks as well as transmission harsh shifting/delayed engagement are significant concerns that can be costly to repair. Air suspension failures and electrical glitches with the infotainment system or battery drain can also add to the ownership expenses. The severity of these issues ranges from minor inconveniences requiring software updates to major repairs involving engine components or suspension systems. Potential buyers should be aware of these potential problems and thoroughly inspect the vehicle before purchase, paying particular attention to the service history and any signs of the reported issues.
