**Introduction:**

The 2017 Porsche Cayenne is a luxury SUV that combines performance and practicality. It is available with a range of engine and transmission options, including:

* 3.6L V6 engine (300 horsepower, 295 lb-ft torque) with an 8-speed Tiptronic S transmission
* 3.0L V6 turbocharged engine (340 horsepower, 332 lb-ft torque) with an 8-speed Tiptronic S transmission
* 4.8L V8 engine (440 horsepower, 406 lb-ft torque) with an 8-speed Tiptronic S transmission
* 3.0L V6 turbocharged diesel engine (240 horsepower, 406 lb-ft torque) with an 8-speed Tiptronic S transmission

The Cayenne is praised for its powerful engines, responsive handling, and luxurious interior. However, it has also faced some criticisms regarding its high price, limited rear seat space, and occasional engine issues.

**Engine**

**3.6L V6**
* **Intake manifold failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Cracking of the intake manifold can lead to coolant or oil leaks, overheating, and engine damage.
    * Solution: Replacement of the intake manifold.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**3.0L V6 Turbo**
* **Turbocharger failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Failure of the turbocharger can cause reduced engine power, black smoke from the exhaust, and a decrease in fuel efficiency.
    * Solution: Replacement of the turbocharger.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $3,000 - $5,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**4.8L V8**
* **No Issues Found**

**3.0L V6 Turbo Diesel**
* **Diesel particulate filter (DPF) clogging:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Clogging of the DPF can lead to reduced engine power, increased fuel consumption, and a burning smell.
    * Solution: Cleaning or replacement of the DPF.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**8-Speed Tiptronic S**
* **Transmission control module (TCM) failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Failure of the TCM can cause the transmission to slip out of gear, shift incorrectly, or remain stuck in one gear.
    * Solution: Replacement of the TCM.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Air suspension failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Failure of the air suspension can cause the vehicle to sag or lean to one side, and can also affect ride quality.
    * Solution: Replacement of the air springs or compressor.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+ per corner
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Battery drain:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Rapid depletion of the battery can occur due to a faulty alternator or electrical component.
    * Solution: Diagnosis and repair of the faulty component.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the component
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Sunroof seal failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Failure of the sunroof seal can lead to water leaks into the cabin.
    * Solution: Replacement of the sunroof seal.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Center console trim peeling:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The trim around the center console can peel off or become damaged.
    * Solution: Replacement of the trim panel.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

The 2017 Porsche Cayenne has received mixed reviews regarding reliability. While it offers exceptional performance and luxury, it has also faced some significant issues that can be costly to repair. These issues include intake manifold failure, turbocharger failure, TCM failure, and air suspension failure. It is important for potential buyers to be aware of these issues before purchasing a Cayenne and to factor in the potential cost of repairs.
