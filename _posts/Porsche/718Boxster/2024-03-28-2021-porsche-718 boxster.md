**Introduction**

The 2021 Porsche 718 Boxster is a two-seat convertible sports car that offers a thrilling driving experience with its responsive handling and powerful engines. It is available with two turbocharged flat-four engine options: a 2.0-liter engine producing 300 horsepower and 280 lb-ft of torque, and a 2.5-liter engine producing 350 horsepower and 309 lb-ft of torque. The Boxster also offers a choice of a six-speed manual transmission or a seven-speed dual-clutch automatic transmission (PDK).

While the 718 Boxster has received rave reviews for its performance, there have been a few issues reported by owners. These include:

**Engine**

**2.0-liter Engine**
* **Oil leaks:** Some owners have experienced oil leaks from the engine, particularly from the oil filter housing. Symptoms may include a burning oil smell, oil stains under the car, or low oil levels. The solution typically involves replacing the oil filter housing gasket or other leaking components. Estimated cost to fix: $200 - $500+
* **Timing chain rattle:** A small number of owners have reported a rattling noise from the engine, particularly during cold starts. The issue is believed to be caused by a faulty timing chain tensioner. The solution involves replacing the timing chain tensioner and related components. Estimated cost to fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
* **Throttle body issues:** Some owners have experienced rough idling or stalling due to faulty throttle body. The solution typically involves cleaning or replacing the throttle body. Estimated cost to fix: $200 - $500+

**2.5-liter Engine**
* **No major issues reported**

**Transmission**

**Six-Speed Manual Transmission**
* **No major issues reported**

**Seven-Speed Dual-Clutch Automatic Transmission (PDK)**
* **Jerky shifting:** Some owners have reported jerky or rough shifting, particularly during downshifts. The issue is often caused by faulty software or a need for transmission fluid replacement. The solution typically involves updating the transmission software or replacing the transmission fluid. Estimated cost to fix: $200 - $500+
* **Clutch shudder:** Some owners have experienced clutch shudder or vibrations when starting from a stop or shifting gears. The issue is often caused by a faulty clutch or flywheel. The solution involves replacing the clutch or flywheel. Estimated cost to fix: $1,000 - $2,000+

* **No major issues reported**

**Electrical**

* **Electrical gremlins:** Some owners have experienced various electrical issues, such as flickering lights, malfunctioning gauges, or battery drain. The cause of the issues can vary and may require extensive troubleshooting. Estimated cost to fix: Varies depending on the specific issue

**Exterior**

* **Convertible top issues:** Some owners have reported issues with the operation of the convertible top, such as difficulty opening or closing, or water leaks. The solution may involve adjusting or replacing components of the top mechanism. Estimated cost to fix: $500 - $1,000+

**Interior**

* **Navigation system issues:** Some owners have experienced bugs or glitches with the infotainment system, particularly the navigation system. The solution typically involves updating the software or replacing the navigation system. Estimated cost to fix: $200 - $500+
* **Squeaky seats:** Some owners have reported squeaky or rattling seats. The issue is often caused by loose bolts or fittings. The solution typically involves tightening or replacing the loose components. Estimated cost to fix: $100 - $200+

**Summary**

Overall, the 2021 Porsche 718 Boxster has received positive reviews for its performance and handling. However, there have been a few issues reported by owners, particularly with the engine and transmission. While some of these issues can be minor and relatively inexpensive to fix, others can be more severe and require costly repairs. It is important for prospective buyers to be aware of these potential issues before purchasing a 718 Boxster.
