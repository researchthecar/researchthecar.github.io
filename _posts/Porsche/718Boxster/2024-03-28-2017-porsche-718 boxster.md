**Introduction:**

The 2017 Porsche 718 Boxster is a mid-engine sports car available in three variants: the base Boxster, the S, and the GTS. The base Boxster uses a 2.0-liter turbocharged flat-4 engine that produces 300 horsepower and 280 lb-ft of torque. The S model utilizes a more potent 2.5-liter turbocharged flat-4 engine that delivers 350 horsepower and 309 lb-ft of torque. Both the base and S models are available with either a 6-speed manual or a 7-speed dual-clutch automatic transmission (PDK). The GTS trim receives a 2.5-liter flat-4 engine producing 365 horsepower and 309 lb-ft of torque, paired exclusively with a 6-speed manual transmission. Enthusiasts appreciate the 718 Boxster's responsive handling, powerful engines, and stylish design; however, some owners have reported issues with various components.

**Engine**
- 2.0-liter Turbocharged Flat-4 Engine (300 hp)
  - **Intake Valve Deposits:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Carbon buildup on intake valves can cause reduced engine power, rough idling, and increased fuel consumption.
    - Solution: Intake valve cleaning
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+
- 2.5-liter Turbocharged Flat-4 Engine (350 hp)
  - **Turbocharger Failure:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Turbocharger failure can lead to loss of boost pressure, reduced engine performance, and potential engine damage.
    - Solution: Turbocharger replacement
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+
- 2.5-liter Flat-4 Engine (365 hp)
  - No Issues Found

**Transmission**
- 6-Speed Manual Transmission
  - **Gear Shift Linkage Issues:**
    - Description/Symptoms: The gear shift linkage can become loose or misaligned, causing difficulty shifting gears.
    - Solution: Gear shift linkage adjustment or replacement
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300
- 7-Speed Dual-Clutch Automatic Transmission (PDK)
  - **Mechatronic Unit Failure:**
    - Description/Symptoms: The mechatronic unit, which controls the transmission, can malfunction, leading to transmission problems such as gear slippage or inability to shift.
    - Solution: Mechatronic unit replacement
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,500 - $4,000+
  - **Clutch Pack Wear:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Excessive clutch wear can occur over time, resulting in slipping or shuddering during gear changes.
    - Solution: Clutch pack replacement
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+

**Suspension**
- **No Issues Found**

**Electrical**
- **Battery Drain:**
  - Description/Symptoms: The battery may drain prematurely due to a faulty electrical component or a parasitic drain.
  - Solution: Electrical system diagnosis and repair
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
- **Infotainment System Issues:**
  - Description/Symptoms: The infotainment system may experience software bugs, glitches, or connectivity problems.
  - Solution: Software updates or component replacement
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 - $1,000+

**Exterior**
- **No Issues Found**

**Interior**
- **Roof Operation Issues:**
  - Description/Symptoms: The retractable roof may malfunction, such as getting stuck during operation or not sealing properly.
  - Solution: Roof mechanism repair or replacement
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+
- **Instrument Cluster Display Problems:**
  - Description/Symptoms: The instrument cluster display may experience flickering, fading, or complete failure.
  - Solution: Instrument cluster replacement
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+

**Summary:**

While the 2017 Porsche 718 Boxster offers an exhilarating driving experience, some owners have reported issues with certain components. The most severe issues appear to be turbocharger failure in the 2.5-liter turbocharged engine and mechatronic unit failure in the 7-speed dual-clutch automatic transmission. These issues can be costly to repair, potentially impacting the overall ownership experience.
