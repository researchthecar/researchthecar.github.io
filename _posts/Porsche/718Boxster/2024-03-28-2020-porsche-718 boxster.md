**Introduction:**

The 2020 Porsche 718 Boxster is a mid-engine sports car available with two engine options and two transmission options. The 2.0L turbocharged engine produces 300 horsepower and 280 lb-ft of torque, while the 2.5L turbocharged engine produces 350 horsepower and 317 lb-ft of torque. Both engines are paired with either a 6-speed manual transmission or a 7-speed dual-clutch automatic transmission. The 718 Boxster is praised for its handling, performance, and luxurious interior, but criticized for its firm ride and lack of storage space.

**Engine**

* **2.0L Turbocharged Engine**
    * **Engine Oil Leak:** Oil leaks have been reported from the valve cover gaskets, causing oil to leak onto the exhaust and create a burning smell. Solution: Replace the valve cover gaskets. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600.
* **2.5L Turbocharged Engine**
    * **Ignition Coil Failure:** Ignition coils have failed prematurely, causing misfires and reduced engine power. Solution: Replace the failed ignition coils. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400.
    * **Intake Valve Carbon Buildup:** Carbon deposits can accumulate on the intake valves over time, reducing airflow and engine performance. Solution: Remove the intake manifold and manually clean the valves. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1000.
* **All Engines**
    * **Camshaft Position Sensor Failure:** The camshaft position sensor can fail, causing the engine to run poorly or not start at all. Solution: Replace the camshaft position sensor. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400.
    * **Coolant Leak:** Coolant leaks have been reported from the water pump, radiator, and hoses. Solution: Replace the leaking component. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800.

**Transmission**

* **6-Speed Manual Transmission**
    * **Shift Linkage Failure:** The shift linkage can fail, making it difficult or impossible to shift gears. Solution: Replace the shift linkage. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1000.

* **7-Speed Dual-Clutch Automatic Transmission**
    * **Clutch Pack Failure:** The clutch pack can fail, causing slipping, shuddering, or loss of power. Solution: Replace the clutch pack. Estimated Cost to Fix: $2000 - $3000.
    * **Mechatronic Unit Failure:** The mechatronic unit can fail, causing the transmission to malfunction or fail completely. Solution: Replace the mechatronic unit. Estimated Cost to Fix: $5000 - $8000.

**Suspension**

* **Front Suspension:**
    * **Strut Mount Failure:** The strut mounts can fail, causing noises and vibration from the front suspension. Solution: Replace the strut mounts. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400.
    * **Lower Control Arm Bushings Failure:** The lower control arm bushings can fail, causing increased wheel hop and compromised handling. Solution: Replace the lower control arm bushings. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600.

* **Rear Suspension:**
    * **Rear Shock Absorber Failure:** The rear shock absorbers can fail, causing the rear suspension to feel soft or bouncy. Solution: Replace the rear shock absorbers. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1000.

**Electrical**

* **All Models:**
    * **Battery Discharge:** The battery can discharge prematurely, especially when the car is not driven regularly. Solution: Replace the battery or update the software. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500.
    * **Electrical Harness Issues:** Electrical harness issues have been reported, causing various electrical problems. Solution: Repair or replace the faulty wiring. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1000.

**Exterior**

* **Headlight Condensation:** Condensation can form inside the headlights, especially after a car wash or heavy rain. Solution: Allow the headlights to air out or replace the headlight assembly. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500.

**Interior**

* **Infotainment System Issues:** The infotainment system can experience glitches, freezes, or connectivity issues. Solution: Update the software or replace the infotainment system. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800.

**Summary:**

The 2020 Porsche 718 Boxster has several potential issues, but most are relatively minor and inexpensive to fix. However, some issues, such as transmission problems and electrical harness issues, can be more costly to repair. Overall, the 718 Boxster is a well-built sports car, but owners should be aware of the potential issues listed above.
