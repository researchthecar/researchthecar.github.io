# 2018 Porsche 718 Boxster Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2018 Porsche 718 Boxster delivers thrilling mid-engine dynamics, razor-sharp handling, and potent turbocharged flat-four power. For used-car buyers looking at this modern roadster, understanding the available powertrain options and general owner sentiment helps ensure a satisfying purchase.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged Flat-4

This base 2.0-liter turbocharged flat-four engine produces approximately 300 horsepower and 280 lb-ft of torque. It offers responsive everyday power and strong efficiency for a sports car, maintaining a solid overall reliability reputation among owners.

### 2.5L Turbocharged Flat-4 (S)

Found in the S trim, this 2.5-liter turbocharged flat-four features a variable turbine geometry turbocharger to generate 350 horsepower and 309 lb-ft of torque. It delivers exhilarating acceleration with generally dependable long-term mechanical performance.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Manual Transmission

The traditional 6-speed manual transmission provides crisp, mechanical gear changes for driving purists. It is generally robust, though clutch wear will depend heavily on driving style and previous owner use.

### 7-Speed PDK Dual-Clutch Transmission

Porsche's lightning-fast 7-speed Doppelkupplung (PDK) automatic transmission offers seamless and rapid gear changes. It is widely praised for performance, though routine fluid maintenance is essential for longevity.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V179000: Air Bags](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Porsche&model=718+Boxster&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** The side air bag impact sensors may not have been properly tightened to the vehicle. In the event of a crash, the air bags may not deploy, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Porsche will notify owners, and dealers will tighten the air bag sensors, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V113000: Fuel System, Gasoline](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Porsche&model=718+Boxster&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** In the event of a crash, a crossmember may detach from the luggage compartment bracket, possibly causing a fuel leak. A fuel leak in the presence of an ignition source can increase the risk of fire.
- **What to do:** Porsche will notify owners, and dealers will secure the crossmember with additional rivets, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.
