**Introduction**

The 2021 Porsche Panamera is a high-performance luxury sedan available with various engine and transmission options. It boasts impressive power and handling, combined with a spacious and luxurious interior. However, like any vehicle, it has experienced some issues that owners have reported.

**Engine**

**3.0L V6 Twin-Turbo** (330 hp, 331 lb-ft torque)
* **Turbocharger Failure**: The turbochargers may experience premature wear, leading to loss of power and engine noise.
  * Solution: Replace the turbochargers.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+
  * Recalls: NHTSA Campaign Number 22V269000
* **Fuel Injectors Clogging**: The fuel injectors can become clogged, causing engine misfires and poor performance.
  * Solution: Clean or replace the fuel injectors.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+

**4.0L V8 Twin-Turbo** (463 hp, 406 lb-ft torque)
* **Timing Chain Tensioner Failure**: The timing chain tensioners may fail, resulting in engine noise, reduced power, and potential catastrophic engine damage.
  * Solution: Replace the timing chain tensioners.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $3,000 - $5,000+
  * Recalls: NHTSA Campaign Number 22V802000

**Transmission**

**8-Speed PDK Dual-Clutch**
* **Clutch Wear**: The clutches may experience premature wear or damage, leading to delayed or inconsistent shifting.
  * Solution: Replace the clutch pack.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+
* **Mechatronic Unit Failure**: The mechatronic unit, which controls the transmission, may malfunction, causing transmission problems such as delayed shifts or lack of engagement.
  * Solution: Replace the mechatronic unit.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $5,000 - $7,000+

**Suspension**

* **Rear Suspension Noise**: Some owners have reported excessive noise from the rear suspension, particularly over bumps or uneven roads.
  * Solution: Inspect and lubricate the suspension components, or replace if necessary.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+

**Electrical**

* **Battery Discharge**: The battery may experience premature discharge, leaving the vehicle unable to start.
  * Solution: Replace the battery and investigate any underlying electrical problems.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
* **Headlight Failure**: The headlights may experience premature failure or condensation buildup inside the housing.
  * Solution: Replace or repair the headlights.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
  * Recalls: NHTSA Campaign Number 20V773000, 21V269000

**Exterior**

* **Trunk Lid Corrosion**: Some owners have reported corrosion issues with the trunk lid, particularly on the underside where water and road salt can accumulate.
  * Solution: Inspect the trunk lid for corrosion and repair or replace if necessary.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $3,000+

**Interior**

* **Infotainment System Glitches**: The infotainment system may experience occasional glitches, such as freezing or restarting.
  * Solution: Software updates or system reset.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 - $500
* **Power Seat Failure**: The power seats may experience malfunctions, such as movement becoming erratic or buttons not responding.
  * Solution: Repair or replace the seat motor or switches.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+

**Summary**

The 2021 Porsche Panamera is generally a reliable and well-engineered vehicle. However, it has experienced some issues, particularly with the engine, transmission, and electrical systems. The severity and cost of repairs can vary depending on the specific issue and the extent of the problem. It's important for owners to be aware of these potential issues and have them addressed promptly to ensure the safety and performance of their vehicle.
