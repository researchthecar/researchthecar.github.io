The 2022 Porsche Panamera is a luxury performance sedan that offers a blend of sportiness and comfort. It is known for its powerful engines, precise handling, and luxurious interior. However, like any vehicle, it has its share of potential issues. Engine options for the 2022 Panamera range from a turbocharged V6 to a powerful V8, and even a hybrid powertrain. For example, the Panamera 4S boasts a 2.9L twin-turbo V6 producing 443 horsepower and 405 lb-ft of torque. Owners often praise the Panamera's driving dynamics and upscale features, but some have reported concerns with electronic glitches and certain mechanical components.

**Engine**

**2.9L Twin-Turbo V6 (Panamera 4S)**
* **Coolant Leaks**
    * Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaks may develop from the coolant pipes located under the intake manifold. This can lead to overheating if not addressed promptly. Symptoms include low coolant levels, coolant warning lights, and visible coolant leaks.
    * Solution: Replacing the coolant pipes and gaskets.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**4.0L Twin-Turbo V8 (Panamera GTS, Turbo)**
* **Oil Leaks**
    * Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks can occur from various locations, including the valve cover gaskets, oil pan gasket, or rear main seal. This can lead to low oil levels and potential engine damage. Symptoms include visible oil leaks, oil spots under the car, and a burning oil smell.
    * Solution: Replacing the leaking gaskets or seals.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $3,000+ depending on the location and severity.
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**8-Speed PDK (All Models)**
* **Hesitation or Jerking During Shifting**
    * Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported experiencing hesitation or jerking during gear changes, particularly at lower speeds. This can be caused by issues with the transmission control module (TCM) or valve body.
    * Solution: Reprogramming the TCM or replacing the valve body.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $4,000+ depending on the repair needed.
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Transmission Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Internal failure of the transmission can occur due to various reasons, leading to complete loss of drive.
    * Solution: Replacement of the PDK transmission.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $12,000 - $18,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Air Suspension Malfunctions**
    * Description/Symptoms: The air suspension system, especially on models equipped with it, can experience issues such as sagging, uneven ride height, or failure to adjust properly. This may be due to faulty air struts, air compressors, or ride height sensors.
    * Solution: Replacing the faulty components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $4,000+ per strut, plus labor for other components.
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Infotainment System Glitches**
    * Description/Symptoms: The infotainment system may experience glitches, such as freezing, screen blackouts, or failure to connect to smartphones. Software updates are often released to address these issues.
    * Solution: Software updates or replacement of the infotainment unit.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 (software update) - $2,000+ (unit replacement).
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Sensor Failures (Parking Sensors, Blind Spot Monitoring)**
    * Description/Symptoms: Malfunctioning parking sensors or blind spot monitoring systems can occur due to faulty sensors or wiring issues. This can lead to inaccurate readings or complete system failure.
    * Solution: Replacing the faulty sensors or repairing the wiring.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+ per sensor.
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **No Issues Found**

**Interior**

* **Sunroof Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported issues with the sunroof, such as difficulty opening or closing, unusual noises, or leaks.
    * Solution: Repair or replacement of the sunroof motor or mechanism.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2022 Porsche Panamera is a high-performance luxury vehicle, and while it offers an exceptional driving experience, it can be prone to certain issues. The most commonly reported problems involve the engine (coolant and oil leaks), transmission (hesitation and potential failures), suspension (air suspension malfunctions), and electrical systems (infotainment glitches and sensor failures). The severity and cost of repairs can vary significantly, with some issues being relatively minor fixes while others can be quite expensive, particularly those involving the engine, transmission, or air suspension. Potential buyers should be aware of these common issues and thoroughly inspect the vehicle before purchasing, and also obtain a pre-purchase inspection by a qualified mechanic familiar with Porsche vehicles.
