# 2017 Porsche Panamera Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2017 Porsche Panamera offers exceptional performance and luxury, blending sports car handling with executive sedan comfort. For prospective buyers, understanding the reliability profile of its powertrain choices and electrical systems is key to a confident purchase.

---

## Engine

### 3.0L Turbo V6

This base 3.0-liter turbocharged V6 engine produces around 330 horsepower and 331 lb-ft of torque, providing robust entry-level performance. It generally offers solid everyday reliability when maintained according to Porsche service schedules.

### 2.9L Twin-Turbo V6

The 2.9-liter twin-turbocharged V6 powers the 4S models, generating approximately 440 horsepower and 405 lb-ft of torque. It balances high-output sports performance with sophisticated engineering, though buyers should verify meticulous maintenance histories.

### 4.0L Twin-Turbo V8

The high-performance 4.0-liter twin-turbo V8 delivers roughly 550 horsepower in the Turbo trim, offering ferocious acceleration. Maintenance costs are notably higher for this complex powerplant, making a pre-purchase inspection essential.

### 2.9L V6 Plug-In Hybrid

The E-Hybrid variant pairs a 2.9-liter V6 with an electric motor to produce around 462 combined horsepower. Buyers should thoroughly check the health and charging capability of the hybrid battery system prior to purchase.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed PDK Dual-Clutch Automatic

The 8-speed Porsche Doppelkupplung (PDK) dual-clutch transmission delivers lightning-fast gear changes and smooth power delivery across all trims. It is generally robust, though proper fluid service intervals are crucial for long-term durability.

## Electrical

### Central Electrical System

Electrical architecture is complex and controls many of the vehicle's luxury and performance features.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V898000: Steering:Electric Power Assist System](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Porsche&model=Panamera&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** An intermittent loss of power steering assist can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Porsche will notify owners, and dealers will reprogram the power steering control unit, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V115000: Electrical System: Instrument Cluster/Panel](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Porsche&model=Panamera&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** The instrument cluster may not provide a visual warning when the brake pads are worn out. As a result, these vehicles fail to comply with Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 135, "Light vehicle brake systems." If the driver is not alerted when brake pads are worn out, the driver may lose control of the vehicle while attempting to brake.
- **What to do:** Porsche will notify owners, and dealers will update the instrument cluster software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V735000: Electrical System: Instrument Cluster/Panel](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Porsche&model=Panamera&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** The instrument cluster may not provide a visual warning when the brake pads are worn out. As a result, these vehicles fail to comply with Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 135, "Light vehicle brake systems." If the driver is not alerted when brake pads are worn out, the vehicle may not respond as expected when braking, increasing the risk.
- **What to do:** Porsche will notify owners, and dealers will reprogram the instrument cluster, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V033000: Electrical System](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Porsche&model=Panamera&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** Humidity may enter the external coolant pump for the climate control system and cause an electrical short circuit. An electrical short circuit increases the risk of a vehicle fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the external coolant pump and inspect and replace the plug-in connection, if necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2017 Porsche Panamera is a sophisticated luxury performance vehicle that requires diligent maintenance and thorough inspection. While major mechanical failures are not widely reported by owners, verifying completion of service campaigns and assessing complex electrical systems will help ensure a satisfying ownership experience.
