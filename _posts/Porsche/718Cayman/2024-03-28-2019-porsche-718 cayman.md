**Introduction:**

The 2019 Porsche 718 Cayman is a mid-engine sports car that combines agile handling, responsive powertrains, and stylish design. It is offered with four engine options: a 2.0-liter turbocharged flat-four that produces 300 horsepower and 280 lb-ft of torque, a 2.5-liter turbocharged flat-four that generates 350 horsepower and 310 lb-ft of torque, a 3.8-liter flat-six that delivers 400 horsepower and 310 lb-ft of torque, and a 4.5-liter flat-six that produces 450 horsepower and 390 lb-ft of torque. Transmission options include a six-speed manual, seven-speed dual-clutch automatic, and eight-speed dual-clutch automatic. The 718 Cayman is praised for its precise handling, exhilarating performance, and elegant interior, but owners have reported some issues with various components.

**Engine:**

**2.0-Liter Turbocharged Flat-Four**

- **Fuel Injector Failure:**
  - Description/Symptoms: Engine misfires, rough idle, loss of power
  - Solution: Replace fuel injectors
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
  - Recalls: None Found

- **Turbocharger Wastegate Failure:**
  - Description/Symptoms: Reduced boost pressure, loss of power, check engine light
  - Solution: Replace turbocharger
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000
  - Recalls: None Found

**2.5-Liter Turbocharged Flat-Four**

- **No Issues Found**

**3.8-Liter Flat-Six**

- **Oil Leaks from Cam Cover Gaskets:**
  - Description/Symptoms: Oil leaking from the top of the engine
  - Solution: Replace cam cover gaskets
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
  - Recalls: None Found

- **Exhaust Manifold Cracking:**
  - Description/Symptoms: Exhaust leak, rattling noise under acceleration
  - Solution: Replace exhaust manifold
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500
  - Recalls: None Found

**4.5-Liter Flat-Six**

- **No Issues Found**

**Transmission:**

**Six-Speed Manual**

- **Synchronizer Wear:**
  - Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears, grinding noises
  - Solution: Replace synchronizers
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000
  - Recalls: None Found

- **Clutch Failure:**
  - Description/Symptoms: Difficulty engaging gears, slipping clutch
  - Solution: Replace clutch
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500
  - Recalls: None Found

**Seven-Speed Dual-clutch Automatic**

- **Mechatronic Unit Failure:**
  - Description/Symptoms: Transmission shifting problems, check engine light
  - Solution: Replace mechatronic unit
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $3,000 - $5,000
  - Recalls: None Found

- **Clutch Pack Wear:**
  - Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, shuddering during shifts
  - Solution: Replace clutch packs
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,000
  - Recalls: None Found

**Eight-Speed Dual-clutch Automatic**

- **No Issues Found**

**Suspension:**

- **Front Suspension Bushing Failure:**
  - Description/Symptoms: Clunking noises over bumps, unstable handling
  - Solution: Replace front suspension bushings
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
  - Recalls: None Found

- **Rear Suspension Coil Spring Breakage:**
  - Description/Symptoms: Sagging rear end, uneven ride height
  - Solution: Replace rear suspension coil springs
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
  - Recalls: None Found

**Electrical:**

- **Battery Drain:**
  - Description/Symptoms: Battery goes dead after short periods of non-use
  - Solution: Diagnose and repair electrical system
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500
  - Recalls: None Found

- **Electrical Component Failures:**
  - Description/Symptoms: Various electrical components, such as lights, gauges, or infotainment system, may fail
  - Solution: Repair or replace affected electrical components
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,000
  - Recalls: None Found

**Exterior:**

- **Rear Spoiler Vibration:**
  - Description/Symptoms: Rear spoiler vibrates at high speeds
  - Solution: Adjust or replace spoiler mounts
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500
  - Recalls: None Found

- **Headlight Condensation:**
  - Description/Symptoms: Condensation inside headlight housings
  - Solution: Replace headlight housing seals
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
  - Recalls: None Found

**Interior:**

- **Seat Adjustment Motor Failure:**
  - Description/Symptoms: Seats do not adjust or adjust slowly
  - Solution: Replace seat adjustment motor
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
  - Recalls: None Found

- **Steering Wheel Control Failures:**
  - Description/Symptoms: Steering wheel controls, such as cruise control or volume, do not work
  - Solution: Repair or replace steering wheel controls
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500
  - Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

The 2019 Porsche 718 Cayman offers a thrilling driving experience, but its reliability has been somewhat compromised by a range of issues in various components. While some problems, such as suspension bushing failure or seat adjustment motor failure, are relatively inexpensive to fix, more severe issues like mechatronic unit failure or turbocharger replacement can be significantly costly. Engine and transmission problems are the most prevalent, with potential expenses reaching up to several thousand dollars. Therefore, while the 718 Cayman may be a desirable sports car, potential buyers should be aware of its potential reliability concerns and factor in the cost of potential repairs when considering purchasing or owning one.
