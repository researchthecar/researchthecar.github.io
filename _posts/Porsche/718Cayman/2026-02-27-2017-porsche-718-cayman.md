# 2017 Porsche 718 Cayman Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2017 Porsche 718 Cayman, buyers are treated to an exceptionally capable mid-engine sports car with sharp handling and potent turbocharged power plants. While generally regarded as a well-engineered performance vehicle, owner reports submitted to safety agencies highlight a few minor cabin and safety quirks worth inspecting during a pre-purchase evaluation.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged Flat-4

This base 2.0-liter turbocharged horizontally opposed four-cylinder engine produces 300 horsepower and 280 lb-ft of torque. It offers brisk acceleration and solid everyday drivability while serving as the entry-level powerplant for the 718 lineup.

### 2.5L Turbocharged Flat-4

Found in the S models, this 2.5-liter turbocharged flat-4 pumps out 350 horsepower and 309 lb-ft of torque. It delivers thrilling performance paired with responsive turbo spooling, though owners should note minor exterior design layout quirks.

**Exhaust Protrusion Burn Hazard**
- **Symptoms:** The central exhaust tip design can protrude noticeably, leading to potential contact burns on legs when loading or unloading cargo or accessing the rear area.
- **Solution:** Exercise caution around the hot exhaust tips after driving, and consider aftermarket guards if necessary.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Manual Transmission

The traditional 6-speed manual gearbox provides short, mechanical throws and engaging pedal feel for purists. It is generally robust when maintained properly with clutch replacements depending heavily on driving style.

### 7-Speed PDK Dual-Clutch Transmission

Porsche's lightning-fast 7-speed Doppelkupplung (PDK) automatic transmission delivers seamless gear changes and exceptional track or street performance. Routine fluid changes are essential to maintain its crisp shift quality over high mileage.

## Safety

### Seat Belts

Owner complaints note occasional mechanical binding or lazy retraction within the seat belt inertia reels.

**Seat Belt Retractor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The passenger or driver side seat belt fails to retract smoothly, leaving the webbing loose and hanging slack against the seat or door frame.
- **Solution:** Inspect the seat belt assembly for proper tension and retraction during a test drive; faulty retractors may require complete unit replacement.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V113000: Fuel System, Gasoline](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Porsche&model=718+Cayman&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** In the event of a crash, a crossmember may detach from the luggage compartment bracket, possibly causing a fuel leak. A fuel leak in the presence of an ignition source can increase the risk of fire.
- **What to do:** Porsche will notify owners, and dealers will secure the crossmember with additional rivets, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V054000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Hoses, Lines/Piping, And Fittings](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Porsche&model=718+Cayman&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** A fuel leak in the presence of an ignition source increases the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Porsche will notify owners, and dealers will install improved fastening screws, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V946000: Air Bags:Side/Window](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Porsche&model=718+Cayman&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** The seat mounted side air bag inflator initiator may fail to ignite during a crash. If the air bag inflator initiator fails to ignite, the side air bag will not deploy, increasing the risk of injury in the event of a crash.
- **What to do:** Porsche will notify owners, and dealers will replace the affected air bag modules, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V134000: Air Bags:Frontal:Passenger Side:Inflator Module](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Porsche&model=718+Cayman&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** The passenger frontal air bag inflator initiator may fail to ignite during a crash. If the air bag inflator initiator fails to ignite, the passenger frontal air bag will not deploy, increasing the risk of injury in the event of a crash.
- **What to do:** Porsche will notify owners, and dealers will replace the affected air bags, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.
