**Introduction**

The 2023 Porsche 718 Cayman is a mid-engine sports car that offers a thrilling driving experience with its precise handling, powerful engine, and sleek design. It is available with two engine options: a 2.0L turbocharged flat-four and a 2.5L turbocharged flat-four. The 2.0L engine produces 300 horsepower and 280 lb-ft of torque, while the 2.5L engine produces 350 horsepower and 309 lb-ft of torque. Both engines are mated to either a 6-speed manual transmission or a 7-speed dual-clutch automatic transmission.

While the 718 Cayman is a well-made vehicle, it is not immune to problems. Here is a list of specific issues that owners have reported:

**Engine**

**2.0L Turbocharged Flat-Four**

* **Intake manifold failure:** The intake manifold can crack, causing a loss of power and fuel economy.
    * Solution: Replace the intake manifold.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,000+
    * Recalls: None Found
    
* **Turbocharger failure:** The turbocharger can fail, causing a loss of power and a loud whining noise.
    * Solution: Replace the turbocharger.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,500 - $3,500+
    * Recalls: None Found
    
**2.5L Turbocharged Flat-Four**

* **Piston ring failure:** The piston rings can fail, causing excessive oil consumption and blue smoke from the exhaust.
    * Solution: Overhaul the engine.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $5,000 - $10,000+
    * Recalls: None Found
    
**Transmission**

**6-Speed Manual Transmission**

* **Synchro failure:** The synchronizers can wear out prematurely, causing difficulty shifting gears.
    * Solution: Replace the synchro unit.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
    * Recalls: None Found
    
**7-Speed Dual-Clutch Automatic Transmission**

* **Clutch failure:** The clutch can wear out prematurely, causing slipping and shuddering when shifting gears.
    * Solution: Replace the clutch.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,000+
    * Recalls: None Found
    
**Suspension**

* **Strut mount failure:** The strut mounts can fail, causing a clunking or rattling noise when driving over bumps.
    * Solution: Replace the strut mounts.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found
    
* **Shock absorber failure:** The shock absorbers can fail, causing the vehicle to ride roughly and handle poorly.
    * Solution: Replace the shock absorbers.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
    * Recalls: None Found
    
**Electrical**

* **Battery drain:** The battery can drain prematurely, even when the vehicle is not in use.
    * Solution: Find and fix the electrical drain.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
    * Recalls: None Found
    
* **Alternator failure:** The alternator can fail, causing the vehicle to lose power and the battery to discharge.
    * Solution: Replace the alternator.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,200+
    * Recalls: None Found
    
**Exterior**

* **Paint defects:** The paint can be prone to chipping and fading, especially on the front bumper and hood.
    * Solution: Repaint the affected areas.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+
    * Recalls: None Found
    
* **Headlight condensation:** The headlights can develop condensation inside the lenses, which can impair visibility.
    * Solution: Replace the headlight assembly.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,200+
    * Recalls: None Found
    
**Interior**

* **Infotainment system bugs:** The infotainment system can experience various bugs, including freezing, lag, and connectivity issues.
    * Solution: Update the software or replace the infotainment unit.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
    * Recalls: None Found
    
* **Climate control malfunctions:** The climate control system can malfunction, causing the air conditioning or heating to not work properly.
    * Solution: Diagnose and repair the malfunctioning component.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+
    * Recalls: None Found
    
**Summary**

Overall, the 2023 Porsche 718 Cayman is a reliable sports car with few major issues. However, like any vehicle, it is not immune to problems. The most severe issues reported by owners include intake manifold failure, turbocharger failure, and piston ring failure. These issues can be expensive to fix, so it is important to be aware of them before purchasing a 718 Cayman.
