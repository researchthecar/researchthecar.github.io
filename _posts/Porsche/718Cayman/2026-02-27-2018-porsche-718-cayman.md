# 2018 Porsche 718 Cayman Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2018 Porsche 718 Cayman delivers exceptional mid-engine handling, sharp steering, and brisk performance. As with any high-performance sports car, prospective buyers should carefully evaluate service history and mechanical condition prior to purchase.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged Flat-4

The base 2.0-liter turbocharged flat-4 engine produces approximately 300 horsepower and 280 lb-ft of torque. It offers strong everyday responsiveness and solid reliability when properly maintained.

### 2.5L Turbocharged Flat-4

The S-trim 2.5-liter turbocharged flat-4 engine pumps out roughly 350 horsepower and 309 lb-ft of torque. This powerplant delivers thrilling acceleration while generally maintaining a dependable mechanical record.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Manual Transmission

The traditional 6-speed manual transmission offers direct, mechanical engagement favored by driving enthusiasts. It is robust and generally trouble-free with regular fluid changes.

### 7-Speed Dual-Clutch (PDK) Automatic Transmission

The lightning-fast 7-speed PDK dual-clutch automatic transmission delivers rapid gear shifts and smooth operation. It is mechanically resilient, provided factory service intervals have been strictly observed.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V179000: Air Bags](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Porsche&model=718+Cayman&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** The side air bag impact sensors may not have been properly tightened to the vehicle. In the event of a crash, the air bags may not deploy, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Porsche will notify owners, and dealers will tighten the air bag sensors, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V113000: Fuel System, Gasoline](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Porsche&model=718+Cayman&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** In the event of a crash, a crossmember may detach from the luggage compartment bracket, possibly causing a fuel leak. A fuel leak in the presence of an ignition source can increase the risk of fire.
- **What to do:** Porsche will notify owners, and dealers will secure the crossmember with additional rivets, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2018 Porsche 718 Cayman is a thrilling and well-engineered sports car combining daily usability with track-ready performance. Buyers should ensure a comprehensive pre-purchase inspection to verify proper mechanical health and consistent maintenance history.
