**Introduction to the 2021 Porsche 718 Cayman**

The 2021 Porsche 718 Cayman is a sports car that provides exhilarating performance and precision handling. It's powered by a turbocharged 2.0-liter four-cylinder engine that produces 300 horsepower and 280 lb-ft of torque. The 718 Cayman is also available with a 2.5-liter four-cylinder engine that produces 350 horsepower and 310 lb-ft of torque. Both engines are paired with a six-speed manual transmission or a seven-speed dual-clutch automatic transmission.

Critics praise the 718 Cayman for its thrilling driving dynamics, responsive steering, and well-appointed interior. However, some have expressed concerns about its lack of rear-seat space and somewhat firm ride.

**Engine**

**2.0L Turbocharged Four-Cylinder**

* **Valve Stem Carbon Buildup:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Reduced engine power, rough idling, and difficulty starting.
    * Solution: Intake valve cleaning and fuel system maintenance.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Timing Chain Tensioner Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Unusual engine noises, especially at startup or acceleration.
    * Solution: Replacement of the timing chain tensioner.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,200 - $1,800
    * Recalls: None Found

**2.5L Turbocharged Four-Cylinder**

* **Fuel Pump Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Engine stalling, hesitation during acceleration, and reduced fuel economy.
    * Solution: Replacement of the fuel pump.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $700 - $1,000
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Oil Leak from Cylinder Head Gasket:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Oil leakage visible on the engine block, particularly from the cylinder head area.
    * Solution: Replacement of the cylinder head gasket.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,000
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**Six-Speed Manual**

* **Shifting Difficulty:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Difficulty engaging gears, especially in cold weather.
    * Solution: Replacement of shift linkage components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800
    * Recalls: None Found

**Seven-Speed Dual-Clutch Automatic**

* **Clutch Wear:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Slipping or shuddering during gear changes, reduced transmission efficiency.
    * Solution: Replacement of clutch packs.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,000
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Rear Suspension Squeak:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Squeaking noises from the rear suspension, particularly when driving over rough surfaces.
    * Solution: Lubrication of suspension components or replacement of worn bushings.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Infotainment System Malfunction:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Touchscreen freezes, loss of connectivity, or audio issues.
    * Solution: Software updates or replacement of infotainment module.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $1,500
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Battery Drain:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Battery drains quickly, even when the vehicle is not in use.
    * Solution: Replacement of battery or diagnosis of electrical system for parasitic draw.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **No Significant Issues Found**

**Interior**

* **Steering Column Knock:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Knocking noises from the steering column when turning or driving over uneven surfaces.
    * Solution: Replacement of steering column components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2021 Porsche 718 Cayman is a highly capable sports car, but it has some known issues that potential buyers should be aware of. The engine and transmission components have the most significant potential issues, with repairs ranging from $500 to over $3,000. The suspension and electrical issues are less severe and typically require repairs under $1,000. Overall, the severity and cost of the issues should be considered when making a purchasing decision.
