**1990 Porsche 944**

The 1990 Porsche 944 is a sports car produced by Porsche from 1982 to 1991. It was available with two engine options: a 2.5-liter naturally aspirated flat-four and a 2.7-liter turbocharged flat-four. Both engines were mated to a five-speed manual transmission.

The 2.5-liter engine produced 150 horsepower and 142 lb-ft of torque, while the 2.7-liter turbocharged engine produced 217 horsepower and 205 lb-ft of torque.

The 944 was praised for its handling, performance, and styling. However, it was also criticized for its high price and lack of practicality.

**Engine**

**2.5-Liter Naturally Aspirated Flat-Four**
* **Head Gasket Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Loss of coolant, overheating, white smoke from the exhaust
    * Solution: Replace the head gasket
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1000 - $2000+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Timing Belt Tensioner Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Squealing noise from the engine, loss of power
    * Solution: Replace the timing belt tensioner
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1000
    * Recalls: None Found

**2.7-Liter Turbocharged Flat-Four**
* **Turbocharger Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, black smoke from the exhaust
    * Solution: Replace the turbocharger
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1500 - $3000+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Intercooler Leakage:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, increased boost pressure
    * Solution: Replace the intercooler
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1000
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 1990 Porsche 944 is a reliable and fun-to-drive sports car. However, it does have some potential issues that owners should be aware of.
