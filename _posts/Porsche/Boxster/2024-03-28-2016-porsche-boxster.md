## Porsche Boxster (2016)

The Porsche Boxster is a mid-engine sports car that has been in production since 1996. The 2016 Boxster is available with two engine options: a 2.7-liter flat-six engine that produces 265 horsepower and 206 lb-ft of torque, and a 3.4-liter flat-six engine that produces 315 horsepower and 266 lb-ft of torque. Both engines are paired with a six-speed manual transmission or a seven-speed dual-clutch automatic transmission.

The Boxster is known for its sharp handling, precise steering, and powerful engines. However, some owners have reported issues with the vehicle's engine, transmission, suspension, electrical system, exterior, and interior.

**Engine**

**2.7-liter Flat-Six Engine**

* **Oil Leaks**
  * Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks can occur from the cylinder head gaskets, valve covers, or oil pan. This can lead to low oil levels, which can damage the engine.
  * Solution: Replace the leaking gaskets or oil pan.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
  * Recalls: None Found
* **Timing Chain Failure**
  * Description/Symptoms: The timing chain can fail, which can cause the engine to lose power or stall.
  * Solution: Replace the timing chain.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+
  * Recalls: None Found

**3.4-liter Flat-Six Engine**

* **Throttle Body Failure**
  * Description/Symptoms: The throttle body can fail, which can cause the engine to idle roughly or stall.
  * Solution: Replace the throttle body.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
  * Recalls: None Found
* **Intake Valve Failure**
  * Description/Symptoms: The intake valves can fail, which can cause the engine to lose power or stall.
  * Solution: Replace the intake valves.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+
  * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**Six-Speed Manual Transmission**

* **Clutch Failure**
  * Description/Symptoms: The clutch can fail, which can make it difficult to shift gears or cause the transmission to slip.
  * Solution: Replace the clutch.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
  * Recalls: None Found

**Seven-Speed Dual-Clutch Automatic Transmission**

* **Mechatronic Unit Failure**
  * Description/Symptoms: The mechatronic unit can fail, which can cause the transmission to shift erratically or not at all.
  * Solution: Replace the mechatronic unit.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+
  * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

**Front Suspension**

* **Control Arm Bushing Failure**
  * Description/Symptoms: The control arm bushings can fail, which can cause the front end of the vehicle to feel loose or unstable.
  * Solution: Replace the control arm bushings.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
  * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

**Battery Failure**
* Description/Symptoms: The battery can fail, which can prevent the vehicle from starting or cause the electrical system to malfunction.
* Solution: Replace the battery.
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
* Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

**Convertible Top Malfunction**
* Description/Symptoms: The convertible top can malfunction, which can prevent it from opening or closing properly.
* Solution: Repair or replace the convertible top components.
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $3,000+
* Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

**Instrument Cluster Failure**
* Description/Symptoms: The instrument cluster can fail, which can prevent the driver from seeing important information such as the speedometer or fuel gauge.
* Solution: Replace the instrument cluster.
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
* Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2016 Porsche Boxster is a well-built sports car with excellent handling and performance. However, some owners have reported issues with the vehicle's engine, transmission, suspension, electrical system, exterior, and interior. The severity and cost of these issues can vary depending on the specific problem. Overall, the Boxster is a reliable vehicle, but it is important to be aware of the potential issues before purchasing one.
