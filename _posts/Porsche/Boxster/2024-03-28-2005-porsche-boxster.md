**Introduction:**

The 2005 Porsche Boxster is a mid-engine sports car powered by a 2.7-liter flat-six engine producing 240 horsepower and 181 lb-ft of torque. It is paired with a 5-speed manual or 5-speed Tiptronic automatic transmission. The Boxster was praised for its sharp handling, responsive engine, and stylish design. However, it faced some criticism for its lack of practicality and high maintenance costs.

**Engine**

**2.7-liter M96 Flat-Six**

* **Intermediate Shaft Bearing Failure:**
  * Description/Symptoms: Grinding noises, loss of power, illuminated check engine light
  * Solution: Replace intermediate shaft bearings and associated components
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,500 - $4,000+
  * Recalls: None Found
* **VarioCam Plus System Failure:**
  * Description/Symptoms: Hesitation, loss of power, rough idle
  * Solution: Replace VarioCam Plus solenoids and associated components
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
  * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**5-Speed Manual**

* **No Issues Found**

**5-Speed Tiptronic Automatic**

* **Torque Converter Failure:**
  * Description/Symptoms: Shuddering, slipping, no engagement
  * Solution: Replace torque converter
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
  * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Control Arm Bushing Wear:**
  * Description/Symptoms: Clunking or rattling noises, poor handling
  * Solution: Replace control arm bushings
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
  * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Alternator Failure:**
  * Description/Symptoms: Dimming headlights, difficulty starting, battery drain
  * Solution: Replace alternator
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
  * Recalls: None Found
* **Battery Drain:**
  * Description/Symptoms: Battery dies prematurely, difficulty starting
  * Solution: Diagnose source of battery drain and repair as needed
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
  * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Convertible Top Motor Failure:**
  * Description/Symptoms: Failure to open or close, erratic operation
  * Solution: Replace convertible top motor and associated components
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
  * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Climate Control Malfunction:**
  * Description/Symptoms: Failure to regulate temperature, airflow problems
  * Solution: Replace climate control module and associated components
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
  * Recalls: None Found
* **Power Window Motor Failure:**
  * Description/Symptoms: Failure of power windows to operate
  * Solution: Replace power window motor
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
  * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2005 Porsche Boxster is a reliable and enjoyable sports car overall. However, it is not without its potential issues. The intermediate shaft bearing failure is a known problem with the M96 flat-six engine and can be expensive to repair. Other issues, such as torque converter failure in the automatic transmission and convertible top motor failure, can also be costly. It is important to factor in the potential for these issues and associated repair costs when considering the purchase of a 2005 Porsche Boxster.
