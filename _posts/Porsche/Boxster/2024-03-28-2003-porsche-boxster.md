**Introduction:**

The 2003 Porsche Boxster is a mid-engine sports car with sleek styling and nimble handling. It is powered by a 2.7-liter flat-six engine that produces 228 horsepower and 192 lb-ft of torque. The Boxster is available with a 5-speed manual or a 5-speed Tiptronic automatic transmission.

**Engine**

**2.7L Flat-Six Engine (Manual Transmission)**

* **Camshaft Adjuster Failure:** The camshaft adjuster can fail, causing rough idling, loss of power, and increased fuel consumption. Replace the camshaft adjuster. $1,000 - $1,500+
* **Intermediate Shaft Bearing Failure (IMS Bearing):** The IMS bearing can fail, causing catastrophic engine damage. Replace the IMS bearing and related components. $5,000 - $10,000+
* **Valve Guide Wear:** The valve guides can wear prematurely, causing excessive oil consumption. Replace the valve guides and related components. $2,000 - $3,000+

**2.7L Flat-Six Engine (Automatic Transmission)**

* **Transmission Fluid Leak:** The transmission fluid lines can leak, causing a loss of fluid and transmission damage. Replace the leaking lines. $500 - $1,000+

**Transmission**

**5-Speed Manual Transmission:**

* **Clutch Failure:** The clutch can wear prematurely, causing slipping and difficulty shifting. Replace the clutch assembly. $1,000 - $2,000+

**5-Speed Tiptronic Automatic Transmission:**

* **Torque Converter Shudder:** The torque converter can develop a shudder when shifting gears. Replace the torque converter. $2,000 - $3,000+
* **Transmission Control Module (TCM) Failure:** The TCM can fail, causing intermittent transmission problems. Replace the TCM. $1,000 - $2,000+

**Suspension**

* **Control Arm Bushing Wear:** The control arm bushings can wear prematurely, causing a variety of handling problems. Replace the control arm bushings. $500 - $1,000+
* **Sway Bar Link Failure:** The sway bar links can fail, causing a clunking noise and reduced handling stability. Replace the sway bar links. $200 - $400+

**Electrical**

* **Alternator Failure:** The alternator can fail, causing electrical system problems. Replace the alternator. $500 - $1,000+
* **Battery Drain:** Certain electrical components can drain the battery prematurely. Identify and replace the faulty components. $200 - $500+

**Exterior**

* **Headlight Lens Fogging:** The headlight lenses can become fogged or cloudy over time, reducing visibility. Replace the headlight lenses. $500 - $1,000+

**Interior**

* **Dashboard Pixel Failure:** The dashboard pixels can fail, causing the display to become distorted or unreadable. Replace the instrument cluster. $500 - $1,000+
* **Sunroof Leak:** The sunroof seals can fail, causing water to leak into the cabin. Replace the sunroof seals. $200 - $500+

**Summary:**

The 2003 Porsche Boxster is a sporty and enjoyable car to drive, but it is not without its issues. The engine and transmission are prone to some serious problems that can be expensive to fix. The suspension, electrical, and exterior components are also susceptible to issues. Overall, the Boxster is a well-built car, but it requires regular maintenance and repairs to keep it running in top condition. The severity and cost of the issues vary, but some of them can be quite expensive to fix.
