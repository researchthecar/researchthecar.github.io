**Introduction:**

The 2001 Porsche Boxster is a mid-engine, rear-wheel-drive sports car that was available with two engine options and two transmission options. The base model came with a 2.7-liter flat-six engine producing 217 horsepower and 189 lb-ft of torque, while the Boxster S model came with a 3.2-liter flat-six engine producing 250 horsepower and 220 lb-ft of torque. Both engines were available with a five-speed manual transmission or a five-speed Tiptronic automatic transmission. The Boxster was praised for its handling, performance, and styling, but it was criticized for its small interior and lack of storage space.

**Engine**

**2.7-liter Flat-Six Engine**

* **Oil leaks from the camshaft seals:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Oil leaking from the engine around the camshafts.
    * Solution: Replace the camshaft seals.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
* **Intake camshaft failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Engine misfiring, loss of power, and increased oil consumption.
    * Solution: Replace the intake camshaft.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,000+
    * Recalls: 2001 Porsche Boxster Recall (NHTSA Campaign ID: 02V067000)

**3.2-liter Flat-Six Engine**

* **No Issues Found**

**Transmission**

**Five-Speed Manual Transmission**

* **Shifting problems:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting into or out of gears, grinding or crunching noises when shifting.
    * Solution: May involve replacing worn components such as shift forks, synchros, or bearings.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+

**Five-Speed Tiptronic Automatic Transmission**

* **Torque converter failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Transmission slipping, shuddering, or overheating.
    * Solution: Replace the torque converter.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,500 - $3,500+
* **Valve body failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Transmission shifting erratically, delayed shifts, or stuck in gear.
    * Solution: Replace the valve body.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+

**Suspension**

* **Rear trailing arm bushings failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Clunking or squeaking noises from the rear suspension, excessive rear tire wear.
    * Solution: Replace the trailing arm bushings.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+

**Electrical**

* **Battery drain:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Battery repeatedly going dead, even after replacement.
    * Solution: Inspect for parasitic draw and replace faulty components, such as alternators or auxiliary batteries.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
* **Ignition switch failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Key gets stuck in the ignition, car won't start.
    * Solution: Replace the ignition switch.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+
    * Recalls: 2001 Porsche Boxster Recall (NHTSA Campaign ID: 01V287000)

**Exterior**

* **Convertible top leaks:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Water entering the cabin through the convertible top.
    * Solution: Replace seals or adjust the top mechanism.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
* **Door lock actuator failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Doors won't lock or unlock with the remote or manually.
    * Solution: Replace the door lock actuators.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+

**Interior**

* **Instrument cluster failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Gauges not working or displaying incorrect information.
    * Solution: Repair or replace the instrument cluster.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
* **Climate control failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Air conditioning or heating not working properly.
    * Solution: Diagnose and repair the faulty component, such as the compressor, blower motor, or control module.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+

**Summary:**

Overall, the 2001 Porsche Boxster is a reliable and well-built sports car, but it is not without its issues. Some of the more common problems include oil leaks, transmission issues, suspension bushing failure, and electrical problems. While some of these issues are relatively minor and inexpensive to fix, others can be more severe and costly. It is important to be aware of these potential problems before purchasing a used Boxster so that you can make an informed decision about whether or not this car is right for you.
