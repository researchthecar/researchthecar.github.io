**Introduction**

The 1991 Bentley Mulsanne is a luxurious sedan powered by a 6.75-liter V8 engine paired with a 3-speed automatic transmission. It boasts 300 horsepower and 433 lb-ft of torque. Despite being a classic vehicle, the Mulsanne has several known issues that potential buyers should be aware of.

**Engine**

* **V8 Engine**

  * **Intake Valve Failure:** Sticking intake valves cause misfires, rough idle, and loss of power. Replacing the affected valves and associated components is necessary. Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,000+
  * **Crankshaft Position Sensor Failure:** A faulty crankshaft position sensor can result in starting issues, engine stalls, and reduced performance. Replacing the sensor is the solution. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+

**Transmission**

* **3-Speed Automatic Transmission**

  * **Shift Solenoid Failure:** Malfunctioning shift solenoids lead to erratic shifting, delayed engagements, and transmission slipping. Replacing the faulty solenoids and updating the transmission software is required. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+

**Suspension**

* **Air Suspension Valve Failure:** Leaking or faulty air suspension valves cause the vehicle to sag on one or more corners. Replacing the affected valves and adjusting the suspension system is necessary. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $1,500+
* **Rear Shock Absorber Failure:** Worn or damaged rear shock absorbers result in a rough ride, reduced handling, and potential suspension damage. Replacing the shock absorbers and associated components is the solution. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,200 - $1,800+

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain:** A parasitic battery drain can occur due to faulty electronics or wiring issues. Identifying and repairing the root cause is necessary. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,200+
* **Alternator Failure:** A failing alternator can lead to battery charging issues, dimming lights, and electrical malfunctions. Replacing the alternator is the solution. Estimated Cost to Fix: $600 - $1,000+

**Exterior**

* **Rubber Trimming Deterioration:** Over time, the rubber trimming around the doors and windows can deteriorate, causing water leaks and wind noise. Replacing the affected trim pieces is required. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
* **Chrome Trim Oxidation:** The chrome trim on the exterior can oxidize and develop a dull appearance. Polishing or replacing the affected chrome pieces is necessary. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+

**Interior**

* **Door Lock Mechanism Failure:** The door lock mechanisms can fail, resulting in difficulty locking or unlocking the doors. Replacing the faulty actuators and repairing the wiring is necessary. Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,200+
* **Climate Control Unit Failure:** A faulty climate control unit can lead to erratic temperature regulation, malfunctioning vents, and electrical issues. Replacing the climate control unit and reprogramming the system is required. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $1,500+

**Summary**

The 1991 Bentley Mulsanne is a luxurious and powerful sedan, but it does have several common issues. The severity and cost of these issues vary, but some can be quite expensive to repair. Potential buyers should be aware of these issues and factor them into their decision-making process.
