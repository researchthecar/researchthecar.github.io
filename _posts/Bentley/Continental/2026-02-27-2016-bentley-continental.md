# 2016 Bentley Continental

### Overview
The 2016 Bentley Continental GT represents a pinnacle of luxury grand touring, offering a blend of potent performance and exquisite craftsmanship. Reliability for this model year is generally strong, reflecting Bentley's commitment to high-quality engineering and materials. While no vehicle is entirely immune to issues, the Continental GT tends to exhibit fewer widespread mechanical or electrical problems compared to more mass-produced luxury vehicles. Owners can expect a robust driving experience, though the complexity and bespoke nature of certain components mean that any repairs, when necessary, can be costly. Regular maintenance is crucial to preserving its performance and longevity.

## Suspension

### Adaptive Air Suspension
**Air Spring Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle may sag on one corner, uneven ride height, warning lights on the dashboard.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the faulty air spring or associated seals.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1500 per corner

**Compressor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Inability to raise or lower the vehicle, constant running of the compressor, suspension warning lights.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the air suspension compressor unit.
- **Cost:** $1000 - $2000


## Electrical

### Infotainment System
**Software Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Screen freezing, unresponsive touch controls, navigation errors, Bluetooth connectivity issues.
- **Solution:** Software updates or a full system reset. In rare cases, replacement of the head unit.
- **Cost:** $200 - $1500


### Battery Drain
**Parasitic Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Battery dies unexpectedly, difficulty starting the vehicle, frequent battery replacement.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis to identify the source of the drain (e.g., faulty module, aftermarket accessory) and repair or removal.
- **Cost:** $300 - $1000

### Summary Verdict
The 2016 Bentley Continental GT demonstrates commendable reliability, particularly concerning its powerful W12 and V8 engines and the smooth ZF 8-speed automatic transmission. The primary areas where owners might encounter issues are the adaptive air suspension, with potential for leaks or compressor failures, and the complex electrical system, which can sometimes exhibit infotainment glitches or battery drain issues. These are not uncommon for high-end vehicles with advanced technology. Overall, the Continental GT is a well-built machine, and with diligent maintenance and prompt attention to any emerging problems, it offers a dependable and luxurious ownership experience.
