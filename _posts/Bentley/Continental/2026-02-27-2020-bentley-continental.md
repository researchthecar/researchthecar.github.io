## Suspension

### Adaptive Air Suspension
**Air Spring Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle may sag on one corner, uneven ride height, warning lights on the dashboard.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the faulty air spring or associated seals.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1500 per corner

**Compressor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Inability to adjust ride height, constant running of the compressor, suspension warning lights.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the air suspension compressor unit.
- **Cost:** $1000 - $2000


## Electrical

### Infotainment System
**Software Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Screen freezing, unresponsiveness, connectivity issues (Bluetooth, Apple CarPlay/Android Auto).
- **Solution:** Software updates or a full system reset. In rare cases, head unit replacement.
- **Cost:** $200 - $1500 (depending on severity and need for replacement)


### Advanced Driver-Assistance Systems (ADAS)
**Sensor Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** False alerts from adaptive cruise control, lane keeping assist, or parking sensors. System deactivation.
- **Solution:** Calibration or replacement of faulty sensors (e.g., radar, cameras).
- **Cost:** $500 - $2500 (depending on sensor type and complexity)

### Summary Verdict
The 2020 Bentley Continental GT demonstrates strong overall reliability, particularly in its core powertrain components (W12 and V8 engines, 8-speed automatic transmission). The primary areas where owners might encounter issues are related to the advanced adaptive air suspension system, which can develop leaks or compressor problems, and the sophisticated electrical systems, including the infotainment and ADAS, which are susceptible to software glitches or sensor malfunctions. These issues, while not pervasive, are typical for high-end luxury vehicles with complex integrated technologies. With proper maintenance and prompt attention to any warning signs, the Continental GT should provide a dependable and luxurious ownership experience.
