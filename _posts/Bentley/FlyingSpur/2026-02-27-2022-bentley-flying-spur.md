# 2022 Bentley Flying Spur Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2022 Bentley Flying Spur is a pinnacle of luxury and performance, offering exquisite craftsmanship alongside formidable powertrain options. For used-car buyers looking at this tier of ultra-luxury sedans, understanding its sophisticated mechanical and electrical systems is key to confident ownership. While owner-reported complaints are sparse for this model year, prospective buyers should still carefully evaluate service history and component complexity.

---

## Engine

### 4.0L Twin-Turbocharged V8

The 4.0-liter twin-turbocharged V8 engine delivers approximately 542 horsepower and 568 lb-ft of torque, offering responsive performance and a refined exhaust note. This engine balances flagship power with everyday drivability, though routine maintenance costs remain high.

### 6.0L Twin-Turbocharged W12

The flagship 6.0-liter twin-turbocharged W12 engine produces roughly 626 horsepower and 664 lb-ft of torque, providing effortlessly smooth and immense acceleration. It represents traditional ultra-luxury engineering, requiring meticulous upkeep by qualified specialists.

### 2.9L V6 Plug-In Hybrid

The 2.9-liter V6 plug-in hybrid powertrain combines a twin-turbo V6 with an electric motor to generate a total output of around 536 horsepower. This modern configuration offers silent electric driving capability alongside traditional Bentley performance.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Dual-Clutch Automatic Transmission

The 8-speed dual-clutch automatic transmission delivers rapid, seamless gear shifts across all powertrain configurations paired with an advanced all-wheel-drive system. It provides a refined driving dynamic appropriate for a high-end luxury sedan.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V975000: Equipment:Other:Owners/Service/Other Manual](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Bentley&model=Flying+Spur&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** The owner's manual incorrectly states that the air bag system will not deploy when the "Passenger Airbag On" symbol is illuminated. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 208, "Occupant Crash Protection." The incorrect information may change how occupants position themselves in relation.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 22V177000: Seat Belts:Front:Retractor](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Bentley&model=Flying+Spur&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 208, "Occupant Crash Protection." A missing ALR on a passenger seat belt may not properly secure a child restraint, increasing the risk of injury in a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and, if necessary, replace the affected seat belt, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V314000: Structure:Critical Fasteners](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Bentley&model=Flying+Spur&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** An incorrectly installed retention bracket can result in the entertainment screen becoming a projectile during a crash, increasing the risk of occupant injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the retention brackets in both front seats, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

The 2022 Bentley Flying Spur remains an exceptional ultra-luxury sedan choice for buyers seeking prestige, comfort, and advanced engineering.
