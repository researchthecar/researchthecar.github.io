# 2021 Bentley Flying Spur Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2021 Bentley Flying Spur represents the pinnacle of ultra-luxury motoring, combining exquisite craftsmanship with effortless performance. As a high-end luxury vehicle, prospective buyers should carefully evaluate its complex systems and mechanical pedigree prior to purchase.

---

## Engine

### 4.0L Twin-Turbocharged V8

This 4.0-liter twin-turbocharged V8 engine produces approximately 542 horsepower and 568 lb-ft of torque, delivering brisk acceleration with refined road manners. It offers a slightly more agile dynamic character compared to the heavier 12-cylinder variant while maintaining high standards of reliability.

### 6.0L Twin-Turbocharged W12

The flagship 6.0-liter twin-turbocharged W12 engine generates a massive 626 horsepower and 664 lb-ft of torque for seamless, turbine-like power delivery. While complex and expensive to maintain, it provides the ultimate ultra-luxury driving experience.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Dual-Clutch Automatic Transmission

This 8-speed dual-clutch automatic transmission delivers rapid, seamless gear changes suited to both relaxed cruising and spirited driving. Routine maintenance and timely fluid changes are critical to ensuring long-term durability in this high-torque application.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V975000: Equipment:Other:Owners/Service/Other Manual](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Bentley&model=Flying+Spur&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The owner's manual incorrectly states that the air bag system will not deploy when the "Passenger Airbag On" symbol is illuminated. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 208, "Occupant Crash Protection." The incorrect information may change how occupants position themselves in relation.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 22V177000: Seat Belts:Front:Retractor](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Bentley&model=Flying+Spur&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 208, "Occupant Crash Protection." A missing ALR on a passenger seat belt may not properly secure a child restraint, increasing the risk of injury in a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and, if necessary, replace the affected seat belt, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V314000: Structure:Critical Fasteners](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Bentley&model=Flying+Spur&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** An incorrectly installed retention bracket can result in the entertainment screen becoming a projectile during a crash, increasing the risk of occupant injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the retention brackets in both front seats, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2021 Bentley Flying Spur is an extraordinary machine offering unmatched luxury and performance. Used-car buyers should prioritize a comprehensive pre-purchase inspection by a marque specialist to ensure all complex mechanical and electronic systems are in optimal condition.
