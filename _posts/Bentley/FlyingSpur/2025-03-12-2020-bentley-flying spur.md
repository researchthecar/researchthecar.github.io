The 2020 Bentley Flying Spur is a luxury sedan known for its opulent interior, powerful engines, and smooth ride. For 2020, it was offered with two engine options: a 6.0-liter W12 producing 626 horsepower and 664 lb-ft of torque, and a 4.0-liter twin-turbocharged V8 producing 542 horsepower and 568 lb-ft of torque. Both engines are paired with an 8-speed dual-clutch transmission. Owners generally praise the Flying Spur for its elegant design, handcrafted interior, and exhilarating performance. Criticisms often center around its high price tag and complex electronics, which can sometimes be problematic.

**Engine**

**6.0L W12**

*   **Oil Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks may appear from various engine seals and gaskets, particularly around the valve covers or rear main seal. Symptoms include visible oil puddles under the car, a burning oil smell, and low oil levels.
    *   Solution: Replacing the leaking seals and gaskets.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $4,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**4.0L Twin-Turbocharged V8**
*   **Turbocharger Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported premature turbocharger failure or wastegate issues. Symptoms may include loss of power, unusual noises from the engine bay (whining, whistling), and diagnostic trouble codes (DTCs) related to turbocharger performance.
    *   Solution: Replacing or rebuilding the turbocharger unit.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $3,000 - $8,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**8-Speed Dual-Clutch**

*   **Hesitation or Jerking During Shifting**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have experienced hesitation, jerking, or rough shifting, particularly at lower speeds or during stop-and-go traffic. This can be caused by issues within the transmission's control unit or clutch packs.
    *   Solution: Software update, transmission calibration, or clutch pack replacement
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $5,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Air Suspension Compressor Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The air suspension compressor may fail, causing the vehicle to sit unevenly or drop to its lowest setting. Warning lights on the dashboard will also illuminate.
    *   Solution: Replacing the air suspension compressor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,500+
    * Recalls: None Found

*   **Air Strut Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Air leaks from the air struts can cause the vehicle to sag or bounce excessively. This can also damage the air suspension compressor.
    *   Solution: Replacing the leaking air strut.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $5,000+ per strut
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Freezing/Crashing**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The infotainment system may freeze, crash, or become unresponsive. This can affect navigation, audio, and climate control functions.
    *   Solution: Software update or replacement of the infotainment system module.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $4,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

*   **Battery Drain**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Excessive battery drain can cause the vehicle to fail to start or exhibit electrical malfunctions.
    *   Solution: Identifying and addressing the source of the drain, which could be a faulty module, wiring issue, or aftermarket accessory. Battery replacement
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Sensor Malfunctions (Parking Sensors, etc.)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Parking sensors or other exterior sensors may malfunction, providing inaccurate readings or failing to operate altogether.
    *   Solution: Replacing the faulty sensor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,000+ per sensor
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Sunroof/Moonroof Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The sunroof or moonroof may become stuck, leak, or make excessive noise during operation.
    *   Solution: Repairing or replacing the sunroof/moonroof motor, tracks, or seals.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2020 Bentley Flying Spur offers a luxurious and powerful driving experience, but potential buyers should be aware of the common issues reported by owners. Engine issues, particularly oil leaks in the W12 and turbocharger problems in the V8, can be expensive to repair. Suspension problems related to the air suspension system and electrical glitches within the infotainment system are also common concerns. Overall, while many issues are minor, some can be costly to address, so a thorough pre-purchase inspection is highly recommended.
