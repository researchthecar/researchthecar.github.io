## Suspension

### Adaptive Air Suspension
**Air Spring Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle sagging on one corner, loss of ride height, warning lights on the dashboard.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the faulty air spring or associated seals.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1,500 per corner


## Electrical

### Infotainment System
**Software Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Screen freezing, unresponsive touch controls, navigation system errors, Bluetooth connectivity issues.
- **Solution:** Software updates or resets. In rare cases, module replacement.
- **Cost:** $150 - $500 for software, $1,000+ for module


### Advanced Driver-Assistance Systems (ADAS)
**Sensor Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** False alerts from adaptive cruise control, lane keeping assist, or parking sensors. System deactivation.
- **Solution:** Cleaning or recalibration of sensors. Replacement of faulty sensors or control modules.
- **Cost:** $300 - $1,200 per sensor/module

### Summary Verdict
The 2022 Bentley Flying Spur demonstrates strong overall reliability, particularly in its core powertrain components (W12 and V8 engines, 8-speed automatic transmission), where no significant widespread issues have been documented. The luxury interior and exterior elements also appear robust. Potential areas for owner attention include the adaptive air suspension, where leaks can occur, and the sophisticated electrical systems, which may experience occasional software glitches or sensor malfunctions. These electrical issues are often resolvable with software updates. While costs for repairs on such a high-end vehicle can be substantial, the Flying Spur generally offers a dependable ownership experience for its segment.
