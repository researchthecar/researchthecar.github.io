## Suspension

### Adaptive Air Suspension
**Air Spring Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle may sag on one corner, uneven ride height, warning lights on the dashboard.
- **Solution:** Replacement of faulty air spring or associated seals.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1500 per corner

**Compressor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Inability to adjust ride height, constant running of compressor, suspension warning lights.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the air suspension compressor unit.
- **Cost:** $700 - $1200


## Electrical

### Infotainment System
**Software Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Screen freezing, unresponsiveness, connectivity issues (Bluetooth, Apple CarPlay/Android Auto).
- **Solution:** Software updates or system reset. In rare cases, head unit replacement.
- **Cost:** $150 - $500 for software updates/diagnostics, $2000+ for head unit replacement


### Advanced Driver-Assistance Systems (ADAS)
**Sensor Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** False warnings from adaptive cruise control, lane keeping assist, or parking sensors. System deactivation.
- **Solution:** Calibration or replacement of faulty sensors (e.g., radar, cameras).
- **Cost:** $300 - $1000 per sensor/module

### Summary Verdict
The 2021 Bentley Flying Spur demonstrates a high level of reliability, characteristic of its luxury segment. The W12 and V8 engines, along with the 8-speed automatic transmission, are robust and have not shown widespread issues. The primary areas where owners might encounter technical concerns are the advanced adaptive air suspension, which can develop leaks or compressor issues, and the sophisticated electrical systems, particularly the infotainment and ADAS, which may experience software glitches or sensor malfunctions. These issues, while not endemic, are typical for vehicles with such complex integrated technology. Overall, the Flying Spur offers a dependable ownership experience, with most potential problems being repairable at a cost commensurate with a luxury vehicle.
