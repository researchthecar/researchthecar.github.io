# 2017 Bentley Flying Spur

The 2017 Bentley Flying Spur is a luxurious sedan that offers a combination of performance, comfort, and style. It is powered by a 6.0-liter W12 engine that produces 616 horsepower and 590 lb-ft of torque. The engine is mated to an 8-speed automatic transmission. Other engine and transmission options include a 4.0-liter V8 engine with 500 horsepower and 487 lb-ft of torque, and a 6.75-liter V8 engine with 528 horsepower and 531 lb-ft of torque.

People who like the 2017 Bentley Flying Spur praise its powerful engine, luxurious interior, and smooth ride. However, some people have reported issues with the vehicle's engine, transmission, suspension, and electrical system.

**Engine**

* **6.0-liter W12 engine**
    * **Intake valve failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: The intake valves can fail, causing the engine to lose power and stall.
        * Solution: The intake valves need to be replaced.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+
        * Recalls: None Found
* **4.0-liter V8 engine**
    * **Turbocharger failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: The turbochargers can fail, causing the engine to lose power and produce excessive smoke.
        * Solution: The turbochargers need to be replaced.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $3,000 - $5,000+
        * Recalls: None Found
* **6.75-liter V8 engine**
    * **No Issues Found**

**Transmission**

* **8-speed automatic transmission**
    * **Transmission slipping**
        * Description/Symptoms: The transmission may slip, causing the vehicle to hesitate or jerk when shifting gears.
        * Solution: The transmission may need to be rebuilt or replaced.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $3,000 - $5,000+
        * Recalls: Recall number: 18V-199
    * **Torque converter failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: The torque converter may fail, causing the transmission to lose power and the vehicle to stall.
        * Solution: The torque converter needs to be replaced.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,000+
        * Recalls: Recall number: 18V-199

**Suspension**

* **Air suspension failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The air suspension may fail, causing the vehicle to sag or ride rough.
    * Solution: The air suspension components need to be replaced.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Battery drain**
    * Description/Symptoms: The battery may drain quickly, even when the vehicle is not in use.
    * Solution: The electrical system needs to be inspected to identify and repair the source of the drain.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Alternator failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The alternator may fail, causing the battery to not charge properly.
    * Solution: The alternator needs to be replaced.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Chrome trim peeling**
    * Description/Symptoms: The chrome trim on the vehicle may peel or flake off over time.
    * Solution: The chrome trim needs to be replaced.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Center console creaking**
    * Description/Symptoms: The center console may creak or rattle when driving over bumps or rough roads.
    * Solution: The center console needs to be disassembled and lubricated or tightened.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2017 Bentley Flying Spur is a luxurious sedan that offers a combination of performance, comfort, and style. However, some people have reported issues with the vehicle's engine, transmission, suspension, and electrical system. The severity of the issues varies, and the cost to fix them can be high. Overall, the Flying Spur is a reliable vehicle with a high level of customer satisfaction.
