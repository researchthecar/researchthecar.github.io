Here is a comprehensive list of common issues and complaints associated with the 2004 Bentley Arnage, categorized for clarity.

The 2004 Bentley Arnage is a luxury sedan known for its handcrafted interior, powerful engine, and opulent features. The primary engine option for this model year is a 6.75-liter turbocharged V8 engine, producing around 450 horsepower and 645 lb-ft of torque. A less common, but available, engine was a naturally aspirated 6.75-liter V8. This engine was rated at 400 horsepower and 616 lb-ft of torque. Both engines were paired with a 4-speed automatic transmission. Owners generally appreciate the car's smooth ride, lavish interior, and distinctive styling. Criticisms often revolve around its high maintenance costs, potential for electrical gremlins, and fuel consumption.

**Engine**

**6.75L Turbocharged V8**

*   **Turbocharger Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Reduced engine power, excessive smoke from the exhaust, whining noise from the turbocharger area.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the turbocharger.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $5,000 - $10,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Coolant Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Coolant loss, overheating, visible leaks around the engine block or radiator. Commonly the coolant pipes leading to and from the turbochargers fail.
    *   Solution: Replace faulty hoses, pipes, or radiator.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Oil Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Oil spots under the car, burning oil smell, low oil level. Common areas include valve cover gaskets, rear main seal, and oil cooler lines.
    *   Solution: Replace gaskets or seals causing the leak.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $2,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**6.75L Naturally Aspirated V8**

* **Camshaft Wear**
    * Description/Symptoms: Ticking noise from the engine, rough idling, reduced performance.
    * Solution: Replacing camshaft and lifters.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $3,000 - $6,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**4-Speed Automatic**

*   **Transmission Slippage**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Hesitation or delay when shifting gears, the engine revving high without a corresponding increase in speed.
    *   Solution: Complete transmission rebuild or replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $3,000 - $8,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Harsh Shifting**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rough or jerky gear changes, especially when shifting between first and second gear.
    *   Solution: Valve body replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Air Suspension Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The car sitting low on one or more corners, failure to maintain ride height, error messages on the dashboard.
    *   Solution: Replacement of faulty air struts, compressor, or valve block.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $6,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Leaking Air Struts**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Hissing sound from the suspension, car sinking overnight, uneven ride height.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the leaking air strut(s).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+ per strut
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Battery Drain Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Car failing to start, needing frequent jump starts, a rapid discharge of the battery.
    *   Solution: Diagnose and repair parasitic drains from faulty modules or accessories.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Central Locking System Malfunctions**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Doors not locking or unlocking correctly, remote key fob not working, alarm system malfunctioning.
    *   Solution: Replacement of door lock actuators or central locking module.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,000+ per door
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Sensor Failures**
    * Description/Symptoms: Check engine light, ABS light, airbag light illumination.
    * Solution: Replace faulty sensors
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+ per sensor
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Rust Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rust forming around the lower body panels, wheel wells, and other areas prone to moisture.
    *   Solution: Repair and repaint affected areas.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $5,000+ depending on the severity
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Headlight/Taillight Condensation**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Moisture buildup inside headlight or taillight lenses.
    *   Solution: Removal and resealing of the lens or replacement of the entire unit.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $2,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Climate Control Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The AC blowing warm air, the heater not working, inconsistent temperature control, noisy blower motor.
    *   Solution: Recharge AC system, replace faulty compressor, heater core, or blower motor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $2,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Navigation System Malfunctions**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The navigation screen freezing, the system not recognizing the location, outdated maps.
    *   Solution: Software update or replacement of the navigation unit.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Seat Motor Problems**
    * Description/Symptoms: The seat not moving in certain directions, strange noises when adjusting seats, inoperative seat functions.
    * Solution: Repair or replace seat motor
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+ per seat
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2004 Bentley Arnage, while a luxurious and powerful vehicle, is prone to a range of issues that can be costly to repair. Engine issues such as turbocharger failures and oil leaks, along with transmission slippage, are major concerns. The air suspension system can be problematic, and electrical gremlins are common. Interior components like the climate control and navigation system can also fail. These issues can range from moderate to severe and can result in repair bills ranging from several hundred to thousands of dollars, making careful inspection and preventative maintenance crucial for prospective owners.
