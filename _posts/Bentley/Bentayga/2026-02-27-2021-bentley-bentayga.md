## Suspension

### Adaptive Air Suspension
**Air Spring Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle sagging on one or more corners, uneven ride height, compressor running excessively.
- **Solution:** Replacement of leaking air spring or associated lines.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1500


## Electrical

### Infotainment System
**Software Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Screen freezing, unresponsiveness, connectivity issues (Bluetooth, Apple CarPlay/Android Auto).
- **Solution:** Software update or system reset. In rare cases, module replacement.
- **Cost:** $200 - $1000


### Battery Drain
**Parasitic Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Battery dying unexpectedly, requiring jump starts.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis to identify the source of the drain (e.g., faulty module, aftermarket accessory) and repair.
- **Cost:** $300 - $800

### Summary Verdict
The 2021 Bentley Bentayga demonstrates a high level of overall reliability, particularly concerning its core powertrain components across all engine and transmission options. The V8, W12, and hybrid powertrains, along with the 8-speed automatic transmission, are robust and have not shown significant widespread issues. The primary areas where minor concerns have been noted are the adaptive air suspension, with potential for air spring leaks, and the electrical system, which can occasionally experience infotainment glitches or parasitic battery drain. These issues, while not endemic, are typical for complex luxury vehicles and generally have manageable repair costs. The exterior and interior components are largely free from reported defects, reflecting Bentley's commitment to quality materials and construction. Owners should be aware of the potential for suspension and electrical gremlins, but overall, the Bentayga offers a dependable luxury SUV experience.
