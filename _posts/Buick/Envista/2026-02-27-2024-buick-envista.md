# 2024 Buick Envista Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2024 Buick Envista introduces a stylish, coupe-inspired crossover profile to the entry-level market, attracting buyers with its sleek design and budget-friendly price point. As a relatively new addition to the automotive landscape, used shoppers should weigh its striking aesthetics against early production growing pains. While many owners enjoy its modern interior layout and efficient daily driving dynamics, early feedback highlights some recurring electronic glitches and design quirks worth noting before making a purchase.

---

## Engine

### 1.2L Turbocharged I3

The Envista is powered by a 1.2-liter turbocharged inline-three engine producing approximately 137 horsepower and 162 lb-ft of torque. It provides adequate power for daily commuting with a strong focus on fuel efficiency, though buyers should expect modest acceleration under heavy loads.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

Power is routed to the front wheels via a conventional 6-speed automatic transmission. This setup offers familiar shifting characteristics compared to the CVTs frequently found in this class, supporting straightforward long-term drivability.

## Suspension

### Rear Suspension

The rear suspension setup handles daily driving adequately but can feel harsh for passengers occupying the rear seats over rough surfaces.

**Stiff Rear Ride Quality**
- **Symptoms:** Rear-seat passengers may experience discomfort or a jarring ride over small bumps and road irregularities due to firm rear suspension tuning.
- **Solution:** Inspect rear shocks and springs during a test drive to ensure they match personal comfort expectations.

## Brakes

### Brake Light Positioning

Owner feedback highlights concerns regarding visibility and positioning of exterior rear lighting components.

**Low-Mounted Brake Lights**
- **Symptoms:** The primary brake lights are positioned low on the bumper rather than within the upper taillight assemblies, which some drivers feel makes them less noticeable to traffic behind.
- **Solution:** No standard hardware fix is available, as this is part of the vehicle's factory exterior lighting design.

## Electrical

### Instrument Panel and Infotainment Display

Issues related to screen blackouts and software glitches in the virtual cockpit and infotainment units have been reported by early owners.

**Display Screen Blackout**
- **Symptoms:** The digital instrument cluster and infotainment screens intermittently go completely blank or black out during vehicle startup or while driving.
- **Solution:** Software updates or module resets performed by a dealership can resolve the glitch.

---

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V744000: Electrical System: Instrument Cluster/Panel](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Buick&model=Envista&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** The software in the Virtual Cockpit Unit Module (VCU) may cause the instrument panel display to intermittently go blank at vehicle startup or while driving. A blank instrument panel will not show critical information, such as vehicle speed and warning lights, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2024 Buick Envista presents an appealing used option for buyers seeking modern styling and efficient performance on a budget. While early owner reports point to manageable software glitches and unique exterior lighting design choices, checking for completed software updates can help ensure a smoother ownership experience. Prospective buyers should thoroughly test drive the vehicle to verify that infotainment systems and ride comfort meet their expectations.
