The 2024 Buick Envista is a stylish and affordable subcompact SUV that offers a compelling blend of value and design. It comes equipped with a 1.2-liter turbocharged inline-3 engine producing 137 horsepower and 162 lb-ft of torque, paired exclusively with a 6-speed automatic transmission. Many owners appreciate its sleek, coupe-like styling and comfortable ride. Criticisms often center around the engine's modest power, particularly when fully loaded or climbing hills, and some find the interior materials to be somewhat basic.

**Engine**

**1.2L Turbocharged Inline-3 (LIH)**

*   **Engine Hesitation/Stalling**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners report experiencing hesitation or stalling during acceleration, particularly when the engine is cold or under load. This may be related to fuel delivery issues, turbocharger lag, or sensor malfunctions.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis by a qualified mechanic is recommended. Potential fixes include cleaning or replacing fuel injectors, replacing the mass airflow sensor, or addressing turbocharger issues.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: Diagnosis $100 - $200, repairs vary significantly depending on the cause (fuel injectors $200-$500, mass airflow sensor $100-$300, turbocharger issues $500-$2000+).
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Excessive Vibration**
    * Description/Symptoms: Some owners have complained about excessive engine vibration, especially at idle. This may be due to issues with engine mounts or imbalances within the engine itself.
    * Solution: Inspect engine mounts for wear or damage. If the mounts are in good condition, a mechanic should investigate potential internal engine imbalances.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: Engine mount replacement $150-$400, internal engine issues diagnosis $100-$200+, repairs vary greatly depending on the cause.
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**6-Speed Automatic (6T40)**

*   **Harsh Shifting**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported experiencing harsh or jerky shifting, particularly between certain gears. This may be indicative of issues with the transmission's valve body or internal components.
    *   Solution: A transmission fluid flush and filter change may resolve minor issues. More serious cases may require valve body replacement or transmission rebuild.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: Transmission fluid flush and filter change $150-$300, valve body replacement $800-$1500, transmission rebuild $2500-$4000.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **No Issues Found**

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Freezing/Crashing**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Several owners have noted instances of the infotainment system freezing, crashing, or exhibiting unresponsive behavior. This can affect access to navigation, audio, and other vehicle functions.
    *   Solution: Software updates may resolve some issues. In more severe cases, the infotainment unit may need to be reset or replaced.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: Software update (covered under warranty or minimal charge), infotainment unit replacement $500-$1500.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Malfunctioning Driver Assist Features**
    * Description/Symptoms: Complaints have surfaced regarding the unreliable performance of driver-assist features such as lane keep assist and automatic emergency braking. These features may provide inaccurate warnings or fail to activate when needed.
    * Solution: Check and recalibrate the sensors used by the driver-assist systems. If the issue persists, the sensors or the system's control module may need to be replaced.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: Sensor recalibration $100-$300, sensor replacement $200-$500+, control module replacement $500-$1000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **No Issues Found**

**Interior**

*   **Rattling/Buzzing Noises**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported experiencing rattling or buzzing noises emanating from various interior components, such as the dashboard, door panels, or center console.
    *   Solution: Identify the source of the noise and secure or insulate the affected components. This may involve tightening fasteners, adding foam padding, or replacing loose clips.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50-$200+ depending on the complexity of the repair.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2024 Buick Envista, while praised for its style and value, does present some potential issues. The most concerning seem to be the hesitation and vibration with the 1.2L engine, along with harsh shifting from the 6-speed transmission. Electrical gremlins with the infotainment system and driver-assist features also appear. The severity of these issues varies, and the cost to repair can range from minor fixes to more substantial expenses, making thorough inspection and potentially a pre-purchase inspection advisable.
