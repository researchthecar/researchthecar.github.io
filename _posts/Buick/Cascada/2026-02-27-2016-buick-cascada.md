# 2016 Buick Cascada Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2016 Buick Cascada is a stylish, four-passenger convertible offering a comfortable cruising experience and a smooth ride. While it delivers open-air enjoyment at an attractive price point on the used market, potential buyers should carefully evaluate top wear and minor powertrain quirks before purchasing.

---

## Engine

### 1.6L Turbocharged I4

Powered by a 1.6-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine producing around 200 horsepower and 207 lb-ft of torque, this option provides adequate power for daily cruising. Overall engine reliability is generally respectable, though routine checks of boosted systems and related plumbing are advised.

**PCV Valve and Deceleration Issues**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may develop an abnormal squealing sound from the engine bay or experience unexpected deceleration and stalling.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified mechanic inspect the PCV system and throttle components, replacing failed valves or sensors as needed.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

Paired exclusively with front-wheel drive, the conventional six-speed automatic transmission delivers smooth gear shifts suited for relaxed touring. It maintains a solid reputation for longevity provided regular fluid maintenance has been performed.

## Exterior

### Convertible Top and Rear Window

The exterior features a power-operated soft top that requires careful inspection for fabric wear and seal integrity during pre-purchase evaluations.

**Rear Window Separation**
- **Symptoms:** The glass rear window begins to pull away or detach entirely from the convertible top fabric, potentially allowing water to leak into the cabin.
- **Solution:** Professional replacement of the convertible top assembly may be required, as temporary adhesive fixes often fail over time.

## Safety

### Seat Belt Presenter

Safety equipment generally performs well, though convenience mechanisms like the front seat belt presenters can experience mechanical wear.

**Seat Belt Motor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The automatic seat belt extender arm fails to present the belt upon entry and may produce a grinding or mechanical noise.
- **Solution:** Replace the faulty seat belt presenter motor assembly through a dealership or specialized repair shop.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V844000: Visibility:Power Window Devices And Controls](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Buick&model=Cascada&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles no longer meet the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 118, "Power-Operated Window Systems." If the windows close automatically or can be closed remotely in ways that do not meet all the requirements of FMVSS 118, the unexpected closure can increase the risk of injury to the passengers.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will replace the CRCM with correct module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2016 Buick Cascada is a compelling option for buyers seeking an affordable convertible cruiser, provided the soft top and rear window assembly are thoroughly inspected for separation. While routine mechanical and electrical systems are generally dependable, addressing minor accessory glitches and verifying top condition will help ensure a satisfying ownership experience.
