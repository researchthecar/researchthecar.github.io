# 2019 Buick Cascada Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2019 Buick Cascada offers an open-air cruising experience with a turbocharged four-cylinder engine and a traditional automatic transmission. While generally comfortable on the road, prospective buyers should inspect the convertible top and rear window assembly closely, as owner reports highlight potential structural separation issues.

---

## Engine

### 1.6L Turbocharged I4

This 1.6-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine produces approximately 200 horsepower and 207 lb-ft of torque. It provides adequate power for daily cruising, though buyers should monitor general fluid levels and turbo maintenance history.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

Equipped with a conventional 6-speed automatic transmission paired with front-wheel drive. It delivers smooth shifts for highway cruising, though proper fluid changes remain essential for long-term durability.

## Exterior

### Convertible Top

Issues related to the folding top mechanism and weather seals.

**Rear Window Separation**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have reported that the rear glass window begins to push upward, detach, or separate from the surrounding convertible top material and frame.
- **Solution:** May require professional upholstery repair or complete replacement of the convertible top assembly depending on the severity of the detachment.

## Summary Verdict

When shopping for a used Buick Cascada, pay close attention to the condition of the convertible top and rear window seals. Thoroughly test all power top functions and check for any signs of water leaks or material separation before purchasing.
