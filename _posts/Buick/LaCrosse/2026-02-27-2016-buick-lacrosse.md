# 2016 Buick LaCrosse Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2016 Buick LaCrosse is a comfortable and well-appointed full-size sedan, offering a quiet ride and robust feature list. However, prospective buyers should review certain recurring owner-reported complaints regarding electronic steering assist, transmission behavior, and auxiliary systems. Understanding these potential trouble spots helps ensure a thorough pre-purchase inspection.

---

## Engine

### 2.4L 4-Cylinder eAssist

The 2.4-liter mild-hybrid eAssist 4-cylinder engine produces 182 horsepower, prioritizing fuel efficiency over spirited acceleration. It generally provides dependable daily service, though minor emissions sensors can occasionally trigger warning lights.

**Evaporative Emissions Leak**
- **Symptoms:** Illuminated check engine light accompanied by diagnostic trouble code P0442 indicating a small leak in the EVAP system.
- **Solution:** Inspect fuel cap, hoses, and purge valves; replace faulty emission components as diagnosed.

### 3.6L V6

The 3.6-liter V6 engine generates 304 horsepower, offering smooth and confident power delivery for highway cruising and passing. This powerplant is generally robust, making it the preferred option for buyers seeking traditional performance.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 6-speed automatic transmission routes power smoothly to front-wheel or all-wheel drive models under normal conditions. Buyers should test-drive thoroughly to check for hesitation, harsh engagement, or shuddering at low speeds.

**Shudder and Harsh Shifting**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle shudders or jerks violently between 20-30 mph or during deceleration, occasionally causing uncomfortable hesitation when coming to a stop.
- **Solution:** Perform a diagnostic evaluation of transmission components, fluid condition, and software calibration; mechanical repairs or transmission replacement may be required in severe cases.

## Steering

### Electronic Power Steering

The electric power assist steering system provides light effort at low speeds and firm road feel on the highway, though some drivers report intermittent assistance failures.

**Steering Assist Loss**
- **Symptoms:** Sudden stiffness in the steering wheel or complete loss of power steering functionality while driving, accompanied by a service power steering or check engine warning light.
- **Solution:** Inspect the electronic power steering module, wiring harnesses, and steering gear; replace failed components as needed.

## Exterior

### Trunk and Hood Latches

Interior convenience features and latches generally hold up well, though mechanical latch failures can occasionally cause cargo lids to open unexpectedly.

**Unintended Latch Release**
- **Symptoms:** Trunk lid or front hood unlatches or opens unexpectedly while parked or driving, potentially obscuring driver visibility.
- **Solution:** Inspect latch mechanisms for wear or mechanical failure and replace broken trunk latches or hood locking components.

### Mirrors and Door Locks

Electrical accessories provide modern convenience, but minor sensor and switch glitches can occasionally require module resets or component replacements.

**Power Mirror and Lock Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Side power mirrors randomly change position by themselves upon vehicle startup, or doors unlock unexpectedly when hitting road bumps.
- **Solution:** Inspect mirror memory modules, switch assemblies, and door lock wiring for loose connections or software glitches.

## Safety

### Air Bag System

The occupant restraint and airbag systems are vital safety components designed to protect passengers in a collision, requiring prompt attention if warning indicators appear.

**Air Bag Warning Light**
- **Symptoms:** Air bag warning light illuminates intermittently while driving, indicating a potential fault in the supplemental restraint system.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified technician scan the airbag control module for diagnostic codes and inspect driver-side or occupant sensor connections.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V651000: Air Bags:Frontal](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Buick&model=LaCrosse&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** In the affected vehicles, certain driving conditions may cause the air bag sensing and diagnostic module (SDM) software to activate a diagnostic test. During this test, deployment of the frontal air bags and the seat belt pretensioners would not occur in the event of a crash.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will reflash the SDM software. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V437000: Air Bags:Frontal](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Buick&model=LaCrosse&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** While being previously remedied for recall 16V-651, the affected vehicles may not have received the complete software update necessary to remedy the recall condition. Without the update, certain driving conditions may cause the air bag sensing and diagnostic module (SDM) software to activate a diagnostic test.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will reflash the SDM software. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2016 Buick LaCrosse offers a refined driving experience, quiet cabin, and a choice of efficient or powerful engines. While overall comfort is high, used car buyers should pay close attention during test drives to verify smooth transmission shifting and proper electronic power steering operation. A comprehensive pre-purchase inspection can catch any lingering electrical or mechanical issues before purchase.
