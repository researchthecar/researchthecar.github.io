# 2019 Buick LaCrosse Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2019 Buick LaCrosse delivers a refined and quiet ride, representing the final model year for this storied nameplate in North America. Used car shoppers will find a spacious cabin and comfortable cruising manners, though a handful of owner-reported electronic and powertrain quirks warrant a careful inspection before purchase.

---

## Engine

### 2.5L 4-Cylinder with eAssist

The 2.5-liter mild-hybrid eAssist 4-cylinder engine pairs electric assist with gasoline power for optimized efficiency. It provides modest performance suitable for daily commuting and relaxed highway cruising.

### 3.6L V6

The 3.6-liter V6 engine produces approximately 310 horsepower and 282 lb-ft of torque, offering strong, smooth acceleration. It serves as the primary powerplant for most LaCrosse models, though buyers should monitor for occasional electronic and throttle response reports.

**Acceleration Delay**
- **Symptoms:** When pressing the accelerator pedal to merge or accelerate, the vehicle temporarily fails to respond until the pedal is pumped repeatedly.
- **Solution:** Consult a qualified technician for diagnostic evaluation, as owners report that initial manufacturer fixes may not be immediately available.

**Cooling Fan Run-On**
- **Symptoms:** The engine cooling fan continues to run continuously even after the vehicle has been turned off.
- **Solution:** Inspect the cooling fan relay and temperature sensors, and replace faulty electrical components as needed.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic (eAssist)

This 6-speed automatic transmission is paired exclusively with the 2.5L eAssist powertrain to balance efficiency and smooth power delivery.

### 9-Speed Automatic

The 9-speed automatic transmission pairs with the 3.6L V6 engine to deliver seamless gear selection and highway efficiency.

**Powertrain Power Loss**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may unexpectedly lose power while driving, triggering multiple warning indicators such as Service Engine and Service Transmission.
- **Solution:** Have the vehicle scanned for diagnostic trouble codes to identify potential powertrain control module or transmission sensor faults.

## Electrical

### Electronic Shifter Mechanism

Owner reports indicate occasional electronic gear selector anomalies that require careful verification during a pre-purchase test drive.

**Unintended Park Engagement**
- **Symptoms:** The electronic shift mechanism may unexpectedly shift the vehicle into park while traveling at low speeds without driver input.
- **Solution:** Inspect the electronic shifter assembly and check for relevant software updates or hardware faults.

### Auto Stop-Start System

Electrical and accessory systems generally perform well, though minor component noises during frequent stop-start cycling have been noted by owners.

**Starter Grinding Noise**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle produces a grinding noise when the auto stop/start system disengages as the vehicle comes to a stop.
- **Solution:** Inspect the starter motor and associated stop-start engagement components for premature wear or alignment issues.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V668000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Buick&model=LaCrosse&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** Missing bolts on the start-stop accumulator endcap could result in a transmission oil leak and may progress to a loss of propulsion, increasing the risk of a crash. A transmission fluid leak in the presence of an ignition source may increase the risk of fire.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the start-stop transmission accumulator and replace it if any bolts are missing, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V576000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Foundation Components:Disc:Caliper](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Buick&model=LaCrosse&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** A reduction of braking performance can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will bleed the vehicle's brake system, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2019 Buick LaCrosse offers a premium driving experience and solid comfort for used car buyers. While isolated owner complaints highlight potential throttle response, cooling fan, and electronic shifter concerns, a thorough test drive and professional pre-purchase inspection can help ensure a reliable ownership experience.
