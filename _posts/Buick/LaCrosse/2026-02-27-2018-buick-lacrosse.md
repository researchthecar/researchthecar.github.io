# 2018 Buick LaCrosse Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2018 Buick LaCrosse, buyers will find a refined and comfortable full-size sedan that blends quiet cruising with modern technology. While owners generally praise its ride quality and styling, a review of owner feedback reveals a few recurring electrical and powertrain complaints that are worth noting during your pre-purchase inspection.

---

## Engine

### 2.5L 4-Cylinder eAssist Mild Hybrid

The 2.5-liter four-cylinder engine paired with the eAssist mild-hybrid system generates approximately 194 horsepower, offering strong fuel efficiency for daily commuting. It provides a smooth, economical power delivery suited for drivers prioritizing efficiency over aggressive acceleration.

### 3.6L V6

The robust 3.6-liter V6 engine produces 310 horsepower and 282 lb-ft of torque, delivering brisk acceleration and confident highway passing power. It is generally dependable, though occasional cooling system component failures like thermostats can occur.

**Thermostat Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The engine may run cold while dual electric fans run constantly, and trouble code P0128 is triggered.
- **Solution:** Inspect and replace the faulty thermostat assembly.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic (eAssist)

This 6-speed automatic transmission is paired exclusively with the eAssist mild-hybrid powertrain, delivering seamless gear changes optimized for fuel economy and smooth city driving.

### 9-Speed Automatic

The 9-speed automatic transmission works alongside the V6 engine to provide responsive shifting and efficient highway cruising. Maintenance history should be verified to ensure smooth operation across all gears.

**Reduced Engine Power and Acceleration Hesitation**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may struggle to accelerate from a complete stop, displaying a reduced engine power warning while gear indicators flash.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified mechanic inspect the transmission control module, throttle connections, and related powertrain software for faults.

## Electrical

### Keyless Access and Ignition

Owner reports indicate occasional glitches with key recognition and vehicle starting systems.

**Key Fob and Starting Failures**
- **Symptoms:** Key fobs may intermittently fail to unlock doors or allow the vehicle to be restarted, even after battery replacement.
- **Solution:** Diagnose the vehicle's receiver module and key fob programming, and replace components if hardware failure is confirmed.

### Battery and Power Management

Electrical system complaints occasionally highlight intermittent starting and power management concerns.

**Unexpected Vehicle Shutdown**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may shut down unexpectedly during operation despite having a replaced battery.
- **Solution:** Perform a thorough diagnostic check of the charging system, wiring harnesses, and update control modules as needed.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V668000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Buick&model=LaCrosse&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** Missing bolts on the start-stop accumulator endcap could result in a transmission oil leak and may progress to a loss of propulsion, increasing the risk of a crash. A transmission fluid leak in the presence of an ignition source may increase the risk of fire.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the start-stop transmission accumulator and replace it if any bolts are missing, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V576000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Foundation Components:Disc:Caliper](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Buick&model=LaCrosse&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** A reduction of braking performance can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will bleed the vehicle's brake system, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V358000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Buick&model=LaCrosse&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** A damaged fuel line can create a fuel leak, increasing the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will replace the high pressure fuel pump, and high pressure fuel pipe, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2018 Buick LaCrosse stands out as a comfortable, well-appointed luxury sedan with strong engine options. While most owners enjoy trouble-free driving, prospective buyers should thoroughly test all electrical features, key fobs, and powertrain responsiveness during a pre-purchase test drive to ensure everything operates smoothly.
