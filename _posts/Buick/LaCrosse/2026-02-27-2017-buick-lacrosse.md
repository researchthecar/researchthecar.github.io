# 2017 Buick LaCrosse Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2017 Buick LaCrosse delivers a smooth, quiet ride and a refined cabin, making it an appealing option for used-car buyers seeking full-size comfort. However, prospective owners should review potential powertrain and electrical quirks reported by drivers, particularly concerning transmission operation and intermittent warning indicators.

---

## Engine

### 3.6L V6 Engine

The 3.6L V6 engine produces approximately 310 horsepower and 282 lb-ft of torque, providing brisk acceleration for the LaCrosse. It is generally regarded as a robust powerplant, though isolated owner reports mention unexpected electronic throttle or warning light activation.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 8-speed automatic transmission pairs with the V6 engine to optimize highway efficiency. While generally smooth, some owners have experienced troublesome shifting behaviors and transmission control warnings.

**Harsh Shifting and Service Warnings**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have reported abrupt, hard shifting, severe jerking during auto-stop events, and sudden 'Service Transmission' warning messages.
- **Solution:** Reprogramming or servicing the transmission control module, or addressing internal component wear at a qualified service center.

## Suspension

### Front Suspension and Struts

The suspension is tuned for comfort, but certain components like bushings and struts can experience wear or noise over time.

**Abnormal Noises and Shuddering**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may hear loud stomping or knocking noises from the front wheel wells during turns, accompanied by wheel shuddering or increased steering effort.
- **Solution:** Inspect suspension bushings, strut assemblies, and control arms for wear or failure.

## Safety

### Audio and Warning Chime Amplifier

Electrical architecture supports advanced infotainment and safety alerts, though select owners experience component failures such as localized amplifier dropouts.

**Amplifier Failure Affecting Chimes**
- **Symptoms:** The sound system amplifier fails, causing all cabin audio—including seatbelt reminders, turn signal clicks, and safety warning chimes—to stop working.
- **Solution:** Inspect and replace the faulty audio amplifier unit.

### Airbag System

Safety systems generally perform reliably, though intermittent sensor or wiring faults can illuminate warning lights that require professional diagnosis.

**Intermittent Airbag Warning Light**
- **Symptoms:** The airbag warning light illuminates intermittently on the dashboard, sometimes triggered when turning the steering wheel.
- **Solution:** Diagnostic scanning and potential repair of clockspring or wiring harness connections in the steering column.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V116000: Steering:Electric Power Assist System](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Buick&model=LaCrosse&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** The electrical connectors for the Electronic Power Steering (EPS) system may not be properly sealed. A loss of power steering may result in increased manual steering effort, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will inspect correct the seal seating and secure the EPS connector seals, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V267000: Suspension:Rear](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Buick&model=LaCrosse&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** If a rear-suspension toe-link disconnects, it may cause a loss of vehicle control, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the left and right rear suspension toe-link assemblies, correcting their installation as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2017 Buick LaCrosse offers a comfortable, upscale driving experience for used-car buyers, but shoppers should thoroughly test drive the vehicle to check for transmission harshness or shifting delays. A pre-purchase inspection focusing on the transmission, front suspension bushings, and electrical accessories is recommended to catch potential issues early.
