# 2020 Buick Regal Sportback Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2020 Buick Regal Sportback is a sleek, European-designed hatchback offering versatile cargo space and a comfortable ride. As shoppers look at this model on the used market, understanding its overall mechanical and electrical behavior is key to a confident purchase. While owner-reported complaints are relatively sparse, checking specific comfort and convenience systems during a test drive remains a smart precaution.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged 4-Cylinder

This 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine produces around 250 horsepower and 295 lb-ft of torque, providing brisk acceleration. It serves as the primary powerplant for the lineup and generally offers solid daily reliability when properly maintained.

## Transmission

### 9-Speed Automatic

The 9-speed automatic transmission channels power smoothly to the front wheels or available all-wheel-drive systems. It helps optimize highway fuel economy while maintaining responsive gear selection.

### 8-Speed Automatic

Paired specifically with front-wheel-drive configurations, this 8-speed automatic provides crisp shifts. It contributes to balanced performance and standard everyday drivability.

## Suspension

### Tire Pressure Monitoring System

Suspension components and monitoring systems hold up well over time, though routine tire and sensor maintenance is always advised.

**TPMS Warning Light Activation**
- **Symptoms:** The tire pressure monitoring system warning light may illuminate while driving at highway speeds, indicating potential pressure drops in factory-equipped tires.
- **Solution:** Inspect tire pressures manually, check for slow leaks or tread damage, and reset or recalibrate the TPMS sensors as necessary.

## Exterior

### Proximity Door Unlock System

Electrical accessories and convenience features generally function well, though occasional owner reports highlight minor keyless entry quirks.

**Intermittent Keyless Entry Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The proximity door unlock feature may intermittently fail to recognize the key fob even when it is in close range, requiring manual unlocking via the fob.
- **Solution:** Diagnostic checks of the key fob signal and vehicle receiver modules can help isolate whether the issue stems from interference or a faulty sensor.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V762000: Seats](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Buick&model=Regal&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** Seats with improper welds may not provide adequate protection in the event of a crash, increasing the risk of an injury.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the crossbar welds on the front seats. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V465000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Power Assist:Vacuum](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Buick&model=Regal&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** 135, "Light vehicle brake systems." A loss of power brake assist can extend the distance required to stop the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the EBCM software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

Overall, standard pre-purchase due diligence will help ensure you find a reliable and well-maintained example.
