# 2018 Buick Regal Sportback Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2018 Buick Regal Sportback, buyers will find a stylish and versatile hatchback offering European driving dynamics. While generally comfortable and well-equipped, owners have reported certain recurring mechanical and electrical concerns, particularly regarding braking systems and powertrain electronics. Reviewing these common owner complaints can help prospective buyers perform targeted inspections and make an informed purchasing decision.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4 (250 hp)

The standard 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine produces approximately 250 horsepower and 295 lb-ft of torque (or 260 lb-ft with AWD), providing brisk acceleration for daily driving. It pairs with either front-wheel drive or all-wheel drive, though buyers should listen for any unusual turbocharger noises or check engine light triggers during a test drive.

## Transmission

### 9-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 9-speed automatic transmission comes paired with front-wheel-drive models, offering smooth and efficient highway cruising. Regular fluid maintenance is recommended to ensure long-term shifting refinement and durability.

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

Found on all-wheel-drive variants, this 8-speed automatic delivers power to all four wheels. Prospective buyers should test for crisp, hesitation-free engagement across all gears during a thorough pre-purchase inspection.

### Shift-to-Park and Start-Stop Electronics

electrical issues involving gear selector sensors and automatic start-stop integration can occasionally cause convenience and battery drain issues.

**Shift-to-Park Message and Engine Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** The dashboard displays a 'Shift to Park' warning even when the vehicle is in park, which can drain the battery, or the engine may occasionally stall when coming to a stop.
- **Solution:** Inspect the gear shifter assembly microswitches and battery health; faulty shifter sensors often require switch replacement.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes and Brake Assist

Braking performance is vital for safety, and owners have noted instances of reduced assist and stiff pedal feels that warrant close inspection.

**Brake Pedal Stiffness and Vacuum Loss**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have reported unexpectedly firm brake pedals, increased stopping distances, or sudden loss of brake assist function.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified mechanic inspect the electronic brake assist system, vacuum pump, and brake lines for potential pressure losses.

## Interior

### Power Seats

Cabin electronics and power adjustments generally hold up well, though mechanical track jams can occur on heavily used driver seats.

**Driver Seat Adjustment Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The front driver's seat becomes stuck and fails to move forward or backward.
- **Solution:** Inspect the seat track mechanisms and metal frame; damaged frames or track motors may need to be replaced.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V576000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Foundation Components:Disc:Caliper](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Buick&model=Regal&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** A reduction of braking performance can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will bleed the vehicle's brake system, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V230000: Seat Belts:Rear/Other](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Buick&model=Regal&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** If the seat belt fails to adequately restrain the passengers in a crash, it can increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the rear outboard seat belts, correcting the routing of the seat belts as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V465000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Power Assist:Vacuum](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Buick&model=Regal&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** 135, "Light vehicle brake systems." A loss of power brake assist can extend the distance required to stop the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the EBCM software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2018 Buick Regal Sportback is an appealing choice for used-car buyers looking for European hatchback utility and smooth performance. While most mechanical systems prove reliable, shoppers should pay special attention to brake pedal feel, electronic shifter messages, and battery health during a pre-purchase inspection to ensure a trouble-free ownership experience.
