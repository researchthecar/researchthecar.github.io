**1996 Buick LeSabre**

The 1996 Buick LeSabre is a full-size sedan that was available with a variety of engine and transmission options. The base engine was a 3.8L V6 that produced 190 horsepower and 230 lb-ft of torque. This engine was paired with a 4-speed automatic transmission. Other engine options included a 3.8L supercharged V6 that produced 240 horsepower and 280 lb-ft of torque, and a 4.6L V8 that produced 270 horsepower and 290 lb-ft of torque. The 3.8L supercharged V6 was paired with a 4-speed automatic transmission, while the 4.6L V8 was paired with a 4-speed automatic transmission or a 5-speed manual transmission.

The LeSabre was praised for its spacious interior, comfortable ride, and powerful engines. However, some owners have reported issues with the vehicle's engine, transmission, suspension, and electrical system.

**Engine**

**3.8L V6**

* **Intake manifold gasket failure:** The intake manifold gasket can fail, causing coolant and oil to leak. This can lead to overheating and engine damage. Symptoms include loss of coolant, oil leaks, and white smoke from the exhaust. The fix is to replace the intake manifold gasket. Estimated cost to fix: $500 - $1,000+
* **Water pump failure:** The water pump can fail, causing the engine to overheat. Symptoms include overheating, coolant leaks, and a squealing noise from the engine. The fix is to replace the water pump. Estimated cost to fix: $200 - $500+

**3.8L Supercharged V6**

* **Intake manifold gasket failure:** Similar to the 3.8L V6, the intake manifold gasket can fail, causing coolant and oil to leak. This can lead to overheating and engine damage. Symptoms include loss of coolant, oil leaks, and white smoke from the exhaust. The fix is to replace the intake manifold gasket. Estimated cost to fix: $500 - $1,000+
* **Supercharger failure:** The supercharger can fail, causing a loss of power and performance. Symptoms include reduced power, a whining noise from the engine, and a check engine light. The fix is to replace the supercharger. Estimated cost to fix: $2,000 - $3,000+

**4.6L V8**

* **Head gasket failure:** The head gasket can fail, causing coolant and oil to leak. This can lead to overheating and engine damage. Symptoms include loss of coolant, oil leaks, and white smoke from the exhaust. The fix is to replace the head gasket. Estimated cost to fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
* **Intake manifold gasket failure:** Similar to the 3.8L V6, the intake manifold gasket can fail, causing coolant and oil to leak. This can lead to overheating and engine damage. Symptoms include loss of coolant, oil leaks, and white smoke from the exhaust. The fix is to replace the intake manifold gasket. Estimated cost to fix: $500 - $1,000+

**Transmission**

**4-Speed Automatic Transmission**

* **Transmission slipping:** The transmission may slip, causing the vehicle to hesitate or jerk when shifting gears. Symptoms include loss of power, harsh shifting, and a check engine light. The fix is to replace the transmission. Estimated cost to fix: $2,000 - $3,000+
* **Torque converter failure:** The torque converter can fail, causing the transmission to slip or shudder. Symptoms include loss of power, transmission shudder, and a check engine light. The fix is to replace the torque converter. Estimated cost to fix: $1,000 - $2,000+

**5-Speed Manual Transmission**

* **Clutch failure:** The clutch can fail, causing the vehicle to be unable to move. Symptoms include difficulty shifting gears, a slipping clutch, and a burning smell. The fix is to replace the clutch. Estimated cost to fix: $500 - $1,000+
* **Transmission grinding:** The transmission may grind when shifting gears. Symptoms include difficulty shifting gears and a grinding noise from the transmission. The fix is to rebuild or replace the transmission. Estimated cost to fix: $2,000 - $3,000+

**Suspension**

* **Strut failure:** The struts can fail, causing the vehicle to ride rough and handle poorly. Symptoms include excessive body roll, poor handling, and a bumpy ride. The fix is to replace the struts. Estimated cost to fix: $500 - $1,000+
* **Sway bar links:** The sway bar links can fail, causing the vehicle to handle poorly. Symptoms include excessive body roll and poor handling. The fix is to replace the sway bar links. Estimated cost to fix: $200 - $500+

**Electrical**

* **Alternator failure:** The alternator can fail, causing the battery to discharge and the vehicle to lose power. Symptoms include dim headlights, a dead battery, and a check engine light. The fix is to replace the alternator. Estimated cost to fix: $500 - $1,000+
* **Battery failure:** The battery can fail, causing the vehicle to be unable to start. Symptoms include a dead battery, difficulty starting the vehicle, and a check engine light. The fix is to replace the battery. Estimated cost to fix: $100 - $200+
* **Starter failure:** The starter can fail, causing the vehicle to be unable to start. Symptoms include a clicking noise when trying to start the vehicle and a check engine light. The fix is to replace the starter. Estimated cost to fix: $500 - $1,000+

**Exterior**

* **No major issues found**

**Interior**

* **Power window failure:** The power window motors can fail, causing the windows to be unable to roll up or down. Symptoms include difficulty operating the windows and a check engine light. The fix is to replace the power window motors. Estimated cost to fix: $500 - $1,000+
* **Door lock failure:** The door locks can fail, causing the doors to be unable to lock or unlock. Symptoms include difficulty locking or unlocking the doors and a check engine light. The fix is to replace the door lock actuators. Estimated cost to fix: $200 - $500+
* **Seat adjustment failure:** The seat adjustment motors can fail, causing the seats to be unable to move. Symptoms include difficulty adjusting the seats and a check engine light. The fix is to replace the seat adjustment motors. Estimated cost to fix: $500 - $1,000+

**Summary**

The 1996 Buick LeSabre is a reliable vehicle that has stood the test of time. However, it is not without its issues. The most common issues reported by owners include engine intake manifold gasket failure, transmission slipping, and suspension strut failure. These issues can be expensive to fix, so it is important to factor them into the cost of ownership.
