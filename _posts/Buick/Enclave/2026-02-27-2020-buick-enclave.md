# 2020 Buick Enclave Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2020 Buick Enclave offers families a spacious and comfortable three-row crossover experience with composed road manners. While generally well-regarded for its cabin comfort and smooth ride, used car buyers should inspect a few recurring mechanical and electrical areas, particularly regarding exhaust components, seat belts, and infotainment electronics.

---

## Engine

### 3.6L V6 Engine

Powered by a 3.6-liter V6 engine producing approximately 310 horsepower and 266 lb-ft of torque, mated to a smooth-shifting automatic transmission. This powertrain provides ample acceleration for highway passing and everyday driving, though buyers should verify exhaust system integrity during a pre-purchase inspection.

**Exhaust Pipe Separation**
- **Symptoms:** A consistent rattling noise from underneath the vehicle, often caused by a separated front exhaust pipe mounting bracket or a leaking flex pipe.
- **Solution:** Inspect the exhaust assembly and replace or reweld brackets and pipes as needed.

## Transmission

### 9-Speed Automatic Transmission

Equipped with a 9-speed automatic transmission available in both front-wheel drive and all-wheel drive configurations. It generally delivers refined gear changes suited for long-distance cruising.

## Steering

### Suspension and Steering

Suspension components handle the bulk of road impacts; listen for clunks or unusual tire wear during a test drive.

**Lateral Arm Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle shaking, premature tire wear, and alignment drift due to worn lateral arms.
- **Solution:** Inspect suspension joints and replace worn lateral arms followed by a professional wheel alignment.

## Electrical

### Infotainment and Backup Camera

Electronics govern many cabin functions, making a thorough test of the touchscreen, camera, and accessories essential before purchase.

**Backup Camera Display Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The infotainment screen displays a black screen or a red triangle with a line through it when shifting into reverse, disabling the rearview camera image.
- **Solution:** Diagnose the infotainment software or replace the faulty camera module/wiring harness.

## Safety

### Seat Belt

Ensuring proper safety restraint operation is critical; test all seat belt buckles during a used vehicle inspection.

**Seat Belt Latch Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The driver's side seat belt fails to click securely into place, requiring multiple insertion attempts or refusing to latch altogether.
- **Solution:** Inspect the seat belt assembly and buckle mechanism, and replace the buckle or latch assembly if worn.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V115000: Tires](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Buick&model=Enclave&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** Over cured tires may develop a break in the sidewall resulting in sudden air loss or a belt edge separation which could lead to a tread/belt loss. Either condition can cause a loss of vehicle control, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the tires, replacing them as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V639000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Buick&model=Enclave&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** Insufficient fuel supply can result in an engine stall without warning, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will replace the fuel pump module. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V684000: Tires](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Buick&model=Enclave&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** Overcured tires may experience a carcass break in the sidewall resulting in a sudden air loss or could develop a belt edge separation which could lead to partial or full tread/belt loss. Either condition increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will inspect vehicles and replace tires that have DOT number and production mold number combinations identified by Continental, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V446000: Air Bags:Side/Window:Curtain:Inflator](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Buick&model=Enclave&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** The diffuser component of the Roof-Rail Air Bag (RRAB) inflator may not have been properly crimped to the inflator and could separate from the inflator during air bag deployment. If the diffuser separates from the inflator during deployment, RRAB performance may be degraded, increasing the risk of injury in a crash.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will replace the suspect RRAB modules, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V643000: Structure:Frame And Members](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Buick&model=Enclave&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** A crack in the frame rail may reduce crash performance, increasing the risk of an injury.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will inspect and, as necessary, replace the vehicle, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V668000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Buick&model=Enclave&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** Missing bolts on the start-stop accumulator endcap could result in a transmission oil leak and may progress to a loss of propulsion, increasing the risk of a crash. A transmission fluid leak in the presence of an ignition source may increase the risk of fire.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the start-stop transmission accumulator and replace it if any bolts are missing, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2020 Buick Enclave is a spacious and generally comfortable family hauler, but used car buyers should perform targeted checks. Pay close attention to the exhaust system for rattles, verify that all seat belt latches click securely, and test the infotainment and backup camera systems for intermittent display failures.
