**Introduction:**

The 2014 Buick Enclave is a midsize SUV that was well-received for its spacious interior, comfortable ride, and powerful V6 engine. It came with two engine options: a 3.6L V6 producing 288 horsepower and 270 lb-ft of torque, mated to a 6-speed automatic transmission, and a 2.4L 4-cylinder producing 182 horsepower and 172 lb-ft of torque, paired with a 6-speed automatic transmission. Despite its positive qualities, the 2014 Enclave has been known to experience several issues.

**Engine**

* **3.6L V6**
    * **Intake Valve Carbon Buildup:** Carbon deposits accumulate on the intake valves over time, leading to reduced engine performance, rough idle, and misfires.
    * **Solution:** Cleaning the intake valves through walnut blasting or chemical cleaning.
    * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $300 - $800+
    * **Recalls:** NHTSA Campaign Number 18V487000
* **2.4L 4-Cylinder**
    * **No Issues Found**

**Transmission**

* **6-Speed Automatic (Both Engines)**
    * **Shift Solenoid Failure:** Faulty shift solenoids cause delayed or harsh shifting, transmission slipping, and loss of power.
    * **Solution:** Replacing the failed shift solenoids.
    * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $500 - $1,500+
    * **Recalls:** None Found

**Suspension**

* **Rear Suspension Links:** Worn or broken rear suspension links cause rattling noises, poor handling, and premature tire wear.
* **Solution:** Replacing the affected suspension links.
* **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $500 - $800+
* **Recalls:** None Found

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain:** A parasitic battery drain can result from a faulty ignition switch, alternator, or other electrical components.
* **Solution:** Diagnosing and repairing the source of the battery drain.
* **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $100 - $500+
* **Recalls:** None Found

**Exterior**

* **Sunroof Leaks:** Water leaking into the cabin from a faulty sunroof seal or drain system.
* **Solution:** Replacing the sunroof seal or repairing the drain system.
* **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $500 - $1,000+
* **Recalls:** None Found

**Interior**

* **Infotainment System Freezing:** The infotainment system may freeze or become unresponsive, requiring a reset.
* **Solution:** Updating the software or replacing the infotainment unit.
* **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $0 - $1,000+ depending on the severity and fix
* **Recalls:** None Found

**Summary:**

The 2014 Buick Enclave has experienced several issues, including engine valve carbon buildup, transmission shift solenoid failures, rear suspension link wear, electrical battery drains, sunroof leaks, and infotainment system freezing. The severity and cost of these issues can vary depending on the specific problem and the extent of the damage. In general, the Enclave has proven to be a reliable vehicle with comfortable and spacious accommodations, but owners should be aware of the potential issues described above.
