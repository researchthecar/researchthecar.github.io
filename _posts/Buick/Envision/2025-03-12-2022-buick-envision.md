Here's a breakdown of common issues and complaints reported by owners of the 2022 Buick Envision, categorized by major component, and formatted for your website. The 2022 Buick Envision is a compact SUV that offers a comfortable ride and stylish design. It's generally praised for its quiet interior, user-friendly technology, and attractive styling. Some common criticisms include its somewhat uninspiring performance from the base engine, a smaller cargo area compared to some competitors, and the push-button gear selector that some find unconventional. The 2022 Envision is exclusively powered by a 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine producing 228 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque and is paired with a 9-speed automatic transmission.

**Engine**

**2.0L Turbocharged I4 (LSY)**

*   **Excessive Oil Consumption**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported higher than expected oil consumption, requiring more frequent oil additions between scheduled oil changes. Symptoms may include the low oil pressure light illuminating, unusual engine noises, and visible blue smoke from the exhaust.
    *   Solution: In some cases, dealers have performed oil consumption tests and, if deemed excessive, replaced piston rings or even the entire engine assembly.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $6,000+ (depending on repair scope; piston ring replacement vs. engine replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Engine Misfires**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Owners have reported instances of engine misfires, resulting in rough idling, reduced power, and the check engine light illuminating. Fault codes often point to ignition coil or fuel injector issues.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis to determine the cause of the misfire and replacement of faulty ignition coils, fuel injectors, or spark plugs.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $500+ (depending on the number of parts needing replacement and labor costs)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**9-Speed Automatic (9T50)**

*   **Harsh Shifting/Hesitation**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported experiencing harsh or jerky shifting, particularly during low-speed maneuvers or when transitioning between gears. Hesitation during acceleration has also been noted.
    *   Solution:  Software updates to the transmission control module (TCM) have been reported to improve shifting behavior. In some cases, valve body replacement has been necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 (for software update) to $1,000 - $2,000+ (for valve body replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Transmission Slipping**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Transmission slipping, especially when cold or under heavy acceleration.  RPMs may increase without a corresponding increase in vehicle speed.
    *   Solution: Some cases may require a transmission replacement. Other times, it is a problem with the internal seals or clutches within the transmission.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $3,500 - $6,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Freezing/Lagging**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The infotainment system occasionally freezes, becomes unresponsive, or experiences significant lag when performing tasks like navigating menus or using smartphone integration features.
    *   Solution: Software updates have been released to address infotainment system glitches. In some instances, replacement of the infotainment unit has been necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 (software update) to $500 - $1500+ (infotainment unit replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Battery Drain Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms:  Reports of excessive battery drain, leading to difficulty starting the vehicle or a completely dead battery, especially after short periods of inactivity.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis to identify parasitic draw. The fix varies depending on the source of the drain; potentially a faulty module or electrical component.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+ (depending on the complexity of the electrical issue)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Sunroof/Moonroof Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Water leaks around the sunroof or moonroof seals, particularly during heavy rain or car washes.
    *   Solution: Cleaning and re-sealing the sunroof/moonroof assembly or replacing the seals.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Rattling/Squeaking Noises**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Various rattling or squeaking noises emanating from the dashboard, door panels, or other interior trim pieces.
    *   Solution: Locating and securing the loose trim pieces or applying sound-deadening materials.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $300+ (depending on the difficulty of the repair and whether performed by a dealer or DIY)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Automatic Emergency Braking (AEB) Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Instances of the AEB system activating unnecessarily, even when there is no apparent obstacle in front of the vehicle.
    *   Solution: recalibration of the system or sensor replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100-$400+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

The 2022 Buick Envision presents a mixed bag in terms of reliability. While generally a comfortable and stylish vehicle, owners have reported issues ranging from minor electrical glitches to more significant engine and transmission problems. The engine and transmission issues, while not universally experienced, can be costly to repair. Electrical gremlins, like the infotainment system problems, are more common and can be frustrating. The issues are overall of moderate severity and are not as costly as some other vehicles. However, they should be considered when purchasing this model year.
