# 2022 Buick Envision Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2022 Buick Envision, buyers will find a stylish and modern compact SUV equipped with a standard turbocharged engine and a smooth shifting automatic transmission. While owner complaints offer valuable insights into potential trouble spots, keep in mind that these reports represent individual experiences rather than guaranteed vehicle defects. A thorough pre-purchase inspection is always recommended before finalizing your purchase.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged I4 Engine

The 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine produces approximately 228 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque, providing responsive daily acceleration. This powerplant is generally dependable, though occasional electrical and telematics integration issues have been reported by owners.

## Transmission

### 9-Speed Automatic Transmission

The nine-speed automatic transmission comes paired with front-wheel drive or all-wheel drive, delivering smooth gear changes and efficient highway cruising. No widespread mechanical transmission failures are highlighted in current owner feedback.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

Brake system feedback points to occasional reports of altered pedal feel and electronic error messages affecting stopping performance.

**Brake Responsiveness Concerns**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have reported instances where depressing the brake pedal resulted in delayed stopping or required increased pedal effort, sometimes accompanied by dashboard error codes.
- **Solution:** Have the brake system inspected immediately by a qualified mechanic to check brake fluid, pads, rotors, and associated electronic stability or brake booster modules.

## Electrical

### Instrument Cluster & Telematics

Electrical complaints center primarily on intermittent telematics and instrument cluster glitches that can impact driver visibility of vehicle vitals.

**Instrument Panel Blackout**
- **Symptoms:** The instrument panel cluster goes completely black while the vehicle remains operational, causing the speedometer, warning lights, and gear display to become non-functional.
- **Solution:** Have a certified technician inspect the instrument cluster, software calibrations, and related fuses to determine if module replacement or reprogramming is required.

**Telematics Module Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** A malfunctioning OnStar telematics module can drain the vehicle battery mid-drive and cause unexpected engine shutdowns or safety feature failures.
- **Solution:** Consult a dealership to check for software updates, test the telematics module, and replace faulty components if necessary.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V903000: Exterior Lighting:Lighting Control Module:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Buick&model=Envision&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** The daytime running lights (DRLs) may not deactivate when the headlights are on. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 108, "Lamps, Reflective Devices, and Associated Equipment." DRLs that do not deactivate as intended may result in a glare, reducing visibility, and increasing the risk.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2022 Buick Envision is a comfortable and well-equipped compact SUV, but prospective buyers should carefully review electrical and brake operation during a test drive. Verifying that all software updates, telematics modules, and safety features are fully functional will help ensure a reliable ownership experience.
