# 2017 Buick Envision Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2017 Buick Envision offers a comfortable ride, quiet cabin, and practical features, making it an appealing choice in the used compact luxury crossover market. However, prospective buyers should review recurring owner complaints concerning certain mechanical and electrical systems before purchasing.

---

## Engine

### 2.5L 4-Cylinder Engine

The 2.5-liter naturally aspirated four-cylinder engine produces approximately 197 horsepower and serves as the standard powerplant for base and mid-level trims. It delivers adequate daily driving performance, though routine maintenance is essential for long-term reliability.

**Engine Warning Lights and Sensor Failures**
- **Symptoms:** Illuminated check engine lights and unexpected engine warning alerts while driving at highway speeds.
- **Solution:** Have the diagnostic trouble codes read by a professional mechanic to identify and replace faulty engine sensors.

### 2.0L Turbocharged 4-Cylinder Engine

The 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine generates around 252 horsepower, providing stronger acceleration and passing power. Buyers considering this option should listen for unusual turbo noises and verify proper boost behavior during test drives.

**Turbocharger Underboost and Power Loss**
- **Symptoms:** Check engine light illuminating accompanied by an underboost code and unpredictable loss of engine power while driving.
- **Solution:** Inspect the turbocharger assembly, wastegate, and related boost lines for leaks or mechanical failure.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

Paired with the base 2.5L engine, this six-speed automatic transmission generally delivers smooth shifts, though proper fluid maintenance is critical.

### Olímpicos 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

Paired with the 2.0L turbocharged engine, this automatic transmission manages higher torque output. Buyers should carefully evaluate shift quality during acceleration tests.

**Transmission Shifting Delays and Diagnostic Codes**
- **Symptoms:** RPMs redlining without timely gear engagement during acceleration, alongside stored OBD-II trouble codes such as P0700.
- **Solution:** Perform a comprehensive diagnostic scan of the transmission control module and inspect fluid condition.

## Brakes

### Hydraulic Brake Lines and Hoses

The service brake system relies on hydraulic lines that require regular inspection for corrosion, wear, and potential leaks.

**Brake Line Rupture and Fluid Loss**
- **Symptoms:** Sudden loss of brake pressure, spongy or unresponsive brake pedal feel, and complete fluid leakage due to a ruptured flexible brake line.
- **Solution:** Immediately replace damaged or worn brake lines and hoses, and bleed the hydraulic brake system completely.

## Steering

### Electronic Power Steering System

The electronic power steering setup provides speed-sensitive steering feel, but electronic sensor or internal motor faults can occasionally disable power assistance.

**Electronic Power Steering Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Sudden loss of power steering assistance accompanied by dashboard warnings such as 'Power Steering Dismissed'.
- **Solution:** Have the electronic power steering module and internal components diagnosed and replaced if internal failure is confirmed.

## Exterior

### Exterior Lighting

The exterior lighting system governs visibility components, where headlight output quality is critical for safe night driving.

**Dim Headlights**
- **Symptoms:** Significantly reduced illumination distance and inadequate beam projection from the standard headlights during night driving.
- **Solution:** Inspect headlight bulb output, housing clarity, and electrical connections to ensure proper beam performance.

## Safety

### Seat Belts

Occupant safety relies heavily on seat belt mechanisms remaining free of mechanical jams or strap degradation.

**Seat Belt Fraying and Latch Sticking**
- **Symptoms:** Driver-side seat belt webbing fraying prematurely against the connector buckle or failing to release when pressed.
- **Solution:** Inspect seat belt assemblies for physical wear and replace damaged seat belt straps and buckles immediately.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V404000: Equipment](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Buick&model=Envision&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** If so, the head restraints would fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 202a, "Head Restraints." If the head restraint posts are not fully engaging in the seat back, the head restraint may loosen or dislodge, increasing the risk of injury in a crash.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the head restraints and tablet holders for the correct installation, reinstalling them as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V358000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Buick&model=Envision&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** A damaged fuel line can create a fuel leak, increasing the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will replace the high pressure fuel pump, and high pressure fuel pipe, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V412000: Structure](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Buick&model=Envision&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 201, "Occupant protection in interior impact." If the service parts do not have energy-absorbing foam blocks, there may be an increased risk of occupant injury in the event of a crash.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will replace the headliner assembly, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V516000: Equipment:Other:Labels](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Buick&model=Envision&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 110, "Tire Selection and Rims." Incorrect weight information may cause an owner to overload the vehicle. Overloading may reduce vehicle control, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will install a corrected label as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.
