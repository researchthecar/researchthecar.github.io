# 2019 Buick Envision Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2019 Buick Envision offers a quiet, comfortable ride and an upscale interior, making it an appealing choice for used crossover shoppers. However, prospective buyers should review specific mechanical and safety complaints closely before purchasing, particularly regarding braking systems and powertrain performance.

---

## Engine

### 2.5L 4-Cylinder Engine

The base 2.5-liter naturally aspirated four-cylinder engine produces 197 horsepower and is paired with a 6-speed automatic transmission. It offers adequate daily driving performance, though owners have reported occasional hesitation and power loss issues.

**Loss of Power and Acceleration**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may experience sudden loss of engine power, sluggish acceleration, or a whining noise from the engine bay.
- **Solution:** Inspect the booster vacuum sensor and turbo or intake components; replace faulty sensors or mechanical parts as needed.

**Turbocharger Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Sudden reduction in speed and power while driving on the highway, often accompanied by recurring boost loss.
- **Solution:** Have the turbocharger assembly inspected by a qualified technician and replace failed turbo units or associated control components.

### 2.0L Turbocharged 4-Cylinder Engine

The available 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine generates 252 horsepower and delivers livelier acceleration paired with a 9-speed automatic transmission. It provides stronger passing power for highway driving while maintaining decent overall refinement.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 6-speed automatic transmission works alongside the base 2.5L engine to deliver smooth, predictable gear shifts under normal commuting conditions.

### 9-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 9-speed automatic transmission is paired with the 2.0L turbo engine and all-wheel-drive configurations, offering tighter gear spacing for improved highway efficiency and responsiveness.

## Brakes

### Brake Assist and Hydraulic System

The service brake system has notable owner complaints regarding intermittent brake assist warnings and unexpected drops in pedal pressure.

**Brake Assist Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The 'Service Brake Assist' warning light illuminates, followed by an extremely firm brake pedal, severely reduced stopping power, or the pedal traveling all the way to the floor.
- **Solution:** Have the braking and vacuum assist systems inspected immediately by a dealer or specialist to diagnose failing master cylinders, boosters, or electronic brake modules.

## Exterior

### Sunroof and Body Hardware

Exterior body components are generally durable, though isolated structural hardware noises and rare glass defects have been documented.

**Sunroof Shattering**
- **Symptoms:** The glass sunroof may suddenly fracture or explode while driving at highway speeds, scattering glass fragments into the cabin.
- **Solution:** Replace the damaged glass panel and inspect the sunroof frame for alignment issues or stress points.

**Door Hinge Popping**
- **Symptoms:** A noticeable popping or cracking sound emanates from the driver or passenger side doors when opening or closing them.
- **Solution:** Inspect and lubricate or replace door check straps and hinge assemblies as necessary.

## Safety

### Forward Collision and Driver Assistance

Advanced safety features generally assist with driver awareness, though sporadic electronic warnings and false collision triggers have been flagged by some owners.

**Unintended Emergency Braking**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may abruptly brake without driver input while the brake assist warning light illuminates on the dashboard.
- **Solution:** Check for diagnostic trouble codes in the collision avoidance module and consult a dealer for software updates or sensor recalibration.
