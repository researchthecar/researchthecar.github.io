# 2018 Buick Envision Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2018 Buick Envision, it is helpful to look past initial impressions and review what owners experience day-to-day. While complaints submitted to federal safety databases represent individual experiences rather than guaranteed factory defects, they highlight specific areas worth inspecting. Prospective buyers should carefully evaluate both powertrain options and pay close attention to reported electrical and steering anomalies during a pre-purchase test drive.

---

## Engine

### 2.5L 4-Cylinder

The naturally aspirated 2.5-liter four-cylinder engine produces around 197 horsepower and serves as the standard powerplant for base trims. It offers adequate efficiency for daily commuting, though owners should monitor fluid levels closely for signs of internal leaks.

**Coolant Leak and Head Gasket Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Coolant leaks internally into the engine, leading to potential overheating and head gasket failure at moderate mileage.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified mechanic perform a pressure test and inspect for coolant contamination. Repairing or replacing a failed head gasket is required.

### 2.0L Turbocharged 4-Cylinder

The optional 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine generates approximately 252 horsepower, providing stronger acceleration and passing power. It is typically paired with higher trim levels and all-wheel drive for a more dynamic driving feel.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 6-speed automatic transmission is paired with the base 2.5L engine, delivering smooth power delivery for front-wheel or all-wheel drive layouts.

### 9-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 9-speed automatic transmission accompanies the 2.0L turbocharged engine, offering refined gear selection and optimized highway cruising efficiency.

## Steering

### Electric Power Steering

Issues involving sudden steering resistance require immediate professional diagnosis to ensure safe vehicle operation.

**Steering Wheel Lockup**
- **Symptoms:** The steering wheel may suddenly become rigid and difficult or impossible to turn while driving or immediately following an auto-stop event.
- **Solution:** Inspect the electric power steering module, battery health, and associated software calibrations for faults.

## Electrical

### Instrument Cluster and Body Control Modules

electrical issues can impact various convenience and safety features, making a comprehensive pre-purchase electrical inspection essential.

**Instrument Cluster and Sensor Failures**
- **Symptoms:** The dashboard instrument panel may go completely blank, causing a loss of speedometer readings and safety warnings, alongside failing tire pressure and airbag sensors.
- **Solution:** Have a technician scan the vehicle computer for diagnostic trouble codes and check wiring harnesses or control module software.
