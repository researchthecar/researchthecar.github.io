# 2016 Buick Envision Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2016 Buick Envision, it is helpful to look past general appeal and examine owner-reported issues across key mechanical and electrical systems. While many owners enjoy its comfortable ride and upscale cabin, some vehicle years carry notable problem areas ranging from suspension delays to powertrain hesitations.

---

## Engine

### 2.5L 4-Cylinder

The 2.5-liter naturally aspirated four-cylinder engine produces around 197 horsepower and 192 lb-ft of torque. It provides adequate power for daily commuting, though buyers should verify routine maintenance history.

### 2.0L Turbocharged 4-Cylinder

The 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine generates roughly 252 horsepower and 295 lb-ft of torque. It offers brisk acceleration for highway driving, but prospective owners should check the turbocharger and cooling systems for longevity.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

The six-speed automatic transmission pairs with front-wheel or all-wheel drive variants. Test-driving this specific powertrain combination carefully is recommended to check for smooth shifting and responsive engagement.

**Acceleration Hesitation and RPM Bouncing**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may experience delayed acceleration when attempting to enter traffic or pass other vehicles, accompanied by fluctuating engine RPMs.
- **Solution:** In severe cases, owners reported needing a full transmission replacement to resolve internal mechanical failures.

## Suspension

### Front Control Arm Assembly

The suspension setup is designed for a smooth, compliant ride quality, but front-end components require thorough pre-purchase inspections.

**Control Arm and Bushing Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have reported structural concerns or failures involving the front passenger control arm assembly, sometimes resulting in extended repair delays due to parts availability.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified mechanic inspect the front control arm bushings and assembly for wear or structural integrity issues before purchase.

## Electrical

### Infotainment and Dashboard Displays

electrical issues can occasionally impact convenience features and cabin technology, making a full accessory check wise during a test drive.

**Dashboard and Screen Blackout**
- **Symptoms:** The dashboard display and radio/navigation screen may completely shut down or go black while driving, disabling instrument readouts.
- **Solution:** Restarting the vehicle sometimes restores function, but persistent issues may require diagnosing software glitches or faulty control modules.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V404000: Equipment](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Buick&model=Envision&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** If so, the head restraints would fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 202a, "Head Restraints." If the head restraint posts are not fully engaging in the seat back, the head restraint may loosen or dislodge, increasing the risk of injury in a crash.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the head restraints and tablet holders for the correct installation, reinstalling them as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V358000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Buick&model=Envision&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** A damaged fuel line can create a fuel leak, increasing the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will replace the high pressure fuel pump, and high pressure fuel pipe, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V412000: Structure](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Buick&model=Envision&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 201, "Occupant protection in interior impact." If the service parts do not have energy-absorbing foam blocks, there may be an increased risk of occupant injury in the event of a crash.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will replace the headliner assembly, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V922000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Buick&model=Envision&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** If there is a loss of brake fluid, braking performance may be reduced or lost, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will replace the master cylinder brake lines, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V516000: Equipment:Other:Labels](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Buick&model=Envision&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 110, "Tire Selection and Rims." Incorrect weight information may cause an owner to overload the vehicle. Overloading may reduce vehicle control, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will install a corrected label as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2016 Buick Envision offers a comfortable cabin and composed driving dynamics, but potential buyers should exercise caution. Pay close attention during a test drive to transmission shift quality, electrical accessory stability, and front suspension condition to avoid unexpected repair hurdles.
