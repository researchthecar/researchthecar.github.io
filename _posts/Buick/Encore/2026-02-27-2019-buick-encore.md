# 2019 Buick Encore Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2019 Buick Encore is a subcompact luxury crossover popular for its maneuverability, quiet cabin, and elevated driving position. While many owners enjoy its daily practicality and city-friendly footprint, used car buyers should carefully review potential powertrain and mechanical concerns reported to federal safety authorities. Being informed about these historical owner complaints can help you spot potential trouble spots during a test drive and pre-purchase inspection.

---

## Engine

### 1.4L Turbocharged 4-Cylinder (LE2 / LUJ)

The 1.4-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine delivers around 138 to 153 horsepower depending on the exact specification. It offers punchy low-end torque for city driving, but owners have reported occasional turbocharger and cooling system complications.

**Engine Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** The engine may unexpectedly stall or hesitate while driving, frequently triggering a check engine light.
- **Solution:** Diagnose fault codes, inspect the turbocharger system, and repair or replace faulty ignition or fuel management components.

**Coolant Leaks and Overheating**
- **Symptoms:** Low coolant levels can trigger dashboard warning messages regarding engine overheating, often due to leaks around the expansion tank, hoses, or gaskets.
- **Solution:** Pressure test the cooling system, inspect the radiator expansion tank and gasket seals, and replace compromised components.

**Turbocharger Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of engine power, unusual hissing or whining noises, or premature failure of the turbocharger unit.
- **Solution:** Inspect turbocharger assembly for mechanical wear or oil starvation; replace the turbocharger unit if internal damage is present.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

Equipped with a standard 6-speed automatic transmission paired with either front-wheel drive or all-wheel drive, offering generally smooth operation with occasional reports of erratic electronic shifting behavior.

**Abrupt Shifting or Lurching**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may unexpectedly surge forward or shift harshly when engaging reverse or drive.
- **Solution:** Inspect transmission control module software, check fluid levels, and test valve body operation.

## Suspension

### Control Arms and Tires

Suspension components provide a comfortable ride quality for a small crossover, though rough roads can stress factory control arms and original tires.

**Control Arm Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Sudden structural failure of the lower control arm, often accelerated by impacts with potholes or metal fatigue near the ball joint.
- **Solution:** Inspect lower control arms and ball joints for bending, cracking, or structural fatigue, and replace damaged suspension assemblies.

**Premature Tire Sidewall Cracking**
- **Symptoms:** Original equipment tires developing premature dry rot, sidewall splitting, or sudden deflation.
- **Solution:** Inspect tire production dates and tread condition regularly; replace aging or cracked tires immediately.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

Brake system complaints highlight occasional rotor thickness variations, pad wear imbalances, and rare instances of hydraulic stiffness.

**Brake Pedal Stiffness and Locking**
- **Symptoms:** The brake pedal can feel extremely hard to depress, or brakes may intermittently lock up at highway speeds.
- **Solution:** Inspect brake booster, hydraulic lines, ABS module, and brake pads for uneven wear or sticking calipers.

## Steering

### Power Steering

Electronic power steering provides light effort for parking and maneuverability, though rare electronic glitches can temporarily interrupt assist.

**Power Steering Interruption**
- **Symptoms:** Temporary loss of power steering assist while driving at highway speeds, requiring a vehicle restart to restore function.
- **Solution:** Scan electronic power steering control modules for diagnostic trouble codes and update software or replace failing steering motors.

## Electrical

### Infotainment and Lighting

Electrical complaints center on minor infotainment audio discrepancies, sensitive light sensors, and occasional moisture in exterior lighting housings.

**Headlight and Condensation Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Moisture condensation accumulating inside exterior tail light assemblies or overly sensitive automatic headlight sensors.
- **Solution:** Inspect light housing seals for proper closure and replace faulty moisture barriers or sensor units.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V774000: Air Bags](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Buick&model=Encore&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** The Sensing Diagnostic Module (SDM) on these vehicle may not have been turned off of "manufacturing mode," preventing the air bags from deploying in the event of a crash. If the air bags do not deploy in the event of a crash, it can increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will reprogram the SDM module to the correct mode, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V668000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Buick&model=Encore&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** Missing bolts on the start-stop accumulator endcap could result in a transmission oil leak and may progress to a loss of propulsion, increasing the risk of a crash. A transmission fluid leak in the presence of an ignition source may increase the risk of fire.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the start-stop transmission accumulator and replace it if any bolts are missing, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2019 Buick Encore is an appealing option for buyers seeking an easy-to-park crossover with a quiet ride, but it requires careful evaluation. Prioritize a thorough pre-purchase inspection focusing on the 1.4L turbocharged engine's cooling system, turbocharger health, and suspension components. Addressing these vital areas before purchase can help ensure a more reliable and satisfying ownership experience.
