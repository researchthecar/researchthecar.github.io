**Introduction**

The 2022 Buick Encore is a subcompact SUV known for its stylish exterior, comfortable interior, and fuel efficiency. It comes standard with a 1.4L turbocharged engine paired with a 6-speed automatic transmission. Other engine and transmission options include a 1.3L turbocharged engine mated to a Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT).

**Engine**

**1.4L Turbocharged**

- **Oil Consumption:** Excessive oil consumption leading to low oil levels and potential engine damage.
    - Solution: Replace piston rings and valve seals.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
    - Recalls: NHTSA Campaign Number: 22V852

**1.3L Turbocharged**

- **No Issues Found**

**Transmission**

**6-Speed Automatic**

- **Shifting Issues:** Hard or delayed shifts, especially during acceleration or deceleration.
    - Solution: Reprogram transmission control module (TCM), replace valve body or solenoids.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+
    - Recalls: None Found

**Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)**

- **No Issues Found**

**Suspension**

- **Rear Suspension Noise:** Clunking or rattling sound from the rear suspension during bumpy roads.
    - Solution: Replace rear sway bar links or bushings.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600

**Electrical**

- **Keyless Entry Issues:** Keyless entry fobs may not work properly or become unresponsive.
    - Solution: Replace keyless entry modules or batteries.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600
    - Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

- **Headlight Condensation:** Moisture buildup inside the headlight lenses.
    - Solution: Replace or reseal headlights.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,000

**Interior**

- **Climate Control Malfunctions:** HVAC system not blowing air or temperature control issues.
    - Solution: Replace HVAC control panel or blend door actuators.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $1,200
    - Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

Overall, the 2022 Buick Encore has some common issues that can be costly to fix, such as oil consumption, transmission shifting problems, and HVAC malfunctions. However, many of these issues are covered by recalls. The severity and cost of these issues vary depending on the specific problem.
