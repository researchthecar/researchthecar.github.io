# 2016 Buick Encore Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used compact SUV, the Buick Encore offers a nimble footprint and quiet cabin, but prospective buyers should carefully review potential mechanical and electrical trouble spots. While many owners enjoy its comfort and efficiency, federal safety and reliability databases highlight several recurring complaints ranging from turbocharger hesitation and coolant leaks to unexpected electrical glitches and power steering failures. Outlined below are the specific system breakdowns to watch for during a pre-purchase inspection.

---

## Engine

### 1.4L Turbocharged 4-Cylinder Engine

The 1.4-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine produces approximately 138 horsepower and 148 lb-ft of torque, providing adequate pep for city commuting. However, its turbo and cooling systems require attentive maintenance to avoid reliability snags.

**Engine Stalling and Power Loss**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers report the engine suddenly sputtering, stalling, or losing motive power mid-drive, creating dangerous situations in traffic.
- **Solution:** Diagnose fuel delivery, throttle body, or electrical ground faults, and replace failing components as necessary.

**Turbocharger Hesitation and Fault Codes**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle triggers a P0299 low boost code, causing the engine to become sluggish, hesitant, and slow to accelerate from a stop.
- **Solution:** Inspect the turbocharger assembly, wastegate, and related vacuum lines for wear or leaks; repair or replace the turbo unit if internal failure is confirmed.

**Coolant Leaks and Overheating**
- **Symptoms:** Coolant levels drop unexpectedly without an obvious external puddle, occasionally leading to engine overheating.
- **Solution:** Pressure-test the cooling system and inspect turbo coolant lines, water pump, and thermostat housing for seepage.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 6-speed automatic transmission routes power smoothly to either the front wheels or all-wheel-drive system. It generally operates without widespread catastrophic failure patterns, though routine fluid changes are recommended.

## Steering

### Power Steering System

Issues with the electronic power steering assist can abruptly increase steering effort, posing physical challenges for drivers when maneuvering at low or high speeds.

**Power Steering Fault and Loss of Assist**
- **Symptoms:** A 'Steering Fault' message appears on the instrument panel, the steering icon illuminates, and the steering wheel becomes extremely heavy and difficult to turn.
- **Solution:** Scan the steering control module for fault codes, check wiring harnesses, and replace the electric power steering assembly if the motor or sensor fails.

## Electrical

### Instrument Panel and Sensors

Electrical anomalies involving dashboard displays and warning chimes can occasionally plague the vehicle's network.

**Dashboard Blanking and Warning Light Failures**
- **Symptoms:** Instrument cluster goes entirely blank or all dashboard warning lights illuminate simultaneously, sometimes accompanied by a sudden vehicle shutdown.
- **Solution:** Inspect battery connections, ground points, and the body control module (BCM) for loose wiring or moisture intrusion.

## Safety

### Air Bag and Seat Belts

Safety restraint components generally protect occupants well, but minor sensor glitches and mechanical latch jams have been documented.

**Passenger Airbag Sensor Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** The passenger airbag indicator incorrectly displays 'OFF' even when an adult occupant is seated in the front passenger seat.
- **Solution:** Inspect the seat occupancy sensor mat, wiring harness connectors, and control module calibration.

**Rear Seat and Seatbelt Jamming**
- **Symptoms:** Rear folding seats or seatbelt mechanisms become physically stuck in the downward position, preventing normal operation.
- **Solution:** Manually inspect the seat latch mechanism and seatbelt retractor for obstructions or jammed webbing, and replace faulty latch components if broken.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V651000: Air Bags:Frontal](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Buick&model=Encore&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** In the affected vehicles, certain driving conditions may cause the air bag sensing and diagnostic module (SDM) software to activate a diagnostic test. During this test, deployment of the frontal air bags and the seat belt pretensioners would not occur in the event of a crash.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will reflash the SDM software. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V437000: Air Bags:Frontal](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Buick&model=Encore&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** While being previously remedied for recall 16V-651, the affected vehicles may not have received the complete software update necessary to remedy the recall condition. Without the update, certain driving conditions may cause the air bag sensing and diagnostic module (SDM) software to activate a diagnostic test.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will reflash the SDM software. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

When evaluating a used model, prospective buyers should prioritize a thorough mechanical inspection focusing on the turbocharger, cooling system, and electronic power steering functionality. Confirming that all recall work has been performed and verifying that the dashboard electronics and safety sensors operate correctly will help ensure a more dependable ownership experience.
