# 2021 Buick Encore Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2021 Buick Encore, it helps to understand what current owners are experiencing on the road. While this subcompact SUV offers nimble handling and city-friendly dimensions, owner feedback submitted to safety regulators points to a few recurring areas of concern, particularly regarding engine emissions equipment and turbocharger reliability. Reviewing these potential problem spots can help you perform a more targeted inspection before finalizing your purchase.

---

## Engine

### 1.4L Turbocharged I4

This 1.4-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine produces around 138 to 155 horsepower, providing adequate pep for daily commuting. Buyers should verify proper maintenance history and listen for any unusual turbocharger noises during a test drive.

### Emissions and Turbocharger System

Owner reports indicate potential vulnerabilities in the emissions and turbo systems that require careful pre-purchase evaluation.

**Purge Sensor and Emissions Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have reported that the check engine light illuminates repeatedly due to purge sensor faults, which can lead to failed emissions testing.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified mechanic inspect the evaporative emission purge system and replace faulty sensors or associated components as needed.

**Turbocharger Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have experienced sudden losses of engine power while driving due to turbocharger failure, sometimes accompanied by diagnostic trouble codes like P0299.
- **Solution:** Inspect the turbocharger unit and associated lines for wear, boost leaks, or mechanical failure, and replace the assembly if necessary.

**Exhaust Flex Pipe and Oxygen Sensor Damage**
- **Symptoms:** Unusual exhaust odors or performance issues may occur when the exhaust flex pipe deteriorates and damages nearby oxygen sensors.
- **Solution:** Replace the damaged flex pipe and any affected oxygen sensors to restore proper exhaust and emissions function.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

The conventional 6-speed automatic transmission pairs with front-wheel drive or all-wheel drive configurations. It generally offers smooth operation, though prospective buyers should still test shift quality thoroughly.

## Brakes

### Brake Pedal and Vacuum Assist

Brake assist concerns can drastically alter stopping distances and warrant immediate inspection.

**Hard Brake Pedal and Assist Loss**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may encounter an unexpectedly firm brake pedal requiring excessive force to stop the vehicle, sometimes accompanied by hill assist warning messages.
- **Solution:** Have the braking system and vacuum or turbo booster components inspected by a professional technician to ensure proper hydraulic and assist function.
