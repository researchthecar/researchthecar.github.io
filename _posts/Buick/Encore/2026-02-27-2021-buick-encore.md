# 2021 Buick Encore Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2021 Buick Encore offers a compact footprint and up-to-date cabin technology that appeals to daily commuters and urban drivers. However, prospective used-car buyers should thoroughly inspect the vehicle's powertrain and electrical systems before purchasing. Owner feedback highlights several recurring mechanical and electronic glitches that can be expensive to resolve out-of-pocket.

---

## Engine

### 1.4L Turbocharged I4

The 1.4L turbocharged four-cylinder engine provides adequate pep for city driving but has been associated with turbocharger wear and EVAP emissions sensor faults.

**Loss of Motive Power and Turbocharger Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Sudden loss of power while accelerating or passing traffic, accompanied by diagnostic trouble codes like P0299.
- **Solution:** Requires diagnosis and replacement of the turbocharger assembly or associated boost components.

**Evaporative Emissions (EVAP) and Purge Sensor Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Illuminated check engine light related to EVAP system leaks, which can cause the vehicle to fail state emissions inspections.
- **Solution:** Replace faulty purge sensors or EVAP system components.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic

The 6-speed automatic transmission generally shifts smoothly under normal driving conditions, though some related shifter mechanism grievances overlap with drivetrain and electrical complaints.

## Brakes

### Hydraulic Brake System

The hydraulic brake system offers firm stopping response under normal use, but isolated reports indicate potential brake booster or module assist failures.

**Hard Brake Pedal and Reduced Assist**
- **Symptoms:** The brake pedal becomes unusually firm and difficult to depress, severely reducing stopping power and triggering warning lights.
- **Solution:** Inspect and replace failing brake boosters, control modules, or associated vacuum components.

## Electrical

### Shifter and Parking Sensor Switch

Cabin electronics are generally user-friendly, though microswitch failures in the shifter mechanism frequently trigger annoying false warnings.

**False 'Shift to Park' Warning**
- **Symptoms:** A persistent warning message instructs the driver to 'Shift to Park' even when the vehicle is already securely in park, sometimes accompanied by continuous beeping that drains the battery and prevents locking.
- **Solution:** Replace the shifter assembly and control knob module.
- **Cost:** $1,000
