# 2020 Buick Encore Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2020 Buick Encore, prospective buyers will find a compact crossover that generally delivers familiar drivability and manageable daily operation. While most owners enjoy trouble-free motoring, reviewing owner-submitted complaints helps highlight areas that may require closer inspection during a pre-purchase walkthrough. Paying careful attention to the turbocharger, electrical accessories, and routine maintenance items can help ensure a smoother ownership experience.

---

## Engine

### 1.4L Turbocharged I4

The 1.4-liter turbocharged inline-four engine produces approximately 138 horsepower and 148 lb-ft of torque, offering adequate pep for city commuting. It serves as the primary powerplant for this model year, though buyers should verify turbocharger health during inspections.

**Turbocharger Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may experience a sudden loss of engine power, accompanied by illuminated check engine lights and reduced performance.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified mechanic inspect the turbocharger assembly for excessive wear or damage, replacing the unit if necessary.
- **Cost:** $1816.00

**Engine Stalling and Fuel System Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle may hesitate, experience unexpected engine stalling, or display warning lights related to fuel delivery.
- **Solution:** Inspect the fuel pump and associated engine control modules for proper operation.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

The conventional 6-speed automatic transmission pairs with front-wheel or all-wheel drive configurations to provide smooth, predictable gear shifts. It generally exhibits solid durability when subjected to routine fluid changes.

## Suspension

### Factory Tires and Wheels

Suspension geometry provides a comfortable ride quality for a subcompact crossover, though factory-equipped tire durability can vary.

**Factory Tire Bubbles**
- **Symptoms:** Original equipment tires may develop unexpected sidewall bulges or bubbles under normal driving conditions.
- **Solution:** Inspect all four tires for structural defects or sidewall damage, and replace affected tires promptly.

## Brakes

### Brake Discs and Pedals

The braking system provides adequate stopping force under normal conditions, but environmental exposure can lead to accelerated rotor wear in certain climates.

**Premature Rotor Rust and Pedal Stiffening**
- **Symptoms:** Rear brake rotors may show advanced rust formation and reduced stopping power, sometimes accompanied by stiff brake pedal feedback and stability control warnings.
- **Solution:** Inspect brake rotors, pads, and hydraulic lines during routine service, replacing worn or oxidized components as needed.

## Electrical

### Electrical and Infotainment System

electrical issues can occasionally affect accessory modules and battery retention, requiring careful diagnostic checks of wiring and software.

**Battery Drain and Starting Concerns**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle requires frequent jump-starts due to unexpected battery discharge, despite standard alternator and battery tests.
- **Solution:** Perform a parasitic draw test to isolate electrical accessories that may remain powered when the vehicle is turned off.

**Accessory Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** Horn may sound continuously upon vehicle startup, and the dashboard radio, phone connectivity, or cruise control may intermittently fail.
- **Solution:** Inspect fuses, steering column clockspring connections, and infotainment software modules for faults.

## Exterior

### Sunroof and Glass

Exterior body panels and glass components are generally durable, though isolated incidents involving sunroof structural stress have been reported.

**Sunroof Shattering**
- **Symptoms:** The factory sunroof glass may fracture or shatter unexpectedly while the vehicle is in motion, producing a loud pop.
- **Solution:** Inspect the sunroof assembly for alignment issues or stress points, and replace the glass panel if damaged.

### Tailgate Latches

Cabin appointments hold up well to daily use, though latch sensors and trunk release mechanisms warrant functional testing before purchase.

**Unexpected Liftgate Opening**
- **Symptoms:** The liftgate warning light may illuminate while driving, and the trunk lid can spontaneously unlatch or raise up.
- **Solution:** Inspect the liftgate latch switch, wiring harness, and release actuator for proper alignment and function.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V537000: Seats:Critical Fasteners](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Buick&model=Encore&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** The rear attachment bolts of one or both front seats may not have been installed. If an occupied front seat is missing the rear attachment bolts, it may move during a crash, increasing risk of injury to the occupant.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will inspect seat attachments for both front seats and install attachment bolts as needed, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V668000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Buick&model=Encore&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** Missing bolts on the start-stop accumulator endcap could result in a transmission oil leak and may progress to a loss of propulsion, increasing the risk of a crash. A transmission fluid leak in the presence of an ignition source may increase the risk of fire.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the start-stop transmission accumulator and replace it if any bolts are missing, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2020 Buick Encore presents a practical option for buyers seeking an easy-to-park crossover with a comfortable ride. While owner reports point to occasional concerns regarding turbocharger longevity, electrical accessory glitches, and brake component wear, a thorough pre-purchase inspection can catch most potential issues. Prospective owners should verify the health of the turbo system, test all interior electronics, and check the condition of the brakes and tires to secure a reliable vehicle.
