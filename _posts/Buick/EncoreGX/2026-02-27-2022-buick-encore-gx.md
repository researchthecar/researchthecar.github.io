# 2022 Buick Encore GX Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used Buick Encore GX, buyers will find a compact crossover that offers modern styling, comfortable seating, and decent fuel efficiency. While general ownership satisfaction is mostly positive, prospective buyers should review known owner complaints regarding minor electrical glitches and shifter sensors to ensure a trouble-free experience.

---

## Engine

### 1.2L Turbocharged I3

The base 1.2-liter turbocharged inline-three engine generates approximately 137 horsepower and 162 lb-ft of torque. It is paired exclusively with a continuously variable transmission (CVT) and front-wheel drive, offering an efficient daily commuting experience.

### 1.3L Turbocharged I3

The available 1.3-liter turbocharged inline-three engine produces 155 horsepower and 174 lb-ft of torque. It provides a bit more pep for highway merging and can be paired with either front-wheel drive or all-wheel drive.

**Check Engine Light and Sluggish Performance**
- **Symptoms:** The check engine light illuminates, and the vehicle may exhibit sluggish acceleration and reduced power while driving.
- **Solution:** Take the vehicle to a qualified service center or dealership to diagnose diagnostic trouble codes (such as P0102) and replace faulty sensors or wiring components as needed.
- **Cost:** $259.00

## Transmission

### Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)

The CVT is utilized on front-wheel-drive models equipped with the 1.2-liter engine. Regular fluid maintenance ensures smooth power delivery during everyday driving.

### 9-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 9-speed traditional automatic transmission is paired with all-wheel-drive models and 1.3-liter front-wheel-drive configurations. It generally offers reliable and refined gear shifts for highway cruising.

## Electrical

### Shifter and Park Sensor

Drivetrain components generally hold up well, though some owners experience issues with electronic park sensor recognition.

**Shift to Park Warning Defect**
- **Symptoms:** When placing the vehicle in park, the information center fails to recognize it, displaying a 'Shift to Park' warning, keeping the radio on, preventing complete shutdown, and causing the vehicle to drain the battery or remain in accessory mode.
- **Solution:** Have a dealer inspect the shifter assembly and replace faulty microswitches or wiring harness connectors.

## Exterior

### Rear Seat LATCH Anchors

The interior cabin provides a comfortable layout, though buyers should verify physical child seat anchor presence if traveling with young children.

**Missing LATCH Anchor Hardware**
- **Symptoms:** Owners attempting to install child safety seats find that lower LATCH anchors are missing despite being marked on the seats and described in the owner's manual.
- **Solution:** Consult a Buick dealership to verify vehicle build specifications and have any missing anchor hardware properly installed or inspected.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V440000: Equipment:Mechanical:Accessory Scissor/Screw/Bottle Jack/Lift:Stand](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Buick&model=Encore+GX&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** A fractured jack can cause the vehicle to collapse, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the jack, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The Buick Encore GX presents a comfortable and reasonably efficient option for used vehicle buyers, particularly when equipped with the upgraded 1.3-liter engine. While overall mechanical reliability is solid, prospective owners should thoroughly test the shifter mechanism for any 'Shift to Park' warnings during a pre-purchase inspection to avoid frustrating electrical inconveniences.
