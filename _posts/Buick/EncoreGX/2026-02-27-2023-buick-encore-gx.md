# 2023 Buick Encore GX Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2023 Buick Encore GX enters the used subcompact SUV market offering modern styling, nimble proportions, and a well-appointed cabin. As drivers report initial mileage experiences, some owners have highlighted emerging electrical glitches, powertrain sensor concerns, and steering anomalies. Prospective buyers should thoroughly test drive prospective vehicles to evaluate driving dynamics, driver-assist features, and all onboard electronics.

---

## Engine

### 1.2L Turbocharged I3

This base 1.2-liter turbocharged three-cylinder engine generates approximately 137 horsepower and is typically paired with a continuously variable transmission. It provides efficient daily commuting performance, though buyers seeking more spirited acceleration may prefer the larger engine option.

### 1.3L Turbocharged I3

This upgraded 1.3-liter turbocharged three-cylinder engine produces around 155 horsepower and offers lively performance for everyday driving. It is generally paired with front-wheel drive via a CVT or all-wheel drive via a 9-speed automatic transmission.

**Auto Stop-Start Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may unexpectedly shut off while driving and fail to restart when the auto stop-start feature engages, occasionally stranding the driver in traffic.
- **Solution:** Owners should consult a certified dealer or technician to inspect the powertrain control module and electrical charging systems for potential software updates or component faults.

**Emissions Purge Pump and ECM Faults**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may experience diagnostic trouble codes such as P0521 or issues related to the evaporative emissions purge pump.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified technician diagnose the specific fault codes and replace or recalibrate faulty emissions system components as necessary.

## Transmission

### Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)

This continuously variable transmission is paired exclusively with front-wheel-drive models, prioritizing fuel efficiency and smooth power delivery in urban environments.

### 9-Speed Automatic Transmission

This traditional nine-speed automatic transmission comes paired with all-wheel-drive configurations, providing confident power delivery across varied road conditions.

**Mechanical Noise and Acceleration Loss**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may produce mechanical noises and refuse to accelerate above low speeds, indicating potential internal mechanical failure.
- **Solution:** Requires professional towing and a comprehensive transmission inspection by a certified service center.

## Steering

### Steering Assembly and ESC

Electric power steering systems deliver speed-sensitive steering assist but rely heavily on stable electronic module communication.

**Unresponsive Steering and Warning Messages**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may experience unexpected vehicle pulling or bouncing, accompanied by dashboard warning messages such as "Service ESC" and "Service Power Steering" alongside reduced brake responsiveness.
- **Solution:** A qualified technician must scan the electronic stability and power steering modules for stored fault codes and inspect steering sensor connections.

## Electrical

### Infotainment and Center Display

The vehicle's central electronics and touchscreen interface manage core convenience and navigation features.

**Intermittent Screen Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** The center display screen may freeze, appear grainy or corrupted, fail to connect to smartphones via USB-C, or unexpectedly activate the backup camera while driving forward.
- **Solution:** Perform a system restart, check for software updates at the dealership, or have the infotainment module inspected if the freezing persists.

## Safety

### Braking and Forward Collision Systems

Active safety systems and conventional hydraulic brakes work together to maintain vehicle control and help prevent collisions.

**Brake Grabbing and Pedal Lockup**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle's brakes may abruptly grab when approaching slower traffic, or the brake pedal may feel completely locked up prior to starting the engine.
- **Solution:** Have the brake pedal assembly, vacuum or electronic assist units, and forward collision avoidance sensors inspected by a professional mechanic.
