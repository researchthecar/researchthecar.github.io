# 2016 Buick Verano Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2016 Buick Verano offers a comfortable, quiet ride and an upscale cabin for a compact sedan, but prospective buyers should carefully evaluate its mechanical and electrical reliability history. While many owners enjoy its smooth highway manners and composed handling, specific recurring complaints regarding powertrain durability, electrical glitches, and safety sensor malfunctions have been reported. A thorough pre-purchase inspection is recommended to ensure the vehicle has been well-maintained and that all electronic systems and major mechanical components operate properly.

---

## Engine

### 2.4L 4-Cylinder Engine

The standard 2.4-liter direct-injected four-cylinder engine produces 180 horsepower and 171 lb-ft of torque, providing adequate daily power. While generally smooth, it requires diligent oil monitoring as some higher-mileage examples experience increased oil consumption.

**Excessive Oil Consumption and Engine Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** The engine may consume significant amounts of oil due to piston ring wear or emissions system issues, potentially leading to sudden engine stalling without adequate warning lights.
- **Solution:** Check engine oil levels frequently between changes and consult a qualified technician to inspect piston rings and the crankcase ventilation system if oil loss is observed.

### 2.0L Turbocharged 4-Cylinder Engine

The available 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine generates a more robust 250 horsepower and 260 lb-ft of torque, offering much quicker acceleration and responsive performance. It delivers strong power for highway passing, though premium fuel is recommended.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 6-speed automatic transmission pairs with both engine options to deliver power to the front wheels. While shifts are generally smooth when functioning correctly, premature wear or module issues can occur in some vehicles.

**Premature Transmission Failure and Harsh Shifting**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may experience harsh gear engagement, sudden vehicle jolts, or complete transmission failure typically approaching higher mileages.
- **Solution:** Inspect transmission fluid condition, check for diagnostic trouble codes, and have a professional evaluate shifting performance during a test drive.

## Electrical

### Body Electrical and Sensors

Electrical complaints center around intermittent body control glitches, sensor corrosion from moisture exposure, and accessory malfunctions.

**Erratic Door Locks and Radio Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** Doors may lock and unlock independently, and the infotainment system or radio may fail to shut off properly after the vehicle is turned off.
- **Solution:** Diagnose body control modules, relays, and door actuators, and replace faulty electrical components as necessary.

**Side Detection Sensor Corrosion**
- **Symptoms:** Blind-spot and side detection sensors can become exposed to moisture, leading to corrosion and subsequent failure of related electronic features.
- **Solution:** Inspect side sensor wiring harnesses and housings for water intrusion and replace corroded modules.
- **Cost:** $2,000

## Safety

### Air Bag System

Safety system grievances primarily involve airbag warning lights and passenger presence detection sensor faults that require professional diagnosis.

**Passenger Seat Occupant Sensor Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** The passenger seat sensor may fail to properly detect an occupant, causing the 'Air Bag Off' or 'Service Air Bag' warning lights to illuminate incorrectly.
- **Solution:** Have a dealership or repair facility recalibrate or replace the front passenger seat weight sensor module.
- **Cost:** $1,720

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V502000: Electrical System:Ignition](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Buick&model=Verano&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 114, "Theft Protection and Rollaway Prevention." If the key is removed without the transmission in PARK, the vehicle may rollaway as occupants are exiting, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will inspect and if necessary replace the key cylinder lock housing, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V595000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Hoses, Lines/Piping, And Fittings](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Buick&model=Verano&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** If the fuel line contacts the engine, the fuel line may wear, resulting in a fuel leak and increasing the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will replace the fuel line assembly, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2016 Buick Verano is a comfortable and quiet compact sedan that can represent an attractive used car value if inspected carefully. Buyers should pay close attention to engine oil levels, transmission shift quality, and the proper operation of all electronic and safety sensors. A comprehensive pre-purchase inspection by a qualified mechanic will help uncover any latent issues before finalizing a purchase.
