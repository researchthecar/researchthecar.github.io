# 2017 Buick Verano Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2017 Buick Verano, buyers will find a compact sedan that generally offers a quiet ride and comfortable interior. However, prospective owners should review available vehicle history carefully, as reports submitted to federal safety regulators highlight intermittent electrical glitches and occasional mechanical concerns. Keeping an eye on these specific areas during a pre-purchase inspection can help ensure a smoother ownership experience.

---

## Engine

### 2.4L 4-Cylinder Engine

The 2.4-liter naturally aspirated four-cylinder engine produces 180 horsepower and 171 lb-ft of torque, providing adequate daily power. It is a generally straightforward powerplant, though buyers should verify routine maintenance history.

### 1.5L Turbocharged 4-Cylinder Engine

The 1.5-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine generates 160 horsepower and 184 lb-ft of torque, offering enhanced low-end responsiveness. This option balances efficiency with pep, though turbo systems require clean oil to maintain longevity.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

The six-speed automatic transmission delivers power smoothly to the front wheels in standard driving conditions. Regular fluid changes are recommended to support long-term reliability and shift quality.

## Steering

### Steering Linkage

The steering system generally performs well, though mechanical wear in the linkage components can occasionally occur.

**Steering Gear Linkage Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have experienced sudden component failure within the steering linkage, making directional control difficult.
- **Solution:** Inspect the steering rack, outer tie rods, and associated linkage for excessive play, wear, or physical damage, and replace worn parts immediately.

## Electrical

### Blind Spot and Detection Systems

Safety technology enhances driver awareness, though sensor modules can occasionally trigger warning messages requiring diagnostic checks.

**Side Detection System Warning**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may repeatedly see a 'service side detection system' message upon vehicle startup, disabling blind-spot monitoring features.
- **Solution:** Scan the vehicle's computer modules for diagnostic trouble codes related to the side radar sensors and check sensor wiring harnesses for moisture or damage.

## Exterior

### Doors and Windows

Interior convenience features are generally comfortable, but door latches and window regulators can occasionally require mechanical attention.

**Door Latch and Window Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** Some owners have discovered doors failing to open, close, or secure properly, alongside front windows that refuse to roll down.
- **Solution:** Inspect door lock actuators, internal door linkages, and the window regulator assembly, replacing damaged mechanisms as needed.

## Interior

### Power Distribution and Dashboard Electronics

Owner reports indicate occasional electrical issues affecting dashboard displays and accessory power.

**Sudden Electrical Shutoff and Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have reported dashboard electronics completely shutting off while driving, accompanied by heavy steering and engine stalling.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified mechanic inspect the battery terminals, main electrical grounds, and ignition switch or power distribution modules for loose connections or faults.

**Power Loss and Stability Control Warnings**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicles may experience temporary power drops accompanied by the radio and power steering cycling on and off, along with 'Service Stability Track' warning messages.
- **Solution:** Diagnose the body control module, wheel speed sensors, and charging system voltage levels to isolate communication or power supply glitches.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V502000: Electrical System:Ignition](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Buick&model=Verano&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 114, "Theft Protection and Rollaway Prevention." If the key is removed without the transmission in PARK, the vehicle may rollaway as occupants are exiting, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will inspect and if necessary replace the key cylinder lock housing, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2017 Buick Verano offers a refined driving dynamic and comfortable cabin for used car buyers, but it is not entirely immune to issues. Particular attention should be paid to the electrical system and door mechanisms during a pre-purchase inspection. Verifying that all electronics and safety features operate correctly will help secure a reliable and satisfying purchase.
