**Introduction to the 2016 Buick Verano**

The 2016 Buick Verano is a compact luxury sedan that offers a comfortable and stylish driving experience. It comes with a variety of engine and transmission options, including a 2.4L naturally aspirated inline-four making 180 horsepower and 171 lb-ft of torque paired with a six-speed automatic transmission, a 2.0L turbocharged inline-four making 250 horsepower and 260 lb-ft of torque paired with a six-speed automatic transmission, and a 1.4L turbocharged inline-four making 153 horsepower and 177 lb-ft of torque paired with a continuously variable automatic transmission (CVT). While the Verano is praised for its comfortable ride, spacious interior, and impressive fuel economy, some owners have reported issues with certain components.

**Engine**

**2.4L Naturally Aspirated Inline-Four**

* **Valve Cover Gasket Leak:** Oil may leak from the valve cover gasket, causing a burning oil smell and potential engine damage.
    * Solution: Replace the valve cover gasket.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
    * Recalls: None Found

**2.0L Turbocharged Inline-Four**

* **Air Intake Tube Cracking:** The air intake tube may crack, causing the engine to lose power.
    * Solution: Replace the air intake tube.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**Six-Speed Automatic Transmission**

* **Transmission Fluid Leak:** Transmission fluid may leak from the transmission pan gasket or other seals, causing transmission slippage and potential failure.
    * Solution: Replace the leaking gasket or seal.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600
    * Recalls: None Found

**Continuously Variable Automatic Transmission (CVT)**

* **CVT Pulley Failure:** The CVT pulley may fail, causing the transmission to shudder or slip.
    * Solution: Replace the CVT pulley.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500
    * Recalls: 17V321000, 15V444000

**Suspension**

* **Rear Suspension Sagging:** The rear suspension may sag over time, causing the rear of the vehicle to sit lower than the front.
    * Solution: Replace the rear suspension components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $600 - $1,000
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain:** The battery may drain prematurely, leaving the vehicle unable to start.
    * Solution: Diagnose and repair the cause of the battery drain, which could be a fault in an electrical component.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Sunroof Malfunction:** The sunroof may fail to open, close, or tilt properly.
    * Solution: Replace or repair the sunroof motor, switch, or other related components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,200
    * Recalls: 15V311000

**Exterior**

* **Headlight Condensation:** Condensation may form inside the headlight assemblies.
    * Solution: Replace the headlight assemblies.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $600
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Windshield Washer Fluid Spraying Incorrectly:** The windshield washer fluid may spray incorrectly or leak.
    * Solution: Replace or adjust the windshield washer pump or tubing.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Climate Control Malfunction:** The climate control system may fail to properly regulate the temperature or airflow.
    * Solution: Diagnose and repair the faulty component, such as the climate control module or actuators.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Loose Seat Components:** The seat upholstery or foam may become loose over time.
    * Solution: Replace or repair the loose seat components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2016 Buick Verano is a generally reliable vehicle, but it has some reported issues that owners should be aware of. The engine and transmission issues can be somewhat costly to repair, especially for the CVT transmission. However, the majority of the issues are less severe and can be resolved with relatively low repair costs. It's important to note that these issues may not affect all Verano models, and proper maintenance and driving habits can extend the life of the vehicle's components.
