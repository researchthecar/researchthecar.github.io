**1994 Buick Skylark**

The 1994 Buick Skylark is a mid-size sedan that was offered with a variety of engine and transmission options. The base engine was a 3.1L V6 producing 160 horsepower and 185 lb-ft of torque. A more powerful 3.3L V6 producing 185 horsepower and 220 lb-ft of torque was also available. Both engines were mated to a 4-speed automatic transmission.

Owners of the 1994 Buick Skylark have reported a number of issues with their vehicles. These issues range from minor annoyances to more serious problems that can affect the safety and reliability of the vehicle.

**Engine**

* **3.1L V6**
    * **Intake manifold gasket failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: Coolant or oil leaking from the intake manifold, overheating, rough idling
        * Solution: Replace the intake manifold gasket
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+
        * Recalls: None Found
* **3.3L V6**
    * **Head gasket failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: Coolant or oil leaking from the head gasket, overheating, white smoke from the exhaust
        * Solution: Replace the head gasket
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

* **4-speed automatic transmission**
    * **Shift solenoid failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: Transmission slipping or refusing to shift gears, check engine light
        * Solution: Replace the shift solenoid
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Front struts**
    * **Strut mount failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: Clunking or rattling noises from the front suspension, uneven tire wear
        * Solution: Replace the strut mounts
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
        * Recalls: None Found
* **Rear shocks**
    * **Shock absorber failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: Bouncy or unstable ride, poor handling
        * Solution: Replace the shock absorbers
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Alternator failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Dimming lights, flickering electronics, difficulty starting the engine
    * Solution: Replace the alternator
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Battery failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Difficulty starting the engine, dead battery
    * Solution: Replace the battery
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Door handles**
    * **Handle failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: Door handle breaking or becoming loose
        * Solution: Replace the door handle
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
        * Recalls: None Found
* **Power window motors**
    * **Motor failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: Power windows not working
        * Solution: Replace the power window motor
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Climate control system**
    * **AC compressor failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: AC not blowing cold air
        * Solution: Replace the AC compressor
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
        * Recalls: None Found
* **Dashboard**
    * **Cracking**
        * Description/Symptoms: Cracks forming on the dashboard
        * Solution: Replace the dashboard
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 1994 Buick Skylark has a number of reported issues that can affect the safety, reliability, and comfort of the vehicle. Some of the most common issues include intake manifold gasket failure, head gasket failure, shift solenoid failure, and strut mount failure. While the severity and cost of these issues can vary, it is important to be aware of them when considering purchasing or owning a 1994 Buick Skylark.
