**Introduction:**

The 1995 Buick Skylark is a midsize sedan that was powered by a 3.1L V6 engine producing 160 horsepower and 185 lb-ft of torque. It was paired with a 4-speed automatic transmission. The Skylark was praised for its comfortable ride, spacious interior, and affordable price point. However, it was criticized for its lack of power and sluggish performance.

**Engine**

**3.1L V6**

* **Intake Manifold Gasket Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Coolant or oil leaks from the intake manifold, causing engine overheating or performance issues.
    * Solution: Replacement of intake manifold gasket.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**4-Speed Automatic**

* **Shift Solenoid Malfunction:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Transmission slipping or delayed shifting, especially during acceleration or gear changes.
    * Solution: Replacement or repair of shift solenoid.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $250 - $600+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Front Strut Mounts Worn:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Noises when turning or driving over bumps, uneven tire wear.
    * Solution: Replacement of strut mounts.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+ for both mounts
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Alternator Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Dimming lights, trouble starting, battery not charging properly.
    * Solution: Replacement of alternator.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Battery Drain:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Battery dies quickly, especially when the car is not running.
    * Solution: Diagnosis and repair of parasitic drain, such as faulty interior lights or electronics.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Headlight Lens Fogging:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Headlight lenses become cloudy or hazy, reducing visibility at night.
    * Solution: Sanding and polishing headlight lenses.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+ per lens
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Dashboard Cracking:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Dashboard develops cracks or splits, especially around the edges or near vents.
    * Solution: Repair or replacement of dashboard.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Overall Assessment:**

The 1995 Buick Skylark is generally a reliable vehicle but has some common issues that can be costly to repair. Engine and transmission problems are relatively rare, but electrical issues and suspension wear can be more frequent. Exterior and interior issues are typically minor and cosmetic, but dashboard cracking can be a significant expense. Overall, the Skylark is a value-oriented sedan with a comfortable ride, but potential buyers should be aware of the potential issues before making a purchase.
