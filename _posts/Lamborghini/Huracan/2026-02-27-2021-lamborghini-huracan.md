# 2021 Lamborghini Huracan Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2021 Lamborghini Huracan is an exotic supercar offering high-performance thrills and everyday usability by supercar standards. When shopping for a used Huracan, buyers should expect high maintenance demands typical of V10 Italian exotics, though powertrain options are generally robust when properly serviced. While widespread structural defects are rare in owner reports, prospective owners should inspect the vehicle thoroughly and verify service history.

---

## Engine

### 5.2L Naturally Aspirated V10

The 5.2-liter naturally aspirated V10 engine produces around 602 to 631 horsepower depending on the specific variant (such as the EVO or STO) and delivers exhilarating performance. This high-revving powerplant is generally robust, though regular professional maintenance and fluid checks are critical for longevity.

## Transmission

### 7-Speed Dual-Clutch Automatic

The 7-speed dual-clutch transmission provides lightning-fast shifts in both automatic and manual paddle-shifted modes. It handles high-output driving well, though clutch wear during heavy track use or low-speed city crawling should be evaluated during a pre-purchase inspection.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V466000: Back Over Prevention:Display Function](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lamborghini&model=Huracan&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** In temperatures below 41°F, the rearview camera may not display due to a software error in the infotainment system. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 111, "Rear Visibility." A rearview camera that does not display an image reduces the driver's rear view, increasing the risk.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the instrument cluster software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V220000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission:Fluid/Lubricant](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lamborghini&model=Huracan&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** Clutch slippage can cause a loss of drive power and increase the risk of a crash. A transmission oil leak in the presence of hot exhaust components can increase the risk of fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and correct the transmission oil level, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V826000: Electrical System](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lamborghini&model=Huracan&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 108, "Lamps, Reflective Devices, and Associated Equipment." Incorrectly adjusted headlights can reduce the visibility of oncoming traffic, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the software to deactivate the headlight functions for Tourist Mode and the Adaptive Front-lighting System (AFS), free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2021 Lamborghini Huracan stands out as a thrilling and relatively dependable choice within the exotic supercar segment. Buyers should prioritize professional pre-purchase inspections, verify service records, and budget accordingly for specialized maintenance and upkeep.
