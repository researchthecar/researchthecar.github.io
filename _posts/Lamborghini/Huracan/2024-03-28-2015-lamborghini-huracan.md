**Introduction**

The 2015 Lamborghini Huracan is a mid-engine sports car that was unveiled at the 2014 Geneva Motor Show. It replaced the Lamborghini Gallardo and was powered by a 5.2-liter naturally aspirated V10 engine that produced 610 horsepower and 413 lb-ft of torque. The Huracan was available with a seven-speed dual-clutch transmission or a six-speed manual transmission.

People who like the Huracan praise its stunning design, blistering performance, and engaging driving dynamics. However, some people have experienced issues with the Huracan, which are detailed below.

**Engine**

**5.2-Liter Naturally Aspirated V10 Engine**

* **Piston Ring Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Loss of compression, excessive oil consumption, misfires
    * Solution: Replace piston rings
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $4,000 - $6,000+
    * Recalls: NHTSA Campaign Number 15V362000
* **Valvetrain Ticking**
    * Description/Symptoms: Ticking or chattering sound from the engine
    * Solution: Replace camshaft or lifters
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**Seven-Speed Dual-Clutch Transmission**

* **Clutch Slipping**
    * Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, difficulty shifting gears
    * Solution: Replace clutch pack
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,500 - $4,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Six-Speed Manual Transmission**

* **Synchronizer Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears, grinding noises
    * Solution: Replace synchronizers
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Front Suspension Coil Spring Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Squeaking or rattling noises from the front suspension, reduced ride height
    * Solution: Replace coil springs
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain**
    * Description/Symptoms: Battery goes dead after sitting for a short period of time
    * Solution: Replace or reprogram battery management system
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **ECU Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Engine misfires, loss of power, difficulty starting
    * Solution: Replace ECU
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Exterior Mirror Issue**
    * Description/Symptoms: Side mirrors do not close properly
    * Solution: Replace mirror motors
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Infotainment System Crashing**
    * Description/Symptoms: Infotainment system freezes or crashes
    * Solution: Update software or replace infotainment unit
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2015 Lamborghini Huracan is a high-performance sports car that has received praise for its stunning design, blistering performance, and engaging driving dynamics. However, some people have experienced issues with the Huracan, including engine, transmission, suspension, electrical, exterior, and interior problems.

The severity and cost of the issues vary depending on the specific problem. Some issues, such as battery drain or mirror issues, are relatively minor and inexpensive to fix. However, other issues, such as piston ring failure or ECU failure, can be more serious and expensive to repair.
