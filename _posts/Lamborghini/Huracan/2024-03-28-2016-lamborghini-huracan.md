**Introduction to the 2016 Lamborghini Huracan**

The 2016 Lamborghini Huracan is a mid-engine sports car that was first introduced in 2014. It is powered by a naturally aspirated 5.2-liter V10 engine that produces 602 horsepower and 413 lb-ft of torque. The Huracan is available with a 7-speed dual-clutch transmission or a 6-speed manual transmission.

The Huracan has been praised for its performance, handling, and design. However, it has also been criticized for its high price, lack of practicality, and uncomfortable ride.

**Engine**

* **5.2-liter V10**

    * **Oil leak:** Oil can leak from the engine's oil pan or valve covers.
    Symptoms: Oil spots under the car, low oil level warning light.
    Solution: Replace the oil pan gasket or valve cover gaskets, or re-seal the components.
    Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2000+
    Recalls: NHTSA ID 19V965000
    * **Timing chain tensioner failure:** The timing chain tensioner can fail, causing the timing chain to skip and the engine to lose power.
    Symptoms: Engine misfires, loss of power, Check Engine light.
    Solution: Replace the timing chain tensioner and timing chain.
    Estimated Cost to Fix: $3000 - $5000+
    Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

* **7-speed dual-clutch transmission**

    * **Clutch slippage:** The clutch can slip, causing the car to lose power or shudder when shifting.
    Symptoms: Loss of power, shuddering when shifting, Check Engine light.
    Solution: Replace the clutch.
    Estimated Cost to Fix: $1000 - $2000+
    Recalls: None Found

* **6-speed manual transmission**

    * **Synchro ring failure:** The synchro rings can fail, making it difficult or impossible to shift gears.
    Symptoms: Grinding noise when shifting, difficulty shifting gears, Check Engine light.
    Solution: Replace the synchro rings.
    Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1000+
    Recalls: None Found

* **No Issues Found**

**Electrical**

* **Battery drain:** The battery can drain prematurely, leaving the car unable to start.
    Symptoms: Car won't start, battery warning light.
    Solution: Replace the battery or diagnose and repair the electrical system.
    Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    Recalls: None Found
* **Electrical gremlins:** Various electrical issues can occur, such as dashboard malfunctions, power window failures, and lighting problems.
    Symptoms: Malfunctioning gauges, power window problems, lighting problems.
    Solution: Diagnose and repair the specific electrical issue.
    Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Panel gaps:** There can be uneven gaps between the body panels, particularly around the doors and hood.
    Symptoms: Uneven gaps between panels, misaligned body panels.
    Solution: Adjust the body panels or replace damaged parts.
    Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2000+
    Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Carbon fiber trim delamination:** The carbon fiber trim on the dashboard and center console can delaminate, causing it to peel or bubble.
    Symptoms: Delaminated carbon fiber trim, peeling or bubbling trim.
    Solution: Replace the affected carbon fiber trim.
    Estimated Cost to Fix: $1000 - $2000+
    Recalls: None Found
* **Loose or rattling interior panels:** The interior panels, such as the door panels or dashboard, can become loose or rattle over time.
    Symptoms: Loose or rattling interior panels, squeaks or rattles when driving.
    Solution: Tighten or replace loose panels.
    Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2016 Lamborghini Huracan is a high-performance sports car that is known for its stunning design and exhilarating driving experience. However, it has also been plagued by a number of issues, including oil leaks, timing chain tensioner failures, clutch slippage, synchro ring failures, electrical problems, panel gaps, and interior trim delamination.

The severity and cost of these issues can vary greatly depending on the specific problem. Some issues, such as electrical gremlins or loose interior panels, may be relatively minor and inexpensive to fix. However, other issues, such as engine failures or transmission problems, can be major and very expensive to repair.
