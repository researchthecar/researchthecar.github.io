The 2021 Lamborghini Huracan Evo is a mid-engine sports car known for its exhilarating performance and striking design. It's powered by a naturally aspirated 5.2-liter V10 engine that produces 602 horsepower and 413 lb-ft of torque in the rear-wheel-drive models and 631 horsepower and 443 lb-ft of torque in the all-wheel-drive models. The engine is paired with a 7-speed dual-clutch transmission. Owners often praise the Huracan's responsive handling, powerful engine, and distinctive exhaust note. Some criticisms include the relatively firm ride, limited storage space, and high running costs.

**Engine**

**5.2L V10**

*   **Oil Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks from various engine seals and gaskets have been reported. This can manifest as visible oil drips under the car, a burning oil smell, or low oil levels indicated on the dashboard.
    *   Solution: Replacing the affected seals and gaskets.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $5,000+ (depending on the leak location and complexity of the repair).
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Fuel Injector Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported issues with the fuel injectors, leading to poor engine performance, misfires, and difficulty starting.
    *   Solution: Replacement of faulty fuel injectors.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+ (depending on the number of injectors needing replacement).
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**7-Speed Dual-Clutch Transmission**

*   **Clutch Wear/Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Premature clutch wear or failure can occur, especially with aggressive driving. Symptoms include slipping during gear changes, jerky acceleration, and difficulty engaging gears.
    *   Solution: Replacing the clutch assembly.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $8,000 - $12,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Mechatronic Unit Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The mechatronic unit, which controls the transmission's hydraulic system, can sometimes fail. This can result in shifting problems, error messages, and complete transmission failure.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the mechatronic unit.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $6,000 - $10,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Magnetic Ride Control (Optional) Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some vehicles equipped with the optional magnetic ride control system have experienced issues with the system's dampers. This can lead to uneven ride quality, clunking noises, and warning lights on the dashboard.
    *   Solution: Replacement of faulty dampers.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+ per damper
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Glitches**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The infotainment system can experience glitches, such as screen freezing, slow response times, and Bluetooth connectivity problems.
    *   Solution: Software updates or replacement of the infotainment unit.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $5,000+ (depending on the severity and if the whole unit needs replaced)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Sensor Malfunctions**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Various sensors throughout the car (e.g., ABS sensors, tire pressure sensors) can malfunction, triggering warning lights and affecting vehicle performance.
    *   Solution: Replacement of faulty sensors.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+ (depending on the sensor)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Carbon Fiber Component Delamination**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Vehicles with optional carbon fiber exterior components (e.g., spoilers, splitters) have experienced delamination or cracking of the carbon fiber.
    *   Solution: Repair or replacement of the affected carbon fiber component.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $10,000+ (depending on the part and the extent of damage)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Sticking Buttons**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported that the buttons on the center console will stick, especially in high-heat environments.
    *   Solution: Button replacement
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+ (depending on location)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

The 2021 Lamborghini Huracan, while an exhilarating performance machine, can be prone to some costly issues. The most concerning are potential engine oil leaks and clutch/mechatronic unit issues with the transmission, which can both result in repair bills exceeding $10,000. Electrical glitches and suspension problems are also potential concerns. Overall, prospective buyers should be aware of these potential issues and factor in the high cost of maintenance and repairs associated with owning a high-performance exotic car like the Huracan.
