Here's a breakdown of common issues and complaints reported by owners of the 2019 Lamborghini Huracan, categorized by component. The 2019 Huracan is admired for its aggressive styling, raw performance, and thrilling driving experience. However, some owners have reported issues ranging from minor annoyances to more significant mechanical problems. The 2019 model year of the Huracan primarily features a 5.2-liter V10 engine. The base Huracan produces around 573 horsepower and 398 lb-ft of torque, while the Performante and Evo variants boost output to approximately 631 horsepower and 443 lb-ft of torque. All models typically utilize a 7-speed dual-clutch transmission. Some common complaints include the car's low ground clearance, which makes it susceptible to scraping, and the relatively stiff ride, especially on rough roads. Others have voiced concerns about the high cost of maintenance and repairs.

**Engine**

**5.2L V10**

*   **Oil Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks are reported from various engine seals and gaskets. This can lead to a burning smell, visible oil spots under the car, and low oil levels.
    *   Solution: Replacing the affected seals and gaskets.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $3000+, depending on the location and complexity of the repair.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Direct Injection Carbon Buildup**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Due to the direct injection system, carbon buildup on the intake valves is a common issue. This can lead to reduced performance, rough idling, and decreased fuel economy.
    *   Solution: Manual cleaning of the intake valves (walnut blasting) or chemical cleaning.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $2000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Coolant Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaks from hoses, the water pump, or the radiator can occur. Symptoms include a sweet smell, low coolant levels, and overheating.
    *   Solution: Replacing the leaking component (hose, water pump, or radiator).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**7-Speed Dual-Clutch Transmission**

*   **Clutch Wear**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Premature clutch wear is reported, especially in cars that are driven aggressively or frequently in stop-and-go traffic. Symptoms include slipping, jerky shifts, and difficulty engaging gears.
    *   Solution: Clutch replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $5,000 - $10,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Mechatronic Unit Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The mechatronic unit, which controls the transmission's shifting, can fail. Symptoms include error messages on the dashboard, difficulty shifting, and complete transmission failure.
    *   Solution: Replacing or rebuilding the mechatronic unit.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $3,000 - $8,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Damper Issues (Magnetic Ride Control)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Models equipped with the optional magnetic ride control suspension can experience damper failures. Symptoms include a rough ride, uneven handling, and error messages related to the suspension.
    *   Solution: Replacing the affected damper(s).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $5,000+ per damper
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Sensor Failures**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Various sensors (e.g., oxygen sensors, wheel speed sensors) can fail, leading to warning lights on the dashboard and potentially affecting engine performance or safety systems.
    *   Solution: Replacing the faulty sensor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+ per sensor
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Battery Drain**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported issues with excessive battery drain, especially when the car is not driven regularly.
    *   Solution: Diagnosing and addressing the source of the drain (e.g., faulty module, parasitic draw). Replacing the battery.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+ for diagnostics; $300 - $1000+ for battery and related repairs.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Automatic Wing Malfunction**
    *   Description/Symptoms: On models equipped with an active rear wing, the mechanism can sometimes fail, resulting in the wing not deploying or retracting properly.
    *   Solution: Repair or replacement of the wing motor and control system.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1000 - $5000+ depending on the extent of the damage.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Infotainment System Glitches**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The infotainment system can experience glitches, such as freezing, slow response times, and connectivity issues.
    *   Solution: Software updates or replacement of the infotainment unit.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 (for software updates) - $2,000+ (for unit replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2019 Lamborghini Huracan is a high-performance vehicle that offers an exhilarating driving experience, but it is not without its potential issues. Engine oil leaks, carbon buildup, and clutch wear are common concerns that can lead to significant repair costs. Electrical issues and suspension problems can also arise. While many of these issues are typical of high-performance vehicles, the cost of repairs can be substantial. Potential buyers should be aware of these potential problems and budget accordingly or obtain a thorough pre-purchase inspection.
