The 2021 Lamborghini Aventador is a stunning supercar known for its aggressive styling, ferocious performance, and luxurious interior. It comes equipped with a naturally aspirated 6.5L V12, delivering an exhilarating 769 horsepower and 531 lb-ft of torque. The only transmission option available for the Aventador is a 7-speed ISR automated manual transmission.

While the Aventador is undoubtedly an exceptional vehicle, it has not been without its fair share of issues. To provide you with a comprehensive understanding of the problems associated with this supercar, I have compiled a detailed list of specific issues categorized by major components:

**Engine**
- **6.5L V12**
- **Intake Valve Failure:** Premature failure of intake valves, leading to reduced engine performance, misfires, and potentially catastrophic engine damage.
- **Solution:** Replacement of affected intake valves.
- **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $5,000 - $10,000+

**Transmission**
- **7-Speed ISR Automated Manual Transmission**
- **Clutch Failure:** Premature wear or failure of the clutch, resulting in difficulty shifting gears, grinding noises, and loss of power.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the clutch assembly.
- **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $3,000 - $5,000+

**Suspension**
- **Front Suspension Bushing Failure:** Failure of the front suspension bushings, causing excessive noise, vibration, and compromised handling.
- **Solution:** Replacement of worn or damaged bushings.
- **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $750 - $1,500

**Electrical**
- **Battery Drain:** Excessive battery drain, leading to premature battery failure and difficulty starting the vehicle.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and repair of the electrical system.
- **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $500 - $1,000+

**Exterior**
- **Carbon Fiber Body Panel Detachment:** Detachment or separation of carbon fiber body panels, potentially compromising the vehicle's structural integrity and aesthetics.
- **Solution:** Reattachment or replacement of affected panels.
- **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $2,000 - $5,000+

**Interior**
- **Infotainment System Freezing:** Occasional freezing or malfunctioning of the infotainment system, affecting navigation, audio, and other features.
- **Solution:** Software updates or hardware replacement.
- **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $500 - $2,000

**Summary**
While the Lamborghini Aventador is a marvel of engineering and performance, it has experienced several issues that have drawn the attention of owners and enthusiasts. The most severe and costly problems include intake valve failure, clutch failure, and front suspension bushing failure. However, it's important to note that these issues do not affect all Aventador models and that the severity and cost of repairs may vary depending on the specific circumstances and severity of the issue.
