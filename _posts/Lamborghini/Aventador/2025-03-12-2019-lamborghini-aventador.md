Here is a breakdown of common issues and complaints reported for the 2019 Lamborghini Aventador, categorized by major components and powertrain options. The 2019 Lamborghini Aventador is a high-performance supercar known for its striking design, powerful engine, and exhilarating driving experience. The main engine option is the 6.5L V12, producing 730-770 horsepower and 509-531 lb-ft of torque, depending on the trim level. A seven-speed ISR automated manual transmission is standard. People love the Aventador for its aggressive styling, incredible acceleration, and distinctive exhaust note. Common dislikes include its relatively harsh ride, limited practicality, and high running costs.

**Engine**

**6.5L V12**

*   **Oil Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks may occur from various engine seals and gaskets. Symptoms include visible oil leaks on the ground or engine components and low oil levels.
    *   Solution: Replacing the leaking seals or gaskets.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $3,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

*   **Ignition Coil Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The ignition coils can fail, leading to misfires, rough running, and a check engine light.
    *   Solution: Replacing the faulty ignition coils.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

*   **Coolant Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaks around coolant hoses, reservoirs, and radiators. Overheating, white steam coming from the engine, or pooling coolant under the vehicle may also be signs.
    *   Solution: Replacing the faulty coolant hoses, reservoirs, or radiator components.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**7-Speed ISR Automated Manual**

*   **Clutch Wear**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The ISR transmission uses a single-clutch system, which can experience premature wear, especially with aggressive driving. Symptoms include slipping, jerky shifts, and difficulty engaging gears.
    *   Solution: Clutch replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $5,000 - $10,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

*   **Hydraulic System Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The hydraulic system that actuates the transmission can experience leaks or pump failures, leading to shifting problems and error messages.
    *   Solution: Repairing or replacing the hydraulic pump or associated components.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $7,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Damper Failures**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The adaptive dampers can fail, leading to a loss of ride control and comfort. Symptoms include a bouncy or unstable ride and error messages.
    *   Solution: Replacing the faulty dampers.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $3,000 - $10,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

*   **Suspension Bushing Wear**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Over time, suspension bushings can wear out, leading to clunking noises and reduced handling precision.
    *   Solution: Replacing the worn suspension bushings.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Sensor Malfunctions**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Various sensors throughout the vehicle can malfunction, leading to error messages, warning lights, and drivability issues.
    *   Solution: Replacing the faulty sensors.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

*   **Battery Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The battery can drain quickly, especially if the car is not driven regularly.
    *   Solution: Replacing the battery or using a battery tender.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

*   **Infotainment System Glitches**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The infotainment system can experience glitches, freezing, and connectivity issues.
    *   Solution: Software updates or component replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 - $2,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Hydraulic Lift System Malfunctions**
    * Description/Symptoms: Front lift will not fully extend or retract, or become intermittent.
    * Solution: Replace the hydraulic pump.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $4,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

*   **Carbon Fiber Delamination**
    *   Description/Symptoms: If equipped with exposed carbon fiber elements, delamination can occur due to UV exposure or impacts.
    *   Solution: Repair or replacement of the affected carbon fiber panels.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $10,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Stitching Coming Undone**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The stitching on the seats or dashboard may come undone.
    *   Solution: Restitching or replacing the affected panels.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $2,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

*   **Dashboard Creaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The dashboard can develop creaks and rattles.
    *   Solution: Tightening fasteners or adding sound-deadening material.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2019 Lamborghini Aventador, while a thrilling supercar, can suffer from several issues. Engine oil and coolant leaks, ignition coil failures, clutch wear, and hydraulic system problems in the transmission are some of the common mechanical concerns. Electrically, sensor malfunctions, battery drainage, and infotainment glitches are also possible. Suspension and damper failures can negatively impact the driving experience. Costs for repairs can range from a few hundred dollars to several thousand dollars, particularly for transmission or carbon fiber issues. Due to the low production volume of this vehicle, some issues have not been well documented and it is not uncommon to see no information on specific issues or recall information.
