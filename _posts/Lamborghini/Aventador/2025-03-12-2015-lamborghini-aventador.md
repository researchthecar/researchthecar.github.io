The 2015 Lamborghini Aventador is a mid-engine supercar known for its aggressive styling and exhilarating performance. It was primarily available with a 6.5-liter V12 engine producing 690 horsepower and 509 lb-ft of torque. Some owners love the raw power, distinctive design, and the unmistakable roar of the V12, while others are put off by the limited practicality, high running costs, and occasional reliability issues. The Aventador sends power to all four wheels through a 7-speed automated manual transmission.

**Engine**

**6.5L V12**

*   **Oil Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks can occur from various locations, including the valve covers, oil pan gasket, and around the oil cooler lines. Symptoms include visible oil drips under the car, a burning oil smell, and low oil levels.
    *   Solution: Replacing the affected gaskets and seals. Inspecting and tightening oil cooler line fittings may resolve minor leaks.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $3000+ depending on the location and severity.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Coil Pack Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Misfires, rough idling, and a check engine light may indicate a failing coil pack. Multiple coil packs may fail over time.
    *   Solution: Replacing the faulty coil pack(s).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+ per coil pack.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**7-Speed ISR (Independent Shifting Rod) Automated Manual**

*   **Clutch Wear/Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The single-clutch automated manual transmission is known for relatively short clutch life, especially with aggressive driving. Symptoms include clutch slippage, difficulty engaging gears, and jerky shifts.
    *   Solution: Replacing the clutch. Upgrading to an aftermarket clutch kit designed for improved durability may be considered.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $8,000 - $15,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Hydraulic Pump Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The hydraulic system responsible for clutch actuation and gear changes can experience pump failures. Symptoms include difficulty shifting, error messages on the dashboard, and a complete inability to select gears.
    *   Solution: Replacing the hydraulic pump.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $5,000 - $10,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Damper/Shock Absorber Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The electronically adjustable dampers can leak fluid, leading to reduced damping performance and a less comfortable ride.
    *   Solution: Replacing the leaking damper(s).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $5,000+ per damper.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Battery Drain**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The Aventador can suffer from battery drain issues if left unattended for extended periods. This is partly due to the numerous electronic systems that remain active even when the car is off.
    *   Solution: Using a battery tender or trickle charger when the car is not in use. Diagnosing and addressing any parasitic draws on the electrical system.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+ depending on the cause.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Sensor Malfunctions**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Various sensors, such as those related to the ABS, stability control, and tire pressure monitoring system (TPMS), can malfunction, triggering warning lights on the dashboard.
    *   Solution: Replacing the faulty sensor(s).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1000+ per sensor.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **No Issues Found**

**Interior**

*   **Sticking Buttons/Controls**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Certain buttons and controls on the center console or dashboard can become sticky or unresponsive due to the degradation of the soft-touch coating.
    *   Solution: Cleaning or replacing the affected buttons/controls.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+ per button/control.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

The 2015 Lamborghini Aventador is a thrilling but demanding supercar. The most common issues revolve around the transmission (clutch wear and hydraulic pump failures), engine oil leaks, and electrical gremlins. Repairs can be very expensive, especially those related to the transmission and suspension. Owners should be prepared for high maintenance costs and the potential for unexpected repairs. The severity of the issues can range from minor inconveniences to major mechanical failures that require extensive and costly repairs.
