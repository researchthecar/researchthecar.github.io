# 2004 Lamborghini Murcielago

### Overview
The 2004 Lamborghini Murciélago, a true supercar icon, represents a pinnacle of Italian automotive engineering. While celebrated for its breathtaking performance and exotic design, like any high-performance vehicle, it can present specific reliability considerations. This analysis focuses on documented technical issues beyond routine maintenance, offering insights into potential areas of concern for owners and enthusiasts. The Murciélago's complex systems, particularly its powerful V12 engine and advanced drivetrain, require specialized attention. Understanding these potential weak points is crucial for maintaining this exceptional machine.

---


## Engine

### 6.2L V12
**Cooling System Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Coolant loss, overheating, visible fluid leaks around hoses and radiator.
- **Solution:** Replacement of faulty hoses, clamps, radiator, or water pump.
- **Cost:** $1,500 - $3,000

**Clutch Actuator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Difficulty shifting gears, clutch pedal not returning, warning lights on the dashboard.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of the clutch actuator and associated hydraulic components.
- **Cost:** $2,000 - $4,500

**Oil Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Visible oil stains under the vehicle, burning oil smell, low oil level.
- **Solution:** Replacement of seals, gaskets, or oil pan.
- **Cost:** $800 - $2,000


## Transmission

### E-Gear (Automated Manual)
**Hydraulic Pump Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Slow or jerky gear changes, inability to select gears, E-gear warning light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the hydraulic pump and fluid.
- **Cost:** $3,000 - $6,000

**Solenoid Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Erratic gear selection, 'gearbox fault' messages.
- **Solution:** Replacement of faulty solenoids.
- **Cost:** $1,000 - $2,500

## Suspension

### Adaptive Damping System
**Shock Absorber Leaks/Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Harsh ride, excessive body roll, fluid leaks from shock absorbers.
- **Solution:** Replacement of faulty shock absorbers.
- **Cost:** $2,000 - $5,000 (per axle)


## Electrical

### Infotainment System
**Screen Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** Flickering display, unresponsive touch screen, dead pixels.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of the infotainment unit.
- **Cost:** $1,000 - $3,000


### Window Regulators
**Failure to Operate**
- **Symptoms:** Power windows not moving up or down.
- **Solution:** Replacement of window regulator motors or mechanisms.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1,200


## Exterior

### Doors
**Scissor Door Mechanism Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Doors not opening or closing smoothly, difficulty latching.
- **Solution:** Lubrication, adjustment, or replacement of hydraulic struts and hinges.
- **Cost:** $1,000 - $3,000

### Climate Control
**AC Compressor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Lack of cold air, unusual noises from the AC system.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the AC compressor and refrigerant recharge.
- **Cost:** $1,500 - $3,000

### Summary Verdict
The 2004 Lamborghini Murciélago, while a mechanical marvel, demands diligent maintenance and specialized care. The most common reported issues revolve around the complex E-gear transmission, particularly its hydraulic system, and potential leaks within the V12 engine's cooling system. Electrical gremlins, such as infotainment screen malfunctions and window regulator failures, are also noted. The unique scissor door mechanisms can also require attention. Owners should budget for specialized servicing and be prepared for potentially high repair costs, especially for drivetrain and hydraulic components. Despite these potential challenges, with proper care, the Murciélago remains a highly desirable and exhilarating supercar.
