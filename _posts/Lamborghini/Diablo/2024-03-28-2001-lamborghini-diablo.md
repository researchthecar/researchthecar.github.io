**Introduction:**

The 2001 Lamborghini Diablo is an Italian supercar powered by a 6.0-liter V12 engine producing 550 horsepower and 457 lb-ft of torque. The engine is paired with a 5-speed manual transmission. The Diablo is known for its aggressive styling, impressive performance, and high reliability. However, like all vehicles, it has had its fair share of issues.

**Engine**

**6.0-liter V12**

* **Timing Chain Tensioner Failure:** The timing chain tensioner can fail, causing the timing chain to slip and potentially damaging the engine.
    * Symptoms: Engine noise, decreased performance, check engine light
    * Solution: Replace timing chain tensioner
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
* **Valve Guide Seal Leakage:** Valve guide seals can leak, causing excessive oil consumption and emissions.
    * Symptoms: Blue smoke from exhaust, increased oil consumption
    * Solution: Replace valve guide seals
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,000+

**Transmission**

**5-speed Manual**

* **Transmission Gear Grind:** The transmission may grind when shifting gears.
    * Symptoms: Grinding noise when shifting, difficulty shifting
    * Solution: Replace or repair transmission gear synchronizers
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,500 - $4,000+

**Suspension**

* **Front Upper Control Arm Bushing Failure:** Front upper control arm bushings can fail, causing excessive tire wear and handling problems.
    * Symptoms: Uneven tire wear, clunking noises, reduced handling
    * Solution: Replace control arm bushings
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
* **Rear Shock Absorber Failure:** Rear shock absorbers can fail, causing a rough ride and poor handling.
    * Symptoms: Rough ride, excessive bounce, reduced handling
    * Solution: Replace rear shock absorbers
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+

**Electrical**

* **Alternator Failure:** The alternator can fail, causing the battery to drain and the vehicle to stall.
    * Symptoms: Electrical system problems, battery warning light, stalling
    * Solution: Replace alternator
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,200+
* **Starter Failure:** The starter can fail, preventing the engine from starting.
    * Symptoms: No cranking when turning key, clicking noise from starter
    * Solution: Replace starter motor
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800+

**Exterior**

* **Door Handle Failure:** Door handles can fail, making it difficult to open the doors.
    * Symptoms: Loose door handle, difficulty opening doors
    * Solution: Replace door handles
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
* **Carbon Fiber Body Panel Cracking:** Carbon fiber body panels can crack, causing cosmetic damage.
    * Symptoms: Visible cracks in carbon fiber panels
    * Solution: Repair or replace carbon fiber panels
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+

**Interior**

* **Air Conditioning Failure:** The air conditioning system can fail, causing discomfort and overheating.
    * Symptoms: No cool air blowing, warm air blowing
    * Solution: Diagnose and repair air conditioning system
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+
* **Window Regulator Failure:** Window regulators can fail, preventing the windows from opening or closing properly.
    * Symptoms: Stuck windows, slow or uneven window movement
    * Solution: Replace window regulators
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
    * Recalls: NHTSA Campaign Number: 01V305000 - Window Regulator Switch May Malfunction

**Summary:**

The 2001 Lamborghini Diablo is a powerful and luxurious supercar that is generally reliable. However, it has some common issues that can be expensive to fix. The severity of the issues ranges from minor inconveniences to major mechanical failures. The estimated cost of repairs can vary depending on the specific issue and the availability of parts.
