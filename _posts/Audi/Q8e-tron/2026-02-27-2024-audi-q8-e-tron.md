# 2024 Audi Q8 e-tron Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2024 Audi Q8 e-tron represents Audi's refined luxury electric SUV lineup, offering dual-motor electric performance and standard Quattro all-wheel drive. As a relatively recent model year, buyers exploring the pre-owned market will find sophisticated styling and modern EV tech, though long-term reliability trends remain closely tied to early adopter software and high-voltage electrical management.

---

## Engine

### Dual-Motor Electric Powertrain

Powered by dual asynchronous electric motors producing approximately 355 horsepower (up to 402 horsepower in boost mode) and 414 lb-ft of torque. This all-electric setup delivers smooth, instant acceleration and seamless power delivery without traditional internal combustion maintenance needs.

## Transmission

### Single-Speed Automatic Transmission

Features a single-speed automatic transmission paired with an electronic all-wheel-drive system. It provides direct, gearless power delivery tailored for luxury EV cruising and efficient energy regeneration.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V428000: Service Brakes, Air:Supply:Hoses, Lines/Piping, And Fittings](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Audi&model=Q8+e-tron&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** Reduced braking ability can cause longer brake pedal travel or extend the distance required to stop the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the brake pressure line as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2024 Audi Q8 e-tron is a compelling luxury electric vehicle offering strong performance and comfort. Prospective used car buyers should verify remaining factory warranty coverage for high-voltage battery and electrical components to ensure peace of mind.
