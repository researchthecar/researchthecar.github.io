# 2017 Audi S6 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2017 Audi S6 delivers exceptional performance from its twin-turbocharged V8 engine, blending luxury with sports-sedan athleticism. However, prospective buyers should carefully review service histories and remain aware of a few reported mechanical vulnerabilities that can lead to expensive repairs.

---

## Engine

### 4.0L Twin-Turbocharged V8

The 2017 Audi S6 features a potent 4.0-liter twin-turbocharged V8 engine producing around 450 horsepower and 406 lb-ft of torque, paired with standard quattro all-wheel drive. While celebrated for its tremendous acceleration and smooth power delivery, buyers should monitor the engine bay for known oil screen and mount issues.

**Turbocharger Oil Screen Clogging**
- **Symptoms:** Owners report a gradual decline in turbo power as the vehicle approaches 50,000 miles, accompanied by random oxygen sensor fault codes and potential turbocharger failure due to oil starvation.
- **Solution:** Inspect and replace the factory turbo oil screens and affected turbocharger components, often requiring an extensive teardown.
- **Cost:** $3000+

**Failed Engine Mounts**
- **Symptoms:** Engine mounts may prematurely fail and require replacement around the 50,000-mile mark during routine servicing.
- **Solution:** Replace the failed engine mounts with new units.

## Transmission

### 7-Speed S-tronic Dual-Clutch Automatic

The 2017 Audi S6 utilizes a quick-shifting 7-speed dual-clutch automatic transmission that channels power seamlessly to all four wheels. It generally offers robust performance, provided routine fluid and clutch maintenance is maintained.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V370000: Air Bags:Sensor:Occupant Classification](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Audi&model=S6&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** Stress or wear of the body-sensing mat within the front passenger seat may cause the Passenger Occupant Detection System (PODS) control module to malfunction. If the PODS module malfunctions, the front passenger air bag may not deploy properly in the event of a crash, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Volkswagen will notify owners, and dealers will install a PODS system repair kit, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V178000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Engine:Gasoline:Turbo/Supercharger](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Audi&model=S6&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** Worn bearings can cause the turbine wheel to contact the turbocharger housing or cause the shaft to break, causing an engine stall and increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the oil strainer and perform an oil change, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2017 Audi S6 is a thrilling luxury sport sedan offering formidable V8 performance and everyday usability. While major powertrain options are generally robust, buyers must thoroughly inspect for the notorious turbo oil screen clogging issue and verify maintenance records before purchasing.
