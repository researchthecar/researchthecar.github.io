Here's a breakdown of common issues and complaints associated with the 2017 Audi S6, formatted for your website.

The 2017 Audi S6 is a performance-oriented sedan offering a blend of luxury and power. It comes standard with a 4.0-liter twin-turbocharged V8 engine producing 450 horsepower and 406 lb-ft of torque. A seven-speed S tronic dual-clutch automatic transmission is the only transmission option. Owners generally praise the S6 for its rapid acceleration, comfortable interior, and stylish design. Common complaints include occasional reliability concerns, high maintenance costs, and the complexity of the infotainment system.

**Engine**

**4.0-Liter Twin-Turbo V8**

*   **Oil Consumption**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported higher-than-normal oil consumption, requiring frequent top-offs between oil changes. Symptoms include the "low oil" warning light illuminating, noticeable oil depletion on the dipstick, and potential exhaust smoke.
    *   Solution: In some cases, a software update to the engine control unit (ECU) can help manage oil consumption. In more severe cases, replacement of piston rings or even the entire engine may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 (ECU update) to $5,000+ (engine replacement).
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Turbocharger Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported turbo failure and or leaks.
    *   Solution: Replace failed turbocharger.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $3,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Coolant Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaks are a known issue. Often the plastic coolant lines become brittle and crack. Can lead to overheating if not addressed.
    *   Solution: Replace the cracked coolant lines with revised parts, possibly metal ones.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**7-Speed S Tronic Dual-Clutch Automatic**

*   **Mechatronic Unit Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The Mechatronic unit, which controls the transmission's shifting functions, can experience failures. Symptoms include harsh shifting, difficulty engaging gears, and transmission slipping.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the Mechatronic unit.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Air Suspension Issues (If Equipped)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: If the vehicle is equipped with the optional air suspension, components like air struts and the air compressor can fail, leading to a sagging suspension, uneven ride height, and error messages on the dashboard.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the faulty air strut or air compressor. Leaks in the air lines also need to be identified and repaired.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $3,000+ per strut, $800-$1500+ for the compressor.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Glitches**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The MMI (Multi Media Interface) system can experience glitches, including freezing, slow response times, and connectivity issues with Bluetooth devices or Apple CarPlay/Android Auto.
    *   Solution: Software updates can sometimes resolve these issues. In more severe cases, replacement of the MMI control unit may be required.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 (Software update) to $1,500+ (MMI Unit Replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Sensor Malfunctions**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Various sensors, such as those related to the parking system, blind spot monitoring, or adaptive cruise control, can malfunction. This can result in error messages and impaired functionality of these systems.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis of the faulty sensor and replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+, depending on the sensor and location.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Battery Drain**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported issues with excessive battery drain, especially when the vehicle is left unused for extended periods.
    *   Solution: Identifying the parasitic draw and addressing the underlying cause. This may involve replacing a faulty module or repairing wiring.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100-$1000+ depending on the underlying issue.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Sunroof Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: The sunroof can experience issues like getting stuck, making unusual noises when opening or closing, or leaking water.
    * Solution: Repair or replacement of the sunroof motor, track, or seals.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300-$1,500+ depending on the issue.
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **No Issues Found**

**Summary**

The 2017 Audi S6 is a powerful and luxurious car, but like many performance vehicles, it can be susceptible to certain issues. Engine problems such as oil consumption and coolant leaks can be costly to address. The S Tronic transmission can also have issues with the Mechatronic unit. Electrical glitches in the infotainment system and sensor malfunctions are also common. Potential buyers should be aware of these potential problems and obtain a pre-purchase inspection by a qualified mechanic familiar with Audi vehicles. Overall, the severity of these issues can range from minor annoyances to major repairs, with costs potentially reaching several thousand dollars.
