**Introduction**

The 1996 Audi S6 is a high-performance variant of the Audi 100 sedan. It was available with a single engine and transmission option:

* 4.2L V8 engine (282 hp, 280 lb-ft)
* 4-speed automatic transmission

The S6 was praised for its powerful engine, sharp handling, and luxurious interior. However, it was also known for its high maintenance costs and potential reliability issues.

**Engine**

* **4.2L V8 Engine**

    * **Intake Manifold Gasket Failure:** Description/Symptoms: Coolant or oil leaks from the intake manifold gasket, leading to overheating, rough idling, and decreased performance. Solution: Replace the intake manifold gasket. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * **Timing Belt Failure:** Description/Symptoms: The timing belt can break, causing catastrophic engine damage. Solution: Replace the timing belt regularly as per the maintenance schedule. Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,200+

**Transmission**

* **4-Speed Automatic Transmission**

    * **Slipping Gears:** Description/Symptoms: The transmission slips out of gear while driving, causing loss of power. Solution: Repair or rebuild the transmission. Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+

**Suspension**

* **Front Suspension:**

    * **Control Arm Bushing Failure:** Description/Symptoms: The control arm bushings wear out, causing the front wheels to lose alignment and the vehicle to pull to one side. Solution: Replace the control arm bushings. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+

* **Rear Suspension:**

    * **Rear Shock Absorber Failure:** Description/Symptoms: The rear shock absorbers wear out, causing the rear of the vehicle to bounce excessively. Solution: Replace the rear shock absorbers. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800+

**Electrical**

* **Battery Discharge:** Description/Symptoms: The battery drains prematurely, leaving the vehicle unable to start. Solution: Replace the battery or identify and repair any electrical drains. Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $500+
* **Electrical Switch Failures:** Description/Symptoms: Various electrical switches within the vehicle may fail, such as the window switches, turn signal switch, or headlights. Solution: Diagnose and replace the faulty switches. Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $400+

**Exterior**

* **Sunroof Leaks:** Description/Symptoms: Water leaks into the vehicle through the sunroof, causing water damage to the interior. Solution: Reseal the sunroof or replace the sunroof assembly if necessary. Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $1,000+

**Interior**

* **Dashboard Pixel Failure:** Description/Symptoms: The pixels on the dashboard display fade or become inoperable, reducing visibility of the gauges and controls. Solution: Replace the dashboard display. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+

**Summary**

The 1996 Audi S6 can be a rewarding vehicle to own, but it is important to be aware of its potential issues. The engine and transmission are known for some serious problems that can be expensive to repair. However, the vehicle's overall handling and performance are still impressive. Regular maintenance and repairs will be necessary to keep the S6 running smoothly, and potential buyers should budget accordingly.
