**Introduction:**

The 2016 Audi TT is a stylish and sporty coupe that comes with a variety of engine and transmission options. The base engine is a 2.0L turbocharged inline-four that produces 220 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque. This engine is paired with a six-speed manual transmission or a seven-speed dual-clutch automatic transmission. The TT S is powered by a 2.0L turbocharged inline-four that produces 310 horsepower and 280 lb-ft of torque. This engine is paired with a six-speed manual transmission or a six-speed dual-clutch automatic transmission. The TT RS is powered by a 2.5L turbocharged inline-five that produces 400 horsepower and 354 lb-ft of torque. This engine is paired with a seven-speed dual-clutch automatic transmission.

The TT is a well-rounded sports car that offers plenty of performance and handling. It is also comfortable and well-equipped. However, there are some potential issues that buyers should be aware of.

**Engine**

**2.0L Turbocharged Inline-Four**

* **Valve Stem Seal Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Loss of engine oil, excessive oil consumption, and smoking from the exhaust.
    * Solution: Replace the valve stem seals.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Timing Chain Tensioner Issues:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Engine rattle, timing chain noise, and potentially catastrophic engine failure.
    * Solution: Replace the timing chain tensioner.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**2.0L Turbocharged Inline-Four (TT S)**

* **No Issues Found**

**2.5L Turbocharged Inline-Five (TT RS)**

* **No Issues Found**

**Transmission**

**Six-Speed Manual Transmission**

* **Synchronizer Wear:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears, grinding noises, and potential transmission failure.
    * Solution: Replace the worn synchronizers.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Seven-Speed Dual-Clutch Automatic Transmission**

* **Mechatronic Unit Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Hard shifting, transmission slipping, and potential transmission failure.
    * Solution: Replace the mechatronic unit.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Strut Mount Bearing Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Clunking noises when turning or going over bumps, uneven tire wear.
    * Solution: Replace the strut mount bearings.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Excessive battery discharge, difficulty starting the vehicle.
    * Solution: Identify and fix the source of the battery drain, which could include faulty modules or electrical components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the cause
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Alternator Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Dimming headlights, reduced electrical output, difficulty starting the vehicle.
    * Solution: Replace the alternator.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Headlight Condensation:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Moisture buildup inside the headlights, reduced visibility at night.
    * Solution: Replace the headlights or reseal the existing ones.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Dashboard Rattling:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Loose dashboard components or rattling sounds from the interior.
    * Solution: Tighten loose components or use sound dampening material.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

Overall, the 2016 Audi TT is a reliable and well-built vehicle. However, there are some potential issues that buyers should be aware of, particularly with the engine and transmission. These issues can be costly to fix, so it is important to factor them into the overall cost of ownership.
