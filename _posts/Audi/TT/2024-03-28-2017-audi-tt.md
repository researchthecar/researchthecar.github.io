**Introduction**

The 2017 Audi TT is a compact sports car that offers a blend of performance and luxury. It is available with a choice of two turbocharged engines and a six-speed manual or a seven-speed dual-clutch automatic transmission. The TT is praised for its sharp handling, comfortable interior, and sleek design. However, it also has a few known issues that potential buyers should be aware of.

**Engine**

* **2.0L TFSI I4** (220 hp, 258 lb-ft)
    * **Intake Valve Carbon Buildup**
        * Description/Symptoms: Over time, carbon deposits can build up on the intake valves, causing a loss of power, rough idling, and increased emissions.
        * Solution: Remove the intake manifold and clean the valves using a specialized solvent.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
        * Recalls: None Found
* **3.2L V6 TFSI** (292 hp, 280 lb-ft)
    * **Timing Chain Tensioner Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: A faulty timing chain tensioner can cause the timing chain to skip, resulting in catastrophic engine damage. Symptoms include a rattling noise from the engine, reduced power, and an illuminated check engine light.
        * Solution: Replace the timing chain, tensioner, and related components.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $4,000 - $6,000+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

* **Six-Speed Manual**
    * **Grinding when Shifting**
        * Description/Symptoms: The transmission may grind when shifting, especially when changing into second or third gear.
        * Solution: Replace the worn synchronizers in the transmission.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
        * Recalls: None Found
* **Seven-Speed Dual-Clutch Automatic**
    * **Delayed Shifting**
        * Description/Symptoms: The transmission may hesitate or delay when shifting gears, particularly when downshifting.
        * Solution: Update the transmission control module software and replace the clutch assembly if necessary.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **No Issues Found**

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain**
    * Description/Symptoms: The battery may drain prematurely, especially when the vehicle is parked for extended periods.
    * Solution: Diagnose the electrical system to identify the source of the drain and repair or replace faulty components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Convertible Top Leaks** (TT Roadster)
    * Description/Symptoms: The convertible top may leak water into the cabin during heavy rain or when cleaning.
    * Solution: Replace the seals or adjust the convertible top mechanism.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Infotainment System Glitches**
    * Description/Symptoms: The infotainment system may experience occasional glitches, such as freezing, lagging, or displaying incorrect information.
    * Solution: Update the infotainment software or replace the head unit if necessary.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

Overall, the 2017 Audi TT is a competent sports car with a few potential issues. The engine and transmission problems can be severe and costly to repair, while the other issues are typically less serious and can be addressed for a reasonable price. Potential buyers should carefully consider these issues before purchasing a used TT and factor in the potential cost of repairs.
