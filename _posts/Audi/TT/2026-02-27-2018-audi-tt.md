# 2018 Audi TT Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2018 Audi TT combines sharp handling, iconic styling, and turbocharged performance in both coupe and roadster configurations. For used-car buyers, evaluating the powertrain options and reviewing vehicle history records for any sporadic electrical or mechanical glitches is key to a confident purchase.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4

This 2.0-liter turbocharged inline-4 engine produces approximately 220 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque, delivering brisk acceleration and respectable efficiency. It is generally robust when maintained on schedule with quality synthetic oil.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Dual-Clutch Automatic

This 6-speed dual-clutch automatic transmission features quick, seamless shifts paired with standard quattro all-wheel drive. Regular fluid changes are vital to ensure long-term mechanical durability.

---

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V076000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Storage:Tank Assembly](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Audi&model=TT&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** In the event of a crash, the fuel tank may become damaged by the fuel tank heat shield bracket . A damaged fuel tank may leak fuel, increasing the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Volkswagen will notify owners, and dealers will install a protective cap on the fuel tank heat shield bracket, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2018 Audi TT is a compelling luxury sports car option for enthusiasts seeking daily-drivable performance and distinctive design. Prospective buyers should prioritize models with comprehensive service histories and conduct a thorough pre-purchase inspection.
