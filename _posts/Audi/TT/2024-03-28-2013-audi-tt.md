**Introduction**

The 2013 Audi TT is a stylish and sporty coupe that offers a thrilling driving experience. It features a 2.0-liter turbocharged inline-4 engine that produces 211 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque paired with a 6-speed manual or 6-speed S tronic dual-clutch automatic transmission. The TT is praised for its sleek design, engaging handling, and comfortable interior. However, it has also been reported for several issues affecting various components.

**Engine**

**2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4 Engine**

* **Oil Consumption:** Excessive oil consumption, leading to low oil levels and potential engine damage.
* **Intake Manifold Failure:** Faulty intake manifold causing reduced engine power and rough idling.
* **Turbocharger Failure:** Premature failure of the turbocharger, resulting in decreased performance and excessive smoke.

**Transmission**

**6-Speed Manual Transmission**

* **Clutch Wear:** Premature wear of the clutch disc, leading to difficulty shifting gears.

**6-Speed S tronic Dual-Clutch Automatic Transmission**

* **Mechatronic Unit Failure:** Malfunctioning mechatronic unit causing gear slippage, delayed shifts, and loss of power.
* **Clutch Pack Failure:** Worn-out clutch packs within the transmission, resulting in slipping and jerking during gear changes.

**Suspension**

* **Squeaky Suspension:** Excessive squeaking noise from the suspension components, particularly in cold weather or over uneven roads.
* **Strut Mount Failure:** Premature failure of the strut mounts, causing rattling and poor handling.
* **Rear Control Arm Bushing Wear:** Worn-out rear control arm bushings leading to excessive vibration and compromised stability.

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain:** Rapid battery drain due to faulty electrical components or parasitic draw.
* **Headlight Failure:** Malfunctioning headlights, either flickering, dimming, or completely failing.
* **Audio System Malfunction:** Intermittent or complete failure of the audio system components, such as the radio, speakers, or navigation system.

**Exterior**

* **Convertible Top Issues:** For the convertible TT model, reported issues include leaks, tearing, and difficulty operating the top.
* **Door Handle Failure:** Malfunctioning door handles, either loose, sticking, or breaking off.
* **Wind Noise:** Excessive wind noise entering the cabin, particularly at higher speeds.

**Interior**

* **Climate Control Malfunction:** Inoperative climate control system, resulting in inconsistent temperature regulation or lack of heating/cooling.
* **Instrument Panel Failure:** Malfunctioning instrument panel or gauges, causing inaccurate or incomplete information display.
* **Door Lock Actuator Failure:** Faulty door lock actuators, leading to difficulty locking or unlocking the doors.

**Summary**

Overall, the 2013 Audi TT has been met with mixed reviews. While it offers an engaging driving experience and stylish design, it has also been plagued by various issues affecting its engine, transmission, suspension, electrical, exterior, and interior components. The severity and cost of these issues vary depending on the specific problem and the necessary repairs. Owners should be prepared for potential maintenance and repair expenses to keep their TT in good condition.
