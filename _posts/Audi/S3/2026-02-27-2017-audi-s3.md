# 2017 Audi S3

### Overview
The 2017 Audi S3 is a performance-oriented compact sedan known for its potent powertrain and engaging driving dynamics. As a premium vehicle, it generally offers a high level of build quality and technological integration. Reliability data suggests that while the core mechanical components are robust, some owners have reported specific issues, particularly related to the dual-clutch transmission and certain electronic features. Regular maintenance is crucial for preserving its performance and longevity. The S3's sporty nature means some components may experience wear faster than in a standard A3, but widespread critical failures are not a dominant concern for this model year.

---


## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged TFSI (EA888 Gen3)
**PCV Valve Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Rough idle, check engine light, potential oil leaks.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the Positive Crankcase Ventilation (PCV) valve assembly.
- **Cost:** $200 - $500

**Water Pump Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Coolant leaks, overheating, sweet smell of coolant.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the water pump and thermostat assembly.
- **Cost:** $600 - $1000


## Transmission

### 6-Speed S tronic (Dual-Clutch Automatic)
**Mechatronic Unit Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Harsh shifting, transmission warning lights, inability to select gears.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of the mechatronic unit.
- **Cost:** $1500 - $3000

**Clutch Pack Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Jerky acceleration, slipping gears, delayed engagement.
- **Solution:** Replacement of clutch packs.
- **Cost:** $2000 - $4000

### Infotainment System
**Software Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Screen freezing, unresponsive touch controls, Bluetooth connectivity issues.
- **Solution:** Software updates or system reset. In some cases, head unit replacement.

### Sensors
**Parking Sensor Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** False alerts, non-functional sensors.

### Summary Verdict
The 2017 Audi S3 is a generally reliable performance sedan, with its 2.0L turbocharged engine proving to be robust. The primary areas of concern tend to be the S tronic dual-clutch transmission, which can experience issues with its mechatronic unit or clutch packs, and occasional electrical gremlins within the infotainment system. While these repairs can be costly, they are not universally reported. Owners should be diligent with maintenance, especially fluid changes for the transmission, to mitigate potential problems. Overall, the S3 offers a compelling blend of performance and luxury with a reasonable expectation of reliability when properly cared for.
