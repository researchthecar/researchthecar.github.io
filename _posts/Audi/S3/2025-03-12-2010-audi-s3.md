The 2010 Audi S3 is a high-performance hatchback known for its sporty handling, powerful engine, and luxurious interior. The sole engine option for the 2010 S3 is a 2.0-liter turbocharged inline-4, producing 265 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque. It came standard with a 6-speed manual transmission, and a 6-speed S-tronic dual-clutch transmission was optional. Owners generally appreciate the car's responsive acceleration, all-wheel drive grip, and premium feel. Some common complaints revolve around its relatively firm ride, potential for costly repairs, and the need for premium fuel.

**Engine**

**2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4 (265 hp, 258 lb-ft)**

*   **Diverter Valve Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Loss of boost pressure, resulting in reduced power and acceleration. May also cause a check engine light. A hissing or fluttering noise from the engine bay may be audible during acceleration.
    *   Solution: Replace the diverter valve. Many owners upgrade to a stronger aftermarket valve to prevent future failures.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300

*   **Cam Follower Wear/Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Ticking or rattling noise from the top of the engine, especially at idle. Can lead to fuel pump damage and more serious engine issues if left unaddressed.
    *   Solution: Replace the cam follower. Regular inspection and replacement are recommended as preventative maintenance.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $150+

*   **Ignition Coil Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Misfires, rough idling, and reduced power. The check engine light may illuminate.
    * Solution: Replace the faulty ignition coil(s). It's often recommended to replace all coils at the same time.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $200+

*   **Timing Chain Tensioner Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Engine rattle at startup that lasts for a few seconds. Can lead to catastrophic engine failure if the timing chain jumps.
    * Solution: Replace the timing chain, tensioner, and related components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+

**Transmission**

**6-Speed Manual Transmission**

*   **Clutch Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Clutch slippage, difficulty shifting, or a chattering noise when engaging the clutch.
    *   Solution: Replace the clutch disc, pressure plate, and potentially the flywheel.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $2000+

**6-Speed S-Tronic Dual-Clutch Transmission**

*   **Mechatronic Unit Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Erratic shifting, hesitation, transmission slipping, or complete loss of drive. Error messages related to the transmission may appear on the dashboard.
    *   Solution: Replace or rebuild the Mechatronic unit.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $5,000+

**Suspension**

*   **Magnetic Ride Dampers Failure (If Equipped)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Reduced ride quality, increased body roll in corners, and uneven tire wear. Error messages related to the suspension may appear.
    *   Solution: Replace the faulty magnetic ride dampers.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1000 per damper

**Electrical**

*   **Central Convenience Control Module Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Intermittent issues with power windows, door locks, interior lighting, and alarm system.
    *   Solution: Diagnose and repair or replace the central convenience control module.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+

**Exterior**

*   **Corrosion Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rust developing around the bottom of doors and wheel wells.
    *   Solution: Remove rust and repaint the affected area. Can also use rust converters to prevent further oxidation
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+

**Interior**

*   **No Issues Found**

**Summary**

The 2010 Audi S3, while offering a thrilling driving experience, can be prone to certain issues that can be costly to repair. Engine issues like diverter valve and cam follower failures, as well as potential problems with the S-Tronic transmission's Mechatronic unit, are among the most concerning. Suspension components like the magnetic ride dampers (if equipped) can also fail and rack up repair costs. Overall, potential buyers should be aware of these common issues and factor in the cost of maintenance and repairs when considering this vehicle.
