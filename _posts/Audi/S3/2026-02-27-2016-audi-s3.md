# 2016 Audi S3

### Overview
The 2016 Audi S3 is a performance-oriented compact sedan known for its potent powertrain and engaging driving dynamics. As a premium vehicle, it generally offers a high level of build quality and technological integration. However, like any performance car, it can be subject to specific issues, particularly related to its turbocharged engine and advanced all-wheel-drive system. This analysis focuses on documented reliability concerns beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential problem areas for owners and prospective buyers of the 2016 S3 model year.

---


## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged TFSI (EA888 Gen3)
**PCV Valve Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Rough idle, increased oil consumption, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the Positive Crankcase Ventilation (PCV) valve assembly.
- **Cost:** $200 - $500

**Water Pump Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Coolant leaks, overheating, sweet smell from coolant.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the water pump and thermostat assembly.
- **Cost:** $600 - $1000

**Carbon Buildup on Valves**
- **Symptoms:** Reduced engine performance, misfires, rough idle.
- **Solution:** Intake valve cleaning (walnut blasting or chemical cleaning).
- **Cost:** $400 - $800


## Transmission

### 6-Speed S tronic (Dual-Clutch Automatic)
**Mechatronic Unit Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Harsh shifting, transmission warning lights, inability to select gears.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of the mechatronic unit.
- **Cost:** $1500 - $3000

**Clutch Pack Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Slipping gears, rough engagement, delayed shifts.
- **Solution:** Replacement of clutch packs.
- **Cost:** $2000 - $4000

### Summary Verdict
The 2016 Audi S3, while a capable and enjoyable performance car, exhibits some common issues associated with its potent powertrain and advanced dual-clutch transmission. The 2.0L TFSI engine can experience problems like PCV valve failures, water pump leaks, and carbon buildup, requiring attention and potential repair costs. The S tronic transmission, though quick-shifting, is also susceptible to mechatronic unit failures and clutch wear. Electrical components, particularly the infotainment system, may encounter software glitches. However, the suspension, exterior, and interior generally hold up well, with no widespread critical failures reported. Regular maintenance and prompt attention to early symptoms are crucial for maintaining the reliability of this performance-oriented Audi.
