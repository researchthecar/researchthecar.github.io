# 2018 Audi S3 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2018 Audi S3 blends everyday compact sedan practicality with turbocharged performance, making it an appealing choice for used-car buyers looking for sporty driving dynamics. While owner feedback is generally positive regarding its performance and handling, prospective buyers should review specific mechanical areas such as the fuel system and transmission operation during a test drive.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4

This 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine produces approximately 292 horsepower and 280 lb-ft of torque, paired standard with quattro all-wheel drive. It offers brisk acceleration and solid overall durability, though owners have occasionally noted peripheral fuel system component concerns.

### Fuel/Propulsion System

Components related to fuel delivery require normal monitoring for leaks or operational inconsistencies.

**Suction Pump Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have reported issues related to fuel delivery and suction pump function, sometimes drawing parallels to known manufacturer service actions on related Audi models.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified technician inspect the fuel delivery system and replace the faulty suction pump if component failure is confirmed.

## Transmission

### 7-Speed S-tronic Dual-Clutch Automatic

The 7-speed dual-clutch automatic transmission delivers rapid gear changes and complements the sporty character of the S3, though dual-clutch systems require attentive maintenance and thorough pre-purchase evaluation.

**Gear Disengagement and Shifting Delays**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have experienced instances where the transmission unexpectedly disengages from gear while driving, causing engine revs to climb without acceleration, or hesitates when engaging reverse.
- **Solution:** Perform a diagnostic scan for transmission error codes and check software calibration or mechatronic unit health.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V198000: Air Bags:Sensor:Occupant Classification:Front Passenger](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Audi&model=S3&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** A deactivated passenger air bag increases the risk of injury during a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the PODS sensor mat and update the PODS control module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2018 Audi S3 is a compelling sport sedan that offers a great mix of luxury and performance for used car shoppers. Buyers should prioritize a thorough pre-purchase inspection focusing on the transmission's shifting behavior and the fuel system to ensure long-term reliability.
