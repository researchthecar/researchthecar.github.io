The 2023 Audi RS Q8 is a high-performance SUV that blends luxury with exhilarating power. It boasts a potent 4.0-liter twin-turbocharged V8 engine that produces 591 horsepower and 590 lb-ft of torque, paired exclusively with an 8-speed Tiptronic automatic transmission. There are no other engine or transmission options available for the 2023 RS Q8. Owners often praise its blistering acceleration, sharp handling for its size, and opulent interior. However, some common criticisms include its high price tag, firm ride quality, and relatively poor fuel economy.

**Engine**

**4.0L Twin-Turbocharged V8**

*   **Turbocharger Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Whining noise from the engine bay, reduced engine power, check engine light. Some owners have reported premature turbocharger failure.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the faulty turbocharger(s).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $4,000 - $8,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Oil Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Oil spots under the vehicle, burning oil smell, low oil level warning light. Leaks often occur around the valve cover gaskets or the oil pan.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the leaking gaskets and/or resealing the oil pan.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**8-Speed Tiptronic Automatic**

*   **Rough Shifting**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Harsh or jerky shifts, especially between lower gears. Some owners have reported hesitation or delayed engagement when accelerating from a stop.
    *   Solution: Software update to the transmission control module (TCM). In some cases, replacement of the valve body may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $3,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Air Suspension Malfunctions**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Vehicle sitting unevenly, suspension warning lights illuminated, inability to adjust ride height. This may be related to a leak in one of the air struts.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis and repair or replacement of the air struts, compressor, or related components.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $4,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Freezing/Crashing**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The MMI (Multi Media Interface) system freezes, becomes unresponsive, or restarts unexpectedly.
    *   Solution: Software update, system reset, or replacement of the infotainment module.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 (Software Update) - $1,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Sensor Faults**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Malfunctions with advanced driver assistance systems (ADAS) such as adaptive cruise control, lane keeping assist, or blind spot monitoring. Warning lights may illuminate.
    *   Solution: Diagnostic scan to identify the faulty sensor and replacement or recalibration of the sensor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **No Issues Found**

**Interior**

*   **Rattling/Squeaking Noises**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Annoying rattling or squeaking noises emanating from the dashboard, door panels, or rear cargo area, particularly on rough roads.
    *   Solution: Locating and isolating the source of the noise, often involving tightening loose trim pieces or adding sound dampening material.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2023 Audi RS Q8, while a performance marvel, appears to have some potential issues that prospective buyers should be aware of. Engine issues such as turbocharger failure and oil leaks can be costly to repair, while transmission issues might be addressed with software updates or more substantial repairs. Suspension and electrical gremlins, like air suspension malfunctions and infotainment system problems, could also lead to unexpected expenses. Interior rattles are more of an annoyance than a major mechanical issue. Overall, the issues range from minor annoyances to potentially expensive repairs, so a thorough pre-purchase inspection is advisable.
