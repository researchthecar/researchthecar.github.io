The 2021 Audi RS Q8 is a high-performance SUV that blends luxury with impressive power. It comes standard with a 4.0-liter twin-turbocharged V8 engine producing 591 horsepower and 590 lb-ft of torque, paired exclusively with an eight-speed automatic transmission. Owners appreciate the RS Q8 for its exhilarating performance, luxurious interior, and aggressive styling. Common criticisms include its high price tag, firm ride quality, and potentially high running costs.

**Engine**

**4.0L Twin-Turbo V8**

*   **Turbocharger Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Whining noise from the engine, loss of power, check engine light.
    *   Solution: Replace the faulty turbocharger(s).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $4,000 - $8,000+ depending on whether one or both need replacement and labor costs.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Oil Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Oil spots under the vehicle, burning oil smell, low oil level. Often around valve cover gaskets or turbocharger lines.
    *   Solution: Replace the leaking gaskets or lines.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,500+ depending on the source of the leak and repair complexity.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Coolant Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Low coolant level, overheating, coolant smell.
    *   Solution: Identify and repair the source of the leak, which may involve replacing hoses, water pump, or other cooling system components.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+ depending on the source and complexity.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**8-Speed Automatic (ZF 8HP)**

*   **Rough Shifting/Hesitation**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Harsh or delayed shifts, particularly between certain gears. May be more noticeable when cold.
    *   Solution: Transmission fluid flush and software update. If the issue persists, valve body replacement or full transmission replacement may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 (fluid flush) - $5,000+ (transmission replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Torque Converter Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Shuddering or vibration at low speeds, especially during light acceleration.
    *   Solution: Torque converter replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Air Suspension Malfunctions**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Vehicle sitting unevenly, suspension warning lights, failure to adjust ride height.
    *   Solution: Diagnose and replace faulty air struts, air compressor, or ride height sensors.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $4,000+ depending on the component.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Freezing/Crashing**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Infotainment screen freezes, unresponsive touch controls, system reboots unexpectedly.
    *   Solution: Software updates. If the issue persists, hardware replacement might be required.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 (software update) - $2,000+ (hardware replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Sensor Failures (e.g., Parking Sensors, Blind Spot Monitoring)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Error messages on the dashboard, system malfunctions.
    *   Solution: Diagnose and replace the faulty sensor(s).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+ per sensor.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Sunroof/Moonroof Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Failure to open or close properly, unusual noises, leaks.
    *   Solution: Repair or replace sunroof components, such as the motor or track.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Seat Motor Malfunctions**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Seat not adjusting properly, motor making noise or failing to operate.
    *   Solution: Replace the faulty seat motor or related components.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Dashboard/Trim Rattles**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Annoying noises coming from the dashboard or trim pieces, especially on rough roads.
    *   Solution: Securing loose trim pieces.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+ depending on the complexity.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2021 Audi RS Q8, while offering exceptional performance and luxury, is subject to a range of potential issues. Engine problems like turbocharger failure and oil leaks can be expensive to fix. Transmission issues can also lead to costly repairs. Electrical glitches and suspension malfunctions are also a concern. Interior issues, such as rattles and malfunctions, are less severe but can detract from the driving experience. Overall, owning a 2021 RS Q8 requires being prepared for potential high repair costs associated with its high-performance nature and complex systems.
