The 2017 Audi RS3 is a high-performance hatchback with impressive specs and capabilities. It features a 2.5 liter turbocharged inline-five engine that produces 400 horsepower and 354 lb-ft of torque, paired with a 7-speed dual-clutch S tronic transmission and standard Quattro all-wheel drive. The RS3 is praised for its agile handling, blistering acceleration, and luxurious interior. However, there have been some issues reported by owners.

**Engine**
* **2.5L Turbocharged Inline-Five**
    * **Intake Manifold Crack:**
        * Description/Symptoms: A crack in the intake manifold can cause a loss of power, rough idle, and check engine light.
        * Solution: Replace the intake manifold.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,200 - $1,800
        * Recalls: None Found
    * **Carbon Buildup:**
        * Description/Symptoms: Carbon deposits on the intake valves and combustion chamber can lead to reduced power, increased fuel consumption, and rough idle.
        * Solution: Clean the intake valves and combustion chamber manually.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600
        * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**
* **7-Speed Dual-Clutch S tronic**
    * **Mechatronic Unit Failure:**
        * Description/Symptoms: Failure of the mechatronic unit can cause shifting issues, transmission slipping, or vehicle stalling.
        * Solution: Replace the mechatronic unit.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $3,000 - $4,000
        * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**
* **No Issues Found**

**Electrical**
* **Battery Drain:**
        * Description/Symptoms: The battery can drain excessively while the vehicle is parked.
        * Solution: Replace the battery or identify and repair electrical draw.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
        * Recalls: None Found
* **ECU Software Issues:**
        * Description/Symptoms: Software bugs in the engine control unit (ECU) can cause various engine and transmission problems, such as reduced power, shifting issues, or check engine light.
        * Solution: Update the ECU software.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 - $200 (if covered under warranty)
        * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**
* **Paint Peeling on Front Spoiler:**
        * Description/Symptoms: The paint on the front spoiler can peel or chip off, exposing the underlying plastic.
        * Solution: Repaint or replace the front spoiler.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
        * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**
* **Steering Wheel Buttons Malfunction:**
        * Description/Symptoms: The buttons on the steering wheel may become intermittent or stop working altogether.
        * Solution: Replace the steering wheel module.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $700
        * Recalls: None Found
* **Infotainment System Lag or Crashes:**
        * Description/Symptoms: The infotainment system may experience lag or crashes, affecting navigation, audio, and other functions.
        * Solution: Software update or module replacement.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 - $800
        * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2017 Audi RS3 is a highly capable and enjoyable performance hatchback. However, it has experienced some issues, including intake manifold cracks, carbon buildup, mechatronic unit failures, battery drain, ECU software problems, paint peeling, and interior electrical issues. While some of these issues can be costly to repair, many are relatively minor and can be addressed through software updates or part replacements. It's important to note that the severity and cost of repairs can vary depending on the specific issue, the affected parts, and the location of the repair. It's recommended to have any RS3-related issues diagnosed and repaired by a qualified technician to ensure proper resolution.
