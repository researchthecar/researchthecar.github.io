# 2020 Audi RS3 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2020 Audi RS3 is a high-performance sport compact known for its fierce acceleration and distinctive five-cylinder soundtrack. While NHTSA owner complaint volumes for this specific model year remain very light, buyers should approach high-performance luxury vehicles with an understanding of potential mechanical and electrical complexities. Thorough pre-purchase inspections are always recommended when shopping for a used enthusiast car.

---

## Engine

### 2.5L Turbocharged 5-Cylinder

This 2.5-liter turbocharged inline-five produces approximately 394 horsepower and 354 lb-ft of torque. It is celebrated for its unique sound and immense power delivery, though proper maintenance and high-grade fuel are essential for long-term health.

## Transmission

### 7-Speed Dual-Clutch Automatic

The 7-speed S-tronic dual-clutch transmission pairs with quattro all-wheel drive to snap off rapid gear changes. Regular fluid changes are critical to maintaining smooth operation and longevity under aggressive driving.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V198000: Air Bags:Sensor:Occupant Classification:Front Passenger](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Audi&model=RS3&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** A deactivated passenger air bag increases the risk of injury during a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the PODS sensor mat and update the PODS control module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2020 Audi RS3 offers blistering performance paired with daily-drivetrain practicality, supported by a clean early owner feedback profile. Buyers should verify service records, check for any modifications, and secure a professional inspection prior to purchase.
