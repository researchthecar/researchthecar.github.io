**Introduction:**

The 2019 Audi e-tron Quattro is an all-electric SUV that boasts a sleek design, spacious interior, and advanced technology. With 355 horsepower and 414 lb-ft of torque, the electric motor delivers impressive performance, while the Quattro all-wheel drive system ensures excellent handling. However, despite its premium positioning, the e-tron Quattro has faced several issues reported by owners.

**Suspension**

**Adaptive Air Suspension**

**Front Air Spring Failure:**
Description/Symptoms: Reduced ride height on the front of the vehicle, uneven tire wear, and decreased handling stability.
Solution: Replace the faulty air spring.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $1000 - $2000+
Recalls: None Found

**Rear Air Shock Failure:**
Description/Symptoms: Sagging rear end, poor ride quality, and potential bottoming out.
Solution: Replace the faulty rear air shock.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1200+
Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

**High-Voltage Battery Issues:**
Description/Symptoms: Reduced battery range, sudden power loss, or battery overheating.
Solution: Replace or reprogram the high-voltage battery.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $10,000 - $20,000+
Recalls: NHTSA Campaign Number 20V-425

**Charging System Malfunctions:**
Description/Symptoms: Difficulties charging the vehicle, slow charging speeds, or charging interruptions.
Solution: Repair or replace the faulty charging components.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2000+
Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

**Center Console Trim Panel Gap:**
Description/Symptoms: Excessive gap between the center console trim panel and the dashboard, creating a rattling noise.
Solution: Realign or replace the center console trim panel.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

The 2019 Audi e-tron Quattro has experienced some reliability issues, particularly with its suspension and electrical components. While the electric motor has not been reported to have any major problems, the high-voltage battery and charging system have raised some concerns. The severity of the issues varies, but many of them are potentially expensive to repair, which could impact the overall ownership experience.
