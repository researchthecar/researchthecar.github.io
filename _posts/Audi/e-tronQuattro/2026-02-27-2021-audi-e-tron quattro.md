# 2021 Audi e-tron Quattro

### Overview
The 2021 Audi e-tron Quattro, a fully electric SUV, generally exhibits strong reliability, reflecting Audi's commitment to quality in its premium electric offerings. As an early entrant in the premium EV segment, it benefits from mature electric powertrain technology. Owners typically report a positive ownership experience with fewer mechanical complexities compared to traditional internal combustion engine vehicles. The focus for reliability data shifts from engine and transmission wear to the integrated electrical systems, battery health, and software performance. While no vehicle is entirely without potential issues, the e-tron Quattro has demonstrated a solid track record for its model year, with most reported concerns being minor or related to software updates.

## Suspension

### Adaptive Air Suspension
**Air Compressor Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle may sag on one or more corners, suspension warning light illuminated, uneven ride height.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the air compressor unit or associated air lines.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1500

**Air Strut Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Gradual loss of ride height, particularly when parked for extended periods, bouncy ride.
- **Solution:** Replacement of leaking air struts.
- **Cost:** $700 - $1200 per strut

### Summary Verdict
The 2021 Audi e-tron Quattro is a reliable electric SUV, with its electric powertrain and battery pack showing no widespread issues. The primary areas of concern are related to the adaptive air suspension, which can experience compressor malfunctions or strut leaks, and occasional software glitches within the infotainment system. These issues are generally repairable and do not indicate systemic failures of the vehicle's core electric components. Overall, the e-tron Quattro offers a dependable ownership experience for an electric vehicle of its generation, with most potential problems being manageable and within the expected range for a premium vehicle.
