# 2019 Audi e-tron Quattro Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2019 Audi e-tron Quattro marks Audi's initial step into the all-electric luxury SUV segment, combining refined styling with a dual-motor setup. While praised for its quiet ride and upscale interior, used-car buyers should review potential electrical system quirks, motor cooling concerns, and sunroof water intrusion risks reported by early owners.

---

## Engine

### Dual Electric Motors (355 hp / 402 hp Boost)

The e-tron relies on asynchronous dual electric motors generating around 355 horsepower in standard driving or up to 402 horsepower temporarily in boost mode. While powertrain performance is smooth, rare reports of internal coolant leaks warrant a careful pre-purchase inspection.

**Coolant Intrusion in Electric Motor**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle experiences a sudden and total loss of propulsion while driving due to internal fluid leaks.
- **Solution:** Requires professional diagnosis and replacement or repair of the affected electric motor assembly.

## Transmission

### Single-Speed Planetary Automatic Transmission

Power is sent to all four wheels via a single-speed automatic transmission paired with electric drive units on each axle. The system is mechanically straightforward and generally free of complex gear-shifting complaints.

## Suspension

### Adaptive Air Suspension

Adaptive air suspension delivers an adjustable ride height and plush comfort, but pneumatic components can occasionally display electronic glitches.

**Drive Select and Air Suspension Drop**
- **Symptoms:** The drive select system malfunctions, causing the air suspension to drop to its lowest possible setting for extended periods.
- **Solution:** Scan suspension control modules for stored fault codes and check ride-height sensors or valve blocks.

## Electrical

### High-Voltage Battery and Charging System

Electrical architecture supports high-voltage charging and complex onboard electronics, though some drivers experience software warnings and module replacements.

**Electrical System Malfunction and Battery Module Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Dashboard warnings indicating electrical system failure, low battery charge during charging, or complete charging system faults.
- **Solution:** Have a certified specialist inspect high-voltage battery modules, software calibration, and charging hardware.

## Exterior

### Sunroof and Body Seals

Exterior body seals and panoramic sunroof assemblies require routine maintenance to ensure water is properly diverted away from sensitive interior electronics.

**Water Ingress via Sunroof**
- **Symptoms:** Rainwater leaks through the roof into the cabin, wetting the infotainment system, shifter, and interior electronics.
- **Solution:** Inspect sunroof drain tubes and seals, and clear any blockages to prevent moisture accumulation.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V434000: Electrical System:Propulsion System:Traction Battery](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Audi&model=e-tron&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** Moisture in the high-voltage system can cause a short-circuit, increasing the risk of fire.
- **What to do:** Audi will notify owners, and dealers will install a new seal and, as necessary, replace the affected system components, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V842000: Electrical System:Propulsion System:Charging:Cable/Cord:Onboard](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Audi&model=e-tron&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** An overheated outlet or charging cable can increase the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will supply a new 220V/240V compact/portable charging cable with an incorporated temperature sensor, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V867000: Electrical System:Propulsion System:Traction Battery Thermal:Management:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Audi&model=e-tron&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** A high-voltage battery that overheats increases the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will install onboard diagnostic software to monitor battery module performance, and replace affected battery modules where necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V621000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Pedals And Linkages](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Audi&model=e-tron&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** Brake pedal detachment can cause a loss of brake function and increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and repair the brake booster, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V900000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Audi&model=e-tron&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 111, "Rear Visibility." A rearview image that does not display reduces the driver's view behind the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2019 Audi e-tron Quattro offers luxury EV motoring at a much lower price point than when new, but it is not without complexity. Prospective buyers should prioritize vehicles with documented dealer service history, verified completion of high-voltage battery recalls, and a thorough inspection of the sunroof seals and electric drive units.
