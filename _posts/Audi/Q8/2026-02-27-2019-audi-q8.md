# 2019 Audi Q8 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2019 Audi Q8 offers striking styling, a luxurious cabin, and confident handling, making it a compelling luxury crossover choice on the used market. However, prospective buyers should approach this model year with care due to a prominent pattern of electrical and mild-hybrid system concerns. Thoroughly inspecting service records and verifying that all applicable campaigns have been addressed is essential before purchasing.

---

## Engine

### 3.0L Turbo V6 (335 hp / 369 lb-ft)

The standard 3.0-liter turbocharged V6 engine produces 335 horsepower and 369 lb-ft of torque, providing smooth and robust acceleration for the Q8. While generally durable, early production models can experience specific accessory leaks that require close monitoring.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Tiptronic Automatic

The 8-speed Tiptronic automatic transmission channels power to Audi's Quattro all-wheel-drive system with refined shifts. Buyers should test drive the vehicle thoroughly to ensure they are comfortable with its throttle engagement characteristics.

**Throttle and Acceleration Lag**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers experience a noticeable 2 to 3-second delay in throttle response when attempting to accelerate after coasting or braking.
- **Solution:** Powertrain control module software updates or adaptive transmission resets may help improve responsiveness.

## Brakes

### Brake Discs and Pads

The braking system delivers strong stopping power, though heavy vehicle mass can accelerate component wear.

**Premature Brake Wear and Pulsation**
- **Symptoms:** Brake pedals pulsate and pads or rotors wear down rapidly under normal driving conditions due to the vehicle's heavy weight.
- **Solution:** Replace brake pads and rotors with quality aftermarket or OEM components designed for heavy-duty performance.

## Electrical

### 48V Mild-Hybrid & Charging System

Issues related to the 48-volt electrical architecture and alternator represent some of the most critical owner-reported problems for this model year.

**Electrical System Malfunction and Alternator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Dashboard warnings trigger multiple system failures, followed by sudden power loss, a dead battery, and the vehicle shutting down completely while driving.
- **Solution:** Replace the failing alternator and associated mild-hybrid components as recommended by qualified technicians.

## Safety

### Seat Belts

Safety equipment generally functions as intended, though minor hardware inconveniences have been reported by owners.

**Twisted Seat Belts**
- **Symptoms:** Seat belt webbing twists easily within the mechanism, making them difficult to latch and uncomfortable to wear.
- **Solution:** Inspect and manually untwist the belt webbing or have the assembly serviced if retraction hardware is at fault.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V149000: Steering](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Audi&model=Q8&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** A sudden loss of loss of steering control may increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Volkswagen will notify owners, and Audi dealers will inspect and, if necessary, replace the steering shaft bolt, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V114000: Suspension:Front:Shock Absorber](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Audi&model=Q8&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** Loose shock absorbers may impair vehicle steering, increasing the risk of crash.
- **What to do:** Audi has notified owners, and dealers will replace the shock absorber forks, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V516000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Audi&model=Q8&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** Fuel pump failure can cause an engine stall, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the fuel pump, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V900000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Audi&model=Q8&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 111, "Rear Visibility." A rearview image that does not display reduces the driver's view behind the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V125000: Lane Departure:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Audi&model=Q8&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** The driver may be unaware that the lane departure warning system is deactivated and rely on a system that is not functional, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2019 Audi Q8 is a sophisticated luxury crossover that delivers impressive performance and comfort, but potential buyers must proceed with caution. Electrical glitches centered around the 48-volt mild-hybrid system and alternator require careful scrutiny during a pre-purchase inspection. Securing a vehicle with a documented service history and remaining aware of possible powertrain lag will help ensure a more satisfying ownership experience.
