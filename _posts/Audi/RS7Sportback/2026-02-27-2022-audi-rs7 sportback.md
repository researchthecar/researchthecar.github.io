## Suspension

### Adaptive Air Suspension
**Air Compressor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle may sag on one or more corners, suspension warning lights, uneven ride height.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the air compressor unit.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1500

**Air Strut Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Gradual sagging of the vehicle, especially after sitting for a period, loss of ride comfort.
- **Solution:** Replacement of leaking air struts.
- **Cost:** $1000 - $2000 per strut


## Electrical

### Infotainment System
**Software Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Occasional freezing, unresponsiveness, or errors with the MMI system, navigation, or connectivity features.
- **Solution:** Software updates or resets. In rare cases, module replacement.
- **Cost:** $100 - $500 (for updates/diagnostics), $1000+ (for module replacement)


### Advanced Driver-Assistance Systems (ADAS)
**Sensor Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** False alerts or non-operation of adaptive cruise control, lane keeping assist, or parking sensors.
- **Solution:** Calibration or replacement of faulty sensors (e.g., radar, cameras).
- **Cost:** $300 - $1000 per sensor

### Summary Verdict
The 2022 Audi RS7 Sportback demonstrates a high level of reliability for its powertrain and core mechanical components, with its potent 4.0L twin-turbo V8 and 8-speed automatic transmission generally free from widespread issues. The primary areas where owners might encounter technical concerns are related to the advanced adaptive air suspension system, which can experience compressor failures or strut leaks, and occasional electrical gremlins within the sophisticated infotainment and ADAS systems. These electrical issues are often software-related and can typically be resolved with updates, though hardware faults may necessitate component replacement. Overall, the RS7 is a robust performance vehicle, but potential buyers should be aware of the potential costs associated with maintaining its advanced suspension and electronic features.
