# 2021 Audi RS7 Sportback

### Overview
The 2021 Audi RS7 Sportback is a high-performance luxury sedan that blends aggressive styling with potent V8 power. As a flagship model, it generally exhibits strong build quality and advanced engineering. However, like any complex vehicle, particularly one focused on performance, there are specific areas where owners have reported concerns. This analysis focuses on documented technical reliability issues beyond standard maintenance, examining common problem areas across its powertrain, electrical systems, and other key components. The aim is to provide a clear picture of potential reliability challenges for prospective and current owners.

---


## Engine

### 4.0L Twin-Turbo V8 (441 kW)
**Oil Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Visible oil spots under the vehicle, burning oil smell, low oil level warnings.
- **Solution:** Inspection and replacement of seals, gaskets, or oil cooler lines.
- **Cost:** $300 - $800

**Turbocharger Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Reduced engine power, whistling noises from the engine bay, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and potential repair or replacement of turbochargers or associated plumbing.
- **Cost:** $2,000 - $5,000+


## Transmission

### 8-Speed Tiptronic Automatic
**Hesitation or Jerking**
- **Symptoms:** Slight delay when shifting gears, noticeable jolts during acceleration or deceleration.
- **Solution:** Software updates for the transmission control module, or internal transmission repairs if necessary.
- **Cost:** $500 - $2,500


## Suspension

### Adaptive Air Suspension
**Air Strut Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle sagging on one corner, uneven ride height, 'suspension fault' warning messages.
- **Solution:** Replacement of leaking air struts or air springs.
- **Cost:** $1,000 - $2,500 per corner


## Electrical

### Infotainment System
**Software Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Screen freezing, unresponsive touch controls, Bluetooth connectivity issues, navigation errors.
- **Solution:** Software updates, system resets, or potential replacement of the infotainment control unit.
- **Cost:** $200 - $1,500

### Summary Verdict
The 2021 Audi RS7 Sportback, while a performance marvel, presents a few potential reliability concerns typical of high-performance vehicles. The 4.0L twin-turbo V8 engine can experience oil leaks and turbocharger issues, requiring significant repair costs. The automatic transmission may exhibit hesitation or jerking, often resolvable with software updates. The adaptive air suspension is prone to strut leaks, which can be costly to fix. Electrical gremlins, particularly with the infotainment system and occasional battery drain, are also noted. While the exterior and interior generally hold up well, owners should be prepared for the potential costs associated with maintaining and repairing the advanced powertrain and suspension systems. Regular servicing and prompt attention to warning signs are crucial for mitigating these issues.
