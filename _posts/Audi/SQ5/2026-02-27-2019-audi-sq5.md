# 2019 Audi SQ5 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2019 Audi SQ5 blends luxury performance with everyday practicality, featuring a turbocharged V6 engine and standard quattro all-wheel drive. While it offers a refined driving experience and impressive acceleration, prospective buyers should review owner-reported issues concerning electrical components, lighting, and powertrain reliability. Thoroughly inspecting service records and verifying whether any open manufacturer coverages have been performed can help mitigate unexpected ownership costs.

---

## Engine

### 3.0L Turbocharged V6 (EA839)

The 3.0-liter turbocharged V6 engine produces approximately 349 horsepower and 369 lb-ft of torque, delivering brisk acceleration. Buyers should monitor for rare internal mechanical noises and verify maintenance history.

**Engine Knocking Noise and Potential Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may notice an abnormal knocking noise emanating from the engine bay, which can point to underlying mechanical wear or piston-related concerns.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified mechanic inspect the engine for internal wear and verify if the vehicle is covered under any manufacturer warranty extensions or bulletins.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Tiptronic Automatic Transmission

The 8-speed automatic transmission pairs with quattro all-wheel drive for smooth, rapid gear shifts. It generally proves durable when routine fluid maintenance is observed.

## Exterior

### Lighting and Control Modules

Electrical complaints for this model frequently involve exterior lighting modules and control units affecting rear visibility.

**Rear Brake Light and Tail Light Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Rear brake lights, tail lights, and turn signals may become completely inoperable, often accompanied by warning messages on the dashboard display.
- **Solution:** Inspect the Comfort System Central Control Module and associated wiring or harnesses for faults, and check for applicable manufacturer service documentation.

### Panoramic Sunroof

Exterior body components are generally solid, though isolated reports highlight issues with panoramic glass durability.

**Sudden Sunroof Glass Shattering**
- **Symptoms:** The panoramic glass roof may fracture or shatter unexpectedly while driving at highway speeds, accompanied by a loud pop resembling a gunshot.
- **Solution:** Replace the damaged glass panel and inspect the frame for any signs of structural twisting or prior impact.

## Safety

### Forward Collision Avoidance

Driver assistance systems provide advanced safety features, though sensor calibration is critical for proper operation.

**Erratic Adaptive Cruise Control Braking**
- **Symptoms:** The adaptive cruise control system may misinterpret environmental factors, such as shadows under bridges, as stationary obstacles and aggressively apply the brakes.
- **Solution:** Ensure vehicle software and sensor calibrations are updated to the latest manufacturer specifications.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V947000: Electrical System:Body Control Module/Bcm](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Audi&model=SQ5&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** A gateway control module shutdown will suddenly reduce engine power, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will install a protective cover on the gateway control module and seal an underbody seam as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V416000: Wheels:Hub](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Audi&model=SQ5&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** The detached trim may become a road hazard, increasing the risk of crash.
- **What to do:** Volkswagen will notify owners, and dealers will secure the wheel arch trim with an additional screw and nut, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V473000: Air Bags:Frontal:Passenger Side:Inflator Module](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Audi&model=SQ5&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** In the event of a crash deploying the passenger's frontal air bag, the instrument panel carrier may break due to incorrect welding. A damaged frontal air bag may not provide adequate protection, increasing the risk of an injury.
- **What to do:** Audi will notify owners, and dealers will replace the instrument panel and the passenger frontal air bag, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V737000: Structure:Body](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Audi&model=SQ5&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** The detached trim may become a road hazard, increasing the risk of crash.
- **What to do:** Audi will notify owners, and dealers will replace the four clips securing the forward part of the rear wheel arch trim, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V277000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Foundation Components:Master Cylinder](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Audi&model=SQ5&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** A short casting can cause a sudden loss of brakes, increasing the risk of crash.
- **What to do:** Audi has notified owners, and dealers will inspect the brake master cylinder, replacing it as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2019 Audi SQ5 is a compelling performance luxury crossover, but buyers should approach with a healthy awareness of potential electrical and engine-related owner complaints. A comprehensive pre-purchase inspection by an independent Audi specialist is strongly recommended to check the condition of lighting modules, powertrain health, and structural glass.
