# 2016 Audi SQ5

### Overview
The 2016 Audi SQ5, a performance-oriented variant of the Q5 SUV, generally presents a strong reliability profile, particularly for its powertrain. Owners often praise its potent engine and responsive transmission. However, like many performance vehicles, it can be susceptible to specific issues related to its advanced systems. This analysis focuses on documented technical problems beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential areas of concern for prospective and current owners. The SQ5's blend of luxury, performance, and practicality makes it a desirable option, but understanding its reliability nuances is key to a positive ownership experience.

---


## Engine

### 3.0L Supercharged V6
**PCV Valve Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Rough idle, increased oil consumption, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the Positive Crankcase Ventilation (PCV) valve assembly.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600

**Supercharger Belt Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Whining noise from the engine bay, reduced engine power.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the supercharger drive belt.
- **Cost:** $200 - $400

## Suspension

### Adaptive Air Suspension (Optional)
**Air Compressor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle sagging on one or more corners, warning lights on the dashboard, loss of adjustable ride height.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the air suspension compressor and potentially air springs.
- **Cost:** $1000 - $2500

**Air Spring Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Gradual sagging of the vehicle, especially after sitting for a period, uneven ride height.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of leaking air springs.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1200 per corner

### Summary Verdict
The 2016 Audi SQ5 is a generally reliable performance SUV, with its core powertrain components (3.0L Supercharged V6 engine and 8-speed automatic transmission) demonstrating good durability. The most commonly reported issues tend to be related to the optional adaptive air suspension, specifically compressor failures and air spring leaks, which can be costly to repair. Minor electrical gremlins within the infotainment system and potential clogging of sunroof drain tubes are also noted. Owners should be aware of these potential issues, especially when purchasing a used model with higher mileage or the optional air suspension. Regular maintenance and prompt attention to any warning signs can help mitigate these concerns and ensure a positive ownership experience.
