The 2020 Audi SQ5 is a performance-oriented SUV that boasts a compelling blend of luxury and athleticism. Powered by a 3.0-liter turbocharged V6 engine producing 349 horsepower and 369 lb-ft of torque, mated to an 8-speed automatic transmission. It provides impressive acceleration and handling. Owners often praise its comfortable interior, stylish design, and engaging driving dynamics. Some common complaints include the infotainment system's complexity, and the relatively firm ride.

**Engine**

**3.0L Turbocharged V6**

*   **Oil Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks can develop from various locations, including the valve cover gaskets, rear main seal, and timing chain cover. Symptoms include visible oil drips under the vehicle, a burning oil smell, and low oil levels.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the leaking gasket or seal.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,500+, depending on the location and complexity of the repair.

*   **Coolant Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaks can occur from hoses, the water pump, or the thermostat housing. Symptoms include low coolant levels, overheating, and a sweet smell.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the leaking component.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $800+

*   **Turbocharger Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Turbocharger failure can manifest as a loss of power, unusual noises (whining or whistling), and/or the check engine light illuminating.
    *   Solution: Turbocharger inspection and potential replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,500+

**Transmission**

**8-Speed Automatic (ZF 8HP)**

*   **Rough Shifting**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported experiencing rough or jerky shifting, particularly between certain gears.
    *   Solution: Software updates or transmission fluid flush may alleviate the issue. In some cases, valve body replacement or transmission rebuild may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150-$500 for fluid flush or software update, $1,000-$4,000+ for valve body replacement or rebuild.

**Suspension**

*   **Air Suspension Problems (if equipped)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some SQ5s come with air suspension, which can experience issues like compressor failure, air leaks in the struts, or ride height inconsistencies.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the faulty compressor, strut, or other air suspension components.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $2,500+

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Glitches**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The MMI infotainment system can experience occasional glitches, such as freezing, lagging, or unexpected reboots.
    *   Solution: Software updates can sometimes resolve these issues. In more severe cases, the infotainment unit may need replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 (software update) - $1,500+ (unit replacement)

*   **Sensor Malfunctions**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Various sensors (e.g., parking sensors, blind-spot monitoring sensors) can malfunction, leading to error messages or improper system operation.
    *   Solution: Sensor replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600+ per sensor.

*   **Battery Drain**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners report issues with excessive battery drain, particularly when the vehicle is not driven frequently.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis of the cause of the drain (e.g., faulty module, parasitic draw) and repair/replacement of the offending component.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+

**Exterior**

*   **Sunroof Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The sunroof may exhibit problems like getting stuck, making excessive noise, or leaking water.
    *   Solution: Lubrication of the tracks, repair of the mechanism, or replacement of seals/components.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $800+

**Interior**

*   **Creaking/Rattling Noises**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported creaking or rattling noises emanating from various interior trim pieces, particularly in the dashboard or door panels.
    *   Solution: Tightening fasteners, adding felt padding, or replacing the affected trim pieces.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $500+ (depending on the severity and complexity)

**Summary**

The 2020 Audi SQ5, while generally reliable, can experience certain issues. Engine issues, particularly oil and coolant leaks, and turbocharger problems, can be costly. Transmission issues are less common but can still lead to significant repair bills. Electrical glitches and sensor malfunctions also contribute to potential ownership costs. Air suspension problems, if equipped, can be another source of expense. While many issues can be resolved with relatively minor repairs, some may require more extensive and costly interventions. The vehicle's complexity and use of advanced technology can increase repair costs.
