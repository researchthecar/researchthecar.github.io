# 2022 Audi SQ5

### Overview
The 2022 Audi SQ5 is a performance-oriented compact luxury SUV that generally offers a high level of build quality and technological integration. As a performance variant, it utilizes a potent V6 engine paired with a sophisticated transmission system, designed for both power delivery and refinement. Reliability data for the 2022 model year suggests a strong overall performance, with most reported issues being minor or related to specific electronic features rather than core mechanical components. Owners generally appreciate the SQ5's driving dynamics, premium interior, and advanced safety systems. However, like many modern vehicles with complex electronics, occasional glitches can occur, though widespread, critical failures are uncommon.

### Infotainment System
**Software Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Screen freezing, unresponsiveness, connectivity issues with Apple CarPlay/Android Auto.

### Driver Assistance Systems
**Sensor Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** False warnings from adaptive cruise control, lane keeping assist, or parking sensors. System deactivation.
- **Solution:** Cleaning or recalibration of sensors. Replacement of faulty sensors.
- **Cost:** $300 - $800

### Summary Verdict
The 2022 Audi SQ5 demonstrates commendable reliability, particularly in its core powertrain components. The 3.0L TFSI V6 engine and 8-speed automatic transmission are robust and generally free from significant issues. The primary areas where minor concerns have been noted are within the optional adaptive air suspension system, which can experience compressor failures, and the complex electrical systems, particularly the infotainment and driver assistance features, which may encounter occasional software glitches or sensor issues. These electrical gremlins are not uncommon in modern luxury vehicles and are often resolvable with software updates or component recalibration. Overall, the SQ5 offers a reliable and enjoyable ownership experience, with most potential issues being manageable and not indicative of systemic mechanical flaws.
