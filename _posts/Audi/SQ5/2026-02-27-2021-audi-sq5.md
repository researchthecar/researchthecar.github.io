# 2021 Audi SQ5 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2021 Audi SQ5, buyers are looking at a luxury performance crossover that combines everyday utility with strong turbocharged power. While many owners enjoy its engaging driving dynamics and refined interior, used car shoppers should review owner-reported concerns ranging from electrical glitches to powertrain and safety system hiccups. A thorough pre-purchase inspection is recommended to verify that all electronic modules and mechanical components are operating as intended.

---

## Engine

### 3.0L Turbocharged V6 (EA839)

The 2021 Audi SQ5 features a 3.0-liter turbocharged V6 engine producing approximately 349 horsepower and 369 lb-ft of torque. It delivers rapid acceleration and athletic performance, though buyers should verify regular maintenance and monitor cooling system components.

**Potential Piston Skirt Concern**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have reported concerns regarding a potential piston skirt defect associated with the EA839 engine architecture, which carries a risk of severe internal engine damage if left unchecked.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified technician perform a thorough pre-purchase inspection, listen for abnormal engine noises, and check service history records.

**Coolant Pump Leak**
- **Symptoms:** Coolant pump failure can lead to coolant leaks and potential engine overheating risks during routine operation.
- **Solution:** Inspect the cooling system for signs of leakage or coolant loss during routine maintenance checks and replace failing pumps promptly.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Tiptronic Automatic

Power is routed through an 8-speed Tiptronic automatic transmission paired with quattro all-wheel drive, offering smooth shifts and responsive gear selection under normal driving conditions.

**Sudden Power Disengagement**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have experienced unexpected losses of motive power where the transmission completely disengages gears while driving in traffic.
- **Solution:** Check for diagnostic trouble codes in the transmission control module and ensure software updates are current.

## Brakes

### Comfort Module and MMI System

Electrical architecture supports advanced infotainment and driver assistance features, but complex module networks can occasionally manifest intermittent warnings or connectivity glitches.

**Comfort Module J393 Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Failure of the comfort control module can trigger multiple electronic warnings—including brake and lighting alerts—and can occasionally prevent the vehicle from starting.
- **Solution:** Diagnose the electrical module and replace or reprogram the comfort control unit as needed.

**MMI and Emergency Call Connectivity**
- **Symptoms:** The Multi Media Interface (MMI) may fail to connect properly, affecting features like the control module for the emergency call system.
- **Solution:** Perform software resets, check system updates, or consult a dealer for module diagnostics.

## Safety

### Driver Assistance and Restraint Systems

Advanced safety and driver-assist technologies provide modern protection, though sensor alignment and module communication issues can occasionally require professional calibration.

**Unintended Forward Collision Activation**
- **Symptoms:** The pre-sense or automatic emergency braking system may occasionally trigger unnecessarily during highway driving, causing sudden seatbelt tightening.
- **Solution:** Have dealership technicians inspect sensor calibration and check for relevant software updates.

**Rear-View Camera Blank Screen**
- **Symptoms:** The infotainment display may show a blank screen when shifting into reverse, hindering visibility behind the vehicle.
- **Solution:** Inspect camera connections, software versions, and display modules for faults.

**Persistent Safety System Malfunction Light**
- **Symptoms:** An persistent airbag or seatbelt warning light may remain illuminated on the dashboard despite component replacements.
- **Solution:** Perform advanced diagnostic troubleshooting on the airbag control module and associated wiring harnesses.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V606000: Seat Belts:Rear/Other:Retractor](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Audi&model=SQ5&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 208, "Occupant Crash Protection." An unsecured child restraint system can increase the risk of injury during a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect, and replace as necessary, the middle-rear seat belt assemblies, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V034000: Tires](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Audi&model=SQ5&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The rear axle alignment may not have been inspected after the repairs for Recall 21V-295 (42L1) were performed. Rear axle misalignment may cause premature or uneven tire wear, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the rear axle, adjust the alignment as necessary, and replace any prematurely or unevenly worn tires, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V947000: Electrical System:Body Control Module/Bcm](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Audi&model=SQ5&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** A gateway control module shutdown will suddenly reduce engine power, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will install a protective cover on the gateway control module and seal an underbody seam as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V295000: Suspension:Rear:Axle:Non-Powered Axle Assembly](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Audi&model=SQ5&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** A broken lock nut may misalign the rear axle, causing a loss of control and increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the lock nuts and, if necessary, the associated bolts, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V742000: Back Over Prevention:Display Function](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Audi&model=SQ5&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 111, "Rear Visibility." A blank rearview camera display can reduce the driver's rear visibility, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the infotainment unit software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2021 Audi SQ5 offers a compelling blend of luxury, technology, and performance, making it an attractive choice on the used market. However, prospective buyers should carefully examine service records and test all electrical, safety, and powertrain functions during a pre-purchase inspection. Being mindful of these reported owner concerns will help ensure a more confident and trouble-free ownership experience.
