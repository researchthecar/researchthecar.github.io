The 2017 Audi A1, a premium subcompact hatchback, generally presents a strong reliability record, reflecting Audi's commitment to quality engineering. This analysis focuses on documented technical problems beyond routine maintenance, examining common areas such as the engine, transmission, suspension, electrical systems, and interior/exterior components. Owners generally report satisfaction, but understanding potential weak points can aid in proactive maintenance and informed ownership.

### Summary Verdict
The 2017 Audi A1 is generally a reliable vehicle, particularly in its powertrain options. The manual transmissions and most engine variants show minimal reported issues. The primary area of concern is the 7-speed S tronic dual-clutch automatic transmission, which can experience issues with its mechatronic unit, leading to costly repairs. Electrical gremlins, such as infotainment glitches and sensor failures, are also noted but are not typically widespread. Overall, with diligent maintenance and awareness of potential S tronic issues, the 2017 A1 offers a dependable premium small car experience.
