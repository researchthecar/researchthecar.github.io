Here's a breakdown of common issues and complaints for the 2017 Audi A1, organized by category and engine/transmission type, following your specified format. The 2017 Audi A1 is a subcompact hatchback known for its stylish design, premium interior, and agile handling. Engine options include a range of petrol and diesel engines. People generally like the A1 for its premium feel in a small package and its fuel efficiency. Common dislikes include its relatively high price compared to competitors and limited rear passenger space.

**Engine**

**1.0L TFSI Petrol**
* **Turbocharger Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, engine warning light, whistling noise from the engine bay.
    * Solution: Replacement of the turbocharger.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1200 - $2500+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Ignition Coil Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Engine misfires, rough idling, engine warning light illuminated.
    * Solution: Replacement of the faulty ignition coil(s).
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**1.4L TFSI Petrol**

* **Oil Consumption**
    * Description/Symptoms: Frequent need to top up engine oil, blue smoke from the exhaust (in severe cases).
    * Solution: Depending on the severity, the solution could range from valve stem seal replacement to piston ring replacement.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $4000+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Water Pump Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaks, overheating, engine warning light.
    * Solution: Replacement of the water pump.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+
    * Recalls: None Found

**1.6L TDI Diesel**
* **Diesel Particulate Filter (DPF) Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: Engine warning light, reduced engine performance, frequent DPF regeneration cycles, potential for DPF clogging.
    * Solution: Forced DPF regeneration (if possible), DPF cleaning, or DPF replacement.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $2000+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Exhaust Gas Recirculation (EGR) Valve Problems**
    * Description/Symptoms: Rough idling, poor acceleration, engine warning light.
    * Solution: EGR valve cleaning or replacement.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**7-Speed S Tronic (Dual Clutch)**

* **Mechatronic Unit Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Harsh shifting, jerky acceleration, transmission slipping, inability to select gears.
    * Solution: Replacement or repair of the mechatronic unit.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1500 - $4000+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Clutch Pack Wear/Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Transmission slipping, especially under load, difficulty engaging gears, shuddering during acceleration.
    * Solution: Replacement of the clutch pack.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2000 - $4500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Noises/Clunking Sounds from Suspension**
    * Description/Symptoms: Knocking or rattling sounds when driving over bumps or uneven surfaces. Often related to worn-out suspension bushings or ball joints.
    * Solution: Inspection and replacement of worn suspension components (bushings, ball joints, sway bar links).
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1000+ (depending on the component and extent of the damage)
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Infotainment System Glitches**
    * Description/Symptoms: Freezing, lagging, unresponsive touchscreen, Bluetooth connectivity issues, navigation problems.
    * Solution: Software update (if available), resetting the system, or replacement of the infotainment unit (in severe cases).
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 (software update) - $2000+ (infotainment unit replacement)
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Central Locking Problems**
    * Description/Symptoms: Doors not locking or unlocking properly, remote key fob malfunctions.
    * Solution: Check and replace the car's battery. Then Check the key fob battery. If the problem persists, have the central locking system diagnosed to find and replace damaged parts.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $5 - $500+ (depending on the repair).
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **No Issues Found**

**Interior**

* **Sunroof Malfunctions**
    * Description/Symptoms: Sunroof not opening or closing properly, getting stuck, making unusual noises.
    * Solution: Lubrication of sunroof tracks, repair or replacement of sunroof motor or mechanism.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $800+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2017 Audi A1, while offering a premium driving experience, is subject to several potential issues. The engine is prone to turbocharger failures in the 1.0L models and oil consumption in the 1.4L models. The 7-speed S Tronic transmission is reported to have mechatronic unit and clutch pack failures, and electrical issues with the infotainment system are prevalent. Suspension components may wear out, resulting in noises. While some fixes are relatively inexpensive, major repairs like transmission or turbocharger replacements can be costly. Prospective buyers should carefully inspect the vehicle's service history and consider a pre-purchase inspection to identify potential issues.
