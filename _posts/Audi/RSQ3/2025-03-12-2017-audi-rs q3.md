The 2017 Audi RS Q3 is a compact performance SUV known for its powerful engine, sporty handling, and stylish design. In some markets, it features a 2.5-liter turbocharged five-cylinder engine producing around 367 horsepower and 343 lb-ft of torque, paired with a 7-speed S tronic dual-clutch transmission. While it offers exhilarating performance and a premium feel, some owners have reported specific issues. Owners tend to like the car's unique engine sound and compact size, but some dislike its firm ride and relatively high price point.

**Engine**

**2.5L Turbocharged Five-Cylinder**

*   **Oil Consumption**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported higher than expected oil consumption. Symptoms can include needing to add oil between scheduled oil changes, low oil level warnings, and in some cases, blue smoke from the exhaust.
    *   Solution: Monitoring oil levels regularly and addressing any leaks promptly. In severe cases, piston ring replacement may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $5,000+ (if piston ring replacement is needed)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Turbocharger Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Whining noises during acceleration, loss of power, check engine light illumination, and potentially black smoke from the exhaust.
    *   Solution: Turbocharger replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,500 - $4,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**7-Speed S tronic Dual-Clutch**

*   **Mechatronic Unit Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Harsh or jerky shifting, delayed engagement, failure to shift, transmission malfunction warnings. These issues are often related to the Mechatronic unit (valve body).
    *   Solution: Mechatronic unit replacement or repair.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Clutch Wear**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Slipping during acceleration, difficulty engaging gears, and shuddering.
    *   Solution: Clutch pack replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Adaptive Damper Failure (if equipped)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Uneven ride height, knocking noises from the suspension, harsh ride, and warning lights on the dashboard.
    *   Solution: Damper replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,500+ per damper.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Glitches**
    *   Description/Symptoms: System freezes, unresponsive touchscreen, Bluetooth connectivity problems, navigation issues.
    *   Solution: Software update, system reset, or head unit replacement (in severe cases).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 (software update) - $1,500+ (head unit replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Sensor Malfunctions**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Faulty readings from sensors (e.g., parking sensors, tire pressure sensors), leading to warning lights or inaccurate data.
    *   Solution: Sensor replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500 per sensor.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **No Issues Found**

**Interior**

*   **Sunroof Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Sunroof fails to open or close properly, makes excessive noise during operation, or leaks.
    *   Solution: Sunroof motor replacement, track repair/lubrication, or seal replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,200+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Seat Heater Malfunction (if equipped)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Seat heaters not working, overheating, or only working intermittently.
    *   Solution: Heater element replacement or wiring repair.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2017 Audi RS Q3, while a performance-oriented vehicle, can present owners with a range of issues. The most concerning are potential engine oil consumption and transmission problems. Electrical issues, particularly with the infotainment system and sensors, are also common. Suspension and interior problems may occur but are less frequent. The cost of repairs can vary significantly, with engine and transmission work being the most expensive. Overall, potential buyers should be aware of these potential issues and factor them into their purchasing decision.
