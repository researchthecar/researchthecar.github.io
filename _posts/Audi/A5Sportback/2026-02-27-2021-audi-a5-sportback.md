# 2021 Audi A5 Sportback Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2021 Audi A5 Sportback, buyers are generally greeted by a stylish, luxurious, and technologically advanced vehicle that blends practicality with sporty performance. However, reviewing owner-submitted complaints provides helpful insight into potential areas that warrant closer inspection during a test drive. While many systems perform reliably, prospective owners should pay attention to reported quirks involving electrical components and transmission behavior.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4 (45 TFSI)

This 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine produces approximately 261 horsepower and 273 lb-ft of torque, offering brisk acceleration and everyday efficiency. It represents the standard powerplant for modern A5 models and generally delivers dependable performance when properly maintained.

## Transmission

### 7-Speed S-tronic Dual-Clutch Automatic

The 7-speed dual-clutch automatic transmission delivers rapid gear changes and pairs with quattro all-wheel drive. While generally smooth, buyers should thoroughly test the transmission's shifting behavior during a pre-purchase inspection.

**Transmission Failure and Gear Shifting Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have reported warning lights regarding transmission failure, limited vehicle operation modes, or sudden cessation of gear shifting while driving.
- **Solution:** Diagnostic scanning by a qualified technician is necessary to identify potential mechatronic or software faults.

## Electrical

### Instrument Panel and Power Systems

The electrical architecture supports numerous advanced displays and driver aids, though occasional glitches have been reported by owners.

**Instrument Cluster Blanking and Power Loss**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have experienced the digital instrument panel blinking and going dark while driving, as well as sudden losses of vehicle power during low-speed maneuvers.
- **Solution:** Have the vehicle's electrical charging system, battery connections, and control modules inspected by an Audi specialist.

## Safety

### Passenger Airbag Sensor

Safety systems operate dependably overall, though minor sensor anomalies can require dealership calibration or component replacement.

**Passenger Airbag Indicator Fluctuation**
- **Symptoms:** The passenger-side airbag warning indicator may turn on and off intermittently regardless of whether the seat is occupied.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the faulty seat sensor or module by an authorized service center typically resolves the issue.

### Forward Collision Avoidance

Driver assistance features enhance protection, but sensor sensitivity can occasionally lead to unexpected system behaviors.

**Pre Sense False Activation**
- **Symptoms:** The automatic emergency braking system may occasionally engage harshly without an actual road hazard present.
- **Solution:** Ensure that camera and radar sensors are clean and check with a dealer for any applicable sensor calibration updates.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V606000: Seat Belts:Rear/Other:Retractor](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Audi&model=A5&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 208, "Occupant Crash Protection." An unsecured child restraint system can increase the risk of injury during a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect, and replace as necessary, the middle-rear seat belt assemblies, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V034000: Tires](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Audi&model=A5&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The rear axle alignment may not have been inspected after the repairs for Recall 21V-295 (42L1) were performed. Rear axle misalignment may cause premature or uneven tire wear, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the rear axle, adjust the alignment as necessary, and replace any prematurely or unevenly worn tires, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V737000: Exterior Lighting:Tail Lights](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Audi&model=A5&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The wiring harness for the rear lights could be missing the anti-abrasion protection in the area of the spare wheel well. Without the scuff protection, the wires may become damaged during a rear crash, disabling the doors from being opened from the outside, causing a loss of hazard lights, and increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Audi will notify owners, and dealers will apply scuff protection, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V295000: Suspension:Rear:Axle:Non-Powered Axle Assembly](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Audi&model=A5&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** A broken lock nut may misalign the rear axle, causing a loss of control and increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the lock nuts and, if necessary, the associated bolts, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V742000: Back Over Prevention:Display Function](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Audi&model=A5&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 111, "Rear Visibility." A blank rearview camera display can reduce the driver's rear visibility, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the infotainment unit software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2021 Audi A5 Sportback offers an appealing mix of luxury and driving dynamics, making it an attractive used car choice. Buyers should prioritize a comprehensive pre-purchase inspection that evaluates the dual-clutch transmission operation and verifies proper functioning of the electrical and driver-assistance systems.
