**Introduction to the 2022 Audi A5 Sportback**

The 2022 Audi A5 Sportback is a stylish and sporty compact luxury sedan that offers a dynamic driving experience. It is available with a range of engine and transmission options, including a 2.0L turbocharged inline-four (248 hp/273 lb-ft), a 3.0L turbocharged V6 (349 hp/369 lb-ft), and a 2.0L turbocharged diesel inline-four (190 hp/295 lb-ft). The A5 Sportback comes standard with a 7-speed dual-clutch transmission, and a 6-speed manual transmission is available on select models.

The A5 Sportback has received positive reviews for its sharp handling, comfortable interior, and advanced technology. However, some owners have reported issues with various aspects of the vehicle.

**Engine**

**2.0L Turbocharged Inline-Four**

* **Oil Leak:** Some vehicles may experience an oil leak from the timing chain cover. Symptoms include an oil leak under the engine and a burning oil smell. The problem is fixed by replacing the timing chain cover gasket. Estimated cost to fix: $500 - $1,000
* **Turbocharger Failure:** Some vehicles have experienced turbocharger failure. Symptoms include a loss of power, excessive smoke from the exhaust, and a Check Engine light. The turbocharger is replaced. Estimated cost to fix: $2,000 - $3,000

**3.0L Turbocharged V6**

* **Valve Train Noise:** Some vehicles may experience excessive valve train noise. Symptoms include a ticking or rattling sound from the engine. Replacing the camshaft and lifters resolves the issue. Estimated cost to fix: $1,500 - $2,500
* **Fuel Pump Failure:** Some vehicles have experienced fuel pump failure. Symptoms include a lack of fuel delivery, a loss of power, and a Check Engine light. The fuel pump is replaced. Estimated cost to fix: $1,000 - $1,500

**Transmission**

**7-Speed Dual-Clutch Transmission**

* **Clutch Chatter:** Some vehicles may experience clutch chatter during shifts. Symptoms include a juddering sensation or noise during gear changes. The clutch is replaced. Estimated cost to fix: $1,500 - $2,000
* **Transmission Fluid Leak:** Some vehicles may experience a transmission fluid leak. Symptoms include a puddle of fluid under the vehicle and a Check Engine light. The transmission is removed, inspected, and repaired as needed. Estimated cost to fix: $1,000 - $2,000

**Suspension**

* **Rear Shock Absorber Failure:** Some vehicles may experience rear shock absorber failure. Symptoms include a rough ride, a loss of stability, and a knocking or rattling sound from the rear of the vehicle. Replacing the rear shock absorbers resolves the issue. Estimated cost to fix: $500 - $1,000
* **Front Spring Breakage:** Some vehicles have experienced a broken front coil spring. Symptoms include a harsh ride and excessive body roll on one side of the vehicle. Replacing the front coil spring solves the problem. Estimated cost to fix: $250 - $500

**Electrical**

* **Electrical System Malfunction:** Some vehicles may experience an electrical system malfunction. Symptoms include intermittent power loss, flickering lights, and a dead battery. A diagnostic test is performed to identify and repair the issue. Estimated cost to fix: $500 - $1,500

**Exterior**

* **Door Lock Malfunction:** Some vehicles have had issues with the door locks not functioning properly. Symptoms include a key fob not unlocking the car, doors not locking, and a door ajar warning light. The door lock is replaced or repaired. Estimated cost to fix: $500 - $1,000

**Interior**

* **Sunroof Rattle:** Some vehicles may have a sunroof that rattles or makes noises when opened or closed. Adjusting or replacing the sunroof tracks and seals resolves the issue. Estimated cost to fix: $500 - $1,000
* **Navigation System Glitch:** Some vehicles have experienced a glitch with the navigation system. Symptoms included a blank screen, incorrect navigation instructions, or system freezes. Updating the navigation system software fixes the problem. Estimated cost to fix: Free (software update)

**Recalls**

* NHTSA Campaign Number 22V810000: Rear seat belt buckle may not engage
* NHTSA Campaign Number 21V826000: Software update for brake system
* NHTSA Campaign Number 21V678000: Fuel pump may fail

**Summary**

While the 2022 Audi A5 Sportback is generally a reliable and well-built vehicle, it has had some reported issues with its engine, transmission, suspension, and electrical systems. The severity of these issues varies, but some can be costly to repair. When considering a used A5 Sportback, it is recommended to have the vehicle inspected by a mechanic to identify any potential problems.
