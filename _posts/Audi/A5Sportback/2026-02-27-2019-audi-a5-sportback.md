# 2019 Audi A5 Sportback Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2019 Audi A5 Sportback combines striking design, luxury features, and dynamic performance, making it an appealing option on the used market. However, prospective buyers should review owner-reported complaints regarding electrical issues, steering components, and driver-assistance systems. While NHTSA reports reflect individual owner experiences rather than guaranteed factory defects, checking maintenance records and performing a thorough pre-purchase inspection is strongly advised.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged I4 (45 TFSI)

This 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine produces approximately 248 horsepower and 273 lb-ft of torque, offering lively acceleration paired with respectable efficiency. It serves as the primary powerplant for the A5 lineup, delivering a refined driving experience when properly maintained.

## Transmission

### 7-Speed S tronic Dual-Clutch Automatic

The 7-speed dual-clutch automatic transmission delivers rapid gear shifts and power delivery via Audi's signature quattro all-wheel-drive system. Routine fluid changes are critical to maintaining smooth operation over the vehicle's lifespan.

## Steering

### Electric Power Steering

Power steering reliability is generally acceptable, though isolated reports of sudden electronic assist failures warrant attention during a test drive.

**Sudden Steering Stiffness**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may experience a sudden 'Steering Malfunction' warning message accompanied by an extremely stiff steering wheel while driving at highway speeds.
- **Solution:** Have the vehicle inspected by a qualified technician to diagnose potential electronic power steering module or sensor failures.

## Electrical

### Driver Assistance & Infotainment

The complex electronic architecture supports numerous convenience and safety features, though occasional software glitches and sensor misfires have been reported by owners.

**Auto Start-Stop and Camera Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have reported unexpected engine cut-offs from the auto start-stop system, loss of the rearview camera with 'SOS System Deactivated' prompts, and erratic cruise control behavior.
- **Solution:** Software updates or module replacements performed by a dealership can often resolve persistent electronic glitches.

## Exterior

### Door Sills and Seals

Exterior body seals and panels are generally well-fitted, though minor drainage issues in door frames can occasionally occur.

**Water Accumulation in Door Wells**
- **Symptoms:** Water collects inside rear door sills or wells after heavy rain or car washes, often producing a sloshing sound during braking or acceleration.
- **Solution:** Inspect and clear door drain plugs or replace compromised weatherstripping seals to prevent moisture buildup.

## Safety

### Pre-Sense Collision Avoidance

Advanced safety features provide an extra layer of protection, but sensitivity issues in specific driving scenarios have occasionally caught drivers off guard.

**Premature Automatic Braking**
- **Symptoms:** The pre-sense safety system may falsely detect oncoming traffic or obstacles during normal turns, triggering unexpected and hard emergency braking.
- **Solution:** Check for available sensor calibration updates or have dealer technicians inspect the collision avoidance radar and camera alignment.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V034000: Tires](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Audi&model=A5&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** The rear axle alignment may not have been inspected after the repairs for Recall 21V-295 (42L1) were performed. Rear axle misalignment may cause premature or uneven tire wear, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the rear axle, adjust the alignment as necessary, and replace any prematurely or unevenly worn tires, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V295000: Suspension:Rear:Axle:Non-Powered Axle Assembly](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Audi&model=A5&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** A broken lock nut may misalign the rear axle, causing a loss of control and increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the lock nuts and, if necessary, the associated bolts, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V874000: Seats](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Audi&model=A5&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** A disabled passenger air bag increases the risk of injury in the event of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the connecting cable and either the heating mat or entire seat cover, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2019 Audi A5 Sportback is a stylish and engaging luxury vehicle that rewards careful shopping. While major mechanical failures are uncommon, buyers should pay close attention to electronic features, driver-assistance calibration, and steering functionality during a pre-purchase inspection. Securing a vehicle with a documented service history will help ensure a more confident ownership experience.
