# 2017 Audi Q7 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The Audi Q7 delivers a polished driving experience, highly praised interior luxury, and capable handling for a three-row luxury SUV. However, prospective buyers shopping in the used market should review historical owner complaints closely, particularly regarding electrical glitches, coolant leaks, and suspension wear. Being aware of these areas can help you budget for potential upkeep and select a well-maintained example.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged I4

The 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine produces approximately 252 horsepower and 273 lb-ft of torque, offering efficient daily performance for the Q7. Buyers looking for standard fuel economy find this option sufficient, though proper maintenance is vital.

### 3.0L Supercharged V6

The 3.0-liter supercharged V6 pushes out around 333 horsepower and 325 lb-ft of torque, providing brisk acceleration and robust towing capability. It is a popular powertrain choice, though cooling system wear requires careful pre-purchase inspection.

**Coolant Leaks and Water Pump Failure**
- **Symptoms:** A sweet coolant smell, visible fluid puddles under the vehicle, or low coolant warning lights, which can escalate to overheating if left unaddressed.
- **Solution:** Inspect the water pump and related cooling system components; replace leaking pumps, thermostats, or hoses as needed.

**Engine Misfires**
- **Symptoms:** Hesitation during acceleration, rough idling, shaking, or illumination of the check engine light.
- **Solution:** Diagnose ignition coils, spark plugs, or fuel injectors, and replace faulty components.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

Equipped with a smooth-shifting 8-speed automatic transmission paired with quattro all-wheel drive, this setup delivers refined gear changes across all trims. Overall mechanical reliability is solid, though fluid changes are recommended at proper service intervals.

## Suspension

### Coil Springs & Rear Suspension

The suspension system maintains high ride comfort, but rear coil springs are known to occasionally crack or break, requiring timely replacement to restore proper vehicle stability.

**Broken Rear Coil Springs**
- **Symptoms:** Unusual clunking noises from the rear, a noticeable vehicle sag, or unstable handling and vibrations at higher speeds.
- **Solution:** Inspect rear coil springs and rubber seats during routine maintenance; replace broken or swollen springs in matching axle pairs.

## Steering

### Power Steering System

Steering feel is precise for a large luxury SUV, though isolated electronic warnings and hardware bolt concerns require prompt attention when they arise.

**Steering Malfunction Warning and Loss of Assist**
- **Symptoms:** Sudden heavy steering effort, dashboard warnings indicating a severe steering malfunction, or looseness due to hardware issues.
- **Solution:** Have a certified technician inspect steering shaft bolts, electronic steering assist modules, or replace the rack and pinion if mechanical failure occurs.

## Exterior

### Exterior Lighting & Side Markers

Exterior lighting systems generally perform well, but sensor sensitivities and integrated LED assemblies can occasionally trigger persistent dashboard error warnings.

**Side Marker and Headlight Warnings**
- **Symptoms:** Dashboard error messages stating that a side-marker or headlight is defective, even though the physical light bulb or LED continues to illuminate normally.
- **Solution:** Inspect wiring harnesses, lighting control modules, or replace sealed headlight assemblies if internal LED circuits trigger false error codes.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V035000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Fuel Injection System:Fuel Rail](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Audi&model=Q7&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** A fuel leak in the presence of an ignition source can increase risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Audi has notified owners, and dealers will replace the left and right fuel rail, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V641000: Seats:Mid/Rear Assembly](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Audi&model=Q7&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** As such, the vehicles do not conform to Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 207, "Seating Systems." If the third row seat does not remain in its adjusted position, there may be an increased risk of injury to an occupant in the event of a crash.
- **What to do:** Audi will notify owners, and dealers will install an additional support bracket to the seat, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V955000: Seat Belts:Pretensioner](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Audi&model=Q7&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** Additionally, certain 2017 Audi Q7, A4 Sedan, A4 Allroad, and 2018 Audi Q5 vehicles are being recalled because the seat belt pretensioners may not activate properly. In the event of a crash, if the air bags and/or the seat belt pretensioners do not inflate or function properly, the vehicle occupants have an increased risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Volkswagen and Audi will notify owners, and dealers will replace the affected air bags and seat belt pretensioners, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V648000: Steering:Electric Power Assist System](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Audi&model=Q7&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** In the affected vehicles, the electric power steering assist control unit may short circuit resulting in a loss of power steering assist. With a loss of power steering assist, extra steering effort will be required, especially at lower speeds, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Audi will notify owners, and dealers will replace the steering rack, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V642000: Electrical System:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Audi&model=Q7&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** The affected vehicles may have been programmed with new software that inadvertently makes the side marker lights inoperative in conjunction with the parking lights or the headlights. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 108, "Lamps, Reflective Devices, and Associated Equipment.".
- **What to do:** Audi will notify owners, and dealers will upload revised software to correct the issue, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V519000: Air Bags:Frontal:Sensor/Control Module-Inactive](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Audi&model=Q7&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** Due to a software issue within the air bag control module, the frontal air bags may deploy with more force than required in certain crashes. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 208, "Occupant Crash Protection." - **What to do:** Volkswagen will notify owners, and dealers will update the air bag control module software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V149000: Steering](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Audi&model=Q7&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** A sudden loss of loss of steering control may increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Volkswagen will notify owners, and Audi dealers will inspect and, if necessary, replace the steering shaft bolt, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

When shopping for a used model, verifying service records for cooling system maintenance and suspension components will go a long way toward ensuring a dependable ownership experience. While electronic warning alerts and minor lighting glitches appear frequently in owner feedback, addressing mechanical basics upfront lets you enjoy the Q7's luxury and performance with confidence.
