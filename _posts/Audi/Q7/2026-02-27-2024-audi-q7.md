## Suspension

### Adaptive Air Suspension
**Air Compressor Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle may sag on one or more corners, suspension warning light illuminated, uneven ride height.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the air compressor unit.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1500

**Air Strut Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Gradual loss of ride height, particularly when parked, bouncing or excessive movement over bumps.
- **Solution:** Replacement of leaking air strut(s).
- **Cost:** $700 - $1200 per strut

## Electrical

### Infotainment System
**Software Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Screen freezing, unresponsive touch controls, intermittent connectivity issues with Apple CarPlay/Android Auto.
- **Solution:** Software updates or system reset. In rare cases, module replacement.
- **Cost:** $150 - $600


### Advanced Driver-Assistance Systems (ADAS)
**Sensor Calibration Issues**
- **Symptoms:** False warnings from safety systems (e.g., lane keeping assist, adaptive cruise control), system deactivation.
- **Solution:** Recalibration of sensors, potential cleaning or replacement of sensor components.
- **Cost:** $200 - $700

### Summary Verdict
The 2024 Audi Q7 demonstrates a generally high level of technical reliability, particularly in its powertrain and core mechanical systems. The available 2.0L and 3.0L turbocharged engines, paired with the 8-speed automatic transmission, are robust and have a solid track record. The primary areas where potential issues have been noted, based on previous model years and the complexity of the systems, are the adaptive air suspension, which can experience compressor or strut leaks, and the advanced electrical systems, including the infotainment and ADAS features, which may encounter software glitches or require sensor recalibration. These are not uncommon for vehicles with such sophisticated technology. Overall, the Q7 is a reliable choice, with most potential issues being repairable and not indicative of fundamental design flaws.
