# 2019 Audi Q7 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used luxury midsize SUV, the 2019 Audi Q7 offers an upscale interior, refined handling, and advanced technology features. However, prospective buyers should review recurring owner-reported complaints regarding electrical glitches, steering warnings, and suspension wear to ensure the vehicle has been properly maintained.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4

This base 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine produces approximately 248 horsepower and 273 lb-ft of torque, offering adequate efficiency and pep for everyday commuting while maintaining balanced overall reliability.

### 3.0L Supercharged V6

The 3.0-liter supercharged V6 engine delivers roughly 333 horsepower and 325 lb-ft of torque for robust acceleration and towing capacity, though owners should watch for occasional fuel delivery component failures.

**Fuel Pump Failure**
- **Symptoms:** A drive system malfunction code appears, followed by the engine shutting off immediately while driving.
- **Solution:** Have the vehicle towed to a qualified service center or dealership to inspect diagnostic codes and replace the faulty fuel pump and module.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

The standard 8-speed automatic transmission pairs with quattro all-wheel drive across all trims, delivering smooth, responsive shifts and generally solid long-term mechanical reliability.

**Shifter and Gear Selection Issues**
- **Symptoms:** The driver is unable to shift the vehicle into park after coming to a stop, occasionally trapping keys or locking doors unexpectedly.
- **Solution:** Inspect the gear selector linkage and electronic shifter module, and service or replace components as required.

## Suspension

### Coil Springs

Component summary for suspension coil springs.

**Premature Coil Spring Breakage**
- **Symptoms:** A loud bang is heard while driving, or a broken piece of front or rear coil spring is discovered resting on the ground or floor.
- **Solution:** Inspect the front and rear suspension springs for structural damage and replace broken coil springs in axle pairs.

## Steering

### Electronic Steering System

Component summary for electronic steering system.

**Steering Malfunction Warning and Loss of Control**
- **Symptoms:** A 'Steering Malfunction, Stop Car' warning pop-up appears on the instrument cluster, accompanied by a sudden and complete loss of steering capability.
- **Solution:** Have the vehicle safely towed and diagnosed by a technician to inspect the electronic power steering module and wiring harnesses.

## Electrical

### Infotainment and Starting System

Component summary for electrical and infotainment systems.

**Starter and Infotainment Freezes**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle fails to crank due to a failed starter, or the infotainment touchscreen freezes and unexpectedly reboots while driving.
- **Solution:** Test the battery and starter assembly for electrical draw or mechanical failure, and update the infotainment software as recommended.

## Exterior

### Sunroof and Glass

Component summary for exterior glass and sunroof.

**Spontaneous Sunroof Shattering**
- **Symptoms:** The panoramic sunroof glass explodes or shatters unexpectedly with a loud pop while driving on clear roadways.
- **Solution:** Clear glass debris from the interior cabin and have the sunroof assembly glass panel completely replaced.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V114000: Suspension:Front:Shock Absorber](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Audi&model=Q7&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** Loose shock absorbers may impair vehicle steering, increasing the risk of crash.
- **What to do:** Audi has notified owners, and dealers will replace the shock absorber forks, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V658000: Structure:Body:Roof And Pillars](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Audi&model=Q7&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** The padding on the vehicle's C-pillar may not absorb enough impact in the event of a crash. In the event of a crash, an unbelted occupant's head may impact the vehicle's C-pillar padding, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Audi will notify owners, and dealers will replace the deformation element on the C-pillars, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V149000: Steering](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Audi&model=Q7&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** A sudden loss of loss of steering control may increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Volkswagen will notify owners, and Audi dealers will inspect and, if necessary, replace the steering shaft bolt, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V093000: Air Bags:Side/Window](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Audi&model=Q7&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** In the event of a crash, the side curtain air bags may not properly inflate. An air bag that does not properly inflate in the event of a crash can increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Volkswagen will notify owners, and dealers will replace the side curtain air bags, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2019 Audi Q7 delivers a high-end driving experience, comfort, and advanced features, but prospective buyers should carefully inspect vehicle history and functionality before purchase. Particular attention should be given to steering warning alerts, suspension components, and electrical electronics to ensure a trouble-free ownership experience.
