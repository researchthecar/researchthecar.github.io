The 2017 Audi Q7 is a luxury SUV known for its comfortable ride, spacious interior, and advanced technology features. Engine options for the 2017 Q7 include a 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine producing 252 horsepower and 273 lb-ft of torque, and a 3.0-liter supercharged V6 engine generating 333 horsepower and 325 lb-ft of torque. Both engines are paired with an eight-speed automatic transmission. Owners often praise the Q7's refined driving experience, luxurious interior, and user-friendly infotainment system. Common criticisms include its high price point and potentially costly maintenance.

**Engine**

**2.0L Turbocharged Four-Cylinder**

*   **Oil Consumption**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Excessive oil consumption, requiring frequent oil top-offs between scheduled oil changes. Owners might notice blue exhaust smoke or low oil level warnings.
    *   Solution: Audi has released updated PCV valves and software updates to address oil consumption issues. If the issue persists, piston ring replacement may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $5,000+ (piston ring replacement can be quite expensive).
    *   Recalls: None Found

**3.0L Supercharged V6**

*   **Coolant Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaking from the supercharger.  This can result in low coolant levels, overheating, and potential engine damage.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the supercharger.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $3,000-$5,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**8-Speed Automatic (Both Engines)**

*   **Harsh Shifting/Hesitation**
    *   Description/Symptoms:  Rough or delayed shifting, especially between certain gears. Owners may notice a "clunking" sound or hesitation when accelerating.
    *   Solution: Software update to the transmission control module (TCM) or replacement of valve body.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $3,000+ (depending on the severity and necessary repair).
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Air Suspension Issues (if equipped)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The vehicle may sag on one or more corners, or the air compressor may run excessively. "Suspension Fault" warnings may appear on the dashboard.
    *   Solution: Replace the leaking air strut, repair or replace the air compressor, or address any leaks in the air lines.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $2,000+ per strut; $500 - $1,500+ for the compressor.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Malfunctions**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The MMI (Multi Media Interface) system may freeze, crash, or exhibit intermittent glitches. Features such as navigation, audio, or Bluetooth connectivity may be affected.
    *   Solution: Software update to the MMI system. In some cases, replacement of the MMI control unit may be required.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $1,500+ (depending on the necessary repair).
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Battery Drain**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The battery drains quickly, especially when the vehicle is not driven regularly. This can lead to starting problems.
    *   Solution: Diagnose and repair any parasitic drains. This may involve replacing a faulty module or repairing wiring issues.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $1,000+ (depending on the source of the drain).
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Headlight Condensation**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Moisture accumulating inside the headlight housings. This can reduce headlight visibility and potentially damage the headlight components.
    *   Solution: Replace the headlight housing or attempt to reseal the existing housing.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+ per headlight.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Sunroof Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Sunroof may get stuck, make excessive noise during operation, or leak water.
    *   Solution: Repair or replace the sunroof motor, tracks, or seals.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,200+.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2017 Audi Q7 offers a luxurious and refined driving experience, but potential buyers should be aware of some common issues. Engine problems, particularly oil consumption in the 2.0T and coolant leaks in the 3.0T, can lead to costly repairs. Electrical issues, such as infotainment system malfunctions and battery drain, are also common concerns. Air suspension issues (if equipped) can also be quite expensive to fix. While the severity and cost of these issues vary, it's advisable to have a pre-purchase inspection performed by a qualified mechanic to identify any potential problems. Addressing these issues proactively can help ensure a more reliable ownership experience.
