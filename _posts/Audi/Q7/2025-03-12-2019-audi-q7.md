Here's a breakdown of common issues and complaints for the 2019 Audi Q7, formatted for your website. The 2019 Audi Q7 is a popular luxury SUV known for its comfortable ride, spacious interior, and advanced technology. Engine options include a 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder producing 248 horsepower and 273 lb-ft of torque, and a 3.0-liter supercharged V6 generating 333 horsepower and 325 lb-ft of torque. Both engines are paired with an eight-speed automatic transmission. Owners often praise the Q7's refined driving experience, upscale cabin, and user-friendly infotainment system. However, some common complaints revolve around the reliability of certain electronic components and the potential for expensive repairs.

**Engine**

**2.0L Turbocharged Four-Cylinder**

*   **Excessive Oil Consumption**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners report higher than expected oil consumption, requiring frequent top-offs between scheduled oil changes. Symptoms may include the low oil pressure light illuminating or the need to add oil every few thousand miles.
    *   Solution: The solution can range from replacing the PCV valve to performing an oil consumption test and potentially replacing piston rings.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $3000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**3.0L Supercharged V6**

*   **Coolant Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaks can occur from various points in the cooling system. Common culprits include the water pump, thermostat housing, and coolant hoses. Symptoms include low coolant level, overheating, and visible coolant leaks.
    *   Solution: Replacing the leaking component, such as the water pump, thermostat housing, or hoses.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**8-Speed Automatic (Both Engines)**

*   **Rough Shifting/Hesitation**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported experiencing rough shifting, hesitation during acceleration, or occasional slipping.
    *   Solution: In some cases, a software update or transmission adaptation reset may resolve the issue. In other cases, valve body replacement or transmission overhaul may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $6000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Air Suspension Issues (If Equipped)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Air suspension systems can experience problems with the air compressor, air struts, or air lines. Symptoms include the vehicle sitting unevenly, failure to raise or lower, or error messages on the dashboard.
    *   Solution: Replacing the faulty component, such as the air compressor, air strut, or air line.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $3000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Glitches**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The MMI infotainment system can sometimes experience glitches, such as freezing, slow response times, or intermittent malfunctions.
    *   Solution: Software updates can often resolve these issues. In some cases, replacing the MMI unit may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 - $1500+ (depending on warranty and if replacement is needed)
    *   Recalls: None Found
*   **Sensor Malfunctions**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Various sensors, such as parking sensors, blind spot monitors, and adaptive cruise control sensors, can malfunction.
    *   Solution: Replacing the faulty sensor and recalibrating the system.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+ per sensor
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Sunroof/Moonroof Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Owners have reported issues with the sunroof not opening or closing properly, or excessive wind noise.
    *   Solution: Repair or replacement of sunroof components such as the motor, tracks, or seals.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Malfunctioning Power Windows/Locks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Power windows or locks may fail to operate correctly, including being unresponsive or exhibiting intermittent behavior.
    *   Solution: Replacing faulty window or lock motors, regulators, or switches.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600+ per door
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2019 Audi Q7 is a generally reliable vehicle, but owners should be aware of potential issues, particularly with the engine (oil consumption and coolant leaks), transmission, and electrical systems. The air suspension, if equipped, can also present costly repairs. While some issues are minor and can be resolved with software updates, others may require significant mechanical work. Owners should factor in the potential for higher maintenance and repair costs when considering purchasing a used 2019 Q7. The cost and severity of these issues can vary, but being informed allows for a better purchase decision and preparation for potential maintenance down the road.
