**Introduction**

The 2007 Audi A4 Quattro is a compact luxury sedan that offers a blend of performance and sophistication. It is powered by a 2.0L turbocharged inline-four engine that produces 200 horsepower and 207 lb-ft of torque. The A4 Quattro is available with a six-speed manual transmission or a Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT).

Owners of the A4 Quattro generally praise its sporty handling, comfortable interior, and impressive fuel economy. However, some have reported issues with certain components of the vehicle.

**Engine**

**2.0L Turbocharged Inline-Four**

* **Oil Consumption:** Some owners have reported excessive oil consumption, leading to reduced engine performance and increased emissions. This issue may be caused by faulty piston rings or valve stem seals.
  * Solution: Replace piston rings or valve stem seals.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000
  * Recalls: None Found
* **Timing Chain Tensioner Failure:** The timing chain tensioner may fail, causing the timing chain to skip or break. This can result in catastrophic engine damage.
  * Solution: Replace timing chain and tensioner.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,500
  * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**Six-Speed Manual Transmission**

* **Shifter Linkage Failure:** The shifter linkage may fail, making it difficult or impossible to shift gears.
  * Solution: Replace shifter linkage.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
  * Recalls: None Found

**CVT**

* **Shuddering or Hesitation:** Some owners have experienced shuddering or hesitation during acceleration or gear changes. This may be caused by a faulty torque converter or belt.
  * Solution: Replace torque converter or belt.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,500
  * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Front Control Arm Bushings:** The front control arm bushings may wear out prematurely, causing a variety of symptoms including vibration, knocking noises, and reduced handling performance.
  * Solution: Replace control arm bushings.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $700
  * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain:** Some owners have reported excessive battery drain, even when the vehicle is turned off. This may be caused by a faulty alternator or starter.
  * Solution: Replace faulty component.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
  * Recalls: None Found
* **HID Headlight Ballast Failure:** The HID headlight ballast may fail, resulting in reduced or no headlight illumination.
  * Solution: Replace ballast.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
  * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Tailgate Lock Failure:** The tailgate lock may fail, making it impossible to open the tailgate.
  * Solution: Replace tailgate lock.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
  * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Instrument Cluster Failure:** The instrument cluster may fail, causing a variety of symptoms including flickering gauges, no illumination, or complete failure.
  * Solution: Replace instrument cluster.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
  * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

Overall, the 2007 Audi A4 Quattro is a reliable and enjoyable vehicle to own. However, some owners have reported specific issues that can be costly to repair. The most severe issues include timing chain tensioner failure and oil consumption, which can both lead to catastrophic engine damage. Other issues, such as battery drain and electrical faults, are less severe but can still be inconvenient and costly to fix.
