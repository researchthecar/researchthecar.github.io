# 2014 Audi A4 Quattro

### Overview
The 2014 Audi A4 Quattro is a well-regarded compact executive car, known for its blend of performance, luxury, and all-wheel-drive capability. Generally, it offers a solid ownership experience with a focus on driving dynamics and premium interior appointments. While most owners report satisfaction, like any vehicle, certain components can experience issues over time. This analysis focuses on documented technical reliability concerns beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential problem areas for the 2014 model year, specifically for the Quattro variants across its available powertrains.

---


## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged TFSI (CCTA/CBFA)
**PCV Valve Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Rough idle, increased oil consumption, check engine light, whistling noise from engine bay.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the Positive Crankcase Ventilation (PCV) valve assembly.
- **Cost:** $200 - $500

**Oil Consumption**
- **Symptoms:** Excessive oil burning between oil changes, blue smoke from exhaust, potential for carbon buildup.
- **Solution:** Can involve piston ring replacement, valve stem seal replacement, or PCV system check.
- **Cost:** $1,500 - $3,000+

**Timing Chain Tensioner Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Rattling noise from the front of the engine, especially on startup; check engine light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the timing chain, tensioners, guides, and related seals.
- **Cost:** $1,200 - $2,500

### Infotainment System
**Occasional Glitches**

### Xenon Headlight Control Module
**Failure**
- **Symptoms:** One or both Xenon headlights not working, flickering.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the headlight control module (ballast).
- **Cost:** $200 - $500

### Summary Verdict
The 2014 Audi A4 Quattro generally presents a reliable profile, particularly concerning its Quattro all-wheel-drive system, transmission options, suspension, and core interior/exterior components. The primary areas of concern tend to be within the 2.0L TFSI engine, specifically related to oil consumption, PCV valve failures, and potential timing chain tensioner issues, which can be costly to repair. Electrical gremlins, such as infotainment system glitches and headlight module failures, are also noted but are less common. Prospective buyers should be aware of these potential engine-related issues and factor in possible maintenance costs, especially for higher-mileage examples.
