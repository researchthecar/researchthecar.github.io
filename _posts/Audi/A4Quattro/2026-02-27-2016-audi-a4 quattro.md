# 2016 Audi A4 Quattro

### Overview
The 2016 Audi A4 Quattro, part of the B8 generation's later years, generally presents a solid reliability record. It benefits from Audi's continuous refinement of its platform and powertrains. Owners often praise its refined driving dynamics, comfortable interior, and Quattro all-wheel-drive system, which enhances traction in various conditions. While no vehicle is entirely without potential issues, the A4 Quattro for this model year tends to be a dependable choice for those seeking a premium compact sedan. Regular maintenance is key to ensuring its longevity and optimal performance, as with any German luxury vehicle.

---


## Engine

### 2.0L TFSI (Turbocharged Direct Injection)
**PCV Valve Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Rough idle, oil leaks, increased oil consumption, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the Positive Crankcase Ventilation (PCV) valve assembly.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600

**Oil Consumption**
- **Symptoms:** Excessive oil level drops between changes, blue smoke from exhaust on startup.
- **Solution:** Can be due to worn piston rings or valve stem seals. Requires diagnosis and potential engine internal repairs.
- **Cost:** $1500 - $4000+

### Summary Verdict
The 2016 Audi A4 Quattro is generally a reliable vehicle, particularly for its class and segment. The most commonly cited issues revolve around the 2.0L TFSI engine, specifically related to the PCV system and potential oil consumption, which can be costly to repair if not addressed proactively. Transmission and suspension components are typically robust. Electrical gremlins, especially with the infotainment system, can occur but are often resolved with software updates. Overall, with diligent maintenance and attention to potential engine concerns, the 2016 A4 Quattro offers a dependable and enjoyable ownership experience.
