# 2019 Audi A4 Quattro

### Overview
The 2019 Audi A4 Quattro is generally considered a reliable vehicle, offering a blend of performance, luxury, and advanced technology. This generation of the A4, particularly with the Quattro all-wheel-drive system, is known for its stable handling and comfortable ride. While most owners report a positive experience, like any vehicle, there are specific areas where potential issues have been noted. This analysis focuses on documented technical reliability concerns beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into common problems, their symptoms, potential solutions, and estimated repair costs for various components and powertrain options.

---


## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged TFSI (190 hp)
**Oil Consumption**
- **Symptoms:** Increased oil level checks required, potential blue smoke from exhaust under acceleration.
- **Solution:** Engine internal inspection, piston ring replacement, or PCV valve replacement.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1500


### 2.0L Turbocharged TFSI (248 hp)
**Coolant Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Low coolant level warning, visible coolant residue, overheating.
- **Solution:** Inspection and replacement of coolant hoses, water pump, or thermostat housing.
- **Cost:** $300 - $700


## Transmission

### 7-Speed S tronic (Dual-Clutch Automatic)
**Mechatronic Unit Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Harsh shifting, delayed engagement, transmission warning lights, inability to select gears.
- **Solution:** Replacement or repair of the mechatronic unit.
- **Cost:** $2000 - $4000

**Clutch Pack Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Jerky acceleration, slipping gears, shuddering during engagement.
- **Solution:** Replacement of clutch packs.
- **Cost:** $1500 - $3000

### Summary Verdict
The 2019 Audi A4 Quattro demonstrates good overall reliability, with the powertrain and chassis generally performing well. The most frequently cited concerns revolve around the engine's oil consumption in certain configurations and potential issues with the S tronic dual-clutch transmission's mechatronic unit or clutch packs, which can be costly to repair. Electrical gremlins, particularly with the infotainment system's software, are also a possibility. However, these issues are not universal, and many owners experience trouble-free ownership. Regular maintenance and prompt attention to any warning signs are crucial for maintaining the A4's long-term reliability and value.
