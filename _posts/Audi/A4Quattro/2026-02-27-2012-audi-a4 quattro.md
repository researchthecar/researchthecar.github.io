# 2012 Audi A4 Quattro

### Overview
The 2012 Audi A4 Quattro, a popular compact executive car, is generally considered a reliable vehicle, particularly for those seeking a blend of performance and luxury. It offers a comfortable ride and a premium interior, with the Quattro all-wheel-drive system providing excellent traction in various conditions. While most owners report a positive experience, like any vehicle, there are specific areas that have seen more attention from a reliability standpoint. This analysis focuses on documented common issues beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential concerns for owners and prospective buyers of the 2012 model year.

---


## Engine

### 2.0L TFSI (CCTA/CBFA)
**PCV Valve Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Rough idle, increased oil consumption, potential stalling, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the Positive Crankcase Ventilation (PCV) valve assembly.
- **Cost:** $200 - $500

**Oil Consumption**
- **Symptoms:** Excessive oil burning between oil changes, blue smoke from exhaust.
- **Solution:** Often related to worn piston rings or PCV system issues; may require engine internal repairs.
- **Cost:** $1,000 - $3,000+

**Timing Chain Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Rattling noise from the front of the engine, especially on startup; check engine light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the timing chain, tensioners, and guides.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1,500

## Electrical

### General Electrical System
**Infotainment System Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Screen freezing, audio cutting out, MMI system unresponsiveness.
- **Solution:** Software updates or replacement of MMI control unit.
- **Cost:** $300 - $1,000

**Xenon Headlight Ballast Failure**
- **Symptoms:** One or both headlights flickering or not turning on.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the headlight ballast.
- **Cost:** $200 - $500

### Summary Verdict
The 2012 Audi A4 Quattro is a competent luxury sedan with a generally good reliability record. The most frequently cited issues revolve around the 2.0L TFSI engine, specifically concerning the PCV valve, oil consumption, and timing chain. These can be costly to repair if they arise. The automatic and manual transmissions, suspension, and exterior/interior components are typically robust. Electrical gremlins, such as infotainment system glitches and headlight ballast failures, are also noted but are not usually indicative of systemic problems. Regular maintenance and prompt attention to early symptoms are key to ensuring long-term reliability for this model.
