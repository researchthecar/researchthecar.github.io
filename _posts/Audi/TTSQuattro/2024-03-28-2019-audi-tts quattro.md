**2019 Audi TTS Quattro**

The 2019 Audi TTS is a compact sports car that offers a blend of performance and luxury. It comes standard with a 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine that produces 288 horsepower and 280 lb-ft of torque. This engine is paired with a seven-speed dual-clutch automatic transmission and Quattro all-wheel drive. The TTS also offers an optional 3.2-liter V6 engine that produces 360 horsepower and 343 lb-ft of torque, paired with a six-speed manual transmission or a seven-speed dual-clutch automatic transmission.

The TTS has received praise for its sharp handling, powerful engines, and stylish design. However, it has also been criticized for its firm ride, small back seat, and high price.

**Engine**

* **2.0-liter Turbocharged Four-Cylinder**
    * **Turbocharger Lag:** The turbocharger can cause a noticeable delay in power delivery, especially at low RPMs.
        * Solution: Upgrading to a larger turbocharger or replacing the wastegate actuator.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
        * Recalls: None Found
    * **Intake Valve Carbon Buildup:** Over time, carbon can build up on the intake valves, reducing airflow and causing engine performance issues.
        * Solution: Regular intake valve cleaning.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+
        * Recalls: None Found

* **3.2-liter V6**
    * **Timing Chain Tensioner Failure:** The timing chain tensioner can fail, causing the timing chain to jump or break, leading to catastrophic engine damage.
        * Solution: Replacing the timing chain tensioner and related components.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $4,000 - $6,000+
        * Recalls: None Found
    * **Fuel Injector Failure:** Fuel injectors can fail, causing engine misfiring and performance issues.
        * Solution: Replacing the failed fuel injectors.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

* **Seven-Speed Dual-Clutch Automatic Transmission**
    * **Clutch Pack Failure:** The clutch packs can wear prematurely, causing slipping and shuddering during gear changes.
        * Solution: Replacing the clutch pack.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,000+
        * Recalls: None Found
* **Six-Speed Manual Transmission**
    * **No Issues Found**

**Suspension**

* **Front Lower Control Arm Bushings:** The front lower control arm bushings can wear out prematurely, causing clunking noises and reduced handling performance.
        * Solution: Replacing the worn bushings.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Battery Discharge:** The battery can discharge prematurely due to a parasitic drain. The MMI navigation system and Bluetooth module are common culprits.
        * Solution: Finding and fixing the source of the parasitic drain.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Headlight Condensation:** Moisture can build up inside the headlights, causing condensation and reduced visibility.
        * Solution: Replacing the headlights.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $1,500+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Infotainment System Freezes:** The infotainment system can occasionally freeze or malfunction.
        * Solution: Resetting the system or updating the software.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: Free - $200+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2019 Audi TTS Quattro is a high-performance sports car that offers a thrilling driving experience. However, it is not without its issues. The engine and transmission can experience serious problems that can be expensive to fix. The suspension, electrical system, and interior also have some known issues. Overall, the TTS is a reliable vehicle, but potential buyers should be aware of the potential problems that may arise.
