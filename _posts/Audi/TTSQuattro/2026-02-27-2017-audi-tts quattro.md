# 2017 Audi TTS Quattro

### Overview
The 2017 Audi TTS Quattro is a performance-oriented compact sports car that generally offers a high level of build quality and driving dynamics. As a premium vehicle, it aims to balance exhilarating performance with everyday usability. Reliability data for the 2017 model year indicates a strong overall performance, with most owners reporting satisfaction. However, like any performance vehicle, specific areas can experience issues, particularly those related to its turbocharged engine and advanced all-wheel-drive system. This analysis focuses on documented technical issues beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential areas of concern for owners and prospective buyers.

---


## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged TFSI (EA888 Gen3)
**PCV Valve Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Rough idle, check engine light, potential oil leaks, increased oil consumption.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the Positive Crankcase Ventilation (PCV) valve assembly.
- **Cost:** $250 - $500

**Water Pump Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Coolant leaks, overheating, sweet smell from coolant, visible coolant residue.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the water pump and thermostat assembly.
- **Cost:** $600 - $1000


## Transmission

### 6-Speed S tronic (Dual-Clutch Automatic)
**Mechatronics Unit Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Harsh shifting, delayed engagement, transmission warning lights, inability to select gears.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of the mechatronics unit.
- **Cost:** $2000 - $4000

**Clutch Pack Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Jerky acceleration, slipping gears, hesitation during shifts.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the clutch packs.
- **Cost:** $1500 - $3000


## Suspension

### Magnetic Ride Control (Adaptive Dampers)
**Damper Leaks/Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Harsh ride, clunking noises over bumps, reduced handling precision, suspension warning light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of faulty magnetic ride control dampers.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1000 per corner

### Summary Verdict
The 2017 Audi TTS Quattro demonstrates good overall reliability, particularly for its performance segment. The 2.0L turbocharged engine is robust, though potential issues with the PCV valve and water pump should be monitored. The S tronic dual-clutch transmission, while offering rapid shifts, can be susceptible to mechatronics unit and clutch pack wear, representing the most significant potential repair costs. The magnetic ride control suspension, while enhancing performance, can also be a source of expensive repairs if dampers fail. Electrical issues are generally minor, often related to infotainment software. With proactive maintenance and awareness of these specific potential issues, the 2017 TTS Quattro can be a reliable and enjoyable sports car.
