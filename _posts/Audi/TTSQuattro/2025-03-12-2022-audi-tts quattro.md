The 2022 Audi TTS Quattro is a stylish and sporty coupe that offers a compelling blend of performance and luxury. The sole engine option is a 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine producing 288 horsepower and 280 lb-ft of torque. It is paired exclusively with a seven-speed S tronic dual-clutch automatic transmission and Quattro all-wheel drive. Owners generally praise the TTS for its sharp handling, quick acceleration, and upscale interior. Some common complaints include the limited rear passenger space, firm ride (especially on models with the optional sport suspension), and infotainment system complexity.

**Engine**

**2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4 (EA888)**

* **Excessive Oil Consumption**
    * Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported higher than expected oil consumption, requiring more frequent top-ups between oil changes. Symptoms include the low oil warning light illuminating and the need to add oil frequently. This issue may stem from piston ring design or valve stem seal issues.
    * Solution: Oil consumption tests to determine the rate of consumption. If excessive, possible solutions include replacing the piston rings or valve stem seals.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $5,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Coolant Leaks**
    * Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaks can occur from various points in the cooling system, including the water pump, hoses, or radiator. Symptoms include a low coolant level warning, visible coolant under the car, or overheating.
    * Solution: Locate the source of the leak and replace the faulty component.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,500+ depending on the location and severity of the leak.
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**7-Speed S tronic Dual-Clutch Transmission**

* **Hesitation or Jerking During Low-Speed Shifting**
    * Description/Symptoms: Some owners experience hesitation or jerky shifting, particularly during low-speed maneuvers such as parking or creeping in traffic. This can be caused by issues with the clutch packs or Mechatronic unit within the transmission.
    * Solution: Transmission adaptation reset, software update, or replacement of the Mechatronic unit or clutch packs.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $5,000+ depending on the cause.
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Transmission Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Complete loss of function of the transmission. Vehicle will not drive, and is accompanied by numerous warnings on the dashboard.
    * Solution: Complete transmission replacement.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $8,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **MagRide Damper Failure (If Equipped)**
    * Description/Symptoms: Models equipped with the optional MagRide adaptive dampers can experience damper failure. Symptoms include a knocking or clunking noise from the suspension, a bouncy or unstable ride, and error messages on the dash.
    * Solution: Replacement of the affected MagRide damper.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,500+ per damper.
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Infotainment System Freezing/Crashing**
    * Description/Symptoms: The MMI infotainment system can sometimes freeze, crash, or exhibit lag. This can affect navigation, audio, and other vehicle functions.
    * Solution: Software updates, system reset, or in extreme cases, replacement of the MMI unit.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 (software update) - $2,000+ (MMI unit replacement).
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Sensor Malfunctions**
    * Description/Symptoms: Failure of one of the many sensors in the vehicle. This could include the backup sensors, the blind spot monitor, the tire pressure sensor, and so on. Common symptons include warnings appearing on the dash.
    * Solution: Replacement of the failed sensor.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500 per sensor.
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **No Issues Found**

**Interior**

* **Rattling/Squeaking Noises**
    * Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported rattles or squeaks emanating from various points within the interior, such as the dashboard, door panels, or seats.
    * Solution: Locating and addressing the source of the rattle or squeak. This may involve tightening fasteners, adding padding, or replacing components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+ depending on the complexity.
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2022 Audi TTS Quattro, while lauded for its performance and design, can present owners with a range of potential issues. The most concerning relate to engine oil consumption, transmission behavior, and potential MagRide suspension failures. While some issues are relatively minor and inexpensive to address, others like engine or transmission problems can be costly. Electrical glitches with the infotainment system are also not uncommon. Overall, potential buyers should be aware of these potential issues and budget accordingly for potential repairs.
