**Introduction**

The 2011 Audi TTS Quattro is a high-performance sports car powered by a turbocharged 2.0L inline-4 engine. It is available with a 6-speed manual transmission or a 6-speed dual-clutch S tronic automatic transmission. The TTS Quattro delivers 265 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque, enabling it to accelerate from 0-60 mph in 4.9 seconds. It is praised for its agile handling, responsive engine, and luxurious interior. However, it also has some drawbacks, including a firm ride and limited rear-seat space.

### **Engine**

#### **2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4**

**Timing Chain Tensioner Failure**
Description/Symptoms: The timing chain tensioner fails to maintain proper tension, resulting in a rattling or ticking sound from the engine. This can cause engine damage if not addressed promptly.
Solution: Replace the timing chain tensioner.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,000
Recalls: None Found

**Carbon Buildup**
Description/Symptoms: Carbon buildup accumulates on the intake valves, reducing airflow and engine performance. This can lead to sluggish acceleration and increased fuel consumption.
Solution: Remove and clean the intake valves.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800
Recalls: None Found

### **Transmission**

#### **6-Speed Manual**

**No Issues Found**

#### **6-Speed S tronic Dual-Clutch Automatic**

**Mechatronic Unit Malfunction**
Description/Symptoms: The mechatronic unit, which controls the transmission's operation, fails. This can cause the transmission to shift erratically, slip, or not engage at all.
Solution: Replace the mechatronic unit.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,000
Recalls: None Found

**Clutch Pack Failure**
Description/Symptoms: The clutch packs in the transmission wear out prematurely, causing the transmission to slip or shudder. This can lead to transmission damage if not repaired.
Solution: Replace the clutch packs.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,200
Recalls: None Found

### **Suspension**

### **Electrical**

**Electrical Gremlins**
Description/Symptoms: Various electrical components malfunction, causing problems such as flickering lights, inoperative gauges, or battery drain.
Solution: Diagnose and replace the faulty components.
Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the issue
Recalls: None Found

### **Exterior**

### **Interior**

**Door Lock Actuator Failure**
Description/Symptoms: The door lock actuators fail, preventing the doors from locking or unlocking properly.
Solution: Replace the door lock actuators.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $300 per door
Recalls: None Found

**SRS Airbag Light Illumination**
Description/Symptoms: The SRS airbag light remains illuminated due to a faulty airbag control module or wiring harness.
Solution: Diagnose and replace the faulty component.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
Recalls: None Found

### **Summary**

The 2011 Audi TTS Quattro is a capable sports car with several notable issues to consider. The most severe problems include timing chain tensioner failure and mechatronic unit malfunction, which can be expensive to repair. Other issues, such as carbon buildup, clutch pack failure, and electrical gremlins, are less severe but still require attention. It's important to factor in these potential costs when budgeting for ownership of this vehicle.
