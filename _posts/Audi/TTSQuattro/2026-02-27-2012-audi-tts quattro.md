# 2012 Audi TTS Quattro

### Overview
The 2012 Audi TTS Quattro, a performance-oriented variant of the TT, is generally regarded as a reliable vehicle, especially considering its sporty nature. It typically features a potent turbocharged engine and the Quattro all-wheel-drive system, contributing to its dynamic driving experience. While owners can expect a higher level of maintenance compared to a standard commuter car, major mechanical failures are not overwhelmingly common. The focus for this model is on performance, which can sometimes lead to more specialized repair needs. Overall, the TTS Quattro offers a compelling blend of performance and practicality, with a reliability record that aligns with expectations for a premium sports coupe.

## Engine

### 2.0L TFSI (CCTA/CBFA)
**PCV Valve Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Rough idle, stalling, check engine light, oil leaks.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the Positive Crankcase Ventilation (PCV) valve assembly.
- **Cost:** $150 - $300

**Oil Consumption**
- **Symptoms:** Excessive oil usage between changes, blue smoke from exhaust.
- **Solution:** Can be due to worn piston rings or valve stem seals; requires internal engine inspection and potential repair.
- **Cost:** $1,500 - $4,000+


## Transmission

### 6-Speed DSG (S tronic)
**Mechatronics Unit Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Harsh shifting, transmission warning lights, inability to select gears.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of the mechatronics unit.
- **Cost:** $2,000 - $4,000

**Clutch Pack Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Slipping gears, rough engagement, delayed shifts.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the clutch packs.
- **Cost:** $1,500 - $3,000

### Summary Verdict
The 2012 Audi TTS Quattro is a capable performance vehicle with a generally solid reliability record. The primary areas of concern tend to be related to the advanced 6-speed DSG transmission, particularly the mechatronics unit and clutch packs, and potential oil consumption issues with the 2.0L TFSI engine. These are not universal but are documented concerns that owners should be aware of. The manual transmission and other systems like suspension, electrical, exterior, and interior components are typically robust. Regular maintenance, especially fluid changes for the transmission and monitoring oil levels, is crucial for long-term reliability.
