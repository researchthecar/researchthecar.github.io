**Introduction**

The 2019 Audi A4 allroad is a versatile wagon that combines the practicality of an SUV with the sleek design of a car. It is powered by a 2.0L turbocharged four-cylinder engine paired with a seven-speed dual-clutch transmission that produces 252 horsepower and 273 lb-ft of torque. The A4 allroad also comes standard with all-wheel drive, providing enhanced traction in challenging weather conditions. While the A4 allroad receives praise for its refined interior, spacious cargo area, and impressive performance, some owners have reported experiencing issues with certain components.

**Engine**

* **2.0L Turbocharged Four-Cylinder**
    * **Carbon Buildup on Intake Valves:**
        * Description/Symptoms: Reduced engine power, hesitation during acceleration, and potential engine damage if left unchecked.
        * Solution: Requires professional cleaning of the intake valves.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * **Oil Consumption:**
        * Description/Symptoms: Excessive oil consumption, leading to low oil levels and potential engine damage.
        * Solution: Varies based on the severity of the issue, may require replacing piston rings or rebuilding the engine.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $5,000+
    * **Timing Chain Tensioner Failure:**
        * Description/Symptoms: Excessive noise from the engine, particularly at low speeds.
        * Solution: Replace the timing chain tensioner.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
* **Recalls:**
    * NHTSA Recall Number 20V567000: Potential failure of the fuel pump control module, leading to a loss of engine power.

**Transmission**

* **Seven-Speed Dual-Clutch**
    * **Transmission Clutches Slipping:**
        * Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears, loss of power during acceleration, and potential transmission damage.
        * Solution: Replace the worn-out clutches.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,000+
    * **Mechatronic Unit Failure:**
        * Description/Symptoms: Transmission malfunction, inability to shift gears, and potential safety hazards.
        * Solution: Replace the mechatronic unit.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $4,000 - $6,000+
* **Recalls:** None Found

**Suspension**

* **Front Suspension Strut Leak:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Leaking fluid from the front suspension struts, leading to reduced ride quality and potential safety hazards.
    * Solution: Replace the leaking struts.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,200+ per strut

**Electrical**

* **Faulty Headlight Wiring:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Intermittent or complete failure of headlights and taillights.
    * Solution: Repair or replace the faulty wiring harness.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+

**Exterior**

* **Door Handle Sensor Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Unable to unlock the doors using the keyless entry system.
    * Solution: Replace the faulty door handle sensor.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+

**Interior**

* **Electrical Issues with Infotainment System:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Intermittent or complete failure of the MMI infotainment system, including navigation, audio, and climate control functions.
    * Solution: Software updates or replacement of the infotainment module.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
* **Squeaky Sunroof:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Excessive noise coming from the sunroof when it is opened or closed.
    * Solution: Lubricate or replace the sunroof tracks.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+

**Summary**

While the 2019 Audi A4 allroad is a well-regarded vehicle, it has experienced some issues with its engine, transmission, suspension, electrical, and interior components. These issues range in severity and repair costs. Engine-related problems, such as carbon buildup, oil consumption, and timing chain tensioner failure can be costly to fix. Transmission issues, particularly with the dual-clutch transmission, are also common and can significantly impact the driving experience. Suspension and electrical concerns can affect safety and comfort. Interior issues, while less severe, can be annoying and detract from the overall ownership experience.
