# 2020 Audi S7 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2020 Audi S7, buyers can expect a high-performance luxury sport sedan driven by sophisticated powertrain engineering and cutting-edge cabin technology. While owner reports to regulatory bodies are relatively limited, prospective owners should still carefully evaluate complex electrical and fuel systems during a pre-purchase inspection.

---

## Engine

### 2.9L Twin-Turbo V6 Mild Hybrid

The 2.9-liter twin-turbocharged V6 mild-hybrid engine produces approximately 444 horsepower and 443 lb-ft of torque, offering rapid acceleration. Buyers should ensure the complex 48V electrical architecture and starter-generator have been thoroughly inspected.

**Starter-Generator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have reported sudden vehicle shutdowns while driving due to starter-generator failure, leaving the car unable to move or shift into park.
- **Solution:** Requires diagnosis and replacement of the failed starter-generator unit by a qualified technician.

### Fuel Delivery System

Sensing mechanisms ensure accurate fuel delivery tracking to the high-performance direct-injection layout.

**Inaccurate Fuel Level Indication**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have experienced unexpected roadside stranding because the fuel gauge incorrectly displayed a third of a tank remaining while the fuel tank was actually empty.
- **Solution:** Inspect fuel level sensors and apply any relevant manufacturer service updates or component replacements.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Tiptronic Automatic

The responsive 8-speed automatic transmission routes power smoothly to the standard Quattro all-wheel-drive system across all driving conditions. It generally maintains a solid reputation for durability when routine fluid maintenance is observed.

## Electrical

### Electrical and Warning System

Advanced multiplex wiring and integrated electronic modules manage everything from driver assists to powertrain control.

**Multiple System Warning Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may experience sudden cascades of dashboard warning lights, including electrical failure, ABS failure, and start-stop system faults while in motion.
- **Solution:** Diagnostic scans are necessary to isolate faulty modules, software glitches, or failing 48V system components.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V034000: Tires](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Audi&model=S7&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** The rear axle alignment may not have been inspected after the repairs for Recall 21V-295 (42L1) were performed. Rear axle misalignment may cause premature or uneven tire wear, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the rear axle, adjust the alignment as necessary, and replace any prematurely or unevenly worn tires, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V159000: Air Bags](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Audi&model=S7&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** The score line on the passenger air bag may not have been manufactured correctly. An incorrectly manufactured air bag may not deploy properly, and could also eject plastic debris into the passenger compartment, increasing the risk of injury during a crash.
- **What to do:** Audi will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the passenger air bag score line, and repair it as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V295000: Suspension:Rear:Axle:Non-Powered Axle Assembly](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Audi&model=S7&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** A broken lock nut may misalign the rear axle, causing a loss of control and increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the lock nuts and, if necessary, the associated bolts, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V155000: Electrical System:Instrument Panel:Fuel Gauge:Fuel Level Float/Sensor/Sending Unit](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Audi&model=S7&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** An inaccurate fuel reading may cause an engine stall due to lack of fuel, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will install a new sensor in the tank, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V861000: Electrical System](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Audi&model=S7&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** A gateway control module shutdown will suddenly reduce engine power, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will install a protective cover, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V900000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Audi&model=S7&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 111, "Rear Visibility." A rearview image that does not display reduces the driver's view behind the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2020 Audi S7 delivers exhilarating performance and high-end luxury, but its intricate mild-hybrid and electronic systems require diligent maintenance history checks. Prospective buyers should prioritize a professional pre-purchase inspection focusing on the 48-volt electrical components and fuel monitoring systems to ensure trouble-free ownership.
