The 2019 Audi A6 Quattro, a mid-size luxury sedan, generally presents a strong profile for reliability, building on Audi's reputation for engineering quality. This analysis focuses on documented technical issues beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential areas of concern for prospective and current owners.

### Adaptive Air Suspension
**Air Spring Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle may sag on one corner, uneven ride height, "suspension fault" warning light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of leaking air spring or associated lines.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1500 per corner


## Electrical

### Infotainment System
**Software Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Screen freezing, unresponsiveness, connectivity issues (Bluetooth, Apple CarPlay/Android Auto).
- **Solution:** Software updates, system resets, or potential module replacement.
- **Cost:** $150 - $600


### Battery Drain
**Parasitic Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Battery dies unexpectedly, difficulty starting the vehicle.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis to identify the source of the drain (e.g., faulty module, aftermarket accessory) and repair or replacement.
- **Cost:** $200 - $800

### Summary Verdict
The 2019 Audi A6 Quattro demonstrates good overall reliability, with its core powertrain and transmission systems performing commendably. The most frequently cited technical concerns tend to be related to the optional Adaptive Air Suspension, where leaks can occur, and occasional electrical gremlins such as infotainment glitches or battery drain issues. These are not universal problems but are worth noting. For owners, proactive maintenance and addressing warning lights promptly are key. The majority of owners should expect a dependable luxury vehicle experience, with potential repair costs leaning towards the higher end for specialized components like air suspension parts or complex electrical module replacements.
