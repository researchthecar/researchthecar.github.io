# 2018 Audi A6 Quattro Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

Evaluating a 2018 Audi A6 Quattro involves looking at a sophisticated executive sedan known for refined performance and upscale features. While owner feedback for this model year is relatively sparse, prospective buyers should still carefully inspect core mechanicals, electrical systems, and vehicle history records prior to purchase.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged I4

The 2.0L turbocharged inline-four engine produces approximately 252 horsepower and 273 lb-ft of torque, providing a responsive and efficient daily driving experience. This powerplant is generally dependable when maintained with timely oil changes and quality fluids.

### 3.0L Supercharged V6

The 3.0L supercharged V6 engine delivers around 340 horsepower and 325 lb-ft of torque for robust acceleration and confident passing power. It is a robust option in the lineup, though routine checks for cooling system and supercharger maintenance are recommended.

### Fuel and Propulsion Wiring

Electrical and fuel delivery harnesses require standard inspection during routine maintenance to prevent rare localized shorts or wiring degradation.

**Vehicle Fire**
- **Symptoms:** An owner reported an unexpected vehicle fire originating near the rear fuel tank area while the vehicle was parked and turned off.
- **Solution:** Inspect the fuel tank area, fuel lines, and surrounding wiring harness for any signs of damage, leaks, or abnormal wear.

## Transmission

### 7-Speed S-Tronic Dual-Clutch Automatic

The 7-speed dual-clutch automatic transmission delivers rapid, seamless gear changes paired with the Quattro all-wheel-drive system. Smooth operation relies heavily on adhering to strict fluid service intervals.

### 8-Speed Tiptronic Automatic

The 8-speed Tiptronic automatic transmission provides smooth and refined shifting characteristics suited for highway cruising and city commuting alike. It stands out as a durable and reliable gearbox option for this luxury sedan.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V035000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Fuel Injection System:Fuel Rail](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Audi&model=A6&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** A fuel leak in the presence of an ignition source can increase risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Audi has notified owners, and dealers will replace the left and right fuel rail, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V370000: Air Bags:Sensor:Occupant Classification](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Audi&model=A6&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** Stress or wear of the body-sensing mat within the front passenger seat may cause the Passenger Occupant Detection System (PODS) control module to malfunction. If the PODS module malfunctions, the front passenger air bag may not deploy properly in the event of a crash, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Volkswagen will notify owners, and dealers will install a PODS system repair kit, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2018 Audi A6 Quattro offers a compelling blend of luxury, technology, and confident all-wheel-drive performance. Buyers should prioritize a comprehensive pre-purchase inspection by an experienced technician to verify the condition of the powertrain, electronics, and overall mechanical health.
