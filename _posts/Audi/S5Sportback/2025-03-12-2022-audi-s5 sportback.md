Here's a breakdown of common issues and complaints reported by owners of the 2022 Audi S5 Sportback, organized by category and component. The 2022 Audi S5 Sportback is a sleek, sporty four-door coupe known for its engaging performance and stylish design. Its main selling point is its balance of practicality and driving fun. A common complaint is the relatively firm ride. The S5 Sportback comes standard with a turbocharged 3.0-liter V6 engine producing 349 horsepower and 369 lb-ft of torque, paired with an 8-speed automatic transmission. There are no other engine or transmission options available for this model year.

**Engine**

**3.0L Turbocharged V6**

*   **Coolant Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaks originating from the water pump or thermostat housing. Symptoms include low coolant levels, engine overheating, and visible coolant puddles under the car.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the faulty water pump and/or thermostat housing, including new gaskets.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Oil Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks may occur from the valve cover gaskets or the rear main seal. This can lead to oil consumption, oil spots on the garage floor, and a burning oil smell.
    *   Solution: Replace the leaking valve cover gasket or rear main seal.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1000+ depending on the source.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**8-Speed Automatic (ZF 8HP55A)**

*   **Rough Shifting/Hesitation**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported rough or delayed shifting, particularly during downshifts or when accelerating from a stop. This may be related to the transmission's adaptive learning software.
    *   Solution: A software reflash of the transmission control module (TCM) may improve shifting behavior. In some cases, replacement of the valve body might be necessary if the issue persists.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300 for software reflash; $1500 - $3000+ for valve body replacement.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Mechatronic Unit Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The Mechatronic unit is the TCU or Transmission Control Unit. Failure can lead to erratic shifting, inability to shift, or complete transmission failure.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the Mechatronic unit.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $2500 - $4000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Adaptive Damper Issues (if equipped)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: For S5 Sportbacks equipped with the optional adaptive damping suspension, some owners have experienced failure of the dampers, leading to a loss of ride quality and handling. Error messages related to the suspension system may appear.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the faulty damper(s).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1500+ per damper (parts and labor).
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Creaking/Popping Noises**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Creaking or popping noises from the front suspension, especially when turning or going over bumps. This can be due to worn sway bar end links or control arm bushings.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the worn sway bar end links or control arm bushings.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+ per side.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Freezing/Crashing**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The MMI (Multi Media Interface) system can occasionally freeze, crash, or exhibit slow response times. This can affect navigation, audio, and vehicle settings.
    *   Solution: A software update may resolve the issue. If not, the MMI unit itself might need replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300 for software update; $1000 - $2500+ for MMI unit replacement.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Sensor Malfunctions**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Problems with sensors such as the parking sensors, blind spot monitoring, or lane departure warning system. These may trigger error messages or cause the systems to function improperly.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis of the faulty sensor and replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+ per sensor, depending on location and complexity.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Headlight Condensation**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Condensation building up inside the headlight housings, reducing visibility and potentially damaging the bulbs or internal components.
    *   Solution: Replacing the headlight housing or resealing the existing housing. Ensure proper ventilation is present.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1500+ per headlight, depending on replacement vs. resealing.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Sunroof Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Sunroof may not open or close properly, or may leak when closed. This can be due to faulty motors, damaged seals, or debris obstructing the tracks.
    *   Solution: Repair or replacement of the sunroof motor, seals, or tracks.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1200+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Rattling/Vibrations**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rattling or vibrations from the dashboard, door panels, or rear deck area. This can be caused by loose trim pieces or components.
    *   Solution: Locating and securing the source of the rattle or vibration. This may involve adding padding or tightening fasteners.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+ depending on the complexity and labor involved.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Seat Motor Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Power seat motors failing, causing the seat to become stuck in one position or unable to adjust properly.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the faulty seat motor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+ per motor.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2022 Audi S5 Sportback can experience a range of issues, from relatively minor electrical glitches to more significant engine and transmission problems. The severity and cost of these issues vary. Engine coolant and oil leaks, along with transmission rough shifting and potential Mechatronic unit failure, represent some of the more concerning and expensive repairs. Owners should be vigilant about maintenance and address any unusual symptoms promptly to mitigate potential damage. While many issues are relatively minor annoyances, some can escalate into costly repairs if left unaddressed.
