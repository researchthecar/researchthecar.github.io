# 2020 Audi S5 Sportback Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2020 Audi S5 Sportback, buyers are generally drawn to its striking styling, upscale interior, and strong turbocharged performance. However, owner reports submitted to safety regulators indicate that technology glitches can occasionally impact daily drivability and convenience. While major mechanical failures are rarely highlighted in early owner feedback, infotainment stability warrants a close inspection during your used car test drive.

---

## Engine

### 3.0L TFSI V6

This 3.0-liter turbocharged V6 engine produces approximately 349 horsepower and 369 lb-ft of torque, delivering brisk acceleration paired with refined road manners. It is generally robust, though regular maintenance is essential to ensure long-term reliability in daily driving.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Tiptronic Automatic

The conventional 8-speed automatic transmission delivers quick, smooth shifts while effectively managing the high output of the turbocharged V6. It pairs well with the standard Quattro all-wheel-drive system and has not been a focal point for major owner complaints.

## Electrical

### Multi Media Interface (MMI) Infotainment System

The central electronics and infotainment architecture control major cabin functions, but software instability can occasionally disrupt the user experience.

**MMI Infotainment System Constant Rebooting**
- **Symptoms:** The central infotainment screen and digital cluster displays randomly restart every 90 seconds to a few minutes during regular drives. This failure temporarily disables connected media, navigation routes, and hands-free phone calls.
- **Solution:** Owners report that dealerships have struggled to provide a permanent fix, though software updates or module replacements may be attempted depending on specific diagnostic codes.

### Driver Assistance and Camera Systems

Advanced safety features rely heavily on stable software integration to maintain uninterrupted camera and sensor functionality.

**Backup Camera and Sensor Loss During MMI Reboots**
- **Symptoms:** When the infotainment system restarts unexpectedly while reversing, drivers temporarily lose access to the backup camera display and parking proximity sensors. This creates potential safety hazards when navigating tight spaces or backing out of driveways.
- **Solution:** Verify if any applicable manufacturer software patches have been applied to address infotainment stability, and test all camera systems thoroughly during a pre-purchase inspection.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V034000: Tires](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Audi&model=S5&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** The rear axle alignment may not have been inspected after the repairs for Recall 21V-295 (42L1) were performed. Rear axle misalignment may cause premature or uneven tire wear, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the rear axle, adjust the alignment as necessary, and replace any prematurely or unevenly worn tires, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V295000: Suspension:Rear:Axle:Non-Powered Axle Assembly](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Audi&model=S5&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** A broken lock nut may misalign the rear axle, causing a loss of control and increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the lock nuts and, if necessary, the associated bolts, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V874000: Seats](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Audi&model=S5&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** A disabled passenger air bag increases the risk of injury in the event of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the connecting cable and either the heating mat or entire seat cover, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V737000: Exterior Lighting:Tail Lights](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Audi&model=S5&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** The wiring harness for the rear lights could be missing the anti-abrasion protection in the area of the spare wheel well. Without the scuff protection, the wires may become damaged during a rear crash, disabling the doors from being opened from the outside, causing a loss of hazard lights, and increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Audi will notify owners, and dealers will apply scuff protection, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2020 Audi S5 Sportback offers an appealing blend of everyday utility and sports car performance, but prospective buyers should verify that all electronics operate smoothly. Pay close attention to the infotainment system during your test drive to ensure it does not suffer from recurring restarts. Overall, a thorough pre-purchase inspection focusing on software updates and electrical modules will help ensure a trouble-free ownership experience.
