# 2021 Audi SQ5 Sportback Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2021 Audi SQ5 Sportback, buyers are generally drawn to its stylish coupe-like silhouette, upscale interior, and punchy turbocharged performance. However, evaluating its reliability involves looking closely at owner reports covering potential powertrain concerns, electrical glitches, and driver-assistance quirks. While these vehicles offer a compelling mix of luxury and sportiness, being aware of reported component failures can help prospective owners inspect prospective vehicles more thoroughly.

---

## Engine

### 3.0L Turbocharged V6 (EA839)

The 2021 Audi SQ5 Sportback is powered by a 3.0-liter turbocharged V6 engine, producing roughly 349 horsepower and 369 lb-ft of torque. It delivers swift acceleration and strong performance, though buyers should watch for reported coolant pump leaks and internal engine concerns.

**Piston Skirt Defect Risk**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have reported concerns regarding a potential piston skirt defect associated with the EA839 engine architecture, which carries a risk of catastrophic engine failure.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified mechanic inspect the engine for unusual internal noises or blow-by, and verify service history and warranty coverage eligibility.

**Coolant Pump Leak**
- **Symptoms:** Routine service inspections have revealed coolant pump weeping or failure, allowing coolant to migrate where it shouldn't be.
- **Solution:** Inspect the water pump and surrounding coolant lines for signs of leakage during a pre-purchase inspection and replace if necessary.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Tiptronic Automatic Transmission

Power is routed through an 8-speed Tiptronic automatic transmission paired with quattro all-wheel drive, providing quick and smooth gear changes under normal driving conditions.

**Sudden Loss of Motive Power**
- **Symptoms:** In isolated incidents, drivers have experienced a sudden, unexpected disengagement of gears while driving in traffic, causing a complete loss of motive power.
- **Solution:** Check for transmission software updates or diagnostic trouble codes related to internal clutch pack or mechatronic unit operation.

## Electrical

### Comfort Module & Infotainment

The electrical architecture drives luxury features and infotainment but can occasionally exhibit module failures or connectivity glitches.

**Comfort Module (J393) Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Failure of the comfort control module can trigger multiple warning lights on the dashboard—including brake, lighting, and security warnings—and may prevent the vehicle from starting.
- **Solution:** Diagnose the J393 comfort control module and replace or reprogram the unit if internal electrical failure is confirmed.

**MMI and Connectivity Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have noted issues with the Multi Media Interface (MMI) failing to connect properly, including control module errors for the emergency call system.
- **Solution:** Perform software resets or update the MMI system firmware at a dealership or service center.

## Safety

### Advanced Driver Assistance Systems

Driver assistance systems enhance safety and convenience, though occasional sensor false triggers and camera display drops have been reported by owners.

**Unintended Forward Collision Braking**
- **Symptoms:** The pre-sense and automatic emergency braking systems can occasionally trigger false positives while driving at highway speeds, causing sudden jerks and seatbelt pre-tensioning.
- **Solution:** Have a dealer check for sensor recalibrations or software updates addressing pre-sense sensitivity.

**Rear-View Camera Blank Screen**
- **Symptoms:** When shifting into reverse, the infotainment display may occasionally show a blank screen instead of the backup camera feed.
- **Solution:** Inspect camera wiring connections and update or reset the MMI display software.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V606000: Seat Belts:Rear/Other:Retractor](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Audi&model=SQ5&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 208, "Occupant Crash Protection." An unsecured child restraint system can increase the risk of injury during a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect, and replace as necessary, the middle-rear seat belt assemblies, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V034000: Tires](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Audi&model=SQ5&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The rear axle alignment may not have been inspected after the repairs for Recall 21V-295 (42L1) were performed. Rear axle misalignment may cause premature or uneven tire wear, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the rear axle, adjust the alignment as necessary, and replace any prematurely or unevenly worn tires, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V947000: Electrical System:Body Control Module/Bcm](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Audi&model=SQ5&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** A gateway control module shutdown will suddenly reduce engine power, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will install a protective cover on the gateway control module and seal an underbody seam as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V295000: Suspension:Rear:Axle:Non-Powered Axle Assembly](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Audi&model=SQ5&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** A broken lock nut may misalign the rear axle, causing a loss of control and increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the lock nuts and, if necessary, the associated bolts, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V742000: Back Over Prevention:Display Function](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Audi&model=SQ5&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 111, "Rear Visibility." A blank rearview camera display can reduce the driver's rear visibility, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the infotainment unit software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2021 Audi SQ5 Sportback blends engaging luxury performance with everyday practicality, but prospective buyers should approach a used model with eyes wide open. While most electronics, engines, and transmissions operate smoothly, owners have highlighted potential issues ranging from coolant leaks and comfort module failures to sensitive driver-assistance alerts. Conducting a comprehensive pre-purchase inspection focusing on the powertrain and electrical systems will help ensure you find a reliable, well-maintained example.
