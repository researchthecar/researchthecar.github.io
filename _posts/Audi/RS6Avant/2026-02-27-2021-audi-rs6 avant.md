# 2021 Audi RS6 Avant

### Overview
The 2021 Audi RS6 Avant is a high-performance luxury wagon that generally exhibits strong reliability, reflecting its premium engineering and build quality. While its potent powertrain and advanced technology contribute to an exhilarating driving experience, like any complex vehicle, it can encounter specific issues. This analysis focuses on documented technical reliability concerns beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential problem areas for owners and enthusiasts. The RS6 Avant's sophisticated systems, particularly its engine and drivetrain, are designed for performance but require diligent attention to maintain optimal function.

---


## Engine

### 4.0L Twin-Turbo V8 (4M55)
**Oil Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Visible oil spots under the vehicle, burning oil smell, low oil level warnings.
- **Solution:** Inspection and replacement of seals, gaskets, or oil cooler lines.
- **Cost:** $300 - $800

**Turbocharger Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Reduced engine power, whistling noises from the engine bay, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and potential repair or replacement of turbochargers or associated plumbing.
- **Cost:** $2,500 - $6,000+

## Suspension

### Adaptive Air Suspension
**Air Strut Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle sagging on one corner, uneven ride height, warning lights on the dashboard.
- **Solution:** Replacement of leaking air struts or air springs.
- **Cost:** $1,000 - $2,500 per corner


## Electrical

### Infotainment System
**Software Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Screen freezing, unresponsive touch controls, connectivity issues (Bluetooth, Apple CarPlay/Android Auto).
- **Solution:** Software updates or resets, potential module replacement.
- **Cost:** $150 - $700


### 12V Battery
**Premature Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Difficulty starting, electrical systems malfunctioning intermittently, battery warning light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the 12V battery.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600

### Summary Verdict
The 2021 Audi RS6 Avant is a highly capable and generally reliable performance wagon. The primary areas where owners might encounter issues are related to its potent 4.0L twin-turbo V8 engine, specifically oil leaks and potential turbocharger concerns, which can be costly to repair. The adaptive air suspension, while providing excellent ride quality, can also be a source of expense if air struts develop leaks. Electrical gremlins, particularly with the infotainment system's software, are also noted. However, for a vehicle of this complexity and performance level, the overall reliability is commendable, with many components showing no widespread issues. Regular maintenance and prompt attention to any warning signs are crucial for long-term ownership satisfaction.
