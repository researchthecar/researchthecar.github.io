# 2021 Audi S7 Sportback Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

Shopping for a used 2021 Audi S7 Sportback means stepping into a high-performance luxury fastback that blends striking style with twin-turbocharged power. While overall owner complaint volume remains relatively low, prospective buyers should review available vehicle history carefully to ensure factory safety campaigns and technical notices have been properly addressed by an authorized service center.

---

## Engine

### 2.9L Twin-Turbo V6 (444 hp)

The 2.9-liter twin-turbocharged V6 engine generates approximately 444 horsepower and 443 lb-ft of torque, paired with a mild-hybrid system. It delivers rapid acceleration and composed performance for daily driving, though premium maintenance is required to ensure long-term durability.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Tiptronic Automatic Transmission

The 8-speed Tiptronic automatic transmission channels power seamlessly to the Quattro all-wheel-drive system across all trims. It offers quick, refined shifts suited to both relaxed cruising and aggressive driving.

## Electrical

### Electrical System

Owner reports indicate occasional electrical system warnings and software-related concerns that can temporarily affect vehicle operation.

**Dashboard Warning and Limp Mode**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may experience sudden dashboard warnings indicating electrical system and transmission malfunctions, potentially forcing the vehicle into limp mode.
- **Solution:** Have the vehicle thoroughly diagnosed by a qualified technician to check battery health, starter-alternator components, and related control modules.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V606000: Seat Belts:Rear/Other:Retractor](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Audi&model=S7&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 208, "Occupant Crash Protection." An unsecured child restraint system can increase the risk of injury during a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect, and replace as necessary, the middle-rear seat belt assemblies, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V034000: Tires](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Audi&model=S7&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The rear axle alignment may not have been inspected after the repairs for Recall 21V-295 (42L1) were performed. Rear axle misalignment may cause premature or uneven tire wear, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the rear axle, adjust the alignment as necessary, and replace any prematurely or unevenly worn tires, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V159000: Air Bags](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Audi&model=S7&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The score line on the passenger air bag may not have been manufactured correctly. An incorrectly manufactured air bag may not deploy properly, and could also eject plastic debris into the passenger compartment, increasing the risk of injury during a crash.
- **What to do:** Audi will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the passenger air bag score line, and repair it as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V295000: Suspension:Rear:Axle:Non-Powered Axle Assembly](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Audi&model=S7&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** A broken lock nut may misalign the rear axle, causing a loss of control and increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the lock nuts and, if necessary, the associated bolts, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V155000: Electrical System:Instrument Panel:Fuel Gauge:Fuel Level Float/Sensor/Sending Unit](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Audi&model=S7&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** An inaccurate fuel reading may cause an engine stall due to lack of fuel, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will install a new sensor in the tank, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V742000: Back Over Prevention:Display Function](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Audi&model=S7&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 111, "Rear Visibility." A blank rearview camera display can reduce the driver's rear visibility, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the infotainment unit software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V861000: Electrical System](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Audi&model=S7&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** A gateway control module shutdown will suddenly reduce engine power, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will install a protective cover, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V601000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Fluid](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Audi&model=S7&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** Incorrect brake fluid can reduce braking ability and increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the brake fluid reservoir cap and, if necessary, replace it, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2021 Audi S7 Sportback offers exhilarating performance and upscale luxury, though buyers should verify that all recall campaigns and electrical updates have been completed. A pre-purchase inspection by an independent Audi specialist is recommended before finalizing your purchase.
