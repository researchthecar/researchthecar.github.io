Here's a breakdown of common issues and complaints for the 2021 Audi S7 Sportback, formatted to your specifications. The 2021 Audi S7 Sportback is a performance-oriented luxury vehicle known for its sleek design, powerful engine, and comfortable interior. It features a turbocharged 2.9-liter V6 engine producing 444 horsepower and 443 lb-ft of torque, paired with an 8-speed automatic transmission and Quattro all-wheel drive. While the S7 offers a great blend of performance and luxury, some owners have reported certain issues. Common praises include its strong acceleration, stylish design, and high-quality interior. Criticisms often revolve around the infotainment system complexity and occasional reliability concerns.

**Engine**

**2.9L Turbocharged V6**

*   **Turbocharger Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Whining noise from the engine, loss of power, check engine light. Can result in complete turbo failure.
    *   Solution: Turbocharger replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $3,000 - $5,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Oil Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Oil spots under the vehicle, burning oil smell, low oil level warning. Common leak points include valve cover gaskets and rear main seal.
    *   Solution: Replacing the leaking gaskets or seals.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,500+, depending on the location of the leak.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Coolant Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Low coolant level, overheating, sweet smell coming from the engine bay. Leaks can occur at coolant hoses, water pump, or radiator.
    *   Solution: Replace the leaking hoses, water pump, or radiator.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $1,200+, depending on the component.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**8-Speed Automatic (ZF 8HP)**

*   **Harsh Shifting**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Jerky or abrupt shifts between gears, especially at low speeds.
    *   Solution: Software update (TCU reflash) or, in severe cases, valve body replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $2,500
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Hesitation/Delay in Shifting**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Delay when accelerating or shifting gears, particularly when downshifting.
    *   Solution: Software update or valve body replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $2,500
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Air Suspension Problems**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Vehicle sagging on one or more corners, error messages related to the suspension, failure to adjust ride height. Possible leaks in air struts or failure of the air compressor.
    *   Solution: Replacing leaking air struts, air compressor, or associated components.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $3,000+ per corner, depending on the component and labor costs.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Adaptive Damper Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Stiff or uncomfortable ride, failure to change damping settings, error messages related to the adaptive damping system.
    *   Solution: Replacing the affected dampers or sensors.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+ per damper.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Freezing/Crashing**
    *   Description/Symptoms: MMI (Multi Media Interface) screen freezing, system reboots, unresponsive touch controls, Bluetooth connectivity issues.
    *   Solution: Software update or replacement of the MMI unit.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $2,000+ depending on if it needs to be replaced.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Sensor Malfunctions (Parking Sensors, Blind Spot Monitoring)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: False alarms, failure to detect objects, error messages.
    *   Solution: Replacing faulty sensors or wiring.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+ per sensor.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Battery Drain**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Car battery draining quickly, especially when the vehicle is parked for extended periods. Can be caused by a faulty module not shutting down properly.
    *   Solution: Identifying and replacing the parasitic draw.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $1,000+ depending on the component.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Sunroof Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Sunroof failing to open or close properly, unusual noises during operation, water leaks.
    *   Solution: Repair or replacement of sunroof motor, tracks, or seals.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Lighting Problems (LED Headlights/Taillights)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Flickering or complete failure of LED lights.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the headlight or taillight assembly.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,500+ per light.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Rattling/Creaking Noises**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rattling or creaking sounds coming from the dashboard, door panels, or seats.
    *   Solution: Identifying and securing the source of the noise.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+ depending on severity.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2021 Audi S7 Sportback, while offering a compelling combination of performance and luxury, can suffer from a range of issues. Engine problems, particularly oil and coolant leaks, and turbocharger issues, can be expensive to repair. Suspension problems such as air suspension failure can also lead to hefty repair bills. Electrical problems with the infotainment system and sensor malfunctions, although less critical, can be frustrating. The severity of these issues ranges from minor inconveniences to major mechanical failures. Costs for repair can vary significantly, from a few hundred dollars for minor fixes to several thousand for more extensive repairs. Potential buyers should be aware of these common issues and ensure a thorough inspection before purchasing.
