**Introduction:**

The 2021 Audi S7 Sportback is a high-performance luxury hatchback powered by a 3.0L TFSI V6 engine paired with an 8-speed Tiptronic transmission. This combination delivers 444 horsepower and 443 lb-ft of torque. The S7 Sportback offers a blend of style, performance, and luxury, but it has also been plagued by several issues.

**Engine**

**3.0L TFSI V6**

* **Carbon Buildup on Intake Valves:** Over time, carbon deposits can accumulate on the intake valves, restricting airflow and reducing engine performance. Symptoms include decreased power, rough idle, and increased fuel consumption. Cleaning the intake valves typically resolves the issue.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**8-Speed Tiptronic**

* **Shift Hesitation:** Some drivers have reported intermittent hesitation or jerkiness during gear changes. The issue can be caused by worn clutches or hydraulic components. Replacing or repairing the affected components usually addresses the problem.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Adaptive Air Suspension Failure:** The adaptive air suspension system can malfunction, causing the vehicle to sag or ride unevenly. The issue can stem from damaged air shocks, leaking air lines, or a faulty control module. Replacing the affected components typically restores proper suspension functionality.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $5,000+
Recalls: None Found

* **Front Control Arm Failure:** Some S7 Sportbacks have experienced premature failure of the front lower control arms. Symptoms include unusual noises while driving, vibration, and misalignment. Replacing the control arms typically addresses the issue.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain Issue:** A software glitch can cause the vehicle's battery to drain prematurely when parked. Updating the software typically fixes the problem.
Estimated Cost to Fix: Free (software update)
Recalls: NHTSA Campaign Number: 22V-222

* **Infotainment System Malfunction:** The infotainment system can occasionally freeze, glitch, or lose connectivity. Resetting the system or updating the software usually resolves the issue.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 - $500+ (if replacement parts are required)
Recalls: None Found

* **Exterior Lighting Issues:** Some S7 Sportbacks have experienced problems with the exterior lighting, including flickering or premature failure of headlights, taillights, or turn signals. Replacing the affected bulbs or lighting assemblies typically addresses the issue.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+ (per light assembly)
Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Panoramic Sunroof Rattle:** Some vehicles have reported a rattling noise coming from the panoramic sunroof. Adjusting or replacing the sunroof guides or seals typically resolves the issue.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

The 2021 Audi S7 Sportback is a powerful and stylish hatchback, but it has been hampered by several issues. Engine problems, suspension failures, electrical glitches, and interior concerns can be costly to repair. Before purchasing a used S7 Sportback, potential buyers are advised to be aware of these potential issues and factor in the potential costs of repairs.
