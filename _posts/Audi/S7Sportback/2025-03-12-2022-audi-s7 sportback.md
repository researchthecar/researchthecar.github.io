The 2022 Audi S7 Sportback is a sleek and powerful luxury sedan known for its sophisticated design, impressive performance, and advanced technology. It's generally praised for its comfortable ride, spacious interior, and attractive styling. However, some common criticisms include its high price point and the complexity of its infotainment system. The standard and only engine option is a 2.9-liter twin-turbocharged V6 engine that produces 444 horsepower and 443 lb-ft of torque, paired with an 8-speed automatic transmission.

**Engine**

**2.9L Twin-Turbo V6**

*   **Turbocharger Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Whining noise from the engine bay, loss of power, check engine light. Potential damage to the engine if left unaddressed.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the faulty turbocharger(s). It is often recommended to replace both turbos at the same time to ensure balanced performance and prevent future issues.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $3,000 - $6,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**8-Speed Automatic (Tiptronic)**

*   **Harsh Shifting/Hesitation**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Noticeable hard shifts, especially between lower gears. Hesitation or delay when accelerating from a stop or low speeds.
    *   Solution: Software reflash of the transmission control module (TCM) may resolve the issue. If the problem persists, valve body replacement or full transmission replacement may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 (reflash) - $6,000+ (transmission replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Freezing/Crashing**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The MMI (Multi Media Interface) system freezes, becomes unresponsive, or restarts unexpectedly. This can affect navigation, audio, and climate control functions.
    *   Solution: Software update for the MMI system. In some cases, a faulty control module may need to be replaced.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 (software update) - $1,000+ (module replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found
*   **Sensor Malfunctions (Parking Sensors/Driver Assistance)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Erratic behavior or failure of parking sensors, adaptive cruise control, lane keep assist, or other driver assistance features. False warnings or inability to activate the systems.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis to identify the faulty sensor or module. Replacement of the malfunctioning sensor or module.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+ per sensor/module.
    *   Recalls: None Found
*   **Battery Drain**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The vehicle's battery drains quickly, especially when the car is not in use. This can lead to difficulty starting the engine or complete battery failure.
    *   Solution: Identify the source of the parasitic drain. Common causes include faulty control modules, aftermarket accessories, or a failing battery. Replace the faulty component and ensure the battery is properly charged.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $1,000+ depending on the cause
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Rattling/Squeaking Noises**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Annoying rattling or squeaking sounds emanating from the dashboard, door panels, or other interior trim pieces. These noises are often more noticeable on rough roads.
    *   Solution: Identify the source of the noise and secure the loose trim pieces. This may involve adding padding or replacing clips.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+ depending on complexity.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Sunroof Issues (Sticking/Motor Failure)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The sunroof may stick or move erratically when opening or closing. In some cases, the motor may fail completely.
    *   Solution: Lubricate the sunroof tracks and mechanism. If the motor is faulty, it will need to be replaced.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $800+ depending on the issue
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Air Suspension Problems**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The air suspension system may fail to maintain the correct ride height, leading to a sagging appearance or a harsh ride. Faulty air struts or compressor.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis of the air suspension system to identify the faulty component(s). Replacement of the air struts, compressor, or other related parts.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $4,000+ depending on the parts needing replacement.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2022 Audi S7 Sportback, while a desirable vehicle, can present owners with a range of potential issues. Electrical problems, particularly with the infotainment system and sensors, appear to be relatively common and can range from minor annoyances to significant expenses. Engine and transmission issues, while potentially less frequent, can result in substantial repair costs. Suspension problems can also be an expensive problem, especially with air suspension. Potential buyers should be aware of these potential issues and factor them into their purchasing decision, or budget accordingly for future repairs.
