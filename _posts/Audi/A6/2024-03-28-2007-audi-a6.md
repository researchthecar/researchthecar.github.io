**Introduction:**

The 2007 Audi A6 is a mid-size luxury sedan that was praised for its elegant styling, powerful engines, and advanced features. It was available with three engine options: a 3.2-liter V6, a 4.2-liter V8, and the high-performance 4.2-liter V8 found in the S6. Transmission choices included a continuously variable transmission (CVT), a six-speed manual transmission, and a six-speed automatic transmission.

**Engine**

* **3.2-liter V6**
    * **Oil Consumption:** Excessive oil consumption due to worn piston rings or valve seals. Symptoms include low oil levels and blue smoke from the exhaust. Solution: Replace piston rings or valve seals. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
    * **Timing Chain Tensioner Failure:** Premature failure of the timing chain tensioner, leading to skipped timing and engine damage. Symptoms include rattling noises from the engine and loss of power. Solution: Replace the timing chain tensioner. Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,200+
    * **Valve Body Failure:** Failure of the valve body in the CVT transmission. Symptoms include erratic shifting, slipping gears, and transmission noises. Solution: Replace the valve body. Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,500 - $4,000+
    * **CVT Transmission Failure:** Premature failure of the CVT transmission due to overheating or wear. Symptoms include loss of power, slipping gears, and transmission noises. Solution: Replace the CVT transmission. Estimated Cost to Fix: $4,000 - $6,000+
    * **None Found**
* **4.2-liter V8**
    * **Carbon Buildup on Intake Valves:** Carbon buildup on the intake valves, leading to reduced engine performance and fuel economy. Symptoms include rough idling, loss of power, and increased fuel consumption. Solution: Clean or replace the intake valves. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * **Secondary Air Pump Failure:** Failure of the secondary air pump, which helps reduce emissions. Symptoms include a check engine light, rough idling, and reduced fuel economy. Solution: Replace the secondary air pump. Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,200+
    * **Ignition Coil Failure:** Premature failure of the ignition coils. Symptoms include misfires, rough idling, and loss of power. Solution: Replace the ignition coils. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
    * **Oil Filter Housing Leak:** Leak from the oil filter housing, leading to oil leaks. Symptoms include low oil levels and oil accumulation around the oil filter. Solution: Replace the oil filter housing gasket. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
    * **Timing Chain Tensioner Failure:** Premature failure of the timing chain tensioner, leading to skipped timing and engine damage. Symptoms include rattling noises from the engine and loss of power. Solution: Replace the timing chain tensioner. Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,200+
* **4.2-liter V8 S6**
    * **None Found**

**Electrical**

* **Battery Discharge:** Premature discharge of the battery due to parasitic drain from faulty electrical components. Symptoms include a dead battery after a short period of time. Solution: Diagnose and repair the faulty electrical component. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+
* **Electrical Harness Issues:** Faulty electrical harnesses, leading to electrical malfunctions and component failure. Symptoms vary depending on the affected component. Solution: Replace the faulty electrical harness. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+
* **Infotainment System Malfunction:** Issues with the infotainment system, including freezing, lagging, or loss of functionality. Symptoms include a non-responsive touchscreen, distorted sound, or system crashes. Solution: Update software or replace the infotainment system. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+
* **Power Window Failure:** Premature failure of the power window motors or switches. Symptoms include inoperable power windows or windows that move slowly or erratically. Solution: Replace the faulty window motor or switch. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
* **None Found**

**Suspension**

* **Front Control Arm Bushings Wear:** Premature wear of the front control arm bushings. Symptoms include excessive noise when driving over bumps, uneven tire wear, and a lack of handling precision. Solution: Replace the worn bushings. Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800+
* **Rear Air Suspension Failure:** Failure of the air suspension system in the rear. Symptoms include a sagging rear end, reduced ride height, and a lack of handling precision. Solution: Repair or replace the air suspension components. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
* **None Found**

**Exterior**

* **Headlight Condensation:** Moisture buildup inside the headlight housings. Symptoms include fogging up of the headlights, reduced visibility at night, and potential electrical issues. Solution: Replace the headlight housings or install new seals. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600+
* **None Found**

**Interior**

* **Door Panel Rattle:** Rattling or squeaking noises from the door panels. Symptoms include audible noises when driving over uneven roads or opening and closing the doors. Solution: Secure loose components or replace the door panels. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+
* **Sunroof Drain Plugs Clogging:** Clogging of the sunroof drain plugs, leading to water buildup and leaks inside the vehicle. Symptoms include water accumulation in the headliner, musty odors, or electrical issues. Solution: Clear the clogged drain plugs. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
* **None Found**

**Summary:**

The 2007 Audi A6 is a generally reliable vehicle, but it is not without its issues. The most common issues affect the engine, transmission, and electrical systems. The severity and cost of these issues vary, but some, such as the timing chain tensioner failure, can lead to significant repairs and expenses. It is important for owners to be aware of potential issues and have their vehicles regularly serviced and maintained to prevent or mitigate them.
