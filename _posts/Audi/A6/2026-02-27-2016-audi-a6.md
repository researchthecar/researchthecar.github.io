# 2016 Audi A6 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2016 Audi A6 blends luxury, advanced technology, and confident performance. For prospective used-car buyers, understanding its common mechanical patterns—such as cooling system maintenance and occasional electrical or brake sensor notes—is key to planning long-term ownership costs.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged 4-Cylinder

The 2.0L turbocharged inline-4 produces around 252 horsepower, offering a balance of everyday efficiency and responsive acceleration. It is a common powerplant for base and mid-tier trims, requiring standard routine maintenance.

### 3.0L Supercharged V6

The 3.0L supercharged V6 pushes roughly 333 horsepower, delivering swift acceleration paired with Audi's quattro all-wheel-drive system. Buyers should monitor the cooling system closely, as water pump wear is a known point of attention.

**Cooling System and Water Pump Failures**
- **Symptoms:** Owners report coolant leaks, low coolant warnings, and sudden engine overheating resulting from premature water pump or thermostat failures.
- **Solution:** Inspect the water pump and thermostat housing for signs of leakage, and replace failed components immediately to prevent overheating.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

The smooth-shifting 8-speed automatic transmission is paired with front-wheel-drive variants, offering refined gear changes and highway efficiency.

### 7-Speed S-tronic Dual-Clutch Transmission

The 7-speed dual-clutch automatic transmission is paired with quattro all-wheel-drive models, delivering rapid gear shifts and a sporty driving dynamic.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

The braking system provides strong stopping power, though some owners have experienced premature rotor vibration at high speeds.

**Brake Rotor Vibration**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may feel heavy steering wheel or pedal vibrations when applying the brakes at highway speeds.
- **Solution:** Inspect brake rotors for uneven wear or warping, and resurface or replace the brake pads and rotors as necessary.

## Steering

### Electronic Steering System

The electromechanical steering provides crisp feedback, but rare electronic module faults can cause sudden loss of power assist.

**Steering Control Module Failure**
- **Symptoms:** A 'Steering Malfunction' warning message appears on the dashboard, and the steering wheel becomes extremely difficult to turn.
- **Solution:** Have the electronic steering control module or steering column components diagnosed and replaced by a qualified technician.

## Safety

### Forward Collision Avoidance

Advanced safety systems enhance highway driving comfort, but sensor alignment and camera sensitivity require proper calibration.

**Adaptive Cruise and Camera Faults**
- **Symptoms:** Dashboard warnings appear regarding the adaptive cruise control or braking guard, sometimes resulting in unexpected brief brake activation.
- **Solution:** Clean the forward-facing camera and radar sensors, and have the system recalibrated or serviced by a dealer if software codes persist.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V035000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Fuel Injection System:Fuel Rail](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Audi&model=A6&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** A fuel leak in the presence of an ignition source can increase risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Audi has notified owners, and dealers will replace the left and right fuel rail, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V862000: Electrical System:Wiring](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Audi&model=A6&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** The extra resistance can cause the connector to overheat, increasing the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Volkswagen will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the auxiliary heater connector, replacing the auxiliary heater and connector, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V370000: Air Bags:Sensor:Occupant Classification](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Audi&model=A6&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** Stress or wear of the body-sensing mat within the front passenger seat may cause the Passenger Occupant Detection System (PODS) control module to malfunction. If the PODS module malfunctions, the front passenger air bag may not deploy properly in the event of a crash, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Volkswagen will notify owners, and dealers will install a PODS system repair kit, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2016 Audi A6 offers a compelling mix of luxury, advanced tech, and robust powertrain choices. While overall reliability is respectable for a luxury sedan, prospective buyers should carefully inspect the cooling system on V6 models and verify that routine service and recalls have been properly addressed.
