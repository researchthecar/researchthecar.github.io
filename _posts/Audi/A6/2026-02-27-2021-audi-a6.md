# 2021 Audi A6 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2021 Audi A6, buyers will find a luxury sedan that blends sophisticated styling with robust performance options. While owners generally praise its refined ride and advanced technology, some complaints have emerged regarding electrical system glitches, cooling system leaks, and occasional advanced driver-assistance sensitivity. Reviewing these owner reports can help prospective buyers perform targeted inspections before finalizing a purchase.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4

The 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine produces approximately 261 horsepower and 273 lb-ft of torque, providing efficient daily power. It features standard quattro all-wheel drive and generally offers a smooth driving experience for everyday commuters.

### 3.0L Turbocharged V6

The 3.0-liter turbocharged V6 generates around 335 horsepower and 369 lb-ft of torque, delivering brisk acceleration and effortless passing power. Buyers considering this engine should routinely inspect cooling components, as isolated owner complaints note potential leaks around the thermostat housing and oil cooler bracket.

**Coolant Leaks and Overheating**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have reported low coolant warning lights, visible coolant puddles, engine overheating, and in severe cases, potential engine damage or seizure.
- **Solution:** Inspect the thermostat housing, water pump, and oil cooler bracket for signs of leaking; replace compromised components and refill the cooling system.

## Transmission

### 7-Speed S-Tronic Dual-Clutch Automatic

The 7-speed dual-clutch automatic transmission delivers rapid gear shifts and pairs with quattro all-wheel drive across the lineup. It is generally responsive and well-matched to both engine choices, with minimal recurring mechanical complaints reported by owners.

## Electrical

### Electrical System and Alternator

The electrical architecture supports numerous comfort and safety features, though some owners have experienced unexpected warnings and module power loss.

**Electrical System Malfunctions and Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may experience warning lights for electrical system malfunctions, failure of electronic components, and unexpected vehicle stalling while driving.
- **Solution:** Check battery health, wiring harnesses, and charging components; address any open manufacturer campaigns related to electrical modules.

## Exterior

### Sunroof and Structure

Exterior panels and trim are generally durable, though rare reports highlight incidents of spontaneous glass breakage.

**Spontaneous Sunroof Shattering**
- **Symptoms:** The glass sunroof may shatter unexpectedly while driving at highway speeds, accompanied by a loud bang.
- **Solution:** Inspect the glass panel for stress cracks or chips, and replace the sunroof assembly if structural compromise is detected.

## Safety

### Forward Collision Avoidance

Advanced safety features provide added peace of mind, though sensor calibration and environmental interference can occasionally cause unexpected system engagement.

**Pre-Sense False Activation**
- **Symptoms:** The Audi Pre-Sense safety system may unexpectedly trigger hard braking events during heavy precipitation or normal maneuvers.
- **Solution:** Ensure sensor lenses and windshield cameras are clean and free of obstruction; consult a dealer for potential software calibration updates.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V606000: Seat Belts:Rear/Other:Retractor](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Audi&model=A6&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 208, "Occupant Crash Protection." An unsecured child restraint system can increase the risk of injury during a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect, and replace as necessary, the middle-rear seat belt assemblies, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V034000: Tires](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Audi&model=A6&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The rear axle alignment may not have been inspected after the repairs for Recall 21V-295 (42L1) were performed. Rear axle misalignment may cause premature or uneven tire wear, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the rear axle, adjust the alignment as necessary, and replace any prematurely or unevenly worn tires, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V159000: Air Bags](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Audi&model=A6&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The score line on the passenger air bag may not have been manufactured correctly. An incorrectly manufactured air bag may not deploy properly, and could also eject plastic debris into the passenger compartment, increasing the risk of injury during a crash.
- **What to do:** Audi will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the passenger air bag score line, and repair it as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V749000: Electrical System](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Audi&model=A6&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The electrical connector can detach from the door crash sensor in either of the front doors. A detached connector can delay the activation of the restraint systems, including the side air bag, curtain air bag and/or seat-belt pretensioners during a side impact crash, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Audi will notify owners, and dealers will inspect, and as necessary, replace the crash sensors in both front doors, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V295000: Suspension:Rear:Axle:Non-Powered Axle Assembly](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Audi&model=A6&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** A broken lock nut may misalign the rear axle, causing a loss of control and increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the lock nuts and, if necessary, the associated bolts, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V155000: Electrical System:Instrument Panel:Fuel Gauge:Fuel Level Float/Sensor/Sending Unit](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Audi&model=A6&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** An inaccurate fuel reading may cause an engine stall due to lack of fuel, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will install a new sensor in the tank, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V742000: Back Over Prevention:Display Function](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Audi&model=A6&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 111, "Rear Visibility." A blank rearview camera display can reduce the driver's rear visibility, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the infotainment unit software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V861000: Electrical System](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Audi&model=A6&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** A gateway control module shutdown will suddenly reduce engine power, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will install a protective cover, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2021 Audi A6 remains a compelling choice in the luxury sedan segment, offering sophisticated styling, strong powertrain options, and an upscale cabin. While most owners enjoy a trouble-free experience, prospective buyers should carefully inspect the vehicle for cooling system leaks, verify that all electrical recall services have been performed, and test the driver-assistance features during a pre-purchase inspection.
