# 2021 Audi A6

### Overview
The 2021 Audi A6 is a luxury sedan known for its refined interior, advanced technology, and comfortable ride. As a premium vehicle, it generally exhibits strong build quality and reliability. This report focuses on documented technical reliability data, excluding routine maintenance and standard wear and tear, to provide a clear picture of potential concerns for owners and prospective buyers of the 2021 model year. We will examine various aspects including its powertrain options, suspension, electrical systems, and interior/exterior components.

**Air Spring Leaks**

- **Solution:** Replacement of leaking air spring or associated lines.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1500 per corner


## Electrical

### Infotainment System
**Software Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Screen freezing, unresponsiveness, connectivity issues (Bluetooth, Apple CarPlay/Android Auto).
- **Solution:** Software updates, system resets, or potential module replacement.
- **Cost:** $150 - $500


### Battery
**Premature Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Difficulty starting, electrical components malfunctioning, battery warning light.
- **Solution:** Battery testing and replacement, investigation of parasitic draw.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600

### Summary Verdict
The 2021 Audi A6 demonstrates a high level of reliability across most of its core systems, including its engine and transmission options. Owners can generally expect a dependable driving experience. The most notable potential issues are related to the optional Adaptive Air Suspension, which can develop leaks, and occasional software glitches within the infotainment system. Battery drain has also been reported by a small number of owners. These issues, while not pervasive, represent the primary areas where owners might encounter unexpected repair costs. Overall, the A6 is a solid choice for those seeking a luxurious and technologically advanced sedan with a good reliability record, provided they are aware of and prepared for potential costs associated with the aforementioned components.
