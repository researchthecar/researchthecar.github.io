**1996 Audi A6**

The 1996 Audi A6 is a mid-size luxury sedan that offers a premium driving experience with a comfortable interior and powerful performance. The A6 comes standard with a 2.8-liter V6 engine that produces 172 horsepower and 184 lb-ft of torque, paired with a 5-speed manual transmission. Other engine and transmission options include:

* 2.8-liter V6 with a 5-speed automatic transmission (172 hp, 184 lb-ft)
* 2.5-liter TDI diesel engine with a 5-speed manual transmission (145 hp, 233 lb-ft)
* 4.2-liter V8 with a 5-speed automatic transmission (300 hp, 295 lb-ft)

Owners generally praise the A6 for its smooth ride, responsive handling, and refined interior. However, there are a few common issues that have been reported.

**Engine**

**2.8-liter V6 (Manual Transmission)**

* **Timing Belt Tensioner Failure:** The timing belt tensioner can fail, causing the timing belt to slip or break. This can result in catastrophic engine damage.
    * **Solution:** Replace the timing belt and tensioner.
    * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $1,000 - $1,500+
    * **Recalls:** None Found

**2.8-liter V6 (Automatic Transmission)**

* **Transmission Slippage:** The transmission can slip or shudder during gear changes, indicating a problem with the transmission control module or solenoids.
    * **Solution:** Re-flash the transmission control module or replace the solenoids.
    * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $500 - $1,000+
    * **Recalls:** None Found

**4.2-liter V8**

* **Ignition Coil Failure:** One or more ignition coils can fail, causing the engine to misfire or run rough.
    * **Solution:** Replace the faulty ignition coils.
    * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $300 - $500+
    * **Recalls:** None Found

**Transmission**

* **5-Speed Manual Transmission:**
    * **No Issues Found**
* **5-Speed Automatic Transmission:**
    * **Torque Converter Failure:** The torque converter can fail, causing the transmission to slip or shudder.
    * **Solution:** Replace the torque converter.
    * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $1,500 - $2,000+
    * **Recalls:** None Found

**Suspension**

* **Front Control Arm Bushings:** The front control arm bushings can wear out prematurely, causing a clunking or rattling noise when driving over bumps or rough roads.
    * **Solution:** Replace the worn bushings.
    * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $200 - $400+
    * **Recalls:** None Found

**Electrical**

* **Alternator Failure:** The alternator can fail, causing the battery to discharge and the car to lose power.
    * **Solution:** Replace the alternator.
    * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $500 - $700+
    * **Recalls:** None Found

**Exterior**

* **Headlight Fogging:** The headlights can fog up due to a faulty seal.
    * **Solution:** Replace the headlight seal.
    * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $200 - $400+
    * **Recalls:** None Found

**Interior**

* **Sunroof Drain Tube Clogging:** The sunroof drain tubes can become clogged, causing water to leak into the cabin.
    * **Solution:** Clean the drain tubes.
    * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $100 - $200
    * **Recalls:** None Found
* **Cup Holder Inserts:** The cup holder inserts can break easily.
    * **Solution:** Replace the cup holder inserts.
    * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $50 - $100
    * **Recalls:** None Found

**Summary**

Overall, the 1996 Audi A6 is a reliable and enjoyable vehicle. However, some potential issues mentioned above should be considered. The severity and cost of these issues vary, and most can be addressed with reasonable repairs.
