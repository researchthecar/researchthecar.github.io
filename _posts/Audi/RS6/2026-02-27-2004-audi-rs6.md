# 2004 Audi RS6

### Overview
The 2004 Audi RS6, a high-performance variant of the A6, is renowned for its potent twin-turbocharged V8 engine and sophisticated all-wheel-drive system. While offering exhilarating performance, like many complex performance vehicles of its era, it can present specific reliability challenges. Owners should be aware of potential issues related to its advanced powertrain, electronics, and the demands placed on its suspension and braking systems due to its performance orientation. Regular and meticulous maintenance is crucial for mitigating these potential concerns and ensuring the longevity of this enthusiast-focused machine.

---


## Engine

### 4.2L Twin-Turbo V8 (C5)
**Turbocharger Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of power, excessive smoke, whistling noises, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of turbochargers.
- **Cost:** $2000 - $4000 per turbocharger, plus labor.

**PCV System Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Rough idle, oil leaks, increased oil consumption, whistling noises.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the Positive Crankcase Ventilation (PCV) system components.
- **Cost:** $300 - $700

**Timing Belt Replacement**
- **Symptoms:** Not applicable (preventative maintenance).
- **Solution:** Scheduled replacement of timing belt, tensioners, and idler pulleys.
- **Cost:** $1000 - $1800


## Transmission

### 5-Speed Tiptronic Automatic (01E)
**Torque Converter Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Slipping gears, harsh shifting, shuddering at low speeds.
- **Solution:** Rebuild or replacement of the torque converter.
- **Cost:** $1500 - $3000

**Valve Body Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Erratic shifting, transmission warning light, failure to engage gears.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of the transmission valve body.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1500


## Suspension

### Adaptive Air Suspension
**Air Spring Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle sagging on one or more corners, compressor running excessively, 'level control system fault' warning.
- **Solution:** Replacement of leaking air springs or air compressor.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1200 per corner for springs, $600 - $1000 for compressor.

**Control Module Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Suspension not adjusting height, inconsistent damping, warning lights.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the adaptive suspension control module.
- **Cost:** $400 - $800


## Electrical

### Infotainment System
**Navigation System Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Screen freezing, incorrect map display, unit not powering on.
- **Solution:** Software update or replacement of the infotainment head unit.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1500


### Window Regulators
**Motor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Power windows not operating, slow window movement, grinding noises.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the window regulator and motor assembly.
- **Cost:** $200 - $400 per window


## Exterior

### Headlight Seals
**Condensation**
- **Symptoms:** Moisture or fogging inside headlight lenses.
- **Solution:** Resealing headlight units or replacement.
- **Cost:** $100 - $300 for resealing, $400 - $800 per headlight assembly.


## Interior

### Seat Heaters
**Element Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Seat heating not working on one or both sides.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the seat heating elements.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600 per seat


--- 
### Summary Verdict
The 2004 Audi RS6 is a performance icon with a complex mechanical and electrical system. While the core V8 engine is robust when properly maintained, potential owners should budget for the upkeep of its twin-turbochargers, PCV system, and the demanding 5-speed Tiptronic transmission. The adaptive air suspension, while offering excellent ride quality, is a known area for potential costly repairs due to leaks or component failures. Electrical gremlins, particularly with the infotainment system and power accessories, can also arise. Despite these potential issues, with diligent maintenance and prompt attention to any developing problems, the RS6 can remain a thrilling and rewarding vehicle.
