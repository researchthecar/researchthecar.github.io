# 2015 Audi TTS

### Overview
The 2015 Audi TTS, a performance-oriented variant of the TT model, generally offers a strong reliability record, particularly given its sporty nature. It is equipped with a potent turbocharged engine and a sophisticated all-wheel-drive system, contributing to its engaging driving dynamics. While most owners report a positive ownership experience with few major mechanical concerns, like any performance vehicle, proactive maintenance is key. This analysis focuses on documented common issues beyond standard wear and tear, providing insights into potential areas of concern for prospective buyers or current owners.

---


## Engine

### 2.0L TFSI Turbocharged Inline-4
**PCV Valve Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Rough idle, check engine light, potential oil consumption.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the Positive Crankcase Ventilation (PCV) valve assembly.
- **Cost:** $200 - $500

**Oil Consumption**
- **Symptoms:** Low oil level warnings, visible smoke from exhaust, fouled spark plugs.
- **Solution:** Can be related to PCV issues, piston ring wear, or turbocharger seals. Diagnosis is crucial for correct repair.
- **Cost:** $500 - $2000+


## Transmission

### 6-Speed S tronic (Dual-Clutch Automatic)
**Mechatronic Unit Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Harsh shifting, transmission warning lights, inability to select gears.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of the mechatronic unit.
- **Cost:** $1500 - $3000+

**Clutch Pack Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Slipping gears, rough engagement, delayed acceleration.
- **Solution:** Replacement of worn clutch packs.
- **Cost:** $1000 - $2500

### Summary Verdict
The 2015 Audi TTS is a reliable performance coupe, with its 2.0L TFSI engine and S tronic transmission being the primary areas where owners might encounter issues. Common problems like PCV valve failure and oil consumption are manageable with proper diagnosis and repair. The S tronic transmission can be prone to mechatronic unit issues or clutch wear, which can be costly. The manual transmission and standard suspension are generally robust. Electrical and interior components are largely trouble-free, with only minor infotainment glitches reported. Overall, the TTS offers a good balance of performance and dependability, provided regular maintenance is performed and potential issues are addressed promptly.
