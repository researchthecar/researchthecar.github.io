**Introduction:**

The 2014 Audi TTS is a compact sports car known for its sleek design, agile handling, and powerful performance. It is available with two engine options: a 2.0L turbocharged inline-4 and a 3.2L V6. The 2.0L engine produces 265 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque, while the 3.2L engine generates 310 horsepower and 302 lb-ft of torque. Transmission options include a 6-speed manual and a 6-speed S tronic dual-clutch transmission. The vehicle is praised for its spirited performance, responsive steering, and premium interior, but some owners have reported various issues.

**Engine**

**2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4**

* **Carbon Buildup on Intake Valves:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Reduced engine performance, rough idling, and decreased fuel economy.
    * Solution: Intake valve cleaning and replacement if necessary.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,200+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Fuel Injector Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Engine misfires, reduced power, and illuminated check engine light.
    * Solution: Replace faulty fuel injectors.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+ per injector
    * Recalls: None Found

**3.2L V6**

* **Timing Chain Tensioner Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Rattling noise from the engine, reduced power, and potential engine damage.
    * Solution: Replace timing chain tensioner and related components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**6-Speed Manual**

* **Grinding or Difficulty Shifting Gears:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Difficult or impossible to shift gears, accompanied by grinding or crunching noises.
    * Solution: Replace faulty clutch or transmission components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**6-Speed S tronic Dual-Clutch**

* **Clutch Pack Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Slipping or jerking transmission, difficulty shifting, and illuminated gearbox malfunction light.
    * Solution: Replace clutch pack and fluid.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,800 - $2,500+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Mechatronic Unit Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Transmission fails to shift, erratic behavior, and illuminated gearbox malfunction light.
    * Solution: Replace mechatronic unit.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,500 - $4,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Rear Upper Control Arm Bushing Wear:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Excessive tire wear, handling issues, and uneven ride.
    * Solution: Replace worn bushings.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Electrical System Malfunctions:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Random electrical issues such as flickering lights, malfunctioning gauges, or radio problems.
    * Solution: Diagnose and repair electrical system faults.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the issue
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Fog Light Condensation:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Moisture buildup inside fog lights, causing poor visibility.
    * Solution: Replace fog light assemblies.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Navigation Screen Malfunction:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Navigation screen freezes, goes blank, or displays incorrect information.
    * Solution: Replace or repair the navigation unit.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Power Seat Motor Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Power seats fail to adjust, move erratically, or make loud noises.
    * Solution: Replace power seat motor.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2014 Audi TTS is a performance-oriented vehicle that offers a thrilling driving experience. However, some owners have encountered issues related to engine reliability, transmission operation, electrical glitches, and interior malfunctions. The severity and cost of these issues vary depending on the problem encountered. Regular maintenance and prompt repairs are crucial to extend the vehicle's lifespan and minimize the occurrence of major issues.
