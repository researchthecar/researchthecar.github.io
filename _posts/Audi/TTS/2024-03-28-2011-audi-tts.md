**2011 Audi TTS**

The 2011 Audi TTS is a high-performance sports car that offers a thrilling driving experience. It is powered by a 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine that produces 265 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque. The engine is mated to a six-speed manual transmission or an optional six-speed S tronic dual-clutch transmission. The TTS comes standard with quattro all-wheel drive.

People who love the 2011 Audi TTS praise its powerful engine, sharp handling, and stylish design. However, some owners have reported experiencing various issues with their vehicles.

**Engine**

**2.0L Turbocharged Four-Cylinder Engine**

* **Intake Valve Deposits:** Carbon deposits can build up on the intake valves, which can lead to reduced engine performance and increased fuel consumption.
* Symptoms: Engine misfires, hesitation, and decreased power
* Solution: Clean the intake valves.
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
* Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**Six-Speed Manual Transmission**

* **Synchronizer Issues:** The synchronizers can wear out prematurely, which can make it difficult to shift gears.
* Symptoms: Grinding noises when shifting, difficulty engaging gears
* Solution: Replace the synchronizers.
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,200 - $2,000+
* Recalls: None Found

**Six-Speed S tronic Dual-Clutch Transmission**

* **Mechatronic Unit Failure:** The mechatronic unit is a complex electro-hydraulic module that controls the transmission. It can fail, leading to transmission problems.
* Symptoms: Transmission slipping, lurching, or refusing to shift gears
* Solution: Replace the mechatronic unit.
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,500 - $4,000+
* Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Lower Control Arm Bushings:** The lower control arm bushings can wear out, causing the suspension to become loose and noisy.
* Symptoms: Clunking or rattling noises from the front suspension, poor handling
* Solution: Replace the lower control arm bushings.
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
* Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Alternator Failure:** The alternator can fail, which can lead to electrical problems and a dead battery.
* Symptoms: Dimming headlights, flickering interior lights, battery warning light on
* Solution: Replace the alternator.
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $600 - $1,000+
* Recalls: 10V342000

**Exterior**

* **Sunroof Drain Plugs:** The sunroof drain plugs can become clogged, leading to water leaking into the cabin.
* Symptoms: Water stains on the headliner, damp carpeting
* Solution: Clean or replace the sunroof drain plugs.
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+
* Recalls: 10V384000

**Interior**

* **Navigation System Malfunctions:** The navigation system can sometimes malfunction, causing it to freeze or display incorrect information.
* Symptoms: Navigation screen unresponsive, incorrect directions displayed
* Solution: Update the navigation software or replace the navigation system.
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
* Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2011 Audi TTS is a high-performance sports car that offers a thrilling driving experience. However, some owners have reported experiencing various issues with their vehicles. The most common issues include intake valve deposits, synchronizer issues in the manual transmission, mechatronic unit failure in the dual-clutch transmission, and lower control arm bushing wear. The severity and cost of these issues vary, but many of the problems can be fixed for a reasonable price.
