# 2017 Audi TTS

### Overview
The 2017 Audi TTS, a performance-oriented compact sports car, generally exhibits strong reliability, reflecting Audi's engineering prowess. It is equipped with a potent 2.0-liter turbocharged engine paired with a dual-clutch S tronic transmission, offering brisk acceleration and engaging driving dynamics. While the TTS is built with quality components, like any performance vehicle, certain areas may require attention over time. This analysis focuses on documented technical issues beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential concerns for owners and prospective buyers. The overall reliability is considered good, with most owners reporting a satisfying ownership experience.

---


## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged TFSI (EA888 Gen3)
**PCV Valve Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Rough idle, check engine light, potential oil leaks.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the Positive Crankcase Ventilation (PCV) valve assembly.
- **Cost:** $200 - $500

**Oil Consumption (Early Models)**
- **Symptoms:** Increased oil level checks required, blue smoke from exhaust.
- **Solution:** Software updates and in some cases, piston ring replacement. This was more prevalent in earlier generations of this engine but can still be a concern.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1500


## Transmission

### 6-Speed S tronic (Dual-Clutch Automatic)
**Mechatronic Unit Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Harsh shifting, transmission warning lights, inability to select gears.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of the mechatronic unit.
- **Cost:** $1500 - $3000

**Clutch Pack Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Slipping gears, rough engagement, delayed acceleration.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the clutch packs.
- **Cost:** $2000 - $4000


## Suspension

### Magnetic Ride Control (Optional)
**Damper Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Harsh ride, clunking noises, suspension warning light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of faulty magnetic ride control dampers.

### Summary Verdict
The 2017 Audi TTS is a reliable performance car, with its primary concerns revolving around the sophisticated S tronic transmission and potential issues with the optional Magnetic Ride Control suspension. The 2.0L TFSI engine is generally robust, though some owners have reported minor oil consumption or PCV valve issues. Electrical gremlins are infrequent, often related to infotainment software. For the Roadster variant, the convertible top mechanism is a point to consider. Overall, the TTS offers a compelling blend of performance and daily usability, with most potential issues being repairable at a moderate cost, especially when addressed proactively.
