**Introduction**

The 2019 Audi RS5 is a high-performance coupe renowned for its potent engine, sharp handling, and luxurious interior. It is powered by a turbocharged 2.9L V6 engine mated to an 8-speed Tiptronic automatic transmission, producing 444 horsepower and 443 lb-ft of torque. The RS5 has received accolades for its thrilling driving experience but has also faced some issues.

**Engine**

**2.9L V6 Turbocharged**

- **Turbocharger Failure**
    - Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, excessive smoke, unusual noises
    - Solution: Turbocharger replacement
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+
    - Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**8-Speed Tiptronic Automatic**

- **Transmission Fluid Leaks**
    - Description/Symptoms: Fluid leaking from the transmission causing rough shifts or loss of power
    - Solution: Transmission fluid repair kit
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
    - Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

- **No Issues Found**

**Electrical**

- **Battery Drain**
    - Description/Symptoms: Battery drains overnight or after a few days of inactivity
    - Solution: Diagnosis and replacement of faulty component
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600+
    - Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

- **Headlight Condensation**
    - Description/Symptoms: Moisture buildup inside the headlights causing poor visibility
    - Solution: Headlight replacement or repair
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000 per headlight
    - Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

- **Infotainment System Malfunctions**
    - Description/Symptoms: Freezing, unresponsive, or intermittent failure of the infotainment system
    - Solution: Software update or hardware replacement
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500
    - Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2019 Audi RS5 is a desirable performance coupe, but it has experienced some issues. The turbocharger failure and transmission fluid leaks are the most prevalent and can be expensive to repair. However, the interior and suspension issues are less common and typically less costly to address. Overall, the severity and cost of the issues vary, and it is essential to consider them when evaluating potential ownership.
