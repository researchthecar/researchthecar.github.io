Alright, here's a breakdown of common issues and complaints associated with the 2019 Audi RS5, formatted for your website's needs.

The 2019 Audi RS5 is a high-performance coupe and sportback known for its exhilarating performance and sleek design. It comes exclusively with a 2.9-liter twin-turbocharged V6 engine producing 444 horsepower and 443 lb-ft of torque, paired with an eight-speed automatic transmission (Tiptronic). Owners generally praise its quick acceleration, sharp handling, and comfortable interior. Common complaints often revolve around the infotainment system complexity, higher maintenance costs associated with a performance vehicle, and the sometimes-firm ride quality.

**Engine**

**2.9L Twin-Turbo V6**

*   **Turbocharger Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Whining noise from the engine bay, loss of power, check engine light. Premature failure of the turbochargers has been reported in some cases.
    *   Solution: Turbocharger replacement. It's recommended to replace both turbos at the same time.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $3,000 - $6,000+ (depending on shop labor rates and turbo brand)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Coolant Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Low coolant warning light, visible coolant leaks under the vehicle, sweet smell.
    *   Solution: Identify and replace the leaking component (water pump, hoses, thermostat housing, etc.).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,500+ (depending on the source of the leak)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Oil Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Oil spots under the car, burning oil smell, low oil level warnings.
    *   Solution: Identify and repair the leak (valve cover gaskets, oil pan gasket, rear main seal).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $1,000+ (depending on the location and severity)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**8-Speed Automatic (Tiptronic)**

*   **Harsh Shifting**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Abrupt or jerky shifts, especially at lower speeds.
    *   Solution: Transmission software update, fluid flush, or valve body replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $2,500+ (depending on the issue)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Delayed Engagement**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Hesitation or delay when shifting into Drive or Reverse.
    *   Solution: Transmission fluid flush, or valve body replacement
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $2,500+ (depending on the issue)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Adaptive Damper Malfunction (If Equipped)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Ride quality becomes harsh or inconsistent, suspension warning lights illuminate.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the faulty adaptive damper(s).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $2,000+ per damper (including labor).
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Creaking/Popping Noises**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Noises coming from the suspension while driving over bumps or turning.
    *   Solution: Inspection and replacement of worn bushings, ball joints, or control arms.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+ (depending on the worn part)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Glitches**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Freezing, slow response times, random reboots, connectivity issues with Apple CarPlay or Android Auto.
    *   Solution: Software updates, system reset, or replacement of the infotainment unit.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 (software update) - $2,000+ (unit replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Sensor Failures (Parking Sensors, Blind Spot Monitoring)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: False alarms, system malfunctions, error messages.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis and replacement of faulty sensors.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+ per sensor (including labor)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Battery Drain Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Dead battery, difficulty starting the car, electrical systems malfunctioning.
    *   Solution: Battery replacement, parasitic draw diagnosis and repair.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Headlight Issues (LED)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: LED lights flickering, dimming, or failing completely.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the headlight assembly.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+ per headlight
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Sunroof Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Sunroof making popping sounds, not opening or closing properly, leaking
    *   Solution: Track realignment, motor repair, sunroof replacement
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $2,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2019 Audi RS5, while a highly desirable performance vehicle, has some potential issues that buyers should be aware of. The most concerning and potentially costly issues revolve around the engine (turbocharger failure, coolant/oil leaks) and electrical system (infotainment glitches). While these issues may not be universal, a thorough pre-purchase inspection is highly recommended. The transmission can also exhibit some shifting issues, and the suspension may require attention as the car ages, especially if equipped with adaptive dampers. Overall, the severity of potential problems ranges from minor annoyances to significant repairs, with costs varying accordingly.
