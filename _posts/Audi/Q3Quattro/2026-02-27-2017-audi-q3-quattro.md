# 2017 Audi Q3 Quattro Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2017 Audi Q3 Quattro offers upscale styling, a nimble footprint, and standard all-wheel drive, making it an appealing option for a used luxury compact crossover. While many owners enjoy its refined cabin and driving dynamics, prospective buyers should review reported owner complaints covering engine, steering, suspension, and electrical systems to make an informed purchasing decision.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4

The 2.0-liter turbocharged inline-4 engine produces around 200 horsepower and 207 lb-ft of torque, providing adequate power for daily commuting. Buyers should monitor oil levels routinely during pre-purchase inspections, as excessive consumption has been reported by some owners.

**Excessive Oil Consumption**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers report rapid oil loss between changes, triggering warning messages such as EPC, Check Engine, or low oil pressure alerts.
- **Solution:** Check oil levels frequently and have a qualified mechanic inspect piston rings and cylinders for wear or damage.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Tiptronic Automatic

The 6-speed Tiptronic automatic transmission delivers smooth and responsive shifts paired with the Quattro all-wheel-drive system. It generally maintains a solid reputation for durability when routine fluid changes are performed.

## Suspension

### Front Coil Springs

The suspension system provides a comfortable ride quality suited for urban driving, though front coil springs should be inspected for structural integrity during a pre-purchase check.

**Broken Front Coil Springs**
- **Symptoms:** Owners may hear sudden noises or notice unexpected vehicle sagging or handling changes caused by fractured coil springs.
- **Solution:** Inspect front suspension components for cracks or breakage and replace damaged coil springs in pairs.

## Steering

### Steering Assembly

The steering system delivers light and maneuverable handling characteristics, though critical steering component failures should be checked prior to purchase.

**Steering Loss**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have reported unexpected loss of steering control while operating the vehicle.
- **Solution:** Have the steering rack, electronic power steering module, and linkage thoroughly examined by a professional technician.

### Steering Wheel Clockspring

Electrical accessories and cabin features generally function well, but steering column wiring components require careful verification.

**Clockspring Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Inadvertent activation of the horn, loss of steering wheel controls, and disabled driver airbag functionality.
- **Solution:** Replace the faulty clockspring assembly to restore proper electrical continuity to the steering wheel components.

## Exterior

### Rear Window Glass

Exterior body panels and glass components are generally durable, but weather-related stress on heated glass can occasionally cause issues.

**Rear Window Cracking**
- **Symptoms:** A creaking noise followed by unexpected cracking of the rear glass when window defrosters are activated in cold weather.
- **Solution:** Inspect rear window defroster grids and replace the rear windshield glass if stress cracks develop.
