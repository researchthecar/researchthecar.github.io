# 2017 Audi Q3 Quattro

### Overview
The 2017 Audi Q3 Quattro is a compact luxury SUV that generally offers a solid ownership experience, blending premium features with Audi's characteristic build quality. It is known for its refined interior, comfortable ride, and competent all-wheel-drive system. While most owners report satisfaction, like any vehicle, there are specific areas where potential issues have been noted. This analysis focuses on documented technical reliability concerns beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into common problems, their symptoms, potential solutions, and estimated repair costs for various components and powertrain options.

---


## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged I4 (190 hp)
**PCV Valve Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Rough idle, whistling noise from engine bay, check engine light, oil leaks.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the Positive Crankcase Ventilation (PCV) valve assembly.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600

**Oil Consumption**
- **Symptoms:** Low oil level warnings, blue smoke from exhaust, fouled spark plugs.
- **Solution:** Can range from piston ring replacement to valve stem seal replacement, depending on the cause.
- **Cost:** $1,500 - $3,000+

## Transmission

### 6-Speed S tronic (Dual-Clutch Automatic)
**Mechatronic Unit Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Harsh shifting, transmission warning lights, inability to select gears, slipping.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of the mechatronic unit.
- **Cost:** $2,000 - $4,000+

**Clutch Pack Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Jerky acceleration, delayed engagement, shuddering during acceleration.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the clutch packs.
- **Cost:** $1,500 - $3,000

**Software Glitches**

### Summary Verdict
The 2017 Audi Q3 Quattro is a generally reliable compact luxury SUV. The most common concerns revolve around the powertrain, specifically potential oil consumption issues and problems with the S tronic dual-clutch transmission's mechatronic unit or clutch packs. Electrical issues, particularly with the infotainment system's software and the power tailgate, are also noted. While the suspension, exterior, and interior components are typically robust, the climate control system can occasionally suffer from blend door actuator failures. Addressing these potential issues proactively and adhering to maintenance schedules can contribute to a positive ownership experience.
