# 2012 Audi Q5

### Overview
The 2012 Audi Q5 is a compact luxury SUV that generally offers a good balance of performance, comfort, and practicality. It's known for its upscale interior, refined driving dynamics, and available Quattro all-wheel-drive system.  While it presents a premium experience, like any vehicle, it has specific areas where owners have reported issues. This analysis focuses on documented technical reliability concerns beyond routine maintenance, covering its various powertrain options and key vehicle systems. Understanding these potential weak points can help prospective buyers and current owners make informed decisions regarding maintenance and potential repairs.

---


## Engine

### 2.0L TFSI (Turbocharged Direct Injection)
**Oil Consumption**
- **Symptoms:** Excessive oil consumption, potential for oil leaks, fouled spark plugs, and catalytic converter damage if not addressed.
- **Solution:** Piston ring replacement, PCV valve replacement, or other internal engine repairs.
- **Cost:** $1,500 - $3,500

**Timing Chain Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Rattling noise from the engine, check engine light, rough idling, and potential engine failure.
- **Solution:** Timing chain and tensioner replacement.
- **Cost:** $1,000 - $2,500

### Summary Verdict
The 2012 Audi Q5, particularly models with the 2.0L TFSI engine, can be prone to oil consumption and timing chain issues, which can be costly to repair. The S tronic dual-clutch transmission also has a documented concern with its mechatronic unit. Other systems like suspension, exterior, and interior generally hold up well, with only isolated electrical gremlins like water pump failures and infotainment glitches being notable. Prospective buyers should be aware of these potential powertrain and transmission concerns and factor in the possibility of these repairs when considering a purchase.
