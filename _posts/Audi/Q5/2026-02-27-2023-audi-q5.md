# 2023 Audi Q5 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2023 Audi Q5 combines luxury, sharp handling, and modern technology into a popular compact luxury SUV package. While many owners enjoy its refined ride and premium interior, prospective used buyers should review initial owner-reported concerns regarding advanced driver assistance features, electronic parking brakes, and occasional software or electrical glitches.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4 (45 TFSI)

This 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine produces approximately 261 horsepower and 273 lb-ft of torque. It provides brisk daily acceleration and strong overall efficiency for the luxury compact SUV class.

### 2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4 Plug-In Hybrid (55 TFSI e)

This plug-in hybrid powertrain pairs a 2.0-liter turbo-four with an electric motor to generate a combined 362 horsepower and 369 lb-ft of torque. It offers brisk performance alongside electric-only driving capability for short commutes.

## Transmission

### 7-Speed S tronic Dual-Clutch Automatic

This 7-speed dual-clutch automatic transmission routes power smoothly through Audi's Quattro all-wheel-drive system. It delivers rapid shifts, though owners should watch for occasional electronic or mechanical control warnings.

**Transmission Malfunction Warning**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may experience a sudden 'Transmission Malfunction! Safely stop vehicle' warning message on the dash accompanied by an unexpected loss of acceleration while driving at highway speeds.
- **Solution:** Have the vehicle towed to a qualified service center or dealership to diagnose diagnostic trouble codes and inspect internal mechatronics or transmission control modules.

## Suspension

### Factory Tires and Spare

Suspension and wheel assemblies maintain a smooth, composed ride quality across various road surfaces.

**Tread Manufacturing Defects**
- **Symptoms:** Original equipment tires may exhibit premature internal fabric belt remnants or separation in the tread under low mileage conditions.
- **Solution:** Inspect tire tread integrity regularly and replace defective tires under manufacturer or tire warranty coverage if manufacturing flaws are confirmed.

## Brakes

### Electronic Parking Brake

Braking components provide strong stopping power, but electronic parking brake automation glitches have been noted by some owners on sloped surfaces.

**Failure to Disengage on Inclines**
- **Symptoms:** The electronic parking brake may fail to release automatically when shifting from park into gear while parked on an incline, resulting in a brake failure warning light and requiring a tow.
- **Solution:** Have the electronic parking brake actuator and related control modules diagnosed by an authorized service center to clear diagnostic trouble codes and replace faulty components if necessary.

## Electrical

### Infotainment and Digital Dash

Cabin electronics feature high-resolution displays and seamless connectivity, though software bugs can occasionally disrupt screen responsiveness and device pairing.

**Screen and Phone Connectivity Failures**
- **Symptoms:** The digital dashboard or central infotainment display may fail to power on promptly upon ignition, or smartphone integration can repeatedly disconnect during active navigation and emergency calls.
- **Solution:** Perform a system reboot or check for factory software updates to resolve persistent communication bugs within the infotainment head unit.

## Safety

### Forward Collision Avoidance System

Advanced safety systems are designed to protect occupants, though sensor calibration sensitivity can occasionally lead to false activation reports from owners.

**Unintended Emergency Braking**
- **Symptoms:** The automatic emergency braking or collision detection system may trigger abruptly and falsely when navigating normal traffic or turns, causing the vehicle to brake harshly.
- **Solution:** Inspect radar and camera sensors for alignment or obstructions, and consult a dealership for any available software updates governing collision mitigation sensitivity.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V842000: Electrical System:Propulsion System:Charging:Cable/Cord:Onboard](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Audi&model=Q5&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** An overheated outlet or charging cable can increase the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will supply a new 220V/240V compact/portable charging cable with an incorporated temperature sensor, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V251000: Air Bags:Sensor:Occupant Classification:Front Passenger](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Audi&model=Q5&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** A deactivated front passenger air bag will not deploy during a crash, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the passenger seat occupant detection system control module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V898000: Electrical System:Propulsion System:Traction Battery](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Audi&model=Q5&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** A high-voltage battery that overheats increases the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will install advanced diagnostic software as the final remedy, anticipated to be available in the second quarter of 2025. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V080000: Electrical System:Propulsion System:Traction Battery](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Audi&model=Q5&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** A high-voltage battery that overheats increases the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will install advanced diagnostic software , free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V294000: Engine](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Audi&model=Q5&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** The screws can loosen, allowing oil to leak and increasing the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the screws as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2023 Audi Q5 remains a compelling luxury compact SUV choice, offering sophisticated styling, comfortable accommodations, and capable performance. While most vehicle systems operate reliably, buyers should thoroughly inspect electronic parking brake operation, infotainment software stability, and advanced safety sensor responses during a pre-purchase test drive.
