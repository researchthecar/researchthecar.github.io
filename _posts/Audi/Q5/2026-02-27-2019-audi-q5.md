# 2019 Audi Q5 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2019 Audi Q5 combines luxury, refined handling, and advanced technology, making it a popular choice in the used compact luxury SUV market. However, prospective buyers should review recurring owner complaints regarding powertrain performance, electrical glitches, and steering components to ensure a reliable ownership experience.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4

The 2.0-liter turbocharged inline-4 engine produces approximately 248 horsepower and 273 lb-ft of torque, delivering brisk acceleration paired with smooth everyday drivability. While generally robust, a small number of owners have reported premature turbocharger and wastegate actuator wear requiring professional service.

**Turbocharger Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Illuminated check engine light, sudden loss of engine power, and reduced acceleration performance.
- **Solution:** Inspect the turbocharger and wastegate actuator, and replace faulty components as necessary.

## Transmission

### 7-Speed S-tronic Dual-Clutch Automatic

The 7-speed S-tronic dual-clutch automatic transmission comes standard with Quattro all-wheel drive, providing quick, precise gear changes. Overall reliability is generally solid, though rare electronic or mechanical faults can trigger unexpected limp-mode behavior.

**Transmission Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Dashboard warnings indicating catastrophic transmission failure and unexpected shifting into manual third gear.
- **Solution:** Have the transmission control module and internal components diagnosed by an authorized specialist.

## Steering

### Power Steering Rack

The electronic power steering system provides responsive handling characteristic of the brand, but sudden rack failures have been documented by some drivers.

**Power Steering Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Dashboard warning messages stating steering malfunction and sudden loss of power steering assistance while driving.
- **Solution:** Replace the defective power steering rack and calibrate the steering control module.

## Exterior

### Exterior Lighting & Modules

Electrical architecture supports advanced driver assists and lighting, though exterior housing failures and moisture intrusion into comfort modules require vigilance.

**Tail Light Assembly Defect**
- **Symptoms:** Faulty bulb or integrated LED warnings for backup and brake lights located within the rear bumper housing.
- **Solution:** Replace the entire rear light assembly module since individual bulbs cannot be easily serviced.

## Safety

### Seat Belts and Occupant Detection

Safety equipment generally performs well in crash tests, though occasional glitches in passenger detection sensors and seat belt chimes can trigger warnings.

**Seat Belt and Sensor Warnings**
- **Symptoms:** Intermittent seat belt warning chimes and occupancy sensor error codes illuminating the airbag light.
- **Solution:** Inspect seat occupancy sensors and related wiring harnesses, and utilize applicable warranty extensions where available.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V947000: Electrical System:Body Control Module/Bcm](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Audi&model=Q5&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** A gateway control module shutdown will suddenly reduce engine power, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will install a protective cover on the gateway control module and seal an underbody seam as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V416000: Wheels:Hub](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Audi&model=Q5&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** The detached trim may become a road hazard, increasing the risk of crash.
- **What to do:** Volkswagen will notify owners, and dealers will secure the wheel arch trim with an additional screw and nut, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V473000: Air Bags:Frontal:Passenger Side:Inflator Module](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Audi&model=Q5&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** In the event of a crash deploying the passenger's frontal air bag, the instrument panel carrier may break due to incorrect welding. A damaged frontal air bag may not provide adequate protection, increasing the risk of an injury.
- **What to do:** Audi will notify owners, and dealers will replace the instrument panel and the passenger frontal air bag, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V737000: Structure:Body](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Audi&model=Q5&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** The detached trim may become a road hazard, increasing the risk of crash.
- **What to do:** Audi will notify owners, and dealers will replace the four clips securing the forward part of the rear wheel arch trim, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V277000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Foundation Components:Master Cylinder](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Audi&model=Q5&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** A short casting can cause a sudden loss of brakes, increasing the risk of crash.
- **What to do:** Audi has notified owners, and dealers will inspect the brake master cylinder, replacing it as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2019 Audi Q5 remains a compelling luxury compact SUV purchase thanks to its sophisticated interior, confident Quattro handling, and strong performance. Buyers should carefully evaluate service history, particularly concerning potential turbocharger wear, steering rack integrity, and rear lighting functionality, to ensure a trouble-free ownership experience.
