# 2017 Audi Q5

### Overview
The 2017 Audi Q5 is a compact luxury SUV that generally offers a solid ownership experience with a focus on refinement and performance. It's known for its comfortable ride, upscale interior, and capable all-wheel-drive system. While most owners report satisfaction, like any vehicle, there are specific areas where potential issues have been noted. This analysis focuses on documented technical reliability concerns beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into common problems, their symptoms, solutions, and estimated repair costs for various components and powertrains.

---


## Engine

### 2.0L TFSI (Turbocharged Direct Injection)
**Oil Consumption**
- **Symptoms:** Excessive oil burning, low oil level warnings, potential for fouled spark plugs and catalytic converter damage.
- **Solution:** Piston ring replacement, PCV valve replacement, or software updates depending on the cause.
- **Cost:** $1,500 - $3,000

**Timing Chain Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Rattling noise from the front of the engine, especially on startup; check engine light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the timing chain, tensioners, and guides.
- **Cost:** $1,000 - $2,500


### 3.0L TFSI (Supercharged)
**PCV System Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Rough idle, oil leaks, whistling noises, potential for increased oil consumption.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the positive crankcase ventilation (PCV) valve assembly.
- **Cost:** $300 - $700

## Electrical

### Infotainment System
**Software Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Screen freezing, unresponsiveness, Bluetooth connectivity issues, navigation problems.
- **Solution:** Software updates, system resets, or replacement of the MMI unit.
- **Cost:** $200 - $1,500


### Sensors
**Parking Sensor Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** False alerts, sensors not detecting obstacles, error messages on the dashboard.
- **Solution:** Cleaning or replacement of faulty parking sensors.
- **Cost:** $200 - $600

### Summary Verdict
The 2017 Audi Q5 is generally a reliable vehicle, particularly in its transmission and core chassis components. The most frequently cited issues tend to be related to the gasoline engines, specifically the 2.0L TFSI, concerning oil consumption and timing chain wear. Electrical gremlins, primarily with the infotainment system and parking sensors, can also occur. While these issues are documented, they are not universal, and many Q5s operate without significant problems. Proactive maintenance, especially monitoring oil levels for the 2.0L engine, can help mitigate some potential concerns. Overall, the Q5 offers a good balance of luxury, performance, and dependability for its class.
