**Introduction:**
The 2012 Audi Q5 is a compact luxury crossover SUV that offers a comfortable and stylish driving experience. It is available with a variety of engine and transmission options, including the 2.0T inline-4 engine paired with an 8-speed Tiptronic automatic transmission. The 2.0T engine produces 211 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque. Audi Q5 owners appreciate the vehicle's spacious interior, impressive performance, and advanced technology features. However, there have been some reported issues with the 2012 Q5.

**Engine**
**2.0T Inline-4 Engine**
* **Intake Manifold Port Flap Failure:**
  * Description/Symptoms: The intake manifold port flaps can fail, causing a loss of power, rough idling, and illuminated check engine light.
  * Solution: Replacement of the intake manifold.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
  * Recalls: None Found
* **Carbon Buildup on Intake Valves:**
  * Description/Symptoms: Carbon buildup on the intake valves can reduce engine performance and fuel efficiency.
  * Solution: Cleaning of the intake valves.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
  * Recalls: None Found
* **Timing Chain Tensioner Failure:**
  * Description/Symptoms: The timing chain tensioner can fail, causing a rattling noise and potential engine damage.
  * Solution: Replacement of the timing chain tensioner.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
  * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**
**8-Speed Tiptronic Automatic Transmission**
* **Mechatronic Unit Failure:**
  * Description/Symptoms: The mechatronic unit, which controls the transmission, can fail, causing shifting problems, loss of power, and illuminated transmission warning light.
  * Solution: Replacement of the mechatronic unit.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $4,000 - $6,000+
  * Recalls: None Found
* **Torque Converter Shudder:**
  * Description/Symptoms: The torque converter can shudder during acceleration or gear changes.
  * Solution: Replacement of the torque converter.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,500 - $4,000+
  * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**
* **Air Suspension Compressor Failure:**
  * Description/Symptoms: The air suspension compressor can fail, causing the vehicle to sag or lose its ability to adjust ride height.
  * Solution: Replacement of the air suspension compressor.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
  * Recalls: None Found
* **Lower Control Arms Worn:**
  * Description/Symptoms: The lower control arms can become worn, causing a knocking or rattling noise and reduced handling.
  * Solution: Replacement of the lower control arms.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
  * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**
* **Electrical System Issues:**
  * Description/Symptoms: Various electrical issues can occur, including battery drain, flickering lights, and malfunctioning electronic components.
  * Solution: Diagnosis and repair of the specific electrical fault.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+
  * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**
* **Sunroof Leaks:**
  * Description/Symptoms: The sunroof can leak water into the cabin.
  * Solution: Resealing of the sunroof.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
  * Recalls: None Found
* **Paint Flaking:**
  * Description/Symptoms: The paint can flake or peel off the vehicle's body.
  * Solution: Refinishing of the affected areas.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
  * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**
* **Door Lock Buttons Break:**
  * Description/Symptoms: The door lock buttons can break, making it difficult to lock or unlock the doors.
  * Solution: Replacement of the door lock buttons.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
  * Recalls: None Found
* **Rear Seatbelts Bind:**
  * Description/Symptoms: The rear seatbelts can bind when retracted, preventing them from being used properly.
  * Solution: Replacement of the seatbelts.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
  * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**
While the 2012 Audi Q5 offers a premium driving experience, it has been reported to have some significant issues. Engine problems such as intake manifold failures and carbon buildup are relatively common, potentially leading to expensive repairs. Transmission issues, including mechatronic unit failures and torque converter shudder, can also be costly to address. Additionally, suspension problems, electrical faults, and exterior issues can add to the vehicle's maintenance costs. Overall, the severity and cost of these issues vary, but potential buyers should do thorough research and consider a warranty or extended coverage to mitigate the risks.
