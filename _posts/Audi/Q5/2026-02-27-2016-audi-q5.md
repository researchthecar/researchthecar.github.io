# 2016 Audi Q5 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2016 Audi Q5, prospective buyers will find a refined luxury crossover that combines strong performance with a comfortable cabin. However, secondhand owners should be aware of certain recurring mechanical and electrical concerns documented by drivers, particularly regarding steering assist components and fuel system integrity.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4

The 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine produces approximately 220 horsepower and provides a lively driving dynamic well-suited for daily commuting. Buyers should verify regular maintenance intervals and check for any history of fuel system leaks.

**Fuel Pump Flange Leak and Odor**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers notice strong fuel odors inside or outside the vehicle, often accompanied by visible fuel leaks near the rear seat or undercarriage.
- **Solution:** Inspection and replacement of the fuel pump flange assembly by a qualified service center.

**Loss of Motive Power**
- **Symptoms:** Unexpected engine hesitation, surging, or sudden loss of power while driving at highway speeds, sometimes prompting dashboard warning lights.
- **Solution:** Diagnostic scanning for powertrain fault codes and replacement of failing fuel delivery or ignition components.

### 3.0L Supercharged V6

The 3.0-liter supercharged V6 engine generates around 272 horsepower, offering brisk acceleration and a smooth power delivery. This performance option is generally robust, provided cooling system and oil maintenance schedules have been strictly maintained.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Tiptronic Automatic Transmission

The conventional 8-speed automatic transmission pairs smoothly with both engine options, delivering prompt gear shifts and dependable highway cruising. Routine fluid changes are recommended to ensure long-term operational durability.

## Steering

### Electromechanical Steering Rack

Issues related to the power steering module can lead to sudden loss of assist, requiring increased driver effort.

**Power Steering Assist Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Sudden loss of power steering assist while driving, accompanied by an illuminated warning light on the instrument cluster, making the steering wheel exceptionally heavy.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the electromechanical steering rack assembly or associated control modules.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V622000: Electrical System:Wiring](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Audi&model=Q5&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** Contact corrosion within the electrical connector for the auxiliary heater may cause the heater wires to overheat or melt. The melted wires can increase the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Audi will notify owners, and dealers will replace the auxiliary heater and, as necessary, update the software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V002000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Cooling System](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Audi&model=Q5&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** If the coolant pump overheats, it can increase the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Audi will notify owners, and dealers will update the software so that the power supply to the coolant pump is deactivated if the pump becomes blocked with debris, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V001000: Visibility:Sun/Moon Roof Assembly](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Audi&model=Q5&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** If this happens, the corrosion can weaken the canister and the inflator could fracture without air bag deployment, propelling fragments into the passenger compartment, striking and causing serious injury to vehicle occupants. An inflator rupture may result in metal fragments striking the driver or other occupants resulting in serious injury or death.
- **What to do:** Audi will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the inflator and either coat it or replace it, as necessary, after removing the surrounding foam, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V229000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Cooling System](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Audi&model=Q5&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** A blocked pump or a short-circuited pump can increase the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Audi will notify owners, and dealers will replace the pumps, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V298000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Audi&model=Q5&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** A fuel leak in the presence of an ignition source increases the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Audi will notify owners, and dealers will replace the fuel pump flange if cracks are present. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2016 Audi Q5 delivers a compelling mix of luxury, practicality, and engaging driving dynamics. While its powertrain choices are generally solid, prospective buyers should thoroughly inspect the vehicle for potential fuel odors, steering assist responsiveness, and subframe condition prior to purchase.
