# 2021 Audi Q5

### Overview
The 2021 Audi Q5 is a popular compact luxury SUV known for its refined interior, comfortable ride, and strong performance. As a model year that falls within the second generation of the Q5, it benefits from established technology and engineering. Reliability for the 2021 model year is generally considered good, with owners reporting satisfaction with its build quality and powertrain. However, like any vehicle, there are specific areas where potential issues have been noted, primarily related to the infotainment system and some minor electrical gremlins. The Q5 offers a balance of luxury, practicality, and a premium driving experience, making it a competitive choice in its segment.

**Air Leak/Compressor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle sagging on one or more corners, compressor running excessively, error messages related to suspension system.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and repair or replacement of leaking air springs, lines, or compressor.

## Electrical

### Infotainment System
**Software Glitches/Freezing**
- **Symptoms:** Touchscreen unresponsive, system freezing, connectivity issues with Apple CarPlay/Android Auto.
- **Solution:** Software updates, system resets, or potential replacement of head unit.
- **Cost:** $100 - $800


### Battery Drain
**Intermittent Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Battery dying unexpectedly, requiring jump starts, warning lights on the dashboard.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis to identify parasitic draw from modules or accessories, repair or replacement of faulty components.
- **Cost:** $200 - $700

### Summary Verdict
The 2021 Audi Q5 demonstrates a strong overall reliability record, particularly concerning its core mechanical components like the engine and transmission. The 2.0L TFSI engines, both with and without the mild-hybrid system, are robust. The 7-speed S tronic transmission is also generally dependable. Potential concerns are more concentrated in the electronic systems, specifically the infotainment interface which can experience software glitches, and occasional reports of battery drain. For models equipped with the optional adaptive air suspension, issues related to leaks or compressor failure can arise. Despite these minor electrical and optional system caveats, the 2021 Q5 is a reliable luxury SUV with good longevity when properly maintained.
