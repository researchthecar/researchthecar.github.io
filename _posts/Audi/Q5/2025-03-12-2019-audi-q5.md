The 2019 Audi Q5 is a luxury compact SUV known for its comfortable ride, upscale interior, and refined driving experience. The most common engine option is the 2.0-liter turbocharged inline-4, which produces 248 horsepower and 273 lb-ft of torque. It is coupled with a 7-speed S-Tronic dual-clutch automatic transmission. The 2019 Q5 has been generally well-received, with owners praising its build quality, technology features, and overall comfort. Common complaints revolve around the infotainment system complexity and occasional electronic glitches.

**Engine**

**2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4 (EA888 Gen 3)**

*   **Excessive Oil Consumption**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners report excessive oil consumption, requiring frequent top-offs between oil changes. Symptoms include the low oil warning light illuminating and noticeable oil consumption on the dipstick.
    *   Solution: In some cases, dealers have performed oil consumption tests to document the issue. Depending on the severity, solutions range from software updates to piston ring replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $3,000 - $5,000+ (if piston ring replacement is required)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Coolant Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaks can occur from various locations, including the water pump, thermostat housing, or coolant hoses. Symptoms include a sweet smell, visible coolant leaks under the vehicle, and the low coolant warning light illuminating.
    *   Solution: Repair or replacement of the leaking component. This could involve replacing hoses, the water pump, or the thermostat housing.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**7-Speed S-Tronic Dual-Clutch Automatic (DL382)**

*   **Hesitation and Jerky Shifting**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported hesitation during acceleration and jerky shifts, especially at lower speeds. This can be more noticeable in stop-and-go traffic.
    *   Solution: A transmission control unit (TCU) software update may improve shifting performance. In some cases, a more involved repair may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300 (software update), $2,000+ (if mechanical repairs are needed)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

No Issues Found

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Freezing/Crashing**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The MMI infotainment system can occasionally freeze, crash, or become unresponsive. This can affect navigation, audio, and other vehicle functions.
    *   Solution: A software update from the dealer may resolve the issue. In more severe cases, replacement of the MMI unit may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300 (software update), $1,000+ (MMI unit replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Sensor Malfunctions (Parking Sensors, Blind Spot Monitoring)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Occasional malfunctions of the parking sensors or blind spot monitoring system. Symptoms include false alerts or complete system failure.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis to identify the faulty sensor, followed by replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+ per sensor, depending on location and type.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Sunroof Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The sunroof can sometimes develop issues such as difficulty opening or closing, or rattling noises.
    *   Solution: Cleaning and lubricating the sunroof tracks may resolve the issue. More complex repairs could involve replacing the sunroof motor or other components.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200 (cleaning/lubrication), $500 - $1,500+ (motor replacement or other repairs)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Rattling Noises**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported rattling noises emanating from the dashboard or door panels.
    *   Solution: Locating and securing the source of the rattle. This may involve adding felt pads or tightening loose components.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+ depending on severity and complexity.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2019 Audi Q5, while generally reliable, can present some issues. The most significant concerns center around potential engine oil consumption and infotainment system glitches. Coolant leaks, jerky shifting, and sensor malfunctions also appear as recurring issues. While some issues can be resolved with software updates, others may require more involved and costly repairs. Overall, while some of these issues can be serious, they are not universally prevalent, and a thorough pre-purchase inspection is highly recommended to mitigate potential risks.
