# 2020 Audi Q5 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2020 Audi Q5 is a luxury compact SUV that balances refined driving dynamics, a quiet interior, and upscale technology. For used car buyers, however, ownership can come with notable electrical and powertrain complexities that require careful pre-purchase inspection.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4

The standard 2.0-liter turbocharged inline-4 engine produces roughly 248 horsepower and 273 lb-ft of torque, offering lively performance and solid fuel efficiency. While generally robust, buyers should monitor fluid levels routinely, as some owners have reported unexpected oil consumption.

**Excessive Oil Consumption**
- **Symptoms:** The engine oil warning light illuminates unexpectedly, requiring frequent manual top-offs shortly after routine service.
- **Solution:** Monitor oil levels regularly and have a mechanic check for leaks or internal consumption issues.

## Transmission

### 7-Speed S-Tronic Dual-Clutch Automatic

The 7-speed S-Tronic dual-clutch automatic transmission delivers rapid, smooth gear changes paired with Audi's quattro all-wheel-drive system. Prospective buyers should thoroughly test-shift the transmission across all driving modes to ensure smooth engagement without hesitation or warning lights.

**Sudden Power Loss and Transmission Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may experience sudden loss of drive power accompanied by transmission warning messages, severely limiting operability.
- **Solution:** Have the transmission control module and fluid inspected by an Audi specialist; software updates or component replacement may be required.

## Electrical

### Gateway Control Module and Electrical System

The electrical architecture supports numerous advanced safety and convenience features, but intermittent module failures can cause significant drivability disruptions.

**Complete Electrical Shutdown**
- **Symptoms:** Multiple dashboard error messages appear suddenly, followed by a total loss of electrical power and engine shutdown while driving.
- **Solution:** Ensure that any applicable gateway control module campaigns or dealer fixes have been properly performed to prevent moisture intrusion.

## Exterior

### Glass and Sunroof

Exterior panels and glass are generally well-fitted, though rare instances of spontaneous glass breakage require careful inspection of window and sunroof assemblies.

**Spontaneous Glass Shattering**
- **Symptoms:** The sunroof or rear window shatters unexpectedly with a loud boom while driving under normal conditions without impact.
- **Solution:** Inspect glass panels for pre-existing stress cracks and verify proper frame alignment before purchase.

## Safety

### Forward Collision Avoidance & Audi Pre Sense

Advanced driver assistance systems enhance everyday protection, though sensor calibration issues and intermittent camera visibility have been noted by owners.

**Inadvertent Emergency Braking**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle unexpectedly triggers emergency braking and abruptly slows down without an actual obstacle or hazard present.
- **Solution:** Have the sensor alignments and software calibrations inspected by a qualified technician.

**Intermittent Backup Camera Display**
- **Symptoms:** The rearview camera image appears completely blank or distorted when shifting into reverse.
- **Solution:** Inspect the camera wiring harness and display module connections for loose pins or water intrusion.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V034000: Tires](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Audi&model=Q5&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** The rear axle alignment may not have been inspected after the repairs for Recall 21V-295 (42L1) were performed. Rear axle misalignment may cause premature or uneven tire wear, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the rear axle, adjust the alignment as necessary, and replace any prematurely or unevenly worn tires, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V947000: Electrical System:Body Control Module/Bcm](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Audi&model=Q5&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** A gateway control module shutdown will suddenly reduce engine power, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will install a protective cover on the gateway control module and seal an underbody seam as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V295000: Suspension:Rear:Axle:Non-Powered Axle Assembly](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Audi&model=Q5&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** A broken lock nut may misalign the rear axle, causing a loss of control and increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the lock nuts and, if necessary, the associated bolts, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V505000: Seats](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Audi&model=Q5&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** Parts of the front seat frame and backrest adjuster may not have been welded properly. In the event of a rear-end crash, the backrest adjustment could break, possibly reducing the amount of occupant restraint, thus increasing risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Volkswagen will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the front seats, and, as necessary, replace the seatback frames, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V737000: Structure:Body](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Audi&model=Q5&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** The detached trim may become a road hazard, increasing the risk of crash.
- **What to do:** Audi will notify owners, and dealers will replace the four clips securing the forward part of the rear wheel arch trim, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V842000: Electrical System:Propulsion System:Charging:Cable/Cord:Onboard](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Audi&model=Q5&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** An overheated outlet or charging cable can increase the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will supply a new 220V/240V compact/portable charging cable with an incorporated temperature sensor, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2020 Audi Q5 offers a compelling blend of luxury, technology, and engaging performance, making it an attractive choice on the used market. However, prospective buyers should verify that all gateway module and electrical updates have been completed, and secure a comprehensive pre-purchase inspection to guard against unforeseen powertrain and electronic surprises.
