# 2022 Audi e-tron S

### Overview
The 2022 Audi e-tron S is a high-performance electric SUV that generally exhibits strong reliability, reflecting Audi's commitment to quality in its EV offerings. As an electric vehicle, it bypasses many traditional internal combustion engine complexities, leading to fewer mechanical failures in areas like the powertrain. However, like any advanced vehicle, it has specific areas that warrant attention. This analysis focuses on documented issues beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential concerns for owners and prospective buyers. The e-tron S leverages advanced technology, and while this contributes to its performance, it also introduces unique potential failure points, primarily within its sophisticated electrical systems and battery management.

## Suspension

### Adaptive Air Suspension
**Air Suspension Leaks/Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle sagging on one or more corners, uneven ride height, warning lights on the dashboard, loss of adjustable ride height functionality.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and repair or replacement of leaking air springs, lines, or compressor. Software updates may also be required.
- **Cost:** $500 - $2500


## Electrical

### Battery Management System
**Charging Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Inconsistent charging speeds, failure to initiate charging, error messages related to charging port or battery temperature.
- **Solution:** Software updates for the BMS, inspection of charging port and cables, potential battery module diagnostics.
- **Cost:** $150 - $1000


### Infotainment System
**Software Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Screen freezing, unresponsive touch controls, navigation system errors, connectivity issues (Bluetooth, Apple CarPlay/Android Auto).
- **Solution:** Software updates, system resets, potential replacement of infotainment module.
- **Cost:** $100 - $1500

### Summary Verdict
The 2022 Audi e-tron S demonstrates a high level of reliability, particularly in its electric powertrain, which benefits from fewer moving parts compared to internal combustion engines. The primary areas of concern, based on available data, are related to the sophisticated electrical systems, including potential glitches with the infotainment and charging systems, and the adaptive air suspension, which can be prone to leaks or malfunctions over time. While these issues are not universally reported, they represent the most common non-wear-and-tear problems. Overall, the e-tron S offers a robust and technologically advanced ownership experience, with most potential issues being manageable through diagnostics and component replacement, though costs can vary.
