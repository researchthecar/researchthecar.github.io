**2013 Audi TT RS**

The 2013 Audi TT RS is a high-performance sports car that is powered by a turbocharged 2.5-liter inline-five engine that produces 360 horsepower and 343 lb-ft of torque. It is available with a six-speed manual transmission or a seven-speed S tronic dual-clutch automatic transmission. The TT RS is praised for its sharp handling, quick acceleration, and comfortable interior. However, it is also known for its stiff ride and high price.

**Engine**

* **2.5L Inline-Five**
    * **Turbocharger Failure:** Description/Symptoms: The turbocharger fails, causing a loss of power and increased exhaust smoke. Solution: Replace the turbocharger. Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,000+. Recalls: None Found
    * **Timing Chain Tensioner Failure:** Description/Symptoms: The timing chain tensioner fails, causing a rattling noise and a loss of power. Solution: Replace the timing chain tensioner. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000. Recalls: None Found
    * **Intake Manifold Failure:** Description/Symptoms: The intake manifold fails, causing a loss of power and increased fuel consumption. Solution: Replace the intake manifold. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000. Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

* **Six-Speed Manual**
    * **Clutch Failure:** Description/Symptoms: The clutch fails, making it difficult or impossible to shift gears. Solution: Replace the clutch. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000. Recalls: None Found
* **Seven-Speed S Tronic Dual-Clutch Automatic**
    * **Mechatronic Unit Failure:** Description/Symptoms: The mechatronic unit fails, causing the transmission to shift erratically or not at all. Solution: Replace the mechatronic unit. Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,000. Recalls: None Found
    * **Dual-Mass Flywheel Failure:** Description/Symptoms: The dual-mass flywheel fails, causing a rattling noise and vibration. Solution: Replace the dual-mass flywheel. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000. Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **No Issues Found**

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain:** Description/Symptoms: The battery drains prematurely, causing the vehicle to be unable to start. Solution: Replace the battery. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500. Recalls: None Found
* **Alternator Failure:** Description/Symptoms: The alternator fails, causing the battery to not charge. Solution: Replace the alternator. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000. Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Dashboard Rattle:** Description/Symptoms: The dashboard rattles when driving over rough roads. Solution: Inspect the dashboard for loose components and tighten them as necessary. Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 - $200. Recalls: None Found
* **Radio Malfunction:** Description/Symptoms: The radio malfunctions, causing it to lose functionality or produce distorted sound. Solution: Replace the radio. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000. Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2013 Audi TT RS is a high-performance sports car that is known for its sharp handling, quick acceleration, and comfortable interior. However, it is also known for its stiff ride and high price. The vehicle has a number of potential issues, including turbocharger failure, timing chain tensioner failure, intake manifold failure, clutch failure, mechatronic unit failure, dual-mass flywheel failure, battery drain, alternator failure, dashboard rattle, and radio malfunction. The severity of these issues can range from minor to major, and the cost to fix them can range from a few hundred dollars to several thousand dollars.
