## Suspension

### Adaptive Air Suspension
**Air Suspension Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle may sag on one or more corners, uneven ride height, warning lights on the dashboard.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and repair or replacement of leaking air springs, lines, or compressor.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1500


## Electrical

### Infotainment System
**Software Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Screen freezing, unresponsive touch controls, navigation system errors, connectivity issues (Bluetooth, Apple CarPlay/Android Auto).
- **Solution:** Software updates, system resets, or potential module replacement.
- **Cost:** $150 - $700


### Driver Assistance Systems
**Sensor Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** False alerts from adaptive cruise control, lane keeping assist, or parking sensors; system deactivation.
- **Solution:** Cleaning, recalibration, or replacement of faulty sensors (e.g., radar, cameras).
- **Cost:** $300 - $1200

### Summary Verdict
The 2022 Audi A8 Quattro demonstrates strong overall reliability, particularly in its powertrain components. The primary areas where owners might encounter issues are related to the sophisticated electronic systems and the advanced adaptive air suspension. Software glitches within the infotainment and driver assistance systems, while not pervasive, can occur and typically require software updates or recalibration. Air suspension leaks are a known potential concern for vehicles equipped with this technology, often manifesting as a sagging ride height. Despite these points, the A8 Quattro is generally considered a dependable luxury sedan, with most reported issues being manageable and not indicative of systemic failures across the model range.
