**Introduction**

The 2009 Audi A8 Quattro is a luxury sedan powered by a 4.2-liter V8 engine. It produces 350 horsepower and 325 lb-ft of torque. The A8 Quattro comes standard with a 6-speed automatic transmission. It is praised for its comfortable ride, spacious interior, and powerful engine but is often criticized for being expensive to repair.

**Engine**

* **4.2-liter V8**

**Transmission**

* **6-speed Automatic**

**Suspension**

* **No Issues Found**

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain**
Description/Symptoms: The battery drains quickly, even when the vehicle is turned off.
Solution: Replace the faulty battery.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
Recalls: None Found
* **Electrical Shorts**
Description/Symptoms: Intermittent electrical issues, such as flickering lights or power window failures.
Solution: Diagnosis and repair of the affected electrical components.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Headlight Condensation**
Description/Symptoms: Moisture builds up inside the headlight assemblies.
Solution: Replace the affected headlight assemblies.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Climate System Failure**
Description/Symptoms: The climate control system malfunctions, leading to improper heating or cooling.
Solution: Replacement of faulty climate control components.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
Recalls: None Found
* **Navigation System Failure**
Description/Symptoms: The navigation system becomes unresponsive or displays inaccurate information.
Solution: Software update or replacement of the navigation unit.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2009 Audi A8 Quattro is a well-received luxury sedan, but it is known for having some expensive repair issues. Electrical problems and interior component failures are the most common concerns. Overall, the severity and cost of these issues should be considered before purchasing this vehicle.
