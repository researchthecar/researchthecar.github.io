The 2004 Audi A8 Quattro is a luxury sedan known for its advanced technology, comfortable ride, and sophisticated styling. It was offered with a 4.2L V8 engine producing 330 horsepower and 317 lb-ft of torque. The vehicle also came with a six-speed automatic transmission. Owners often praise the A8 for its luxurious interior, powerful engine, and smooth ride. However, some common complaints include high maintenance costs, complex electronics, and the age-related issues that come with owning a sophisticated vehicle.

**Engine**

**4.2L V8**

*   **Timing Belt Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The timing belt is critical for synchronizing the crankshaft and camshaft, and failure can lead to catastrophic engine damage. Symptoms include rough running, ticking noises, or complete engine failure.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the timing belt, water pump, tensioners, and idler pulleys is the recommended service.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Valve Cover Gasket Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks from the valve cover gaskets, causing a burning smell and potentially leading to low oil levels.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the valve cover gaskets.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Oil leaks (general)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks from various places in the engine, such as the front crankshaft seal, rear crankshaft seal, oil pan, oil cooler, etc. These can be a result of age.
    *   Solution: Replacing the leaking seals or gaskets
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $2000+ depending on leak location and repair difficulty
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**Six-Speed Automatic**

*   **Torque Converter Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Shuddering during acceleration, especially at low speeds, and potential stalling.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the torque converter.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Valve Body Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Harsh or erratic shifting, slipping between gears, or failure to shift.
    *   Solution: Valve body replacement or rebuild.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $2,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Mechatronic Unit Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The mechatronic unit is the brain of the transmission and controls shifting functions. A failing mechatronic unit can cause erratic shifting, limp mode, or complete transmission failure.
    *   Solution: Replacement or repair of the mechatronic unit.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Air Suspension Compressor Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The air suspension compressor provides the necessary air pressure to keep the air struts inflated. When the air suspension compressor fails, it can cause the suspension to sag, leading to a harsh ride and reduced handling.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the air suspension compressor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Air Strut Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The air struts can develop leaks over time, causing the vehicle to sit unevenly, particularly overnight. This can also strain the air suspension compressor.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the leaking air struts.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,500+ per strut
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Control Arm Bushing Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Knocking or clunking noises from the suspension, especially when going over bumps.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the control arms or bushings.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+ per control arm
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Battery Drain Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Battery drains overnight or within a few days, often due to a faulty module or component not shutting down properly.
    *   Solution: Diagnostic testing to identify the source of the drain, followed by replacement or repair of the faulty component.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+ depending on the source
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Central Convenience Module Malfunctions**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Issues with door locks, windows, interior lighting, and alarm system due to a failing central convenience module.
    *   Solution: Replacement or reprogramming of the central convenience module.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,200+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Instrument Cluster Problems**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Flickering or dead displays, inaccurate readings, or complete failure of the instrument cluster.
    *   Solution: Repair or replacement of the instrument cluster.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $1,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Infotainment System Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The Multi Media Interface (MMI) system can experience software glitches, screen failures, or complete system shutdown.
    *   Solution: Software update, component repair, or replacement of the MMI unit.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Headlight Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Problems with the adaptive headlights (if equipped) not self-leveling properly or failing to function.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the headlight assembly or repair of the adaptive headlight system components.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+ per headlight
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Sunroof Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The sunroof may leak or fail to open/close properly.
    *   Solution: Replacement of sunroof components or the entire sunroof assembly
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300-$1,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Pixel Loss in MMI Screen**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Pixels fade or disappear on the MMI (Multi Media Interface) screen.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the screen or the entire MMI unit.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Door Lock Actuator Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Doors failing to lock or unlock, requiring manual operation.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the faulty door lock actuator.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500 per door
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

The 2004 Audi A8 Quattro, while offering a luxurious experience, is prone to several age-related issues that can result in high maintenance costs. Engine problems like timing belt failure and oil leaks, transmission issues related to the torque converter and valve body, and air suspension malfunctions are the most significant concerns. Electrical issues affecting the convenience and infotainment systems are also common. Overall, potential buyers should be prepared for potentially costly repairs and maintenance, especially when dealing with the complex air suspension and electronic systems.
