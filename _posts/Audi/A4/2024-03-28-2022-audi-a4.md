**Introduction:**
The 2022 Audi A4 is a luxury sedan renowned for its sleek design, sophisticated interior, and potent performance. It's available with a choice of four engines: a 201-horsepower 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder, a 349-horsepower 3.0-liter turbocharged V6, a 444-horsepower 2.9-liter twin-turbocharged V6, and a 197-horsepower 2.0-liter turbocharged diesel four-cylinder. Transmission options include a seven-speed dual-clutch automatic and an eight-speed automatic. While the A4 is generally well-regarded, it does have some common issues that potential buyers should be aware of.

**Engine**
- **2.0-liter Turbocharged Four-Cylinder**
    - **Oil Consumption:** Excessive oil consumption has been reported, causing low oil levels and potential engine damage.
        - Solution: Engine rebuild or replacement.
        - Estimated Cost to Fix: $3,000 - $6,000+
    - **Timing Chain Tensioner Failure:** The timing chain tensioner can fail prematurely, leading to the timing chain skipping and engine damage.
        - Solution: Timing chain and tensioner replacement.
        - Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
        - Recalls: None Found
- **3.0-liter Turbocharged V6**
    - **Fuel Pump Failure:** The fuel pump can malfunction, leading to engine stalling or difficulty starting.
        - Solution: Fuel pump replacement.
        - Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
    - **Ignition Coil Failure:** The ignition coils can fail, causing misfires and reduced engine performance.
        - Solution: Ignition coil replacement.
        - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800+
        - Recalls: None Found
- **444-horsepower 2.9-liter Twin-Turbocharged V6**
    - No Issues Found

**Transmission**
- **Seven-Speed Dual-Clutch Automatic**
    - **Clunking or Jerking:** Some owners experience clunking or jerking during gear changes, especially at low speeds.
        - Solution: Software update or transmission replacement.
        - Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,500+
        - Recalls: NHTSA ID: 20V375000
- **Eight-Speed Automatic**
    - **Delayed Shifting:** The transmission may delay shifting gears, causing hesitation or difficulty accelerating.
        - Solution: Software update or transmission valve body replacement.
        - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
        - Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**
- **Front Suspension Noise:** Some owners report hearing a rattling or knocking noise from the front suspension, particularly over rough roads.
    - Solution: Inspection and repair of the suspension components.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
- **Rear Suspension Alignment:** The rear suspension may become misaligned over time, leading to uneven tire wear and handling issues.
    - Solution: Suspension alignment.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300+

**Electrical**
- **Battery Drain:** The battery can discharge quickly, even when the vehicle is not in use.
    - Solution: Identifying and fixing the source of the parasitic drain.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the cause
        - Recalls: NHTSA ID: 21V436000
- **ECU Failure:** The engine control unit (ECU) can malfunction, causing various engine problems and decreased performance.
    - Solution: ECU replacement.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,000+
        - Recalls: NHTSA ID: 20V444000

**Exterior**
- **Windshield Leaking:** Some owners experience water leaking into the cabin through the windshield.
    - Solution: Windshield replacement or resealing.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800+
- **Headlight Condensation:** Condensation can form inside the headlights, affecting visibility.
    - Solution: Headlight replacement or ventilation improvement.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+

**Interior**
- **Cracked Dash:** The dashboard can develop cracks over time, especially in vehicles exposed to direct sunlight.
    - Solution: Dashboard replacement.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,000+
- **Sunroof Rattle:** The sunroof can rattle or squeak when closed.
    - Solution: Sunroof track lubrication or repair.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+

**Summary:**
The 2022 Audi A4 is a capable and luxurious vehicle, but it is not without its issues. Potential buyers should be aware of the common problems, especially with the engines and electrical components. The severity and cost of the issues vary, but they can be significant in some cases.
