# 2014 Audi A4

### Overview
The 2014 Audi A4 is a well-regarded compact executive sedan, known for its premium interior, refined driving dynamics, and available all-wheel drive. Across its various engine and transmission configurations, the A4 generally exhibits solid build quality. However, like any vehicle, specific components can experience issues over time. This report focuses on documented technical reliability concerns beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential problem areas for owners and prospective buyers of the 2014 model year.

---


## Engine

### 2.0L TFSI (CCTA/CBFA)
**PCV Valve Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Rough idle, increased oil consumption, check engine light, whistling noise from engine.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the Positive Crankcase Ventilation (PCV) valve assembly.
- **Cost:** $200 - $500

**Oil Consumption**
- **Symptoms:** Excessive oil burning between oil changes, blue smoke from exhaust.
- **Solution:** Can be related to PCV system, piston rings, or valve stem seals. May require engine internal repairs.
- **Cost:** $500 - $2500+

**Timing Chain Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Rattling noise from the front of the engine, especially on startup; check engine light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the timing chain, tensioners, guides, and related seals.
- **Cost:** $1000 - $2500+

### 7-Speed S tronic Dual-Clutch (0B5)
**Mechatronic Unit Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Harsh shifting, transmission warning lights, inability to select gears, slipping.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of the mechatronic unit.
- **Cost:** $1500 - $3000+

**Clutch Pack Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Shuddering during acceleration, rough engagement, slipping.
- **Solution:** Replacement of clutch packs.
- **Cost:** $1000 - $2500+

### Summary Verdict
The 2014 Audi A4 offers a generally reliable ownership experience, particularly with its standard chassis and manual transmission variants. The most common technical concerns tend to center around the 2.0L TFSI engine, specifically issues with the PCV valve, oil consumption, and timing chain components. The S tronic dual-clutch transmission can also present issues with its mechatronic unit and clutch packs. Electrical gremlins, such as infotainment glitches and sensor failures, are not uncommon. While these issues can be costly to repair, they are often manageable with proper diagnosis and maintenance. Overall, the A4 remains a solid choice, but potential buyers should be aware of these specific potential problem areas.
