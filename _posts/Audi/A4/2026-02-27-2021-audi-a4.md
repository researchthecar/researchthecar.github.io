# 2021 Audi A4 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2021 Audi A4 combines refined luxury, sharp handling, and modern technology into a popular compact executive sedan. While generally praised for its driving dynamics and upscale interior, used-car buyers should review potential owner-reported concerns regarding electronics and powertrain behavior.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4 (40 TFSI)

The 40 TFSI produces approximately 201 horsepower and 236 lb-ft of torque, offering a balanced blend of efficiency and daily drivability. This entry-level turbocharged engine is generally dependable when regular maintenance schedules are maintained.

### 2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4 (45 TFSI)

The 45 TFSI upgrades output to roughly 261 horsepower and 273 lb-ft of torque, paired with standard Quattro all-wheel drive for spirited performance. It provides robust acceleration though some owners note occasional low-speed hesitation.

**Powertrain Vibration and Jerking**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have reported vehicle trembling, floor shaking, abnormal engine noises, and jerking during acceleration in lower gears, particularly in stop-and-go traffic.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified technician inspect the transmission software, mounts, and engine performance parameters for anomalies.

## Transmission

### 7-Speed S tronic Dual-Clutch Automatic

The 7-speed dual-clutch automatic transmission delivers rapid, seamless gear shifts across front-wheel drive and Quattro models. Routine fluid changes are vital to preserving its responsive shift quality over high mileage.

## Suspension

### Tires and Wheels

The suspension system delivers a sophisticated ride quality that balances comfort and agility, though factory-equipped tires should be closely inspected during a pre-purchase evaluation.

**Premature Tire Belt Separation**
- **Symptoms:** Original equipment tires may exhibit tread hop or vibrational feedback due to internal belt shifting at relatively low mileage.
- **Solution:** Inspect tire condition and tread wear regularly, and replace tires showing signs of structural degradation or separation.

## Electrical

### Instrument Cluster Display

The electrical architecture supports advanced driver aids and the digital dashboard, though digital screen stability can occasionally require software intervention.

**Virtual Cockpit Failure and Crashing**
- **Symptoms:** The main instrument cluster screen may freeze, display a blank screen with a red warning triangle, or continuously attempt to start up and crash, leaving the driver without a speedometer or fuel gauge.
- **Solution:** Consult an authorized dealer or specialist to check for software updates or module replacements if internal hardware has failed.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V606000: Seat Belts:Rear/Other:Retractor](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Audi&model=A4&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 208, "Occupant Crash Protection." An unsecured child restraint system can increase the risk of injury during a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect, and replace as necessary, the middle-rear seat belt assemblies, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V034000: Tires](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Audi&model=A4&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The rear axle alignment may not have been inspected after the repairs for Recall 21V-295 (42L1) were performed. Rear axle misalignment may cause premature or uneven tire wear, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the rear axle, adjust the alignment as necessary, and replace any prematurely or unevenly worn tires, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V295000: Suspension:Rear:Axle:Non-Powered Axle Assembly](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Audi&model=A4&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** A broken lock nut may misalign the rear axle, causing a loss of control and increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the lock nuts and, if necessary, the associated bolts, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V742000: Back Over Prevention:Display Function](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Audi&model=A4&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 111, "Rear Visibility." A blank rearview camera display can reduce the driver's rear visibility, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the infotainment unit software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2021 Audi A4 remains a compelling choice for a used luxury sedan thanks to its upscale cabin and engaging performance options. Prospective buyers should verify the proper operation of the digital instrument cluster and test drive the vehicle thoroughly to ensure smooth low-speed powertrain engagement.
