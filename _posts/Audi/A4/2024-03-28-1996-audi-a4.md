**Introduction**

The 1996 Audi A4 is a compact executive car that was available in sedan and station wagon body styles. It was offered with a variety of engine and transmission options, including:

* 1.6-liter inline-four engine with 101 horsepower and 103 lb-ft of torque, paired with a 5-speed manual or 4-speed automatic transmission.
* 1.8-liter inline-four engine with 125 horsepower and 122 lb-ft of torque, paired with a 5-speed manual or 4-speed automatic transmission.
* 2.8-liter V6 engine with 172 horsepower and 184 lb-ft of torque, paired with a 5-speed manual or 4-speed automatic transmission.

The A4 was praised for its handling, performance, and interior quality. However, it was also criticized for its high price and lack of standard features.

**Engine**

**1.6-liter inline-four engine**

* **Oil leaks:** The camshaft seals and oil pan gasket can develop leaks, leading to oil loss and potential engine damage.
    * Solution: The camshaft seals and oil pan gasket need to be replaced.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1000
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Timing belt failure:** The timing belt can break prematurely, causing catastrophic engine damage.
    * Solution: The timing belt and related components need to be replaced.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1000 - $1500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**1.8-liter inline-four engine**

* **Intake manifold gasket failure:** The intake manifold gasket can fail, causing vacuum leaks and engine performance problems.
    * Solution: The intake manifold gasket needs to be replaced.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Coolant leaks:** The water pump or radiator can develop leaks, leading to coolant loss and overheating.
    * Solution: The water pump or radiator needs to be replaced.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1000
    * Recalls: None Found

**2.8-liter V6 engine**

* **Valve cover gasket leaks:** The valve cover gaskets can develop leaks, causing oil leaks and potential engine damage.
    * Solution: The valve cover gaskets need to be replaced.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Mass airflow sensor failure:** The mass airflow sensor can fail, causing engine performance problems and reduced fuel economy.
    * Solution: The mass airflow sensor needs to be replaced.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**5-speed manual transmission**

* **Synchronizer wear:** The synchronizers in the transmission can wear out, making it difficult to shift gears.
    * Solution: The transmission needs to be rebuilt or replaced.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1500 - $2500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**4-speed automatic transmission**

* **Shift solenoid failure:** The shift solenoids in the transmission can fail, causing shifting problems.
    * Solution: The shift solenoids need to be replaced.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1000
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Torque converter failure:** The torque converter can fail, causing transmission slippage and loss of power.
    * Solution: The torque converter needs to be replaced.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1000 - $1500+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Transmission fluid leaks:** The transmission pan gasket or other seals can develop leaks, leading to fluid loss and transmission damage.
    * Solution: The transmission pan gasket or other seals need to be replaced.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Strut mounts:** The strut mounts can wear out, causing rattling noises and reduced handling.
    * Solution: The strut mounts need to be replaced.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Control arm bushings:** The control arm bushings can wear out, causing clunking noises and reduced handling.
    * Solution: The control arm bushings need to be replaced.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Alternator failure:** The alternator can fail, causing battery drain and loss of electrical power.
    * Solution: The alternator needs to be replaced.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1000
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Battery drain:** The battery can drain prematurely due to faulty electrical components or a parasitic draw.
    * Solution: The faulty electrical components need to be identified and repaired or the parasitic draw needs to be eliminated.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Door lock actuators:** The door lock actuators can fail, causing the doors to lock or unlock unexpectedly.
    * Solution: The door lock actuators need to be replaced.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Climate control issues:** The climate control system can malfunction, causing inconsistent temperatures or lack of airflow.
    * Solution: The climate control system components need to be diagnosed and repaired.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Sunroof leaks:** The sunroof can develop leaks, causing water damage to the interior.
    * Solution: The sunroof seals need to be replaced or the sunroof drain tubes need to be cleaned.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 1996 Audi A4 is a reliable and well-rounded vehicle. However, like any car, it can experience certain issues over time. The most common problems are related to the engine, transmission, and electrical system. These issues can be costly to repair, but they are generally not major or life-threatening. Overall, the A4 is a good choice for a compact executive car, but buyers should be aware of its potential issues before making a purchase.
