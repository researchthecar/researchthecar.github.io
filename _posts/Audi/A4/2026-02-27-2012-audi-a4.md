# 2012 Audi A4

### Overview
The 2012 Audi A4 is a well-regarded compact executive car, known for its refined interior, comfortable ride, and engaging driving dynamics. It offers a range of powertrain options, including efficient turbocharged gasoline engines and a capable all-wheel-drive system (Quattro). While generally reliable, like any vehicle, it has specific areas that have seen more frequent reports of issues. This analysis focuses on documented technical problems beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential concerns for owners and prospective buyers of the 2012 model year.

---


## Engine

### 2.0L TFSI (CCTA/CBFA)
**Oil Consumption**
- **Symptoms:** Excessive oil consumption, potential for oil leaks, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Piston ring replacement, PCV valve replacement, or updated engine components.
- **Cost:** $1,500 - $3,000

**Timing Chain Tensioner Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Rattling noise from the engine, especially on startup; potential for catastrophic engine failure if not addressed.
- **Solution:** Replacement of timing chain, tensioners, guides, and related components.
- **Cost:** $1,200 - $2,500

### 7-Speed S tronic (DL501) - AWD
**Mechatronic Unit Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Harsh shifting, transmission warning lights, loss of drive.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of the mechatronic unit.
- **Cost:** $2,000 - $4,000

**Clutch Pack Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Slipping gears, rough engagement, shuddering during acceleration.
- **Solution:** Replacement of clutch packs.
- **Cost:** $1,800 - $3,500

### Summary Verdict
The 2012 Audi A4 presents a generally reliable ownership experience, particularly with its V6 engine and manual transmission variants. However, the popular 2.0L TFSI engine has known issues with oil consumption and timing chain tensioners that require attention and can be costly to repair. The S tronic dual-clutch transmission also has documented problems with its mechatronic unit and clutch packs. Electrical gremlins, such as infotainment system glitches and power window issues, are also reported. Prospective buyers should be aware of these potential engine and transmission concerns and factor in possible repair costs, especially for higher-mileage vehicles.
