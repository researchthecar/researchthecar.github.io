Here's a breakdown of common issues and complaints associated with the 2022 Audi S8, formatted for your website.

The 2022 Audi S8 is a high-performance luxury sedan, known for its powerful engine, comfortable ride, and advanced technology. The only engine option is a 4.0-liter twin-turbocharged V8 producing 563 horsepower and 590 lb-ft of torque. It is paired with an 8-speed automatic transmission. Many owners praise the S8's blend of performance and comfort. Dislikes often center around the high price point and the complexity of the infotainment system, which can sometimes be finicky.

**Engine**

**4.0L Twin-Turbo V8**

*   **Turbocharger Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Whining noise from the engine bay, loss of power, check engine light. Can be caused by oil starvation or bearing failure.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the affected turbocharger(s). Inspection of oil lines for blockage.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $3,000 - $6,000+ (depending on if one or both turbos need replacement, and labor rates)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Oil Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Oil drips under the car, burning oil smell, low oil level warning light. Often originates from valve cover gaskets, timing chain cover, or rear main seal.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the leaking gasket or seal. Thorough cleaning of affected areas.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+ (depending on the location of the leak and complexity of the repair)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**8-Speed Automatic (ZF 8HP)**

*   **Rough Shifting/Hesitation**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Jerky shifts, hesitation when accelerating, particularly between certain gears (e.g., 2nd to 3rd or 5th to 6th).
    *   Solution: Transmission fluid flush and filter replacement. In some cases, a software update may resolve the issue. If the problem persists, valve body replacement or transmission rebuild may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 (fluid flush) - $5,000+ (transmission rebuild)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Torque Converter Shudder**
    *   Description/Symptoms: A vibrating or shuddering sensation, especially at low speeds or under light load. May feel like driving over rumble strips.
    *   Solution: Torque converter replacement.  In some cases, a specialized transmission fluid additive may temporarily alleviate the symptoms.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Air Suspension Compressor Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Vehicle sitting low, especially on one side. "Suspension Fault" warning light. Noisy compressor operation, or compressor not running at all.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the air suspension compressor. Check for leaks in the air lines and struts.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,200 - $2,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Air Strut Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Vehicle sitting low, especially on one corner. Hissing sound coming from the strut. Uneven ride height.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the leaking air strut.  May be necessary to replace in pairs.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+ per strut
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Freezing/Crashing**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The MMI (Multi Media Interface) system freezes, becomes unresponsive, or spontaneously reboots. Affects navigation, audio, and other vehicle functions.
    *   Solution: Software update. In some cases, replacement of the MMI control unit.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 (if software update resolves the issue) - $2,000+ (MMI unit replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Sensor Malfunctions (Parking Sensors, Blind Spot Monitoring)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: False alarms or no response from parking sensors or blind spot monitoring system. Warning lights on the dashboard.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis and replacement of the faulty sensor(s). Check wiring and connections.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+ per sensor (depending on location and complexity of the repair)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Adaptive Headlight Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Headlights not leveling properly, error message on the dashboard, headlights flickering.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the headlight control module or the entire headlight assembly.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $3,000+ (depending on the component that needs replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Sunroof Malfunction**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Sunroof not opening or closing properly, getting stuck, or making excessive noise.
    *   Solution: Lubrication of sunroof tracks. In some cases, replacement of the sunroof motor or regulator.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $1,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2022 Audi S8, while a desirable high-performance luxury sedan, does have some potential issues. Engine and transmission problems can be costly, particularly turbocharger failure or the need for a transmission rebuild. Electrical gremlins, especially with the infotainment system, are also a common complaint. Suspension issues with the air suspension system can also lead to significant repair bills. Overall, while many owners experience no issues, potential buyers should be aware of these common problems and budget accordingly for potential repairs. The severity of the issues ranges from minor inconveniences to potentially very expensive repairs.
