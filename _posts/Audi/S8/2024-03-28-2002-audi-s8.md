**Introduction**

The 2002 Audi S8 is a high-performance luxury sedan powered by a 4.2-liter V8 engine mated to a 5-speed automatic transmission. It delivers 360 horsepower and 317 lb-ft of torque, providing a thrilling driving experience. The S8 boasts a refined interior, advanced technology, and a sleek exterior design. While it offers an exceptional driving experience, several common issues have been reported with this vehicle.

**Engine**

**4.2L V8**

-* **Timing Chain Tensioner Failure:** The timing chain tensioner can fail, leading to a rattling noise and potentially catastrophic engine damage.
    - Solution: Replace timing chain tensioner.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
*- **Intake Manifold Gasket Leak:** The intake manifold gasket can develop leaks, causing rough idling, hesitation, and poor fuel economy.
    - Solution: Replace intake manifold gasket.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800
*- **Oil Pump Failure:** The oil pump can fail, resulting in insufficient oil lubrication and potential engine damage.
    - Solution: Replace oil pump.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
-* **Cam Position Sensor Malfunction:** The camshaft position sensor can malfunction, causing engine misfires, stalling, and reduced power.
    - Solution: Replace camshaft position sensor.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
* **PCV Valve Failure:** The positive crankcase ventilation (PCV) valve can fail, leading to oil leaks, engine sludge, and decreased performance.
    - Solution: Replace PCV valve.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200

**Transmission**

**5-Speed Automatic**

-* **Solenoid Failure:** The transmission solenoids can fail, causing delayed or harsh shifting, gear slippage, and transmission failure.
    - Solution: Replace failed solenoids.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
*- **Torque Converter shudder:** The torque converter can develop shuddering when shifting, indicating potential failure.
    - Solution: Replace torque converter.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
*- **Transmission Oil Leak:** The transmission can develop leaks at various seals and gaskets, resulting in loss of fluid and potential transmission damage.
    - Solution: Repair or replace leaking components.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500

**Suspension**

-* **Air Suspension Compressor Failure:** The air suspension compressor can fail, causing the suspension to malfunction and affect ride height.
    - Solution: Replace air suspension compressor.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,000+
-* **Strut or Shock Failure:** The struts or shocks can fail over time, leading to unstable handling, reduced ride comfort, and uneven tire wear.
    - Solution: Replace struts or shocks.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,500+ per axle
-* **Control Arm Bushing Wear:** The control arm bushings can wear or tear, causing excessive vibration, clunking noises, and reduced handling.
    - Solution: Replace worn or torn bushings.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+ per corner

**Electrical**

-* **Battery Discharge:** Electrical issues can lead to battery discharge, preventing the vehicle from starting.
    - Solution: Identify and repair electrical component drain.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the source of the drain
-* **Alternator Failure:** The alternator can fail, leading to a loss of electrical power and battery depletion.
    - Solution: Replace alternator.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
-* **Headlight Malfunction:** The headlights can malfunction due to issues with the bulbs, wiring, or control modules.
    - Solution: Repair or replace faulty components.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+

**Exterior**

-* **Taillight Bulb Failure:** The taillight bulbs can fail, reducing visibility and safety.
    - Solution: Replace burnt-out bulbs.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $200
-* **Door Lock Actuator Failure:** The door lock actuators can fail, preventing doors from locking or unlocking.
    - Solution: Replace faulty actuators.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+ per door

**Interior**

-* **Heater Blower Motor Failure:** The heater blower motor can fail, affecting heating and cooling performance.
    - Solution: Replace blower motor.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+
-* **Navigation System Malfunction:** The navigation system can malfunction due to software or hardware issues.
    - Solution: Update or repair navigation system.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+
-* **Climate Control Panel Failure:** The climate control panel can malfunction, affecting temperature and airflow settings.
    - Solution: Repair or replace climate control panel.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+

**Summary**

The 2002 Audi S8 is a powerful and luxurious vehicle, but it is not without its issues. The engine, transmission, and suspension components can experience various problems that can be costly to repair. Electrical issues, exterior faults, and interior malfunctions can also be encountered. Overall, the severity and cost of these issues can be significant, potentially impacting the owner's experience with the vehicle.
