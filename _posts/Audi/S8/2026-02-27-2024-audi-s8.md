## Suspension

### Adaptive Air Suspension
**Air Suspension Leaks/Compressor Failures**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle sagging on one or more corners, 'Suspension Malfunction' warning light, uneven ride height.
- **Solution:** Repair or replace leaking air struts, hoses, or the air compressor. Regular checks for leaks are recommended.
- **Cost:** $800 - $2500


## Electrical

### Infotainment System
**Software Glitches/Freezing**
- **Symptoms:** Touchscreen unresponsiveness, system freezing, intermittent connectivity issues with Apple CarPlay/Android Auto.
- **Solution:** Software updates, system resets, or potential replacement of the infotainment control unit.
- **Cost:** $300 - $1500


### Advanced Driver-Assistance Systems (ADAS)
**Sensor Calibration Issues**
- **Symptoms:** False warnings from adaptive cruise control, lane keeping assist, or parking sensors; system deactivation.
- **Solution:** Recalibration of radar, camera, or ultrasonic sensors. May require specialized equipment.
- **Cost:** $200 - $700

### Summary Verdict
The 2024 Audi S8 demonstrates strong technical reliability, particularly in its core powertrain components (engine and transmission), where no widespread issues have been reported. The 4.0L twin-turbo V8 and 8-speed automatic transmission are robust. However, potential areas for concern include the adaptive air suspension, which can experience leaks or compressor failures, and the advanced electrical systems. Infotainment software glitches and ADAS sensor calibration can occur, requiring software updates or recalibration. While these are not indicative of systemic failure, they represent the most common technical deviations from expected performance. Overall, the S8 offers a reliable luxury performance experience, with most reported issues being manageable and repairable.
