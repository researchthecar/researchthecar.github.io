# 2017 Audi S8

### Overview
The 2017 Audi S8, a high-performance luxury sedan, generally exhibits strong reliability, reflecting its premium engineering and build quality. While no vehicle is entirely immune to issues, the S8 tends to perform well in terms of mechanical and electrical dependability for its class and performance level. Owners can expect a robust driving experience, though as with any sophisticated German automobile, proactive maintenance and addressing potential issues promptly are key to long-term satisfaction. This analysis focuses on documented common problems beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential areas of concern for prospective and current owners.

## Suspension

### Adaptive Air Suspension
**Air Spring Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle sagging on one or more corners, 'air suspension system fault' warning light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of leaking air springs or associated lines.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1500 per corner

**Compressor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Inability to raise or lower the vehicle, constant running of the compressor, warning lights.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the air suspension compressor unit.
- **Cost:** $700 - $1200


## Electrical

### Infotainment System
**Software Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Screen freezing, unresponsiveness, navigation errors, Bluetooth connectivity issues.
- **Solution:** Software updates, system resets, or potential module replacement.
- **Cost:** $150 - $500


### Lighting Systems
**LED Headlight Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** Individual LED failure, flickering, or complete headlight outage.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the headlight control module or the entire headlight assembly.
- **Cost:** $1000 - $2500 per side

### Summary Verdict
The 2017 Audi S8 is a reliable performance luxury sedan, with its 4.0L twin-turbo V8 engine and 8-speed automatic transmission being particularly robust. The primary areas where owners might encounter issues are related to the sophisticated adaptive air suspension, with potential for leaks or compressor failures, and certain electrical components like the infotainment system and LED headlights, which can experience software glitches or module failures. Interior climate control actuators can also be a point of concern. Despite these potential issues, which are not uncommon for high-end vehicles with advanced technology, the S8 offers a generally dependable ownership experience when properly maintained.
