## Suspension

### Adaptive Air Suspension
**Air Suspension Leaks/Compressor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle sagging on one or more corners, 'system fault' warning messages, uneven ride height.
- **Solution:** Repair or replace leaking air struts, hoses, or the air compressor. Regular inspection of suspension components is recommended.
- **Cost:** $800 - $2500


## Electrical

### Infotainment System
**Software Glitches/Freezing**
- **Symptoms:** Touchscreen unresponsiveness, system freezing, intermittent connectivity issues with Apple CarPlay/Android Auto.
- **Solution:** Software updates, system resets, or potential replacement of the infotainment control unit.
- **Cost:** $300 - $1500


### Driver Assistance Systems
**Sensor Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** False warnings from adaptive cruise control, lane keeping assist, or parking sensors; system deactivation.
- **Solution:** Cleaning of sensors, recalibration, or replacement of faulty sensors or control modules.
- **Cost:** $400 - $1200

### Summary Verdict
The 2020 Audi S8 presents a generally reliable profile for a high-performance luxury vehicle. The powertrain, including the 4.0L twin-turbo V8 engine and 8-speed automatic transmission, shows no widespread reported issues. The primary areas of concern tend to be related to the advanced technology integrated into the car. The adaptive air suspension can experience leaks or compressor failures, and the sophisticated electrical systems, particularly the infotainment and driver assistance features, may encounter software glitches or sensor malfunctions. These issues, while not universal, are the most commonly cited technical concerns. With diligent maintenance and prompt attention to any warning signs, the 2020 S8 can offer a dependable and exhilarating ownership experience.
