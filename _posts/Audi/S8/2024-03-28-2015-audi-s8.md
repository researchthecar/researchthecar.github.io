**Introduction:**
The 2015 Audi S8 is a high-performance luxury sedan powered by a 4.0L twin-turbocharged V8 engine. It boasts 520 horsepower and 580 lb-ft of torque, making it one of the quickest sedans on the market. The S8 comes standard with an 8-speed automatic transmission and Quattro all-wheel drive. Its impressive handling and luxurious interior make it popular among enthusiasts and luxury car buyers alike. However, despite its reputation, the 2015 Audi S8 has faced several issues reported by owners.

**Engine:**
- **4.0L Twin-Turbocharged V8**
  - **Intake Valve Carbon Buildup**
    - Description/Symptoms: Over time, carbon deposits can accumulate on the intake valves, reducing airflow and causing performance issues such as rough idling, reduced power, and increased emissions.
    - Solution: Regular intake valve cleaning is recommended to remove carbon buildup. It involves using specialized cleaning agents and equipment.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,200+
  - **Turbocharger failure**
    - Description/Symptoms: The turbochargers may fail prematurely due to excessive heat or lack of lubrication. Symptoms include a lack of power, excessive smoke from the exhaust, and unusual noises.
    - Solution: Replacing the turbochargers is necessary. It requires specialized knowledge and equipment.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $3,000 - $6,000+
  - **Timing Chain Issues**
    - Description/Symptoms: The timing chain may stretch or break over time, causing the engine to lose timing and potentially leading to catastrophic failure. Symptoms include a rattling noise from the engine, rough idling, and a loss of power.
    - Solution: Replacing the timing chain and related components is necessary. It is a labor-intensive repair.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,500 - $4,000+

**Transmission:**
- **8-Speed Automatic**
  - **Torque Converter shudder**
    - Description/Symptoms: The torque converter may experience shuddering or vibrations during acceleration or shifting. It can be caused by worn or damaged components within the torque converter.
    - Solution: Replacing or repairing the torque converter is necessary. It requires removing and disassembling the transmission.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,500+

**Suspension:**
- **Adaptive Air Suspension**
  - **Air Spring Leaks**
    - Description/Symptoms: The air springs in the adaptive air suspension may develop leaks over time, causing the suspension to lose ride height or become uneven.
    - Solution: Replacing the leaking air springs is necessary.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+ per corner
  - **Air Compressor Failure**
    - Description/Symptoms: The air compressor for the adaptive air suspension may fail, leading to a loss of ride height or inability to adjust the suspension.
    - Solution: Replacing the air compressor is necessary.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+

**Electrical:**
- **Infotainment System Glitches**
    - Description/Symptoms: The MMI infotainment system may experience glitches, such as freezing, lagging, or losing connectivity.
    - Solution: Software updates or replacing the infotainment module may be necessary.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
  - **Electrical Malfunctions**
    - Description/Symptoms: Various electrical components may experience malfunctions, leading to issues such as inoperative lights, power loss, or drained batteries.
    - Solution: Diagnosis and repair of the specific malfunctioning component is required.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the component

**Exterior:**
- **Sunroof Issues**
    - Description/Symptoms: The panoramic sunroof may experience problems such as leaks, rattling, or failure to open or close properly.
    - Solution: Repairing or replacing the sunroof or its components may be necessary.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $3,000+

**Interior:**
- **HVAC Malfunctions**
    - Description/Symptoms: The heating, ventilation, and air conditioning (HVAC) system may experience malfunctions, such as weak airflow, temperature control issues, or unpleasant odors.
    - Solution: Diagnosis and repair of the specific faulty component is required.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the component
  - **Seat Adjustment Problems**
    - Description/Symptoms: The power-adjustable seats may experience issues such as failed motors, worn switches, or uneven adjustment.
    - Solution: Repair or replacement of the faulty components may be necessary.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+

**Summary:**
The 2015 Audi S8 is a powerful and luxurious sedan, but it has not been without its issues. Several owners have reported problems with the engine, transmission, suspension, electrical system, and interior. The severity and cost of these issues vary, but some can be quite expensive to repair. Potential buyers should be aware of these potential problems before purchasing a used 2015 Audi S8.
