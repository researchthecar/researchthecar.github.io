# 2002 Audi RS4

### Overview
The 2002 Audi RS4, a performance-oriented variant of the B5 generation, is renowned for its potent twin-turbocharged V6 engine and sophisticated all-wheel-drive system. While offering exhilarating driving dynamics, like many high-performance vehicles of its era, it can present specific reliability concerns that owners should be aware of. This analysis focuses on documented, non-wear-and-tear related issues that have been reported by owners and technicians, providing insights into potential problem areas and their associated repair costs. Understanding these potential pitfalls can help prospective buyers and current owners manage maintenance and ensure the longevity of this iconic performance car.

---


## Engine

### 2.7L Twin-Turbocharged V6 (APB)
**Turbocharger Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of power, excessive smoke from exhaust, whining noises from the turbo area.
- **Solution:** Replacement of affected turbocharger(s).
- **Cost:** $1,500 - $3,000 per turbo

**PCV System Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Rough idle, oil leaks, increased oil consumption, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the Positive Crankcase Ventilation (PCV) system components.
- **Cost:** $300 - $700

**Coolant Flange Leak**
- **Symptoms:** Coolant leaks, overheating, sweet smell from engine bay.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the plastic coolant flange with an upgraded metal version.
- **Cost:** $200 - $400


## Transmission

### 6-Speed Manual (01E)
**Clutch Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Slipping clutch, difficulty engaging gears, burning smell.
- **Solution:** Clutch kit replacement (clutch disc, pressure plate, release bearing).
- **Cost:** $800 - $1,500

**Synchronizer Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Grinding noise when shifting, difficulty shifting into certain gears.
- **Solution:** Transmission rebuild or replacement of synchronizer rings.
- **Cost:** $1,500 - $3,000


## Suspension

### Standard Chassis
**Control Arm Bushing Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Clunking noises over bumps, vague steering feel, uneven tire wear.
- **Solution:** Replacement of worn control arm bushings or complete control arm assembly.
- **Cost:** $400 - $800

**Shock Absorber Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Bouncy ride, reduced handling, visible leaks from shocks.
- **Solution:** Replacement of shock absorbers.
- **Cost:** $600 - $1,000


## Electrical

### General Electrical System
**Window Regulator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Power windows not operating, slow window movement, grinding noise from door.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the window regulator mechanism.
- **Cost:** $200 - $400

**Ignition Coil Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Misfires, rough idle, check engine light, reduced engine power.
- **Solution:** Replacement of faulty ignition coil(s).
- **Cost:** $100 - $250 per coil

### Summary Verdict
The 2002 Audi RS4 is a highly desirable performance car, but its advanced engineering and age mean certain components are prone to failure. The twin-turbo V6 engine, while powerful, can suffer from turbocharger issues, PCV system problems, and coolant flange leaks. The manual transmission, though robust, may require clutch replacements or internal repairs over time. Suspension components like control arm bushings and shock absorbers are also common wear items that can develop issues. Electrical gremlins, such as window regulators and ignition coils, can also arise. Regular maintenance and proactive attention to these known problem areas are crucial for maintaining the RS4's performance and reliability.
