# 2019 Audi A3 Quattro Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2019 Audi A3 Quattro, prospective buyers will find a refined and agile compact luxury car equipped with standard all-wheel drive. While owner feedback is generally limited, reports point to occasional electrical and powertrain quirks that require attention during a pre-purchase inspection.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4

The 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine generates approximately 228 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque, providing strong acceleration for daily driving. This powerplant is generally robust, though regular maintenance is vital to avoid long-term performance issues.

## Transmission

### 7-Speed S-tronic Dual-Clutch Automatic

The 7-speed dual-clutch automatic transmission delivers rapid gear changes and pairs exclusively with the Quattro all-wheel-drive system. Proper fluid service intervals are essential to maintain smooth operation over time.

**Transmission Failure Warning and Shifting Irregularities**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may experience a 'Transmission Failure' warning message on the dashboard, illuminated check engine lights, and unexpected shifts between automatic and manual modes.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified technician scan for diagnostic trouble codes and inspect the mechatronic unit and clutch packs for wear or software glitches.

## Exterior

### Exterior Lighting

Electrical complaints are minimal but can involve expensive lighting components if failures occur out of warranty.

**Headlight Assembly Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have reported premature failure of the headlight assembly components, resulting in non-functional lights.
- **Solution:** Inspect the entire headlight assembly and associated wiring harnesses for internal faults, and replace defective components as needed.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V198000: Air Bags:Sensor:Occupant Classification:Front Passenger](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Audi&model=A3&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** A deactivated passenger air bag increases the risk of injury during a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the PODS sensor mat and update the PODS control module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V815000: Tires:Pressure Monitoring And Regulating Systems](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Audi&model=A3&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** The tire pressure monitoring system may not detect an air pressure loss in adequate time when deflating occurs among all four tires simultaneously. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 138, "Tire Pressure Monitoring Systems." An undetected sudden loss of pressure in all four tires.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the TPMS software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V110000: Fuel System, Gasoline](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Audi&model=A3&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** A fuel leak in the presence of an ignition source increases the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the suction pump, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2019 Audi A3 Quattro offers engaging driving dynamics and upscale features, making it an appealing used luxury choice. Buyers should carefully evaluate the transmission and electrical systems during an inspection to ensure everything operates smoothly.
