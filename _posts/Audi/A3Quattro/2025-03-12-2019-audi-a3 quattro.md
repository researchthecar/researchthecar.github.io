The 2019 Audi A3 Quattro is a compact luxury sedan known for its sporty handling, upscale interior, and refined driving experience. The A3 offers a blend of practicality and premium features, making it a popular choice in its segment. The 2019 A3 is available with a 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine, producing 228 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque, paired with a 7-speed S tronic dual-clutch transmission. Some of the things people like about the A3 are its agile handling, stylish design, and standard technology features like Apple CarPlay and Android Auto. Conversely, some common complaints include the sometimes-firm ride, the relatively small rear seat, and the higher price point compared to non-luxury compact cars.

**Engine**

**2.0L Turbocharged Four-Cylinder (228 hp/258 lb-ft)**

*   **Coolant Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaking from the water pump or thermostat housing, leading to low coolant levels and potential overheating.
    *   Solution: Replace the faulty water pump and/or thermostat housing.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800+

*   **Oil Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Oil leaking from valve cover gasket or rear main seal, leading to oil consumption and potential engine damage.
    *   Solution: Replace the valve cover gasket or rear main seal.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1200+

**Transmission**

**7-Speed S tronic Dual-Clutch**

*   **Rough Shifting/Hesitation**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Jerky or delayed shifts, especially when accelerating or decelerating. May also experience hesitation when starting from a stop.
    *   Solution: Software update to the transmission control module (TCM). If the problem persists, the transmission may need to be rebuilt or replaced.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 (software update) - $7,000+ (transmission replacement)

**Suspension**

*   **No Issues Found**

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Freezing/Crashing**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The MMI (Multi Media Interface) system freezes, crashes, or becomes unresponsive, requiring a restart.
    *   Solution: Software update for the MMI system. In some cases, the MMI unit may need to be replaced.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 (software update if under warranty) - $1,500+ (MMI unit replacement)

*   **Battery Drain**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Premature battery drain, requiring frequent jump-starts or battery replacements.
    *   Solution: Identify the parasitic draw causing the battery drain (often related to faulty modules or aftermarket accessories). Repair or replace the affected component.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+

**Exterior**

*   **No Issues Found**

**Interior**

*   **Sunroof Rattling/Creaking**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Sunroof rattles or creaks when driving over bumps or uneven surfaces.
    *   Solution: Lubricate the sunroof tracks and seals. If the rattling persists, the sunroof mechanism may need adjustment or repair.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $300+

*   **Dashboard Creaking**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Dashboard panels creaking, rattling, or squeaking.
    *   Solution: Install foam strips or felt pads behind the panels to reduce friction and movement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $200+

**Summary**

The 2019 Audi A3 Quattro experiences a range of issues, from minor interior rattles to more significant engine and transmission problems. Engine-related issues such as coolant and oil leaks are common, while transmission problems manifest as rough shifting and hesitation. Electrical problems, particularly with the infotainment system, can also be frustrating. While some issues can be resolved with software updates or minor repairs, others, like transmission replacements, can be very costly. The severity of these issues can range from minor inconveniences to major mechanical failures, and the cost of repairs can vary widely depending on the specific problem and the repair shop.
