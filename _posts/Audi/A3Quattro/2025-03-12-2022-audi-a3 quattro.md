The 2022 Audi A3 Quattro is a compact luxury sedan that offers a blend of style, performance, and technology. The A3 Quattro model primarily features a 2.0-liter turbocharged inline-4 engine producing around 201 horsepower and 221 lb-ft of torque, paired with a 7-speed S tronic dual-clutch automatic transmission. Some owners appreciate its sporty handling, fuel efficiency, and upscale interior. Common dislikes often include the sometimes complicated infotainment system and the relatively firm ride. Other available engine options for the 2022 A3 include a 2.0-liter turbocharged inline-4 engine producing around 228 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque (A3 45 TFSI).

**Engine**

**2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4 (201 HP/221 lb-ft)**

*   **Excessive Oil Consumption**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported higher than expected oil consumption, requiring more frequent oil top-offs between scheduled oil changes. This may be indicated by the low oil level warning light illuminating on the dashboard.
    *   Solution: In some cases, a software update may help, but more severe cases may require inspection for leaks or even engine component replacement (piston rings, valve seals).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 (software update) to $1,000 - $5,000+ (engine repairs).
    *   Recalls: None Found

**2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4 (228 HP/258 lb-ft)**

*   **No Issues Found**

**Transmission**

**7-Speed S tronic Dual-Clutch Automatic**

*   **Hesitation/Rough Shifting**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have described experiencing hesitation or rough shifting, particularly during low-speed maneuvers or when accelerating from a stop. This can manifest as a delay in gear engagement or a noticeable jerkiness when the transmission shifts.
    *   Solution: A software update to the transmission control module (TCM) may resolve the issue. In more severe cases, replacement of the mechatronic unit within the transmission or clutch replacement might be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 (software update) to $2,000 - $5,000+ (transmission repairs).
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **No Issues Found**

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Freezing/Glitches**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The infotainment system may occasionally freeze, become unresponsive, or experience glitches in functionality (e.g., navigation issues, audio cutouts).
    *   Solution: Often, a software update to the infotainment system will resolve the problem. In some cases, a system reset or replacement of the infotainment unit may be required.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 (software update) to $500 - $2,000+ (component replacement).
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Sensor Malfunctions (Parking Sensors, etc.)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Issues with parking sensors or other driver-assistance system sensors may occur, resulting in inaccurate readings or system failures.
    *   Solution: Diagnostic testing to identify the faulty sensor and replacement of the affected sensor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+ (sensor replacement).
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **No Issues Found**

**Interior**

*   **Rattling/Squeaking Noises**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported experiencing rattling or squeaking noises emanating from various interior components, such as the dashboard, door panels, or seats.
    *   Solution: Identifying the source of the noise and applying felt tape, tightening fasteners, or replacing worn components.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $500+ (depending on the severity and location of the rattle/squeak).
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

The 2022 Audi A3 Quattro generally exhibits a mix of minor electrical and interior issues, with the most concerning potential problems revolving around the engine and transmission. While excessive oil consumption and rough shifting can be costly to address if they require component replacements, software updates often resolve these issues. Infotainment glitches and sensor malfunctions are relatively common but typically less expensive to fix. Overall, the severity and cost of potential issues vary, but it's advisable to be aware of these common complaints when considering a used 2022 Audi A3 Quattro. A thorough inspection, including a check of the infotainment system and a test drive to assess transmission performance, is recommended before purchase.
