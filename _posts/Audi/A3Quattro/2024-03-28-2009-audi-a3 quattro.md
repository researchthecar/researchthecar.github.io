**2009 Audi A3 Quattro**

The 2009 Audi A3 Quattro is a luxury subcompact car that offers a comfortable and sporty driving experience. It is powered by a 2.0L TFSI inline-4 engine that produces 200 horsepower and 207 lb-ft of torque, paired with a standard 6-speed manual or an optional 6-speed automatic transmission. People like the A3 Quattro for its sleek styling, responsive handling, and well-appointed interior. However, it has some issues that potential buyers should be aware of.

**Engine**

* **2.0L TFSI (6-Speed Manual)**
   * **Carbon Buildup on Intake Valves**
      * Description/Symptoms: Reduced engine power, misfires, rough idle
      * Solution: Remove and clean intake valves and/or replace intake manifold
      * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
      * Recalls: None Found
   * **Timing Chain Tensioner Failure**
      * Description/Symptoms: Rattling noise at engine start-up, reduced engine power
      * Solution: Replace timing chain tensioner
      * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
      * Recalls: 15V-624

* **2.0L TFSI (6-Speed Automatic)**
   * **Mechatronic Unit Failure**
      * Description/Symptoms: Transmission slipping, harsh shifting, loss of power
      * Solution: Replace mechatronic unit
      * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+
      * Recalls: 15V-196

**Transmission**

* **6-Speed Manual**
   * **No Issues Found**
* **6-Speed Automatic**
   * **Solenoid Pack Failure**
      * Description/Symptoms: Delayed or harsh shifting, loss of gears
      * Solution: Replace solenoid pack
      * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
      * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Front Control Arm Bushings Wear**
   * Description/Symptoms: Squeaking or clunking noise over bumps, reduced handling
   * Solution: Replace control arm bushings
   * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
   * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain**
   * Description/Symptoms: Battery goes dead after sitting for a period of time
   * Solution: Diagnose and repair electrical drain, possibly replace battery
   * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
   * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Door Lock Actuator Failure**
   * Description/Symptoms: Door locks do not work properly, doors may not lock or unlock
   * Solution: Replace door lock actuator
   * Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300+
   * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Climate Control Panel Malfunction**
   * Description/Symptoms: Climate control system does not work properly, buttons may not function
   * Solution: Replace climate control panel
   * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+
   * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2009 Audi A3 Quattro has some potential issues that can be expensive to repair. The most serious issues are the timing chain tensioner failure and the mechatronic unit failure, which can both cost thousands of dollars to fix. However, many of the other issues are relatively minor and can be fixed for a few hundred dollars. Overall, the A3 Quattro is a reliable and enjoyable car to drive, but potential buyers should be aware of these issues before making a purchase.
