**2005 Audi A3 Quattro**

The 2005 Audi A3 Quattro is a luxurious compact car that offers a sporty driving experience. It is available with a 2.0L turbocharged inline-4 engine that produces 200 horsepower and 207 lb-ft of torque. The engine is paired with a 6-speed manual transmission or a 6-speed automatic transmission.

Owners of the 2005 Audi A3 Quattro appreciate its stylish design, comfortable interior, and impressive performance. However, there have been some reported issues with the vehicle that potential buyers should be aware of.

**Engine**

**2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4**

* **Fuel Pump Failure:** The fuel pump can fail, causing the engine to stall or run poorly. The fuel pump must be replaced. $1,000 - $1,500+
* **Timing Belt Failure:** The timing belt can break, causing catastrophic engine damage. The timing belt should be replaced every 60,000 miles. $1,200 - $1,800+
* **Turbocharger Failure:** The turbocharger can fail, reducing engine power and causing a loss of boost. The turbocharger must be replaced. $2,000 - $3,000+

**Transmission**

**6-Speed Manual**

* **Clutch Failure:** The clutch can wear out, causing difficulty shifting gears or slipping. The clutch must be replaced. $1,000 - $1,500+
* **Synchronizer Failure:** The synchronizers can fail, making it difficult to shift gears. The transmission must be rebuilt. $2,000 - $3,000+

**6-Speed Automatic**

* **Transmission Control Module Failure:** The transmission control module (TCM) can fail, causing the transmission to shift erratically or not shift at all. The TCM must be replaced. $1,500 - $2,000+
* **Torque Converter Failure:** The torque converter can fail, causing the transmission to slip or shudder. The torque converter must be replaced. $2,000 - $3,000+

**Suspension**

**No Issues Found**

**Electrical**

* **Alternator Failure:** The alternator can fail, causing the battery to not charge. The alternator must be replaced. $500 - $800+
* **Battery Failure:** The battery can fail, preventing the vehicle from starting. The battery must be replaced. $200 - $400+
* **Electrical Harness Damage:** The electrical harness can become damaged, causing electrical problems throughout the vehicle. The damaged harness must be repaired. $500 - $1,000+

**Exterior**

* **Sunroof Leakage:** The sunroof can leak water into the cabin. The sunroof seal must be replaced or repaired. $500 - $1,000+
* **Headlight Condensation:** The headlights can develop condensation on the inside. The headlight assemblies must be replaced or repaired. $500 - $1,200+

**Interior**

* **Seat Adjustment Failure:** The seat adjustment controls can fail, making it difficult to adjust the seat. The seat adjustment motor or control module must be replaced. $500 - $1,000+
* **Climate Control Failure:** The climate control system can fail, causing the heating or cooling to not work properly. The climate control module or other components must be replaced. $500 - $1,500+

**Summary**

The 2005 Audi A3 Quattro is a well-rounded vehicle that offers impressive performance and a luxurious interior. However, there have been some reported issues with the engine, transmission, electrical system, and exterior. These issues can be costly to repair, so it is important to be aware of them before purchasing a used A3 Quattro.
