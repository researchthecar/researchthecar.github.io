# 2017 Audi RS7

### Overview
The 2017 Audi RS7, a high-performance grand tourer, generally exhibits strong reliability, reflecting its premium engineering and build quality. While its potent twin-turbocharged V8 engine and sophisticated quattro all-wheel-drive system are designed for exhilarating performance, they also require diligent maintenance. Owners typically report satisfaction with the vehicle's durability, with most issues being isolated incidents rather than widespread problems. Common areas of attention, though infrequent, can include certain electronic systems and the adaptive suspension components, which are complex by nature. Overall, the RS7 offers a compelling blend of luxury, performance, and dependable operation for discerning drivers.

---


## Engine

### 4.0L Twin-Turbocharged V8
**Turbocharger Wastegate Actuator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Reduced engine power, boost leaks, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of faulty wastegate actuator or turbocharger assembly.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1500

**PCV Valve Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Rough idle, oil leaks, potential engine misfires.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the positive crankcase ventilation (PCV) valve.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600

## Suspension

### Adaptive Air Suspension
**Air Spring Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle sagging on one or more corners, 'air suspension malfunction' warning light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of leaking air springs or air compressor.
- **Cost:** $1000 - $2500


## Electrical

### Infotainment System Glitches
**Occasional Freezing or Unresponsiveness**
- **Symptoms:** Infotainment screen freezes, MMI system becomes unresponsive, navigation issues.
- **Solution:** Software update or reset of the MMI system. In rare cases, module replacement.
- **Cost:** $200 - $800


### Parking Sensor Malfunctions
**False Alarms or Non-Functioning Sensors**
- **Symptoms:** Audible warnings when no obstacles are present, or failure to detect obstacles.
- **Solution:** Cleaning or replacement of faulty parking sensors.
- **Cost:** $300 - $700

### Summary Verdict
The 2017 Audi RS7 is a robust performance vehicle with a generally positive reliability record. The primary areas that may require attention are related to the complex twin-turbocharged engine's ancillary components like wastegate actuators and PCV valves, and the adaptive air suspension system, which can develop leaks. Electrical gremlins, particularly with the infotainment system and parking sensors, are not uncommon but are typically resolved with software updates or component replacement. Overall, for a vehicle of its performance caliber, the RS7 offers commendable reliability, provided it receives regular and specialized maintenance.
