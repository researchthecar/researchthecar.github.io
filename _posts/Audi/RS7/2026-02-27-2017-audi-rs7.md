# 2017 Audi RS7 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2017 Audi RS7 is an ultra-high-performance luxury sport sedan known for its breathtaking twin-turbo V8 power and upscale cabin. While heavily built, buyers considering a used model should note that complex high-performance machinery requires meticulous maintenance, and owner-reported issues tend to center around advanced electronics, auxiliary cooling, and powertrain peripherals rather than fundamental mechanical flaws.

---

## Engine

### 4.0L Twin-Turbo V8 Engine

The 4.0-liter twin-turbocharged V8 produces approximately 560 horsepower (and up to 605 hp in Performance trims) paired with standard quattro all-wheel drive. It delivers ferocious acceleration and robust highway performance, though prospective buyers should budget for premium maintenance costs associated with high-output European turbo engines.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Tiptronic Automatic Transmission

The conventional 8-speed Tiptronic automatic transmission handles high torque outputs smoothly while offering quick shifts via paddle shifters. It generally proves durable under normal operation, provided fluid service intervals are maintained.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V370000: Air Bags:Sensor:Occupant Classification](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Audi&model=RS7&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** Stress or wear of the body-sensing mat within the front passenger seat may cause the Passenger Occupant Detection System (PODS) control module to malfunction. If the PODS module malfunctions, the front passenger air bag may not deploy properly in the event of a crash, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Volkswagen will notify owners, and dealers will install a PODS system repair kit, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V096000: Equipment:Other:Labels](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Audi&model=RS7&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 110, "Tire Selection and Rims." If the operator were to load the vehicle based on information on the tire information label, they could overload the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Audi will notify owners, and dealers will replace the tire information label, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V178000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Engine:Gasoline:Turbo/Supercharger](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Audi&model=RS7&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** Worn bearings can cause the turbine wheel to contact the turbocharger housing or cause the shaft to break, causing an engine stall and increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the oil strainer and perform an oil change, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2017 Audi RS7 remains an exhilarating and sophisticated choice for luxury performance buyers, blending supercar-shaming straight-line speed with everyday hatchback utility. Because no prominent recurring mechanical complaints dominate public logs, a thorough pre-purchase inspection by an Audi specialist and verification of a strict service history are the best safeguards for a confident used purchase.
