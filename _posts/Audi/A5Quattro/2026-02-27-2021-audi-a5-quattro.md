# 2021 Audi A5 Quattro Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2021 Audi A5 Quattro, buyers will find a stylish and technologically advanced luxury vehicle equipped with standard all-wheel drive. While owner feedback highlights solid driving dynamics and modern cabin amenities, prospective buyers should review available complaint reports concerning powertrain performance and electronic systems. Conducting a thorough pre-purchase inspection can help ensure all mechanical and driver-assistance features operate properly.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4 (45 TFSI)

The 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine produces approximately 261 horsepower and 273 lb-ft of torque, paired with a 12-volt mild-hybrid system. It offers brisk acceleration and balanced efficiency, representing the standard powerplant for the A5 Quattro lineup.

## Transmission

### 7-Speed S tronic Dual-Clutch Automatic

The seven-speed dual-clutch automatic transmission delivers rapid gear changes and transfers power smoothly to the Quattro all-wheel-drive system. Buyers should verify proper shift quality during a test drive, as owner reports note occasional electronic or mechanical engagement warnings.

**Transmission Failure Warnings and Shifting Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have reported warning messages indicating transmission failure, limited vehicle use, or sudden loss of gear shifting capability while driving.
- **Solution:** Have the transmission control module and mechanical components diagnosed by a qualified specialist or dealer.

## Electrical

### Instrument Panel and Power Delivery

Monitors electrical system stability, battery health, and dashboard display functionality.

**Instrument Cluster Blanking and Power Loss**
- **Symptoms:** The digital instrument panel may blink and go dark while driving, or the vehicle may experience a sudden loss of power at low speeds accompanied by warning lights.
- **Solution:** Inspect the electrical charging system, battery connections, and module software updates at a service center.

## Safety

### Air Bag System

Covers occupant detection sensors, seatbelts, and supplemental restraint systems.

**Passenger Air Bag Indicator Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** The passenger side air bag indicator light may constantly turn on and off regardless of whether a passenger is seated.
- **Solution:** Have the seat occupant detection sensor and associated wiring inspected and replaced if necessary.

### Forward Collision Avoidance

Encompasses camera and radar-based driver assistance features such as automatic emergency braking.

**Unintended Automatic Emergency Braking**
- **Symptoms:** The pre sense automatic braking system may engage aggressively without an apparent road hazard present.
- **Solution:** Check for software updates and ensure the camera and radar sensors on the windshield and bumpers are clean and properly calibrated.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V606000: Seat Belts:Rear/Other:Retractor](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Audi&model=A5&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 208, "Occupant Crash Protection." An unsecured child restraint system can increase the risk of injury during a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect, and replace as necessary, the middle-rear seat belt assemblies, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V034000: Tires](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Audi&model=A5&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The rear axle alignment may not have been inspected after the repairs for Recall 21V-295 (42L1) were performed. Rear axle misalignment may cause premature or uneven tire wear, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the rear axle, adjust the alignment as necessary, and replace any prematurely or unevenly worn tires, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V737000: Exterior Lighting:Tail Lights](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Audi&model=A5&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The wiring harness for the rear lights could be missing the anti-abrasion protection in the area of the spare wheel well. Without the scuff protection, the wires may become damaged during a rear crash, disabling the doors from being opened from the outside, causing a loss of hazard lights, and increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Audi will notify owners, and dealers will apply scuff protection, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V295000: Suspension:Rear:Axle:Non-Powered Axle Assembly](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Audi&model=A5&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** A broken lock nut may misalign the rear axle, causing a loss of control and increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the lock nuts and, if necessary, the associated bolts, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V742000: Back Over Prevention:Display Function](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Audi&model=A5&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 111, "Rear Visibility." A blank rearview camera display can reduce the driver's rear visibility, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the infotainment unit software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2021 Audi A5 Quattro provides an engaging driving experience and a high-end interior, but potential buyers should remain vigilant regarding reported electrical glitches, advanced safety system sensitivity, and transmission warnings. A comprehensive pre-purchase inspection focusing on the electronics and powertrain will help minimize ownership surprises.
