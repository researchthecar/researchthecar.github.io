# 2016 Audi A5 Quattro

### Overview
The 2016 Audi A5 Quattro, a stylish and performance-oriented coupe, convertible, and Sportback, generally exhibits good reliability, reflecting Audi's premium build quality. While no vehicle is entirely without potential issues, the A5 Quattro's common concerns tend to be manageable and often related to specific components rather than widespread systemic failures. Owners generally appreciate its driving dynamics and interior comfort. This analysis focuses on documented technical issues beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential areas of concern for prospective and current owners.

## Engine

### 2.0L TFSI (Turbocharged Direct Injection)
**Oil Consumption**
- **Symptoms:** Excessive oil consumption between oil changes, potential for oil leaks.
- **Solution:** Engine internal inspection, piston ring replacement, or PCV valve replacement.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1500

**Timing Chain Tensioner**
- **Symptoms:** Rattling noise from the engine, especially on startup; check engine light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of timing chain, tensioners, and guides.
- **Cost:** $1200 - $2000

### Summary Verdict
The 2016 Audi A5 Quattro is a generally reliable vehicle, with most reported issues being specific to certain components rather than systemic flaws. The 2.0L TFSI engine can experience oil consumption and timing chain issues, while the S tronic dual-clutch transmission may develop problems with its mechatronic unit. Electrical gremlins in the infotainment system are also noted. However, the Quattro all-wheel-drive system, manual transmission, and other major components like suspension and interior elements are typically robust. With diligent maintenance and addressing potential issues promptly, the A5 Quattro can offer a dependable and enjoyable ownership experience.
