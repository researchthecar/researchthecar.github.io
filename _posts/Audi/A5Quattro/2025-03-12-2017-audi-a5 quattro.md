The 2017 Audi A5 Quattro is a stylish and sporty coupe, known for its refined driving experience, elegant interior, and standard all-wheel drive. The 2017 A5 Quattro's primary engine option is a 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder producing 220 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque. Some owners appreciate the A5's sleek design and comfortable ride, while others find the rear seats cramped and the infotainment system less intuitive than some competitors. The transmission option is either a six-speed manual or an eight-speed automatic.

**Engine**

**2.0L Turbocharged I4**

*   **Excessive Oil Consumption**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported higher than normal oil consumption, requiring frequent topping off between oil changes. Symptoms include the low oil warning light illuminating and the need to add oil every few thousand miles.
    *   Solution: The fix can vary, starting with oil consumption tests at the dealership. If consumption is excessive, potential repairs can range from replacing PCV valve components to piston ring replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $5,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

* **Coolant Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaking from various locations in the engine bay and having to refill coolant regularly.
    *   Solution: Replacement of leaking components such as hoses, water pump, or coolant reservoir.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**8-Speed Automatic**

*   **Rough Shifting/Hesitation**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported experiencing rough shifting, particularly between lower gears, or hesitation when accelerating. This can manifest as jerky movements or delays in gear changes.
    *   Solution: In some cases, a transmission software update can resolve the issue. In more severe cases, valve body replacement or complete transmission rebuild/replacement may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 (software update) - $5,000+ (transmission replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **No Issues Found**

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Freezing/Crashing**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The MMI (Multi Media Interface) system can freeze, become unresponsive, or crash unexpectedly. This can affect navigation, audio, and other vehicle functions.
    *   Solution: Try resetting the MMI system or having the software updated at the dealership. In severe cases, the MMI control unit might need replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 (reset) - $1,500+ (MMI unit replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found
*   **Battery Drain**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The car's battery drains quickly, even when the vehicle is not in use. This can lead to difficulty starting the car or requiring frequent jump starts.
    *   Solution: Identifying the source of the parasitic drain through diagnostic testing. Common culprits include faulty modules, aftermarket accessories, or a failing battery.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Headlight Condensation**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Moisture builds up inside the headlight housing, causing condensation. This can reduce headlight brightness and potentially damage the bulb or electrical components.
    *   Solution: Removing the headlight assembly and drying it out. Resealing the headlight housing to prevent moisture from re-entering. Replacing the headlight assembly if the condensation has caused significant damage.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $1,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Sunroof Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Sunroof may become stuck, fail to open or close properly, or make excessive noise during operation.
    *   Solution: Lubricating the sunroof tracks, replacing faulty sunroof motor or damaged components of the sunroof mechanism.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found
*   **Door Lock Actuator Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The door lock actuator fails, causing the door to not lock or unlock properly, or at all.
    *   Solution: Replacing the faulty door lock actuator.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2017 Audi A5 Quattro can have some issues that owners should be aware of. The most concerning appear to be related to engine oil consumption and transmission issues, which can potentially lead to high repair costs. Electrical issues, particularly with the infotainment system, are also relatively common. While some issues like headlight condensation may be more cosmetic, others like battery drain can be quite inconvenient. Before purchasing a 2017 Audi A5 Quattro, it's recommended to have it inspected by a qualified mechanic to check for these common problems.
