The 2008 Audi A5 Quattro is a stylish and sporty coupe known for its elegant design and engaging driving experience. The 2008 A5 Quattro was available with a 3.2L V6 engine producing 265 horsepower and 243 lb-ft of torque or a 2.0L Inline-4 Turbo engine producing 211 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque, both with Quattro all-wheel drive. The 3.2L V6 models came with either a 6-speed manual or a 6-speed automatic transmission, and the 2.0L came with a 6-speed manual or a Tiptronic automatic. Owners generally appreciate the A5's sophisticated styling, comfortable interior, and confident handling. Some common complaints include higher maintenance costs compared to other vehicles, and potential issues with the car's electrical system.

**Engine**

**3.2L V6**
*   **Timing Chain Tensioner Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: A rattling noise upon startup, especially after the car has been sitting for a while. This can lead to catastrophic engine damage if the tensioner fails completely, causing the timing chain to slip.
    *   Solution: Replace the timing chain tensioners, guides, and possibly the chain itself.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

* **Oil Leaks**
    * Description/Symptoms: Oil pooling under the car, low oil level warnings, and/or burning oil smell. Common leak locations include the valve cover gaskets and rear main seal.
    * Solution: Replace the leaking gaskets and seals.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**2.0L Inline-4 Turbo**

* **Excessive Oil Consumption**
    * Description/Symptoms: The engine consumes an abnormally high amount of oil, requiring frequent top-offs. This is often due to piston ring design.
    * Solution: Replacement of the pistons and piston rings with an updated design.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $4,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Diverter Valve Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Loss of boost, reduced engine power, and potential for a check engine light.
    * Solution: Replace the diverter valve with an updated version.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**6-Speed Automatic (Tiptronic)**
*   **Valve Body Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Harsh or delayed shifting, slipping between gears, and potential for the transmission to go into "limp mode."
    *   Solution: Replace or rebuild the valve body.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $2,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Control Arm Bushing Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Clunking or rattling noises from the suspension, especially when going over bumps. Poor handling and uneven tire wear can also occur.
    *   Solution: Replace the worn control arm bushings or the entire control arm assembly.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Power Window Regulator Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Windows that move slowly, erratically, or not at all.
    *   Solution: Replace the faulty window regulator.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Central Convenience Control Module Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Intermittent electrical problems such as malfunctioning interior lights, door locks, or alarm system.
    *   Solution: Diagnose and repair or replace the central convenience control module.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Water Leaks into Tail Lights**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Condensation or water accumulation inside the tail light housings.
    *   Solution: Replace the tail light seals or the entire tail light assembly.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Navigation/MMI System Malfunctions**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Freezing, slow response, or complete failure of the Multi Media Interface (MMI) system.
    *   Solution: Software update, component repair, or replacement of the MMI unit.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $2,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**
The 2008 Audi A5 Quattro presents a mix of potential issues. The engine problems, especially those related to timing chains and oil consumption, are of the highest severity and cost the most to repair, but are less common than other issues like suspension, electrical, and interior problems, which can be moderate to repair. Potential buyers should be aware of these potential issues and seek a pre-purchase inspection by a qualified mechanic.
