# 2014 Audi A5 Quattro

### Overview
The 2014 Audi A5 Quattro, known for its stylish coupe and Sportback designs, generally offers a solid ownership experience with a focus on performance and premium features. This model year saw the A5 continue with its established powertrain options and chassis. While Audi vehicles are typically associated with quality engineering, like any complex machine, certain components can experience issues over time. This analysis focuses on documented, non-wear-and-tear related problems reported by owners and technicians for the 2014 A5 Quattro, covering its primary mechanical and electrical systems to provide a comprehensive reliability overview.

---


## Engine

### 2.0L TFSI (Turbocharged Direct Injection)
**PCV Valve Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Rough idle, check engine light, oil consumption, whistling noise from engine.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the Positive Crankcase Ventilation (PCV) valve assembly.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600

**Oil Consumption**
- **Symptoms:** Excessive oil level drops between changes, blue smoke from exhaust on startup.
- **Solution:** Can be related to PCV system, piston rings, or valve stem seals. Requires diagnosis and potential component replacement.
- **Cost:** $500 - $2000+

### Summary Verdict
The 2014 Audi A5 Quattro is a generally reliable vehicle, particularly with the more robust 3.0L supercharged V6 engine and its associated transmissions. The most commonly cited issues revolve around the 2.0L TFSI engine, specifically related to the PCV system and potential oil consumption, which can be costly to repair if not addressed promptly. Electrical gremlins, particularly with the MMI infotainment system, can also occur, often requiring software updates. However, major failures in the drivetrain, suspension, and body are uncommon. Regular maintenance and addressing early signs of the aforementioned engine issues are key to ensuring long-term reliability for this stylish Audi.
