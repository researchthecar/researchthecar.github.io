Here is a breakdown of common issues and complaints reported by owners of the 2019 Audi A5 Quattro, categorized by component and considering different engine/transmission options.

The 2019 Audi A5 Quattro is a stylish and sporty compact executive car, praised for its sleek design, comfortable interior, and engaging driving dynamics. Available as a coupe, sportback (four-door hatchback), and cabriolet (convertible), the A5 offers a blend of practicality and luxury. The most common engine option is a 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder producing 248 horsepower and 273 lb-ft of torque. Some owners appreciate the A5's fuel efficiency and agile handling. Common dislikes include the relatively small rear seats in the coupe and cabriolet, and the complexity of the infotainment system.

**Engine**

**2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4 (248 hp / 273 lb-ft)**

*   **Oil Consumption**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported higher than expected oil consumption, requiring more frequent topping off between scheduled oil changes.
    *   Solution: The solution varies depending on the severity. It can range from monitoring oil levels more closely to replacing piston rings or even the entire engine in extreme cases.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $8,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Coolant Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaks can occur from various points in the cooling system, including the water pump, hoses, and radiator. Symptoms include low coolant levels, overheating, and visible leaks.
    *   Solution: Replacing the leaking component, such as the water pump or a hose.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**7-Speed S Tronic Dual-Clutch Automatic**

*   **Hesitation/Rough Shifting**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners report hesitation during acceleration or rough shifting, especially at lower speeds. This can be caused by issues with the transmission's mechatronic unit or clutch packs.
    *   Solution: A software update may resolve the issue in some cases. If the problem persists, replacing the mechatronic unit or clutch packs might be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $5,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Total transmission failure in some instances.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the entire transmission.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $7,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **No Issues Found**

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Freezing/Crashing**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The MMI infotainment system can sometimes freeze, crash, or become unresponsive. This can affect navigation, audio, and other vehicle functions.
    *   Solution: A software update may resolve the issue. In some cases, the MMI unit may need to be replaced.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 (software update) - $1,500+ (MMI unit replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Sensor Malfunctions (Parking Sensors, etc.)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Malfunctioning parking sensors are a relatively common complaint.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the faulty sensor(s).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Sunroof Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Sunroof malfunctions, including failure to open or close properly, or unusual noises during operation.
    *   Solution: Repair or replacement of the sunroof motor, track, or other components.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,200+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Rattling/Creaking Noises**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported rattling or creaking noises from the dashboard, door panels, or rear deck, especially when driving on rough roads.
    *   Solution: Identifying and securing the source of the noise, which may involve adding padding or tightening loose components.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2019 Audi A5 Quattro, while generally reliable, does exhibit some common issues. Engine-related problems such as oil consumption and coolant leaks can be costly to address, especially if they escalate to major repairs. Transmission issues, particularly with the dual-clutch system, also represent a significant potential expense. Electrical issues like infotainment glitches and sensor malfunctions can be frustrating, and interior rattles can detract from the vehicle's luxury feel. While not every A5 will experience these problems, potential buyers should be aware of these common complaints and factor them into their purchasing decision and maintenance budget. Overall, the issues range from minor inconveniences to potentially expensive repairs.
