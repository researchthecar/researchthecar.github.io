The 2022 Audi A5 Quattro is a stylish and sporty compact executive car, appreciated for its elegant design, comfortable ride, and sophisticated technology. The A5 Quattro offers a blend of luxury and performance that appeals to a wide range of drivers. The standard engine is a 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder producing 261 horsepower and 273 lb-ft of torque, paired with a seven-speed S tronic dual-clutch automatic transmission. Some owners praise its refined interior and user-friendly infotainment system, while others express concerns about reliability and the cost of maintenance. There are no other engine and transmission options available for the 2022 Audi A5 Quattro.

**Engine**

**2.0L Turbocharged Four-Cylinder**

*   **Excessive Oil Consumption**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners report higher than expected oil consumption, requiring frequent top-offs between oil changes. This can manifest as the low oil warning light illuminating.
    *   Solution: In some cases, a software update can help, but more severe cases may require replacement of the piston rings or even the entire engine.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $8,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**7-Speed S tronic Dual-Clutch Automatic**

*   **Rough or Jerky Shifting**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported experiencing rough or jerky shifts, particularly at lower speeds or during stop-and-go traffic. This can also manifest as a "clunk" when shifting between gears.
    *   Solution: A software update to the transmission control module (TCM) might resolve the issue. If the problem persists, the mechatronic unit (valve body) within the transmission may need to be replaced.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $3,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

No Issues Found

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Freezing/Lagging**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The MMI infotainment system can occasionally freeze, lag, or become unresponsive. This may require a system reboot or a software update to resolve. Some owners also report connectivity issues with Apple CarPlay or Android Auto.
    *   Solution: Try restarting the system. If the issue persists, check for software updates. In some cases, a full system reset or component replacement might be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 (software update) - $1,500+ (component replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Sensor Malfunctions**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Issues with various sensors, such as parking sensors or blind spot monitoring sensors, have been reported. These may cause false alarms or complete failure of the sensor system.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis of the faulty sensor is required, followed by replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+ per sensor
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Tail Light LED Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Premature failure of the LED tail lights has been reported by some owners. Individual LEDs or entire light assemblies may stop working.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the faulty tail light assembly.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $700+ per tail light
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Sunroof Rattling/Squeaking**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rattling or squeaking noises emanating from the sunroof area, especially when driving on uneven surfaces.
    *   Solution: Cleaning and lubricating the sunroof seals and tracks may alleviate the noise. In some cases, adjustment or replacement of sunroof components is necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2022 Audi A5 Quattro, while generally a well-regarded vehicle, does have some common issues that prospective buyers should be aware of. The engine's potential for oil consumption and the transmission's occasional rough shifting are significant concerns. Electrical glitches with the infotainment system and sensor malfunctions also appear with some frequency. Exterior tail light failures and interior sunroof noises round out the list of common complaints. While many of these issues can be resolved with software updates or relatively minor repairs, some, like engine repairs, can be quite costly, so it is important to have an inspection done by a certified mechanic before purchasing a used version of this car.
