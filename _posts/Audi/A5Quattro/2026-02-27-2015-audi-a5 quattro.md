# 2015 Audi A5 Quattro

### Overview
The 2015 Audi A5 Quattro, known for its stylish coupe and Sportback designs, generally offers a solid ownership experience with a focus on performance and premium features. While Audi's Quattro all-wheel-drive system is a hallmark of its capability, like any vehicle, it has specific areas that have seen more attention from owners regarding reliability. This analysis focuses on documented technical issues beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential concerns for prospective buyers and current owners. The data presented aims to highlight common problems reported by owners and technicians, offering a clearer picture of the A5 Quattro's long-term dependability across its various mechanical and electrical systems.

## Engine

### 2.0L TFSI (Turbocharged Direct Injection)
**Oil Consumption**
- **Symptoms:** Excessive oil consumption between oil changes, potential for oil leaks.
- **Solution:** Piston ring replacement, PCV valve replacement, or oil consumption test and potential engine rebuild.
- **Cost:** $1,500 - $3,500

**Timing Chain Tensioner Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Rattling noise from the engine, especially on startup; check engine light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of timing chain, tensioners, guides, and related components.
- **Cost:** $1,200 - $2,500

### Summary Verdict
The 2015 Audi A5 Quattro presents a generally reliable package, particularly with the 3.0L Supercharged V6 engine and the Tiptronic automatic transmission, which show few widespread issues. The 2.0L TFSI engine, while common, has documented concerns regarding oil consumption and timing chain tensioner wear, which can be costly to repair. The S tronic dual-clutch transmission has also seen reports of mechatronic unit failures. Electrical gremlins, primarily software-related in the infotainment system, are present but often resolvable with updates. Overall, the A5 Quattro is a desirable vehicle, but potential buyers should be aware of the specific engine and transmission variants and their associated potential repair costs, especially concerning the 2.0L TFSI and S tronic transmissions.
