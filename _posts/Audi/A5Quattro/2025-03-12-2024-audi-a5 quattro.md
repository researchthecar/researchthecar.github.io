The 2024 Audi A5 Quattro is a stylish and sporty compact executive car available as a coupe, sportback (four-door hatchback), and cabriolet. This analysis focuses on the most common issues reported for the 2024 A5 Quattro, specifically equipped with the 2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4 engine, which produces 261 horsepower and 273 lb-ft of torque. The 2.0L engine is paired with a 7-speed S tronic dual-clutch transmission. Owners generally praise the A5 for its sleek design, comfortable interior, and engaging driving dynamics, while common complaints often revolve around the infotainment system complexity, the cost of maintenance, and some reported electronic gremlins.

**Engine**

**2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4**

*   **Excessive Oil Consumption (Potential)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported noticing the need to add oil between scheduled oil changes. This may indicate increased oil consumption, although it's not yet widely reported.
    *   Solution: Monitor oil levels regularly. If consumption is excessive, a dealer inspection is recommended to check for leaks or potential engine issues. The dealer will perform an oil consumption test to determine the severity. In some cases, replacing the PCV valve or piston rings might be necessary, depending on the root cause.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $5,000+ (depending on the severity and repair needed, ranging from PCV replacement to engine rebuild).
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**7-Speed S tronic Dual-Clutch**

*   **Hesitation/Rough Shifting (Potential)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported occasional hesitation during acceleration or rough shifts, particularly at lower speeds or when the transmission is cold. This isn't a widespread issue, but worth noting.
    *   Solution: A software update from the dealer may resolve the issue. In more severe cases, inspection of the transmission fluid level and condition, or internal inspection of the transmission may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 - $500 (for software updates or fluid changes) to $2,000+ (if internal repairs are required).
     * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

No Issues Found

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Glitches**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The MMI infotainment system can sometimes exhibit glitches such as freezing, slow response times, or random reboots. Connectivity issues with Apple CarPlay or Android Auto have also been reported.
    *   Solution: Performing a system reset can sometimes resolve minor glitches. In other cases, a software update from the dealer may be required. If the problem persists, hardware replacement might be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 (for a reset) to $100 - $300+ (for software updates) to $1,000+ (for hardware replacement).
    * Recalls: None Found

*   **Sensor Malfunctions (Potential)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported issues with various sensors, such as parking sensors, blind spot monitoring, or the backup camera. These may result in error messages or the systems not functioning correctly.
    *   Solution: Diagnose the faulty sensor and replace it. This often involves using a diagnostic scan tool to identify the specific sensor causing the problem.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+ (depending on the sensor and labor costs).
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Poorly-Aligned Panels**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some new owners have noted that panels on the exterior may not be perfectly aligned from the factory. This can include headlights, bumpers, and other trim pieces.
    *   Solution: Adjustment of the panels by a body shop or dealer technician to match the vehicle's specifications.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+ (depending on the severity of the problem and the body shop's labor rates).
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Rattling/Squeaking Noises**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have experienced rattling or squeaking noises emanating from the dashboard, door panels, or rear deck, especially on rough roads.
    *   Solution: Identifying and isolating the source of the noise, and then applying felt tape, foam padding, or tightening loose components. This may require dealer intervention or a specialist.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+ (depending on the complexity of the repair and labor costs).
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

While the 2024 Audi A5 Quattro 2.0T is generally a reliable and enjoyable vehicle, some owners have reported issues. The most common complaints revolve around the infotainment system, and potentially some sensor issues. Engine oil consumption, transmission, and panel alignment issues have also been reported by a small amount of owners. The severity and cost of these issues vary, but addressing them promptly can help prevent further damage and ensure a better ownership experience. As the 2024 model year is relatively new, the frequency and scope of these issues may evolve over time.
