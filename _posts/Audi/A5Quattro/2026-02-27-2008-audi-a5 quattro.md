# 2008 Audi A5 Quattro

### Overview
The 2008 Audi A5 Quattro, a stylish coupe known for its premium feel and performance, generally presents a solid reliability record. However, like many vehicles of its era, it is not without its potential issues. Owners have reported some specific concerns primarily related to the powertrain and certain electronic components. Regular maintenance is crucial for longevity, but understanding common failure points can help in proactive ownership and cost management. This analysis focuses on documented, non-wear-and-tear related problems across various configurations of the 2008 A5 Quattro.

---


## Engine

### 2.0L TFSI (BGB/BPG/BPJ/BWE)
**PCV Valve Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Rough idle, increased oil consumption, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the Positive Crankcase Ventilation (PCV) valve assembly.
- **Cost:** $150 - $300

**Timing Chain Tensioner Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Rattling noise from the engine, especially on startup; potential for severe engine damage if ignored.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the timing chain tensioner and guides.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1500


### 3.2L FSI (BKH/CALA)
**Carbon Buildup on Intake Valves**
- **Symptoms:** Reduced engine power, rough idle, decreased fuel efficiency, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Direct intake valve cleaning using specialized tools and solvents.
- **Cost:** $400 - $800

**Oil Consumption**
- **Symptoms:** Higher than normal oil levels needing frequent top-ups.
- **Solution:** Can be due to worn piston rings or valve stem seals; requires diagnosis and potential internal engine repair.
- **Cost:** $1000 - $3000+

### 6-Speed Tiptronic Automatic (09L/09L)
**Mechatronic Unit Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Harsh shifting, slipping gears, transmission warning lights, inability to select gears.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of the mechatronic unit.
- **Cost:** $1500 - $3000

**Torque Converter Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Shuddering at low speeds, slipping, delayed engagement.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the torque converter.
- **Cost:** $1000 - $2000

### Adaptive Air Suspension (Optional)
**Air Spring Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle sagging on one corner, compressor running excessively, suspension warning light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of leaking air springs or seals.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1200 per corner

**Compressor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle not raising, constant compressor noise, suspension warning light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the air suspension compressor.
- **Cost:** $600 - $1000

**Power Window Regulator Failure**

### Summary Verdict
The 2008 Audi A5 Quattro offers a compelling blend of style and performance, with a generally respectable reliability record. Key areas to monitor include potential issues with the PCV valve and timing chain tensioners on the 2.0L TFSI engine, and carbon buildup or oil consumption on the 3.2L FSI. Automatic transmission owners may encounter problems with the mechatronic unit or torque converter. While the standard suspension is robust, optional air suspension systems can be a source of costly repairs. Electrical gremlins, particularly with the infotainment system, are also noted. Overall, with diligent maintenance and awareness of these common issues, the A5 Quattro can be a rewarding ownership experience.
