The 2022 Audi Q2 is a subcompact luxury SUV known for its stylish design, nimble handling, and premium interior. Engine options typically include a range of turbocharged petrol engines. A popular choice is the 1.5-liter TFSI four-cylinder engine, producing around 150 horsepower and 184 lb-ft of torque. Transmission options are typically a 6-speed manual or a 7-speed S tronic dual-clutch automatic. Owners often praise the Q2 for its comfortable ride, user-friendly technology, and upscale feel, however, some find the rear seat space limited. While generally reliable, some common issues have been reported.

**Engine**

**1.5L TFSI**

*   **Oil Consumption**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported higher than expected oil consumption, requiring frequent top-ups between service intervals. This can manifest as low oil level warnings or the need to add oil every few thousand miles.
    *   Solution: Monitoring oil levels regularly is crucial. In some cases, a software update to the engine control unit (ECU) may help. If the problem persists, more invasive solutions like piston ring replacement may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $3000+ depending on the severity and repair needed.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Freezing/Lagging**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The MMI infotainment system may occasionally freeze, lag, or become unresponsive. This can affect navigation, audio, and climate control functions.
    *   Solution: Restarting the system or updating the software may resolve the issue. In some cases, the infotainment unit may need to be replaced.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 (software update) - $1,500+ (unit replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Sensor Malfunctions**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported issues with various sensors, such as parking sensors, lane departure warning sensors, or blind-spot monitoring sensors. These malfunctions can trigger false warnings or disable the affected system.
    *   Solution: Diagnosing the specific sensor fault and replacing the faulty sensor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+ per sensor
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Rattling Noises**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rattling noises emanating from the dashboard, door panels, or rear cargo area. These noises can be distracting, especially on rough roads.
    *   Solution: Identifying the source of the rattle and tightening loose panels, applying sound-dampening material, or replacing worn clips.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+ depending on the complexity.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Water Leaks**
    * Description/Symptoms: Water leaking into the cabin, typically near the sunroof or door seals.
    * Solution: Replacing or resealing the sunroof seal or door seals. Inspecting and cleaning any drain passages.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $150-$750+ depending on severity.
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2022 Audi Q2, while generally a well-received vehicle, does have some reported issues. Engine oil consumption and electrical glitches with the infotainment system appear to be the most prominent concerns. Interior rattles and sensor malfunctions also surface as potential problems. While some issues are minor and easily resolved with software updates or adjustments, others can be more costly, requiring component replacements. Potential buyers should be aware of these common complaints and thoroughly inspect the vehicle before purchasing, and consider extending the warranty for added peace of mind.
