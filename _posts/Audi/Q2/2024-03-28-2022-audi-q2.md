**Introduction:**

The 2022 Audi Q2 is a subcompact SUV that offers a sporty and luxurious driving experience. It is available with a choice of two gasoline engines, a 1.4-liter turbocharged inline-4 and a 2.0-liter turbocharged inline-4, paired with a 6-speed manual transmission or a 7-speed dual-clutch automatic transmission. The Q2 is praised for its sharp handling, comfortable interior, and high-quality materials. However, like any vehicle, it has some reported issues that owners and reviewers have encountered.

**Engine**

**1.4-liter Turbocharged Inline-4**

**Piston Ring Failure**
Description/Symptoms: Excessive oil consumption, blue smoke from exhaust
Solution: Engine rebuild or replacement
Estimated Cost to Fix: $4,000 - $8,000+
Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**6-Speed Manual Transmission**

**Grinding Gears**
Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears, especially into second gear
Solution: Transmission repair or replacement
Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,200 - $3,000
Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

**Front Suspension Strut Failure**
Description/Symptoms: Clunking or knocking noises from front suspension, uneven tire wear
Solution: Replacement of front suspension struts
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,200
Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

**Battery Discharge**
Description/Symptoms: Vehicle battery drains prematurely
Solution: Battery replacement or alternator repair
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+
Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

**Tailgate Latch Failure**
Description/Symptoms: Tailgate door won't open or close properly
Solution: Replacement of tailgate latch
Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500
Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

**Touchscreen Lag**
Description/Symptoms: Infotainment system touchscreen becomes unresponsive or slow
Solution: Software update or touchscreen replacement
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500
Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

Overall, the 2022 Audi Q2 has received positive reviews for its performance and features. However, there are some reported issues that owners may want to be aware of, such as piston ring failure (1.4-liter engine), grinding gears (6-speed manual transmission), and premature battery discharge. While these issues can be costly to fix, the severity and likelihood of occurrence vary depending on the specific issue and maintenance history of the vehicle.
