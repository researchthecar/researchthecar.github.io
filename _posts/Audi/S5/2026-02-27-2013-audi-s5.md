# 2013 Audi S5

### Overview
The 2013 Audi S5, a performance-oriented variant of the A5, offers a compelling blend of sporty dynamics and luxury. It was available with potent V6 and V8 engines, paired with either a manual or automatic transmission. While generally considered a reliable vehicle, like any performance car, it can experience specific issues related to its high-performance components and advanced technology. Owners should be aware of potential concerns in areas such as the engine, transmission, and electrical systems, though many of these are not widespread and can be mitigated with proper maintenance. The S5's sophisticated engineering means that repairs, when needed, can sometimes be costly.

---


## Engine

### 3.0L Supercharged V6
**PCV Valve Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Rough idle, check engine light, oil leaks, whistling noise from engine bay.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the Positive Crankcase Ventilation (PCV) valve assembly.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600

**Supercharger Belt Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of power, whining noise from the front of the engine, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the supercharger drive belt.
- **Cost:** $200 - $400


### 4.2L V8
**Timing Chain Tensioner Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Rattling noise from the engine, especially on startup; check engine light; potential for severe engine damage if ignored.
- **Solution:** Replacement of timing chain tensioners, guides, and potentially the timing chain itself.
- **Cost:** $2000 - $4000+


## Transmission

### 6-Speed Manual
**Clutch Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Slipping clutch, difficulty shifting gears, burning smell.
- **Solution:** Clutch kit replacement (clutch disc, pressure plate, release bearing).
- **Cost:** $1000 - $2000


### 7-Speed S tronic (Dual-Clutch Automatic)
**Mechatronic Unit Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Harsh shifting, transmission warning lights, inability to select gears.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of the mechatronic unit.
- **Cost:** $2500 - $5000+

**Transmission Fluid Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Leaking fluid, rough shifting, potential for transmission damage.
- **Solution:** Repair of leaks, often involving seals or transmission pan.
- **Cost:** $300 - $700

## Electrical

### General Electrical System
**Infotainment System Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Screen freezing, unresponsive controls, Bluetooth connectivity issues.
- **Solution:** Software updates or potential replacement of the MMI unit.
- **Cost:** $500 - $2000

**Sensor Failures**
- **Symptoms:** Various warning lights (ABS, traction control, check engine), erratic gauge readings.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and replacement of faulty sensors (e.g., ABS wheel speed sensor, O2 sensor).
- **Cost:** $200 - $600 per sensor

### Summary Verdict
The 2013 Audi S5 is a capable performance coupe with a generally solid reliability record. However, potential buyers and owners should be aware of specific issues that can arise, particularly with the more complex powertrain components. The 3.0L supercharged V6 is generally more reliable than the 4.2L V8, which can suffer from costly timing chain issues. The S tronic dual-clutch transmission can also be a source of expensive repairs. Electrical gremlins, especially with the infotainment system, are not uncommon. Despite these potential drawbacks, with diligent maintenance and prompt attention to any emerging problems, the 2013 S5 can provide an exhilarating and enjoyable ownership experience.
