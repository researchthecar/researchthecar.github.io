# 2016 Audi S5

### Overview
The 2016 Audi S5, a performance-oriented variant of the A5 lineup, generally offers a robust and engaging driving experience. It is powered by a potent V6 engine and available with either a manual or automatic transmission, catering to driving enthusiasts. While the S5 is built with Audi's characteristic quality, like any performance vehicle, certain components may experience issues over time. This analysis focuses on documented, non-wear-related reliability concerns across key systems, providing insights for potential owners and maintenance planning.

---


## Engine

### 3.0L Supercharged V6 (TFSI)
**PCV Valve Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Rough idle, increased oil consumption, whistling noise from the engine bay.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the Positive Crankcase Ventilation (PCV) valve assembly.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600

**Oil Leaks (Valve Cover Gaskets)**
- **Symptoms:** Visible oil on the engine, burning oil smell, oil spots under the vehicle.
- **Solution:** Replacement of valve cover gaskets.
- **Cost:** $500 - $900

### Summary Verdict
The 2016 Audi S5 demonstrates good overall reliability, particularly with its robust 3.0L supercharged V6 engine. The manual transmission is generally trouble-free. However, owners should be aware of potential issues with the S tronic dual-clutch automatic transmission's mechatronic unit, which can be a significant repair. Electrical gremlins, primarily related to the infotainment system, are also noted. Addressing these potential issues proactively can ensure a more dependable ownership experience for this performance-oriented coupe.
