Okay, here's a breakdown of common issues and complaints associated with the 2022 Audi S5, categorized as you requested.

The 2022 Audi S5 is a stylish and sporty coupe, Sportback (four-door coupe), and Cabriolet (convertible) offering impressive performance and a luxurious interior. All models are powered by a 3.0-liter turbocharged V6 engine producing 349 horsepower and 369 lb-ft of torque, paired exclusively with an 8-speed automatic transmission. Audi's Quattro all-wheel-drive system is standard. Owners often praise the S5 for its engaging driving dynamics, refined interior, and attractive styling. Common dislikes include the sometimes-complicated infotainment system and the limited rear passenger space in the coupe and convertible models.

**Engine**

**3.0L Turbocharged V6 (EA839)**

*   **Coolant Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaks originating from the thermostat housing or water pump area. This can result in low coolant levels, overheating, and white smoke from the exhaust.
    *   Solution: Replacing the thermostat housing and/or water pump.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $1200+ depending on location and shop
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Oil Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks from the valve cover gaskets, timing cover, or rear main seal. Visible oil drips under the car or burning oil smell are common symptoms.
    *   Solution: Replacing the leaking gaskets or seals.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $2000+ depending on the leak location
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**8-Speed Automatic (ZF8HP55A)**

*   **Rough Shifting/Hesitation**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported experiencing rough or delayed shifting, especially during downshifts or when accelerating from a stop. This can manifest as a noticeable "clunk" or hesitation.
    *   Solution: In some cases, a transmission control module (TCM) software update can resolve the issue. In more severe cases, valve body replacement or full transmission replacement may be required.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 (Software Update) - $6000+ (Transmission Replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Torque Converter Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Vibration or shuddering at low speeds or during light acceleration. The torque converter may not be locking up properly.
    *   Solution: Torque converter replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1500 - $3500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Adaptive Damper Malfunction (If Equipped)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: If the vehicle is equipped with the optional adaptive damping suspension, some owners have reported failures of the dampers (shock absorbers). This can result in a harsh or bouncy ride, error messages on the dashboard, and poor handling.
    *   Solution: Replacing the faulty damper(s).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $2000+ per damper, depending on location.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Glitches**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The MMI (Multi Media Interface) infotainment system can experience occasional glitches, freezing, or slow response times. This includes issues with the touchscreen, navigation, and audio system.
    *   Solution: A software update is often the first step. In some cases, the MMI control unit may need to be replaced.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 (Software Update) - $1500+ (MMI Unit Replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Sensor Failures (Parking Sensors, Blind Spot Monitoring)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Failure of parking sensors or the blind spot monitoring system can occur. This results in error messages and the respective systems ceasing to function.
    *   Solution: Replacing the faulty sensor(s).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600+ per sensor, depending on location.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Sunroof Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported issues with the sunroof, including difficulty opening or closing, excessive noise, or water leaks.
    *   Solution: Sunroof motor replacement, track adjustment, or resealing.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Rattles and Squeaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rattles and squeaks originating from the dashboard, door panels, or rear deck area.
    *   Solution: Identifying and isolating the source of the noise. This may involve tightening fasteners, adding felt pads, or replacing worn trim pieces.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+ depending on the location and complexity of the issue.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

The 2022 Audi S5 is a generally reliable vehicle, but like any car, it can experience issues. The most commonly reported problems involve the engine (coolant and oil leaks), transmission (rough shifting), and infotainment system glitches. While some issues, like infotainment glitches, may be minor inconveniences, others, such as engine leaks or transmission problems, can be more serious and costly to repair. Overall, the severity of the issues ranges from minor annoyances to potentially expensive repairs. Pre-purchase inspections are recommended.
