# 2018 Audi S5

### Overview
The 2018 Audi S5, a performance-oriented variant of the A5 lineup, generally presents a strong reliability record, particularly for its powertrain. Owners often praise its potent engine options and sophisticated quattro all-wheel-drive system. However, like many performance vehicles, there are specific areas where potential issues have been noted, primarily related to the advanced technology and high-performance components. This analysis focuses on documented technical reliability concerns beyond routine maintenance, offering insights into common problems and their potential resolutions for owners and prospective buyers.

---


## Engine

### 3.0L TFSI V6 (Turbocharged)
**PCV Valve Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Rough idle, whistling noises, increased oil consumption, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the Positive Crankcase Ventilation (PCV) valve assembly.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600

**Oil Leaks (Valve Cover Gaskets)**
- **Symptoms:** Visible oil on engine components, burning oil smell, oil spots under the vehicle.
- **Solution:** Replacement of valve cover gaskets.
- **Cost:** $500 - $900

### Summary Verdict
The 2018 Audi S5 demonstrates good overall reliability, with its core powertrain components being particularly robust. The primary concerns tend to be related to the advanced electronic systems, such as infotainment glitches and occasional battery drain issues, which are not uncommon in modern luxury vehicles. Engine-specific issues like PCV valve failures and minor oil leaks are documented but generally manageable. The transmission and suspension systems have shown a high degree of reliability. Regular maintenance and prompt attention to any electrical gremlins are recommended to ensure continued dependable performance.
