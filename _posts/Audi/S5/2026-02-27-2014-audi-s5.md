# 2014 Audi S5

### Overview
The 2014 Audi S5, a performance-oriented variant of the A5, offers a compelling blend of sporty dynamics and premium comfort. It was available with a potent V6 engine and either a manual or automatic transmission, catering to driving enthusiasts. Generally, the S5 is considered a reliable vehicle, especially when properly maintained. However, like any performance car, certain components may require more attention over time. This analysis focuses on documented technical issues beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential areas of concern for owners and prospective buyers.

---


## Engine

### 3.0L Supercharged V6 (TFSI)
**PCV Valve Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Rough idle, whistling noise, oil leaks, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the Positive Crankcase Ventilation (PCV) valve assembly.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600

**Supercharger Belt Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Reduced engine power, whining noise from the engine bay.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the supercharger drive belt.
- **Cost:** $200 - $400

### Summary Verdict
The 2014 Audi S5 demonstrates good overall reliability, particularly concerning its powertrain options. The 3.0L supercharged V6 engine is robust, though owners should be aware of potential PCV valve issues and the need for timely supercharger belt replacement. Both the manual and automatic transmissions are generally dependable. Electrical gremlins, primarily related to the infotainment system, are the most commonly cited non-wear-and-tear issues. Exterior and interior components are typically well-built and hold up well. With diligent maintenance, the 2014 S5 should provide a rewarding ownership experience.
