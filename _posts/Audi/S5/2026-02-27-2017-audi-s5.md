# 2017 Audi S5

### Overview
The 2017 Audi S5, a performance-oriented grand tourer, generally exhibits a strong reliability record, reflecting Audi's commitment to engineering quality. This model year, particularly with its potent V6 engine and available quattro all-wheel-drive system, offers a compelling blend of power and refinement. While most owners report a positive experience, like any performance vehicle, certain components may require attention over time. This analysis focuses on documented technical issues beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential areas of concern for owners and prospective buyers.

---


## Engine

### 3.0L TFSI Supercharged V6
**PCV Valve Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Rough idle, increased oil consumption, potential for oil leaks, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the Positive Crankcase Ventilation (PCV) valve assembly.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600

**Oil Leaks (Valve Cover Gaskets)**
- **Symptoms:** Visible oil on the engine block, burning oil smell, low oil level.
- **Solution:** Replacement of valve cover gaskets.
- **Cost:** $500 - $900

### Summary Verdict
The 2017 Audi S5 is a well-built performance coupe with a generally positive reliability outlook. The primary areas that may require attention are related to the engine's PCV system and potential oil leaks from valve cover gaskets, which are not uncommon for performance engines of this era. Electrical issues are typically minor and often software-related. The transmission, suspension, exterior, and interior components are largely robust. With diligent maintenance and addressing any early signs of the noted engine issues, the 2017 S5 should provide a rewarding ownership experience for enthusiasts.
