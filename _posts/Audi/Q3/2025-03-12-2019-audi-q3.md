The 2019 Audi Q3 represents the second generation of Audi's subcompact luxury crossover. It offers a blend of practicality, technology, and style that appeals to a wide range of buyers. The 2019 Q3 is equipped with a 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine, producing 228 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque. The sole transmission option for the 2019 Q3 is an 8-speed automatic. Owners generally appreciate the Q3's upscale interior, nimble handling, and user-friendly technology. Some common complaints revolve around the somewhat firm ride, the higher price point compared to competitors, and potential issues with the infotainment system.

**Engine**

**2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4 (228 hp, 258 lb-ft)**

*   **Coolant Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Coolant levels drop quickly, overheating, coolant smell. Visible coolant leaks may be present near the water pump or coolant reservoir.
    *   Solution: Replace leaking components (water pump, hoses, reservoir). Pressure test the coolant system.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $1200+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Oil Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Oil spots under the car, burning oil smell, low oil level.
    *   Solution: Replacing valve cover gaskets, oil pan gasket, or rear main seal depending on the source of the leak.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**8-Speed Automatic**

*   **Hesitation/Rough Shifting**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Delay when accelerating from a stop, harsh or jerky shifts between gears, particularly at lower speeds.
    *   Solution: Software update/reprogramming of the transmission control module (TCM). In severe cases, valve body replacement or complete transmission replacement might be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $500 (software update), $800 - $3000+ (valve body/transmission replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Premature Bushing Wear**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Clunking or rattling noises from the suspension, especially when going over bumps. Steering may feel loose or imprecise.
    *   Solution: Replacement of worn bushings in control arms, sway bar links, or other suspension components.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: System freezing, unresponsive touchscreen, Bluetooth connectivity problems, navigation errors.
    *   Solution: Software updates. If the issue persists, the infotainment unit may need to be replaced.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 (software update) - $1500+ (unit replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Sensor Malfunctions (e.g., Parking Sensors)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: False alarms, failure to detect objects, error messages.
    *   Solution: Diagnose the faulty sensor and replace it.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Sunroof Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Sunroof motor failing, glass panel not sealing correctly, water leaks.
    *   Solution: Replacing the sunroof motor, adjusting the sunroof panel, or replacing the weather stripping.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300-$1000
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Rattling/Squeaking Noises**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Various rattles and squeaks emanating from the dashboard, door panels, or rear cargo area, especially when driving on rough roads.
    *   Solution: Locate and isolate the source of the noise. This often involves tightening loose panels, adding sound dampening material, or replacing worn clips.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: Highly variable, depending on the location and complexity of the repair. $50 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2019 Audi Q3, while generally reliable, can experience issues with the engine (coolant and oil leaks), transmission (rough shifting), suspension (bushing wear), electrical system (infotainment glitches), and interior trim (rattles). The severity ranges from minor inconveniences to more costly repairs, particularly with the transmission. Potential buyers should be aware of these common complaints and ensure a thorough inspection of the vehicle before purchase, paying close attention to the infotainment system and the smoothness of the transmission.
