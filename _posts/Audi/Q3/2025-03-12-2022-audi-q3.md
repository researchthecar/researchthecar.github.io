The 2022 Audi Q3 is a subcompact luxury SUV known for its stylish design, nimble handling, and user-friendly technology. Engine options include a 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder offered in two states of tune. The 40 TFSI engine produces 184 horsepower and 221 lb-ft of torque, while the 45 TFSI engine generates 228 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque. Both engines are paired with an 8-speed automatic transmission. People generally appreciate the Q3's upscale interior, comfortable ride, and advanced features. Some criticisms include its relatively small cargo area and occasionally hesitant throttle response.

**Engine**

**2.0L Turbocharged 4-Cylinder (40 TFSI - 184 hp/221 lb-ft)**

*   **No Issues Found**

**2.0L Turbocharged 4-Cylinder (45 TFSI - 228 hp/258 lb-ft)**

*   **Oil Consumption**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported higher than expected oil consumption, requiring more frequent top-offs between oil changes. Symptoms include low oil warning light illumination and the need to add oil every few thousand miles.
    *   Solution: Monitor oil levels regularly. If excessive consumption is confirmed by an Audi dealership, potential fixes may include replacing PCV valve, valve stem seals, or piston rings, or performing an oil consumption test to pinpoint the root cause.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $5000+ depending on the severity and needed repairs.
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**8-Speed Automatic Transmission**

*   **Rough Shifting/Hesitation**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported experiencing rough or jerky shifting, particularly at lower speeds or during downshifts. Hesitation when accelerating from a stop has also been mentioned.
    *   Solution: A software update from the dealership might resolve the issue. In more severe cases, transmission valve body replacement or a full transmission rebuild may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 (Software Update) - $5,000+ (Transmission Rebuild/Replacement)
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **No Issues Found**

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Freezing/Lag**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The MMI infotainment system can sometimes freeze, become unresponsive, or exhibit significant lag, particularly when using Apple CarPlay or Android Auto.
    *   Solution: Restarting the system might temporarily resolve the issue. Check for software updates from Audi. In persistent cases, a hardware replacement of the infotainment unit might be required.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 (Software Update) - $1,500+ (Infotainment Unit Replacement)
    * Recalls: None Found

*   **Sensor Malfunctions**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Malfunctions in various sensors, such as parking sensors or blind spot monitoring sensors, have been reported. Symptoms can include false alarms, system deactivation, or error messages on the dashboard.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis by an Audi technician is necessary to identify the faulty sensor. Replacement of the sensor is the likely fix.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+ per sensor depending on the specific sensor.
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **No Issues Found**

**Interior**

*   **Rattling/Squeaking Noises**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported experiencing rattling or squeaking noises emanating from various parts of the interior, such as the dashboard, door panels, or rear hatch area.
    *   Solution: Identifying the source of the noise is crucial. Dealership technicians or experienced mechanics can use specialized tools to locate and address the source, which may involve tightening loose components, applying felt tape to prevent rubbing, or replacing damaged parts.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+ depending on the severity and location of the noise.
    * Recalls: None Found

**Overall Summary**

The 2022 Audi Q3 appears to have a relatively low frequency of major mechanical issues. The most commonly reported problems revolve around the infotainment system, transmission behavior, and potential oil consumption for the 45 TFSI engine. While the potential for expensive repairs exists (especially for transmission or engine-related issues), many problems are minor and can be addressed relatively inexpensively. Potential buyers should be aware of these issues and carefully inspect the vehicle, especially the infotainment system, and request service records to identify any past problems.
