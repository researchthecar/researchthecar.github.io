# 2019 Audi Q3 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used luxury subcompact SUV, evaluating powertrain options and common owner-reported grievances helps ensure a dependable ownership experience. While many owners enjoy the refined styling and modern tech, owner reports highlight specific areas—such as intermittent fuel-related stalls and electrical anomalies—that warrant careful inspection before purchase.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged I4

This 2.0-liter turbocharged inline-4 engine produces around 228 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque, providing spirited acceleration for daily driving. It pairs standard Quattro all-wheel drive with smooth power delivery, though prospective buyers should verify proper fuel system operation.

**Engine Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may unexpectedly lose power or stall while actively driving, presenting a sudden safety hazard.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified mechanic inspect the fuel pump and powertrain control modules for intermittent faults or stored diagnostic codes.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Tiptronic Automatic

The conventional 8-speed automatic transmission delivers seamless gear changes and works in harmony with the standard all-wheel-drive system. It generally offers solid reliability when routine fluid maintenance intervals are observed.

## Electrical

### Infotainment Touchscreen

Cabin electronics manage various comfort and safety features, though occasional software glitches can affect display responsiveness.

**Touchscreen Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The infotainment display may remain blank or become unresponsive upon startup, preventing the rearview camera from activating.
- **Solution:** Software updates or module resets performed by a dealership can often resolve intermittent screen unresponsiveness.

## Interior

### Sunroof and Drainage

The interior features upscale materials and a spacious layout, though cabin integrity relies heavily on clear weather stripping and drainage paths.

**Water Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Occupants may notice water dripping from the sunroof panel or headliner during heavy rain, potentially leading to damp interior trim.
- **Solution:** Clear clogged sunroof drain tubes or inspect the roof sealing components to prevent moisture intrusion.

## Safety

### Forward Collision Avoidance

Advanced driver assistance systems provide added peace of mind, but sensor calibration is critical to avoid unwarranted automated braking interventions.

**False Activation**
- **Symptoms:** The pre-sense warning system may unexpectedly trigger emergency braking and tighten seatbelts without an apparent obstacle ahead.
- **Solution:** Ensure camera and radar sensors are clean and check with a dealer for any applicable sensor calibration updates.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V027000: Steering:Critical Fasteners](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Audi&model=Q3&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** The circlip can detach and catch on surrounding components, causing the steering system to lock and increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Audi will notify the owner, and a dealer will replace the steering rack, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V273000: Air Bags:Frontal:Passenger Side:Inflator Module](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Audi&model=Q3&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** The passenger air bag may not properly deploy through the instrument panel tear seam. In the event of a crash, the passenger air bag may not properly deploy and inflate, and small plastic parts may enter the cabin, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Audi will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the instrument panel and replace it, if necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V815000: Tires:Pressure Monitoring And Regulating Systems](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Audi&model=Q3&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** The tire pressure monitoring system may not detect an air pressure loss in adequate time when deflating occurs among all four tires simultaneously. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 138, "Tire Pressure Monitoring Systems." An undetected sudden loss of pressure in all four tires.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the TPMS software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

Overall, this vehicle offers an appealing blend of luxury, utility, and confident performance. Prospective buyers should thoroughly test drive the vehicle to check for any hesitation or unexpected engine stalls, inspect the cabin for signs of moisture from the sunroof, and ensure all electronic displays and safety sensors function properly.
