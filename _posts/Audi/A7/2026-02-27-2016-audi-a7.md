# 2016 Audi A7 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2016 Audi A7 combines striking fastback styling, a luxurious interior, and powerful supercharged performance. While owners generally praise its driving dynamics and refinement, used-car buyers should review potential owner-reported concerns ranging from minor electrical glitches to notable steering and airbag sensor complaints.

---

## Engine

### 3.0L Supercharged V6 (333 hp)

The 3.0-liter supercharged V6 produces 333 horsepower and 325 lb-ft of torque, offering brisk acceleration and smooth power delivery. It is generally robust, though routine checks for minor coolant loss and fuel odors are recommended during a pre-purchase inspection.

**Coolant Consumption**
- **Symptoms:** Dried coolant residue around the engine bay and a need to frequently top off coolant every few thousand miles.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified mechanic pressure test the cooling system and inspect for slow leaks around fittings or the supercharger intercoolers.

**Fuel Odor**
- **Symptoms:** A noticeable smell of gasoline inside the cabin when the vehicle is idling with the climate control running.
- **Solution:** Inspect fuel lines, injectors, and related components for leaks, and check against any applicable manufacturer service bulletins.

## Transmission

### 7-Speed S-Tronic Dual-Clutch Automatic

The 7-speed dual-clutch automatic transmission sends power to Audi's quattro all-wheel-drive system with rapid, crisp shifts. It provides a sporty driving feel, though prospective buyers should test drive to ensure smooth engagement from a stop.

## Steering

### Electronic Power Steering

Issues related to electronic power steering assist failures have been reported by some drivers, presenting a significant safety concern if power steering is lost.

**Steering Loss Warning**
- **Symptoms:** Sudden loss of steering assistance accompanied by a dashboard warning message stating 'Do Not Drive Vehicle, Steering Defective.'
- **Solution:** Diagnose electronic power steering control modules and software calibration, which may require professional servicing or component replacement.

## Safety

### Airbag System

Owner reports highlight intermittent faults with passenger airbag status indicators, which require careful inspection to ensure proper safety system functionality.

**Passenger Airbag Sensor Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** The passenger airbag light repeatedly turns on and off while driving, even with a passenger seated, or illuminates when empty.
- **Solution:** Inspect the occupant detection sensor mat in the passenger seat and associated wiring harnesses for faults.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V035000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Fuel Injection System:Fuel Rail](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Audi&model=A7&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** A fuel leak in the presence of an ignition source can increase risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Audi has notified owners, and dealers will replace the left and right fuel rail, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V370000: Air Bags:Sensor:Occupant Classification](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Audi&model=A7&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** Stress or wear of the body-sensing mat within the front passenger seat may cause the Passenger Occupant Detection System (PODS) control module to malfunction. If the PODS module malfunctions, the front passenger air bag may not deploy properly in the event of a crash, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Volkswagen will notify owners, and dealers will install a PODS system repair kit, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V293000: Air Bags:Side/Window](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Audi&model=A7&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** The affected vehicles have head curtain air bags that may not deploy properly in the event of a crash. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 226, "Ejection Mitigation." ## Summary Verdict

The 2016 Audi A7 delivers an impressive blend of luxury and performance, but prospective buyers should perform a thorough pre-purchase inspection. Pay close attention to cooling system integrity, electronic steering functionality, and airbag sensor status to avoid unexpected repair bills.
