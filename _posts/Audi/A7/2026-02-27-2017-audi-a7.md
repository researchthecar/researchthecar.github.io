# 2017 Audi A7 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2017 Audi A7, buyers will find a luxury four-door fastback that pairs striking styling with refined performance. While overall owner complaint volume is very low for this model year, prospective buyers should still carefully evaluate standard pre-purchase inspection points, particularly regarding complex electronic features and powertrain operation.

---

## Engine

### 3.0L Supercharged V6

The 3.0-liter supercharged V6 engine delivers robust power with approximately 333 horsepower and smooth acceleration. It is generally regarded as a durable powerplant, though occasional driver reports highlight quirks with electronic driver aids.

**Start/Stop System Engagement**
- **Symptoms:** The automatic start/stop fuel-saving engine feature engages prematurely before the vehicle comes to a complete stop, creating an aggressive braking sensation.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified technician inspect the vehicle's engine management software and sensor inputs for potential updates or calibrations.

## Transmission

### 7-Speed S-tronic Dual-Clutch Automatic

The 7-speed S-tronic dual-clutch automatic transmission delivers rapid gear shifts and pairs with Audi's quattro all-wheel-drive system. Routine fluid maintenance is critical to ensuring long-term reliability and smooth operation.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V035000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Fuel Injection System:Fuel Rail](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Audi&model=A7&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** A fuel leak in the presence of an ignition source can increase risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Audi has notified owners, and dealers will replace the left and right fuel rail, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V370000: Air Bags:Sensor:Occupant Classification](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Audi&model=A7&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** Stress or wear of the body-sensing mat within the front passenger seat may cause the Passenger Occupant Detection System (PODS) control module to malfunction. If the PODS module malfunctions, the front passenger air bag may not deploy properly in the event of a crash, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Volkswagen will notify owners, and dealers will install a PODS system repair kit, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V955000: Seat Belts:Pretensioner](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Audi&model=A7&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** Additionally, certain 2017 Audi Q7, A4 Sedan, A4 Allroad, and 2018 Audi Q5 vehicles are being recalled because the seat belt pretensioners may not activate properly. In the event of a crash, if the air bags and/or the seat belt pretensioners do not inflate or function properly, the vehicle occupants have an increased risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Volkswagen and Audi will notify owners, and dealers will replace the affected air bags and seat belt pretensioners, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V293000: Air Bags:Side/Window](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Audi&model=A7&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** The affected vehicles have head curtain air bags that may not deploy properly in the event of a crash. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 226, "Ejection Mitigation." The 2017 Audi A7 offers an appealing mix of luxury, performance, and distinctive design for used-car buyers. Because complaints are sparse, a thorough pre-purchase inspection focusing on the vehicle's mechanical and electronic systems remains the best way to ensure a confident purchase.
