## 1992 Audi 90 Quattro

The 1992 Audi 90 Quattro is a mid-size luxury sedan that was offered with a variety of engine and transmission options. It was known for its performance, handling, and spacious interior. However, like all vehicles, it had its share of issues.

### Engine

- **2.3L 5-cylinder**
    - **Oil leaks from camshaft seal:** Oil leaks from the camshaft seal, causing a burning oil smell and possible engine damage.
      - Solution: Replace the camshaft seal.
      - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
      - Recalls: 97V130000
    - **Intake manifold gasket failure:** The intake manifold gasket can fail, causing coolant leaks, overheating, and loss of power.
      - Solution: Replace the intake manifold gasket.
      - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800+
      - Recalls: None Found
- **2.8L V6**
    - **Timing belt failure:** The timing belt can fail, causing catastrophic engine damage.
      - Solution: Replace the timing belt according to the manufacturer's recommended intervals.
      - Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $1,500+
      - Recalls: None Found

### Transmission

- **5-speed manual:**
    - **Shifting problems:** The transmission may experience shifting problems, such as difficulty shifting into or out of gear.
      - Solution: Repair or replace the transmission components causing the issue.
      - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
      - Recalls: None Found
- **4-speed automatic:**
    - **Transmission slipping:** The transmission may slip or shudder during gear changes.
      - Solution: Repair or replace the transmission components causing the issue.
      - Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
      - Recalls: None Found

### Suspension

- **Front suspension noise:** The front suspension can develop noise, such as clunking or rattling, while driving over bumps or rough roads.
  - Solution: Replace the worn or damaged suspension components.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
  - Recalls: None Found

### Electrical

- **Electrical gremlins:** The electrical system can experience various issues, such as flickering lights, power window problems, or instrument panel malfunctions.
  - Solution: Diagnose and repair the faulty electrical components.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the issue
  - Recalls: None Found

### Exterior

- **Rust:** The body of the vehicle can develop rust, particularly in areas where the paint has been damaged or chipped.
  - Solution: Repair the rust by sanding, treating, and repainting the affected areas.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+
  - Recalls: None Found

### Interior

- **Power window failure:** The power windows can fail to operate, either due to electrical issues or mechanical problems.
  - Solution: Repair or replace the faulty power window components.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+
  - Recalls: None Found
- **HVAC issues:** The heating, ventilation, and air conditioning (HVAC) system may malfunction, causing problems with heating, cooling, or air flow.
  - Solution: Diagnose and repair the faulty HVAC components.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the issue
  - Recalls: None Found

### Summary

The 1992 Audi 90 Quattro was a capable and comfortable sedan, but it was not without its issues. Engine leaks, transmission problems, suspension noise, electrical gremlins, rust, and interior failures were all potential concerns for owners. The severity and cost of these issues varied, but some could be costly to repair. It is important to be aware of these potential problems before purchasing a used Audi 90 Quattro and to budget for repairs if necessary.
