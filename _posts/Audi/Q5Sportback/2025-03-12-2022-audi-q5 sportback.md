The 2022 Audi Q5 Sportback is a stylish and sporty compact SUV coupe. The most common engine is the 2.0-liter turbocharged inline-4 producing 261 horsepower and 273 lb-ft of torque. A plug-in hybrid (PHEV) variant is also available, combining the 2.0-liter turbo engine with an electric motor for a combined output of 362 horsepower. All Q5 Sportback models come standard with a 7-speed S tronic dual-clutch automatic transmission and Quattro all-wheel drive. Owners generally appreciate the Q5 Sportback for its sleek design, comfortable interior, and refined driving experience. Common complaints revolve around the sloping roofline impacting rear passenger headroom and cargo space compared to the standard Q5, as well as the infotainment system complexity.

**Engine**

**2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4 (45 TFSI)**

*   **Excessive Oil Consumption**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported higher than expected oil consumption, requiring frequent top-offs between scheduled oil changes. Symptoms include low oil level warnings and the need to add oil every few thousand miles.
    *   Solution: In some cases, a software update to the engine control unit (ECU) can help mitigate the issue. In more severe cases, replacement of piston rings or even the entire engine may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 (ECU Update) - $5,000+ (Engine Replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Turbocharger Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, whistling sound from the engine bay, check engine light. This is a less common but reported issue.
    *   Solution: Turbocharger replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4 Plug-in Hybrid (55 TFSI e)**

*   **Electric Motor Malfunction**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Reduced electric driving range, failure to switch to electric mode, error messages related to the electric motor or battery.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis and repair of the electric motor or related components. Replacement of the electric motor may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $6,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**7-Speed S tronic Dual-Clutch Automatic**

*   **Harsh Shifting/Hesitation**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rough or jerky shifts, particularly at lower speeds. Hesitation or delay when accelerating from a stop or during gear changes.
    *   Solution: A software update to the transmission control module (TCM) may resolve the issue. In some cases, clutch replacement or valve body repair/replacement may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 (Software Update) - $4,000+ (Clutch Replacement/Valve Body)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Mechatronic Unit Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Complete loss of gear engagement, transmission warning lights, inability to shift gears.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the mechatronic unit.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,500 - $4,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Air Suspension Issues (If Equipped)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: If equipped with the optional air suspension, owners have reported issues with air leaks, compressor failure, and uneven ride height.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the affected air spring, compressor, or associated components.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $2,000+ per component
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Freezing/Crashing**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The MMI infotainment system can freeze, crash, or become unresponsive. This can affect navigation, audio, and other vehicle functions.
    *   Solution: A software update may resolve the issue. In some cases, replacement of the MMI unit may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 (Software Update) - $1,500+ (MMI Unit Replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Sensor Malfunctions (Parking Sensors, Blind Spot Monitoring)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Erratic or inaccurate readings from parking sensors or blind spot monitoring system. False alarms or failure to detect obstacles.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis and replacement of the faulty sensor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+ per sensor
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Panoramic Sunroof Problems**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rattling noises, difficulty opening or closing, water leaks.
    *   Solution: Lubrication of the sunroof tracks, repair or replacement of the sunroof motor or seals.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $1,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Rattling Noises**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Various rattling noises from the dashboard, door panels, or rear cargo area.
    *   Solution: Locating and securing the source of the rattle. This may involve adding padding or tightening loose components.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $500+ (depending on the complexity)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2022 Audi Q5 Sportback, while generally a reliable vehicle, has potential issues ranging from minor infotainment glitches and rattles to more serious engine and transmission problems. Engine issues like excessive oil consumption and turbocharger failure can be expensive to address, as can transmission issues related to the mechatronic unit. Air suspension issues, if equipped, can also add to the cost of ownership. It's crucial for prospective buyers to be aware of these potential issues and to have the vehicle thoroughly inspected before purchase.
