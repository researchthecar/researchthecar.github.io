### Adaptive Air Suspension (Optional)
**Air Compressor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle may sag on one or more corners, suspension warning light illuminated, uneven ride height.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the air compressor unit.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1500

**Air Spring Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Gradual sagging of the vehicle, especially after sitting for a period, loss of ride height control.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of leaking air springs.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1200 per spring


## Electrical

### Infotainment System
**Software Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Screen freezing, unresponsiveness, connectivity issues with Apple CarPlay/Android Auto, navigation errors.
- **Solution:** Software updates, system resets, or potential module replacement.
- **Cost:** $100 - $500


### Advanced Driver-Assistance Systems (ADAS)
**Sensor Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** False warnings from adaptive cruise control, lane keeping assist, or parking sensors; system deactivation.
- **Solution:** Cleaning of sensors, recalibration, or replacement of faulty sensors/modules.
- **Cost:** $200 - $1000

### Summary Verdict
The 2022 Audi Q5 Sportback demonstrates strong overall reliability, with its core powertrain (2.0L Turbocharged engine and 7-speed S tronic transmission) showing no significant widespread issues. The primary areas where owners might encounter technical concerns are with optional features, particularly the Adaptive Air Suspension, which can be prone to compressor failures or air spring leaks. Electrical systems, especially the infotainment and ADAS, may occasionally experience software glitches or sensor issues that typically require updates or recalibration. Exterior and interior components are generally robust. For owners opting for the air suspension, budgeting for potential repairs is advisable. Otherwise, the Q5 Sportback is expected to be a dependable luxury SUV.
