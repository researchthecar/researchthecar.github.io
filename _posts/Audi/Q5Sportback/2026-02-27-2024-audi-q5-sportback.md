# 2024 Audi Q5 Sportback Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a pre-owned 2024 Audi Q5 Sportback, buyers will find a stylish and technologically advanced luxury crossover coupe. While early owner feedback points to a generally robust driving experience, a few recurring electronic and driver-assistance quirks have surfaced in driver logs. Reviewing vehicle history and performing a thorough test drive of all safety and convenience features will help ensure a trouble-free ownership experience.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4 (45 TFSI)

This 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine produces approximately 261 horsepower and 273 lb-ft of torque. It provides brisk acceleration for daily driving while maintaining competitive fuel efficiency for a luxury crossover.

### 2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4 Plug-In Hybrid (55 TFSI e)

This plug-in hybrid powertrain combines a 2.0-liter turbo engine with an electric motor to generate a combined 362 horsepower and 369 lb-ft of torque. It offers brisk performance and electric-only driving capability, though buyers should verify battery health on used models.

## Transmission

### 7-Speed S tronic Dual-Clutch Automatic

This 7-speed dual-clutch automatic transmission routes power smoothly to Audi's quattro all-wheel-drive system. It delivers crisp, rapid gear changes under normal driving conditions.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

The braking system delivers strong stopping power suited for a luxury vehicle, though routine pad and rotor checks are recommended.

**Premature Rotor Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Some owners have experienced accelerated wear on brake rotors earlier in the vehicle's lifecycle than expected under normal driving conditions.
- **Solution:** Inspect brake pads and rotors during a pre-purchase inspection and replace components as necessary.

## Electrical

### Liftgate

Exterior panels and power accessories generally function well, though automated features require familiarization.

**Unexpected Tailgate Closure**
- **Symptoms:** The hands-free kick sensor or key fob proximity can inadvertently trigger the automatic liftgate to close while loading or unloading cargo.
- **Solution:** Be mindful of key fob placement and foot sensor activation zones while accessing the rear cargo area.

## Safety

### Forward Collision Avoidance

Active safety systems provide added peace of mind, though sensor sensitivity can occasionally cause erratic behavior.

**Pre-Sense False Braking**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers report that the Audi Pre-Sense system occasionally triggers sudden, unexpected emergency braking events when no obstacles are present, creating a risk of being rear-ended.
- **Solution:** Have a dealer check for software updates related to driver assistance systems and adjust sensitivity settings if available.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V294000: Engine](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Audi&model=Q5+Sportback&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** The screws can loosen, allowing oil to leak and increasing the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the screws as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2024 Audi Q5 Sportback remains a compelling choice for luxury crossover buyers, blending refined performance with modern amenities. While isolated complaints highlight occasional sensitivity in automated safety systems and power liftgates, these are largely manageable characteristics rather than systemic mechanical failures. Prospective buyers should prioritize a comprehensive test drive and a pre-purchase inspection focusing on electronics and brakes.
