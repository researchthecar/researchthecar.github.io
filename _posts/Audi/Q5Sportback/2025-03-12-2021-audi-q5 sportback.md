Here's a breakdown of common issues and complaints related to the 2021 Audi Q5 Sportback, formatted for your website:

The 2021 Audi Q5 Sportback is a stylish and sporty compact SUV coupe, offering a blend of practicality and sleek design. It's available with a 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine producing 261 horsepower and 273 lb-ft of torque, paired with a 7-speed S tronic dual-clutch automatic transmission. Some owners praise the Q5 Sportback for its attractive styling, comfortable interior, and user-friendly technology. Common criticisms include a compromised rear headroom compared to the standard Q5, a higher price point for similar features, and occasional electronic glitches.

**Engine**

**2.0L Turbocharged Four-Cylinder (45 TFSI)**

*   **Excessive Oil Consumption**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners report higher than expected oil consumption, requiring more frequent top-ups between scheduled oil changes. The low oil warning light may illuminate.
    *   Solution: In some cases, Audi dealers have performed oil consumption tests and replaced piston rings or short blocks to address the issue.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $6,000+ (depending on the severity and repair needed).
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Turbocharger Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, whistling or whining noises from the engine bay, check engine light illumination.
    *   Solution: Turbocharger replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**7-Speed S tronic Dual-Clutch Automatic**

*   **Rough Shifting/Hesitation**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners experience jerky or delayed shifts, particularly at lower speeds. Hesitation or clunking noises can occur during acceleration or deceleration.
    *   Solution: Software updates to the transmission control module (TCM) or, in more severe cases, transmission replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 (for software updates) - $6,000+ (for replacement).
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Adaptive Damper Malfunction (if equipped)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Erratic ride quality, suspension warning lights illuminated, uneven handling. This issue is specifically relevant to vehicles equipped with the optional adaptive damping suspension system.
    *   Solution: Replacement of faulty dampers or associated control modules.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $2,000+ per damper
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Multimedia Interface (MMI) System Freezing/Crashing**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The MMI system (which controls navigation, audio, and vehicle settings) may freeze, crash, or become unresponsive. The screen may go blank or display error messages.
    *   Solution: Software updates, resetting the system, or, in some cases, replacement of the MMI unit.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 (for software updates) - $1,500+ (for MMI unit replacement).
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Sensor failures related to Driver Assistance Systems**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Audi Side Assist(Blind Spot Monitoring), Audi Pre Sense (Forward Collision Warning), Lane Assist errors, and Adaptive Cruise Control may display error messages or not function properly.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis and replacement of faulty sensors (radar, camera, ultrasonic)
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+ (depending on the sensor)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Tail Light Condensation**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Moisture buildup within the tail light housings.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the tail light assembly. Some owners have had success with warranty claims.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+ per tail light
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Sunroof Rattling/Squeaking**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Annoying rattling or squeaking noises coming from the sunroof area while driving, especially on uneven roads.
    *   Solution: Adjustment, lubrication, or replacement of sunroof components.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Seat Heater Malfunction**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Seat heaters not working, inconsistent heating, or overheating.
    *   Solution: Replacement of heating elements, control modules, or wiring repair.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+ per seat
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2021 Audi Q5 Sportback generally proves to be a reliable vehicle, but some owners encounter issues, particularly with the engine (oil consumption), transmission (rough shifting), and electrical systems (MMI). While many of these issues can be resolved with software updates or minor repairs, more serious problems such as turbocharger failure or transmission replacement can be costly. Potential buyers should be aware of these potential issues and consider extended warranty options for added peace of mind.
