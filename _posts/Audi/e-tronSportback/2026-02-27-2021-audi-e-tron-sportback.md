# 2021 Audi e-tron Sportback Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2021 Audi e-tron Sportback delivers sleek styling and refined electric performance, but prospective buyers should carefully review owner-reported reliability data. While luxury electric vehicles offer smooth and quiet operation, early model years can present unique challenges surrounding high-voltage electrical architecture and associated software controls. Taking time to inspect service history and verify that all necessary updates have been performed can help ensure a more confident ownership experience.

---

## Engine

### Dual Electric Motor Powertrain (355 - 402 hp)

The e-tron Sportback uses dual asynchronous electric motors powered by a high-voltage lithium-ion battery pack, producing up to 402 horsepower with boost engaged. This all-wheel-drive electric powertrain delivers instantaneous torque and smooth acceleration, though overall longevity heavily relies on proper battery thermal management and software maintenance.

## Transmission

### Single-Speed Fixed Gear Transmission

Power is routed to the wheels via a lightweight single-speed planetary gearbox paired with each axle for permanent Quattro all-wheel drive. This fixed-gear setup is mechanically straightforward and eliminates traditional multi-speed shifting hesitation, contributing to a seamless driving dynamic.

## Electrical

### High-Voltage Electrical System

The electrical and infotainment architecture manages critical vehicle functions and driver displays, but owners have reported intermittent software glitches and high-voltage warnings.

**Electrical System Malfunction and Drive Disengagement**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may experience sudden warning messages on the instrument cluster regarding electrical system faults, occasionally resulting in a complete loss of propulsion or vehicle shutdown while driving.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified dealership inspect the high-voltage battery modules, charging port seals, and related control software for faults or moisture intrusion.

**MMI Display Blanking**
- **Symptoms:** The Multi Media Interface (MMI) screens can go blank intermittently while driving, causing temporary loss of access to navigation, climate controls, and vehicle settings.
- **Solution:** Software updates or replacement of faulty USB interfaces and display modules by an authorized service center may be required to restore stable operation.
- **Cost:** $1,045.50

## Exterior

### Sunroof

Exterior glass and trim components generally hold up well, though rare reports highlight incidents of unprovoked sunroof glass breakage.

**Spontaneous Sunroof Glass Fracture**
- **Symptoms:** The panoramic glass roof may shatter unexpectedly with a loud noise while driving, even without any apparent impact from external objects.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the glass panel assembly by a specialized technician is necessary when structural glass failure occurs.

## Safety

### Braking and Collision Avoidance

Advanced safety systems and regenerative braking mechanisms are designed to enhance security, though software anomalies can occasionally trigger unexpected behaviors.

**Partial Brake Failure and Phantom Braking**
- **Symptoms:** Operators have reported instances of reduced initial brake pedal response or unexpected phantom braking events where the vehicle abruptly slows down while moving at low speeds.
- **Solution:** Diagnostic evaluations by certified technicians can check brake software calibration and sensor alignment to ensure proper responsiveness.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V900000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Audi&model=e-tron+Sportback&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 111, "Rear Visibility." A rearview image that does not display reduces the driver's view behind the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V240000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Pedals And Linkages](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Audi&model=e-tron+Sportback&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** Brake pedal detachment can cause a loss of brake function and increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and tighten the pushrod screw joint as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2021 Audi e-tron Sportback is an appealing choice for buyers entering the luxury EV market, offering impressive comfort and everyday usability. However, because early electric models rely heavily on complex software and high-voltage systems, verifying a comprehensive dealer service history is essential. Prospective owners should ensure all software updates have been applied and test every electrical feature thoroughly before finalizing a purchase.
