**Introduction:**

The 2020 Audi e-tron Sportback is a fully electric luxury SUV. It features a single electric motor that produces 355 horsepower and 414 lb-ft of torque. The e-tron Sportback accelerates from 0 to 60 mph in 5.7 seconds and has a top speed of 124 mph. The estimated range of the e-tron Sportback is up to 218 miles on a single charge.

**Electrical:**

* **12-volt battery failures**
    * Description/Symptoms: The 12-volt battery may fail prematurely, leading to problems starting the vehicle or operating the electrical system.
    * Solution: Replace the 12-volt battery.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100-$250
    * Recalls: NHTSA Campaign Number: 20V326000
* **Software glitches**
    * Description/Symptoms: The vehicle's infotainment system or other electronic components may experience software glitches, leading to unexpected behavior or malfunctions.
    * Solution: Update the vehicle's software.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $0-$100
* **Charging system malfunctions**
    * Description/Symptoms: The vehicle's charging system may malfunction, preventing it from charging properly.
    * Solution: Repair or replace the charging system components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500-$2,000

**Exterior:**

* **Door handle issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: The door handles may become loose or difficult to operate.
    * Solution: Replace the door handles.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200-$500

**Interior:**

* **Center console lid malfunction**
    * Description/Symptoms: The center console lid may not open or close properly.
    * Solution: Replace the center console lid.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200-$400
* **Interior trim rattles**
    * Description/Symptoms: The interior trim pieces may rattle or make noises while driving.
    * Solution: Secure or replace the loose trim pieces.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100-$300

**Summary:**

Overall, the 2020 Audi e-tron Sportback has relatively few reported issues. The most common problems are related to the electrical system, particularly the 12-volt battery and software glitches. The cost to fix these issues can vary from minimal to moderate, but no major mechanical problems have been widely reported.
