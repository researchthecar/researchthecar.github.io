# 2020 Audi e-tron Sportback Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a pre-owned 2020 Audi e-tron Sportback, buyers are stepping into the luxury electric SUV market with appealing styling and smooth performance. While owner feedback highlights advanced features and comfortable cruising, early model years can present unique electrical and body integrity challenges that prospective owners should inspect closely.

---

## Engine

### Dual Electric Motor (e-tron 55 quattro)

The dual asynchronous electric motors generate an output of up to 355 horsepower (boostable to 402 horsepower) paired with electric all-wheel drive. This powertrain delivers brisk, silent acceleration, though buyers should verify battery health and charging equipment operation prior to purchase.

## Transmission

### Single-Speed Direct-Drive Transmission

A single-speed automatic reduction gearbox delivers power seamlessly to the Quattro all-wheel-drive system without traditional gear changes. It is generally robust and mechanical trouble is rare, though proper coolant maintenance for the electric drive units remains important.

## Electrical

### Connectivity and SOS Box

Electrical architecture supports numerous advanced luxury and safety features, but complex software integrations can occasionally trigger module warnings.

**Connectivity Box Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Warning messages or system failures related to the emergency SOS feature following battery voltage fluctuations or service resets.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified technician diagnose the telematics module and perform necessary software resets or module replacements.

### Drive System and Propulsion Electronics

High-voltage electrical systems manage power delivery and propulsion, requiring thorough diagnostic scans during a pre-purchase inspection.

**Drive System Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Unexpected loss of vehicle speed while driving accompanied by warning lights for the drive system and electrical system.
- **Solution:** Requires professional diagnostic scanning by an Audi specialist to inspect high-voltage components and inverter software.

## Interior

### Sunroof Assembly

Exterior body seals and sunroof drains require periodic inspection to prevent moisture intrusion into the cabin and electrical architecture.

**Sunroof Water Leak**
- **Symptoms:** Water leaking into the cabin from the panoramic sunroof area, potentially threatening interior electronics.
- **Solution:** Inspect drain tubes for blockages or seals for degradation, and clear or replace components as needed.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V900000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Audi&model=e-tron+Sportback&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 111, "Rear Visibility." A rearview image that does not display reduces the driver's view behind the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V240000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Pedals And Linkages](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Audi&model=e-tron+Sportback&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** Brake pedal detachment can cause a loss of brake function and increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and tighten the pushrod screw joint as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2020 Audi e-tron Sportback offers a refined electric driving experience, but buyers should approach with careful due diligence regarding its high-voltage and body electronics. A comprehensive pre-purchase inspection focusing on software updates, sunroof drainage, and electrical health will help ensure a more confident ownership experience.
