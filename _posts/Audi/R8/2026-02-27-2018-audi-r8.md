# 2018 Audi R8 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2018 Audi R8 is a high-performance supercar sharing much of its DNA with exotic Italian machinery, offering everyday usability alongside blistering track-ready performance. Buyers looking at a used R8 should evaluate both powertrain options carefully, keeping in mind that exotic maintenance and specialized repairs come with the territory. While major mechanical complaints in owner reports are rare, prospective purchasers should thoroughly inspect vehicle history, electronic systems, and safety equipment before finalizing a purchase.

---

## Engine

### 5.2L V10 (Base)

A naturally aspirated 5.2-liter V10 engine producing approximately 540 horsepower and 398 lb-ft of torque. It delivers exhilarating high-revving performance and solid overall mechanical durability when properly maintained.

### 5.2L V10 (V10 plus)

An upgraded naturally aspirated 5.2-liter V10 engine tuned to churn out roughly 602 horsepower and 413 lb-ft of torque. This higher-output variant provides track-focused power delivery with typical high-end exotic reliability.

## Transmission

### 7-Speed S tronic Dual-Clutch Automatic

A quick-shifting 7-speed dual-clutch automatic transmission paired standard with all-wheel drive across the R8 lineup. It offers rapid gear changes and refined cruising capability, though clutch wear should be monitored during pre-purchase inspections.

## Safety

### Air Bags

Owner reports for safety systems are extremely scarce, though isolated severe incidents highlight the critical importance of ensuring all airbag modules and electronic event recorders are fully functional.

**Side Airbag and EDR Failure in Severe Collision**
- **Symptoms:** Following a fatal side-impact collision, the side airbag on the impact side failed to deploy while other airbags and rollover systems functioned, and the event data recorder (EDR) experienced recording limitations.
- **Solution:** Have the vehicle's Supplemental Restraint System (SRS) and electronic sensors inspected by an authorized specialist if any warning lights appear or following any structural repairs.

## Summary Verdict

The 2018 Audi R8 remains an exceptionally thrilling exotic sports car option for used buyers who are prepared for high-end ownership costs. Because owner-reported complaints are sparse, focusing on a thorough professional pre-purchase inspection, comprehensive service records, and verification of all safety and electronic systems is the best strategy for a confident purchase.
