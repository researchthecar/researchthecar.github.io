# 2014 Audi R8

### Overview
The 2014 Audi R8, a celebrated supercar, generally exhibits strong mechanical and electrical reliability, reflecting its premium engineering and build quality. While exotic cars can present unique maintenance needs, the R8 from this model year has a reputation for robust performance and fewer widespread critical failures compared to some competitors. Owners typically report satisfaction with its powertrain and core systems. However, as with any high-performance vehicle, proactive maintenance and addressing minor issues promptly are key to ensuring long-term dependability and enjoying the R8's exhilarating driving experience.

## Electrical

### General Electrical System
**Infotainment System Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Screen freezing, unresponsive controls, Bluetooth connectivity issues.
- **Solution:** Software updates or replacement of the MMI unit.
- **Cost:** $500 - $2,000

**Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Difficulty starting, dead battery, electrical components malfunctioning.
- **Solution:** Check for parasitic draws, replace battery if necessary.
- **Cost:** $200 - $500

### Summary Verdict
The 2014 Audi R8 is a highly reliable supercar, with its V8 and V10 engines demonstrating exceptional durability. The manual transmission is virtually trouble-free. The S tronic dual-clutch transmission, while generally good, can occasionally experience issues with its mechatronic unit, representing the most significant powertrain concern. Suspension components, particularly the optional Magnetic Ride dampers, can require attention over time. Electrical gremlins, such as infotainment glitches and battery drain, are not uncommon but are typically manageable. Overall, the R8 offers a strong reliability record for its class, provided it receives diligent maintenance.
