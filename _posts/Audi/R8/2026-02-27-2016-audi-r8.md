# 2016 Audi R8

### Overview
The 2016 Audi R8, a second-generation supercar, is celebrated for its exhilarating performance and sophisticated engineering. Built around a mid-engine, all-wheel-drive platform, it offers a potent combination of power and handling. The model year saw the introduction of a naturally aspirated V8 and a more powerful V10 engine, both paired with a lightning-fast S tronic dual-clutch transmission. While generally regarded as a reliable exotic, like any high-performance vehicle, specific areas can experience issues under certain conditions. This analysis focuses on documented technical reliability concerns beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential problem areas for owners and enthusiasts.

## Electrical

### Infotainment System
**Software Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Screen freezing, unresponsive controls, navigation errors.
- **Solution:** Software update or system reset. In rare cases, head unit replacement.
- **Cost:** $200 - $1200


### Lighting Systems
**LED Headlight Failures**
- **Symptoms:** Individual LED segments not illuminating, flickering, or complete failure.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the headlight module.

### Summary Verdict
The 2016 Audi R8 is a remarkably reliable supercar, especially considering its high-performance nature. The naturally aspirated V8 and V10 engines, along with the S tronic transmission, are robust and have few documented widespread issues. Potential concerns are primarily concentrated in specific electronic components, such as infotainment system glitches and LED headlight failures, which can be costly to repair. The Magnetic Ride Control suspension can also experience sensor issues. For the Spyder variant, convertible top mechanisms are a point to monitor. Overall, the R8 offers a strong reliability record for its class, with most issues being manageable and not indicative of fundamental design flaws.
