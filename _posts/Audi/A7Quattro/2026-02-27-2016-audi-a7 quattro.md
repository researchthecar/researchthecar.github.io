# 2016 Audi A7 Quattro

### Overview
The 2016 Audi A7 Quattro is a luxury four-door coupe known for its sleek design, potent performance, and advanced technology. As a premium vehicle, its reliability is generally considered good, with owners often praising its build quality and driving dynamics. However, like any complex automobile, certain areas may experience issues over time. This report focuses on documented technical reliability concerns, excluding routine maintenance and normal wear and tear, to provide a clear picture of potential mechanical and electrical challenges specific to this model year and its various configurations. Understanding these potential issues can help owners prepare for and address them proactively.

---


## Engine

### 3.0L TFSI Supercharged V6
**PCV Valve Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Rough idle, increased oil consumption, check engine light, whistling noise from the engine.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the Positive Crankcase Ventilation (PCV) valve assembly.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600

**Oil Leaks (Valve Cover Gaskets)**
- **Symptoms:** Visible oil on the engine, burning oil smell, oil spots under the vehicle.
- **Solution:** Replacement of valve cover gaskets.
- **Cost:** $500 - $900

### Summary Verdict
The 2016 Audi A7 Quattro generally offers a solid reliability record, particularly concerning its powertrain and core components. The most frequently cited issues tend to be related to the optional Adaptive Air Suspension, with potential for leaks, and some engine-specific concerns like PCV valve failures and oil leaks, common in many turbocharged and supercharged engines. Electrical gremlins, particularly with the MMI infotainment system, can occur but are often resolvable with software updates. Overall, the A7 is a well-built luxury vehicle, but potential buyers should be aware of the costs associated with maintaining the advanced suspension and the possibility of minor engine-related repairs. Regular maintenance and prompt attention to warning signs are key to ensuring long-term reliability.
