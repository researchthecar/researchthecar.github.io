# 2024 Audi Q4 e-tron Sportback Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2024 Audi Q4 e-tron Sportback brings stylish all-electric luxury to the compact crossover segment, sharing its platform with other Volkswagen Group EVs. While early adopters praise its cabin comfort, smooth ride, and practical layout, federal safety reports and owner feedback highlight several electronic and mechanical quirks. Prospective buyers should thoroughly review vehicle history records and ensure all open software and electrical campaigns have been addressed by a certified dealer.

---

## Engine

### Q4 e-tron 40 / 45 Single Motor (Rear-Wheel Drive)

This rear-wheel-drive powertrain utilizes a single permanent magnet synchronous motor producing roughly 201 to 282 horsepower depending on the specific tuning. It offers adequate acceleration for daily commuting and efficient power delivery, though it lacks the all-weather grip of the dual-motor variants.

### Q4 e-tron 50 / 55 Dual Motor (Quattro All-Wheel Drive)

Equipped with dual asynchronous and synchronous electric motors driving all four wheels, this setup generates approximately 295 to 335 horsepower for brisk acceleration. It provides confident traction in various weather conditions, though complex multi-motor management systems can occasionally introduce software-related warning lights.

## Transmission

### Single-Speed Direct-Drive Transmission

This single-speed automatic reduction gearbox manages power transfer from the electric motors directly to the wheels with seamless, shift-free acceleration. It is mechanically simple and generally robust, though rare owner reports have noted transient gear selection or power-loss prompts linked to underlying electronic module faults.

### Electrical and Powertrain Control Systems

Advanced electrical architecture powers the vehicle's infotainment, safety suites, and high-voltage drivetrain. Owners have occasionally experienced complex electronic warnings and system resets.

**Electrical System Failures and Drive Warnings**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle displays sudden electrical malfunction warnings, instructs the driver to pull over and stop, or experiences complete electrical shutdowns.
- **Solution:** Check for open recall campaigns regarding the electrical system and have software modules updated or faulty components replaced by an authorized dealer.

## Suspension

### Tires and Wheels

The suspension is tuned to handle the heavier weight of an electric battery pack while maintaining a composed ride quality. Tire wear can be accelerated due to vehicle weight and instant torque delivery.

**Premature Tread Wear and Chunking**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have observed chunks of rubber pulling away from factory tire treads prematurely or experienced unexpected low-pressure warnings.
- **Solution:** Inspect tire treads regularly for abnormal wear patterns and replace tires with proper EV-rated load-index replacements if structural tread damage occurs.

## Brakes

### Brake System

The braking system blends regenerative deceleration with traditional hydraulic friction brakes. While normal operation is smooth, owners have flagged intermittent electronic glitches affecting pedal feel and braking response.

**Unintended Braking and Pedal Response Anomalies**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have reported instances of sudden, unexpected hard braking events while accelerating from a complete stop, or delayed engagement during low-speed regenerative braking.
- **Solution:** Have a certified Audi technician inspect the brake control modules, regenerative braking software, and wheel speed sensors for fault codes or required updates.

## Safety

### Acoustic Pedestrian Alert and Safety Sensors

Active safety systems and pedestrian alerts are critical for modern electric vehicles. Malfunctions in warning speakers or assist sensors can occasionally disable driver-aid features.

**Intermittent Pedestrian Warning Sound Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The exterior pedestrian warning sound required for electric vehicles during low-speed operation or neutral can intermittently fail to activate.
- **Solution:** Have the vehicle's acoustic vehicle alerting system (AVAS) module and wiring inspected and serviced by a technician.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V842000: Electrical System:Propulsion System:Charging:Cable/Cord:Onboard](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Audi&model=Q4+e-tron&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** An overheated outlet or charging cable can increase the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will supply a new 220V/240V compact/portable charging cable with an incorporated temperature sensor, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V361000: Exterior Lighting:Lighting Control Module:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Audi&model=Q4+e-tron&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 108, "Lamps, Reflective Devices, and Associated Equipment." Incorrect headlight control module software may cause the parking lights not to operate as intended when the headlights are turned on and the turn signals are activated, which can reduce.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the headlight control module with a new data set, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V125000: Electrical System:12V/24V/48V Battery](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Audi&model=Q4+e-tron&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** A loss of drive power increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the OCDC, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2024 Audi Q4 e-tron Sportback offers an appealing blend of EV efficiency and luxury branding, but used car shoppers should exercise diligence. Review the vehicle's service history for any electrical campaign completions and verify proper operation of the brakes and safety sensors during a test drive. Purchasing a certified pre-owned model or securing an extended warranty can provide additional peace of mind against complex electronic out-of-warranty repairs.
