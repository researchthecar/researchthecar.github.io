Here's a breakdown of common issues and complaints for the 2016 Audi A3 e-tron, organized by category and component, following your requested format. The 2016 Audi A3 e-tron is a plug-in hybrid vehicle that combines a 1.4-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine with an electric motor. The combined output is 204 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque. The A3 e-tron offers all-electric driving range and fuel efficiency benefits, which owners generally appreciate. Some common dislikes include limited cargo space due to the battery pack, and potential long-term battery degradation. Other Audi A3 models are available with either a 1.8-liter or 2.0-liter turbocharged gasoline engine. All A3 e-tron models use a 6-speed dual-clutch automatic transmission.

**Electric Motor**

* **Electric Motor Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Complete failure of the electric motor, resulting in a loss of electric driving capability. Can manifest as a warning light or diagnostic trouble code (DTC) related to the motor.
    * Solution: Replacement of the electric motor.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $7,000 - $10,000+

* **High Voltage Battery Degradation**
    * Description/Symptoms: Reduced all-electric range compared to original specifications. Noticeable drop in the vehicle's ability to hold a charge.
    * Solution: In some cases, individual modules within the battery pack can be replaced. In more severe cases, the entire battery pack may need replacement.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $5,000 - $10,000+

**Engine**

**1.4L Turbocharged 4-Cylinder Engine**

*   **Oil Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks from various locations, including the valve cover gasket, timing cover, or rear main seal. Visible oil drips under the car or a burning oil smell.
    *   Solution: Replacing the faulty gaskets or seals.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+

* **Coolant Leaks**
    * Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaks, often from the water pump or coolant hoses. Low coolant level warning light or visible coolant puddles.
    * Solution: Replacing the faulty water pump or coolant hoses.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $700+

**Transmission**

**6-Speed Dual-Clutch Automatic Transmission (DQ200)**

*   **Mechatronic Unit Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Erratic shifting, hesitation, or complete loss of gear engagement. Warning lights related to the transmission. Diagnostic Trouble Codes (DTCs) indicating mechatronic unit faults.
    *   Solution: Replacement or rebuilding of the mechatronic unit.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+

* **Clutch Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: Slipping clutch during acceleration, jerky shifts, or clutch shudder.
    * Solution: Clutch replacement.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+

**Suspension**

*   **No Issues Found**

**Electrical**

*   **Charging System Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Inability to charge the battery, slow charging times, or error messages related to the charging system.
    *   Solution: Diagnosing and repairing or replacing faulty charging components, such as the on-board charger or charging port.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+

*   **Software Glitches**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Infotainment system freezing or crashing, display issues, or malfunctioning driver-assistance features.
    *   Solution: Software updates or, in more severe cases, replacement of the affected control module.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+

**Exterior**

* **No Issues Found**

**Interior**

*   **Sunroof Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Sunroof failing to open or close properly, unusual noises during operation, or water leaks.
    *   Solution: Repairing or replacing the sunroof motor, tracks, or seals.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,200+

**Summary**

The 2016 Audi A3 e-tron offers a unique blend of efficiency and performance, but like any vehicle, it has its potential issues. Common concerns revolve around the electric motor and high-voltage battery, which can lead to significant repair costs. The dual-clutch transmission is also a potential trouble spot. While other systems may experience problems, these are less frequent and generally less expensive to address. Prospective buyers should be aware of these potential issues and carefully inspect the vehicle's service history before purchasing.
