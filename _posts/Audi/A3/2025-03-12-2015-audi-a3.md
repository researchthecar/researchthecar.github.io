The 2015 Audi A3 is a compact luxury car known for its stylish design, comfortable ride, and advanced technology features. It was offered in sedan and hatchback (Sportback) body styles. Powertrain options included a 1.8-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine producing 170 horsepower and 200 lb-ft of torque, and a 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine making 220 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque. Both engines were paired with a 6-speed S tronic dual-clutch automatic transmission. People generally appreciate the A3 for its fuel efficiency, upscale interior, and nimble handling. Common dislikes include the relatively small rear seat space and the potential for costly repairs, typical of European luxury vehicles.

**Engine**

**1.8L TFSI**
*   **Oil Consumption**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Excessive oil consumption, requiring frequent top-offs between scheduled oil changes. Can lead to engine damage if not monitored.
    *   Solution: Replacing piston rings.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,500 - $4,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**2.0L TFSI**
*   **Water Pump Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaks, overheating, engine warning light illumination. This engine uses a plastic water pump housing that can crack and leak over time.
    *   Solution: Replace the water pump and thermostat assembly.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,200+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Ignition Coil Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Misfires, rough idling, reduced power, check engine light. Often due to coil pack deterioration.
    *   Solution: Replace faulty ignition coil(s).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $400+ (depending on how many coils need replacing)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**6-Speed S tronic (DSG) Dual-Clutch Automatic**
*   **Mechatronic Unit Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rough or jerky shifting, delayed engagement, warning lights on the dashboard, complete loss of gear selection in some cases. The Mechatronic unit controls the transmission's shifting functions.
    *   Solution: Replace the Mechatronic unit or have it rebuilt.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,800 - $3,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Clutch Wear**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Slipping during acceleration, shuddering upon takeoff, difficulty shifting smoothly. Due to clutch packs wearing out over time.
    *   Solution: Replace the clutch packs.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Front Control Arm Bushing Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Clunking noises from the front suspension, especially over bumps, vague steering feel, uneven tire wear.
    *   Solution: Replace the control arm bushings or the entire control arm assembly.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+ per side
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Screen freezing, Bluetooth connectivity problems, navigation malfunctions, system reboots.
    *   Solution: Software updates, replacement of the infotainment unit.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,500+ (depending on the issue and whether replacement is needed)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Power Window Regulator Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Window not operating, slow or erratic window movement, clicking noises from the door panel.
    *   Solution: Replace the faulty window regulator.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+ per window
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Sunroof Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: Sunroof not closing or opening, loud clunking noises during operation, water leaking into the cabin.
    *   Solution: Replacing the sunroof motor or lubricating the sunroof tracks.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Dashboard Display Cracking**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Cracks appearing on the dashboard display screen, especially in hot climates.
    *   Solution: Replace the dashboard display screen.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2015 Audi A3, while offering a premium driving experience, has potential issues that buyers should be aware of. Engine issues like oil consumption and water pump failures can be costly to repair. The S tronic transmission can also present significant maintenance expenses with the Mechatronic unit and clutch wear. Electrical and interior issues, while less severe, can still be irritating. Overall, potential buyers should factor in the possibility of higher-than-average repair costs associated with this vehicle.
