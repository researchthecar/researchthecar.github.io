The 2004 Audi A3 (8P) marked the introduction of the second generation of this popular hatchback. It offered a blend of practicality, sporty handling, and premium features, making it a desirable option for many. Common engine choices included various gasoline and diesel options. The 1.6L gasoline engine produced around 102 horsepower and 102 lb-ft of torque. Other options included 1.8L Turbo and 2.0L Turbo gasoline engines, along with 1.9L TDI and 2.0L TDI diesel engines. People often liked the A3 for its refined interior, responsive steering, and comfortable ride. However, potential drawbacks included higher maintenance costs compared to mainstream brands and potential issues related to the complex electronics.

**Engine**

**1.6L Gasoline Engine**
*   **Ignition Coil Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Misfires, rough idling, check engine light illumination.
    *   Solution: Replace faulty ignition coil(s).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Oxygen Sensor Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Poor fuel economy, check engine light, rough running.
    *   Solution: Replace faulty oxygen sensor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**1.8L Turbo Gasoline Engine**
*   **Diverter Valve Failure (Revision D)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Loss of boost pressure, reduced acceleration, hissing noise.
    *   Solution: Replace diverter valve with an updated version.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $250+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **PCV (Positive Crankcase Ventilation) Valve Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rough idle, oil leaks, poor fuel economy, check engine light.
    *   Solution: Replace faulty PCV valve and associated hoses if necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $350+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**2.0L Turbo Gasoline Engine (TFSI)**
*   **Cam Follower Failure (High-Pressure Fuel Pump)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Ticking noise from the engine, reduced power, check engine light.  If left unaddressed, can cause significant damage to the camshaft.
    *   Solution: Replace the cam follower.  Inspect the camshaft for damage and replace if necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+ (cam follower only), $500 - $1500+ (camshaft replacement if needed)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Timing Chain Tensioner Failure (Early Models)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rattling noise on startup, particularly when cold. If left unchecked, can lead to timing chain skipping and severe engine damage.
    *   Solution: Replace the timing chain tensioner, chain, and associated guides.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $2000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**1.9L TDI Diesel Engine**
*   **EGR (Exhaust Gas Recirculation) Valve Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Poor performance, rough idling, excessive smoke, check engine light.
    *   Solution: Clean or replace the EGR valve.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Turbocharger Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, excessive smoke, whistling noise from the turbo.
    *   Solution: Diagnose and repair or replace the turbocharger.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $2000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**2.0L TDI Diesel Engine**
*   **Injector Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rough running, misfires, excessive smoke, poor fuel economy.
    *   Solution: Replace faulty injector(s).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $1200+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Diesel Particulate Filter (DPF) Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Reduced performance, warning lights, potentially limp mode. Often caused by short trips not allowing the DPF to regenerate.
    *   Solution: Attempt to force regeneration. If that fails, clean or replace the DPF.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $2000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**Manual Transmissions (All Engines)**
No Issues Found

**Automatic Transmissions (All Engines)**
*   **Torque Converter Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Slipping, shuddering, stalling, and poor acceleration.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the torque converter
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800-$1500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Control Arm Bushing Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Clunking noises, poor handling, uneven tire wear.
    *   Solution: Replace worn control arm bushings, or entire control arms.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600+ per side.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Strut Mount Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Noisy suspension, particularly over bumps.
    *   Solution: Replace strut mounts.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300-$700
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Central Convenience Module Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Problems with power windows, central locking, interior lights, and alarm system.
    *   Solution: Diagnose the specific issue and repair or replace the central convenience module.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Instrument Cluster Problems**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Flickering displays, inaccurate readings, complete failure.
    *   Solution: Repair or replace the instrument cluster.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Door Lock Actuator Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Door not locking or unlocking properly.
    *   Solution: Replace faulty door lock actuator.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $400+ per door
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Sunroof Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Sunroof motor failure, leaks, difficulty opening or closing.
    *   Solution: Repair or replace the sunroof motor or seals.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

The 2004 Audi A3 can be a great car, but potential buyers should be aware of the common issues. Engine problems, particularly with the 2.0L TFSI (cam follower and timing chain) and TDI (injectors, DPF), can be costly. Suspension and electrical issues can also arise. Overall, the severity and cost of potential repairs range from relatively minor to substantial, emphasizing the importance of a pre-purchase inspection.
