The 2022 Audi A3 is a compact luxury sedan known for its stylish design, sporty handling, and upscale interior. It's available with two engine options: a 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder producing 201 horsepower and 221 lb-ft of torque (40 TFSI), and a more powerful version of the same engine producing 306 horsepower and 295 lb-ft of torque (45 TFSI). Both engines are paired with a 7-speed S tronic dual-clutch automatic transmission. People generally like the A3 for its refined driving experience, tech-forward cabin, and fuel efficiency. Common dislikes include the relatively small rear seats and a sometimes-firm ride.

**Engine**

**2.0L Turbocharged 4-Cylinder (40 TFSI)**

*   **Oil Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks can occur from various locations, including the valve cover gasket, oil pan gasket, and rear main seal. Symptoms include visible oil drips under the car, a burning oil smell, and low oil levels.
    *   Solution: Replacing the affected gasket or seal is the typical fix.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1000+, depending on the source of the leak.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**2.0L Turbocharged 4-Cylinder (45 TFSI)**

*   **No Issues Found**

**Transmission**

**7-Speed S tronic Dual-Clutch Automatic**

*   **Hesitation/Rough Shifting**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported hesitation during acceleration and rough or jerky shifting, particularly at lower speeds. This can sometimes be related to the mechatronic unit (transmission control unit) or clutch wear.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis by a qualified technician is crucial. Potential solutions include software updates, mechatronic unit replacement, or clutch replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 (software update) - $4,000+ (mechatronic unit or clutch replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **No Issues Found**

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Glitches**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Intermittent freezing, lagging, or random restarts of the MMI (Multi Media Interface) infotainment system. Connectivity issues with Apple CarPlay or Android Auto.
    *   Solution: Software updates are often the first step. In some cases, replacement of the MMI unit may be required.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 (software update) - $1,500+ (MMI unit replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found
*   **Sensor Malfunctions (Parking Sensors, Blind Spot Monitoring)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Faulty parking sensors that give false readings or fail to activate. Issues with the blind spot monitoring system not functioning correctly.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis to identify the faulty sensor, followed by replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+ per sensor.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **No Issues Found**

**Interior**

*   **Rattling/Squeaking Noises**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rattling or squeaking noises emanating from the dashboard, door panels, or rear deck area while driving.
    *   Solution: Locating the source of the noise and applying sound-deadening material or tightening loose components.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+ depending on the complexity.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2022 Audi A3 generally appears to be a reliable vehicle, but it is not without its potential issues. The most commonly reported problems involve the infotainment system, potential transmission issues, and minor rattling noises within the interior. The severity of these issues ranges from minor annoyances to potentially costly repairs, particularly concerning the transmission. While some issues are common across the model range, others seem to be more prevalent with specific engine/transmission configurations. It is recommended that prospective buyers thoroughly inspect the vehicle, paying close attention to the operation of the transmission and the infotainment system, and review the vehicle's service history.
