## 2016 Audi A3

The 2016 Audi A3 is a compact luxury sedan that offers a balance of performance, style, and comfort. It is available with a choice of two engine options: a 1.8-liter turbocharged inline-four engine with 170 horsepower and 199 lb-ft of torque, or a 2.0-liter turbocharged inline-four engine with 220 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque. Both engines are paired with a six-speed dual-clutch automatic transmission.

The A3 has received praise for its stylish design, comfortable interior, and responsive handling. However, there have been some issues reported with the vehicle, which are detailed below.

### Engine
- **1.8-liter turbocharged inline-four engine**
 - **Intake valve carbon buildup**
  - Description/Symptoms: Over time, carbon deposits can build up on the intake valves, which can restrict airflow and cause the engine to run rough. Symptoms include reduced power, hesitation, and increased fuel consumption.
  - Solution: The intake valves must be cleaned by a mechanic. This typically involves removing the intake manifold and using a chemical cleaner to dissolve the carbon deposits.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
  - Recalls: None Found
- **2.0-liter turbocharged inline-four engine**
 - **Timing chain tensioner failure**
  - Description/Symptoms: The timing chain tensioner can fail, which can cause the timing chain to skip or break. This can lead to catastrophic engine damage. Symptoms include a rattling noise from the engine, reduced power, and an illuminated check engine light.
  - Solution: The timing chain and tensioner must be replaced. This is a major repair that requires the engine to be partially disassembled.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,500 - $4,000+
  - Recalls: None Found

### Transmission
- **Six-speed dual-clutch automatic transmission**
 - **Clutch pack failure**
  - Description/Symptoms: The clutch pack in the transmission can fail, which can cause the transmission to slip or shudder. This can lead to difficulty shifting gears, reduced power, and an illuminated check engine light.
  - Solution: The clutch pack must be replaced. This is a major repair that requires the transmission to be removed from the vehicle.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,000+
  - Recalls: None Found

### Suspension
- **Front suspension control arm bushings**
 - **Premature wear**
  - Description/Symptoms: The front suspension control arm bushings can wear out prematurely, which can cause the suspension to feel loose or unstable. Symptoms include a clunking noise from the front of the vehicle, uneven tire wear, and reduced handling performance.
  - Solution: The control arm bushings must be replaced. This is a relatively minor repair that can be performed by a mechanic.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+
  - Recalls: None Found

### Electrical
- **Electrical system*
> - **Battery drain**
>  - Description/Symptoms: The battery can drain quickly, even when the vehicle is not in use. This can lead to the vehicle not starting, or the battery dying while driving. Symptoms include a clicking noise when starting the vehicle, dim headlights, and an illuminated battery warning light.
>  - Solution: The electrical system must be diagnosed by a mechanic to determine the cause of the battery drain. This may involve testing the battery, alternator, and other electrical components.
>  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
>  - Recalls: None Found

### Exterior
- **Headlights**
 - **Condensation**
  - Description/Symptoms: Condensation can form inside the headlights, which can impair visibility at night. This is a common problem on the Audi A3.
  - Solution: The headlights must be replaced with new ones that are not prone to condensation.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
  - Recalls: None Found

### Interior
- **Dashboard**
 - **Cracking**
  - Description/Symptoms: The dashboard can crack, especially around the air vents. This can occur due to exposure to sunlight or extreme temperatures.
  - Solution: The dashboard can be replaced, or the cracks can be repaired by a professional.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+
  - Recalls: None Found

## Summary
Overall, the 2016 Audi A3 is a reliable and well-built vehicle. However, there have been some issues reported with the engine, transmission, suspension, and electrical system. The severity of these issues varies, and the cost to fix them can be significant. It is important to research the potential issues with this vehicle before making a purchasing decision.
