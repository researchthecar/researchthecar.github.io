# 2014 Audi S4

### Overview
The 2014 Audi S4, a performance-oriented variant of the A4 sedan, is generally regarded as a reliable vehicle, particularly given its performance capabilities. It features a potent 3.0L supercharged V6 engine paired with either a manual or automatic transmission, and a sophisticated all-wheel-drive system. While it offers an engaging driving experience, like any performance car, it can be susceptible to specific issues related to its high-performance components. This analysis focuses on documented, non-wear-and-tear related reliability concerns for the 2014 model year, providing insights into potential problem areas and their associated costs to help prospective owners make informed decisions.

---


## Engine

### 3.0L Supercharged V6 (TFSI)
**PCV Valve Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Rough idle, whistling noises, oil leaks, increased oil consumption, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the Positive Crankcase Ventilation (PCV) valve assembly.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600

**Supercharger Belt Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of power, whining noise from the engine bay, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the supercharger drive belt.
- **Cost:** $200 - $400

**Oil Leaks (Valve Cover Gaskets)**
- **Symptoms:** Visible oil leaks around the top of the engine, burning oil smell, potential for oil to drip onto exhaust components.
- **Solution:** Replacement of valve cover gaskets.
- **Cost:** $500 - $900


## Transmission

### 6-Speed Manual
**Clutch Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Slipping clutch, difficulty shifting gears, burning smell, shuddering during engagement.
- **Solution:** Clutch replacement (clutch kit including pressure plate, disc, and release bearing).
- **Cost:** $1200 - $2000


### 7-Speed S tronic (Dual-Clutch Automatic)
**Mechatronics Unit Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Harsh shifting, transmission slipping, inability to engage gears, transmission warning lights.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of the mechatronics unit.
- **Cost:** $2000 - $4000

**Transmission Fluid Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Leaking fluid from the transmission pan or seals, potential for shifting issues if fluid level is low.
- **Solution:** Repair of leaks, often involving replacing seals or the transmission pan gasket.
- **Cost:** $300 - $700

## Electrical

### Infotainment System
**MMI System Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Screen freezing, unresponsive controls, audio issues, navigation problems.
- **Solution:** Software updates or potential replacement of MMI control unit.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1500


### Lighting Control Module
**Xenon Headlight Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** Headlights flickering, not turning on, or turning off unexpectedly.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the headlight control module or ballast.
- **Cost:** $400 - $800

### Summary Verdict
The 2014 Audi S4 presents a generally solid reliability record for a performance sedan. The 3.0L supercharged V6 engine, while powerful, has a few documented issues such as PCV valve failures and oil leaks, which are not uncommon for forced-induction engines. Transmission reliability is good, though the S tronic automatic can be prone to mechatronics unit issues. Electrical gremlins, particularly with the MMI infotainment system, can occur. Overall, the S4 is a capable and enjoyable car, but potential buyers should be aware of these specific potential repair costs, especially concerning the engine and automatic transmission components. Regular maintenance and addressing early signs of issues are key to long-term ownership satisfaction.
