# 2020 Audi S4

### Overview
The 2020 Audi S4, a performance-oriented variant of the A4 sedan, is generally regarded as a reliable vehicle, particularly given its performance capabilities. It features a potent turbocharged V6 engine paired with a responsive dual-clutch transmission, offering an engaging driving experience. The Quattro all-wheel-drive system enhances its handling and stability. While most owners report a positive ownership experience with minimal unexpected issues, like any performance vehicle, some components may require more attention over time. This analysis focuses on documented technical reliability concerns beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential areas of focus for owners and prospective buyers.

### Summary Verdict
The 2020 Audi S4 demonstrates strong overall reliability, with its core powertrain components (engine and transmission) showing no significant widespread issues. The Quattro all-wheel-drive system and adaptive suspension are also generally robust. The most commonly reported, albeit minor, concerns tend to be in the electrical system, specifically related to occasional infotainment glitches and potential sensor malfunctions in driver assistance features. These are often resolvable with software updates or minor component replacements. Exterior and interior components are largely free from widespread defects. For owners, proactive software checks and sensor cleanliness can help mitigate minor electrical annoyances, contributing to a positive and dependable ownership experience.
