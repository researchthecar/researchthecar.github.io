Here's a comprehensive breakdown of common issues and complaints associated with the 2016 Audi A5, categorized by component, engine, and transmission options.

The 2016 Audi A5 is a stylish and sporty coupe/convertible, known for its elegant design, engaging driving dynamics, and well-appointed interior. Engine options include a 2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4 producing 220 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque. The engine can be paired with either a 6-speed manual or an 8-speed automatic transmission. Some owners praise the A5 for its comfortable ride, refined cabin, and fuel efficiency. Common complaints include higher repair costs compared to some competitors, and potential for electronic glitches.

**Engine**

**2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4 (220 hp, 258 lb-ft)**

*   **Oil Consumption**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Excessive oil consumption, requiring frequent oil top-offs between scheduled oil changes. May manifest as a low oil warning light.
    *   Solution: In some cases, a redesigned PCV valve or piston rings may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $3000+ (depending on the severity and whether piston ring replacement is needed)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Timing Chain Tensioner Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rattling noise upon cold start, potential for catastrophic engine damage if the tensioner fails completely.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the timing chain tensioner, chain, and associated components.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1500 - $3000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**8-Speed Automatic Transmission**

*   **Rough Shifting**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Harsh or jerky shifting, particularly between certain gears. May be more noticeable when the transmission is cold.
    *   Solution: Transmission fluid change may alleviate minor issues. More severe cases may require valve body replacement or transmission rebuild/replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 (fluid change) - $5000+ (rebuild/replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**6-Speed Manual Transmission**

*No Issues Found*

**Suspension**

*   **Control Arm Bushing Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Clunking or creaking noises from the front suspension, especially when going over bumps. Premature tire wear.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the worn control arm bushings or complete control arm assembly.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1000+ per side
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Battery Drain**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Vehicle battery drains quickly, especially when the car is not driven frequently. Can cause starting problems.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis to identify the parasitic draw. Potential culprits include faulty modules (e.g., infotainment, comfort control), or wiring issues.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $1000+ (depending on the source of the drain)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Infotainment System Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Freezing, crashing, or slow response times of the MMI (Multi Media Interface) system. Bluetooth connectivity problems.
    *   Solution: Software updates can sometimes resolve these issues. In more severe cases, replacement of the MMI unit may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 (software update) - $1500+ (MMI unit replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Water Leaks (Convertible Models)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Water leaks into the cabin, particularly around the convertible top seals during rain or car washes.
    *   Solution: Replacement or adjustment of the convertible top seals. Cleaning and lubrication of the seals can also help.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $1000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Sunroof Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Sunroof may get stuck, make excessive noise when opening/closing, or fail to close completely.
    *   Solution: Inspection and lubrication of the sunroof tracks. Replacement of the sunroof motor or other components may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2016 Audi A5 can be a rewarding car to own, but potential buyers should be aware of the common issues reported. Engine issues such as oil consumption and timing chain tensioner failure can be expensive to repair. Electrical glitches are also a potential source of frustration. While some issues are relatively minor, others can lead to significant repair bills. Pre-purchase inspections and thorough maintenance records are essential when considering a used 2016 A5.
