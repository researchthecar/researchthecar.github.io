**Introduction:** The 2018 Audi A5 is a compact luxury coupe that offers a smooth and refined driving experience. It comes with a choice of two engine options: a 2.0-liter inline-four and a 3.0-liter V6. Both engines are mated to a seven-speed dual-clutch transmission. The A5 has been praised for its upscale interior, sleek exterior design, and engaging driving dynamics. However, there have been some reported issues with the vehicle, which are detailed below.

**Engine**

**2.0-Liter Inline-Four**

**Leaking Coolant Reservoir:**
Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaking from the plastic coolant reservoir, causing a loss of coolant and overheating.
Solution: Replace the coolant reservoir.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300
Recalls: None Found

**3.0-Liter V6**

**Turbocharger Failure:**
Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, excessive smoke, and loud noises from the engine bay.
Solution: Replace the turbocharger.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000
Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**7-Speed Dual-Clutch Transmission**

**Jerking and Shuddering:**
Description/Symptoms: Transmission hesitating or shuddering during gear shifts.
Solution: Update transmission software or replace clutch assembly.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000
Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

**No Issues Found**

**Electrical**

**Battery Drain:**
Description/Symptoms: Battery draining quickly, even when the car is turned off.
Solution: Replace battery or check for parasitic drain.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300
Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

**No Issues Found**

**Interior**

**Inoperative Infotainment System:**
Description/Symptoms: Touchscreen becoming unresponsive or displaying errors.
Solution: Update infotainment software or replace the head unit.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500
Recalls: None Found

**Summary:** Overall, the 2018 Audi A5 is a reliable and enjoyable vehicle. However, it has experienced some issues with the engine, transmission, and electrical systems. The severity of these issues varies, with some being minor and others requiring major repairs. The estimated cost to fix these issues can range from a few hundred dollars to several thousand dollars. It is recommended to have the vehicle regularly serviced by a qualified mechanic to prevent or detect any potential problems.
