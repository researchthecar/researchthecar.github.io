# 2019 Audi A5 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2019 Audi A5 delivers a compelling mix of sharp styling, agile handling, and upscale interior tech. While owner-reported complaints are relatively sparse, prospective buyers should review specific electronic and steering concerns raised by drivers.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4

The 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine produces approximately 248 horsepower and 273 lb-ft of torque, providing brisk acceleration for daily driving. This powertrain is generally well-regarded for smooth power delivery and balanced efficiency, though routine turbo and ignition maintenance is key to long-term health.

## Transmission

### 7-Speed S-tronic Dual-Clutch Automatic

Equipped with a 7-speed dual-clutch automatic transmission paired with quattro all-wheel drive, this setup offers rapid gear shifts and sporty responsiveness. Buyers should ensure proper fluid service intervals have been maintained to keep shift quality crisp.

### 6-Speed Manual

A 6-speed manual transmission was available on select configurations for enthusiasts seeking a more engaging, hands-on driving experience. This traditional gearbox is sturdy, though clutch wear will depend heavily on previous driving habits.

## Steering

### Electric Power Steering System

Issues related to the electric power steering assist have been noted by some owners, requiring careful inspection during a pre-purchase evaluation.

**Sudden Steering Loss or Stiffness**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have reported a sudden 'Steering Malfunction' warning message accompanied by a complete loss of power assist, making the steering wheel extremely stiff and difficult to turn while at highway speeds.
- **Solution:** Have the vehicle inspected by a qualified technician to diagnose the electronic power steering rack, wiring harness, or steering control module for potential failure.

## Electrical

### Driver Assistance and Infotainment Electronics

electrical issues involving driver assistance suites, cameras, and start-stop functions can occasionally disrupt normal vehicle operation.

**Auto Start-Stop and Pre-Sense Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have experienced erratic behavior with the auto start-stop system shutting the engine off unexpectedly, alongside the pre-sense safety system prematurely engaging the brakes during routine turns.
- **Solution:** Update vehicle software modules or consult a dealership to check sensor alignment and battery health affecting the start-stop and driver-assist features.

**Backup Camera and SOS System Fault**
- **Symptoms:** An 'SOS System Deactivated' warning message can appear upon startup, rendering the rearview camera temporarily inoperable.
- **Solution:** Perform a system reset or have the telematics and camera control modules diagnosed for software corruption or loose connections.

## Exterior

### Door Seals and Body Structure

Exterior weatherproofing issues can occasionally lead to trapped water in door cavities if drainage points become obstructed.

**Water Accumulation in Door Sills**
- **Symptoms:** Water collects inside the rear passenger door well or sill, resulting in a noticeable sloshing sound when braking or accelerating after rainstorms or car washes.
- **Solution:** Clear out clogged door drain plugs and inspect the weatherstripping seals for proper fitment and integrity.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V034000: Tires](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Audi&model=A5&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** The rear axle alignment may not have been inspected after the repairs for Recall 21V-295 (42L1) were performed. Rear axle misalignment may cause premature or uneven tire wear, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the rear axle, adjust the alignment as necessary, and replace any prematurely or unevenly worn tires, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V295000: Suspension:Rear:Axle:Non-Powered Axle Assembly](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Audi&model=A5&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** A broken lock nut may misalign the rear axle, causing a loss of control and increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the lock nuts and, if necessary, the associated bolts, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V874000: Seats](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Audi&model=A5&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** A disabled passenger air bag increases the risk of injury in the event of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the connecting cable and either the heating mat or entire seat cover, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2019 Audi A5 is a stylish and capable luxury cruiser, but prospective buyers should conduct a thorough test drive to check for proper steering feel, electronic stability, and infotainment functionality. Ensuring all software updates and routine maintenance are up to date will help secure a more dependable ownership experience.
