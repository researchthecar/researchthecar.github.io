**Introduction:**

The 2010 Audi A5 is a stylish and sporty coupe that offers a wide range of engine and transmission options. It boasts an upscale interior, impressive handling, and a capable quattro all-wheel-drive system. However, the A5 has also encountered a number of issues since its release.

**Engine**

**2.0T FSI Inline-4 (211 hp, 258 lb-ft)**

* **High-Pressure Fuel Pump Failure:** Symptoms include rough idling, stalling, and reduced engine power. The fuel pump must be replaced. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000-$1,500+
* **Turbocharger Actuator Issues:** Symptoms include reduced boost pressure and whistling noises. The actuator must be replaced. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500-$800
* **Timing Chain Tensioner Failure:** Symptoms include rattling noises from the engine. The timing chain tensioner and timing chain must be replaced. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500-$2,500

**3.0T Supercharged V6 (272 hp, 295 lb-ft)**

* **Water Pump Failure:** Symptoms include overheating and coolant leaks. The water pump must be replaced. Estimated Cost to Fix: $800-$1,200
* **Fuel Injector Issues:** Symptoms include hard starting, rough idling, and reduced engine power. The fuel injectors must be replaced. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500-$700
* **Intake Manifold Leaks:** Symptoms include vacuum leaks and reduced engine performance. The intake manifold must be resealed. Estimated Cost to Fix: $400-$600

**Transmission**

**6-speed Manual**

* **Clutch Slave Cylinder Failure:** Symptoms include difficulty shifting gears or the clutch not disengaging properly. The clutch slave cylinder must be replaced. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300-$500

**8-speed Automatic**

* **Mechatronic Unit Failure:** Symptoms include shifting delays, slipping gears, or the transmission not engaging. The mechatronic unit must be replaced. Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,500-$4,000

**Suspension**

* **Front Control Arm Bushings Wear:** Symptoms include clunking noises when driving over bumps. The control arm bushings must be replaced. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300-$500
* **Rear Spring Sagging:** Symptoms include reduced ride height and uneven tire wear. The rear springs must be replaced. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500-$800

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain:** Symptoms include a dead battery after the car has been parked. The source of the battery drain must be isolated and repaired. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100-$500
* **Headlight Condensation:** Symptoms include water accumulation inside the headlights. The headlights must be resealed. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200-$300

**Exterior**

* **Trunk Lid Misalignment:** Symptoms include gaps between the trunk lid and the body. The trunk lid must be adjusted and aligned. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100-$200

**Interior**

* **Door Panel Rattling:** Symptoms include noises from the door panels when driving over bumps. The door panels must be removed and padded. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200-$400
* **Climate Control Module Failure:** Symptoms include inoperative climate control functions. The climate control module must be replaced. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500-$700
* **Navigation System Malfunction:** Symptoms include freezing or crashing of the navigation system. The navigation system software must be updated or the unit must be replaced. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500-$1,000

**Summary:**

While the 2010 Audi A5 offers a lot to like, it has encountered a number of issues that can be costly to repair. Problems with the fuel pump, turbocharger, and transmission are particularly concerning. The severity and cost of these issues can vary, so it's important to be aware of them before purchasing a used A5.
