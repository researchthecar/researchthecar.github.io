# 2008 Audi A5

### Overview
The 2008 Audi A5, a stylish coupe known for its elegant design and premium feel, generally offers a solid ownership experience. However, like many vehicles of its era, it is not immune to specific mechanical and electrical concerns. Owners have reported issues primarily related to the engine, particularly with certain powertrain configurations, and some electrical gremlins can surface over time. The interior and exterior generally hold up well, but attention to specific components is advised for long-term reliability. Regular maintenance is crucial for mitigating potential problems and ensuring the longevity of this luxury vehicle.

---


## Engine

### 2.0L TFSI (BGB/BPJ)
**Oil Consumption**
- **Symptoms:** Excessive oil consumption, blue smoke from exhaust, potential for engine damage if not monitored.
- **Solution:** Piston ring replacement, PCV valve replacement, or engine rebuild.
- **Cost:** $1,500 - $3,500

**Timing Chain Tensioner Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Rattling noise from the engine, especially on startup; check engine light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of timing chain, tensioners, and guides.
- **Cost:** $1,000 - $2,500


### 3.2L FSI (BKH)
**Timing Chain Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Rattling noise from the engine, particularly on cold starts; check engine light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of timing chain, tensioners, and guides.
- **Cost:** $1,200 - $2,800


### 4.2L FSI (BVH)
**Timing Chain Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Rattling noise from the engine, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of timing chains, tensioners, and guides.
- **Cost:** $2,000 - $4,000

### 6-Speed Tiptronic Automatic (09L)
**Torque Converter Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Shuddering, slipping, or delayed engagement.
- **Solution:** Torque converter replacement.
- **Cost:** $1,000 - $2,000

**Valve Body Problems**
- **Symptoms:** Harsh shifting, transmission fault lights.
- **Solution:** Valve body repair or replacement.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1,800


### Multitronic CVT (8J)
**CVT Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Jerking, slipping, loss of drive, transmission warning lights.
- **Solution:** CVT unit replacement or rebuild.
- **Cost:** $3,000 - $6,000

**Infotainment System Glitches**

**Tail Light Condensation**
- **Symptoms:** Moisture inside the tail light assemblies.

### Summary Verdict
The 2008 Audi A5 is a visually appealing car with a generally good mechanical foundation, but potential buyers should be aware of specific engine and transmission concerns. The 2.0L TFSI engine is prone to oil consumption and timing chain issues, while the 3.2L and 4.2L FSI engines can also suffer from timing chain wear. Automatic transmissions, particularly the Multitronic CVT, have a higher incidence of failure. Electrical gremlins like infotainment glitches and window regulator problems are also noted. Despite these potential issues, with diligent maintenance and awareness of common faults, the 2008 A5 can remain a reliable and enjoyable luxury coupe.
