# 2014 Audi A5

### Overview
The 2014 Audi A5, a stylish and sporty coupe, convertible, and liftback, generally offers a solid ownership experience with a focus on premium features and driving dynamics. While it shares many components with other Audi models of its era, specific reliability trends can emerge. This analysis focuses on documented technical issues beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential areas of concern for owners and prospective buyers. We will examine the performance and potential pitfalls of its various engine and transmission configurations, as well as common issues across key vehicle systems.

---


## Engine

### 2.0L TFSI (Turbocharged Direct Injection)
**Oil Consumption**
- **Symptoms:** Excessive oil consumption, potential for oil leaks, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Piston ring replacement, PCV valve replacement, or updated engine components.
- **Cost:** $1,500 - $3,000

**Timing Chain Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Rattling noise from the front of the engine, especially on startup; potential for timing deviation codes.
- **Solution:** Replacement of timing chain, tensioners, guides, and potentially camshaft adjusters.
- **Cost:** $1,200 - $2,500

## Electrical

### Infotainment System
**Audi MMI Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Screen freezing, unresponsiveness, Bluetooth connectivity issues, navigation system errors.
- **Solution:** Software updates, control module reset, or replacement of MMI control unit.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1,500


### Sensors
**ABS/ESP Sensor Failures**
- **Symptoms:** ABS and traction control warning lights illuminated, potential for system malfunction.
- **Solution:** Replacement of faulty wheel speed sensors or ABS control module.
- **Cost:** $300 - $700

### Summary Verdict
The 2014 Audi A5 presents a generally reliable profile, particularly with its more robust engine and transmission options. The 2.0L TFSI engine is the most commonly cited for potential issues, primarily related to oil consumption and timing chain wear, which can be costly to repair. The Multitronic CVT transmission has also seen reports of slipping and hesitation. Electrical gremlins, particularly with the MMI infotainment system and ABS/ESP sensors, are not uncommon. However, many owners report satisfaction with the overall build quality and driving experience. Regular maintenance and prompt attention to early warning signs are crucial for mitigating potential repair costs and ensuring long-term dependability.
