# 2021 Audi A5 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2021 Audi A5, buyers will find a stylish and technologically advanced luxury vehicle known for its refined performance. While overall owner feedback highlights typical modern luxury car ownership experiences, prospective buyers should review available owner reports covering intermittent electrical glitches, transmission warnings, and safety system sensitivities.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4 (45 TFSI)

The 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine produces approximately 261 horsepower and 273 lb-ft of torque, providing brisk acceleration for daily driving. This powerplant is generally well-regarded for balancing responsive power delivery with respectable efficiency in daily use.

## Transmission

### 7-Speed S tronic Dual-Clutch Automatic

The 7-speed S tronic dual-clutch automatic transmission delivers rapid gear shifts paired with Quattro all-wheel drive. While generally smooth and responsive, prospective buyers should verify proper shifting behavior during a test drive.

**Transmission Failure Warnings and Engagement Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have reported sudden transmission failure warning lights appearing on the dashboard, accompanied by instructions to pull over or drive with limited functionality.
- **Solution:** Have the vehicle scanned by an authorized service center or qualified technician to diagnose diagnostic trouble codes and inspect transmission control modules.

## Electrical

### Instrument Panel and Power Systems

Electrical components govern advanced infotainment, digital gauges, and critical safety features, making proper diagnostic checks essential during a pre-purchase inspection.

**Instrument Cluster Blanking and Power Loss**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have experienced the instrument panel blinking and going completely dark while driving, or temporary power loss after shifting gears.
- **Solution:** Inspect the vehicle's electrical charging system, battery health, and software modules for updates or failing control units.

## Safety

### Passenger Airbag and Pre Sense System

Advanced safety systems offer high-tech protection but rely on sensitive sensors and software calibration that require careful verification.

**Intermittent Airbag Indicator and Unwanted Braking**
- **Symptoms:** The passenger-side airbag indicator light may intermittently turn on and off regardless of occupancy, and automatic emergency braking systems can occasionally trigger unexpectedly.
- **Solution:** Consult a dealer or specialist to inspect seat occupancy sensors, calibrate driver-assistance cameras, and check for relevant software updates.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V606000: Seat Belts:Rear/Other:Retractor](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Audi&model=A5&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 208, "Occupant Crash Protection." An unsecured child restraint system can increase the risk of injury during a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect, and replace as necessary, the middle-rear seat belt assemblies, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V034000: Tires](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Audi&model=A5&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The rear axle alignment may not have been inspected after the repairs for Recall 21V-295 (42L1) were performed. Rear axle misalignment may cause premature or uneven tire wear, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the rear axle, adjust the alignment as necessary, and replace any prematurely or unevenly worn tires, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V737000: Exterior Lighting:Tail Lights](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Audi&model=A5&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The wiring harness for the rear lights could be missing the anti-abrasion protection in the area of the spare wheel well. Without the scuff protection, the wires may become damaged during a rear crash, disabling the doors from being opened from the outside, causing a loss of hazard lights, and increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Audi will notify owners, and dealers will apply scuff protection, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V295000: Suspension:Rear:Axle:Non-Powered Axle Assembly](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Audi&model=A5&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** A broken lock nut may misalign the rear axle, causing a loss of control and increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the lock nuts and, if necessary, the associated bolts, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V742000: Back Over Prevention:Display Function](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Audi&model=A5&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 111, "Rear Visibility." A blank rearview camera display can reduce the driver's rear visibility, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the infotainment unit software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2021 Audi A5 delivers a compelling mix of luxury, style, and performance, but prospective buyers should perform a thorough inspection prior to purchase. Pay close attention to transmission shift quality, electrical accessory operation, and driver-assistance sensor behavior during a test drive to ensure everything functions as intended.
