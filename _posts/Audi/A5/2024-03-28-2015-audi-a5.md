**2015 Audi A5**

The Audi A5 is a compact executive car available in coupe and convertible body styles. It comes standard with a 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine producing 220 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque. Optional engine options include a 3.0-liter turbocharged V6 with 272 horsepower and 295 lb-ft of torque, and a 3.0-liter supercharged V6 with 333 horsepower and 325 lb-ft of torque. The A5 is offered with either a six-speed manual transmission or a seven-speed dual-clutch automatic transmission.

The A5 is praised for its stylish design, comfortable interior, and powerful engines. However, some owners have reported issues with the vehicle's engine, transmission, suspension, and electrical system.

**Engine**

* **2.0-liter Turbocharged Four-Cylinder Engine**
    * **Premature Timing Chain Wear**
        * Description/Symptoms: The timing chain can wear prematurely, causing the engine to lose power and eventually fail. Symptoms include a rattling noise from the engine, reduced engine performance, and a check engine light.
        * Solution: The timing chain and associated components need to be replaced.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+
        * Recalls: None Found
* **3.0-liter Turbocharged V6 Engine**
    * **Intake Valve Carbon Buildup**
        * Description/Symptoms: Carbon deposits can build up on the intake valves, restricting airflow and reducing engine performance. Symptoms include rough idle, hesitation during acceleration, and a decrease in fuel economy.
        * Solution: The intake valves need to be cleaned or replaced.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
        * Recalls: None Found
* **3.0-liter Supercharged V6 Engine**
    * **No Issues Found**

**Transmission**

* **Six-Speed Manual Transmission**
    * **Difficulty Shifting Gears**
        * Description/Symptoms: The transmission may be difficult to shift gears, especially when cold. Symptoms include grinding noises when shifting, the shifter feeling loose or notchy, and difficulty engaging certain gears.
        * Solution: The transmission may need to be repaired or replaced.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
        * Recalls: None Found
* **Seven-Speed Dual-Clutch Automatic Transmission**
    * **Mechatronic Unit Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: The mechatronic unit, which controls the transmission, can fail. Symptoms include transmission slipping, shuddering, or refusing to shift gears.
        * Solution: The mechatronic unit needs to be replaced.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $3,000 - $5,000+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Front Suspension**
    * **Premature Ball Joint Wear**
        * Description/Symptoms: The ball joints in the front suspension can wear prematurely, causing clunking noises, reduced steering precision, and uneven tire wear.
        * Solution: The ball joints need to be replaced.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
        * Recalls: None Found
* **Rear Suspension**
    * **No Issues Found**

**Electrical**

* **Battery Issues**
    * **Premature Battery Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: The battery may fail prematurely, causing the vehicle to lose power or not start. Symptoms include dimming headlights, difficulty starting the engine, and a check engine light.
        * Solution: The battery needs to be replaced.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
        * Recalls: None Found
* **Electrical Gremlins**
    * **Random Electrical Issues**
        * Description/Symptoms: The vehicle may experience random electrical issues, such as faulty instrument cluster readings, power window failures, or malfunctioning lights.
        * Solution: The electrical system needs to be diagnosed and repaired.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the issue
        * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Headlights**
    * **Condensation in Headlights**
        * Description/Symptoms: Condensation may form inside the headlights, causing fogging or water droplets to accumulate.
        * Solution: The headlights need to be replaced or resealed.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
        * Recalls: None Found
* **Roof**
    * **Convertible Roof Leaks**
        * Description/Symptoms: The convertible roof may leak, allowing water to enter the cabin. Symptoms include water stains on the interior, mold or mildew growth, and unpleasant odors.
        * Solution: The convertible roof seals need to be replaced or repaired.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Infotainment System**
    * **Lagging Touchscreen**
        * Description/Symptoms: The touchscreen may lag or become unresponsive, making it difficult to use the infotainment system.
        * Solution: The infotainment system may need to be updated or replaced.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
        * Recalls: None Found
* **Climate Control**
    * **A/C Compressor Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: The A/C compressor may fail, resulting in a loss of air conditioning. Symptoms include warm or humid air blowing from the vents, and a lack of cold air.
        * Solution: The A/C compressor and associated components need to be replaced.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Overall Summary**

The 2015 Audi A5 is a stylish and powerful car, but it has been known to experience a range of issues, including engine timing chain wear, transmission problems, suspension issues, electrical gremlins, headlight condensation, convertible roof leaks, infotainment system lag, and A/C compressor failure. The severity and cost of these problems can vary depending on the specific issue and the extent of the damage. Potential buyers should be aware of these issues and factor them into their purchase decision.
