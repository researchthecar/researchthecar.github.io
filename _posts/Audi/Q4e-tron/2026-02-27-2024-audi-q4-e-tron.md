# 2024 Audi Q4 e-tron Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The Audi Q4 e-tron brings luxury EV driving to a popular crossover segment, but early adopters have reported notable electronic and safety-related hiccups. While many owners enjoy its quiet ride and spacious cabin, prospective used-car buyers should carefully review owner feedback regarding complex software systems and intermittent drivetrain alerts.

---

## Engine

### Single Motor RWD (Rear-Wheel Drive)

The single-motor powertrain option utilizes a rear-mounted permanent magnet synchronous motor delivering approximately 201 horsepower. It offers smooth, efficient everyday performance for daily commuting.

### Dual Motor AWD (Quattro)

The dual-motor setup adds a front asynchronous motor to provide all-wheel-drive capability, bumping total output up to around 295 horsepower. This configuration provides confident traction in inclement weather while maintaining balanced luxury-EV driving dynamics.

## Transmission

### Single-Speed Automatic Reduction Gear

Electric vehicles utilize a single-speed direct-drive reduction transmission to deliver seamless, shiftless power from standstill to highway speeds. This unit requires minimal maintenance and is generally robust over the vehicle's lifespan.

### AWD and Sensor Systems

Advanced safety features and electronic monitoring systems add convenience but can introduce intermittent sensor warnings that require professional diagnosis.

**All-Wheel Drive and Seat Occupancy Sensor Faults**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicles may repeatedly display four-wheel drive system malfunction warnings or experience operational glitches tied to driver's seat occupancy sensors.
- **Solution:** Consult an Audi technician to diagnose sensor harnesses, seat-switch wiring, and AWD control modules.

## Brakes

### Brake System

The Q4 e-tron braking system combines regenerative braking with traditional friction brakes, though complex software integration has led to occasional owner complaints regarding unexpected pedal behavior.

**Unintended Braking and Low-Speed Response Delay**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have reported instances of sudden, hard braking events without driver input, or delayed engagement of brakes while operating in regenerative braking modes at low speeds.
- **Solution:** Have the dealership inspect regenerative braking software calibration, brake switch sensors, and electronic control modules for pending updates.

## Electrical

### Electrical System

The vehicle relies heavily on integrated electrical architecture, which has occasionally generated owner-reported warnings and software-related power interruptions.

**Sudden System Malfunctions and Vehicle Shutdown**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have experienced sudden electrical system warnings instructing them to pull over, accompanied by complete vehicle shut-downs or loss of core functionality.
- **Solution:** Check vehicle history for open safety recalls related to electrical system faults and ensure all software modules are updated by an authorized service center.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V842000: Electrical System:Propulsion System:Charging:Cable/Cord:Onboard](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Audi&model=Q4+e-tron&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** An overheated outlet or charging cable can increase the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will supply a new 220V/240V compact/portable charging cable with an incorporated temperature sensor, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V361000: Exterior Lighting:Lighting Control Module:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Audi&model=Q4+e-tron&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 108, "Lamps, Reflective Devices, and Associated Equipment." Incorrect headlight control module software may cause the parking lights not to operate as intended when the headlights are turned on and the turn signals are activated, which can reduce.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the headlight control module with a new data set, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V125000: Electrical System:12V/24V/48V Battery](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Audi&model=Q4+e-tron&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** A loss of drive power increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the OCDC, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

When shopping for a used Audi Q4 e-tron, prioritize models with a clean vehicle history report and verification that all factory electrical recalls have been completed. A thorough pre-purchase inspection focusing on software versions, brake system responsiveness, and auxiliary electronics will help ensure a more confident ownership experience.
