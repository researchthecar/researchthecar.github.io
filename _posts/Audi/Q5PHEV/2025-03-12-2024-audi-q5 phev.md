Alright, here's a breakdown of common issues and complaints associated with the 2024 Audi Q5 PHEV, formatted for your website.

The 2024 Audi Q5 PHEV is a plug-in hybrid SUV that combines luxury with efficiency. The Q5 lineup also offers traditional gasoline engines, including a 40 TFSI producing 201 horsepower and 236 lb-ft of torque and a 45 TFSI that produces 261 horsepower and 273 lb-ft of torque. The Q5 PHEV, however, boasts a turbocharged 2.0-liter four-cylinder engine paired with an electric motor, delivering a combined 362 horsepower and 369 lb-ft of torque. Owners often praise the Q5 PHEV for its comfortable ride, stylish interior, and impressive fuel economy when utilizing the electric motor. Criticisms often revolve around the higher price point compared to non-hybrid models, the limited all-electric range, and occasionally complex infotainment system.

**Electric Motor**

**2.0-Liter Turbocharged Four-Cylinder with Electric Motor (PHEV)**

*   **Reduced Electric Range/Charging Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners report the actual all-electric range falling short of the advertised range, especially in colder temperatures. Others experience issues with the charging process, including slow charging speeds or the charging process failing to initiate or complete.
    *   Solution: Verify proper charging cable connection and charging station functionality. Check the vehicle's charging settings and ensure they are optimized. A software update from the dealer may be necessary to address some charging-related glitches. Battery issues are rare but might require replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: Software update is typically free. Battery replacement can be very expensive ($8,000-$15,000+).
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**7-Speed S tronic Dual-Clutch Transmission**

*   **Hesitation/Jerky Shifts**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have noted occasional hesitation or jerky shifts, particularly at low speeds or during stop-and-go traffic. This is more prevalent in early production models.
    *   Solution: A software update to the transmission control module (TCM) may resolve the issue. In some cases, a transmission fluid flush or, rarely, valve body replacement might be needed.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: Software update is typically free or low cost. Transmission fluid flush is around $300-$500. Valve body replacement can be $1,500 - $3,000+.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Freezing/Glitches**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Reports of the infotainment system freezing, lagging, or exhibiting other glitches. This can include issues with the navigation system, Bluetooth connectivity, or unresponsive touch screen.
    *   Solution: A software update from the dealer is the most common solution. In some cases, resetting the system or replacing the infotainment unit may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: Software update is typically free. Infotainment unit replacement can range from $1,000 - $3,000+.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Sensor Malfunctions (Parking Sensors, Driver Assist Systems)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Intermittent or persistent errors related to parking sensors, lane departure warning, adaptive cruise control, or other driver-assist features.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis to identify the faulty sensor. Replacement of the defective sensor is usually required. Calibration of the system may also be needed.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200-$800+ depending on the sensor and calibration needs.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Rattling/Squeaking Noises**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported rattling or squeaking noises emanating from the dashboard, door panels, or other interior trim pieces.
    *   Solution: Locating and isolating the source of the noise. This may involve tightening loose fasteners, adding felt padding, or replacing the affected trim piece.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies widely depending on the severity and location of the noise. Can range from a simple DIY fix to several hundred dollars at a shop.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **LED Lighting Issues (Headlights, Taillights)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: LED headlights or taillights failing prematurely or exhibiting flickering issues.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the affected LED unit.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1500+ per headlight/taillight assembly, depending on the specific light and labor costs.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2024 Audi Q5 PHEV, while generally a reliable and well-regarded vehicle, does have some common issues. The electric range and charging issues are prominent for PHEV owners. The infotainment system glitches are common across modern vehicles. The cost to fix these issues can vary widely, with software updates typically being free, but more significant repairs, such as battery or infotainment system replacement, can be expensive. However, keep in mind that vehicles in their early years of production are more likely to have issues compared to later years in production.
