**Introduction**

The 2022 Audi Q5 PHEV is a luxury compact SUV that offers the versatility of a plug-in hybrid powertrain. It is powered by a 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine paired with a 17.9 kWh battery pack and an electric motor. The powertrain delivers a combined output of 362 horsepower and 369 lb-ft of torque. The Q5 PHEV comes with an eight-speed automatic transmission and standard quattro all-wheel drive.

Owners appreciate the Q5 PHEV's comfortable and spacious interior, impressive fuel economy, and smooth driving dynamics. However, some have reported issues with the vehicle's electrical system, suspension, and exterior.

**Electrical**

- **Battery Pack Failure**
   - Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, decreased fuel economy, battery warning light illumination
   - Solution: Battery pack replacement
   - Estimated Cost to Fix: $10,000 - $15,000+
   - Recalls: None Found
- **Electrical System Overheating**
   - Description/Symptoms: Smoke or burning smell coming from under the hood, warning lights illumination, loss of electrical power
   - Solution: Inspection and repair or replacement of faulty electrical components
   - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+
   - Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

- **Infotainment System Malfunction**

   - Solution: Software update or replacement of infotainment module

**Summary**

The 2022 Audi Q5 PHEV has received mixed reviews regarding its reliability. While some owners have experienced significant issues with the electrical system, others have reported few problems. The severity and cost of repairs can vary depending on the specific issue and the necessary replacement parts. Overall, it is important to consider potential issues with the electrical system and infotainment system before purchasing this vehicle.
