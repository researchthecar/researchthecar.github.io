# 2019 Hyundai Kona Electric Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2019 Hyundai Kona Electric, prospective buyers will find an appealing, efficient electric crossover that offers punchy performance and solid daily usability. However, owner reports submitted to safety authorities indicate certain recurring mechanical and electrical concerns—particularly regarding drive units and battery systems—that warrant careful inspection before purchase. While many owners enjoy trouble-free daily driving, reviewing service records and verifying completed recall work are crucial steps for anyone considering this EV.

---

## Engine

### Permanent Magnet Synchronous Electric Motor (201 hp)

The Kona Electric features a permanent magnet synchronous electric motor producing 201 horsepower and 291 lb-ft of torque, delivering brisk acceleration. Overall reliability is generally good, though early models have experienced occasional reports of internal motor noise and component failure.

**Internal Motor Noise and Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have reported a progressively worsening clicking or ticking noise under acceleration and deceleration, sometimes leading to internal motor failure.
- **Solution:** Requires professional diagnosis by a certified dealer or EV technician, which may involve motor replacement under warranty.

**Unexpected Acceleration**
- **Symptoms:** A small number of owners reported incidents where the vehicle accelerated rapidly from a stop without driver input and failed to slow down when the brake pedal was pressed.
- **Solution:** Have the throttle pedal assembly, software calibration, and motor control units thoroughly inspected by a qualified technician.

## Transmission

### Single-Speed Reduction Gearbox

Power is sent to the front wheels via a simple single-speed reduction transmission. While mechanically straightforward compared to traditional multi-speed gearboxes, a subset of owners have experienced gear reduction unit wear or failure requiring repair.

**Reduction Gear Unit (GRU) Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may notice an increasingly loud mechanical noise while driving, which can potentially lead to the gear drive locking up and causing a sudden loss of propulsion.
- **Solution:** Inspection of the reduction gear unit is necessary; failing units require replacement of the gear assembly.

## Electrical

### High-Voltage Battery and Charging System

The electrical architecture manages the high-voltage lithium-ion battery pack and onboard charging systems. Ensuring past safety updates and battery inspection campaigns have been performed is essential for long-term peace of mind.

**Charging Equipment and Battery Delays**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have noted difficulties with external charging hardware or experienced long delays waiting for manufacturer battery safety recall remedies and parts.
- **Solution:** Verify through a dealership VIN check that all high-voltage battery safety campaigns and software updates have been fully completed.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V127000: Electrical System:Propulsion System:Traction Battery](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Hyundai&model=Kona+Electric&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** An electrical short in the Li-ion battery increases the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Once parts are available, dealers will inspect the Battery System Assembly (BSA), and replace it if necessary. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V630000: Electrical System:Propulsion System:Traction Battery:Management System/Energy Control Module (Bms/Becm):Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Hyundai&model=Kona+Electric&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** An electrical short in the Li-ion battery increases the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Hyundai will notify owners, and dealers will update the Battery Management System (BMS) software and inspect the Li-ion battery, replacing it if necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V748000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Power Assist:Electric:Control Module:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Hyundai&model=Kona+Electric&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** Reduced braking power could lengthen the distance required to stop the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Hyundai will notify owners, and dealers will update the IEB software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2019 Hyundai Kona Electric is a compelling used electric vehicle choice thanks to its range and driving dynamics, but it demands thorough due diligence. Buyers should prioritize checking vehicle history reports to confirm that all manufacturer recalls—particularly those concerning the electrical system and battery—have been addressed. A pre-purchase inspection by an EV-certified mechanic focusing on the electric motor and reduction gear unit will help ensure a reliable ownership experience.
