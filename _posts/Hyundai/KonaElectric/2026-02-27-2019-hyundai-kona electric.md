# 2019 Hyundai Kona Electric

### Overview
The 2019 Hyundai Kona Electric is a compact electric SUV that has generally received positive reviews for its practicality, range, and technology. As an electric vehicle, its reliability profile differs significantly from internal combustion engine counterparts, focusing on battery health, electric motor performance, and charging systems. Owners often praise its urban maneuverability and user-friendly infotainment. While specific component failures are less common than in traditional vehicles, potential areas of concern for any EV include battery degradation over time and issues with the charging infrastructure or onboard charger. Overall, the Kona Electric has demonstrated a solid reliability record for its model year, with most reported issues being minor or related to software.

### Battery Pack
**Battery Degradation**
- **Symptoms:** Reduced driving range over time, slower charging speeds.
- **Solution:** Battery replacement (typically covered under warranty for a significant period).
- **Cost:** $5,000 - $15,000+

**Onboard Charger Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Failure to charge, intermittent charging, error messages related to charging.

### Summary Verdict
The 2019 Hyundai Kona Electric generally exhibits good reliability, particularly in its powertrain components which are simpler than those in gasoline cars. The primary concerns for electric vehicles of this era revolve around the long-term health of the battery pack, with degradation being a natural process, and potential issues with the onboard charging system. Software glitches in the infotainment system are also a possibility, though often resolvable with updates. Other systems like suspension, exterior, and interior components have shown no widespread significant issues. Owners should be aware of battery warranty terms and potential costs associated with out-of-warranty battery replacement, though this is a long-term consideration.
