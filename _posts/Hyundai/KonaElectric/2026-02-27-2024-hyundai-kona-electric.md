# 2024 Hyundai Kona Electric Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2024 Hyundai Kona Electric, prospective buyers will find a modern, zero-emission subcompact crossover that generally offers a smooth driving experience and ample everyday utility. However, owner reports submitted to safety agencies highlight a few recurring electronic and powertrain hiccups worth keeping on your radar during a test drive. Reviewing these specific owner experiences can help you ask the right questions and ensure any potential software or hardware issues have been properly inspected.

---

## Engine

### Standard Range Electric Motor (133 hp)

This base electric motor produces approximately 133 horsepower and 188 lb-ft of torque, driving the front wheels. It provides peppy city performance and efficient everyday commuting, though long-term reliability for this newer platform relies heavily on software health.

### Long Range Electric Motor (201 hp)

Paired with a larger battery pack, this electric motor generates roughly 201 horsepower and 188 lb-ft of torque for quicker acceleration and extended driving range. While generally responsive, some owners have flagged intermittent power delivery concerns that warrant a thorough inspection.

**Motive Power Loss and Acceleration Hesitation**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have reported unexpected losses of motive power or hesitation while depressing the accelerator pedal, particularly when driving up inclines.
- **Solution:** Have a dealer check for diagnostic trouble codes and perform any applicable powertrain control module or battery management software updates.

## Transmission

### Single-Speed Direct Drive Transmission

The single-speed direct drive transmission delivers seamless power to the front wheels without traditional gear shifting. It is mechanically simple and generally trouble-free, though it remains tied to the vehicle's overarching electronic control systems.

## Electrical

### Instrument Cluster and Infotainment Screens

The digital cockpit relies heavily on interconnected screens and software modules, which have occasionally exhibited intermittent blackout issues reported by drivers.

**Intermittent Blackout of Displays and Headlights**
- **Symptoms:** Owners note that the instrument panel, infotainment screen, and exterior headlights can temporarily go completely black or cut out while driving before rebooting.
- **Solution:** Inspect software versions and electrical connections; dealerships may need to apply targeted software patches or replace faulty display modules.

## Safety

### Steering Column and Advanced Safety Integration

The electronic power steering system integrates with lane-keeping and collision-avoidance aids, meaning software glitches can occasionally influence steering feel.

**Intermittent Steering Lockup**
- **Symptoms:** Rare complaints indicate the steering wheel may feel locked or stiff during a turn, coinciding with advanced driver-assistance activations.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified technician inspect the steering assembly, electronic power steering motor, and related safety system sensors.

## Summary Verdict

The 2024 Hyundai Kona Electric brings appealing efficiency and modern technology to the used electric vehicle market, but buyers should proceed with a careful pre-purchase inspection. Pay close attention to how the vehicle accelerates under load, and verify that all infotainment, lighting, and safety software updates have been completed by a certified dealer to minimize the risk of electronic glitches.
