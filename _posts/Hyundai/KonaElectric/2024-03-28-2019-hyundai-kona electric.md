**Introduction to the 2019 Hyundai Kona Electric**

The Hyundai Kona Electric is an all-electric subcompact crossover SUV. It offers an estimated driving range of 258 miles on a single charge, making it a compelling option for drivers looking for an eco-friendly alternative. The Kona Electric is powered by a single electric motor that produces 201 horsepower and 291 lb-ft of torque. The motor is paired with a single-speed transmission.

The Kona Electric has been praised for its spacious interior, comfortable ride, and impressive driving range. However, some owners have reported issues with the vehicle's reliability and overall quality.

**Electric Motor**

**Electrical**

- **Battery degradation:** Some owners have reported that their Kona Electric's battery capacity has decreased over time, resulting in a reduced driving range.
- **Battery thermal management:** Owners have reported issues with the battery overheating in hot weather, which can reduce the vehicle's range and performance.
- **Charging port issues:** Several owners have reported problems with the charging port, including difficulty plugging in the charger and charging interruptions.

**Transmission**

**Single-speed transmission**
- **Transmission failure:** A few owners have reported that their Kona Electric's transmission has failed, resulting in the vehicle being unable to drive.

**Summary**

The 2019 Hyundai Kona Electric is an impressive all-electric vehicle with a spacious interior and impressive driving range. However, some owners have reported issues with the vehicle's reliability and overall quality, including battery degradation, charging port issues, and transmission failures. The severity and cost of these issues can vary, depending on the specific problem.
