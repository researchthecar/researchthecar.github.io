# 2020 Hyundai Kona Electric Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2020 Hyundai Kona Electric delivers lively performance, impressive efficiency, and a practical crossover shape, but prospective buyers should carefully review known mechanical and electrical pain points. While overall owner satisfaction can be high due to low running costs, owner complaints highlight recurring concerns with powertrain behavior, regenerative braking systems, and electrical components. Understanding these common trouble spots will help you evaluate potential used models with confidence.

---

## Engine

### Permanent Magnet Synchronous Electric Motor (201 hp)

The Kona Electric features a front-mounted permanent magnet synchronous motor generating approximately 201 horsepower and 291 lb-ft of torque, powered by a 64 kWh lithium-ion battery pack. This powertrain provides brisk acceleration and strong efficiency, though buyers should watch for rare reports of sudden power loss.

**Motor Stalling and Motive Power Loss**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers report sudden unexpected engine or propulsion stalling while driving at various speeds, accompanied by warning lights and a total loss of motive power.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified EV technician inspect the high-voltage system, inverter, and associated software modules for diagnostic trouble codes.

## Transmission

### Single-Speed Direct Drive Reduction Gear

The electric drivetrain utilizes a simple single-speed direct-drive reduction gear box rather than a conventional multi-speed transmission. This setup generally offers robust reliability and minimal maintenance, though minor mechanical noises or control module glitches have occasionally been reported.

**Drive Unit Hesitation and Noise**
- **Symptoms:** Unusual grinding sounds or delayed response when attempting to accelerate after decelerating or driving at low speeds.
- **Solution:** Inspect the reduction gear assembly and fluid condition, and ensure all relevant powertrain software updates have been applied.

## Brakes

### Regenerative Braking System

The regenerative braking system captures energy during deceleration to recharge the high-voltage battery while blending friction brakes for stopping. While generally seamless, intermittent electronic warnings and actuator glitches require careful inspection.

**Regenerative Brake Failure Warning**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers experience intermittent dashboard warnings indicating regenerative brake failure, which can occasionally coincide with reduced or delayed mechanical braking responsiveness.
- **Solution:** Inspect brake fluid levels, wheel speed sensors, and the integrated electronic brake actuator module for fault codes.

## Steering

### Electric Power Steering Column

The vehicle employs an electric power steering system that provides adjustable feel and light low-speed maneuverability. Owners have occasionally noted mechanical noise from the steering column housing requiring component replacement.

**Steering Column Noise and Resistance**
- **Symptoms:** Unusual clicking or clunking noises from the steering wheel and column when turning, accompanied by potential dashboard warnings for the electric power steering system.
- **Solution:** Inspect the steering column housing, intermediate shaft, and locking bolts for wear or looseness, replacing components as necessary.

## Electrical

### 12-Volt Battery and Charging System

Electrical architecture relies on both a high-voltage traction pack and a standard 12-volt battery to run vehicle accessories and computers. Battery drain issues and exterior lighting bulb wear are among the more frequently cited electrical gripes.

**12V Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** The 12-volt accessory battery frequently dies or fails to hold a charge, leaving the vehicle unable to power up accessory systems even when the high-voltage battery is fully charged.
- **Solution:** Test the 12-volt battery health and check the DC-to-DC converter module operation or recent software updates.

## Safety

### Forward Collision-Avoidance Assist

Advanced driver assistance features add an extra layer of security, but sensitive camera and radar systems can occasionally produce false alerts or unwanted braking inputs.

**Unexpected Autonomous Braking**
- **Symptoms:** The forward collision-avoidance system triggers false-positive warnings or sudden unexpected emergency braking maneuvers during normal traffic flow.
- **Solution:** Clean the windshield camera and front radar sensors, and verify that sensor calibration and software versions are up to date.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V127000: Electrical System:Propulsion System:Traction Battery](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Hyundai&model=Kona+Electric&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** An electrical short in the Li-ion battery increases the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Once parts are available, dealers will inspect the Battery System Assembly (BSA), and replace it if necessary. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V630000: Electrical System:Propulsion System:Traction Battery:Management System/Energy Control Module (Bms/Becm):Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Hyundai&model=Kona+Electric&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** An electrical short in the Li-ion battery increases the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Hyundai will notify owners, and dealers will update the Battery Management System (BMS) software and inspect the Li-ion battery, replacing it if necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V748000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Power Assist:Electric:Control Module:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Hyundai&model=Kona+Electric&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** Reduced braking power could lengthen the distance required to stop the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Hyundai will notify owners, and dealers will update the IEB software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2020 Hyundai Kona Electric is an appealing choice for used EV buyers seeking strong range and everyday usability, but it demands a thorough pre-purchase inspection. Pay close attention to the health of the 12-volt battery system, verify that all recall and software updates have been completed, and test drive the vehicle to check for any regenerative braking or steering noises. Choosing a well-documented model with verified dealer service history will help mitigate potential ownership risks.
