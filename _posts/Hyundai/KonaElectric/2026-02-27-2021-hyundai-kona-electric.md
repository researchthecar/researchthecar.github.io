# 2021 Hyundai Kona Electric Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2021 Hyundai Kona Electric offers an appealing all-electric driving experience with respectable range and daily usability for used-car shoppers. While powertrain options are streamlined for electric mobility, prospective buyers should review available owner feedback regarding everyday ownership and electrical system quirks before purchasing.

---

## Engine

### Permanent Magnet Synchronous Motor (201 hp)

This 201-hp permanent magnet synchronous motor delivers robust torque and smooth acceleration for everyday driving. It serves as the sole electric drive option for this model year, offering generally reliable performance with zero tailpipe emissions.

## Transmission

### Single-Speed Reduction Gear Transmission

The single-speed reduction gear transmission provides seamless power delivery without traditional gear shifts. Its simple mechanical layout contributes to low maintenance requirements and dependable daily operation.

---

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V748000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Power Assist:Electric:Control Module:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Hyundai&model=Kona+Electric&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** Reduced braking power could lengthen the distance required to stop the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Hyundai will notify owners, and dealers will update the IEB software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V941000: Electrical System:Propulsion System:Traction Battery:Thermal Management:Fluid/Coolant](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Hyundai&model=Kona+Electric&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** Coolant contacting the EPCU circuit board may cause a loss of drive power, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the EPCU, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

Overall, the 2021 Hyundai Kona Electric remains a compelling choice in the used electric vehicle market thanks to its efficient powertrain and practical hatchback design. Buyers should conduct a thorough inspection of the vehicle's high-voltage battery health, charging capabilities, and electrical accessories prior to finalizing a purchase.
