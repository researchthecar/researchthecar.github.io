**2005 Hyundai Tiburon**

The 2005 Hyundai Tiburon is a compact sports car with two available engine and transmission options: a 2.0-liter inline-4 with a 5-speed manual transmission or a 4-speed automatic transmission, and a 2.7-liter V6 with a 6-speed manual transmission or a 5-speed automatic transmission. The 2.0-liter engine produces 138 horsepower and 136 lb-ft of torque, while the 2.7-liter V6 produces 172 horsepower and 181 lb-ft of torque.

Owners of the 2005 Hyundai Tiburon generally praise its sporty handling, responsive steering, and affordable price. However, some common issues have been reported with the vehicle, including:

**Engine**

**2.0-liter Inline-4**

* **Timing belt tensioner failure:** The timing belt tensioner can fail, causing the timing belt to slip and potentially damage the engine. Symptoms include a rattling noise from the engine, difficulty starting the engine, and a loss of power. The solution is to replace the timing belt tensioner. Estimated cost to fix: $500 - $800
* **Valve cover gasket leak:** The valve cover gasket can leak oil, causing a burning oil smell and a loss of oil. The solution is to replace the valve cover gasket. Estimated cost to fix: $200 - $400
* **Intake manifold gasket leak:** The intake manifold gasket can leak coolant, causing a loss of coolant and reduced engine performance. The solution is to replace the intake manifold gasket. Estimated cost to fix: $250 - $450

**2.7-liter V6**

* **No major engine issues reported**

**Transmission**

**5-speed Manual Transmission**

* **Transmission fluid leak:** The transmission fluid can leak from the transmission case, causing a loss of transmission fluid and difficulty shifting gears. The solution is to replace the transmission case seal. Estimated cost to fix: $200 - $400
* **Clutch failure:** The clutch can fail, causing difficulty shifting gears or a loss of power. The solution is to replace the clutch. Estimated cost to fix: $600 - $1,000

**4-speed Automatic Transmission**

* **Transmission slipping:** The transmission can slip gears, causing a loss of power and difficulty accelerating. The solution is to replace the transmission. Estimated cost to fix: $1,500 - $2,500

**6-speed Manual Transmission**

* **No major transmission issues reported**

**5-speed Automatic Transmission**

* **No major transmission issues reported**

**Suspension**

* **Strut mount failure:** The strut mounts can fail, causing a clunking noise and a loss of stability. The solution is to replace the strut mounts. Estimated cost to fix: $400 - $600
* **Sway bar link failure:** The sway bar links can fail, causing a rattling noise and a loss of stability. The solution is to replace the sway bar links. Estimated cost to fix: $200 - $400

**Electrical**

* **Alternator failure:** The alternator can fail, causing a loss of electrical power and a dead battery. The solution is to replace the alternator. Estimated cost to fix: $400 - $600
* **Battery drain:** The battery can drain quickly, causing difficulty starting the vehicle. The solution is to diagnose and repair the source of the battery drain. Estimated cost to fix: $200 - $600

**Exterior**

* **Headlight condensation:** Condensation can build up inside the headlights, causing poor visibility at night. The solution is to replace the headlights with seal. Estimated cost to fix: $400 - $800
* **Tail light failure:** The tail lights can fail, reducing visibility at night. The solution is to replace the tail light bulbs. Estimated cost to fix: $100 - $200

**Interior**

* **Window switch failure:** The window switches can fail, causing the windows to become inoperable. The solution is to replace the window switches. Estimated cost to fix: $100 - $200
* **A/C compressor failure:** The A/C compressor can fail, causing the air conditioning to stop working. The solution is to replace the A/C compressor. Estimated cost to fix: $600 - $1,000

**Summary**

The 2005 Hyundai Tiburon is a generally reliable vehicle with some common issues to be aware of. The most severe and expensive issues include timing belt tensioner failure, transmission slipping, and A/C compressor failure. However, many of the issues are relatively minor and can be fixed at a reasonable cost.
