**Introduction**

The 2007 Hyundai Tiburon is a compact sports coupe that offers a sporty driving experience and a stylish design. It is available with two engine options: a 2.0L 4-cylinder engine with 140 horsepower and 133 lb-ft of torque, or a 2.7L V6 engine with 172 horsepower and 181 lb-ft of torque. Transmission options include a 5-speed manual transmission, a 4-speed automatic transmission, or a 6-speed manual transmission.

Owners of the 2007 Hyundai Tiburon generally praise its sporty handling, responsive steering, and comfortable interior. However, there have been some reported issues with the vehicle, including:

**Engine**

**2.0L 4-Cylinder Engine**

* **Intake Manifold Gasket Failure:** The intake manifold gasket may fail, causing a vacuum leak and reduced engine performance. Symptoms include rough idling, stalling, and decreased fuel economy. It is fixed by replacing the intake manifold gasket. Estimated cost: $200 - $400
* **Timing Belt Failure:** The timing belt may fail, causing severe engine damage. Symptoms include a rattling noise from the engine, difficulty starting, and reduced engine power. It is fixed by replacing the timing belt and related components. Estimated cost: $500 - $1,000+

**2.7L V6 Engine**

* **Valve Cover Gasket Leak:** The valve cover gasket may leak oil, causing a loss of engine oil and reduced engine performance. Symptoms include an oil leak from the engine, a burning oil smell, and decreased oil pressure. It is fixed by replacing the valve cover gasket. Estimated cost: $150 - $300

**Transmission**

**5-Speed Manual Transmission**

* **Synchronizer Failure:** The synchronizers may fail, causing difficulty shifting gears. Symptoms include grinding noises when shifting, difficulty engaging gears, and a popping noise when shifting into gear. It is fixed by replacing the synchronizers. Estimated cost: $500 - $800

**4-Speed Automatic Transmission**

* **Slipping Transmission:** The transmission may slip, causing a loss of power and difficulty accelerating. Symptoms include erratic shifting, delayed engagement, and a whining noise from the transmission. It is fixed by rebuilding or replacing the transmission. Estimated cost: $1,500 - $3,000+

**6-Speed Manual Transmission**

* **No Issues Found**

**Suspension**

* **Strut Mount Bearing Failure:** The strut mount bearings may fail, causing a clunking noise when turning or driving over bumps. Symptoms include a rattling noise from the suspension, reduced steering response, and uneven tire wear. It is fixed by replacing the strut mount bearings. Estimated cost: $200 - $400

**Electrical**

* **Alternator Failure:** The alternator may fail, causing a loss of electrical power and difficulty starting the vehicle. Symptoms include a dimming of lights, a flickering dashboard, and difficulty starting the engine. It is fixed by replacing the alternator. Estimated cost: $300 - $600
* **Battery Drain:** The battery may drain prematurely, causing difficulty starting the vehicle. Symptoms include a dead battery, a clicking noise when starting the engine, and reduced electrical power. It is fixed by testing the battery and electrical system to find the source of the drain. Estimated cost: $100 - $400

**Exterior**

* **Faded Headlights:** The headlights may fade or yellow over time, reducing visibility. It is fixed by replacing the headlights. Estimated cost: $200 - $400
* **Door Lock Failure:** The door locks may fail to engage or disengage, causing difficulty entering or exiting the vehicle. Symptoms include a door that will not open or close, a rattling noise from the door lock, and difficulty locking or unlocking the doors. It is fixed by replacing the door lock actuator or solenoid. Estimated cost: $150 - $300

**Interior**

* **Power Window Switch Failure:** The power window switch may fail, causing the windows to not operate properly. Symptoms include windows that will not open or close, windows that open or close intermittently, and a clicking noise from the switch. It is fixed by replacing the power window switch. Estimated cost: $100 - $200

**Summary**

Overall, the 2007 Hyundai Tiburon is a reliable and sporty vehicle with a few potential issues. The engine and transmission issues can be costly to repair, but they are relatively uncommon. The suspension, electrical, exterior, and interior issues are generally less severe and less expensive to fix.
