# 2022 Hyundai Tucson Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2022 Hyundai Tucson introduced a striking redesign, advanced technology, and a diverse lineup of powertrains, including internal combustion, hybrid, and plug-in hybrid options. While many owners praise its modern styling, comfortable ride, and practical interior space, used-car buyers should approach this model year with care. A notable cluster of owner complaints centers around engine and fuel system performance, specifically concerning fuel injector reliability, engine misfires, and unexpected stalling. Thoroughly reviewing service histories and ensuring any powertrain updates have been addressed is strongly recommended before purchasing.

---

## Engine

### 2.5L 4-Cylinder Engine

The base 2.5-liter naturally aspirated four-cylinder engine produces approximately 187 horsepower and 178 lb-ft of torque. It provides adequate daily power for routine commuting, though it has generated significant owner complaints regarding fuel injector malfunctions.

**Fuel Injector Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Owners report sudden illumination of warning lights, engine misfires, rough idling, hesitation during acceleration, and occasional loss of motive power while driving.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified mechanic inspect the fuel injection system. Faulty injectors require replacement with updated components.

**Engine Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may unexpectedly lose power, stall while driving at various speeds, or experience difficulty restarting after coming to a stop.
- **Solution:** Diagnose the underlying fuel delivery or electrical fault, which frequently traces back to failing fuel injectors or related engine management sensors.

### 1.6L Turbocharged 4-Cylinder Hybrid / Plug-In Hybrid Powertrain

The 1.6-liter turbocharged hybrid and plug-in hybrid systems pair forced induction with an electric motor to generate a combined 226 to 261 horsepower. While offering punchy acceleration and improved fuel economy, some owners report complex electronic and hybrid powertrain warnings.

**Hybrid System Shutdown and Limp Mode**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers experience unexpected dashboard warnings instructing them to pull over safely, followed by a total loss of motive power or entry into restrictive limp mode.
- **Solution:** Inspect hybrid powertrain control modules and electrical harnesses for faults; software updates or component replacements may be necessary.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

Paired with the base 2.5L engine, this traditional 8-speed automatic delivers smooth, predictable gear changes under normal driving conditions.

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

Used alongside the 1.6L turbocharged hybrid and plug-in hybrid setups, this 6-speed automatic transmission is engineered specifically to balance electric motor assist with traditional gear shifting.

## Exterior

### Panoramic Sunroof

The available panoramic glass roof adds an open, airy feel to the cabin but has been the subject of rare reports involving spontaneous breakage.

**Spontaneous Glass Shattering**
- **Symptoms:** The panoramic sunroof glass may suddenly fracture or shatter outward with a loud pop while driving at highway speeds, even without external impacts.
- **Solution:** Inspect the sunroof assembly for stress points and replace shattered glass panels under comprehensive insurance or warranty where applicable.

## Safety

### Forward Collision-Avoidance System

Driver assistance features like forward collision warning enhance everyday safety, but some motorists report erratic behavior from automatic braking systems.

**Phantom Braking**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may unexpectedly and aggressively apply the service brakes while driving under bridges or on open highways without any actual obstacles present.
- **Solution:** Check for available sensor calibrations and software updates at a dealership to recalibrate the forward collision-avoidance radar and cameras.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V938000: Electrical System:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Hyundai&model=Tucson&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** The software installed in the Integrated Central Control Unit (ICU) may be incompatible with the vehicle, which could result in inoperative headlights and/or taillights. Inoperative headlights or taillights can decrease visibility for both the driver and surrounding traffic, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 23V038000: Structure:Body:Roof And Pillars](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Hyundai&model=Tucson&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** A detached roof molding could become a road hazard, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace, or secure the roof moldings, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V893000: Trailer Hitches](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Hyundai&model=Tucson&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** Moisture accumulation may cause the trailer lighting to fail or an electrical short circuit. Nonfunctioning trailer lights can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will install a cap of the wiring, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2022 Hyundai Tucson offers impressive styling, a comfortable ride, and an appealing array of features and powertrain choices. However, prospective buyers should review vehicle service records carefully, paying close attention to potential fuel injector failures, engine stalling incidents, and electronic safety system behavior. Verifying that all powertrain updates have been properly completed will help ensure a much more dependable ownership experience.
