# 2016 Hyundai Tucson Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2016 Hyundai Tucson, buyers will find a stylish and feature-packed compact SUV that has unfortunately accumulated notable complaints regarding powertrain hesitation and body hardware failures. While it offers comfortable road manners and flexible engine choices, prospective owners should thoroughly investigate vehicle service histories and perform comprehensive test drives to check for mechanical anomalies before purchasing.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L 4-Cylinder Engine

The naturally aspirated 2.0-liter four-cylinder engine generates approximately 164 horsepower and serves as the standard powerplant for lower trims, delivering adequate daily performance.

**Engine Stalling and Power Loss**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle experiences unexpected stalls, illuminates check engine lights, and occasionally suffers a severe loss of motive power while driving.
- **Solution:** Diagnose fault codes, inspect electronic sensors or fuel systems, and repair or replace failing engine components.

### 1.6L Turbocharged 4-Cylinder Engine

The 1.6-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine produces around 175 horsepower, offering punchy low-end torque paired exclusively with a dual-clutch transmission.

**Excessive Oil Consumption**
- **Symptoms:** Engine burns oil rapidly, requiring frequent top-offs between oil changes and potentially triggering low oil level warnings.
- **Solution:** Monitor oil levels closely and consult a dealership or mechanic for potential internal engine inspections or repairs.

## Transmission

### 7-Speed Dual-Clutch Automatic Transmission

The 7-speed dual-clutch automatic transmission is paired with the 1.6L turbo engine, offering quick shifts but attracting significant owner complaints regarding drivability and clutch wear.

**Clutch Failure and Shuddering**
- **Symptoms:** Rough engagement, noticeable shuddering during acceleration from a stop, delayed throttle response, and complete clutch or transmission failure.
- **Solution:** Perform transmission software updates, replace worn clutch assemblies, or replace the transmission unit if internal damage is present.

## Exterior

### Door Latches and Door Locks

Interior cabin hardware components, particularly door latches and locking mechanisms, have shown a high propensity for premature failure.

**Door Latch and Actuator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Doors fail to open from either the inside or outside, leaving passengers trapped or preventing proper vehicle entry and secure locking.
- **Solution:** Replace the malfunctioning door lock actuators or integrated latch mechanisms.

## Interior

### Power Window Regulators

Electrical accessories such as power window regulators and dashboard warning systems have reported frequent operational glitches.

**Window Motor and Regulator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Power windows drop unexpectedly, fail to roll up or down, and leave the cabin exposed to weather elements.
- **Solution:** Replace the failed window motor and regulator assemblies.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V628000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission:Control Module (Tcm/Pcm/Tecm)](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Hyundai&model=Tucson&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** Certain driving conditions and high temperatures may cause the vehicle to not move if the accelerator pedal is pressed and released repeatedly. Inability to move the vehicle while in traffic may increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Hyundai will notify owners, and dealers will update the Transmission Control Module software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V842000: Electrical System:Wiring](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Hyundai&model=Tucson&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 108, " Lamps, Reflective Devices, and Associated Equipment." If the trailer brake lights stay illuminated while being used, other drivers may be confused, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Hyundai will notify owners, and dealers will replace the affected accessory trailer hitch wiring harnesses, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V147000: Exterior Lighting:Tail Lights](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Hyundai&model=Tucson&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard Number 108, " Lamps, Reflective Devices, and Associated Equipment." Failure to illuminate the trailer's turn signal lights would prevent the communication of the driver's intent, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Hyundai will notify owners, and dealers will update the tow hitch harness, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V348000: Latches/Locks/Linkages:Hood:Latch](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Hyundai&model=Tucson&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** In the affected vehicles, if the primary hood latch has been released, and the vehicle is driven, the secondary hood latch may not hold adequately secure the hood to prevent it from opening while the vehicle is in motion. If the primary hood latch is inadvertently released and the secondary latch cannot secure the hood, the hood may unexpectedly open while.
- **What to do:** Hyundai will notify owners, and dealers will replace the secondary hood latch, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V543000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Antilock/Traction Control/Electronic Limited Slip:Control Unit/Module](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Hyundai&model=Tucson&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** The Anti-lock Brake Hydraulic Electronic Control Unit (HECU) could corrode internally and cause an electrical short, possibly resulting in an engine compartment fire. An engine compartment fire can increase the risk of injury or crash.
- **What to do:** Hyundai will notify owners, and dealers will replace the HECU fuse. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2016 Hyundai Tucson presents mixed reliability for used car buyers. While it offers a comfortable ride and appealing features, prospective owners should exercise caution due to prominent complaints involving dual-clutch transmission shudder, engine performance hiccups, and widespread door latch or window regulator failures. Verifying maintenance records and confirming whether transmission or latch repairs have already been performed is highly recommended before finalizing any purchase.
