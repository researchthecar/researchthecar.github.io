**Introduction:**

The 2023 Hyundai Tucson is a compact SUV praised for its spacious interior, advanced safety features, and fuel efficiency. It is available with two engine and transmission options: a 187-hp 2.5-liter four-cylinder engine paired with an 8-speed automatic transmission, and a 227-hp 1.6-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine mated to an 8-speed dual-clutch automatic transmission.

While the Tucson has received positive reviews overall, a few issues have been reported by owners.

**Engine:**

**2.5-liter Four-Cylinder Engine:**

* **Check Engine Light Due to Fuel Filter Contamination:** Description/Symptoms: The check engine light illuminates, and the vehicle may experience reduced power or a rough idle. Solution: Replacing the contaminated fuel filter. Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300+
* **Fuel Injector Failure:** Description/Symptoms: Difficulty starting the engine, reduced power, or increased fuel consumption. Solution: Replacing the faulty fuel injectors. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
* **Timing Chain Tensioner Failure:** Description/Symptoms: A rattling noise coming from the engine, especially at startup. Solution: Replacing the timing chain tensioner. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+

**1.6-liter Turbocharged Four-Cylinder Engine:**

* **No Issues Found**

**Transmission:**

**8-Speed Automatic Transmission:**

* **Shifting Issues:** Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears, slipping gears, or delayed engagement. Solution: Reprogramming the transmission control module or replacing the solenoid pack. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,000+
* **Transmission Fluid Leak:** Description/Symptoms: Visible transmission fluid leak from the transmission pan or seals. Solution: Replacing the leaking seals or transmission pan. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+

**8-Speed Dual-Clutch Automatic Transmission:**

* **Clutch Pack Failure:** Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears, vibration during acceleration, or loss of power. Solution: Replacing the faulty clutch pack. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
* **Mechatronic Unit Failure:** Description/Symptoms: Transmission stuck in gear, difficulty shifting, or loss of power. Solution: Replacing the mechatronic unit. Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,000+

**Suspension:**

* **Strut Mount Bearing Failure:** Description/Symptoms: Clunking or squeaking noises when turning or driving over bumps. Solution: Replacing the faulty strut mount bearings. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+

**Electrical:**

* **Battery Drain:** Description/Symptoms: Difficulty starting the vehicle or dead battery. Solution: Diagnosing electrical systems and replacing faulty components. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
* **Malfunctioning Infotainment System:** Description/Symptoms: Intermittent touch response, display issues, or software glitches. Solution: Updating software or replacing faulty components. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $1,000+

**Exterior:**

* **Loose Tailgate Handle:** Description/Symptoms: The tailgate handle becomes loose and does not open or close properly. Solution: Tightening or replacing the tailgate handle. Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300+

**Interior:**

* **Plastic Panel Squeaks and Rattles:** Description/Symptoms: Loose plastic panels in the interior that create squeaking or rattling noises. Solution: Applying foam insulation or tightening the panels. Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 - $200+

**Summary:**

Overall, the 2023 Hyundai Tucson has received positive reviews, but there are some known issues that owners have reported. The severity and cost of these issues can vary depending on the individual case. Potential buyers should be aware of these issues and consider them when making a decision to purchase this vehicle.
