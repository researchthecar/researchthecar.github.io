**Introduction:**

The 2017 Hyundai Tucson is a compact SUV available with two engine options: a 2.0L 4-cylinder and a 2.4L 4-cylinder. The 2.0L engine produces 164 horsepower and 151 lb-ft of torque, while the 2.4L engine produces 181 horsepower and 175 lb-ft of torque. Both engines can be paired with either a 6-speed automatic or 6-speed manual transmission.

Owners appreciate the Tucson's spacious interior, comfortable ride, and impressive fuel economy. However, they criticize its sluggish acceleration and lack of premium features.

**Engine**

**2.0L 4-Cylinder**

**No Issues Found**

**2.4L 4-Cylinder**

**Excessive Oil Consumption**
Description/Symptoms: Engine consumes an excessive amount of oil, requiring frequent top-offs.
Solution: Replace piston rings and replace entire engine in severe cases.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $6,000+
Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**6-Speed Automatic**

**Shifting Issues**
Description/Symptoms: Transmission hesitates or refuses to shift gears.
Solution: Update transmission control module software, replace transmission fluid, or replace transmission.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $4,000+
Recalls: NHTSA 20V-451

**Suspension**

**No Issues Found**

**Electrical**

**Electrical System Failure**
Description/Symptoms: Loss of electrical power to various components, including headlights, taillights, and dashboard gauges.
Solution: Replace faulty wiring harness or electrical components.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+
Recalls: NHTSA 21V-469

**Exterior**

**No Issues Found**

**Interior**

**Loose Dashboard Trim**
Description/Symptoms: Dashboard trim panels may become loose or detached.
Solution: Reattach trim panels with adhesive or replace them.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300
Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2017 Hyundai Tucson is a generally reliable vehicle with a few potential issues. The 2.4L engine's excessive oil consumption can be a serious and expensive problem. Transmission shifting issues can also be costly to repair. However, the majority of issues are relatively minor and inexpensive to fix.
