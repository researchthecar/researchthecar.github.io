**Introduction:**

The 2012 Hyundai Tucson is a compact crossover SUV renowned for its reliability, versatility, and fuel efficiency. It's powered by a choice of two engines paired with either a six-speed automatic or six-speed manual transmission. Despite its overall popularity, the 2012 Tucson has been plagued by a range of issues affecting different components.

**Engine**

**2.0L Theta II Gasoline Engine**

* **Intake Valve Carbon Buildup:**
   - Description/Symptoms: Reduced engine power, rough idle, and increased fuel consumption due to carbon buildup on intake valves.
   - Solution: Valve cleaning or replacement.
   - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
* **Timing Chain Failure:**
   - Description/Symptoms: Loud rattling noises from the engine, engine failure, or loss of power.
   - Solution: Timing chain replacement.
   - Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
* **Engine Misfire or Knock:**
   - Description/Symptoms: Irregular engine idle, loss of power, or knocking sounds.
   - Solution: Spark plug or coil replacement, fuel injector cleaning, or engine repair.
   - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+

**Transmission**

**Six-Speed Automatic Transmission**

* **Slipping or Jerking:**
   - Description/Symptoms: Transmission slipping gears, rough shifting, or hesitation.
   - Solution: Transmission fluid change or replacement.
   - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,200+

**Six-Speed Manual Transmission**

* **No Issues Found**

**Suspension**

* **Rear Suspension Noise:**
   - Description/Symptoms: Rattle or clunking noise coming from the rear suspension.
   - Solution: Replacement of rear sway bar links or bushings.
   - Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $500+
* **Front Strut Failure:**
   - Description/Symptoms: Leaking struts, reduced ride quality, or excessive bounce.
   - Solution: Strut replacement.
   - Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+

**Electrical**

* **Electrical System Failure:**
   - Description/Symptoms: Battery draining quickly, electronic failures, or flickering headlights.
   - Solution: Alternator or battery replacement.
   - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+
* **Headlight Switch Failure:**
   - Description/Symptoms: Headlights intermittently working or not functioning at all.
   - Solution: Headlight switch replacement.
   - Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+
* **Clock Spring Failure:**
   - Description/Symptoms: Airbag warning light illuminated, horn not working, or cruise control malfunctions.
   - Solution: Clock spring replacement.
   - Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800+

**Exterior**

* **Hood Latch Failure:**
   - Description/Symptoms: Hood not opening or remaining latched.
   - Solution: Hood latch replacement.
   - Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+

**Interior**

* **Rear Seat Folding Issue:**
   - Description/Symptoms: Rear seats not folding down completely.
   - Solution: Adjustment or replacement of rear seat hinges.
   - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
* **Climate Control Malfunction:**
   - Description/Symptoms: Difficulty controlling temperature or fan speed.
   - Solution: Climate control module repair or replacement.
   - Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $600+

**Summary:**

While the 2012 Hyundai Tucson offers a compelling overall package, it has been plagued by a range of issues affecting various components. The severity of these issues varies, with some being relatively minor and inexpensive to fix, while others, such as timing chain failure or electrical system failure, can be more costly and time-consuming to repair. It's important for potential owners to be aware of these issues before making a purchase decision and to budget accordingly for potential repairs or maintenance.
