# 2017 Hyundai Tucson Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2017 Hyundai Tucson is a compact crossover SUV that offers a comfortable ride, practical interior space, and competitive styling. However, used-car buyers should approach this model year with caution due to a notable volume of owner complaints regarding powertrain performance and electrical concerns.

---

## Engine

### 1.6L Turbocharged Inline-4

The 1.6-liter turbocharged inline-4 engine produces approximately 175 horsepower and is paired exclusively with a dual-clutch transmission. While offering good pep and efficiency, this engine-transmission combination is frequently cited in owner complaints for drivability and hesitation issues.

**Engine Stalling and Loss of Power**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers report unexpected engine stalling, hesitation, and sudden loss of motive power while driving or attempting to accelerate in traffic.
- **Solution:** Diagnostic evaluation by a certified technician; software updates or component replacement may be required depending on the root cause.

### 2.0L Naturally Aspirated Inline-4

The 2.0-liter naturally aspirated inline-4 engine makes around 164 horsepower and serves as the baseline power plant for lower trims. It tends to exhibit fewer complex drivability complaints than the turbocharged variant.

### 2.4L Naturally Aspirated Inline-4

The 2.4-liter naturally aspirated inline-4 engine delivers about 181 horsepower on higher trims. Buyers should verify service history and check for past engine campaign completions.

**Abnormal Engine Knock and Oil Consumption**
- **Symptoms:** Unusual knocking noises from under the hood accompanied by illuminated check engine oil warning lights and increased RPMs.
- **Solution:** Inspect the engine for wear, verify oil levels, and check for applicable manufacturer service campaigns or knock sensor updates.

## Transmission

### 7-Speed Dual-Clutch Automatic Transmission

This 7-speed dual-clutch automatic transmission is paired with the 1.6L turbo engine. It is known for rough operation and premature clutch wear issues reported by owners.

**Clutch Failure and Acceleration Hesitation**
- **Symptoms:** Severe hesitation, jerky shifting, and shuddering from a complete stop, sometimes culminating in total transmission or clutch assembly failure.
- **Solution:** Replace worn clutch packs, actuators, or the transmission assembly as diagnosed by a qualified technician.

### 6-Speed Conventional Automatic Transmission

The traditional 6-speed automatic transmission is paired with the non-turbo engines and generally provides smoother, more conventional shifting behavior with fewer catastrophic complaints.

## Exterior

### Door Latch and Mechanism

Interior door hardware can experience mechanical wear resulting in trapped occupants or doors that refuse to latch or unlatch properly.

**Door Latch Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Rear or driver-side doors fail to open from either the inside or outside due to mechanical cable or latch binding.
- **Solution:** Replace the faulty door latch assembly or internal operating cables.

### Exterior Body Structure

Exterior panels generally hold up well, though certain paint colors are prone to premature clear coat failure and flaking.

**Paint Peeling and Delamination**
- **Symptoms:** Factory paint begins peeling off in large chunks from the hood, roof, and doors, exposing bare metal to potential rust.
- **Solution:** Repaint affected body panels; consult dealership for potential goodwill or paint warranty coverage if applicable.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V842000: Electrical System:Wiring](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Hyundai&model=Tucson&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 108, " Lamps, Reflective Devices, and Associated Equipment." If the trailer brake lights stay illuminated while being used, other drivers may be confused, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Hyundai will notify owners, and dealers will replace the affected accessory trailer hitch wiring harnesses, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V727000: Engine](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Hyundai&model=Tucson&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** A damaged engine can increase the risk of a fire or it can cause an engine stall, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the engine. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V543000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Antilock/Traction Control/Electronic Limited Slip:Control Unit/Module](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Hyundai&model=Tucson&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** The Anti-lock Brake Hydraulic Electronic Control Unit (HECU) could corrode internally and cause an electrical short, possibly resulting in an engine compartment fire. An engine compartment fire can increase the risk of injury or crash.
- **What to do:** Hyundai will notify owners, and dealers will replace the HECU fuse. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2017 Hyundai Tucson presents a mixed ownership experience. While nicely packaged and feature-rich, prospective buyers should thoroughly test-drive models equipped with the 1.6L turbo engine and dual-clutch transmission to check for hesitation or shifting shudder. Prioritizing models with complete service records and checking for completed engine and sensor campaigns is strongly advised.
