# 2019 Hyundai Tucson Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2019 Hyundai Tucson is a compact crossover that offers a comfortable ride, user-friendly tech, and respectable cabin space. However, prospective used-car buyers should approach this model year with caution due to a notable volume of owner complaints regarding engine performance, electrical glitches, and unexpected stalling. Careful pre-purchase inspections and a thorough vehicle history check are strongly recommended.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L 4-Cylinder Engine

The base 2.0-liter naturally aspirated four-cylinder engine produces approximately 161 horsepower and 150 lb-ft of torque, providing adequate power for daily commutes. It is paired with a traditional automatic transmission, but buyers should monitor for oil consumption and potential driveability concerns.

**Engine Stalling and Loss of Power**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle may suddenly shake, lose motive power, or stall completely while driving or making low-speed turns.
- **Solution:** Diagnostic inspection at a qualified service center; software updates, sensor replacements, or major engine repairs may be required depending on the root cause.

**Excessive Oil Consumption**
- **Symptoms:** Engine oil levels drop significantly between scheduled oil changes, requiring frequent top-offs.
- **Solution:** Monitor oil levels regularly and consult a technician to check for leaks or internal component wear.

### 2.4L 4-Cylinder Engine

The available 2.4-liter direct-injection four-cylinder engine generates around 181 horsepower and 175 lb-ft of torque, offering stronger acceleration for highway driving. While generally capable, this engine option shares some of the owner-reported reliability concerns regarding misfires and electrical warnings.

**Engine Misfire and Shaking**
- **Symptoms:** Check engine light flashes, the vehicle shakes severely, and engine misfires occur, often linked to specific cylinders.
- **Solution:** Inspect ignition coils and spark plugs; replacement of faulty ignition components or fuel system service may be necessary.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 6-speed automatic transmission delivers smooth, conventional gear shifts when paired with the base engine option. It generally exhibits dependable mechanical behavior with relatively few widespread owner complaints.

### 6-Speed Shiftronic Automatic Transmission

The 6-speed automatic transmission paired with upper trims provides seamless power delivery to either front-wheel drive or all-wheel drive systems. Regular fluid changes help ensure long-term durability.

## Steering

### Electronic Power Steering

The power steering system provides light and responsive handling under normal conditions, though rare sensor malfunctions can lead to a sudden loss of assist.

**Steering Jerk and Power Assist Loss**
- **Symptoms:** The steering wheel may jerk abruptly or electronic power steering assist can fail, making the steering heavily resistant.
- **Solution:** Inspect the Motor Driven Power Steering (MDPS) sensor and associated electrical components for faults.

## Electrical

### Electrical System

Electrical complaints center around intermittent warning lights, screen failures, and audio system glitches that may require software updates or module replacements.

**Dashboard and Lighting Blackout**
- **Symptoms:** Interior dashboard screens and lights go completely dark intermittently while the vehicle is running.
- **Solution:** Have the wiring harnesses, battery connections, and body control modules inspected by a professional technician.

## Exterior

### Structure and Paint

Exterior body panels are generally durable, though some owners have reported premature paint peeling and minor structural trim issues.

**Hood Paint Peeling**
- **Symptoms:** Clear coat or paint begins bubbling and peeling off body panels such as the hood without external impact.
- **Solution:** Consult a professional auto body shop for repainting or touch-up services, as factory warranty coverage may have expired.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V543000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Antilock/Traction Control/Electronic Limited Slip:Control Unit/Module](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Hyundai&model=Tucson&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** The Anti-lock Brake Hydraulic Electronic Control Unit (HECU) could corrode internally and cause an electrical short, possibly resulting in an engine compartment fire. An engine compartment fire can increase the risk of injury or crash.
- **What to do:** Hyundai will notify owners, and dealers will replace the HECU fuse. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2019 Hyundai Tucson balances everyday practicality with attractive styling, but potential buyers must weigh these benefits against reported powertrain and electrical vulnerabilities. Prioritize vehicles with a documented service history, verify that all applicable software updates have been completed, and insist on a comprehensive pre-purchase inspection before finalizing a deal.
