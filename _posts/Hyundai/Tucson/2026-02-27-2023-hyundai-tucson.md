# 2023 Hyundai Tucson

### Overview
The 2023 Hyundai Tucson presents a compelling package with its modern design, spacious interior, and a variety of powertrain options catering to different needs. Hyundai has generally focused on delivering a reliable vehicle, and the Tucson is a strong contender in the compact SUV segment. This analysis focuses on documented technical reliability concerns, excluding routine maintenance and expected wear. We will examine common issues reported by owners and technicians across its different engine and transmission configurations, providing insights into potential problem areas and their associated repair costs to help prospective buyers and current owners make informed decisions.

### Summary Verdict
The 2023 Hyundai Tucson demonstrates strong overall reliability, with no significant widespread mechanical failures reported across its various engine and transmission options. The standard 2.5L engine and its associated 8-speed automatic transmission appear particularly robust. The hybrid and plug-in hybrid variants also benefit from Hyundai's established powertrain technology, showing no major concerns. Minor electrical gremlins, primarily related to the infotainment system's responsiveness and connectivity, are the most commonly cited issues, though these are typically resolved with software updates. Suspension, exterior, and interior components are generally holding up well. The Tucson is a reliable choice in its segment, with potential buyers able to select their preferred powertrain with confidence.
