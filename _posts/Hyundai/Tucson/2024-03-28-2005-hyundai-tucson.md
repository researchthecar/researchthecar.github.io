**Introduction**

The 2005 Hyundai Tucson is a compact SUV that offers two engine options: a 2.0L 4-cylinder and a 2.7L V6. Both engines are paired with a 4-speed automatic transmission. The Tucson was praised for its spacious interior, comfortable ride, and affordability. However, it also received some criticism for its lack of power, especially with the 2.0L engine.

**Engine**

**2.0L 4-cylinder**

* **Timing Belt Failure:** The timing belt may fail prematurely, leading to catastrophic engine damage.
    * Description/Symptoms: Engine stalls without warning, loud noises from the engine compartment.
    * Solution: Replace timing belt and inspect other components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,200+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Oxygen Sensor Failure:** The oxygen sensor may fail, affecting fuel efficiency and performance.
    * Description/Symptoms: Check engine light illuminates, decreased fuel economy.
    * Solution: Replace oxygen sensor.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $300+
    * Recalls: None Found

**2.7L V6**

* **Intake Manifold Gasket Leak:** The intake manifold gasket may leak, causing a loss of coolant and performance issues.
    * Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaks, engine overheating, rough idle.
    * Solution: Replace intake manifold gasket.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $600+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**4-Speed Automatic**

* **Transmission Slipping:** The transmission may slip between gears or fail to engage.
    * Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears, delayed shifting, loss of power.
    * Solution: Repair or replace transmission.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Front Suspension Strut Failure:** The front suspension struts may fail, leading to poor handling and uneven tire wear.
    * Description/Symptoms: Clunking noises, excessive body roll, uneven tire wear.
    * Solution: Replace suspension struts.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $600+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Rear Sway Bar Link Failure:** The rear sway bar link may fail, causing a clunking noise and unstable handling.
    * Description/Symptoms: Clunking noise from the rear suspension, unstable handling around corners.
    * Solution: Replace sway bar link.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Alternator Failure:** The alternator may fail, leading to a loss of electrical power.
    * Description/Symptoms: Dimming headlights, battery warning light illuminates, engine dies.
    * Solution: Replace alternator.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $600+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Window Switch Failure:** The window switch may fail, preventing operation of the power windows.
    * Description/Symptoms: Window buttons unresponsive, windows do not move.
    * Solution: Replace window switch.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Tailgate Rust:** The tailgate may rust, especially around the license plate area.
    * Description/Symptoms: Rust spots, bubbling paint.
    * Solution: Rust repair or tailgate replacement.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Headliner Sagging:** The headliner may sag, especially in hot climates.
    * Description/Symptoms: Headliner material detaching from the roof.
    * Solution: Repair or replace headliner.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Dashboard Rattling:** The dashboard may rattle, causing a distracting noise.
    * Description/Symptoms: Rattling noise coming from the dashboard.
    * Solution: Tighten dashboard screws or apply anti-rattle tape.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2005 Hyundai Tucson is a reliable and affordable compact SUV. However, it is not without its issues. Some of the more common problems include timing belt failure, transmission slipping, and suspension strut failure. These issues can be costly to repair, but they are relatively rare. Overall, the Tucson is a good value for the money, especially when considering its low cost of ownership.
