# 2024 Hyundai Tucson Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2024 Hyundai Tucson offers a bold exterior design, a comfortable cabin, and a variety of powertrain options including standard gas, hybrid, and plug-in hybrid setups. However, prospective buyers should review driver-reported concerns regarding electronic safety systems, powertrain behavior, and minor structural components. While many owners enjoy its modern tech and practicality, checking service history and conducting a thorough test drive can help ensure a trouble-free ownership experience.

---

## Engine

### 2.5L 4-Cylinder Engine

The standard 2.5-liter naturally aspirated four-cylinder engine produces 187 horsepower and 178 lb-ft of torque, providing adequate power for daily commuting with a conventional eight-speed automatic transmission.

**Engine Stalling and Drivability**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers report unexpected engine stalling, illuminated check engine lights, and loss of power during acceleration or long-distance drives.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified technician scan for diagnostic trouble codes and inspect fuel delivery, ignition, and software calibration.

### 1.6L Turbocharged Hybrid / Plug-In Hybrid Engine

The 1.6-liter turbocharged hybrid and plug-in hybrid powertrains combine forced induction with electric assist to deliver improved fuel economy and responsive power, paired with a six-speed automatic transmission.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

The conventional 8-speed automatic transmission paired with the base gas engine generally shifts smoothly and offers reliable daily operation.

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 6-speed automatic transmission found in hybrid models manages power delivery between the gasoline engine and electric motor with refined shift logic.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

The braking system provides strong stopping power, but electronic integration with driver aids requires careful verification during pre-purchase inspections.

**Brake System Inconsistency**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have noted unexpected brake responsiveness or concerns regarding collision mitigation features failing to engage during sudden stops.
- **Solution:** Have brake pads, rotors, and ABS sensors inspected during routine maintenance intervals.

## Electrical

### Vehicle Speed Control & Electronics

Modern electrical architectures manage complex infotainment and driver assist systems, which can occasionally display software glitches.

**Electrical Warning Lights and Throttle Anomalies**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have reported unexpected warning lights and rare instances of erratic throttle response or unintended speed assist feature activation at low speeds.
- **Solution:** Scan electronic control modules for stored trouble codes and ensure software updates are current.

## Safety

### Forward Collision Avoidance

Advanced driver assistance features add confidence on the road, though sensitive radar and camera hardware can occasionally generate false alerts.

**Unexpected Emergency Braking**
- **Symptoms:** The automatic emergency braking system can occasionally trigger false positives, causing the vehicle to brake suddenly in normal traffic or when turning vehicles are present.
- **Solution:** Verify that exterior sensors and cameras are clean and check with a dealership for any relevant software updates.

### Seat Belts

The occupant restraint system features standard airbags and multi-point seatbelts, requiring routine inspection of rear latch hardware.

**Rear Center Seat Belt Latch Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have reported that the center rear seatbelt buckle fails to latch properly or that the plastic housing separates from the latch mechanism.
- **Solution:** Inspect rear seatbelt assemblies for physical damage and replace faulty buckle mechanisms if necessary.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V556000: Equipment:Other:Labels](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Hyundai&model=Tucson&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 110, "Tire Selection and Rims." A label that incorrectly states spare tire pressure when there is no spare tire on-board may cause confusion during an emergency tire replacement, increasing the risk of a crash or injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the tire and loading information label, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V526000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission:Fluid/Lubricant:Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Hyundai&model=Tucson&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** An electric oil pump assembly that overheats increases the risk of a vehicle fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the electric oil pump controller, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V412000: Steering:Electric Power Assist System](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Hyundai&model=Tucson&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** The circuit board in the motor-driven power steering assembly may short circuit, resulting in a loss of power steering assist. A loss of power steering assist can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the motor-driven power steering electric power pack, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V893000: Trailer Hitches](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Hyundai&model=Tucson&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** Moisture accumulation may cause the trailer lighting to fail or an electrical short circuit. Nonfunctioning trailer lights can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will install a cap of the wiring, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2024 Hyundai Tucson is a versatile and feature-packed compact SUV that appeals to families and commuters alike. While owner complaints highlight occasional issues with driver-assistance sensitivity, seatbelt latches, and sporadic powertrain or electrical hiccups, the vehicle remains a competitive choice in its class. Buyers should prioritize a comprehensive pre-purchase inspection and verify that all open safety campaigns have been completed.
