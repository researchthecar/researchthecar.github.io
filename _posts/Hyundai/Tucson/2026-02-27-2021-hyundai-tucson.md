# 2021 Hyundai Tucson Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2021 Hyundai Tucson offers a comfortable daily driving experience with a practical cabin layout, but prospective buyers should review owner-reported reliability trends. While many owners experience trouble-free operation, specific clusters of complaints highlight potential trouble areas involving engine performance, safety electronics, and braking components. Careful pre-purchase inspections and reviewing service records can help mitigate unexpected ownership costs.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L 4-Cylinder Engine

The standard 2.0-liter naturally aspirated four-cylinder engine produces 161 horsepower and 150 lb-ft of torque, providing adequate power for daily commuting. It offers straightforward mechanical reliability, though routine fluid monitoring is recommended.

### 2.4L 4-Cylinder Engine

The available 2.4-liter naturally aspirated four-cylinder engine generates 181 horsepower and 175 lb-ft of torque for improved highway passing power. Buyers should monitor oil levels and watch for potential powertrain hesitation issues reported by some owners.

**Loss of Motive Power and Acceleration Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers experience sudden loss of power, illuminated check engine lights, and severe vehicle hesitation or failure to accelerate when depressing the gas pedal.
- **Solution:** Diagnostic scanning is required to identify fault codes, potentially leading to sensor replacement or software updates.

**Excessive Oil Consumption**
- **Symptoms:** Oil levels drop significantly below minimum marks on the dipstick between regular oil change intervals without visible external leaks.
- **Solution:** Monitor oil levels frequently and consult a dealer for oil consumption testing and potential warranty evaluation.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

The conventional 6-speed automatic transmission pairs with the engine options to deliver smooth, traditional gear shifts. Overall owner feedback indicates it is generally dependable, though occasional responsiveness concerns have been noted.

**Intermittent Drivetrain Hesitation**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may intermittently fail to respond smoothly when the accelerator is depressed during driving.
- **Solution:** Check for transmission control module software updates or mechanical inspection by a qualified technician.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

Braking components require regular inspection to ensure consistent pedal feel and stopping performance under all driving conditions.

**Brake Pedal Softness and Noise**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers note a hissing or buzzing noise during low-speed braking accompanied by a soft or spongy brake pedal feel.
- **Solution:** Inspect the brake hydraulic system, booster, and fluid lines for wear or component failure.

## Electrical

### Electrical and Climate Systems

Electrical and climate control systems generally function reliably, though occasional electronic module and air conditioning component failures can occur.

**ABS/ESC Module Failure and AC Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Dashboard warning lights illuminate for stability and anti-lock braking systems, or the air conditioning compressor fails to engage.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified technician diagnose electrical control modules and replace faulty AC compressors or switches.

## Exterior

### Glass and Sunroof

Exterior glass panels offer great visibility, though spontaneous glass breakage reports warrant a careful inspection of the sunroof and rear windshield.

**Unexpected Rear Window and Sunroof Shattering**
- **Symptoms:** The rear hatch glass or panoramic sunroof shatters unexpectedly while driving or parked, often accompanied by a loud pop.
- **Solution:** Replace the shattered glass panel and inspect the frame for stress points or manufacturing defects.

## Safety

### Forward Collision Avoidance System

Advanced safety features provide added peace of mind but can be costly to diagnose if sensor calibration or electronic faults develop.

**Unintended Automatic Braking and Sensor Faults**
- **Symptoms:** The forward collision avoidance system unexpectedly triggers emergency braking or displays warning messages to check the collision system.
- **Solution:** Inspect camera and radar sensors for obstructions, or have the dealership recalibrate and inspect the safety control modules.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V543000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Antilock/Traction Control/Electronic Limited Slip:Control Unit/Module](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Hyundai&model=Tucson&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The Anti-lock Brake Hydraulic Electronic Control Unit (HECU) could corrode internally and cause an electrical short, possibly resulting in an engine compartment fire. An engine compartment fire can increase the risk of injury or crash.
- **What to do:** Hyundai will notify owners, and dealers will replace the HECU fuse. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2021 Hyundai Tucson is a well-rounded compact SUV with comfortable road manners and useful features, making it a popular choice on the used market. However, buyers should perform a thorough inspection checking for potential engine oil consumption, forward collision sensor behavior, and electrical functionality. Verifying service history and ensuring open safety campaigns are addressed will help secure a dependable ownership experience.
