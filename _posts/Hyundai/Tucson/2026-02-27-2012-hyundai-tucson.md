# 2012 Hyundai Tucson

### Overview
The 2012 Hyundai Tucson is a compact SUV that generally offers a good balance of value and features. Reliability for this model year is considered average to good, with most owners reporting satisfaction. Common issues tend to be minor and related to specific components rather than widespread systemic failures. Owners should be aware of potential concerns in the engine and electrical systems, though these are not universal. Overall, the Tucson is a practical choice for daily commuting and light family use, with maintenance costs typically falling within the expected range for its class.

## Engine

### 2.0L Theta II GDI (Gasoline Direct Injection)
**Oil Consumption**
- **Symptoms:** Excessive oil burning, potentially leading to low oil levels and engine damage if not monitored.
- **Solution:** Regular oil level checks and top-offs. In some cases, engine internal component inspection or repair may be necessary.
- **Cost:** $100 - $1500 (depending on severity and repair)

**Carbon Buildup**
- **Symptoms:** Reduced engine performance, rough idling, potential misfires.
- **Solution:** Professional intake valve cleaning or fuel system cleaning.
- **Cost:** $200 - $500

## Electrical

### Standard Electrical System
**Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Battery dies prematurely, difficulty starting the vehicle.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis to identify parasitic draw from accessories or faulty components. Battery replacement may be needed.
- **Cost:** $150 - $500

**Infotainment System Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Screen freezing, unresponsiveness, Bluetooth connectivity issues.
- **Solution:** Software updates or potential replacement of the head unit.
- **Cost:** $300 - $1000

### Summary Verdict
The 2012 Hyundai Tucson presents a generally reliable option in the compact SUV segment. While not immune to issues, the most commonly reported problems, such as oil consumption in certain engines and occasional electrical glitches, are often manageable with proactive maintenance and timely repairs. The transmission systems, both automatic and manual, have shown decent durability. Owners should prioritize regular checks of fluid levels and be attentive to any unusual noises or performance changes. Overall, the Tucson offers a solid value proposition for buyers seeking a practical and reasonably dependable vehicle, with repair costs typically aligning with industry averages for its class.
