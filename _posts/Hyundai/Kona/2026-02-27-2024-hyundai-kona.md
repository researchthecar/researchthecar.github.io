# 2024 Hyundai Kona Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2024 Hyundai Kona brings a bold redesign, a roomier interior, and modern tech to the subcompact SUV segment. While it offers appealing features and updated styling, early owners have raised concerns regarding electrical glitches, intermittent motive power issues, and drivetrain behavior. Prospective buyers should thoroughly inspect vehicle electronics and verify service records during a test drive.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L 4-Cylinder Engine

The base 2.0-liter naturally aspirated four-cylinder engine produces approximately 147 horsepower and is paired with a continuously variable transmission. It provides adequate daily power for commuting with respectable fuel economy.

### 1.6L Turbocharged 4-Cylinder Engine

The available 1.6-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine generates roughly 190 horsepower, offering punchier acceleration for spirited driving. It is typically mated to an 8-speed automatic transmission.

**Unexpected Engine Shutdown / Power Reduction**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have reported dashboard warnings indicating the engine has turned off while driving, resulting in a loss of acceleration or automatic shifting into neutral.
- **Solution:** Have a certified mechanic or dealership check powertrain control modules and software updates for diagnostic trouble codes.

## Transmission

### Intelligent Variable Transmission (IVT)

The continuously variable transmission works alongside the base 2.0L engine to optimize fuel efficiency during regular commuting.

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 8-speed automatic transmission paired with the 1.6L turbo engine provides smooth gear shifts and handles highway cruising effectively.

### All-Wheel Drive System

The available all-wheel-drive system enhances traction in adverse weather conditions but requires proper maintenance to prevent mechanical binding.

**AWD Binding or Locking Up**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers notice the all-wheel drive system binding during low-speed turns, creating a sensation that the rear wheels are slipping or dragging.
- **Solution:** Have the differential fluid and AWD control modules inspected by a qualified technician.

## Steering

### Steering and Power Assistance

Electronic power steering provides light effort at low speeds and firmer feedback on the highway.

**Intermittent Loss of Power Steering**
- **Symptoms:** Some drivers experienced sudden unexpected steering adjustments followed by a complete loss of power steering assistance and motive power.
- **Solution:** Inspect electrical harnesses, steering control modules, and check for active manufacturer service bulletins related to power delivery.

## Electrical

### Infotainment and Display Systems

The vehicle relies heavily on integrated digital screens and advanced electronics for infotainment and driver controls.

**Infotainment Screen Glitching and Reboots**
- **Symptoms:** Owners note that digital displays, backup camera feeds, or infotainment screens may freeze, flash, go blank, or spontaneously reboot while driving.
- **Solution:** Software updates performed by a dealership can resolve many interface bugs; persistent display failures may require hardware replacement.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V650000: Seat Belts:Rear/Other:Retractor](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Hyundai&model=Kona&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** The seat belt assemblies in the right and left rear seats may not function properly. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard numbers 208, "Occupation Crash Protection" and 209, "Seat Belt Assemblies." A seat belt that does not function properly may not adequately restrain an occupant in a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the seat belt retractor as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V901000: Electrical System:12V/24V/48V Battery:Cables](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Hyundai&model=Kona&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** The 12-Volt battery cables may chafe against the engine control module bracket and short circuit during a crash. A short circuit increases the risk of an engine compartment fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will install sheathing over the wiring, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V308000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Exhaust System:Emission Control:Gas Recirculation Valve (Egr Valve)](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Hyundai&model=Kona&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** An electrical short circuit may occur in the exhaust gas recirculation (EGR) valve assembly, which can result in a loss of drive power. A loss of drive power increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the exhaust gas recirculation valve assembly, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

When shopping for a used 2024 Hyundai Kona, prioritize models that have had their software updates properly applied and verified through a pre-purchase inspection. While the vehicle offers great style and modern features, paying close attention to electrical system stability and steering functionality during a test drive will help ensure a reliable ownership experience.
