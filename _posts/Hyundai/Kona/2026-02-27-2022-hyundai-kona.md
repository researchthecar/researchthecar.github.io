# 2022 Hyundai Kona Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2022 Hyundai Kona offers a stylish subcompact crossover design, nimble handling, and a choice of efficient powertrain options, making it a popular choice for daily commuters. However, used-car buyers should review potential owner-reported reliability concerns, particularly relating to electrical accessories, engine performance hiccups, and transmission behaviors. While many owners enjoy trouble-free driving, inspecting service records and verifying that all recall work has been completed is strongly advised.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L 4-Cylinder Engine

A naturally aspirated 2.0-liter 4-cylinder engine producing around 147 horsepower, standard on lower trims. It provides adequate baseline performance for city driving and dependable fuel economy.

**Engine Stalling and Loss of Motive Power**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have reported unexpected engine stalls, sudden loss of motive power, or rough running conditions while operating the vehicle.
- **Solution:** Diagnostic inspection by a certified technician; software updates or replacement of faulty internal components may be required.

### 1.6L Turbocharged 4-Cylinder Engine

A turbocharged 1.6-liter 4-cylinder engine generating approximately 195 horsepower, found on sportier trims. It offers punchy acceleration but requires diligent maintenance of its cooling and turbo systems.

**Coolant Leaks and Warning Lights**
- **Symptoms:** Illuminated check engine lights accompanied by low coolant levels or fluid spots beneath the vehicle.
- **Solution:** Pressure-test the cooling system and replace compromised hoses, clamps, or radiator components as needed.

## Transmission

### Intelligent Variable Transmission (IVT)

A smooth-shifting continuously variable type transmission paired with the base 2.0L engine, designed to optimize fuel efficiency for everyday commuting.

**Harsh Shifting and Hesitation**
- **Symptoms:** Unusual jolting during acceleration, hesitation when engaging gears, or transmission control module warnings.
- **Solution:** Update transmission control software or inspect internal mechanical components for premature wear.

### 7-Speed Dual-Clutch Transmission (DCT)

A sporty 7-speed dual-clutch automatic transmission paired with the 1.6L turbocharged engine, offering rapid gear changes for a more engaging driving dynamic.

**Overheating and Power Interruption**
- **Symptoms:** Transmission overheating warnings on inclines, shuddering at low speeds, or temporary loss of forward drive capability.
- **Solution:** Check transmission fluid condition, update clutch actuation software, or repair damaged clutch packs.

## Safety

### Horn Assembly

Manages vehicle electrical accessories, body control functions, and the primary alert systems.

**Premature Horn Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Pressing the steering wheel horn button fails to produce any sound, reducing safety awareness on the road.
- **Solution:** Replace the failed horn assembly with an updated part.
- **Cost:** Varies by dealer labor rates

## Summary Verdict

The 2022 Hyundai Kona remains an attractive option in the subcompact SUV segment, balancing modern styling, tech features, and respectable efficiency. While most owners report a satisfying ownership experience, used-car shoppers should be mindful of recurring complaints regarding electrical horn failures and potential powertrain hesitation. A thorough pre-purchase inspection and a review of maintenance records will help ensure you find a reliable example.
