# 2023 Hyundai Kona Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2023 Hyundai Kona is a stylish and feature-packed subcompact SUV that appeals to daily commuters and small families alike. While it offers solid fuel efficiency and nimble handling, potential buyers should review known owner complaints regarding electrical glitches, advanced safety sensor anomalies, and transmission hesitation. Being aware of these areas will help you thoroughly inspect a used model before purchasing.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L 4-Cylinder Engine

The base 2.0-liter four-cylinder engine produces 147 horsepower and 132 lb-ft of torque, providing adequate power for daily commuting. It is generally regarded as a reliable powerplant requiring standard routine maintenance.

### 1.6L Turbocharged 4-Cylinder Engine

The turbocharged 1.6-liter four-cylinder engine generates 195 horsepower and 195 lb-ft of torque for a much punchier acceleration experience. Buyers looking for sportier performance will appreciate this option, though proper turbo cool-down habits are recommended for longevity.

## Transmission

### Intelligent Variable Transmission (IVT)

Paired with the base 2.0L engine, this continuously variable transmission is designed to maximize fuel efficiency. It delivers smooth power delivery during city driving.

### 7-Speed Dual-Clutch Transmission (DCT)

Matched with the 1.6L turbocharged engine, this 7-speed dual-clutch transmission offers quick shifts but can exhibit low-speed drivability quirks.

**Transmission Hesitation and Jerking**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers report hesitation when accelerating from a stop, as well as rough jerking movements in low-speed stop-and-go traffic.
- **Solution:** Software updates or control module reprogramming performed by a dealer can sometimes improve shift quality.

## Electrical

### Horn Assembly

Electrical system concerns primarily center around accessory components like the horn failing unexpectedly.

**Intermittent or Inoperable Horn**
- **Symptoms:** The horn fails to sound when pressed, often working intermittently or stopping completely, sometimes linked to freezing temperatures.
- **Solution:** Have the dealer inspect and replace the faulty horn assembly or relay under warranty if applicable.

## Safety

### Forward Collision-Avoidance System

While advanced driver assistance systems enhance safety, owners have reported erratic behavior from collision avoidance sensors.

**Random Warnings and Unexpected Braking**
- **Symptoms:** The forward collision warning system may trigger false alarms, and in some cases, the vehicle may apply unexpected braking.
- **Solution:** Check for manufacturer service bulletins or software updates that recalibrate the camera and radar sensors.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V526000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission:Fluid/Lubricant:Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Hyundai&model=Kona&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** An electric oil pump assembly that overheats increases the risk of a vehicle fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the electric oil pump controller, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2023 Hyundai Kona is a practical and attractive small SUV, but prospective buyers should perform a thorough test drive to check for dual-clutch transmission low-speed behavior and verify that all safety sensors and the horn are operating correctly. Addressing these potential quirks during a pre-purchase inspection can save you from unexpected headaches down the road.
