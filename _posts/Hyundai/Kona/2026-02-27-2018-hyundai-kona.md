# 2018 Hyundai Kona Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2018 Hyundai Kona is a stylish and nimble subcompact crossover that appeals to used car buyers seeking modern features and easy maneuverability. However, prospective buyers should review recurring owner complaints concerning engine performance, electrical glitches, and transmission behavior before purchasing.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L 4-Cylinder Engine

The base 2.0-liter naturally aspirated four-cylinder engine produces around 147 horsepower and 132 lb-ft of torque, offering adequate daily commuting power. It is generally straightforward to maintain, though some owners have reported unexpected stalling and drivability concerns.

**Engine Stalling and Loss of Power**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers report unexpected engine stalling while driving, often accompanied by sputtering, jerking, flashing oil warning lights, or loud clunking and grinding noises.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified mechanic inspect the fuel system, electrical sensors, and engine internals; repairs may require replacing faulty spark plugs, sensors, or addressing internal mechanical wear.

### 1.6L Turbocharged 4-Cylinder Engine

The available 1.6-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine generates 175 horsepower and 195 lb-ft of torque, providing a more spirited acceleration experience. It pairs with a dual-clutch transmission and requires attentive maintenance to ensure long-term reliability.

## Transmission

### 7-Speed Dual-Clutch Transmission

The 7-speed dual-clutch automatic transmission delivers quick shifts when paired with the turbocharged engine, but some drivers experience rough engagement and hesitation during stop-and-go driving.

**Transmission Shaking and Jerking**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle jolts, shakes, or hesitates during slow-speed maneuvering such as in parking lots, drive-thrus, or heavy freeway traffic.
- **Solution:** Inspect the transmission control module and clutch actuators; software updates or clutch pack adjustments may be required by a dealership.

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

The conventional 6-speed automatic transmission is paired with the base 2.0L engine, providing smooth and traditional gear shifts for everyday driving conditions.

## Electrical

### Infotainment and Cabin Electronics

The electrical system manages the Kona's cabin technology and driver aids, with occasional owner reports of screen freezing and accessory power glitches.

**Radio Freezing and Backup Camera Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The radio remains powered on after turning off the vehicle and opening the door, while the backup camera fails to display properly.
- **Solution:** Disconnect and reconnect the vehicle's 12-volt battery to reset the system, or have a dealer check for infotainment software updates.

## Summary Verdict

The 2018 Hyundai Kona offers an attractive package with agile handling and a well-equipped interior, but used car shoppers should perform a thorough pre-purchase inspection. Pay close attention to engine smoothness and transmission operation during test drives to ensure the vehicle has not suffered from the stalling or shifting hesitation reported by some owners.
