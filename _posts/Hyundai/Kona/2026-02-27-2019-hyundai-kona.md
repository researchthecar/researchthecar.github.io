# 2019 Hyundai Kona Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2019 Hyundai Kona is a stylish and feature-packed subcompact crossover that has gained popularity on the used market. However, prospective buyers should carefully review powertrain performance and owner-reported reliability concerns, particularly regarding engine operation and electrical accessories. While many owners enjoy its nimble handling and modern tech, investigating service histories and specific mechanical conditions is strongly recommended before purchasing.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L 4-Cylinder Engine

The base 2.0-liter naturally aspirated four-cylinder engine produces around 147 horsepower, offering adequate daily commuting power. Buyers should verify maintenance records and monitor fluid consumption closely.

**Engine Stalling and Loss of Power**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle suddenly loses motive power while driving or stalls completely at highway speeds, sometimes illuminating warning lights or failing to restart immediately.
- **Solution:** Diagnostic evaluation by a qualified mechanic or dealership to check fuel delivery, ignition systems, and engine control modules.

**Excessive Oil Consumption**
- **Symptoms:** Engine oil levels drop significantly between regular service intervals, potentially leading to rough running or unexpected stalling if not closely monitored.
- **Solution:** Monitor oil levels frequently, perform oil consumption tests, and consult a technician for potential internal engine repairs or component replacements.

### 1.6L Turbocharged 4-Cylinder Engine

The optional 1.6-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine delivers a livelier 175 horsepower and stronger low-end torque. Routine maintenance and timely oil changes are critical for longevity on turbocharged powertrains.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

Paired with the base 2.0L engine, this traditional 6-speed automatic provides smooth and conventional gear shifts for daily driving.

### 7-Speed Dual-Clutch Transmission (DCT)

Paired with the 1.6L turbocharged engine, the 7-speed dual-clutch transmission offers quick shifts but can exhibit hesitant or jerky behavior in heavy stop-and-go traffic.

**Transmission Slippage and Harsh Shifting**
- **Symptoms:** Unexpected gear slippage during acceleration or sudden harsh downshifting that slows the vehicle abruptly.
- **Solution:** Inspect transmission fluid condition, check for updated control module software calibrations, or service clutch actuators as recommended by a technician.

## Exterior

### Door Latches and Lock Actuators

Electrical accessories generally function well, though door lock actuators and latches are known trouble spots that can require component replacement.

**Inoperable Door Locks**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle doors or rear trunk fail to unlock or open using electronic key fobs or manual inside/outside handles, trapping passengers or limiting access.
- **Solution:** Replace faulty door lock actuators and latches.
- **Cost:** $530 parts and labor reported by dealer for latch assembly replacement.

### Paint and Exterior Trim

Body panels and exterior finishes require standard upkeep, though isolated complaints highlight premature clear coat peeling and trim wear.

**Paint Peeling and Exterior Trim Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Exterior clear coat or paint begins to peel from body panels such as the hood, exposing primer layers, and roof railings show premature wear.
- **Solution:** Consult a professional auto body shop for repainting or trim restoration, noting that out-of-warranty cosmetic repairs are typically owner-funded.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V301000: Engine](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Hyundai&model=Kona&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** The piston oil rings may not have been properly heat-treated, which could result in engine damage. A damaged engine may stall, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and, if necessary, replace the engine, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2019 Hyundai Kona presents a mixed picture for used-car buyers, balancing agile handling and modern features against notable owner complaints regarding engine stalling, oil consumption, and door lock failures. Prospective buyers should thoroughly inspect the service history, test drive both the powertrain and electrical accessories, and ensure any open recalls have been addressed before finalizing a purchase.
