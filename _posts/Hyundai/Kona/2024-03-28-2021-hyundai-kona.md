**Introduction to the 2021 Hyundai Kona**

The 2021 Hyundai Kona is a subcompact crossover SUV known for its fuel-efficient engine options, spacious interior, and advanced safety features. It comes with three engine options: a 147-horsepower 2.0L 4-cylinder engine, a 175-horsepower 1.6L turbocharged 4-cylinder engine, and a 201-horsepower 2.0L turbocharged 4-cylinder engine. Transmission options include a 6-speed automatic transmission, a 7-speed dual-clutch transmission, and a continuously variable transmission (CVT). The Kona has received praise for its sharp handling, comfortable ride, and user-friendly infotainment system. However, some owners have reported issues with certain components.

**Engine**

**2.0L 4-cylinder Engine**

* **Oil Consumption:** Some owners have reported excessive oil consumption, causing the engine to burn oil and potentially leading to engine damage.
* **Description/Symptoms:** Low oil levels, engine knocking, increased exhaust smoke.
* **Solution:** Repair or replacement of piston rings, valve stem seals, or other affected components.
* **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $500 - $2,000+
* **Recalls:** None Found

**1.6L Turbocharged 4-cylinder Engine**

* **Turbocharger Failure:** A faulty turbocharger can cause a loss of power, reduced fuel efficiency, and increased exhaust emissions.
* **Description/Symptoms:** Intermittent power loss, excessive smoke from exhaust, turbocharger whining or grinding noise.
* **Solution:** Replacement of turbocharger and related components.
* **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $2,000 - $3,000+
* **Recalls:** None Found

**2.0L Turbocharged 4-cylinder Engine**

* **High-Pressure Fuel Pump Failure:** A defective high-pressure fuel pump can lead to engine misfires, stalling, and reduced performance.
* **Description/Symptoms:** Rough engine idle, difficulty starting, poor acceleration.
* **Solution:** Replacement of high-pressure fuel pump.
* **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $800 - $1,200
* **Recalls:** None Found

**Transmission**

**6-speed Automatic Transmission**

* **Slipping Gears:** Some owners have experienced transmission slipping out of gear while driving, causing a loss of power and a potential safety hazard.
* **Description/Symptoms:** Hesitation or jerking during gear changes, difficulty shifting gears, transmission warning light illuminated.
* **Solution:** Transmission repair or replacement.
* **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $2,500 - $4,000+
* **Recalls:** None Found

**7-speed Dual-Clutch Transmission**

* **Shifting Issues:** Some drivers have reported problems with smooth shifting, including delayed or hesitant gear changes.
* **Description/Symptoms:** Hard or jerky shifts, delayed acceleration, transmission warning light illuminated.
* **Solution:** Software updates or transmission component replacement.
* **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $500 - $1,500
* **Recalls:** None Found

**Suspension**

* **Front Strut Noise:** Some owners have reported a rattling or clunking noise coming from the front struts while driving over rough roads.
* **Description/Symptoms:** Noise from front suspension over bumps, uneven tire wear.
* **Solution:** Replacement of front struts and related components.
* **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $600 - $1,000
* **Recalls:** None Found

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain:** Some owners have experienced rapid battery drain, leading to a dead battery and difficulty starting the vehicle.
* **Description/Symptoms:** Battery goes dead after short periods of non-use, electrical system problems.
* **Solution:** Identification and repair of electrical component causing the drain.
* **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $300 - $600
* **Recalls:** None Found

**Exterior**

* **Roof Rack Assembly:** Some owners have reported issues with the roof rack assembly, including loose or rattling components.
* **Description/Symptoms:** Noise from roof rack, loose or damaged mounting hardware.
* **Solution:** Tightening or replacement of loose components.
* **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $100 - $500
* **Recalls:** None Found

**Interior**

* **Dashboard Creaks and Rattles:** Some owners have complained about creaking or rattling noises coming from the dashboard area.
* **Description/Symptoms:** Audible noises from dashboard, especially over rough roads.
* **Solution:** Disassembly and reassembly of dashboard components, installation of sound-dampening material.
* **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $200 - $500
* **Recalls:** None Found

**Summary**

While the 2021 Hyundai Kona is generally a reliable vehicle, some owners have reported issues with certain components. The severity of the issues varies, with some being relatively minor and inexpensive to fix, while others can be more serious and costly. It is important to note that not all Konas will experience these issues, and the frequency of occurrence may vary depending on factors such as driving habits and maintenance practices.
