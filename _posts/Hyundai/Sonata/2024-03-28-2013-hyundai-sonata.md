**2013 Hyundai Sonata**

The 2013 Hyundai Sonata is a midsize sedan that is available with a 2.4-liter inline-four engine or a 2.0-liter turbocharged inline-four engine. Both engines are mated to a six-speed automatic transmission. The 2.4-liter engine produces 198 horsepower and 184 pound-feet of torque, while the 2.0-liter turbocharged engine produces 274 horsepower and 269 pound-feet of torque.

Drivers like the Sonata's comfortable ride, spacious interior, and good fuel economy. However, some drivers have reported issues with the vehicle's engine, transmission, suspension, and electrical system.

**Engine**

**2.4-Liter Inline-Four Engine**

* **Intake Valve Deposits**
    * Description/Symptoms: This issue causes the engine to run rough and idle erratically. It can also lead to decreased fuel efficiency and power loss.
    * Solution: The solution to this issue is to clean the intake valves.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Timing Chain Tensioner Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: This issue can cause the timing chain to skip, which can lead to catastrophic engine damage. Symptoms of this issue include a rattling noise from the engine, decreased engine power, and increased fuel consumption.
    * Solution: The solution to this issue is to replace the timing chain tensioner.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**2.0-Liter Turbocharged Inline-Four Engine**

* **Turbocharger Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: This issue can cause the engine to lose power, produce excessive smoke, and make a whistling noise.
    * Solution: The solution to this issue is to replace the turbocharger.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Fuel Injector Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: This issue can cause the engine to run rough, hesitate, and stall. It can also lead to decreased fuel efficiency.
    * Solution: The solution to this issue is to replace the fuel injectors.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

* **Transmission Slipping**
    * Description/Symptoms: This issue can cause the transmission to slip out of gear, making it difficult to accelerate or reverse. It can also lead to increased fuel consumption and decreased performance.
    * Solution: The solution to this issue is to rebuild or replace the transmission.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Transmission Control Module Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: This issue can cause the transmission to shift erratically, hesitate, or refuse to shift out of park. It can also lead to increased fuel consumption and decreased performance.
    * Solution: The solution to this issue is to replace the transmission control module.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Strut Mount Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: This issue can cause the vehicle to handle poorly, bounce excessively, and make a clunking noise.
    * Solution: The solution to this issue is to replace the strut mounts.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Sway Bar Link Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: This issue can cause the vehicle to handle poorly, make a clunking noise, and experience excessive body roll.
    * Solution: The solution to this issue is to replace the sway bar links.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Battery Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: This issue can cause the vehicle to fail to start or experience electrical problems.
    * Solution: The solution to this issue is to replace the battery.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Alternator Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: This issue can cause the battery to drain quickly, leading to a loss of electrical power.
    * Solution: The solution to this issue is to replace the alternator.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Climate Control Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: This issue can cause the climate control system to malfunction, leading to inconsistent temperatures or a lack of air flow.
    * Solution: The solution to this issue is to replace the climate control module.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Power Window Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: This issue can cause the power windows to fail to operate or become inoperable.
    * Solution: The solution to this issue is to replace the window motor or switch.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2013 Hyundai Sonata has some issues that can affect its performance and reliability. The most severe issues include timing chain tensioner failure, turbocharger failure, and transmission slipping. These issues can be expensive to fix, and they can lead to major problems if not addressed promptly. However, it's important to note that not all Sonatas will experience these issues, and many owners have reported positive experiences with their vehicles.
