**Introduction:**

The 2001 Hyundai Sonata is a mid-size sedan known for its spacious interior, fuel efficiency, and affordable price. It is available with a choice of two engine options: a 2.4-liter inline-four producing 149 horsepower and 160 lb-ft of torque, or a 3.0-liter V6 producing 194 horsepower and 208 lb-ft of torque. Both engines can be paired with either a 5-speed manual or 4-speed automatic transmission.

Despite its positive qualities, the 2001 Hyundai Sonata has been plagued by a number of issues, which have been widely reported by owners and mechanics.

**Engine**

**2.4-liter Inline-Four**

* **Timing Belt Failure:** 
Premature failure of the timing belt can lead to catastrophic engine damage. 
Symptoms include: engine stalling, rough idle, and reduced power. 
Solution: Replace the timing belt and tensioner. 
Estimated Cost to Fix: $600 - $1,000+ 
Recalls: None Found

**3.0-liter V6**

* **Intake Manifold Gasket Failure:** 
A faulty intake manifold gasket can cause coolant and oil leaks. 
Symptoms include: overheating, engine misfires, and reduced power. 
Solution: Replace the intake manifold gasket. 
Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $600
Recalls: 03V163000

**Transmission**

**5-Speed Manual**

* **Clutch Slipping:** 
Worn clutch plates can cause the clutch to slip, resulting in reduced power transfer and difficulty shifting gears. 
Symptoms include: difficulty engaging gears, grinding noises, and burning smell. 
Solution: Replace the clutch assembly. 
Estimated Cost to Fix: $600 - $1,200+ 
Recalls: None Found

**4-Speed Automatic**

* **Transmission Fluid Leak:** 
A leak in the transmission fluid pan or lines can cause the transmission to overheat and fail. 
Symptoms include: slipping gears, delayed shifts, and transmission whining noises. 
Solution: Repair or replace the leaking components. 
Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $700+ 
Recalls: 07V085000

**Suspension**

* **Rear Subframe Rust:** 
The rear subframe can rust and corrode, weakening its structural integrity. 
Symptoms include: creaking or squeaking noises, misaligned wheels, and poor handling. 
Solution: Inspect and repair the rear subframe as necessary. 
Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+ 
Recalls: 07V158000

**Electrical**

* **Faulty Alternator:** 
A failing alternator can prevent the battery from charging properly, leading to electrical system problems. 
Symptoms include: dim or flickering lights, dead battery, and electrical component malfunctions. 
Solution: Replace the alternator. 
Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $600+ 
Recalls: 04V340000

* **Battery Drain:** 
A parasitic drain on the battery can discharge it over time, leading to difficulty starting the vehicle. 
Symptoms include: dead battery, difficulty starting the engine, and electrical component malfunctions. 
Solution: Diagnose and repair the source of the drain. 
Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+ 
Recalls: 06V050000

**Exterior**

* **Door Lock Failure:** 
The door lock actuators can fail, preventing the doors from locking or unlocking properly. 
Symptoms include: difficulty locking or unlocking the doors, buzzing noises, and inoperable remote keyless entry. 
Solution: Replace the faulty door lock actuator(s). 
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+ 
Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Instrument Cluster Failure:** 
The instrument cluster may fail to display vital information, such as speed and fuel level. 
Symptoms include: blank or flickering gauges, incorrect readings, and warning lights malfunctioning. 
Solution: Replace the instrument cluster. 
Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+ 
Recalls: None Found

* **Airbag Deployment Failure:** 
The airbags may fail to deploy properly in the event of a crash. 
Symptoms include: airbag warning light illuminated, airbag system malfunctions, and inconsistent airbag deployment. 
Solution: Inspect and repair the airbag system as necessary. 
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+ 
Recalls: 02V438000

**Summary**

The 2001 Hyundai Sonata has a number of significant issues that can impact its reliability and safety. The most severe and costly issues include timing belt failure, transmission fluid leaks, rear subframe rust, airbag deployment failure, and electrical problems. Owners should be aware of these issues and address them promptly to avoid costly repairs and potential safety hazards.
