## 2022 Hyundai Sonata

The 2022 Hyundai Sonata is a midsize sedan that offers a stylish design, a comfortable interior, and a variety of features. It is available with a choice of four-cylinder or V6 engines and a variety of transmission options.

Some of the things that people like about the Sonata include its spacious interior, its comfortable seats, and its user-friendly infotainment system. However, some people have complained about the Sonata's lack of power with the base engine, its stiff suspension, and its lack of premium features in the base trims.

### Engine

**2.5L Inline-4**

* **Excessive Oil Consumption:** Some owners have reported that their Sonatas are consuming excessive amounts of oil, requiring frequent top-offs. The issue is believed to be caused by a faulty piston ring design.
    * Solution: Engine rebuild or replacement
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $3,000 - $5,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Timing Chain Noise:** A small number of owners have reported hearing a rattling noise from the engine, especially on cold starts. The noise is believed to be caused by a faulty timing chain.
    * Solution: Timing chain replacement
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000
    * Recalls: None Found

**1.6T Inline-4**

* **No Issues Found**

**2.5T Inline-4**

* **Turbocharger Failure:** A small number of owners have reported experiencing turbocharger failure, resulting in a loss of power and increased engine noise. The issue is believed to be caused by a faulty turbocharger design.
    * Solution: Turbocharger replacement
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500
    * Recalls: None Found

### Transmission

**6-Speed Automatic (2.5L)**

* **Shifting Issues:** Some owners have reported experiencing shifting issues, such as delayed shifts, hard shifts, or slipping gears. The issue may be caused by a faulty transmission control module or a mechanical problem within the transmission.
    * Solution: Transmission control module replacement or transmission rebuild
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $3,000
    * Recalls: None Found

**8-Speed Automatic (1.6T)**

* **No Issues Found**

**8-Speed Dual-Clutch Transmission (2.5T)**

* **Clutch Pack Failure:** A small number of owners have reported experiencing clutch pack failure, resulting in a loss of power and transmission shuddering. The issue is believed to be caused by a faulty clutch pack design.
    * Solution: Clutch pack replacement
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500
    * Recalls: None Found

### Suspension

* **Harsh Ride:** Some owners have complained about the Sonata's stiff suspension, which can lead to a rough ride over bumps and uneven roads. The issue may be exacerbated by the Sonata's low-profile tires.
    * Solution: Upgrading to more comfortable tires or shock absorbers
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

### Electrical

* **Electrical System Issues:** A small number of owners have reported experiencing electrical system issues, such as flickering lights, dead batteries, and inoperative accessories. The cause of these issues may vary.
    * Solution: Varies depending on the issue
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: 22V-121: Headlight Malfunction

### Exterior

* **No Issues Found**

### Interior

* **Climate Control Malfunctions:** Some owners have reported experiencing climate control malfunctions, such as the air conditioner not blowing cold air or the heater not working properly. The issue may be caused by a faulty climate control module or a problem with the HVAC system.
    * Solution: Climate control module replacement or HVAC repair
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Infotainment System Issues:** A small number of owners have reported experiencing infotainment system issues, such as the screen freezing, the system rebooting randomly, or the navigation system not working properly. The issue may be caused by a software glitch or a faulty hardware component.
    * Solution: Software update or infotainment system replacement
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

## Summary

Overall, the 2022 Hyundai Sonata is a reliable and well-built vehicle. However, there are a few potential issues that owners should be aware of, including excessive oil consumption, timing chain noise, and turbocharger failure. The severity and cost of these issues vary, but most can be resolved with a relatively straightforward repair.
