**Introduction:**

The 1996 Hyundai Sonata is a mid-size sedan offered with a 2.0L inline-four engine and either a 5-speed manual transmission or a 4-speed automatic transmission. It produces 130 horsepower and 133 lb-ft of torque. While the Sonata offers spacious seating and a comfortable ride, it has been known to experience some issues.

**Engine**

**2.0L Inline-Four**

* **Intake Manifold Gasket Failure:** The intake manifold gasket can fail, causing a vacuum leak. Symptoms include rough idling, poor acceleration, and decreased fuel efficiency. The solution is to replace the intake manifold gasket. Estimated cost: $200 - $300+
* **Engine Knock:** Engine knock may occur due to worn or damaged piston rings or connecting rod bearings. Symptoms include a knocking sound from the engine, especially under load. The solution is to rebuild or replace the engine. Estimated cost: $2,000 - $3,000+
* **Oil Consumption:** Excessive oil consumption may be caused by worn piston rings or valve seals. Symptoms include low oil levels and blue smoke from the exhaust. The solution is to replace the piston rings or valve seals. Estimated cost: $500 - $1,000+

**Transmission**

**5-Speed Manual**

* **Clutch Failure:** The clutch may wear out over time, leading to slipping or difficulty engaging gears. Symptoms include difficulty shifting gears, especially under load. The solution is to replace the clutch. Estimated cost: $600 - $1,000+
* **Transmission Noise:** Transmission noise, such as grinding or whining, may indicate worn or damaged gears or bearings. The solution is to rebuild or replace the transmission. Estimated cost: $1,500 - $2,500+

**4-Speed Automatic**

* **Slipping:** The transmission may slip gears, causing the vehicle to hesitate or lose power. Symptoms include a delay in acceleration or a sudden drop in speed. The solution is to rebuild or replace the transmission. Estimated cost: $1,500 - $2,500+
* **Shift Solenoid Failure:** A faulty shift solenoid may prevent the transmission from shifting gears properly. Symptoms include difficulty or delayed gear shifting. The solution is to replace the shift solenoid. Estimated cost: $200 - $500+

**Suspension**

* **Front Strut Mounts:** The front strut mounts may wear out or become damaged, leading to a clunking or rattling noise when driving over bumps. The solution is to replace the strut mounts. Estimated cost: $200 - $400+
* **Rear Shock Absorbers:** The rear shock absorbers may fail, resulting in a bouncy ride or poor handling. Symptoms include excessive body roll and a rough ride. The solution is to replace the shock absorbers. Estimated cost: $250 - $500+

**Electrical**

* **Battery Failure:** The battery may fail, leaving the vehicle unable to start. Symptoms include a dead battery or difficulty starting the vehicle. The solution is to replace the battery. Estimated cost: $100 - $200+
* **Alternator Failure:** The alternator may fail, causing the battery to discharge and the vehicle to lose power. Symptoms include dimming headlights, a flickering dashboard, and a dead battery. The solution is to replace the alternator. Estimated cost: $500 - $800+
* **Starter Failure:** The starter may fail, preventing the vehicle from starting. Symptoms include a clicking noise when trying to start the vehicle or no response when turning the ignition key. The solution is to replace the starter. Estimated cost: $300 - $500+

**Exterior**

* **Rust:** The vehicle may be prone to rust on the body panels, especially in areas such as the wheel wells and undercarriage. The solution is to repair or replace the rusted panels. Estimated cost: $500 - $2,000+

**Interior**

* **Climate Control Malfunction:** The climate control system may malfunction, resulting in inadequate heating or cooling. Symptoms include inconsistent temperature control or a lack of airflow. The solution is to diagnose and repair the faulty components of the climate control system. Estimated cost: $200 - $800+
* **Door Lock Failure:** The door locks may fail to operate properly, either manually or remotely. Symptoms include difficulty opening or closing the doors or a lack of response to the remote key fob. The solution is to replace the faulty door lock actuators or switches. Estimated cost: $100 - $300+
* **Window Regulator Failure:** The window regulators may fail, preventing the windows from rolling up or down properly. Symptoms include difficulty opening or closing the windows or a window that falls out of alignment. The solution is to replace the window regulators. Estimated cost: $200 - $500+

**Summary:**

The 1996 Hyundai Sonata is a reliable vehicle that offers good value for its price. However, it has been known to experience some issues with its engine, transmission, suspension, electrical system, and interior components. While some of these issues are relatively minor and inexpensive to fix, others can be more serious and costly to repair. It is important to consider these potential issues when purchasing or owning a 1996 Hyundai Sonata and to budget accordingly for maintenance and repairs.
