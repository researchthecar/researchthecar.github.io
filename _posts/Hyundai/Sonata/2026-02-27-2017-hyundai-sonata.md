# 2017 Hyundai Sonata Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2017 Hyundai Sonata is a popular midsize sedan known for its comfortable ride, spacious cabin, and user-friendly technology. However, used car buyers should approach this model year with caution due to notable powertrain complaints and recurrent body paint concerns.

---

## Engine

### 2.4L 4-Cylinder

The base 2.4-liter naturally aspirated four-cylinder engine produces around 185 horsepower and offers adequate everyday performance, though it requires careful monitoring of oil levels and engine health.

**Engine Stalling and Power Loss**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers report unexpected engine stalling while driving, accompanied by warning lights and a sudden loss of motive power.
- **Solution:** Have a diagnostic scan performed for stored trouble codes (such as P1326) and check with a local dealer for applicable powertrain software updates or warranty extensions.

**Excessive Oil Consumption**
- **Symptoms:** Owners note abnormally rapid oil consumption, often requiring frequent top-offs between oil changes and occasionally accompanied by burning smells.
- **Solution:** Monitor oil levels regularly and consult a dealer or certified mechanic to document consumption rates and determine if internal engine repairs are needed.

### 1.6L Turbocharged 4-Cylinder

The 1.6-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine is paired with a dual-clutch transmission to prioritize fuel economy and lower-end torque.

### 2.0L Turbocharged 4-Cylinder

The 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine generates around 245 horsepower, providing a sportier driving experience for higher-tier trims.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

The traditional 6-speed automatic transmission delivers smooth, reliable gear shifts and is standard on most conventional models.

### 7-Speed Dual-Clutch Transmission

The 7-speed dual-clutch transmission offers quick shifts paired exclusively with the 1.6L turbo engine for enhanced efficiency.

## Steering

### Electric Power Steering

Issues with electronic power steering feedback and warning lights have been reported by some owners.

**Steering Wheel Stiffening and Warning Lights**
- **Symptoms:** The steering wheel suddenly becomes extremely difficult to turn, accompanied by illumination of the traction control and steering warning lights.
- **Solution:** Inspect the electric power steering module and associated wiring connections, and have diagnostic trouble codes scanned.

## Exterior

### Exterior Paint

Cosmetic paint defects and premature peeling have been a notable complaint area for certain exterior color finishes.

**Paint Peeling and Corrosion**
- **Symptoms:** Factory paint begins to bubble, peel, or chip prematurely, particularly on horizontal surfaces like the roof and hood.
- **Solution:** Check if the vehicle qualifies for manufacturer paint warranty extensions or consult a professional auto body shop for repainting.

### Headlights and Lighting

electrical issues regarding exterior lighting components can cause premature bulb failure.

**Headlight Bulb Burnout and Melting**
- **Symptoms:** Low beam headlight bulbs burn out repeatedly, sometimes resulting in melted electrical connectors or housings.
- **Solution:** Inspect bulb connectors and wiring harnesses for heat damage, and replace compromised sockets along with bulbs.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V956000: Air Bags:Frontal:Driver Side:Inflator Module](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Hyundai&model=Sonata&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** In these vehicles, the end seal for the driver's frontal air bag inflator may not have been properly installed, possibly resulting in reduced inflation of the frontal air bag in the event of a crash. In the event of a crash, an air bag that does not inflate fully increases the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Hyundai will notify owners, and dealers will replace the driver's frontal air bag module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V749000: Exterior Lighting:Turn Signal](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Hyundai&model=Sonata&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** The Smart Junction Box (SJB) software logic may not properly interpret the signals received from the multifunction switch, which could cause the turn signal to activate in the opposite direction of what the driver intended. A turn signal that activates in the opposite direction of what was intended by the driver increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the SJB software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V619000: Latches/Locks/Linkages:Trunk Lid:Latch](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Hyundai&model=Sonata&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** The trunk latch may become damaged, preventing the opening of the trunk from the inside. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 401, "Internal Trunk Release." A person inside the trunk compartment may become trapped, increasing their risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the trunk latch base, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

When shopping for a used 2017 Hyundai Sonata, carefully inspect the vehicle service history, check for completed recall campaigns, and verify that the engine does not exhibit excessive oil consumption or stalling symptoms. A thorough pre-purchase inspection by an independent mechanic is highly recommended.
