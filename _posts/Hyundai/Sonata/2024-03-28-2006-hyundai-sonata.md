The 2006 Hyundai Sonata was available with three different engine and transmission options:

- 2.4L inline-4 with 4-speed automatic transmission
- 2.4L inline-4 with 5-speed manual transmission
- 3.3L V6 with 5-speed automatic transmission

The Sonata was praised for its spacious interior, comfortable ride, and value for money. However, it was criticized for its lackluster performance, poor fuel economy, and cheap-feeling interior materials.

**Engine**

**2.4L Inline-4 with 4-Speed Automatic Transmission**
- **Knocking Noise in Engine**
    Description/Symptoms: A knocking noise is heard from the engine, especially when accelerating or under load.
    Solution: Replace the engine's connecting rod bearings.
    Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
    Recalls: None Found
- **Engine Oil Leak**
    Description/Symptoms: Engine oil leaks from the valve cover gasket or oil pan gasket.
    Solution: Replace the leaking gasket.
    Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
    Recalls: None Found

**2.4L Inline-4 with 5-Speed Manual Transmission**

No Issues Found

**3.3L V6 with 5-Speed Automatic Transmission**

- **Intake Manifold Gasket Failure**
    Description/Symptoms: The intake manifold gasket fails, causing coolant and oil leaks.
    Solution: Replace the intake manifold gasket.
    Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $600+
    Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**4-Speed Automatic Transmission**

- **Slipping or Jerking Transmission**
    Description/Symptoms: The transmission slips or jerks when shifting gears or accelerating.
    Solution: Replace or rebuild the transmission.
    Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,500+
    Recalls: None Found

**5-Speed Manual Transmission**

No Issues Found

**Electrical**

- **Electrical Shorts**
    Description/Symptoms: Electrical shorts cause problems with various electrical components, such as lighting, power windows, and door locks.
    Solution: Diagnose and repair the electrical short.
    Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+
    Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

No Issues Found

**Interior**

- **Dashboard Cracking**
    Description/Symptoms: The dashboard cracks and deteriorates over time.
    Solution: Replace the dashboard.
    Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    Recalls: None Found
- **Headliner Sagging**
    Description/Symptoms: The headliner sags and separates from the roof.
    Solution: Reattach or replace the headliner.
    Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
    Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2006 Hyundai Sonata has several potential issues, particularly with the engine, transmission, and electrical systems. While some repairs are relatively inexpensive, major issues like a knocking engine or transmission replacement can be costly. Before purchasing a used Sonata, it is advisable to have it thoroughly inspected by a qualified mechanic to identify any potential problems.
