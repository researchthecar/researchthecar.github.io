# 2016 Hyundai Sonata Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2016 Hyundai Sonata offers a comfortable ride, spacious cabin, and practical features, making it an appealing choice for a used midsize sedan. However, prospective buyers should approach certain powertrain configurations and model years with caution due to notable engine reliability concerns reported by owners. Thoroughly checking maintenance history, open recall statuses, and previous engine replacements is strongly recommended before purchasing.

---

## Engine

### 2.4L 4-Cylinder

A 2.4-liter naturally aspirated four-cylinder engine producing around 185 horsepower, serving as the volume-selling powerplant for the Sonata lineup. While efficient and smooth during normal operation, this engine family has faced significant scrutiny regarding premature internal wear and oil consumption.

**Engine Failure and Rod Bearing Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle enters emergency limp mode, illuminates warning lights, displays P1326 codes, or suffers sudden engine seizure and failure.
- **Solution:** Requires engine inspection, possible software updates, or complete engine replacement under manufacturer warranty/campaigns.

**Excessive Oil Consumption and Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** Engine loses power, misfires, struggles to idle, and consumes high amounts of oil leading to potential stalling.
- **Solution:** Diagnostic testing, spark plug or cylinder repairs, and monitoring oil levels or replacing piston rings/short blocks.

### 1.6L Turbocharged 4-Cylinder

A turbocharged 1.6-liter four-cylinder engine paired with a dual-clutch transmission, focused on maximizing fuel efficiency while delivering low-end torque.

### 2.0L Turbocharged 4-Cylinder

A performance-oriented 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine generating approximately 245 horsepower for sportier trims.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

A traditional 6-speed automatic transmission paired with standard gasoline engines, known for smooth shifts and generally dependable everyday performance.

### 7-Speed Dual-Clutch Transmission

A 7-speed dual-clutch automatic transmission mated exclusively to the 1.6L turbo engine, offering quick gear changes geared toward efficiency.

## Steering

### Power Steering System

Electric power-assist steering system providing adjustable steering effort.

**Intermittent Power Steering Loss**
- **Symptoms:** Steering wheel becomes stiff or seizes entirely during low-speed turns, accompanied by EPS and traction control warning lights.
- **Solution:** Inspect electronic power steering motor, steering column modules, and related sensors for faults.

## Exterior

### Exterior Lighting

Controls exterior illumination including headlights, turn signals, and brake lights.

**Headlight and Taillight Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Headlight assemblies or brake light bulbs fail prematurely, causing visibility issues and warning indications.
- **Solution:** Replace burnt-out bulbs, faulty wiring harnesses, or entire headlight assemblies as necessary.

### Paint and Exterior Finish

Exterior body panels and factory paint finishes protecting the vehicle structure from environmental elements.

**Paint Peeling and Chipping**
- **Symptoms:** Factory paint, particularly on white or pearl finishes, begins bubbling, peeling, or chipping away from body panels.
- **Solution:** Repaint affected body panels through professional auto body repair services.

## Safety

### Air Bags and Sensors

Supplemental restraint systems and internal occupant-detection sensors designed to protect vehicle passengers during impacts.

**Occupant Classification Sensor Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Passenger airbag warning lights illuminate or passenger seat sensors fail to properly detect seated occupants.
- **Solution:** Inspect wiring or replace affected seat cushion modules and sensor components.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V726000: Visibility:Sun/Moon Roof Assembly](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Hyundai&model=Sonata&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** If the sunroof detaches from the vehicle it could become a road hazard, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Hyundai will notify owners, and dealers will repair the wind deflector anchor plate, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V359000: Parking Brake:Indicator Light](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Hyundai&model=Sonata&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** If the parking brake indicator is not illuminated when the parking brake is applied, the driver may operate the vehicle with the parking brake partially engaged, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Hyundai will notify owners, and dealers will replace the parking brake switch, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V615000: Air Bags](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Hyundai&model=Sonata&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** The affected vehicles have a driver-side frontal air bag that may not adequately protect the driver's neck from injury in the event of a crash. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 208, "Occupant Crash Protection." A frontal air bag that does not adequately protect the driver's.
- **What to do:** Hyundai will notify owners, and dealers will replace the driver's frontal air bag module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V232000: Air Bags:Frontal](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Hyundai&model=Sonata&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** In these vehicles, the driver's frontal air bag may be missing a chemical enhancer required for the second stage air bag deployment, resulting in the second stage not deploying in certain crashes. The primary stage will deploy as intended.
- **What to do:** Hyundai will notify owners, and dealers will replace the driver's frontal air bag module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 15V678000: Electrical System: Instrument Cluster/Panel](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Hyundai&model=Sonata&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** Since the vehicles have ABS malfunction lights that do not illuminate, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 135, "Light Vehicle Brake Systems." If the driver is alerted there is a brake system malfunction, but not specifically with the ABS system, the driver may operate the vehicle.
- **What to do:** Hyundai will notify owners, and dealers will update the ABS control unit software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V749000: Exterior Lighting:Turn Signal](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Hyundai&model=Sonata&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** The Smart Junction Box (SJB) software logic may not properly interpret the signals received from the multifunction switch, which could cause the turn signal to activate in the opposite direction of what the driver intended. A turn signal that activates in the opposite direction of what was intended by the driver increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the SJB software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2016 Hyundai Sonata presents a mixed ownership proposition for used-car shoppers. While its comfort, interior room, and feature set are strong selling points, potential buyers must exercise caution regarding reported engine longevity issues, particularly oil consumption and internal wear on 2.4L models. Anyone considering this vehicle should prioritize models with documented maintenance records, completed manufacturer campaigns, and a clean pre-purchase inspection.
