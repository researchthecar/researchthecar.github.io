# 2021 Hyundai Sonata Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2021 Hyundai Sonata is a stylish and feature-packed midsize sedan that offers strong value and a comfortable ride. However, prospective buyers should review known owner complaints regarding engine operation, fuel systems, and electrical components before making a purchase decision.

---

## Engine

### 2.5L 4-Cylinder

The base 2.5-liter naturally aspirated four-cylinder engine produces 191 horsepower and 181 lb-ft of torque, providing balanced everyday performance. While generally reliable, some owners have reported cooling system and drivability concerns.

**Coolant Leak**
- **Symptoms:** An internal coolant leak can develop from components such as the Exhaust Heat Recovery System (EHRS) in the center muffler assembly.
- **Solution:** Inspect the cooling system and exhaust heat recovery components; replace leaking parts or assemblies as needed.

**Loss of Motive Power**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may experience sudden hesitation, loss of acceleration, or unresponsiveness while depressing the accelerator pedal.
- **Solution:** Diagnose engine management codes and inspect fuel or powertrain control modules.

### 1.6L Turbocharged 4-Cylinder

The 1.6-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine generates 180 horsepower and 195 lb-ft of torque, offering lively low-end acceleration. Buyers should ensure proper maintenance records when considering this forced-induction option.

**Engine Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may unexpectedly stall while driving or fail to maintain a steady idle, sometimes accompanied by warning lights.
- **Solution:** Check for diagnostic trouble codes and inspect ignition, fuel delivery, or sensor components.

### 2.5L Turbocharged 4-Cylinder (N Line)

Exclusive to the performance-oriented N Line trim, the 2.5-liter turbocharged engine pumps out a robust 290 horsepower and 311 lb-ft of torque. It delivers thrilling acceleration for enthusiasts seeking sport-sedan dynamics.

### 2.0L Hybrid 4-Cylinder

The hybrid powertrain pairs a 2.0-liter four-cylinder engine with an electric motor to generate a combined 192 horsepower. It offers exceptional fuel economy for daily commuting and long-distance driving.

### Fuel System

Components responsible for fuel delivery and pressure regulation occasionally experience operational faults reported by owners.

**Fuel Pump Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Failure of the fuel pump can prevent the vehicle from starting or cause unexpected stalling while driving.
- **Solution:** Test fuel pressure and replace the failing fuel pump assembly.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

Standard models utilize an 8-speed conventional automatic transmission that delivers smooth and efficient gear shifts during normal driving conditions.

**Shifting Delays and Shifter Faults**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may encounter delayed gear engagement, harsh jerking, or dashboard messages indicating a shifter system malfunction.
- **Solution:** Update transmission control modules or service the transmission assembly as prescribed by diagnostic procedures.

### 8-Speed Wet Dual-Clutch Transmission

Found exclusively on the high-performance N Line trim, this wet dual-clutch transmission provides lightning-fast gear changes to match the engine's sporty power output.

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission (Hybrid)

The hybrid variant relies on a dedicated 6-speed automatic transmission designed to seamlessly blend gasoline and electric power sources for optimal efficiency.

## Exterior

### Door Locks

Cabin electrical accessories and convenience features are generally reliable, though isolated actuator and battery drain complaints have been documented.

**Door Lock Actuator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Power door locks and associated switches may fail to respond consistently, requiring repeated attempts or manual intervention.
- **Solution:** Replace the faulty door lock actuator mechanism.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V524000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Hoses, Lines/Piping, And Fittings](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Hyundai&model=Sonata&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** A fuel leak increases the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and tighten, or replace the fuel pipe as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V00M000: Visibility:Windshield](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Hyundai&model=Sonata&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** During manufacturing, the windshield may not have been properly bonded to the vehicle, allowing it to detach in a crash. A windshield that detaches from a vehicle during a crash can increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will remove and reinstall the front windshield panel, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V746000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Hyundai&model=Sonata&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** Loss of drive power increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the transmission, as necessary. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V796000: Fuel System, Gasoline](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Hyundai&model=Sonata&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** A melted fuel tank can leak, increasing the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the check valve. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2021 Hyundai Sonata remains an attractive used vehicle option thanks to its modern styling, comprehensive features, and diverse powertrain lineup. While most owners report a satisfying driving experience, buyers should thoroughly inspect the cooling system, electrical accessories, and transmission operation during a pre-purchase test drive.
