# 2017 Hyundai Santa Fe Sport

### Overview
The 2017 Hyundai Santa Fe Sport is a popular mid-size SUV known for its practicality and value. Reliability data for this model year generally indicates a solid performance, with most owners reporting satisfaction. However, like any vehicle, there are specific areas that have seen more attention from repair shops and owner forums. This analysis focuses on documented technical issues beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential problem areas for the 2.0L Turbo and 2.4L naturally aspirated engines, as well as the 6-speed automatic transmission.

---


## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4
**Oil Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Visible oil spots under the vehicle, burning oil smell, low oil level.
- **Solution:** Replacement of leaking seals or gaskets (e.g., valve cover gasket, oil pan gasket).
- **Cost:** $200 - $600

**Turbocharger Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of power, whistling noises from the engine bay, increased oil consumption.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of the turbocharger assembly.
- **Cost:** $1,000 - $2,500


### 2.4L Naturally Aspirated Inline-4
**Oil Consumption**
- **Symptoms:** Frequent need to top up oil, blue smoke from exhaust, especially on startup.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis may lead to piston ring replacement or other internal engine repairs.
- **Cost:** $1,500 - $3,500


## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission
**Hesitation or Jerking**
- **Symptoms:** Rough shifting, delayed engagement, or sudden jolts during acceleration or deceleration.
- **Solution:** Software updates, transmission fluid flush and replacement, or internal transmission repair.
- **Cost:** $300 - $1,500

## Electrical

### Infotainment System
**Screen Freezing or Unresponsiveness**
- **Symptoms:** Touchscreen becomes unresponsive, system freezes, or reboots unexpectedly.
- **Solution:** Software updates or replacement of the infotainment unit.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1,200


### Battery Drain
**Premature Battery Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle fails to start, battery warning light illuminates.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis to identify parasitic draw or faulty alternator; battery replacement.
- **Cost:** $200 - $500

### Summary Verdict
The 2017 Hyundai Santa Fe Sport offers a generally reliable ownership experience. The most frequently cited technical concerns revolve around the engine, particularly oil leaks and potential turbocharger issues on the 2.0L turbo model, and oil consumption on the 2.4L engine. Transmission issues, while not pervasive, can manifest as shifting problems. Electrical gremlins, such as infotainment system glitches and battery drain, are also noted. However, suspension, exterior, and interior components are largely free of widespread defects. With proactive maintenance and awareness of these potential issues, the Santa Fe Sport remains a sensible choice for families seeking a capable and affordable SUV.
