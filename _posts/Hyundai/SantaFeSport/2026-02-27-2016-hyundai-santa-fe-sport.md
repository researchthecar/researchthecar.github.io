# 2016 Hyundai Santa Fe Sport Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2016 Hyundai Santa Fe Sport is a popular midsize crossover known for its comfortable interior, spacious cargo room, and balanced ride quality. However, prospective buyers should approach this model year with caution due to well-documented powertrain and safety concerns. Chief among these are severe engine reliability issues tied to the Theta II engine family, alongside auxiliary electrical and structural complaints. Thoroughly reviewing maintenance and recall history is essential before purchasing.

---

## Engine

### 2.4L 4-Cylinder Engine

The base 2.4-liter naturally aspirated four-cylinder engine produces approximately 185 horsepower and 178 lb-ft of torque. While adequate for daily commuting and fuel economy, this Theta II powerplant has a history of high-profile reliability concerns, including connecting rod bearing wear and potential fire risks.

**Engine Stalling and Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicles may experience unexpected engine stalling while driving in traffic, sudden loss of motive power, or catastrophic internal mechanical failure.
- **Solution:** May require engine replacement or connecting rod bearing service under manufacturer campaigns.

**Engine Fire Risk**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle may emit warning lights or suddenly catch fire after being parked or while operating.
- **Solution:** Ensure all applicable safety recalls and wiring harness inspections have been completed by a certified dealer.

### 2.0L Turbocharged 4-Cylinder Engine

The optional 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine delivers a more robust 240 horsepower and 260 lb-ft of torque, offering significantly better acceleration for passing and towing. Like the base engine, it shares foundational Theta II architecture and requires close attention to its service history.

**Electrical Warning Lights and Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** Illuminating dashboard warning lights accompanied by limp mode, sudden stalling, or total loss of motive power.
- **Solution:** Diagnostic scanning, sensor replacement, or electrical harness repair.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

The standard 6-speed automatic transmission routes power smoothly to either front-wheel drive or all-wheel drive models. It generally exhibits good mechanical durability compared to the vehicle's engine options.

## Brakes

### Hydraulic Service Brakes

The braking system provides adequate stopping power under normal conditions, though electronic control module concerns and occasional ABS warning activations have been reported by owners.

**ABS Warning and Brake Locking**
- **Symptoms:** Illumination of the ABS warning light or unexpected locking of the brakes while driving at low speeds.
- **Solution:** Inspect ABS module, wheel speed sensors, and hydraulic control units; perform relevant recall repairs.

## Exterior

### Glass and Sunroof

Exterior body panels are generally sturdy, but owners should inspect panoramic sunroof models for stress fractures or previous glass replacement records.

**Spontaneous Glass Shattering**
- **Symptoms:** Panoramic sunroof or windshield glass shattering or exploding outward without impact while driving or parked.
- **Solution:** Replace the damaged glass panel and inspect for frame alignment or manufacturing stress points.

### Latches and Hood

Cabin appointments offer functional ergonomics and passenger comfort, though hardware components like door and hood latches warrant physical inspection.

**Hood Latch Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Hood unlatches and flies open while driving, obscuring the windshield and creating a severe visibility hazard.
- **Solution:** Replace or service the hood latch mechanism under applicable manufacturer recall campaigns.

## Safety

### Airbags and Restraints

Occupant safety systems feature standard advanced airbags, though dashboard warning lights and sensor faults require immediate diagnostic verification to ensure proper deployment readiness.

**Airbag Warning Light and Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Airbag warning light remaining illuminated on the dashboard or failure of airbags to deploy during a collision.
- **Solution:** Diagnose clockspring, occupant classification sensors, or airbag control module faults.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V358000: Latches/Locks/Linkages:Hood:Latch](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Hyundai&model=Santa+Fe+Sport&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** If the hood is not securely closed or the primary latch is inadvertently released and the secondary latch is not engaged, the hood could unexpectedly open while driving, increasing the risk of a vehicle crash.
- **What to do:** Hyundai will notify owners, and dealers will replace the secondary latch cable, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2016 Hyundai Santa Fe Sport presents a mixed opportunity for used car buyers. While it offers impressive feature content, styling, and passenger space for the price, the potential for catastrophic engine failure or fire risks in the Theta II engine lineup demands extreme caution. Buyers must verify that all engine-related recalls have been completed by a dealer and obtain a pre-purchase inspection from an independent mechanic before finalizing a deal.
