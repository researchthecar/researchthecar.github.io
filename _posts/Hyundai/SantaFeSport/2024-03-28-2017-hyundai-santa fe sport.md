**Introduction:**

The 2017 Hyundai Santa Fe Sport is a midsize crossover SUV that offers a combination of practicality, affordability, and style. It is available with three engine options: a 185-horsepower 2.4L GDI inline-4, a 240-horsepower 2.0L turbocharged GDI inline-4, and a 240-horsepower 3.3L V6. Transmission options include a 6-speed automatic and an 8-speed automatic. The Santa Fe Sport is known for its spacious interior, comfortable ride, and user-friendly technology. However, some owners have reported issues with various components.

**Engine**

**2.4L GDI**

* **Excessive Oil Consumption:**
    * Description/Symptoms: High oil consumption, leading to low oil levels that can cause engine damage.
    * Solution: Oil consumption monitoring and regular oil top-ups. In some cases, the engine may need to be rebuilt.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $5,000+
    * Recalls: 20V632

* **Timing Chain Tensioner Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Failure of the timing chain tensioner, which can lead to chain skipping and engine damage.
    * Solution: Replacing the timing chain tensioner and timing chain.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500
    * Recalls: None Found

**2.0L Turbocharged GDI**

* **Turbocharger Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Premature failure of the turbocharger, resulting in reduced engine power and performance.
    * Solution: Replacing the turbocharger.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,500
    * Recalls: None Found

**3.3L V6**

* **No Issues Found**

**Transmission**

**6-Speed Automatic**

* **Transmission Slipping:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Hesitation or slipping during gear changes, accompanied by a jerking sensation.
    * Solution: Replacing the transmission fluid and filter. In some cases, the entire transmission may need to be rebuilt.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $4,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**8-Speed Automatic**

* **No Issues Found**

**Suspension**

* **Front Suspension Noise:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Rattling or clunking noises from the front suspension, particularly over rough roads.
    * Solution: Replacing the front suspension strut mounts or bearings.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,200
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Electrical Shorts:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Intermittent electrical issues, such as power loss or flickering lights.
    * Solution: Identifying and replacing the faulty electrical components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+
    * Recalls: 20V207

**Exterior**

* **Roof Rack Crossbars Loosening:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The roof rack crossbars becoming loose or detaching, especially when carrying heavy loads.
    * Solution: Tightening the crossbars or replacing them if damaged.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500
    * Recalls: 19V321, 19V643

**Interior**

* **Center Console Storage Bin Breaking:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The plastic storage bin in the center console breaks or cracks.
    * Solution: Replacing the storage bin.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $150
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Sunroof Leaks:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Water leaking into the cabin through the sunroof, especially during heavy rain.
    * Solution: Replacing the sunroof seals or drainage tubes.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500
    * Recalls: 19V052

**Summary:**

Overall, the 2017 Hyundai Santa Fe Sport has received mixed reviews from owners. While it offers a comfortable and spacious interior, as well as a range of engine and transmission options, some models have experienced issues with excessive oil consumption, turbocharger failure, and transmission slippage. Electrical shorts and sunroof leaks have also been reported. The severity and cost of these issues can vary depending on the specific problem and the affected parts. It is recommended to thoroughly research and consider the potential issues before purchasing a used 2017 Hyundai Santa Fe Sport.
