**Introduction:**

The 2013 Hyundai Santa Fe Sport was a midsize SUV available with a choice of two engine and transmission options. The base engine was a 2.4-liter four-cylinder producing 190 horsepower and 181 lb-ft of torque, paired with a six-speed automatic transmission. The optional engine was a 3.3-liter V6 generating 290 horsepower and 252 lb-ft of torque, paired with a six-speed automatic transmission.

**Owners praised the Santa Fe Sport for its spacious interior, comfortable ride, and impressive fuel economy. However, some reported issues with specific components.**

**Engine**

**2.4L Four-Cylinder**

* **Premature Cam Phaser Failure:** Failure of the cam phaser gear can cause the engine to stall or misfire.
    * Solution: Replace the cam phaser gear.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $600+
    * Recalls: None Found

**3.3L V6**

* **Intake Valve Carbon Buildup:** Over time, carbon deposits can accumulate on the intake valves, leading to reduced engine power and performance.
    * Solution: Clean the intake valves and intake manifold.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**Six-Speed Automatic**

* **Shift Solenoid Failure:** Failure of the shift solenoid can cause the transmission to shift erratically or fail to shift altogether.
    * Solution: Replace the shift solenoid.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Rear Spring Breakage:** The rear coil springs may fracture, resulting in a loss of ride height and handling.
    * Solution: Replace the rear coil springs.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Alternator Failure:** Premature alternator failure can lead to a loss of electrical power and battery depletion.
    * Solution: Replace the alternator.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Loose Side Mirror:** The side mirrors may become loose and vibrate excessively.
    * Solution: Tighten or replace the side mirror bracket.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Climate Control Malfunction:** The climate control system may malfunction, leading to ineffective heating or cooling.
    * Solution: Diagnose and repair the faulty component within the system.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

Overall, the 2013 Hyundai Santa Fe Sport is a reliable vehicle with generally minor issues. However, potential buyers should be aware of the possibility of engine, transmission, suspension, electrical, and interior problems. The estimated cost of repairs for these issues can range from a few hundred dollars to well over $1,000, depending on the severity of the issue. It is recommended to have the vehicle inspected thoroughly by a reputable mechanic before purchasing.
