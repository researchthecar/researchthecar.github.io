The 2015 Hyundai Santa Fe Sport is a popular compact SUV known for its spacious interior, comfortable ride, and stylish design. It offers a choice between two engines: a 2.4L naturally aspirated inline-4 producing 190 horsepower and 181 lb-ft of torque, and a more powerful 2.0L turbocharged inline-4 generating 264 horsepower and 269 lb-ft of torque. Both engines are paired with a 6-speed automatic transmission. Owners often praise the Santa Fe Sport for its value, features, and comfortable seating, while some criticisms include the base engine's perceived lack of power and occasionally reported issues with reliability.

**Engine**

**2.4L Inline-4**

*   **Engine Failure due to connecting rod bearing failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Engine knocking, oil consumption, complete engine failure. Often occurs without significant warning. May be related to manufacturing debris left in the engine during assembly.
    *   Solution: Engine replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $4,000 - $6,000+
    *   Recalls: 17V226000, 17V224000, 19V566000, 20V750000

*   **Catalytic Converter Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Check engine light illuminated (often with code P0420), decreased fuel economy, sluggish acceleration, and a sulfur-like smell.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the catalytic converter.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**2.0L Turbo Inline-4**

*   **Turbocharger Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, whistling or whining noise from the turbocharger, check engine light illuminated.
    *   Solution: Turbocharger replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**6-Speed Automatic (Both Engines)**

*   **Harsh Shifting/Hesitation**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rough or jerky gear changes, hesitation when accelerating, particularly between 1st and 2nd gear, or 2nd and 3rd.
    *   Solution: Transmission fluid flush and software update. In some cases, valve body replacement or complete transmission replacement may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 (flush/update) - $3,000+ (replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Rear Suspension Noise**
    * Description/Symptoms: Clunking or rattling noise coming from the rear suspension when driving over bumps or uneven surfaces.
    * Solution: Inspection and replacement of worn bushings, sway bar links, or struts.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Battery Drain**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Battery dies overnight or after short periods of inactivity. Difficulty starting the vehicle.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis and repair of parasitic drain (e.g., faulty door sensor, aftermarket electronics, or faulty module). Battery replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+ (diagnosis), $150+ (battery)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Faulty Mass Airflow Sensor (MAF)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Check engine light, rough idling, stalling, poor fuel economy, hesitation during acceleration
    *   Solution: Replacement of the MAF sensor
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Airbag Malfunction**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Airbag warning light illuminated, indicating a potential issue with the airbag system.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis and repair of the airbag system, which may involve replacing the clock spring, airbag module, or sensors.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+
    *   Recalls: 17V226000, 22V901000

**Exterior**

*   **No Issues Found**

**Interior**

*   **Panoramic Sunroof Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Sunroof stuck open or closed, motor failure, leaks, cracking glass.
    *   Solution: Repair or replacement of the sunroof motor, tracks, or glass panel.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2015 Hyundai Santa Fe Sport has a mix of potential issues that prospective buyers should be aware of. The most serious and costly issue is the potential for engine failure, particularly with the 2.4L engine, which is subject to multiple recalls. Transmission problems, while not always catastrophic, can lead to drivability concerns. Electrical issues like battery drain can be frustrating, while sunroof problems and suspension noises can impact the overall driving experience. Buyers should carefully inspect the vehicle's service history, consider a pre-purchase inspection by a qualified mechanic, and ensure all applicable recalls have been addressed to mitigate potential risks. The cost of these repairs can vary widely, but engine replacements can be substantial.
