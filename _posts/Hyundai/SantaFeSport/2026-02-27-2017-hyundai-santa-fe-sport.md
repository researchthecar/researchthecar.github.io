# 2017 Hyundai Santa Fe Sport Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2017 Hyundai Santa Fe Sport is a popular compact crossover that offers a comfortable ride, practical interior space, and a generous feature set for the money. However, used car buyers should approach this model year with caution due to a notable history of owner complaints centered around engine reliability, oil consumption, and potential stalling issues. While it offers strong value on paper, thoroughly inspecting a prospective vehicle's service history and engine condition is essential before purchasing.

---

## Engine

### 2.4L 4-Cylinder

A naturally aspirated 2.4-liter 4-cylinder engine producing around 185 horsepower, serving as the standard base powerplant for the Santa Fe Sport. While adequate for daily commuting, this engine option is frequently associated with owner reports of high oil consumption and unexpected stalling incidents.

**Engine Stalling and Excessive Oil Consumption**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers report unexpected engine stalling while driving, illuminated warning lights, and significantly increased oil consumption leading to low fluid levels.
- **Solution:** Diagnostic inspection by a certified mechanic; repairs may range from software updates and sensor replacements to major engine component overhauls or complete engine replacement depending on internal wear.

### 2.0L Turbo 4-Cylinder

A turbocharged 2.0-liter 4-cylinder engine pushing roughly 240 horsepower, providing significantly peppier acceleration and passing power. Buyers opting for the turbo should closely monitor maintenance records and watch for signs of oil consumption or turbo-related hesitation.

**Loss of Motive Power and Misfires**
- **Symptoms:** Sudden loss of acceleration power, illuminated check engine lights, rough idling, and cylinder misfires while under way.
- **Solution:** Inspect ignition coils, spark plugs, and fuel delivery systems; professional diagnosis may require addressing turbocharger components or internal engine wear.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

A conventional 6-speed automatic transmission paired with both engine choices across front-wheel and all-wheel-drive configurations. It generally offers smooth, predictable shifting and does not suffer from the same high volume of owner complaints as the available engine options.

## Brakes

### Hydraulic Brake System

The service brake setup provides adequate stopping power but has generated owner concerns regarding hydraulic component performance and warning light illuminations.

**Brake System and ABS Warning Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** Brake warning lamps or traction control lights illuminating intermittently, or concerns regarding hydraulic brake system performance.
- **Solution:** Have the ABS module, brake fluid levels, and hydraulic lines inspected by a qualified technician.

## Steering

### Steering Column and Electronic Steering

The electronic power steering system provides light and adjustable feedback for easy maneuvering, though some owners have reported intermittent stiffness and steering sensor glitches.

**Steering Wheel Sticking and Sensor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The steering wheel may feel sticky, become hard to keep centered without extra force, or occasionally experience stiffness requiring a vehicle restart to clear.
- **Solution:** Inspect the steering column sensor and electronic power steering assembly; replacement of faulty sensors or steering components may be required.

## Exterior

### Body Paint and Structure

Exterior body panels generally hold up well, but specific factory paint options—notably white pearl finishes—have a known history of severe adhesion failure and peeling.

**Paint Peeling and Flaking**
- **Symptoms:** White pearl paint bubbling, chipping, and peeling off in large sheets from exterior panels such as the hood and roof.
- **Solution:** Professional repainting or body shop evaluation for affected exterior panels.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V358000: Latches/Locks/Linkages:Hood:Latch](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Hyundai&model=Santa+Fe+Sport&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** If the hood is not securely closed or the primary latch is inadvertently released and the secondary latch is not engaged, the hood could unexpectedly open while driving, increasing the risk of a vehicle crash.
- **What to do:** Hyundai will notify owners, and dealers will replace the secondary latch cable, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V365000: Seat Belts:Front:Anchorage](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Hyundai&model=Santa+Fe+Sport&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** If the bolt was not sufficiently tightened, driver's seat belt may come unanchored in a collision, increasing the risk of injury to the occupant.
- **What to do:** Hyundai will notify owners and dealers will verify that the driver's seatbelt anchor is properly secured, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V056000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Antilock/Traction Control/Electronic Limited Slip:Control Unit/Module](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Hyundai&model=Santa+Fe+Sport&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** The Anti-Lock Brake System (ABS) module could malfunction and cause an electrical short, which could result in an engine compartment fire. An engine compartment fire while parked or driving can increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the ABS multi-fuse, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2017 Hyundai Santa Fe Sport is an appealing used crossover due to its comfort and feature list, but prospective buyers must exercise caution. Because of documented owner reports concerning engine oil consumption, potential stalling, and paint peeling, a comprehensive pre-purchase inspection and a verified service history are strongly advised. Prioritize models with documented oil consumption testing and well-maintained service records to minimize potential ownership risks.
