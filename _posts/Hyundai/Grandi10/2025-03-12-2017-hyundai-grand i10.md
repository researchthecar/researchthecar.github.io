Here's a breakdown of common issues reported for the 2017 Hyundai Grand i10, organized by category and engine/transmission configuration.

The 2017 Hyundai Grand i10 is a subcompact car praised for its fuel efficiency, maneuverability, and value. However, it also receives criticism for its somewhat underpowered engine and basic interior features. The 2017 model year was offered with a 1.0-liter 3-cylinder engine producing around 66 horsepower and 70 lb-ft of torque, and a 1.2-liter 4-cylinder engine producing around 82 horsepower and 84 lb-ft of torque. Both engines were available with either a 5-speed manual transmission or a 4-speed automatic transmission.

**Engine**

**1.0L 3-Cylinder Engine**

*   **Engine Vibration**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Excessive vibration, especially at idle, sometimes accompanied by unusual noises.
    *   Solution: Check engine mounts for damage or wear. Replace as needed. Inspect and replace spark plugs if necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $400+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**1.2L 4-Cylinder Engine**

*   **Hesitation During Acceleration**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The engine hesitates or stumbles when accelerating, particularly from a stop.
    *   Solution: Check the fuel injectors for proper function and clean or replace them as needed. Check the air intake system for obstructions.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**5-Speed Manual Transmission**

*   **Difficulty Shifting into Gear**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Difficulty engaging gears, especially 1st or reverse. Grinding noises may be present.
    *   Solution: Check and adjust the clutch cable. If the problem persists, inspect the clutch disc, pressure plate, and throwout bearing. Replace worn components.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**4-Speed Automatic Transmission**

*   **Harsh Shifting**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Abrupt or jerky shifts between gears.
    *   Solution: Perform a transmission fluid flush and refill. If the problem continues, the valve body may need inspection and repair/replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $600+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Worn Suspension Bushings**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Clunking or rattling noises from the suspension, especially when driving over bumps. Vehicle may feel less stable in turns.
    *   Solution: Inspect and replace worn suspension bushings (control arm bushings, sway bar bushings).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Battery Drain**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The battery drains quickly, especially when the car is not driven for a few days.
    *   Solution: Perform a parasitic draw test to identify the source of the drain. Common culprits include faulty relays, aftermarket accessories, or a failing alternator.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $400+
    *   Recalls: None Found
*   **Failing Starter Motor**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Engine fails to start, slow cranking, clicking noises.
    *   Solution: Replace the starter motor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Headlight Condensation**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Moisture buildup inside the headlight lens.
    *   Solution: Remove the headlight assembly and dry it out. Check the seals and vents for damage and repair or replace as needed.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $300+ (depending on whether DIY or professional repair, and if new headlight assembly is needed)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Air Conditioning Malfunction**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Air conditioning blows warm air or fails to cool effectively.
    *   Solution: Check the refrigerant level and recharge if necessary. Inspect the compressor, condenser, and evaporator for leaks or damage.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $800+ (depending on the component needing repair/replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2017 Hyundai Grand i10 generally exhibits minor issues. Engine vibration and hesitation are reported for the 1.0L and 1.2L engines, respectively. Transmission issues include difficulty shifting with the manual transmission and harsh shifting with the automatic. Suspension and electrical problems, such as worn bushings and battery drain, can occur. While some issues may require more extensive repairs, many can be addressed with relatively inexpensive solutions. Overall, the severity of these issues is moderate, with repair costs generally ranging from affordable to moderate.
