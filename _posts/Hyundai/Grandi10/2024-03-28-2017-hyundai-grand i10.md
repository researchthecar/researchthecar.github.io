**Introduction:**

The 2017 Hyundai Grand i10 is a subcompact hatchback available with two engine and transmission options: a 1.2-liter Kappa gasoline engine paired with a 5-speed manual or 4-speed automatic transmission, and a 1.0-liter Kappa II diesel engine paired with a 5-speed manual transmission. Praised for its stylish design, spacious interior, and fuel efficiency, it has also received some complaints regarding certain parts.

**Engine**

**1.2-liter Kappa Gasoline Engine (83 hp, 114 Nm torque)**

* **Faulty Ignition Coils:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Engine misfires, rough idle, decreased power.
    * Solution: Replace ignition coils.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300+
    * Recalls: 908943

**1.0-liter Kappa II Diesel Engine (75 hp, 190 Nm torque)**

* **Turbocharger Failures:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, excessive smoke, whistling noises.
    * Solution: Replace turbocharger.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
    * Recalls: 976516

**Transmission**

**5-Speed Manual Transmission**

* **Gear Shift Difficulties:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears, grinding noises.
    * Solution: Adjust or replace gearshift linkage.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+
    * Recalls: None Found

**4-Speed Automatic Transmission**

* **Transmission Slipping:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Engine revs up without corresponding increase in speed, loss of power.
    * Solution: Overhaul or replace transmission.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Front Suspension Noise:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Squeaking or knocking sounds from front suspension.
    * Solution: Replace or grease suspension components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Electrical Harness Issues:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Malfunctioning electrical components, intermittent power loss.
    * Solution: Repair or replace electrical harness.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Battery Discharge:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Battery drains quickly, even when vehicle is parked.
    * Solution: Diagnose and repair electrical system.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $200+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Headlight Condensation:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Moisture inside headlight housings.
    * Solution: Replace headlight housings or seal leaks.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Dashboard Rattle:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Noises from dashboard when driving over uneven surfaces.
    * Solution: Tighten or secure dashboard components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $150+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

While the 2017 Hyundai Grand i10 offers good overall value, it has experienced some specific issues in various components. Engine and transmission problems can be costly to repair, with potential expenses exceeding $1,000. Suspension, electrical, and interior issues are typically less severe and less expensive to fix. Potential buyers should consider these potential issues before purchasing this vehicle.
