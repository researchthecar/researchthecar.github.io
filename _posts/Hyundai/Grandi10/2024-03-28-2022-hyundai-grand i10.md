**Introduction:**

The 2022 Hyundai Grand i10 is a compact hatchback available with three engine options: a 1.2-liter Kappa engine producing 83 horsepower and 114 Nm of torque, a 1.0-liter T-GDi engine producing 100 horsepower and 172 Nm of torque, and a 1.2-liter Kappa engine producing 69 horsepower and 95 Nm of torque. Transmission options include a 5-speed manual, a 4-speed automatic, and a continuously variable transmission (CVT).

**Engine**

* **1.2-liter Kappa**
    * **Timing chain failure:** Symptoms include rattling noises from the engine, decreased engine performance, and potential engine damage. Solution: Replace the timing chain and related components. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
    * **Oil leaks:** Symptoms include visible oil leaks from the engine, low oil levels, and potential engine damage. Solution: Replace or repair the leaking component, such as gaskets or seals. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+
* **1.0-liter T-GDi**
    * **Turbocharger failure:** Symptoms include reduced engine power, whistling noises, and potential smoke from the engine. Solution: Replace the turbocharger. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
* **1.2-liter Kappa (69 horsepower)**
    * **No Issues Found**

**Transmission**

* **5-speed Manual**
    * **Gear grinding:** Symptoms include difficulty shifting gears, grinding noises, and potential transmission damage. Solution: Replace the worn gear or components. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
* **4-speed Automatic**
    * **Transmission slippage:** Symptoms include hesitation or slipping when shifting gears, difficulty accelerating, and potential transmission damage. Solution: Replace or repair the transmission. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $3,000+
* **CVT**
    * **CVT shudder:** Symptoms include vibration or shaking when accelerating, especially at low speeds. Solution: Flush the transmission fluid or replace the CVT belt. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+

**Suspension**

* **Worn struts:** Symptoms include rough ride, reduced handling, and potential tire damage. Solution: Replace the struts. Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800+

**Electrical**

* **Electrical shorts:** Symptoms can vary depending on the location of the short, such as flickering lights, battery drain, or engine problems. Solution: Repair or replace the affected wiring harness. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+
* **Battery failure:** Symptoms include difficulty starting the vehicle, weak lighting, and potential electrical system damage. Solution: Replace the battery. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+

**Exterior**

* **Door handle problems:** Symptoms include loose door handles, difficulty opening doors, or broken door handles. Solution: Replace or repair the door handles. Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $500+

**Interior**

* **Sun visor problems:** Symptoms include loose sun visors, broken hinges, or inability to stay in place. Solution: Replace or repair the sun visors. Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $200+
* **Dashboard rattling:** Symptoms include rattling or vibrating noises from the dashboard, especially on rough roads. Solution: Tighten or replace the loose components. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+

**Summary:**

Overall, the 2022 Hyundai Grand i10 has a range of issues, with the engine and transmission categories having the most significant concerns. Timing chain failures and turbocharger problems can be costly to repair, while transmission issues can also lead to expensive repairs. Electrical shorts and exterior problems are also common. However, the severity and frequency of these issues may vary depending on driving conditions and maintenance practices.
