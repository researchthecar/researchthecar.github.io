The 2015 Hyundai Grand i10 is a subcompact hatchback known for its fuel efficiency, nimble handling, and relatively spacious interior for its class. Engine options typically include a 1.0-liter inline-3 petrol engine (approximately 66 horsepower and 70 lb-ft of torque) and a 1.2-liter inline-4 petrol engine (approximately 82 horsepower and 84 lb-ft of torque), depending on the market. Transmission options are generally a 5-speed manual or a 4-speed automatic. Owners appreciate the Grand i10 for its ease of parking, affordability, and decent feature set for its price point. Common complaints include the relatively low power output, particularly noticeable when fully loaded or on inclines, and some concerns about long-term durability of certain components.

**Engine**

**1.2-liter Kappa Petrol Engine**

*   **Engine Oil Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks may occur from the valve cover gasket, oil pan gasket, or crankshaft seal. Symptoms include visible oil drips under the car, a burning oil smell, and low oil levels.
    *   Solution: Replacing the leaking gasket or seal.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Engine Knocking**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Engine may experience knocking sounds during acceleration or when under load, especially at higher mileage. This could be due to carbon buildup, improper fuel octane, or worn engine components.
    *   Solution: Carbon cleaning of the engine, use of higher octane fuel, or more extensive engine repair.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**1.0-liter Petrol Engine**
*   No Issues Found

**Transmission**

**5-Speed Manual Transmission**

*   **Difficulty Shifting**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Difficulty engaging certain gears, particularly first or reverse. Grinding noises during gear changes.
    *   Solution: Adjustment or replacement of the clutch cable, or clutch kit replacement if the clutch disc is worn.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $600+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**4-Speed Automatic Transmission**

*   **Harsh Shifting**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rough or jerky shifts between gears. Transmission slipping or hesitating during acceleration.
    *   Solution: Transmission fluid flush and filter replacement. If the problem persists, valve body repair or replacement may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Worn Suspension Bushings**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Clunking or rattling noises from the front or rear suspension, especially when driving over bumps. Reduced handling precision and stability.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the worn bushings (e.g., control arm bushings, sway bar bushings).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $400+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Strut Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Bouncy ride, excessive body roll during cornering, and oil leaks from the struts.
    *   Solution: Strut replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Battery Drain**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The car's battery drains quickly, especially when the vehicle is not driven for extended periods. Can be due to a parasitic draw from an electrical component.
    *   Solution: Diagnosing and repairing the source of the parasitic draw (e.g., faulty sensor, aftermarket accessory). Battery replacement if it is damaged or worn out.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $200+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Sensor Failure (Oxygen Sensor, Crankshaft Position Sensor)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Check engine light illumination. Rough engine idling, poor fuel economy, or difficulty starting.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the faulty sensor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Headlight Bulb Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Headlight bulb is no longer illuminated.
    *   Solution: Bulb replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $10 - $30+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Door Handle Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Exterior door handle breaks or becomes difficult to use.
    *   Solution: Door handle replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $200+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Air Conditioning Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Air conditioning system blows warm air or no air at all. Could be due to refrigerant leaks, compressor failure, or issues with the blower motor.
    *   Solution: Refrigerant recharge, compressor replacement, or blower motor repair.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $80 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Power Window Malfunction**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Power windows operate slowly or fail to operate entirely.
    *   Solution: Window motor replacement, switch replacement, or regulator repair.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $400+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2015 Hyundai Grand i10 generally faces minor to moderate issues. The most significant potential expenses relate to engine oil leaks, transmission problems (particularly harsh shifting in the automatic), and air conditioning system malfunctions. Suspension bushing wear is common but relatively inexpensive to address. Electrical problems, such as sensor failures and battery drains, can also occur. Most issues are not extremely severe but should be addressed to prevent further damage. Overall, pre-purchase inspections focusing on these areas are advisable.
