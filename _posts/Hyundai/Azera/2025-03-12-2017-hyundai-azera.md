The 2017 Hyundai Azera is a full-size sedan known for its comfortable ride, spacious interior, and long list of standard features. It offers a smooth and quiet driving experience, making it a good choice for those prioritizing comfort over sporty handling. A common complaint is its somewhat uninspired styling. The 2017 Azera comes equipped with a single engine option, a 3.3-liter V6 engine that produces 293 horsepower and 255 lb-ft of torque. It is paired with a 6-speed automatic transmission. People appreciate the Azera's value for money, given its upscale features and affordable price point. Some dislikes include the aforementioned styling and the lack of a more fuel-efficient engine option.

**Engine**

**3.3L V6**

*   **Engine Oil Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks from various engine seals and gaskets, most commonly the valve cover gaskets and the oil pan gasket. Symptoms include visible oil leaks under the car, a burning oil smell, and low oil levels.
    *   Solution: Replace the leaking gasket or seal.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Excessive Engine Knocking/Ticking Noise**
    *   Description/Symptoms: A noticeable knocking or ticking noise emanating from the engine, often more pronounced when the engine is cold or under load. Could indicate worn rod bearings or lifters.
    *   Solution: Diagnose the source of the noise (rod bearing, lifter, etc.) and replace the faulty components. An engine flush may solve the issue if it is oil starvation related.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $2,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**6-Speed Automatic**

*   **Harsh Shifting/Slipping**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The transmission hesitates or slips between gears, shifts roughly, or fails to engage gears properly.
    *   Solution: Perform a transmission fluid flush and change the filter. If symptoms persist, further inspection and repair may be required including valve body replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $3,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **No Issues Found**

**Electrical**

*   **Battery Drain**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The battery drains overnight or within a short period, making it difficult to start the car.
    *   Solution: Identify the parasitic draw by testing the current draw. Common culprits include aftermarket accessories or a faulty module.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Infotainment System Freezing/Crashing**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The infotainment system freezes, becomes unresponsive, or crashes frequently.
    *   Solution: Update the infotainment system software. If problems persist, the head unit may need to be replaced.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 - $1,200+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **No Issues Found**

**Interior**

*   **Sunroof Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Water leaks into the cabin from the sunroof, especially during heavy rain.
    *   Solution: Clean the sunroof drain tubes and check the sunroof seal. If the seal is damaged, it will need to be replaced.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $400+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2017 Hyundai Azera, while generally reliable, exhibits some common issues. Engine oil leaks and transmission issues are relatively frequent and can be costly to repair. Electrical issues with the infotainment system and battery drain also occur. Sunroof leaks can be an annoyance. Potential buyers should inspect these areas carefully before purchasing a used Azera. While not all vehicles will experience these issues, awareness of these potential problems can help inform buying decisions and proactive maintenance. The severity and cost of the issues are moderate overall.
