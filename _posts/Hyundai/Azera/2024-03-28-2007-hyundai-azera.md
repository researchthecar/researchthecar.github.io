**Introduction to the 2007 Hyundai Azera**

The 2007 Hyundai Azera is a midsize sedan available in four different trim levels: GLS, SE, Limited, and Ultimate. It offers a luxurious interior, powerful engine options, and a comfortable ride.

Powering the Azera is a choice of two engines: a 3.3-liter V6 producing 234 horsepower and 226 lb-ft of torque, or a 3.8-liter V6 that delivers 263 horsepower and 257 lb-ft of torque. Both engines are paired with a five-speed automatic transmission.

**Engine**

**3.3-liter V6**

* **Intake manifold gasket failure:** Symptoms include coolant or oil leaks, overheating, and reduced engine performance. Solution: Replace the intake manifold gasket. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800+
* **Camshaft position sensor failure:** Symptoms include rough idling, stalling, and difficulty starting. Solution: Replace the camshaft position sensor. Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300+
* **Knock sensor failure:** Symptoms include a knocking sound from the engine, reduced engine performance, and increased fuel consumption. Solution: Replace the knock sensor. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
* **Oxygen sensor failure:** Symptoms include increased fuel consumption, reduced engine performance, and an illuminated check engine light. Solution: Replace the oxygen sensor. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+

**3.8-liter V6**

* **Valve cover gasket failure:** Symptoms include oil leaks from the valve cover. Solution: Replace the valve cover gasket. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
* **Timing chain tensioner failure:** Symptoms include a rattling or ticking noise from the engine, reduced engine performance, and an illuminated check engine light. Solution: Replace the timing chain tensioner. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1000+
* **Water pump failure:** Symptoms include coolant leaks, overheating, and reduced engine performance. Solution: Replace the water pump. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
* **Crankshaft position sensor failure:** Symptoms include difficulty starting, stalling, and an illuminated check engine light. Solution: Replace the crankshaft position sensor. Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300+

**Transmission**

* **Transmission solenoid failure:** Symptoms include erratic shifting, slipping gears, or a complete lack of engagement. Solution: Replace the faulty transmission solenoid. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+
* **Valve body failure:** Symptoms include delayed or harsh shifting, gear slippage, or a complete loss of power. Solution: Replace the valve body. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1000+
* **Torque converter failure:** Symptoms include shuddering or vibration during acceleration, slipping, or a complete lack of engagement. Solution: Replace the torque converter. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1000 - $2000+

**Suspension**

* **Front strut failure:** Symptoms include excessive bouncing or vibration, uneven tire wear, and decreased ride stability. Solution: Replace the front struts. Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800+
* **Rear shock absorber failure:** Symptoms include excessive bouncing or vibration, uneven tire wear, and decreased ride stability. Solution: Replace the rear shock absorbers. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
* **Control arm bushing failure:** Symptoms include clunking or rattling noises from the suspension, uneven tire wear, and decreased ride stability. Solution: Replace the control arm bushings. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+

**Electrical**

* **Battery failure:** Symptoms include difficulty starting the vehicle, dim headlights, or a dead battery. Solution: Replace the battery. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
* **Starter failure:** Symptoms include a clicking sound when attempting to start the vehicle, no response from the starter, or a prolonged cranking time. Solution: Replace the starter. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
* **Alternator failure:** Symptoms include dim headlights, a dead battery, or an illuminated check battery light. Solution: Replace the alternator. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800+

**Exterior**

* **Headlight condensation:** Symptoms include condensation or water droplets inside the headlight assembly. Solution: Seal the headlight assembly or replace the headlight assembly. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
* **Tail light bulb failure:** Symptoms include a burned-out tail light bulb. Solution: Replace the tail light bulb. Estimated Cost to Fix: $10 - $50+
* **Door lock actuator failure:** Symptoms include difficulty locking or unlocking the doors, or the door locks not functioning at all. Solution: Replace the door lock actuator. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+

**Interior**

* **Power window switch failure:** Symptoms include difficulty operating the power windows or a complete lack of response. Solution: Replace the power window switch. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+
* **Climate control panel failure:** Symptoms include difficulty adjusting the climate control settings, a lack of response from the climate control panel, or an illuminated check climate control light. Solution: Replace the climate control panel. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
* **Center console lid failure:** Symptoms include a lid that does not open or close properly, or a broken lid. Solution: Replace the center console lid. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+

**Summary**

Overall, the 2007 Hyundai Azera is a reliable and well-rounded midsize sedan. However, it is not without its issues. The most common problems reported by owners include engine gasket failures, transmission solenoid failures, suspension issues, electrical problems, and minor interior issues. The severity of these issues varies, and the cost of repairs can range from a few hundred dollars to over a thousand dollars. It is important to note that not all Azera models experience these issues, and the likelihood of encountering problems can vary depending on factors such as maintenance history and driving habits.
