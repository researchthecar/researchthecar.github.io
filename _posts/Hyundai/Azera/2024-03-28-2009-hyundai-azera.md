**Introduction:**

The 2009 Hyundai Azera is a mid-size sedan powered by a 3.3-liter V6 engine producing 263 horsepower and 229 lb-ft of torque. It is available with a 5-speed automatic transmission. The Azera has a comfortable interior, a spacious rear seat, and a responsive steering system. However, it may not be the most exciting car to drive, and some people find its fuel economy to be lacking.

**Engine**

* **3.3L V6**
    * **Valve Body Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: The valve body, which controls the flow of transmission fluid, can fail, causing the transmission to slip or shift erratically.
        * Solution: Replace the valve body.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

* **5-speed Automatic**
    * **Shift Delay**
        * Description/Symptoms: The transmission may hesitate or delay when shifting gears.
        * Solution: Update the transmission control software or replace the transmission solenoid.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Rear Control Arm Bushing Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: The bushings that connect the control arms to the rear suspension can wear out, causing noise, vibration, and poor handling.
        * Solution: Replace the control arm bushings.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Alternator Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: The alternator, which generates electricity for the vehicle, can fail, causing the battery to discharge and the vehicle to stall.
        * Solution: Replace the alternator.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
        * Recalls: 10V-362

**Exterior**

* **Headlight Condensation**
        * Description/Symptoms: Moisture can accumulate inside the headlight assemblies, causing fogging and reduced visibility.
        * Solution: Replace the headlight assemblies or seal the housing.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Door Panel Rattle**
        * Description/Symptoms: The door panels may rattle or vibrate when driving over rough roads.
        * Solution: Tighten the door panel screws or apply soundproofing material.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

The 2009 Hyundai Azera has a few common issues, including valve body failure, shift delay, and rear control arm bushing failure. These issues can range in severity and cost from minor to major. However, overall, the Azera is a reliable and comfortable vehicle.
