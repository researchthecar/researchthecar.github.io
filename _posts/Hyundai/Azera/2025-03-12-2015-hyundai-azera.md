The 2015 Hyundai Azera is a full-size sedan known for its comfortable ride, spacious interior, and value for the money. It came standard with a 3.3-liter V6 engine producing 293 horsepower and 255 lb-ft of torque, paired with a 6-speed automatic transmission. People generally like the Azera for its smooth ride, upscale features, and quiet cabin. Common dislikes include its somewhat bland styling and the lack of a sportier driving experience.

**Engine**

**3.3L V6**

*   **Engine Stalling/Failure to Start**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The engine may stall while driving or fail to start. This issue has been attributed to a faulty crankshaft position sensor (CKPS). The check engine light may illuminate with a corresponding diagnostic trouble code (DTC).
    *   Solution: Replacing the crankshaft position sensor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Oil Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks may develop from various locations, including the valve cover gaskets or the oil pan gasket. Burning oil smell may be present.
    *   Solution: Replacing the leaking gasket or seal.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+, depending on the location of the leak.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**6-Speed Automatic**

*   **Rough Shifting**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported rough or harsh shifting between gears, particularly during acceleration or deceleration.
    *   Solution: A software update to the transmission control module (TCM) may resolve the issue. In some cases, a transmission flush or replacement of faulty solenoids may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 (software update) - $300+ (solenoid replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Rear Suspension Noise**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Reports of clunking or rattling noises coming from the rear suspension, especially when driving over bumps. This has been attributed to worn or loose rear suspension components.
    *   Solution: Inspect and replace worn suspension components, such as sway bar links, bushings, or shock absorbers.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $400+, depending on the components needing replacement.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Battery Drain Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Premature battery drain, leading to difficulty starting the vehicle. This can be caused by a parasitic draw from a faulty electrical component or a failing battery.
    *   Solution: Diagnose and repair the source of the parasitic draw. Replace the battery if it is faulty.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+, depending on the source of the drain.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Navigation/Infotainment System Malfunctions**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Navigation system freezing, unresponsive touch screen, or other infotainment system glitches.
    *   Solution: Software update or replacement of the infotainment unit.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $800
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Power Seat Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Power seats failing to move or adjust properly, intermittent operation. This could be a faulty switch or seat motor.
    *   Solution: Replace the faulty seat switch or motor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $400
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Sunroof Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Sunroof malfunctioning, including failing to open or close properly, or getting stuck.
    *   Solution: Cleaning and lubricating the sunroof tracks, or replacing the sunroof motor or regulator.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2015 Hyundai Azera, while generally reliable, has experienced some issues related to its engine, transmission, suspension, electrical system, interior, and exterior. The most frequently reported issues include engine stalling due to a faulty crankshaft position sensor, rough transmission shifting, and rear suspension noises. The severity of these issues ranges from minor inconveniences to more significant problems requiring repair or component replacement. Repair costs can vary widely, with some fixes being relatively inexpensive, while others can be more costly.
