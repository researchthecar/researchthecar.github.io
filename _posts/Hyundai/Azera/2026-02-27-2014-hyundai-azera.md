# 2014 Hyundai Azera

### Overview
The 2014 Hyundai Azera is a full-size sedan that generally offers a comfortable ride and a good range of features for its class. As with any vehicle, there are specific areas where owners have reported issues. This analysis focuses on common technical reliability concerns beyond routine maintenance. The Azera for this model year primarily came with a single engine and transmission configuration, simplifying some aspects of reliability assessment. However, attention to specific components within these systems, as well as other vehicle systems like electrical and interior, is important for understanding potential long-term ownership costs and experiences.

## Electrical

### Infotainment System
**Screen Freezing/Unresponsiveness**
- **Symptoms:** The infotainment screen may freeze, become unresponsive to touch, or reboot unexpectedly.
- **Solution:** Software updates or replacement of the infotainment unit.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1500


### Battery Drain
**Intermittent Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** The battery may drain prematurely, leading to starting issues. This can sometimes be related to parasitic draw from certain modules.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis to identify the source of the drain, which may involve checking modules, wiring, or replacing the battery.
- **Cost:** $150 - $500


## Exterior

### Headlights
**Condensation in Headlight Housings**
- **Symptoms:** Moisture or condensation can form inside the headlight assemblies, potentially affecting light output and longevity.
- **Solution:** Sealing the housings or replacing the headlight assembly.
- **Cost:** $300 - $800 per assembly


## Interior

### Seat Heaters
**Malfunctioning Seat Heaters**
- **Symptoms:** The heating elements in the seats may stop working or work intermittently.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the heating element or seat control module.
- **Cost:** $300 - $700 per seat


### Sunroof
**Sunroof Rattle or Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Some owners report rattles from the sunroof mechanism or occasional leaks, particularly after heavy rain.
- **Solution:** Adjustment of the sunroof, cleaning of drainage channels, or repair/replacement of seals.
- **Cost:** $100 - $400

### Summary Verdict
The 2014 Hyundai Azera demonstrates good overall mechanical reliability, with its primary engine and transmission configurations showing no widespread significant issues. However, potential owners should be aware of some common electrical gremlins, such as infotainment system glitches and intermittent battery drain, which can be costly to repair. Exterior issues like headlight condensation and interior concerns such as malfunctioning seat heaters and sunroof rattles are also noted. While these are not critical mechanical failures, they can impact the ownership experience and add to maintenance costs. Regular inspections and addressing these specific components proactively can help maintain the Azera's comfort and functionality.
