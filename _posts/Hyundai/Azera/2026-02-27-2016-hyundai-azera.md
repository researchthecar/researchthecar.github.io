# 2016 Hyundai Azera

### Overview
The 2016 Hyundai Azera is a full-size sedan known for its comfortable ride, spacious interior, and upscale features. Reliability for this model year is generally considered good, with owners reporting fewer significant mechanical issues compared to some competitors. The Azera typically comes equipped with a robust V6 engine and a smooth automatic transmission, contributing to a positive ownership experience. While most owners are satisfied, like any vehicle, there are a few areas that have seen occasional reports of concern, primarily related to minor electrical glitches and some interior trim durability. However, major powertrain failures are uncommon, making it a solid choice for those seeking a dependable large sedan.

## Electrical

### Infotainment System
**Infotainment Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Occasional freezing, unresponsiveness, or Bluetooth connectivity issues.
- **Solution:** Software updates or system reset. In some cases, replacement of the head unit may be required.
- **Cost:** $100 - $1500


### Keyless Entry/Start System
**Key Fob Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Intermittent failure to recognize key fob, leading to difficulty starting the vehicle.
- **Solution:** Battery replacement in the key fob or reprogramming of the system.
- **Cost:** $50 - $200

### Summary Verdict
The 2016 Hyundai Azera presents itself as a reliable option in the large sedan segment. Its core mechanical components, the 3.3L V6 engine and 6-speed automatic transmission, are robust and rarely present significant issues. The suspension system also holds up well. The most commonly cited areas for concern are within the electrical system, specifically minor glitches with the infotainment unit and occasional key fob recognition problems. Interior trim can also show signs of wear over time. Overall, the Azera offers a dependable ownership experience with a low probability of major mechanical failures, making it a sensible choice for buyers prioritizing comfort and value.
