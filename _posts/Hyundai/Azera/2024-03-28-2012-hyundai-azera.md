**Introduction:**

The 2012 Hyundai Azera is a midsize sedan known for its spacious cabin, upscale amenities, and impressive fuel efficiency. It comes with a 3.3-liter V6 engine paired with a 6-speed automatic transmission, delivering 293 horsepower and 255 lb-ft of torque. The Azera also offers a solid driving experience with responsive handling and a quiet ride. However, like any vehicle, the Azera has its share of issues that owners have reported.

**Engine**

**3.3L V6**

**Valve Cover Gasket Leak**
Description/Symptoms: Oil leaking from the top of the engine, visible on the valve covers.
Solution: Replace the valve cover gaskets.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $250 - $400
Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**6-Speed Automatic**

**Slipping**
Description/Symptoms: Transmission struggles to engage gears or shifts erratically.
Solution: Overhaul or replace the transmission.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000
Recalls: 13V294000

**Suspension**

**Strut Mount Bearing Failure**
Description/Symptoms: Clunking or popping noise when turning or going over bumps.
Solution: Replace the strut mount bearings.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200
Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

**Battery Drain**
Description/Symptoms: Vehicle battery drains quickly when parked.
Solution: Diagnose and repair or replace the faulty electrical component causing the drain.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $600
Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

**Sunroof Water Leak**
Description/Symptoms: Water leaks into the cabin through the sunroof.
Solution: Inspect and repair or replace the sunroof seals or drain tubes.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

**Rear Seat HVAC Failure**
Description/Symptoms: Rear seat passengers experience insufficient heating or cooling.
Solution: Diagnose and repair or replace the faulty HVAC components.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600
Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

The 2012 Hyundai Azera is a generally reliable vehicle, but it is not without its issues. The most common problems reported by owners include valve cover gasket leaks, transmission slipping, strut mount bearing failure, battery drain, and sunroof water leaks. The severity and cost of these issues vary from minor and inexpensive to major and costly. It is important to have these issues addressed promptly to prevent further damage and ensure the safety and reliability of the vehicle.
