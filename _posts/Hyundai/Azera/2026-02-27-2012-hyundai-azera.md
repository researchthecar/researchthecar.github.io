# 2012 Hyundai Azera

### Overview
The 2012 Hyundai Azera, a full-size sedan, generally presents a solid reliability record for its model year. It was designed to offer a comfortable and well-equipped driving experience, often competing with more premium offerings. While no vehicle is entirely without potential issues, the Azera typically avoids widespread, recurring problems that would significantly impact ownership. Owners generally report satisfaction with its performance and features, with most maintenance falling into the expected realm of routine upkeep. However, like any vehicle, specific components can occasionally experience failures, and understanding these potential areas can help prospective buyers and current owners.

### Summary Verdict
The 2012 Hyundai Azera is generally considered a reliable vehicle, with its powertrain (3.3L V6 engine and 6-speed automatic transmission) demonstrating good durability. Most reported issues are minor and infrequent, with the electrical system, particularly the infotainment unit, being the most common area for concern, often manifesting as software glitches. Exterior and interior components, as well as the suspension, tend to hold up well. Routine maintenance is key to ensuring longevity, but major mechanical failures are not a widespread characteristic of this model. Overall, it offers a dependable ownership experience for its class.
