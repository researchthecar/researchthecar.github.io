The 2012 Hyundai Azera is a full-size sedan known for its comfortable ride, spacious interior, and generous standard features. It came with a single engine option: a 3.3-liter V6 engine producing 293 horsepower and 255 lb-ft of torque. This engine was paired with a 6-speed automatic transmission. Owners generally appreciate the Azera's value proposition, smooth ride, and upscale interior. However, some common complaints include electrical glitches, some drivetrain issues, and some suspension problems.

**Engine**

**3.3L V6**

*   **Engine Knocking/Ticking**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported a knocking or ticking noise coming from the engine, especially when cold. In some cases, it's described as a 'lifter tick'.
    *   Solution: In some cases, a revised oil control valve (OCV) can resolve the issue. In more severe cases, internal engine components like lifters or connecting rod bearings may need replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $3,000+ depending on the severity of the issue and whether it is internal
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Excessive Oil Consumption**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Owners have reported higher than normal oil consumption.
    *   Solution: Repair or replace the engine.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $3,000+ depending on the severity of the issue and whether it is internal
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**6-Speed Automatic**

*   **Harsh Shifting/Slipping**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have experienced harsh or erratic shifting, particularly between certain gears. Transmission slippage or hesitation during acceleration is another reported symptom.
    *   Solution: A software update to the Transmission Control Module (TCM) may resolve the issue. In other cases, valve body replacement or a complete transmission rebuild/replacement may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $4,000+ depending on repair needed.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Rear Suspension Noises**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Reports of clunking or creaking noises emanating from the rear suspension, especially when traversing bumps or uneven road surfaces.
    *   Solution: Inspection of rear suspension components, including shocks, struts, bushings, and sway bar links. Replacement of worn or damaged parts.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+ depending on repair needed.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Navigation/Infotainment System Malfunctions**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Issues with the navigation system freezing, crashing, or displaying incorrect information. Intermittent issues with Bluetooth connectivity and audio system functionality.
    *   Solution: Software update to the infotainment system. Replacement of the head unit if the problem persists.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $1000+ depending on repair needed.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Battery Drain Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Premature battery drain, resulting in difficulty starting the vehicle or requiring frequent jump starts.
    *   Solution: Diagnose and address parasitic drain sources (e.g., faulty relay, malfunctioning module). Ensure the battery is properly charged and in good condition.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $500+ depending on repair needed.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Sunroof Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Sunroof may not open or close properly. In some instances, the sunroof can become stuck.
    *   Solution: The sunroof motor, track, and switch should all be inspected and replaced if needed.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $700+ depending on repair needed.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Headlight Condensation**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Moisture accumulating inside the headlight housings, leading to reduced visibility and potential damage to the headlight components.
    *   Solution: Remove the headlight assembly and dry it out. Seal any cracks or openings in the housing to prevent moisture from re-entering. Headlight housing replacement might be needed in some cases.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $600+ depending on repair needed.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2012 Hyundai Azera presents a mix of potential issues, with electrical problems and suspension noises being the most frequently reported. Engine knocking and transmission shifting concerns also surface but are less common. Repair costs can vary significantly based on the nature and severity of the problem, ranging from relatively inexpensive fixes to more costly engine or transmission repairs. Overall, while the Azera offers a comfortable and well-equipped driving experience, potential buyers should be aware of these common issues and ensure a thorough inspection before purchase.
