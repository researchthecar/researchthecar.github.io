# 2017 Hyundai Azera

### Overview
The 2017 Hyundai Azera, a full-size sedan, was in its final model year, offering a blend of comfort, technology, and a refined driving experience. It was generally considered a reliable vehicle, particularly for its segment, with owners often praising its smooth ride, spacious interior, and comprehensive feature set. While no vehicle is entirely without potential issues, the Azera for this year did not suffer from widespread, recurring mechanical or electrical problems that would significantly detract from its overall dependability. Most reported concerns were minor and related to specific component failures rather than systemic design flaws. This data reflects common owner-reported issues and professional assessments for the 2017 model year.

### Summary Verdict
The 2017 Hyundai Azera is a solid choice for a reliable full-size sedan. Its powertrain (3.3L V6 and 6-speed automatic) demonstrates good durability with no significant widespread issues reported. The suspension and exterior components are also generally robust. The most common areas for concern, though not prevalent, involve the infotainment system experiencing occasional glitches and, less frequently, premature battery failure or HVAC blower motor issues. These are typically manageable repairs. Overall, the Azera offers a dependable ownership experience, especially when considering its comfortable ride and feature set.
