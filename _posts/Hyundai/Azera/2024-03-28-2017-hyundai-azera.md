**Introduction:**

The 2017 Hyundai Azera is a mid-size sedan offering a comfortable and spacious interior. It comes with a standard 3.3-liter V6 engine that produces 293 horsepower and 255 lb-ft of torque, paired with a 6-speed automatic transmission. Other engine options include a 2.4-liter 4-cylinder engine with 188 horsepower and a 2.0-liter turbocharged 4-cylinder engine with 248 horsepower. Transmission options for these engines include a 6-speed manual transmission, a 6-speed automatic transmission, and a 7-speed dual-clutch transmission.

Owners generally appreciate the Azera's interior space, comfortable ride, and value for money. However, some have expressed concerns about its uninspiring driving dynamics and poor fuel economy.

**Engine**

**3.3-liter V6**

* **Intake Valve Sticking:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Reduced engine power, rough idle, misfires
    * Solution: Replace intake valves and valve guides
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,000+
* **Timing Chain Tensioner Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Engine noise, reduced engine power, check engine light
    * Solution: Replace timing chain tensioner, timing chain, and guides
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $1,500
* **Piston Ring Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Excessive oil consumption, blue smoke from exhaust, loss of engine power
    * Solution: Replace piston rings and possibly pistons
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,500 - $3,000+

**2.4-liter 4-cylinder**

* **Fuel Injector Clogging:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Hard starting, rough idle, hesitation during acceleration
    * Solution: Replace fuel injectors
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+

**2.0-liter Turbocharged 4-cylinder**

* **Turbocharger Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, excessive oil consumption, check engine light
    * Solution: Replace turbocharger
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+

**Transmission**

**6-speed Automatic**

* **Torque Converter Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Slipping transmission, shuddering during acceleration, loss of forward gears
    * Solution: Replace torque converter
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,200 - $1,800+

**6-speed Manual**

* **Clutch Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears, slipping clutch, burning smell
    * Solution: Replace clutch
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+

**Suspension**

* **Front Strut Mount Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Clunking or rattling noise over bumps, uneven tire wear
    * Solution: Replace strut mounts
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800+

* **Rear Shock Absorber Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Poor ride quality, excessive body roll, bouncing over bumps
    * Solution: Replace shock absorbers
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $900+

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Battery goes dead after sitting for a period of time
    * Solution: Identify and repair/replace faulty components (e.g., faulty alternator, parasitic draw)
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the source of the issue

* **Electrical System Malfunctions:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Intermittent electrical problems (e.g., lights flickering, power windows failing)
    * Solution: Inspect electrical connections, identify and repair/replace faulty components
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the specific issue

**Exterior**

* **Rear Bumper Sagging:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Rear bumper hangs lower than the body, uneven gaps around the trunk
    * Solution: Adjust bumper brackets
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+

* **Headlight Condensation:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Water droplets or condensation inside the headlight assembly
    * Solution: Replace headlight assembly or repair seals
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+

**Interior**

* **Infotainment System Glitches:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Touchscreen freezes, navigation errors, audio issues
    * Solution: Update or reset software, replace head unit
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the specific issue

* **Climate Control Malfunctions:**
    * Description/Symptoms: AC or heater not working properly, uneven temperature distribution
    * Solution: Diagnose and repair or replace faulty components (e.g., compressor, blower motor)
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the specific issue

**Summary**

The 2017 Hyundai Azera is generally a reliable vehicle, but it has some potential issues that owners should be aware of. Engine problems, such as intake valve sticking and timing chain tensioner failure, can be costly to repair. Transmission issues, including torque converter failure and clutch failure, can also be expensive. However, most of the other issues are less severe and can be fixed for a reasonable cost.
