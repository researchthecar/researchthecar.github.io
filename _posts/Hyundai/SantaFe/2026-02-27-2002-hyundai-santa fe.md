# 2002 Hyundai Santa Fe

### Overview
The 2002 Hyundai Santa Fe, a compact SUV, offered a blend of practicality and value for its era. It was generally considered a reliable vehicle for its segment, particularly in its initial years. However, like many vehicles of this age, certain components are more prone to issues than others. Owners often report a good experience with the powertrain, but attention should be paid to potential issues with the transmission, particularly in higher mileage examples, and some electrical gremlins can surface. The suspension is generally robust, but age-related wear is common. Overall, the 2002 Santa Fe is a decent option if well-maintained, but potential buyers should be aware of common problem areas.

## Transmission

### 4-Speed Automatic
**Harsh Shifting / Slipping**
- **Symptoms:** Jerky gear changes, delayed engagement, or slipping between gears.
- **Solution:** Transmission fluid and filter change, solenoid replacement, or complete transmission rebuild.
- **Cost:** $300 - $1500+

**Transmission Cooler Line Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Fluid leaks, overheating transmission, potential for transmission damage.
- **Solution:** Replacement of transmission cooler lines.
- **Cost:** $200 - $500

## Suspension

### Standard Chassis
**Worn Ball Joints and Control Arm Bushings**
- **Symptoms:** Clunking noises over bumps, uneven tire wear, vague steering feel.
- **Solution:** Replacement of worn ball joints and control arm bushings.
- **Cost:** $400 - $800

**Leaking Struts/Shocks**
- **Symptoms:** Bouncy ride, reduced handling, visible fluid leaks on shock/strut bodies.
- **Solution:** Replacement of worn struts or shocks.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600


## Electrical

### Standard Electrical System
**Window Regulator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Power windows not operating, slow operation, or falling down inside the door.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the window regulator assembly.
- **Cost:** $200 - $400

**Instrument Cluster Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** Inaccurate gauge readings, flickering lights, or complete failure of the cluster.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of the instrument cluster.
- **Cost:** $300 - $700

**HVAC Blower Motor Resistor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** HVAC fan only works on high speed, or not at all.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the blower motor resistor.
- **Cost:** $100 - $250

### Summary Verdict
The 2002 Hyundai Santa Fe is a generally dependable compact SUV, especially considering its age and market position. The 2.4L and 2.7L engines are known for their robustness and typically do not present major issues. The primary areas of concern tend to be the 4-speed automatic transmission, which can develop shifting problems or cooler line leaks, and certain electrical components like window regulators and the instrument cluster. Suspension wear, particularly ball joints and bushings, is also common due to age. While not immune to problems, addressing these potential issues can lead to a reliable vehicle. Regular maintenance is key to longevity.
