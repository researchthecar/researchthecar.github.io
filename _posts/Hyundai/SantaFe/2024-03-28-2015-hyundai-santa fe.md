## 2015 Hyundai Santa Fe

The 2015 Hyundai Santa Fe is a midsize SUV that offers a comfortable and spacious interior, a strong engine lineup, and a variety of features. It is available with three engine options: a 2.4L inline-4, a 3.3L V6, and a 2.0L turbocharged inline-4. Transmission options include a 6-speed automatic, a 7-speed dual-clutch automatic, and an 8-speed automatic.

The Santa Fe has received praise for its comfortable ride, spacious interior, and strong engine performance. However, some owners have reported issues with the vehicle's engine, transmission, suspension, and electrical components.

### Engine

**2.4L Inline-4**

**Engine Oil Consumption**
Description/Symptoms: Excessive oil consumption, often requiring more than 1 quart of oil per 1,000 miles.
Solution: Replace piston rings.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
Recalls: None Found

**3.3L V6**

**Oil Pan Leak**
Description/Symptoms: Leak of engine oil from the oil pan gasket.
Solution: Replace oil pan gasket.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
Recalls: None Found

**2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4**

**Turbocharger Failure**
Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, excessive smoke from the exhaust, or a loud whistling noise from the turbocharger.
Solution: Replace turbocharger.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
Recalls: None Found

### Transmission

**6-Speed Automatic**

**Transmission Slipping**
Description/Symptoms: Transmission slipping out of gear, or difficulty shifting gears.
Solution: Replace transmission.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,500 - $5,000+
Recalls: None Found

**7-Speed Dual-Clutch Automatic**

**Clutch Pack Failure**
Description/Symptoms: Jerking or shuddering during gear changes, or complete loss of power.
Solution: Replace clutch pack.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
Recalls: 16V533000, 17V387000

**8-Speed Automatic**

**No Issues Found**

### Suspension

**Front Suspension Noise**
Description/Symptoms: Squeaking or rattling noise from the front suspension when driving over bumps.
Solution: Replace sway bar end links.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
Recalls: None Found

### Electrical

**Alternator Failure**
Description/Symptoms: Electrical system malfunctions, battery not charging, or warning lights illuminated on the dashboard.
Solution: Replace alternator.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
Recalls: None Found

### Exterior

**Tailgate Latch Failure**
Description/Symptoms: Difficulty opening or closing the tailgate, or tailgate may open unexpectedly.
Solution: Replace tailgate latch.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
Recalls: 17V459000

### Interior

**Climate Control Malfunction**
Description/Symptoms: Difficulty adjusting temperature or airflow from the climate control system, or system not working at all.
Solution: Replace climate control module.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
Recalls: 15V582000

**Summary**

The 2015 Hyundai Santa Fe has received mixed reviews from owners, with some reporting significant issues with the vehicle's engine, transmission, and electrical components. Engine oil consumption and turbocharger failure can be particularly costly to repair. Suspension and interior issues are less severe, but can still be annoying and inconvenient. Overall, it is important for potential buyers to be aware of the potential issues with this vehicle before making a purchase.
