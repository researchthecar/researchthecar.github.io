**Introduction**

The 2014 Hyundai Santa Fe is a midsize SUV that offers a comfortable interior, a spacious cargo area, and a variety of engine and transmission options. It comes standard with a 2.4-liter four-cylinder engine that produces 190 horsepower and 181 lb-ft of torque, paired with a six-speed automatic transmission. Other engine options include a 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine that produces 264 horsepower and 269 lb-ft of torque, mated to a six-speed automatic transmission, and a 3.3-liter V6 engine that produces 290 horsepower and 252 lb-ft of torque, paired with a six-speed automatic transmission. All-wheel drive is available on all models.

While the Santa Fe is generally a reliable vehicle, it has had a few minor issues reported by owners.

**Engine**

* **2.4-liter Four-Cylinder Engine:**
    * **Excessive Oil Consumption:** Some owners have reported excessive oil consumption, resulting in low oil levels and potential engine damage.
        * Solution: Check oil levels regularly and top up as needed. Some cases may require a new piston ring or engine replacement.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $50+ for oil top-up, $1,000 - $5,000+ for piston ring replacement or engine overhaul
        * Recalls: None Found
* **2.0-liter Turbocharged Four-Cylinder Engine:**
    * **Turbocharger Failure:** A few owners have experienced turbocharger failure, leading to reduced engine power and a whistling noise.
        * Solution: Replace the turbocharger.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
        * Recalls: None Found
* **3.3-liter V6 Engine:**
    * **No Issues Found**

**Transmission**

* **Six-Speed Automatic Transmission:**
    * **Transmission Shifting Issues:** Some owners have reported issues with the transmission shifting gears smoothly, resulting in hesitation or jerking.
        * Solution: Update transmission software or replace the transmission.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $2,000+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **No Issues Found**

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain:** A few owners have experienced battery drain, which may result in difficulty starting the vehicle.
    * Solution: Check for parasitic battery drain and replace any faulty components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $400+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Electrical Shorts:** Some owners have reported electrical shorts, leading to dashboard malfunctions or other electrical issues.
    * Solution: Diagnose and repair the electrical short.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Tailgate Hinge Failure:** Some owners have reported tailgate hinge failure, resulting in the tailgate not opening or closing properly.
    * Solution: Replace the tailgate hinge.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    * Recalls: 14V829000

**Interior**

* **Dashboard Squeaks and Rattles:** Some owners have experienced dashboard squeaks and rattles, particularly in hot or cold temperatures.
    * Solution: Locate and tighten the source of the noise or apply sound-deadening material.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $200+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

Overall, the 2014 Hyundai Santa Fe is a generally reliable vehicle. However, it has had a few minor issues reported by owners, including engine oil consumption, transmission shifting issues, electrical shorts, and exterior tailgate hinge failure. The severity and cost of these issues vary, but most are relatively inexpensive to fix.
