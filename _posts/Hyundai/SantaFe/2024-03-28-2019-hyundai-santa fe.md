**Introduction**
The 2019 Hyundai Santa Fe is a mid-size SUV that offers a comfortable and spacious interior, along with a variety of features and engine options. It is available with a choice of two gasoline engines: a 2.4-liter inline-four with 185 horsepower and 178 lb-ft of torque, or a 2.0-liter turbocharged inline-four with 235 horsepower and 260 lb-ft of torque. Both engines are paired with an eight-speed automatic transmission. The Santa Fe is known for its fuel efficiency, spacious interior, and long list of standard features. However, it has also been criticized for its lack of power and refinement.

**Engine**

**2.4-liter Inline-Four**

* **Knocking Noise**
    * Description/Symptoms: A knocking noise coming from the engine
    * Solution: Replace the engine block
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $3,000 - $5,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**2.0-liter Turbocharged Inline-Four**

* **Oil Leaks**
    * Description/Symptoms: Oil leaking from the engine
    * Solution: Replace the oil cooler
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**8-Speed Automatic**

* **Shifting Problems**
    * Description/Symptoms: Hesitation or difficulty shifting gears
    * Solution: Update the transmission software or replace the transmission
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Rear Suspension Sagging**
    * Description/Symptoms: The rear of the vehicle sagging
    * Solution: Replace the rear springs or shocks
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain**
    * Description/Symptoms: The battery draining quickly
    * Solution: Find and fix the source of the drain
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Cracked Windshield**
    * Description/Symptoms: A crack in the windshield
    * Solution: Replace the windshield
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Infotainment System Glitches**
    * Description/Symptoms: The infotainment system freezing or crashing
    * Solution: Update the infotainment software or replace the infotainment unit
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2019 Hyundai Santa Fe is a generally reliable vehicle, but it has been known to experience some issues with its engine, transmission, and suspension. The most severe issue is the knocking noise from the 2.4-liter engine, which can require a costly engine replacement. The cost to fix the issues with the Santa Fe can range from a few hundred dollars to several thousand dollars, depending on the severity of the issue.
