# 2016 Hyundai Santa Fe Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2016 Hyundai Santa Fe offers a comfortable ride, spacious cabin, and strong feature list that make it an appealing option on the used market. However, prospective buyers should approach with caution due to significant owner-reported powertrain and electrical concerns. Reviewing service history and verifying engine health are essential steps before purchasing.

---

## Engine

### 2.4L Theta II 4-Cylinder

The base 2.4-liter naturally aspirated four-cylinder engine produces around 185 horsepower, offering adequate daily efficiency for everyday commuting.

### 2.0L Turbocharged 4-Cylinder

The 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder pushes output to roughly 240 horsepower, providing livelier acceleration for highway passing and towing.

### 3.3L Lambda II V6

The 3.3-liter V6 engine delivers a smooth 290 horsepower and strong towing capability, but it is tied to several notable owner complaints regarding internal failures and coolant loss.

**Catastrophic Engine Failure and Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers report sudden loss of motive power, engine knocking noises, illumination of warning lights, and in severe cases, internal engine components such as connecting rods breaking through the engine block.
- **Solution:** Requires professional mechanical diagnosis, which frequently involves engine rebuilding or complete engine replacement.

**Coolant Leaks and Overheating**
- **Symptoms:** Unexplained coolant loss, engine overheating, and dashboard temperature warnings indicating potential head gasket or engine block compromise.
- **Solution:** Inspect cooling system components, hoses, and cylinder head gaskets; replace failed parts as necessary.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

The standard six-speed automatic transmission pairs with both front-wheel and all-wheel drive configurations, generally delivering smooth shifts though occasional shifting hesitation is reported.

**Gear Shifting Hesitation and Jerking**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle experiences sudden jerking, harsh downshifts, hesitation when accelerating from a stop, or getting stuck in a single gear.
- **Solution:** Check transmission fluid condition and software calibration; software updates or valve body service may be required.

## Exterior

### Sunroof and Glass

Exterior body components are generally durable, though panoramic sunroof breakage remains a notable hazard reported by some owners.

**Spontaneous Sunroof Shattering**
- **Symptoms:** Panoramic glass sunroof panels can shatter spontaneously while driving at highway speeds, producing a loud pop and showering the cabin with glass shards.
- **Solution:** Replace the damaged sunroof glass assembly and clear out glass debris from tracks.

## Safety

### Seat Belts

Safety component complaints primarily focus on seatbelt and seat anchorage positioning during collisions.

**Seat and Seat Belt Security**
- **Symptoms:** Reports indicate seat positioning failures or seatbelt mechanism issues during impacts or normal usage.
- **Solution:** Inspect seat anchorages and seatbelt retractors; replace damaged hardware.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V358000: Latches/Locks/Linkages:Hood:Latch](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Hyundai&model=Santa+Fe&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** If the hood is not securely closed or the primary latch is inadvertently released and the secondary latch is not engaged, the hood could unexpectedly open while driving, increasing the risk of a vehicle crash.
- **What to do:** Hyundai will notify owners, and dealers will replace the secondary latch cable, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V145000: Seat Belts:Front:Warning Light/Devices](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Hyundai&model=Santa+Fe&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of the Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 208, "Occupant Crash Protection." Without a warning to remind the front seat occupants that their seat belts are not buckled, they may forget to buckle their seat belt, increasing their risk of injury in the event of a crash.
- **What to do:** Hyundai will notify owners, and dealers will inspect and reroute the wires in the front seat belt buckle harnesses to their proper locations, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V056000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Antilock/Traction Control/Electronic Limited Slip:Control Unit/Module](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Hyundai&model=Santa+Fe&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** The Anti-Lock Brake System (ABS) module could malfunction and cause an electrical short, which could result in an engine compartment fire. An engine compartment fire while parked or driving can increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the ABS multi-fuse, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

While the 2016 Hyundai Santa Fe offers plenty of utility and comfort for family buyers, its reliability profile is marred by serious potential engine and electrical complaints. Buyers should prioritize models with comprehensive service records, perform a thorough pre-purchase inspection, and verify whether any engine warranty replacements or manufacturer updates have been completed.
