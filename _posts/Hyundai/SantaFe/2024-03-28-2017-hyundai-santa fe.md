**2017 Hyundai Santa Fe**

The 2017 Hyundai Santa Fe is a midsize SUV that offers a comfortable and spacious interior, along with a range of engine and transmission options. It is powered by a 2.4L four-cylinder engine with 185 horsepower and 178 lb-ft of torque, a 2.0L turbocharged four-cylinder engine with 240 horsepower and 260 lb-ft of torque, or a 3.3L V6 engine with 290 horsepower and 252 lb-ft of torque. Transmission options include a six-speed automatic, eight-speed automatic, and dual-clutch automatic.

Overall, the 2017 Hyundai Santa Fe is a reliable and well-equipped SUV. It offers a comfortable and spacious interior, along with a range of engine and transmission options. However, it has some issues that can be costly to fix.

**Engine**

**2.4L Four-Cylinder**
- **Piston ring failure:** The piston rings can fail, causing excessive oil consumption and engine damage. Symptoms include blue smoke from the exhaust, increased oil consumption, and a knocking noise from the engine. The solution is to replace the piston rings. Estimated cost to fix: $2,000 - $3,000+
- **Timing chain failure:** The timing chain can fail, causing the engine to lose timing and potentially catastrophic damage. Symptoms include a rattling noise from the engine, loss of power, and a check engine light. The solution is to replace the timing chain. Estimated cost to fix: $1,500 - $2,000+
- **Valve train noise:** The valve train can make excessive noise, such as ticking or clattering. Symptoms include a noisy engine, particularly at idle or low RPM. The solution is to replace the valve train components. Estimated cost to fix: $500 - $1,000+

**2.0L Turbocharged Four-Cylinder**
- **Turbocharger failure:** The turbocharger can fail, causing a loss of power and increased emissions. Symptoms include a lack of power, black smoke from the exhaust, and a check engine light. The solution is to replace the turbocharger. Estimated cost to fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
- **Fuel injector failure:** The fuel injectors can fail, causing the engine to run rough or stall. Symptoms include a rough idle, hesitation during acceleration, and a check engine light. The solution is to replace the fuel injectors. Estimated cost to fix: $500 - $1,000+
- **Knock sensor failure:** The knock sensor can fail, causing the engine to run poorly and potentially damage itself. Symptoms include a knocking noise from the engine, poor performance, and a check engine light. The solution is to replace the knock sensor. Estimated cost to fix: $200 - $500+

**3.3L V6**
- **Intake manifold failure:** The intake manifold can fail, causing a loss of power and increased emissions. Symptoms include a lack of power, hesitation during acceleration, and a check engine light. The solution is to replace the intake manifold. Estimated cost to fix: $1,000 - $1,500+
- **Water pump failure:** The water pump can fail, causing the engine to overheat. Symptoms include overheating, coolant leaks, and a squealing noise from the engine. The solution is to replace the water pump. Estimated cost to fix: $500 - $1,000+
- **Transmission mount failure:** The transmission mount can fail, causing vibrations and noise. Symptoms include vibrations from the transmission, a clunking noise when shifting gears, and a check engine light. The solution is to replace the transmission mount. Estimated cost to fix: $200 - $500+

**Transmission**

**Six-Speed Automatic**
- **Torque converter failure:** The torque converter can fail, causing shuddering, slipping, and loss of power. Symptoms include shuddering during acceleration, slipping gears, and a check engine light. The solution is to replace the torque converter. Estimated cost to fix: $1,000 - $1,500+
- **Valve body failure:** The valve body can fail, causing shifting problems and loss of power. Symptoms include delayed or harsh shifts, slipping gears, and a check engine light. The solution is to replace the valve body. Estimated cost to fix: $1,000 - $1,500+
- **Transmission control module failure:** The transmission control module (TCM) can fail, causing shifting problems and loss of power. Symptoms include delayed or harsh shifts, slipping gears, and a check engine light. The solution is to replace the TCM. Estimated cost to fix: $500 - $1,000+

**Eight-Speed Automatic**
- **No Issues Found**

**Dual-Clutch Automatic**
- **Clutch failure:** The clutch can fail, causing slipping and loss of power. Symptoms include slipping gears, a burning smell from the transmission, and a check engine light. The solution is to replace the clutch. Estimated cost to fix: $1,000 - $1,500+
- **Mechatronic unit failure:** The mechatronic unit can fail, causing shifting problems and loss of power. Symptoms include delayed or harsh shifts, slipping gears, and a check engine light. The solution is to replace the mechatronic unit. Estimated cost to fix: $1,000 - $1,500+

**Suspension**
- **Strut mount failure:** The strut mounts can fail, causing a clunking noise from the suspension. Symptoms include a clunking noise when hitting bumps or potholes, a rough ride, and excessive tire wear. The solution is to replace the strut mounts. Estimated cost to fix: $200 - $500+
- **Sway bar link failure:** The sway bar links can fail, causing a clunking noise from the suspension. Symptoms include a clunking noise when turning or hitting bumps, a rough ride, and excessive tire wear. The solution is to replace the sway bar links. Estimated cost to fix: $100 - $300+

**Electrical**
- **Battery drain:** The battery can drain excessively, leaving the vehicle unable to start. Symptoms include a dead battery, difficulty starting the vehicle, and electrical problems. The solution is to find the source of the drain and fix it. Estimated cost to fix: $0 - $500+
- **Alternator failure:** The alternator can fail, causing the battery to drain and the vehicle to lose power. Symptoms include a dead battery, difficulty starting the vehicle, and electrical problems. The solution is to replace the alternator. Estimated cost to fix: $500 - $1,000+
- **Starter failure:** The starter can fail, preventing the vehicle from starting. Symptoms include difficulty starting the vehicle, a clicking noise from the starter, and a check engine light. The solution is to replace the starter. Estimated cost to fix: $500 - $1,000+

**Exterior**
- **Headlight failure:** The headlights can fail, causing reduced visibility at night. Symptoms include dim or flickering headlights, a check engine light, and a warning message on the dashboard. The solution is to replace the headlight assemblies. Estimated cost to fix: $500 - $1,000+
- **Taillight failure:** The taillights can fail, causing reduced visibility at night and potential safety hazards. Symptoms include dim or flickering taillights, a check engine light, and a warning message on the dashboard. The solution is to replace the taillight assemblies. Estimated cost to fix: $200 - $500+

**Interior**
- **Infotainment system failure:** The infotainment system can fail, causing the loss of audio, navigation, and other features. Symptoms include a black screen, frozen or unresponsive system, and a loss of sound. The solution is to restart the infotainment system or replace it if necessary. Estimated cost to fix: $500 - $1,000+
- **Air conditioning failure:** The air conditioning system can fail, causing the vehicle to become hot and uncomfortable. Symptoms include a lack of cold air, a warm or humid interior, and a musty smell. The solution is to diagnose the problem and repair or replace the faulty component. Estimated cost to fix: $500 - $1,500+
- **Seat heater failure:** The seat heaters can fail, causing the seats to become cold and uncomfortable. Symptoms include a lack of heat from the seat heaters, a check engine light, and a warning message on the dashboard. The solution is to replace the seat heater elements. Estimated cost to fix: $200 - $500+

**Summary**

The 2017 Hyundai Santa Fe has a variety of issues that can be costly to fix. These issues include engine problems, transmission problems, suspension problems, electrical problems, exterior problems, and interior problems. Some of the most severe issues include engine failure, transmission failure, and suspension failure. These issues can be dangerous and can lead to accidents. It is important to be aware of these issues before purchasing a 2017 Hyundai Santa Fe and to have the vehicle inspected by a qualified mechanic before making a decision.
