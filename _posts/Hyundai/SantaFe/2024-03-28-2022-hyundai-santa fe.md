**2022 Hyundai Santa Fe**

The 2022 Hyundai Santa Fe is a midsize SUV that offers a comfortable and spacious interior, plenty of safety features, and a competitive price. It is available with two engine options: a 2.5L 4-cylinder that produces 191 horsepower and 181 lb-ft of torque, and a 2.5L turbocharged 4-cylinder that produces 277 horsepower and 311 lb-ft of torque. Both engines are paired with an 8-speed automatic transmission.

People like the Santa Fe for its roomy interior, comfortable seats, and user-friendly infotainment system. However, some drivers have reported issues with the vehicle's engine, transmission, suspension, and electrical systems.

**Engine**

* **2.5L 4-cylinder**
    * **Excessive oil consumption:** Some owners have reported that their Santa Fe's consume excessive amounts of oil, leading to low oil levels and potential engine damage.
        * Solution: Replace the piston rings or entire engine.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $6,000+
    * **Timing chain failure:** The timing chain may stretch or break, causing the engine to lose timing and potentially catastrophically fail.
        * Solution: Replace the timing chain and related components.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,500+
    * **Intake manifold leak:** An intake manifold leak can cause a loss of power, poor fuel economy, and rough idling.
        * Solution: Replace the intake manifold.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
* **2.5L Turbocharged 4-cylinder**
    * **Turbcharger failure:** The turbocharger may fail, leading to a loss of power and increased emissions.
        * Solution: Replace the turbocharger.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
    * **High-pressure fuel pump failure:** The high-pressure fuel pump may fail, causing the engine to stall or lose power.
        * Solution: Replace the high-pressure fuel pump.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $1,500+

**Transmission**

* **8-speed Automatic Transmission**
    * **Slipping or shuddering:** Some drivers have reported that their Santa Fe's transmission slips or shudders during gear changes.
        * Solution: Replace the transmission fluid or torque converter.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
    * **Delay in gear engagement:** The transmission may delay in engaging certain gears, causing hesitation or jerks.
        * Solution: Update the transmission control module software.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+

**Suspension**

* **Front suspension noise:** Some owners have reported a clunking or rattling noise coming from the front suspension when driving over bumps or uneven surfaces.
        * Solution: Replace the front strut mounts or sway bar links.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600+
* **Rear suspension sag:** The rear suspension may sag when carrying heavy loads, affecting handling and stability.
        * Solution: Replace the rear springs or shock absorbers.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+

**Electrical**

* **Battery drain:** Some owners have experienced premature battery drain, especially during cold weather.
        * Solution: Replace the battery or update the vehicle's software.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
* **Electrical glitches:** Some drivers have reported random electrical glitches, such as flickering lights or instrument panel failures.
        * Solution: Update the vehicle's software or replace the affected components.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the issue

**Exterior**

* **Sunroof leaks:** Some owners have reported that their Santa Fe's sunroof leaks during heavy rain or car washes.
        * Solution: Replace the sunroof seal or adjust the sunroof alignment.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+

**Interior**

* **Seatbelt chime malfunction:** Some drivers have reported that the seatbelt chime does not activate when a passenger is not wearing their seatbelt.
        * Solution: Replace the seatbelt buckle or seatbelt control module.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
* **Climate control issues:** Some owners have experienced problems with the climate control system, such as uneven temperature distribution or failure of the A/C compressor.
        * Solution: Replace the A/C compressor or update the climate control software.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+

**Summary**

Overall, the 2022 Hyundai Santa Fe is a reliable and well-equipped SUV, but it has experienced some issues related to its engine, transmission, suspension, electrical system, and interior. The severity and cost of these issues vary depending on the specific problem. Some of the issues can be resolved through software updates or minor repairs, while others may require more extensive and expensive fixes.
