**Introduction:**

The 2007 Hyundai Santa Fe is a mid-size SUV offered with two engine options: a 2.7L V6 and a 3.3L V6. Both engines are paired with a 4-speed automatic transmission. Known for its spacious cabin, comfortable ride, and impressive safety features, the Santa Fe has received praise for its practicality and value. However, some owners have reported issues with various components of the vehicle.

**Engine:**

**2.7L V6**

* **Timing Belt Failure:** The timing belt may fail prematurely, causing extensive engine damage.
  * Symptoms: Engine stalls, rough idling, loss of power, rattling noises
  * Solution: Replace the timing belt and components
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
* **Piston Ring Failure:** The piston rings may fail, leading to excessive oil consumption and engine damage.
  * Symptoms: Blue smoke from exhaust, low oil levels, engine knocking
  * Solution: Repair or replace the piston rings
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $5,000+
* **Camshaft Position Sensor Failure:** The camshaft position sensor may fail, causing engine misfires and decreased performance.
  * Symptoms: Engine misfiring, rough idling, reduced power
  * Solution: Replace the camshaft position sensor
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
* **Recalls:**
  * NHTSA Recall Number 07V172000: Timing belt failure

**3.3L V6**

* **Knock Sensor Failure:** The knock sensor may fail, causing the engine to run poorly and potentially damaging the engine.
  * Symptoms: Engine knocking, reduced performance
  * Solution: Replace the knock sensor
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
* **Valve Cover Gasket Leak:** The valve cover gasket may fail, leading to an oil leak.
  * Symptoms: Oil leak on the engine, engine noise
  * Solution: Replace the valve cover gasket
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600
* **Recalls:**
  * None Found

**Transmission:**

**4-Speed Automatic Transmission**

* **Transmission Slipping:** The transmission may slip gears, causing a lack of power and difficulty shifting.
  * Symptoms: Sluggish acceleration, slipping gears, gear hunting
  * Solution: Repair or replace the transmission
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+
* **Shift Solenoid Failure:** The shift solenoids may fail, leading to difficulty shifting gears or the transmission getting stuck in a particular gear.
  * Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears, transmission stuck in gear
  * Solution: Replace the shift solenoids
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $1,000
* **Torque Converter Failure:** The torque converter may fail, causing vibration, slipping, and loss of power.
  * Symptoms: Vibration, slipping, loss of power, overheating
  * Solution: Replace the torque converter
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
* **Recalls:**
  * None Found

**Suspension:**

* **Front Suspension Strut Leaking:** The front suspension struts may leak, causing a loss of ride quality and handling.
  * Symptoms: Sagging front end, reduced handling, noise from suspension
  * Solution: Replace the suspension struts
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $600 - $1,200
* **Rear Sway Bar Link Failure:** The rear sway bar link may fail, leading to a loss of stability and handling.
  * Symptoms: Knocking noises from the rear suspension, excessive body roll
  * Solution: Replace the sway bar link
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400

**Electrical:**

* **Battery Discharge:** The battery may discharge prematurely due to a parasitic draw or electrical component failure.
  * Symptoms: Battery dies prematurely, difficulty starting
  * Solution: Inspect the electrical system for a parasitic draw or faulty component
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies based on the component causing the issue
* **Electrical Shorts:** Electrical shorts may occur in various components, causing a variety of issues.
  * Symptoms: Blown fuses, erratic electrical behavior, component failure
  * Solution: Repair or replace the faulty component
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies based on the component that needs repair or replacement
* **Recalls:**
  * None Found

**Exterior:**

* **Sunroof Drain Plugs:** The sunroof drain plugs may become clogged, causing water to leak into the vehicle's interior.
  * Symptoms: Water leaking into the cabin, water damage
  * Solution: Clear the drain plugs
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: Minimal

**Interior:**

* **Headliner Sagging:** The headliner may sag over time.
  * Symptoms: Loose or sagging headliner
  * Solution: Repair or replace the headliner
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
* **HVAC System Failure:** The HVAC system may fail, resulting in a loss of heating or cooling.
  * Symptoms: No heating or cooling, air flow issues
  * Solution: Repair or replace the HVAC system components
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+
* **Recalls:**
  * None Found

**Summary:**

The 2007 Hyundai Santa Fe has been known to experience a range of issues affecting various components. The severity and cost of these issues vary depending on the specific problem. While some issues, such as electrical shorts and interior trim issues, may be relatively minor and inexpensive to repair, others, such as timing belt failure and transmission problems, can be more serious and costly. Overall, it is essential for owners to be aware of these potential issues and address them promptly to ensure the safety and reliability of their vehicles.
