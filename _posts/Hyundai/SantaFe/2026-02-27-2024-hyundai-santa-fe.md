# 2024 Hyundai Santa Fe Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2024 Hyundai Santa Fe arrives with a completely redesigned, bold boxy styling and a spacious three-row interior. As a relatively new model year, early owners have reported a variety of electronic, powertrain, and mechanical grievances. While many drivers enjoy its comfort and features, prospective buyers should carefully review service records and test drive thoroughly to ensure all updates and initial bugs have been addressed.

---

## Engine

### 2.5L Turbocharged Inline-4

The 2.5-liter turbocharged inline-4 engine produces approximately 277 horsepower and 311 lb-ft of torque, offering brisk acceleration for daily driving. It provides solid capability for families, though early production models feature occasional complaints regarding abrupt power loss.

**Loss of Motive Power**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers report sudden acceleration failure, dropping to low speeds like 25 mph, or entering limp mode while driving on highways.
- **Solution:** Have a certified technician diagnose potential engine management codes or throttle body software glitches; software updates or component replacements may be necessary.

### 1.6L Turbocharged Hybrid Inline-4

The 1.6-liter turbocharged hybrid powertrain pairs an electric motor with a gas engine to deliver an estimated total output of 226 horsepower, maximizing fuel efficiency for city and highway commutes. It is generally a smooth option, though electronic integration requires monitoring.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Dual-Clutch Automatic (Wet Type)

The 8-speed wet dual-clutch automatic transmission delivers quick, responsive shifts paired with the 2.5L turbo engine. While efficient, drivers have experienced occasional low-speed shuddering or shift hesitation.

**Transmission Shudder and Jerking**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle experiences unexpected jerking, shuddering, or clunking during acceleration or gear changes, sometimes accompanied by a flashing gear indicator.
- **Solution:** Visit a dealership to check for transmission control module (TCM) software updates or clutch adaptation resets.

### 6-Speed Automatic (Hybrid)

The conventional 6-speed automatic transmission found in hybrid models provides smooth gear transitions geared toward maximizing fuel economy. It generally avoids the dual-clutch low-speed complaints.

### Shifter and Park Mechanism

The electronic shifter and parking pawl mechanisms dictate how the vehicle secures itself when stationary. Unintended movement reports necessitate careful attention to park-lock recalls.

**Unexpected Roll-Away in Park**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may unexpectedly move or roll away after being shifted into the park position, potentially leading to stationary collisions.
- **Solution:** Ensure all relevant software recalls and transmission linkage inspections are promptly completed by an authorized dealer.

## Electrical

### Digital Display and Instrument Cluster

The cabin relies heavily on dual panoramic digital screens and advanced driver-assistance wiring. Complex electrical architecture can occasionally prompt glitchy behavior or temporary screen blackouts.

**Instrument Cluster Blackout**
- **Symptoms:** The main digital instrument panel and infotainment screens completely go black while driving, rebooting suddenly to display the startup logo.
- **Solution:** Consult a dealer for software flashes or infotainment system module inspections.

## Safety

### Seat Belts

Safety equipment includes comprehensive airbags and multi-row seatbelt configurations, though early owner feedback highlights occasional mechanical annoyances with seat belt buttons and anchors.

**Seat Belt Button or Buckle Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Occupants report difficulty unbuckling seat belts, loose anchors, or issues with middle-row safety restraints functioning correctly.
- **Solution:** Have the dealer inspect the seat belt assembly and replace faulty buckles or retractors under warranty.

## Summary Verdict

The 2024 Hyundai Santa Fe is a striking and versatile family SUV, but as a first-model-year vehicle, it has accumulated early owner complaints regarding powertrain hesitation, electronic screen blackouts, and transmission behavior. Used car buyers should verify that all factory recalls have been cleared and perform an extended test drive to ensure smooth operation before purchasing.
