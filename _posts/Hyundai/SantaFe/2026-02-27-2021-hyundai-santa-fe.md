# 2021 Hyundai Santa Fe Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2021 Hyundai Santa Fe is a popular midsize crossover that offers a comfortable ride, a well-appointed cabin, and generous tech features. However, prospective buyers should carefully review owner-reported reliability trends, particularly concerning powertrain and electrical systems. While many owners enjoy their daily commutes and road trips, a notable cluster of complaints highlights potential issues with engine stalling, transmission performance, and specific electrical components.

---

## Engine

### 2.5L 4-Cylinder (Naturally Aspirated)

The standard 2.5-liter naturally aspirated four-cylinder engine produces approximately 191 horsepower. It offers adequate power for daily commuting, though some drivers report unexpected stalling and drivability concerns.

**Engine Stalling and Loss of Motive Power**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle may unexpectedly lose power while driving, stall in traffic, or fail to restart smoothly.
- **Solution:** Diagnostic evaluation by a qualified technician to check fuel delivery, sensors, or software calibration.

### 2.5L 4-Cylinder Turbocharged

The available 2.5-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine generates around 281 horsepower, providing much punchier acceleration and stronger passing power for highway driving.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Dual-Clutch Automatic Transmission

Paired with select trims and engines, this 8-speed dual-clutch automatic transmission delivers quick shifts. However, buyers should test-drive thoroughly to check for smooth operation and verify that any prior transmission software campaigns or repairs were successfully completed.

**Transmission Shudder and Shifter Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle may experience transmission shudder, fail to engage gears properly, display shifter warning lights, or enter limp mode.
- **Solution:** Software updates, module inspection, or component replacement as indicated by diagnostic trouble codes.

## Electrical

### Backup Camera and Horn Assemblies

Electrical accessories such as the rearview camera and horn have generated recurring owner complaints regarding intermittent failures.

**Backup Camera Display Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Rearview camera displays a blank or black screen intermittently, reducing visibility when reversing.
- **Solution:** Inspect camera wiring harness or replace the camera unit as needed.

**Horn Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle horns stop working entirely without warning.
- **Solution:** Replace faulty horn assemblies.

## Exterior

### Sunroof

Exterior body and glass components are generally durable, though a small number of reports mention spontaneous sunroof glass breakage.

**Sunroof Shattering**
- **Symptoms:** Panoramic sunroof glass may fracture or shatter unexpectedly while driving at highway speeds.
- **Solution:** Inspect sunroof assembly and replace damaged glass panels.

## Safety

### Forward Collision and Seat Belts

Advanced safety features generally enhance protection, but owners have occasionally reported unexpected forward collision warning alerts or sudden braking interventions.

**Unexpected Emergency Braking**
- **Symptoms:** Automatic emergency braking system may activate unexpectedly without an immediate obstacle present.
- **Solution:** Have dealer recalibrate sensor systems and check for software updates.

**Seat Belt Retractor Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Seat belts may fail to retract smoothly or properly lock into place during use.
- **Solution:** Inspect seat belt mechanisms and replace faulty assemblies.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V524000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Hoses, Lines/Piping, And Fittings](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Hyundai&model=Santa+Fe&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** A fuel leak increases the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and tighten, or replace the fuel pipe as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V00M000: Visibility:Windshield](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Hyundai&model=Santa+Fe&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** During manufacturing, the windshield may not have been properly bonded to the vehicle, allowing it to detach in a crash. A windshield that detaches from a vehicle during a crash can increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will remove and reinstall the front windshield panel, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V185000: Air Bags: Air Bag/Restraint Control Module](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Hyundai&model=Santa+Fe&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The Occupant Detection System (ODS) module may not deactivate the air bag upon detection of a child restraint system in the front passenger seat. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 208, "Occupant Crash Protection." In the event of a crash, deployment of the front passenger air.
- **What to do:** Hyundai will notify owners, and dealers will replace the ODS module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V308000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Hoses, Lines/Piping, And Fittings](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Hyundai&model=Santa+Fe&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The fuel pipe connecting the high pressure fuel pump to the fuel rail may not have been tightened properly. An insufficiently tightened fuel pipe may leak fuel, increasing the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 22V746000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Hyundai&model=Santa+Fe&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** Loss of drive power increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the transmission, as necessary. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V181000: Trailer Hitches](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Hyundai&model=Santa+Fe&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** Water accumulation on the tow hitch harness module printed circuit board (PCB) may cause an electrical short, which can result in a fire. A fire while parked or driving can increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will install a new fuse and wire extension kit as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V879000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Hyundai&model=Santa+Fe&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 111, "Rear Visibility." A rearview image that does not display reduces the driver's visibility and increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the rearview camera, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

When shopping for a used 2021 Hyundai Santa Fe, perform a comprehensive inspection focusing on the powertrain, transmission shifting quality, and all electrical accessories. Ensure that any relevant dealer service campaigns and recalls have been properly addressed. A thorough test drive and a verified maintenance history will help ensure a more confident purchase.
