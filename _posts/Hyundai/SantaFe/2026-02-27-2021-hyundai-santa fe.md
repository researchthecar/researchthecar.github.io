# 2021 Hyundai Santa Fe

### Overview
The 2021 Hyundai Santa Fe generally presents a strong reliability profile, reflecting Hyundai's commitment to quality and durability. This mid-size SUV offers a comfortable ride, practical interior, and a range of powertrain options designed to balance performance and efficiency. While most owners report a positive ownership experience with minimal unexpected repairs, like any vehicle, there are specific areas where minor concerns have been noted. This analysis focuses on documented technical issues beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential areas of attention for owners and prospective buyers.

### 8-Speed Dual-Clutch (DCT) (for 2.5L Turbo)
**Hesitation or rough shifting**
- **Symptoms:** Occasional jerky acceleration, delayed engagement, or slight shuddering during gear changes, particularly at low speeds.
- **Solution:** Software update for transmission control module, or in some cases, transmission fluid flush and refill.
- **Cost:** $150 - $350

## Electrical

### Infotainment System
**Screen freezing or unresponsiveness**
- **Symptoms:** Infotainment display occasionally becomes unresponsive to touch inputs or freezes, requiring a system reset.
- **Solution:** Software update for the infotainment unit, or a hard reset of the system.
- **Cost:** $0 - $150 (if software update is needed and not covered under warranty)


### Battery
**Premature battery drain**
- **Symptoms:** Difficulty starting the vehicle after periods of inactivity, or dashboard warning lights related to battery voltage.
- **Solution:** Check for parasitic draw from accessories or faulty charging system components. Battery replacement may be necessary.
- **Cost:** $200 - $400

### Summary Verdict
The 2021 Hyundai Santa Fe demonstrates commendable reliability across most of its systems. The naturally aspirated and turbocharged 2.5L engines are robust, and the standard 8-speed automatic transmission is generally trouble-free. Owners of models equipped with the 8-speed dual-clutch transmission (DCT) might encounter occasional minor shifting anomalies, typically resolvable with software updates. Electrical gremlins, such as infotainment screen freezes or premature battery drain, are infrequent but have been noted. Overall, the Santa Fe is a dependable choice, with potential issues being minor and manageable, contributing to a positive ownership experience.
