# 2017 Hyundai Santa Fe Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2017 Hyundai Santa Fe, prospective buyers will find a spacious and feature-packed midsize SUV that offers strong family appeal. However, reviewing owner feedback reveals notable areas of concern, particularly regarding engine reliability, electrical issues, and transmission behavior. While many drivers enjoy its comfort and practicality, keeping a close eye on maintenance records and powertrain health is essential before purchasing.

---

## Engine

### 2.4L 4-Cylinder Engine

A naturally aspirated 2.4-liter four-cylinder engine producing around 185 horsepower, serving as the base power plant for standard trims. Routine maintenance and oil monitoring are recommended for long-term reliability.

### 3.3L V6 Engine

A robust 3.3-liter V6 engine pushing approximately 290 horsepower, providing confident acceleration for higher trims and towing duties. Buyers should monitor oil levels closely and check for past software update compliance.

**Engine Stalling and Loss of Power**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers report unexpected engine stalling while driving, often accompanied by warning lights and a sudden loss of motive power on the highway or city streets.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis typically involves checking powertrain control modules, fuel delivery systems, and updating engine software under applicable service campaigns.

**Excessive Oil Consumption**
- **Symptoms:** Owners note a rapid drop in oil levels between changes, requiring frequent quart top-offs to prevent internal engine damage.
- **Solution:** Perform regular oil checks and consult a qualified technician for oil consumption tests or potential piston ring evaluations.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

A conventional 6-speed automatic transmission paired with front-wheel or all-wheel drive configurations. While generally durable, software updates and fluid condition are vital to maintain smooth operation.

**Hard Shifting and Erratic Engagement**
- **Symptoms:** The transmission may experience severe harsh downshifting or surging when slowing down, particularly in lower gears, or harsh shifting during cold starts.
- **Solution:** Reprogramming or updating the transmission control module (TCM) or replacing worn valve body components can sometimes alleviate shift harshness.

## Steering

### Steering Column and Mechanism

The electronic power steering system provides adjustable effort modes but has exhibited reports of mechanical binding over time.

**Sticking or Binding Steering Wheel**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers notice a noticeable locking or sticking sensation in the steering wheel during turns, which may require forceful manipulation and cause sudden directional shifts.
- **Solution:** Inspection of the steering intermediate shaft and electronic power steering components is required; affected parts may need lubrication or replacement.

## Electrical

### Body Electrical and Accessories

Electrical system complaints highlight occasional issues with auxiliary features, door latches, and starting reliability.

**Random Starting and Power Latch Failures**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicles may experience intermittent no-start conditions requiring jumps, or power liftgates and door locks failing to operate correctly.
- **Solution:** Test battery health, charging systems, and wiring harnesses; faulty door actuators or liftgate struts may require replacement.

## Safety

### Airbags and Seat Belts

Safety equipment generally functions as intended, though occasional steering-wheel clockspring wear can disable supplemental restraint and horn functions.

**Airbag Warning Light and Clockspring Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The airbag warning light illuminates on the dashboard, often caused by an internal clockspring circuit fault that also affects the horn.
- **Solution:** Inspect the steering wheel clockspring assembly and replace the faulty component to restore proper airbag and horn connectivity.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V142000: Tires:Pressure Monitoring And Regulating Systems](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Hyundai&model=Santa+Fe&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** The affected vehicles have a tire pressure monitoring system (TPMS) that may not have been set in the correct mode during vehicle assembly, and therefore will not provide an appropriate warning in the event of an underinflated tire. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 138.
- **What to do:** Hyundai will notify owners, and dealers will replace the TPMS sensors, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V358000: Latches/Locks/Linkages:Hood:Latch](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Hyundai&model=Santa+Fe&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** If the hood is not securely closed or the primary latch is inadvertently released and the secondary latch is not engaged, the hood could unexpectedly open while driving, increasing the risk of a vehicle crash.
- **What to do:** Hyundai will notify owners, and dealers will replace the secondary latch cable, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V578000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Engine](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Hyundai&model=Santa+Fe&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** The engine bearing wear may cause the vehicle to stall, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Hyundai will notify all owners, and dealers will inspect and replace the engine, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V842000: Electrical System:Wiring](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Hyundai&model=Santa+Fe&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 108, " Lamps, Reflective Devices, and Associated Equipment." If the trailer brake lights stay illuminated while being used, other drivers may be confused, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Hyundai will notify owners, and dealers will replace the affected accessory trailer hitch wiring harnesses, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V145000: Seat Belts:Front:Warning Light/Devices](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Hyundai&model=Santa+Fe&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of the Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 208, "Occupant Crash Protection." Without a warning to remind the front seat occupants that their seat belts are not buckled, they may forget to buckle their seat belt, increasing their risk of injury in the event of a crash.
- **What to do:** Hyundai will notify owners, and dealers will inspect and reroute the wires in the front seat belt buckle harnesses to their proper locations, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V056000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Antilock/Traction Control/Electronic Limited Slip:Control Unit/Module](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Hyundai&model=Santa+Fe&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** The Anti-Lock Brake System (ABS) module could malfunction and cause an electrical short, which could result in an engine compartment fire. An engine compartment fire while parked or driving can increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the ABS multi-fuse, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2017 Hyundai Santa Fe is an appealing used midsize SUV that offers generous passenger space and strong V6 performance. However, prospective buyers should thoroughly inspect vehicle history reports, verify that all engine and transmission software updates have been completed, and check for steering smoothness and electrical stability. Conducting a comprehensive pre-purchase inspection by a qualified mechanic is strongly advised to catch any hidden powertrain or mechanical concerns.
