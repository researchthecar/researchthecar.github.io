# 2023 Hyundai Santa Fe Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2023 Hyundai Santa Fe is a popular midsize crossover known for its comfortable cabin, generous feature list, and versatile cargo room. However, prospective buyers should review owner-reported complaints regarding certain powertrain, electrical, and structural systems. While many drivers enjoy smooth daily commuting, mechanical and electronic glitches reported to safety authorities highlight areas that warrant careful pre-purchase inspection.

---

## Engine

### 2.5L Naturally Aspirated Inline-4

The base 2.5-liter four-cylinder engine produces 191 horsepower and 181 lb-ft of torque, providing adequate power for routine daily driving. It pairs with an 8-speed automatic transmission and is generally regarded as a straightforward, dependable powertrain option.

### 2.5L Turbocharged Inline-4

The turbocharged 2.5-liter engine generates a robust 281 horsepower and 311 lb-ft of torque, offering much quicker acceleration and confident passing power. It is paired with an 8-speed wet dual-clutch transmission.

**Loss of Motive Power and Limp Mode**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may suddenly enter limp mode, abruptly decelerating with a non-responsive gas pedal, while displaying sensor reference error codes.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified technician scan for diagnostic trouble codes and inspect electrical sensors or throttle body components.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

The traditional 8-speed automatic transmission mated to the base engine offers smooth gear changes and lacks the complexity of dual-clutch units, making it a solid choice for longevity.

### 8-Speed Wet Dual-Clutch Transmission (DCT)

Paired exclusively with the turbocharged engine, this 8-speed wet dual-clutch transmission delivers quick shifts for spirited driving, though some owners have noted lower-gear hesitation and software-related concerns.

**Transmission Hesitation and Gear Sticking**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may hesitate, skip lower gears, or become stuck in first gear while in Drive mode.
- **Solution:** Update transmission control software or consult a dealership for component diagnostics and potential clutch actuator service.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

The braking system provides adequate stopping power under normal conditions, but multiple owner reports highlight premature rotor warping and front-end shaking under frequent braking.

**Premature Rotor Warping and Front-End Vibration**
- **Symptoms:** Heavy steering wheel or front-end vibration and shaking when applying the brakes at highway speeds due to heat-induced rotor distortion.
- **Solution:** Inspect brake rotors for excessive runout, machine the rotors if within service limits, or replace warped rotors and pads with heavy-duty aftermarket alternatives.

## Exterior

### Panoramic Sunroof and Glass

Exterior styling and lighting are generally robust, but rare instances of spontaneous panoramic glass breakage require careful inspection.

**Spontaneous Glass Shattering**
- **Symptoms:** The panoramic sunroof or fixed roof glass panel suddenly shatters or explodes with a loud pop while driving.
- **Solution:** Clear broken glass debris, inspect the frame for structural stress, and replace the shattered glass panel under comprehensive insurance or warranty if applicable.

## Safety

### Horn Assembly

Electrical accessories generally function well, though moisture-related horn failures have been widely reported by owners.

**Inoperable Horn Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The horn fails to sound when pressed, posing a safety hazard during emergency traffic situations.
- **Solution:** Inspect the horn wiring and replace the horn assembly, often affected by moisture intrusion.

### Forward Collision-Avoidance

Advanced safety features enhance protection, but sensor sensitivity can occasionally cause phantom braking events in adverse weather or complex traffic.

**Unintended Emergency Braking**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle unexpectedly triggers collision warnings and applies sudden automated braking while driving normally or using cruise control.
- **Solution:** Keep radar and camera sensors clean from snow and debris, and have the dealer check for sensor recalibration updates.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V181000: Trailer Hitches](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Hyundai&model=Santa+Fe&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** Water accumulation on the tow hitch harness module printed circuit board (PCB) may cause an electrical short, which can result in a fire. A fire while parked or driving can increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will install a new fuse and wire extension kit as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2023 Hyundai Santa Fe remains a feature-packed and comfortable midsize SUV choice for families, but buyers should conduct a thorough inspection before purchasing. Pay close attention to front brake rotor condition, test the horn and advanced safety sensors, and verify smooth operation from the transmission—particularly on turbocharged models equipped with the dual-clutch transmission. A comprehensive pre-purchase inspection by an independent mechanic will help uncover any lingering electrical or mechanical issues.
