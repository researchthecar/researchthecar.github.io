# 2024 Hyundai Ioniq 6 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2024 Hyundai Ioniq 6 is a striking, aerodynamic electric sedan praised for its long range, rapid charging capabilities, and modern interior technology. While overall ownership satisfaction is high among early adopters, used-car buyers should pay close attention to potential electrical and charging system glitches. Reviewing service records for completed software updates and hardware campaigns is essential before finalizing a purchase.

---

## Engine

### Single-Motor Standard Range Electric Powertrain

Features a rear-mounted permanent magnet synchronous motor producing approximately 149 horsepower paired with a single-speed reduction gear. This base setup offers smooth acceleration and efficiency for daily commuting.

### Single-Motor Long Range Electric Powertrain

Utilizes a rear-mounted motor pushing about 225 horsepower paired with a single-speed reduction gear, delivering strong highway performance and impressive driving range. Reliability for this configuration is generally robust outside of known charging module concerns.

### Dual-Motor Long Range AWD Electric Powertrain

Combines dual electric motors to generate a combined 320 horsepower and 446 lb-ft of torque with all-wheel drive. It provides brisk acceleration and confident handling in inclement weather, though it shares the same foundational electrical architecture as lower trims.

## Transmission

### Single-Speed Reduction Gear Transmission

A direct-drive single-speed automatic transmission that delivers seamless power delivery without traditional shifting hesitations. It requires minimal maintenance and proves highly dependable in everyday driving conditions.

## Steering

### Turn Signal Lever

Houses the multi-function switches attached to the steering column for signaling and lighting control.

**Turn Signal Lever Sag**
- **Symptoms:** A loud crack is heard when engaging the turn signal, after which the lever sags or feels loose inside its steering column housing.
- **Solution:** Replace the damaged turn signal stalk and internal steering column switch assembly.

## Electrical

### Integrated Charging Control Unit (ICCU)

Manages high-voltage battery charging and power distribution to vehicle accessories, serving as a critical focal point for early electrical complaints.

**ICCU and 12V Battery Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers experience a sudden loss of motive power, dashboard warnings to check the electrical system, and rapid depletion of the 12V battery resulting in vehicle shutdown.
- **Solution:** Have a dealership inspect the charging system and ensure all updated ICCU software and replacement fuses or modules have been installed.

### Horn Assembly

Controls acoustic signaling for pedestrian and driver safety alerts.

**Intermittent or Complete Horn Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle horn stops working temporarily or quits functioning completely without warning.
- **Solution:** Dealer inspection and replacement of the faulty horn assembly or associated relay/wiring components.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V204000: Electrical System:12V/24V/48V Battery](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Hyundai&model=Ioniq+6&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** The Integrated Charging Control Unit (ICCU) may become damaged and stop charging the 12-Volt battery, which can result in a loss of drive power. A loss of drive power increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the ICCU and its fuse, as necessary. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V283000: Power Train:Driveline:Differential Unit](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Hyundai&model=Ioniq+6&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** The differential gear bolts in the gear drive unit may not be tightened properly, which can result in a loss of drive power or cause the differential to lock up. A loss of drive power or differential lock up can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the gear drive unit, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V868000: Electrical System:12V/24V/48V Battery](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Hyundai&model=Ioniq+6&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** The Integrated Charging Control Unit (ICCU) may become damaged and stop charging the 12-volt battery, which can result in a loss of drive power. A loss of drive power increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the ICCU and its fuse, as necessary. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V606000: Electrical System:Propulsion System:Charging:Port](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Hyundai&model=Ioniq+6&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** A detached charging port door panel can create a road hazard for other vehicles, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will apply an adhesive on the charging port door assembly, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V218000: Seat Belts:Front:Anchorage](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Hyundai&model=Ioniq+6&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** A detached seat belt anchor will not adequately restrain the seat occupant, increasing the risk of injury in a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and reinforce or replace the seat belt anchors, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2024 Hyundai Ioniq 6 is a technologically advanced electric vehicle that offers style, comfort, and strong driving dynamics. While major mechanical components are remarkably trouble-free due to the simplicity of electric powertrains, prospective buyers must carefully verify the status of electrical recalls—specifically regarding the charging control unit. Securing a model with all factory updates applied ensures a much more reliable ownership experience.
