# 2024 Hyundai Ioniq 5 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2024 Hyundai Ioniq 5 is a stylish, highly praised all-electric crossover offering impressive driving dynamics and rapid charging capabilities. However, prospective buyers should approach used models with caution due to prominent electrical system complaints. Specifically, recurring issues regarding the Integrated Charging Control Unit (ICCU) can lead to sudden loss of motive power and unexpected vehicle shutdowns, presenting significant safety concerns on the road.

---

## Engine

### Single-Motor Electric Powertrain (Standard/Long Range)

Rear-wheel drive electric motor producing approximately 168 to 225 horsepower depending on the battery pack configuration. Offers efficient daily commuting performance and strong overall reliability.

## Transmission

### Dual-Motor AWD Electric Powertrain

All-wheel drive dual-motor configuration delivering 320 horsepower and swift acceleration. While potent, this setup is tied to widespread reports of electrical charging and propulsion control failures.

**Loss of Motive Power**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers report sudden warnings such as 'Stop vehicle and check power supply,' followed by a loss of propulsion and vehicle stalling while in motion.
- **Solution:** Requires dealership diagnosis, often involving software updates, fuse replacements, or total replacement of the Integrated Charging Control Unit (ICCU).

### Single-Speed Direct-Drive Transmission

A single-speed reduction gear transmission paired with the electric motor options. Known for smooth, seamless power delivery without traditional gear shifts and generally trouble-free mechanical performance.

## Electrical

### Integrated Charging Control Unit (ICCU)

Manages high-voltage and low-voltage charging systems. Failures within this unit are a primary source of electrical complaints for this model year.

**ICCU Failure and Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Sudden electrical system warnings, loss of 12V battery charge, dashboard warning lights, and occasional popping noises from beneath the vehicle leading to a shutdown.
- **Solution:** Dealer inspection and replacement of the faulty ICCU and associated fuses or wiring harnesses.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V204000: Electrical System:12V/24V/48V Battery](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Hyundai&model=Ioniq+5&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** The Integrated Charging Control Unit (ICCU) may become damaged and stop charging the 12-Volt battery, which can result in a loss of drive power. A loss of drive power increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the ICCU and its fuse, as necessary. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V868000: Electrical System:12V/24V/48V Battery](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Hyundai&model=Ioniq+5&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** The Integrated Charging Control Unit (ICCU) may become damaged and stop charging the 12-volt battery, which can result in a loss of drive power. A loss of drive power increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the ICCU and its fuse, as necessary. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V432000: Electrical System:Propulsion System:Traction Battery](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Hyundai&model=Ioniq+5&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** The high voltage battery cells may contain misaligned electrodes, which can lead to a fire while parked or driving. A fire increases the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the high voltage battery system assembly, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

While the 2024 Hyundai Ioniq 5 excels in design, comfort, and EV performance, its reliability is significantly impacted by well-documented electrical issues centering around the ICCU. Buyers looking at a used model should verify that all recall services and software updates have been performed by a certified dealer before purchase, and test drive the vehicle thoroughly to ensure electrical systems operate without error.
