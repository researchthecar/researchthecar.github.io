# 2023 Hyundai Ioniq 5 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2023 Hyundai Ioniq 5 stands out in the electric vehicle market for its striking retro-futuristic styling, spacious interior, and ultra-fast charging capabilities. However, prospective used-car buyers need to be aware of prominent electrical and powertrain concerns that have affected a subset of owners, particularly involving the vehicle's charging control systems.

---

## Engine

### Standard Range Electric Motor (Rear-Wheel Drive)

The standard-range rear-wheel-drive configuration pairs a single electric motor with a smaller battery pack, generating approximately 168 horsepower. This option provides efficient daily commuting performance with smooth power delivery and minimal mechanical complexity.

### Long Range Electric Motor (Rear-Wheel Drive)

The long-range rear-wheel-drive setup uses a single electric motor rated at roughly 225 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque. It offers an excellent balance of highway performance and driving range, generally proving robust in daily use.

### Dual-Motor Electric Propulsion System (All-Wheel Drive)

The dual-motor all-wheel-drive configuration combines front and rear motors to produce a combined 320 horsepower and 446 lb-ft of torque, delivering brisk acceleration and all-weather capability. While the motors themselves are generally reliable, this setup shares integration with the vehicle's broader high-voltage electrical architecture.

## Transmission

### Single-Speed Reduction Gear Transmission

The single-speed direct-drive transmission provides power seamlessly without traditional gear shifts. It features a simplified mechanical design that eliminates traditional transmission wear points and generally offers high reliability.

## Suspension

### OEM Tires and Foam Lining

Suspension components are generally sturdy, though factory-equipped tire foam lining issues have caused vibrations mimicking mechanical suspension faults.

**Internal Tire Foam Separation**
- **Symptoms:** Sound-absorbing foam inside Original Equipment Manufacturer tires can dislodge, leading to severe wheel vibrations and balancing issues at highway speeds.
- **Solution:** Inspect tires for internal foam detachment and replace affected tires with properly balanced units.

## Brakes

### Regenerative Braking and Brake Lights

The electronic braking system integrates traditional friction brakes with regenerative stopping. While stopping power is generally robust, software integration with one-pedal modes has drawn owner scrutiny.

**Regenerative Braking Light Activation Delay**
- **Symptoms:** When utilizing one-pedal driving modes (i-Pedal), decelerating vehicle speeds may not immediately trigger external brake lights, raising rear-end collision concerns.
- **Solution:** Consult dealership service departments for available software enhancements or adjustments concerning regenerative braking calibration.

## Electrical

### Integrated Charging Control Unit (ICCU)

The high-voltage electrical architecture relies heavily on the ICCU to manage power distribution and charging. Failures in this unit represent the most widely reported technical concern for this model year.

**ICCU Failure and Loss of Motive Power**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers frequently report dashboard warning lights, critical 12V battery drain, and a sudden loss of motive power or complete vehicle shutdown while driving.
- **Solution:** Requires dealer diagnosis and replacement of the Integrated Charging Control Unit (ICCU) and associated fuses, along with completion of applicable manufacturer updates.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V065000: Power Train:Driveline:Driveshaft](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Hyundai&model=Ioniq+5&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** A loss of drive power increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the rear inner driveshaft, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V204000: Electrical System:12V/24V/48V Battery](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Hyundai&model=Ioniq+5&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** The Integrated Charging Control Unit (ICCU) may become damaged and stop charging the 12-Volt battery, which can result in a loss of drive power. A loss of drive power increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the ICCU and its fuse, as necessary. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V868000: Electrical System:12V/24V/48V Battery](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Hyundai&model=Ioniq+5&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** The Integrated Charging Control Unit (ICCU) may become damaged and stop charging the 12-volt battery, which can result in a loss of drive power. A loss of drive power increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the ICCU and its fuse, as necessary. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V432000: Electrical System:Propulsion System:Traction Battery](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Hyundai&model=Ioniq+5&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** The high voltage battery cells may contain misaligned electrodes, which can lead to a fire while parked or driving. A fire increases the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the high voltage battery system assembly, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2023 Hyundai Ioniq 5 is a compelling electric crossover praised for its comfort, charging speed, and performance. However, used-car buyers must verify that all electrical recall services and Integrated Charging Control Unit (ICCU) updates have been properly completed by a certified dealer to minimize the risk of unexpected electrical failures.
