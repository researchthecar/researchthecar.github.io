# 2022 Hyundai Ioniq 5 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2022 Hyundai Ioniq 5 is a distinctive, highly praised all-electric crossover that offers rapid charging, futuristic styling, and impressive interior space. However, prospective buyers should approach this model year with caution due to prominent, widely reported electrical issues involving the Integrated Charging Control Unit (ICCU). While it delivers exceptional driving dynamics and advanced technology, verifying whether crucial electrical updates and component replacements have been completed is essential for a confident purchase.

---

## Engine

### Standard Range Electric Motor (Single Motor RWD)

Produces approximately 168 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque paired with a rear-wheel-drive layout. This setup provides efficient everyday commuting performance and solid reliability for daily drivers.

### Long Range Electric Motor (Single Motor RWD)

Delivers around 225 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque to the rear wheels, maximizing driving range. It balances peppy acceleration with smooth highway cruising capability.

## Transmission

### Dual Motor AWD Electric Powertrain

Generates 320 horsepower and 446 lb-ft of torque distributed to all four wheels for rapid acceleration and enhanced all-weather traction. This high-output configuration shares the same underlying electrical architecture susceptible to early charging and power management faults.

**Loss of Motive Power and Propulsion Failures**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers experience sudden electrical warnings, loss of propulsion, or unexpected shifts into neutral while operating the vehicle at various speeds.
- **Solution:** Requires diagnostic evaluation by a certified dealer, often leading to software updates or replacement of the Integrated Charging Control Unit (ICCU) and associated high-voltage fuses.

### Single-Speed Direct Drive Transmission

Utilizes a simple single-speed reduction gear coupled to the electric drive motor for seamless, shift-free power delivery. This layout requires minimal maintenance and rarely exhibits mechanical transmission failures.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes and Hill Hold

Regenerative and hydraulic braking systems provide strong stopping power, though some owners report quirks regarding hill-hold behavior on slopes.

**Incline Rollback Behavior**
- **Symptoms:** When coming to a complete stop on a slight incline, the vehicle may briefly roll backward after a short pause unless additional pressure is applied to the brake pedal.
- **Solution:** Be aware of regenerative braking and hill-hold engagement characteristics; consult a dealer if brake responsiveness feels inconsistent.

## Steering

### Steering Assembly and Wheels

Delivers responsive electric power steering feel, though a subset of owners experience persistent high-speed vibrations that can be difficult to eliminate.

**Highway Speed Steering Vibration**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers notice a pronounced vibration or shaking sensation in the steering wheel and cabin at highway speeds, typically between 55 and 65 mph.
- **Solution:** Perform thorough wheel balancing, check tire uniformity, and inspect internal tire noise-canceling foam or wheel alignment components.

## Electrical

### Integrated Charging Control Unit (ICCU) and Electrical System

Manages high-voltage battery charging and 12V accessory power. This system represents the single most significant vulnerability for this model year.

**ICCU Failure and 12V Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle displays dashboard warnings such as 'Check electric vehicle system' or 'Stop vehicle and check power supply,' followed by a dead 12V battery and inability to charge or start the vehicle.
- **Solution:** Have a dealership inspect the vehicle history to ensure recall software and hardware updates have been applied, which may involve replacing a failed ICCU unit and main fuse.

## Exterior

### Windshield and Glass

Features a modern hatchback profile, though factory glass durability has been a frequent point of frustration among early owners.

**Frequent Windshield Cracking**
- **Symptoms:** The front windshield develops chips, stress fractures, or full cracks with minimal provocation or minor road debris impacts.
- **Solution:** Inspect the windshield thoroughly for existing chips or cracks before purchasing, and verify comprehensive insurance glass coverage.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V324000: Parking Brake:Electrical:Control Module:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Hyundai&model=Ioniq+5&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** Vehicle rollaway can increase the risk of a crash or injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the Shifter Control Unit software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V204000: Electrical System:12V/24V/48V Battery](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Hyundai&model=Ioniq+5&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** The Integrated Charging Control Unit (ICCU) may become damaged and stop charging the 12-Volt battery, which can result in a loss of drive power. A loss of drive power increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the ICCU and its fuse, as necessary. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V868000: Electrical System:12V/24V/48V Battery](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Hyundai&model=Ioniq+5&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** The Integrated Charging Control Unit (ICCU) may become damaged and stop charging the 12-volt battery, which can result in a loss of drive power. A loss of drive power increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the ICCU and its fuse, as necessary. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2022 Hyundai Ioniq 5 remains an appealing option for used EV buyers seeking cutting-edge design, fast charging speeds, and a spacious cabin. However, prospective owners must carefully verify vehicle service histories to ensure all electrical and ICCU updates have been properly executed. Due to reports of high-voltage charging failures and sudden loss of motive power, a pre-purchase inspection by an EV-certified technician is strongly advised.
