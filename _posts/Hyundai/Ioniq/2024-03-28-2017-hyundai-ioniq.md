**Introduction**

The 2017 Hyundai Ioniq is a hybrid or electric vehicle available in three trims: Blue, SE, and Limited. The hybrid model has a 1.6-liter four-cylinder engine paired with a six-speed dual-clutch transmission, while the electric model has a 111-hp electric motor powered by a 28 kWh lithium-ion battery. The Ioniq is praised for its fuel efficiency, spacious interior, and advanced safety features, but some owners have reported various issues.

**Engine**

**1.6L Four-Cylinder Engine**

* **Engine Stalling:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The engine suddenly shuts off while driving, especially during low-speed maneuvers or when the air conditioner is activated.
    * Solution: Update the engine control module (ECM) software.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
    * Recalls: NHTSA Campaign Number 21V662000
* **Low Engine Power:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The engine lacks power and struggles to accelerate, particularly when climbing hills or carrying a heavy load.
    * Solution: Replace the throttle position sensor.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $250+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**Six-Speed Dual-Clutch Transmission**

* **Shifting Hesitation:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The transmission hesitates or jerks when shifting gears, especially during low-speed maneuvers or when the transmission is cold.
    * Solution: Update the transmission control module (TCM) software.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
    * Recalls: NHTSA Campaign Number 20V659000
* **Clutch Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The clutch slips or fails to engage, making it difficult to shift gears or move the vehicle.
    * Solution: Replace the clutch assembly.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $1,500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Rear Suspension Noise:**
    * Description/Symptoms: A clunking or rattling noise coming from the rear suspension, especially when driving over bumps or rough roads.
    * Solution: Replace the rear stabilizer bar bushings.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $250+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Front Strut Mount Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Excessive noise or vibration from the front end of the vehicle, especially when driving over bumps or rough roads.
    * Solution: Replace the front strut mounts.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $300+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Battery Discharge:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The battery drains quickly, even when the vehicle is not being driven.
    * Solution: Check for parasitic drains caused by faulty accessories or modules.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the source of the drain
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Electrical System Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Various electrical components fail to function, including lights, power windows, and audio system.
    * Solution: Troubleshoot the electrical system and replace any faulty components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Headlight Condensation:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Moisture accumulates inside the headlight assemblies, fogging up the lenses.
    * Solution: Replace the headlight assemblies or seal any leaks.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Paint Peeling:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The paint on the vehicle's exterior peels or chips prematurely.
    * Solution: Repaint the affected areas.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Dashboard Crack:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Cracks develop on the plastic dashboard, especially in areas exposed to sunlight.
    * Solution: Replace the dashboard assembly.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Climate Control Malfunction:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The climate control system fails to regulate temperature or airflow properly.
    * Solution: Diagnose and repair the malfunctioning component, such as the climate control module or actuators.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

While the 2017 Hyundai Ioniq offers fuel efficiency and advanced features, it has been plagued by several issues affecting the engine, transmission, suspension, electrical system, and interior. The severity of these issues varies, but some can be costly to repair. Owners should be aware of these potential problems and address them promptly to ensure the safety and reliability of their vehicle.
