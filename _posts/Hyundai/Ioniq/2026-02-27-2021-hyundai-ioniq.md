# 2021 Hyundai Ioniq

### Overview
The 2021 Hyundai Ioniq is a compact car offered in three distinct powertrain variants: a hybrid (HEV), a plug-in hybrid (PHEV), and a fully electric (EV) model. This diversification allows consumers to choose the level of electrification that best suits their needs. Generally, the Ioniq has been recognized for its fuel efficiency and practicality. Reliability data for the 2021 model year indicates a strong performance across most key areas, with specific attention paid to the unique components of each powertrain. Owners generally report a positive ownership experience with minimal unexpected repairs, reflecting Hyundai's commitment to quality in its electrified offerings.

### Summary Verdict
The 2021 Hyundai Ioniq demonstrates strong overall reliability, particularly in its powertrain components across all three variants (HEV, PHEV, EV). The hybrid and electric systems are robust, with no significant widespread failures reported. The primary area where minor issues have been noted is the infotainment system, which can occasionally experience software glitches requiring updates or resets. Other systems like suspension, exterior, and interior components are generally trouble-free. Owners can expect a dependable vehicle with minimal unexpected repair costs, especially when adhering to regular maintenance schedules. The Ioniq is a solid choice for those seeking an efficient and reliable electrified vehicle.
