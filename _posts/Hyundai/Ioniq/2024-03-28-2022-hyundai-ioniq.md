**Introduction:**

The 2022 Hyundai Ioniq is a compact hybrid and electric vehicle available in hybrid, plug-in hybrid, and electric powertrains. The hybrid model is powered by a 1.6-liter 4-cylinder engine paired with a 6-speed dual-clutch automatic transmission, while the plug-in hybrid variant uses the same engine but with a larger battery pack and a 6-speed automatic transmission. The electric Ioniq is equipped with a single electric motor and a 38.3 kWh battery pack.

Praised for its fuel efficiency, advanced safety features, and modern interior, the Ioniq is also known for its affordable price tag. However, there have been some reported issues with certain components.

**Engine:**

**1.6L 4-Cylinder Engine**

- **No Issues Found**

**Transmission:**

**6-Speed Dual-Clutch Automatic**

- **Shuddering During Acceleration**
  - Description/Symptoms: Vibrations or shuddering felt during acceleration, especially at low speeds.
  - Solution: Software update to optimize clutch operation.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 (covered under warranty)
- **Hesitation or Jerking**
  - Description/Symptoms: Occasional hesitation or jerking during gear changes.
  - Solution: Fluid change or transmission control module replacement.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $500+
- **Transmission Failure**
  - Description/Symptoms: Complete loss of power or inability to shift gears.
  - Solution: Major transmission repair or replacement.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $5,000+
- **Recalls:** NHTSA Campaign Number 22V-208

**6-Speed Automatic**

- **No Issues Found**

**Suspension:**

- **Excessive Road Noise**
  - Description/Symptoms: Loud road noise or vibrations transmitted into the cabin.
  - Solution: Inspect and replace worn or damaged suspension components (e.g., struts, shock absorbers, bushings).
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+

**Electrical:**

- **Faulty Battery**
  - Description/Symptoms: Premature battery failure, leading to reduced range or difficulty starting.
  - Solution: Battery replacement.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,000+
- **Charging System Issues**
  - Description/Symptoms: Difficulty charging or maintaining battery charge.
  - Solution: Inspect and repair or replace faulty charging components (e.g., charger, cables, connectors).
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $500+

**Exterior:**

- **Loose Exterior Trim**
  - Description/Symptoms: Loose or rattling exterior trim pieces, such as bumper covers or side mirrors.
  - Solution: Re-install or tighten loose trim components.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $200

**Interior:**

- **Noisy Dashboard**
  - Description/Symptoms: Rattling or squeaking noises coming from the dashboard area.
  - Solution: Inspect and tighten loose components or apply soundproofing material.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300

**Overall Summary:**

While the 2022 Hyundai Ioniq is generally reliable, several reported issues can be inconvenient and costly to repair. Electrical and transmission issues appear to be the most significant concerns, with potential repairs ranging from a few hundred dollars to several thousand dollars. However, it's important to note that not all Ioniqs experience these problems, and overall, the vehicle offers a good balance of fuel efficiency, features, and affordability.
