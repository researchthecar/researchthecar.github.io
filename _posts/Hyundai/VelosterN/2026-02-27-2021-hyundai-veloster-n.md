# 2021 Hyundai Veloster N Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2021 Hyundai Veloster N is a driver-focused hot hatchback known for its engaging performance and distinctive three-door design. While it delivers plenty of thrills for the enthusiast, prospective buyers should review available owner feedback regarding electric power steering behavior and related electrical warnings to ensure a trouble-free driving experience.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4

This 2.0-liter turbocharged inline-4 engine produces 275 horsepower and 260 lb-ft of torque when equipped with the Performance Package. It provides strong, responsive acceleration and forms the heart of the Veloster N's sporty driving dynamics.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Manual Transmission

The traditional 6-speed manual transmission features standard rev-matching and a direct mechanical feel. It is a favored choice among enthusiasts seeking maximum engagement.

### 8-Speed Wet Dual-Clutch Transmission

The 8-speed wet dual-clutch automatic transmission includes paddle shifters and N Grin Shift technology for temporary boosts in torque. It offers rapid shifts for drivers preferring an automatic setup.

## Steering

### Electric Power Steering System

Owner reports indicate occasional concerns regarding electric power assist failures, requiring careful pre-purchase inspection.

**Intermittent Power Steering Loss**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have reported sudden, unexpected loss of power steering while driving at highway speeds, accompanied by warning lights on the dashboard.
- **Solution:** Restarting the vehicle temporarily resolves the issue, but affected owners should have the steering control module and electrical connections inspected by a professional.

## Safety

### Advanced Driver Assistance Sensors

Driver assistance systems generally function well, though intermittent sensor sensitivity has been noted in owner feedback.

**Lane Departure and Blind Spot Warnings**
- **Symptoms:** Owners note false error messages or warnings from safety systems, occasionally coinciding with changes in weather or tires.
- **Solution:** Have dealership technicians recalibrate or inspect radar and camera sensors if false warnings persist.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V746000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Hyundai&model=Veloster+N&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** Loss of drive power increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the transmission, as necessary. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V528000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump:Control/Drive Module](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Hyundai&model=Veloster+N&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** A loss of drive power can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update engine control module software. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2021 Hyundai Veloster N is an exciting and generally capable hot hatch, but used car shoppers should pay close attention to steering system operation and electronic safety warnings during a test drive. Verifying that any applicable manufacturer campaigns have been properly addressed will help ensure a reliable ownership experience.
