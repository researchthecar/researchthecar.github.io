# 2017 Hyundai H200

### Overview
The 2017 Hyundai H200, a versatile light commercial vehicle, generally exhibits a solid reliability record, reflecting Hyundai's commitment to durability in its commercial offerings. While specific widespread issues are not commonly reported, like any vehicle, individual components can experience problems over time. This analysis focuses on documented technical concerns beyond routine maintenance. The H200 is known for its practical design and functional interior, making it a popular choice for businesses. Its powertrain options are typically robust, designed for demanding use. Overall, the H200 is considered a dependable workhorse, with most reported issues being isolated incidents rather than systemic flaws.

---


## Engine

### 2.5L CRDi Diesel
**Turbocharger Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of power, whistling noises, black smoke from exhaust.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the turbocharger unit.
- **Cost:** $1200 - $2500

**EGR Valve Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Check engine light, rough idling, reduced engine performance, increased emissions.
- **Solution:** Cleaning or replacement of the EGR valve.
- **Cost:** $300 - $700


## Transmission

### 5-Speed Manual
**Clutch Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Slipping clutch, difficulty shifting gears, burning smell.
- **Solution:** Replacement of clutch kit (clutch disc, pressure plate, release bearing).
- **Cost:** $600 - $1200


### 4-Speed Automatic
**Transmission Fluid Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Leaking fluid, rough shifting, overheating transmission.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of seals and gaskets, or transmission flush.
- **Cost:** $200 - $600


## Suspension

### Standard Chassis
**Worn Bushings**
- **Symptoms:** Clunking noises over bumps, vague steering feel, uneven tire wear.
- **Solution:** Replacement of worn suspension bushings.
- **Cost:** $300 - $700


## Electrical

### Standard Electrical System
**Alternator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Dimming headlights, battery warning light, dead battery.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the alternator.
- **Cost:** $400 - $800

### Summary Verdict
The 2017 Hyundai H200 demonstrates good overall reliability, particularly for its intended commercial use. The 2.5L CRDi diesel engine is generally robust, though turbocharger and EGR valve issues can arise with age and mileage. Transmission reliability is decent, with manual clutches and automatic fluid leaks being the most common concerns. Suspension components like bushings can wear, leading to noise and handling degradation. Electrical issues, such as alternator failure, are not uncommon in vehicles of this age. Exterior and interior components are typically durable, with no widespread systemic problems reported. Regular maintenance is key to mitigating most potential issues.
