# 2019 Hyundai Veloster Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2019 Hyundai Veloster brings sporty styling and unique asymmetric three-door styling to the compact hatchback market. While generally praised for its nimble handling and distinct personality, used car buyers should research specific mechanical and electrical tendencies reported by owners. Paying close attention to powertrain performance and door functionality during a pre-purchase inspection can help avoid unexpected headaches.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L 4-Cylinder

A naturally aspirated 2.0-liter four-cylinder engine making around 147 horsepower, serving as the base powerplant for lower trims. It offers dependable daily driving characteristics for commuters.

### 1.6L Turbocharged 4-Cylinder

A punchy 1.6-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine producing approximately 201 horsepower, designed for sportier performance trims. Buyers should verify proper maintenance records and watch for drivability concerns.

**Engine Stalling and Loss of Power**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers report sudden illumination of warning lights followed by an unexpected loss of motive power or complete engine stalling while driving.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified mechanic inspect the electrical system, fuel delivery, and engine control modules for fault codes and update software if necessary.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Manual Transmission

A traditional six-speed manual gearbox providing direct engagement for driving enthusiasts on select trims. Routine clutch checks are recommended during a used vehicle inspection.

### 7-Speed Dual-Clutch Automatic Transmission

A quick-shifting dual-clutch automatic transmission paired with select turbocharged engines. Smooth operation and responsive gear selection depend heavily on proper prior maintenance.

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

A conventional six-speed automatic transmission paired with non-turbo models. It offers smooth, predictable shifting for everyday driving.

## Exterior

### Door Latches and Actuators

Exterior body hardware features distinctive styling, though certain weather-related mechanical quirks have been noted by owners.

**Cold Weather Door Handle Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Outer door handles fail to operate or unlatch the doors when parked in freezing or cold temperatures, requiring entry through alternative doors.
- **Solution:** Replace faulty door lock actuators or latch mechanisms with updated components designed to resist moisture and freezing.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V301000: Engine](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Hyundai&model=Veloster&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** The piston oil rings may not have been properly heat-treated, which could result in engine damage. A damaged engine may stall, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and, if necessary, replace the engine, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V311000: Latches/Locks/Linkages:Doors:Latch](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Hyundai&model=Veloster&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** In certain side impact crashes, the driver's door latch housing and locking cable can become damaged, allowing the driver's side door to open. The door opening could result in occupant ejection or increase the risk of injury in the event of a crash.
- **What to do:** Hyundai will notify owners, and dealers will replace the driver's side door latch assembly and lower channel, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2019 Hyundai Veloster is an engaging and distinctive compact hatchback, but prospective buyers should perform a thorough evaluation. Pay particular attention to engine performance under acceleration and test all door handles in colder conditions if possible. A comprehensive pre-purchase inspection by a trusted mechanic will help ensure the vehicle's long-term reliability.
