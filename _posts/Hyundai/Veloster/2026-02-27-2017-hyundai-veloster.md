# 2017 Hyundai Veloster

### Overview
The 2017 Hyundai Veloster is a sporty compact car known for its distinctive asymmetrical door design and engaging driving dynamics. Reliability for this model year is generally considered good, with owners reporting fewer significant mechanical issues compared to some competitors. Common areas of concern, while not widespread, tend to be concentrated in specific components rather than systemic failures. The Veloster offers a range of powertrain options, and while most perform reliably, some specific combinations have seen isolated reports of issues. Overall, the 2017 Veloster presents a solid choice for those seeking a stylish and fun-to-drive vehicle with a reasonable expectation of dependability, provided routine maintenance is performed.

### Summary Verdict
The 2017 Hyundai Veloster generally exhibits good reliability, particularly with its naturally aspirated engine and manual transmission. The turbocharged engine and the DCT automatic transmission are the primary areas where minor issues have been reported, such as turbocharger concerns and transmission hesitation. Electrical gremlins, mainly related to the infotainment system, are also noted but typically resolvable with software updates. Suspension, exterior, and interior components are largely free of widespread complaints. With regular maintenance and attention to potential turbo or DCT issues, the 2017 Veloster should provide a dependable and enjoyable ownership experience.
