# 2017 Hyundai Veloster Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2017 Hyundai Veloster offers distinctive asymmetric styling and nimble handling, but prospective buyers should approach with careful mechanical due diligence. Owner reports indicate potential vulnerabilities centered around engine longevity, transmission shifting concerns, and exterior finish degradation. Careful inspection of maintenance history and a thorough pre-purchase mechanical evaluation are strongly advised.

---

## Engine

### 1.6L 4-Cylinder (Naturally Aspirated)

The base 1.6-liter naturally aspirated four-cylinder engine produces 132 horsepower. It provides efficient daily driving characteristics, though buyers should monitor for oil consumption and knocking noises.

**Engine Stalling and Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicles may unexpectedly stall while driving at highway speeds, followed by abnormal knocking noises and difficulties restarting.
- **Solution:** Requires professional diagnostic evaluation; severe internal engine damage may necessitate engine replacement.

### 1.6L Turbocharged 4-Cylinder

The turbocharged 1.6-liter four-cylinder engine increases output to 201 horsepower, offering considerably punchier performance for enthusiast drivers. Premium fuel is recommended for optimal operation.

## Transmission

### 7-Speed Dual-Clutch Automatic Transmission

The 7-speed dual-clutch automatic transmission delivers quick gear changes on turbo models, but test-drive shifting smoothness closely for signs of wear.

**Gear Slippage and Shifting Delays**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers experience clicking noises during gear changes, hesitation when shifting, and occasional transmission slippage.
- **Solution:** Have a certified technician inspect transmission control modules, clutch actuators, and fluid condition.

### 6-Speed Manual Transmission

The traditional 6-speed manual transmission offers direct engagement for base models, generally proving straightforward to maintain if the clutch is inspected for wear.

## Exterior

### Sunroof

Exterior components generally hold up well, though glass and trim elements require careful inspection.

**Spontaneous Sunroof Shattering**
- **Symptoms:** The panoramic or standard glass sunroof may shatter unexpectedly while driving, creating a loud explosion-like noise and showering the cabin with glass.
- **Solution:** Replace the damaged glass panel and inspect the frame for alignment or stress points.

## Safety

### Air Bag System

Safety systems include standard airbags and active stability controls, though electronic sensor faults can occasionally trigger warning lights.

**Airbag Warning Light and Sensor Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** The airbag warning light illuminates intermittently due to faulty seat sensors, or airbags may fail to deploy as expected in certain impact scenarios.
- **Solution:** Inspect seat wiring harnesses and replace malfunctioning occupant detection sensors.

## Summary Verdict

The 2017 Hyundai Veloster is an appealing hatchback for buyers seeking unique styling and engaging road manners. However, because some owners have reported significant engine and transmission concerns, prospective buyers should prioritize vehicles with documented service records and complete a comprehensive pre-purchase inspection.
