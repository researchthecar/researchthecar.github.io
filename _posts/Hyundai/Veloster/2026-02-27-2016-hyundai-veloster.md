# 2016 Hyundai Veloster Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2016 Hyundai Veloster offers distinctive asymmetric styling and a sporty personality, but used-car buyers should approach with caution due to several recurring mechanical and electrical pain points. Owners have frequently reported significant powertrain troubles, including engine stalling and excessive oil consumption, alongside frustrating issues with dual-clutch transmissions and interior electronics. Thoroughly inspecting the service history and verifying whether safety recalls have been completed is essential before purchasing.

---

## Engine

### 1.6L 4-Cylinder Engine

The base 1.6-liter naturally aspirated four-cylinder engine produces 132 horsepower, providing adequate efficiency for daily commuting. While generally straightforward, this powerplant can be prone to high mileage oil consumption and stalling concerns.

**Engine Stalling and Loss of Power**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle experiences sudden engine stalling, illumination of warning lights, and a total loss of motive power while driving.
- **Solution:** Diagnostic testing by a qualified mechanic to identify root electrical or mechanical failures; component replacement as needed.

**Excessive Oil Consumption**
- **Symptoms:** Engine burns oil rapidly between regular service intervals, potentially leading to internal engine wear or damage.
- **Solution:** Monitor oil levels frequently and consult a dealer or mechanic for diagnostic evaluation and potential warranty remedies.

### 1.6L Turbocharged 4-Cylinder Engine

The 1.6-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine boosts output to 201 horsepower, delivering significantly livelier acceleration. Buyers should closely inspect turbocharged models for proper maintenance and adherence to safety recalls.

**Turbo Engine Stalling and Bearing Concerns**
- **Symptoms:** Engine hesitates, stalls unexpectedly in traffic, or displays warning indicators related to internal mechanical wear.
- **Solution:** Inspect engine health, verify recall status for rod bearing or crank damage, and repair or replace failing components.

## Transmission

### 7-Speed Dual-Clutch Automatic Transmission

The 7-speed dual-clutch automatic transmission offers quick gear changes for a sportier driving feel. However, it is a frequent source of owner complaints regarding rough engagement, shuddering, and clutch wear.

**Clutch Failure and Jerky Shifting**
- **Symptoms:** Transmission shudders, fails to engage gears properly, hesitates when accelerating, or experiences unintended neutral shifts.
- **Solution:** Inspect the dual-clutch assembly and transmission control module; software updates, clutch pack replacement, or module reprogramming may be required.

### 6-Speed Manual Transmission

The traditional 6-speed manual transmission pairs well with the Veloster's sporty character and generally avoids the complex mechanical complaints of the automatic options.

**Clutch Pedal Linkage Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Clutch pedal drops flat to the floorboard and fails to return automatically, making gear changes difficult.
- **Solution:** Inspect hydraulic slave and master cylinders or pedal return springs; replace worn hydraulic components.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

Brake system reliability is critical, and intermittent soft pedal complaints require immediate professional inspection.

**Intermittent Soft Brake Pedal**
- **Symptoms:** Brake pedal intermittently sinks loosely toward the floor without immediately engaging the brakes, creating safety concerns.
- **Solution:** Inspect master cylinder, brake fluid lines, and bleeding system components thoroughly.

## Electrical

### Climate Control and Infotainment Electronics

Cabin electronics can develop frustrating failures, particularly heat-warped touchscreens and melting blower motor wiring that disables climate controls.

**Melting Blower Motor Switch and Touchscreen Failure**
- **Symptoms:** AC control knobs become stuck, blower motor wiring melts due to excessive current, and infotainment touchscreens warp or stop responding.
- **Solution:** Replace damaged wiring plugs, climate control resistor switches, or faulty display units.

## Exterior

### Door Handles and Body Hardware

Exterior hardware components like door handles are prone to mechanical binding or breakage over time.

**Inoperative Exterior Door Handles**
- **Symptoms:** Passenger exterior door handles fail to operate, requiring occupants to open the door using the interior handle regardless of lock status.
- **Solution:** Replace broken internal door handle latch mechanisms or linkage cables.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V746000: Engine](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Hyundai&model=Veloster&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** A damaged engine can increase the risk of a fire or it can cause an engine stall, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Hyundai will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the engine. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2016 Hyundai Veloster is an appealing option for buyers seeking an affordable, sporty hatchback with unique styling. However, prospective owners should exercise caution due to a notable history of engine stalling, oil consumption issues, and dual-clutch transmission complaints. Prior to purchase, obtain a comprehensive pre-purchase inspection, review maintenance records carefully, and ensure all relevant manufacturer safety campaigns have been addressed.
