**1991 Hyundai Scoupe**

The Hyundai Scoupe is a sporty compact car manufactured from 1988 to 1995. It was available with a variety of engine and transmission options, including a 1.5L SOHC I4, a 1.5L DOHC I4, and a 2.0L DOHC I4. Transmission options included a 5-speed manual and a 4-speed automatic. The Scoupe was praised for its handling and performance, but criticized for its lack of interior space and comfort.

**Engine**

**1.5L SOHC I4**
* **Head gasket failure:** Blown head gaskets can cause coolant and oil to leak into the cylinders, leading to overheating and engine damage. Symptoms include white smoke from the exhaust, coolant loss, and a rough idle. The solution is to replace the head gasket. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1000+
* **Timing belt failure:** Timing belts can snap, causing the engine to lose timing and potentially damage the valves. Symptoms include a sudden loss of power and a rattling noise from the engine. The solution is to replace the timing belt. Estimated Cost to Fix: $250 - $500+

**1.5L DOHC I4**
* **No Issues Found**

**2.0L DOHC I4**
* **Intake manifold gasket failure:** Intake manifold gaskets can fail, causing a vacuum leak that can lead to rough idling, poor performance, and increased fuel consumption. Symptoms include a hissing sound from the engine and a decrease in engine power. The solution is to replace the intake manifold gasket. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+

**Transmission**

**5-speed Manual**
* **Clutch failure:** Clutches can wear out over time, causing difficulty shifting gears and a loss of power. Symptoms include slipping gears, grinding noises when shifting, and a burning smell. The solution is to replace the clutch. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1000+
* **Synchronizer failure:** Synchronizers can fail, making it difficult or impossible to shift gears. Symptoms include grinding noises when shifting and difficulty engaging gears. The solution is to replace the synchronizers. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1000+

**4-speed Automatic**
* **Solenoid failure:** Solenoids can fail, causing transmission slipping, harsh shifting, or a loss of gears. Symptoms include delayed or erratic shifting and a check engine light. The solution is to replace the solenoids. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+

**Suspension**

* **Strut failure:** Struts can fail, causing a loss of ride comfort, handling, and stability. Symptoms include abouncy ride, excessive body roll, and uneven tire wear. The solution is to replace the struts. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+ per axle

* **Tie rod failure:** Tie rods can fail, causing the vehicle to pull to one side while driving. Symptoms include a loose steering feel, excessive tire wear, and a clunking sound when turning. The solution is to replace the tie rods. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+ per side

**Electrical**

* **Alternator failure:** Alternators can fail, causing the battery to drain and the vehicle to lose power. Symptoms include dim headlights, a flickering dashboard, and a dead battery. The solution is to replace the alternator. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
* **Starter failure:** Starters can fail, making it difficult or impossible to start the vehicle. Symptoms include a clicking noise when turning the key, a lack of response from the engine, and a dead battery. The solution is to replace the starter. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+

**Exterior**

* **Rear hatch release failure:** The rear hatch release can fail, making it difficult or impossible to open the hatch. Symptoms include a broken or jammed release lever and a stuck hatch. The solution is to replace the release mechanism. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+

**Interior**

* **Dashboard cracking:** The dashboard can crack over time due to heat and UV exposure. Symptoms include visible cracks on the dashboard surface. The solution is to replace or repair the dashboard. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
* **Seat belt failure:** Seat belts can fail, increasing the risk of injury in an accident. Symptoms include frayed or torn seat belt webbing and a lack of tension when the belt is buckled. The solution is to replace the seat belt. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+ per seat

**Summary**

The 1991 Hyundai Scoupe is a reliable and affordable sports car. However, it has some potential issues that should be considered before purchasing. The most common issues are with the engine, transmission, and suspension. Fortunately, these issues are relatively easy and inexpensive to fix. Overall, the Scoupe is a good choice for those looking for a fun and affordable sports car.
