**Introduction**

The 2013 Hyundai Elantra GT is a compact hatchback that offers a sporty and stylish driving experience. It is available with a variety of engine and transmission options, including a 1.8-liter four-cylinder engine with 148 horsepower and 131 lb-ft of torque, and a 2.0-liter four-cylinder engine with 173 horsepower and 154 lb-ft of torque. The Elantra GT comes standard with a six-speed manual transmission, but a six-speed automatic transmission is also available.

People who like the Elantra GT appreciate its sporty handling, comfortable interior, and affordable price. However, some people have reported issues with the vehicle's engine, transmission, and other components.

**Engine**

**1.8-Liter Four-Cylinder Engine**

* **Engine Oil Consumption:** Some Elantra GTs with the 1.8-liter engine have been reported to consume excessive amounts of oil. This can lead to engine damage if not addressed.
    * Solution: Replace the piston rings and valve seals.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Timing Chain Issues:** The timing chain on some Elantra GTs with the 1.8-liter engine can stretch or break, which can lead to catastrophic engine failure.
    * Solution: Replace the timing chain and related components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,000
    * Recalls: None Found

**2.0-Liter Four-Cylinder Engine**

* **No Issues Found**

**Transmission**

**Six-Speed Manual Transmission**

* **Clutch Issues:** Some Elantra GTs with the six-speed manual transmission have experienced clutch problems, such as slipping or chattering.
    * Solution: Replace the clutch assembly.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $1,500
    * Recalls: None Found

**Six-Speed Automatic Transmission**

* **Transmission Fluid Leaks:** Some Elantra GTs with the six-speed automatic transmission have been reported to leak transmission fluid. This can lead to transmission damage if not addressed.
    * Solution: Replace the transmission seals.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Windshield Cracking:** Some Elantra GTs have experienced windshield cracking, particularly on the passenger side.
    * Solution: Replace the windshield.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Interior Trim Issues:** Some Elantra GTs have experienced problems with the interior trim, such as rattling or peeling.
    * Solution: Replace or repair the affected trim pieces.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2013 Hyundai Elantra GT is a popular compact hatchback that offers a sporty and stylish driving experience. However, some people have reported issues with the vehicle's engine, transmission, and other components. The severity of these issues varies, but some can be expensive to repair. Overall, the Elantra GT is a reliable vehicle, but it is important to be aware of the potential issues before purchasing one.
