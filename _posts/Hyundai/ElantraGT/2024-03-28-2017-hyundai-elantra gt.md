**2017 Hyundai Elantra GT Overview**

The 2017 Hyundai Elantra GT is a compact hatchback that offers a sporty and practical alternative to sedans. It is available with two engine options: a 2.0-liter four-cylinder with 161 horsepower and 150 lb-ft of torque, and a 1.6-liter turbocharged four-cylinder with 201 horsepower and 195 lb-ft of torque. Transmission choices include a six-speed manual, seven-speed dual-clutch automatic (DCT), and six-speed automatic.

Owners praise the Elantra GT's stylish design, spacious interior, and well-equipped features. However, some have reported issues with certain components.

**Engine**

**2.0-liter Four-Cylinder**

* **Intake Valve Deposits**
    * Description/Symptoms: Carbon buildup on the intake valves restricts airflow, leading to reduced engine power, rough idling, and increased fuel consumption.
    * Solution: Removing and cleaning the intake valves.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600
    * Recalls: None Found

**1.6-liter Turbocharged Four-Cylinder**

* **Oil Consumption**
    * Description/Symptoms: Excessive oil consumption, requiring frequent top-ups.
    * Solution: Replacing the piston rings and oil pump.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,200 - $2,500
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**Six-Speed Manual**

* **Grinding Noises**
    * Description/Symptoms: Noisy shifting, particularly in lower gears.
    * Solution: Replacing the synchronizers.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $1,500
    * Recalls: None Found

**Seven-Speed Dual-Clutch Automatic (DCT)**

* **Slippage/Hesitation**
    * Description/Symptoms: Inability to engage gears smoothly, resulting in hesitation or slipping.
    * Solution: Replacing the clutch plates and mechatronic unit.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,500
    * Recalls: 17V461000

**Six-Speed Automatic**

* **Shifting Problems**
    * Description/Symptoms: Delayed or rough gear changes, accompanied by clunking or jerking.
    * Solution: Updating the transmission control module (TCM) software or replacing the transmission valve body.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,200
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Rear Suspension Noise**
    * Description/Symptoms: Rattling or squeaking from the rear suspension, especially over bumps or uneven roads.
    * Solution: Replacing or lubricating the rear sway bar links and bushings.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain**
    * Description/Symptoms: Battery losing charge prematurely, requiring frequent jump-starts.
    * Solution: Testing the electrical system to identify the source of the drain (e.g., faulty battery, alternator, or electrical device).
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the source of the drain
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Door Lock Problems**
    * Description/Symptoms: Difficulty locking or unlocking the doors using the key fob or button.
    * Solution: Replacing the door lock actuators.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Climate Control Malfunction**
    * Description/Symptoms: HVAC system not functioning properly, resulting in insufficient heating or cooling.
    * Solution: Replacing the blend door actuator.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

Overall, the 2017 Hyundai Elantra GT has its fair share of potential issues. While some of these issues are minor and relatively inexpensive to fix, others can be more severe and costly. Serious problems such as engine oil consumption and transmission slipping can significantly impact the reliability and longevity of the vehicle. Before purchasing an Elantra GT, it is important to be aware of these issues and factor them into your decision.
