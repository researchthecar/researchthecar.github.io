# 2016 Hyundai Genesis Coupe Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2016 Hyundai Genesis Coupe represents the final year of the rear-wheel-drive sports coupe, known for its sharp styling and potent standard V6 power. While overall owner feedback is mixed, prospective buyers should review available maintenance records and inspect powertrain components closely before purchasing a used model.

---

## Engine

### 3.8L V6 Engine

The 3.8-liter Lambda V6 engine produces 348 horsepower and 295 lb-ft of torque, providing brisk acceleration. It is generally a robust powerplant, though owners should verify proper electronic throttle operation.

**Spontaneous Acceleration**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle may experience sudden, unexpected full-throttle acceleration during startup or while driving.
- **Solution:** Inspect the electronic throttle body, accelerator pedal sensor, and wiring harness for faults.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Manual Transmission

The traditional 6-speed manual transmission pairs with the V6 engine for a direct driver connection. Buyers should thoroughly test-shift through all gears to ensure smooth synchronization.

**Gear Grinding and Shifting Difficulty**
- **Symptoms:** Manual transmission may produce grinding noises during gear changes or resist engaging into gear.
- **Solution:** Check transmission fluid condition, clutch hydraulics, and synchronizers for excessive wear.

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

The in-house designed 8-speed automatic transmission features steering-wheel-mounted paddle shifters and delivers smooth, responsive gear changes. It offers a refined driving experience for daily commuting and cruising.

## Suspension

### Tires and Wheels

Performance tires on sport coupes experience high wear rates and require attentive maintenance.

**Premature Tire Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Tires may experience sudden tread separation or blowouts under normal driving conditions.
- **Solution:** Replace damaged tires and regularly monitor tire pressure, alignment, and tread depth.

## Electrical

### Body Electrical and Lighting

electrical issues can occasionally affect body accessories and lighting controls on older models.

**Accessory and Lighting Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Automatic headlights may fail to activate, door locks may become unresponsive, and interior chimes or dashboard lights may malfunction intermittently.
- **Solution:** Inspect the body control module (BCM), fuses, and associated wiring connections for intermittent faults.

## Exterior

### Body Structure

Body structure integrity is generally sound, though isolated spot-weld concerns have been reported.

**A-Pillar Hinge Area Cracking**
- **Symptoms:** Unusual popping or structural noises can be heard from the A-pillar hinge area when driving over bumps.
- **Solution:** Inspect the affected body welds and consult a professional structural repair specialist if cracking is found.

## Safety

### Air Bag System

Safety systems are crucial to inspect, and any persistent airbag warning lights should be diagnosed immediately.

**Failure to Deploy**
- **Symptoms:** Air bags may fail to deploy during a significant collision or rollover event.
- **Solution:** Have the Supplemental Restraint System (SRS) control module and sensors inspected if any warning lights are illuminated.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V722000: Air Bags:Sensor:Occupant Classification](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Hyundai&model=Genesis+Coupe&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** In the event of a crash necessitating deployment of the passenger frontal air bag, the disconnected OCS would, by default, cause the first stage, and only the first stage, of the air bag to deploy, whether there is a child seat in the front seat and the bag should not deploy or if the crash is severe and both stages should deploy. Either scenario increases.
- **What to do:** Hyundai will notify owners, and dealers will secure the OCS connector to prevent it from disconnecting, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V248000: Air Bags:Frontal](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Hyundai&model=Genesis+Coupe&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** A detached driver's air bag increases the risk of injury in the event of a crash.
- **What to do:** Hyundai will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the mounting bolts and retighten them as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2016 Hyundai Genesis Coupe offers engaging rear-wheel-drive performance and strong V6 power, making it an appealing option for enthusiasts. While owner complaints indicate potential areas of concern such as manual transmission shifting stiffness and minor electrical glitches, a thorough pre-purchase inspection can help identify underlying issues before buying.
