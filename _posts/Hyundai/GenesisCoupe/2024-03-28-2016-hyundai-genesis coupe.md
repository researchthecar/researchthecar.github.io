The 2016 Hyundai Genesis Coupe is a sporty coupe that offers a powerful engine and responsive handling. It is available with a 3.8-liter V6 engine that produces 348 horsepower and 295 lb-ft of torque, paired with an 8-speed automatic transmission or a 6-speed manual transmission.

Owners of the 2016 Hyundai Genesis Coupe have reported various issues with their vehicles. These issues include:

**Engine**

* **3.8L V6 Engine**
    * **Knocking Noise**
        * Description/Symptoms: A knocking noise coming from the engine, especially when accelerating.
        * Solution: Replacing the engine's connecting rods and pistons.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $5,000 - $10,000+
        * Recalls: NHTSA Campaign Number 17V439000
    * **Oil Consumption**
        * Description/Symptoms: Excessive oil consumption, leading to low oil levels and potential engine damage.
        * Solution: Replacing the piston rings and valve stem seals.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

* **8-Speed Automatic Transmission**
    * **Shifting Problems**
        * Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears, slipping gears, or delayed gear engagement.
        * Solution: Updating the transmission control module software or replacing the transmission valve body.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+
        * Recalls: None Found
* **6-Speed Manual Transmission**
    * **Grinding Noise**
        * Description/Symptoms: Grinding noise when shifting gears, especially in cold weather.
        * Solution: Replacing the clutch and flywheel.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,500+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Front Suspension Noise**
    * Description/Symptoms: Squeaking or rattling noise from the front suspension, especially over bumps.
    * Solution: Replacing the front strut mounts.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Electrical System Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Sudden loss of electrical power, affecting the engine, lights, and other electrical components.
    * Solution: Replacing the main electrical harness.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $3,000+
    * Recalls: NHTSA Campaign Number 16V379000

**Exterior**

* **Door Lock Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Door locks not functioning properly, leaving the vehicle vulnerable to theft.
    * Solution: Replacing the door lock actuators.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Climate Control Malfunction**
    * Description/Symptoms: Climate control system not functioning properly, leading to uncomfortable cabin temperatures.
    * Solution: Replacing the climate control module or blower motor.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Window Regulator Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Power windows not functioning properly, getting stuck, or becoming slow.
    * Solution: Replacing the window regulator assembly.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2016 Hyundai Genesis Coupe has several potential issues, particularly with its engine and transmission. The severity of these issues can range from minor annoyances to serious mechanical problems. The cost to fix these issues can also be significant, with some repairs exceeding $5,000. However, the 2016 Hyundai Genesis Coupe remains a popular choice for those seeking a sporty and affordable coupe.
