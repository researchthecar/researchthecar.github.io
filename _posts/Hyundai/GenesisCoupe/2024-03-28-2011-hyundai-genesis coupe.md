**2011 Hyundai Genesis Coupe Overview**

The 2011 Hyundai Genesis Coupe is a sporty coupe that offers a powerful engine, stylish design, and a comfortable interior. It is available with two engine options: a 3.8-liter V6 and a 2.0-liter turbocharged inline-four. Both engines are mated to either a six-speed manual transmission or a six-speed automatic transmission. The Genesis Coupe is praised for its performance, handling, and value, but it has received some complaints about its cramped rear seats and limited visibility.

**Engine**

**3.8-Liter V6**

* **Faulty Timing Chain Tensioner:** The timing chain tensioner may fail, causing the timing chain to skip or break. This can lead to severe engine damage.
    * **Symptoms:** Engine rattling, reduced power, and illuminated Check Engine light.
    * **Solution:** Replace the timing chain tensioner and timing chain.
    * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $600 - $2,000+
    * **Recalls:** NHTSA ID: 14V352000, 16V591000

**2.0-Liter Turbocharged Inline-Four**

* **Excessive Carbon Buildup on Intake Valves:** Carbon can accumulate on the intake valves over time, reducing airflow and engine performance.
    * **Symptoms:** Loss of power, reduced fuel economy, and engine misfires.
    * **Solution:** Clean the intake valves manually or use a chemical cleaner.
    * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $200 - $600

**Transmission**

**Six-Speed Manual Transmission**

* **Broken Shift Fork:** The shift fork may break, making it difficult or impossible to shift gears.
    * **Symptoms:** Difficulty shifting gears, grinding noises, and illuminated Check Engine light.
    * **Solution:** Replace the shift fork.
    * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $400 - $800

**Six-Speed Automatic Transmission**

* **Slipping Transmission:** The transmission may slip when shifting gears, causing a loss of power.
    * **Symptoms:** Transmission slipping between gears, loss of power, and illuminated Check Engine light.
    * **Solution:** Rebuild or replace the transmission.
    * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $1,500 - $3,000+
    * **Recalls:** NHTSA ID: 14V352000, 16V591000

**Suspension**

* **Front Strut Mount Failure:** The front strut mount may fail, causing the strut to lose its alignment.
    * **Symptoms:** Noise when turning, uneven tire wear, and reduced handling.
    * **Solution:** Replace the strut mount.
    * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $200 - $400+

**Electrical**

* **Alternator Failure:** The alternator may fail, preventing the vehicle from charging the battery.
    * **Symptoms:** Dim headlights, dead battery, and illuminated Check Engine light.
    * **Solution:** Replace the alternator.
    * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $400 - $800

* **Battery Drain:** The battery may drain quickly, even when the vehicle is turned off.
    * **Symptoms:** Dead battery, difficulty starting the vehicle, and illuminated Check Engine light.
    * **Solution:** Identify and fix the source of the battery drain, such as a faulty electrical component.
    * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** Varies depending on the source of the drain.

**Exterior**

* **Rear Spoiler Separation:** The rear spoiler may separate from the vehicle at high speeds.
    * **Symptoms:** Rear spoiler rattling or coming loose.
    * **Solution:** Reattach the spoiler and reinforce the mounting points.
    * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $100 - $200+

**Interior**

* **Heater Core Leak:** The heater core may develop a leak, allowing coolant to enter the passenger compartment.
    * **Symptoms:** Foggy windows, coolant smell in the cabin, and low coolant levels.
    * **Solution:** Replace the heater core.
    * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $600 - $1,200+

* **Navigation Screen Malfunction:** The navigation screen may malfunction, displaying incorrect information or freezing.
    * **Symptoms:** Inaccurate navigation directions, frozen screen, and illuminated Check Engine light.
    * **Solution:** Update or replace the navigation system software.
    * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $200 - $400+

**Summary**

The 2011 Hyundai Genesis Coupe has received mixed reviews regarding its reliability. Some owners have reported severe engine and transmission issues, while others have had relatively few problems. The severity and cost of issues can vary depending on the specific part that fails. It is important to note that the Genesis Coupe is a performance-oriented vehicle, and some issues may be associated with aggressive driving or modifications.
