The 2008 Hyundai Elantra is a compact sedan known for its fuel efficiency and value. Engine options for the 2008 Elantra included a 2.0L inline-4 cylinder engine, producing around 138 horsepower and 136 lb-ft of torque. Transmission options were a 5-speed manual or a 4-speed automatic. Owners generally appreciate the Elantra's affordability, fuel economy, and spacious interior for its class. Common dislikes include relatively bland styling, a somewhat unrefined ride, and in some cases, concerns about long-term reliability.

**Engine**

**2.0L Inline-4 Cylinder (G4GC)**

* **Check Engine Light Due to Failed Catalytic Converter**
    * Description/Symptoms: The check engine light illuminates, often accompanied by OBDII codes related to catalytic converter efficiency (e.g., P0420). The car may fail emissions testing.
    * Solution: Replacement of the catalytic converter. Ensure upstream oxygen sensors are functioning correctly to prevent premature failure of the new converter.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $700+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Engine Misfires Due to Faulty Ignition Coils**
    * Description/Symptoms: Rough idling, hesitation upon acceleration, check engine light illumination with misfire codes (e.g., P0300, P0301, P0302, P0303, P0304).
    * Solution: Replacement of the faulty ignition coil(s). Replacing spark plugs simultaneously is recommended.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**4-Speed Automatic**

* **Harsh Shifting**
    * Description/Symptoms: Abrupt or jerky shifts between gears, particularly noticeable during acceleration or deceleration.
    * Solution: The Transmission Control Module (TCM) may need to be updated/reflashed with updated software. In some cases, flushing the transmission fluid or replacing the solenoid pack may be required if the issue persists.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Worn Struts and Shocks**
    * Description/Symptoms: Bouncy ride, excessive body roll during cornering, nose-diving during braking, and uneven tire wear. Noises such as clunking or squeaking can also be heard.
    * Solution: Replacement of worn struts and shocks. Replacing strut mounts and bump stops is also recommended for optimal performance.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+ (for all four corners)
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Noisy Sway Bar End Links**
    * Description/Symptoms: Clunking or rattling noises from the front or rear suspension, especially when driving over bumps or uneven surfaces.
    * Solution: Replacement of the sway bar end links.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Faulty Window Regulator**
    * Description/Symptoms: Windows that operate slowly, erratically, or fail to operate at all. Grinding noises may be heard when attempting to use the window.
    * Solution: Replacement of the window regulator assembly.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $400+ per window
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Failed Instrument Cluster Lighting**
    * Description/Symptoms: Dim or non-functional gauges on the instrument cluster, making it difficult to read information while driving at night.
    * Solution: Replacement of faulty bulbs or LEDs within the instrument cluster. In severe cases, the entire instrument cluster may need to be replaced.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $400+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **No Issues Found**

**Interior**

* **Cracked Dashboard**
    * Description/Symptoms: Cracks appearing on the dashboard surface, often due to sun exposure and temperature changes.
    * Solution: Replacement of the entire dashboard (expensive) or use of a dashboard cover. Some repair shops offer dashboard refinishing services.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 (cover) - $1000+ (replacement)
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2008 Hyundai Elantra, while a reliable and economical car, is prone to a few common issues as it ages. Catalytic converter failure, suspension component wear, and electrical problems such as faulty window regulators are among the most frequent complaints. While the severity of these issues varies, some repairs, like catalytic converter replacement or dashboard replacement, can be relatively costly. Overall, prospective buyers should be aware of these potential problems and factor them into their purchasing decision, budgeting for potential repairs and inspections.
