**Introduction:**

The 2002 Hyundai Elantra is a compact sedan known for its affordability and fuel economy. It is available with a 1.6L or 2.0L engine paired with a 5-speed manual or 4-speed automatic transmission. The 1.6L engine produces 105 horsepower and 106 lb-ft of torque, while the 2.0L engine generates 140 horsepower and 133 lb-ft of torque. The Elantra offers a comfortable and spacious interior with a generous amount of legroom and headroom. However, it receives criticism for its lack of power, especially with the 1.6L engine.

**Engine**

**1.6L Engine**

* **Timing Belt Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The timing belt can snap, causing catastrophic engine damage. Symptoms include sudden loss of power, difficulty starting, and unusual noises from the engine.
    * Solution: Replace the timing belt and inspect the other components of the timing system.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $1,500+
    * Recalls: 04V514000

* **Oil Sludge Buildup:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Oil sludge can accumulate in the engine, leading to reduced oil flow and potential engine damage. Symptoms include low oil pressure, overheating, and reduced fuel efficiency.
    * Solution: Regular oil changes and maintenance can help prevent sludge buildup. In severe cases, the engine may need to be rebuilt.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+

**2.0L Engine**

* **No Issues Found**

**Transmission**

**5-Speed Manual Transmission**

* **Synchros Wear:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The synchronizers in the transmission can wear out, causing difficulty shifting gears. Symptoms include grinding noises when shifting and difficulty engaging gears.
    * Solution: Replace the worn synchronizers.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
    * Recalls: None Found

**4-Speed Automatic Transmission**

* **Torque Converter Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The torque converter can fail, causing a loss of power and transmission slippage. Symptoms include slipping gears, shuddering, and loss of acceleration.
    * Solution: Replace the torque converter.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $1,500
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Strut Mount Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The strut mounts can fail, causing a clunking noise and reduced handling. Symptoms include uneven tire wear and excessive body roll.
    * Solution: Replace the strut mounts.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Sway Bar Link Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The sway bar links can fail, causing a rattling noise and reduced handling. Symptoms include unusual noises during turns and increased body roll.
    * Solution: Replace the sway bar links.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Alternator Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The alternator can fail, causing the battery to drain and the vehicle to stall. Symptoms include dimming lights, electrical problems, and difficulty starting the vehicle.
    * Solution: Replace the alternator.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Starter Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The starter can fail, causing the vehicle to not start. Symptoms include a clicking noise when attempting to start the engine and no engine cranking.
    * Solution: Replace the starter.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Sunroof Seal Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The sunroof seal can fail, allowing water to leak into the cabin. Symptoms include water dripping from the sunroof and a musty smell in the interior.
    * Solution: Replace the sunroof seal.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Antenna Base Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The antenna base can fail, causing the antenna to fall off. Symptoms include no reception and a loose antenna.
    * Solution: Replace the antenna base.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Climate Control Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The climate control system can fail, affecting the ability to adjust cabin temperature. Symptoms include malfunctioning AC or heater and inconsistent airflow.
    * Solution: Diagnose the specific issue and replace the faulty component (e.g., blower motor, compressor).
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Power Window Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The power window motors can fail, causing the power windows to become inoperable. Symptoms include windows that do not move or move slowly.
    * Solution: Replace the failed window motor.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

The 2002 Hyundai Elantra is generally affordable to maintain, with most issues being relatively minor and inexpensive to fix. However, the engine and transmission can have more serious problems that can be costly to repair. It is important to perform regular maintenance and inspections to help prevent potential issues.
