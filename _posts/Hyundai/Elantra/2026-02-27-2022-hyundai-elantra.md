# 2022 Hyundai Elantra Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2022 Hyundai Elantra is a stylish, feature-packed compact sedan offering strong fuel economy and a comfortable ride. However, prospective buyers should review known owner complaints concerning powertrain operation, electrical accessories, and safety systems before purchasing.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L 4-Cylinder Engine

This naturally aspirated 2.0-liter four-cylinder engine produces approximately 147 horsepower and 132 lb-ft of torque, serving as the standard powerplant for base trims. It generally provides dependable daily commuting performance, though isolated owner reports note potential fuel dilution concerns.

**Fuel Dilution in Engine Oil**
- **Symptoms:** The engine oil level rises significantly above the full mark on the dipstick and emits a strong odor of raw gasoline.
- **Solution:** Have the vehicle inspected by a certified technician to diagnose fuel system performance and replace contaminated engine oil.

### 1.6L Turbocharged 4-Cylinder Engine

Found in performance-oriented trims, this turbocharged 1.6-liter engine delivers spirited acceleration and engaging driving dynamics for enthusiasts.

### 1.6L Hybrid 4-Cylinder Engine

This hybrid powertrain pairs a 1.6-liter four-cylinder engine with an electric motor to maximize fuel efficiency for high-mileage drivers.

## Transmission

### Intelligent Variable Transmission (IVT)

The Intelligent Variable Transmission (IVT) is paired with the standard 2.0L engine to optimize fuel economy during daily driving.

**Shifting Irregularities and Hesitation**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers experience delayed or sluggish acceleration, gear slipping, shaking, shuddering, or harsh shifting.
- **Solution:** Consult a dealership or professional mechanic to inspect transmission control modules and fluid condition.

### 7-Speed Dual-Clutch Transmission

This 7-speed dual-clutch automatic transmission delivers quick, sporty gear changes in select performance configurations.

### 6-Speed Manual Transmission

Available on select enthusiast models, this traditional 6-speed manual transmission gives drivers direct control over gear selection.

## Electrical

### Electrical System & Horn

Electrical system concerns primarily center around intermittent infotainment display failures and unexpected horn malfunctions.

**Inoperable Horn**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle horn stops working unexpectedly, preventing the driver from alerting nearby motorists.
- **Solution:** Inspect the horn fuse, relay, and wiring harness, and replace the horn assembly if necessary.

**Infotainment Display Blanking**
- **Symptoms:** The infotainment display screen goes completely black, disabling navigation and backup camera functions.
- **Solution:** Reset or update the infotainment software, or replace faulty display hardware if issues persist.

## Safety

### Seat Belts

Safety equipment generally performs well in crash testing, though owners have noted recurring complaints regarding seat belt hardware.

**Seat Belt Latch and Retractor Concerns**
- **Symptoms:** Occupants report concerns regarding seat belt latching mechanisms and retraction timing during normal use.
- **Solution:** Have seat belt assemblies inspected and serviced by an authorized dealer.

### Forward Collision Avoidance

Advanced driver assistance features provide added security but can trigger diagnostic alerts requiring sensor calibration.

**Forward Safety System Warning**
- **Symptoms:** Warning messages regarding the forward safety system illuminate alongside diagnostic trouble codes.
- **Solution:** Clear diagnostic codes and recalibrate forward-facing sensors and cameras at a qualified service center.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V123000: Seat Belts:Front](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Hyundai&model=Elantra&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** In the event of a crash, the front passenger-side seat belt pretensioner may explode upon deployment. An exploding seat belt pretensioner can project metal fragments into the vehicle, striking vehicle occupants and resulting in injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the seat belt pretensioners, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V218000: Seat Belts:Front](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Hyundai&model=Elantra&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** In the event of a crash, the front driver and passenger-side seat belt pretensioners may explode upon deployment. Exploding seat belt pretensioners can project metal fragments into the vehicle, strike vehicle occupants, and result in injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the seat belt pretensioners, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V354000: Seat Belts:Front](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Hyundai&model=Elantra&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** In the event of a crash, the front driver-side and/or passenger-side seat belt pretensioners may explode upon deployment. An exploding seat belt pretensioner can project metal fragments into the vehicle, strike vehicle occupants, and result in injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will secure the seat belt pretensioner(s) with a cap, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V632000: Air Bags:Frontal](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Hyundai&model=Elantra&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** The emblem on the cover of the driver's front air bag may not have been welded properly, which can result in the emblem detaching upon deployment. A detached air bag cover emblem may strike a vehicle occupant, which can increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the air bag, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V879000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Hyundai&model=Elantra&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 111, "Rear Visibility." A rearview image that does not display reduces the driver's visibility and increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the rearview camera, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2022 Hyundai Elantra is an attractive option for used car buyers seeking modern technology and great fuel efficiency. While most owners report a satisfactory driving experience, buyers should thoroughly test drive the vehicle to check transmission smoothness, verify that all electrical features and the horn operate correctly, and ensure safety warning systems are fully functional.
