**Introduction to the 2003 Hyundai Elantra**

The 2003 Hyundai Elantra is a compact sedan that offers a comfortable and reliable driving experience. It is powered by a 2.0-liter DOHC four-cylinder engine that produces 138 horsepower and 136 lb-ft of torque. This engine is paired with either a five-speed manual or four-speed automatic transmission. The Elantra is praised for its spacious interior, affordable price, and fuel efficiency. However, it has received some criticism for its lack of performance and handling.

**Engine**

**2.0L DOHC Four-Cylinder Engine**

* **Leaking Head Gasket:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Oil or coolant leaking from the area where the head gasket meets the engine block.
    * Solution: Replace the head gasket.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**Five-Speed Manual Transmission**

* **Synchronizer Wear:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears, especially into second or third gear.
    * Solution: Replace the synchronizer rings.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Four-Speed Automatic Transmission**

* **Torque Converter Clutch Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Transmission slipping or shuddering while driving, especially when shifting gears.
    * Solution: Replace the torque converter clutch.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $1,500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Strut Mount Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Clunking or rattling noise when driving over bumps.
    * Solution: Replace the strut mounts.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $300+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Window Regulator Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Difficulty rolling up or down the windows.
    * Solution: Replace the window regulator motor or switch.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Alternator Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Car not starting or battery dying.
    * Solution: Replace the alternator.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $700+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Rusting of Exhaust System:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Corrosion on the exhaust system, leading to holes or leaks.
    * Solution: Replace the rusted parts of the exhaust system.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Climate Control Malfunction:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Difficulty regulating temperature inside the vehicle.
    * Solution: Replace the climate control module or blower motor.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Power Window Switch Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Buttons on the power window switch no longer working.
    * Solution: Replace the power window switch.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

Overall, the 2003 Hyundai Elantra is a reliable and affordable vehicle. However, it is not without its issues. The most common problems include engine leaks, transmission failures, and electrical malfunctions. The severity of these issues can vary, and the cost of repairs can range from minor to significant. It is important to consider these potential issues when making a decision about purchasing a used 2003 Hyundai Elantra.
