**Introduction:**

The 1996 Hyundai Elantra is a compact sedan powered by a single engine option. The 1.8L SOHC 4-cylinder engine produces 125 horsepower and 133 lb-ft of torque. It is offered with a standard 5-speed manual transmission or an optional 4-speed automatic transmission. The Elantra is praised for its affordability, fuel efficiency, and spacious interior. However, it has received criticism for its lackluster performance and bland styling.

**Engine**

**1.8L SOHC 4-cylinder**

* **Excessive oil consumption:** Oil consumption may exceed 1 quart per 1,000 miles. This can be caused by worn piston rings or valve seals. Repair involves replacing the affected parts.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**5-speed Manual**

* **Hard shifting:** Difficulty shifting gears may be experienced. This can be caused by worn synchros or a problem with the shift linkage. Repair involves replacing the affected parts.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**4-speed Automatic**

* **Slipping gears:** The transmission may slip out of gear during acceleration or deceleration. This can be caused by worn clutch plates or a problem with the valve body. Repair involves rebuilding or replacing the transmission.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Strut failure:** The front struts may fail prematurely, leading to a loss of handling and stability. Symptoms include knocking noises and uneven tire wear. Repair involves replacing the struts.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $700+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Battery failure:** The battery may fail prematurely, resulting in difficulty starting the vehicle. This can be caused by a faulty voltage regulator or a problem with the charging system. Repair involves replacing the battery or diagnosing and repairing the electrical system.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Door handle failure:** The exterior door handles may break or become loose. This can be caused by faulty springs or wear and tear. Repair involves replacing the door handles.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Window regulator failure:** The window regulators may fail, causing the windows to become stuck or inoperable. Repair involves replacing the regulators.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **HVAC blower motor failure:** The blower motor for the HVAC system may fail, resulting in no airflow from the vents. Repair involves replacing the blower motor.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $300+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 1996 Hyundai Elantra is a reliable and affordable vehicle, but it has some common issues that can affect its performance, reliability, and comfort. The engine and transmission issues are the most severe and expensive to repair, while the suspension and electrical issues are less common and less costly. Overall, the Elantra is a good value for the price, but it is important to be aware of its potential issues before purchasing.
