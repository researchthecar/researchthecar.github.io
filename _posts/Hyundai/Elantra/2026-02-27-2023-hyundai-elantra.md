# 2023 Hyundai Elantra Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2023 Hyundai Elantra brings striking styling, impressive fuel efficiency, and a robust technology suite to the compact sedan market. For prospective used-car buyers, it offers a comfortable ride and modern safety features, though shoppers should carefully evaluate powertrain performance and electrical behavior during a pre-purchase test drive.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Naturally Aspirated I4

The standard 2.0-liter four-cylinder engine produces roughly 147 horsepower and 132 lb-ft of torque, providing adequate daily acceleration and strong fuel economy. It serves as the volume-selling powerplant across base and mid-tier trims.

**Engine Stalling and Power Interruption**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have experienced unexpected engine sputtering and stalling while slowing down or maintaining city speeds.
- **Solution:** Diagnostic evaluation by a certified technician is required to check fuel delivery, sensors, and powertrain control modules.

**Unintended Neutral Shift**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may unexpectedly lose drive engagement or drop into neutral while in motion, requiring the driver to pull over and restart.
- **Solution:** Software updates or transmission control module inspection may be necessary.

### 1.6L Turbocharged I4

Found in sportier trims like the N Line, this turbocharged 1.6-liter engine generates around 201 horsepower and 195 lb-ft of torque, offering significantly punchier performance for enthusiastic drivers.

### 1.6L Hybrid I4

The hybrid powertrain pairs a 1.6-liter four-cylinder engine with an electric motor to deliver exceptional fuel efficiency and smooth low-speed electric cruising, though complex transitions occasionally lead to complaints.

**Hybrid Powertrain Hesitation**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers note persistent jerking, harsh downshifts, and rough transitions between electric motor and gasoline engine operation when slowing to a stop.
- **Solution:** Have the dealership check for hybrid system software updates and inspect actuator components.

## Transmission

### Intelligent Variable Transmission (IVT)

The continuously variable automatic transmission (marketed as an IVT) is designed to maximize fuel economy during daily commutes while maintaining responsive step-off ratios.

**Transmission Slippage and Engagement Delay**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers report hesitation or slipping when attempting to accelerate from a stop or while shifting gears.
- **Solution:** Inspect transmission fluid condition, wiring harnesses, and control software for faults.

## Electrical

### Infotainment and Display Screen

Manages vehicle connectivity, infotainment, and driver convenience features, though occasional software glitches have been reported.

**Infotainment System Reboot and Blackout**
- **Symptoms:** The dashboard touch screen or infotainment display intermittently goes completely black, resets, or freezes while driving.
- **Solution:** Perform a system software update or replace faulty head unit hardware if symptoms persist.

## Safety

### Advanced Driver Assistance Systems (ADAS)

Includes automated safety features designed to protect occupants, though sensor sensitivity can occasionally trigger false warnings or unexpected interventions.

**Unintended Emergency Braking**
- **Symptoms:** The forward collision avoidance or rear cross-traffic system may erroneously detect non-existent hazards, causing abrupt and unprompted emergency braking.
- **Solution:** Calibrate camera and radar sensors through an authorized service center.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V632000: Air Bags:Frontal](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Hyundai&model=Elantra&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** The emblem on the cover of the driver's front air bag may not have been welded properly, which can result in the emblem detaching upon deployment. A detached air bag cover emblem may strike a vehicle occupant, which can increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the air bag, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V526000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission:Fluid/Lubricant:Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Hyundai&model=Elantra&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** An electric oil pump assembly that overheats increases the risk of a vehicle fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the electric oil pump controller, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2023 Hyundai Elantra is a stylish and feature-rich compact car that appeals to budget-conscious commuters and efficiency seekers. While most owners enjoy trouble-free driving, prospective buyers should thoroughly test the transmission engagement, hybrid powertrain transition smoothness, and driver-assistance braking behaviors to ensure everything operates as expected.
