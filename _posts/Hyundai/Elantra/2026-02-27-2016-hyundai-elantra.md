# 2016 Hyundai Elantra Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2016 Hyundai Elantra, buyers will find a popular, compact sedan that offers respectable fuel economy and decent everyday utility. However, owner reports and complaint data highlight a few recurring pain points, particularly regarding engine reliability, sudden stalling, and minor body or latch concerns. While many owners enjoy trouble-free commutes, prospective buyers should carefully review service histories and inspect critical mechanical components before finalizing a purchase.

---

## Engine

### 1.8L 4-Cylinder Engine

The 1.8-liter four-cylinder engine produces approximately 145 horsepower and 130 lb-ft of torque, offering adequate power for daily commuting. Routine maintenance and oil level checks are vital to ensure long-term mechanical durability.

### 2.0L 4-Cylinder Engine

The 2.0-liter four-cylinder engine generates around 173 horsepower, providing brisk acceleration for the compact sedan. Buyers should monitor oil consumption closely, as neglected maintenance or internal wear can lead to serious mechanical trouble.

**Engine Stalling and Power Loss**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may unexpectedly lose motive power or stall completely while driving at various speeds, accompanied by warning lights on the dashboard.
- **Solution:** Diagnose the underlying fuel delivery, electrical, or sensor faults; replace faulty components as required by professional inspection.

**Excessive Oil Consumption and Knocking**
- **Symptoms:** Low oil levels, illuminated oil pressure warning lights, and an audible knocking noise coming from the lower end of the engine, often with metal debris found in the oil filter.
- **Solution:** Requires professional mechanical inspection; severely damaged engines may necessitate complete engine replacement or rebuilding.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

The six-speed automatic transmission channels power smoothly to the front wheels under normal operating conditions. Prospective buyers should test drive the vehicle thoroughly to verify crisp, hesitation-free shifting.

**Transmission Slippage and Drivetrain Instability**
- **Symptoms:** Sudden loss of hydraulic pressure causing the automatic transmission to slip out of gear while maintaining steady highway speeds.
- **Solution:** Inspect transmission fluid condition and internal hydraulic controls; service or overhaul the transmission assembly as needed.

### 6-Speed Manual Transmission

The traditional six-speed manual transmission gives drivers direct control over gear selection. It generally exhibits solid mechanical durability when paired with regular clutch maintenance.

## Brakes

### Brake Lines and Hardware

Brake components require periodic inspection for rust accumulation and hydraulic leaks, especially in regions that utilize road salt during winter months.

**Brake Line Corrosion and Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Abrupt loss of brake function resulting from rusted-out or ruptured brake lines leaking brake fluid.
- **Solution:** Inspect brake lines and backing plates for corrosion; replace compromised metal brake lines and bleed the hydraulic system.

## Exterior

### Trunk and Door Latches

Interior latches and door mechanisms can occasionally bind or fail, requiring mechanical adjustments or hardware replacements.

**Latch and Lock Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** Doors or trunk lids fail to latch properly upon closing, requiring repeated slamming or leaving the rear cargo area stuck shut.
- **Solution:** Adjust, lubricate, or replace faulty latch mechanisms and door linkage assemblies.

## Safety

### Air Bag System

Safety systems generally protect occupants well, but warning light illuminations and sensor faults require prompt professional diagnosis.

**Air Bag Warning Light and Deployment Concerns**
- **Symptoms:** The airbag warning light remains illuminated on the instrument cluster, or side airbags deploy during an impact while front airbags fail to deploy.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified technician scan the supplemental restraint system (SRS) diagnostic trouble codes and replace faulty sensors or modules.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V146000: Latches/Locks/Linkages:Trunk Lid:Latch](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Hyundai&model=Elantra&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** The trunk latch may become damaged, preventing the opening of the trunk from the inside. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 401, "Internal Trunk Release." A person inside the trunk compartment may become trapped, increasing their risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the trunk latch base sub-assembly, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V307000: Air Bags:Frontal:Driver Side:Inflator Module](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Hyundai&model=Elantra&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** The driver's air bag inflator may rupture during deployment. An air bag inflator that ruptures may result in sharp metal fragments striking the driver or passengers, increasing the risk of injury or death.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the air bag inflator, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2016 Hyundai Elantra remains an appealing option for budget-conscious used car buyers due to its comfortable ride and practical features. However, prospective owners must remain vigilant about potential engine oil consumption, sudden stalling reports, and brake line corrosion. Prioritizing a vehicle with a documented service history and conducting a pre-purchase inspection by a trusted mechanic will greatly reduce ownership risks.
