**Introduction:**

The 2022 Hyundai Elantra is a compact sedan that offers a refined driving experience, impressive fuel economy, and a spacious interior. It is available with a 2.0-liter four-cylinder engine or a 1.6-liter four-cylinder engine. The 2.0-liter engine produces 147 horsepower and 132 lb-ft of torque, while the 1.6-liter engine produces 128 horsepower and 114 lb-ft of torque. The Elantra is paired with either a six-speed automatic transmission, an eight-speed automatic transmission, or a continuously variable transmission (CVT).

Overall, the Elantra is praised for its comfortable interior, fuel-efficient performance, and advanced safety features. However, it has also been criticized for its lack of driving dynamics and slightly cramped rear seats.

**Engine**

**2.0-Liter Four-Cylinder Engine**

* **Knocking or pinging noise under acceleration:**
    * Description/Symptoms: A rattling or knocking sound may be heard when accelerating, particularly at low speeds.
    * Solution: This issue may be caused by carbon buildup on the valves or fuel injectors. Regular cleaning and maintenance can help resolve the problem.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $200+
    * Recalls: None Found

**1.6-Liter Four-Cylinder Engine**

* **No Issues Found**

**Transmission**

**Six-Speed Automatic Transmission**

* **Jerking or hesitation when shifting gears:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The transmission may hesitate or jerk while shifting gears, particularly when changing from a lower gear to a higher gear.
    * Solution: This issue may be caused by faulty solenoid valves within the transmission. Replacing the solenoids can resolve the problem.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Eight-Speed Automatic Transmission**

* **Delay in engaging gears:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The transmission may take several seconds to engage gears after starting the vehicle or when shifting from park to drive.
    * Solution: Updating the transmission control module software can address this issue.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 (Software Update Only)
    * Recalls: NHTSA Campaign Number 22V531000

**Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)**

* **Slipping or loss of power:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The CVT may slip or lose power during acceleration, causing the vehicle to hesitate or lose speed.
    * Solution: Replacing the CVT fluid and filter, or rebuilding the CVT can resolve this issue.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Excessive body roll or sway:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The vehicle may lean or sway excessively when cornering or driving on uneven roads.
    * Solution: Installing upgraded sway bars or struts can improve the handling and stability of the vehicle.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Battery drain:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The vehicle's battery may drain prematurely, especially when the vehicle is not in use.
    * Solution: Identifying and replacing faulty electrical components, such as the alternator or starter, can resolve this issue.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Condensation or water droplets inside taillights:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Water droplets may accumulate inside the taillights, causing condensation or fogging.
    * Solution: Replacing the taillights with sealed units can prevent the issue.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $300+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Rattling or squeaking noise from dashboard or center console:**
    * Description/Symptoms: A rattling or squeaking sound may be heard from the dashboard or center console, particularly on rough roads.
    * Solution: Applying foam or sound deadening material to the affected areas can reduce the noise.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $200+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

The 2022 Hyundai Elantra is an overall reliable vehicle. However, some reported issues include knocking or pinging noises from the 2.0-liter engine, transmission hesitation or jerking, suspension sway, electrical drain, condensation in taillights, and rattling or squeaking noises from the interior. The severity of these issues varies, but most can be resolved at a reasonable cost.
