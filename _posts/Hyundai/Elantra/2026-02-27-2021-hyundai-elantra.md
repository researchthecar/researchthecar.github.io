# 2021 Hyundai Elantra Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2021 Hyundai Elantra brings striking styling, impressive fuel efficiency, and a robust technology suite to the compact sedan market. As a used vehicle, it generally offers solid commuter-friendly value, but prospective buyers should review specific mechanical and technological feedback. Owner reports highlight occasional powertrain concerns and driver-assist system anomalies that warrant a thorough pre-purchase inspection.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L 4-Cylinder Engine

The standard 2.0-liter naturally aspirated four-cylinder engine produces 147 horsepower and 132 lb-ft of torque, mated to an Intelligent Variable Transmission (IVT). It serves as an efficient, reliable daily driver powerplant for base trims, though owners have occasionally reported unexpected stalling incidents.

**Engine Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle unexpectedly stalls while driving, prompting dashboard warning lights and loss of motive power.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified mechanic inspect diagnostic trouble codes, fuel delivery systems, and software calibration.

### 1.6L Turbocharged 4-Cylinder Engine

The turbocharged 1.6-liter four-cylinder powers sportier variants like the N Line, delivering 201 horsepower and 195 lb-ft of torque. It offers spirited performance for enthusiast drivers while maintaining reasonable overall efficiency.

## Transmission

### Intelligent Variable Transmission (IVT)

The IVT utilizes a chain-belt design that mimics traditional gear steps to provide smooth acceleration and excellent fuel economy on standard trims.

### 7-Speed Dual-Clutch Transmission (DCT)

Found on performance-oriented trims, the 7-speed dual-clutch automatic provides lightning-fast shifts but can occasionally exhibit low-speed shudder or shifting irregularities noted by owners.

**Unintended Neutral Shift and Shuddering**
- **Symptoms:** Transmission slips into neutral unexpectedly during acceleration, causes high revs, or exhibits rough shifting and hesitation.
- **Solution:** Update transmission control module (TCM) software or inspect clutch actuators and mechanical components.

## Exterior

### Horn and Body Electronics

Electrical systems govern numerous convenience and safety accessories, with owner complaints centering primarily on premature horn failures.

**Horn Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle horn stops working intermittently or permanently due to blown fuses or internal horn assembly failures.
- **Solution:** Replace the defective horn assembly and verify fuse block integrity.

## Safety

### Forward Collision-Avoidance System

Advanced safety features enhance driver protection, but sensor calibrations can occasionally lead to phantom braking events or false positive alerts.

**Unexpected Braking and False Warnings**
- **Symptoms:** The forward collision system activates emergency braking unexpectedly or triggers warning lights without an imminent hazard.
- **Solution:** Check camera and radar sensors for blockages or misalignment, and ensure sensor software is up to date.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V796000: Seat Belts:Front](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Hyundai&model=Elantra&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** In the event of a crash, the front driver-side and/or passenger-side seat belt pretensioner(s) may explode upon deployment. An exploding seat belt pretensioner could project metal fragments into the vehicle, striking vehicle occupants and resulting in injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the seat belt pretensioners, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V00M000: Visibility:Windshield](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Hyundai&model=Elantra&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** During manufacturing, the windshield may not have been properly bonded to the vehicle, allowing it to detach in a crash. A windshield that detaches from a vehicle during a crash can increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will remove and reinstall the front windshield panel, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V302000: Seats:Front Assembly:Recliner](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Hyundai&model=Elantra&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** An improperly welded seat back recliner could separate from the seat back frame during a crash, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and if necessary, replace the seat back frame assembly, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V218000: Seat Belts:Front](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Hyundai&model=Elantra&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** In the event of a crash, the front driver and passenger-side seat belt pretensioners may explode upon deployment. Exploding seat belt pretensioners can project metal fragments into the vehicle, strike vehicle occupants, and result in injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the seat belt pretensioners, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V354000: Seat Belts:Front](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Hyundai&model=Elantra&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** In the event of a crash, the front driver-side and/or passenger-side seat belt pretensioners may explode upon deployment. An exploding seat belt pretensioner can project metal fragments into the vehicle, strike vehicle occupants, and result in injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will secure the seat belt pretensioner(s) with a cap, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V879000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Hyundai&model=Elantra&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 111, "Rear Visibility." A rearview image that does not display reduces the driver's visibility and increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the rearview camera, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2021 Hyundai Elantra is an attractive, feature-rich compact sedan that delivers strong value on the used market. While most mechanical systems prove dependable, buyers should carefully test the transmission behavior—especially on DCT-equipped models—and verify that all safety sensors and electronics operate correctly during a pre-purchase test drive.
