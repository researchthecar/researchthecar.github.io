Here's a breakdown of common issues and complaints for the 2015 Hyundai Elantra, organized as requested.

The 2015 Hyundai Elantra is a compact car known for its fuel efficiency, affordability, and spacious interior. It's available with a 1.8-liter inline-4 engine producing 145 horsepower and 130 lb-ft of torque. Some consumers praise its value and features for the price, while others find the ride quality and engine performance to be less refined compared to competitors. The 2015 Elantra came with a 6-speed manual or 6-speed automatic transmission. Common complaints revolve around engine issues, interior build quality, and some electrical glitches.

**Engine**

**1.8L Nu Engine (G4NB)**

*   **Engine Knocking/Ticking**
    *   Description/Symptoms: A knocking or ticking noise coming from the engine, especially during cold starts or acceleration. This can be caused by worn connecting rod bearings, piston slap, or issues with the hydraulic lash adjusters.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis by a mechanic is crucial to pinpoint the exact cause. Depending on the severity, the repair can range from replacing the lash adjusters to rebuilding or replacing the engine.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $1000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Excessive Oil Consumption**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Noticeably low oil levels between oil changes, blue smoke from the exhaust, and/or a burning oil smell. This can be due to worn piston rings or valve stem seals.
    *   Solution: A compression test and leak-down test can help determine the source of the oil consumption. The repair might involve replacing piston rings, valve stem seals, or, in severe cases, an engine rebuild or replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $3000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**6-Speed Automatic (A6MF1/A6GF1)**

*   **Rough Shifting/Hesitation**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Harsh or jerky shifts, especially between 1st and 2nd gear, or a delay in shifting. Can be caused by worn valve body components, low transmission fluid, or failing solenoids.
    *   Solution: Start by checking the transmission fluid level and condition. A transmission flush and filter replacement might help. If the problem persists, a mechanic might need to diagnose and replace the valve body or solenoids.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $1500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Transmission Slipping**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Engine revving without a corresponding increase in vehicle speed, especially on inclines. This indicates internal wear within the transmission.
    *   Solution: If slipping is present, a full transmission rebuild or replacement is likely needed.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $2500 - $4500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Rear Shock Absorber Leaks/Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Bouncy ride, excessive body roll when cornering, and visible fluid leaking from the rear shock absorbers.
    *   Solution: Replace the rear shock absorbers. It is recommended to replace them in pairs for balanced performance.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Intermittent Radio/Infotainment System Malfunctions**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Radio turning off and on randomly, screen freezing, or Bluetooth connectivity issues.
    *   Solution: Check the connections to the radio and infotainment unit. If the problem persists, the unit might need to be replaced or reprogrammed.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Airbag Warning Light**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The airbag warning light stays illuminated on the dashboard. This can indicate a problem with the airbag system, including faulty sensors, wiring issues, or a malfunctioning airbag control module.
    *   Solution: A diagnostic scan is needed to determine the fault code. The repair could involve replacing a sensor, repairing wiring, or replacing the airbag control module.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $1000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Headlight Condensation**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Moisture buildup inside the headlight housings.
    *   Solution: Remove the headlights and dry them out. Ensure the seals are intact or replace them. Some owners drill a small drain hole.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 - $300
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Sun Visor Sagging/Breaking**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Sun visors that do not stay in the up position, or the internal components breaking.
    *   Solution: Replace the sun visors.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $150
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Bluetooth connectivity issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Bluetooth pairing fails, or drops while in use.
    *   Solution: Check for a software update, or replace the bluetooth module.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0-$300
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2015 Hyundai Elantra has a mix of potential issues, with engine and transmission concerns being the most significant in terms of cost and severity. Oil consumption and transmission problems can lead to costly repairs. Electrical glitches, while less severe, can be annoying. Potential buyers should pay close attention to engine noises and transmission performance during a pre-purchase inspection.
