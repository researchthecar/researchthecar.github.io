# 2017 Hyundai Elantra Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2017 Hyundai Elantra is a compact sedan offering solid fuel economy, comfortable ride quality, and a user-friendly interior. While it is generally a practical daily commuter, used-car buyers should inspect the vehicle carefully for known problem areas. Complaints predominantly center around engine performance, door latch and lock mechanisms, and exterior paint durability.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L 4-Cylinder

The 2.0-liter naturally aspirated four-cylinder engine produces approximately 147 horsepower and 132 lb-ft of torque, providing adequate performance for daily commuting. It is generally dependable, though owners have reported occasional stalling and misfire issues.

**Engine Stalling and Loss of Motive Power**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicles may experience sudden engine stalls or total loss of motive power while driving in traffic, often without prior warning lights.
- **Solution:** Diagnostic inspection of the fuel system, ignition components, or timing assembly is required. Repairs depend on the root cause.

**Engine Misfire**
- **Symptoms:** Engine bogs down, runs rough, or illuminates the check engine light due to cylinder misfires.
- **Solution:** Inspect and replace worn ignition coils, spark plugs, or faulty fuel injectors as needed.

### 1.4L Turbocharged 4-Cylinder

The 1.4-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine, paired with an Eco trim, prioritizes fuel efficiency with around 128 horsepower and 156 lb-ft of torque. It offers responsive low-end power but requires regular maintenance to prevent turbo and direct-injection buildup.

### 1.6L Turbocharged 4-Cylinder

The 1.6-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine powers sportier trims, pushing roughly 201 horsepower and 195 lb-ft of torque. It delivers lively acceleration for driving enthusiasts, though prospective buyers should verify proper service history.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Manual Transmission

The traditional 6-speed manual transmission offers direct shift control and straightforward mechanical reliability for base trims.

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

The conventional 6-speed automatic transmission delivers smooth, reliable gear shifts and pairs well with the base 2.0L engine.

### 7-Speed Dual-Clutch Transmission

The 7-speed dual-clutch transmission provides quick gear changes for turbocharged models, though buyers should test for smooth engagement at low speeds.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

The braking system provides adequate stopping power under normal conditions, but mechanical sticking in calipers has been reported.

**Brake Sticking or Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Brake pedal feel becomes unresponsive or brake calipers stick, leading to increased stopping distances or localized overheating.
- **Solution:** Inspect brake pads, rotors, and calipers; replace seized calipers or worn hydraulic components.

## Steering

### Steering Column

Steering components generally provide precise control, though column bearing and motor connector wear can develop over time.

**Steering Play and Binding**
- **Symptoms:** Steering feels loose, binds, or becomes difficult to turn, sometimes accompanied by noises when turning the wheel.
- **Solution:** Inspect steering components and replace worn intermediate shaft bearings or electric power steering connectors as necessary.

## Exterior

### Door Latches and Locks

Cabin features are generally durable, but door lock actuators and latches are frequent points of failure.

**Door Latch and Actuator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Doors fail to unlock or open properly from either the inside or outside, creating potential passenger exit issues.
- **Solution:** Replace the faulty door latch assembly or power door lock actuator.

### Exterior Paint

Body panels hold up well against structural rust, but paint adhesion issues have been widely noted by owners.

**Paint Peeling and Corrosion**
- **Symptoms:** Clear coat and paint begin to peel or chip prematurely on horizontal surfaces like the hood, roof, and areas around door frames.
- **Solution:** Professional repainting or touch-up of affected body panels is required.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V213000: Steering:Electric Power Assist System](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Hyundai&model=Elantra&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** Loss of power steering assist would require higher steering effort, especially at lower speeds, which may increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Hyundai will notify owners, and dealers will replace the EPS motor, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V956000: Air Bags:Frontal:Driver Side:Inflator Module](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Hyundai&model=Elantra&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** In these vehicles, the end seal for the driver's frontal air bag inflator may not have been properly installed, possibly resulting in reduced inflation of the frontal air bag in the event of a crash. In the event of a crash, an air bag that does not inflate fully increases the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Hyundai will notify owners, and dealers will replace the driver's frontal air bag module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V063000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Power Assist:Hydraulic](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Hyundai&model=Elantra&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** A loss of power brake assist can lengthen the distance needed to stop the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Hyundai has notified owners, and dealers will replace the brake system booster assembly, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V232000: Air Bags:Frontal](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Hyundai&model=Elantra&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** In these vehicles, the driver's frontal air bag may be missing a chemical enhancer required for the second stage air bag deployment, resulting in the second stage not deploying in certain crashes. The primary stage will deploy as intended.
- **What to do:** Hyundai will notify owners, and dealers will replace the driver's frontal air bag module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2017 Hyundai Elantra is a feature-packed and affordable used compact car, but buyers should conduct a thorough pre-purchase inspection. Pay close attention to engine idle quality, test all door locks and latches, and inspect the body for any signs of premature paint peeling. Ensuring a comprehensive service history will help mitigate potential ownership surprises.
