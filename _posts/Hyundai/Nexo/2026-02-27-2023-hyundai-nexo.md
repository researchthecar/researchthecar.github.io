# 2023 Hyundai Nexo Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2023 Hyundai Nexo is an advanced hydrogen fuel cell electric vehicle available exclusively in select regions with hydrogen fueling infrastructure. While it offers an eco-friendly driving experience and innovative technology, prospective used-car buyers must carefully evaluate local fueling availability and potential propulsion system reports before purchasing.

---

## Engine

### 161-hp Hydrogen Fuel Cell Electric Motor

The Nexo features a 95-kW fuel cell stack paired with a 161-horsepower electric motor producing 291 lb-ft of torque. This alternative-fuel powertrain provides smooth, zero-emission operation but requires careful consideration of regional hydrogen fueling infrastructure.

**Fuel and Propulsion System Limitations**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have reported material operability limitations and restricted real-world usability related to hydrogen fuel availability and propulsion system constraints.
- **Solution:** Verify local hydrogen station reliability and infrastructure before committing to ownership.

**Hydrogen Leak Following Service**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have experienced hydrogen leaks after dealer-performed recall service related to the thermally-activated pressure relief device.
- **Solution:** Have the fuel storage and pressure relief systems inspected immediately by a certified technician if a leak or odor is detected.

## Transmission

### Single-Speed Reduction Gear Automatic

A direct-drive single-speed transmission delivers power seamlessly to the front wheels without traditional gear shifts. This system is generally trouble-free and requires minimal mechanical maintenance.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

Issues reported with the braking system primarily involve sporadic electronic alerts rather than mechanical brake failure, though any warning messages should be inspected.

**Intermittent Emergency Brake Application Warnings**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have experienced sudden, brief emergency brake warning messages and automatic application alerts on the instrument cluster that disappear after a few seconds.
- **Solution:** Have the vehicle's collision avoidance and braking sensor systems scanned for diagnostic trouble codes by a qualified professional.

---

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V761000: Fuel System, Other:Storage:Tank Assembly:Pressure Relief Devices](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Hyundai&model=Nexo&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** The thermally activated pressure relief device (TPRD) may break and cause a hydrogen gas leak, which can result in a fire while parked. A vehicle fire increases the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the TPRD, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2023 Hyundai Nexo is a cutting-edge fuel cell vehicle that appeals to eco-conscious buyers in areas with established hydrogen infrastructure. However, used-car shoppers should thoroughly research local fueling station stability, verify completion of safety recalls, and carefully inspect the propulsion system before finalizing a purchase.
