# 2020 Hyundai Nexo Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2020 Hyundai Nexo is an innovative hydrogen fuel cell electric vehicle offering zero-emission driving and distinct styling. While it provides an alternative powertrain option for eco-conscious drivers, prospective buyers should carefully weigh the complexities of hydrogen infrastructure and specific owner-reported propulsion concerns before purchasing.

---

## Engine

### 95 kW Fuel Cell Stack with 135 kW Electric Motor

The 2020 Nexo utilizes a 95 kW hydrogen fuel cell stack paired with a 135 kW electric motor, producing 161 horsepower and 291 lb-ft of torque. This hydrogen propulsion system offers smooth, quiet acceleration but requires specialized maintenance and access to limited hydrogen refueling infrastructure.

**Fuel Cell Stack and Sudden Power Loss**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have reported experiencing unexpected and sudden power loss while operating the vehicle, particularly when driving uphill, on highways, or while attempting to overtake other cars.
- **Solution:** Diagnostic evaluation by an authorized Hyundai hydrogen service facility is required to inspect the fuel cell system and associated components.

**Accelerator Pedal Unresponsiveness**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may fail to respond appropriately to driver inputs from the accelerator pedal, creating dangerous driving situations.
- **Solution:** Have the fuel cell system and accelerator pedal control modules inspected and serviced by qualified technicians.

## Transmission

### Single-Speed Reduction Gear

Power is sent to the front wheels via a single-speed reduction gear transmission standard on all Nexo models. This direct-drive unit operates smoothly without traditional gear shifting and generally requires minimal mechanical maintenance.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V213000: Electrical System:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Hyundai&model=Nexo&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** Unintended vehicle movement increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Hyundai will notify owners, and dealers will reprogram the RSPA software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V748000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Power Assist:Electric:Control Module:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Hyundai&model=Nexo&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** Reduced braking power could lengthen the distance required to stop the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Hyundai will notify owners, and dealers will update the IEB software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V258000: Fuel System, Other:Delivery](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Hyundai&model=Nexo&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** A hydrogen gas leak can increase the risk of a fire while parked or driving.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the hydrogen gas detection sensors, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V761000: Fuel System, Other:Storage:Tank Assembly:Pressure Relief Devices](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Hyundai&model=Nexo&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** The thermally activated pressure relief device (TPRD) may break and cause a hydrogen gas leak, which can result in a fire while parked. A vehicle fire increases the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the TPRD, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2020 Hyundai Nexo presents a unique ownership proposition centered around hydrogen fuel cell technology. Due to owner-reported concerns regarding sudden power loss and the limited availability of hydrogen fueling stations, buyers should thoroughly research local infrastructure and service support before committing to a purchase.
