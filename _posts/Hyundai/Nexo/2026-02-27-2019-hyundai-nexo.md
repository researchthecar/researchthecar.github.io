# 2019 Hyundai Nexo

### Overview
The 2019 Hyundai Nexo represents a significant step forward in hydrogen fuel cell technology, offering a zero-emission driving experience with impressive range and performance. As a pioneering vehicle in this segment, its reliability is a key consideration for potential buyers. This analysis focuses on documented technical issues, excluding routine maintenance and general wear and tear, to provide a clear picture of the Nexo's long-term dependability. While the technology is advanced, understanding common or potential problem areas is crucial for owners and the automotive industry.

---


## Engine

### 1.9L Fuel Cell Stack
**Fuel Cell Stack Degradation**
- **Symptoms:** Reduced power output, decreased efficiency, increased hydrogen consumption.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the fuel cell stack. This is a complex and costly repair.
- **Cost:** $15,000 - $25,000

**Hydrogen Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Audible hissing sounds, 'rotten egg' smell (sulfur compounds), warning lights on the dashboard.
- **Solution:** Inspection and repair of hydrogen lines, seals, or valves. Requires specialized equipment and safety protocols.
- **Cost:** $500 - $2,000

### Summary Verdict
The 2019 Hyundai Nexo, as a first-generation hydrogen fuel cell vehicle, exhibits a mixed reliability profile. The core electric powertrain and chassis components generally perform well with no widespread issues reported. However, the advanced nature of the fuel cell technology introduces potential concerns, particularly regarding the longevity and cost of the fuel cell stack, and the critical safety aspect of hydrogen leaks. Electrical systems, especially the infotainment and the 12V auxiliary battery, have seen some isolated reports of glitches or premature failure. While the Nexo offers a compelling glimpse into future mobility, potential buyers should be aware of the specialized maintenance requirements and the higher potential cost associated with fuel cell stack replacement.
