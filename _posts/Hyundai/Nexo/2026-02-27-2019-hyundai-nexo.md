# 2019 Hyundai Nexo Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2019 Hyundai Nexo is an innovative hydrogen fuel cell electric vehicle that offers zero-emission driving and unique styling. As a specialized alternative-fuel vehicle, prospective used-car buyers should carefully evaluate hydrogen fueling infrastructure availability in their area alongside standard vehicle maintenance and component longevity.

---

## Engine

### Hydrogen Fuel Cell Powertrain

The Nexo utilizes a 95 kW fuel cell stack paired with a 40 kW battery pack to deliver 161 horsepower and 291 lb-ft of torque to an electric motor. This advanced hydrogen propulsion system provides smooth, quiet acceleration but requires specialized servicing and access to hydrogen refueling stations.

## Transmission

### Single-Speed Reduction Gear

Power is sent to the front wheels via a single-speed direct-drive reduction transmission typical of electric and fuel cell vehicles. This setup minimizes mechanical complexity and generally requires minimal maintenance throughout the life of the vehicle.

### Powertrain Control

Electrical and powertrain control systems manage the delicate balance between the fuel cell stack, battery, and electric drive motor.

**Acceleration Delay**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have reported a noticeable delay or reduction in speed when depressing the accelerator pedal at high speeds.
- **Solution:** Have the vehicle's electronic control modules inspected and updated by a certified Hyundai technician.

## Brakes

### Regenerative Braking System

The braking system integrates traditional friction brakes with advanced regenerative braking to recapture energy during deceleration.

**Regenerative Brake System Failure**
- **Symptoms:** A warning message may appear indicating a regenerative brake system failure, resulting in dramatically reduced braking effectiveness, limited vehicle speed, and a failure to hold position.
- **Solution:** Inspect the braking system sensors and software, and consult a dealer for potential component replacement or software updates.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V748000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Power Assist:Electric:Control Module:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Hyundai&model=Nexo&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** Reduced braking power could lengthen the distance required to stop the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Hyundai will notify owners, and dealers will update the IEB software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V228000: Fuel System, Other:Storage:Tank Assembly:Filling/Charging](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Hyundai&model=Nexo&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** Following hydrogen refueling, the inlet receptacle filter could detach and cause a hydrogen fuel leak. A hydrogen leak in the presence of an ignition source can increase the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the hydrogen fuel inlet receptacle, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V258000: Fuel System, Other:Delivery](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Hyundai&model=Nexo&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** A hydrogen gas leak can increase the risk of a fire while parked or driving.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the hydrogen gas detection sensors, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V761000: Fuel System, Other:Storage:Tank Assembly:Pressure Relief Devices](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Hyundai&model=Nexo&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** The thermally activated pressure relief device (TPRD) may break and cause a hydrogen gas leak, which can result in a fire while parked. A vehicle fire increases the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the TPRD, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2019 Hyundai Nexo presents a fascinating eco-friendly option for buyers living near established hydrogen infrastructure, but it requires careful due diligence. Used-car shoppers should thoroughly inspect vehicle history reports, verify that all open recalls have been addressed, and test the regenerative braking and acceleration systems before purchasing.
