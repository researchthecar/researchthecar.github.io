**Introduction**
The 2023 Hyundai Nexo is a hydrogen-powered electric vehicle with a range of up to 380 miles. It has a single electric motor that produces 161 horsepower and 291 lb-ft of torque, and it is paired with a 6-speed automatic transmission. The Nexo is praised for its impressive range, comfortable interior, and advanced technology. However, it is also known for its high price and limited fueling infrastructure.

**Electrical**

- **High-Voltage Battery Drain**
Description/Symptoms: The high-voltage battery may drain prematurely, causing the vehicle to lose power.
Solution: Replace the high-voltage battery.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $15,000 - $20,000+
Recalls: NHTSA Recall Number 22V-536

**Exterior**

- **Body Panel Gaps**
Description/Symptoms: The body panels may have uneven gaps, creating an unappealing appearance.
Solution: Adjust or replace the body panels.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

- **Touchscreen Malfunction**
Description/Symptoms: The touchscreen may become unresponsive or display incorrect information.
Solution: Update the software or replace the touchscreen.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
Recalls: None Found

- **Seat Belt Warning Chime**
Description/Symptoms: The seat belt warning chime may not sound when a passenger is not wearing their seat belt.
Solution: Replace the seat belt buckle assembly.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $250 - $500+
Recalls: NHTSA Recall Number 22V-600

**Summary**
The 2023 Hyundai Nexo has a limited number of reported issues. The most severe problem is the high-voltage battery drain, which can be expensive to fix. The other issues, such as body panel gaps, touchscreen malfunctions, and seat belt warning chimes, are less serious and less costly to address. Overall, the Nexo is a reliable vehicle, but buyers should be aware of the potential issues before purchasing.
