# 2021 Hyundai Nexo Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2021 Hyundai Nexo is an innovative hydrogen fuel cell electric vehicle that offers zero-emission driving and distinctive crossover styling. As a specialized alternative-fuel vehicle, shoppers should carefully weigh its cutting-edge powertrain against limited hydrogen refueling infrastructure and specialized maintenance requirements. While owner reports for this model year are very sparse, prospective buyers should still verify that all manufacturer campaigns have been properly addressed by an authorized service center.

---

## Engine

### 95 kW Fuel Cell Stack with 120 kW Electric Motor

This hydrogen fuel cell system delivers 161 horsepower and 291 lb-ft of torque to power the electric motor. It provides smooth, silent acceleration, though long-term reliability heavily depends on proper fuel cell maintenance and regional hydrogen station availability.

## Transmission

### Single-Speed Reduction Gearbox

A direct-drive single-speed transmission channels power seamlessly from the electric motor to the front wheels. This setup is mechanically simple and typically requires minimal maintenance compared to conventional multi-speed gearboxes.

## Electrical

### Hydrogen Sensor

The electrical architecture manages complex power distribution between the fuel cell stack, high-voltage battery, and hydrogen safety monitoring systems.

**Hydrogen Sensor Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Owners may experience improper readings or failures from the onboard hydrogen detection sensors.
- **Solution:** Have the hydrogen sensor diagnostics run by a certified Hyundai technician and replace faulty sensor units as necessary.

## Safety

### Forward Collision Avoidance

Advanced driver assistance systems provide collision mitigation, though sensor calibration and system performance must be verified during inspection.

**Forward Collision Braking Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may fail to automatically apply the brakes when approaching obstacles during testing scenarios.
- **Solution:** Take the vehicle to an authorized dealer to inspect and recalibrate the collision avoidance sensors and software.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V258000: Fuel System, Other:Delivery](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Hyundai&model=Nexo&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** A hydrogen gas leak can increase the risk of a fire while parked or driving.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the hydrogen gas detection sensors, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V761000: Fuel System, Other:Storage:Tank Assembly:Pressure Relief Devices](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Hyundai&model=Nexo&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The thermally activated pressure relief device (TPRD) may break and cause a hydrogen gas leak, which can result in a fire while parked. A vehicle fire increases the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the TPRD, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2021 Hyundai Nexo is a technologically advanced choice for buyers living in regions with established hydrogen infrastructure, but it requires careful due diligence. Prospective owners should ensure that all safety campaigns are completed and thoroughly test all electronic safety and monitoring features prior to purchase.
