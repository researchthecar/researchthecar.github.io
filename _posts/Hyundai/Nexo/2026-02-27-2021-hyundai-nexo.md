# 2021 Hyundai Nexo

### Overview
The 2021 Hyundai Nexo is a hydrogen fuel cell electric vehicle (FCEV) that represents a significant step in alternative fuel technology. As a relatively new platform, its long-term reliability is still being established, but initial data suggests a strong performance. The Nexo is designed with advanced systems to manage its unique powertrain, focusing on efficiency and environmental friendliness. Owners generally report a positive experience, appreciating its quiet operation and innovative features. While specific component failures are rare, as with any advanced vehicle, understanding potential issues and their resolutions is key to maintaining optimal performance and longevity.

### Summary Verdict
The 2021 Hyundai Nexo demonstrates strong initial reliability, particularly given its advanced and novel hydrogen fuel cell powertrain. Major mechanical failures are not commonly reported, and the vehicle's core systems, including the fuel cell stack, battery, and electric motor, appear robust. The primary area where minor issues have been noted is within the infotainment system, typically manifesting as occasional software glitches that are usually resolved with updates. The hydrogen storage system, a critical component, has also shown no widespread reliability concerns. Overall, the Nexo is a reliable option for those seeking an eco-friendly vehicle, with most potential issues being minor and software-related.
