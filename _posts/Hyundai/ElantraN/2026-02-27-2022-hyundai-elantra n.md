# 2022 Hyundai Elantra N

### Overview
The 2022 Hyundai Elantra N is a performance-oriented variant of the popular compact sedan, designed for driving enthusiasts. It typically features a potent turbocharged engine and a sport-tuned suspension system. As a relatively new model, especially in its performance guise, long-term reliability data is still accumulating. However, initial reports and the underlying platform's history suggest a generally robust vehicle, with most reported issues being minor or related to the high-performance nature of the car. Owners should be aware of the specific maintenance requirements for a performance vehicle.

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged I4
**Overheating during sustained track use**
- **Symptoms:** Engine temperature gauge rising rapidly, potential loss of power.
- **Solution:** Ensure proper coolant levels, consider upgraded cooling system for track use.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1500 (for upgrades)

**Minor oil consumption**
- **Symptoms:** Slight decrease in oil level between changes, especially under spirited driving.
- **Solution:** Regular oil level checks and top-offs.
- **Cost:** Minimal (cost of oil)

### Summary Verdict
The 2022 Hyundai Elantra N demonstrates good initial reliability, particularly for its powertrain. The 2.0L turbo engine is generally robust, though owners engaging in track driving should be mindful of cooling system performance. The DCT can occasionally exhibit minor shift anomalies, but these are often resolved with software updates or routine maintenance. The manual transmission has shown no significant issues. Other systems like suspension, electrical, exterior, and interior components are performing well with no widespread problems reported. Overall, the Elantra N is a reliable performance sedan, provided it receives appropriate maintenance, especially if used for demanding driving conditions.
