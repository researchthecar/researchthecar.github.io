# 2003 Hyundai XG350

### Overview
The 2003 Hyundai XG350, a mid-size luxury sedan, aimed to offer premium features at a competitive price point. While generally considered a reliable vehicle for its era, like any car, it has specific areas that have seen more attention from owners regarding potential issues. This analysis focuses on documented technical reliability concerns beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into common problems and their potential resolutions for owners and prospective buyers.

---


## Engine

### 3.5L V6 (G6CU)
**Timing Belt Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Sudden engine stoppage, loss of power, potential for severe internal engine damage if belt breaks.
- **Solution:** Regular replacement of the timing belt and tensioner according to manufacturer's schedule is crucial. If failure occurs, engine rebuild or replacement may be necessary.
- **Cost:** $600 - $1200

**Oil Leaks (Valve Cover Gaskets)**
- **Symptoms:** Visible oil on engine components, burning oil smell, low oil level.
- **Solution:** Replacement of valve cover gaskets.
- **Cost:** $250 - $500


## Transmission

### 5-Speed Automatic (F5A51)
**Harsh Shifting / Slipping**
- **Symptoms:** Jerky gear changes, delayed engagement, engine revving without acceleration.
- **Solution:** Transmission fluid and filter change, solenoid replacement, or in severe cases, transmission rebuild.
- **Cost:** $300 - $800 (for fluid/filter/solenoid), $1500 - $3000 (for rebuild)


## Suspension

### Standard Chassis
**Worn Control Arm Bushings**
- **Symptoms:** Clunking noises over bumps, vague steering feel, uneven tire wear.
- **Solution:** Replacement of control arm bushings or the entire control arm assembly.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600


## Electrical

### Body Control Module (BCM)
**Intermittent Electrical Gremlins**
- **Symptoms:** Power windows, locks, or interior lights malfunctioning intermittently or failing completely.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and potential reprogramming or replacement of the BCM.
- **Cost:** $400 - $800


### Alternator
**Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Battery warning light illuminated, dimming headlights, starting problems.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the alternator.
- **Cost:** $350 - $650

### Summary Verdict
The 2003 Hyundai XG350 offers a generally dependable ownership experience, with its V6 engine and automatic transmission proving to be reasonably robust. However, potential owners should be aware of the critical need for timely timing belt replacement to avoid catastrophic engine failure. Other common concerns include oil leaks from valve cover gaskets, potential transmission issues like harsh shifting, and electrical problems often linked to the Body Control Module. Suspension components, particularly control arm bushings, may require attention as the vehicle ages. While exterior and interior components are typically free of widespread defects, proactive maintenance and addressing these specific potential issues will contribute to the long-term reliability of the XG350.
