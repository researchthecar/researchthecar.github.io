**Introduction:**

The 2013 Hyundai Elantra Coupe is a compact car that offers a sporty driving experience. It is available with two engine options: a 1.8-liter four-cylinder engine producing 148 horsepower and 131 lb-ft of torque, and a 2.0-liter four-cylinder engine producing 173 horsepower and 154 lb-ft of torque. Both engines can be paired with either a six-speed manual transmission or a six-speed automatic transmission.

Drivers praise the Elantra Coupe for its responsive handling, comfortable interior, and affordable price. However, it is not without its flaws, including a small trunk and a lack of rear-seat legroom.

**Engine**

**1.8-Liter Four-Cylinder Engine**

* **Intake Valve Carbon Buildup:** Carbon buildup on the intake valves can cause reduced engine performance, rough idling, and misfiring.
    * Solution: Clean the intake valves manually or with a chemical cleaner.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300+
    * Recalls: None Found

**2.0-Liter Four-Cylinder Engine**

* **No Major Issues Found**

**Transmission**

**Six-Speed Manual Transmission**

* **Shifter Linkage Failure:** The shifter linkage can fail, making it difficult or impossible to shift gears.
    * Solution: Replace the shifter linkage.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+
    * Recalls: NHTSA Campaign Number: 19V427000 (Warranty Extension)

**Six-Speed Automatic Transmission**

* **No Major Issues Found**

**Suspension**

* **Rear Suspension Strut Mount Failure:** The rear suspension strut mounts can fail, causing clunking noises and reduced handling.
    * Solution: Replace the rear suspension strut mounts.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $300+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain:** The battery can drain prematurely, leaving the vehicle unable to start.
    * Solution: Identify and repair the source of the battery drain.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the cause
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Electrical Short Circuits:** Electrical short circuits can occur, causing various electrical problems such as flickering lights, non-functional gauges, or even fires.
    * Solution: Locate and repair the short circuit.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the location and severity of the short
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Headlight Moisture Accumulation:** Moisture can accumulate inside the headlight housings, leading to condensation and reduced visibility.
    * Solution: Replace the headlight housing seals or the entire headlight assembly.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300+ per headlight
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Door Handle Failure:** The door handles can fail, making it difficult or impossible to open the doors from the outside.
    * Solution: Replace the door handle.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+ per door
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Climate Control Malfunction:** The climate control system may malfunction, causing inconsistent temperatures or airflow.
    * Solution: Diagnose and repair the faulty component or replace the climate control module.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Dashboard Squeaks and Rattles:** The dashboard can develop annoying squeaks and rattles over time.
    * Solution: Identify and address the source of the noise, such as loose screws or panels.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $150+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

The 2013 Hyundai Elantra Coupe is a generally reliable vehicle, but it is not without its issues. The most common problems include intake valve carbon buildup (1.8L engine), shifter linkage failure (6-speed manual transmission), and rear suspension strut mount failure. While these issues can be costly to fix, they are not typically severe and can be addressed with proper maintenance and repairs.
