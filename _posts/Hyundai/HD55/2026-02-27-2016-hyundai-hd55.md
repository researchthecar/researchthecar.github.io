# 2016 Hyundai HD55

### Overview
The 2016 Hyundai HD55, a light-duty commercial vehicle, is generally recognized for its robust construction and operational efficiency, making it a popular choice for various business applications. Reliability data for this model indicates a solid performance profile, with most reported issues being minor or related to specific component wear rather than systemic failures. The vehicle's design prioritizes durability for its intended use in commercial settings. While specific engine and transmission options exist, the overall trend suggests good mechanical integrity across the range. Owners and fleet managers can expect a dependable workhorse with proper maintenance, though attention to certain areas, as detailed below, can further enhance longevity and prevent potential disruptions.

---


## Engine

### 2.5L Turbo Diesel (D4CB)
**Glow Plug Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Difficulty starting in cold weather, rough idling, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of faulty glow plugs.
- **Cost:** $150 - $300

**Turbocharger Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of power, whistling noises, black smoke from exhaust.
- **Solution:** Turbocharger inspection and potential rebuild or replacement.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1500


## Transmission

### 5-Speed Manual Transmission
**Clutch Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Slipping clutch, difficulty shifting gears, burning smell.
- **Solution:** Clutch kit replacement (clutch disc, pressure plate, release bearing).
- **Cost:** $500 - $900


### 6-Speed Manual Transmission
**Synchronizer Ring Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Grinding noise when shifting gears, difficulty engaging gears.
- **Solution:** Transmission internal inspection and replacement of worn synchronizer rings.
- **Cost:** $700 - $1200


## Suspension

### Standard Leaf Spring Suspension
**Leaf Spring Breakage**
- **Symptoms:** Sagging rear end, uneven ride height, clunking noises over bumps.
- **Solution:** Replacement of broken leaf springs.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600 per axle


## Electrical

### General Electrical System
**Alternator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Dimming headlights, battery warning light illuminated, electrical components malfunctioning.
- **Solution:** Alternator testing and replacement.
- **Cost:** $350 - $600

### Summary Verdict
The 2016 Hyundai HD55 demonstrates a commendable level of reliability for a commercial vehicle. The primary areas of concern tend to be related to the powertrain, specifically the potential for glow plug issues in the diesel engine and clutch wear in manual transmissions, which are not uncommon for vehicles in this class that undergo heavy use. Turbocharger wear and leaf spring issues are also noted but appear to be manageable with timely maintenance. The electrical system, exterior, and interior components generally perform well with no widespread critical failures reported. Overall, the HD55 is a dependable option, with most potential issues being addressable through routine servicing and proactive component checks, ensuring its continued utility as a work vehicle.
