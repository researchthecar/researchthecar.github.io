# 2017 Hyundai HD55

### Overview
The 2017 Hyundai HD55 is a commercial light-duty truck designed for utility and reliability in demanding work environments. As a commercial vehicle, its design prioritizes durability and operational efficiency. While specific widespread technical issues are not extensively documented in public consumer forums, as is common for many commercial vehicles where data is often proprietary or shared within fleet management circles, general trends for vehicles of this class suggest focusing on powertrain, suspension, and electrical systems for potential concerns. Maintenance and operational load significantly influence the longevity of components. This analysis focuses on documented or commonly anticipated issues for this segment.

---


## Engine

### 2.5L Diesel (D4CB)
**Turbocharger Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of power, excessive smoke, whining noise from the engine bay.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the turbocharger unit.
- **Cost:** $1,200 - $2,500

**Glow Plug Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Difficulty starting in cold weather, engine warning light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of faulty glow plugs.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600


## Transmission

### 5-Speed Manual Transmission
**Clutch Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Slipping clutch, difficulty shifting gears, burning smell.
- **Solution:** Clutch kit replacement (clutch disc, pressure plate, release bearing).
- **Cost:** $700 - $1,200


### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission
**Transmission Fluid Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Leaking fluid, rough shifting, overheating.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of seals, gaskets, or transmission cooler lines.
- **Cost:** $400 - $900


## Suspension

### Standard Leaf Spring Suspension
**Leaf Spring Breakage**
- **Symptoms:** Sagging rear end, uneven ride height, clunking noises over bumps.
- **Solution:** Replacement of broken leaf springs.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1,000


## Electrical

### General Electrical System
**Alternator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Dimming headlights, battery warning light, electrical components malfunctioning.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the alternator.
- **Cost:** $500 - $900

### Summary Verdict
The 2017 Hyundai HD55 demonstrates a generally robust mechanical foundation, typical for its class. The most frequently cited potential issues revolve around the powertrain, specifically the turbocharger and glow plugs for the diesel engine, and clutch wear for manual transmissions. Automatic transmission fluid leaks and leaf spring breakage are also points to monitor, especially under heavy use. Electrical system reliability is generally good, though alternator failure can occur. Exterior and interior components are typically durable. Regular maintenance and addressing minor issues promptly are key to ensuring the long-term reliability and operational efficiency of the HD55.
