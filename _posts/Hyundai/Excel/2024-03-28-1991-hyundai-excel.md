**Introduction:**

The 1991 Hyundai Excel is a subcompact car known for its affordability and fuel efficiency. It was offered with two engine options: a 1.3L SOHC I4 and a 1.5L SOHC I4. Transmission options included a 5-speed manual and 3-speed automatic. Despite its budget-friendly nature, the Excel has received mixed reviews, with some owners praising its reliability and simplicity, while others have reported various issues.

**Engine**

**1.3L SOHC I4**

* **Excessive Oil Consumption:** The engine may burn excessive oil, leading to low oil levels and potential engine damage.
* **Description/Symptoms:** Blue smoke from the exhaust, low oil level indicator light, noisy engine.
* **Solution:** Replace worn piston rings or valve seals.
* **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $500 - $1,000+
* **Recalls:** None Found

**1.5L SOHC I4**

* **No Issues Found**

**Transmission**

**5-Speed Manual**

* **Hard Shifting:** The transmission may be difficult to shift gears, especially into reverse.
* **Description/Symptoms:** Difficulty engaging gears, grinding noises.
* **Solution:** Replace worn clutch or transmission seals.
* **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $300 - $700+
* **Recalls:** None Found

**3-Speed Automatic**

* **Transmission Slipping:** The transmission may slip or fail to engage gears properly.
* **Description/Symptoms:** Vehicle struggles to accelerate, sudden loss of power.
* **Solution:** Rebuild or replace the transmission.
* **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $1,500 - $3,000+
* **Recalls:** None Found

**Suspension**

* **Premature Strut Failure:** The front struts may wear out prematurely, affecting ride quality and handling.
* **Description/Symptoms:** Excessive bounce, clunking noises, uneven tire wear.
* **Solution:** Replace the struts.
* **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $400 - $800+
* **Recalls:** None Found

**Electrical**

* **Electrical Short Circuit:** A short circuit in the wiring harness may cause various electrical malfunctions.
* **Description/Symptoms:** Blown fuses, flickering lights, power loss.
* **Solution:** Locate and repair the short circuit.
* **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $200 - $500+
* **Recalls:** None Found

**Exterior**

* **Rust:** The body panels, especially around the wheel arches and lower door edges, may be prone to rust.
* **Description/Symptoms:** Visible rust spots, bubbling paint.
* **Solution:** Repair and repaint the affected areas.
* **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $500 - $1,500+
* **Recalls:** None Found

**Interior**

* **Dash Panel Cracking:** The dashboard panel may develop cracks over time.
* **Description/Symptoms:** Visible cracks or gaps in the dashboard.
* **Solution:** Replace the dashboard panel.
* **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $300 - $600+
* **Recalls:** None Found

**Other**

* **Fuel Pump Failure:** The fuel pump may fail, causing the engine to stall or run poorly.
* **Description/Symptoms:** Difficulty starting or stalling, poor fuel economy.
* **Solution:** Replace the fuel pump.
* **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $200 - $400+
* **Recalls:** None Found

**Summary:**

The 1991 Hyundai Excel has a mixed reputation, with some owners reporting various issues and others being satisfied with its reliability. The most common problems include excessive oil consumption, hard shifting, premature strut failure, and electrical short circuits. While some repairs are relatively inexpensive, others, such as transmission rebuilds, can be costly. Proper maintenance and regular inspections can help prevent or minimize these issues.
