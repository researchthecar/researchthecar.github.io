# 2020 Hyundai Palisade Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2020 Hyundai Palisade is a well-regarded three-row midsize SUV offering upscale features, spacious seating, and a comfortable ride. However, prospective buyers should review recurring owner complaints covering engine performance, suspension wear, and specific safety and body hardware items. While it offers strong value, thorough pre-purchase inspections are recommended to catch potential mechanical or electrical concerns.

---

## Engine

### 3.8L V6 Engine

Equipped with a 3.8-liter V6 engine producing approximately 291 horsepower and 262 lb-ft of torque, providing confident acceleration for daily driving and towing. Buyers should monitor oil consumption levels and watch for intermittent electronic warning or stalling reports.

**Engine Stalling and Loss of Power**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicles may unexpectedly stall or experience a loss of motive power while driving, often accompanied by warning lights on the instrument cluster.
- **Solution:** Diagnostic scanning by a qualified technician is required to identify faulty sensors, software glitches, or electrical component failures.

**Excessive Oil Consumption**
- **Symptoms:** Engine burns oil rapidly between service intervals without triggering immediate low-oil or pressure warning indicators.
- **Solution:** Monitor oil levels frequently and have an authorized service center perform an oil consumption test if rapid loss is observed.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

Features an 8-speed automatic transmission paired with front-wheel drive or optional all-wheel drive, offering smooth and efficient gear shifts. General operation is typically reliable, though routine fluid maintenance is advised.

## Suspension

### Rear Shocks and Suspension

The suspension provides a compliant ride, though rear shock absorbers on certain trims may wear out prematurely, leading to unwanted noise and a rougher ride quality.

**Premature Shock Wear and Clunking Noises**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers report loud thumping, squeaking, or knocking noises from the rear end, along with a bumpy ride over rough terrain.
- **Solution:** Inspect rear shocks and replace worn components or self-leveling shock assemblies as needed.

## Exterior

### Sunroof Assembly

Exterior body hardware is generally durable, though panoramic sunroof mechanisms require careful inspection to prevent mechanical jamming.

**Sunroof Sticking and Jammed Mechanism**
- **Symptoms:** The panoramic sunroof or its internal wind deflector breaks or jams, leaving the roof stuck in a partially open position.
- **Solution:** Repair or replace broken internal plastic guide tracks and wind deflector components.
- **Cost:** $3400

## Safety

### Seat Belts

Safety equipment includes comprehensive airbag and restraint systems, though specific recalls and owner complaints highlight potential latching issues in the second row.

**Seat Belt Buckle Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Second-row seat belt buckles fail to latch or secure properly.
- **Solution:** Inspect affected seat belt buckles and have faulty assemblies replaced through authorized recall procedures or dealership service.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V802000: Air Bags:Side/Window](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Hyundai&model=Palisade&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** The mounting bolts used to secure the side curtain air bags may damage the side curtain air bags during deployment. Damaged side curtain air bags may not inflate properly in the event of a crash, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Hyundai will notify owners, and dealers will install a protective cover on the mounting bolts, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V627000: Visibility:Windshield Wiper/Washer:Motor](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Hyundai&model=Palisade&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** The windshield wiper motor may become inoperative, causing the wipers to fail. Windshield wiper failure can reduce visibility, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the driver-side windshield wiper arm, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V633000: Trailer Hitches](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Hyundai&model=Palisade&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** Debris and moisture accumulation on the tow hitch harness module printed circuit board (PCB) may cause an electrical short, which can result in a fire. A fire while parked or driving can increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will install a new fuse and wire extension kit, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V607000: Seat Belts: Rear/Other:Buckle Assembly](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Hyundai&model=Palisade&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** Seat belt buckles that fail to latch may not properly restrain occupants in a crash, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the seat belt buckle assemblies, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V034000: Air Bags:Side/Window:Curtain](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Hyundai&model=Palisade&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** The side curtain air bags for third-row occupants may deploy improperly in a crash. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 226, "Ejection Mitigation." Improper side curtain air bag deployment increases the risk of injury during a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will install a protective film over the rear window and remove padding, as necessary free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2020 Hyundai Palisade is a compelling choice for families seeking a feature-rich, comfortable crossover. Prospective buyers should carefully verify that all safety recalls—particularly concerning seat belts—have been completed, and check for signs of rear suspension noise, sunroof binding, or engine oil consumption during a pre-purchase inspection.
