# 2024 Hyundai Palisade Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2024 Hyundai Palisade remains a popular three-row midsize SUV known for its upscale cabin, spacious seating, and smooth highway ride. However, prospective buyers should review owner complaints regarding several recurring mechanical and electrical concerns. While federal safety equipment like seat belts and forward collision systems offer strong baseline protection, owner reports highlight potential issues ranging from brake responsiveness over rough surfaces to suspension noises and unexpected driver-assistance activations.

---

## Engine

### 3.8L V6 GDI

Equipped with a 3.8-liter V6 direct-injection engine producing roughly 291 horsepower and 262 lb-ft of torque. It provides confident acceleration for everyday driving and towing, generally maintaining a solid reputation for mechanical longevity.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

Features an 8-speed automatic transmission paired with either front-wheel drive or all-wheel drive. Shifts are typically smooth and well-managed, matching the comfort-oriented character of the SUV.

## Suspension

### Rear Shocks and Suspension

The suspension tuning delivers a compliant, family-friendly ride, though occasional rear shock anomalies have been documented by owners.

**Rear Shock Knocking and Fracture Reports**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may hear a clicking, knocking, or rolling noise from the rear shock absorber area over bumps, with rare reports of premature shock wear or failure affecting ride stability.
- **Solution:** Inspect rear shock absorbers for leaks or structural damage and replace worn units under warranty where applicable.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

The braking system provides firm stopping power under normal conditions, though some owners have experienced disconcerting feedback or delayed response on uneven road surfaces.

**Brake Responsiveness and Shaking**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers report unexpected vehicle shaking, longer stopping distances, or temporary unresponsiveness when trying to apply brakes over bumpy surfaces or railroad tracks.
- **Solution:** Have the ABS sensors, brake pads, and rotors inspected by a qualified technician. Software updates or hardware checks may be required if anti-lock braking systems engage prematurely.

## Steering

### Steering Assembly

Power steering assist is generally light and responsive, but isolated complaints indicate potential electronic or mechanical binding during tight maneuvers.

**Intermittent Steering Stiffness and Vibration**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers note occasional steering wheel stiffness, erratic vibration during turns, or temporary loss of smooth control at low speeds.
- **Solution:** Inspect electronic power steering connections, steering shafts, and front suspension components for binding or sensor faults.

## Safety

### Seat Belts

Passive safety systems are comprehensive, but supply chain delays for specific recall components have left some owners waiting for permanent fixes.

**Seat Belt Latch and Recall Backorders**
- **Symptoms:** Owners note difficulties or safety concerns regarding seat belt hardware, often corresponding with active safety recall campaigns experiencing parts delays.
- **Solution:** Check the vehicle identification number (VIN) for open recalls and consult a local dealership for authorized component replacements as soon as parts become available.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V526000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission:Fluid/Lubricant:Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Hyundai&model=Palisade&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** An electric oil pump assembly that overheats increases the risk of a vehicle fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the electric oil pump controller, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V599000: Air Bags:Sensor:Occupant Classification:Front Passenger](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Hyundai&model=Palisade&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 208, "Occupant Crash Protection." Deactivated front passenger air bags will not deploy as intended, increasing the risk of injury during a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the ODS software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V106000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Engine:Hard Parts Internal/Mechanical](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Hyundai&model=Palisade&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** A loss of drive power increases the risk of a crash. A hole in the engine block increases the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the engine sub-assembly, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V607000: Seat Belts: Rear/Other:Buckle Assembly](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Hyundai&model=Palisade&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** Seat belt buckles that fail to latch may not properly restrain occupants in a crash, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the seat belt buckle assemblies, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V034000: Air Bags:Side/Window:Curtain](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Hyundai&model=Palisade&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** The side curtain air bags for third-row occupants may deploy improperly in a crash. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 226, "Ejection Mitigation." Improper side curtain air bag deployment increases the risk of injury during a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will install a protective film over the rear window and remove padding, as necessary free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2024 Hyundai Palisade presents an appealing package for used-car buyers seeking comfort, modern technology, and generous passenger space. While major powertrain mechanical failures are uncommon, buyers should carefully verify that all open safety recalls—particularly those involving seat belts—have been addressed. A thorough pre-purchase inspection focusing on the brakes, steering responsiveness, and rear suspension will help ensure a trouble-free ownership experience.
