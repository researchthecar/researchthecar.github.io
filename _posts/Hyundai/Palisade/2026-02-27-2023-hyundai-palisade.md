# 2023 Hyundai Palisade Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2023 Hyundai Palisade is a well-regarded midsize three-row SUV offering comfortable seating, an upscale cabin, and a strong suite of standard features. However, prospective buyers should review a handful of recurring owner complaints regarding safety recalls, brake behavior on uneven surfaces, and suspension components.

---

## Engine

### 3.8L V6 Engine

The Palisade is powered by a 3.8-liter V6 engine producing approximately 291 horsepower and 262 lb-ft of torque, providing smooth and capable acceleration for daily driving and highway cruising.

**Engine Stalling or Hesitation**
- **Symptoms:** Illuminated check engine lights, unexpected jerking, or intermittent engine stalling during operation.
- **Solution:** Diagnostic scanning by a certified technician; potential replacement of faulty fuel injectors or sensors.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

An 8-speed automatic transmission routes power smoothly to either the front wheels or an available all-wheel-drive system, generally offering refined shifting behavior under normal conditions.

## Suspension

### Rear Shock Absorbers

The suspension delivers a plush, compliant ride tailored for family comfort, though rear shocks have been a notable wear point for some owners.

**Rear Shock Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Clunking, knocking, or rattling noises from the rear suspension, along with diminished ride quality and stability.
- **Solution:** Replace worn or failed rear shock absorbers.

## Brakes

### Brake System

The service brake system provides reliable stopping power under normal conditions, though some owners report unexpected pedal feedback on rough terrain.

**Brake Stutter on Uneven Surfaces**
- **Symptoms:** Brakes may stutter, lurch, or feel like the anti-lock braking system is prematurely activating when stopping over bumps or uneven ground at low speeds.
- **Solution:** Inspect brake sensors, ABS module, and suspension linkage for proper calibration and wear.

## Exterior

### Sunroof and Glass

Exterior styling and visibility features are generally well-received, though panoramic glass panels warrant careful inspection for stress cracks.

**Spontaneous Sunroof Shattering**
- **Symptoms:** Loud explosive sound followed by the panoramic sunroof glass shattering spontaneously while driving.
- **Solution:** Inspect and replace damaged glass panels under comprehensive insurance or applicable warranty coverage.

## Safety

### Seat Belts and Air Bags

Occupant safety systems include advanced airbags and multi-point seat belts, though active recall campaigns require close attention from prospective buyers.

**Seat Belt and Ejection Mitigation Recalls**
- **Symptoms:** Owner notifications regarding recall campaigns involving seat belts and ejection mitigation standards where parts or remedies may have initial delays.
- **Solution:** Check the VIN against official recall campaigns and have authorized dealers perform necessary manufacturer remedies once available.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V180000: Visibility/Wiper](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Hyundai&model=Palisade&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** Windshield wiper failure can reduce visibility, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the driver-side windshield wiper arm, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V415000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Power Assist](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Hyundai&model=Palisade&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** The brake booster diaphragm may become misaligned and cause an internal vacuum leak, which can result in a loss of power brake assist. A loss of power brake assist can extend the distance required to stop, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the brake booster as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V526000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission:Fluid/Lubricant:Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Hyundai&model=Palisade&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** An electric oil pump assembly that overheats increases the risk of a vehicle fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the electric oil pump controller, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V607000: Seat Belts: Rear/Other:Buckle Assembly](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Hyundai&model=Palisade&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** Seat belt buckles that fail to latch may not properly restrain occupants in a crash, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the seat belt buckle assemblies, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V034000: Air Bags:Side/Window:Curtain](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Hyundai&model=Palisade&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** The side curtain air bags for third-row occupants may deploy improperly in a crash. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 226, "Ejection Mitigation." Improper side curtain air bag deployment increases the risk of injury during a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will install a protective film over the rear window and remove padding, as necessary free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2023 Hyundai Palisade remains a compelling choice for families seeking a spacious and feature-rich SUV, but used car buyers should verify that all open safety recalls have been addressed. A thorough pre-purchase inspection focusing on the rear suspension, brakes, and electrical accessories will help ensure a reliable ownership experience.
