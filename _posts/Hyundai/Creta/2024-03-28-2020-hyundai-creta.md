**Introduction**

The 2020 Hyundai Creta is a compact SUV that offers a combination of practicality and style. It comes with two engine options: a 1.6-liter gasoline engine and a 1.5-liter diesel engine. Both engines are mated to a 6-speed manual transmission or an optional 6-speed automatic transmission. People like the Creta for its spacious cabin, comfortable ride, and impressive fuel efficiency. However, it has also been reported to have several issues.

**Engine**

* **1.6-liter Gasoline Engine**
    * **Intake manifold gaskets leak**
        * Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaks, overheating engine
        * Solution: Replace intake manifold gaskets
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
    * **Timing chain tensioner failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: Rattling noise from engine, loss of power
        * Solution: Replace timing chain tensioner
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
* **1.5-liter Diesel Engine**
    * **Injector failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: Difficulty starting, black smoke from exhaust
        * Solution: Replace fuel injectors
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600

**Transmission**

* **6-speed Manual Transmission**
    * **Gear grinding**
        * Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears, grinding noises
        * Solution: Replace or repair transmission gears
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,500
* **6-speed Automatic Transmission**
    * **Torque converter shudder**
        * Description/Symptoms: Vibration felt when accelerating or decelerating
        * Solution: Replace torque converter
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000
    * **Transmission slipping**
        * Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears, vehicle not responding to input
        * Solution: Rebuild or replace transmission
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,000

**Suspension**

* **No Issues Found**

**Electrical**

* **Battery drain**
    * Description/Symptoms: Battery dies after short periods of time
    * Solution: Check for parasitic drain; possible replacement of defective battery or alternator
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500
* **Faulty wiring harness**
    * Description/Symptoms: Electrical problems such as flickering lights, faulty gauges
    * Solution: Repair or replace affected wiring harness
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600

**Exterior**

* **Sunroof leaks**
    * Description/Symptoms: Water leaking into cabin through sunroof
    * Solution: Replace sunroof seals or install sunroof drain tubes
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
* **Rear bumper loose**
    * Description/Symptoms: Rear bumper rattling or misaligned
    * Solution: Tighten or replace rear bumper bolts
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200

**Interior**

* **Squeaky dashboard**
    * Description/Symptoms: Noises coming from dashboard
    * Solution: Apply lubricant to dashboard joints
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 - $50
* **Loose interior panels**
    * Description/Symptoms: Interior panels rattling or coming loose
    * Solution: Tighten or replace interior panel clips
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200

**Summary**

The 2020 Hyundai Creta is a compact SUV that has received positive feedback for its spacious cabin, comfortable ride, and fuel efficiency. However, it has also been reported to have several issues, including engine leaks, timing chain tensioner failure, injector failure, transmission gear grinding, torque converter shudder, transmission slipping, battery drain, faulty wiring harness, sunroof leaks, loose rear bumper, squeaky dashboard, and loose interior panels. The severity of these issues varies, and some can be relatively inexpensive to fix, while others may require major repairs. It is important for potential Creta owners to be aware of these issues before making a purchase decision.
