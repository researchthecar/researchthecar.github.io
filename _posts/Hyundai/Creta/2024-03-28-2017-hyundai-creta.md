**Introduction:**

The 2017 Hyundai Creta is a subcompact SUV available with two engine and transmission options. The 1.6-liter diesel engine with a 6-speed manual transmission and the 1.6-liter petrol engine with a 6-speed automatic transmission. The Creta is known for its spacious interiors, comfortable ride, and fuel efficiency. However, like any other vehicle, it has been reported to have certain issues.

**Engine**
* **1.6-liter Diesel Engine**
 **Engine Oil Leaks:**
  * Description/Symptoms: Leaking engine oil can be seen from the engine bay.
  * Solution: Replace the valve cover gasket or crankshaft seal.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300
  * Recalls: None Found
 **Turbocharger Failure:**
  * Description/Symptoms: Reduced power output, black smoke from the exhaust, and a whistling noise from the engine.
  * Solution: Replace the turbocharger.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
  * Recalls: None Found
* **1.6-liter Petrol Engine**
 **Timing Chain Issue:**
  * Description/Symptoms: Rattling noise from the engine, particularly at startup.
  * Solution: Replace the timing chain and tensioner.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800
  * Recalls: None Found
 **Fuel Injector Failure:**
  * Description/Symptoms: Rough idling, reduced fuel economy, and difficulty starting.
  * Solution: Replace the faulty fuel injector.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
  * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**
* **6-speed Manual Transmission**
 **Gear Grinding:**
  * Description/Symptoms: Grinding noise when shifting gears.
  * Solution: Lubricate the gearshift mechanism or replace the transmission fluid.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200
  * Recalls: None Found
 **Clutch Wear:**
  * Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears, slipping clutch, or shuddering.
  * Solution: Replace the clutch disc, pressure plate, and release bearing.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $600 - $1,000
  * Recalls: None Found
* **6-speed Automatic Transmission**
 **Shift Solenoid Failure:**
  * Description/Symptoms: Transmission slipping or delayed shifting.
  * Solution: Replace the faulty shift solenoid.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500
  * Recalls: None Found
 **Transmission Fluid Leak:**
  * Description/Symptoms: Transmission fluid leaking from the transmission pan or lines.
  * Solution: Tighten the drain plug or replace the leaking gasket or line.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $250
  * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**
* **Front Suspension:**
 **Strut Mount Bearing Failure:**
  * Description/Symptoms: Knocking or clunking noise when driving over bumps or uneven surfaces.
  * Solution: Replace the strut mount bearing.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
  * Recalls: None Found
* **Rear Suspension:**
 **Shock Absorber Failure:**
  * Description/Symptoms: Bouncy ride, poor handling, and clunking noise over bumps.
  * Solution: Replace the worn-out shock absorbers.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $700
  * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**
* **Battery Drain:**
  * Description/Symptoms: Battery discharges quickly, even when the car is not in use.
  * Solution: Check for a parasitic drain by testing electrical components and disconnecting them one by one.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300
  * Recalls: None Found
* **Electrical Short:**
  * Description/Symptoms: Blown fuses, flickering lights, or electrical components not working.
  * Solution: Trace the electrical system to identify and repair the short circuit.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $400
  * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**
* **Door Handle Failure:**
  * Description/Symptoms: Door handle breaking off or becoming loose.
  * Solution: Replace the door handle.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200
  * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**
* **Sunroof Leak:**
  * Description/Symptoms: Water leaking into the cabin through the sunroof.
  * Solution: Clean and inspect the drainage system, or replace the sunroof seal.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
  * Recalls: None Found
* **Air Conditioning System Failure:**
  * Description/Symptoms: Blowing warm air, insufficient cooling, or compressor noise.
  * Solution: Recharge the AC system, replace the compressor, or repair the refrigerant lines.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,000+
  * Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

Overall, the 2017 Hyundai Creta seems to have some common issues with its engine, transmission, and certain components. While the severity of these issues varies, some of them can be quite expensive to repair. It is important for owners to be aware of these potential problems and to have their vehicles regularly inspected by a qualified mechanic to prevent major breakdowns.
