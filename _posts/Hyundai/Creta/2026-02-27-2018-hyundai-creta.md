## Electrical

### Infotainment System
**Occasional Glitches/Freezing**
- **Symptoms:** Screen becomes unresponsive, navigation issues, Bluetooth connectivity problems.
- **Solution:** Software update, system reset, or replacement of the unit.
- **Cost:** $100 - $1500 (depending on repair)


### Battery Drain
**Intermittent Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle fails to start, battery dies prematurely.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis to identify parasitic draw from accessories or faulty modules, battery replacement if necessary.
- **Cost:** $200 - $600


## Exterior

### Paintwork
**Minor Paint Chips**
- **Symptoms:** Small chips or imperfections on the paint surface, particularly on the hood and front bumper.
- **Solution:** Touch-up paint application, professional detailing.
- **Cost:** $50 - $300


### Headlight Condensation
**Condensation Inside Headlights**
- **Symptoms:** Fogging or moisture buildup inside the headlight assembly.
- **Solution:** Ensure seals are intact, professional cleaning or replacement of headlight unit if severe.
- **Cost:** $50 - $500

### Summary Verdict
The 2018 Hyundai Creta demonstrates strong overall reliability, with its powertrain and core mechanical components performing commendably. The most frequently cited technical concerns tend to be minor, such as occasional infotainment system glitches and potential for battery drain, which are not uncommon in modern vehicles. Exterior issues like minor paint chips and headlight condensation are also reported but are generally manageable. For prospective buyers, focusing on a well-maintained example and being aware of these minor electrical and exterior points should lead to a positive ownership experience.
