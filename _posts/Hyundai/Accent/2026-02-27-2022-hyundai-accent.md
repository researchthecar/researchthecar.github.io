# 2022 Hyundai Accent Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2022 Hyundai Accent is a subcompact sedan offering practical daily transportation and commendable fuel economy for budget-conscious buyers. While generally straightforward, shoppers should review owner feedback concerning miscellaneous electrical, steering, and braking quirks. As NHTSA complaints represent owner experiences rather than guaranteed factory flaws, a careful inspection during a test drive is recommended.

---

## Engine

### 1.6L 4-Cylinder Engine

The 2022 Hyundai Accent is powered by a 1.6-liter naturally aspirated four-cylinder engine generating approximately 120 horsepower and 113 lb-ft of torque. This powerplant prioritizes fuel efficiency and provides reliable, straightforward operation for daily commuting.

## Transmission

### Intelligent Variable Transmission (IVT)

Power is sent to the front wheels via a continuously variable transmission marketed as an Intelligent Variable Transmission (IVT). It is designed to maximize fuel economy, delivering smooth acceleration under normal driving conditions.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

Brake system performance is generally adequate, though rare owner reports highlight unexpected stopping concerns.

**Brake Pedal Response**
- **Symptoms:** Isolated driver complaints note sudden difficulties or unexpected resistance when attempting to slow the vehicle down.
- **Solution:** Inspect brake pads, rotors, and hydraulic lines thoroughly during a pre-purchase evaluation.

## Steering

### Ignition and Steering Column

Steering and security components require attention regarding potential ignition vulnerabilities and physical column integrity.

**Ignition and Security Vulnerability**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may be susceptible to break-ins and damage to the ignition cylinder due to attempts to exploit missing immobilizer security features.
- **Solution:** Ensure all applicable software updates or physical security steering wheel locks are utilized to deter theft attempts.

## Electrical

### Instrument Cluster and Dashboard

Electrical complaints center sporadically on dashboard displays and intermittent instrument cluster behavior.

**Instrument Cluster Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have reported instances where the dashboard lights up incorrectly, turns off repeatedly, or affects vehicle speed control functions like cruise control.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified technician inspect the instrument cluster wiring and software modules for faults.

## Safety

### Passenger Air Bag Sensor

Safety system grievances mainly involve intermittent sensor warnings within the occupant detection architecture.

**Airbag Indicator Light Irregularity**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have noted inconsistent behavior from the passenger airbag warning lights even when seats are unoccupied.
- **Solution:** Have the occupant classification system and seat sensors diagnosed by a dealership or specialist.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V311000: Steering:Column](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Hyundai&model=Accent&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** A bolt in the steering column may not be secured properly, which can cause the steering column to detach from the steering rack and result in a loss of steering control. Loss of steering control can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and tighten the lower steering column u-joint retention bolt, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V354000: Seat Belts:Front](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Hyundai&model=Accent&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** In the event of a crash, the front driver-side and/or passenger-side seat belt pretensioners may explode upon deployment. An exploding seat belt pretensioner can project metal fragments into the vehicle, strike vehicle occupants, and result in injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will secure the seat belt pretensioner(s) with a cap, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2022 Hyundai Accent remains an appealing, fuel-efficient choice for daily driving, provided buyers verify vehicle history and confirm proper functioning of its electronics and ignition systems. A thorough pre-purchase inspection can catch any lingering cosmetic, tire, or minor electrical quirks before finalizing a purchase.
