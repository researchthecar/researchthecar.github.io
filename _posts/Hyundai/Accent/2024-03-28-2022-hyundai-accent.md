**Introduction**

The 2022 Hyundai Accent is a subcompact sedan known for its affordability, fuel efficiency, and user-friendly interior. It is available with two engine options: a 1.6L inline-four and a 1.6L inline-four with an Atkinson cycle. Both engines are paired with a continuously variable transmission (CVT).

Despite its positive qualities, the 2022 Accent has encountered some issues reported by owners. These issues vary in severity and cost to fix.

**Engine**

**1.6L Inline-Four**

* **Engine Stalling at Idle**
    * Description/Symptoms: The engine stalls abruptly while the vehicle is idling, particularly when the air conditioner is engaged.
    * Solution: Replace the engine throttle body and reprogram the engine control unit (ECU).
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,000
    * Recalls: NHTSA Campaign Number 23V108000

**1.6L Inline-Four with Atkinson Cycle**

* **No Issues Found**

**Transmission**

**Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)**

* **Jerky Shifting**
    * Description/Symptoms: The transmission hesitates or jerks during gear changes, especially at low speeds.
    * Solution: Reprogram the transmission control module (TCM) or replace the CVT valve body.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,200
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **No Issues Found**

**Electrical**

* **Faulty Door Lock Actuators**
    * Description/Symptoms: The door locks may fail to operate properly, causing the doors to remain unlocked or unable to open.
    * Solution: Replace the faulty door lock actuators.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400 per door

**Exterior**

* **Hood Latch Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The hood latch may fail to engage, allowing the hood to open unexpectedly while driving.
    * Solution: Replace the hood latch assembly.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $250 - $450

**Interior**

* **Loose Headliner**
    * Description/Symptoms: The fabric lining of the ceiling may come loose and sag, particularly in the front and rear areas.
    * Solution: Reattach or replace the headliner.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300

**Summary**

Overall, the 2022 Hyundai Accent has encountered a limited number of issues that are typically minor in nature and relatively inexpensive to fix. The most common problems involve engine stalling and transmission shifting issues. Despite these issues, the Accent remains a reliable and affordable vehicle for its class.
