**Introduction:**

The 2004 Hyundai Accent is a subcompact sedan that was available with a 1.6L inline-4 engine and a 5-speed manual or 4-speed automatic transmission. It offered a budget-friendly option with decent fuel economy and a spacious interior for its size. However, it was criticized for its lackluster performance and unimpressive handling.

**Engine**

**1.6L Inline-4**

* **Knocking/Piston Slap Noise:**
    * Description/Symptoms: A rattling or knocking sound that intensifies during acceleration or under load.
    * Solution: Replace the engine's short block.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,500 - $3,500+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Timing Belt Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The timing belt can snap without warning, causing catastrophic engine damage.
    * Solution: Replace the timing belt and tensioner according to the maintenance schedule.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**5-Speed Manual**

* **Hard Shifting:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears or gear slippage.
    * Solution: Replace the clutch assembly or transmission synchronizers.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500
    * Recalls: None Found

**4-Speed Automatic**

* **Transmission Slipping:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The transmission hesitates or slips while shifting.
    * Solution: Rebuild or replace the transmission.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Strut Mounts Wear:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Noise and vibration when driving over bumps.
    * Solution: Replace the strut mounts.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400 per strut

**Electrical**

* **Alternator Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Battery warning light illuminates, car stalls or has difficulty starting.
    * Solution: Replace the alternator.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Headlight Switch Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Headlights do not turn on or turn off intermittently.
    * Solution: Replace the headlight switch.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $250
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Power Window Switch Malfunction:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Power windows fail to operate.
    * Solution: Replace the power window switch.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $150

**Summary:**

The 2004 Hyundai Accent has a number of potential issues, particularly with the engine and transmission. The severity of these issues can vary, with some being relatively minor and inexpensive to fix while others can be major and costly. It's important for owners to be aware of these potential problems and have them addressed promptly to avoid more serious issues and unexpected expenses.
