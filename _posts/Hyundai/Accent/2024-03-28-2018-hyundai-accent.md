**2018 Hyundai Accent**

The 2018 Hyundai Accent is a subcompact sedan offered with a 1.6-liter four-cylinder engine paired with either a six-speed manual transmission or a six-speed automatic transmission. The engine produces 130 horsepower and 119 lb-ft of torque.

People like the Accent for its affordability, fuel efficiency, and spacious interior. However, some common complaints include a lack of power, a noisy cabin, and a stiff ride.

**Engine**

**1.6-liter Four-Cylinder**

* **Excessive Oil Consumption**
    * Description/Symptoms: The engine may burn excessive oil, leading to low oil levels and potential engine damage.
    * Solution: Replace the piston rings and oil seals.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Timing Chain Tensioner Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The timing chain tensioner may fail, causing the timing chain to slip and potentially damaging the engine.
    * Solution: Replace the timing chain tensioner.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**Six-Speed Manual**

* **Difficulty Shifting Gears**
    * Description/Symptoms: The gears may be difficult to shift, especially when the transmission is cold.
    * Solution: Replace the transmission shift cables or linkage.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600
    * Recalls: None Found

**Six-Speed Automatic**

* **Transmission Slipping**
    * Description/Symptoms: The transmission may slip while shifting gears or when accelerating.
    * Solution: Replace the transmission valve body or torque converter.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Premature Strut Wear**
    * Description/Symptoms: The front struts may wear out prematurely, causing a rough ride and poor handling.
    * Solution: Replace the front struts.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain**
    * Description/Symptoms: The battery may drain quickly, even when the car is turned off.
    * Solution: Diagnose and repair the electrical system, such as replacing the battery or alternator.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Electrical Short**
    * Description/Symptoms: An electrical short may occur, causing various electrical components to malfunction.
    * Solution: Locate and repair the electrical short.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Sunroof Leaks**
    * Description/Symptoms: The sunroof may leak during heavy rain or car washes.
    * Solution: Replace the sunroof seals or adjust the sunroof alignment.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Dashboard Rattle**
    * Description/Symptoms: The dashboard may rattle when driving over rough roads.
    * Solution: Secure the dashboard components using adhesive or screws.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2018 Hyundai Accent has some potential issues, particularly with the engine, transmission, and suspension. The severity and cost of these issues can vary significantly, so it's important to have the vehicle thoroughly inspected before purchasing.
