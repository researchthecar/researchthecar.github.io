# 2007 Hyundai Accent

### Overview
The 2007 Hyundai Accent, a subcompact car, was generally positioned as an affordable and economical option. While it offered decent fuel efficiency and a straightforward driving experience, its long-term reliability can be a mixed bag. Owners often report satisfaction with its basic functionality and low purchase price. However, like many vehicles in its class and age, certain components may be more prone to issues than others. This analysis focuses on common reported problems, excluding routine maintenance, to provide a clearer picture of potential reliability concerns for prospective buyers or current owners.

## Transmission

### 5-Speed Manual
**No Major Issues**
- **Symptoms:** No widespread issues reported.
- **Solution:** No widespread issues reported.
- **Cost:** N/A


### 4-Speed Automatic
**Slipping or Delayed Shifting**
- **Symptoms:** Transmission may hesitate to shift gears, or feel like it's slipping between gears. Can sometimes be accompanied by a "check engine" light.
- **Solution:** Transmission fluid flush and filter replacement, or internal transmission repair/rebuild.
- **Cost:** $300 - $1200


## Suspension

### Standard Chassis
**Worn Ball Joints and Tie Rod Ends**
- **Symptoms:** Clunking noises over bumps, vague steering feel, uneven tire wear.
- **Solution:** Replacement of worn ball joints and tie rod ends.
- **Cost:** $250 - $600

**Premature Strut/Shock Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Bouncy ride, excessive body roll, visible fluid leaks from struts/shocks.
- **Solution:** Replacement of worn struts or shocks.
- **Cost:** $400 - $800


## Electrical

### General Electrical System
**Faulty Power Window Regulators**
- **Symptoms:** Power windows failing to operate, slow operation, or making grinding noises.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the power window regulator assembly.
- **Cost:** $150 - $350

**Instrument Cluster Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Intermittent or complete failure of gauges (speedometer, tachometer, fuel gauge), warning lights staying on or not illuminating.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of the instrument cluster.
- **Cost:** $300 - $700


## Exterior

### Body Panels and Paint
**Rust Formation**
- **Symptoms:** Corrosion, particularly on wheel arches, rocker panels, and around windows, especially in areas with harsh winters and road salt.
- **Solution:** Rust repair and repainting, or rust prevention treatments.
- **Cost:** $200 - $1500+


## Interior

### Cabin Materials
**Wear on Seat Upholstery**
- **Symptoms:** Tearing or excessive wear on fabric or leather seat surfaces, especially on the driver's seat.
- **Solution:** Seat cover installation or professional upholstery repair/replacement.
- **Cost:** $100 - $500

**HVAC Blower Motor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** No airflow from vents, or intermittent airflow, regardless of fan speed setting.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the HVAC blower motor and resistor.
- **Cost:** $200 - $450

### Summary Verdict
The 2007 Hyundai Accent is a budget-friendly vehicle that generally performs its basic duties well. Its 1.6L engine is considered robust with no widespread major issues. The manual transmission is typically trouble-free. However, the automatic transmission can sometimes exhibit issues with shifting. Suspension components like ball joints and tie rods may require attention as the vehicle ages, and strut wear is also noted. Electrical gremlins can include power window regulators and instrument cluster malfunctions. On the exterior, rust can be a concern in certain climates. Interior wear on seats and HVAC blower motor failures are also reported. Overall, while not without its potential problems, many of these are common for vehicles of this age and class, and can often be addressed with reasonable repair costs.
