Here's a comprehensive list of common issues and complaints for the 2017 Hyundai Accent, organized by category and component.

The 2017 Hyundai Accent is a subcompact car that offers good value for its price. It comes standard with a 1.6-liter four-cylinder engine producing 130 horsepower and 119 lb-ft of torque. Transmission options include a 6-speed manual and a 6-speed automatic. Owners generally appreciate the Accent's fuel efficiency, ease of use, and affordable price point. Common dislikes include its basic interior, limited rear seat space, and lack of advanced safety features compared to newer vehicles.

**Engine**

**1.6L GDI Inline-4**

*   **Engine Knocking/Pinging**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners report a knocking or pinging sound coming from the engine, especially under acceleration or when the engine is under load. This can be more pronounced when using lower-octane fuel.
    *   Solution: Hyundai recommends using the correct octane fuel as specified in the owner's manual. Carbon buildup may also cause the issue. If the problem persists, a dealer may need to diagnose and address potential issues such as faulty knock sensors or improper timing. Sometimes an ECU flash can resolve this issue.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $500+ depending on the source of the problem.
    * Recalls: None Found

*   **Oil Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks can develop from various locations on the engine, including the valve cover gasket, oil pan gasket, or front crankshaft seal. Symptoms include visible oil drips under the car and a burning oil smell.
    *   Solution: Replacing the leaking gasket or seal is the typical repair.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $500+ depending on the leak location.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**6-Speed Automatic Transmission**

*   **Harsh Shifting/Jerking**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners experience harsh or jerky shifts, particularly between certain gears. This can be more noticeable when the transmission is cold.
    *   Solution: A transmission fluid change with the correct Hyundai/Kia spec fluid can sometimes resolve the issue. In more severe cases, valve body issues or internal transmission damage may require professional diagnosis and repair. In rare cases a new transmission can be required.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $3,000+ depending on the issue.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**6-Speed Manual Transmission**
*   **Clutch Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners report premature clutch wear, slipping, or difficulty engaging gears.
    *   Solution: Clutch replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $600 - $1,200+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Worn Suspension Components**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Clunking noises coming from the suspension, especially when going over bumps. Poor handling and uneven tire wear may also be present.
    *   Solution: Inspection and replacement of worn components such as struts, shocks, sway bar links, or bushings.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+ per component replaced.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Battery Drain**
    * Description/Symptoms: Battery drains prematurely which can result in a no start condition
    * Solution: Replace the battery and check the alternator to make sure that it is within the correct voltage.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300
    * Recalls: None Found

*   **Daytime Running Lights (DRL) Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: DRLs stop working, either intermittently or completely.
    *   Solution: Replacing the DRL bulbs or the DRL module.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $200
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Headlight Condensation**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Moisture buildup inside the headlight housings.
    *   Solution: Replacement of headlight assembly, or attempting to seal the existing housing (temporary fix).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+ per headlight assembly.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Air Conditioning Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Weak or non-existent AC output.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis of the AC system, which may include checking for leaks, compressor failure, or a faulty blend door actuator. Refrigerant recharge, component replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $800+ depending on the issue.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2017 Hyundai Accent has a mix of minor and moderate issues. Engine-related problems like knocking and oil leaks can range in severity and cost. The transmission can exhibit harsh shifting, and the clutch in the manual transmission may wear prematurely. Electrical issues are mostly minor, but battery issues will require replacing the battery and checking the alternator voltage. Suspension components may need replacement with age. The AC system needs to be checked and fixed, especially in hotter climates. Overall, most issues are manageable, but potential buyers should inspect these areas carefully before purchasing.
