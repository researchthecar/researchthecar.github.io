**Introduction**

The 2014 Hyundai Accent is a subcompact sedan available with two engine options: a 1.6-liter inline-four and a 1.6-liter inline-four with direct injection. The 1.6-liter engine produces 138 horsepower and 123 lb-ft of torque, while the 1.6-liter GDI engine produces 148 horsepower and 131 lb-ft of torque. Both engines are paired with a six-speed manual transmission or a six-speed automatic transmission.

Owners of the 2014 Hyundai Accent praise its fuel efficiency, affordability, and maneuverability. However, they also report several issues with the vehicle, including engine problems, transmission issues, and electrical problems.

**Engine**

**1.6-liter Inline-Four**

* **Engine knocking:** Description/Symptoms: A knocking sound coming from the engine, especially when accelerating or under load. Solution: Replacing the piston rings and/or connecting rod bearings. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
* **Oil leaks from valve cover gasket:** Description/Symptoms: Oil leaking from the valve cover gasket, causing a burning oil smell in the engine bay. Solution: Replacing the valve cover gasket. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
* **Timing chain noise:** Description/Symptoms: A rattling or grinding noise coming from the engine, especially on cold starts. Solution: Replacing the timing chain. Estimated Cost to Fix: $600 - $1,000

**1.6-liter Inline-Four GDI**

* **Fuel injector failure:** Description/Symptoms: The fuel injectors fail, causing misfiring and engine performance issues. Solution: Replacing the fuel injectors. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800+
* **Intake manifold runner control valve failure:** Description/Symptoms: The intake manifold runner control valve fails, causing a loss of power and performance. Solution: Replacing the intake manifold runner control valve. Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $600+

**Transmission**

**Six-Speed Manual Transmission**

* **Grinding gears:** Description/Symptoms: The transmission grinds when shifting gears, especially into first or second gear. Solution: Replacing the synchronizers. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $700
* **Clutch failure:** Description/Symptoms: The clutch fails, causing the car to slip out of gear and making it difficult to shift. Solution: Replacing the clutch. Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,200+

**Six-Speed Automatic Transmission**

* **Transmission slipping:** Description/Symptoms: The transmission slips out of gear while driving. Solution: Replacing the transmission solenoid pack. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800+
* **Delayed engagement:** Description/Symptoms: The transmission hesitates or delays when shifting gears. Solution: Replacing the transmission fluid and filter. Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300

**Suspension**

* **Strut mount failure:** Description/Symptoms: The strut mount fails, causing a clunking noise when driving over bumps. Solution: Replacing the strut mount. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $300 per strut

**Electrical**

* **Battery drain:** Description/Symptoms: The battery drains prematurely, leaving the car unable to start. Solution: Replacing the battery or repairing the electrical system. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
* **Electrical short:** Description/Symptoms: The electrical system experiences a short, causing fuses to blow and components to fail. Solution: Repairing the electrical short. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+

**Exterior**

* **Door handle breakage:** Description/Symptoms: The door handle breaks, making it difficult to open the door. Solution: Replacing the door handle. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200

**Interior**

* **Window regulator failure:** Description/Symptoms: The window regulator fails, causing the window to become stuck or fall down. Solution: Replacing the window regulator. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+
* **Dashboard rattling:** Description/Symptoms: The dashboard rattles or vibrates when driving over rough roads. Solution: Tightening the dashboard mounting points. Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the dealer

**Summary**

The 2014 Hyundai Accent is a fuel-efficient and affordable vehicle with several reported issues. The engine problems, transmission issues, and electrical problems are the most serious and costly to repair. Owners may also experience issues with the suspension, exterior, and interior of the vehicle. However, these issues are typically minor and less expensive to fix.
