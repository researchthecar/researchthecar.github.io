**Introduction**

The 2016 Hyundai Equus is a full-size luxury sedan known for its spacious interior, smooth ride, and powerful engine. It is powered by a standard 5.0-liter V8 engine that produces 429 horsepower and 376 lb-ft of torque. Transmission options include a 6-speed automatic and an 8-speed automatic.

**Engine**

* **5.0-liter V8**
    * **Intake Valve Carbon Buildup**
        * Description/Symptoms: Over time, carbon deposits can build up on the intake valves, restricting airflow and reducing engine performance. Symptoms may include reduced power, hesitation, and rough idling.
        * Solution: Intake valve cleaning or walnut blasting to remove carbon deposits.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
        * Recalls: None Found
    * **Timing Chain Guide Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: The timing chain guides can wear prematurely, causing the timing chain to skip or break. This can lead to catastrophic engine damage. Symptoms may include a rattling noise from the engine, reduced power, and Check Engine Light illumination.
        * Solution: Replacement of the timing chain guides and timing chain.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $3,000 - $6,000+
        * Recalls: 19V-619

**Transmission**

* **6-speed Automatic**
    * **Torque Converter Damper Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: The torque converter damper can fail, causing vibrations and a noticeable shudder during acceleration.
        * Solution: Replacement of the torque converter.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,000+
        * Recalls: None Found
* **8-speed Automatic**
    * **Valve Body Malfunction**
        * Description/Symptoms: The valve body can malfunction, leading to shifting issues, hesitation, and slipping gears.
        * Solution: Replacement of the valve body.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Air Suspension Compressor Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The air suspension compressor can fail, causing the vehicle to sag or ride low.
    * Solution: Replacement of the air suspension compressor.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $1,500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain Issue**
    * Description/Symptoms: The vehicle may experience a parasitic battery drain, leading to a dead battery.
    * Solution: Diagnosis and repair of the electrical source causing the drain, such as a faulty module or wiring issue.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Rear View Camera Malfunction**
    * Description/Symptoms: The rear view camera may malfunction, displaying a blank screen or distorted image.
    * Solution: Replacement of the rear view camera.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800+
    * Recalls: 21V-629

**Exterior**

* **Headlight Condensation**
    * Description/Symptoms: Moisture may accumulate inside the headlight housing, leading to fogging and reduced visibility.
    * Solution: Replacement of the headlight assembly.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800+ pair
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Climate Control Panel Malfunction**
    * Description/Symptoms: The climate control panel may malfunction, leading to issues with temperature control, fan speed, and airflow direction.
    * Solution: Replacement of the climate control panel.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Rear Sunshade Motor Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The motor for the rear window sunshade may fail, preventing the sunshade from operating.
    * Solution: Replacement of the rear window sunshade motor.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2016 Hyundai Equus is a well-regarded vehicle with a comfortable interior and powerful engine. However, it is not without its share of issues. The most concerning problems are the timing chain guide failure and transmission torque converter damper failure, which can be expensive to repair. The electrical problems and exterior condensation issue are less severe but still worth noting. Overall, the Equus has a moderate number of issues, some of which can be costly to fix.
