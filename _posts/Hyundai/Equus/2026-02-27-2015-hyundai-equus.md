# 2015 Hyundai Equus

### Overview
The 2015 Hyundai Equus, a full-size luxury sedan, aimed to compete with established German and Japanese rivals by offering a blend of opulent features, powerful performance, and a compelling value proposition. Under the hood, it was primarily equipped with a potent V8 engine, paired with an automatic transmission, designed for smooth and effortless cruising. The vehicle's construction emphasized comfort and refinement, with a focus on a quiet cabin and a compliant ride. While generally well-received for its amenities and power, like any vehicle, it has specific areas where owners have reported concerns. This analysis focuses on documented technical reliability issues beyond routine maintenance.

## Suspension

### Adaptive Control Suspension
**Air Suspension Leaks/Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle sagging on one or more corners, uneven ride height, 'air suspension fault' warning light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of leaking air springs, air compressor, or associated lines. Sometimes requires recalibration.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1500


## Electrical

### Infotainment System
**Screen Freezing/Unresponsiveness**
- **Symptoms:** Touchscreen becomes unresponsive, system freezes, navigation glitches.
- **Solution:** Software update or replacement of the infotainment unit.
- **Cost:** $300 - $1200


### Battery Drain
**Intermittent Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Battery dies unexpectedly, requiring jump starts or replacement.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis to identify parasitic draw from modules (e.g., infotainment, comfort modules) and repair or replace faulty components.
- **Cost:** $200 - $800

### Climate Control System
**Dual-Zone Climate Control Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** Inconsistent temperature control between driver and passenger sides, blower motor issues.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and repair/replacement of blend door actuators, climate control module, or blower motor resistor.
- **Cost:** $300 - $900

### Summary Verdict
The 2015 Hyundai Equus generally presents a strong reliability record, particularly in its powertrain. The 5.0L V8 engine and 8-speed automatic transmission are robust and rarely present significant issues. However, potential owners should be aware of a few specific areas. The adaptive air suspension system can be a source of costly repairs if leaks or component failures occur. Electrical gremlins, such as infotainment system glitches and intermittent battery drain, are also noted concerns that may require professional diagnosis. Interior climate control issues can also arise. Despite these points, the Equus offers a luxurious experience with a solid foundation, provided these potential issues are addressed proactively.
