**Introduction:**

The 2011 Hyundai Genesis is a mid-size luxury sedan powered by a 3.8L V6 engine with 290 horsepower and 264 lb-ft of torque. It comes standard with a six-speed automatic transmission and is available with an optional five-speed automatic transmission. The Genesis is praised for its spacious interior, refined ride, and strong performance but is criticized for its outdated infotainment system and firm suspension.

**Engine:**

**3.8L V6**

* **Intake Manifold Issue:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Intake manifold gaskets fail, causing vacuum leaks and reduced engine power.
    * Solution: Replace intake manifold gaskets.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,200+
* **Timing Chain Issue:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Timing chain stretches or breaks, leading to engine damage.
    * Solution: Replace timing chain and related components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+
* **Valve Train Noise:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Excessive noise from valvetrain components, such as lifters or rocker arms.
    * Solution: Replace or adjust affected components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
* **Recalls:**
    * N/A

**Transmission:**

**Six-Speed Automatic**

* **Slipping Gears:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Transmission fails to engage gears or slips while driving.
    * Solution: Replace or rebuild transmission.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
* **Shift Solenoid Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Transmission shifts erratically or refuses to shift.
    * Solution: Replace shift solenoid(s).
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
* **Recalls:**
    * NHTSA Campaign Number: 11V171000 - Transmission Control Module Software Update

**Five-Speed Automatic**

* **No Issues Found**

**Suspension:**

* **Strut Mount Noise:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Knocking or rattling noise from front suspension when driving over bumps.
    * Solution: Replace strut mounts.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800+
* **Rear Shock Absorber Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Leaking or worn rear shock absorbers cause poor ride handling.
    * Solution: Replace rear shock absorbers.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800+

**Electrical:**

* **Electrical System Drain:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Battery drains quickly even when the vehicle is not in use.
    * Solution: Diagnose and repair electrical system issue.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the specific issue
* **Headlight Switch Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Headlights fail to turn on or switch between modes.
    * Solution: Replace headlight switch.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
* **Recalls:**
    * NHTSA Campaign Number: 11E015000 - Headlamp Relay

**Exterior:**

* **Trunk Lid Release Issue:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Trunk lid does not open when the release button is pressed.
    * Solution: Replace or adjust trunk lid release mechanism.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
* **Sunroof Seal Leaks:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Water leaks into the cabin through the sunroof seals.
    * Solution: Replace or adjust sunroof seals.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+

**Interior:**

* **Climate Control Malfunction:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Climate control system fails to regulate temperature or air flow.
    * Solution: Diagnose and repair climate control system.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the specific issue
* **Radio Antenna Issue:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Radio reception is weak or intermittent.
    * Solution: Replace or adjust radio antenna.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+

**Summary:**

Overall, the 2011 Hyundai Genesis is a reliable vehicle with some potential issues. The severity and frequency of these issues vary, and the estimated cost of repairs can range from minor to major. However, it is important to note that these issues are not widespread and the Genesis is generally considered to be a well-built and dependable car.
