**2021 Hyundai Venue**

The 2021 Hyundai Venue is a subcompact crossover SUV that offers a comfortable and spacious interior, user-friendly technology features, and good fuel efficiency. It is available with a 1.6-liter four-cylinder engine and six-speed manual, intelligent variable transmission (IVT), or seven-speed dual-clutch transmission (DCT).

Engine: 1.6-liter four-cylinder

No Issues Found

Transmission: Six-Speed Manual

**Rough Shifting**
Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears smoothly, especially when shifting into first or second gear. There may also be a grinding or popping sound during shifting.
Solution: Replacing or adjusting the shift linkage, clutch disc, or transmission fluid may resolve the issue.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
Recalls: None Found

Transmission: Intelligent Variable Transmission (IVT)

**Transmission Shuddering**
Description/Symptoms: The transmission may shudder or vibrate during acceleration or deceleration, especially at low speeds. This can be caused by worn or damaged transmission components, such as belts or pulleys.
Solution: Replacing the transmission fluid, pulleys, or belts may alleviate the shuddering.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
Recalls: None Found

Transmission: Seven-Speed Dual-Clutch Transmission (DCT)

No Issues Found

Suspension

**Rear Suspension Noise**
Description/Symptoms: A clunking or rattling noise may be heard from the rear suspension when driving over rough roads or making sharp turns. This could be caused by worn or loose suspension components, such as the sway bar links or bushings.
Solution: Replacing the affected suspension components should eliminate the noise.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
Recalls: None Found

Electrical

No Issues Found

Exterior

**Wind Noise**
Description/Symptoms: Excessive wind noise may be heard from around the windows or doors while driving at highway speeds. This can be caused by poor sealing around the doors or windows, or by misaligned body panels.
Solution: Adjusting or replacing the door or window seals, or realigning the body panels should reduce the wind noise.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
Recalls: None Found

**Broken Tail Light**
Description/Symptoms: The tail light may become cracked, broken, or malfunctioning, causing it to fail to illuminate properly. This could be due to a collision, vandalism, or a manufacturing defect.
Solution: Replacing the broken tail light assembly is necessary to restore proper lighting.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
Recalls: None Found

Interior

**Loose Headliner**
Description/Symptoms: The headliner fabric may become loose and sag, especially in warm weather. This can be caused by poor adhesive or heat damage.
Solution: Reattaching or replacing the headliner fabric or padding can fix this issue.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
Recalls: None Found

**Cracked Dashboard**
Description/Symptoms: The dashboard may develop cracks or splits, especially in areas exposed to sunlight. This can be caused by excessive heat or UV exposure, or by improper installation.
Solution: Replacing the cracked dashboard is typically required to resolve this issue.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2021 Hyundai Venue is generally a reliable and well-built vehicle, but it does have some potential issues. The most common problems reported by owners include rough shifting in the manual transmission, transmission shuddering in the IVT, rear suspension noise, excessive wind noise, broken tail lights, loose headliner, and cracked dashboard. The severity and cost of these issues can vary depending on the specific cause and the extent of the damage. It is important to have any potential issues inspected and repaired by a qualified mechanic as soon as possible to avoid further damage or safety concerns.
