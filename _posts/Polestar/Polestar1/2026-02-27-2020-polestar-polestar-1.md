# 2020 Polestar Polestar 1 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2020 Polestar 1 is a low-volume, high-performance plug-in hybrid GT car that combines a turbocharged and supercharged 2.0-liter four-cylinder engine with dual rear electric motors. Because of its limited production numbers and carbon-fiber body construction, it is an exotic grand tourer rather than a mainstream commuter vehicle. Buyers exploring the pre-owned market should keep in mind that specialized servicing and parts availability will require access to dedicated certified facilities.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged & Supercharged I4 Hybrid

This 2.0-liter four-cylinder engine utilizes both a supercharger and a turbocharger to deliver robust power paired with electric propulsion, producing a combined output of 619 horsepower and 738 lb-ft of torque. It offers exhilarating performance but relies on complex hybrid and forced-induction systems that require meticulous maintenance.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

An 8-speed automatic transmission handles power delivery to the front wheels while seamlessly integrating with the hybrid powertrain. It provides smooth shifts suited for grand touring, though long-term durability depends heavily on proper fluid maintenance.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V926000: Electrical System:12V/24V/48V Battery](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Polestar&model=Polestar+1&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** Defective high-voltage battery cells may cause the battery to overheat when fully charged. The high-voltage battery overheating increases the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the high-voltage battery cells, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V361000: Electrical System:12V/24V/48V Battery](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Polestar&model=Polestar+1&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** Defective high-voltage battery cells may cause the battery to overheat when fully charged. The high-voltage battery overheating increases the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the high-voltage battery pack and restore the electric driving range as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V835000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Pedals And Linkages](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Polestar&model=Polestar+1&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** Brake pedal detachment can cause a loss of brake function and increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will tighten the pushrod screw joint, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2020 Polestar 1 remains an exclusive and visually striking plug-in hybrid grand tourer with impressive performance credentials. Due to its niche status, scarcity of independent repair data, and complex powertrain integration, prospective buyers should thoroughly inspect service histories and verify access to specialized technician support before purchasing.
