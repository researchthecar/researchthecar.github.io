**2020 Polestar Polestar 1**

The 2020 Polestar Polestar 1 is a plug-in hybrid grand touring coupe that combines a 2.0L twin-charged inline-4 gasoline engine with two electric motors for a combined 619 horsepower and 738 lb-ft of torque. The gasoline engine powers the front wheels through an 8-speed automatic transmission, while the electric motors power the rear wheels. The Polestar 1 is praised for its striking design, luxurious interior, and impressive performance, but criticisms include its high price and limited electric-only range.

**Engine**

* **2.0L Twin-Charged Inline-4 Gasoline Engine**

    * **Turbocharger Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: Excessive smoke from the exhaust, loss of power, and a whistling or whining sound from the turbocharger.
        * Solution: Replacement of the turbocharger.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,200 - $2,000+
        * Recalls: None Found
    * **Fuel Pump Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: Difficulty starting the vehicle, loss of power while driving, and a buzzing or clicking sound from the fuel tank.
        * Solution: Replacement of the fuel pump.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

* **8-Speed Automatic Transmission**

    * **Shifting Problems**
        * Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears, slipping gears, or a grinding noise when shifting.
        * Solution: Repair or replacement of the transmission.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $5,000+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Front Suspension**

    * **Strut Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: Noise from the front of the vehicle when going over bumps, uneven tire wear, and a sagging ride height.
        * Solution: Replacement of the struts.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $600 - $1,200+
        * Recalls: None Found

* **Rear Suspension**

    * **Shock Absorber Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: Reduced ride comfort, excessive body roll, and a bouncing or floating sensation when driving.
        * Solution: Replacement of the shock absorbers.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Battery**

    * **Premature Battery Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: Rapid loss of battery life, decreased driving range, and difficulty charging the battery.
        * Solution: Replacement of the battery.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $5,000 - $10,000+
        * Recalls: None Found

* **Electrical Harness**

    * **Wiring Harness Faults**
        * Description/Symptoms: Electrical malfunctions, such as flickering lights, inoperative accessories, or problems starting the vehicle.
        * Solution: Repair or replacement of the wiring harness.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,000+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Body Panels**

    * **Panel Gaps**
        * Description/Symptoms: Uneven gaps between body panels, causing a misaligned appearance.
        * Solution: Adjustment or replacement of body panels.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Climate Control**

    * **AC Compressor Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: Inability to cool the cabin, warm air blowing from the vents, and a lack of refrigerant.
        * Solution: Replacement of the AC compressor.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,500+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2020 Polestar Polestar 1 faces some reliability issues that can be costly to repair. While the vehicle offers a high level of performance and luxury, potential buyers should be aware of potential problems with the turbocharger, fuel pump, and battery. It is recommended to thoroughly inspect the vehicle and consider an extended warranty to mitigate the potential for significant repair expenses.
