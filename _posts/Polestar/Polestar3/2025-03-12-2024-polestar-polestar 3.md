The 2024 Polestar 3 is an all-electric performance SUV from the Swedish automaker. It boasts a sleek, minimalist design and advanced technology. Available powertrains include two dual-motor configurations. The Long Range Dual Motor produces 489 horsepower and 620 lb-ft of torque, while the Performance Pack equipped version boosts output to 517 horsepower and 671 lb-ft of torque. Some owners appreciate the Polestar 3's sophisticated styling, powerful acceleration, and advanced driver-assistance features. Conversely, some common dislikes include a relatively high price point, limited charging infrastructure in some areas, and the potential for software glitches that are common in new EVs.

**Electric Motor**

**Long Range Dual Motor (489 hp / 620 lb-ft)**

*   **Software Glitches Affecting Performance and Range Calculation**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Owners report inconsistent range estimations, sudden drops in indicated range, and instances where the car struggles to deliver the expected power output. This can lead to range anxiety and affect the overall driving experience.
    *   Solution: Software updates from Polestar are often deployed to address these issues.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: Covered under warranty.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Performance Pack (517 hp / 671 lb-ft)**

*   **Battery Management System (BMS) Errors**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported BMS errors that can lead to reduced charging speeds, inaccurate battery level readings, or even the vehicle entering a "limp home" mode. These errors can sometimes be triggered by rapid charging or extreme temperatures.
    *   Solution: Requires diagnosis and potential replacement of BMS components by a certified Polestar technician. In some cases, a software update can resolve the issue.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: Covered under warranty.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Air Suspension Malfunctions (Optional)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Owners who opted for the air suspension system sometimes report issues with the system not leveling properly, uneven ride height, or complete failure of the system, resulting in a very stiff ride. Error messages are also common.
    *   Solution: The air suspension system must be diagnosed by a certified technician. Repairs may involve replacing air struts, compressors, or control modules.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: Covered under warranty.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Freezing/Crashing**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The Android Automotive-based infotainment system occasionally freezes, crashes, or experiences lag. This can affect navigation, audio playback, and access to vehicle settings.
    *   Solution: Performing a system reset or waiting for software updates from Polestar can resolve the issue.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: Covered under warranty.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **12V Battery Drain Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported that the 12V battery, which powers auxiliary systems, drains prematurely, leading to the car not starting. This can be caused by software bugs or issues with the car's charging system.
    *   Solution: Diagnosing the cause of the drain is crucial. It may involve replacing the 12V battery, updating software, or repairing the charging system.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: Covered under warranty if caused by a defect. Otherwise, $150 - $300+ for battery replacement.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Sensor Malfunctions (Parking Sensors, Cameras)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported that parking sensors or cameras become unreliable or stop functioning altogether, leading to difficulty parking or using driver-assistance features. This can be due to sensor damage, software glitches, or wiring issues.
    *   Solution: Inspection and replacement of faulty sensors or cameras. Software updates might also be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: Covered under warranty.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Creaking and Rattling Noises**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported creaking and rattling noises emanating from various interior panels, particularly the dashboard and door panels. This can be more pronounced in colder weather.
    *   Solution: Identifying the source of the noise and applying sound-deadening materials or tightening loose panels.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: Covered under warranty.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2024 Polestar 3, while praised for its performance and design, has encountered some common issues. The most prevalent problems seem to revolve around electrical components, including infotainment glitches, BMS errors, and software-related issues affecting range calculations. Suspension problems also arise. The severity of these issues ranges from minor inconveniences to potentially impacting the vehicle's drivability. Most repairs are covered under warranty, though out-of-pocket expenses for minor issues like 12V battery replacements or sensor malfunctions can be expected.
