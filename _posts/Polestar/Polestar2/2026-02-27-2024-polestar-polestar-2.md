# 2024 Polestar Polestar 2 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2024 Polestar 2 represents a significant evolutionary step for the brand's all-electric fastback, introducing reworked electric motor options and an improved rear-wheel-drive bias for single-motor variants. While buyers praise its styling, performance, and interior comfort, early adopter owner feedback highlights a handful of software and electrical bugs—most notably concerning camera functionality and climate controls. Prospective buyers should carefully review software versions and vehicle history during shopping.

---

## Engine

### Single Motor (Standard Range)

This base single-motor configuration utilizes a permanent magnet synchronous motor producing roughly 270 hp and 361 lb-ft of torque, sending power to the rear wheels. It offers an efficient, punchy daily-driving experience with very few mechanical powertrain complaints.

### Single Motor (Long Range)

Pairing a larger battery pack with a rear-mounted motor generating around 299 hp and 361 lb-ft of torque, this variant maximizes cruising distance. It balances extended range capabilities with the updated rear-wheel-drive dynamics introduced for the model year.

### Dual Motor (Long Range)

Utilizing dual asynchronous/synchronous electric motors to produce approximately 421 hp and 546 lb-ft of torque with all-wheel drive, this performance-focused option delivers rapid acceleration. The heavy-duty setup is generally robust, though complex software manages torque distribution across both axles.

## Transmission

### Single-Speed Direct-Drive Transmission

A fixed-gear, single-speed reduction transmission manages power delivery across all motor configurations. This direct-drive unit lacks traditional gear shifting, resulting in minimal mechanical wear and high overall reliability.

## Suspension

### Tires and Wheels

The chassis suspension handles the substantial weight of the battery pack, placing higher demands on wheel alignment and tire longevity.

**Accelerated Rear Tire Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Factory-installed grand touring tires on rear-wheel-drive configurations may wear through tread prematurely due to the vehicle's instant torque and heavy curb weight.
- **Solution:** Monitor tire tread depth regularly and replace with high-load-rated EV-specific tires when necessary.

## Electrical

### Climate Control System

The central touchscreen and digital architecture control nearly all vehicle functions, making software stability critical for everyday usability.

**Unresponsive Climate Interface**
- **Symptoms:** On humid evenings or sudden weather shifts, climate control buttons can gray out on the central touchscreen, locking out access to front or rear defrosters and severely compromising cabin visibility.
- **Solution:** Perform an infotainment system reboot or check for dealer-installed software patches addressing touchscreen responsiveness.

### Backup Camera and Surround View

Advanced driver assistance systems and parking aids rely heavily on exterior sensors and cameras, which can occasionally experience software-related glitches.

**Intermittent Camera Failure**
- **Symptoms:** When shifting into reverse, the rearview camera display may remain blank, show a temporary unavailable warning, or unexpectedly cycle through 360-degree views.
- **Solution:** Apply manufacturer over-the-air (OTA) software updates intended to resolve camera module communication errors.

## Summary Verdict

The 2024 Polestar 2 is an appealing choice for used electric vehicle buyers seeking Scandinavian design, strong performance, and competitive range figures. While its core electric motors and single-speed transmissions are mechanically dependable, shoppers should verify that all over-the-air software updates have been successfully applied to mitigate recurring touchscreen and backup camera glitches. A pre-purchase inspection focusing on software versions, tire wear, and accessory electronics is recommended.
