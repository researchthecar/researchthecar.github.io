# 2022 Polestar Polestar 2 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The Polestar 2 is an all-electric fastback that combines sleek Scandinavian design with engaging performance. As an EV, it relies on electric drive motors and a single-speed direct-drive transmission rather than a traditional internal combustion powertrain. While many owners praise its driving dynamics and interior comfort, prospective used car buyers should pay close attention to software stability, electrical charging systems, and suspension components.

---

## Engine

### Single-Motor Electric Drive

The single-motor electric drive offers efficient rear-wheel-drive propulsion with solid torque delivery. Overall reliability is generally tied to stable software calibration for power delivery and charging.

**False Charging Disconnect and Acceleration Lag**
- **Symptoms:** Software updates have occasionally triggered false 'Disconnect Charging Cable' messages immobilizing the vehicle, or introduced an acceleration lag upon startup.
- **Solution:** Install subsequent software patches or visit an authorized service center to reset powertrain software calibration.

### Dual-Motor Electric Drive

The dual-motor setup provides high-output all-wheel-drive performance and brisk acceleration. It shares the same underlying electrical architecture and software controls as the single-motor variant.

## Transmission

### Single-Speed Direct Drive

The single-speed direct-drive transmission routes power seamlessly from the electric motors to the wheels without traditional gear shifting. It is mechanically simple and generally robust, though occasional powertrain communication faults can trigger restricted 'turtle mode' operation.

## Suspension

### Suspension Struts and Bearings

Suspension components, particularly on models equipped with performance damping packages, can develop rattles or clunks over rough pavement that require component replacement.

**Front Strut Rattling and Rear Clunking**
- **Symptoms:** Noticeable knocking, clunking, or rattling noises emanating from the front or rear suspension over bumps at low speeds.
- **Solution:** Inspect and replace worn or loose strut bearings and suspension bushings.

## Electrical

### Backup Camera and Infotainment Display

Rear visibility features heavily rely on central touchscreen software stability, which can occasionally experience lag or temporary camera feed dropouts.

**Backup Camera Unavailable and Infotainment Freezing**
- **Symptoms:** The central display freezes upon startup, or the rearview camera fails to activate when shifting into reverse, displaying a 'Camera is temporarily unavailable' error.
- **Solution:** Perform infotainment system resets or apply manufacturer software updates designed to improve camera startup times.

## Exterior

### On-Board Charger and Door Locks

Electrical and charging architecture issues, such as on-board charger failures and accessory lock glitches, require careful inspection when shopping for a used model.

**On-Board Charger Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle suddenly stops accepting Level 2 or DC fast charging without prior warning.
- **Solution:** Replace the on-board charger (OBC) unit.

**Inoperable Door Latches**
- **Symptoms:** Door locks become unresponsive while parked, requiring the key fob for entry.
- **Solution:** Diagnose door lock actuators and update or replace faulty latch control modules.

## Summary Verdict

The Polestar 2 is an appealing and distinctive electric vehicle option on the used market, offering strong performance and modern tech. However, prospective buyers should thoroughly test all electrical accessories, verify that infotainment and backup camera software updates have been properly applied, and listen closely for any suspension noises during a test drive.
