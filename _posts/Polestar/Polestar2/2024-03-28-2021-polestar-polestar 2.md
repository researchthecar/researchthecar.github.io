**Introduction**

The 2021 Polestar Polestar 2 is a premium electric vehicle that offers a combination of performance and sustainability. It is available in two trims: Standard Range Single Motor and Long Range Dual Motor. The Standard Range model features a single electric motor that produces 228 horsepower and 243 lb-ft of torque, while the Long Range model has two electric motors that deliver 408 horsepower and 487 lb-ft of torque. Both models have a top speed of 115 mph and can accelerate from 0-60 mph in under 6 seconds.

Owners appreciate the Polestar 2's sleek design, comfortable interior, and engaging driving experience. However, there have been some reported issues with certain components.

**Suspension**

* **No Issues Found**

**Electrical**

* **12V Battery Issue**
    Description/Symptoms: Premature failure of the 12V battery, leading to electrical system malfunctions such as loss of power.
    Solution: Replacement of the 12V battery.
    Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
    Recalls: None Found
* **Infotainment System Glitch**
    Description/Symptoms: Occasional freezing or lagging of the infotainment system, requiring a restart.
    Solution: Software updates or reset of the system.
    Estimated Cost to Fix: Free (under warranty)
    Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Convertible Top Issue (Polestar 2 Convertible)**
    Description/Symptoms: Malfunction of the convertible top mechanism, resulting in difficulties in opening or closing the top.
    Solution: Repair or replacement of affected components.
    Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    Recalls: 22V-497

**Interior**

* **Climate Control Malfunction**
    Description/Symptoms: Failure of the climate control system to effectively regulate cabin temperature.
    Solution: Repair or replacement of the climate control module or related components.
    Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
    Recalls: 22V-826
* **Navigation System Error**
    Description/Symptoms: Errors or failures in the navigation system, leading to incorrect directions or loss of functionality.
    Solution: Software updates or replacement of the navigation module.
    Estimated Cost to Fix: Free (under warranty)
    Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

Overall, the 2021 Polestar Polestar 2 is a well-built and reliable vehicle. However, there have been some issues reported with the electrical system, exterior components, and interior features. The severity of these issues ranges from minor inconveniences to more serious problems that require repair or replacement. The estimated cost of repairs varies depending on the specific issue and the affected component.
