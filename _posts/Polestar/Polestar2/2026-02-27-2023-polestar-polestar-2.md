# 2023 Polestar Polestar 2 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2023 Polestar 2, buyers will find an appealing, fully electric fastback that blends minimalist Scandinavian design with athletic performance. However, owner reports highlight that software stability and camera reliability warrant close inspection during a pre-purchase test drive.

---

## Engine

### Single Motor Electric Powertrain

The single-motor variant features a rear-wheel-drive layout producing roughly 299 horsepower and 361 lb-ft of torque, offering brisk acceleration and efficient everyday driving. This setup is generally robust mechanically, though owners should verify that all software updates have been properly applied.

### Dual Motor Electric Powertrain

The dual-motor variant pairs front and rear electric motors for all-wheel-drive capability, generating up to 421 horsepower (or 455 horsepower with the Performance Pack). It delivers rapid acceleration and confident all-weather traction, anchored by a reliable electric drive unit architecture.

## Transmission

### Single-Speed Direct-Drive Transmission

The single-speed direct-drive transmission routes power seamlessly from the electric motor(s) to the wheels without traditional gear shifting. This simplified design eliminates conventional multi-gear mechanical wear points for a smooth and low-maintenance driving experience.

## Electrical

### Back Over Prevention / Backup Camera

The backup camera and associated safety systems rely heavily on the vehicle's central software architecture, which can occasionally experience temporary dropouts.

**Intermittent Backup Camera Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers frequently report that the rearview backup camera displays a "temporarily unavailable" message, goes entirely black, or flickers off and on when shifting into reverse.
- **Solution:** Dealerships address software-related camera glitches through over-the-air updates or infotainment module resets, though some owners experience recurring issues.

### Infotainment System

The Android Automotive-powered infotainment hub controls a vast majority of the vehicle's interior functions, making software stability critical for everyday usability.

**Infotainment Screen Freezing and Reboots**
- **Symptoms:** The central display screen may unexpectedly freeze, crash, or reboot while driving, temporarily disabling climate controls, turn signal audio cues, and backup camera visibility.
- **Solution:** Rebooting the system manually or installing the latest software patches typically restores normal operation.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V476000: Suspension:Front:Control Arm:Lower Ball Joint](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Polestar&model=Polestar+2&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** The control arm disconnecting from the steering knuckle can cause a loss of steering, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the ball joint fastening bolt as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V477000: Equipment:Electrical:Infotainment:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Polestar&model=Polestar+2&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 111, "Rear Visibility." A rearview camera image that does not display decreases the driver's visibility, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 25V280000: Back Over Prevention:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Polestar&model=Polestar+2&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** The rearview camera image may not display when the vehicle is placed in reverse. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 111, "Rear Visibility." A rearview camera image that does not display reduces the driver's view of what is behind the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the vehicle software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2023 Polestar 2 is a stylish and capable electric vehicle, but potential buyers should thoroughly check the infotainment and backup camera functionality during a test drive. Ensuring that all past software updates have been performed by a dealer will help mitigate common electronic glitches.
