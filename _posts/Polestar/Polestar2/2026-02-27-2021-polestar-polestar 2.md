# 2021 Polestar Polestar 2

### Overview
The 2021 Polestar Polestar 2, an all-electric performance sedan, has generally demonstrated strong reliability in its inaugural year. As an electric vehicle, its mechanical complexity is inherently lower than internal combustion engine counterparts, leading to fewer traditional powertrain issues. Early reports indicate a robust build quality and well-integrated software. However, as with any new model, some minor electronic glitches and user interface quirks have been noted, which are not uncommon for first-generation vehicles. Overall, the Polestar 2 presents a compelling package for consumers seeking a blend of performance, technology, and a positive ownership experience, with a focus on addressing any early-stage software or minor component refinements.

### Summary Verdict
The 2021 Polestar Polestar 2 exhibits a commendable level of reliability for a debut model, particularly in its core electric powertrain components. The absence of widespread issues in the engine and transmission categories is a strong indicator of robust engineering. The primary areas for attention are within the electrical system, specifically minor infotainment glitches that are often resolved with software updates, and a potential for premature failure of the 12V auxiliary battery, a common concern in many EVs. Exterior and interior components have largely proven durable. Overall, the Polestar 2 offers a reliable electric vehicle experience, with most reported issues being minor and addressable through software or standard component replacement.
