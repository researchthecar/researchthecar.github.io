The 2017 Lincoln Continental is a full-size luxury sedan that was offered with a 3.7-liter V6 engine producing 305 horsepower and 280 lb-ft of torque, or a 3.0-liter twin-turbocharged V6 that produced 400 horsepower and 400 lb-ft of torque. Both engines could be paired with a 6-speed automatic transmission.

**Engine**

**3.7-liter V6**

* **Engine Stalling**
    * Description/Symptoms: Vehicle randomly stalls while at idle or low speeds.
    * Solution: Reprogram Powertrain Control Module (PCM)
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $400
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Oil Leaks**
    * Description/Symptoms: Oil leak from engine block, oil pan, or oil filter housing
    * Solution: Replace leaking component(s), inspect for other leaks or damage
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Timing Chain Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Reduction in engine power, rough idle, excessive engine noise, misfires, illuminated check engine light
    * Solution: Replace timing chain and related components
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $5,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**3.0-liter Twin-Turbocharged V6**

* **Turbocharger Failures**
    * Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, excessive smoke, turbocharger noises, check engine light
    * Solution: Replace turbocharger(s)
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,500 - $4,000+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Intake Valve Deposits**
    * Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, reduced fuel economy, rough idle, misfires, illuminated check engine light
    * Solution: Clean or replace intake valves, decarbonize engine
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,200
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**6-Speed Automatic Transmission**

* **Shifting Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: Transmission slips, delays in shifting, rough shifting, loss of gears
    * Solution: Replace transmission control module (TCM), overhaul transmission
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $3,000+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Transmission Fluid Leaks**
    * Description/Symptoms: Transmission fluid leaks from pan, seals, or lines
    * Solution: Repair or replace leaking components
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,000
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Air Suspension Malfunctions**
    * Description/Symptoms: Suspension too soft or stiff, compressor failure, air leaks
    * Solution: Repair or replace faulty air suspension components
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Control Arm Bushings**
    * Description/Symptoms: Excessive road noise, vibration, steering wobble
    * Solution: Replace worn control arm bushings
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain**
    * Description/Symptoms: Battery drains overnight or within a few days, even if the car is not used
    * Solution: Test and replace faulty battery or alternator, inspect for electrical leaks
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Electrical Shorts**
    * Description/Symptoms: Blown fuses, flickering lights, radio malfunctions, other electrical issues
    * Solution: Repair or replace damaged electrical wiring
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Headlight Condensation**
    * Description/Symptoms: Moisture inside headlight assemblies
    * Solution: Replace leaky headlights, inspect for damage
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Climate Control Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: Air conditioning or heating problems, uneven temperature, blower fan not working
    * Solution: Repair or replace faulty climate control components
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Infotainment System Malfunctions**
    * Description/Symptoms: Touchscreen issues, software bugs, Bluetooth connectivity problems
    * Solution: Update software, replace infotainment modules
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2017 Lincoln Continental is a luxurious sedan with a spacious interior and powerful engines. However, it has had some reliability issues, including engine stalling, transmission problems, suspension malfunctions, and electrical shorts. The severity and cost of these issues can vary, with some costing hundreds of dollars to fix while others can be much more expensive. Potential buyers should be aware of these issues before considering a purchase.
