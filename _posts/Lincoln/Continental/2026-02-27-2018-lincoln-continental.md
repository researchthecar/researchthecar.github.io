# 2018 Lincoln Continental Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2018 Lincoln Continental serves as a distinct American luxury sedan offering a spacious cabin and smooth ride quality. While it delivers executive-level comfort, potential buyers should review historical owner complaints concerning electrical anomalies, door latch operations, and intermittent body accessory glitches.

---

## Engine

### 3.7L V6

The standard naturally aspirated 3.7-liter V6 engine delivers dependable baseline power for daily driving. It provides smooth acceleration while avoiding the added mechanical complexity of forced induction.

### 2.7L EcoBoost Twin-Turbo V6

The 2.7-liter twin-turbocharged EcoBoost V6 offers robust low-end torque and responsive performance. Buyers should ensure regular oil maintenance to support long-term turbocharger health.

### 3.0L EcoBoost Twin-Turbo V6

The high-output 3.0-liter twin-turbocharged V6 pairs with all-wheel drive to deliver strong acceleration and refined power delivery. Proper cooling and lubrication maintenance remain key for this high-performance configuration.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

Paired primarily with the base V6 engine, this conventional 6-speed automatic provides generally dependable gear changes. Routine fluid changes help maintain smooth everyday operation.

### 6F55 6-Speed SelectShift Automatic Transmission

This heavier-duty 6-speed automatic handles the higher torque outputs of the EcoBoost engines. It offers predictable shifting behavior suited for relaxed highway cruising.

## Electrical

### Electrical System

Electrical complaints center on sporadic battery drainage and intermittent auxiliary switch responsiveness.

**Unexpected Battery Drain and Alarm Activation**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle battery may drain unexpectedly while parked, accompanied by sporadic flashing headlights, warning lights, and unintended horn activation.
- **Solution:** Diagnose parasitic electrical draws, test the battery and alternator, and update or replace malfunctioning body control modules.

## Exterior

### Door Latch

Owners have reported occasional difficulties with power door latches and physical locking mechanisms.

**Door Latch and Locking Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle doors may fail to latch securely when closed, sometimes requiring extra force or slamming during cold weather, or electronic door handles may become unresponsive.
- **Solution:** Inspect and replace faulty door latch assemblies or electronic release mechanisms as needed.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V077000: Latches/Locks/Linkages:Doors:Latch](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lincoln&model=Continental&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** The door latch pawl motors may not function properly, possibly resulting in a door latch not fully engaging. A door latch that is not fully engaged may result in a door opening while driving, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will replace the latch assemblies on all four doors, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V644000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lincoln&model=Continental&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** Lack of a clear rearview camera image can reduce the driver's visibility of what is behind the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the rearview camera, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2018 Lincoln Continental provides an appealing blend of luxury and V6 power options, but prospective buyers should inspect electronic accessories and door latches thoroughly during a test drive. Verifying that all safety campaigns and door latch repairs have been properly completed will help ensure a more dependable ownership experience.
