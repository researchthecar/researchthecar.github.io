**Introduction to the 1999 Lincoln Continental**

The 1999 Lincoln Continental is a full-size luxury sedan that was offered with two engine options: a 4.6-liter V8 and a 5.4-liter V8. Both engines were paired with a four-speed automatic transmission. The Continental was praised for its spacious interior, comfortable ride, and powerful engines. However, it was criticized for its high fuel consumption and outdated exterior design.

**Engine**

**4.6-liter V8**

* **Intake Manifold Gasket Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Coolant leak, loss of power, rough idle
    * Solution: Replace intake manifold gasket
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Timing Chain Tensioner Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Rattle or ticking noise from the engine, loss of power, reduced fuel economy
    * Solution: Replace timing chain tensioner and timing chain
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**5.4-liter V8**

* **Cam Phaser Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Rough idle, loss of power, reduced fuel economy
    * Solution: Replace cam phaser
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,200+
    * Recalls: 01V149000
* **Spark Plug Blowout**
    * Description/Symptoms: Misfiring, loss of power, reduced fuel economy
    * Solution: Replace spark plugs and inspect cylinder head
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

* **Four-Speed Automatic**

* **Transmission Slipping**
    * Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears, loss of power, delayed engagement
    * Solution: Repair or replace transmission
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Torque Converter Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, shuddering, overheating
    * Solution: Replace torque converter
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $1,500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Front Suspension Strut Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Noise over bumps, poor handling, loss of steering control
    * Solution: Replace strut assembly
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Rear Suspension Spring Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Sagging rear end, reduced load capacity
    * Solution: Replace rear springs
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain**
    * Description/Symptoms: Battery dies quickly, difficulty starting
    * Solution: Inspect electrical system for parasitic draw, replace battery if necessary
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Alternator Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Charging system warning light on, battery not charging
    * Solution: Replace alternator
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Headlight Assembly Fogging**
    * Description/Symptoms: Reduced headlight visibility
    * Solution: Replace headlight assembly
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Door Lock Actuator Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Difficulty locking or unlocking doors
    * Solution: Replace door lock actuator
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $250+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Dashboard Panel Cracking**
    * Description/Symptoms: Cracked dashboard panel
    * Solution: Replace dashboard panel
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Cup Holder Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Broken or loose cup holders
    * Solution: Replace cup holders
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $100+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 1999 Lincoln Continental has several potential issues, particularly with its engine and transmission. The cost to fix these issues can be significant, especially for major components like the timing chain and transmission. However, the vehicle's spacious interior, comfortable ride, and powerful engines still make it a popular choice for those looking for a classic luxury sedan.
