**2018 Lincoln Continental**

The 2018 Lincoln Continental is a full-size luxury sedan that debuted as an all-new model for the 2017 model year. It is offered with a choice of two engines: a turbocharged 3.0-liter V6 or a twin-turbocharged 3.7-liter V6. Both engines are paired with a 6-speed automatic transmission. The Continental also features a host of advanced safety and technology features, including adaptive cruise control, blind-spot monitoring, lane departure warning, and a 360-degree camera system.

Owners of the 2018 Lincoln Continental have reported a number of issues with their vehicles, including:

**Engine**

* **Turbocharged 3.0-liter V6**
    * **Turbocharger failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: The turbocharger may fail, causing a loss of power and increased fuel consumption.
        * Solution: Replace the turbocharger.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,500 - $4,000
     * **Timing chain failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: The timing chain may fail, causing the engine to lose power and stall.
        * Solution: Replace the timing chain and related components.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $4,000 - $6,000
 * **Twin-turbocharged 3.7-liter V6**
    * **Valve stem seal failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: The valve stem seals may fail, causing the engine to burn oil and smoke.
        * Solution: Replace the valve stem seals.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500
    * **Intake manifold failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: The intake manifold may fail, causing the engine to lose power and run rough.
        * Solution: Replace the intake manifold.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000

**Transmission**

* **6-speed automatic transmission**
    * **Shifting problems**
        * Description/Symptoms: The transmission may shift harshly or refuse to shift gears.
        * Solution: Replace the transmission control module or rebuild the transmission.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000
    * **Fluid leaks**
        * Description/Symptoms: The transmission may leak fluid, causing the transmission to overheat and fail.
        * Solution: Replace the transmission seals or lines.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500

**Suspension**

* **Front suspension struts**
    * **Leaking**
        * Description/Symptoms: The front suspension struts may leak fluid, causing the suspension to lose its damping ability.
        * Solution: Replace the front suspension struts.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,200

**Electrical**

* **Battery issues**
    * **Premature failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: The battery may fail prematurely, leaving the vehicle stranded.
        * Solution: Replace the battery.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400

**Exterior**

* **Door handles**
    * **Sticking or failing**
        * Description/Symptoms: The door handles may stick or fail to open the doors.
        * Solution: Replace the door handles.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500

**Interior**

* **Infotainment system**
    * **Glitches and malfunctions**
        * Description/Symptoms: The infotainment system may experience glitches or malfunctions, such as freezing or crashing.
        * Solution: Update the software or replace the infotainment system.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500

**Summary**

The 2018 Lincoln Continental has been plagued by a number of serious issues, including engine and transmission failures, suspension problems, and electrical issues. These issues can be expensive to repair and can lead to the vehicle being stranded or even unsafe to drive. While the Continental is a luxurious and well-equipped vehicle, its many problems make it a risky choice for consumers.
