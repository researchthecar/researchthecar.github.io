# 2017 Lincoln Continental Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The Lincoln Continental brings classic luxury and smooth cruising back to the full-size sedan market. While it offers a quiet cabin and robust features, prospective buyers should inspect certain mechanical and body systems closely. Several recurring issues have been reported by owners, particularly concerning door latches and braking components.

---

## Engine

### 3.7L V6

A naturally aspirated 3.7-liter V6 engine producing around 305 horsepower, serving as the standard power plant for front-wheel-drive or all-wheel-drive models. It provides adequate power for daily cruising with generally dependable mechanical performance.

### 2.7L Twin-Turbocharged V6

A twin-turbocharged 2.7-liter EcoBoost V6 generating approximately 335 horsepower, offering brisk acceleration and refined power delivery. Buyers should monitor for standard turbo maintenance and fluid leaks.

### 3.0L Twin-Turbocharged V6

A high-output 3.0-liter twin-turbo V6 pushing 400 horsepower, exclusive to all-wheel-drive configurations for maximum performance. Routine inspection of cooling and oil seals is recommended.

**Cooling System and Oil Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Burning oil odors or sudden coolant system overheating resulting in a mixed coolant and oil condition.
- **Solution:** Inspect engine gaskets, oil coolers, and cooling components immediately if overheating or fluid contamination occurs.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

A conventional 6-speed automatic transmission paired with select engines, offering predictable and smooth shifts. Regular fluid changes help ensure long-term durability.

## Brakes

### Hydraulic Brake System

The hydraulic braking setup provides stopping power, but owners have reported critical intermittent failures requiring immediate system inspection.

**Brake Pedal Travel and Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Brake pedal drops to the floorboard with little to no resistance, causing a sudden loss of hydraulic braking power.
- **Solution:** Inspect master cylinders, brake lines, and hydraulic fluid pressure immediately upon any sign of soft pedal travel.

## Exterior

### Door Latches

Interior door handles and latches are frequent points of owner complaints, sometimes requiring costly assembly replacements.

**Door Latch and Locking Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Doors fail to open from either the inside or outside, or swing open while driving due to malfunctioning latches.
- **Solution:** Inspect door latch mechanisms and actuators; replacement of faulty latch assemblies may be required.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V077000: Latches/Locks/Linkages:Doors:Latch](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lincoln&model=Continental&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** The door latch pawl motors may not function properly, possibly resulting in a door latch not fully engaging. A door latch that is not fully engaged may result in a door opening while driving, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will replace the latch assemblies on all four doors, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V123000: Air Bags:Frontal:Driver Side:Inflator Module](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lincoln&model=Continental&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** In the event of a crash, the driver's frontal air bag may not fully inflate or the air bag cushion may detach from the air bag module. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 208, "Occupant Crash Protection." During deployment, if the air bag does not fully inflate or the cushion.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will replace the driver frontal air bag module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V731000: Exterior Lighting:Headlights](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lincoln&model=Continental&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** Equipment." Reduced turn signal visibility to other drivers and pedestrians can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the headlights, replacing them as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V644000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lincoln&model=Continental&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** Lack of a clear rearview camera image can reduce the driver's visibility of what is behind the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the rearview camera, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The Lincoln Continental is a comfortable luxury cruiser, but potential buyers must perform a thorough pre-purchase inspection. Pay close attention to the operation of all doors and latches, test the brake system thoroughly for any pedal softness, and check the engine bay for signs of fluid leaks or overheating history.
