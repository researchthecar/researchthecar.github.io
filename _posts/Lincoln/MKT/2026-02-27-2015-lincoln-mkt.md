# 2015 Lincoln MKT

### Overview
The 2015 Lincoln MKT is a full-size luxury crossover known for its spacious interior and comfortable ride. As with any vehicle, it has specific areas that have seen more attention from owners regarding reliability. This analysis focuses on documented technical issues beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential concerns for owners and prospective buyers. We will examine the powertrain, suspension, electrical systems, and other key areas to offer a comprehensive reliability picture for this model year.

---


## Engine

### 3.7L V6
**Cooling System Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Coolant loss, overheating, visible puddles under the vehicle.
- **Solution:** Replacement of leaking hoses, water pump, or radiator.
- **Cost:** $300 - $700


### 3.5L EcoBoost V6
**Turbocharger Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of power, whistling noises from the engine bay, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of turbocharger assembly.
- **Cost:** $1,200 - $2,500

**Intake Manifold Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Rough idle, vacuum leaks, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the intake manifold.
- **Cost:** $400 - $800


## Transmission

### 6-Speed SelectShift Automatic
**Hesitation or Shuddering**
- **Symptoms:** Jerky acceleration, delayed engagement, or shuddering during gear changes.
- **Solution:** Transmission fluid flush and filter replacement, or internal transmission repair.
- **Cost:** $300 - $1,500

## Electrical

### Infotainment System
**SYNC System Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Screen freezing, unresponsiveness, Bluetooth connectivity issues.
- **Solution:** Software updates, system reset, or module replacement.
- **Cost:** $100 - $600


### Battery Drain
**Intermittent Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Dead battery after sitting, electrical components malfunctioning.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis of parasitic draw, replacement of faulty module or component.
- **Cost:** $200 - $500

## Interior

### Climate Control System
**Blend Door Actuator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Inability to control temperature or airflow direction.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the blend door actuator.
- **Cost:** $200 - $500


### Seat Actuators
**Power Seat Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Power seats not moving or moving erratically.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of seat motor or control module.
- **Cost:** $300 - $700

### Summary Verdict
The 2015 Lincoln MKT presents a generally solid reliability record, with most reported issues falling into predictable categories for a luxury vehicle of its size and age. The EcoBoost engine option, while offering more power, has a slightly higher propensity for issues like turbocharger problems and intake manifold leaks compared to the standard V6. Transmission concerns, primarily related to hesitation or shuddering, are not uncommon and often addressable with maintenance or minor repairs. Electrical gremlins, particularly with the SYNC infotainment system and occasional battery drain, are also noted. The suspension, exterior, and interior components generally hold up well, with fewer widespread complaints. Overall, proactive maintenance and addressing early signs of the aforementioned issues can contribute to a positive ownership experience.
