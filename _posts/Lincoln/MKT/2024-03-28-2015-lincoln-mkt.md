**Introduction:**

The 2015 Lincoln MKT is a luxury midsize SUV available with three engine options: a 3.7L V6 (303 hp, 278 lb-ft), a 3.5L V6 EcoBoost (365 hp, 403 lb-ft), and a 2.7L V6 EcoBoost (340 hp, 370 lb-ft). Transmission options include a 6-speed automatic for the 3.7L V6 and a 6-speed SelectShift automatic for the 3.5L and 2.7L V6 EcoBoost.

The MKT is praised for its spacious interior, comfortable ride, and powerful engines. However, it has also been criticized for its subpar fuel economy and dated infotainment system.

**Engine**

**3.7L V6**

* **Timing Chain Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: A stretched or broken timing chain can cause the engine to lose timing, resulting in reduced engine power, rough idling, and potential engine damage.
    * Solution: Replace the timing chain, guides, and tensioners.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**3.5L V6 EcoBoost**

* **Turbocharger Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: A faulty turbocharger can lead to reduced boost pressure, loss of power, and increased exhaust smoke.
    * Solution: Replace the turbocharger.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,000+
    * Recalls: NHTSA Campaign Number 21V440000

* **Fuel Injector Malfunction:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Clogged or malfunctioning fuel injectors can cause rough idling, hesitation during acceleration, and poor fuel economy.
    * Solution: Clean or replace the fuel injectors.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
    * Recalls: None Found

**2.7L V6 EcoBoost**

* **No Issues Found**

**Transmission**

**6-Speed Automatic (3.7L V6)**

* **Transmission Slippage:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The transmission may slip gears or fail to engage, resulting in reduced acceleration or a loss of power.
    * Solution: Repair or rebuild the transmission.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**6-Speed SelectShift Automatic (3.5L and 2.7L V6 EcoBoost)**

* **Valve Body Issues:**
    * Description/Symptoms: A faulty valve body can cause delayed shifting, hard shifts, or a loss of gears.
    * Solution: Replace the valve body.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $1,500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Strut Mount Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Worn strut mounts can cause a rattling or clunking noise, as well as reduced stability and handling.
    * Solution: Replace the strut mounts.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain:**
    * Description/Symptoms: An excessive current drain can lead to a dead battery, dimming lights, or electrical malfunctions.
    * Solution: Diagnose and repair the source of the drain, such as a faulty alternator or parasitic draw.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Headlight Malfunction:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Headlights may fail to illuminate or flicker intermittently.
    * Solution: Replace the headlight assembly or electrical components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: NHTSA Campaign Number 17V472000

**Exterior**

* **Sunroof Leaks:**
    * Description/Symptoms: A faulty sunroof seal can allow water to leak into the cabin, causing damage to the interior.
    * Solution: Replace the sunroof seal or repair the sunroof drain system.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Panoramic Sunroof Noise:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The panoramic sunroof may rattle or create excessive wind noise.
    * Solution: Adjust or replace the sunroof glass or frame.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Navigation System Malfunction:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The navigation system may fail to display maps or provide accurate directions.
    * Solution: Update the software or replace the navigation unit.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

The 2015 Lincoln MKT has several potential issues that can vary in severity and cost to fix. Some of the most common problems include timing chain failure, turbocharger failure, transmission slippage, strut mount failure, and electrical issues. While many of these issues can be addressed through repairs or replacements, some may require more significant maintenance or overhauls, which can lead to higher costs. Overall, it's important for owners to be aware of these potential issues and budget accordingly for repairs or maintenance as needed.
