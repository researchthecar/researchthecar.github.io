# 2017 Lincoln MKX

### Overview
The 2017 Lincoln MKX, a mid-size luxury SUV, generally presents a solid reliability record, reflecting Lincoln's focus on comfort and refinement. While no vehicle is entirely free from potential issues, the MKX has demonstrated good performance in key areas. Owners often praise its quiet cabin, comfortable ride, and upscale interior. However, like many vehicles in its class, some specific components have seen occasional reports of concern, particularly related to the powertrain and certain electronic features. This analysis focuses on documented technical issues beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential areas that may require attention over the vehicle's lifespan.

---


## Engine

### 2.7L EcoBoost V6
**Coolant Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Visible coolant puddles under the vehicle, low coolant level warning light, engine overheating.
- **Solution:** Replacement of leaking hoses, seals, or potentially the water pump.
- **Cost:** $300 - $700

**Turbocharger Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of power, whistling or hissing sounds from the engine bay, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of the turbocharger assembly.
- **Cost:** $1200 - $2500

### Summary Verdict
The 2017 Lincoln MKX generally offers a reliable ownership experience, particularly with the 3.7L V6 engine. The 2.7L EcoBoost V6, while offering more power, has seen some reports of coolant leaks and turbocharger issues, which can be costly to repair. Transmission issues, such as hesitation or shuddering, are not uncommon and may require attention. Electrical gremlins, especially with the infotainment system and potential battery drain, are also noted. Owners should be aware of potential issues with adaptive suspension components if equipped and LED headlights. Overall, the MKX is a comfortable and capable luxury SUV, but potential buyers should consider these specific technical points when evaluating a pre-owned model.
