# 2017 Lincoln MKX Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2017 Lincoln MKX offers a comfortable ride, upscale cabin features, and practical everyday utility for midsize luxury SUV shoppers. While it provides a pleasant driving experience, used-car buyers should review potential mechanical and electrical quirks reported by owners, including powertrain glitches, accessory concerns, and minor body hardware issues.

---

## Engine

### 3.7L V6 Engine

The standard 3.7-liter V6 engine delivers around 303 horsepower and 278 lb-ft of torque, providing smooth everyday acceleration. It is generally robust, though owners have occasionally noted electrical sensor or stalling anomalies.

### 2.7L EcoBoost Twin-Turbo V6 Engine

The optional 2.7-liter twin-turbocharged EcoBoost V6 generates approximately 335 horsepower and 380 lb-ft of torque for stronger passing power. Buyers should inspect turbo and cooling systems during a pre-purchase examination.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed SelectShift Automatic Transmission

A 6-speed automatic transmission pairs with both engine choices in front-wheel or all-wheel-drive configurations. It delivers composed gear changes, though proper fluid maintenance is key to long-term longevity.

## Suspension

### Wheel

Suspension and wheel hardware generally hold up well, but owners frequently report issues with swelling wheel hardware.

**Swollen Lug Nuts**
- **Symptoms:** Factory lug nuts swell over time, making it extremely difficult or impossible to fit a standard socket on them when trying to change a flat tire.
- **Solution:** Replace the factory two-piece lug nuts with solid aftermarket alternatives.
- **Cost:** $179

## Safety

### Seat Belt

Safety equipment is generally dependable, though occasional seatbelt mechanism failures have been documented by owners.

**Driver Seat Belt Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** The driver-side seatbelt fails to extend or latch properly, preventing proper restraint use without triggering a dashboard warning light.
- **Solution:** Inspect and replace the faulty seatbelt assembly and associated mechanical components.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V469000: Service Brakes](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lincoln&model=MKX&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** A ruptured front brake hose will cause a brake fluid leak, lengthening the distance needed to stop the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will replace the front brake hoses with the new hoses that have a revised braid material, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V809000: Electrical System:12V/24V/48V Battery:Cables](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lincoln&model=MKX&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** The battery positive cable harness may contact the transmission shifter cable bracket resulting in a short circuit. An electrical short increases the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will remove the battery harness clips and elbow guide, and install a wire channel shield and protective sleeve to the battery cable harness, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V414000: Seats:Front Assembly:Power Adjust](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lincoln&model=MKX&modelYear=2017)**

## Summary Verdict

Overall, the 2017 Lincoln MKX is a capable luxury crossover that rewards buyers who prioritize comfort and interior appointments. When shopping for a used model, verify that standard maintenance has been performed, inspect the wheels for swollen lug nuts, and ensure all safety restraints and electronics operate correctly.
