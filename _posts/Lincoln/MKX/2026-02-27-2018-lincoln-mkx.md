# 2018 Lincoln MKX Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2018 Lincoln MKX is a midsize luxury crossover offering a comfortable ride, spacious cabin, and quiet interior. While it delivers strong performance and upscale features, potential buyers should review owner-reported issues involving emissions components and water leaks when evaluating a pre-owned model.

---

## Engine

### 3.7L V6 Engine

The 3.7-liter naturally aspirated V6 engine produces around 303 horsepower and 278 lb-ft of torque, offering smooth and reliable power delivery for daily driving. It is generally robust, though occasional emissions equipment concerns have been reported by owners.

**Catalytic Converter Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Illuminated check engine lights, persistent diagnostic trouble codes, and potential engine stalling or rough running.
- **Solution:** Inspect the emissions system and replace faulty catalytic converters if premature failure occurs.

### 2.7L EcoBoost V6 Engine

The 2.7-liter twin-turbocharged EcoBoost V6 engine generates approximately 335 horsepower and 380 lb-ft of torque, providing brisk acceleration and strong passing power. It pairs strong performance with competitive everyday drivability.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed SelectShift Automatic Transmission

The 6-speed automatic transmission delivers smooth shifts and works well with both front-wheel and all-wheel-drive configurations, contributing to a refined luxury driving experience.

## Exterior

### Exterior Lighting and Body

Electrical and exterior body accessories generally function as intended, though occasional issues with auxiliary lighting housings have been documented.

**High-Mount Brake Light Damage**
- **Symptoms:** The third brake light lens or housing may crack, detach, or pull away from the vehicle during automated car washes or normal driving.
- **Solution:** Replace the rear high-mount brake light assembly with an updated part.

### Interior Climate and Body Sealing

Cabin comfort features are typically dependable, but owners should thoroughly check for interior moisture or water intrusion during a pre-purchase inspection.

**Water Leaks and Interior Moisture**
- **Symptoms:** Standing water pooling in floorboards, damp carpeting, trunk or spare tire areas filling with water, and associated musty odors.
- **Solution:** Inspect sunroof drains, rear hatch seals, and body panels to locate water intrusion points and reseal or replace affected components.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V469000: Service Brakes](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lincoln&model=MKX&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** A ruptured front brake hose will cause a brake fluid leak, lengthening the distance needed to stop the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will replace the front brake hoses with the new hoses that have a revised braid material, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V612000: Latches/Locks/Linkages:Doors:Latch](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lincoln&model=MKX&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** Vehicles with shorter door striker bolts fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 206, "Door Locks and Door Retention Components" potentially resulting in the doors opening in the event of a crash. If the doors open in the event of a crash, there is an increased risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will replace the door striker bolts, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V414000: Seats:Front Assembly:Power Adjust](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lincoln&model=MKX&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** The seat cushion frame edge may contact the wire harness when the seat occupant uses the tilt function of the seat, potentially damaging one or more wires within the harness, which can result in either inadvertent deployment of the air bag or nondeployment of the air bag in the event of a crash. An air bag that deploys inadvertently increases the risk of a.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will install protective flocking tape onto the exposed edge of the 16-way seat cushion frames, and inspect the wire harness for damage, and repair or replace it as needed, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V548000: Electrical System:Alternator/Generator/Regulator](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lincoln&model=MKX&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** The power supply cables at the starter and the alternator may not have been properly secured, possibly resulting in an electrical arc. An electrical arc can increase the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the alternator and starter motor power supply cables to verify that they are properly secured, correcting them as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V544000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Foundation Components:Hoses, Lines/Piping, And Fittings](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lincoln&model=MKX&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** A brake fluid leak can extend the distance required to stop the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the brake jounce hoses and adjust brake jounce hoses with no damage present, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2018 Lincoln MKX provides a comfortable and well-appointed luxury crossover experience, but prospective buyers should inspect the vehicle carefully for signs of past water leaks or emissions component wear. A comprehensive pre-purchase inspection will help ensure the chosen vehicle is in top working order.
