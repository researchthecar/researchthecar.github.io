# 2016 Lincoln MKX Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2016 Lincoln MKX offers a comfortable luxury ride, spacious cabin, and quiet interior that appeals to used midsize SUV buyers. However, prospective owners should review owner-reported reliability trends carefully, particularly regarding engine mechanical concerns, drivetrain components, and electrical glitches. While it delivers strong performance when properly maintained, researching service histories for past repairs will help ensure a dependable ownership experience.

---

## Engine

### 3.7L V6 Engine

The standard 3.7-liter V6 engine produces around 303 horsepower and 278 lb-ft of torque, providing smooth acceleration for everyday driving. Buyers should monitor maintenance records closely, as some owners have reported intermittent stalling and water pump concerns.

**Engine Stalling and Power Loss**
- **Symptoms:** The engine may suddenly stall or experience a loss of motive power while driving in traffic or at highway speeds.
- **Solution:** Diagnose the underlying fuel or electrical cause, which may involve addressing water pump failure or internal component issues.

### 2.7L EcoBoost Twin-Turbo V6 Engine

The optional 2.7-liter twin-turbocharged EcoBoost V6 delivers 335 horsepower and 380 lb-ft of torque, offering stronger low-end pull and spirited performance. It provides an engaging driving dynamic, though routine turbocharger and cooling system checks are recommended.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed SelectShift Automatic Transmission

The 6-speed automatic transmission delivers power smoothly to either the front wheels or the available all-wheel-drive system. Routine fluid changes help maintain shifting quality and overall longevity.

## Suspension

### Wheels

The suspension and wheel assemblies generally offer a composed ride quality, though minor hardware quirks like swollen lug nuts frequently frustrate owners during routine maintenance.

**Swollen Lug Nuts**
- **Symptoms:** Factory lug nuts can swell over time, preventing standard tire irons or sockets from fitting properly during a flat tire change.
- **Solution:** Replace the factory two-piece lug nuts with solid aftermarket alternatives.
- **Cost:** $225

## Electrical

### Electrical System

The vehicle relies heavily on advanced electronics and digital displays, which can occasionally experience intermittent glitches, sensor faults, or battery drain issues.

**Battery Drain and Starting Failures**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may fail to start intermittently or experience total losses of electrical power due to battery drain or component faults.
- **Solution:** Test the battery and charging system, and inspect for parasitic electrical draws or malfunctioning cabin modules.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V632000: Steering:Electric Power Assist System](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lincoln&model=MKX&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** If the bolts corrode, the steering gear motor may detach from the gear housing resulting in a loss of power steering assist, requiring a greater steering effort, especially at lower speeds, and increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will replace the steering gear motor bolts and apply a wax sealer. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V469000: Service Brakes](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lincoln&model=MKX&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** A ruptured front brake hose will cause a brake fluid leak, lengthening the distance needed to stop the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will replace the front brake hoses with the new hoses that have a revised braid material, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V809000: Electrical System:12V/24V/48V Battery:Cables](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lincoln&model=MKX&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** The battery positive cable harness may contact the transmission shifter cable bracket resulting in a short circuit. An electrical short increases the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will remove the battery harness clips and elbow guide, and install a wire channel shield and protective sleeve to the battery cable harness, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V414000: Seats:Front Assembly:Power Adjust](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lincoln&model=MKX&modelYear=2016)**

## Summary Verdict

The 2016 Lincoln MKX remains a comfortable and feature-rich luxury SUV option on the used market, but buyers should approach with informed caution. Verifying that major recalls and mechanical services—such as cooling system checks and electrical inspections—have been performed will significantly reduce the risk of unexpected repair bills.
