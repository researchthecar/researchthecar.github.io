# 2023 Lincoln Navigator Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2023 Lincoln Navigator is a full-size luxury SUV that blends high-end comfort with strong utility. While owner reports suggest it delivers a refined driving experience, used-car buyers should note that early-production electronic and accessory glitches have surfaced in owner feedback. As with any modern luxury vehicle heavily reliant on digital architecture, checking for software updates and performing a thorough test of all cabin technology is recommended.

---

## Engine

### 3.5L Twin-Turbocharged V6

The 2023 Lincoln Navigator comes standard with a potent 3.5-liter twin-turbocharged V6 engine producing approximately 440 horsepower and 510 lb-ft of torque. This powerplant delivers brisk acceleration and strong towing capability while maintaining everyday drivability.

## Transmission

### 10-Speed SelectShift Automatic

Power is routed through a 10-speed SelectShift automatic transmission paired with either rear-wheel drive or four-wheel drive. The transmission is designed to optimize both highway efficiency and low-end pulling power.

## Electrical

### Infotainment and Backup Camera

Electrical and screen-related complaints center on intermittent freezing and startup delays.

**Screen Delay and Freezing**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have reported significant startup delays with the infotainment system and backup camera, occasionally resulting in frozen screens that render safety systems temporarily unusable.
- **Solution:** Software updates performed by a dealership can often resolve freezing or slow-loading infotainment displays.

### Power Running Boards

The exterior features convenience items like power running boards, which occasionally exhibit erratic behavior.

**Erratic Operation and Defaulting to Off**
- **Symptoms:** Power running boards may deploy and retract independently without input, or they may automatically default to the off position despite being set to auto.
- **Solution:** Inspect the running board motors, wiring harnesses, and software settings; dealerships may need to reset or replace faulty sensors.

## Exterior

### Exterior Lighting

Exterior lighting systems generally operate well, though rare positioning quirks during startup have been noted.

**Headlight Motion Check Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Upon startup, the adaptive headlights perform a self-check motion but may occasionally remain stuck in the downward position.
- **Solution:** A dealership inspection can determine if a headlight module or software recalibration is required to correct the motion range.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V128000: Power Train:Axle Assembly](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lincoln&model=Navigator&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** The front axle pinion may not have been heat-treated, which can cause it to fracture. A fractured pinion can result in a loss of 4-wheel drive and may cause the front wheels to lock up, increasng the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the front axle pinion gear set. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V420000: Equipment:Other:Owners/Service/Other Manual](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lincoln&model=Navigator&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 202, "Head Restraints." Without instructions, the head restraints may be improperly adjusted, increasing the risk of injury in the event of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 24V852000: Equipment:Other:Labels](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lincoln&model=Navigator&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirement Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 208, "Occupant Crash Protection." Without a label that shows the proper restraint usage guidelines, the occupants may have an increased risk of injury in the event of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 25V314000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Foundation Components:Hoses, Lines/Piping, And Fittings](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lincoln&model=Navigator&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** A loss of brake function increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the front brake line and replace the brake line or air cleaner outlet pipe, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V315000: Back Over Prevention:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lincoln&model=Navigator&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** A software error may cause the rearview camera image to delay, freeze, or not display when the vehicle is in reverse. A frozen or missing rearview camera image can reduce the driver's view behind the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 26V104000: Electrical System: Integrated Trailer Brake Control](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lincoln&model=Navigator&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 108, " Lamps, Reflective Devices, and Associated Equipment." Trailer lights or brakes that do not function can reduce the driver’s ability to control the trailer and decrease the trailers visibility to other road users, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 26V165000: Back Over Prevention:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lincoln&model=Navigator&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** Loss of the rearview camera image and certain driver assistance features, can reduce the driver’s ability to detect hazards, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 26V204000: Visibility:Windshield Wiper/Washer:Linkages](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lincoln&model=Navigator&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** Windshield wiper failure can reduce visibility, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the wiper arms, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2023 Lincoln Navigator offers a compelling mix of luxury, spaciousness, and robust performance from its twin-turbo V6 engine. While owner complaints indicate potential frustrations with electronic accessories, infotainment startup delays, and power running boards, these issues do not point to systemic mechanical failure. Prospective buyers should carefully verify that all electronic features, cameras, and convenience tech operate correctly before finalizing their purchase.
