**Introduction**

The 2014 Lincoln Navigator is a full-size luxury SUV that offers a powerful 3.5L EcoBoost V6 engine with 370 horsepower and 430 lb-ft of torque. It comes standard with a 6-speed automatic transmission and is available in both rear-wheel drive and four-wheel drive configurations. Some of the liked features include its elegant styling, comfortable interior, and user-friendly infotainment system. However, it is criticized for its fuel economy, stiff suspension, and lack of some advanced driver-assist features.

**Engine**

**3.5L EcoBoost V6**

* **Turbocharger Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Excessive oil consumption, decreased engine power, check engine light
    * Solution: Replace turbocharger
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
    * Recalls: NHTSA Campaign Number: 19V018
* **Timing Chain Tensioner Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Rattling or knocking noise from engine, check engine light
    * Solution: Replace timing chain tensioner
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**6-Speed Automatic**

* **Shifting Issues:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Rough or delayed shifting, slipping gears
    * Solution: Replace transmission fluid, reprogram transmission control module, or replace transmission
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $3,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Air Suspension Compressor Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Vehicle sagging, rough ride, warning light on dashboard
    * Solution: Replace air suspension compressor
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Adaptive Damping System Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Excessive bouncing or body roll, warning light on dashboard
    * Solution: Replace adaptive damping system components
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Vehicle struggles to start, warning lights on dashboard
    * Solution: Test battery and alternator, replace if necessary
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Tailgate Wiring Harness Issue:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Malfunctioning tailgate latch, rearview camera not working
    * Solution: Replace tailgate wiring harness
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
    * Recalls: NHTSA Campaign Number: 17V415

**Exterior**

* **Headlight Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Dim or flickering headlights
    * Solution: Replace headlight bulbs or ballast
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Door Handle Corrosion:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Door handle becomes stuck or difficult to operate
    * Solution: Replace door handle
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Climate Control Malfunction:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Blower motor not working, AC not cooling or heating
    * Solution: Replace climate control module or blower motor
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Center Console Latch Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Center console lid not closing or opening properly
    * Solution: Replace center console latch
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

Overall, the 2014 Lincoln Navigator has several potential issues, including turbocharger failures, transmission problems, and suspension malfunctions. While some of these issues can be minor inconveniences, others can be expensive to fix. The severity and cost of the repairs will depend on the specific problem and the affected parts.
