The 2022 Lincoln Navigator is a full-size luxury SUV known for its spacious interior, comfortable ride, and abundant features. It generally receives positive reviews for its opulent cabin and smooth power delivery. However, some owners have reported issues with its complex technology and overall reliability. The 2022 Navigator is equipped with a 3.5L EcoBoost V6 engine that produces 450 horsepower and 510 lb-ft of torque, paired with a 10-speed automatic transmission. Owners appreciate the Navigator's luxurious appointments and smooth ride but sometimes complain about the infotainment system's complexity and occasional electronic glitches.

**Engine**

**3.5L EcoBoost V6**

*   **Turbocharger Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, excessive turbo noise (whining or screeching), check engine light illumination, potential for oil leaks. Some owners have reported premature turbocharger failure.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the faulty turbocharger(s).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Wastegate Actuator Problems**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Reduced engine performance, hesitation during acceleration, turbo lag, check engine light. The wastegate actuator may malfunction, leading to improper turbo boost control.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the wastegate actuator or the entire turbocharger assembly.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**10-Speed Automatic**

*   **Harsh Shifting/Slipping**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rough or jerky gear changes, noticeable slipping between gears, delayed engagement, particularly between lower gears. Some report these issues becoming more pronounced over time.
    *   Solution: Software reflash/reprogramming of the transmission control module (TCM). In more severe cases, valve body replacement or complete transmission overhaul/replacement may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+ (reflash), $800 - $2,500+ (valve body), $4,000 - $8,000+ (transmission replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Delayed Engagement**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Noticeable delay when shifting from Park or Neutral into Drive or Reverse.
    *   Solution: In some cases, a software update can resolve this. In others, it might indicate an issue with the transmission's internal components requiring repair or replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+ (reflash), $1,000 - $5,000+ (internal repairs)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Air Suspension Malfunctions**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The vehicle may sag on one or more corners, ride height may be uneven, "check suspension" warnings appear on the dashboard, compressor may run constantly or fail to engage. This is a common issue on models equipped with air suspension.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis and replacement of faulty air struts/shocks, air compressor, ride height sensors, or air lines.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $2,000+ per strut/shock, $500 - $1,500+ (compressor)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Freezing/Crashing**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The SYNC 4 infotainment system may freeze, become unresponsive, crash, or exhibit various glitches. This can affect navigation, audio, climate control, and other vehicle functions.
    *   Solution: Software updates, system resets, or in some cases, replacement of the infotainment system module.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 (software update, if available), $500 - $2,000+ (module replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Camera System Problems**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Issues with the surround-view camera system, including distorted images, black screens, camera failures, or error messages.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis and replacement of faulty camera(s) or the camera control module.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,000+ per camera, $500 - $1,500+ (module replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Battery Drain**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The battery drains quickly, especially when the vehicle is not in use. This can lead to difficulty starting the vehicle or a completely dead battery.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis and repair of parasitic draws. Often caused by faulty modules not shutting down completely.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Power Running Board Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The power running boards may fail to deploy or retract properly, make excessive noise, or get stuck in one position.
    *   Solution: Lubrication of joints, repair of damaged linkages, or replacement of the running board motor or the entire running board assembly.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+ (lubrication/minor repairs), $500 - $2,000+ (motor/assembly replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Seat Motor Malfunctions**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Power seats may not move in all directions or may become stuck. Individual seat motors can fail.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the faulty seat motor(s) or the entire seat track assembly.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+ per motor, $500 - $1,500+ (track assembly)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2022 Lincoln Navigator can present owners with a mix of issues. Engine and transmission issues, particularly with the turbochargers and 10-speed transmission, can lead to significant repair costs. Electrical problems with the infotainment system and camera systems are also frequently reported and can detract from the overall ownership experience. Air suspension malfunctions can be costly. While not every Navigator will experience all these problems, potential buyers should be aware of these common complaints and factor them into their purchasing decision. The severity of these issues can range from minor annoyances to expensive repairs, making careful inspection and a thorough pre-purchase inspection essential.
