# 2016 Lincoln Navigator Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used full-size luxury SUV, the model offers a spacious cabin and strong towing capabilities, but prospective buyers should inspect critical mechanical and electrical systems carefully. Reviewing owner feedback and technical logs highlights specific areas—such as brake reliability, powertrain electronic glitches, and accessory operation—that warrant close attention during a pre-purchase inspection.

---

## Engine

### 3.5L Twin-Turbocharged V6

This 3.5-liter EcoBoost twin-turbocharged V6 produces approximately 380 horsepower and 460 lb-ft of torque, providing robust acceleration and towing power. While generally capable, owners have occasionally experienced drivability hiccups and power loss related to electronic controls and fuel management.

**Loss of Motive Power**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have reported momentary or complete losses of engine power while driving at highway speeds, sometimes accompanied by sputtering or illuminated check engine lights.
- **Solution:** Diagnose throttle body operation, fuel delivery systems, or powertrain control modules, and replace faulty components as necessary.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 6-speed automatic transmission channels power smoothly to either the rear wheels or a four-wheel-drive system in normal operation. However, intermittent electrical glitches can trigger harsh gear drops that require professional diagnostic evaluation.

**Abrupt Downshifting and Speedometer Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The transmission may unexpectedly drop into a lower gear at highway speeds while dashboard gauges drop to zero, potentially causing momentary wheel lock-up.
- **Solution:** Inspect vehicle speed sensors, wiring harnesses, and transmission control modules for faults or software updates.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

The braking hardware is designed to haul a heavy luxury SUV to a stop, but hydraulic or master cylinder leaks can compromise stopping confidence.

**Brake Pedal Softness and Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers experience a sudden loss of firm brake pedal pressure, making the vehicle increasingly difficult to stop safely.
- **Solution:** Thoroughly inspect the master cylinder, brake lines for fluid leaks, and hydraulic booster components.

## Exterior

### Side Mirrors and Exterior Lighting

Body hardware and exterior accessories generally hold up well, though isolated electrical shorts in mirror heaters or switches require prompt inspection to prevent damage.

**Exterior Mirror Smoke and Burning Odor**
- **Symptoms:** Smoke can emanate from the exterior side view mirrors while the vehicle is parked and turned off, accompanied by a burning plastic smell.
- **Solution:** Inspect mirror wiring and internal heating elements, and replace damaged mirror assemblies.

## Interior

### Power Running Boards and Interior Electronics

Convenience features like motorized running boards and climate control electronics add a touch of luxury, though mechanical wear and electrical moisture exposure can cause occasional operational failures.

**Running Boards Stuck in Down Position**
- **Symptoms:** Power running boards fail to retract, remaining locked in the down position and collecting ice and snow during winter weather.
- **Solution:** Clean and lubricate the hinge mechanisms or replace faulty running board motors and control modules.

**[Recall 22V648000: Visibility:Defroster/Defogger/Hvac System:Fan/Motor](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lincoln&model=Navigator&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** Front blower motor failure can increase the risk of a vehicle interior fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the front blower motor assembly, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V091000: Visibility:Rearview Mirrors/Devices:Exterior](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lincoln&model=Navigator&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** The LED logo lights on the exterior mirrors may short circuit. An electrical short circuit in the exterior mirror increases the risk of fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will disconnect the LED light wires, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V572000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lincoln&model=Navigator&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** A rearview camera that displays an inverted, distorted, or blank image can reduce the driver’s view behind the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the rearview camera as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

Overall, this vehicle delivers impressive passenger space, towing capability, and comfort, but used-car buyers should perform a diligent inspection. Pay special attention to brake system integrity, powertrain electronic stability, and accessory functions such as power running boards during a test drive to ensure a reliable ownership experience.
