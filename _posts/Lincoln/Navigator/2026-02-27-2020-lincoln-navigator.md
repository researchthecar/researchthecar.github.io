# 2020 Lincoln Navigator Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used luxury full-size SUV, the Lincoln Navigator delivers impressive power, commanding road presence, and an upscale cabin. However, prospective buyers should review owner-reported mechanical concerns and service histories closely before purchasing. While many owners enjoy trouble-free luxury cruising, certain powertrain and electrical glitches have been documented in owner complaints.

---

## Engine

### 3.5L Twin-Turbocharged V6

The 3.5-liter twin-turbocharged V6 engine produces approximately 450 horsepower and 510 lb-ft of torque, providing robust acceleration for this large SUV. While generally capable for towing and highway driving, owners have occasionally reported unexpected mechanical breakdowns.

**Sudden Engine Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle experiences sudden power loss, engine shuddering, and illumination of warning lights without prior warning.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified mechanic inspect the engine condition, timing components, and diagnostic codes. Depending on the extent of internal damage, major engine repairs or replacement may be necessary.

## Transmission

### 10-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 10-speed automatic transmission pairs with the V6 engine to optimize fuel delivery and highway cruising. Buyers should thoroughly test-drive the vehicle to check for smooth gear transitions and prompt engagement.

**Unintended Neutral Shift and Harsh Engagement**
- **Symptoms:** Transmission unexpectedly shifts into neutral while driving, hesitates during acceleration, exhibits harsh shifting, or loses power on the highway.
- **Solution:** Inspect transmission fluid condition and check for updated valve body components or internal drum wear. Service bulletin repairs, such as replacing redesigned internal components like the CDF drum, may be required.

## Electrical

### Battery and Charging System

Manages power distribution across numerous vehicle accessories, though some owners experience intermittent electrical drains.

**Parasitic Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle battery drains completely overnight or after short periods of non-use, preventing the vehicle from starting.
- **Solution:** Perform a parasitic draw test to isolate faulty modules or aftermarket accessories pulling current while the vehicle is off.

## Exterior

### Exterior Lighting

Exterior lighting assemblies feature complex LED arrays that may require complete housing replacement if moisture seals fail.

**Headlight and Taillight Moisture Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Moisture accumulation inside headlight or taillight housings, eventually leading to lighting failure.
- **Solution:** Inspect housing seals for cracks or gaps and replace affected headlight or taillight assemblies as needed.

## Safety

### Driver Assistance and Collision Warning

Advanced safety features assist with collision mitigation, though sensor calibration is critical for proper operation.

**Erratic Collision Avoidance Alerts**
- **Symptoms:** Forward collision warning system triggers false alarms at safe distances or fails to activate when approaching obstacles quickly.
- **Solution:** Clean sensor lenses and camera areas, and have a dealer recalibrate the collision warning radar and camera systems.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V366000: Seats:Mid/Rear Assembly](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lincoln&model=Navigator&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** The attachment bracket for the driver's side second-row seat headrest may not have been sufficiently welded to the seat frame. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 202, "Head Restraints." An improperly welded headrest bracket may have reduced strength and may not adequately restrain an occupant in a crash.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the seat structure and replace it as needed, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V262000: Electrical System: Instrument Cluster/Panel](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lincoln&model=Navigator&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** If the driver is unaware that the Pre-Collision Assist feature is not enabled, they may rely on a system that is not functional, increasing the risk of a crash or injury.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will reprogram software for the Body Control Module, Antilock Braking System, Instrument Panel Cluster and Headlamp Control Modules, and reset the Tire Pressure Monitor System, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V250000: Visibility:Windshield Wiper/Washer:Linkages](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lincoln&model=Navigator&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** Inoperative windshield wipers can reduce visibility in certain driving conditions, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace both front windshield wiper arms, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V420000: Equipment:Other:Owners/Service/Other Manual](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lincoln&model=Navigator&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 202, "Head Restraints." Without instructions, the head restraints may be improperly adjusted, increasing the risk of injury in the event of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 23V598000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lincoln&model=Navigator&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** The rearview camera, or 360-degree view camera if equipped, may not display a rearview image when the vehicle is placed in reverse. Loss of the rearview camera image can reduce the driver's rear visibility, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the rearview camera, and update the software and wiring as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V099000: Seat Belts:Pretensioner](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lincoln&model=Navigator&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** The seat belt pretensioner in the driver and/or front passenger seat may inadvertently deploy and lock the seat belt, which will not allow the belt to retract or extend. Seat belts that do not retract or extend may fail to restrain an occupant as intended, increasing the risk of injury in a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the seat belt retractors, as necessary. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V852000: Equipment:Other:Labels](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lincoln&model=Navigator&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirement Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 208, "Occupant Crash Protection." Without a label that shows the proper restraint usage guidelines, the occupants may have an increased risk of injury in the event of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 25V197000: Seat Belts:Pretensioner](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lincoln&model=Navigator&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** The seat belt pretensioner in the driver and/or front passenger seat may inadvertently lock the seat belt, which will not allow the belt to retract or extend. Seat belts that do not retract or extend can fail to restrain an occupant as intended, increasing the risk of injury in a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the seat belt retractors as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

When evaluating a used Lincoln Navigator, prioritize models with comprehensive service records, particularly regarding transmission performance and regular fluid changes. Conduct a thorough test drive to verify smooth gear shifts and test all electronic accessories before finalizing your purchase.
