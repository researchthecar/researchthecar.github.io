# 2018 Lincoln Navigator Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2018 Lincoln Navigator delivers luxury, robust towing capability, and spacious accommodations, but prospective buyers should carefully review its mechanical track record. Owner reports highlight recurring areas of concern, notably involving the engine, transmission, and steering systems. Checking service history and performing a thorough pre-purchase inspection can help uncover potential underlying issues before buying.

---

## Engine

### 3.5L Twin-Turbocharged V6

The 3.5-liter twin-turbocharged EcoBoost V6 generates approximately 450 horsepower and 510 lb-ft of torque, providing strong acceleration and impressive towing performance. While powerful and efficient for a large luxury SUV, owners have reported reliability concerns related to timing chain and cam phaser wear.

**Cam Phaser Rattle**
- **Symptoms:** A noticeable metallic rattling or knocking noise originating from the engine bay during cold starts.
- **Solution:** Requires replacement of the worn cam phasers and associated timing components.

**Loss of Motive Power**
- **Symptoms:** Sudden shuddering, hesitation, or unexpected loss of engine power while driving.
- **Solution:** Diagnostic testing by a qualified technician to address failing timing or variable cam timing (VCT) components.

## Transmission

### 10-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 10-speed automatic transmission pairs with the V6 engine to optimize highway cruising and fuel economy. However, some owners have experienced premature shifting faults and mechanical failures.

**Transmission Slipping and Hard Shifting**
- **Symptoms:** Severe jerking, harsh gear engagements, sudden shifts into neutral, or a complete loss of forward propulsion while driving at speed.
- **Solution:** Have the transmission thoroughly inspected; repairs may involve valve body service, software reflashes, or internal component replacement.

## Steering

### Steering Column and Rack

Issues involving unusual noises and mechanical binding within the steering column have been reported by owners shortly after purchase.

**Clunking and Metallic Noise**
- **Symptoms:** Audible clunking, grinding, or metallic contact noises when turning the steering wheel or operating the power adjustment features.
- **Solution:** Inspect the steering column and intermediate shaft for wear, and replace damaged mechanical components as necessary.

## Exterior

### Door Latches and Sunroof

Exterior hardware complaints largely center around latch mechanisms and sunroof rail durability.

**Door Latch and Sunroof Track Failures**
- **Symptoms:** Doors failing to stay securely latched while driving or sunroof plastic rail tracks breaking, leaving the roof stuck open.
- **Solution:** Replace the faulty door lock actuators or repair/replace the sunroof track mechanism.

## Safety

### Seat Belts

Owner complaints highlight safety concerns regarding seatbelt locking mechanisms failing to operate properly.

**Seatbelt Retractor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Seatbelts locking up entirely and preventing passengers from buckling, or aggressively retracting with excessive force during use.
- **Solution:** Replace the malfunctioning seatbelt assembly and retractor mechanism.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V392000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lincoln&model=Navigator&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** These vehicles have high pressure fuel pump assemblies with welds that can fracture, which may cause an oil or fuel leak. A fuel leak in the presence of an ignition source may increase the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will replace the high pressure fuel pump and associated components, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V805000: Seats:Mid/Rear Assembly](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lincoln&model=Navigator&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** The second row center seat track assemblies may be missing one or both of the J-Channel reinforcement brackets, possibly allowing the seat to move in the event of a crash. In the event of a crash, a seat that moves may not properly restrain the seat occupant, increasing their risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the second row center seat for any missing J-channel reinforcement brackets, replacing the center seat frame assembly as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V213000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lincoln&model=Navigator&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** A roll pin may not have been installed in the transmission, potentially causing a loss of the "Park" function. Despite the transmission shift lever being placed in 'Park' and the instrument panel display indicating 'Park,' the vehicle may roll away after it has been exited if the parking brake has not been applied, increasing the risk of injury or a crash.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the transmission to make sure it has the roll pin, installing one if it is missing, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V058000: Visibility:Power Window Devices And Controls](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lincoln&model=Navigator&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 118, "Power-Operated Window Systems." If the window does not automatically reverse when an obstruction is found, it can increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the second row window electronic control modules, and replace them as necessary based on their build date codes, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V420000: Equipment:Other:Owners/Service/Other Manual](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lincoln&model=Navigator&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 202, "Head Restraints." Without instructions, the head restraints may be improperly adjusted, increasing the risk of injury in the event of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 23V598000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lincoln&model=Navigator&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** The rearview camera, or 360-degree view camera if equipped, may not display a rearview image when the vehicle is placed in reverse. Loss of the rearview camera image can reduce the driver's rear visibility, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the rearview camera, and update the software and wiring as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V099000: Seat Belts:Pretensioner](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lincoln&model=Navigator&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** The seat belt pretensioner in the driver and/or front passenger seat may inadvertently deploy and lock the seat belt, which will not allow the belt to retract or extend. Seat belts that do not retract or extend may fail to restrain an occupant as intended, increasing the risk of injury in a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the seat belt retractors, as necessary. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V852000: Equipment:Other:Labels](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lincoln&model=Navigator&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirement Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 208, "Occupant Crash Protection." Without a label that shows the proper restraint usage guidelines, the occupants may have an increased risk of injury in the event of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2018 Lincoln Navigator is a feature-rich luxury utility vehicle, but prospective buyers should approach with caution due to documented powertrain and mechanical complaints. Prioritize models with comprehensive service records verifying that major engine and transmission updates or repairs have been completed. A rigorous pre-purchase inspection by an independent mechanic is strongly advised before finalizing any purchase.
