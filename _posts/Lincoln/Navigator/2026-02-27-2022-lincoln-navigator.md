# 2022 Lincoln Navigator Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2022 Lincoln Navigator delivers luxury, robust towing capability, and an expansive cabin, but used car buyers should carefully review owner-reported mechanical and electronic feedback. While owner complaints to the NHTSA are individual reports rather than proof of systemic defects, noting recurring patterns helps prospective buyers conduct thorough pre-purchase inspections.

---

## Engine

### 3.5L Twin-Turbocharged EcoBoost V6

This 3.5-liter twin-turbocharged V6 generates approximately 440 horsepower and 510 lb-ft of torque, providing strong acceleration and towing performance. It serves as the standard powerplant for the Navigator lineup, balancing heavy-duty capability with complex turbo plumbing.

## Transmission

### 10-Speed SelectShift Automatic Transmission

Paired with the EcoBoost V6, this 10-speed automatic routes power to the rear wheels or intelligent four-wheel-drive systems. While designed for smooth operation and efficiency, some owners have experienced rough shifting behaviors.

**Violent Shifting and Gear Hunting**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have reported harsh shifting, sudden power jolts before gear changes, hesitation, and sporadic downshifting at low speeds.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified technician inspect transmission software calibration, fluid condition, and control modules for updates or mechanical wear.

## Brakes

### Brake Lines and Hydraulics

Hydraulic brake components demand rigorous inspection to ensure safe stopping performance under heavy loads.

**Brake Fluid Loss and Line Security**
- **Symptoms:** Complete loss of brake fluid and subsequent loss of braking capability can occur if brake lines rub against the frame or become unclipped.
- **Solution:** Inspect all brake lines and retaining clips during routine service to ensure lines are securely fastened and free from chafing.

## Electrical

### Infotainment and Accessory Modules

The Navigator relies heavily on advanced digital architecture and control modules to manage comfort, infotainment, and convenience features.

**Screen Blanking and Module Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The central display monitor may go completely blank while driving, which can disrupt secondary features such as retractable running boards and climate controls.
- **Solution:** Inspect electrical modules, check for software updates, and replace faulty control units if necessary.

## Exterior

### Moonroof Glass

The expansive exterior glass panels offer an airy cabin feel but require careful inspection for structural stress.

**Spontaneous Glass Shattering**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have reported instances where the panoramic moonroof glass suddenly imploded or shattered while parked or driving without external impact.
- **Solution:** Inspect the moonroof assembly for stress cracks or alignment issues and replace damaged glass panels under comprehensive insurance or warranty if applicable.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V193000: Electrical System: Integrated Trailer Brake Control](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lincoln&model=Navigator&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** Due to a software error, a towed trailer equipped with an electric or electric-over hydraulic brake system may not brake. Loss of trailer brake functionality could result in extended stopping distance, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the integrated trailer brake control module software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V455000: Exterior Lighting:Lighting Control Module:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lincoln&model=Navigator&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** The headlight control module software may cause the side marker and daytime running lights to fail. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 108, "Lamps, Reflective Devices, and Associated Equipment." A loss of exterior lighting can reduce visibility and increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the headlight control module software and recalibrate the headlights, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V420000: Equipment:Other:Owners/Service/Other Manual](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lincoln&model=Navigator&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 202, "Head Restraints." Without instructions, the head restraints may be improperly adjusted, increasing the risk of injury in the event of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 24V852000: Equipment:Other:Labels](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lincoln&model=Navigator&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirement Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 208, "Occupant Crash Protection." Without a label that shows the proper restraint usage guidelines, the occupants may have an increased risk of injury in the event of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 25V132000: Electrical System: Integrated Trailer Brake Control](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lincoln&model=Navigator&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** Due to a software error, a towed trailer equipped with an electric or electric-over hydraulic brake system may not brake. Loss of trailer brake functionality can extend the distance required to stop, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the integrated trailer brake control module software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V314000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Foundation Components:Hoses, Lines/Piping, And Fittings](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lincoln&model=Navigator&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** A loss of brake function increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the front brake line and replace the brake line or air cleaner outlet pipe, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V315000: Back Over Prevention:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lincoln&model=Navigator&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** A software error may cause the rearview camera image to delay, freeze, or not display when the vehicle is in reverse. A frozen or missing rearview camera image can reduce the driver's view behind the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 25V403000: Exterior Lighting:Lighting Control Module:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lincoln&model=Navigator&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** The headlight control module software may cause the side marker and daytime running lights to fail. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 108, "Lamps, Reflective Devices, and Associated Equipment." A loss of exterior lighting can reduce visibility and increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the headlight control module software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2022 Lincoln Navigator is a sophisticated and capable luxury full-size SUV, but prospective buyers should perform a detailed inspection of its electronics, transmission shift quality, and brake lines. Ensuring all software updates are applied and checking for any unresolved open recalls will help secure a more reliable ownership experience.
