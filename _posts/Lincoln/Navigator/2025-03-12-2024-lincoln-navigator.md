The 2024 Lincoln Navigator is a full-size luxury SUV known for its opulent interior, spacious cabin, and smooth ride. The Navigator comes standard with a twin-turbocharged 3.5L V6 engine producing 450 horsepower and 510 lb-ft of torque, paired with a 10-speed automatic transmission. People often praise the Navigator for its comfortable seating, advanced technology features, and powerful engine. However, some common complaints include its large size making it difficult to maneuver in tight spaces, and its fuel economy.

**Engine**

**3.5L Twin-Turbocharged V6**

*   **Turbocharger Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Whining or whistling noise from the engine, loss of power, check engine light illuminated.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the faulty turbocharger unit.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,500 - $4,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Coolant Intrusion**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Engine misfire, white smoke coming from the exhaust, coolant loss without visible leaks.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the entire engine.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $12,000 - $15,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**10-Speed Automatic**

*   **Rough or Delayed Shifting**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Hesitation when shifting between gears, harsh or jerky shifts, particularly between lower gears.
    *   Solution: Software reflash of the transmission control module (TCM). If the problem persists, replacement of the valve body or, in severe cases, the entire transmission.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: Software reflash: $150-$300; Valve body replacement: $1,200-$2,000; Transmission replacement: $5,000 - $8,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Air Suspension Compressor Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Vehicle sitting unevenly, warning light on the dashboard indicating suspension issues, loud hissing noise from the compressor area.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the air suspension compressor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Air Suspension Strut Leak**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Vehicle sagging on one side, bouncy ride, and suspension warning lights.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the leaking air suspension strut.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+ per strut
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **SYNC 4 Infotainment System Freezing/Crashing**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The touchscreen freezes or becomes unresponsive, the system reboots unexpectedly, error messages appear.
    *   Solution: Software update, or in severe cases, replacement of the APIM (Accessory Protocol Interface Module).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: Software update: $0 - $200 (if not covered under warranty); APIM replacement: $800 - $1,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Battery Drain Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Dead battery overnight or after a short period of inactivity, difficulty starting the vehicle.
    *   Solution: Identifying and addressing the parasitic drain. This may involve updating software for one or more modules, or replacing the battery.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+ (depending on the cause and parts needed)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Automatic Running Board Malfunction**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Running boards not deploying or retracting properly, making grinding noises, or getting stuck in either the extended or retracted position.
    *   Solution: Repair or replacement of the running board motor or the entire running board assembly.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: Motor replacement: $400-$800; Assembly replacement: $1,200 - $2,500+ per side
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Seat Motor Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Seat no longer moves forward, backward, up, or down; grinding noises when attempting to adjust the seat.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the faulty seat motor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800+ per motor
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2024 Lincoln Navigator, while luxurious and powerful, has some reported issues across various systems. Engine problems like turbocharger and coolant intrusion can be very costly. Transmission issues like rough shifting and are fairly common, with repair costs potentially reaching significant amounts. Suspension issues, electrical glitches with the SYNC 4 system, and problems with the automatic running boards and seats can also contribute to owner dissatisfaction. Overall, while none of the issues above are particularly common on this vehicle, the severity and cost associated with some of these repairs can be high. It's important for prospective buyers to be aware of these potential problems and ensure proper maintenance and warranty coverage.
