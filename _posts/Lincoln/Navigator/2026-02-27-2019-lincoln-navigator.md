# 2019 Lincoln Navigator Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2019 Lincoln Navigator is a full-size luxury SUV known for its spacious cabin, upscale features, and robust towing capability. While it offers impressive comfort and utility, used-car buyers should pay close attention to powertrain performance and specific cabin electronics. Reviewing maintenance records and performing a thorough pre-purchase inspection can help uncover potential underlying concerns.

---

## Engine

### 3.5L Twin-Turbocharged V6

Equipped with a 3.5-liter twin-turbocharged V6 engine producing approximately 450 horsepower and 510 lb-ft of torque, providing strong acceleration and towing power. Overall engine architecture is generally solid, though routine maintenance of cooling and turbo systems is essential.

## Transmission

### 10-Speed Automatic Transmission

Paired with a 10-speed automatic transmission available in rear-wheel drive or four-wheel drive configurations. While it aids fuel economy and highway cruising, some owners report shift quality complaints that require software updates or mechanical inspection.

**Hard Downshifts and Lurching**
- **Symptoms:** The transmission may experience harsh downshifts, jerking into gear, or abrupt wheel speed reduction after extended driving.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified technician inspect transmission software calibration, fluid condition, and valve body operation.

**Unintended Neutral Shift**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle unexpectedly drops out of gear while driving or accelerating, acting as if it has been shifted into neutral.
- **Solution:** Check for applicable powertrain control module updates or mechanical transmission component wear.

## Electrical

### Infotainment and Instrument Cluster

Electrical and infotainment systems offer advanced connectivity and display features, though occasional glitches in screens and displays have been reported by owners.

**Blank Dashboard or Display Failures**
- **Symptoms:** The digital instrument cluster or navigation screen may occasionally go completely blank or fail to display vital vehicle data upon startup.
- **Solution:** Perform a system reset, check software versions, or have the module inspected by a dealership.

## Safety

### Seat Belts

Safety equipment includes advanced restraint systems, though front seat belt retractor concerns have been noted in owner complaints and related recall campaigns.

**Seat Belt Retractor Seizure**
- **Symptoms:** Front driver or passenger seat belt retractors may unexpectedly lock up, seize, or fail to retract properly.
- **Solution:** Have the seat belt assemblies inspected and replaced if affected by manufacturer recall or component failure.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V076000: Electrical System: Instrument Cluster/Panel](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lincoln&model=Navigator&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** At vehicle start-up, the Instrument Panel Cluster Assembly (IPC) may not function, showing a blank display. Driving with an inoperative cluster can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Ford has notified owners, and dealers will reprogram the Instrument Panel Cluster Assembly (IPC), free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V773000: Suspension:Rear](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lincoln&model=Navigator&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** The rear suspension toe link fasteners may not have been properly tightened to the frame, possibly allowing separation from the frame. A disconnected toe link can cause a sudden change to vehicle handling, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Ford has notified owners, and dealers will inspect and tighten the rear toe link fasteners, check and adjust the rear toe alignment, and inspect the rear tires for abnormal tire wear, replacing them if necessary. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V420000: Equipment:Other:Owners/Service/Other Manual](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lincoln&model=Navigator&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 202, "Head Restraints." Without instructions, the head restraints may be improperly adjusted, increasing the risk of injury in the event of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 23V598000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lincoln&model=Navigator&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** The rearview camera, or 360-degree view camera if equipped, may not display a rearview image when the vehicle is placed in reverse. Loss of the rearview camera image can reduce the driver's rear visibility, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the rearview camera, and update the software and wiring as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V099000: Seat Belts:Pretensioner](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lincoln&model=Navigator&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** The seat belt pretensioner in the driver and/or front passenger seat may inadvertently deploy and lock the seat belt, which will not allow the belt to retract or extend. Seat belts that do not retract or extend may fail to restrain an occupant as intended, increasing the risk of injury in a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the seat belt retractors, as necessary. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V852000: Equipment:Other:Labels](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lincoln&model=Navigator&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirement Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 208, "Occupant Crash Protection." Without a label that shows the proper restraint usage guidelines, the occupants may have an increased risk of injury in the event of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 25V197000: Seat Belts:Pretensioner](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lincoln&model=Navigator&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** The seat belt pretensioner in the driver and/or front passenger seat may inadvertently lock the seat belt, which will not allow the belt to retract or extend. Seat belts that do not retract or extend can fail to restrain an occupant as intended, increasing the risk of injury in a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the seat belt retractors as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V344000: Seat Belts:Pretensioner](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lincoln&model=Navigator&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** The seat belt pretensioner in the driver and/or front passenger seat may inadvertently lock the seat belt, which will not allow the belt to retract or extend. Seat belts that do not retract or extend can fail to restrain an occupant as intended, increasing the risk of injury in a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the seat belt retractors as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2019 Lincoln Navigator delivers luxurious comfort and strong performance, making it a compelling option in the full-size SUV segment. Prospective buyers should carefully evaluate transmission shift quality, verify all electrical and infotainment functions operate correctly, and confirm that all safety recalls—particularly regarding seat belts—have been addressed.
