# 2017 Lincoln Navigator Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2017 Lincoln Navigator is a full-size luxury SUV offering robust towing capabilities, spacious seating, and a comfortable ride. While it delivers strong utility, prospective buyers should review known owner concerns related to electrical accessories, powertrain operation, and certain safety equipment.

---

## Engine

### 3.5L Twin-Turbocharged V6

Equipped with a 3.5-liter twin-turbocharged EcoBoost V6 producing approximately 380 horsepower and 430 lb-ft of torque. This engine provides strong acceleration and towing power, though routine monitoring of fluid levels is recommended.

**Fuel System and Dilution Concerns**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have reported issues such as fuel pump failures or rising oil levels caused by fuel leaking into the crankcase.
- **Solution:** Inspect the fuel pump module and ensure proper diagnosis by a certified mechanic; replace failing components as necessary.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

Paired with a 6-speed automatic transmission available in both rear-wheel drive and four-wheel drive configurations. It generally offers smooth operation, but electronic glitches can occasionally trigger limp-mode or warning indicators.

**Unexpected Downshifting**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may unexpectedly downshift while driving on the highway, accompanied by warning lights on the instrument cluster.
- **Solution:** Have the transmission control module and diagnostic trouble codes checked by a qualified technician.

## Brakes

### Hydraulic Brake System

The braking system provides adequate stopping power for a heavy luxury SUV, but diligent inspection of hydraulic components is vital for safe operation.

**Brake Fluid Leaks and Pedal Travel**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may experience a soft brake pedal, increased stopping distances, or fluid pooling near the rear of the vehicle.
- **Solution:** Inspect brake lines, master cylinders, and boosters thoroughly for leaks and wear, addressing any failing parts immediately.

## Steering

### Power Steering System

Power steering assist relies heavily on electrical system integrity; intermittent faults can temporarily increase steering effort.

**Stiff Steering and AdvanceTrac Warnings**
- **Symptoms:** Upon vehicle startup, drivers may encounter hill start and AdvanceTrac warning messages coupled with heavy, stiff steering effort.
- **Solution:** Diagnose electronic power steering modules and battery voltage levels to ensure proper sensor communication.

## Electrical

### Back Over Prevention Camera

Safety electronics help assist with maneuvering this large vehicle, though display reliability issues have been noted by multiple owners.

**Intermittent Rearview Camera Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The rearview camera display may fail to activate or remain blank when shifting into reverse.
- **Solution:** Check wiring harnesses and camera modules, and consult a dealer regarding applicable manufacturer service campaigns.

## Exterior

### Exterior Lighting and Controls

Electrical accessories govern convenience features and exterior illumination, requiring careful testing during a pre-purchase inspection.

**Turn Signal and Mirror Light Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Rapidly toggling turn signal stalks can disable signal functionality, and side mirror puddle lights may experience operational faults.
- **Solution:** Inspect multi-function switches and lighting modules for internal failures; replace switches as required.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V150000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Power Assist](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lincoln&model=Navigator&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** These conditions can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the brake master cylinder. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V648000: Visibility:Defroster/Defogger/Hvac System:Fan/Motor](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lincoln&model=Navigator&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** Front blower motor failure can increase the risk of a vehicle interior fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the front blower motor assembly, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V091000: Visibility:Rearview Mirrors/Devices:Exterior](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lincoln&model=Navigator&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** The LED logo lights on the exterior mirrors may short circuit. An electrical short circuit in the exterior mirror increases the risk of fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will disconnect the LED light wires, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V236000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Foundation Components:Master Cylinder](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lincoln&model=Navigator&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** A brake fluid leak can cause reduced brake function and extend the distance required to stop, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the brake master cylinder. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V572000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lincoln&model=Navigator&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** A rearview camera that displays an inverted, distorted, or blank image can reduce the driver’s view behind the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the rearview camera as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2017 Lincoln Navigator remains a capable and luxurious choice for buyers needing substantial towing and passenger space. However, potential owners should perform a comprehensive pre-purchase inspection paying close attention to the brake lines, rearview camera function, and electronic accessories.
