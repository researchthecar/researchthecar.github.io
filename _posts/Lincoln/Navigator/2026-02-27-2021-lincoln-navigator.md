# 2021 Lincoln Navigator Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2021 Lincoln Navigator, buyers will find a large, luxurious full-size SUV offering high-end comfort and robust hauling capability. While its cabin appointments and power delivery impress owners, prospective buyers should review real-world reports noting occasional drivetrain quirks, electrical glitches, and body accessories that require careful inspection.

---

## Engine

### 3.5L Twin-Turbocharged V6

Powered by a 3.5-liter twin-turbocharged V6 producing roughly 450 horsepower and 510 lb-ft of torque, this engine offers strong towing and smooth acceleration. Long-term reliability depends heavily on consistent maintenance and monitoring of the fuel and induction systems.

**Engine Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers report unexpected engine hesitation or stalling while attempting to accelerate, creating dangerous driving situations.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified mechanic inspect the fuel system and electronic throttle body for faults, and verify all relevant powertrain updates are completed.

## Transmission

### 10-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 10-speed automatic transmission works across all trims and rear- or four-wheel-drive configurations to optimize fuel economy and power delivery. While generally responsive, some owners have encountered complex shifting faults that demand thorough pre-purchase evaluation.

**Slipping and Unexpected Neutral Shifting**
- **Symptoms:** The transmission may suddenly slip out of gear, drop into neutral under acceleration, or display powertrain malfunction warnings.
- **Solution:** Diagnostic scans by a professional technician are required; repairs may range from software recalibrations to major internal overhauls or component replacements.

## Steering

### Steering Column and Power Steering

The power-adjustable steering column and electronic power steering assist provide effortless maneuverability for a large SUV, though mechanical wear can occasionally affect column motors and assist modules.

**Steering Column Grinding and Loss of Power Assist**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may hear grinding noises when adjusting the power tilt/telescoping steering wheel or experience sudden power steering failure making the wheel very stiff.
- **Solution:** Inspect the steering column mechanism and electronic steering components; faulty steering assemblies may need to be replaced.

## Electrical

### Electrical and Exterior Lighting

Advanced electronics power everything from cabin conveniences to safety features, making a thorough battery and wiring check essential during a pre-purchase inspection.

**Burning Smell and Rear Light Bar Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Water intrusion can cause the rear light bar assembly to fail, while remote-start usage can occasionally produce burning odors and panel melting.
- **Solution:** Inspect the rear light bar for moisture buildup and check vehicle wiring harnesses for any signs of thermal damage or high-resistance electrical faults.

### Backup Camera and Visibility

Driver-assistance and safety systems heavily rely on multi-camera arrays to assist with parking and maneuvering this large utility vehicle safely.

**Backup Camera Failure in Warm Cabins**
- **Symptoms:** The rearview camera display may fail to activate or remain blank when entering a warm vehicle until the cabin air cools down.
- **Solution:** Have the backup camera module and display system diagnosed by a dealer or technician, as software updates or hardware replacement may be necessary.

## Exterior

### Glass and Windshield Wipers

Exterior panels and glass features define the Navigator's upscale look, but buyers should carefully inspect sunroof assemblies and wiper hardware for weathering or alignment defects.

**Panoramic Sunroof Shading and Wiper Arm Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The panoramic glass roof can experience spontaneous breakage or shattering, and passenger windshield wiper arms may loosen or detach during heavy rain.
- **Solution:** Examine the sunroof glass for stress points and inspect windshield wiper linkages to ensure proper arm tension and secure attachment.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V805000: Air Bags:Sensor:Side Impact](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lincoln&model=Navigator&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The front-door side impact pressure sensor modules may have been damaged during supplier assembly, which can cause the side impact air bags to not deploy properly in a crash. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 214, "Side Impact Protection." An air bag that does not deploy properly.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the driver and passenger front door side impact sensors, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V250000: Visibility:Windshield Wiper/Washer:Linkages](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lincoln&model=Navigator&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** Inoperative windshield wipers can reduce visibility in certain driving conditions, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace both front windshield wiper arms, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V346000: Engine](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lincoln&model=Navigator&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** An engine compartment fire may occur while driving or parked, even with the ignition in the "Off" position. A vehicle fire increases the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the battery junction box, as necessary, and remove a ground wire. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V420000: Equipment:Other:Owners/Service/Other Manual](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lincoln&model=Navigator&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 202, "Head Restraints." Without instructions, the head restraints may be improperly adjusted, increasing the risk of injury in the event of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 23V598000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lincoln&model=Navigator&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The rearview camera, or 360-degree view camera if equipped, may not display a rearview image when the vehicle is placed in reverse. Loss of the rearview camera image can reduce the driver's rear visibility, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the rearview camera, and update the software and wiring as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V852000: Equipment:Other:Labels](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lincoln&model=Navigator&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirement Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 208, "Occupant Crash Protection." Without a label that shows the proper restraint usage guidelines, the occupants may have an increased risk of injury in the event of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 25V442000: Back Over Prevention:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lincoln&model=Navigator&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** A software error may cause the rearview camera to display a blank image, or the image may remain on the display after the backing event has ended. Both of these situations can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the rearview camera software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V455000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lincoln&model=Navigator&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** An engine stall increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the low pressure fuel pump on super duty vehicles and update the powertrain control module (PCM) software on all other affected vehicles, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2021 Lincoln Navigator delivers impressive power, space, and comfort, but used-car buyers should perform a rigorous pre-purchase inspection. Pay close attention to transmission shift quality, electronic steering function, exterior glass, and the electrical system to avoid unexpected repair bills after purchase.
