# 2020 Lincoln Aviator Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2020 Lincoln Aviator offers a luxurious cabin, advanced tech, and strong performance, but used-car buyers should approach with caution. Owners have reported a mix of complex electrical glitches, unexpected rolling or parking shift defects, and transmission concerns that require careful inspection before purchase.

---

## Engine

### 3.0L Twin-Turbocharged V6

The 3.0-liter twin-turbocharged V6 engine produces around 400 horsepower and 415 lb-ft of torque, delivering brisk acceleration for the midsize luxury SUV. While generally robust, some owners have noted intermittent startup noise and rough initial engagement when shifting into gear.

## Transmission

### 10-Speed SelectShift Automatic

The 10-speed automatic transmission routes power to either the rear wheels or all four wheels. While it provides smooth highway cruising when functioning correctly, it is associated with reports of slipping and internal mechanical wear.

**Transmission Slipping and Harsh Shifting**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers experience unexpected transmission slipping, loss of forward power, hard engagement, and sometimes require major internal repairs like valve body or complete transmission replacements.
- **Solution:** Have a certified specialist inspect transmission operation and check for fluid leaks or software updates; severe internal wear may require component replacement.

## Brakes

### Shifter and Parking Mechanism

Electrical architecture manages numerous convenience and safety modules, but complex cabin electronics and gear-selector wiring have drawn owner complaints.

**Unexpected Vehicle Roll or Park Defect**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may unexpectedly roll after being placed in park, or fail to register the correct gear selection upon startup, creating rollaway hazards.
- **Solution:** Inspect parking pawl mechanisms, ensure software updates for gear selection are applied, and always engage the manual parking brake on inclines.

## Safety

### Axle Shafts and Warning Lights

The drivetrain components distribute power efficiently, though auxiliary seals and electronic monitoring sensors require periodic maintenance.

**Grinding Noises and Electrical Warnings**
- **Symptoms:** Owners note sudden grinding noises from failed axle shaft seals and erratic dashboard warning lights impacting driver-assist and safety features.
- **Solution:** Inspect axle shaft seals for leaks and check diagnostic trouble codes related to body control modules and parking assist sensors.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V859000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Hoses, Lines/Piping, And Fittings](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lincoln&model=Aviator&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** A fuel leak in the presence of an ignition source increases the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will install a longer protective sleeve on the liquid fuel line, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V788000: Engine](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lincoln&model=Aviator&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** With a loose motor mount, motor vibration may cause the axle to disconnect from the engine and could result in loss of power, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will remove and replace the right hand motor mount fasteners, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V154000: Suspension:Rear](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lincoln&model=Aviator&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** The rear lower control arm (RLCA) bolts that attach the rear lower control arms to the wheel knuckles may not have been heat-treated, which could cause them to break. Broken RLCA bolts can cause a loss of steering control, and increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will replace both RLCA bolts, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V735000: Back Over Prevention:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lincoln&model=Aviator&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** A rearview camera that does not display an image reduces the driver's rear view, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the Image Processing Module (IPMB) software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V223000: Back Over Prevention:Display Function](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lincoln&model=Aviator&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** The image processing module may be unable to provide video feed to the display, which could result in a loss of the backup camera image. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of the Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 111, "Rear Visibility." A backup camera that displays a blank or distorted image reduces the driver's rear.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will update the image processing module software with the latest level, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V693000: Power Train:Driveline:Driveshaft](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lincoln&model=Aviator&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** Either condition can increase the risk of a crash. The drive shafts can also deform and separate from the driveline and potentially contact the fuel tank, increasing the risk of fuel leaks and/or fire.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the drive shaft label inspected and replace it as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V534000: Visibility:Defroster/Defogger/Hvac System:Compressor](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lincoln&model=Aviator&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** The wire harness may not be secured properly, possibly contacting the air conditioning (A/C) compressor pulley, and resulting in damage to electrical wires, or the A/C compressor drive belt. Damaged electrical wires may short-circuit, increasing the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the harness and A/C compressor belt for damage, replacing them as necessary, and install a tie strap to secure the wire harness, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V497000: Air Bags:Critical Fasteners](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lincoln&model=Aviator&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** The side air bag and/or seat back module attachments may not have been properly tightened in the front seat backs. If the side air bag or seat back fasteners were not properly tightened, the side air bag deployment could be affected, increasing the risk of injury during a crash.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will remove front seat back panels from one or both front seats (VIN-specific) and verify that the fasteners have been properly tightened, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

When shopping for a used 2020 Lincoln Aviator, verify that all powertrain software updates have been performed and inspect the transmission and parking brake systems thoroughly. A pre-purchase inspection by a qualified mechanic is strongly recommended to catch any hidden electrical or mechanical issues before buying.
