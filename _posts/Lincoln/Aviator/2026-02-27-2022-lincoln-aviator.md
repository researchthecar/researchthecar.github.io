# 2022 Lincoln Aviator Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2022 Lincoln Aviator offers a luxurious midsize SUV experience with powerful performance and premium amenities, but prospective buyers should inspect common owner-reported vulnerabilities closely. While powertrain options generally provide strong performance, owner feedback highlights a mix of electrical, exterior, and fuel system concerns that warrant careful attention during a pre-purchase inspection.

---

## Engine

### 3.0L Twin-Turbocharged V6

The 3.0-liter twin-turbocharged V6 engine produces approximately 400 horsepower and 415 lb-ft of torque, delivering brisk acceleration for the midsize luxury SUV. Overall reliability is generally respectable, though buyers should monitor for occasional reports of fuel pressure fluctuations and stalling.

**Engine Stalling and Fuel Pressure Loss**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle may experience unexpected engine stalling while driving at various speeds or sudden loss of fuel pressure where the vehicle fails to respond to the gas pedal.
- **Solution:** Have a certified mechanic inspect the fuel system and consult dealership bulletins for applicable software updates or fuel component replacements.

### 3.0L V6 Grand Touring Plug-in Hybrid

The Grand Touring plug-in hybrid powertrain pairs the 3.0-liter twin-turbo V6 with an electric motor to generate an impressive 494 horsepower and 630 lb-ft of torque. It offers strong electrified performance and efficiency, though buyers should verify proper hybrid system operation during an inspection.

## Transmission

### 10-Speed SelectShift Automatic Transmission

The 10-speed automatic transmission routes power smoothly through rear-wheel drive or intelligent all-wheel-drive configurations. It generally performs well in daily driving, though proper fluid maintenance is critical for long-term durability.

## Electrical

### Infotainment and Display Systems

Electrical components govern numerous convenience and safety features across the vehicle, making a thorough functional check of infotainment and automated sensors essential.

**Screen Display and Night Mode Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** The infotainment display may fail to switch to night mode during nighttime driving, producing an excessively bright screen that causes driver distraction.
- **Solution:** Check for system software updates through the dealership or reset the infotainment display.

### Wipers and Visibility Sensors

Exterior trim and visibility systems require attention to ensure weather-stripping, sensors, and wiper operations function reliably in all conditions.

**Autosense Wiper Operation Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Automatic rain-sensing wipers may fail to clear the windshield adequately during steady precipitation, impacting outward visibility.
- **Solution:** Have the autosense wiper sensor inspected and recalibrated or replaced if unresponsive.

### Seating Mechanisms

Interior cabin features provide high-end comfort, though power-operated seating components can occasionally exhibit switch or track glitches.

**Second-Row Electric Slide Switch Sticking**
- **Symptoms:** The electrical switch used to slide and fold the second-row seats for third-row access may stick or operate randomly.
- **Solution:** Inspect the seat switch wiring and replace faulty control switches if necessary.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V088000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Storage:Tank Assembly:Filler Pipe And Cap](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lincoln&model=Aviator&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** The fuel filler tube may detach during a crash. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 301, "Fuel System Integrity." A detached fuel filler tube can result in a fuel leak, increasing the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will install a fuel vapor line redundant locking clip, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V247000: Seats:Front Assembly:Head Restraint](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lincoln&model=Aviator&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** A detached head restraint cushion can increase the risk of injury during a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the front-row passenger and driver head restraints, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V454000: Structure:Frame And Members](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lincoln&model=Aviator&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard numbers 208, "Occupant Crash Protection" and 301, "Fuel System Integrity." Improperly heat-treated engine rails may reduce the vehicle's structural protection in a crash, increasing the risk of injury. In addition, an engine rail may detach and contact the.
- **What to do:** Dealers will perform an engine rail strength test and replace the rails as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V953000: Seat Belts:Front:Warning Light/Devices](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lincoln&model=Aviator&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 208, "Occupant Crash Protection." An audible warning chime that does not properly alert of an unbelted seat belt can increase the risk of injury during a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the audio control module software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V022000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lincoln&model=Aviator&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** Loss of the rearview camera image can reduce the driver's rear visibility, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the image processing module (IPMB) software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V342000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lincoln&model=Aviator&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** Loss of the rearview camera image can reduce the driver's rear visibility, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the 360-degree camera, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V598000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lincoln&model=Aviator&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** The rearview camera, or 360-degree view camera if equipped, may not display a rearview image when the vehicle is placed in reverse. Loss of the rearview camera image can reduce the driver's rear visibility, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the rearview camera, and update the software and wiring as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V626000: Electrical System:Propulsion System:Traction Battery](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lincoln&model=Aviator&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** A manufacturing defect in one or more of the high voltage battery cells may result in an internal short circuit and battery failure. Battery failure can cause a loss of drive power, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will perform a Battery Energy Control Module (BECM) diagnostic test and replace the high voltage battery pack, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2022 Lincoln Aviator is an appealing luxury SUV packed with advanced features and robust engine choices, but potential buyers should perform a diligent inspection. Pay close attention to electrical features, wiper sensors, and powertrain performance during a test drive to ensure a trouble-free ownership experience.
