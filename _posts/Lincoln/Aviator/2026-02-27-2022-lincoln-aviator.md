# 2022 Lincoln Aviator

### Overview
The 2022 Lincoln Aviator, a luxury three-row SUV, generally presents a strong profile in terms of reliability, particularly for its powertrain options. Lincoln has focused on delivering a refined driving experience with robust engineering. While no vehicle is entirely immune to potential issues, the Aviator's design and componentry have largely held up well in its initial model years. Owners can expect a comfortable and capable vehicle, with most reported concerns being minor or isolated incidents rather than widespread systemic failures. This analysis focuses on documented technical reliability, excluding routine maintenance and expected wear.

### Summary Verdict
The 2022 Lincoln Aviator demonstrates commendable reliability, especially concerning its core powertrain components, the 3.0L and 2.7L Twin-Turbo V6 engines paired with the 10-speed automatic transmission. These systems are generally robust and free from widespread reported issues. The primary areas where minor concerns have surfaced include the adaptive air suspension, with occasional reports of compressor malfunctions, and the electrical system, particularly the infotainment unit which can experience software glitches. Battery drain issues, though not pervasive, have also been noted. Overall, the Aviator offers a solid reliability record for its class, with most potential issues being manageable and not indicative of systemic design flaws.
