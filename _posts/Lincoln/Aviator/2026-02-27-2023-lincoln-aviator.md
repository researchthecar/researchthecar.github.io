# 2023 Lincoln Aviator Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2023 Lincoln Aviator delivers strong performance, luxurious styling, and high-tech amenities, but prospective buyers should review owner complaints regarding powertrain performance and electronic glitches. While vehicle reports reflect typical modern luxury car complexities, checking service history for unresolved bulletins or recalls is recommended.

---

## Engine

### 3.0L Twin-Turbocharged V6

The standard 3.0-liter twin-turbocharged V6 engine produces around 400 horsepower and 415 lb-ft of torque, offering brisk acceleration. It provides strong everyday drivability, though owners should monitor for occasional powertrain software or sensor issues.

**Engine Stalling and Low Oil Pressure**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have reported unexpected engine hesitation, hiccupping during acceleration, followed by sudden low oil pressure and low battery warning lights.
- **Solution:** Have a certified technician diagnose diagnostic trouble codes (DTCs), check oil levels, and ensure all relevant powertrain software updates have been applied.

### 3.0L Twin-Turbocharged V6 Plug-In Hybrid

The Grand Touring plug-in hybrid powertrain pairs the 3.0L V6 with an electric motor to generate a combined 494 horsepower and 630 lb-ft of torque. It delivers seamless electric-assist power, though its complex electrical systems require thorough inspection when buying used.

## Transmission

### 10-Speed SelectShift Automatic

The 10-speed automatic transmission routes power smoothly to the rear wheels or intelligent all-wheel-drive system. It is generally responsive, though prospective buyers should test-shift through all gears to ensure smooth engagement.

## Electrical

### Back Over Prevention / Camera System

Driver assistance and camera features provide modern convenience but rely heavily on stable software and clear sensors.

**Rear-View Camera Glitches and Image Distortion**
- **Symptoms:** Owners note that the backup camera screen can freeze, distort, or flip the image to display the front view instead of the rear during operation.
- **Solution:** Verify if the vehicle has received the necessary manufacturer camera software updates or hardware replacements.

### Infotainment and 360-Degree Camera

The electrical architecture supports numerous luxury features, though software bugs can occasionally disrupt multimedia displays and camera systems.

**Center Screen Blackout and Camera Failures**
- **Symptoms:** The main infotainment display can go completely black while driving or fail to function properly when a smartphone is plugged in.
- **Solution:** Perform a system reboot or consult a dealership for software patches addressing persistent display interface bugs.

## Interior

### Cabin Trim and Seating

The interior offers upscale materials and spacious seating layouts, though physical trim attachments should be verified during a pre-purchase inspection.

**Interior Trim Seating and Glass Incidents**
- **Symptoms:** Rare reports include loose passenger grab handles or issues with third-row seat adjustment mechanisms.
- **Solution:** Inspect interior trim components, mounting points, and seat adjustment hardware for secure installation.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V953000: Seat Belts:Front:Warning Light/Devices](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lincoln&model=Aviator&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 208, "Occupant Crash Protection." An audible warning chime that does not properly alert of an unbelted seat belt can increase the risk of injury during a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the audio control module software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V022000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lincoln&model=Aviator&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** Loss of the rearview camera image can reduce the driver's rear visibility, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the image processing module (IPMB) software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V070000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lincoln&model=Aviator&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** The transmission may contain a loose bolt which could prevent the transmission from engaging the park gear, although the gear shifter position may indicate that the vehicle has been shifted to "PARK." The inability to secure the vehicle in the park position can result in a rollaway, increasing the risk of a crash or injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and, if necessary, replace the transmissions, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V342000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lincoln&model=Aviator&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** Loss of the rearview camera image can reduce the driver's rear visibility, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the 360-degree camera, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V598000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lincoln&model=Aviator&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** The rearview camera, or 360-degree view camera if equipped, may not display a rearview image when the vehicle is placed in reverse. Loss of the rearview camera image can reduce the driver's rear visibility, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the rearview camera, and update the software and wiring as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V368000: Back Over Prevention:Display Function](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lincoln&model=Aviator&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** A flickering or distorted rearview camera image can reduce the driver's rear visibility, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will install shielding on the accessory protocol image module (APIM), free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V852000: Equipment:Other:Labels](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lincoln&model=Aviator&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirement Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 208, "Occupant Crash Protection." Without a label that shows the proper restraint usage guidelines, the occupants may have an increased risk of injury in the event of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 25V159000: Back Over Prevention:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lincoln&model=Aviator&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** Loss of the rearview camera image can reduce the driver's rear visibility, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the Image Processing Module software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2023 Lincoln Aviator is a sophisticated luxury SUV offering robust power and refined comfort. While most mechanical functions are solid, buyers should carefully inspect electrical systems, infotainment displays, and camera operations. Verifying that all open safety campaigns and software updates have been completed will help ensure a dependable ownership experience.
