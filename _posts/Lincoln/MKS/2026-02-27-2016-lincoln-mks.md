# 2016 Lincoln MKS Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2016 Lincoln MKS serves as a full-size luxury sedan offering generous cabin space and comfortable highway cruising. Buyers exploring the used market should review available owner reports covering electrical anomalies and powertrain responsiveness. While these owner-reported incidents offer insight into potential problem areas, inspecting a prospective vehicle thoroughly remains a vital step.

---

## Engine

### 3.7L Ti-VCT V6

The standard 3.7-liter naturally aspirated V6 engine produces approximately 305 horsepower and 280 lb-ft of torque. It provides reliable daily performance and decent efficiency for a large luxury sedan.

### 3.5L EcoBoost Twin-Turbocharged V6

The available 3.5-liter twin-turbocharged EcoBoost V6 generates roughly 365 horsepower and 350 lb-ft of torque, paired with standard all-wheel drive. It offers strong acceleration, though turbocharged components require attentive maintenance.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed SelectShift Automatic

The 6-speed automatic transmission features manual-shift capability and routes power smoothly to either the front wheels or all four wheels. Regular fluid changes help maintain long-term shifting quality.

## Electrical

### Electrical System

Owner reports indicate occasional electrical and throttle control hiccups that can lead to unexpected engine stalls or warning lights.

**Engine Stalling and Power Loss**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have reported instances where the vehicle unexpectedly loses power or cuts off completely, requiring several minutes before it will restart.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified technician diagnose the electrical system, battery connections, and throttle mechanism for intermittent faults.

## Safety

### Forward Collision Avoidance & Climate Controls

Safety and convenience electronics generally function well, though isolated owner complaints highlight glitchy collision warnings and HVAC control modules.

**Blower Motor and Forward Warning Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have experienced HVAC blower motor resistor issues causing erratic fan speeds and smoke, alongside unprompted forward collision warnings.
- **Solution:** Inspect and replace faulty blower motor resistor modules and have the collision avoidance sensors recalibrated if false warnings persist.

## Summary Verdict

The 2016 Lincoln MKS delivers a quiet, spacious ride with capable powertrain choices. Prospective buyers should carefully evaluate vehicle history, test all electronic features, and check for any signs of electrical or throttle irregularities during a pre-purchase inspection.
