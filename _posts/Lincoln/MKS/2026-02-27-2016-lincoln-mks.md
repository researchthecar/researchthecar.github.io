# 2016 Lincoln MKS

### Overview
The 2016 Lincoln MKS, a full-size luxury sedan, aimed to blend American comfort with a premium driving experience. It offered a choice between two potent EcoBoost engines, paired with a capable automatic transmission. While generally considered a solid vehicle, like any model, it has specific areas where owners have reported issues. This analysis focuses on documented technical reliability concerns, excluding routine maintenance and normal wear and tear, to provide a clear picture of potential long-term ownership considerations for the 2016 MKS.

---


## Engine

### 3.7L V6
**Cooling System Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Coolant loss, overheating, visible puddles under the vehicle.
- **Solution:** Replacement of leaking hoses, water pump, or radiator.
- **Cost:** $300 - $700

**Oil Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Burning oil smell, oil spots under the vehicle, low oil level.
- **Solution:** Replacement of valve cover gaskets, oil pan gasket, or crankshaft seals.
- **Cost:** $250 - $600


### 3.5L EcoBoost V6
**Turbocharger Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of power, whistling noises from the engine bay, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of turbocharger assemblies.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1500

**Intake Manifold Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Rough idle, poor engine performance, check engine light with lean codes.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the intake manifold.
- **Cost:** $400 - $700


## Transmission

### 6-Speed SelectShift Automatic
**Hesitation or Shuddering**
- **Symptoms:** Jerky shifts, delayed engagement, shuddering during acceleration.
- **Solution:** Transmission fluid flush and filter replacement, or internal transmission repair/rebuild.
- **Cost:** $400 - $1200

## Electrical

### General Electrical System
**Infotainment System Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Screen freezing, unresponsiveness, Bluetooth connectivity issues.
- **Solution:** Software updates, or replacement of the infotainment module.
- **Cost:** $300 - $900

**Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Difficulty starting, dead battery, electrical components malfunctioning.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis of parasitic draw, replacement of faulty modules or battery.
- **Cost:** $200 - $500

### Summary Verdict
The 2016 Lincoln MKS presents a generally reliable profile, with its powertrain components being the most common source of reported issues. The 3.7L V6 and 3.5L EcoBoost V6 engines can experience cooling system leaks, oil leaks, and turbocharger problems respectively, with associated repair costs varying. Transmission issues, primarily related to shifting quality, are also noted. Electrical gremlins, particularly with the infotainment system and occasional battery drain, are present. Interior components like seat actuators can also fail. However, the suspension and exterior elements appear to be robust. Overall, while not immune to problems, the MKS offers a decent ownership experience if potential buyers are aware of and prepared for these specific technical concerns.
