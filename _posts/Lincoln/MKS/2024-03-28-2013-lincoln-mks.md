**Introduction to the 2013 Lincoln MKS**

The 2013 Lincoln MKS is a full-size luxury sedan known for its spacious interior, powerful engine, and luxurious amenities. It comes equipped with a 3.7-liter V6 engine generating 304 horsepower and 279 lb-ft of torque, paired with a 6-speed automatic transmission. Buyers can also opt for the 3.5-liter EcoBoost V6 engine that produces 365 horsepower and 350 lb-ft of torque, mated to a 6-speed automatic transmission. Despite its impressive qualities, some owners have reported experiencing specific issues with the MKS.

**Engine**

**3.7-Liter V6 Engine**

* **Valve Cover Gasket Leak**
    * Description/Symptoms: Oil leakage from the valve cover gasket, causing a burning oil smell in the engine bay.
    * Solution: Replacement of the valve cover gasket.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $250 - $400+
    * Recalls: None Found

**3.5-Liter EcoBoost V6 Engine**

* **Turbocharger Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, decreased fuel economy, and turbocharger-related noises.
    * Solution: Replacement of the turbocharger assembly.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**6-Speed Automatic Transmission**

* **Shifting Hesitations**
    * Description/Symptoms: Transmission delays in shifting gears, resulting in a rough and jerky ride.
    * Solution: Software updates or transmission fluid replacement.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Strut Mount Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Noise and vibration from the front suspension, reduced steering response.
    * Solution: Replacement of the strut mounts.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Alternator Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Loss of electrical power, dimming headlights, or battery discharge.
    * Solution: Replacement of the alternator.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Headlight Condensation**
    * Description/Symptoms: Moisture buildup inside the headlight housings, leading to fogging and reduced visibility.
    * Solution: Replacement or resealing of the headlight housings.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Sunroof Drainage Issue**
    * Description/Symptoms: Leaking sunroof when it rains, causing water to drip into the interior.
    * Solution: Cleaning or replacement of the sunroof drain tubes.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2013 Lincoln MKS is a solid luxury sedan, but it has experienced some specific issues. The valve cover gasket leak and turbocharger failure in the engine can be serious and expensive repairs. Transmission shifting hesitations can be annoying but are typically less costly to resolve. Suspension issues, electrical issues, and exterior problems are also reported but are generally less severe. Interior issues, such as sunroof leakage, are relatively minor but can still be bothersome. Overall, these issues can vary in severity and cost, so potential buyers should carefully consider the repairs that may be necessary.
