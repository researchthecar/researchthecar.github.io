**Introduction to the 2013 Lincoln MKZ**

The 2013 Lincoln MKZ is a midsize luxury sedan that offers a comfortable and refined ride. It is available with a variety of engine and transmission options, including a 3.7-liter V6 with a six-speed automatic transmission, a 3.5-liter V6 with a six-speed automatic transmission, and a 2.0-liter four-cylinder with a continuously variable automatic transmission (CVT). The MKZ is praised for its spacious interior, impressive fuel economy, and advanced technology features. However, some owners have reported various issues with the vehicle.

**Engine**

**3.7-liter V6**

* **Cam Phaser Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Rough idle, loss of power, check engine light
    * Solution: Replace cam phasers
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1000 - $2000+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Intake Manifold Gasket Leak:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaks, rough idle, reduced engine power
    * Solution: Replace intake manifold gasket
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**3.5-liter V6**

* **Turbocharger Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Reduced engine power, increased oil consumption, whistling noise
    * Solution: Replace turbocharger
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2000 - $3000+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Timing Chain Rattle:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Rattling noise on startup, reduced engine power
    * Solution: Replace timing chain and tensioners
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1000 - $2000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**2.0-liter Four-Cylinder**

* **Fuel Injector Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Engine misfire, rough idle, reduced fuel economy
    * Solution: Replace fuel injectors
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**Six-Speed Automatic**

* **Torque Converter Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Shuddering, slipping, loss of power
    * Solution: Replace torque converter
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1500 - $2500+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Valve Body Solenoid Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Shifting problems, delayed engagement, harsh shifts
    * Solution: Replace valve body solenoid
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Continuously Variable Automatic (CVT)**

* **CVT Belt Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Slipping, jerking, loss of power
    * Solution: Replace CVT belt
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2000 - $3000+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **CVT Control Module Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Transmission malfunction codes, shifting problems
    * Solution: Replace CVT control module
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1000 - $2000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Control Arm Bushing Wear:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Clunking noises, uneven tire wear
    * Solution: Replace control arm bushings
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drainage:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Dead battery, difficulty starting
    * Solution: Inspect and repair electrical system for parasitic draw
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **HVAC Malfunction:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Inoperative climate control, no heat or AC
    * Solution: Repair or replace HVAC components
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on affected parts
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Headlight Condensation:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Moisture inside headlight lenses
    * Solution: Replace headlight assembly
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Power Window Malfunction:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Inoperative power windows, slow or noisy operation
    * Solution: Repair or replace window motor or switch
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Navigation System Crash:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Navigation system freezes or crashes
    * Solution: Software update
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: Free at dealership
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2013 Lincoln MKZ has received praise for its high-quality interior, impressive fuel economy, and advanced technology. However, some owners have reported issues with the engine, transmission, suspension, electrical system, and interior components. While most of the issues are relatively minor and inexpensive to fix, some, such as engine or transmission failures, can be costly. Overall, the severity and cost of the issues vary depending on the specific problem encountered.
