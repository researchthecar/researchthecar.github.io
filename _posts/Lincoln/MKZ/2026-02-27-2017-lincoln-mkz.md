# 2017 Lincoln MKZ Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2017 Lincoln MKZ offers a blend of luxury, comfort, and distinct powertrain options, making it an appealing choice on the used market. However, buyers should approach with a degree of caution. Review data indicates notable recurring problem areas, particularly concerning engine cooling and braking reliability, which require careful inspection prior to purchase.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L EcoBoost Turbocharged I4

The 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine produces around 245 horsepower and provides a balance of efficiency and punchy mid-range acceleration. While generally smooth, this option carries documented risks of coolant intrusion and related head gasket or cylinder concerns that buyers should investigate closely.

**Coolant Intrusion / Coolant Leak**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of coolant, rough running, engine misfires during startup, shaking, and illuminated check engine lights.
- **Solution:** Diagnostic testing to confirm coolant intrusion into the cylinders; often requires extensive engine repair or a complete engine replacement.

### 3.0L Twin-Turbocharged V6

The high-output 3.0-liter twin-turbo V6 delivers up to 400 horsepower primarily paired with all-wheel drive, offering brisk performance and a refined driving dynamic. It adds significant power over the base four-cylinder, though complex turbo plumbing requires diligent maintenance history tracking.

### 2.0L I4 Hybrid

The 2.0-liter Atkinson-cycle hybrid powertrain pairs an electric motor with a four-cylinder engine to maximize fuel economy around town. It is a popular choice for commuters seeking a softer, efficiency-focused luxury cruiser.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed SelectShift Automatic

The traditional 6-speed automatic transmission is paired with various engine options across front- and all-wheel-drive layouts. While shifts are generally smooth, owners have reported occasional torque converter wear and gear engagement issues.

**Power Train / Transmission Slippage**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle entering limp mode, failure to shift into higher gears, unusual vibrations, rumbling noises, or transmission slipping.
- **Solution:** Inspection of the transmission assembly, torque converter, and powertrain control module; may require component rebuild or replacement.

## Brakes

### Hydraulic Brake System

The foundation braking system provides adequate stopping power for this midsize luxury sedan. However, sudden hydraulic line leaks and spongy pedal travel are serious, documented failure points that warrant immediate pre-purchase evaluation.

**Brake Pedal Failure and Fluid Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Brake warning lights illuminating, brake pedal sinking straight to the floor, sudden loss of brake fluid, and drastically reduced stopping capability.
- **Solution:** Inspection of hydraulic brake lines, master cylinder, and related components, followed by bleeding the system and replacing failed parts.

## Electrical

### Infotainment and Navigation (SYNC / APIM)

The cabin technology relies heavily on Ford's SYNC interface and associated electronic control modules. Glitches and unresponsive display units are common user complaints.

**Navigation and Display Freezing**
- **Symptoms:** GPS location offsetting by miles, backup camera display freezing entirely, or the infotainment screen locking up for extended periods.
- **Solution:** Perform software updates, reset the system, or replace the accessory protocol interface module (APIM) if hardware failure persists.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V390000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission:Torque Converter](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lincoln&model=MKZ&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** If the torque converter weld studs fail, the torque converter will not be connected to the engine flexplate and the vehicle will lose the ability to move, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will replace the torque converters, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V167000: Steering: Steering Wheel/Handle Bar](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lincoln&model=MKZ&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** A steering wheel detachment can cause a loss of steering control, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will replace the steering wheel bolt with a longer bolt, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V427000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission:Torque Converter](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lincoln&model=MKZ&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** If the torque converter weld studs fail, the torque converter will not be connected to the engine flexplate and the vehicle will lose the ability to move, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Ford or Lincoln will notify owners, and dealers will replace the torque converters, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V162000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Foundation Components:Hoses, Lines/Piping, And Fittings](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lincoln&model=MKZ&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** A brake fluid leak will increase brake pedal travel and extend the distance needed to stop the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the front brake hoses, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2017 Lincoln MKZ combines upscale styling with comfortable road manners, but potential buyers need to exercise caution. Pay close attention to service records for any signs of engine coolant intrusion or transmission shifting anomalies, and ensure a thorough inspection of the hydraulic brakes is performed prior to completing a purchase.
