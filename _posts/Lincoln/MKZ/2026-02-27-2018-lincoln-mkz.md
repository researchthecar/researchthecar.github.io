# 2018 Lincoln MKZ Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2018 Lincoln MKZ, prospective buyers will find a stylish midsize luxury sedan offering a comfortable ride and upscale cabin features. However, reviewing owner-reported reliability data reveals important mechanical areas to inspect closely before purchasing, particularly concerning powertrain integrity and certain accessory systems.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged 4-Cylinder

This standard 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine produces approximately 245 horsepower and delivers strong low-end torque for daily driving. While generally peppy and efficient, buyers should carefully check service history and watch for potential cooling system issues.

**Coolant Intrusion**
- **Symptoms:** Illuminated check engine light, engine overheating, white smoke from the exhaust, or misfires on startup leading to reduced engine power.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified mechanic inspect the cooling system and cylinders for leakage; severely affected engines may require a complete engine replacement.

### 3.0L Twin-Turbo V6

The available 3.0-liter twin-turbocharged V6 pumps out up to 400 horsepower when paired with all-wheel drive, offering brisk acceleration and a refined highway cruising character. No major recurring mechanical defect clusters were highlighted for this specific engine option in available owner data.

### 2.0L Hybrid

The 2.0-liter hybrid powertrain pairs a four-cylinder gas engine with an electric motor to maximize fuel economy for city-focused commuters. It provides smooth transitions and reduced trips to the fuel pump with standard front-wheel drive.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed SelectShift Automatic

A conventional 6-speed automatic transmission handles power delivery for the standard turbo engine. Buyers should verify smooth shifting behavior and check for any signs of fluid leaks during a pre-purchase inspection.

**Fluid Leaks and Internal Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Dark or burning transmission fluid, sudden fluid loss, diagnostic trouble codes like P0741 or P1744, or slipping gears.
- **Solution:** Inspect transmission fluid condition and level, check axle seals, and repair or replace components showing internal wear.

### CVT Automatic (Hybrid)

The electronic continuously variable transmission utilized in hybrid variants offers seamless acceleration optimized for fuel efficiency. It generally operates quietly without traditional shift shock.

## Exterior

### Panoramic Moonroof

The large panoramic glass roof enhances cabin light and openness but requires careful inspection of the glass surface and seals.

**Unexpected Glass Shattering**
- **Symptoms:** Loud explosion sound followed by sudden shattering of the panoramic glass roof panel without impact from debris.
- **Solution:** Inspect the moonroof assembly for stress cracks and verify if glass replacement coverage applies.

### Exterior Lighting

Exterior lighting systems provide good illumination but can occasionally suffer from weather seal degradation leading to moisture entry.

**Rear LED Light Strip Water Intrusion**
- **Symptoms:** Moisture accumulation inside the rear liftgate LED strip causing an electrical short and keeping reverse lights illuminated constantly.
- **Solution:** Seal or replace the rear light assembly to restore proper electrical operation and pass state inspections.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V167000: Steering: Steering Wheel/Handle Bar](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lincoln&model=MKZ&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** A steering wheel detachment can cause a loss of steering control, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will replace the steering wheel bolt with a longer bolt, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V162000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Foundation Components:Hoses, Lines/Piping, And Fittings](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lincoln&model=MKZ&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** A brake fluid leak will increase brake pedal travel and extend the distance needed to stop the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the front brake hoses, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2018 Lincoln MKZ is a compelling choice for used luxury sedan buyers wanting comfortable amenities and strong engine options, particularly the high-output V6. However, prospective owners must carefully verify the service history for coolant intrusion symptoms on the 2.0L engine and inspect the transmission and exterior seals. A thorough pre-purchase inspection by a trusted mechanic is strongly advised.
