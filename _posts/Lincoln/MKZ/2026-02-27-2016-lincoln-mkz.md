# 2016 Lincoln MKZ Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2016 Lincoln MKZ offers a distinctive blend of luxury, comfort, and multiple powertrain choices, including a standard EcoBoost engine and a hybrid variant. While many owners enjoy its smooth ride and high-end feature set, used car buyers should approach certain model years with care. Common trouble spots include potential engine coolant intrusion on the 2.0-liter turbocharged engine, power steering assist failures, and recurring glitches with the rearview camera system.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L EcoBoost Turbocharged 4-Cylinder

This 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine produces around 240 horsepower, offering a balance of efficiency and punchy performance. However, prospective buyers should thoroughly inspect service records for cooling system health, as coolant intrusion into the cylinders has been reported.

**Coolant Intrusion**
- **Symptoms:** The engine runs rough upon startup, loses coolant slowly, and may eventually trigger an overheating warning with smoke from the engine compartment.
- **Solution:** Requires professional engine diagnosis and, in severe cases, engine replacement.
- **Cost:** Cost varies widely depending on repair scope, potentially requiring complete engine replacement.

### 3.7L V6

The naturally aspirated 3.7-liter V6 engine delivers roughly 300 horsepower for drivers seeking smoother, traditional V6 power and confident acceleration. It generally avoids the specific coolant intrusion concerns found in the smaller turbo engine.

### 2.0L Hybrid 4-Cylinder

Paired with an electric motor to generate a combined output of around 188 horsepower, this hybrid powertrain prioritizes fuel economy and smooth city driving. It represents a solid choice for buyers focused on efficiency.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 6-speed automatic transmission works across gas-powered trims to deliver smooth gear changes. No widespread, severe transmission-specific failure patterns dominate owner complaints for this model year.

### CVT (Hybrid)

The continuously variable transmission mated to the hybrid powertrain manages power delivery efficiently. It generally maintains a reliable reputation provided routine maintenance is followed.

## Suspension

### Wheels and Lug Nuts

The suspension provides a plush, comfortable highway ride, but owners should watch for minor hardware wear like swollen lug nuts common to Ford/Lincoln vehicles of this era.

**Swollen Lug Nuts**
- **Symptoms:** Factory-capped lug nuts swell over time, making it difficult or impossible to fit a standard tire iron over them during a roadside flat tire change.
- **Solution:** Replace factory lug nuts with solid, aftermarket alternatives.
- **Cost:** Approximately $166 for replacement parts and labor at a service center.

## Brakes

### Hydraulic Brake System

While standard stopping performance is adequate when maintained, occasional hydraulic or mechanical coupler issues can lead to unexpected brake pedal resistance.

**Brake Pedal Stiffness and Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The brake pedal becomes stiff and the vehicle fails to stop properly, or brake lights remain illuminated while the car is parked.
- **Solution:** Inspect hydraulic lines, master cylinder, and related mechanical linkages for binding or failure.
- **Cost:** Varies by specific component replaced.

## Steering

### Electric Power Steering System

The electronic power assist setup provides light, adjustable steering feel under normal conditions, but sudden losses of assist require expensive component replacements.

**Loss of Power Steering Assist**
- **Symptoms:** The steering wheel becomes heavy and extremely difficult to turn, particularly at low speeds, or the steering gear motor detaches from the housing.
- **Solution:** Replace the faulty electric steering gear motor or complete steering assembly.
- **Cost:** Approximately $2,000 for complete steering repair.

## Electrical

### Backup Camera and Display

In-cabin electronics generally function well, though the rearview camera system is prone to intermittent black screens and reversed video feeds.

**Rearview Camera Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** The infotainment screen displays a blank image, shows an error message, or presents an inverted (reversed) rearview image.
- **Solution:** Replace the failing backup camera unit or update/reset the display module.
- **Cost:** Varies depending on whether dealer programming or full camera replacement is required.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V632000: Steering:Electric Power Assist System](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lincoln&model=MKZ&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** If the bolts corrode, the steering gear motor may detach from the gear housing resulting in a loss of power steering assist, requiring a greater steering effort, especially at lower speeds, and increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will replace the steering gear motor bolts and apply a wax sealer. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V167000: Steering: Steering Wheel/Handle Bar](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lincoln&model=MKZ&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** A steering wheel detachment can cause a loss of steering control, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will replace the steering wheel bolt with a longer bolt, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 15V618000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Storage](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lincoln&model=MKZ&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** As a result, the fuel tanks may crack in the event of a crash and thus the vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 301, "Fuel System Integrity." A fuel tank that cracks may leak fuel, increasing the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will replace the fuel tank, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V177000: Latches/Locks/Linkages:Doors:Latch](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lincoln&model=MKZ&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** A door that is not securely latched could open while the vehicle is in motion, increasing the risk of injury to a vehicle occupant.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and Ford or Lincoln dealers will replace the side door latches, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2016 Lincoln MKZ delivers attractive styling and a comfortable ride, but used car shoppers should perform a rigorous inspection before purchasing. Prioritize models with complete service histories, paying special attention to the health of the 2.0-liter EcoBoost engine to rule out coolant intrusion. Additionally, verify that the power steering operates smoothly and test all electronic features, particularly the rearview camera, to avoid costly out-of-pocket surprises.
