# 2020 Lincoln MKZ Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

Shopping for a used 2020 Lincoln MKZ means looking at a stylish, comfortable midsize luxury sedan that represents the final model year for the nameplate. Buyers will find a refined interior and a smooth ride, but as with any used vehicle, it pays to understand potential trouble spots reported by owners. While owner reports do not prove inherent factory defects, reviewing specific pain points can help you inspect a used model more thoroughly before finalizing your purchase.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged I4

This 2.0-liter turbocharged inline-four engine produces approximately 245 horsepower and 275 lb-ft of torque, offering a balanced mix of efficiency and everyday performance. It generally proves to be a reliable choice for daily commuting when properly maintained.

### 3.0L Twin-Turbocharged V6

The potent 3.0-liter twin-turbo V6 generates up to 400 horsepower and 400 lb-ft of torque, delivering robust acceleration especially when paired with all-wheel drive. Buyers should ensure thorough maintenance records, as high-output turbocharged engines demand diligent fluid changes.

### 2.0L Hybrid I4

This hybrid powertrain pairs a 2.0-liter four-cylinder engine with an electric motor to maximize fuel economy for city driving. It represents a dependable and efficient option for commuters prioritizing lower fuel costs.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

This 6-speed automatic transmission is paired with the hybrid powertrain, offering smooth and predictable gear changes. It has a generally solid reputation for longevity under normal driving conditions.

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

This 8-speed automatic transmission works alongside the 2.0-liter turbo and 3.0-liter twin-turbo engines to provide responsive shifting and highway efficiency. Buyers should test drive the vehicle to ensure smooth engagement across all gears.

## Steering

### Steering Column and Mechanism

The power steering system normally provides effortless handling, though rare owner reports highlight severe steering lockup issues.

**Steering Lockup**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may experience the steering wheel and wheels becoming completely stuck while driving, creating a dangerous loss of vehicle control.
- **Solution:** Have the steering assembly, electronic power steering motor, and linkage thoroughly inspected by a qualified technician.

## Exterior

### Panoramic Moonroof

The exterior features sleek styling and an available panoramic glass roof, which adds an open-air feel but requires careful inspection.

**Moonroof Shattering**
- **Symptoms:** The panoramic glass roof may spontaneously fracture or shatter while driving at highway speeds, producing a loud noise resembling a gunshot.
- **Solution:** Inspect the panoramic glass for any preexisting stress cracks or chips, and verify proper roof alignment.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V442000: Back Over Prevention:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lincoln&model=MKZ&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** A software error may cause the rearview camera to display a blank image, or the image may remain on the display after the backing event has ended. Both of these situations can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the rearview camera software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2020 Lincoln MKZ offers a compelling blend of luxury, comfort, and distinct powertrain choices, ranging from efficient hybrids to high-output twin-turbo V6 models. While isolated owner complaints highlight rare concerns like steering lockup and spontaneous glass breakage, these do not represent widespread systemic failures. Prospective buyers should perform a detailed pre-purchase inspection focusing on the steering, electronics, and panoramic glass to ensure a worry-free ownership experience.
