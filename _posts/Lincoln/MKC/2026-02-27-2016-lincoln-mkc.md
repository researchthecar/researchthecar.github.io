# 2016 Lincoln MKC Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2016 Lincoln MKC offers a luxury compact crossover experience with competitive tech and comfortable ride quality. However, prospective buyers should carefully review potential mechanical and electrical concerns, particularly regarding certain engine configurations and recurring minor system faults.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L EcoBoost I4

The 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine produces around 240 horsepower and provides a blend of pep and efficiency, but it is known for critical coolant intrusion issues in some units.

**Coolant Intrusion**
- **Symptoms:** Engine misfires, white smoke, check engine light, and low coolant levels due to fluid leaking into the cylinders.
- **Solution:** Diagnostic checks at a dealership often confirm the failure, which typically requires a complete long block engine replacement.
- **Cost:** $176.55

### 2.3L EcoBoost I4

The larger 2.3-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine ups performance to roughly 285 horsepower, delivering stronger acceleration for highway passing and towing capability.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed SelectShift Automatic

The 6-speed automatic transmission is paired with front-wheel or all-wheel drive systems, offering smooth everyday shifts though occasional harsh engagement has been reported by owners.

**Harsh Shifting and Lurching**
- **Symptoms:** Occasional hard shifting at low speeds or sudden harsh grabbing and lunging during acceleration and deceleration.
- **Solution:** Inspect transmission control modules and software calibration, though dealerships may struggle to duplicate intermittent faults.

### Shift Mechanism

**Shifter or Park Defect**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle fails to shift out of park, or shifting into drive inadvertently selects neutral instead.
- **Solution:** Inspect the shifter linkage and bushings; replace worn components to restore proper gear engagement.

## Exterior

### Exterior Lighting

**Headlight Assembly Security**
- **Symptoms:** Headlight assemblies or mounting components loosen due to failing clips and springs.
- **Solution:** Replace damaged retaining clips and secure or replace the headlight assembly housing.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V183000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Engine](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lincoln&model=MKC&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** Overheating of the engine block increase the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will replace the engine block heater, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V378000: Engine](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lincoln&model=MKC&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** The battery monitor sensor may short-circuit and overheat, causing a fire in the engine compartment while parked or driving. An engine compartment fire while parked or driving can increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will add an in-line fuse to the battery monitor sensor power circuit, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V572000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lincoln&model=MKC&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** A rearview camera that displays an inverted, distorted, or blank image can reduce the driver’s view behind the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the rearview camera as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V685000: Equipment:Electrical:Engine Block Heater](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lincoln&model=MKC&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** The engine block heater may crack and develop a coolant leak, causing it to short circuit when the block heater is plugged in. An electrical short circuit can increase the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the block heater, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V011000: Equipment:Electrical:Engine Block Heater](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lincoln&model=MKC&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** The engine block heater may crack and develop a coolant leak, causing it to short circuit when the block heater is plugged in. An electrical short circuit can increase the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the block heater, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

When shopping for a used model, verify whether engine cooling system repairs have been performed or if the vehicle has been thoroughly inspected for coolant intrusion. A comprehensive pre-purchase inspection focusing on the powertrain and electronics will help mitigate unexpected out-of-pocket expenses.
