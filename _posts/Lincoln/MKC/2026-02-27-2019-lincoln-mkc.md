# 2019 Lincoln MKC Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2019 Lincoln MKC offers a refined luxury compact SUV experience with comfortable seating, a quiet cabin, and composed road manners. However, prospective buyers should approach this model year with careful consideration due to significant recurring engine and electrical concerns reported by owners. While it delivers strong performance and upscale features, mechanical due diligence is strongly recommended prior to purchase.

---

## Engine

### 1.5L EcoBoost I4

A turbocharged 1.5-liter four-cylinder engine producing around 181 horsepower and 185 lb-ft of torque. It provides adequate efficiency for daily commuting, though it is less common than the larger displacement option.

### 2.0L EcoBoost I4

A turbocharged 2.0-liter four-cylinder engine generating approximately 245 horsepower and 275 lb-ft of torque. It delivers brisk acceleration and confident highway passing, but is notably associated with serious coolant intrusion issues requiring careful inspection.

**Coolant Intrusion**
- **Symptoms:** Engine misfires, rough starting, loss of power, and low coolant levels without an obvious external leak, often accompanied by a check engine light.
- **Solution:** Requires extensive engine repairs or a complete engine block replacement.

**Coolant Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Unexplained loss of engine coolant, pooling fluid under the vehicle, and potential engine overheating.
- **Solution:** Inspect cooling system components, hoses, and connections; replace damaged parts as necessary.

### 2.3L EcoBoost I4

A high-output turbocharged 2.3-liter four-cylinder engine pushing roughly 285 horsepower and 305 lb-ft of torque. It provides lively performance and robust towing capability for its class.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed SelectShift Automatic Transmission

A traditional 6-speed automatic transmission paired with either front-wheel or all-wheel drive. It offers smooth gear shifts and generally dependable operation when properly maintained.

## Suspension

### Wheels and Hubs

Suspension and wheel components responsible for ride comfort, load support, and smooth rotational movement.

**Wheel Bearing Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Loud humming, growling, or 'woomp' noises originating from the wheel area while driving, which can worsen with vehicle speed.
- **Solution:** Replace the worn wheel bearing and hub assembly.

**Swelling Lug Nuts**
- **Symptoms:** Outer caps of factory lug nuts swell or distort, making them difficult or impossible to remove with standard roadside tire irons.
- **Solution:** Replace factory lug nuts with solid, aftermarket one-piece lug nuts.

## Steering

### Power Steering

Electric power-assist steering system designed to provide responsive handling at low and high speeds.

**Power Steering Assist Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Sudden loss of power steering functionality accompanied by a 'steering assist fault service required' alert on the dashboard.
- **Solution:** Diagnostic inspection of the electronic power steering assembly, often requiring replacement of the steering rack or motor.
- **Cost:** $5,700

## Electrical

### Electrical System & Backup Camera

Controls onboard electronics, safety systems, and display screens, with prominent owner complaints centering around rearview camera malfunctions.

**Backup Camera Distortion and Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Rearview camera displays a blank, black, blue, or distorted image, accompanied by a 'Camera Not Available' warning message.
- **Solution:** Replace faulty camera components or wiring harnesses as directed by manufacturer service campaigns.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V378000: Engine](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lincoln&model=MKC&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** The battery monitor sensor may short-circuit and overheat, causing a fire in the engine compartment while parked or driving. An engine compartment fire while parked or driving can increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will add an in-line fuse to the battery monitor sensor power circuit, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V572000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lincoln&model=MKC&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** A rearview camera that displays an inverted, distorted, or blank image can reduce the driver’s view behind the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the rearview camera as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V685000: Equipment:Electrical:Engine Block Heater](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lincoln&model=MKC&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** The engine block heater may crack and develop a coolant leak, causing it to short circuit when the block heater is plugged in. An electrical short circuit can increase the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the block heater, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2019 Lincoln MKC blends luxury and performance, but prospective buyers must exercise caution regarding potential powertrain and electrical snags. Coolant intrusion on the 2.0L EcoBoost engine is a severe issue to watch out for during pre-purchase inspections. Thoroughly verify the status of open recalls, test all electronic features, and obtain a comprehensive mechanical evaluation before finalizing your purchase.
