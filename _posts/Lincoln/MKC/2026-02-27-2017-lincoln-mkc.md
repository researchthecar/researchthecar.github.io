# 2017 Lincoln MKC Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2017 Lincoln MKC offers a quiet ride and an upscale cabin, blending luxury with everyday compact SUV utility. However, used car shoppers should approach with caution, as owner reports highlight significant powertrain concerns, notably related to engine coolant intrusion and transmission performance.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L EcoBoost Turbocharged I4

A 2.0-liter turbocharged inline-4 engine producing approximately 240 horsepower, offering a balance of efficiency and punchy power. Buyers should thoroughly inspect service records for any history of cooling system issues.

**Coolant Intrusion**
- **Symptoms:** Low coolant levels in the overflow tank, illuminated check engine light, and engine misfires (such as P0302).
- **Solution:** Requires professional diagnosis and potentially engine block or cylinder head replacement depending on severity.

**Coolant Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Low coolant warnings and fluid puddles under the vehicle.
- **Solution:** Inspect cooling system hoses, radiator, and gaskets; replace leaking components as needed.

### 2.3L EcoBoost Turbocharged I4

A larger 2.3-liter turbocharged inline-4 engine pushing around 285 horsepower, providing stronger acceleration and towing capability. Like its smaller sibling, it demands careful attention to cooling system health.

**Head Gasket and Coolant Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Overheating, white smoke from the exhaust, and coolant loss.
- **Solution:** Replace damaged head gaskets and check for warped cylinder heads.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed SelectShift Automatic Transmission

A conventional 6-speed automatic transmission paired with front-wheel or all-wheel drive. While generally smooth when healthy, some owners report premature slipping and torque converter issues.

**Transmission Slipping and Hard Shifts**
- **Symptoms:** Abnormal banging noises between gears, transmission slipping while driving at various speeds, and severe vibrations during acceleration.
- **Solution:** Inspect the torque converter and internal transmission components; rebuild or replace the transmission if mechanical wear is present.

## Electrical

### Backup Camera and Infotainment

Handles convenience features, displays, and driver assist camera systems. Glitches in the rear-view display are common complaints.

**Rear View Camera Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** The backup camera displays a blue screen, goes dark, shows 'camera unavailable' error messages, or displays a wavy/distorted image.
- **Solution:** Check camera wiring connections or replace the faulty camera module.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V378000: Engine](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lincoln&model=MKC&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** The battery monitor sensor may short-circuit and overheat, causing a fire in the engine compartment while parked or driving. An engine compartment fire while parked or driving can increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will add an in-line fuse to the battery monitor sensor power circuit, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V572000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lincoln&model=MKC&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** A rearview camera that displays an inverted, distorted, or blank image can reduce the driver’s view behind the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the rearview camera as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V685000: Equipment:Electrical:Engine Block Heater](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lincoln&model=MKC&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** The engine block heater may crack and develop a coolant leak, causing it to short circuit when the block heater is plugged in. An electrical short circuit can increase the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the block heater, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2017 Lincoln MKC presents an attractive package for used luxury SUV buyers, but major reported powertrain vulnerabilities—specifically engine coolant intrusion and transmission shifting anomalies—mean a pre-purchase inspection by a qualified mechanic is mandatory. Ensure all open recalls are completed and verify that the cooling system is free of leaks before purchasing.
