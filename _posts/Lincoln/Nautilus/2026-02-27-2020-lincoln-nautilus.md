# 2020 Lincoln Nautilus Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2020 Lincoln Nautilus, buyers will find a stylish and comfortable midsize luxury SUV equipped with respectable powertrain options. However, owner reports highlight notable areas of concern—particularly regarding transmission shift quality, potential powertrain longevity, and exterior lighting components. Prospective owners should carefully evaluate these systems during a test drive and review service records before finalizing a purchase.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged I4

The standard 2.0-liter turbocharged inline-four engine produces 250 horsepower and offers a balance of efficiency and daily drivability. While generally adequate for most drivers, some owners have reported hesitation and rough running behavior under light acceleration.

**Intermittent Acceleration Stutter**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may experience a slight jerk, hesitation, or stutter during light acceleration or at lower speeds, often without illuminating any diagnostic warning lights.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified mechanic inspect the ignition components, throttle body, and powertrain control module for intermittent faults.

### 2.7L Twin-Turbocharged V6

The available 2.7-liter twin-turbocharged V6 churns out an impressive 335 horsepower and 380 lb-ft of torque, delivering robust acceleration and confident highway passing. It provides a more refined, powerful driving experience for those prioritizing performance.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

Paired across the lineup, the 8-speed automatic transmission aims to optimize fuel economy and smooth power delivery. However, harsher shift quality and isolated failures in higher-mileage examples warrant careful pre-purchase inspection.

**Lower Gear Jerking and Hard Shifting**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may notice harsh engagement, stuttering, or pronounced jerking when shifting through lower gears, particularly in stop-and-go traffic.
- **Solution:** Check transmission fluid condition, update control modules, or consult a specialist for mechanical internal inspection if severe slipping occurs.
- **Cost:** $10,188

**Complete Transmission Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Sudden loss of drive capability or total transmission breakdown while driving at highway speeds, often requiring towing.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the transmission assembly.

## Exterior

### Exterior Lighting

Lighting systems generally perform well, though the center brake light assembly is prone to premature failure.

**High-Mount Brake Light Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The center high-mount stop lamp fails prematurely due to internal component faults, reducing rear visibility for trailing drivers.
- **Solution:** Replace the high-mount brake light assembly.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V575000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lincoln&model=Nautilus&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 111, "Rear Visibility." Reduced rearward visibility can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will replace the rearview camera, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V011000: Power Train:Driveline:Driveshaft](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lincoln&model=Nautilus&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** An inadequate amount of lubricant in the rear drive unit may cause seizure of the rear drive axle. If the rear drive unit seizes, there may be a loss of drive and/or a loss of vehicle control, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will check the rear drive unit lubricant level. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V414000: Seats:Front Assembly:Power Adjust](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lincoln&model=Nautilus&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** The seat cushion frame edge may contact the wire harness when the seat occupant uses the tilt function of the seat, potentially damaging one or more wires within the harness, which can result in either inadvertent deployment of the air bag or nondeployment of the air bag in the event of a crash. An air bag that deploys inadvertently increases the risk of a.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will install protective flocking tape onto the exposed edge of the 16-way seat cushion frames, and inspect the wire harness for damage, and repair or replace it as needed, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V442000: Back Over Prevention:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lincoln&model=Nautilus&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** A software error may cause the rearview camera to display a blank image, or the image may remain on the display after the backing event has ended. Both of these situations can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the rearview camera software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V550000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lincoln&model=Nautilus&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** Missing or loose bolts on the start stop accumulator endcap could result in a transmission fluid leak and may progress to a loss of transmission function. A transmission fluid leak in the presence of an ignition source may increase the risk of fire.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will replace the start/stop accumulator, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2020 Lincoln Nautilus delivers a quiet cabin, plush ride, and strong engine options, making it an attractive pre-owned luxury choice. Buyers should, however, pay close attention to transmission shift smoothness during a test drive and verify the operational status of exterior lights and electronic features. Securing an extended warranty or a thorough independent pre-purchase inspection can offer extra peace of mind.
