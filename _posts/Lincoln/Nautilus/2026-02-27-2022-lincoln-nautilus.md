# 2022 Lincoln Nautilus Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2022 Lincoln Nautilus offers a plush ride and refined cabin, making it an appealing option in the midsize luxury SUV segment. However, prospective buyers should review known owner complaints concerning powertrain operation and exterior body structure. Being informed about these specific areas will help you thoroughly evaluate a used model before purchasing.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged I4

This base engine produces roughly 250 horsepower and 275 lb-ft of torque, providing adequate everyday acceleration for daily commuting. It balances reasonable fuel economy with turbocharged power, though buyers should verify routine maintenance history.

### 2.7L Twin-Turbocharged V6

The larger V6 engine delivers a robust 335 horsepower and 380 lb-ft of torque for stronger highway passing power. It pairs with standard all-wheel drive, offering a refined and powerful driving dynamic for luxury buyers.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission (8F35)

The 8-speed automatic transmission manages power delivery across different trims, but owner reports indicate potential low-speed drivability concerns. Thoroughly test driving a used model at various speeds is recommended to check shift quality.

**Low-Speed Jerking and Shifting Hesitation**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers report that the vehicle may sputter, jerk, or hesitate at low speeds, typically between 10 and 60 mph. In some cases, component replacements like the torque converter have not fully resolved the shifting behavior.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified mechanic inspect transmission operation and check for any applicable manufacturer service updates or software calibrations.

## Exterior

### Door Hinge and Sheet Metal

Exterior body components are generally durable, though structural stress around specific high-wear areas like door hinges warrants physical inspection.

**Front Door Hinge Cracking**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have observed significant cracking in the sheet metal and check strap area around the front door hinges, which could pose structural integrity concerns if left unaddressed.
- **Solution:** Inspect the door hinge and check strap mounting areas for visible fractures and consult a dealer regarding applicable customer satisfaction programs.

---

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V635000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Engine:Hard Parts Internal/Mechanical](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lincoln&model=Nautilus&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** A loss of drive power increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will perform an engine cycle test and replace the engine as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V315000: Back Over Prevention:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lincoln&model=Nautilus&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** A software error may cause the rearview camera image to delay, freeze, or not display when the vehicle is in reverse. A frozen or missing rearview camera image can reduce the driver's view behind the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

When shopping for a used 2022 Lincoln Nautilus, pay close attention to low-speed transmission behavior during a test drive and thoroughly inspect the front door hinge areas for structural stress. Conducting a comprehensive pre-purchase inspection can help catch these reported issues early and ensure a more confident ownership experience.
