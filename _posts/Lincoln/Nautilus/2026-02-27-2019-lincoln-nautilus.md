# 2019 Lincoln Nautilus Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used luxury midsize crossover like the Lincoln Nautilus, prospective buyers will find a refined cabin and comfortable ride, but ownership history requires careful scrutiny. Powertrain durability and electronic stability represent key areas of concern based on owner reports submitted to safety authorities. Checking service records for prior transmission and engine work is essential before purchasing.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L EcoBoost Turbocharged I4

The 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine produces around 250 horsepower and offers adequate everyday efficiency. Buyers should inspect this engine closely for signs of coolant leaks and combustion chamber issues.

**Coolant Intrusion**
- **Symptoms:** Engine misfires, white smoke from the exhaust, and coolant loss due to fluid leaking into the cylinder bores.
- **Solution:** May require extensive engine repair or a complete engine replacement depending on cylinder wall damage.

**Engine Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle suddenly stalls or shuts down unexpectedly when coming to a stop or while operating at low speeds.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis by a qualified technician to address underlying fuel, electrical, or powertrain control module faults.

### 2.7L EcoBoost Twin-Turbocharged V6

The 2.7-liter twin-turbo V6 delivers a robust 335 horsepower for strong acceleration and highway passing power. It provides a refined driving feel but shares similar complex turbocharger and cooling architectures that require diligent maintenance.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 8-speed automatic transmission channels power smoothly under normal conditions, though some units develop noticeable drivability quirks. Verifying smooth gear shifts during a test drive is critical.

**Transmission Shudder and Harsh Shifting**
- **Symptoms:** Noticeable jerking, shuddering, or harsh engagement when accelerating through lower gears, particularly between 25 and 45 mph.
- **Solution:** Inspection by a transmission specialist; severe cases may require torque converter replacement or a full transmission rebuild.
- **Cost:** $250 diagnostic fee noted by owners; major repairs out of pocket can be substantial.

## Steering

### Power Steering System

The steering system delivers light, maneuverable handling at low speeds, though electronic adaptive assist faults can temporarily disrupt control.

**Adaptive Steering Fault**
- **Symptoms:** Sudden loss of power steering assist accompanied by warning chimes, or the steering wheel unexpectedly pulling to one side.
- **Solution:** Inspect steering control modules, wiring harnesses, and adaptive steering actuators for faults.

## Exterior

### Exterior Lighting and Accessories

Electrical architecture supports numerous luxury amenities, but intermittent glitches in lighting and dashboard displays require thorough checking.

**Headlight and Instrument Cluster Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** Headlights flickering or failing to operate correctly, alongside instrument panels becoming blank or unresponsive.
- **Solution:** Check battery health, ground connections, and lighting control modules for corrosion or failure.

## Safety

### Seat Belts

Safety restraint components generally perform their vital duty, but mechanical sticking in the seat belt retractors has been reported by owners.

**Seat Belt Retractor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Front or rear seat belts become stuck, fail to extend smoothly, or stop retracting properly.
- **Solution:** Replace the malfunctioning seat belt assembly.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V076000: Electrical System: Instrument Cluster/Panel](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lincoln&model=Nautilus&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** At vehicle start-up, the Instrument Panel Cluster Assembly (IPC) may not function, showing a blank display. Driving with an inoperative cluster can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Ford has notified owners, and dealers will reprogram the Instrument Panel Cluster Assembly (IPC), free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V031000: Lane Departure: Lane Keep: Steering Assist](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lincoln&model=Nautilus&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** The Lane Center Assist system may not adequately detect if a driver has their hands off the steering wheel for a prolonged period of time. If the system remains active despite the driver not having their hands on the wheel, the driver's inattention may increase the risk of crash.
- **What to do:** Ford has notified owners, and dealers will update the Power Steering Control Module software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V414000: Seats:Front Assembly:Power Adjust](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lincoln&model=Nautilus&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** The seat cushion frame edge may contact the wire harness when the seat occupant uses the tilt function of the seat, potentially damaging one or more wires within the harness, which can result in either inadvertent deployment of the air bag or nondeployment of the air bag in the event of a crash. An air bag that deploys inadvertently increases the risk of a.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will install protective flocking tape onto the exposed edge of the 16-way seat cushion frames, and inspect the wire harness for damage, and repair or replace it as needed, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V806000: Air Bags:Frontal](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lincoln&model=Nautilus&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** The plastic cover on the driver frontal air bag module may detach during air bag deployment. If the air bag cover separates during deployment, it can increase the risk of injury in the event of a crash.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will replace the driver air bag module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V442000: Back Over Prevention:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lincoln&model=Nautilus&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** A software error may cause the rearview camera to display a blank image, or the image may remain on the display after the backing event has ended. Both of these situations can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the rearview camera software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V550000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lincoln&model=Nautilus&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** Missing or loose bolts on the start stop accumulator endcap could result in a transmission fluid leak and may progress to a loss of transmission function. A transmission fluid leak in the presence of an ignition source may increase the risk of fire.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will replace the start/stop accumulator, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V372000: Electrical System: Instrument Cluster/Panel](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lincoln&model=Nautilus&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** At vehicle start-up, the Instrument Panel Cluster Assembly (IPC) may not function, showing a blank display. As a result, these vehicles fail to comply with Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 101, "Controls and Displays." A blank instrument cluster that fails to display critical safety information, such as vehicle speed or safety system.
- **What to do:** Dealers will reprogram the Instrument Panel Cluster Assembly (IPC) software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V377000: Lane Departure: Lane Keep: Steering Assist](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lincoln&model=Nautilus&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** The lane center assist system may not adequately detect if a driver has their hands off the steering wheel for a prolonged period of time. Failure to alert the driver with the intended hands-off the wheel warnings may increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the power steering control module software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

When evaluating a used model, prioritize examples with documented, comprehensive service records and clean pre-purchase inspection results. Pay close attention to powertrain operation during a test drive to rule out transmission shudder and engine performance inconsistencies.
