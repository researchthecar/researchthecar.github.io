# 2023 Lincoln Nautilus Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2023 Lincoln Nautilus offers a quiet, luxurious ride packed with modern technology and comfortable styling. While owner reports to NHTSA are generally sparse for this model year, prospective buyers should review a few reported electronics and mechanical glitches.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4

The standard 2.0-liter turbocharged inline-4 engine produces around 250 horsepower and 275 lb-ft of torque, offering adequate daily acceleration. This engine serves as the base powertrain for the lineup with generally dependable operation.

### 2.7L Twin-Turbocharged V6

The optional 2.7-liter twin-turbo V6 delivers 335 horsepower and 380 lb-ft of torque for robust performance and passing power. It pairs with all-wheel drive to provide strong output, though buyers should monitor overall maintenance history.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 8-speed automatic transmission delivers power smoothly across front-wheel and all-wheel-drive configurations. Overall mechanical durability is typically sound, though rare electronic shift anomalies have been reported by owners.

**Unexpected Shift to Neutral**
- **Symptoms:** While accelerating onto a highway on-ramp, the vehicle may unexpectedly shift into neutral and illuminate the check engine light.
- **Solution:** Have a certified repair facility diagnose the transmission control module and software for any stored diagnostic trouble codes.

### Automatic Emergency Braking

Driver-assist technologies and advanced braking components are designed to mitigate collisions. Owners should thoroughly test brake feel and sensor responsiveness during a pre-purchase test drive.

**Brake Pedal Travel and AEB Failure**
- **Symptoms:** During low-speed emergency maneuvers, applying full force to the brake pedal may fail to bring the vehicle to a timely stop while pre-collision warnings fail to engage.
- **Solution:** Have the braking system inspected for hydraulic pressure issues, sensor alignment, and software calibration.

## Steering

### Steering Column Adjustments

Power-adjustable steering columns add convenience for multiple drivers. Isolated reports indicate potential electronic switch or module faults causing unwanted movement.

**Uncommanded Tilt and Telescopic Movement**
- **Symptoms:** The steering column tilt and telescopic adjustment features may move independently on their own while the vehicle is being driven.
- **Solution:** Have the dealership inspect the steering column switch assembly, wiring harness, and control modules for internal faults.

## Electrical

### Infotainment and Backup Camera

The Nautilus relies heavily on digital displays and advanced connectivity features. While generally responsive, software-related glitches with cameras and smartphone integration can occasionally frustrate drivers.

**Delayed or Freezing Backup Camera**
- **Symptoms:** The backup camera screen may remain black for nearly a minute upon startup, freeze on a single still frame, or experience intermittent reboots.
- **Solution:** Inspect software versions and have the dealer perform relevant infotainment system updates or module replacements if necessary.

**Apple CarPlay GPS Mapping Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** When using Apple CarPlay, navigation directions may lag behind the vehicle's actual location or jump rapidly between erratic directions.
- **Solution:** Disconnect and reconnect the smartphone, update the phone's operating system, or reset the vehicle's infotainment system connection settings.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V198000: Child Seat:Vehicle Tether Anchor](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lincoln&model=Nautilus&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** The child seat tether wire welds on the second-row left-side seat back frame may be insufficient, which can reduce the seat's ability to properly restrain a child during a crash. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 225, "Child Restraint Anchorage Systems." A child that is not properly.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the left-side second-row seat back frame, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V439000: Suspension:Rear:Shock Absorber](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lincoln&model=Nautilus&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** A damaged brake hose can extend the distance required to stop the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the rear shocks, rear brake hoses, rear wheel speed sensor, and rear half shafts as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V315000: Back Over Prevention:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lincoln&model=Nautilus&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** A software error may cause the rearview camera image to delay, freeze, or not display when the vehicle is in reverse. A frozen or missing rearview camera image can reduce the driver's view behind the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2023 Lincoln Nautilus provides a refined luxury crossover experience with strong engine options and a comfortable cabin. Buyers should thoroughly inspect the vehicle's electronics, infotainment stability, and steering adjustments during a test drive to ensure everything operates as intended.
