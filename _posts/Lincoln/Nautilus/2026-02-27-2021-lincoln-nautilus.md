# 2021 Lincoln Nautilus Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2021 Lincoln Nautilus, buyers will find a stylish and comfortable midsize luxury crossover that offers a plush ride and modern tech. However, owner reports submitted to safety authorities point to potential mechanical and electrical hiccups worth keeping on your radar. Reviewing service records and conducting a thorough pre-purchase inspection can help you spot any lingering powertrain or structural concerns before buying.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged I4

The base 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine produces around 250 horsepower and offers adequate everyday performance for daily commuting. It generally operates reliably, though buyers should always verify proper maintenance history during a used vehicle inspection.

### 2.7L Twin-Turbocharged V6

The optional 2.7-liter twin-turbocharged V6 delivers a robust 335 horsepower and strong acceleration for passing maneuvers. While punchy and smooth, some owners have reported major internal coolant leaks tied to head gasket failures.

**Coolant Intrusion and Head Gasket Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may experience improper acceleration, a lurching sensation when pressing the gas pedal, or engine performance loss due to coolant intrusion on cylinder #6.
- **Solution:** Requires professional diagnosis, which may involve replacing the head gasket or performing extensive engine repairs.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 8-speed automatic transmission pairs with both engine choices to provide smooth gear changes under normal operating conditions. Prospective buyers should carefully test drive the vehicle to check for any hesitation, harsh shifting, or slipping.

**Shifting Hesitation and Premature Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have reported sudden jerking into gear, slipping transmission behavior, rising RPMs without acceleration, or complete transmission failure while driving.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified mechanic inspect the transmission control module and fluid condition, which may necessitate component repair or total transmission replacement.

### Power Liftgate and Powertrain Modules

Electrical systems control many of the vehicle's luxury features, making a complete diagnostic scan vital during your shopping process.

**Power Liftgate Unexpected Closure**
- **Symptoms:** The power liftgate may unexpectedly shut down or close while being accessed in public parking lots, creating a safety hazard.
- **Solution:** Have the liftgate sensors, wiring, and control module tested by a technician to determine if recalibration or part replacement is needed.

## Electrical

### Backup Camera and Display

Advanced safety features and driver-assist technologies provide added peace of mind, though software glitches can occasionally affect day-to-day usability.

**Backup Camera Display Delay**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may experience a significant delay of several seconds between real-time movement and what appears on the backup camera screen.
- **Solution:** Consult a dealership to check for software updates or camera module replacements that address video feed lag.

## Exterior

### Door Check and Welds

The exterior body panels and doors generally hold up well, but specific structural complaints highlight potential issues with door check durability.

**Door Check and Weld Cracking**
- **Symptoms:** Opening the front driver's door produces a loud cracking sound, and the door may lack a proper swing stop as structural metal tears around the spot welds.
- **Solution:** Requires inspection by a body shop or dealership to repair or reinforce the damaged door frame and spot welds.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V635000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Engine:Hard Parts Internal/Mechanical](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lincoln&model=Nautilus&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** A loss of drive power increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will perform an engine cycle test and replace the engine as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V315000: Back Over Prevention:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lincoln&model=Nautilus&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** A software error may cause the rearview camera image to delay, freeze, or not display when the vehicle is in reverse. A frozen or missing rearview camera image can reduce the driver's view behind the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2021 Lincoln Nautilus provides a quiet cabin, smooth ride, and strong engine choices that appeal to used luxury vehicle shoppers. However, prospective buyers should carefully evaluate powertrain performance, test all electrical accessories, and inspect the door hinges and structure during a pre-purchase test drive. Ensuring past maintenance was properly performed can significantly reduce the risk of unexpected repair bills.
