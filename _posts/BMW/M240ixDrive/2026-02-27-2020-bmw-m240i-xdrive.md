# 2020 BMW M240i xDrive Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2020 BMW M240i xDrive pairs a robust turbocharged inline-six engine with confident all-weather traction, making it an appealing option for enthusiasts seeking daily usability and strong performance. While major drivetrain mechanical failures are uncommon, prospective buyers should review available owner feedback and conduct a thorough pre-purchase inspection to verify overall vehicle health.

---

## Engine

### 3.0L Turbocharged Inline-6 (B58)

The 3.0-liter turbocharged inline-6 engine (B58) produces approximately 335 horsepower and 369 lb-ft of torque. It is widely regarded as one of BMW's more reliable and refined modern powertrains.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Sport Automatic Transmission

The 8-speed automatic transmission delivers swift, smooth shifts and power to the xDrive all-wheel-drive system. It generally exhibits solid mechanical durability under normal driving conditions.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V352000: Air Bags:Knee Bolster](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=M240i&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** An improperly folded and assembled air bag may not deploy as intended, increasing risk of injury.
- **What to do:** BMW will notify owners, and dealers will replace the knee air bag modules, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2020 BMW M240i xDrive offers a compelling blend of luxury, everyday practicality, and strong performance. Buyers looking at a used model should prioritize a comprehensive mechanical inspection and verify maintenance records to ensure the vehicle has been well cared for.
