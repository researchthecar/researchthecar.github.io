# 2023 BMW M240i xDrive Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2023 BMW M240i xDrive is a high-performance compact luxury coupe offering engaging driving dynamics and everyday usability. Powered by a robust turbocharged inline-six paired with an all-wheel-drive system, it delivers strong performance alongside modern technology and refined interior comfort. As a relatively new model year with limited widespread owner complaints reported to federal safety authorities, prospective buyers generally find it to be an exciting yet mechanically sound pre-owned choice, though individual vehicle inspection and a review of maintenance history remain important steps.

---

## Engine

### 3.0L Turbocharged Inline-6 (B58)

The B58 3.0-liter turbocharged inline-six engine produces approximately 382 horsepower and 369 lb-ft of torque. It is widely regarded by enthusiasts and mechanics as one of the most reliable and refined modern performance engines on the market.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Sport Automatic Transmission

This 8-speed torque-converter automatic transmission features steering-wheel-mounted paddle shifters and delivers lightning-fast, smooth gear changes. It is paired exclusively with the xDrive all-wheel-drive system and has established a strong reputation for durability.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V039000: Air Bags:Side/Window](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=M240i&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** The second-row seat backrest cover panels for the side air bags may break during deployment of the side air bags. A backrest cover panel that breaks during side air bag deployment may cause the air bag to deploy improperly, increasing the risk of injury to second-row seat occupants.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the rear seat backrest side air bag cover panels, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V099000: Visibility:Power Window Devices And Controls](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=M240i&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 118, "Power-operated Window Systems." Unintentionally closing the windows or sunroof without the key present can increase the risk of injury to occupants inside the vehicle.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the software for the Electronic Control Unit (ECU), free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.
