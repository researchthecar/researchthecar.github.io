# 2018 BMW M240i xDrive Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2018 BMW M240i xDrive blends high performance with everyday usability, drawing praise from driving enthusiasts for its robust powertrain. While major mechanical failures are uncommon, used car buyers should inspect auxiliary systems, particularly regarding owner-reported electrical and lighting quirks.

---

## Engine

### 3.0L Turbocharged Inline-6 (B58)

The 3.0-liter turbocharged inline-six engine produces 335 horsepower and 369 lb-ft of torque. It is widely regarded as a powerful and generally reliable powerplant for this model generation.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic with Paddle Shifters

The ZF 8-speed automatic transmission delivers crisp and rapid shifts paired with the xDrive all-wheel-drive system. It offers smooth operation and has a strong reputation for durability with standard maintenance.

### 6-Speed Manual

The traditional 6-speed manual transmission caters to purists seeking direct engagement with the drivetrain. It provides a rewarding driving experience and solid long-term mechanical reliability.

## Electrical

### Audio and Infotainment

Cabin electronics are generally modern and responsive, though occasional audio module or amplifier concerns have been documented by owners.

**Audio System Failure and Crackling**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have reported complete loss of sound from the radio and auxiliary inputs, occasionally preceded by extremely loud crackling noises.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis typically requires inspecting the audio amplifier and replacing the unit if internal failure is confirmed.

## Exterior

### Exterior Lighting

Lighting systems provide excellent visibility, but isolated failures within integrated LED taillight assemblies have been noted.

**LED Taillight Failure**
- **Symptoms:** LED turn signals or tail lamp assemblies may fail prematurely at relatively low mileage.
- **Solution:** Inspect the tail lamp assemblies and replace the affected LED unit as necessary.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V465000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Engine](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=M240i&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** A vehicle stall can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** BMW will notify owners, and dealers will replace the crankshaft sensor, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V719000: Electrical System: Instrument Cluster/Panel](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=M240i&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 101, "Control and Displays." If the display does not illuminate during start up or does not illuminate various control and warning symbols while driving, it can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** BMW will notify owners, and dealers will update the instrument cluster display software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2018 BMW M240i xDrive remains a compelling choice for enthusiasts looking for engaging performance and year-round traction. Prospective buyers should thoroughly test all electronics, audio functions, and exterior lighting during a pre-purchase inspection to ensure everything operates as intended.
