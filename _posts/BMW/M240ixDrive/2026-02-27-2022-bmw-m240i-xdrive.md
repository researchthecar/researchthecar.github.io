# 2022 BMW M240i xDrive Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a pre-owned 2022 BMW M240i xDrive, buyers can generally expect a high-performing sports coupe blending luxury with everyday usability. While owner reports to safety regulators are sparse, prospective buyers should still inspect specific areas like exterior lighting and steering components during a pre-purchase evaluation.

---

## Engine

### 3.0L Turbocharged Inline-6 (B58)

The 2022 BMW M240i xDrive features a 3.0-liter turbocharged inline-six engine (B58) producing approximately 382 horsepower and 369 lb-ft of torque. This powerplant is widely praised for its robust performance, smooth power delivery, and generally solid mechanical reliability.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

Power is routed through an 8-speed ZF automatic transmission paired with xDrive all-wheel drive, offering quick shifts and refined cruising capability. This transmission is known for being durable and responsive in both daily driving and spirited conditions.

## Steering

### Steering Gear Assembly

Steering feedback is typically sharp and precise, though owners have raised specific durability concerns regarding the steering gear assembly.

**Steering Gear Failure Concerns**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have reported concerns regarding potential steering gear assembly wear or failure, prompting requests for safety recall expansions.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified technician inspect the steering rack, gear assembly, and electronic power steering components for play, noise, or binding.

## Exterior

### Headlights

Electrical and lighting systems are generally advanced and reliable, though physical lens durability on front headlights warrants careful inspection.

**Headlight Lens Cracking**
- **Symptoms:** Headlight assemblies may develop stress cracks over time from regular driving conditions, potentially allowing moisture ingress.
- **Solution:** Inspect both headlight assemblies thoroughly for hairline cracks or moisture buildup, and replace the affected units if necessary.
