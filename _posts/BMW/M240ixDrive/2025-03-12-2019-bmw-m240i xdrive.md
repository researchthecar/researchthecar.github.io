The 2019 BMW M240i xDrive is a popular compact performance car known for its engaging driving dynamics and powerful engine. Its turbocharged inline-6 engine delivers exhilarating acceleration and responsive handling, making it a favorite among enthusiasts. The M240i also offers a comfortable interior and a range of modern features. However, some owners have reported issues with reliability and maintenance costs, impacting the overall ownership experience. The 2019 M240i xDrive comes standard with a 3.0-liter turbocharged inline-6 engine (B58) producing 335 horsepower and 369 lb-ft of torque, paired with an 8-speed automatic transmission. While some love the power and handling, others dislike the firm ride and potential maintenance costs.

**Engine**

**B58 3.0L Turbocharged Inline-6**

* **Oil Filter Housing Gasket Leak**
    * Description/Symptoms: Oil leak from the oil filter housing, leading to a burning oil smell and potential oil level depletion.
    * Solution: Replacement of the oil filter housing gasket.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Coolant Leaks (Water Pump/Thermostat Housing)**
    * Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaks often occur from the water pump or thermostat housing. Symptoms include low coolant levels, overheating, and visible coolant puddles.
    * Solution: Replacement of the faulty water pump or thermostat housing.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**8-Speed Automatic (ZF 8HP)**

* **Harsh Shifting/Hesitation**
    * Description/Symptoms: Some owners report occasional harsh shifting, particularly at lower speeds, and hesitation when accelerating. In some cases, this may also cause the car to shudder.
    * Solution: A software update may resolve the issue. In more severe cases, valve body or mechatronic unit replacement may be necessary.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 (software update) - $2000+ (valve body/mechatronic unit)
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **No Issues Found**

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: Premature battery drain, leading to difficulty starting the car, especially after it has been sitting for a period of time. This can sometimes trigger "increased battery discharge" warnings.
    * Solution: Diagnosis and repair of parasitic drain on the electrical system. Could involve replacing a faulty module, fixing wiring issues, or replacing the battery.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+ (depending on the cause)
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Infotainment System Glitches**
    * Description/Symptoms: Intermittent glitches in the iDrive infotainment system, including freezing, lagging, or unexpected restarts.
    * Solution: Software updates may resolve some issues. In severe cases, the iDrive module might need replacement.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 (software update) - $1500+ (iDrive module replacement)
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **No Issues Found**

**Interior**

* **Rattling/Creaking Noises**
    * Description/Symptoms: Rattling or creaking noises coming from the dashboard, trim panels, or seats, especially on rough roads.
    * Solution: Securing loose trim pieces, tightening bolts, or applying felt tape to contact points.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $300+ (depending on complexity and labor)
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2019 BMW M240i xDrive, while praised for its performance, has some common issues. Engine oil and coolant leaks, particularly from the oil filter housing and water pump, are recurring problems, potentially leading to moderate repair costs. Transmission issues such as harsh shifting and electrical glitches including battery drain and infotainment system problems can also surface. While some problems can be addressed with software updates, others require more substantial repairs. The severity and cost of these issues can vary significantly, but potential buyers should be aware of these potential maintenance concerns.
