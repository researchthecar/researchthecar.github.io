The 2022 BMW M240i xDrive is a popular compact performance coupe known for its blend of power, handling, and luxury. Powered by a 3.0-liter turbocharged inline-six engine producing 382 horsepower and 369 lb-ft of torque, it delivers exhilarating acceleration and a sporty driving experience. The M240i xDrive comes standard with an 8-speed automatic transmission and all-wheel drive. Other engine options are not available for the M240i, though other 2 series models may have different engine options. Some owners praise the car's performance, agile handling, and luxurious interior. Common complaints include a harsh ride on rough roads, limited rear seat space, and some infotainment quirks.

**Engine**

**3.0L Turbocharged Inline-Six (B58)**

*   **VANOS Solenoid Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Check engine light, rough idling, decreased engine performance, and potential stalling.
    *   Solution: Replace the faulty VANOS solenoid.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Coolant Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Low coolant levels, overheating, visible coolant leaks under the car, and coolant smell. Can originate from the coolant expansion tank or hoses.
    *   Solution: Replace the leaking coolant expansion tank or hoses.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**8-Speed Automatic (ZF 8HP)**

*   **Rough Shifting**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Harsh or jerky shifts, especially during low-speed acceleration or deceleration.
    *   Solution: Some owners report improvement with a transmission fluid flush and software update. In more severe cases, valve body replacement may be required.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $2000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Harsh Ride Quality**
    *   Description/Symptoms: A stiff and uncomfortable ride, especially over bumps and uneven road surfaces. This is often cited as a characteristic of the M Sport suspension.
    *   Solution: Some owners switch to aftermarket adjustable suspension to improve the ride. There are no other solutions.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1000 - $3000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Glitches**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Freezing, lagging, and unexpected restarts of the iDrive infotainment system. Bluetooth connectivity issues are also reported.
    *   Solution: Software updates usually resolve these issues, but sometimes a system reset or component replacement is needed.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 (software update) - $1000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Sensor Malfunctions**
    *   Description/Symptoms: False readings from parking sensors or other driver-assistance sensors.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the faulty sensor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **No Issues Found**

**Interior**

*   **Rattling/Squeaking Noises**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Annoying rattles and squeaks emanating from the dashboard, door panels, or other interior trim pieces.
    *   Solution: Locating and securing the loose trim pieces or applying sound-deadening material to eliminate the noise.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2022 BMW M240i xDrive generally experiences typical issues for a performance vehicle, with most common problems relating to the engine, transmission, electrical system, and interior. The severity of these issues ranges from minor inconveniences like infotainment glitches and rattles to more serious concerns like VANOS solenoid failure or transmission problems. The cost of repairs can vary significantly, from relatively inexpensive sensor replacements to more substantial expenses for engine or transmission work. Potential buyers should be aware of these potential issues and budget accordingly for maintenance and repairs.
