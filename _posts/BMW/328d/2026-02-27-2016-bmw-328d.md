# 2016 BMW 328d

### Overview
The 2016 BMW 328d, part of the F30 generation, is generally regarded as a reliable vehicle, particularly praised for its fuel efficiency and strong diesel performance. It features a robust 2.0-liter turbocharged diesel engine paired with either a ZF 8-speed automatic or a 6-speed manual transmission. While the powertrain is solid, like any vehicle, it has specific areas that have seen more attention from owners. This analysis focuses on documented technical issues beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential concerns for owners and prospective buyers.

---


## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged Diesel (B47D20)
**Diesel Particulate Filter (DPF) Regeneration Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Check engine light, reduced engine power, increased fuel consumption, exhaust smell.
- **Solution:** Forced regeneration, DPF cleaning, or replacement.
- **Cost:** $500 - $2000

**EGR Cooler Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Coolant loss, white smoke from exhaust, coolant smell in cabin.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the EGR cooler.
- **Cost:** $600 - $1200

**AdBlue (DEF) System Faults**
- **Symptoms:** AdBlue warning lights, reduced engine power, emissions system faults.
- **Solution:** Refilling AdBlue tank, sensor replacement, or NOx sensor replacement.
- **Cost:** $300 - $1000

## Electrical

### General Electrical System
**Battery Drain Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Difficulty starting, electrical components malfunctioning, battery warning light.
- **Solution:** Diagnosing parasitic draw, replacing faulty modules, or battery replacement.
- **Cost:** $200 - $800

**iDrive System Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Screen freezing, unresponsiveness, audio issues.
- **Solution:** Software updates, module reset, or head unit replacement.
- **Cost:** $300 - $1500

### Summary Verdict
The 2016 BMW 328d offers a compelling blend of performance and economy, with its diesel powertrain proving generally robust. The most commonly cited technical concerns revolve around the emissions system, specifically the Diesel Particulate Filter (DPF) and EGR cooler, which can require attention and associated repair costs. Electrical gremlins, such as battery drain and iDrive system glitches, are also noted but are not universally experienced. The ZF 8-speed automatic transmission and the manual gearbox, along with the suspension, exterior, and interior components, are largely free from significant widespread issues. Regular maintenance and prompt attention to warning lights are key to maintaining the reliability of this model.
