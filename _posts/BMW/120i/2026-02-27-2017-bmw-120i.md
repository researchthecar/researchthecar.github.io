# 2017 BMW 120i

### Overview
The 2017 BMW 120i, part of the F20/F21 generation, is generally considered a reliable vehicle, particularly for its segment. It offers a blend of sporty driving dynamics and premium interior appointments. This model year benefits from BMW's extensive engineering and quality control. While no car is entirely free from potential issues, the 120i typically performs well in terms of mechanical and electrical dependability. Owners generally report satisfaction with its performance and build quality, with most common concerns revolving around specific component wear rather than widespread systemic failures. Regular maintenance is key to ensuring its long-term reliability.

### Summary Verdict
The 2017 BMW 120i is a solid choice for a compact premium hatchback, with a strong reliability record. The powertrain, including the 2.0L turbocharged engine and the 8-speed automatic transmission, is robust and generally trouble-free. Suspension components are also typically durable. The most common areas for potential concern are within the electrical system, specifically occasional infotainment glitches and, in some cases, premature battery failure. These are not endemic to the model but are worth noting. Overall, with diligent maintenance, the 120i should provide a dependable and enjoyable ownership experience.
