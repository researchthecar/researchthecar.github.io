# 2021 BMW 228i xDrive Gran Coupe Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2021 BMW 228i xDrive Gran Coupe, buyers generally find a stylish and engaging entry-level luxury sedan with standard all-wheel drive. While modern BMW powerplants offer strong performance and efficiency, prospective owners should review owner-reported feedback concerning brakes, lighting, and body components. Conducting a thorough pre-purchase inspection helps verify that these potential trouble spots have been properly addressed.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged I4 Engine

This 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine produces approximately 228 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque, delivering brisk acceleration paired with solid everyday efficiency. Overall, this powerplant is generally considered robust, though owners have occasionally reported component wear such as engine mount degradation.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

Equipped with a responsive 8-speed automatic transmission paired with xDrive all-wheel drive, this setup provides smooth and quick gear shifts. It typically offers dependable operation under normal driving conditions.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

Owner reports indicate occasional premature wear and unusual noises from the rear braking system.

**Rear Brake Noise and Premature Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may notice abnormal grinding or squealing noises coming from the rear brakes, often accompanied by the illumination of the service brake warning light.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified mechanic inspect the rear brake pads and rotors, replacing them if excessive wear or scoring is found.

## Exterior

### Daytime Running Light Modules

Electrical system feedback highlights potential thermal issues within front exterior lighting modules.

**DRL Module Overheating**
- **Symptoms:** Daytime running lights may fail or flicker, and owners may notice heat damage affecting the internal fiber optic tubes within the headlight housing.
- **Solution:** Replace the affected daytime running light module promptly to prevent further thermal damage to the internal optical components.

### Sunroof

Exterior body glass reports include rare instances of unexpected sunroof structural failures.

**Spontaneous Sunroof Glass Shattering**
- **Symptoms:** The panoramic sunroof glass may shatter unexpectedly while driving at highway speeds, accompanied by a loud pop.
- **Solution:** Inspect the sunroof glass for any stress fractures and have damaged glass assembly units replaced professionally.

### Door Latches and Linkages

Interior cabin hardware feedback points to occasional mechanical friction within door opening mechanisms.

**Door Bracket Scraping Noise**
- **Symptoms:** A noticeable scraping or binding sound originates from inside the front door brackets when attempting to open or close the door.
- **Solution:** Lubricate or adjust the door hinges, check alignment, and replace worn internal linkage brackets if necessary.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V554000: Seat Belts](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=228i&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** As such, these assemblies fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 208, "Occupant Crash Protection." During a crash, a seat belt retractor that does not function as intended increases the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the seat belt retractors (including the seat belts) and, if necessary, replace the retractors, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V091000: Visibility:Power Window Devices And Controls](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=228i&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 118, "Power-operated Window Systems." If the sunroof continues to close after the vehicle is shut off, it may increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** BMW will notify owners, and dealers will update the sunroof electronic coding software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V584000: Seat Belts](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=228i&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 208, "Occupant Crash Protection." An audible chime that does not properly alert the driver to fasten their seat belt increases the risk of injury in a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the receiver audio module software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.
