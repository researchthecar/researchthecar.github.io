# 2020 BMW 228i xDrive Gran Coupe Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2020 BMW 228i xDrive Gran Coupe, buyers will find a stylish, compact luxury sedan paired with standard all-wheel drive. While owner feedback is generally limited, prospective purchasers should carefully evaluate mechanical and electrical systems during a pre-purchase inspection to ensure long-term peace of mind.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4 (B48)

The 2.0-liter turbocharged inline-4 engine produces approximately 228 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque, offering brisk acceleration for daily driving. This powerplant is generally well-regarded for its refined power delivery and efficiency in modern BMW applications.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

Power is routed through a smooth-shifting 8-speed automatic transmission paired with xDrive all-wheel drive. It provides responsive gear changes and maintains highway efficiency.

### Gear Selector Switch (GWS) and Battery

Electrical and shifting controls generally operate reliably, though occasional electronic glitches have been reported by owners.

**Drivetrain Malfunction and Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may experience a sudden drivetrain malfunction warning message accompanied by a drained battery and an unresponsive gear selector switch.
- **Solution:** Have a certified technician diagnose the gear selector switch, reprogram or replace the faulty control module, and test the battery health.

## Suspension

### Wheels and Tires

The suspension provides a firm, sporty ride quality typical of the brand, though low-profile wheel setups require careful navigation over rough pavement.

**Premature Tire Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Low-profile tires may be prone to going flat or sustaining sidewall damage when encountering minor road hazards or potholes.
- **Solution:** Inspect tire tread depths and sidewalls regularly, and consider replacing damaged low-profile tires promptly.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V091000: Visibility:Power Window Devices And Controls](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=228i&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 118, "Power-operated Window Systems." If the sunroof continues to close after the vehicle is shut off, it may increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** BMW will notify owners, and dealers will update the sunroof electronic coding software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V584000: Seat Belts](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=228i&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 208, "Occupant Crash Protection." An audible chime that does not properly alert the driver to fasten their seat belt increases the risk of injury in a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the receiver audio module software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2020 BMW 228i xDrive Gran Coupe offers engaging driving dynamics and modern luxury features for used-car buyers. While isolated reports highlight potential electrical and tire concerns, a thorough pre-purchase inspection will help ensure the vehicle is in solid mechanical condition before purchase.
