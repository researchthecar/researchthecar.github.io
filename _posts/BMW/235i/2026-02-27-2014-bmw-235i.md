# 2014 BMW 235i

### Overview
The 2014 BMW 235i, part of the F22 generation, is a performance-oriented compact luxury coupe known for its engaging driving dynamics and potent turbocharged inline-six engine. As a relatively modern vehicle, its overall reliability is generally considered good, especially when compared to older performance cars. However, like any vehicle, it has specific areas that owners and technicians have noted for potential issues. This analysis focuses on documented technical reliability concerns beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into common problems, their symptoms, solutions, and estimated repair costs for the primary engine and transmission configurations.

---


## Engine

### 3.0L Turbocharged Inline-6 (N55B30)
**Water Pump Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Overheating, coolant leaks, warning lights on the dashboard.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the electric water pump and thermostat assembly.
- **Cost:** $700 - $1200

**Turbocharger Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of power, whistling noises, increased oil consumption, boost leaks.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of the turbocharger unit and associated plumbing.
- **Cost:** $1500 - $3000

**PCV Valve Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Rough idle, oil leaks, potential engine misfires, blue smoke from exhaust.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the Positive Crankcase Ventilation (PCV) valve system.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600

## Electrical

### General Electrical System
**Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Difficulty starting, electrical components malfunctioning intermittently, battery dying prematurely.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and repair of parasitic draw, or replacement of the battery.
- **Cost:** $200 - $800

**Sensor Failures (e.g., ABS, Wheel Speed)**
- **Symptoms:** Warning lights (ABS, traction control), erratic speedometer readings, braking issues.
- **Solution:** Replacement of faulty sensors.
- **Cost:** $300 - $700

### Summary Verdict
The 2014 BMW 235i offers a compelling blend of performance and luxury, with a generally solid reliability record. The potent N55 engine, while powerful, has a few documented weak points such as the electric water pump, turbocharger, and PCV system, which can require attention. Both the 8-speed automatic and 6-speed manual transmissions are considered robust. Electrical gremlins, particularly battery drain and sensor issues, can occur. Interior electronics, especially the infotainment system, may experience occasional glitches. Overall, with proactive maintenance and addressing known issues promptly, the 235i can be a reliable and enjoyable performance car.
