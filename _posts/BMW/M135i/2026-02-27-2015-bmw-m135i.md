# 2015 BMW M135i

### Overview
The 2015 BMW M135i, a high-performance compact hatchback, is generally regarded as a reliable vehicle, especially considering its potent powertrain. It offers a thrilling driving experience with strong acceleration and agile handling. While most owners report satisfaction, like any performance-oriented car, specific components can experience issues under demanding use. This analysis focuses on documented technical reliability concerns beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential problem areas for owners and prospective buyers. The data aims to offer a balanced perspective on the M135i's long-term dependability.

---


## Engine

### 3.0L Turbocharged Inline-6 (N55)
**Water Pump Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Overheating, coolant leaks, warning lights on the dashboard.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the electric water pump and thermostat assembly.
- **Cost:** $700 - $1200

**Turbocharger Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of power, whistling noises, increased oil consumption.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of the turbocharger unit.
- **Cost:** $1500 - $3000

**PCV Valve Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Rough idle, oil leaks, potential engine misfires.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the Positive Crankcase Ventilation (PCV) valve.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600

### Summary Verdict
The 2015 BMW M135i is a robust performance vehicle with a generally strong reliability record. The potent 3.0L turbocharged inline-six engine, while capable of delivering exhilarating performance, has a few documented potential failure points, notably the electric water pump, turbocharger, and PCV valve. These issues, while not universal, are worth noting for potential buyers. Both the 8-speed automatic and 6-speed manual transmissions have proven to be reliable. The suspension, electrical systems, exterior, and interior components are largely free from widespread reported issues, indicating good build quality in these areas. Regular maintenance and addressing early signs of the aforementioned engine issues can contribute to long-term ownership satisfaction.
