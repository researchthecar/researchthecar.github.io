# 2016 BMW M135i

### Overview
The 2016 BMW M135i, a high-performance compact hatchback, is generally regarded as a reliable vehicle, especially considering its potent powertrain. It offers a compelling blend of sporty dynamics and everyday usability. While most owners report a positive ownership experience, like any performance-oriented car, it can be susceptible to specific issues related to its powerful engine and sophisticated systems. This analysis focuses on documented, non-wear-and-tear related problems that have been reported by owners and technicians, providing insights into potential areas of concern and their associated repair costs.

---


## Engine

### 3.0L Turbocharged Inline-6 (N55B30)
**Water Pump Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Overheating, coolant leaks, warning lights on the dashboard.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the electric water pump and thermostat.
- **Cost:** $700 - $1200

**Turbocharger Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of power, whistling noises, increased oil consumption.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of the turbocharger assembly.
- **Cost:** $1500 - $3000

**PCV Valve Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Rough idle, oil leaks, potential engine misfires.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the Positive Crankcase Ventilation (PCV) valve.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600

### Summary Verdict
The 2016 BMW M135i is a robust performance car with a generally good reliability record. The potent N55 engine, while capable, has a few documented issues such as water pump failures, turbocharger problems, and PCV valve issues that owners should be aware of. These are not universal but have been reported with enough frequency to warrant mention. The transmissions, both automatic and manual, are typically very reliable. Electrical gremlins, particularly with the infotainment system and occasional battery drain, can occur. Overall, with proper maintenance and attention to potential engine-related issues, the M135i should provide an engaging and relatively dependable driving experience for its class.
