# 2012 BMW 128i

### Overview
The 2012 BMW 128i, part of the E82 generation, is generally regarded as a reliable vehicle, particularly known for its engaging driving dynamics and robust inline-six engine. This model year benefits from BMW's engineering prowess, offering a premium compact coupe experience. While most owners report a positive ownership experience with minimal unexpected repairs, like any vehicle, certain components can occasionally present issues. This analysis focuses on documented, non-wear-and-tear related problems that may arise, providing insights into potential repair areas and their associated costs to help prospective and current owners.

---


## Engine

### 3.0L N52B30 I6
**Oil Pan Gasket Leak**
- **Symptoms:** Oil spots under the vehicle, burning oil smell, low oil level warnings.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the oil pan gasket.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600

**Valve Cover Gasket Leak**
- **Symptoms:** Oil smell, visible oil leaks around the valve cover, potential misfires if severe.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the valve cover gasket.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600

**Cooling System Leaks (Water Pump/Thermostat)**
- **Symptoms:** Overheating, coolant leaks, 'check engine' light, steam from engine bay.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the water pump and/or thermostat.
- **Cost:** $500 - $900

## Electrical

### General Electrical System
**Window Regulator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Power windows not operating, grinding noise when operating windows.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the window regulator assembly.
- **Cost:** $250 - $500

**Xenon Headlight Ballast Failure**
- **Symptoms:** One or both xenon headlights not turning on or flickering.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the headlight ballast.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600

### Summary Verdict
The 2012 BMW 128i stands out as a reliable choice within the premium compact segment. Its primary powertrain, the 3.0L N52 inline-six engine, is known for its durability, though common age-related leaks such as the oil pan and valve cover gaskets can occur. The automatic and manual transmissions are generally robust. Electrical gremlins are infrequent but can include issues with window regulators and headlight ballasts. Overall, the 128i offers a dependable driving experience with a manageable set of potential repairs, primarily related to aging seals and specific electrical components. Regular maintenance is key to ensuring its continued reliability.
