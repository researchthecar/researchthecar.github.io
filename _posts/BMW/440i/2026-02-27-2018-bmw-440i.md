# 2018 BMW 440i Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used BMW 4 Series, prospective buyers often weigh its sharp styling and engaging driving dynamics against potential long-term maintenance costs. Reviewing owner feedback provides a helpful window into common trouble spots, allowing you to prioritize pre-purchase inspections on key mechanical and electrical subsystems.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4 (430i)

The 2.0-liter turbocharged inline-4 engine produces around 248 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque, offering an efficient yet lively daily driving experience. Buyers should carefully inspect the cooling system for any signs of leaks or wear during a pre-purchase evaluation.

**Coolant Leaks and Overheating Warnings**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have reported unexpected low coolant warning lights and visible fluid puddles underneath the vehicle.
- **Solution:** Inspect coolant hoses, lines, and the water pump for leaks; replace damaged components and refill coolant.

**Engine Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may unexpectedly lose motive power while driving, requiring a restart.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified technician diagnose the fuel delivery, cooling, and electrical systems for underlying faults causing stalls.

### 3.0L Turbocharged Inline-6 (440i)

The 3.0-liter turbocharged inline-6 engine generates approximately 320 horsepower and 330 lb-ft of torque, delivering smooth and robust performance. This powertrain is generally well-regarded, though routine maintenance remains vital for long-term reliability.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 8-speed ZF automatic transmission provides quick, seamless shifts across rear-wheel-drive and xDrive configurations. It is widely considered durable, though fluid changes are recommended at higher mileages.

### 6-Speed Manual Transmission

The available 6-speed manual transmission caters to enthusiasts seeking a more connected driving experience. It exhibits solid mechanical durability when the clutch is properly maintained.

## Safety

### Seat Belt Assembly

Occupant safety systems generally perform reliably, but occasional issues with seat belt retraction or warning sensors warrant attention.

**Seat Belt Failure and False Warnings**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have noted instances where the seat belt failed to properly restrain or retract, or where buckle alert chimes sounded incorrectly.
- **Solution:** Inspect seat belt retractors, buckles, and sensors; replace faulty assemblies as needed.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V507000: Seat Belts:Front](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=440i&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** If the vehicle has an incompatible vehicle-sensitive locking mechanism, the seat belt may not restrain the seat occupant in the event of a crash, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** BMW will notify owners, and dealers will inspect and, if necessary replace, the front seat belt assemblies, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The vehicle offers strong performance and luxury appeal, but prospective owners should remain vigilant regarding cooling system upkeep and electronic sensors. A thorough pre-purchase inspection focusing on the engine bay and auxiliary systems will help ensure a satisfying ownership experience.
