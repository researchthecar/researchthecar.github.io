# 2016 BMW 650i Gran Coupe

### Overview
The 2016 BMW 650i Gran Coupe, a luxurious four-door coupe, generally offers a high level of performance and comfort. As a premium vehicle, its reliability is a key consideration for potential owners. While the 650i Gran Coupe is built with quality components, like any complex machine, it can experience specific issues. This report focuses on documented technical reliability concerns, excluding routine maintenance and standard wear and tear, to provide a clear picture of potential problem areas for this model year. Owners can expect a generally robust vehicle, but awareness of common, albeit not universal, issues can aid in proactive maintenance and informed decision-making.

---


## Engine

### 4.4L Twin-Turbo V8 (N63TU)
**Turbocharger Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Reduced power, increased oil consumption, whistling noises.
- **Solution:** Turbocharger replacement or repair.
- **Cost:** $2,500 - $5,000

**Ignition Coil Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Misfires, rough idling, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of faulty ignition coils.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600 per coil

**Fuel Injector Problems**
- **Symptoms:** Rough idle, poor fuel economy, engine misfires, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of faulty fuel injectors.
- **Cost:** $400 - $800 per injector

## Suspension

### Adaptive Drive System
**Active Roll Stabilization Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** Increased body roll during cornering, error messages on the dashboard.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of active anti-roll bars and related sensors.
- **Cost:** $1,500 - $4,000

### Summary Verdict
The 2016 BMW 650i Gran Coupe is a powerful and luxurious vehicle with a generally good reliability record, especially concerning its transmission and exterior/interior components. However, potential owners should be aware of specific issues that can arise with the potent 4.4L twin-turbo V8 engine, such as turbocharger problems, ignition coil failures, and fuel injector issues, which can be costly to repair. The adaptive suspension system can also present challenges with its active roll stabilization. Electrical gremlins, particularly with the iDrive infotainment system and occasional battery drain, are also noted. While not indicative of every vehicle, these are the most commonly reported technical concerns that may require attention.
