# 2020 BMW M8 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2020 BMW M8 delivers breathtaking performance as a high-end luxury grand tourer, powered by a twin-turbocharged V8 engine. While offering exotic-level power and advanced technology, prospective used-car buyers should carefully inspect powertrain and auxiliary systems. Owner reports indicate occasional complex mechanical and electrical anomalies that require thorough pre-purchase verification.

---

## Engine

### 4.4L Twin-Turbo V8 (S63)

The 4.4-liter twin-turbocharged S63 V8 engine produces massive horsepower and torque, delivering supercar-level acceleration. It requires meticulous maintenance and close monitoring of its complex cooling and induction systems to ensure long-term dependability.

**Coolant Leak Leading to Misfire**
- **Symptoms:** Engine misfires and sudden loss of power while driving.
- **Solution:** Inspect the coolant expansion tank and surrounding lines; replace damaged injectors and leaking components.

**Unintended Acceleration**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle suddenly surges to high RPMs without accelerator pedal input, failing to slow down normally.
- **Solution:** Have the electronic throttle body, accelerator pedal sensor, and engine control module thoroughly diagnosed.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed M Steptronic Automatic

The 8-speed automatic transmission delivers lightning-fast gear changes and power distribution to the all-wheel-drive system. It is generally robust, though regular fluid maintenance is recommended for high-performance driving.

## Exterior

### Windshield Defroster

Exterior lighting, body panels, and glass systems generally hold up well, though climate control interaction with cabin glass requires proper maintenance.

**Slow Defogging in Wet Conditions**
- **Symptoms:** Windshield becomes rapidly foggy during rain, and the defroster takes excessive time to clear visibility.
- **Solution:** Check climate control module operation, cabin air filter condition, and defroster blower functionality.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V096000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=M8&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** When shifting into Reverse, a small portion of the rearview image may be obscured, or the screen may not illuminate. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 111, "Rear Visibility." If the driver relies on only the rearview camera image, the missing or reduced rearview image view.
- **What to do:** BMW will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the rearview camera software and update it if necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V062000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=M8&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** The rotor and shaft within the integrated brake system may not have been welded properly and could separate during a hard braking event, possibly resulting in a loss of the Antilock Brake System (ABS) and braking assistance. A loss of ABS and a reduction in braking assistance can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** BMW will notify owners, and dealers will replace the integrated brake system, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V883000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=M8&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** The transmission wiring harness can become damaged, resulting in an electrical short circuit. An electrical short circuit can cause the transmission to shift to neutral resulting in a loss of propulsion, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** BMW will notify owners, and dealers will replace any damaged transmission harnesses. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2020 BMW M8 is an extraordinary high-performance luxury vehicle, but its complexity demands a rigorous pre-purchase inspection by a qualified specialist. Buyers should prioritize checking the cooling and throttle control systems. A clean service history and verified maintenance records are essential before making a purchase.
