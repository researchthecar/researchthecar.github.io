The 2022 BMW 330i xDrive is a popular luxury sedan known for its sporty handling, comfortable interior, and stylish design. The 330i xDrive features a 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine producing 255 horsepower and 295 lb-ft of torque. Other engine options for the 2022 3 Series include the more powerful M340i with a 3.0-liter turbocharged inline-six, and the plug-in hybrid 330e. All models utilize an 8-speed automatic transmission. Owners generally praise the 330i xDrive for its engaging driving dynamics, refined interior, and technology features. Common complaints often revolve around the high cost of maintenance and repairs, and some dislike the run-flat tires that contribute to a harsher ride quality.

**Engine**

**2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4 (B48)**

*   **Oil Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Oil leaking from the oil filter housing gasket, or valve cover gasket. Can result in oil consumption and a burning smell.
    *   Solution: Replace the oil filter housing gasket or valve cover gasket.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+
    * Recalls: None Found

*   **Coolant Leaks**
    * Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaking from various locations, including the water pump, thermostat housing, or radiator hoses. Can lead to overheating.
    * Solution: Replace the leaking component and replenish the coolant.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1000+ depending on the component
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**8-Speed Automatic (ZF 8HP)**

*   **Rough Shifting/Hesitation**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported occasional rough shifting or hesitation, particularly during low-speed gear changes or when accelerating from a stop. Some owners have reported the issue will only occur in certain Drive Modes.
    *   Solution: Software update, transmission service including fluid and filter change, or in severe cases, valve body replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200-$300 for software, $400-$800+ for fluid and filter change, $1500 - $3000+ for valve body.
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Premature Bushing Wear**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Premature wear of suspension bushings, particularly in the front control arms. Can lead to clunking noises, vibrations, and imprecise handling.
    *   Solution: Replace worn bushings or the entire control arm assembly.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1000+ per side.
     * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **iDrive System Glitches**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have experienced occasional glitches or freezes with the iDrive infotainment system. This can include unresponsive touchscreens, Bluetooth connectivity issues, or navigation errors.
    *   Solution: Software update, system reset, or replacement of the iDrive control unit.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 (software update), $500 - $2000+ (control unit replacement).
     * Recalls: None Found

*   **Sensor Malfunctions**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Malfunctions in various sensors, such as the parking sensors, blind spot monitoring sensors, or tire pressure monitoring system (TPMS) sensors.
    *   Solution: Replace the faulty sensor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+ per sensor.
     * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Adaptive Headlight Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Adaptive headlights not functioning correctly, including failure to self-level, erratic movement, or error messages.
    *   Solution: Recalibration or replacement of headlight components.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1500+ depending on the component.
     * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Seat Creaking/Rattling**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Creaking or rattling noises coming from the seats, particularly the driver's seat.
    *   Solution: Tighten seat bolts, lubricate seat tracks, or replace worn seat components.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $500+ depending on the repair needed.
     * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2022 BMW 330i xDrive is generally a reliable vehicle, but owners have reported some common issues. Engine oil and coolant leaks appear to be the most frequently reported mechanical problems, while electrical glitches and sensor malfunctions are also prevalent. Suspension bushing wear and interior noises can also be concerns. The severity of these issues varies, and repair costs can range from a few hundred dollars for minor fixes to several thousand for more extensive repairs. Overall, potential buyers should be aware of these common problems and factor in the potential cost of maintenance and repairs when considering the purchase of a 2022 BMW 330i xDrive.
