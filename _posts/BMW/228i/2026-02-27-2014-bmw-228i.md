# 2014 BMW 228i

### Overview
The 2014 BMW 228i, part of the F22 generation, is generally considered a reliable vehicle, especially given its performance-oriented nature. It typically features a potent turbocharged four-cylinder engine paired with either a manual or automatic transmission. While BMWs are known for their driving dynamics, this model year has shown a decent track record for reliability. Owners generally report satisfaction with the powertrain and overall build quality. However, as with any performance vehicle, proactive maintenance is key to ensuring long-term dependability. Potential owners should be aware of common areas that might require attention over time, though widespread critical failures are not a dominant theme for this model.

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4 (N20B20)
**Timing Chain Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Rattling noise from the front of the engine, especially on startup; check engine light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the timing chain, tensioners, and guides.
- **Cost:** $1,200 - $2,500

**PCV Valve Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Rough idle, oil leaks, increased oil consumption, whistling noise.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the Positive Crankcase Ventilation (PCV) valve assembly.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600

## Electrical

### General Electrical System
**Water Pump Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Coolant leaks, engine overheating, coolant warning light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the electric water pump and thermostat assembly.
- **Cost:** $700 - $1,200

**Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Difficulty starting, electrical components malfunctioning, battery warning light.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and potential replacement of battery, alternator, or parasitic drain source.
- **Cost:** $200 - $800

### Summary Verdict
The 2014 BMW 228i offers a compelling blend of performance and luxury, with a generally positive reliability outlook. The primary concerns revolve around potential timing chain wear and PCV valve issues in the 2.0L turbocharged engine, which are not uncommon for this engine family but are manageable with timely repairs. The automatic and manual transmissions are robust. Electrical gremlins, particularly related to the water pump and battery drain, can occur but are not pervasive. Overall, the 228i is a solid choice for enthusiasts seeking a sporty coupe, provided it has been maintained diligently. Regular inspections and addressing minor issues promptly will contribute significantly to its long-term dependability.
