# 2016 BMW 228i

### Overview
The 2016 BMW 228i, part of the F22 generation, is generally regarded as a reliable performance coupe. It offers a blend of sporty driving dynamics and premium features. The model primarily utilizes a potent turbocharged four-cylinder engine paired with either a manual or automatic transmission. While most owners report a positive experience, like any vehicle, there are specific areas that have seen occasional reports of issues. This analysis focuses on documented technical problems beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential concerns for prospective buyers or current owners.

---


## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4 (N20/B48)
**PCV Valve Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Rough idle, increased oil consumption, potential stalling, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the Positive Crankcase Ventilation (PCV) valve assembly.
- **Cost:** $200 - $500

**Water Pump Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Coolant leaks, engine overheating, low coolant level warning.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the electric water pump and thermostat assembly.
- **Cost:** $500 - $900

**Timing Chain Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Rattling noise from the front of the engine, especially on startup; check engine light with timing codes.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the timing chain, tensioners, and guides.
- **Cost:** $1000 - $2000

### Summary Verdict
The 2016 BMW 228i demonstrates good overall reliability, particularly with its robust powertrain. The 2.0L turbocharged engine, while generally strong, has a few documented potential issues such as PCV valve failure, water pump leaks, and timing chain wear, which can be costly to repair if they occur. The ZF 8-speed automatic transmission is highly regarded for its durability. Electrical gremlins, like battery drain and infotainment glitches, are not uncommon in modern BMWs and can require specialized diagnosis. Beyond these specific points, the suspension, exterior, and interior components are typically reliable, with no widespread systemic failures reported. Regular maintenance is key to mitigating some of these potential issues.
