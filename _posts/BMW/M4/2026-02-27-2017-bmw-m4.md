# 2017 BMW M4 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2017 BMW M4 delivers high-performance thrills backed by potent turbocharged inline-six engine options and engaging transmission choices. While overall mechanical complaints remain minimal in owner reporting, prospective buyers should carefully review safety recalls and ensure all manufacturer campaigns have been completed.

---

## Engine

### 3.0L Twin-Turbocharged Inline-6 (S55)

The standard 3.0-liter twin-turbocharged inline-six produces approximately 425 horsepower and 406 lb-ft of torque. It offers ferocious performance, though buyers should watch for standard high-performance maintenance needs and cooling system health.

### 3.0L Twin-Turbocharged Inline-6 Competition Package (S55)

The Competition Package bumps output to 444 horsepower while sharpening suspension and exhaust tuning. This variant shares the robust core architecture of the standard S55 engine with heightened track-oriented capability.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Manual Transmission

A traditional 6-speed manual transmission offers a direct, analog connection for enthusiast drivers. It is generally robust when paired with the stock clutch and power levels.

### 7-Speed Dual-Clutch Transmission (DCT)

The 7-speed dual-clutch automatic provides lightning-fast gear changes and snappy daily drivability. Routine fluid services are essential to maintain smooth operation over high mileage.

### Power Train Assembly

Drivetrain components are generally sturdy, though administrative and parts availability bottlenecks for open campaigns have frustrated some owners.

**Recall Part Availability Delays**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have reported long waiting periods for open power train recall replacement parts to become available through dealerships.
- **Solution:** Contact an authorized BMW dealer with your VIN to check for open campaigns and schedule necessary recall repairs as parts are stocked.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V713000: Power Train:Driveline:Driveshaft](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=M4&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** A loss of vehicle propulsion can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** BMW will notify vehicle owners, and dealers will replace the driveshaft, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V115000: Air Bags](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=M4&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** The affected vehicles have seat-mounted side air bag inflator initiators that may fail to ignite during a crash. If the air bag inflator initiator fails to ignite, the seat-mounted air bag will not deploy, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** BMW will notify owners, and dealers will replace the air bag modules, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V914000: Air Bags](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=M4&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** The affected vehicles have seat- mounted side air bag inflator initiators that may fail to ignite during a crash. If the air bag inflator initiator fails to ignite, the seat-mounted air bag will not deploy, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** BMW will notify owners, and dealers will replace the air bag modules, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2017 BMW M4 is a formidable performance machine, but used buyers must prioritize a thorough pre-purchase inspection and verification of recall history. Checking vehicle history reports for open manufacturer campaigns is essential before finalizing a purchase.
