# 2018 BMW M4 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2018 BMW M4, buyers can expect thrilling performance backed by robust twin-turbocharged powertrain engineering. While the car delivers exceptional track-ready dynamics and daily drivability, prospective owners should carefully inspect specific areas like the fuel system during a pre-purchase inspection to ensure long-term reliability and peace of mind.

---

## Engine

### 3.0L Twin-Turbocharged Inline-6 (S55)

The 3.0-liter twin-turbocharged inline-six engine (S55) produces approximately 425 to 444 horsepower, delivering immense power and sharp throttle response. It is generally robust, though standard pre-purchase inspections should check for fuel system and cooling component integrity.

**Fuel Tank Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may notice a strong fuel odor around the vehicle or observe fuel pooling near the rear after filling up the tank.
- **Solution:** Have the fuel tank inspected by a qualified technician and replace the fuel tank assembly if a crack or seal failure is confirmed.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Manual Transmission

The traditional 6-speed manual transmission offers a direct, engaging shifting experience for enthusiasts. Regular fluid maintenance keeps the clutch and synchronizers operating smoothly over high mileage.

### 7-Speed Dual-Clutch Transmission (DCT)

The 7-speed dual-clutch automatic transmission delivers lightning-fast gear changes and seamless power delivery in both automatic and manual modes. Routine fluid services are essential to maintain crisp clutch engagement and longevity.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V507000: Seat Belts:Front](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=M4&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** If the vehicle has an incompatible vehicle-sensitive locking mechanism, the seat belt may not restrain the seat occupant in the event of a crash, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** BMW will notify owners, and dealers will inspect and, if necessary replace, the front seat belt assemblies, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2018 BMW M4 remains a compelling choice for performance enthusiasts seeking high-end luxury and sports car agility. While owner reports indicate potential attention needed around the fuel storage system, a thorough pre-purchase inspection will help identify any hidden concerns before finalizing your purchase.
