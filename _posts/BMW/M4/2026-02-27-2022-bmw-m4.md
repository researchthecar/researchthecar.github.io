# 2022 BMW M4

### Overview
The 2022 BMW M4, a high-performance variant of the 4 Series, is engineered for exhilarating driving dynamics and track-ready capability. It features potent twin-turbocharged inline-six engines and a choice of transmissions, including a manual and an automatic. While generally well-regarded for its performance and build quality, like any complex vehicle, it can experience specific issues. This report focuses on documented technical reliability concerns beyond standard maintenance, providing insights into potential problem areas for owners and enthusiasts.

---


## Engine

### 3.0L Twin-Turbocharged Inline-6 (S58)
**Cooling System Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Coolant loss, overheating, visible coolant residue around hoses and connections.
- **Solution:** Replacement of leaking hoses, seals, or radiator.
- **Cost:** $300 - $700

**Turbocharger Wastegate Actuator Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Check engine light, reduced engine power, boost pressure issues.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the wastegate actuator or turbocharger assembly.
- **Cost:** $1,500 - $3,000

## Electrical

### Infotainment System
**Connectivity Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Bluetooth pairing problems, Apple CarPlay/Android Auto unresponsiveness, screen freezing.
- **Solution:** Software updates, system resets, or module replacement.
- **Cost:** $200 - $800


### 12V Battery Drain
**Premature Battery Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Difficulty starting, electrical components malfunctioning intermittently, battery warning light.
- **Solution:** Battery replacement, diagnosis of parasitic draw.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600

### Summary Verdict
The 2022 BMW M4 demonstrates strong reliability, particularly in its core mechanical components like the engine and transmission, especially the manual variant. The primary concerns tend to be related to the advanced electronic systems, such as infotainment connectivity, and occasional issues with specific engine ancillaries like wastegate actuators or cooling system components. Early detection and addressing of coolant leaks are advisable. While not widespread, some owners have reported premature 12V battery failures. Overall, the M4 is a robust performance vehicle, but owners should be aware of potential electronic glitches and the importance of maintaining the cooling system.
