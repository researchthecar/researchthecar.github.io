**Introduction**

The 2015 BMW M4 is a high-performance coupe that boasts a twin-turbocharged 3.0-liter inline-6 engine with 425 horsepower and 406 lb-ft of torque. It is paired with a 7-speed dual-clutch transmission. The car is praised for its thrilling engine performance, sharp handling, and luxurious interior. However, it has faced some issues that owners should be aware of.

**Engine**

* **S55 3.0L Twin-Turbocharged Inline-6**

    * **Oil Pump Failure:**
        * Description/Symptoms: The oil pump may fail prematurely, causing a loss of oil pressure and potentially leading to engine damage. The symptoms may include low oil pressure warning lights, grinding noises, and a sudden drop in engine power.
        * Solution: Replace the oil pump and associated components.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,500+
        * Recalls: NHTSA Campaign Number 17V761
    * **Connecting Rod Bearing Failure:**
        * Description/Symptoms: The connecting rod bearings may fail prematurely, causing knocking noises, vibrations, and reduced engine performance.
        * Solution: Replace the connecting rod bearings and associated components.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $4,000 - $6,000+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

* **7-Speed Dual-Clutch Transmission (DCT)**

    * **Mechatronic Unit Failure:**
        * Description/Symptoms: The mechatronic unit, which controls the transmission, may fail, causing gear shifting problems, clutch shuddering, and sudden loss of drive.
        * Solution: Replace the mechatronic unit and reprogram the transmission.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $3,000 - $4,500+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Adaptive M Suspension:**

    * **No Issues Found**

**Electrical**

* **Battery Discharge:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The battery may discharge prematurely, leaving the car unable to start.
    * Solution: Identify and fix the source of the electrical drain, such as a faulty alternator or battery.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Headlight Wiring Harness Damage:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The headlight wiring harness may become damaged, causing the headlights to malfunction.
    * Solution: Repair or replace the damaged wiring harness.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Carbon Fiber Roof Panel Delamination:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The carbon fiber roof panel may delaminate from the body of the car, causing a bulging or rippling appearance.
    * Solution: Replace the carbon fiber roof panel.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **iDrive Navigation System Freezing:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The iDrive navigation system may freeze or malfunction, disrupting the use of navigation, entertainment, and other features.
    * Solution: Update the iDrive software or replace the navigation module.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Door Lock Actuator Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The door lock actuators may fail, preventing the doors from locking or unlocking properly.
    * Solution: Replace the faulty door lock actuators.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

Overall, the 2015 BMW M4 is a highly regarded performance car but has faced some reliability issues that can be costly to repair. Owners should be aware of potential problems with the oil pump, connecting rod bearings, mechatronic unit, and electrical components. It is advisable to have the car regularly maintained and to address any issues promptly to avoid more severe and expensive repairs down the road.
