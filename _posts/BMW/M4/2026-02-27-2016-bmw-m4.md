# 2016 BMW M4

### Overview
The 2016 BMW M4, a high-performance coupe, is generally regarded as a reliable vehicle, especially considering its potent powertrain. However, like many performance-oriented cars, it can be susceptible to specific issues that require attention. The twin-turbocharged inline-six engine, while powerful, has seen some reports of turbocharger-related problems and oil leaks. The dual-clutch transmission, while offering rapid shifts, has also been a point of concern for some owners, with occasional reports of rough shifting or electronic faults. Other areas like suspension and electrical systems have a more mixed reliability record, with some owners reporting issues that deviate from standard maintenance. Overall, the M4 offers an exhilarating driving experience, but potential buyers should be aware of these specific technical considerations.

---


## Engine

### 3.0L Twin-Turbocharged Inline-6 (S55B30)
**Turbocharger Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of power, whistling noises, increased exhaust smoke.
- **Solution:** Turbocharger replacement or rebuild.
- **Cost:** $2,500 - $5,000

**Oil Leaks (Valve Cover Gasket, Oil Pan Gasket)**
- **Symptoms:** Visible oil stains under the vehicle, burning oil smell, low oil level.
- **Solution:** Replacement of leaking gaskets.
- **Cost:** $400 - $900

**Cooling System Issues (Water Pump, Thermostat)**
- **Symptoms:** Overheating, coolant leaks, warning lights on the dashboard.
- **Solution:** Replacement of faulty water pump or thermostat.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1,000


## Transmission

### 7-Speed M-DCT Dual-Clutch Automatic
**Rough Shifting / Jerking**
- **Symptoms:** Hesitation during shifts, jerky acceleration, transmission warning lights.
- **Solution:** Software update, clutch pack replacement, or transmission control module repair.
- **Cost:** $1,500 - $4,000

**Transmission Control Module (TCM) Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Inability to shift gears, 'transmission failure' warning message.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the TCM.
- **Cost:** $1,000 - $2,500

## Suspension

### Adaptive M Suspension
**Electronic Damper Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Harsh ride, clunking noises, 'suspension malfunction' warning.
- **Solution:** Replacement of faulty adaptive dampers.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1,500 per corner


## Electrical

### General Electrical System
**Infotainment System Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Screen freezing, unresponsive controls, Bluetooth connectivity issues.
- **Solution:** Software updates, head unit reset, or replacement.
- **Cost:** $200 - $1,200

**Battery Drain Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Difficulty starting, dead battery, electrical components not working.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis of parasitic draw, replacement of faulty module or battery.
- **Cost:** $300 - $800

### Summary Verdict
The 2016 BMW M4 is a performance-focused vehicle with a generally good reliability record for its class. The primary areas of concern revolve around the powertrain, specifically potential turbocharger issues and oil leaks from the engine, as well as the dual-clutch transmission's tendency for rough shifting or electronic faults. The adaptive suspension can also present issues with its electronic dampers. While electrical gremlins like infotainment glitches and battery drain can occur, they are not overwhelmingly common. For potential owners, regular maintenance and awareness of these specific potential problems are key to ensuring long-term satisfaction with this exhilarating sports coupe.
