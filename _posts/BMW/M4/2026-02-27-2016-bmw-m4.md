# 2016 BMW M4 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a 2016 BMW M4, buyers are typically looking for high-performance track-ready capability wrapped in a luxury coupe or convertible body style. While the twin-turbocharged inline-six engine provides exhilarating power, used-car buyers should carefully review maintenance records and inspect critical components like the front suspension knuckles and cooling system for potential wear or owner-reported concerns.

---

## Engine

### 3.0L Twin-Turbocharged I6 (S55)

The 3.0-liter twin-turbocharged inline-six engine produces approximately 425 horsepower and 406 lb-ft of torque, delivering blistering acceleration. It is generally robust when properly maintained, though buyers should monitor for cooling and fuel system integrity.

**Cooling System and Drivetrain Anomalies**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have reported low coolant levels or cooling system dryness accompanied by unusual transmission shifting behavior such as skipping gears.
- **Solution:** Inspect the cooling system hoses, radiator, and fluid levels regularly, and have a qualified mechanic diagnose any unusual drivetrain or transmission codes.

**Fuel System Leak**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may notice a puddle of fuel pooling underneath the rear driver side near the fuel tank after refueling.
- **Solution:** Have the fuel tank, lines, and connections inspected immediately by a professional to locate and replace any failing seals or components.

## Transmission

### 7-Speed Dual-Clutch Transmission (DCT)

The 7-speed dual-clutch automatic transmission offers rapid, seamless gear changes suited for both spirited driving and daily commutes. Routine fluid changes are vital for maintaining long-term shift quality.

### 6-Speed Manual Transmission

The traditional 6-speed manual transmission provides an engaging, hands-on driving experience for purists. It remains mechanically straightforward, though clutch wear depends heavily on prior driving habits.

## Steering

### Front Steering Knuckles / Carriers

Suspension components endure significant stress under high-performance driving, making thorough pre-purchase undercarriage inspections essential.

**Cracked Front Steering Knuckles**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have discovered structural cracks in the billet aluminum front steering knuckles or bearing carriers during routine maintenance or inspections.
- **Solution:** Inspect the front suspension and steering knuckles thoroughly for fracturing, and replace damaged components with updated manufacturer parts.

## Electrical

### Seats

Cabin electronics and power seats generally hold up well, but intermittent electrical glitches should be checked during a test drive.

**Seat Heater Smoke**
- **Symptoms:** Activation of the seat heater has reportedly caused smoke to emit from the passenger seat.
- **Solution:** Have the vehicle's electrical wiring, seat heating elements, and fuses inspected and repaired by a qualified technician.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V713000: Power Train:Driveline:Driveshaft](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=M4&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** A loss of vehicle propulsion can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** BMW will notify vehicle owners, and dealers will replace the driveshaft, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V653000: Structure:Frame And Members](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=M4&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** Reuse of the existing bolts may cause the bolts to loosen. Loose rear sub-frame bolts may adversely affect vehicle handling and control, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** BMW will notify owners, and dealers will replace the rear sub-frame bolts with new bolts, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 15V782000: Power Train:Driveline:Driveshaft](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=M4&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** The affected vehicles have a driveshaft with a slip-joint that may not have been filled with grease. If the slip-joint fails, there would be a loss of power to the rear axle and therefore the vehicle would stop moving, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** BMW will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the driveshaft, replacing it as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2016 BMW M4 is an exceptional performance machine that rewards enthusiastic drivers, but it requires diligent upkeep. Prospective buyers should prioritize a comprehensive pre-purchase inspection by an independent BMW specialist, paying close attention to the front suspension knuckles, cooling system, and service history to avoid unexpected out-of-pocket repair expenses.
