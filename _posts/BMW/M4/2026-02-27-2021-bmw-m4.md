# 2021 BMW M4 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2021 BMW M4, buyers can expect a high-performance luxury sports coupe powered by potent turbocharged inline-six engine options paired with responsive manual or automatic transmissions. While these track-capable machines offer thrilling driving dynamics, prospective buyers should carefully evaluate vehicle history, maintenance records, and potential wear-and-tear items typical of high-output sports cars.

---

## Engine

### 3.0L Twin-Turbocharged Inline-6 (S58)

The standard S58 3.0-liter twin-turbocharged inline-six produces approximately 473 horsepower and 406 lb-ft of torque, delivering robust acceleration and track-ready performance. Known for strong overall mechanical integrity, it requires meticulous maintenance and frequent fluid changes to ensure long-term reliability.

### 3.0L Twin-Turbocharged Inline-6 Competition (S58)

The high-output S58 variant found in the Competition models bumps output to around 503 horsepower and 479 lb-ft of torque. It offers blistering performance with solid engineering foundations, though aggressive driving styles can accelerate consumable wear.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Manual Transmission

Available exclusively on base models, this traditional 6-speed manual gearbox provides an engaging, driver-focused experience. It is mechanically robust for street use, provided the clutch has not been abused by previous owners.

### 8-Speed M Steptronic Automatic Transmission

This quick-shifting 8-speed automatic transmission handles high torque loads effortlessly and delivers smooth daily cruising alongside lightning-fast gear changes. It is generally dependable, though routine fluid maintenance remains essential.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

High-performance braking systems on sports coupes can occasionally exhibit noise characteristics inherent to aggressive pad compounds, though owner reports suggest verifying proper component function.

**Excessive Brake Noise**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle brakes produce very loud squealing noises during normal operation, accompanied by a performance feel that some drivers find lacking despite low vehicle mileage.
- **Solution:** Have the brake pads and rotors inspected by a qualified technician to check for glazing, pad compound characteristics, or wear.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V554000: Seat Belts](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=M4&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** As such, these assemblies fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 208, "Occupant Crash Protection." During a crash, a seat belt retractor that does not function as intended increases the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the seat belt retractors (including the seat belts) and, if necessary, replace the retractors, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V062000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=M4&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The rotor and shaft within the integrated brake system may not have been welded properly and could separate during a hard braking event, possibly resulting in a loss of the Antilock Brake System (ABS) and braking assistance. A loss of ABS and a reduction in braking assistance can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** BMW will notify owners, and dealers will replace the integrated brake system, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2021 BMW M4 is a formidable performance coupe that pairs thrilling powertrain options with high-end luxury. While driver reports indicate minor concerns like brake noise, the overall mechanical platform is robust. Used car buyers should prioritize comprehensive pre-purchase inspections and verify diligent maintenance histories.
