## Suspension

### Adaptive Air Suspension with Alpina Sport Tuning
**Air Spring Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle sagging on one corner, loss of ride height, 'air suspension malfunction' warning light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of leaking air spring or associated seals.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1500 per corner

**Air Compressor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Inability to raise or lower the vehicle, constant running of the compressor, 'air suspension malfunction' warning light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the air suspension compressor unit.
- **Cost:** $700 - $1200


## Electrical

### Infotainment System (iDrive 7)
**Software Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Intermittent freezing, unresponsive touch screen, Bluetooth connectivity issues, navigation errors.
- **Solution:** Software updates, system resets, or module replacement if hardware is faulty.
- **Cost:** $150 - $800


### Advanced Driver Assistance Systems (ADAS)
**Sensor Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** False warnings from parking sensors, adaptive cruise control, or lane keeping assist; system deactivation.
- **Solution:** Cleaning, recalibration, or replacement of faulty sensors (e.g., radar, camera, ultrasonic).
- **Cost:** $300 - $1000 per sensor/module

### Summary Verdict
The 2017 BMW Alpina B7 is a highly engineered performance sedan with a strong reliability record for its core powertrain and transmission. The primary areas where owners might encounter issues are related to the sophisticated adaptive air suspension system, particularly air spring leaks or compressor failures, and potential glitches within the advanced infotainment and driver assistance electronics. These are not uncommon for vehicles with such complex systems. Regular maintenance and prompt attention to warning lights are crucial. Overall, for a vehicle of its caliber and performance focus, the B7 demonstrates commendable technical reliability, with most reported issues being repairable and not indicative of fundamental design flaws.
