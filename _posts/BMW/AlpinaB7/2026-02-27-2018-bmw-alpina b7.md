## Suspension

### Adaptive Air Suspension
**Air Spring Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle may sag on one corner, suspension warning light illumination, uneven ride height.
- **Solution:** Replacement of leaking air spring or associated lines.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1500 per corner

**Air Compressor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Inability to raise or lower the vehicle, constant running of the compressor, suspension warning light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the air suspension compressor unit.
- **Cost:** $700 - $1200


## Electrical

### Infotainment System
**Software Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Screen freezing, unresponsive touch controls, intermittent system reboots, navigation issues.
- **Solution:** Software updates, system resets, or potential module replacement.
- **Cost:** $200 - $800


### Battery Drain
**Parasitic Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Dead battery after short periods of inactivity, warning lights related to battery voltage.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis to identify parasitic draw from modules or accessories, repair or replacement of faulty component.
- **Cost:** $300 - $1000+

### Summary Verdict
The 2018 BMW Alpina B7 generally exhibits strong reliability, particularly in its powertrain. The 4.4L twin-turbo V8 and 8-speed automatic transmission are robust and well-suited for the car's performance demands. Potential areas of concern are primarily related to the advanced adaptive air suspension system, which can develop leaks or compressor issues over time, and some electrical gremlins such as infotainment glitches or parasitic battery drain, common in highly optioned luxury vehicles. Interior climate control actuators can also be a point of failure. Despite these potential issues, the B7's overall reliability is commendable for a high-performance luxury sedan, provided regular maintenance and prompt attention to any emerging problems.
