# 2016 BMW 640i xDrive

### Overview
The 2016 BMW 640i xDrive, a luxurious grand tourer, generally offers a strong reliability record, reflecting BMW's engineering prowess. It is equipped with a potent turbocharged inline-six engine paired with an eight-speed automatic transmission, both known for their robust performance and durability. While the vehicle is built with high-quality materials and advanced technology, like any complex machine, it can experience specific issues over time. This analysis focuses on documented, non-wear-and-tear related problems that owners might encounter, providing insights into potential repair areas and their associated costs to help prospective and current owners make informed decisions.

---


## Engine

### 3.0L Twin-Turbocharged Inline-6 (N55)
**Turbocharger Wastegate Rattle**
- **Symptoms:** Audible rattling or ticking noise from the turbocharger area, especially during cold starts or light acceleration.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the turbocharger wastegate actuator or the entire turbocharger assembly.
- **Cost:** $1,200 - $2,500

**PCV Valve Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Rough idle, increased oil consumption, blue smoke from exhaust, potential engine misfires.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the Positive Crankcase Ventilation (PCV) valve and potentially the valve cover.
- **Cost:** $400 - $800

**Water Pump Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Overheating, coolant leaks, warning lights on the dashboard, reduced heating performance.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the electric water pump and thermostat assembly.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1,500

## Suspension

### Adaptive Drive System
**Active Roll Stabilization Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Increased body roll during cornering, warning lights related to suspension system, clunking noises.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and repair or replacement of active anti-roll bars and related sensors/modules.

## Electrical

### Infotainment System (iDrive)
**iDrive System Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Screen freezing, unresponsiveness, audio cutting out, navigation system errors.
- **Solution:** Software updates, module reset, or replacement of the iDrive control unit or display.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1,500


### Battery Drain
**Parasitic Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Dead battery after short periods of inactivity, electrical components malfunctioning intermittently.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis to identify the source of the drain (e.g., faulty module, aftermarket accessory) and repair or replacement.
- **Cost:** $300 - $1,000

### Summary Verdict
The 2016 BMW 640i xDrive is a generally reliable vehicle, particularly in its powertrain. The 3.0L turbocharged inline-six engine and the 8-speed automatic transmission are well-regarded for their durability. However, potential owners should be aware of specific issues that can arise, such as wastegate rattle on the turbocharger, PCV valve failures, and electric water pump issues, all of which can lead to significant repair costs. The advanced suspension systems, while offering excellent handling, can also be a source of expensive repairs if components like the active roll stabilization fail. Electrical gremlins, particularly with the iDrive system and occasional battery drain, are also noted. Despite these potential issues, with proper maintenance and addressing problems promptly, the 2016 640i xDrive can remain a dependable luxury vehicle.
