# 2018 BMW 640i xDrive Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used luxury grand tourer, evaluating the mechanical health and known owner reports helps ensure a confident purchase. This overview details common owner-reported areas of concern for the vehicle, providing a balanced look at what to inspect before buying.

---

## Engine

### 3.0L Turbocharged Inline-6 (B58)

The 3.0-liter turbocharged inline-6 engine (B58) produces approximately 335 horsepower and 332 lb-ft of torque, offering strong performance and generally robust long-term reliability compared to older BMW powerplants.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission with xDrive

This vehicle utilizes an 8-speed automatic transmission paired with an all-wheel-drive system, delivering smooth gear changes and responsive power delivery with very few widespread reliability complaints.

## Interior

### Panoramic Sunroof

Interior cabin amenities are generally high quality, though occasional issues with the sunroof sunshade mechanism have been noted.

**Sunroof Shade Sagging**
- **Symptoms:** The panoramic sunroof curtain fails to retract properly, sagging downward and potentially blocking rear visibility or interior views.
- **Solution:** Take the vehicle to a service center for inspection and mechanical repair or replacement of the shade mechanism.

## Safety

### Air Bag

Safety components generally perform well, though passenger seat sensor issues have been reported by some owners.

**Passenger Seat Occupant Sensor Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** The passenger-side airbag warning light illuminates unexpectedly and remains on, indicating a system malfunction.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified technician diagnose the sensor mat, which may require replacement.
- **Cost:** $2600 to $3000

---

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V465000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Engine](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=640i&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** A vehicle stall can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** BMW will notify owners, and dealers will replace the crankshaft sensor, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V684000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=640i&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 111, "Rearview Mirrors." The lack of an image in the back-up camera display increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** BMW will notify owners, and dealers will update the back-up camera software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The vehicle offers compelling luxury and performance, particularly supported by a solid powertrain. Buyers should carefully check the operation of the panoramic sunroof shade and verify that all safety warning lights, specifically related to the passenger airbag sensor, function correctly during a pre-purchase inspection.
