The 2018 BMW 530e is a plug-in hybrid variant of the G30 5-Series, offering a blend of performance and efficiency. It features a turbocharged 2.0-liter four-cylinder engine paired with an electric motor, producing a combined output of 248 horsepower and 310 lb-ft of torque. The other engine options available on the 2018 5-series are the 2.0 liter inline-4 cylinder and 3.0 liter inline-6 cylinder gasoline engines and 3.0 liter inline-6 cylinder diesel engine. All engine options are equipped with an 8-speed automatic transmission. Owners often praise the 530e for its comfortable ride, fuel economy in hybrid mode, and luxurious interior. However, some have criticized its limited electric-only range and higher price point compared to non-hybrid models.

**Electric Motor**

* **Electric Motor Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Complete failure of the electric motor, rendering the car unable to drive in electric mode. Error messages related to the electric drive system appear on the dashboard.
    * Solution: Replacement of the electric motor.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $8,000 - $12,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Battery Pack**

* **Reduced Battery Capacity**
    * Description/Symptoms: Over time, the battery's ability to hold a charge diminishes, leading to a shorter electric-only range than originally advertised.
    * Solution: Battery module replacement or a full battery pack replacement depending on the severity.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $8,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**8-Speed Automatic Transmission**

* **Rough or Delayed Shifting**
    * Description/Symptoms: Harsh shifts between gears, noticeable delays when shifting, especially during acceleration or deceleration.
    * Solution: Software update, transmission fluid flush, or valve body replacement.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 (software update) - $2,500+ (valve body)
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Charging Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: The car fails to charge, either at home or at public charging stations. Error messages related to the charging system are displayed.
    * Solution: Inspect and replace the charging port, wiring, or onboard charger.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $2,000+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **iDrive System Malfunctions**
    * Description/Symptoms: Freezing, crashing, or unresponsive behavior of the iDrive infotainment system.
    * Solution: Software update, replacement of the iDrive module.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 (software update) - $1,500+ (module replacement)
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Sensor Failures (Parking Sensors, etc.)**
    * Description/Symptoms: Parking sensors malfunctioning, providing false alarms or failing to detect obstacles.
    * Solution: Replacement of faulty sensors.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+ per sensor
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Adaptive Headlight Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: Adaptive headlights fail to adjust properly, or throw error codes.
    * Solution: Recalibration or replacement of the headlight unit.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $2,000+ per headlight unit.
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Seat Adjustment Motor Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Electric seat adjustments stop working, leaving the seat stuck in one position.
    * Solution: Replacement of the seat motor or seat track assembly.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $1,200+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Sunroof Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: Sunroof gets stuck open or closed, makes excessive noise during operation, or leaks.
    * Solution: Repair or replacement of the sunroof motor, tracks, or seals.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2018 BMW 530e presents a mix of potential issues. Electrical problems are a common area of concern, with charging issues, iDrive malfunctions, and sensor failures. The electric motor and battery pack can face issues with capacity and complete failures, which are expensive to remedy. The transmission can also present rough shifting issues which are also costly to repair. Overall, the 2018 BMW 530e can be a great car when properly maintained, but keep an eye out for the common issues.
