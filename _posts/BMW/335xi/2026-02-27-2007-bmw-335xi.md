# 2007 BMW 335xi

### Overview
The 2007 BMW 335xi, a performance-oriented all-wheel-drive sedan, is known for its potent twin-turbocharged inline-six engine and engaging driving dynamics. While generally well-regarded for its sporty character and premium features, like many complex luxury vehicles of its era, it is not without its potential reliability concerns. Owners often report a mixed bag of experiences, with some enjoying trouble-free ownership while others encounter specific, recurring issues, particularly related to the powertrain and its associated systems. Regular and diligent maintenance is crucial for mitigating these potential problems and ensuring the longevity of this sophisticated German automobile. This analysis focuses on documented common issues beyond routine maintenance.

---


## Engine

### 3.0L Twin-Turbocharged Inline-6 (N54)
**Fuel Injector Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Rough idling, misfires, engine stalling, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of faulty fuel injectors.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1500 per injector, potentially more for a set.

**Turbocharger Issues (Wastegate Rattle/Failure)**
- **Symptoms:** Whining noise from turbos, loss of power, boost leaks, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of turbochargers and/or wastegate actuators.
- **Cost:** $1500 - $3000 per turbocharger assembly.

**High-Pressure Fuel Pump (HPFP) Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Engine sputtering, hesitation under acceleration, long cranking times, engine stalling, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the high-pressure fuel pump.
- **Cost:** $700 - $1200.

**PCV Valve System Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Oil leaks, rough idle, increased oil consumption, whistling noises.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the crankcase ventilation (PCV) valve system.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600.

## Electrical

### General Electrical System
**Water Pump Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Overheating, coolant leaks, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the electric water pump and thermostat.
- **Cost:** $600 - $1000.

**Idrive System Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Screen freezing, unresponsiveness, navigation issues.
- **Solution:** Software updates or replacement of the iDrive control unit.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1500.

### Summary Verdict
The 2007 BMW 335xi offers a compelling blend of performance and all-weather capability. However, potential buyers should be aware of common issues, particularly with the N54 engine's fuel delivery and turbocharger systems, as well as the electric water pump. These components can be costly to repair or replace. While the transmission and chassis generally prove reliable, the electrical system can present its own set of challenges. Thorough pre-purchase inspection and a history of consistent maintenance are highly recommended to mitigate the risk of significant repair expenses. Despite these potential drawbacks, for those who prioritize driving engagement and are prepared for the associated upkeep, the 335xi can still be a rewarding vehicle.
