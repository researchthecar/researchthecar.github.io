# 2024 BMW i7 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2024 BMW i7 represents the cutting edge of luxury electric motoring, offering effortless performance and an ultra-plush cabin. However, early adopter owners have reported several electronic and mechanical concerns that potential used car buyers should carefully inspect. Particular attention should be paid to advanced driver assistance systems, suspension integrity, and door-operation electronics.

---

## Engine

### Dual-Motor Electric Drive

This high-output dual-motor electric setup powers the all-wheel-drive variants, delivering rapid acceleration and refined, silent operation. It is generally robust, though a small number of early owners have experienced motive power interruptions linked to electronic software campaigns.

### Single-Motor Electric Drive

The single-motor rear-wheel-drive configuration provides smooth, efficient everyday cruising power. While the hardware proves durable, owners should ensure that all powertrain control software updates have been completed.

**Unexpected Loss of Motive Power**
- **Symptoms:** An "Inability to Drive" warning message appears on the instrument cluster, followed by a sudden loss of motive power while driving.
- **Solution:** Have a dealer check for and apply applicable manufacturer software updates and campaign remedies.

## Transmission

### Single-Speed Direct Drive

The single-speed direct-drive transmission provides seamless power delivery without shifting hesitation. It operates quietly and reliably, with no widespread mechanical failures reported by drivers.

### Automatic Door and Safety Electronics

Cabin electronics manage numerous conveniences, though overly sensitive switchgear can occasionally trigger unexpected automation.

**Unintentional Door Opening**
- **Symptoms:** The driver's door opens automatically during travel or while reversing after the occupant's knee accidentally strikes the door armrest button.
- **Solution:** Update the vehicle software at a dealership to restrict automatic door opening functionality to low speeds under 3 MPH.

## Suspension

### Air Suspension System

The sophisticated air suspension delivers a peerless ride quality, but owners have occasionally experienced sudden deflation and tire or rim damage.

**Air Suspension Corner Drop**
- **Symptoms:** One corner of the vehicle suddenly sags or drops to the road while driving, requiring a tow to the dealership.
- **Solution:** Inspect air struts, lines, and control modules for leaks or electrical faults, and replace damaged suspension components as necessary.

## Brakes

### Hydraulic and Regenerative Braking System

The braking system blends regenerative deceleration with traditional hydraulics, though isolated owner reports indicate potential responsiveness anomalies.

**Intermittent Brake Pedal Unresponsiveness**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle occasionally fails to respond promptly when the brake pedal is depressed, raising serious safety concerns.
- **Solution:** Have the brake actuator, regenerative braking modules, and hydraulic system thoroughly inspected by a certified technician.

## Safety

### Forward Collision Avoidance and Parking Assist

Advanced safety suites offer robust protection, but overly aggressive collision avoidance sensors can occasionally misinterpret normal driving environments.

**Phantom Emergency Braking**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle suddenly engages automatic emergency braking on the freeway or highway without any obstructions or hazards ahead.
- **Solution:** Check for sensor calibrations, clean camera and radar lenses, and ensure all relevant driver assistance software updates are installed.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V700000: Electrical System:Adas:Driver Monitoring:Camera/Sensor](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=i7&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** The ground connection to the steering wheel may not have been attached correctly, resulting in an inoperative hands-on detection system. An inoperative hands-on detection system may fail to detect when the driver's hands are not on the steering wheel and therefore not engage the emergency stopping assistant system, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and reattach the ground connection as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V104000: Electronic Stability Control (Esc)](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=i7&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** Either of these scenarios can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the integrated brake system, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V697000: Electronic Stability Control (Esc):Control Module](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=i7&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** In addition, the Antilock Brake System (ABS) and Dynamic Stability Control (DSC) system may not function. Either of these scenarios can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the integrated brake system, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V395000: Electrical System:Propulsion System:Traction Motor:Controller:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=i7&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** A loss of drive power increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 25V470000: Electrical System:Propulsion System:Traction Battery](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=i7&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** The high-voltage battery cell modules may not have been assembled properly, resulting in stress on the module frame and possible module failure. Battery module failure may lead to a shutdown of the high-voltage system which can result in a loss of drive power, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace specific high-voltage battery cell modules, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V422000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Power Assist:Electric:Control Module](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=i7&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** Either of these scenarios can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2024 BMW i7 is an exceptionally luxurious and technologically advanced electric vehicle, but prospective buyers should verify that all electronic campaigns and software updates have been performed. Pay close attention during a test drive to ensure the braking system, automated door switches, and driver assistance sensors operate without unexpected interventions.
