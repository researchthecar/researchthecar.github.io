# 2017 BMW M6 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

Shopping for a high-performance luxury grand tourer like the 2017 BMW M6 means balancing exhilarating twin-turbocharged power with the complexities of advanced German engineering. While formal complaint data is sparse for this model year, prospective buyers should still carefully evaluate standard pre-purchase inspection routines, especially concerning body seals and electrical compartments.

---

## Engine

### 4.4L Twin-Turbo V8 (S63)

The 4.4-liter twin-turbocharged V8 engine delivers an impressive 560 horsepower and 500 lb-ft of torque (up to 600 hp with the Competition package). It provides ferocious acceleration and high-revving performance, though proper maintenance and strict adherence to service intervals are vital for long-term reliability.

## Transmission

### 7-Speed Dual-Clutch Transmission (DCT)

The 7-speed dual-clutch transmission offers lightning-fast gear changes and multiple shift logic settings tailored for both daily driving and track use. It is robust when maintained properly, but buyers should test engagement smoothness during low-speed maneuvers.

## Brakes

### High-Mount Brake Light and Trim

Exterior trim and lighting assemblies generally hold up well, though owners should inspect seals and fasteners during routine maintenance.

**Loose Rear Third Brake Light and Water Leak**
- **Symptoms:** The rear third brake light can become loose and detach, allowing water to leak into the roof structure, seat belt mechanisms, and rear under-seat battery compartment.
- **Solution:** Inspect the rear brake light mounting nuts behind interior trim panels, re-secure or replace damaged hardware, and check affected interior areas for moisture or mold.

---

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V094000: Exterior Lighting:Brake Lights](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=M6&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** The third brake light may become loose, possibly allowing the light to detach from the vehicle. If the third brake light detaches from the vehicle, it would no longer provide the required lighting to following traffic, or it may become a road hazard, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** BMW will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the third brake light and properly secure it with new fasteners. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V914000: Air Bags](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=M6&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** The affected vehicles have seat- mounted side air bag inflator initiators that may fail to ignite during a crash. If the air bag inflator initiator fails to ignite, the seat-mounted air bag will not deploy, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** BMW will notify owners, and dealers will replace the air bag modules, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2017 BMW M6 is an elite performance machine offering world-class power and luxury, backed by limited reported owner complaints. Buyers should prioritize a thorough mechanical inspection, checking high-stress powertrain components as well as reviewing interior and trunk areas for any signs of past water intrusion.
