# 2018 BMW 440i xDrive Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2018 BMW 440i xDrive offers a compelling blend of luxury, striking design, and strong performance, particularly anchored by its renowned turbocharged inline-six engine. While generally regarded as a well-engineered and engaging daily driver, prospective buyers should review potential owner-reported concerns regarding cooling systems, seat belts, and occasional electrical anomalies.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4 (430i)

A 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine producing around 248 horsepower, offering strong efficiency and punchy everyday responsiveness.

### 3.0L Turbocharged Inline-6 (440i)

A smooth 3.0-liter turbocharged inline-six engine delivering approximately 320 horsepower and 330 lb-ft of torque, praised for strong performance and overall mechanical robustness.

### Engine Cooling System

The cooling system manages engine temperatures for the turbocharged powertrains, though minor leaks and localized hose or pump concerns have been noted in owner feedback.

**Coolant Leaks and Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have reported unexpected coolant leaks under the vehicle, illuminated coolant warning lights, and occasional engine stalling or loss of motive power while driving.
- **Solution:** Inspect coolant hoses, lines, and the water pump for leaks or degradation, and replace faulty components as necessary.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

An 8-speed ZF automatic transmission paired with xDrive all-wheel drive, widely recognized for quick, refined shifting and long-term durability.

### 6-Speed Manual Transmission

A traditional 6-speed manual gearbox available on select configurations, offering an engaging driver-focused experience.

## Safety

### Seat Belts

Safety restraints generally function securely, though occasional latch sensors and retractor mechanisms can require dealer attention.

**Seat Belt Retraction and Alert Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have noted instances where front seat belts fail to retract properly or secure as expected, accompanied by false seatbelt warning chimes while buckled.
- **Solution:** Have the seat belt buckle assembly and retractor mechanism inspected by a certified technician; replace faulty sensors or assemblies.

---

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V507000: Seat Belts:Front](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=440i&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** If the vehicle has an incompatible vehicle-sensitive locking mechanism, the seat belt may not restrain the seat occupant in the event of a crash, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** BMW will notify owners, and dealers will inspect and, if necessary replace, the front seat belt assemblies, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2018 BMW 440i xDrive is an exhilarating and generally well-built luxury sport vehicle, particularly shining when equipped with the robust inline-six powertrain. While most mechanical systems prove dependable, used-car buyers should thoroughly inspect the cooling system for potential leaks and verify proper operation of electronic safety features and seat belts during a pre-purchase inspection.
