# 2022 BMW M3 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2022 BMW M3 delivers high-performance driving dynamics powered by its twin-turbocharged inline-six engine. While generally robust, used-car buyers should review powertrain service histories carefully, as high-performance components can experience accelerated wear depending on prior usage.

---

## Engine

### 3.0L Twin-Turbo Inline-6 (S58)

The S58 3.0-liter twin-turbocharged inline-six produces between 473 and 503 horsepower depending on the standard or Competition trim. This powerhouse is generally robust, though buyers should watch for signs of hard driving or modified engine tuning.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Manual Transmission

The 6-speed manual transmission is available exclusively on the base rear-wheel-drive M3 model, offering an engaging analog driving experience.

**Clutch Slippage and Engagement Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may experience clutch slipping under acceleration and abnormal clutch pedal engagement.
- **Solution:** Inspect the clutch assembly and replace worn clutch components or pressure plates as necessary.

### 8-Speed M Steptronic Automatic Transmission

The 8-speed automatic transmission handles high torque loads efficiently and is paired with both Competition and xDrive all-wheel-drive variants.

## Summary Verdict

The 2022 BMW M3 is an exhilarating performance sedan, but its complex mechanical systems require careful pre-purchase inspection. Buyers should verify the condition of the clutch and powertrain to ensure the vehicle has not been abused.
