# 2023 BMW M3 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2023 BMW M3 delivers high-performance driving dynamics backed by robust engineering. While owner reports to NHTSA represent subjective experiences rather than proven manufacturing defects, prospective buyers should review available data to make an informed purchasing decision.

---

## Engine

### 3.0L Twin-Turbocharged Inline-6 (S58)

The S58 3.0-liter twin-turbocharged inline-6 engine produces between 473 and 503 horsepower depending on the variant. It is known for exceptional power delivery and robust build quality in standard driving conditions.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed M Steptronic Automatic Transmission

The 8-speed automatic transmission provides rapid and precise gear shifts tailored to high-performance applications. It generally offers dependable daily operation and smooth power transfer.

### 6-Speed Manual Transmission

The traditional 6-speed manual transmission caters to purists seeking direct mechanical engagement. It remains a reliable option when properly maintained.

---

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V118000: Electrical System:Body Control Module:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=M3&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** Doors that do not lock as intended while the vehicle is in motion may open during a crash, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the ECU software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2023 BMW M3 stands as a formidable high-performance sports sedan with strong powertrain options. Buyers should conduct a standard pre-purchase inspection to verify overall vehicle condition.
