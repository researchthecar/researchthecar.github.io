# 2017 BMW M2

### Overview
The 2017 BMW M2, a performance-oriented compact luxury coupe, is generally regarded as a robust and reliable vehicle, especially considering its high-performance nature. Built on a solid platform and utilizing BMW's well-engineered M-division components, it offers an engaging driving experience with a commendable track record for durability. While no car is entirely immune to issues, the M2's common problems tend to be relatively minor and often related to specific components rather than widespread systemic failures. Owners generally report satisfaction with its mechanical integrity, with most maintenance falling into expected service intervals for a sports car. This analysis focuses on documented technical issues beyond routine maintenance.

### Summary Verdict
The 2017 BMW M2 demonstrates strong technical reliability, with its core mechanical components (engine and transmission) showing a remarkable absence of widespread issues. The performance-oriented N55 engine and the available M-DCT or manual transmissions are robust. Minor concerns are primarily limited to occasional infotainment glitches and potential intermittent battery drain, which are not uncommon in modern vehicles with complex electronics. These issues are generally addressable through software updates or targeted diagnostics. Overall, the M2 is a well-built performance car with a reliability record that aligns with expectations for its class and performance focus, making it a solid choice for enthusiasts prioritizing driving dynamics and mechanical integrity.
