# 2017 BMW M2 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2017 BMW M2, buyers are getting a highly praised performance coupe known for its engaging driving dynamics and potent inline-six engine. While generally robust, used-car buyers should review owner-reported feedback regarding electronics, visibility, and specific engine peripherals.

---

## Engine

### 3.0L Turbocharged Inline-6 (N55)

The 2017 BMW M2 features a 3.0-liter turbocharged inline-six engine (N55) producing 365 horsepower and 343 lb-ft of torque. It delivers exhilarating performance, though buyers should watch for minor peripheral component issues like the factory charge pipe.

**Charge Pipe Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The factory plastic charge pipe connecting the intercooler to the throttle body can crack or disconnect under boost, leading to a sudden loss of power and a loud under-hood hiss.
- **Solution:** Replace the failure-prone factory plastic pipe with an aftermarket metal upgrade kit.

## Transmission

### 7-Speed Dual-Clutch Automatic

The quick-shifting 7-speed dual-clutch transmission offers lightning-fast gear changes and paddle shifters for an engaging track-ready experience. It requires regular fluid maintenance to ensure long-term reliability.

### 6-Speed Manual

The traditional 6-speed manual transmission provides a direct, connected driving feel favored by enthusiasts. It is mechanically robust when paired with proper clutch maintenance.

## Electrical

### Exterior Lighting and Instrument Illumination

Electrical complaints for this model primarily center around visibility concerns regarding the dashboard instrument cluster under specific lighting conditions.

**Daytime Instrument Cluster Readability**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers report that the analog gauge markings on the instrument cluster can be difficult to read during daylight, in tunnels, or under heavy shade due to a lack of proper daytime backlighting.
- **Solution:** Ensure headlight settings are appropriately adjusted, or verify instrument cluster illumination settings via the iDrive system.
