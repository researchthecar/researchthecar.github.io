# 2016 BMW M2 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a pre-owned 2016 BMW M2, buyers will find a high-performance sports coupe built to deliver engaging driving dynamics. While traditional mechanical complaints in the available data are minimal, prospective owners should still evaluate specific cabin visibility details and general wear items during a pre-purchase inspection.

---

## Engine

### 3.0L Turbocharged Inline-6 (N55)

The 2016 BMW M2 features a 3.0-liter turbocharged inline-6 engine (N55) producing approximately 365 horsepower and 343 lb-ft of torque. This powerplant delivers strong performance and is generally well-regarded, though proper maintenance history is essential for high-performance turbocharged applications.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Manual Transmission

The traditional 6-speed manual transmission offers a direct, engaging shifting experience for enthusiasts. Buyers should inspect the clutch condition and overall shifting smoothness during a test drive.

### 7-Speed Dual-Clutch Transmission (DCT)

The 7-speed dual-clutch automatic transmission provides lightning-fast gear changes and everyday convenience. Buyers should ensure smooth operation in stop-and-go traffic as part of a thorough inspection.

## Electrical

### Instrument Cluster

Cabin electrical systems are generally robust, though some owners report visibility challenges with specific gauge face designs.

**Poor Gauge Contrast and Readability**
- **Symptoms:** Instrument cluster numbers and gauges can become difficult to read or illegible under certain daytime lighting conditions, dusk, dawn, or when wearing sunglasses.
- **Solution:** Be aware of the contrast limitation during daytime test drives; updating or modifying instrument cluster settings may offer slight improvements depending on available aftermarket solutions.

---

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V653000: Structure:Frame And Members](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=M2&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** Reuse of the existing bolts may cause the bolts to loosen. Loose rear sub-frame bolts may adversely affect vehicle handling and control, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** BMW will notify owners, and dealers will replace the rear sub-frame bolts with new bolts, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2016 BMW M2 remains a compelling option for driving enthusiasts seeking sharp handling and potent inline-six performance. Reviewers and owners report very few recurring mechanical complaints, though checking visibility of the instrument cluster and verifying a complete service history will help ensure a satisfying ownership experience.
