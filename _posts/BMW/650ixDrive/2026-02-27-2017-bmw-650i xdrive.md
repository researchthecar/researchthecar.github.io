# 2017 BMW 650i xDrive

### Overview
The 2017 BMW 650i xDrive, a luxurious grand tourer, is generally regarded as a reliable vehicle, especially considering its performance-oriented nature. Powered by a potent V8 engine and featuring the xDrive all-wheel-drive system, it offers a compelling blend of power and refinement. While most owners report a positive ownership experience, like any high-performance vehicle, certain components may require attention over time. This analysis focuses on documented technical issues beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential areas of concern for prospective and current owners.

---


## Engine

### 4.4L Twin-Turbo V8 (N63TU2)
**Turbocharger Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Reduced engine power, increased oil consumption, whistling noises from the engine bay.
- **Solution:** Inspection and potential replacement of turbochargers and associated lines.
- **Cost:** $3,000 - $6,000

**Ignition Coil Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Misfires, rough idling, check engine light illumination.
- **Solution:** Replacement of faulty ignition coils and spark plugs.
- **Cost:** $400 - $800

**Fuel Injector Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Rough idle, poor fuel economy, engine misfires, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and replacement of faulty fuel injectors.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1,500

## Suspension

### Adaptive Drive System
**Active Roll Stabilization Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** Increased body roll during cornering, warning lights on the dashboard related to suspension.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and repair or replacement of active anti-roll bars and related sensors.
- **Cost:** $2,000 - $5,000

### Summary Verdict
The 2017 BMW 650i xDrive presents a generally strong reliability record for a high-performance luxury coupe. The 4.4L twin-turbo V8 engine, while powerful, is the primary area where owners might encounter specific technical issues, particularly concerning the turbochargers, ignition coils, and fuel injectors. The robust ZF 8-speed automatic transmission and the xDrive system are typically very reliable. Potential electrical gremlins, such as iDrive system glitches and battery drain, are also noted. Suspension components, especially the advanced adaptive systems, can be costly to repair if issues arise. Overall, the 650i xDrive offers a rewarding driving experience, but owners should be prepared for potentially higher maintenance and repair costs associated with its advanced technology and performance capabilities.
