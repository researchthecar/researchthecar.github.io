# 2018 BMW 650i xDrive Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used luxury cruiser, understanding common owner complaints helps set realistic expectations for maintenance and upkeep. While owner reports do not prove factory defects, they highlight potential areas that may require attention during your inspection.

---

## Engine

### 3.0L Turbocharged Inline-6 (640i)

This 3.0-liter turbocharged inline-six engine produces approximately 335 horsepower and provides smooth, responsive power for everyday driving. It is generally regarded as a robust powertrain option within the lineup when properly maintained.

### 4.4L Twin-Turbocharged V8 (650i)

The 4.4-liter twin-turbocharged V8 delivers a robust 445 horsepower and strong torque for effortless highway passing. Buyers should anticipate higher routine maintenance and fuel consumption compared to the inline-six variants.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Sport Automatic Transmission

This 8-speed automatic transmission comes paired with all-wheel drive and offers quick, seamless gear changes. It is widely praised for its refinement and durability in normal operating conditions.

## Interior

### Panoramic Sunroof Shade

Interior convenience features, including the sunroof and sunshade mechanisms, should be thoroughly tested during a pre-purchase inspection.

**Sunroof Curtain Sagging**
- **Symptoms:** The panoramic sunroof shade or curtain fails to retract properly, sagging down and obstructing rear visibility or falling into the cabin area.
- **Solution:** Inspect the sunshade track mechanism and replace the sagging curtain assembly as needed.

## Safety

### Passenger Air Bag Sensor

Airbag and occupant detection systems are critical safety features that require prompt diagnosis if warning lights appear.

**Passenger Airbag Warning Light**
- **Symptoms:** The passenger-side airbag malfunction or restraint warning light illuminates unexpectedly on the dashboard without an actual occupant in the seat.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified technician diagnose the seat occupancy sensor mat and replace the faulty component if necessary.
- **Cost:** $2,600 - $3,000
