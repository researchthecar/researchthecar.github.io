# 2016 BMW 535d Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2016 BMW 535d combines luxury, performance, and strong fuel economy thanks to its diesel powerplant. While overall owner feedback indicates a solid driving experience, used-car buyers should carefully evaluate powertrain condition, maintenance history, and auxiliary systems.

---

## Engine

### 3.0L TwinPower Turbo Diesel Inline-6 (255 hp / 413 lb-ft)

This 3.0-liter turbocharged diesel inline-6 produces 255 horsepower and 413 lb-ft of torque, offering robust low-end grunt and excellent highway efficiency. Buyers should verify meticulous maintenance, especially regarding emissions equipment and fuel systems.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed ZF Automatic Transmission

An 8-speed automatic transmission handles power delivery smoothly and efficiently across rear-wheel-drive and xDrive configurations. It is generally robust when fluid changes are maintained on schedule.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V755000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Exhaust System:Emission Control:Gas Recirculation Valve (Egr Valve)](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=535d&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** The melting intake manifold can increase the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** BMW will notify owners, and dealers will inspect and replace the EGR cooler as necessary. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V907000: Fuel System, Diesel](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=535d&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** The EGR cooler may leak internally, causing coolant to mix with diesel engine soot, which could result in smoldering particles and a melting intake manifold. A melting intake manifold increases the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the EGR cooler and inspect the intake manifold, replacing it as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V586000: Fuel System, Diesel:Delivery:Fuel Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=535d&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** Fuel pump failure can cause an engine stall while driving, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the high-pressure fuel pump, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V914000: Air Bags](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=535d&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** The affected vehicles have seat- mounted side air bag inflator initiators that may fail to ignite during a crash. If the air bag inflator initiator fails to ignite, the seat-mounted air bag will not deploy, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** BMW will notify owners, and dealers will replace the air bag modules, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 15V718000: Child Seat](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=535d&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles may fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 225, "Child Restraint Anchorage Systems." If the child seat is not properly attached to the lower anchors, it can increase the child's risk of injury in the event of a crash.
- **What to do:** BMW will notify owners, and dealers will inspect and, if necessary, repair the inboard lower anchor on the left rear seat, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2016 BMW 535d is a sophisticated luxury sedan offering impressive torque and highway efficiency. Prospective buyers should prioritize models with comprehensive service records and have a specialist inspect the diesel emissions and powertrain components prior to purchase.
