# 2016 BMW X6 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2016 BMW X6 delivers a dynamic driving experience coupled with coupe-like styling, luxury features, and robust powertrain options. However, prospective buyers should review owner-reported complaints regarding electrical functions and mechanical driveline components to ensure a well-maintained vehicle.

---

## Engine

### 3.0L Turbocharged Inline-6

This 3.0-liter turbocharged inline-6 engine powers the sDrive35i and xDrive35i models, producing around 300 horsepower. It provides smooth everyday acceleration, though proper maintenance of ignition components is key to long-term reliability.

**Cylinder Misfire**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle runs rough and triggers the check engine warning light, with diagnostic scanners indicating a misfire in a specific cylinder.
- **Solution:** Diagnose the ignition coils and spark plugs, replacing faulty components as necessary.

### 4.4L Twin-Turbocharged V8

The 4.4-liter twin-turbo V8 powers the xDrive50i and high-performance X6 M models, delivering robust output ranging from 445 up to 567 horsepower. While incredibly fast, this high-output engine requires diligent maintenance and close attention to cooling and oil systems.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

Paired across all variants, this 8-speed automatic transmission delivers quick, refined shifts and contributes to efficient highway cruising.

## Suspension

### Air Suspension System

The optional air suspension delivers superior ride comfort but can incur costly repairs if air struts or compressors degrade over time.

**Air Suspension Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle's air suspension system may fail, leading to an uneven ride height or complete system collapse shortly after warranty periods expire.
- **Solution:** Replace failing air springs, compressors, or associated control lines.
- **Cost:** Low 4 figure cost

## Steering

### Power Steering System

Issues related to electronic power steering assist and steering shaft feedback have been noted by some owners.

**Steering Lockup and Vibration**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have reported that the steering wheel fails to function or turn upon starting the vehicle, or shakes violently when turned completely to one side.
- **Solution:** Inspect the electronic power steering rack, software modules, and steering components for failure.

### Turn Signal and Control Systems

electrical issues involving interior switches and driver aids can occasionally affect convenience features.

**Turn Signal Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle's turn signal system fails to operate when attempting to make turns.
- **Solution:** Inspect and replace the steering column switch cluster or faulty control modules.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V823000: Child Seat](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=X6&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** The lower anchor bars may become damaged over time when used with an ISOFIX-type, rigid-style connector, child restraint system. Damaged lower anchor bars may increase the child's risk of injury in the event of a crash.
- **What to do:** BMW will notify owners, and dealers will weld a reinforcing bracket to the lower anchor bars and the vehicle body, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2016 BMW X6 is an engaging luxury crossover that blends high-end performance with striking design. While major mechanical failures are relatively uncommon, buyers should carefully inspect the vehicle's driveline, air suspension, and electrical systems before purchase. Securing a comprehensive pre-purchase inspection from a qualified technician is strongly recommended.
