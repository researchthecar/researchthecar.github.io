# 2017 BMW X6 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

Shopping for a used 2017 BMW X6 means stepping into a sleek, coupe-inspired luxury crossover with plenty of performance flair. While owner-reported complaints are relatively sparse for this model year, prospective buyers should still carefully evaluate major powertrain options and verify vehicle history prior to purchase.

---

## Engine

### 3.0L Turbocharged Inline-6 (sDrive35i / xDrive35i)

This 3.0-liter turbocharged inline-6 produces approximately 300 horsepower and 300 lb-ft of torque, offering brisk acceleration for daily driving. It is generally regarded as a smooth and capable powerplant for the X6 lineup when properly maintained.

### 4.4L Twin-Turbocharged V8 (xDrive50i)

The 4.4-liter twin-turbo V8 churns out roughly 445 horsepower and 480 lb-ft of torque for robust, high-performance capability. Buyers looking at this V8 option should prioritize thorough pre-purchase inspections and meticulous service records.

### 4.4L Twin-Turbocharged V8 (X6 M)

Reserved for the high-output X6 M variant, this aggressive 4.4-liter twin-turbo V8 pumps out an impressive 567 horsepower. It delivers thrilling track-ready performance but demands vigilant maintenance from its owners.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

Paired across the model lineup, this 8-speed automatic transmission provides smooth and responsive shifting capabilities. It is widely praised for its refinement and complements both inline-6 and V8 engine configurations effectively.

## Safety

### Airbags

Airbag system components generally operate reliably, though occasional electronic or sensor alerts can occur.

**Airbag Warning Light Illumination**
- **Symptoms:** The check airbag and seat belt warning lights illuminate upon starting the vehicle and stay on until the engine is turned off.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified technician scan the supplemental restraint system (SRS) module and inspect seat belt tensioners or wiring harnesses for faults.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V823000: Child Seat](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=X6&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** The lower anchor bars may become damaged over time when used with an ISOFIX-type, rigid-style connector, child restraint system. Damaged lower anchor bars may increase the child's risk of injury in the event of a crash.
- **What to do:** BMW will notify owners, and dealers will weld a reinforcing bracket to the lower anchor bars and the vehicle body, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V327000: Equipment:Other:Labels](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=X6&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** The vehicles are equipped with 21-inch tires, however the Tire Information Label states that the vehicles are equipped with 20-inch tires. As such, these vehicles fail to conform to Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 110, "Tire Selection and Rims." The incorrect tire size on the label does not comply with FMVSS number 110.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2017 BMW X6 offers striking styling and dynamic powertrain choices, backed by a generally manageable owner complaint history for this model year. Used-car buyers should ensure a comprehensive mechanical inspection is completed, paying close attention to electronics and engine health before finalizing a deal.
