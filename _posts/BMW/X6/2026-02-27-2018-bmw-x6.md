# 2018 BMW X6 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2018 BMW X6, buyers will find a stylish coupe-SUV offering robust performance and upscale luxury. While owner feedback is generally limited, prospective purchasers should remain mindful of potential mechanical and electrical quirks reported to federal safety regulators. A thorough pre-purchase inspection by a qualified technician is always recommended to ensure all systems operate properly.

---

## Engine

### 3.0L Turbocharged Inline-6

This 3.0-liter turbocharged inline-six engine produces approximately 300 horsepower and 300 lb-ft of torque, offering brisk acceleration for daily driving. It pairs smooth power delivery with reasonable efficiency for a luxury crossover, though regular maintenance is essential to ensure long-term reliability.

### 4.4L Twin-Turbocharged V8

The potent 4.4-liter twin-turbo V8 delivers robust performance with outputs exceeding 440 horsepower, tailored for enthusiasts seeking high-end acceleration. Buyers should anticipate higher maintenance demands and fuel consumption compared to the inline-six option.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 8-speed automatic transmission provides rapid, smooth gear changes and complements both available engine choices across various trim levels. It is generally robust, though proper fluid maintenance is critical for longevity.

### Gear Shifter Mechanism

Components related to gear selection and transmission operation.

**Unexpected Shifting to Park**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may unexpectedly shift into PARK while operating in DRIVE without driver input, creating a severe safety hazard in traffic.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified technician inspect the shifter assembly and transmission control modules for electronic or mechanical faults.

## Steering

### Front Suspension and Steering

Steering linkage and front assembly parts.

**Front End Instability**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may experience front-end instability, handling wandering, or general suspension looseness during normal operation.
- **Solution:** Inspect steering components, control arm bushings, and alignment settings for premature wear or damage.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V614000: Wheels:Lugs/Nuts/Bolts/Studs](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=X6&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** The wheel bolts on these vehicles may not have been properly tightened. The improperly tightened wheel bolts may loosen and break, affecting vehicle handling and control and increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** BMW will notify owners, and dealers will replace and tighten all of the wheel bolts, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V439000: Suspension:Front:Control Arm:Lower Arm](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=X6&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** The pivot bolt on the front lower-left control arm may not have been properly hardened, which may cause the control arm to break. If the lower control arm breaks, vehicle handling and control can be affected, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** BMW will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the lower-left control arms and replace if necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V823000: Child Seat](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=X6&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** The lower anchor bars may become damaged over time when used with an ISOFIX-type, rigid-style connector, child restraint system. Damaged lower anchor bars may increase the child's risk of injury in the event of a crash.
- **What to do:** BMW will notify owners, and dealers will weld a reinforcing bracket to the lower anchor bars and the vehicle body, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V727000: Suspension](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=X6&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** Bolts that secure the front suspension may not have been properly tightened. Loose bolts can affect the performance of the suspension, thereby increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** BMW will notify owners, and dealers will inspect and tighten the wishbone suspension bolts, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2018 BMW X6 is an engaging luxury crossover that blends athletic styling with strong powertrain options. While available complaint reports highlight isolated concerns regarding unexpected gear shifts and front-end stability, the overall volume of owner grievances is low. Buyers should perform a comprehensive inspection focusing on the suspension and drivetrain before completing a purchase.
