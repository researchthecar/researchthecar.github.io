# 2022 BMW X6 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2022 BMW X6 offers a striking coupe-like roofline paired with strong performance and luxury amenities. As shoppers consider this stylish SUV on the used market, evaluating typical owner feedback and powertrain options helps ensure a confident purchase.

---

## Engine

### 3.0L Turbocharged Inline-6

This 3.0-liter turbocharged inline-six engine produces approximately 335 horsepower and 331 lb-ft of torque, powering sDrive40i and xDrive40i models. It is generally praised for its smooth power delivery and everyday drivability.

### 4.4L Twin-Turbocharged V8

The 4.4-liter twin-turbocharged V8 engine delivers robust performance with around 523 horsepower in M50i variants. This powerhouse offers exhilarating acceleration for buyers seeking maximum performance.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

This 8-speed automatic transmission is paired across the lineup, delivering quick and seamless shifts. It is widely regarded as a durable and refined unit in modern BMW applications.

### Auto-Start Powertrain Interaction

Suspension and low-speed powertrain interaction reports warrant attention during test drives.

**Forward Lurching at Low Speeds**
- **Symptoms:** When slowing down near 4-5 mph, the vehicle may engage the auto-start system and momentarily add power, causing an unexpected forward lurch.
- **Solution:** Consult a dealer or specialist regarding potential engine management software updates that address low-speed auto-start behavior.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

Brake system feedback remains limited, but physical inspection during a test drive is always recommended.

**Brake Design Concerns**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have reported concerns regarding brake performance feel and manufacturer claim determinations.
- **Solution:** Inspect brake pads, rotors, and fluid condition during a pre-purchase inspection to ensure proper stopping performance.

## Electrical

### Rear Sensor System

Owner reports highlight occasional gripes regarding driver-assistance sensor behavior.

**Rear Sensor Ineffectiveness**
- **Symptoms:** The rear sensor system may fail to alert the driver to pedestrians or cross-traffic while backing out of a parking space.
- **Solution:** Have the dealer or a qualified technician inspect the sensor alignment and software modules, noting that dealer troubleshooting may sometimes yield limited adjustments.

---

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V267000: Visibility:Sun/Moon Roof Assembly](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=X6&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 118, "Power-Operated Window Systems." A sunroof that closes without the key fob present inside the vehicle can increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will reprogram the roof function control unit, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2022 BMW X6 combines high-end luxury with potent engine options and sharp handling. While isolated owner complaints touch on driver-assistance quirks and low-speed powertrain behavior, a thorough pre-purchase inspection will help ensure you find a well-sorted example.
