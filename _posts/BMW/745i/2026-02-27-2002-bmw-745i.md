# 2002 BMW 745i

### Overview
The 2002 BMW 745i, a flagship luxury sedan, represented a significant technological leap for BMW, featuring advanced electronics and a powerful V8 engine. While offering a luxurious and dynamic driving experience, its complexity also introduced potential reliability concerns, particularly in its early production years. Owners often reported issues related to the sophisticated electronic systems, the advanced V8 engine, and the automatic transmission. Maintenance and repair costs can be substantial due to the specialized nature of the components and the need for expert diagnosis. Despite these potential drawbacks, for those who maintain them diligently, the 745i can still offer a rewarding ownership experience.

---


## Engine

### 4.4L V8 (N62B44)
**Cooling System Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Coolant loss, overheating, visible leaks from hoses, water pump, or radiator.
- **Solution:** Replacement of leaking components such as the water pump, thermostat housing, or radiator.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1200

**Valve Stem Seals**
- **Symptoms:** Blue smoke on startup or when decelerating, increased oil consumption.
- **Solution:** Replacement of valve stem seals, often requiring cylinder head removal.
- **Cost:** $2500 - $4500

**Oil Pan Gasket Leak**
- **Symptoms:** Oil leaks visible from the bottom of the engine.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the oil pan gasket.
- **Cost:** $400 - $800


## Transmission

### 6-speed Automatic (GM 5L40-E / ZF 6HP26)
**Transmission Fluid Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Leaking fluid from the transmission pan or cooler lines, rough shifting, slipping gears.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of leaking seals, pan gasket, or cooler lines.
- **Cost:** $300 - $700

**Mechatronic Unit Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Erratic shifting, transmission warning lights, failure to engage gears.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of the mechatronic unit.
- **Cost:** $1500 - $3000

**Torque Converter Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Shuddering at low speeds, slipping, delayed engagement.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the torque converter.
- **Cost:** $1000 - $2000


## Suspension

### Adaptive Drive System
**Active Roll Stabilization (ARS) Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Fluid leaks from hydraulic lines or actuators, warning lights on the dashboard, reduced handling stability.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of leaking hydraulic lines, pumps, or actuators.
- **Cost:** $800 - $2000

**Air Suspension Compressor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle sagging on one or more corners, compressor not running, suspension warning lights.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the air suspension compressor.
- **Cost:** $700 - $1500


## Electrical

### iDrive System
**Infotainment System Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Screen freezing, system reboots, unresponsive controls, navigation errors.
- **Solution:** Software updates, module reset, or replacement of the iDrive controller or head unit.
- **Cost:** $300 - $1500

**Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Dead battery after short periods of inactivity, electrical components malfunctioning.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and repair of parasitic draw from modules or faulty components.
- **Cost:** $200 - $600

**Sensor Failures (ABS, DSC)**
- **Symptoms:** ABS and DSC warning lights illuminated, loss of traction control or anti-lock braking.
- **Solution:** Replacement of faulty wheel speed sensors or ABS/DSC module.
- **Cost:** $300 - $800

## Interior

### Standard Interior
**Headliner Sagging**
- **Symptoms:** Fabric lining of the roof detaching.
- **Solution:** Re-gluing or replacement of the headliner.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600

**Heated Seat Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** One or both heated seats not working.
- **Solution:** Replacement of heating elements or seat control modules.
- **Cost:** $400 - $800

### Summary Verdict
The 2002 BMW 745i is a complex luxury vehicle with a higher propensity for electrical and mechanical issues compared to less technologically advanced cars. Key areas of concern include the V8 engine's cooling system and valve stem seals, the sophisticated automatic transmission, and the intricate iDrive electronic system. While suspension components like the Adaptive Drive can also present costly repairs, many standard wear items are typical for a vehicle of this size and age. Owners should be prepared for potentially high maintenance and repair costs, especially for issues related to the advanced electronics and powertrain. Regular, specialized maintenance is crucial for mitigating these risks.
