# 2004 BMW 745i

### Overview
The 2004 BMW 745i, a flagship luxury sedan, offers a potent combination of performance and advanced technology for its era. Powered by a robust V8 engine and featuring sophisticated electronic systems, it aimed to deliver a premium driving experience. However, like many complex luxury vehicles of this vintage, the 745i is known to have specific areas that require attention to maintain its reliability and performance. Owners should be aware of potential issues, particularly concerning the electronic systems, cooling system, and certain powertrain components, which can contribute to ownership costs. Regular and diligent maintenance is crucial for mitigating these potential problems and ensuring the longevity of this high-performance vehicle.

---


## Engine

### 4.4L V8 (N62B44)
**Valve Stem Seals**
- **Symptoms:** Blue smoke on startup or deceleration, oil consumption.
- **Solution:** Replacement of valve stem seals.
- **Cost:** $1,500 - $3,000

**Cooling System Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Overheating, coolant loss, visible leaks from hoses, water pump, or expansion tank.
- **Solution:** Replacement of faulty components (water pump, thermostat, hoses, expansion tank).
- **Cost:** $800 - $1,500

**Oil Pan Gasket Leak**
- **Symptoms:** Oil drips under the vehicle, oil smell.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the oil pan gasket.
- **Cost:** $600 - $1,000


## Transmission

### 6-speed Automatic (GM 5L40-E/GA6HP26Z)
**Transmission Fluid Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Rough shifting, slipping gears, low transmission fluid level.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of transmission seals, transmission flush, or rebuild.
- **Cost:** $1,000 - $4,000+

**Mechatronic Unit Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Erratic shifting, transmission warning lights, inability to select gears.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of the mechatronic unit.
- **Cost:** $1,200 - $2,500


## Suspension

### Adaptive Drive System
**Active Roll Stabilization (ARS) Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Clunking noises over bumps, reduced handling stability, fluid leaks from hydraulic lines.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of hydraulic lines, pumps, or actuators.
- **Cost:** $1,000 - $3,000+

**Air Suspension Compressor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle sagging on one side, 'air suspension malfunction' warning light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the air suspension compressor.
- **Cost:** $700 - $1,200


## Electrical

### iDrive System
**Infotainment System Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Screen freezing, system reboots, non-responsive controls.
- **Solution:** Software updates, module reset, or replacement of head unit.
- **Cost:** $500 - $2,000+


### Body Control Module (BCM)
**Intermittent Electrical Faults**
- **Symptoms:** Malfunctioning lights, power windows, door locks, or other accessories.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and potential replacement or reprogramming of the BCM.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1,500


### ABS/DSC Module
**ABS/DSC Module Failure**
- **Symptoms:** ABS, DSC, and brake warning lights illuminated, loss of ABS/DSC functionality.
- **Solution:** Replacement and programming of the ABS/DSC module.
- **Cost:** $1,000 - $2,000

### Summary Verdict
The 2004 BMW 745i is a sophisticated luxury sedan that, while offering a compelling driving experience, comes with potential reliability concerns typical of high-end German engineering from its era. Key areas to monitor include the engine's cooling system and valve stem seals, transmission fluid leaks and mechatronic unit issues, and various electrical components such as the iDrive system, BCM, and ABS/DSC module. The advanced suspension systems, while enhancing comfort and handling, can also be a source of costly repairs. Prospective buyers should budget for potential maintenance and repairs, as parts and labor for these complex systems can be significant. Despite these potential drawbacks, with diligent maintenance and prompt attention to emerging issues, the 745i can remain a rewarding vehicle to own.
