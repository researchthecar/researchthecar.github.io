# 2012 BMW 550i xDrive

### Overview
The 2012 BMW 550i xDrive, part of the F10 generation, is a luxury executive sedan known for its potent performance and sophisticated engineering. Powered by a robust V8 engine and featuring BMW's xDrive all-wheel-drive system, it offers a compelling blend of power, comfort, and all-weather capability. While generally well-regarded for its driving dynamics and premium interior, like any complex vehicle, it has specific areas that have seen more attention from owners regarding reliability. This analysis focuses on documented technical issues beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential concerns for owners and prospective buyers of this model year.

---


## Engine

### 4.4L Twin-Turbo V8 (N63B44O0)
**Turbocharger Oil Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Oil residue around turbochargers, potential for blue smoke from exhaust, reduced engine performance.
- **Solution:** Replacement of turbocharger seals and lines, potentially turbocharger replacement.
- **Cost:** $1,500 - $3,000

**Fuel Injector Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Rough idling, misfires, check engine light, difficulty starting.
- **Solution:** Replacement of faulty fuel injectors.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1,500

**Ignition Coil Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Engine misfires, rough idle, check engine light, reduced power.
- **Solution:** Replacement of faulty ignition coils.
- **Cost:** $400 - $800

### Summary Verdict
The 2012 BMW 550i xDrive offers a luxurious and powerful driving experience. Its primary reliability concerns tend to center around the potent 4.4L twin-turbo V8 engine, particularly regarding oil leaks from the turbochargers and issues with fuel injectors and ignition coils. The advanced suspension systems, while enhancing performance, can also be a source of costly repairs if components like the active roll stabilization fail. Electrical gremlins, such as iDrive system glitches and battery drain, are also noted. While the transmission and exterior/interior components are generally robust, potential buyers should be aware of these specific engine and electrical vulnerabilities and factor in potential maintenance costs.
