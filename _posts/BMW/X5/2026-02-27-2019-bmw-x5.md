# 2019 BMW X5 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2019 BMW X5 delivers strong performance, luxurious comfort, and advanced technology. However, prospective buyers should review owner complaints regarding electrical glitches, drivetrain concerns, and occasional engine or braking issues. While many owners enjoy a premium driving experience, checking service records and verifying completed safety campaigns is essential before purchasing a used model.

---

## Engine

### 3.0L Turbocharged Inline-6 (sDrive40i / xDrive40i)

A 3.0-liter turbocharged inline-6 engine producing roughly 335 horsepower, known for smooth power delivery and strong efficiency in the luxury midsize SUV segment.

**Oil Pump and Starter Concerns**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have reported low oil level warnings or potential oil pump failures, alongside starter motor issues that can lead to electrical failure and secondary damage to charging components.
- **Solution:** Have the vehicle inspected by a qualified technician for starter recalls, oil leaks, and oil pump operation.

### 4.4L Twin-Turbo V8 (xDrive50i)

A potent 4.4-liter twin-turbocharged V8 engine pushing approximately 456 horsepower, offering brisk acceleration for demanding drivers.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

An 8-speed ZF automatic transmission paired with rear-wheel or intelligent all-wheel drive, praised for quick and seamless gear changes.

**Unintended Neutral Shift**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicles may unexpectedly shift into neutral while driving or coming to a stop, occasionally accompanied by powertrain warnings.
- **Solution:** Diagnostic scanning by a BMW specialist is recommended to check for software updates or internal transmission control faults.

### xDrive Transfer Case

Distributes torque between front and rear axles for enhanced all-weather traction.

**Transfer Case Jutter**
- **Symptoms:** Jerking, binding, or shuddering sensations when executing low-speed turns or reversing.
- **Solution:** A transfer case fluid flush or replacement may be required, depending on wear levels.

## Electrical

### Shark Fin Antenna & Telematics

Manages communication, infotainment, and internal bus networks; vulnerabilities can develop if exterior moisture seals degrade.

**Water Intrusion and Corrosion**
- **Symptoms:** Failure of the emergency call system, Bluetooth connectivity loss, and electrical malfunctions caused by rainwater leaking through defective roof antenna seals.
- **Solution:** Inspect the roof antenna seal for cracks or leaks and repair or replace damaged telematics modules.

## Exterior

### Panoramic Sunroof

Provides cabin light and open-air views but requires close inspection for structural or seal integrity.

**Spontaneous Glass Shattering**
- **Symptoms:** The glass panel of the panoramic sunroof may crack or shatter unexpectedly while driving at highway speeds.
- **Solution:** Replace the damaged glass assembly and inspect the surrounding frame for stress points.

## Safety

### Forward Collision Avoidance

Relies on cameras and sensors to monitor traffic hazards and apply emergency braking when potential collisions are detected.

**Unwanted Automatic Braking**
- **Symptoms:** The automatic emergency braking system may occasionally trigger sudden stops when no obstacles are present in the roadway.
- **Solution:** Check for software updates for camera and radar modules at an authorized service center.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V918000: Equipment:Other:Labels](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=X5&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 208, "Occupant crash protection." Without a label that shows the proper restraint usage guidelines, the occupants may have an increased risk of injury in the event of a crash.
- **What to do:** BMW notified owners, and dealers will inspect the driver and passenger sun visor, replacing the visors as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V513000: Fuel System, Other:Delivery:Fuel Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=X5&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** The high pressure fuel pump may not have been properly attached to the engine, which may cause the fuel pump flange to crack, resulting in a fuel leak. A fuel leak in the presence of an ignition source can increase the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** BMW will notify owners, and dealers will replace the high pressure fuel pumps, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V732000: Electrical System](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=X5&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** The capacitors within the Body Domain Controller (BDC) may short circuit and overheat. If the BDC short circuits, other nearby components may also overheat, increasing the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** BMW dealers will replace the BDC control unit, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V096000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=X5&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** When shifting into Reverse, a small portion of the rearview image may be obscured, or the screen may not illuminate. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 111, "Rear Visibility." If the driver relies on only the rearview camera image, the missing or reduced rearview image view.
- **What to do:** BMW will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the rearview camera software and update it if necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V541000: Wheels:Lugs/Nuts/Bolts/Studs](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=X5&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** Loose bolts may allow a wheel to detach from the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** BMW will notify owners, and dealers will replace and tighten the left side wheel bolts, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V684000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=X5&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 111, "Rearview Mirrors." The lack of an image in the back-up camera display increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** BMW will notify owners, and dealers will update the back-up camera software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V434000: Steering](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=X5&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** The steering column shaft bearing plate may not have been properly attached to the vehicle, allowing it to loosen. A loose bearing plate can affect the vehicle's handling and control, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** BMW will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the steering column shaft bearing plate and properly attach or replace it, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V062000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=X5&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** The rotor and shaft within the integrated brake system may not have been welded properly and could separate during a hard braking event, possibly resulting in a loss of the Antilock Brake System (ABS) and braking assistance. A loss of ABS and a reduction in braking assistance can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** BMW will notify owners, and dealers will replace the integrated brake system, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2019 BMW X5 provides a compelling mix of luxury, advanced features, and powerful engine options, but buyers should approach with informed caution. Potential issues involving electrical water leaks from the roof antenna, transfer case wear, and sporadic sensor alerts mean a thorough pre-purchase inspection is vital. Reviewing service history and ensuring all open recalls have been addressed will help secure a more reliable ownership experience.
