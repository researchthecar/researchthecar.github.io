# 2017 BMW X5 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2017 BMW X5 offers a luxurious interior, composed handling, and a range of robust engine choices. However, used car buyers should approach this model with awareness of potential mechanical and electrical complexities. While it delivers an engaging driving experience, maintenance costs can be high, and owners have reported various issues spanning fuel systems, cooling, and electronic components.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4 Hybrid (sDrive40e / xDrive40e)

A plug-in hybrid option pairing a 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine with an electric motor, producing around 308 combined horsepower. It offers efficiency but introduces added electrical complexity for used buyers.

**Loss of Motive Power and Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers experience sudden drivetrain malfunction warnings, which can lead to unexpected engine shutdown while driving at highway or street speeds.
- **Solution:** Diagnostic evaluation by a qualified technician; may require software updates or replacement of failing electrical and hybrid powertrain components.

### 3.0L Turbocharged Inline-6 (sDrive35i / xDrive35i)

A smooth and widely popular 3.0-liter turbocharged inline-6 engine delivering 300 horsepower. It provides a balanced blend of everyday performance and refinement.

**Fuel Pump and Delivery Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicles may experience engine stalling, difficulty starting, or fuel odors originating from the fuel pump and tank area.
- **Solution:** Inspect and replace faulty fuel pumps, pressure sensors, or leaking fuel tank components.

**Cooling System and Electrical Failures**
- **Symptoms:** Engine overheating accompanied by warning lights, electrical accessory failure, or water pump malfunction due to coolant or liquid exposure.
- **Solution:** Replace the failing water pump, corroded radiator fan assemblies, and damaged wiring connectors.

### 3.0L Turbocharged Diesel Inline-6 (xDrive35d)

A torque-rich diesel inline-6 engine producing 255 horsepower and 413 lb-ft of torque, appreciated for strong highway fuel economy but requiring attentive emissions system maintenance.

### 4.4L Twin-Turbocharged V8 (xDrive50i)

A powerful V8 engine putting out 445 horsepower for brisk acceleration, though it demands rigorous upkeep and is prone to higher overall operating costs.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

A refined 8-speed automatic transmission paired across rear-wheel and all-wheel-drive configurations, known for smooth and prompt gear changes.

## Brakes

### Brake System

Provides strong stopping power, though some owners have experienced electronic parking brake glitches and premature pad or rotor wear.

**Electronic Parking Brake and Pedal Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Unexpected electronic parking brake engagement while coming to a stop, unusually heavy pedal resistance, or brake squeaking and vehicle vibration.
- **Solution:** Inspect brake pads, rotors, and electronic parking brake actuators; service or replace worn components.

## Safety

### Seat Belts and Airbags

Equipped with comprehensive active and passive safety features, though occasional restraint system warnings require prompt professional diagnosis.

**Restraint System Warning and Buckle Faults**
- **Symptoms:** Illumination of restraint system warning lights, intermittent airbag messages, or loose seat belt buckles.
- **Solution:** Diagnose restraint system fault codes at a service center and replace malfunctioning seat belt tensioners or sensors.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V755000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Exhaust System:Emission Control:Gas Recirculation Valve (Egr Valve)](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=X5&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** The melting intake manifold can increase the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** BMW will notify owners, and dealers will inspect and replace the EGR cooler as necessary. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V907000: Fuel System, Diesel](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=X5&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** The EGR cooler may leak internally, causing coolant to mix with diesel engine soot, which could result in smoldering particles and a melting intake manifold. A melting intake manifold increases the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the EGR cooler and inspect the intake manifold, replacing it as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V586000: Fuel System, Diesel:Delivery:Fuel Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=X5&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** Fuel pump failure can cause an engine stall while driving, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the high-pressure fuel pump, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V189000: Air Bags:Frontal:Passenger Side:Inflator Module](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=X5&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** These vehicles are equipped with passenger frontal air bag modules using certain air bag inflators that may not vent properly in the event of a crash necessitating deployment of the passenger frontal air bag. If the inflator cannot vent properly, the inflator may rupture, resulting in metal fragments striking the vehicle occupants and causing serious injury.
- **What to do:** BMW has notified owners, and dealers will replace the air bag modules, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V327000: Equipment:Other:Labels](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=X5&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** The vehicles are equipped with 21-inch tires, however the Tire Information Label states that the vehicles are equipped with 20-inch tires. As such, these vehicles fail to conform to Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 110, "Tire Selection and Rims." The incorrect tire size on the label does not comply with FMVSS number 110.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 24V608000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Cooling System:Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=X5&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** An improperly sealed electrical connector on the water pump may be exposed to water and short circuit. An electrical short increases the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the water pump and plug connector as necessary, and install a protective shield, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2017 BMW X5 remains a compelling luxury midsize SUV choice for buyers prioritizing performance, comfort, and sophisticated styling. However, prospective owners should verify service history carefully, keeping in mind that electrical quirks, cooling system upkeep, and potential fuel system or hybrid drivetrain concerns can lead to unexpected repair bills.
