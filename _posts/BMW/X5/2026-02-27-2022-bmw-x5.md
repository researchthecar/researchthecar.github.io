# 2022 BMW X5 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2022 BMW X5 delivers strong luxury performance, advanced technology, and a refined ride. However, prospective buyers should review recurring owner complaints regarding drivetrain components, electrical glitches, and driver-assistance systems. Being an intricate luxury vehicle, proper maintenance history is essential.

---

## Engine

### 3.0L Turbocharged Inline-6 (sDrive40i / xDrive40i)

This 3.0-liter turbocharged inline-6 engine generates approximately 335 horsepower and 331 lb-ft of torque, offering brisk acceleration and smooth power delivery. It is generally considered robust and serves as the popular, reliable backbone of the X5 lineup.

### 3.0L Turbocharged Inline-6 Plug-In Hybrid (xDrive45e)

Pairing a 3.0-liter turbocharged inline-6 with an electric motor, the xDrive45e plug-in hybrid produces a combined 389 horsepower and 443 lb-ft of torque while offering electric-only driving range. While efficient and powerful, the complex hybrid cooling and auxiliary climate systems require careful inspection.

**Coolant Leak and Internal A/C Compressor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Low coolant warnings, potential overheating risks, and internal component contamination from a failing electric A/C compressor.
- **Solution:** Inspect cooling systems, replace leaking hoses, and swap out contaminated compressors and associated fluid lines if internal failure occurs.

### 4.4L Twin-Turbocharged V8 (M50i)

The potent 4.4-liter twin-turbo V8 churns out an impressive 523 horsepower and 553 lb-ft of torque, turning the X5 into a high-performance machine. While exhilarating, it demands strict adherence to maintenance schedules and can carry higher long-term running costs.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

Featuring a widely praised 8-speed automatic transmission paired with standard or optional xDrive all-wheel drive, this unit shifts seamlessly and responds quickly. It exhibits strong baseline mechanical durability across all engine configurations.

### Transfer Case and Differentials

The all-wheel-drive system relies on a transfer case and front differential to distribute power effectively, but some owners report premature binding and shuddering issues.

**Transfer Case Binding and Front Differential Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Jerking, shuddering, or locking up sensations at low speeds and during tight turns.
- **Solution:** Have the transfer case fluid serviced or replace the transfer case assembly and front differential if mechanical wear is advanced.

## Electrical

### Electronics and Shifter

Advanced cabin electronics and sensitive control modules govern much of the X5's interior functions, occasionally presenting software or moisture-intrusion quirks.

**Power Supply and Shifter Moisture Sensitivity**
- **Symptoms:** Unexpected vehicle power supply warning messages, loss of electrical power, or electronic shifters becoming unresponsive when exposed to liquid spills or moisture.
- **Solution:** Inspect battery connections, address charging system faults, and replace or shield moisture-damaged shifter modules.

## Exterior

### Sunroof

The large panoramic glass roof enhances cabin light and ventilation, though isolated reports of spontaneous shattering require mindful inspection.

**Spontaneous Sunroof Glass Shattering**
- **Symptoms:** The panoramic sunroof glass suddenly explodes or cracks outward while driving under normal road conditions without external impacts.
- **Solution:** Inspect sunroof assembly framing for structural stress points and replace shattered glass panels under warranty or insurance coverage.

## Safety

### Forward Collision Avoidance

Active safety suites provide robust collision mitigation, though occasional sensor false positives can trigger unprompted braking events.

**Unexpected Automatic Braking**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle suddenly and unexpectedly applies the brakes on the highway or near intersections without any preceding traffic or obstacles.
- **Solution:** Have authorized service centers recalibrate collision sensors and check for software updates related to driver-assistance packages.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V267000: Visibility:Sun/Moon Roof Assembly](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=X5&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 118, "Power-Operated Window Systems." A sunroof that closes without the key fob present inside the vehicle can increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will reprogram the roof function control unit, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2022 BMW X5 remains a compelling luxury SUV choice, blending comfort, prestige, and exceptional powertrain options. While major mechanical failures are relatively infrequent, prospective buyers should thoroughly inspect the vehicle's transfer case, test all driver-assistance safety features, and review service records to ensure lasting peace of mind.
