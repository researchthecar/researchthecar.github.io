# 2017 BMW 440i xDrive Gran Coupe Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used BMW 440i xDrive Gran Coupe, buyers can generally expect a powerful and engaging driving experience paired with luxury appointments. While overall owner satisfaction is high, used car shoppers should keep an eye on potential owner-reported trouble spots such as engine cooling components and interior electrical systems.

---

## Engine

### 3.0L Turbocharged I6 Engine (B58)

The 3.0L turbocharged inline-6 engine (B58) produces approximately 320 horsepower and 330 lb-ft of torque, delivering robust acceleration and smooth power delivery. It is widely regarded by enthusiasts as a powerful and generally robust powerplant, though routine maintenance of cooling and housing components remains essential.

### Engine Cooling System

Owner complaints highlight potential vulnerabilities in plastic cooling components and oil filter housings, which can lead to unexpected coolant loss.

**Coolant Leak and Oil Filter Housing Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may notice low coolant warning messages on the instrument cluster, sweet-smelling fluid odors, or pooling coolant underneath the vehicle.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified technician inspect the thermostat, radiator hoses, and oil filter housing; replace any cracked plastic fittings or gaskets.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 8-speed automatic transmission features quick, seamless shifts and pairs standard with the xDrive all-wheel-drive system. It is known for strong reliability under normal operating conditions, provided fluid changes are maintained according to schedule.

## Safety

### Air Bag and Restraint System

Air bag system warnings and sensor faults can intermittently disable occupant detection features, requiring professional diagnostic checks.

**Passenger Seat Occupancy Sensor Warning**
- **Symptoms:** The airbag warning light illuminates intermittently, or a message indicating a passenger restraint system or seat occupancy failure appears on the dashboard.
- **Solution:** Diagnostic scanning at a dealership or specialist is necessary to isolate faulty seat occupancy sensors or wiring harness connections.

### Seat Belt Assembly

Issues involving seat belt anchoring and tensioner warnings have been reported by some vehicle owners.

**Seat Belt Latch and Tensioner Disengagement**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers report seat belts unexpectedly detaching from fixed anchor points or tensioner warning alerts triggering on the dashboard.
- **Solution:** Inspect seat belt anchor points, tensioners, and mounting hardware; replace any worn or compromised latch mechanisms.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V115000: Air Bags](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=440i&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** The affected vehicles have seat-mounted side air bag inflator initiators that may fail to ignite during a crash. If the air bag inflator initiator fails to ignite, the seat-mounted air bag will not deploy, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** BMW will notify owners, and dealers will replace the air bag modules, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.
