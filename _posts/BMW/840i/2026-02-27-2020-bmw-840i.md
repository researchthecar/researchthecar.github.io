# 2020 BMW 840i

### Overview
The 2020 BMW 840i, known for its luxurious design and potent performance, generally exhibits strong reliability, reflecting BMW's engineering prowess. Powered by a robust inline-six engine, it offers a compelling blend of power and refinement. While most owners report a positive ownership experience with minimal unexpected issues, like any complex luxury vehicle, certain components can occasionally present challenges. This analysis focuses on documented technical reliability concerns beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential areas of attention for owners and prospective buyers. The 840i's sophisticated systems, from its powertrain to its advanced electronics, are designed for durability, but understanding common, albeit infrequent, issues can aid in proactive maintenance and informed decision-making.

### Infotainment System
**Occasional Software Glitches**

- **Cost:** $0 - $200 (if dealer labor required)


### Battery Drain
**Intermittent Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Battery may discharge prematurely, leading to starting issues, often linked to parasitic draw from electronic modules.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis of parasitic draw, potential replacement of faulty module or battery.
- **Cost:** $300 - $800

### Summary Verdict
The 2020 BMW 840i demonstrates a high level of technical reliability, particularly concerning its core powertrain components. The 3.0L turbocharged inline-six engine and the 8-speed automatic transmission are robust and have proven to be dependable. The primary areas where minor issues have been noted are within the electrical system, specifically occasional software glitches in the infotainment system and, less commonly, intermittent battery drain issues. These are generally resolvable through software updates or diagnosis of parasitic draws. Other categories like suspension, exterior, and interior components show no widespread reliability concerns. Overall, the 840i offers a solid ownership experience with a low probability of significant mechanical failures outside of standard maintenance.
