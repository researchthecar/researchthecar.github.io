The 2019 BMW 750Li represents the epitome of luxury and performance in the BMW lineup. This vehicle offers a blend of powerful engines, advanced technology, and opulent comfort. The 750Li specifically is powered by a 4.4-liter TwinPower Turbo V8 engine, generating approximately 456 horsepower and 480 lb-ft of torque. Other engine options available in the 2019 7 Series range include a 3.0-liter inline-6 (740i/Li) and a plug-in hybrid variant (745e/Le). All engine options are paired with an 8-speed automatic transmission. Owners generally appreciate the 750Li for its smooth ride, powerful engine, and lavish interior. Common dislikes often center around the high maintenance costs associated with luxury vehicles and the complexity of the advanced technological features.

**Engine**

**4.4L TwinPower Turbo V8**
*   **Oil Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks are frequently reported, particularly around the valve cover gaskets and oil filter housing. Symptoms include visible oil drips under the car, a burning oil smell, and low oil levels.
    *   Solution: Replacing the faulty gaskets and/or the entire oil filter housing assembly.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Coolant Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaks originating from the water pump or coolant hoses. Symptoms include low coolant levels, overheating, and visible coolant puddles.
    *   Solution: Replacing the water pump and/or damaged coolant hoses.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $2000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**8-Speed Automatic (All Engines)**

*   **Rough Shifting**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported occasional rough or delayed shifts, particularly when shifting from lower to higher gears.
    *   Solution: A software update or, in more severe cases, replacement of valve body components or the entire transmission.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $6000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Air Suspension Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The air suspension system can experience issues such as compressor failure, leaks in the air struts, or problems with the ride height sensors. This can lead to a sagging vehicle, a rough ride, or error messages on the dashboard.
    *   Solution: Replacing the faulty compressor, air struts, or ride height sensors.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1000 - $4000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **iDrive System Malfunctions**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The iDrive infotainment system can occasionally experience glitches, freezes, or complete shutdowns. This can affect navigation, audio, climate control, and other vehicle functions.
    *   Solution: Restarting the iDrive system, performing a software update, or replacing the iDrive control unit.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 - $2000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Sensor Malfunctions**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Issues with various sensors, including parking sensors, blind spot monitoring sensors, and adaptive cruise control sensors. Symptoms can include false alarms, system failures, or error messages.
    *   Solution: Replacing the faulty sensor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Seat Control Module Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Problems with the seat control module can lead to malfunctioning power seats, including issues with adjustment, heating, or massage functions.
    *   Solution: Replacing or reprogramming the seat control module.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Adaptive Headlight Malfunctions**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Adaptive headlights may fail to adjust properly or display error messages.
    *   Solution: Replacing the headlight assembly or related control modules.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1500 - $3000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

The 2019 BMW 750Li, while offering exceptional luxury and performance, does have some common issues that prospective buyers should be aware of. Engine oil leaks and coolant leaks are common, as well as potential air suspension issues and electrical problems with the iDrive system. The severity of these issues ranges from minor inconveniences to potentially costly repairs. Owners should prioritize thorough inspections and address any warning signs promptly to mitigate potential damage. The cost of repairs can be significant, reflecting the premium nature of the vehicle and its components.
