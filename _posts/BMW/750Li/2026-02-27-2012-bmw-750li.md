# 2012 BMW 750Li

### Overview
The 2012 BMW 750Li, a flagship luxury sedan, offers a blend of potent performance and opulent comfort. Powered by a robust V8 engine and paired with a sophisticated automatic transmission, it aims for a premium driving experience. While generally well-regarded for its engineering, like any complex vehicle, it has specific areas that have seen more attention from owners regarding reliability. This analysis focuses on documented technical issues beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential concerns for owners and prospective buyers of this model year.

---


## Engine

### 4.4L Twin-Turbo V8 (N63B44O0)
**Oil Consumption**
- **Symptoms:** Excessive oil burning, frequent need to top up oil, blue smoke from exhaust.
- **Solution:** Piston ring replacement, valve stem seal replacement, PCV system service.
- **Cost:** $3,000 - $6,000

**Turbocharger Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of power, whistling noises, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Turbocharger replacement or rebuild.
- **Cost:** $2,500 - $5,000 per turbo

**Ignition Coil Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Misfires, rough idling, check engine light, reduced engine performance.
- **Solution:** Replacement of faulty ignition coils.
- **Cost:** $100 - $300 per coil

**Fuel Injector Problems**
- **Symptoms:** Rough idle, misfires, poor fuel economy, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of faulty fuel injectors.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600 per injector


## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic (6HP26)
**Mechatronic Unit Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Harsh shifting, transmission slipping, inability to select gears, transmission warning light.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of the mechatronic unit.
- **Cost:** $1,500 - $3,000

**Torque Converter Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Shuddering at low speeds, slipping, delayed engagement.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the torque converter.
- **Cost:** $1,000 - $2,000


## Suspension

### Adaptive Drive System
**Active Roll Stabilization (ARS) Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Fluid leaks from hydraulic lines, clunking noises over bumps, reduced handling stability.
- **Solution:** Replacement of leaking hydraulic lines or actuators.
- **Cost:** $800 - $2,000

**Air Suspension Compressor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle sagging on one side, 'air suspension malfunction' warning, uneven ride height.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the air suspension compressor.
- **Cost:** $700 - $1,500


## Electrical

### iDrive System
**Infotainment System Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Screen freezing, unresponsiveness, audio issues, navigation problems.
- **Solution:** Software updates, module reset, or replacement of the iDrive controller or head unit.
- **Cost:** $300 - $1,500


### Battery Drain
**Parasitic Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Dead battery after short periods of inactivity, electrical components malfunctioning.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and repair of faulty modules or circuits causing the drain.
- **Cost:** $200 - $800

### Summary Verdict
The 2012 BMW 750Li is a luxurious and powerful vehicle, but its complexity means potential owners should be aware of specific reliability concerns. The 4.4L twin-turbo V8 engine is prone to issues like excessive oil consumption, turbocharger problems, and ignition coil/injector failures, which can be costly to repair. The automatic transmission can experience mechatronic unit or torque converter issues. The advanced suspension systems, particularly the Adaptive Drive, may develop leaks or compressor failures. Electrical gremlins, such as iDrive system glitches and parasitic battery drain, are also noted. While the exterior and interior are generally robust, the mechanical and electrical systems require diligent maintenance and can incur significant repair costs.
