# 2018 BMW 750Li

### Overview
The 2018 BMW 750Li, a flagship luxury sedan, generally exhibits a strong reliability record, reflecting BMW's commitment to engineering excellence. While no vehicle is entirely immune to issues, the 750Li's advanced technology and premium build quality contribute to a positive ownership experience for most. Owners can expect a sophisticated driving experience with a focus on comfort and performance. However, as with any high-end vehicle, potential owners should be aware of the specific areas that may require attention over time, particularly concerning its complex powertrain and advanced electronic systems. Regular maintenance is key to ensuring the longevity and optimal performance of this luxury automobile.

## Suspension

### Adaptive Air Suspension
**Air Spring Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle may sag on one corner, uneven ride height, warning lights on the dashboard.
- **Solution:** Replacement of leaking air springs or associated lines.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1500 per corner

**Air Compressor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Inability to adjust ride height, constant running of the compressor, warning lights.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the air suspension compressor.
- **Cost:** $700 - $1200


## Electrical

### Infotainment System (iDrive)
**Software Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Freezing, unresponsiveness, occasional system reboots, connectivity issues.
- **Solution:** Software updates, system resets, or potential module replacement.
- **Cost:** $150 - $500


### Battery Drain
**Parasitic Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Battery dies unexpectedly, difficulty starting the vehicle, electrical components malfunctioning.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis to find the source of the drain (e.g., faulty module, aftermarket accessory) and repair or replacement.
- **Cost:** $300 - $1000+

### Summary Verdict
The 2018 BMW 750Li is a highly capable luxury sedan with a generally good reliability rating. The powertrain, consisting of the 4.4L twin-turbo V8 and 8-speed automatic transmission, is robust and has few reported widespread issues. Potential concerns tend to lie within the complex electronic systems and the adaptive air suspension. Issues like software glitches in the iDrive system, parasitic battery drain, and potential leaks or compressor failures in the air suspension are the most commonly cited, though not necessarily prevalent. These issues, while potentially costly to repair, are often manageable with proper diagnosis and maintenance. Overall, the 750Li offers a reliable and luxurious experience, provided owners are prepared for the potential maintenance needs of a high-end, technologically advanced vehicle.
