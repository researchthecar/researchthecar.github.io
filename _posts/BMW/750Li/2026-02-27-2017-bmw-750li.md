# 2017 BMW 750Li

### Overview
The 2017 BMW 750Li, a flagship luxury sedan, generally offers a high level of refinement and advanced technology. As with any complex vehicle, particularly in its segment, certain areas may experience issues over time. This analysis focuses on documented technical reliability concerns beyond routine maintenance. The 750Li is equipped with a potent V8 engine and an advanced automatic transmission, both of which are generally robust but can present specific challenges as mileage increases. Electrical systems, due to the extensive features, are also an area to monitor. Overall, the 750Li aims for a premium ownership experience, and understanding potential reliability points can aid in proactive maintenance.

---


## Engine

### 4.4L Twin-Turbo V8 (N63TU2)
**Oil Consumption**
- **Symptoms:** Excessive oil burning, potentially leading to low oil levels and engine damage if not monitored.
- **Solution:** Regular oil level checks, potential PCV valve replacement, or in severe cases, engine rebuild.
- **Cost:** $500 - $2,500

**Turbocharger Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of power, whistling noises, boost leaks.
- **Solution:** Inspection and potential replacement of turbochargers or associated plumbing.
- **Cost:** $2,000 - $5,000+

**Ignition Coil/Spark Plug Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Misfires, rough idling, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of faulty ignition coils and spark plugs.
- **Cost:** $400 - $800


## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic (ZF 8HP)
**Mechatronics Unit Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Harsh shifting, transmission warning lights, inability to select gears.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of the mechatronics unit.
- **Cost:** $1,500 - $3,000

**Torque Converter Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Shuddering at low speeds, slipping, delayed engagement.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the torque converter.
- **Cost:** $1,000 - $2,500


## Suspension

### Adaptive Drive System
**Air Suspension Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle sagging on one or more corners, uneven ride height, compressor running excessively.
- **Solution:** Replacement of leaking air springs or seals, or compressor.
- **Cost:** $600 - $1,500 per corner

**Active Roll Stabilization Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** Increased body roll in corners, warning lights related to suspension.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and potential replacement of hydraulic actuators or control modules.
- **Cost:** $2,000 - $4,000+


## Electrical

### Infotainment System (iDrive)
**Screen Freezing/Unresponsiveness**
- **Symptoms:** Infotainment screen becomes unresponsive or freezes, requiring a system reset.
- **Solution:** Software updates or potential replacement of the head unit.
- **Cost:** $500 - $2,000


### Door Handle Electronics
**Exterior Door Handle Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Automatic door handles not extending or retracting properly, keyless entry issues.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the affected door handle assembly.
- **Cost:** $400 - $800


### Battery Drain
**Parasitic Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Battery dies unexpectedly, requiring jump starts or replacement.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis of parasitic draw from modules or accessories, repair or replacement of faulty components.
- **Cost:** $300 - $1,000+

### Summary Verdict
The 2017 BMW 750Li is a sophisticated luxury vehicle with a powerful engine and advanced features. While generally reliable for its class, potential owners should be aware of specific issues that can arise, particularly concerning the 4.4L twin-turbo V8's oil consumption and turbocharger health, as well as potential complexities within the adaptive suspension and electrical systems, especially the iDrive infotainment and battery management. Transmission reliability is generally good, but mechatronics and torque converter issues can occur. Proactive maintenance, regular fluid checks, and prompt attention to warning signs are crucial for maintaining the 750Li's performance and longevity.
