# 2019 BMW 750Li

### Overview
The 2019 BMW 750Li, a flagship luxury sedan, generally offers a high level of refinement and performance. As with any high-performance vehicle, particularly those with complex powertrains and advanced technology, some areas may require more attention than others. This analysis focuses on documented technical reliability concerns beyond routine maintenance. The 750Li is equipped with a potent V8 engine and an advanced transmission system, contributing to its dynamic driving experience. While the overall build quality is commendable, specific components and systems have shown tendencies for issues that owners should be aware of. This data aims to provide a clear picture of potential reliability hotspots for the 2019 model year.

---


## Engine

### 4.4L Twin-Turbo V8 (N63TU3)
**Oil Consumption**
- **Symptoms:** Increased oil level checks required, potential blue smoke from exhaust under acceleration.
- **Solution:** Regular oil level monitoring, potential PCV valve replacement, or in severe cases, engine internal inspection.
- **Cost:** $300 - $1,200

**Turbocharger Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of power, whistling noises from the engine bay, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Inspection and potential replacement of turbochargers or associated lines.
- **Cost:** $2,500 - $5,000+

## Suspension

### Adaptive Air Suspension
**Air Spring Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle sagging on one or more corners, 'air suspension malfunction' warning light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of leaking air springs or air compressor.
- **Cost:** $800 - $2,000 per corner

**Sensor Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** Erratic ride height adjustments, suspension warning lights.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and replacement of faulty ride height sensors.
- **Cost:** $300 - $700


## Electrical

### Infotainment System
**Software Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Screen freezing, unresponsive touch controls, Bluetooth connectivity issues.
- **Solution:** Software updates or resets, potential head unit replacement.
- **Cost:** $200 - $1,500


### Battery Drain
**Parasitic Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Battery dying prematurely, difficulty starting the vehicle.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis to identify the source of the drain (e.g., faulty module, aftermarket accessory) and repair.
- **Cost:** $200 - $800

### Seat Actuators
**Malfunctioning Seat Adjustments**

### Summary Verdict
The 2019 BMW 750Li is a luxurious and powerful sedan, but like many high-end vehicles, it has specific areas that may require attention. The 4.4L twin-turbo V8 engine, while potent, can be prone to oil consumption and potential turbocharger issues, which can be costly to repair. The adaptive air suspension, a key feature for comfort, can develop leaks or sensor problems. Electrically, infotainment system glitches and parasitic battery drain are the most commonly cited concerns. While the transmission and exterior components are generally reliable, interior components like seat actuators can occasionally fail. Regular maintenance and prompt attention to warning signs are crucial for maintaining the reliability of this sophisticated vehicle.
