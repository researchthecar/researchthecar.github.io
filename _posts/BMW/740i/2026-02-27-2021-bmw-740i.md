# 2021 BMW 740i

### Overview
The 2021 BMW 740i, a flagship luxury sedan, generally presents a strong reliability profile, reflecting BMW's commitment to engineering excellence in its higher-tier models. While no vehicle is entirely immune to potential issues, the 740i for this model year has demonstrated commendable durability. Owners can expect a refined driving experience with a focus on comfort and performance. The primary areas of concern, though infrequent, tend to be concentrated in specific electronic modules and, in some cases, the complex powertrain systems. However, for the most part, the 740i offers a reliable ownership experience, with most reported issues being minor or isolated incidents rather than widespread defects. Regular maintenance is key to ensuring its long-term performance and preventing common wear-and-tear problems.

## Suspension

### Adaptive Air Suspension
**Air Suspension Leaks/Compressor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle sagging on one or more corners, uneven ride height, compressor running excessively, warning lights on the dashboard.
- **Solution:** Repair or replace leaking air springs, struts, or the air compressor. Software updates may also be necessary.
- **Cost:** $800 - $2500


## Electrical

### Infotainment System
**iDrive Glitches/Freezing**
- **Symptoms:** Infotainment screen freezing, unresponsiveness, random reboots, connectivity issues with Apple CarPlay/Android Auto.
- **Solution:** Software updates for the iDrive system, hard resets, or potential replacement of the head unit.
- **Cost:** $300 - $1500


### Battery Drain
**Premature Battery Failure/Parasitic Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Difficulty starting the vehicle, battery warning lights, electrical components malfunctioning intermittently.
- **Solution:** Diagnose and repair parasitic drain sources, replace the battery if necessary.
- **Cost:** $400 - $1000

### Summary Verdict
The 2021 BMW 740i is a generally reliable luxury sedan, with its core powertrain components (3.0L Turbocharged Inline-6 engine and 8-speed automatic transmission) showing excellent durability. The most commonly reported issues, though not widespread, relate to the adaptive air suspension, specifically leaks or compressor failures, and certain electrical components such as the iDrive infotainment system experiencing glitches or premature battery drain. Interior components like seat actuators and heating elements can also occasionally fail. Despite these potential issues, which are typical for a complex luxury vehicle, the 740i offers a solid ownership experience when properly maintained. Addressing potential issues promptly can prevent more significant problems and ensure the longevity of this sophisticated vehicle.
