# 2022 BMW 430i xDrive Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2022 BMW 430i xDrive, buyers will find a stylish and capable luxury sport coupe or convertible backed by modern performance. While the vehicle offers engaging driving dynamics, prospective owners should review available owner feedback regarding potential powertrain and electrical items as part of their evaluation process.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4 (B48)

This 2.0-liter turbocharged inline-4 engine produces approximately 255 horsepower and 295 lb-ft of torque, offering a strong balance of everyday efficiency and spirited acceleration. It is generally regarded as a robust powerplant, though buyers should verify that all factory service bulletins and recall notices have been properly addressed.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed ZF Automatic with xDrive All-Wheel Drive

The 8-speed automatic transmission paired with the xDrive all-wheel-drive system delivers quick, smooth shifts and confident traction in varying weather conditions. Overall, the drivetrain is sophisticated, though individual owner reports highlight rare electronic or mechanical shifting glitches.

**Unexpected Shifting to Neutral**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may occasionally experience the transmission independently shifting into neutral while driving at various speeds without any warning lights illuminating.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified technician or dealership inspect the transmission control module and software calibration.

## Electrical

### Starter and Ignition Electrical System

Owner reports note important safety recalls concerning potential starter electrical issues that require manufacturer remediation.

### Backup Camera and Visibility

Safety equipment generally provides comprehensive protection, though auxiliary features like parking cameras can occasionally exhibit software-related display drops.

**Rear Vision Camera Deactivation**
- **Symptoms:** The rearview camera may deactivate when the park assist system is turned off, leaving the driver without an image while reversing.
- **Solution:** Consult a dealership to check for software updates or module resets addressing the park assist and camera integration.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V267000: Visibility:Sun/Moon Roof Assembly](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=430i&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 118, "Power-Operated Window Systems." A sunroof that closes without the key fob present inside the vehicle can increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will reprogram the roof function control unit, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V118000: Electrical System:Body Control Module:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=430i&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** Doors that do not lock as intended while the vehicle is in motion may open during a crash, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the ECU software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V584000: Seat Belts](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=430i&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 208, "Occupant Crash Protection." An audible chime that does not properly alert the driver to fasten their seat belt increases the risk of injury in a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the receiver audio module software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V636000: Electrical System:Starter Assembly:Relay](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=430i&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** The engine starter relay may corrode, causing the relay to overheat and short circuit. A short circuit in the starter relay may increase the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the engine starter, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V056000: Electrical System:Starter Assembly](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=430i&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** The engine starter may overheat and catch fire due to wear on an internal component. Fire increases the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the engine starter, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.
