# 2021 BMW 430i xDrive Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2021 BMW 430i xDrive, buyers will find a stylish and capable luxury sport coupe or convertible backed by confident turbo-four performance. While mechanical reliability for the powertrain is generally solid, prospective owners should review owner-reported complaints and active safety advisories carefully before purchasing.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4 (B48)

The 2.0-liter turbocharged inline-4 engine produces approximately 255 horsepower and 294 lb-ft of torque. It provides brisk acceleration and decent efficiency, though buyers should verify all open campaigns have been addressed by a certified dealer.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission with xDrive

This ZF-sourced 8-speed automatic transmission paired with the intelligent xDrive all-wheel-drive system delivers exceptionally smooth and quick shifts. It is widely regarded as one of the most reliable and refined transmissions in its class.

## Safety

### Seat Belt

Issues related to seat belt extenders and retractors have been noted by owners, requiring careful inspection of all safety harnesses.

**Seat Belt Presenter and Retractor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Owners report that the automatic seat belt extender arm or chest strap can become shredded, flimsy, or break repeatedly, while retractor mechanisms may fail shortly after repair attempts.
- **Solution:** Inspect the seat belt presenters and retractors for physical wear or failure, and have damaged assemblies replaced by a dealer.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V554000: Seat Belts](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=430i&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** As such, these assemblies fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 208, "Occupant Crash Protection." During a crash, a seat belt retractor that does not function as intended increases the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the seat belt retractors (including the seat belts) and, if necessary, replace the retractors, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V096000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=430i&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** When shifting into Reverse, a small portion of the rearview image may be obscured, or the screen may not illuminate. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 111, "Rear Visibility." If the driver relies on only the rearview camera image, the missing or reduced rearview image view.
- **What to do:** BMW will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the rearview camera software and update it if necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V176000: Air Bags:Side/Window:Thorax](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=430i&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** In certain side-impact crashes, the head-thorax air bag may not deploy as intended. An air bag that deploys incorrectly can increase the risk of injury in a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the software, at no charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V584000: Seat Belts](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=430i&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 208, "Occupant Crash Protection." An audible chime that does not properly alert the driver to fasten their seat belt increases the risk of injury in a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the receiver audio module software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V636000: Electrical System:Starter Assembly:Relay](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=430i&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The engine starter relay may corrode, causing the relay to overheat and short circuit. A short circuit in the starter relay may increase the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the engine starter, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V056000: Electrical System:Starter Assembly](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=430i&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The engine starter may overheat and catch fire due to wear on an internal component. Fire increases the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the engine starter, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.
