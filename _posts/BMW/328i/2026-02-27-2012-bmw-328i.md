# 2012 BMW 328i

### Overview
The 2012 BMW 328i, part of the F30 generation, is generally regarded as a solid vehicle with a good balance of performance and luxury. It is equipped with a potent turbocharged inline-six engine (in some markets) or a new turbocharged four-cylinder, paired with either a manual or automatic transmission. While many owners report a positive ownership experience, like any vehicle, it has specific areas that have seen more attention from repair shops. This analysis focuses on documented common issues beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential reliability concerns for prospective buyers and current owners.

---


## Engine

### N52B30 3.0L Naturally Aspirated Inline-6
**Valve Cover Gasket Leak**
- **Symptoms:** Oil smell in the engine bay, visible oil on the engine block, potential for oil dripping.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the valve cover gasket.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600

**PCV Valve Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Rough idle, increased oil consumption, whistling noises from the engine, potential for oil leaks.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the Positive Crankcase Ventilation (PCV) valve assembly.
- **Cost:** $200 - $450


### N20B20 2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4
**Timing Chain Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Rattling noise from the front of the engine, especially on startup; check engine light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the timing chain, tensioners, and guides.
- **Cost:** $1200 - $2500

**Water Pump Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Overheating, coolant leaks, warning lights on the dashboard.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the electric water pump and thermostat.
- **Cost:** $600 - $1000

## Electrical

### General Electrical System
**Sunroof Drain Clogs**
- **Symptoms:** Water leaks into the cabin, potentially affecting electronics and headliner.
- **Solution:** Clearing of sunroof drain tubes.
- **Cost:** $150 - $300

**Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Difficulty starting, electrical components malfunctioning intermittently, battery dying prematurely.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and repair of parasitic draw or replacement of battery.
- **Cost:** $200 - $800

### Summary Verdict
The 2012 BMW 328i offers a compelling driving experience, and its overall reliability is good, especially considering its performance-oriented nature. The most commonly cited issues revolve around the engine, particularly the valve cover gasket and PCV valve on the naturally aspirated inline-six, and potential timing chain or water pump problems on the newer turbocharged four-cylinder. Transmission options, both automatic and manual, are generally robust. Electrical gremlins, such as battery drain and sunroof drain issues, are not uncommon but are typically manageable. With proactive maintenance and attention to these specific potential problem areas, the 2012 328i can be a reliable and enjoyable luxury sedan.
