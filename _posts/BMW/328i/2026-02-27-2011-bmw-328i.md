# 2011 BMW 328i Reliability & Common Issues

### Overview

The 2011 BMW 328i is a highly regarded luxury compact vehicle appreciated for its athletic handling and premium cabin. However, used-car buyers should understand that several common reliability issues have been reported. The following summary highlights major issue trends based on a sample of consumer complaints reported to the NHTSA; these reports represent a sample of owner experiences and do not mean every vehicle will experience these problems.

---

## Engine

### 3.0L Inline-6 (N52 / N51 SULEV)

The naturally aspirated 3.0-liter inline-six engine (N52, or N51 in SULEV emission states) produces approximately 230 horsepower and 200 lb-ft of torque. While mechanically robust in terms of its block and pistons, it requires careful monitoring of electronic accessories and plastic/rubber components that degrade over time. Proactive cooling system maintenance is highly recommended.

**VANOS Camshaft Gear Bolt Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Owners report a low oil pressure warning light, sudden loss of motive power, engine stalling, metallic knocking sounds from under the hood, or metallic shavings found in the engine oil.
- **Solution:** Replace the sheared or detached VANOS gear bolts. A manufacturer recall (NHTSA Campaign 23V707000) was issued for certain vehicles, though some owners report being denied SULEV or recall coverage if their VIN was not explicitly included.

**PCV Valve Heater (Blowby Heater) Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** A prominent burning oil odor coming through the HVAC vents (especially while idling), smoke from the engine bay, fluctuating RPMs, or engine stalling.
- **Solution:** Replace the Positive Crankcase Ventilation (PCV) heater valve. This issue is often related to NHTSA recall campaign 22V119000.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic (GM 6L45) / 6-Speed Manual

Power is sent to either the rear or all four wheels (xDrive) via a standard 6-speed manual or a GM-sourced 6-speed automatic transmission. The manual transmission is extremely durable, while the automatic is generally reliable but can exhibit shuddering or gear hunting in high-mileage examples.

**Torque Converter Shudder**
- **Symptoms:** Vibration or shuddering under acceleration and difficulty setting emission monitors for smog certification.
- **Solution:** Replace the torque converter and inspect the transmission fluid.

**Front Driveshaft Bolt Loosening (xDrive models)**
- **Symptoms:** Driveline vibrations, rumbling noises in the cabin at highway speeds, or complete loss of power to the front wheels.
- **Solution:** Replace or retighten the front driveshaft attachment bolts. Select 2011 328i xDrive models were subject to NHTSA Campaign 11V476000 for loose driveshaft bolts.

## Suspension

### Chassis and Wheels

**Run-Flat Tire Sidewall Failures**
- **Symptoms:** Frequent tire blowouts occurring where the tire bead intersects the wheel rim, often triggered by minor road imperfections.
- **Solution:** Replace damaged tires. Some owners opt to transition to conventional passenger tires for improved ride comfort and durability.

## Brakes

### Braking System

**Brake Booster or Pressure Sensor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** A stiff or locked brake pedal making it highly difficult to depress the brakes and stop the vehicle safely.
- **Solution:** Diagnose and replace the brake pressure sensor, vacuum lines, or the brake booster assembly.

## Steering

### Steering Column and Controls

**Heated Steering Wheel Overheating**
- **Symptoms:** The steering wheel becomes excessively hot to the touch, potentially causing minor burns.
- **Solution:** Diagnose the steering wheel heating thermostat and replace the control switch or heating element.

## Electrical

### Body Control Modules & Wiring

**Footwell Module (FRM) Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Power windows fail to operate, headlights remain stuck in a dim or on position, and turn signals, interior lights, or door locks become completely inoperable. This failure often occurs immediately after a battery replacement or jump-start.
- **Solution:** Replace or reprogram the Footwell Module (FRM).
- **Cost:** $1,250 to $1,300

**Positive Battery Cable & B+ Terminal Power Loss**
- **Symptoms:** Intermittent electrical glitches, complete vehicle power failure, or stalling without warning.
- **Solution:** Replace the positive battery cable from front to rear. Many vehicles were serviced under NHTSA Campaign 13V044000, though some owners report their specific VINs were excluded.

**Comfort Access Door Handle Short Circuit**
- **Symptoms:** Comfort access features stop working, fuse #61 blows repeatedly, and the passenger door handle becomes hot to the touch, melts, or emits smoke.
- **Solution:** Disconnect and replace the shorted comfort access door handle assembly.

## Exterior

### Glass and Trim

**Spontaneous Moonroof Shattering**
- **Symptoms:** A sudden, extremely loud explosion sound while driving at highway speeds, resulting in shattered glass pushing outward or raining into the cabin.
- **Solution:** Replace the glass sunroof/moonroof assembly and repair any resulting paint scratches.

**Melting Interior Door Handle Covers**
- **Symptoms:** The plastic/rubber material on the interior door pulls degrades, turning into a sticky, black residue that rubs off on hands.
- **Solution:** Replace the inner door handle trim pieces with updated aftermarket or OEM parts.

## Interior

### Cabin Seating and Controls

**Seat Heating Element Overheating**
- **Symptoms:** The seat heater gets dangerously hot even on its lowest setting, in some cases causing the leather seat covers to melt.
- **Solution:** Replace the seat heating element pads or control module.

## Safety

### Restraint Systems and Airbags

**Passenger Restraint Sensor (OC3 Mat) Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The passenger restraint warning light illuminates on the dash, and the passenger airbag remains deactivated even when a passenger is seated in the front.
- **Solution:** Replace the front passenger seat occupancy sensor mat (OC3 mat) to ensure proper airbag deployment during a collision.
- **Cost:** Over $1,000 for diagnosis and replacement

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V017000: Air Bags:Frontal:Driver Side:Inflator Module](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=328i&modelYear=2011)**
- **Issue:** An inflator explosion may result in sharp metal fragments striking the driver or other occupants resulting in serious injury or death.
- **What to do:** BMW will notify owners, and dealers will replace the driver air bag. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V676000: Electrical System:Wiring](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=328i&modelYear=2011)**
- **Issue:** Wiring that overheats could cause the electrical connectors to melt, and increase the risk of a fire, even when the vehicle is unattended.
- **What to do:** BMW will notify owners, and dealers will replace the wiring and electrical connectors, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V683000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Exhaust System:Emission Control:Crankcase (Pcv)](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=328i&modelYear=2011)**
- **Issue:** The heater for the positive crankcase ventilation (PCV) valve may short circuit. An electrical short can cause the parts within the PCV valve to melt, increasing the risk of a fire, even when the vehicle is not in use.
- **What to do:** BMW will notify owners, and dealers will replace the PCV valve heater, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 14V176000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Engine](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=328i&modelYear=2011)**
- **Issue:** An engine stall increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** BMW will notify owners, and dealers will replace the four VANOS housing bolts for both VANOS units (intake and exhaust), free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 13V044000: Electrical System](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=328i&modelYear=2011)**
- **Issue:** If there is a loss of electrical power to the vehicle, the vehicle may unexpectedly stall, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** BMW will notify owners, and dealers will replace the positive battery cable connector and secure it with an improved method, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 11V476000: Power Train:Driveline:Driveshaft](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=328i&modelYear=2011)**
- **Issue:** THE FRONT DRIVESHAFT ATTACHMENT BOLTS MAY NOT HAVE BEEN TIGHTENED TO SPECIFICATION. IF THE BOLTS WERE NOT TIGHTENED TO SPECIFICATION, THEY MAY BECOME LOOSE.
- **What to do:** BMW WILL NOTIFY OWNERS, AND DEALERS WILL REPLACE THE FRONT DRIVESHAFT ATTACHMENT BOLTS FREE OF CHARGE. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V513000: Air Bags:Frontal:Driver Side:Inflator Module](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=328i&modelYear=2011)**
- **Issue:** The original steering wheel may have been replaced with a sport or M-sport steering wheel equipped with an inflator that can explode during deployment. An inflator explosion may result in sharp metal fragments striking the driver or other occupants, resulting in serious injury or death.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the driver's air bag module as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

### Summary Verdict

The 2011 BMW 328i is a driver-focused vehicle with a solid inline-6 engine, but buyers should anticipate potential electrical and engine component issues. Key failure points include the VANOS camshaft gear bolts (which can cause engine failure), the Footwell Module (which disables critical lighting and window operations), and the passenger seat airbag sensors. We highly recommend verifying that all recall work has been performed on the VANOS system, the PCV heater, and the battery cables before purchasing.
