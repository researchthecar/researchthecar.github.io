# 2021 BMW 420i Gran Coupe Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2021 BMW 420i Gran Coupe, buyers will find a stylish and engaging luxury vehicle backed by BMW's refined engineering. While overall owner satisfaction remains strong, prospective buyers should review specific component feedback regarding interior convenience features and safety mechanisms to ensure a trouble-free ownership experience.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4 (B48)

The 2.0-liter turbocharged inline-4 engine produces approximately 184 horsepower and 221 lb-ft of torque, offering a balanced blend of efficiency and responsive power for daily driving. This powerplant is generally well-regarded for its refined operation and durability when properly maintained.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

Equipped with a smooth-shifting 8-speed automatic transmission paired with rear-wheel drive or xDrive all-wheel drive, this setup delivers quick gear changes and highway efficiency. It is widely considered one of the most reliable and responsive automatic gearboxes in its class.

## Safety

### Seat Belt

The safety restraint systems provide robust protection, though some owner complaints focus on cosmetic and mechanical wear of the automated seat belt arms and retractors.

**Seat Belt Presenter and Retractor Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have reported issues with the automated seat belt presenter arms appearing flimsy or breaking, as well as chest straps fraying and retractors failing prematurely.
- **Solution:** Inspect the seat belt presenters and webbing thoroughly during a pre-purchase evaluation; damaged components may require assembly replacement.
