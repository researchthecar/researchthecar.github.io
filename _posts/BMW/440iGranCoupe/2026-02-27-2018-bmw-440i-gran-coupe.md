# 2018 BMW 440i Gran Coupe Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2018 BMW 440i Gran Coupe combines sleek styling and engaging performance, anchored by a smooth powertrain lineup. While generally well-regarded by enthusiasts, prospective buyers should review known owner complaints regarding cooling system leaks, electrical glitches, and minor interior or safety system concerns.

---

## Engine

### 3.0L Turbocharged Inline-6 (B58)

The 3.0-liter turbocharged inline-six (B58) engine produces approximately 320 horsepower and 330 lb-ft of torque. It is widely praised for its smooth power delivery and robust performance, though regular maintenance of the cooling system is critical.

**Coolant Leaks and Overheating Warnings**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have reported coolant puddles underneath the vehicle accompanied by illuminated low coolant warning lights.
- **Solution:** Inspect coolant hoses, lines, and the water pump for leaks; replace damaged components and top off fluid.

**Engine Stalling and Power Loss**
- **Symptoms:** Unexpected loss of motive power while driving, occasionally linked to cooling system or auxiliary water pump issues.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified technician diagnose fault codes, check cooling components, and service the electrical system.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

The ZF 8-speed automatic transmission offers quick, refined shifts paired with rear-wheel drive or xDrive all-wheel drive. It is generally considered a highly reliable and responsive unit with minimal widespread mechanical complaints.

## Electrical

### Body Electrical and Accessories

Complex vehicle electronics control comfort and driver-assist features, which can occasionally exhibit sporadic software glitches.

**Spontaneous Window and Sunroof Operation**
- **Symptoms:** Doors may unlock independently, and windows or sunroofs may open unexpectedly while the vehicle is parked or in motion.
- **Solution:** Inspect body control modules and software programming for faults or moisture intrusion.

### Seats and Lumbar Support

Cabin comfort amenities are premium and durable, though minor power-seat adjustment mechanisms can occasionally malfunction.

**Unsolicited Lumbar Inflation**
- **Symptoms:** Driver's seat lumbar support may inflate automatically without driver input, causing discomfort and distraction.
- **Solution:** Inspect the seat control switch and pneumatic lumbar bladder system for electrical shorts or internal valve sticking.

## Safety

### Seat Belts

Cabin safety components generally function as intended, though occasional seat belt latch and sensor complaints have been logged.

**Seat Belt Retraction and Warning Failures**
- **Symptoms:** Front driver-side seat belts may fail to retract properly or secure occupants as needed, while warning chimes can trigger incorrectly.
- **Solution:** Inspect seat belt assembly, buckles, and sensors at a dealership or service center for necessary replacement.

---

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V507000: Seat Belts:Front](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=440i&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** If the vehicle has an incompatible vehicle-sensitive locking mechanism, the seat belt may not restrain the seat occupant in the event of a crash, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** BMW will notify owners, and dealers will inspect and, if necessary replace, the front seat belt assemblies, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2018 BMW 440i Gran Coupe is a compelling luxury sport vehicle anchored by a strong powertrain. Buyers should perform a thorough pre-purchase inspection focusing on the cooling system, electrical accessories, and safety restraints to ensure a trouble-free ownership experience.
