# 2017 BMW 440i Gran Coupe Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used luxury sport model like this BMW, evaluating powertrain performance alongside known mechanical and electrical pain points is essential. While these vehicles deliver engaging driving dynamics and refined interior styling, owners have reported recurring concerns regarding cooling system components and electronic warning systems. Reviewing these specific areas before purchasing can help you anticipate potential maintenance needs and negotiate a fair price.

---

## Engine

### 3.0L Turbocharged Inline-6 Engine (B58)

This 3.0-liter turbocharged inline-6 engine produces approximately 320 horsepower and 330 lb-ft of torque, offering robust acceleration and smooth power delivery. While generally robust, buyers should closely monitor the cooling system and plastic engine components for leaks and wear over time.

**Coolant Leak and Oil Filter Housing Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers experience sudden low coolant warnings, visible fluid leaks, or vehicle overheating due to failures in plastic engine components such as the oil filter housing or thermostat.
- **Solution:** Inspect the engine bay for coolant residue around the oil filter housing and replace damaged plastic housings or gaskets with updated parts.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 8-speed automatic transmission provides rapid, seamless shifts and pairs exceptionally well with rear-wheel or all-wheel drive configurations. It enjoys a solid reputation for long-term durability provided standard fluid maintenance schedules are followed.

## Safety

### Air Bag and Seat Belt Sensors

Safety systems generally perform well, though electronic sensor faults in the cabin can trigger warning lights requiring professional diagnostic scanning.

**Passenger Restraint and Seat Occupancy Warning**
- **Symptoms:** Illumination of the air bag warning light accompanied by dashboard messages indicating passenger restraint system failures or seat occupancy sensor faults.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified technician diagnose the seat occupancy mat and wiring harnesses, replacing faulty sensors or modules as necessary.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V115000: Air Bags](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=440i&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** The affected vehicles have seat-mounted side air bag inflator initiators that may fail to ignite during a crash. If the air bag inflator initiator fails to ignite, the seat-mounted air bag will not deploy, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** BMW will notify owners, and dealers will replace the air bag modules, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

This luxury model offers strong performance and distinctive styling, but potential buyers should perform a thorough pre-purchase inspection.
