The 2022 BMW 440i Gran Coupe is a stylish and sporty four-door coupe that offers a blend of performance and practicality. It's powered by a 3.0-liter turbocharged inline-six engine, producing 382 horsepower and 369 lb-ft of torque. It is available with an 8-speed automatic transmission. Some owners appreciate its sleek design, powerful engine, and comfortable interior. Common complaints include the sometimes controversial styling (specifically the large kidney grilles), the firm ride, and the complexity of the infotainment system. Other engine options for the 2022 4 Series Gran Coupe include the 430i, which is a 2.0-Liter 4-Cylinder Turbo engine producing 255 hp and 295 lb-ft of torque.

**Engine**

**B58 3.0L Turbocharged Inline-Six**

*   **Oil Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks may occur from the valve cover gasket or the oil filter housing. Symptoms include visible oil drips under the car, a burning oil smell, and low oil levels.
    *   Solution: Replacing the valve cover gasket or the oil filter housing gasket.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

* **Coolant Leaks**
    * Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaks may occur from the thermostat housing or radiator hoses. Symptoms include visible coolant drips under the car, an overheating engine, and low coolant levels.
    * Solution: Replacing the thermostat housing or radiator hoses.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600+
    * Recalls: None Found

**B48 2.0L Turbocharged Inline-Four**

* **No Issues Found**

**Transmission**

**ZF 8HP 8-Speed Automatic Transmission**

*   **Harsh Shifting/Hesitation**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners report experiencing harsh shifting, particularly during low-speed maneuvers or when shifting between gears. Hesitation during acceleration is also reported.
    *   Solution: In some cases, a software update can address the issue. In more severe cases, valve body replacement may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 (software update) - $2000+ (valve body replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Adaptive M Suspension Issues (If Equipped)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Cars equipped with the Adaptive M Suspension may experience failure of the electronic dampers. Symptoms include a rough ride, uneven handling, and error messages related to the suspension system.
    *   Solution: Replacing the faulty electronic dampers.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1000 - $2000+ per damper
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **iDrive Infotainment System Glitches**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The iDrive system can experience occasional glitches, such as freezing, slow response times, or connectivity issues with Apple CarPlay or Android Auto.
    *   Solution: Software updates can sometimes resolve these issues. A system reset or, in rare cases, hardware replacement may be required.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 (software update) - $1500+ (hardware replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Sensor Malfunctions**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Various sensors, such as those related to parking assist or driver assistance systems, may malfunction, leading to error messages or impaired functionality.
    *   Solution: Diagnosing and replacing the faulty sensor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+ per sensor
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Tail Light Cracking/Fogging**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported issues with the tail lights, specifically cracking of the lens or fogging inside the light assembly.
    *   Solution: Replacing the affected tail light assembly.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $700+ per tail light
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Seat Squeaks/Rattles**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Squeaks or rattles may develop in the seats, particularly in the power-adjustable mechanisms.
    *   Solution: Tightening or lubricating the seat components. In some cases, replacement of worn parts may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Sunroof Issues (If Equipped)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported issues with the sunroof, such as it getting stuck, making excessive noise, or leaking.
    *   Solution: Cleaning and lubricating the sunroof tracks, repairing or replacing the sunroof motor, or replacing the sunroof seal.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $1000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2022 BMW 440i Gran Coupe is generally a reliable vehicle, but some owners have reported issues with the engine (oil/coolant leaks), transmission (harsh shifting), suspension (adaptive damper failure), electrical system (iDrive glitches, sensor malfunctions), exterior (tail light issues), and interior (seat squeaks, sunroof issues). The severity and cost of these issues can vary widely, from minor software glitches to major component replacements. The most expensive repairs tend to be related to the adaptive suspension, transmission valve body, or iDrive system. Regular maintenance and prompt attention to any warning signs can help to mitigate these potential problems.
