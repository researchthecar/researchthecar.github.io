# 2022 BMW Z4 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2022 BMW Z4 delivers a compelling blend of open-top performance and luxury roadster refinement. When shopping for a used model, buyers will find robust powertrain options and modern technology features, though it is always wise to thoroughly inspect individual vehicle service histories and verify that all necessary safety recalls have been completed by an authorized dealer.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4 (sDrive30i)

This 2.0-liter turbocharged inline-4 engine produces approximately 255 horsepower and 295 lb-ft of torque. It provides brisk acceleration for the base trim while maintaining reasonable efficiency for daily driving.

### 3.0L Turbocharged Inline-6 (M40i)

This 3.0-liter turbocharged inline-6 engine puts out around 382 horsepower and 369 lb-ft of torque. It offers thrilling performance and smooth power delivery for enthusiasts seeking stronger acceleration.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

This smooth 8-speed automatic transmission is paired with both available engines across the lineup, delivering quick, responsive shifts and highway cruising efficiency.

## Electrical

### Electrical and Visibility Systems

Owner reports note potential delays in obtaining parts for specific visibility-related safety campaigns, though overall electrical systems remain modern and generally stable.

**Recall Parts Availability Delay**
- **Symptoms:** Owners may receive recall notices regarding visibility and electrical system components where replacement parts are temporarily backordered.
- **Solution:** Contact a local certified dealership to check parts availability and schedule the necessary recall repair free of charge once components arrive.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V099000: Visibility:Power Window Devices And Controls](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=Z4&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 118, "Power-operated Window Systems." Unintentionally closing the windows or sunroof without the key present can increase the risk of injury to occupants inside the vehicle.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the software for the Electronic Control Unit (ECU), free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V636000: Electrical System:Starter Assembly:Relay](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=Z4&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** The engine starter relay may corrode, causing the relay to overheat and short circuit. A short circuit in the starter relay may increase the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the engine starter, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V056000: Electrical System:Starter Assembly](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=Z4&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** The engine starter may overheat and catch fire due to wear on an internal component. Fire increases the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the engine starter, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2022 BMW Z4 is an exciting and well-engineered luxury roadster that offers strong performance from either of its turbocharged engine choices. While early owner feedback includes a note regarding recall part wait times, no widespread mechanical failure trends have emerged. Buyers should prioritize well-maintained examples with documented service records and verify that all safety recalls have been addressed.
