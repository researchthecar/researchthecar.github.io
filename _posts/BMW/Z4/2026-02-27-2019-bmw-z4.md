# 2019 BMW Z4 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2019 BMW Z4, understanding its core mechanical makeup and owner-reported trouble spots helps set realistic expectations. As a modern luxury roadster, it relies heavily on sophisticated electronics and robust turbocharged powertrains. While owner feedback is generally limited, prospective buyers should review specific mechanical and electrical components closely during a pre-purchase inspection.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged I4 (sDrive30i)

This 2.0-liter turbocharged inline-four engine produces approximately 255 horsepower and 295 lb-ft of torque, offering lively acceleration for the base trim. It is generally regarded as a willing and efficient powerplant for daily cruising.

### 3.0L Turbocharged Inline-6 (M40i)

The 3.0-liter turbocharged inline-six engine generates around 382 horsepower and 369 lb-ft of torque, delivering thrilling performance in the M40i trim. This powerplant combines strong dynamics with solid overall long-term reliability when properly maintained.

**Faulty Grille Shutters**
- **Symptoms:** Owners report that the active grille shutters fail prematurely, sometimes leading to persistent dashboard warnings or operational faults.
- **Solution:** Inspect and replace the active grille shutter assembly if it fails to operate correctly.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

This ZF 8-speed automatic transmission is paired across the Z4 lineup, providing rapid gear changes and refined cruising behavior. It is widely praised in the automotive industry for smooth operation and strong durability.

## Suspension

### Control Arm Ball Joints

Steering components generally provide sharp and responsive handling characteristic of a sports roadster.

**Premature Ball Joint Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may notice excessive play in the control arm ball joints during routine inspections or experience loose steering feel earlier than expected.
- **Solution:** Inspect front suspension control arms for play and replace worn ball joints or control arm assemblies as necessary.

## Electrical

### Electrical System

The vehicle relies heavily on complex wiring and control modules that govern infotainment, safety, and convenience features.

**Electrical Recall Notification Delays**
- **Symptoms:** Owners may experience delays in obtaining replacement parts for open manufacturer electrical campaigns.
- **Solution:** Contact an authorized dealership to check for open recall status and schedule repairs as parts become available.

## Safety

### Forward Collision Avoidance

Advanced safety systems add an extra layer of protection, though sensor calibration should be verified.

**Automatic Emergency Braking Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have reported instances where the automatic emergency braking system failed to engage as expected during low-speed collision scenarios.
- **Solution:** Have dealership technicians inspect sensor alignment and run diagnostic scans on the forward collision avoidance module.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V684000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=Z4&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 111, "Rearview Mirrors." The lack of an image in the back-up camera display increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** BMW will notify owners, and dealers will update the back-up camera software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V732000: Engine](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=Z4&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** If the counterbalance shaft loosens, severe engine damage could occur, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** BMW will notify owners, and dealers will replace the engine, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V355000: Steering:Linkages:Tie Rod Assembly](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=Z4&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** In certain driving conditions, such as high temperatures and rough road surfaces, a steering gear tie rod may become damaged, possibly resulting in a fractured tie rod. A damaged or fractured tie rod may affect vehicle handling and control, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** BMW and Toyota will notify owners, and dealers will replace the steering gear tie rods, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V598000: Engine](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=Z4&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** During engine start, the engine management software may cause loss of braking assist. Loss of braking assist can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the engine management software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V516000: Exterior Lighting:Headlights](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=Z4&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 108, "Lamps, Reflective Devices and Associated Equipment." Should the headlamps be adjusted horizontally, there could be increased glare and reduced visibility for oncoming drivers, or reduced visibility for the vehicle operator, thereby increasing.
- **What to do:** BMW will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the headlamp vertical and horizontal adjustment plugs, correcting them as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V636000: Electrical System:Starter Assembly:Relay](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=Z4&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** The engine starter relay may corrode, causing the relay to overheat and short circuit. A short circuit in the starter relay may increase the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the engine starter, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2019 BMW Z4 is a dynamic and engaging roadster backed by potent engine options and a refined transmission. While few isolated complaints highlight potential concerns with front suspension wear, active grille shutters, and driver-assistance features, the vehicle remains a compelling choice. Buyers should prioritize a thorough pre-purchase inspection focusing on the suspension and electrical systems to ensure a trouble-free ownership experience.
