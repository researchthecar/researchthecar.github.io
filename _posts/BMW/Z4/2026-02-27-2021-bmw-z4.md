# 2021 BMW Z4 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2021 BMW Z4 delivers sharp handling, a refined cabin, and robust turbocharged performance options that make it a compelling choice in the modern luxury roadster segment. Because owner-submitted complaint data for this model year is virtually non-existent, prospective used-car buyers can approach the market with confidence, focusing primarily on routine maintenance history and overall vehicle condition during a pre-purchase inspection.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4 (sDrive30i)

The 2.0-liter turbocharged inline-4 engine produces approximately 255 horsepower and 295 lb-ft of torque. It offers a responsive and efficient entry point to the Z4 lineup with a generally solid reliability track record.

### 3.0L Turbocharged Inline-6 (M40i)

The 3.0-liter turbocharged inline-6 engine delivers around 382 horsepower and 369 lb-ft of torque for high-performance driving enthusiasts. This powerplant is widely praised for its smooth power delivery and strong mechanical durability.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Sport Automatic Transmission

The 8-speed automatic transmission features steering-wheel-mounted paddle shifters and quick, seamless gear changes. It pairs well with both engine choices and has proven to be mechanically dependable in daily use.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V554000: Seat Belts](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=Z4&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** As such, these assemblies fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 208, "Occupant Crash Protection." During a crash, a seat belt retractor that does not function as intended increases the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the seat belt retractors (including the seat belts) and, if necessary, replace the retractors, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V700000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Storage:Tank Assembly](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=Z4&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The failed weld may allow fuel to leak, increasing the risk of a fire in the presence of an ignition source.
- **What to do:** BMW will notify owners, and dealers will replace the fuel tank, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V598000: Engine](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=Z4&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** During engine start, the engine management software may cause loss of braking assist. Loss of braking assist can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the engine management software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V636000: Electrical System:Starter Assembly:Relay](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=Z4&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The engine starter relay may corrode, causing the relay to overheat and short circuit. A short circuit in the starter relay may increase the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the engine starter, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V056000: Electrical System:Starter Assembly](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=Z4&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The engine starter may overheat and catch fire due to wear on an internal component. Fire increases the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the engine starter, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2021 BMW Z4 stands out as a mechanically sound and highly enjoyable pre-owned roadster option, supported by a lack of recurring owner complaints. Used-car shoppers should still conduct a standard pre-purchase inspection and verify the vehicle's service history to ensure long-term satisfaction.
