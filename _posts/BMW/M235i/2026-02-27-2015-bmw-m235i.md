# 2015 BMW M235i

### Overview
The 2015 BMW M235i, a performance-oriented compact coupe, generally exhibits strong reliability, reflecting BMW's engineering prowess. It is powered by a potent turbocharged inline-six engine known for its robust performance. While the M235i is built with quality components, like any performance vehicle, certain areas may experience issues over time, particularly those subjected to high stress. This analysis focuses on documented, non-wear-and-tear related reliability concerns specific to the model year and its available powertrains, providing insights for potential owners and maintenance planning.

---


## Engine

### 3.0L Turbocharged Inline-6 (N55)
**Turbocharger Wastegate Rattle**
- **Symptoms:** Audible rattling or ticking noise from the turbocharger area, especially on cold starts or at low RPMs. May not always be accompanied by performance loss.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the wastegate actuator or the entire turbocharger assembly.
- **Cost:** $1,200 - $2,500

**PCV Valve Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Rough idle, increased oil consumption, potential whistling noises, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the Positive Crankcase Ventilation (PCV) valve.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600

### Summary Verdict
The 2015 BMW M235i is a highly capable and generally reliable performance coupe. The primary powertrain, the 3.0L turbocharged inline-six engine, is robust but can occasionally exhibit issues such as wastegate rattle and PCV valve failure, which are relatively common for this engine family. Both the 8-speed automatic and 6-speed manual transmissions have proven to be durable with no widespread reported problems. Other systems like suspension, electrical, exterior, and interior components are typically reliable, with no significant recurring issues noted beyond standard maintenance. Overall, the M235i offers a compelling blend of performance and dependability for enthusiasts.
