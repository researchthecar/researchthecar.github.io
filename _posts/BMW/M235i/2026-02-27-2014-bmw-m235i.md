# 2014 BMW M235i

### Overview
The 2014 BMW M235i, a performance-oriented coupe, generally exhibits strong reliability, reflecting BMW's engineering prowess. It is powered by a potent turbocharged inline-six engine, known for its power delivery and responsiveness. The available transmissions, both automatic and manual, are robust. While the M235i is built with quality components, like any performance vehicle, certain areas may require attention over time. This analysis focuses on documented common issues beyond standard maintenance, providing insights into potential reliability concerns for owners and prospective buyers.

---


## Engine

### 3.0L Twin-Scroll Turbocharged Inline-6 (N55)
**Turbocharger Wastegate Rattle**
- **Symptoms:** Audible rattling noise from the turbocharger area, especially on cold starts or at low RPMs. Can sometimes lead to reduced boost pressure.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the wastegate actuator or the entire turbocharger assembly.
- **Cost:** $1,200 - $2,500

**PCV Valve Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Rough idle, increased oil consumption, blue smoke from exhaust, potential oil leaks.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the Positive Crankcase Ventilation (PCV) valve.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600

## Electrical

### General Electrical System
**Water Pump Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Coolant leaks, engine overheating, warning lights on the dashboard.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the electric water pump and thermostat assembly.
- **Cost:** $700 - $1,200

**Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Difficulty starting, electrical components malfunctioning or not working, battery warning light.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and repair of parasitic drain, or battery replacement.
- **Cost:** $200 - $800

### Summary Verdict
The 2014 BMW M235i is a reliable performance car with a strong mechanical foundation. The primary areas of concern tend to be related to the turbocharger system, specifically wastegate rattle and PCV valve failures, which are not uncommon for turbocharged engines of this era. Electrical issues, such as water pump failures and occasional battery drain, are also documented. However, the transmissions (both automatic and manual) and suspension systems are generally very robust. With proactive maintenance and addressing potential issues promptly, the M235i can offer an engaging and dependable driving experience.
