The 2014 BMW M235i is a sporty and compact performance car known for its engaging driving dynamics, powerful engine, and relatively luxurious interior. Available as a coupe or convertible, it caters to enthusiasts seeking a fun-to-drive experience. The most popular engine option is the turbocharged 3.0-liter inline-6 (N55) producing 320 horsepower and 330 lb-ft of torque. Other options include the 2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4. Transmission options included a 6-speed manual and an 8-speed automatic. What people generally like about the M235i is its blend of performance, handling, and practicality. Downsides often mentioned include a somewhat firm ride and potentially expensive maintenance costs.

**Engine**

**N55 3.0L Turbocharged Inline-6**

*   **Oil Filter Housing Gasket Leak**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Oil leak from the oil filter housing, noticeable oil stains on the engine, burning oil smell, low oil level warning.
    *   Solution: Replace the oil filter housing gasket.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **VANOS Solenoid Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rough idle, reduced engine power, check engine light. Diagnostic trouble codes (DTCs) related to VANOS (Variable Valve Timing).
    *   Solution: Replace the faulty VANOS solenoid(s).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **High Pressure Fuel Pump (HPFP) Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Long crank times, engine misfires, reduced engine power, stalling, check engine light.
    *   Solution: Replace the high-pressure fuel pump.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*No Issues Found*

**Transmission**

**ZF 8HP 8-Speed Automatic**

*   **Mechatronic Unit Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Harsh or delayed shifting, slipping between gears, transmission fault codes, "limp mode."
    *   Solution: Replace or rebuild the mechatronic unit (valve body).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1500 - $3000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**6-Speed Manual**

*No Issues Found*

**Suspension**

*   **Cracked Front Coil Springs**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Clicking or popping noises from the front suspension, uneven ride height, visible crack in the coil spring.
    *   Solution: Replace the front coil springs (recommended to replace in pairs).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Battery Drain Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Car not starting after being parked for a short time, frequent jump starts, error messages related to low voltage.
    *   Solution: Diagnose parasitic drain, check battery condition, replace faulty modules or components causing the drain.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+ (depending on the cause)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Faulty Window Regulator**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Window not operating properly, slow or jerky window movement, clicking noises from the door panel.
    *   Solution: Replace the window regulator.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Failing Adaptive Headlight Motors**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Headlights no longer turning, error code messages on the dash
    *   Solution: Replace the entire Adaptive Headlight assembly.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1000 - $2000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Sticking iDrive Controller**
    *   Description/Symptoms: iDrive controller is difficult to turn or press, sticking in certain positions.
    *   Solution: Replace the iDrive controller.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $700+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2014 BMW M235i, while generally reliable, can experience certain issues related to its performance-oriented nature and complex systems. Engine issues such as oil leaks and VANOS problems are common, as well as transmission problems. Electrical gremlins, like battery drain issues, can also occur. Repair costs can range from a few hundred dollars to several thousand depending on the severity and complexity of the issue. Prospective buyers should be aware of these potential problems and have the vehicle inspected by a qualified mechanic before purchase.
