# 2016 BMW M235i Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2016 BMW M235i offers an engaging driving experience with robust performance credentials, making it an appealing choice for enthusiasts looking at the used market. However, like any performance-oriented luxury vehicle, prospective buyers should carefully evaluate potential maintenance histories. While owner complaints in federal databases remain relatively sparse, reports touch on isolated mechanical, electrical, and body hardware concerns that are worth keeping on your radar during a pre-purchase inspection.

---

## Engine

### 3.0L Turbocharged Inline-6 (N55)

The 2016 M235i features a 3.0-liter turbocharged inline-six engine (N55) producing approximately 320 horsepower and 330 lb-ft of torque. It delivers strong, smooth power delivery and is generally well-regarded mechanically, though surrounding fuel and cooling systems require routine observation as mileage climbs.

**Fuel Odor and Tank Cracking**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may notice an unprovoked fuel odor inside or around the vehicle, sometimes traced back to cracks in the fuel tank housing.
- **Solution:** Inspect the fuel tank assembly thoroughly and replace the tank if structural cracking is confirmed by a qualified technician.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Sport Automatic / 6-Speed Manual

Power is sent to the wheels via either a responsive 6-speed manual or a quick-shifting 8-speed ZF automatic transmission, both paired with rear-wheel drive or xDrive all-wheel drive. These gearboxes are widely praised for their refinement and durability, though rare case seepage can occur.

**Transmission Case Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Fluid pooling under the vehicle or wet spots observed directly on the transmission housing exterior.
- **Solution:** Have a professional mechanic inspect the source of the leak; depending on severity, component sealing or casing replacement may be required.

## Brakes

### Brake Lighting Circuit

The braking hardware itself offers strong stopping power, but electrical integration with the rear lighting harness warrants careful pre-purchase checking.

**Rear Brake Light Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Brake lights fail intermittently or stop working entirely due to localized wiring harness degradation or connector melting near the light assembly.
- **Solution:** Inspect the rear tail lamp wiring harnesses and bulb carriers for melting or corrosion, and repair or replace affected wiring connectors.

## Exterior

### Sunroof Assembly

Exterior styling is sleek and aggressive, though owners should inspect glass panels and trim for any signs of stress.

**Spontaneous Sunroof Shattering**
- **Symptoms:** The glass panel of the sunroof may make a loud popping noise and fracture suddenly while driving under normal conditions without external impact.
- **Solution:** Replace the shattered glass panel and clear out any remaining glass debris from the track mechanism.

## Safety

### Seat Belts

Cabin safety features operate reliably overall, though mechanical wear on seatbelt retractors can occasionally hinder smooth everyday operation.

**Slow Retraction and Twisting**
- **Symptoms:** The driver-side seatbelt retracts sluggishly, twists easily during use, and can become jammed inside the B-pillar trim.
- **Solution:** Clean the seatbelt webbing and guide loop, or inspect the retraction mechanism and replace the assembly if binding persists.

## Summary Verdict

The 2016 BMW M235i remains an exciting, high-performing luxury sport coupe that rewards drivers with sharp handling and brisk acceleration. While owner-reported complaints highlight a few specific issues—such as rare transmission case leaks, rear lighting wiring quirks, and isolated sunroof glass incidents—they do not point to systemic powertrain failure. Buyers should prioritize a comprehensive pre-purchase inspection focusing on the undercarriage, electrical connectors, and fluid levels to ensure a trouble-free ownership experience.
