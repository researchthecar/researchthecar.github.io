# 2016 BMW M235i

### Overview
The 2016 BMW M235i, a performance-oriented compact luxury coupe, is generally regarded as a reliable vehicle, especially considering its potent powertrain. It offers a compelling blend of sporty handling and everyday usability. While most owners report a positive ownership experience with minimal unexpected issues, like any performance car, certain components may require more attention than a standard commuter vehicle. This analysis focuses on documented, non-wear-and-tear related reliability concerns specific to the M235i's performance-oriented nature and its available configurations.

---


## Engine

### 3.0L Twin-Scroll Turbocharged Inline-6 (N55)
**Turbocharger Wastegate Rattle**
- **Symptoms:** Audible rattling noise from the turbocharger area, particularly noticeable on startup or at low RPMs. May not always be accompanied by performance loss.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the wastegate actuator or the entire turbocharger assembly.
- **Cost:** $1,200 - $2,500

**PCV System Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Rough idle, increased oil consumption, potential for oil leaks, and sometimes check engine lights.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the Positive Crankcase Ventilation (PCV) valve or the entire valve cover assembly.
- **Cost:** $300 - $700

### Summary Verdict
The 2016 BMW M235i demonstrates strong overall reliability, particularly for its performance segment. The N55 engine, while robust, has a couple of known, albeit not universally experienced, issues such as wastegate rattle and PCV system problems. These are generally manageable with timely diagnosis and repair. Both the 8-speed automatic and 6-speed manual transmissions are highly regarded for their durability. Other systems like suspension, electrical, exterior, and interior components are typically free from widespread, significant reliability concerns. Regular maintenance is key to ensuring the longevity of this enthusiast-focused vehicle.
