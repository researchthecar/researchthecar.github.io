# 2021 BMW 745Le xDrive

### Overview
The 2021 BMW 745Le xDrive, a plug-in hybrid variant of the flagship 7 Series sedan, combines luxury, performance, and efficiency. It features a sophisticated powertrain designed for both electric-only driving and enhanced gasoline performance. As with any complex luxury vehicle, understanding potential reliability concerns is crucial for owners. This analysis focuses on documented technical issues beyond standard maintenance, providing insights into specific components and their potential failure points. The data aims to offer a clear picture of the 745Le xDrive's reliability profile, highlighting areas of strength and potential concern for prospective and current owners.

---


## Engine

### 3.0L Turbocharged Inline-6 Plug-in Hybrid
**Cooling System Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Coolant loss, engine overheating, visible puddles under the vehicle.
- **Solution:** Inspection and replacement of leaking hoses, seals, or radiator.
- **Cost:** $300 - $700

**Hybrid Battery Pack Degradation**
- **Symptoms:** Reduced electric-only range, decreased overall fuel efficiency, warning lights.
- **Solution:** Battery diagnostics and potential replacement of the hybrid battery module.
- **Cost:** $5,000 - $15,000+

## Suspension

### Adaptive Air Suspension
**Air Spring Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle sagging on one or more corners, uneven ride height, compressor running excessively.
- **Solution:** Leak detection and replacement of faulty air springs or lines.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1,200 per corner

**Air Compressor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Inability to adjust ride height, constant compressor noise, suspension warning lights.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the air suspension compressor unit.
- **Cost:** $600 - $1,500


## Electrical

### Summary Verdict
The 2021 BMW 745Le xDrive generally presents a solid reliability record for its powertrain and core mechanical components. The primary areas of concern tend to revolve around the sophisticated hybrid system, particularly the longevity and potential degradation of the high-voltage battery pack, which can represent a significant repair cost. Additionally, the advanced adaptive air suspension system, while offering superior comfort, can be prone to leaks in air springs or compressor failures over time. Electrical gremlins, often manifesting as infotainment system glitches or premature 12V battery failure, are also noted. Owners should be prepared for potentially higher maintenance costs associated with these advanced technologies compared to less complex vehicles. Regular servicing and prompt attention to any warning signs are recommended to maintain optimal performance and longevity.
