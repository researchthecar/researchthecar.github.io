# 2021 BMW 745e xDrive

### Overview
The 2021 BMW 745e xDrive, a plug-in hybrid variant of the flagship 7 Series, combines luxury, performance, and electrified efficiency. It features a sophisticated powertrain designed for both spirited driving and reduced emissions. As with any advanced luxury vehicle, understanding its potential reliability points is crucial for owners. This analysis focuses on documented technical issues beyond standard maintenance, providing insights into common concerns and their potential resolutions for this specific model year and configuration. The aim is to offer a clear picture of the vehicle's technical robustness.

---


## Engine

### 3.0L Turbocharged Inline-6 Plug-in Hybrid
**Hybrid Battery Degradation**
- **Symptoms:** Reduced electric-only range, decreased overall power output, charging issues.
- **Solution:** Battery replacement (expensive) or specialized hybrid system diagnostics and repair.
- **Cost:** $5,000 - $15,000+

**Cooling System Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Overheating, coolant loss, visible leaks under the vehicle.
- **Solution:** Inspection and replacement of hoses, radiator, water pump, or thermostat.
- **Cost:** $300 - $1,200

## Suspension

### Adaptive Air Suspension
**Air Spring Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle sagging on one or more corners, uneven ride height, compressor running excessively.
- **Solution:** Replacement of leaking air springs or air compressor.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1,500 per corner (spring), $400 - $1,000 (compressor)


## Electrical

### Infotainment System
**Software Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Screen freezing, unresponsive touch controls, connectivity issues (Bluetooth, CarPlay/Android Auto).
- **Solution:** Software updates, system resets, or module replacement.
- **Cost:** $100 - $1,500


### Hybrid System Electronics
**Control Module Failures**
- **Symptoms:** Warning lights related to hybrid system, loss of power, inability to charge.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and replacement of faulty hybrid control modules.
- **Cost:** $1,000 - $3,000+

### Summary Verdict
The 2021 BMW 745e xDrive generally exhibits good reliability for its powertrain and core mechanical components, with the exception of potential issues common to advanced hybrid systems. The primary concerns revolve around the longevity and potential degradation of the hybrid battery, which can be a significant repair cost. Additionally, some owners have reported issues with the adaptive air suspension, specifically air spring leaks, and occasional glitches within the complex infotainment and hybrid control electronics. While the engine and transmission are robust, proactive maintenance and awareness of these specific hybrid-related and electronic components are recommended for optimal long-term ownership.
