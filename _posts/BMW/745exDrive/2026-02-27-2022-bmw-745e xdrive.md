# 2022 BMW 745e xDrive

### Overview
The 2022 BMW 745e xDrive, a plug-in hybrid variant of the flagship 7 Series, combines luxury, performance, and electrified efficiency. As a high-end luxury sedan, it is equipped with advanced technology and a sophisticated powertrain designed for a refined driving experience. Reliability data for this specific model year and configuration indicates a generally strong performance, with most reported issues being minor or related to the complex hybrid system's integration. Owners generally appreciate the blend of power from the gasoline engine and electric motor, contributing to a quiet and potent ride. However, as with any technologically advanced vehicle, attention to specific components within the hybrid system and electronics is warranted.

---


## Engine

### 3.0L Turbocharged Inline-6 Plug-in Hybrid
**Hybrid Battery Cooling System**
- **Symptoms:** Reduced electric range, "Check Hybrid System" warning light, potential overheating.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and repair or replacement of faulty cooling pump, hoses, or coolant.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1500

**Electric Motor Performance Degradation**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of electric-only acceleration, reduced overall power output.
- **Solution:** Software updates or potential replacement of electric motor components.
- **Cost:** $1000 - $3000

### Adaptive Air Suspension
**Air Spring Leaks**

### Summary Verdict
The 2022 BMW 745e xDrive demonstrates good overall reliability, particularly in its conventional powertrain and structural components. The primary areas of concern revolve around the sophisticated plug-in hybrid system, specifically the battery cooling and electric motor performance, which can incur significant repair costs if issues arise. Electrical gremlins within the infotainment system and the auxiliary battery are also noted, though often resolvable with software updates or straightforward replacements. While not immune to the complexities of its advanced technology, the 745e xDrive offers a generally dependable luxury experience, provided regular maintenance and prompt attention to hybrid system warnings are observed.
