# 2011 BMW 750i

### Overview
The 2011 BMW 750i, a flagship luxury sedan, offers a potent combination of performance and opulence. Powered by a robust V8 engine and sophisticated drivetrain, it aims to deliver a refined driving experience. While generally well-regarded for its comfort and technology, like any complex luxury vehicle, it has specific areas that have seen more attention from owners regarding reliability. This analysis focuses on documented technical issues beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential concerns for owners and prospective buyers of this model year.

---


## Engine

### 4.4L Twin-Turbo V8 (N63)
**Turbocharger Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of power, increased oil consumption, blue smoke from exhaust, turbo whine.
- **Solution:** Replacement of turbochargers. Often recommended to address oil consumption issues concurrently.
- **Cost:** $3,000 - $6,000

**Fuel Injector Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Rough idle, misfires, check engine light, poor fuel economy.
- **Solution:** Replacement of faulty fuel injectors.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1,500

**Valve Stem Seal Leakage**
- **Symptoms:** Blue smoke on startup (especially after sitting), increased oil consumption.
- **Solution:** Replacement of valve stem seals. This is a labor-intensive repair.
- **Cost:** $2,500 - $4,500

- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of active anti-roll bars and related hydraulic components.

## Electrical

### General Electrical System
**iDrive System Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Infotainment system freezing, screen flickering, unresponsive controls.
- **Solution:** Software updates or replacement of the iDrive control unit or display.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1,500

**Battery Drain Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Difficulty starting, battery warning lights, electrical components malfunctioning.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and repair of parasitic draws, or replacement of the battery and/or alternator.
- **Cost:** $300 - $1,000

### Summary Verdict
The 2011 BMW 750i is a luxurious and powerful vehicle, but its complexity, particularly the N63 V8 engine, presents potential reliability concerns. Owners should be aware of common issues such as turbocharger problems, fuel injector failures, and valve stem seal leaks, which can be costly to repair. While the transmission and suspension are generally robust, specific electronic systems like the iDrive and potential battery drain issues require attention. Regular maintenance and prompt addressing of early symptoms are crucial for mitigating these risks and ensuring a more dependable ownership experience.
