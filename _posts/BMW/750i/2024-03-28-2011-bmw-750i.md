**2011 BMW 750i**

The 2011 BMW 750i is a luxury sedan powered by a 4.4-liter V8 engine making 400 horsepower and 450 lb-ft of torque. It is available with either a 6-speed automatic or 8-speed automatic transmission. The 750i is known for its comfortable interior, powerful engine, and advanced technology features. However, it has also been reported to have some issues.

**Engine**

**4.4L V8**

* **Intake Valve Carbon Buildup**
    * Description/Symptoms: Carbon deposits build up on the intake valves, restricting airflow and causing a loss of power and fuel economy.
    * Solution: Cleaning the intake valves with a chemical solution or manually removing the deposits.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **High-Pressure Fuel Pump Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The high-pressure fuel pump fails, causing a loss of fuel pressure and engine stalling.
    * Solution: Replacing the high-pressure fuel pump.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**6-Speed Automatic**

* **Torque Converter Shudder**
    * Description/Symptoms: The torque converter shudders or vibrates during acceleration or gear changes.
    * Solution: Replacing the torque converter.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,000+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Transmission Fluid Leak**
    * Description/Symptoms: Transmission fluid leaks from the transmission pan or seals.
    * Solution: Replacing the leaking components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**8-Speed Automatic**

* **Mechatronic Unit Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The mechatronic unit, which controls the transmission, fails, causing the transmission to malfunction.
    * Solution: Replacing the mechatronic unit.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $3,000 - $5,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Air Suspension Compressor Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The air suspension compressor fails, causing the suspension to sag or become unresponsive.
    * Solution: Replacing the air suspension compressor.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Control Arm Bushing Wear**
    * Description/Symptoms: The control arm bushings wear out, causing the suspension to become loose and noisy.
    * Solution: Replacing the control arm bushings.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,200+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Battery Discharge**
    * Description/Symptoms: The battery discharges rapidly, even when the vehicle is not in use.
    * Solution: Identifying and disconnecting the source of the battery drain.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the source of the drain
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Headlight Condensation**
    * Description/Symptoms: Condensation forms inside the headlight assemblies, causing fogging or water droplets.
    * Solution: Replacing the headlight assemblies.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
    * Recalls: NHTSA Campaign ID: 21E020000

**Exterior**

* **Sunroof Rattle**
    * Description/Symptoms: The sunroof rattles or makes noise when opened or closed.
    * Solution: Adjusting the sunroof mechanism or replacing the sunroof seals.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **iDrive System Malfunction**
    * Description/Symptoms: The iDrive infotainment system freezes, crashes, or becomes unresponsive.
    * Solution: Updating the software or replacing the iDrive control unit.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Navigation System Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The navigation system fails to provide accurate directions or display maps.
    * Solution: Updating the navigation software or replacing the navigation module.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,200+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2011 BMW 750i is a highly capable and luxurious sedan, but it is not without its issues. Some of these issues, such as the intake valve carbon buildup and high-pressure fuel pump failure, can be costly to repair. However, many of the other issues are relatively minor and can be fixed for a reasonable price. Overall, the 750i is a solid choice for those seeking a high-performance and technologically advanced vehicle, but it is important to be aware of the potential issues before making a purchase decision.
