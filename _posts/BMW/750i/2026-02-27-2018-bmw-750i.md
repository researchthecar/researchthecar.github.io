# 2018 BMW 750i

### Overview
The 2018 BMW 750i, a flagship luxury sedan, is generally regarded as a reliable vehicle, especially considering its complex engineering and high-performance nature. It offers a sophisticated driving experience with advanced technology and premium comfort. While most owners report a positive ownership experience, like any high-end automobile, certain areas may require attention over time. This analysis focuses on documented technical issues beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential areas of concern for prospective and current owners. The 750i is equipped with a potent V8 engine and advanced drivetrain, contributing to its performance but also presenting specific points for reliability assessment.

---


## Engine

### 4.4L Twin-Turbo V8 (N63TU2)
**Oil Consumption**
- **Symptoms:** Excessive oil consumption, potential for blue smoke from exhaust, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Regular oil level checks, potential PCV valve replacement, or in severe cases, engine internal component inspection and repair.
- **Cost:** $300 - $1,500

**Turbocharger Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of power, whistling noises from the engine bay, increased oil consumption.
- **Solution:** Inspection and potential replacement of turbocharger seals, lines, or the entire turbocharger unit.
- **Cost:** $2,000 - $5,000

**Ignition Coil Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Rough idling, misfires, check engine light, reduced engine performance.
- **Solution:** Replacement of faulty ignition coils and spark plugs.
- **Cost:** $400 - $800

## Suspension

### Adaptive Air Suspension
**Air Spring Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle sagging on one or more corners, suspension warning light, uneven ride height.
- **Solution:** Replacement of leaking air springs or associated lines.
- **Cost:** $800 - $2,000

**Compressor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Inability to adjust ride height, suspension warning light, constant vehicle sag.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the air suspension compressor.
- **Cost:** $700 - $1,500


## Electrical

### Infotainment System
**iDrive Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Screen freezing, unresponsive controls, Bluetooth connectivity issues, navigation system errors.
- **Solution:** Software updates, system resets, or potential replacement of the iDrive controller or head unit.
- **Cost:** $200 - $1,200

### Summary Verdict
The 2018 BMW 750i offers a generally strong reliability record for a luxury performance sedan. The 4.4L twin-turbo V8 engine, while powerful, has some documented tendencies for oil consumption and potential turbocharger or ignition coil issues, which are not uncommon for high-performance engines of this era. The ZF 8-speed automatic transmission is robust and typically problem-free. The adaptive air suspension, while providing excellent comfort, can be a source of repair costs due to potential leaks or compressor failures. Electrical gremlins, particularly with the infotainment system and occasional battery drain, are also noted. Overall, the 750i is a reliable choice if routine checks and prompt attention to potential issues are maintained, especially concerning the engine and suspension components.
