# 2017 BMW 750i

### Overview
The 2017 BMW 750i, a flagship luxury sedan, is generally regarded as a reliable vehicle, especially considering its complex engineering and high-performance nature. It offers a sophisticated driving experience with advanced technology and premium comfort. While most owners report a positive ownership experience, like any high-end vehicle, certain areas can occasionally present issues. This analysis focuses on documented technical reliability concerns beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential problem areas and their typical resolutions for the specific powertrain configurations available for this model year. Understanding these potential issues can help prospective buyers and current owners manage expectations and maintenance.

---


## Engine

### 4.4L Twin-Turbo V8 (N63TU2)
**Turbocharger Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Reduced engine power, increased oil consumption, whistling noises from the engine bay.
- **Solution:** Inspection and potential replacement of turbochargers and associated lines.
- **Cost:** $3,000 - $6,000

**Ignition Coil and Spark Plug Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Misfires, rough idling, check engine light illumination.
- **Solution:** Replacement of faulty ignition coils and spark plugs.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1,000

**Fuel Injector Problems**
- **Symptoms:** Rough idle, poor fuel economy, engine misfires, difficulty starting.
- **Solution:** Replacement of affected fuel injectors.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1,500

## Suspension

### Adaptive Drive (Optional)
**Active Roll Stabilization Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** Increased body roll during cornering, warning lights on the dashboard related to suspension.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and potential replacement of active anti-roll bars or related sensors and hydraulic components.
- **Cost:** $2,500 - $5,000


### Standard Air Suspension
**Air Spring Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle sagging on one corner, compressor running excessively, suspension warning light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of leaking air springs or air compressor.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1,800


## Electrical

### Summary Verdict
The 2017 BMW 750i, equipped with the 4.4L Twin-Turbo V8, presents a generally reliable luxury sedan experience. The primary areas that may require attention beyond standard maintenance include potential issues with the turbochargers, ignition system, and fuel injectors, which are common for high-performance engines. The ZF 8-speed automatic transmission is robust and rarely reports widespread problems. Suspension components, particularly the optional Adaptive Drive and standard air suspension, can occasionally develop leaks or electronic faults. Electrical systems, especially the iDrive infotainment and battery management, may experience software glitches or drain issues. Overall, while not immune to the complexities of a luxury performance vehicle, the 750i offers solid reliability, with most potential issues being addressable through specialized repair.
