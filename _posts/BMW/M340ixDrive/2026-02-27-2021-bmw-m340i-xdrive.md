# 2021 BMW M340i xDrive Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2021 BMW M340i xDrive, buyers are looking at one of the more praised performance sedans on the market, combining everyday luxury with robust power. While owner reports to federal safety regulators are relatively limited, prospective buyers should still review specific powertrain specifications, potential maintenance concerns, and general owner feedback before finalizing a purchase.

---

## Engine

### 3.0L Turbocharged Inline-6 (B58TU)

The 3.0-liter turbocharged inline-six engine (B58TU) produces approximately 382 horsepower and 369 lb-ft of torque, offering rapid acceleration and generally strong mechanical durability. It serves as the exclusive powerplant for this performance-oriented sport sedan.

**Thermoplastic Oil Pump Component Concerns**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have expressed concern regarding the internal thermoplastic components of the variable-displacement oil pump, which some reports suggest could potentially lead to severe engine performance issues.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified mechanic inspect the oil pump assembly and housing during a pre-purchase inspection for any signs of material degradation or early wear.

**Drivetrain Error and Burning Odor**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may occasionally notice an intermittent burning smell or experience sudden drivetrain error codes appearing on the dashboard.
- **Solution:** Diagnostic scanning by a dealership or specialist is required to pull stored fault codes and track down the exact source of any fluid leaks or electrical glitches.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 8-speed automatic transmission delivers crisp, rapid gear changes paired with the xDrive all-wheel-drive system, maintaining a reputation for smooth operation and responsive performance.

**Unexpected Gear Shifting**
- **Symptoms:** In rare instances, drivers have reported the vehicle independently shifting between reverse, drive, and park while maneuvering at low speeds.
- **Solution:** Software updates or inspection of the electronic gear selector switch and transmission control module may be necessary if erratic shifting occurs.

## Steering

### Front Suspension and Steering Linkage

Monitors front-end mechanical feedback, ensuring responsive handling while watching out for early wear in bushings or steering joints.

**Creaking Noise and Stiff Steering**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may notice a low creaking or groaning noise coming from the front wheels when traveling at slow speeds over small bumps, eventually accompanied by a stiff steering feel when turning.
- **Solution:** Inspect front suspension bushings, control arms, and steering components for wear, and lubricate or replace affected hardware as needed.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V554000: Seat Belts](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=M340i&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** As such, these assemblies fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 208, "Occupant Crash Protection." During a crash, a seat belt retractor that does not function as intended increases the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the seat belt retractors (including the seat belts) and, if necessary, replace the retractors, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V761000: Electrical System:12V/24V/48V Battery](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=M340i&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The connection between the 48-Volt battery and the starter-generator may not have been assembled properly, which can cause increased electrical resistance at the connections. Increased electrical resistance can increase the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** BMW will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the connection between the 48-Volt battery and the starter-generator assembly, replacing it if necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V096000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=M340i&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** When shifting into Reverse, a small portion of the rearview image may be obscured, or the screen may not illuminate. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 111, "Rear Visibility." If the driver relies on only the rearview camera image, the missing or reduced rearview image view.
- **What to do:** BMW will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the rearview camera software and update it if necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V298000: Seat Belts:Front:Buckle Assembly](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=M340i&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** An improperly installed seat belt assembly may not restrain an occupant as expected, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** BMW will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the front seat belt buckle assembly connection and, if necessary, replace it, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V211000: Seats](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=M340i&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The seat frame may not have been welded properly to the seat rails. An improperly welded seat frame may not properly restrain the occupant during a crash, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the seat frame and back rest, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V584000: Seat Belts](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=M340i&modelYear=2021)**

- **What to do:** Dealers will update the receiver audio module software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2021 BMW M340i xDrive offers an impressive blend of luxury and sports-car performance, backed by a generally well-regarded powertrain. While isolated owner complaints point to potential oil pump component considerations and rare electronic shift anomalies, a thorough pre-purchase inspection by a knowledgeable technician will help ensure the vehicle is in top mechanical condition.
