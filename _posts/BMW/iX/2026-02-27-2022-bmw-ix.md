# 2022 BMW iX Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2022 BMW iX represents a bold entry into the luxury electric SUV space, combining cutting-edge technology with high-end comfort. However, as an early model-year electric vehicle, owner reports highlight a few key areas of concern regarding steering alerts, power delivery, and electrical software glitches. Prospective buyers should review service history and ensure all pertinent manufacturer updates have been completed before purchase.

---

## Engine

### Dual-Motor Electric Powertrain (M60)

The high-performance M60 variant ups the power output to over 600 horsepower with dual electric motors for instantaneous torque delivery. It shares most underlying architecture with the standard model while delivering enhanced performance.

## Transmission

### Dual-Motor Electric Powertrain (xDrive50)

The xDrive50 features dual electric motors generating around 516 horsepower, providing rapid acceleration and all-wheel drive capability. While generally powerful and smooth, early software management has occasionally led to unexpected propulsion warnings for some owners.

**Loss of Motive Power**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers experience unexpected power loss errors while driving and accelerating, sometimes accompanied by dashboard blackouts or GPS errors.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified dealership inspect the powertrain software and electrical systems for diagnostic fault codes and perform necessary updates or component replacements.

### Single-Speed Direct Drive Transmission

Utilizes a single-speed automatic transmission paired with dual-motor all-wheel drive for seamless, shift-free power delivery. The mechanical setup is generally robust and free of traditional multi-speed gear wear concerns.

## Steering

### Steering Rack and Assistance

Electronic power steering components provide precise feedback, though some early owners have experienced intermittent warning messages regarding steering effort and assistance.

**Reduced Driver Steering / Changed Steering Effort**
- **Symptoms:** Warning messages appear on the dashboard indicating reduced driver steering or changed steering effort, instructing the driver to take the vehicle to a service center.
- **Solution:** Dealer inspection is required to evaluate the steering system and update or replace faulty electronic steering modules as needed.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V240000: Air Bags: Air Bag/Restraint Control Module:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=iX&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** The air bag malfunction indicator light and display message may not illuminate in the event of a problem with the air bag control or pedestrian protection systems, due to incorrect software. An air bag malfunction indicator light that fails to warn the driver of a problem increases the risk of injury in a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will reprogram the air bag control unit software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V483000: Electrical System: Instrument Cluster/Panel](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=iX&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** While in Valet Parking Mode, the Central Information Display (CID) may not display critical safety information, such as warning messages and/or warning lights. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 101, "Control and Displays." A display that does not show critical information, such as.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the display's head unit software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V541000: Electrical System:Propulsion System:Traction Battery](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=iX&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** A short-circuit in a high voltage battery increases the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the high voltage battery, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V944000: Electrical System:Propulsion System:Traction Battery Thermal:Management:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=iX&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** The high voltage battery electronic control unit (ECU) software may cause an interruption of electrical power. An interruption of electrical power may result in a sudden loss of drive power, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the high voltage battery ECU software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V026000: Back Over Prevention:Warnings:External/Pedestrian Alert](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=iX&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 141, "Minimum Sound Requirements for Hybrid and Electric Vehicles." Without external warning sounds, pedestrians may be unaware of an approaching vehicle, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the external artificial sound generator software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V079000: Air Bags:Frontal](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=iX&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** The front driver's air bag may have been manufactured incorrectly and may not deploy as intended during a crash. A driver's air bag that does not deploy as intended during a crash increases the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the driver's air bag, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V295000: Electrical System:Propulsion System:Traction Battery](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=iX&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** An engine stall increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the high voltage battery cell monitoring circuit, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V409000: Vehicle Speed Control:Cruise Control](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=iX&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** A sudden increase in vehicle speed can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the vehicle software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2022 BMW iX offers impressive electric performance and luxury, but its status as an introductory model year means prospective buyers should exercise caution. Pay close attention to software stability, steering warning messages, and propulsion reliability. Buyers should verify that a trusted mechanic or dealership has addressed any pending software notices prior to finalizing a purchase.
