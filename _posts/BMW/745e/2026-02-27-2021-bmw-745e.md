# 2021 BMW 745e

### Overview
The 2021 BMW 745e, a plug-in hybrid variant of the flagship 7 Series sedan, combines luxury, performance, and electrified efficiency. It features a sophisticated powertrain designed for both spirited driving and reduced emissions. As with any advanced vehicle, understanding potential reliability concerns is crucial for owners. This analysis focuses on documented technical issues beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into common problem areas and their potential resolutions. The 745e's complex hybrid system and premium features necessitate a detailed look at its reliability profile.

---


## Engine

### 3.0L Turbocharged Inline-6 Plug-in Hybrid
**Hybrid Battery Degradation**
- **Symptoms:** Reduced electric-only range, decreased overall power output, charging issues.
- **Solution:** Battery pack replacement.
- **Cost:** $10,000 - $20,000+

**Cooling System Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Overheating, coolant loss, warning lights.
- **Solution:** Repair or replace leaking hoses, water pump, or radiator.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1,500

## Suspension

### Adaptive Air Suspension
**Air Spring Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle sagging on one or more corners, uneven ride height, compressor running excessively.
- **Solution:** Replace leaking air springs or air compressor.
- **Cost:** $800 - $2,500 per corner for springs, $500 - $1,200 for compressor.


## Electrical

### Infotainment System
**Software Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Screen freezing, unresponsive touch controls, connectivity issues (Bluetooth, Apple CarPlay/Android Auto).
- **Solution:** Software updates, system resets, or module replacement.
- **Cost:** $200 - $1,000


### Hybrid System Electronics
**Control Module Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** Hybrid system warning lights, loss of power, charging faults.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and replacement of faulty control modules.
- **Cost:** $1,000 - $3,000

### Summary Verdict
The 2021 BMW 745e, while offering a luxurious and technologically advanced driving experience, presents some potential reliability concerns primarily related to its complex hybrid powertrain and associated electronics. Issues such as hybrid battery degradation and cooling system leaks are significant but not uncommon for vehicles of this type. Electrical gremlins within the infotainment and hybrid control modules can also arise. The air suspension, a common feature in luxury sedans, can experience leaks. While the engine and transmission are generally robust, the hybrid components and advanced electrical systems require diligent maintenance and can lead to costly repairs if issues develop. Owners should be prepared for potential expenses related to these advanced systems.
