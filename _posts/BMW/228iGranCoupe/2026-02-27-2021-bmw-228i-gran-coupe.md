# 2021 BMW 228i Gran Coupe Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2021 BMW 228i Gran Coupe, buyers will find a stylish and engaging entry-level luxury sedan backed by solid German engineering. While overall owner satisfaction is generally high, reviewing owner-reported feedback highlights a few specific areas—such as brakes, electrical accessories, and trim hardware—that warrant a careful pre-purchase inspection.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4 (228i)

Powered by a 2.0-liter turbocharged inline-4 engine producing approximately 228 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque, this powerplant offers brisk acceleration and modern efficiency. It generally delivers dependable performance when maintained according to factory schedules, though minor component wear can occasionally surface.

**Engine Mount Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have reported premature engine mount failure resulting in dealership service recommendations at relatively low mileage.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified mechanic inspect the engine mounts during a pre-purchase evaluation and replace any worn components as necessary.
- **Cost:** $1200

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

Equipped with a responsive 8-speed automatic transmission paired with front-wheel or all-wheel drive, this unit delivers smooth and rapid gear changes. It has a strong reputation for durability with no major recurring owner complaints reported.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

The braking system provides strong stopping power, though some owners experience early wear on rear brake components.

**Rear Brake Noise and Premature Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may notice an abnormal grinding or squealing sound coming from the rear brakes, often accompanied by the service brake warning light.
- **Solution:** Inspect rear brake pads and rotors for premature wear, and replace the worn friction materials and discs as needed.

## Exterior

### Exterior Lighting

Electrical accessories and lighting systems offer modern convenience features, but individual modules can occasionally experience thermal issues.

**Daytime Running Light Module Overheating**
- **Symptoms:** Daytime running light modules can overheat and cause thermal damage to the internal fiber optic tubes within the headlight housing.
- **Solution:** Replace the affected DRL module promptly upon noticing discoloration or lighting failure to prevent damage to the entire headlight assembly.

### Visibility and Sunroof

Exterior panels and glass components give the vehicle a sleek profile, though rare structural glass incidents have been noted.

**Sunroof Glass Fracture**
- **Symptoms:** The panoramic sunroof glass may unexpectedly shatter or explode while driving at highway speeds.
- **Solution:** Inspect the sunroof glass for any preexisting stress cracks or chips before purchase, and check that window seals are properly fitted.

### Door Latches and Locks

Cabin appointments are driver-focused and luxurious, though minor hardware binding can occasionally affect entry doors.

**Door Bracket Scraping**
- **Symptoms:** A noticeable scraping or binding sound can occur from inside the door brackets when attempting to open the front doors.
- **Solution:** Lubricate or adjust the door hinges and internal bracket mechanisms to restore smooth operation.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V554000: Seat Belts](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=228i&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** As such, these assemblies fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 208, "Occupant Crash Protection." During a crash, a seat belt retractor that does not function as intended increases the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the seat belt retractors (including the seat belts) and, if necessary, replace the retractors, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V091000: Visibility:Power Window Devices And Controls](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=228i&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 118, "Power-operated Window Systems." If the sunroof continues to close after the vehicle is shut off, it may increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** BMW will notify owners, and dealers will update the sunroof electronic coding software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V584000: Seat Belts](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=228i&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 208, "Occupant Crash Protection." An audible chime that does not properly alert the driver to fasten their seat belt increases the risk of injury in a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the receiver audio module software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.
