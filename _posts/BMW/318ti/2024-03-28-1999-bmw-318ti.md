**Introduction:**

The 1999 BMW 318ti is a compact hatchback that offers agility and performance. It is powered by a 1.9-liter engine and comes standard with a 5-speed manual transmission. Other engine and transmission options include:

* 1.9-liter engine with a 4-speed automatic transmission
* 2.5-liter engine with a 5-speed manual or automatic transmission
* 3.0-liter engine with a 5-speed manual or automatic transmission

The 318ti has received praise for its handling, sporty design, and comfortable interior. However, it has also been criticized for its lack of rear seat space and its relatively high price.

**Engine**

**1.9-liter Engine**

* **Head gasket failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaks, overheating, white smoke from the exhaust
    * Solution: Replace the head gasket
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1000 - $2000+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Timing belt failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Engine stalls, no start
    * Solution: Replace the timing belt
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1000
    * Recalls: None Found

**2.5-liter Engine**

* **No Issues Found**

**3.0-liter Engine**

* **No Issues Found**

**Transmission**

**5-speed Manual Transmission**

* **Shifting problems**
    * Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears, grinding noises
    * Solution: Replace the clutch master cylinder or slave cylinder
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1000
    * Recalls: None Found

**4-speed Automatic Transmission**

* **Transmission slipping**
    * Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, erratic shifting
    * Solution: Rebuild or replace the transmission
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2000 - $3000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Rear suspension clunking**
    * Description/Symptoms: Noises from the rear suspension, especially over bumps
    * Solution: Replace the rear suspension strut mounts
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Electrical gremlins**
    * Description/Symptoms: Problems with various electrical systems, including the lights, wipers, and windows
    * Solution: Diagnose and replace the faulty components
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Rust on the underbody**
    * Description/Symptoms: Corrosion on the metal parts of the underbody
    * Solution: Clean and apply rustproofing
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Dashboard cracks**
    * Description/Symptoms: Cracks on the dashboard panel
    * Solution: Replace the dashboard panel
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1000
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

The 1999 BMW 318ti is a reliable vehicle, but it does have some common issues. The most severe issues include head gasket failure and transmission slipping, which can be costly to repair. Other issues, such as electrical gremlins and rust, are less severe but can still be annoying. Overall, the 318ti is a good choice for those looking for a sporty and fun-to-drive hatchback.
