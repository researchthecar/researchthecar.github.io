# 2017 BMW M240i

### Overview
The 2017 BMW M240i, a performance-oriented compact coupe, generally exhibits strong reliability, reflecting BMW's engineering prowess. Powered by the robust B58 turbocharged inline-six engine, it offers exhilarating performance with a good track record. The available transmissions, both the ZF 8-speed automatic and the manual, are also well-regarded for their durability. While the M240i is built to a high standard, like any performance vehicle, certain components may require attention over time. This analysis focuses on documented common issues beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential areas of concern for owners and prospective buyers.

### Summary Verdict
The 2017 BMW M240i is a highly reliable performance car, particularly noted for its robust B58 engine and well-engineered transmissions. The most commonly cited issues, though not widespread, tend to be related to the electrical system, specifically occasional infotainment glitches and, in some cases, premature battery failure. These are generally manageable with software updates or battery replacement. The suspension, exterior, and interior components demonstrate good durability. Overall, the M240i offers a compelling blend of performance and dependability, with potential buyers advised to be aware of the minor electrical concerns.
