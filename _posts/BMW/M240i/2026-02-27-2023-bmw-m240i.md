# 2023 BMW M240i Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2023 BMW M240i delivers thrilling performance paired with modern luxury, establishing itself as a compelling choice for enthusiasts looking for a daily-drivable sports coupe. Because this model year is relatively new to the pre-owned market, long-term defect trends are still emerging, but initial owner feedback highlights a remarkably robust powertrain. Prospective buyers should still conduct a thorough pre-purchase inspection, particularly checking electronics and fluid levels, to ensure a worry-free ownership experience.

---

## Engine

### 3.0L Turbocharged Inline-6 (B58)

The 3.0-liter turbocharged inline-six engine produces approximately 382 horsepower and 369 lb-ft of torque. Widely praised for its exceptional smoothness and strong reliability history, it serves as the high-performance heart of the M240i.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Sport Automatic Transmission

This ZF 8-speed automatic transmission features paddle shifters and quick, seamless gear changes tailored to the M240i's sporting character. It is generally regarded as one of the most reliable and responsive automatic gearboxes on the market.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V039000: Air Bags:Side/Window](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=M240i&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** The second-row seat backrest cover panels for the side air bags may break during deployment of the side air bags. A backrest cover panel that breaks during side air bag deployment may cause the air bag to deploy improperly, increasing the risk of injury to second-row seat occupants.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the rear seat backrest side air bag cover panels, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V099000: Visibility:Power Window Devices And Controls](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=M240i&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 118, "Power-operated Window Systems." Unintentionally closing the windows or sunroof without the key present can increase the risk of injury to occupants inside the vehicle.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the software for the Electronic Control Unit (ECU), free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.
