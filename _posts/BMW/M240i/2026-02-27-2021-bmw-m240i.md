# 2021 BMW M240i

### Overview
The 2021 BMW M240i, part of the F22 generation's final model year, is generally regarded as a reliable performance coupe. It benefits from BMW's extensive engineering and refinement of the B58 engine and ZF 8-speed automatic transmission, which are known for their robustness. While it offers exhilarating driving dynamics, like any performance vehicle, potential owners should be aware of specific areas that might require attention beyond routine maintenance. This analysis focuses on documented, non-wear-and-tear related issues reported by owners and technicians, providing a technical reliability overview for this model.

### Summary Verdict
The 2021 BMW M240i demonstrates strong overall reliability, particularly with its acclaimed B58 engine and ZF 8-speed automatic transmission, which are known for their durability and performance. The manual transmission option also holds up well. Beyond standard maintenance, the most commonly cited potential issues are related to the electrical system, specifically occasional infotainment glitches and, in some cases, premature battery failure. These are not typically indicative of fundamental design flaws but rather common electronic component wear or usage patterns. Suspension, exterior, and interior components are generally robust. Prospective buyers can expect a dependable performance vehicle with a low probability of major mechanical failures, provided it has been maintained appropriately.
