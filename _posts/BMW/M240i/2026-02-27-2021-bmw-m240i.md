# 2021 BMW M240i Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2021 BMW M240i stands out as a high-performance luxury coupe and convertible, offering a thrilling driving experience paired with everyday usability. Known for its robust inline-six engine and refined driving dynamics, it generally enjoys a strong reputation among enthusiasts looking for balanced performance and modern tech. When shopping for a used model, buyers can appreciate a powertrain that has proven relatively stout, though standard pre-purchase inspections remain a wise step for any high-performance German luxury vehicle.

---

## Engine

### 3.0L Turbocharged Inline-6 (B58)

The 3.0-liter turbocharged inline-six engine produces approximately 335 horsepower and 369 lb-ft of torque. Widely regarded as one of BMW's more reliable modern powerplants, it offers smooth power delivery and strong tuning potential with few widespread mechanical defects reported.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed ZF Automatic Transmission

This 8-speed automatic transmission delivers rapid, smooth shifts and is paired with either rear-wheel drive or xDrive all-wheel drive. It is generally celebrated for both its performance responsiveness and long-term durability in daily driving conditions.

### 6-Speed Manual Transmission

The traditional 6-speed manual transmission is offered for purists seeking maximum engagement on rear-wheel-drive configurations. It features a mechanical clutch setup that remains robust under normal driving conditions, provided previous owners did not heavily abuse it.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V554000: Seat Belts](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=M240i&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** As such, these assemblies fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 208, "Occupant Crash Protection." During a crash, a seat belt retractor that does not function as intended increases the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the seat belt retractors (including the seat belts) and, if necessary, replace the retractors, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V342000: Structure:Body:Roof And Pillars](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=M240i&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The support may separate from the roof liner and fall into the passenger compartment, increasing the risk of injury during a crash necessitating the deployment of the head air bag.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and repair or replace the roof liner as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.
