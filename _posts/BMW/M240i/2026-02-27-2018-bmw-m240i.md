# 2018 BMW M240i Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2018 BMW M240i is widely celebrated for its thrilling performance, premium interior, and the remarkably reliable B58 inline-six engine under the hood. While owner reports to regulatory bodies are sparse, used-car buyers should still perform a thorough inspection of luxury electronics and vehicle lighting systems, which occasionally present minor hiccups.

---

## Engine

### 3.0L Turbocharged Inline-6 (B58)

The 2018 BMW M240i features a 3.0-liter turbocharged inline-six engine pushing approximately 335 horsepower and 369 lb-ft of torque. Widely known as the B58, this powerplant has earned a stellar reputation for robust performance and strong long-term reliability compared to its predecessors.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic

The 8-speed automatic transmission delivers lightning-fast gear changes and smooth power delivery across rear-wheel and all-wheel-drive configurations. It is generally robust and trouble-free when paired with routine fluid changes.

### 6-Speed Manual

The traditional 6-speed manual transmission offers an engaging, direct connection for enthusiast drivers. It is mechanically durable, though buyers should verify clutch health during a pre-purchase inspection.

## Electrical

### Audio and Infotainment Amplifier

The M240i cabin relies heavily on advanced electronic modules to govern audio and connectivity features.

**Radio and Auxiliary Sound Loss**
- **Symptoms:** Owners may experience a complete lack of sound from the radio or auxiliary inputs, sometimes accompanied by extremely loud intermittent crackling noises.
- **Solution:** Diagnose the audio system and replace the failing amplifier if internal failure is confirmed.

## Exterior

### LED Taillights

Exterior lighting relies on modern LED assemblies that can occasionally fail outside of standard warranty coverage.

**Rear Turn Signal Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The integrated LED turn signals within the taillight assemblies may fail prematurely, resulting in exterior lighting faults.
- **Solution:** Replace the affected rear LED taillight assembly to restore proper lighting function.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V465000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Engine](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=M240i&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** A vehicle stall can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** BMW will notify owners, and dealers will replace the crankshaft sensor, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V719000: Electrical System: Instrument Cluster/Panel](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=M240i&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 101, "Control and Displays." If the display does not illuminate during start up or does not illuminate various control and warning symbols while driving, it can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** BMW will notify owners, and dealers will update the instrument cluster display software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2018 BMW M240i stands out as a dependable and highly rewarding sports coupe, anchored by an exceptional powertrain. Although minor electrical and lighting grievances have been reported by individual owners, they do not point to systemic engineering defects. Prospective buyers should prioritize a standard pre-purchase inspection to confirm all electronics, lighting, and mechanical components operate as intended.
