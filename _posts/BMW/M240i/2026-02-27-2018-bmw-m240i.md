# 2018 BMW M240i

### Overview
The 2018 BMW M240i, a potent compact luxury sports coupe, is generally regarded as a reliable vehicle, especially considering its performance-oriented nature. It combines a powerful engine with a refined chassis and a well-appointed interior. While most owners report a positive ownership experience with minimal unexpected issues, like any performance car, certain components may require more attention over time. This analysis focuses on documented, non-wear-and-tear related reliability concerns reported by owners and technicians, excluding routine maintenance. The M240i's robust engineering contributes to its overall strong reliability record.

---


## Engine

### 3.0L Turbocharged Inline-6 (B58)
**Coolant Leaks (Water Pump/Thermostat)**
- **Symptoms:** Coolant level dropping, visible coolant residue, engine overheating warnings.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the faulty water pump or thermostat housing.
- **Cost:** $600 - $1000

**PCV Valve Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Rough idle, increased oil consumption, potential whistling noise from the engine.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the Positive Crankcase Ventilation (PCV) valve assembly.
- **Cost:** $300 - $500

### Summary Verdict
The 2018 BMW M240i demonstrates strong overall reliability, particularly for its segment. The B58 engine is a robust unit, with the most commonly reported non-wear-related issues stemming from the cooling system (water pump, thermostat) and the PCV valve. These are generally manageable repairs. Both the 8-speed automatic and 6-speed manual transmissions are well-regarded for their durability. Electrical gremlins, primarily related to the infotainment system, are infrequent and often resolved with software updates. The suspension, exterior, and interior components are generally free from widespread defects. With proactive maintenance and attention to potential cooling system issues, the M240i offers a reliable and exhilarating driving experience.
