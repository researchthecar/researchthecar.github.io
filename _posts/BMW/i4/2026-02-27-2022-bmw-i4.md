# 2022 BMW i4

### Overview
The 2022 BMW i4 represents BMW's foray into the premium electric sedan market, offering a blend of sporty driving dynamics and advanced technology. As an electric vehicle, its reliability profile differs significantly from traditional internal combustion engine cars. Key areas of focus for electric vehicles include battery health, charging systems, and electric motor performance. Early reports suggest a generally strong reliability for the i4, with owners appreciating its build quality and the refinement of its electric powertrain. However, as with any new model, particularly one with cutting-edge electric technology, there can be isolated incidents or software-related glitches that are addressed through updates or dealer service. This analysis focuses on documented technical issues beyond standard maintenance.

## Electrical

### High-Voltage Battery Pack
**Battery Management System Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Occasional range estimation inaccuracies, slow charging speeds, or dashboard warning lights related to battery performance.
- **Solution:** Software updates for the Battery Management System (BMS) are typically required. In rare cases, component diagnostics and potential replacement may be necessary.
- **Cost:** $0 - $500 (for diagnostics/software updates under warranty, potentially higher if out of warranty and component replacement is needed)


### Infotainment System
**Software Freezes and Connectivity Issues**
- **Symptoms:** The iDrive infotainment screen may freeze, become unresponsive, or experience intermittent connectivity problems with Apple CarPlay/Android Auto or Bluetooth.
- **Solution:** Remote software upgrades (over-the-air) or dealer-performed software updates are the primary solutions. Rebooting the system can sometimes resolve temporary glitches.
- **Cost:** $0 - $300 (for diagnostics/software updates under warranty, potentially higher if out of warranty and hardware issues arise)


### 12V Auxiliary Battery
**Premature Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Various electrical systems may malfunction or fail to operate, including lights, power windows, and infotainment, often accompanied by dashboard warnings about electrical system faults.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the 12V auxiliary battery. Ensure the replacement is compatible with the vehicle's complex electrical architecture.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600

### Summary Verdict
The 2022 BMW i4 demonstrates a promising start in terms of technical reliability, particularly within its electric powertrain components. The primary areas of concern, as with many new EVs, revolve around software glitches within the battery management and infotainment systems, which are often resolvable through software updates. The 12V auxiliary battery has shown some instances of premature failure, a known potential issue in modern vehicles with high electrical demands. Beyond these specific electrical points, the core mechanical and structural elements of the i4 appear robust. Owners should be aware of the potential for software-related issues and ensure their vehicle receives timely updates. Overall, the i4 is positioned as a reliable electric luxury sedan, with most reported issues being manageable through standard dealership service.
