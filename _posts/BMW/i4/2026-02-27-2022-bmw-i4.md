# 2022 BMW i4 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2022 BMW i4 marks the debut of BMW's all-electric luxury gran coupé, bringing traditional athletic driving dynamics and high-tech interior amenities to the EV market. As a first-model-year vehicle, it introduces advanced electric powertrains and cutting-edge driver assistance technologies that offer impressive performance. While initial owner feedback highlights some early electrical, charging, and software-related concerns typical of a brand-new platform, shopping for a used model remains an exciting prospect for buyers seeking luxury EV performance.

---

## Engine

### Single Motor eDrive40

The eDrive40 features a rear-mounted single electric motor generating approximately 335 horsepower and 317 lb-ft of torque. It provides brisk, smooth acceleration paired with strong driving range and reliable everyday electric performance.

## Transmission

### Single-Speed Direct-Drive Transmission

The i4 utilizes a specialized single-speed automatic transmission designed for electric powertrains across all trims. It provides seamless, gearless power delivery with minimal mechanical complexity and high operational reliability.

## Electrical

### Dual Motor M50

The M50 performance variant utilizes a dual-motor, all-wheel-drive setup pushing out up to 536 horsepower and 586 lb-ft of torque. It delivers exhilarating acceleration and sporty handling, though buyers should verify that all powertrain control software is fully up to date.

**Unexpected Acceleration**
- **Symptoms:** An owner reported an incident where the vehicle unexpectedly surged forward or took off while parked.
- **Solution:** Have the vehicle inspected by an authorized BMW service center immediately to check software calibration and control modules.

## Exterior

### Charging Port Door

The electrical architecture manages everything from high-voltage charging systems to complex cabin displays, though early models occasionally report localized sensor or hardware glitches.

**Charging Port Access Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The motorized door covering the charging port can fail to open, potentially leaving drivers unable to charge the vehicle.
- **Solution:** Inspect the charging door mechanism and have the actuator motor or assembly replaced by a dealership if it becomes unresponsive.

## Safety

### Driver Assistance Systems

Advanced safety suites incorporate numerous cameras and radar sensors designed to enhance collision avoidance and driver awareness.

**Driver Assistance Permanent Restriction**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may experience sudden warning notifications indicating that safety features like adaptive cruise control and lane departure warnings have become permanently restricted.
- **Solution:** Have the software modules reset and scanned for fault codes by a qualified BMW technician to restore camera and radar functionality.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V483000: Electrical System: Instrument Cluster/Panel](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=i4&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** While in Valet Parking Mode, the Central Information Display (CID) may not display critical safety information, such as warning messages and/or warning lights. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 101, "Control and Displays." A display that does not show critical information, such as.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the display's head unit software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V541000: Electrical System:Propulsion System:Traction Battery](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=i4&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** A short-circuit in a high voltage battery increases the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the high voltage battery, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V944000: Electrical System:Propulsion System:Traction Battery Thermal:Management:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=i4&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** The high voltage battery electronic control unit (ECU) software may cause an interruption of electrical power. An interruption of electrical power may result in a sudden loss of drive power, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the high voltage battery ECU software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V026000: Back Over Prevention:Warnings:External/Pedestrian Alert](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=i4&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 141, "Minimum Sound Requirements for Hybrid and Electric Vehicles." Without external warning sounds, pedestrians may be unaware of an approaching vehicle, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the external artificial sound generator software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V118000: Electrical System:Body Control Module:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=i4&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** Doors that do not lock as intended while the vehicle is in motion may open during a crash, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the ECU software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V449000: Electrical System:Propulsion System:Hybrid Management](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=i4&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** The high voltage battery combined charging unit (CCU) may not have been manufactured correctly, resulting in improperly connected and/or assembled components inside the CCU. An improperly connected or assembled CCU can interrupt electrical power while driving and result in an engine stall, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the high voltage battery combined charging unit (CCU), free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V395000: Electrical System:Propulsion System:Traction Motor:Controller:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=i4&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** A loss of drive power increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 25V470000: Electrical System:Propulsion System:Traction Battery](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=i4&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** The high-voltage battery cell modules may not have been assembled properly, resulting in stress on the module frame and possible module failure. Battery module failure may lead to a shutdown of the high-voltage system which can result in a loss of drive power, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace specific high-voltage battery cell modules, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2022 BMW i4 is an engaging and sophisticated electric luxury vehicle that delivers strong performance and modern tech. Because it is a first-year production model, prospective buyers should carefully review service history, confirm that all software updates and open recalls have been completed, and thoroughly test electronic features and charging functions during a pre-purchase inspection.
