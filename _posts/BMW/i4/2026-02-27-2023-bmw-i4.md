# 2023 BMW i4 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The BMW i4 brings dynamic luxury performance to the electric vehicle market, but prospective buyers should review certain recurring issues reported by owners. Driver feedback highlights potential concerns regarding electric drive propulsion, high-voltage electrical management, and exterior glass durability. While many drivers enjoy its refined interior and handling, paying close attention to drivetrain warnings and charging system performance is recommended during a pre-purchase inspection.

---

## Engine

### Electric Drive Motor

The electric drive motor system delivers instant torque and robust acceleration. However, early models show sensitivity to software glitches and high-voltage faults that can result in unexpected propulsion interruptions.

**Loss of Motive Power**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers experience unexpected drivetrain and chassis malfunction warnings followed by a sudden loss of propulsion or power while driving.
- **Solution:** Diagnostic scanning by a certified technician is required to identify software faults or high-voltage component failures.

## Transmission

### Single-Speed Direct Drive

The single-speed direct drive transmission offers seamless power delivery without traditional gear shifts. Reliability is generally strong, though occasional electronic control module faults have been noted.

**Shifter and Parking Mechanism Faults**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicles display drivetrain malfunction warnings, shut off unexpectedly, or fail to shift into park or drive properly.
- **Solution:** Software updates or replacement of electronic shifter assemblies may be necessary to restore proper gear selection.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

The regenerative braking system integrates friction brakes with electric motor resistance for efficient energy capture. Proper software calibration is essential for smooth pedal feel and predictable stopping power.

**Regenerative Braking Inconsistency**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicles may lurch forward unexpectedly when regenerative braking or B-mode is engaged, or experience delayed stopping response.
- **Solution:** Have the braking system inspected by a qualified technician to verify software calibration and brake actuator performance.

## Electrical

### High-Voltage Battery and Charging System

The electrical architecture manages battery cooling, charging, and onboard power distribution. Owners have occasionally reported charging compatibility hurdles and high-voltage warning alerts.

**Drivetrain Fault and Charging Restrictions**
- **Symptoms:** Owners receive warnings such as 'Drive train fault, restart not possible' or experience difficulties connecting to high-speed DC fast chargers.
- **Solution:** Dealer inspection and diagnostic software updates are typically required to resolve charging communication or thermal management faults.

## Exterior

### Sunroof Glass

Exterior components generally hold up well, though isolated incidents involving spontaneous sunroof glass failure have caused concern among owners.

**Spontaneous Glass Shattering**
- **Symptoms:** The panoramic sunroof glass explodes or shatters unexpectedly while driving under normal road conditions.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the sunroof glass assembly and clearing of any residual debris.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V944000: Electrical System:Propulsion System:Traction Battery Thermal:Management:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=i4&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** The high voltage battery electronic control unit (ECU) software may cause an interruption of electrical power. An interruption of electrical power may result in a sudden loss of drive power, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the high voltage battery ECU software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V026000: Back Over Prevention:Warnings:External/Pedestrian Alert](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=i4&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 141, "Minimum Sound Requirements for Hybrid and Electric Vehicles." Without external warning sounds, pedestrians may be unaware of an approaching vehicle, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the external artificial sound generator software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V118000: Electrical System:Body Control Module:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=i4&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** Doors that do not lock as intended while the vehicle is in motion may open during a crash, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the ECU software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V295000: Electrical System:Propulsion System:Traction Battery](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=i4&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** An engine stall increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the high voltage battery cell monitoring circuit, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V449000: Electrical System:Propulsion System:Hybrid Management](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=i4&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** The high voltage battery combined charging unit (CCU) may not have been manufactured correctly, resulting in improperly connected and/or assembled components inside the CCU. An improperly connected or assembled CCU can interrupt electrical power while driving and result in an engine stall, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the high voltage battery combined charging unit (CCU), free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V395000: Electrical System:Propulsion System:Traction Motor:Controller:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=i4&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** A loss of drive power increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 25V470000: Electrical System:Propulsion System:Traction Battery](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=i4&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** The high-voltage battery cell modules may not have been assembled properly, resulting in stress on the module frame and possible module failure. Battery module failure may lead to a shutdown of the high-voltage system which can result in a loss of drive power, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace specific high-voltage battery cell modules, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The vehicle offers compelling luxury and performance, but prospective buyers should thoroughly inspect the high-voltage electrical system, test drive the regenerative braking, and verify that all software updates have been applied. Checking for past drivetrain error codes can help ensure a worry-free ownership experience.
