# 2018 BMW 640i xDrive Gran Coupe Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2018 BMW 640i xDrive Gran Coupe, buyers will find a stylish and capable luxury cruiser backed by a robust turbocharged inline-six engine. While owner feedback highlights a generally smooth powertrain experience, prospective owners should review known areas of concern involving interior accessories, safety sensors, and drivetrain components. Conducting a thorough pre-purchase inspection can help uncover minor mechanical or electronic quirks before finalizing a deal.

---

## Engine

### 3.0L Turbocharged Inline-6 Engine (B58)

The 3.0-liter turbocharged inline-six engine (B58) produces approximately 320 horsepower and 330 lb-ft of torque, offering strong, smooth acceleration and solid long-term mechanical reliability compared to older BMW powerplants.

**Unexpected Engine Revving**
- **Symptoms:** The engine may occasionally rev unexpectedly or cause unintended forward movement while coming to a stop with the brakes applied.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified technician inspect the electronic throttle body, accelerator pedal sensor, and vehicle speed control system for faults.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission with xDrive

The 8-speed automatic transmission paired with xDrive all-wheel drive delivers lightning-fast and seamless gear changes with minimal reported mechanical failures.

## Interior

### Panoramic Sunroof Shade

Cabin electronics and trims are generally premium, though mechanical sunroof shade failures have been reported by owners.

**Sagging or Detached Sunroof Curtain**
- **Symptoms:** The panoramic sunroof sunshade or curtain may fail to retract properly, sag significantly, or droop down into the cabin, blocking rear visibility.
- **Solution:** Inspect the sunroof shade assembly tracks and clips; replacement or repair of the sunshade mechanism may be necessary.

## Safety

### Air Bag Occupant Sensor

Advanced safety systems provide solid protection, but sensitive passenger seat occupancy mats can trigger persistent warning lights.

**Passenger Airbag Warning Light**
- **Symptoms:** The passenger airbag malfunction warning light illuminates randomly on the dashboard and stays lit even when the seat is unoccupied.
- **Solution:** Have the dealer or specialist diagnose the seat occupancy sensor mat; replacement of the mat is often required to clear the warning.
- **Cost:** $2,600 to $3,000

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V465000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Engine](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=640i&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** A vehicle stall can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** BMW will notify owners, and dealers will replace the crankshaft sensor, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V684000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=640i&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 111, "Rearview Mirrors." The lack of an image in the back-up camera display increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** BMW will notify owners, and dealers will update the back-up camera software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2018 BMW 640i xDrive Gran Coupe delivers impressive performance and luxury, but buyers should remain mindful of potential out-of-pocket costs associated with electronic sensor failures and trim wear. Prioritize models with documented service histories and verify all cabin electronics and safety lights operate properly during a pre-purchase test drive.
