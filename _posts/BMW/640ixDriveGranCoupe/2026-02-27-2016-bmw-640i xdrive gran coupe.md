# 2016 BMW 640i xDrive Gran Coupe

### Overview
The 2016 BMW 640i xDrive Gran Coupe, a luxurious four-door coupe, generally offers a strong reliability record, reflecting BMW's engineering prowess. It combines performance with comfort, making it a desirable option in its segment. While most owners report a positive ownership experience, like any complex vehicle, there are specific areas that have seen occasional attention. This analysis focuses on documented technical issues beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential areas of concern for prospective buyers and current owners. The xDrive all-wheel-drive system and the potent turbocharged inline-six engine are key features that contribute to its appeal, but understanding their reliability is crucial.

---


## Engine

### 3.0L Turbocharged Inline-6 (N55)
**Turbocharger Wastegate Actuator**
- **Symptoms:** Check engine light, reduced engine power, rattling noise from turbo area.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the wastegate actuator or the entire turbocharger assembly.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1500

**PCV Valve Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Rough idle, oil leaks, blue smoke from exhaust, increased oil consumption.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the Positive Crankcase Ventilation (PCV) valve assembly.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600

## Suspension

### Adaptive Drive System
**Active Roll Stabilization Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Warning lights on the dashboard, noticeable body roll during cornering, clunking noises.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of the active anti-roll bars or related hydraulic components.
- **Cost:** $1500 - $3000+


## Electrical

### Infotainment System (iDrive)
**Occasional Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Screen freezing, unresponsiveness, Bluetooth connectivity issues, navigation errors.
- **Solution:** Software updates, hard resets, or potential replacement of the head unit.
- **Cost:** $200 - $1000


### Battery Drain
**Parasitic Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Dead battery after short periods of inactivity, electrical components malfunctioning.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis to identify the source of the drain (e.g., faulty module, aftermarket accessory) and repair.
- **Cost:** $300 - $800

### Summary Verdict
The 2016 BMW 640i xDrive Gran Coupe is a generally reliable luxury vehicle, with its powertrain and transmission exhibiting good durability. The most commonly reported issues tend to be related to specific electronic components, such as the iDrive system experiencing occasional glitches, and potential parasitic battery drain requiring diagnosis. The engine, while robust, can sometimes present issues with the turbocharger wastegate actuator or PCV valve. The adaptive suspension system, while advanced, can be a source of more significant repair costs if malfunctions occur. Overall, proactive maintenance and addressing early warning signs are key to maintaining the reliability of this sophisticated German coupe.
