# 2014 BMW 640i xDrive Gran Coupe

### Overview
The 2014 BMW 640i xDrive Gran Coupe, a luxurious four-door coupe, generally offers a strong reliability record, particularly for its powertrain. It combines performance with comfort, making it a desirable vehicle. While most owners report a positive ownership experience, like any complex luxury vehicle, there are specific areas that have seen occasional reports of issues. This analysis focuses on documented technical reliability concerns beyond routine maintenance, aiming to provide a clear picture for potential owners or current enthusiasts.

---


## Engine

### 3.0L Twin-Turbocharged Inline-6 (N55)
**Turbocharger Wastegate Rattle**
- **Symptoms:** Audible rattling noise from the turbocharger area, especially on cold starts or at low RPMs. Can sometimes lead to reduced performance.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the wastegate actuator or the entire turbocharger assembly.
- **Cost:** $1,200 - $2,500

**PCV Valve Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Rough idle, increased oil consumption, blue smoke from exhaust, potential for oil leaks.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the Positive Crankcase Ventilation (PCV) valve assembly.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600

**Active Roll Stabilization Malfunction**

## Electrical

### iDrive Infotainment System
**Software Glitches/Freezing**
- **Symptoms:** Infotainment screen freezing, unresponsiveness, navigation errors, Bluetooth connectivity issues.
- **Solution:** Software updates, system resets, or potential replacement of the head unit.
- **Cost:** $200 - $1,500


### Door Handle Sensors
**Keyless Entry Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Difficulty or inability to unlock/lock doors using the keyless entry system.
- **Solution:** Replacement of faulty door handle sensors or related wiring.
- **Cost:** $400 - $800

### Summary Verdict
The 2014 BMW 640i xDrive Gran Coupe is a generally reliable luxury vehicle, with its robust 3.0L inline-6 engine and 8-speed automatic transmission being standout performers. The most commonly cited issues tend to be related to the optional Adaptive Drive suspension components and occasional electrical gremlins within the iDrive system or keyless entry sensors. While the turbocharger wastegate rattle and PCV valve issues are known, they are often addressable with proper maintenance and timely repairs. Overall, the 640i xDrive offers a compelling blend of performance and luxury, with a reliability profile that, while not flawless, is competitive within its segment, provided potential buyers are aware of and prepared for these specific potential concerns.
