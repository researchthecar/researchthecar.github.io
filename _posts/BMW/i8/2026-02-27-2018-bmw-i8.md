# 2018 BMW i8

### Overview
The 2018 BMW i8 is a plug-in hybrid sports car that combines a turbocharged 1.5-liter 3-cylinder gasoline engine with an electric motor. This innovative powertrain offers impressive performance and fuel efficiency. Reliability for the i8 is generally strong, reflecting BMW's engineering prowess. However, as with any complex vehicle, certain areas may experience issues. This report focuses on documented technical reliability concerns, excluding routine maintenance and standard wear and tear, to provide a clear picture of potential owner concerns.

---


## Engine

### 1.5L Turbocharged 3-Cylinder Hybrid System
**Hybrid Battery Degradation**
- **Symptoms:** Reduced electric-only range, decreased overall performance, warning lights on the dashboard.
- **Solution:** Battery pack replacement.
- **Cost:** $5,000 - $15,000+

**Cooling System Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Overheating, coolant loss, visible leaks under the vehicle.
- **Solution:** Repair or replace leaking hoses, seals, or radiator.
- **Cost:** $300 - $800

### Summary Verdict
The 2018 BMW i8 demonstrates good overall reliability, particularly for its advanced hybrid powertrain and unique construction. The most significant potential concern revolves around the hybrid battery, which can degrade over time and is costly to replace. Electrical issues, primarily related to the infotainment system's software, are also noted. However, major mechanical failures in the engine or transmission are uncommon. Owners should be mindful of potential cooling system leaks and the lifespan of the 12V battery. With proper maintenance and attention to these specific areas, the i8 can be a reliable and exhilarating vehicle.
