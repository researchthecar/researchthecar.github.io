# 2019 BMW i8

### Overview
The 2019 BMW i8 is a plug-in hybrid sports car renowned for its striking design and innovative powertrain. As a halo vehicle, it generally exhibits strong build quality and reliability, reflecting BMW's engineering prowess. However, like any complex vehicle, particularly one with advanced hybrid technology, certain areas may require attention over time. This analysis focuses on documented technical issues beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential concerns for owners and prospective buyers. The i8's unique combination of a turbocharged gasoline engine and electric motors presents a different reliability profile compared to conventional vehicles, with a focus on the integration and longevity of these advanced systems.

### Summary Verdict
The 2019 BMW i8 demonstrates a commendable level of reliability, particularly given its advanced hybrid powertrain and unique design. The core engine and transmission components are generally robust. The most significant potential reliability concern lies with the hybrid battery system, where degradation over time is a possibility, leading to reduced electric range and performance, with replacement costs being substantial. Electrical gremlins within the infotainment system can occur, usually resolvable with software updates. The distinctive butterfly doors, while iconic, may occasionally develop hinge issues. Overall, for a vehicle of its complexity and performance focus, the i8 offers a solid reliability record, with most issues being manageable or covered under warranty for a significant period.
