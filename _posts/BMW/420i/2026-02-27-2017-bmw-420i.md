# 2017 BMW 420i Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used luxury sport coupe or convertible, this model offers engaging driving dynamics and refined styling. However, prospective buyers should carefully review maintenance records and be aware of common mechanical and electrical owner grievances reported to safety authorities.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4 (B48)

The 2.0-liter turbocharged inline-4 engine produces approximately 248 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque, providing brisk acceleration. While generally robust, buyers should monitor the cooling system closely for plastic housing degradation.

**Coolant Leaks and Housing Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers report sudden low coolant warnings, visible fluid leaks, and potential engine overheating due to cracked plastic components in the cooling system.
- **Solution:** Inspect the thermostat, oil filter housing, and radiator hoses; replace degraded plastic components and gaskets promptly.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

The ZF 8-speed automatic transmission delivers smooth, rapid shifts and pairs well with rear-wheel or all-wheel drive configurations. It is widely regarded as durable with routine fluid maintenance.

## Safety

### Air Bag and Restraint System

Airbag and occupant detection sensors generally perform reliably, though sensor wiring and mat failures can trigger warning lamps.

**Passenger Occupancy Sensor Fault**
- **Symptoms:** Illuminated airbag warning lights and dashboard alerts indicating a passenger restraint system or seat occupancy detection failure.
- **Solution:** Have a certified technician diagnose the passenger seat occupancy mat and associated wiring harness for faults.

### Seat Belt

Seat belts and tensioners keep occupants secure, but mechanical latch points require periodic inspection.

**Seat Belt Attachment and Tensioner Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Owners note seat belts unexpectedly unlatching from fixed mounting points or experiencing tensioner warnings.
- **Solution:** Inspect seat belt anchor points and replace faulty tensioners or latches as required.
