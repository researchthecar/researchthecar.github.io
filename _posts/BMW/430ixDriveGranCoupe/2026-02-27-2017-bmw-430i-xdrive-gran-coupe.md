# 2017 BMW 430i xDrive Gran Coupe Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2017 BMW 430i xDrive Gran Coupe, buyers will find a stylish and engaging luxury vehicle that offers a balance of all-weather capability and sporty handling. While generally well-regarded for its driving dynamics, prospective owners should review owner-reported feedback regarding cooling system vulnerabilities and electronic sensor alerts. Keeping up with routine inspections can help address minor issues before they escalate.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4 (B48)

The 2.0-liter turbocharged inline-4 engine (B48) produces approximately 248 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque, providing brisk acceleration and respectable fuel economy. This modern powerplant is generally robust, though buyers should watch out for coolant leaks originating from aging plastic fittings.

**Coolant Leaks and Housing Failures**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have reported dashboard warnings indicating low coolant levels, often accompanied by visible fluid leaks stemming from plastic components such as the oil filter housing or thermostat.
- **Solution:** Inspect plastic cooling components and replace cracked housings, gaskets, or the thermostat as needed.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 8-speed automatic transmission paired with xDrive all-wheel drive delivers smooth, rapid shifts and well-managed power delivery across various driving conditions. It enjoys a solid reputation for long-term durability when properly maintained.

## Safety

### Air Bag and Seat Occupancy Sensors

Safety electronics generally function reliably, but passenger seat occupancy sensors can trigger intermittent warning lights.

**Passenger Restraint System Warning**
- **Symptoms:** The airbag warning light may illuminate on the dashboard, displaying messages related to a passenger restraint system or seat occupancy detection failure.
- **Solution:** Diagnostic scanning by a qualified technician is required to identify faulty seat sensors, wiring harnesses, or control modules for replacement.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V115000: Air Bags](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=430i&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** The affected vehicles have seat-mounted side air bag inflator initiators that may fail to ignite during a crash. If the air bag inflator initiator fails to ignite, the seat-mounted air bag will not deploy, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** BMW will notify owners, and dealers will replace the air bag modules, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.
