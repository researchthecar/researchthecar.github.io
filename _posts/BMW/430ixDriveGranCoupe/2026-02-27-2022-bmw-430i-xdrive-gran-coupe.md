# 2022 BMW 430i xDrive Gran Coupe Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2022 BMW 430i xDrive Gran Coupe, buyers will find a stylish and capable luxury car that delivers strong performance. However, reviewing reported owner experiences and open safety items is an important part of the evaluation process. While many owners enjoy the driving dynamics, understanding potential powertrain and electrical concerns can help you make a well-informed purchasing decision.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4 (B48)

This 2.0-liter turbocharged inline-4 engine produces around 255 horsepower and 294 lb-ft of torque, providing brisk acceleration for daily driving. It balances responsive power delivery with respectable efficiency, making it a popular choice in the lineup.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Sport Automatic

The 8-speed sport automatic transmission paired with xDrive all-wheel drive delivers smooth and rapid gear changes. It is generally praised for its refined operation, though occasional electronic or mechanical glitches have been reported by some owners.

**Unexpected Shifting to Neutral**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers note that the vehicle may occasionally shift into neutral independently while driving at various speeds without any warning lights illuminating.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified technician or dealership inspect the transmission control module and software for potential faults.

### Rear Differential

The xDrive all-wheel-drive system distributes power efficiently across all four wheels, but prospective buyers should check the underside for fluid leaks during a pre-purchase inspection.

**Rear Differential Fluid Leak and Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have experienced fluid leaks from the rear differential, potentially leading to premature internal wear and the need for a complete unit replacement.
- **Solution:** Inspect the differential housing for active leaks and replace seals or the entire differential assembly as necessary.

## Safety

### Backup Camera and Restraint Systems

Driver assistance features enhance everyday usability, though intermittent electronic glitches can occasionally affect visibility systems.

**Rear Vision Camera Deactivation**
- **Symptoms:** The rearview camera may occasionally deactivate when the park assist system is turned off, leaving the driver without a display image while reversing.
- **Solution:** Consult a dealership for potential software updates or module diagnostics if camera display issues persist.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V267000: Visibility:Sun/Moon Roof Assembly](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=430i&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 118, "Power-Operated Window Systems." A sunroof that closes without the key fob present inside the vehicle can increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will reprogram the roof function control unit, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V118000: Electrical System:Body Control Module:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=430i&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** Doors that do not lock as intended while the vehicle is in motion may open during a crash, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the ECU software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V584000: Seat Belts](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=430i&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 208, "Occupant Crash Protection." An audible chime that does not properly alert the driver to fasten their seat belt increases the risk of injury in a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the receiver audio module software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V636000: Electrical System:Starter Assembly:Relay](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=430i&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** The engine starter relay may corrode, causing the relay to overheat and short circuit. A short circuit in the starter relay may increase the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the engine starter, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V056000: Electrical System:Starter Assembly](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=430i&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** The engine starter may overheat and catch fire due to wear on an internal component. Fire increases the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the engine starter, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.
