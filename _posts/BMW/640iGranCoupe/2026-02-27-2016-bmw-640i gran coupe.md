# 2016 BMW 640i Gran Coupe

### Overview
The 2016 BMW 640i Gran Coupe, a luxurious four-door coupe, generally offers a strong reliability record, reflecting BMW's engineering prowess. It combines performance with practicality, making it a desirable option in the premium segment. While most owners report a positive ownership experience, like any vehicle, certain components can occasionally present issues. This analysis focuses on documented technical reliability concerns beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential areas of attention for prospective and current owners. The vehicle is known for its potent powertrain and sophisticated chassis, which contribute to its driving dynamics but can also be sources of specific technical considerations.

---


## Engine

### 3.0L Twin-Turbocharged Inline-6 (N55)
**Water Pump Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Overheating, coolant leaks, warning lights on the dashboard.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the electric water pump and thermostat assembly.
- **Cost:** $700 - $1200

**Turbocharger Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of power, whistling noises, increased oil consumption.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of the turbocharger unit.
- **Cost:** $1500 - $3000

**PCV Valve Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Rough idle, oil leaks, potential engine stalling.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the Positive Crankcase Ventilation (PCV) valve system.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600

### Summary Verdict
The 2016 BMW 640i Gran Coupe demonstrates good overall reliability, with its robust 3.0L twin-turbo engine and 8-speed automatic transmission being generally dependable. The most commonly reported technical concerns revolve around the electric water pump, turbocharger, and PCV valve for the engine, and occasional software glitches or battery drain issues within the electrical system. Optional adaptive suspension components can also be a source of repair costs if they fail. However, these issues are not universally experienced, and many owners enjoy trouble-free operation. Regular maintenance and prompt attention to any warning signs are crucial for maintaining the vehicle's long-term reliability and performance.
