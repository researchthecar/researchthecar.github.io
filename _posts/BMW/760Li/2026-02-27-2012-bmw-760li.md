# 2012 BMW 760Li

### Overview
The 2012 BMW 760Li, a flagship luxury sedan, is renowned for its potent V12 engine and opulent interior. As a high-performance vehicle, it generally offers a refined driving experience. However, like any complex machine, especially one with a sophisticated powertrain and advanced technology, certain areas can be prone to issues as the vehicle ages. This analysis focuses on documented technical reliability concerns, excluding routine maintenance and standard wear and tear, to provide a clear picture of potential ownership challenges for this model year.

---


## Engine

### 6.0L Twin-Turbocharged V12 (N74B60)
**Turbocharger Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of power, increased exhaust smoke, whistling noises from the engine bay.
- **Solution:** Replacement of affected turbocharger(s).
- **Cost:** $2,500 - $5,000

**Ignition Coil Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Misfires, rough idling, check engine light illumination.
- **Solution:** Replacement of faulty ignition coils.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600

**Cooling System Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Coolant loss, overheating, visible leaks from hoses or radiator.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of leaking hoses, radiator, or water pump.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1,200


## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic (ZF 8HP90)
**Transmission Control Module (TCM) Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Erratic shifting, transmission warning lights, inability to select gears.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the Transmission Control Module.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1,500

**Mechatronics Unit Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Harsh shifting, delayed engagement, slipping gears.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of the mechatronics unit.
- **Cost:** $1,500 - $3,000


## Suspension

### Adaptive Drive System
**Active Roll Stabilization Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** Increased body roll in corners, warning lights related to suspension.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of active anti-roll bars or related sensors.
- **Cost:** $1,000 - $3,000

**Air Suspension Compressor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle sagging on one or more corners, loss of ride height adjustment.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the air suspension compressor.
- **Cost:** $700 - $1,300


## Electrical

### Infotainment System
**iDrive System Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Screen freezing, unresponsiveness, audio issues, navigation problems.
- **Solution:** Software updates, or replacement of the head unit or control module.
- **Cost:** $500 - $2,000


### Body Control Module (BCM)
**BCM Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** Intermittent issues with lights, windows, locks, or other electronic accessories.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and reprogramming or replacement of the Body Control Module.
- **Cost:** $600 - $1,200

### Summary Verdict
The 2012 BMW 760Li, while offering a luxurious and powerful experience, presents potential reliability concerns primarily centered around its complex V12 engine, advanced transmission, and sophisticated electronic systems. Owners may encounter issues with turbochargers, ignition coils, and cooling systems, as well as transmission control modules and mechatronics. The adaptive suspension and infotainment system are also areas where malfunctions can occur. Electrical components, particularly the iDrive system and Body Control Module, can be sources of intermittent problems. Exterior and interior components generally show good reliability. Prospective buyers should be prepared for potentially higher maintenance and repair costs associated with a high-performance luxury vehicle of this caliber.
