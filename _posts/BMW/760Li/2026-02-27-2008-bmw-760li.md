# 2008 BMW 760Li

### Overview
The 2008 BMW 760Li, a flagship luxury sedan, is known for its potent V12 engine and opulent interior. While generally offering a refined driving experience, like any high-performance vehicle of its era, it can present specific reliability concerns. This analysis focuses on documented issues beyond routine maintenance, highlighting areas that may require attention for owners. The V12 engine, while a marvel of engineering, can be complex and costly to maintain. Similarly, the advanced electronic systems and sophisticated suspension components, while contributing to its luxurious feel, are potential sources of repair. Owners should be aware of these potential pitfalls to ensure the longevity and optimal performance of their 760Li.

---


## Engine

### 6.0L V12 (N73B60)
**Ignition Coil Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Misfires, rough idling, check engine light, reduced engine power.
- **Solution:** Replacement of faulty ignition coils. It's often recommended to replace them in pairs or sets.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600 per coil, plus labor.

**Valve Stem Seal Leakage**
- **Symptoms:** Blue smoke from exhaust, especially on startup or deceleration, oil consumption.
- **Solution:** Replacement of valve stem seals. This is a labor-intensive job requiring cylinder head removal.
- **Cost:** $3,000 - $6,000+

**Oil Pan Gasket Leak**
- **Symptoms:** Visible oil leaks from the bottom of the engine, oil smell.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the oil pan gasket. May require dropping the oil pan.
- **Cost:** $400 - $800


## Transmission

### 6-speed Automatic (GM 6L80/6L90)
**Transmission Fluid Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Leaking fluid, slipping gears, harsh shifting, transmission warning light.
- **Solution:** Inspection and repair of leaks, often from seals or the transmission cooler lines. Fluid and filter change may be necessary.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1,500

**Torque Converter Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Shuddering at low speeds, slipping, delayed engagement, unusual noises.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the torque converter. This is a significant repair.
- **Cost:** $1,500 - $3,000+


## Suspension

### Adaptive Drive System
**Active Roll Stabilization (ARS) Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** Increased body roll in corners, warning lights on the dashboard, stiff or uneven ride.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and repair or replacement of faulty ARS components (e.g., hydraulic pump, actuators, sensors).
- **Cost:** $1,000 - $3,000+

**Air Suspension Leaks (if equipped)**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle sagging on one or more corners, uneven ride height, compressor running excessively.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of leaking air springs, lines, or compressor.
- **Cost:** $500 - $2,000+


## Electrical

### Infotainment System (iDrive)
**iDrive Controller Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** Unresponsive controller, freezing screen, erratic menu navigation.
- **Solution:** Software updates or replacement of the iDrive controller unit.
- **Cost:** $300 - $800

**Battery Drain Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Dead battery after short periods of inactivity, electrical components malfunctioning intermittently.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis to identify parasitic drain sources (e.g., faulty modules, door locks, comfort access system) and repair.
- **Cost:** $200 - $1,000+

### Summary Verdict
The 2008 BMW 760Li is a complex luxury vehicle where reliability is heavily influenced by its advanced V12 engine and sophisticated electronic and suspension systems. While the core structure and interior materials are generally robust, potential owners should budget for maintenance and repairs related to ignition systems, valve seals, transmission components, and the advanced adaptive suspension. Electrical gremlins, particularly with the iDrive system and battery drain, are also noted. Proactive maintenance and addressing issues promptly are crucial for maintaining this high-performance sedan.
