# 2017 BMW 320i

### Overview
The 2017 BMW 320i, part of the F30 generation, is generally considered a reliable vehicle, especially given its sporty nature and luxury appointments. It offers a balanced driving experience with a focus on performance and comfort. While most owners report a positive ownership experience, like any vehicle, there are specific areas that have seen more attention from a reliability standpoint. This analysis focuses on documented technical issues beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential concerns for prospective buyers or current owners. The 320i typically comes equipped with a turbocharged four-cylinder engine and an automatic transmission, both of which have their own reliability profiles.

---


## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4 (B48)
**Coolant Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Visible coolant puddles under the vehicle, low coolant warning light, engine overheating.
- **Solution:** Replacement of leaking gaskets, hoses, or the water pump.
- **Cost:** $300 - $700

**Turbocharger Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of power, whistling noises from the engine bay, increased oil consumption.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of the turbocharger assembly.
- **Cost:** $1,200 - $2,500

**Infotainment System Glitches**

### Summary Verdict
The 2017 BMW 320i presents a generally strong reliability record, with its powertrain being a particular highlight. The 2.0L turbocharged engine and the 8-speed automatic transmission are robust, though occasional coolant leaks and turbocharger concerns have been noted. Electrical gremlins, such as infotainment glitches and battery drain, are the most frequently reported issues beyond standard wear. The suspension, exterior, and interior components have proven to be quite durable. Overall, the 320i offers a reliable luxury sedan experience, provided potential buyers are aware of and prepared for the possibility of addressing minor electrical or engine-related repairs.
