# 2017 BMW 320i Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used luxury sport sedan, this model often catches the eye with its sharp handling and premium badge. However, prospective buyers should review owner-reported reliability data carefully. While many enjoy the responsive driving dynamics, recurring mechanical and electrical concerns—particularly involving the cooling system, fuel delivery, and parking brake hardware—require close attention during a pre-purchase inspection.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4 (B48)

A 2.0-liter turbocharged inline-4 engine producing around 180 horsepower, offering a balance of efficiency and punchy mid-range acceleration. Routine maintenance and diligent cooling system upkeep are vital for long-term reliability.

**Coolant Leak**
- **Symptoms:** Low coolant warnings, sweet-smelling vapor, or visible puddles under the engine bay, potentially leading to overheating.
- **Solution:** Inspect hoses, radiator, and fittings for cracks or degradation; replace failed cooling system components.

**Engine Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** Unexpected engine hesitation, sputtering, or sudden stalling while driving or coming to a stop.
- **Solution:** Diagnose fuel delivery and ignition systems, and replace faulty fuel pumps or sensors as needed.

### Fuel

Fuel delivery components manage pressurized fuel supply to the direct injection system, though pump wear can occasionally cause driveability concerns.

**Fuel Pump Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Difficulty starting the vehicle, extended crank times, or sudden power loss due to fluctuating fuel pressure.
- **Solution:** Test fuel rail pressure and replace failing low-pressure or high-pressure fuel pumps.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

An 8-speed ZF automatic transmission paired with rear-wheel drive or xDrive all-wheel drive, widely praised for quick, smooth shifts and strong durability.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

Standard disc braking hardware provides confident stopping power, though physical bracket fasteners underneath require periodic inspection.

**Brake Cable and Clip Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Grinding or scraping noises from the center-underside of the vehicle as the parking brake cable drops onto the driveshaft.
- **Solution:** Inspect parking brake cable retention clips and replace broken plastic fasteners to secure the cable away from moving parts.

## Safety

### Seat Belt

Occupant safety restraint systems include airbags and adjustable seat belts designed to protect passengers in frontal and side impacts.

**Seat Belt Latch Issue**
- **Symptoms:** Seat belt warning lights, failure of the buckle to securely latch, or intermittent operation of the restraint sensor.
- **Solution:** Inspect seat belt receivers and wiring harnesses; replace faulty latch assemblies or sensors.

Thoroughly inspecting the cooling system, fuel delivery components, and underbody parking brake lines can save used car buyers from unexpected repair bills.
