# 2016 BMW 528i Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

Shopping for a used 2016 BMW 528i means looking at a luxury midsize sedan equipped with turbocharged performance and refined road manners. While the vehicle offers strong driver appeal and a comfortable cabin, used-car buyers should thoroughly review mechanical records and test drive prospective models to ensure previous owners kept up with maintenance.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4 (N20)

The 2.0-liter turbocharged inline-4 engine produces approximately 240 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque. It provides brisk acceleration and decent efficiency, though prospective buyers should inspect the timing chain and oil system history.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 8-speed automatic transmission delivers smooth and rapid shifts paired with rear-wheel drive or xDrive all-wheel drive. It is generally robust when fluid changes are performed at recommended intervals.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

- **Issue:** The vehicle-sensitive locking mechanism of the seat belt Emergency Locking Retractor (ELR) for the driver's seat may not lock as designed. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of the Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 209, "Seat Belt Assemblies." - **What to do:** BMW will notify owners, and dealers will replace the driver's seat belt assembly, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V914000: Air Bags](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=528i&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** The affected vehicles have seat- mounted side air bag inflator initiators that may fail to ignite during a crash. If the air bag inflator initiator fails to ignite, the seat-mounted air bag will not deploy, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** BMW will notify owners, and dealers will replace the air bag modules, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 15V718000: Child Seat](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=528i&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles may fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 225, "Child Restraint Anchorage Systems." If the child seat is not properly attached to the lower anchors, it can increase the child's risk of injury in the event of a crash.
- **What to do:** BMW will notify owners, and dealers will inspect and, if necessary, repair the inboard lower anchor on the left rear seat, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V608000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Cooling System:Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=528i&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** An improperly sealed electrical connector on the water pump may be exposed to water and short circuit. An electrical short increases the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the water pump and plug connector as necessary, and install a protective shield, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2016 BMW 528i blends luxury and engaging performance, making it an attractive pre-owned option. Buyers should prioritize examples with detailed service histories, perform a comprehensive pre-purchase inspection, and budget for potential upkeep associated with luxury German engineering.
