# 2013 BMW 550i GT xDrive

### Overview
The 2013 BMW 550i GT xDrive, a luxury Gran Turismo model, offers a potent blend of performance and practicality. Powered by a robust twin-turbocharged V8 engine and equipped with BMW's xDrive all-wheel-drive system, it provides a dynamic driving experience. While generally well-regarded for its upscale interior and advanced features, like any complex luxury vehicle, it has specific areas that have seen more attention from owners regarding reliability. This analysis focuses on documented technical issues beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential concerns for prospective buyers or current owners.

---


## Engine

### 4.4L Twin-Turbocharged V8 (N63B44O0)
**Turbocharger Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Reduced engine power, increased oil consumption, turbocharger warning lights.
- **Solution:** Replacement of turbochargers and associated lines. Often addressed under extended warranty or recall campaigns.
- **Cost:** $3,000 - $6,000

**Fuel Injector Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Rough idling, misfires, check engine light, poor fuel economy.
- **Solution:** Replacement of faulty fuel injectors. It's often recommended to replace all eight.
- **Cost:** $1,200 - $2,500

**Ignition Coil Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Engine misfires, rough running, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of faulty ignition coils. Typically replaced as needed.
- **Cost:** $400 - $800

**Oil Consumption**
- **Symptoms:** Frequent need to top up engine oil, blue smoke from exhaust under acceleration.
- **Solution:** Can be related to valve stem seals, piston rings, or PCV system. Repairs can be extensive.
- **Cost:** $2,000 - $5,000+

### Summary Verdict
The 2013 BMW 550i GT xDrive is a capable luxury vehicle, but its high-performance twin-turbo V8 engine is the primary source of potential reliability concerns. Issues such as turbocharger problems, fuel injector failures, and significant oil consumption are documented and can lead to substantial repair costs. The transmission and most other systems, including suspension, electrical, exterior, and interior, are generally reliable with no widespread issues reported. Prospective buyers should be aware of the engine's potential for costly repairs and factor in the possibility of maintenance beyond standard wear and tear, especially if purchasing a vehicle outside of an extended warranty.
