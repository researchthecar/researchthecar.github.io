Here's a breakdown of common issues and complaints reported by owners of the 2013 BMW 550i GT xDrive, formatted for your website and including relevant details. The 2013 BMW 550i GT xDrive is a luxury hatchback known for its spacious interior, comfortable ride, and powerful engine. It features a turbocharged 4.4-liter V8 engine producing 445 horsepower and 480 lb-ft of torque, coupled with an eight-speed automatic transmission. Other engine options available for the 2013 5 Series GT include a 3.0L inline-6. Some owners appreciate the car's unique design, luxurious features, and smooth driving experience. Common complaints include higher maintenance costs, complex electronics, and the potential for expensive repairs as the car ages.

**Engine**

**N63B44O0 (4.4L Twin-Turbo V8)**

*   **Oil Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks are common, often originating from valve cover gaskets, the oil filter housing, or turbocharger oil lines. Symptoms include visible oil leaks, burning oil smell, and low oil levels.
    *   Solution: Replacing the leaking gaskets or lines. Thorough inspection of the entire system to identify all leaks.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2000+, depending on the location and complexity of the leak.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Coolant Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaks can occur from various points, including the water pump, radiator hoses, and expansion tank. Symptoms include a sweet smell, low coolant levels, and overheating.
    *   Solution: Replacing the faulty component(s). Pressure testing the system to pinpoint leaks.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1500+, depending on the component.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **VANOS Solenoid Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The Variable Valve Timing (VANOS) system can experience solenoid failures. Symptoms include rough idling, reduced power, and check engine light.
    *   Solution: Replacing the faulty VANOS solenoid(s).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800, depending on the number of solenoids replaced.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Fuel Injector Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Direct fuel injectors on the N63 engine are known to fail, causing rough idling, misfires, and fuel delivery problems.
    *   Solution: Replacing the faulty fuel injector(s).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+ per injector, plus labor.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**N55 (3.0L Inline-6 Turbocharged)**

*   No Issues Found

**Transmission**

**ZF 8HP Automatic Transmission (8HP45)**

*   **Valve Body Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Slipping between gears, harsh shifting, delayed engagement, and transmission malfunction warnings.
    *   Solution: Replacing or rebuilding the valve body.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1500 - $3000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Mechatronic Unit Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Similar to valve body issues, including slipping, erratic shifting, and error messages.
    *   Solution: Replacing the Mechatronic unit.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $2000 - $4000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Air Suspension Failure (Rear)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The rear air suspension system can experience failures, causing the rear of the car to sag, ride harshly, or display warning lights.
    *   Solution: Replacing the air springs (bags) or air compressor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $2000+ per air spring or compressor replacement.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Adaptive Drive/Dynamic Damper Control Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Malfunctions in the adaptive suspension system can lead to a rough ride, warning lights, and reduced handling performance.
    *   Solution: Diagnosing the specific fault (sensor, damper, control module) and replacing the faulty component.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2500+, depending on the component and labor.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Battery Drain**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The car's complex electronics can contribute to excessive battery drain, especially when the car is not driven frequently. Symptoms include difficulty starting, a dead battery, and electrical malfunctions.
    *   Solution: Diagnosing the parasitic drain (faulty module, short circuit) and repairing the issue.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1000+, depending on the complexity of the issue.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **iDrive System Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The iDrive system can experience glitches, freezing, and display errors.
    *   Solution: Software updates, replacement of the iDrive control unit.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Panoramic Sunroof Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The panoramic sunroof can experience issues with its opening and closing mechanism, leading to jamming, noise, or leaks.
    *   Solution: Repairing or replacing the sunroof mechanism.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Power Seat Malfunctions**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Power seats can develop malfunctions, causing them to stop moving, get stuck in one position, or make unusual noises.
    *   Solution: Replacing the faulty seat motor or switch.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1000+ per seat
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2013 BMW 550i GT xDrive, while offering a luxurious and powerful driving experience, is prone to certain issues that potential buyers should be aware of. Engine oil and coolant leaks, along with VANOS and fuel injector problems in the N63 engine, can lead to significant repair costs. Transmission issues, especially with the ZF 8HP valve body and mechatronic unit, are also potential concerns. Suspension problems, primarily with the air suspension system, can further add to the ownership costs. Electrical gremlins and interior component failures contribute to the overall maintenance burden. The severity of these issues ranges from minor inconveniences to major repairs, with costs varying accordingly. The 550i GT xDrive can be an enjoyable vehicle, but only with diligent maintenance and budgeting for potential repairs.
