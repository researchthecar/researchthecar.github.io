# 2012 BMW 740Li

### Overview
The 2012 BMW 740Li, a flagship luxury sedan, generally offers a high level of comfort and performance. As with many complex luxury vehicles, its reliability can be a mixed bag, with some areas demonstrating robust engineering while others may present specific concerns. This analysis focuses on documented technical issues beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential areas of attention for owners and prospective buyers. The 740Li is equipped with sophisticated technology and powerful engines, which, while contributing to its driving experience, can also be sources of potential repair needs. Understanding these specific points can help in managing ownership costs and ensuring the longevity of this premium vehicle.

---


## Engine

### 3.0L Twin-Turbocharged Inline-6 (N54/N55)
**Turbocharger Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of power, increased oil consumption, whistling noises.
- **Solution:** Turbocharger replacement or repair.
- **Cost:** $1,500 - $3,000

**Fuel Injector Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Rough idling, misfires, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of faulty fuel injectors.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1,500

**Water Pump Failure (Electric)**
- **Symptoms:** Overheating, coolant leaks, warning lights.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the electric water pump and thermostat.
- **Cost:** $700 - $1,200

## Suspension

### Adaptive Drive System
**Active Roll Stabilization Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** Increased body roll, warning lights on the dashboard, unusual noises during cornering.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of active anti-roll bars and related hydraulic components.
- **Cost:** $2,000 - $5,000+


### Air Suspension (Rear)
**Air Spring Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle sagging on one side, especially after sitting, compressor running excessively.
- **Solution:** Replacement of leaking air springs.
- **Cost:** $600 - $1,000 per side


## Electrical

### iDrive System
**Infotainment System Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Screen freezing, unresponsiveness, audio issues, navigation problems.
- **Solution:** Software updates, control unit reset, or replacement of the iDrive controller or head unit.
- **Cost:** $500 - $2,000


### Battery Drain
**Parasitic Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Dead battery after short periods of inactivity, electrical components malfunctioning.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and repair of faulty modules or circuits causing the drain.
- **Cost:** $300 - $1,000+

### Summary Verdict
The 2012 BMW 740Li, while offering a luxurious and powerful driving experience, exhibits some common reliability concerns typical of high-end German sedans. The 3.0L twin-turbo engine can be prone to issues with turbochargers, fuel injectors, and the electric water pump, requiring significant repair costs. The sophisticated suspension systems, particularly the adaptive and air suspension components, can also be a source of expensive repairs if they fail. Electrical gremlins, especially within the iDrive infotainment system and potential battery drain issues, are also noted. The transmission, however, is generally considered robust. Owners should budget for potential maintenance and repairs, particularly for the engine and suspension, to maintain the vehicle's optimal performance and luxury.
