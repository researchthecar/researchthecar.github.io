# 2016 BMW 640i

### Overview
The 2016 BMW 640i, part of the F13 generation, is generally considered a reliable luxury grand tourer, especially given its performance-oriented nature. It typically features a potent turbocharged inline-six engine paired with a smooth automatic transmission. While owners generally report a positive ownership experience, like any complex vehicle, certain areas can experience issues over time. This analysis focuses on documented, non-wear-and-tear related problems reported by owners and technicians, excluding routine maintenance. The focus is on providing a clear picture of potential technical vulnerabilities beyond standard upkeep.

---


## Engine

### 3.0L Twin-Turbocharged Inline-6 (N55)
**Water Pump Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Overheating, coolant leaks, warning lights on the dashboard.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the electric water pump and thermostat.
- **Cost:** $700 - $1200

**Turbocharger Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of power, whistling noises, increased oil consumption.
- **Solution:** Turbocharger inspection and potential rebuild or replacement.
- **Cost:** $1500 - $3000

**PCV Valve Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Rough idle, oil leaks, potential engine misfires.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the Positive Crankcase Ventilation (PCV) valve assembly.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600

## Electrical

### Infotainment System (iDrive)
**Software Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Screen freezing, unresponsive controls, Bluetooth connectivity issues.
- **Solution:** Software updates or system resets. In severe cases, module replacement.
- **Cost:** $200 - $1000


### Battery Drain
**Parasitic Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Dead battery after short periods of inactivity, electrical components malfunctioning.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis to find the source of the drain (e.g., faulty module, wiring issue) and repair.
- **Cost:** $300 - $800

### Summary Verdict
The 2016 BMW 640i is a well-built luxury vehicle with a generally strong reliability record. The primary areas of concern tend to be related to the complex turbocharged engine, particularly the water pump and PCV system, and potential issues with the adaptive suspension components. Electrical gremlins, such as infotainment glitches and parasitic battery drain, can also occur. For convertible models, the roof mechanism is an area to monitor. Despite these potential issues, which are not uncommon for high-performance luxury cars of this era, the 640i offers a rewarding driving experience when properly maintained. Addressing potential problems proactively can ensure continued enjoyment and reliability.
