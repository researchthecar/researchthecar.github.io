# 2017 BMW 640i

### Overview
The 2017 BMW 640i, part of the F13 generation, is generally regarded as a reliable luxury grand tourer. It combines potent performance with a comfortable ride and a well-appointed interior. The primary powertrain is a robust inline-six turbocharged engine paired with an eight-speed automatic transmission, known for its smooth operation and decent fuel economy for its class. While BMW vehicles often carry a premium for maintenance and repairs, the 640i from this model year tends to avoid widespread, recurring mechanical failures. Owners typically report satisfaction with its build quality and long-term dependability, provided regular maintenance is performed. Potential issues, though not endemic, can arise in areas common to many modern luxury vehicles, such as electronics and specific sensor failures.

### Summary Verdict
The 2017 BMW 640i is a well-built luxury coupe/convertible with a strong reliability record for its class. The powertrain, consisting of the 3.0L turbocharged inline-six and the 8-speed automatic transmission, is particularly robust and rarely presents significant issues beyond routine maintenance. Potential concerns are more likely to be found in the sophisticated electronic systems, such as the iDrive infotainment or advanced suspension components, which can be costly to repair if they fail. However, these are not considered widespread problems. Overall, the 640i offers a compelling blend of performance and luxury with good long-term reliability, making it a solid choice for those seeking a premium grand tourer.
