# 2018 BMW 640i Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2018 BMW 640i, prospective buyers will find a luxurious and powerful grand tourer equipped with sophisticated engineering. While overall owner satisfaction tends to focus on luxury and performance, reviewing common owner reports helps highlight potential trouble spots such as electronic glitches, interior accessories, and occasional airbag warnings. As with any European luxury vehicle, thorough pre-purchase inspections and a careful review of maintenance history are essential.

---

## Engine

### 3.0L Turbocharged Inline-6 (B58)

The 3.0-liter turbocharged inline-6 (B58) engine produces approximately 335 horsepower and 332 lb-ft of torque, offering smooth power delivery and respectable efficiency for its class. It is generally regarded as one of BMW's more robust modern powerplants, though proper maintenance remains vital.

**Check Engine Light and Drivetrain Warnings**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have reported the check engine light illuminating unexpectedly while driving at various speeds.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified technician run a diagnostic scan to identify fault codes and replace any failing sensors or components.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

This 8-speed automatic transmission pairs with rear-wheel drive or xDrive all-wheel drive to deliver rapid, smooth gear changes and excellent highway cruising capability. It is widely praised for its durability when serviced at recommended intervals.

## Interior

### Panoramic Sunroof Shade

Cabin appointments are upscale and feature-rich, though motorized interior window shades require attention to ensure proper operation.

**Sagging Sunroof Curtain**
- **Symptoms:** The panoramic sunroof shade or curtain fails to retract properly, sagging downward and blocking rear visibility.
- **Solution:** Inspect the sunroof cassette mechanism and replace the sagging shade assembly or clips.

## Safety

### Passenger Air Bag Sensor

Safety systems generally perform well, though front passenger occupancy sensors can occasionally trigger persistent warning lights.

**Passenger Airbag Warning Light**
- **Symptoms:** The passenger-side airbag malfunction or restraint warning light illuminates randomly and stays on.
- **Solution:** The seat sensor mat typically requires replacement by a dealership or specialist.
- **Cost:** $2,600 - $3,000

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V465000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Engine](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=640i&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** A vehicle stall can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** BMW will notify owners, and dealers will replace the crankshaft sensor, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V684000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=640i&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 111, "Rearview Mirrors." The lack of an image in the back-up camera display increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** BMW will notify owners, and dealers will update the back-up camera software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2018 BMW 640i combines striking design, potent inline-six performance, and high-end cabin luxury. While most mechanical systems prove dependable, buyers should verify the proper operation of interior accessories like the sunroof shade and ensure that safety restraint sensors are free of error codes. Prioritizing a vehicle with a documented service history will help secure a rewarding ownership experience.
