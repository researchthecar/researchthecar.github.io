# 2012 BMW 640i

### Overview
The 2012 BMW 640i, part of the F12/F13 generation, is a luxury grand tourer known for its potent performance and sophisticated design. Powered by a robust turbocharged inline-six engine and paired with a smooth automatic transmission, it offers a compelling driving experience. While generally well-regarded for its build quality and engineering, like any vehicle, it has specific areas where owners have reported issues. This analysis focuses on documented technical reliability concerns beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential problem areas for prospective and current owners.

---


## Engine

### 3.0L Twin-Turbocharged Inline-6 (N55)
**Water Pump Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Coolant leaks, engine overheating, warning lights on the dashboard.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the electric water pump and thermostat.
- **Cost:** $700 - $1200

**Turbocharger Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of power, whistling noises from the engine bay, increased oil consumption.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of the turbocharger assembly.
- **Cost:** $1500 - $3000

**PCV Valve Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Rough idle, increased oil consumption, potential oil leaks, whistling sounds.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the Positive Crankcase Ventilation (PCV) valve assembly.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600

### General Electrical System
**iDrive System Glitches**

**Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Difficulty starting the car, electrical components malfunctioning intermittently.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and replacement of faulty modules causing parasitic drain or battery replacement.

### Summary Verdict
The 2012 BMW 640i is a reliable luxury vehicle, particularly concerning its powertrain and chassis components. The 3.0L turbocharged inline-six engine, while generally robust, has a few documented potential failure points, notably the electric water pump, turbocharger, and PCV valve, which can lead to significant repair costs if they fail. The 8-speed automatic transmission is highly regarded for its durability. Electrical gremlins, such as iDrive system glitches and battery drain, are the most commonly cited non-routine issues. Overall, the 640i offers a strong reliability record for its class, with most issues being manageable with timely maintenance and repair.
