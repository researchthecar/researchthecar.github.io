# 2015 BMW 640i

### Overview
The 2015 BMW 640i, a luxurious grand tourer, generally offers a high level of build quality and performance. It is equipped with a potent turbocharged inline-six engine and a smooth automatic transmission, contributing to a refined driving experience. While BMW vehicles are known for their sophisticated engineering, like any complex machine, some areas may experience issues over time. This analysis focuses on documented technical reliability concerns beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential problem areas for owners and prospective buyers of the 2015 model year.

---


## Engine

### 3.0L Twin-Turbocharged Inline-6 (N55)
**Water Pump Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Overheating, coolant leaks, warning lights on the dashboard.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the electric water pump and thermostat.
- **Cost:** $700 - $1200

**Turbocharger Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of power, whistling noises, increased oil consumption.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of the turbocharger assembly.
- **Cost:** $1500 - $3000

**PCV Valve Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Rough idle, oil leaks, blue smoke from exhaust.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the Positive Crankcase Ventilation (PCV) valve.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600

### Summary Verdict
The 2015 BMW 640i demonstrates good overall reliability, particularly with its robust powertrain. The 3.0L twin-turbocharged inline-six engine, while generally dependable, can experience issues with the water pump, turbocharger, and PCV valve, which are common for turbocharged engines of this era. The 8-speed automatic transmission is highly regarded for its durability. Electrical gremlins, such as iDrive system glitches and battery drain, are the most frequently cited non-wear-and-tear issues. Suspension, exterior, and interior components are typically solid. Proactive maintenance and addressing early signs of potential problems can help ensure a reliable ownership experience.
