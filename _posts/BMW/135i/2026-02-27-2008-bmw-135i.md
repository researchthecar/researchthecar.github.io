# 2008 BMW 135i

### Overview
The 2008 BMW 135i, powered by the potent N54 twin-turbocharged inline-six engine, offers a compelling blend of performance and luxury. While generally well-regarded for its driving dynamics, like many performance-oriented vehicles of its era, it has specific areas that warrant attention from an ownership perspective. This data focuses on documented reliability concerns beyond routine maintenance, highlighting common issues that owners may encounter. Understanding these potential pitfalls can help prospective buyers and current owners budget for and address necessary repairs, ensuring the longevity and enjoyment of this sporty coupe or convertible.

---


## Engine

### 3.0L Twin-Turbocharged Inline-6 (N54)
**High-Pressure Fuel Pump (HPFP) Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Stalling, rough idle, difficulty starting, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the HPFP.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1500

**Turbocharger Wastegate Rattle**
- **Symptoms:** Rattling noise from the engine bay, particularly on cold start or deceleration.
- **Solution:** Replacement of wastegate actuators or turbochargers.
- **Cost:** $1500 - $3000+

**Fuel Injector Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Rough idle, misfires, poor performance, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of faulty fuel injectors.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600 per injector

**PCV Valve Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Oil leaks, rough idle, increased oil consumption.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the crankcase ventilation valve assembly.
- **Cost:** $400 - $700

### Summary Verdict
The 2008 BMW 135i is a performance-oriented vehicle with a strong enthusiast following. Its primary reliability concerns revolve around the N54 engine, particularly the high-pressure fuel pump, wastegate actuators, and fuel injectors, which can be costly to repair. The electric water pump is another common failure point. While the manual transmission is generally robust, the automatic transmission has also proven reliable. Other systems like suspension, exterior, and interior components are typically less problematic. Owners should be prepared for potential maintenance costs associated with the advanced turbocharged engine, but with proper care and addressing known issues, the 135i can remain a rewarding car to own.
