# 2012 BMW 135i

### Overview
The 2012 BMW 135i, known for its sporty handling and potent turbocharged engine, generally offers a solid ownership experience. However, like many performance-oriented vehicles, it has specific areas that have seen more attention from owners regarding reliability. This analysis focuses on documented common issues beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential concerns for prospective or current owners. The N55 engine, while powerful, has had some specific thermal and fuel system-related concerns that are worth noting. Similarly, certain transmission components have shown a propensity for wear under spirited driving conditions. Understanding these potential weak points can help in proactive maintenance and budgeting for repairs.

---


## Engine

### 3.0L Twin-Turbocharged Inline-6 (N55)
**Water Pump and Thermostat Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Overheating, coolant leaks, warning lights on the dashboard.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the electric water pump and thermostat assembly.
- **Cost:** $700 - $1200

**Turbocharger Wastegate Rattle**
- **Symptoms:** Audible rattling noise from the turbocharger, especially on cold starts or at low RPMs.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the turbocharger wastegate actuator or the entire turbocharger assembly.
- **Cost:** $1500 - $3000

**Fuel Injector Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Rough idle, misfires, check engine light, reduced engine power.
- **Solution:** Replacement of faulty fuel injectors.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1500


## Transmission

### 6-Speed Manual
**Clutch Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Slipping clutch, difficulty shifting gears, burning smell.
- **Solution:** Clutch replacement (clutch kit, pressure plate, release bearing).
- **Cost:** $1000 - $2000


### 6-Speed Automatic (ZF 6HP19)
**Torque Converter Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Shuddering during acceleration, slipping gears, delayed engagement.
- **Solution:** Rebuild or replacement of the torque converter.
- **Cost:** $1200 - $2500

**Mechatronic Unit Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Harsh shifting, transmission warning lights, inability to select gears.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of the mechatronic unit.
- **Cost:** $1500 - $3000

## Electrical

### General Electrical System
**Power Steering Pump Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of power steering assist, whining noise from the pump.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the electric power steering pump.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1500

**iDrive System Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Screen freezing, unresponsive controls, audio issues.
- **Solution:** Software updates or replacement of the iDrive control unit.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1500

### Summary Verdict
The 2012 BMW 135i is a capable performance car with a generally good reliability record for its class. The most common concerns revolve around the N55 engine's cooling system (water pump/thermostat) and turbocharger components, as well as potential issues with the automatic transmission's torque converter and mechatronic unit. Electrical gremlins, particularly with the power steering and iDrive system, can also arise. While the manual transmission and suspension systems are relatively robust, owners should be aware of the potential for clutch wear. Proactive maintenance, especially concerning cooling system integrity and fluid changes for the transmission, is recommended to mitigate these potential issues and ensure a rewarding ownership experience.
