# 2023 BMW 230i Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2023 BMW 230i, buyers will find a sporty and modern luxury coupe that pairs engaging handling with strong turbocharged performance. Because this model year is relatively new to the pre-owned market, long-term pattern failures are still emerging, but early driver feedback highlights typical luxury-car electrical and trim quirks. Reviewing both powertrain specifications and any reported owner concerns can help you make an informed decision on your next vehicle purchase.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4

This 2.0-liter TwinPower turbo inline-4 engine produces approximately 255 horsepower and 295 lb-ft of torque. It offers a lively balance of daily efficiency and spirited acceleration for the luxury sport coupe segment.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Sport Automatic

This 8-speed automatic transmission delivers quick, smooth shifts and is paired with either rear-wheel drive or xDrive all-wheel drive. It is widely regarded as a responsive and refined unit in modern BMW applications.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V039000: Air Bags:Side/Window](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=230i&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** The second-row seat backrest cover panels for the side air bags may break during deployment of the side air bags. A backrest cover panel that breaks during side air bag deployment may cause the air bag to deploy improperly, increasing the risk of injury to second-row seat occupants.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the rear seat backrest side air bag cover panels, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V099000: Visibility:Power Window Devices And Controls](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=230i&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 118, "Power-operated Window Systems." Unintentionally closing the windows or sunroof without the key present can increase the risk of injury to occupants inside the vehicle.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the software for the Electronic Control Unit (ECU), free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V056000: Electrical System:Starter Assembly](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=230i&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** The engine starter may overheat and catch fire due to wear on an internal component. Fire increases the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the engine starter, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.
