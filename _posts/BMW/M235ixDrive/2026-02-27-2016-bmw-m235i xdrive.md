# 2016 BMW M235i xDrive

### Overview
The 2016 BMW M235i xDrive, a performance-oriented coupe, generally exhibits strong reliability, reflecting BMW's engineering prowess. It is powered by a potent turbocharged inline-six engine known for its robust performance. While the xDrive all-wheel-drive system enhances traction and handling, it introduces additional complexity. Owners generally report a positive ownership experience with the M235i, appreciating its driving dynamics and build quality. However, as with any performance vehicle, proactive maintenance and attention to specific components are crucial for long-term dependability. This analysis focuses on documented common issues beyond standard wear and tear.

---


## Engine

### 3.0L Turbocharged Inline-6 (N55)
**Water Pump Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Coolant leaks, engine overheating, warning lights on the dashboard.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the electric water pump and thermostat assembly.
- **Cost:** $700 - $1200

**Turbocharger Wastegate Rattle**
- **Symptoms:** Audible rattling noise from the turbocharger, especially on deceleration or at low RPMs.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the turbocharger wastegate actuator or the entire turbocharger assembly.
- **Cost:** $1500 - $3000

### Summary Verdict
The 2016 BMW M235i xDrive is a reliable performance car, with its primary concerns revolving around the engine's cooling system (water pump) and potential wastegate issues on the N55 engine. The transmission options, both automatic and manual, are generally robust. Electrical gremlins with the iDrive system are infrequent but can occur. Overall, the M235i xDrive offers a compelling blend of performance and dependability, provided routine maintenance is diligently performed and potential issues are addressed promptly. It stands as a solid choice for enthusiasts seeking a capable and engaging driving experience with a good reliability record for its class.
