# 2017 BMW 650i

### Overview
The 2017 BMW 650i, a grand tourer known for its blend of performance and luxury, generally exhibits strong reliability, reflecting BMW's engineering standards for its higher-tier models. While no vehicle is entirely immune to issues, the 650i from this model year has a reputation for robust mechanicals and a well-built interior. Common areas of concern, though not widespread, tend to be related to the complex electronic systems and occasional specific component failures, rather than systemic design flaws. Owners generally report a satisfying ownership experience, with maintenance costs aligning with premium vehicle expectations. This analysis focuses on documented technical issues beyond routine maintenance.

---


## Engine

### 4.4L Twin-Turbo V8 (N63B44O2)
**Turbocharger Oil Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Blue smoke from exhaust, oil smell, reduced engine power.
- **Solution:** Replacement of turbocharger seals and lines.
- **Cost:** $1,500 - $3,000

**Ignition Coil Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Rough idling, misfires, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of faulty ignition coils.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600

## Suspension

### Adaptive Drive System
**Active Roll Stabilization Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Warning lights on the dashboard, reduced handling stability, clunking noises.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of active anti-roll bars or related sensors.
- **Cost:** $2,000 - $5,000


## Electrical

### Infotainment System
**iDrive Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Screen freezing, unresponsiveness, audio cutting out.
- **Solution:** Software updates or module replacement.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1,500


### Battery Drain
**Parasitic Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Dead battery after short periods of inactivity, electrical components malfunctioning.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and repair of faulty modules or wiring causing drain.
- **Cost:** $400 - $1,000

### Summary Verdict
The 2017 BMW 650i is a reliable luxury coupe, with its powerful 4.4L twin-turbo V8 and 8-speed automatic transmission proving to be robust. The primary areas where owners might encounter issues are related to the sophisticated electrical systems, such as iDrive glitches and potential battery drain, and less commonly, specific components within the adaptive suspension. Turbocharger oil leaks and ignition coil failures are also noted, though not to a degree that significantly detracts from the overall reliability. For prospective buyers, ensuring these specific electrical and engine components have been well-maintained or addressed can lead to a very satisfying ownership experience.
