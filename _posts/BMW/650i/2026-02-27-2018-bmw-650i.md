# 2018 BMW 650i Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used luxury grand tourer, evaluating common owner reports can provide a clearer picture of long-term ownership. While these vehicles offer impressive performance and comfort, prospective buyers should review potential trouble spots regarding electronics, cabin accessories, and safety systems before making a purchase.

---

## Engine

### 3.0L Turbocharged Inline-6 (640i)

The 3.0-liter turbocharged inline-6 engine delivers smooth power and respectable efficiency for everyday driving. Regular maintenance is key to ensuring long-term durability on this powertrain option.

### 4.4L Twin-Turbocharged V8 (650i)

The 4.4-liter twin-turbocharged V8 engine produces robust horsepower and strong torque for high-performance cruising. Buyers should inspect the engine bay closely for any signs of fluid leaks or heat-related wear.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 8-speed automatic transmission provides rapid, smooth gear changes paired with rear-wheel drive or xDrive all-wheel drive. Routine fluid checks help maintain reliable operation over high mileage.

## Interior

### Panoramic Sunroof Shade

Cabin convenience features generally hold up well, though mechanical shades can experience wear.

**Sunroof Curtain Sagging**
- **Symptoms:** The panoramic sunroof sunshade or curtain fails to retract properly, sags downward, and can obstruct rear visibility or passenger space.
- **Solution:** Inspect the sunroof cassette track and mechanism; repair or replace the sagging shade assembly as needed.

## Safety

### Air Bag

Owner reports indicate occasional issues with the supplemental restraint system sensors.

**Passenger Seat Occupant Sensor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The passenger airbag warning light or malfunction indicator illuminates randomly on the dashboard.
- **Solution:** Requires diagnosis by a qualified technician and potential replacement of the passenger seat occupancy sensor mat.
- **Cost:** $2,600 to $3,000
