# 2021 BMW 540i xDrive Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2021 BMW 540i xDrive offers a compelling blend of luxury, technology, and robust performance, anchored by its smooth inline-six engine. While generally praised for its refined driving dynamics, used-car buyers should pay close attention to reported electrical system concerns, specifically related to starter components and battery management, as well as occasional mechanical wear items like motor mounts.

---

## Engine

### 3.0L Turbocharged Inline-6 (B58)

Powered by a 3.0-liter turbocharged inline-6 engine producing around 335 horsepower and 331 lb-ft of torque, this powertrain is widely celebrated for its smoothness and power delivery. Overall reliability is strong, though buyers should verify that starter and electrical recalls have been properly addressed.

**Starter and Electrical Concerns**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle fails to start, accompanied by electrical warning lights and potential short circuits.
- **Solution:** Have the vehicle inspected by a qualified technician or dealership to address starter-related recall campaigns and harness checks.

**Collapsed Motor Mounts**
- **Symptoms:** Vibration or abnormal movement felt from the engine bay during operation.
- **Solution:** Inspect motor mounts for signs of wear or failure and replace them as necessary.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

Paired with an 8-speed automatic transmission and xDrive all-wheel drive, gear shifts are exceptionally crisp and responsive. This transmission setup has a solid reputation for durability under normal driving conditions.

## Steering

### Wheels and Steering

Steering feel is precise and weighted well for a luxury sport sedan, though wheel durability can occasionally be tested by poor road surfaces.

**Highway Speed Vibration**
- **Symptoms:** Intermittent steering wheel or vehicle vibration while driving at highway speeds due to out-of-round factory wheels.
- **Solution:** Have wheels balanced, trued, or replaced if structural distortion is confirmed.

## Electrical

### Electrical System & Battery Management

Electrical architecture is complex, and owners have noted sporadic complaints regarding battery management systems impacting auxiliary functions.

**Battery Management and Braking System Faults**
- **Symptoms:** Multiple vehicle defects triggered by battery management issues, including unexpected firmness or travel in the brake pedal.
- **Solution:** Check battery health, charging systems, and software updates to ensure proper module communication.

## Exterior

### Sunroof and Visibility

Exterior panels and visibility components generally hold up well, though isolated glass incidents have been reported.

**Sunroof Shattering**
- **Symptoms:** Sudden explosion or shattering of the glass sunroof while driving at highway speeds.
- **Solution:** Inspect the sunroof glass for stress fractures and replace the assembly if damage occurs.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V598000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=540i&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 111, "Rear Visibility." If the driver relies on only the rearview camera image, the reduced rearview image view can increase the risk of a crash or injury.
- **What to do:** BMW will notify owners, and dealers will inspect and, if necessary, program the rearview camera with updated software. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V554000: Seat Belts](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=540i&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** As such, these assemblies fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 208, "Occupant Crash Protection." During a crash, a seat belt retractor that does not function as intended increases the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the seat belt retractors (including the seat belts) and, if necessary, replace the retractors, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V761000: Electrical System:12V/24V/48V Battery](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=540i&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The connection between the 48-Volt battery and the starter-generator may not have been assembled properly, which can cause increased electrical resistance at the connections. Increased electrical resistance can increase the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** BMW will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the connection between the 48-Volt battery and the starter-generator assembly, replacing it if necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V096000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=540i&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** When shifting into Reverse, a small portion of the rearview image may be obscured, or the screen may not illuminate. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 111, "Rear Visibility." If the driver relies on only the rearview camera image, the missing or reduced rearview image view.
- **What to do:** BMW will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the rearview camera software and update it if necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V046000: Power Train:Driveline:Driveshaft](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=540i&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** A broken output shaft can lead to a loss of drive power or a rollaway condition in a parked vehicle (if the parking brake is not engaged), increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** BMW will notify owners, and dealers will replace the output shaft(s), free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2021 BMW 540i xDrive remains an appealing option for used luxury car shoppers who value performance and comfort. Prospective buyers should thoroughly review service records, ensure all open electrical and starter campaigns are completed, and perform a pre-purchase inspection checking the motor mounts and wheels.
