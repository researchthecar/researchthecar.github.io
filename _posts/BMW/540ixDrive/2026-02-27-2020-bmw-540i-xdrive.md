# 2020 BMW 540i xDrive Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2020 BMW 540i xDrive offers a compelling blend of luxury, technology, and turbocharged inline-six performance. While generally regarded as a sophisticated and capable luxury sport sedan, used-car buyers should review owner-reported complaints regarding electrical issues, auxiliary systems, and localized engine components. Being informed about these specific areas helps ensure a thorough pre-purchase inspection before bringing home a pre-owned 5-Series.

---

## Engine

### 3.0L Turbocharged Inline-6 (B58)

The 3.0-liter turbocharged inline-six (B58) engine produces approximately 335 horsepower and 331 lb-ft of torque, delivering smooth power and strong overall reliability. Buyers should monitor for reported oil pump and drivability concerns during inspections.

**Oil Pump Component Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Potential drop in oil pressure which may lead to engine seizure or sudden loss of motive power.
- **Solution:** Inspect the internal oil pump components for wear or failure; replacement with updated parts may be necessary.

**Acceleration Hesitation**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle hesitates or stumbles when depressing the accelerator pedal from a complete stop or at various speeds, accompanied by a check engine light.
- **Solution:** Perform a diagnostic scan to check for fault codes and update software or replace failing engine management sensors as needed.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 8-speed automatic transmission paired with xDrive all-wheel drive delivers crisp, seamless shifting and robust long-term durability. No widespread recurring powertrain-specific transmission defects are highlighted in current owner reports.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

Braking performance is typically strong and confident, though individual component wear or hydraulic issues should be carefully checked.

**Brake Pedal Responsiveness**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may experience delayed deceleration or extended stopping distances when the brake pedal is depressed.
- **Solution:** Inspect brake pads, rotors, hydraulic lines, and brake booster components for proper operation.

## Steering

### Power Steering System

Power steering feels responsive and well-weighted, though rare electrical glitches can affect assist functionality.

**Loss of Steering Assistance**
- **Symptoms:** Sudden deactivation or loss of power steering assist while driving.
- **Solution:** Have the steering module and related electrical connections inspected, and verify whether applicable recalls have been completed.

## Electrical

### Audio and Infotainment Speakers

Electrical systems are generally advanced and reliable, though specific cabin components can occasionally be affected by moisture or sensor visibility quirks.

**Water Incursion in Premium Sound System**
- **Symptoms:** Water entering through poor drainage designs can damage front door midrange speakers in vehicles equipped with optional premium sound systems.
- **Solution:** Inspect door seals and speaker housings for moisture damage, and clear or modify drainage paths as needed.

### Instrument Cluster Display

Cabin displays offer sharp graphics, but drivers have reported occasional visibility challenges stemming from ambient light sensor tuning.

**Display Brightness and Lighting Indicators**
- **Symptoms:** The digital instrument cluster may become unreadable due to aggressive daytime dimming sensors, or lack a clear low-beam headlight indicator.
- **Solution:** Check instrument cluster settings and software calibrations with a qualified technician.

## Safety

### Air Bag System

Safety equipment is comprehensive, but routine diagnostic checks are recommended to ensure all occupant sensors and airbags function correctly.

**Passenger Seat Airbag Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Diagnostic scans reveal a fault or defect within the passenger seat airbag system requiring component replacement.
- **Solution:** Perform a diagnostic inspection of the Supplemental Restraint System (SRS) and replace faulty airbag sensors or modules.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V598000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=540i&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 111, "Rear Visibility." If the driver relies on only the rearview camera image, the reduced rearview image view can increase the risk of a crash or injury.
- **What to do:** BMW will notify owners, and dealers will inspect and, if necessary, program the rearview camera with updated software. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V096000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=540i&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** When shifting into Reverse, a small portion of the rearview image may be obscured, or the screen may not illuminate. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 111, "Rear Visibility." If the driver relies on only the rearview camera image, the missing or reduced rearview image view.
- **What to do:** BMW will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the rearview camera software and update it if necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V046000: Power Train:Driveline:Driveshaft](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=540i&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** A broken output shaft can lead to a loss of drive power or a rollaway condition in a parked vehicle (if the parking brake is not engaged), increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** BMW will notify owners, and dealers will replace the output shaft(s), free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V684000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=540i&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 111, "Rearview Mirrors." The lack of an image in the back-up camera display increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** BMW will notify owners, and dealers will update the back-up camera software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V598000: Engine](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=540i&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** During engine start, the engine management software may cause loss of braking assist. Loss of braking assist can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the engine management software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.
