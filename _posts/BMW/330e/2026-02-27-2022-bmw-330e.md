# 2022 BMW 330e Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

Shopping for a used 2022 BMW 330e means looking at a sophisticated plug-in hybrid luxury sport sedan that blends traditional driving dynamics with electrified efficiency. While overall owner feedback for this model year is light, buyers should carefully review service records and electronics functionality when evaluating a pre-owned model.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4 Plug-in Hybrid (B48)

This 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine is paired with an electric motor to produce a combined 288 horsepower and 310 lb-ft of torque. It offers a smooth power delivery with strong efficiency, though buyers should ensure all hybrid-system software updates are up to date.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Sport Automatic Transmission

The ZF 8-speed automatic transmission delivers crisp, rapid gear changes and integrates seamlessly with the electric drive unit. It is widely regarded as a highly durable and refined gearbox in modern BMW applications.

## Brakes

### Service Brake System

The braking system generally provides confident stopping power, though complex brake-by-wire integration with regenerative braking requires proper software calibration.

**Brake Responsiveness During Acceleration Incident**
- **Symptoms:** An owner reported an instance where brakes failed to halt the vehicle quickly during a sudden unintended acceleration event following a software update.
- **Solution:** Have a certified technician inspect the brake system components and verify that all vehicle software and hybrid control modules are operating on current calibrations.

The 2022 BMW 330e is an appealing choice for used-car buyers seeking a balance of luxury, performance, and plug-in hybrid capability.
