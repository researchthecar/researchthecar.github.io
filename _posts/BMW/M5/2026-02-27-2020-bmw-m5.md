# 2020 BMW M5 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2020 BMW M5, buyers are looking at a high-performance luxury sedan that pairs ferocious twin-turbo V8 power with everyday executive comfort. While these German performance machines offer thrilling driving dynamics, prospective owners should pay close attention to maintenance history, fluid leaks, and cooling system integrity. Reviewing owner reports helps highlight potential trouble spots to check during a pre-purchase inspection.

---

## Engine

### 4.4L Twin-Turbocharged V8 (600 hp / 553 lb-ft)

The 4.4-liter twin-turbocharged V8 engine delivers immense power, ranging from 600 horsepower in standard form up to 617 in Competition variants, paired with standard all-wheel drive. While generally robust under proper maintenance, high-strung performance engines require vigilant monitoring of cooling and ignition systems.

**Coolant Expansion Tank Leak**
- **Symptoms:** A leaking coolant expansion tank can drip fluid directly onto the fuel injectors, ignition coils, and spark plugs below, triggering a drivetrain malfunction warning and forcing the car into limp mode. Affected vehicles may also begin to shudder, misfire, and stall.
- **Solution:** Inspect the expansion tank and surrounding components for coolant seepage. Replace the failing tank and any damaged ignition components or wiring as necessary.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed M Steptronic Automatic Transmission

The 8-speed automatic transmission features Drivelogic shift modes and sends power seamlessly to the M xDrive all-wheel-drive system. It is generally praised for crisp, rapid shifts and strong durability under heavy performance use.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V598000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=M5&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 111, "Rear Visibility." If the driver relies on only the rearview camera image, the reduced rearview image view can increase the risk of a crash or injury.
- **What to do:** BMW will notify owners, and dealers will inspect and, if necessary, program the rearview camera with updated software. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V883000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=M5&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** The transmission wiring harness can become damaged, resulting in an electrical short circuit. An electrical short circuit can cause the transmission to shift to neutral resulting in a loss of propulsion, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** BMW will notify owners, and dealers will replace any damaged transmission harnesses. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V684000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=M5&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 111, "Rearview Mirrors." The lack of an image in the back-up camera display increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** BMW will notify owners, and dealers will update the back-up camera software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2020 BMW M5 is a masterclass in modern high-performance engineering, offering supercar-level acceleration paired with luxury sedan refinement. While major mechanical failures are not widely reported, buyers should thoroughly inspect the engine bay for fluid leaks, particularly around the cooling system and ignition components. Securing a comprehensive pre-purchase inspection from a qualified technician can provide great peace of mind before finalizing your purchase.
