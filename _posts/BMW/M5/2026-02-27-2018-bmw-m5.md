# 2018 BMW M5 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

Shopping for a used 2018 BMW M5 offers thrilling performance from its twin-turbo V8, but buyers should approach high-performance luxury sedans with a careful eye on maintenance history. While owner-reported complaints are relatively limited, specific mechanical vulnerabilities—particularly around the cooling system—require close inspection before purchasing.

---

## Engine

### 4.4L Twin-Turbocharged V8 (S63)

The 4.4-liter twin-turbocharged V8 engine (S63) pushes out around 600 horsepower, delivering blistering acceleration and robust track-ready capability. Buyers should verify routine cooling system maintenance and check for any history of fluid leaks.

**Coolant Expansion Tank Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Owners report coolant leaks originating from the expansion tank, which can potentially drip onto sensitive engine components like ignition coils and fuel wiring.
- **Solution:** Inspect the expansion tank for cracks or leaks and replace the assembly immediately if failure is detected.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed M Steptronic Automatic

The 8-speed M Steptronic automatic transmission pairs with an advanced all-wheel-drive system to deliver rapid, seamless gear changes. It is generally robust, though electronic anomalies should be checked during a pre-purchase inspection.

**Unexpected Shift to Neutral**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle may unexpectedly jerk while driving at highway speeds and shift out of drive into neutral, leading to sudden deceleration.
- **Solution:** Diagnostic scanning by a qualified technician is required to check for transmission control module software glitches or sensor faults.

## Safety

### Passenger Restraint System

Advanced safety systems protect occupants, though sensor and wiring issues can occasionally trigger dashboard warnings.

**Intermittent Restraint System Error**
- **Symptoms:** The iDrive system intermittently displays a passenger restraint system and seat belt warning, which can temporarily disable the front passenger airbag.
- **Solution:** Inspect the seat occupancy sensor, wiring harness, and buckle assembly, and replace faulty components as necessary.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V323000: Electrical System:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=M5&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** In certain driving conditions, the engine control unit software may cause the fuel pump to stop. If the fuel pump stops, the vehicle will stall, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** BMW will notify owners, and dealers will update the engine control unit software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V684000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=M5&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 111, "Rearview Mirrors." The lack of an image in the back-up camera display increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** BMW will notify owners, and dealers will update the back-up camera software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V473000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Storage:Tank Assembly](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=M5&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** The fuel level sensor in the fuel tank may become stuck against an in-tank fuel line, causing the fuel gauge to display an inaccurate and possibly larger amount of fuel than is actually available. An incorrect fuel reading may lead to the vehicle stalling and increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** BMW will notify owners, and dealers will secure the in-tank fuel line to keep the fuel line from contacting the fuel level sensor, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2018 BMW M5 is an exceptionally fast and capable luxury sport sedan, but potential owners must budget for premium upkeep. Prioritize models with comprehensive service records, and ensure a pre-purchase inspection thoroughly examines the cooling system and electronic components.
