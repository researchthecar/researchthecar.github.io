The 2022 BMW M5 is a high-performance sedan powered by a 4.4-liter twin-turbocharged V8 engine. This engine produces 600 horsepower and 553 lb-ft of torque in the standard M5 and 617 horsepower in the M5 Competition. All M5 models use an 8-speed automatic transmission. Drivers appreciate the M5's exhilarating performance, comfortable interior, and advanced technology. Some common dislikes include the high price tag, potential for expensive maintenance, and firm ride, especially in the Competition trim.

**Engine**

**4.4L Twin-Turbo V8**

*   **Oil Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks can occur from various points on the engine, including valve cover gaskets, oil pan gasket, and turbocharger oil lines. Symptoms include visible oil leaks under the car, a burning oil smell, and low oil level warnings.
    *   Solution: Replacing the affected gaskets or lines will resolve the issue. Retorquing certain bolts can also solve the issue.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,500+ depending on the source of the leak.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Coolant Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaks can occur around the water pump, coolant hoses, or expansion tank. Symptoms include low coolant level warnings, visible coolant leaks, and engine overheating.
    *   Solution: Replace the leaking components, hoses, or clamps.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,000+ depending on the location and parts needed.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**8-Speed Automatic (ZF 8HP)**

*   **Rough Shifting/Hesitation**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported experiencing rough or jerky shifts, particularly during low-speed driving. There can also be a hesitation when accelerating from a stop.
    *   Solution: In some cases, a software update of the transmission control module (TCM) can resolve the issue. A transmission fluid flush and filter change might also help. In more severe cases, valve body replacement could be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $3,000+ depending on the severity.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Torque Converter Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Humming or whining noises or abnormal vibrations at low speed can indicate a torque converter issue.
    *   Solution: Torque converter replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $4,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Adaptive Damper Malfunctions**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The M5 features adaptive dampers to control ride quality. Failures in the dampers or their control system can lead to a harsh or bouncy ride, error messages on the dashboard, or inconsistent handling.
    *   Solution: Diagnose the faulty damper or control module and replace it.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $2,000+ per damper.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **iDrive System Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported issues with the iDrive infotainment system, including freezing, lagging, or unexpected reboots.
    *   Solution: Software updates can often resolve these issues. In some cases, a replacement of the iDrive control unit may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 (software update) - $1,500+ (control unit replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Sensor Malfunctions (Parking Sensors, Driver Assists)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Issues can arise with parking sensors, driver-assistance systems (like lane departure warning or adaptive cruise control), and other sensors around the vehicle. This can manifest as false alarms, system malfunctions, or error messages.
    *   Solution: Diagnose and replace the faulty sensor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+ per sensor.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **No Issues Found**

**Interior**

*   **Trim Rattles**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rattles and creaks can develop in the interior trim pieces, especially around the dashboard, door panels, or center console.
    *   Solution: Tighten or replace any loose fasteners and apply felt tape or foam to reduce friction between parts.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $500+ depending on the source and complexity.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Seat Motor Failures**
    * Description/Symptoms: Failure of the seat motor in power seats. Can be a simple failure, or complete seat malfunction.
    * Solution: Replace seat motor.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1500+ depending on part costs.
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

The 2022 BMW M5 is a performance-oriented vehicle with a few common issues. Engine and transmission issues, while not widespread, can be costly to repair. Electrical glitches and interior trim rattles are less severe but can still be frustrating. Potential buyers should be aware of these potential problems and factor in the cost of maintenance and repairs when considering purchasing a used M5.
