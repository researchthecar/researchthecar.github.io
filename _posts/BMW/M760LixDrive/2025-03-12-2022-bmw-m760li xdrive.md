The 2022 BMW M760Li xDrive is a luxury performance sedan known for its powerful V12 engine, opulent interior, and comfortable ride. While the M760Li offers a compelling blend of performance and luxury, some owners have reported specific issues. The M760Li xDrive has a 6.6L Twin-Turbocharged V12 engine producing 603 horsepower and 627 lb-ft of torque. The engine is paired with an 8-speed automatic transmission. The M760Li is lauded for its smooth ride and powerful engine, but criticized for its high price, complicated technology, and limited practicality due to its size.

**Engine**

**6.6L Twin-Turbocharged V12**

*   **Oil Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks may develop from the valve cover gaskets, oil filter housing, or oil pan gasket. Symptoms include visible oil drips under the car, a burning oil smell, and low oil levels.
    *   Solution: Replacing the leaking gaskets or seals.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+, depending on the location of the leak and labor costs.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Coolant Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaks can occur from hoses, the water pump, or the radiator. Symptoms include a sweet smell, low coolant levels, and overheating.
    *   Solution: Replacing the leaking hoses, water pump, or radiator.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,500+, depending on the location of the leak and labor costs.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**8-Speed Automatic Transmission**

*   **Rough Shifting**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported experiencing rough or jerky shifting, particularly during low-speed maneuvers.
    *   Solution: In some cases, a software update can resolve the issue. In other cases, the transmission valve body or the entire transmission might need to be replaced.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 (software update) - $8,000+ (transmission replacement).
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Air Suspension Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The M760Li utilizes an air suspension system for a smooth ride. Problems can arise with the air struts or the air compressor, leading to a sagging vehicle, a rough ride, or warning lights on the dashboard.
    *   Solution: Replacing the faulty air struts or air compressor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $4,000+ per strut or for the compressor replacement.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **iDrive System Malfunctions**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The iDrive infotainment system can experience glitches, freezing, or random reboots.
    *   Solution: Software updates, replacement of the iDrive module.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $1,500+, depending on the specific issue.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Sensor Failures**
    * Description/Symptoms: Various sensors throughout the vehicle (e.g., parking sensors, adaptive cruise control sensors) can fail, leading to warning lights and malfunctions of related systems.
    * Solution: Replacing the faulty sensor.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+, depending on the sensor.
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Adaptive Headlight Malfunctions**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The adaptive headlights may not function properly, resulting in error messages or improper headlight aiming.
    *   Solution: Diagnosing and replacing faulty headlight components or modules.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+, depending on the extent of the damage.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Seat Motor Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The seat motor may fail, making it difficult or impossible to adjust the seat position.
    * Solution: Replacing the faulty seat motor.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,000+, depending on labor costs.
    * Recalls: None Found

*   **Sunroof Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: The sunroof may not open or close properly, or it may leak.
    * Solution: Repairing or replacing the sunroof mechanism or seals.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,500+, depending on the issue.
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2022 BMW M760Li xDrive, while a high-performance luxury vehicle, can be subject to several potential issues. Engine oil and coolant leaks are common, as well as issues with the transmission. Electrical glitches, particularly with the iDrive system and sensors, can also occur. Suspension problems, specifically related to the air suspension, can be costly. These issues can range from minor inconveniences to significant repairs, with costs ranging from a few hundred dollars for software updates to several thousand dollars for suspension or transmission replacements.
