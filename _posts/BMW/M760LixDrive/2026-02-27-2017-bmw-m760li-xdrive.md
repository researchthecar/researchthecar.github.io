# 2017 BMW M760Li xDrive Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2017 BMW M760Li xDrive represents the pinnacle of the 7 Series lineup, featuring a twin-turbocharged V12 engine and standard all-wheel drive. As an ultra-luxury performance flagship, it offers immense power and sophisticated engineering, but prospective buyers should approach with an understanding of the complex maintenance requirements associated with high-end German performance vehicles.

---

## Engine

### 6.6L Twin-Turbo V12

This 6.6-liter twin-turbocharged V12 engine produces approximately 601 horsepower and 590 lb-ft of torque, delivering effortless and immense acceleration. It is an exceptionally complex power plant requiring meticulous maintenance and specialized servicing.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Sport Automatic

The 8-speed automatic transmission pairs smoothly with the V12 engine, featuring steering-wheel-mounted paddle shifters and all-wheel drive integration. It provides refined gear changes under normal driving and crisp shifts when pushed.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V377000: Engine And Engine Cooling](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=M760Li&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** If oil leaks onto the front brake discs, a greater distance may be needed to stop the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** BMW will notify owners, and dealers will replace the engine oil cooler line, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2017 BMW M760Li xDrive is a rare and exceptionally fast luxury sedan that demands careful inspection before purchase. Buyers should prioritize a comprehensive pre-purchase inspection by a qualified specialist to evaluate the condition of its complex engine and electronic systems.
