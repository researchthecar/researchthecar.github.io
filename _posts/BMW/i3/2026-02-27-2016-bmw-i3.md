# 2016 BMW i3

### Overview
The 2016 BMW i3 is a unique electric vehicle known for its innovative design and urban-friendly dimensions. As an early entrant in the premium electric compact segment, its reliability is a key consideration for potential buyers. While electric powertrains generally offer fewer mechanical parts than traditional internal combustion engines, specific components and the overall integration of technology can present unique challenges. This analysis focuses on documented reliability concerns beyond standard maintenance, providing insights into potential issues across various systems.

### Summary Verdict
The 2016 BMW i3 generally exhibits good reliability, particularly in its core electric powertrain and chassis components. The electric motor and single-speed transmission have proven robust. However, potential owners should be aware of specific issues that have been reported, such as premature failure of the 12V auxiliary battery and occasional glitches within the infotainment system. The optional range extender, being a small internal combustion engine, is more prone to typical engine-related issues like warning lights and oil leaks, though these are not considered widespread. Overall, the i3 is a reliable choice for an electric vehicle of its era, with most potential issues being manageable and not indicative of systemic flaws.
