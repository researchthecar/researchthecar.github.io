# 2016 BMW i3 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The BMW i3 offers an innovative, eco-conscious city driving experience with its lightweight carbon-fiber-reinforced polymer body and unique electric or range-extender powertrain setups. As a used buy, it appeals to daily commuters looking for high efficiency and agile handling. However, owners should be aware of a few recurring mechanical and structural quirks—ranging from roof panel delamination to occasional steering and airbag sensor warnings—that require careful inspection prior to purchase.

---

## Engine

### Electric Motor / Drive Unit

The pure electric variant utilizes a synchronous electric motor producing roughly 170 horsepower and 184 lb-ft of torque, delivering brisk acceleration for urban environments. Overall reliability for the electric drive unit is generally high, though early models require routine checks of high-voltage components.

### Range Extender (REx) 0.65L 2-Cylinder

The REx option adds a small 0.65-liter two-cylinder gasoline generator to recharge the battery pack, providing extended travel range. While convenient for road trips, the complex dual-fuel system introduces additional maintenance considerations compared to the pure EV.

**Sudden Loss of Propulsion at Low State of Charge**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may experience an abrupt loss of motive power at very low battery states of charge when relying on the range extender, forcing drivers to pull over safely.
- **Solution:** Avoid running the vehicle down to near-zero states of charge, and have the REx software and fuel system inspected by a qualified technician.

## Transmission

### Single-Speed Fixed Gear Transmission

The single-speed automatic transmission delivers seamless power to the rear wheels without traditional gear shifting. It is mechanically simple and robust, though rare electronic fault codes can trigger limpmode warnings.

**Drivetrain and Transmission Malfunction Warnings**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may occasionally encounter sudden dashboard alerts indicating a transmission malfunction or drivetrain stop warning upon startup.
- **Solution:** Perform a diagnostic scan to check for underlying software or electronic sensor faults, and update vehicle control modules if necessary.

## Steering

### Electric Power Steering

The electric power steering system provides light, nimble maneuvering well-suited for tight city parking. Intermittent loss of assist has been reported by some owners, requiring careful diagnostic evaluation.

**Intermittent Power Steering Loss and Failure to Return to Center**
- **Symptoms:** The steering wheel may suddenly become heavy and difficult to maneuver during driving, or fail to properly return to center after making a turn.
- **Solution:** Inspect the electric power steering rack and control modules. Cycling the vehicle ignition often temporarily restores operation while faults are diagnosed.

## Exterior

### Carbon Fiber Roof Structure

The exposed carbon-fiber-reinforced plastic roof structure provides lightweight strength but requires attention to cosmetic protective coatings in sunny climates. Inspect the roof surface closely for signs of peeling or bubbling during a pre-purchase walkaround.

**Roof Panel Delamination and Bubbling**
- **Symptoms:** The translucent protective outer laminate coat on the carbon-fiber roof can bubble, wrinkle, peel, or deteriorate under prolonged sun exposure.
- **Solution:** Consult a specialized body shop to evaluate and reseal or refinish the roof panel before moisture can infiltrate the underlying structure.

## Safety

### Air Bag System

The i3 is equipped with a comprehensive supplemental restraint system designed to protect occupants in frontal and side impacts. Sensor and wiring harness faults can occasionally trigger warning lights that disable parts of the system.

**Driver Airbag Warning Light and Faulty Module**
- **Symptoms:** The airbag warning light illuminates on the dashboard due to high resistance or faults detected within the steering wheel airbag module wiring.
- **Solution:** Have a certified technician diagnose fault codes and replace faulty airbag wiring harnesses or modules as needed.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V720000: Air Bags:Frontal](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=i3&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** Unbelted, small adult, drivers may be at a higher risk of neck injury in the event of a frontal crash. These vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 208, "Occupant Crash Protection." During a frontal impact, unbelted, small adult, drivers could be at a higher risk of neck injury.
- **What to do:** BMW will notify owners, and dealers will update the Driver air bag control unit software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V088000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Hoses, Lines/Piping, And Fittings](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=i3&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** A fuel vapor leak in the presence of an ignition source can increase the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** BMW will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the fuel vent line, replacing it as necessary, and install a clip to prevent it from chafing, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The BMW i3 presents a compelling and eco-friendly option for used car buyers who prioritize cutting-edge design and efficient daily commuting. While its electric architecture and single-speed transmission are generally robust, prospective buyers should thoroughly inspect the vehicle for recurring quirks such as roof panel laminate wear, power steering irregularities, and airbag warning lights. Checking the service history and confirming proper battery and range-extender health will help ensure a satisfying ownership experience.
