# 2017 BMW i3 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2017 BMW i3 stands out as a distinctive, lightweight electric vehicle built with a carbon-fiber-reinforced polymer passenger cell. While its avant-garde design and nimble handling make it an appealing city commuter, prospective buyers should carefully evaluate potential trouble spots. Owners have reported issues ranging from cosmetic body concerns to complex electrical and drive system faults.

---

## Engine

### Electric Drive Motor (BEV)

The pure electric (BEV) configuration utilizes a synchronous electric motor producing approximately 170 horsepower and 184 lb-ft of torque. This powertrain offers instant torque delivery for urban driving, though owners should verify smooth operation during test drives.

**Electric Motor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle's electric motor may suddenly shut down while driving, causing an abrupt loss of propulsion or unexpected wheel lockup.
- **Solution:** Requires professional diagnostic testing and potential replacement of the electric motor electronics (EME) or drive unit.

### Range Extender (REx) Two-Cylinder Engine

The optional Range Extender (REx) variant adds a 650cc two-cylinder gasoline engine acting as a generator to sustain battery state of charge. It provides added peace of mind for longer trips, though it introduces additional mechanical complexity to maintain.

## Transmission

### Single-Speed Direct-Drive Transmission

Power is transmitted to the rear wheels via a single-speed fixed-ratio automatic transmission. This setup is generally mechanically robust and provides seamless, shift-free acceleration.

## Steering

### Electric Power Steering System

The electric power steering system provides light, maneuverable feedback tailored for city parking and tight turns.

**Loss of Power Assist**
- **Symptoms:** The steering wheel may suddenly become very heavy or unresponsive, pulling to one side and mimicking a total loss of power steering assist.
- **Solution:** Have the electronic steering assembly and associated wiring inspected by a qualified technician.

## Exterior

### Carbon Fiber Roof Panel

Exterior panels feature lightweight composite materials that require careful inspection for surface wear and weathering.

**Roof Clear Coat Delamination and Bubbling**
- **Symptoms:** The clear coat over the carbon-fiber roof begins to blister, scale, peel, or completely delaminate at highway speeds.
- **Solution:** Inspect the roof surface for early signs of blistering; professional refinishing or panel replacement may be necessary.

## Safety

### Occupant Restraint System

Advanced safety systems include multiple airbags and electronic stability controls designed to protect occupants in a collision.

**Passenger Restraint System Fault**
- **Symptoms:** An intermittent warning message appears indicating a driver restraint or passenger sensor failure, suggesting airbags may not deploy correctly.
- **Solution:** Scan the Supplemental Restraint System (SRS) module for fault codes and replace faulty seat occupancy sensors or wiring harnesses.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V720000: Air Bags:Frontal](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=i3&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** Unbelted, small adult, drivers may be at a higher risk of neck injury in the event of a frontal crash. These vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 208, "Occupant Crash Protection." During a frontal impact, unbelted, small adult, drivers could be at a higher risk of neck injury.
- **What to do:** BMW will notify owners, and dealers will update the Driver air bag control unit software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V088000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Hoses, Lines/Piping, And Fittings](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=i3&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** A fuel vapor leak in the presence of an ignition source can increase the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** BMW will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the fuel vent line, replacing it as necessary, and install a clip to prevent it from chafing, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V914000: Air Bags](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=i3&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** The affected vehicles have seat- mounted side air bag inflator initiators that may fail to ignite during a crash. If the air bag inflator initiator fails to ignite, the seat-mounted air bag will not deploy, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** BMW will notify owners, and dealers will replace the air bag modules, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

Purchasing a pre-owned vehicle requires a thorough pre-purchase inspection, particularly regarding the exterior carbon-fiber roof condition and the operational health of the electric motor electronics. While the vehicle offers efficient urban transport, checking for historical drivetrain or restraint system warnings will help ensure a more reliable ownership experience.
