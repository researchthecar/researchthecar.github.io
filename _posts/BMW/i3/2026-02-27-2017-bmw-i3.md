# 2017 BMW i3

### Overview
The 2017 BMW i3 is a unique electric vehicle known for its innovative design and urban-friendly dimensions. As an all-electric car, its powertrain differs significantly from traditional internal combustion engine vehicles. Reliability for the i3 generally centers around its electric drivetrain, battery, and associated electronic systems. While electric vehicles typically have fewer moving parts than gasoline cars, potential issues can arise from the complex battery management system, charging components, and software integration. Owners often report high satisfaction with the i3's performance and efficiency, with reliability concerns being relatively minor and often related to software updates or specific component failures rather than widespread systemic problems.

### Range Extender (Optional)
**Engine Malfunction Indicator Light**
- **Symptoms:** Check engine light may illuminate due to issues with the small gasoline engine used as a range extender.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and repair of the gasoline engine, potentially involving fuel system or ignition components.
- **Cost:** $300 - $800

**Range Extender Not Engaging**
- **Symptoms:** The gasoline engine fails to start or engage when battery charge is low.
- **Solution:** Troubleshooting the range extender control module, fuel pump, or starter motor.
- **Cost:** $400 - $1000

## Electrical

### High-Voltage Battery
**Battery Degradation**
- **Symptoms:** Gradual decrease in range over time, though typically covered by warranty for a significant period.
- **Solution:** Battery replacement if degradation exceeds warranty limits.
- **Cost:** $5000 - $15000+

**Charging System Faults**
- **Symptoms:** Inability to charge the vehicle, charging interruptions, or error messages related to the charging port or onboard charger.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and repair of charging port, onboard charger, or related control modules.
- **Cost:** $500 - $2000


### Infotainment System
**Software Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Occasional freezing, unresponsiveness, or errors with the iDrive system, navigation, or connectivity features.
- **Solution:** Software updates or resets; in rare cases, module replacement.
- **Cost:** $100 - $700


### 12V Auxiliary Battery
**Premature Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Electrical system malfunctions, warning lights, or inability to start the vehicle (even with a charged high-voltage battery).
- **Solution:** Replacement of the 12V auxiliary battery.
- **Cost:** $200 - $400

### Summary Verdict
The 2017 BMW i3 demonstrates good overall reliability, particularly in its core electric powertrain. The most common concerns tend to be related to the optional range extender engine (if equipped) and the complex electrical systems, including the high-voltage battery and infotainment. Software glitches can occur but are often resolved with updates. The 12V auxiliary battery is a known point of failure in many electric vehicles and should be monitored. For the most part, owners can expect a dependable driving experience, with potential issues being manageable and often covered by warranty in the early years.
