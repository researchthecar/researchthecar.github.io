# 2019 BMW i3 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2019 BMW i3 stands out in the used EV market with its distinctive carbon-fiber-reinforced polymer architecture and eco-friendly interior materials. For prospective buyers, understanding the available powertrain options and monitoring potential electrical or steering components are key to a confident purchase. While owner-reported complaints for this model year are relatively sparse, checking vehicle history and remaining battery health will help ensure a smooth ownership experience.

---

## Engine

### Electric Motor / Drive Unit

Equipped with a synchronous electric motor producing approximately 170 horsepower in standard form or 184 horsepower in the i3s variant, delivering brisk city acceleration. These electric drive units are generally robust and eliminate traditional internal combustion maintenance headaches, though buyers should verify high-voltage battery health.

### Range Extender (REx) 650cc Two-Cylinder

An optional two-cylinder gasoline generator that acts as a range extender, adding emergency mileage when the battery runs low. It requires traditional combustion engine maintenance like oil changes and fuel system checks, and adds extra mechanical complexity compared to the pure BEV version.

## Transmission

### Single-Speed Fixed Gear Transmission

Utilizes a single-speed automatic transmission paired with a direct-drive setup for seamless, shift-free power delivery. This transmission option is mechanically simple and proves highly reliable during daily driving.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V599000: Hybrid Propulsion System](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=i3&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** A shut down of high-voltage electrical power would result in a loss of propulsion, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** BMW will notify owners, and dealers will replace the EME module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2019 BMW i3 is an innovative and engaging city commuter, whether chosen in pure electric form or with the range extender. With minimal widespread mechanical complaints reported for this model year, buyers can focus their inspection on high-voltage battery degradation, specialized tire wear, and proper functioning of any equipped range extender. Thorough pre-purchase inspections remain recommended for peace of mind.
