# 2018 BMW i3 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2018 BMW i3 stands out as an unconventional, lightweight electric vehicle featuring distinctive styling and an available range-extending gasoline engine. As buyers look at the pre-owned EV market, understanding the foundational powertrain options and any reported owner concerns helps guide a smart purchasing decision. While overall complaints for this model year remain relatively modest, evaluating both battery-electric and range-extended variants ensures you select the right setup for your daily driving needs.

---

## Engine

### Electric Motor (170 hp)

This standard 170-horsepower electric motor delivers brisk acceleration and instant torque characteristic of modern EVs. It generally proves mechanically reliable for daily commuting without traditional internal combustion maintenance.

### 0.65L Range Extender (REx) 2-Cylinder Engine

The optional 650cc two-cylinder range-extender engine provides backup electricity for the battery pack, generating around 38 horsepower. Buyers considering this variant should verify proper maintenance history and routine operation of the fuel system.

## Transmission

### 1-Speed Direct Drive Automatic

This single-speed automatic transmission delivers power smoothly and directly to the rear wheels without traditional gear shifting. It is mechanically simple and rarely exhibits significant mechanical failures.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V652000: Equipment:Electrical](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=i3&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** Capacitors within the TurboCord Portable Chargers may fail, possibly resulting in a shock hazard or a fire. An electrical shock or a fire can increase the risk of injury or death.
- **What to do:** BMW will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the charging cable, and replace it if necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V310000: Hybrid Propulsion System](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=i3&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** A shut down of high-voltage electrical power would result in a loss of propulsion, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** BMW will notify owners, and dealers will replace the EME module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V720000: Air Bags:Frontal](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=i3&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** Unbelted, small adult, drivers may be at a higher risk of neck injury in the event of a frontal crash. These vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 208, "Occupant Crash Protection." During a frontal impact, unbelted, small adult, drivers could be at a higher risk of neck injury.
- **What to do:** BMW will notify owners, and dealers will update the Driver air bag control unit software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2018 BMW i3 offers an innovative, eco-friendly driving experience with minimal reported systemic defects. Prospective buyers should inspect the condition of the high-voltage battery and thoroughly test all electrical accessories. Choosing between the pure electric and the range-extended version depends heavily on your daily mileage requirements and access to charging infrastructure.
