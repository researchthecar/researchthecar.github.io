# 2020 BMW M340i Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a pre-owned 2020 BMW M340i, buyers are getting a performance-oriented sport sedan powered by the highly regarded B58 turbocharged inline-six engine. While the vehicle generally earns high marks for its engaging driving dynamics, impressive power delivery, and everyday usability, prospective owners should review specific owner-reported mechanical and electrical concerns. Paying close attention to engine component durability, drivetrain noises, and brake system maintenance history will help ensure a reliable ownership experience.

---

## Engine

### 3.0L Turbocharged Inline-6 (B58)

The 3.0-liter turbocharged inline-six engine (B58) produces approximately 382 horsepower and 369 lb-ft of torque, delivering swift acceleration paired with solid overall engineering. However, early production models feature internal oil pump components that can experience durability issues leading to unexpected engine stall.

**Oil Pump Component Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Sudden engine stall, loss of motive power while driving, or illuminated check engine and oil pressure warning lights.
- **Solution:** Inspect the internal oil pump components for wear or cracking, and replace the oil pump module if component failure is detected.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Sport Automatic Transmission

Equipped with an 8-speed ZF automatic transmission featuring steering wheel-mounted paddle shifters, this gearbox delivers quick, smooth shifts across rear-wheel-drive and xDrive all-wheel-drive configurations with minimal reported reliability concerns.

## Suspension

### Factory Wheels and Run-Flat Tires

The suspension is tuned for responsive handling, but factory setups utilizing low-profile run-flat tires are prone to frequent punctures and sidewall blowouts on rough road surfaces.

**Frequent Tire Blowouts and Sidewall Damage**
- **Symptoms:** Repeated sudden tire deflation, bubbling on the sidewall, or structural wheel damage after encountering minor road imperfections.
- **Solution:** Replace damaged run-flat tires and consider transitioning to standard tires with a dedicated repair kit if local road conditions are frequently harsh.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes and Electronic Parking Brake

The high-performance braking system offers strong stopping power, though occasional electrical glitches involving the electronic parking brake mechanism have been reported by owners.

**Electronic Parking Brake Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Parking brake fails to disengage properly, requiring manual overrides or dealer service interventions.
- **Solution:** Diagnose the electronic parking brake actuator and switch assembly; replacement of the center console switch panel may be required.
- **Cost:** $3,000

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V600000: Seats](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=M340i&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** Due to improper welding, the rear center headrest may fold in the event of a rear impact crash. If the headrest folds down in a crash, there would be an increased risk of injury.
- **What to do:** BMW will notify owners, and dealers will replace the rear center headrest, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V096000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=M340i&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** When shifting into Reverse, a small portion of the rearview image may be obscured, or the screen may not illuminate. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 111, "Rear Visibility." If the driver relies on only the rearview camera image, the missing or reduced rearview image view.
- **What to do:** BMW will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the rearview camera software and update it if necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V684000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=M340i&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 111, "Rearview Mirrors." The lack of an image in the back-up camera display increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** BMW will notify owners, and dealers will update the back-up camera software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V755000: Structure:Body:Trunk Lid](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=M340i&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 401, "Internal Trunk Release." If a person were inside the trunk compartment, they may not be able to get out, increasing their risk of injury.
- **What to do:** BMW will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the interior trunk release cable and properly install the cable, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V164000: Seat Belts:Front:Buckle Assembly](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=M340i&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** The sensors in the front seat belt buckles may inaccurately detect a belted occupant as unbelted, possibly causing the air bag and seat belt pretensioners to deploy improperly in the event of a crash. If the air bag and/or seat belt pretensioners deploy improperly in the event of a crash, there is an increased risk of injury to the driver and front.
- **What to do:** BMW will notify owners, and dealers will inspect and replace the front seat belt buckles, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V355000: Steering:Linkages:Tie Rod Assembly](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=M340i&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** In certain driving conditions, such as high temperatures and rough road surfaces, a steering gear tie rod may become damaged, possibly resulting in a fractured tie rod. A damaged or fractured tie rod may affect vehicle handling and control, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** BMW and Toyota will notify owners, and dealers will replace the steering gear tie rods, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V598000: Engine](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=M340i&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** During engine start, the engine management software may cause loss of braking assist. Loss of braking assist can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the engine management software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V211000: Seats](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=M340i&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** The seat frame may not have been welded properly to the seat rails. An improperly welded seat frame may not properly restrain the occupant during a crash, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the seat frame and back rest, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2020 BMW M340i is a thrilling and capable sport sedan that delivers exceptional performance, but it requires careful pre-purchase inspection. Buyers should verify the condition of the engine lubrication system, inspect the wheels and tires for wear, and ensure all electronic parking brake and driver-assistance features function properly before finalizing a purchase.
