**Introduction**

The 2018 BMW M6 Gran Coupe is a high-performance luxury sedan that exudes power and elegance. It is available with two engine options: a 4.4-liter twin-turbocharged V8 and a 5.0-liter twin-turbocharged V10. The V8 engine produces 560 horsepower and 500 lb-ft of torque, while the V10 engine generates 560 horsepower and 575 lb-ft of torque. Both engines are paired with a 7-speed dual-clutch transmission.

Owners of the M6 Gran Coupe appreciate its impressive performance, luxurious interior, and sleek exterior design. However, some have experienced issues with various components of the vehicle.

**Transmission**

**7-Speed Dual-Clutch**

**Hesitation or Jerking During Gear Changes**
Description/Symptoms: The transmission may hesitate or jerk during gear changes, especially when shifting from low to high gears.
Solution: Replace the transmission fluid.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

**Sunroof Motor Failure**
Description/Symptoms: The sunroof motor may fail, causing the sunroof to become inoperable.
Solution: Replace the sunroof motor.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

**Navigation System Malfunction**
Description/Symptoms: The navigation system may malfunction, displaying incorrect directions or freezing.
Solution: Update the navigation software or replace the navigation module.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500
Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2018 BMW M6 Gran Coupe is a highly capable and luxurious vehicle, but it is not without its issues. The electrical and exterior components seem to be the most problematic, with the sunroof motor failure and navigation system malfunction being the most common issues. The severity and cost of these issues can vary, but overall, they are not major concerns for most owners.
