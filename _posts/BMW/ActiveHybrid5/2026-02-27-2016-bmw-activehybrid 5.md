# 2016 BMW ActiveHybrid 5

### Overview
The 2016 BMW ActiveHybrid 5, a sophisticated blend of luxury sedan comfort and hybrid efficiency, generally presents a strong reliability profile. As a model year that benefited from BMW's established hybrid technology, it avoids many of the teething problems found in earlier iterations. Owners can expect a refined driving experience with a focus on performance and fuel economy. While no vehicle is entirely immune to issues, the ActiveHybrid 5 demonstrates a commendable level of engineering robustness. This analysis focuses on documented common problems beyond standard maintenance, providing insights into potential areas of concern for prospective and current owners.

### Hybrid Battery System
**Reduced Hybrid System Performance**
- **Symptoms:** Decreased electric-only range, reduced acceleration assist, dashboard warning lights related to the hybrid system.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and potential replacement of hybrid battery modules or related control units.
- **Cost:** $3,000 - $8,000+


### Infotainment System (iDrive)
**Occasional Glitches or Freezing**
- **Symptoms:** Screen freezing, unresponsiveness to inputs, audio cutting out.
- **Solution:** Software updates, hard resets, or potential replacement of the head unit.
- **Cost:** $500 - $2,500

### Summary Verdict
The 2016 BMW ActiveHybrid 5 is a generally reliable vehicle, particularly in its core powertrain components. The combination of the robust inline-six engine and the well-regarded ZF 8-speed automatic transmission contributes to its solid mechanical foundation. The primary areas where owners might encounter issues are related to the hybrid battery system, which can degrade over time and lead to performance reductions, and occasional electronic glitches within the iDrive infotainment system. These electrical concerns, while not universal, are the most commonly cited potential drawbacks. Overall, with proper maintenance, the ActiveHybrid 5 offers a dependable and luxurious hybrid driving experience.
