# 2017 BMW M140i

### Overview
The 2017 BMW M140i, a potent hot hatchback, is generally regarded as a reliable performance vehicle. Powered by BMW's robust B58 inline-six engine, it offers exhilarating performance with a commendable track record. While no car is entirely immune to issues, the M140i's engineering and build quality contribute to a positive ownership experience. Common areas of attention, as with many performance cars, can revolve around drivetrain components under heavy use and specific electronic modules. However, widespread catastrophic failures are not a common theme for this model year, suggesting good overall engineering and manufacturing.

## Engine

### 3.0L Turbocharged Inline-6 (B58B30)
**Coolant Leaks (Water Pump/Thermostat)**
- **Symptoms:** Coolant level dropping, engine overheating, visible coolant residue.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the water pump and/or thermostat housing.
- **Cost:** $500 - $900

**Turbocharger Wastegate Rattle**
- **Symptoms:** A rattling noise from the engine bay, particularly on deceleration or at low RPMs.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the turbocharger or wastegate actuator.
- **Cost:** $1500 - $3000

### General Electrical System
**Infotainment System Glitches**

**Battery Drain**

### Summary Verdict
The 2017 BMW M140i is a highly desirable performance hatchback with a generally strong reliability record. The B58 engine is a robust unit, and the ZF 8-speed automatic transmission is known for its durability. While minor issues like coolant leaks from the water pump/thermostat and occasional infotainment glitches can occur, they are not indicative of systemic problems. Owners should be mindful of potential wastegate rattle on higher-mileage examples and ensure regular maintenance. Overall, the M140i offers a compelling blend of performance and reliability for its class, provided it has been well-maintained.
