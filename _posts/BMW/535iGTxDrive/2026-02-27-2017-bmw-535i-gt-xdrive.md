# 2017 BMW 535i GT xDrive Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2017 BMW 535i GT xDrive combines luxury, spaciousness, and all-weather capability into a distinctive fastback grand tourer. Prospective used-car buyers should evaluate both the smooth powertrain options and overall vehicle condition carefully, as modern BMW models require diligent maintenance to avoid costly repairs as they age.

---

## Engine

### 3.0L Turbocharged Inline-6 (N55/B58)

The 3.0-liter turbocharged inline-six engine produces approximately 300 to 320 horsepower, offering robust acceleration and refined performance. It is generally well-regarded among enthusiasts, provided routine oil service intervals are strictly maintained.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission with xDrive

This ZF 8-speed automatic transmission delivers swift, smooth gear changes and pairs seamlessly with the intelligent xDrive all-wheel-drive system. It boasts a strong reliability track record in modern BMW applications.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V914000: Air Bags](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=535i&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** The affected vehicles have seat- mounted side air bag inflator initiators that may fail to ignite during a crash. If the air bag inflator initiator fails to ignite, the seat-mounted air bag will not deploy, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** BMW will notify owners, and dealers will replace the air bag modules, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2017 BMW 535i GT xDrive offers a sophisticated driving experience with capable powertrain options. Buyers looking at a used model should prioritize a thorough pre-purchase inspection and verify complete service history to ensure long-term dependability.
