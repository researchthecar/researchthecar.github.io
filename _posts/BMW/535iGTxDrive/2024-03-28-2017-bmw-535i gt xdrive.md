## 2017 BMW 535i GT xDrive

The 2017 BMW 535i GT xDrive is a performance-oriented hatchback that offers a blend of luxury, practicality, and sporty handling. The xDrive system provides all-wheel drive for additional stability and grip in all weather conditions. The 535i GT comes standard with a 3.0-liter turbocharged inline-six engine that produces 300 horsepower and 300 lb-ft of torque, paired with an eight-speed automatic transmission. Other engine and transmission options include a 2.0-liter turbocharged inline-four with 240 horsepower and 260 lb-ft of torque, mated to an eight-speed automatic transmission, and a 3.0-liter turbocharged inline-six with 400 horsepower and 450 lb-ft of torque, coupled with an eight-speed automatic transmission with paddle shifters.

The 535i GT is praised for its powerful engine, agile handling, and spacious interior. However, it has also been criticized for its high price, limited cargo space, and mediocre fuel economy.

### Engine

**3.0-liter Turbocharged Inline-Six**

**Valve Steam Seal Failure**
Description/Symptoms: Decreased engine oil levels, blue or gray smoke from the exhaust, and rough idling
Solution: Replacement of the valve steam seals
Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,000+
Recalls: None Found

**High-Pressure Fuel Pump Failure**
Description/Symptoms: Engine stalling, difficulty starting the engine, and loss of power
Solution: Replacement of the high-pressure fuel pump
Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,200 - $1,800+
Recalls: None Found

**VANOS Solenoid Failure**
Description/Symptoms: Reduced engine power, rough idling, and increased fuel consumption
Solution: Replacement of the VANOS solenoid
Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,200+
Recalls: None Found

### Transmission

**Eight-Speed Automatic**

**Transmission Fluid Leak**
Description/Symptoms: Visible leaks from the transmission, low transmission fluid levels, and slipping gears
Solution: Repair or replacement of the transmission seals or gaskets
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
Recalls: None Found

**TCM Module Failure**
Description/Symptoms: Transmission shifts erratically, delays in shifting, or the transmission may not engage
Solution: Replacement of the Transmission Control Module (TCM)
Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $1,500+
Recalls: None Found

### Suspension

**Front Control Arm Bushings**
Description/Symptoms: Clunking or rattling sounds from the front suspension, poor handling, and premature tire wear
Solution: Replacement of the worn bushings
Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
Recalls: None Found

**Rear Spring Perches**
Description/Symptoms: Rust or corrosion on the rear spring perches, which can lead to spring failure and loss of vehicle stability
Solution: Replacement of the spring perches
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800+
Recalls: None Found

### Electrical

**Battery Discharge**
Description/Symptoms: Battery drains quickly, even when the vehicle is parked and turned off
Solution: Diagnosis of the electrical system to identify the source of the parasitic drain and repair or replacement of the faulty component
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
Recalls: None Found

**Electrical Harness Faults**
Description/Symptoms: Various electrical issues, such as flickering lights, dashboard malfunctions, or difficulty starting the engine
Solution: Repair or replacement of the affected section of the electrical harness
Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the severity of the fault
Recalls: None Found

### Exterior

**Sunroof Drain Plugs**
Description/Symptoms: Clogged sunroof drain plugs can cause water to leak into the vehicle's interior
Solution: Cleaning or replacement of the drain plugs
Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
Recalls: None Found

**Tailgate Seal Failure**
Description/Symptoms: Water or moisture leaking into the vehicle through the tailgate seal
Solution: Replacement of the tailgate seal
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $300+
Recalls: None Found

### Interior

**Dashboard Squeaks and Rattles**
Description/Symptoms: Annoying creaking or rattling noises from the dashboard or interior trim
Solution: Tightening or replacing the mounting points or components that are causing the noise
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
Recalls: None Found

### Summary

The 2017 BMW 535i GT xDrive has a number of potential issues, some of which can be costly to repair. The most common issues include engine valve steam seal failure, high-pressure fuel pump failure, and transmission fluid leaks. While the 535i GT offers a comfortable and luxurious driving experience, it's important to factor in the potential costs associated with these issues before making a purchase decision.
