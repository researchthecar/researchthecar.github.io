# 2012 BMW 335i xDrive

### Overview
The 2012 BMW 335i xDrive, part of the E90/E92/E93 generation, is a performance-oriented luxury sedan and coupe known for its potent turbocharged engines and sophisticated xDrive all-wheel-drive system. While generally well-regarded for its driving dynamics and premium feel, like any complex vehicle, it has specific areas that have seen more frequent reports of issues. This analysis focuses on common technical reliability concerns beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential problem areas for owners and prospective buyers. Understanding these potential issues can help in proactive maintenance and budgeting for repairs.

---


## Engine

### 3.0L Twin-Turbocharged Inline-6 (N55)
**High-Pressure Fuel Pump (HPFP) Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Stalling, rough idling, engine misfires, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the high-pressure fuel pump.
- **Cost:** $700 - $1200

**Turbocharger Wastegate Rattle**
- **Symptoms:** Rattling noise from the engine bay, especially on startup or deceleration.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the turbocharger wastegate actuator or the entire turbocharger assembly.
- **Cost:** $1500 - $3000+

**Oil Leaks (Valve Cover Gasket, Oil Pan Gasket)**
- **Symptoms:** Visible oil spots under the car, burning oil smell, low oil level.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the leaking gasket(s).
- **Cost:** $400 - $900

## Electrical

### General Electrical System
**Water Pump Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Overheating, coolant leaks, check engine light, thermostat warnings.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the electric water pump and thermostat.
- **Cost:** $700 - $1200

**Power Steering System Leaks/Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Stiff steering, whining noise from the power steering pump, fluid leaks.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of the power steering pump or hoses.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1000

### Summary Verdict
The 2012 BMW 335i xDrive offers a compelling blend of performance and luxury. While its core mechanical components, particularly the ZF 8-speed automatic transmission and manual gearbox, are generally robust, owners should be aware of potential issues with the N55 engine's high-pressure fuel pump and wastegate actuators. Electrical components like the water pump and power steering system have also been noted for occasional failures. Proactive maintenance, especially concerning the fuel system and regular fluid checks, can mitigate some of these risks. Despite these potential concerns, the 335i xDrive remains a desirable vehicle for enthusiasts when properly maintained.
