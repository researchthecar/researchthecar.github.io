**2010 BMW 335i xDrive**

The 2010 BMW 335i xDrive is a luxury sports sedan that is powered by a 3.0-liter twin-turbocharged inline-6 engine that produces 300 horsepower and 300 lb-ft of torque. The xDrive system provides all-wheel drive for improved traction in all weather conditions. Other engine options available for the 335i include a 2.5-liter inline-6 and a 3.0-liter inline-6 with a single turbocharger. Transmission options include a 6-speed manual, a 6-speed automatic, and a 7-speed dual-clutch automatic.

The 335i is known for its responsive handling, powerful engine, and luxurious interior. However, there have been some reported issues with the vehicle.

**Engine**

* **N54 3.0-liter Twin-Turbocharged Inline-6**
    * **High-Pressure Fuel Pump Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: The high-pressure fuel pump can fail, causing the engine to lose power or stall.
        * Solution: Replace the high-pressure fuel pump.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
        * Recalls: 12V-161
    * **Wastegate Rattle**
        * Description/Symptoms: The wastegates in the turbochargers can rattle, causing a noise when the engine is under boost.
        * Solution: Replace the wastegates.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
        * Recalls: None Found
    * **Valve Cover Gasket Leak**
        * Description/Symptoms: The valve cover gasket can leak oil, causing smoke and oil consumption.
        * Solution: Replace the valve cover gasket.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500
        * Recalls: None Found

* **N52 2.5-liter Inline-6**
    * **Oil Filter Housing Gasket Leak**
        * Description/Symptoms: The oil filter housing gasket can leak oil, causing oil consumption.
        * Solution: Replace the oil filter housing gasket.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
        * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

* **6-Speed Manual**
    * **No Issues Found**
* **6-Speed Automatic**
    * **Mechatronic Unit Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: The mechatronic unit, which controls the transmission, can fail, causing the transmission to malfunction.
        * Solution: Replace the mechatronic unit.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,000+
        * Recalls: None Found
    * **Transmission Fluid Leak**
        * Description/Symptoms: The transmission can leak fluid, causing the transmission to overheat or malfunction.
        * Solution: Repair the leak and replace the transmission fluid.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
        * Recalls: None Found
* **7-Speed Dual-Clutch Automatic**
    * **Clutch Pack Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: The clutch pack in the transmission can fail, causing the transmission to slip or shudder.
        * Solution: Replace the clutch pack.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,500 - $3,500+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Front Control Arm Bushing Wear**
    * Description/Symptoms: The front control arm bushings can wear out, causing the suspension to become loose and noisy.
    * Solution: Replace the control arm bushings.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Battery Discharge**
    * Description/Symptoms: The battery can discharge prematurely, causing the vehicle to have difficulty starting.
    * Solution: Replace the battery.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Alternator Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The alternator can fail, causing the vehicle to lose power.
    * Solution: Replace the alternator.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **No Issues Found**

**Interior**

* **Interior Trim Panel Rattle**
    * Description/Symptoms: The interior trim panels can rattle, especially when driving on rough roads.
    * Solution: Tighten the trim panels or replace the clips that hold them in place.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2010 BMW 335i xDrive is a well-built and reliable vehicle, but it has some known issues that can be expensive to fix. The most common issues are related to the engine, transmission, and suspension. Some of these issues can be prevented by following the manufacturer's recommended maintenance schedule. However, other issues are more likely to occur due to the age and mileage of the vehicle. Overall, the 335i xDrive is a great choice for those who are looking for a powerful and luxurious sports sedan, but it is important to be aware of the potential issues that can come with it.
