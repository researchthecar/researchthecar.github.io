# 2016 BMW 340i Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2016 BMW 340i introduced the acclaimed B58 turbocharged inline-six engine, marking a notable step forward in performance and refinement compared to its predecessor. While generally regarded as an enthusiast favorite with strong powertrain credentials, used car buyers should still carefully review vehicle history records and inspect routine wear items.

---

## Engine

### 3.0L Turbocharged Inline-6 (B58)

The 3.0-liter turbocharged inline-six (B58) produces approximately 320 horsepower and 330 lb-ft of torque. It is widely praised for its smooth power delivery, impressive tuning potential, and significantly improved reliability over older BMW powerplants.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

The ZF 8-speed automatic transmission offers rapid, seamless shifts and pairs exceptionally well with the inline-six engine across rear-wheel and all-wheel-drive configurations.

### 6-Speed Manual Transmission

The traditional 6-speed manual transmission caters to purists seeking a more engaging driving experience, though it is considerably rarer in the used market.

## Summary Verdict

The 2016 BMW 340i stands out as a compelling choice for used luxury sport sedan buyers, largely anchored by its robust B58 engine and responsive transmissions. Prospective owners should prioritize finding examples with thorough maintenance documentation and budget for routine European car upkeep.
