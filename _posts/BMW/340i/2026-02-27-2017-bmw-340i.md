# 2017 BMW 340i

### Overview
The 2017 BMW 340i, part of the F30 generation, is generally considered a reliable performance sedan, particularly praised for its potent engine and engaging driving dynamics. While it offers a premium experience, like any vehicle, it has specific areas where owners have reported issues. This analysis focuses on documented technical problems beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential concerns for prospective buyers or current owners. The data aims to highlight common failure points and their associated costs to offer a comprehensive reliability picture.

---


## Engine

### 3.0L Turbocharged Inline-6 (B58)
**Coolant Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Visible coolant puddles under the vehicle, low coolant warning light, engine overheating.
- **Solution:** Replacement of leaking hoses, seals, or water pump.
- **Cost:** $300 - $700

**PCV Valve Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Rough idle, increased oil consumption, whistling noises from the engine bay.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the Positive Crankcase Ventilation (PCV) valve assembly.
- **Cost:** $200 - $500

### Battery Drain
**Premature Battery Failure**

### Summary Verdict
The 2017 BMW 340i is a strong contender in the luxury sport sedan segment, offering a generally reliable ownership experience. The powerful B58 engine is robust, though some owners have encountered minor coolant leaks and PCV valve issues, which are typically manageable. The ZF 8-speed automatic transmission is highly regarded for its durability. Electrical gremlins, particularly with the iDrive system and occasional battery drain, are the most frequently cited non-routine issues. Suspension, exterior, and interior components are largely free from widespread problems. Overall, with proactive maintenance and attention to potential electrical quirks, the 340i should provide a rewarding and dependable driving experience.
