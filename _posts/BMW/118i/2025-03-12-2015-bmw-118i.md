The 2015 BMW 118i is a popular compact car known for its sporty handling and premium feel. The base model typically features a 1.5-liter turbocharged three-cylinder engine producing around 134 horsepower and 162 lb-ft of torque. Other engine options available in some markets include a 1.6-liter turbocharged four-cylinder and a 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder with varying power outputs. Transmission options usually include a 6-speed manual or an 8-speed automatic. Owners often praise the 118i for its fuel efficiency and driving dynamics, while some common complaints revolve around the relatively small rear passenger space and occasional electronic glitches.

**Engine**

**1.5L Turbocharged Three-Cylinder (B38 Engine)**

*   **Timing Chain Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Excessive timing chain noise, especially on cold starts. Check engine light might illuminate with codes related to timing issues.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the timing chain, guides, and tensioner.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1200 - $2000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Oil Leaks from Valve Cover Gasket**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Oil visible around the valve cover, burning smell, low oil level.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the valve cover gasket.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600
    *   Recalls: None Found

**2.0L Turbocharged Four-Cylinder (N20 Engine)**

*   **Timing Chain Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rattling noise from the engine, especially on startup. Check engine light with codes related to camshaft position.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the timing chain, sprockets, and guides. Updated parts should be used to prevent recurrence.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1500 - $2500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**8-Speed Automatic (ZF 8HP)**

*   **Rough Shifting/Hesitation**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Noticeable hesitation or jerky shifts, particularly between certain gears.
    *   Solution: Transmission fluid change, software update, or valve body replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 (fluid change), $100 - $300 (software update), $800 - $1500+ (valve body)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **No Issues Found**

**Electrical**

*   **iDrive System Malfunctions**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Screen freezing, system rebooting, or complete failure to operate.
    *   Solution: Software update, replacement of the iDrive controller, or replacement of the iDrive unit.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300 (software update), $300 - $800 (controller), $800 - $2000+ (iDrive unit)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Battery Drain Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Car battery draining overnight or after a short period of inactivity.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis and repair of parasitic drain, replacement of faulty modules, or replacement of the battery.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $1000+ depending on the source of the drain.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Door Handle Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: Door handle becoming loose or failing to activate the door locking mechanism.
    * Solution: Replacement of the door handle.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Dashboard Creaking/Rattling**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Annoying creaking or rattling sounds emanating from the dashboard area, particularly on rough roads.
    *   Solution: Securing loose panels, adding sound-dampening materials, or replacing damaged components.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+ depending on the complexity.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2015 BMW 118i can be a fun and rewarding car to own, but potential buyers should be aware of the common issues. Engine issues such as timing chain problems and oil leaks can be costly to repair, especially on the N20 engine. The ZF 8-speed transmission can also exhibit some issues with rough shifting. Electrical issues with the iDrive system and battery drain are also potential concerns. While some fixes are relatively inexpensive, major repairs can be substantial, so a thorough inspection before purchase is recommended.
