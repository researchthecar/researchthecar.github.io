# 2019 BMW X1 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2019 BMW X1, buyers will find a stylish and capable luxury subcompact SUV that delivers engaging driving dynamics and a practical cabin. While owner reports to federal regulators are relatively modest, used-car shoppers should review potential owner-reported areas concerning engine cooling, electrical telematics, and drivetrain components.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4 (B48)

The 2.0-liter turbocharged inline-4 engine (B48) produces approximately 228 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque, providing lively acceleration for daily driving. It is generally robust, though routine inspection of cooling components is advised as vehicles age.

**Coolant Leaks and Thermostat Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Owners may experience low coolant warnings, visible fluid puddles, or engine temperature warnings due to coolant leaks originating from the thermostat housing.
- **Solution:** Inspect the cooling system for leaks, and replace faulty thermostats or gaskets as needed.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Steptronic Automatic Transmission

The 8-speed automatic transmission delivers smooth and rapid gear changes while pairing with either front-wheel drive or xDrive all-wheel drive systems. It is widely regarded as a dependable unit with few recurring mechanical complaints.

### Rear Differential and Drivetrain

Drivetrain components operate smoothly under normal conditions, but rear axle and differential assemblies should be checked during a pre-purchase inspection if unusual vibrations occur during tight maneuvers.

**Low-Speed Vibration When Turning**
- **Symptoms:** Some drivers note a noticeable shaking or vibration sensation coming from the rear of the vehicle when executing low-speed turns.
- **Solution:** Inspect the rear differential, vent components, and drivetrain fluids according to technical recommendations.

## Electrical

### Telematics Control Unit / Emergency Call System

Electrical architecture controls major convenience and safety features, though minor issues related to software or moisture exposure can occasionally appear.

**SOS Call System Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may see intermittent or persistent warning messages on the instrument cluster regarding a malfunction of the emergency call (SOS) system.
- **Solution:** Check for moisture intrusion around the roof antenna and have the telematics control unit or battery inspected and replaced if necessary.

## Exterior

### Moonroof

Exterior glass and trim are generally durable, though panoramic roof assemblies warrant careful visual inspection.

**Spontaneous Glass Fracture**
- **Symptoms:** In rare instances, the panoramic moonroof glass may shatter unexpectedly while driving at highway speeds.
- **Solution:** Inspect the glass panel for pre-existing chips or stress fractures and replace the sunroof assembly if compromised.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V601000: Steering:Linkages:Tie Rod Assembly](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=X1&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** A broken steering tie rod would lead to a loss of vehicle control, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** BMW will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the vehicles and replace the tie rods and ball joints as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V074000: Exterior Lighting:Tail Lights](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=X1&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** Contaminated hardware may cause the attachment brackets of the taillight housings to crack, causing the lights to loosen or possibly detach from the vehicle. If a taillight detaches from the vehicle, it would no longer provide the required lighting to following traffic, or it may become a road hazard, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** BMW will notify owners, and dealers will properly secure the taillights, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V349000: Structure:Body:Roof And Pillars](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=X1&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** In the event of a crash where the occupants head contacts the B-pillar, the pillar may not absorb an adequate amount of the impact. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 201, "Occupant Protection in Interior Impact." ## Summary Verdict

The 2019 BMW X1 offers an appealing blend of luxury, utility, and driving performance, making it a strong contender in the pre-owned crossover market. While most owners enjoy trouble-free motoring, buyers should perform a thorough pre-purchase inspection focusing on the cooling system, electrical telematics functionality, and drivetrain smoothness to ensure a confident purchase.
