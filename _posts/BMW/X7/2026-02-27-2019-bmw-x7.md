# 2019 BMW X7 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2019 BMW X7 is a luxurious and spacious full-size SUV offering high-end comfort and powerful engine choices. However, used-car buyers should approach early-production models with careful inspection, as owner reports highlight a few notable mechanical and electrical pain points.

---

## Engine

### 3.0L Turbocharged Inline-6 (xDrive40i)

The 3.0L turbocharged inline-6 engine (B58) produces around 335 horsepower, balancing smooth performance and strong everyday utility. It is generally robust, though early variants have generated owner concerns regarding internal oil pump components.

**Oil Pump Component Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Illuminated check engine light, engine overheating, or potential risk of internal component failure.
- **Solution:** Inspect the variable-displacement oil pump and consult a specialist or dealer for diagnostic checks.

### 4.4L Twin-Turbocharged V8 (xDrive50i)

The 4.4L twin-turbo V8 churns out an impressive 456 horsepower for effortless, rapid acceleration. While powerful, it requires diligent maintenance schedules to prevent long-term component wear.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

This widely used 8-speed automatic transmission delivers swift, refined shifts matched to BMW's intelligent all-wheel-drive system. It maintains a solid reputation for durability when properly serviced.

### Transfer Case

The all-wheel-drive drivetrain distributes power seamlessly across all four wheels, though high-mileage or early models can experience transfer case wear requiring component replacement.

**Transfer Case Wear and Binding**
- **Symptoms:** Grinding noises when turning at slow speeds and a locking sensation in the steering system.
- **Solution:** Replace the transfer case unit if internal mechanical wear has occurred.

## Electrical

### Starter and Electrical System

Electrical architecture is complex, supporting numerous cabin conveniences and driver aids. Owners have reported occasional starter motor failures that can prevent the vehicle from starting.

**Starter Motor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle fails to crank or start, leaving the driver stranded.
- **Solution:** Have the starter inspected and software updates applied or replace the starter motor if failed.

### Roof Antenna Seal

Cabin electronics generally function well, though environmental exposure can occasionally compromise exterior seals and lead to localized moisture issues.

**Water Leaks via Shark Fin Antenna**
- **Symptoms:** Water ingress affecting the Telematics Control Unit, disabling Emergency SOS features and disrupting GPS navigation.
- **Solution:** Inspect and reseal or replace the roof-mounted antenna housing.

### Third-Row Seats

The cabin is appointed with luxury materials and flexible three-row seating configurations, though motorized seat mechanisms can occasionally trigger error codes.

**Third-Row Backrest Lock Error**
- **Symptoms:** Dashboard warning messages indicating that the left or right third-row seat backrest is not in its proper end position.
- **Solution:** Clear any obstructions and reset the seat mechanism, or have the latch sensor inspected by a dealer.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V386000: Air Bags:Side/Window](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=X7&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** The driver and passenger head air bags may not have been properly aligned with the trim during installation. The head air bags may be impaired during deployment, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** BMW will notify owners, and dealers will inspect and, as necessary, reinstall the head air bags, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V513000: Fuel System, Other:Delivery:Fuel Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=X7&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** The high pressure fuel pump may not have been properly attached to the engine, which may cause the fuel pump flange to crack, resulting in a fuel leak. A fuel leak in the presence of an ignition source can increase the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** BMW will notify owners, and dealers will replace the high pressure fuel pumps, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V096000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=X7&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** When shifting into Reverse, a small portion of the rearview image may be obscured, or the screen may not illuminate. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 111, "Rear Visibility." If the driver relies on only the rearview camera image, the missing or reduced rearview image view.
- **What to do:** BMW will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the rearview camera software and update it if necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V541000: Wheels:Lugs/Nuts/Bolts/Studs](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=X7&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** Loose bolts may allow a wheel to detach from the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** BMW will notify owners, and dealers will replace and tighten the left side wheel bolts, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V135000: Seats](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=X7&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** The attachment bolts between the seat adapter and the vehicle may not have been properly tightened. If the bolts were not properly tightened, in the event of a crash, the occupants may have an increased risk of injury.
- **What to do:** BMW dealers will replace the attachment bolts for the seat adapter, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V684000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=X7&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 111, "Rearview Mirrors." The lack of an image in the back-up camera display increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** BMW will notify owners, and dealers will update the back-up camera software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V062000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=X7&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** The rotor and shaft within the integrated brake system may not have been welded properly and could separate during a hard braking event, possibly resulting in a loss of the Antilock Brake System (ABS) and braking assistance. A loss of ABS and a reduction in braking assistance can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** BMW will notify owners, and dealers will replace the integrated brake system, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V267000: Visibility:Sun/Moon Roof Assembly](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=X7&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 118, "Power-Operated Window Systems." A sunroof that closes without the key fob present inside the vehicle can increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will reprogram the roof function control unit, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2019 BMW X7 is an exceptionally comfortable and capable luxury flagship, but prospective buyers should prioritize models with comprehensive service histories. Checking for unresolved electrical recalls, transfer case smoothness during tight turns, and verifying proper operation of the third-row seating and starter will help ensure a more confident purchase.
