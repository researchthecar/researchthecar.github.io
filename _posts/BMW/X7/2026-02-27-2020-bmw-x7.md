# 2020 BMW X7 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2020 BMW X7 delivers flagship luxury, cutting-edge technology, and robust performance, but prospective buyers should carefully review potential mechanical and electrical trouble spots. While the vehicle offers impressive capability on long journeys, owner reports highlight concerns ranging from drivetrain wear to complex electronic glitches. Understanding these recurring issues helps ensure a more informed purchasing decision when shopping the pre-owned market.

---

## Engine

### 3.0L Turbocharged Inline-6 (B58)

The 3.0-liter turbocharged inline-six engine produces approximately 335 horsepower and 330 lb-ft of torque, providing smooth acceleration. It is generally robust, though occasional reports note oil pressure and stalling concerns.

**Engine Stalling and Oil Pressure Warnings**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may experience sudden engine stalling or dashboard warnings regarding low oil pressure and potential pump failure.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified mechanic inspect the oil pump and electronic control modules; replace failing sensors or the oil pump assembly as necessary.

### 4.4L Twin-Turbocharged V8 (N63)

The 4.4-liter twin-turbo V8 generates roughly 456 to 523 horsepower depending on the tune, offering effortless high-speed power. Buyers should monitor cooling and starter system components closely during pre-purchase inspections.

**Starter Motor and Starting Failures**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may unexpectedly fail to start or stall while driving, sometimes accompanied by electrical starter failure.
- **Solution:** Inspect the starter motor and related electrical wiring, and replace faulty starter assemblies.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 8-speed automatic transmission pairs smoothly with all available engines across standard all-wheel-drive configurations. It is widely praised for rapid, refined shifting and long-term durability.

### Transfer Case and Differential

The all-wheel-drive power distribution components require attentive fluid maintenance to prevent premature mechanical wear.

**Drivetrain Shudder and Differential Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers experience heavy shuddering when turning or an unstable driving dynamic due to front differential or transfer case wear.
- **Solution:** Check transfer case fluid condition or replace failing differential and transfer case units.
- **Cost:** $8,000

## Suspension

### Air Suspension and Control Arms

The sophisticated suspension setup provides an exceptionally plush ride quality, but heavy vehicle weight can accelerate bushing and joint wear.

**Thrust Arm Bushing Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Premature wear on front thrust arm bushings can produce abnormal wheel noises or handling looseness when cornering.
- **Solution:** Inspect control arm bushings during routine maintenance and replace worn suspension components.

## Steering

### Steering Column and Assist

Power steering assist mechanisms deliver responsive handling, though occasional column noise or electronic assist warnings have been reported.

**Increased Steering Effort and Locking**
- **Symptoms:** A warning message for increased steering effort may appear on the dash, occasionally accompanied by a stiff or locked steering wheel and squeaking noises.
- **Solution:** Inspect the electronic steering rack and steering column components; replace faulty sensors or steering assemblies.

## Interior

### Power Seating Mechanism

Interior cabin amenities are luxurious and feature extensive power adjustments, though motorized third-row mechanisms can occasionally experience electronic faults.

**Third-Row Seat Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Third-row powered seats may fail during operation, triggering dashboard warnings that seats are unlocked or trapping passengers.
- **Solution:** Inspect motorized seat switches and calibration modules; replace faulty seat control switches.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V631000: Steering:Rack And Pinion:Rack](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=X7&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** A loss of steering control increases risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** BMW will notify owners, and dealers will replace the steering rack, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V052000: Exterior Lighting](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=X7&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** The rear reflex reflectors may not reflect an adequate amount of light. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 108, "Lamps, Reflective Devices, and Associated Equipment." An inadequate amount of reflected light will decrease the vehicle's visibility to other drivers, increasing.
- **What to do:** BMW will notify owners, and dealers will replace both rear reflex reflectors, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V096000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=X7&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** When shifting into Reverse, a small portion of the rearview image may be obscured, or the screen may not illuminate. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 111, "Rear Visibility." If the driver relies on only the rearview camera image, the missing or reduced rearview image view.
- **What to do:** BMW will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the rearview camera software and update it if necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V684000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=X7&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 111, "Rearview Mirrors." The lack of an image in the back-up camera display increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** BMW will notify owners, and dealers will update the back-up camera software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V528000: Exterior Lighting:Headlights](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=X7&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 108, "Lamps, Reflective Devices, and Associated Equipment." Oncoming drivers may be confused by the blue light of approaching vehicles, which could potentially increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** BMW will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the headlamps and, if necessary, replace them, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V062000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=X7&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** The rotor and shaft within the integrated brake system may not have been welded properly and could separate during a hard braking event, possibly resulting in a loss of the Antilock Brake System (ABS) and braking assistance. A loss of ABS and a reduction in braking assistance can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** BMW will notify owners, and dealers will replace the integrated brake system, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V465000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=X7&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** The starter motor may have an extra bolt that could potentially fall into the transmission housing and cause an engine stall. An engine stall increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** BMW will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the starter motor for the extra bolt, removing it if necessary. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V678000: Suspension:Front:Control Arm](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=X7&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** If the weld between the front axle support and the right front control arm bearing bracket separates, vehicle handling and control may be affected, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** BMW will notify owners, and dealers will replace the front axle support, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2020 BMW X7 is a premier luxury SUV offering sublime comfort and strong powertrain choices, but used buyers should proceed with a thorough pre-purchase inspection. Particular attention should be paid to the service history of the transfer case, front differential, and electrical systems to avoid costly unexpected repairs.
