# 2012 BMW 335i

### Overview
The 2012 BMW 335i, part of the E90/E92/E93 generation, is generally considered a robust performance sedan, but like any complex vehicle, it has specific areas that have seen more attention from owners and technicians. This model year is known for its potent turbocharged engines and engaging driving dynamics. While many owners report high satisfaction, potential buyers should be aware of common issues that can arise, particularly concerning the powertrain and certain electronic components. Regular maintenance is crucial for longevity, but understanding these specific potential failure points can help in proactive ownership and budgeting for repairs.

---


## Engine

### 3.0L Twin-Turbocharged Inline-6 (N54)
**Fuel Injector Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Rough idle, misfires, check engine light, reduced power.
- **Solution:** Replacement of faulty fuel injectors.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1500

**Turbocharger Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of power, whistling noises, smoke from exhaust.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of turbochargers.
- **Cost:** $2000 - $4000

**Water Pump and Thermostat Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Overheating, coolant leaks, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the electric water pump and thermostat.
- **Cost:** $700 - $1200

**PCV Valve Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Rough idle, oil leaks, blue smoke from exhaust.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the Positive Crankcase Ventilation (PCV) valve assembly.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600


### 3.0L Twin-Turbocharged Inline-6 (N55)
**Turbocharger Wastegate Rattle**
- **Symptoms:** Rattling noise from the turbocharger area, especially on cold start or deceleration.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the turbocharger or wastegate actuator.
- **Cost:** $1500 - $3000

**Water Pump and Thermostat Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Overheating, coolant leaks, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the electric water pump and thermostat.
- **Cost:** $700 - $1200

**Fuel Injector Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Misfires, rough idle, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of faulty fuel injectors.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1500


## Transmission

### 6-Speed Manual
**Clutch Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Slipping clutch, difficulty shifting gears, burning smell.
- **Solution:** Clutch kit replacement (clutch disc, pressure plate, release bearing).
- **Cost:** $1000 - $2000


### 6-Speed Automatic (ZF 6HP19/21/26)
**Mechatronic Unit Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Harsh shifting, transmission slipping, error codes related to solenoids or sensors.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of the mechatronic unit.
- **Cost:** $1500 - $3000

**Torque Converter Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Shuddering at low speeds, slipping, rough engagement.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the torque converter.
- **Cost:** $1000 - $2000

## Electrical

### General Electrical System
**iDrive System Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Screen freezing, navigation issues, audio problems.
- **Solution:** Software updates or module replacement.
- **Cost:** $300 - $1000

**Headlight Ballast Failure**
- **Symptoms:** One or both headlights not working, flickering.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the headlight ballast.
- **Cost:** $200 - $500

**Window Regulator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Power windows not operating, window getting stuck.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the window regulator and motor assembly.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600

### Summary Verdict
The 2012 BMW 335i offers a compelling blend of performance and luxury. While its turbocharged engines and sophisticated transmissions provide exhilarating driving, they are also the most common sources of potential issues, including fuel injector problems, turbocharger wear, and automatic transmission mechatronic unit failures. Electrical gremlins like iDrive glitches and headlight ballast issues can also occur. The suspension, exterior, and interior generally hold up well. Prospective buyers should be prepared for potentially higher maintenance costs compared to less performance-oriented vehicles, with engine and transmission repairs being the most significant financial considerations.
