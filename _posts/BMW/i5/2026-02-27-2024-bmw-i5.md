# 2024 BMW i5 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2024 BMW i5 brings executive electric luxury to the midsize segment, offering impressive technology and refined driving dynamics. However, as an initial model year electric vehicle, early owner feedback highlights several emerging electrical, braking, and powertrain concerns that potential buyers should evaluate carefully.

---

## Engine

### Single Motor eDrive40 Electric Propulsion System

The rear-mounted single-motor powertrain produces approximately 335 horsepower, delivering smooth, responsive acceleration. Owners generally praise its efficiency and everyday driveability, though early production units have logged rare electrical propulsion faults.

### Cooling and Thermal Management System

The high-voltage battery thermal management system regulates temperatures during DC fast charging, though some owners report unusual operational noises and thermal valve alerts.

**Coolant Valve and Battery Thermal Warnings**
- **Symptoms:** Constant banging or clunking sounds, high fan noise during or after fast charging, and battery temperature warnings.
- **Solution:** Consult a dealer for coolant valve inspections and pending manufacturer service remedies.

## Transmission

### Single-Speed Fixed Gear Transmission

A direct-drive single-speed transmission channels power seamlessly to the wheels without traditional gear shifting. This unit operates quietly and reliably with minimal mechanical maintenance required.

## Brakes

### Integrated Braking and Stability System

The brake-by-wire and regenerative braking systems blend friction and electrical stopping power, though some owners have experienced stability control alerts and hydraulic system warnings.

**Brake System and Chassis Stabilization Warnings**
- **Symptoms:** Dashboard warnings indicate that the brake system and chassis stabilization require additional pedal force during operation or parking maneuvers.
- **Solution:** Have the vehicle inspected under relevant electronic stability and hydraulic braking campaigns or software updates at a dealership.

## Steering

### Electric Power Steering System

The electronic power steering provides precise handling feedback under normal conditions, but rare system failures can cause a total loss of steering assistance.

**Complete Loss of Power Steering**
- **Symptoms:** Sudden loss of power steering assistance while driving or stopped, accompanied by instrument cluster warning messages.
- **Solution:** Requires professional diagnosis of the electronic steering rack and associated 12V/high-voltage electrical feeds.

## Electrical

### Dual Motor M60 xDrive Electric Propulsion System

The dual-motor all-wheel-drive setup outputs up to 593 horsepower for high-performance driving. While powerful and dynamic, owners have reported isolated incidents involving unexpected high-voltage system shutdowns.

**Sudden Propulsion Loss**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle displays a red warning message and symbol in the gauge cluster indicating a shutdown, followed shortly by a complete loss of electric propulsion.
- **Solution:** Requires diagnostic evaluation by a certified dealer to check software and high-voltage electrical components.

## Exterior

### Panoramic Sunroof Glass

The large panoramic glass roof enhances cabin light and openness, but isolated incidents of spontaneous shattering have been reported.

**Spontaneous Glass Fracture**
- **Symptoms:** The panoramic sunroof glass shatters or blows out suddenly with a loud noise under normal driving conditions without impact.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the panoramic glass assembly through insurance or dealer warranty support.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V700000: Electrical System:Adas:Driver Monitoring:Camera/Sensor](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=i5&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** The ground connection to the steering wheel may not have been attached correctly, resulting in an inoperative hands-on detection system. An inoperative hands-on detection system may fail to detect when the driver's hands are not on the steering wheel and therefore not engage the emergency stopping assistant system, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and reattach the ground connection as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V885000: Forward Collision Avoidance: Warnings:External/Pedestrian Alert](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=i5&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 141, "Minimum Sound Requirements for Hybrid and Electric Vehicles." Without external warning sounds, pedestrians may be unaware of an approaching vehicle, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the external artificial sound generator software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V104000: Electronic Stability Control (Esc)](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=i5&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** Either of these scenarios can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the integrated brake system, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V135000: Electrical System:Propulsion System:Traction Battery](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=i5&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** An overheated battery module increases the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the high-voltage battery module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V697000: Electronic Stability Control (Esc):Control Module](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=i5&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** In addition, the Antilock Brake System (ABS) and Dynamic Stability Control (DSC) system may not function. Either of these scenarios can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the integrated brake system, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V395000: Electrical System:Propulsion System:Traction Motor:Controller:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=i5&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** A loss of drive power increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 26V422000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Power Assist:Electric:Control Module](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=i5&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** Either of these scenarios can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2024 BMW i5 is an advanced luxury electric sedan that delivers strong performance and comfort, but its first-model-year status means buyers should verify that all recall campaigns and software updates have been completed. Prioritize vehicles with documented dealer service history and thoroughly test all electrical, braking, and steering functions during a pre-purchase inspection.
