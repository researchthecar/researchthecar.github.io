# 2016 BMW 335i GT xDrive

### Overview
The 2016 BMW 335i GT xDrive, a versatile Gran Turismo variant of the popular 3 Series, offers a blend of sporty performance and practicality. Powered by a robust inline-six engine and equipped with BMW's xDrive all-wheel-drive system, it aims to provide a dynamic driving experience in various conditions. This model generally benefits from BMW's reputation for engineering quality, but like any vehicle, it has specific areas that warrant attention from an ownership perspective. Understanding potential reliability concerns can help owners budget for maintenance and address issues proactively, ensuring the longevity and enjoyment of this premium sedan.

---


## Engine

### 3.0L Twin-Turbocharged Inline-6 (N55)
**Water Pump Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Overheating, coolant leaks, warning lights on the dashboard.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the electric water pump and thermostat.
- **Cost:** $700 - $1200

**Turbocharger Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of power, whistling noises, increased oil consumption.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of the turbocharger assembly.
- **Cost:** $1500 - $3000

**PCV Valve Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Rough idle, oil leaks, potential engine misfires.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the Positive Crankcase Ventilation (PCV) valve assembly.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600

### Summary Verdict
The 2016 BMW 335i GT xDrive is a generally reliable vehicle, particularly praised for its powertrain and all-wheel-drive system. The primary areas of concern tend to be related to the engine's ancillary components, such as the electric water pump and PCV valve, which can require replacement over time. Turbocharger issues, while less common, can be costly. Electrical gremlins, especially with the iDrive system and occasional battery drain, are also noted. However, with proactive maintenance and addressing these potential issues, owners can expect a dependable and enjoyable driving experience from this sporty GT.
