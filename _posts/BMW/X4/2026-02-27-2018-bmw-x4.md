# 2018 BMW X4 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2018 BMW X4, buyers will find a stylish coupe-SUV offering sporty performance and luxury features. While owner reports to NHTSA are relatively sparse, prospective buyers should review key cooling system and powertrain electronic concerns before making a purchase.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged I4 (xDrive30i)

This 2.0-liter turbocharged inline-four engine produces approximately 248 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque, offering a balance of efficiency and daily pep. It serves as the standard powerplant for the xDrive30i trim.

### 3.0L Turbocharged I6 (M40i)

This 3.0-liter turbocharged inline-six engine delivers a robust 355 horsepower and 369 lb-ft of torque, powering the performance-oriented M40i trim. Buyers should monitor the cooling system closely for potential leaks.

**Coolant Leak and Water Pump Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have reported sudden engine overheating without prior warning lights, burning smells, or extreme corrosion around the water pump leading to coolant leaks and electrical damage.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified mechanic inspect the water pump and cooling system lines for leaks, corrosion, or electrical contamination, and replace failed components immediately.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic

This 8-speed automatic transmission is paired with all-wheel drive across the lineup, delivering quick, smooth shifts and responsive performance.

**Unintended Shifting into Park**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have experienced the vehicle independently shifting into park while coming to a stop at traffic signals, beginning as an intermittent issue.
- **Solution:** Have the electronic shifter assembly and transmission control module diagnosed by a professional technician for faults or software updates.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

The braking system provides strong stopping power suited for a luxury sport crossover, though proper maintenance and post-service checks are vital.

**Brake Pedal Response and Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have reported abnormal brake response following service visits, potentially leading to compromised stopping power or accidents.
- **Solution:** Inspect the braking hydraulic system, pads, rotors, and brake booster functionality thoroughly, ensuring proper bleeding and component installation.

---

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V608000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Cooling System:Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=X4&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** An improperly sealed electrical connector on the water pump may be exposed to water and short circuit. An electrical short increases the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the water pump and plug connector as necessary, and install a protective shield, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2018 BMW X4 combines luxury styling with engaging driving dynamics, but potential buyers should exercise caution with cooling system longevity and electronic shifter operation. A thorough pre-purchase inspection focusing on the water pump and electrical harnesses is strongly recommended.
