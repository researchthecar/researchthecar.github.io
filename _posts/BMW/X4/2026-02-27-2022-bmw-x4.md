# 2022 BMW X4 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2022 BMW X4, buyers will find a stylish coupe-SUV offering robust performance and modern luxury. While owner-reported complaints are relatively sparse, prospective buyers should review specific mechanical and driver-assist areas during a pre-purchase inspection to ensure everything operates smoothly.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4 (xDrive30i)

The 2.0-liter turbocharged inline-4 engine produces approximately 248 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque, offering a balanced mix of daily efficiency and punchy power. This powertrain is generally robust when maintained with regular oil changes and proper service intervals.

### 3.0L Turbocharged Inline-6 (M40i)

The 3.0-liter turbocharged inline-6 engine puts out roughly 382 horsepower and 369 lb-ft of torque, delivering brisk acceleration and refined performance. It is widely praised for its durability, though routine upkeep remains essential for long-term reliability.

### 3.0L Twin-Turbocharged Inline-6 (X4 M)

The high-performance twin-turbo inline-6 engine generates up to 503 horsepower in Competition spec, providing thrilling track-ready capabilities. Buyers should expect higher maintenance overhead due to its high-strung performance nature.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

This 8-speed automatic transmission pairs with all available engine options and standard all-wheel drive, delivering quick, smooth gear changes. It is regarded as one of the most dependable and responsive automatic units on the market.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

Brake system feedback indicates sporadic owner concerns regarding pedal feel and stopping smoothness.

**Brake Pedal Softness and Abrupt Locking**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may occasionally experience a soft brake pedal travel followed by sudden locking, which can cause the vehicle to pull or veer sharply.
- **Solution:** Have a certified technician inspect the brake hydraulic system, master cylinder, and anti-lock braking module for any anomalies.

**Jerking and Lunging During Application**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may exhibit unexpected lunging, rocking, or jerking motions when the brakes are applied during routine stopping.
- **Solution:** Consult a dealer or brake specialist to evaluate brake pad material, rotor condition, and software calibration for the regenerative or stability systems.

## Exterior

### Tailgate and Body Panels

Minor interior trim and body corrosion protection issues have been noted by individual owners.

**Anti-Corrosion Substance Leakage**
- **Symptoms:** An anti-corroding protective substance inside the tailgate panel may leak, potentially causing interior material damage.
- **Solution:** Inspect the interior tailgate panels for signs of fluid or protective wax leakage and clean or repair affected components.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

- **Issue:** The rear license plate may not be illuminated properly. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 108, "Lamps, Reflective Devices, and Associated Equipment." The incorrectly positioned license plate lamp cutouts could cause confusion to other drivers on the road, increasing the risk of a.
- **What to do:** Dealers will modify the rear license plate bracket, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V646000: Equipment:Other:Labels](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=X4&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 110, "Tire Selection and Rims." Incorrect tire size and pressure information could result in the wrong tires being installed, or improperly inflated tires, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the tire information label, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V070000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=X4&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** The mechatronics unit inside the transmission may not have been assembled properly, which could result in an internal leak. An internal transmission leak can result in a vehicle rollaway, increasing the risk of a crash or injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the transmission mechatronics unit, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V636000: Electrical System:Starter Assembly:Relay](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=X4&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** The engine starter relay may corrode, causing the relay to overheat and short circuit. A short circuit in the starter relay may increase the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the engine starter, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V056000: Electrical System:Starter Assembly](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=X4&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** The engine starter may overheat and catch fire due to wear on an internal component. Fire increases the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the engine starter, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2022 BMW X4 generally proves to be a solid pre-owned luxury choice with strong powertrain options and minimal widespread complaints. Prospective buyers should perform a thorough test drive focusing on brake responsiveness and inspect interior cargo areas for any fluid leaks.
