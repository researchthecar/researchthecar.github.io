# 2017 BMW X4 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

Shopping for a used 2017 BMW X4 offers coupe-like styling and sporty performance, but prospective buyers should carefully review owner feedback regarding potential electrical and driveline anomalies. While owner complaints provide valuable insight into real-world ownership experiences, they represent individual reports rather than systemic defects. A thorough pre-purchase inspection by a qualified technician is recommended before finalizing any purchase.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4 (xDrive28i)

The 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine produces approximately 240 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque, paired with standard all-wheel drive. This powerplant offers a balance of efficiency and punchy acceleration, though proper maintenance is critical for long-term reliability.

### 3.0L Turbocharged Inline-6 (M40i)

The 3.0-liter turbocharged inline-six engine generates around 355 horsepower and 343 lb-ft of torque for the performance-focused M40i trim. It delivers exhilarating acceleration and robust power delivery, though used buyers should expect higher routine maintenance and fuel costs.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 8-speed automatic transmission provides smooth and rapid shifts across all trim levels. It is generally robust, though electronic integration issues can occasionally manifest as erratic behavior.

## Steering

### Electronic Power Steering

Steering assists rely heavily on stable electrical voltage, making proper system diagnosis essential if warning lights appear.

**Intermittent Power Steering Loss**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may experience sudden warning lights and a temporary loss of power steering assistance during vehicle operation.
- **Solution:** Inspect the steering module, wiring harnesses, and battery voltage supply for faults or intermittent connections.

## Electrical

### Infotainment and Dashboard Screens

Owner reports highlight occasional electronic glitches affecting cabin displays and module communication.

**Screen Flickering and Display Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Dashboard and infotainment screens may flicker or remain blank for a few seconds, accompanied by various warning messages.
- **Solution:** Have the vehicle's electrical system, battery health, and software modules diagnosed by a professional technician.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V333000: Child Seat:Latch Strap](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=X4&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** The affected vehicles have lower anchor bars for securing child restraint seats that may become damaged when using the European-ISOFIX-type, rigid-style connector, child restraint system. Damaged lower anchor bars may increase the child's risk of injury in the event of a crash.
- **What to do:** BMW will notify owners, and dealers will weld a reinforcing bracket to the lower anchor bars and the vehicle body, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V608000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Cooling System:Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=X4&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** An improperly sealed electrical connector on the water pump may be exposed to water and short circuit. An electrical short increases the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the water pump and plug connector as necessary, and install a protective shield, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2017 BMW X4 combines engaging driving dynamics with distinctive styling, but used buyers should verify the operational health of its complex electrical systems. Reviewing service records and confirming that all applicable recall campaigns have been completed will help ensure a more confident ownership experience.
