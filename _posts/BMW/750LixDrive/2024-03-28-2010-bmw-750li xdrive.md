**Introduction:** The 2010 BMW 750Li xDrive is a luxury sedan powered by a 4.4L twin-turbocharged V8 engine producing 400 horsepower and 450 lb-ft of torque. It comes with an 8-speed automatic transmission and has an all-wheel drive system. The 750Li is known for its spacious interior, comfortable ride, and powerful engine. However, it has received criticism for its high cost and complex infotainment system.

**Engine**
* **N63 4.4L V8 Twin-Turbo**
    * **High-pressure fuel pump failure:** Symptoms: Difficulty starting, reduced engine power, rough idling. Solution: Replace the high-pressure fuel pump. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+. Recalls: N/A
    * **Turbocharger wastegate rattle:** Symptoms: Rattling noise during acceleration. Solution: Replace the turbocharger wastegate actuator. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+. Recalls: N/A
    * **Valve stem seal failure:** Symptoms: Blue smoke from the exhaust, oil consumption. Solution: Replace the valve stem seals. Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+. Recalls: N/A

**Interior**

**Summary:** The 2010 BMW 750Li xDrive is a well-built luxury sedan, but it is not without its issues. The engine and transmission have potential problems that can be expensive to fix, while the electrical and suspension systems can also develop problems. The interior and exterior of the vehicle are generally reliable, but the climate control and navigation systems can fail. Overall, the severity and cost of the issues with the 750Li can vary, and it is important to factor in potential repair costs when considering this vehicle.
