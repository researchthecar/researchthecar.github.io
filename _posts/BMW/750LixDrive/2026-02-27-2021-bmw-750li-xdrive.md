# 2021 BMW 750Li xDrive Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2021 BMW 750Li xDrive represents the pinnacle of luxury, delivering effortless performance, supreme cabin comfort, and confident all-weather grip. As with many high-end German flagship sedans, prospective buyers should carefully weigh the sophisticated engineering against potential long-term maintenance complexities. Reviewing service history and ensuring thorough pre-purchase inspections remain vital steps for anyone shopping the used market.

---

## Engine

### 4.4L Twin-Turbocharged V8 (523 hp)

This potent 4.4-liter twin-turbocharged V8 produces approximately 523 horsepower and 553 lb-ft of torque, delivering rapid and smooth acceleration. It provides exceptional highway power matched with sophisticated refinement, though routine maintenance costs tend to run high.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Sport Automatic Transmission

Equipped with an 8-speed sport automatic transmission paired with xDrive all-wheel drive, gear changes are seamless and lightning-fast. The system complements the high-output V8 effectively while maintaining smooth cruising behavior.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V096000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=750Li&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** When shifting into Reverse, a small portion of the rearview image may be obscured, or the screen may not illuminate. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 111, "Rear Visibility." If the driver relies on only the rearview camera image, the missing or reduced rearview image view.
- **What to do:** BMW will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the rearview camera software and update it if necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V379000: Child Seat:Tether: Connector/Clip](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=750Li&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The top child seat tether anchor on the rear middle seat may not be readily accessible. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 225, Section 6.1(b) "Child Restraint Anchorage Systems." A child restraint system placed on the vehicle's rear middle seat without utilizing the three-point.
- **What to do:** BMW will notify owners, and dealers will install a top tether anchor cover at the rear middle seating position. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V062000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=750Li&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The rotor and shaft within the integrated brake system may not have been welded properly and could separate during a hard braking event, possibly resulting in a loss of the Antilock Brake System (ABS) and braking assistance. A loss of ABS and a reduction in braking assistance can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** BMW will notify owners, and dealers will replace the integrated brake system, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.
