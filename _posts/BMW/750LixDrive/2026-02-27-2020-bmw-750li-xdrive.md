# 2020 BMW 750Li xDrive Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2020 BMW 750Li xDrive represents the pinnacle of luxury, offering immense power from its twin-turbocharged V8 engine combined with all-weather capability. As a sophisticated flagship sedan, prospective used-car buyers should weigh its high-end performance and comfort against the potential complexities and maintenance costs inherent in modern German luxury vehicles.

---

## Engine

### 4.4L Twin-Turbocharged V8 Engine

This 4.4-liter twin-turbo V8 produces roughly 523 horsepower and 553 lb-ft of torque, delivering effortless and rapid acceleration. Buyers should expect high maintenance demands and keep a close eye on cooling and turbo systems as mileage increases.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Sport Automatic Transmission

Equipped with an 8-speed automatic transmission paired with xDrive all-wheel drive, this setup provides seamless gear changes and responsive performance. Routine fluid inspections will help ensure long-term mechanical durability.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V096000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=750Li&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** When shifting into Reverse, a small portion of the rearview image may be obscured, or the screen may not illuminate. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 111, "Rear Visibility." If the driver relies on only the rearview camera image, the missing or reduced rearview image view.
- **What to do:** BMW will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the rearview camera software and update it if necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V379000: Child Seat:Tether: Connector/Clip](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=750Li&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** The top child seat tether anchor on the rear middle seat may not be readily accessible. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 225, Section 6.1(b) "Child Restraint Anchorage Systems." A child restraint system placed on the vehicle's rear middle seat without utilizing the three-point.
- **What to do:** BMW will notify owners, and dealers will install a top tether anchor cover at the rear middle seating position. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V684000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=750Li&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 111, "Rearview Mirrors." The lack of an image in the back-up camera display increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** BMW will notify owners, and dealers will update the back-up camera software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V062000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=750Li&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** The rotor and shaft within the integrated brake system may not have been welded properly and could separate during a hard braking event, possibly resulting in a loss of the Antilock Brake System (ABS) and braking assistance. A loss of ABS and a reduction in braking assistance can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** BMW will notify owners, and dealers will replace the integrated brake system, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

The 2020 BMW 750Li xDrive is a supremely comfortable and powerful flagship luxury sedan that requires careful inspection before purchase. While no major recurring failure patterns dominate owner complaints for this model year, maintaining a comprehensive service history is essential to keeping ownership costs manageable.
