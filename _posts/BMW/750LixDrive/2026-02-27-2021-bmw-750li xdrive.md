## Suspension

### Adaptive Air Suspension
**Air Spring Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle may sag on one corner, uneven ride height, warning lights on the dashboard.
- **Solution:** Replacement of leaking air spring or associated lines.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1500 per corner

**Compressor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Inability to adjust ride height, constant running of compressor, vehicle may sag.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the air suspension compressor.
- **Cost:** $700 - $1200


## Electrical

### Infotainment System
**Software Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Screen freezing, unresponsiveness, connectivity issues (Bluetooth, Apple CarPlay/Android Auto).
- **Solution:** Software updates, system resets, or potential module replacement.
- **Cost:** $150 - $500 (for updates/diagnostics), $1000+ (for module replacement)


### Battery Drain
**Parasitic Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Battery dying prematurely, difficulty starting the vehicle, electrical components malfunctioning.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis to identify the source of the drain (e.g., faulty module, aftermarket accessory) and repair or replacement.
- **Cost:** $200 - $800 (depending on cause)

### Summary Verdict
The 2021 BMW 750Li xDrive demonstrates strong reliability in its powertrain, with the 4.4L Twin-Turbo V8 engine and 8-speed automatic transmission generally free from widespread issues. The primary areas that may require attention are related to the adaptive air suspension, where leaks or compressor failures can occur, and occasional electrical gremlins, particularly with the infotainment system's software or parasitic battery drain. These issues, while not universal, are the most commonly cited technical concerns beyond standard maintenance. Overall, the 750Li xDrive offers a dependable luxury experience, but owners should be aware of potential costs associated with suspension and electrical system repairs.
