# 2017 BMW 220i Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2017 BMW 220i, buyers will find a sporty and engaging compact luxury car known for its dynamic handling and upscale interior. While overall owner feedback is relatively light, prospective buyers should pay close attention to cooling system performance, tire durability, and interior restraint electronics during a pre-purchase inspection.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4 (B48)

The 2.0-liter turbocharged inline-4 engine (B48) produces approximately 248 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque, offering lively acceleration and solid efficiency. Routine cooling system maintenance and vigilance regarding fluid levels are recommended for long-term reliability.

**Coolant Loss and Overheating**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may notice low coolant warning messages, unexpected drops in coolant levels, or engine overheating while driving at highway speeds.
- **Solution:** Inspect coolant hoses, connections, and the expansion tank for leaks, and replace damaged components as needed.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Sport Automatic Transmission

The 8-speed ZF automatic transmission delivers smooth and rapid shifts paired with rear-wheel drive or all-wheel drive configurations. It is generally regarded as a robust and dependable unit with minimal owner-reported complaints.

### 6-Speed Manual Transmission

The traditional 6-speed manual transmission caters to enthusiast drivers seeking direct gear control. It exhibits solid mechanical durability when paired with standard clutch maintenance.

## Suspension

### Wheels and Tires

Factory suspension tuning emphasizes agile handling, though low-profile tires are prone to road impact damage.

**Run-Flat Tire Sidewall Damage**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may experience tire bubbles, tread separation, or premature wear when factory low-profile run-flat tires impact road potholes or rough surfaces.
- **Solution:** Replace damaged tires and consider transitioning to standard tires with a proper roadside repair kit if low-profile ride quality is a concern.
- **Cost:** $1300

## Brakes

### Parking Brake

Braking components provide strong stopping power, though occasional parking brake switch or actuator faults have been reported.

**Parking Brake Inoperability**
- **Symptoms:** The emergency or parking brake fails to engage or hold the vehicle, often without triggering dashboard warning lights.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified technician inspect the parking brake actuator, cables, and electronic control modules.

## Safety

### Airbags and Restraints

Passive safety systems are comprehensive, but sensitive occupancy sensor mats can occasionally fail over time.

**Passenger Seat Sensor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Illuminated restraint warning lights or failure of the passenger seatbelt to lock properly during braking due to a faulty occupant sensor mat.
- **Solution:** Replace the faulty passenger seat occupancy sensor mat and recalibrate the restraint system.

## Summary Verdict

The 2017 BMW 220i is an appealing choice for used car buyers seeking engaging driving dynamics and premium features. While owner feedback points to a few isolated concerns regarding cooling system leaks, run-flat tire durability, and passenger seat sensors, a thorough pre-purchase inspection should help ensure a reliable ownership experience.
