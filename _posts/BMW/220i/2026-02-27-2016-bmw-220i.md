# 2016 BMW 220i

### Overview
The 2016 BMW 220i, part of the F22 generation, is generally regarded as a reliable vehicle, particularly given its sporty nature. It typically features a potent turbocharged four-cylinder engine paired with either a manual or automatic transmission. Owners often praise its engaging driving dynamics and premium interior. While no car is entirely free from potential issues, the 220i has demonstrated a solid track record for its core mechanical components. Common areas to monitor, as with many modern vehicles, can include certain electrical systems and specific wear items, though widespread critical failures are not prevalent. Overall, it represents a well-built compact luxury coupe.

### Summary Verdict
The 2016 BMW 220i demonstrates good overall reliability, with its core powertrain components (2.0L B48 engine and available automatic/manual transmissions) showing no significant widespread issues. The chassis and exterior also appear robust. The primary areas where minor concerns have been noted are within the electrical system, specifically occasional infotainment glitches and potential battery drain issues, which are not uncommon in modern vehicles with complex electronics. These issues are generally manageable with software updates or component diagnosis. For prospective buyers, focusing on a well-maintained example with a history of regular servicing is recommended, but the 220i is a solid choice in its segment.
