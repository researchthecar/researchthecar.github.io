# 2018 BMW 220i Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2018 BMW 220i, buyers will find a sporty and engaging compact luxury car that balances everyday usability with rear-wheel-drive dynamics. While generally well-regarded, prospective owners should review owner-reported complaints regarding powertrain electronics and cooling components, alongside common real-world wheel and tire durability concerns.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4 (B48)

The 2.0-liter turbocharged inline-4 engine (B48) generates approximately 248 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque, providing brisk acceleration and respectable efficiency. It is generally robust, though buyers should monitor cooling components and electronic sensors for potential owner-reported faults.

**Engine Shutdown and Drivetrain Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may unexpectedly shut down while driving at highway speeds accompanied by a drivetrain error message on the instrument cluster, resulting in a sudden loss of power.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified technician diagnose the powertrain control modules, fuel system, or electronic sensors to identify the root cause of the unexpected shutdown.

**Coolant Pipe Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The coolant pipe connecting the cylinder head to the coolant reservoir may break unexpectedly, triggering a warning lamp and leaving the vehicle stranded.
- **Solution:** Inspect the cooling system hoses and plastic fittings for signs of brittleness or leaks, and replace any compromised coolant lines immediately.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

This 8-speed automatic transmission delivers smooth, rapid shifts and pairs effectively with the rear-wheel-drive layout. No major recurring mechanical defects have been widely reported by owners.

## Suspension

### Wheels and Run-Flat Tires

Suspension components generally maintain stable handling, though low-profile tire setups are susceptible to impact damage on rough roads.

**Run-Flat Tire Blowouts from Potholes**
- **Symptoms:** Factory low-profile run-flat tires may experience sudden blowouts or sidewall failures when encountering sharp potholes or road hazards.
- **Solution:** Inspect tires and wheels regularly for structural damage, and consider transitioning to standard tires with a more forgiving sidewall if local road conditions are poor.
