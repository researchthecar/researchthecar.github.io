# 2019 BMW 220i Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a 2019 BMW 220i, buyers are typically looking for a blend of engaging rear-wheel-drive dynamics and everyday luxury. While formal complaint volume for this specific model and year is minimal, prospective owners should still carefully evaluate overall mechanical health during a pre-purchase inspection.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4 (B48)

The 2.0-liter turbocharged inline-4 engine (B48) typically produces around 248 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque, providing spirited acceleration for daily driving. It is generally regarded as a robust powertrain, though proper cooling system maintenance is vital.

**Coolant Low and Engine Overheating**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may receive dashboard warnings regarding low coolant, followed promptly by an instruction to pull over immediately to prevent severe engine damage.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified mechanic inspect the cooling system for leaks, test the water pump, and verify proper thermostat operation.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Sport Automatic

This 8-speed automatic transmission delivers quick, seamless gear changes and pairs smoothly with the turbo-4 engine. It is widely praised for both refinement and reliability in modern BMW applications.

The 2019 BMW 220i offers an attractive mix of performance and luxury, backed by limited reported owner complaints.
