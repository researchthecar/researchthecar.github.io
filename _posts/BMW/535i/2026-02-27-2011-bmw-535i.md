# 2011 BMW 535i

### Overview
The 2011 BMW 535i, part of the F10 generation, is a luxury executive sedan known for its potent performance and refined driving dynamics. Powered by a robust turbocharged inline-six engine and typically paired with an advanced automatic transmission, it offers a compelling blend of power and comfort. While generally well-regarded for its engineering, like any complex vehicle, it has specific areas that have seen more attention from owners regarding reliability. This analysis focuses on documented common issues beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential concerns for owners and prospective buyers.

---


## Engine

### 3.0L Twin-Turbocharged Inline-6 (N55)
**Turbocharger Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of power, whistling noises, blue smoke from exhaust.
- **Solution:** Turbocharger replacement.
- **Cost:** $1,500 - $3,000

**Water Pump Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Overheating, coolant leaks, warning lights on the dashboard.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the electric water pump and thermostat.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1,500

**PCV Valve Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Rough idle, oil leaks, increased oil consumption, whistling noise.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the Positive Crankcase Ventilation (PCV) valve assembly.
- **Cost:** $400 - $700

### Summary Verdict
The 2011 BMW 535i offers a premium driving experience, but potential buyers should be aware of specific reliability concerns, particularly within the engine. The 3.0L twin-turbocharged N55 engine is prone to issues with the turbocharger, electric water pump, and PCV valve, which can be costly to repair. The 8-speed automatic transmission has proven to be quite reliable. Electrical gremlins, such as iDrive system glitches and battery drain, can also occur. While the suspension, exterior, and interior generally hold up well, proactive maintenance and inspection of the noted engine and electrical components are recommended for long-term ownership satisfaction.
