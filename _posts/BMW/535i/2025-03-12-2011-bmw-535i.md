Here's a comprehensive list of common issues and complaints for the 2011 BMW 535i, categorized by component and engine/transmission option, adhering to your specified format.

The 2011 BMW 535i is part of the F10 generation, known for its blend of luxury, performance, and technology. The 535i model features a turbocharged 3.0-liter inline-6 engine (N55). This engine produces 300 horsepower and 300 lb-ft of torque. It was offered with either an 8-speed automatic transmission or a 6-speed manual. Many owners appreciate the 535i for its comfortable ride, spacious interior, and strong acceleration. However, common complaints include the potential for high maintenance costs, electrical gremlins, and some specific engine issues related to the N55 powerplant. Other engine options were available in the 5 Series lineup, but the 535i specifically used the N55 engine.

**Engine**

**N55 3.0L Turbocharged Inline-6**

*   **Oil Leaks (Valve Cover Gasket)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Oil leaking onto the exhaust manifold, causing a burning smell. Visible oil residue around the valve cover. Low oil level warnings.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the valve cover gasket.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **VANOS Solenoid Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rough idling, decreased engine performance, stalling, and fault codes related to the VANOS system.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the faulty VANOS solenoids.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **High Pressure Fuel Pump (HPFP) Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Long cranking times, hesitation during acceleration, engine misfires, and potential stalling. Check engine light.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the high-pressure fuel pump.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1500
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Water Pump Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Overheating, coolant leaks, and potential engine damage.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the water pump and thermostat.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $600 - $1000
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**8-Speed Automatic Transmission**

*   **Mechatronic Sleeve Leak**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Transmission fluid leak near the transmission pan. Slipping or harsh shifting, error messages relating to the transmission on the dashboard.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the mechatronic sleeve and fluid.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1000
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Valve Body Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Harsh shifting, delayed engagement, transmission slipping, and potential failure to shift.
    *   Solution: Replacement or rebuild of the valve body.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1500 - $3000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**6-Speed Manual Transmission**
*   **Clutch Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Slipping clutch, difficulty shifting, unusual noises.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the clutch disc, pressure plate, and throw-out bearing.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1200 - $2500
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Control Arm Bushing Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Clunking noises from the front suspension, especially when going over bumps. Loose or vague steering feel.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the control arm bushings or the entire control arm assembly.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800 per arm
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Adaptive Damper Issues (if equipped with Adaptive Drive)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Uneven ride quality, error messages related to the adaptive suspension system.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the faulty adaptive dampers.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1500 per damper
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Battery Drain Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Dead battery after short periods of inactivity, difficulty starting the car.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis of the parasitic drain and repair of the faulty component. Common culprits include the comfort access system, iDrive system, and faulty modules.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $1000+ (depending on the source of the drain)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **iDrive System Malfunctions**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Freezing, rebooting, or complete failure of the iDrive system.
    *   Solution: Software update, replacement of the iDrive controller, or replacement of the entire iDrive unit.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Tail Light Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Burnt out or malfunctioning tail lights.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the tail light assembly.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500 per assembly
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Door Handle Issues (Comfort Access)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Door handles failing to unlock or lock the car when using the comfort access feature.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the faulty door handle sensor or the entire door handle assembly.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800 per handle
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Sunroof Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Sunroof failing to open or close properly, getting stuck, or making unusual noises.
    *   Solution: Repair or replacement of the sunroof motor, guides, or cables.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1000
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2011 BMW 535i, while a desirable vehicle for its performance and luxury, is prone to a range of issues that can lead to significant repair costs. Engine-related problems, particularly oil leaks and HPFP failure, are common concerns. Transmission issues, especially with the automatic, can also be expensive to address. Electrical gremlins, suspension component wear, and sunroof malfunctions are other potential sources of trouble. Due to the age of the vehicle, many of the parts are nearing the end of their life and will need to be replaced at some point. Potential buyers should be aware of these potential issues and factor in maintenance costs when considering the purchase of a 2011 BMW 535i.
