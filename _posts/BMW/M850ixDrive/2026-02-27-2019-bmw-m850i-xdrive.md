# 2019 BMW M850i xDrive Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2019 BMW M850i xDrive delivers exceptional performance with its twin-turbocharged V8 engine and sophisticated all-wheel-drive system. While formal complaints are sparse for this model year, prospective buyers should carefully inspect high-performance luxury vehicles for cooling system quirks and general electronic complexity.

---

## Engine

### 4.4L Twin-Turbocharged V8 Engine

The 4.4-liter twin-turbocharged V8 engine produces 523 horsepower and 553 lb-ft of torque, offering blistering acceleration. It generally runs strong, though complex cooling and auxiliary systems require attentive maintenance.

**Coolant Odor**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may detect an abnormal coolant odor inside the cabin upon starting the vehicle, even without active warning lights on the dashboard.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified technician inspect the cooling system hoses, reservoir, and connections for minor leaks or seepage.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Sport Automatic Transmission

The 8-speed sport automatic transmission provides rapid, seamless shifts and pairs standard with the xDrive all-wheel-drive system. It is robust and well-matched to the high-output V8 powerplant.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V513000: Fuel System, Other:Delivery:Fuel Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=M850i&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** The high pressure fuel pump may not have been properly attached to the engine, which may cause the fuel pump flange to crack, resulting in a fuel leak. A fuel leak in the presence of an ignition source can increase the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** BMW will notify owners, and dealers will replace the high pressure fuel pumps, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V096000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=M850i&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** When shifting into Reverse, a small portion of the rearview image may be obscured, or the screen may not illuminate. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 111, "Rear Visibility." If the driver relies on only the rearview camera image, the missing or reduced rearview image view.
- **What to do:** BMW will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the rearview camera software and update it if necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V684000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=M850i&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 111, "Rearview Mirrors." The lack of an image in the back-up camera display increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** BMW will notify owners, and dealers will update the back-up camera software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2019 BMW M850i xDrive is a high-performance grand tourer with limited complaint history in early owner reports. Buyers should prioritize a comprehensive pre-purchase inspection focusing on the cooling and electrical systems to ensure long-term reliability.
