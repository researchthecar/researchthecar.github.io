# 2021 BMW 540i Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2021 BMW 540i delivers an impressive blend of luxury, advanced technology, and spirited performance, anchored by its smooth inline-six engine. While owners generally praise its driving dynamics and comfort, prospective buyers should review known owner complaints regarding electrical systems and localized hardware concerns. As with any modern luxury vehicle, checking service history and ensuring outstanding manufacturer campaigns have been addressed is essential for a confident purchase.

---

## Engine

### 3.0L Turbocharged Inline-6 (B58)

The 3.0-liter turbocharged inline-six engine (B58) produces approximately 335 horsepower and 331 lb-ft of torque, offering robust acceleration and strong refinement. It is widely regarded as one of the brand's more reliable modern powertrains, though proper maintenance remains vital.

**Starter and Electrical Warnings**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have reported starter failures and related electrical warning lights, with concerns regarding potential short circuits in the engine bay area.
- **Solution:** Have the vehicle inspected by a qualified technician or dealer to check starter health and verify that any applicable manufacturer service campaigns have been completed.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

The smooth-shifting 8-speed automatic transmission pairs with rear-wheel drive or xDrive all-wheel drive to deliver rapid gear changes and effortless cruising. No widespread, recurring transmission mechanical failures are prominent among owner complaints.

## Steering

### Wheels and Steering Alignment

Steering feel is generally direct and responsive, though large alloy wheels can occasionally be sensitive to road imperfections or minor rim deformations.

**Highway Speed Steering Vibration**
- **Symptoms:** Intermittent steering wheel or vehicle vibration at highway speeds, sometimes attributed to out-of-round factory wheels.
- **Solution:** Inspect wheels for trueness and ensure proper balancing and four-wheel alignment during a test drive.

## Electrical

### Electrical System & Battery Management

Complex vehicle electronics govern most cabin and powertrain functions, requiring thorough diagnostic checks during pre-purchase inspections.

**Starter and Power Distribution Concerns**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have noted notifications regarding electrical system campaigns or remote starter component issues, sometimes experiencing delays in replacement parts.
- **Solution:** Verify via the vehicle identification number (VIN) whether open electrical or starter campaigns apply and ensure authorized dealers have performed necessary updates.

## Exterior

### Sunroof and Glass

Exterior styling and trim are durable, but large glass panels warrant careful visual inspection.

**Glass Breakage**
- **Symptoms:** Isolated reports of panoramic sunroof glass shattering unexpectedly while driving at highway speeds.
- **Solution:** Inspect the sunroof glass and seals closely for any pre-existing stress fractures or damage before finalizing a purchase.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V598000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=540i&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 111, "Rear Visibility." If the driver relies on only the rearview camera image, the reduced rearview image view can increase the risk of a crash or injury.
- **What to do:** BMW will notify owners, and dealers will inspect and, if necessary, program the rearview camera with updated software. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V554000: Seat Belts](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=540i&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** As such, these assemblies fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 208, "Occupant Crash Protection." During a crash, a seat belt retractor that does not function as intended increases the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the seat belt retractors (including the seat belts) and, if necessary, replace the retractors, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V761000: Electrical System:12V/24V/48V Battery](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=540i&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The connection between the 48-Volt battery and the starter-generator may not have been assembled properly, which can cause increased electrical resistance at the connections. Increased electrical resistance can increase the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** BMW will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the connection between the 48-Volt battery and the starter-generator assembly, replacing it if necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V096000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=540i&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** When shifting into Reverse, a small portion of the rearview image may be obscured, or the screen may not illuminate. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 111, "Rear Visibility." If the driver relies on only the rearview camera image, the missing or reduced rearview image view.
- **What to do:** BMW will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the rearview camera software and update it if necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V046000: Power Train:Driveline:Driveshaft](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=BMW&model=540i&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** A broken output shaft can lead to a loss of drive power or a rollaway condition in a parked vehicle (if the parking brake is not engaged), increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** BMW will notify owners, and dealers will replace the output shaft(s), free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2021 BMW 540i is a high-performing luxury sedan backed by a potent powertrain and strong overall road manners. While most systems operate reliably, buyers should carefully verify vehicle history reports, ensure all electrical and starter recalls have been cleared, and perform a thorough pre-purchase inspection focusing on electronics, wheels, and cabin features.
