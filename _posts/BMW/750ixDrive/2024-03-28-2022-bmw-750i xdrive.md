**2022 BMW 750i xDrive**

The 2022 BMW 750i xDrive is a luxury sedan powered by a 4.4-liter twin-turbocharged V8 engine producing 523 horsepower and 553 lb-ft of torque. It comes standard with an 8-speed automatic transmission and all-wheel drive.

Other engine and transmission options available for the 7 Series include:

* 3.0-liter turbocharged inline-6 engine (375 horsepower, 398 lb-ft of torque) with an 8-speed automatic transmission
* 4.0-liter turbocharged V8 engine (600 horsepower, 553 lb-ft of torque) with an 8-speed automatic transmission
* 3.0-liter inline-6 engine with an electric motor (389 horsepower, 443 lb-ft of torque) with an 8-speed automatic transmission
* 3.0-liter inline-6 engine with two electric motors (536 horsepower, 549 lb-ft of torque) with an 8-speed automatic transmission

People like the 750i xDrive for its refined performance, luxurious interior, and advanced technology features. However, some common complaints include its high price, limited rear headroom, and complex infotainment system.

**Engine**

**4.4-liter V8**

**Oil Leaks**
Description/Symptoms: Leaks may occur from the valve cover gaskets, oil filter housing, or turbocharger seals. Symptoms include blue smoke from the exhaust, low oil level on the dipstick, and a burning oil smell.
Solution: Replace the faulty gaskets or seals.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+

**Turbocharger Failure**
Description/Symptoms: Turbos may fail due to excessive wear or oil starvation. Symptoms include loss of power, black smoke from the exhaust, and a grinding or whistling noise.
Solution: Replace the turbochargers.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,500 - $5,000+

**Transmission**

**8-Speed Automatic**

**Shifting Problems**
Description/Symptoms: Transmission may slip, hesitate, or jerk during gear changes.
Solution: Update the transmission control unit software or replace transmission components.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $3,000+

**Recalls:**
* NHTSA Campaign Number: 21V451000 - Electric coolant hose may rupture
* NHTSA Campaign Number: 22V369000 - Side airbags may not deploy properly

**Electrical**

**Battery Drain**

Solution: Update the software or replace the head unit.

**Summary**

The 2022 BMW 750i xDrive is a luxury sedan with good overall reliability. However, it is not without its issues, including oil leaks, turbocharger failure, and electrical problems. The severity and cost of these issues vary depending on the specific problem and the extent of the damage.
