The 2014 BMW 750i xDrive is a luxury sedan offering a blend of performance and comfort. It came equipped with a 4.4L TwinPower Turbo V8 engine producing 445 horsepower and 480 lb-ft of torque, paired with an 8-speed automatic transmission. What people like about the vehicle includes its powerful engine, luxurious interior, and comfortable ride. Common complaints often revolve around the high cost of maintenance, potential for expensive repairs, and the complexity of the vehicle's electronics.

**Engine**

**4.4L TwinPower Turbo V8 (N63TU)**

*   **Oil Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks are common in the N63TU engine. These leaks often originate from valve cover gaskets, oil filter housing gaskets, and turbocharger oil lines. Symptoms include visible oil leaks on the engine or garage floor, burning oil smell, and low oil level warnings.
    *   Solution: Replacing the leaking gaskets or lines is typically required. Sometimes the valve cover itself can develop cracks and need to be replaced.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+ (depending on the location and complexity of the leak).
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Coolant Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaks can occur from various points in the cooling system, including the expansion tank, hoses, and water pump. Overheating, low coolant level warnings, and visible coolant leaks are common symptoms.
    *   Solution: Identifying and replacing the leaking component.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,500+ (depending on the source of the leak).
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Turbocharger Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The N63TU engine has twin turbochargers. Potential issues include boost leaks, failing wastegate actuators, and turbocharger failure. Symptoms include reduced power, engine knocking or pinging, and error codes related to turbocharger performance.
    *   Solution: Repair or replacement of the faulty turbocharger components, including the turbocharger itself.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $5,000+ (depending on the specific issue and if one or both turbos need replacement).
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**8-Speed Automatic (ZF 8HP)**

*   **Mechatronic Unit Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The ZF 8HP transmission can experience mechatronic unit failure. This can cause harsh or erratic shifting, delayed engagement, and transmission malfunction warnings.
    *   Solution: Replacement or rebuilding of the mechatronic unit.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Slipping/Harsh Shifting**
    * Description/Symptoms: Slipping between gears or harsh shifting, particularly at lower speeds, can indicate issues with the transmission's internal components or solenoids.
    * Solution: Fluid flush, replacement of solenoids, or a full transmission rebuild may be necessary.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $5,000+ (depending on the severity and complexity of the repair)
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Air Suspension Compressor Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The 750i xDrive is equipped with air suspension. The air suspension compressor can fail, leading to uneven ride height, suspension malfunction warnings, and a noisy compressor.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the air suspension compressor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Air Strut Leaks**
    * Description/Symptoms: Air struts can develop leaks over time, causing the vehicle to sag on one or more corners, leading to poor ride quality and handling.
    * Solution: Replacement of the leaking air strut(s).
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+ per strut
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Battery Drain**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The 750i xDrive can suffer from excessive battery drain, especially if the vehicle is not driven regularly. This can lead to a dead battery and difficulty starting the vehicle. The issue may be a software error or a control module draining power when it should not be.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis of the cause of the drain (often requires specialized BMW diagnostic tools) and repair or replacement of the faulty component or software update.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $1,000+ (depending on the source of the drain).
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **iDrive System Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The iDrive system can experience glitches, freezing, and software malfunctions.
    *   Solution: Software updates or replacement of the iDrive module.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,500+ (depending on the issue).
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Adaptive Headlight Malfunctions**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The adaptive headlights can experience malfunctions, causing them to not adjust properly or display error messages.
    *   Solution: Repair or replacement of the headlight control module or the headlight assembly itself.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+ (per headlight).
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Sunroof Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The sunroof mechanism can fail, causing it to get stuck, make excessive noise, or leak.
    *   Solution: Repair or replacement of the sunroof motor, tracks, or seals.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2014 BMW 750i xDrive, while a luxurious and powerful vehicle, is prone to several common issues, particularly related to the engine (oil and coolant leaks, turbocharger problems), transmission (mechatronic unit failures, harsh shifting), air suspension, and electrical systems. The severity of these issues can range from minor inconveniences to major repairs. The cost to fix these issues can be significant, making it crucial to have a pre-purchase inspection performed by a qualified BMW mechanic.
