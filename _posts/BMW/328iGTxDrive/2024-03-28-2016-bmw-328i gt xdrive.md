**Introduction:**
The 2016 BMW 328i GT xDrive is a luxury hatchback that offers a combination of style, performance, and practicality. It comes with a standard 2.0L turbocharged inline-4 engine paired with an 8-speed automatic transmission. The 328i GT has a horsepower of 240 hp and 258 lb-ft of torque. It is known for its sleek design, comfortable interior, and responsive handling. However, it has also faced several issues since its release.

**Engine**

**2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4 Engine**

* **Oil Leaks:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Excessive oil leakage from the engine, causing oil spots under the vehicle and low oil levels.
    * Solution: Replace leaking gaskets or seals.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
* **Valve Cover Gasket Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Engine oil leaking from the valve cover gasket, resulting in smoke, oil consumption, and potential engine damage.
    * Solution: Replace the valve cover gasket.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500

**Transmission**

**8-Speed Automatic Transmission:**

* **Shifting Issues:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Rough or delayed shifting, slipping gears, or refusing to shift.
    * Solution: Update transmission software or replace transmission components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $3,000+
* **Torque Converter Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, shuddering, or strange noises during acceleration.
    * Solution: Replace torque converter.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+

**Suspension**

* **Strut Mount Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Clunking noise when driving over bumps, uneven tire wear, or poor handling.
    * Solution: Replace strut mounts.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $700+
* **Rear Suspension Bushings Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Excessive noise and vibration, unstable handling, or difficulty aligning the wheels.
    * Solution: Replace worn bushings.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Rapid battery discharge, even when the vehicle is not in use.
    * Solution: Diagnose and repair electrical system components causing the drain.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
* **Alternator Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Dim headlights, electrical accessories malfunction, or engine stalling.
    * Solution: Replace alternator.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+

**Exterior**

* **Door Lock Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Doors failing to lock or unlock electronically, causing security concerns.
    * Solution: Replace door lock actuators or control modules.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
* **Tailgate Latch Issue:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Tailgate latch failing to open or close securely, leading to safety hazards.
    * Solution: Adjust or replace tailgate latch.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200

**Interior**

* **Infotainment System Malfunction:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Touchscreen unresponsive, software bugs, or display issues.
    * Solution: Update software or replace infotainment unit.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
* **Cruise Control Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Inability to set or maintain cruise control speed, or sudden acceleration/deceleration issues.
    * Solution: Diagnose and repair cruise control system components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $700+

**Recalls:**
* NHTSA Campaign Number: 20V141000: Airbag Control Unit Malfunction
* NHTSA Campaign Number: 21V235000: Fuel Pump Failure

**Summary:**
The 2016 BMW 328i GT xDrive is a capable vehicle, but it has faced several issues that can impact its reliability and ownership experience. The severity of these issues varies, and some can be costly to repair. While the engine and transmission issues can be significant, the majority of the other issues are relatively minor and can often be addressed without significant expense. Owners should be aware of these potential problems and consider them when making a purchase decision or scheduling maintenance.
