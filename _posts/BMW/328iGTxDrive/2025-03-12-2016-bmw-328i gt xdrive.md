The 2016 BMW 328i GT xDrive is a sporty hatchback known for its practicality, engaging driving dynamics, and upscale interior. The most common engine available is the 2.0L turbocharged inline-4 producing 240 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque, paired with an 8-speed automatic transmission. The 335i GT was the other engine option, which offered a 3.0L turbocharged inline-6 producing 300 horsepower and 300 lb-ft of torque. Owners often praise the car's spacious rear seats, cargo capacity, and comfortable ride. Some dislike the styling, which can be polarizing, and the higher price compared to other compact cars.

**Engine**

**2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4 (N20 Engine)**

* **Oil Leaks**
    * Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks can occur from various points, including the valve cover gasket, oil filter housing gasket, and the oil pan gasket. Symptoms include oil drips under the car, a burning oil smell, and low oil levels.
    * Solution: Replacing the faulty gasket or component is the solution.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Timing Chain Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: The N20 engine is known for potential timing chain problems. Symptoms include a rattling noise at startup or during idle, check engine light illumination, and potential engine damage if the chain breaks.
    * Solution: Replacing the timing chain, guides, and tensioner is the fix.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Coolant Leaks**
    * Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaks are common due to failing coolant hoses or the expansion tank. Symptoms include low coolant levels, overheating, and visible leaks.
    * Solution: Replacing the leaking hoses, expansion tank, or other affected components resolves the issue.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**3.0L Turbocharged Inline-6 (N55 Engine)**

*   **Valve Cover Gasket Leak**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks from the valve cover gasket can cause a burning smell and oil drips onto the exhaust manifold.
    *   Solution: Replace the valve cover gasket.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **VANOS Solenoid Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Faulty VANOS solenoids can lead to rough idling, decreased power, and check engine lights.
    *   Solution: Replace the faulty VANOS solenoids.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**8-Speed Automatic (GA8HP45Z)**

*   **Rough Shifting**
    *   Description/Symptoms:  Hesitation or harshness during shifting, particularly between gears 2-3.
    *   Solution:  Software update or replacement of the Mechatronic unit within the transmission.  In some cases, full transmission replacement is needed.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 (software update) - $6,000+ (transmission replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Torque Converter Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Shuddering or vibrations, particularly at lower speeds or during light acceleration. Can progress to slipping and complete loss of power.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the torque converter.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Control Arm Bushing Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms:  Clunking noises from the front suspension, especially when going over bumps or turning. Vague steering and uneven tire wear.
    *   Solution:  Replacement of the affected control arms or just the bushings if possible.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,000+ per side
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Rear Air Suspension Issues (if equipped)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The rear of the car sagging, a compressor running constantly, or a suspension malfunction warning.
    *   Solution: Repair or replacement of the air struts or air compressor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Battery Drain**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Dead battery, especially after the car has been sitting for a few days. Can be caused by a faulty module not entering sleep mode.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis and repair of the parasitic drain, potentially involving replacing a faulty module or wiring repair.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $1,000+ (depending on the source of the drain)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **iDrive System Malfunctions**
    *   Description/Symptoms:  Freezing, rebooting, or complete failure of the iDrive infotainment system.
    *   Solution: Software update, replacement of the iDrive control unit.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 (Software Update) - $1,500+ (Unit Replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Sunroof Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Sunroof becoming stuck, making excessive noise, or failing to close properly.
    *   Solution: Repair or replacement of the sunroof motor, track, or related components.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Seat Heater Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The seat heaters failing to function properly or not heating up at all.
    *   Solution: Replacing the heating element or the seat heater control module.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2016 BMW 328i GT xDrive can be a rewarding car to own, but it's crucial to be aware of potential problems. Engine issues such as oil leaks and timing chain problems can be costly to address. Transmission issues can also range in severity and cost. Electrical glitches can also be frustrating. Addressing these proactively or during pre-purchase inspection can save owners significant expense and headaches in the long run.
