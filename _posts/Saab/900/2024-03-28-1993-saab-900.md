**Introduction to the 1993 Saab 900**

The 1993 Saab 900 is a compact executive hatchback that combines Swedish design aesthetics with a focus on safety and performance. It came with two engine options: a 2.0-liter inline-4 and a 2.3-liter turbocharged inline-4. Both engines could be paired with a 5-speed manual or a 4-speed automatic transmission.

**Engine**

**2.0-liter Inline-4**

* **Head gasket failure:** Blowing head gasket leading to coolant and oil leaks, overheating, and engine damage.
* Solution: Replace head gasket and resurface cylinder head.
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500

**2.3-liter Turbocharged Inline-4**

* **Blown turbocharger:** Failure of the turbocharger resulting in reduced engine power, excessive oil consumption, and smoke from the exhaust.
* Solution: Replace turbocharger and associated components.
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $3,000 - $4,000

**Transmission**

**5-speed Manual**

* **Shift fork wear:** Worn shift forks leading to difficulty shifting gears or getting stuck in gear.
* Solution: Replace shift forks and associated components.
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,200

**4-speed Automatic**

* **Overdrive failure:** Failure of the overdrive unit within the transmission, causing loss of power in higher gears.
* Solution: Rebuild or replace transmission.
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,200 - $2,000

**Suspension**

* **Front strut mounts:** Deteriorated strut mounts causing excessive noise and vibration over bumps.
* Solution: Replace strut mounts.
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400

* **Rear trailing arm bushings:** Worn bushings leading to unstable handling, squeaking noises, and uneven tire wear.
* Solution: Replace trailing arm bushings.
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500

**Electrical**

* **Alternator failure:** Failure of the alternator leading to charging system issues, battery drain, and loss of electrical power.
* Solution: Replace alternator.
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800

* **Electrical gremlins:** Intermittent electrical issues, such as malfunctioning gauges, flickering lights, or power window failure.
* Solution: Inspect and troubleshoot electrical system, trace wires for shorts or loose connections.
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+

**Exterior**

* **Keypad failures:** Failure of the keypad used to unlock the doors, causing difficulty accessing the vehicle.
* Solution: Replace keypad.
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400

* **Rear hatch latch problems:** Faulty latch causing the rear hatch to open unexpectedly or not latch properly.
* Solution: Replace latch and associated components.
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500

**Interior**

* **Plastic dash cracking:** Cracking and deterioration of the dashboard due to sun exposure or age.
* Solution: Replace dashboard or use dash cover.
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800

* **Faulty climate control:** Failure of the climate control system, leading to lack of heating or cooling.
* Solution: Diagnose and repair climate control components, such as the blower motor, control module, or AC compressor.
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+

**Summary**

The 1993 Saab 900 is a stylish and well-engineered vehicle, but it does have some common issues that can be expensive to repair. The most severe and costly issues are related to engine problems, such as head gasket failure or blown turbocharger. Other issues, such as suspension wear and electrical gremlins, are less serious but can still be inconvenient and expensive to fix. Overall, potential owners should be aware of these potential problems and factor them into their purchasing decision.
