**Introduction:**

The 1995 Saab 900 is a compact executive car that was praised for its safety, performance, and unique styling. It was available with a range of engine and transmission options, including a choice between a turbocharged and naturally aspirated 2.3-liter engine, and a choice between a 5-speed manual or 4-speed automatic transmission. While the 900 was well-received, it also had its fair share of issues.

### **Engine**

**2.3-liter Naturally Aspirated Engine**

* **Intake Manifold Gasket Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Coolant or oil leaks from the intake manifold area, accompanied by engine overheating or rough idling.
    * Solution: Replace the intake manifold gasket.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Valve Cover Gasket Leak:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks from under the valve cover, leading to engine oil loss and potential smoke from the engine bay.
    * Solution: Replace the valve cover gasket.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $300
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Head Gasket Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Coolant or oil leaks between the cylinder head and engine block, causing overheating, white smoke from the exhaust, or coolant contamination in the oil.
    * Solution: Replace the head gasket. Major engine work that can be costly.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**2.3-liter Turbocharged Engine**

* **Turbocharger Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, excessive smoke from the exhaust, or loud whining noises from the turbocharger area.
    * Solution: Replace the turbocharger.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Boost Control Valve Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Insufficient or excessive boost pressure, leading to performance issues or potential engine damage.
    * Solution: Replace the boost control valve.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300
    * Recalls: None Found

### **Transmission**

**5-Speed Manual Transmission**

* **Clutch Master Cylinder Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears, spongy clutch pedal feel, or clutch pedal going to the floor.
    * Solution: Replace the clutch master cylinder.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Synchronizer Ring Wear:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Difficulty or grinding noise when shifting gears, especially when cold.
    * Solution: Replace the affected synchronizer rings.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**4-Speed Automatic Transmission**

* **Transmission Fluid Leaks:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Leaking transmission fluid from the pan gasket, seals, or lines, leading to low fluid levels and potential transmission damage.
    * Solution: Repair the leak and replace the lost fluid.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Shift Solenoid Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Irregular or harsh shifting, difficulty shifting into specific gears, or transmission not engaging.
    * Solution: Replace the faulty shift solenoid.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500
    * Recalls: None Found

### **Suspension**

* **Front Suspension Strut Mount Bearing Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Clunking or rattling noises from the front suspension, especially over bumps or rough roads.
    * Solution: Replace the strut mount bearings.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Rear Suspension Trailing Arm Bushings Wear:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Excessive play or clunking noises from the rear suspension, leading to poor handling and alignment issues.
    * Solution: Replace the worn bushings.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500
    * Recalls: None Found

### **Electrical**

* **Battery Drain:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Difficulty starting the car, electrical issues such as flickering lights or malfunctioning gauges.
    * Solution: Identify and fix the source of the parasitic drain, such as a faulty alternator, starter, or electrical component.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the cause
    * Recalls: None Found

### **Exterior**

* **Door Lock Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Inability to lock or unlock the doors using the key or remote.
    * Solution: Replace the door lock actuators.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Sunroof Leaks:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Water leaking into the cabin from the sunroof area.
    * Solution: Inspect and reseal the sunroof gaskets or drain tubes.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300
    * Recalls: None Found

### **Interior**

* **Dashboard Cracks:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Cracks or splitting on the dashboard, especially around the instrument cluster or air vents.
    * Solution: Replace or repair the dashboard.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Seat Belt Buckle Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Inability to latch or unlatch the seat belt properly.
    * Solution: Replace the faulty seat belt buckle.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200
    * Recalls: None Found

### **Summary**

While the 1995 Saab 900 offered a unique combination of performance, safety, and style, it also had its share of issues. Engine problems, such as head gasket failures and turbocharger issues, could be severe and costly. Transmission issues, while less common, could also lead to expensive repairs. Suspension and electrical problems were generally less severe and more straightforward to fix. Exterior and interior issues, such as door lock failures and dashboard cracks, were less concerning but could still affect the overall experience of owning the car. It's important to note that the severity and cost of issues can vary depending on factors such as mileage, maintenance history, and the specific year and model of the vehicle.
