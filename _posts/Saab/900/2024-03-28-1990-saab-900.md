**Introduction:**

The 1990 Saab 900 is a stylish and luxurious sedan that gained popularity for its impressive performance and safety features. It was offered with a variety of engine and transmission options, including a 2.0-liter turbocharged engine with 155 horsepower and 190 pound-feet of torque, paired with a 5-speed manual or 4-speed automatic transmission. Other engine options included a 2.1-liter naturally aspirated engine and a 2.3-liter turbocharged engine.

**Engine**

**2.0-Liter Turbocharged Engine**

* **Blown Head Gasket:** Symptoms include overheating, coolant loss, and white smoke from the exhaust. Solution involves replacing the head gasket and associated components. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $1,500+
* **Cracked Pistons:** Symptoms include misfiring, loss of compression, and excessive oil consumption. Solution requires engine disassembly and replacement of pistons. Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,000+
* **Turbocharger Failure:** Symptoms include a loss of boost pressure, reduced engine performance, and a whistling or grinding noise. Solution involves replacing the turbocharger unit. Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,500+

**2.1-Liter Naturally Aspirated Engine**

* **Vacuum Leak:** Symptoms include rough idle, poor fuel economy, and difficulty starting. Solution involves finding and sealing the vacuum leak. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200
* **Ignition Coil Failure:** Symptoms include misfiring, loss of power, and difficulty starting. Solution involves replacing the ignition coil. Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $250

**Transmission**

**5-Speed Manual Transmission**

* **Clutch Failure:** Symptoms include slipping gears, grinding noises, and difficulty shifting. Solution involves replacing the clutch assembly. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
* **Synchronizer Wear:** Symptoms include difficulty shifting gears, particularly when downshifting. Solution involves replacing the synchronizer rings. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+

**4-Speed Automatic Transmission**

* **Transmission Slippage:** Symptoms include slipping gears, delayed engagement, and loss of power. Solution involves replacing or rebuilding the transmission. Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+
* **Torque Converter Failure:** Symptoms include overheating, shuddering, and a lack of power. Solution involves replacing the torque converter. Estimated Cost to Fix: $600 - $1,200+

**Suspension**

* **Front Ball Joint Wear:** Symptoms include uneven tire wear, steering vibration, and clunking noises. Solution involves replacing the ball joints. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
* **Rear Shock Absorber Failure:** Symptoms include a bouncy ride, poor handling, and increased stopping distance. Solution involves replacing the shock absorbers. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+

**Electrical**

* **Alternator Failure:** Symptoms include dimming lights, battery discharge, and a warning light on the dashboard. Solution involves replacing the alternator. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+
* **Ignition Switch Failure:** Symptoms include difficulty starting the car or an unexpected shut-off. Solution involves replacing the ignition switch. Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300+
* **Window Motor Failure:** Symptoms include inoperable windows. Solution involves replacing the window motor. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+

**Exterior**

* **Headlight Dimming:** Symptoms include reduced visibility at night. Solution involves replacing the bulbs or checking the electrical connections. Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $150+
* **Tail Light Malfunction:** Symptoms include inoperable tail lights. Solution involves replacing the bulbs or checking the electrical connections. Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $150+

**Interior**

* **Climate Control Malfunction:** Symptoms include inoperable air conditioning or heating. Solution involves diagnosing and repairing the faulty component, such as the blower motor, compressor, or control unit. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+
* **Power Window Switch Failure:** Symptoms include inoperable power windows. Solution involves replacing the switch. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+

**Summary:**

The 1990 Saab 900 is a reliable vehicle with impressive performance and features. However, like any car, it is not immune to issues, particularly with the engine and transmission. These issues can be expensive to repair, but proactive maintenance and timely repairs can help extend the vehicle's lifespan and minimize the overall cost of ownership.
