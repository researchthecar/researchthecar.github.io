**Introduction:**

The 1998 Saab 900 is a compact luxury car that comes with a choice of engines and transmissions. The base engine is a 2.0L inline-4 that produces 135 horsepower and 128 lb-ft of torque, paired with a 5-speed manual transmission. Other engine options include a 2.3L turbocharged inline-4 that produces 185 horsepower and 221 lb-ft of torque, and a 2.5L V6 that produces 170 horsepower and 168 lb-ft of torque. The 2.3L turbocharged engine is available with either a 5-speed manual or a 4-speed automatic transmission, while the 2.5L V6 comes only with the 4-speed automatic.

People appreciate the Saab 900's comfortable and well-equipped interior, as well as its responsive handling. However, some common complaints include its high repair costs, limited cargo space, and outdated infotainment system.

**Engine**

**2.0L Inline-4**

* **Head gasket failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaks, overheating, decreased engine performance.
    * Solution: Replace head gasket and potentially other affected components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Intake manifold gasket leak:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Air leak, rough idle, decreased engine performance.
    * Solution: Replace intake manifold gasket.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+

**2.3L Turbocharged Inline-4**

* **Turbocharger failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Loss of boost pressure, decreased engine performance, smoke from exhaust.
    * Solution: Replace or rebuild turbocharger.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,200 - $2,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Wastegate failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Boost pressure issues, decreased engine performance, check engine light.
    * Solution: Replace wastegate actuator or entire turbocharger assembly.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,500+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Ignition cassette failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Misfiring, decreased engine performance, check engine light.
    * Solution: Replace ignition cassette.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $600+
    * Recalls: None Found

**2.5L V6**

* **Intake valve failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Loss of compression, decreased engine performance, check engine light.
    * Solution: Replace intake valves and potentially other affected components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**5-Speed Manual**

* **Clutch failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting, slipping, burning smell.
    * Solution: Replace clutch assembly.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,200+
    * Recalls: None Found

**4-Speed Automatic**

* **Transmission fluid leak:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Leak from transmission pan or seals, low transmission fluid levels.
    * Solution: Replace leaking component and top up transmission fluid.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Shift solenoid failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Delayed or harsh shifting, check engine light.
    * Solution: Replace shift solenoid.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $700+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Front strut mount failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Noise from front suspension, poor handling.
    * Solution: Replace strut mount.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Rear shock absorber failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Bouncy or unstable rear suspension.
    * Solution: Replace rear shock absorbers.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $600+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Alternator failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Battery not charging, dimming lights.
    * Solution: Replace alternator.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Starter motor failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Engine not starting, clicking sound from starter.
    * Solution: Replace starter motor.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Sunroof drain blockage:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Water leaks into cabin when sunroof is closed.
    * Solution: Clear sunroof drains.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: Under $100

**Interior**

* **Dashboard cracking:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Cracks appear on dashboard surface.
    * Solution: Replace or repair the dashboard.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800+

* **Climate control unit failure:**
     * Description/Symptoms: Malfunctioning climate control system, no AC or heat. 
     * Solution: Replace climate control unit. 
     * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800+ 

**Summary:**

The 1998 Saab 900 is a reliable car overall, but it has some common issues that can be expensive to fix. The most serious potential issue is intake valve failure in the 2.5L V6 engine, which can cost thousands of dollars to repair. Other common issues include head gasket failure, turbocharger failure, and transmission fluid leaks. Repair costs for these issues typically range from a few hundred dollars to over $2,000.
