**Introduction:**

The 1991 Saab 900 is a stylish and sporty compact car. It is powered by a 2.0-liter, 16-valve inline-4 engine that produces 135 horsepower and 126 lb-ft of torque. The engine is paired with a 5-speed manual transmission or a 4-speed automatic transmission.

People like the Saab 900 for its performance, handling, and unique styling. However, it has been known to have some reliability issues.

**Engine**

**2.0-liter, 16-valve inline-4**

* **Blown head gasket:** Symptoms include overheating, white smoke from the exhaust, and loss of coolant. Solution: Replace the head gasket. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
* **Oil leaks:** Symptoms include oil stains on the ground under the engine, low oil levels, and burning oil. Solution: Replace the leaking seals or gaskets. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,000
* **Timing belt failure:** Symptoms include loss of engine power, a loud bang or snap from the engine, and bent valves. Solution: Replace the timing belt immediately. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+

**Transmission**

**5-speed manual**

* **Clutch failure:** Symptoms include difficulty shifting gears, slipping gears, and a burning smell from the clutch. Solution: Replace the clutch. Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,500
* **Transmission noise:** Symptoms include grinding or whining noises when shifting gears. Solution: May require rebuilding or replacing the transmission. Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,000+

**4-speed automatic**

* **Transmission slipping:** Symptoms include delayed or erratic shifting, loss of power, and shuddering. Solution: May require rebuilding or replacing the transmission. Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+

**Suspension**

* **Front suspension wear:** Symptoms include loose or rattling suspension components, poor handling, and uneven tire wear. Solution: Replace the worn components. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500
* **Rear suspension wear:** Symptoms include sagging rear end, poor handling, and uneven tire wear. Solution: Replace the worn components. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500

**Electrical**

* **Ignition switch failure:** Symptoms include difficulty starting the vehicle or the vehicle stalling while driving. Solution: Replace the ignition switch. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
* **Battery drain:** Symptoms include a dead battery after the vehicle has been sitting for a period of time. Solution: Find and fix the source of the drain. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
* **Alternator failure:** Symptoms include dim lights, loss of electrical power, or a dead battery. Solution: Replace the alternator. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000

**Exterior**

* **Rust:** Symptoms include bubbles in the paint, rust spots, and holes in the body panels. Solution: Repair or replace the affected panels. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $5,000+
* **Plastic trim fading or cracking:** Symptoms include faded or cracked plastic trim on the exterior of the vehicle. Solution: Replace the affected trim pieces. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500

**Interior**

* **Loose or rattling interior panels:** Symptoms include loose or rattling door panels, dashboard panels, or seats. Solution: Tighten or replace the affected panels or components. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500
* **Electrical switch failures:** Symptoms include inoperable electrical switches for windows, lights, or other accessories. Solution: Replace the faulty switch. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300

**Summary:**

The 1991 Saab 900 is a fun-to-drive car that has some well-known reliability issues. The engine and transmission are the most likely components to fail, and these repairs can be expensive. However, many of the other issues are relatively minor and can be fixed relatively cheaply.
