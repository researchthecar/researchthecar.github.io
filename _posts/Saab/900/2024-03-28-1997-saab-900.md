**Introduction:**

The 1997 Saab 900 is a compact executive car renowned for its quirky design, turbocharged performance, and safety features. It was available with a range of engine and transmission options, including:

* 2.0L turbocharged 4-cylinder (150 hp, 185 lb-ft torque) with 5-speed manual or 4-speed automatic transmission
* 2.3L turbocharged 4-cylinder (170 hp, 210 lb-ft torque) with 5-speed manual or 4-speed automatic transmission

**Engine**

* **2.0L Turbocharged 4-Cylinder**
    * **Blown Head Gasket:**
        * Description/Symptoms: Overheating, white smoke from exhaust, coolant leaks
        * Solution: Replace head gasket
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
        * Recalls: None Found
    * **Turbocharger Failure:**
        * Description/Symptoms: Reduced power, oil leaks, whistling noises
        * Solution: Replace turbocharger
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
        * Recalls: None Found
* **2.3L Turbocharged 4-Cylinder**
    * **Boost Leak:**
        * Description/Symptoms: Reduced power, check engine light
        * Solution: Repair or replace boost hoses, intercooler, or wastegate
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

* **5-Speed Manual**
    * **Difficulty Shifting Gears:**
        * Description/Symptoms: Grating noises, hard to engage gears
        * Solution: Replace transmission fluid, adjust or replace clutch
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
        * Recalls: None Found
* **4-Speed Automatic**
    * **Slipping Gears:**
        * Description/Symptoms: Transmission lurches or delays when shifting
        * Solution: Replace or rebuild transmission
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,000+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **No Issues Found**

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Dead battery, difficulty starting
    * Solution: Replace battery, check for parasitic draw
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Alternator Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Battery not charging, dimming lights
    * Solution: Replace alternator
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Door Lock Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Inability to lock or unlock doors
    * Solution: Replace door lock actuator
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Dashboard Cracking:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Cracks on the dashboard surface
    * Solution: Replace dashboard
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Power Window Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Inability to operate power windows
    * Solution: Replace window switch or motor
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

The 1997 Saab 900 is a reliable and quirky car that has stood the test of time. However, like any vehicle, it can experience certain issues that range from minor to major. The most common issues include engine head gasket failure, turbocharger problems, transmission slippage, and electrical problems like battery drain and alternator failure. While these issues are not always cheap to fix, they are generally not catastrophic and can be managed with proper maintenance and repairs.
