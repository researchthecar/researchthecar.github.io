**Introduction**

The 1992 Saab 900 is a compact executive car that was available in sedan, convertible, and hatchback body styles. It offered a range of engine and transmission options, including:

* **Engine Options:**
    * 2.0L turbocharged inline-4 (165 hp, 188 lb-ft torque)
    * 2.3L turbocharged inline-4 (185 hp, 221 lb-ft torque)
* **Transmission Options:**
    * 5-speed manual
    * 4-speed automatic

The Saab 900 was praised for its performance, handling, and distinctive design. However, it also had several common issues that owners experienced.

**Engine**
- **2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4**
    - **Head Gasket Failure:** Blown head gaskets were a common issue with the 2.0L turbocharged inline-4 engine. Symptoms included overheating, coolant leaks, and white smoke from the exhaust. Solution: Replacing the head gaskets. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,200 - $2,000+.
    - **Turbocharger Failure:** Turbocharger failure was another common problem on the 2.0L turbocharged inline-4. Symptoms included reduced power, increased oil consumption, and whistling noises. Solution: Replacing the turbocharger. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+.
- **2.3L Turbocharged Inline-4**
    - **Oil Leaks:** Oil leaks were a common problem with the 2.3L turbocharged inline-4 engine. Symptoms included visible oil leaks from the engine seals, gaskets, and turbocharger. Solution: Replacing the faulty seals or gaskets. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+.
    - **Ignition Module Failure:** Ignition module failure could cause the 2.3L turbocharged inline-4 to experience misfiring, rough idling, and poor performance. Solution: Replacing the ignition module. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+.

**Transmission**
- **5-speed Manual**
    - **Gearbox Noise:** A noisy gearbox was a common issue with the 5-speed manual transmission. Symptoms included grinding noises, whining, or buzzing sounds while shifting gears. Solution: Replacing the gearbox or faulty components within the gearbox. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+.
- **4-speed Automatic**
    - **Transmission Slippage:** Transmission slippage was a common issue with the 4-speed automatic transmission. Symptoms included the transmission failing to engage gears, slipping between gears, or jerking during acceleration. Solution: Overhauling or replacing the transmission. Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,500+.
    - **Torque Converter Failure:** Torque converter failure could cause the 4-speed automatic transmission to experience shuddering, slippage, or overheating. Solution: Replacing the torque converter. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,200 - $2,000+.

**Suspension**
    - **Front End Vibration:** Excessive vibration from the front end while driving could indicate worn or damaged suspension components, such as ball joints or tie rods. Solution: Inspecting and replacing the faulty suspension components. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600+.

**Electrical**
    - **Alternator Failure:** Alternator failure was a common issue on the Saab 900. Symptoms included dim headlights, flickering gauges, and battery discharge. Solution: Replacing the alternator. Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800+.
    - **Battery Drain:** Excessive battery drain could be caused by faulty electrical components or a parasitic draw. Solution: Identifying and repairing the source of the drain. Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $500+.

**Exterior**
    - **Plastic Trim Fading:** The exterior plastic trim on the Saab 900 could fade and become brittle over time due to sun exposure. Solution: Replacing the faded trim pieces. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+.

**Interior**
    - **Climate Control Malfunctions:** The climate control system on the Saab 900 could experience various malfunctions, including blower motor failure, temperature sensor issues, or faulty blend doors. Solution: Diagnosing and repairing the faulty components. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+.
    - **Interior Door Lock Failure:** The interior door locks on the Saab 900 could fail, making it difficult to lock or unlock the doors. Solution: Replacing the faulty door lock mechanisms. Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300+.

**Summary**

The 1992 Saab 900 had several common issues that owners experienced. These issues ranged from minor inconveniences to major repairs, with costs varying widely depending on the severity of the problem. Potential buyers should be aware of these issues and factor them into their decision-making process.
