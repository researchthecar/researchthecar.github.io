The Saab 9000 is a mid-size luxury car that was produced from 1984 to 1998. The 1995 model year is notable for being the last year of production for the Saab 9000 in the United States. The 9000 was characterized by its distinctive hatchback design and was available with a range of engine and transmission options. Various versions of the Saab 9000 were known for their powerful turbocharged engines, spacious interiors, and strong safety features. However, like any vehicle, the 1995 Saab 9000 has its share of potential issues that potential owners should be aware of.

**Engine**

**2.3 L B234 (16-valve)**
* **Cylinder Head Warping:**
  * Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaks, overheating, loss of power
  * Solution: Replace cylinder head or have it resurfaced
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,200 - $2,500+
  * Recalls: None Found
* **Oil Pressure Loss:**
  * Description/Symptoms: Oil pressure warning light illuminates, engine noise, engine damage
  * Solution: Replace oil pump, oil pan, or other affected components
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
  * Recalls: None Found
* **Intake Manifold Gasket Failure:**
  * Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaks, overheating, loss of power
  * Solution: Replace intake manifold gasket
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
  * Recalls: None Found

**2.0 L B204 (16-valve)**
* **Crankshaft Sensor Failure:**
  * Description/Symptoms: No start, engine stalls, misfires
  * Solution: Replace crankshaft sensor
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
  * Recalls: None Found
* **Exhaust Manifold Cracking:**
  * Description/Symptoms: Exhaust leaks, engine noise
  * Solution: Replace exhaust manifold
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800+
  * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**5-speed Manual**
* **Clutch Master Cylinder Failure:**
  * Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears, clutch pedal feels spongy
  * Solution: Replace clutch master cylinder
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
  * Recalls: None Found
* **Synchronizer Failure:**
  * Description/Symptoms: Grating noise when shifting gears, difficulty shifting into gear
  * Solution: Replace synchronizers
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,500+
  * Recalls: None Found

**4-speed Automatic (AW30)**
* **Torque Converter Failure:**
  * Description/Symptoms: Slipping gears, loss of power, overheating
  * Solution: Replace torque converter
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $1,800+
  * Recalls: None Found
* **Transmission Fluid Leaks:**
  * Description/Symptoms: Transmission fluid leaks, low fluid levels
  * Solution: Replace seals or gaskets
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600+
  * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Front Control Arm Bushing Wear:**
  * Description/Symptoms: Clunking noises, poor handling
  * Solution: Replace control arm bushings
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
  * Recalls: None Found
* **Rear Shock Absorber Failure:**
  * Description/Symptoms: Bouncing, poor handling, loss of stability
  * Solution: Replace shock absorbers
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800+
  * Recalls: None Found
* **Sway Bar Link Wear:**
  * Description/Symptoms: Clunking noises, poor handling
  * Solution: Replace sway bar links
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
  * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Alternator Failure:**
  * Description/Symptoms: Battery not charging, electrical system malfunction
  * Solution: Replace alternator
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800+
  * Recalls: None Found
* **Battery Drain:**
  * Description/Symptoms: Battery drains quickly, difficulty starting
  * Solution: Inspect for parasitic draw, test battery and replace if needed
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600+
  * Recalls: None Found
* **Starter Failure:**
  * Description/Symptoms: No start, clicking noise when turning key
  * Solution: Replace starter
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
  * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Sunroof Leaks:**
  * Description/Symptoms: Water leaks into the cabin when it rains
  * Solution: Replace sunroof seals or drains
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
  * Recalls: None Found
* **Headlight Failure:**
  * Description/Symptoms: Headlights do not turn on or are dim
  * Solution: Replace headlight bulbs or check wiring
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+
  * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Dashboard Cracking:**
  * Description/Symptoms: Cracks on the dashboard
  * Solution: Replace or repair dashboard
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
  * Recalls: None Found
* **Seat Heaters Not Working:**
  * Description/Symptoms: Seat heaters do not heat up
  * Solution: Replace seat heater elements or check wiring
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
  * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

Overall, the 1995 Saab 9000 is a vehicle that is known for its performance, style, and safety features. However, potential owners should be aware of the various issues that can arise, particularly with regard to the engine and transmission components. The severity of the issues can range from minor inconveniences to more serious problems that can affect the reliability and performance of the vehicle. The cost of repairs can also be significant, especially for major component failures. It is important for potential owners to research these issues thoroughly and factor in the potential costs of repairs when considering the purchase of a 1995 Saab 9000.
