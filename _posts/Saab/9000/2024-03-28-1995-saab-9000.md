**Introduction**

The 1995 Saab 9000 is a mid-size sedan with a reputation for safety, comfort, and performance. It offers various engine and transmission options, including:

* 2.0L 4-cylinder with 5-speed manual or 4-speed automatic (133 hp / 144 lb-ft torque)
* 2.3L 4-cylinder with 5-speed manual or 4-speed automatic (150 hp / 156 lb-ft torque)
* 2.5L V6 with 4-speed automatic (170 hp / 180 lb-ft torque)

Enthusiasts appreciate the 9000's turbocharged engine options for their increased power and handling. However, certain issues have been reported with specific components.

**Engine**

**2.0L 4-cylinder with 5-speed manual**

* **Oil Leak from Crankshaft Seal:** Oil leakage can occur from a faulty crankshaft seal, resulting in low oil levels and potential engine damage. Solution: Replace the crankshaft seal. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800
* **Timing Chain Tensioner Failure:** The timing chain tensioner may fail, causing the timing chain to skip and damage the engine's valves. Solution: Replace the timing chain tensioner and inspect the timing chain for damage. Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1200

**2.3L 4-cylinder with 5-speed manual**

* **Intake Manifold Gasket Failure:** The intake manifold gasket can fail, causing vacuum leaks and rough engine idle. Solution: Replace the intake manifold gasket. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400

**2.5L V6 with 4-speed automatic**

* **Transmission Fluid Leaks:** The transmission fluid lines or cooler can leak, leading to low transmission fluid levels and potential transmission damage. Solution: Inspect and repair the leaking component. Estimated Cost to Fix: $250 - $500
* **Throttle Body Position Sensor Failure:** The throttle body position sensor may fail, resulting in erratic engine performance, stalling, or difficulty starting. Solution: Replace the throttle body position sensor. Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300
* **Mass Airflow Sensor Failure:** The mass airflow sensor may fail, leading to incorrect air-fuel mixture and performance issues. Solution: Replace the mass airflow sensor. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400

**Transmission**

**5-speed manual**

* **Clutch Master Cylinder Leak:** The clutch master cylinder can leak fluid, causing difficulty shifting gears. Solution: Replace the clutch master cylinder. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
* **Transmission Fluid Leak:** The transmission fluid seals or lines can develop leaks, resulting in low fluid levels and potential transmission damage. Solution: Inspect and repair the leaking component. Estimated Cost to Fix: $250 - $500

**4-speed automatic**

* **Transmission Solenoid Failure:** The transmission solenoids may fail, causing shifting issues or transmission slippage. Solution: Replace the faulty solenoid. Estimated Cost to Fix: $250 - $500
* **Torque Converter Failure:** The torque converter may fail, resulting in transmission shudder, slipping, or overheating. Solution: Replace the torque converter. Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1200

**Suspension**

* **Rear Suspension Spring Failure:** The rear suspension springs can prematurely fail, causing a loss of ride height and performance. Solution: Replace the rear suspension springs. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800

**Electrical**

* **Ignition Coil Failure:** The ignition coil may fail, causing misfires and difficulty starting. Solution: Replace the ignition coil. Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300
* **Alternator Failure:** The alternator may fail, resulting in battery drain and electrical system issues. Solution: Replace the alternator. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800
* **Electrical Shorts:** Electrical shorts can occur due to faulty wiring or components, causing various electrical problems. Solution: Diagnose and repair the electrical short. Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the location of the short

**Exterior**

* **Headlight Switch Failure:** The headlight switch may fail, resulting in intermittent or inoperative headlights. Solution: Replace the headlight switch. Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300

**Interior**

* **Window Regulator Failure:** The window regulators may fail, causing difficulty in raising or lowering the windows. Solution: Replace the faulty window regulator. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
* **Dashboard Pixelation:** The dashboard pixels may fail or dim over time, affecting visibility. Solution: Repair or replace the affected pixels. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800

**Summary**

The 1995 Saab 9000 generally has a good reliability rating, but it is not without its potential issues. Engine and transmission problems can be expensive to fix, but most other issues are relatively minor and affordable. Regular maintenance and timely repairs are essential for maximizing the vehicle's longevity and performance.
