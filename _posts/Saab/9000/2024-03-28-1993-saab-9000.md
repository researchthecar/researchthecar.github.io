**Introduction**

The 1993 Saab 9000 is a mid-size luxury sedan that was praised for its innovative design, handling, and safety features. It was offered with a range of engine and transmission options, including:

- 2.0L inline-4, 133 hp, 128 lb-ft (standard)
- 2.3L inline-4, 155 hp, 149 lb-ft (optional)
- 2.3L V6, 172 hp, 173 lb-ft (optional)
- 3.0L V6, 210 hp, 198 lb-ft (optional)

- 5-speed manual transmission
- 4-speed automatic transmission
- 5-speed automatic transmission (with 3.0L V6 only)

People favored the Saab 9000 for its comfortable ride, spacious interior, and unique styling. However, it also had some reported issues.

**Engine**

**2.0L inline-4**

- **Intake Manifold Gasket Failure**
    - Description/Symptoms: Leaking coolant or oil, overheating engine
    - Solution: Replace the intake manifold gasket
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500
    - Recalls: None Found
- **Crankshaft Position Sensor Failure**
    - Description/Symptoms: Stalling, rough idling, difficulty starting
    - Solution: Replace the crankshaft position sensor
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
    - Recalls: None Found

**2.3L inline-4**

- **Oil Pressure Sensor Failure**
    - Description/Symptoms: Low oil pressure warning light, engine damage
    - Solution: Replace the oil pressure sensor
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300
    - Recalls: None Found
- **Ignition Coil Failure**
    - Description/Symptoms: Misfiring, loss of power, engine stalling
    - Solution: Replace the ignition coil
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $300
    - Recalls: None Found

**2.3L V6**

- **Timing Belt Failure**
    - Description/Symptoms: Engine stalls, catastrophic engine damage
    - Solution: Replace the timing belt
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $600 - $1,000+
    - Recalls: None Found
- **Water Pump Failure**
    - Description/Symptoms: Coolant leak, overheating engine
    - Solution: Replace the water pump
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500
    - Recalls: None Found

**3.0L V6**

- **Throttle Position Sensor Failure**
    - Description/Symptoms: Rough idling, hesitation on acceleration
    - Solution: Replace the throttle position sensor
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $300
    - Recalls: None Found
- **Transmission Control Module Failure**
    - Description/Symptoms: Transmission shifts erratically, transmission doesn't engage
    - Solution: Replace the transmission control module
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
    - Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**5-speed Manual Transmission**

- **Shift Linkage Failure**
    - Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears, grinding noises
    - Solution: Replace the shift linkage
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500
    - Recalls: None Found

**4-speed Automatic Transmission**

- **Transmission Fluid Leak**
    - Description/Symptoms: Leaking transmission fluid, slipping gears
    - Solution: Fix the leak (e.g., replace seals, hoses)
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
    - Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

- **Control Arm Bushing Failure**
    - Description/Symptoms: Noises over bumps, reduced handling
    - Solution: Replace the control arm bushings
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500
    - Recalls: None Found
- **Strut Assembly Failure**
    - Description/Symptoms: Rough ride, poor handling, uneven tire wear
    - Solution: Replace the strut assembly
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $600+
    - Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

- **Alternator Failure**
    - Description/Symptoms: Battery not charging, electrical system issues
    - Solution: Replace the alternator
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800
    - Recalls: None Found
- **Battery Drain Issue**
    - Description/Symptoms: Battery loses charge quickly
    - Solution: Determine the cause of the drain and fix it (e.g., faulty component, parasitic draw)
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the cause
    - Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

- **Rust on Body Panels**
    - Description/Symptoms: Visible rust on sheet metal
    - Solution: Repair rust damage or replace panels
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on severity
    - Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

- **Climate Control Malfunction**
    - Description/Symptoms: Heating or cooling system not functioning properly
    - Solution: Repair or replace climate control components
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $700+
    - Recalls: None Found
- **Instrument Cluster Failure**
    - Description/Symptoms: Gauges or warning lights not working
    - Solution: Replace the instrument cluster
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $600+
    - Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 1993 Saab 9000 had some notable issues, particularly with its engines and transmissions. The most severe and costly issues include timing belt failure (3.0L V6), transmission control module failure (3.0L V6), and alternator failure. However, many of the other issues can be relatively inexpensive to fix. Overall, the Saab 9000 is still considered a reliable and enjoyable vehicle, although it is recommended to be aware of its potential issues before purchasing one.
