The 1993 Saab 9000 is a mid-size luxury sedan that was available with a variety of engine and transmission options. The most popular engine option was the 2.3-liter turbocharged inline-four, which produced 150 horsepower and 188 lb-ft of torque. Other engine options included a 2.0-liter turbocharged inline-four, a 2.3-liter naturally aspirated inline-four, and a 3.0-liter V6. The 9000 was also available with either a five-speed manual transmission or a four-speed automatic transmission.

Despite its overall reliability, the 1993 Saab 9000 has several common issues that owners should be aware of.

**Engine**

**2.3-liter Turbocharged Inline-Four**
- **Intake Manifold Gasket Failure:** This issue is characterized by a loss of power, poor fuel economy, and an increase in emissions. The intake manifold gasket can fail due to heat and age, causing a vacuum leak. The solution is to replace the intake manifold gasket. The estimated cost of repair is $500 - $700+.
- **Turbocharger Failure:** Turbocharger failure can lead to a loss of power, increased oil consumption, and blue or black smoke from the exhaust. The turbocharger can fail due to a variety of reasons, such as excessive heat, lack of lubrication, or foreign object damage. The solution is to replace the turbocharger. The estimated cost of repair is $1,000 - $2,000+.

**2.0-liter Turbocharged Inline-Four**
- **No Issues Found**

**2.3-liter Naturally Aspirated Inline-Four**
- **No Issues Found**

**3.0-liter V6**
- **Throttle Body Failure:** This issue can cause the engine to stall, idle erratically, or hesitate when accelerating. The throttle body is responsible for controlling the amount of air that enters the engine. The solution is to replace the throttle body. The estimated cost of repair is $300 - $500+.

**Transmission**

**Five-Speed Manual Transmission**
- **No Issues Found**

**Four-Speed Automatic Transmission**
- **Transmission Slipping:** This issue can cause the transmission to slip out of gear, especially when shifting under load. The transmission can slip due to worn clutch plates, a faulty torque converter, or a lack of transmission fluid. The solution is to repair or replace the transmission. The estimated cost of repair is $1,500 - $3,000+.

**Suspension**

- **Strut Mount Failure:** This issue can cause a clunking or rattling noise from the front suspension. The strut mount is responsible for connecting the strut to the vehicle body. The solution is to replace the strut mount. The estimated cost of repair is $200 - $400+ per side.

**Electrical**

- **Central Locking Failure:** This issue can cause the central locking system to malfunction, making it difficult to lock or unlock the doors. The central locking system can fail due to a variety of reasons, such as a faulty door lock actuator, a blown fuse, or a problem with the wiring harness. The solution is to diagnose and repair the fault. The estimated cost of repair varies depending on the cause of the failure.
- **Alternator Failure:** This issue can cause the battery to discharge and the vehicle to stall. The alternator is responsible for generating electricity to power the vehicle's electrical systems. The solution is to replace the alternator. The estimated cost of repair is $500 - $800+.

**Exterior**

- **Body Rust:** The 1993 Saab 9000 is prone to rust, especially in areas where salt is used on the roads. Rust can occur on the fenders, doors, trunk lid, and other body panels. The solution is to treat the rust and prevent further damage. The estimated cost of repair varies depending on the extent of the rust damage.

**Interior**

- **Dashboard Cracking:** The dashboard of the 1993 Saab 9000 is prone to cracking, especially in areas that are exposed to sunlight. The solution is to replace the dashboard or repair the cracks. The estimated cost of repair varies depending on the severity of the cracks.
- **Climate Control Failure:** The climate control system of the 1993 Saab 9000 is prone to failure, which can cause the air conditioning or heating system to malfunction. The solution is to diagnose and repair the fault. The estimated cost of repair varies depending on the cause of the failure.

**Recalls**

- NHTSA Campaign Number: 93V158000 - Power Window Master Switch May Overheat and Cause Fire
- NHTSA Campaign Number: 92V185000 - Fuel Line May Leak

**Summary**

The 1993 Saab 9000 is a generally reliable vehicle, but it does have some common issues that owners should be aware of. These issues can vary in severity and cost, but they are generally not major problems. Overall, the 9000 is a good choice for drivers who are looking for a mid-size luxury sedan that is both comfortable and reliable.
