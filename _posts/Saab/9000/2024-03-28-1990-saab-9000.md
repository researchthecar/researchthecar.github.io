**Introduction:**

The 1990 Saab 9000 is a mid-size luxury sedan that was praised for its advanced engineering, safety features, and comfortable ride. It was powered by a 2.3-liter turbocharged inline-4 engine that produced 175 horsepower and 188 lb-ft of torque. The sole transmission option was a 5-speed manual.

Despite its strengths, the 9000 was not without its problems. Here's a comprehensive list of common issues reported by owners:

**Engine**

* **2.3-liter Turbocharged Inline-4**

    * **Head Gasket Failure:** Blown head gaskets can occur, causing coolant and oil leaks, white smoke from the exhaust, and engine overheating. Solution: Replace the head gasket. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
    * **Timing Belt Failure:** Failure of the timing belt can lead to catastrophic engine damage. Solution: Replace the timing belt and associated components at recommended intervals. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $900+
    * **Turbocharger Failure:** The turbocharger can fail, resulting in reduced power, smoke from the exhaust, or whining noises. Solution: Replace or rebuild the turbocharger. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+

**Transmission**

* **5-Speed Manual**

    * **Clutch Failure:** The clutch can wear out over time, leading to slipping, difficulty shifting, or a burning smell. Solution: Replace the clutch and pressure plate. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+

**Suspension**

* **Rear Axle Bushing Failure:** Worn rear axle bushings can cause clunking noises, wandering, or unstable handling. Solution: Replace the rear axle bushings. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
* **Front Lower Control Arm Failure:** The front lower control arms can fail, leading to vibrations, noise, or uneven tire wear. Solution: Replace the control arms. Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $600+

**Electrical**

* **Alternator Failure:** The alternator can fail, resulting in loss of power, charging system warning light, or dead battery. Solution: Replace the alternator. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
* **Ignition Switch Failure:** Failure of the ignition switch can prevent the vehicle from starting. Solution: Replace the ignition switch. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
* **Electrical Gremlins:** Various electrical problems can occur, such as flickering lights, power window failures, or malfunctioning dashboard instruments. Solution: Diagnose and repair the specific electrical issue. Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies widely

**Exterior**

* **Windshield Wiper Motor Failure:** The windshield wiper motor can fail, causing the wipers to malfunction. Solution: Replace the wiper motor. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
* **Broken Antenna Mast:** The antenna mast can break or retract, affecting radio reception. Solution: Replace the antenna mast. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+

**Interior**

* **HVAC Blower Motor Failure:** The HVAC blower motor can fail, resulting in a loss of airflow from the vents. Solution: Replace the blower motor. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
* **Heater Core Leak:** The heater core can leak, causing coolant to enter the passenger compartment and fog up the windows. Solution: Replace the heater core. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
* **Faulty Window Regulators:** The power window regulators can fail, causing the windows to get stuck or operate erratically. Solution: Replace the window regulators. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+ per window

**Other**

* **Brake Master Cylinder Failure:** The brake master cylinder can fail, resulting in a loss of braking pressure. Solution: Replace the master cylinder. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
* **Fuel Pump Failure:** The fuel pump can fail, causing the engine to stall or run erratically. Solution: Replace the fuel pump. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+

**Summary:**

While the Saab 9000 was a well-engineered vehicle, it was not immune to issues. Some of the more serious problems, such as head gasket failure or timing belt failure, can be costly to repair. However, many of the issues are relatively minor and affordable to fix. With proper maintenance and care, the 9000 can still be a reliable and enjoyable vehicle to own.
