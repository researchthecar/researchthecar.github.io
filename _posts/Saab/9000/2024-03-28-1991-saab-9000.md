**Introduction:**
The 1991 Saab 9000 is a mid-size luxury sedan known for its safety features, comfortable ride, and spacious interior. It was powered by a 2.3L inline-4 engine paired with a 5-speed manual or 4-speed automatic transmission. The 9000 was praised for its handling, performance, and safety features, but some owners have reported various issues over the years.

**Engine**
* **2.3L Inline-4 Engine with Manual Transmission:**
    * **Blown Head Gasket:**
        * Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaks, overheating, white smoke from exhaust.
        * Solution: Replace head gasket, bolts, and head if necessary.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
    * **Oil Leaks:**
        * Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks from valve cover gasket, oil pan gasket, or crankshaft seal.
        * Solution: Replace leaking component.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
* **2.3L Inline-4 Engine with Automatic Transmission:**
    * **Transmission Slipping:**
        * Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears, loss of power.
        * Solution: Rebuild or replace transmission.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,000+
    * **Solenoid Failure:**
        * Description/Symptoms: Transmission shifts erratically or refuses to shift.
        * Solution: Replace solenoid.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+

**Suspension**
* **Front Control Arm Bushings:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Clunking or rattling noise when driving over bumps, uneven tire wear.
    * Solution: Replace control arm bushings.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+
* **Rear Subframe Mounts:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Vibration or noise when accelerating or braking, uneven tire wear.
    * Solution: Replace subframe mounts.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+

**Electrical**
* **Alternator Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Dimming lights, difficulty starting the engine, charging light on the dashboard.
    * Solution: Replace alternator.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
* **Battery Drain:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Battery dies after sitting overnight.
    * Solution: Test electrical components for shorts or excessive draw, replace faulty component.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+

**Exterior**
* **Rust:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Corrosion on body panels, particularly around wheel wells and underbody.
    * Solution: Repair or replace rusted components, apply rust-resistant coating.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on severity

**Interior**
* **Dashboard Cracking:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Cracks in the plastic dashboard, particularly around the glove box and radio.
    * Solution: Replace dashboard or repair cracks.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500

**Summary:**
While the Saab 9000 has its strengths, it is prone to several common issues that can be costly to repair. Engine problems, such as head gasket failure and oil leaks, are prevalent. Transmission issues, particularly in models with automatic transmissions, can also be expensive to fix. Suspension components, like control arm bushings and subframe mounts, often need replacement over time. Electrical issues, such as alternator failure and battery drain, are also common. Rust can be a problem on older models, while interior issues like dashboard cracking can detract from the vehicle's appearance.
