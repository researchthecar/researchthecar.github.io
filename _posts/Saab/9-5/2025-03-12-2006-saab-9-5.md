The 2006 Saab 9-5 is a mid-size luxury car that was known for its distinctive styling, comfortable ride, and turbocharged engines. Engine options included a 2.3L Turbocharged Inline-4 producing 260 hp and 258 lb-ft of torque. The available transmissions were either a 5-speed manual or a 5-speed automatic. Owners generally liked the 9-5 for its unique character and strong performance, while common dislikes included reliability issues and relatively high maintenance costs.

**Engine**

**2.3L Turbocharged Inline-4**
*   **Sludge Buildup**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Lack of regular oil changes and low-quality oil can lead to sludge buildup in the engine, which can restrict oil flow and cause engine damage. Symptoms include engine knocking, oil pressure light illumination, and eventual engine failure.
    *   Solution: Regular oil changes with high-quality synthetic oil, cleaning the oil pan and oil pickup screen, or engine flush. In severe cases, engine replacement may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 (oil change) - $4000+ (engine replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Crank Position Sensor Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The crank position sensor can fail, causing the engine to stall, not start, or run erratically.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the crank position sensor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Direct Ignition Cassette (DIC) Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Misfires, rough running, and difficulty starting are common symptoms. The check engine light will often illuminate.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the DIC unit.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**5-Speed Automatic**
*   **Valve Body Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Harsh shifting, slipping between gears, or failure to shift.
    *   Solution: Replacement or repair of the valve body.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1500
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Torque Converter Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Shuddering, stalling, or difficulty engaging gears.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the torque converter.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $2000
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Rear Springs Sagging/Breaking**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rear of the car sitting low, especially when loaded. Can lead to poor handling and a rough ride.
    *   Solution: Replacement of rear springs.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Control Arm Bushings Wear**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Clunking noises from the suspension, especially over bumps, and vague steering.
    *   Solution: Replacement of worn control arm bushings or the entire control arm.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $400 per control arm.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **SID (Saab Information Display) Pixel Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Pixels on the SID display fade or disappear, making it difficult to read information.
    *   Solution: Replacement or repair of the SID unit.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $400
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **ABS Module Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: ABS light is on, ABS not functioning, possible brake performance issues.
    *   Solution: Replacement or repair of the ABS module.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Rust**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rust formation on the rocker panels, wheel wells, and around the fuel filler door are common.
    *   Solution: Rust repair or panel replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Heater Core Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Leaking coolant inside the cabin, foggy windows, sweet smell inside the car, and a loss of heat.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the heater core. This is a labor-intensive job.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1500
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**
The 2006 Saab 9-5, while appreciated for its unique character and turbocharged performance, faces a range of potential issues that owners should be aware of. These range from relatively inexpensive fixes like sensor and bushing replacements to more significant concerns such as engine sludge and transmission valve body issues. The cost to maintain and repair this vehicle can vary widely. Considering the age of the vehicle, thorough inspection and preventative maintenance are crucial for potential buyers and current owners.
