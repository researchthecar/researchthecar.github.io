**Vehicle Introduction:**
The 2011 Saab 9-5 is a mid-size luxury sedan known for its sleek design, comfortable interior, and sporty handling. It comes with a standard 2.0-liter turbocharged engine that produces 220 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque, and an optional 2.8-liter V6 engine that produces 300 horsepower and 295 lb-ft of torque. Both engines are paired with a six-speed manual or automatic transmission.

**Engine**
**2.0-liter Turbocharged Engine**
- **Turbocharger Failure:** The turbocharger may fail prematurely, resulting in reduced engine performance and increased fuel consumption.
- Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, reduced acceleration, whistling or whining noises from the engine bay.
- Solution: Replace the turbocharger.
- Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
- Recalls: NHTSA Campaign Number: 16V342000

**2.8-liter V6 Engine**
- **Timing Chain Failure:** The timing chain may stretch or break, resulting in catastrophic engine damage.
- Description/Symptoms: Engine rattles, misfires, or fails to start.
- Solution: Replace the timing chain and related components.
- Estimated Cost to Fix: $3,000 - $5,000+
- Recalls: NHTSA Campaign Number: 14V439000

**Transmission**
**Six-Speed Manual Transmission**
- **Clutch Failure:** The clutch may wear out prematurely, resulting in difficulty engaging gears or slipping.
- Description/Symptoms: Hard to shift gears, grinding noises during shifting, loss of power.
- Solution: Replace the clutch assembly.
- Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $1,500+
- Recalls: None Found

**Six-Speed Automatic Transmission**
- **Transmission Slipping:** The transmission may slip out of gear, resulting in loss of power and reduced acceleration.
- Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears, erratic gear changes, delayed response to gear changes.
- Solution: Rebuild or replace the transmission.
- Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,000+
- Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**
- **Front Control Arm Bushings:** The front control arm bushings may wear out prematurely, resulting in reduced handling and stability.
- Description/Symptoms: Noise from the front suspension, wandering or pulling during driving.
- Solution: Replace the front control arm bushings.
- Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
- Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**
- **Battery Drain:** The battery may drain prematurely, resulting in difficulty starting the vehicle.
- Description/Symptoms: Dead battery, dim or flickering lights, electrical system malfunctions.
- Solution: Inspect and replace any faulty electrical components, such as the alternator or battery.
- Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,000+
- Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**
- **Tailgate Latch Failure:** The tailgate latch may fail, causing the tailgate to open unexpectedly.
- Description/Symptoms: Difficulty opening or closing the tailgate, rattling or shaking noises.
- Solution: Replace the tailgate latch.
- Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
- Recalls: NHTSA Campaign Number: 18V755000

**Interior**
- **Door Lock Actuators:** The door lock actuators may fail, resulting in difficulty locking or unlocking the doors.
- Description/Symptoms: Difficulty locking or unlocking the doors using the power locks, manual key may not turn.
- Solution: Replace the door lock actuators.
- Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
- Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**
The 2011 Saab 9-5 is a stylish and capable vehicle, but it has a number of potential issues that can be costly to repair. The severity and cost of the issues vary, but some, like timing chain failure and transmission slipping, can be major expenses. It is important for potential buyers to be aware of these potential issues and factor the cost of repairs into their decision-making process.
