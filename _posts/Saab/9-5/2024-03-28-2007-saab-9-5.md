**Introduction:**

The 2007 Saab 9-5 is a midsize luxury sedan offered with a choice of two engines: a 2.3T I4 and a 3.0T V6. The 2.3T produces 260 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque, while the 3.0T generates 296 horsepower and 260 lb-ft of torque. Both engines are paired with a 5-speed automatic transmission. The 9-5 is praised for its stylish design, comfortable interior, and responsive handling. However, it is criticized for its high price and limited availability.

**Engine**

**2.3T I4**

* **Turbocharger Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, excessive smoke, unusual noises from the engine bay
    * Solution: Replace the turbocharger
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Intake Manifold Leak:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Rough idle, reduced performance, abnormal engine noises
    * Solution: Replace the intake manifold gasket
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600
    * Recalls: None Found

**3.0T V6**

* **Timing Chain Stretch:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Rattling or ticking noises from the engine, reduced engine power
    * Solution: Replace the timing chain and gears
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Spark Plug Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Misfiring, engine hesitation, reduced fuel efficiency
    * Solution: Replace the spark plugs
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**5-Speed Automatic**

* **Transmission Slipping:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears, hesitation or jerking during acceleration
    * Solution: Rebuild or replace the transmission
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,500 - $4,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Strut Mount Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Clunking or rattling noises over bumps, reduced steering responsiveness
    * Solution: Replace the strut mounts
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Control Arm Bushing Wear:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Loose or sloppy steering, premature tire wear
    * Solution: Replace the control arm bushings
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Alternator Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Loss of electrical power, dimming lights, battery warning light illuminated
    * Solution: Replace the alternator
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Headlight Switch Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Intermittent or inoperable headlights
    * Solution: Replace the headlight switch
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Headlight Condensation:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Moisture inside the headlight housing
    * Solution: Inspect and seal any leaks, replace headlights if necessary
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Switchgear Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Inoperable buttons or switches on the dash or center console
    * Solution: Replace the faulty switches
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Climate Control Malfunction:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Ineffective heating or cooling, malfunctioning blower motor
    * Solution: Diagnose and repair or replace the climate control system
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

The Saab 9-5 is a stylish and well-equipped sedan, but it is not without its issues. Common problems include turbocharger failure, timing chain stretch, and transmission slipping. The cost of repairs can be significant, especially for major components like the engine and transmission. Overall, while the 9-5 is a desirable vehicle, it is important to be aware of the potential repair costs before purchasing.
