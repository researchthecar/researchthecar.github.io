The 2009 Saab 9-7x is a mid-size SUV based on the Chevrolet Trailblazer platform. Engine options included the 4.2L Inline-6, the 5.3L V8, and the 6.0L V8. The 4.2L produced 291 hp and 277 lb-ft of torque. A common point of praise for the 9-7x is its comfortable ride and relatively sporty handling for an SUV, combined with Saab's distinctive styling cues. Criticisms often focus on the interior quality not matching the premium brand image and its fuel economy.

**Engine**

**4.2L Inline-6**
*   **Camshaft Actuator Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Ticking noise from the engine, especially at idle. Check engine light may illuminate with codes related to camshaft position. Can cause rough idling and reduced performance.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the camshaft actuator/phaser.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1000+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Evaporator Emissions Purge Solenoid Valve Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Check engine light is on. Common code is P0449 - Evaporative Emission Control System Vent Valve/Solenoid Circuit. Gas smell may be present.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the purge valve solenoid.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100-$200+
    * Recalls: None Found

**5.3L V8**

*   **Oil Consumption**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Excessive oil consumption, often requiring frequent top-offs between oil changes. Blue smoke from the exhaust may be present.
    *   Solution: Depending on the severity, the solution could range from using a thicker oil to replacing valve seals or piston rings. In severe cases, an engine rebuild or replacement might be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 (thicker oil/additives) to $3,000+ (engine rebuild/replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

* **Active Fuel Management (AFM) Lifter Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Misfires, rough idling, and a noticeable ticking or knocking sound from the engine. Check engine light is likely illuminated.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the failed AFM lifters. Some owners choose to delete the AFM system entirely with aftermarket parts.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**6.0L V8**
* No Issues Found

**Transmission**

**All Transmissions (Specifically 4L60E)**

*   **Transmission Slipping/Hard Shifting**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Slipping between gears, hard or delayed shifts, especially when cold. May also experience shuddering or jerking.
    *   Solution: Could be due to low fluid, worn clutches, or a failing valve body. A transmission flush and filter change might help in mild cases, but a rebuild or replacement is often necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 (flush and filter) to $1,500 - $3,500+ (rebuild/replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Air Suspension Compressor Failure (If Equipped)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The rear of the vehicle sagging, a warning light on the dash, and a failure of the air compressor to inflate the rear air springs.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the air suspension compressor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $400-$800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Rear Air Suspension Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rear of the vehicle sagging over time, hissing sound near the rear air springs.
    *   Solution: Replacement of leaking air springs.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+ per air spring
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Ignition Switch Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Intermittent starting problems, stalling, or the vehicle not starting at all.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the ignition switch.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
    * Recalls: None Found

*   **Door Lock Actuator Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Door locks failing to lock or unlock with the remote or door switch.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the faulty door lock actuator.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150-$300+ per door
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **HVAC Blower Motor Resistor Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Blower motor only working on certain speeds or not working at all.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the blower motor resistor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100-$200+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Rear Wiper Motor Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rear wiper not working or moving erratically.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the rear wiper motor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150-$300+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Sunroof Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Sunroof not opening or closing properly, getting stuck, or making unusual noises.
    *   Solution: Could be due to a broken track, motor failure, or debris in the mechanism. Cleaning, lubrication, or component replacement may be needed.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2009 Saab 9-7x, while offering a unique Saab experience on a GM platform, suffers from common issues associated with its mechanical underpinnings. Engine issues, particularly oil consumption with the 5.3L V8, and potential transmission problems can be costly. Electrical gremlins, such as ignition switch and door lock actuator failures, can be bothersome. Suspension issues with the air ride system are common, especially as the vehicle ages. While some repairs are relatively inexpensive, major engine or transmission work can represent a significant financial burden. Potential buyers should be aware of these potential pitfalls and thoroughly inspect any prospective purchase.
