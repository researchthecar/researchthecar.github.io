**Introduction:**

The 2008 Saab 9-7x was a mid-size SUV based on the TrailBlazer platform from Chevrolet. It came with two engine options: a 4.2-liter inline-6 and a 5.3-liter V8. Transmission options included a 4-speed automatic for the I6 and a 6-speed automatic for the V8. Known for its rugged exterior, comfortable interior, and powerful engines, the 9-7x also had its fair share of issues.

**Engine**

**4.2-liter Inline-6**

* **Intake Manifold Gasket Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Coolant leakage, engine overheating, rough idling.
    * Solution: Replace intake manifold gasket.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: 13V357000

**5.3-liter V8**

* **Piston Ring Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Oil consumption, engine knocking, reduced power.
    * Solution: Rebuild engine or replace pistons and rings.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $5,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Active Fuel Management Malfunction:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Reduced fuel economy, engine hesitation, check engine light.
    * Solution: Replace or repair AFM system.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500

**Transmission**

**4-speed Automatic**

* **Shift Solenoid Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Harsh shifting, transmission slipping, inability to shift gears.
    * Solution: Replace shift solenoid.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $700

**6-speed Automatic**

* **Torque Converter Shudder:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Vibrations at low speeds, especially when accelerating.
    * Solution: Replace or rebuild torque converter.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000

* **Transmission Fluid Leak:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Fluid leaking from transmission, resulting in low fluid levels and transmission damage.
    * Solution: Inspect and fix the source of the leak, replace fluid as needed.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500

**Suspension**

* **Front Lower Control Arm Bushing Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Clunking or rattling noises, uneven tire wear, poor handling.
    * Solution: Replace control arm bushings.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600

* **Rear Shock Absorber Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Reduced ride quality, bouncy or unstable handling, uneven tire wear.
    * Solution: Replace shock absorbers.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800

**Electrical**

* **Headlight Switch Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Headlights not turning on or flickering, dash gauges malfunctioning.
    * Solution: Replace headlight switch.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400

* **Battery Drain:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Battery dies after short periods of non-use.
    * Solution: Identify and fix the source of the electrical drain, typically related to a parasitic draw from a faulty component.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+

**Exterior**

* **Rust:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Body panels rusting prematurely, leading to structural issues.
    * Solution: Rust repair, rust prevention treatments.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies greatly depending on the extent of rust damage

**Interior**

* **Heater Core Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: No heat coming from heater vents, coolant leak.
    * Solution: Replace heater core.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+

* **Steering Wheel Controls Malfunction:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Buttons or controls on the steering wheel not working, including cruise control and audio controls.
    * Solution: Replace or repair steering wheel control module.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500

**Summary:**

The 2008 Saab 9-7x had a range of issues, some of which were severe and expensive to fix. Engine problems, particularly with the 5.3-liter V8, were the most costly, while electrical and interior issues were relatively minor. However, the prevalence of rust and suspension problems also contributed to the overall reliability concerns.
