**1999 Saab 9-5**

The 1999 Saab 9-5 is a mid-size luxury sedan that was praised for its performance, handling, and safety features. It was available with a range of engine and transmission options, including:

* 2.3-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine (170 hp, 210 lb-ft torque) mated to a 5-speed manual or 4-speed automatic transmission
* 3.0-liter naturally aspirated V6 engine (200 hp, 195 lb-ft torque) mated to a 5-speed automatic transmission
* 2.3-liter turbocharged V6 engine (230 hp, 258 lb-ft torque) mated to a 5-speed automatic transmission

**Engine (2.3-liter Turbocharged Four-Cylinder)**

* **Intake Manifold Leak:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Engine misfires, decreased performance, and possible check engine light illumination (P0171, P0174)
    * Solution: Replace intake manifold gaskets
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Turbocharger Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Reduced engine power, whining or whistling noises, excessive oil consumption
    * Solution: Replace turbocharger assembly
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,500+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Head Gasket Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaks, overheating, engine misfires
    * Solution: Replace head gasket
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Engine (3.0-liter Naturally Aspirated V6)**

* **Camshaft Position Sensor Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Difficulty starting, rough idling, engine misfires
    * Solution: Replace camshaft position sensor
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Crankshaft Position Sensor Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: No spark, engine will not start
    * Solution: Replace crankshaft position sensor
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $250 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found
