**Introduction:**

The 2007 Saab 09-May is a compact luxury sedan that was offered with two engine options: a 2.0L turbocharged inline-4 and a 2.3L turbocharged inline-4. Both engines were paired with a 5-speed manual transmission or a 6-speed automatic transmission. The 2.0L engine produced 150 horsepower and 162 lb-ft of torque, while the 2.3L engine produced 175 horsepower and 207 lb-ft of torque. The 09-May was praised for its stylish design, comfortable interior, and responsive handling. However, it was also criticized for its high price and lack of standard features.

**Engine**
**2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4 with 5-Speed Manual Transmission**
- **Oil Leak from Timing Chain Cover:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Leaking oil from the front of the engine, visible as a dark, oily substance around the timing chain cover.
    - Solution: Replace the timing chain cover gasket.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+
- **Intake Manifold Gasket Failure:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Vacuum leak from the intake manifold, causing rough idling, stalling, and reduced power.
    - Solution: Replace the intake manifold gasket.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $300+

**2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4 with 6-Speed Automatic Transmission**
- **No Issues Found**

**2.3L Turbocharged Inline-4 with 5-Speed Manual Transmission**
- **Turbocharger Failure:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Loss of boost pressure, reduced power, and a whistling noise from the engine.
    - Solution: Replace the turbocharger.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,200 - $1,500+
- **Head Bolt Failure:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Coolant leak from the head gasket, overheating, and loss of power.
    - Solution: Replace the head bolts and head gasket.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,200+

**2.3L Turbocharged Inline-4 with 6-Speed Automatic Transmission**
- **No Issues Found**

**Transmission**
**5-Speed Manual Transmission**
- **Clutch Failure:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Slipping or chattering of the clutch, difficulty shifting gears, or the car stalling while driving.
    - Solution: Replace the clutch disc, pressure plate, and throwout bearing.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $700+
- **Shift Linkage Problems:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears, grinding noises when shifting, or the gear selector becoming loose.
    - Solution: Adjust or replace the shift linkage components.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300+

**6-Speed Automatic Transmission**
- **Torque Converter Shudder:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Vibration or shuddering when the transmission is shifting gears.
    - Solution: Replace the torque converter.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,200+
- **Solenoid Failure:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Transmission slipping or refusing to shift gears.
    - Solution: Replace the faulty solenoid.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+

**Suspension**
- **Front Control Arm Bushings Failure:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Squealing or clunking noises from the front suspension, excessive tire wear, or poor handling.
    - Solution: Replace the control arm bushings.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
- **Rear Shock Absorber Failure:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Bumpy or unstable ride, loss of handling, or uneven tire wear.
    - Solution: Replace the rear shock absorbers.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+

**Electrical**
- **Battery Drain:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Battery dies prematurely or has difficulty starting, lights dim or flicker, or the dashboard displays electrical faults.
    - Solution: Diagnose and repair the cause of the battery drain, such as a faulty alternator, starter, or wiring.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+
- **Ignition Coil Failure:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Misfires, rough idling, or difficulty starting the car.
    - Solution: Replace the faulty ignition coil.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $150+

**Exterior**
- **No Issues Found**

**Interior**
- **Climate Control Malfunction:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Air conditioning or heater not working properly, uneven temperature distribution, or excessive noise from the climate control system.
    - Solution: Diagnose and repair the fault in the climate control system, such as a faulty compressor, evaporator, or temperature sensor.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600+

**Summary**

The 2007 Saab 09-May is a reliable vehicle with a few common issues that can be relatively inexpensive to fix. While there are some major issues to be aware of, such as turbocharger failure and head bolt failure, they are not as common. Overall, the 09-May is a comfortable and stylish vehicle that offers a good driving experience.
