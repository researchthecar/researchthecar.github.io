**Introduction**

The 2011 Saab 9-5 is a mid-size luxury sedan that offers a comfortable and refined driving experience. It is powered by a 2.0L turbocharged 4-cylinder engine that produces 220 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque, paired with a 6-speed manual or 6-speed automatic transmission. The 9-5 is known for its stylish design, spacious interior, and advanced safety features. However, it has also been plagued by several issues over the years.

**Engine**

**2.0L Turbocharged 4-Cylinder Engine**

* **Turbocharger failure:** The turbocharger can fail prematurely, causing a loss of power and increased exhaust emissions. Symptoms include a whistling sound from the engine, reduced boost pressure, and increased fuel consumption. The solution is to replace the turbocharger, which can cost between $1,000 and $2,000+.
* **PCV valve failure:** The PCV valve can fail, causing excessive oil consumption and engine sludge buildup. Symptoms include blue smoke from the exhaust, rough idling, and decreased engine performance. The solution is to replace the PCV valve, which costs around $100.
* **Oil leaks:** The 2.0L engine is prone to oil leaks from various seals and gaskets. Symptoms include oil spots under the vehicle and a low oil level on the dipstick. The solution is to replace the leaking seals or gaskets, which can cost between $200 and $500+.

**Transmission**

**6-Speed Manual Transmission**

* **Grinding gears:** The manual transmission can develop grinding gears, making it difficult to shift smoothly. Symptoms include a grinding noise when shifting and difficulty engaging gears. The solution is to replace the worn synchronizers or gears, which can cost between $500 and $1,000+.

**6-Speed Automatic Transmission**

* **Shifting problems:** The automatic transmission can develop shifting problems, such as rough shifting, delayed shifts, or slipping gears. Symptoms include a shuddering or jerking sensation when shifting and difficulty accelerating. The solution is to service the transmission, which can cost between $200 and $600+.

**Suspension**

* **Strut mount failure:** The strut mounts can fail, causing clunking or knocking noises from the front suspension. Symptoms include a rattling noise over bumps and reduced handling stability. The solution is to replace the strut mounts, which can cost between $200 and $400+.
* **Sway bar link failure:** The sway bar links can fail, causing clunking or rattling noises from the suspension. Symptoms include excessive body roll during cornering and decreased ride comfort. The solution is to replace the sway bar links, which can cost between $100 and $200+.
* **CV joint failure:** The CV joints can fail, causing a clicking or popping noise from the wheels. Symptoms include vibration during acceleration or deceleration. The solution is to replace the CV joint, which can cost between $200 and $600+.

**Electrical**

* **Battery failure:** The battery can fail prematurely, causing difficulty starting the vehicle. Symptoms include difficulty starting, weak headlights, and a dead battery. The solution is to replace the battery, which can cost between $100 and $200+.
* **Alternator failure:** The alternator can fail, causing the vehicle to lose electrical power. Symptoms include dim headlights, a flickering battery light, and a dead battery. The solution is to replace the alternator, which can cost between $500 and $1,000+.
* **Electrical gremlins:** The 9-5 is known for experiencing electrical gremlins, such as flickering lights, intermittent failures of various electronic components, and draining batteries. The solution is to diagnose and repair the specific electrical issue, which can vary in cost.

**Exterior**

* **Window trim molding failure:** The window trim molding can fail, causing the trim to detach or fall off the vehicle. Symptoms include a loose or missing window trim and a rattling noise. The solution is to replace the window trim molding, which can cost between $100 and $300+.
* **Door lock failure:** The door locks can fail, causing difficulty locking or unlocking the doors. Symptoms include a stuck door lock, a non-functioning key fob, or a rattling noise from the door. The solution is to replace the door lock actuator, which can cost between $200 and $500+.

**Interior**

* **Center console lid latch failure:** The center console lid latch can fail, causing the lid to open unexpectedly. Symptoms include a loose or rattling center console lid and difficulty closing the lid. The solution is to replace the center console lid latch, which can cost around $100.
* **Dashboard rattle:** The dashboard can develop a rattle, causing a buzzing or rattling noise from the interior. Symptoms include a rattling noise from the dashboard and excessive vibration. The solution is to identify the source of the rattle and repair or replace the affected component, which can vary in cost.

**Summary**

The 2011 Saab 9-5 offers a comfortable and refined driving experience, but it has also been plagued by various issues over the years. The most common problems include turbocharger failure, PCV valve failure, oil leaks, grinding gears, shifting problems, strut mount failure, and electrical gremlins. The severity of these issues can range from minor inconveniences to major and expensive repairs. Potential buyers should be aware of these issues and consider the cost of potential repairs when considering a 9-5.
