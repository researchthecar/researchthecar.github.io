**Introduction:**

The 2003 Saab 9-3 is a premium sedan powered by a 2.0-liter turbocharged inline-4 engine that produces 210 horsepower and 221 lb-ft of torque. It is offered with either a five-speed manual or five-speed automatic transmission. The 9-3 is praised for its stylish design, excellent handling, and comfortable interior. However, it is known to have some common issues.

**Engine**

**2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4**

* **Intake Valve Carbon Buildup:** Carbon buildup on the intake valves restricts airflow, causing rough idle, reduced power, and increased fuel consumption.
   * Solution: Clean or replace the intake valves.
   * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
   * Recalls: None Found
* **Turbocharger Failure:** The turbocharger may fail due to excessive wear or heat. Symptoms include excessive smoke, loss of power, and high-pitched whining noises.
   * Solution: Replace the turbocharger.
   * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
   * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**Five-Speed Manual**

* **Clutch Master Cylinder Failure:** The clutch master cylinder may fail, causing the clutch to slip or feel spongy.
   * Solution: Replace the clutch master cylinder.
   * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
   * Recalls: None Found
* **Gearbox Grinding:** The gearbox may grind when shifting gears, especially when cold.
   * Solution: Adjust or replace the gearbox synchros.
   * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
   * Recalls: None Found

**Five-Speed Automatic**

* **Transmission Solenoid Failure:** The transmission solenoids may fail, causing the transmission to slip or shift erratically.
   * Solution: Replace the transmission solenoids.
   * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
   * Recalls: None Found
* **Torque Converter Failure:** The torque converter may fail, causing the transmission to slip or shudder.
   * Solution: Replace the torque converter.
   * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
   * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Front Control Arm Bushings:** The front control arm bushings may wear out or fail, causing rattling, clunking, or poor handling.
   * Solution: Replace the control arm bushings.
   * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
   * Recalls: None Found
* **Rear Shock Absorbers:** The rear shock absorbers may fail, causing the rear of the vehicle to sag or bounce excessively.
   * Solution: Replace the rear shock absorbers.
   * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
   * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Headlight Switch Failure:** The headlight switch may fail, causing the headlights to not work or flicker.
   * Solution: Replace the headlight switch.
   * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
   * Recalls: None Found
* **Battery Drain:** The battery may drain prematurely due to a parasitic current draw.
   * Solution: Inspect and repair the electrical system for faulty components or wiring.
   * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
   * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Sunroof Drainage Problems:** The sunroof drains may become clogged, causing water to leak into the interior.
   * Solution: Clean or replace the sunroof drains.
   * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+
   * Recalls: None Found
* **Center Console Lid Malfunction:** The center console lid may not latch properly or may become loose.
   * Solution: Adjust or replace the center console lid latch.
   * Estimated Cost to Fix: Free (warranty repair)

**Exterior**

* **Door Handle Breakage:** The door handles may break or become loose due to excessive wear or cold weather.
   * Solution: Replace the door handles.
   * Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $150+
   * Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

The 2003 Saab 9-3 is a stylish and fun-to-drive car, but it is known to have some common issues. Many of the issues are relatively minor and inexpensive to fix, but more serious problems, such as turbocharger failure or transmission problems, can be costly. Depending on the severity and frequency of the issues, the 9-3 may not be the most reliable choice for all drivers.
