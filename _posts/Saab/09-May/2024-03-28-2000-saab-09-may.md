**Introduction:**

The 2000 Saab 9-5 is a mid-sized luxury sedan available with a choice of two engines and two transmissions. The base engine is a 2.3-liter turbocharged inline-four that produces 185 horsepower and 221 pound-feet of torque. The optional engine is a 3.0-liter turbocharged V6 that produces 200 horsepower and 229 pound-feet of torque. Both engines are paired with a standard five-speed manual transmission or an optional five-speed automatic transmission. The Saab 9-5 is praised for its comfortable interior, responsive handling, and stylish design. However, it is criticized for its high price and limited rear-seat space.

**Engine**

**2.3-liter Turbocharged Inline-Four**

* **Blown head gasket:** Description/Symptoms: Overheating, coolant leaks, white smoke from the exhaust. Solution: Replace the head gasket. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+ Recalls: None Found
* **Turbocharger failure:** Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, excessive smoke from the exhaust. Solution: Replace the turbocharger. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+ Recalls: None Found
* **Engine oil leaks:** Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks from the engine block or oil pan. Solution: Replace the leaking gasket or seal. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+ Recalls: None Found

**3.0-liter Turbocharged V6**

* **Crankshaft seal leak:** Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks from the rear of the engine. Solution: Replace the crankshaft seal. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+ Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**Five-Speed Manual Transmission**

* **Synchronizer problems:** Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears, grinding noises. Solution: Replace the synchronizers. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+ Recalls: None Found
* **Clutch failure:** Description/Symptoms: Slipping clutch, difficulty engaging gears. Solution: Replace the clutch kit. Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,200+ Recalls: None Found

**Five-Speed Automatic Transmission**

* **Transmission slipping:** Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, delayed shifting. Solution: Repair or replace the transmission. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+ Recalls: NHTSA Campaign ID: 14V393000

* **Bushing wear:** Description/Symptoms: Loose or worn bushings can cause excessive play in the suspension. Solution: Replace the worn bushings. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+ Recalls: None Found

* **Air conditioning problems:** Description/Symptoms: Air conditioning not blowing cold air. Solution: Diagnose and repair air conditioning system. Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies Recalls: None Found
* **Electrical problems:** Description/Symptoms: Electrical components may malfunction, such as dashboard lights, windows, or sunroof. Solution: Diagnose and repair electrical system. Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

The 2000 Saab 9-5 has some potential issues, but most are relatively minor and inexpensive to fix. The most severe issues include blown head gaskets, turbocharger failure, transmission slipping, and heater core failure. Overall, the Saab 9-5 is a reliable and comfortable vehicle, but it is important to be aware of its potential issues before purchasing one.
