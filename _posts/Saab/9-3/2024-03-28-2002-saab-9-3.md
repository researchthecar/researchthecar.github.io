**Introduction**

The 2002 Saab 9-3 is a compact luxury car powered by a 2.0L turbocharged 4-cylinder engine producing 205 horsepower and 221 lb-ft of torque. It is available with either a 5-speed manual transmission or a 5-speed automatic transmission. The 9-3 is known for its sporty handling, comfortable interior, and distinctive Scandinavian design. However, like any vehicle, it has its share of issues.

**Engine**

**2.0L Turbocharged 4-Cylinder**

* **Intake Manifold Leak:** A faulty plastic intake manifold can crack, causing a vacuum leak. Symptoms may include decreased power, rough idling, and increased fuel consumption. **Solution: Replace intake manifold.** **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $500 - $1000+ **Recalls:** None Found
* **Camshaft Position Sensor Failure:** A failing camshaft position sensor can provide incorrect timing information to the engine's computer, leading to engine performance issues. Symptoms may include hard starting, stalling, and reduced power. **Solution: Replace camshaft position sensor.** **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $200 - $400+ **Recalls:** None Found
* **Turbocharger Failure:** The turbocharger can fail prematurely, resulting in a lack of boost and decreased engine power. Symptoms may include a whistling noise, black smoke from the exhaust, and a loss of acceleration. **Solution: Replace turbocharger.** **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $1500 - $2500+ **Recalls:** None Found

**Transmission**

**5-Speed Manual**

* **Clutch Master Cylinder Failure:** A faulty clutch master cylinder can prevent the clutch from fully engaging, resulting in difficulty shifting gears. Symptoms may include a spongy clutch pedal and difficulty getting into gear. **Solution: Replace clutch master cylinder.** **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $400 - $800+ **Recalls:** None Found

**5-Speed Automatic**

* **Transmission Solenoid Failure:** A faulty transmission solenoid can prevent the transmission from shifting properly. Symptoms may include delayed or erratic shifting, slipping gears, and a check engine light. **Solution: Replace transmission solenoid.** **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $500 - $1000+ **Recalls:** None Found

**Suspension**

* **Front Suspension Lower Control Arm Bushings:** The front suspension lower control arm bushings can wear out prematurely, causing knocking or clunking noises when driving over bumps. Symptoms may also include uneven tire wear. **Solution: Replace lower control arm bushings.** **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $300 - $500+ **Recalls:** None Found
* **Rear Suspension Trailing Arm Bushings:** The rear suspension trailing arm bushings can also wear out prematurely, resulting in similar symptoms to the front suspension bushings. **Solution: Replace trailing arm bushings.** **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $300 - $500+ **Recalls:** None Found

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain:** A parasitic draw on the battery can drain the battery prematurely, making it difficult to start the vehicle. Symptoms may include a dead battery after sitting overnight. **Solution: Diagnose and repair source of parasitic draw.** **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $150 - $500+ **Recalls:** None Found
* **Ignition Cassette Failure:** A faulty ignition cassette can prevent the spark plugs from firing properly, leading to misfires and rough running. Symptoms may include a check engine light, reduced power, and difficulty starting. **Solution: Replace ignition cassette.** **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $200 - $400+ **Recalls:** None Found

**Exterior**

* **Headlight Switch Failure:** A faulty headlight switch can prevent the headlights from turning on or off properly. Symptoms may include intermittent headlights or headlights that do not turn on at all. **Solution: Replace headlight switch.** **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $100 - $200+ **Recalls:** None Found
* **Sunroof Leak:** A leaking sunroof can allow water to enter the vehicle's interior, causing damage to the headliner and electronics. Symptoms may include water dripping from the headliner or pooling on the floor. **Solution: Diagnose and repair sunroof drain system or replace sunroof cassette.** **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $500 - $1000+ **Recalls:** None Found

**Interior**

* **Dashboard Cluster Pixel Failure:** The LCD pixels on the dashboard cluster can fail prematurely, causing the instrument panel to become illegible. Symptoms may include missing digits or characters on the display. **Solution: Replace dashboard cluster.** **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $400 - $800+ **Recalls:** None Found
* **Heater Core Leak:** A leaking heater core can allow coolant to enter the passenger compartment, fogging up the windshield or causing a musty smell. Symptoms may also include a low coolant level. **Solution: Replace heater core.** **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $500 - $1000+ **Recalls:** None Found

**Summary**

Overall, the 2002 Saab 9-3 is a reliable and well-built vehicle. However, like any used car, it can develop certain issues over time. The most common issues include intake manifold leaks, camshaft position sensor failures, and suspension bushing wear. The severity and cost of these issues can vary depending on the specific parts and labor involved. It is important to have the vehicle regularly inspected and maintained to help prevent or address any potential problems.
