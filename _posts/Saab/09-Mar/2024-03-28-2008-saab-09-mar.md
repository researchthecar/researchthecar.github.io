**Introduction:**

The 2008 Saab 09-Mar is a compact sedan offered with a turbocharged 2.0L inline-4 engine producing 200 horsepower and 221 lb-ft of torque, paired with a 5-speed manual or 6-speed automatic transmission. It is known for its sporty handling, comfortable interior, and distinctive design. However, like any vehicle, it has some known issues that owners may encounter.

**Engine:**

* **2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4:**
    * **Intake Camshaft Failure:**
        * Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, hard starting, engine stalling, check engine light
        * Solution: Replace the intake camshaft
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1000 - $2000+
        * Recalls: None Found
    * **Turbocharger Oil Leak:**
        * Description/Symptoms: Oil leaking from the turbocharger, blue smoke from exhaust
        * Solution: Replace the turbocharger oil feed line
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1000
        * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission:**

* **5-Speed Manual:**
    * **Synchronizer Wear:**
        * Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears, grinding noises
        * Solution: Replace the synchronizer assembly
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1000
        * Recalls: None Found
* **6-Speed Automatic:**
    * **Solenoid Failure:**
        * Description/Symptoms: Transmission slipping, delayed shifts
        * Solution: Replace the solenoid
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1000
        * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension:**

* **Front Lower Control Arm Bushings:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Premature wear, clunking noises over bumps
    * Solution: Replace the control arm bushings
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Rear Shock Absorbers:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Poor ride quality, excessive body roll
    * Solution: Replace the shock absorbers
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical:**

* **Alternator Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Dim headlights, dead battery
    * Solution: Replace the alternator
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1000
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Ignition Coil Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Misfires, rough idling, check engine light
    * Solution: Replace the ignition coil
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior:**

* **Sunroof Drain Plugs Clogging:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Water leaking into the cabin
    * Solution: Clean or replace the drain plugs
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior:**

* **Heater Control Valve Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Inability to control cabin temperature
    * Solution: Replace the heater control valve
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Instrument Cluster Malfunction:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Dim or flickering gauges, incorrect readings
    * Solution: Replace the instrument cluster
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1000
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

While the 2008 Saab 09-Mar is a well-built vehicle, it is not without its issues. Some of the common problems involve the engine's intake camshaft, turbocharger, and manual transmission's synchronizers. Additionally, electrical components such as the alternator and ignition coil may fail, and interior parts like the heater control valve and instrument cluster can malfunction. The severity and cost of these repairs can vary depending on the specific issue and the location of the work. Overall, the 09-Mar is a reliable vehicle, but it is important to be aware of these potential problems and factor them into ownership costs.
