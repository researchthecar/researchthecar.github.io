**Introduction**

The 2005 Saab 9-3 is a compact executive car produced by Saab Automobile. It was offered with a range of engine and transmission options, including:

* **Engines**:
    * 2.0T I4 (150 hp, 192 lb-ft)
    * 2.0T I4 (210 hp, 221 lb-ft)
    * 2.8 V6 (250 hp, 258 lb-ft)
* **Transmissions**:
    * 5-speed manual
    * 6-speed manual
    * 5-speed automatic

The 9-3 was praised for its handling, performance, and safety, but it also had some notable issues.

**Engine**

**2.0T I4 (150 hp)**

* **Turbocharger Failure:** The turbocharger can fail prematurely, causing a loss of power and increased oil consumption.
    * Solution: Replace the turbocharger.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500
* **Oil Consumption:** Excessive oil consumption is a common issue, often due to worn piston rings or valve seals.
    * Solution: Overhaul the engine.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $3,000 - $5,000

**2.0T I4 (210 hp)**

* **Timing Chain Tensioner Failure:** The timing chain tensioner can fail, causing the timing chain to jump and potentially damage the engine.
    * Solution: Replace the timing chain and tensioner.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,000
* **Water Pump Failure:** The water pump can fail prematurely, leading to overheating.
    * Solution: Replace the water pump.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000

**2.8 V6**

* **Intake Manifold Gasket Leak:** The intake manifold gasket can leak, causing a vacuum leak and decreased performance.
    * Solution: Replace the intake manifold gasket.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
* **Power Steering Pump Leak:** The power steering pump can leak, causing a loss of power steering assistance.
    * Solution: Replace the power steering pump.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600

**Transmission**

**5-speed Manual**

* **Synchronizer Failure:** The synchronizers can wear out prematurely, making it difficult to shift gears.
    * Solution: Replace the synchronizers.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000

**6-speed Manual**

* **Shift Linkage Failure:** The shift linkage can fail, preventing the driver from shifting gears.
    * Solution: Replace the shift linkage.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000

**5-speed Automatic**

* **Transmission Fluid Leak:** The transmission can develop leaks, causing a loss of fluid and decreased performance.
    * Solution: Repair the leak and replace the transmission fluid.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500
* **Solenoid Failure:** The solenoids can fail, causing shifting problems.
    * Solution: Replace the solenoids.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000

**Suspension**

* **Control Arm Bushing Failure:** The control arm bushings can wear out prematurely, leading to decreased handling and increased noise.
    * Solution: Replace the control arm bushings.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600

**Electrical**

* **Alternator Failure:** The alternator can fail prematurely, causing a loss of electrical power.
    * Solution: Replace the alternator.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
* **Battery Drain:** Excessive battery drain can occur, causing the battery to die prematurely.
    * Solution: Inspect and repair the electrical system for leaks.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the source of the drain

**Exterior**

* **Sunroof Seal Failure:** The sunroof seal can fail, causing water leaks.
    * Solution: Replace the sunroof seal.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000

**Interior**

* **HVAC Malfunction:** The HVAC system can malfunction, causing issues with heating or cooling.
    * Solution: Repair or replace the HVAC components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the specific issue

**Summary**

The 2005 Saab 9-3 has a number of potential issues, particularly with its engine and transmission. The severity and cost of these issues vary, but some can be expensive to repair. Overall, the 9-3 is a reliable car with good performance and handling, but it's important to be aware of its potential problems before making a purchase.
