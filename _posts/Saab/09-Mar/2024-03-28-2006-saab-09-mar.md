The Saab 9-3 is a compact executive car produced by Saab from 2002 to 2011. It replaced the Saab 900, and was available in both sedan and convertible body styles. The 2006 Saab 9-3 comes standard with a 210-horsepower, 2.0-liter turbocharged engine and a 5-speed manual transmission. Other engine and transmission options include:

* 2.0-liter turbocharged engine with 250 horsepower and a 6-speed manual transmission
* 2.0-liter turbocharged engine with 250 horsepower and a 6-speed automatic transmission
* 2.8-liter turbocharged engine with 280 horsepower and a 6-speed automatic transmission

The Saab 9-3 is praised for its handling, performance, and safety, but some owners have reported issues with its engine, transmission, suspension, electrical system, and interior.

**Engine**

**2.0-liter turbocharged engine with 210 horsepower**

* **Intake manifold failure**
> Description/Symptoms: The intake manifold can crack, causing a loss of power and a rough idle.
> Solution: Replace the intake manifold.
> Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
> Recalls: None Found

* **Turbocharger failure**
> Description/Symptoms: The turbocharger can fail, causing a loss of power and a whistling sound.
> Solution: Replace the turbocharger.
> Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
> Recalls: None Found

**2.0-liter turbocharged engine with 250 horsepower**

* **Intake manifold failure**
> Description/Symptoms: The intake manifold can crack, causing a loss of power and a rough idle.
> Solution: Replace the intake manifold.
> Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
> Recalls: None Found

* **Turbocharger failure**
> Description/Symptoms: The turbocharger can fail, causing a loss of power and a whistling sound.
> Solution: Replace the turbocharger.

**2.8-liter turbocharged engine with 280 horsepower**

* **Oil leakage**
> Description/Symptoms: The engine can leak oil from the valve cover gasket or the oil pan gasket.
> Solution: Replace the leaking gasket.
> Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
> Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**5-speed manual transmission**

* **Synchronizer failure**
> Description/Symptoms: The synchronizers can fail, causing difficulty shifting gears.
> Solution: Replace the synchronizers.
> Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
> Recalls: None Found

**6-speed manual transmission**

* **Synchronizer failure**
> Description/Symptoms: The synchronizers can fail, causing difficulty shifting gears.
> Solution: Replace the synchronizers.
> Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
> Recalls: None Found

**6-speed automatic transmission**

* **Transmission slippage**
> Description/Symptoms: The transmission can slip, causing a loss of power and a rough ride.
> Solution: Replace the transmission.
> Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+
> Recalls: 14V396000, 14V451000, 14V535000

**Suspension**

* **Strut failure**
> Description/Symptoms: The struts can fail, causing a loss of stability and handling.

> Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
> Recalls: None Found

* **Control arm failure**
> Description/Symptoms: The control arms can fail, causing a loss of stability and handling.

> Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
> Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Alternator failure**

* **Starter failure**
> Description/Symptoms: The starter can fail, preventing the engine from starting.

**Exterior**

* **No Issues Found**

**Interior**

* **Climate control issues**
> Description/Symptoms: The climate control system can malfunction, causing the temperature inside the vehicle to be too hot or too cold.
> Solution: Replace the climate control module.
> Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
> Recalls: 14V527000

* **Power window failure**
> Description/Symptoms: The power windows can fail, preventing them from opening or closing.
> Solution: Replace the power window motor or switch.
> Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
> Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2006 Saab 9-3 is a reliable car, but it is not without its issues. The most common problems are with the engine, transmission, and suspension. The cost of repairs can be high, but there are many recalls that cover the cost of some of the most common problems. Overall, the Saab 9-3 is a good car for the price, but buyers should be aware of the potential problems before purchasing one.
