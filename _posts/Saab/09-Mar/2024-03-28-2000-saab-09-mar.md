**Introduction**

The 2000 Saab 09-3 is a compact executive car available in sedan and combi (wagon) body styles. It features a turbocharged 2.0L 4-cylinder engine that produces 150 horsepower and 184 lb-ft of torque. The engine is paired with a standard 5-speed manual transmission or an optional 4-speed automatic transmission.

Owners of the 2000 Saab 09-3 generally appreciate its sporty handling, comfortable interior, and unique styling. However, some common issues have been reported with the vehicle.

**Engine**

* **2.0L Turbocharged Engine**
    * **Head gasket failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaks, overheating, white smoke from exhaust
        * Solution: Replace head gasket
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $1,500+
        * Recalls: None Found
    * **Turbocharger failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, whistling or grinding noises from engine
        * Solution: Replace turbocharger
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

* **5-speed Manual Transmission**
    * **Shifting difficulty**
        * Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears, grinding noises
        * Solution: Replace clutch or transmission
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
        * Recalls: None Found
    * **Clutch failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: Slipping or burning clutch, difficulty shifting gears
        * Solution: Replace clutch
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
        * Recalls: None Found
* **4-speed Automatic Transmission**
    * **Transmission slipping**
        * Description/Symptoms: Vehicle may hesitate or slip while shifting gears
        * Solution: Repair or replace transmission
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Strut failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Leaking struts, bouncy or unstable ride
    * Solution: Replace struts
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000 per pair
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Alternator failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Battery not charging, dimming headlights, electrical malfunctions
    * Solution: Replace alternator
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Ignition switch failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Difficulty starting vehicle, key stuck in ignition
    * Solution: Replace ignition switch
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Sunroof leaks**
    * Description/Symptoms: Water leaks into cabin through sunroof
    * Solution: Repair or replace sunroof seal
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,000
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Dashboard rattling**
    * Description/Symptoms: Rattle or vibration from dashboard
    * Solution: Inspect and tighten loose components
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: Free to low
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2000 Saab 09-3 is a generally reliable vehicle, but it has some common issues that can be costly to fix. The engine is relatively durable, but head gasket and turbocharger failures are known to occur. The transmission can also experience issues, particularly with the automatic transmission. Other problems include suspension strut failure, electrical malfunctions, sunroof leaks, and dashboard rattling. Overall, the severity and cost of these issues are moderate, and the vehicle's overall reliability is still considered to be above average.
