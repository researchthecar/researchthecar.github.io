# 2024 Lucid Air Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The Lucid Air delivers exceptional luxury and staggering performance, but as a high-tech modern electric vehicle, prospective buyers should approach with cautious optimism. Owner reports submitted to NHTSA highlight occasional software instability, sudden power loss, and drivetrain hiccups that can disrupt driving. While luxury EVs offer a compelling driving experience, checking for completed software updates and service history is crucial before purchasing.

---

## Engine

### Single-Motor Electric Powertrain

The single-motor rear-wheel-drive setup offers efficient everyday cruising power while maintaining the model's signature luxury driving dynamics. It serves as the baseline powertrain option for the lineup.

### Tri-Motor Electric Powertrain

The high-performance tri-motor setup unleashes extreme power figures exceeding a thousand horsepower for track-capable acceleration. It represents the pinnacle of the vehicle's engineering capabilities.

## Transmission

### Dual-Motor Electric Powertrain

The dual-motor electric powertrain configurations produce robust horsepower and instantaneous torque across all-wheel-drive setups. This high-output system provides world-class acceleration, though early production software and electrical integration have occasionally led to sudden power loss reported by owners.

**Unexpected Powertrain Shutdown**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle displays error messages and suddenly shuts down or loses propulsion while driving, stranding the driver in traffic.
- **Solution:** Requires towing to a service center for diagnosis and software or hardware reset.

### Single-Speed Direct-Drive Transmission

The single-speed direct-drive transmission efficiently transfers electric motor output to the wheels with seamless, clutchless operation. It is standard across all model trims and powertrains.

**Shift-to-Park Refusal**
- **Symptoms:** Following over-the-air software updates, the vehicle may randomly refuse to shift out of Park into Drive or Reverse.
- **Solution:** May require a system reboot or dealer-level software patch to restore normal gear selection functionality.

## Electrical

### Infotainment and Control Screens

The advanced digital cockpit relies heavily on interconnected displays and software modules to control vital vehicle functions. Owner feedback suggests that certain software versions can introduce temporary stability glitches.

**Screen Blackouts and Profile Resets**
- **Symptoms:** Cabin screens repeatedly black out while driving, drop phone calls, and unexpectedly reset driver profiles mid-trip.
- **Solution:** Awaiting further software patches from the manufacturer to stabilize system updates.

### Access and Entry System

Keyless entry and proximity sensing systems manage seamless vehicle access and climate preparation. Delays in response time can occasionally cause inconvenience during daily operation.

**Delayed Vehicle Wake and Unlock**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle takes an excessive amount of time to wake up and unlock when approached by the driver.
- **Solution:** Verify key fob battery strength and check for software updates addressing proximity recognition times.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V076000: Forward Collision Avoidance: Adaptive Cruise Control:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lucid&model=Air&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** The software may cause a fault while the adaptive cruise control is on, resulting in a loss of drive power. A sudden loss of drive power increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 24V495000: Visibility:Defroster/Defogger/Hvac System](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lucid&model=Air&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** A high voltage coolant heater that fails to defrost the windshield can reduce the driver's view, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 24V836000: Electrical System:Propulsion System](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lucid&model=Air&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** A loss of power to the rear drive unit can result in a complete loss of drive power, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Lucid will replace the wiring harness, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V669000: Power Train:Driveline:Driveshaft](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lucid&model=Air&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** A disconnected half shaft can result in a loss of drive power, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 25V670000: Back Over Prevention:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lucid&model=Air&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 111, "Rear Visibility." A rearview image that does not appear or fails to accurately depict behind the vehicle increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 26V017000: Back Over Prevention:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lucid&model=Air&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** The rearview camera image may not display when the vehicle is placed in reverse. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 111, "Rear Visibility." A rearview image that does not display reduces the driver's view behind the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 26V193000: Power Train:Driveline:Driveshaft](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lucid&model=Air&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** A disconnected half shaft can result in a loss of drive power, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 26V309000: Electrical System:Propulsion System:Inverter](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lucid&model=Air&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** A loss of drive power increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** If a failure is detected Lucid will replace the inverter, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

Purchasing a used Lucid Air means buying into cutting-edge electric vehicle technology, which brings both incredible performance and potential software-related growing pains. While powertrain and infotainment complaints highlight areas that require careful inspection, ensuring all factory updates have been applied can mitigate many reported concerns. Buyers should thoroughly test all electronics, locking mechanisms, and powertrain responsiveness during a pre-purchase test drive.
