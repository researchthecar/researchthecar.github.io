# 2023 Lucid Air Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2023 Lucid Air is an impressive, high-performance luxury electric vehicle known for its exceptional range, sleek styling, and advanced technology. As an early-production model from a newer luxury EV maker, prospective used buyers should approach with careful optimism, keeping an eye on potential software glitches, sensor behaviors, and auxiliary electrical components.

---

## Engine

### Permanent Magnet Synchronous Dual-Motor Electric Powertrain

This dual-motor electric setup produces upwards of 480 to over 1,000 horsepower depending on the trim level, delivering instantaneous torque and immense acceleration. As an EV powertrain, it has fewer moving parts than an internal combustion engine, though early software integration requires thorough vetting by buyers.

## Transmission

### Single-Speed Direct-Drive Transmission

The single-speed direct-drive transmission provides seamless power delivery without traditional gear shifts. Overall mechanical durability is high, though software-managed gear selection logic has experienced occasional anomalies.

**Unexpected Directional Behavior**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle occasionally shifted into the forward direction despite reverse being selected, or vice versa, creating potential maneuvering hazards.
- **Solution:** Have the vehicle's powertrain control module and drive selector software updated by an authorized service center.

## Brakes

### Advanced Driver Assistance Systems

Advanced safety and driver-assistance features provide cutting-edge protection but can occasionally exhibit sensitivity or calibration issues.

**Unintended Acceleration Concerns**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have reported brief, unexpected surges in speed or failure to slow down promptly when the brake pedal is depressed in certain traffic scenarios.
- **Solution:** Ensure all ADAS and vehicle control software is up to date, and have diagnostic scans performed if unusual throttle response occurs.

## Electrical

### Electrical and Infotainment Systems

The vehicle relies heavily on integrated digital screens and complex wiring harnesses, making thorough electrical checks essential for a used purchase.

**Dashboard Screen and Camera Failures**
- **Symptoms:** The main dashboard display and rearview camera views occasionally fail or display a warning triangle, leaving drivers without crucial visibility aids.
- **Solution:** Reboot the infotainment system or visit a service center to check for software patches addressing display modules.

## Exterior

### Seats and Door Latches

Cabin appointments are upscale and modern, though early-model trim materials and electronic latches may show signs of wear.

**Interior Trim and Door Latch Inconsistencies**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have experienced premature wear or cracking on leather/vinyl seating surfaces, alongside doors failing to unlatch consistently.
- **Solution:** Inspect seat upholstery condition carefully before purchase and have dealer technicians lubricate or adjust electronic door latches.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V110000: Electrical System:Propulsion System:Fuses, Relays, Contacts, And Shunts](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lucid&model=Air&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** The electrically activated contact switches that transfer energy to the drive motors, may unexpectedly open, cutting off power to the electric motors. Power loss will shut down electric motors, causing a loss of drive power and increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Lucid Service Centers will update the vehicle software and replace the contactor switches, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V520000: Back Over Prevention:Display Function](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lucid&model=Air&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 111, "Rear Visibility." A rearview camera image that does not display while in reverse decreases the driver's visibility, increasing the risk of injury or crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 23V521000: Visibility/Wiper](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lucid&model=Air&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 104, "Windshield Wiping and Washing Systems." Inoperative windshield wipers can reduce visibility, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Lucid Service Centers will inspect, tighten the wiper arm nuts, and replace damaged parts as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V523000: Electrical System:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lucid&model=Air&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** Software versions 2.0.64 and older may not respond properly to an inverter power module failure, which can result in a loss of drive power without warning. A loss of drive power without warning increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 23V708000: Seats:Mid/Rear Assembly:Seat Heater/Cooler](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lucid&model=Air&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** Under certain conditions, temperature monitoring for the rear seat heaters could become inoperative and result in the rear seats overheating. Overheated rear seats can increase the risk of a burn injury.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 24V011000: Visibility:Defroster/Defogger/Hvac System](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lucid&model=Air&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** A high voltage coolant heater that fails to defrost the windshield can reduce the driver's view, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Lucid will update the software to identify a high voltage coolant heater failure and provide a warning to the driver. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V076000: Forward Collision Avoidance: Adaptive Cruise Control:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lucid&model=Air&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** The software may cause a fault while the adaptive cruise control is on, resulting in a loss of drive power. A sudden loss of drive power increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 24V497000: Electrical System:Propulsion System:Traction Battery Thermal:Management:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Lucid&model=Air&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** When a hardware communication fault is detected, the High Voltage Interlock (HVIL) software could disconnect the high voltage contactors while the vehicle is in motion, causing a loss of drive power. A loss of drive power increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2023 Lucid Air offers unmatched luxury EV credentials, but its status as an early model year means buyers should prioritize comprehensive pre-purchase inspections. Ensure all software recalls and updates have been completed by the previous owner to minimize electronic or drive-logic quirks.
