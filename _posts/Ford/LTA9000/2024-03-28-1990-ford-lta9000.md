**Introduction**

The 1990 Ford LTA9000 is a heavy-duty pickup truck offered with a variety of engine and transmission options. It is powered by either a 7.5L V8 gasoline engine or a 7.3L V8 diesel engine, paired with either a 5-speed manual transmission or a 4-speed automatic transmission. The LTA9000 has a reputation for durability, towing capacity, and off-road capabilities. However, it has also been known to experience certain issues.

**Engine**

* **7.5L V8 Gasoline Engine**
    * **Valve Cover Gasket Leak:** Oil leaks from the valve cover gaskets, causing a burning oil smell and reduced oil levels.
    * Solution: Replace the valve cover gaskets.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
* **7.3L V8 Diesel Engine**
    * **Fuel Injector Failure:** Fuel injectors can fail, causing rough idling, poor fuel economy, and decreased engine power.
    * Solution: Replace the failed fuel injectors.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600

**Transmission**

* **5-Speed Manual Transmission**
    * **Synchronizer Wear:** Synchronizers can wear out, causing difficulty shifting gears.
    * Solution: Replace the worn synchronizers.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $600 - $1,000
* **4-Speed Automatic Transmission**
    * **Torque Converter Failure:** The torque converter can fail, causing slipping gears and loss of power.
    * Solution: Replace the torque converter.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000

**Suspension**

* **Front Suspension:**
    * **Ball Joint Wear:** Ball joints can wear out, causing clunking noises, reduced steering precision, and increased tire wear.
    * Solution: Replace the worn ball joints.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400 per joint
* **Rear Suspension:**
    * **Leaf Spring Failure:** Leaf springs can break, causing the rear of the vehicle to sag and affect handling.
    * Solution: Replace the broken leaf springs.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000 per spring

**Electrical**

* **Alternator Failure:** The alternator can fail, resulting in charging system issues and a dead battery.
    * Solution: Replace the alternator.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600
* **Battery Drain:** The battery can drain prematurely due to parasitic electrical draw from faulty components or accessories.
    * Solution: Diagnose and repair the source of the electrical draw.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+

**Exterior**

* **Headlight Lens Fogging:** The headlight lenses can fog up, reducing visibility at night.
    * Solution: Replace the fogged lenses or restore the original lenses using a restoration kit.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200 per lens
* **Door Handle Corrosion:** The door handles can corrode, making them difficult to operate.
    * Solution: Replace the corroded door handles.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $100 per handle

**Interior**

* **Dash Panel Cracking:** The dash panel can crack over time due to sun exposure and age.
    * Solution: Replace the dash panel.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
* **Window Regulator Failure:** The window regulators can fail, preventing the windows from rolling up or down.
    * Solution: Replace the failed window regulators.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400 per window

**Summary**

The 1990 Ford LTA9000 is a capable truck, but it is not without its issues. Common problems include valve cover leaks, fuel injector failure, suspension component wear, electrical issues, headlight lens fogging, and interior components failing. While these issues can be costly to repair, the severity and frequency of these problems vary depending on the specific component and the condition of the vehicle. Regular maintenance and proper care can help minimize the risk of experiencing these issues.
