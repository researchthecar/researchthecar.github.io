The 2018 Ford Police Interceptor Utility is a specialized version of the Ford Explorer designed for law enforcement use. It's known for its durability, spacious interior, and powerful engine options. Popular engine choices include the 3.7L Ti-VCT V6 (304 hp and 279 lb-ft of torque) and the 3.5L EcoBoost V6 (365 hp and 350 lb-ft of torque). Both engines were paired with a 6-speed automatic transmission. Owners often appreciate the vehicle's robust performance and cargo capacity, while common complaints involve fuel economy and occasional maintenance issues related to the heavy-duty use it often endures.

**Engine**

**3.5L EcoBoost V6**

*   **Turbocharger Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Whining noise from the turbocharger, loss of power, check engine light.
    *   Solution: Turbocharger replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $2000 - $3000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**3.7L Ti-VCT V6**

*   **Water Pump Failure (Internal)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaking from the front of the engine, overheating, engine damage. The water pump is located inside the engine.
    *   Solution: Engine disassembly and water pump replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1500 - $3000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

*   **6-Speed Automatic Transmission**

    *   **Harsh Shifting/Slipping**
        *   Description/Symptoms: Rough or delayed shifts, slipping between gears, transmission warning light.
        *   Solution: Transmission fluid flush, valve body replacement or transmission rebuild/replacement.
        *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $4,000+
        *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Rear Suspension Sagging/Weak Springs**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rear of the vehicle sitting lower than normal, reduced ride quality, bottoming out.
    *   Solution: Replacement of rear springs.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Front Wheel Bearing Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Grinding or humming noise from the wheel area, excessive play in the wheel.
    *   Solution: Wheel bearing replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Wiring Harness Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Intermittent electrical problems, malfunctioning lights, issues with the infotainment system, phantom drains on the battery. Often caused by added police equipment and modifications that damage factory wiring.
    *   Solution: Inspection and repair/replacement of damaged wiring harnesses.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Alternator Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Battery not charging, dimming lights, vehicle stalling.
    *   Solution: Alternator replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Door Hinge Wear/Sagging**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Doors sagging, difficult to close, unusual noises when opening/closing.
    *   Solution: Door hinge replacement or adjustment.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **HVAC Blower Motor Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: No air blowing from vents, weak airflow, noisy blower motor.
    *   Solution: Blower motor replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2018 Ford Police Interceptor Utility has a mix of potential issues stemming from its heavy-duty usage. Engine problems, particularly turbocharger failures on the EcoBoost and water pump failures on the Ti-VCT, can be costly. Transmission issues like harsh shifting are also a concern. Suspension wear and electrical problems, especially those related to wiring, are not uncommon. While some repairs are relatively inexpensive, major engine or transmission work can be significant. Buyers should thoroughly inspect the vehicle and check service records before purchasing to identify any existing or potential problems.
