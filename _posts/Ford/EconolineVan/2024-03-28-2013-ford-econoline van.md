**Introduction:**

The 2013 Ford Econoline Van is a full-size cargo or passenger van. It is available with two engine options: a 4.6L V8 engine and a 5.4L V8 engine. Both engines are paired with a 6-speed automatic transmission. The Econoline Van is known for its spacious interior, powerful engines, and versatility. However, it has also been the subject of several complaints and recalls related to various issues.

**Engine**

**4.6L V8 Engine**

* **Intake Manifold Failure:** The intake manifold may crack or warp, causing coolant or oil leaks. This can lead to overheating or engine damage.
    * Solution: Replace intake manifold with updated version.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,200 - $2,000+
    * Recalls: 13V255000
* **Cam Phaser Failure:** The camshaft phaser may fail, causing irregular engine idle, decreased fuel economy, and engine noise.
    * Solution: Replace camshaft phaser and related components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
    * Recalls: 10V159000

**5.4L V8 Engine**

* **Spark Plug Failure:** The spark plugs may fail prematurely, causing engine misfires, reduced power, and increased fuel consumption.
    * Solution: Replace spark plugs and ignition coils.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Oil Leak from Cam Cover:** The camshaft cover may leak oil, resulting in low oil levels and potential engine damage.
    * Solution: Replace camshaft cover gasket.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**6-Speed Automatic Transmission**

* **Transmission Slipping:** The transmission may slip or hesitate during gear changes, causing erratic shifting and reduced power.
    * Solution: Rebuild or replace transmission.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,500 - $5,000+
    * Recalls: 12V561000
* **Torque Converter Failure:** The torque converter may fail, resulting in transmission shudder, noisy operation, and decreased vehicle performance.
    * Solution: Replace torque converter.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,200 - $1,800+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Ball Joint Wear:** The ball joints in the front suspension may wear prematurely, causing loose steering, vibration, and increased tire wear.
    * Solution: Replace ball joints.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $600+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Rear Leaf Spring Failure:** The rear leaf springs may sag or break, causing poor handling, decreased ride height, and uneven tire wear.
    * Solution: Replace rear leaf springs.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $600 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Electrical Harness Issues:** The electrical harness may experience short circuits or loose connections, leading to electrical problems such as flickering lights, dead batteries, and electrical system malfunctions.
    * Solution: Repair or replace faulty wiring and connectors.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Alternator Failure:** The alternator may fail to charge the battery, causing electrical problems and potential engine stalling.
    * Solution: Replace alternator.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,200+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Rust and Corrosion:** The body panels and underbody components of the Econoline Van are prone to rust and corrosion, especially in areas where salt is used on roads.
    * Solution: Apply anti-rust coatings and perform regular maintenance to prevent corrosion.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on severity of corrosion.
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Door Lock Problems:** The door locks may fail to operate properly, causing doors to stick or not lock securely.
    * Solution: Repair or replace door lock mechanisms.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Climate Control Malfunctions:** The climate control system may malfunction, resulting in inadequate heating or cooling, and excessive noise.
    * Solution: Diagnose and repair faulty components (e.g., blend door actuator, blower motor).
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Seat Adjustment Issues:** The seat adjustment mechanisms may fail, making it difficult to adjust the seats to a comfortable position.
    * Solution: Repair or replace seat adjustment components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

The 2013 Ford Econoline Van has received mixed reviews. While it offers spaciousness and powerful engines, it has also been plagued by various issues that can affect its reliability and safety. The engine and transmission problems, in particular, can be costly to repair. The suspension, electrical, exterior, and interior components have also experienced problems to varying degrees of severity. Potential buyers should carefully consider the potential costs associated with these issues before purchasing this vehicle.
