**Introduction:**

The 2007 Ford Freestar is a minivan that was available with a variety of engine and transmission options. Some of the most popular engine options included the 3.9L V6 and the 4.2L V6. Transmission options included a 6-speed automatic transmission and a Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT). The Freestar was praised for its spacious interior, comfortable ride, and reasonable fuel economy. However, it was also criticized for its lack of power, poor handling, and uninspired design.

**Engine**

**3.9L V6**

* **Oil leaks:** The 3.9L V6 is known to develop oil leaks from the valve covers, oil pan, and rear main seal. Symptoms of an oil leak include a visible puddle of oil under the vehicle, low oil levels on the dipstick, and a burning oil smell. The solution to an oil leak is to replace the leaking gasket or seal. The estimated cost to fix an oil leak is $200 - $500.
* **Intake manifold gasket failure:** The intake manifold gasket can fail on the 3.9L V6, causing a vacuum leak. Symptoms of an intake manifold gasket failure include a rough idle, loss of power, and poor fuel economy. The solution to an intake manifold gasket failure is to replace the gasket. The estimated cost to fix an intake manifold gasket failure is $300 - $600.

**4.2L V6**

* **Spark plug failure:** The 4.2L V6 is known to have issues with spark plug failure. Symptoms of spark plug failure include a rough idle, misfires, and poor acceleration. The solution to spark plug failure is to replace the spark plugs. The estimated cost to fix spark plug failure is $100 - $200.
* **Timing chain failure:** The 4.2L V6 can also experience timing chain failure. Symptoms of timing chain failure include a loud rattling noise from the engine, a loss of power, and eventually engine failure. The solution to timing chain failure is to replace the timing chain. The estimated cost to fix timing chain failure is $1,000 - $2,000.

**Transmission**

**6-speed automatic transmission**

* **Transmission slipping:** The 6-speed automatic transmission can experience transmission slipping, which is a condition where the transmission does not engage properly and the vehicle loses power. Symptoms of transmission slipping include a loss of power, a delayed response when shifting gears, and a burning smell. The solution to transmission slipping is to replace the transmission. The estimated cost to fix transmission slipping is $2,000 - $4,000.

**CVT**

* **Transmission shuddering:** The CVT can experience transmission shuddering, which is a condition where the transmission vibrates excessively. Symptoms of transmission shuddering include a vibration that is felt through the steering wheel and the seat, and a loss of power. The solution to transmission shuddering is to replace the CVT. The estimated cost to fix transmission shuddering is $2,500 - $4,000.

**Suspension**

* **Front strut failure:** The front struts can fail on the Freestar, causing the vehicle to ride rough and handle poorly. Symptoms of front strut failure include a clunking noise from the front of the vehicle, excessive tire wear, and a decrease in ride height. The solution to front strut failure is to replace the struts. The estimated cost to fix front strut failure is $500 - $800.
* **Rear shock failure:** The rear shocks can also fail on the Freestar, causing the vehicle to ride rough and handle poorly. Symptoms of rear shock failure include a clunking noise from the rear of the vehicle, excessive tire wear, and a decrease in ride height. The solution to rear shock failure is to replace the shocks. The estimated cost to fix rear shock failure is $300 - $600.

**Electrical**

* **Electrical system failure:** The Freestar can experience electrical system failure, which can cause a variety of problems, including the inability to start the vehicle, the failure of the lights or gauges, and the loss of power steering or brakes. Symptoms of electrical system failure include a dead battery, a flickering or dimming of the lights, and a loss of power to the vehicle. The solution to electrical system failure is to diagnose and repair the faulty electrical component. The estimated cost to fix electrical system failure can vary depending on the severity of the problem.

**Exterior**

* **Body panel rust:** The Freestar can experience body panel rust, which is a condition where the metal panels of the vehicle begin to rust and corrode. Symptoms of body panel rust include visible rust on the body panels, peeling paint, and a weakened body structure. The solution to body panel rust is to replace the rusted panels. The estimated cost to fix body panel rust can vary depending on the severity of the rust.

**Interior**

* **Dashboard cracking:** The dashboard of the Freestar can crack, which can be unsightly and may affect the operation of the vehicle. Symptoms of dashboard cracking include visible cracks in the dashboard, a loose or rattling dashboard, and a loss of functionality of the dashboard controls. The solution to dashboard cracking is to replace the dashboard. The estimated cost to fix dashboard cracking is $500 - $1,000.
* **Door lock failure:** The door locks of the Freestar can fail, which can make it difficult to enter or exit the vehicle. Symptoms of door lock failure include a door that cannot be opened or closed, a door that locks or unlocks unexpectedly, and a door that does not lock or unlock with the key fob. The solution to door lock failure is to replace the door lock actuator. The estimated cost to fix door lock failure is $200 - $400.

**Summary**

The 2007 Ford Freestar is a minivan that has a number of potential issues. Some of the more severe issues, such as transmission failure and timing chain failure, can be expensive to fix. However, many of the other issues, such as oil leaks and electrical system failure, are relatively minor and can be fixed for a reasonable cost. Overall, the Freestar is a reliable vehicle that offers a good value for the price.
