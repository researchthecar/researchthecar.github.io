**Introduction**

The 2013 Ford F650 is a heavy-duty pickup truck available in various configurations and drivetrain options. It features a powerful engine lineup and a rugged chassis. Despite its capabilities, some owners have reported issues with specific components.

The F650 is offered with the following engine and transmission options:

* 6.7L Power Stroke V8 Turbo Diesel (390 hp, 735 lb-ft torque)
* 6.7L Power Stroke V8 Turbo Diesel (440 hp, 860 lb-ft torque)
* 6-speed automatic transmission
* 10-speed automatic transmission

Owners generally appreciate the F650's hauling and towing abilities, robust construction, and comfortable interior. However, they have expressed concerns about some reliability issues.

**Engine**

**6.7L Power Stroke V8 Turbo Diesel (390 hp)**

* **EGR Cooler Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaks and loss, overheating, reduced power
    * Solution: Replace EGR cooler
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Head Gasket Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: White smoke from exhaust, overheating, coolant leaks
    * Solution: Replace head gaskets
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000
    * Recalls: None Found

**6.7L Power Stroke V8 Turbo Diesel (440 hp)**

* **Turbodiesel Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, black smoke from exhaust, turbocharger noise
    * Solution: Replace turbocharger
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,000
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**6-Speed Automatic**

* **Transmission Slippage:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears, delayed engagement, loss of power
    * Solution: Rebuild or replace transmission
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $3,000 - $5,000
    * Recalls: NHTSA Campaign Number: 14V497000

**10-Speed Automatic**

* **No Issues Found**

**Suspension**

* **Leaf Spring Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Sagging suspension, rough ride, noise
    * Solution: Replace leaf springs
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Alternator Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Battery not charging, warning lights, dimming headlights
    * Solution: Replace alternator
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Rust Prone Bed:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Rust forming on the truck bed
    * Solution: Replace or treat rusted areas
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Climate Control Issues:**
    * Description/Symptoms: HVAC system not working properly, temperature fluctuations
    * Solution: Repair or replace climate control module
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2013 Ford F650 has received mixed reviews regarding reliability. Some owners have experienced significant issues with the engine, transmission, and electrical systems. These issues can be costly to repair, but recalls have been issued for some concerns, such as transmission slippage. Other issues, such as rust-prone bed and climate control problems, are less severe but can still be frustrating for owners. While the F650 offers impressive capabilities, potential buyers should be aware of the potential issues associated with this vehicle before making a decision.
