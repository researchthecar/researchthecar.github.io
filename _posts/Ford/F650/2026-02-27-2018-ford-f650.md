# 2018 Ford F650 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2018 Ford F-650 is a heavy-duty commercial truck built for demanding work environments, typically powered by robust diesel or gasoline powertrains. Because these trucks often see heavy commercial duty, buyers should inspect the chassis, powertrain, and electrical systems thoroughly. While owner-reported complaints for this model year are relatively sparse, reviewing specific engine and transmission options helps ensure the truck matches your hauling requirements.

---

## Engine

### 6.7L Power Stroke V8 Turbo Diesel

This turbocharged diesel engine delivers high horsepower and heavy torque outputs tailored for commercial towing and hauling. It is generally robust when properly maintained, though buyers should verify service records for emissions and fuel system upkeep.

### 6.8L Triton V10 Gasoline

A large-displacement gasoline V8/V10 option designed for medium-duty applications requiring simpler maintenance than a diesel. It offers dependable operation for fleet use, though fuel economy is lower compared to its diesel counterpart.

## Transmission

### TorqShift 6-Speed Automatic

A heavy-duty automatic transmission engineered to handle high gross combined weight ratings in commercial settings. It includes tow/haul modes to manage heavy loads effectively.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V237000: Exterior Lighting:Brake Lights](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=F650&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** If the brake lights incorrectly illuminate, it can confuse other drivers, possibly increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Curbtender will notify owners and dealers will replace the brake lights, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

When shopping for a used 2018 Ford F-650, prioritize a comprehensive pre-purchase inspection focusing on the commercial powertrain, brakes, and frame condition. Reviewing maintenance logs is essential given the heavy-duty nature of these vehicles. Overall, a well-maintained F-650 can serve as a dependable backbone for commercial fleets.
