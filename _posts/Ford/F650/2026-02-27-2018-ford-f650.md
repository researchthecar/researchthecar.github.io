# 2018 Ford F650

### Overview
The 2018 Ford F-650 is a heavy-duty commercial truck designed for demanding vocational applications. Reliability for this model is generally considered robust, particularly concerning its powertrain, which is built for durability under significant load. Common issues, when they arise, tend to be related to specific component failures rather than widespread design flaws. Owners and fleet managers can expect a dependable workhorse, but as with any commercial vehicle, proactive maintenance and awareness of potential problem areas are key to maximizing uptime and minimizing unexpected repair costs. This analysis focuses on documented technical issues beyond routine maintenance.

## Engine

### 6.7L Power Stroke V8 Diesel
**EGR Cooler Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Coolant loss, white smoke from exhaust, overheating.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the EGR cooler assembly.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1500

**Fuel Injector Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Rough idle, loss of power, increased exhaust smoke, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of faulty fuel injectors.
- **Cost:** $1000 - $2000

### Summary Verdict
The 2018 Ford F-650 demonstrates strong reliability, particularly in its core powertrain components when properly maintained. The 6.7L Power Stroke diesel, while generally robust, has a few documented potential issues like EGR cooler leaks and fuel injector problems that can arise under heavy use. The 7.3L gasoline engine and the available transmissions are noted for their durability with no significant widespread issues reported. Electrical components, specifically the alternator, can be a point of failure. Suspension, exterior, and interior components are typically reliable for this class of vehicle. Overall, the F-650 is a dependable choice for commercial applications, with most issues being repairable and not indicative of systemic flaws.
