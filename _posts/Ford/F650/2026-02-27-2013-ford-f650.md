# 2013 Ford F650

### Overview
The 2013 Ford F650 is a heavy-duty commercial truck designed for demanding vocational applications. Reliability for this model year is generally considered robust, particularly for its core powertrain components, which are built for durability and high mileage. However, as with any commercial vehicle, specific issues can arise depending on usage, maintenance, and the particular configuration. This analysis focuses on documented technical reliability concerns beyond standard wear and tear, providing insights into potential problem areas and their associated costs for common engine and transmission options.

---


## Engine

### 6.7L Power Stroke V8 Turbo Diesel
**EGR Cooler Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Coolant loss, white smoke from exhaust, overheating, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the EGR cooler assembly.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1500

**Fuel Injector Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Rough idle, loss of power, increased fuel consumption, black smoke.
- **Solution:** Replacement of faulty fuel injectors.
- **Cost:** $1200 - $2500

## Electrical

### Standard Electrical System
**Alternator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Dimming lights, battery warning light, starting issues, dead battery.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the alternator.
- **Cost:** $400 - $800

**Wiring Harness Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Intermittent electrical faults, corrosion, short circuits.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of damaged sections of the wiring harness.
- **Cost:** $300 - $900


## Exterior

### Body Panels and Frame
**Corrosion on Frame Rails**
- **Symptoms:** Rust and degradation of the frame, particularly in areas exposed to salt and moisture.
- **Solution:** Frame repair, rust treatment, or in severe cases, frame replacement.
- **Cost:** $500 - $3000+

### Summary Verdict
The 2013 Ford F650 demonstrates solid reliability for its intended commercial use, with its heavy-duty diesel engines and robust transmissions being the primary strengths. The 6.7L Power Stroke engine, while powerful, has a few documented issues like EGR cooler and injector problems that can be costly to repair. The Caterpillar C7 engine option is known for its durability. Transmission options, particularly the Allison automatics, are generally trouble-free. Electrical components like alternators and wiring harnesses can be points of failure, as can frame corrosion in harsh environments. Overall, with proper maintenance and addressing potential issues promptly, the F650 can be a dependable workhorse.
