# 2006 Ford GT

### Overview
The 2006 Ford GT stands as a modern supercar icon, celebrated for its performance and retro styling. While generally considered a robust and well-engineered vehicle, like any high-performance machine, it has specific areas that warrant attention from an ownership perspective. This analysis focuses on documented technical reliability, excluding routine maintenance and common wear items. The GT's relatively low production numbers mean that some issues might be less widely reported than on mass-produced vehicles, but common themes have emerged among owners and specialists. Understanding these potential points of concern can help prospective and current owners manage expectations and maintenance costs.

---


## Engine

### 5.4L Supercharged V8
**Supercharger Belt Slippage**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of boost, whining noise from the engine bay, reduced engine power.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the supercharger belt and potentially tensioner pulley.
- **Cost:** $200 - $500

**Coolant Leaks (Intercooler System)**
- **Symptoms:** Visible coolant leaks around the intercooler plumbing, potential for overheating.
- **Solution:** Inspection and replacement of hoses, clamps, or intercooler seals.
- **Cost:** $300 - $700


## Transmission

### Ricardo 6-Speed Manual
**Clutch Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Slipping clutch, difficulty shifting gears, burning smell.
- **Solution:** Clutch assembly replacement (clutch disc, pressure plate, release bearing).
- **Cost:** $1,500 - $3,000

**Synchronizer Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Grinding noise when shifting into certain gears, difficulty engaging gears.
- **Solution:** Transmission internal repair or rebuild, focusing on worn synchronizer rings.
- **Cost:** $2,000 - $4,500

### Summary Verdict
The 2006 Ford GT is a remarkably reliable supercar, especially considering its performance capabilities and specialized nature. The primary areas of concern tend to be related to the high-stress components of the powertrain, such as the supercharger belt and clutch wear, which are not uncommon for vehicles of this caliber. Electrical gremlins, often linked to the Body Control Module, can also arise but are typically manageable. Overall, the GT's engineering is sound, and with proper maintenance and attention to its specific needs, it remains a dependable exotic. Owners should budget for potential clutch replacements and be aware of the supercharger system's requirements.
