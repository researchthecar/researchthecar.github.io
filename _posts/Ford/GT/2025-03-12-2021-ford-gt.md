Here's a breakdown of common issues and complaints for the 2021 Ford GT, formatted for your website.

The 2021 Ford GT is a mid-engine, two-seater sports car representing Ford's performance pinnacle. It is available exclusively with a 3.5-liter EcoBoost V6 engine producing 660 horsepower and 550 lb-ft of torque. Power is delivered through a seven-speed dual-clutch transmission. Praised for its exceptional handling, aerodynamic design, and exclusivity, the GT also faces criticism regarding its high price, limited availability, and compromises in everyday usability.

**Engine**

**3.5L EcoBoost V6**

*   **Turbocharger Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Whining noise from the engine bay, reduced power, check engine light illuminated. This can be caused by bearing failure within the turbocharger or issues with the wastegate actuator.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the affected turbocharger unit.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $5,000 - $8,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Coolant Intrusion**
    *   Description/Symptoms: White smoke from exhaust, coolant loss, engine misfire.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the affected engine.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $25,000 - $40,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Cracked Exhaust Manifolds**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Ticking noise from the engine, especially when cold, exhaust fumes detected near the engine bay.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the cracked exhaust manifold(s).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,500 - $5,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**7-Speed Dual-Clutch Transmission**

*   **Harsh Shifting**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Jerky or abrupt shifts, especially at low speeds or during downshifts. This can be due to calibration issues or wear in the clutch packs.
    *   Solution: Transmission software reflash/recalibration. Replacement of clutch packs or mechatronic unit in severe cases.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $10,000+ (depending on the repair)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Transmission Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Complete transmission failure.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the affected transmission unit.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $25,000 - $35,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Hydraulic Suspension Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Uneven ride height, failure to adjust ride height, error messages related to the suspension system. The GT utilizes a complex hydraulic suspension system, and problems can arise with the hydraulic pumps, lines, or actuators.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis and repair/replacement of the faulty component within the hydraulic suspension system.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $3,000 - $10,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Freezing/Crashing**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The infotainment screen freezes, becomes unresponsive, or crashes intermittently.
    *   Solution: Software update. Replacement of the infotainment unit in severe cases.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 - $2,000+ (depending on warranty coverage)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Sensor Malfunctions**
    *   Description/Symptoms: False warnings, malfunctioning driver-assistance features, or issues with engine performance due to sensor failures.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis and replacement of the faulty sensor(s).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+ (per sensor)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Delamination of Carbon Fiber Body Panels**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Separation or bubbling of the clear coat or outer layer of the carbon fiber panels. This can be caused by UV exposure or manufacturing defects.
    *   Solution: Repair or replacement of the affected carbon fiber panel.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $5,000 - $20,000+ (depending on the extent of the damage and the panel affected)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Air Conditioning Malfunctions**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Inconsistent cooling, failure to blow cold air, or unusual noises from the A/C system.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis and repair/replacement of the faulty A/C component (compressor, condenser, evaporator, etc.).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $3,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2021 Ford GT, while a high-performance marvel, is not immune to issues. The most significant concerns revolve around the engine (particularly turbocharger and coolant issues) and transmission, potentially leading to very high repair costs. Electrical gremlins and carbon fiber body panel issues are also notable. While most issues are not widespread, the potential severity and expense associated with them warrant careful consideration for prospective buyers.
