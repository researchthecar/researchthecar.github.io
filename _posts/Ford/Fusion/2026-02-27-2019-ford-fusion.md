# 2019 Ford Fusion Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2019 Ford Fusion offers a comfortable ride, intuitive tech, and a handsome exterior styling package, making it a popular choice in the midsize sedan market. However, prospective buyers should look closely at maintenance history, particularly regarding engine durability and powertrain operation. While many owners enjoy trouble-free daily commuting, a notable portion of driver complaints centers around significant mechanical issues that can prove costly if outside warranty coverage.

---

## Engine

### 1.5L EcoBoost 4-Cylinder

The 1.5-liter turbocharged inline-4 engine produces around 181 horsepower and emphasizes fuel efficiency. Owners should inspect this option carefully for signs of fluid loss or overheating.

**Coolant Intrusion**
- **Symptoms:** Low coolant levels, white smoke from the exhaust, engine misfires, rough running, and illuminated check engine lights.
- **Solution:** May require professional diagnosis and potentially a full engine block or cylinder head replacement depending on the severity of the damage.

### 2.0L EcoBoost 4-Cylinder

The 2.0-liter turbocharged inline-4 puts out approximately 245 horsepower, providing lively acceleration and standard all-wheel drive on select trims. It is generally robust but shares some vulnerability to cooling system issues.

**Coolant Leaks and Engine Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** Unexplained loss of coolant, sudden engine stalling, rough idling, and illuminated warning indicators.
- **Solution:** Inspect cooling lines and gaskets for leaks; replace faulty components or damaged seals as needed.

### 2.5L Duratec 4-Cylinder

The naturally aspirated 2.5-liter 4-cylinder engine generates roughly 175 horsepower, offering a simpler mechanical design with fewer turbo-related complications for daily drivers.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

The standard 6-speed automatic transmission paired with select engines provides smooth gear changes, though owners have reported occasional linkage and internal component durability hurdles.

**Shift Cable Bushing Defect**
- **Symptoms:** Inability to shift gears properly, the shifter feeling loose, or the car failing to register that it has been placed in park.
- **Solution:** Inspect the shift cable and replace degraded or broken plastic bushings with updated reinforcement parts.

**Torque Converter Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Premature gear engagement issues, stuck shift solenoids, or sudden loss of drive power at highway speeds.
- **Solution:** Requires professional transmission inspection and potential torque converter or internal component replacement.

## Electrical

### Infotainment and Backup Camera

Cabin electronics generally deliver solid connectivity features, but intermittent glitches in display screens and backup cameras remain common complaints.

**Backup Camera Failure or Freezing**
- **Symptoms:** Rearview camera display turns solid blue, freezes during operation, or shows a black screen with an 'unavailable' warning message.
- **Solution:** Software updates, module resets, or replacement of the rearview camera assembly may be required.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V440000: Electrical System:Propulsion System:Traction Battery:Management System/Energy Control Module (Bms/Becm)](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Fusion&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** The battery energy control module (BECM) may become damaged due to excessive voltage and current flow. A damaged BECM can result in a loss of drive power or fire, increasing the risk of a crash or injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the battery and update the BECM software for free, or Ford will repurchase the vehicle. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V442000: Back Over Prevention:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Fusion&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** A software error may cause the rearview camera to display a blank image, or the image may remain on the display after the backing event has ended. Both of these situations can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the rearview camera software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V685000: Equipment:Electrical:Engine Block Heater](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Fusion&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** The engine block heater may crack and develop a coolant leak, causing it to short circuit when the block heater is plugged in. An electrical short circuit can increase the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the block heater, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

When shopping for a used Ford Fusion, prospective buyers should prioritize models with comprehensive service records and verify that engine cooling and transmission shift linkage systems operate correctly. Arranging a pre-purchase inspection by an independent mechanic is strongly advised to catch potential fluid leaks or hidden mechanical wear before finalizing your purchase.
