# 2012 Ford Fusion

### Overview
The 2012 Ford Fusion is a mid-size sedan that offered a range of powertrain options, including efficient four-cylinder engines and a more powerful V6. It was generally well-regarded for its comfortable ride, spacious interior, and decent fuel economy. Reliability for the 2012 model year is considered good to very good, with most owners reporting few significant mechanical issues outside of routine maintenance. Common areas of concern, though not widespread, tend to be related to specific electronic components or minor interior wear. Overall, the Fusion presented itself as a dependable choice in its segment for 2012.

### General Electrical System
**Infotainment System Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Occasional freezing or unresponsiveness of the MyFord Touch system.
- **Solution:** Software updates or system reset.
- **Cost:** $0 - $150

**Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Premature battery failure or intermittent electrical issues possibly linked to parasitic draw.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and repair of parasitic draw, battery replacement.
- **Cost:** $150 - $400

### Summary Verdict
The 2012 Ford Fusion is a solid choice for a used mid-size sedan, offering good overall reliability. While most owners experience trouble-free operation, potential buyers should be aware of minor electrical quirks, particularly with the infotainment system, and the possibility of premature battery drain. Interior wear on the driver's seat is also a common cosmetic concern. These issues are generally not deal-breakers and are often manageable with reasonable repair costs. With its range of engine options and comfortable ride, the 2012 Fusion remains a dependable and practical vehicle.
