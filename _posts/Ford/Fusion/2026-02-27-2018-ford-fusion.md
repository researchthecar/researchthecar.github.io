# 2018 Ford Fusion Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2018 Ford Fusion is a stylish and practical midsize sedan offering comfortable ride quality, a quiet cabin, and a broad lineup of engines ranging from fuel-efficient EcoBoost options to hybrids. However, prospective buyers should exercise caution when evaluating certain model years, as the Fusion has developed a reputation for some severe, costly mechanical issues that require careful pre-purchase inspection.

---

## Engine

### 1.5L EcoBoost 4-Cylinder

A turbocharged 1.5-liter four-cylinder engine making roughly 181 horsepower, popular for its balance of fuel economy and pep. Unfortunately, this engine is heavily scrutinized for a severe coolant intrusion defect that can cause premature failure.

**Coolant Intrusion**
- **Symptoms:** Coolant leaks into the cylinders, leading to engine misfires, white exhaust smoke, overheating, and eventual catastrophic engine failure.
- **Solution:** Replacing the engine block or installing a complete replacement engine.
- **Cost:** $12,185.28

### 2.0L EcoBoost 4-Cylinder

A stronger turbocharged 2.0-liter four-cylinder engine producing about 240 horsepower, providing peppy acceleration and responsive handling. While generally more robust than the 1.5L, it can still experience cooling system and gasket concerns.

**Coolant Leaks and Misfires**
- **Symptoms:** Low coolant levels, illumination of the check engine light, rough idle, and engine hesitation or stalling.
- **Solution:** Inspect cooling system components, hoses, and head gaskets; replace damaged parts as necessary.

### 2.5L Duratec 4-Cylinder

A naturally aspirated 2.5-liter four-cylinder engine pushing roughly 175 horsepower, valued for its simpler design compared to the complex EcoBoost turbos. It serves as the base powerplant for lower trims.

**Engine Block Cracking and Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** Unexpected engine stalling, coolant loss, and illuminated warning indicators.
- **Solution:** Professional diagnostic testing and engine block or component replacement.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

A standard 6-speed automatic transmission paired with select gas engines, offering conventional shifting dynamics. It is generally dependable, though linkage and shifter mechanism complaints occasionally surface.

**Shifter and Park Position Defects**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle fails to recognize when it is placed in Park, displays warning messages like 'Transmission Not In Park', or locks up and prevents gear selection.
- **Solution:** Inspect the shifter cable bushing and linkage components; replace worn or failed parts.

## Suspension

### Wheels and Lug Nuts

Components securing the wheels to the vehicle hub, prone to a widely documented Ford aesthetic cap swelling issue.

**Swollen Lug Nuts**
- **Symptoms:** Outer metal caps on factory lug nuts expand or distort, preventing standard tire irons and sockets from fitting during a flat tire change.
- **Solution:** Replace the factory two-piece lug nuts with solid aftermarket alternatives.
- **Cost:** $120

## Steering

### Power Steering System

Electric power-assisted steering system providing light effort at low speeds and firmer feedback on highways.

**Electric Power Steering Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Sudden loss of power steering assistance, making the steering wheel extremely heavy and difficult to turn, often accompanied by warning lights.
- **Solution:** Diagnose the electronic steering module and wiring harness; replace failed steering gear assemblies if necessary.

## Electrical

### Back-Over Prevention Camera

Rearview camera system designed to assist drivers with visibility when reversing.

**Backup Camera Display Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The infotainment display shows a completely black screen or freezes on a static image when shifting into reverse.
- **Solution:** Inspect camera wiring connections, software modules, or replace the backup camera unit.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V167000: Steering: Steering Wheel/Handle Bar](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Fusion&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** A steering wheel detachment can cause a loss of steering control, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will replace the steering wheel bolt with a longer bolt, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V162000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Foundation Components:Hoses, Lines/Piping, And Fittings](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Fusion&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** A brake fluid leak will increase brake pedal travel and extend the distance needed to stop the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the front brake hoses, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2018 Ford Fusion is a comfortable and well-rounded commuter car, but potential buyers must proceed with extreme caution—especially regarding models equipped with the 1.5L EcoBoost engine due to the severe risk of coolant intrusion. Buyers should prioritize a comprehensive pre-purchase inspection that includes a cooling system pressure test. Opting for naturally aspirated or higher-trim engines while verifying that routine maintenance and recalls have been addressed can significantly reduce ownership risks.
