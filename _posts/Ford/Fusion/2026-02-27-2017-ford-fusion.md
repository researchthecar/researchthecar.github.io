# 2017 Ford Fusion Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2017 Ford Fusion offers a comfortable ride, handsome styling, and a wide array of engine and transmission configurations. However, used car buyers should approach certain model years and powertrains with caution. Chief among the concerns for this model are reports of engine coolant intrusion and persistent issues with shifter and parking mechanisms in the drivetrain.

---

## Engine

### 1.5L EcoBoost 4-Cylinder

A turbocharged 1.5-liter four-cylinder engine producing around 181 horsepower, known to have prominent reports of coolant leaking into the cylinders.

**Coolant Intrusion**
- **Symptoms:** Check engine light illuminates, coolant levels drop without external leaks, and the engine misfires.
- **Solution:** Requires professional inspection; severe cases demand engine block or full engine replacement.

### 2.0L EcoBoost 4-Cylinder

A turbocharged 2.0-liter four-cylinder engine generating roughly 240 horsepower, offering brisk acceleration alongside standard cooling system watch-outs.

**Coolant Leaks and Overheating**
- **Symptoms:** Low coolant warnings, sweet odors, and engine overheating messages on the dashboard.
- **Solution:** Inspect coolant hoses, water pumps, and gaskets; replace leaking components as needed.

### 2.5L Duratec 4-Cylinder

A naturally aspirated 2.5-liter four-cylinder engine delivering approximately 175 horsepower, generally recognized as a simpler and more robust baseline option.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

Standard six-speed automatic transmission paired with multiple engine options, requiring attention to linkage durability over time.

**Shifter Cable and Bushing Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Inability to shift out of park, discrepancy between gear selector and dashboard indicator, or vehicle rolling while in park.
- **Solution:** Replace the damaged transmission shifter cable and deteriorated shift linkage bushing.

## Suspension

### Wheels and Hardware

Suspension and wheel assembly hardware that generally holds up well, save for troublesome factory-equipped lug nuts.

**Swollen Lug Nuts**
- **Symptoms:** Factory two-piece lug nuts deform or swell, making standard socket wrenches unable to grip them during tire changes.
- **Solution:** Replace swollen factory lug nuts with solid, single-piece aftermarket alternatives.

## Brakes

### Hydraulic Brake System

Standard hydraulic braking setup designed for everyday stopping performance, though certain units have experienced secondary hydraulic and booster concerns.

**Brake Pedal Softness or Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Spongy brake pedal feel, extended stopping distances, or illuminated brake warning indicators.
- **Solution:** Inspect hydraulic lines, bleed the brake system, and service components according to recall or diagnostic procedures.

## Steering

### Electronic Power Steering

Electric power-assist steering system providing light maneuverability at low speeds, with occasional reports of sudden assist loss.

**Steering Assist Fault**
- **Symptoms:** Dashboard warnings stating 'steering assist fault service required' and a sudden loss of power steering functionality.
- **Solution:** Diagnose electronic steering control modules or replace the steering rack assembly if mechanical binding occurs.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V167000: Steering: Steering Wheel/Handle Bar](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Fusion&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** A steering wheel detachment can cause a loss of steering control, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will replace the steering wheel bolt with a longer bolt, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V874000: Seats:Mid/Rear Assembly](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Fusion&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** During a crash, the improper welds may fail, allowing any stowed luggage to move into the passenger compartment, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will replace the left hand, second row seat back frame, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V427000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission:Torque Converter](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Fusion&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** If the torque converter weld studs fail, the torque converter will not be connected to the engine flexplate and the vehicle will lose the ability to move, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Ford or Lincoln will notify owners, and dealers will replace the torque converters, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V162000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Foundation Components:Hoses, Lines/Piping, And Fittings](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Fusion&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** A brake fluid leak will increase brake pedal travel and extend the distance needed to stop the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the front brake hoses, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2017 Ford Fusion is a stylish and capable sedan, but prospective buyers should inspect vehicles carefully before purchasing. Pay close attention to service history regarding engine coolant levels and ensure the transmission shifts smoothly through all gears without linkage play. Opting for a pre-purchase inspection by a qualified mechanic can help mitigate risks associated with common engine and drivetrain complaints.
