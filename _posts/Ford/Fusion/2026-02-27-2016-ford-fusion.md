# 2016 Ford Fusion Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2016 Ford Fusion is a stylish and comfortable midsize sedan that offers a solid ride and a range of efficient powertrain options. However, prospective buyers should look closely at its historical reliability record, as owners have reported several prominent mechanical and electrical concerns. Paying careful attention to engine cooling health, transmission performance, and steering assistance functionality will help you spot a reliable model on the used market.

---

## Engine

### 1.5L EcoBoost Turbocharged I4

A small-displacement turbocharged four-cylinder engine designed for improved fuel efficiency. Buyers should prioritize service records verifying cooling system health.

**Coolant Intrusion**
- **Symptoms:** Coolant loss without an external puddle, white exhaust smoke, engine overheating, and dashboard warning lights.
- **Solution:** May require professional diagnostic testing and potentially engine block or cylinder head replacement if coolant intrusion has damaged internal components.

### 2.0L EcoBoost Turbocharged I4

A punchy turbocharged four-cylinder delivering stronger passing power while maintaining reasonable efficiency. Routine inspection of cooling components is strongly recommended.

**Coolant Leak and Overheating**
- **Symptoms:** Unexplained loss of coolant, rising temperature gauges, and cracked radiator or gasket failures.
- **Solution:** Inspect hoses, radiator, and head gasket integrity; replace compromised cooling components promptly.

### 2.5L Duratec I4

A naturally aspirated four-cylinder engine known for straightforward maintenance and dependable daily commuting. It serves as a reliable entry-level powertrain choice.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic

A conventional multi-speed automatic transmission paired with select gas engines. Regular fluid changes help maintain smooth shifting over high mileages.

**Shifter or Park Defect**
- **Symptoms:** Difficulty selecting gears, failure to register when the vehicle is in park, or rolling away after shifting.
- **Solution:** Inspect and replace worn shifter bushings or damaged shift cables.

### CVT eCVT

An electronically controlled continuously variable transmission utilized in hybrid configurations for optimized efficiency. It offers smooth, seamless acceleration during city driving.

## Suspension

### Wheel Lug Nuts

Wheel mounting hardware secures the tires to the vehicle hubs. Upgrading vulnerable factory lug nuts prevents roadside removal headaches.

**Swollen Lug Nuts**
- **Symptoms:** Lug nuts expanding or stripping, making standard tire irons unable to remove them during a flat tire change.
- **Solution:** Replace factory two-piece capped lug nuts with solid steel aftermarket alternatives.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

The hydraulic brake system provides standard stopping power for the midsize sedan. Pre-purchase inspections should thoroughly evaluate brake line integrity and pad wear.

**Brake Pedal Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Brake pedal dropping completely to the floor or sudden loss of adequate hydraulic braking pressure.
- **Solution:** Inspect brake lines, hoses, master cylinders, and bleed the hydraulic system.

## Steering

### Power Steering Assist

The electronic power-assist steering system provides light maneuverability at low speeds. Owners should verify that steering assist functions smoothly during a test drive.

**Steering Assist Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Sudden heavy steering effort, loss of power assist while driving, and warning lights on the instrument cluster.
- **Solution:** Inspect steering software, wiring harness connections, and electronic power steering motor assemblies.

## Exterior

### Door Latch

Cabin door latches and locking mechanisms ensure secure passenger closure. Operating each door during an inspection helps ensure proper latch engagement.

**Door Latch Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Doors failing to close securely, popping open while driving, or refusing to latch shut.
- **Solution:** Replace faulty door latch mechanisms within the door assembly.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V632000: Steering:Electric Power Assist System](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Fusion&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** If the bolts corrode, the steering gear motor may detach from the gear housing resulting in a loss of power steering assist, requiring a greater steering effort, especially at lower speeds, and increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will replace the steering gear motor bolts and apply a wax sealer. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V471000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission:Lever And Linkage:Floor Shift](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Fusion&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** The condition could allow the driver to move the shift lever to Park and remove the ignition key, while the transmission may not be in Park, with no warning message or audible chime. If the vehicle is exited without the transmission being in Park and without the parking brake being applied, the vehicle may unexpectedly move, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will replace the shifter cable bushing, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V362000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission:Lever And Linkage:Floor Shift](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Fusion&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** The condition could allow the driver to move the shift lever to Park and remove the ignition key, while the transmission may not be in Park, with no warning message or audible chime. If the vehicle is exited without the transmission being in Park and without the parking brake being applied, the vehicle may unexpectedly move, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will replace the shifter cable bushing, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V875000: Seat Belts:Front](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Fusion&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** In the event of a crash, heat from the deployment of a seat belt pretensioner may cause a front seat belt anchor cable to fail. If the driver or front passenger seatbelt cables cannot properly restrain the front seat occupants, they would be at an increased risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will apply a protective coating to the seat belt cable, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V167000: Steering: Steering Wheel/Handle Bar](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Fusion&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** A steering wheel detachment can cause a loss of steering control, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will replace the steering wheel bolt with a longer bolt, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V250000: Seat Belts:Front:Anchorage](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Fusion&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** The front driver seat track assembly's rear riser may have been improperly welded to the seat track rail, and as a result, the driver may not be adequately restrained in a crash. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) numbers 207, "Seating Systems" and 210, "Seat Belt Assembly.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the front driver seat track rail, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 15V618000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Storage](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Fusion&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** As a result, the fuel tanks may crack in the event of a crash and thus the vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 301, "Fuel System Integrity." A fuel tank that cracks may leak fuel, increasing the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will replace the fuel tank, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V177000: Latches/Locks/Linkages:Doors:Latch](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Fusion&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** A door that is not securely latched could open while the vehicle is in motion, increasing the risk of injury to a vehicle occupant.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and Ford or Lincoln dealers will replace the side door latches, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

When shopping for a used 2016 Ford Fusion, thoroughness is key. Pay special attention to the engine's cooling system health to rule out potential coolant leaks or intrusion issues, and test the steering and braking systems during your test drive. Choosing a model with a well-documented maintenance history can help you secure a comfortable and dependable commuter car.
