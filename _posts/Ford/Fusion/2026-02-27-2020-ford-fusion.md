# 2020 Ford Fusion Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2020 Ford Fusion marks the final model year for this popular midsize sedan, offering a comfortable ride, intuitive tech, and a traditional styling profile. While it makes for an appealing commuter car on the used market, prospective buyers should review specific powertrain reliability trends—particularly concerning EcoBoost engine coolant management and automatic transmission durability. Thoroughly inspecting service histories and checking for any unresolved shifting or electrical issues will help ensure a dependable purchase.

---

## Engine

### 1.5L EcoBoost 3-Cylinder / 4-Cylinder Turbo

A turbocharged small-displacement engine providing efficient daily driving dynamics. Reliability is generally moderate, with certain units requiring close monitoring of cooling systems.

### 2.0L EcoBoost 4-Cylinder

A turbocharged 2.0-liter four-cylinder engine generating around 245 horsepower. It delivers energetic acceleration and highway passing power, though buyers should watch for historical reports of coolant leaks.

**Coolant Intrusion / Leak**
- **Symptoms:** Sudden coolant loss, engine overheating, white smoke from the exhaust, or illumination of warning lights while driving.
- **Solution:** Requires professional diagnostic evaluation; severe cases may necessitate cylinder head or engine replacement.

### 2.5L Duratec 4-Cylinder

A naturally aspirated 2.5-liter four-cylinder engine making roughly 175 horsepower. Known for being the simplest and potentially most durable conventional powertrain option in the lineup.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

A conventional 6-speed automatic transmission paired with select non-hybrid configurations. It offers predictable shifts, though some owners report drivetrain mechanical wear over higher mileages.

**Flexplate and Shifting Concerns**
- **Symptoms:** Unusual rattling noises, slipping gears, harsh engagement, or failure to properly register when the vehicle is placed in park.
- **Solution:** Inspect transmission fluid condition, check linkage bushings, and replace damaged flexplate or internal components as needed.

### eCVT (Hybrid / Plug-In Hybrid)

An electronically controlled continuously variable transmission dedicated to hybrid models. It generally offers smooth operation and strong efficiency ratings.

### Shifter Mechanism & Electronics

Manages interior convenience features, gear selection displays, and safety module sensors. Electrical faults can occasionally trigger erratic warning messages.

**'Transmission Not in Park' / Shift System Fault**
- **Symptoms:** Illuminated warning messages, inability to shift out of park, or erroneous alerts regarding gear selection.
- **Solution:** Inspect and replace worn shifter cable bushing kits or faulty gear selector modules.

## Steering

### Steering and Front Suspension Control Arms

Governs directional control and front wheel articulation. Suspension component wear requires regular inspection during routine maintenance.

**Lower Control Arm Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Sudden snapping or fracturing of the front lower control arm during low-speed turns, resulting in compromised steering control.
- **Solution:** Replace damaged control arms and perform a complete front-end suspension and wheel alignment check.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V440000: Electrical System:Propulsion System:Traction Battery:Management System/Energy Control Module (Bms/Becm)](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Fusion&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** The battery energy control module (BECM) may become damaged due to excessive voltage and current flow. A damaged BECM can result in a loss of drive power or fire, increasing the risk of a crash or injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the battery and update the BECM software for free, or Ford will repurchase the vehicle. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V442000: Back Over Prevention:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Fusion&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** A software error may cause the rearview camera to display a blank image, or the image may remain on the display after the backing event has ended. Both of these situations can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the rearview camera software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V685000: Equipment:Electrical:Engine Block Heater](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Fusion&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** The engine block heater may crack and develop a coolant leak, causing it to short circuit when the block heater is plugged in. An electrical short circuit can increase the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the block heater, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2020 Ford Fusion provides a comfortable, well-equipped driving experience for used sedan buyers, but it is not without potential mechanical pitfalls. Prospective owners should pay close attention to engine coolant levels, test drive the vehicle to verify smooth gear engagement without shifter errors, and inspect the front suspension components. Purchasing a model with a documented service history or remaining warranty coverage offers the best peace of mind.
