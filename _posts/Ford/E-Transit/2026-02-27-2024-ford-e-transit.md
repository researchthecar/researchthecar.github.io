# 2024 Ford E-Transit Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2024 Ford E-Transit brings all-electric capability to the commercial van segment, offering businesses a zero-emission fleet option with familiar utility. As a newer platform, used buyers should weigh the benefits of electric ownership against potential software quirks and limited early real-world long-term data. Careful inspection of the high-voltage battery health and onboard electronics is recommended before purchase.

---

## Engine

### Electric Motor

The E-Transit utilizes a single electric motor delivering approximately 266 horsepower and 317 lb-ft of torque, driving the rear wheels. This battery-electric setup offers smooth, instantaneous power delivery with minimal moving parts compared to traditional internal combustion engines, resulting in lower routine mechanical maintenance.

## Transmission

### Single-Speed Direct Drive Transmission

Power is sent to the rear wheels via a single-speed direct drive automatic transmission. The simplified gearbox design eliminates traditional multi-speed shifting hesitation and reduces drivetrain complexity for commercial fleet applications.

---

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V860000: Power Train:Axle Assembly:Axle Shaft](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=E-Transit&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** A loss of drive power increases the risk of a crash. In addition, a half shaft that is not fully engaged to the primary drive unit can result in a vehicle rollaway if the parking brake is not applied, increasing the risk of a crash or injury.
- **What to do:** Dealer will inspect and replace the rear half shaft and power drive unit components as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2024 Ford E-Transit is an appealing option for businesses transitioning to electric fleets, featuring a simple direct-drive electric powertrain. While early reliability appears stable, used buyers should thoroughly inspect the vehicle's electronic systems, charging hardware, and battery health to ensure optimal performance.
