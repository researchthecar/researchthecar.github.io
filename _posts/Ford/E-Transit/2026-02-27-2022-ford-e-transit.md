# 2022 Ford E-Transit Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2022 Ford E-Transit brings all-electric capability to the commercial van market, offering businesses a zero-emission alternative for urban delivery and fleet use. As an early model year for this electric platform, prospective buyers should review available owner feedback regarding electrical systems, charging components, and auxiliary vehicle functions.

---

## Engine

### Single Electric Motor

The E-Transit utilizes a single rear-mounted electric motor producing approximately 266 horsepower and 317 lb-ft of torque. This powertrain delivers smooth, direct power suited for stop-and-go commercial driving without traditional internal combustion maintenance needs.

## Transmission

### Single-Speed Direct-Drive Transmission

Power is sent to the rear wheels via a single-speed direct-drive transmission designed for simplicity and efficiency. This setup eliminates traditional multi-speed shifting hesitation and reduces moving parts.

---

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V860000: Power Train:Axle Assembly:Axle Shaft](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=E-Transit&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** A loss of drive power increases the risk of a crash. In addition, a half shaft that is not fully engaged to the primary drive unit can result in a vehicle rollaway if the parking brake is not applied, increasing the risk of a crash or injury.
- **What to do:** Dealer will inspect and replace the rear half shaft and power drive unit components as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2022 Ford E-Transit represents a modern electric solution for commercial fleets, trading traditional combustion engine maintenance for an advanced battery-electric architecture. While major mechanical failures are rare, used buyers should thoroughly inspect the high-voltage charging systems, onboard electronics, and auxiliary power features before purchasing.
