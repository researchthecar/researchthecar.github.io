**Introduction:**

The 2005 Ford E-150 is a full-size cargo van offered with a variety of engine and transmission options. It was popular for spacious cargo area, reliability, and towing capacity. On the downside, it was criticized for its dated interior, uncomfortable seats, and poor fuel economy.

**Engine**

**5.4L V8**

* **Intake manifold gasket failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaks, overheating, and rough idling
    * Solution: Replacement of the intake manifold gasket
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**4.6L V8**

* **Spark plug failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Engine misfires, decreased power, and difficult starting
    * Solution: Replacement of the spark plugs
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**4-Speed Automatic (4R75W)**

* **Torque converter shudder**
    * Description/Symptoms: Hesitation or shuddering when shifting gears
    * Solution: Replacing the torque converter
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1200+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Worn ball joints**
    * Description/Symptoms: Clunking or popping noises when turning, loose steering
    * Solution: Replacement of the ball joints
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Leaking shocks**
    * Description/Symptoms: Rough ride, decreased handling, uneven tire wear
    * Solution: Replacement of the shocks
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Faulty ignition switch**
    * Description/Symptoms: Difficulty starting, engine stalling, security light flashing
    * Solution: Replacement of the ignition switch
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    * Recalls: 05S34

* **Alternator failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Dimming lights, electrical system malfunctions, battery not charging
    * Solution: Replacement of the alternator
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Rust on body panels**
    * Description/Symptoms: Corrosion and deterioration of metal panels
    * Solution: Repair or replacement of rusted panels
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Cracked dashboard**
    * Description/Symptoms: Visible cracks or splits on the dashboard
    * Solution: Replacement of the dashboard or repair with epoxy
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Faulty door locks**
    * Description/Symptoms: Difficulty locking or unlocking doors, erratic behavior
    * Solution: Replacement of the door lock actuators
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

The 2005 Ford E-150 has a range of issues that are typical for vehicles of its age and mileage. The severity and cost of these issues vary, but some of the more serious problems, such as intake manifold gasket failure and ignition switch failure, can be costly to repair. Overall, it's important for owners to maintain their vehicles regularly to prevent or mitigate these issues.
