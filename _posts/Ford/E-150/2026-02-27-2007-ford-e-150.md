# 2007 Ford E-150

### Overview
The 2007 Ford E-150 is a workhorse van known for its utility and robust construction. While generally reliable for its intended purpose, like any vehicle, it has specific areas that have seen more attention from owners regarding potential issues. This analysis focuses on common mechanical and electrical concerns reported by owners, excluding routine maintenance. Understanding these potential weak points can help owners anticipate and address problems, ensuring the longevity and performance of their E-150.

---


## Engine

### 4.6L Triton V8
**Spark Plug Blowout**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of engine power, misfires, ticking noise from the engine, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Repair or replace stripped spark plug threads, often requiring specialized tools or inserts.
- **Cost:** $300 - $800

**Intake Manifold Gasket Leak**
- **Symptoms:** Coolant leaks, engine overheating, rough idle, white smoke from exhaust.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the intake manifold gaskets.
- **Cost:** $400 - $700


### 5.4L Triton V8
**Spark Plug Blowout**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of engine power, misfires, ticking noise from the engine, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Repair or replace stripped spark plug threads, often requiring specialized tools or inserts.
- **Cost:** $300 - $800

**Camshaft Synchronizer (Cam Sync) Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Rattling noise from the engine, especially at idle or startup, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the camshaft synchronizer.
- **Cost:** $500 - $900

## Transmission

### 4R75E (4-Speed Automatic)
**Torque Converter Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Slipping gears, shuddering, delayed engagement, fluid leaks.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the torque converter and potentially seals.
- **Cost:** $700 - $1500

**Overheating**
- **Symptoms:** Transmission slipping, harsh shifting, burning smell.
- **Solution:** Installation of an auxiliary transmission cooler, flushing and refilling transmission fluid.
- **Cost:** $200 - $500


## Suspension

### Standard Chassis
**Ball Joint Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Clunking noises over bumps, uneven tire wear, steering wander.
- **Solution:** Replacement of worn ball joints.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600

**Leaf Spring Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Sagging rear end, clunking noises, reduced load capacity.
- **Solution:** Replacement of broken or sagging leaf springs.
- **Cost:** $400 - $800


## Electrical

### Standard Electrical System
**Alternator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Dimming headlights, battery warning light, electrical components not working, dead battery.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the alternator.
- **Cost:** $400 - $700

**Window Motor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Power windows not operating.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the window motor and regulator.
- **Cost:** $200 - $400

### Summary Verdict
The 2007 Ford E-150 demonstrates a generally solid reliability profile, particularly for its class and age. The most frequently cited issues revolve around the Triton V8 engines, specifically spark plug retention and intake manifold gaskets, along with potential torque converter problems in the automatic transmission. Suspension components like ball joints and leaf springs can also require attention due to the vehicle's typical heavy usage. Electrical issues, such as alternator failure, are not uncommon. Despite these points, the E-150 remains a dependable choice for those needing a capable and durable van, provided these potential issues are addressed proactively.
