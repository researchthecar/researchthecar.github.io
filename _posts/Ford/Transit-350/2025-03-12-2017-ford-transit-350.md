Here's a breakdown of common issues reported by owners of the 2017 Ford Transit-350, categorized for clarity and designed for your website. The 2017 Ford Transit-350 is a popular cargo and passenger van known for its versatility and spaciousness. The 2017 model year offered several engine options, including a 3.7L Ti-VCT V6 (275 hp, 260 lb-ft of torque), a 3.5L EcoBoost V6 (310 hp, 400 lb-ft of torque), and a 3.2L Power Stroke I5 diesel (185 hp, 350 lb-ft of torque). All engines were paired with a 6-speed automatic transmission. Owners often appreciate the Transit's customizable configurations and cargo capacity but sometimes cite concerns regarding reliability and repair costs.

**Engine**

**3.5L EcoBoost V6**

*   **Turbocharger Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, unusual noises from the engine, check engine light illuminated. Can be caused by oil leaks or general wear.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the turbocharger(s).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Coolant Intrusion**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Coolant can leak into the cylinders causing the engine to misfire.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the entire engine.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $7,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**3.7L Ti-VCT V6**

*   **Rough Idle/Misfire**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The engine idles roughly, misfires, check engine light illuminated with codes relating to misfires or lean conditions. This can be caused by worn spark plugs, faulty ignition coils, or vacuum leaks.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis of the cause and replacement of the affected components (spark plugs, coil packs, vacuum lines).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+ depending on the cause and severity.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**3.2L Power Stroke I5 Diesel**

*   **Diesel Particulate Filter (DPF) Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The DPF can become clogged, leading to reduced engine performance, increased fuel consumption, and the DPF warning light illuminating. This is often related to driving habits (short trips not allowing the DPF to regenerate properly).
    *   Solution: Forced regeneration of the DPF (performed by a mechanic), or replacement of the DPF if it is severely clogged.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 (forced regeneration) - $2,000+ (DPF replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **EGR Valve Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rough running, check engine light, reduced power, increased exhaust smoke.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the EGR valve.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**6-Speed Automatic (All Engine Options)**

*   **Harsh Shifting/Slipping**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The transmission may shift harshly between gears or slip, especially when accelerating. This can be related to low transmission fluid, contaminated fluid, or internal wear of the transmission components.
    *   Solution: Transmission fluid flush and filter replacement. If the problem persists, internal transmission repair or replacement may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400 (fluid flush), $2,000 - $4,000+ (transmission repair/replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Torque Converter Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Shuddering or vibration, especially at lower speeds, along with potential stalling. This can indicate torque converter failure.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the torque converter.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $2,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Rear Leaf Spring Sagging/Cracking**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The rear of the van sags, especially when loaded. Leaf springs may exhibit visible cracks or breakage.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the leaf springs. Upgrading to heavy-duty springs may be recommended, especially for vans frequently carrying heavy loads.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Door Lock Actuator Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The power door locks may intermittently fail to lock or unlock, or may not function at all.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the faulty door lock actuator.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $400+ per door.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Wiring Harness Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Intermittent electrical problems, such as lights flickering or systems not functioning correctly. Can be caused by chafing or corrosion in the wiring harness, particularly in areas exposed to the elements.
    *   Solution: Inspection and repair or replacement of the affected wiring harness sections.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+ depending on the complexity of the repair.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Sliding Door Problems**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The sliding door can be difficult to open or close, requiring excessive force. This can be due to misaligned tracks, worn rollers, or a faulty latch mechanism.
    *   Solution: Adjustment of the door alignment, lubrication of the tracks and rollers, or replacement of worn components.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Side Mirror Vibration**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Excessive vibration of the side mirrors at highway speeds, making it difficult to see clearly.
    *   Solution: Tightening or replacement of the mirror assembly. Aftermarket mirror options are also available.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $300+ per mirror.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **HVAC Blower Motor Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The HVAC blower motor stops working, resulting in no airflow from the vents. This can be due to a faulty blower motor resistor or a failing blower motor itself.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the blower motor resistor or the blower motor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $400+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Seat Belt Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Seat belts failing to retract, or not locking properly.
    *   Solution: Inspection and repair or replacement of the seat belt assembly.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $400+ per seatbelt.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2017 Ford Transit-350 can experience a range of issues. Engine problems, particularly with the EcoBoost turbochargers, can be costly. Transmission issues are a concern across all engine options. Suspension and electrical problems are also relatively common. While some issues are minor, others can lead to significant repair bills. Potential buyers should carefully inspect the vehicle's service history, paying close attention to any signs of the issues mentioned above. Due diligence is key to mitigating the risk of unexpected repairs.
