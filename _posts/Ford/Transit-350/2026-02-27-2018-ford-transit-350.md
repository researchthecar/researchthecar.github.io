# 2018 Ford Transit-350

### Overview
The 2018 Ford Transit-350 is a versatile and robust commercial van known for its utility and adaptability. Generally, it exhibits good reliability, particularly in its core mechanical components. However, like any vehicle, specific areas can experience issues. This analysis focuses on documented technical problems beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential concerns for owners and fleet managers. We will examine the engine, transmission, suspension, electrical, exterior, and interior systems, detailing common problems, their symptoms, solutions, and estimated repair costs where applicable. Understanding these potential issues can help in proactive maintenance and informed decision-making.

### 3.5L EcoBoost V6 (Twin-Turbo Gasoline)
**Turbocharger Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Reduced power, whistling noises, increased oil consumption.
- **Solution:** Inspect and replace turbocharger seals or the entire unit. Check for oil leaks and boost leaks.
- **Cost:** $1,200 - $2,500

**Coolant Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Visible coolant puddles, overheating, sweet smell.
- **Solution:** Inspect hoses, water pump, and radiator for leaks. Replace faulty components.
- **Cost:** $300 - $800


### 3.2L I5 Power Stroke (Diesel)
**Diesel Particulate Filter (DPF) Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Check engine light, reduced engine power, increased fuel consumption, exhaust smell.
- **Solution:** Perform forced regeneration, clean or replace DPF. Ensure proper engine oil is used.
- **Cost:** $800 - $2,000

**EGR System Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Rough idling, poor performance, black smoke, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Clean or replace Exhaust Gas Recirculation (EGR) valve and cooler. Check for carbon buildup.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1,200

## Electrical

### General Electrical System
**Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle fails to start, dim lights, electrical components malfunctioning.
- **Solution:** Test battery and charging system. Inspect for parasitic draws from aftermarket accessories or faulty modules.
- **Cost:** $150 - $500

**Sensor Failures (e.g., ABS, Speed Sensor)**
- **Symptoms:** ABS warning light, speedometer inaccuracies, traction control issues.
- **Solution:** Diagnose and replace faulty sensors. Check wiring harnesses for damage.
- **Cost:** $200 - $600

### Summary Verdict
The 2018 Ford Transit-350 generally demonstrates solid reliability, especially concerning its transmission and suspension systems, which show no widespread reported issues. The gasoline engines, particularly the 3.7L V6, are quite dependable. However, the 3.5L EcoBoost V6 can experience turbocharger and coolant leak issues, while the 3.2L Power Stroke diesel is more prone to DPF and EGR system problems, common in modern diesel powertrains. Electrical gremlins like battery drain and sensor failures are also noted, though not excessively prevalent. Overall, with proper maintenance, especially for the diesel engine, the Transit-350 is a reliable workhorse. Owners should be aware of potential turbocharger and diesel emission system concerns.
