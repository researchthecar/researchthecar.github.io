Here is a comprehensive list of common issues and complaints reported by owners of the 2019 Ford Transit-350, categorized by major component. The 2019 Ford Transit-350 is a popular van used for both commercial and recreational purposes. It offers a variety of configurations and capabilities. Engine options for the 2019 Transit-350 include a 3.7L Ti-VCT V6 (275 horsepower, 260 lb-ft of torque), a 3.5L EcoBoost V6 (310 horsepower, 400 lb-ft of torque), and a 3.2L Power Stroke I5 diesel (185 horsepower, 350 lb-ft of torque). All engines are paired with a 6-speed automatic transmission. Owners often praise the Transit's spacious interior, payload capacity, and relatively comfortable ride. Common complaints include issues with the transmission, electrical gremlins, and some engine-related problems.

**Engine**

**3.5L EcoBoost V6**

*   **Turbocharger Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Reduced power, check engine light, unusual noises from the engine bay. Can sometimes be caused by leaking coolant lines.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the faulty turbocharger(s) and coolant lines.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Engine Misfires**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rough idling, hesitation during acceleration, check engine light. This can be caused by faulty ignition coils or spark plugs.
    *   Solution: Replacing faulty ignition coils and/or spark plugs. Diagnosis to determine the root cause is critical.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**3.7L Ti-VCT V6**

*   **Excessive Oil Consumption**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Low oil level warning, needing to add oil frequently between oil changes, blue smoke from the exhaust.
    *   Solution: Could involve piston ring replacement or addressing valve stem seal leaks after other possible problems are ruled out. A compression and leakdown test would be required.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $5,000+ depending on the cause and the extent of the repair.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**3.2L Power Stroke I5 Diesel**

*   **Diesel Particulate Filter (DPF) Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Reduced engine power, frequent regeneration cycles, "Exhaust Filter Overload" message. This can be caused by a faulty DPF sensor or issues with the regeneration system.
    *   Solution: Replacing the DPF sensor, performing a forced regeneration, or replacing the DPF if it's severely clogged.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $3,000+ depending on the specific problem.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**6-Speed Automatic (All Engine Options)**

*   **Harsh Shifting/Slipping**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Abrupt or delayed gear changes, slipping between gears, transmission overheating. This can be caused by worn valve body components, low transmission fluid, or internal damage.
    *   Solution: Replacing the valve body, flushing and refilling the transmission fluid, or a complete transmission rebuild/replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 (fluid change) - $5,000+ (rebuild/replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Torque Converter Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Shuddering at highway speeds, unusual noises from the transmission area, stalling.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the torque converter.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $3,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Rear Leaf Spring Sagging**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rear end of the van sits lower than normal, reduced ride height, poor handling, clunking noise over bumps. This is often exacerbated when the van is frequently loaded near its maximum payload capacity.
    *   Solution: Replacing the rear leaf springs with upgraded or heavy-duty springs.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Battery Drain**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The van is difficult to start or won't start after sitting for a short period. This could be caused by a parasitic draw from an aftermarket accessory or a faulty module.
    *   Solution: Identifying and repairing the source of the parasitic draw or replacing the battery.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 (battery replacement) - $500+ (diagnosing and repairing parasitic draw)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Sensor Failures (ABS, TPMS, etc.)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Warning lights on the dashboard, inaccurate readings from sensors.
    *   Solution: Replacing the faulty sensor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+ per sensor
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Door Lock Actuator Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: One or more door locks fail to operate with the key fob or interior switch.
    *   Solution: Replacing the faulty door lock actuator.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150-$400+ per door
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Sliding Door Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Difficulty opening or closing the sliding door, sticking, unusual noises. This can be caused by worn rollers, damaged tracks, or a faulty latch mechanism.
    *   Solution: Replacing the rollers, repairing or replacing the track, or adjusting/replacing the latch mechanism.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Running Board/Step Corrosion**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rusting and corrosion of the running boards or side steps, particularly in areas with harsh winters or exposure to road salt.
    *   Solution: Replacing the running boards or side steps.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **HVAC Blower Motor Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: No air blowing from the vents, weak airflow, unusual noises from the blower motor area.
    *   Solution: Replacing the blower motor resistor or the blower motor itself.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Seat Upholstery Wear/Tear (Beyond Normal Wear)**
    * Description/Symptoms: Premature ripping or tearing of seat fabric, especially in high-use areas.
    * Solution: Replacement of the seat fabric.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300-$1,000+ depending on the severity and the shop performing the work.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

The 2019 Ford Transit-350 has a range of potential issues, with the transmission and electrical systems being the most common sources of complaints. Engine problems are also reported, but vary depending on the specific engine option. The severity and cost of these issues can range from minor sensor replacements to major engine or transmission overhauls. Buyers should be aware of these potential problems and carefully inspect the vehicle before purchasing.
