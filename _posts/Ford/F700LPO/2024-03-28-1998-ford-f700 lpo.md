## Introduction

The 1998 Ford F700 LPO is a heavy-duty pickup truck with a powerful V8 engine and a payload capacity of up to 17,250 pounds. The F700 is available with a wide range of engine and transmission options, including a 7.3L Power Stroke turbocharged diesel engine that produces 210 horsepower and 425 lb-ft of torque. The F700 is also available with a 6.8L V10 gasoline engine that produces 245 horsepower and 420 lb-ft of torque. The F700 is a popular choice for heavy-duty hauling and commercial use. Owners praise the truck's powerful engine, rugged construction, and comfortable interior. However, the F700 is also known for a number of common problems.

**Engine**

**7.3L Power Stroke**

* **Turbocharger failure:** The turbocharger can fail due to a variety of issues, including oil starvation, worn bearings, or a damaged housing. Symptoms include loss of power, increased fuel consumption, and smoking from the exhaust. The turbocharger can be rebuilt or replaced. Estimated cost to fix: $1,000 - $2,500+
* **Cylinder head gasket failure:** The cylinder head gasket can fail due to a variety of issues, including overheating, poor maintenance, or a manufacturing defect. Symptoms include coolant leaks, white smoke from the exhaust, and overheating. The cylinder head gasket can be replaced. Estimated cost to fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
* **Fuel injector failure:** The fuel injectors can fail due to a variety of issues, including contamination in the fuel, electrical problems, or a manufacturing defect. Symptoms include rough idling, decreased power, and black smoke from the exhaust. The fuel injectors can be replaced. Estimated cost to fix: $400 - $800+

**6.8L V10**

* **Intake manifold gasket failure:** The intake manifold gasket can fail due to a variety of issues, including overheating, poor maintenance, or a manufacturing defect. Symptoms include coolant leaks, rough idling, and decreased power. The intake manifold gasket can be replaced. Estimated cost to fix: $500 - $1,000+
* **Spark plug failure:** The spark plugs can fail due to a variety of issues, including normal wear and tear, contamination in the fuel, or a manufacturing defect. Symptoms include rough idling, decreased power, and increased fuel consumption. The spark plugs can be replaced. Estimated cost to fix: $200 - $400+
* **Coil failure:** The coil can fail due to a variety of issues, including overheating, poor maintenance, or a manufacturing defect. Symptoms include rough idling, decreased power, and increased fuel consumption. The coil can be replaced. Estimated cost to fix: $100 - $200+

* **Transmission noise:** The transmission can make a variety of noises, including whining, grinding, or clunking. Symptoms can vary depending on the issue. The transmission may need to be rebuilt or replaced. Estimated cost to fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
