**1996 Ford Econoline Super Duty**

The 1996 Ford Econoline Super Duty is a full-size van that was available with a variety of engine and transmission options. It was powered by a standard 4.6L V8 engine that produced 210 horsepower and 290 lb-ft of torque. Other engine options included a 5.8L V8 engine that produced 245 horsepower and 330 lb-ft of torque, and a 7.5L V8 engine that produced 275 horsepower and 420 lb-ft of torque. Transmission options included a four-speed automatic transmission and a five-speed manual transmission.

The Econoline Super Duty was a popular choice for commercial and family use. It was praised for its spacious interior, powerful engine, and rugged construction. However, it also had some common issues that owners should be aware of.

**Engine**

**4.6L V8 Engine**

* **Spark plug blowout:** This can cause a loss of compression and power, and can lead to engine damage if not repaired.
  * Solution: Replace the spark plugs and/or spark plug wires.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
* **Intake manifold gasket failure:** This can cause coolant and oil leaks, which can lead to overheating and engine damage.
  * Solution: Replace the intake manifold gasket.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
* **Timing chain tensioner failure:** This can cause a rattling noise and can lead to engine damage if not repaired.
  * Solution: Replace the timing chain tensioner.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
* **Valve cover gasket failure:** This can cause oil leaks, which can lead to reduced engine performance.
  * Solution: Replace the valve cover gaskets.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+

**5.8L V8 Engine**

* **Head gasket failure:** This can cause coolant and oil leaks, which can lead to overheating and engine damage.
  * Solution: Replace the head gasket.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
* **Water pump failure:** This can cause overheating and engine damage if not repaired.
  * Solution: Replace the water pump.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
* **Ignition coil failure:** This can cause engine misfires and reduced performance.
  * Solution: Replace the ignition coil.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+

**7.5L V8 Engine**

* **Rod bearing failure:** This can cause a knocking noise and can lead to engine damage if not repaired.
  * Solution: Replace the rod bearings.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,000+
* **Main bearing failure:** This can cause a rattling noise and can lead to engine damage if not repaired.
  * Solution: Replace the main bearings.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,000+
* **Oil pump failure:** This can cause a loss of oil pressure and can lead to engine damage if not repaired.
  * Solution: Replace the oil pump.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+

**Transmission**

**Four-Speed Automatic Transmission**

* **TCC solenoid failure:** This can cause the transmission to shift erratically or not shift at all.
  * Solution: Replace the TCC solenoid.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
* **Torque converter failure:** This can cause the transmission to slip or shudder.
  * Solution: Replace the torque converter.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
* **Valve body failure:** This can cause the transmission to shift erratically or not shift at all.
  * Solution: Replace the valve body.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+

**Five-Speed Manual Transmission**

* **Clutch failure:** This can cause the transmission to slip or shudder.
  * Solution: Replace the clutch.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
* **Synchromesh failure:** This can cause the transmission to grind when shifting gears.
  * Solution: Replace the synchromesh.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
* **Shift linkage failure:** This can cause the transmission to shift erratically or not shift at all.
  * Solution: Replace the shift linkage.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+

**Suspension**

* **Ball joint failure:** This can cause the suspension to become unstable and can lead to tire wear.
  * Solution: Replace the ball joints.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+ per side
* **Control arm failure:** This can cause the suspension to become unstable and can lead to tire wear.
  * Solution: Replace the control arms.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+ per side
* **Stabilizer bar link failure:** This can cause the suspension to become unstable and can lead to tire wear.
  * Solution: Replace the stabilizer bar links.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+ per side

**Electrical**

* **Alternator failure:** This can cause the battery to drain and can lead to the vehicle not starting.
  * Solution: Replace the alternator.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
* **Battery failure:** This can cause the vehicle not to start.
  * Solution: Replace the battery.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
* **Starter failure:** This can cause the vehicle not to start.
  * Solution: Replace the starter.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+

**Exterior**

* **Rust:** This can occur on the body panels, frame, and other metal components.
  * Solution: Repair or replace the affected components.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the severity of the rust.
* **Headlight failure:** This can make it difficult to see at night.
  * Solution: Replace the headlight bulbs.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $20 - $50+ per bulb

**Interior**

* **Dashboard cracking:** This can occur on the dashboard and other interior plastic components.
  * Solution: Replace the affected components.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the severity of the cracking.
* **Seat frame failure:** This can cause the seat to collapse or become unstable.
  * Solution: Replace the seat frame.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+ per seat
* **Heater core failure:** This can cause the vehicle to overheat or not have heat.
  * Solution: Replace the heater core.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+

**Summary**

The 1996 Ford Econoline Super Duty is a reliable and versatile vehicle, but it is not without its problems. The most common issues include engine problems, transmission problems, suspension problems, and electrical problems. The severity of these issues can vary depending on the specific model and year of the vehicle. The estimated cost to fix these issues can also vary depending on the severity of the problem and the location of the repair. Overall, the 1996 Ford Econoline Super Duty is a good choice for those who need a spacious and powerful van, but it is important to be aware of the potential problems that can occur.
