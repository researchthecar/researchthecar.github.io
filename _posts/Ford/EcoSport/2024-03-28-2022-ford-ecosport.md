## 2022 Ford EcoSport

The 2022 Ford EcoSport is a subcompact SUV that offers a blend of utility and affordability. It comes standard with a 2.0-liter four-cylinder engine paired with a six-speed automatic transmission, producing 166 horsepower and 150 lb-ft of torque. A 1.0-liter turbocharged three-cylinder engine with 123 horsepower and 125 lb-ft of torque is also available, paired with a six-speed manual transmission.

Owners appreciate the EcoSport's spacious interior, user-friendly infotainment system, and affordable price point. However, some have reported concerns related to its overall reliability and performance.

### Engine

**2.0L Four-Cylinder**
- **Engine Misfires:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Rough idling, hesitation during acceleration, decreased fuel efficiency.
    - Solution: Replace spark plugs and ignition coils.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+
    - Recalls: None Found
- **Intake Manifold Gasket Failure:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaks, overheating, loss of coolant.
    - Solution: Replace intake manifold gasket.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $700+
    - Recalls: None Found

**1.0L Turbocharged Three-Cylinder**
- **Turbocharger Failure:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, whistling or whining noise, decreased fuel efficiency.
    - Solution: Replace turbocharger.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
    - Recalls: None Found
- **Fuel Pump Failure:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Engine stalling, difficulty starting, reduced fuel pressure.
    - Solution: Replace fuel pump.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    - Recalls: None Found

### Transmission

**Six-Speed Automatic Transmission**
- **Shifting Issues:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears, delayed shifts, hard shifts.
    - Solution: Replace transmission fluid, flush transmission, or rebuild transmission.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $3,000+
    - Recalls: None Found

**Six-Speed Manual Transmission**
- **Clutch Failure:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears, slipping clutch, shuddering when engaging.
    - Solution: Replace clutch assembly.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,200+
    - Recalls: None Found

### Suspension
- **Strut Failure:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Clunking or knocking noise, difficulty steering, uneven tire wear.
    - Solution: Replace struts.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800+ per pair
    - Recalls: None Found
- **Rear Wheel Bearings:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Humming or growling noise, excessive heat buildup.
    - Solution: Replace wheel bearings.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+ per side
    - Recalls: None Found

### Electrical
- **Alternator Failure:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Difficulty starting, flickering lights, battery warning light.
    - Solution: Replace alternator.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800+
    - Recalls: None Found
- **Battery Drain:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Battery dies quickly, requires frequent jump-starts.
    - Solution: Identify and repair source of electrical draw, such as a parasitic battery drain.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    - Recalls: None Found

### Exterior
- **Paint Peeling (Specific Areas):**
    - Description/Symptoms: Peeling or chipping paint on specific areas, such as the roof, hood, or doors.
    - Solution: Repaint affected areas.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+ per area
    - Recalls: None Found

### Interior
- **Dashboard Fade/Warpage:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Dashboard becomes faded, discolored, or warped due to sun exposure.
    - Solution: Replace dashboard.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    - Recalls: None Found
- **Seat Belt Pretensioner Issues:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Seat belts fail to engage properly or deploy during a crash.
    - Solution: Inspect and replace seat belt pretensioners as needed.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+ per seat belt
    - Recalls:
        - 20F8: Seat belt pretensioner may not deploy properly

### Summary

The 2022 Ford EcoSport offers some advantages, but it also has some significant issues that affect various components. Engine misfires, intake manifold gasket failures, turbocharger problems, and transmission issues are among the most common concerns. Additionally, suspension components, electrical systems, and interior elements have been known to fail. The cost of repairs can range from a few hundred dollars to over $3,000, making it important for potential buyers to be aware of these issues and the associated expenses.
