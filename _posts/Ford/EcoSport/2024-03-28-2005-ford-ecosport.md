**Introduction**
The 2005 Ford EcoSport is a compact SUV that offers versatility and affordability. It is available with two engine options and two transmission options. The base engine is a 2.0-liter four-cylinder that produces 151 horsepower and 171 lb-ft of torque. The optional engine is a 3.0-liter V6 that produces 203 horsepower and 206 lb-ft of torque. Both engines are available with a five-speed manual transmission or a four-speed automatic transmission.

Commonly liked features of the EcoSport include its spacious interior, affordable price point, and good fuel economy. Common complaints include its lack of power, poor handling, and cramped back seat.

**Engine**

* **2.0-liter four-cylinder**
    * **Intake manifold gasket failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Coolant or oil leak from the intake manifold gasket. This can lead to overheating and engine damage.
    * Solution: Replace the intake manifold gasket.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800
    * Recalls: NHTSA Campaign Number: 05V231000
* **3.0-liter V6**
    * **Timing chain tensioner failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: A rattling noise from the engine, reduced engine power, and eventually engine failure.
    * Solution: Replace the timing chain tensioner.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,200 - $1,800
    * Recalls: NHTSA Campaign Number: 05V369000

**Transmission**

* **Five-speed manual transmission**
    * **Transmission gear grinding**
    * Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears, grinding or popping noises, and possible transmission failure.
    * Solution: Replace the worn gears or synchros in the transmission.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,200 - $2,000+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Four-speed automatic transmission**
    * **Transmission fluid leak**
    * Description/Symptoms: A leak from the transmission pan or lines, low transmission fluid levels, and slipping gears.
    * Solution: Replace the leaking part and add new transmission fluid.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Front strut failure**
* Description/Symptoms: Worn strut mounts, rattling or clunking noises, and impaired handling.
* Solution: Replace the front struts.
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800
* Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Electrical system failure**
* Description/Symptoms: Battery draining, flickering lights, and intermittent electrical issues.
* Solution: Troubleshoot and replace faulty electrical components, such as the alternator or battery.
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+
* Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Body panel fitment issues**
* Description/Symptoms: Misaligned body panels, gaps between panels, and rattles.
* Solution: Adjust or replace the affected body panels.
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
* Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Air conditioning system failure**
* Description/Symptoms: No cold air, weak airflow, or compressor noise.
* Solution: Recharge or replace the refrigerant, or inspect and repair the compressor.
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+
* Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2005 Ford EcoSport has a range of issues, from relatively minor electrical and interior problems to more severe engine and transmission concerns. The cost to fix these issues can vary significantly depending on the severity of the problem and the specific parts involved. While some issues may be covered under a warranty, others can be expensive to repair. It's important for potential buyers to be aware of these potential issues before purchasing a used EcoSport.
