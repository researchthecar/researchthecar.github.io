**Introduction**

The 2011 Ford EcoSport is a subcompact SUV that offers a combination of affordability, practicality, and fuel efficiency. It is available with two engine options: a 2.0-liter Duratec inline-four and a 1.6-liter Ecoboost turbocharged inline-four. Both engines are paired with a five-speed manual transmission or a six-speed PowerShift dual-clutch transmission. Despite its compact size, the EcoSport offers a roomy interior with plenty of cargo space. However, it has received some complaints from owners regarding various issues.

**Engine**

**2.0-liter Duratec Inline-Four**

- **Intake Manifold Gasket Failure**
  - Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaks, overheating, reduced engine power
  - Solution: Replace intake manifold gasket
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+
  - Recalls: None Found

- **Timing Belt Tensioner Failure**
  - Description/Symptoms: Engine rattling, reduced engine power, check engine light
  - Solution: Replace timing belt tensioner
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
  - Recalls: None Found

**1.6-liter Ecoboost Turbocharged Inline-Four**

- **Turbocharger Failure**
  - Description/Symptoms: Reduced engine power, black smoke from exhaust, whistling sound from engine
  - Solution: Replace turbocharger
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
  - Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**Five-Speed Manual Transmission**

- **Clutch Failure**
  - Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears, slipping gears
  - Solution: Replace clutch assembly
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
  - Recalls: None Found

**Six-Speed PowerShift Dual-Clutch Transmission**

- **Transmission Shudder**
  - Description/Symptoms: Vibrations when shifting gears, especially from 1st to 2nd
  - Solution: Replace or reprogram transmission control module
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500
  - Recalls: 14N01, 14S01, 15V170000

- **Hesitation or Delay When Shifting Gears**
  - Description/Symptoms: Slow response when shifting gears, especially when accelerating
  - Solution: Update transmission software or replace transmission
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+
  - Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

- **Front Suspension Strut Mounts**
  - Description/Symptoms: Clunking or rattling sounds from front suspension, poor handling
  - Solution: Replace strut mounts
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600
  - Recalls: None Found

- **Rear Suspension Coil Springs**
  - Description/Symptoms: Sagging rear suspension, loss of ride height
  - Solution: Replace rear springs
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
  - Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

- **Battery Drain**
  - Description/Symptoms: Vehicle battery drains prematurely, difficulty starting
  - Solution: Diagnose and repair electrical system issues
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
  - Recalls: None Found

- **Alternator Failure**
  - Description/Symptoms: Battery warning light on, flickering headlights, difficulty starting
  - Solution: Replace alternator
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600
  - Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

- **Headlight Condensation**
  - Description/Symptoms: Moisture inside headlights
  - Solution: Replace or reseal headlights
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
  - Recalls: None Found

- **Rear Door Handles**
  - Description/Symptoms: Difficult to open rear doors, broken handles
  - Solution: Replace rear door handles
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300
  - Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

- **Dashboard Rattle**
  - Description/Symptoms: Rattling sounds from dashboard area
  - Solution: Diagnose and fix loose components
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $400
  - Recalls: None Found

- **Center Console Bezels**
  - Description/Symptoms: Scratches or fading on center console bezels
  - Solution: Replace center console bezels
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300
  - Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

Overall, the 2011 Ford EcoSport is a generally reliable vehicle, but it has experienced some issues with its engine, transmission, and suspension. The severity of these issues ranges from minor inconveniences to more serious problems that can affect drivability. The cost of repairs can vary depending on the specific issue and the labor rates in your area. However, it is important to note that many of these issues have been addressed by Ford through recalls and software updates, so owners should ensure that their vehicles are up to date on all recommended maintenance and repairs.
