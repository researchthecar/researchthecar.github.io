**Introduction**

The 2009 Ford EcoSport is a subcompact SUV that was offered with a 2.0-liter Duratec 4-cylinder engine and a 4-speed automatic transmission. It produced 140 horsepower and 145 lb-ft of torque. The EcoSport was praised for its affordability, fuel efficiency, and spacious interior. However, it was also criticized for its underpowered engine and lack of features.

**Electrical**

* **Faulty ignition switch:** The ignition switch can fail, causing the vehicle to stall or not start. Symptoms include difficulty starting the vehicle, intermittent stalling, or the key getting stuck in the ignition. Replacing the ignition switch is the solution, and the estimated cost to fix is $250 - $400+.
* **Recall number: 14V619000: ** This recall was issued in 2014 for vehicles manufactured between 2011-2014. The ignition switch could fail, causing the engine to stall, the airbags to not deploy, or the vehicle to roll away while in park. The solution was to replace the ignition switch.

Interior

* **Cracked dashboard:** The dashboard can crack, especially in vehicles exposed to extreme heat. Symptoms include visible cracks on the dashboard. Replacing the dashboard is the solution, and the estimated cost to fix is $500 - $1000+.
* **Loose interior panels:** The interior panels can become loose, causing rattles and noises. Symptoms include rattling or buzzing sounds coming from the interior of the vehicle. Reattaching or replacing the loose panels is the solution, and the estimated cost to fix is $100 - $200+.

**Summary**

The 2009 Ford EcoSport is a reliable and affordable vehicle. However, it is not without its issues. The most common issues are faulty ignition switches and cracked dashboards. The cost to fix these issues can range from $100 to $1000+. Overall, the EcoSport is a good value for the money, but potential buyers should be aware of these potential issues before purchasing one.
