**Introduction:**

The 2019 Ford EcoSport is a subcompact SUV available with two engine options: a 1.0L EcoBoost three-cylinder and a 2.0L Ti-VCT four-cylinder. The 1.0L engine generates 123 horsepower and 125 pound-feet of torque, while the 2.0L engine produces 166 horsepower and 149 pound-feet of torque. Transmission options include a six-speed automatic for both engines and a six-speed manual for the 2.0L engine. The EcoSport is known for its compact size, fuel efficiency, and affordable price, but it has received some criticism for its lack of power and interior space.

**Engine**

**1.0L EcoBoost Three-Cylinder**

* **Turbocharger Failure:** Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, reduced fuel economy, whistling noise from the engine. Solution: Replace the turbocharger. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $1,500+ Recalls: None Found
* **Spark Plug Issues:** Description/Symptoms: Engine misfires, rough idle, check engine light. Solution: Replace the spark plugs. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200 Recalls: None Found

**2.0L Ti-VCT Four-Cylinder**

* **No Issues Found**

**Transmission**

**Six-Speed Automatic (Both Engines)**

* **Transmission Fluid Leak:** Description/Symptoms: Fluid leak under the vehicle, slipping gears, delayed or harsh shifting. Solution: Repair or replace the leaking component. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600+ Recalls: None Found
* **Shift Solenoid Failure:** Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears, transmission stuck in one gear. Solution: Replace the faulty solenoid. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800+ Recalls: None Found

**Six-Speed Manual (2.0L Engine)**

* **Clutch Issues:** Description/Symptoms: Slipping clutch, difficulty shifting gears, grinding noises. Solution: Replace the clutch. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800+ Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Front Strut Mount Bearing Failure:** Description/Symptoms: Clunking or rattling noises from the front suspension, uneven tire wear. Solution: Replace the strut mount bearings. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+ Recalls: None Found
* **Rear Shock Mount Failure:** Description/Symptoms: Loud noises from the rear suspension, bouncy ride. Solution: Replace the rear shock mounts. Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $600+ Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Alternator Failure:** Description/Symptoms: Dimming lights, difficulty starting the vehicle, electrical system issues. Solution: Replace the alternator. Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $600+ Recalls: None Found
* **Battery Drain:** Description/Symptoms: Battery dies quickly, especially after short trips. Solution: Identify and fix the parasitic drain. Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $200+ Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Body Panel Gaps:** Description/Symptoms: Uneven gaps between body panels, noticeable from various angles. Solution: Adjust or replace the affected body panels. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+ Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Wind Noise:** Description/Symptoms: Excessive wind noise entering the cabin, especially at highway speeds. Solution: Identify and seal or adjust the sources of the noise. Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $200+ Recalls: None Found
* **Dash Rattle:** Description/Symptoms: Rattles or squeaks coming from the dashboard. Solution: Identify and eliminate the source of the rattle. Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $200+ Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

The 2019 Ford EcoSport has several potential issues, particularly with its engine, transmission, and suspension. While some of these issues are minor and relatively inexpensive to fix, others can be more costly and may require significant repairs. Overall, the severity and cost of the problems vary depending on the specific issue and the affected components. It is important for owners and potential buyers to be aware of these potential problems and consider them when making purchasing or maintenance decisions.
