**2004 Ford EcoSport**

The 2004 Ford EcoSport is a compact SUV that was available with two engine options: a 2.0-liter Duratec inline-4 engine and a 3.0-liter Duratec V6 engine. Both engines were paired with a 5-speed manual transmission or a 4-speed automatic transmission.

People who like the EcoSport praise its affordability, fuel efficiency, and spacious interior. However, some common complaints include its lack of power, poor handling, and dated interior.

**Engine**

**2.0-liter Duratec I4**

* **Cylinder head gasket failure:** Blown gaskets can cause coolant and oil leaks, overheating, and engine damage. Solution: Replace the cylinder head gasket. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
* **Intake manifold gasket failure:** A leaking gasket can cause vacuum leaks, engine performance issues, and a decrease in fuel efficiency. Solution: Replace the intake manifold gasket. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
* **Timing belt failure:** A broken timing belt can cause catastrophic engine failure. Solution: Replace the timing belt and tensioner at the recommended intervals. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000

**3.0-liter Duratec V6**

* **Cam phaser failure:** Worn or damaged cam phasers can cause a rough idle, reduced engine power, and increased fuel consumption. Solution: Replace the cam phasers. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
* **Valve cover gasket leak:** A leaking gasket can cause oil leaks and engine performance issues. Solution: Replace the valve cover gasket. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500

**Transmission**

**5-speed Manual Transmission**

* **Clutch failure:** A worn or damaged clutch can cause difficulty shifting gears or slipping gears. Solution: Replace the clutch. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000

**4-speed Automatic Transmission**

* **Transmission fluid leak:** A leaking transmission can cause slipping gears, delayed shifting, and loss of power. Solution: Repair the leak and replace any lost transmission fluid. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
* **Torque converter failure:** A faulty torque converter can cause shuddering, slipping gears, and transmission overheating. Solution: Replace the torque converter. Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,500+

**Suspension**

* **Strut mount failure:** Worn or damaged strut mounts can cause clunking noises, poor handling, and reduced ride comfort. Solution: Replace the strut mounts. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
* **Sway bar link failure:** Broken or worn sway bar links can cause clunking noises and poor handling. Solution: Replace the sway bar links. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200

**Electrical**

* **Battery failure:** A weak or dead battery can prevent the vehicle from starting. Solution: Replace the battery. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200
* **Alternator failure:** A faulty alternator can prevent the battery from charging, which can lead to a dead battery. Solution: Replace the alternator. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000

**Exterior**

* **Door handle failure:** Broken or sticking door handles can make it difficult to enter or exit the vehicle. Solution: Replace the door handles. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200

**Interior**

* **Climate control malfunction:** A faulty climate control module can cause the air conditioning or heating system to malfunction. Solution: Replace the climate control module. Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800+
* **Power window failure:** Broken or inoperative power window motors or switches can prevent the windows from opening or closing. Solution: Replace the faulty components. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500

**Summary**

The 2004 Ford EcoSport has a number of potential issues that can affect its performance, reliability, and safety. While some of these issues are relatively minor and inexpensive to fix, others can be more serious and costly to repair. Overall, the EcoSport is a budget-friendly SUV with some known issues that potential owners should be aware of before purchasing.
