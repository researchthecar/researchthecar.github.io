# 2015 Ford Mustang

### Overview
The 2015 Ford Mustang, marking the debut of the sixth generation, introduced a significant redesign with a more global appeal and a new independent rear suspension across all trims. This generation aimed to blend classic Mustang styling with modern performance and technology. Reliability data for this model year generally indicates a solid performance, with most reported issues being relatively minor or specific to certain configurations. Owners often praise its improved handling, powerful engine options, and updated interior. However, as with any vehicle, some common areas warrant attention, particularly concerning specific powertrain combinations and electronic features.

## Engine

### 2.3L EcoBoost
**Turbocharger Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of power, whistling noises, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Turbocharger inspection, repair, or replacement.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1500

**Coolant Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Overheating, visible coolant puddles, sweet smell.
- **Solution:** Inspection and replacement of hoses, gaskets, or water pump.
- **Cost:** $200 - $600

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Manual (Getrag MT82)
**Shifter Notches/Grinding**
- **Symptoms:** Difficulty shifting into gears, grinding noises, stiff shifter.
- **Solution:** Transmission fluid flush, clutch adjustment, or internal transmission repair/replacement.
- **Cost:** $300 - $1200


### 6-Speed Automatic (SelectShift)
**Hesitation/Jerking**
- **Symptoms:** Delayed acceleration, rough shifts, occasional jerking.
- **Solution:** Transmission fluid service, software update, or transmission control module (TCM) inspection.
- **Cost:** $200 - $700

### Summary Verdict
The 2015 Ford Mustang is generally considered a reliable vehicle, especially given its performance-oriented nature. The introduction of the independent rear suspension was a significant upgrade that improved handling without introducing widespread reliability concerns. The V6 and V8 engines have proven robust. The 2.3L EcoBoost, while offering excellent performance, has seen some reports of turbocharger and coolant issues, though these are not universal. Transmission reliability is decent, with the manual gearbox occasionally exhibiting shifter feel issues, and the automatic sometimes having minor hesitation. Electrical gremlins, particularly with the SYNC infotainment system, are the most common non-powertrain complaints. Overall, the 2015 Mustang offers a good balance of performance and dependability, with most potential issues being manageable.
