# 2019 Ford Mustang Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2019 Ford Mustang combines classic muscle-car styling with modern performance, offering a range of capable engines from the fuel-efficient EcoBoost four-cylinder to the iconic V8 GT. While it provides an engaging driving experience, prospective buyers should review recurring owner complaints regarding certain engine cooling concerns, interior door latches, and transmission behavior. Being aware of these areas can help you inspect a used model more thoroughly before purchasing.

---

## Engine

### 2.3L EcoBoost Turbocharged Inline-4

A turbocharged 2.3-liter four-cylinder engine producing around 310 horsepower and 350 lb-ft of torque, offering lively performance and solid fuel economy for daily driving.

**Coolant Intrusion**
- **Symptoms:** Owners report coolant loss, white exhaust smoke, engine misfires, and overheating warnings caused by coolant leaking into the engine cylinders.
- **Solution:** Professional inspection is required, which may involve head gasket replacement or an engine block/assembly replacement in severe cases.
- **Cost:** $2,200

### 5.0L Coyote V8

A naturally aspirated 5.0-liter V8 engine pushing out 460 horsepower and 420 lb-ft of torque, delivering classic muscle car acceleration, robust power, and a distinctive exhaust note.

## Transmission

### 10-Speed Automatic Transmission

A 10-speed automatic transmission designed to optimize fuel efficiency and power delivery across a wide gear range, though some owners report rough shifts over time.

**Shifting Hesitation and Jolt**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers experience noticeable hesitation followed by a harsh jolt or clunking noise during acceleration or when shifting between park, reverse, and drive.
- **Solution:** Have a dealer check for updated powertrain control modules or inspect internal components for wear.

### Getrag MT82 6-Speed Manual Transmission

A traditional 6-speed manual transmission preferred by driving enthusiasts for direct control, paired with a clutch system that requires regular inspection for wear.

## Suspension

### Wheel Lug Nuts

Wheels and associated hardware responsible for supporting vehicle weight and maintaining tire attachment.

**Swollen Lug Nuts**
- **Symptoms:** The outer metal caps on factory two-piece lug nuts swell over time, preventing standard tire wrenches from fitting and complicating flat tire changes or tire rotations.
- **Solution:** Replace the factory two-piece lug nuts with solid one-piece aftermarket alternatives.

## Electrical

### Backup Camera and Display

Electrical architecture governing cabin displays, backup cameras, power windows, and auxiliary convenience features.

**Intermittent Camera Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The infotainment display shows a blue screen, displays a 'backup camera not available' message, or flickers intermittently when shifting into reverse.
- **Solution:** Inspect camera wiring harnesses, software settings, or replace the rearview camera assembly if faulty.

## Exterior

### Door Latches

Interior cabin components including door handles, locks, and latches that control secure door closure.

**Door Unlatching While Driving**
- **Symptoms:** Driver or passenger doors unexpectedly unlock and pop slightly ajar while traveling at highway speeds, triggering a door ajar warning light.
- **Solution:** Inspect door latch mechanisms and lock actuators for mechanical failure or electrical glitches.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V263000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission:Gear Position Indication (Prndl)](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Mustang&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** If the driver exits the vehicle without the transmission being shifted into park, unintended vehicle movement may result, increasing the risk of injury or crash.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will reprogram the instrument cluster, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V076000: Electrical System: Instrument Cluster/Panel](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Mustang&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** At vehicle start-up, the Instrument Panel Cluster Assembly (IPC) may not function, showing a blank display. Driving with an inoperative cluster can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Ford has notified owners, and dealers will reprogram the Instrument Panel Cluster Assembly (IPC), free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V382000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission:Control Module (Tcm/Pcm/Tecm)](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Mustang&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** A rearview camera or reverse light that is disabled reduces the driver's rear view, increasing the risk of a crash. Disabled driver assist features (traction control, forward collision warning, blind spot warning, cross traffic indicator) may increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the PCM software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V442000: Back Over Prevention:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Mustang&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** A software error may cause the rearview camera to display a blank image, or the image may remain on the display after the backing event has ended. Both of these situations can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the rearview camera software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V572000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Mustang&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** A rearview camera that displays an inverted, distorted, or blank image can reduce the driver’s view behind the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the rearview camera as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V372000: Electrical System: Instrument Cluster/Panel](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Mustang&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** At vehicle start-up, the Instrument Panel Cluster Assembly (IPC) may not function, showing a blank display. As a result, these vehicles fail to comply with Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 101, "Controls and Displays." A blank instrument cluster that fails to display critical safety information, such as vehicle speed or safety system.
- **What to do:** Dealers will reprogram the Instrument Panel Cluster Assembly (IPC) software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2019 Ford Mustang is a thrilling and generally enjoyable sports car, but used buyers should perform targeted checks before finalizing a purchase. Pay special attention to the service history of EcoBoost models for signs of coolant leaks, test drive automatics carefully to ensure smooth gear shifts, and verify that the door latches and backup camera operate correctly. Investing in a pre-purchase inspection can save you from unexpected repair bills down the road.
