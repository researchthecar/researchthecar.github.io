# 2021 Ford Mustang Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2021 Ford Mustang, buyers will find an iconic modern muscle car that delivers thrilling performance paired with everyday usability. Available with both punchy turbocharged four-cylinder engines and roaring V8 options, the Mustang caters to diverse driving styles. However, prospective buyers should review owner complaints regarding powertrain behavior, electrical glitches, and visibility accessories to ensure a trouble-free ownership experience.

---

## Engine

### 2.3L EcoBoost Turbocharged Inline-4

The 2.3-liter turbocharged EcoBoost inline-4 produces roughly 310 to 330 horsepower, offering a balanced mix of efficiency and lively acceleration. It is a popular entry point for daily driving, though buyers should verify fuel system service records.

**Engine Stalling and Hesitation**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicles may experience engine hesitation, loss of motive power, or unexpected stalling while driving.
- **Solution:** Check for open recall campaigns and have the low-pressure fuel pump inspected or replaced by a qualified dealer.

### 5.0L Ti-VCT V8

The legendary 5.0-liter Coyote V8 delivers 450 to 470 horsepower, providing traditional muscle car performance and a distinct exhaust note. It remains a favorite among enthusiasts seeking high-output thrills.

## Transmission

### 10-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 10-speed automatic transmission is designed to optimize fuel economy and acceleration across various driving conditions, though some owners report rough shifting behavior.

**Shifting Irregularities and Unexpected Neutral Shifts**
- **Symptoms:** The transmission may hesitate, shift abruptly, make clunking or whining noises, or unexpectedly slip into neutral while driving.
- **Solution:** Have a professional technician inspect the transmission control module, update software, or check mechanical components for wear.

### 6-Speed Manual Transmission

The traditional 6-speed manual transmission gives drivers direct control over gear selection, appealing to purists who prefer an engaging, hands-on driving dynamic.

## Suspension

### Suspension and Alignment

The suspension setup delivers sharp handling and responsive cornering, though regular alignment checks are recommended to maximize tire longevity.

**Front Suspension Popping and Tire Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may hear popping noises from the wheel wells during tight turns or notice accelerated inner tread tire wear.
- **Solution:** Check suspension components for loose fasteners and verify proper wheel alignment specifications.

## Electrical

### Back Over Prevention

Provides rear visibility aids to assist with parking and reversing maneuvers, though display reliability can occasionally falter.

**Backup Camera Display Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** The rearview camera image may go completely blank, display distorted or flipped video feeds, or flash between black and blue screens.
- **Solution:** Inspect camera wiring connections, update software, or replace the camera unit if internal hardware has failed.

## Exterior

### Door Latch and Structure

Cabin features and hardware offer a driver-focused cockpit, but physical latch integrity should be thoroughly checked during a pre-purchase inspection.

**Door Latch Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Doors may unexpectedly unlatch or pop open to the secondary position while driving at highway speeds.
- **Solution:** Inspect and adjust or replace the door latch assembly to ensure secure closure.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V980000: Air Bags:Side/Window:Curtain](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Mustang&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard numbers 226, "Ejection Mitigation," and 214, "Side Impact Protection." An air bag that does not inflate properly can increase the risk of injury in a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the side curtain air bags, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V083000: Air Bags:Knee Bolster](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Mustang&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** An insufficient weld on the front passenger knee air bag may result in an improper air bag deployment. An improper air bag deployment can increase the risk of injury during a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the front passenger knee air bag, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V727000: Electrical System:Body Control Module/Bcm](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Mustang&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The brake fluid level sensor may not activate the visual warning indicator when the brake fluid is low. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 135, "Light Vehicle Brake Systems." Without a low brake fluid warning light, the vehicle may be driven with low brake fluid, reducing braking.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the body control module software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V442000: Back Over Prevention:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Mustang&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** A software error may cause the rearview camera to display a blank image, or the image may remain on the display after the backing event has ended. Both of these situations can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the rearview camera software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V455000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Mustang&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** An engine stall increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the low pressure fuel pump on super duty vehicles and update the powertrain control module (PCM) software on all other affected vehicles, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V689000: Electrical System:Body Control Module/Bcm](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Mustang&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The brake fluid level sensor may not activate the visual warning indicator when the brake fluid is low. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 135, "Light Vehicle Brake Systems." Without a low brake fluid warning light, the vehicle may be driven with low brake fluid, reducing braking.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the body control module software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2021 Ford Mustang is an exciting and capable sports car choice for used vehicle shoppers, offering strong engine options and sharp styling. While most models perform reliably, buyers should carefully inspect the vehicle's transmission shifting behavior, test all backup camera functions, and verify that all safety recalls—particularly those concerning fuel system components—have been properly addressed by a certified dealer.
