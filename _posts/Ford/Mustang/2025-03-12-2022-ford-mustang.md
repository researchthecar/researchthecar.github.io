The 2022 Ford Mustang is a popular sports car known for its performance and iconic styling. Engine options include the 2.3L EcoBoost I4 (310-330 hp, 350-350 lb-ft of torque), the 5.0L Coyote V8 (450-470 hp, 410-420 lb-ft of torque), and the supercharged 5.2L Predator V8 (760 hp, 625 lb-ft of torque) found in the Shelby GT500. Transmission options consist of a 6-speed manual and a 10-speed automatic, depending on the engine and trim level. Owners often praise the Mustang's powerful engines, sporty handling, and attractive design. Common complaints include a cramped rear seat, limited visibility, and some concerns about interior plastic quality.

**Engine**

**2.3L EcoBoost I4**

*   **No Issues Found**

**5.0L Coyote V8**

*   **Excessive Oil Consumption**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners report high oil consumption, requiring frequent top-offs between oil changes. This can manifest as a noticeable decrease in oil level on the dipstick.
    *   Solution: In some cases, Ford has addressed this issue by replacing piston rings or the entire engine. However, the fix can vary depending on the severity and cause of the consumption. Some owners have also found that using a thicker oil can help reduce consumption.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $3,000 - $8,000+ (depending on the repair)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**5.2L Predator V8 (Shelby GT500)**

*   **No Issues Found**

**Transmission**

**6-Speed Manual**

*   **No Issues Found**

**10-Speed Automatic (10R80)**

*   **Harsh Shifting/Jerking**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners experience harsh shifting, jerking, or clunking, particularly during low-speed maneuvers or when shifting between certain gears. This is not always experienced on all models.
    *   Solution: A software reflash of the Transmission Control Module (TCM) can sometimes resolve the issue. In more severe cases, valve body replacement or transmission replacement may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300 (reflash), $1,000 - $4,000+ (replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **No Issues Found**

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Freezing/Crashing**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The SYNC 4 infotainment system can sometimes freeze, crash, or become unresponsive, requiring a system reset or reboot.
    *   Solution: Software updates can often resolve these issues. Checking for updates and installing the latest version of SYNC can help. In some cases, a hard reset of the system may be required.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 (software updates), $100 - $300 (diagnostic and repair)
    *   Recalls: None Found
*   **Battery Drain**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The vehicle's battery drains quickly, especially when sitting for extended periods of time. This can lead to difficulty starting the engine.
    *   Solution: Have the battery and charging system tested by a qualified mechanic. Identify and address any parasitic drain issues. If the battery is defective, replace it with a new one.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Rear Window Louver Fitment Issues (if equipped)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: On models equipped with factory or aftermarket rear window louvers, some owners have reported poor fitment or rattling noises due to loose mounting hardware.
    *   Solution: Adjust or replace mounting hardware, add padding or shims to improve fitment.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $200+ (depending on the fix)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Loose Trim Pieces**
    * Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported that certain interior trim pieces, such as those on the dashboard, door panels, or center console, may come loose or rattle over time.
    * Solution: The trim pieces can be re-secured by a technician. The solution often involves replacing clips, adding padding, or tightening screws.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $200
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2022 Ford Mustang generally presents a mixed bag of potential issues. The 5.0L Coyote V8 can sometimes suffer from oil consumption, which can be a significant and costly repair. The 10-speed automatic transmission can also exhibit harsh shifting behavior, though software updates may resolve the issue. Electrical glitches with the infotainment system and battery drain are also possible, though these are generally less severe. Exterior fitment issues are mostly cosmetic and can be easily addressed. Overall, while the Mustang offers a thrilling driving experience, potential buyers should be aware of these common problems and factor them into their purchasing decision.
