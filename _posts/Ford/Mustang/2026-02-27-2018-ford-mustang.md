# 2018 Ford Mustang Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2018 Ford Mustang delivers iconic American muscle styling and strong performance across its engine lineup, but buyers should approach used models with an understanding of a few notable mechanical and body hardware complaints. While many owners enjoy trouble-free driving, reported issues involving coolant leaks on EcoBoost models and unexpected door latch openings require careful inspection prior to purchase. Checking service records and verifying that safety recalls have been completed will help ensure a more reliable ownership experience.

---

## Engine

### 2.3L EcoBoost Turbocharged Inline-4

A turbocharged 2.3-liter four-cylinder engine producing around 310 horsepower and 350 lb-ft of torque, offering lively performance and solid fuel economy. Buyers should thoroughly inspect the cooling system and check for signs of coolant loss or engine misfires.

**Coolant Intrusion and Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Engine sputtering, white smoke from the tailpipe, illuminated check engine lights, and engine misfires caused by coolant leaking into the cylinders.
- **Solution:** Requires professional diagnosis and potentially head gasket replacement, cylinder head replacement, or a complete engine assembly replacement depending on severity.

### 5.0L Ti-VCT V8

A high-output 5.0-liter naturally aspirated V8 generating 460 horsepower and 420 lb-ft of torque, delivering classic muscle car performance and an aggressive exhaust note. This powertrain is generally robust, though routine maintenance is essential for high-mileage or track-driven examples.

## Transmission

### 10-Speed Automatic Transmission

A modern 10-speed automatic transmission designed to maximize acceleration and highway efficiency across both engine options. While generally smooth, some owners report hesitation or rough shifting behavior requiring software recalibration.

**Shifting Delays and Harsh Engagement**
- **Symptoms:** Transmission hesitating, aggressive jerking when shifting between park and drive, or unexpected lurches while attempting to accelerate.
- **Solution:** Check transmission fluid condition, update control modules, or consult a transmission specialist for internal component inspection if slipping or harsh shifting persists.

### Getrag MT82 6-Speed Manual Transmission

A traditional 6-speed manual gearbox providing direct driver engagement for enthusiasts pairing it with either the EcoBoost or GT V8 engines. Buyers should test clutch feel and shift crispness during a pre-purchase test drive.

## Brakes

### Brake Pedal and Parking Brake

Braking performance is strong and confidence-inspiring when maintained properly, though minor switch failures and parking brake adjustments can arise.

**Brake Light Switch and Parking Brake Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** Brake lights remaining permanently illuminated when parked with the ignition off, or parking brakes failing to fully engage or release.
- **Solution:** Inspect and replace broken plastic pedal bumpers or faulty brake light switches, and adjust or repair parking brake cables as needed.

## Electrical

### Instrument Cluster and Infotainment

Electrical architecture supports advanced infotainment and digital gauges, but occasional screen blackouts and cold-weather touchscreen glitches have been noted by owners.

**Blank Display Screens**
- **Symptoms:** Instrument clusters or touchscreen infotainment displays remaining blank upon vehicle startup or failing to turn on in cooler temperatures.
- **Solution:** Check for software updates, inspect related fuses and wiring harnesses, or replace malfunctioning display modules.

## Exterior

### Door Latch and Structure

Interior build quality is generally sporty and driver-focused, though door hardware reliability requires verification to prevent unexpected passenger door openings.

**Doors Unlatching Unexpectedly**
- **Symptoms:** Passenger doors unlatching and opening independently while driving at highway speeds, accompanied by door-ajar warning lights and chimes.
- **Solution:** Inspect door latch mechanisms and locking linkages, and have faulty door latches replaced by a qualified service center.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18E035000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Engine:Gasoline:Turbo/Supercharger](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Mustang&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** A fuel leak can increase the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 17V814000: Electrical System:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Mustang&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** If the ignition is turned off when the transmission is not in PARK, the instrument cluster may not illuminate the PRNDL gear position display and the operator may not receive the "Key in Ignition" chime when the driver's door is opened. As such, these vehicles fails to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 102.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will update the instrument cluster software, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V213000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Mustang&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** A roll pin may not have been installed in the transmission, potentially causing a loss of the "Park" function. Despite the transmission shift lever being placed in 'Park' and the instrument panel display indicating 'Park,' the vehicle may roll away after it has been exited if the parking brake has not been applied, increasing the risk of injury or a crash.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the transmission to make sure it has the roll pin, installing one if it is missing, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V572000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Mustang&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** A rearview camera that displays an inverted, distorted, or blank image can reduce the driver’s view behind the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the rearview camera as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2018 Ford Mustang is an exciting and generally rewarding performance car, but potential buyers should exercise caution. Prioritize a thorough pre-purchase inspection focusing on the EcoBoost engine's cooling system, transmission shift quality, and door latch operation. Ensuring that all open safety recalls have been addressed will give you peace of mind before finalizing your purchase.
