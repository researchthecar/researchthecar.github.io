# 2004 Ford Mustang

### Overview
The 2004 Ford Mustang, a popular choice for performance enthusiasts, offered a range of powertrain options and a classic rear-wheel-drive platform. While generally considered a robust vehicle for its era, like any mass-produced automobile, it has certain areas that have seen more reported issues than others. This analysis focuses on common mechanical and electrical concerns reported by owners and technicians, excluding routine maintenance. Understanding these potential problem areas can help current and prospective owners make informed decisions regarding maintenance and repair.

---


## Engine

### 3.9L Essex V6
**Intake Manifold Gasket Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Coolant leaks, engine misfires, rough idle, overheating.
- **Solution:** Replacement of intake manifold gaskets.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600

**Spark Plug Blow-Out**
- **Symptoms:** Engine misfires, loss of power, ticking noise from engine.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of spark plug threads, often requiring specialized kits.
- **Cost:** $400 - $800


### 4.6L Modular V8 (Pony/GT)
**Intake Manifold Gasket Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Coolant leaks, engine misfires, rough idle, overheating.
- **Solution:** Replacement of intake manifold gaskets.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600

**Timing Chain Tensioner Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Rattling noise from the front of the engine, especially on startup.
- **Solution:** Replacement of timing chain tensioners and guides.
- **Cost:** $600 - $1200


## Transmission

### 4-Speed Automatic (4R70W)
**Torque Converter Shudder**
- **Symptoms:** Vibration or shuddering during acceleration, especially at lower speeds.
- **Solution:** Torque converter replacement or transmission fluid service.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1000

**Slipping Gears**
- **Symptoms:** Delayed engagement, difficulty shifting, engine revving without acceleration.
- **Solution:** Transmission rebuild or replacement.
- **Cost:** $1500 - $3000


### 5-Speed Manual (T-45/T-5)
**Clutch Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Slipping clutch, difficulty shifting, burning smell.
- **Solution:** Clutch kit replacement (clutch disc, pressure plate, release bearing).
- **Cost:** $500 - $900

**Shifter Linkage Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Stiff or loose shifter, difficulty engaging gears.
- **Solution:** Adjustment or replacement of shifter linkage components.
- **Cost:** $150 - $300


## Suspension

### Standard Chassis
**Worn Ball Joints and Tie Rod Ends**
- **Symptoms:** Clunking noises over bumps, vague steering, uneven tire wear.
- **Solution:** Replacement of worn ball joints and tie rod ends.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600

**Rear Axle Seal Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Grease or fluid leaks from the rear axle area.
- **Solution:** Replacement of rear axle seals.
- **Cost:** $200 - $400


## Electrical

### General Electrical System
**Window Motor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Power windows not operating.
- **Solution:** Replacement of window motor.
- **Cost:** $200 - $400

**Alternator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Battery warning light illuminated, dimming headlights, starting issues.
- **Solution:** Replacement of alternator.
- **Cost:** $400 - $700


## Exterior

### Body Panels and Trim
**Paint Fading/Peeling**
- **Symptoms:** Discoloration, bubbling, or peeling of the paint, especially on the roof and hood.
- **Solution:** Repainting affected areas.
- **Cost:** $500 - $2000+

**Headlight Haze/Yellowing**
- **Symptoms:** Reduced visibility due to oxidized headlight lenses.
- **Solution:** Headlight restoration or replacement.
- **Cost:** $100 - $300


## Interior

### Interior Components
**HVAC Blower Motor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** No air coming from vents or intermittent airflow.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the HVAC blower motor.
- **Cost:** $200 - $400

**Seat Fabric Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Tearing or excessive wear on seat upholstery, particularly on the driver's side bolster.
- **Solution:** Seat cover replacement or professional upholstery repair.
- **Cost:** $200 - $600

### Summary Verdict
The 2004 Ford Mustang, particularly models equipped with the V8 engines, is generally considered a reliable vehicle with a strong enthusiast following. Common issues tend to be related to age and mileage, such as intake manifold gasket leaks and, on V8 models, timing chain tensioner wear. Automatic transmissions can sometimes exhibit torque converter shudder or slipping. Suspension components like ball joints and tie rods are also typical wear items. Electrical gremlins, such as failing window motors and alternators, can occur. Exterior paint issues and interior wear are also not uncommon given the age of the vehicle. Overall, with proper maintenance and addressing these known potential problems, the 2004 Mustang can remain a dependable and enjoyable car.
