# 2006 Ford Mustang

### Overview
The 2006 Ford Mustang, a popular choice for performance enthusiasts, offered a range of powertrain options and a classic design. While generally considered a robust vehicle, like any car, it has specific areas that have seen more attention from owners regarding reliability. This analysis focuses on documented technical issues beyond routine maintenance, examining common problems reported across its various engine and transmission configurations. Understanding these potential pitfalls can help owners make informed decisions about maintenance and repairs, ensuring the longevity and optimal performance of their Mustang.

---


## Engine

### 4.0L V6 (SOHC)
**Camshaft Synchronizer Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Rattling noise from the front of the engine, especially at idle or low RPMs.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the camshaft synchronizer assembly.
- **Cost:** $400 - $700


### 4.6L V8 (SOHC)
**Spark Plug Blowout**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of engine power, misfires, ticking noise, potential for spark plug to be ejected from cylinder head.
- **Solution:** Requires specialized tools to re-thread or repair the cylinder head, or in severe cases, cylinder head replacement.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1500

**Intake Manifold Gasket Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Coolant leaks, engine overheating, rough idle, potential for coolant to enter cylinders.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the intake manifold gaskets.
- **Cost:** $500 - $900


## Transmission

### 5-Speed Manual (T-5)
**Clutch Pedal Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Stiff clutch pedal, difficulty shifting gears, clutch not fully disengaging.
- **Solution:** Inspection and potential replacement of clutch master cylinder, slave cylinder, or clutch assembly.
- **Cost:** $600 - $1200


### 5-Speed Automatic (5R55S)
**Harsh Shifting / Slipping**
- **Symptoms:** Jerky gear changes, delayed engagement, slipping between gears.
- **Solution:** Transmission fluid and filter change, or internal transmission repair/rebuild.
- **Cost:** $500 - $2500+

## Electrical

### General Electrical System
**Window Regulator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Power windows not operating, slow window movement, grinding noise.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the window regulator and motor assembly.
- **Cost:** $200 - $400

**Instrument Cluster Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** Gauges not working, warning lights staying on, dim or flickering displays.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of the instrument cluster.
- **Cost:** $400 - $800

### Summary Verdict
The 2006 Ford Mustang is a generally reliable vehicle, particularly for its class and era. The V8 models, while offering exhilarating performance, are more prone to specific issues like spark plug blowout and intake manifold leaks, which can be costly to repair. The V6 engine has a notable concern with camshaft synchronizer failure. Transmission issues, especially with the automatic, can arise and vary in repair cost. Electrical components like window regulators and instrument clusters are also areas where owners may encounter problems. Overall, with diligent maintenance and awareness of these common issues, the 2006 Mustang can provide many years of enjoyable driving.
