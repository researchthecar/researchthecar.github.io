# 2016 Ford Mustang Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used Ford Mustang, buyers will find a blend of iconic styling, rear-wheel-drive performance, and everyday usability. However, reviewing owner-reported experiences reveals several recurring mechanical and electrical concerns that warrant a careful inspection before purchase. Prospective owners should pay close attention to engine health, body hardware, and interior electronics.

---

## Engine

### 3.7L V6

The base 3.7-liter V6 engine produces around 300 horsepower and 280 lb-ft of torque, offering solid entry-level performance and generally dependable daily operation.

### 2.3L EcoBoost Turbocharged Inline-4

The 2.3-liter EcoBoost turbocharged four-cylinder engine delivers approximately 310 horsepower and 320 lb-ft of torque, balancing efficiency with brisk acceleration, though buyers should watch for cooling-related issues.

**Coolant Intrusion and Head Gasket Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Engine misfires, white smoke from the exhaust, low coolant levels, and illuminated check engine lights.
- **Solution:** Requires professional engine inspection and potentially replacing the head gasket or cylinder head assembly.

**Engine Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** Sudden loss of engine power while driving, rough idling, and difficulty restarting.
- **Solution:** Diagnose fuel system components, purge valves, or powertrain control modules for faults.

### 5.0L V8

The powerful 5.0-liter V8 engine pushes out around 435 horsepower and 400 lb-ft of torque, providing classic muscle car performance for enthusiast drivers.

**Coolant Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Low coolant warning lights, visible fluid pooling under the vehicle, and sweet-smelling odors.
- **Solution:** Inspect hoses, water pump, and gaskets, replacing damaged components as needed.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Manual Transmission

The Getrag 6-speed manual transmission gives drivers direct control over gear selection, though mechanical wear can sometimes lead to stiff shifting or clutch concerns.

**Clutch and Shifting Difficulties**
- **Symptoms:** Clunking noises when engaging gears, gear clash during shifts, or unexpected loss of motive power.
- **Solution:** Inspect clutch assembly, linkage, and transmission internals for wear.

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 6-speed automatic transmission features paddle shifters for manual control, providing smooth gear changes for daily commuting and cruising.

## Suspension

### Wheel and Lug Nuts

The suspension and wheel assemblies deliver agile handling and a firm ride quality suited for a sporty coupe or convertible.

**Swollen Lug Nuts**
- **Symptoms:** Factory lug nuts becoming distorted or swollen, making standard tire removal tools difficult or impossible to fit.
- **Solution:** Replace factory two-piece lug nuts with solid aftermarket alternatives.

## Brakes

### Brake Pedal Assembly

The braking system provides strong stopping power, but small plastic pedal stops can degrade over time and trigger electrical malfunctions.

**Brake Light Switch and Pedal Pad Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Brake lights remaining illuminated even when the vehicle is turned off, or the vehicle entering limp mode due to faulty sensor pads.
- **Solution:** Replace the deteriorated brake pedal stop pad and faulty position sensor.

## Electrical

### Infotainment and Camera System

Electrical accessories provide modern connectivity and convenience, but infotainment displays and backup camera systems have been known to exhibit intermittent glitches.

**Backup Camera and Display Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Rearview camera image appearing blank, blurry, or flickering, and touchscreen display screens going completely dark.
- **Solution:** Update software, repair wiring harnesses, or replace the backup camera and display module.

## Exterior

### Door Latches

Interior cabin components are generally resilient, though door latch mechanisms require vigilance regarding secure closing.

**Doors Opening While Driving**
- **Symptoms:** Driver or passenger doors unexpectedly swinging open or failing to remain securely latched while the vehicle is in motion.
- **Solution:** Inspect and replace faulty door latch mechanisms.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 15V707000: Seat Belts](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Mustang&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** The front and rear seat belts and child seat tether anchors may have been damaged in shipping from the supplier to the vehicle assembly plant and may not adequately restrain the occupant in a crash. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard numbers 208, "Occupant Crash Protection," and 225, "Child.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will replace the front and rear seat belt assemblies and the child seat tether anchors, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V779000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Cooling System:Hoses/Lines/Piping/Fittings](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Mustang&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** A sudden loss of engine oil may cause engine failure, increasing the risk of a crash. Additionally, an oil leak, in the presence of an ignition source, increases the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will replace the engine oil cooler tube assembly, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V082000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Mustang&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** A rearview camera that displays a blank or distorted image can reduce the driver's rear view, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and repair the decklid wiring harness and/or replace the rearview camera, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V572000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Mustang&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** A rearview camera that displays an inverted, distorted, or blank image can reduce the driver’s view behind the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the rearview camera as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V614000: Seat Belts:Pretensioner](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Mustang&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** A corroded cable can break, preventing the seat belt from properly restraining an occupant, and increasing the risk of injury during a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace both front seat belt anchor pretensioner assemblies, as necessary. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

Overall, this vehicle offers exciting performance and striking design, but used-car buyers should perform a thorough inspection prior to purchase. Pay close attention to EcoBoost engine coolant levels, test all interior electronics and backup cameras, and ensure the door latches and brake pedal components operate correctly.
