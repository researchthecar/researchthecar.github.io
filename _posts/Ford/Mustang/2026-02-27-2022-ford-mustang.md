# 2022 Ford Mustang Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2022 Ford Mustang delivers iconic American muscle performance and modern cabin tech, but prospective buyers should carefully review owner-reported complaints and safety recalls. While many drivers enjoy its powerful engine options and responsive handling, some owners have experienced mechanical and electrical glitches ranging from minor software hiccups to notable safety concerns. Checking service records and verifying that all open recalls have been completed by a certified dealership is an essential step before purchasing.

---

## Engine

### 2.3L EcoBoost Turbocharged Inline-4

The 2.3-liter turbocharged inline-4 engine produces around 310 horsepower and 350 lb-ft of torque, offering a balanced mix of efficiency and spirited performance. It serves as the standard entry-level powerplant for the Mustang lineup.

### 5.0L Coyote V8

The naturally aspirated 5.0-liter V8 engine pushes out roughly 450 to 470 horsepower depending on the specific GT trim and package, delivering traditional high-displacement muscle car acceleration. It is a robust performance option prized for its exhaust note and top-end power.

### Fuel System

Monitors fuel delivery and pressure across both EcoBoost and V8 configurations to ensure consistent power supply.

**Engine Shudder and Fuel System Concerns**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have reported experiencing unexpected engine shuddering while driving at highway speeds, alongside notifications regarding fuel system safety campaigns.
- **Solution:** Have a certified dealer inspect the fuel system components and perform any required manufacturer-recommended safety recall remedies.

## Transmission

### 10-Speed SelectShift Automatic

This 10-speed automatic transmission is paired with both EcoBoost and GT models, providing quick gear changes and optimized highway cruising efficiency.

### Getrag MT82 6-Speed Manual

The 6-speed manual transmission gives enthusiast drivers direct mechanical control over gear selection, though proper clutch maintenance is vital for long-term durability.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

Responsible for hydraulic deceleration and stopping power across all four wheels.

**Stiff Brake Pedal and Reduced Stopping Response**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may experience a hard brake pedal feel accompanied by continued vehicle roll when attempting to come to a complete stop.
- **Solution:** Check brake booster function, vacuum lines, and hydraulic pressure; replace malfunctioning booster components immediately.

## Steering

### Steering Column and U-Joint

Provides power-assisted directional control, translating steering wheel inputs smoothly to the front wheels.

**Sticky Steering U-Joint**
- **Symptoms:** The steering wheel may catch, hitch, or become difficult to turn smoothly, particularly at speeds above 50 mph.
- **Solution:** Have the steering intermediate shaft and universal joints inspected and replaced if mechanical binding is detected.

## Electrical

### Electrical System & Screens

Manages vehicle wiring, battery power distribution, and digital display modules throughout the cabin.

**Instrument Cluster and Infotainment Blackout**
- **Symptoms:** Both the instrument cluster and infotainment screens may suddenly go blank while driving, resulting in a temporary loss of visible gauges and turn signal indicators.
- **Solution:** Inspect software versions, wiring harnesses, and battery health; visit a dealer for module updates or replacements if necessary.

## Exterior

### Door Latches and Glass

Includes exterior body panels, door assemblies, weather stripping, and window alignment mechanisms.

**Unintended Door Opening**
- **Symptoms:** Doors may unexpectedly unlatch or swing open while driving at normal road speeds, accompanied by a door-ajar warning message.
- **Solution:** Inspect door latches, strikers, and electronic locking mechanisms for proper alignment or defect, and consult a dealer for latch repairs.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V083000: Air Bags:Knee Bolster](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Mustang&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** An insufficient weld on the front passenger knee air bag may result in an improper air bag deployment. An improper air bag deployment can increase the risk of injury during a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the front passenger knee air bag, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V334000: Forward Collision Avoidance: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Mustang&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** A front-facing camera that is not functioning properly may restrict or disable certain features including Pre-Collision Assist, Adaptive Cruise Control, Lane Keeping System, Driver Alert, and Auto High Beam Control, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will realign the front-facing camera, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V070000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Mustang&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** The transmission may contain a loose bolt which could prevent the transmission from engaging the park gear, although the gear shifter position may indicate that the vehicle has been shifted to "PARK." The inability to secure the vehicle in the park position can result in a rollaway, increasing the risk of a crash or injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and, if necessary, replace the transmissions, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V727000: Electrical System:Body Control Module/Bcm](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Mustang&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** The brake fluid level sensor may not activate the visual warning indicator when the brake fluid is low. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 135, "Light Vehicle Brake Systems." Without a low brake fluid warning light, the vehicle may be driven with low brake fluid, reducing braking.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the body control module software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V493000: Steering:Electric Power Assist System](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Mustang&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** The secondary steering torque sensor may not be calibrated properly, which can cause the steering wheel to turn side to side against the driver's intentions. A steering wheel that turns unintentionally can cause a loss of vehicle control and increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the power steering control module (PSCM) software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V096000: Steering:Electric Power Assist System](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Mustang&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** The secondary steering torque sensor may not be calibrated properly, which can cause the steering wheel to turn side to side against the driver's intentions. A steering wheel that turns unintentionally can cause a loss of vehicle control and increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the power steering control module (PSCM) software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V442000: Back Over Prevention:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Mustang&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** A software error may cause the rearview camera to display a blank image, or the image may remain on the display after the backing event has ended. Both of these situations can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the rearview camera software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V455000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Mustang&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** An engine stall increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the low pressure fuel pump on super duty vehicles and update the powertrain control module (PCM) software on all other affected vehicles, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2022 Ford Mustang is an exciting rear-wheel-drive sports car that offers thrilling performance, but buyers should approach used models with due diligence. Reviewing vehicle history reports for odometer accuracy, checking for unresolved electrical or door-latch issues, and confirming that all official factory safety recalls have been addressed will help ensure a safer and more dependable ownership experience.
