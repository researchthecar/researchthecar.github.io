# 2020 Ford Mustang Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2020 Ford Mustang offers iconic styling and punchy performance, but used-car buyers should review potential trouble spots before purchasing. While owner satisfaction is generally high, owner reports highlight recurring concerns relating to the powertrain, electrical system, and safety components. Taking the time to thoroughly inspect a pre-owned model can save you from unexpected repair bills.

---

## Engine

### 2.3L EcoBoost Turbocharged Inline-4

This turbocharged 4-cylinder engine produces around 310 horsepower and 350 lb-ft of torque, offering an entry-level balance of fuel efficiency and spirited acceleration for daily driving.

### 5.0L Coyote V8

The naturally aspirated 5.0L V8 pumps out 460 horsepower (or higher in specialized trims), delivering classic muscle car performance alongside a distinctive exhaust note.

**Coolant Intrusion & Head Gasket Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Low coolant levels, white smoke from the exhaust, engine overheating, and illuminated check engine lights indicating misfires.
- **Solution:** Requires professional diagnosis and potentially cylinder head or engine block replacement depending on severity.

**Loss of Motive Power & Throttle Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Sudden hesitation, jerky acceleration, or complete loss of power while driving due to failing intake manifold runner control (IMRC) components or throttle faults.
- **Solution:** Inspect and replace broken actuator rods or faulty throttle body assemblies.

## Transmission

### 10-Speed SelectShift Automatic

This 10-speed automatic transmission improves highway fuel economy and quick gear changes, though some owners report rough engagement over time.

**Harsh Shifting and Gear Slipping**
- **Symptoms:** Hard slamming into gear, sudden downshifts to first gear while driving, and failure to engage proper gears leading to delayed acceleration.
- **Solution:** Reprogram the powertrain control module (PCM), inspect valve bodies, or rebuild/replace internal components if mechanical wear has occurred.

### Getrag MT82 6-Speed Manual

A traditional 6-speed manual gearbox providing direct driver engagement for enthusiasts, paired primarily with the EcoBoost and GT trims.

**Clutch and Gear Engagement Failures**
- **Symptoms:** Difficulty shifting between gears, clutch pedal sticking or failing to return properly, and premature clutch wear.
- **Solution:** Inspect the clutch assembly, hydraulic lines, and master/slave cylinders; replace worn clutch components as necessary.

## Brakes

### Hydraulic Brake System

Provides stopping power across various driving conditions, though maintenance of brake lines and switches is critical for safety.

**Brake Pedal Response Loss**
- **Symptoms:** Pedal feels soft or unresponsive, leading to extended stopping distances or failure to slow the vehicle adequately.
- **Solution:** Inspect brake lines, master cylinders, and brake light switches for wear, fluid leaks, or electrical faults.

## Steering

### Electric Power Steering System

Electric power-assist steering provides adjustable steering feel but can experience intermittent sensor or module faults.

**Steering Assist Fault**
- **Symptoms:** Warning messages on the dash indicating steering assist fault, hill start assist unavailability, and sudden heavy steering resistance.
- **Solution:** Diagnostic scan of the electronic power steering module and wiring connections; replacement of failing steering rack components if required.

## Electrical

### Infotainment and Backup Camera

Controls internal displays, infotainment features, and safety camera feeds, with occasional software glitches reported by owners.

**Backup Camera Display Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Rearview camera displays a blue or black screen, distorted images, or inverted/mirrored video feeds.
- **Solution:** Check for applicable software updates or replace faulty camera hardware and wiring harnesses.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V306000: Forward Collision Avoidance: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Mustang&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** The camera may be misaligned to the vehicle and features that are dependent on the camera such as Pre-Collision Assist, Adaptive Cruise Control, Lane Keeping System, Driver Alert, and Auto High Beam Control may not function as intended. If the driver is unaware that the Pre-Collision Assist feature is not operating effectively, they may rely on a system.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will perform the Lane Departure Warning System camera alignment procedure, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V573000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Pedals And Linkages](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Mustang&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** A broken brake pedal could affect braking ability, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will replace the brake pedal bracket assembly, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V263000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission:Gear Position Indication (Prndl)](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Mustang&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** If the driver exits the vehicle without the transmission being shifted into park, unintended vehicle movement may result, increasing the risk of injury or crash.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will reprogram the instrument cluster, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V575000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Mustang&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 111, "Rear Visibility." Reduced rearward visibility can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will replace the rearview camera, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V382000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission:Control Module (Tcm/Pcm/Tecm)](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Mustang&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** A rearview camera or reverse light that is disabled reduces the driver's rear view, increasing the risk of a crash. Disabled driver assist features (traction control, forward collision warning, blind spot warning, cross traffic indicator) may increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the PCM software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V727000: Electrical System:Body Control Module/Bcm](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Mustang&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** The brake fluid level sensor may not activate the visual warning indicator when the brake fluid is low. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 135, "Light Vehicle Brake Systems." Without a low brake fluid warning light, the vehicle may be driven with low brake fluid, reducing braking.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the body control module software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V442000: Back Over Prevention:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Mustang&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** A software error may cause the rearview camera to display a blank image, or the image may remain on the display after the backing event has ended. Both of these situations can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the rearview camera software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V695000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Mustang&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** A rearview camera that fails to properly display an image can reduce the driver’s view behind the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the rearview camera as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2020 Ford Mustang is a thrilling daily driver or weekend cruiser, but potential buyers should perform a thorough mechanical inspection before finalizing a purchase. Pay close attention to engine coolant levels, transmission shifting smoothness, and the operation of onboard electronics like the backup camera. Selecting a model with a clean service history and remaining factory warranty coverage can significantly reduce ownership risk.
