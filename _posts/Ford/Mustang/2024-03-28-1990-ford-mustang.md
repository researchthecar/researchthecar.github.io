**Introduction:**

The 1990 Ford Mustang is a popular muscle car that offers a thrilling driving experience. It is available with a variety of engine and transmission options, including a 2.3L inline-4, a 3.0L V6, and a 5.0L V8. The 5.0L V8 produces 225 horsepower and 300 lb-ft of torque, making it the most powerful option. Despite its popularity, the 1990 Mustang has some issues that owners should be aware of.

**Engine**

**2.3L Inline-4**

* **Intake manifold gasket failure:** The intake manifold gasket can fail, causing coolant and oil leaks. This can lead to overheating and engine damage if not repaired promptly.
    * Solution: Replace the intake manifold gasket.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500
    * Recalls: None Found

**3.0L V6**

* **Coolant leak from water pump:** The water pump can develop a leak, causing coolant to leak out. This can lead to overheating and engine damage if not repaired promptly.
    * Solution: Replace the water pump.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
    * Recalls: None Found

**5.0L V8**

* **Head gasket failure:** The head gasket can fail, causing coolant and oil to mix. This can lead to catastrophic engine damage if not repaired promptly.
    * Solution: Replace the head gasket.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**5-speed Manual**

* **Clutch failure:** The clutch can wear out over time, causing difficulty shifting gears.
    * Solution: Replace the clutch.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
    * Recalls: None Found

**AOD 4-speed Automatic**

* **Transmission slipping:** The transmission can slip when shifting gears, causing loss of power and acceleration.
    * Solution: Repair or replace the transmission.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $3,000
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Front strut mount failure:** The front strut mount can fail, causing a clunking noise and reduced handling.
    * Solution: Replace the strut mount.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Alternator failure:** The alternator can fail, causing electrical system problems, such as battery drain and dimming lights.
    * Solution: Replace the alternator.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Door handle failure:** The door handle can break, making it difficult to open the door.
    * Solution: Replace the door handle.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Center console lid failure:** The center console lid can break or become loose, making it difficult to access the storage compartment.
    * Solution: Replace the center console lid.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $150
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

The 1990 Ford Mustang is a powerful and fun-to-drive muscle car, but it has some issues that owners should be aware of. The most severe issues are the head gasket failure and the transmission slipping. These issues can lead to catastrophic engine or transmission damage if not repaired promptly. The cost to fix these issues can be significant, so it is important to budget for potential repairs.
