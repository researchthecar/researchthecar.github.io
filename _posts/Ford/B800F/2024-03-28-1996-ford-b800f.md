**Introduction**

The 1996 Ford B800F is a heavy-duty pickup truck available with a variety of engine and transmission options. It is known for its powerful engines and rugged construction. Some of the more popular options include the 7.3L Power Stroke diesel engine and the 5-speed manual transmission. People like the B800F for its towing and hauling capabilities, as well as its off-road performance. However, it is not without its issues.

**Engine**

**7.3L Power Stroke Diesel**

* **Injector Failure:** The injectors can fail, causing a loss of power and increased fuel consumption.
    * Solution: Replace the injectors.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
    * Recalls: 03V384000, 04V274000
* **Turbocharger Failure:** The turbocharger can fail, resulting in a loss of power and increased exhaust smoke.
    * Solution: Replace the turbocharger.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
    * Recalls: 04V431000
* **Head Gasket Failure:** The head gasket can fail, causing a loss of coolant and overheating.
    * Solution: Replace the head gasket.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**5-Speed Manual**

* **Clutch Failure:** The clutch can wear out prematurely, causing difficulty shifting gears or a loss of power.
    * Solution: Replace the clutch.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Synchro Failure:** The synchronizers can wear out, making it difficult or impossible to shift gears.
    * Solution: Replace the synchronizers.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Leaf Spring Failure:** The leaf springs can break or sag, resulting in a loss of handling and ride quality.
    * Solution: Replace the leaf springs.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Shock Absorber Failure:** The shock absorbers can wear out, causing a loss of ride quality and handling.
    * Solution: Replace the shock absorbers.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Alternator Failure:** The alternator can fail, causing a loss of electrical power and a dead battery.
    * Solution: Replace the alternator.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Starter Failure:** The starter can fail, making it difficult or impossible to start the engine.
    * Solution: Replace the starter.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Rust:** The B800F is prone to rust, especially on the undercarriage and body panels.
    * Solution: Treat rust with a rust inhibitor or have it repaired by a body shop.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the severity of the rust.
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Dashboard Cracking:** The dashboard can crack over time, especially in hot climates.
    * Solution: Replace the dashboard or have it repaired by an upholstery shop.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Seat Upholstery Tearing:** The seat upholstery can tear or wear out over time.
    * Solution: Replace the seat upholstery or have it repaired by an upholstery shop.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 1996 Ford B800F has several potential issues that can affect its reliability and performance. The most severe and expensive issues include engine and transmission failures, which can cost thousands of dollars to repair. However, many of the issues are relatively minor and can be fixed for a few hundred dollars or less. Overall, the B800F is a reliable and capable truck, but it is important to be aware of its potential issues before purchasing one.
