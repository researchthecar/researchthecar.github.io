# 2015 Ford Expedition

### Overview
The 2015 Ford Expedition is a full-size SUV known for its robust towing capacity and spacious interior, making it a popular choice for families and those needing significant cargo space. This generation, particularly the 2015 model year, generally exhibits good reliability, benefiting from a well-established platform. However, like any vehicle, it has specific areas that have seen more attention from owners regarding potential issues. This analysis focuses on documented technical reliability concerns beyond routine maintenance, covering its primary powertrain options and other key vehicle systems.

---


## Engine

### 3.5L EcoBoost V6
**Turbocharger Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of power, whistling noises, increased oil consumption, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of affected turbocharger(s).
- **Cost:** $1,200 - $2,500

**Coolant Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Visible coolant puddles under the vehicle, low coolant level, overheating, sweet smell.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of leaking hoses, gaskets, or water pump.
- **Cost:** $300 - $800


## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic (6R75/6R80)
**Hesitation/Shuddering**
- **Symptoms:** Jerking or shuddering during gear changes, delayed engagement, rough shifting.
- **Solution:** Transmission fluid and filter change, potential valve body or torque converter repair/replacement.
- **Cost:** $500 - $2,000

### Adaptive Air Suspension (Optional)
**Air Spring Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle sagging on one corner, compressor running excessively, 'air suspension fault' warning light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of leaking air springs or air lines.
- **Cost:** $600 - $1,500 per corner

**Compressor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle not leveling, compressor not engaging, 'air suspension fault' warning light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the air suspension compressor.
- **Cost:** $700 - $1,200


## Electrical

### General Electrical System
**Infotainment System Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Screen freezing, unresponsiveness, Bluetooth connectivity issues, audio problems.
- **Solution:** Software updates, potential module reset or replacement.
- **Cost:** $100 - $600

**Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Difficulty starting, dead battery, electrical components malfunctioning intermittently.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis of parasitic draw, replacement of faulty module or battery.
- **Cost:** $200 - $800

### Summary Verdict
The 2015 Ford Expedition, primarily equipped with the 3.5L EcoBoost V6 engine and a 6-speed automatic transmission, demonstrates a generally solid reliability record. The most frequently cited technical concerns revolve around the turbochargers and potential coolant leaks within the engine, as well as occasional transmission shuddering or hesitation. For models with the optional adaptive air suspension, issues with air springs and the compressor are noted. Electrical gremlins, particularly with the infotainment system and occasional battery drain, are also reported. However, the exterior and interior components are largely free of widespread issues. Overall, while not immune to problems, the Expedition offers a dependable ownership experience for its class, with most issues being repairable within a reasonable cost range.
