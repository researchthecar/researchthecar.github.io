# 2017 Ford Expedition

### Overview
The 2017 Ford Expedition is a full-size SUV known for its robust towing capacity and spacious interior. Generally, this model year demonstrates good reliability, particularly with its powertrain. Owners often praise its comfortable ride and the performance of its EcoBoost engine. However, like any vehicle, there are specific areas where potential issues have been noted, primarily related to the infotainment system and some minor electrical components. The overall build quality is solid, making it a dependable choice for families and those needing significant cargo or towing space. Regular maintenance is key to ensuring its longevity.

---


## Engine

### 3.5L EcoBoost V6
**Turbocharger Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of power, whistling noises from the engine bay, increased oil consumption.
- **Solution:** Inspection and potential replacement of turbocharger seals or the entire unit.
- **Cost:** $1,200 - $2,500

**Cooling System Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Overheating, visible coolant leaks, sweet smell from the engine.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of hoses, water pump, or radiator.
- **Cost:** $400 - $900


## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic (6R75/6R80)
**Hesitation or Shuddering**
- **Symptoms:** Jerking or shuddering during gear changes, delayed engagement.
- **Solution:** Transmission fluid flush and filter replacement, or potential internal transmission repair.
- **Cost:** $300 - $1,500

**Air Spring Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle sagging on one side, compressor running excessively, warning lights on the dashboard.
- **Solution:** Replacement of leaking air springs or air compressor.
- **Cost:** $600 - $1,200


## Electrical

### Infotainment System (SYNC 3)
**Screen Freezing or Unresponsiveness**
- **Symptoms:** Touchscreen becomes unresponsive, system freezes, reboots unexpectedly.
- **Solution:** Software update, or replacement of the infotainment module.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1,500

### Summary Verdict
The 2017 Ford Expedition is a generally reliable large SUV. The 3.5L EcoBoost engine and the 6-speed automatic transmission are robust, though occasional issues with turbochargers and transmission shudder can occur. The optional air suspension is a point of concern for some owners due to potential leaks. Electrical gremlins, particularly with the SYNC 3 infotainment system, are the most frequently reported non-wear-and-tear issues. Overall, with proactive maintenance and attention to known problem areas, the 2017 Expedition offers a dependable ownership experience for its class.
