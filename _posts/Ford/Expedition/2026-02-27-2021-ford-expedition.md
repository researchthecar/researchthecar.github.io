# 2021 Ford Expedition Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2021 Ford Expedition is a full-size SUV offering spacious three-row seating, robust towing capability, and a powerful twin-turbo V6 engine. However, prospective buyers should carefully review owner-reported reliability data. While it delivers impressive utility and comfort, the model has accumulated notable complaints concerning its powertrain, particularly regarding transmission shifting behavior and internal component wear, as well as minor electrical and body accessory concerns.

---

## Engine

### 3.5L EcoBoost V6

The 3.5-liter twin-turbocharged EcoBoost V6 produces between 375 and 400 horsepower depending on the trim level, delivering strong acceleration and heavy-duty towing capacity. Overall, the engine performs well mechanically, though buyers should watch for occasional fuel system complaints.

**Fuel Pump Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle fails to start, experiences sudden engine stalling, or loses power while driving, frequently requiring towing.
- **Solution:** Diagnose the fuel system and replace the failing fuel pump module.

## Transmission

### 10-Speed Automatic Transmission

Paired exclusively with the 3.5L V6, the 10-speed automatic transmission aims to optimize fuel economy and power delivery. However, this assembly generates the highest volume of owner concern, specifically regarding erratic shifting and clutch drum wear.

**Transmission Slipping and Hard Shifting**
- **Symptoms:** Severe jerking, harsh shifts between gears (particularly 3rd through 5th), transmission slipping, and temporary loss of motive power.
- **Solution:** Inspect internal transmission components for wear, such as the CDF clutch drum, and perform necessary mechanical repairs or TCM updates.

## Exterior

### Exterior Lighting

Electrical complaints are generally minor, centered around exterior lighting and accessory power management rather than total system failures.

**Headlight and Daytime Running Light Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Daytime running light LEDs fade or develop a purple hue, reducing visibility and consistency for oncoming traffic.
- **Solution:** Replace the affected headlight assembly.

### Windshield Wipers

Exterior body and visibility components are mostly trouble-free, though occasional reports note unexpected failures in wiper operation.

**Wiper Motor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Windshield wipers operate sluggishly before failing entirely without warning during rainstorms.
- **Solution:** Replace the faulty wiper motor or linkage assembly.

### Door Latches and Locks

Cabin hardware is generally comfortable and durable, though door latch mechanisms require attention if owners report sticking or failing to secure in colder climates.

**Door Latch Freezing and Unlatching**
- **Symptoms:** Doors fail to latch securely in cold weather, or vehicle doors inadvertently unlatch and fly open while driving.
- **Solution:** Inspect and repair or replace door latch mechanisms according to manufacturer service bulletins.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V805000: Air Bags:Sensor:Side Impact](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Expedition&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The front-door side impact pressure sensor modules may have been damaged during supplier assembly, which can cause the side impact air bags to not deploy properly in a crash. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 214, "Side Impact Protection." An air bag that does not deploy properly.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the driver and passenger front door side impact sensors, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V250000: Visibility:Windshield Wiper/Washer:Linkages](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Expedition&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** Inoperative windshield wipers can reduce visibility in certain driving conditions, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace both front windshield wiper arms, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V346000: Engine](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Expedition&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** An engine compartment fire may occur while driving or parked, even with the ignition in the "Off" position. A vehicle fire increases the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the battery junction box, as necessary, and remove a ground wire. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V420000: Equipment:Other:Owners/Service/Other Manual](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Expedition&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 202, "Head Restraints." Without instructions, the head restraints may be improperly adjusted, increasing the risk of injury in the event of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 24V852000: Equipment:Other:Labels](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Expedition&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirement Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 208, "Occupant Crash Protection." Without a label that shows the proper restraint usage guidelines, the occupants may have an increased risk of injury in the event of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 25V048000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Expedition&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** Rearview camera failure can reduce the driver's view of what is behind the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the rearview camera as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V442000: Back Over Prevention:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Expedition&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** A software error may cause the rearview camera to display a blank image, or the image may remain on the display after the backing event has ended. Both of these situations can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the rearview camera software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V729000: Visibility:Sun/Moon Roof Assembly](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Expedition&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** A detached wind deflector can become a road hazard and increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and repair the moonroof wind deflector as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

When shopping for a used 2021 Ford Expedition, prioritize models with a documented service history and a thorough pre-purchase inspection focusing heavily on the 10-speed automatic transmission. While the SUV offers unmatched passenger space and towing prowess, verifying that any transmission shifting anomalies or open recall items have been properly addressed will help ensure a dependable ownership experience.
