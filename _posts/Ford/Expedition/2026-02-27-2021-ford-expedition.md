# 2021 Ford Expedition

### Overview
The 2021 Ford Expedition is a full-size SUV known for its spacious interior, robust towing capacity, and comfortable ride. As a family-oriented vehicle, it generally offers a good balance of features and practicality. Reliability data for the 2021 model year indicates a generally solid performance, with most reported issues being minor or isolated. The powertrain, particularly the 3.5L EcoBoost V6 engine, is a strong performer and has proven to be relatively dependable. However, like many modern vehicles, some owners have encountered specific, though not widespread, concerns related to electronics and the infotainment system. Overall, the Expedition is considered a reliable choice in its segment, with most common issues being addressable without significant cost or inconvenience.

## Electrical

### Infotainment System
**Screen Freezing/Unresponsiveness**
- **Symptoms:** The infotainment screen may intermittently freeze, become unresponsive to touch, or reboot unexpectedly.
- **Solution:** Software updates for the infotainment system are often available and can resolve these glitches. In some cases, a hard reset of the system may be required.
- **Cost:** $0 - $150 (Software update may be free, diagnostic if needed)

**Connectivity Issues (Bluetooth/Apple CarPlay/Android Auto)**
- **Symptoms:** Difficulty connecting or maintaining a stable connection with smartphones via Bluetooth or wired/wireless CarPlay/Android Auto.
- **Solution:** Ensure phone software is up-to-date. Try re-pairing the device. Check for vehicle software updates. Sometimes, a faulty USB port or cable can be the cause.
- **Cost:** $0 - $200 (Software updates free, potential diagnostic/cable replacement)


### 12V Battery
**Premature Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Difficulty starting the vehicle, dimming lights, or electrical system malfunctions.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the 12V battery.
- **Cost:** $200 - $400

### Summary Verdict
The 2021 Ford Expedition demonstrates good overall reliability, with its core mechanical components like the 3.5L EcoBoost V6 engine and 10-speed automatic transmission performing commendably. The majority of reported issues are concentrated in the electrical system, specifically with the infotainment unit experiencing occasional freezing or connectivity problems, and isolated reports of premature 12V battery failure. These are not systemic failures but rather common electronic quirks found in many modern vehicles. Suspension, exterior, and interior components are generally robust. Owners can expect a dependable large SUV, with potential minor electronic glitches being the most likely, though not guaranteed, concern.
