# 2019 Ford Expedition

### Overview
The 2019 Ford Expedition is a full-size SUV known for its spacious interior, robust towing capabilities, and comfortable ride. Built on a body-on-frame chassis, it offers a blend of truck-like durability and family-friendly features. For the 2019 model year, the Expedition continued to offer its potent EcoBoost V6 engine, paired with a smooth-shifting 10-speed automatic transmission. Reliability data suggests that while the Expedition is generally a dependable vehicle, certain components have seen isolated reports of issues. Owners generally appreciate its performance and utility, making it a popular choice for larger families and those with active lifestyles requiring significant cargo or towing capacity. This analysis focuses on documented technical issues beyond routine maintenance.

---


## Engine

### 3.5L EcoBoost V6
**Turbocharger Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Reduced engine power, whistling noises, increased oil consumption.
- **Solution:** Inspection and potential replacement of turbocharger seals or the entire unit.
- **Cost:** $1,200 - $2,500

**Coolant Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Visible coolant puddles under the vehicle, overheating, sweet smell.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and repair of leaking hoses, water pump, or intake manifold gaskets.
- **Cost:** $300 - $800


## Transmission

### 10-Speed Automatic (10R75)
**Hesitation or Shuddering**
- **Symptoms:** Jerky shifts, delayed engagement, noticeable shudder during acceleration or deceleration.
- **Solution:** Software updates for transmission control module, fluid flush, or internal component inspection/repair.
- **Cost:** $200 - $1,500

## Electrical

### Infotainment System
**Screen Freezing or Unresponsiveness**
- **Symptoms:** Touchscreen becomes unresponsive, system freezes, audio/visual glitches.
- **Solution:** Software updates, hard reset of the system, or replacement of the infotainment unit.
- **Cost:** $300 - $1,200

### Tailgate Actuators
**Power Liftgate Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Liftgate fails to open or close automatically, slow operation.
- **Solution:** Inspection and replacement of liftgate motor or associated sensors/switches.
- **Cost:** $300 - $700


## Interior

### Climate Control System
**Blower Motor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** No air coming from vents, or air only comes from certain vents.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the cabin blower motor or resistor.
- **Cost:** $200 - $500


### Seat Adjustments
**Power Seat Motor Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Power seats fail to move forward, backward, or adjust height.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and replacement of faulty seat motors or control modules.
- **Cost:** $250 - $600

### Summary Verdict
The 2019 Ford Expedition is a largely reliable full-size SUV, particularly concerning its core powertrain and chassis components. The 3.5L EcoBoost V6 and 10-speed automatic transmission are generally robust, though isolated reports of turbocharger issues and transmission shudder do exist and may require attention. Electrical gremlins, such as infotainment system glitches and occasional battery drain, are present but not pervasive. Minor issues with interior components like blower motors and power seat adjustments are also noted. Overall, the Expedition offers a dependable ownership experience for its class, with most reported issues being repairable without excessive cost, especially when addressed promptly.
