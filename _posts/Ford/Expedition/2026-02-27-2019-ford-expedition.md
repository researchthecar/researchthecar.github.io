# 2019 Ford Expedition Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2019 Ford Expedition is a full-size SUV offering impressive towing capacity, spacious interior accommodations, and modern features. However, prospective buyers should review known powertrain and safety concerns carefully before purchasing. While it provides strong utility and family-friendly comfort, reports highlight several recurring mechanical and electrical issues that require attention.

---

## Engine

### 3.5L EcoBoost V6

Equipped with a 3.5-liter twin-turbocharged EcoBoost V6 producing approximately 375 to 400 horsepower, this engine delivers strong acceleration and towing capability. Buyers should listen closely for startup rattles indicating potential timing component wear.

**Timing Chain Rattle and Phazer Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Excessive rattling noise from the engine during startup, accompanied by potential warning lights or diagnostic trouble codes.
- **Solution:** Inspect timing components and replace worn cam phasers or timing chains as needed.

## Transmission

### 10-Speed Automatic Transmission (10R80)

Utilizing a 10-speed automatic transmission across available trims and drivetrains, this unit helps optimize fuel economy and power delivery. However, some owners experience harsh shifting, shuddering, and unexpected gear engagement issues.

**Transmission Slipping and Harsh Shifting**
- **Symptoms:** Jerking, delayed gear recognition, or sudden drops into neutral while driving or attempting to accelerate.
- **Solution:** Reprogram the powertrain control module, update transmission software, or service internal components such as the CDF drum.

## Suspension

### Suspension Struts and Shocks

The suspension system provides a comfortable ride suited for long road trips, but early wear and fluid leaks on factory struts have been reported.

**Premature Strut Leaks and Excessive Bouncing**
- **Symptoms:** Leaking shocks, an overly bouncy ride quality, or uneven rear tire wear.
- **Solution:** Inspect suspension dampeners and replace leaking struts or shocks.

## Exterior

### Exterior Lighting

Electrical accessories and exterior lighting generally function well, though specific LED daytime running light assemblies can degrade prematurely.

**Daytime Running Light Fading**
- **Symptoms:** LED daytime running lights fading in brightness, flickering, or discoloring on one side of the vehicle.
- **Solution:** Replace the affected headlight or LED assembly.

### Console Trim and Door Latches

Cabin appointments are spacious and versatile, though minor interior trim hazards and cold-weather door latch issues require occasional attention.

**Sharp Console Trim and Door Latch Freezing**
- **Symptoms:** Sharp edges on interior center console trim causing lacerations, or rear door latches failing to secure in freezing weather.
- **Solution:** File or replace sharp trim components and lubricate or service door latch mechanisms.

## Safety

### Seat Belts

Safety equipment generally provides robust occupant protection, though front seat belt pretensioners and retractor mechanisms have been a notable source of owner complaints.

**Seat Belt Pretensioner and Retractor Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Seat belts locking up excessively tight, retracting too rapidly, or triggering airbag and seat belt warning lights.
- **Solution:** Inspect and replace faulty seat belt assemblies or pretensioners in accordance with manufacturer guidelines.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V263000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission:Gear Position Indication (Prndl)](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Expedition&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** If the driver exits the vehicle without the transmission being shifted into park, unintended vehicle movement may result, increasing the risk of injury or crash.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will reprogram the instrument cluster, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V633000: Seats:Front Assembly:Recliner](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Expedition&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) numbers 202, "Head Restraints" and 207, "Seating Systems." A seat back with an improperly assembled recliner mechanism may have reduced strength and may not adequately restrain an occupant in a crash, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and Ford and Lincoln dealers will inspect the seat structure and replace them, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V773000: Suspension:Rear](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Expedition&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** The rear suspension toe link fasteners may not have been properly tightened to the frame, possibly allowing separation from the frame. A disconnected toe link can cause a sudden change to vehicle handling, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Ford has notified owners, and dealers will inspect and tighten the rear toe link fasteners, check and adjust the rear toe alignment, and inspect the rear tires for abnormal tire wear, replacing them if necessary. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V420000: Equipment:Other:Owners/Service/Other Manual](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Expedition&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 202, "Head Restraints." Without instructions, the head restraints may be improperly adjusted, increasing the risk of injury in the event of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 24V099000: Seat Belts:Pretensioner](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Expedition&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** The seat belt pretensioner in the driver and/or front passenger seat may inadvertently deploy and lock the seat belt, which will not allow the belt to retract or extend. Seat belts that do not retract or extend may fail to restrain an occupant as intended, increasing the risk of injury in a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the seat belt retractors, as necessary. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V852000: Equipment:Other:Labels](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Expedition&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirement Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 208, "Occupant Crash Protection." Without a label that shows the proper restraint usage guidelines, the occupants may have an increased risk of injury in the event of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 25V197000: Seat Belts:Pretensioner](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Expedition&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** The seat belt pretensioner in the driver and/or front passenger seat may inadvertently lock the seat belt, which will not allow the belt to retract or extend. Seat belts that do not retract or extend can fail to restrain an occupant as intended, increasing the risk of injury in a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the seat belt retractors as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V886000: Electrical System: Instrument Cluster/Panel](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Expedition&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** If the driver exits the vehicle without the transmission being shifted into park, unintended vehicle movement may result, increasing the risk of injury or crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the instrument cluster panel software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2019 Ford Expedition is a capable and spacious full-size SUV, but used car buyers should perform a thorough mechanical inspection before purchasing. Pay close attention to the transmission's shifting behavior and listen for any startup engine rattles. Verifying that all safety recalls and necessary component updates have been completed will help ensure a more reliable ownership experience.
