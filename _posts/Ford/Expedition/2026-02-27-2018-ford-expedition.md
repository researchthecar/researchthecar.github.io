# 2018 Ford Expedition Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2018 Ford Expedition offers robust towing capability, spacious three-row seating, and a powerful twin-turbo V6 engine, making it a popular choice for families needing large-SUV utility. However, used-car buyers should approach this model year with care due to a noticeable pattern of mechanical complaints. Powertrain concerns, particularly regarding transmission shifting behavior and engine cam phaser longevity, are the most critical areas to inspect prior to purchase.

---

## Engine

### 3.5L EcoBoost V6

The 3.5-liter EcoBoost twin-turbocharged V6 produces approximately 375 to 400 horsepower and 470 to 480 lb-ft of torque, delivering swift acceleration and strong towing capacity. While generally robust, early versions of this generation can develop timing chain and cam phaser wear that requires attentive maintenance.

**Cam Phaser Wear and Cold Start Rattle**
- **Symptoms:** A loud rattling or knocking noise from the engine during cold starts, which may eventually trigger diagnostic trouble codes or electrical warning lights.
- **Solution:** Replace the defective cam phasers and associated timing components with updated revised parts.

**Engine Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** Unexpected engine stalling while driving in traffic or idling, occasionally accompanied by a loss of motive power.
- **Solution:** Inspect the powertrain control module (PCM), throttle body, and fuel delivery systems; apply necessary software updates or component replacements.

## Transmission

### 10-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 10-speed automatic transmission is paired across the lineup to optimize fuel economy and power delivery. However, owners frequently report rough shifting, slipping, and internal component wear that demand careful pre-purchase road testing.

**Transmission Slipping and Harsh Shifting**
- **Symptoms:** Severe transmission slipping, sudden violent gear changes (such as dropping from high gears to first gear unexpectedly), and hesitation while accelerating.
- **Solution:** Inspect internal transmission components, such as the CDF drum, and update transmission control module (TCM) calibration or perform a transmission rebuild/replacement as needed.

**Unintended Neutral Shift**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle unexpectedly drops out of gear into neutral while driving, resulting in a sudden loss of motive power in traffic.
- **Solution:** Diagnose the transmission valve body, shift actuators, and internal clutch drums for wear or hydraulic pressure loss.

## Suspension

### Wheels and Hardware

Suspension and wheel hardware components generally hold up well, though standard wear items like struts and factory wheel nuts require routine inspection.

**Swollen Lug Nuts**
- **Symptoms:** Factory two-piece lug nuts swell over time, making it impossible to fit a standard tire iron or socket over them during a flat tire change.
- **Solution:** Replace the factory lug nuts with solid aftermarket one-piece replacements.

## Electrical

### Infotainment and Lighting

Electrical systems control a wide array of cabin conveniences and safety features, though intermittent module failures and screen issues are commonly reported by owners.

**Infotainment Screen and APIM Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The central touchscreen and rearview camera display freeze, operate intermittently, or fail completely.
- **Solution:** Replace the Accessory Protocol Interface Module (APIM) or update the infotainment software.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V392000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Expedition&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** These vehicles have high pressure fuel pump assemblies with welds that can fracture, which may cause an oil or fuel leak. A fuel leak in the presence of an ignition source may increase the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will replace the high pressure fuel pump and associated components, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V805000: Seats:Mid/Rear Assembly](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Expedition&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** The second row center seat track assemblies may be missing one or both of the J-Channel reinforcement brackets, possibly allowing the seat to move in the event of a crash. In the event of a crash, a seat that moves may not properly restrain the seat occupant, increasing their risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the second row center seat for any missing J-channel reinforcement brackets, replacing the center seat frame assembly as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V775000: Seats:Mid/Rear Assembly](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Expedition&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 202a, "Head Restraints", number 207, "Seating Systems", and number 210, "Seat Belt Anchorage System." If the seat back is not adequately secured, and/or the seat occupant is improperly positioned, there can be an increased risk of injury in a.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the second row outboard seats for proper seat adjustment travel, replacing the seat frame assembly if necessary. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V213000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Expedition&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** A roll pin may not have been installed in the transmission, potentially causing a loss of the "Park" function. Despite the transmission shift lever being placed in 'Park' and the instrument panel display indicating 'Park,' the vehicle may roll away after it has been exited if the parking brake has not been applied, increasing the risk of injury or a crash.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the transmission to make sure it has the roll pin, installing one if it is missing, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V058000: Visibility:Power Window Devices And Controls](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Expedition&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 118, "Power-Operated Window Systems." If the window does not automatically reverse when an obstruction is found, it can increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the second row window electronic control modules, and replace them as necessary based on their build date codes, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V214000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission:Gear Position Indication (Prndl)](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Expedition&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** The gear shift cable clip may not be properly seated, allowing the transmission to be in a different gear than indicated by the gear shift lever position. Either scenario increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the shift cable locking clip and properly seat it, if necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V420000: Equipment:Other:Owners/Service/Other Manual](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Expedition&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 202, "Head Restraints." Without instructions, the head restraints may be improperly adjusted, increasing the risk of injury in the event of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 24V099000: Seat Belts:Pretensioner](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Expedition&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** The seat belt pretensioner in the driver and/or front passenger seat may inadvertently deploy and lock the seat belt, which will not allow the belt to retract or extend. Seat belts that do not retract or extend may fail to restrain an occupant as intended, increasing the risk of injury in a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the seat belt retractors, as necessary. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2018 Ford Expedition is a capable and spacious full-size SUV, but it requires a thorough pre-purchase inspection. Buyers should pay close attention to the transmission for harsh shifting or slipping, listen closely for cold-start engine rattles indicative of cam phaser wear, and verify that all electrical features and recall services are fully up to date.
