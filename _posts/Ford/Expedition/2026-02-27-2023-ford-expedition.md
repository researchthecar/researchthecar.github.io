# 2023 Ford Expedition Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2023 Ford Expedition, buyers will find a large, capable full-size SUV offering robust towing and generous cabin space. While most owners enjoy its powerful EcoBoost performance and comfortable ride, early production data reveals some notable owner complaints regarding electrical glitches, braking concerns, and occasional powertrain hiccups. Prospective buyers should carefully inspect service records and verify whether any relevant manufacturer campaigns have been completed.

---

## Engine

### 3.5L EcoBoost V6

The standard 3.5-liter twin-turbocharged V6 engine produces between 380 and 440 horsepower depending on the trim level, providing brisk acceleration and strong towing capacity. It is generally robust, though proper maintenance and timely oil changes are critical to longevity.

## Transmission

### 10-Speed Automatic Transmission

Paired across all trims with rear-wheel or four-wheel drive, the 10-speed automatic transmission aims to optimize fuel economy and power delivery. Some owners report shift quality issues that may require software recalibration.

**Hard Shifting and Power Delivery**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may experience harsh gear engagements, sudden hard shifts, or temporary loss of acceleration resembling neutral while driving.
- **Solution:** Have a certified technician inspect transmission fluid condition, check for updated control module software, or evaluate internal mechanical components.

## Brakes

### Hydraulic Brake System

Braking components provide strong stopping power for this heavy SUV, though electronic brake booster failures and pedal feel inconsistencies have been flagged in owner complaints.

**Brake Pedal Softness and Electronic Booster Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The brake pedal may feel unusually soft or travel straight to the floor, accompanied by warning lights and increased stopping distances.
- **Solution:** Inspect the electronic brake booster assembly and ensure all relevant safety recalls or software updates have been properly applied.

## Electrical

### Electrical and Instrument Cluster

The vehicle relies heavily on complex electronic architectures for safety alerts, gauges, and accessory control. Intermittent display blackouts and weather-strip water leaks are areas to watch.

**Water Intrusion and Screen Blackouts**
- **Symptoms:** The instrument panel or infotainment screens may go completely dark while driving, or water entry near the cowl can cause junction box corrosion and starting failures.
- **Solution:** Inspect the hood cowl sealing for proper drainage and check the battery junction box for signs of moisture intrusion or corrosion.

## Exterior

### Roof and Glass Components

Exterior features like power running boards and panoramic glass add luxury appeal but can occasionally present mechanical or structural durability issues.

**Panoramic Sunroof Shattering**
- **Symptoms:** The panoramic sunroof or moonroof glass may shatter spontaneously while driving at highway speeds without prior impact.
- **Solution:** Have the glass panel and surrounding frame inspected for alignment or stress points, and replace damaged assemblies promptly.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V128000: Power Train:Axle Assembly](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Expedition&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** The front axle pinion may not have been heat-treated, which can cause it to fracture. A fractured pinion can result in a loss of 4-wheel drive and may cause the front wheels to lock up, increasng the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the front axle pinion gear set. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V420000: Equipment:Other:Owners/Service/Other Manual](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Expedition&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 202, "Head Restraints." Without instructions, the head restraints may be improperly adjusted, increasing the risk of injury in the event of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 24V852000: Equipment:Other:Labels](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Expedition&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirement Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 208, "Occupant Crash Protection." Without a label that shows the proper restraint usage guidelines, the occupants may have an increased risk of injury in the event of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 25V314000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Foundation Components:Hoses, Lines/Piping, And Fittings](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Expedition&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** A loss of brake function increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the front brake line and replace the brake line or air cleaner outlet pipe, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V315000: Back Over Prevention:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Expedition&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** A software error may cause the rearview camera image to delay, freeze, or not display when the vehicle is in reverse. A frozen or missing rearview camera image can reduce the driver's view behind the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 26V104000: Electrical System: Integrated Trailer Brake Control](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Expedition&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 108, " Lamps, Reflective Devices, and Associated Equipment." Trailer lights or brakes that do not function can reduce the driver’s ability to control the trailer and decrease the trailers visibility to other road users, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 26V204000: Visibility:Windshield Wiper/Washer:Linkages](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Expedition&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** Windshield wiper failure can reduce visibility, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the wiper arms, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V368000: Structure:Interior Panels:Center Console](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Expedition&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** Vehicle occupants may contact the center console's sharp edges, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the center consoles as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2023 Ford Expedition is a highly capable and spacious family hauler, but used-car buyers should approach with a healthy level of diligence. While its powertrain options deliver strong performance, potential purchasers should thoroughly check for electrical issues, transmission shift smoothness, and proper braking operation during a pre-purchase inspection. Verifying that all manufacturer updates have been completed will significantly improve peace of mind.
