# 2023 Ford Expedition

### Overview
The 2023 Ford Expedition, a full-size SUV known for its spacious interior and robust towing capabilities, generally presents a strong reliability profile. Built on a proven platform and featuring a potent EcoBoost V6 engine, it aims to offer a dependable experience for families and those needing significant hauling capacity. While most owners report satisfaction, like any complex vehicle, certain areas can experience issues. This analysis focuses on documented technical problems beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential concerns for the 2023 model year across its various configurations.

---


## Engine

### 3.5L EcoBoost V6
**Turbocharger Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of power, whistling noises from the engine bay, increased exhaust smoke.
- **Solution:** Inspection and potential replacement of turbocharger assembly or related hoses and seals.
- **Cost:** $1,200 - $2,500

**Cooling System Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Overheating, visible coolant leaks under the vehicle, sweet smell from the engine bay.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and repair of leaking hoses, water pump, or radiator.
- **Cost:** $400 - $900


## Transmission

### 10-Speed Automatic (10R80)
**Hesitation or Jerking**
- **Symptoms:** Rough shifting, delayed engagement, or noticeable jerks during gear changes, especially at lower speeds.
- **Solution:** Software updates for transmission control module, or internal transmission inspection and repair if necessary.
- **Cost:** $200 - $1,500

### Summary Verdict
The 2023 Ford Expedition demonstrates a generally solid reliability record, particularly concerning its core mechanical components like the engine and transmission. The 3.5L EcoBoost V6 and the 10-speed automatic transmission are robust, though isolated reports of turbocharger issues, cooling system leaks, and transmission hesitation do exist. Electrical gremlins, primarily related to the infotainment system's responsiveness and occasional battery drain, are the most frequently cited non-routine problems. Exterior and interior components, along with the suspension system, appear to be holding up well with no widespread concerns. Owners should be aware of potential electrical quirks and ensure timely maintenance of the cooling system and turbochargers.
