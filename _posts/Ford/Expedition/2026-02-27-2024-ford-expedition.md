# 2024 Ford Expedition Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2024 Ford Expedition delivers robust full-size utility, strong towing capability, and a spacious cabin for families. However, prospective buyers should review owner-reported concerns regarding advanced driver assistance features, powertrain components, and high-speed vibrations when evaluating used options.

---

## Engine

### 3.5L EcoBoost V6

The standard 3.5-liter twin-turbocharged EcoBoost V6 produces approximately 380 to 440 horsepower depending on the trim level. It provides strong acceleration and high towing capacity, though buyers should ensure all factory updates and fluid maintenance are up to date.

## Transmission

### 10-Speed SelectShift Automatic

Equipped with a 10-speed automatic transmission paired with rear-wheel or four-wheel drive, offering smooth shifting and optimized highway fuel efficiency. While most operate reliably, early-production mechanical leaks and rare mobility failures have been reported.

**Transmission Fluid Leak and Internal Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Fluid leaks originating from hairline cracks in the transmission housing or sudden loss of acceleration and mobility while driving.
- **Solution:** Have the transmission housing and seals inspected by a qualified technician; components damaged by leaks may require replacement under warranty.

### Suspension & Drivetrain

Suspension components generally provide a comfortable, controlled ride for a large SUV, though persistent high-speed vibration reports warrant a thorough test drive before purchase.

**High-Speed Highway Vibration**
- **Symptoms:** Severe vehicle vibration felt throughout the entire SUV at speeds ranging from 60 to over 80 mph.
- **Solution:** Check wheel balancing, tire condition, and driveline alignment; consult a dealer for specialized diagnostic procedures if vibrations persist.

## Exterior

### Panoramic Moonroof

The large panoramic moonroof opens up the cabin nicely, though rare reports of spontaneous glass breakage highlight a potential vulnerability to inspect.

**Spontaneous Glass Shattering**
- **Symptoms:** Loud popping noise followed by the sudden shattering of the panoramic glass moonroof while driving under normal conditions.
- **Solution:** Inspect the glass panel for stress fractures and consult insurance or dealership representatives regarding replacement coverage.

## Safety

### Forward Collision & Driver Assist

Advanced safety suites offer modern peace of mind, but sensor calibration sensitivities can occasionally trigger false warnings or unexpected system behavior.

**Unexpected Braking and Sensor Faults**
- **Symptoms:** Intermittent warning messages for front camera or braking malfunctions, alongside rare instances of unexpected automatic braking.
- **Solution:** Have dealership technicians inspect radar sensors, camera modules, and software calibration for fault codes.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V851000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Engine:Oil/Lubrication](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Expedition&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** A rapid oil leak can result in an engine stall, increasing the risk of a crash. In addition, an oil leak in the presence of an ignition source such as hot engine or exhaust components, can increase the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the engine cup plug alignment, and replace the plug if necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V852000: Equipment:Other:Labels](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Expedition&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirement Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 208, "Occupant Crash Protection." Without a label that shows the proper restraint usage guidelines, the occupants may have an increased risk of injury in the event of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 25V198000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Engine:Oil/Lubrication](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Expedition&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** A rapid oil leak can result in an engine stall, increasing the risk of a crash. In addition, an oil leak in the presence of an ignition source such as hot engine or exhaust components, can increase the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the engine cup plug alignment, and replace the plug if necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V314000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Foundation Components:Hoses, Lines/Piping, And Fittings](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Expedition&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** A loss of brake function increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the front brake line and replace the brake line or air cleaner outlet pipe, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V315000: Back Over Prevention:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Expedition&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** A software error may cause the rearview camera image to delay, freeze, or not display when the vehicle is in reverse. A frozen or missing rearview camera image can reduce the driver's view behind the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 26V104000: Electrical System: Integrated Trailer Brake Control](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Expedition&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 108, " Lamps, Reflective Devices, and Associated Equipment." Trailer lights or brakes that do not function can reduce the driver’s ability to control the trailer and decrease the trailers visibility to other road users, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 26V368000: Structure:Interior Panels:Center Console](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Expedition&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** Vehicle occupants may contact the center console's sharp edges, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the center consoles as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2024 Ford Expedition is a highly capable full-size SUV offering exceptional passenger space and towing prowess. While many owners enjoy trouble-free driving, buyers should conduct a thorough inspection paying close attention to transmission housing integrity, high-speed ride smoothness, and the functioning of advanced driver assistance electronics.
