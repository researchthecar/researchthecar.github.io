# 2016 Ford Expedition Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used full-size SUV, the 2016 Ford Expedition offers robust towing capability and generous passenger space, powered by its modern EcoBoost architecture. However, prospective buyers should review owner complaints regarding powertrain performance, braking concerns, and accessory electronics. Keeping up with routine maintenance and verifying recall completion are key steps toward ensuring a dependable ownership experience.

---

## Engine

### 3.5L EcoBoost V6

A 3.5-liter twin-turbocharged V6 engine producing approximately 365 horsepower and 420 lb-ft of torque, delivering strong low-end pulling power paired with modern efficiency. Buyers should inspect closely for cold-start timing component noises.

**Cam Phaser and Timing Chain Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Owners report a noticeable rattling noise upon starting a cold engine, which can progress to timing chain wear if unaddressed.
- **Solution:** Inspect for startup rattle during pre-purchase evaluation; replacement of cam phasers and timing components may be required.

**Loss of Motive Power**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may suddenly stop accelerating while driving, accompanied by warning lights and a drop into a limp or coasting mode.
- **Solution:** Diagnose throttle body operation, electronic sensors, or powertrain control modules for fault codes.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

A 6-speed automatic transmission paired with either rear-wheel drive or four-wheel drive configurations. While generally durable, smooth operation across all gears should be verified during a test drive.

**Inadvertent Downshifting**
- **Symptoms:** The transmission may unexpectedly downshift into a lower gear while driving at speed, potentially causing abrupt deceleration or wheel slip.
- **Solution:** Check transmission control module software updates and inspect internal valve bodies or wiring harnesses.

## Suspension

### Wheel and Lug Nuts

Wheel hardware and suspension components endure substantial stress supporting the vehicle weight, making regular inspections of boots, joints, and lug hardware recommended.

**Swollen Lug Nuts**
- **Symptoms:** Factory-capped lug nuts can swell or deform over time, making it difficult to attach standard tire irons during roadside flat repairs.
- **Solution:** Replace factory two-piece lug nuts with solid aftermarket alternatives.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

The hydraulic brake system requires routine inspection of pads, rotors, and fluid lines to maintain confident stopping performance in a heavy full-size SUV.

**Brake Pedal Travel and Stopping Delays**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers note instances of extended pedal travel, spongy pedal feel, or delayed braking response requiring extra stopping distance.
- **Solution:** Inspect master cylinders, brake fluid condition, lines, and vacuum or hydraulic boosters for leaks or pressure loss.

## Steering

### Electric Power Steering

The electric power-assist steering system provides light maneuverability at low speeds, though electronic faults can occasionally disable assist.

**Steering Assist Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Power steering assist may cut out intermittently, resulting in a stiff steering wheel and warning faults displayed on the dash.
- **Solution:** Scan the steering control module for trouble codes and inspect electrical connections or steering gear assemblies.

## Electrical

### Back-Up Camera

Visibility aids assist with maneuvering this large SUV, though electrical connectivity issues can affect rearview camera reliability.

**Intermittent or Inoperative Camera Display**
- **Symptoms:** The rearview camera display may show a blank screen, distorted images, or fail to activate when shifting into reverse.
- **Solution:** Check wiring harnesses and camera modules, and consult local dealers regarding open safety recalls.

**[Recall 22V648000: Visibility:Defroster/Defogger/Hvac System:Fan/Motor](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Expedition&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** Front blower motor failure can increase the risk of a vehicle interior fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the front blower motor assembly, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V572000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Expedition&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** A rearview camera that displays an inverted, distorted, or blank image can reduce the driver’s view behind the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the rearview camera as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2016 Ford Expedition remains a capable family hauler and towing vehicle, but buyers should conduct a thorough inspection before purchasing. Pay close attention to engine startup noises, transmission shift quality, brake responsiveness, and the functionality of electrical accessories. Verifying that all safety recalls have been completed by a certified dealer will help secure a more reliable driving experience.
