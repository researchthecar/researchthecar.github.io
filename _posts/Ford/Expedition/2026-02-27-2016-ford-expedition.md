# 2016 Ford Expedition

### Overview
The 2016 Ford Expedition is a full-size SUV known for its robust towing capacity and spacious interior, making it a popular choice for families and those needing significant cargo space. Built on a truck-like chassis, it offers a commanding presence and capable performance. Reliability for the 2016 model year is generally considered good, with the EcoBoost V6 engine proving to be a strong performer. However, like any vehicle, there are specific areas where owners have reported issues. This analysis focuses on documented technical problems beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential concerns for prospective buyers and current owners.

---


## Engine

### 3.5L EcoBoost V6
**Turbocharger Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of power, whistling noises from the engine bay, increased oil consumption.
- **Solution:** Turbocharger replacement or repair.
- **Cost:** $1,200 - $2,500

**Cooling System Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Overheating, visible coolant leaks, sweet smell from the engine.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of hoses, water pump, or radiator.
- **Cost:** $400 - $1,000


## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic (6R75/6R80)
**Hesitation or Shuddering**
- **Symptoms:** Rough shifting, delayed engagement, shuddering during acceleration.
- **Solution:** Transmission fluid flush and filter replacement, potential internal repairs or transmission rebuild.
- **Cost:** $300 - $3,000+


## Suspension

### Independent Front Suspension / Solid Rear Axle
**Air Suspension Compressor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle sagging on one or more corners, 'Service Air Suspension' warning light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the air suspension compressor.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1,000


## Electrical

### General Electrical System
**Infotainment System Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Touchscreen unresponsiveness, freezing, Bluetooth connectivity issues.
- **Solution:** Software updates, system reset, or replacement of the infotainment module.
- **Cost:** $100 - $1,500

**Battery Drain Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Dead battery, electrical components not working, warning lights.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis of parasitic draw, replacement of faulty modules or battery.
- **Cost:** $200 - $800

### Summary Verdict
The 2016 Ford Expedition offers a solid ownership experience with its powerful EcoBoost engine and spacious cabin. While generally reliable, potential owners should be aware of possible issues with the turbochargers and cooling system on the 3.5L EcoBoost engine, as well as transmission hesitation or shuddering. Electrical gremlins, particularly with the infotainment system and occasional battery drain, are also noted. The air suspension system can be a point of failure, leading to costly repairs if the compressor fails. Overall, with proper maintenance and awareness of these potential weak points, the 2016 Expedition remains a capable and dependable large SUV.
