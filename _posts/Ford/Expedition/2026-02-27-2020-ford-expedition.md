# 2020 Ford Expedition Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2020 Ford Expedition is a full-size SUV offering spacious three-row seating, strong towing capabilities, and a compliant ride. While it delivers impressive utility for families, used-car buyers should pay close attention to potential drivetrain and electrical concerns reported by owners. Evaluating maintenance records and checking for completed recall campaigns are important steps before purchasing.

---

## Engine

### 3.5L EcoBoost V6

The 3.5-liter twin-turbocharged EcoBoost V6 produces approximately 375 to 400 horsepower, providing robust acceleration and towing power. It is generally dependable, though occasional electronic or fuel management issues have been documented by owners.

**Engine Stalling and Loss of Power**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle unexpectedly stalls while driving, and the instrument panel may go blank, forcing the driver to coast to the side of the road.
- **Solution:** Diagnostic testing by a qualified technician to inspect electrical connections, throttle body, or fuel system components.

## Transmission

### 10-Speed SelectShift Automatic

Paired with the EcoBoost V6, the 10-speed automatic transmission helps optimize fuel economy and highway cruising. However, some drivers have experienced rough shifting intervals and mechanical concerns requiring professional intervention.

**Harsh Shifting and Slipping**
- **Symptoms:** The transmission experiences delayed engagement, harsh gear changes, violent shaking, or sudden slipping while trying to accelerate.
- **Solution:** Reprogramming the powertrain control module, replacing valve bodies, or performing a complete transmission rebuild/replacement depending on internal wear.

**Unintended Neutral Shift and Park Defects**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle unexpectedly drops into neutral or experiences parking pawl engagement issues that can lead to unexpected rolling or shift system failure warnings.
- **Solution:** Inspect the shifter linkage and address any applicable manufacturer safety recalls related to the parking pawl.

## Exterior

### Exterior Lighting

The electrical system powers extensive cabin technology and safety features, though headlight assembly degradation has been noted as a recurring out-of-pocket expense.

**Daytime Running Light Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Passenger-side daytime running lights turn dim, change to a purple hue, or fail completely.
- **Solution:** Replace the affected headlight assembly as individual internal components are often not serviceable separately.
- **Cost:** $2000

### Sunroof and Glass

Exterior body panels and glass require standard maintenance, though sporadic reports of unprovoked glass breakage warrant careful inspection of used models.

**Sudden Glass Shattering**
- **Symptoms:** The panoramic sunroof or rear glass panels shatter or explode outward spontaneously while driving or parked.
- **Solution:** Replace the shattered glass panel and inspect the frame for alignment or stress points.

## Safety

### Seat Belts

Safety equipment generally performs well, but sporadic owner complaints point toward seat belt locking irregularities in the rear rows.

**Seat Belt Pre-tensioner Locking**
- **Symptoms:** Second-row seat belts unexpectedly tighten and lock up excessively, causing discomfort or restriction for passengers.
- **Solution:** Inspect seat belt assemblies and replace faulty pre-tensioner mechanisms according to dealership guidelines.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V366000: Seats:Mid/Rear Assembly](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Expedition&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** The attachment bracket for the driver's side second-row seat headrest may not have been sufficiently welded to the seat frame. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 202, "Head Restraints." An improperly welded headrest bracket may have reduced strength and may not adequately restrain an occupant in a crash.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the seat structure and replace it as needed, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V575000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Expedition&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 111, "Rear Visibility." Reduced rearward visibility can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will replace the rearview camera, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V574000: Seat Belts](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Expedition&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** In the event of a crash, the incorrect classification would enable the passenger air bag to deploy instead of disabling it, increasing the risk of injury to the seat occupant.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will replace the seat belt buckle, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V197000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission:Gear Position Indication (Prndl)](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Expedition&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** The transmission shift cable lock clip may not be fully seated, allowing the transmission to actually be in a different gear than the gear shift position selected by the driver. If the transmission selection does not match the indicated gear selection, and the parking brake is not applied, unintended vehicle movement can occur, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will inspect and correct the shift cable locking clip installation, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V217000: Seat Belts](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Expedition&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** In the event of a crash, the restraint system may not provide the intended level of protection, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will replace the front passenger seatbelt buckle, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V262000: Electrical System: Instrument Cluster/Panel](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Expedition&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** If the driver is unaware that the Pre-Collision Assist feature is not enabled, they may rely on a system that is not functional, increasing the risk of a crash or injury.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will reprogram software for the Body Control Module, Antilock Braking System, Instrument Panel Cluster and Headlamp Control Modules, and reset the Tire Pressure Monitor System, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V633000: Seats:Front Assembly:Recliner](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Expedition&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) numbers 202, "Head Restraints" and 207, "Seating Systems." A seat back with an improperly assembled recliner mechanism may have reduced strength and may not adequately restrain an occupant in a crash, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and Ford and Lincoln dealers will inspect the seat structure and replace them, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V250000: Visibility:Windshield Wiper/Washer:Linkages](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Expedition&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** Inoperative windshield wipers can reduce visibility in certain driving conditions, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace both front windshield wiper arms, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2020 Ford Expedition is a highly capable full-size SUV that handles family road trips and heavy towing with ease. Prospective buyers should thoroughly test drive the vehicle to verify smooth transmission operation and inspect all electronics, lighting, and safety systems. Ensuring that all past recall services have been performed by a certified dealer will help secure a more reliable ownership experience.
