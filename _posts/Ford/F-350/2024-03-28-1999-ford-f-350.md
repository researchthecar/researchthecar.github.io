**Introduction**
The 1999 Ford F-350 is a heavy-duty pickup truck available with various engine and transmission options. It is known for its powerful engines and rugged construction but has also faced some common issues over the years.

**Engine**
* **7.3L Power Stroke Diesel**
    * **Injector Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: Leaking injectors, hard starting, rough idle, decreased power
        * Solution: Replace injectors
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+
        * Recalls: 00S35, 00S36
    * **Turbocharger Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, excessive smoke, whistling noise
        * Solution: Replace turbocharger
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
        * Recalls: None Found
* **6.8L Triton V10**
    * **Intake Manifold Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaks, overheating, loss of power
        * Solution: Replace intake manifold
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
        * Recalls: 01S06
    * **Spark Plug Blowout**
        * Description/Symptoms: Misfires, rough idle, loss of power
        * Solution: Replace spark plugs and repair cylinder head if necessary
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**
* **TorqShift Automatic Transmission**
    * **Slippage**
        * Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears, loss of power
        * Solution: Replace or rebuild transmission
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $6,000+
        * Recalls: None Found
* **Manual Transmission**
    * **Clutch Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: Slipping clutch, difficulty shifting gears
        * Solution: Replace clutch
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**
* **Front Suspension**
    * **Ball Joint Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: Clunking or popping noises, uneven tire wear
        * Solution: Replace ball joints
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
        * Recalls: None Found
    * **Coil Spring Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: Sagging front end, rough ride
        * Solution: Replace coil springs
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
        * Recalls: None Found
* **Rear Suspension**
    * **Leaf Spring Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: Sagging rear end, excessive bounce
        * Solution: Replace leaf springs
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**
* **Alternator Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: Dimming lights, difficulty starting, battery warning light
        * Solution: Replace alternator
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
        * Recalls: None Found
* **Battery Drain**
        * Description/Symptoms: Battery goes dead quickly
        * Solution: Diagnose and repair cause of battery drain (e.g., parasitic draw)
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on cause
        * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**
* **Hood Latch Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: Hood pops open unexpectedly
        * Solution: Replace hood latch
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
        * Recalls: 99S17
* **Tailgate Hinge Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: Tailgate sags or does not close properly
        * Solution: Replace tailgate hinges
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**
* **Power Window Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: Windows do not operate properly, stuck open or closed
        * Solution: Replace window regulator or motor
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600+
        * Recalls: None Found
* **Gauge Cluster Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: Gauges do not function or display inaccurate readings
        * Solution: Replace gauge cluster
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 1999 Ford F-350 faces some common issues, particularly with its engine, transmission, and suspension. Injector failure, turbocharger failure, and transmission slippage are among the most severe and expensive problems to fix, costing thousands of dollars. While the interior and electrical components have fewer issues, power window and gauge cluster failures can still be problematic. Overall, the F-350 is a capable truck but buyers should be aware of these potential issues before purchasing.
