Here's a breakdown of common issues and complaints for the 2004 Ford Econoline, organized by category and component, using the format you requested. The 2004 Ford Econoline, also known as the E-Series, is a full-size van popular for commercial use and passenger transport. Engine options included the 4.2L Essex V6 (200 hp and 260 lb-ft of torque), the 4.6L Triton V8 (225 hp and 290 lb-ft of torque), the 5.4L Triton V8 (255 hp and 350 lb-ft of torque), and the 6.8L Triton V10 (305 hp and 420 lb-ft of torque). Transmission options were primarily the 4R70W 4-speed automatic transmission and the E4OD 4-speed automatic transmission. Owners often appreciate the van's robust build, spacious interior, and towing capacity, while common complaints revolve around fuel economy, age-related maintenance, and some specific component failures.

**Engine**

**4.2L Essex V6**

*   **Intake Manifold Gasket Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaks externally, often detected by a sweet smell and coolant loss. Can lead to overheating if not addressed.
    *   Solution: Replace the intake manifold gasket.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**4.6L Triton V8**

*   **Spark Plug Blowout**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Spark plugs can eject from the cylinder head, often due to stripped threads. This results in a loud popping noise, misfire, and loss of power. More prevalent on early 2V Triton engines.
    *   Solution: Repair the spark plug threads using a thread repair kit (e.g., Time-Sert) or replace the cylinder head.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1000+
    *   Recalls: None Found
*   **Idle Air Control (IAC) Valve Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rough idle, stalling, especially at low speeds or when coming to a stop.
    *   Solution: Replace the IAC valve.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200
    *   Recalls: None Found

**6.8L Triton V10**

*   **Spark Plug Blowout**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Spark plugs can eject from the cylinder head, often due to stripped threads. This results in a loud popping noise, misfire, and loss of power. More prevalent on early 2V Triton engines.
    *   Solution: Repair the spark plug threads using a thread repair kit (e.g., Time-Sert) or replace the cylinder head.

    *   Solution: Replace the exhaust manifold and/or repair broken studs.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $1200+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**4R70W 4-Speed Automatic**

*   **Slipping or Harsh Shifting**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Transmission slips between gears or shifts harshly. Often caused by worn clutch packs or valve body issues.
    *   Solution: Transmission rebuild or replacement. Valve body repair or replacement may also be an option if the issue is isolated.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $350 (Valve Body Repair/Replacement), $1500 - $4000+ (Rebuild/Replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**E4OD 4-Speed Automatic**

*   **Complete Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Transmission loses all gears and fails to move. Can be caused by internal component failure.
    *   Solution: Transmission rebuild or replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1500 - $4000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Worn Ball Joints**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Clunking or popping noises from the front suspension, especially when turning or going over bumps. Can also cause uneven tire wear.
    *   Solution: Replace the worn ball joints.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600+ per side
    *   Recalls: None Found
*   **Worn Leaf Spring Bushings (Rear)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Squeaking or clunking noises from the rear suspension. Reduced ride quality and stability.
    *   Solution: Replace the leaf spring bushings.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $400+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Alternator Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Dimming headlights, battery not charging, vehicle stalling.
    *   Solution: Replace the alternator.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
    *   Recalls: None Found
*   **Door Lock Actuator Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Power door locks not working properly, erratic behavior.
    *   Solution: Replace the faulty door lock actuator.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+ per door
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Rust on Rocker Panels and Wheel Wells**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Visible rust formation on the lower body panels, especially in areas exposed to road salt and moisture.
    *   Solution: Rust repair (cutting out and welding in new panels) or panel replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2000+ per area, depending on severity

    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Headliner Sagging**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The headliner fabric detaches from the backing and sags down, obstructing vision.
    *   Solution: Replace or re-glue the headliner fabric.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+

    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2004 Ford Econoline, while a durable and practical van, is prone to certain age-related issues. Engine issues such as spark plug blowout and intake manifold leaks can be moderately expensive to repair. Transmission problems, especially with slipping or harsh shifting, can be a significant concern. Suspension wear, particularly ball joints and leaf spring bushings, is common. Electrical issues like alternator and door lock actuator failures are also typical. Rust is a prevalent issue, especially in regions with harsh winters. Overall, potential buyers should carefully inspect the vehicle for these issues and factor in potential repair costs. The severity and cost of these issues range from minor inconveniences to major repairs, so a thorough inspection and understanding of the vehicle's history are essential.
