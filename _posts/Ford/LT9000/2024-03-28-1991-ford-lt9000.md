**Introduction:**

The 1991 Ford LT9000 is a heavy-duty commercial truck, known for its durability and performance. It is powered by a Cummins NTC 350 or Caterpillar 3406E engine, and comes with Allison HT740 or Fuller RT-11509B transmission options. The LT9000 offers a comfortable interior and spacious cabin, making it a popular choice for long-haul transportation and construction work.

**Engine**

**Cummins NTC 350**

**Head Gasket Failure:**
Description/Symptoms: Coolant and oil mixing, white smoke from exhaust, overheating
Solution: Replace head gasket
Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+

**Intake Valve Failure:**
Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, rough idling, misfiring
Solution: Replace intake valves
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000

**Oil Pump Failure:**
Description/Symptoms: Low oil pressure, engine damage
Solution: Replace oil pump
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500

**Transmission**

**Allison HT740**

**Transmission Slippage:**
Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears, loss of power
Solution: Rebuild or replace transmission
Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+

**Cooler Line Failure:**
Description/Symptoms: Transmission fluid leaks, overheating
Solution: Replace cooler lines
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500

**Shifting Issues:**
Description/Symptoms: Hard shifting, delayed shifts
Solution: Adjust or replace shift solenoids
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000

**Fuller RT-11509B**

**Clutch Plate Wear:**
Description/Symptoms: Slipping clutch, difficulty shifting
Solution: Replace clutch plate
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000

**Input Shaft Bearing Failure:**
Description/Symptoms: Grating noise during shifting, loss of power
Solution: Replace input shaft bearing
Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+

**Suspension**

**Leaf Spring Failure:**
Description/Symptoms: Sagging rear end, poor handling
Solution: Replace leaf springs
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000

**Airbag Failure:**
Description/Symptoms: Sagging suspension, reduced ride height
Solution: Replace airbags
Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+

**Electrical**

**Starter Failure:**
Description/Symptoms: Engine won't start, clicking sound
Solution: Replace starter
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500

**Alternator Failure:**
Description/Symptoms: Battery not charging, electrical system problems
Solution: Replace alternator
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500

**Wiring Harness Issues:**
Description/Symptoms: Electrical malfunctions, lights not working
Solution: Diagnose and repair wiring harness
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+

**Exterior**

**No Issues Found**

**Interior**

**Dashboard Lights Malfunctioning:**
Description/Symptoms: Gauge cluster or warning lights not working
Solution: Replace faulty bulbs or wiring
Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500

**Seat Adjustment Failure:**
Description/Symptoms: Seats not adjusting electrically
Solution: Repair or replace seat adjustment motors
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000

**HVAC System Failure:**
Description/Symptoms: No heat or AC, blower motor not working
Solution: Diagnose and repair HVAC components
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+

**Summary:**

Overall, the 1991 Ford LT9000 is a reliable and robust commercial truck, but it is not immune to certain issues. While some problems, such as head gasket failure and transmission slippage, can be costly to repair, most issues are relatively minor and inexpensive to fix. The truck's durability and spacious interior make it a popular choice for demanding work applications.
