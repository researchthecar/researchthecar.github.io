**1996 Ford Club Wagon**

The 1996 Ford Club Wagon was a popular passenger van, available with a variety of engine and transmission options. It offered ample seating capacity, a comfortable interior, and reliable performance. However, like any vehicle, it had its share of issues that owners experienced over the years.

**Engine**

* **5.4L V8**
    * **Head gasket failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: Leaking coolant, overheating, white smoke from exhaust
        * Solution: Replace head gasket
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,200 - $2,000+
        * Recalls: None Found
* **4.6L V8**
    * **Intake manifold gasket leak**
        * Description/Symptoms: Coolant leak, rough idle, loss of power
        * Solution: Replace intake manifold gasket
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
        * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

* **4-speed automatic**
    * **Slipping gears**
        * Description/Symptoms: Trouble shifting gears, lack of power
        * Solution: Overhaul or replace transmission
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,000+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Front suspension**
    * **Ball joint wear**
        * Description/Symptoms: Clunking noise when turning, loose steering
        * Solution: Replace ball joints
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
        * Recalls: None Found
* **Rear suspension**
    * **Leaf spring breakage**
        * Description/Symptoms: Sagging rear end, rough ride
        * Solution: Replace leaf springs
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800
        * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Battery**
    * **Premature failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: Difficulty starting, dim lights
        * Solution: Replace battery
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200
        * Recalls: None Found
* **Alternator**
    * **Voltage regulator failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: Overcharging or undercharging battery
        * Solution: Replace alternator
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
        * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Body panels**
    * **Rust and corrosion**
        * Description/Symptoms: Rust spots, peeling paint
        * Solution: Repair or replace body panels
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies widely depending on severity
        * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Dashboard**
    * **Cracks and fading**
        * Description/Symptoms: Aesthetic issue
        * Solution: Replace dashboard
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
        * Recalls: None Found
* **Power windows**
    * **Motor failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: Windows not operating properly
        * Solution: Replace window motor
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
        * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

Overall, the 1996 Ford Club Wagon was a reliable vehicle with a few common issues. The severity and cost of these issues varied widely depending on the severity and age of the vehicle. Some issues, such as electrical problems and interior wear, were relatively minor and affordable to fix. However, more serious issues, such as engine and transmission problems, could be expensive to repair.
