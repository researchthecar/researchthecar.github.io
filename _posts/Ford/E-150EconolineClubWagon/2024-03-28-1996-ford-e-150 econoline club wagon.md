**Introduction:**
The 1996 Ford E-150 Econoline Club Wagon is a full-size van available with multiple engine and transmission options. The standard engine is a 4.9L V8 with 205 horsepower and 282 lb-ft of torque. A 5.8L V8 with 215 horsepower and 310 lb-ft of torque is optional. Both engines are mated to a 4-speed automatic transmission. The Club Wagon is known for its spacious interior, comfortable ride, and versatility, but it has also been plagued by several issues.

**Engine**

**4.9L V8**
* **Intake manifold gasket failure:** Coolant leaking from the intake manifold gaskets; overheating; engine misfiring; rough idle. Solution: Replace the intake manifold gaskets. Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $600+
* **Head gasket failure:** Coolant leaking from the head gaskets; overheating; engine misfiring; white smoke from the exhaust. Solution: Replace the head gaskets. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,000+

**5.8L V8**
* **Camshaft position sensor failure:** Engine stalling; rough idle; reduced power. Solution: Replace the camshaft position sensor. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $300+

**Transmission**

**4-speed automatic**
* **Transmission slipping:** Transmission slipping gears; difficulty shifting; delayed engagement. Solution: Repair or replace the transmission. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,200 - $2,500+

**Suspension**
* **Front ball joint failure:** Loose or worn ball joints; clunking or popping noises; steering problems. Solution: Replace the ball joints. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
* **Rear leaf spring failure:** Sagging rear suspension; uneven tire wear; poor handling. Solution: Replace the leaf springs. Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $600+

**Electrical**
* **Starter solenoid failure:** Engine not starting; clicking noise from the starter. Solution: Replace the starter solenoid. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
* **Alternator failure:** Battery not charging; dimming headlights; electrical problems. Solution: Replace the alternator. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+
* **Wiring harness issues:** Electrical shorts; intermittent electrical problems; blown fuses. Solution: Repair or replace the affected wiring harness. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600+

**Exterior**
* **Door lock failure:** Inoperable door locks; doors not opening or closing properly. Solution: Replace the door lock actuators or switches. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
* **Power window failure:** Power windows not operating; switches not working. Solution: Replace the power window motors or switches. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+

**Interior**
* **Climate control problems:** AC or heater not working properly; temperature not regulating. Solution: Diagnose and repair the climate control system components. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600+
* **Electrical problems:** Dashboard lights not working; gauges not functioning; switches not operating. Solution: Diagnose and repair the affected electrical components. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $400+

**Summary:**
The 1996 Ford E-150 Econoline Club Wagon has a number of potential issues, including engine gasket failures, transmission problems, suspension issues, electrical problems, and interior problems. The severity of these issues can vary, but they can be expensive to fix. Potential owners should be aware of these issues before purchasing this vehicle.
