# 2024 Ford Ranger Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2024 Ford Ranger arrives as a thoroughly redesigned midsize truck, bringing fresh styling, updated technology, and a revised powertrain lineup to the market. Because it represents the initial year of a brand-new generation, prospective used-car buyers should approach it with standard first-year model caution, keeping an eye on potential software bugs and early production quirks. While early owner feedback highlights strong utility and modern driving dynamics, monitoring how these modern systems hold up over time is recommended.

---

## Engine

### 2.3L EcoBoost Turbocharged Inline-4

The base 2.3-liter EcoBoost four-cylinder engine produces approximately 270 horsepower and 310 lb-ft of torque, providing capable daily drivability and towing. It is a carryover architecture known for decent efficiency, though buyers should regularly check fluid levels and maintenance intervals.

### 2.7L EcoBoost Twin-Turbocharged V6

The available 2.7-liter twin-turbo V6 delivers roughly 315 horsepower and 400 lb-ft of torque, offering robust towing and passing power. This powerplant adds extra muscle for demanding tasks while maintaining a smooth delivery, though long-term reliability on this platform in the Ranger warrants continued observation.

### 3.0L EcoBoost Twin-Turbocharged V6 (Raptor)

Exclusive to the high-performance Raptor trim, the 3.0-liter twin-turbo V6 pushes out around 405 horsepower and 430 lb-ft of torque. It is engineered for high-speed off-road performance, though its complex forced-induction systems mean buyers should verify meticulous service histories.

## Transmission

### 10-Speed SelectShift Automatic Transmission

Paired across the engine lineup, the 10-speed automatic transmission aims to optimize fuel economy and power delivery. While gear ratios are well-spaced for towing and highway cruising, buyers should test drive thoroughly to ensure smooth shifting behavior across all gears.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V597000: Visibility:Power Window Devices And Controls](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Ranger&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** When closing the windows with the global closing feature, the windows may not automatically reverse after detecting an object. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 118, "Power-Operated Window Systems." A closing window may exert excessive force by pinching a driver or passenger before.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the driver door module (DDM) and the passenger door module (PDM) software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V770000: Suspension:Front:Control Arm:Upper Ball Joint](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Ranger&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** The front upper control arm ball joint nut may not have been installed or tightened properly, allowing the front upper control arm to detach from the knuckle assembly. A detached control arm can cause a loss of vehicle steering and control, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and verify that the ball joint fastener is tightened properly. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V848000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Hoses, Lines/Piping, And Fittings](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Ranger&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** A fuel vapor leak in the presence of an ignition source can increase the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect for a fuel vapor line bracket and install a tie strap to secure the fuel vapor line to the bracket, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V164000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission:Fluid/Lubricant:Valves/Valve Body](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Ranger&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** Unexpected vehicle movement increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the transmission main control valve body, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V237000: Visibility:Power Window Devices And Controls](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Ranger&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** When closing the windows with the global closing feature, the windows may not automatically reverse after detecting an object. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 118, "Power-Operated Window Systems." A closing window may exert excessive force and pinch a driver or passenger before.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the driver door module (DDM) and the passenger door module (PDM) software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V310000: Suspension:Front:Control Arm:Upper Ball Joint](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Ranger&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** A detached control arm can cause a loss of vehicle steering and control, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and tighten, or replace the ball joint fastener as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V315000: Back Over Prevention:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Ranger&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** A software error may cause the rearview camera image to delay, freeze, or not display when the vehicle is in reverse. A frozen or missing rearview camera image can reduce the driver's view behind the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 25V541000: Air Bags:Side/Window:Curtain](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Ranger&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** The side curtain air bags may contact the B-Pillar inner reinforcements and tear during deployment. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 226, "Ejection Mitigation." Air bags that deploy improperly during a crash increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will install protective shields, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2024 Ford Ranger offers compelling performance, modern technology, and capable engine options for midsize truck shoppers. As a first-year redesign, it is wise to inspect any used model thoroughly, verify that all software updates have been applied, and ensure a clean pre-purchase inspection before finalizing a deal.
