# 2024 Ford Ranger Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a pre-owned 2024 Ford Ranger, buyers will find a modern midsize pickup that delivers capable performance and utility. However, early owner feedback highlights several areas to watch, particularly concerning electronic drivetrain shifts, throttle responsiveness, and specific accessory or structural components. Reviewing these known pain points can help prospective buyers inspect vehicles thoroughly before purchasing.

---

## Engine

### 2.3L Turbocharged I4

The 2.3L turbocharged inline-4 engine serves as the standard powerplant for the Ranger, offering solid torque for daily driving and towing. Buyers should verify proper throttle response during test drives.

**Throttle Hesitation**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers experience a sudden lack of acceleration response when pressing the gas pedal, particularly when turning or merging into moving traffic.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified technician inspect powertrain control modules, throttle body assemblies, and software calibration updates.

**Loss of Motive Power**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may unexpectedly lose power or fail to respond to throttle inputs while underway, creating hazardous driving situations.
- **Solution:** Diagnose engine management codes and check fuel delivery or electronic throttle control components.

### 3.0L Twin-Turbo V6

Available on higher-performance trims, the 3.0L twin-turbo V6 delivers robust horsepower and strong acceleration capabilities. It provides an energetic drive for enthusiasts seeking extra muscle.

## Transmission

### 10-Speed Automatic

The 10-speed automatic transmission is paired across the lineup to optimize fuel economy and power delivery. While generally smooth, buyers should pay attention to shift quality and electronic selector behavior.

**Unintended Drive Mode and 4WD Engagement**
- **Symptoms:** The transmission or drive system unexpectedly shifts into four-wheel drive or specialized off-road modes without driver input from the selector.
- **Solution:** Inspect electronic transfer case actuators, wiring harnesses, and control modules for faults or software glitches.

**Hard Downshifts and Mechanical Noise**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers note harsh downshifts at low speeds that can feel like a rear-end collision, accompanied by abnormal sounds from the drivetrain.
- **Solution:** Check transmission fluid levels, update adaptive shift tables, and inspect internal components for wear.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

The braking system provides adequate stopping power under normal conditions, but individual component wear issues such as warped rotors should be checked during a pre-purchase inspection.

**Brake Pedal Travel and Warped Rotors**
- **Symptoms:** Excessive brake pedal travel before engagement or premature brake rotor warping causing vibration and reduced stopping confidence.
- **Solution:** Inspect brake pads, rotors, and hydraulic lines, and replace warped components as necessary.

## Steering

### Steering Assist

Electronic power steering systems deliver light, maneuverable handling at low speeds and stable feedback on the highway. Drivers should watch for intermittent electronic warning messages.

**Steering Assist Faults**
- **Symptoms:** Illuminated warning messages indicating steering assist malfunctions alongside erratic instrument cluster gauge behavior.
- **Solution:** Have a dealer or specialist scan the vehicle's electrical system and steering control modules for diagnostic trouble codes.

## Electrical

### Visibility and Camera Systems

Exterior visibility systems aid in everyday parking and towing maneuvers, though software-related display blocks can hinder rear visibility until addressed.

**Backup Camera Display Obstruction**
- **Symptoms:** Persistent warning messages block portions of the rear camera display when towing or operating certain features.
- **Solution:** Check for software updates that may resolve camera display overlay errors.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V597000: Visibility:Power Window Devices And Controls](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Ranger&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** When closing the windows with the global closing feature, the windows may not automatically reverse after detecting an object. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 118, "Power-Operated Window Systems." A closing window may exert excessive force by pinching a driver or passenger before.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the driver door module (DDM) and the passenger door module (PDM) software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V770000: Suspension:Front:Control Arm:Upper Ball Joint](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Ranger&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** The front upper control arm ball joint nut may not have been installed or tightened properly, allowing the front upper control arm to detach from the knuckle assembly. A detached control arm can cause a loss of vehicle steering and control, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and verify that the ball joint fastener is tightened properly. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V848000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Hoses, Lines/Piping, And Fittings](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Ranger&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** A fuel vapor leak in the presence of an ignition source can increase the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect for a fuel vapor line bracket and install a tie strap to secure the fuel vapor line to the bracket, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V164000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission:Fluid/Lubricant:Valves/Valve Body](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Ranger&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** Unexpected vehicle movement increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the transmission main control valve body, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V237000: Visibility:Power Window Devices And Controls](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Ranger&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** When closing the windows with the global closing feature, the windows may not automatically reverse after detecting an object. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 118, "Power-Operated Window Systems." A closing window may exert excessive force and pinch a driver or passenger before.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the driver door module (DDM) and the passenger door module (PDM) software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V310000: Suspension:Front:Control Arm:Upper Ball Joint](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Ranger&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** A detached control arm can cause a loss of vehicle steering and control, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and tighten, or replace the ball joint fastener as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V315000: Back Over Prevention:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Ranger&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** A software error may cause the rearview camera image to delay, freeze, or not display when the vehicle is in reverse. A frozen or missing rearview camera image can reduce the driver's view behind the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 25V541000: Air Bags:Side/Window:Curtain](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Ranger&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** The side curtain air bags may contact the B-Pillar inner reinforcements and tear during deployment. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 226, "Ejection Mitigation." Air bags that deploy improperly during a crash increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will install protective shields, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2024 Ford Ranger offers strong utility and multiple powertrain choices, making it a competitive midsize truck option. However, used-car buyers should thoroughly test-drive candidate vehicles to check for smooth transmission shifting, responsive throttle behavior, and the absence of electronic drivetrain glitches. A comprehensive pre-purchase inspection will help ensure all systems operate as intended.
