# 2020 Ford Ranger Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2020 Ford Ranger offers capable midsize truck performance and strong towing numbers, but prospective buyers should look closely at its mechanical history. While owners appreciate its utility and fuel economy, the vehicle has accumulated significant complaints regarding transmission behavior, electrical glitches, and component failures. Paying careful attention to these known trouble spots during a pre-purchase inspection can save buyers from expensive surprises down the road.

---

## Engine

### 2.3L EcoBoost I4

The 2.3-liter turbocharged EcoBoost inline-four produces 270 horsepower and 310 lb-ft of torque, providing robust acceleration and towing capability for a midsize truck. Overall durability is generally good, though owners occasionally report unexpected stalling and electronic warning lights.

**Engine Stalling and Loss of Power**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle may intermittently stall, enter a reduced-power limp mode, or illuminate electrical warning lights on the dashboard.
- **Solution:** Diagnostic scan required to check for powertrain control module codes, software updates, or failing sensors.

## Transmission

### 10-Speed Automatic Transmission

Paired exclusively with the EcoBoost engine, the 10-speed automatic transmission delivers responsive gearing for fuel efficiency and towing. However, it is also the subject of the most frequent and severe owner complaints regarding harsh engagement and mechanical failure.

**Transmission Slipping, Shuddering, and Harsh Shifting**
- **Symptoms:** The 10-speed automatic may experience erratic shifting, hard shifts between gears, severe shuddering, or complete loss of engagement while driving.
- **Solution:** Reprogramming the powertrain control module, replacing faulty valve bodies, or in severe cases, complete transmission replacement.
- **Cost:** $4000+

## Brakes

### Brake System

The standard braking setup provides adequate stopping power under normal conditions, though some drivers note unusual pedal feel that requires prompt attention.

**Soft Brake Pedal and Brake Failure Warnings**
- **Symptoms:** Brake pedal feels spongy, travels lower than the accelerator pedal, or exhibits delayed stopping response.
- **Solution:** Bleeding the anti-lock braking system (ABS) modules and checking brake lines for air or fluid leaks.
- **Cost:** $150

## Electrical

### Backup Camera and Infotainment

Electrical accessories generally function well, but infotainment and parking safety cameras are prone to intermittent software freezes and display corruption.

**Backup Camera Freezing or Blank Screen**
- **Symptoms:** The rearview camera display intermittently freezes, shows image distortion, or goes completely black when shifting into reverse.
- **Solution:** Inspect camera wiring harnesses, apply software updates, or replace the rearview camera assembly.

## Interior

### HVAC System

The interior layout is functional and durable, though comfort systems can suffer from early mechanical wear in climate control distribution doors.

**Blend Door Actuator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Clicking sounds from behind the dashboard and failure of the cabin heater or defrost to direct air properly.
- **Solution:** Replace the faulty blend door actuator assembly with the redesigned updated part.

## Safety

### Seat Belts

Standard safety equipment is comprehensive, but child safety and passenger seat belt mechanisms require careful checking to ensure reliable latching and retraction.

**Seat Belt Retraction Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Rear or child seat belts fail to retract smoothly or lock up improperly during normal use.
- **Solution:** Inspect seat belt assemblies and replace faulty retractors under warranty or service guidelines.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V765000: Seats:Mid/Rear Assembly](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Ranger&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** Additionally, the child seat may not attach properly to the lower-rear anchors. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 225, "Child Restraint Anchorage System." An improperly attached child seat can increase the risk of injury in a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the rear-lower child restraint anchorages for correct alignment and adjust as necessary. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V811000: Seat Belts:Front:Webbing](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Ranger&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** The front passenger outboard seat belt may not have been assembled properly, allowing the seat belt to be misrouted. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard numbers 208, "Occupant Crash Protection" and 209, "Seat Belt Assemblies." A misrouted seat belt may not adequately restrain an occupant in a.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the front row outboard seat belts and replace them, if necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V575000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Ranger&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 111, "Rear Visibility." Reduced rearward visibility can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will replace the rearview camera, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V197000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission:Gear Position Indication (Prndl)](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Ranger&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** The transmission shift cable lock clip may not be fully seated, allowing the transmission to actually be in a different gear than the gear shift position selected by the driver. If the transmission selection does not match the indicated gear selection, and the parking brake is not applied, unintended vehicle movement can occur, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will inspect and correct the shift cable locking clip installation, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V379000: Equipment:Other:Labels](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Ranger&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 110, "Tire Selection and Rims." An overloaded vehicle increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 25V442000: Back Over Prevention:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Ranger&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** A software error may cause the rearview camera to display a blank image, or the image may remain on the display after the backing event has ended. Both of these situations can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the rearview camera software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V685000: Equipment:Electrical:Engine Block Heater](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Ranger&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** The engine block heater may crack and develop a coolant leak, causing it to short circuit when the block heater is plugged in. An electrical short circuit can increase the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the block heater, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

When shopping for a used model, perform an extended test drive that specifically tests the transmission's shifting quality across all gears, as rough shifting or slipping can indicate costly repairs. Verify that all dashboard electronics, climate control blend doors, and the backup camera function correctly before finalizing your purchase.
