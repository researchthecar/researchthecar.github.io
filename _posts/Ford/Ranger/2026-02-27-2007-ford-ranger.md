# 2007 Ford Ranger

### Overview
The 2007 Ford Ranger, a compact pickup truck, was known for its robust and utilitarian nature. It offered a range of engine and transmission options to suit various needs, from basic workhorse duties to more capable off-road applications. While generally considered a reliable vehicle for its era, like any automotive product, it has specific areas where owners have reported issues. This analysis focuses on common mechanical and electrical concerns, excluding routine maintenance, to provide a clear picture of potential reliability challenges for the 2007 model year.

---


## Engine

### 2.3L I4
**Intake Manifold Runner Control (IMRC) Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Check Engine Light, rough idle, loss of power, poor fuel economy.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the IMRC actuator or the entire intake manifold.
- **Cost:** $300 - $700

**Camshaft Position Sensor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Check Engine Light, stalling, difficulty starting, rough idle.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the camshaft position sensor.
- **Cost:** $150 - $300


### 3.0L V6
**Intake Manifold Gasket Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Coolant leaks, white smoke from exhaust, overheating, rough idle.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the intake manifold gaskets.
- **Cost:** $400 - $800

**Camshaft Position Sensor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Check Engine Light, stalling, difficulty starting, rough idle.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the camshaft position sensor.
- **Cost:** $150 - $300


### 4.0L V6
**Timing Chain Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Rattling noise from the front of the engine, Check Engine Light, potential engine damage if ignored.
- **Solution:** Replacement of timing chain, guides, and tensioners.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1500

**Camshaft Position Sensor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Check Engine Light, stalling, difficulty starting, rough idle.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the camshaft position sensor.
- **Cost:** $150 - $300


## Transmission

### 5-Speed Manual Transmission (M5OD-R1)
**Clutch Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Slipping clutch, difficulty shifting gears, burning smell.
- **Solution:** Clutch replacement (clutch kit).
- **Cost:** $600 - $1200


### 5-Speed Automatic Transmission (5R55E)
**Torque Converter Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Shuddering, slipping, delayed engagement, fluid leaks.
- **Solution:** Replacement or rebuild of the torque converter.
- **Cost:** $700 - $1500

**Solenoid Pack Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Harsh shifting, transmission slipping, failure to shift into certain gears, Check Engine Light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the transmission solenoid pack.
- **Cost:** $400 - $800


## Suspension

### Standard Chassis
**Ball Joint Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Clunking noises over bumps, uneven tire wear, steering looseness.
- **Solution:** Replacement of worn ball joints.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600

**Leaf Spring Sagging**
- **Symptoms:** Rear end sag, reduced load-carrying capacity, rougher ride.
- **Solution:** Replacement of leaf springs.
- **Cost:** $400 - $900


## Electrical

### General Electrical System
**Alternator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Dimming headlights, battery warning light, electrical components malfunctioning, dead battery.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the alternator.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600

**Window Regulator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Power windows not working, slow or jerky window operation.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the window regulator assembly.
- **Cost:** $200 - $400


## Exterior

### Body Panels & Trim
**Rust on Frame and Body**
- **Symptoms:** Visible rust, especially in areas prone to salt exposure (e.g., Midwest, coastal regions).
- **Solution:** Rust treatment, repair, or replacement of affected panels/frame sections.
- **Cost:** $500 - $3000+


## Interior

### Interior Components
**HVAC Blower Motor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** No air coming from vents, or air only coming from certain vents, regardless of fan speed setting.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the HVAC blower motor.
- **Cost:** $200 - $400


--- 
### Summary Verdict
The 2007 Ford Ranger is a generally dependable truck, particularly for its intended use as a work vehicle or for light-duty hauling. The most common issues tend to revolve around the engine's intake system components (especially on the 2.3L and 3.0L), and the automatic transmission's torque converter and solenoid pack. Suspension components like ball joints and leaf springs can also wear over time, as expected for a truck. Electrical issues like alternator failure are not uncommon for vehicles of this age. Rust can be a significant concern depending on the climate the truck has lived in. Overall, with regular maintenance and attention to these common problem areas, the 2007 Ranger can remain a reliable option.
