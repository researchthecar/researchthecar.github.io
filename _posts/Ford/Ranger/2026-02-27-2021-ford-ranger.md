# 2021 Ford Ranger Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2021 Ford Ranger brings a capable midsize pickup option to the market, anchored by a turbocharged four-cylinder engine and a modern automatic transmission. While it offers solid utility and towing performance for daily work and weekend getaways, prospective buyers should review available owner feedback regarding minor electrical quirks and accessory operation. Overall, a pre-purchase inspection remains a smart step to ensure all mechanical and electronic systems are functioning properly.

---

## Engine

### 2.3L EcoBoost Turbo Inline-4

The 2.3L EcoBoost inline-4 generates 270 horsepower and 310 lb-ft of torque, providing strong low-end acceleration and towing capability. It is generally regarded as a robust and dependable powerplant for daily driving.

## Transmission

### 10-Speed SelectShift Automatic

The 10-speed automatic transmission is paired with both rear-wheel drive and four-wheel drive configurations. It delivers smooth highway cruising and optimized gear selection, though proper fluid maintenance is key to long-term durability.

## Recall Check

**[Recall 21V765000: Seats:Mid/Rear Assembly](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Ranger&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** Additionally, the child seat may not attach properly to the lower-rear anchors. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 225, "Child Restraint Anchorage System." An improperly attached child seat can increase the risk of injury in a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the rear-lower child restraint anchorages for correct alignment and adjust as necessary. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V811000: Seat Belts:Front:Webbing](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Ranger&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The front passenger outboard seat belt may not have been assembled properly, allowing the seat belt to be misrouted. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard numbers 208, "Occupant Crash Protection" and 209, "Seat Belt Assemblies." A misrouted seat belt may not adequately restrain an occupant in a.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the front row outboard seat belts and replace them, if necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V379000: Equipment:Other:Labels](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Ranger&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 110, "Tire Selection and Rims." An overloaded vehicle increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 25V442000: Back Over Prevention:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Ranger&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** A software error may cause the rearview camera to display a blank image, or the image may remain on the display after the backing event has ended. Both of these situations can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the rearview camera software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V685000: Equipment:Electrical:Engine Block Heater](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Ranger&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The engine block heater may crack and develop a coolant leak, causing it to short circuit when the block heater is plugged in. An electrical short circuit can increase the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the block heater, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2021 Ford Ranger presents a strong package for used midsize truck buyers, combining a punchy turbocharged engine with capable hauling attributes. Without widespread mechanical red flags from owner reports, it stands as a dependable choice in its class, provided individual vehicle history and condition check out during a test drive.
