# 2019 Ford Ranger Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2019 Ford Ranger brings a modern midsize truck option to the used market, featuring a turbocharged powertrain and useful towing capability. While it offers solid utility, prospective buyers should review specific mechanical patterns reported by owners. Particular attention should be paid to powertrain behavior and suspension components during a pre-purchase inspection.

---

## Engine

### 2.3L EcoBoost Turbocharged I4

The 2.3-liter EcoBoost turbocharged four-cylinder engine produces 270 horsepower and 310 lb-ft of torque, providing strong acceleration and towing capability for a midsize truck. It is generally robust, though routine maintenance of the turbocharging and ignition systems is key to long-term dependability.

**Engine Stalling and Misfires**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle experiences unexpected engine hesitation, shuddering sensations on acceleration, and occasional stalling.
- **Solution:** Inspect ignition components, spark plugs, and fuel delivery systems; replace worn parts as necessary.

## Transmission

### 10-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 10-speed automatic transmission is paired with the 2.3L engine to optimize fuel economy and power delivery across a wide range of driving conditions. Some owners have reported shift quality concerns, making a thorough test drive essential.

**Transmission Shudder and Harsh Shifting**
- **Symptoms:** Erratic gear changes, hesitation when shifting between gears, and a noticeable shudder or lunging sensation during stop-and-go driving.
- **Solution:** Reprogram or update the transmission control module software, or inspect internal components according to manufacturer service bulletins.

**Loss of Power and Delayed Engagement**
- **Symptoms:** Delayed engagement when shifting into reverse or drive, along with temporary loss of motive power when pulling away from stops.
- **Solution:** Check transmission fluid levels, inspect valve bodies, and perform diagnostic testing on the transmission control module.

## Suspension

### Rear Leaf Springs

The rear suspension utilizes traditional leaf springs designed to support payload and towing duties. Buyers should inspect the rear spring packs for signs of corrosion, sagging, or fracture.

**Leaf Spring Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Clunking or snapping noises from the rear suspension, followed by visible cracking or breaking of the rear leaf springs near the mounts.
- **Solution:** Inspect both rear leaf spring assemblies for corrosion or structural cracking and replace damaged leaf springs.

## Exterior

### Climate Control System

Cabin electronics manage various comfort and convenience features, though climate control actuator failures have been noted by several owners.

**Temperature Blend Door Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Constant clicking or snapping noises behind the dashboard, with the HVAC system failing to properly adjust air temperature or direct airflow.
- **Solution:** Replace the defective blend door actuator or heater box components.

## Safety

### Restraints and Airbags

Safety systems include standard airbags and active driver assists, though warning lights related to occupant sensors require prompt professional diagnosis.

**Airbag Warning Indicator**
- **Symptoms:** Illuminated airbag warning lamp on the instrument cluster indicating a fault within the passenger-side occupant detection or airbag circuit.
- **Solution:** Diagnose the restraint control module and inspect seat belt pretensioners and airbag wiring harnesses.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V839000: Exterior Lighting:Tail Lights](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Ranger&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** Intermittent or inoperative tail lamps can result in loss of stop, turn, rear position, and reverse lamp functions, which reduces the vehicle's visibility and may not fully alert other drivers that the vehicle is braking, turning, or reversing, increasing the risk of crash.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the inline connector for loose terminals and replace the connectors, if necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V137000: Visibility:Defroster/Defogger/Hvac System:Fan/Motor](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Ranger&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** A resistive electrical short increases the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will inspect and replace the HVAC blower motor, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V811000: Seat Belts:Front:Webbing](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Ranger&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** The front passenger outboard seat belt may not have been assembled properly, allowing the seat belt to be misrouted. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard numbers 208, "Occupant Crash Protection" and 209, "Seat Belt Assemblies." A misrouted seat belt may not adequately restrain an occupant in a.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the front row outboard seat belts and replace them, if necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V071000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Ranger&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** As a result, these vehicles fail to comply with Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 114, "Theft Protection." If the vehicle can be shifted out of Park without the brake applied, the vehicle may unexpectedly move. Unintentional vehicle movement can increase the risk of injury or crash.
- **What to do:** Ford has notified owners, and dealers will inspect and repair the transmission floor shift selector interlock assembly, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V518000: Seat Belts](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Ranger&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** Improperly assembled seat belts may not adequately restrain occupants in a crash, increasing their risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will inspect and, if necessary, replace the right front seat belt assembly, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V726000: Visibility:Defroster/Defogger/Hvac System:Fan/Motor](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Ranger&modelYear=2019)**

- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the HVAC blower motor production date, replacing it as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V366000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission:Lever And Linkage:Floor Shift](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Ranger&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** Either scenario increases the risk of crash or injury.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will inspect and tighten the transmission shift cable bracket fasteners as necessary, and verify the proper transmission shift operation, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V379000: Equipment:Other:Labels](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Ranger&modelYear=2019)**

## Summary Verdict

The 2019 Ford Ranger is a capable midsize pickup with strong towing credentials and an efficient turbocharged engine. Prospective buyers should prioritize a comprehensive test drive to evaluate 10-speed transmission shift quality and thoroughly inspect the rear suspension leaf springs and HVAC actuators during a pre-purchase inspection.
