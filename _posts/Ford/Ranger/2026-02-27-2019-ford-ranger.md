# 2019 Ford Ranger

### Overview
The 2019 Ford Ranger, a popular mid-size pickup truck, generally exhibits a solid reliability record, building on Ford's truck manufacturing heritage. It offers a blend of capability and modern features, making it a competitive option in its segment. While most owners report satisfactory experiences, like any vehicle, there are specific areas where potential issues have been noted. This analysis focuses on documented technical reliability concerns, excluding routine maintenance and common wear-and-tear items, to provide a clear picture for potential buyers and current owners. We will examine the primary engine and transmission options, as well as other key vehicle systems.

---


## Engine

### 2.3L EcoBoost (I4)
**Turbocharger Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of power, whistling noises, increased oil consumption.
- **Solution:** Turbocharger replacement or repair.
- **Cost:** $1,200 - $2,500

**Coolant Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Visible coolant puddles under the vehicle, overheating, low coolant level.
- **Solution:** Inspection and replacement of leaking hoses, gaskets, or water pump.
- **Cost:** $300 - $800


## Transmission

### 10-Speed Automatic (10R80)
**Hesitation/Jerking**
- **Symptoms:** Rough shifting, delayed engagement, noticeable jerks during acceleration or deceleration.
- **Solution:** Software updates, transmission fluid flush and fill, or internal component repair.
- **Cost:** $200 - $1,500

### Summary Verdict
The 2019 Ford Ranger demonstrates good overall reliability, particularly in its suspension, exterior, and interior components, which show no widespread reported issues. The primary areas of concern revolve around the 2.3L EcoBoost engine, with potential for turbocharger problems and coolant leaks, and the 10-speed automatic transmission, which can sometimes exhibit shifting irregularities. Electrical systems, specifically the Sync infotainment and potential battery drain issues, are also areas to monitor. While these issues are not universal, they represent the most commonly cited technical problems. Addressing these potential concerns proactively can contribute to a more dependable ownership experience.
