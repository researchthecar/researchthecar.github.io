# 2022 Ford Ranger Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used midsize truck, evaluating historical patterns helps prospective owners anticipate potential repair needs and ownership costs. This reliability assessment compiles owner-reported feedback and common trouble spots to give buyers a clearer picture of what to expect behind the wheel.

---

## Engine

### 2.3L EcoBoost Turbo I4

The 2.3-liter turbocharged inline-four engine produces 270 horsepower and 310 lb-ft of torque, providing strong low-end pulling power and competitive fuel economy. While generally robust, buyers should inspect the upper engine bay for minor fluid leaks and moisture exposure around the ignition components.

**Valve Cover and Pump Seal Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Oil leaks or high-pressure pump seal issues can develop around the engine components, though warning messages may not always appear initially.
- **Solution:** Inspect valve covers and high-pressure fuel pump seals for leaks, and replace damaged gaskets or covers as necessary.

**Ignition and Spark Plug Moisture Intrusion**
- **Symptoms:** Engine misfires and ignition coil failure can occur if water reaches the spark plug wells.
- **Solution:** Check for proper engine cover installation and replace damaged ignition coils and spark plugs.

## Transmission

### 10-Speed Automatic

The 10-speed automatic transmission channels power efficiently to the rear wheels or four-wheel-drive system. Some owners report shifting roughness and mechanical concerns requiring software updates or major service.

**Harsh Shifting and Gear Engagement**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may experience delayed engagement, rough shifting between intermediate gears, or unexpected jerking during acceleration.
- **Solution:** Check for applicable manufacturer service bulletins regarding transmission calibration updates or fluid replacement.

**Gear Selection and Limp Mode Faults**
- **Symptoms:** Illuminated wrench icons, unexpected locking into high gears, or total mechanical failure can strand the vehicle.
- **Solution:** Diagnose transmission control module codes and perform fluid services or mechanical overhauls depending on the extent of internal wear.

## Electrical

### Charging and Starting System

Electrical gremlins occasionally affect starting and accessory modules, requiring thorough diagnostic testing.

**Intermittent No-Start and Module Faults**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may fail to crank or start with the key, or experience sudden electrical power loss while driving.
- **Solution:** Test the battery condition, charging output, and associated electrical control modules for faults.

## Exterior

### Rear Window Glass

Exterior body components are generally durable, though isolated instances of spontaneous rear glass breakage have been reported.

**Spontaneous Rear Window Shattering**
- **Symptoms:** The rear cab window glass may fracture or completely shatter suddenly without any direct physical impact.
- **Solution:** Replace the damaged rear window assembly.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V451000: Visibility:Windshield](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Ranger&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** The windshield may not have been properly bonded to the vehicle, which could allow it to detach during a crash. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 212, "Windshield Mounting." A windshield that separates from a vehicle during a crash can increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will remove and reinstall the windshield, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V685000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Hoses, Lines/Piping, And Fittings](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Ranger&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** An improperly tightened fuel line connection may result in a fuel leak, increasing the risk of fire.
- **What to do:** Ford will inspect and replace the fuel line assembly, if needed, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V379000: Equipment:Other:Labels](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Ranger&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 110, "Tire Selection and Rims." An overloaded vehicle increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 25V442000: Back Over Prevention:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Ranger&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** A software error may cause the rearview camera to display a blank image, or the image may remain on the display after the backing event has ended. Both of these situations can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the rearview camera software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V685000: Equipment:Electrical:Engine Block Heater](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Ranger&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** The engine block heater may crack and develop a coolant leak, causing it to short circuit when the block heater is plugged in. An electrical short circuit can increase the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the block heater, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

Overall, this vehicle offers capable performance and utility, but prospective buyers should conduct a thorough pre-purchase inspection. Pay close attention to transmission shift quality and test all electrical accessories and powertrain components during a test drive to ensure everything operates smoothly.
