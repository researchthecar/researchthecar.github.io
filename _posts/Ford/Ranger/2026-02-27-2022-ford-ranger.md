# 2022 Ford Ranger Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2022 Ford Ranger brings a capable midsize truck option to the market, anchored by its modern turbocharged powertrain. While overall owner feedback is generally positive regarding its utility and daily drivability, prospective buyers should review available powertrain configurations and understand potential ownership quirks reported in early-model feedback.

---

## Engine

### 2.3L EcoBoost I4

This 2.3-liter turbocharged inline-four engine produces approximately 270 horsepower and 310 lb-ft of torque, providing strong low-end pulling power. It serves as the standard powerplant for the lineup, balancing everyday efficiency with solid midsize truck capability.

## Transmission

### 10-Speed SelectShift Automatic

A 10-speed automatic transmission paired with either rear-wheel drive or four-wheel drive. It offers smooth highway cruising ratios and responsive gear selection, though buyers should test drive to ensure shifting behavior suits their preferences.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V451000: Visibility:Windshield](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Ranger&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** The windshield may not have been properly bonded to the vehicle, which could allow it to detach during a crash. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 212, "Windshield Mounting." A windshield that separates from a vehicle during a crash can increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will remove and reinstall the windshield, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V685000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Hoses, Lines/Piping, And Fittings](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Ranger&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** An improperly tightened fuel line connection may result in a fuel leak, increasing the risk of fire.
- **What to do:** Ford will inspect and replace the fuel line assembly, if needed, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V379000: Equipment:Other:Labels](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Ranger&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 110, "Tire Selection and Rims." An overloaded vehicle increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 25V442000: Back Over Prevention:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Ranger&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** A software error may cause the rearview camera to display a blank image, or the image may remain on the display after the backing event has ended. Both of these situations can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the rearview camera software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V685000: Equipment:Electrical:Engine Block Heater](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Ranger&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** The engine block heater may crack and develop a coolant leak, causing it to short circuit when the block heater is plugged in. An electrical short circuit can increase the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the block heater, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2022 Ford Ranger presents a solid option for used midsize truck buyers looking for modern efficiency and towing strength. With standard powertrain configurations well-established on the market, routine pre-purchase inspections and test drives remain the best approach for verifying mechanical health.
