# 2022 Ford Ranger

### Overview
The 2022 Ford Ranger is a popular mid-size pickup truck known for its capability and versatility. Reliability data for this model year generally indicates a solid performance, with most owners reporting satisfaction. However, as with any vehicle, there are specific areas that have seen occasional reports of issues. This analysis focuses on documented technical problems beyond routine maintenance, covering key systems like the engine, transmission, suspension, electrical, exterior, and interior components. The aim is to provide a clear picture of potential concerns for prospective buyers and current owners.

---


## Engine

### 2.3L EcoBoost (I4)
**Turbocharger Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of power, whistling noises, increased oil consumption.
- **Solution:** Turbocharger inspection, repair, or replacement.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1500

**Coolant Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Overheating, visible coolant puddles, sweet smell.
- **Solution:** Inspection and replacement of leaking hoses, gaskets, or water pump.
- **Cost:** $300 - $700


## Transmission

### 10-Speed Automatic (10R80)
**Hesitation or Shuddering**
- **Symptoms:** Jerky shifts, delayed engagement, noticeable shudder during acceleration.
- **Solution:** Transmission fluid flush and fill, software update, or internal component repair.
- **Cost:** $400 - $1200

### Summary Verdict
The 2022 Ford Ranger demonstrates good overall reliability, with the powertrain and chassis generally performing well. The most frequently cited technical concerns revolve around the 10-speed automatic transmission's shifting behavior and occasional issues with the infotainment system and turbocharger on the 2.3L EcoBoost engine. Electrical gremlins, such as battery drain and infotainment glitches, are also noted but do not appear to be systemic. Exterior and interior components are largely free of widespread problems, with minor reports of tailgate stiffness. For owners, addressing transmission software updates and being mindful of potential turbocharger and electrical system checks can contribute to a more seamless ownership experience.
