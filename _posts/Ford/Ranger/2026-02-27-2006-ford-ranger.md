# 2006 Ford Ranger

### Overview
The 2006 Ford Ranger, a compact pickup truck, is generally considered a reliable vehicle, especially for its era. It was known for its robust construction and straightforward mechanicals, making it a popular choice for work and personal use. While no vehicle is entirely free from potential issues, the Ranger typically performs well with proper maintenance. Common areas of concern tend to be related to age and mileage, such as minor electrical glitches or wear on suspension components. However, major powertrain failures are not frequently reported for most configurations, contributing to its reputation as a dependable, albeit basic, truck.

## Suspension

### Standard Chassis
**Ball Joint Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Clunking noises from the front end, especially over bumps; uneven tire wear; steering wander.
- **Solution:** Replacement of worn ball joints.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600

**Leaf Spring Sagging**
- **Symptoms:** Rear end appears lower than normal, reduced load-carrying capacity, potential for bottoming out.
- **Solution:** Replacement of worn or broken leaf springs.
- **Cost:** $400 - $800


## Electrical

### General Electrical System
**Alternator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Dimming headlights, battery warning light illuminated, difficulty starting, electrical components malfunctioning.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the alternator.
- **Cost:** $400 - $700

**Window Regulator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Power windows not operating, slow window movement, grinding noises when operating windows.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the window regulator mechanism.
- **Cost:** $200 - $400


## Exterior

### Body and Frame
**Frame Rust**
- **Symptoms:** Visible rust on the frame rails, particularly in areas exposed to salt and moisture; potential structural weakening.
- **Solution:** Rust treatment, repair, or frame replacement (depending on severity).
- **Cost:** $500 - $3000+

**Tailgate Latch Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Difficulty opening or closing the tailgate, latch sticking.
- **Solution:** Lubrication or replacement of tailgate latch components.
- **Cost:** $100 - $250


## Interior

### Cabin Components
**HVAC Blower Motor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** No air coming from vents, or air only coming from certain vents; unusual noises from the dashboard when HVAC is on.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the blower motor or resistor.
- **Cost:** $200 - $450

**Seat Upholstery Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Tearing or excessive wear on seat fabric or vinyl, especially on driver's seat.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of seat covers/upholstery.
- **Cost:** $150 - $500

### Summary Verdict
The 2006 Ford Ranger is a generally reliable compact pickup truck. Its powertrain options (2.3L I4, 3.0L V6, 4.0L V6) and transmissions (manual and automatic) are known for their durability with proper maintenance. The most common issues tend to be related to age and mileage, such as suspension wear (ball joints, leaf springs), minor electrical faults (alternator, window regulators), and potential rust on the frame in certain climates. Interior components like the HVAC blower motor can also fail over time. Overall, the Ranger offers good value and dependability for its class, with most repairs being relatively straightforward and affordable.
