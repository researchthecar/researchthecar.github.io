# 2017 Ford F-250 Super Duty

### Overview
The 2017 Ford F-250 Super Duty is a heavy-duty pickup truck engineered for demanding work and towing. It is known for its robust construction and powerful engine options. Reliability for this model year is generally strong, with owners reporting satisfaction with its performance and durability. However, like any vehicle, specific components can experience issues. This analysis focuses on documented technical reliability concerns, excluding routine maintenance and normal wear and tear, to provide a clear picture of potential problem areas for owners and prospective buyers.

### 6.7L Power Stroke V8 Diesel
**DPF Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Check Engine Light illumination, reduced engine power, rough idling, increased fuel consumption.
- **Solution:** DPF regeneration cycle, DPF cleaning, or DPF replacement.
- **Cost:** $500 - $2,500

**EGR System Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Check Engine Light, rough idle, loss of power, black smoke from exhaust.
- **Solution:** Cleaning or replacement of EGR valve and cooler.
- **Cost:** $400 - $1,200

### Summary Verdict
The 2017 Ford F-250 Super Duty demonstrates good overall reliability, particularly with its gasoline engine and standard transmission. The diesel engine, while powerful, has some documented issues related to emissions control systems like the DPF and EGR, which can be costly to repair. Transmission issues, specifically with the torque converter on the diesel variant, are also noted. Electrical components and sensors can occasionally fail, leading to warning lights and performance issues. The exterior and interior generally hold up well. For owners of the diesel model, proactive maintenance of the emissions system is recommended to mitigate potential problems and associated repair costs.
