**Introduction:**
The 2017 Ford F-250 Super Duty is a heavy-duty pickup truck available with a range of engine and transmission options. The base engine is a 6.7L Power Stroke V8 diesel, producing 440 horsepower and 925 lb-ft of torque. Optional engines include a 6.2L V8 gasoline engine and a 7.3L V8 gasoline engine. Transmission options include a 6-speed automatic, a 10-speed automatic, and a manual transmission. The F-250 Super Duty is known for its towing and hauling capabilities, as well as its spacious interior and rugged exterior. However, it also faces some common issues.

**Engine**

**6.7L Power Stroke V8 Diesel**

* **Turbocharger failure:** Symptoms include loss of power, excessive smoke, and increased oil consumption. Solution: Replace the turbocharger. Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,000+
* **Fuel injector failure:** Symptoms include hard starting, rough idling, and reduced fuel economy. Solution: Replace the fuel injectors. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000

**6.2L V8 Gasoline**

* **Intake manifold gasket failure:** Symptoms include coolant leaks, engine overheating, and reduced performance. Solution: Replace the intake manifold gasket. Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800
* **Spark plug failure:** Symptoms include misfiring, rough idling, and reduced fuel economy. Solution: Replace the spark plugs. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400

**7.3L V8 Gasoline**

* **No Issues Found**

**Transmission**

**6-Speed Automatic**

* **Torque converter shudder:** Symptoms include vibrations during acceleration and a slipping transmission. Solution: Replace the torque converter. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500
* **Transmission fluid leak:** Symptoms include a low transmission fluid level and slipping gears. Solution: Repair or replace the leaking transmission component. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000

**10-Speed Automatic**

* **Shifting problems:** Symptoms include delayed shifts, hard shifts, and slipping gears. Solution: Reprogram the transmission control module or replace the transmission. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+
* **Transmission cooler failure:** Symptoms include overheating transmission fluid and transmission failure. Solution: Replace the transmission cooler. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+

**Manual Transmission**

* **Clutch wear:** Symptoms include difficulty shifting gears and slipping gears. Solution: Replace the clutch. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $1,500

**Suspension**

* **Leaf spring breakage:** Symptoms include sagging rear end, reduced ride height, and difficulty towing or carrying heavy loads. Solution: Replace the leaf springs. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000 per leaf spring
* **Shock absorber failure:** Symptoms include a rough ride, reduced handling, and poor stability. Solution: Replace the shock absorbers. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400 per shock

**Electrical**

* **Battery drain:** Symptoms include difficulty starting the vehicle, dim lights, and malfunctioning electrical components. Solution: Replace the battery or repair electrical system components. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600+
* **Alternator failure:** Symptoms include a flickering dashboard light, dim lights, and difficulty starting the vehicle. Solution: Replace the alternator. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000

**Exterior**

* **Tailgate latch failure:** Symptoms include difficulty opening or closing the tailgate, loose tailgate, and rattling noises. Solution: Repair or replace the tailgate latch. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
* **Headlight bulb failure:** Symptoms include dim or flickering headlights, reduced visibility, and failed inspection. Solution: Replace the headlight bulbs. Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $200 depending on the bulbs

**Interior**

* **Center console lid failure:** Symptoms include a broken lid, loose hinges, or difficulty opening or closing. Solution: Replace the center console lid. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500
* **HVAC system failure:** Symptoms include a lack of heat or air conditioning, blower motor noise, or temperature control issues. Solution: Repair or replace HVAC components. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
* **Integrated Control Panel (ICP) failure:** Symptoms include a flickering or blank screen, difficulty using touch controls, or malfunctioning navigation system. Solution: Replace the ICP. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+

**Summary:**

The 2017 Ford F-250 Super Duty has several issues that can affect its performance, reliability, and comfort. Engine and transmission issues appear to be the most common and can be costly to repair. Suspension and electrical problems are also present, while some interior components have reported failures. The severity and cost of these issues vary depending on the specific problem and the affected part. Overall, owners may experience some inconvenience and potential expenses associated with these issues.
