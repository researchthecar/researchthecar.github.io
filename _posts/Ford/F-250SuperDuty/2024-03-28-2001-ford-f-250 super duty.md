**2001 Ford F-250 Super Duty**

The 2001 Ford F-250 Super Duty is a heavy-duty pickup truck known for its strength and capabilities. It is available with several engine and transmission options, including:

- 5.4L V8 (260 horsepower, 350 lb-ft of torque)
- 7.3L V8 (250 horsepower, 425 lb-ft of torque)
- 6.8L V10 (310 horsepower, 425 lb-ft of torque)
- 6.0L Power Stroke diesel V8 (235 horsepower, 500 lb-ft of torque)
- 4-speed automatic transmission (4R100)
- 5-speed manual transmission (MT82)
- 6-speed manual transmission (ZF S6-650)

Owners generally praise the F-250 Super Duty for its towing and payload capabilities, rugged design, and off-road performance. However, like any vehicle, it has had its share of issues.

**Engine**

**5.4L V8**

- **Intake manifold gasket failure:** The intake manifold gasket can fail, causing coolant and oil leaks. Symptoms include white smoke from the exhaust, overheating, and decreased engine performance. Solution: Replace the intake manifold gasket. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,200

- **Spark plug blowout:** The spark plugs can blow out of the cylinder head, causing severe engine damage. Symptoms include a loud bang, loss of power, and smoke from the engine bay. Solution: Replace the spark plugs and repair the cylinder head. Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $5,000+

**7.3L V8**

- **Injector failure:** The fuel injectors can fail, causing rough idling, hesitation, and loss of power. Solution: Replace the fuel injectors. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000

- **Turbocharger failure:** The turbocharger can fail, resulting in reduced engine power and increased exhaust smoke. Solution: Replace the turbocharger. Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,500

**6.8L V10**

- **Timing chain failure:** The timing chain can fail, causing catastrophic engine damage. Symptoms include a rattling noise from the engine, loss of power, and overheating. Solution: Replace the timing chain. Estimated Cost to Fix: $3,000 - $6,000+

**6.0L Power Stroke V8**

- **Head gasket failure:** The head gaskets can fail, leading to coolant leaks, overheating, and engine damage. Symptoms include white smoke from the exhaust, coolant loss, and decreased engine performance. Solution: Replace the head gaskets. Estimated Cost to Fix: $3,000 - $6,000+

- **Injector stiction:** The fuel injectors can become stuck, causing rough idling, hesitation, and decreased fuel economy. Solution: Clean the fuel injectors or replace them if necessary. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500

- **Turbocharger failure:** The turbocharger can fail, resulting in reduced engine power and increased exhaust smoke. Solution: Replace the turbocharger. Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,500

**Transmission**

**4-speed automatic (4R100)**

- **Slipping transmission:** The transmission may slip gears, causing hesitation and power loss. Symptoms include delayed acceleration, rough shifting, and increased transmission fluid temperature. Solution: Repair or replace the transmission. Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000

**5-speed manual (MT82)**

- **Grinding gears:** The transmission may grind when shifting into or out of gear. Symptoms include a grinding noise, rough shifting, and difficulty finding gears. Solution: Replace the synchronizers or gears. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000

**6-speed manual (ZF S6-650)**

- **No issues found**

**Suspension**

- **Front suspension clunking:** The front suspension may emit clunking noises when encountering bumps or uneven road surfaces. Symptoms include a clunking or popping noise from the front of the vehicle. Solution: Replace the sway bar bushings or ball joints. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500

- **Rear suspension leaf spring breakage:** The rear suspension leaf springs can break or crack, causing the vehicle to sag and handle poorly. Symptoms include a noticeable drop in the rear of the vehicle, decreased load capacity, and increased vibration. Solution: Replace the leaf springs. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000

**Electrical**

- **Alternator failure:** The alternator may fail, causing the battery to discharge and the vehicle to lose power. Symptoms include dim headlights, a battery warning light, and starting problems. Solution: Replace the alternator. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,200

- **Starter failure:** The starter may fail, preventing the vehicle from starting. Symptoms include no cranking when turning the key, a clicking noise, or a grinding noise. Solution: Replace the starter. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500

**Exterior**

- **Tailgate paint peeling:** The tailgate paint may peel or chip, revealing the bare metal underneath. Solution: Repair or repaint the tailgate. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500

- **Door lock failure:** The door locks may fail to operate, preventing the doors from locking or unlocking. Solution: Replace the door lock actuators. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400

**Interior**

- **HVAC blower motor failure:** The HVAC blower motor may fail, causing the heating and air conditioning system to stop working. Symptoms include no air flow from the vents, a burning smell, or an intermittent blower motor. Solution: Replace the blower motor. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500

- **Clock display failure:** The clock display may fail, causing the time and date to be incorrect or unreadable. Solution: Replace the clock display. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $250

**Summary**

Overall, the 2001 Ford F-250 Super Duty is a reliable and capable truck. However, it has experienced various issues, particularly with the engine and transmission. The severity and cost of these issues can vary depending on the specific problem and the affected components. Regular maintenance and proper care can help minimize the risk of encountering these problems.
