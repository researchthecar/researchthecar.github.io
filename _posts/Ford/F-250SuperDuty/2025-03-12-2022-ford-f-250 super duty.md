The 2022 Ford F-250 Super Duty is a heavy-duty pickup truck known for its towing and payload capabilities. Engine options include the 6.2L Boss SOHC 2-Valve Flex Fuel V8 (385 hp and 430 lb-ft of torque), the 7.3L Godzilla OHV 2-Valve PFI Gas V8 (430 hp and 475 lb-ft of torque), and the 6.7L Power Stroke V8 Turbo Diesel (475 hp and 1,050 lb-ft of torque). The 6.2L is paired with a 6-speed automatic, while the 7.3L and 6.7L are paired with a 10-speed automatic transmission. Owners often praise its robust performance, work-ready capabilities, and available technology. Common dislikes revolve around its size (making it difficult to maneuver in tight spaces), stiff ride (especially when unloaded), and high price tag.

**Engine**

**6.7L Power Stroke V8 Turbo Diesel**

*   **Turbocharger Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, excessive black smoke, unusual whistling or whining noises from the engine bay, potential check engine light. Can result from oil leaks, debris ingestion, or component failure within the turbocharger.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the turbocharger, inspecting and cleaning associated components (intercooler, intake piping), addressing any underlying oil leaks.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,500 - $4,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **High-Pressure Fuel Pump (HPFP) Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Hard starting, stalling, lack of power, fuel in the oil, and potential complete engine failure. Failure is often caused by contamination of the fuel system or internal wear and tear.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the HPFP, fuel filter replacement, fuel system cleaning, and potentially injector replacement, depending on the extent of the damage.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $5,000 - $10,000+ (can vary greatly depending on the extent of damage and parts replaced).
    *   Recalls: None Found

**7.3L Godzilla OHV 2-Valve PFI Gas V8**

*   **Valve Train Noise/Tick**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Ticking or tapping noise coming from the top of the engine, especially noticeable at idle and low speeds. May be caused by worn valve lifters, rocker arms, or other valve train components.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis to pinpoint the source of the noise. Replacement of the affected components (lifters, rockers, pushrods).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $4,000+ depending on the extent of the repair.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**6.2L Boss SOHC 2-Valve Flex Fuel V8**

*   No Issues Found

**Transmission**

**10-Speed Automatic (paired with 6.7L and 7.3L)**

*   **Harsh or Erratic Shifting**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Abrupt or jerky gear changes, especially between certain gears. Transmission may feel like it's "hunting" for the right gear.
    *   Solution: Software updates to the transmission control module (TCM), valve body replacement, or in severe cases, complete transmission replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+ for software update; $1,500 - $4,000+ for valve body; $5,000 - $8,000+ for transmission replacement.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Delayed Engagement**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Noticeable delay when shifting from park to drive or reverse. The vehicle may hesitate before moving.
    *   Solution: Transmission fluid level check and top-off, software updates, or potentially valve body or transmission replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $150 for fluid check/top-off; $100 - $300+ for software update; $1,500 - $4,000+ for valve body; $5,000 - $8,000+ for transmission replacement.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**6-Speed Automatic (paired with 6.2L)**

*   No Issues Found

**Suspension**

*   **Steering Wander/Looseness**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Vehicle tends to drift or wander on the road, requiring constant steering corrections to maintain a straight line. Loose or vague steering feel.
    *   Solution: Inspection and replacement of worn steering components (tie rod ends, ball joints, steering linkage), front end alignment.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,500+ depending on the components replaced.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Freezing/Malfunctioning**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The Sync infotainment system freezes, becomes unresponsive, or exhibits glitches. Common issues include delayed response to touch inputs, screen flickering, and connectivity problems (Bluetooth, Apple CarPlay/Android Auto).
    *   Solution: Software updates to the Sync system, hard reset of the system, or replacement of the APIM (Accessory Protocol Interface Module).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 (software update if available); $500 - $1,500+ for APIM replacement.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Camera System Malfunctions**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The camera system, including backup camera, surround view camera, or trailer reverse guidance camera, may intermittently fail, display distorted images, or show a black screen.
    *   Solution: Check camera connections, software updates, or camera replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+ for software update; $200 - $800+ for camera replacement.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Tailgate Latch Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Tailgate may be difficult to open or close, or the latch mechanism may fail entirely. Sometimes the power release stops working.
    *   Solution: Latch mechanism adjustment, lubrication, or replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $400+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Seat Heater Malfunction**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Seat heaters fail to heat, or only heat intermittently. May affect one or both front seats.
    *   Solution: Replacement of heating element or module.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2022 Ford F-250 Super Duty, while a capable truck, exhibits common issues. The 6.7L diesel can suffer from turbocharger and HPFP failures, leading to significant repair costs. The 7.3L gas engine may exhibit valve train noise. Transmission issues, particularly with the 10-speed automatic, can include harsh shifting and delayed engagement. Electrical glitches with the infotainment and camera systems are also prevalent. While some issues can be addressed with software updates, others, such as turbocharger or HPFP failures, can result in hefty repair bills. Overall, potential buyers should be aware of these issues and factor them into their purchasing decision, especially when considering a used model with higher mileage.
