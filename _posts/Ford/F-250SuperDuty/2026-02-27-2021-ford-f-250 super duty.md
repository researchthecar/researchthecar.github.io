# 2021 Ford F-250 Super Duty

### Overview
The 2021 Ford F-250 Super Duty is a heavy-duty pickup truck engineered for demanding work and towing applications. It offers robust powertrains and a durable chassis designed to withstand significant stress. Reliability for the 2021 model year is generally strong, with owners reporting satisfaction with its core functionality. However, as with any complex vehicle, specific components and systems can experience issues. This analysis focuses on documented technical reliability concerns beyond routine maintenance, covering its various engine and transmission configurations, as well as other key vehicle systems.

### 6.7L Power Stroke V8 Diesel
**Diesel Particulate Filter (DPF) Regeneration Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Check Engine light illumination, reduced engine power, incomplete DPF regeneration cycles.
- **Solution:** Forced regeneration, DPF cleaning, or replacement. Software updates may also address some issues.
- **Cost:** $300 - $1500

**Fuel Injector Problems**
- **Symptoms:** Rough idling, misfires, decreased fuel economy, difficulty starting.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and replacement of faulty fuel injectors.
- **Cost:** $600 - $1200 per injector

## Electrical

### General Electrical System
**Infotainment System Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Screen freezing, unresponsiveness, Bluetooth connectivity issues, occasional reboots.
- **Solution:** Software updates, hard reset of the system, or potential module replacement.
- **Cost:** $100 - $700

**Trailer Towing Electrical Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Malfunctioning trailer lights, trailer brake controller errors, intermittent power loss to trailer connections.
- **Solution:** Inspection and repair of wiring harnesses, connectors, and control modules.
- **Cost:** $150 - $500

### Summary Verdict
The 2021 Ford F-250 Super Duty demonstrates solid reliability, particularly with its gasoline V8 engines. The 6.7L Power Stroke diesel, while powerful, shows some potential for DPF and fuel injector issues, which are not uncommon for modern diesel powertrains and can incur moderate repair costs. The 10-speed automatic transmission may occasionally exhibit minor drivability quirks requiring software attention. Electrical systems, especially the infotainment and trailer-related components, can sometimes present glitches. Overall, the F-250 is a robust truck, and most reported issues are manageable and not indicative of systemic failure across the model year.
