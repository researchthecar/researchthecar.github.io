**Introduction:**

The 2003 Ford F-250 Super Duty is a heavy-duty pickup truck known for its powerful engines, rugged capabilities, and spacious interior. Engine options include a 5.4L V8, 6.0L V8, and 7.3L V8, paired with either a 4-speed automatic or 5-speed manual transmission. The 5.4L V8 produces 300 horsepower and 365 lb-ft of torque, while the 6.0L V8 generates 325 horsepower and 560 lb-ft of torque. The 7.3L V8 delivers impressive power with 235 horsepower and 505 lb-ft of torque. Owners appreciate the F-250's towing and payload capacities, off-road capabilities, and comfortable cabin. However, there have been some issues reported with certain components.

**Engine**

**5.4L V8**

* **Intake Manifold Problems:** Cracking intake manifolds can cause coolant leaks, vacuum leaks, and performance issues. Replacing the manifold typically costs $1,000 - $1,500+.
* **Spark Plug Blowouts:** Blown spark plugs can cause misfires and decrease engine performance. Repair usually involves replacing the spark plugs and spark plug threads, costing around $500 - $750+.
* **Timing Chain Tensioner Failure:** Failure of the timing chain tensioner can result in chain skipping or breakage, leading to significant engine damage. Replacement typically costs $1,500 - $2,500+.

**6.0L V8**

* **EGR Cooler Failure:** The exhaust gas recirculation (EGR) cooler can fail, causing coolant leaks and performance issues. Replacing the EGR cooler costs $1,500 - $2,500+.
* **Injector Sticking:** Sticking injectors can affect engine performance and fuel economy. Replacing the injectors typically costs $1,000 - $2,000+.
* **Turbocharger Failure:** Turbocharger failure can lead to reduced boost pressure and engine power loss. Replacing the turbocharger typically costs $2,000 - $3,000+.

**7.3L V8**

* **Injector Sticking:** Sticking injectors can affect engine performance and fuel economy. Replacing the injectors typically costs $1,000 - $2,000+.

**Transmission**

**4-Speed Automatic**

* **Slipping Gears:** Gears slipping out of engagement can cause loss of power and drivability issues. Repairing or replacing the transmission typically costs $2,000 - $4,000+.

**5-Speed Manual**

* **Grinding Gears:** Grinding occurs when shifting gears, indicating worn or damaged components. Repairing the transmission can cost around $1,500 - $2,500+.

**Suspension**

* **Ball Joint Failure:** Worn or damaged ball joints can cause clunking or squealing noises and affect steering and handling. Replacing ball joints typically costs $500 - $1,000+.
* **Leaf Spring Sagging:** Sagging leaf springs can affect ride height and cause contact with the bump stops. Replacing leaf springs costs around $1,000 - $2,000+.

**Electrical**

* **Alternator Failure:** A failing alternator can cause electrical system issues and battery charging problems. Replacing the alternator typically costs $500 - $800+.

**Exterior**

* **Rusty Tailgate:** The tailgate can rust due to exposure to moisture and road salt. Repairing or replacing the tailgate typically costs $500 - $1,200+.

**Interior**

* **HVAC Blower Motor Failure:** The HVAC blower motor can fail, affecting climate control functionality. Replacing the blower motor typically costs $300 - $500+.
* **Cruise Control Switch Failure:** The cruise control switch can malfunction, preventing cruise control use. Replacing the switch typically costs $250 - $400+.

**Summary:**

The 2003 Ford F-250 Super Duty has some notable issues, particularly with the 6.0L V8 engine and 4-speed automatic transmission. The intake manifold, EGR cooler, injector, and turbocharger issues can be costly to repair, while transmission problems can also incur significant expenses. Electrical and interior issues are relatively minor in comparison, but still represent potential inconvenience and repair costs. Buyers should carefully consider these issues when considering this vehicle and budget accordingly for potential repairs.
