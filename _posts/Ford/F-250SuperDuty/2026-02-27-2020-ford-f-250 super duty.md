# 2020 Ford F-250 Super Duty

### Overview
The 2020 Ford F-250 Super Duty is a heavy-duty pickup truck designed for demanding work and towing applications. It offers robust performance and a range of powerful engine and transmission options. Reliability for this model year is generally strong, with most reported issues being isolated incidents rather than widespread manufacturing defects. Owners typically praise its capability and durability, though like any complex vehicle, certain components may experience issues over time. This analysis focuses on documented technical reliability concerns beyond standard maintenance.

### 6.7L Power Stroke V8 Diesel
**Fuel Injector Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Rough idling, loss of power, increased exhaust smoke, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of faulty fuel injectors.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1500

**EGR Cooler Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Coolant loss, white smoke from exhaust, overheating.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the EGR cooler.
- **Cost:** $700 - $1200

### Summary Verdict
The 2020 Ford F-250 Super Duty demonstrates solid reliability, particularly for its intended heavy-duty use. The gasoline 6.2L V8 engine and the 6-speed automatic transmission are exceptionally robust with no significant widespread issues reported. The diesel 6.7L Power Stroke engine, while powerful, has a few documented concerns such as fuel injector problems and EGR cooler leaks, which are repairable. The 10-speed automatic transmission can occasionally exhibit harsh shifting, often resolvable with software updates. Electrical issues are minor, primarily related to infotainment glitches. Overall, the F-250 is a dependable choice for those needing substantial towing and hauling capabilities, with most potential issues being manageable.
