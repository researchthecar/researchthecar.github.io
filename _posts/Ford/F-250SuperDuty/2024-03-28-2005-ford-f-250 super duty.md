## 2005 Ford F-250 Super Duty Detailed Issues

The 2005 Ford F-250 Super Duty was a full-size pickup truck that was available with several different engine and transmission options. It was powered by a standard 5.4L V8 engine that produced 300 horsepower and 455 lb-ft of torque, an available 6.0L Power Stroke diesel engine that produced 325 horsepower and 570 lb-ft of torque, and an available 6.8L V10 engine that produced 362 horsepower and 457 lb-ft of torque. The F-250 Super Duty was offered with a 6-speed manual transmission or a 5-speed automatic transmission.

People like the 2005 Ford F-250 Super Duty for its powerful engines, rugged construction, and spacious interior. However, some owners have reported problems with the vehicle's engine, transmission, suspension, electrical system, exterior, and interior.

**Engine**

**5.4L V8**

* **Intake manifold gasket failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The intake manifold gasket can fail, causing a loss of coolant and engine overheating. The engine may also run rough or stall.
    * Solution: The intake manifold gasket must be replaced.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Spark plug blowout**
    * Description/Symptoms: The spark plugs can blow out of the cylinder head, causing a loss of compression and engine damage. The engine may also run rough or stall.
    * Solution: The spark plugs must be replaced and the cylinder head may need to be repaired.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**6.0L Power Stroke Diesel**

* **Turbocharger failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The turbocharger can fail, causing a loss of power and reduced fuel efficiency. The engine may also smoke or make a whistling noise.
    * Solution: The turbocharger must be replaced.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,000+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Injector failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The fuel injectors can fail, causing a loss of power, rough idling, and increased emissions. The engine may also smoke or stall.
    * Solution: The fuel injectors must be replaced.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**6.8L V10**

* **Intake valve failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The intake valves can fail, causing a loss of power and reduced fuel efficiency. The engine may also run rough or stall.
    * Solution: The intake valves must be replaced.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**6-Speed Manual**

* **Clutch failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The clutch can fail, causing difficulty shifting gears or a loss of power. The transmission may also make a grinding noise.
    * Solution: The clutch must be replaced.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**5-Speed Automatic**

* **Transmission fluid leak**
    * Description/Symptoms: The transmission fluid can leak from the transmission, causing a loss of fluid and transmission damage. The transmission may also slip or shift erratically.
    * Solution: The transmission fluid leak must be repaired.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Torque converter failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The torque converter can fail, causing a loss of power and transmission damage. The transmission may also slip or shift erratically.
    * Solution: The torque converter must be replaced.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Front suspension ball joint failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The front suspension ball joints can fail, causing a loss of control and tire wear. The vehicle may also make a clunking noise when turning.
    * Solution: The front suspension ball joints must be replaced.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Alternator failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The alternator can fail, causing a loss of power and battery drain. The vehicle may also have difficulty starting.
    * Solution: The alternator must be replaced.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Wiring harness damage**
    * Description/Symptoms: The wiring harness can become damaged, causing electrical problems such as loss of power, flickering lights, or inoperative accessories.
    * Solution: The wiring harness must be repaired or replaced.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Tailgate rust**
    * Description/Symptoms: The tailgate can rust, causing the paint to peel and the tailgate to become difficult to open or close.
    * Solution: The tailgate can be replaced or repaired.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Dashboard cracking**
    * Description/Symptoms: The dashboard can crack, causing the vehicle to look unsightly.
    * Solution: The dashboard can be replaced or repaired.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

## Summary

Overall, the 2005 Ford F-250 Super Duty is a reliable and capable vehicle. However, it has some potential issues that owners should be aware of. The most serious issues include engine failure, transmission failure, and suspension failure. These issues can be expensive to fix, but they are relatively rare. Most owners are satisfied with the F-250 Super Duty and find it to be a good value for the money.
