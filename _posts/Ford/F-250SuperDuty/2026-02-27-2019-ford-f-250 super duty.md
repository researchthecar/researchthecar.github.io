# 2019 Ford F-250 Super Duty

### Overview
The 2019 Ford F-250 Super Duty is a heavy-duty pickup truck engineered for demanding work and towing applications. It offers robust performance with a choice of powerful engines and durable transmissions. Reliability for this model year is generally strong, reflecting Ford's commitment to building work-ready vehicles. While specific issues are infrequent, understanding potential weak points can help owners maintain their trucks effectively. This analysis focuses on documented technical reliability concerns beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into common problems, their symptoms, solutions, and estimated repair costs for various components and powertrain options.

### 6.7L Power Stroke V8 Diesel
**EGR Cooler Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Coolant loss, white smoke from exhaust, overheating.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the EGR cooler assembly.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1500

**Turbocharger Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of power, excessive smoke, whistling noises.
- **Solution:** Turbocharger inspection, cleaning, or replacement.
- **Cost:** $1200 - $2500

### Summary Verdict
The 2019 Ford F-250 Super Duty demonstrates commendable reliability, particularly for its class. The gasoline 6.2L V8 engine and its associated 6R100 transmission are exceptionally robust with no significant widespread issues reported. The more powerful 6.7L Power Stroke diesel engine, while generally reliable, has a few documented concerns, primarily related to the EGR cooler and turbocharger, which can incur moderate to significant repair costs. The 6R140 transmission paired with the diesel can occasionally exhibit torque converter shudder. Other systems like suspension, electrical, exterior, and interior components are largely free from widespread defects. Overall, the F-250 is a dependable choice for heavy-duty needs, with potential issues being manageable and primarily concentrated in the diesel powertrain.
