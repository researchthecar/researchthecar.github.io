# 2018 Ford F-250 Super Duty

### Overview
The 2018 Ford F-250 Super Duty is a heavy-duty pickup truck engineered for demanding work and towing applications. It is known for its robust construction and powerful engine options. Reliability data for this model year generally indicates a strong performance, particularly concerning its core powertrain components. While specific issues are infrequent, some owners have reported isolated incidents related to certain systems. This analysis focuses on documented, non-wear-and-tear related problems across key vehicle categories, providing insights into potential repair needs and associated costs for owners.

### 6.7L Power Stroke V8 Diesel
**Fuel Injector Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Rough idle, loss of power, increased exhaust smoke, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of faulty fuel injectors.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1500 per injector

**EGR Cooler Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Coolant loss, white smoke from exhaust, overheating.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the Exhaust Gas Recirculation (EGR) cooler.
- **Cost:** $700 - $1200

### Summary Verdict
The 2018 Ford F-250 Super Duty demonstrates solid reliability, especially for its intended heavy-duty purpose. The gasoline engine option is particularly robust with no significant reported issues. The diesel engine, while powerful, has a few potential concerns like fuel injector problems and EGR cooler leaks, which can be costly to repair but are not universally experienced. The automatic transmissions are generally dependable, though the diesel variant's transmission can occasionally exhibit torque converter shudder. Electrical system issues are typically isolated sensor failures. Overall, the F-250 is a reliable workhorse, with most potential problems being manageable and not indicative of systemic design flaws.
