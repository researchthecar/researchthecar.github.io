**Introduction:**
The 2003 Ford E-150 Club Wagon is a full-size van available with a choice of three engines: a 4.6L V8, a 5.4L V8, and a 6.8L V10. It also offers multiple transmission options: a 4-speed automatic, a 5-speed automatic, and a 6-speed manual. The 4.6L V8 generates 225 horsepower and 290 lb-ft of torque, the 5.4L V8 produces 260 horsepower and 355 lb-ft of torque, and the 6.8L V10 delivers 310 horsepower and 425 lb-ft of torque. Owners appreciate the van's spacious interior, powerful engines, and reliable performance. However, they often encounter various issues with different components over time.

**Engine**
* **4.6L V8**
    * **Spark Plug Failure:** The spark plugs can foul or wear prematurely, causing misfires and engine performance issues.
    * Solution: Replace the spark plugs.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200
* **5.4L V8**
    * **Cam Phaser Failure:** The cam phasers, which adjust the timing of the camshafts, can fail, leading to reduced engine performance and increased fuel consumption.
    * Solution: Replace the cam phasers.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500
* **6.8L V10**
    * **Intake Manifold Gasket Leak:** The intake manifold gasket can develop leaks, causing a loss of engine vacuum and reduced performance.
    * Solution: Replace the intake manifold gasket.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000

**Transmission**
* **4-Speed Automatic:**
    * **Transmission Slipping:** The transmission may start to slip, resulting in delayed shifts or a loss of power.
    * Solution: Overhaul or replace the transmission.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,000
* **5-Speed Automatic:**
    * **Governor Pressure Sensor Failure:** The governor pressure sensor can malfunction, affecting transmission shift points and causing drivability problems.
    * Solution: Replace the governor pressure sensor.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
* **6-Speed Manual:**
    * No Issues Found

**Suspension**
* **Front Lower Control Arm Bushing Wear:** The rubber bushings in the front lower control arms can wear out prematurely, leading to clunking noises and reduced handling.
* Solution: Replace the front lower control arm bushings.
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
* **Rear Leaf Spring Failure:** The rear leaf springs can break or sag, causing a decrease in ride height and handling issues.
* Solution: Replace the rear leaf springs.
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000

**Electrical**
* **Alternator Failure:** The alternator can fail, resulting in a loss of electrical power and a dead battery.
* Solution: Replace the alternator.
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
* **Battery Drain:** The battery can drain prematurely due to parasitic electrical draws from faulty components or accessories.
* Solution: Identify and fix the source of the parasitic draw.
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300

**Exterior**
* **Rust on Underbody and Frame:** The underbody and frame components can develop rust due to exposure to road salt and moisture.
* Solution: Rust treatment or frame replacement.
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000+ (Depending on the severity of rust)

**Interior**
* **HVAC Blower Motor Failure:** The HVAC blower motor can fail, resulting in a lack of airflow from the heating and air conditioning system.
* Solution: Replace the HVAC blower motor.
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
* **Dashboard Rattle:** The dashboard may rattle or make noises due to loose components or poorly fitted trim panels.
* Solution: Identify and secure the source of the rattle.
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200

**Summary:**
The 2003 Ford E-150 Club Wagon is a capable and versatile van, but it is not without its issues. Engine problems can be costly to fix, particularly the cam phaser failure on the 5.4L V8. Transmission issues, suspension wear, and electrical problems are also common. Exterior rust and interior rattles can affect the overall appearance and comfort of the van. While these issues vary in severity and cost to repair, it is important to be aware of them when considering purchasing or owning this vehicle. Regular maintenance and timely repairs can help extend its lifespan and reduce the likelihood of major problems.
