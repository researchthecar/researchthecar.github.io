The 2022 Ford E-350 Super Duty is a full-size van primarily used for commercial purposes, offering a robust platform for various upfits. While the E-350 Super Duty only has one Engine, the 7.3L Godzilla V8 producing 350 horsepower and 468 lb-ft of torque. A key selling point is its strong payload and towing capacity, making it a workhorse for businesses. However, some owners report a less refined ride compared to more modern vans and fuel economy is typical of a large displacement gas engine.

**Engine**

**7.3L "Godzilla" V8**

*   **Engine Oil Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks originating from the valve covers or oil pan gasket. Symptoms include visible oil drips under the vehicle and a burning oil smell.
    *   Solution: Replace the valve cover gaskets and/or oil pan gasket. Ensure proper torque of bolts during reinstallation.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+ depending on the location of the leak and labor rates.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**TorqShift 10-Speed Automatic**

*   **Harsh or Erratic Shifting**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The transmission exhibits harsh upshifts or downshifts, or may shift erratically. This can occur at various speeds and under different load conditions.
    *   Solution: A software update or reprogramming of the transmission control module (TCM) may correct the issue. In some cases, a valve body replacement may be required.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $150 for a software update, $800 - $2000+ for valve body replacement.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Freezing/Crashing**
    * Description/Symptoms: The infotainment system (if equipped) may freeze, crash, or become unresponsive. This can affect navigation, radio, and other vehicle functions.
    * Solution: Attempt a system reset. If the issue persists, a software update from the dealer might be necessary. In some cases, the APIM (Accessory Protocol Interface Module) may need replacement.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 for reset, $50 - $150 for software update, $500 - $1200+ for APIM replacement.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Battery Drain Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Premature battery failure or excessive battery drain, especially if the vehicle sits unused for extended periods.
    *   Solution: Investigate parasitic drain sources, such as aftermarket accessories or faulty modules. Have the battery and charging system tested.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300 for testing and diagnosis, $150 - $400+ for battery replacement.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Sliding Door Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: Difficulty opening or closing the sliding door. This could be caused by a misaligned door, a faulty latch, or a problem with the power-assist mechanism (if equipped).
    * Solution: Adjust the door alignment, lubricate the latch mechanism, or replace faulty components like the latch or power-assist motor.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $200+ for adjustment/lubrication, $100 - $500+ for parts replacement.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **HVAC Malfunctions**
    * Description/Symptoms: The heating or air conditioning system fails to function properly. This can include blowing only hot or cold air, weak airflow, or unusual noises.
    * Solution: Diagnose the system for issues like a faulty blower motor, compressor, blend door actuator, or refrigerant leaks. Replace the defective component.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $1000+ depending on the component needing replacement.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

No Issues Found

**Summary**

The 2022 Ford E-350 Super Duty, while a capable work van, has potential issues to watch out for. Engine oil leaks and transmission shifting problems seem to be the most significant mechanical concerns. Electrical issues, such as the infotainment system malfunctioning and battery drain problems also exist. Exterior and Interior issues mainly revolve around door mechanisms and HVAC. Overall, the severity of the issues varies from minor inconveniences to potentially costly repairs. Pre-purchase inspections by a qualified mechanic are advisable to mitigate potential problems.
