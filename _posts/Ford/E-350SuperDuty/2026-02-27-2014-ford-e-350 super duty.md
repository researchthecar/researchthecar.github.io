# 2014 Ford E-350 Super Duty

### Overview
The 2014 Ford E-350 Super Duty is a robust full-size van known for its workhorse capabilities. As a commercial vehicle, its design prioritizes durability and utility. Reliability data for this model year generally indicates a solid performance, particularly in its core mechanical components. However, like any vehicle, specific issues can arise, often related to the powertrain or electrical systems, especially with higher mileage or demanding usage. This analysis focuses on documented common problems beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential repair areas and their associated costs for owners and fleet managers.

---


## Engine

### 5.4L Triton V8
**Spark Plug Blowout**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of engine power, rough idling, ticking noise from the engine.
- **Solution:** Requires specialized tools to extract broken spark plug and re-thread or use a repair kit.
- **Cost:** $300 - $700

**Camshaft Position Sensor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Check Engine Light illumination, rough idle, stalling, poor fuel economy.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the camshaft position sensor.
- **Cost:** $150 - $300


### 6.8L Triton V10
**Spark Plug Blowout**

- **Cost:** $300 - $700

**Intake Manifold Gasket Leak**
- **Symptoms:** Coolant leaks, overheating, rough idle, Check Engine Light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the intake manifold gaskets.

## Transmission

### 4R75E (4-Speed Automatic)
**Torque Converter Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Slipping gears, shuddering, delayed engagement, fluid leaks.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the torque converter and potentially transmission fluid flush.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1500

**Transmission Fluid Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Leaking fluid under the vehicle, shifting problems, overheating.
- **Solution:** Inspection and repair of seals, gaskets, or cooler lines.
- **Cost:** $200 - $600


### 5R110W (5-Speed Automatic)
**Solenoid Pack Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Harsh shifting, inability to shift into certain gears, Check Engine Light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the transmission solenoid pack.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1000

**Overheating**
- **Symptoms:** Transmission slipping, burning smell, delayed shifts, especially under load.
- **Solution:** Check and repair transmission cooler, flush fluid, or rebuild transmission if severe.
- **Cost:** $300 - $1200


## Suspension

### Standard Chassis
**Leaf Spring Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Sagging rear end, clunking noises over bumps, reduced load capacity.
- **Solution:** Replacement of broken or worn leaf springs.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1000

**Ball Joint Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Clunking or knocking sounds, uneven tire wear, steering wander.
- **Solution:** Replacement of worn ball joints.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600


## Electrical

### Standard Electrical System
**Alternator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Dimming headlights, battery warning light, starting problems, dead battery.

- **Cost:** $400 - $700

**Window Regulator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Power windows not operating, grinding noise when operating.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the window regulator mechanism.

### Summary Verdict
The 2014 Ford E-350 Super Duty demonstrates good overall reliability, especially concerning its core powertrain components when properly maintained. The most frequently reported issues tend to be related to the spark plug blowout on V8 engines, common in Ford modular engines of this era, and potential torque converter or solenoid issues in the automatic transmissions, particularly with heavy use. Electrical components like the alternator and window regulators can also require attention. Suspension components, especially leaf springs, may show wear due to the vehicle's intended heavy-duty use. Proactive maintenance and addressing early signs of trouble can significantly mitigate repair costs and ensure the longevity of this capable work vehicle.
