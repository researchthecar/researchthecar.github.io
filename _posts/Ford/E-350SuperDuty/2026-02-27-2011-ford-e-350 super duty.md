# 2011 Ford E-350 Super Duty

### Overview
The 2011 Ford E-350 Super Duty is a robust full-size van designed for heavy-duty work and commercial applications. Its reputation for durability is generally strong, particularly in its powertrain and chassis components. However, like any vehicle, it has specific areas that have seen more attention from owners regarding reliability. This analysis focuses on documented issues beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential concerns for owners and fleet managers. The E-350's utilitarian nature means it's often subjected to demanding conditions, which can highlight certain vulnerabilities over time. Understanding these specific points can aid in proactive maintenance and informed purchasing decisions.

---


## Engine

### 5.4L Triton V8
**Spark Plug Blowout**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of engine power, rough idling, misfires, audible popping sound from engine.
- **Solution:** Requires specialized tools for extraction and replacement with updated spark plugs and potentially thread inserts.
- **Cost:** $400 - $800

**Camshaft Position Sensor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Check Engine Light illumination, rough idle, stalling, poor fuel economy.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the faulty camshaft position sensor.
- **Cost:** $150 - $300


### 6.8L Triton V10
**Spark Plug Blowout**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of engine power, rough idling, misfires, audible popping sound from engine.
- **Solution:** Requires specialized tools for extraction and replacement with updated spark plugs and potentially thread inserts.
- **Cost:** $400 - $800

**Intake Manifold Gasket Leak**
- **Symptoms:** Vacuum leaks, rough idle, Check Engine Light, coolant loss.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the intake manifold gaskets.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600


### 4.6L Triton V8
**Camshaft Position Sensor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Check Engine Light illumination, rough idle, stalling, poor fuel economy.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the faulty camshaft position sensor.
- **Cost:** $150 - $300


## Transmission

### 4R75E 4-Speed Automatic
**Torque Converter Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Slipping gears, shuddering, delayed engagement, fluid leaks.
- **Solution:** Rebuild or replacement of the torque converter and potentially transmission fluid flush.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1500

**Transmission Fluid Cooler Line Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Fluid leaks under the vehicle, low transmission fluid level, overheating transmission.
- **Solution:** Replacement of leaking transmission cooler lines.
- **Cost:** $200 - $400


### 5R110W 5-Speed Automatic
**Solenoid Pack Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Harsh shifting, delayed gear engagement, transmission warning light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the transmission solenoid pack.
- **Cost:** $400 - $700


## Suspension

### Standard Chassis
**Leaf Spring Sagging/Breakage**
- **Symptoms:** Rear end sag, uneven ride height, clunking noises over bumps, reduced load capacity.
- **Solution:** Replacement of damaged or worn leaf springs.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1000

**Ball Joint Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Clunking or knocking noises when turning or going over bumps, uneven tire wear, steering wander.
- **Solution:** Replacement of worn ball joints.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600


## Electrical

### Alternator
**Alternator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Dimming headlights, battery warning light, electrical system malfunctions, dead battery.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the alternator.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600


### Body Control Module (BCM)
**BCM Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** Intermittent electrical issues, power window problems, interior light issues, dashboard warning lights.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and potential reprogramming or replacement of the BCM.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1000


## Exterior

### Body Panels
**Rust/Corrosion**
- **Symptoms:** Visible rust on body panels, wheel wells, and frame, especially in areas with harsh winters or coastal environments.
- **Solution:** Rust treatment, panel repair, or replacement.
- **Cost:** $200 - $1500+


### Door Latches/Mechanisms
**Sticking or Failing Door Latches**
- **Symptoms:** Doors difficult to open or close, doors not latching securely, interior door lock switch issues.
- **Solution:** Lubrication, adjustment, or replacement of door latch mechanisms.
- **Cost:** $150 - $300


## Interior

### HVAC System
**Blend Door Actuator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Inability to control temperature (stuck on hot or cold), air only coming from certain vents.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the faulty blend door actuator.
- **Cost:** $200 - $400


### Dashboard/Trim
**Cracking Dashboard**
- **Symptoms:** Cracks appearing on the dashboard surface, particularly in hot climates.
- **Solution:** Dashboard replacement or repair (often cosmetic).
- **Cost:** $300 - $800

### Summary Verdict
The 2011 Ford E-350 Super Duty is a workhorse vehicle that generally offers good reliability for its class. The most commonly reported issues revolve around the Triton V8 engines, specifically spark plug blowouts and camshaft position sensor failures, which are known issues for this engine family. Transmission issues, particularly with the torque converter and cooler lines, can also arise. Suspension components like leaf springs and ball joints may require attention due to the heavy-duty nature of the van. Electrical gremlins, such as alternator failure and BCM issues, are not uncommon. Exterior rust can be a concern in certain climates, and interior components like HVAC blend doors and dashboards may also present problems. Despite these potential issues, the E-350 remains a capable and durable option when properly maintained.
