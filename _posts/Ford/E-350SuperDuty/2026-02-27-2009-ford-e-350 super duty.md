# 2009 Ford E-350 Super Duty

### Overview
The 2009 Ford E-350 Super Duty is a robust full-size van known for its workhorse capabilities. As a commercial vehicle, its design prioritizes durability and utility. While generally reliable, like any vehicle of this age and type, it can experience specific issues related to its powertrain, suspension, and electrical systems, particularly under heavy use. This analysis focuses on documented common problems beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential repair areas and associated costs for owners and fleet managers.

---


## Engine

### 5.4L Triton V8
**Spark Plug Blowout**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of engine power, rough idling, misfires, audible popping sound from engine.
- **Solution:** Requires specialized tools to extract broken spark plug fragments and re-tap the cylinder head. Often involves replacing spark plugs and potentially ignition coils.
- **Cost:** $400 - $900

**Camshaft Synchronizer Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Rattling or ticking noise from the front of the engine, especially at idle or low RPMs.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the camshaft synchronizer and potentially the camshaft phaser.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1000

## Transmission

### 4R75E 4-Speed Automatic
**Torque Converter Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Slipping gears, shuddering during acceleration, delayed engagement, fluid leaks.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the torque converter and potentially transmission fluid flush.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1500

**Overheating**
- **Symptoms:** Transmission slipping, harsh shifting, burning smell.
- **Solution:** Installation of an auxiliary transmission cooler, fluid flush, and inspection for internal damage.
- **Cost:** $300 - $700


### 5R110W 5-Speed Automatic
**Torque Converter Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Slipping gears, shuddering during acceleration, delayed engagement, fluid leaks.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the torque converter and potentially transmission fluid flush.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1500

**Solenoid Pack Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Harsh shifting, transmission not shifting into certain gears, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the transmission solenoid pack.
- **Cost:** $400 - $800


## Suspension

### Standard Chassis
**Leaf Spring Sagging/Breakage**
- **Symptoms:** Rear end of the vehicle appears lower than normal, clunking noises over bumps, reduced load capacity.
- **Solution:** Replacement of damaged or worn leaf springs.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1200

**Ball Joint Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Clunking or knocking noises from the front suspension, uneven tire wear, steering wander.
- **Solution:** Replacement of worn ball joints.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600


## Electrical

### General Electrical System
**Alternator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Dimming headlights, battery warning light illuminated, electrical components malfunctioning, difficulty starting.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the alternator.
- **Cost:** $400 - $700

**Wiring Harness Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Intermittent electrical problems, flickering lights, non-functional accessories, corrosion on connectors.
- **Solution:** Inspection, repair, or replacement of damaged wiring harnesses and connectors.
- **Cost:** $200 - $800


## Exterior

### Body and Trim
**Rust on Body Panels**
- **Symptoms:** Visible corrosion, bubbling paint, holes in body panels, especially around wheel wells and lower door edges.
- **Solution:** Rust repair, panel replacement, and repainting.
- **Cost:** $300 - $1500+

**Door Latch/Lock Actuator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Doors not locking or unlocking properly, difficulty opening or closing doors.
- **Solution:** Replacement of faulty door latches or lock actuators.
- **Cost:** $200 - $500


## Interior

### Cabin Components
**HVAC Blower Motor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** No air coming from vents or weak airflow, regardless of fan speed setting.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the HVAC blower motor and resistor.
- **Cost:** $200 - $500

**Dashboard Cracking**
- **Symptoms:** Visible cracks on the dashboard surface, especially in hot climates.
- **Solution:** Dashboard replacement or repair (often cosmetic).
- **Cost:** $500 - $1500

### Summary Verdict
The 2009 Ford E-350 Super Duty is a capable commercial vehicle with a generally solid reputation for durability. However, owners should be aware of potential issues, particularly with the Triton V8 and V10 engines, such as spark plug blowouts and camshaft synchronizer problems. Transmission issues, especially with the torque converter and overheating, can also arise. Suspension components like leaf springs and ball joints may require attention due to the vehicle's heavy-duty nature. Electrical system components like the alternator and wiring harnesses are also points to monitor. While interior and exterior components can experience wear and tear, specific issues like rust and HVAC blower motor failure are common for vehicles of this age. Regular maintenance and prompt attention to these potential problems are key to ensuring the longevity and reliability of the E-350 Super Duty.
