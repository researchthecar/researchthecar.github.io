**Introduction:**

The 2005 Ford E-350 Super Duty is a full-size van offered with several engine and transmission options. It's popular for its spacious interior, ample towing and payload capacities, and versatility for various commercial and personal uses. However, like any vehicle, it has its fair share of issues.

Engine:

**5.4L V8**
* **Intake Manifold Gasket Failure:** Warped or cracked intake manifold gaskets cause coolant and oil leaks, leading to overheating and engine damage. Solution: Replace intake manifold gaskets. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800+

**6.0L V8 Powerstroke Diesel**
* **Head Bolt Failure:** Weak head bolts can fail, resulting in coolant leaks, overheating, and engine damage. Solution: Replace head bolts. Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,000+
* **Fuel Injector Failure:** Clogged or faulty fuel injectors can cause poor engine performance, reduced fuel economy, and increased emissions. Solution: Replace fuel injectors. Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $600+
* **EGR Cooler Failure:** The exhaust gas recirculation (EGR) cooler can become clogged or leak, leading to reduced engine power, increased emissions, and overheating. Solution: Replace EGR cooler. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $1,500+

Transmission:

**4R100 Automatic**
* **Slipping or Hard Shifting:** Worn clutches or bands can cause the transmission to slip or shift harshly. Solution: Rebuild or replace transmission. Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,000+

**5R110W Automatic**
* **Overheating:** Heavy towing or extended high-speed driving can cause the transmission to overheat, resulting in burnt clutches and other internal damage. Solution: Replace transmission cooler or upgrade to a heavy-duty model. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+

Suspension:

* **Rear Axle Seal Failure:** Leaking rear axle seals can contaminate brake pads with grease, reducing braking effectiveness. Solution: Replace axle seals. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+
* **Leaf Spring Breakage:** Overloaded or fatigued leaf springs can break, affecting ride quality and handling. Solution: Replace leaf springs. Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800+

Electrical:

* **Battery Drain:** Faulty alternator, parasitic draw, or damaged wiring can cause the battery to drain prematurely. Solution: Diagnose and repair electrical system issues. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
* **Headlight Switch Failure:** Malfunctioning headlight switch can cause intermittent or complete loss of headlights. Solution: Replace headlight switch. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+

Exterior:

* **Rust on Underbody and Frame:** Exposed metal components can rust over time, especially in humid or salty environments. Solution: Inspect and treat rust, or replace affected parts. Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on severity and parts needed

Interior:

* **HVAC Control Module Failure:** Faulty HVAC control module can cause issues with heating, ventilation, and air conditioning. Solution: Replace HVAC control module. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
* **Dashboard Rattle:** Poorly fitted or loose dashboard components can create rattling noises while driving. Solution: Tighten or replace loose parts. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+

**Summary:**

The 2005 Ford E-350 Super Duty is a capable and versatile vehicle but faces several common issues. Engine concerns, particularly with the 6.0L Powerstroke diesel, can be severe and expensive to fix. Transmission and suspension issues are also prevalent, but their severity and cost vary depending on the specific problem. Electrical and interior issues are relatively minor and less costly to address. However, the potential for rust on the underbody and frame is a significant concern for owners. Overall, it's essential to consider the potential expenses associated with these issues when purchasing or maintaining a 2005 Ford E-350 Super Duty.
