# 2008 Ford E-350 Super Duty

### Overview
The 2008 Ford E-350 Super Duty is a robust full-size van designed for heavy-duty use, often serving commercial fleets and as a base for RV conversions. Its reputation is built on durability and capability, particularly in its powertrain options. While generally reliable, like any vehicle, it can experience specific issues as it ages. This analysis focuses on documented common problems beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential areas of concern for owners and fleet managers. Understanding these potential issues can help in proactive maintenance and budgeting for repairs.

---


## Engine

### 5.4L Triton V8
**Spark Plug Blowout**
- **Symptoms:** Misfires, rough idling, loss of power, ticking noise from engine.
- **Solution:** Replace spark plugs with updated design and use anti-seize compound. May require thread repair.
- **Cost:** $300 - $700

**Camshaft Position Sensor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Check Engine Light, rough idle, stalling, poor fuel economy.
- **Solution:** Replace faulty camshaft position sensor.
- **Cost:** $150 - $300


### 6.0L Power Stroke V8 Diesel
**EGR Cooler Failure**
- **Symptoms:** White smoke from exhaust, coolant loss, overheating, loss of power.
- **Solution:** Replace EGR cooler with an upgraded unit. Often done in conjunction with other cooling system repairs.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1500

**Oil Cooler Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Overheating, loss of engine power, potential for catastrophic engine failure if ignored.
- **Solution:** Replace oil cooler. Often recommended to replace oil cooler lines and flush the system.
- **Cost:** $700 - $1200

**Head Gasket Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Coolant leaks, white smoke, overheating, loss of power.
- **Solution:** Replace head gaskets and head studs with stronger aftermarket units.
- **Cost:** $2000 - $4000+


### 6.8L Triton V10
**Spark Plug Blowout**
- **Symptoms:** Misfires, rough idling, loss of power, ticking noise from engine.
- **Solution:** Replace spark plugs with updated design and use anti-seize compound. May require thread repair.
- **Cost:** $300 - $700

**Camshaft Position Sensor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Check Engine Light, rough idle, stalling, poor fuel economy.
- **Solution:** Replace faulty camshaft position sensor.
- **Cost:** $150 - $300


## Transmission

### 4R75E 4-Speed Automatic
**Torque Converter Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Slipping, shuddering, delayed engagement, overheating.
- **Solution:** Rebuild or replace torque converter and transmission fluid.
- **Cost:** $1000 - $2500

**Solenoid Pack Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Harsh shifting, failure to shift into certain gears, transmission warning light.
- **Solution:** Replace transmission solenoid pack.
- **Cost:** $400 - $800


### 5R110W TorqShift 5-Speed Automatic
**Torque Converter Clutch (TCC) Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Shuddering during acceleration, slipping, failure to lock up.
- **Solution:** Replace torque converter and related seals/clutches.
- **Cost:** $1200 - $2800

**Internal Seal Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Slipping gears, delayed engagement, low fluid pressure.
- **Solution:** Transmission overhaul or replacement.
- **Cost:** $2500 - $5000+


## Suspension

### Standard Chassis
**Ball Joint Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Clunking noises over bumps, uneven tire wear, steering wander.
- **Solution:** Replace worn ball joints.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600

**Leaf Spring Sagging/Breakage**
- **Symptoms:** Rear end sag, reduced load capacity, rattling noises.
- **Solution:** Replace or reinforce leaf springs.
- **Cost:** $400 - $900


## Electrical

### General System
**Alternator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Dimming headlights, battery warning light, electrical system malfunctions, dead battery.
- **Solution:** Replace alternator.
- **Cost:** $400 - $700

**Instrument Cluster Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** Gauges not working, warning lights staying on, intermittent power loss to cluster.
- **Solution:** Repair or replace instrument cluster.
- **Cost:** $300 - $800


## Exterior

### Body Panels & Trim
**Rust on Body Panels**
- **Symptoms:** Visible corrosion, bubbling paint, perforation of metal, especially in salt-belt regions.
- **Solution:** Rust repair and repainting. May require panel replacement.
- **Cost:** $500 - $2000+ per panel

**Door Latch/Lock Actuator Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Doors not locking/unlocking properly, difficulty opening/closing doors.
- **Solution:** Replace faulty door latch or lock actuator.
- **Cost:** $200 - $500 per door


## Interior

### HVAC System
**Blend Door Actuator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Inability to control temperature (stuck on hot or cold), air only coming from certain vents.
- **Solution:** Replace faulty blend door actuator.
- **Cost:** $200 - $400


### Seat Upholstery
**Wear and Tear on Heavy Use Seats**
- **Symptoms:** Torn fabric, worn padding, especially on driver's seat in high-mileage vehicles.
- **Solution:** Repair or replace seat covers/cushions.
- **Cost:** $150 - $500 per seat

### Summary Verdict
The 2008 Ford E-350 Super Duty is a workhorse vehicle with a generally strong reputation for durability, especially concerning its chassis and basic functionality. However, specific powertrain options, particularly the 6.0L Power Stroke diesel, are known for significant and costly potential failures (EGR, oil cooler, head gaskets) that require diligent maintenance and potentially proactive upgrades. The gasoline V8 and V10 engines are more reliable but can suffer from spark plug ejection and sensor issues. Transmission issues, while not universal, can be expensive to repair. Suspension components like ball joints and leaf springs are subject to wear due to the vehicle's heavy-duty nature. Electrical components like alternators and instrument clusters can also present problems. Overall, while capable, potential buyers or owners should be aware of the specific engine and transmission vulnerabilities and budget accordingly for maintenance and potential repairs.
