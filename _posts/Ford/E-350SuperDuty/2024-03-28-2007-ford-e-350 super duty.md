**Introduction:**

The 2007 Ford E-350 Super Duty is a full-size van that is available with a variety of engine and transmission options. It is a popular choice for commercial and heavy-duty use.

**Engine:**

* 4.6L V8 (225 hp, 293 lb-ft)
* 5.4L V8 (300 hp, 420 lb-ft)
* 6.0L V8 (325 hp, 570 lb-ft)
* 6.0L Power Stroke Diesel (325 hp, 560 lb-ft)

**Transmission:**

* 4-speed automatic
* 5-speed automatic
* 6-speed automatic

**Engine**

**Valve Seat Failure**
Description/Symptoms: Worn or damaged valve seats can lead to a loss of compression, engine knocking, and excessive oil consumption.
Solution: Replace the valve seats and related components.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,500+

**Spark Plug Failure**
Description/Symptoms: Faulty spark plugs can cause misfires, rough idle, and reduced engine power.
Solution: Replace the spark plugs.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+

**Intake Manifold Runner Control Valve Failure**
Description/Symptoms: A malfunctioning intake manifold runner control valve can lead to rough idle, stalling, and decreased engine performance.
Solution: Replace the intake manifold runner control valve.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+

**Transmission**

**4-Speed Automatic Transmission Failure**
Description/Symptoms: The 4-speed automatic transmission may experience slipping gears, shuddering, or complete failure.
Solution: Rebuild or replace the transmission.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,500 - $4,000+

**5-Speed Automatic Transmission Overheating**
Description/Symptoms: Excessive heat buildup in the 5-speed automatic transmission can cause transmission damage and failure.
Solution: Repair or replace the transmission cooler, lines, or fluid pump.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+

**Electrical**

**Alternator Failure**
Description/Symptoms: A faulty alternator can lead to battery drain, dimming headlights, and electrical system malfunctions.
Solution: Replace the alternator.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+

**Battery Drain**
Description/Symptoms: A parasitic draw on the battery can cause it to drain prematurely.
Solution: Identify and repair the source of the draw, such as faulty lights or accessories.
Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the source of the drain

**Exterior**

**Tailgate Rust**
Description/Symptoms: The tailgate can develop rust due to exposure to moisture and road salt.
Solution: Replace the tailgate or apply rust-proofing measures.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+

**Interior**

**Control Panel Malfunction**
Description/Symptoms: The interior control panel may experience electrical problems, causing buttons or switches to fail.
Solution: Replace or repair the control panel.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+

**Climate Control System Failure**
Description/Symptoms: The climate control system may fail to regulate temperature, causing excessive heat or cold in the cabin.
Solution: Diagnose and repair the faulty components within the climate control system.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+

**Summary:**

The 2007 Ford E-350 Super Duty is a reliable vehicle for heavy-duty use. However, it is important to be aware of potential issues such as engine valve seat failure, transmission overheating, and rust on the tailgate. Repair costs can be significant, but most issues can be resolved by following the recommended solutions.
