**Introduction:**

The 2012 Ford E-350 Super Duty is a heavy-duty cargo van offered with various engine and transmission options. It has a 6.8L V10 engine that produces 305 horsepower and 420 lb-ft of torque. The E-350 Super Duty is praised for its spacious cargo area and powerful engine, but it has been criticized for its rough ride and poor fuel economy.

**Engine**
- 6.8L V10
---
**Fuel Injector Failure**
- Description/Symptoms: Stalling, reduced engine power, rough idle, and check engine light
- Solution: Replace fuel injectors
- Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800+
- Recalls: N/A

**Transmission**
- 6-speed automatic

**Summary:**

The 2012 Ford E-350 Super Duty has some reliability concerns with its engine and transmission. Fuel injector failure and transmission slipping are the most common issues reported. The cost of repairs can be significant, ranging from $400 to over $2,500. However, it's important to note that these issues may not affect all E-350 Super Duty models and may vary depending on factors like maintenance and driving habits.
