**2019 Ford E-350 Super Duty**

The 2019 Ford E-350 Super Duty is a full-size van offered with multiple engine and transmission options. The 5.4L V8 engine produces 305 horsepower and 365 lb-ft of torque, while the 6.8L V10 engine produces 325 horsepower and 460 lb-ft of torque. The 6-speed automatic transmission is the only transmission option. The E-350 Super Duty is praised for its powerful engines, spacious interior, and towing capacity. However, it is criticized for its poor fuel economy, stiff ride, and outdated interior.

**Engine**

**5.4L V8**

* **Excessive Oil Consumption:** The piston rings may fail, leading to excessive oil consumption and decreased engine power. Symptoms include low oil levels, blue smoke from the exhaust, and an illuminated check engine light. The solution is to replace the piston rings, which costs around $1,200 - $1,600+.
* **Intake Manifold Gasket Failure:** The intake manifold gasket may fail, causing coolant and oil leaks. Symptoms include overheating, coolant loss, and an illuminated check engine light. The solution is to replace the intake manifold gasket, which costs around $400 - $600+.
* **Cam Phaser Failure:** The cam phasers may fail, causing reduced engine power, rough idle, and an illuminated check engine light. The solution is to replace the cam phasers, which costs around $1,000 - $1,400+.

**6.8L V10**

* **Spark Plug Blowout:** The spark plugs may blow out of the cylinder head, causing engine damage. Symptoms include loss of power, misfiring, and an illuminated check engine light. The solution is to replace the spark plugs and repair the cylinder head, which costs around $2,000 - $3,000+.
* **Fuel Injector Failure:** The fuel injectors may fail, causing poor fuel economy, rough idling, and an illuminated check engine light. The solution is to replace the fuel injectors, which costs around $1,000 - $1,200+.
* **Intake Valve Failure:** The intake valves may fail, causing reduced engine power, misfiring, and an illuminated check engine light. The solution is to replace the intake valves, which costs around $1,500 - $2,000+.

**Transmission**

* **6-Speed Automatic:** No Issues Found

**Suspension**

* **Strut Mount Failure:** The strut mounts may fail, causing a clunking noise when driving over bumps. Symptoms include a loose or wobbly steering wheel, excessive tire wear, and reduced handling. The solution is to replace the strut mounts, which costs around $300 - $500+.

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain:** The battery may drain prematurely, especially when the vehicle is parked. Symptoms include difficulty starting the engine, dim headlights, and a dead battery. The solution is to identify and fix the source of the battery drain, which could be a faulty alternator, starter, or electrical component. This can cost anywhere from $200 - $1,000+ depending on the complexity of the issue.
* **Alternator Failure:** The alternator may fail, causing the battery to drain and the engine to stall. Symptoms include a dim dashboard, flickering headlights, and an illuminated check engine light. The solution is to replace the alternator, which costs around $500 - $800+.

**Exterior**

* **Door Handle Breakage:** The door handles may break, making it difficult to open the doors. Symptoms include a loose or broken door handle, and difficulty opening the door. The solution is to replace the door handle, which costs around $100 - $200+.

**Interior**

* **HVAC Malfunction:** The HVAC system may malfunction, causing insufficient heating or cooling. Symptoms include poor airflow, strange noises, and an illuminated check engine light. The solution is to diagnose and repair the faulty component, which could be a blower motor, compressor, or blend door. This can cost anywhere from $300 - $1,000+ depending on the issue.
* **Cruise Control Inoperability:** The cruise control may become inoperable. Symptoms include the inability to set or maintain a cruising speed. The solution is to replace the cruise control switch or module, which costs around $200 - $400+.
* **Seatbelt Buckle Malfunction:** The seatbelt buckle may malfunction, preventing the seatbelt from latching properly. Symptoms include a loose or stuck seatbelt buckle, and an illuminated check engine light. The solution is to replace the seatbelt buckle, which costs around $100 - $200+.

**Summary**

The 2019 Ford E-350 Super Duty has a number of potential issues, particularly with the engine and suspension components. Some of these issues can be costly to repair, especially if they are not addressed promptly. However, the overall reliability of the E-350 Super Duty is considered average for its class.
