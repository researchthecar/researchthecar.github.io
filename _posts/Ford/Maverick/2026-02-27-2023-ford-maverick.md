# 2023 Ford Maverick Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2023 Ford Maverick brings compact utility and impressive fuel economy to the small truck market, making it an appealing option for budget-conscious buyers. However, prospective owners should review common owner-reported issues ranging from electrical battery drains to drivetrain and braking concerns. Keeping a close eye on service history and ensuring open recalls are addressed can help buyers secure a more dependable driving experience.

---

## Engine

### 1.5L EcoBoost 3-Cylinder

The 1.5L EcoBoost 3-cylinder engine offers efficient turbocharged performance suited for daily commuting. It provides adequate power for light-duty tasks while maintaining competitive fuel economy.

### 2.0L EcoBoost 4-Cylinder

The 2.0L EcoBoost 4-cylinder engine produces robust horsepower and torque for stronger acceleration and towing capability. While generally reliable, some owners have reported throttle body and motive power issues.

**Throttle Body Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may experience unexpected engine stalling accompanied by illuminated warning lights.
- **Solution:** Inspect and replace the faulty throttle body component.

**Loss of Motive Power**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers report a sudden drop in speed and an inability to accelerate while pressing the gas pedal.
- **Solution:** Diagnostic scan of powertrain control modules and software updates or hardware replacement as needed.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 8-speed automatic transmission pairs with the 2.0L EcoBoost engine to deliver smooth, responsive shifts across various driving conditions.

### CVT Hybrid Transmission

The continuously variable transmission (CVT) is designed for maximum efficiency in hybrid configurations. Though efficient, owners have noted occasional powertrain malfunction warnings and unexpected neutral shifts.

**Unintended Neutral Shift**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may unexpectedly shift into neutral or park while driving, leading to a sudden loss of drive power.
- **Solution:** Have a dealership inspect the transmission control module and apply any required software updates or mechanical repairs.

## Suspension

### CV Axles and Suspension

Drivetrain components generally transfer power smoothly, but front axle wear can lead to noticeable vibrations and require early replacement.

**Axle Vibration and Premature Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Pronounced vibrations, shuddering, or clicking noises occur through the front end and steering wheel during acceleration.
- **Solution:** Inspect CV axles and replace worn or damaged front axle assemblies.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

The braking system provides adequate stopping power under normal conditions, though intermittent pedal feel irregularities have been reported by owners.

**Brake Pedal Responsiveness Fluctuations**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers experience a sudden feeling of lost braking action near the end of a stop, followed by harsh braking engagement.
- **Solution:** Inspect brake fluid levels, vacuum lines, and anti-lock braking system modules for faults.

## Electrical

### 12V Battery and Charging System

Electrical architecture supports modern infotainment and driver assistance, but hybrid models in particular have experienced chronic 12V battery drain and deep sleep issues.

**Parasitic Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** The 12V battery repeatedly discharges, causing the vehicle to enter 'Deep Sleep' mode or disabling interior features.
- **Solution:** Update vehicle software to the latest calibration levels and replace damaged batteries under warranty where applicable.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V160000: Exterior Lighting:Tail Lights](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Maverick&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** A missing electrical circuit may cause the trailer taillights to not illuminate as intended when utilizing the vehicle trailer tow electrical connector. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 108, " Lamps, Reflective Devices, and Associated Equipment." Trailer taillights that do not.
- **What to do:** Dealers will add an electrical circuit to the engine bay wiring harness, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V380000: Engine](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Maverick&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** In the event of an engine failure, engine oil and fuel vapor may be released into the engine compartment and accumulate near ignition sources such as hot engine or exhaust components, possibly resulting in an engine compartment fire. An engine compartment fire increases the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the engine long-block in 2023 Escape and Corsair vehicles. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V848000: Exterior Lighting:Turn Signal:Bulbs](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Maverick&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 108, "Lamps, Reflective Devices, and Associated Equipment." A nonfunctioning exterior turn signal will not notify other drivers that the vehicle is turning, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the body control module software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V140000: Electrical System: Instrument Cluster/Panel](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Maverick&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** The Instrument Panel Cluster may not properly illuminate warning lights or gauges. An instrument panel that does not illuminate critical safety information, such as the speedometer or warning lights, can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the instrument panel cluster software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V223000: Tires](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Maverick&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** A loss of vehicle handling and control increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the tires, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V267000: Electrical System:Body Control Module/Bcm](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Maverick&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** An undetected low battery charge can result in a loss of electrical accessories such as hazard lights, or cause a loss of drive power, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will recalibrate the body control module and power train control module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V293000: Exterior Lighting:Tail Lights](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Maverick&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** The Body Control Module (BCM) may inadvertently deactivate one or both rear tail lights. Rear tail lights that fail to illuminate can reduce the vehicle's visibility to other drivers, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the BCM software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V330000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission:Control Module (Tcm/Pcm/Tecm)](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Maverick&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** The Hybrid Powertrain Control Module (HPCM) software may cause the vehicle to shift into neutral unexpectedly, causing a loss of drive power. A loss of drive power increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the HPCM software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2023 Ford Maverick offers practical versatility and strong fuel economy, but prospective buyers should inspect used models carefully. Pay close attention to potential electrical battery drains, transmission behavior, and front axle vibrations during a test drive. Verifying that all open safety recalls have been resolved will help ensure a more reliable ownership experience.
