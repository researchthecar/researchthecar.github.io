# 2022 Ford Maverick Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2022 Ford Maverick introduced a fresh compact pickup option to the market, combining impressive fuel economy with everyday utility. While owners have praised its versatility and value, early production models have drawn notable complaints regarding electrical system glitches, powertrain vibrations, and minor structural or interior trim durability issues. Buyers evaluating a used 2022 Maverick should thoroughly inspect the vehicle's electrical health and drivetrain components before purchasing.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L EcoBoost I4

The 2.0L EcoBoost turbocharged four-cylinder engine produces 250 horsepower and 277 lb-ft of torque, providing strong acceleration for daily driving and towing. It is widely paired with all-wheel drive, though owners should remain vigilant regarding occasional electrical and stalling complaints.

**Engine Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicles may experience unexpected engine stalling while slowing down or coming to a stop, sometimes accompanied by warning lights.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis by a qualified technician to check for fuel delivery, software, or sensor faults; software updates or component replacement may be required.

### 2.5L Hybrid I4

The 2.5L hybrid powertrain pairs a naturally aspirated four-cylinder engine with an electric motor to generate a combined 191 horsepower, prioritizing exceptional city fuel economy. Front-wheel drive is standard on this setup, making it a popular choice for efficiency-focused commuters.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 8-speed automatic transmission is coupled with the 2.0L EcoBoost engine, offering smooth gear selection under normal operating conditions. Some owners have reported drivetrain vibrations and mechanical concerns that warrant a careful pre-purchase inspection.

**Powertrain and Drivetrain Shudder**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may feel unexpected vibrations, shuddering during acceleration, or mechanical noises from the axle and transmission areas.
- **Solution:** Inspect CV axles and transmission mounts for wear or failure; replacement of affected components may be necessary.

### Electronic Continuously Variable Transmission (eCVT)

The eCVT works seamlessly with the 2.5L hybrid powertrain to optimize power delivery and maximize fuel efficiency. It generally operates quietly, though buyers should verify proper hybrid system warning lights and operation during a test drive.

## Steering

### Electric Power Steering

The electric power steering system provides light and responsive handling under normal conditions, but is sensitive to fluctuations in vehicle electrical voltage.

**Steering Assist Loss**
- **Symptoms:** Sudden loss of power steering assist or unexpected steering wheel feedback during low-speed turns, often linked to voltage drops.
- **Solution:** Inspect electrical connections, battery voltage stability, and power steering control modules for faults.

## Electrical

### Electrical Distribution and Battery System

The electrical architecture governs critical vehicle systems, but some owners have experienced premature 12V battery depletion and unexpected warning lights.

**12V Battery Drain and Warning Lights**
- **Symptoms:** Illuminated warning lights on the dash, sudden drops in battery voltage, and intermittent no-start conditions or electrical system failures.
- **Solution:** Test the 12-volt battery health, check charging system output, and update or replace faulty control modules as needed.

## Exterior

### Door Structure and Hardware

Exterior body panels and doors provide standard protection, though a subset of owners have reported premature wear and weld stress around the front door checks.

**Door Check and Limiter Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Popping noises, creaking, broken spot welds near the door hinges, and door alignment issues that can interfere with window operation.
- **Solution:** Repair or reinforce damaged door check mounts and spot welds; severely affected doors may require structural repair or replacement.

## Safety

### Seat Belts and Airbags

Occupant safety systems generally function as designed, though sensor communication glitches can trigger persistent warning chimes and lights.

**Seat Belt Monitor Fault**
- **Symptoms:** Dashboard warning messages indicating a rear seat belt monitor fault, often accompanied by an airbag warning light and disabled auto start-stop functionality.
- **Solution:** Have a dealership inspect the seat belt buckle sensors and wiring harnesses to clear diagnostic fault codes.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V979000: Seat Belts:Rear/Other:Anchorage](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Maverick&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** The rear seat belt buckles may not be properly attached to the rear floor assembly. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard numbers 208, "Occupant Crash Protection," and 210, "Seat Belt Assembly Anchorages." Improperly attached seat belts may not adequately restrain an occupant in a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will repair the rear seat belt buckle attachments, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V976000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Storage](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Maverick&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 301, "Fuel System Integrity." A damaged fuel tank may result in a fuel leak, increasing the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the fuel tank as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V193000: Electrical System: Integrated Trailer Brake Control](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Maverick&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** Due to a software error, a towed trailer equipped with an electric or electric-over hydraulic brake system may not brake. Loss of trailer brake functionality could result in extended stopping distance, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the integrated trailer brake control module software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V484000: Engine And Engine Cooling](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Maverick&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** In the event of an engine failure, engine oil and fuel vapor may be released into the engine compartment and accumulate near ignition sources such as hot engine or exhaust components, possibly resulting in an engine compartment fire. An engine compartment fire increases the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will modify the under engine shield and active grille shutter, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V594000: Air Bags:Side/Window:Curtain](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Maverick&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** The side-curtain air bags may not deploy properly. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 226, "Ejection Mitigation." Improper deployment of an air bag increases the risk of injury in a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the left and right side-curtain air bag modules with a new design, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V730000: Exterior Lighting:Brake Lights](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Maverick&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** Under certain circumstances, the Antilock Brake System (ABS) module may cause the brake lights to illuminate without the driver applying the brakes. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 108, "Lamps, Reflective Devices, and Associated Equipment." Unintended illumination of the brake.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the software in the ABS module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V380000: Engine](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Maverick&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** In the event of an engine failure, engine oil and fuel vapor may be released into the engine compartment and accumulate near ignition sources such as hot engine or exhaust components, possibly resulting in an engine compartment fire. An engine compartment fire increases the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the engine long-block in 2023 Escape and Corsair vehicles. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V848000: Exterior Lighting:Turn Signal:Bulbs](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Maverick&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 108, "Lamps, Reflective Devices, and Associated Equipment." A nonfunctioning exterior turn signal will not notify other drivers that the vehicle is turning, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the body control module software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2022 Ford Maverick is an appealing and versatile compact truck, but prospective buyers should approach early-production models with a measure of caution. Reviewing vehicle service records for prior electrical updates, battery replacements, and axle or drivetrain inspections is highly recommended. Conducting a comprehensive pre-purchase test drive will help ensure that all powertrain, steering, and electrical functions operate smoothly.
