**Introduction:**

The 1997 Ford Escort was a compact sedan and wagon offered with a variety of engine and transmission options. The base engine was a 2.0L inline-four with 110 horsepower and 140 lb-ft of torque. Optional engine choices included a 2.3L inline-four and a 3.0L V6, paired with either a 5-speed manual or 4-speed automatic transmission.

While the Escort was generally praised for its affordability and fuel efficiency, it had several issues reported by owners.

**Engine**

**2.0L Inline-Four**

* **Intake Manifold Gasket Failure:** Symptoms include coolant leaks, overheating, and poor engine performance. Solution: Replace the intake manifold gasket. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
* **Timing Belt Failure:** Symptoms include engine overheating, loss of power, and a loud noise coming from the engine. Solution: Replace the timing belt and other related components. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
* **Head Gasket Failure:** Symptoms include coolant leaks, overheating, and white smoke from the exhaust. Solution: Replace the head gasket. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+

**2.3L Inline-Four**

* **No Major Issues Found**

**3.0L V6**

* **Valve Covers Gaskets Leak:** Symptoms include oil leaks around the valve covers. Solution: Replace the valve covers gaskets. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200

**Transmission**

**5-Speed Manual**

* **Shift Fork Failure:** Symptoms include difficulty shifting gears, grinding noises, and popping out of gear. Solution: Replace the shift fork. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+

**4-Speed Automatic**

* **Torque Converter Failure:** Symptoms include slipping or shuddering during shifts, and delayed engagement. Solution: Replace the torque converter. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $1,500+
* **Solenoid Failure:** Symptoms include erratic shifting, delayed shifts, and no engagement in certain gears. Solution: Replace the faulty solenoid. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+

**Suspension**

* **Strut Mounts Fail:** Symptoms include clunking noises, poor handling, and uneven tire wear. Solution: Replace the strut mounts. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
* **Lower Control Arms Fail:** Symptoms include clunking noises, poor handling, and uneven tire wear. Solution: Replace the lower control arms. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+

**Electrical**

* **Alternator Failure:** Symptoms include dim headlights, flickering lights, and dead battery. Solution: Replace the alternator. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+
* **Battery Drain:** Symptoms include a dead battery even after replacing the battery. Solution: Locate and fix the source of the electrical drain. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+
* **Electrical System Fault:** Symptoms include random electrical issues, such as non-working lights, gauges, or power windows. Solution: Diagnose and repair the faulty electrical component. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+

**Exterior**

* **Door Handle Fail:** Symptoms include difficulty opening the door, broken door handle, or sticking handle. Solution: Replace the door handle. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
* **Rust Issues:** Symptoms include rust spots on the body, frame, or other metal components. Solution: Repair and protect against further rust. Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the severity of the rust

**Interior**

* **Broken Window Crank:** Symptoms include difficulty rolling up or down the window. Solution: Replace the window crank. Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $100
* **Dashboard Cracks:** Symptoms include cracks or fading on the dashboard. Solution: Replace the dashboard or cover it with a dash cover. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+

**Summary:**

The 1997 Ford Escort has several potential issues that can affect its reliability and overall ownership experience. Engine and transmission problems are the most common and can be expensive to repair. Suspension issues are also common and can impact handling and safety. Electrical problems, while less severe, can be frustrating to diagnose and fix. Exterior issues such as rust and door handle failure can also be a concern. Overall, the 1997 Ford Escort is a budget-friendly vehicle, but it is important to be aware of its potential problems before making a purchase decision.
