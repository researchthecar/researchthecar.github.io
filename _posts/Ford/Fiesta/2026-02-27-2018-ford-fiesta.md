# 2018 Ford Fiesta Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2018 Ford Fiesta offers nimble handling and efficient powertrain choices, making it a popular choice for budget-conscious commuters. However, used car buyers should exercise caution, particularly regarding transmission performance and certain recurring electrical and powertrain issues reported by owners.

---

## Engine

### 1.6L Ti-VCT 4-Cylinder

This naturally aspirated 4-cylinder engine produces 120 horsepower and 112 lb-ft of torque, providing standard motivation for base trims. It is generally regarded as a durable mechanical unit, though auxiliary components like purge valves can occasionally fail.

### 1.6L EcoBoost Turbocharged 4-Cylinder

Exclusive to the high-performance ST trim, this turbocharged engine pumps out 197 horsepower and 202 lb-ft of torque. It delivers spirited acceleration and enthusiast appeal, though prospective buyers should inspect closely for signs of aggressive prior driving.

### Fuel System

Issues in this area primarily concern emissions equipment and auxiliary fuel delivery hardware.

**Purge Valve and Fuel System Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers report premature failure of the fuel canister purge valve and issues with the capless gas fuel filler system.
- **Solution:** Replace the faulty fuel vapor purge valve and inspect the capless filler neck.

## Transmission

### PowerShift 6-Speed Dual-Clutch Automatic

The automated manual dual-clutch transmission is known for reliability concerns and drivability complaints, making a thorough test drive essential.

**Transmission Shudder, Slipping, and Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Owners report severe transmission shudder, unexpected loss of motive power, grinding noises, and sudden RPM flares or 'Transmission Malfunction' warnings.
- **Solution:** Requires professional diagnosis, which may involve clutch pack replacement, actuator service, or transmission software updates.

### 5-Speed Manual

Paired primarily with standard models, this traditional manual gearbox is generally preferred over the automatic, though mechanical wear on linkages and clutch pedals can still occur.

**Clutch Pedal and Gear Engagement Issues**
- **Symptoms:** The clutch pedal may randomly remain fully depressed on the floor requiring manual retrieval, and gears like third gear may pop out or lose grip under load.
- **Solution:** Inspect and repair the clutch pedal assembly, linkages, or internal manual synchronizers and gears.

## Electrical

### Backup Camera and Display

Visibility and driver-assistance aids are generally helpful when functioning, though display hardware issues have frustrated some owners.

**Distorted or Failing Backup Camera**
- **Symptoms:** The backup camera display may show distorted images, go completely black intermittently, or display a 'not available' message.
- **Solution:** Diagnose the camera wiring, display unit, and tailgate harness; replace components if faulty.
- **Cost:** $200.00 diagnostic fee reported by owner

## Exterior

### Body Control Module and Horn

Electrical system concerns typically revolve around intermittent module behavior and auxiliary component failures.

**Horn Failure and Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle horn may short or die without warning, and a failing body control module can cause brake light malfunctions and parasitic battery drains.
- **Solution:** Replace the failed horn assembly or test and replace the body control module as necessary.

### Latch and Trunk Mechanisms

Cabin components are mostly straightforward, though occasional latch and lock malfunctions are reported.

**Random Trunk Opening**
- **Symptoms:** The trunk may pop open unexpectedly while driving or when shifting the vehicle into gear.
- **Solution:** Inspect the trunk latch mechanism and electronic release switch for internal shorts or misalignment.

## Summary Verdict

When shopping for a used 2018 Ford Fiesta, prospective buyers should prioritize models with a manual transmission to avoid the well-documented quirks of the dual-clutch automatic. Be sure to thoroughly test all electrical accessories, check the backup camera display, and listen closely for any unusual noises or shudders during a test drive.
