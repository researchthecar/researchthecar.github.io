# 2016 Ford Fiesta Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2016 Ford Fiesta offers a nimble driving experience and impressive fuel economy, making it a popular choice for budget-conscious commuters. However, prospective buyers should exercise extreme caution, particularly regarding its troubled powertrain options. While standard maintenance items are manageable, chronic mechanical issues can turn an affordable used car into a financial burden.

---

## Engine

### 1.6L Ti-VCT I-4

The base 1.6-liter naturally aspirated four-cylinder engine produces 120 horsepower and 112 lb-ft of torque. It provides adequate power for daily commuting and generally proves mechanically simpler than its turbocharged counterpart, though occasional cooling or relay glitches have been reported.

### 1.6L EcoBoost Turbocharged I-4

Exclusive to the high-performance ST trim, this turbocharged engine generates 197 horsepower and 202 lb-ft of torque. It delivers spirited acceleration and engaging driving dynamics, though it requires attentive maintenance and premium fuel.

## Transmission

### PowerShift 6-Speed Dual-Clutch Automatic

The available six-speed dual-clutch automatic transmission is notoriously problematic, frequently suffering from premature clutch wear, shuddering, and control module failures. Prospective buyers are strongly advised to steer clear of automatic models in favor of manuals.

**Transmission Control Module and Clutch Failures**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers experience severe vehicle shuddering during acceleration, harsh gear shifts, slipping gears, hesitation, and complete loss of motive power accompanied by warning lights.
- **Solution:** Requires diagnosis by a qualified technician, which frequently involves replacing the transmission control module (TCM), clutch assembly, or slave cylinder.

### 5-Speed Manual

The traditional five-speed manual transmission pairs well with the base engine and largely avoids the severe reliability headaches plaguing the automatic alternative, offering a much safer bet for used buyers.

## Suspension

### Wheel Lug Nuts

The factory wheel hardware is prone to distortion, which can leave stranded motorists unable to remove a flat tire without specialized tools.

**Swollen Lug Nuts**
- **Symptoms:** The outer chrome caps on the factory lug nuts swell or distort over time, making it impossible to fit a standard socket over them during a flat tire change.
- **Solution:** Replace the factory lug nuts with solid, one-piece aftermarket alternatives to ensure you can remove the wheels safely.

## Exterior

### Door Latches

Door latch mechanisms are prone to internal spring failures, preventing doors from staying shut securely and posing a noticeable safety annoyance.

**Door Latch Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Doors fail to latch securely when closed, can pop open while driving or from the inside without touching the handle, and may cause interior lights or door-ajar warnings to stay illuminated.
- **Solution:** Replace the defective door latch assemblies on the affected doors.

## Summary Verdict

The 2016 Ford Fiesta demands careful inspection before purchase, primarily due to widespread and severe complaints regarding its PowerShift automatic transmission. If you are shopping for this model, strongly prioritize versions equipped with a manual transmission to sidestep costly mechanical failures. Additionally, check that the wheels are free of swollen lug nuts and that all doors latch securely.
