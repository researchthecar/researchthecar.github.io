# 2017 Ford Fiesta Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2017 Ford Fiesta, buyers should weigh its nimble handling and fuel efficiency against notable powertrain concerns. While standard commuter models offer affordable daily driving, prospective owners need to pay close attention to mechanical and electrical histories, particularly regarding automatic transmissions.

---

## Engine

### 1.6L Ti-VCT I4

The base 1.6-liter naturally aspirated four-cylinder engine produces 120 horsepower, providing adequate power for city commutes with straightforward maintenance requirements outside of occasional EVAP system quirks.

**Engine Stalling and Canister Purge Valve**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle may stall after refueling or experience rough idling and unexpected engine hesitation.
- **Solution:** Replace the faulty canister purge valve solenoid and clear related EVAP codes.

### 1.6L EcoBoost Turbocharged I4

Exclusive to the high-performance ST trim, this turbocharged engine delivers 197 horsepower and spirited acceleration for enthusiast drivers, though it demands diligent maintenance.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Powershift Dual-Clutch Automatic

This automated manual transmission has been a frequent source of owner dissatisfaction due to shuddering and premature clutch wear, making thorough test drives essential.

**Clutch Failure and Transmission Slipping**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle experiences severe shuddering during acceleration, harsh shifting between lower gears, slipping, or loss of reverse capability.
- **Solution:** Inspect clutch components and the transmission control module; professional overhaul or clutch replacement may be necessary.

### 5-Speed Manual

The traditional manual gearbox provides a more engaging drive and largely avoids the reliability headaches associated with the dual-clutch automatic unit.

## Suspension

### Wheel

Standard suspension components hold up well, but hardware issues like swollen lug nuts can cause frustrating delays during routine tire maintenance.

**Swollen Lug Nuts**
- **Symptoms:** Factory lug nuts become distorted or swollen, preventing standard tire irons from fitting properly during roadside tire changes.
- **Solution:** Replace the factory cap-style lug nuts with solid aftermarket alternatives.

## Summary Verdict

The 2017 Ford Fiesta is an appealing, budget-friendly subcompact, but buyers must proceed with caution. Prioritize models equipped with the manual transmission to bypass the well-documented dual-clutch automatic issues, and ensure a comprehensive pre-purchase inspection is completed before finalizing any deal.
