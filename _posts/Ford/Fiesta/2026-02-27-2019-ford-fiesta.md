# 2019 Ford Fiesta Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2019 Ford Fiesta is a compact and nimble car that offers efficient daily driving dynamics, but potential used-car buyers should review its mechanical and electrical history carefully. While many owners enjoy its fuel economy and driving character, NHTSA complaints highlight specific areas of concern including powertrain operations, braking components, and persistent rearview camera malfunctions. Prospective owners should thoroughly inspect these systems before completing a purchase.

---

## Engine

### 1.6L Ti-VCT 4-Cylinder

The standard 1.6-liter naturally aspirated four-cylinder engine produces around 120 horsepower, providing adequate performance for commuting. It is generally straightforward mechanically, though owners have reported occasional hesitation and noise issues.

**Engine Stalling and Hesitation**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers report occasional engine hesitation, abnormal noises from the engine compartment, or unexpected power delivery fluctuations during acceleration.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified mechanic inspect the ignition components, throttle body, and fuel injection system for faults.

### 1.6L EcoBoost Turbocharged 4-Cylinder

Exclusive to the high-performance ST trim, this turbocharged engine delivers approximately 197 horsepower for an engaging driving experience. Routine maintenance and inspection of turbo components are recommended for used buyers.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed PowerShift Dual-Clutch Automatic Transmission

This dual-clutch automatic transmission pairs with the standard engine to maximize fuel efficiency. However, it has a history of owner complaints regarding rough shifting, clutch wear, and operational hesitation.

**Transmission Clutch Failure and Shifting Delays**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle may hesitate to accelerate, jerk severely when changing gears, get stuck in lower gears, or display overheating warnings.
- **Solution:** Inspect the clutch actuators and transmission module; software updates or mechanical clutch replacements may be required.

### 5-Speed Manual Transmission

The traditional 5-speed manual transmission is paired with the base engine and offers direct driver engagement. While generally robust, hydraulic clutch components can experience wear and failure over time.

**Clutch Slave Cylinder Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The clutch pedal may stick to the floorboard, preventing the driver from cleanly changing gears while operating the vehicle.
- **Solution:** Replace the failing hydraulic clutch slave cylinder and flush the fluid reservoir.

### 6-Speed Manual Transmission

Exclusive to the ST trim, this 6-speed manual gearbox handles the higher torque output of the EcoBoost engine. It provides sporty shift characteristics for enthusiast drivers.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes and ABS

The braking system provides standard stopping power, but owner reports indicate potential concerns with sticking brake calipers and ABS module malfunctions.

**Brake Caliper Seizing and ABS Module Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Brake calipers may stick or lock up during driving, causing vehicle pulling, excessive heat, or rocking motions. ABS modules can also fail, requiring system modifications.
- **Solution:** Inspect brake pads, rotors, and calipers for uneven wear or sticking, and test the anti-lock braking control module.

## Electrical

### Electrical and Backup Camera System

Electrical complaints for this model frequently center around infotainment display quirks and malfunctioning rearview camera systems.

**Rearview Camera Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** The backup camera display may freeze, display black and white lines, show a blank screen, or display a 'camera not available' message.
- **Solution:** Check camera wiring harnesses and software connections, or participate in relevant manufacturer recall service remedies when parts are available.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V629000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Foundation Components:Disc:Caliper](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Fiesta&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** The swollen seals can cause the brakes to drag, potentially overheating the brakes and increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will flush the brake fluid and replace the front brake calipers and brake hoses, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V951000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Fiesta&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** A rearview camera that displays a blank or distorted image can reduce the drivers view of what is behind the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the rearview camera as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V695000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Fiesta&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** A rearview camera that fails to properly display an image can reduce the driver’s view behind the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the rearview camera as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2019 Ford Fiesta remains an affordable and efficient choice, but buyers should exercise caution. Pay close attention during a test drive to ensure smooth transmission operation, responsive braking, and proper electrical functionality. Verifying that all safety recalls have been addressed by a dealer is strongly recommended before finalizing a purchase.
