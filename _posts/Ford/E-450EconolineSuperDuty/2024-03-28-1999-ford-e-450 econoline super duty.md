**1999 Ford E-450 Econoline Super Duty**

The 1999 Ford E-450 Econoline Super Duty is a full-size van designed for heavy-duty commercial or personal use. It is offered with a range of gasoline and diesel engine options, paired with either a manual or automatic transmission. The E-450 is known for its spacious cargo area and powerful engines, making it popular with businesses and individuals who require a reliable and capable vehicle. However, like all vehicles, the E-450 has some known issues that owners should be aware of.

**Engine**
* **7.3L Power Stroke Diesel**
    * **Injector Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: The fuel injectors can become clogged or malfunction, leading to reduced engine power, black smoke, and decreased fuel efficiency.
        * Solution: Replace the affected fuel injectors.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
        * Recalls: None Found
    * **Turbocharger Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: The turbocharger can fail, resulting in a loss of power, increased exhaust smoke, and whistling or rattling noises.
        * Solution: Replace the turbocharger.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
        * Recalls: None Found
* **4.6L V8**
    * **Intake Manifold Gasket Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: The intake manifold gasket can fail, leading to vacuum leaks, reduced engine performance, and increased fuel consumption.
        * Solution: Replace the intake manifold gasket.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
        * Recalls: None Found
* **5.4L Triton V8**
    * **Spark Plug Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: The spark plugs can fail, causing misfires, rough idling, and decreased engine power.
        * Solution: Replace the spark plugs.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**
* **4R100 Automatic**
    * **Slipping**
        * Description/Symptoms: The transmission may slip or hesitate when shifting gears, resulting in a loss of power or acceleration.
        * Solution: Rebuild or replace the transmission.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
        * Recalls: None Found
* **ZF S6-650 Manual**
    * **Clutch Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: The clutch can wear out or fail, leading to difficulty shifting gears or a lack of engagement.
        * Solution: Replace the clutch.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $600 - $1,200+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**
* **Front Ball Joint Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The front ball joints can wear out or fail, causing loose or wobbly steering, uneven tire wear, and a clunking or grinding noise when turning.
    * Solution: Replace the ball joints.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+ per side
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Rear Leaf Spring Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The rear leaf springs can break or sag, leading to a loss of load-carrying capacity, a rough ride, and premature tire wear.
    * Solution: Replace the leaf springs.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+ per side
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**
* **Alternator Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The alternator can fail, leading to a loss of electrical power, difficulty starting the engine, and a warning light on the dashboard.
    * Solution: Replace the alternator.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Starter Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The starter motor can fail, preventing the engine from starting.
    * Solution: Replace the starter motor.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**
* **Rusty Exhaust System**
    * Description/Symptoms: The exhaust system can rust and deteriorate, leading to leaks, decreased performance, and increased noise.
    * Solution: Replace the affected exhaust components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Tail Light Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The tail lights can fail due to bulb burnout, electrical issues, or damage.
    * Solution: Replace the affected tail lights.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+ per side
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**
* **Door Lock Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The door locks can fail, making it difficult or impossible to lock or unlock the doors.
    * Solution: Replace the affected door lock actuators.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+ per door
    * Recalls: None Found
* **HVAC System Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The HVAC system can fail due to compressor failure, blower motor issues, or refrigerant leaks.
    * Solution: Diagnose and repair the affected HVAC components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 1999 Ford E-450 Econoline Super Duty is a capable and versatile vehicle that is generally reliable. However, like any vehicle, it has some known issues that owners should be aware of. The engine, transmission, and suspension components are generally reliable, but some issues can be costly to repair. The electrical and exterior components are fairly minor and affordable to fix. The interior issues can be frustrating but are often not major concerns. Overall, the E-450 is a solid vehicle with a good track record, but it is important to consider the potential repair costs when making a purchase decision.
