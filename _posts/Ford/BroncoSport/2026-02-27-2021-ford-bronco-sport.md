# 2021 Ford Bronco Sport Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2021 Ford Bronco Sport brings rugged styling and practical capability to the compact SUV segment, but used car buyers should carefully review its reliability history. Owner reports filed with federal safety authorities highlight notable mechanical and electrical concerns, particularly regarding brake performance, powertrain operation, and fuel system components. While many drivers enjoy its versatility and handling, prospective owners should inspect the vehicle thoroughly and confirm that all relevant service actions have been performed.

---

## Engine

### 1.5L EcoBoost 3-Cylinder

The base 1.5-liter EcoBoost turbocharged three-cylinder engine generates approximately 181 horsepower and 190 lb-ft of torque. It offers nimble daily performance, though buyers should watch for reported fuel injector and stalling concerns.

**Engine Stalling and Loss of Power**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers experience unexpected engine stalling while driving in traffic, accompanied by illuminated warning lights and loss of motive power.
- **Solution:** Diagnostic inspection by a qualified mechanic to check for fuel delivery, sensor, or software faults, followed by required component replacement.

**Fuel Injector Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Fuel injector cracks or leaks can lead to raw fuel odors under the hood, rough running, and potential under-hood fire risks.
- **Solution:** Inspection and replacement of affected fuel injectors in accordance with manufacturer repair programs.

### 2.0L EcoBoost 4-Cylinder

The available 2.0-liter EcoBoost turbocharged four-cylinder engine produces around 250 horsepower and 277 lb-ft of torque, providing stronger acceleration and passing power. It is generally paired with an upgraded all-wheel-drive system.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

An 8-speed automatic transmission handles gear selection across the Bronco Sport lineup, paired with standard or advanced four-wheel drive. While generally smooth, some owners have reported unexpected shifting behavior and linkage concerns.

**Unintended Neutral Shift and Parking Defects**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may unexpectedly shift into neutral or fail to engage park properly, occasionally leading to rollaway incidents or shift fault warnings.
- **Solution:** Check transmission software, linkage, and shift modules; perform necessary software updates or mechanical adjustments.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

The braking system provides adequate stopping power under normal conditions, but a recurring cluster of complaints regarding brake booster vacuum line failures deserves close inspection during a pre-purchase evaluation.

**Brake Pedal Stiffness and Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The brake pedal suddenly becomes extremely hard to press, resulting in a dramatic loss of braking power and extended stopping distances.
- **Solution:** Inspect the brake booster, vacuum hoses, and master cylinder; replace damaged or leaking brake booster vacuum lines.

## Electrical

### Electrical System and Gateway Module

The electrical architecture supports advanced infotainment and driver assistance features, though occasional software glitches and module communication errors have been documented by owners.

**Electrical Warning Lights and Module Communication Failures**
- **Symptoms:** Various warning messages cycle continuously across the instrument cluster, and diagnostic scan tools may fail to connect due to gateway module issues.
- **Solution:** Reprogram or replace faulty gateway modules and update vehicle software at a qualified service center.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V074000: Suspension:Front:Control Arm](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Bronco+Sport&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** An incorrect lower control arm may affect vehicle handling and control, increasing the risk of a rollover crash.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will inspect and replace the front lower control arms, if necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V075000: Suspension:Rear](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Bronco+Sport&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** Loose or missing bolts may affect the vehicle's handling and control, increasing the risk of a crash. It could also reduce the vehicle's performance in a rear impact crash, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the rear suspension bolts, and secure or replace the bolts, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V809000: Visibility:Sun/Moon Roof Assembly](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Bronco+Sport&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The moonroof glass may not have been bonded correctly to the moonroof frame, which could result in the glass panel detaching from the vehicle. A detached glass panel can become a road hazard, and increase the risk of a crash or injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the moonroof glass date code and replace the glass, if necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V625000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump:Control/Drive Module](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Bronco+Sport&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The fuel delivery module may leak inside the fuel tank, causing low fuel pressure which could result in an engine stall. An engine stall increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the fuel delivery module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V011000: Power Train:Driveline:Driveshaft](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Bronco+Sport&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** An inadequate amount of lubricant in the rear drive unit may cause seizure of the rear drive axle. If the rear drive unit seizes, there may be a loss of drive and/or a loss of vehicle control, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will check the rear drive unit lubricant level. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V922000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Foundation Components:Disc:Caliper](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Bronco+Sport&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 135, "Light Vehicle Brake Systems." During certain situations, the driver may have to apply more brake pedal force, and the distance required to stop the vehicle may be extended, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the front brake pads, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V191000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Engine:Oil/Lubrication](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Bronco+Sport&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** An oil leak in the presence of an ignition source such as hot engine or exhaust components, can increase the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the oil separator for damage or oil leaks, and replace the oil separator and seals, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V859000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Fuel Injection System](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Bronco+Sport&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** A fuel leak in the presence of an ignition source such as hot engine or exhaust components, can increase the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the engine control software to include fuel injector leak detection and install a drain tube, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2021 Ford Bronco Sport is an appealing compact SUV with distinct styling and everyday usability, but used car buyers must approach with caution. Pay close attention to brake pedal responsiveness, potential fuel system issues, and transmission shifting behavior during a test drive. Verifying that all factory updates and repairs have been completed will help ensure a more dependable ownership experience.
