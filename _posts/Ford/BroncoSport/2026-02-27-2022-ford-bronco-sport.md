# 2022 Ford Bronco Sport Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The Ford Bronco Sport brings rugged styling and compact utility to the small SUV segment, but prospective used buyers should look closely at owner-reported mechanical and electrical concerns. While many owners enjoy its everyday drivability and capability, recurring complaints highlight potential trouble spots ranging from cooling system leaks and powertrain stumbles to electrical glitches and braking noises.

---

## Engine

### 1.5L EcoBoost 3-Cylinder

The 1.5L turbocharged EcoBoost 3-cylinder engine produces approximately 181 horsepower and 190 lb-ft of torque, offering lively daily performance. However, buyer reports indicate that monitoring the cooling system and potential fuel or drivability quirks is essential.

**Coolant Leaks and Water Pump Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Low coolant levels, overheating warnings, or visible puddles; some owners have reported repeat water pump failures.
- **Solution:** Inspect the cooling system for leaks and replace failing water pumps or hoses as necessary.

**Engine Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** Unexpected engine hesitation, sputtering, or complete stalling while driving or at idle.
- **Solution:** Diagnose fuel delivery and ignition systems, and update control modules if a software calibration issue is found.

### 2.0L EcoBoost 4-Cylinder

The larger 2.0L turbocharged 4-cylinder engine pushes output to around 250 horsepower and 277 lb-ft of torque, delivering a noticeably stronger acceleration experience. While generally robust, buyers should verify that any fuel system recalls or injector inspections have been completed.

**Fuel Injector Leaks and Odors**
- **Symptoms:** Strong fuel smells inside or outside the vehicle, rough running, or diagnostic trouble codes indicating fuel system faults.
- **Solution:** Inspect fuel injectors and associated lines, replacing leaking components under applicable safety campaigns or dealer service procedures.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

The standard 8-speed automatic transmission pairs with standard all-wheel drive across the lineup. Prospective buyers should thoroughly test-drive the vehicle to check for smooth engagement and gear changes.

**Shifting Delays and Power Loss**
- **Symptoms:** Hesitation when engaging gears, shuddering during low-speed acceleration, or failure to properly shift out of park.
- **Solution:** Perform a powertrain diagnostic check to update transmission control modules or service mechanical internal components if failure occurs.

### Rear Drive Unit and Differential

The all-wheel-drive system relies on a rear differential and drive unit that can experience premature wear or venting concerns. Buyers should listen closely for unusual grinding noises during a pre-purchase test drive.

**Rear Differential Noise and Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Grinding or moaning noises from the rear of the vehicle when backing up or turning at low angles.
- **Solution:** Inspect the rear differential and vent hose assembly; service or replace the rear drive unit according to technical service guidelines.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

The braking system generally provides confident stopping power, but owners have flagged persistent low-speed brake noises and rare booster failures.

**Brake Noise and Booster Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Loud squeaking, moaning noises when reversing, or unexpected loss of brake assist.
- **Solution:** Inspect brake pads, rotors, and the brake booster assembly; replace worn or defective hardware.

## Electrical

### Electrical System

Modern electronics govern much of the vehicle's cabin and safety features, though minor software glitches and battery draws have been reported by owners.

**Battery Drain and Warning Lights**
- **Symptoms:** Spurious warning lights on the dashboard, blank instrument clusters, or unexpected battery failure.
- **Solution:** Test the battery health and charging system, and ensure all relevant software recalls have been applied by a dealer.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V625000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump:Control/Drive Module](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Bronco+Sport&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** The fuel delivery module may leak inside the fuel tank, causing low fuel pressure which could result in an engine stall. An engine stall increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the fuel delivery module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V922000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Foundation Components:Disc:Caliper](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Bronco+Sport&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 135, "Light Vehicle Brake Systems." During certain situations, the driver may have to apply more brake pedal force, and the distance required to stop the vehicle may be extended, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the front brake pads, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V191000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Engine:Oil/Lubrication](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Bronco+Sport&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** An oil leak in the presence of an ignition source such as hot engine or exhaust components, can increase the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the oil separator for damage or oil leaks, and replace the oil separator and seals, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V686000: Exterior Lighting:Lighting Control Module](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Bronco+Sport&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 108, "Lamps, Reflective Devices, and Associated Equipment." Flickering parking lights can reduce visibility, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the LED Driver module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V787000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump:Control/Drive Module](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Bronco+Sport&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** The fuel pump control module (FPCM) bracket may not be secured to the fuel tank assembly, which can lead to FPCM failure and cause an engine stall. An engine stall can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the fuel tank, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V859000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Fuel Injection System](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Bronco+Sport&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** A fuel leak in the presence of an ignition source such as hot engine or exhaust components, can increase the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the engine control software to include fuel injector leak detection and install a drain tube, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V187000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Fuel Injection System](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Bronco+Sport&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** A fuel leak in the presence of an ignition source such as hot engine or exhaust components, can increase the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the engine control software to include fuel injector leak detection and install a drain tube, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V267000: Electrical System:Body Control Module/Bcm](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Bronco+Sport&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** An undetected low battery charge can result in a loss of electrical accessories such as hazard lights, or cause a loss of drive power, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will recalibrate the body control module and power train control module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

When shopping for a used Ford Bronco Sport, verifying maintenance records and checking for completed recall campaigns is crucial. Pay close attention to cooling system components, potential rear differential noises, and electrical health during your inspection to ensure a reliable ownership experience.
