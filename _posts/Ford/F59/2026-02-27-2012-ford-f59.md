# 2012 Ford F59

### Overview
The 2012 Ford F59 is a commercial chassis cab vehicle designed for vocational use, often serving as a base for various upfits like box trucks, utility vehicles, and RVs. Its robust construction and focus on utility mean that reliability data often centers on the powertrain and chassis components that bear significant loads. While specific model year issues are less commonly documented in consumer-facing reliability reports compared to passenger vehicles, the F59's reputation is built on its durability and ability to withstand demanding work environments. Maintenance and component longevity are heavily influenced by the specific application and operational intensity.

---


## Engine

### 6.8L Triton V10
**Spark Plug Blowout**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of power, rough idling, engine misfires, audible popping sound from engine.
- **Solution:** Requires re-threading of spark plug holes with inserts (e.g., Heli-Coil) or replacement of cylinder heads.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1500

**Intake Manifold Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Vacuum leaks, rough idle, check engine light, poor fuel economy.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the intake manifold gasket or the entire intake manifold.
- **Cost:** $300 - $700


### 5.4L Triton V8
**Camshaft Position Sensor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Stalling, rough idle, check engine light, difficulty starting.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the camshaft position sensor.
- **Cost:** $150 - $300

**Variable Valve Timing (VVT) System Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Rattling noise on startup, rough idle, loss of power, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of VVT solenoids, phasers, or timing chains.
- **Cost:** $600 - $1200


## Transmission

### 5R110W Automatic
**Torque Converter Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Slipping, shuddering, delayed engagement, overheating.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the torque converter and transmission fluid flush.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1800

**Solenoid Pack Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Harsh shifting, failure to shift into certain gears, transmission warning light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the transmission solenoid pack.
- **Cost:** $400 - $900


## Suspension

### Heavy-Duty Chassis
**Leaf Spring Breakage**
- **Symptoms:** Sagging rear end, uneven ride height, clunking noises over bumps.
- **Solution:** Replacement of broken leaf springs.
- **Cost:** $400 - $1000

**Ball Joint Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Clunking or popping noises when turning, uneven tire wear, steering looseness.
- **Solution:** Replacement of worn ball joints.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600


## Electrical

### Standard Electrical System
**Alternator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Dimming headlights, battery warning light, dead battery, electrical system malfunctions.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the alternator.
- **Cost:** $400 - $800

**Wiring Harness Corrosion/Damage**
- **Symptoms:** Intermittent electrical issues, non-functioning lights or accessories, starting problems.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of damaged wiring sections.
- **Cost:** $200 - $700

### Summary Verdict
The 2012 Ford F59, as a commercial chassis, exhibits reliability characteristics typical of heavy-duty vehicles. The primary concerns revolve around its robust powertrain options, particularly the V10 and V8 engines, where issues like spark plug blowout and VVT system problems can arise under heavy use. The 5R110W automatic transmission is generally durable but can experience torque converter or solenoid issues. Suspension components, especially leaf springs, are subject to wear due to load-bearing duties. Electrical systems, while generally sound, can be susceptible to alternator failure or wiring issues over time. Exterior and interior components are typically less problematic, reflecting the vehicle's utilitarian design. Regular maintenance and addressing issues promptly are crucial for maximizing the lifespan and reliability of the F59.
