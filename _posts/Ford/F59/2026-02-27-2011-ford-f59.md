# 2011 Ford F59

### Overview
The 2011 Ford F59 is a commercial chassis designed for vocational applications, often used for cutaway vans, shuttle buses, and delivery vehicles. Its robust construction and focus on utility mean that reliability data often centers on heavy-duty components and their performance under demanding conditions. While specific model year data can be scarce for such specialized vehicles, general trends for Ford's commercial chassis of this era suggest a focus on durability. Owners and fleet managers typically prioritize powertrain longevity and chassis integrity. This analysis will delve into reported issues across key systems, providing insights into potential weak points and areas of strength for the 2011 F59.

---


## Engine

### 6.8L Triton V10
**Intake Manifold Gasket Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Coolant leaks, engine overheating, rough idle.
- **Solution:** Replacement of intake manifold gaskets.
- **Cost:** $400 - $700

**Spark Plug Blow-Out**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of power, misfires, ticking noise from engine.
- **Solution:** Thread repair kits or replacement of cylinder head.
- **Cost:** $600 - $1200


### 5.4L Triton V8
**Camshaft Position Sensor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Check Engine Light, rough idle, stalling, difficulty starting.
- **Solution:** Replacement of camshaft position sensor.
- **Cost:** $150 - $300

**Exhaust Manifold Cracks**
- **Symptoms:** Ticking noise on startup, exhaust leak sound, Check Engine Light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of cracked exhaust manifold(s).
- **Cost:** $400 - $800


## Transmission

### 5R110W Automatic
**Torque Converter Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Slipping, shuddering, delayed engagement, overheating.
- **Solution:** Rebuild or replacement of torque converter and transmission fluid service.
- **Cost:** $1200 - $2500

**Solenoid Pack Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Harsh shifting, failure to shift into certain gears, transmission warning light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of transmission solenoid pack.
- **Cost:** $500 - $900


## Suspension

### Heavy-Duty Chassis
**Leaf Spring Breakage**
- **Symptoms:** Sagging rear end, uneven ride height, clunking noises.
- **Solution:** Replacement of broken leaf springs.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600 per spring

**Ball Joint Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Clunking or popping noises when turning, uneven tire wear, steering looseness.
- **Solution:** Replacement of worn ball joints.
- **Cost:** $300 - $500


## Electrical

### Standard Wiring Harness
**Alternator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Dimming lights, battery warning light, dead battery.
- **Solution:** Replacement of alternator.
- **Cost:** $400 - $700

**Fuse Box Corrosion**
- **Symptoms:** Intermittent electrical component failures, blown fuses.
- **Solution:** Cleaning or replacement of fuse box.
- **Cost:** $200 - $400


## Exterior

### Body Mounts
**Rubber Mount Deterioration**
- **Symptoms:** Increased vibration, rattling noises, potential for body shifting.
- **Solution:** Replacement of deteriorated body mount bushings.
- **Cost:** $200 - $400


## Interior

### HVAC System
**Blower Motor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** No airflow from vents, inconsistent fan speed.
- **Solution:** Replacement of blower motor or resistor.
- **Cost:** $200 - $400

### Summary Verdict
The 2011 Ford F59, as a commercial chassis, exhibits reliability patterns typical of heavy-duty vehicles. The Triton V8 and V10 engines, while generally robust, can experience issues like intake manifold leaks and spark plug blow-out (V10), or camshaft sensor and exhaust manifold problems (V8). The 5R110W transmission is prone to torque converter and solenoid pack issues under heavy use. Suspension components, particularly leaf springs and ball joints, require attention due to the vehicle's load-carrying nature. Electrical systems can be affected by alternator failures and fuse box corrosion. Exterior body mounts and interior HVAC blower motors are also noted areas for potential repair. Overall, the F59 is a workhorse, but consistent maintenance and awareness of these specific potential issues are crucial for long-term operational reliability.
