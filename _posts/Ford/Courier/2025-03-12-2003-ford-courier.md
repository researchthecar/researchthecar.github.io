Here's a comprehensive breakdown of common issues reported with the 2003 Ford Courier, formatted for your website.

The 2003 Ford Courier is a light commercial vehicle popular in Australia, New Zealand, and other markets. It was available in various body styles, including cab chassis, pickup, and van. While known for its reliability and practicality, certain issues have been reported by owners. The engine options include a 2.5L Petrol Inline-4 (approximately 86 kW / 115 hp and 206 Nm / 152 lb-ft of torque) and a 2.5L Diesel Inline-4 (approximately 63 kW / 84 hp and 197 Nm / 145 lb-ft of torque). People generally appreciate the Courier for its affordability, ease of maintenance, and utility. Common dislikes include relatively low power output, basic interior, and potential for rust in some climates.

**Engine**

**2.5L Petrol Inline-4**

*   **Rough Idle/Stalling**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Engine idles roughly, stalls at idle, or hesitates during acceleration.
    *   Solution: Clean or replace the idle air control (IAC) valve. Check for vacuum leaks.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $200+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Check Engine Light - Catalytic Converter Efficiency**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Check Engine Light illuminates with codes related to catalytic converter efficiency (e.g., P0420). May cause car to fail emissions testing.
    *   Solution: Replace the catalytic converter. Inspect for exhaust leaks upstream of the converter.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**2.5L Diesel Inline-4**

*   **Fuel Injector Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rough running, excessive smoke, poor fuel economy. Could stem from injectors that are clogged, leaking, or are faulty.
    *   Solution: Test and clean/replace faulty fuel injectors.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Turbocharger Failure (if equipped)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, excessive smoke, whining noise from the turbocharger.
    *   Solution: Replace the turbocharger. Investigate and address the cause of the failure (e.g., oil starvation).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**Manual Transmission (Applies to Both Engines)**

*   **Difficulty Shifting/Grinding Gears**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Difficulty engaging gears, grinding noises during shifting, especially between 2nd and 3rd gear.
    *   Solution: Inspect and replace worn synchronizers, clutch disc, or pressure plate. Check and adjust clutch cable.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Clutch Slipping**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Engine revs high while accelerating, but the vehicle does not accelerate proportionally.
    *   Solution: Replace the clutch disc, pressure plate, and throwout bearing.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Worn Suspension Bushings**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Clunking noises from the suspension, particularly when going over bumps. Poor handling and steering response.
    *   Solution: Replace worn control arm bushings, sway bar bushings, and shock absorber bushings.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Leaf Spring Sagging (Rear)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rear of the vehicle sits low. Reduced load-carrying capacity.
    *   Solution: Replace the leaf springs or add helper springs.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Alternator Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Battery warning light illuminates, dimming headlights, difficulty starting the engine, or a dead battery.
    *   Solution: Replace the alternator.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $400+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Starter Motor Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Clicking noise when trying to start the engine, or the engine fails to crank.
    *   Solution: Replace the starter motor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Rust**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rust forming on the body, especially in areas prone to moisture and road salt (e.g., wheel wells, rocker panels, lower doors).
    *   Solution: Repair rusted areas by cutting out the rust, welding in new metal, and repainting. Preventative measures include rustproofing treatments.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1000+ (depending on severity)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Dashboard Cracking**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Cracks appearing in the dashboard, particularly in hot climates.
    *   Solution: Replace the dashboard (expensive) or install a dashboard cover.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Broken Door Handles (Interior)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Interior door handles break, making it difficult to open the doors from the inside.
    *   Solution: Replace the broken door handle.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $20 - $100+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2003 Ford Courier, while generally reliable, can experience a range of issues common to vehicles of its age. Engine and transmission problems, particularly those related to fuel injectors, turbochargers (diesel), clutch wear, and synchronizers, can be moderately expensive to repair. Rust is a significant concern, especially in certain climates. Electrical issues like alternator and starter failures are fairly common and relatively inexpensive to fix. Overall, potential buyers should carefully inspect the vehicle for rust, listen for unusual noises from the engine and transmission, and have a mechanic perform a thorough pre-purchase inspection.
