# 2016 Ford Escape Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used compact SUV, the model year offers a versatile cabin and engaging driving dynamics, but buyers need to navigate several well-documented mechanical trouble spots. While the vehicle provides practical daily transportation, recurring owner complaints highlight significant engine reliability concerns and occasional steering and transmission hiccups. Careful inspection and verification of service history are strongly advised before purchasing.

---

## Engine

### 2.5L iVCT 4-Cylinder

This naturally aspirated 2.5-liter four-cylinder engine produces approximately 168 horsepower and 170 lb-ft of torque. It serves as the base powerplant and generally exhibits fewer complex mechanical failures than its turbocharged counterparts.

### 1.6L EcoBoost Turbocharged 4-Cylinder

The 1.6-liter EcoBoost turbocharged engine generates around 178 horsepower and 184 lb-ft of torque, balancing pep and efficiency. However, it is central to some of the most critical coolant intrusion and gasket complaints reported by owners.

**Coolant Intrusion and Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** White smoke from the tailpipe, low coolant levels, engine overheating, and a rough idle or knocking upon startup.
- **Solution:** Requires professional diagnosis; severe cases often necessitate cylinder head replacement or a completely new engine.
- **Cost:** $12,000 for replacement engine

**Engine Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** Unexpected engine stalling while driving, accompanied by illumination of warning lights on the dashboard.
- **Solution:** Diagnose and replace faulty fuel system or powertrain management components as required.

### 2.0L EcoBoost Turbocharged 4-Cylinder

The 2.0-liter EcoBoost delivers robust performance with 240 horsepower and 270 lb-ft of torque, making it ideal for towing and highway passing. Buyers should carefully check service records for signs of cooling system repairs.

**Head Gasket Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of coolant, engine overheating, white exhaust smoke, and external fluid leaks around the cylinder head.
- **Solution:** Replace the damaged head gasket and inspect the cylinder head for warping or cracking.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed SelectShift Automatic

Paired across front-wheel and all-wheel-drive configurations, this six-speed automatic transmission generally shifts smoothly when properly maintained. Some owners report shift linkage and slippage issues that require professional attention.

**Shifter or Park Defect**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle rolling while in park, failure to engage the correct gear, or remaining stuck in neutral when shifting.
- **Solution:** Inspect and replace worn shift linkage cables, bushings, or internal transmission control modules.

**Transmission Slipping and Hesitation**
- **Symptoms:** Delayed engagement, slipping gears while accelerating, clanging noises, and illumination of warning lights.
- **Solution:** Perform comprehensive fluid and diagnostic checks; rebuild or replace internal clutches and valve bodies if mechanical wear is present.

## Suspension

### Wheels and Hardware

Suspension and wheel components are generally robust, though factory hardware choices present frustrating maintenance roadblocks during roadside emergencies.

**Swollen Lug Nuts**
- **Symptoms:** Cap/aluminum covering on factory lug nuts distorts and swells, preventing standard tire irons or sockets from fitting during a flat tire change.
- **Solution:** Replace factory two-piece lug nuts with durable solid aftermarket alternatives.

## Steering

### Electric Power Steering

The electronic power-assist steering system provides light, responsive handling during normal operation. A subset of drivers has experienced sudden steering assist losses, posing significant drivability concerns.

**Steering Assist Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Sudden loss of power steering functionality, displaying a steering fault message and making the steering wheel extremely heavy to turn.
- **Solution:** Inspect the electronic power assist steering (EPAS) module and replace the steering gear assembly if electrical or mechanical failure occurs.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

- **Issue:** Either scenario increases the risk of a crash or injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the under hood shift bushing and add a protective cap over the shift cable bushing, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V695000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Escape&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** A rearview camera that fails to properly display an image can reduce the driver’s view behind the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the rearview camera as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V011000: Equipment:Electrical:Engine Block Heater](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Escape&modelYear=2016)**

- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the block heater, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

This model year appeals to buyers looking for a comfortable and responsive compact SUV, but it demands cautious evaluation. Particular attention should be paid to the condition of the EcoBoost engines, as coolant intrusion or head gasket failures can lead to extremely expensive repairs. Buyers should verify that all open recalls are addressed, test all electrical features and steering responsiveness, and seek a pre-purchase inspection from an independent mechanic.
