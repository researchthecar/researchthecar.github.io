# 2018 Ford Escape Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2018 Ford Escape is a popular compact crossover that offers nimble handling, an intuitive infotainment system, and a comfortable cabin. However, used car buyers should approach certain powertrain configurations with caution due to significant owner-reported engine and transmission issues.

---

## Engine

### 2.5L Duratec I4

A naturally aspirated 2.5-liter four-cylinder engine producing around 168 horsepower, serving as the base power plant for lower trims. It generally avoids the severe coolant intrusion issues plaguing the smaller turbocharged engines.

### 1.5L EcoBoost I4

A turbocharged 1.5-liter four-cylinder engine designed for improved fuel economy, though it carries substantial complaints regarding internal coolant leaks.

**Coolant Intrusion and Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Engine begins to misfire, runs rough, illuminates the check engine light, and may produce white smoke from the exhaust as coolant leaks into the cylinders.
- **Solution:** Requires professional diagnosis and potentially a complete engine block replacement.

### 2.0L EcoBoost I4

A more powerful turbocharged 2.0-liter four-cylinder engine offering brisk performance, but similarly affected by reported coolant intrusion problems.

**Coolant Intrusion**
- **Symptoms:** Sudden engine misfires, rough idling, illumination of warning lights, and rapid loss of coolant or acceleration.
- **Solution:** Inspection by a qualified mechanic; often necessitates major engine repairs or replacement.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed SelectShift Automatic

A six-speed automatic transmission paired across various trims, with some owner reports highlighting premature failure and driveability concerns.

**Transmission Failure and Hesitation**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle jerks during acceleration, displays 'Service Transmission Now' warnings, enters limp mode, or suffers sudden loss of motive power.
- **Solution:** Requires diagnostic testing by a dealer or transmission specialist, often leading to torque converter service or full transmission replacement.

## Suspension

### Wheel Lug Nuts

Manages wheel attachment and ride stability, with common cosmetic and practical complaints regarding factory-installed hardware.

**Swollen Lug Nuts**
- **Symptoms:** Factory lug nuts become swollen and distorted, preventing standard tire irons or sockets from fitting properly during a flat tire change.
- **Solution:** Replace the factory two-piece lug nuts with solid aftermarket alternatives.

## Steering

### Power Steering System

Handles directional control, though occasional electronic steering assist faults can temporarily increase steering effort.

**Steering Assist Fault**
- **Symptoms:** A 'Steering Assist Fault' warning message appears on the dash, and the steering wheel becomes exceptionally difficult to turn.
- **Solution:** Have the electronic power-assisted steering module and sensors diagnosed and repaired by a professional technician.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V803000: Air Bags:Side/Window](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Escape&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** The side curtain air bags on these vehicles have a component that may detach during deployment of the inflatable curtain air bag. If the component detaches during deployment, it may enter the passenger compartment and be a projectile within the vehicle cabin, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will replace the driver and passenger side curtain air bags, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V104000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Foundation Components:Hoses, Lines/Piping, And Fittings](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Escape&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** A brake fluid leak may lengthen the distance needed to stop the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the brake hoses, replacing any that are incorrect, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V413000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission:Gear Position Indication (Prndl)](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Escape&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** Either scenario increases the risk of a crash or injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the under hood shift bushing and add a protective cap over the shift cable bushing, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V011000: Equipment:Electrical:Engine Block Heater](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Escape&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** The engine block heater may crack and develop a coolant leak, causing it to short circuit when the block heater is plugged in. An electrical short circuit can increase the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the block heater, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

When shopping for a used 2018 Ford Escape, careful inspection of the service history and powertrain selection is critical. While base models equipped with the non-turbocharged engine tend to avoid the severe coolant intrusion troubles reported in EcoBoost variants, buyers should thoroughly test drive the vehicle to check for transmission hesitation, warning lights, and steering faults before purchasing.
