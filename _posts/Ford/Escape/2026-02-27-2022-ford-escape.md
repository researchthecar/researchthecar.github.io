# 2022 Ford Escape Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2022 Ford Escape offers a versatile compact crossover package with multiple powertrain choices, making it a popular choice for daily commuting and family use. While it provides good fuel economy and modern tech features, used-car buyers should carefully inspect specific areas—particularly powertrain performance, fuel system components, and recurring cabin accessory quirks. Reviewing maintenance records and confirming whether necessary recall services have been performed will help ensure a more dependable ownership experience.

---

## Engine

### 1.5L EcoBoost 3-Cylinder

A turbocharged 1.5-liter three-cylinder engine producing around 181 horsepower, serving as the standard powerplant for base trims. It balances fuel efficiency with adequate power for daily driving, though owners should monitor for potential fuel system and stalling concerns.

**Engine Stalling and Fuel Delivery Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have reported unexpected engine stalling while driving or at idle, sometimes accompanied by rough starting and fuel-related warning lights.
- **Solution:** Diagnostic testing by a qualified technician is required to check fuel injector operation, purge valves, and related engine management software.

### 2.0L EcoBoost 4-Cylinder

A larger turbocharged 2.0-liter four-cylinder engine generating approximately 250 horsepower, paired with standard all-wheel drive. It offers brisk acceleration and strong towing capacity for its class, though routine maintenance is crucial for longevity.

### 2.5L Hybrid / Plug-In Hybrid

A 2.5-liter four-cylinder hybrid setup paired with an electric motor to deliver excellent fuel economy and smooth urban driving dynamics. It is a preferred choice for buyers prioritizing efficiency and reduced fuel stops.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

An 8-speed automatic transmission paired with gas-powered models to optimize power delivery and highway efficiency. Proper fluid maintenance is essential to prevent premature wear and shifting harshness.

**Transmission Slipping and Hard Shifting**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers experience rough gear engagement, transmission shudder during low-speed operation, or delayed acceleration when attempting to speed up.
- **Solution:** Have a certified mechanic inspect transmission fluid condition, check for diagnostic trouble codes, and perform software updates or mechanical repairs as needed.

### eCVT Automatic Transmission

An electronically controlled continuously variable transmission utilized in Hybrid and Plug-In Hybrid models. It delivers seamless power transitions and maximizes overall hybrid efficiency.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

The hydraulic braking system responsible for stopping the vehicle safely under various driving conditions.

**Reduced Braking Power and Abnormal Noises**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers note abnormal grinding or sliding noises when applying the brakes, alongside reduced braking power requiring deeper pedal travel.
- **Solution:** Inspect brake pads, rotors, and hydraulic lines immediately to replace worn components and restore normal braking performance.

## Interior

### Panoramic Sunroof Shade

Cabin trim and overhead sunroof assemblies equipped on select models with upgraded glass roofs.

**Sunshade Sagging and Detachment**
- **Symptoms:** The panoramic sunroof fabric shade or internal support clips break or come unglued, causing the shade to sag into the cabin or block rear visibility.
- **Solution:** Repair or replace damaged clips, rails, or the entire shade assembly, keeping in mind that warranty coverage for trim pieces can vary.
- **Cost:** $1,400 - $1,700 for complete dealership repair if not covered by warranty

## Safety

### Driver Assistance Systems

Active safety and camera suites designed to assist with collision avoidance and driver awareness.

**Erroneous Emergency Braking**
- **Symptoms:** The automatic emergency braking system may erroneously engage while driving at highway speeds, accompanied by pre-collision warning messages.
- **Solution:** Have a dealer check sensor alignment, clear diagnostic codes, and apply any necessary software recalibrations.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V922000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Foundation Components:Disc:Caliper](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Escape&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 135, "Light Vehicle Brake Systems." During certain situations, the driver may have to apply more brake pedal force, and the distance required to stop the vehicle may be extended, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the front brake pads, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V149000: Electrical System:Propulsion System:Traction Battery](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Escape&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** A sudden loss of drive power increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the high voltage battery, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V191000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Engine:Oil/Lubrication](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Escape&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** An oil leak in the presence of an ignition source such as hot engine or exhaust components, can increase the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the oil separator for damage or oil leaks, and replace the oil separator and seals, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V484000: Engine And Engine Cooling](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Escape&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** In the event of an engine failure, engine oil and fuel vapor may be released into the engine compartment and accumulate near ignition sources such as hot engine or exhaust components, possibly resulting in an engine compartment fire. An engine compartment fire increases the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will modify the under engine shield and active grille shutter, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V859000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Fuel Injection System](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Escape&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** A fuel leak in the presence of an ignition source such as hot engine or exhaust components, can increase the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the engine control software to include fuel injector leak detection and install a drain tube, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V380000: Engine](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Escape&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** In the event of an engine failure, engine oil and fuel vapor may be released into the engine compartment and accumulate near ignition sources such as hot engine or exhaust components, possibly resulting in an engine compartment fire. An engine compartment fire increases the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the engine long-block in 2023 Escape and Corsair vehicles. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V187000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Fuel Injection System](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Escape&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** A fuel leak in the presence of an ignition source such as hot engine or exhaust components, can increase the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the engine control software to include fuel injector leak detection and install a drain tube, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V330000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission:Control Module (Tcm/Pcm/Tecm)](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Escape&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** The Hybrid Powertrain Control Module (HPCM) software may cause the vehicle to shift into neutral unexpectedly, causing a loss of drive power. A loss of drive power increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the HPCM software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2022 Ford Escape is a versatile compact SUV with appealing powertrain choices and modern features, but buyers should approach with informed caution. Pay close attention to transmission shift quality during a test drive, verify that all safety and fuel-related recall services have been completed, and inspect interior trim items like the panoramic sunroof shade. Prioritizing a well-documented maintenance history will help minimize the risk of unexpected repair bills.
