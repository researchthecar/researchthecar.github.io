# 2021 Ford Escape Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2021 Ford Escape is a compact crossover offering generous passenger room, agile handling, and a variety of efficient powertrain choices ranging from turbocharged three- and four-cylinder engines to hybrid and plug-in hybrid variants. However, prospective buyers should inspect vehicles carefully for mechanical and structural complaints reported by owners. Common areas of concern include brake noise, transmission shifting irregularities, and door structure alignment issues.

---

## Engine

### 1.5L EcoBoost 3-Cylinder

A turbocharged 1.5-liter three-cylinder engine producing around 181 horsepower, serving as the base powerplant for many trims. It offers competitive fuel economy but requires diligent monitoring of cooling system components.

**Coolant Leak**
- **Symptoms:** Low coolant warning messages, engine overheating, and white smoke or vapor from the engine bay.
- **Solution:** Inspect cooling system lines and components; replace leaking hoses, seals, or gaskets as needed.

### 2.0L EcoBoost 4-Cylinder

A punchy turbocharged 2.0-liter four-cylinder engine generating approximately 250 horsepower, paired with standard all-wheel drive on select trims for strong performance.

**Engine Stalling and Hesitation**
- **Symptoms:** Engine sputtering during acceleration, rough idle, jumpy RPMs, and potential unexpected stalling.
- **Solution:** Diagnose fuel delivery and ignition components, update powertrain control module software, or replace faulty torque converters.

### 2.5L Hybrid / Plug-In Hybrid

A 2.5-liter four-cylinder hybrid system focused on maximizing fuel efficiency with electric motor assistance, offering smooth power delivery for daily commuting.

## Transmission

### 8F35 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

An eight-speed automatic transmission paired with gas-powered EcoBoost engines, designed to balance highway efficiency with responsive gear selection.

**Transmission Shudder and Slipping**
- **Symptoms:** Severe slipping at speed, violent shuddering conditions, hard shifts, and bucking or jerking during low-speed driving.
- **Solution:** Reprogram or flash the powertrain control module, inspect transmission internals, or replace worn torque converters and transmission assemblies.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

Standard hydraulic disc braking system designed to bring the compact SUV to a controlled stop under normal driving conditions.

**Brake Noise and Failure Concerns**
- **Symptoms:** Loud screaming or abnormal noises when applying brakes in reverse or slow-motion traffic, alongside perceived pedal travel issues.
- **Solution:** Inspect brake pads, rotors, and calipers; clean or replace contaminated hardware and friction components.

## Exterior

### Door Structure and Panels

Exterior body panels and door structures responsible for passenger compartment integrity and weather sealing.

**Door and Fender Alignment**
- **Symptoms:** Misaligned door spot welds, panel gaps around fenders and hoods, and latch alignment concerns.
- **Solution:** Adjust panel brackets and hinges or repair structural weld points as required by a qualified body shop.

## Safety

### Seat Belts

Occupant restraint systems including seat belts, pre-tensioners, and associated warning indicators.

**Seat Belt Retractor and Warning Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Seat belt warning lights illuminating incorrectly or mechanical binding linked to temperature fluctuations.
- **Solution:** Inspect seat belt assemblies, sensors, and wiring harnesses; replace faulty latch mechanisms or retractors.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V869000: Electrical System:Propulsion System:Traction Battery](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Escape&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** A sudden loss of drive power increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** A dealer will replace the high voltage battery, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V625000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump:Control/Drive Module](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Escape&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The fuel delivery module may leak inside the fuel tank, causing low fuel pressure which could result in an engine stall. An engine stall increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the fuel delivery module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V922000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Foundation Components:Disc:Caliper](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Escape&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 135, "Light Vehicle Brake Systems." During certain situations, the driver may have to apply more brake pedal force, and the distance required to stop the vehicle may be extended, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the front brake pads, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V149000: Electrical System:Propulsion System:Traction Battery](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Escape&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** A sudden loss of drive power increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the high voltage battery, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V191000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Engine:Oil/Lubrication](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Escape&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** An oil leak in the presence of an ignition source such as hot engine or exhaust components, can increase the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the oil separator for damage or oil leaks, and replace the oil separator and seals, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V484000: Engine And Engine Cooling](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Escape&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** In the event of an engine failure, engine oil and fuel vapor may be released into the engine compartment and accumulate near ignition sources such as hot engine or exhaust components, possibly resulting in an engine compartment fire. An engine compartment fire increases the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will modify the under engine shield and active grille shutter, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V859000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Fuel Injection System](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Escape&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** A fuel leak in the presence of an ignition source such as hot engine or exhaust components, can increase the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the engine control software to include fuel injector leak detection and install a drain tube, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V380000: Engine](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Escape&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** In the event of an engine failure, engine oil and fuel vapor may be released into the engine compartment and accumulate near ignition sources such as hot engine or exhaust components, possibly resulting in an engine compartment fire. An engine compartment fire increases the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the engine long-block in 2023 Escape and Corsair vehicles. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2021 Ford Escape presents a practical choice for used-car shoppers seeking modern tech and good fuel economy, but it requires a thorough pre-purchase inspection. Buyers should pay close attention to transmission shift quality during a test drive and check for any history of engine cooling or brake noises. Verifying that all open recalls have been completed by a certified dealer is strongly recommended before finalizing a purchase.
