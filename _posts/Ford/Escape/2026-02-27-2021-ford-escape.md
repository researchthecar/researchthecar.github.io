# 2021 Ford Escape

### Overview
The 2021 Ford Escape offers a range of powertrain options, including efficient hybrid and plug-in hybrid variants alongside traditional gasoline engines. This generation of the Escape aims to balance fuel economy with practicality for everyday use. Reliability data for the 2021 model year indicates a generally solid performance, with some specific powertrain configurations showing a higher propensity for certain issues than others. Owners have reported a few recurring problems, primarily related to the powertrain and some electrical components, though widespread critical failures are not a dominant theme. Regular maintenance is key to ensuring the longevity of any vehicle, and the Escape is no exception.

### Summary Verdict
The 2021 Ford Escape presents a mixed reliability profile. The 1.5L EcoBoost engine has a notable issue with coolant ingress, which can be costly to repair. The 2.0L EcoBoost and the hybrid powertrains appear to be more robust. Transmission issues, particularly with the 8-speed automatic, can manifest as hesitation or rough shifting. Electrical gremlins, such as infotainment system glitches and battery drain, are also reported. Overall, while not plagued by widespread critical failures, potential buyers should be aware of the specific engine concerns and consider the hybrid variants for potentially better long-term reliability. Regular maintenance and prompt attention to any warning signs are crucial.
