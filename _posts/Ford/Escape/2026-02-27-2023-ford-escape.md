# 2023 Ford Escape Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2023 Ford Escape offers a modern compact SUV design with multiple powertrain choices, including traditional gas, hybrid, and plug-in hybrid options. However, used car buyers should carefully review owner-reported complaints regarding electrical glitches, screen blackouts, and occasional transmission or engine drivability concerns. While many features impress, verifying software updates and powertrain service history is strongly advised before purchasing.

---

## Engine

### 1.5L EcoBoost 3-Cylinder

A turbocharged 1.5-liter three-cylinder engine producing around 181 horsepower, serving as the standard power plant for lower trims. It offers competitive fuel economy but requires attentive maintenance to avoid potential driveability concerns.

**Engine Stalling and Power Loss**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have reported unexpected engine stalling or sudden loss of motive power while driving in traffic.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified mechanic inspect the engine management system, fuel delivery, and diagnostic codes.

### 2.0L EcoBoost 4-Cylinder

A punchy turbocharged 2.0-liter four-cylinder engine generating roughly 250 horsepower, standard on higher trims like the ST-Line Select and Platinum. It provides brisk acceleration and confident highway passing power.

**Exhaust Fumes and Acceleration Hesitation**
- **Symptoms:** Unusual exhaust odors entering the cabin and hesitation or loud noises during acceleration.
- **Solution:** Inspect the exhaust system, turbocharger components, and intake plumbing for leaks or failures.

### 2.5L Hybrid / Plug-In Hybrid

A 2.5-liter four-cylinder engine paired with an electric motor system delivering strong efficiency ratings. It represents a popular choice for commuters seeking reduced fuel consumption.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

An eight-speed automatic transmission mated to the gas engines. Buyers should thoroughly test-shift the transmission and check service records for any signs of premature mechanical wear.

**Violent Shifting and Internal Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Sudden violent shifts, loud banging noises, illuminated service gear shifter warnings, and eventual loss of drive capability.
- **Solution:** Check transmission fluid for metal fragments and consult a transmission specialist for rebuild or replacement if internal mechanical failure occurs.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

Standard disc braking system responsible for stopping duties. Any reported pedal softness or loss of assist demands prompt professional evaluation.

**Temporary Loss of Braking Assist**
- **Symptoms:** Brake pedal traveling uncomfortably close to the floor or temporary reduction in brake power assist.
- **Solution:** Inspect the brake hydraulic system, booster functionality, and brake fluid condition immediately.

## Electrical

### Infotainment and Instrument Cluster

Manages vehicle instrumentation, safety warnings, and the SYNC infotainment interface. Software stability and module reliability are critical for seamless daily operation.

**Blank Screen and Warning Lights**
- **Symptoms:** Dashboard displays, backup cameras, or infotainment touchscreens turning completely dark or triggering random warning lights.
- **Solution:** Check for available manufacturer software updates or have the accessory protocol interface module inspected by a dealer.

## Exterior

### Sunroof and Trim

Covers exterior glass, roof panels, and decorative trim pieces. Routine inspection helps prevent wind noise and loose trim detachments.

**Loose Roof Molding and Glass Breakage**
- **Symptoms:** Roof drip rail or sunroof molding coming loose due to adhesive failure, or unexpected sunroof glass shattering.
- **Solution:** Inspect exterior trim attachment points and ensure sunroof seals and moldings are securely fastened.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V859000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Fuel Injection System](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Escape&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** A fuel leak in the presence of an ignition source such as hot engine or exhaust components, can increase the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the engine control software to include fuel injector leak detection and install a drain tube, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V380000: Engine](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Escape&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** In the event of an engine failure, engine oil and fuel vapor may be released into the engine compartment and accumulate near ignition sources such as hot engine or exhaust components, possibly resulting in an engine compartment fire. An engine compartment fire increases the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the engine long-block in 2023 Escape and Corsair vehicles. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V506000: Electrical System: Instrument Cluster/Panel](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Escape&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 101, "Control and Displays." An inoperable instrument panel cannot show critical safety information, such as the speedometer or warning lights, which may increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the module or instrument panel as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V440000: Forward Collision Avoidance: Adaptive Cruise Control](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Escape&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** Misalignment of the radar module can result in the Adaptive Cruise Control (ACC) misclassifying an overhead object as an object in the vehicle's path, causing the vehicle to unexpectedly slow and/or stop. Other safety systems that depend on the radar model (Pre-Collision Assist, Forward Collision Warning, and Auto Emergency Braking) could have diminished or.
- **What to do:** Dealers will adjust the angle of the radar module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V634000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Fuel Injection System:Injectors](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Escape&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** A fuel leak in the presence of an ignition source increases the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will install new fuel injector O-rings and O-ring support discs, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V852000: Equipment:Other:Labels](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Escape&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirement Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 208, "Occupant Crash Protection." Without a label that shows the proper restraint usage guidelines, the occupants may have an increased risk of injury in the event of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 24V954000: Electrical System:Propulsion System:Traction Battery:Management System/Energy Control Module (Bms/Becm)](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Escape&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** A manufacturing defect in one or more of the high voltage battery cells may result in an internal short circuit and battery failure. Battery failure can cause a loss of drive power, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will perform a battery energy control module (BECM) software update, and replace the high voltage battery pack, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V315000: Back Over Prevention:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Escape&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** A software error may cause the rearview camera image to delay, freeze, or not display when the vehicle is in reverse. A frozen or missing rearview camera image can reduce the driver's view behind the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2023 Ford Escape is a versatile compact SUV with appealing engine choices and modern tech, but prospective buyers should proceed with due diligence. Pay close attention to transmission shift quality during a test drive, check for functioning infotainment screens, and verify that all open recalls and software updates have been properly addressed by a certified dealer.
