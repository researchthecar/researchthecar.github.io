# 2015 Ford Escape

### Overview
The 2015 Ford Escape is a compact SUV that offered a range of engine and transmission choices, aiming to balance performance with fuel efficiency. Generally, the 2015 model year is considered to have a decent reliability record, though like any vehicle, it has specific areas that have seen more attention from owners and mechanics. Common concerns tend to revolve around the powertrain, particularly with certain engine variants, and some electrical components. However, many owners report a positive ownership experience with routine maintenance being the primary requirement. This analysis focuses on documented technical issues beyond standard wear and tear.

---


## Engine

### 1.6L EcoBoost
**Coolant Control Valve Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Coolant leaks, overheating, engine warning light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the coolant control valve and associated seals.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600

**Turbocharger Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of power, whistling noises, increased oil consumption.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of the turbocharger assembly.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1500


### 2.0L EcoBoost
**Turbocharger Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of power, whistling noises, increased oil consumption.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of the turbocharger assembly.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1500

**Cooling System Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Coolant loss, overheating, visible leaks.
- **Solution:** Inspection and repair of hoses, radiator, water pump, or thermostat housing.
- **Cost:** $400 - $900

## Transmission

### 6F35 Automatic Transmission
**Harsh Shifting / Hesitation**
- **Symptoms:** Jerky gear changes, delayed engagement, slipping.
- **Solution:** Transmission fluid flush and filter replacement, software update, or internal transmission repair.
- **Cost:** $300 - $1200

**Transmission Fluid Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Leaking fluid, potential for overheating and damage.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of seals, transmission lines, or transmission pan.
- **Cost:** $200 - $700

### Summary Verdict
The 2015 Ford Escape presents a mixed reliability profile. The 1.6L and 2.0L EcoBoost engines have documented issues with coolant leaks and turbocharger problems, which can be costly to repair. The automatic transmission can also experience shifting issues and leaks. Electrical gremlins, particularly related to the Body Control Module and infotainment system, are also noted concerns. The 2.5L naturally aspirated engine and the standard chassis, exterior, and interior components generally fare better with no widespread reported issues. Prospective buyers should be aware of the potential for powertrain and electrical repairs, especially with the turbocharged engines.
