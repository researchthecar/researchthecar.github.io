# 2019 Ford Escape Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2019 Ford Escape offers practical utility, comfortable daily driving dynamics, and a range of engine choices that appeal to varied commuter needs. However, prospective buyers should carefully research powertrain history, as specific engine configurations are associated with notable durability concerns that can require extensive repairs. Reviewing service records and verifying vehicle maintenance before purchase is strongly recommended.

---

## Engine

### 1.5L EcoBoost 3-Cylinder / 4-Cylinder Turbo

The 1.5-liter EcoBoost engine is a turbocharged powerplant focused on fuel efficiency for everyday driving. While adequate for daily commutes, this configuration carries well-documented risks of internal coolant leakage that buyers must inspect thoroughly.

**Coolant Intrusion and Head Gasket Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Engine overheats, low coolant levels without an external leak, rough idle on startup, white exhaust smoke, and illuminated check engine lights.
- **Solution:** Requires professional engine diagnosis; severe cases demand cylinder head replacement or a complete engine assembly swap.
- **Cost:** $8,000 to $15,000

### 2.0L EcoBoost 4-Cylinder Turbo

The 2.0-liter EcoBoost engine delivers stronger horsepower and torque for enhanced highway passing performance and towing capability. It generally demonstrates fewer widespread internal coolant failures than smaller displacement options.

### 2.5L Duratec 4-Cylinder

The naturally aspirated 2.5-liter engine serves as a simpler, traditional baseline powertrain option focused on straightforward mechanical reliability. It lacks turbocharger complexity, making maintenance more predictable for budget-conscious owners.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

Equipped with a 6-speed automatic transmission across most trims, this drivetrain handles daily commuting duties with conventional shift intervals. Routine fluid changes are essential to maintain smooth operation over higher mileages.

**Transmission Slipping and Hard Shifting**
- **Symptoms:** Delayed engagement, sudden jerking or hard shifting between gears, and hesitation during acceleration.
- **Solution:** Have a certified transmission specialist inspect the unit for internal wear, update control modules, or perform fluid flushes and component rebuilds.

## Suspension

### Tires and Wheels

Suspension components absorb standard road imperfections adequately, though factory tire wear patterns should be carefully verified during a pre-purchase inspection.

**Premature Tread Separation**
- **Symptoms:** Sudden vehicle shaking, vibration at highway speeds, and visible tread separation along the tire edges.
- **Solution:** Inspect tire production dates and tread condition, replacing worn or separating tires immediately.

## Steering

### Electric Power Steering

The electric power steering system provides light, adjustable feedback at low speeds but relies heavily on stable electrical voltage from the vehicle's charging system.

**Steering Assist Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Sudden loss of power steering assist accompanied by warning messages on the instrument cluster, making the steering wheel heavy to turn.
- **Solution:** Inspect electrical charging systems and battery health, or replace the electronic power steering module as diagnosed by a technician.

## Exterior

### Exterior Lighting and Electronics

Electrical accessories generally function well, though specific assembly layouts can complicate straightforward maintenance tasks like replacing exterior bulbs.

**Headlight Bulb Access and Replacement Complexity**
- **Symptoms:** Burned-out headlights that are difficult to access for routine homeowner bulb replacement.
- **Solution:** Take the vehicle to a qualified service center or disassemble necessary front-end components to access the rear of the headlight housing.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V155000: Tires](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Escape&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** Over cured tires may develop a break in the sidewall, resulting in sudden air loss or belt edge separation which could lead to tread/belt loss. Either condition can cause a loss of vehicle control, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the tires, and replace them as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V413000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission:Gear Position Indication (Prndl)](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Escape&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** Either scenario increases the risk of a crash or injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the under hood shift bushing and add a protective cap over the shift cable bushing, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V011000: Equipment:Electrical:Engine Block Heater](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Escape&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** The engine block heater may crack and develop a coolant leak, causing it to short circuit when the block heater is plugged in. An electrical short circuit can increase the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the block heater, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2019 Ford Escape can be an appealing, versatile compact SUV for everyday driving, provided you inspect it thoroughly before purchase. Buyers should exercise extreme caution regarding potential engine coolant intrusion issues on smaller EcoBoost powerplants, which can lead to catastrophic failure. Always prioritize models with documented service histories, and consider getting a professional pre-purchase inspection to verify engine and transmission health.
