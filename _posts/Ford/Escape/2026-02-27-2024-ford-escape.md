# 2024 Ford Escape Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2024 Ford Escape, buyers will find a modern compact SUV offering a range of efficient powertrain choices, versatile cargo space, and contemporary tech features. However, owner reports and initial feedback point to potential growing pains, particularly concerning sudden engine stalling, electrical glitches, and component-specific issues that prospective owners should inspect closely.

---

## Engine

### 1.5L EcoBoost 3-Cylinder

The base 1.5-liter turbocharged three-cylinder engine produces approximately 181 horsepower and 190 lb-ft of torque, providing adequate power for daily commuting while prioritizing fuel efficiency. Buyers should note that while generally responsive, early owner feedback highlights occasional reliability concerns regarding drivability.

### 2.0L EcoBoost 4-Cylinder

The punchy 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine delivers a robust 250 horsepower and 280 lb-ft of torque, offering lively performance and confident towing capability. Buyers should verify proper maintenance history and check for any outstanding powertrain service bulletins.

**Engine Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may unexpectedly stall or lose power when coming to a stop or attempting to pull into intersections.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified technician inspect the powertrain control modules, fuel system, and associated sensors for faults or software updates.

**Engine Misfire**
- **Symptoms:** Illuminated check engine light accompanied by rough running, hesitation, and engine misfire codes during acceleration.
- **Solution:** Diagnose ignition components, fuel injectors, and update engine management software as needed.

### 2.5L Hybrid / Plug-in Hybrid

The 2.5-liter hybrid and plug-in hybrid options pair a four-cylinder engine with an electric motor to deliver smooth power delivery and impressive fuel economy ratings. These electrified variants represent solid choices for efficiency-focused buyers, though comprehensive pre-purchase electrical inspections are recommended.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 8-speed conventional automatic transmission is paired with the EcoBoost engine options, delivering seamless gear changes and optimized highway efficiency. Routine fluid inspections are recommended to ensure long-term durability.

### CVT Hybrid Transmission

The electronically controlled continuously variable transmission works in tandem with the hybrid powertrains to maximize energy efficiency. It provides smooth, gearless operation tailored for city and suburban driving.

## Electrical

### Electrical System & Infotainment

The vehicle relies heavily on complex digital displays and electronic control modules to manage comfort and safety systems, though early models show susceptibility to screen blackouts and transient warnings.

**Instrument and Infotainment Screen Failures**
- **Symptoms:** The digital dashboard or central infotainment display may go completely blank, freeze, or display warning alerts intermittently.
- **Solution:** Perform software updates or replace faulty display modules as prescribed by manufacturer service bulletins.

## Safety

### Air Bag and Restraint System

Occupant protection features are comprehensive, but intermittent warning indicators suggest that prospective buyers should verify that all restraint sensors are fully operational.

**Air Bag Warning Light**
- **Symptoms:** Illumination of the airbag warning indicator on the instrument cluster, suggesting a fault within the supplemental restraint system.
- **Solution:** Scan the restraint control module for diagnostic trouble codes and inspect seat belt pretensioners and wiring harnesses.

### Forward Collision & Driver Assist

Advanced driver assistance features enhance safety on the road, though occasional sensor misreads and warning lights have been reported by vehicle owners.

**Electrical Warning Lights and Assist Faults**
- **Symptoms:** Sudden flashing of electrical warning lights accompanied by temporary fault messages for adaptive cruise control and collision avoidance systems.
- **Solution:** Inspect camera and radar sensors for obstructions, and check for software calibration updates at a certified dealership.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V440000: Forward Collision Avoidance: Adaptive Cruise Control](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Escape&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** Misalignment of the radar module can result in the Adaptive Cruise Control (ACC) misclassifying an overhead object as an object in the vehicle's path, causing the vehicle to unexpectedly slow and/or stop. Other safety systems that depend on the radar model (Pre-Collision Assist, Forward Collision Warning, and Auto Emergency Braking) could have diminished or.
- **What to do:** Dealers will adjust the angle of the radar module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V634000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Fuel Injection System:Injectors](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Escape&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** A fuel leak in the presence of an ignition source increases the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will install new fuel injector O-rings and O-ring support discs, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V852000: Equipment:Other:Labels](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Escape&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirement Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 208, "Occupant Crash Protection." Without a label that shows the proper restraint usage guidelines, the occupants may have an increased risk of injury in the event of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 24V850000: Electrical System: Instrument Cluster/Panel](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Escape&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** The passenger side instrument panel cover may have been manufactured incorrectly, which can cause the air bag not to deploy as intended. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 208, "Occupant Crash Protection." During air bag deployment, the air bag cover may break and project fragments.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the passenger side instrument panel cover, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V954000: Electrical System:Propulsion System:Traction Battery:Management System/Energy Control Module (Bms/Becm)](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Escape&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** A manufacturing defect in one or more of the high voltage battery cells may result in an internal short circuit and battery failure. Battery failure can cause a loss of drive power, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will perform a battery energy control module (BECM) software update, and replace the high voltage battery pack, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V315000: Back Over Prevention:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Escape&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** A software error may cause the rearview camera image to delay, freeze, or not display when the vehicle is in reverse. A frozen or missing rearview camera image can reduce the driver's view behind the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 25V343000: Electrical System](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Escape&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** An overheated engine block heater can increase the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the block heater element and inspect the electrical cord, replacing it if necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V372000: Engine](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Escape&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** An oil leak can result in loss of drive power, increasing the risk of a crash. In addition, an oil leak in the presence of an ignition source such as hot engine or exhaust components, can increase the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the cylinder head assembly as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2024 Ford Escape presents a compelling package with versatile interior space, strong engine choices, and modern amenities. However, prospective used car buyers should perform a thorough test drive and pre-purchase inspection to rule out potential engine stalling tendencies and minor electrical or infotainment glitches.
