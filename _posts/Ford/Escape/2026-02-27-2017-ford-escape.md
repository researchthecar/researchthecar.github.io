# 2017 Ford Escape Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2017 Ford Escape is a versatile compact crossover that remains popular on the used market for its comfortable cabin, sharp handling, and user-friendly technology. Available with three different engine options and a six-speed automatic transmission, it offers configurations suited for various driving preferences. However, prospective buyers should navigate this model year with care, as owner complaints point to a few highly significant mechanical vulnerabilities, particularly regarding the turbocharged engines and transmission performance. While these reported issues represent the experiences of specific owners and do not mean every used Escape will fail, understanding these common patterns is essential for finding a reliable example.

---

## Engine

### 1.5L EcoBoost Turbocharged I4

The mainstream 1.5-liter turbocharged engine produces approximately 179 horsepower and 177 lb-ft of torque. While it is highly common in the SE and SEL trims and offers good fuel economy, it is widely scrutinized on the used market due to frequent reports of internal coolant leaks.

**Coolant Intrusion and Internal Engine Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Symptoms include white smoke from the tailpipe on startup, a rapidly dropping coolant level without any visible external leaks, engine misfires (often triggering a check engine light), rough idling, and severe shaking or sputtering.
- **Solution:** Often requires a complete engine block replacement. Ford has addressed this issue in Technical Service Bulletin (TSB) 22-2229 with a redesigned engine block, but repairs outside of warranty are typically paid out-of-pocket by the owner.
- **Cost:** $8,000 to $12,000

### 2.0L EcoBoost Turbocharged I4

The optional 2.0-liter turbocharged engine delivers a robust 245 horsepower and 275 lb-ft of torque, providing excellent acceleration and moderate towing capability. However, it shares the same internal coolant intrusion vulnerability found in the smaller EcoBoost engine of this era.

**Coolant Leak into Cylinder Bores**
- **Symptoms:** Owners report cylinder misfires (commonly cylinders 1 and 4), engine shaking, disappearing coolant from the reservoir, and check engine lights.
- **Solution:** Typically requires a complete engine replacement. Dealerships routinely cite TSB 22-2229 as the guidance for this failure.
- **Cost:** Approximately $11,000

### 2.5L Duratec naturally aspirated I4

The base 2.5-liter naturally aspirated engine produces 168 horsepower and 170 lb-ft of torque, sending power exclusively to the front wheels. It is widely considered the most durable and trouble-free engine option in the 2017 lineup, as it lacks turbochargers and the associated design vulnerabilities of the EcoBoost models.

### General / multiple configurations

**Evaporative Purge Valve Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Difficulty restarting the engine immediately after refueling, stalling at low speeds or at stops, and an illuminated check engine light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the evaporative emissions purge valve and associated canister components.

**Intermittent No-Start and Diagnostic Fault Messages**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may intermittently refuse to crank (powering only accessory lights) or display warning messages such as 'faulty engine service immediately' alongside sensor faults. In rare instances, a sudden 'clunk' and immediate loss of power can occur at highway speeds.
- **Solution:** Dealers and independent mechanics often struggle to replicate the issue as it may not store permanent diagnostic codes. Inspection of the main wiring harness and sensor connectors is recommended.

## Transmission

### 6F35 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 6F35 six-speed automatic transmission is paired with all engine configurations across both front-wheel and all-wheel-drive models. While it shifting smoothly when properly maintained, it is a frequent source of wear-related complaints as the vehicle accumulates mileage.

**Torque Converter Shudder and Internal Mechanical Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Severe shaking or shuddering under braking loads or at idle, hard or delayed gear shifts, unexpected engine stalling when trying to accelerate from a stop, or a sudden loss of propulsion in active traffic.
- **Solution:** Often requires a complete replacement of the transmission assembly or the torque converter. Keeping up with regular transmission fluid service can help, but structural degradation usually demands mechanical rebuilding.
- **Cost:** Approximately $4,000

## Suspension

### Standard Chassis Components

**Swollen OEM Lug Nuts**
- **Symptoms:** The factory-installed two-piece chrome-capped lug nuts swell and deform over time, making it impossible to remove them using a standard roadside lug wrench during a tire change.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the swollen factory lug nuts with high-quality, solid one-piece aftermarket steel lug nuts.
- **Cost:** $267 to $284

**Premature Tire Cracking**
- **Symptoms:** Visible cracks forming within the tread grooves and along the outer sidewalls of the tires, even on relatively new rubber.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the affected tires and a full wheel alignment check.

## Steering

### Steering Rack and Pinion

**Loss of Power Steering Assist and Mechanical Noise**
- **Symptoms:** Loud popping, clunking, or grinding sounds when turning the wheel, steering pulling heavily to one side, or a sudden failure of the electronic steering assist that makes the vehicle difficult to control.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the electronic steering rack and pinion assembly.

## Exterior

### Exterior Lighting and Electronic Controls

**Frequent Low-Beam Headlight Burnout**
- **Symptoms:** Low-beam headlight bulbs fail prematurely and repeatedly, sometimes requiring replacement every few months.
- **Solution:** Inspection of the headlight wiring harness and connectors for resistance or voltage spikes, followed by the installation of heavy-duty replacement bulbs.

**Water Accumulation in Taillight Assemblies**
- **Symptoms:** Moisture and standing water visible inside the taillight housings after rain or a car wash, which can dim the lights or short out the bulbs and hatch release electronics.
- **Solution:** Resealing the taillight gaskets or replacing the assemblies with updated parts featuring improved seals.

**Auto Start-Stop Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** When coming to a stop, the vehicle may stall or present a warning message on the dashboard instructing the driver to shift to Park and manually restart the engine.
- **Solution:** Requires checking the auxiliary battery health, starter condition, or updating the powertrain control module (PCM) software.

### Paint and Body Structure

**Paint Peeling and Flaking**
- **Symptoms:** Paint bubbling, blistering, and peeling away in large sections, particularly along the front windshield columns (A-pillars), roof, and rear tailgate, exposing bare metal.
- **Solution:** Sanding, priming, and repainting the affected body panels to prevent corrosion.

**Cabin and Roof Water Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Water leaking through the headliner, pooling in the spare tire well, or dampening the carpets after heavy rain.
- **Solution:** Resealing the roof rack mounting points, clearing out clogged sunroof drain tubes, or replacing compromised door and hatch weatherstripping.

## Safety

### Safety Restraints and Driving Aids

**Seat Belt Retraction Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The front seat belts fail to retract when unbuckled or fail to lock securely during normal extension or sudden braking.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the seat belt pretensioner and retractor assembly.
- **Cost:** Up to $800

**Airbag Trim Separation and Backup Camera Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The passenger-side dashboard trim separating around the airbag deployment area, or the backup camera display showing lines, a blue/black screen, or a 'Camera Not Available' message.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the camera module or securing the dashboard trim panels.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V617000: Visibility:Power Window Devices And Controls](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Escape&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 118, "Power-Operated Window Systems." If a window closes on a body part, it can increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Ford has notified owners, and dealers will update the power window operating system software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V413000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission:Gear Position Indication (Prndl)](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Escape&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** Either scenario increases the risk of a crash or injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the under hood shift bushing and add a protective cap over the shift cable bushing, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V011000: Equipment:Electrical:Engine Block Heater](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Escape&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** The engine block heater may crack and develop a coolant leak, causing it to short circuit when the block heater is plugged in. An electrical short circuit can increase the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the block heater, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V374000: Visibility:Power Window Devices And Controls](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Escape&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 118, "Power-Operated Window Systems." A closing window that does not reverse direction as expected can pinch a driver or passenger before retracting, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the power window operating system software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2017 Ford Escape is a highly functional and modern compact SUV, but it represents a high-risk purchase if bought without a detailed service history. The widespread reports of internal coolant leaks on the 1.5-liter and 2.0-liter EcoBoost engines, coupled with transmission and torque converter vulnerabilities, mean used-car buyers must shop carefully. For those who want the simplest, most reliable experience, finding an Escape with the naturally aspirated 2.5-liter engine is highly recommended, as it bypasses the design flaws of the EcoBoost powerplants entirely. If you prefer the power of a turbocharged model, always verify if the engine block has already been replaced under warranty or if a cooling system pressure test shows no signs of internal leakage. Additionally, check for transmission shifting hesitation during a test drive, and inspect the windshield pillars and spare tire well for paint damage and water entry.
