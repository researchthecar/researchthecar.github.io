# 2016 Ford Focus Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used compact car, the model brings agile handling, efficient engine choices, and a comfortable ride. However, prospective buyers should approach with caution due to widely documented mechanical concerns—particularly involving its automatic dual-clutch transmission system. Thoroughly evaluating the vehicle's maintenance history and confirming how the powertrain behaves during a test drive are essential steps before making a purchase.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Ti-VCT I4

This naturally aspirated 2.0-liter four-cylinder engine produces 160 horsepower and 146 lb-ft of torque, offering responsive daily driving dynamics and solid baseline efficiency. It is generally robust mechanically, though peripheral fuel system and electronic sensors can occasionally trigger drivability concerns.

**Engine Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicles may experience unexpected engine stalling while driving or coming to a stop, often accompanied by illuminated warning lights.
- **Solution:** Diagnose fault codes and inspect the fuel system or related powertrain sensors; replace faulty components as necessary.

**Canister Purge Valve Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Illuminated check engine lights and difficulty starting after refueling due to a stuck or failing purge valve.
- **Solution:** Replace the canister purge valve and inspect the EVAP system lines.

### 1.0L EcoBoost Turbo I4

A compact 1.0-liter turbocharged three-cylinder engine generating 123 horsepower and 125 lb-ft of torque, designed for maximum fuel economy. While generally peppy in city traffic, buyers should closely monitor cooling system integrity and turbo maintenance.

### 2.0L EcoBoost Turbo I4

High-output 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine reserved for performance variants, putting out 252 horsepower and 270 lb-ft of torque. It delivers exhilarating acceleration and sporty capability, though it demands rigorous maintenance to prevent long-term turbocharger stress.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed PowerShift Dual-Clutch Automatic

This automated manual transmission pairs with mainstream front-wheel-drive trims to maximize highway efficiency. Unfortunately, it is well-known for recurring shuddering, clutch wear, and drivability complaints.

**Transmission Shudder and Clutch Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Noticeable shaking or shuddering during acceleration, harsh gear engagement, delayed shifting, and ultimate internal clutch component failure.
- **Solution:** Inspect the clutch actuators and plates; replacement of clutch assemblies or transmission control modules is frequently required by professional technicians.

**Transmission Slipping and Control Module Faults**
- **Symptoms:** Unexpected skipping of gears while driving at speed, loss of motive power, or failure of the transmission control module to correctly recognize gear selection.
- **Solution:** Reprogram or replace the transmission control module and inspect internal hydraulic or electrical components for fluid leaks and wear.

### 5-Speed / 6-Speed Manual

Traditional manual transmissions paired with select engine options. They offer direct gear control and bypass the reliability headaches associated with the automated dual-clutch units.

## Suspension

### Wheels and Lug Nuts

Wheel hardware and suspension components generally hold up well, though standard maintenance items like lug nuts require attention.

**Swollen Lug Nuts**
- **Symptoms:** Factory two-piece lug nuts swell over time, making it impossible to fit standard socket wrenches over them during tire changes.
- **Solution:** Replace factory lug nuts with durable one-piece aftermarket alternatives.

## Steering

### Electric Power Steering

Provides speed-sensitive steering assistance, though intermittent electronic faults can cause unexpected heavy steering resistance.

**Steering Assist Fault**
- **Symptoms:** Dashboard warnings indicating a steering assist fault, accompanied by a sudden loss of power steering or extremely heavy steering effort.
- **Solution:** Inspect electronic steering control modules and wiring harnesses; replace the steering gear assembly if internal motor failure is diagnosed.

## Electrical

### Audio and Infotainment Electronics

Handles interior entertainment and cabin connectivity, susceptible to occasional audio module hardware failures.

**Audio System Popping and Screeching**
- **Symptoms:** Radio functionality cutting out randomly and emitting loud, sudden popping or screeching noises from the vehicle speakers.
- **Solution:** Replace the audio control module (ACM) to restore normal radio operation and eliminate electrical audio feedback.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V735000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Storage](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Focus&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** The excessive vaccuum may result in an engine stall while driving, without warning and without the ability to restart the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will reprogram the Powertrain Control Module. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V845000: Power Train:Manual Transmission](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Focus&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** A transmission fluid leak in the presence of an ignition source such as hot engine or exhaust components can increase the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will update vehicles with software that detects and prevents prolonged clutch slip, and will replace the clutch, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V052000: Structure:Body](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Focus&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** Improperly welded apron joints may reduce the front-end structural integrity of the vehicle, increasing the risk of injury in the event of a crash.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the apron joints, repairing the vehicle, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V169000: Power Train:Manual Transmission](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Focus&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** A transmission fluid leak in the presence of an ignition source such as hot engine or exhaust components can increase the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will update the Focus vehicles with software that detects and prevents prolonged clutch slip, and will replace the clutch, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V698000: Latches/Locks/Linkages:Hatchback/Liftgate:Lock](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Focus&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 206, "Door Locks and Door Retention Components." With only a single press needed, the driver may accidently unlatch the hatchback, increasing the risk of injury to any unsecured passengers in the back.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will reprogram the Body Control Module with updated software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V905000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Engine:Oil/Lubrication:Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Focus&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** A loss of oil pressure can result in an engine stall and a loss of power braking assist, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the oil pump tensioner assembly and oil pump drive belt, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V011000: Equipment:Electrical:Engine Block Heater](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Focus&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** The engine block heater may crack and develop a coolant leak, causing it to short circuit when the block heater is plugged in. An electrical short circuit can increase the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the block heater, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

When considering a used model, inspect the vehicle's service records closely, especially to verify if any automatic transmission clutch repairs or control module replacements have been performed. Opting for a manual transmission variant can bypass the most common drivetrain headaches. Always complete an extended test drive to check for any hesitation, shuddering, or electrical warnings before finalizing a purchase.
