# 2018 Ford Focus Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used compact car, the model brings a mix of nimble handling, efficient engine options, and notable powertrain concerns that buyers should navigate with care. While these vehicles offer attractive styling and daily usability, persistent issues with the dual-clutch automatic transmission and related control modules require careful inspection before purchase. Prospective owners should thoroughly evaluate service histories, particularly regarding known transmission and fuel system repairs.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Ti-VCT GDI I-4 Engine

The standard naturally aspirated 2.0-liter four-cylinder engine produces 160 horsepower and 146 lb-ft of torque, providing adequate daily performance for standard commutes.

**Engine Stalling and Purge Valve Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicles may experience unexpected engine stalling while driving or difficulty starting after refueling, often triggered by a failing canister purge valve.
- **Solution:** Replace the faulty canister purge valve and associated fuel vapor lines.

### 1.0L EcoBoost Turbocharged I-3 Engine

The available 1.0-liter turbocharged three-cylinder engine delivers 123 horsepower and strong fuel economy, though premium maintenance is essential for longevity.

## Transmission

### PowerShift 6-Speed Automatic Dual-Clutch Transmission

The automated manual dual-clutch transmission delivers direct power transfer and efficiency, but its history of reliability complaints makes careful pre-purchase testing vital.

**Transmission Clutch Failure and Shuddering**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers frequently report severe shuddering, shaking during acceleration from a stop, delayed gear engagement, and premature clutch wear.
- **Solution:** Replace the dual-clutch assembly and update or replace the transmission control module as required.

**Transmission Control Module (TCM) Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Sudden loss of motive power, abrupt slowing in traffic, or failure to shift into reverse or drive.
- **Solution:** Diagnose the electronic control unit and replace the transmission control module.

### 5-Speed Manual Transmission

The traditional manual transmission offers a reliable and engaging driving experience without the complex automated clutch shudder issues found in automatic variants.

## Steering

### Electric Power Steering System

The electric power assist steering system provides light, responsive handling during normal operation.

**Power Steering Loss and Rack Stiffening**
- **Symptoms:** The steering wheel may suddenly become extremely stiff, difficult to turn, or lose power steering assistance entirely while driving at low speeds.
- **Solution:** Inspect the steering rack for cracking or mechanical damage and replace the steering gear assembly if necessary.

## Electrical

### Infotainment and Backup Camera

The interior electrical architecture manages cabin convenience features and infotainment connectivity.

**Sync Display and Backup Camera Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The dashboard infotainment display, touchscreen, and rearview camera may go completely dark or work only intermittently.
- **Solution:** Diagnose the infotainment software, wiring harness, or replace the camera module as needed.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V735000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Storage](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Focus&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** The excessive vaccuum may result in an engine stall while driving, without warning and without the ability to restart the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will reprogram the Powertrain Control Module. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V845000: Power Train:Manual Transmission](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Focus&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** A transmission fluid leak in the presence of an ignition source such as hot engine or exhaust components can increase the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will update vehicles with software that detects and prevents prolonged clutch slip, and will replace the clutch, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V905000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Engine:Oil/Lubrication:Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Focus&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** A loss of oil pressure can result in an engine stall and a loss of power braking assist, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the oil pump tensioner assembly and oil pump drive belt, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V011000: Equipment:Electrical:Engine Block Heater](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Focus&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** The engine block heater may crack and develop a coolant leak, causing it to short circuit when the block heater is plugged in. An electrical short circuit can increase the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the block heater, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V012000: Equipment:Electrical:Engine Block Heater](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Focus&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** The engine block heater may crack and develop a coolant leak, causing it to short circuit when the block heater is plugged in. An electrical short circuit can increase the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the block heater, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V369000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Storage:Evaporative Emissions:Canister](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Focus&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** The Canister Purge Valve (CPV) may malfunction, causing the engine to stall unexpectedly while driving. An engine that stalls while driving increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the powertrain control module (PCM) software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V376000: Power Train:Manual Transmission](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Focus&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** A transmission fluid leak in the presence of an ignition source such as hot engine or exhaust components can increase the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

When considering a used model, inspecting the transmission behavior during a test drive is crucial. Prioritize manual transmission models if you want to avoid automated clutch issues, or ensure any automatic candidate has a fully documented service history regarding clutch and control module repairs.
