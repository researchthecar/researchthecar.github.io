# 2014 Ford Focus Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2014 Ford Focus is a stylish, fuel-efficient compact car that offers sharp driving dynamics and a modern interior. While highly popular in the used-car market, its reputation is strongly divided. Potential buyers must be cautious, as some configurations suffer from widely documented mechanical issues, while others remain exceptionally reliable commuter cars. This summary highlights owner-reported experiences to help you find a dependable example.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Ti-VCT GDI I-4

This is the standard 2.0-liter four-cylinder engine found on most Focus trims, producing 160 horsepower and 146 lb-ft of torque. Mechanically, the engine itself is relatively durable, but its overall reliability is frequently overshadowed by issues with the automatic transmission and fuel purge systems. It is best enjoyed when paired with a manual gearbox.

**Canister Purge Valve Failure and Fuel Tank Damage**
- **Symptoms:** A noticeable smell of gasoline around the vehicle, abnormal shaking, and engine stalling shortly after refueling. Over time, a stuck-open purge valve can create a strong vacuum effect that physically deforms or ruins the fuel tank.
- **Solution:** Replace the canister purge valve. If the fuel tank has collapsed or sustained damage due to excessive vacuum, the entire fuel tank must be replaced.
- **Cost:** Approximately $414 for the purge valve replacement, and around $2,088 for a full fuel tank replacement and powertrain control module reset.

**Engine Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** The engine may stall unexpectedly while driving, halting at intersections, or during deceleration.
- **Solution:** Diagnose and repair the throttle body, fuel delivery system, or reset associated powertrain control module software.

### 2.0L EcoBoost Turbo I-4

Exclusive to the high-performance Focus ST, this turbocharged engine pumps out 252 horsepower and 270 lb-ft of torque. It is highly regarded for its robust construction, engaging power delivery, and excellent tuning potential.

### Electric Drive Motor

Powering the Focus Electric, this zero-emissions motor produces 143 horsepower. While smooth and virtually maintenance-free, buyers should be aware that the early EV battery technology has a very limited range and high replacement costs.

**High-Voltage Battery and Charger Failures**
- **Symptoms:** A dashboard warning advising the driver to stop immediately, followed by a sudden loss of motive power or a refusal to restart.
- **Solution:** Diagnose and replace the high-voltage battery pack or onboard charger components.
- **Cost:** Battery replacement and electrical system overhaul can cost up to $15,000 out of warranty.

## Transmission

### 6-speed PowerShift Dual-Clutch Automatic (DPS6)

This dual-clutch automated manual transmission is infamous for its lack of refinement and exceptionally high failure rate. It is the primary source of owner dissatisfaction and should be approached with extreme caution.

**Dual-Clutch Shuddering, Slipping, and Clutch Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Severe shuddering or vibration when accelerating from a stop, delayed acceleration, gear slipping, and an inability to shift into reverse or past second gear.
- **Solution:** Replace the clutch assembly, actuators, and associated seals, followed by transmission programming adaptive learning.
- **Cost:** Actuator replacement costs about $658, while a complete clutch, fork, and seal rebuild generally runs between $3,300 and $4,000.

**Transmission Control Module (TCM) Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Sudden loss of motive power while driving, engine no-start conditions, gears failing to engage, and "Transmission Fault Service Now" or hot transmission warnings on the dashboard.
- **Solution:** Replace and reprogram the Transmission Control Module (TCM).
- **Cost:** Out-of-warranty TCM replacement typically costs around $1,800.

### 5-speed Manual

Offered on standard trims, this conventional manual transmission is highly reliable, robust, and completely bypasses the failures of the dual-clutch automatic.

### 6-speed Manual

Standard in the ST trim, this engaging gearbox is engineered to handle higher torque demands and has proven to be incredibly durable.

### Single-Speed Direct Drive

Paired with the Electric model, this simple unit provides smooth, seamless power delivery with very few reported moving-part failures.

## Steering

### Power Steering System

**Electronic Power Assist Steering (EPAS) Failure**
- **Symptoms:** A sudden, total loss of power steering assist while driving or upon startup, making the steering wheel incredibly stiff and difficult to turn, often accompanied by a yellow or red "Steering Assist Fault" dash light.
- **Solution:** Replace the electronic steering rack and pinion assembly.

## Electrical

### Audio and Accessories

**Loud Popping and Crackling from Speakers**
- **Symptoms:** Extremely loud, sudden, and startling static popping or crackling noises coming from the radio speakers. This occurs randomly and can persist even when the car is completely shut off, locked, and the keys are removed.
- **Solution:** Replace the radio/stereo head unit assembly or service the internal wiring harness.

## Exterior

### Body Control Electronics

**Windshield Wiper Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Wipers failing to move or turning on automatically at high speeds and refusing to shut off, even with the ignition keys removed. This often correlates with wet weather or car washes.
- **Solution:** Check the Body Control Module (BCM) for moisture/corrosion and replace affected wiring or the wiper motor.

### Cabin Door Components

**Door Latch Failures**
- **Symptoms:** Doors refusing to stay latched when closed, or unexpectedly swinging open while the vehicle is in motion, accompanied by a constant door-ajar warning.
- **Solution:** Replace the latch mechanism inside the affected door panel.

## Safety

### Airbags and Restraints

**Airbag Warning Light or Erroneous Deployment**
- **Symptoms:** The airbag warning light illuminating on the dash, signaling that a service is required, or rare reports of driver-side knee/curtain airbags deploying without a collision.
- **Solution:** Diagnose and replace faulty airbag sensors, clocksprings, or seatbelt tensioner assemblies.

## Summary Verdict

The 2014 Ford Focus is a dual-personality compact. If you are shopping for an automatic model, we strongly recommend looking elsewhere. The PowerShift dual-clutch transmission is plagued by persistent shuddering, slipping, and expensive electronic module failures that can compromise driver safety and command thousands in repairs. However, if you are comfortable driving a manual transmission, a used manual-equipped Focus or Focus ST is a hidden gem. These traditional manual models avoid almost all of the vehicle's major mechanical headaches, offering sharp handling and excellent value. Before buying any 2014 Focus, thoroughly test-drive it, verify that the doors latch and unlatch cleanly, check the steering operation for any stiffness, and listen closely to the audio system and wiper functions.
