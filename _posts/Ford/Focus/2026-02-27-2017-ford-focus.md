# 2017 Ford Focus Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2017 Ford Focus, buyers will find a compact car that offers nimble handling and efficient performance, but one that also carries well-documented reliability concerns. Powertrain performance heavily depends on the chosen configuration, though widespread owner reports highlight recurring mechanical hurdles that require careful inspection before purchase.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Ti-VCT I-4 Engine

The naturally aspirated 2.0-liter four-cylinder engine produces approximately 160 horsepower and serves as the standard powerplant for most models, generally providing reliable operation outside of reported electrical and fuel component hiccups.

**Engine Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle unexpectedly stalls while driving or coming to a stop, often without warning lights illuminating beforehand.
- **Solution:** Inspect the fuel system and purge valves or update engine management software.

### 1.0L EcoBoost Turbocharged I-4 Engine

The 1.0-liter turbocharged three-cylinder engine generates around 123 horsepower, prioritizing high fuel efficiency while maintaining straightforward mechanical fundamentals.

## Transmission

### PowerShift 6-Speed Dual-Clutch Automatic Transmission

The 6-speed dual-clutch automatic transmission is notorious for persistent shifting shudder, clutch wear, and control module failures. Prospective buyers are strongly advised to verify service history or consider manual-equipped models to avoid costly repairs.

**Transmission Shudder and Clutch Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle experiences severe shuddering during acceleration, jerking when turning corners, delayed engagement, and eventual complete clutch failure.
- **Solution:** Replace the clutch assembly and associated transmission components.
- **Cost:** $4,000

**Transmission Control Module (TCM) Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Sudden and total loss of transmission engagement and motive power while driving at highway speeds, dropping the vehicle into a no-power state.
- **Solution:** Diagnose and replace the failing Transmission Control Module.

### 5-Speed Manual Transmission

The traditional 5-speed manual transmission pairs with the base engine, offering a much more mechanically dependable driving experience compared to its automatic counterpart.

## Suspension

### Wheels and Hardware

**Swollen Lug Nuts**
- **Symptoms:** Factory two-piece lug nuts expand and deform over time, making it difficult or unsafe to remove wheels during tire rotations or flat repairs.
- **Solution:** Replace factory lug nuts with solid, one-piece aftermarket alternatives.

## Steering

### Power Steering

**Power Steering Assist Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Power steering abruptly shuts off while driving or maneuvering at low speeds, accompanied by a service message and heavy steering effort.
- **Solution:** Inspect the electronic power assist steering rack and related electrical connections.

## Electrical

### Electrical System

**Unexpected Engine Stalling and Power Loss**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle stalls at intersections or while idling, sometimes leaving occupants temporarily unable to open doors due to electrical power interruption.
- **Solution:** Inspect battery terminals, main wiring harnesses, and charging systems.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V735000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Storage](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Focus&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** The excessive vaccuum may result in an engine stall while driving, without warning and without the ability to restart the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will reprogram the Powertrain Control Module. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V515000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Storage](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Focus&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** The excessive vacuum may result in an engine stall while driving, without warning and without the ability to restart the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will reprogram the Powertrain Control Module. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V845000: Power Train:Manual Transmission](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Focus&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** A transmission fluid leak in the presence of an ignition source such as hot engine or exhaust components can increase the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will update vehicles with software that detects and prevents prolonged clutch slip, and will replace the clutch, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V528000: Seat Belts:Rear/Other](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Focus&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 207, "Seating Systems," and 210, "Seat Belt Assembly Anchorages." The reduced seat back strength may increase the risk of injury in the event of a crash.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will inspect and replace the rear second left side rear seat back as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V698000: Latches/Locks/Linkages:Hatchback/Liftgate:Lock](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Focus&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 206, "Door Locks and Door Retention Components." With only a single press needed, the driver may accidently unlatch the hatchback, increasing the risk of injury to any unsecured passengers in the back.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will reprogram the Body Control Module with updated software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V905000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Engine:Oil/Lubrication:Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Focus&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** A loss of oil pressure can result in an engine stall and a loss of power braking assist, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the oil pump tensioner assembly and oil pump drive belt, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V011000: Equipment:Electrical:Engine Block Heater](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Focus&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** The engine block heater may crack and develop a coolant leak, causing it to short circuit when the block heater is plugged in. An electrical short circuit can increase the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the block heater, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2017 Ford Focus requires cautious evaluation, particularly regarding its problematic dual-clutch automatic transmission. Buyers searching for a used model will minimize major financial risk by targeting manual transmission variants or ensuring that any automatic model has thorough documentation of prior clutch and module replacements.
