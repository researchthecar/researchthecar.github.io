Here's a comprehensive breakdown of common issues and complaints associated with the 2009 Ford Explorer Sport Trac, organized by category and component. The 2009 Ford Explorer Sport Trac is a mid-size pickup truck known for its comfortable ride, spacious cabin, and versatile cargo bed. It was available with two engine options: a 4.0L V6 producing 210 horsepower and 254 lb-ft of torque, and a 4.6L V8 generating 292 horsepower and 300 lb-ft of torque. Both engines could be paired with a 5-speed automatic transmission. People appreciate the Sport Trac for its unique blend of truck utility and SUV comfort, but common complaints include subpar fuel economy, some reliability concerns, and dated interior design.

**Engine**

**4.0L V6**

*   **Timing Chain Rattle**
    *   Description/Symptoms: A rattling noise, especially upon cold start, indicating wear or looseness in the timing chain components. Can also cause engine misfires and poor performance.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the timing chain, tensioners, and guides.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Camshaft Position Sensor Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Engine stalling, rough idle, check engine light illumination, and difficulty starting.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the camshaft position sensor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300
    *   Recalls: None Found

**4.6L V8**

*   **Spark Plug Breakage During Removal**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Spark plugs prone to seizing in the cylinder head, often breaking upon attempted removal. This can lead to difficult and expensive repairs.
    *   Solution: Careful spark plug removal using penetrating oil and specialized tools. If broken, specialized extraction tools or cylinder head removal may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $1,000+ (depending on the severity of the breakage)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Oil Leaks (Valve Cover Gaskets, Oil Pan Gasket)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks visible around the valve covers or oil pan, leading to oil consumption and potential engine damage if not addressed.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the leaking gaskets.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**5-Speed Automatic (Both Engines)**

*   **Transmission Slipping/Harsh Shifting**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Hesitation during acceleration, erratic or harsh shifting between gears, and/or complete loss of gear engagement.
    *   Solution: In some cases, a fluid flush and filter change can help. More severe cases may require valve body repair/replacement or a complete transmission rebuild/replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 (fluid flush) - $3,000+ (rebuild/replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Torque Converter Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Shuddering or vibration at highway speeds, particularly during light acceleration.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the torque converter.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Front Wheel Bearing Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Humming, grinding, or roaring noise from the front wheels, increasing with speed.  Loose steering or vibration may also be present.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the affected wheel bearing(s).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500 per side
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Rear Leaf Spring Sagging/Breakage**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rear of the vehicle sitting lower than normal, reduced load-carrying capacity, and potential clunking noises. In severe cases, leaf springs can break.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the rear leaf springs.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $1,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Power Window Motor Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Window failing to move up or down, or moving very slowly.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the power window motor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $400 per window
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Blend Door Actuator Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Inconsistent climate control, such as hot air blowing from one vent and cold air from another. Clicking noises behind the dashboard may also be present.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the faulty blend door actuator.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600+ (depending on location and accessibility)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Rear Liftgate Strut Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rear liftgate failing to stay open on its own, requiring manual support.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the liftgate struts.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $200
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Sunroof Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Sunroof failing to open or close properly, getting stuck, or leaking water.
    *   Solution: Depending on the cause, solutions can range from cleaning and lubrication to motor replacement or track repair.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2009 Ford Explorer Sport Trac, while a versatile vehicle, has several common issues that potential buyers should be aware of. Engine problems, particularly with timing chains and spark plug breakage, can be costly. Transmission issues, while not always severe, can lead to expensive repairs if left unaddressed. Suspension and electrical problems are also common. Overall, the severity of these issues ranges from minor inconveniences to major mechanical failures, with repair costs potentially exceeding several thousand dollars. A thorough inspection by a qualified mechanic is highly recommended before purchasing a used 2009 Ford Explorer Sport Trac.
