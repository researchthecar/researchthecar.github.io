**Introduction:**
The 2009 Ford Explorer Sport Trac is a midsize pickup truck based on the Ford Explorer SUV. It was available with a choice of two engines: a 4.0L V6 and a 4.6L V8. Both engines were paired with a 5-speed automatic transmission. The Explorer Sport Trac was praised for its spacious interior, comfortable ride, and off-road capabilities. However, it also had some notable issues.

**Engine**
- **4.0L V6 - Head Gasket Failure:**
  - Description/Symptoms: Blown head gaskets can lead to coolant leaks, overheating, and engine damage. Symptoms may include white smoke from the exhaust, low coolant levels, and a rough idle.
  - Solution: Replacement of the head gaskets is required.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
  - Recalls: None Found
- **4.6L V8 - Timing Chain Tensioner Failure:**
  - Description/Symptoms: A faulty timing chain tensioner can lead to a noisy engine, reduced power, and potential engine damage. Symptoms may include a rattling noise from the engine, especially on startup, and a reduction in engine power.
  - Solution: Replacement of the timing chain tensioner is required.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
  - Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**
- **5-Speed Automatic - Hard Shifting:**
  - Description/Symptoms: The transmission may experience hard or delayed shifts, slipping gears, or refusing to shift altogether.
  - Solution: Transmission fluid replacement or repair may be necessary.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
  - Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**
- **Front Lower Control Arms - Bushing Failure:**
  - Description/Symptoms: Worn or damaged bushings in the front lower control arms can lead to excessive suspension movement and noise. Symptoms may include a clunking noise when turning or braking, and uneven tire wear.
  - Solution: Replacement of the bushings or the entire control arms may be necessary.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
  - Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**
- **Alternator Failure:**
  - Description/Symptoms: A faulty alternator may not provide sufficient power to the electrical system, leading to dim headlights, flickering electronics, and potential battery drain.
  - Solution: Replacement of the alternator is required.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
  - Recalls: None Found
- **Ignition Coil Failure:**
  - Description/Symptoms: A faulty ignition coil can lead to misfires, engine stalling, and reduced performance. Symptoms may include a rough idle, poor acceleration, and an illuminated check engine light.
  - Solution: Replacement of the ignition coils is required.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
  - Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**
- **Rear Bumper Corrosion:**
  - Description/Symptoms: The rear bumper may be susceptible to rust and corrosion, especially in areas with heavy salt use.
  - Solution: Repair or replacement of the rear bumper may be necessary.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
  - Recalls: None Found

**Interior**
- **Dashboard Rattling:**
  - Description/Symptoms: The dashboard may rattle or make noises due to loose fasteners or components.
  - Solution: Tightening loose fasteners or replacing damaged components may be necessary.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
  - Recalls: None Found
- **Power Window Switch Failure:**
  - Description/Symptoms: The power window switches may become faulty, leading to windows that do not operate properly.
  - Solution: Replacement of the power window switches is required.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
  - Recalls: None Found

**Summary**
The 2009 Ford Explorer Sport Trac is a capable and versatile vehicle, but it has some significant issues that can be expensive to fix. Engine problems, transmission issues, and suspension failures can all be costly to repair. While interior and exterior issues may not be as severe, they can still detract from the overall ownership experience. Potential buyers should be aware of these issues before purchasing a used Explorer Sport Trac.
