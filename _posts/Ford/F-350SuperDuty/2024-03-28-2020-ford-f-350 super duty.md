**2020 Ford F-350 Super Duty**

The 2020 Ford F-350 Super Duty is a heavy-duty pickup truck that offers a range of engine and transmission options. The standard engine is a 6.7L Power Stroke diesel V8, which produces 450 horsepower and 1,050 lb-ft of torque. Optional engines include a 7.3L gas V8 with 430 horsepower and 475 lb-ft of torque, and a 6.2L gas V8 with 385 horsepower and 430 lb-ft of torque. Transmission options include a 10-speed automatic transmission and a 6-speed automatic transmission.

The F-350 Super Duty has a reputation for durability and towing capacity, but it has also been plagued by a number of issues.

**Engine**

**6.7L Power Stroke Diesel V8**

* **Fuel Injector Failure:** Symptoms include rough idling, poor fuel economy, and black smoke from the exhaust. Solution: Replace the fuel injectors. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
* **Turbocharger Failure:** Symptoms include loss of power, excessive smoke from the exhaust, and a loud whining noise. Solution: Replace the turbocharger. Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,000+

**Transmission**

**10-Speed Automatic Transmission**

* **Transmission Slippage:** Symptoms include difficulty shifting gears, delayed engagement, and shuddering. Solution: Replace the transmission fluid and filter. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
* **Valve Body Failure:** Symptoms include harsh shifting, delayed engagement, and complete loss of gears. Solution: Replace the valve body. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+

**Suspension**

* **Leaf Spring Breakage:** Symptoms include a sagging rear end, uneven tire wear, and a rough ride. Solution: Replace the leaf springs. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
* **Shock Absorber Failure:** Symptoms include a rough ride, poor handling, and excessive body roll. Solution: Replace the shock absorbers. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain:** Symptoms include a weak battery, dimmed lights, and difficulty starting the engine. Solution: Replace the battery or diagnose and repair the electrical system. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
* **Alternator Failure:** Symptoms include a weak battery, flickering lights, and a warning light on the dashboard. Solution: Replace the alternator. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+

**Exterior**

* **Front Bumper Cracking:** Symptoms include a cracked or broken front bumper. Solution: Replace the front bumper. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
* **Tailgate Corrosion:** Symptoms include rust spots and peeling paint on the tailgate. Solution: Sand and repaint the tailgate. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+

**Interior**

* **Dashboard Rattle:** Symptoms include a rattling noise coming from the dashboard. Solution: Tighten or replace the loose components. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+
* **Seat Heater Failure:** Symptoms include a seat heater that does not work properly. Solution: Repair or replace the seat heater. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+

**Summary**

The 2020 Ford F-350 Super Duty is a capable and durable truck, but it has been plagued by a number of issues. The most common issues include fuel injector failure, turbocharger failure, transmission slippage, and leaf spring breakage. The severity of these issues ranges from minor inconveniences to major repairs, and the cost to fix them can be significant.
