**Introduction:**

The 2007 Ford F-350 Super Duty is a heavy-duty pickup truck offered with various engine and transmission options. It boasts a reliable chassis, potent engines, and impressive towing capabilities. However, it has faced some common issues over the years.

**Engine**

* **6.0L PowerStroke Diesel Engine**
    * **Oil Cooler Failure:** Premature failure of the oil cooler leads to coolant and oil mixing, resulting in engine damage.
        * Solution: Replace oil cooler.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
        * Recalls: 07V477000, 08V502000
    * **EGR Valve Problems:** Faulty EGR (exhaust gas recirculation) valves can cause rough idling, reduced performance, and smoke emission.
        * Solution: Replace EGR valve.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800+
        * Recalls: 06E04, 07E02
    * **Injector Problems:** Leaking or clogged injectors lead to poor fuel economy, misfires, and engine noise.
        * Solution: Replace or clean injectors.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+
        * Recalls: 07E03, 08V481000
* **6.4L PowerStroke Diesel Engine**
    * **Turbocharger Problems:** Wear or leaks in the turbocharger cause loss of power, reduced boost pressure, and black smoke emission.
        * Solution: Replace turbocharger.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
        * Recalls: N/A
    * **Head Gasket Failure:** Leaking or blown head gasket results in coolant or oil leaks, overheating, and reduced performance.
        * Solution: Replace head gasket.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,500 - $4,000+
        * Recalls: 10E07, 11V345000
* **5.4L Triton V8 Gas Engine**
    * **Spark Plug Failure:** Premature wear or breakage of spark plugs can cause misfires, rough idling, and reduced performance.
        * Solution: Replace spark plugs.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
        * Recalls: 07E05, 08E03
    * **Intake Manifold Failure:** Cracks or leaks in the intake manifold can lead to vacuum leaks, reduced engine efficiency, and increased fuel consumption.
        * Solution: Replace intake manifold.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $600 - $1,200+
        * Recalls: 07E08

**Transmission**

* **TorqShift 6-Speed Automatic Transmission**
    * **Torque Converter Problems:** Worn or damaged torque converter causes shuddering, slipping, or delayed engagement.
        * Solution: Replace torque converter.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,200 - $2,000+
        * Recalls: 10V439000, 11V092000
    * **Clutch Pack Failure:** Worn or damaged clutch packs result in transmission slipping, gear engagement issues, and overheating.
        * Solution: Rebuild or replace transmission.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,500 - $4,000+
        * Recalls: 08V523000
* **TorqShift 5-Speed Automatic Transmission**
    * **Input Shaft Seal Failure:** Worn or damaged input shaft seal causes transmission fluid leaks.
        * Solution: Replace input shaft seal.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800+
        * Recalls: N/A
    * **Pump Failure:** Faulty transmission pump leads to insufficient fluid pressure, causing overheating and gear engagement issues.
        * Solution: Replace transmission pump.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $1,800+
        * Recalls: 07S36, 07S43

**Suspension**

* **Leaf Spring Failure:** Overloaded or damaged leaf springs can sag, break, or cause alignment issues.
    * Solution: Replace leaf springs.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+ per axle
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Ball Joint Wear:** Worn or damaged ball joints affect steering precision, cause clunking or vibration, and can lead to wheel separation.
    * Solution: Replace ball joints.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800+ per wheel
    * Recalls: 08E04

**Electrical**

* **Battery Issues:** Failed or weak battery results in difficulty starting, electrical system malfunction, or premature aging of components.
    * Solution: Replace battery.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Alternator Failure:** Faulty alternator fails to recharge the battery, causing electrical system problems and battery drain.
    * Solution: Replace alternator.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Headlight Condensation:** Moisture buildup inside the headlight assembly causes reduced visibility and potential damage to the electrical components.
    * Solution: Replace headlights with sealed assemblies.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Tailgate Latch Failure:** Worn or damaged tailgate latch allows the tailgate to rattle or fall open.
    * Solution: Replace tailgate latch.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
    * Recalls: 10S49

**Interior**

* **Dashboard Cracking:** UV damage or temperature fluctuations can cause cracks on the dashboard surface.
    * Solution: Replace or repair dashboard (if possible).
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Radio/Navigation System Malfunction:** Faulty or outdated software, loose connections, or hardware failure cause issues with radio, navigation, or audio functions.
    * Solution: Software update, hardware repair, or replacement.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+
    * Recalls: 11E05

**Summary:**

The 2007 Ford F-350 Super Duty has faced several significant issues, particularly with the 6.0L PowerStroke diesel engine, TorqShift 6-speed automatic transmission, and suspension components. The severity and cost of these issues vary depending on the specific problem and the affected components. Owners should be aware of potential expenses and consider regular maintenance and inspection to prevent or mitigate these issues.
