# 2022 Ford F-350 Super Duty

### Overview
The 2022 Ford F-350 Super Duty is engineered for heavy-duty work, offering robust performance and capability. Reliability data for this model year indicates a generally strong performance, particularly in its core powertrain components. While specific issues are infrequent, potential concerns can arise with certain engine and transmission configurations under extreme or prolonged heavy use. The F-350's construction prioritizes durability, which is reflected in its overall reliability ratings, though like any complex vehicle, occasional component failures can occur. This analysis focuses on documented technical issues beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential areas of concern for owners and fleet managers.

### 6.7L Power Stroke V8 Diesel
**Fuel Injector Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Rough idling, reduced power, increased exhaust smoke, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of faulty fuel injectors.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1500

**DPF Regeneration Failures**
- **Symptoms:** Increased fuel consumption, reduced engine power, check engine light, DPF warning light.
- **Solution:** Forced DPF regeneration or cleaning/replacement of the Diesel Particulate Filter.
- **Cost:** $500 - $2000

### 10-Speed TorqShift Automatic (for 7.3L V8 and 6.7L Diesel)
**Torque Converter Shudder**
- **Symptoms:** Vibration or shuddering during acceleration or at certain speeds.
- **Solution:** Software update or replacement of the torque converter.
- **Cost:** $700 - $1200

**Transmission Fluid Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Visible fluid leaks, low transmission fluid level, slipping gears.
- **Solution:** Inspection and repair of seals, lines, or transmission casing.
- **Cost:** $300 - $800

### Summary Verdict
The 2022 Ford F-350 Super Duty demonstrates solid reliability, especially concerning its gasoline engines and the robust 10-speed automatic transmission. The 6.7L Power Stroke diesel, while powerful, shows some susceptibility to fuel injector issues and DPF regeneration problems, which are not uncommon for modern diesel powertrains under heavy use. Other systems like suspension, electrical, exterior, and interior components are generally free from widespread reported defects. Owners should be mindful of the specific maintenance requirements for the diesel engine and transmission, particularly if operating in demanding conditions, to mitigate potential issues and ensure long-term durability.
