# 2018 Ford F-350 Super Duty

### Overview
The 2018 Ford F-350 Super Duty is a heavy-duty pickup truck engineered for demanding work and towing applications. Known for its robust construction and powerful engine options, it generally offers strong reliability. However, like any complex vehicle, specific components can experience issues. This analysis focuses on documented technical reliability concerns beyond routine maintenance, covering its primary powertrain options and other key vehicle systems. Owners can expect a durable platform, but awareness of potential, albeit not widespread, issues can aid in proactive maintenance and informed repairs.

### 6.7L Power Stroke V8 Diesel
**DPF Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Check Engine Light illumination, reduced engine power, rough idling, increased fuel consumption.
- **Solution:** Regeneration cycle, DPF cleaning, or replacement.
- **Cost:** $500 - $2500

**EGR Cooler Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** White smoke from exhaust, coolant loss, overheating, sweet smell from exhaust.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the EGR cooler.
- **Cost:** $400 - $1000

### Summary Verdict
The 2018 Ford F-350 Super Duty demonstrates a solid reliability record, particularly with its gasoline engine option. The 6.7L Power Stroke diesel, while powerful, has a few documented issues such as DPF problems and EGR cooler leaks, which are not uncommon for modern diesel powertrains and can be costly to repair if not addressed promptly. Transmission options are generally robust. Electrical system quirks are the most frequently cited non-powertrain issues, though typically not critical failures. Overall, the F-350 is a dependable workhorse, with potential issues being manageable and largely related to the complexities of its heavy-duty diesel engine.
