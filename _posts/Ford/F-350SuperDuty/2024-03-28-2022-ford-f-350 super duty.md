**2022 Ford F-350 Super Duty**

The 2022 Ford F-350 Super Duty is a heavy-duty pickup truck that offers a powerful engine lineup and a variety of trim levels. It is powered by a 6.7-liter Power Stroke V8 turbodiesel engine that produces 475 horsepower and 1,050 lb-ft of torque. The F-350 Super Duty is also available with a 7.3-liter gasoline V8 engine that produces 430 horsepower and 475 lb-ft of torque. Both engines are mated to a 10-speed automatic transmission.

People like the F-350 Super Duty for its powerful engine, impressive towing and payload capacities, and comfortable interior. However, some owners have reported issues with the vehicle's engine, transmission, suspension, electrical system, and interior.

**Engine**

* **6.7-liter Power Stroke V8 turbodiesel engine**
    * **Fuel injector failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: Engine misfires, loss of power, increased fuel consumption
        * Solution: Replace fuel injectors
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
        * Recalls: NHTSA Campaign Number: 22V-383
    * **Turbocharger failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, black smoke from exhaust, whistling noise
        * Solution: Replace turbocharger
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,000+
        * Recalls: NHTSA Campaign Number: 22V-902
* **7.3-liter gasoline V8 engine**
    * **No Issues Found**

**Transmission**

* **10-speed automatic transmission**
    * **Shifting problems**
        * Description/Symptoms: Hard shifting, delayed shifts, slipping gears
        * Solution: Software update, transmission repair or replacement
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+
        * Recalls: NHTSA Campaign Number: 22V-192

**Suspension**

* **Rear leaf springs**
    * **Sagging**
        * Description/Symptoms: The rear of the vehicle sits lower than the front, reduced load-carrying capacity
        * Solution: Replace leaf springs
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Electrical shorts**
    * Description/Symptoms: Blown fuses, flickering lights, electrical components not working
    * Solution: Identify and repair the source of the short
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
        * Recalls: None Found
* **Battery drain**
    * Description/Symptoms: Battery dies quickly, even after a short period of time
    * Solution: Identify and fix the source of the drain
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Bed rust**
    * Description/Symptoms: Rust appears on the bed of the vehicle
    * Solution: Apply rustproofing treatment or replace the bed
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+
        * Recalls: None Found
* **Tailgate latch failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The tailgate does not latch properly, causing it to open while driving
    * Solution: Replace the tailgate latch
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Dashboard rattles**
    * Description/Symptoms: The dashboard rattles while driving
    * Solution: Tighten screws or add insulation to reduce vibration
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
        * Recalls: None Found
* **Seatbelt malfunction**
    * Description/Symptoms: The seatbelt does not retract properly or does not latch correctly
    * Solution: Replace the seatbelt assembly
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
        * Recalls: NHTSA Campaign Number: 22V-148

**Summary**

The 2022 Ford F-350 Super Duty is a powerful and capable truck, but it has been plagued by a number of issues. The most common problems seem to be with the engine, transmission, and interior. While some of these issues are relatively minor and inexpensive to fix, others can be more serious and costly. Overall, the severity and cost of the issues with the F-350 Super Duty vary depending on the specific problem.
