**Introduction to the 2018 Ford F-350 Super Duty**

The 2018 Ford F-350 Super Duty is a heavy-duty pickup truck known for its powerful engines, rugged design, and impressive towing and hauling capabilities. It is available with a range of engine and transmission options, including:

- 6.2L V8 gasoline engine (385 horsepower, 430 lb-ft of torque)
- 6.7L Power Stroke V8 turbo diesel engine (450 horsepower, 935 lb-ft of torque)
- 7.3L V8 gasoline engine (430 horsepower, 475 lb-ft of torque)
- 6-speed automatic transmission
- 10-speed automatic transmission

Owners appreciate the F-350 Super Duty's strong performance, comfortable cabin, and versatile features. However, it has also been reported to have some issues in certain areas.

**Engine**

**6.2L V8 Gasoline Engine**

- **Fuel injector failure:** Fuel injectors can become clogged or fail, resulting in engine misfires, reduced power, and difficulty starting. Replacement of the faulty injectors is required. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
- **Intake manifold gasket leak:** The intake manifold gasket can fail, causing oil or coolant leaks. Replacement of the gasket is necessary. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+

**6.7L Power Stroke V8 Turbo Diesel Engine**

- **Diesel particulate filter (DPF) issues:** The DPF can become clogged with soot, causing reduced engine power and increased fuel consumption. Cleaning or replacement of the DPF may be needed. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
- **Turbocharger failure:** The turbocharger can fail due to wear, contamination, or other issues, resulting in reduced boost pressure and impaired engine performance. Replacement of the turbocharger is often required. Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,000+

**7.3L V8 Gasoline Engine**

- **Cam phaser failure:** The cam phaser, a timing device, can fail, causing rough idling, engine noise, and reduced power. Replacement of the cam phaser and related components is necessary. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
- **Valve train noise:** Excessive noise from the valve train, such as ticking or clattering, can be caused by worn valve components or poor lubrication. Adjustment or replacement of the affected components may be required. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+

**Transmission**

**6-Speed Automatic Transmission**

- **Shifting issues:** The transmission may experience hard shifting, slipping, or delayed engagement. Rebuilding or replacing the transmission may be necessary. Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $5,000+

**10-Speed Automatic Transmission**

- **Torque converter shudder:** The torque converter can develop a shuddering sensation during acceleration or gear changes. Replacement of the torque converter may be required. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+

**Suspension**

- **Rear leaf spring failure:** The rear leaf springs can crack or break, leading to a noticeable sag in the rear suspension. Replacement of the faulty springs is necessary. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
- **Shock absorber leaks:** The shock absorbers can develop leaks, resulting in reduced ride comfort and handling. Replacement of the shock absorbers is typically recommended. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+

**Electrical**

- **Battery drain:** The battery may drain prematurely or fail to hold a charge due to electrical system issues such as parasitic draws or faulty components. Diagnosis and repair of the underlying electrical problem is required. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
- **HID headlight failure:** The HID headlights may fail or experience reduced brightness due to bulb failure or ballast issues. Replacement of the affected components is often necessary. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+

**Exterior**

- **Tailgate cable failure:** The tailgate cables can break or fray, preventing the tailgate from opening or closing properly. Replacement of the cables is necessary. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+

**Interior**

- **Infotainment system glitches:** The infotainment system may experience software glitches, such as freezing, slow response, or connectivity issues. Software updates or hardware replacements may be required. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+

**Summary**

Overall, the 2018 Ford F-350 Super Duty is a capable and highly regarded pickup truck. However, it has been reported to have some issues, particularly in the areas of the engine, transmission, and suspension. The severity and cost of these issues can vary depending on the specific problem and the extent of the repairs needed. It is important for owners to address these issues promptly to ensure the safe and reliable operation of their vehicles.
