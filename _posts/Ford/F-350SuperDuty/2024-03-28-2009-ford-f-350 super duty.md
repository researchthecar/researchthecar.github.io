**2009 Ford F-350 Super Duty**

The 2009 Ford F-350 Super Duty is a heavy-duty pickup truck available with multiple engine and transmission options. It features impressive towing and payload capacities. The F-350 Super Duty is known for its durability, powerful engines, and spacious interior. However, like any vehicle, it has some shortcomings, including fuel efficiency and a limited range of technology features.

**Engine**
- 5.4L V8 Triton Engine (300 hp, 465 lb-ft torque)
- 6.8L V10 Triton Engine (362 hp, 457 lb-ft torque)
- 6.4L V8 Power Stroke Diesel Engine (350 hp, 650 lb-ft torque)

**Transmission**
- TorqShift 6-speed Automatic Transmission
- TorqShift 5-speed Automatic Transmission

**Engine**
- **Injection Pressure Regulator Failure**
  - Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, rough idling, increased fuel consumption
  - Solution: Replace injection pressure regulator
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
- **Oil Cooler Failure (6.4L Diesel Engine Only)**
  - Description/Symptoms: Overheating, coolant leaks, loss of coolant
  - Solution: Replace oil cooler
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
- **Turbocharger Failure (6.4L Diesel Engine Only)**
  - Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, black smoke from exhaust
  - Solution: Replace turbocharger
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,800 - $3,000+
- **EGR Cooler Failure (6.4L Diesel Engine Only)**
  - Description/Symptoms: Overheating, loss of coolant
  - Solution: Replace EGR cooler
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+

**Transmission**
- **TorqShift 6-speed Transmission Failure**
  - Description/Symptoms: Slipping, shuddering, delayed shifting
  - Solution: Rebuild or replace transmission
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,500 - $5,000+
- **TorqShift 5-speed Transmission Failure**
  - Description/Symptoms: Slipping, hard shifting, loss of gears
  - Solution: Rebuild or replace transmission
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+

**Suspension**
- **Leaf Spring Failure**
  - Description/Symptoms: Broken leaf springs, sagging rear end
  - Solution: Replace leaf springs
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+

**Electrical**
- **Alternator Failure**
  - Description/Symptoms: Dimming lights, electrical system malfunction
  - Solution: Replace alternator
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
- **Battery Terminal Corrosion**
  - Description/Symptoms: Difficult starting, power loss
  - Solution: Clean or replace battery terminals
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $250+

**Exterior**
- **Rust on Frame**
  - Description/Symptoms: Visible rust on the frame of the vehicle
  - Solution: Repair or replace frame
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $5,000+

**Interior**
- **Climate Control Malfunction**
  - Description/Symptoms: Inconsistent air temperature, failure to defrost windows
  - Solution: Replace climate control module
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+
- **Power Window Failure**
  - Description/Symptoms: Windows fail to operate or become stuck
  - Solution: Replace window motor or switch
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $250 - $500+

**Summary**

The 2009 Ford F-350 Super Duty has a range of potential issues that vary in severity and cost to fix. Some issues, such as the TorqShift transmission failure, can be major and expensive to repair. However, many other issues are relatively minor and can be addressed with less significant expenses. Overall, the F-350 Super Duty is a reliable and capable truck, but it is important to be aware of its potential issues before making a purchase decision.
