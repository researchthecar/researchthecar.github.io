# 2017 Ford F-350 Super Duty

### Overview
The 2017 Ford F-350 Super Duty is a heavy-duty pickup truck designed for demanding work and towing applications. Built on a robust platform, it generally offers strong reliability, particularly in its core powertrain components. However, like any complex vehicle, specific issues can arise. This analysis focuses on documented technical reliability concerns, excluding routine maintenance and normal wear and tear, across its various engine and transmission configurations. Understanding these potential problem areas can help owners anticipate and address issues proactively, ensuring the longevity and performance of their Super Duty.

### 6.7L Power Stroke V8 Diesel
**EGR Cooler Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Coolant loss, white smoke from exhaust, overheating.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the EGR cooler.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1500

**Fuel Injector Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Rough idle, loss of power, increased fuel consumption, engine misfires.
- **Solution:** Replacement of faulty fuel injectors.
- **Cost:** $1000 - $2000

**Torque Converter Shudder**
- **Symptoms:** Vibration or shuddering during acceleration, especially at lower speeds.
- **Solution:** Transmission fluid flush and filter replacement, or torque converter replacement.

### Standard Electrical System
**Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Dead battery after sitting, intermittent electrical component failures.

**Trailer Brake Controller Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** Trailer brakes not engaging or engaging erratically.
- **Solution:** Software updates or replacement of the trailer brake controller module.

### Summary Verdict
The 2017 Ford F-350 Super Duty demonstrates solid reliability, especially for its intended heavy-duty use. The gasoline 6.2L V8 engine and its associated transmission are generally trouble-free. The 6.7L Power Stroke diesel, while powerful, has a few documented issues, primarily related to the EGR cooler and fuel injectors, which can be costly to repair. Transmission issues are rare but can include torque converter shudder in the diesel variant. Electrical gremlins like battery drain and trailer brake controller problems are not uncommon but are typically manageable. Overall, the F-350 is a dependable workhorse, with the diesel engine requiring more attention to potential specific component failures.
