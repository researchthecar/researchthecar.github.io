# 2009 Ford F-350 Super Duty

### Overview
The 2009 Ford F-350 Super Duty is a heavy-duty pickup truck known for its robust construction and towing capabilities. Reliability for this model year is generally considered good, especially for its intended purpose of heavy work. However, like any vehicle, specific components and powertrains can exhibit certain issues. This analysis focuses on documented, non-wear-and-tear related problems reported by owners and technicians, covering the primary engine and transmission options available for the 2009 model year. Understanding these potential issues can help owners with proactive maintenance and repair planning.

---


## Engine

### 5.4L Triton V8
**Spark Plug Blowout**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of engine power, rough idling, misfires, audible popping sound from engine bay.
- **Solution:** Requires specialized tools to extract broken spark plug fragments and re-tap the cylinder head. Often involves replacing spark plugs and potentially ignition coils.
- **Cost:** $400 - $800

**Camshaft Phasers**
- **Symptoms:** Rattling noise from the engine, especially at idle or upon startup; potential loss of power.
- **Solution:** Replacement of camshaft phasers and related timing components.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1500


### 6.8L Triton V10
**Spark Plug Blowout**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of engine power, rough idling, misfires, audible popping sound from engine bay.
- **Solution:** Requires specialized tools to extract broken spark plug fragments and re-tap the cylinder head. Often involves replacing spark plugs and potentially ignition coils.
- **Cost:** $400 - $800

**Camshaft Phasers**
- **Symptoms:** Rattling noise from the engine, especially at idle or upon startup; potential loss of power.
- **Solution:** Replacement of camshaft phasers and related timing components.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1500


### 6.4L Power Stroke V8 Diesel
**EGR Cooler Failure**
- **Symptoms:** White smoke from exhaust, coolant loss, overheating, potential engine misfires.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the EGR cooler. Often recommended to upgrade to a more robust aftermarket unit.
- **Cost:** $700 - $1200

**Turbocharger Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of power, excessive smoke, whining noises from the turbo.
- **Solution:** Inspection and potential replacement of the turbocharger assembly.
- **Cost:** $1000 - $2500

**Fuel Injector Problems**
- **Symptoms:** Rough running, poor fuel economy, black smoke, hard starting.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and replacement of faulty fuel injectors.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1600


## Transmission

### 5R110W (TorqShift) Automatic
**Torque Converter Shudder**
- **Symptoms:** Vibration or shuddering during acceleration, especially at lower speeds.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the torque converter and potentially transmission fluid flush.
- **Cost:** $600 - $1200

**Solenoid Pack Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Harsh shifting, delayed engagement, transmission slipping, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the transmission solenoid pack.
- **Cost:** $400 - $800

### Summary Verdict
The 2009 Ford F-350 Super Duty demonstrates solid reliability for its class, particularly with its gasoline V8 engines. The 6.4L Power Stroke diesel, while powerful, has documented issues with its EGR system, turbochargers, and fuel injectors that can be costly to repair. The 5R110W automatic transmission can experience torque converter shudder and solenoid issues. The manual ZF6 transmission is generally very robust. Other systems like suspension, electrical, exterior, and interior show no widespread common failures beyond typical wear and tear. Owners should be aware of the specific potential issues related to the diesel engine and automatic transmission for optimal long-term ownership.
