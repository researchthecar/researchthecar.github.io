**2001 Ford F-350 Super Duty**

The 2001 Ford F-350 Super Duty is a heavy-duty pickup truck offered in various configurations with different engine and transmission options. It is powered by the 7.3L Power Stroke V8 diesel engine, mated to either a 5-speed TorqShift automatic transmission or a 6-speed manual transmission. The F-350 Super Duty is known for its exceptional towing and hauling capabilities, making it popular among commercial and recreational users. However, it has also been subject to various issues reported by owners.

**Engine**

**7.3L Power Stroke V8 Diesel**

* **Injector Failure**: Leaking or clogged injectors result in poor engine performance, rough idling, and black smoke from the exhaust. Replacement of faulty injectors and fuel system cleaning is necessary. $500 - $1,000+
* **Turbocharger Malfunction**: Faulty turbochargers can cause reduced engine power, excessive smoke, and unusual noises. Replacement or repair of the turbocharger may be required. $1,000 - $2,000+
* **Head Gasket Failure**: Warped or blown head gaskets lead to coolant leaks and overheating. Major engine repairs, including head gasket replacement, resurfacing, and coolant system flush, are needed to resolve the issue. $2,000 - $4,000+

**Transmission**

**5-Speed TorqShift Automatic**

* **Torque Converter Failure**: A faulty torque converter can cause transmission slipping, overheating, and shuddering. Replacement of the torque converter is usually necessary. $1,500 - $2,500+
* **Transmission Control Module (TCM) Issues**: Electrical problems in the TCM can result in erratic transmission behavior, gear shifting problems, and warning lights. TCM replacement or reprogramming is often required. $500 - $1,000+

**Suspension**

* **Leaf Spring Failures**: Broken or cracked leaf springs can affect ride quality, handling, and load-bearing capabilities. Replacement of the affected leaf springs is necessary. $500 - $1,000+
* **Front End Wobble**: Worn or loose suspension components can cause the vehicle to wobble or vibrate at highway speeds. Inspection and replacement of faulty components, such as ball joints, tie rods, or steering dampener, may be required. $200 - $500+

**Electrical**

* **Alternator Failure**: A faulty alternator can lead to battery drain, electrical malfunctions, and warning light illumination. Replacement of the alternator is necessary to restore proper charging. $300 - $500+
* **Battery Terminal Corrosion**: Corroded battery terminals can hinder electrical connections, affecting starting, lights, and other electrical components. Cleaning or replacing the battery terminals and terminals is necessary. $50 - $200+

**Exterior**

* **Tailgate Sticking**: Misalignment or wear in the tailgate hinge mechanism can cause the tailgate to stick or fail to open smoothly. Realignment or replacement of the hinge assembly may be necessary. $200 - $500+
* **Door Handle Failure**: Broken or malfunctioning door handles can make it difficult to enter or exit the vehicle. Replacement of faulty door handles is required. $100 - $200+

**Interior**

* **Dashboard Rattle**: Vibrations from the road or engine can cause the dashboard to rattle or buzz. Identifying and securing loose components or applying sound dampening materials can resolve the issue. $100 - $500+
* **Power Window Failure**: Malfunctioning switches, motors, or wiring can cause the power windows to fail to operate. Replacement or repair of faulty components is usually necessary. $200 - $500+

**Summary**

The 2001 Ford F-350 Super Duty is a capable heavy-duty pickup truck with a proven track record of performance. However, it has been subject to several issues that can affect its reliability and longevity. While some issues are relatively minor and inexpensive to fix, others can be more severe and costly. It is important for owners to be aware of these potential issues and take proactive maintenance measures to prevent or mitigate them.
