**Introduction:**

The 1995 Ford P800 is a full-size pickup truck known for its spacious interior and robust towing capabilities. It comes equipped with a 7.3L V8 diesel engine mated to a 4-speed automatic or manual transmission. Despite its rugged design, the P800 has been plagued by several notable issues.

**Engine**

**7.3L V8 Diesel**

* **Fuel Injector O-Ring Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Leaking fuel, poor engine performance, difficulty starting.
    * Solution: Replace fuel injector O-rings.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
    * Recalls: None Found
* **High-Pressure Oil Pump Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Low oil pressure, engine noise, loss of power.
    * Solution: Replace high-pressure oil pump.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $1,500
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Turbocharger Boost Control Solenoid Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Reduced engine power, black smoke from exhaust.
    * Solution: Replace turbocharger boost control solenoid.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**4-Speed Automatic**

* **Torque Converter Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Slipping transmission, delayed shifting.
    * Solution: Replace torque converter.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,200 - $1,800
    * Recalls: None Found

**4-Speed Manual**

* **Clutch Master Cylinder Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Difficulty engaging clutch, clutch pedal sinks to the floor.
    * Solution: Replace clutch master cylinder.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Rear Leaf Spring Breakage:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Sagging rear end, excessive body roll, harsh ride.
    * Solution: Replace rear leaf springs.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $600 - $1,000
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Alternator Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Dimming headlights, electrical accessories malfunctioning, battery not charging.
    * Solution: Replace alternator.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Rust on Body Panels:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Rust spots on exterior body panels, especially around wheel wells and fenders.
    * Solution: Rust repair, paint touch-up.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Power Window Motor Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Inoperative power windows.
    * Solution: Replace power window motor.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
    * Recalls: None Found
* **HVAC Blower Motor Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: No airflow from HVAC system.
    * Solution: Replace HVAC blower motor.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

The 1995 Ford P800 has a number of significant issues, particularly with its engine and transmission. These issues can be costly to repair, and the rust problems can significantly affect the resale value of the vehicle. While the P800 has some desirable qualities, such as its spacious interior and towing capabilities, the potential for expensive repairs and long-term corrosion issues should be carefully considered before purchasing one of these trucks.
