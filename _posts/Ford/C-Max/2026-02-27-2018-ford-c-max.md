# 2018 Ford C-Max Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2018 Ford C-Max offers shoppers an efficient hybrid option with practical hatchback utility. As you shop the used market, reviewing powertrain options and common owner-reported trouble spots will help you determine if this hybrid fits your daily driving needs.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L I-4 Hybrid Engine

The 2.0-liter Atkinson-cycle four-cylinder engine is paired with an electric motor to generate a combined 188 horsepower. This powertrain is generally robust, though buyers should evaluate overall mechanical condition during a pre-purchase inspection.

## Transmission

### eCVT Automatic Transmission

An electronically controlled continuously variable transmission (eCVT) channels power to the front wheels. It delivers seamless acceleration and solid reliability under normal driving conditions.

---

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V413000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission:Gear Position Indication (Prndl)](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=C-Max&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** Either scenario increases the risk of a crash or injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the under hood shift bushing and add a protective cap over the shift cable bushing, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2018 Ford C-Max is a sensible choice for efficiency-minded commuters, provided it passes a thorough mechanical check. Prioritize well-maintained models with documented service history to minimize unexpected repair costs.
