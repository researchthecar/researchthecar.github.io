# 2019 Ford Transit Connect Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2019 Ford Transit Connect is a compact utility and passenger van favored for urban delivery fleets and family transport due to its manageable size and efficiency. While it offers strong utility, used-car buyers should carefully evaluate its mechanical history. Owner reports and technical service data highlight potential concerns regarding brake component longevity, transmission performance, and powertrain reliability that require a thorough pre-purchase inspection.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Duratec I4

The standard 2.0-liter naturally aspirated four-cylinder engine produces approximately 150 horsepower and 144 lb-ft of torque, paired with an 8-speed automatic transmission. It provides adequate power for city deliveries and light cargo hauling with reasonable fuel economy, though buyers should verify regular oil maintenance.

### 1.5L EcoBlue Turbo Diesel I4

The available 1.5-liter turbocharged diesel engine offers high torque and excellent fuel efficiency for high-mileage commercial duties, utilizing an 8-speed automatic transmission. While efficient, diesel variants demand strict adherence to emissions system and fuel filter maintenance schedules.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed SelectShift Automatic

The 8-speed automatic transmission manages power delivery across front-wheel-drive models, but some owners report harsh shifting and premature mechanical wear.

**Transmission Slipping and Hard Engagement**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers experience hard shifts, particularly in lower gears, sudden deceleration, or a total loss of drive power while underway.
- **Solution:** Requires diagnostic inspection by a professional transmission technician; may necessitate valve body service, software updates, or a complete mechanical overhaul.

**Sudden Transmission Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may experience unexpected drivetrain lockups, loud clunking noises, or complete inability to engage gears while driving.
- **Solution:** Inspection by a qualified mechanic to determine if internal component replacement or transmission unit replacement is required.

## Brakes

### Brake Calipers and Hydraulic Control Unit

The service brake system provides adequate stopping power under normal conditions, but certain owners have reported premature component wear and caliper sticking.

**Brake Caliper Seizure**
- **Symptoms:** Front or rear brake calipers may seize closed, causing the vehicle to pull to one side, generating severe heat, and prematurely wearing out brake pads and rotors.
- **Solution:** Inspect brake lines, flush fluid, and replace seized brake calipers along with affected pads and rotors.

**ABS Hydraulic Control Unit Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Premature failure of the Anti-lock Braking System Hydraulic Control Unit, leading to degraded brake performance.
- **Solution:** Replace the faulty ABS Hydraulic Control Unit and bleed the brake system.

## Electrical

### Backup Camera and Display

Safety features generally perform as expected, though electronic display glitches can occasionally affect rearview visibility.

**Backup Camera Display Freeze**
- **Symptoms:** When shifting into reverse, the rearview camera image freezes for several seconds before turning completely black, creating a visibility hazard.
- **Solution:** Have the infotainment software and camera module inspected by a dealership for updates or replacement parts.

## Exterior

### Windshield Seals and Glass

Exterior panels are durable, but poorly sealed windshield perimeters can allow water intrusion.

**Windshield Water Leaks and Wind Noise**
- **Symptoms:** Water entering the cabin around the upper driver-side windshield or overhead interior lights during heavy rain, accompanied by excessive wind noise at highway speeds.
- **Solution:** Inspect windshield adhesive seals and re-seal or replace the front windshield glass as necessary.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V791000: Air Bags:Frontal:Driver Side:Inflator Module](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Transit+Connect&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 208, "Occupant Crash Protection." Air bag modules missing the first-stage booster propellant will not deploy as intended in a crash, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will replace the affected air bag module on either the driver or passenger side, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V636000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Cooling System](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Transit+Connect&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** If the engine overheats, it can result in engine damage and increase the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will update the PCM software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V550000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Transit+Connect&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** Missing or loose bolts on the start stop accumulator endcap could result in a transmission fluid leak and may progress to a loss of transmission function. A transmission fluid leak in the presence of an ignition source may increase the risk of fire.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will replace the start/stop accumulator, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V376000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission:Gear Position Indication (Prndl)](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Transit+Connect&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** Either scenario increases the risk of a crash or injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the underhood shifter cable bushing, and add a protective cap, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V413000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission:Gear Position Indication (Prndl)](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Transit+Connect&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** Either scenario increases the risk of a crash or injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the under hood shift bushing and add a protective cap over the shift cable bushing, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V294000: Visibility:Fixed Panaramic Roof/Sky Light Assembly](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Transit+Connect&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** A roof panel that detaches while driving can create a road hazard and increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will remove, clean, and reinstall the panoramic roof panel, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V196000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission:Control Module (Tcm/Pcm/Tecm)](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Transit+Connect&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** An engine that overheats increases the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the powertrain control module (PCM) software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V442000: Back Over Prevention:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Transit+Connect&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** A software error may cause the rearview camera to display a blank image, or the image may remain on the display after the backing event has ended. Both of these situations can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the rearview camera software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

Purchasing a used 2019 Ford Transit Connect requires diligence, particularly regarding the transmission and braking systems. While the van offers exceptional cargo versatility and efficiency, prospective buyers should obtain a comprehensive pre-purchase inspection focusing on transmission shift quality, brake caliper movement, and potential fluid leaks. Confirming that all applicable recalls have been completed will help ensure a safer and more reliable ownership experience.
