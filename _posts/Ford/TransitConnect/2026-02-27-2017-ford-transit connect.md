# 2017 Ford Transit Connect

### Overview
The 2017 Ford Transit Connect is a versatile compact commercial van known for its practicality and fuel efficiency. It offers a range of powertrain options designed to balance performance and economy for various business needs. Reliability for the 2017 model year is generally considered good, with owners reporting fewer significant mechanical issues compared to some competitors. However, like any vehicle, specific components can experience problems. This analysis focuses on documented, non-wear-and-tear related issues across key systems, providing insights into potential repair needs and associated costs for different configurations.

## Engine

### 2.5L Duratec (Naturally Aspirated)
**Cooling System Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Coolant loss, overheating, visible leaks from hoses or water pump.
- **Solution:** Replacement of leaking hoses, water pump, or radiator.
- **Cost:** $300 - $700


### 1.6L EcoBoost (Turbocharged)
**Turbocharger Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of power, whistling noises, increased oil consumption.

### Summary Verdict
The 2017 Ford Transit Connect generally demonstrates good reliability, particularly for its class. The most commonly cited issues tend to be related to the cooling system across both engine options and potential transmission problems with the automatic variant. The turbocharged EcoBoost engine, while offering better performance, has a slightly higher potential for turbocharger-related issues. Electrical gremlins, such as infotainment glitches and battery drain, are also noted but not overwhelmingly prevalent. Overall, with regular maintenance and prompt attention to any emerging symptoms, the Transit Connect should provide dependable service for commercial use.
