# 2021 Ford Transit Connect Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2021 Ford Transit Connect, prospective buyers will find a versatile compact van designed for urban commercial or personal utility. While many owners enjoy its maneuverability and cargo capacity, available owner reports point to potential powertrain and electrical concerns that warrant a careful inspection before purchase.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Duratec I4

The 2.0-liter naturally aspirated four-cylinder engine produces around 162 horsepower and 144 lb-ft of torque, serving as the standard power plant. It provides adequate pep for city delivery duties, though buyers should verify routine maintenance history.

### 1.5L EcoBoost Turbo I4

The available 1.5-liter turbocharged three-cylinder EcoBoost engine offers improved low-end torque and fuel economy. Buyers should listen for smooth operation and check for any history of intermittent warning lights.

**Check Engine and Misfire Warnings**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers report intermittent service engine soon lights and malfunction indicator lamps illuminating, sometimes accompanied by rough running or potential misfire codes.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified technician diagnose any active diagnostic trouble codes, check fuel delivery components, and ensure software updates are current.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 8-speed automatic transmission pairs with both engine options to drive the front wheels. Due to owner reports concerning shifting quality and premature failure, a pre-purchase inspection focused on this unit is strongly recommended.

**Transmission Shifting and Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have experienced incorrect shifting, lagging, stuttering, and in severe cases, sudden loss of vehicle propulsion while driving or total transmission failure requiring replacement.
- **Solution:** Test drive the vehicle thoroughly through all gears, check the transmission fluid condition, and review service records for any past transmission repairs.

## Brakes

### ABS Module and Brakes

The braking system provides reliable stopping power under normal conditions, but isolated reports of sudden wheel lockups highlight the importance of inspecting brake modules and actuators.

**Brake Lockup**
- **Symptoms:** The automatic braking system module can malfunction mid-drive, causing multiple tires to gradually or completely lock up and rendering the vehicle immovable.
- **Solution:** Inspect the braking system, anti-lock brake module, and wheel speed sensors for faults or warning signs.

## Electrical

### Body Electrical and Sealing

Cabin electronics generally function as intended, though body sealing vulnerabilities can occasionally introduce water and create secondary electrical faults.

**Windshield Leak and Electrical Damage**
- **Symptoms:** A poorly sealed or flexing windshield can allow water to leak into the cabin, leading to potential electrical system damage.
- **Solution:** Inspect the upper windshield seal for gaps or missing adhesive and check interior wiring harnesses for signs of moisture intrusion.

## Exterior

### Rear Hatch Window

Exterior panels and glass are generally durable, though rare instances of spontaneous rear window breakage have been reported by owners.

**Spontaneous Glass Shattering**
- **Symptoms:** The rear window glass in the liftgate or hatch door can spontaneously shatter while the vehicle is in motion.
- **Solution:** Inspect the rear glass for preexisting stress fractures and verify proper alignment of the hatch hinges and defroster connections.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V376000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission:Gear Position Indication (Prndl)](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Transit+Connect&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** Either scenario increases the risk of a crash or injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the underhood shifter cable bushing, and add a protective cap, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V592000: Seat Belts:Front:Retractor](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Transit+Connect&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 208, "Occupant Crash Protection." An unsecured child restraint system can increase the risk of injury during a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the front passenger seat belt assembly, and replace the assembly as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V413000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission:Gear Position Indication (Prndl)](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Transit+Connect&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** Either scenario increases the risk of a crash or injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the under hood shift bushing and add a protective cap over the shift cable bushing, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V442000: Back Over Prevention:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Transit+Connect&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** A software error may cause the rearview camera to display a blank image, or the image may remain on the display after the backing event has ended. Both of these situations can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the rearview camera software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2021 Ford Transit Connect remains a practical choice for small business owners and delivery drivers needing compact utility. However, prospective buyers should exercise caution by conducting a thorough pre-purchase inspection, paying special attention to transmission operation, brake module performance, and body sealing.
