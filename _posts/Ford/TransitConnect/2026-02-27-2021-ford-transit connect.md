# 2021 Ford Transit Connect

### Overview
The 2021 Ford Transit Connect is a versatile compact commercial van known for its practicality and fuel efficiency. Designed for urban delivery and light commercial use, it offers a range of powertrain options to suit different needs. Reliability data for the 2021 model year indicates a generally solid performance, with most reported issues being minor or specific to certain configurations. Owners and fleet managers can expect a dependable vehicle for daily operations, though attention to specific components may be beneficial for long-term ownership. This analysis focuses on documented technical issues beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential areas of concern for the 1.5L EcoBoost and 2.0L GDI engines, and their associated transmissions.

---


## Engine

### 1.5L EcoBoost (3-cylinder)
**Coolant Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Visible coolant puddles under the vehicle, low coolant level warning light, engine overheating.
- **Solution:** Inspection and replacement of leaking hoses, seals, or the water pump.
- **Cost:** $300 - $700

**Turbocharger Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of power, whistling noises from the engine bay, increased exhaust smoke.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and potential replacement of the turbocharger or related plumbing.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1500

## Electrical

### General Electrical System
**Infotainment System Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Screen freezing, unresponsiveness, Bluetooth connectivity issues, audio problems.
- **Solution:** Software updates, system resets, or potential replacement of the infotainment unit.
- **Cost:** $100 - $800

**Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle failing to start, dimming lights, electrical components malfunctioning intermittently.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis of parasitic draw, testing and replacement of battery or alternator if faulty.
- **Cost:** $200 - $600

### Summary Verdict
The 2021 Ford Transit Connect demonstrates good overall reliability, particularly with the 2.0L GDI engine and its standard 8-speed automatic transmission, which show minimal reported technical issues. The 1.5L EcoBoost engine, while generally robust, has seen some isolated reports of coolant leaks and turbocharger concerns, which are not considered widespread but warrant attention. Electrical gremlins, primarily related to the infotainment system and occasional battery drain, are also noted but often resolvable with software updates or standard diagnostics. The suspension, exterior, and interior components are largely free of significant recurring problems. For fleet operators and individual owners, the Transit Connect is a dependable choice, with most potential issues being manageable and repairable within reasonable cost parameters.
