# 2020 Ford Transit Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The Ford Transit remains a dominant choice in the full-size commercial and passenger van market, offering immense versatility for fleets, camper conversions, and daily work use. While most vehicles perform reliably under heavy commercial duty, used buyers should be aware of reported owner concerns involving transmission shifting behaviors, rear brake caliper binding, and occasional electrical issues.

---

## Engine

### 3.5L V6 PFDI

This naturally aspirated 3.5-liter port-and-direct-injection V6 serves as the capable base engine, producing around 275 horsepower and 260 lb-ft of torque. It offers straightforward, dependable performance well-suited for light delivery and passenger hauling.

### 3.5L EcoBoost V6

The twin-turbocharged EcoBoost V6 steps up output to roughly 310 horsepower and 400 lb-ft of torque, providing robust towing capacity and effortless highway acceleration. It is a popular upgrade for heavier conversions and high-payload fleets.

## Transmission

### 10-Speed Automatic

A 10-speed automatic transmission is paired across the engine lineup, designed to optimize fuel economy and power delivery. While generally effective, some owners report shifting irregularities that require professional diagnosis.

**Harsh Shifting and Gear Ratio Faults**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers experience hard, jerky shifting, delayed engagement into lower or higher gears, and sporadic loss of smooth acceleration.
- **Solution:** Diagnostic scans may reveal historical diagnostic trouble codes such as P0734, often requiring software reflashes, valve body service, or transmission overhauls per technical service bulletins.

## Brakes

### Rear Brake Calipers

The braking system provides solid stopping power for a large van, though fleet and heavy-use operators have highlighted rear brake component binding.

**Rear Brake Caliper Seizing**
- **Symptoms:** Brake pads fail to retract properly, causing rear brakes to overheat, freeze up, and produce premature wear or total brake system failure.
- **Solution:** Inspect rear brake assemblies and replace seized brake calipers and worn pads as necessary.

## Electrical

### Electrical System

Electrical architecture supports numerous onboard systems and aftermarket upfits, though parasitic draws and auxiliary equipment can occasionally drain the battery.

**Battery Drain and Starting Issues**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle frequently fails to start due to unexpected battery voltage depletion within a short timeframe.
- **Solution:** Test battery health, check for parasitic electrical draws from aftermarket or upfitted accessories, and verify charging system output.

### Backup Camera

Safety electronics generally assist with driver awareness, though rearview camera display glitches are a recognized owner complaint.

**Rearview Camera Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The backup camera display turns completely black, blue, or static, rendering the reverse camera system unavailable.
- **Solution:** Inspect camera wiring connections, software modules, or replace the rearview camera assembly.

**[Recall 21V978000: Power Train:Driveline:Driveshaft](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Transit&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** The bolts that attach the rear driveshaft to the transfer case may become loose or damaged, which can result in separation of the driveshaft from the transfer case. Driveshaft separation can result in a loss of drive power, or unintended movement while parked if the parking brake is not engaged, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the driveshaft bolts. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The Ford Transit remains an indispensable tool for commercial operators and van-lifers alike, offering strong powertrain choices and exceptional utility. Used buyers should thoroughly test-drive the vehicle to check for smooth 10-speed transmission shifting, inspect the rear brakes for uneven wear or caliper sticking, and verify that all electrical accessories and safety cameras function properly.
