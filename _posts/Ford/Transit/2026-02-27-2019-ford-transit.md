# 2019 Ford Transit Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2019 Ford Transit is a popular full-size commercial and passenger van prized for its versatility and configuration options. However, used-car buyers should review potential trouble spots, particularly concerning brake components and powertrain behavior, as owner complaints indicate certain recurring mechanical challenges.

---

## Engine

### 3.7L V6

The naturally aspirated 3.7-liter V6 engine delivers dependable baseline power for daily commercial duties. It generally offers solid mechanical endurance, though buyers should verify regular maintenance history.

### 3.5L EcoBoost V6

The twin-turbocharged 3.5-liter EcoBoost V6 provides substantial horsepower and strong low-end torque for heavy hauling. While capable, turbocharged powerplants demand strict adherence to oil change intervals to prevent long-term wear.

### 3.2L I5 Power Stroke Diesel

The 3.2-liter inline-5 diesel engine offers high torque output geared toward heavy towing applications. Owners have occasionally reported powertrain performance hitches and internal transmission component wear.

**Powertrain Hesitation and Power Loss**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle jerks during acceleration, experiences unexpected power loss, or hesitates when shifting into reverse.
- **Solution:** Inspect the torque converter, planetary assembly bushings, and hydraulic control unit for internal wear or failure.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic

The 6-speed automatic transmission routes power smoothly in normal conditions, but some owners report premature wear, hard engagements, and eventual slipping.

**Hard Engagement and Slipping**
- **Symptoms:** The transmission exhibits harsh shifting between first and second gears, slips under load, or triggers warning lights.
- **Solution:** Perform a comprehensive diagnostic evaluation; fluid flushes may not resolve mechanical wear, requiring internal component or transmission replacement.

## Brakes

### Brake Calipers and ABS Hydraulic Control Unit

The braking system requires careful inspection on used models due to reports of seized calipers and ABS control unit malfunctions.

**Seized Calipers and Brake Drag**
- **Symptoms:** Brake calipers seize closed causing the vehicle to pull to one side, overheated rotors, and premature wear of brake pads.
- **Solution:** Inspect brake lines, flush fluid, and replace seized brake calipers and damaged pads or rotors.

**ABS Hydraulic Control Unit Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Brake pedal feels unresponsive, or unexpected brake lockups occur, accompanied by dashboard warning indicators.
- **Solution:** Replace the malfunctioning ABS Hydraulic Control Unit.

## Safety

### Seat Belts and Backup Camera

Safety equipment generally performs well, though occasional complaints cite seat belt mechanism binding and rearview camera display delays.

**Seat Belt Retractor Sticking**
- **Symptoms:** Seat belts fail to pull out smoothly or remain stuck in place when occupants attempt to buckle up.
- **Solution:** Inspect the seat belt assembly and replace faulty retractors or buckles.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V103000: Power Train:Driveline:Driveshaft](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Transit&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** If the front driveshaft detaches from the vehicle, drive power will be lost, increasing the risk of a crash. The detached driveshaft may also create a road hazard, increasing the risk of a crash for following traffic.
- **What to do:** XL will notify owners, and dealers will replace the front driveshaft with an updated one, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V439000: Structure:Emergency:Escape/Egress/Exit](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Transit&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** Temperature and vibration stresses may cause the tempered glass roof hatch to shatter. If the tempered glass roof hatch shatters, glass fragments may fall onto passengers, increasing the risk of an injury.
- **What to do:** LA West will notify owners, and dealers will replace the hatch lids, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V250000: Seat Belts:Front:Retractor](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Transit&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** The seat belt retractor block out zone may have not been set correctly, potentially resulting in a seat belt that may not properly secure the occupant. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 209, "Seat Belt Assemblies." **[Recall 21V631000: Parking Brake:Conventional:Mechanical:Linkage And Cable](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Transit&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** Parking brake failure can result in unintended vehicle movement, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the passenger side parking brake cable with a cable that provides sufficient clearance to the damper shield, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

- **Issue:** Fractured studs may allow the wheel to detach, increasing the risk of crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and if necessary, replace the rear axle shaft assembly, axle shaft mounting bolts, and rear wheel lug nuts, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

- **Issue:** When the back seat cushion is rotated from the stowed position to the upright position, the seat cushion may not remain locked into place when under load, such as in a crash or a sudden stop. In the event of a crash, if the seat back moves, the seat occupant has an increased risk of injury.

## Summary Verdict

When shopping for a used Ford Transit, prospective buyers should conduct a thorough test drive paying close attention to transmission shift quality and brake performance. While many units serve commercial fleets reliably, verifying service records for brake caliper maintenance and transmission health is essential to avoid unexpected repair bills.
