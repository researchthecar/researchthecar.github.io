# 2017 Ford Transit Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2017 Ford Transit is a versatile and popular full-size commercial and passenger van offering multiple roof heights, wheelbase configurations, and robust engine options. While it serves as a reliable workhorse for many fleets and independent owners, used-car buyers should inspect powertrain components and electronic accessories carefully. Owner complaints highlight potential issues ranging from driveshaft and transmission anomalies to electrical and camera corrosion.

---

## Engine

### 3.7L V6

The naturally aspirated 3.7-liter V6 engine produces approximately 275 horsepower and 260 lb-ft of torque, serving as the standard entry-level powerplant. It offers straightforward maintenance and adequate capability for light-duty commercial applications.

### 3.5L EcoBoost V6

The twin-turbocharged 3.5-liter EcoBoost V6 delivers 310 horsepower and 400 lb-ft of torque, providing strong low-end pulling power for heavy loads and towing. Buyers should monitor for turbo-related heat stress, exhaust manifold leaks, and fluid consumption.

**Exhaust Manifold Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Exhaust leaks, smoke under the hood, and broken manifold bolts causing loud ticking noises.
- **Solution:** Replace the warped exhaust manifolds and broken studs with updated hardware.

**Oil and Coolant Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Low coolant levels, burning oil smells, and smoke from oil dripping onto hot exhaust components.
- **Solution:** Inspect gaskets and seals, and replace leaking components as needed.

### 3.2L Power Stroke I5 Diesel

The 3.2-liter inline-five Power Stroke turbodiesel generates 185 horsepower and 350 lb-ft of torque, tailored for high-mileage commercial duty. It provides strong torque delivery and improved fuel economy for heavy hauling, though diesel emission systems require attentive maintenance.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

Equipped with a 6-speed SelectShift automatic transmission across all engine choices, the Transit relies on this gearbox to manage high commercial duty cycles. Buyers should test-drive thoroughly to ensure smooth shifting and watch for torque converter or unexpected gear engagement issues.

**Unexpected Downshifting and Torque Converter Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Abrupt downshifting at highway speeds or failure of the torque converter, causing loss of smooth power delivery.
- **Solution:** Inspect transmission operation, update powertrain control modules, or replace worn internal transmission components.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

The braking system must contend with substantial vehicle weight, making regular inspection of pads, rotors, and calipers essential for safe operation.

**Soft Brake Pedal and Caliper Binding**
- **Symptoms:** Brake pedal traveling too close to the floor despite bleeding, or rear brake calipers hanging up leading to premature pad and rotor wear.
- **Solution:** Inspect brake lines for air, check ABS module function, and service or replace binding brake calipers.

## Steering

### Power Steering System

The electric power assist steering system generally provides light maneuvering effort for a large van, though intermittent electronic failures have been reported by some owners.

**Intermittent Power Assist Loss**
- **Symptoms:** Steering effort suddenly increases, causing power assist to work intermittently or stop functioning.
- **Solution:** Inspect power steering electrical connections, electronic modules, or steering gear assembly for faults.

## Electrical

### Backup Camera and Airbags

Safety equipment and visibility aids are critical on full-size vans, though auxiliary systems like rearview cameras can suffer from environmental wear and harness corrosion.

**Backup Camera Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Rearview camera displays a white screen or fails completely due to wiring harness corrosion.
- **Solution:** Inspect and replace corroded camera wiring cables or faulty camera units.

## Exterior

### Wiring and Lighting Systems

electrical issues can arise from moisture exposure in exterior housings and wiring harnesses, potentially affecting cabin electronics and trailer lighting.

**Moisture Intrusion and Wiring Corrosion**
- **Symptoms:** Faulty tail light seals allowing moisture in, leading to shorted fuses, erratic dashboard electronics, and non-functioning accessories.
- **Solution:** Replace compromised light seals, repair corroded wiring harnesses, and clear affected ground points.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V103000: Power Train:Driveline:Driveshaft](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Transit&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** If the front driveshaft detaches from the vehicle, drive power will be lost, increasing the risk of a crash. The detached driveshaft may also create a road hazard, increasing the risk of a crash for following traffic.
- **What to do:** XL will notify owners, and dealers will replace the front driveshaft with an updated one, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V275000: Electrical System:Wiring](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Transit&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** Water can enter the tow modules and corrode the wiring causing issues such as rapidly flashing turn signals, loss of instrument panel display, an electrical short and possibly the deployment of seatbelt pretensioner. An electrical short can increase the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will add a drainage hole in the driver's door stepwell and incorporate a fuse into the vehicle's wiring harness, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V333000: Seats](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Transit&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** When the back seat cushion is rotated from the stowed position to the upright position, the seat cushion may not remain locked into place when under load, such as in a crash or a sudden stop. - **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2017 Ford Transit is a capable and highly configurable choice for commercial fleets or camper conversions, but used buyers should proceed with a thorough pre-purchase inspection. Pay close attention to powertrain service history—specifically checking for EcoBoost exhaust leaks, transmission shifting behavior, and driveshaft components—alongside a careful check of the electrical system and backup camera functionality.
