Here's a breakdown of common issues and complaints for the 2022 Ford Transit, categorized by major component, engine/transmission option, and detailed information where available.

The 2022 Ford Transit is a popular cargo and passenger van known for its versatility and spacious interior. Engine options include a 3.5L PFDI V6 (275 hp, 262 lb-ft torque), a 3.5L EcoBoost V6 (310 hp, 400 lb-ft torque), and a 2.0L Turbo Diesel (195 hp, 360 lb-ft torque). The 3.5L PFDI V6 and the 2.0L Turbo Diesel are paired with a 10-speed automatic transmission. Some owners appreciate the Transit's customizable cargo space and available technology features. Common complaints include reliability issues, specifically electrical problems, and concerns about the cost of repairs.

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Freezing/Crashing**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The SYNC 4 infotainment system may freeze, crash, or become unresponsive. This can affect navigation, audio, and other vehicle functions.
    *   Solution: Software updates from Ford can sometimes resolve the issue. If not, the APIM (Accessory Protocol Interface Module) may need to be replaced.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1200+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Battery Drain**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The vehicle's battery drains quickly, especially when the van is not driven for several days. This can lead to difficulty starting the vehicle.
    *   Solution: Identifying the parasitic drain is key. This may involve testing various electrical components and circuits to find the source. Possible culprits include faulty modules, aftermarket accessories, or a defective battery.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+ (depending on the source of the drain)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Backup Camera Malfunction**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The backup camera display may be distorted, flicker, or fail to display an image. This can make reversing and parking difficult and unsafe.
    *   Solution: Check the camera connections and wiring for damage. If the wiring is intact, the camera module may need to be replaced.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Engine**

**3.5L PFDI V6**

*   **Rough Idle/Misfire**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The engine may exhibit a rough idle, hesitation, or misfire. This can be caused by faulty ignition coils, spark plugs, or fuel injectors.
    *   Solution: Diagnose the specific cause using a diagnostic scan tool. Replace any faulty components.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $400+ (depending on the component)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**3.5L EcoBoost V6**

*   **Coolant Intrusion**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Coolant intrusion into the engine cylinders due to a cracked head or block, often noticed by white smoke and an engine misfire.
    *   Solution: Replacing the cracked head or block, and flushing the cooling system.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $3,000 - $7,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**2.0L Turbo Diesel**

*   **Fuel Injector Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Difficulty starting, rough idling, decreased fuel efficiency, and black smoke from the exhaust.
    *   Solution: Replace the faulty fuel injectors.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $2,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**10-Speed Automatic (All Engines)**

*   **Harsh Shifting/Hesitation**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The transmission may exhibit harsh shifting, hesitation, or slipping between gears. This can be caused by low transmission fluid, a faulty valve body, or damaged clutch packs.
    *   Solution: Check and top off the transmission fluid. If the problem persists, the valve body or the entire transmission may need to be replaced. A software update may also resolve shifting issues.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 (fluid top off) - $5,000+ (transmission replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Torque Converter Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Shuddering at low speeds, especially under light acceleration. Can also manifest as abnormal noises or vibrations.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the torque converter.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Premature Bushing Wear**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Clunking or squeaking noises from the suspension, especially when going over bumps. This can be caused by premature wear of the suspension bushings.
    *   Solution: Replace the worn suspension bushings.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+ (per bushing replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Rattling/Squeaking Noises**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rattling or squeaking noises from the interior panels, especially in the cargo area. This can be caused by loose panels, trim pieces, or cargo restraints.
    *   Solution: Tighten or replace any loose panels, trim pieces, or cargo restraints. Add sound dampening material to reduce noise.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $300+ (depending on the source and complexity)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Sliding Door Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Difficult to open or close. The powered sliding door may fail to operate.
    *   Solution: Lubricate door tracks and hinges. Check and replace any worn rollers or cables. For powered doors, inspect the motor and wiring.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $800+ (depending on the issue)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2022 Ford Transit can experience a range of issues, with electrical problems being a common complaint. Engine and transmission issues vary depending on the specific configuration. Suspension and interior issues are also present, contributing to overall owner dissatisfaction. Repair costs can vary significantly depending on the severity of the issue, ranging from minor fixes to major component replacements. While some problems may be relatively inexpensive to address, others can be quite costly. Before purchasing a used 2022 Ford Transit, it is recommended to have it thoroughly inspected by a qualified mechanic to identify any potential problems.
