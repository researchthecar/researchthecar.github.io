# 2016 Ford Transit Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2016 Ford Transit is a versatile, highly capable full-size van popular for commercial fleets and camper conversions alike. However, prospective buyers should review owner-reported complaints and safety recall history carefully before purchasing. Key areas of concern involve drivetrain components like the driveshaft flex coupling, engine performance issues, and electronics such as backup camera failures.

---

## Engine

### 3.7L Ti-VCT V6

The base 3.7-liter naturally aspirated V6 produces around 275 horsepower and 260 lb-ft of torque, offering reliable daily performance for light-to-moderate hauling needs.

### 3.5L EcoBoost V6

The twin-turbocharged 3.5-liter EcoBoost V6 generates roughly 310 horsepower and 400 lb-ft of torque, providing robust towing capabilities but carrying higher potential complexity and maintenance requirements.

**Engine Stalling and Loss of Motive Power**
- **Symptoms:** Sudden engine hesitation, entry into limp mode, excessive black smoke, or complete stalling while driving at highway speeds.
- **Solution:** Diagnose fuel system components, inspect high-pressure fuel pumps, and ensure all relevant powertrain and fuel-related recalls have been completed by a certified dealer.

### 3.2L I5 Power Stroke Diesel

The 3.2-liter turbo-diesel inline-five delivers approximately 185 horsepower and 350 lb-ft of torque, designed for high-mileage commercial duties and improved fuel economy under heavy loads.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed SelectShift Automatic

The 6-speed SelectShift automatic transmission pairs with all engine choices to deliver power to the rear wheels, though some owners have experienced erratic shifting and gear disengagement.

**Unplanned Gear Disengagement and Shifting Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Transmission unexpectedly kicks out of sixth gear into lower gears or completely disengages while driving at highway speeds.
- **Solution:** Have a professional technician inspect transmission control modules, software calibrations, and internal mechanical components for wear.

### Driveshaft Flexible Coupling

The driveshaft assembly utilizes flexible rubber couplings to transfer power, which require careful tracking to ensure safety recalls have addressed premature degradation.

**Driveshaft Coupling Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Abnormal popping or vibrating sounds from underneath the vehicle, followed by potential detachment of the driveshaft from the transmission.
- **Solution:** Verify that manufacturer recall repairs for the flexible driveshaft coupling have been properly installed and physically inspected.

## Suspension

### Factory Tires and Chassis Suspension

The suspension is tuned to handle heavy cargo loads, but commercial use places high demands on tires and wheel alignment components.

**Premature Tire Wear and Tread Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Abnormal tread chipping, separation, or rapid wear across factory-equipped commercial tires.
- **Solution:** Inspect tire production dates and tread depth regularly, replacing compromised commercial tires with high-load-rated alternatives.

## Brakes

### ABS Tone Rings and Parking Brake

The braking system generally provides adequate stopping power for a large van, but watch for premature cracking of wheel speed sensor tone rings and parking brake service bulletins.

**ABS Tone Ring Cracking**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of hill-assist, anti-lock braking (ABS), and traction control features due to cracked rear tone rings.
- **Solution:** Inspect rear wheel hubs and replace cracked tone rings as necessary.

## Electrical

### Backup Camera and Display System

Electrical accessories are generally straightforward, though the rearview camera system frequently draws owner complaints regarding intermittent failure and inverted displays.

**Rear View Camera Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Backup camera display turns completely blank, shows a blue screen, or inverts the image upside down.
- **Solution:** Check for software updates or replace the failing backup camera unit and wiring harness.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V103000: Power Train:Driveline:Driveshaft](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Transit&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** If the front driveshaft detaches from the vehicle, drive power will be lost, increasing the risk of a crash. The detached driveshaft may also create a road hazard, increasing the risk of a crash for following traffic.
- **What to do:** XL will notify owners, and dealers will replace the front driveshaft with an updated one, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V275000: Electrical System:Wiring](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Transit&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** Water can enter the tow modules and corrode the wiring causing issues such as rapidly flashing turn signals, loss of instrument panel display, an electrical short and possibly the deployment of seatbelt pretensioner. An electrical short can increase the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will add a drainage hole in the driver's door stepwell and incorporate a fuse into the vehicle's wiring harness, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V333000: Seats](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Transit&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** When the back seat cushion is rotated from the stowed position to the upright position, the seat cushion may not remain locked into place when under load, such as in a crash or a sudden stop. In the event of a crash, if the seat back moves, the seat occupant has an increased risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

When shopping for a used 2016 Ford Transit, prioritize vehicles with comprehensive service records and verified completion of safety recalls, particularly regarding the driveshaft coupling and fuel systems. A pre-purchase inspection by a commercial truck or van specialist is strongly recommended to catch potential suspension, brake, and transmission wear early.
