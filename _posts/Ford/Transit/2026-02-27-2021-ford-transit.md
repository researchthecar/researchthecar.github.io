# 2021 Ford Transit Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2021 Ford Transit is a versatile and popular full-size commercial van, offering robust capability for businesses and conversion enthusiasts alike. When shopping for a used model, evaluating its powertrain choices and noting any reported owner complaints can help you secure a dependable workhorse.

---

## Engine

### 3.5L V6 PFDI

The standard 3.5-liter port-fuel and direct-injection V6 engine produces approximately 275 horsepower and 262 lb-ft of torque. It serves as a dependable, naturally aspirated base option for everyday delivery and utility tasks.

### 3.5L EcoBoost V6

The twin-turbocharged 3.5-liter EcoBoost V6 generates roughly 310 horsepower and 410 lb-ft of torque. It provides strong pulling power for heavy loads and towing, though buyers should ensure proper maintenance history.

## Transmission

### 10-Speed SelectShift Automatic

The 10-speed automatic transmission pairs with both engine options to optimize fuel efficiency and power delivery across various driving conditions. It generally offers smooth operation, though proper fluid maintenance is key to long-term durability.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V592000: Seat Belts:Front:Retractor](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Transit&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 208, "Occupant Crash Protection." An unsecured child restraint system can increase the risk of injury during a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the front passenger seat belt assembly, and replace the assembly as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V499000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Hoses, Lines/Piping, And Fittings](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Transit&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The fuel hose between the inline fuel filter and the Injection Pump Module (IPM) may not be properly secured, which can cause a gas leak. A gasoline leak in the presence of an ignition source may increase the risk of fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the fuel hose and clamps as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V442000: Back Over Prevention:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Transit&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** A software error may cause the rearview camera to display a blank image, or the image may remain on the display after the backing event has ended. Both of these situations can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the rearview camera software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V876000: Equipment Adaptive/Mobility:Wheelchair Restraints/Securement:Latch/Anchor:](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Transit&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The retractors may not lock, preventing the wheelchair from being properly secured. An unsecured wheelchair can move during transit, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2021 Ford Transit stands as a capable choice in the full-size van segment, backed by strong engine options and practical utility. Used-car buyers should perform a thorough mechanical inspection, verify maintenance records, and test drive the vehicle to ensure it meets their specific commercial or recreational needs.
