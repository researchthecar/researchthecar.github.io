# 2018 Ford Transit Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2018 Ford Transit is a popular and versatile full-size van utilized by businesses and camper conversion enthusiasts alike. While it offers robust capability and multiple powertrain choices, used-car buyers should carefully inspect potential purchases for common mechanical and electronic hiccups. Reviewing owner reports highlights areas like transmission durability, engine cooling concerns, and recurring electrical issues that are worth noting before making a purchase.

---

## Engine

### 3.7L Ti-VCT V6

The naturally aspirated 3.7-liter V6 produces approximately 275 horsepower and 260 lb-ft of torque, serving as the dependable base engine for light-to-medium-duty commercial applications.

### 3.5L EcoBoost V6

The twin-turbocharged 3.5-liter EcoBoost V6 delivers strong low-end power with roughly 310 horsepower and 400 lb-ft of torque, making it ideal for heavy hauling, though buyers should verify cooling system health.

**Coolant Intrusion**
- **Symptoms:** Illuminated check engine light, engine misfires, and loss of coolant.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified mechanic perform a diagnostic check to inspect for coolant leaks or intrusion into the cylinders.

### 3.2L I5 Power Stroke Turbo Diesel

The 3.2-liter inline-five turbo diesel engine provides strong torque output around 295 lb-ft and high fuel efficiency for long-distance driving, though it requires diligent maintenance.

## Transmission

### SelectShift 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 6-speed automatic transmission pairs across gas and diesel variants to deliver smooth power delivery, though proper fluid maintenance is critical to avoid longevity issues.

**Torque Converter and Lead Frame Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Abnormal shuddering during acceleration, harsh or random downshifting, and diagnostic trouble codes related to speed sensors.
- **Solution:** Inspect transmission fluid condition, check for stored diagnostic trouble codes, and consult a transmission specialist if torque converter or lead frame replacement is required.

## Electrical

### Infotainment and Display System

Electrical accessories govern critical driver aids such as infotainment displays and backup cameras, which can occasionally act up due to environmental exposure.

**Center Display and Backup Camera Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** The 8-inch center display screen flashes on and off in hot or humid conditions, making the backup camera and navigation difficult to use.
- **Solution:** Have the dealership or an electrical technician inspect the display module and wiring harness connections.

### Doors and Latches

Exterior body closures and weather sealing protect the cabin, though heavy commercial use can lead to minor alignment and sensor quirks.

**Sliding Door Sensor and Door Flex**
- **Symptoms:** Sliding door open indicator illuminating incorrectly or front doors flexing under high crosswinds at highway speeds.
- **Solution:** Adjust door striker plates, inspect door seals, and clean sliding door electronic contact points.

## Safety

### Seat Belts

Safety restraint components must be thoroughly inspected to ensure secure anchorage and reliable occupant protection.

**Seat Belt Mounting Design**
- **Symptoms:** Shoulder harness mounted directly to the seat structure instead of the A-pillar, raising concerns about rapid passenger exit.
- **Solution:** Inspect the seat belt anchor points and ensure proper adjustment during a pre-purchase inspection.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V103000: Power Train:Driveline:Driveshaft](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Transit&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** If the front driveshaft detaches from the vehicle, drive power will be lost, increasing the risk of a crash. The detached driveshaft may also create a road hazard, increasing the risk of a crash for following traffic.
- **What to do:** XL will notify owners, and dealers will replace the front driveshaft with an updated one, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V439000: Structure:Emergency:Escape/Egress/Exit](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Transit&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** Temperature and vibration stresses may cause the tempered glass roof hatch to shatter. If the tempered glass roof hatch shatters, glass fragments may fall onto passengers, increasing the risk of an injury.
- **What to do:** LA West will notify owners, and dealers will replace the hatch lids, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V333000: Seats](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Transit&modelYear=2018)**

## Summary Verdict

The 2018 Ford Transit remains a highly capable choice for commercial fleets and van-lifers, but buyers should exercise due diligence. Prioritize a comprehensive pre-purchase inspection that focuses on transmission shifting behavior, engine cooling performance, and the functionality of onboard electronics to avoid unexpected repair bills.
