# 2022 Ford Transit Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2022 Ford Transit is a popular and highly customizable full-size commercial van, offering various roof heights, wheelbase lengths, and powertrain configurations suited for both commercial fleets and custom camper conversions. While widely praised for its utility, driving dynamics, and modern tech features, used-car buyers should pay close attention to potential electrical and camera system glitches, as well as occasional powertrain-related complaints.

---

## Engine

### 3.5L V6

The standard naturally aspirated 3.5-liter V6 engine provides dependable baseline performance for moving heavy loads. It offers a solid balance of everyday reliability and utility for commercial operations.

**Loss of Motive Power**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle may experience sudden jerking, delayed or non-existent throttle response, and warning messages such as collision detection disabled or loss of propulsion.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified technician diagnose the electronic throttle body, powertrain control module, or related fuel system components.

### 3.5L EcoBoost V6

The twin-turbocharged 3.5-liter EcoBoost V6 delivers robust horsepower and substantial low-end torque for heavy hauling and towing. Buyers should ensure regular maintenance intervals were strictly followed.

## Transmission

### 10-Speed Automatic

The 10-speed automatic transmission pairs with both V6 engine options to optimize fuel economy and power delivery across a wide gear range. Smooth operation is typical, though software updates are occasionally required to maintain optimal shift quality.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

The braking system provides adequate stopping power for a large commercial vehicle, but vacuum pump operation should be verified during pre-purchase inspections.

**Loss of Brake Vacuum Assist**
- **Symptoms:** Brake warning light illuminates on the dashboard accompanied by a noticeable reduction in braking effectiveness.
- **Solution:** Inspect the brake vacuum pump and related lines immediately, replacing faulty components as needed.

## Electrical

### Backup and 360-Degree Camera System

Electrical complaints on this model frequently center around infotainment display connectivity and camera feed glitches.

**Camera Display Failure**
- **Symptoms:** When shifting into reverse or activating the 360-degree camera view, the screen may intermittently display a blank blue or black image, a distorted picture, or freeze completely.
- **Solution:** Check for applicable manufacturer recall software updates or have the camera module and wiring inspected by a dealer.

### Backup Camera and Driver Assistance

Driver assistance features generally enhance safety, though software glitches can temporarily disable critical backing aids.

**Intermittent Camera Inoperability**
- **Symptoms:** Rearview camera becomes inoperable while reversing, accompanied by pre-collision assist warning messages on the instrument cluster.
- **Solution:** Ensure all relevant safety recall camera software updates have been performed by an authorized service center.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V592000: Seat Belts:Front:Retractor](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Transit&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 208, "Occupant Crash Protection." An unsecured child restraint system can increase the risk of injury during a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the front passenger seat belt assembly, and replace the assembly as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V186000: Back Over Prevention:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Transit&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** The image processing module for the 360-degree camera system may not process the image from the rearview camera under certain rear loading vehicle conditions. Loss of the rearview camera image while reversing can reduce the driver's view of what is behind the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the Image Processing Module B (IPMB) software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V183000: Back Over Prevention:Display Function](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Transit&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** A camera that does not display a rearview image while reversing can reduce the driver's view of what is behind the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the audio control module configuration settings, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V384000: Equipment:Other:Labels](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Transit&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard numbers 110, "Tire Selection and Rim", and 120, "Wheels and Rims-Other Than Passenger Cars." Missing tire information could lead to an incorrect tire or rim being installed, or incorrect tire pressure, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the Safety Certification Label, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V415000: Electrical System: Instrument Cluster/Panel](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Transit&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** The Instrument Panel Cluster may not properly illuminate telltales or gauges and the speedometer and tachometer pointers. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard numbers 101, "Control and Displays," and 102, "Trans Shift Lever Seq/Starter Interlock/Trans Braking Effect," and 105, "Hydraulic and.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the instrument panel cluster software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V621000: Back Over Prevention:Display Function](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Transit&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** A missing rearview camera image reduces the driver's visibility of what is behind the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will install the audio control module and program the unit, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V791000: Electrical System: Instrument Cluster/Panel](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Transit&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** The heating, ventilation, and air-conditioning (HVAC) system's controls, including those for defog and defrost, may become inoperative. Loss of the ability to control the defrost or defog function decreases the driver's visibility under certain driving conditions, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the remote climate control module software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V499000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Hoses, Lines/Piping, And Fittings](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Transit&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** The fuel hose between the inline fuel filter and the Injection Pump Module (IPM) may not be properly secured, which can cause a gas leak. A gasoline leak in the presence of an ignition source may increase the risk of fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the fuel hose and clamps as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2022 Ford Transit remains a versatile and capable choice for commercial users and van life enthusiasts alike. While prospective buyers should carefully check for unresolved camera recalls, electrical glitches, and brake vacuum system concerns, a thorough pre-purchase inspection can help secure a reliable workhorse.
