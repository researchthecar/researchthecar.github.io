**Vehicle Introduction:**

The 1997 Ford E-350 Econoline is a full-size van that is offered with multiple engine and transmission options. The most common engine option is the 7.3L V8 diesel engine, which produces 225 horsepower and 450 lb-ft of torque. Other engine options include a 4.6L V8, a 5.4L V8, and a 6.8L V10. The E-350 Econoline is available with a variety of transmission options, including a 4-speed automatic, a 5-speed manual, and a 6-speed automatic.

People who like the E-350 Econoline appreciate its spacious interior, powerful engine options, and versatile cargo capacity. However, some people dislike the vehicle's poor fuel economy and lack of modern amenities.

**Engine**

**7.3L V8 Diesel**

* **Injection Pump Failure:**
    - Description/Symptoms: The injection pump fails, causing the engine to stall or run poorly.
    - Solution: Replace the injection pump.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,500+
    - Recalls: None Found

* **Turbocharger Failure:**
    - Description/Symptoms: The turbocharger fails, resulting in reduced engine power and increased exhaust smoke.
    - Solution: Replace the turbocharger.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
    - Recalls: None Found

**4.6L V8**

* **Intake Manifold Gasket Failure:**
    - Description/Symptoms: The intake manifold gasket fails, causing a vacuum leak and engine misfires.
    - Solution: Replace the intake manifold gasket.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    - Recalls: None Found

**5.4L V8**

* **Camshaft Position Sensor Failure:**
    - Description/Symptoms: The camshaft position sensor fails, causing the engine to run rough or stall.
    - Solution: Replace the camshaft position sensor.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    - Recalls: None Found

**6.8L V10**

* **Head Gasket Failure:**
    - Description/Symptoms: The head gasket fails, causing coolant and oil to leak into the combustion chambers.
    - Solution: Replace the head gasket.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,500 - $4,000+
    - Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**4-Speed Automatic**

* **Transmission Slipping:**
    - Description/Symptoms: The transmission slips or hesitates when shifting gears.
    - Solution: Rebuild or replace the transmission.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
    - Recalls: None Found

* **Overdrive Failure:**
    - Description/Symptoms: The transmission fails to engage overdrive gear, resulting in reduced highway fuel economy.
    - Solution: Replace the overdrive solenoid.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    - Recalls: None Found

**5-Speed Manual**

* **Clutch Failure:**
    - Description/Symptoms: The clutch slips or becomes difficult to engage.
    - Solution: Replace the clutch assembly.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
    - Recalls: None Found

* **Transmission Noise:**
    - Description/Symptoms: The transmission makes a whining or grinding noise when in gear.
    - Solution: Replace the affected gears or bearings.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
    - Recalls: None Found

**6-Speed Automatic**

* **Shift Solenoid Failure:**
    - Description/Symptoms: The shift solenoid fails, causing the transmission to shift erratically or not at all.
    - Solution: Replace the shift solenoid.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
    - Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Leaf Spring Failure:**
    - Description/Symptoms: The leaf springs break or sag, causing the vehicle to ride rough and handle poorly.
    - Solution: Replace the leaf springs.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
    - Recalls: None Found

* **Shock Absorber Failure:**
    - Description/Symptoms: The shock absorbers wear out, resulting in a bumpy ride and reduced handling.
    - Solution: Replace the shock absorbers.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+
    - Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Alternator Failure:**
    - Description/Symptoms: The alternator fails, causing the battery to drain and the vehicle to stall.
    - Solution: Replace the alternator.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    - Recalls: None Found

* **Starter Failure:**
    - Description/Symptoms: The starter fails, preventing the engine from starting.
    - Solution: Replace the starter.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+
    - Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Rust:**
    - Description/Symptoms: The vehicle develops rust on the body panels, especially in areas prone to moisture.
    - Solution: Repair or replace the rusted panels.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $5,000+
    - Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Dashboard Cracking:**
    - Description/Symptoms: The dashboard develops cracks due to sun exposure or age.
    - Solution: Replace the dashboard.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
    - Recalls: None Found

* **Seat Upholstery Tearing:**
    - Description/Symptoms: The seat upholstery tears or becomes worn due to use or sun exposure.
    - Solution: Replace or reupholster the seats.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+
    - Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

The 1997 Ford E-350 Econoline is a versatile van that is generally reliable. However, it is prone to certain issues, particularly in its engine and transmission. The severity of these issues can range from minor to major, and the cost to fix them can be significant. Potential buyers should be aware of these issues and factor them into their purchase decision.
