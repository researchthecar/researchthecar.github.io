**Introduction:**

The 1994 Ford E-350 Econoline was a versatile full-size van offered with various engine and transmission options. It was praised for its spacious interior and durability, but also criticized for its thirsty engine and rough ride.

**Engine**

**5.8L V8 (225 hp, 360 lb-ft torque)**

* **Intake Manifold Gasket Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaks, engine overheating, reduced power
    * Solution: Replace intake manifold gasket
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Head Gasket Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaks, engine overheating, white smoke from exhaust
    * Solution: Replace head gasket(s)
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Fuel Pump Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Engine stalling, difficulty starting
    * Solution: Replace fuel pump
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+
    * Recalls: None Found

**7.5L V8 (245 hp, 420 lb-ft torque)**

* **Oil Pan Gasket Leak:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks from oil pan
    * Solution: Replace oil pan gasket
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Timing Chain Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Rattling noise from engine, loss of power
    * Solution: Replace timing chain and gears
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**4-Speed Automatic (A4LD)**

* **Transmission Slipping:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Poor acceleration, delayed shifting
    * Solution: Rebuild or replace transmission
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,500+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Overdrive Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Inability to shift into overdrive gear
    * Solution: Replace overdrive solenoid
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**5-Speed Automatic (5R110W)**

* **Torque Converter Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Shuddering, slipping, no forward or reverse gears
    * Solution: Replace torque converter
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Valve Body Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Erratic shifting, no shifting
    * Solution: Replace valve body
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,200+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Ball Joint Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Clunking noise from front suspension, loose steering
    * Solution: Replace ball joints
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+ per side
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Leaf Spring Breakage:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Sagging rear suspension, uneven tire wear
    * Solution: Replace leaf springs
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,200+ per axle
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Starter Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Difficulty starting, grinding noise from starter
    * Solution: Replace starter
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Alternator Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Dimming headlights, dead battery
    * Solution: Replace alternator
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Rust on Body Panels:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Rust spots and holes on exterior panels
    * Solution: Repair or replace panels
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on extent of damage
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **A/C Compressor Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Inability to cool cabin
    * Solution: Replace A/C compressor
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $600 - $1,500+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Power Window Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Windows not rolling up or down
    * Solution: Replace power window motor or switch
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+ per window
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

The 1994 Ford E-350 Econoline is a reliable and versatile vehicle, but it is not without its issues. Common engine concerns include intake manifold and head gasket failures. Transmission problems can range from slipping to failure. Suspension components such as ball joints and leaf springs may need replacement over time. Electrical issues typically involve the starter and alternator. Rust can also be a concern on older models. While the interior is generally reliable, A/C compressor failures and power window problems have been reported. Overall, the severity and cost of issues can vary, but regular maintenance and timely repairs can help extend the lifespan of the vehicle.
