**2018 Ford F-550 Super Duty**

The 2018 Ford F-550 Super Duty is a heavy-duty pickup truck that offers a range of engine and transmission options. The standard engine is a 6.2L V8 with 385 horsepower and 430 pound-feet of torque. Optional engines include a 6.7L Power Stroke diesel V8 with 450 horsepower and 930 pound-feet of torque, and a 7.3L V8 with 430 horsepower and 475 pound-feet of torque. Transmission options include a 6-speed automatic, a 10-speed automatic, and a 6-speed manual.

The F-550 Super Duty is a popular choice for heavy-duty towing and hauling tasks. It offers a spacious cabin with comfortable seating for up to six passengers. The truck also comes with a wide range of features, including a touch-screen infotainment system, a rearview camera, and a trailer brake controller.

However, the F-550 Super Duty has also been plagued by a number of issues. Here is a comprehensive list of the specific problems people are having with the vehicles:

**Engine**

**6.2L V8**

* **Intake manifold failure:** The intake manifold can crack, causing a loss of power and fuel efficiency. The solution is to replace the intake manifold. Estimated cost to fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
* **Timing chain failure:** The timing chain can stretch or break, causing the engine to lose power or stall. The solution is to replace the timing chain and related components. Estimated cost to fix: $2,000 - $4,000+
* **Fuel injector failure:** The fuel injectors can fail, causing a loss of power or a rough idle. The solution is to replace the fuel injectors. Estimated cost to fix: $500 - $1,000+
* **EGR valve failure:** The EGR valve can fail, causing a loss of power or a rough idle. The solution is to replace the EGR valve. Estimated cost to fix: $300 - $500+

**6.7L Power Stroke V8**

* **Turbocharger failure:** The turbocharger can fail, causing a loss of power or a rough idle. The solution is to replace the turbocharger. Estimated cost to fix: $2,000 - $4,000+
* **Diesel particulate filter (DPF) failure:** The DPF can become clogged, causing a loss of power or a rough idle. The solution is to clean or replace the DPF. Estimated cost to fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
* **Injector failure:** The injectors can fail, causing a loss of power or a rough idle. The solution is to replace the injectors. Estimated cost to fix: $500 - $1,000+
* **High-pressure fuel pump (HPFP) failure:** The HPFP can fail, causing a loss of power or a rough idle. The solution is to replace the HPFP. Estimated cost to fix: $1,000 - $2,000+

* **Leaf spring failure:** The leaf springs can break or sag, causing the truck to ride rough or lose stability. The solution is to replace the leaf springs. Estimated cost to fix: $500 - $1,000+
* **Shock absorber failure:** The shock absorbers can wear out prematurely, causing the truck to ride rough or lose stability. The solution is to replace the shock absorbers. Estimated cost to fix: $200 - $400+
* **Sway bar link failure:** The sway bar links can wear out prematurely, causing the truck to handle poorly or make noise. The solution is to replace the sway bar links. Estimated cost to fix: $100 - $200+
