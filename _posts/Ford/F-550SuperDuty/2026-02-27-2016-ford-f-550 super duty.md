# 2016 Ford F-550 Super Duty

### Overview
The 2016 Ford F-550 Super Duty is a heavy-duty commercial truck designed for demanding tasks, offering robust performance and capability. Reliability for this model year is generally strong, particularly with its core powertrain components. Owners can expect a durable vehicle suited for towing, hauling, and vocational use. While specific issues are infrequent, like any complex machine, certain areas may occasionally require attention. This analysis focuses on documented technical reliability concerns beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential problem areas and their associated costs for the available engine and transmission configurations.

---


## Engine

### 6.7L Power Stroke V8 Turbo Diesel
**EGR Cooler Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Coolant loss, white smoke from exhaust, overheating.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the EGR cooler.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1500

**Turbocharger Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of power, excessive smoke, whistling noises.
- **Solution:** Turbocharger inspection, cleaning, or replacement.
- **Cost:** $1200 - $2500

### Summary Verdict
The 2016 Ford F-550 Super Duty demonstrates commendable reliability, especially for its class. The 6.7L Power Stroke diesel engine, while robust, has occasional reports of EGR cooler leaks and turbocharger issues, which are manageable with timely repairs. The 6R140 transmission is a strong performer with no significant widespread reliability concerns. Suspension, electrical, exterior, and interior components are generally well-built and hold up to the demands of commercial use. Overall, the F-550 is a dependable workhorse, with most potential issues being specific to the diesel powertrain and not indicative of systemic flaws.
