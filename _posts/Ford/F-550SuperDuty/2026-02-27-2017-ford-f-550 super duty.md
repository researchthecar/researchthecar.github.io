# 2017 Ford F-550 Super Duty

### Overview
The 2017 Ford F-550 Super Duty is a heavy-duty commercial truck designed for demanding work applications. Reliability for this model year is generally strong, particularly with its robust powertrain options. Owners and fleet managers can expect solid performance, though like any commercial vehicle, specific components may experience issues under heavy use. This analysis focuses on documented, non-wear-and-tear related problems reported by owners and repair data, aiming to provide a clear picture of potential reliability concerns across key vehicle systems. The F-550's construction prioritizes durability, which is reflected in its overall reliability ratings.

---


## Engine

### 6.7L Power Stroke V8 Turbo Diesel
**EGR Cooler Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Coolant loss, white smoke from exhaust, overheating.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the EGR cooler assembly.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1500

**Turbocharger Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of power, whistling noises, black smoke.
- **Solution:** Turbocharger inspection, cleaning, or replacement.
- **Cost:** $1200 - $2500

### Summary Verdict
The 2017 Ford F-550 Super Duty demonstrates good overall reliability, especially concerning its powertrain. The 6.7L Power Stroke diesel engine, while generally robust, has seen some reports of EGR cooler leaks and turbocharger issues, which are not uncommon for high-mileage or heavily worked diesel engines. The 6R140 transmission is a strong performer with no widespread reliability concerns. Electrical components like the alternator and infotainment system can occasionally present issues, but these are typically repairable. Exterior and interior components are largely free of significant, recurring problems. For fleet owners, proactive maintenance and addressing early signs of the noted engine issues will contribute to sustained reliability.
