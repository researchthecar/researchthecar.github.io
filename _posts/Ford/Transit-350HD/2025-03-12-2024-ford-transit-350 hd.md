Okay, here's a breakdown of potential issues and complaints for the 2024 Ford Transit-350 HD, focusing on common configurations and following your specified format.

The 2024 Ford Transit-350 HD is a popular choice for commercial and recreational users due to its versatility and cargo capacity. Engine options include a 3.5L EcoBoost V6 producing 310 horsepower and 400 lb-ft of torque and a 3.5L PFDi V6 rated at 275 horsepower and 262 lb-ft of torque. Both engines are paired with a 10-speed automatic transmission. Owners often praise the Transit's spacious interior and customization options but sometimes express concerns about fuel economy and certain reliability issues.

**Engine**

**3.5L EcoBoost V6**

*   **Turbocharger Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Whining noises, loss of power, check engine light. Potential for turbo failure due to oil starvation or excessive wear.
    *   Solution: Turbocharger replacement. Thorough inspection of oil lines and related components to ensure proper lubrication.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Direct Fuel Injection Problems**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rough idling, misfires, decreased fuel economy. Carbon buildup on intake valves is common with direct injection engines.
    *   Solution: Intake valve cleaning (walnut blasting or chemical cleaning). Installation of a catch can to mitigate future buildup.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**3.5L PFDi V6**

*   **No Issues Found**

**Transmission**

**10-Speed Automatic (10R80)**

*   **Harsh Shifting/Clunking**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Noticeable harsh shifts between gears, especially at lower speeds. Clunking noises during shifting or acceleration.
    *   Solution: Software reflash/reprogramming of the transmission control module (TCM). In some cases, valve body replacement may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300 (reflash); $800 - $2,000+ (valve body)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Delayed Engagement**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Delay between shifting into drive or reverse and the vehicle actually moving. Can be more pronounced when the transmission is cold.
    *   Solution: Check transmission fluid level and condition. Software update/reprogramming. Internal inspection of transmission if problem persists.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $150 (fluid/check); $100 - $300 (reflash); $1,000 - $3,000+ (internal repair/replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Rear Leaf Spring Squeaking**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Annoying squeaking noise coming from the rear suspension, especially when driving over bumps.
    *   Solution: Lubricating the leaf spring bushings. In severe cases, replacement of the bushings or leaf springs may be required.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $150 (lubrication); $200 - $500+ (bushings/springs)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Freezing/Crashing**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The infotainment system (SYNC 4) intermittently freezes, crashes, or becomes unresponsive.
    *   Solution: Software update for the SYNC system.  Hard reset of the system. Potential replacement of the APIM (accessory protocol interface module) if the problem persists.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 (update); $100 - $300 (diagnostic/reset); $500 - $1,500+ (APIM replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Sensor Malfunctions (e.g., Blind Spot Monitoring)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Blind spot monitoring system gives false alerts or stops working altogether.  Parking sensors may also exhibit similar issues.
    *   Solution: Diagnostic scan to identify the faulty sensor. Replacement of the malfunctioning sensor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+ (per sensor)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Sliding Door Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Difficulty opening or closing the sliding door. Motor malfunction (if equipped with power sliding door).
    *   Solution: Lubricating door tracks and hinges.  Repair or replacement of the power sliding door motor or related components.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $150 (lubrication); $300 - $1,000+ (motor/components)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Rattling/Vibrations**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Various rattles and vibrations coming from the interior trim panels, especially in the cargo area.
    *   Solution: Identifying the source of the rattle and securing the loose trim panels. Applying sound deadening material.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $200+ (depending on complexity)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2024 Ford Transit-350 HD, while generally well-regarded, exhibits some potential issues. The 3.5L EcoBoost engine could have Turbocharger Issues. The 10-speed transmission can be problematic with harsh shifting and delayed engagement. Electrical issues such as infotainment glitches and sensor malfunctions are also reported. Suspension squeaks and interior rattles are common annoyances. The severity of these issues ranges from minor inconveniences to more costly repairs. Potential buyers should thoroughly inspect the vehicle, review its maintenance history, and be aware of these potential problem areas.
