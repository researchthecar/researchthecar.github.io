**Introduction:**

The 2017 Ford Transit-350 HD is a heavy-duty commercial van that comes with three engine and transmission configurations:

- 3.5L V6 with 10-speed automatic transmission (275 horsepower, 420 lb-ft torque)
- 3.5L EcoBoost V6 with 10-speed automatic transmission (310 horsepower, 400 lb-ft torque)
- 3.2L Power Stroke Diesel with 6-speed automatic transmission (185 horsepower, 350 lb-ft torque)

The Transit-350 HD is known for its spacious cargo area, powerful engines, and impressive towing and payload capacities. However, some owners have reported various issues with the vehicle, as outlined below:

**Engine**

**3.5L V6**

- **Fuel Injector Failure:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Rough idling, decreased engine power, and check engine light illumination
    - Solution: Replace faulty fuel injectors
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    - Recalls: None Found
- **Oil Leak from Valve Cover Gasket:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Oil leaking from the valve cover area, noticeable burning oil smell
    - Solution: Replace valve cover gasket
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    - Recalls: None Found

**3.5L EcoBoost V6**

- **Turbocharger Actuator Issues:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Limited engine power, turbocharger not engaging, check engine light illumination
    - Solution: Replace faulty turbocharger actuator
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
    - Recalls: None Found

**3.2L Power Stroke Diesel**

- **High-Pressure Fuel Pump Failure:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Difficulty starting, engine stalling, decreased engine power
    - Solution: Replace high-pressure fuel pump
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+
    - Recalls: None Found
- **Injector O-Ring Leaks:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Fuel leaking from injector O-rings, noticeable fuel smell
    - Solution: Replace faulty injector O-rings
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
    - Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**10-Speed Automatic**

- **Shifting Problems:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Hesitation or jerking during gear shifts, lack of response from transmission
    - Solution: Reprogram transmission control module (TCM) or replace transmission solenoids
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+
    - Recalls: None Found
- **Transmission Fluid Leak:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Leaking transmission fluid, visible on the ground or undercarriage
    - Solution: Identify and fix the source of the leak, replace seals or gaskets as necessary
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+
    - Recalls: None Found

**6-Speed Automatic**

- **Transmission Slipping:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Engine RPMs increasing without a corresponding increase in speed, loss of power
    - Solution: Repair or rebuild transmission
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
    - Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

- **Worn Front Control Arm Bushings:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Clunking or rattling noise from the front suspension, uneven tire wear
    - Solution: Replace worn control arm bushings
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    - Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

- **Faulty Battery Sensor:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Incorrect battery charge readings, premature battery discharge
    - Solution: Replace faulty battery sensor
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $250+
    - Recalls: None Found
- **Alternator Failure:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Dimming headlights, loss of electrical power, battery not charging
    - Solution: Replace faulty alternator
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    - Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

- **Rust on Body Panels:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Rust forming on the body, typically on the wheel arches, door frames, or cargo door
    - Solution: Repair and refinish rusted areas, apply rust protection
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+
    - Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

- **Dashboard Rattle:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Noises and vibrations originating from the dashboard area
    - Solution: Identify and fix the source of the rattle, tighten loose screws or replace faulty parts
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    - Recalls: None Found
- **Climate Control Malfunction:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Heating or cooling system not functioning properly, temperature not regulating
    - Solution: Repair or replace faulty climate control components
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+
    - Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2017 Ford Transit-350 HD has been reported to have various issues, ranging in severity and cost. Engine problems are relatively common, particularly with the 3.5L V6 and 3.5L EcoBoost V6 engines. Transmission issues, specifically with the 10-speed automatic, have also been reported. Other issues include suspension wear, electrical malfunctions, exterior rust, and interior rattles. Overall, the severity and cost of these issues can vary depending on the specific problem and the condition of the vehicle. It is recommended to consult with a qualified mechanic for diagnosis and repair.
