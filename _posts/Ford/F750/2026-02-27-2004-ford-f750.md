# 2004 Ford F750

### Overview
The 2004 Ford F-750 is a heavy-duty commercial truck designed for demanding vocational tasks. Reliability for this model year is generally considered robust, particularly for its core powertrain components, reflecting its purpose-built nature. However, like any commercial vehicle, specific issues can arise depending on usage, maintenance, and the particular configuration. This analysis focuses on documented technical reliability concerns beyond routine maintenance, examining common engine and transmission options, as well as other key vehicle systems. Understanding these potential weak points can aid in proactive maintenance and informed purchasing decisions for fleet managers and owner-operators.

---


## Engine

### 7.2L Caterpillar C7 Diesel
**Fuel Injector Problems**
- **Symptoms:** Rough idling, loss of power, increased exhaust smoke, engine misfires.
- **Solution:** Replacement of faulty fuel injectors.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1500 per injector

**EGR Cooler Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** White smoke from exhaust, coolant loss, overheating, sweet smell in exhaust.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the EGR cooler.
- **Cost:** $600 - $1200


### 5.9L Cummins ISB Diesel
**Fuel Pump Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Difficulty starting, engine stalling, loss of power, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the fuel lift pump.
- **Cost:** $400 - $800

**Turbocharger Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of power, excessive smoke, whistling noises from the engine bay.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of the turbocharger.
- **Cost:** $1000 - $2500


### 7.8L Ford Power Stroke Diesel
**Injector Harness Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Rough running, misfires, hard starting, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the injector harness.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600

**Water Pump Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Coolant leaks from the front of the engine, overheating.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the water pump.
- **Cost:** $400 - $700


## Transmission

### Allison 2000 Series Automatic
**Torque Converter Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Slipping gears, shuddering during acceleration, delayed engagement.
- **Solution:** Rebuild or replacement of the torque converter.
- **Cost:** $1200 - $2500


### Allison 3000 Series Automatic
**Transmission Fluid Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Visible fluid leaks, low fluid level, potential for overheating and gear damage.
- **Solution:** Repair of seals and gaskets, topping up fluid.
- **Cost:** $300 - $700


### Eaton Fuller Manual Transmissions (Various Models)
**Clutch Actuator Problems**
- **Symptoms:** Stiff clutch pedal, difficulty shifting gears, grinding noises.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the clutch actuator or related components.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1000


## Suspension

### Heavy-Duty Leaf Spring Suspension
**Leaf Spring Breakage**
- **Symptoms:** Sagging rear end, uneven ride height, clunking noises over bumps.
- **Solution:** Replacement of broken leaf springs.
- **Cost:** $400 - $800 per axle


### Air Suspension (Optional)
**Air Bag Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle sagging on one side, slow leak down, compressor running excessively.
- **Solution:** Replacement of leaking air bags or air lines.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600 per corner


## Electrical

### Standard Electrical System
**Alternator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Battery warning light illuminated, dimming headlights, electrical system malfunctions.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the alternator.
- **Cost:** $400 - $800

**Wiring Harness Corrosion**
- **Symptoms:** Intermittent electrical issues, non-functioning lights or accessories, corrosion on connectors.
- **Solution:** Cleaning and sealing of corroded connections, or replacement of harness sections.
- **Cost:** $200 - $600


## Exterior

### Body Panels and Frame
**Frame Rust (Severe Climates)**
- **Symptoms:** Visible rust on frame rails, potential structural weakening.
- **Solution:** Frame repair or treatment, depending on severity.
- **Cost:** $500 - $2000+

**Door Latch Mechanism Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Doors not opening or closing properly, difficulty securing doors.
- **Solution:** Lubrication or replacement of door latch components.
- **Cost:** $150 - $300


## Interior

### Cabin Components
**HVAC Blower Motor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** No airflow from vents, or intermittent airflow, regardless of fan speed setting.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the HVAC blower motor.
- **Cost:** $200 - $400

**Dashboard Gauge Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** Inaccurate readings or complete failure of speedometer, tachometer, or other gauges.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of faulty gauge cluster or individual gauges.
- **Cost:** $300 - $700


--- 
### Summary Verdict
The 2004 Ford F-750, while a capable workhorse, exhibits some common reliability concerns typical of heavy-duty trucks. The diesel engines, particularly the Caterpillar C7 and Cummins ISB, can experience issues with fuel injectors, fuel pumps, and turbochargers. Transmission reliability is generally good with Allison automatics, though torque converters and fluid leaks can occur. Suspension components like leaf springs and air bags are subject to wear and potential breakage under heavy use. Electrical systems can suffer from alternator failures and wiring corrosion, especially in harsh environments. Interior components like the HVAC blower motor and dashboard gauges are also potential points of failure. Proactive maintenance and addressing issues promptly are crucial for maximizing the lifespan and operational efficiency of these vehicles.
