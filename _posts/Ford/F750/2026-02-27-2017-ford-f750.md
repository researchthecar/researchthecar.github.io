# 2017 Ford F750

### Overview
The 2017 Ford F-750 is a heavy-duty commercial truck designed for demanding vocational applications. Reliability is a key factor for fleet operators and business owners, and this model generally performs well under strenuous conditions. Ford's F-750 is known for its robust construction and the availability of powerful, proven powertrains. While specific issues can arise with any vehicle, particularly in commercial use, the F-750's design prioritizes durability and serviceability. This analysis focuses on documented technical reliability concerns beyond routine maintenance, examining common failure points and their associated repair information for various configurations.

---


## Engine

### 6.7L Power Stroke V8 Diesel
**EGR Cooler Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Coolant loss, white smoke from exhaust, overheating.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the EGR cooler assembly.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1500

**Fuel Injector Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Rough idle, loss of power, increased fuel consumption, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of faulty fuel injectors.
- **Cost:** $1200 - $2500


### 6.8L Triton V10 Gas
**Spark Plug Blow-out**
- **Symptoms:** Misfires, rough running, loss of power, audible popping sound from engine.
- **Solution:** Installation of updated spark plug inserts or replacement of cylinder heads.
- **Cost:** $600 - $1800

**Intake Manifold Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Vacuum leaks, rough idle, check engine light with lean codes.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the intake manifold gasket or the manifold itself.
- **Cost:** $400 - $800

### Summary Verdict
The 2017 Ford F-750 demonstrates solid reliability, particularly with its robust diesel powertrain options. The 6.7L Power Stroke engine, while capable, can experience EGR cooler leaks and fuel injector issues, which are common in high-mileage diesel applications. The 6.8L V10 gas engine may face spark plug blow-out and intake manifold leaks. Automatic and manual transmission options are generally dependable. Electrical system components like alternators and wiring harnesses are the most frequently cited areas for potential repair. Overall, the F-750 is a workhorse that, with proper maintenance, offers good long-term reliability for commercial use, with most reported issues being repairable and not indicative of systemic design flaws.
