# 2019 Ford F750

### Overview
The 2019 Ford F-750 is a heavy-duty commercial truck designed for demanding vocational applications. Reliability is a key consideration for fleet operators and business owners. This analysis focuses on documented technical issues beyond routine maintenance, examining common problem areas across its available powertrains and major systems. While generally robust, specific components and configurations can exhibit certain tendencies that impact operational uptime and repair costs. Understanding these potential issues is crucial for proactive maintenance and informed purchasing decisions.

## Engine

### 6.7L Power Stroke V8 Diesel
**Diesel Particulate Filter (DPF) Regeneration Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Check Engine light illumination, reduced engine power, increased fuel consumption, exhaust smell.
- **Solution:** Forced regeneration, DPF cleaning, or replacement. Software updates may also be required.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1,500

**EGR System Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** Rough idling, loss of power, black smoke from exhaust, Check Engine light.
- **Solution:** Cleaning or replacement of EGR valve and cooler. Potential leaks in associated plumbing.
- **Cost:** $400 - $1,200

### Summary Verdict
The 2019 Ford F-750 generally demonstrates good reliability, particularly with its gasoline engine option. The diesel engine, while powerful, can experience issues related to emissions control systems like the DPF and EGR, which are common in modern diesel powertrains and require diligent maintenance. Transmission and chassis components are typically robust for heavy-duty use. Electrical, exterior, and interior systems show no widespread critical failures. For operators relying on the diesel variant, proactive monitoring and maintenance of the emissions system are recommended to minimize downtime and repair expenses. Overall, the F-750 is a dependable workhorse when properly cared for.
