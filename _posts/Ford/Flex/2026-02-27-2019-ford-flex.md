# 2019 Ford Flex Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2019 Ford Flex offers a distinct boxy design and generous interior passenger and cargo space, making it a popular choice for families looking for utility. While it provides a comfortable ride and practical features, used-car buyers should research specific mechanical tendencies. Notable areas of concern include rearview camera failures and exterior trim durability, alongside critical engine considerations involving the cooling system.

---

## Engine

### 3.5L Ti-VCT V6

The standard naturally aspirated 3.5-liter V6 engine produces approximately 287 horsepower and 254 lb-ft of torque. It provides adequate power for daily driving and standard family hauling duties.

### 3.5L EcoBoost Twin-Turbocharged V6

The available 3.5-liter twin-turbocharged EcoBoost V6 generates 365 horsepower and 350 lb-ft of torque, paired with standard all-wheel drive. While it delivers brisk acceleration, buyers must monitor for internal water pump wear that can jeopardize the engine.

**Water Pump Failure and Coolant Leak**
- **Symptoms:** Low coolant levels, coolant visible on the lower part of the engine, unusual engine noises, or sudden engine stall and severe internal engine damage if coolant leaks into the oil system.
- **Solution:** Inspect the water pump and weep hole regularly; replace the water pump immediately if signs of leakage appear to prevent catastrophic engine failure.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed SelectShift Automatic Transmission

The 6-speed automatic transmission is paired with either front-wheel drive or all-wheel drive depending on the engine selection. It generally offers smooth shifting, though routine fluid maintenance is recommended for longevity.

## Electrical

### Back Over Prevention

The backup camera and parking assistance features help improve visibility, but electrical display glitches have been widely reported.

**Rearview Camera Distortion and Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The rearview camera display shows a distorted, inverted, intermittent, or completely blank video image when shifting into reverse.
- **Solution:** Check for open manufacturer recalls regarding rearview camera software updates or hardware replacements, though remedy parts may face availability delays.

### Electrical System

Electrical accessories and wiring require normal oversight, though isolated reports indicate occasional unexpected powertrain power loss.

**Unexpected Engine Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle suddenly loses power and stalls while driving without warning indicator lights, requiring multiple attempts to restart.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified mechanic diagnose the ignition switch, fuel delivery system, or electrical ground connections.

## Exterior

### Pillar Trim and Body Moldings

Exterior body components generally hold up well, with the exception of certain decorative pillar trim pieces prone to loosening.

**Detaching B-Pillar and Door Trim**
- **Symptoms:** Exterior B-pillar or door trim panels loosen while driving or going through car washes, potentially striking windows or detaching completely.
- **Solution:** Inspect exterior trim security and replace damaged or loose clips and adhesive moldings before they detach entirely on the highway.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V548000: Electrical System:Alternator/Generator/Regulator](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Flex&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** The power supply cables at the starter and the alternator may not have been properly secured, possibly resulting in an electrical arc. An electrical arc can increase the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the alternator and starter motor power supply cables to verify that they are properly secured, correcting them as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V951000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Flex&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** A rearview camera that displays a blank or distorted image can reduce the drivers view of what is behind the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the rearview camera as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V695000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Flex&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** A rearview camera that fails to properly display an image can reduce the driver’s view behind the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the rearview camera as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V732000: Structure:Body](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Flex&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** Door trim that detaches while driving can create a road hazard, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will repair or replace the B-pillar trim as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2019 Ford Flex remains an appealing choice for buyers seeking unique styling and exceptional passenger room. Prospective owners should carefully inspect the vehicle's maintenance history, verify the status of open rearview camera recalls, and check for any signs of coolant leaks associated with the water pump. A thorough pre-purchase inspection by a trusted mechanic can help identify these potential trouble spots before finalizing a purchase.
