# 2018 Ford Flex Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2018 Ford Flex is a distinctive, boxy three-row crossover known for its spacious interior and polarizing styling. While many owners appreciate its comfort and utility, used car buyers should pay close attention to potential powertrain vulnerabilities, exterior trim detachment, and camera glitches. Reviewing maintenance records and verifying whether safety updates have been performed are key steps before purchasing.

---

## Engine

### 3.5L V6 (Naturally Aspirated)

The base 3.5-liter naturally aspirated V6 engine produces approximately 287 horsepower and 254 lb-ft of torque, offering adequate power for daily driving. It provides standard motivation for lower trims, though buyers should remain mindful of standard coolant pump maintenance intervals.

### 3.5L EcoBoost V6 (Twin-Turbocharged)

The available 3.5-liter twin-turbocharged EcoBoost V6 generates a robust 365 horsepower and 350 lb-ft of torque, paired with standard all-wheel drive. It delivers strong acceleration but shares the same internal water pump design considerations as the base V6.

**Internal Water Pump Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Antifreeze leaks mixing into engine oil, potentially leading to catastrophic engine damage if ignored.
- **Solution:** Monitor coolant levels regularly; replace the water pump proactively or service the engine immediately if coolant contamination is suspected.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed SelectShift Automatic Transmission

The 6-speed automatic transmission handles power delivery across front-wheel and all-wheel-drive configurations. Regular fluid service is recommended to maintain smooth operation.

**Hard Shifting and Powertrain Hesitation**
- **Symptoms:** Rough gear engagement, delayed shifts, and lower-than-expected fuel efficiency.
- **Solution:** Check transmission fluid condition and update powertrain control modules if necessary.

## Steering

### Electronic Power Steering

Electric power-assist steering provides responsive handling adjustments across varying speeds.

**Power Steering Loss**
- **Symptoms:** Sudden heavy steering feel or complete loss of power steering assistance while driving at high speeds.
- **Solution:** Inspect steering rack electrical connections and check for diagnostic trouble codes in the power steering module.

## Electrical

### APIM / SYNC Infotainment System

The electrical architecture governs cabin tech, driver assistance features, and infotainment display functions.

**Infotainment and Backup Camera Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Intermittent audio popping, navigation freeze, missing touchscreen prompts, and backup camera distortion or black screen.
- **Solution:** Reset or replace the Accessory Protocol Interface Module (APIM) or update the infotainment software.

## Exterior

### B-Pillar Trim and Keypad

Exterior trim elements include decorative and functional door pillars, weatherstripping, and keyless entry pads.

**Detaching Exterior B-Pillar Trim**
- **Symptoms:** Driver's side exterior door trim or keypad loosens at highway speeds and may detach completely.
- **Solution:** Inspect exterior door trim clips and replace unsecured moldings before they detach on the road.

## Safety

### Rear Toe Links

The rear suspension setup relies on control arms and toe links to maintain proper alignment and handling stability.

**Rear Suspension Toe Link Fracture**
- **Symptoms:** Sudden loss of vehicle control, loud snapping noises, or unusual wheel alignment while driving.
- **Solution:** Inspect rear suspension components for rust or stress, and ensure applicable rear suspension safety campaigns have been completed.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V072000: Suspension:Rear](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Flex&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** A fractured rear toe link will cause a sudden change in vehicle handling and increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will replace the rear suspension toe links, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V695000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Flex&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** A rearview camera that fails to properly display an image can reduce the driver’s view behind the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the rearview camera as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V732000: Structure:Body](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Flex&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** Door trim that detaches while driving can create a road hazard, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will repair or replace the B-pillar trim as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2018 Ford Flex remains an appealing choice for families seeking maximum cargo room and distinctive styling, but it requires careful vetting. Buyers should thoroughly inspect the vehicle for suspension wear, verify the status of rear camera and trim recalls, and listen closely for any engine coolant anomalies during a pre-purchase inspection.
