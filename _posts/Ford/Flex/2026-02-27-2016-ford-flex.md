# 2016 Ford Flex Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2016 Ford Flex offers a distinct boxy design, expansive interior room, and comfortable ride quality that makes it a popular choice for families. However, prospective buyers should carefully review potential mechanical and electrical trouble spots. While it provides solid utility, paying close attention to maintenance history and known recurring failures can save buyers from unexpected repair bills.

---

## Engine

### 3.5L Ti-VCT V6

The standard naturally aspirated 3.5-liter V6 engine produces around 287 horsepower and 254 lb-ft of torque, delivering adequate daily power. This traditional setup is generally straightforward, though routine cooling system maintenance remains important.

### 3.5L EcoBoost Twin-Turbo V6

The optional twin-turbocharged EcoBoost V6 boosts output to 365 horsepower and 350 lb-ft of torque for robust acceleration. Buyers should closely inspect the cooling system and turbo components, as performance maintenance is critical on forced-induction setups.

**Cooling System and Misfires**
- **Symptoms:** The engine may overheat or experience sudden misfires, sometimes leading to unexpected stalling in traffic.
- **Solution:** Inspect water pumps, coolant levels, and ignition components regularly; replace failing cooling parts immediately.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed SelectShift Automatic

The 6-speed automatic transmission is paired with front-wheel drive or available all-wheel drive. Monitoring fluid condition and transfer case health is vital to ensuring long-term durability.

**Power Transfer Unit and Fluid Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Burning fluid smells, unusual drivetrain noises, or catastrophic internal failure of the power transfer unit and transmission casing.
- **Solution:** Check transmission and transfer case fluid condition frequently; replace failing units or seals as required.

## Steering

### Power Steering System

Electronic power steering provides light effort at low speeds, but sudden assist failures can compromise vehicle control.

**Steering Assist Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The steering wheel suddenly becomes extremely heavy and difficult to turn while driving, accompanied by advance traction control or steering failure warning lights.
- **Solution:** Diagnose electronic power-assist steering modules and wiring connections; replace faulty steering components as necessary.

## Electrical

### Backup Camera and Wiring

Cabin electronics generally function well, though rear visibility systems are prone to intermittent connectivity or camera hardware failures.

**Rear Camera Display Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** The backup camera displays a blank, black, upside-down, or flickering image, or presents a camera unavailable warning.
- **Solution:** Inspect camera wiring harnesses and display modules; replace defective backup camera units.

## Exterior

### Door Latches

Interior hardware and trim hold up reasonably well over time, though individual door latch mechanisms can suffer from internal spring fatigue.

**Fractured Latch Springs**
- **Symptoms:** Doors fail to close properly or may pop open while the vehicle is in motion due to broken internal pawl springs.
- **Solution:** Replace the damaged door latch assembly.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V925000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Engine:Gasoline:Turbo/Supercharger](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Flex&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** An oil leak, in the presence of an ignition source, increases the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will inspect, and if necessary, replace the turbocharger oil supply tubes, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V072000: Suspension:Rear](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Flex&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** A fractured rear toe link will cause a sudden change in vehicle handling and increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will replace the rear suspension toe links, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V695000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Flex&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** A rearview camera that fails to properly display an image can reduce the driver’s view behind the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the rearview camera as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2016 Ford Flex remains a practical and spacious choice for hauling passengers and cargo, but used-car buyers should perform a thorough pre-purchase inspection. Pay close attention to cooling system integrity, powertrain fluid conditions, and electronic accessories like the backup camera and power steering. Verifying maintenance records will significantly reduce the risk of inheriting unresolved mechanical issues.
