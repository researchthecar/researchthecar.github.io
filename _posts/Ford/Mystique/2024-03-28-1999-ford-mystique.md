**Introduction to the 1999 Ford Mystique**

The 1999 Ford Mystique is a mid-size sedan that was offered with a variety of engine and transmission options. The base engine was a 2.0L Zetec inline-four, which produced 125 horsepower and 135 lb-ft of torque. A 2.5L V6 was also available, producing 170 horsepower and 165 lb-ft of torque. Both engines could be paired with either a 5-speed manual transmission or a 4-speed automatic transmission.

The Mystique was praised for its comfortable interior, spacious trunk, and affordable price. However, it was also criticized for its lack of power, poor handling, and low-quality materials.

**Engine**

**2.0L Zetec I4**

* **Intake manifold gasket failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Coolant or oil leaks from the intake manifold gasket, causing overheating or engine damage.
    * Solution: Replace the intake manifold gasket.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Timing belt failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The timing belt snaps, causing the engine to stall or seize.
    * Solution: Replace the timing belt.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800+
    * Recalls: None Found

**2.5L V6**

* **Intake manifold gasket failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Coolant or oil leaks from the intake manifold gasket, causing overheating or engine damage.
    * Solution: Replace the intake manifold gasket.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $600+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Spark plug failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Worn or damaged spark plugs cause misfires, rough idle, and reduced power.
    * Solution: Replace the spark plugs.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**5-Speed Manual Transmission**

* **Clutch failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The clutch slips, chatters, or becomes difficult to engage.
    * Solution: Replace the clutch.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Transmission mount failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Worn or damaged transmission mounts cause excessive vibration and noise.
    * Solution: Replace the transmission mounts.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
    * Recalls: None Found

**4-Speed Automatic Transmission**

* **Transmission solenoid failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Faulty solenoids cause the transmission to shift erratically or slip out of gear.
    * Solution: Replace the transmission solenoid.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Torque converter failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: A failing torque converter causes shuddering, slipping, or loss of power.
    * Solution: Replace the torque converter.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Struts/Shock absorbers failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Worn or damaged struts/shock absorbers cause poor handling, excessive bounce, and tire wear.
    * Solution: Replace the struts/shock absorbers.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $600+ per axle
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Control arm bushings failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Worn or damaged control arm bushings cause excessive vibration and noise, and can affect alignment.
    * Solution: Replace the control arm bushings.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $300+ per side
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Alternator failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: A failing alternator causes dim lights, difficulty starting, and battery drain.
    * Solution: Replace the alternator.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $600+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Battery failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: A dead battery prevents the vehicle from starting.
    * Solution: Replace the battery.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Headlight failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Dim or inoperable headlights.
    * Solution: Replace the headlight bulbs.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $100+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Tail light failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Dim or inoperable tail lights.
    * Solution: Replace the tail light bulbs.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $20 - $50+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Power window failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Inoperable power windows.
    * Solution: Replace the power window motor or switch.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $300+ per window
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Heater core failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Leaking heater core causes coolant leaks and a musty smell in the cabin.
    * Solution: Replace the heater core.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $600+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 1999 Ford Mystique has a number of potential issues, particularly with its engine and transmission. These issues can range in severity from minor inconveniences to major repairs. The cost of repairs can also be significant, especially for engine or transmission problems.

Overall, the 1999 Ford Mystique is a vehicle with a mixed reputation. It offers a comfortable interior, spacious trunk, and affordable price, but it is let down by its lack of power, poor handling, and low-quality materials. Additionally, the potential for costly repairs can be a major concern for owners.
