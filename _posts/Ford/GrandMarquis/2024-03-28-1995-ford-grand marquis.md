**Introduction:**

The 1995 Ford Grand Marquis is a full-size sedan known for its spacious interior, comfortable ride, and powerful engine. It is available with a choice of two engines: a 4.6-liter V8 and a 5.0-liter V8. The 4.6-liter V8 produces 215 horsepower and 290 lb-ft of torque, while the 5.0-liter V8 makes 230 horsepower and 320 lb-ft of torque. Both engines are paired with a 4-speed automatic transmission.

Despite its positive attributes, the 1995 Ford Grand Marquis has had its fair share of issues over the years. These issues range from minor annoyances to more serious problems that can affect the vehicle's performance, safety, and overall reliability.

**Engine:**

* **4.6-liter V8**
    * **Intake manifold gasket failure:** The intake manifold gasket can fail, causing a vacuum leak and engine performance issues. Symptoms include rough idling, loss of power, and poor fuel economy. The solution is to replace the intake manifold gasket. Estimated cost to fix: $500 - $800+
    * **Spark plug wire failure:** The spark plug wires can become damaged or worn, causing a misfire. Symptoms include rough idling, hesitation during acceleration, and poor fuel economy. The solution is to replace the spark plug wires. Estimated cost to fix: $200 - $300+
    * **Water pump failure:** The water pump can fail, causing the engine to overheat. Symptoms include high engine temperature, coolant leaks, and a squealing noise from the engine. The solution is to replace the water pump. Estimated cost to fix: $400 - $600+
* **5.0-liter V8**
    * **No major issues found**

**Transmission:**

* **4-speed automatic transmission:**
    * **Transmission slipping:** The transmission may slip or hesitate during shifting. Symptoms include difficulty shifting gears, slipping between gears, and loss of power. The solution is to rebuild or replace the transmission. Estimated cost to fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
    * **Transmission fluid leaks:** The transmission may develop leaks, causing fluid loss and performance issues. Symptoms include low transmission fluid levels, slipping gears, and difficulty shifting. The solution is to fix the leaks and replace the transmission fluid. Estimated cost to fix: $200 - $500+
    * **Failed torque converter:** The torque converter can fail, causing the transmission to slip or shudder. Symptoms include rough shifting, loss of power, and decreased fuel economy. The solution is to replace the torque converter. Estimated cost to fix: $500 - $800+

**Suspension:**

* **Front suspension:**
    * **Strut failure:** The front struts can fail, causing the vehicle to ride rough, handle poorly, and have excessive tire wear. Symptoms include a rough ride, excessive body roll, and uneven tire wear. The solution is to replace the struts. Estimated cost to fix: $500 - $800+
    * **Ball joint failure:** The ball joints can fail, causing the vehicle to pull to one side while driving. Symptoms include a wandering steering wheel, uneven tire wear, and a clunking noise from the front suspension. The solution is to replace the ball joints. Estimated cost to fix: $200 - $400+
* **Rear suspension:**
    * **Leaf spring failure:** The rear leaf springs can fail, causing the vehicle to sag in the rear. Symptoms include a low ride height, excessive body roll, and a rough ride. The solution is to replace the leaf springs. Estimated cost to fix: $300 - $500+

**Electrical:**

* **Battery failure:** The battery can fail, causing the vehicle to have difficulty starting or not starting at all. Symptoms include dim headlights, slow engine cranking, and a dead battery. The solution is to replace the battery. Estimated cost to fix: $100 - $200+
* **Alternator failure:** The alternator can fail, causing the vehicle to lose electrical power. Symptoms include dim headlights, flickering dashboard lights, and a dead battery. The solution is to replace the alternator. Estimated cost to fix: $300 - $500+
* **Starter failure:** The starter can fail, causing the vehicle to have difficulty starting or not starting at all. Symptoms include a clicking noise when trying to start the engine, slow engine cranking, and a dead battery. The solution is to replace the starter. Estimated cost to fix: $200 - $400+

**Exterior:**

* **Door lock failure:** The door locks can fail, causing the doors to lock or unlock unintentionally. Symptoms include doors that lock or unlock on their own, difficulty locking or unlocking the doors, and a malfunctioning key fob. The solution is to repair or replace the door lock actuators. Estimated cost to fix: $200 - $400+
* **Window regulator failure:** The window regulators can fail, causing the windows to become inoperable. Symptoms include windows that are slow to move, windows that get stuck in the up or down position, and windows that fall out of the door. The solution is to repair or replace the window regulators. Estimated cost to fix: $300 - $500+

**Interior:**

* **Dashboard cracking:** The dashboard can crack over time, especially in hot climates. Symptoms include visible cracks on the dashboard, peeling or discolored dashboard material, and a rattling noise from the dashboard. The solution is to repair or replace the dashboard. Estimated cost to fix: $400 - $800+
* **Climate control failure:** The climate control system can fail, causing the air conditioner or heater to not work properly. Symptoms include weak or no air flow from the vents, incorrect temperature settings, and a malfunctioning display. The solution is to repair or replace the climate control system. Estimated cost to fix: $300 - $600+

**Summary:**

The 1995 Ford Grand Marquis is a well-liked vehicle that offers a comfortable ride, spacious interior, and powerful engine. However, it is not without its problems. The vehicle has had issues with the engine, transmission, suspension, electrical system, and interior. The severity of these issues can range from minor annoyances to more serious problems that can affect the vehicle's performance, safety, and overall reliability. The cost to fix these issues can vary depending on the severity of the problem and the location of the repair.
