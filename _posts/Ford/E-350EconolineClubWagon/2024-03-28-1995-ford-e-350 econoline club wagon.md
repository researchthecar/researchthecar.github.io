**Introduction**

The 1995 Ford E-350 Econoline Club Wagon is a popular full-size van known for its spacious interior, comfortable ride, and reliable performance. It is available with a range of engine and transmission options, including a 5.8L V8 engine paired with a 4-speed automatic transmission. People generally praise the E-350 Econoline for its versatility, affordability, and durability. However, like any vehicle, it has some known issues that potential owners should be aware of.

**Engine**

**5.8L V8**

* **Intake Manifold Gasket Failure:**
  - Description/Symptoms: Coolant or oil leaking from the intake manifold, rough idle, engine overheating
  - Solution: Replace intake manifold gasket
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
  - Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**4-Speed Automatic**

* **Transmission Slipping or Jerking:**
  - Description/Symptoms: Transmission slipping or failing to engage gears, delayed or rough shifting
  - Solution: Rebuild or replace transmission
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
  - Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Front Suspension Wear:**
  - Description/Symptoms: Excessive noise or vibration from the front suspension, uneven tire wear
  - Solution: Replace worn suspension components (ball joints, tie rods, bushings)
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+
  - Recalls: None Found

* **Rear Axle Seal Leak:**
  - Description/Symptoms: Leaking oil from the rear axle seal
  - Solution: Replace rear axle seal
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+
  - Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Electrical Shorts:**
  - Description/Symptoms: Intermittent electrical issues, blown fuses, flickering lights
  - Solution: Diagnose and repair electrical shorts
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $1,000+
  - Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Rust on Body Panels:**
  - Description/Symptoms: Rust forming on the body panels, especially around the wheel wells and lower doors
  - Solution: Rust repair or replacement of affected panels
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+
  - Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Interior Trim Panel Degradation:**
  - Description/Symptoms: Interior trim panels cracking or fading over time
  - Solution: Replace or repair affected panels
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
  - Recalls: None Found

* **Heater Core Failure:**
  - Description/Symptoms: No heat from the heater, coolant leaking into the cabin
  - Solution: Replace heater core
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
  - Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

Overall, the 1995 Ford E-350 Econoline Club Wagon is a reliable and versatile vehicle with a few known issues. While some of these issues, such as transmission problems, can be costly to fix, others, like suspension wear, are relatively minor and inexpensive to address. Potential owners should be aware of these issues and factor them into their purchasing decision.
