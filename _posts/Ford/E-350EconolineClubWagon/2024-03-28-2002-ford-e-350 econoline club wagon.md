**Introduction:**

The 2002 Ford E-350 Econoline Club Wagon is a popular choice for families and commercial users due to its spacious interior, powerful engine, and reliable performance. It is available with a variety of engine and transmission options, including a 5.4L V8, 6.8L V10, and 7.3L V8 diesel engine, as well as a 4-speed or 5-speed automatic transmission. The E-350 is known for its rugged construction, but like any vehicle, it has its fair share of issues.

**Engine**

**5.4L V8**

* **Intake manifold failure:** A common issue with the 5.4L V8 engine is the failure of the intake manifold. This can cause a variety of symptoms, including a loss of power, poor fuel economy, and a rough idle.
    *Solution: Replace the intake manifold.
    *Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $1,500+
    *Recalls: N/A

* **Spark plug blowout:** Another common issue with the 5.4L V8 engine is spark plug blowout. This can cause a loss of power, a rough idle, and a misfire.
    *Solution: Replace the spark plugs and install new spark plug threads.
    *Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
    *Recalls: N/A

**6.8L V10**

* **Intake manifold failure:** Similar to the 5.4L V8 engine, the 6.8L V10 is also susceptible to intake manifold failure.
    *Solution: Replace the intake manifold.
    *Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,000+
    *Recalls: N/A

* **EGR valve failure:** The EGR valve can fail on the 6.8L V10, which can lead to a loss of power and reduced fuel economy.
    *Solution: Replace the EGR valve.
    *Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500
    *Recalls: N/A

**7.3L V8 Diesel**

* **Turbocharger failure:** Turbocharger failure is a common issue on the 7.3L V8 diesel engine. This can cause a loss of power, a whistling noise, and increased smoke emissions.
    *Solution: Replace the turbocharger.
    *Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
    *Recalls: N/A

* **Injector failure:** Injector failure can also occur on the 7.3L V8 diesel engine, which can cause a loss of power, a rough idle, and increased fuel consumption.
    *Solution: Replace the faulty injectors.
    *Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500
    *Recalls: N/A

**Transmission**

**4-speed Automatic**

* **Transmission slipping:** The 4-speed automatic transmission can develop issues with slipping, which can cause the vehicle to lose power at higher speeds.
    *Solution: Rebuild the transmission or replace it.
    *Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
    *Recalls: N/A

**5-speed Automatic**

* **Torque converter failure:** The torque converter can fail on the 5-speed automatic transmission, which can cause a shuddering or slipping sensation when shifting gears.
    *Solution: Replace the torque converter.
    *Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,500
    *Recalls: N/A

**Suspension**

* **Ball joint failure:** The ball joints in the front suspension can wear out over time, which can cause the vehicle to pull to one side while driving.
    *Solution: Replace the ball joints.
    *Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
    *Recalls: N/A

* **Strut failure:** The struts in the front suspension can also fail, which can cause the vehicle to ride roughly and handle poorly.
    *Solution: Replace the struts.
    *Estimated Cost to Fix: $600 - $1,200
    *Recalls: N/A

**Electrical**

* **Alternator failure:** The alternator can fail on the E-350, which can cause the battery to drain and the vehicle to lose power.
    *Solution: Replace the alternator.
    *Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800
    *Recalls: N/A

* **Battery drain:** The E-350 can also experience battery drain, which can be caused by a variety of factors, such as a faulty alternator, a bad battery, or a parasitic draw.
    *Solution: Diagnose and fix the underlying cause of the battery drain.
    *Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the cause
    *Recalls: N/A

**Exterior**

* **Rust:** The E-350 is prone to rust, especially in areas with high humidity or salt exposure.
    *Solution: Regularly wash and wax the vehicle to protect it from rust.
    *Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the severity of the rust
    *Recalls: N/A

* **Door handle failure:** The door handles on the E-350 can fail, making it difficult to open the doors.
    *Solution: Replace the door handles.
    *Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
    *Recalls: N/A

**Interior**

* **Dashboard cracking:** The dashboard on the E-350 can crack over time, especially in hot climates.
    *Solution: Replace the dashboard or install a dash cover.
    *Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500
    *Recalls: N/A

* **Seat foam degradation:** The seat foam in the E-350 can degrade over time, causing the seats to become uncomfortable.
    *Solution: Replace the seat foam.
    *Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600
    *Recalls: N/A

**Summary:**

The 2002 Ford E-350 Econoline Club Wagon is a reliable and versatile vehicle, but it is not without its issues. Some of the most common problems include engine intake manifold failure, spark plug blowout, turbocharger failure, transmission slipping, and rust. While some of these issues are relatively minor and inexpensive to fix, others can be quite costly to repair. It is important to factor in the potential repair costs when considering purchasing an E-350.
