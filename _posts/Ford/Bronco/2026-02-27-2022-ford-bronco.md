# 2022 Ford Bronco Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2022 Ford Bronco offers a rugged, highly capable off-road design and nostalgic styling that has captured major buyer interest. However, prospective buyers should review owner-reported reliability data carefully, as the vehicle has accumulated notable complaints and recalls concerning fuel system performance, steering system binding, hardtop structural defects, and electrical glitches.

---

## Engine

### 2.3L EcoBoost I4

The base 2.3-liter turbocharged inline-four engine produces approximately 300 horsepower and 325 lb-ft of torque, providing respectable daily drivability and trail capability while serving as the standard powerplant.

### 2.7L EcoBoost V6

The available 2.7-liter twin-turbocharged V6 ups performance to roughly 330 horsepower and 415 lb-ft of torque, offering punchy acceleration for highway passing and demanding off-road maneuvers.

### Fuel System

Complaints center on fuel pump operation and engine hesitation, occasionally resulting in unexpected stalling or drivability concerns.

**Fuel Pump Failure & Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers report unexpected engine stalling, hesitation under acceleration, and sudden loss of motive power while driving, frequently requiring towing.
- **Solution:** Diagnostic inspection by a qualified technician; affected components or fuel pumps may require replacement under manufacturer guidelines or recall campaigns.

## Transmission

### 7-Speed Manual Transmission

The unique 7-speed manual transmission features a dedicated crawler gear and pairs exclusively with the 2.3L engine, offering an engaging driving experience for traditionalists.

### 10-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 10-speed automatic transmission delivers smooth, quick gear shifts across varied driving conditions and is available on both engine configurations.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes & Parking Brakes

Brake complaints highlight concerns with parking brake holding capability and anti-lock brake module anomalies during specialized towing setups.

**Brake System & Parking Brake Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicles may experience unexpected electronic brake or ABS module activation during flat-towing, or unintended release of the parking brake causing the vehicle to roll.
- **Solution:** Check for applicable service updates and recall bulletins addressing ABS module software or mechanical parking brake adjustments.

## Steering

### Steering Rack and Column

Steering system reliability warrants close inspection, as owners have reported intermittent steering lockups and heavy steering resistance linked to steering gear faults.

**Steering Assist Failure and Binding**
- **Symptoms:** Steering effort becomes extremely stiff, or the steering wheel intermittently locks up while driving without prior warning, creating serious handling hazards.
- **Solution:** Have the steering gear and electronic assist components inspected; steering rack replacement is often required to resolve severe binding or internal failures.

## Exterior

### Hardtop Body Structure

Body hardware and hardtop construction have generated notable owner grievances regarding premature wear, cracking, and window seal integrity.

**Hardtop Delamination and Cracking**
- **Symptoms:** Owners notice visible cracking, blistering, or structural delamination on the exterior skin of the Molded-In-Color (MIC) hardtop roof panels.
- **Solution:** Inspect the roof panels for structural flaws; dealership evaluation may qualify the vehicle for roof panel replacement under warranty or special programs.

## Safety

### Advanced Driver Assistance & Airbags

Safety electronics can experience intermittent fault triggers, disabling advanced driver assistance features and prompting restraint system warning lamps.

**Safety System Warning Lights & Sensor Failures**
- **Symptoms:** Instrument clusters display sudden warning lights indicating pre-collision braking, adaptive cruise control, hill start assist, and airbag restraint systems are disabled.
- **Solution:** Diagnostic scanning at a dealership is necessary to isolate faulty wheel speed sensors, radar modules, or restraint system wiring harness connections.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V411000: Latches/Locks/Linkages:Doors:Lock](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Bronco&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** A malfunctioning child safety lock increases the risk of injury to a child occupant.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the passenger-side child safety locks door child safety lock and latch, and replace them, if necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V451000: Visibility:Windshield](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Bronco&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** The windshield may not have been properly bonded to the vehicle, which could allow it to detach during a crash. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 212, "Windshield Mounting." A windshield that separates from a vehicle during a crash can increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will remove and reinstall the windshield, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V825000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Bronco&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 111, "Rear Visibility." A rearview image that continues to be displayed after the vehicle is shifted out of reverse may distract the driver, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 22V928000: Air Bags:Sensor:Side Impact](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Bronco&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** The right or left side impact sensors may not be properly secured to the vehicle. An improperly secured side impact sensor may cause the air bags to unintentionally deploy, or not to deploy as intended, increasing the risk of injury in a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the side impact sensor attachments and repair, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V070000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Bronco&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** The transmission may contain a loose bolt which could prevent the transmission from engaging the park gear, although the gear shifter position may indicate that the vehicle has been shifted to "PARK." The inability to secure the vehicle in the park position can result in a rollaway, increasing the risk of a crash or injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and, if necessary, replace the transmissions, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V155000: Steering](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Bronco&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** A steering wheel that locks-up can result in a complete loss of steering control, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the steering gear, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V165000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Bronco&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 111, "Rear Visibility." A rearview image that continues to be displayed after the vehicle is shifted out of reverse may distract the driver, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 23V358000: Seat Belts:Critical Fasteners](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Bronco&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 208, "Occupant Crash Protection." Difficulty accessing the seat belt latches may result in occupants not wearing their seat belts, increasing the risk of injury in a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will add a sliding clip latch stop to the driver and front passenger seat belts, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2022 Ford Bronco is an exciting and highly capable adventure vehicle, but used-car buyers should perform a rigorous pre-purchase inspection. Pay close attention to the steering gear operation, service history regarding open recalls, hardtop roof condition, and electrical system functionality to ensure any persistent owner-reported issues have been properly addressed.
