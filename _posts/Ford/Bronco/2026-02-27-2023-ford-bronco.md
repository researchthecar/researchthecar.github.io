# 2023 Ford Bronco Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2023 Ford Bronco is a highly capable off-road SUV that attracts buyers with its rugged styling, removable top, and modern technology. However, owners have reported various issues ranging from powertrain and brake concerns to electrical and exterior quirks. While many drivers enjoy its performance on and off the pavement, potential buyers should be aware of these recurring complaints when inspecting a used model.

---

## Engine

### 2.3L EcoBoost Turbocharged I4

The 2.3-liter turbocharged inline-four produces roughly 300 horsepower and serves as the base engine for the Bronco, offering a balanced mix of efficiency and everyday drivability.

**Engine Misfire**
- **Symptoms:** The engine may begin to run rough, trigger diagnostic trouble codes, and experience cylinder misfires.
- **Solution:** Diagnose the misfire source, inspect ignition coils and spark plugs, and replace faulty components as necessary.

### 2.7L EcoBoost Twin-Turbocharged V6

The 2.7-liter twin-turbo V6 delivers around 330 horsepower for stronger acceleration and robust towing capability, though it requires careful monitoring for electrical and fueling glitches.

**Engine Stalling and Performance Hesitation**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have noted unexpected engine stalling, electrical warnings, and drivability concerns.
- **Solution:** Check for stored diagnostic trouble codes, update powertrain control modules, and inspect electrical connections.

## Transmission

### 7-Speed Manual Transmission

The 7-speed manual features a dedicated crawler gear for off-road maneuvering and pairs exclusively with the 2.3L engine.

**Manual Shifting Grind and Popping Out of Gear**
- **Symptoms:** The transmission may grind when shifting into certain gears, such as third gear, or occasionally pop out of gear while driving.
- **Solution:** Inspect internal synchronizers, linkage, and clutch assembly; repair or replace worn transmission components.

### 10-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 10-speed automatic provides smooth power delivery across a wide range of driving conditions and comes paired with either engine option.

**Clutch and Gear Engagement Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have reported issues with gear engagement, unexpected neutral shifts, and unusual grinding noises during operation.
- **Solution:** Perform diagnostic testing on the transmission control module, check fluid levels, and service internal mechanical components if needed.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

The hydraulic braking system provides stopping power suited for heavy off-road use, though some owners have experienced inconsistent pedal feel on inclines.

**Brake Pedal Softness and Forward Lurching**
- **Symptoms:** When stopping on an incline, the brakes may initially engage fully before releasing momentarily, causing the vehicle to lurch forward before stopping abruptly.
- **Solution:** Inspect the brake booster, master cylinder, and anti-lock brake system modules for proper operation.

## Electrical

### Electrical System

The electrical architecture governs modern driver assists, infotainment features, and sensor networks, which can occasionally experience communication glitches.

**Camera and Sensor Communication Faults**
- **Symptoms:** Front or rear cameras and driver-assist sensors may intermittently stop working or display black backup camera screens.
- **Solution:** Inspect camera modules and wiring harnesses for damage or corrosion; replace faulty modules as needed.

## Exterior

### Visibility and Windshield Wipers

Exterior visibility components include automatic wipers and lighting systems designed to maintain clear sightlines in adverse weather.

**Erratic Wiper Speeds**
- **Symptoms:** Windshield wipers may change speeds without driver input, slowing down to an intermittent pace during steady or heavy rain.
- **Solution:** Inspect the wiper motor assembly and multi-function switch for internal faults.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V070000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Bronco&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** The transmission may contain a loose bolt which could prevent the transmission from engaging the park gear, although the gear shifter position may indicate that the vehicle has been shifted to "PARK." The inability to secure the vehicle in the park position can result in a rollaway, increasing the risk of a crash or injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and, if necessary, replace the transmissions, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V155000: Steering](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Bronco&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** A steering wheel that locks-up can result in a complete loss of steering control, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the steering gear, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V283000: Wheels:Lugs/Nuts/Bolts/Studs](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Bronco&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** Loose lug nuts may result in wheel detachment while driving, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect, and if necessary, retighten the lug nuts, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V358000: Seat Belts:Critical Fasteners](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Bronco&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 208, "Occupant Crash Protection." Difficulty accessing the seat belt latches may result in occupants not wearing their seat belts, increasing the risk of injury in a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will add a sliding clip latch stop to the driver and front passenger seat belts, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V379000: Equipment:Other:Labels](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Bronco&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 110, "Tire Selection and Rims." An overloaded vehicle increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 23V546000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission:Internal:Park Pawl](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Bronco&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** A vehicle rollaway can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the transmission, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V142000: Visibility:Glass, Side/Rear](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Bronco&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** Detached rear glass panels may create a road hazard and increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and reinstall the rear glass panels on Bronco vehicles. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V189000: Latches/Locks/Linkages:Doors:Lock](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Bronco&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** A malfunctioning child safety lock that allows a rear door to be opened unintentionally can increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the child safety locks and replace the rear door latch, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2023 Ford Bronco is an exciting and highly capable adventure vehicle, but prospective buyers should perform a thorough inspection. Pay close attention to transmission shift quality, brake pedal feel on inclines, and the proper operation of electrical features and cameras. Reviewing service history and checking for completed safety recalls will help ensure a more reliable ownership experience.
