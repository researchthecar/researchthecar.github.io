# 2024 Ford Bronco Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2024 Ford Bronco is a highly capable, rugged off-road SUV that appeals to adventure seekers and everyday drivers alike. While its bold styling and impressive capability draw many buyers, owner complaints indicate certain recurring pain points to keep in mind during your search. Reviewing key mechanical and body components can help prospective buyers spot common trouble areas before making a purchase.

---

## Engine

### 2.3L EcoBoost Turbocharged I4

The 2.3L EcoBoost turbocharged inline-four engine produces approximately 300 horsepower and 325 lb-ft of torque. It provides a solid balance of everyday efficiency and dependable power for both street and trail driving.

### 2.7L EcoBoost Twin-Turbocharged V6

The 2.7L EcoBoost twin-turbo V6 bumps output to roughly 330 horsepower and 415 lb-ft of torque. This larger engine option offers robust acceleration and confident towing capability for heavier workloads.

### 3.0L EcoBoost Twin-Turbocharged V6

Exclusive to high-performance Raptor trims, the 3.0L EcoBoost V6 delivers over 400 horsepower for aggressive off-road performance. It is built to handle extreme terrain with substantial power reserves.

## Transmission

### 7-Speed Manual Transmission

The 7-speed manual transmission features a dedicated crawler gear for technical off-road maneuvers. It pairs exclusively with the 2.3L engine for an engaging, hands-on driving experience.

### 10-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 10-speed automatic transmission delivers smooth gear shifts across various driving conditions and is available across nearly all engine configurations. It optimizes both highway fuel economy and low-end trail control.

## Suspension

Suspension concerns primarily involve damper and shock reservoir mounting integrity.

**Shock Absorber Reservoir Detachment**
- **Symptoms:** Reports indicate that shock absorber reservoirs can experience detachment issues under certain operating conditions.
- **Solution:** Inspect the suspension dampers and shock reservoirs for secure mounting hardware and check against manufacturer campaign records.

## Brakes

### Brake

Brake system complaints center around occasional pedal feel inconsistencies and unusual mechanical noises.

**Soft Pedal and Unexpected Resistance**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have noted soft brake pedals or situations where increased foot pressure was required to bring the vehicle to a halt.
- **Solution:** Check brake fluid levels, inspect lines for leaks, and have the anti-lock brake system and master cylinder evaluated if pedal feel is spongy.

## Exterior

### Structure

Issues related to the removable hardtop structure include cracking and panel defects reported by some owners.

**Hardtop Cracking and Delamination**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have reported cracks developing across the molded-in-color (MIC) removable hardtop panels, sometimes allowing moisture intrusion.
- **Solution:** Inspect all removable roof sections closely for hairline fractures, warping, or water leaks, and consult a dealer regarding potential warranty replacements.

### Exterior Lighting

Electrical reports highlight intermittent front headlight issues and accessory glitches.

**Headlight Illumination Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Some drivers experienced front headlight failure or reduced forward illumination during inclement weather such as snow.
- **Solution:** Verify headlight bulb connections, inspect wiring harnesses, and check for software or control module glitches affecting lighting circuits.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V142000: Visibility:Glass, Side/Rear](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Bronco&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** Detached rear glass panels may create a road hazard and increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and reinstall the rear glass panels on Bronco vehicles. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V189000: Latches/Locks/Linkages:Doors:Lock](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Bronco&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** A malfunctioning child safety lock that allows a rear door to be opened unintentionally can increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the child safety locks and replace the rear door latch, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V770000: Suspension:Front:Control Arm:Upper Ball Joint](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Bronco&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** The front upper control arm ball joint nut may not have been installed or tightened properly, allowing the front upper control arm to detach from the knuckle assembly. A detached control arm can cause a loss of vehicle steering and control, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and verify that the ball joint fastener is tightened properly. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V025000: Suspension:Rear:Shock Absorber](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Bronco&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** An external reservoir that detaches from a vehicle can create a road hazard, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the rear shock absorbers, as necessary free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V164000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission:Fluid/Lubricant:Valves/Valve Body](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Bronco&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** Unexpected vehicle movement increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the transmission main control valve body, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V308000: Suspension:Front:Control Arm:Lower Arm](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Bronco&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** The front lower control arm fasteners may not have been tightened properly, allowing the control arm to detach from the frame. A detached control arm can cause a loss of vehicle steering and control, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and verify that the lower control arm fasteners are tightened properly. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V303000: Latches/Locks/Linkages:Doors:Lock](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Bronco&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** A malfunctioning child safety lock that allows a rear door to be opened unintentionally can increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the child safety locks and replace the rear door latch, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V310000: Suspension:Front:Control Arm:Upper Ball Joint](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Bronco&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** A detached control arm can cause a loss of vehicle steering and control, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and tighten, or replace the ball joint fastener as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2024 Ford Bronco offers unmatched off-road capability and distinct styling, making it a compelling choice for used car buyers. While owner reports point to specific items like hardtop panel durability and occasional brake or lighting concerns, thorough pre-purchase inspections can help ensure you find a reliable example. Be sure to verify that any applicable campaigns or dealer inspections have been completed before finalizing your purchase.
