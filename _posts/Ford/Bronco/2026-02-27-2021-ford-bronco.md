# 2021 Ford Bronco Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The Ford Bronco has captured massive enthusiasm as a rugged, modern off-roader, but early production models—particularly the debut year—carry some notable owner complaints and recall histories that shoppers should evaluate closely. While its design and capability shine on trails and highways alike, prospective buyers should inspect specific powertrain, fuel delivery, and suspension components to ensure prior issues have been properly addressed.

---

## Engine

### 2.3L EcoBoost Turbo I4

The base 2.3-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine produces approximately 300 horsepower and 325 lb-ft of torque. It offers a strong balance of everyday efficiency and capability, serving as a popular choice for daily driving and light trail use.

### 2.7L EcoBoost Turbo V6

The optional 2.7-liter twin-turbocharged V6 churns out roughly 330 horsepower and 415 lb-ft of torque, delivering robust acceleration and towing confidence. It is a favored upgrade for off-road enthusiasts looking for extra muscle.

**Loss of Motive Power and Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers experience unexpected engine stalling or sudden loss of motive power while driving, often accompanied by warning lights.
- **Solution:** Diagnostic evaluation by a dealer to inspect fuel delivery and engine management systems, and apply necessary factory updates or repairs.

### Fuel Pump

Delivers pressurized fuel directly to the direct-injection system. Owner reports indicate occasional premature pump failures.

**Fuel Pump Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle may stall unexpectedly or fail to start due to an in-tank fuel pump failure, frequently stranding drivers.
- **Solution:** Replace the failed in-tank fuel pump assembly with an updated unit.

## Transmission

### 10-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 10-speed automatic transmission is paired widely across the lineup, offering smooth highway cruising ratios and crawler gears. It generally performs well when properly maintained.

### 7-Speed Manual Transmission

Featuring an ultra-low crawler gear, the 7-speed manual transmission caters directly to purists tackling technical off-road obstacles. It provides engaging control but is restricted to specific engine configurations.

## Suspension

### Rear Shock Absorbers

Designed to handle punishing off-road terrain, especially on packages equipped with upgraded dampening systems. Suspension components require careful inspection for wear and secure mounting.

**Shock Reservoir Detachment**
- **Symptoms:** Rear shock reservoirs or components may loosen or detach while driving after initial recall service attempts.
- **Solution:** Inspect and correctly secure or replace affected suspension components according to updated manufacturer service guidelines.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

Hydraulic braking system designed to haul down the heavy off-road SUV safely under varying load conditions.

**Brake Booster Vacuum Line Fault**
- **Symptoms:** Brake warning lamps illuminate and brake pedal feel becomes unusually squishy, requiring excessive pedal travel to stop.
- **Solution:** Inspect and replace faulty vacuum tubes or brake booster components.

## Electrical

### Wiring Harness and Sensors

Manages complex driver assistance systems, lighting, and convenience features throughout the vehicle cabin and exterior.

**Wiring Harness Chafing and Sensor Faults**
- **Symptoms:** Rear wiring harnesses rub against body panels or bumpers, causing warning lights for blind spot monitoring, cross-traffic alerts, or parking assist systems.
- **Solution:** Inspect the wiring harness for abrasion, repair damaged wires, and secure the harness away from pinch points.

## Exterior

### Windshield and Washers

Exposed exterior elements including glass, wiper mechanisms, and body structures prone to environmental wear.

**Washer Fluid Reservoir Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Windshield washer fluid reservoirs crack or fail to hold fluid, preventing proper windshield cleaning while driving.
- **Solution:** Replace the leaking washer fluid reservoir tank.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V982000: Forward Collision Avoidance: Sensing System: Radar](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Bronco&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** Any of these scenarios can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will adjust the angle of the cruise control radar module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V770000: Air Bags:Frontal](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Bronco&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** During passenger air bag deployment, an air bag vent could open prematurely and result in an improper deployment. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 208, "Occupant Crash Protection." Improper air bag deployment increases the risk of injury during a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the passenger air bag, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V411000: Latches/Locks/Linkages:Doors:Lock](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Bronco&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** A malfunctioning child safety lock increases the risk of injury to a child occupant.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the passenger-side child safety locks door child safety lock and latch, and replace them, if necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V825000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Bronco&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 111, "Rear Visibility." A rearview image that continues to be displayed after the vehicle is shifted out of reverse may distract the driver, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 22V928000: Air Bags:Sensor:Side Impact](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Bronco&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The right or left side impact sensors may not be properly secured to the vehicle. An improperly secured side impact sensor may cause the air bags to unintentionally deploy, or not to deploy as intended, increasing the risk of injury in a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the side impact sensor attachments and repair, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V165000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Bronco&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 111, "Rear Visibility." A rearview image that continues to be displayed after the vehicle is shifted out of reverse may distract the driver, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 23V358000: Seat Belts:Critical Fasteners](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Bronco&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 208, "Occupant Crash Protection." Difficulty accessing the seat belt latches may result in occupants not wearing their seat belts, increasing the risk of injury in a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will add a sliding clip latch stop to the driver and front passenger seat belts, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V598000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Bronco&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The rearview camera, or 360-degree view camera if equipped, may not display a rearview image when the vehicle is placed in reverse. Loss of the rearview camera image can reduce the driver's rear visibility, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the rearview camera, and update the software and wiring as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The Ford Bronco is an exciting and highly capable choice for adventure seekers, but buyers looking at a pre-owned model should exercise caution. Prioritize inspecting the vehicle's service history for completed safety recalls, fuel pump performance, and any suspension or electrical harness updates. Ensuring these potential trouble spots have been resolved will greatly improve overall ownership reliability.
