# 2016 Ford Taurus Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2016 Ford Taurus is a large, comfortable sedan offering substantial highway presence and a roomy cabin, but prospective buyers should review key reliability and safety areas. While powertrain options generally hold up well with routine maintenance, owner complaints highlight recurring concerns regarding electrical accessories, rear suspension components, and rearview camera performance. Keeping a close eye on known trouble spots during a pre-purchase inspection will help ensure a dependable ownership experience.

---

## Engine

### 3.5L V6 Engine

The standard naturally aspirated 3.5-liter V6 produces 288 horsepower and provides smooth, adequate acceleration for daily driving. It is generally robust, though buyers should watch for traditional cooling system concerns like water pump wear.

### 3.5L EcoBoost V6 Engine

Exclusive to the high-performance SHO trim, this twin-turbocharged V6 churns out 365 horsepower and standard all-wheel drive. It delivers strong acceleration but requires diligent maintenance due to its complex forced-induction setup.

### 2.0L EcoBoost Turbocharged 4-Cylinder Engine

This turbocharged 4-cylinder engine makes 240 horsepower, focusing on improved fuel economy over the standard V6. It offers peppy mid-range torque but is less commonly found in the Taurus lineup.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed SelectShift Automatic Transmission

Paired across most trims with front-wheel or all-wheel drive, this 6-speed automatic transmission delivers smooth shifts under normal conditions. Routine fluid changes are recommended to promote long-term durability.

## Suspension

### Rear Suspension and Toe Links

The rear suspension setup provides a comfortable ride quality, but rear toe link bushings can degrade over time and require replacement.

**Rear Toe Link Bushing Separation**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may hear an unusually loud noise from the rear over bumps, or experience sudden difficulty maneuvering if a rear toe link separates from the wheel assembly.
- **Solution:** Inspect rear suspension bushings and replace worn or separated lateral/toe links.

## Steering

### Power Steering Assist

The electronic power steering system is generally responsive, but rare failures can cause a sudden heavy steering feel.

**Steering Assist Loss or Binding**
- **Symptoms:** Unexpected loss of power steering assist or steering stiffness, making the wheel difficult to turn.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified mechanic inspect the electronic power steering module, battery connections, or rack and pinion assembly.

## Electrical

### Infotainment and Body Control Modules

Electrical accessories and infotainment modules occasionally experience software glitches that require resets or component updates.

**Screen Freezing and Accessory Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** The touchscreen display or APIM system may freeze, fail to respond, or cause connected hands-free phone features to stop working.
- **Solution:** Perform a system reset, update software, or replace the faulty control module if unresponsive.

### Backup Camera and Display

Rearview camera operation can become intermittent, affecting backward visibility during low-speed maneuvers.

**Intermittent or Blank Rearview Camera**
- **Symptoms:** The rearview camera display turns blank, cuts in and out, or occasionally inverts the image while shifting into reverse.
- **Solution:** Inspect wiring harnesses, camera connections, and address applicable manufacturer campaigns when replacement parts become available.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V925000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Engine:Gasoline:Turbo/Supercharger](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Taurus&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** An oil leak, in the presence of an ignition source, increases the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will inspect, and if necessary, replace the turbocharger oil supply tubes, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V072000: Suspension:Rear](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Taurus&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** A fractured rear toe link will cause a sudden change in vehicle handling and increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will replace the rear suspension toe links, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V611000: Structure:Exterior Trim](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Taurus&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** Door trim that detaches while driving can create a road hazard, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will repair or replace the B-pillar trim as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V695000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Taurus&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** A rearview camera that fails to properly display an image can reduce the driver’s view behind the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the rearview camera as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2016 Ford Taurus presents as a solid, spacious cruiser, but prospective buyers should perform a thorough check of its electronics, rearview camera functionality, and rear suspension components. While major mechanical failures are infrequent outside of standard maintenance items, verifying that safety recalls and electrical systems are fully operational will help ensure a worry-free purchase.
