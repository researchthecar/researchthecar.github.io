# 2017 Ford Taurus Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2017 Ford Taurus, buyers will find a spacious full-size sedan with comfortable road manners and decent power options. However, owner reports and technical history highlight a few distinct areas worth checking before purchase, particularly concerning electronics, rearview cameras, and engine cooling components.

---

## Engine

### 2.7L EcoBoost V6

The 2.7L twin-turbocharged EcoBoost V6 provides strong acceleration with around 365 horsepower, exclusive to the sporty SHO trim. It offers spirited performance for everyday driving, though buyers should verify maintenance records on turbocharged models.

### 3.5L Duratec V6

The naturally aspirated 3.5L V6 produces roughly 288 horsepower and serves as the standard engine for most Taurus trims. While generally dependable, buyers must monitor for internal water pump wear, as a failure can cause major engine complications.

**Water Pump Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Coolant leaks pooling under the vehicle or sudden engine overheating without immediate warning, which can lead to internal engine damage if unaddressed.
- **Solution:** Replace the internal water pump and inspect the engine for secondary thermal damage.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 6-speed automatic transmission is paired with front-wheel drive or all-wheel drive depending on the trim level. It delivers smooth shifts for highway cruising, though routine fluid changes are recommended to ensure long-term durability.

## Electrical

### Infotainment and Backup Camera

Cabin electronics generally function well, but infotainment and camera glitches are among the more frequently reported owner grievances.

**Inconsistent or Inverted Backup Camera Display**
- **Symptoms:** The rearview camera displays an upside-down or fuzzy image, shows a blue/black screen, or states that the camera is unavailable.
- **Solution:** Diagnose the camera module and wiring harness, or replace faulty components as necessary.

**APIM Module Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Unresponsive infotainment touchscreen, system freezing, or complete loss of SYNC system functions.
- **Solution:** Replace or repair the Accessory Protocol Interface Module (APIM).
- **Cost:** $900

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V530000: Steering](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Taurus&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** If the heat shield detaches, the steering gear may overheat, increasing the steering effort required by the driver, especially at lower speeds, and increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 18V141000: Electrical System:Ignition](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Taurus&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 114, "Theft Protection." If the key is removed from the ignition while the vehicle is not in the PARK position and the parking brake is not applied, the vehicle may roll away, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will replace the shifter assembly, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V072000: Suspension:Rear](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Taurus&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** A fractured rear toe link will cause a sudden change in vehicle handling and increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will replace the rear suspension toe links, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V611000: Structure:Exterior Trim](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Taurus&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** Door trim that detaches while driving can create a road hazard, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will repair or replace the B-pillar trim as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2017 Ford Taurus is a roomy cruiser with solid highway performance, but prospective buyers should thoroughly test electronics—specifically the backup camera and infotainment screen—and inspect the cooling system for any signs of water pump wear. Reviewing service records and confirming that all applicable safety recalls have been completed will help ensure a more dependable ownership experience.
