# 2018 Ford Taurus Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used full-size sedan, the 2018 Ford Taurus offers a spacious cabin and a comfortable highway ride. However, prospective buyers should review potential trouble spots reported by owners, particularly concerning electronics, powertrain components, and rear suspension hardware. While many owners enjoy its traditional sedan feel and substantial trunk space, paying close attention to maintenance records and prior repairs can help avoid unexpected headaches down the road.

---

## Engine

### 3.5L V6 Duratec

The standard naturally aspirated 3.5-liter V6 engine produces approximately 288 horsepower and 254 lb-ft of torque, providing adequate power for everyday driving. It generally offers straightforward mechanical reliability, though owners have occasionally reported coolant leaks requiring prompt attention.

### 2.0L EcoBoost Turbocharged I4

The optional 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine delivers around 240 horsepower and 270 lb-ft of torque, prioritizing improved fuel efficiency. Buyers evaluating this powerplant should verify regular oil change intervals to ensure long-term turbocharger health.

### 3.5L EcoBoost Twin-Turbo V6

Reserved for high-performance SHO models, this twin-turbocharged V6 churns out 365 horsepower and 350 lb-ft of torque paired with standard all-wheel drive. It offers brisk acceleration, but buyers should inspect the vehicle thoroughly for signs of aggressive driving or deferred maintenance.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed SelectShift Automatic

The six-speed automatic transmission routes power to either the front wheels or all four wheels depending on configuration. While generally durable, occasional shifting irregularities and electronic communication glitches have been reported by owners.

**Transmission Hesitation and Warning Messages**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may experience unexpected jerking, hesitation while reversing, or sudden warning displays such as "Transmission not in Park" accompanied by stalling.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified technician inspect the transmission control module, software calibrations, and fluid condition for signs of wear.

## Suspension

### Rear Suspension and Toe Links

The rear suspension setup provides stable handling for a large sedan, but worn or failing rear control links can compromise vehicle stability and require component replacement.

**Rear End Shifting and Control Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have reported that the rear end of the vehicle may feel loose, drift on uneven surfaces, or shift unexpectedly when applying the brakes firmly.
- **Solution:** Inspect rear suspension components, including toe links and lateral arms, for excessive play, wear, or structural failure, and replace damaged parts.

## Electrical

### Backup Camera and Display

Driver assistance features rely heavily on stable electrical integration, and rearview camera glitches are a recognized annoyance for several owners.

**Intermittent or Inverted Camera Display**
- **Symptoms:** The rearview camera image may occasionally flicker, display upside down, show a blank or blue screen, or fail to display altogether when shifting into reverse.
- **Solution:** Inspect the camera wiring harness, software, and display unit connectors; faulty cameras or modules may need to be replaced.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V141000: Electrical System:Ignition](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Taurus&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 114, "Theft Protection." If the key is removed from the ignition while the vehicle is not in the PARK position and the parking brake is not applied, the vehicle may roll away, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will replace the shifter assembly, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V072000: Suspension:Rear](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Taurus&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** A fractured rear toe link will cause a sudden change in vehicle handling and increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will replace the rear suspension toe links, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V611000: Structure:Exterior Trim](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Taurus&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** Door trim that detaches while driving can create a road hazard, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will repair or replace the B-pillar trim as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V695000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Taurus&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** A rearview camera that fails to properly display an image can reduce the driver’s view behind the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the rearview camera as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2018 Ford Taurus remains an appealing option for buyers seeking a large, comfortable cruiser with plenty of room. Prospective owners should carefully inspect the vehicle's rear suspension components and test all electronic features—particularly the backup camera and transmission responsiveness—during a pre-purchase test drive. Ensuring a thorough mechanical inspection will help secure a dependable example.
