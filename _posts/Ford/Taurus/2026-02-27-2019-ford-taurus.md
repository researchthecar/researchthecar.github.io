# 2019 Ford Taurus Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2019 Ford Taurus marks the final model year for this iconic nameplate, offering a spacious cabin and a comfortable highway ride. Used-car shoppers should weigh its traditional full-size sedan benefits against a handful of reported electrical, camera, and mechanical quirks gathered from owner complaints.

---

## Engine

### 2.5L I4 EcoBoost

A turbocharged 2.5-liter four-cylinder engine providing efficient daily driving performance. It is a rare option for late-model configurations, balancing modest power output with practical fuel economy.

### 3.5L V6 Duratec

A naturally aspirated 3.5-liter V6 engine producing approximately 288 horsepower. It serves as the standard powerplant for most models, delivering dependable everyday performance when properly maintained.

**Coolant Leaks from Water Pump**
- **Symptoms:** The cabin heater and air conditioning systems may fail to operate properly, often accompanied by coolant leaking from the water pump.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified mechanic inspect the cooling system and replace the failing water pump promptly to prevent potential engine overheating.

### 3.5L Twin-Turbocharged V6 EcoBoost

A high-output twin-turbo V6 engine generating around 365 horsepower, exclusively powering the performance-oriented SHO trim. It offers brisk acceleration and all-wheel drive confidence for enthusiast drivers.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed SelectShift Automatic

A six-speed automatic transmission paired with front-wheel or all-wheel drive configurations. It provides generally smooth gear shifts, though routine fluid checks are recommended for long-term health.

## Suspension

### Rear Suspension Toe Links

The rear suspension setup is generally compliant, though individual joint components like toe links require periodic inspection.

**Premature Rear Toe Link Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may notice loose handling or abnormal rear tire wear at relatively low mileage. This mirrors similar concerns found in earlier model years.
- **Solution:** Inspect rear suspension components during routine maintenance and replace worn toe links with updated parts if excessive play is detected.

## Electrical

### Backup Camera System

Safety electronics include standard driver aids and backup systems, though rearview camera display glitches remain a common owner complaint.

**Distorted or Blank Reverse Camera**
- **Symptoms:** When shifting into reverse, the backup camera display may show a blank screen, a blue screen, a distorted image, or freeze on the display when shifting back into drive.
- **Solution:** Have a dealership or technician inspect the camera wiring harness, software, or display module for faults.

## Exterior

### Windshield Wipers

Electrical accessories generally function well, though occasional switch or wiper module failures have been reported by owners.

**Wiper Speed Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Windshield wipers may activate only on the highest speed setting and refuse to slow down unless the entire system is switched off.
- **Solution:** Diagnose the wiper switch or multi-function control module and replace faulty electrical components as needed.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V611000: Structure:Exterior Trim](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Taurus&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** Door trim that detaches while driving can create a road hazard, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will repair or replace the B-pillar trim as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V695000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Taurus&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** A rearview camera that fails to properly display an image can reduce the driver’s view behind the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the rearview camera as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2019 Ford Taurus remains a comfortable and roomy cruiser for used-car buyers, provided you thoroughly check the backup camera, cooling system, and rear suspension during a pre-purchase inspection. While most mechanical systems prove sturdy, paying attention to these specific owner-reported areas will help ensure a reliable ownership experience.
