# 2022 Ford Mustang Mach-E Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2022 Ford Mustang Mach-E brings all-electric performance and distinctive styling to the crossover market, offering flexible range and responsive handling. While praised for its engaging drive and tech-forward cabin, prospective buyers should review owner-reported concerns regarding high-voltage electrical components, software glitches, and occasional mechanical anomalies.

---

## Engine

### Standard Range Electric Motor (Single/Dual Motor)

The Standard Range configuration utilizes permanent magnet AC synchronous motors paired with a 70 kWh usable battery pack, delivering solid everyday acceleration and efficiency. It offers a balanced entry point into EV ownership, though buyers should verify that high-voltage electrical updates have been completed.

**Loss of Motive Power**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicles may unexpectedly lose drive power or display high-voltage battery warnings, sometimes resulting in reduced speed or stalling while driving.
- **Solution:** Requires diagnostic evaluation by a certified dealer to address high-voltage battery junction box (HVBJB) or related electrical software issues.

### Extended Range Electric Motor (Single/Dual Motor)

The Extended Range configuration pairs larger motor setups with a 91 kWh usable battery, pushing out higher horsepower figures and longer driving ranges. It provides strong performance across various trims, with generally robust powertrain reliability outside of isolated electrical software complaints.

## Transmission

### Single-Speed Fixed Gear Transmission

The Mach-E relies on a single-speed direct-drive transmission coupled directly to the electric motor assembly. This layout eliminates traditional multi-gear shifting complexity, leading to smooth power delivery and minimal required mechanical maintenance.

## Brakes

### Electronic Parking Brake and Service Brakes

Braking systems integrate regenerative braking with traditional friction brakes, heavily influenced by one-pedal driving software.

**Parking Brake Fault**
- **Symptoms:** The electric parking brake can occasionally become stuck in the engaged position, preventing the vehicle from rolling or moving on its wheels.
- **Solution:** Inspection of wiring harnesses and actuator components, often addressed via manufacturer service programs.

## Exterior

### Door Latches and Locking System

Electrical architecture governs keyless entry, digital instrumentation, and extensive infotainment features, which occasionally experience software bugs.

**Intermittent Door Latch Operation**
- **Symptoms:** Doors may occasionally fail to latch securely, register false open warnings, or unlock/open intermittently.
- **Solution:** Dealer inspection and adjustment or replacement of electronic door latch mechanisms and software modules.
