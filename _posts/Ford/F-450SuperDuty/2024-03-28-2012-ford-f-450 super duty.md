**2012 Ford F-450 Super Duty**

The 2012 Ford F-450 Super Duty is a heavy-duty pickup truck that combines impressive power with superior hauling and towing capabilities. It is powered by a 6.7L Power Stroke turbodiesel V8 engine, which delivers 400 horsepower and 800 lb-ft of torque. Transmission options include a 6-speed TorqShift automatic transmission.

**Engine**

* **6.7L Power Stroke Turbodiesel V8**
    * **Excessive Oil Consumption:** Oil consumption may exceed 1 quart per 1,000 miles. Symptoms include low oil pressure warnings, engine knocking, and decreased performance. Solution involves replacing piston rings and/or valve seals. Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $5,000+
    * **Injector Sticking or Failure:** Injectors can become stuck open or fail, leading to misfires, rough idling, and reduced fuel efficiency. Replacement of the affected injectors is required. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000 per injector
    * **Turbine Failure:** The turbine wheel in the turbocharger can fail, resulting in a loss of boost pressure and reduced performance. Replacement of the turbocharger is necessary. Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,000+

**Transmission**

* **6-Speed TorqShift Automatic**
    * **Shifting Hesitation or Harshness:** Transmission may exhibit slow or rough shifting between gears, accompanied by clunking or jerking. Solution involves updating the transmission software or replacing the valve body. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
    * **Torque Converter Lockup Problems:** The torque converter may fail to lock up properly, leading to reduced power and fuel economy. Replacement of the torque converter is required. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+

**Suspension**

* **Front Ball Joint Failure:** Ball joints connecting the upper control arms to the steering knuckles can wear out or fail, causing excessive play in the steering and uneven tire wear. Replacement of the ball joints is necessary. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000 per side
* **Leaf Spring Breakage:** Leaf springs in the rear suspension can break, resulting in a sagging or tilted rear end and poor ride quality. Replacement of the leaf springs is required. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+

**Electrical**

* **Battery Discharge:** Batteries may lose charge prematurely due to a faulty alternator or parasitic draw. Testing and replacement of the alternator or repair of the electrical system is necessary. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
* **Electrical Harness Damage:** Electrical harnesses can become damaged or frayed, leading to electrical malfunctions and loss of functionality. Repair or replacement of the affected harnesses is required. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+

**Exterior**

* **Rear Bumper Rust:** Rear bumpers can develop rust spots due to insufficient corrosion protection. Refinishing or replacement of the bumper is necessary. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
* **Headlight Condensation:** Headlights may develop condensation inside the lens, affecting visibility in wet weather. Replacing the affected headlights is necessary. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500 per headlight

**Interior**

* **Steering Wheel Buttons Malfunction:** Steering wheel buttons for audio, cruise control, and other functions may become unresponsive or malfunction. Replacement of the steering wheel control module is necessary. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
* **Center Console Storage Bin Breakage:** The center console storage bin may break or crack due to improper design. Replacement of the bin or modification of the storage area is necessary. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500

**Summary**

The 2012 Ford F-450 Super Duty is a powerful and capable heavy-duty pickup, but it has experienced several significant issues. The engine, transmission, and electrical components have been particularly prone to problems, leading to costly repairs. While some issues can be resolved with software updates or minor repairs, others may require extensive replacements.
