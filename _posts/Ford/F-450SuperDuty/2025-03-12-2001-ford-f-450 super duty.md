The 2001 Ford F-450 Super Duty is a heavy-duty work truck known for its towing and hauling capabilities. Available engine options include the 7.3L Power Stroke Turbo Diesel V8, the 6.8L Triton V10, and the 5.4L Triton V8. The 7.3L Power Stroke, a popular choice, produces 275 horsepower and 525 lb-ft of torque. Owners appreciate the truck's robust build and reliability, especially the early Power Stroke diesels. Common complaints include the age-related issues typical of older vehicles, such as rust, worn suspension components, and electrical problems.

**Engine**

**7.3L Power Stroke Turbo Diesel V8**

*   **Cam Position Sensor Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Stalling, rough running, or a no-start condition. Check engine light may illuminate with a code related to the cam position sensor.
    *   Solution: Replace the cam position sensor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $150+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Injector Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Misfires, rough idle, white smoke from the exhaust, and decreased fuel economy.
    *   Solution: Diagnose and replace faulty injectors.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+ per injector (including labor)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Turbocharger Problems**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, excessive black smoke, and unusual noises from the turbocharger.
    *   Solution: Inspect the turbocharger for damage or wear and replace if necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1000 - $2500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**6.8L Triton V10**

*   **Spark Plug Ejection**
    *   Description/Symptoms: A loud popping noise followed by a noticeable misfire and loss of power. This occurs when a spark plug is ejected from the cylinder head, stripping the threads in the process.
    *   Solution: Repair or replace the cylinder head. Helicoil inserts can be used to repair the threads.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2000+ depending on the extent of the damage and repair method.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Exhaust Manifold Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Ticking or hissing sound, especially upon cold start. Can lead to reduced fuel economy and performance.
    *   Solution: Replace the exhaust manifold gasket or the manifold itself if warped or cracked.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**5.4L Triton V8**

*   **Spark Plug Ejection**
    *   Description/Symptoms: A loud popping noise followed by a noticeable misfire and loss of power. This occurs when a spark plug is ejected from the cylinder head, stripping the threads in the process.
    *   Solution: Repair or replace the cylinder head. Helicoil inserts can be used to repair the threads.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2000+ depending on the extent of the damage and repair method.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**4R100 Automatic Transmission**

*   **Slipping or Erratic Shifting**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The transmission may slip between gears, shift harshly, or fail to shift properly.
    *   Solution: Often caused by worn valve body components or internal clutch wear. A transmission rebuild or replacement may be required.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1500 - $4000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Torque Converter Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Shuddering at low speeds, especially when accelerating from a stop. May also experience stalling.
    *   Solution: Replace the torque converter. It's often recommended to flush the transmission and replace the filter at the same time.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Worn Ball Joints and Tie Rod Ends**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Loose steering, wandering, uneven tire wear, and clunking noises from the front end.
    *   Solution: Replace worn ball joints and tie rod ends.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+ per side
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Leaf Spring Sagging or Cracking**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rear end sagging, reduced load capacity, and a rough ride.
    *   Solution: Replace the leaf springs.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1500+ per side
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Alternator Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Dimming headlights, a dead battery, and warning lights on the dashboard.
    *   Solution: Replace the alternator.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Wiring Harness Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Intermittent electrical problems, such as lights flickering or gauges not working. Corrosion and damaged wiring are common, especially in areas exposed to the elements.
    *   Solution: Repair or replace the damaged sections of the wiring harness.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $1000+ depending on the severity and location of the damage.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Rust**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rust is a common issue, particularly in areas with road salt use. This can affect the body panels, frame, and undercarriage.
    *   Solution: Repair or replace rusted panels. Apply rust prevention coatings to the frame and undercarriage.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $5000+ depending on severity and area affected
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Cracked Dashboard**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Cracking of the dashboard, especially in hot climates.
    *   Solution: Dashboard replacement or installation of a dash cover.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2001 Ford F-450 Super Duty, while a capable truck, suffers from common issues related to its age and heavy-duty use. Engine problems, such as spark plug ejection and injector issues, can be costly. Transmission problems are also a major concern. Suspension wear and electrical gremlins are typical maintenance items. Rust is a significant concern depending on geographical location. Overall, potential buyers should be aware of these issues and factor in potential repair costs when considering purchasing this vehicle.
