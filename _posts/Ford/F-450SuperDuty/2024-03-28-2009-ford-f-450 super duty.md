**Introduction:**

The 2009 Ford F-450 Super Duty is a heavy-duty pickup truck known for its powerful engines, rugged capabilities, and impressive towing and hauling capacities. It is available with a variety of engine and transmission options, including:

* 6.4L Power Stroke V8 Diesel (350 hp / 650 lb-ft)
* 6.8L Triton V10 Gasoline (362 hp / 457 lb-ft)
* 5-speed TorqShift automatic transmission
* 6-speed TorqShift automatic transmission

Owners appreciate the F-450's towing and hauling capabilities, off-road performance, and comfortable interior. However, there are some issues to be aware of before purchasing.

**Engine**

**6.4L Power Stroke V8 Diesel**

* **Turbocharger Failure**: The turbocharger can fail, leading to a loss of power and increased exhaust smoke. Replacing the turbocharger is necessary and can cost $2,500 - $3,500+.
* **EGR Cooler Failure**: The exhaust gas recirculation (EGR) cooler can fail, resulting in coolant leaks and reduced engine performance. Replacing the EGR cooler typically costs $1,000 - $1,500+.
**6.8L Triton V10 Gasoline**

* **Spark Plug Failure**: Spark plugs can fail prematurely, causing engine misfires and decreased fuel economy. Replacing spark plugs typically costs $200 - $300+.

**Transmission**

**5-speed TorqShift Automatic**

* **Transmission Slippage**: The transmission may slip, making it difficult to accelerate or maintain speed. A transmission rebuild is often required and can cost $2,000 - $3,000+.
**6-speed TorqShift Automatic**

* **Torque Converter Shudder**: The torque converter can develop a shudder when the vehicle is accelerating or shifting. Replacing the torque converter typically costs $1,500 - $2,000+.

**Suspension**

* **Leaf Spring Breakage**: The leaf springs can break, causing the rear of the vehicle to sag and affecting handling. Replacing leaf springs typically costs $500 - $1,000+.

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain**: A parasitic draw can drain the battery, making it difficult to start the vehicle. Identifying and repairing the electrical drain can cost $200 - $500+.

**Exterior**

* **Tailgate Latch Failure**: The tailgate latch can fail, making it difficult to open or close the tailgate. Replacing the latch typically costs $100 - $200+.

**Interior**

* **Dashboard Rattle**: The dashboard can develop a rattle or vibration at certain speeds or road conditions. Identifying and repairing the source of the rattle can cost $200 - $500+.
* **HVAC Malfunction**: The heating, ventilation, and air conditioning (HVAC) system may malfunction, leading to temperature control issues. Repairing the HVAC system can cost $500 - $1,000+.

**Summary:**

The 2009 Ford F-450 Super Duty is a capable and powerful truck, but it is not without its issues. Common problems include turbocharger failure, EGR cooler failure, transmission slippage, torque converter shudder, and electrical problems. While not all F-450s will experience these issues, it's important to be aware of them before purchasing. The severity and cost of repairs can vary depending on the specific issue and the extent of the damage.
