# 2021 Ford F-450 Super Duty

### Overview
The 2021 Ford F-450 Super Duty is engineered for heavy-duty work, offering robust performance and capability. Reliability data for this model year indicates a generally strong performance, particularly in its core powertrain components. While the F-450 is built to withstand demanding conditions, like any heavy-duty truck, specific areas can experience issues under strenuous use or with prolonged mileage. This analysis focuses on documented technical reliability concerns beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential problem areas for owners and fleet managers.

### 6.7L Power Stroke V8 Diesel
**Fuel Injector Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Rough idling, reduced power, engine misfires, increased exhaust smoke.
- **Solution:** Replacement of faulty fuel injectors.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1500 per injector

**Diesel Particulate Filter (DPF) Regeneration Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Check Engine light illumination, reduced engine performance, increased fuel consumption, potential for DPF clogging.
- **Solution:** Forced regeneration, cleaning, or replacement of the DPF.
- **Cost:** $500 - $2000

### Summary Verdict
The 2021 Ford F-450 Super Duty demonstrates commendable reliability, especially in its gasoline engine options and transmissions, which show no significant widespread issues. The diesel engine, while powerful, has some documented concerns regarding fuel injectors and DPF regeneration, which are not uncommon for heavy-duty diesel applications and can be costly to repair. Electrical components, particularly the infotainment system, may experience occasional glitches. Overall, the F-450 is a robust truck built for demanding tasks, with most potential issues being manageable and typical for its class and intended use.
