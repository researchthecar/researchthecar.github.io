Here's a breakdown of common issues and complaints for the 2012 Ford F-450 Super Duty, categorized by major components, engine options, and transmission options, following the format you requested.

The 2012 Ford F-450 Super Duty is a heavy-duty truck designed for towing and hauling. It offers impressive capabilities and is favored for its robust construction and powerful engine options. Available engine options for the 2012 F-450 include the 6.7L Power Stroke V8 Turbo Diesel (400 hp, 800 lb-ft of torque) and the 6.2L Triton V8 gasoline engine (385 hp, 405 lb-ft of torque). Both engines are paired with the 6R140 TorqShift 6-speed automatic transmission. Owners often praise the F-450's towing capacity and overall durability, but common complaints revolve around fuel economy, ride quality, and certain mechanical issues that can arise with age and heavy use.

**Engine**

**6.7L Power Stroke V8 Turbo Diesel**

*   **Turbocharger Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, excessive black smoke, unusual turbocharger noise (whining or screeching). Can be caused by oil starvation, foreign object damage, or general wear and tear.
    *   Solution: Turbocharger replacement or rebuild. Diagnosis and repair of oil supply issues.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Diesel Particulate Filter (DPF) Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Frequent DPF regeneration cycles, "Exhaust Filter Full" message on the dashboard, reduced engine power. Can be caused by short trips, excessive idling, or faulty sensors.
    *   Solution: Forced DPF regeneration by a technician, DPF cleaning or replacement, sensor replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **High Pressure Fuel Pump (HPFP) Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Engine stalling, no start condition, fuel system contamination. This is a common issue due to fuel quality and the design of the pump.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the HPFP, fuel system cleaning, potentially replacing fuel injectors and other fuel system components.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $4,000 - $10,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**6.2L Triton V8 Gasoline**

*   **Spark Plug Breakage During Removal**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Difficult spark plug removal, spark plugs breaking off in the cylinder head. This is often due to dissimilar metal corrosion and improper torque during installation.
    *   Solution: Specialized tools for spark plug extraction, cylinder head repair or replacement in severe cases.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $2,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Oil Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks from various locations, including valve cover gaskets, oil pan gasket, and front crankshaft seal.
    *   Solution: Replace leaking gaskets and seals.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+ depending on the location and severity of the leak
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**6R140 TorqShift 6-Speed Automatic**

*   **Harsh Shifting**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Abrupt or jerky shifting between gears, especially during acceleration or deceleration.
    *   Solution: Transmission fluid flush and filter change, valve body cleaning or replacement, potential solenoid replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Torque Converter Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Shuddering or vibration during torque converter lockup, especially at highway speeds.
    *   Solution: Torque converter replacement, transmission flush.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $2,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Leaf Spring Sagging or Cracking**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Reduced ride height, especially in the rear, poor ride quality, visible cracks in leaf springs. This is more common with heavy usage.
    *   Solution: Leaf spring replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+ per spring
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Worn Ball Joints and Tie Rod Ends**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Loose steering, uneven tire wear, clunking noises from the front end.
    *   Solution: Replacement of ball joints and tie rod ends.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Sensor Failures (EGT, MAF, etc.)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Check engine light, poor engine performance, reduced fuel economy.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis and replacement of faulty sensors.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+ per sensor
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Alternator Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Battery light on, dimming headlights, vehicle stalling.
    *   Solution: Alternator replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Rust on Undercarriage and Body Panels**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Visible rust on frame, suspension components, and body panels, especially in areas exposed to road salt.
    *   Solution: Rust repair, panel replacement, rustproofing treatments.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $5,000+ (depending on severity)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **HVAC Blend Door Actuator Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Inability to control temperature settings, clicking noises from the dashboard.
    *   Solution: Blend door actuator replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2012 Ford F-450 Super Duty is a capable truck, but owners should be aware of potential issues. The 6.7L Power Stroke diesel engine is prone to turbocharger, DPF, and HPFP issues, which can be expensive to repair. The 6.2L gasoline engine can have spark plug and oil leak problems. The 6R140 transmission can experience harsh shifting and torque converter issues. Suspension components like leaf springs, ball joints, and tie rod ends may require replacement with age and heavy use. Electrical issues such as sensor failures and alternator problems can also occur. Rust is a common concern, especially in regions with road salt. Addressing these issues promptly can help maintain the F-450's reliability and performance. The severity and cost of these issues range from relatively minor repairs to major overhauls, so a thorough inspection is recommended before purchasing a used 2012 F-450.
