**Introduction:**

The 2017 Ford F-450 Super Duty is a heavy-duty pickup truck known for its robust construction, powerful engines, and impressive towing capabilities. It is offered with multiple engine and transmission options, including a 6.7L Power Stroke V8 turbodiesel engine mated to a 6-speed automatic transmission and a 6.8L Triton V10 gasoline engine paired with a 10-speed automatic transmission.

Despite its impressive capabilities, the 2017 Ford F-450 Super Duty has been reported to have several issues affecting various components.

**Engine**

**6.7L Power Stroke V8 Turbodiesel**

- **Turbocharger Failure:** Description/Symptoms: The turbocharger may fail prematurely, resulting in loss of power, reduced fuel economy, and excessive smoke from the exhaust. Solution: Replace the turbocharger assembly. Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+ Recalls: NHTSA Campaign Number: 17V875000
- **EGR Cooler Failure:** Description/Symptoms: The exhaust gas recirculation (EGR) cooler can develop leaks or become clogged, leading to poor engine performance, reduced fuel economy, and increased emissions. Solution: Replace the EGR cooler assembly. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+ Recalls: NHTSA Campaign Number: 17V794000
- **Fuel Injector Failure:** Description/Symptoms: Fuel injectors can become clogged or fail, causing rough idling, poor acceleration, and decreased fuel economy. Solution: Replace the affected fuel injector(s). Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+ Recalls: None Found

**6.8L Triton V10 Gasoline**

- **Spark Plug Failure:** Description/Symptoms: Spark plugs can foul or become damaged over time, resulting in misfires, reduced engine power, and poor fuel economy. Solution: Replace the spark plugs and ignition coils if necessary. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+ Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**6-speed Automatic Transmission**

- **Transmission Slipping:** Description/Symptoms: The transmission may slip or fail to engage gears smoothly, leading to delayed acceleration, gear hunting, and shuddering. Solution: Overhaul or replace the transmission. Estimated Cost to Fix: $3,000 - $6,000+ Recalls: NHTSA Campaign Number: 17V825000

**Suspension**

- **Ball Joint Failure:** Description/Symptoms: Ball joints connecting the suspension components can wear out prematurely, causing steering instability, clunking noises, and uneven tire wear. Solution: Replace the affected ball joint(s). Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+ Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

- **Battery Drain:** Description/Symptoms: The battery may drain prematurely, leaving the vehicle unable to start. Solution: Inspect and replace the battery and alternator if necessary. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+ Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

- **Headlight Condensation:** Description/Symptoms: Headlights may accumulate moisture inside, reducing visibility and compromising the functionality of the bulbs. Solution: Seal the headlights and replace any damaged bulbs. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+ Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

- **Dashboard Distortion:** Description/Symptoms: The dashboard may become warped or distorted due to exposure to heat or sunlight. Solution: Replace the dashboard. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+ Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

While the 2017 Ford F-450 Super Duty offers impressive capabilities, it has been reported to have several issues affecting various components, including the engine, transmission, suspension, electrical system, exterior, and interior. Some of these issues are relatively minor and inexpensive to fix, while others can be more severe and costly. Owners should be aware of the potential problems and factor in the cost of repairs when considering purchasing this vehicle.
