The 2022 Ford F-450 Super Duty is a heavy-duty truck known for its impressive towing and hauling capabilities. Engine options include the 6.2L V8 gasoline engine (385 hp, 430 lb-ft of torque), the 7.3L "Godzilla" V8 gasoline engine (430 hp, 475 lb-ft of torque), and the 6.7L "Power Stroke" V8 turbo-diesel engine (475 hp, 1,050 lb-ft of torque). All engines are paired with a 10-speed automatic transmission. Owners often praise the F-450 for its robust performance and comfortable interior (especially in higher trims), but some criticize its large size and high price point.

**Engine**

**6.7L Power Stroke V8 Turbo-Diesel**

*   **CP4 Fuel Pump Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Sudden engine failure, often without warning. Metal debris from the failing CP4 pump contaminates the entire fuel system.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the CP4 fuel pump, fuel injectors, fuel rails, and fuel lines. Thorough cleaning of the fuel tank. Installation of a CP4 failure prevention kit.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $8,000 - $12,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Turbocharger Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, excessive black smoke, whistling or grinding noises from the turbocharger area.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the turbocharger. Inspect and replace any related hoses or pipes if damaged.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $3,000 - $6,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**10-Speed Automatic (10R140)**

*   **Harsh Shifting/Clunking**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rough or jerky shifts, especially between lower gears. A noticeable "clunk" noise when shifting.
    *   Solution: Software recalibration of the Transmission Control Module (TCM). If the problem persists, valve body replacement may be necessary. Complete transmission replacement for severe cases.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300 (recalibration), $1,500 - $3,000 (valve body), $6,000 - $10,000+ (replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Delayed Engagement**
    *   Description/Symptoms: A noticeable delay between shifting into drive or reverse and the transmission actually engaging.
    *   Solution: Check transmission fluid level and condition. Perform a transmission fluid flush and filter change. If the issue persists, valve body or internal component damage may be present, requiring further diagnosis and repair.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300 (fluid flush), $1,500 - $3,000 (valve body), $6,000 - $10,000+ (replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Front End "Death Wobble"**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Violent shaking of the front end, typically at highway speeds, often triggered by a bump or uneven road surface.
    *   Solution: Replacement of steering damper (stabilizer). Inspection and replacement of worn steering components (tie rod ends, ball joints). Ensure proper tire balance and alignment.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+ (depending on parts replaced)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Freezing/Crashing**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The SYNC infotainment system freezes, crashes, or becomes unresponsive.
    *   Solution: Software update for the SYNC system. In some cases, a hardware replacement (APIM - Accessory Protocol Interface Module) may be required.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 (software update if available), $500 - $1,500+ (APIM replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Blind Spot Monitoring System Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Blind spot monitoring system intermittently fails to function, generating false alerts, or not detecting vehicles in the blind spot.
    *   Solution: Check the sensors for damage or obstructions. Recalibration of the blind spot monitoring system. Replacement of faulty sensors.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300 (recalibration), $300 - $800+ (sensor replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Tailgate Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Tailgate will not open, or is difficult to open. The power tailgate function may malfunction.
    *   Solution: Check tailgate latch mechanisms for damage or obstruction. Lubricate the latch mechanism. Replace faulty tailgate actuators or sensors.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $200 (lubrication/adjustment), $200 - $600+ (actuator/sensor replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Seat Heater Malfunction**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Seat heaters fail to heat, or only heat intermittently.
    *   Solution: Check wiring and connections to the seat heater elements. Replacement of faulty seat heater elements or control module.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+ (depending on parts replaced)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2022 Ford F-450 Super Duty, while a powerful and capable truck, can be prone to certain issues. The most significant concerns revolve around the 6.7L diesel engine's CP4 fuel pump failure, which can be extremely costly to repair. Transmission issues, particularly harsh shifting, are also relatively common. While other problems like infotainment glitches and tailgate malfunctions are less severe, they can still be frustrating. Overall, potential buyers should be aware of these common issues and factor in potential repair costs when considering purchasing a 2022 F-450, especially one equipped with the 6.7L diesel engine.
