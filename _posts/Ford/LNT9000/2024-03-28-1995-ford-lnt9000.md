**Introduction**

The 1995 Ford LNT9000 is a heavy-duty commercial truck known for its powerful engine and rugged construction. It is available with a variety of engine and transmission options, including:

* **Engine Options:**
    * 12.7L inline-6 diesel (350 hp, 800 lb-ft torque)
    * 14.6L inline-6 diesel (400 hp, 1200 lb-ft torque)
* **Transmission Options:**
    * 7-speed Eaton Fuller manual transmission
    * 8-speed ZF AS Tronic automated transmission

Overall, the Ford LNT9000 is a reliable and capable truck that is appreciated for its performance, durability, and ease of maintenance. However, it is not without its issues.

**Engine**

**12.7L Inline-6 Diesel**

* **Cylinder Head Gasket Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaks, overheating, white smoke from exhaust
    * Solution: Replace cylinder head gaskets
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
* **Turbocharger Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, black smoke from exhaust
    * Solution: Replace turbocharger
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+

**14.6L Inline-6 Diesel**

* **Intake Valve Sticking:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Rough idle, power loss, engine knocking
    * Solution: Clean or replace intake valves
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+

**Transmission**

**7-Speed Eaton Fuller Manual Transmission**

* **Synchronizer Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears, grinding noises
    * Solution: Replace synchronizers
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
* **Clutch Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Slipping clutch, difficulty engaging gears
    * Solution: Replace clutch
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+

**8-Speed ZF AS Tronic Automated Transmission**

* **Transmission Control Module (TCM) Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Erratic shifting, transmission fault codes
    * Solution: Replace TCM
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+

**Suspension**

* **Leaf Spring Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Broken or cracked leaf springs, sagging suspension
    * Solution: Replace leaf springs
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+ per leaf spring

**Electrical**

* **Alternator Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Dimming lights, battery not charging
    * Solution: Replace alternator
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+

**Exterior**

* **Hood Hinge Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Hood not opening or closing properly
    * Solution: Replace hood hinges
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+

**Interior**

* **Dashboard Cluster Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Inaccurate readings, flickering or dimming lights
    * Solution: Replace dashboard cluster
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
* **Air Conditioning Compressor Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Lack of cold air, loud noises from compressor
    * Solution: Replace air conditioning compressor
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+

**Summary**

While the Ford LNT9000 is a dependable truck overall, it is not immune to issues. The most common concerns reported by owners include engine gasket failures, turbocharger failures, and transmission problems. Electrical and suspension issues are less prevalent but can still arise. Interior issues are generally minor and inexpensive to fix. The severity and cost of the problems vary depending on the specific issue and the vehicle's age and mileage.
