Here is a breakdown of common issues and complaints for the 2021 Ford Transit-250, categorized by component. The 2021 Ford Transit-250 is a popular van known for its versatility and cargo capacity. Available engines for the 2021 model include a 3.5L PFDI V6 producing 275 horsepower and 262 lb-ft of torque, and a 3.5L EcoBoost V6 producing 310 horsepower and 400 lb-ft of torque. A single transmission option is available: a 10-speed automatic. Owners generally appreciate the Transit's spacious interior, comfortable ride, and customization options. Common dislikes include fuel economy and some reliability concerns.

**Engine**

**3.5L PFDI V6**

*   **Engine Misfires**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rough idling, reduced power, check engine light. Potential causes include faulty spark plugs, ignition coils, or fuel injectors.
    *   Solution: Diagnose the cause of the misfire using a scan tool. Replace faulty spark plugs, ignition coils, or fuel injectors as needed.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+ depending on the component needing replacement and labor costs.
    *   Recalls: None Found

* **Excessive Oil Consumption**
    * Description/Symptoms: Oil levels dropping faster than expected, blue smoke from exhaust, and possible engine knocking.
    * Solution: Inspect for leaks first. If no leaks are found, investigate potential issues with the piston rings or PCV valve. Repair or replace as necessary.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,500+, depending on whether it's a PCV valve replacement or requires engine work.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**3.5L EcoBoost V6**

*   **Turbocharger Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Whining noise from the engine, reduced power, check engine light.
    *   Solution: Inspect the turbocharger for damage or leaks. Replace the turbocharger if necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Intercooler Condensation/Water Ingestion**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Stumbling, hesitation, or loss of power, especially in humid conditions.
    *   Solution: Install a modified intercooler or relocate the existing one to prevent water accumulation.  Some owners drill a small weep hole in the intercooler to allow condensation to drain. (Note: This is not a Ford-approved solution)
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+ depending on the chosen solution.
    *   Recalls: None Found

* **Engine Misfires**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rough idling, reduced power, check engine light. Potential causes include faulty spark plugs, ignition coils, or fuel injectors.
    *   Solution: Diagnose the cause of the misfire using a scan tool. Replace faulty spark plugs, ignition coils, or fuel injectors as needed.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+ depending on the component needing replacement and labor costs.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**10-Speed Automatic**

*   **Harsh Shifting/Slipping**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Abrupt or delayed shifts, slipping between gears, or clunking noises during shifting.
    *   Solution: Check the transmission fluid level and condition. Perform a transmission fluid flush and filter replacement. If the problem persists, further diagnosis and repair by a qualified technician may be required, potentially involving valve body replacement or transmission rebuild.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 (fluid flush) - $3,000+ (transmission rebuild or replacement).
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Rough Downshifts**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Harsh or jerky downshifts, especially when decelerating.
    *   Solution: Software updates or reprogramming of the transmission control module (TCM) might resolve the issue. In some cases, mechanical repairs to the transmission may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $1,000+ depending on whether it is a software issue or a mechanical problem.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Rear Leaf Spring Squeaking**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Squeaking noise coming from the rear suspension, especially when going over bumps.
    *   Solution: Lubricate the leaf spring bushings with silicone-based grease. If the bushings are worn, they may need to be replaced.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $20 - $50 (lubrication) or $200 - $500+ (bushing replacement).
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Battery Drain Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The battery drains overnight, especially when the vehicle is not in use.
    *   Solution: Check for parasitic draws by testing each fuse. Ensure all lights and accessories are turned off.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100-$500+ depending on the cost of the part causing the parasitic draw.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Infotainment System Freezing/Crashing**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The infotainment screen freezes, becomes unresponsive, or crashes intermittently.
    *   Solution: Try resetting the system by holding the power button. Software updates may be available. If the problem persists, the infotainment system may need to be replaced.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 (software update) - $1,000+ (system replacement).
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Sliding Door Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Difficulty opening or closing the sliding door, sticking, or unusual noises.
    *   Solution: Inspect the door tracks and rollers for obstructions. Lubricate the tracks and rollers. Adjust the door alignment if necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $20 (lubrication) - $500+ (roller/track replacement and adjustment).
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Rattling Noises**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rattling noises coming from various parts of the interior, such as the dashboard or door panels.
    *   Solution: Locate the source of the rattling. Tighten loose screws or clips. Use sound-deadening material to reduce vibration.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 - $200+ depending on the source and complexity of the fix.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2021 Ford Transit-250 can have some reliability issues that are not uncommon, but can require time to properly diagnose and repair. The engine issues range in severity depending on the cause, but turbo issues can be a costly repair. The transmission issues can also be costly, as transmissions are a complex component. These issues are on the lower end of the spectrum but are some things to consider when purchasing one of these vehicles.
