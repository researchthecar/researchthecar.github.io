# 2024 Ford Edge Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2024 Ford Edge offers comfortable passenger space and modern tech, but prospective used buyers should note several reported electrical and powertrain issues. Early owner feedback highlights occasional software glitches, powertrain shudders, and liftgate sensor irregularities.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L EcoBoost Turbocharged I4

The standard 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine produces 250 horsepower and 280 lb-ft of torque, paired with an 8-speed automatic transmission. It provides adequate acceleration for daily commuting while maintaining competitive efficiency, though some owners have experienced electrical system hiccups.

**Electrical Warning Lights and Engine Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have reported instrument clusters going dark, sudden illumination of warning lights, and unexpected engine shutdown or restart while driving.
- **Solution:** Take the vehicle to a certified dealer for diagnostic testing of the charging system, wiring harnesses, and Powertrain Control Module (PCM).

### 2.7L EcoBoost Twin-Turbo V6

Available exclusively on the ST trim, the 2.7-liter twin-turbo V6 generates 335 horsepower and 380 lb-ft of torque with standard all-wheel drive. It delivers brisk performance and a sportier driving dynamic for enthusiasts.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 8-speed automatic transmission is paired with the 2.0L EcoBoost engine across front-wheel and all-wheel drive models, offering smooth gear selection under normal driving conditions.

**Transmission Shudder and Torque Converter Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle experiences noticeable shudders, particularly at speeds under 40 mph, and may refuse to engage gears properly.
- **Solution:** Have a dealer inspect the transmission and torque converter for internal wear or software calibration updates.

### 7-Speed / 8-Speed Performance Automatic

The performance-tuned automatic transmission paired with the ST's V6 engine delivers responsive shifting to match its sport-oriented driving dynamics.

## Electrical

### Infotainment and Seat Heaters

Electrical accessories provide modern convenience, but screen freezes and seat heater faults require careful pre-purchase inspection.

**SYNC Screen Freezing and Seat Heater Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** The touchscreen infotainment system may intermittently display a black screen or fail to switch inputs, while seat heaters can malfunction and overheat.
- **Solution:** Software updates can resolve SYNC display issues, while malfunctioning seat elements require component replacement by a dealer.

### Power Liftgate and Seats

Cabin amenities offer high-end comfort, though automated tailgate sensors and power seat mechanics can occasionally misbehave.

**Unintended Liftgate Operation and Seat Motor Noises**
- **Symptoms:** Hands-free liftgate sensors may trigger closure or opening unexpectedly when near the bumper, and power seat motors can produce grinding noises.
- **Solution:** Inspect liftgate sensor sensitivity settings and have faulty power seat assemblies repaired or replaced.

## Exterior

### Panoramic Glass Roof

The panoramic sunroof opens up the cabin to natural light, though rare reports of spontaneous glass failure warrant thorough inspection of the roof panel.

**Sunroof Spontaneous Shattering**
- **Symptoms:** The panoramic glass roof panel may crack or shatter suddenly while driving without any apparent external impact.
- **Solution:** Replace the damaged glass panel under warranty or comprehensive insurance coverage, checking the frame alignment.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V315000: Back Over Prevention:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Edge&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** A software error may cause the rearview camera image to delay, freeze, or not display when the vehicle is in reverse. A frozen or missing rearview camera image can reduce the driver's view behind the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 26V124000: Back Over Prevention:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Edge&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** A rearview camera that fails to display an image can reduce the driver’s view behind the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2024 Ford Edge remains a comfortable midsize crossover with strong engine options and practical utility. Buyers should carefully check the powertrain for signs of transmission shudder, verify that all electrical features and backup cameras operate correctly, and inspect the panoramic sunroof glass prior to purchase.
