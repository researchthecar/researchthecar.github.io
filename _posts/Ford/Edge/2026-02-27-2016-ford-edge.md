# 2016 Ford Edge Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2016 Ford Edge is a spacious and comfortable midsize crossover that appeals to families and commuters alike, offering responsive handling and a quiet ride. However, prospective buyers should approach used models with caution due to several prominent mechanical concerns documented by owners. Powertrain durability, particularly regarding engine coolant leaks and differential issues, requires careful inspection before purchase.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L EcoBoost Turbocharged I4

A turbocharged 2.0-liter four-cylinder engine generating around 245 horsepower and 275 lb-ft of torque, providing a balance of efficiency and power. Buyers should thoroughly inspect this option for coolant intrusion history.

**Coolant Intrusion and Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Engine misfires, rough idle, white smoke from the exhaust, low coolant levels, and illuminated check engine lights.
- **Solution:** May require professional diagnosis and potentially a complete engine block or cylinder head replacement if coolant leaks into the cylinders.

**Head Gasket Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Overheating, coolant loss, and blown head gasket warnings.
- **Solution:** Replace the head gasket and inspect the cylinder head for warping or damage.

### 3.5L V6

A naturally aspirated 3.5-liter V6 engine producing roughly 280 horsepower and 250 lb-ft of torque, known for smooth power delivery. It generally avoids the severe coolant intrusion concerns seen in the smaller EcoBoost option.

### 2.7L EcoBoost Twin-Turbo V6

A high-performance twin-turbocharged 2.7-liter V6 pushing out about 315 horsepower and 350 lb-ft of torque, exclusive to the Sport trim. It offers brisk acceleration but demands strict adherence to maintenance schedules.

## Transmission

### 6F35 6-Speed Automatic

A traditional 6-speed automatic transmission paired across various trims and drivetrain configurations. While it shifts smoothly when maintained, routine fluid changes are vital to ensure long-term reliability.

### All-Wheel Drive Rear Differential

AWD models distribute power to the rear wheels as needed, but owners should listen closely for vibrations or mechanical noises from the rear axle during test drives.

**Rear Differential Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Loud shaking, shuddering, popping, or binding in the rear end of the vehicle when accelerating from a stop or turning.
- **Solution:** Service or replace the rear differential unit (RDU).
- **Cost:** $5,000

## Suspension

### Wheel Lug Nuts

Suspension and wheel hardware generally hold up well, though factory wheel fasteners are a known nuisance.

**Swollen Lug Nuts**
- **Symptoms:** OEM lug nuts become swollen and deformed, making them impossible to remove with a standard tire iron during a flat tire change.
- **Solution:** Replace factory two-piece lug nuts with solid aftermarket alternatives.

## Steering

### Electric Power Steering

The electric power steering system provides light and accurate handling under normal conditions, but sudden assist failures can pose safety risks.

**Steering Assist Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Sudden loss of power steering assist, stiff steering wheel, and warning messages on the instrument cluster.
- **Solution:** Inspect steering software, wiring harness, or replace the electronic power steering rack assembly if necessary.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V632000: Steering:Electric Power Assist System](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Edge&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** If the bolts corrode, the steering gear motor may detach from the gear housing resulting in a loss of power steering assist, requiring a greater steering effort, especially at lower speeds, and increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will replace the steering gear motor bolts and apply a wax sealer. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V733000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Antilock/Traction Control/Electronic Limited Slip](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Edge&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 126, "Electronic Stability Control Systems" and number 135, "Light Vehicle Brake Systems." If the operator is not warned of an ABS or ESC malfunction and the ESC loses engine torque control, there would be an increased risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will replace the ABS module, and update the service software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V123000: Air Bags:Frontal:Driver Side:Inflator Module](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Edge&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** In the event of a crash, the driver's frontal air bag may not fully inflate or the air bag cushion may detach from the air bag module. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 208, "Occupant Crash Protection." During deployment, if the air bag does not fully inflate or the cushion.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will replace the driver frontal air bag module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V469000: Service Brakes](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Edge&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** A ruptured front brake hose will cause a brake fluid leak, lengthening the distance needed to stop the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will replace the front brake hoses with the new hoses that have a revised braid material, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V413000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission:Gear Position Indication (Prndl)](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Edge&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** Either scenario increases the risk of a crash or injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the under hood shift bushing and add a protective cap over the shift cable bushing, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V544000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Foundation Components:Hoses, Lines/Piping, And Fittings](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Edge&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** A brake fluid leak can extend the distance required to stop the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the brake jounce hoses and adjust brake jounce hoses with no damage present, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V572000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Edge&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** A rearview camera that displays an inverted, distorted, or blank image can reduce the driver’s view behind the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the rearview camera as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2016 Ford Edge is appealing for its cabin comfort and driving dynamics, but potential buyers must perform a rigorous pre-purchase inspection. Pay close attention to the 2.0L EcoBoost engine for signs of coolant leaks or intrusion, and test-drive AWD models carefully to ensure the rear differential is quiet and vibration-free.
