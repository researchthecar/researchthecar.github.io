# 2021 Ford Edge Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2021 Ford Edge is a midsize two-row crossover that offers a spacious cabin, user-friendly technology, and composed handling. However, prospective used car buyers should carefully inspect powertrain and electrical systems, as owner reports highlight some recurring concerns regarding transmission operation, infotainment glitches, and sudden component failures outside standard warranty windows.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L EcoBoost Turbocharged Inline-4

The standard 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine produces 250 horsepower and 275 lb-ft of torque, paired with an 8-speed automatic transmission. It offers a strong balance of everyday efficiency and usable power, though some owners report driveability quirks related to emissions components and software tuning.

**EGR Valve and Powertrain Hesitation**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicles may experience jerking, sputtering, or loss of power during acceleration, often without triggering a check engine light.
- **Solution:** Inspect and replace faulty EGR valves or update the powertrain control module (PCM) software.

### 2.7L EcoBoost Twin-Turbocharged V6

Reserved for the sporty ST trim, the 2.7-liter twin-turbo V6 generates a robust 335 horsepower and 380 lb-ft of torque, mated to a sport-tuned 7-speed automatic transmission and standard all-wheel drive. It delivers brisk performance for driving enthusiasts while maintaining standard midsize SUV utility.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 8-speed automatic transmission manages power for standard four-cylinder models. While generally smooth when functioning correctly, it is the subject of notable owner complaints regarding premature wear, shuddering, and costly internal component failures.

**Transmission Shudder and Harsh Shifting**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may notice a shuddering sensation, slipping gears, delayed engagement into reverse, or harsh jumps during acceleration and deceleration.
- **Solution:** Flush and refill transmission fluid, inspect torque converters, or consult a dealer for specialized software updates and mechanical repairs.
- **Cost:** $10,000

## Electrical

### Infotainment and Display Screen

The electrical architecture handles keyless entry, advanced driver assistance features, and the SYNC infotainment interface. Software bugs and screen blackouts are common user gripes requiring module updates or hardware replacement.

**Screen Glitches and Blackouts**
- **Symptoms:** The central infotainment screen or backup camera display may occasionally glitch, freeze, or go completely blank during vehicle operation.
- **Solution:** Reprogram the APIM module or replace the internal computer components behind the screen.
- **Cost:** $1,500

## Safety

### Seat Belts

Safety systems generally perform reliably, though isolated complaints point to latching irregularities and warning indicators within the seat belt assembly.

**Seat Belt Latch Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** Front or rear passenger seat belts may present latching difficulties or trigger diagnostic warning lights.
- **Solution:** Have the seat belt assembly inspected and replaced by a certified dealership.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V011000: Power Train:Driveline:Driveshaft](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Edge&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** An inadequate amount of lubricant in the rear drive unit may cause seizure of the rear drive axle. If the rear drive unit seizes, there may be a loss of drive and/or a loss of vehicle control, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will check the rear drive unit lubricant level. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V377000: Seat Belts:Front:Retractor](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Edge&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The driver's seat belt retractor pretensioner assembly may not function correctly when activated during a crash. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 208, "Occupant Crash Protection." A seatbelt assembly that does not function as intended can increase the risk of injury during a.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the driver's seat belt retractor pretensioner assembly, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V151000: Back Over Prevention:Display Function](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Edge&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** A software error may cause the rearview camera to display a blank or distorted image when the vehicle is in reverse. A rearview camera that displays a blank or distorted image can reduce the driver's view of what is behind the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 24V635000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Engine:Hard Parts Internal/Mechanical](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Edge&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** A loss of drive power increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will perform an engine cycle test and replace the engine as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V160000: Back Over Prevention:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Edge&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** A software error may cause the rearview camera to display a blank or distorted image when the vehicle is in reverse. A rearview camera that displays a blank or distorted image can reduce the driver's view of what is behind the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the rearview camera software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V315000: Back Over Prevention:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Edge&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** A software error may cause the rearview camera image to delay, freeze, or not display when the vehicle is in reverse. A frozen or missing rearview camera image can reduce the driver's view behind the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 26V124000: Back Over Prevention:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Edge&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** A rearview camera that fails to display an image can reduce the driver’s view behind the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2021 Ford Edge remains an appealing used crossover choice thanks to its comfortable ride and modern features, but buyers should proceed with caution. Be sure to thoroughly test drive the vehicle to check for any transmission shudder or harsh shifting, and verify that all infotainment and safety electronics operate correctly. Checking service records for past powertrain and software updates is highly recommended.
