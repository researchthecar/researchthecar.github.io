# 2017 Ford Edge Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2017 Ford Edge is a midsize two-row crossover that offers a comfortable ride, spacious cabin, and composed handling. However, prospective buyers should approach certain powertrain configurations with caution due to prominent engine reliability concerns reported by owners.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L EcoBoost Turbocharged Inline-4

A 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine producing around 245 horsepower and 275 lb-ft of torque. While peppy and efficient, this specific option carries significant owner complaints regarding coolant intrusion into the cylinders.

**Coolant Intrusion**
- **Symptoms:** Check engine light illuminates, engine misfires on startup, and coolant levels drop without an external leak.
- **Solution:** Requires extensive engine repairs or complete engine block replacement in severe cases.

### 3.5L V6

A naturally aspirated 3.5-liter V6 engine pushing approximately 280 horsepower and 250 lb-ft of torque. Known for offering smooth, traditional power delivery with fewer reported turbo-specific issues.

### 2.7L EcoBoost Twin-Turbo V6

A high-output 2.7-liter twin-turbocharged V6 delivering 315 horsepower and 350 lb-ft of torque, exclusive to the Sport trim for punchy, performance-oriented driving.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed SelectShift Automatic Transmission

A traditional 6-speed automatic transmission paired across front-wheel and all-wheel-drive configurations. While generally durable, some owners report premature mechanical failure and erratic shifting.

**Premature Failure and Erratic Shifting**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle slips out of gear while driving, fails to engage gears properly, or experiences sudden loss of acceleration.
- **Solution:** Transmission rebuild or complete unit replacement.

## Electrical

### Back Over Prevention Camera

Park assist and backup display technology that can exhibit intermittent connectivity or camera degradation.

**Backup Camera Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Rearview camera image intermittently displays blank, flickers, freezes, or shows distorted colors.
- **Solution:** Replace the rearview camera assembly or update system software.

## Exterior

### Wheel Lug Nuts

Standard factory wheel hardware prone to cosmetic and functional swelling over time.

**Swollen Lug Nuts**
- **Symptoms:** Exterior caps on factory lug nuts swell up, preventing standard lug wrenches from fitting properly during a flat tire change.
- **Solution:** Replace factory two-piece lug nuts with solid aftermarket alternatives.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V390000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission:Torque Converter](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Edge&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** If the torque converter weld studs fail, the torque converter will not be connected to the engine flexplate and the vehicle will lose the ability to move, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will replace the torque converters, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V205000: Structure:Body:Roof And Pillars](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Edge&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 214, "Side Impact Protection." If the vehicle has reduced lateral structural integrity, the occupants are at an increased risk of injury in the event of a side-impact crash.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will repair the windshield header, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V123000: Air Bags:Frontal:Driver Side:Inflator Module](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Edge&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** In the event of a crash, the driver's frontal air bag may not fully inflate or the air bag cushion may detach from the air bag module. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 208, "Occupant Crash Protection." During deployment, if the air bag does not fully inflate or the cushion.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will replace the driver frontal air bag module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V427000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission:Torque Converter](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Edge&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** If the torque converter weld studs fail, the torque converter will not be connected to the engine flexplate and the vehicle will lose the ability to move, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Ford or Lincoln will notify owners, and dealers will replace the torque converters, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V469000: Service Brakes](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Edge&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** A ruptured front brake hose will cause a brake fluid leak, lengthening the distance needed to stop the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will replace the front brake hoses with the new hoses that have a revised braid material, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V413000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission:Gear Position Indication (Prndl)](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Edge&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** Either scenario increases the risk of a crash or injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the under hood shift bushing and add a protective cap over the shift cable bushing, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V544000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Foundation Components:Hoses, Lines/Piping, And Fittings](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Edge&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** A brake fluid leak can extend the distance required to stop the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the brake jounce hoses and adjust brake jounce hoses with no damage present, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V572000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Edge&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** A rearview camera that displays an inverted, distorted, or blank image can reduce the driver’s view behind the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the rearview camera as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

When shopping for a used model, thoroughly inspect service records and pay close attention to the 2.0L EcoBoost engine for signs of coolant loss. Prioritize pre-purchase inspections by a qualified mechanic to ensure the engine and transmission are functioning properly before buying.
