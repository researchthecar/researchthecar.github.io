# 2009 Ford Edge

### Overview
The 2009 Ford Edge, a mid-size crossover SUV, offered a blend of practicality and comfort for families. It was generally considered a reliable vehicle for its model year, with a focus on everyday usability. While most owners reported a positive experience, like any vehicle, it had specific areas that could experience issues over time. This analysis focuses on documented technical reliability concerns beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential problem areas for the 2009 Ford Edge across its various configurations.

---


## Engine

### 3.5L V6 Duratec
**Intake Manifold Runner Control (IMRC) Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Check Engine light illumination, rough idle, loss of power, P2004, P2005, P2006 codes.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the IMRC actuator or the entire intake manifold assembly.
- **Cost:** $300 - $700

**Coolant Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Visible coolant puddles under the vehicle, low coolant level, overheating, sweet smell.
- **Solution:** Inspection and replacement of leaking hoses, water pump, or thermostat housing.
- **Cost:** $200 - $600


## Transmission

### 6F50 6-Speed Automatic
**Hesitation or Shuddering**
- **Symptoms:** Jerking or shuddering during acceleration or gear changes, delayed engagement.
- **Solution:** Transmission fluid and filter change, software update, or internal transmission repair.
- **Cost:** $400 - $1500+


## Suspension

### Standard Chassis
**Worn Struts/Shocks**
- **Symptoms:** Bouncy ride, excessive body roll, clunking noises over bumps, uneven tire wear.
- **Solution:** Replacement of front or rear struts/shocks.
- **Cost:** $400 - $800


## Electrical

### General Electrical System
**Faulty Alternator**
- **Symptoms:** Dimming headlights, battery warning light, electrical components malfunctioning, dead battery.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the alternator.
- **Cost:** $400 - $700

**Power Window Motor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Power windows not operating, slow window operation, clicking noises.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the power window motor and regulator assembly.
- **Cost:** $200 - $400

### Summary Verdict
The 2009 Ford Edge generally presents a reliable profile, particularly with its 3.5L V6 engine. The most commonly cited issues revolve around the intake manifold runner control system and potential coolant leaks, which are repairable. Transmission issues, while not pervasive, can manifest as hesitation or shuddering, often addressed by fluid services or more involved repairs. Suspension components like struts and shocks may require replacement due to wear. Electrical concerns, such as alternator failure and power window motor issues, are also noted. Overall, the 2009 Edge is a solid choice, but prospective buyers should be aware of these potential mechanical and electrical points.
