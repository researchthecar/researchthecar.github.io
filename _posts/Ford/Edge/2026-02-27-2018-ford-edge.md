# 2018 Ford Edge Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used Ford Edge, prospective buyers will find a comfortable, spacious midsize crossover that offers decent tech and smooth ride quality. However, evaluating mechanical reliability is crucial, as owner reports highlight significant powertrain concerns—most notably related to coolant leaks and intrusion in certain engine options. Thoroughly checking maintenance records and having a trusted mechanic inspect the vehicle before purchase can help avoid expensive repair bills.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L EcoBoost Turbocharged I4

A turbocharged 4-cylinder engine offering around 245 horsepower, balancing efficiency with pep. Owners have reported serious issues involving coolant leaking into the engine cylinders.

**Coolant Intrusion**
- **Symptoms:** Check engine light illumination, rough idling upon startup, white smoke, and disappearing coolant without external puddles.
- **Solution:** May require engine replacement or cylinder head service depending on severity.

### 3.5L V6

A naturally aspirated V6 providing roughly 280 horsepower for robust highway passing power. While generally stout, it requires careful monitoring of the cooling system.

**Coolant Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Low coolant levels, overheating warnings, and sweet-smelling fluid loss under the vehicle.
- **Solution:** Repairing or replacing leaking gaskets, hoses, or water pumps.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed SelectShift Automatic

A traditional 6-speed automatic transmission paired with front-wheel or all-wheel drive. It generally shifts smoothly, though some owners report torque converter and linkage concerns.

**Torque Converter and Shifting Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Heavy vibration when approaching a stop, illumination of the wrench warning light, or failure to shift into Park properly.
- **Solution:** Diagnose transmission components and replace worn torque converters or shifter cable bushings.

## Suspension

### Wheel Lug Nuts

Standard factory wheels and hardware that are generally durable, aside from a common Ford issue with capped lug nuts expanding over time.

**Swollen Lug Nuts**
- **Symptoms:** Difficulty fitting standard socket wrenches onto factory lug nuts during tire rotations or flat repairs due to outer cap swelling.
- **Solution:** Replace factory two-piece lug nuts with solid aftermarket alternatives.

## Electrical

### Accessory Protocol Interface Module (APIM)

Controls infotainment, climate displays, and rearview camera feeds. Failures are relatively common across various model years.

**Infotainment and Backup Camera Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Distorted backup camera display, 'Camera not Available' messages, or total touchscreen unresponsiveness.
- **Solution:** Reprogramming or replacing the APIM module behind the dashboard.

## Exterior

### Panoramic Sunroof and Glass

Exterior glass and trim components. While mostly trouble-free, occasional spontaneous shattering has been reported with large panoramic sunroofs.

**Unexpected Glass Shattering**
- **Symptoms:** Loud explosion sound followed by the panoramic sunroof or rear window shattering spontaneously while driving or parked.
- **Solution:** Replace shattered glass panels and inspect the frame for alignment issues.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V390000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission:Torque Converter](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Edge&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** If the torque converter weld studs fail, the torque converter will not be connected to the engine flexplate and the vehicle will lose the ability to move, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will replace the torque converters, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V612000: Latches/Locks/Linkages:Doors:Latch](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Edge&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** Vehicles with shorter door striker bolts fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 206, "Door Locks and Door Retention Components" potentially resulting in the doors opening in the event of a crash. If the doors open in the event of a crash, there is an increased risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will replace the door striker bolts, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V548000: Electrical System:Alternator/Generator/Regulator](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Edge&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** The power supply cables at the starter and the alternator may not have been properly secured, possibly resulting in an electrical arc. An electrical arc can increase the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the alternator and starter motor power supply cables to verify that they are properly secured, correcting them as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V469000: Service Brakes](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Edge&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** A ruptured front brake hose will cause a brake fluid leak, lengthening the distance needed to stop the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will replace the front brake hoses with the new hoses that have a revised braid material, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V413000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission:Gear Position Indication (Prndl)](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Edge&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** Either scenario increases the risk of a crash or injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the under hood shift bushing and add a protective cap over the shift cable bushing, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V544000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Foundation Components:Hoses, Lines/Piping, And Fittings](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Edge&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** A brake fluid leak can extend the distance required to stop the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the brake jounce hoses and adjust brake jounce hoses with no damage present, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V572000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Edge&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** A rearview camera that displays an inverted, distorted, or blank image can reduce the driver’s view behind the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the rearview camera as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The vehicle presents a mixed reliability picture for used car buyers. While it offers a comfortable ride, modern features, and strong engine options, potential purchasers must carefully inspect for critical engine coolant issues and infotainment glitches. Verifying service history and ensuring a comprehensive pre-purchase inspection can mitigate these risks.
