# 2023 Ford Edge Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2023 Ford Edge, buyers will find a comfortable, midsize crossover that generally offers solid daily drivability. However, owner reports highlight a few recurring technical glitches, particularly regarding infotainment screens and powertrain responsiveness. While these owner-submitted grievances do not prove inherent manufacturing defects, they provide helpful areas to inspect during a used vehicle test drive.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L EcoBoost Turbocharged Inline-4

This standard 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine produces approximately 250 horsepower and 280 lb-ft of torque, providing adequate power for daily commuting. It is paired with an all-wheel-drive system and generally runs reliably, though some owners have reported hesitation or acceleration lag.

### 2.7L EcoBoost Twin-Turbocharged V6

Available exclusively on the sporty ST trim, this 2.7-liter twin-turbo V6 generates 335 horsepower and 380 lb-ft of torque for a much punchier driving dynamic. It utilizes a standard all-wheel-drive layout and offers robust performance for buyers seeking extra passing power.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

The standard 8-speed automatic transmission handles power delivery for the 2.0L engine across most trims, focusing on smooth operation and efficiency. While generally compliant, a small portion of owners have reported shuddering and erratic shifting behavior.

**Unexpected Shuddering and Downshifting**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may experience sudden vehicle shuddering at highway speeds or unexpected harsh downshifting without warning lights.
- **Solution:** Have a certified technician inspect the transmission control module and fluid condition; software updates or hardware overhaul may be required.

### 7-Speed Performance Automatic Transmission

Matched specifically to the high-output 2.7L V6 in the ST trim, this 7-speed automatic provides quicker shift response times to complement the vehicle's sport-tuned suspension and engine.

## Electrical

### Infotainment Display Screen

The interior technology relies heavily on central touchscreen displays for vehicle controls and safety camera feeds, which can occasionally experience software or electrical glitches.

**Screen Blackout and Distortion**
- **Symptoms:** The central infotainment screen or backup camera display may randomly go completely black, flicker, or show distorted images.
- **Solution:** Restart the vehicle or have the dealer check for software updates, battery health issues, or loose display wiring harnesses.

## Interior

### Hands-Free Power Liftgate

Convenience features like the hands-free liftgate add everyday utility, though sensor sensitivity can occasionally cause unexpected operation.

**Erratic Liftgate Activation**
- **Symptoms:** The power liftgate may spontaneously open or close while loading cargo or when a person leans near the rear bumper.
- **Solution:** Inspect the rear bumper sensors for debris, moisture, or misalignment, and consult a dealer for potential module reprogramming.

## Recall Check

- **Issue:** The child seat tether wire welds on the second-row left-side seat back frame may be insufficient, which can reduce the seat's ability to properly restrain a child during a crash. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 225, "Child Restraint Anchorage Systems." A child that is not properly.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the left-side second-row seat back frame, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V596000: Exterior Lighting:Headlights](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Edge&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 108, "Lamps, Reflective Devices, and Associated Equipment." A headlight that is too bright or aimed incorrectly may cause a glare to other road users, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the right headlight assembly as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V315000: Back Over Prevention:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Edge&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** A software error may cause the rearview camera image to delay, freeze, or not display when the vehicle is in reverse. A frozen or missing rearview camera image can reduce the driver's view behind the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 26V124000: Back Over Prevention:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Edge&modelYear=2023)**

- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2023 Ford Edge is a capable midsize crossover with solid engine options and comfortable road manners. Prospective buyers should thoroughly check the infotainment screen functionality, verify smooth transmission operation during a test drive, and ensure all power liftgate sensors respond predictably before finalizing a purchase.
