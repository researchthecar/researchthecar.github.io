# 2019 Ford Edge Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2019 Ford Edge is a midsize crossover offering spacious seating and advanced technology, but used car buyers should approach with caution due to several prominent mechanical concerns. Owner feedback and technical data reveal notable trouble spots centered around drivetrain vibration and engine cooling systems. While it provides a comfortable ride and modern features, thorough pre-purchase inspections are strongly recommended to avoid costly repairs.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L EcoBoost Turbocharged I4

A 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine producing around 250 horsepower and 275 lb-ft of torque, serving as the standard power plant for most trims. It offers a balanced mix of performance and efficiency, though buyers should monitor for cooling system integrity.

### 2.7L EcoBoost Twin-Turbocharged V6

A high-output 2.7-liter twin-turbo V6 exclusive to the performance-oriented ST trim, generating 335 horsepower and 380 lb-ft of torque. While powerful, this engine option is associated with significant owner complaints regarding coolant leaks and potential internal gasket or block issues.

**Coolant Leak and Intrusion**
- **Symptoms:** Low coolant levels, white smoke from the exhaust, rough starting, and illuminated warning lights.
- **Solution:** Requires professional diagnosis; severe coolant intrusion into the cylinders often necessitates extensive engine repair or complete engine replacement.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

An 8-speed automatic transmission paired with standard models, designed to optimize fuel economy and smooth power delivery. However, it is a frequent source of owner grievances regarding rough shifting and premature torque converter wear.

**Transmission Shudder and Torque Converter Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Noticeable shuddering or chattering during acceleration and deceleration, hesitant shifting, inconsistent RPMs, and potential metal debris in the transmission fluid.
- **Solution:** Typically requires replacing both the torque converter and damaged transmission components, which can involve substantial out-of-pocket expenses.

## Suspension

### Wheels and Lug Nuts

Factory wheel hardware is prone to aesthetic and functional degradation, creating roadside maintenance hurdles.

**Swollen Lug Nuts**
- **Symptoms:** Factory lug nuts become deformed or swollen over time, preventing standard tire irons or sockets from fitting properly during flat tire changes.
- **Solution:** Replace the factory two-piece lug nuts with durable solid aftermarket alternatives.

## Electrical

### Infotainment and Backup Camera

In-cabin electronics and driver-assistance displays can exhibit intermittent glitches and display failures.

**Backup Camera and Display Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Infotainment display freezing, unresponsive touch controls, or the backup camera showing a blank black screen or distorted image.
- **Solution:** May require software updates, camera module replacement, or wiring harness repair.

## Exterior

### Glass and Liftgate

Exterior glass components, particularly rear hatches and sunroofs, carry rare reports of spontaneous breakage.

**Spontaneous Rear Glass Shattering**
- **Symptoms:** Rear liftgate glass or sunroof shattering unexpectedly without external impact or obvious vandalism.
- **Solution:** Replace the shattered glass panel and inspect the frame alignment.

## Safety

### Seat Belts

Cabin occupant restraint systems have received reports of mechanical binding and locking failures.

**Seat Belt Retractor and Locking Defect**
- **Symptoms:** Seat belts becoming stuck in retracted positions, failing to adjust, or remaining permanently locked.
- **Solution:** Inspect and replace faulty seat belt assembly mechanisms.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V550000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Edge&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** Missing or loose bolts on the start stop accumulator endcap could result in a transmission fluid leak and may progress to a loss of transmission function. A transmission fluid leak in the presence of an ignition source may increase the risk of fire.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will replace the start/stop accumulator, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V766000: Seat Belts:Front:Webbing](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Edge&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** The driver-side seat belt pretensioner anchor may have been improperly crimped, possibly resulting in the seat belt webbing detaching from the anchor in the event of a crash. In the event of a crash, seat belt webbing that detaches from the anchor will not properly restrain the driver, increasing their risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Ford has notified owners, and dealers will inspect and, if necessary, replace the driver's side pretensioner assembly, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V442000: Back Over Prevention:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Edge&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** A software error may cause the rearview camera to display a blank image, or the image may remain on the display after the backing event has ended. Both of these situations can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the rearview camera software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2019 Ford Edge presents a mixed proposition for used car shoppers. While it offers strong performance and ample cabin space, prospective buyers must exercise caution regarding potential powertrain durability issues, specifically transmission shudder and cooling system concerns. Prioritize vehicles with documented service histories, perform a rigorous pre-purchase inspection focusing on the transmission and engine coolant levels, and consider extended warranty coverage for added peace of mind.
