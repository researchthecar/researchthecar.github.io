# 2022 Ford Edge Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2022 Ford Edge offers a comfortable midsize crossover ride with generous cabin space and modern technology features. However, prospective buyers should review recurring owner complaints concerning powertrain operation, electrical accessories, and select body components. Being aware of these potential trouble spots helps shoppers inspect specific systems closely before purchasing a used model.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L EcoBoost Turbocharged I4

A 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine producing approximately 250 horsepower and 275 lb-ft of torque, serving as the standard powerplant for most Edge trims. It provides capable acceleration and balanced daily efficiency, though buyers should watch for associated powertrain shudder symptoms.

### 2.7L EcoBoost Twin-Turbo V6

A performance-focused 2.7-liter twin-turbocharged V6 generating 335 horsepower and 380 lb-ft of torque, standard on the ST trim. This engine delivers robust acceleration paired with standard all-wheel drive, though routine maintenance is crucial for longevity.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

An 8-speed automatic transmission paired with the standard 2.0L engine. While it provides smooth gear ratios for highway cruising, some owners have reported shifting hesitations and shuddering over time.

**Transmission Shudder and Hesitation**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers experience noticeable shaking, vibration, or shuddering during acceleration, particularly from lower speeds, along with delayed throttle response or harsh gear shifts.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified mechanic inspect the transmission control module, fluid condition, and torque converter operation for signs of internal wear or software calibration updates.

### 7-Speed / 8-Speed Performance Automatic Transmission

A performance-tuned automatic transmission paired exclusively with the 2.7L V6 engine on the ST trim to handle higher torque output. It generally shifts promptly, but buyers should test-drive thoroughly to ensure smooth engagement.

## Electrical

### Infotainment and Wireless Charger

Electrical accessories, screens, and charging pads generally function well, though specific comfort features like the wireless charging pad can run excessively warm.

**Wireless Phone Charger Overheating**
- **Symptoms:** Mobile devices placed on the built-in wireless charging pad become excessively hot during short drives, sometimes causing the device to stop charging.
- **Solution:** Avoid using the built-in charging pad if your device overheats rapidly, or use a traditional wired connection for charging.

## Safety

### Seat Belts

Safety systems are largely robust, but individual components like seat belt retractors warrant a physical check during a used car inspection.

**Front Passenger Seat Belt Retraction Issue**
- **Symptoms:** The front passenger seat belt retracts tightly and fails to loosen, restricting movement for the occupant.
- **Solution:** Inspect the seat belt retractor mechanism and have the assembly replaced by a dealer if locking or retraction failure persists.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V151000: Back Over Prevention:Display Function](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Edge&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** A software error may cause the rearview camera to display a blank or distorted image when the vehicle is in reverse. A rearview camera that displays a blank or distorted image can reduce the driver's view of what is behind the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 24V635000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Engine:Hard Parts Internal/Mechanical](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Edge&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** A loss of drive power increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will perform an engine cycle test and replace the engine as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V160000: Back Over Prevention:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Edge&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** A software error may cause the rearview camera to display a blank or distorted image when the vehicle is in reverse. A rearview camera that displays a blank or distorted image can reduce the driver's view of what is behind the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the rearview camera software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V315000: Back Over Prevention:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Edge&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** A software error may cause the rearview camera image to delay, freeze, or not display when the vehicle is in reverse. A frozen or missing rearview camera image can reduce the driver's view behind the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 26V124000: Back Over Prevention:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Edge&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** A rearview camera that fails to display an image can reduce the driver’s view behind the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2022 Ford Edge is a practical and comfortable midsize SUV, but prospective buyers should perform a thorough test drive to check for transmission shudder, hesitation, or shifting irregularities. Inspecting electrical accessories, backup camera functions, and seat belt mechanisms during a pre-purchase inspection will help ensure a reliable ownership experience.
