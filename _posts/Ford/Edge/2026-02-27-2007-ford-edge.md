# 2007 Ford Edge

### Overview
The 2007 Ford Edge is a mid-size crossover SUV that offered a comfortable ride and practical interior for its time. It was primarily equipped with a 3.5L V6 engine paired with a 6-speed automatic transmission. While generally considered a decent option in its segment, like any vehicle, it has specific areas where owners have reported issues. This analysis focuses on common mechanical and electrical problems, excluding routine maintenance, to provide a clearer picture of its long-term reliability.

---


## Engine

### 3.5L V6
**Intake Manifold Gasket Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Coolant leaks, engine misfires, rough idle, potential overheating.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the intake manifold gasket.
- **Cost:** $400 - $700

**Variable Cam Timing (VCT) System Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Rattling noise on startup, check engine light, poor performance.
- **Solution:** Replacement of VCT solenoids or phasers.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1000


## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic (6F50)
**Harsh Shifting / Delayed Engagement**
- **Symptoms:** Jerky gear changes, difficulty engaging gears, transmission warning light.
- **Solution:** Software update, transmission fluid flush and fill, or internal transmission repair.
- **Cost:** $300 - $1500+


## Suspension

### Standard Chassis
**Worn Control Arm Bushings**
- **Symptoms:** Clunking noises over bumps, poor handling, uneven tire wear.
- **Solution:** Replacement of control arm bushings or the entire control arm assembly.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600


## Electrical

### General Electrical System
**Faulty Window Regulators**
- **Symptoms:** Power windows not operating, slow window movement, grinding noises.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the window regulator motor and mechanism.
- **Cost:** $200 - $400

**Instrument Cluster Failures**
- **Symptoms:** Gauges not working, dim or dead display, intermittent power loss to cluster.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of the instrument cluster.
- **Cost:** $400 - $800

### Summary Verdict
The 2007 Ford Edge, powered by its 3.5L V6 engine and 6-speed automatic transmission, presents a mixed reliability profile. Common concerns revolve around the engine's intake manifold gaskets and the VCT system, as well as potential transmission issues leading to harsh shifting. Electrical components like window regulators and the instrument cluster have also been noted as potential failure points. While the exterior and interior generally hold up well, prospective buyers should be aware of these mechanical and electrical vulnerabilities. Regular maintenance and prompt attention to early symptoms are crucial for mitigating repair costs and ensuring longevity.
