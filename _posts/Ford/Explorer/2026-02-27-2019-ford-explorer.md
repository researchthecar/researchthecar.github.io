# 2019 Ford Explorer

### Overview
The 2019 Ford Explorer, a popular mid-size SUV, generally offers a solid ownership experience with a focus on family utility and comfortable cruising. It was available with several powertrain options, catering to different driving needs and preferences. While most owners report satisfaction, like any vehicle, some common areas have seen more attention from repair shops. This analysis focuses on documented technical reliability concerns beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential issues across key vehicle systems for the 2019 model year.

---


## Engine

### 2.3L EcoBoost (Turbocharged I4)
**Turbocharger Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of power, whistling noises, increased oil consumption.
- **Solution:** Turbocharger replacement.
- **Cost:** $1,200 - $2,500

**Cooling System Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Overheating, coolant smell, visible coolant puddles.
- **Solution:** Repair or replace leaking hoses, water pump, or radiator.
- **Cost:** $400 - $900

### 3.5L EcoBoost (Twin-Turbocharged V6)
**Turbocharger Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Reduced acceleration, exhaust smoke, engine warning light.
- **Solution:** Turbocharger inspection and potential replacement.
- **Cost:** $1,500 - $3,000

**Intake Manifold Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Rough idle, check engine light, vacuum leaks.
- **Solution:** Replacement of intake manifold gaskets or the manifold itself.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1,000


## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic (6F50/6F55)
**Hesitation or Shuddering**
- **Symptoms:** Jerky shifts, delayed engagement, shuddering during acceleration.
- **Solution:** Transmission fluid flush and filter change, potential internal repairs or transmission replacement.
- **Cost:** $300 - $800 (fluid service), $2,000 - $4,500 (rebuild/replacement)

## Electrical

### Infotainment System
**Sync System Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Screen freezing, unresponsiveness, Bluetooth connectivity issues.
- **Solution:** Software updates, system reset, or replacement of the module.
- **Cost:** $100 - $500


### Battery Drain
**Intermittent Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Dead battery, difficulty starting, electrical components malfunctioning.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis of parasitic draw from modules or accessories, repair or replacement of faulty components.
- **Cost:** $200 - $600

### Summary Verdict
The 2019 Ford Explorer is a generally reliable SUV, particularly for those opting for the naturally aspirated V6 engine. The EcoBoost engines, while offering good performance, have a higher propensity for turbocharger and cooling system-related issues. Transmission concerns, primarily related to shifting quality and shuddering, are present but often addressable with proper maintenance. Electrical gremlins, especially with the infotainment system and occasional battery drain, are also noted. Overall, with diligent maintenance and awareness of potential EcoBoost engine and transmission nuances, the 2019 Explorer can be a dependable choice for families.
