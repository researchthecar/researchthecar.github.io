# 2019 Ford Explorer Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2019 Ford Explorer is a popular midsize SUV that offers a spacious cabin, robust powertrain choices, and plenty of features for families. However, prospective buyers should review ownership feedback carefully, as numerous drivers have reported recurring concerns regarding engine stalling, fuel pump failures, and exterior trim detachment. While many owners enjoy its comfort and utility, checking service records for completed recalls and mechanical updates is highly recommended before purchasing.

---

## Engine

### 3.5L V6 (Naturally Aspirated)

This dependable naturally aspirated 3.5-liter V6 engine produces roughly 290 horsepower and provides steady acceleration for everyday driving. It generally serves as a durable baseline choice for the Explorer lineup.

### 3.5L EcoBoost V6 (Turbocharged)

The turbocharged 3.5-liter EcoBoost V6 delivers strong performance with around 365 horsepower, making it ideal for towing and highway passing. Buyers should expect higher fuel consumption and watch for proper maintenance history.

### 2.3L EcoBoost 4-Cylinder (Turbocharged)

A turbocharged 2.3-liter four-cylinder engine generating approximately 280 horsepower, balancing reasonable fuel economy with adequate daily power. It is a common choice for buyers seeking efficiency in a midsize SUV.

### Fuel System

Supplies pressurized fuel to the engine options; complaints highlight occasional fuel pump and module failures leading to unexpected stalling.

**Fuel Pump Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers experience sudden loss of engine power, rapid deceleration while driving at highway speeds, and a failure to start without prior warning lights.
- **Solution:** Replace the failed fuel pump and associated module.

### Engine Assembly

Core engine components manage combustion and power delivery, though some owner reports note unexpected stalling incidents.

**Engine Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle unexpectedly stalls when stopping at traffic lights or idling, requiring the driver to restart the engine.
- **Solution:** Diagnose underlying electrical or fuel delivery faults and replace affected engine control components as needed.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

This 6-speed automatic transmission is paired with front-wheel or all-wheel drive configurations, delivering smooth shifts under normal conditions but occasionally developing torque converter shudder.

**Transmission Shudder and Torque Converter Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle experiences shaking, vibration, or shuddering during acceleration, accompanied at times by powertrain warning lights.
- **Solution:** Inspect the transmission for debris and replace the torque converter or rebuild the transmission assembly.

## Exterior

### Structure and Trim

Exterior body structure and trim elements define the vehicle's appearance, though specific pillar trim pieces have been prone to loosening and detaching.

**Exterior Pillar Trim Detachment**
- **Symptoms:** The A-pillar or B-pillar exterior trim pieces suddenly detach or fly off while driving at highway speeds.
- **Solution:** Secure or replace exterior trim clips and panels, often addressed via applicable manufacturer recall procedures.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V316000: Structure:Body](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Explorer&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** The retention pins could loosen and allow the roof rail covers to detach from the vehicle. A detached roof rail cover can create a road hazard, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will install push-pins and replace any damaged rail clips and roof rail covers, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V633000: Seats:Front Assembly:Recliner](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Explorer&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) numbers 202, "Head Restraints" and 207, "Seating Systems." A seat back with an improperly assembled recliner mechanism may have reduced strength and may not adequately restrain an occupant in a crash, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and Ford and Lincoln dealers will inspect the seat structure and replace them, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V031000: Structure:Body:Roof And Pillars](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Explorer&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** The A-pillar trim retention clips may not be properly engaged, allowing the trim to detach. A detached trim piece can fall off the vehicle, becoming a road hazard and increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the A-pillar trim as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V012000: Equipment:Electrical:Engine Block Heater](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Explorer&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** The engine block heater may crack and develop a coolant leak, causing it to short circuit when the block heater is plugged in. An electrical short circuit can increase the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the block heater, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V101000: Suspension:Rear](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Explorer&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** A rear toe link fracture can result in a loss of steering control, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the toe links, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V448000: Structure:Body](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Explorer&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** A detached roof rail cover can create a road hazard, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the roof rail covers and as necessary, repair or replace the push-pins, rail clips, or roof rail covers, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2019 Ford Explorer is a capable and versatile family SUV, but prospective buyers should perform a thorough inspection. Pay close attention to transmission shift smoothness to rule out torque converter issues, verify that fuel system components operate correctly, and check that all exterior pillar trim recalls have been properly completed by a dealer.
