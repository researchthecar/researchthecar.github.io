# 2016 Ford Explorer Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used Ford Explorer, it is important to review owner-reported complaints and known mechanical tendencies. While this generation of the Explorer offers spacious seating and versatile capability, certain model years accumulate significant feedback around steering system concerns, cooling system vulnerabilities, and trim durability. Buyers should thoroughly evaluate service records and perform a comprehensive pre-purchase inspection.

---

## Engine

### 2.3L EcoBoost Turbocharged I4

A turbocharged 2.3-liter four-cylinder engine producing around 280 horsepower and 310 lb-ft of torque, offering an efficient balance of everyday performance.

**Coolant Leaks and Overheating**
- **Symptoms:** Low coolant levels, sweet odors, and engine temperature warning lights illuminating on the dashboard.
- **Solution:** Inspect water pump, hoses, and radiator connections; replace leaking components immediately to prevent engine damage.

**Engine Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** Unexpected engine shutdown while driving or idling, often accompanied by warning lights.
- **Solution:** Diagnose fault codes, inspect fuel delivery and ignition systems, and replace faulty sensors or fuel system components.

### 3.5L V6 (Naturally Aspirated)

A naturally aspirated 3.5-liter V6 engine generating approximately 290 horsepower and 255 lb-ft of torque, providing reliable baseline power for daily driving.

**Water Pump Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Coolant leaking into engine oil or onto the ground, unusual noises from the front of the engine, and overheating warnings.
- **Solution:** Replace the internal water pump and associated timing components before catastrophic engine failure occurs.

### 3.5L EcoBoost Twin-Turbo V6

A high-output twin-turbocharged 3.5-liter V6 engine delivering around 365 horsepower and 350 lb-ft of torque, primarily found in Sport and Platinum trims with all-wheel drive.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed SelectShift Automatic

A conventional 6-speed automatic transmission paired across front-wheel and all-wheel drive configurations, requiring routine fluid changes for longevity.

**Torque Converter Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Rough idling, hesitation during acceleration, and shuddering or slipping while shifting gears.
- **Solution:** Inspect transmission operation, flush fluid if appropriate, or rebuild/replace the failing torque converter and transmission unit.

## Suspension

### Rear Suspension

Independent rear suspension designed to balance ride comfort and handling capability, though prone to wear in critical linkage joints.

**Rear Toe Link and Suspension Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Clunking noises over bumps, uneven tire wear, and sudden loss of vehicle stability or control while driving.
- **Solution:** Inspect rear suspension links and control arms for corrosion or fracture; replace worn suspension components promptly.

## Steering

### Electronic Power Assist Steering

The electronic power steering system provides speed-sensitive steering assist but has generated notable owner reports regarding sudden assist loss.

**Steering Rack and Assist Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Sudden loss of power steering, stiff steering wheel, and warning messages such as 'Steering Loss, Stop Safely' or 'Service Steering Now'.
- **Solution:** Inspect electronic steering gear assembly and wiring harnesses; replace the steering rack assembly if internal motor or sensor failure occurs.

## Exterior

### Body Trim and Windshield Appliques

Exterior body trim and glass seals that require periodic inspection to prevent loose weatherstripping or detached pillar covers.

**A-Pillar Trim Detachment**
- **Symptoms:** Windshield trim pieces vibrating, coming loose, or flying off completely while driving at highway speeds.
- **Solution:** Replace loose exterior A-pillar trim clips and retainers to secure exterior moldings properly.

## Safety

### Air Bag and Seat Belts

Occupant safety systems including front and side airbags designed to protect passengers in collisions.

**Air Bag Warning Light and Deployment Concerns**
- **Symptoms:** Illuminated airbag warning indicators on the instrument cluster or failure of supplemental restraint systems to deploy during impacts.
- **Solution:** Scan diagnostic trouble codes for the airbag control module and inspect seat belt pretensioners and wiring connections.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V435000: Suspension:Rear](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Explorer&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** A fractured rear toe link will cause a sudden change in vehicle handling and increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will replace the rear suspension toe links, and inspect both rear toe link ball joints, replacing the rear wheel knuckle(s), if necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17E048000: Wheels:Hub](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Explorer&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** A loss of the wheel or wheel end can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 16V475000: Seats:Front Assembly:Recliner](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Explorer&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** The seat back frame may have insufficient welds which may not meet the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) numbers 202, "Head Restraints" and 207, "Seating Systems." The seat back may not adequately restrain the occupant during a crash, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will inspect and, if necessary, replace the seat back frame, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V767000: Seats](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Explorer&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** Loose power seat adjuster pivot bolts may cause the front seat cushions to detach and not properly restrain the seat occupant in the event of a crash. In the event of a crash, a seat occupant that is not properly restrained has an increased risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the tightness of the power seat track upper pivot link bolt, either applying threadlocker or replacing the lift link and hardware, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 15V605000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Storage:Tank Assembly:Mounting](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Explorer&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** During vehicle production, the fuel tank attachment bolts may not have been properly tightened. As a result, the fuel tank straps may fracture and the fuel tank could separate from the vehicle.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will tighten the fuel tank attachment bolts, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V183000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Engine](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Explorer&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** Overheating of the engine block increase the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will replace the engine block heater, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 15V464000: Parking Brake](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Explorer&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** The affected vehicles have a parking brake that may not fully engage when applied. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 135, "Light Vehicle Brake Systems." - **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will test the operation of the parking brake system, and if necessary, replace the parking brake control assembly, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V316000: Structure:Body](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Explorer&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** The retention pins could loosen and allow the roof rail covers to detach from the vehicle. A detached roof rail cover can create a road hazard, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will install push-pins and replace any damaged rail clips and roof rail covers, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

Purchasing a used Ford Explorer requires careful attention to potential mechanical trouble spots, notably electronic steering assist failures, cooling system integrity, and suspension wear. Buyers should verify that all open safety campaigns have been addressed and secure a professional pre-purchase inspection before finalizing a deal.
