# 2016 Ford Explorer

### Overview
The 2016 Ford Explorer is a mid-size SUV that offers a comfortable ride and a decent amount of space for families. It came with a choice of three engines: a 2.3L EcoBoost, a 3.5L Ti-VCT, and a 3.5L EcoBoost. All engines were paired with a 6-speed automatic transmission. While generally considered a reliable vehicle, some common issues have been reported by owners, particularly concerning the powertrain and certain electrical components. Regular maintenance is key to ensuring the longevity of this model.

---


## Engine

### 2.3L EcoBoost
**Turbocharger Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of power, whistling noises from the engine bay, increased oil consumption.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the turbocharger assembly.
- **Cost:** $1,200 - $2,500

**Coolant Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Overheating, visible coolant puddles under the vehicle, sweet smell from the engine.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of leaking hoses, gaskets, or the water pump.
- **Cost:** $300 - $800


### 3.5L Ti-VCT
**Timing Chain Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Rattling noise from the front of the engine, especially on startup; check engine light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the timing chain, tensioners, and guides.
- **Cost:** $1,500 - $3,000

**Intake Manifold Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Rough idle, poor fuel economy, check engine light with lean codes.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the intake manifold gasket or the manifold itself.
- **Cost:** $400 - $900


### 3.5L EcoBoost
**Carbon Buildup on Intake Valves**
- **Symptoms:** Misfires, rough idle, reduced engine performance, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Direct injection cleaning service or manual cleaning of valves.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1,200

**Turbocharger Coolant Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Low coolant level, overheating, visible leaks around turbochargers.
- **Solution:** Replacement of leaking turbocharger coolant lines or seals.
- **Cost:** $600 - $1,500


## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic (6F50/6F55)
**Harsh Shifting / Slipping**
- **Symptoms:** Jerky gear changes, delayed engagement, slipping between gears.
- **Solution:** Transmission fluid flush and filter replacement, or transmission rebuild/replacement.
- **Cost:** $500 - $3,500

**Transmission Overheating**
- **Symptoms:** Transmission fluid smells burnt, shifting problems, warning lights.
- **Solution:** Check and repair cooling system, flush transmission fluid, or transmission cooler replacement.
- **Cost:** $400 - $1,000

## Electrical

### Infotainment System (SYNC)
**Screen Freezing/Unresponsive**
- **Symptoms:** Touchscreen becomes unresponsive, system freezes or reboots randomly.
- **Solution:** Software update, hard reset, or replacement of the infotainment module.
- **Cost:** $200 - $1,000


### Battery Drain
**Intermittent Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Dead battery after sitting for a short period, electrical components malfunctioning.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis to find parasitic draw, repair or replace faulty module/component.
- **Cost:** $300 - $800

## Interior

### Climate Control System
**Blend Door Actuator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Inability to control temperature or direct airflow to specific vents.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the blend door actuator(s).
- **Cost:** $250 - $600


### Seat Heaters
**Malfunctioning Seat Heaters**
- **Symptoms:** One or both seat heaters not working, or working intermittently.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and replacement of heating elements or control modules.
- **Cost:** $300 - $700

### Summary Verdict
The 2016 Ford Explorer presents a mixed reliability profile. While the chassis and exterior components are generally robust, owners have reported issues primarily with the powertrain, specifically concerning the turbochargers on EcoBoost engines and potential timing chain problems on the 3.5L Ti-VCT. Transmission issues like harsh shifting are also noted. Electrical gremlins, such as infotainment system glitches and battery drain, can be frustrating. Interior components like climate control actuators and seat heaters have also seen some failures. Addressing these potential issues proactively through diligent maintenance and prompt repairs can help mitigate long-term problems and ensure a more reliable ownership experience.
