# 2017 Ford Explorer Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2017 Ford Explorer, buyers will find a spacious and versatile midsize SUV that remains popular on the secondhand market. However, prospective owners should carefully research specific mechanical and structural problem areas reported by previous drivers. While it offers a comfortable ride and strong utility, keeping an eye out for engine cooling issues, steering concerns, and exterior trim durability will help ensure a more confident purchase.

---

## Engine

### 3.5L V6 (Naturally Aspirated)

The standard 3.5-liter naturally aspirated V6 engine produces around 290 horsepower and 255 lb-ft of torque, offering dependable everyday power for daily driving and family hauling.

**Coolant Leaks and Water Pump Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers note coolant loss, puddles under the vehicle, and illuminated temperature warning lights indicating potential water pump failure.
- **Solution:** Replace the failing water pump and affected cooling system components promptly to prevent severe engine damage.

### 3.5L EcoBoost V6

The turbocharged 3.5-liter EcoBoost V6 delivers high-performance output, generating roughly 365 horsepower for brisk acceleration and enhanced towing capabilities.

**Coolant Intrusion**
- **Symptoms:** Rough starting, warning lights, and coolant loss pointing toward internal cylinder coolant intrusion.
- **Solution:** Professional diagnostic testing is required; severe cases may necessitate extensive engine repairs or replacement.

### 2.3L EcoBoost 4-Cylinder

The 2.3-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine provides an efficient alternative, pushing approximately 280 horsepower while balancing fuel economy for daily commuting.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

Equipped with a standard 6-speed automatic transmission paired across various trims and drivetrains, offering smooth shifting and reliable power delivery when properly maintained.

## Steering

### Steering Rack

The steering system relies on an electronic power-assist setup designed to give balanced feedback at various speeds, though component wear can lead to assist failures.

**Steering Rack Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Stiff steering wheel operation, unexpected resistance when turning, or warning lights related to power steering assistance.
- **Solution:** Inspect the electronic power assisted steering assembly and replace the steering rack if internal component failure is confirmed.

## Exterior

### Exterior Trim and Structure

Exterior body finishes and trim elements give the vehicle its rugged aesthetic, but specific exterior pillar appliqués are prone to loosening over time.

**Detaching Exterior Trim**
- **Symptoms:** Loose A-pillar and B-pillar exterior trim pieces vibrating or detaching entirely while driving at highway speeds.
- **Solution:** Secure or replace pillar trim fasteners and clips once replacement components become accessible.

## Safety

### Seat Belt

Safety restraint systems are critical for occupant protection, though rear seat belts occasionally experience mechanical binding or retraction difficulties.

**Seat Belt Latch and Retractor Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Seat belts becoming stuck in fully extended positions, failing to retract smoothly, or intermittent warning lights regarding buckling.
- **Solution:** Inspect seat belt mechanisms and replace faulty retractors or buckles to ensure proper passenger restraint.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V651000: Seats](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Explorer&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** Occupants my contact the sharp edges when reaching between the power front seat and center console, increasing their risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will install protective tape on the exposed inside edge and tab of the power seat frames, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V435000: Suspension:Rear](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Explorer&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** A fractured rear toe link will cause a sudden change in vehicle handling and increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will replace the rear suspension toe links, and inspect both rear toe link ball joints, replacing the rear wheel knuckle(s), if necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17E048000: Wheels:Hub](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Explorer&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** A loss of the wheel or wheel end can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 17V530000: Steering](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Explorer&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** If the heat shield detaches, the steering gear may overheat, increasing the steering effort required by the driver, especially at lower speeds, and increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 17V332000: Seats:Front Assembly:Recliner](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Explorer&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** In the event of a crash, the seat back frame may not restrain the occupant due to having inadequate welds. As such, these vehicles may not meet the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) numbers 202a, "Head Restraints" and 207, "Seating Systems." If the occupant is not adequately restrained in the event of a crash, they have an.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will inspect and, if necessary, replace the seat back frame, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V925000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Engine:Gasoline:Turbo/Supercharger](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Explorer&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** An oil leak, in the presence of an ignition source, increases the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will inspect, and if necessary, replace the turbocharger oil supply tubes, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V316000: Structure:Body](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Explorer&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** The retention pins could loosen and allow the roof rail covers to detach from the vehicle. A detached roof rail cover can create a road hazard, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will install push-pins and replace any damaged rail clips and roof rail covers, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V537000: Suspension:Rear](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Explorer&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** A rear toe-link fracture can result in a loss of steering control, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace as necessary, the cross-axis ball joint (CABJ) knuckle, and replace the rear suspension toe links, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2017 Ford Explorer remains an appealing choice for families seeking space and capability, but used-car buyers should perform a thorough mechanical inspection before purchasing. Pay close attention to cooling system health, check for any signs of internal engine leaks, and verify that all safety recalls and exterior trim pieces have been properly addressed by a qualified service center.
