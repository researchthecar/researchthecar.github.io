# 2024 Ford Explorer Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2024 Ford Explorer continues to be a popular choice in the midsize three-row SUV segment, offering a spacious cabin, modern technology, and capable powertrain choices. However, early owner reports submitted to NHTSA highlight a few recurring mechanical, structural, and electrical concerns that prospective buyers should keep in mind. While many modern vehicles experience minor teething issues in their first year of heavy consumer use, being aware of these reported trouble spots can help you thoroughly inspect a potential purchase.

---

## Engine

### 2.3L EcoBoost Turbocharged I4

This 2.3-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine generates approximately 300 horsepower and 310 lb-ft of torque, serving as the standard powerplant for most Explorer trims. It provides a balanced blend of everyday drivability and efficiency, though buyers should watch for routine maintenance records during a pre-purchase inspection.

### 3.0L EcoBoost Twin-Turbocharged V6

The robust 3.0-liter twin-turbo V6 pushes out around 400 horsepower and 415 lb-ft of torque, powering performance-oriented trims like the ST. It delivers strong acceleration and towing capability, though its higher output places greater thermal and mechanical demands on supporting systems.

## Transmission

### 10-Speed SelectShift Automatic

Paired with both engine options, this 10-speed automatic transmission manages power delivery across rear-wheel-drive and intelligent four-wheel-drive configurations. While generally smooth, some early owners have experienced erratic shifting characteristics that require professional diagnosis.

**Hard Shifting and Jerking**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have reported harsh jerking or abrupt shifting behavior while accelerating, passing, or slowing down in normal drive modes.
- **Solution:** Take the vehicle to a qualified service center or dealer to check for transmission control module (TCM) software updates or fluid calibration issues.

## Suspension

### Front Struts and Suspension

The front suspension system absorbs road imperfections to maintain ride comfort, but early owner feedback highlights premature noise and component wear requiring replacement.

**Premature Strut Failure and Front-End Noise**
- **Symptoms:** Owners note abnormal clunking, rattling noises at lower speeds, or visual front strut fluid leaks and component wear at very low mileage.
- **Solution:** Inspect front shocks and struts for wear or leakage, and replace faulty components under warranty or through a suspension specialist.

## Electrical

### Infotainment and Center Display

Electrical architecture controls major cabin convenience features, though occasional screen blanking and interface glitches have been documented by early drivers.

**Blank Center Display**
- **Symptoms:** The main center display screen goes completely blank, causing climate control and infotainment functions to become unresponsive.
- **Solution:** Restart the ignition cycle or visit a dealership for potential software updates and diagnostic scans of the display module.

## Exterior

### Seats and Latch Linkages

Interior mechanisms and seating adjustments provide everyday versatility, but minor mechanical jams in folding components have been reported by users.

**Third-Row Headrest Jamming**
- **Symptoms:** Third-row headrests become locked in the upright position and fail to fold down properly when configuring the cabin space.
- **Solution:** Inspect the manual release mechanism or have the headrest locking assembly serviced at a dealership.

## Safety

### Forward Collision Avoidance

Advanced driver assistance systems enhance safety but rely heavily on accurately mounted exterior sensors and cameras that can be sensitive to physical bracket stress.

**Pre-Collision Sensor Blocked Warnings**
- **Symptoms:** Frequent dashboard warnings indicating the pre-collision sensor is blocked, often traced by owners to vulnerable mounting brackets.
- **Solution:** Have a dealer or technician inspect the sensor bracket alignment and secure or replace damaged mounting hardware.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V685000: Hybrid Propulsion System](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Explorer&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** The 12-Volt battery cable may chafe against the Belt-integrated Starter Generat (BiSG) and short circuit. A short circuit increases the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will install a tie strap between the battery cable and high voltage cable, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V852000: Equipment:Other:Labels](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Explorer&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirement Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 208, "Occupant Crash Protection." Without a label that shows the proper restraint usage guidelines, the occupants may have an increased risk of injury in the event of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 25V393000: Seats:Mid/Rear Assembly](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Explorer&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** The switch for the easy-entry second-row outer seats may bind or stick, resulting in the seats unlatching, folding, or sliding unexpectedly while driving. A seat that moves unexpectedly may not properly restrain an occupant during a crash, increasing their risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the second-row seat switch, and replace the bezel around the switch as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V442000: Back Over Prevention:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Explorer&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** A software error may cause the rearview camera to display a blank image, or the image may remain on the display after the backing event has ended. Both of these situations can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the rearview camera software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V687000: Trailer Hitches](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Explorer&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** Either of these conditions can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and tighten the trailer hitch bolts, or replace them as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V012000: Equipment:Electrical:Engine Block Heater](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Explorer&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** The engine block heater may crack and develop a coolant leak, causing it to short circuit when the block heater is plugged in. An electrical short circuit can increase the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the block heater, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V123000: Back Over Prevention:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Explorer&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 111, "Rear Visibility." An incorrectly displayed rearview image can reduce the driver’s view behind the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the accessory protocol interface module (APIM) software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V159000: Seat Belts](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Explorer&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** A loose seat belt buckle may not properly restrain an occupant during a crash, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the second-row outer seat belt lower anchor bolts and replace or repair them as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

When shopping for a used 2024 Ford Explorer, it is essential to conduct a thorough pre-purchase inspection that accounts for these early owner-reported concerns. Pay close attention to the front suspension for any clunking noises, test all infotainment and driver-assistance features for proper operation, and evaluate the transmission's shifting behavior during a test drive. Verifying remaining factory warranty coverage can provide valuable peace of mind against unforeseen mechanical or electrical repairs.
