**Introduction:**

The 2007 Ford Explorer is a midsize SUV known for its spacious interior, towing capacity, and off-road capabilities. It offers two engine options: a 4.0L V6 and a 4.6L V8. Transmission options include a 5-speed automatic with the V6 engine and a 6-speed automatic with the V8 engine. While the Explorer has received praise for its comfort and versatility, it has also faced criticism for its fuel economy and reliability issues.

**Engine**

**4.0L V6**

* **Intake Manifold Runner Control Valve (IMRC) Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The IMRC valve fails to control the flow of air into the engine, leading to performance issues, reduced fuel economy, and a potential for a check engine light.
    * Solution: Replace the IMRC valve.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Spark Plug Blowout:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The spark plugs can blow out of the cylinder head, causing a rough running engine, misfires, and potential damage to the engine.
    * Solution: Replace the spark plugs and, if necessary, repair the damaged cylinder head.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**4.6L V8**

* **Camshaft Position Sensor Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The camshaft position sensor fails, causing the engine to run erratically or stall.
    * Solution: Replace the camshaft position sensor.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Intake Manifold Gasket Leak:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The intake manifold gasket fails, allowing coolant and oil to leak. This can lead to overheating, rough running, and potential engine damage.
    * Solution: Replace the intake manifold gasket.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**5-Speed Automatic (V6)**

* **Transmission Control Module (TCM) Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The TCM fails, leading to shifting issues, a loss of power, or transmission slipping.
    * Solution: Replace the TCM.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,200+
    * Recalls: None Found

**6-Speed Automatic (V8)**

* **Valve Body Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The valve body fails, causing delayed or slipping gears, rough shifting, or a loss of power.
    * Solution: Replace the valve body.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Front Suspension Strut Mount Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The front suspension strut mounts wear out, leading to clunking noises, poor handling, and potential strut damage.
    * Solution: Replace the strut mounts.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Rear Control Arm Bushing Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The rear control arm bushings wear out, causing a clunking noise, poor alignment, and decreased handling stability.
    * Solution: Replace the control arm bushings.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Alternator Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The alternator fails, leading to a loss of electrical power, dim headlights, or a dead battery.
    * Solution: Replace the alternator.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Battery Drain:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The battery drains excessively, leading to frequent battery replacements or difficulty starting the vehicle.
    * Solution: Diagnose and repair the cause of the battery drain, which can include faulty electrical components or accessories.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the cause
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Headlight Switch Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The headlight switch fails, causing unreliable headlight operation or a loss of headlights altogether.
    * Solution: Replace the headlight switch.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Dashboard Cracking:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The dashboard material cracks or warps due to heat or UV exposure.
    * Solution: Replace the dashboard.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Rear Seat Release Cable Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The cable connecting the rear seat release lever to the latch fails, hindering access to the third row of seats.
    * Solution: Replace the cable.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

Overall, the 2007 Ford Explorer has several reported issues, ranging from moderate to severe. Engine and transmission issues can be costly to repair and may affect the vehicle's reliability. While the SUV offers spaciousness and off-road capability, it is important to consider the potential maintenance costs associated with these problems.
