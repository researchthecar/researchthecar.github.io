# 2022 Ford Explorer Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2022 Ford Explorer is a popular midsize three-row SUV offering spacious seating, modern technology, and capable handling. However, prospective buyers should review recurring owner complaints regarding powertrain hesitation, electrical quirks, and interior component failures. While it delivers strong performance and family utility, paying close attention to maintenance history and prior recall completions is recommended.

---

## Engine

### 2.3L EcoBoost Turbocharged I4

The 2.3-liter turbocharged inline-four engine produces approximately 300 horsepower and 310 lb-ft of torque. It serves as the standard powerplant for base, XLT, and Limited trims, balancing daily drivability with respectable fuel efficiency.

### 3.0L EcoBoost Twin-Turbocharged V6

The 3.0-liter twin-turbo V6 generates roughly 400 horsepower and 415 lb-ft of torque, powering performance-oriented trims like the ST and Platinum. It offers brisk acceleration but demands careful verification of fuel system recall history.

**Fuel Pump and Propulsion Failures**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle experiences sudden loss of power while driving at highway speeds, shuddering, and an inability to maintain speed, often accompanied by check engine lights.
- **Solution:** Inspect fuel system components and follow dealership procedures for applicable recalls or software updates.

**Engine Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** Engine unexpectedly cuts out or fails to start reliably, leaving drivers stranded.
- **Solution:** Diagnose fuel delivery and electrical sensors to isolate root causes.

## Transmission

### 10-Speed SelectShift Automatic

The 10-speed automatic transmission is paired across all engine options to optimize fuel economy and power delivery. While generally smooth, some owners report shifting delays and rough engagement.

**Shifter Delay and Engagement Defects**
- **Symptoms:** Noticeable delays or hesitation when shifting into reverse or drive, sometimes resulting in sudden movement or clunking sounds.
- **Solution:** Check transmission control module programming and inspect linkage or mechanical mounts.

## Electrical

### Alternator and Wiring Harness

Electrical architecture supports advanced driver-assistance features and infotainment, though minor harness chafing and sensor warnings have been noted in owner reports.

**Alternator Pulley Wire Chafing**
- **Symptoms:** The alternator pulley makes physical contact with the wiring harness, rubbing through insulation and exposing bare wires.
- **Solution:** Inspect the wiring harness routing near the alternator and secure or insulate lines to prevent short circuits.

## Interior

### Panoramic Sunroof and Sunshade

Exterior equipment includes available panoramic glass roofs, which add an open feel but can experience mechanical fabric shade sagging over time.

**Sunshade Detachment and Sagging**
- **Symptoms:** The panoramic sunroof sunshade cloth detaches from its tracks, sagging into the cabin and obstructing rear visibility.
- **Solution:** Replace or repair the sunroof shade assembly and roller tracks.

## Safety

### Airbags and Restraints

Safety features encompass comprehensive airbag arrays and advanced driver aids, though random warning lights and seatbelt retraction concerns have been reported by drivers.

**Airbag Warning Indicators**
- **Symptoms:** Dashboard warning lights illuminate for the supplemental restraint system, indicating potential faults in side or passenger airbag circuits.
- **Solution:** Have a dealership or qualified technician scan diagnostic trouble codes and inspect seat wiring or sensors.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V088000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Storage:Tank Assembly:Filler Pipe And Cap](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Explorer&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** The fuel filler tube may detach during a crash. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 301, "Fuel System Integrity." A detached fuel filler tube can result in a fuel leak, increasing the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will install a fuel vapor line redundant locking clip, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V454000: Structure:Frame And Members](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Explorer&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard numbers 208, "Occupant Crash Protection" and 301, "Fuel System Integrity." Improperly heat-treated engine rails may reduce the vehicle's structural protection in a crash, increasing the risk of injury. In addition, an engine rail may detach and contact the.
- **What to do:** Dealers will perform an engine rail strength test and replace the rails as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V685000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Hoses, Lines/Piping, And Fittings](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Explorer&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** An improperly tightened fuel line connection may result in a fuel leak, increasing the risk of fire.
- **What to do:** Ford will inspect and replace the fuel line assembly, if needed, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V022000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Explorer&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** Loss of the rearview camera image can reduce the driver's rear visibility, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the image processing module (IPMB) software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V070000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Explorer&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** The transmission may contain a loose bolt which could prevent the transmission from engaging the park gear, although the gear shifter position may indicate that the vehicle has been shifted to "PARK." The inability to secure the vehicle in the park position can result in a rollaway, increasing the risk of a crash or injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and, if necessary, replace the transmissions, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V069000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission:Control Module (Tcm/Pcm/Tecm)](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Explorer&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** A park system that is damaged may not shift into "PARK," resulting in a vehicle rollaway and increasing the risk of injury or crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the park system for damage and repair as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V199000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission:Control Module (Tcm/Pcm/Tecm)](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Explorer&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** Without the electronic parking brake applied, the vehicle could rollaway if the driveshaft disconnects. Either of these scenarios can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the PCM software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V305000: Back Over Prevention:Display Function](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Explorer&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 111, "Rear Visibility." A rearview camera that does not display an image can reduce the driver's rear view, increasing the risk of a crash or injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the ICS software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2022 Ford Explorer remains an appealing choice for families seeking a capable and well-equipped SUV, but buyers should proceed with diligence. Ensure all open safety recalls—particularly those concerning fuel delivery and rearview cameras—have been completed by a certified dealer. A thorough pre-purchase inspection focusing on the powertrain, electrical harnesses, and sunroof operation will help ensure a reliable ownership experience.
