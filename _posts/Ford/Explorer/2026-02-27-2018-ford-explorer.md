# 2018 Ford Explorer

### Overview
The 2018 Ford Explorer is a popular mid-size SUV known for its versatility and family-friendly features. From a technical reliability standpoint, it generally performs well, though like any vehicle, it has specific areas that have seen more attention from owners and technicians. This analysis focuses on documented issues beyond routine maintenance, examining common problems reported across various components and powertrains. The Explorer offers a range of engine options, including a 2.3L EcoBoost, a 3.5L Ti-VCT, and a 3.5L EcoBoost, paired with a 6-speed automatic transmission. Understanding these potential issues can help owners with proactive maintenance and informed repair decisions.

---


## Engine

### 2.3L EcoBoost
**Turbocharger Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of power, whistling noises, increased oil consumption.
- **Solution:** Turbocharger replacement or repair.
- **Cost:** $1,200 - $2,500

**Coolant Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Overheating, visible coolant puddles under the vehicle, sweet smell.
- **Solution:** Repair or replace leaking hoses, gaskets, or water pump.
- **Cost:** $300 - $800


### 3.5L Ti-VCT
**Timing Chain Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Rattling noise from the front of the engine, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Timing chain and tensioner replacement.
- **Cost:** $1,500 - $3,000

**Intake Manifold Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Rough idle, check engine light, poor engine performance.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the intake manifold gasket or the manifold itself.
- **Cost:** $400 - $700


### 3.5L EcoBoost
**Turbocharger Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Significant loss of power, exhaust smoke, unusual noises from the engine bay.
- **Solution:** Replacement of turbochargers.
- **Cost:** $2,000 - $4,000

**Coolant Crossover Pipe Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Coolant loss, overheating, visible leaks near the engine's valley.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the coolant crossover pipe.
- **Cost:** $500 - $900


## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic (6F50/6F55)
**Harsh Shifting / Hesitation**
- **Symptoms:** Jerky gear changes, delayed engagement, slipping between gears.
- **Solution:** Transmission fluid flush and filter replacement, potential valve body or solenoid repair.
- **Cost:** $400 - $1,500

**Transmission Overheating**
- **Symptoms:** Burning smell, transmission warning light, sluggish performance.
- **Solution:** Transmission cooler flush or replacement, potential internal transmission repair.
- **Cost:** $600 - $2,000


## Suspension

### Standard Chassis
**Worn Control Arm Bushings**
- **Symptoms:** Clunking noises over bumps, vague steering feel, uneven tire wear.
- **Solution:** Replacement of control arm bushings or the entire control arm assembly.
- **Cost:** $400 - $800

**Strut Mount Bearing Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Popping or creaking noises when turning the steering wheel, especially at low speeds.
- **Solution:** Replacement of strut mount bearings.
- **Cost:** $200 - $400


## Electrical

### Infotainment System
**Screen Freezing/Unresponsiveness**
- **Symptoms:** Touchscreen becomes unresponsive, system freezes or reboots randomly.
- **Solution:** Software update, or replacement of the infotainment module.
- **Cost:** $300 - $1,000


### Battery Drain
_No major issues reported for this configuration._

## Exterior

### Door Latch Actuators
**Door Latch Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Doors not locking or unlocking properly, difficulty opening or closing doors.
- **Solution:** Replacement of faulty door latch actuators.
- **Cost:** $250 - $500 per door


### Sunroof Mechanism
**Sunroof Not Operating Correctly**
- **Symptoms:** Sunroof fails to open, close, or seal properly; unusual noises.
- **Solution:** Lubrication, adjustment, or replacement of sunroof motor/mechanism.
- **Cost:** $400 - $900


## Interior

### HVAC Blower Motor
**HVAC Blower Motor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** No air coming from vents, or intermittent airflow, regardless of fan speed setting.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the HVAC blower motor and resistor.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600


### Seat Heater Elements
**Seat Heater Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** One or both front seat heaters not working.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the seat heating element or related wiring.
- **Cost:** $300 - $700 per seat


--- 
### Summary Verdict
The 2018 Ford Explorer presents a generally solid reliability record, with most reported issues being manageable and not indicative of systemic failure across the model. The EcoBoost engines, while offering good performance, have shown some susceptibility to turbocharger and cooling system issues, particularly the 3.5L EcoBoost. The 6-speed automatic transmission can experience shifting problems that may require attention. Electrical gremlins, especially with the infotainment system, and some interior component failures like door latches and HVAC blowers are also noted. Owners should be aware of these potential problem areas and ensure regular maintenance to mitigate risks. Overall, the Explorer is a dependable choice for families, but proactive checks on the noted components are advisable.
