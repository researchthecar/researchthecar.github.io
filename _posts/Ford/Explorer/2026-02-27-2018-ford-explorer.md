# 2018 Ford Explorer Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2018 Ford Explorer is a popular midsize three-row SUV offering plenty of cabin space and family-friendly features, but it comes with a mixed reliability record. Prospective buyers should carefully evaluate powertrain options, as well as recurring mechanical and structural complaints reported by owners.

---

## Engine

### 3.5L Ti-VCT V6

The standard naturally aspirated 3.5-liter V6 produces around 290 horsepower and provides adequate power for daily driving. Buyers should monitor the cooling system closely for leaks.

**Coolant Leak and Intrusion**
- **Symptoms:** Coolant loss, engine overheating, and warning lights or engine stalling on the highway.
- **Solution:** Inspect water pump and cooling system components regularly; replace failing water pumps or damaged internal engine components as necessary.

### 3.5L EcoBoost V6

The twin-turbocharged 3.5-liter EcoBoost V6 generates approximately 365 horsepower, offering brisk acceleration for higher trims. It requires attentive maintenance regarding cooling and turbo components.

**Coolant Leaks and Electrical Warnings**
- **Symptoms:** Illuminated warning lights, engine running rough, and potential coolant leaks.
- **Solution:** Diagnose cooling system pressure and check electrical sensor connections.

### 2.3L EcoBoost I4

The turbocharged 2.3-liter four-cylinder engine balances efficiency with around 280 horsepower. Routine inspection of fuel and exhaust systems is recommended for used buyers.

**Engine Stalling and Fuel Odors**
- **Symptoms:** Unexpected engine stalls while driving, rough idling, or noticeable fuel and exhaust odors entering the cabin.
- **Solution:** Inspect fuel lines, exhaust seals, and throttle body components for carbon buildup or leaks.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 6-speed automatic transmission pairs with the Explorer's various engine choices to deliver standard shifting. Pre-purchase inspection should include a thorough test drive checking for slip or delayed engagement.

**Power Loss and Transmission Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Illuminated wrench warning light, sudden loss of vehicle power, or complete transmission failure leaving the driver stranded.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified mechanic inspect transmission fluid condition, valve body, and internal components; rebuild or replace transmission if mechanical failure occurs.

## Exterior

### Structure and Trim

Exterior structural elements and body panels generally hold up well, though some owners report cosmetic corrosion issues and loose trim pieces.

**Detaching Exterior Trim and Windshield Pillars**
- **Symptoms:** Exterior A-pillar trim or windshield molding detaching or flying off while driving at highway speeds.
- **Solution:** Re-secure or replace loose exterior trim clips and moldings.

**Paint Corrosion and Bubbling**
- **Symptoms:** Paint bubbling, peeling, or corrosion appearing on exterior aluminum panels such as the hood.
- **Solution:** Repaint or repair affected body panels according to professional auto body standards.

## Safety

### Seat Belts

Safety restraints are critical for family transport, making it important to verify all seat belt latches click securely during a pre-purchase test.

**Seat Belt Buckle Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Seat belts failing to click securely into the buckle, accompanied by an illuminated seat belt warning light.
- **Solution:** Inspect and replace faulty seat belt buckle assemblies.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V633000: Seats:Front Assembly:Recliner](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Explorer&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) numbers 202, "Head Restraints" and 207, "Seating Systems." A seat back with an improperly assembled recliner mechanism may have reduced strength and may not adequately restrain an occupant in a crash, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and Ford and Lincoln dealers will inspect the seat structure and replace them, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V807000: Fuel System, Gasoline](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Explorer&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** A fuel leak in the presence of an ignition source can increase the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will replace the fuel line assembly, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V316000: Structure:Body](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Explorer&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** The retention pins could loosen and allow the roof rail covers to detach from the vehicle. A detached roof rail cover can create a road hazard, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will install push-pins and replace any damaged rail clips and roof rail covers, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V031000: Structure:Body:Roof And Pillars](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Explorer&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** The A-pillar trim retention clips may not be properly engaged, allowing the trim to detach. A detached trim piece can fall off the vehicle, becoming a road hazard and increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the A-pillar trim as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V685000: Equipment:Electrical:Engine Block Heater](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Explorer&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** The engine block heater may crack and develop a coolant leak, causing it to short circuit when the block heater is plugged in. An electrical short circuit can increase the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the block heater, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V101000: Suspension:Rear](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Explorer&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** A rear toe link fracture can result in a loss of steering control, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the toe links, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V448000: Structure:Body](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Explorer&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** A detached roof rail cover can create a road hazard, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the roof rail covers and as necessary, repair or replace the push-pins, rail clips, or roof rail covers, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2018 Ford Explorer offers strong utility and a comfortable ride, but potential buyers should thoroughly check service history for cooling system maintenance and transmission health. Conducting a pre-purchase inspection with a trusted mechanic will help uncover hidden issues with trim, body corrosion, or powertrain components before buying.
