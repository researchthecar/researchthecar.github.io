# 2015 Ford Explorer

### Overview
The 2015 Ford Explorer is a mid-size SUV known for its comfortable ride and versatile interior. It offers a range of engine options, including a fuel-efficient turbocharged four-cylinder and a more powerful V6, paired with a capable automatic transmission. Reliability for the 2015 model year is generally considered average to good, with most owners reporting satisfaction. However, like any vehicle, there are specific areas that have seen more attention from mechanics and owners. This report details common issues reported for the 2015 Ford Explorer across its key systems, focusing on components that have experienced more than typical wear and tear.

---


## Engine

### 2.0L EcoBoost (Turbocharged I4)
**Turbocharger Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of power, whistling noises from the engine bay, increased oil consumption.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the turbocharger assembly.
- **Cost:** $1200 - $2500

**Cooling System Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Overheating, visible coolant leaks, sweet smell from the engine.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of hoses, water pump, or radiator.
- **Cost:** $400 - $900


### 3.5L V6
**Intake Manifold Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Rough idle, check engine light, vacuum leaks.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the intake manifold gasket or the manifold itself.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600

**Cooling System Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Overheating, visible coolant leaks, sweet smell from the engine.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of hoses, water pump, or radiator.
- **Cost:** $400 - $900


### 3.5L EcoBoost V6 (Sport Model)
**Turbocharger Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Reduced power, unusual noises, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of turbochargers and associated plumbing.
- **Cost:** $1500 - $3000

**Cooling System Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Overheating, visible coolant leaks, sweet smell from the engine.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of hoses, water pump, or radiator.
- **Cost:** $400 - $900


## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic (6F50/6F55)
**Hesitation or Shuddering**
- **Symptoms:** Jerking or shuddering during gear changes, delayed engagement.
- **Solution:** Transmission fluid flush and filter replacement, potential internal component repair or rebuild.
- **Cost:** $300 - $1500+

**Overheating**
- **Symptoms:** Transmission warning light, burning smell, slipping gears.
- **Solution:** Check and top off transmission fluid, inspect transmission cooler, potential transmission rebuild.
- **Cost:** $200 - $1200+

### Summary Verdict
The 2015 Ford Explorer presents a generally reliable package, with its most common reported issues centering around the powertrain, particularly the turbocharged engines and the automatic transmission. Turbocharger failures and cooling system leaks are notable concerns for the EcoBoost variants, while the V6 models can experience intake manifold issues. Transmission problems, such as shuddering or overheating, are also reported, often requiring fluid services or more extensive repairs. Electrical gremlins, primarily with the infotainment system and occasional battery drain, are present but not overwhelmingly widespread. Suspension, exterior, and interior components are largely free of significant, recurring problems. Regular maintenance and prompt attention to early symptoms can help mitigate many of these potential issues.
