# 2021 Ford Explorer Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2021 Ford Explorer is a popular midsize three-row SUV offering plenty of passenger space, modern technology, and multiple capable engine choices. However, prospective buyers should review recurring owner-reported complaints carefully, particularly around powertrain performance, electrical glitches, and interior trim issues.

---

## Engine

### 2.3L EcoBoost Turbocharged I4

The standard 2.3-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine generates approximately 300 horsepower and 310 lb-ft of torque, providing balanced performance and efficiency for daily driving.

**Loss of Motive Power / Acceleration Delay**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers report stepping on the accelerator and experiencing a sudden delay or loss of power before the vehicle finally responds.
- **Solution:** Consult a dealer for diagnostic updates, though some owners report recurring behavior without an immediate fix.

### 3.0L EcoBoost Twin-Turbocharged V6

The available 3.0-liter twin-turbo V6 delivers robust performance with roughly 400 horsepower, powering higher trims like the ST and Platinum with strong towing capability.

### 3.3L V6 Hybrid

The 3.3-liter hybrid V6 powertrain pairs a traditional gas engine with an electric motor to boost fuel economy while maintaining solid utility for family hauling.

## Transmission

### 10-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 10-speed automatic transmission routes power across the Explorer lineup, though some owners report hesitation and engagement delays.

**Delayed Gear Engagement and Shifting Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers experience noticeable delays when shifting between Park, Reverse, and Drive, alongside rough or hesitant gear changes.
- **Solution:** Have a certified technician inspect transmission control modules and fluid levels for software updates or mechanical wear.

## Exterior

### Exterior Lighting

Exterior lighting systems provide solid visibility, but premium headlight assemblies can be costly to service if electronic components fail.

**Headlight Low-Beam Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Low-beam headlights fail prematurely, particularly on upper trim levels like the Platinum, triggering expensive assembly replacement quotes.
- **Solution:** Check modules or replace the headlight assembly as required.
- **Cost:** $2,000 to $3,000

## Interior

### Panoramic Sunroof Shade

Interior features are generally modern and comfortable, though panoramic roof shades require careful inspection for wear.

**Sagging Sunshade**
- **Symptoms:** The interior panoramic sunroof shade or roller breaks, detaches, and sags downward to block the driver's rear-view visibility.
- **Solution:** Replace the broken roller mechanism or sunshade assembly.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V870000: Suspension:Rear](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Explorer&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** A rear suspension module with incorrect components may affect vehicle handling, ride height, and braking characteristics, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the rear suspension module, and replace any incorrect parts, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V788000: Engine](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Explorer&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** With a loose motor mount, motor vibration may cause the axle to disconnect from the engine and could result in loss of power, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will remove and replace the right hand motor mount fasteners, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V735000: Back Over Prevention:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Explorer&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** A rearview camera that does not display an image reduces the driver's rear view, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the Image Processing Module (IPMB) software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V652000: Air Bags:Side/Window:Curtain](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Explorer&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The side air bag attachment weld studs could detach during the deployment of the side air bag, resulting in an improper deployment. Improper deployment of a side air bag increases the risk of injury during a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the side air bag assemblies, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V022000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Explorer&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** Loss of the rearview camera image can reduce the driver's rear visibility, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the image processing module (IPMB) software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V069000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission:Control Module (Tcm/Pcm/Tecm)](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Explorer&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** A park system that is damaged may not shift into "PARK," resulting in a vehicle rollaway and increasing the risk of injury or crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the park system for damage and repair as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V199000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission:Control Module (Tcm/Pcm/Tecm)](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Explorer&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** Without the electronic parking brake applied, the vehicle could rollaway if the driveshaft disconnects. Either of these scenarios can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the PCM software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V305000: Back Over Prevention:Display Function](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Explorer&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 111, "Rear Visibility." A rearview camera that does not display an image can reduce the driver's rear view, increasing the risk of a crash or injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the ICS software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2021 Ford Explorer offers impressive versatility, space, and powertrain choices, but used-car buyers should thoroughly inspect the transmission shifting behavior, electrical systems, and panoramic sunroof components. A comprehensive pre-purchase inspection can help catch these potential problem areas before finalizing a purchase.
