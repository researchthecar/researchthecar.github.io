# 2021 Ford Explorer

### Overview
The 2021 Ford Explorer, a popular mid-size SUV, offers a range of powertrain options and a comfortable interior. While generally considered a solid vehicle, like any model, it has specific areas where owners have reported issues. This analysis focuses on documented technical reliability concerns beyond routine maintenance, examining common problems reported by owners and repair data. We will break down reliability by engine and transmission variants, as well as other key vehicle systems such as suspension, electrical, exterior, and interior components.

---


## Engine

### 2.3L EcoBoost I-4
**Coolant Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Visible coolant puddles under the vehicle, low coolant level warning, engine overheating.
- **Solution:** Replacement of leaking hoses, seals, or water pump.
- **Cost:** $200 - $600

**Turbocharger Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of power, whistling noises from the engine bay, increased oil consumption.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of the turbocharger assembly.
- **Cost:** $1,000 - $2,500

## Transmission

### 10-Speed Automatic (10R60)
**Hesitation and Jerking**
- **Symptoms:** Rough shifting, delayed acceleration, noticeable jerks during gear changes.
- **Solution:** Software updates for transmission control module, or internal transmission repair/rebuild.
- **Cost:** $500 - $3,000

**Overheating**
- **Symptoms:** Transmission warning light, burning smell, slipping gears.
- **Solution:** Flushing transmission fluid, replacing transmission cooler, or internal transmission repair.
- **Cost:** $300 - $1,500

### Infotainment System
**Screen Freezing/Unresponsiveness**
- **Symptoms:** Touchscreen becomes unresponsive, system freezes or restarts randomly.

### Battery Drain
**Intermittent Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle fails to start, battery dies unexpectedly.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and repair of parasitic draw from electrical components, or battery replacement.
- **Cost:** $200 - $700

### Summary Verdict
The 2021 Ford Explorer demonstrates a generally good reliability record, particularly with its V6 and hybrid powertrains. The most frequently reported issues tend to be concentrated in the 2.3L EcoBoost engine, specifically concerning coolant leaks and turbocharger performance, and the 10-speed automatic transmission, which can exhibit hesitation and overheating. Electrical gremlins, such as infotainment system unresponsiveness and intermittent battery drain, are also noted concerns. However, suspension, exterior, and interior components are largely free from widespread problems. For potential buyers, opting for the V6 or hybrid engine may offer a more robust long-term ownership experience, while being aware of and prepared for potential transmission and electrical issues is advisable.
