# 2023 Ford Explorer Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2023 Ford Explorer delivers a spacious cabin, family-friendly utility, and confident road manners, making it a popular choice in the midsize SUV segment. However, used car buyers should pay careful attention to potential trouble spots, as owner reports highlight concerns ranging from suspension noises and premature component wear to advanced driver-assistance glitches. Reviewing service records and conducting a thorough pre-purchase inspection can help ensure a prospective vehicle is in solid working order.

---

## Engine

### 2.3L EcoBoost Turbocharged I4

This base turbocharged 4-cylinder engine produces approximately 300 horsepower and 310 lb-ft of torque, providing adequate daily power and decent fuel efficiency. It is generally reliable, though routine maintenance is essential to avoid potential turbo-related or cooling issues.

### 3.0L EcoBoost Twin-Turbocharged V6

Found in higher trims like the ST and Platinum, this potent twin-turbo V6 pushes out around 400 horsepower and 415 lb-ft of torque for robust performance. While highly capable and fun to drive, buyers should watch for signs of heavy prior wear or coolant intrusion issues documented in similar EcoBoost applications.

## Transmission

### 10-Speed SelectShift Automatic

Paired with both engine options, this 10-speed automatic transmission delivers smooth highway cruising and responsive gearing across rear-wheel and all-wheel-drive configurations. Overall mechanical durability is satisfactory, though buyers should test-drive thoroughly to ensure crisp shifting without hesitation or harsh engagement.

## Suspension

### Front and Rear Suspension / Struts

Suspension components provide a comfortable ride, but early wear on shocks, struts, and subframe bushings can lead to unwanted noises and compromised handling.

**Suspension Clunking and Strut Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers notice faint popping, knocking sounds, or loud clunks when going over bumps at low speeds, sometimes causing a floaty ride feel.
- **Solution:** Inspect front struts, rear shock absorbers, and suspension bushings for leaks or wear, replacing damaged components as needed.

## Steering

### Steering and Lane-Keep Assist

The electric power steering system is generally precise, though advanced driver-assistance features can occasionally trigger false warnings or unexpected interventions.

**Lane-Keep and Hands-On Detection Faults**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle incorrectly warns that the driver's hands are off the steering wheel or unexpectedly applies braking intervention during highway driving.
- **Solution:** Check software calibrations and steering wheel sensors at a certified dealership to ensure proper ADAS functionality.

## Electrical

### Electrical System and Accessories

Modern electrical architecture supports numerous comfort and safety features, but occasional module glitches or sudden power losses have been reported by owners.

**Sudden Power Loss and No-Start Condition**
- **Symptoms:** After turning off the vehicle, electrical power completely shuts down, rendering door locks, key fobs, and the ignition unresponsive.
- **Solution:** Test the battery, main power distribution fuses, and charging system connections for loose terminals or premature failure.

## Safety

### Forward Collision Avoidance

Safety and driver-assist technologies add a strong layer of protection, but sensitive bumper- and windshield-mounted sensors can be vulnerable to minor impacts or calibration errors.

**Pre-Collision Assist Unavailable**
- **Symptoms:** Dashboard warning messages display that adaptive cruise control or pre-collision assist systems are unavailable due to sensor faults.
- **Solution:** Inspect front-mounted radar and camera sensors for physical damage or alignment issues, and recalibrate or replace faulty units.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V022000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Explorer&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** Loss of the rearview camera image can reduce the driver's rear visibility, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the image processing module (IPMB) software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V070000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Explorer&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** The transmission may contain a loose bolt which could prevent the transmission from engaging the park gear, although the gear shifter position may indicate that the vehicle has been shifted to "PARK." The inability to secure the vehicle in the park position can result in a rollaway, increasing the risk of a crash or injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and, if necessary, replace the transmissions, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V342000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Explorer&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** Loss of the rearview camera image can reduce the driver's rear visibility, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the 360-degree camera, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V597000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Engine:Gasoline:Turbo/Supercharger](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Explorer&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** An oil leak in the presence of an ignition source such as hot engine or exhaust components, can increase the risk of a fire. In addition, the loss of oil can result in an engine stall, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the turbo oil supply line assembly, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V686000: Back Over Prevention:Display Function](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Explorer&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 111, "Rear Visibility." A rearview camera that does not properly display an image can reduce the driver's rear view, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the rearview camera wire harness and repair any loose splices, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V091000: Suspension:Front:Macpherson Strut](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Explorer&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** Either of these scenarios can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the fasteners, and tighten or install them as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V852000: Equipment:Other:Labels](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Explorer&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirement Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 208, "Occupant Crash Protection." Without a label that shows the proper restraint usage guidelines, the occupants may have an increased risk of injury in the event of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 25V159000: Back Over Prevention:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Explorer&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** Loss of the rearview camera image can reduce the driver's rear visibility, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the Image Processing Module software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2023 Ford Explorer is a capable and versatile midsize SUV that offers strong engine choices and generous cabin space. While it provides a comfortable ride, buyers should thoroughly inspect the suspension for premature wear and verify that all electronic safety systems and driver aids operate without error. A comprehensive pre-purchase inspection will help catch any lingering mechanical or electrical quirks before finalizing a purchase.
