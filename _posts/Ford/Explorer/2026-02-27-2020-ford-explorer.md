# 2020 Ford Explorer

### Overview
The 2020 Ford Explorer, a mid-size SUV, offers a range of powertrain options and a redesigned platform. While generally well-received for its updated styling and improved driving dynamics, like any vehicle, it has specific areas where owners have reported concerns. This analysis focuses on documented technical reliability issues, excluding routine maintenance and normal wear and tear. We will examine common problems reported across its various engine and transmission configurations, as well as other key vehicle systems such as suspension, electrical, exterior, and interior components.

---


## Engine

### 2.3L EcoBoost I4
**Turbocharger Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of power, whistling noises, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Turbocharger replacement.
- **Cost:** $1,200 - $2,500


### 3.0L EcoBoost V6
**Coolant Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Visible coolant puddles, low coolant level warning, engine overheating.
- **Solution:** Repair or replace leaking hoses, gaskets, or water pump.
- **Cost:** $300 - $800

### Summary Verdict
The 2020 Ford Explorer demonstrates a generally solid reliability record, with most reported issues concentrated in specific powertrain components and the infotainment system. The EcoBoost engines, particularly the 2.3L, have seen some reports of turbocharger problems, while the 3.0L V6 can experience coolant leaks. The 10-speed automatic transmission has also been a source of concern for some owners, manifesting as hesitation or rough shifting. Electrical gremlins, such as battery drain and infotainment screen malfunctions, are also noted. However, the hybrid powertrain and other major systems like suspension, exterior, and interior components appear to be holding up well, with no widespread critical failures documented. Regular maintenance and addressing early signs of these specific issues are recommended for optimal long-term reliability.
