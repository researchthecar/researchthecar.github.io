# 2020 Ford Explorer Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2020 Ford Explorer introduced a redesigned platform and versatile powertrain options, making it a popular choice for families and road trippers alike. However, prospective buyers should approach this model year with caution due to a higher-than-average volume of early owner complaints and reported glitches. Common areas of concern include electronic issues, rearview camera failures, and transmission hesitation or unexpected behavior.

---

## Engine

### 2.3L EcoBoost Turbo I4

This turbocharged 2.3-liter four-cylinder engine produces approximately 300 horsepower and 310 lb-ft of torque, serving as the standard powerplant for base, XLT, and Limited trims. It offers a solid blend of everyday drivability and efficiency, though owners occasionally report cold-start hesitation and electrical warnings.

### 3.0L EcoBoost Twin-Turbo V6

The high-output 3.0-liter twin-turbo V6 pushes out around 400 horsepower and 415 lb-ft of torque, powering performance-oriented trims like the ST and Platinum. While it delivers brisk acceleration and strong towing capability, buyers should thoroughly inspect the powertrain history for signs of hard use.

## Transmission

### 10-Speed SelectShift Automatic

Paired across all engine options, the 10-speed automatic transmission aims to optimize fuel economy and power delivery. However, it is a frequent focal point for owner complaints regarding erratic shifting and software-related drivability concerns.

**Unexpected Shifting and Gear Slippage**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers experience hard shifts, sudden downshifts while cruising at highway speeds, or momentary hesitation and loss of motive power when accelerating from a stop.
- **Solution:** Software updates performed by a dealership can sometimes resolve shift logic issues; mechanical wear may require professional valve body or transmission service.

**Unintended Park Engagement**
- **Symptoms:** The transmission can unexpectedly shift into park while the vehicle is in motion, leading to severe inoperability and potential safety hazards.
- **Solution:** Requires professional diagnostic evaluation and possible replacement of faulty transmission control modules or shift linkage components.

## Electrical

### Back Over Prevention

Safety technologies including driver-assist features and rearview cameras provide vital support, though intermittent software glitches can temporarily disable the display.

**Backup Camera Display Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The infotainment screen turns completely blue or black when shifting into reverse, or the backup camera image becomes severely grainy.
- **Solution:** Inspect camera wiring connections, update software, or replace the rearview camera module if hardware failure is confirmed.

## Exterior

### Exterior Lighting

Electrical system complaints on this model span lighting modules, auxiliary sensors, and infotainment wiring, occasionally requiring component replacements.

**Headlight Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Headlamps or individual side beams stop working prematurely, reducing nighttime visibility and triggering warning alerts.
- **Solution:** Replace the affected headlamp assembly or faulty wiring harness components.

## Interior

### Seat Trim and Hardware

Cabin appointments are generally modern and comfortable, but owners have reported isolated issues with interior trim durability and mechanical seat release buttons.

**Active Headrest and Seat Backing Separation**
- **Symptoms:** Plastic backing panels on front seats detach unexpectedly, exposing internal components or side airbags, often due to clips breaking.
- **Solution:** Re-secure or replace broken mounting clips and damaged seat backing panels.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V859000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Hoses, Lines/Piping, And Fittings](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Explorer&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** A fuel leak in the presence of an ignition source increases the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will install a longer protective sleeve on the liquid fuel line, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V788000: Engine](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Explorer&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** With a loose motor mount, motor vibration may cause the axle to disconnect from the engine and could result in loss of power, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will remove and replace the right hand motor mount fasteners, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V154000: Suspension:Rear](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Explorer&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** The rear lower control arm (RLCA) bolts that attach the rear lower control arms to the wheel knuckles may not have been heat-treated, which could cause them to break. Broken RLCA bolts can cause a loss of steering control, and increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will replace both RLCA bolts, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V735000: Back Over Prevention:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Explorer&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** A rearview camera that does not display an image reduces the driver's rear view, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the Image Processing Module (IPMB) software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V549000: Seat Belts: Rear/Other:Buckle Assembly](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Explorer&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** The affected vehicles are equipped with second row center seat belt buckles that may not be properly secured to the seat. Vehicles with seat belt buckles that are not properly secured may fail to restrain an occupant during a crash, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will replace the second row center seat assembly, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V693000: Power Train:Driveline:Driveshaft](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Explorer&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** Either condition can increase the risk of a crash. The drive shafts can also deform and separate from the driveline and potentially contact the fuel tank, increasing the risk of fuel leaks and/or fire.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the drive shaft label inspected and replace it as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V575000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Explorer&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 111, "Rear Visibility." Reduced rearward visibility can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will replace the rearview camera, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V730000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Storage](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=Explorer&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** The fuel tank inlet check valve may not be fully welded to the fuel tank shell. An incomplete weld securing the inlet check valve to the fuel tank shell could result in a substantial fuel leak during a crash, increasing the risk of fire.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will replace the fuel tank shell, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2020 Ford Explorer offers impressive passenger space, strong engine choices, and modern tech, but its first-year redesign status brings notable reliability caveats. Buyers should prioritize models with complete service histories, verifying that all relevant powertrain updates and safety recalls have been addressed by a certified dealer. A thorough pre-purchase inspection focusing on transmission shift quality and electrical accessories is strongly recommended.
