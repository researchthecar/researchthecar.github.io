# 2021 Ford F-150 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2021 Ford F-150 introduces a fully redesigned generation with advanced technology, interior styling, and diverse powertrain choices, including a new hybrid option. As a first-model-year vehicle, prospective buyers should review available mechanical options and monitor early-ownership feedback carefully.

---

## Engine

### 3.3L Ti-VCT V6

The 3.3L naturally aspirated V6 produces 290 horsepower and 265 lb-ft of torque, serving as the baseline engine. It offers straightforward, dependable performance for basic work truck applications.

### 2.7L EcoBoost V6

The 2.7L twin-turbocharged V6 generates 325 horsepower and 400 lb-ft of torque. It is a popular choice balancing daily drivability with strong low-end acceleration.

### 3.5L EcoBoost V6

The 3.5L twin-turbocharged V6 puts out 400 horsepower and 500 lb-ft of torque, tailored for heavier towing and high-capacity hauling needs. It is widely utilized for its robust power delivery.

### 5.0L Ti-VCT V8

The 5.0L naturally aspirated V8 delivers 400 horsepower and 410 lb-ft of torque. It remains a traditional favorite for buyers seeking classic V8 sound and capability.

### 3.5L PowerBoost V6 Hybrid

The 3.5L PowerBoost hybrid combines twin-turbo V6 power with electric assist to generate 430 horsepower and 570 lb-ft of torque while offering mobile generator capabilities. It represents an innovative high-output option for modern truck owners.

### 3.0L Power Stroke V6 Diesel

The 3.0L turbo-diesel V6 produces 250 horsepower and 440 lb-ft of torque, prioritizing long-distance fuel efficiency and torque. Ford discontinued this option after early model years, making service history particularly important.

## Transmission

### 10-Speed SelectShift Automatic

The 10-speed automatic transmission is paired across nearly all engine configurations to optimize fuel economy and power delivery. Buyers should evaluate shifting smoothness during a test drive to ensure proper operation.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V986000: Power Train:Driveline:Driveshaft](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=F-150&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** Underbody heat and noise insulators may loosen and contact the aluminum driveshaft, which could damage the driveshaft and cause it to fracture. Additionally, unintended movement could occur while parked if the parking brake is not engaged.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and repair the driveshaft as necessary, and properly attach the underbody insulators, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V090000: Visibility:Windshield:Critical Fasteners](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=F-150&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** During manufacturing, the windshield may not have been properly bonded to the vehicle, allowing it to detach in a crash. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 212, "Windshield Mounting." In the event of a crash, if the windshield separates from the vehicle, it can increase the.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will remove and reinstall the windshield, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V805000: Air Bags:Sensor:Side Impact](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=F-150&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The front-door side impact pressure sensor modules may have been damaged during supplier assembly, which can cause the side impact air bags to not deploy properly in a crash. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 214, "Side Impact Protection." An air bag that does not deploy properly.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the driver and passenger front door side impact sensors, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V533000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=F-150&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The electronic brake booster may leak brake fluid into the electronic control unit, which could result in reduced braking performance. Reduced braking performance can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the electronic brake booster assembly, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V653000: Seat Belts:Front:Webbing](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=F-150&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** A misrouted seatbelt may not adequately restrain an occupant in a crash, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the front seatbelts and, if the webbing is misrouted, will replace the seatbelt retractor and pretensioner, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V381000: Power Train:Driveline:Driveshaft](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=F-150&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The two-piece driveshafts may have been improperly assembled, which could result in driveshaft separation. Driveshaft separation may result in a sudden loss of drive power, or unintended vehicle movement if the vehicle is in park without the parking brake applied, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the driveshaft, and replace it if necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V407000: Steering:Gear Box (Other Than Rack And Pinion)](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=F-150&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The steering gear may have internal damage, which could result in difficulty steering, or the inability to rotate the steering wheel while driving. Loss of steering control can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Vehicles will be towed in for service, and dealers will remove and replace the steering gear, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V142000: Visibility:Windshield Wiper/Washer:Motor](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=F-150&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The front windshield wiper motor may become inoperative, causing the wipers to fail. Inoperative windshield wipers can reduce visibility in certain conditions, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the front windshield wiper motor, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2021 Ford F-150 offers immense capability, versatile engine choices, and modern features, but being a redesigned model year warrants a thorough pre-purchase inspection. Inspect the powertrain, electronics, and overall mechanical condition carefully before finalizing a purchase.
