# 2017 Ford F-150

### Overview
The 2017 Ford F-150 is a highly capable and popular full-size pickup truck known for its robust construction and versatile performance. This model year benefits from Ford's extensive experience with its aluminum-alloy body and a wide range of powertrain options, including several EcoBoost engines and traditional V8s, paired with either a 6-speed or 10-speed automatic transmission. Generally, the 2017 F-150 demonstrates strong reliability, with owners reporting satisfaction with its durability and performance in various conditions. However, like any vehicle, specific components can experience issues over time. This analysis focuses on documented technical reliability concerns beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential problem areas and their associated costs for different configurations.

## Engine

### 3.5L EcoBoost V6
**Timing Chain Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Rattling noise from the front of the engine, especially on startup; check engine light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of timing chain, tensioners, and guides.
- **Cost:** $1,200 - $2,500

**Turbocharger Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of power, whistling noises from the engine bay, increased oil consumption.
- **Solution:** Replacement of affected turbocharger(s).
- **Cost:** $1,500 - $3,000

### 10-Speed Automatic
**Harsh Shifting / Jerking**
- **Symptoms:** Rough gear changes, particularly between lower gears; hesitation.
- **Solution:** Software update for transmission control module; in some cases, internal transmission repair or replacement.

## Electrical

### General Electrical System
**Infotainment System Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Screen freezing, unresponsiveness, Bluetooth connectivity issues.
- **Solution:** Software updates, system resets, or replacement of the infotainment module.
- **Cost:** $200 - $1,500

**Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle failing to start, battery dying prematurely.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis of parasitic draw, replacement of faulty modules or wiring.
- **Cost:** $300 - $800

### Summary Verdict
The 2017 Ford F-150 generally exhibits good reliability, particularly with its V8 and naturally aspirated V6 engines. The EcoBoost engines, while offering excellent performance and fuel economy, have a few documented issues, primarily related to the timing chain and turbochargers, especially on the 3.5L variant. The 10-speed automatic transmission, new for some models, can occasionally exhibit harsh shifting, often resolvable with software updates. Electrical issues like infotainment glitches and battery drain are not uncommon but are typically manageable. Overall, the F-150 is a solid choice, but potential buyers should be aware of the specific powertrain configurations and their associated reliability trends.
