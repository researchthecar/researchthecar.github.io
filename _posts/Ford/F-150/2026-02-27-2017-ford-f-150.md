# 2017 Ford F-150 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

Shopping for a used Ford F-150 means navigating a truck known for capability and broad engine choices, but buyers should also weigh a few common mechanical and body concerns. While the F-150 remains a popular full-size option on the used market, owners have frequently flagged issues ranging from stubborn door latches in cold weather to occasional driverseat or powertrain hiccups. A thorough pre-purchase inspection can help uncover whether these common trouble spots have already been addressed.

---

## Engine

### 2.7L EcoBoost V6

A twin-turbocharged V6 offering strong low-end torque and competitive fuel economy for daily driving and light towing. Reliability is generally good, though early timing components require attentive maintenance.

**Timing Chain and Phaser Noise**
- **Symptoms:** Owners report an abnormal loud rattling or ticking sound from the engine compartment upon startup, occasionally accompanied by illumination of warning lights.
- **Solution:** Inspect timing chain components, tensioners, and phasers; replace worn parts as necessary.

### 3.5L EcoBoost V6

A high-output twin-turbo V6 delivering robust horsepower and heavy-duty towing capacity. Buyers appreciate the strong performance, though turbocharged plumbing and timing components should be checked during inspections.

### 3.5L Ti-VCT V6

The base naturally aspirated V6 engine offering steady, straightforward performance for fleet and basic work truck configurations. It provides simpler mechanics than its turbocharged counterparts.

### 5.0L Ti-VCT V8

A classic naturally aspirated V8 delivering traditional power, distinct exhaust notes, and high towing limits. It is a favorite among buyers looking for traditional truck capability without turbochargers.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic

A proven multi-speed automatic paired with select engine options, offering dependable day-to-day operation. Electrical lead frame wear is a known point of failure to watch for.

**Lead Frame and Shift Faults**
- **Symptoms:** Sudden, violent downshifting while driving, unexpected harsh engagement from park to drive, or transmission slipping into lower gears causing unexpected deceleration.
- **Solution:** Diagnose transmission fault codes (such as output sensor codes) and replace the molded lead frame or faulty sensors.

### 10-Speed Automatic

A modern transmission co-developed to optimize fuel economy and towing responsiveness across wide speed ranges. Regular software updates and fluid checks are recommended for smooth longevity.

## Suspension

### Wheels and Lug Nuts

Standard suspension components hold up well to rough roads, though minor peripheral annoyances like swollen lug nuts frequently frustrate owners.

**Swollen Lug Nuts**
- **Symptoms:** Factory two-piece lug nuts swell or deform over time, making it extremely difficult or impossible to fit a standard lug wrench during a flat tire change.
- **Solution:** Replace factory two-piece lug nuts with solid, one-piece aftermarket alternatives.

## Brakes

### Hydraulic Brake System

Provides adequate stopping power for heavy loads, though routine maintenance of pads and rotors is essential.

**Brake Noise and Component Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Abnormal screeching or scraping sounds when depressing the brake pedal, or reduced stopping confidence in slippery conditions.
- **Solution:** Inspect brake pads, rotors, and hydraulic lines; replace worn friction materials or address active recalls.

## Electrical

### Infotainment and Backup Camera

Electrical accessories generally function reliably, but intermittent display glitches and backup camera feed losses are recurring complaints.

**Intermittent Backup Camera Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Rearview camera displays a blue screen, distorted image, or goes completely blank intermittently when shifting into reverse.
- **Solution:** Inspect camera wiring harness, connectors, and camera module; replace faulty hardware if necessary.

## Exterior

### Door Latches

Cabin hardware is generally robust, but freezing or failing door latches are a well-documented nuisance for owners in colder climates.

**Door Latch and Freeze Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Doors fail to close securely, become stuck inoperable, or unlatch unexpectedly while driving, particularly during freezing or wet weather conditions.
- **Solution:** Inspect and replace faulty door latch assemblies or cables as specified by service bulletins.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V894000: Engine](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=F-150&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** When the heater is plugged in, prolonged corrosion could cause a resistive short, increasing the risk of overheated or melted wiring which can increase the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will disable the block heater cable, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V278000: Engine](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=F-150&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** A damaged cable could cause a resistive short, increasing the risk of overheated or melted wiring, increasing the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will disable the engine block heater from being used. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V568000: Seat Belts:Pretensioner](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=F-150&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** If a front seat belt pretensioner deploys as the result of a crash, the sparks may ignite materials such as carpeting or insulation within the B-pillar area. A vehicle fire could result if materials ignite inside the vehicle.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will remove the B-pillar insulation material and wiring harness tape, and install heat resistant tape, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V652000: Latches/Locks/Linkages:Doors:Latch](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=F-150&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** A door that opens while driving can increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the door latch actuation cables and repair if necessary, and install water shields over the door latches, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V529000: Air Bags:Frontal:Passenger Side:Inflator Module](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=F-150&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** The air bag inflator within the passenger frontal air bag module may rupture in the event of a crash. An inflator rupture may result in metal fragments striking the vehicle occupants resulting in serious injury or death.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will replace the passenger frontal air bag module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The Ford F-150 is a capable and versatile full-size truck with a strong lineup of engines and configurations, making it a compelling choice on the used market. Buyers should pay close attention during a test drive to verify smooth transmission shifting, normal engine startup sounds, and properly functioning door latches and backup cameras. Prioritizing a vehicle with a documented service history will help mitigate potential headaches.
