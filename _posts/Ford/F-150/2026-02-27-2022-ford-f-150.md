# 2022 Ford F-150 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2022 Ford F-150 remains a highly capable and versatile full-size pickup truck, offering a wide array of engine options and modern technology. While many owners enjoy its strong towing capacity and comfortable interior, used-car buyers should review certain recurring problem areas. Owner complaints highlight potential concerns regarding electrical accessories, transmission performance, and specific mechanical components. Thorough pre-purchase inspections and verification of completed service history are recommended when shopping for a used model.

---

## Engine

### 2.7L EcoBoost V6

A twin-turbocharged V6 offering a strong balance of everyday driveability and fuel efficiency. It provides ample horsepower and torque for light-to-moderate towing and hauling duties.

### 3.5L EcoBoost V6

A high-output twin-turbocharged V6 engineered for heavy-duty towing and payload capacity. It delivers robust horsepower and substantial low-end torque for demanding workloads.

### 3.5L PowerBoost Hybrid V6

A hybrid powertrain combining a twin-turbo V6 with an electric motor to boost both power output and fuel economy. It also features an integrated onboard power generation system.

**Engine Stalling and Fuel System Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may experience engine sputtering, difficulty starting when parked on an incline with a full fuel tank, or unexpected engine stalling while operating.
- **Solution:** Have the vehicle evaluated by a qualified service center to inspect fuel system components and apply any applicable manufacturer remedies.

### 5.0L Ti-VCT V8

A traditional naturally aspirated V8 engine favored for traditional truck performance and exhaust note. It delivers strong high-RPM horsepower and reliable pulling capability.

## Transmission

### 10-Speed Automatic

A 10-speed automatic transmission utilized across most powertrain configurations to optimize fuel efficiency and power delivery. Smooth operation depends heavily on proper maintenance and software tuning.

**Harsh Shifting and Internal Component Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may notice unexpected violent downshifting at certain speeds, warning lights related to the park pawl, or internal mechanical failure associated with components like the CDF drum.
- **Solution:** Consult a transmission specialist for diagnostic testing; repairs may involve software updates, valve body service, or internal component replacement.

## Brakes

### Electronic Parking Brake

Handles hydraulic service braking and electronic parking brake engagement.

**Brake Seizure and Fault Warnings**
- **Symptoms:** Dashboard warnings for parking brake service may appear unexpectedly, or the rear brakes may engage or seize during driving or parking maneuvers.
- **Solution:** Have the parking brake actuators and electronic control modules inspected and replaced if found to be malfunctioning.

## Electrical

### Infotainment and Audio

Controls cabin entertainment, touchscreen displays, and premium audio output.

**Loud Audio System Popping and Static**
- **Symptoms:** Occupants may experience sudden, deafening loud popping noises or static from the audio speakers upon starting the vehicle.
- **Solution:** Check for software updates or take the vehicle to a dealer for audio amplifier and head unit diagnostics.

## Exterior

### Exterior Lighting

Manages exterior illumination, including headlights, daytime running lamps, and tail lights.

**Daytime Running Light and Tail Light Failure**
- **Symptoms:** LED daytime running lights may fail prematurely on one or both sides, or tail light assemblies may experience water intrusion leading to electrical failure.
- **Solution:** Inspect lighting housings for moisture and replace faulty LED assemblies or seals as necessary.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V986000: Power Train:Driveline:Driveshaft](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=F-150&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** Underbody heat and noise insulators may loosen and contact the aluminum driveshaft, which could damage the driveshaft and cause it to fracture. Additionally, unintended movement could occur while parked if the parking brake is not engaged.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and repair the driveshaft as necessary, and properly attach the underbody insulators, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V193000: Electrical System: Integrated Trailer Brake Control](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=F-150&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** Due to a software error, a towed trailer equipped with an electric or electric-over hydraulic brake system may not brake. Loss of trailer brake functionality could result in extended stopping distance, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the integrated trailer brake control module software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V253000: Steering:Column](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=F-150&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** Damaged or separated wiring may result in the steering column not moving as intended in certain crash conditions, increasing the risk of injury in a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the steering column wiring harness and repair the wiring, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V623000: Power Train:Driveline:Driveshaft](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=F-150&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** Underbody heat and noise insulators may loosen and contact the aluminum driveshaft, which could damage the driveshaft and cause it to fracture. Additionally, unintended movement could occur while parked if the parking brake is not engaged.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and repair the driveshaft, as necessary, and properly attach the underbody insulators, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V675000: Wheels:Lugs/Nuts/Bolts/Studs](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=F-150&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** Incorrectly tightened wheel lug nuts can loosen and result in wheel separation from the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will tighten or replace the wheel lug nuts, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V686000: Exterior Lighting:Lighting Control Module](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=F-150&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 108, "Lamps, Reflective Devices, and Associated Equipment." Flickering parking lights can reduce visibility, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the LED Driver module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V684000: Air Bags](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=F-150&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** The right-side instrument panel cover may have been manufactured incorrectly, which can cause the air bag to not deploy as intended. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 208, "Occupant Crash Protection." During air bag deployment, the air bag cover my break and project fragments into.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the right-side instrument panel cover, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V842000: Visibility:Windshield Wiper/Washer:Motor](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=F-150&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** The front windshield wiper motor may become inoperative, causing the wipers to fail. Inoperative windshield wipers can reduce visibility in certain conditions, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the front windshield wiper motor, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2022 Ford F-150 is a formidable and feature-rich choice in the used full-size truck market, provided buyers carefully evaluate its condition. Potential purchasers should pay close attention to transmission shift quality, parking brake operation, and electrical accessories during a test drive. Verifying that all open recalls and necessary software updates have been completed by a certified dealer will help ensure a more reliable ownership experience.
