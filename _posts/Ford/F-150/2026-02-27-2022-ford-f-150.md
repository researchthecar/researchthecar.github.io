# 2022 Ford F-150 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2022 Ford F-150 continues to be a top-tier full-size pickup truck, offering a vast array of powerful engine choices, advanced technology, and robust capability. For used-car buyers, reviewing potential owner-reported concerns regarding powertrain options and electrical systems can help guide a smart purchase. While overall modern truck complexity means paying close attention to specific configurations is wise, the F-150 remains a formidable choice on the pre-owned market.

---

## Engine

### 3.3L V6

The 3.3L V6 is a naturally aspirated engine producing around 290 horsepower and 265 lb-ft of torque, serving as the dependable base option for light-duty tasks. It provides straightforward performance with a traditional layout suited for basic utility.

### 2.7L EcoBoost V6

The 2.7L twin-turbocharged EcoBoost V6 generates roughly 325 horsepower and 400 lb-ft of torque, balancing lively acceleration with respectable efficiency. It is a popular choice for daily driving and light towing.

### 3.5L EcoBoost V6

The 3.5L twin-turbocharged EcoBoost V6 puts out about 400 horsepower and 500 lb-ft of torque, delivering strong pulling power and maximum towing capacity. It is well-regarded for heavy-duty hauling, though buyers should verify regular maintenance history.

### 5.0L V8

The classic 5.0L naturally aspirated V8 develops approximately 400 horsepower and 410 lb-ft of torque, offering traditional V8 sound and capability. It is a favored option for traditional truck enthusiasts seeking simple displacement.

### 3.5L PowerBoost Hybrid V6

The 3.5L PowerBoost Hybrid V6 combines twin-turbo V6 power with an electric motor to produce 430 horsepower and 570 lb-ft of torque, while also featuring onboard generator capabilities. It provides high output and efficiency, though complex hybrid systems warrant careful inspection.

### 3.5L High-Output EcoBoost V6

Exclusive to high-performance trims like the Raptor, this high-output twin-turbo V6 churns out 450 horsepower and 510 lb-ft of torque. It is engineered for aggressive off-road driving and high-speed maneuvers.

## Transmission

### 10-Speed Automatic Transmission

Paired across nearly the entire lineup, the 10-speed automatic transmission aims to optimize both fuel economy and power delivery. Buyers should test drive thoroughly to ensure smooth shifting across all gears.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V986000: Power Train:Driveline:Driveshaft](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=F-150&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** Underbody heat and noise insulators may loosen and contact the aluminum driveshaft, which could damage the driveshaft and cause it to fracture. Additionally, unintended movement could occur while parked if the parking brake is not engaged.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and repair the driveshaft as necessary, and properly attach the underbody insulators, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V193000: Electrical System: Integrated Trailer Brake Control](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=F-150&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** Due to a software error, a towed trailer equipped with an electric or electric-over hydraulic brake system may not brake. Loss of trailer brake functionality could result in extended stopping distance, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the integrated trailer brake control module software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V253000: Steering:Column](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=F-150&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** Damaged or separated wiring may result in the steering column not moving as intended in certain crash conditions, increasing the risk of injury in a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the steering column wiring harness and repair the wiring, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V623000: Power Train:Driveline:Driveshaft](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=F-150&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** Underbody heat and noise insulators may loosen and contact the aluminum driveshaft, which could damage the driveshaft and cause it to fracture. Additionally, unintended movement could occur while parked if the parking brake is not engaged.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and repair the driveshaft, as necessary, and properly attach the underbody insulators, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V675000: Wheels:Lugs/Nuts/Bolts/Studs](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=F-150&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** Incorrectly tightened wheel lug nuts can loosen and result in wheel separation from the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will tighten or replace the wheel lug nuts, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V686000: Exterior Lighting:Lighting Control Module](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=F-150&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 108, "Lamps, Reflective Devices, and Associated Equipment." Flickering parking lights can reduce visibility, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the LED Driver module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V684000: Air Bags](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=F-150&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** The right-side instrument panel cover may have been manufactured incorrectly, which can cause the air bag to not deploy as intended. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 208, "Occupant Crash Protection." During air bag deployment, the air bag cover my break and project fragments into.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the right-side instrument panel cover, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V842000: Visibility:Windshield Wiper/Washer:Motor](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=F-150&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** The front windshield wiper motor may become inoperative, causing the wipers to fail. Inoperative windshield wipers can reduce visibility in certain conditions, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the front windshield wiper motor, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2022 Ford F-150 offers an impressive suite of engines and configurations, making it adaptable to almost any truck buyer's needs. Prospective owners should perform a comprehensive test drive to check for smooth transmission operation and ensure all electronics function properly. With careful inspection, a used 2022 model can prove to be a capable and durable garage addition.
