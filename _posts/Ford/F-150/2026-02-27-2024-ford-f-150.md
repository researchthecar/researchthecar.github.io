# 2024 Ford F-150 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2024 Ford F-150 continues to be a top-tier full-size pickup offering a diverse range of robust engine and powertrain configurations to suit varied towing and daily driving needs. While prospective buyers appreciate its capability, modern technology, and comfort, owner feedback highlights several recurring technical challenges. Chief among these are electrical glitches involving exterior lighting and digital displays, alongside sporadic transmission and gear shifter concerns.

---

## Engine

### 2.7L EcoBoost V6

A twin-turbocharged V6 offering a strong balance of everyday power and efficiency. Known for smooth power delivery, though early production models see occasional electrical and sensor management complaints.

### 3.5L EcoBoost V6

A high-output twin-turbocharged V6 designed for substantial towing capability and strong horsepower. Generally robust, though high-stress powertrain operation yields occasional owner-reported shifting or control module alerts.

### 5.0L Ti-VCT V8

A traditional naturally aspirated V8 delivering classic American truck performance and high towing capacity. Highly regarded for mechanical durability, though isolated complaints point to auxiliary electronic and speed control integration.

### 3.5L PowerBoost Hybrid V6

A specialized hybrid powertrain combining a twin-turbo V6 with electric assist for superior fuel economy and onboard mobile power generation. Complex electrical architecture requires careful inspection of auxiliary modules.

### Electric Drive Motor (Lightning)

Dual-motor all-wheel-drive electric propulsion delivering instantaneous torque and silent operation. Highly capable, though some early units report high-voltage module or software integration bugs.

## Transmission

### 10-Speed Automatic

A 10-speed automatic transmission paired across most gas engine options to optimize fuel economy and power delivery. While generally smooth, some owners experience occasional erratic shifting behavior and electronic gear selector lockouts.

**Shifter Lock and Gear Engagement Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The gearshift module may lock into position with dashboard warning lights blinking, preventing the driver from shifting into park or starting the vehicle.
- **Solution:** Have the dealer inspect the gearshift module and update or replace faulty electronic shift control components.

**Rough or Hard Downshifting**
- **Symptoms:** Occasional hard downshifts or unexpected jerking while decelerating or traveling on inclines, which can feel harsh and potentially accelerate wear.
- **Solution:** Reprogram or update the powertrain control module with the latest manufacturer software calibration.

### Single-Speed Direct Drive

A dedicated single-speed direct drive transmission used in the F-150 Lightning electric models, offering seamless power transfer without traditional gear changes.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

Handles primary stopping power and integration with driver-assist safety systems. Electronic sensor faults can occasionally trigger limp or warning states.

**Anti-Lock Brake and Stability Control Faults**
- **Symptoms:** Multiple warning lights illuminate simultaneously (ABS, traction control, hill start assist, and brake fault) accompanied by a temporary loss of optimal braking response.
- **Solution:** Inspect wheel speed sensors, brake control modules, and wiring harnesses for intermittent faults.

## Electrical

### Infotainment and Instrument Cluster

Controls driver displays, audio, and navigation interfaces. Screen blackouts and temporary software freezes are documented owner grievances.

**Digital Dashboard and Screen Blanking**
- **Symptoms:** The digital instrument cluster or main infotainment screen may suddenly go completely black or fail to turn on during vehicle operation.
- **Solution:** Perform software resets, check battery connections, or replace the accessory protocol interface module (APIM) if internal failures persist.

## Exterior

### Exterior Lighting

Manages exterior illumination, including headlights and accent lamps. Headlight reliability is generally acceptable, though numerous owner reports cite visibility or operational complaints.

**Headlight Operation Concerns**
- **Symptoms:** Headlights may experience unexpected operational failures or create visibility issues for oncoming drivers due to alignment or intensity concerns.
- **Solution:** Inspect headlight assemblies, wiring harnesses, and control modules for proper function and secure connections.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V329000: Steering:Column](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=F-150&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** A loss of steering control increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the lower steering shaft fastener, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V486000: Visibility:Windshield](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=F-150&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** The windshield may not have been properly bonded to the vehicle, allowing it to detach during a crash. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 212, "Windshield Mounting." A windshield that detaches from a vehicle during a crash can increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will remove and reinstall the windshield, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V851000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Engine:Oil/Lubrication](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=F-150&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** A rapid oil leak can result in an engine stall, increasing the risk of a crash. In addition, an oil leak in the presence of an ignition source such as hot engine or exhaust components, can increase the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the engine cup plug alignment, and replace the plug if necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V852000: Equipment:Other:Labels](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=F-150&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirement Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 208, "Occupant Crash Protection." Without a label that shows the proper restraint usage guidelines, the occupants may have an increased risk of injury in the event of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 25V022000: Steering](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=F-150&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** A fractured steering gear can cause the steering to bind or lock, resulting in a loss of steering control and increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the steering gear assembly, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V164000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission:Fluid/Lubricant:Valves/Valve Body](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=F-150&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** Unexpected vehicle movement increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the transmission main control valve body, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V198000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Engine:Oil/Lubrication](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=F-150&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** A rapid oil leak can result in an engine stall, increasing the risk of a crash. In addition, an oil leak in the presence of an ignition source such as hot engine or exhaust components, can increase the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the engine cup plug alignment, and replace the plug if necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V315000: Back Over Prevention:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=F-150&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** A software error may cause the rearview camera image to delay, freeze, or not display when the vehicle is in reverse. A frozen or missing rearview camera image can reduce the driver's view behind the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2024 Ford F-150 remains a highly capable, versatile full-size truck with extensive powertrain options ranging from efficient EcoBoost V6s to powerful V8s and electric configurations. While its overall mechanical platform is strong, prospective buyers should thoroughly inspect the vehicle's electrical systems, digital displays, and 10-speed transmission shifting behavior during a pre-purchase test drive. Ensuring all applicable software updates and open recalls have been completed will help secure a more dependable ownership experience.
