# 2020 Ford F-150 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used full-size truck, evaluating its overall mechanical health and common trouble spots helps ensure a dependable ownership experience. While many configurations provide strong capability and performance, prospective buyers should pay close attention to powertrain behavior, electrical features, and auxiliary components. Reviewing known owner-reported issues offers valuable insight into what areas may require inspection before finalizing a purchase.

---

## Engine

### 3.3L V6

This naturally aspirated base V6 engine produces roughly 290 horsepower and 265 lb-ft of torque, providing reliable baseline performance for daily driving duties.

**Engine Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may unexpectedly stall while operating, accompanied by warning lights on the instrument cluster and requiring a restart.
- **Solution:** Diagnose fault codes, inspect electronic throttle and ignition components, and replace faulty sensors or modules as needed.

### 2.7L EcoBoost V6

A twin-turbocharged V6 offering around 325 horsepower and 400 lb-ft of torque, balancing strong low-end acceleration with everyday usability.

**Electrical Warning Lights and Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** Illuminated dashboard warning indicators coupled with unexpected engine stalling or reduced motive power.
- **Solution:** Check powertrain control module codes and inspect wiring harnesses or electrical connections for secure operation.

### 3.5L EcoBoost V6

A high-output twin-turbo V6 generating up to 375 horsepower and 470 lb-ft of torque, designed for robust towing and heavy hauling capability.

### 5.0L V8

A traditional naturally aspirated V8 delivering about 395 horsepower and 400 lb-ft of torque, popular for its classic power delivery and exhaust note.

### 3.0L Power Stroke V6 Diesel

A turbocharged diesel engine focusing on high torque delivery and optimized highway fuel efficiency.

## Transmission

### 10-Speed Automatic

This widely utilized 10-speed automatic transmission pairs with multiple engine options to optimize gear ratios, though proper software calibration and regular fluid checks are essential for smooth operation.

**Transmission Slipping and Shifting Delays**
- **Symptoms:** Unintended neutral shifts, harsh upshifts or downshifts, and slipping feelings while accelerating through highway speeds.
- **Solution:** Update transmission control module software, inspect fluid condition, or rebuild/replace internal components if mechanical wear is identified.

## Brakes

### Brake System

The braking hardware provides solid stopping power, though electronic monitoring messages should be inspected promptly if they appear.

**Brake Warning and Performance Concerns**
- **Symptoms:** Illuminated dashboard messages regarding parking brake or hydraulic system operation during drives.
- **Solution:** Inspect brake fluid levels, electronic parking brake actuators, and pad/rotor wear to ensure correct system calibration.

## Exterior

### Door Lock Actuators

Electrical accessories such as power locks and cabin convenience features generally perform well, though occasional actuator wear can occur.

**Door Lock Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Doors fail to lock or unlock via the key fob or interior power switch, frequently affecting specific doors.
- **Solution:** Replace the malfunctioning door lock actuator assembly within the affected door panel.

### Sunroof and Glass

Exterior trim and glass components are robust, but optional panoramic sunroof assemblies require periodic clearing of drain channels to prevent moisture intrusion.

**Water Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Rainwater leaking into the cabin from the sunroof assembly due to alignment or drainage blockages.
- **Solution:** Clear clogged drain tubes or adjust and replace sunroof weatherstripping seals.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V903000: Tires:Bead](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=F-150&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** The spare tire could have been damaged when it was installed on the rim. The damage can cause the tire to rapidly lose air pressure or detach from the wheel, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will replace the spare tire, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V097000: Exterior Lighting:Headlights](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=F-150&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** The Daytime Running Lamps (DRL) may remain activated instead of dimming to parking lamps when the Master Lighting Switch (MLS) is manually rotated from the "Autolamps" switch position to "Headlamps On." As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 108, "Lamps, Reflective Devices and.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will reprogram the body control module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V805000: Electrical System:12V/24V/48V Battery:Cables](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=F-150&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** The engine could also stall. Inoperative vehicle systems or an engine stall can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the positive battery cable/BMS/PDB joint for excess sealant adhesive and remove any excessive adhesive, reassemble the joint, and retorque the fastener, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V575000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=F-150&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 111, "Rear Visibility." Reduced rearward visibility can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will replace the rearview camera, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V467000: Electrical System:Starter Assembly](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=F-150&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** Electrical arcing can increase the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will replace the starter motor B+ power supply attachment nut, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V197000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission:Gear Position Indication (Prndl)](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=F-150&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** The transmission shift cable lock clip may not be fully seated, allowing the transmission to actually be in a different gear than the gear shift position selected by the driver. If the transmission selection does not match the indicated gear selection, and the parking brake is not applied, unintended vehicle movement can occur, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will inspect and correct the shift cable locking clip installation, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V633000: Seats:Front Assembly:Recliner](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=F-150&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) numbers 202, "Head Restraints" and 207, "Seating Systems." A seat back with an improperly assembled recliner mechanism may have reduced strength and may not adequately restrain an occupant in a crash, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and Ford and Lincoln dealers will inspect the seat structure and replace them, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V250000: Visibility:Windshield Wiper/Washer:Linkages](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=F-150&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** Inoperative windshield wipers can reduce visibility in certain driving conditions, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace both front windshield wiper arms, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

Overall, this vehicle offers a robust mix of capability and diverse engine configurations, making it a strong contender in the used truck market. Buyers should thoroughly test drive the vehicle to check for smooth 10-speed transmission shifting and verify that all electrical accessories, door locks, and safety features operate correctly. Arranging a pre-purchase inspection by a qualified mechanic will help catch minor issues like sunroof drainage maintenance or early component wear before purchase.
