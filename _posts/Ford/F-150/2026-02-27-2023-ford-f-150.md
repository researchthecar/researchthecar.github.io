# 2023 Ford F-150 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2023 Ford F-150 continues to be a dominant full-size pickup offering strong capability and an extensive array of engine, drivetrain, and technology choices. However, prospective buyers should review recurring owner complaints involving unexpected electrical behaviors, lighting water intrusion, and certain powertrain or braking anomalies. While it remains a popular and capable truck, checking service history and verifying that any open recalls have been completed is strongly advised.

---

## Engine

### 3.5L EcoBoost V6

Twin-turbocharged V6 offering high horsepower and strong towing capacity, serving as a popular choice for heavy-duty truck buyers seeking high output.

**Loss of Motive Power**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers report sudden engine hesitation, cutting out at stoplights, or activation of safety warnings resulting in a temporary loss of power.
- **Solution:** Have a certified technician diagnose diagnostic trouble codes, inspect fuel and ignition systems, and apply any relevant manufacturer software updates.

### 5.0L Ti-VCT V8

Naturally aspirated V8 engine known for traditional power delivery and deep exhaust note, appealing to traditional truck buyers.

### 2.7L EcoBoost V6

Smaller displacement twin-turbo V6 balancing everyday drivability and fuel economy with respectable boost performance.

### 3.5L PowerBoost V6 Hybrid

Hybrid powertrain combining a twin-turbo V6 with an electric motor to provide high output and mobile generator capabilities via Pro Power Onboard.

### Electric Drive Motor

Dual-motor electric powertrain exclusive to the F-150 Lightning, delivering instant torque and silent acceleration.

**Battery Range and Charging Discrepancies**
- **Symptoms:** F-150 Lightning owners have experienced lower than expected real-world driving range compared to EPA estimates.
- **Solution:** Consult dealership service for battery health diagnostics and ensure all vehicle control modules are running the latest software calibrations.

## Transmission

### 10-Speed Automatic

10-speed automatic transmission paired across multiple engine options, designed to optimize fuel efficiency and towing performance.

**Harsh Shifting and Delayed Engagement**
- **Symptoms:** The 10R80 transmission can exhibit erratic shifting, delayed engagement when moving between Park or Reverse and Drive, and harsh downshifts or shudders under load.
- **Solution:** Reprogram the powertrain control module or inspect internal valve bodies and fluid condition; consult a dealer for updated transmission calibration strategies.

## Brakes

### Hydraulic Brake System

Standard hydraulic disc brake system designed to handle heavy payload and towing stopping demands.

**Temporary Loss of Brake Pedal Pressure**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have reported instances where pressing the brake pedal resulted in a temporary loss of firm pressure or delayed stopping response before pressure returned.
- **Solution:** Thoroughly inspect brake lines, master cylinder, and anti-lock brake system (ABS) modules for leaks, air pockets, or component failure.

## Exterior

### Exterior Lighting and Tail Lamps

Exterior lighting systems including LED headlamps, taillights, and trailer lighting modules responsible for visibility and signaling.

**Tail Light Water Intrusion and Electrical Flickering**
- **Symptoms:** Moisture buildup inside rear light assemblies can cause flickering, warning messages, or complete failure of exterior running lights and taillights.
- **Solution:** Inspect and reseal or replace affected tail lamp assemblies to prevent water intrusion from compromising internal electronics.

### Panoramic Sunroof

Large glass roof option providing cabin light and ventilation, though susceptible to rare stress fractures.

**Spontaneous Glass Shattering**
- **Symptoms:** The glass panel in the panoramic sunroof can crack or shatter unexpectedly while driving at highway speeds, accompanied by a loud pop.
- **Solution:** Inspect the sunroof frame for structural stress and replace damaged glass panels under warranty or comprehensive insurance where applicable.

## Safety

### Forward Collision Avoidance

Advanced driver assistance systems intended to monitor traffic and mitigate or avoid front-end collisions.

**Unintended Emergency Braking**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers note unexpected activation of the automatic emergency braking system, causing the vehicle to brake forcefully or lock wheels without an immediate obstacle.
- **Solution:** Have the dealer check forward-facing camera and radar sensor alignments, and verify that collision avoidance control modules have the latest software updates.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V070000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=F-150&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** The transmission may contain a loose bolt which could prevent the transmission from engaging the park gear, although the gear shifter position may indicate that the vehicle has been shifted to "PARK." The inability to secure the vehicle in the park position can result in a rollaway, increasing the risk of a crash or injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and, if necessary, replace the transmissions, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V072000: Air Bags:Frontal](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=F-150&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** The instrument panel top cover on the passenger-side may prevent the air bag from deploying as intended. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 208, "Occupant Crash Protection." An air bag that does not deploy as intended increases the risk of injury in a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the passenger instrument panel top cover, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V128000: Power Train:Axle Assembly](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=F-150&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** The front axle pinion may not have been heat-treated, which can cause it to fracture. A fractured pinion can result in a loss of 4-wheel drive and may cause the front wheels to lock up, increasng the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the front axle pinion gear set. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V509000: Parking Brake:Electrical](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=F-150&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** Contact with the rear axle housing may damage the wiring harness, causing the electric parking brake to activate unexpectedly. Unexpected activation of the electric parking brake while driving increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will install a protective tie strap, tape wrap, and replace the harness as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V847000: Steering:Column](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=F-150&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** A deactivated air bag will not deploy during a crash, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the clock spring as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V896000: Power Train:Axle Hubs](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=F-150&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** Both of these conditions can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the left and right rear axle shaft assemblies, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V852000: Equipment:Other:Labels](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=F-150&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirement Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 208, "Occupant Crash Protection." Without a label that shows the proper restraint usage guidelines, the occupants may have an increased risk of injury in the event of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 25V315000: Back Over Prevention:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=F-150&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** A software error may cause the rearview camera image to delay, freeze, or not display when the vehicle is in reverse. A frozen or missing rearview camera image can reduce the driver's view behind the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2023 Ford F-150 is a powerhouse in the full-size truck market, providing exceptional utility, high towing capacities, and diverse powertrain choices ranging from traditional V8s to hybrid and all-electric configurations. While the majority of owners enjoy trouble-free service, buyers should perform a thorough inspection checking for 10-speed transmission shift quality, proper operation of electrical and trailer lighting modules, and any history of unaddressed recalls. Prospective buyers are encouraged to secure a comprehensive pre-purchase inspection before finalizing their deal.
