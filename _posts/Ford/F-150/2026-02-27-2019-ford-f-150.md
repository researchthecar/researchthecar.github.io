# 2019 Ford F-150 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2019 Ford F-150 continues to be a top choice in the full-size pickup market, offering a robust lineup of engine and transmission choices tailored for towing, hauling, and daily driving. While it brings impressive capability and modern tech to the table, used car buyers should approach shopping with a balanced perspective, keeping an eye out for potential mechanical quirks reported by owners.

---

## Engine

### 3.3L V6

The base 3.3L naturally aspirated V6 engine produces approximately 290 horsepower and 265 lb-ft of torque, serving as a dependable, straightforward option for fleet and basic utility configurations.

### 2.7L EcoBoost V6

The 2.7L twin-turbocharged EcoBoost V6 delivers around 325 horsepower and 400 lb-ft of torque, offering a strong balance of everyday driveability and turbocharged pep.

### 3.5L EcoBoost V6

The high-output 3.5L twin-turbocharged EcoBoost V6 puts out up to 375 or 450 horsepower (depending on the tune) and 470-510 lb-ft of torque, making it a favorite for heavy towing and maximum capability.

### 5.0L V8

The classic 5.0L naturally aspirated Coyote V8 generates approximately 395 horsepower and 400 lb-ft of torque, providing traditional V8 performance and exhaust sound.

### 3.0L Power Stroke V6 Diesel

The 3.0L Power Stroke V6 turbo diesel delivers roughly 250 horsepower and 440 lb-ft of torque, geared toward buyers looking for high torque figures and long-distance fuel economy.

## Transmission

### 10-Speed SelectShift Automatic

The 10-speed automatic transmission pairs across most of the engine lineup to maximize fuel efficiency and power delivery, though used buyers should test-drive thoroughly to ensure smooth shifting.

### 6-Speed SelectShift Automatic

The 6-speed automatic transmission is found paired with specific base engine configurations, offering a simpler mechanical design with proven reliability in older truck iterations.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V097000: Exterior Lighting:Headlights](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=F-150&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** The Daytime Running Lamps (DRL) may remain activated instead of dimming to parking lamps when the Master Lighting Switch (MLS) is manually rotated from the "Autolamps" switch position to "Headlamps On." As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 108, "Lamps, Reflective Devices and.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will reprogram the body control module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V805000: Electrical System:12V/24V/48V Battery:Cables](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=F-150&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** The engine could also stall. Inoperative vehicle systems or an engine stall can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the positive battery cable/BMS/PDB joint for excess sealant adhesive and remove any excessive adhesive, reassemble the joint, and retorque the fastener, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V894000: Engine](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=F-150&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** When the heater is plugged in, prolonged corrosion could cause a resistive short, increasing the risk of overheated or melted wiring which can increase the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will disable the block heater cable, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V633000: Seats:Front Assembly:Recliner](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=F-150&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) numbers 202, "Head Restraints" and 207, "Seating Systems." A seat back with an improperly assembled recliner mechanism may have reduced strength and may not adequately restrain an occupant in a crash, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and Ford and Lincoln dealers will inspect the seat structure and replace them, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V278000: Engine](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=F-150&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** A damaged cable could cause a resistive short, increasing the risk of overheated or melted wiring, increasing the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will disable the engine block heater from being used. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V252000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=F-150&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 111, "Rear Visibility." A rearview camera that displays a blank or distorted image can reduce the drivers view of what is behind the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the rearview camera, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V852000: Equipment:Other:Labels](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=F-150&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirement Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 208, "Occupant Crash Protection." Without a label that shows the proper restraint usage guidelines, the occupants may have an increased risk of injury in the event of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 25V442000: Back Over Prevention:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=F-150&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** A software error may cause the rearview camera to display a blank image, or the image may remain on the display after the backing event has ended. Both of these situations can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the rearview camera software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2019 Ford F-150 offers an extensive range of powertrain options to suit almost any truck buyer's needs, backed by solid overall capability. While specific owner-reported complaints can vary across different engines and trims, a thorough pre-purchase inspection and test drive will go a long way in ensuring you find a reliable, well-maintained vehicle.
