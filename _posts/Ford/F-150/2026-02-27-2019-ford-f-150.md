# 2019 Ford F-150 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The Ford F-150 remains one of the most popular and versatile full-size trucks on the market, offering a broad range of capable engine and transmission configurations. However, used-car buyers should review potential trouble spots, particularly regarding advanced powertrain combinations and electrical accessories. Careful pre-purchase inspection can help identify vehicles that have received updated components or necessary software interventions.

---

## Engine

### 3.5L EcoBoost V6

A twin-turbocharged V6 engine delivering around 375 horsepower and 470 lb-ft of torque, providing strong towing capacity. While powerful, early production models can experience cam phaser wear that requires mechanical attention.

**Cam Phaser and Timing Chain Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Loud rattling or knocking noises from the engine compartment during start-up, often accompanied by check engine lights.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified mechanic inspect and replace worn cam phasers, timing chains, and associated tensioners.

### 5.0L Coyote V8

A naturally aspirated V8 engine producing roughly 395 horsepower and 400 lb-ft of torque, favored for traditional capability. It is a robust powerplant, though routine fluid monitoring is recommended.

### 2.7L EcoBoost V6

A smaller displacement twin-turbo V6 generating about 325 horsepower and 400 lb-ft of torque, balancing everyday drivability with efficiency. It generally offers solid reliability for daily commuting and light towing.

### 3.3L V6

A naturally aspirated base V6 engine rated at approximately 290 horsepower and 265 lb-ft of torque. It serves as a durable, straightforward option for basic fleet or utility applications.

### 3.0L Power Stroke V6 Diesel

A turbocharged diesel option offering high torque output and strong highway fuel economy. Maintenance requires attentive emissions system upkeep.

## Transmission

### 10-Speed Automatic

A modern multi-speed automatic transmission designed to optimize fuel efficiency and power delivery across a wide gear range. Some drivers report rough shift quality or hesitation requiring software updates or mechanical adjustments.

**Harsh Shifting and Gear Slipping**
- **Symptoms:** Erratic gear changes, harsh downshifts, hesitation when accelerating, or unexpected drops into unintended gears.
- **Solution:** Update transmission control modules, inspect internal components, or service valve bodies as diagnosed by a professional.

## Electrical

### Back Over Prevention Camera

The rearview camera and display system aid parking and trailer hitching. Intermittent video loss or distortion is a frequently reported electronic glitch.

**Backup Camera Display Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The backup camera display turns completely blank, blue, or severely distorted when shifting into reverse.
- **Solution:** Inspect internal camera wiring, replace failing camera units, or perform applicable manufacturer software updates.

## Exterior

### Body Control Module and Cowl Seals

Manages various cabin electronics, wiring harnesses, and comfort features. Exterior seal degradation can occasionally allow moisture to reach sensitive interior electronics.

**Water Intrusion and Corrosion**
- **Symptoms:** Water leaking past exterior cowl seals into the cabin interior, causing electrical corrosion and intermittent module malfunctions.
- **Solution:** Replace faulty cowl seals and inspect the body control module for water damage, replacing corroded components as necessary.

### Rear Sliding Glass Window

Cabin glass components designed for ventilation and visibility. Rare instances of spontaneous glass breakage have been documented by vehicle owners.

**Spontaneous Rear Window Shattering**
- **Symptoms:** The middle rear sliding glass window suddenly shatters or explodes during normal vehicle acceleration or operation.
- **Solution:** Replace the rear window assembly with updated factory or aftermarket glass components.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V097000: Exterior Lighting:Headlights](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=F-150&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** The Daytime Running Lamps (DRL) may remain activated instead of dimming to parking lamps when the Master Lighting Switch (MLS) is manually rotated from the "Autolamps" switch position to "Headlamps On." As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 108, "Lamps, Reflective Devices and.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will reprogram the body control module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V805000: Electrical System:12V/24V/48V Battery:Cables](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=F-150&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** The engine could also stall. Inoperative vehicle systems or an engine stall can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the positive battery cable/BMS/PDB joint for excess sealant adhesive and remove any excessive adhesive, reassemble the joint, and retorque the fastener, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V894000: Engine](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=F-150&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** When the heater is plugged in, prolonged corrosion could cause a resistive short, increasing the risk of overheated or melted wiring which can increase the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will disable the block heater cable, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V633000: Seats:Front Assembly:Recliner](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=F-150&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) numbers 202, "Head Restraints" and 207, "Seating Systems." A seat back with an improperly assembled recliner mechanism may have reduced strength and may not adequately restrain an occupant in a crash, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and Ford and Lincoln dealers will inspect the seat structure and replace them, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V278000: Engine](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=F-150&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** A damaged cable could cause a resistive short, increasing the risk of overheated or melted wiring, increasing the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will disable the engine block heater from being used. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V252000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=F-150&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 111, "Rear Visibility." A rearview camera that displays a blank or distorted image can reduce the drivers view of what is behind the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the rearview camera, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V852000: Equipment:Other:Labels](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=F-150&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirement Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 208, "Occupant Crash Protection." Without a label that shows the proper restraint usage guidelines, the occupants may have an increased risk of injury in the event of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 25V442000: Back Over Prevention:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=F-150&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** A software error may cause the rearview camera to display a blank image, or the image may remain on the display after the backing event has ended. Both of these situations can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the rearview camera software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

When shopping for a pre-owned model, test driving the vehicle thoroughly to evaluate 10-speed transmission shift quality and listening closely for engine startup noises are essential steps. Ensuring all open recalls and camera updates have been completed will significantly improve your ownership experience.
