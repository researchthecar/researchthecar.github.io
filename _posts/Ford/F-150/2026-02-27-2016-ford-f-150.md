# 2016 Ford F-150 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2016 Ford F-150, buyers will find a highly capable light-duty truck known for its lightweight aluminum body and versatile engine lineup. However, prospective owners should review common owner-reported trouble spots, including recurring door latch failures in cold weather, brake system concerns, and powertrain anomalies.

---

## Engine

### 2.7L EcoBoost V6

The 2.7L twin-turbocharged EcoBoost V6 produces approximately 325 horsepower and 375 lb-ft of torque, offering brisk acceleration and competitive towing for everyday tasks.

**Engine Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle may experience unexpected engine stalling while driving, accompanied by dashboard warning lights.
- **Solution:** Diagnose fault codes and inspect throttle body, sensors, or fuel delivery components for failure.

### 3.5L EcoBoost V6

The 3.5L twin-turbocharged EcoBoost V6 delivers 365 horsepower and 420 lb-ft of torque, providing strong low-end pulling power suited for heavier towing needs.

### 3.5L V6

The naturally aspirated 3.5L V6 serves as the base engine, generating 282 horsepower and 253 lb-ft of torque for standard utility requirements.

### 5.0L V8

The 5.0L Ti-VCT V8 puts out 385 horsepower and 387 lb-ft of torque, offering traditional V8 capability and exhaust note.

**Exhaust Manifold Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Abnormally high-pitched ticking or whistling noises coming from the engine bay or exhaust manifold, often caused by warped manifolds or broken studs.
- **Solution:** Replace the damaged exhaust manifold, gasket, and retaining bolts.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic

The 6-speed automatic transmission pairs across multiple engine choices to balance highway efficiency with towing performance.

**Harsh Shifting and Limp Mode**
- **Symptoms:** Transmission may unexpectedly drop into a lower gear, flash gear indicators, or enter limp mode while driving.
- **Solution:** Inspect and replace the transmission lead frame or associated internal electronic components.
- **Cost:** $1000

## Suspension

### Wheels and Lug Nuts

Suspension and wheel components handle everyday loads well, though minor wheel hardware maintenance issues are common.

**Swollen Lug Nuts**
- **Symptoms:** Factory two-piece lug nuts become swollen or distorted, making standard socket removal during tire changes difficult or impossible.
- **Solution:** Replace factory lug nuts with solid aftermarket alternatives.

## Brakes

### Brake Hydraulic System

The service brake system provides standard stopping capabilities, though some owners report hydraulic and pedal response concerns.

**Brake Pedal Softness and Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Brake pedal may feel loose, travel too far down, or experience a sudden loss of stopping power.
- **Solution:** Inspect the master cylinder, brake fluid levels, and hydraulic lines for leaks or internal failure.

## Steering

### Electric Power Steering

The electric power steering system provides adjustable feedback and low effort at low speeds.

**Steering Assist Loss**
- **Symptoms:** Power steering assist may fail intermittently, making the steering wheel heavy and difficult to turn.
- **Solution:** Inspect the electronic power assist rack and associated wiring connections for faults.

## Exterior

### Door Latches

Cabin elements are generally durable, but door latch mechanisms require attention due to weather-related operational failures.

**Freezing Door Latches**
- **Symptoms:** Doors fail to open or latch properly, particularly during cold weather conditions or after winter storms.
- **Solution:** Service or replace the door latch assemblies with revised manufacturer parts.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V894000: Engine](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=F-150&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** When the heater is plugged in, prolonged corrosion could cause a resistive short, increasing the risk of overheated or melted wiring which can increase the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will disable the block heater cable, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V278000: Engine](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=F-150&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** A damaged cable could cause a resistive short, increasing the risk of overheated or melted wiring, increasing the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will disable the engine block heater from being used. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V568000: Seat Belts:Pretensioner](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=F-150&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** If a front seat belt pretensioner deploys as the result of a crash, the sparks may ignite materials such as carpeting or insulation within the B-pillar area. A vehicle fire could result if materials ignite inside the vehicle.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will remove the B-pillar insulation material and wiring harness tape, and install heat resistant tape, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V652000: Latches/Locks/Linkages:Doors:Latch](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=F-150&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** A door that opens while driving can increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the door latch actuation cables and repair if necessary, and install water shields over the door latches, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V526000: Seat Belts:Rear/Other](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=F-150&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 210, "Seat Belt Assembly Anchorages." If the left rear inflatable seat belt buckle separates from it's mounting bracket, the seat occupant may not be adequately restrained, increasing their risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the seat belt assembly, replacing the attaching rivet as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V331000: Latches/Locks/Linkages:Doors:Latch](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=F-150&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** These accessory door handle covers may cause the doors to unlatch in a side impact collision. As such, these vehicles may fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 206, "Door Locks and Door Retention Components." If the doors unlatch and open in a side impact collision, the occupants have an increased risk.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will remove the accessory covers and refund the purchase price. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V475000: Seats:Front Assembly:Recliner](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=F-150&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** The seat back frame may have insufficient welds which may not meet the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) numbers 202, "Head Restraints" and 207, "Seating Systems." The seat back may not adequately restrain the occupant during a crash, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will inspect and, if necessary, replace the seat back frame, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V767000: Seats](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=F-150&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** Loose power seat adjuster pivot bolts may cause the front seat cushions to detach and not properly restrain the seat occupant in the event of a crash. In the event of a crash, a seat occupant that is not properly restrained has an increased risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the tightness of the power seat track upper pivot link bolt, either applying threadlocker or replacing the lift link and hardware, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2016 Ford F-150 remains a popular choice on the used truck market for its strong towing capacities and comfortable interior. Buyers should thoroughly inspect potential vehicles for transmission lead frame issues, exhaust manifold leaks on V8 models, and ensure that door latches and brake systems operate correctly.
