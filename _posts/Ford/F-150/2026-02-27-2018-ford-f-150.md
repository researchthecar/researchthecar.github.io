# 2018 Ford F-150 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2018 Ford F-150 is a capable and popular full-size truck offering a wide range of potent engine and transmission configurations. While it delivers strong towing capabilities and modern features, prospective used car buyers should carefully inspect certain mechanical and structural areas. Common pain points reported by owners include transmission shifting irregularities, door latches freezing in cold weather, and occasional engine electrical or stalling concerns.

---

## Engine

### 3.3L V6

A naturally aspirated V6 engine providing dependable base-level performance for everyday utility. It pairs with an automatic transmission and is generally considered straightforward to maintain.

### 2.7L EcoBoost V6

A twin-turbocharged V6 balancing strong low-end torque with competitive fuel economy. It is a popular choice for daily driving and light towing.

**Electrical Warning Lights and Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers experience unexpected dashboard warning lights, engine stalling, or illuminated check engine alerts.
- **Solution:** Diagnostic scanning at a service center to isolate faulty sensors or software calibration issues, followed by component replacement if necessary.

### 3.5L EcoBoost V6

A high-output twin-turbocharged V6 engineered for heavy towing and robust hauling performance. Buyers should listen closely for any timing chain rattles during cold starts.

**Engine Timing and Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** Illuminated warning lights and engine hesitation or stalling during startup and low-speed operation.
- **Solution:** Inspection of timing components or throttle body service and software updates.

### 5.0L V8

A traditional naturally aspirated V8 delivering classic American truck power and a deep exhaust note. It remains a favorite for buyers prioritizing towing capacity and V8 performance.

**Rough Idle and Shudder**
- **Symptoms:** Abnormal shaking, rough idling, or shuddering when the vehicle is cold started.
- **Solution:** Powertrain control module reprogramming or mechanical inspection of the fuel and ignition systems.

## Transmission

### 10-Speed Automatic

A modern 10-speed automatic transmission designed to optimize fuel efficiency and power delivery across a wide gear range. Some owners experience rough shifts and hesitation that may require software updates or servicing.

**Transmission Slipping and Shifting Delays**
- **Symptoms:** Harsh shifting, hesitation when selecting gears, sudden drop to lower gears, or unintended slips while driving.
- **Solution:** Reprogramming the transmission control module or performing mechanical valve body and clutch inspections.

## Suspension

### Wheels and Hardware

Wheel mounting hardware and tires designed to support heavy payload capacities.

**Swollen Lug Nuts**
- **Symptoms:** Outer metal caps on lug nuts swell or distort, making standard tire irons difficult to fit during flat tire changes.
- **Solution:** Replace factory lug nuts with solid aftermarket alternatives to prevent future removal issues.

## Exterior

### Door Latches

Cabin entry hardware and door structures that can experience operational sticking in sub-freezing conditions.

**Freezing Door Latches**
- **Symptoms:** Doors fail to open during cold weather or refuse to latch shut properly once opened until the cabin warms up.
- **Solution:** Apply specialized lubrication or replace latch mechanisms according to manufacturer service bulletins.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V097000: Exterior Lighting:Headlights](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=F-150&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** The Daytime Running Lamps (DRL) may remain activated instead of dimming to parking lamps when the Master Lighting Switch (MLS) is manually rotated from the "Autolamps" switch position to "Headlamps On." As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 108, "Lamps, Reflective Devices and.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will reprogram the body control module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V983000: Latches/Locks/Linkages:Tailgate:Latch](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=F-150&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** The tailgate latches may unlatch unexpectedly while driving. Unexpected tailgate opening may allow unrestrained cargo to fall out of the truck bed and become a road hazard, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the left-hand and right-hand tailgate latches, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V155000: Tires](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=F-150&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** Over cured tires may develop a break in the sidewall, resulting in sudden air loss or belt edge separation which could lead to tread/belt loss. Either condition can cause a loss of vehicle control, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the tires, and replace them as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21E027000: Suspension:Front:Control Arm:Upper Ball Joint](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=F-150&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** An incorrectly installed ball joint may cause the ball joint stud to break, which can compromise front wheel control, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** DRiV will notify owners, and dealers will inspect, and if necessary replace the ball joint, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V894000: Engine](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=F-150&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** When the heater is plugged in, prolonged corrosion could cause a resistive short, increasing the risk of overheated or melted wiring which can increase the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will disable the block heater cable, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V392000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=F-150&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** These vehicles have high pressure fuel pump assemblies with welds that can fracture, which may cause an oil or fuel leak. A fuel leak in the presence of an ignition source may increase the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will replace the high pressure fuel pump and associated components, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V633000: Seats:Front Assembly:Recliner](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=F-150&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) numbers 202, "Head Restraints" and 207, "Seating Systems." A seat back with an improperly assembled recliner mechanism may have reduced strength and may not adequately restrain an occupant in a crash, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and Ford and Lincoln dealers will inspect the seat structure and replace them, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V278000: Engine](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ford&model=F-150&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** A damaged cable could cause a resistive short, increasing the risk of overheated or melted wiring, increasing the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Ford will notify owners, and dealers will disable the engine block heater from being used. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2018 Ford F-150 is a highly capable truck with strong engine choices and impressive utility, but used car shoppers should perform a thorough pre-purchase inspection. Pay close attention to transmission shift quality during a test drive, verify that all doors operate smoothly in cold weather, and check for any history of engine electrical or timing concerns. Addressing these known trouble spots ensures a more reliable ownership experience.
