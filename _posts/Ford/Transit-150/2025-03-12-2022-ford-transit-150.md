Here's a comprehensive breakdown of common issues and complaints associated with the 2022 Ford Transit-150, categorized by major components and specific configurations, following your requested format.

The 2022 Ford Transit-150 is a popular full-size van used for commercial and personal purposes, known for its versatility and spaciousness. The Transit-150 offers several engine options, including a 3.5L PFDI V6 producing 275 horsepower and 262 lb-ft of torque, a 3.5L EcoBoost V6 rated at 310 horsepower and 400 lb-ft of torque, and a 3.5L EcoBoost High Output V6 putting out 310 horsepower and 400 lb-ft of torque. All engine options are paired with a 10-speed automatic transmission. Common positive attributes include its comfortable ride, customizable configurations, and ample cargo space. Some criticisms include the vehicle's fuel economy and potential for certain mechanical issues.

**Engine**

**3.5L PFDI V6**

*   **No Issues Found**

**3.5L EcoBoost V6**

*   **Hesitation/Stuttering During Acceleration**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Owners report experiencing hesitation or stuttering, particularly during moderate to heavy acceleration. This can feel like a temporary loss of power or a jerky sensation.
    *   Solution: This issue can stem from a variety of sources. Start by checking spark plugs and coils for any signs of damage or wear. Consider also inspecting the fuel system for clogs or failing components. If these parts are in good condition, then a reflash or software update of the PCM may resolve the issue.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $1,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**3.5L EcoBoost High Output V6**

*   **No Issues Found**

**Transmission**

**10-Speed Automatic Transmission (10R80)**

*   **Harsh Shifting/Clunking**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported experiencing harsh shifting between gears, accompanied by a noticeable clunking noise. This is more pronounced when shifting at lower speeds.
    *   Solution: A common solution involves a software update or re-flash of the transmission control module (TCM). In some cases, replacing the valve body within the transmission can resolve the issue.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $2,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Transmission Slipping**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The transmission may slip out of gear or hesitate to engage, particularly when accelerating from a stop or during gear changes.
    *   Solution: This could be a more severe issue requiring a full transmission rebuild or replacement, as slipping can be indicative of worn clutch packs or internal damage.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $3,000 - $7,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Rear Leaf Spring Squeaking**
    *   Description/Symptoms: A noticeable squeaking noise emanating from the rear suspension, particularly when driving over bumps or uneven surfaces.
    *   Solution: Lubricating the leaf spring bushings with a silicone-based lubricant can often temporarily alleviate the squeaking. In more severe cases, replacing the leaf spring bushings may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Freezing/Crashing**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The SYNC infotainment system intermittently freezes, becomes unresponsive, or crashes, requiring a restart.
    *   Solution: Start with a soft reset of the SYNC system. If the issue persists, a software update from Ford may be available. In rare cases, the APIM (Accessory Protocol Interface Module) may need to be replaced.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Backup Camera Malfunction**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The backup camera displays a distorted image, shows a blank screen, or fails to activate when the vehicle is in reverse.
    *   Solution: Check the camera connections for any loose or damaged wiring. If the wiring is intact, the camera module itself may need to be replaced.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Sliding Door Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The sliding door experiences difficulty opening or closing smoothly. It may require excessive force or get stuck intermittently.
    *   Solution: Lubricate the door tracks and rollers. Ensure the door is properly aligned. If the issue persists, the door latch mechanism or the motor for power-sliding doors may need replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Rattling Noise from Dash**
    *   Description/Symptoms: A rattling noise originating from within the dashboard, often heard when driving over rough roads.
    *   Solution: Locate the source of the rattle (often loose wiring harnesses or trim pieces). Secure the loose components with foam padding or clips.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 - $200+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Headliner Sagging**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The headliner material begins to sag or detach from the roof of the vehicle.
    *   Solution: Re-gluing the headliner material is a common repair. In more severe cases, replacing the entire headliner may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $700+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2022 Ford Transit-150, while generally reliable, can experience several issues. The most concerning problems are with the 10-speed transmission, which can lead to costly repairs if not addressed promptly. Electrical glitches and interior noise issues are less severe but can be frustrating. Overall, prospective buyers should be aware of these potential problems and thoroughly inspect the vehicle before purchasing. Be prepared for the possibility of repairs ranging from minor fixes to more significant transmission work.
