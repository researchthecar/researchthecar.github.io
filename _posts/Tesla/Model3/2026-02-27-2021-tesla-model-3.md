# 2021 Tesla Model 3 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2021 Tesla Model 3 offers compelling electric vehicle performance, cutting-edge technology, and strong efficiency. However, used car buyers should approach shopping with a clear understanding of its common electronic and driver-assistance quirks. While traditional mechanical wear is generally lower than gas-powered vehicles, software-dependent features and high-voltage electrical components attract notable owner complaints.

---

## Engine

### Standard Range Plus Permanent Magnet Synchronous Rear Motor

The single-motor rear-wheel-drive powertrain delivers efficient daily commuting power and solid acceleration. It offers an approachable entry point to EV ownership with minimal traditional mechanical complexity, though high-voltage battery monitoring systems warrant close inspection.

### Long Range / Performance Dual Motor AWD

The dual-motor setup adds an induction motor up front to provide all-wheel drive, considerably faster acceleration, and extended driving range. These units are generally robust, but prospective buyers should inspect service records for any past high-voltage traction battery or inverter interventions.

## Transmission

### Single-Speed Fixed Gear Direct Drive

The direct-drive single-speed transmission eliminates traditional gear shifting, resulting in a smooth and highly reliable power delivery system. It requires very little maintenance beyond occasional fluid checks, making transmission mechanical failures exceptionally rare.

## Steering

### Steering Assembly

Electronic power steering failures can temporarily remove power assist, requiring significantly more driver effort to maneuver the vehicle.

**Steering Assist Loss**
- **Symptoms:** The electronic power steering suddenly becomes extremely stiff, accompanied by warning messages indicating reduced power assist.
- **Solution:** Have the electronic steering rack and 12V / high-voltage electrical connections diagnosed and replaced if internal failures are found.

## Electrical

### Vehicle Speed Control

Speed control issues frequently tie into automated cruise and regenerative braking functions, which can occasionally misinterpret road conditions.

**Unexpected Braking**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle suddenly decelerates or slams on the brakes while traveling at highway speeds using cruise control or driver-assistance modes.
- **Solution:** Check for software updates, recalibrate cameras and sensors, or have the Autopilot computer inspected by a qualified technician.

### High-Voltage Battery and Charging System

While high-voltage batteries are covered under factory warranty for a lengthy period, total pack failures or charging faults can prove costly out-of-pocket if outside warranty coverage.

**Battery Management Error and Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Sudden loss of driving range, inability to charge, or dashboard error codes (such as BMS_a079 or BMS_a074) indicating traction battery failure.
- **Solution:** Have the high-voltage battery diagnostic history checked by a technician; failed packs typically require module or full battery assembly replacement.

## Safety

### Forward Collision

Safety systems heavily rely on camera vision, which can occasionally miscue emergency braking interventions when visibility or road markings are compromised.

**Phantom Braking and Collision Avoidance Errors**
- **Symptoms:** The forward collision avoidance or emergency braking system unexpectedly activates, causing abrupt slowing or stopping without a genuine road hazard.
- **Solution:** Ensure camera firmware is up to date and have sensor calibrations checked if false triggers persist.

### Seat Belt

Restraint system components should be carefully evaluated during a pre-purchase inspection to ensure proper latching and sensor connectivity.

**Seat Belt and Restraint Complaints**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have reported concerns regarding seat belt latching or safety performance during severe incidents.
- **Solution:** Inspect seat belt buckles and pretensioners for physical damage or error codes during a pre-purchase inspection.

## Summary Verdict

The 2021 Tesla Model 3 offers rapid acceleration, impressive efficiency, and minimal routine maintenance compared to traditional gas vehicles. However, prospective buyers should thoroughly verify high-voltage battery health, check for any history of phantom braking or sensor warnings, and ensure all open software updates or recalls have been properly addressed.
