# 2022 Tesla Model 3 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2022 Tesla Model 3 offers sharp handling, impressive acceleration, and advanced tech features that appeal to electric vehicle buyers. However, used-car shoppers should review owner reports concerning automated driving systems and select body and suspension components before purchasing.

---

## Engine

### Model 3 Long Range AWD & Performance

Dual-motor all-wheel-drive configurations offering substantial horsepower and quick acceleration paired with enhanced traction in various weather conditions. These trims deliver robust electric performance with minimal traditional powertrain maintenance needs.

## Transmission

### Single-Speed Fixed Gear Direct Drive

A direct-drive single-speed transmission channels motor output directly to the wheels without traditional gear shifting. It is mechanically simple and generally reliable over the life of the vehicle.

## Suspension

### Control Arms and Suspension Bushings

The suspension setup delivers a firm, sporty ride quality but has exhibited occasional premature wear in front control arm joints and bushings reported by owners.

**Control Arm Bushing Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Premature tearing of front lower control arm bushings can occur, potentially leading to unusual noises or component separation.
- **Solution:** Inspect front control arms and replace worn bushings or assemblies as needed.

## Electrical

### Model 3 Rear-Wheel Drive (Standard Range)

Equipped with a rear-mounted permanent magnet synchronous electric motor producing standard acceleration and efficiency for daily commuting. It provides immediate torque delivery and a simplified single-speed drivetrain setup.

**Unexpected Braking**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may abruptly decelerate or activate phantom braking while operating under cruise control or driver assistance modes.
- **Solution:** Software updates and keeping camera calibration clear can sometimes mitigate sensor misinterpretations, though driver attentiveness remains required.

## Exterior

### Exterior Lighting and Liftgate

Electrical and body accessory systems manage a wide array of convenience and visibility features across the vehicle.

**Tail Lamp Condensation and Liftgate Strut Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Moisture accumulation inside rear tail light housings or power lift-gate struts bending and damaging rear glass.
- **Solution:** Replace affected tail lamp assemblies or updated liftgate struts with revised part numbers.

## Safety

### Forward Collision & Autopilot System

Advanced safety features rely heavily on camera and software integration to manage collision avoidance and adaptive driving aids.

**Phantom Braking and Forward Collision Warnings**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers experience unexpected braking events or false collision warnings while utilizing cruise control or automated driving assistance.
- **Solution:** Ensure vehicle software is up to date and clean sensor/camera areas, though underlying system sensitivities may persist.
