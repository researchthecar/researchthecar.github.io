# 2019 Tesla Model 3 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2019 Tesla Model 3 offers an appealing blend of electric performance, advanced technology, and minimalist design. However, prospective buyers should review owner complaints regarding driver-assistance behaviors, high-voltage battery components, and suspension wear to ensure a well-maintained vehicle.

---

## Engine

### Standard Range Plus Electric Motor

Rear-wheel drive electric motor setup producing around 283 horsepower, offering efficient daily driving performance with low mechanical maintenance requirements.

### Long Range & Performance Dual Motor

Dual-motor all-wheel drive setup delivering approximately 346 to 450 horsepower, known for brisk acceleration and strong regenerative braking capabilities.

## Transmission

### Single-Speed Fixed Gear Transmission

Direct-drive single-speed transmission paired with the electric powertrain, delivering seamless power delivery without traditional gear shifts.

## Suspension

### Control Arms and Suspension Links

The front and rear suspension handles cornering well but can experience premature wear on control arms and lateral links, requiring careful inspection during a pre-purchase evaluation.

**Control Arm and Ball Joint Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Clunking noises over bumps, premature bushing wear, or in severe cases, separation of suspension components affecting handling.
- **Solution:** Inspect front control arms and lateral links for play or torn boots, and replace worn suspension assemblies.

## Brakes

### Brake Lines & High-Voltage Isolation

While regenerative braking reduces standard brake pad wear, physical brake lines and seals should be inspected for environmental corrosion.

**Brake Line Corrosion**
- **Symptoms:** Visible rust on brake lines within the front wheel wells, potentially leading to fluid leaks.
- **Solution:** Inspect metal brake lines for corrosion, especially in regions that use winter road salt, and replace affected lines.

## Exterior

### Door Handles

Exterior trim items like door handles can experience cosmetic and structural wear over time.

**Chrome Delamination and Sharp Edges**
- **Symptoms:** Peeling chrome finish on the underside of exterior door handles, creating sharp edges.
- **Solution:** Replace affected door handle assemblies or apply protective overlays to prevent cuts.

## Safety

### Forward Collision & Cruise Control

Driver assistance systems provide advanced capabilities but occasionally generate false collision warnings or sudden braking events.

**Unexpected Phantom Braking**
- **Symptoms:** Sudden and unprompted deceleration while using traffic-aware cruise control or autopilot on highways.
- **Solution:** Ensure vehicle software is up to date and clean sensor areas, though driver vigilance remains necessary during automated driving modes.

### Air Bag & Seat Belts

Seat belt and airbag sensors can occasionally trigger dashboard warnings indicating faults in the occupant detection system.

**Passenger Restraint Warning**
- **Symptoms:** Illuminated airbag warning lights and error messages regarding the front passenger safety restraint system.
- **Solution:** Inspect passenger seat sensor wiring harnesses and replace faulty connectors or sensors as needed.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V037000: Electrical System:Adas:Autonomous/Self Driving:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Tesla&model=Model+3&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** Failing to stop at a stop sign can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 21V835000: Suspension:Critical Fasteners](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Tesla&model=Model+3&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** A lateral link separation could shift the wheel alignment, causing instability and increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 22V045000: Electrical System:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Tesla&model=Model+3&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** The audible chime may not activate when the vehicle starts and the driver has not buckled their seat belt. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 208, "Occupant Crash Protection." The driver may be unaware that their seat belt is not fastened, increasing the risk of injury during a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 21V389000: Seat Belts](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Tesla&model=Model+3&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** One or both fasteners that secure the front seat shoulder belt to the b-pillar may not be properly attached. An improperly attached fastener may prevent the seat belt system from performing as designed, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Tesla Service will inspect and repair both fasteners if necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V00D000: Electrical System:Wiring: Rear Compartment/Trunk](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Tesla&model=Model+3&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** A rearview camera that does not function reduces the driver's rear view, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Tesla Service will inspect and install a guide protector and new cable harness as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V387000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Foundation Components:Disc:Caliper](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Tesla&model=Model+3&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** Contact with the rim may cause a loss of tire pressure, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Tesla Service will inspect and tighten, or replace the caliper bolts as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V846000: Forward Collision Avoidance: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Tesla&model=Model+3&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** A communication error may cause false forward-collision warning (FCW) or unexpected activation of the automatic emergency brake (AEB) system. Unexpected activation of the AEB system may cause the car to stop suddenly, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 19V719000: Equipment:Other:Labels](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Tesla&model=Model+3&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** The driver-side sun visor may not have the required air bag warning information. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 208, "Occupant Crash Protection." Failure to provide the air bag warning information increases the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Tesla will notify owners, and Tesla Service Centers will inspect the visor and replace it, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2019 Tesla Model 3 is an innovative and engaging electric vehicle, but used car shoppers should perform a thorough inspection. Pay close attention to suspension component wear, check for proper operation of driver assistance features, and verify that all safety sensors and door handles are in good condition before purchasing.
