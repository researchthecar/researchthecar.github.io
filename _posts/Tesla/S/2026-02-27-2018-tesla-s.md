# 2018 Tesla S

### Overview
The 2018 Tesla Model S, an all-electric luxury sedan, is generally recognized for its advanced technology and performance. As an electric vehicle, its reliability profile differs significantly from internal combustion engine cars, with fewer mechanical components prone to wear. However, like any complex vehicle, certain areas have seen more attention from owners and technicians. This analysis focuses on documented technical issues beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into common concerns and their potential resolutions for the 2018 model year.

## Suspension

### Air Suspension
**Air Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle sagging on one or more corners, compressor running frequently, error messages related to suspension height.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of air springs, lines, or compressor.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1,500

**Sensor Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Incorrect ride height, suspension error messages, uneven ride.
- **Solution:** Replacement of suspension height sensors.
- **Cost:** $300 - $700

## Electrical

### 12V Battery
**Premature Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Various electrical systems malfunctioning (infotainment, lights, door handles), inability to 'wake up' the car.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the 12V auxiliary battery.
- **Cost:** $200 - $500


### Infotainment System
**Screen Freezing/Unresponsiveness**
- **Symptoms:** Touchscreen becomes unresponsive, system reboots unexpectedly, features become unavailable.
- **Solution:** Software updates, hard reset, or replacement of the central computer/screen unit.
- **Cost:** $500 - $2,000+


### Door Handles
**Retraction/Extension Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Door handles do not present or retract automatically, sometimes stuck in one position.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of the door handle mechanism.
- **Cost:** $300 - $700


### Autopilot Hardware
**Sensor/Camera Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Autopilot features intermittently unavailable, error messages related to cameras or sensors.
- **Solution:** Cleaning, recalibration, or replacement of sensors/cameras.
- **Cost:** $200 - $1,000+

### Summary Verdict
The 2018 Tesla Model S demonstrates strong reliability, particularly in its electric powertrain, which has significantly fewer moving parts than traditional internal combustion engines. The primary concerns tend to revolve around the longevity and potential failure of the high-voltage battery pack, though these are often mitigated by Tesla's extensive battery warranty. Electrical components, such as the infotainment screen and 12V auxiliary battery, have seen some reports of issues. Suspension components, especially the air suspension system, can require attention due to leaks or sensor problems. Overall, the Model S offers a reliable ownership experience, with most issues being addressable through software updates or component replacement, albeit sometimes at a significant cost if out of warranty.
