**Introduction**

The 2017 Tesla Model S is a fully electric luxury sedan known for its high-performance capabilities, advanced technology, and sleek design. Some of its notable features include a spacious cabin with a panoramic sunroof, a 17-inch touchscreen display, and a range of over 300 miles per charge for select models. However, like any vehicle, it has had some reported issues that owners should be aware of.

**Electrical**

* **12-volt Battery Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The 12-volt auxiliary battery fails, causing problems with starting the vehicle, using the infotainment system, and operating the power windows.
    * Solution: Replace the 12-volt battery.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300+
    * Recalls: NHTSA Campaign Number 22V388000

* **High-Voltage Battery Pack Malfunction**
    * Description/Symptoms: The high-voltage battery pack experiences issues, affecting the vehicle's range, performance, and ability to charge.
    * Solution: Diagnosis and repair of the battery pack, which may involve replacing individual modules or the entire pack.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $10,000+
    * Recalls: NHTSA Campaign Number 20V560000

* **Charging Port Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: The charging port fails to connect properly or experiences overheating, leading to charging problems.
    * Solution: Replacement of the charging port assembly.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
    * Recalls: NHTSA Campaign Number 21V410000

* **Electrical Wiring Harness Damage**
    * Description/Symptoms: Rodents or other animals can chew on the electrical wiring harness, causing electrical malfunctions and potential fire hazards.
    * Solution: Inspection and repair of the damaged wiring harness.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+
    * Recalls: NHTSA Campaign Number 21V355000

**Exterior**

* **Door Handle Malfunction**
    * Description/Symptoms: The door handles fail to open or close properly, making it difficult to enter or exit the vehicle.
    * Solution: Replacement of the door handle assembly.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: NHTSA Campaign Number 20V660000

* **Window Regulator Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The power window regulators fail, causing windows to become stuck or move erratically.
    * Solution: Replacement of the window regulator assembly.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Center Console Touchscreen Malfunction**
    * Description/Symptoms: The center console touchscreen becomes unresponsive or displays glitches, affecting the vehicle's navigation, entertainment, and climate control functions.
    * Solution: Replacement of the touchscreen assembly.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Brake Pedal Sensor Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: The brake pedal sensor malfunctions, causing the brake lights to remain on continuously or the cruise control to disable.
    * Solution: Replacement of the brake pedal sensor.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    * Recalls: NHTSA Campaign Number 20V368000

**Summary**

While the 2017 Tesla Model S is a generally reliable vehicle, it has experienced a number of issues that can be costly to repair. The severity and frequency of these issues can vary depending on the specific model, year, and individual vehicle. Electrical and interior issues seem to be the most prevalent, with potential high-voltage battery pack malfunctions being the most concerning and expensive. It is important for owners to be aware of these issues and factor them into their maintenance and ownership costs.
