**Electrical**

* **Battery Pack Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Premature battery degradation, reduced range, loss of power
    * Solution: Battery pack replacement
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $15,000 - $20,000+
    * Recalls: 15V844, 16V493, 16V697
* **Control Module Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Electrical malfunctions, software glitches, touchscreen issues
    * Solution: Control module replacement
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $3,000
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Charging System Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: Slow charging, intermittent charging, charging errors
    * Solution: Charging port replacement, wiring harness repair
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500
    * Recalls: 13E009, 13E010, 14E005

**Exterior**

* **Door Latch Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Doors failing to lock or open properly
    * Solution: Door latch replacement
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
    * Recalls: 13E012, 15V283
* **Chrome Trim Corrosion**
    * Description/Symptoms: Chrome trim on exterior components (e.g., grille, window surrounds) corroding or peeling
    * Solution: Trim replacement or repair
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Sunroof Rattle**
    * Description/Symptoms: Rattle or noise coming from sunroof when closed
    * Solution: Sunroof adjustment or repair
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Center Console Creaks**
    * Description/Symptoms: Creaking or rattling noises from center console area
    * Solution: Center console disassembly and tightening of components
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

The 2012 Tesla Model S is a well-regarded electric vehicle, but it has had its fair share of issues. The most common problems reported by owners involve the electrical system, including battery pack failures and control module failures. Exterior issues like door latch failures and chrome trim corrosion are also relatively prevalent. The interior issues are generally less severe, with rattles and creaking noises being the most common complaints.

Overall, the cost of repairs for the 2012 Tesla Model S can vary depending on the specific issue and the labor rates in your area. Some issues, such as door latch failures and sunroof rattles, can be relatively inexpensive to fix. However, major repairs like battery pack replacement can be quite costly.
