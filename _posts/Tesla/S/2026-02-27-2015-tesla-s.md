# 2015 Tesla S

### Overview
The 2015 Tesla Model S, a luxury electric sedan, is generally recognized for its innovative technology and performance. As an all-electric vehicle, it eschews traditional internal combustion engines and transmissions, focusing instead on its battery pack, electric motors, and sophisticated software. Reliability data for the Model S often centers on its unique electric powertrain components, battery health, and the integrated electronic systems. While many owners report high satisfaction, specific areas can experience issues, particularly as the vehicle ages and accumulates mileage. Understanding these potential points of concern is crucial for prospective buyers and current owners.

### Battery Pack
**Degradation**
- **Symptoms:** Reduced range over time, slower charging speeds.
- **Solution:** Battery replacement (expensive) or software recalibration.
- **Cost:** $5,000 - $20,000+

**Coolant Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Coolant level warnings, potential overheating.
- **Solution:** Repair or replace coolant lines and seals.
- **Cost:** $300 - $800


## Suspension

### Air Suspension
**Air Strut Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle sagging on one corner, 'suspension fault' warnings.
- **Solution:** Replace leaking air strut.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1,200 per strut

**Compressor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Inability to raise or lower the vehicle, constant compressor running.
- **Solution:** Replace air suspension compressor.
- **Cost:** $400 - $900

## Electrical

### 12V Battery
**Premature Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Various electrical gremlins, inability to start, dash warnings.
- **Solution:** Replace 12V auxiliary battery.
- **Cost:** $200 - $500


### Infotainment System
**Screen Freezing/Unresponsiveness**
- **Symptoms:** Touchscreen becomes unresponsive, system reboots required.
- **Solution:** Software updates, potential screen replacement.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1,500


### Door Handles
**Retracting Mechanism Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Door handles do not present or retract properly.
- **Solution:** Repair or replace door handle mechanism.
- **Cost:** $300 - $700

### Summary Verdict
The 2015 Tesla Model S offers a compelling electric driving experience with generally good reliability, especially concerning its core electric powertrain. However, potential owners should be aware of specific issues that can arise, particularly with the battery pack's long-term health and degradation, which can be a significant expense. The air suspension system, if equipped, is another area prone to issues like leaks and compressor failures. Electrical components, such as the 12V battery and the large infotainment screen, can also present problems. While many of these issues are not unique to Tesla, their cost can be higher due to the specialized nature of the vehicle. Regular maintenance and awareness of these common failure points are recommended.
