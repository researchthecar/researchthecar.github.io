# 2021 Tesla S

### Overview
The 2021 Tesla Model S, a premium electric sedan, generally exhibits strong reliability, benefiting from its advanced electric powertrain and sophisticated technology. As an electric vehicle, it bypasses many traditional internal combustion engine (ICE) complexities, leading to fewer mechanical failures in areas like the engine and transmission. However, like any complex vehicle, it is not immune to issues. Common areas of concern tend to revolve around its advanced software, battery system, and certain electronic components. Owners often report positive experiences regarding the driving dynamics and the innovative features, but occasional glitches and specific component failures do occur, necessitating attention from specialized technicians. The overall reliability is considered good, with most issues being addressable through software updates or component replacement.

## Suspension

### Adaptive Air Suspension
**Air Suspension Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle may sag on one or more corners, uneven ride height, warning lights on the dashboard.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and repair or replacement of leaking air springs, lines, or compressor.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1500

**Control Arm Bushing Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Clunking noises over bumps, vague steering feel, uneven tire wear.
- **Solution:** Replacement of worn control arm bushings or the entire control arm assembly.
- **Cost:** $400 - $900


## Electrical

### 12V Battery
**Premature Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Various electrical systems malfunctioning (infotainment, lights, door locks), vehicle not starting.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the 12V auxiliary battery.
- **Cost:** $200 - $400


### Infotainment System
**Software Glitches/Freezing**
- **Symptoms:** Touchscreen unresponsive, system crashes, navigation issues, audio problems.
- **Solution:** Software updates, hard resets, or in rare cases, replacement of the infotainment unit.
- **Cost:** $0 - $2500


### Charging System
**Charging Port Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Difficulty connecting or disconnecting charger, intermittent charging, charging error messages.
- **Solution:** Inspection and potential repair or replacement of the charging port assembly.
- **Cost:** $300 - $800

### Summary Verdict
The 2021 Tesla Model S demonstrates good overall reliability, particularly in its core electric powertrain which eliminates many common ICE-related failures. The most frequently reported issues tend to be concentrated in the vehicle's advanced electrical systems, including the infotainment unit and the 12V auxiliary battery. Suspension components, specifically the air suspension system, can also present issues like leaks or wear. While the electric drive unit and transmission are generally robust, owners should be aware of potential electronic glitches and the need for specialized servicing. The exterior and interior components are largely reliable, with occasional reports of door handle malfunctions. Despite these points, the Model S offers a compelling package with a strong reliability record for an advanced electric vehicle.
