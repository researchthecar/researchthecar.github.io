# 2016 Tesla S

### Overview
The 2016 Tesla Model S, an all-electric luxury sedan, is generally recognized for its advanced technology and performance. As an electric vehicle, its reliability profile differs significantly from internal combustion engine cars, focusing on battery, electric motor, and software systems. While many owners report a positive ownership experience, some common areas of concern have emerged over time, primarily related to specific electronic components, suspension wear, and occasional software glitches. The absence of traditional engine and transmission components means that failure points are concentrated in different systems. Overall, the Model S offers a compelling package for those seeking cutting-edge electric mobility, with a reliability record that, while not flawless, is competitive within its segment, especially considering its technological complexity.

- **Solution:** Replacement of leaking air springs, lines, or compressor. Software recalibration may be needed.

## Electrical

### 12V Battery
**Premature 12V Battery Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Various electrical systems malfunctioning (infotainment, lights, door handles), inability to 'wake up' the car.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the 12V battery.
- **Cost:** $200 - $500


### Infotainment System
**Touchscreen Glitches/Freezing**
- **Symptoms:** Screen becomes unresponsive, freezes, or displays errors. Loss of control over vehicle functions.
- **Solution:** Software updates, hard resets, or in rare cases, replacement of the touchscreen unit.
- **Cost:** $500 - $2000


### Door Handles
**Retracting Door Handle Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Door handles fail to extend or retract properly, sometimes getting stuck in one position.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of the door handle mechanism.
- **Cost:** $300 - $700

### Summary Verdict
The 2016 Tesla Model S demonstrates good overall reliability, particularly in its core electric powertrain components. The primary areas of concern tend to be related to the sophisticated electronics, such as the touchscreen and 12V battery, and occasional issues with the air suspension system. These are not typically catastrophic failures but can lead to inconvenience and repair costs. The absence of traditional combustion engine and transmission issues is a significant advantage. With proper maintenance and awareness of potential electronic quirks, the 2016 Model S remains a reliable choice for an electric luxury vehicle, though owners should budget for potential electronic or suspension repairs.
