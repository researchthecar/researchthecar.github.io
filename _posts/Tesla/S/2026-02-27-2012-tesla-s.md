# 2012 Tesla S

### Overview
The 2012 Tesla Model S, a pioneering all-electric luxury sedan, represented a significant leap in automotive technology. While lauded for its performance and innovative features, early production models can exhibit specific reliability concerns common to new, complex vehicle architectures. This analysis focuses on documented technical issues beyond standard maintenance, providing insights into potential areas of concern for owners and prospective buyers. The Model S's reliance on advanced battery and software systems means that some issues are unique to its electric powertrain and integrated electronics.

---


## Engine

### Electric Powertrain (All Models)
**Battery Pack Degradation**
- **Symptoms:** Reduced range over time, slower charging speeds.
- **Solution:** Battery pack replacement (expensive) or software updates to manage degradation.
- **Cost:** $5,000 - $20,000+

**Drive Unit Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of power, whining noises, complete failure to move.
- **Solution:** Drive unit replacement.
- **Cost:** $3,000 - $7,000

## Suspension

### Standard Suspension
**Air Suspension Leaks (if equipped)**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle sagging, uneven ride height, compressor running excessively.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of air springs, lines, or compressor.
- **Cost:** $500 - $2,000


## Electrical

### Infotainment System
**Touchscreen Glitches/Freezing**
- **Symptoms:** Screen unresponsive, system reboots, loss of functionality.
- **Solution:** Software updates, screen replacement.
- **Cost:** $100 - $1,500


### 12V Battery Failure
**Premature 12V Battery Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Various electrical systems malfunctioning, inability to 'wake up' the car.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the 12V auxiliary battery.
- **Cost:** $200 - $500


### Door Handle Malfunction
**Automatic Door Handle Sticking**
- **Symptoms:** Door handles fail to retract or extend, difficulty opening doors.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of door handle mechanism.
- **Cost:** $300 - $800

### Summary Verdict
The 2012 Tesla Model S, while a groundbreaking vehicle, exhibits some reliability concerns typical of early-generation, high-tech electric cars. The most significant potential issues revolve around the electric powertrain, specifically battery pack degradation and drive unit failures, which can be costly to repair. Electrical gremlins, such as infotainment system glitches and 12V battery issues, are also noted. While the chassis and exterior are generally robust, owners should be aware of potential air suspension problems if equipped. Regular software updates and diligent maintenance are crucial for mitigating some of these issues.
