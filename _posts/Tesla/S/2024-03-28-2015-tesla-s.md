**Introduction:**

The 2015 Tesla Model S is an all-electric luxury sedan that offers a combination of performance, technology, and comfort. It is available with various electric motor and battery options, providing a range of up to 300 miles. While the Model S has gained popularity for its innovative features, it has also encountered some issues.

**Electric Motor**

**Performance Issues:**

* **Reduced Acceleration:** Sudden loss of acceleration due to a software glitch.
* **Battery Output Limiter Fault:** The vehicle enters a reduced performance mode due to a fault in the battery output limiter module.
Description/Symptoms: The vehicle may experience a sudden loss of power or limited acceleration.
Solution: Software update or battery output limiter module replacement.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 - $500
Recalls: NHTSA Campaign Number: 15V528000

**Battery**

**Battery Management System (BMS) Issues:**

* **Battery Disconnect:** The BMS may shut down the battery, causing the vehicle to lose power.
Description/Symptoms: The vehicle may suddenly lose power or fail to start.
Solution: BMS software update or battery pack replacement.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $5,000
Recalls: NHTSA Campaign Number: 16V667000

**Suspension**

**Upper Control Arm Bushing Failure:**

* **Suspension Noise:** Worn or failed upper control arm bushings can produce a clunking or rattling noise when driving over rough roads.
Description/Symptoms: The vehicle will make a clunking or rattling noise from the front suspension.
Solution: Replacement of upper control arm bushings.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $600
Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

**Battery Charging Issues:**

* **Slow Charging Rate:** The vehicle may charge slowly or not charge at all.
Description/Symptoms: The vehicle may not charge or may charge at a slower rate than expected.
Solution: Inspection and repair of charging hardware, including the vehicle's charging port, charger, or charging circuit.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 - $500
Recalls: NHTSA Campaign Number: 15V516000

**Interior**

**Center Console Screen Malfunctions:**

* **Screen Freezing:** The touchscreen information display may freeze or become unresponsive.
Description/Symptoms: The center console screen may freeze or become unresponsive to touch commands.
Solution: Software update or screen replacement.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 - $1,000
Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

**Door Handle Malfunctions:**

* **Door Handle Failure:** The door handles may fail to open or close properly.
Description/Symptoms: The door handles may not open when pressed or may fail to close completely.
Solution: Replacement of door handles.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

The 2015 Tesla Model S has experienced a range of issues, including performance problems due to electric motor and battery issues, suspension noise caused by worn control arm bushings, slow charging rates, center console screen malfunctions, and door handle failures. The severity of these issues can vary from minor annoyances to potential safety concerns. The cost of repairs can range from a few hundred dollars to several thousand dollars. While Tesla has issued recalls for some issues, it is recommended that owners consult with authorized service centers for timely diagnosis and repair to minimize the risk of further problems.
