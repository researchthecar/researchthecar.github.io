# 2014 Tesla S

### Overview
The 2014 Tesla Model S, an all-electric luxury sedan, is generally recognized for its innovative technology and performance. As an electric vehicle, its reliability profile differs significantly from traditional internal combustion engine cars. Key areas of focus for this model year include battery performance, electric motor durability, and the sophisticated electronic systems that manage vehicle functions. While many owners report high satisfaction, certain components and systems have seen more attention than others. This analysis focuses on documented technical issues beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential concerns for owners and prospective buyers.

### Battery Pack (All Variants)
**Degradation**
- **Symptoms:** Reduced range over time, slower charging speeds.
- **Solution:** Battery replacement (expensive) or software updates to manage performance.
- **Cost:** $5,000 - $20,000+

**Cooling System Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Overheating warnings, reduced performance, potential battery damage.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of cooling system components (hoses, pumps, radiators).

**Air Leaks / Compressor Failure**

## Electrical

### 12V Battery
**Premature Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Various electrical systems malfunctioning, inability to 'wake up' the car, dashboard warnings.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the 12V auxiliary battery.
- **Cost:** $200 - $400


### Infotainment System / Touchscreen
**Software Glitches / Freezing**
- **Symptoms:** Screen becomes unresponsive, navigation issues, audio problems, system reboots.
- **Solution:** Software updates, hard resets, or potential replacement of the touchscreen unit.
- **Cost:** $500 - $2,000


### Door Handles
**Retraction/Extension Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Door handles fail to pop out or retract, leading to difficulty opening doors.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of the door handle mechanism.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600 per handle

### Summary Verdict
The 2014 Tesla Model S demonstrates strong reliability in its core electric powertrain components, with the electric motor and single-speed transmission generally proving robust. However, potential owners should be aware of battery degradation over time, which is a common characteristic of all EVs and can lead to significant replacement costs. Electrical systems, particularly the 12V auxiliary battery and the large touchscreen infotainment unit, have seen some reports of issues. Optional air suspension systems can also present repair challenges. While many aspects are reliable, proactive maintenance and awareness of these specific potential issues are recommended for long-term ownership.
