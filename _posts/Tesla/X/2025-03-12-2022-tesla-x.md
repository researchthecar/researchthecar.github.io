The 2022 Tesla Model X is an all-electric SUV known for its distinctive falcon-wing doors, advanced technology, and impressive performance. It is available in two main configurations: the Long Range and the Plaid. The Long Range offers an estimated 348 miles of range and delivers brisk acceleration, while the Plaid boasts a blistering 0-60 mph time of around 2.5 seconds and an estimated range of 333 miles, making it one of the quickest SUVs on the market. The Model X offers all-wheel drive as standard and is praised for its spacious cabin, comfortable ride, and access to Tesla's Supercharger network. However, some owners have reported concerns about build quality, reliability, and the complexity of some of the vehicle's features.
Here is the information about the 2022 Tesla Model X common problems and issues:

**Electric Motor**

**Long Range**
*   **Motor Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners report complete motor failure requiring replacement. Symptoms include reduced power, error messages, and inability to drive the vehicle.
    *   Solution: Motor replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $5,000 - $10,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Plaid**
*   **Motor Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Similar to the Long Range, the Plaid variant has also seen reports of motor failures, sometimes occurring at relatively low mileage.
    *   Solution: Motor replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $5,000 - $10,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Air Suspension Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported issues with the air suspension system, including failure to adjust properly, sagging, or complete system failure. Error messages related to the suspension system are also common.
    *   Solution: Repair or replacement of the air suspension components, such as air struts, compressor, or control module.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $4,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **12V Battery Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Premature failure of the 12V auxiliary battery. Symptoms include warning lights, failure to start, and general electrical malfunctions.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the 12V battery.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Touchscreen Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Touchscreen malfunctions, including freezing, lagging, or complete failure. This can affect access to essential vehicle functions.
    *   Solution: Software updates, or replacement of the touchscreen unit.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **MCU (Media Control Unit) Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The MCU controls the central display and many vehicle functions. Failures can result in a blank screen, loss of infotainment, and other system malfunctions.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the MCU.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Falcon Wing Door Alignment Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Misalignment of the falcon wing doors, leading to difficulty opening or closing, wind noise, and potential water leaks.
    *   Solution: Adjustment or replacement of door hinges, sensors, or hydraulic components.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Sensor Malfunctions (Autopilot)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Issues with Autopilot sensors, including cameras and radar, leading to errors and reduced functionality of driver-assistance systems.
    *   Solution: Calibration, repair, or replacement of faulty sensors.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Seat Motor Problems**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Failure of the electric seat motors, resulting in inability to adjust seat position.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the faulty seat motor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **HVAC System Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Problems with the heating, ventilation, and air conditioning (HVAC) system, including reduced airflow, unusual noises, or failure to produce hot or cold air.
    *   Solution: Repair or replacement of HVAC components such as the compressor, blower motor, or control module.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $1,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2022 Tesla Model X, while offering impressive performance and technology, is not without its issues. The most concerning problems involve the electric motors and air suspension systems, which can be expensive to repair. Electrical issues with the touchscreen, MCU, and 12V battery are also relatively common. Additionally, the complex falcon-wing doors can present alignment and functionality challenges. While some issues are minor, others can be quite costly to resolve, potentially impacting the overall ownership experience.
