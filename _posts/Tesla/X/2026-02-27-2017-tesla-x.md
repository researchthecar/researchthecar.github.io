# 2017 Tesla X

### Overview
The 2017 Tesla Model X, an all-electric SUV, is known for its innovative features and performance. As an electric vehicle, its reliability profile differs significantly from traditional internal combustion engine vehicles. Key areas of focus for electric powertrains include battery health, electric motor performance, and charging systems. While generally robust, some owners have reported specific issues related to software, door mechanisms, and suspension components. This analysis focuses on documented technical issues beyond standard maintenance, providing insights into potential areas of concern for owners and prospective buyers of the 2017 Model X.

## Battery

### Lithium-ion Battery Pack
**Degradation over time**
- **Symptoms:** Reduced range, slower charging speeds.
- **Solution:** Battery replacement (typically covered under warranty for a specified period/mileage).
- **Cost:** $10,000 - $20,000+ (out of warranty)

**Battery thermal management issues**
- **Symptoms:** Overheating warnings, reduced performance in extreme temperatures.
- **Solution:** Software updates, potential component replacement within the thermal management system.

## Suspension

### Adaptive Air Suspension
**Air suspension leaks/compressor failure**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle sagging on one or more corners, 'Suspension Fault' warning messages, uneven ride height.
- **Solution:** Replacement of leaking air struts, air springs, or compressor.
- **Cost:** $800 - $2,000 per corner/component

**Control arm bushings wear**
- **Symptoms:** Clunking noises over bumps, vague steering feel.
- **Solution:** Replacement of worn bushings or control arms.
- **Cost:** $400 - $900


## Electrical

### 12V Battery
**Premature failure**
- **Symptoms:** Various electrical systems malfunctioning, inability to start the vehicle, '12V Battery' warnings.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the 12V auxiliary battery.
- **Cost:** $200 - $500


### Infotainment System/Software
**Software glitches/freezing**
- **Symptoms:** Touchscreen unresponsiveness, navigation issues, audio problems, failure to update.
- **Solution:** Software updates (OTA or service center), hard resets, potential hardware replacement if persistent.
- **Cost:** $0 - $1,500 (depending on warranty and cause)


### Charging System
**Onboard charger issues**
- **Symptoms:** Inability to charge, slow charging speeds, charging error messages.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and potential replacement of the onboard charger unit.
- **Cost:** $1,000 - $3,000


## Exterior

### Falcon Wing Doors
**Sensor malfunctions/obstruction issues**
- **Symptoms:** Doors not opening/closing fully, error messages, unexpected stops during operation.
- **Solution:** Sensor recalibration, cleaning of sensors, potential replacement of sensors or door mechanism components.
- **Cost:** $300 - $1,500


### Door Handles
**Retracting mechanism failure**
- **Symptoms:** Door handles not popping out or retracting properly.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of the door handle mechanism.
- **Cost:** $400 - $800


## Interior

### HVAC System
**Air conditioning/heater performance issues**
- **Symptoms:** Reduced cooling or heating effectiveness, unusual noises from the HVAC unit.
- **Solution:** Recharge refrigerant, check for leaks, replace cabin air filter, or service HVAC components.
- **Cost:** $200 - $1,000


### Seat Motors
**Power seat adjustment failure**
- **Symptoms:** Seats not moving forward/backward, up/down, or reclining.
- **Solution:** Replacement of faulty seat motor or control module.
- **Cost:** $300 - $700

### Summary Verdict
The 2017 Tesla Model X, being an electric vehicle, presents a different set of potential reliability concerns compared to traditional cars. While the electric powertrain itself is generally robust with no widespread mechanical failures reported for the motors or single-speed transmission, owners should be aware of potential battery degradation over its lifespan and issues with the thermal management system. The adaptive air suspension is a notable area where leaks or compressor failures can occur, leading to costly repairs. Electrical gremlins, particularly with the 12V battery and infotainment software glitches, are also documented. The unique Falcon Wing doors, while impressive, can be a source of mechanical and sensor issues. Overall, the Model X offers advanced technology, but potential owners should factor in the possibility of higher repair costs for certain components, especially outside of warranty, and the importance of software updates for optimal performance.
