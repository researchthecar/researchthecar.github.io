# 2023 Tesla X

### Overview
The 2023 Tesla Model X, as an all-electric SUV, presents a unique reliability profile compared to traditional internal combustion engine vehicles. Its advanced technology, including a large touchscreen interface, sophisticated driver-assistance systems, and electric powertrain, are key areas of focus for reliability. While electric vehicles generally have fewer moving parts than their gasoline counterparts, potential issues can arise from complex software, battery systems, and integrated electronics. This analysis focuses on documented technical issues beyond standard maintenance, aiming to provide a clear picture of the Model X's dependability for potential owners.

## Suspension

### Adaptive Air Suspension
**Air Suspension Leaks/Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle may sag on one side, uneven ride height, error messages related to suspension.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and repair or replacement of leaking air struts, lines, or compressor.
- **Cost:** $500 - $2000


## Electrical

### Infotainment System
**Software Glitches/Freezing**
- **Symptoms:** Touchscreen unresponsive, navigation issues, audio problems, system reboots.
- **Solution:** Software updates, hard resets, or potential replacement of the central computer unit.
- **Cost:** $100 - $2500


### 12V Battery
**Premature Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle not starting, electrical system malfunctions, error messages.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the 12V auxiliary battery.
- **Cost:** $200 - $500


### Door Handles
**Sticking/Retraction Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Automatic door handles fail to present or retract properly.
- **Solution:** Lubrication, adjustment, or replacement of the door handle mechanism.
- **Cost:** $300 - $800

### Summary Verdict
The 2023 Tesla Model X demonstrates strong reliability in its core electric powertrain and battery systems, benefiting from the inherent simplicity of electric drivetrains. However, potential owners should be aware of reported issues primarily concentrated in the electrical system, particularly concerning the infotainment touchscreen's software and occasional 12V battery failures. The adaptive air suspension can also be a source of concern, with some instances of leaks or malfunctions. Exterior and interior components generally fare well, with no widespread critical failures noted. Overall, the Model X offers a technologically advanced driving experience with a generally good reliability record, though vigilance regarding software updates and potential suspension or electrical component issues is advised.
