The 2022 Tesla Model Y is an all-electric compact SUV known for its impressive performance, long range, and advanced technology. It comes standard with a dual motor, all-wheel drive configuration. The Long Range version boasts approximately 384 horsepower and 376 lb-ft of torque, while the Performance version delivers around 450 horsepower and 471 lb-ft of torque. What people generally like about the Model Y is its rapid acceleration, spacious interior, the Supercharger network, and the Autopilot driver-assistance system. Common complaints include build quality issues, occasional software glitches, and the minimalist interior design not appealing to everyone.

**Electric Motor**

**Dual Motor (Long Range and Performance)**

*   **Drive Unit Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported complete drive unit failures, resulting in the vehicle being unable to move. Symptoms include error messages on the display and a loud grinding noise prior to failure.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the entire drive unit.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $5,000 - $7,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Loss of Propulsion**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Loss of propulsion can occur when the vehicle detects an anomaly, resulting in the vehicle being unable to move.
    *   Solution: Inspection of the vehicle and motor and replacement of components
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $6,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Suspension Noise**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Clicking, popping, or creaking sounds emanating from the suspension, particularly when going over bumps or during turning.
    *   Solution: Replacement of affected suspension components (e.g., control arms, bushings, ball joints).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **12V Battery Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Premature failure of the 12V auxiliary battery, leading to vehicle malfunction, failure to start, or error messages.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the 12V battery. Tesla has also released software updates to improve 12V battery management.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Display Screen Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Display freezing, black screen, or touch screen unresponsiveness.
    *   Solution: Software reboot or, in some cases, replacement of the display screen unit.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Phantom Braking with Autopilot**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The Autopilot system initiating sudden, unwarranted braking events when no obstacles are present.
    *   Solution: Tesla has released multiple software updates aimed at improving the Autopilot system and reducing phantom braking incidents. However, the issue may still persist for some owners.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: N/A (Software update fix, if applicable)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Misaligned Body Panels**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Gaps between body panels are inconsistent or larger than expected, leading to aesthetic issues and potential for water ingress.
    *   Solution: Adjustment or replacement of body panels.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Taillight Malfunction**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Taillight failure or intermittent operation
    *   Solution: Replacement of the taillight assembly
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Rattling/Buzzing Noises**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Various rattling and buzzing noises emanating from the dashboard, door panels, or other interior components.
    *   Solution: Locating and isolating the source of the noise, followed by tightening or replacing the affected components.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Seat Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Seat motors failing or becoming inoperable.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the seat motor
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**
The 2022 Tesla Model Y, while highly praised for its performance and technology, has exhibited some recurring issues. The most concerning are related to the drive unit, which can be quite expensive to repair. Other common problems involve electrical components like the 12V battery and display screen, along with exterior and interior build quality concerns. Severity ranges from minor annoyances like rattles to major functional failures of critical components. While some problems can be addressed with software updates, others require costly component replacements. Potential buyers should be aware of these potential issues and factor them into their purchasing decision.
