# 2022 Tesla 3

While electric vehicles generally have fewer moving parts than their gasoline counterparts, specific components and software can still present challenges. ## Electrical

### Battery Pack
**Battery Degradation**
- **Symptoms:** Gradual decrease in range and charging speed over time.
- **Solution:** Battery management system recalibration, or in severe cases, battery replacement (covered under warranty for a specified period/mileage).
- **Cost:** $10,000 - $20,000+ (out of warranty)


### Infotainment System
**Software Glitches/Freezing**
- **Symptoms:** Screen unresponsive, navigation issues, audio problems, camera malfunctions.
- **Solution:** Software updates (over-the-air or service center), screen replacement if hardware failure.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1,500 (screen replacement)


### 12V Battery
**Premature Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle not starting, electrical system errors, accessory malfunctions.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the 12V auxiliary battery.
- **Cost:** $200 - $400


## Exterior

### Body Panels & Paint
**Panel Gaps & Fitment**
- **Symptoms:** Inconsistent or wide gaps between body panels, minor paint imperfections.
- **Solution:** Adjustment of panels by a service center, paint touch-up.
- **Cost:** $100 - $500 (minor adjustments/touch-ups)


### Door Handles
**Sticking/Failure to Retract**
- **Symptoms:** Flush door handles not popping out or retracting smoothly.
- **Solution:** Lubrication, adjustment, or replacement of the handle mechanism.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600


## Interior

### Summary Verdict
The 2022 Tesla Model 3 demonstrates strong reliability in its core electric powertrain and transmission, benefiting from the simplicity of fewer moving parts. The primary areas of concern tend to be related to its advanced electrical systems, particularly software glitches within the infotainment system and the long-term health of the 12V auxiliary battery. While battery degradation is a natural process for all EVs, it's a significant long-term consideration. Minor issues with exterior panel fitment and door handle operation have also been noted. Overall, the Model 3 offers a reliable ownership experience, with most reported issues being manageable through software updates or relatively straightforward repairs, though out-of-warranty battery replacement can be a substantial expense.
