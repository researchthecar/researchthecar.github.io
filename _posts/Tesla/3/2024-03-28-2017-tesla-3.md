**Introduction:**

The 2017 Tesla Model 3 is an all-electric luxury sedan. It is available with two electric motor and battery options. The Standard Range Plus version produces 283 horsepower and 325 lb-ft of torque, while the Long Range version produces 353 horsepower and 390 lb-ft of torque. The Model 3 has a sleek and modern design, a spacious interior, and advanced technology features. It is praised for its acceleration, handling, and range, but criticized for its stiff ride and limited cargo space.

**Exterior**

**Body Panel Alignment Issues**
Description/Symptoms: Misaligned body panels, gaps between panels, uneven fitment
Solution: Realignment or replacement of affected panels
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+
Recalls: None Found

**Headlight Condensation**
Description/Symptoms: Water droplets or condensation inside headlight assembly
Solution: Replacement of affected headlight assembly
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

**Touchscreen Malfunctions**
Description/Symptoms: Non-responsive or intermittent touch response, screen freezing or blackouts
Solution: Replacement of touchscreen assembly
Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,200 - $1,500+
Recalls: NHTSA 21V-014

**HVAC System Issues**
Description/Symptoms: Weak or inconsistent airflow, excessive noise, temperature control problems
Solution: Replacement of HVAC components (e.g., blower motor, evaporator core, compressor)
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+
Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

**Front Suspension Noises**
Description/Symptoms: Clunking or rattling noises from front end during turns or over bumps
Solution: Replacement of lower control arms or bushings
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
Recalls: None Found

**No Issues Found**

**Electrical**

**Battery Pack Issues**
Description/Symptoms: Reduced battery capacity, loss of range, overheating
Solution: Replacement of battery pack
Estimated Cost to Fix: $10,000 - $15,000+
Recalls: NHTSA 18V-266

**Charging Issues**
Description/Symptoms: Difficulty charging, slow charging, charging interruptions
Solution: Replacement of charging components (e.g., charging port, onboard charger)
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+
Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

The 2017 Tesla Model 3 is a technologically advanced vehicle, but it has experienced a number of issues, particularly in the electrical and exterior categories. Some of these issues can be costly to repair, and several have prompted recalls from the National Highway Traffic Safety Administration (NHTSA). While the Model 3 offers strong performance and features, potential buyers should be aware of the potential issues before making a purchase decision.
