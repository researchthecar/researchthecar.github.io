The 2022 Tesla Model 3 is a popular electric vehicle praised for its performance, technology, and minimalist design. It comes in several configurations, including Rear-Wheel Drive, Long Range, and Performance models. The Rear-Wheel Drive model offers an estimated 272 miles of range and produces around 283 horsepower, while the Long Range model boasts an estimated 358 miles of range and produces around 449 horsepower, and the Performance model offers an estimated 315 miles of range and produces around 580 horsepower. The Model 3 is liked for its quick acceleration, advanced autopilot features, and over-the-air software updates. Common dislikes include build quality issues, reliance on the touchscreen interface, and limited access to service centers in some areas.

**Electric Motor**

**Rear-Wheel Drive**
*   **Reduced Battery Capacity**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported a lower than expected battery capacity, resulting in less range than advertised. This can manifest as a noticeable drop in range after charging to 100%.
    *   Solution: Tesla service can perform a battery diagnostic test to assess the battery's health and determine if a replacement or software recalibration is necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: Battery replacement can be very expensive, potentially costing $12,000 - $20,000+, depending on the extent of the damage and warranty coverage.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Premature Suspension Component Wear**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported premature wear of suspension components, such as control arms and bushings. This can manifest as clunking noises, uneven tire wear, and degraded handling.
    *   Solution: Replacement of worn suspension components.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1500+, depending on which components need replacement and labor costs.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **12V Battery Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Failure of the 12V auxiliary battery. This can prevent the car from starting or cause various electrical malfunctions.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the 12V battery.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
    *   Recalls: None Found
*   **Display Screen Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported issues with the main display screen, including freezing, blacking out, or becoming unresponsive.
    *   Solution: Software update or hardware replacement of the screen.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2000+, depending on the severity and whether a software update resolves the issue.
    *   Recalls: None Found
*   **Phantom Braking**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The car's autopilot system initiates sudden and unexpected braking for no apparent reason.
    *   Solution: Tesla has released software updates to address this issue, but it may still occur in some instances.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: Usually resolved by software update. If hardware replacement is needed, it can be much higher.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Misaligned Body Panels**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Inconsistent gaps between body panels, leading to a lack of visual uniformity.
    *   Solution: Adjustment or replacement of body panels.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+, depending on the severity and the need for panel replacement.
    *   Recalls: None Found
*   **Rear Taillight Water Intrusion**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Water accumulating inside the rear taillight housings.
    *   Solution: Replacement or resealing of the taillight assemblies.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+ for replacement, depending on the specific taillight.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Rattling and Squeaking Noises**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Various interior components, such as dashboard panels, seats, and trim pieces, may generate rattling or squeaking noises while driving.
    *   Solution: Tightening or replacement of loose components, or application of sound-dampening materials.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $500+, depending on the source and complexity of the issue.
    *   Recalls: None Found
*   **Seat Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Issues with the seats such as the motors failing or the seats not being aligned correctly.
    *   Solution: Repair or replacement of the seat motor. Repair or replacement of the seat mount.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $250 - $1000+, depending on the severity and what part needs replaced.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2022 Tesla Model 3 has some reported issues, ranging from minor interior rattles to more significant problems with the battery and electrical systems. The cost to fix these issues can vary greatly, with some being relatively inexpensive while others, like battery replacement, can be very costly. While not necessarily widespread, potential buyers should be aware of these common complaints and thoroughly inspect any prospective vehicle.
