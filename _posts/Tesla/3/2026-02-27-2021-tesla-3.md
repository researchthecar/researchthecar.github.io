# 2021 Tesla 3

### Overview
The 2021 Tesla Model 3 is a popular electric vehicle known for its performance, technology, and range. As an all-electric vehicle, it fundamentally differs from traditional internal combustion engine cars, particularly in its powertrain. This analysis focuses on reported technical reliability data, excluding routine maintenance and expected wear. The Model 3's electric drivetrain generally offers high reliability due to fewer moving parts compared to gasoline engines. However, like any complex vehicle, specific components and systems can experience issues. This report details common areas of concern and their typical resolutions, providing a technical overview for owners and potential buyers.

## Suspension

### Standard Suspension
**Uneven Tire Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Premature or uneven wear on tires, potentially leading to noise and reduced handling.
- **Solution:** Wheel alignment and tire rotation.
- **Cost:** $100 - $250

**Strut Mount Bearing Noise**
- **Symptoms:** Clunking or popping noises from the front suspension, especially at low speeds or when turning.
- **Solution:** Replacement of strut mount bearings.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600


## Electrical

### 12V Battery
**Premature Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Various electrical system malfunctions, including infotainment screen issues, door handle problems, and inability to start.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the 12V battery.

### Summary Verdict
The 2021 Tesla Model 3 demonstrates strong reliability, particularly in its electric powertrain which has fewer mechanical components than traditional vehicles. The most commonly reported issues tend to be related to the 12V battery, infotainment system glitches, and occasional suspension noise or door handle malfunctions. Tire wear is also a factor to monitor. Overall, the Model 3 offers a reliable ownership experience, with most issues being addressable through software updates or component replacements that are generally within the expected range for a modern vehicle. Proactive maintenance, such as regular tire rotations and checking the cabin air filter, can help mitigate some potential problems.
