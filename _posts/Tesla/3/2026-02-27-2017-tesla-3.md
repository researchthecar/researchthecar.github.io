# 2017 Tesla 3

### Overview
The 2017 Tesla Model 3, representing Tesla's entry into the more accessible premium sedan market, has generally demonstrated good reliability, particularly concerning its electric powertrain. Early production models did encounter some build quality and software-related concerns, which are not uncommon for new vehicle launches. However, the core electric drive unit and battery systems have proven robust. Owners have reported fewer mechanical failures compared to traditional internal combustion engine vehicles. The focus for reliability often shifts from mechanical wear to electronic systems and software updates, which Tesla actively manages. Overall, the Model 3 offers a compelling package with a strong emphasis on its advanced technology and efficient electric propulsion.

## Electrical

### Battery Pack
**Battery Degradation**
- **Symptoms:** Gradual decrease in range over time.
- **Solution:** Battery management system (BMS) is designed to optimize longevity. Replacement typically covered under warranty if significant degradation occurs.
- **Cost:** $0 - $15,000+


### Infotainment System
**Software Glitches/Freezing**
- **Symptoms:** Touchscreen becomes unresponsive, navigation issues, audio problems.
- **Solution:** Software updates (OTA), hard resets, or potential screen replacement.
- **Cost:** $0 - $1,500


### 12V Battery
**Premature Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle may not power on, electrical systems malfunction.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the 12V auxiliary battery.
- **Cost:** $200 - $400


## Exterior

### Body Panels
**Panel Gaps and Fitment**
- **Symptoms:** Uneven gaps between body panels, misaligned doors or trunk.
- **Solution:** Adjustment or repair by a Tesla service center.
- **Cost:** $0 - $500


### Door Handles
**Sticking/Failure to Deploy**
- **Symptoms:** Flush door handles may not pop out or retract properly.
- **Solution:** Lubrication, adjustment, or replacement of the handle mechanism.
- **Cost:** $100 - $300


## Interior

### Build Quality
**Trim Rattles and Squeaks**
- **Symptoms:** Audible noises from interior panels, dashboard, or seats.
- **Solution:** Identification and securing of loose trim pieces, application of sound dampening materials.
- **Cost:** $0 - $300


### HVAC System
**Cabin Air Filter Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Reduced airflow, unusual odors.
- **Solution:** Replacement of cabin air filters.
- **Cost:** $50 - $150

### Summary Verdict
The 2017 Tesla Model 3 generally exhibits good reliability, especially in its core electric powertrain components. While the electric drive units and battery packs are robust, potential issues tend to arise in the sophisticated electrical and software systems, such as infotainment glitches and the occasional 12V battery failure. Early production models may also show minor inconsistencies in exterior panel fitment and interior trim. However, Tesla's over-the-air updates and service network address many of these concerns. For owners, understanding the nuances of EV maintenance and being aware of potential electronic quirks is key to a positive ownership experience.
