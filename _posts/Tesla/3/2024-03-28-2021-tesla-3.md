**Introduction:**

The 2021 Tesla Model 3 is an electric sedan renowned for its sleek design, advanced technology, and impressive performance. It is offered with a single electric motor and a choice of two battery pack options: Standard Range Plus and Long Range. The Standard Range Plus has 303 horsepower and 310 lb-ft of torque, while the Long Range delivers 353 horsepower and 390 lb-ft of torque. Owners appreciate the Model 3's high-tech features, responsive handling, and refined interior. However, like any vehicle, it has its share of reported issues.

**Suspension:**

**Uneven Tire Wear:**
Description/Symptoms: Premature or uneven wear on one or more tires
Solution: Alignment adjustment or tire replacement
Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $250+
Recalls: None Found

**Clunking Noises:**
Description/Symptoms: Noises from the suspension, particularly over bumps
Solution: Replacement of faulty components such as control arms or sway bars
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
Recalls: None Found

**Electrical:**

**Battery Drain Problems:**
Description/Symptoms: Excessive discharge of the battery, leading to difficulty starting or loss of power
Solution: Diagnosis and repair of electrical system issues, including battery replacement if necessary
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+
Recalls: NHTSA Campaign ID Number: 20V919000

**HVAC System Malfunctions:**
Description/Symptoms: Issues with the heating, ventilation, and air conditioning system, such as insufficient cooling or heating
Solution: Diagnosis and replacement of faulty components, including HVAC modules or compressors
Estimated Cost to Fix: $250 - $1,000+
Recalls: NHTSA Campaign ID Number: 22V944000

**Exterior:**

**Misaligned Body Panels:**
Description/Symptoms: Uneven gaps or misalignment between body panels such as doors or trunks
Solution: Adjustment or replacement of body panels
Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
Recalls: None Found

**Loose Weatherstripping:**
Description/Symptoms: Gaps or loose weatherstripping around windows or doors, leading to water intrusion
Solution: Replacement of damaged or loose weatherstripping
Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $150+
Recalls: None Found

**Interior:**

**Headliner Noise:**
Description/Symptoms: Noises or rattling sounds coming from the headliner
Solution: Tightening or replacement of headliner components
Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+
Recalls: None Found

**Creaking Sunroof:**
Description/Symptoms: Noises or creaking sounds from the sunroof
Solution: Lubrication or adjustment of sunroof components
Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $200+
Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

The 2021 Tesla Model 3 is generally a reliable vehicle, but it is not without its issues. Some of the reported problems, such as uneven tire wear and battery drain, can be significant inconveniences. However, most issues are relatively minor and can be addressed with reasonable repair costs. Overall, the Model 3 remains a solid choice for those seeking a technologically advanced and eco-friendly vehicle.
