# 2018 Dodge Durango Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2018 Dodge Durango is a muscular, spacious midsize SUV prized for its towing capacity, aggressive styling, and powerful engine lineup. Prospective used buyers should weigh its strong performance and family-friendly utility against several notable recurring owner complaints, particularly concerning electrical issues, active headrest failures, and moisture intrusion in rear lighting assemblies.

---

## Engine

### 3.6L Pentastar V6

The 3.6L Pentastar V6 produces approximately 295 horsepower and 260 lb-ft of torque, serving as the standard reliable powerplant for base and mid-tier trims while offering decent fuel economy for a large SUV.

### 5.7L HEMI V8

The 5.7L HEMI V8 delivers 360 horsepower and 390 lb-ft of torque, providing robust towing capability and classic V8 acceleration, though some owners report internal valvetrain and gasket issues over time.

**Valvetrain Wear and Misfires**
- **Symptoms:** Increased valvetrain noise, engine shaking, loss of power, and a flashing check engine light caused by cylinder misfires.
- **Solution:** Diagnose the affected cylinder and replace damaged lifters, camshafts, or spark plugs as necessary.

**Head Gasket Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Illuminated check engine light, coolant loss, and engine overheating symptoms.
- **Solution:** Replace compromised head gaskets and inspect cylinder heads for warping.

### 6.4L HEMI V8

Reserved for the high-performance SRT trim, the 6.4L HEMI V8 pumps out 475 horsepower for rapid acceleration and exceptional capability, though it commands a steeper price and lower fuel efficiency.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

The ZF-sourced 8-speed automatic transmission delivers smooth, responsive gear changes across all engine options and generally proves to be a durable component with few widespread mechanical failures reported.

## Electrical

### Infotainment System

The Uconnect infotainment setup is feature-rich, but older units are prone to bubbling, freezing, and ghost-touch screen failures.

**Uconnect Screen Delamination and Glitching**
- **Symptoms:** The touchscreen begins to delaminate, registering ghost touches that randomly adjust climate controls, activate features, or dial phone numbers without user input.
- **Solution:** Replace the faulty touchscreen display unit.

## Exterior

### Exterior Tail Lights

Rear exterior lighting assemblies frequently suffer from seal failures that allow moisture to ruin internal components.

**Tail Light Water Intrusion**
- **Symptoms:** Water pooling inside the racetrack LED tail light assembly, causing sections of the light to fail or become entirely inoperable.
- **Solution:** Replace the sealed tail light assembly to correct moisture infiltration and restore proper lighting.

## Safety

### Active Head Restraints

Cabin seats feature active safety headrests that have drawn complaints for deploying spontaneously under normal driving conditions.

**Unexpected Headrest Deployment**
- **Symptoms:** The active front passenger or driver headrest forcefully deploys forward without warning or impact, striking occupants in the head.
- **Solution:** Reset or replace the deployed internal mechanical restraint mechanism.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V280000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Dodge&model=Durango&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** If the incorrect park lock rod is installed, the transmission may not shift into 'PARK' and keep the vehicle from moving, increasing the risk of unintended vehicle movement and the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will install the correct park lock rod, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V332000: Electrical System:Wiring](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Dodge&model=Durango&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** These vehicles are being recalled to address a defect that could prevent the cruise control system from disengaging. If, when using cruise control, there is a short circuit within the vehicle's wiring, the driver may not be able to shut off the cruise control either by depressing the brake pedal or manually turning the system off once it has been engaged.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the software, and perform a software flash on the engine or powertrain control module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V524000: Electrical System:Wiring:Front Underhood](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Dodge&model=Durango&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** A vehicle stall can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will replace the powertrain control module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V426000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Antilock/Traction Control/Electronic Limited Slip:Control Unit/Module](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Dodge&model=Durango&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** A vehicle that starts and shifts out of Park without applying the brake pedal can result in vehicle rollaway, increasing the risk of a crash without prior warning and/or injury to others outside of the vehicle.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 24V838000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Antilock/Traction Control/Electronic Limited Slip:Control Unit/Module](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Dodge&model=Durango&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** Disabled ABS and ESC systems can reduce vehicle handling and control in certain driving situations. Any of these scenarios can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the integrated pressure transducer and update the ABS module software, as necessary free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2018 Dodge Durango balances strong utility, muscular styling, and impressive engine options, making it an appealing used choice for families needing serious towing power. However, buyers should thoroughly inspect used models for recurring electrical concerns like failing tail light seals, Uconnect screen glitches, and unexpected active headrest deployments. Prioritizing a comprehensive pre-purchase inspection can help avoid costly surprises.
