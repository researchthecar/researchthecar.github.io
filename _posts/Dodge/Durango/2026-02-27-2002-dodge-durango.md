# 2002 Dodge Durango

### Overview
The 2002 Dodge Durango, a popular mid-size SUV, offered a blend of utility and comfort for its era. It was available with a range of V8 engines and a robust transmission, catering to those needing towing capability and family hauling. While generally considered a solid vehicle for its time, like any model with significant mileage, certain areas have shown a propensity for issues. This analysis focuses on common mechanical and electrical concerns reported by owners, excluding routine maintenance, to provide a clearer picture of its long-term reliability.

---


## Engine

### 4.7L Magnum V8
**Crankshaft Position Sensor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Stalling, rough idle, no-start condition, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the crankshaft position sensor.
- **Cost:** $150 - $300

**Intake Manifold Gasket Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Coolant leaks, rough idle, overheating, sweet smell of coolant.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the intake manifold gaskets.
- **Cost:** $400 - $700

## Suspension

### Standard Chassis
**Ball Joint Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Clunking noises over bumps, uneven tire wear, steering wander.
- **Solution:** Replacement of worn ball joints.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600

**Sway Bar Link Bushing Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Clunking or rattling noises, especially when turning or going over uneven surfaces.
- **Solution:** Replacement of worn sway bar link bushings.
- **Cost:** $100 - $250


## Electrical

### Standard Electrical System
**Window Regulator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Power windows not operating, slow window movement, grinding noises from the door.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the window regulator assembly.
- **Cost:** $200 - $400

**Instrument Cluster Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Gauges not working, flickering lights, dead pixels on displays.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of the instrument cluster.
- **Cost:** $400 - $800

### Summary Verdict
The 2002 Dodge Durango generally presents as a capable SUV with a few common areas of concern that owners should be aware of. The V8 engines, particularly the 4.7L, can experience issues with the crankshaft position sensor and intake manifold gaskets, which are repairable. Suspension components like ball joints and sway bar links may require attention due to wear. Electrical gremlins, such as failing window regulators and instrument clusters, are also noted. However, the transmissions and exterior components are typically robust. With proactive maintenance and attention to these specific potential problems, the 2002 Durango can remain a reliable vehicle for its intended purpose.
