# 2023 Dodge Durango

### Overview
The 2023 Dodge Durango presents a generally robust mechanical and electrical profile, reflecting Dodge's focus on durability for its popular SUV. While specific model years and configurations can influence individual experiences, the overall trend for the 2023 model year indicates a vehicle that holds up well under typical driving conditions. Owners generally report satisfaction with its performance and build quality. However, as with any vehicle, certain components may be more prone to issues than others, particularly those related to advanced technology or high-stress areas. This analysis focuses on documented technical reliability concerns, excluding routine maintenance and expected wear.

### Battery Drain
**Intermittent Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle may fail to start due to a depleted battery, often linked to parasitic draw from electronic modules.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis to identify the source of the draw, repair or replacement of faulty modules or wiring.
- **Cost:** $200 - $800

### Summary Verdict
The 2023 Dodge Durango demonstrates a strong overall reliability, particularly in its powertrain options which include the 3.6L Pentastar V6, 5.7L HEMI V8, 6.4L HEMI V8, and the supercharged 6.2L HEMI V8, all of which have no widespread reported issues. The 8-speed automatic transmission also appears to be a reliable unit. Suspension components are generally sound. The primary areas where minor concerns have been noted are within the electrical system, specifically the infotainment unit which can experience occasional glitches, and a possibility of intermittent battery drain. Exterior and interior components are largely free from significant widespread defects. For potential buyers, addressing any electrical system concerns proactively is advisable, but the Durango is generally considered a dependable choice for its segment.
