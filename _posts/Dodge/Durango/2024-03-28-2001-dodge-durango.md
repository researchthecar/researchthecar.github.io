**Introduction:**
The 2001 Dodge Durango is a mid-size SUV known for its spacious interior, rugged capabilities, and powerful engine options. It was available with a choice of three engines and two transmissions.

**Engine**

**5.2L V8**
* **Intake manifold gasket failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaks, overheating, engine misfires
    * Solution: Replace the intake manifold gasket
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Cylinder head gasket failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaks, overheating, white smoke from exhaust
    * Solution: Replace the cylinder head gasket
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**4.7L V8**
* **Crankshaft position sensor failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Stalling, difficulty starting, engine misfires
    * Solution: Replace the crankshaft position sensor
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
    * Recalls: None Found

**3.9L V6**
* **No Issues Found**

**Transmission**

**4-Speed Automatic (42RE)**
* **Transmission slipping:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Vehicle loses power, hesitates when shifting
    * Solution: Overhaul or replace the transmission
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**5-Speed Automatic (545RFE)**
* **No Issues Found**

**Suspension**

* **Front lower control arm bushings wear out:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Clunking sounds from front suspension, uneven tire wear
    * Solution: Replace the control arm bushings
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Rear leaf springs crack:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Sagging rear end, excessive body roll
    * Solution: Replace the leaf springs
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Battery drains quickly:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Battery dies prematurely, electrical accessories fail
    * Solution: Check for parasitic draw, replace the battery or alternator
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Alternator failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Dim headlights, dashboard lights, starting problems
    * Solution: Replace the alternator
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Headlight switch failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Headlights not working, intermittent lighting
    * Solution: Replace the headlight switch
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Air conditioning system failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: No cold air, weak airflow
    * Solution: Diagnose and repair the AC system
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Heater core leaks:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Sweet smell in cabin, fogged-up windows
    * Solution: Replace the heater core
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**
The 2001 Dodge Durango has several potential issues, but they are mostly minor and can be resolved with a reasonable cost. The most severe issues include engine gasket failures and transmission problems, which can be costly to repair. Overall, the Durango is a reliable SUV, but it's essential to be aware of these potential issues before purchasing one.
