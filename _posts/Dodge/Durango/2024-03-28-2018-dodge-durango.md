**2018 Dodge Durango**

The 2018 Dodge Durango is a full-size SUV available with a choice of three engines: a 3.6L V6, a 5.7L V8, or a 6.4L V8. The V6 produces 293 horsepower and 260 lb-ft of torque, while the V8 produces 360 horsepower and 390 lb-ft of torque. The 6.4L V8 is available exclusively with the SRT model and produces 475 horsepower and 470 lb-ft of torque. All engines are mated to an 8-speed automatic transmission.

The Durango is praised for its spacious and comfortable interior, as well as its powerful engines and impressive towing capacity. However, some owners have reported issues with the vehicle's reliability.

**Engine**

**3.6L V6**

* **Intake Valve Sticking**
    * Description/Symptoms: The intake valves can become stuck, causing a loss of power and a rough idle.
    * Solution: The intake valves need to be cleaned or replaced.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Timing Chain Noise**
    * Description/Symptoms: The timing chain can make a rattling noise, especially at startup.
    * Solution: The timing chain and tensioners need to be replaced.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**5.7L V8**

* **Lifter Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The lifters can fail, causing a ticking noise and a loss of power.
    * Solution: The lifters need to be replaced.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Intake Manifold Leak**
    * Description/Symptoms: The intake manifold can develop a leak, causing a loss of power and a rough idle.
    * Solution: The intake manifold needs to be replaced.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**8-Speed Automatic**

* **Shifting Problems**
    * Description/Symptoms: The transmission can have difficulty shifting gears, especially when accelerating or decelerating.
    * Solution: The transmission fluid needs to be changed or the transmission needs to be rebuilt.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $2,000+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Torque Converter Shudder**
    * Description/Symptoms: The torque converter can shudder when the vehicle is accelerating.
    * Solution: The torque converter needs to be replaced.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Front Strut Mount Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The front strut mount can fail, causing a clunking noise and a rough ride.
    * Solution: The strut mount needs to be replaced.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+
    * Recalls: NHTSA Campaign Number: 20V547000
* **Rear Leaf Spring Sag**
    * Description/Symptoms: The rear leaf springs can sag, causing the rear of the vehicle to sit lower than the front.
    * Solution: The leaf springs need to be replaced.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain**
    * Description/Symptoms: The battery can drain quickly, even when the vehicle is not in use.
    * Solution: The battery needs to be replaced or the parasitic drain needs to be found and fixed.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Electrical Shorts**
    * Description/Symptoms: The vehicle can experience electrical shorts, causing various electrical problems.
    * Solution: The electrical system needs to be inspected and repaired.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Hood Paint Peeling**
    * Description/Symptoms: The paint on the hood can peel or chip, especially in hot climates.
    * Solution: The hood needs to be repainted.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Tailgate Rust**
    * Description/Symptoms: The tailgate can develop rust, especially around the license plate area.
    * Solution: The tailgate needs to be replaced or repaired.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Dashboard Rattle**
    * Description/Symptoms: The dashboard can rattle or creak, especially when driving on rough roads.
    * Solution: The dashboard needs to be tightened or repaired.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Loose Headliner**
    * Description/Symptoms: The headliner can come loose, especially in hot climates.
    * Solution: The headliner needs to be reattached.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2018 Dodge Durango is a powerful and spacious SUV, but it has some reliability issues. The most common issues are with the engine, transmission, and electrical system. The severity of the issues can vary, and the cost to fix can range from a few hundred dollars to over $2,000.
