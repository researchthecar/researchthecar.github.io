**Introduction:**

The 1998 Dodge Durango is a mid-size SUV available with various engine and transmission options. The 5.2L V8 engine generates 230 horsepower and 300 lb-ft of torque, while the 5.9L V8 produces 245 horsepower and 335 lb-ft of torque. The available transmissions include a 4-speed automatic and a 5-speed manual. The Durango is praised for its spacious interior, powerful engines, and off-road capabilities, but it is criticized for its poor fuel economy and dated interior design.

**Engine**

**5.2L V8**

* **Ignition Coil Failure:**
    - Symptoms: Engine misfires, rough idling
    - Solution: Replace ignition coils
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300+
    - Recalls: None Found
* **Oxygen Sensor Failure:**
    - Symptoms: Check engine light, reduced fuel efficiency
    - Solution: Replace oxygen sensor
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
    - Recalls: None Found
* **Intake Manifold Gasket Leak:**
    - Symptoms: Coolant leaks, rough idling
    - Solution: Replace intake manifold gasket
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $300+
    - Recalls: None Found

**5.9L V8**

* **Cam Position Sensor Failure:**
    - Symptoms: Engine misfires, hard starting
    - Solution: Replace cam position sensor
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200
    - Recalls: None Found
* **Oil Pressure Sensor Failure:**
    - Symptoms: Check engine light, low oil pressure readings
    - Solution: Replace oil pressure sensor
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $150
    - Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**4-Speed Automatic**

* **Transmission Slipping:**
    - Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears, loss of acceleration
    - Solution: Rebuild or replace transmission
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
    - Recalls: None Found
* **Solenoid Failure:**
    - Symptoms: Harsh shifting, delayed engagement
    - Solution: Replace solenoid pack
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
    - Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Ball Joint Failure:**
    - Symptoms: Clunking sound when turning or going over bumps
    - Solution: Replace ball joints
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
    - Recalls: None Found
* **Shocks and Struts Wear:**
    - Symptoms: Reduced ride quality, excessive body roll
    - Solution: Replace shocks and struts
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
    - Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Alternator Failure:**
    - Symptoms: Dimming lights, battery warning light, difficulty starting
    - Solution: Replace alternator
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
    - Recalls: None Found
* **Starter Motor Failure:**
    - Symptoms: No response when attempting to start
    - Solution: Replace starter motor
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $350
    - Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Tailgate Handle Failure:**
    - Symptoms: Difficulty opening tailgate
    - Solution: Replace tailgate handle
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $100
    - Recalls: None Found
* **Power Window Regulator Failure:**
    - Symptoms: Windows don't roll up or down properly
    - Solution: Replace window regulator motor
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300 per window
    - Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **HVAC Blower Motor Failure:**
    - Symptoms: No air flow from vents
    - Solution: Replace blower motor
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200
    - Recalls: None Found
* **Dashboard Lights Burning Out:**
    - Symptoms: Inoperable gauges or warning lights
    - Solution: Replace burnt-out bulbs
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $150
    - Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 1998 Dodge Durango has a few potential issues that owners should be aware of. While some repairs, such as replacing ignition coils or oxygen sensors, are relatively inexpensive, more major repairs, such as transmission rebuilds or suspension overhauls, can be costly. Overall, the severity of these issues ranges from minor annoyances to significant expenses.
