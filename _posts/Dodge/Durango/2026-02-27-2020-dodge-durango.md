# 2020 Dodge Durango Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2020 Dodge Durango offers robust towing capabilities and spacious three-row seating, making it a popular choice for families needing utility and performance. However, used-car buyers should inspect potential vehicles closely, as owner complaints point to recurring electrical issues, exterior lighting water intrusion, and occasional powertrain concerns.

---

## Engine

### 3.6L Pentastar V6

The 3.6-liter Pentastar V6 produces 295 horsepower and 260 lb-ft of torque, serving as the standard engine for everyday drivability and decent fuel efficiency. It is generally dependable, though higher-mileage models should be monitored for valvetrain wear.

**Cam and Lifter Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Engine power failure, shaking, or ticking noises originating from worn valvetrain components.
- **Solution:** Inspect the valvetrain during pre-purchase evaluations and replace damaged camshafts and lifters if excessive wear is found.

### 5.7L HEMI V8

The 5.7-liter HEMI V8 pushes out 360 horsepower and 390 lb-ft of torque, providing strong acceleration and impressive towing capacity. It is a favored choice for buyers needing heavy-duty hauling performance.

### 6.4L HEMI V8

Exclusive to the high-performance SRT trim, the 6.4-liter HEMI V8 delivers a massive 475 horsepower and 470 lb-ft of torque. It transforms the SUV into a high-performance machine but demands careful mechanical inspection.

## Transmission

### TorqueFlite 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

Paired across all engine options, the ZF-derived 8-speed automatic transmission delivers smooth and responsive gear changes. While generally robust, individual units may exhibit rough shifting behavior that requires professional diagnosis.

**Harsh Shifting**
- **Symptoms:** Loud noises and harsh physical engagement when shifting into reverse or drive.
- **Solution:** Have a certified technician inspect transmission software calibration, fluid condition, and mounts.

## Steering

### Electric Power Steering

The electric power steering system provides light and adjustable feedback at low speeds, though unexpected assist failures have been reported by some owners.

**Power Steering Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Sudden loss of power assist accompanied by dashboard warning messages, making the vehicle difficult to steer.
- **Solution:** Inspect the steering gear, electronic control modules, and wiring harnesses for faults.

## Exterior

### Exterior Lighting

Electrical complaints center heavily around rear lighting seal integrity and auxiliary accessory electronics.

**Tail Light Water Intrusion**
- **Symptoms:** Moisture collecting inside the rear housings, causing LED elements to short out or fail prematurely.
- **Solution:** Inspect tail light assemblies for cracks or faulty seals and replace the affected light units.

## Safety

### Seats and Restraints

Cabin convenience features and interior trim are generally durable, but safety restraint mechanisms require careful verification.

**Active Head Restraint Deployment**
- **Symptoms:** Active head restraint system deploys unexpectedly while driving, striking occupants.
- **Solution:** Inspect and replace malfunctioning active head restraint actuator mechanisms.

### Air Bag System

Safety systems protect occupants effectively during normal operation, though intermittent warning indicators should always be checked prior to purchase.

**Air Bag Warning Light**
- **Symptoms:** Air bag warning light illuminates on the instrument cluster without an obvious trigger.
- **Solution:** Perform a diagnostic scan to identify faulty sensors, clocksprings, or module connections.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V191000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Dodge&model=Durango&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 111, "Rearview Mirrors." The lingering rearview image can distract the driver, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Chrysler has notified owners, and dealers will update the radio display software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V280000: Air Bags:Critical Fasteners](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Dodge&model=Durango&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** A loose or missing fastener may cause the side curtain air bag to deploy incorrectly, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will tighten or replace the fastener, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2020 Dodge Durango is a powerful and practical family SUV with strong engine choices and generous utility. While many owners enjoy trouble-free driving, prospective buyers should thoroughly inspect the vehicle for tail light moisture intrusion, steering assist functionality, and potential valvetrain noise on V6 models. A comprehensive pre-purchase inspection by a qualified mechanic will help ensure you secure a reliable example.
