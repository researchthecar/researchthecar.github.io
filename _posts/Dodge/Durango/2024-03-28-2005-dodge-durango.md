**Introduction:**

The 2005 Dodge Durango is a mid-size SUV available with two engine and four transmission options. The 3.7L V6 engine produces 210 horsepower and 235 lb-ft of torque, while the 4.7L V8 engine generates 235 horsepower and 300 lb-ft of torque. The available transmissions include a 4-speed automatic, a 5-speed automatic, a 6-speed manual, and a 5-speed automatic with overdrive. The Durango received praise for its powerful engines, spacious interior, and towing capabilities, but was criticized for its poor fuel economy and lack of modern features.

**Engine**

**3.7L V6**

**Spark Plug Failure**
* Symptoms: Engine misfiring, rough idling, decreased power
* Solution: Replace spark plugs
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $300+

**Intake Manifold Gasket Leak**
* Symptoms: Coolant leak, overheating, loss of power
* Solution: Replace intake manifold gasket
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $600+

**4.7L V8**

**Cylinder Head Failure**
* Symptoms: Loss of coolant, overheating, white smoke from exhaust
* Solution: Replace cylinder head
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,000+

**Recalls:**

* None Found

**Transmission**

**4-Speed Automatic**

**Transmission Slipping**
* Symptoms: Transmission slipping gears, difficulty shifting
* Solution: Rebuild or replace transmission
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+

**5-Speed Automatic**

**Torque Converter Failure**
* Symptoms: Transmission slipping, shuddering, overheating
* Solution: Replace torque converter
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,200+

**6-Speed Manual**

**Clutch Failure**
* Symptoms: Difficulty shifting, grinding noises, slipping gears
* Solution: Replace clutch
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800+

**5-Speed Automatic with Overdrive**

**No Issues Found**

**Suspension**

**Front Control Arm Bushing Failure**
* Symptoms: Clunking noises, vibration, uneven tire wear
* Solution: Replace control arm bushings
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+

**Rear Leaf Spring Failure**
* Symptoms: Sagging rear end, reduced towing capacity
* Solution: Replace leaf springs
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $600+

**Electrical**

**Battery Drain**
* Symptoms: Battery dies prematurely
* Solution: Check for parasitic draw, replace battery if necessary
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 - $200+

**Alternator Failure**
* Symptoms: Dim headlights, electrical system malfunction
* Solution: Replace alternator
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+

**Exterior**

**Tail Light Assembly Failure**
* Symptoms: Tail lights not working, water damage inside tail light assembly
* Solution: Replace tail light assembly
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+

**Interior**

**Seat Heater Failure**
* Symptoms: Seat heater not producing heat
* Solution: Replace seat heater element
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+

**Navigation System Failure**
* Symptoms: Navigation system not functioning properly
* Solution: Update software, replace navigation unit if necessary
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 - $500+

**Summary:**

The 2005 Dodge Durango has a number of potential issues, including engine problems, transmission failures, and electrical malfunctions. However, the severity and cost of these issues vary widely depending on the specific problem and the age and condition of the vehicle. Overall, the Durango is a reliable vehicle, but it is important to be aware of the potential issues before purchasing one.
