# 2019 Dodge Durango Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2019 Dodge Durango offers a muscular appearance, three-row utility, and a range of robust engine choices, making it a popular choice for families and towing enthusiasts alike. However, prospective buyers should review known owner complaints regarding specific electrical and mechanical trouble spots, notably around lighting assemblies and powertrain concerns.

---

## Engine

### 3.6L Pentastar V6

The 3.6L Pentastar V6 produces 295 horsepower and 260 lb-ft of torque, serving as the standard reliable base engine for everyday driving and moderate utility.

**Coolant Leaks and Overheating**
- **Symptoms:** Coolant pooling under the vehicle, sweet smell, or engine overheating due to failing or rubbing hoses.
- **Solution:** Inspect coolant hoses and lines regularly for wear, and replace damaged hoses or clamps.
- **Cost:** $150 - $400

### 5.7L HEMI V8

The 5.7L HEMI V8 delivers 360 horsepower and 390 lb-ft of torque, offering strong towing capabilities and classic V8 performance.

**Head Gasket Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of coolant, white smoke from the exhaust, engine misfires, or overheating.
- **Solution:** Replace blown head gaskets and inspect the cylinder heads and camshaft for secondary damage.
- **Cost:** $1,500 - $3,000

### 6.4L HEMI V8

Exclusive to the high-performance SRT trim, the 6.4L HEMI V8 churns out an impressive 475 horsepower and 470 lb-ft of torque.

**Engine Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** Unexpected engine stalling while shifting gears or driving at various speeds, requiring a restart.
- **Solution:** Diagnose powertrain control module (PCM) software and inspect fuel or transmission sensor inputs.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

Paired across all engine options, the ZF-sourced 8-speed automatic transmission generally delivers smooth, responsive gear changes that optimize fuel economy and performance.

**Rough Shifting and Winding Up**
- **Symptoms:** Transmission feels like it winds up before harsh shifting, or fails to engage overdrive smoothly.
- **Solution:** Reprogram the transmission control module or check for internal hydraulic pressure drops.

## Exterior

### Exterior Lighting

The exterior lighting system incorporates distinctive LED racetrack taillights, though owners frequently report sealing vulnerabilities.

**Tail Light Water Intrusion**
- **Symptoms:** Moisture, condensation, or pooling water inside the rear tail light or racetrack light assembly, causing bulbs to fail.
- **Solution:** Replace the faulty tail light assembly and failing OEM seals to prevent water re-entry.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1,500+

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V191000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Dodge&model=Durango&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 111, "Rearview Mirrors." The lingering rearview image can distract the driver, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Chrysler has notified owners, and dealers will update the radio display software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V183000: Power Train:Driveline:Differential Unit](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Dodge&model=Durango&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** A sudden loss of power while driving or loss of the transmission PARK function can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will replace the front differential, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V426000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Antilock/Traction Control/Electronic Limited Slip:Control Unit/Module](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Dodge&model=Durango&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** A vehicle that starts and shifts out of Park without applying the brake pedal can result in vehicle rollaway, increasing the risk of a crash without prior warning and/or injury to others outside of the vehicle.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 24V838000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Antilock/Traction Control/Electronic Limited Slip:Control Unit/Module](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Dodge&model=Durango&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** Disabled ABS and ESC systems can reduce vehicle handling and control in certain driving situations. Any of these scenarios can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the integrated pressure transducer and update the ABS module software, as necessary free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2019 Dodge Durango is a capable and spacious three-row SUV with strong engine choices and a smooth automatic transmission. While it offers solid utility, used car shoppers should carefully inspect the vehicle for common issues such as rear tail light moisture intrusion and potential engine cooling or gasket leaks. A thorough pre-purchase inspection can help uncover these common pain points before buying.
