# 2003 Dodge Durango

### Overview
The 2003 Dodge Durango, a mid-size SUV, offered a range of powertrain options and a robust, truck-like platform. While generally considered a capable vehicle for its time, certain model years and specific configurations experienced some recurring issues. Owners often reported on the durability of its V8 engines and the functionality of its four-wheel-drive system. However, like many vehicles of this era, electrical gremlins and interior wear were not uncommon. This analysis focuses on documented mechanical and electrical faults beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential problem areas for prospective buyers or current owners.

---


## Engine

### 4.7L Magnum V8
**Crankshaft Position Sensor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Stalling, rough idle, no-start condition, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the crankshaft position sensor.
- **Cost:** $150 - $300

**Intake Manifold Gasket Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Coolant leaks, overheating, rough idle, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the intake manifold gaskets.
- **Cost:** $400 - $700

## Suspension

### Standard Chassis
**Ball Joint Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Clunking noises, uneven tire wear, steering instability.
- **Solution:** Replacement of worn ball joints.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600

**Worn Control Arm Bushings**
- **Symptoms:** Clunking over bumps, vague steering feel, alignment issues.
- **Solution:** Replacement of control arm bushings.
- **Cost:** $250 - $500


## Electrical

### General Electrical System
**Window Regulator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Power windows not operating, slow window movement, grinding noises.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the window regulator assembly.
- **Cost:** $200 - $400

**Instrument Cluster Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** Gauges not working, flickering lights, dead pixels.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of the instrument cluster.
- **Cost:** $400 - $800

### Summary Verdict
The 2003 Dodge Durango presents a mixed reliability profile. The larger V8 engines (5.9L) tend to be more robust, while the 4.7L V8 can experience issues like crankshaft position sensor failures and intake manifold leaks. Transmission problems, particularly with the torque converter, are noted. Suspension components like ball joints and bushings are common wear items. Electrical issues, such as window regulators and instrument clusters, are also reported. While not plagued by catastrophic failures across the board, potential buyers should be aware of these specific problem areas and factor in potential repair costs for a vehicle of this age.
