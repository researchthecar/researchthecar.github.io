Here's a breakdown of common issues reported for the 2019 Dodge Durango, categorized by component. The 2019 Dodge Durango is a popular SUV known for its spacious interior, powerful engine options, and towing capacity. It's often praised for its comfortable ride and user-friendly infotainment system, while some common complaints revolve around fuel economy and reliability issues. Engine options include the 3.6L Pentastar V6 (293 hp and 260 lb-ft of torque), the 5.7L Hemi V8 (360 hp and 390 lb-ft of torque), and the 6.4L Hemi V8 (475 hp and 470 lb-ft of torque). All engines are paired with an 8-speed automatic transmission.

**Engine**

**3.6L Pentastar V6**

*   **Tick/Knock Noise**
    *   Description/Symptoms: A ticking or knocking noise coming from the engine, especially when cold. This may be related to the rocker arms, lifters, or piston slap.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the faulty rocker arms, lifters, or pistons. In some cases, an updated oiling system may be recommended.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2500+ (depending on the specific repair needed)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**5.7L Hemi V8**

*   **Hemi Tick**
    *   Description/Symptoms: A noticeable ticking sound, typically more prominent at idle or low speeds. Often attributed to exhaust manifold bolts breaking or lifter issues.
    *   Solution: Replacing broken exhaust manifold bolts or replacing faulty lifters.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1500+ (depending on the specific repair needed)
    *   Recalls: None Found
*   **Oil Consumption**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Excessive oil consumption, requiring frequent top-offs between oil changes. This may be related to piston rings or valve stem seals.
    *   Solution: Performing an oil consumption test to determine the extent of the issue. Replacing the piston rings or valve stem seals.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1500 - $4000+ (depending on the severity and repair method)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**6.4L Hemi V8**

*   No Issues Found

**Transmission**

**8-Speed Automatic Transmission (All Engines)**

*   **Rough Shifting/Hesitation**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Harsh or delayed shifts, particularly between certain gears. Hesitation when accelerating from a stop. The transmission may clunk when shifting.
    *   Solution: Flashing/reprogramming the TCM (Transmission Control Module) with the latest software. In more severe cases, valve body replacement or transmission rebuild/replacement may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+ for software updates; $500 - $3000+ for more extensive repairs
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Rear Suspension Noise**
    *   Description/Symptoms: A clunking or rattling noise from the rear suspension, especially when going over bumps. This may be related to worn sway bar links or bushings.
    *   Solution: Replacing the worn sway bar links or bushings.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Uconnect System Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Infotainment system freezing, crashing, or experiencing glitches. Bluetooth connectivity problems. Navigation system malfunctions.
    *   Solution: Updating the Uconnect software to the latest version. Resetting the Uconnect system. In some cases, the Uconnect module may need to be replaced.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 (for software updates) - $500+ (for module replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found
*   **Backup Camera Malfunction**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Backup camera not displaying an image, displaying a distorted image, or intermittently failing.
    *   Solution: Checking the camera connections and wiring. Replacing the backup camera.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Power Liftgate Problems**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Power liftgate not opening or closing properly, getting stuck, or making unusual noises.
    *   Solution: Adjusting or replacing the liftgate struts or motor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Dashboard Cracking**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Cracking of the dashboard, particularly in warmer climates or with prolonged exposure to sunlight.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the dashboard (often a costly repair). Installation of a dashboard cover or sunshade to prevent further damage.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2019 Dodge Durango faces a range of potential issues. Engine problems, particularly with the 5.7L Hemi V8 (oil consumption) and 3.6L V6 (ticking noises), can be costly to address. Transmission issues, like rough shifting, are also a concern. Electrical problems with the Uconnect system are common, but often resolvable with software updates. While some issues are relatively minor and inexpensive to fix (like sway bar links), others, like dashboard cracking or major engine/transmission repairs, can be significant expenses. Overall, prospective buyers should carefully inspect the vehicle's service history and consider a pre-purchase inspection by a qualified mechanic.
