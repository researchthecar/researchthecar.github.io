**Introduction:**

The 2009 Dodge Durango is a full-size SUV offered with several engine and transmission options. It comes standard with a 4.7L V8 engine producing 303 horsepower and 330 lb-ft of torque. Optional engine choices include a 5.7L Hemi V8 with 390 horsepower and 407 lb-ft of torque and a 3.7L V6 with 210 horsepower and 230 lb-ft of torque. All engines are paired with a five-speed automatic transmission. The Durango is praised for its spacious interior, powerful engines, and towing capacity, while common complaints include a thirsty fuel economy and dated interior design.

**Engine**

**4.7L V8 Engine**

**Intake Manifold Issue**
Description/Symptoms: The plastic intake manifold can crack, causing coolant and vacuum leaks. This can lead to overheating, poor engine performance, and illuminated Check Engine Light.
Solution: Replace the intake manifold.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $1000 - $1500+
Recalls: None Found

**5.7L Hemi V8 Engine**

**Lifter Tick**
Description/Symptoms: Lifters in the engine can become worn and noisy. This creates a ticking sound from the engine.
Solution: Replace the lifters and associated components.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $1500 - $2500+
Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**5-Speed Automatic Transmission**

**Slipping Gears**
Description/Symptoms: The transmission may slip out of gear while driving, causing a loss of power and acceleration.
Solution: Rebuild or replace the transmission.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $2000 - $3000+
Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

**Front Suspension Ball Joints**
Description/Symptoms: The ball joints in the front suspension can wear out, causing clunking noises and reduced steering stability.
Solution: Replace the ball joints.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1000
Recalls: None Found

**Rear Suspension Springs**
Description/Symptoms: The rear suspension springs can sag or break, affecting ride height and handling.
Solution: Replace the rear suspension springs.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600
Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

**Alternator Failure**
Description/Symptoms: The alternator may fail, causing a loss of electrical power and a dead battery.
Solution: Replace the alternator.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800
Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

**Headliner Sagging**
Description/Symptoms: The headliner fabric may sag or detach from the roof.
Solution: Repair or replace the headliner.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600
Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

**Tailgate Corrosion**
Description/Symptoms: The tailgate may rust or corrode over time, especially around the handle area.
Solution: Replace or repair the tailgate.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1000
Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

The 2009 Dodge Durango has several known issues, some of which can be costly to repair. Engine problems, such as intake manifold cracking and lifter tick, are common in the 4.7L and 5.7L engines. The transmission may also experience slipping gears, requiring a rebuild or replacement. Other issues include worn-out ball joints in the front suspension, sagging suspension springs, and alternator failure. Interior problems are limited to headliner sagging, while exterior concerns include tailgate corrosion. Overall, the severity and cost of the issues can vary depending on the specific problem and the extent of the repair required.
