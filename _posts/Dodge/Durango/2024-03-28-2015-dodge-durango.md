The 2015 Dodge Durango is a full-size SUV that offers a combination of power, space, and versatility. It's available with a choice of three engines: a 3.6-liter V6 with 290 horsepower and 260 lb-ft of torque, a 5.7-liter V8 with 360 horsepower and 390 lb-ft of torque, and a 6.4-liter V8 with 475 horsepower and 470 lb-ft of torque. Transmission options include an 8-speed automatic for the V6 and V8 engines, and a 5-speed automatic for the 6.4-liter V8.

People like the Durango for its spacious interior, comfortable ride, and powerful engines. However, it has also been plagued by a number of issues, some of which can be expensive to fix.

**Engine**

* **3.6-liter V6**
    * **Intake manifold failure:** The intake manifold can crack, causing a loss of power and fuel economy. The solution is to replace the intake manifold. Estimated cost to fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
    * **Timing chain failure:** The timing chain can stretch or break, which can cause catastrophic engine damage. The solution is to replace the timing chain and related components. Estimated cost to fix: $2,500 - $4,000+
* **5.7-liter V8**
    * **Valve seat failure:** The valve seats can fail, causing a loss of compression and power. The solution is to replace the valve seats. Estimated cost to fix: $2,000 - $3,000+
    * **Oil pump failure:** The oil pump can fail, causing a loss of oil pressure and lubrication. The solution is to replace the oil pump. Estimated cost to fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
* **6.4-liter V8**
    * **No issues found**

**Transmission**

* **8-speed automatic**
    * **Shifting problems:** The transmission may shift harshly or refuse to shift gears. The solution is to replace the transmission control module or the transmission itself. Estimated cost to fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
    * **Torque converter failure:** The torque converter can fail, causing a loss of power and acceleration. The solution is to replace the torque converter. Estimated cost to fix: $1,200 - $2,000+
* **5-speed automatic**
    * **No issues found**

**Suspension**

* **Front suspension:** The front suspension components can wear out prematurely, causing a loss of ride quality and handling. The solution is to replace the worn components. Estimated cost to fix: $500 - $1,000+
* **Rear suspension:** The rear suspension components can wear out prematurely, causing a loss of ride quality and handling. The solution is to replace the worn components. Estimated cost to fix: $500 - $1,000+

**Electrical**

* **Battery failure:** The battery can fail prematurely, causing the vehicle to lose power. The solution is to replace the battery. Estimated cost to fix: $100 - $200+
* **Alternator failure:** The alternator can fail prematurely, causing the vehicle to lose power. The solution is to replace the alternator. Estimated cost to fix: $500 - $1,000+
* **Starter failure:** The starter can fail prematurely, causing the vehicle to refuse to start. The solution is to replace the starter. Estimated cost to fix: $300 - $500+

**Exterior**

* **Headlight failure:** The headlights can fail prematurely, causing reduced visibility at night. The solution is to replace the headlights. Estimated cost to fix: $200 - $500+
* **Taillight failure:** The taillights can fail prematurely, causing reduced visibility at night. The solution is to replace the taillights. Estimated cost to fix: $100 - $300+
* **Mirror failure:** The mirrors can fail prematurely, causing reduced visibility. The solution is to replace the mirrors. Estimated cost to fix: $200 - $400+

**Interior**

* **Climate control failure:** The climate control system can fail prematurely, causing the vehicle to lose heat or air conditioning. The solution is to replace the climate control module or the entire climate control system. Estimated cost to fix: $500 - $1,500+
* **Navigation system failure:** The navigation system can fail prematurely, causing the vehicle to lose navigation capabilities. The solution is to replace the navigation system. Estimated cost to fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
* **Radio failure:** The radio can fail prematurely, causing the vehicle to lose audio capabilities. The solution is to replace the radio. Estimated cost to fix: $300 - $800+

**Summary**

The 2015 Dodge Durango is a powerful and spacious SUV, but it has also been plagued by a number of issues, some of which can be expensive to fix. The most serious issues include intake manifold failure, timing chain failure, valve seat failure, oil pump failure, and transmission problems. If you're considering buying a 2015 Dodge Durango, be sure to do your research and be prepared for the possibility of expensive repairs.
