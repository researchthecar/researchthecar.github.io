# 2017 Dodge Durango Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2017 Dodge Durango is a muscular and capable midsize SUV that offers strong towing capabilities and spacious three-row seating. However, potential buyers should carefully evaluate common owner-reported reliability concerns, particularly relating to exterior lighting, interior safety features, and powertrain accessories.

---

## Engine

### 3.6L V6 295 hp

The standard 3.6-liter Pentastar V6 produces 295 horsepower and 260 lb-ft of torque, offering a balanced mix of everyday drivability and respectable fuel economy for daily commuting.

### 5.7L HEMI V8 360 hp

The optional 5.7-liter HEMI V8 delivers a robust 360 horsepower and 390 lb-ft of torque, providing exceptional towing capability and traditional V8 performance, though it requires attention to cooling system maintenance.

**Coolant Leak and Overheating**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle overheats unexpectedly while idling in traffic, accompanied by coolant pooling under the vehicle and a dashboard temperature warning.
- **Solution:** Inspect cooling lines, water pump, and radiator assembly; replace compromised hoses or leaking components.

**Fuel Pump Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Engine cranks but fails to start, or stalls unexpectedly while driving due to fuel delivery interruption.
- **Solution:** Replace the faulty fuel pump assembly inside the fuel tank.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

Paired with both engine options, the ZF-sourced 8-speed automatic transmission delivers smooth, responsive gear changes and helps optimize highway fuel efficiency across all trim levels.

## Exterior

### Exterior Lighting Assembly

Exterior lighting issues are heavily concentrated around rear light seal failures, which can prove costly to resolve out of warranty.

**Tail Light Water Intrusion**
- **Symptoms:** Moisture accumulation inside the rear 'racetrack' light bar, leading to LED burnout, partial failure of running lights, and electrical short circuits.
- **Solution:** Replace the entire rear light assembly with an updated unit to prevent future moisture intrusion.

## Safety

### Active Headrest

Cabin comfort is generally high, though unexpected mechanical deployment of the active safety headrests remains a notable nuisance.

**Inadvertent Headrest Deployment**
- **Symptoms:** Front seat active head restraint deploys violently forward without warning or vehicle impact, potentially startling the driver or passengers.
- **Solution:** Inspect and replace the internal retaining clips or replace the damaged headrest assembly.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V332000: Electrical System:Wiring](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Dodge&model=Durango&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** These vehicles are being recalled to address a defect that could prevent the cruise control system from disengaging. If, when using cruise control, there is a short circuit within the vehicle's wiring, the driver may not be able to shut off the cruise control either by depressing the brake pedal or manually turning the system off once it has been engaged.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the software, and perform a software flash on the engine or powertrain control module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V541000: Power Train:Axle Assembly:Axle Shaft](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Dodge&model=Durango&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** If a halfshaft is too short, it may disengage from the differential causing a loss of drive and increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Chrysler has notified owners, and dealers will replace both halfshafts, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2017 Dodge Durango remains an attractive option for buyers seeking utility, strong engine choices, and robust towing performance. However, shoppers should thoroughly inspect the vehicle for common pain points—specifically checking the rear racetrack tail lights for internal moisture and verifying that the cooling system and headrests are functioning properly before completing a purchase.
