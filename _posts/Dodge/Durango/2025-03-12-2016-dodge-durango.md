Here's a breakdown of common issues and complaints associated with the 2016 Dodge Durango, categorized by major components and engine/transmission options. The 2016 Dodge Durango is a popular SUV known for its roomy interior, powerful engine options, and towing capability. Owners often praise its comfortable ride and user-friendly infotainment system. However, some common complaints include electrical issues, specifically with the Uconnect system, and occasional reports of drivetrain problems. The 2016 Durango offered three engine options: a 3.6L Pentastar V6 (290-295 hp, 260 lb-ft of torque), a 5.7L Hemi V8 (360 hp, 390 lb-ft of torque), and a 6.4L Hemi V8 (475 hp, 470 lb-ft of torque). All engines are paired with an 8-speed automatic transmission.

**Engine**

**3.6L Pentastar V6**

*   **Tick Noise**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners report a noticeable ticking noise coming from the engine, especially at idle or low speeds. This may be caused by valve train components, potentially the rocker arms or lifters.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis to identify the exact source of the ticking, which may involve replacing the rocker arms or lifters.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Oil Filter Housing Leak**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaking.
    *   Solution: Replace the oil filter housing.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**5.7L Hemi V8**

*   **Lifter Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Ticking noise that worsens with engine temperature
    *   Solution: Replace the failed lifter(s) and potentially the camshaft if damaged.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1500 - $3000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**6.4L Hemi V8**

*   **Lifter Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Ticking noise that worsens with engine temperature
    *   Solution: Replace the failed lifter(s) and potentially the camshaft if damaged.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1500 - $3000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**8-Speed Automatic Transmission (All Engine Options)**

*   **Harsh Shifting/Hesitation**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners experience harsh or erratic shifting, particularly between certain gears. This can also manifest as hesitation during acceleration.
    *   Solution: Transmission software update, valve body replacement, or in severe cases, transmission replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300 (software update), $800 - $1500 (valve body), $3000 - $6000 (transmission replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Rear Shock Absorber Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Premature wear/failure of the rear shock absorbers.
    *   Solution: Replace the rear shock absorbers.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $700
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Uconnect System Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The Uconnect infotainment system can experience various glitches, including freezing, slow response times, screen blackouts, and Bluetooth connectivity problems.
    *   Solution: Software updates, system resets, or in some cases, replacement of the Uconnect module.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 (software update), $100 - $300 (system reset/diagnosis), $500 - $1500+ (module replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Power Liftgate Malfunction**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The power liftgate may fail to open or close properly, get stuck, or exhibit erratic behavior.
    *   Solution: Check and replace the liftgate struts, motor, or control module.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+ depending on the component.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **TPMS Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: The tire pressure monitoring system may not work correctly or display incorrect tire pressures.
    * Solution: Replace or reprogram the TPMS sensors.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $150+ per sensor (replacement), $50 - $100 (reprogramming)
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **No Issues Found**

**Interior**

*   **Seat Heater Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Seat heaters not working
    * Solution: Replace heater element
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2016 Dodge Durango has some common issues that potential buyers should be aware of. The most prevalent concern the electrical system, specifically the Uconnect infotainment system, which can be frustrating due to its glitches. Engine ticking and potential lifter failures, particularly with the V8 engines, can lead to more significant repair costs. While the transmission generally performs well, some owners report harsh shifting or hesitation. Overall, while the Durango offers a lot in terms of space, power, and comfort, it's essential to be aware of these potential issues and have a pre-purchase inspection performed to assess the vehicle's condition. The severity of these issues ranges from minor inconveniences to potentially expensive repairs, so careful consideration and preventative maintenance are crucial.
