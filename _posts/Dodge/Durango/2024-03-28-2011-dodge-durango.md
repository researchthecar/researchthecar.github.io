**Introduction:**
The 2011 Dodge Durango is a mid-size SUV with five trim levels: Express, Heat, Crew, R/T, and Citadel. It came with two engine options: a 3.6L Pentastar V6 and a 5.7L HEMI V8. The Pentastar V6 produced 290 horsepower and 260 lb-ft of torque, while the HEMI V8 produced 360 horsepower and 390 lb-ft of torque. Transmission options included a 5-speed automatic for the V6 and a 6-speed automatic for the V8.

Durango owners appreciate its spacious interior, powerful engines, and impressive towing capacity. However, they have also reported several issues with the vehicle.

**Engine**
* **3.6L Pentastar V6**
  * **Intake Manifold Gasket Leak:**
    Description/Symptoms: A leak develops between the intake manifold and cylinder head, causing a loss of coolant and reduced engine performance.
    Solution: Replace the intake manifold gasket and any damaged components.
    Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
  * **Exhaust Manifold Gasket Leak:**
    Description/Symptoms: A leak develops between the exhaust manifold and cylinder head, causing a loss of power and increased exhaust noise.
    Solution: Replace the exhaust manifold gasket and any damaged components.
    Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $1,200+
  * **Timing Chain Tensioner Failure:**
    Description/Symptoms: The timing chain tensioner fails, causing the timing chain to skip or break, leading to catastrophic engine damage.
    Solution: Replace the timing chain, tensioner, and related components.
    Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+
* **5.7L HEMI V8**
  * **No Issues Found**

**Transmission**
* **5-Speed Automatic (V6)**
  * **Transmission Slipping:**
    Description/Symptoms: The transmission slips or hesitates when shifting gears, causing a loss of power and drivability issues.
    Solution: Rebuild or replace the transmission.
    Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,500 - $4,000+
* **6-Speed Automatic (V8)**
  * **Torque Converter Failure:**
    Description/Symptoms: The torque converter fails, causing a loss of drive power and a distinct shudder or vibration.
    Solution: Replace the torque converter and transmission fluid.
    Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,500+

**Suspension**
* **Front Suspension Strut Mount Failure:**
    Description/Symptoms: The front suspension strut mounts fail, causing excessive tire wear and a noisy ride.
    Solution: Replace the strut mounts and hardware.
    Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $700+
* **Rear Suspension Coil Spring Sag:**
    Description/Symptoms: The rear suspension coil springs sag, causing a loss of ride height and handling issues.
    Solution: Replace the coil springs and any damaged components.
    Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $1,000+

**Electrical**
* **Electrical Short in Headlight Wiring Harness:**
    Description/Symptoms: An electrical short develops in the headlight wiring harness, causing premature bulb failure and potential electrical problems.
    Solution: Repair or replace the damaged wiring harness.
    Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600+

**Exterior**
* **Sunroof Panel Separation:**
    Description/Symptoms: The sunroof panel separates from the roofline, allowing water to leak into the cabin.
    Solution: Reattach the sunroof panel and seal any gaps.
    Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,000+

**Interior**
* **Front Door Lock Actuator Failure:**
    Description/Symptoms: The front door lock actuators fail, making it difficult or impossible to open or close the door.
    Solution: Replace the lock actuator and related components.
    Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
* **Instrument Cluster Malfunction:**
    Description/Symptoms: The instrument cluster malfunctions, causing inaccurate readings or complete failure of the gauges.
    Solution: Repair or replace the instrument cluster.
    Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,200+

**Summary**
The 2011 Dodge Durango has several reported issues that can be costly to repair. The intake manifold gasket leak is the most common issue, followed by the timing chain tensioner failure and the transmission slipping. Suspension issues can also be expensive to remedy, while the electrical and interior problems are typically less severe and less expensive to fix.
