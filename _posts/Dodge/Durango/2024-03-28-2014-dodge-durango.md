**Introduction**

The 2014 Dodge Durango is a full-size SUV available with various engine and transmission options. It offers spacious seating, a powerful V8 engine, and excellent towing capacity. However, like all vehicles, the Durango has its share of reported issues.

**Engine**

**5.7L V8**

* **Cylinder Head Gasket Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Engine overheating, coolant leaks, white smoke from exhaust
    * Solution: Replace cylinder head gasket
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Timing Chain Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Engine rattling, loss of power, check engine light
    * Solution: Replace timing chain and gears
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $1,500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**3.6L V6**

* **Oil Consumption Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: Excessive oil consumption, low oil levels
    * Solution: Piston ring replacement or engine overhaul
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**8-Speed Automatic Transmission**

* **Harsh Shifting**
    * Description/Symptoms: Transmission shifts abruptly, especially during low speeds
    * Solution: Transmission fluid change or software update
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Rear Sway Bar Link Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Clunking or popping noises from suspension, poor handling
    * Solution: Replace rear sway bar links
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
* **Front Control Arm Bushing Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Vibration during braking or acceleration, uneven tire wear
    * Solution: Replace control arm bushings
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $250 - $500

**Electrical**

* **Alternator Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Battery discharge, dim headlights, flickering dashboard lights
    * Solution: Replace alternator
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800
* **Headlight Condensation**
    * Description/Symptoms: Moisture inside headlight assemblies
    * Solution: Replace headlight assemblies
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500

**Exterior**

* **Tailgate Rust**
    * Description/Symptoms: Rust forming around the tailgate handle or license plate area
    * Solution: Replace tailgate
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000

**Interior**

* **Dash Rattle**
    * Description/Symptoms: Rattling or buzzing noises coming from the dashboard
    * Solution: Tighten loose panels or apply sound deadening material
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200
* **Sunroof Leaks**
    * Description/Symptoms: Water leaks into the interior through the sunroof
    * Solution: Adjust sunroof alignment or replace sunroof seals
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600

**Summary**

While the 2014 Dodge Durango has its share of reported issues, most are relatively minor and inexpensive to fix. However, more serious problems like cylinder head gasket failure and timing chain failure can be costly. Overall, the Durango remains a popular choice among full-size SUV buyers, offering a good balance of power, space, and features.
