**Introduction:**

The 2008 Dodge Durango is a mid-size SUV that offers a variety of engine and transmission options. It was available with a 3.7L V6 engine, a 4.7L V8 engine, a 5.7L V8 engine, and a 6-speed automatic transmission or a 5-speed automatic transmission. The Durango is known for its spacious interior, powerful engines, and towing capacity, but it has also been plagued by a number of issues.

**Engine**

**3.7L V6 Engine**

* **Intake manifold failure:** The intake manifold can crack and cause a vacuum leak, which can lead to a loss of power, engine overheating, and other problems. The solution is to replace the intake manifold. Estimated cost to fix: $1,000-$2,000
* **Cylinder head failure:** The cylinder head can crack and cause a loss of coolant, which can lead to engine damage. The solution is to replace the cylinder head. Estimated cost to fix: $2,000-$3,000

**4.7L V8 Engine**

* **Timing chain failure:** The timing chain can fail and cause the engine to lose power or stall. The solution is to replace the timing chain and gears. Estimated cost to fix: $1,500-$2,500

**5.7L V8 Engine**

* **Valve seat failure:** The valve seats can fail and cause a loss of compression, which can lead to a loss of power and engine damage. The solution is to replace the valve seats. Estimated cost to fix: $2,000-$3,000
* **Piston ring failure:** The piston rings can fail and cause excessive oil consumption. The solution is to replace the piston rings. Estimated cost to fix: $1,000-$2,000

**Transmission**

**6-Speed Automatic Transmission**

* **Torque converter failure:** The torque converter can fail and cause the transmission to slip or shudder. The solution is to replace the torque converter. Estimated cost to fix: $1,500-$2,500
* **Transmission control module failure:** The transmission control module can fail and cause the transmission to shift erratically or not shift at all. The solution is to replace the transmission control module. Estimated cost to fix: $500-$1,000

**5-Speed Automatic Transmission**

* **Input shaft bearing failure:** The input shaft bearing can fail and cause a grinding noise when shifting gears. The solution is to replace the input shaft bearing. Estimated cost to fix: $500-$1,000
* **Output shaft seal failure:** The output shaft seal can fail and cause a leak in the transmission pan. The solution is to replace the output shaft seal. Estimated cost to fix: $100-$200

**Suspension**

* **Ball joint failure:** The ball joints can fail and cause a clunking noise when turning or hitting bumps. The solution is to replace the ball joints. Estimated cost to fix: $200-$400 per joint
* **Control arm failure:** The control arms can fail and cause the wheels to go out of alignment. The solution is to replace the control arms. Estimated cost to fix: $500-$1,000 per arm
* **Sway bar link failure:** The sway bar links can fail and cause a clunking noise when turning or hitting bumps. The solution is to replace the sway bar links. Estimated cost to fix: $100-$200 per link

**Electrical**

* **Alternator failure:** The alternator can fail and cause the battery to discharge. The solution is to replace the alternator. Estimated cost to fix: $500-$1,000
* **Battery failure:** The battery can fail and cause the vehicle to not start. The solution is to replace the battery. Estimated cost to fix: $100-$200
* **Electrical harness failure:** The electrical harness can fail and cause a variety of electrical problems, such as lights not working, windows not rolling up or down, or the engine not starting. The solution is to replace the damaged part of the electrical harness. Estimated cost to fix: $500-$1,500

**Exterior**

* **Roof rack crossbars failure:** The roof rack crossbars can fail and break off. The solution is to replace the roof rack crossbars. Estimated cost to fix: $100-$200
* **Tailgate handle failure:** The tailgate handle can fail and break off. The solution is to replace the tailgate handle. Estimated cost to fix: $100-$200

**Interior**

* **Heater core failure:** The heater core can fail and leak coolant into the passenger compartment. The solution is to replace the heater core. Estimated cost to fix: $500-$1,000
* **Air conditioning condenser failure:** The air conditioning condenser can fail and cause the air conditioning to not work. The solution is to replace the air conditioning condenser. Estimated cost to fix: $500-$1,000
* **Instrument cluster failure:** The instrument cluster can fail and cause the gauges to not work. The solution is to replace the instrument cluster. Estimated cost to fix: $500-$1,000

**Summary**

The 2008 Dodge Durango has a number of potential issues, including engine failures, transmission failures, and electrical problems. These issues can be expensive to fix, and some of them can lead to serious safety concerns. While the Durango is a popular SUV, consumers should be aware of the potential issues before they purchase one.
