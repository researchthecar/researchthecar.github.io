# 2021 Dodge Durango

### Overview
The 2021 Dodge Durango, a popular three-row SUV, generally offers a robust and reliable performance, particularly with its potent engine options. Owners often praise its towing capacity and comfortable ride. While most owners report a positive ownership experience, like any vehicle, there are specific areas where minor concerns have been noted. This analysis focuses on documented technical issues beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential areas of attention for the various powertrain configurations and other key vehicle systems. The Durango's build quality and engineering contribute to its overall dependability for families and those needing substantial utility.

### Summary Verdict
The 2021 Dodge Durango demonstrates strong overall reliability, especially concerning its core powertrain components across all engine and transmission options. The Pentastar V6 and HEMI V8 engines are generally robust, and the 8-speed automatic transmission performs dependably. The primary areas of concern are minor electrical issues, such as occasional infotainment system glitches and intermittent battery drain, which can be resolved with software updates or component diagnosis. Other categories like suspension, exterior, and interior show no widespread technical problems. For potential buyers, focusing on a thorough pre-purchase inspection, particularly for the electrical system, is advisable.
