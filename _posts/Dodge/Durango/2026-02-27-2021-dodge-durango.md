# 2021 Dodge Durango Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used Dodge Durango, buyers generally find a muscular, spacious midsize SUV with strong towing capabilities and aggressive styling. Across its model run, owners have reported certain recurring mechanical and electrical concerns that warrant a careful pre-purchase inspection. Knowing the specific engine and transmission combinations, along with common trouble spots, can help you navigate the used market confidently.

---

## Engine

### 3.6L Pentastar V6

The 3.6-liter Pentastar V6 produces around 295 horsepower and 260 lb-ft of torque, serving as the standard baseline engine for the Durango. It offers adequate daily driving performance and decent fuel efficiency, though routine maintenance of the cooling and ignition systems is key.

### 5.7L HEMI V8

The 5.7-liter HEMI V8 pushes out 360 horsepower and 390 lb-ft of torque, providing robust towing capacity and strong acceleration. While generally durable, buyers should inspect for coolant leaks and valve train health during a test drive.

**Coolant Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Engine overheating warnings, low coolant levels, or visible puddles underneath the vehicle.
- **Solution:** Inspect coolant lines, hoses, and rear heater connections; replace any degraded or leaking components.

### 6.4L HEMI V8 (SRT 392)

The 6.4-liter HEMI V8 delivers an impressive 475 horsepower and 470 lb-ft of torque, tailored for performance enthusiasts. It transforms the Durango into a high-speed sport utility but demands strict adherence to maintenance schedules.

### 6.2L Supercharged HEMI V8 (SRT Hellcat)

The supercharged 6.2-liter HEMI V8 generates a massive 710 horsepower, offering extreme track-capable performance. This low-volume variant is exceptionally powerful, though tires and powertrain components endure heavy stress.

## Transmission

### TorqueFlite 8-Speed Automatic

Paired across all engine options, the ZF-sourced 8-speed automatic transmission delivers crisp, smooth shifts and optimized fuel economy. While generally reliable, intermittent electronic selector messages and slip complaints have been noted by some owners.

**Shifter and Gear Engagement Faults**
- **Symptoms:** Dashboard warnings instructing the driver to switch to park or select a gear, unexpected acceleration slip, or failure to shift out of gear.
- **Solution:** Check electronic shifter module software, selector switch continuity, and transmission control module diagnostics.

## Exterior

### Exterior Lighting and Assemblies

Electrical complaints frequently center on exterior lighting malfunctions and moisture intrusion in rear lamp housings. Thoroughly testing all lights before purchase is recommended.

**Headlight and Daytime Running Light Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Premature burnout or intermittent operation of front headlights, LED daytime running lights, or tail light assemblies.
- **Solution:** Inspect wiring harnesses, connectors, and replace faulty lighting modules or entire assemblies as needed.

**Tail Light Water Intrusion**
- **Symptoms:** Moisture accumulation inside the rear racetrack taillight assembly, leading to short-circuited LEDs and partial light failure.
- **Solution:** Replace compromised gaskets or install an updated rear lamp assembly with improved seals.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V842000: Structure:Body](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Dodge&model=Durango&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The rear spoiler may not be aligned properly with the roof, resulting in damage to the spoiler during liftgate use. A damaged rear spoiler could detach and become a road hazard, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the rear spoiler, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V280000: Air Bags:Critical Fasteners](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Dodge&model=Durango&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** A loose or missing fastener may cause the side curtain air bag to deploy incorrectly, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will tighten or replace the fastener, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V140000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Antilock/Traction Control/Electronic Limited Slip:Control Unit/Module](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Dodge&model=Durango&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 126, "Electronic Stability Control Systems." An ESC malfunction indicator light that fails to illuminate during an electronic stability control problem can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the ABS control module software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V154000: Steering:Rack And Pinion](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Dodge&model=Durango&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** Difficulty steering, or a loss of steering control, can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the steering gear assembly, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V284000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Exhaust System:Emission Control:Gas Recirculation Valve (Egr Valve)](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Dodge&model=Durango&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** An unexpected loss of drive power increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the EGR valve assembly, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V115000: Structure:Body:Hatchback/Liftgate](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Dodge&model=Durango&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The rear spoiler may not be aligned properly with the roof, resulting in damage to the spoiler during liftgate use. A damaged rear spoiler can detach and become a road hazard, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the rear spoiler, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V436000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Dodge&model=Durango&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 111, "Rear Visibility." A rearview camera image that does not display decreases the driver's visibility, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the radio software or the software will be OTA, "Over-The-Air," free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The Dodge Durango offers a compelling blend of utility, powerful engine choices, and confident handling for used-car buyers. However, prospective owners should pay close attention to exterior lighting operation, check for any signs of moisture inside tail light assemblies, and verify smooth transmission engagement during a test drive. Conducting a thorough pre-purchase inspection will help ensure you select a well-maintained example.
