**Introduction:**

The 2022 Dodge Durango is a full-size SUV with a powerful lineup of engine and transmission options. It offers the 3.6L Pentastar V6 with an 8-speed automatic transmission, a 5.7L HEMI V8 with an 8-speed automatic transmission, and a 6.4L HEMI V8 with an 8-speed automatic transmission. The Durango is praised for its spacious cabin, robust performance, and modern features. However, like any vehicle, it has its share of reported issues.

### Engine

**3.6L Pentastar V6**
- **Cylinder Deactivation Issues:**
  - Description/Symptoms: Engine misfires, rough idling, and reduced fuel efficiency.
  - Solution: Replace affected engine components, update software.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+
  - Recalls: None Found
- **Timing Chain Failure:**
  - Description/Symptoms: Rattle or ticking noise, check engine light, loss of power.
  - Solution: Replace timing chain, tensioners, and related components.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,500+
  - Recalls: None Found

**5.7L HEMI V8**
- **Hydraulic Lifter Failure:**
  - Description/Symptoms: Ticking noise, reduced engine power.
  - Solution: Replace hydraulic lifters and associated components.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,500+
  - Recalls: None Found

**6.4L HEMI V8**
- **Valve Spring Failure:**
  - Description/Symptoms: Loud ticking noise, loss of power, engine damage.
  - Solution: Replace affected valve springs and related components.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,500 - $5,000+
  - Recalls: None Found

### Transmission

**8-Speed Automatic Transmission**
- **Shifting Hesitation:**
  - Description/Symptoms: Transmission slips, delayed response to gear changes.
  - Solution: Update transmission software, replace transmission solenoids or valve body.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+
  - Recalls: None Found
- **Torque Converter Shudder:**
  - Description/Symptoms: Vibration during acceleration, especially at low speeds.
  - Solution: Replace torque converter or transmission fluid.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
  - Recalls: None Found

### Suspension

- **Front Stabilizer Bar Link Failure:**
  - Description/Symptoms: Clunking or rattling noise when turning, reduced stability.
  - Solution: Replace affected stabilizer bar links.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
  - Recalls: None Found

### Electrical

- **Battery Drain:**
  - Description/Symptoms: Battery dies prematurely, even with new battery.
  - Solution: Diagnose and replace defective electrical component (e.g., parasitic draw).
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,500+
  - Recalls: None Found
- **Lighting Issues:**
  - Description/Symptoms: Headlights, taillights, or other lighting components malfunctioning.
  - Solution: Replace bulbs, fuses, or wiring as needed.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
  - Recalls: None Found

### Exterior

- **Liftgate Latch Failure:**
  - Description/Symptoms: Liftgate opens unexpectedly or fails to open properly.
  - Solution: Replace liftgate latch or mechanism.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
  - Recalls: None Found

### Interior

- **Infotainment Screen Lag:**
  - Description/Symptoms: Touchscreen display freezes or responds slowly.
  - Solution: Update software, replace infotainment module.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+
  - Recalls: None Found

### Summary

The 2022 Dodge Durango has been reported to have some issues, particularly with the engine and transmission. However, it's important to note that not all vehicles will experience these problems, and the severity and cost of repairs can vary depending on the specific issue and individual vehicle. It's always recommended to have any issues diagnosed and repaired by a qualified mechanic to ensure optimal performance and longevity of your vehicle.
