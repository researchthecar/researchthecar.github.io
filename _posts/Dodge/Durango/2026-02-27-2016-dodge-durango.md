# 2016 Dodge Durango

### Overview
The 2016 Dodge Durango is a capable and versatile SUV, offering robust performance and a comfortable ride. It generally presents a solid reliability record, particularly with its well-regarded powertrain options. Owners often praise its spacious interior and towing capacity. While most owners report a positive experience, like any vehicle, there are specific areas that have seen more attention from a reliability standpoint. This analysis focuses on documented technical issues beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential concerns for prospective buyers and current owners of the 2016 model year.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic (845RE)
**Hesitation/Shuddering**
- **Symptoms:** Occasional transmission shudder or hesitation during gear changes, particularly at lower speeds.
- **Solution:** Software updates for the transmission control module (TCM) are often the first step. In more severe cases, fluid changes or internal transmission repairs may be necessary.
- **Cost:** $150 - $1200

## Electrical

### Infotainment System
**Uconnect System Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Occasional freezing, unresponsiveness, or Bluetooth connectivity issues with the Uconnect infotainment system.
- **Solution:** Software updates for the Uconnect system can resolve many glitches. In some instances, a system reset or replacement of the module may be required.
- **Cost:** $100 - $800


### Battery Drain
**Intermittent Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Some owners have reported intermittent battery drain issues, leading to a dead battery.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis to identify the parasitic draw is crucial. This could involve faulty modules, aftermarket accessories, or wiring issues.
- **Cost:** $150 - $600

### Summary Verdict
The 2016 Dodge Durango is generally considered a reliable vehicle, especially concerning its powertrain options. The Pentastar V6 and HEMI V8 engines have proven to be robust with no widespread significant issues. The primary areas that have seen some owner reports relate to the 8-speed automatic transmission, which can occasionally exhibit shuddering or hesitation, often resolvable with software updates or fluid services. Electrical gremlins, particularly with the Uconnect infotainment system and occasional battery drain, are also noted but typically manageable. Overall, the Durango offers a dependable experience for its class, with most potential issues being relatively minor and addressable.
