# 2016 Dodge Durango Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2016 Dodge Durango is a capable and muscular three-row SUV offering strong towing capacity and a comfortable ride. However, prospective used buyers should review common owner complaints, particularly concerning electrical issues, exterior lighting water intrusion, and transmission behavior.

---

## Engine

### 3.6L V6

The 3.6-liter Pentastar V6 produces approximately 295 horsepower and provides a solid balance of everyday performance and efficiency for the Durango.

**Fuel Pump Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Fuel pump failure leading to vehicle stalling or failure to start.
- **Solution:** Replace the faulty fuel pump assembly.

### 5.7L HEMI V8

The 5.7-liter HEMI V8 generates 360 horsepower, offering robust towing capacity and strong acceleration for drivers needing maximum capability.

**Unexpected RPM Drop**
- **Symptoms:** Sudden unexpected decrease in engine RPM resulting in a temporary loss of vehicle speed while driving.
- **Solution:** Diagnose electronic throttle control, fuel delivery, or powertrain control module issues.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

The ZF-sourced 8-speed automatic transmission delivers smooth gear changes and helps optimize fuel economy across both engine options, though some owners report harsh low-speed downshifts.

**Rough Downshifts**
- **Symptoms:** Hard or jerky downshifting from 2nd to 1st gear, causing the vehicle to lurch forward when coming to a stop.
- **Solution:** Reprogram or update the powertrain control module software, or inspect internal valve body components.

## Electrical

### Shifter and Fuse Box

Cabin electronics and shifter-related warnings can occasionally display false alerts or indicate mechanical parking status issues.

**Shift to Park Warning and Electrical Short**
- **Symptoms:** Error messages reading 'Shift to P before changing gears', intermittent failure to register park, or specific fuses melting in the fuse box.
- **Solution:** Inspect the shifter assembly microswitches and wiring harness, or replace damaged fuse blocks.

## Exterior

### Exterior Lighting & Racetrack Taillights

Electrical complaints often center around exterior lighting seals allowing water intrusion into the distinctive rear LED assemblies.

**Tail Light Water Intrusion**
- **Symptoms:** Moisture and water pooling inside the rear LED racetrack taillight assembly, causing segments to fail or completely short out running and brake lights.
- **Solution:** Replace the compromised taillight assembly with an updated or properly sealed unit.
- **Cost:** Over $1,500 for full assembly replacement

## Safety

### Active Headrests

Safety concerns primarily focus on occupant restraint components like active headrests experiencing premature internal plastic wear.

**Inadvertent Headrest Deployment**
- **Symptoms:** The active front headrest deploys unexpectedly while driving or while parked, due to internal plastic retaining clip degradation.
- **Solution:** Replace the damaged active headrest mechanism.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V332000: Electrical System:Wiring](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Dodge&model=Durango&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** These vehicles are being recalled to address a defect that could prevent the cruise control system from disengaging. If, when using cruise control, there is a short circuit within the vehicle's wiring, the driver may not be able to shut off the cruise control either by depressing the brake pedal or manually turning the system off once it has been engaged.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the software, and perform a software flash on the engine or powertrain control module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V814000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Hoses, Lines/Piping, And Fittings](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Dodge&model=Durango&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** A fuel leak in the presence of an ignition source, can increase the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the engine assembly for damage to the fuel rail crossover tube, replacing it as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V168000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Foundation Components:Disc:Caliper](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Dodge&model=Durango&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** A cracked brake caliper may lengthen the distance needed to stop the vehicle and increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the front left brake caliper and depending on its casting date, replace it, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2016 Dodge Durango remains a compelling choice for families seeking utility and performance, provided it receives a thorough pre-purchase inspection. Buyers should pay close attention to rear LED taillight condition, transmission shift quality at low speeds, and the functionality of interior electronics.
