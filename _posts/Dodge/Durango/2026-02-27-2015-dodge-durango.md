# 2015 Dodge Durango

### Overview
The 2015 Dodge Durango is a capable and spacious SUV that offers a comfortable ride and strong performance, particularly with its available V8 engine. Reliability for this model year is generally considered good, with most owners reporting satisfaction. However, like any vehicle, there are specific areas that have seen more attention from mechanics and owners. This report focuses on documented technical issues beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential concerns for the 2015 Durango across its various configurations.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic (845RE)
**Hesitation/Shuddering**
- **Symptoms:** Transmission may hesitate during acceleration or exhibit a slight shudder, particularly at lower speeds. Some instances of rough shifting have been reported.
- **Solution:** Software updates for the transmission control module (TCM) are often the first step. In more severe cases, fluid changes or internal component inspection may be necessary.
- **Cost:** $150 - $500

## Electrical

### General Electrical System
**Uconnect System Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Infotainment system (Uconnect) can occasionally freeze, become unresponsive, or experience Bluetooth connectivity issues.
- **Solution:** Software updates for the Uconnect system are typically available. A system reset or, in rare cases, replacement of the module may be required.
- **Cost:** $100 - $400

**Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Some owners have reported premature battery drain, potentially linked to parasitic draw from certain modules.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis to identify the source of the parasitic draw is crucial. This may involve checking modules, wiring, or aftermarket accessories.
- **Cost:** $100 - $300

### Summary Verdict
The 2015 Dodge Durango generally presents a solid reliability record, especially considering its size and capabilities. The powertrain options, the 3.6L V6 and 5.7L V8, are robust and do not have widespread critical failures reported. The primary areas of concern tend to be minor, such as occasional glitches with the Uconnect infotainment system and some reports of transmission hesitation, which can often be resolved with software updates or routine maintenance. Battery drain is another less common but noted issue. Overall, the Durango is a dependable choice for families and those needing towing capacity, with most potential issues being manageable and not indicative of systemic design flaws.
