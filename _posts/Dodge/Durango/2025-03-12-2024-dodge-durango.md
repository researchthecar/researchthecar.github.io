The 2024 Dodge Durango is a popular SUV known for its muscular styling, powerful engine options, and impressive towing capacity. It offers a blend of performance and practicality, making it a favorite among families and enthusiasts alike. Engine options include the 3.6L Pentastar V6 (295 hp, 260 lb-ft of torque), the 5.7L Hemi V8 (360 hp, 390 lb-ft of torque), and the formidable 6.4L Hemi V8 (475 hp, 470 lb-ft of torque) found in the SRT 392. All engines are paired with an 8-speed automatic transmission. People often praise the Durango's spacious interior, comfortable ride, and aggressive appearance. Common criticisms include fuel economy (particularly with the V8 engines) and some aspects of the interior material quality compared to competitors.

**Transmission**

*All Engine Options are Paired to the Same 8-Speed Automatic Transmission*

*   **Harsh or Delayed Shifting**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners report experiencing harsh or delayed shifts, particularly between lower gears. This can manifest as a noticeable "clunk" or hesitation during acceleration.
    *   Solution: A software update to the transmission control module (TCM) may resolve the issue. In some cases, a more thorough inspection and potential valve body replacement may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+ (software update), $500 - $1500+ (valve body replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **No Issues Found**

**Electrical**

*   **Uconnect System Freezing or Crashing**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The Uconnect infotainment system may freeze, crash, or become unresponsive. This can affect navigation, audio, climate control, and other vehicle functions.
    *   Solution: Performing a hard reset of the Uconnect system may temporarily resolve the issue. A software update from the dealer may be required for a permanent fix. In some instances, hardware replacement may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 (hard reset), $0 - $200 (software update), $500 - $1500+ (hardware replacement, if needed)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Backup Camera Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The backup camera may fail to display an image, show a distorted image, or experience intermittent functionality.
    *   Solution: Check the camera lens for obstructions. Ensure the wiring harness connecting the camera to the Uconnect system is secure. A software update may be necessary. If the camera itself is defective, replacement is required.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 (cleaning lens), $0 - $200 (software update), $200 - $500+ (camera replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Rattling Noises from Dashboard or Trim**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Owners may experience rattling noises originating from the dashboard, door panels, or other interior trim pieces, particularly on rough roads.
    *   Solution: Identifying the source of the rattle and securing the affected trim piece can resolve the issue. This may involve adding padding, tightening screws, or replacing clips.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $300+ (depending on the complexity and dealer labor rates)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2024 Dodge Durango appears to be a relatively reliable vehicle. The most common issues reported involve the 8-speed automatic transmission (harsh shifting) and the Uconnect infotainment system (freezing/crashing). Interior rattles are also noted. While the issues aren't widespread, they can be annoying and potentially costly to repair, particularly if they require hardware replacement. Potential buyers should ensure that the Uconnect system is up-to-date and carefully test the transmission during a test drive.
