# 2024 Dodge Durango Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2024 Dodge Durango continues to offer muscular performance, bold styling, and impressive towing capacity for SUV shoppers. However, early owner reports indicate some potential electronic, shifter, and driver-assistance glitches that prospective buyers should keep in mind.

---

## Engine

### 3.6L Pentastar V6

The standard 3.6-liter Pentastar V6 produces 293 horsepower and 260 lb-ft of torque, offering a balanced mix of everyday drivability and efficiency. It serves as the base engine across lower trims.

### 5.7L HEMI V8

The 5.7-liter HEMI V8 generates 360 horsepower and 390 lb-ft of torque, providing robust towing capability and strong acceleration. It is a popular choice for buyers looking for traditional V8 muscle.

### 6.4L HEMI V8

Found in the SRT 392, the 6.4-liter HEMI V8 pumps out an impressive 475 horsepower and 470 lb-ft of torque for high-performance driving. It delivers thrilling speed at the expense of fuel economy.

### 6.2L Supercharged HEMI V8

Reserved for the high-end SRT Hellcat, this supercharged engine unleashes a massive 710 horsepower and 645 lb-ft of torque. It turns the Durango into an ultra-performance SUV.

## Transmission

### TorqueFlite 8-Speed Automatic

The TorqueFlite 8-speed automatic transmission pairs with all available engine options across rear-wheel and all-wheel-drive configurations. It generally offers smooth gear changes, though electronic shifter behavior requires attention.

**Unexpected Shifting to Neutral**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers report that the electronic gear shifter may intermittently shift into neutral or slide back into drive without the release button being pressed or the brake applied.
- **Solution:** Have a certified dealership inspect the electronic shifter assembly and update any relevant powertrain control modules.

## Steering

### Front Suspension and Steering

Steering component feedback indicates occasional front-end noises that can be difficult to pinpoint during service visits.

**Unresolved Front-End Noise**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have experienced persistent front-end noises that technicians can hear but struggle to diagnose or repair.
- **Solution:** Consult a dealership or specialist to check front suspension components, bushings, and steering connections for premature wear or loose fasteners.

## Electrical

### Infotainment and Driver Assistance

Electrical system reports highlight occasional glitches with touchscreen displays, rearview cameras, and active safety sensors.

**Screen Blackouts and Cruise Control Failures**
- **Symptoms:** The front navigational screen and backup camera may intermittently black out, while forward collision warning and adaptive cruise control can experience functional dropouts.
- **Solution:** Software updates performed by a dealership may resolve display and driver-assist calibration glitches.

## Summary Verdict

The 2024 Dodge Durango is a powerful and spacious SUV, but used-car buyers should thoroughly inspect the vehicle's electronics, shifter operation, and driver-assistance features during a test drive. Verifying that all software updates have been applied can help ensure a smoother ownership experience.
