The 2016 Dodge Vision, also known as the Dodge Dart in some markets, was a compact sedan aimed at providing a sporty and fuel-efficient driving experience. Engine options included a 2.0L inline-4 (approximately 160 hp and 148 lb-ft of torque), a 1.4L turbocharged inline-4 (approximately 160 hp and 184 lb-ft of torque), and a 2.4L inline-4 (approximately 184 hp and 171 lb-ft of torque). Transmission options varied depending on the engine, including 6-speed manual, 6-speed automatic, and 6-speed dual-clutch automatic transmissions. Owners often praised the Dart for its distinctive styling and available technology features, while some criticized its cramped rear seating and reliability issues.

**Engine**

**2.0L Inline-4**

*   **Engine Stalling**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Engine stalls at idle or while driving, especially after the engine has warmed up. Can be intermittent and difficult to diagnose.
    *   Solution: Potential causes include a faulty throttle body, vacuum leaks, or a malfunctioning crankshaft position sensor. Diagnose the specific cause using diagnostic tools and replace the faulty component.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**1.4L Turbocharged Inline-4 (Multiair)**

*   **Oil Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks from various locations, including the turbocharger oil lines, valve cover gasket, and oil pan gasket. Visible oil spots under the car and a burning oil smell may be present.
    *   Solution: Replace the leaking gasket or seal. For turbocharger oil line leaks, replace the affected oil line and ensure proper torque specifications are followed.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $600+
    * Recalls: None Found

*   **Turbocharger Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Reduced engine power, whistling or screeching noises from the engine bay, and potential for engine damage. Diagnostic codes related to turbocharger boost may be present.
    *   Solution: Replace the failed turbocharger unit. Ensure proper oil supply and maintenance to prevent future failures.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **MultiAir Actuator Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Rough idling, misfires, loss of power, and the check engine light will illuminate.
    * Solution: Replace the MultiAir actuator unit.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1200+
    * Recalls: None Found

**2.4L Inline-4**

*   **Excessive Oil Consumption**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The engine consumes an excessive amount of oil, requiring frequent oil top-ups. Blue smoke may be visible from the exhaust, and the check engine light may illuminate.
    *   Solution: Potential causes include worn piston rings or valve stem seals. A compression test and leak-down test can help identify the source of the problem. Depending on the severity, the engine may require a rebuild or replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $3000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**6-Speed Automatic**

*   **Harsh Shifting**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rough or jerky shifts between gears, especially when cold. The transmission may also slip or hesitate during acceleration.
    *   Solution: Start with a transmission fluid flush and filter replacement. If the problem persists, further diagnosis may be required to identify faulty solenoids or valve body issues.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Transmission Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The transmission will fail to shift properly, or at all. There may be a complete loss of power transmission.
    * Solution: Replace the transmission with a new or refurbished unit.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2500 - $5000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**6-Speed Dual-Clutch Automatic (DCT)**

*   **Poor Shift Quality**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Hesitation, clunking noises, and jerky shifts, especially at low speeds. The transmission may also overheat in stop-and-go traffic.
    *   Solution: A software update or recalibration of the transmission control module (TCM) may improve shift quality. In some cases, clutch replacement may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $2000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Premature Strut/Shock Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Bouncy ride, excessive body roll, and uneven tire wear. Leaking fluid may be visible on the struts or shocks.
    *   Solution: Replace the affected struts or shocks. It's recommended to replace them in pairs to ensure even handling.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Uconnect System Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The Uconnect infotainment system may freeze, crash, or exhibit other glitches. Bluetooth connectivity issues and navigation problems may also occur.
    *   Solution: Try performing a system reset or updating the Uconnect software to the latest version. If the problem persists, the Uconnect module may need to be replaced.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 - $1000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Power Window Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Power windows may stop working or operate intermittently. The window motor or regulator may be faulty.
    *   Solution: Replace the faulty window motor or regulator. Check the window switch for proper operation as well.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $400+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Dead Battery**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The battery will die frequently. The vehicle will need to be jumped to start.
    *   Solution: Replace the dead battery. Check the alternator to make sure it is charging the battery.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $400+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Fog Light Cracking**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The fog light lens crack and shatter without any impact.
    *   Solution: Replace the cracked or shattered fog light assembly.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Dashboard Cracking**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Cracks appearing on the dashboard, especially in warmer climates.
    *   Solution: Can be covered with a dash cover, or the dash needs to be replaced.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $1500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2016 Dodge Vision/Dart has a mix of potential issues that owners should be aware of. Engine problems, particularly with the 1.4L Turbo and 2.4L engines, can lead to significant repair costs. Transmission issues, especially with the DCT, can also be concerning. Electrical glitches and interior issues like dashboard cracking are common annoyances. Overall, while the Dart offers appealing styling and features, potential buyers should carefully inspect the vehicle's maintenance history and be prepared for potential repairs. The severity and cost of these issues can vary, but addressing them promptly is essential for maintaining the vehicle's reliability and value.
