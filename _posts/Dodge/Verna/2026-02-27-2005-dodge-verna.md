## Suspension

### Standard Chassis
**Worn Bushings**
- **Symptoms:** Clunking noises over bumps, imprecise steering, uneven tire wear.
- **Solution:** Replacement of worn suspension bushings.
- **Cost:** $150 - $350

**Strut Mount Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Popping or creaking noises when turning, rough ride, uneven vehicle height.
- **Solution:** Replacement of strut mounts.
- **Cost:** $200 - $450


## Electrical

### General Electrical System
**Faulty Window Regulators**
- **Symptoms:** Power windows failing to operate, slow or jerky window movement.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the window regulator mechanism.
- **Cost:** $150 - $300 per window

**Alternator Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Dimming headlights, battery warning light illuminated, difficulty starting the engine.
- **Solution:** Testing and potential replacement of the alternator.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600


## Exterior

### Body Panels and Trim
**Rusting Wheel Arches and Sills**
- **Symptoms:** Visible rust formation, bubbling paint, potential perforation of metal.
- **Solution:** Rust treatment and repair, potentially panel replacement.
- **Cost:** $200 - $800 per area

**Faded Headlight Lenses**
- **Symptoms:** Yellowed or cloudy headlights, reduced light output, poor visibility at night.
- **Solution:** Headlight restoration kit or replacement of headlight assemblies.
- **Cost:** $50 - $250


## Interior

### Cabin Components
**HVAC Blower Motor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** No airflow from vents, inconsistent fan speed, unusual noises from the dashboard.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the HVAC blower motor.
- **Cost:** $150 - $350

**Worn Seat Fabric/Bolsters**
- **Symptoms:** Tears, thinning fabric, compressed foam in seat cushions and bolsters.
- **Solution:** Seat repair or replacement of seat covers.
- **Cost:** $100 - $500 per seat

### Summary Verdict
The 2005 Dodge Verna, while offering basic transportation, exhibits some common age-related issues, particularly in its suspension and electrical systems. Owners should be aware of potential problems with worn suspension bushings and strut mounts, which can lead to noise and handling degradation. Electrical gremlins like faulty window regulators and alternator failures are also noted. Exterior rust, especially around wheel arches and sills, is a concern for vehicles in certain climates. Interior wear, such as HVAC blower motor issues and seat degradation, is also present. Engine and transmission options appear relatively robust with no widespread critical failures reported, making them less of a concern compared to other components.
