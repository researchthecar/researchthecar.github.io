**Introduction**

The 1999 Dodge Ram 3500 is a heavy-duty pickup truck praised for its powerful engine options, rugged design, and impressive towing and hauling capabilities. It offers a range of engine and transmission combinations:

* 5.9L V8 Magnum (245 hp, 345 lb-ft torque)
* 8.0L V10 Magnum (310 hp, 450 lb-ft torque)
* 4-speed automatic transmission (47RE)
* 5-speed manual transmission (NV4500)

While admired for its capabilities, the 1999 Dodge Ram 3500 has also faced various issues that owners have reported.

**Engine**

**5.9L V8 Magnum**

* **Intake Manifold Gasket Failure**
Description/Symptoms: Leaking coolant, overheating, rough idling
Solution: Replace intake manifold gaskets
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
Recalls: None Found

* **Valve Seat Recession**
Description/Symptoms: Reduced engine power, misfiring, excessive exhaust smoke
Solution: Replace cylinder heads
Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000+
Recalls: None Found

**8.0L V10 Magnum**

* **Timing Chain Failure**
Description/Symptoms: Engine rattling or skipping, loss of power
Solution: Replace timing chain and related components
Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**4-speed Automatic (47RE)**

* **Output Shaft Speed Sensor Failure**
Description/Symptoms: Intermittent or erratic shifting, check engine light
Solution: Replace output shaft speed sensor
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $300
Recalls: None Found

* **Sun Shell Failure**
Description/Symptoms: Loss of forward gears, slipping or shuddering during shifts
Solution: Rebuild or replace transmission
Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,000+
Recalls: None Found

**5-speed Manual (NV4500)**

* **Input Shaft Bearing Failure**
Description/Symptoms: Grinding or whining noise, difficulty shifting
Solution: Replace input shaft bearing
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Front Suspension Wear**
Description/Symptoms: Loose or worn ball joints, tie rods, control arms, leading to poor handling and tire wear
Solution: Replace worn components
Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on components
Recalls: None Found

* **Rear Leaf Spring Breakage**
Description/Symptoms: Broken leaf springs cause suspension sag, reduced towing capacity
Solution: Replace leaf springs
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Alternator Failure**
Description/Symptoms: Dimming headlights, electrical system malfunctions
Solution: Replace alternator
Estimated Cost to Fix: $250 - $500
Recalls: None Found

* **Starter Failure**
Description/Symptoms: Engine fails to start or starts intermittently
Solution: Replace starter
Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500
Recalls: 04V225000, 05V050000

**Exterior**

* **Door Handle Failure**
Description/Symptoms: Loose or broken door handles
Solution: Replace door handles
Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200
Recalls: None Found

* **Tailgate Latch Failure**
Description/Symptoms: Tailgate opens unintentionally while driving
Solution: Replace tailgate latch
Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $250
Recalls: 04V225000, 05V050000

**Interior**

* **Dashboard Instrument Panel Failure**
Description/Symptoms: Malfunctioning gauges or displays
Solution: Replace instrument panel
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
Recalls: None Found

* **HVAC Blower Motor Failure**
Description/Symptoms: No air flow from vents
Solution: Replace blower motor
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $300
Recalls: 04V225000, 05V050000

**Summary**

The 1999 Dodge Ram 3500 has faced a range of issues across various components, including serious problems like engine failures and transmission rebuilds. Some of these issues can be costly to repair, while others are more minor and affordable to fix. Overall, the severity and cost of these issues should be considered when evaluating the reliability and potential ownership costs of this vehicle.
