**Introduction:** The 2007 Dodge Ram 3500 is a heavy-duty pickup truck offered with a range of engine and transmission options. The base engine is a 5.7L V8 producing 345 horsepower and 407 lb-ft of torque. Also available are a 6.7L Cummins turbocharged inline-6 diesel engine producing 350 horsepower and 610 lb-ft of torque, and a 8.0L V10 engine producing 340 horsepower and 450 lb-ft of torque. Transmission options include a 6-speed manual, 6-speed automatic, and a 4-speed automatic. The Ram 3500 is praised for its rugged construction, powerful engines, and spacious interior. However, it has also been criticized for its high fuel consumption and lackluster handling.

**Engine**
**5.7L V8**
- **Intake Manifold Runner Control Valve Failure:** Symptoms include rough idling, stalling, and reduced engine power. The solution is to replace the intake manifold runner control valve. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
- **Exhaust Manifold Leak:** Symptoms include a ticking or tapping noise from the engine, reduced engine power, and increased emissions. The solution is to replace the exhaust manifold. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
**6.7L Cummins Turbocharged Inline-6 Diesel**
- **Turbocharger Failure:** Symptoms include black smoke coming from the exhaust, decreased engine power, and reduced fuel economy. The solution is to replace the turbocharger. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
- **Injector Failure:** Symptoms include rough idling, white smoke coming from the exhaust, and decreased fuel economy. The solution is to replace the injector. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,200+
**8.0L V10**
- **Valve Seat Wear:** Symptoms include increased oil consumption, misfiring, and decreased engine power. The solution is to replace the valve seats. Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $5,000+

**Transmission**
**6-Speed Manual**
- **Grinding Noises:** Symptoms include grinding noises when shifting gears. The solution is to replace the transmission. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $4,000+
**6-Speed Automatic**
- **Shift Solenoid Failure:** Symptoms include delayed or erratic shifting, and slipping gears. The solution is to replace the shift solenoid. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,000+
**4-Speed Automatic**
- **Torque Converter Failure:** Symptoms include shuddering during acceleration, slipping gears, and overheating. The solution is to replace the torque converter. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,500+

**Suspension**
- **Leaf Spring Failure:** Symptoms include a sagging or uneven ride, and noise from the suspension. The solution is to replace the leaf springs. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
- **Shock Absorber Failure:** Symptoms include a rough ride, reduced handling, and excessive bouncing. The solution is to replace the shock absorbers. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600+

**Electrical**
- **Battery Drain:** Symptoms include a dead battery after sitting for a period of time. The solution is to identify and fix the parasitic drain. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
- **Headlight Switch Failure:** Symptoms include flickering or non-functioning headlights. The solution is to replace the headlight switch. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+

**Exterior**
- **Tailgate Handle Failure:** Symptoms include difficulty opening or closing the tailgate. The solution is to replace the tailgate handle. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
- **Door Handle Failure:** Symptoms include difficulty opening or closing the door. The solution is to replace the door handle. Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $400+

**Interior**
- **HVAC Control Module Failure:** Symptoms include inoperative climate control, or erratic temperature control. The solution is to replace the HVAC control module. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600+
- **Cluster Failure:** Symptoms include flickering or non-functioning gauges. The solution is to replace the cluster. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+

**Recalls:**

**Summary:** The 2007 Dodge Ram 3500 has a range of potential issues that can vary in severity and cost. The most common issues affect the engine, transmission, and suspension. Repair costs can range from a few hundred dollars to several thousand dollars. It's important to note that not all Ram 3500s will experience these issues, and the overall reliability will depend on factors such as maintenance history and driving conditions.
