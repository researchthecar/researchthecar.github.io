**Introduction:**

The 1992 Dodge Monaco is a full-size sedan that offers a comfortable ride and a spacious interior. It comes standard with a 3.3L V6 engine and a 3-speed automatic transmission. Other engine options include a 3.5L V6 and a 5.2L V8.

People like the Monaco for its roomy interior, comfortable seats, and powerful engines. However, some common issues have been reported with the vehicle.

**Engine**

**3.3L V6**

* **Intake manifold gasket failure:** Symptoms include coolant leaks, overheating, and decreased engine performance. Solution: Replace the intake manifold gasket. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
* **Cylinder head gasket failure:** Symptoms include coolant leaks, overheating, and white smoke from the exhaust. Solution: Replace the cylinder head gasket. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
* **Timing belt failure:** Symptoms include a loud squealing noise from the engine, loss of power, and engine stalling. Solution: Replace the timing belt and tensioner. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
* **Oil pump failure:** Symptoms include low oil pressure, knocking sounds from the engine, and engine damage. Solution: Replace the oil pump. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+

**3.5L V6**

* **No issues found**

**5.2L V8**

* **Intake manifold gasket failure:** Symptoms include coolant leaks, overheating, and decreased engine performance. Solution: Replace the intake manifold gasket. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+
* **Valve cover gasket leak:** Symptoms include oil leaks from the engine and a burning oil smell. Solution: Replace the valve cover gaskets. Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300+
* **Exhaust manifold leak:** Symptoms include a loud exhaust noise and decreased engine performance. Solution: Replace the exhaust manifold gaskets. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+

**Transmission**

**3-Speed Automatic**

* **Transmission slipping:** Symptoms include difficulty shifting gears, delayed engagement, and loss of power. Solution: Repair or replace the transmission. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
* **Torque converter failure:** Symptoms include shuddering or slipping when shifting gears, and overheating. Solution: Replace the torque converter. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+

**Suspension**

* **Strut failure:** Symptoms include a rough ride, uneven tire wear, and clunking noises from the suspension. Solution: Replace the struts. Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800+
* **Sway bar link failure:** Symptoms include clunking noises from the suspension, decreased handling, and uneven tire wear. Solution: Replace the sway bar links. Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300+

**Electrical**

* **Alternator failure:** Symptoms include dim lights, difficulty starting the vehicle, and a warning light on the dashboard. Solution: Replace the alternator. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+
* **Starter failure:** Symptoms include difficulty starting the vehicle, clicking sounds from the engine, and a warning light on the dashboard. Solution: Replace the starter. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
* **Battery failure:** Symptoms include difficulty starting the vehicle, dim lights, and a warning light on the dashboard. Solution: Replace the battery. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+

**Exterior**

* **Rust:** Symptoms include rusted body panels, fenders, and undercarriage. Solution: Repair or replace the rusted parts. Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the extent of the damage.
* **Paint peeling:** Symptoms include peeling or chipping paint on the exterior of the vehicle. Solution: Repaint the affected areas. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
* **Door latch failure:** Symptoms include difficulty opening or closing the doors, and doors opening unexpectedly. Solution: Replace the door latches. Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300+

**Interior**

* **Dashboard cracks:** Symptoms include cracks or splits on the dashboard. Solution: Repair or replace the dashboard. Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the extent of the damage.
* **Seat fabric tearing:** Symptoms include torn or ripped seat upholstery. Solution: Repair or replace the seat covers. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
* **Window regulator failure:** Symptoms include difficulty raising or lowering the windows, and windows falling off track. Solution: Replace the window regulator. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+

**Summary:**

The 1992 Dodge Monaco is a spacious and comfortable vehicle that has some common issues. Engine issues, such as intake manifold gasket failure and timing belt failure, can be expensive to fix. Transmission issues, such as slipping and torque converter failure, can also be costly. However, many of the other issues, such as suspension and electrical problems, are relatively inexpensive to repair. Overall, the severity and cost of the issues with the Monaco will vary depending on the specific problem.
