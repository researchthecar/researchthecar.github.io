**Introduction:**
The 1996 Dodge Stealth is a sports car known for its performance and sleek design. It came with two engine options: a 3.0L V6 and a twin-turbocharged 3.0L V6. The V6 produced 160 horsepower and 180 lb-ft of torque, while the twin-turbocharged V6 produced 320 horsepower and 315 lb-ft of torque. All Stealth models came with a five-speed manual transmission or a four-speed automatic transmission.

**Engine**
**3.0L V6**
- **Blown head gasket:** Symptoms include overheating, white smoke from the exhaust, and loss of coolant. Solution: Replace the head gasket. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
- **Timing belt failure:** Symptoms include a sudden loss of power and a rattling noise. Solution: Replace the timing belt. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000

**3.0L Twin-Turbocharged V6**
- **Turbo failure:** Symptoms include decreased power, increased oil consumption, and blue smoke from the exhaust. Solution: Replace the turbochargers. Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,000
- **Intake manifold gasket failure:** Symptoms include coolant and oil leaks. Solution: Replace the intake manifold gasket. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800
- **Fuel injector problems:** Symptoms include rough idle, stalling, and decreased fuel economy. Solution: Replace the fuel injectors. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500

**Transmission**
**Five-Speed Manual**
- **Synchronizer issues:** Symptoms include difficulty shifting gears, especially into second gear. Solution: Replace the synchros. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800

**Four-Speed Automatic**
- **Transmission slipping:** Symptoms include delayed gear changes and loss of power. Solution: Replace the transmission. Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,000+

**Suspension**
- **Strut failure:** Symptoms include a rough ride, uneven tire wear, and fluid leaks. Solution: Replace the struts. Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $600
- **Control arm bushing failure:** Symptoms include clunking noises and vibrations. Solution: Replace the control arm bushings. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400

**Electrical**
- **Alternator failure:** Symptoms include dim headlights, flickering lights, and a dead battery. Solution: Replace the alternator. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500

**Exterior**
- **Door lock actuators:** Symptoms include difficulty unlocking or locking the doors. Solution: Replace the door lock actuators. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $300

**Interior**
- **Climate control system failure:** Symptoms include no heat or air conditioning. Solution: Replace the climate control module. Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $600

**Summary:**
The 1996 Dodge Stealth has several known issues that can be expensive to fix. The engine and transmission are prone to failures that can cost thousands of dollars to repair. The suspension and electrical systems also have some common problems. While the Stealth is a desirable sports car, it is important to be aware of the potential costs associated with maintaining it.
