The 2004 Dodge SX 2.0, also known as the Neon in some markets, was a compact car offering a blend of affordability and practicality. The main engine option was a 2.0L inline-4 cylinder engine, producing approximately 132 horsepower and 130 lb-ft of torque. Other engine options in different markets, or in the same markets but different trims, included a 1.6L or 1.8L inline 4. Transmission options primarily consisted of a 5-speed manual and a 4-speed automatic. Owners often appreciated its fuel efficiency and nimble handling, while common complaints centered around reliability issues and the overall build quality, particularly regarding interior components.

**Engine**

**2.0L Inline-4 (DOHC)**

*   **Oil Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks can occur from various locations including the valve cover gasket, oil pan gasket, and rear main seal. Symptoms include visible oil drips, burning oil smell, and low oil levels.
    *   Solution: Replacing the affected gasket or seal.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $300+ depending on the leak location.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Crankshaft Position Sensor Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Difficulty starting the engine, stalling, or the engine cutting out while driving. The check engine light will typically illuminate.
    *   Solution: Replacing the crankshaft position sensor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**4-Speed Automatic**

*   **Slipping or Erratic Shifting**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The transmission slips between gears, hesitates during shifts, or shifts erratically. This can be more noticeable when the transmission is cold.
    *   Solution: Fluid flush and filter change. If the problem persists, a transmission rebuild or replacement may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 (fluid flush) - $2000+ (rebuild/replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Transmission Mount Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Excessive vibration, especially at low speeds. Clunking noises during acceleration or deceleration.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the transmission mount.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Strut and Shock Absorber Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Bouncy ride, poor handling, excessive body roll in turns, and uneven tire wear.
    *   Solution: Replacing the worn struts and/or shock absorbers.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600+ per axle (pair)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Sway Bar Link Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Clunking or rattling noises from the front or rear suspension, especially when driving over bumps.
    *   Solution: Replacing the worn sway bar links.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $200 per link
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Power Window Motor Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Power windows stop working or operate intermittently.
    *   Solution: Replacing the power window motor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300 per window
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Instrument Cluster Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Gauges malfunction, such as the speedometer or tachometer not working correctly. The instrument cluster may flicker or go completely dark.
    *   Solution: Repair or replacement of the instrument cluster.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Rust**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rust formation, particularly around the wheel wells, rocker panels, and door edges.
    *   Solution: Rust repair and repainting. In severe cases, panel replacement may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $1000+ depending on severity
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Dashboard Cracking**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Cracks appearing on the dashboard surface, often due to exposure to sunlight and heat.
    *   Solution: Dashboard replacement or installation of a dashboard cover.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 (cover) - $800+ (replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**
The 2004 Dodge SX 2.0, while appreciated for its fuel economy and handling, does exhibit some common reliability concerns. Engine oil leaks and transmission issues, such as slipping, can be costly to address. Suspension components and electrical faults, like power window failures, also contribute to the overall maintenance burden. Rust and interior issues like dashboard cracking are aesthetic concerns that detract from the vehicle's value. Overall, potential buyers should be aware of these potential problems and factor them into their purchase decision and ongoing maintenance budget.
