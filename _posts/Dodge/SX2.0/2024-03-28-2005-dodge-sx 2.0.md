**2005 Dodge SX 2.0**

The 2005 Dodge SX 2.0 is a compact sedan equipped with a 2.0-liter 4-cylinder engine rated at 148 horsepower and 165 lb-ft of torque. It is paired with a 5-speed manual or a 4-speed automatic transmission. The SX 2.0 is known for its sporty handling, fuel efficiency, and affordable price. However, it has several notable issues that affect its reliability and usability.

## Engine
**2.0L 4-Cylinder Engine**

- **Intake Manifold Failure:**
    - Description/Symptoms: The intake manifold develops cracks, causing vacuum leaks and performance issues. This leads to rough idling, stalling, and decreased fuel economy.
    - Solution: Replace the intake manifold.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    - Recalls: NHTSA Campaign Number 08V504000

- **Water Pump Failure:**
    - Description/Symptoms: The water pump fails, leading to an overheating engine. This can result in blown head gaskets, warped cylinder heads, and engine seizure.
    - Solution: Replace the water pump.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+
    - Recalls: None Found

## Transmission
**5-Speed Manual Transmission**

- **Synchro Mesh Issues:**
    - Description/Symptoms: The synchronizers in the transmission wear out prematurely, causing difficulty shifting gears. This can lead to grinding, popping, and stuck gears.
    - Solution: Replace the synchronizers.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800+
    - Recalls: None Found

**4-Speed Automatic Transmission**

- **TCC Solenoid Failure:**
    - Description/Symptoms: The Torque Converter Clutch (TCC) solenoid fails, causing the transmission to slip and shudder during gear changes. This can also lead to increased wear on other transmission components.
    - Solution: Replace the TCC solenoid.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
    - Recalls: None Found

- **Transmission Control Module (TCM) Failure:**
    - Description/Symptoms: The TCM fails, causing erratic transmission behavior. This can manifest as delayed gear changes, stuck gears, or a complete loss of power.
    - Solution: Replace the TCM.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    - Recalls: None Found

## Suspension
- **Struts and Shocks Wear:**
    - Description/Symptoms: The struts and shocks wear out prematurely, leading to a bumpy and uncomfortable ride. This can also cause uneven tire wear and affect handling.
    - Solution: Replace the struts and shocks.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
    - Recalls: None Found

## Electrical
- **Battery Drain:**
    - Description/Symptoms: The battery drains prematurely, leaving you stranded. This can be caused by a faulty alternator, starter, or parasitic drain.
    - Solution: Diagnose the source of the drain and repair or replace the affected component.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the source of the drain
    - Recalls: None Found

- **Electrical Short in Wiring Harness:**
    - Description/Symptoms: A short in the wiring harness can cause various electrical issues, such as flickering lights, power loss, and potential fire hazards.
    - Solution: Repair or replace the affected portion of the wiring harness.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the extent of the damage
    - Recalls: None Found

## Exterior
- **Rust on Body Panels:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Rust develops on the body panels, especially in areas exposed to road salt or moisture. This can lead to perforation and structural issues.
    - Solution: Rust repair or panel replacement.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
    - Recalls: None Found

## Interior
- **Power Window Switch Failure:**
    - Description/Symptoms: The power window switch fails, rendering the affected window inoperable.
    - Solution: Replace the power window switch.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
    - Recalls: None Found

- **Dashboard Squeaks and Rattles:**
    - Description/Symptoms: The dashboard develops squeaks and rattles, particularly over rough roads.
    - Solution: Identify and tighten or lubricate the source of the noise.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the extent of the noise
    - Recalls: None Found

## Summary

The 2005 Dodge SX 2.0 offers a sporty driving experience and fuel efficiency but is marred by several reliability issues. The intake manifold failure, water pump failure, and transmission problems are severe and can lead to costly repairs. While some issues, such as dashboard noises and power window switch failures, are less severe, they can still be inconvenient and detract from the overall ownership experience.

If considering a 2005 Dodge SX 2.0, it is important to be aware of these potential problems and factor in the cost of repairs. Regular maintenance and inspections can help identify issues early and minimize their impact.
