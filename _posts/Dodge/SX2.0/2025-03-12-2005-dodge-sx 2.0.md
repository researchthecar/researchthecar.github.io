The 2005 Dodge SX 2.0, also known as the Neon in some markets, was a compact sedan known for its affordability and decent fuel economy. The engine of focus here is the 2.0L inline-4 cylinder engine, producing around 132 horsepower and 130 lb-ft of torque. Other engine and transmission options were not available. Some owners appreciated its simple design and ease of maintenance, while others disliked its somewhat basic interior and reputation for reliability issues.

**Engine**

**2.0L Inline-4 Cylinder**

*   **Valve Seat Issue**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Dropping valve seats can lead to engine misfires, loss of compression, and potentially catastrophic engine damage. This issue is more common with high mileage.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the cylinder head or engine rebuild.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Oil Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks are common, especially from the valve cover gasket, oil pan gasket, and rear main seal. Symptoms include visible oil leaks and low oil levels.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the leaking gasket or seal.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+ depending on the leak's location.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Coolant Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaks can occur from various locations, including the water pump, radiator hoses, and radiator itself.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the leaking component.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+ depending on the leak's location.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**Automatic Transmission**

*   **Erratic Shifting/Slipping**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Issues with the automatic transmission include erratic shifting, slipping between gears, and delayed engagement.
    *   Solution: Transmission flush and filter change may help in minor cases. More severe cases may require transmission rebuild or replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 (flush) to $2,000+ (rebuild/replacement).
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Manual Transmission**

*No Issues Found*

**Suspension**

*   **Worn Struts/Shocks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Reduced ride quality, excessive bouncing, and poor handling.
    *   Solution: Replacement of struts and/or shocks.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+ for all four corners.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Worn Control Arm Bushings**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Clunking noises from the front end, especially when going over bumps.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the control arm bushings or the entire control arm.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $500+ per side.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Power Window Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Power windows failing to operate, often due to a faulty window motor or switch.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the window motor or switch.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300 per window.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Sensor Failures (Crankshaft, Camshaft)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Engine stalling, poor performance, and check engine light illumination.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the faulty sensor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+ per sensor, including diagnostics.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Rust Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rust can develop on the rocker panels, wheel wells, and underbody.
    *   Solution: Rust repair or panel replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+ depending on the severity.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Dashboard Cracking**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Cracking or splitting of the dashboard, especially in areas exposed to sunlight.
    *   Solution: Dashboard replacement or repair.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Headliner Sagging**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The headliner fabric detaches from the roof and begins to sag.
    *   Solution: Headliner replacement or repair.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $400
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2005 Dodge SX 2.0, while affordable, suffers from a range of potential issues. Engine problems such as valve seat dropping and oil leaks are common and can be costly. Transmission issues can range from minor shifting problems to major failures. Suspension components wear out, leading to ride and handling issues. Electrical and interior problems also contribute to the overall ownership experience. Overall, the issues are of moderate severity and can range from relatively inexpensive fixes to significant repairs. Potential buyers should be aware of these issues and carefully inspect any vehicle before purchase.
