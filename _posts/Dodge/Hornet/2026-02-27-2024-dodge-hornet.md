# 2024 Dodge Hornet Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2024 Dodge Hornet introduces an aggressive Italian-inspired compact crossover design shared with the Alfa Romeo Tonale, offering lively performance and distinct styling. However, early owner reports to federal regulators highlight several recurring areas of concern, particularly regarding complex electrical glitches, powertrain fluid management, and alignment-related tire wear. Prospective buyers should thoroughly check service history and verify that all necessary updates have been completed.

---

## Engine

### 1.3L Turbocharged Inline-4 Plug-In Hybrid (R/T)

The R/T trim features a 1.3-liter turbocharged four-cylinder plug-in hybrid engine paired with an electric motor, producing a combined 288 horsepower and 383 lb-ft of torque. It offers brisk acceleration and electric-only driving capability, though the complex electrified systems require attentive maintenance.

### 2.0L Hurricane 4 Turbocharged Inline-4 (GT)

The GT trim is powered by a 2.0-liter turbocharged Hurricane 4 engine pushing out 268 horsepower and 295 lb-ft of torque. It provides strong traditional gasoline performance, though buyers should monitor fluid levels during routine inspections.

**Coolant Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have reported unexpected coolant loss, empty reservoirs, and potential engine overheating risks.
- **Solution:** Inspect cooling lines and pressure test the system to locate leaks, replacing faulty components or fittings as needed.

**Fuel System Overflow**
- **Symptoms:** Fuel may overflow unexpectedly from the filler neck while attempting to refuel the vehicle.
- **Solution:** Have the dealership inspect the fuel filler neck, vapor lines, and tank venting system for blockages or component failure.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic (R/T PHEV)

A 6-speed automatic transmission manages power delivery for the R/T plug-in hybrid model, working seamlessly with the electric motor to optimize efficiency and performance.

### 9-Speed Automatic (GT)

A 9-speed automatic transmission handles gear selection in the GT model, offering wide gear ratios designed to balance highway cruising efficiency with responsive acceleration.

## Suspension

### Alignment and Tires

Suspension geometry is generally sound, though factory alignment tolerances should be verified during pre-purchase inspections to prevent early tire wear.

**Premature Tire Wear Due to Alignment**
- **Symptoms:** Front tires exhibit accelerated inner tread wear caused by improper factory wheel alignment settings.
- **Solution:** Perform a comprehensive four-wheel alignment and inspect tire tread depth, replacing worn tires as necessary.

## Electrical

### Infotainment and Start-Stop System

Electrical architecture complaints range from minor software glitches to major power distribution faults that can impact core vehicle functions.

**Start-Stop System and Infotainment Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers experience unexpected warning symbols and erratic start-stop behavior, frequently following software or radio updates.
- **Solution:** Visit a certified dealership to check for updated software calibrations or module resets addressing start-stop logic.

**Complete Electrical Failures**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicles may experience sudden no-start conditions, loss of power steering assistance, or severe internal electrical system faults.
- **Solution:** Inspect the battery, charging system, wiring harnesses, and control modules for shorts or loose connections.

## Exterior

### Wheels and Trim

Exterior finishes and body panels are generally attractive, though isolated component detachment incidents highlight the importance of thorough mechanical checkups.

**Wheel Rim Detachment**
- **Symptoms:** Unusual reports of wheel rims separating from the vehicle under normal driving conditions, causing secondary body damage.
- **Solution:** Inspect wheel lug torque and wheel assembly integrity regularly to ensure proper mounting.

## Safety

### Advanced Driver Assistance Systems (ADAS)

Advanced safety features enhance daily driving confidence, but owner reports indicate sensitivity to adverse environmental conditions.

**Weather-Sensitive ADAS Failures**
- **Symptoms:** Forward collision avoidance and lane departure warning systems frequently display warning lights or become temporarily disabled in rain, fog, or morning condensation.
- **Solution:** Keep sensor and camera mounting areas clean and free of debris, and consult a dealer if sensor calibration errors persist.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V623000: Electrical System:Propulsion System: Cables:Fastener/Connector](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Dodge&model=Hornet&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** The 12-volt battery positive cable and/or high voltage connector cable may not be tightened properly, which can overheat the electrical connections and result in a fire while parked or driving. A fire while parked or driving can increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and tighten the cable connections as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V696000: Back Over Prevention:Warnings:External/Pedestrian Alert](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Dodge&model=Hornet&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 141, "Minimum Sound Requirements for Hybrid and Electric Vehicles." Failure to alert pedestrians when backing up increases the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the rear pedestrian siren connection and, if necessary, secure and/or replace the connector or siren, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V175000: Equipment:Other:Labels](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Dodge&model=Hornet&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 110, "Tire Selection and Rims." A vehicle with an incorrect maximum capacity weight value may be overloaded, which can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will add an overlay label to the tire placard information, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V209000: Equipment:Other:Labels](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Dodge&model=Hornet&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 110, "Tire Selection and Rims." Missing rim and tire size information could lead to an incorrect wheel being installed, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will apply a corrected label, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V752000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Pedals And Linkages](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Dodge&model=Hornet&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** A loss of brake function increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will reinforce the brake pedal arm, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V246000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Dodge&model=Hornet&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** The display module may not show the rearview camera image. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 111, "Rear Visibility." A rearview image that does not display reduces the driver's view of what is behind the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the software or replace the display as necessary free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2024 Dodge Hornet brings distinctive styling and engaging performance to the compact crossover segment, but potential buyers should approach early models with care. While powertrain options offer plenty of punch, reported electrical glitches, cooling concerns, and ADAS sensitivities warrant a close inspection. Be sure to obtain a comprehensive pre-purchase inspection and verify that all dealer service bulletins and recall campaigns have been properly addressed.
