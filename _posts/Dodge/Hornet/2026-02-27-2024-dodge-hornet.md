# 2024 Dodge Hornet Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The Dodge Hornet offers a sporty crossover package with distinct performance-oriented powertrain options, but prospective buyers should approach with caution due to early model-year complaints. Initial owner feedback highlights notable electrical, braking, and powertrain concerns that warrant a thorough pre-purchase inspection.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged I4

The 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine produces approximately 268 horsepower and 295 lb-ft of torque, providing robust acceleration for the GT trim. While generally punchy, owners have reported isolated concerns regarding coolant leaks and associated fluid losses.

**Coolant Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Low coolant levels and empty coolant reservoirs noticed during routine maintenance or after short drives.
- **Solution:** Inspect cooling system components, check water pump functionality, and top off or pressure test the system.

### 1.3L Turbocharged Plug-In Hybrid I4

The 1.3-liter turbocharged plug-In hybrid powertrain pairs an electric motor with a small gas engine to deliver an estimated 288 horsepower and strong low-end torque for the R/T trim. It offers efficient everyday driving dynamics alongside enhanced performance capabilities.

## Transmission

### 9-Speed Automatic

The nine-speed automatic transmission routes power in gas-powered variants, offering generally smooth gear changes while optimizing highway cruising efficiency.

### 6-Speed Automatic

The six-speed automatic transmission works alongside hybrid components in select configurations to manage hybrid power delivery, though early feedback includes complaints regarding shifting smoothness.

**Shifting Irregularities and Gear Engagement**
- **Symptoms:** Unintended shifting behavior, rough engagement sounds, or unexpected gear transitions while maneuvering.
- **Solution:** Diagnostic scan for powertrain control module updates or transmission software flashes.

## Brakes

### Hydraulic Brake System

The braking hardware provides adequate stopping power, but owners have flagged critical concerns related to warning messages and hydraulic system behavior.

**Brake System Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** Sudden warning messages concerning brake failure or compromised hydraulic pressure.
- **Solution:** Verify completion of applicable manufacturer service bulletins and inspect brake lines and master cylinders.

## Interior

### Wiring Harness and Dashboard Electronics

Complex vehicle electronics manage infotainment and driver aids, though early reports indicate vulnerability to short circuits and harness issues.

**Electrical Shorts and Warning Lights**
- **Symptoms:** Dash warning lights illuminating unexpectedly, screen blackouts, or blown fuses related to interior accessories.
- **Solution:** Inspect wiring harnesses, replace damaged USB modules or dash wiring, and check for localized shorts.

## Safety

### Forward Collision Avoidance

Active safety features aim to protect occupants, though sensor sensitivity in poor weather conditions has generated owner complaints.

**Advanced Driver Assistance System (ADAS) Faults**
- **Symptoms:** Warning messages, system deactivation during inclement weather, or unexpected limp mode triggers.
- **Solution:** Clean sensor areas around the windshield and bumpers, and update ADAS software modules at a dealership.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V623000: Electrical System:Propulsion System: Cables:Fastener/Connector](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Dodge&model=Hornet&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** The 12-volt battery positive cable and/or high voltage connector cable may not be tightened properly, which can overheat the electrical connections and result in a fire while parked or driving. A fire while parked or driving can increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and tighten the cable connections as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V696000: Back Over Prevention:Warnings:External/Pedestrian Alert](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Dodge&model=Hornet&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 141, "Minimum Sound Requirements for Hybrid and Electric Vehicles." Failure to alert pedestrians when backing up increases the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the rear pedestrian siren connection and, if necessary, secure and/or replace the connector or siren, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V175000: Equipment:Other:Labels](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Dodge&model=Hornet&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 110, "Tire Selection and Rims." A vehicle with an incorrect maximum capacity weight value may be overloaded, which can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will add an overlay label to the tire placard information, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V209000: Equipment:Other:Labels](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Dodge&model=Hornet&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 110, "Tire Selection and Rims." Missing rim and tire size information could lead to an incorrect wheel being installed, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will apply a corrected label, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V752000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Pedals And Linkages](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Dodge&model=Hornet&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** A loss of brake function increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will reinforce the brake pedal arm, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V246000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Dodge&model=Hornet&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** The display module may not show the rearview camera image. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 111, "Rear Visibility." A rearview image that does not display reduces the driver's view of what is behind the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the software or replace the display as necessary free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2024 Dodge Hornet delivers sharp styling and engaging performance, particularly in its R/T hybrid guise, but it carries a higher-than-average volume of early owner complaints regarding electrical gremlins and braking alerts. Prospective buyers should verify that all open campaigns and software updates have been completed by a certified dealer before purchase.
