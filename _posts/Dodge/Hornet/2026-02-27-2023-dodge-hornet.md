# 2023 Dodge Hornet Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used Dodge Hornet, buyers will find an engaging compact SUV sharing its platform and mechanical DNA with European crossovers. As a relatively new addition to the market, early model years display a typical pattern of first-year quirks and software-related teething pains reported by owners. Prospective buyers should inspect the electrical systems and active safety features closely during a test drive to ensure everything operates smoothly.

---

## Engine

### 1.3L Turbocharged Inline-4 Hybrid (R/T)

The plug-in hybrid R/T powertrain pairs a 1.3-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine with an electric motor to deliver around 288 horsepower and strong low-end torque. Buyers looking for electrified performance should verify that all high-voltage charging components and hybrid software updates have been properly maintained.

### 2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4 (GT)

The conventional GT model features a 2.0-liter Hurricane turbocharged four-cylinder engine producing roughly 268 horsepower and 295 lb-ft of torque. This standard powerplant offers brisk acceleration and conventional mechanical simplicity compared to the hybrid variant.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic (Plug-In Hybrid)

Paired exclusively with the plug-in hybrid powertrain, this 6-speed automatic transmission manages power distribution between the gas engine and electric drive system. Regular fluid maintenance and software checks ensure smooth operation.

### 9-Speed Automatic (GT)

The standard GT model utilizes a 9-speed automatic transmission paired with all-wheel drive for highway cruising efficiency and responsive gear selection. Buyers should test drive the vehicle across various speeds to confirm seamless shifting behavior.

## Steering

### Steering Column

Steering components generally feel precise, though mechanical noises require targeted inspection.

**Steering Column Noise**
- **Symptoms:** A loud cranking or screeching noise accompanied by a knocking sound can develop from the steering wheel during prolonged driving.
- **Solution:** Inspect the steering column and intermediate shaft components for lubrication issues or wear.

## Electrical

### Battery and Infotainment

Battery management and accessory power modules can occasionally experience unexpected draws or reset issues.

**Battery Drain and Display Failures**
- **Symptoms:** The electrical system can drain the battery if the vehicle is left parked for short periods, leading to unresponsive infotainment screens and disabled backup cameras upon restart.
- **Solution:** Check battery health, ensure software updates are current, and inspect for parasitic electrical draws.

## Exterior

### Door Latches and Locks

Electrical and body control systems require attention, particularly regarding electronic door locks and auxiliary power drains.

**Electronic Door Latch Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Owners report that electronic door locks and linkages fail, preventing doors from opening from either the interior or exterior.
- **Solution:** Have a dealer or qualified mechanic inspect and replace faulty door latch assemblies and electronic actuators.

## Safety

### Forward Collision and Cruise Control

Advanced driver-assistance features rely heavily on clean sensors and stable software to prevent intermittent warning lights.

**Adaptive Cruise Control and Camera Warnings**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers experience unexpected adaptive cruise control dropouts accompanied by warning messages indicating that cameras or sensors are blocked or require service.
- **Solution:** Clear sensor zones, check for windshield alignment issues, and consult a dealer for applicable software recalibrations.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V623000: Electrical System:Propulsion System: Cables:Fastener/Connector](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Dodge&model=Hornet&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** The 12-volt battery positive cable and/or high voltage connector cable may not be tightened properly, which can overheat the electrical connections and result in a fire while parked or driving. A fire while parked or driving can increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and tighten the cable connections as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V696000: Back Over Prevention:Warnings:External/Pedestrian Alert](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Dodge&model=Hornet&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 141, "Minimum Sound Requirements for Hybrid and Electric Vehicles." Failure to alert pedestrians when backing up increases the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the rear pedestrian siren connection and, if necessary, secure and/or replace the connector or siren, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V175000: Equipment:Other:Labels](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Dodge&model=Hornet&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 110, "Tire Selection and Rims." A vehicle with an incorrect maximum capacity weight value may be overloaded, which can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will add an overlay label to the tire placard information, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V246000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Dodge&model=Hornet&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** The display module may not show the rearview camera image. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 111, "Rear Visibility." A rearview image that does not display reduces the driver's view of what is behind the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the software or replace the display as necessary free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The vehicle offers compelling performance and modern features, but buyers should carefully evaluate early-production electrical quirks, door latch operation, and driver-assistance sensor behavior. A thorough pre-purchase inspection focusing on electronics and battery health is strongly recommended before finalizing a purchase.
