# 2016 Dodge Journey Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The Dodge Journey offers an affordable entry point into the midsize crossover market, featuring spacious seating options and a user-friendly infotainment system. However, prospective buyers should approach used models with caution due to a notable history of recurring mechanical and electrical complaints. Reviewing common trouble spots can help you spot potential red flags during a test drive and determine if a specific vehicle has been well-maintained.

---

## Engine

### 2.4L 4-Cylinder

A 2.4-liter naturally aspirated four-cylinder engine producing approximately 173 horsepower and 166 lb-ft of torque, paired with a 4-speed automatic transmission. This base engine provides adequate power for daily commuting but can feel sluggish when the vehicle is fully loaded.

### 3.6L V6

A 3.6-liter Pentastar V6 engine generating 283 horsepower and 260 lb-ft of torque, matched with a 6-speed automatic transmission. It delivers much stronger acceleration and towing capability than the base four-cylinder, though it is associated with occasional stalling and electrical issues.

**Engine Stalling and Loss of Power**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers report that the engine may suddenly stall while driving or lose motive power completely without prior warning, occasionally illuminating check engine or ABS lights.
- **Solution:** Diagnose the underlying electrical or sensor faults, which frequently involves inspecting or replacing components such as the crankshaft sensor or powertrain control modules.

## Transmission

### 4-Speed Automatic

A traditional four-speed automatic transmission paired exclusively with the base four-cylinder front-wheel-drive models. Routine fluid changes are essential to maximize its lifespan.

### 6-Speed Automatic

A six-speed automatic transmission found on V6 models and select trims, offering smoother gear changes. However, mechanical wear can lead to pump failures and slipping over time.

**Transmission Slippage and Pump Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Owners experience transmission slipping during acceleration, sudden hesitation, whining noises, or complete loss of drive gear engagement.
- **Solution:** Requires professional transmission inspection, replacement of failing internal pumps, or potential transmission rebuild/replacement.

## Brakes

### Anti-lock Brake System (ABS) Module

The braking system provides adequate stopping power for daily driving, though electrical issues within the stability control modules are a known point of failure.

**ABS and Traction Control Warning Lights**
- **Symptoms:** The ABS, brake, and traction control warning lights illuminate simultaneously on the dashboard due to an internal module failure.
- **Solution:** Have a mechanic scan for diagnostic trouble codes and replace the faulty electronic stability control or ABS module as needed.

## Steering

### Power Steering System

The steering system provides reasonable maneuverability, but aging power steering hoses and pumps are prone to leaks and stiffness.

**Stiff Steering and Fluid Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** The steering wheel becomes extremely stiff or resistant when turning at lower speeds, accompanied by abnormal popping sounds and fluid puddles under the vehicle.
- **Solution:** Inspect power steering hoses, lines, and the pump for leaks or damage, and replace worn components before refilling fluid.

## Electrical

### Electrical Accessories

Electrical system issues can affect convenience features, climate control, and dashboard illumination, requiring careful inspection of switches and modules.

**Uncontrolled Climate Control and Instrument Cluster Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** The air conditioning system turns on or off by itself, or dashboard instrument lights randomly shut off while driving.
- **Solution:** Diagnose the HVAC control head, body control module, or wiring harnesses for shorts and replace faulty electrical components.

## Interior

### Active Headrests

The cabin features versatile seating configurations and ample storage compartments, but mechanical flaws in the active safety headrests can cause sudden malfunctions.

**Spontaneous Headrest Deployment**
- **Symptoms:** The front seat active headrests unexpectedly pop forward or deploy completely while the vehicle is parked or being driven, preventing them from being easily reset.
- **Solution:** Inspect the internal deployment mechanism and replace the damaged headrest assembly or securing clips.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V332000: Electrical System:Wiring](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Dodge&model=Journey&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** These vehicles are being recalled to address a defect that could prevent the cruise control system from disengaging. If, when using cruise control, there is a short circuit within the vehicle's wiring, the driver may not be able to shut off the cruise control either by depressing the brake pedal or manually turning the system off once it has been engaged.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the software, and perform a software flash on the engine or powertrain control module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V907000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Engine](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Dodge&model=Journey&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** If the engine stalls, there is an increased the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will replace the crankshaft or camshaft sensor connector, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V461000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Dodge&model=Journey&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** If the transmission loses hydraulic pressure, the vehicle may lose motive power, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will replace the transmission pump, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V273000: Steering:Hydraulic Power Assist:Hose, Piping, And Connections](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Dodge&model=Journey&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** A loss of power steering assist may require extra steering effort, especially at lower speeds, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will replace the power steering return hoses, steel tubes, and power steering oil cooler, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

While the crossover offers practical passenger space and flexible utility, used-car buyers should thoroughly inspect the vehicle for electrical anomalies, transmission smoothness, and brake module operation. A comprehensive pre-purchase inspection by a qualified mechanic is strongly recommended to catch any hidden mechanical or steering issues before finalizing a purchase.
