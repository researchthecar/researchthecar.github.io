# 2018 Dodge Journey Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2018 Dodge Journey, prospective buyers will find a budget-friendly midsize crossover with three rows of seating, but one that carries a mixed reliability record. While it offers versatile packaging for the price, owners frequently report recurring electrical issues, interior trim quirks, and suspension or brake concerns. Evaluating specific mechanical health and prior maintenance history is essential before purchasing.

---

## Engine

### 2.4L 4-Cylinder

The standard 2.4-liter four-cylinder engine produces 173 horsepower and 166 lb-ft of torque, paired with a 4-speed automatic transmission. It provides adequate power for basic commuting but can feel sluggish when the vehicle is fully loaded, with typical fuel economy for its class.

### 3.6L V6

The optional 3.6-liter Pentastar V6 engine delivers a much stronger 283 horsepower and 260 lb-ft of torque, mated to a 6-speed automatic transmission. It offers smoother, more confident acceleration and is available with all-wheel drive, though it shares some of the electronic and drivability complaints noted across the model line.

**Engine Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** Unexpected engine stalling while driving, often accompanied by warning lights on the dashboard.
- **Solution:** Diagnose fault codes, inspect electronic control modules, and replace faulty sensors or wiring as needed.

## Transmission

### 4-Speed Automatic

This older four-speed automatic transmission is paired exclusively with the base four-cylinder engine. It features a dated design that lacks the gear ratios of modern competitors, impacting highway efficiency and responsiveness.

### 6-Speed Automatic

The six-speed automatic transmission accompanies the V6 engine option and provides smoother, better-spaced gear shifts than the base four-speed. Routine fluid checks help maintain long-term shifting quality.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

Brake components require regular inspection due to owner reports of accelerated wear and inconsistent pedal response.

**Brake System Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Premature brake wear, spongy pedal feel, or warning lights indicating reduced braking performance.
- **Solution:** Inspect brake pads, rotors, calipers, and hydraulic lines, replacing worn components as necessary.

## Electrical

### Backup Camera and Display

Electrical accessories, particularly entertainment and camera displays, are common trouble spots for this model year.

**Backup Camera Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The rearview camera display turns completely black, freezes, displays inverted or blurry images, or shows error lines when shifting into reverse.
- **Solution:** Inspect the tailgate wiring harness and replace the camera unit or display module if internal failure is found.

## Exterior

### Exterior Lighting

Lighting systems can experience premature bulb burnout and housing condensation issues that require component replacement.

**Headlight and Tail Light Failures**
- **Symptoms:** Headlights appear excessively dim, fail intermittently, or suffer from moisture accumulation inside the housing.
- **Solution:** Check bulb connections, replace failed housings or bulbs, and ensure weather seals are intact.

## Interior

### Active Headrests

Cabin appointments are generally durable, though unexpected mechanical releases in the seating hardware can startle occupants.

**Unintended Headrest Deployment**
- **Symptoms:** Front seat active headrests suddenly pop or deploy forward spontaneously without any vehicle impact or warning.
- **Solution:** Replace the damaged internal mechanism or reset the active headrest assembly according to manufacturer specifications.

## Summary Verdict

The 2018 Dodge Journey appeals to buyers seeking an affordable three-row crossover with straightforward interior packaging. However, prospective owners should thoroughly test all electronics, including the backup camera and lighting, and verify proper mechanical condition. A comprehensive pre-purchase inspection is highly recommended to catch lingering electrical or brake concerns before finalizing a deal.
