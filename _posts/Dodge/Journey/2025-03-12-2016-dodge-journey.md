The 2016 Dodge Journey is a mid-size crossover SUV known for its affordability and available third-row seating. Engine options for the 2016 model year include a 2.4L inline-4 engine producing 173 horsepower and 166 lb-ft of torque and a more powerful 3.6L V6 engine generating 283 horsepower and 260 lb-ft of torque. The 2.4L engine is paired with a 4-speed automatic transmission, while the 3.6L engine is mated to a 6-speed automatic transmission. People often appreciate the Journey's spacious interior, family-friendly features, and budget-friendly price. However, common complaints include lackluster fuel economy, outdated technology, and some reliability concerns.

**Engine**

**2.4L Inline-4**

*   **Engine Misfires**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rough idling, hesitation during acceleration, check engine light illumination with codes related to cylinder misfires (e.g., P0301, P0302, P0303, P0304). May be caused by worn spark plugs, faulty ignition coils, or vacuum leaks.
    *   Solution: Diagnose the cause of the misfire. Replace spark plugs and/or ignition coils as needed. Check for and repair any vacuum leaks.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**3.6L V6**

*   **Oil Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Oil spots under the vehicle, burning oil smell, low oil level, and potential engine damage if left unaddressed. Common leak locations include the oil cooler adapter and valve cover gaskets.
    *   Solution: Identify the source of the leak and replace the faulty gasket or component.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**4-Speed Automatic (paired with 2.4L engine)**

*   **Harsh Shifting**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Hard or jerky shifting between gears, especially during acceleration or deceleration.
    *   Solution: Check transmission fluid level and condition. Perform a transmission fluid flush. If the issue persists, the valve body may need to be inspected and repaired or replaced.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 (fluid flush) - $1000+ (valve body repair/replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**6-Speed Automatic (paired with 3.6L engine)**

*   **Rough Shifting/Slipping**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Hesitation during acceleration, slipping between gears, harsh or delayed shifting, and potential transmission failure.
    *   Solution: Check the transmission fluid level and condition. A complete transmission service or replacement may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 (fluid flush) - $3000+ (transmission replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Premature Wear of Struts and Shocks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Bumpy ride, excessive bouncing, poor handling, and uneven tire wear.
    *   Solution: Replace worn struts and shocks.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+ per axle
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Worn Ball Joints and Control Arm Bushings**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Clunking noises from the front end, especially over bumps. Loose steering and poor alignment.
    *   Solution: Replace worn ball joints and control arm bushings. An alignment is typically required after these repairs.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600+ per side.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Failing TIPM (Totally Integrated Power Module)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Multiple electrical issues, including stalling, non-starting, malfunctioning lights, erratic power window operation, and malfunctioning fuel pump.
    *   Solution: The TIPM may need to be reprogrammed or replaced.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Faulty Door Lock Actuators**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Doors not locking or unlocking properly, requiring manual operation.
    *   Solution: Replace the faulty door lock actuator.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $400 per door.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Headlight Condensation**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Moisture buildup inside the headlight lenses, reducing visibility.
    *   Solution: Replacing the headlight assembly.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600 per headlight.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Dashboard Cracking**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Cracks appearing on the dashboard, especially in sun-exposed areas.
    *   Solution: Dashboard replacement, dash cover installation.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 (dash cover) - $1500+ (dashboard replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Faulty Uconnect Touchscreen**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Touchscreen freezes, unresponsive buttons, and Bluetooth connectivity issues.
    *   Solution: Software update, Uconnect module replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 (software update) - $800+ (module replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2016 Dodge Journey is a budget-friendly option in the mid-size crossover segment, but it's essential to be aware of potential issues. Common problems include oil leaks and transmission issues, especially with the 6-speed automatic transmission. Electrical problems related to the TIPM can also be costly to repair. While some repairs are relatively inexpensive, others, such as TIPM replacement or transmission overhaul, can be quite significant. Careful inspection and a pre-purchase inspection by a qualified mechanic are recommended before buying a used 2016 Dodge Journey.
