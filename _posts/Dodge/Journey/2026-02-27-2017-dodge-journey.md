# 2017 Dodge Journey Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2017 Dodge Journey offers family-friendly versatility with available three-row seating, but prospective used-car buyers should carefully evaluate its mechanical and electrical track record. While it remains an affordable option on the used market, owners have frequently reported issues ranging from sudden electrical glitches and unprovoked active headrest deployments to powertrain and steering concerns. Careful inspection and a thorough test drive are recommended before purchasing.

---

## Engine

### 2.4L 4-Cylinder

The base 2.4-liter four-cylinder engine produces 173 horsepower and 166 lb-ft of torque, providing adequate power for daily commuting but struggling when fully loaded with passengers.

### 3.6L V6

The 3.6-liter Pentastar V6 produces 283 horsepower and 260 lb-ft of torque, offering much stronger acceleration and towing capability than the base four-cylinder engine.

**Engine Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle suddenly stalls during a journey, accompanied by warning codes and requiring a tow.
- **Solution:** Diagnose fault codes, inspect sensors or fuel delivery components, and replace failed parts.

**Oil Filter Housing Leak**
- **Symptoms:** Visible oil leaks or pooling originating from the oil filter housing area on the engine.
- **Solution:** Replace the leaking oil filter housing assembly with an updated unit.

## Transmission

### 4-Speed Automatic

The traditional 4-speed automatic transmission is paired with the base four-cylinder front-wheel-drive configuration, offering basic mechanical operation.

### 6-Speed Automatic

The 6-speed automatic transmission works alongside front-wheel drive or all-wheel drive models, but some owners have reported premature wear, rough shifting, and failure.

**Transmission Failure and Clunking**
- **Symptoms:** Severe hesitation, overheating, clunking noises during gear shifts, or complete loss of transmission function.
- **Solution:** Have a professional transmission specialist inspect the unit; rebuild or replace the transmission if internal failure is confirmed.

## Electrical

### Electrical System

electrical issues involving cabin accessories, window switches, and warning lights are notable points of concern for used-car shoppers.

**Glitching Accessories and Wiring Failures**
- **Symptoms:** Radio, air conditioning, power windows, and door locks intermittently cut out or stop functioning, sometimes due to broken harness wires.
- **Solution:** Inspect door wire harnesses for damaged or severed wires, and repair or replace faulty switches.

## Safety

### Seats

Cabin features include flexible seating configurations, though unexpected mechanical component releases in the seats have caused owner complaints.

**Unprovoked Active Headrest Deployment**
- **Symptoms:** A loud popping sound occurs followed by the driver or front passenger active head restraint deploying suddenly without a collision.
- **Solution:** Replace or reset the deployed active headrest mechanism.

### Air Bag

Safety restraint systems generally protect occupants, but warning light illuminations require prompt diagnostic attention to ensure proper functionality.

**Air Bag Warning Light**
- **Symptoms:** Air bag warning light illuminates unexpectedly on the instrument cluster, indicating a fault in the supplemental restraint system.
- **Solution:** Scan the SRS module for diagnostic trouble codes and repair faulty sensors, wiring, or components.

### Seat Belt

Rear seat belt latches should be thoroughly checked during a pre-purchase inspection to verify smooth operation.

**Seat Belt Latch Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Second-row seat belts intermittently fail to release when the buckle button is pressed.
- **Solution:** Inspect seat belt buckles and replace malfunctioning assembly mechanisms as needed.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V332000: Electrical System:Wiring](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Dodge&model=Journey&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** These vehicles are being recalled to address a defect that could prevent the cruise control system from disengaging. If, when using cruise control, there is a short circuit within the vehicle's wiring, the driver may not be able to shut off the cruise control either by depressing the brake pedal or manually turning the system off once it has been engaged.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the software, and perform a software flash on the engine or powertrain control module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V723000: Exterior Lighting](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Dodge&model=Journey&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** The amber side reflex reflector assembled into the headlamp may not reflect brightly enough. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 108, "Lamps, Reflective Devices, and Associated Equipment." A dim reflector may reduce the visibility of the vehicle to other drivers, increasing the risk of.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the headlamp assemblies, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2017 Dodge Journey can serve as budget-friendly family transportation, but buyers should proceed with caution and verify the condition of critical mechanical and electrical systems. Prioritize models with a documented service history, and pay close attention to transmission shift quality, electrical accessory operation, and potential fluid leaks during a test drive.
