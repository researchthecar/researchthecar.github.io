# 2014 Dodge Journey

### Overview
The 2014 Dodge Journey, a mid-size crossover SUV, offered a range of powertrain options and a practical interior. While generally considered a decent family vehicle, like any model, it has specific areas where owners have reported issues. This analysis focuses on documented technical reliability concerns, excluding routine maintenance and normal wear and tear. We will examine common problems reported across its engine, transmission, suspension, electrical, exterior, and interior systems to provide a comprehensive reliability overview for potential owners or those maintaining the vehicle.

---


## Engine

### 2.0L I4 Turbo Diesel (EJG)
**EGR Valve Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Check engine light, rough idling, reduced power, black smoke from exhaust.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the EGR valve.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600

**Turbocharger Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of power, whistling noises, increased exhaust smoke.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of the turbocharger assembly.
- **Cost:** $1000 - $2500


### 2.4L I4 (ED3)
**No Major Issues**
- **Symptoms:** No widespread issues reported.
- **Solution:** No widespread issues reported.
- **Cost:** No widespread issues reported.


### 3.6L V6 (Pentastar)
**No Major Issues**
- **Symptoms:** No widespread issues reported.
- **Solution:** No widespread issues reported.
- **Cost:** No widespread issues reported.


## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic (62TE)
**Hesitation and Shifting Problems**
- **Symptoms:** Jerky shifts, delayed engagement, slipping gears.
- **Solution:** Transmission fluid flush and filter replacement, or internal transmission repair/rebuild.
- **Cost:** $300 - $1500 (flush/filter), $2000 - $4000 (rebuild)

**Torque Converter Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Shuddering at low speeds, slipping, overheating.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the torque converter.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1500


## Suspension

### Standard Chassis
**Worn Control Arm Bushings**
- **Symptoms:** Clunking noises over bumps, poor handling, uneven tire wear.
- **Solution:** Replacement of control arm bushings or the entire control arm assembly.
- **Cost:** $300 - $700

**Strut Mount Bearing Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Popping or creaking noises when turning the steering wheel, especially at low speeds.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the strut mount bearings.
- **Cost:** $200 - $500


## Electrical

### General Electrical System
**Faulty TIPM (Totally Integrated Power Module)**
- **Symptoms:** Intermittent electrical issues, non-starting, warning lights, power window malfunctions.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the TIPM module.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1000

**Battery Drain Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Dead battery, vehicle won't start, electrical components acting erratically.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and repair of parasitic draw, or replacement of faulty modules causing the drain.
- **Cost:** $150 - $500


## Exterior

### Body Panels and Trim
**No Major Issues**
- **Symptoms:** No widespread issues reported.
- **Solution:** No widespread issues reported.
- **Cost:** No widespread issues reported.


## Interior

### Cabin Components
**HVAC Blower Motor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** No air coming from vents, or intermittent fan operation.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the blower motor or resistor.
- **Cost:** $150 - $400

**Uconnect System Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Touchscreen unresponsiveness, Bluetooth connectivity issues, system freezing.
- **Solution:** Software update or replacement of the Uconnect module.
- **Cost:** $300 - $800

### Summary Verdict
The 2014 Dodge Journey presents a mixed reliability profile. The 3.6L Pentastar V6 and 2.4L gasoline engines are generally robust with no widespread reported issues. However, the 2.0L turbo diesel variant has documented concerns with its EGR valve and turbocharger. The 6-speed automatic transmission can experience shifting problems and torque converter issues, requiring attention. Suspension components like control arm bushings and strut mounts are also noted for potential wear. Electrical gremlins, particularly related to the TIPM and battery drain, are a significant concern. Interior issues are less critical, with HVAC blower motors and the Uconnect system being the most common complaints. Overall, while the Journey offers practicality, potential buyers should be aware of these specific reliability points, especially concerning the diesel engine and electrical system.
