# 2022 Dodge Journey

### Overview
The 2022 Dodge Journey, a rebadged and updated version of the Fiat Freemont, aims to offer a budget-friendly three-row SUV option. While it attempts to provide practicality and space, its underlying architecture, which dates back several years, presents certain reliability considerations. This analysis focuses on documented technical issues beyond standard maintenance, drawing from available data to highlight potential areas of concern for owners. The vehicle is primarily offered with a naturally aspirated gasoline engine and a six-speed automatic transmission, with variations in trim levels affecting feature availability. Understanding these specific components and their reported issues is crucial for prospective buyers and current owners seeking to maintain their Journey.

## Electrical

### Infotainment System
**Screen Freezing/Unresponsiveness**
- **Symptoms:** Infotainment screen may freeze, become unresponsive to touch, or reboot unexpectedly.
- **Solution:** Software update or replacement of the infotainment unit.
- **Cost:** $300 - $1200


### Battery Drain
**Premature Battery Discharge**
- **Symptoms:** Battery may drain faster than expected, leading to starting issues. Can be related to parasitic draw from certain modules.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis of parasitic draw, potential module replacement or software recalibration.
- **Cost:** $150 - $500

### Summary Verdict
The 2022 Dodge Journey, while offering a spacious interior at a competitive price point, exhibits a mixed reliability profile. The powertrain, consisting of the 2.4L Tigershark engine and 6-speed automatic transmission, appears to be a strong point with no widespread issues reported. However, electrical components, particularly the infotainment system and potential battery drain issues, have seen some owner complaints, suggesting a need for vigilance. Interior components like the HVAC blower motor can also be a point of failure. Owners should be aware of these potential electrical and HVAC concerns and factor in possible repair costs beyond routine maintenance. Overall, it's a functional vehicle for its intended purpose, but potential buyers should weigh the cost savings against the possibility of encountering specific electrical or HVAC-related repairs.
