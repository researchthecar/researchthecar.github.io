# 2020 Dodge Journey Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2020 Dodge Journey serves as an affordable, family-oriented crossover offering three rows of seating in a relatively compact footprint. For its final model year, Dodge simplified the lineup to feature a single engine and transmission combination. While budget-conscious shoppers may find its low entry price and interior flexibility appealing, used car buyers should approach with caution due to reported owner complaints regarding engine performance, electrical glitches, and visibility concerns.

---

## Engine

### 2.4L 4-Cylinder Engine

The 2020 Dodge Journey is powered by a 2.4-liter naturally aspirated four-cylinder engine producing 173 horsepower and 166 lb-ft of torque. It provides adequate power for daily commuting, though it can feel strained when the vehicle is fully loaded with passengers.

**Engine Stalling and Loss of Motive Power**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle suddenly loses power and stalls while driving at highway speeds, accompanied by a check engine light and potential overheating or steam from under the hood.
- **Solution:** Diagnose the underlying cause of the stall, which may involve inspecting the fuel system, electrical components, or cooling system elements like the thermostat.

## Transmission

### 4-Speed Automatic Transmission

A 4-speed automatic transmission routes power exclusively to the front wheels. While mechanically straightforward, it offers fewer gears than modern competitors, which can impact highway fuel efficiency and acceleration responsiveness.

## Electrical

### Rearview Camera System

Safety equipment includes standard airbags and driver assistance basics, though the backup camera system has generated significant owner complaints regarding display failures.

**Distorted or Inverted Backup Camera Display**
- **Symptoms:** The rearview camera image displays upside down, flickers intermittently, shows lines, or fails completely when shifting into reverse.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the rearview camera unit or application of manufacturer software updates where applicable.

## Exterior

### Exterior Lighting and Dashboard Controls

Owner reports highlight occasional electrical issues affecting exterior illumination, dash displays, and infotainment responsiveness.

**Headlight Failure and Moisture Ingress**
- **Symptoms:** Headlights fail to operate properly or moisture accumulates inside the headlight housing lenses, compromising nighttime visibility.
- **Solution:** Inspect headlight seals for cracks or leaks; replace faulty headlight bulbs, assemblies, or cracked seals as necessary.

---

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V552000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Dodge&model=Journey&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** Cracks in the microprocessor may cause the vehicle's rearview camera image not to display properly. A rearview image that does not display properly reduces the driver's view behind the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the rearview camera, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2020 Dodge Journey presents a mixed proposition for used vehicle buyers. On one hand, its accessible pricing and standard three-row seating make it tempting for families on a strict budget. On the other hand, recurring owner complaints concerning unexpected engine stalls, electrical annoyances, and faulty backup cameras suggest that thorough pre-purchase inspections are essential. Prospective buyers should carefully verify the mechanical condition and test all electronics before finalizing a purchase.
