The 2009 Dodge Nitro is a compact SUV known for its distinctive, boxy styling and truck-like appearance. It offered a rugged alternative to more rounded competitors. The 2009 Nitro was available with a 3.7L V6 engine producing 210 horsepower and 235 lb-ft of torque or a more powerful 4.0L V6 engine rated at 260 horsepower and 265 lb-ft of torque. Transmission options included a 4-speed automatic (3.7L) and a 5-speed automatic (4.0L). Owners often appreciate the Nitro's unique looks and spacious cargo area, but dislike its fuel economy, ride quality, and some reliability concerns.

**Engine**

**3.7L V6**

* **Oil Leaks**
    * Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks are commonly reported, often originating from the valve cover gaskets or oil pan gasket. This can lead to oil spots on the driveway and a burning oil smell.
    * Solution: Replacing the leaking gaskets is usually required.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $400+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Rough Idle/Stalling**
    * Description/Symptoms: The engine may experience a rough idle or stall, especially at lower speeds. This can often be attributed to a dirty or malfunctioning throttle body.
    * Solution: Cleaning or replacing the throttle body is typically necessary.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+
    * Recalls: None Found

**4.0L V6**

* **Overheating**
    * Description/Symptoms: The 4.0L engine is prone to overheating, often due to a failing water pump or radiator. Symptoms include the temperature gauge rising into the red zone and steam coming from under the hood.
    * Solution: Replacing the water pump, radiator, or both is the common fix.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $700+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Engine Misfires**
    * Description/Symptoms: Engine misfires can occur due to faulty ignition coils or spark plugs. Symptoms include rough running, loss of power, and the check engine light illuminating.
    * Solution: Replacing the faulty ignition coils and/or spark plugs is the solution.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $400+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**4-Speed Automatic (3.7L)**

* **Harsh Shifting**
    * Description/Symptoms: The 4-speed automatic transmission can sometimes exhibit harsh or erratic shifting between gears.
    * Solution: A transmission fluid flush and filter change *may* resolve the issue. In more severe cases, valve body repair or replacement might be necessary.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $800+
    * Recalls: None Found

**5-Speed Automatic (4.0L)**

* **Delayed Engagement**
    * Description/Symptoms: Some owners report a delay in engagement when shifting from park or neutral into drive or reverse.
    * Solution: Often caused by low transmission fluid or internal wear. A fluid flush and filter change should be done first, then internal repairs may be needed if the problem persists.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $1500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Front End Noise**
    * Description/Symptoms: Clunking or rattling noises from the front suspension are commonly reported, often caused by worn sway bar links or ball joints.
    * Solution: Replacing the worn sway bar links or ball joints.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $400+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Rear Leaf Spring Sagging**
    * Description/Symptoms: The rear leaf springs can sag over time, resulting in a lower ride height and reduced load-carrying capacity.
    * Solution: Replacing the leaf springs.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **TIPM (Totally Integrated Power Module) Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: The TIPM is a known trouble spot in the 2009 Nitro. It can cause various electrical problems, including fuel pump relay failure (leading to stalling), starting issues, and erratic operation of accessories.
    * Solution: Replacing the TIPM or repairing the fuel pump relay are the common fixes.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1500+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Power Window Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Power windows failing to operate, or operating intermittently, is a common complaint. This is often attributed to a faulty window motor or regulator.
    * Solution: Replacing the faulty window motor or regulator is usually required.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $400+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Rust (Especially in Salt Belt States)**
    * Description/Symptoms: Rust formation, especially on the undercarriage and around wheel wells, is a prevalent issue, particularly in areas with harsh winters and road salt usage.
    * Solution: Rust repair or panel replacement depending on the severity. Rust prevention measures are recommended.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $2000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Dashboard Cracking**
    * Description/Symptoms: The dashboard can develop cracks over time, especially in vehicles exposed to high heat and sunlight.
    * Solution: Dashboard replacement or using a dashboard cover to conceal the cracks.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 (cover) - $1000+ (replacement)
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Headliner Sagging**
    * Description/Symptoms: The headliner fabric can detach from the backing board and sag, particularly in older vehicles.
    * Solution: Headliner replacement.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

The 2009 Dodge Nitro has a range of potential issues, with electrical problems related to the TIPM being among the most concerning due to their potential impact on vehicle operation and cost to repair. Engine and transmission issues vary depending on the specific configuration, but oil leaks, overheating, and shifting problems are common. Suspension issues like worn sway bar links and sagging leaf springs can affect ride quality. Rust is a concern, especially in colder climates, and interior problems like dashboard cracking and headliner sagging can detract from the vehicle's appearance. The severity of these issues ranges from minor annoyances to potentially serious mechanical failures, and the cost of repair can vary widely depending on the specific problem and the extent of the damage.
