**Introduction:**

The 2009 Dodge Nitro is a compact SUV that offers an optional Hemi V8 engine and a 4-wheel drive system. It comes standard with a 3.7L V6 engine that produces 210 horsepower and 235 lb-ft of torque. The available 4.0L V6 engine produces 260 horsepower and 265 lb-ft of torque, and the optional 5.7L Hemi V8 engine produces 370 horsepower and 400 lb-ft of torque. Transmission options include a 4-speed automatic, a 5-speed automatic, and a 6-speed manual.

The Nitro has a spacious interior and a comfortable ride, but it has been criticized for its lack of fuel efficiency and subpar handling.

**Engine**
**3.7L V6**

* **Intake manifold failure:** The intake manifold can crack, causing coolant and oil leaks. Symptoms include overheating, decreased engine performance, and a check engine light. The solution is to replace the intake manifold. Estimated cost to fix: $800 - $1,500+
* **Piston ring failure:** The piston rings can fail, causing excessive oil consumption and blue smoke from the exhaust. Symptoms include decreased engine performance, increased oil consumption, and a check engine light. The solution is to rebuild the engine. Estimated cost to fix: $2,000 - $3,500+

**4.0L V6**

* **Timing chain failure:** The timing chain can stretch or break, causing the engine to lose timing. Symptoms include a rattling noise from the engine, decreased engine performance, and a check engine light. The solution is to replace the timing chain. Estimated cost to fix: $1,000 - $1,800+

**5.7L Hemi V8**

* **Lifter failure:** The lifters can fail, causing ticking noises from the engine. Symptoms include a ticking noise from the engine, decreased engine performance, and a check engine light. The solution is to replace the lifters. Estimated cost to fix: $500 - $1,000+

**Transmission**
**4-Speed Automatic**

* **Torque converter failure:** The torque converter can fail, causing shuddering or slipping during gear changes. Symptoms include shuddering or slipping during gear changes, decreased engine performance, and a check engine light. The solution is to replace the torque converter. Estimated cost to fix: $1,200 - $2,000+

**5-Speed Automatic**

* **Solenoid failure:** The solenoids can fail, causing shifting problems. Symptoms include hard shifting, delayed shifting, and a check engine light. The solution is to replace the solenoids. Estimated cost to fix: $300 - $800+

**6-Speed Manual**

* **Synchronizer failure:** The synchronizers can fail, causing grinding noises during gear changes. Symptoms include grinding noises during gear changes, difficulty shifting gears, and a check engine light. The solution is to replace the synchronizers. Estimated cost to fix: $400 - $900+

**Suspension**

* **Front suspension strut failure:** The front suspension struts can fail, causing a clunking noise from the front end. Symptoms include a clunking noise from the front end, decreased handling, and a check engine light. The solution is to replace the front suspension struts. Estimated cost to fix: $500 - $1,000+

**Electrical**

* **Body control module failure:** The body control module can fail, causing a variety of electrical problems. Symptoms include electrical problems with the windows, locks, and lights. The solution is to replace the body control module. Estimated cost to fix: $500 - $1,200+
* **Battery cable corrosion:** The battery cables can corrode, causing electrical problems. Symptoms include electrical problems with the starter, lights, and other electrical components. The solution is to clean or replace the battery cables. Estimated cost to fix: $100 - $300+

**Exterior**

* **Door handle failure:** The door handles can fail, causing difficulty opening the doors. Symptoms include difficulty opening the doors, a broken door handle, and a check engine light. The solution is to replace the door handles. Estimated cost to fix: $200 - $500+

**Interior**

* **Window regulator failure:** The window regulators can fail, causing the windows to become inoperable. Symptoms include windows that will not roll up or down, a broken window regulator, and a check engine light. The solution is to replace the window regulators. Estimated cost to fix: $300 - $800+
* **Dashboard trim peeling:** The dashboard trim can peel, causing an unsightly appearance. Symptoms include peeling dashboard trim, a damaged dashboard, and a check engine light. The solution is to replace the dashboard trim. Estimated cost to fix: $200 - $500+

**Summary:**

The 2009 Dodge Nitro has some significant issues that can be costly to repair, especially engine and transmission problems. However, it also has some appealing features, such as a spacious interior and a comfortable ride. Overall, potential buyers should be aware of the potential issues before making a purchase.
