Here's a breakdown of common issues and complaints for the 2019 Dodge Grand Caravan, following your specified format.

The 2019 Dodge Grand Caravan is a popular minivan known for its affordability, practicality, and Stow 'n Go seating. It comes standard with a 3.6L Pentastar V6 engine producing 283 horsepower and 260 lb-ft of torque, paired with a 6-speed automatic transmission. Owners generally appreciate the Grand Caravan's spacious interior, versatile seating, and value for the money. Common complaints revolve around its dated design, lower fuel economy compared to newer minivans, and some reliability concerns.

**Engine**

**3.6L Pentastar V6**

*   **Oil Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks, often from the oil cooler adapter or valve cover gaskets. Can result in visible oil spots under the vehicle, burning oil smell, and low oil levels.
    *   Solution: Replace the oil cooler adapter seals or the valve cover gaskets. Ensure proper torque during installation.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+ depending on the source and if any other components were damaged
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Engine Misfires**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Engine misfires can occur due to faulty ignition coils, spark plugs, or fuel injectors. Symptoms include rough idling, loss of power, and the check engine light illuminating.
    *   Solution: Diagnose the cause of the misfire using a scan tool. Replace faulty ignition coils, spark plugs, or fuel injectors as needed.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $400+ per cylinder depending on the parts needed and labor
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**6-Speed Automatic**

*   **Rough Shifting**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Harsh or erratic shifting between gears. May be more noticeable during acceleration or deceleration.
    *   Solution: Perform a transmission fluid flush and filter replacement. If the issue persists, further diagnostics may be required to check for valve body issues or internal damage.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300+ for fluid flush, $500 - $2000+ for valve body repair or replacement
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Transmission Slipping**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The transmission slips between gears causing a momentary loss of power while driving. This may be more noticeable during acceleration or when climbing hills.
    *   Solution: Check the transmission fluid level and condition, replacing it if needed. If the problem persists, valve body replacement or complete transmission rebuild may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300+ for fluid flush, $500 - $3000+ for valve body repair or transmission rebuild
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Rear Suspension Sagging**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The rear of the vehicle sits lower than normal, particularly when loaded with passengers or cargo. This can lead to a bouncy ride and reduced handling.
    *   Solution: Replace the rear shocks or coil springs. In some cases, air suspension components (if equipped) may need to be inspected and repaired.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Sliding Door Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Sliding doors may fail to open or close properly, either manually or automatically. This can be caused by a faulty motor, damaged wiring, or a malfunctioning sensor.
    *   Solution: Inspect the sliding door motor, wiring harness, and sensors. Replace faulty components as needed.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Sticking Ignition**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Ignition sticks and will not turn from the off position to the on position.
    *   Solution: Replace the Ignition lock cylinder or the entire ignition switch.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Rusting on body panels**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Premature rusting occurring on body panels such as the hood, doors, and fenders.
    *   Solution: Repair and repaint the affected areas. Applying rust inhibitors can help prevent future occurrences.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1000+ per panel
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Power Window Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Power windows stop working, often due to a faulty window motor or regulator.
    *   Solution: Replace the window motor and/or regulator.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $400+ per window
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Center Console Cracking**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Center console cracking and peeling.
    *   Solution: Replace center console.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2019 Dodge Grand Caravan, while a budget-friendly and practical minivan, does have some potential issues to be aware of. The most prevalent concerns appear to be related to the engine (oil leaks, misfires), transmission (rough shifting), and electrical components (sliding doors, power windows). Suspension issues like rear sagging can also occur. The severity of these problems can range from minor inconveniences to more costly repairs. Addressing these issues promptly can help extend the life and reliability of the vehicle.
