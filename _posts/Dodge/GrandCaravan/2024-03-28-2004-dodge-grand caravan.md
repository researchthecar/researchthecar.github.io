**Introduction:**

The 2004 Dodge Grand Caravan is a popular minivan known for its spacious interior and versatile seating configurations. It is powered by a variety of engine and transmission options, including:

* 3.3L V6 (180 hp, 215 lb-ft torque) with a 4-speed automatic transmission
* 3.8L V6 (205 hp, 242 lb-ft torque) with a 4-speed automatic transmission

**Engine:**

* **3.3L V6:**
    * **Valve Cover Gasket Leak:** Oil leaks from the valve cover gasket, causing oil consumption and engine noise.
        * Solution: Replace the valve cover gasket.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
        * Recalls: N/A
    * **Intake Manifold Gasket Leak:** Coolant leaks from the intake manifold gasket, causing overheating and performance issues.
        * Solution: Replace the intake manifold gasket.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600
        * Recalls: N/A
* **3.8L V6:**
    * **Cylinder Head Warping:** The cylinder head can warp over time, causing coolant and oil leaks as well as engine overheating.
        * Solution: Replace the cylinder head.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1500
        * Recalls: N/A

**Transmission:**

* **4-Speed Automatic:**
    * **Shifting Problems:** The transmission may slip or refuse to shift gears, causing erratic performance.
        * Solution: Replace or overhaul the transmission.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1000 - $2500
        * Recalls: N/A
    * **Torque Converter Failure:** The torque converter may fail, causing a loss of power and transmission overheating.
        * Solution: Replace the torque converter.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1000
        * Recalls: N/A

**Suspension:**

* **Front Suspension Strut Failure:** The front suspension struts can prematurely fail, leading to reduced ride comfort and handling.
        * Solution: Replace the suspension struts.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600
        * Recalls: N/A
* **Rear Suspension Sway Bar End Links:** The rear suspension sway bar end links can break, causing a clunking noise and reduced stability.
        * Solution: Replace the sway bar end links.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200
        * Recalls: N/A

**Electrical:**

* **Battery Discharge:** The battery can discharge prematurely, leaving the vehicle unable to start.
        * Solution: Check for parasitic battery drain and repair or replace the necessary components.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300
        * Recalls: N/A
* **Alternator Failure:** The alternator may fail, preventing the vehicle from charging its battery.
        * Solution: Replace the alternator.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600
        * Recalls: N/A

**Exterior:**

* **Door Handle Failure:** The exterior door handles can become loose or break, making it difficult to open the doors.
        * Solution: Replace the door handles.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200
        * Recalls: N/A
* **Power Window Motor Failure:** The power window motors can fail, preventing the windows from being raised or lowered.
        * Solution: Replace the power window motor.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
        * Recalls: N/A

**Interior:**

* **HVAC Blower Motor Failure:** The HVAC blower motor may fail, resulting in no airflow from the heating or air conditioning system.
        * Solution: Replace the blower motor.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
        * Recalls: N/A
* **Dashboard Cracking:** The dashboard can crack over time, compromising its appearance and durability.
        * Solution: Replace or repair the dashboard.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1000
        * Recalls: N/A

**Summary:**

The 2004 Dodge Grand Caravan has a range of potential issues that buyers should be aware of before purchasing. Engine, transmission, and electrical problems are the most common, and can be expensive to repair. However, the minivan's spacious interior and versatility make it a popular choice for families and drivers who need a reliable vehicle for daily use.
