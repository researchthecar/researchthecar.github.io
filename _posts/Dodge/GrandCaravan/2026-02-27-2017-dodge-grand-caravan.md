# 2017 Dodge Grand Caravan Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2017 Dodge Grand Caravan, prospective buyers will find a versatile family hauler equipped with a dependable engine and standard automatic transmission. However, owner reports highlight a few recurring mechanical and electrical pain points worth keeping on your radar. Familiarizing yourself with these common trouble spots can help you negotiate a fair price and inspect potential vehicles more thoroughly before buying.

---

## Engine

### 3.6L V6 Engine

The 2017 Dodge Grand Caravan comes standard with a 3.6-liter Pentastar V6 engine generating approximately 283 horsepower and 260 lb-ft of torque. This powerplant offers strong acceleration for highway merging and daily driving, though buyers should watch out for occasional cooling and oil leaks.

**Engine Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle suddenly stalls while driving at low speeds or in traffic, sometimes accompanied by smoke or failure to restart.
- **Solution:** Diagnose electrical or fuel system components and replace faulty parts as needed.

**Oil Filter Housing Leak**
- **Symptoms:** Pooling oil or coolant leaks around the oil filter housing unit.
- **Solution:** Replace the plastic oil filter housing assembly with an upgraded or replacement part.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

Mated exclusively to the V6 engine, the 6-speed automatic transmission delivers power smoothly to the front wheels. While generally durable, higher-mileage models can occasionally suffer from unexpected wear or shifting irregularities.

**Premature Transmission Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of drive capability, severe slipping, or unexpected shifting into neutral while driving at highway speeds.
- **Solution:** Repair or replace internal transmission components or install a remanufactured unit.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

Braking performance is adequate for a heavy minivan, but older or rust-belt vehicles require careful inspection of brake lines and calipers.

**Brake Line Corrosion and Seized Calipers**
- **Symptoms:** Sudden loss of braking pressure due to rusted brake lines or grinding noises caused by stuck rear brake calipers.
- **Solution:** Replace corroded brake lines, flex hoses, or stuck brake calipers.

## Exterior

### Sliding Door Lock Actuators

The electrical system is largely trouble-free except for a prominent pattern of sliding door actuator failures that can cause doors to jam.

**Door Latch and Actuator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Sliding doors become stuck in place, fail to lock or unlock via power switches or remotes, or make loud grinding noises.
- **Solution:** Replace the faulty door lock actuators and latches.

## Interior

### Active Headrests

The cabin offers flexible seating and ample storage, but owners frequently report issues with jammed rear sliding door latches and unexpected active headrest deployments.

**Inadvertent Headrest Deployment**
- **Symptoms:** Front seat headrests suddenly snap forward and deploy as if an accident occurred, even when unoccupied.
- **Solution:** Replace the deployed active headrest assembly.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V524000: Electrical System:Wiring:Front Underhood](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Dodge&model=Grand+Caravan&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** A vehicle stall can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will replace the powertrain control module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V278000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Storage:Tank Assembly](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Dodge&model=Grand+Caravan&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** A fuel leak in the presence of an ignition source can increase the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 25V876000: Equipment Adaptive/Mobility:Wheelchair Restraints/Securement:Latch/Anchor:](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Dodge&model=Grand+Caravan&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** The retractors may not lock, preventing the wheelchair from being properly secured. An unsecured wheelchair can move during transit, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2017 Dodge Grand Caravan remains an affordable and practical choice for families needing maximum passenger and cargo versatility. While its V6 engine and transmission provide solid performance, buyers should thoroughly test all sliding door functions, inspect for oil or coolant leaks, and check brake lines prior to purchase.
