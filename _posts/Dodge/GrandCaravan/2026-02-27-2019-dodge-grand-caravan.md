# 2019 Dodge Grand Caravan Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2019 Dodge Grand Caravan remains a popular choice for budget-conscious families needing extensive passenger and cargo space. While it offers a practical cabin and familiar drivability, used-car buyers should carefully inspect certain mechanical and electrical areas. Reviewing common owner feedback helps highlight potential pain points before making a purchase.

---

## Engine

### 3.6L V6 Engine

Powered by a dependable 3.6-liter Pentastar V6 engine producing 283 horsepower and 260 lb-ft of torque, this minivan provides confident acceleration for highway merging and daily driving. Overall mechanical durability is decent, though occasional valvetrain or oil filter housing issues can develop over time.

**Cylinder Head and Valvetrain Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Engine loss of power, failure to accelerate, sudden forward lurch when stopped, and illuminated check engine light pointing to cylinder failure.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified mechanic inspect compression and replace defective cylinder heads, exhaust valves, or associated components.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

Equipped with a 6-speed automatic transmission paired with front-wheel drive, the Grand Caravan delivers smooth shifts under normal conditions. Buyers should verify proper shift quality during a test drive, as higher-mileage units can occasionally experience torque converter or internal wear issues.

**Transmission Slippage and Torque Converter Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Abnormal grinding sounds, sudden slipping while driving, stalling at traffic lights, or catastrophic loss of drive power.
- **Solution:** Check transmission fluid condition and level; consult a transmission specialist for rebuild or torque converter replacement if mechanical internal failure occurs.

## Exterior

### Sliding Door Actuators and Latches

The electrical system handles numerous cabin and convenience features, with power sliding doors being a frequently reported area of concern as vehicles age.

**Door Lock Actuator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Sliding doors become stuck in the locked position and fail to open manually, via key fob, interior buttons, or overhead console switches.
- **Solution:** Replace the failed door lock actuator assembly.

## Safety

### Active Headrests

Interior comfort is generally a strong suit for this minivan, though safety mechanisms like active headrests can occasionally malfunction.

**Unintended Headrest Deployment**
- **Symptoms:** Front seat headrests pop forward unexpectedly while driving, striking occupants in the head or neck without warning.
- **Solution:** Inspect the internal headrest deployment mechanism and sensors; replace faulty components or reset the active restraint system.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V759000: Seats](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Dodge&model=Grand+Caravan&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** The welds on the outboard rear seat strikers for the second row bench and second row bucket seats may fail during a front impact crash. The front outboard seat strikers for the third row bench seats may fail in the event of a rear impact crash.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will weld in a striker reinforcement bracket on the second and third row outboard seat strikers, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V523000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Foundation Components:Disc:Caliper](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Dodge&model=Grand+Caravan&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** A reduction of braking performance can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will bleed the vehicle's brake system, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V278000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Storage:Tank Assembly](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Dodge&model=Grand+Caravan&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** A fuel leak in the presence of an ignition source can increase the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 25V876000: Equipment Adaptive/Mobility:Wheelchair Restraints/Securement:Latch/Anchor:](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Dodge&model=Grand+Caravan&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** The retractors may not lock, preventing the wheelchair from being properly secured. An unsecured wheelchair can move during transit, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2019 Dodge Grand Caravan remains an attractive option for budget-conscious families looking for maximum utility. While its powertrain provides solid performance, used buyers should thoroughly test the transmission, inspect the 3.6L engine for service history, and check all power sliding doors and interior electronics before finalizing a purchase.
