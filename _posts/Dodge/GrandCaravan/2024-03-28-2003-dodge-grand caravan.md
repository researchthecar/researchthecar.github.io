**Introduction:**

The 2003 Dodge Grand Caravan is a versatile minivan known for its spacious interior, ample seating, and affordable price. It offers a range of engine and transmission options, including:

* 2.4L 4-cylinder engine with 150 horsepower and 165 lb-ft of torque
* 3.3L V6 engine with 180 horsepower and 215 lb-ft of torque
* 3.8L V6 engine with 215 horsepower and 250 lb-ft of torque
* 4-speed automatic transmission
* 6-speed automatic transmission

**Engine**

**2.4L 4-cylinder**

* **Excessive Oil Consumption:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Burning excessive amounts of oil, leading to low oil levels and potential engine damage
    * Solution: Repair or replace piston rings and oil control valves
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Intake Manifold Gasket Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Leaking coolant or oil due to a faulty gasket, causing overheating or engine performance issues
    * Solution: Replace the intake manifold gasket
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600
    * Recalls: None Found

**3.3L V6**

* **No Issues Found**

**3.8L V6**

* **Timing Chain Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Worn or stretched timing chain, causing timing problems and potential engine failure
    * Solution: Replace the timing chain, tensioners, and guides
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**4-speed Automatic**

* **Slipping or Shuddering:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Transmission slipping gears or shuddering during acceleration or shifting
    * Solution: Repair or rebuild the transmission
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**6-speed Automatic**

* **No Issues Found**

**Suspension**

* **Premature Strut Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Worn or damaged struts, causing bumpy or unstable handling
    * Solution: Replace the struts and mounts
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Battery draining rapidly even when the vehicle is turned off
    * Solution: Diagnose and repair or replace faulty electrical components
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Door Handle Breakage:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Exterior door handles breaking off due to weak plastic construction
    * Solution: Replace the door handles
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Power Window Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Power windows failing to operate due to faulty switches or motors
    * Solution: Replace the window switch or motor
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **HVAC Blower Motor Resistance:**
    * Description/Symptoms: HVAC blower motor not working properly due to a faulty resistor
    * Solution: Replace the HVAC blower motor resistor
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

The 2003 Dodge Grand Caravan has a range of common issues, including engine oil consumption, transmission problems, suspension issues, electrical problems, and interior component failures. The severity and cost of these issues vary, with some requiring major repairs. Overall, the 2003 Grand Caravan is a reliable vehicle but may require regular maintenance and repairs to maintain its performance.
