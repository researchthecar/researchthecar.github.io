# 2020 Dodge Grand Caravan Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2020 Dodge Grand Caravan remains a popular choice for budget-conscious families needing extensive cargo and passenger space. While it offers a tried-and-true powertrain setup, used car buyers should pay attention to potential powertrain quirks and electrical or safety system glitches reported by owners.

---

## Engine

### 3.6L V6 24V VVT

Equipped with the 3.6-liter Pentastar V6 engine producing approximately 283 horsepower and 260 lb-ft of torque, providing capable acceleration for a minivan. This engine is generally robust, though occasional owner reports note auxiliary component or catalytic converter concerns.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic

Paired with a 6-speed automatic transmission driving the front wheels, delivering smooth daily cruising. However, buyers should thoroughly test-drive this unit as transmission shudder and pump failures are noted in owner complaints.

**Transmission Shudder and Pump Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may shudder, fail to shift gears properly, lunge forward unexpectedly, or experience total loss of propulsion while driving.
- **Solution:** Have the transmission inspected by a qualified technician; replacement of the transmission pump or the entire transmission assembly may be required.

## Interior

### Dashboard Display and Power Locks

Electrical complaints are generally minor but can cause inconvenient battery drains and unexpected door locking behavior.

**Interior Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Interior dashboard display lights fail to turn off after the vehicle is parked, leading to a battery drain and a subsequent no-start condition.
- **Solution:** Diagnose the electrical draw and replace any faulty modules, switches, or wiring responsible for keeping the instrument cluster active.

**Unintended Door Locking**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle doors automatically lock on their own shortly after closing them with the engine running.
- **Solution:** Inspect the body control module and door latch lock switches for internal electrical short circuits.

## Safety

### Airbags and Seat Belts

Safety system alerts point to potential wiring harness wear near the front seats, which can illuminate warning chimes and trigger diagnostic trouble codes.

**Seat Belt and Airbag Sensor Faults**
- **Symptoms:** The airbag warning light and seat belt indicator chimes activate intermittently, often due to a frayed driver-side seat wire harness or thorax squib sensor code.
- **Solution:** Inspect and replace damaged wiring harnesses or faulty seat-mounted airbag sensors.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V876000: Equipment Adaptive/Mobility:Wheelchair Restraints/Securement:Latch/Anchor:](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Dodge&model=Grand+Caravan&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** The retractors may not lock, preventing the wheelchair from being properly secured. An unsecured wheelchair can move during transit, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

When shopping for a used Dodge Grand Caravan, pay close attention to transmission shift quality and ensure all electrical features and safety warning lights function correctly during a pre-purchase test drive.
