# 2016 Dodge Charger Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2016 Dodge Charger delivers bold American muscle styling, spacious cabin accommodations, and a wide array of powerful engine options. While it offers impressive rear-wheel-drive or all-wheel-drive performance, prospective buyers should inspect the vehicle carefully for known electrical, fuel system, and interior trim durability issues reported by owners.

---

## Engine

### 3.6L V6 Engine

The standard 3.6-liter Pentastar V6 engine produces approximately 292 to 300 horsepower, paired with a smooth-shifting automatic transmission. It offers a balanced blend of everyday efficiency and dependable daily driving capability.

### 5.7L HEMI V8 Engine

The 5.7-liter HEMI V8 engine generates 370 horsepower, delivering classic muscle car performance and robust towing capability. It is a popular choice for buyers seeking stronger acceleration and traditional V8 sound.

### 6.4L HEMI V8 Engine

The 6.4-liter SRT HEMI V8 engine pushes out an aggressive 485 horsepower for high-performance track and street capability. It requires premium maintenance and careful driving history review.

### 6.2L Supercharged HEMI V8 Engine

The supercharged 6.2-liter Hellcat V8 engine outputs a massive 707 horsepower for extreme performance enthusiasts. It demands meticulous mechanical inspection due to high-stress operation.

### Fuel

Owner complaints highlight occasional fuel odor issues near the rear seats and rare fuel pump electrical connection failures.

**Fuel Smell or Leak**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers notice a strong raw fuel odor inside or around the vehicle, particularly after refueling the gas tank.
- **Solution:** Inspect the fuel pump module, tank connections, and lines for leaks or cracked seals, replacing faulty components as necessary.

**Fuel Pump Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may fail to start, stall unexpectedly, or show melted electrical connections near the fuel pump harness.
- **Solution:** Replace the damaged fuel pump assembly and inspect or repair the electrical wiring harness and connectors.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

The TorqueFlite 8-speed automatic transmission generally delivers crisp and responsive gear shifts across all trims. Rare owner complaints focus on driveshaft and carrier bearing wear affecting the powertrain.

**Drivetrain and Power Train Vibration**
- **Symptoms:** Unusual thumping noises or mechanical binding sensations originating from the driveshaft or transmission areas during acceleration.
- **Solution:** Inspect the driveshaft, carrier bearing, and transmission mounts for wear or failure, and replace damaged assemblies.

## Exterior

### Structure

Body hardware is generally solid, though interior door panels are prone to warping and separating from the frame over time.

**Warped Door Panels**
- **Symptoms:** Interior door panels warp, buckle, and pull away from the door frame near the windows, making doors difficult to open or close properly.
- **Solution:** Replace the affected interior door trim panels or repair mounting clips and backing structures.

## Safety

### Seat Belt

Standard safety equipment functions well overall, with minor owner reports regarding seat belt latch reliability.

**Seat Belt Buckle Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Seat belt buckles fail to latch securely or stick intermittently during daily use.
- **Solution:** Inspect the seat belt assembly and replace faulty buckle mechanisms or clips.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18E053000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission:Control Module (Tcm/Pcm/Tecm)](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Dodge&model=Charger&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** The PCMs are being recalled to address a defect that could prevent the cruise control system from disengaging. If, when using cruise control, there is a short circuit within the vehicle's wiring, the driver may not be able to shut off the cruise control either by depressing the brake pedal or manually turning the system off once it has been engaged.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will either perform a software update or replace the PCM, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V332000: Electrical System:Wiring](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Dodge&model=Charger&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** These vehicles are being recalled to address a defect that could prevent the cruise control system from disengaging. If, when using cruise control, there is a short circuit within the vehicle's wiring, the driver may not be able to shut off the cruise control either by depressing the brake pedal or manually turning the system off once it has been engaged.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the software, and perform a software flash on the engine or powertrain control module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V281000: Power Train:Driveline:Driveshaft](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Dodge&model=Charger&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** If the front driveshaft detaches from the vehicle it may become a road hazard, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will replace the front driveshaft, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V824000: Equipment](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Dodge&model=Charger&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** Chrysler (FCA US LLC) is recalling various Dodge, Chrysler, and RAM vehicles equipped with Kidde Plastic-Handle or Push Button 'Pindicator' Fire Extinguishers. These extinguishers may become clogged, preventing the extinguisher from discharging as expected or requiring excessive force to activate the extinguisher.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 17V097000: Power Train:Axle Assembly:Axle Shaft](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Dodge&model=Charger&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** A loss of motive power can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will replace all eight front driveshaft bolts, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V043000: Structure](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Dodge&model=Charger&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** A car that falls unexpectedly increases the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will provide owners with wheel chocks and instructions for their use, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2016 Dodge Charger is an engaging and powerful full-size sedan, but used car buyers should verify that all recalls have been completed and thoroughly check for interior door panel separation and fuel odor symptoms. A comprehensive pre-purchase mechanical inspection will help ensure the powertrain and electrical systems are in solid working order.
