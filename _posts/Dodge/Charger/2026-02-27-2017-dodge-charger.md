# 2017 Dodge Charger Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2017 Dodge Charger, buyers will find a full-size sedan known for its aggressive styling, spacious interior, and muscular engine lineup ranging from the efficient Pentastar V6 to powerhouse HEMI V8s. While it offers an engaging driving experience and strong straight-line performance, potential owners should inspect specific trouble areas such as infotainment system performance, interior door panel durability, and electrical accessories.

---

## Engine

### 3.6L Pentastar V6

The 3.6-liter Pentastar V6 produces approximately 292 to 300 horsepower, serving as the dependable and efficient base engine for everyday driving. It generally offers solid mechanical reliability when properly maintained.

**Fuel Odor or Leak**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may notice a strong smell of fuel or observe signs of a fuel leak near the tank.
- **Solution:** Inspect fuel lines, tank seals, and related components, and replace any leaking parts.

### 5.7L HEMI V8

The 5.7-liter HEMI V8 pushes out around 370 horsepower, delivering classic muscle car performance and a deep exhaust note. It is paired with a robust multi-displacement system for highway cruising efficiency.

### 6.4L HEMI V8

The 6.4-liter HEMI V8 generates a formidable 485 horsepower, powering the Scat Pack trims with track-ready performance. It requires attentive maintenance due to its high-output nature.

### 6.2L Supercharged HEMI V8

The supercharged 6.2-liter HEMI V8 unleashes 707 horsepower in the SRT Hellcat, providing elite performance figures. This engine is built for enthusiast driving and demands meticulous upkeep.

## Transmission

### TorqueFlite 8-Speed Automatic

**Transmission Control Module and Shifting Concerns**
- **Symptoms:** Illuminated service shifter messages, erratic shifting behavior, or diagnostic trouble codes pointing to module or performance faults.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified technician diagnose transmission control module software or replace faulty internal electronic components.

### TorqueFlite 8-Speed Automatic

The TorqueFlite 8-speed automatic transmission delivers crisp, smooth shifts across all V6 and V8 engine configurations. It is generally praised for durability, though electronic controls require monitoring.

## Electrical

### Infotainment and Display System

Electrical accessories and touchscreen modules generally function well, though specific units suffer from screen aging and interface glitches.

**Uconnect Screen Delamination and Ghost Touch**
- **Symptoms:** The touchscreen begins to bubble or delaminate, causing it to randomly activate features like seat heaters, change radio stations, or dial phone numbers independently.
- **Solution:** Replace the faulty infotainment screen digitizer unit or the entire display assembly.

## Exterior

### Door Panels and Trim

Exterior panels and structure remain solid, but interior trim pieces mounted to doors are prone to adhesive failure over time.

**Interior Door Panel Separation**
- **Symptoms:** Interior door panels warp, peel away from the door frame, or separate near the window line, potentially interfering with door locking mechanisms.
- **Solution:** Re-secure, glue, or replace the affected door panels to restore proper fit and function.

## Safety

### Seat Belts

Safety systems provide reliable protection in collisions, but secondary latch components should be verified during a pre-purchase inspection.

**Seat Belt Latch Release Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Rear or passenger seat belts may intermittently fail to lock securely or unexpectedly unbuckle during use.
- **Solution:** Inspect seat belt buckle assemblies and replace faulty latches as necessary.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V332000: Electrical System:Wiring](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Dodge&model=Charger&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** These vehicles are being recalled to address a defect that could prevent the cruise control system from disengaging. If, when using cruise control, there is a short circuit within the vehicle's wiring, the driver may not be able to shut off the cruise control either by depressing the brake pedal or manually turning the system off once it has been engaged.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the software, and perform a software flash on the engine or powertrain control module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V281000: Power Train:Driveline:Driveshaft](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Dodge&model=Charger&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** If the front driveshaft detaches from the vehicle it may become a road hazard, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will replace the front driveshaft, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V097000: Power Train:Axle Assembly:Axle Shaft](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Dodge&model=Charger&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** A loss of motive power can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will replace all eight front driveshaft bolts, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V496000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Engine](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Dodge&model=Charger&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** If an EOC line fails, oil may spray onto the windshield or contact a hot surface, impairing driver visibility and increasing the risk of a crash or fire.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will replace the EOC lines, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2017 Dodge Charger remains a compelling choice for buyers seeking full-size comfort, rear-wheel-drive dynamics, and powerful engine options. While mechanical setups are largely dependable, prospective buyers should thoroughly test the Uconnect touchscreen for ghost inputs and inspect the cabin for warping door panels. A comprehensive pre-purchase inspection will help ensure these common cosmetic and electronic quirks have been addressed.
