# 2017 Dodge Charger

### Overview
The 2017 Dodge Charger is a full-size sedan known for its powerful engine options and rear-wheel-drive platform, offering a blend of performance and practicality. Reliability for this model year is generally considered average to good, with most reported issues being relatively minor and not affecting the core functionality of the vehicle. Owners often praise its robust powertrain options, particularly the V8 engines, and its comfortable ride. However, like many vehicles in its class, some owners have encountered specific issues related to electronics and the transmission, though these are not universally reported across all vehicles. Regular maintenance is key to ensuring the longevity of any Charger.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic (845RE)
**Hesitation/Jerking**
- **Symptoms:** Transmission may exhibit occasional hesitation during acceleration or a slight jerking sensation.
- **Solution:** Software update for transmission control module, fluid flush and replacement.
- **Cost:** $150 - $500

**Slipping Gears**
- **Symptoms:** In rare cases, drivers have reported instances of the transmission slipping between gears.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis of internal transmission components, potential rebuild or replacement.
- **Cost:** $2,000 - $4,500

## Electrical

### Infotainment System
**Uconnect Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Occasional freezing, unresponsiveness, or Bluetooth connectivity issues with the Uconnect system.
- **Solution:** Software update for the Uconnect module, hard reset.
- **Cost:** $100 - $300


### Battery Drain
**Intermittent Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Some owners have reported intermittent battery drain, leading to difficulty starting the vehicle.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis to identify parasitic draw, check for faulty modules or accessories.
- **Cost:** $150 - $600

### Summary Verdict
The 2017 Dodge Charger presents a generally reliable package, especially considering its performance-oriented nature. The powertrain options, from the V6 to the various HEMI V8s, are robust and typically do not suffer from widespread critical failures. The most commonly cited areas for potential concern are the 8-speed automatic transmission, which can occasionally exhibit minor shifting anomalies or, less frequently, slipping, and certain electrical components, particularly the Uconnect infotainment system which may experience software glitches. Battery drain issues, while not prevalent, have also been noted by a small number of owners. Overall, with diligent maintenance and addressing minor electrical or transmission quirks promptly, the 2017 Charger can be a dependable vehicle.
