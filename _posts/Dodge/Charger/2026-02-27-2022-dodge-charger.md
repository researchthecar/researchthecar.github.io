# 2022 Dodge Charger

### Overview
The 2022 Dodge Charger presents a mixed bag of reliability, with its robust powertrain options generally performing well, but some electrical and interior components showing areas for improvement. Owners often praise the performance and durability of the V6 and V8 engines, which are the heart of the Charger's appeal. However, like many modern vehicles, the Charger is not immune to the complexities of advanced electronics and interior materials that can sometimes fall short of expectations. This analysis focuses on documented technical issues beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential concerns for prospective and current owners.

## Electrical

### Infotainment System
**Uconnect Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Occasional freezing, unresponsiveness, or Bluetooth connectivity issues.
- **Solution:** Software updates, hard resets, or potential module replacement.
- **Cost:** $100 - $500


### Battery Drain
**Intermittent Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Battery may discharge prematurely, leading to starting issues. Often linked to parasitic draw from electronic modules.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis of parasitic draw, replacement of faulty modules or battery.
- **Cost:** $200 - $800

## Interior

### Seat Upholstery
**Premature Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Some reports of accelerated wear on cloth or leather seat bolsters, especially on driver's side.
- **Solution:** Professional cleaning, repair, or replacement of seat covers.
- **Cost:** $300 - $1000


### Interior Trim
**Rattles and Squeaks**
- **Symptoms:** Minor rattles or squeaks developing from dashboard or door panels over time.
- **Solution:** Locating and securing loose trim pieces, applying sound dampening material.
- **Cost:** $100 - $300

### Summary Verdict
The 2022 Dodge Charger demonstrates strong mechanical reliability, particularly with its range of V6 and V8 HEMI engines and the robust 8-speed automatic transmission, which are generally free from significant widespread issues. The primary areas of concern tend to be in the electrical system, with occasional infotainment glitches and potential battery drain issues that require diagnosis. The interior, while generally well-built, can sometimes exhibit minor rattles or premature wear on upholstery. Overall, the Charger is a dependable vehicle for its performance segment, with most potential issues being manageable and not indicative of fundamental powertrain flaws.
