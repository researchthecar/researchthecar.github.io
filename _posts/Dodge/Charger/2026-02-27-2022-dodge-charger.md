# 2022 Dodge Charger Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2022 Dodge Charger, buyers will find a bold full-size sedan known for its spacious cabin, iconic muscle-car styling, and powerful engine lineup ranging from efficient V6s to high-output supercharged V8s. While modern Chargers offer plenty of thrills and grand-touring comfort, prospective owners should inspect the vehicle thoroughly for any signs of powertrain surging, electrical glitches, or fluid leaks. Reviewing maintenance records and performing a thorough pre-purchase inspection can help ensure a trouble-free ownership experience.

---

## Engine

### 3.6L V6 (Pentastar)

The 3.6-liter Pentastar V6 produces around 292 to 300 horsepower and serves as the standard powerplant for base and all-wheel-drive trims. It offers a practical balance of everyday driveability, decent fuel economy, and generally dependable daily operation.

### 5.7L V8 (HEMI)

The 5.7-liter HEMI V8 pushes out 370 horsepower, delivering traditional muscle-car performance and a throaty exhaust note for R/T models. It is a robust engine option, though buyers should watch for normal wear associated with high-performance driving.

### 6.4L V8 (HEMI Scat Pack)

The 6.4-liter naturally aspirated HEMI V8 generates 485 horsepower for Scat Pack trims, offering thrilling acceleration and track-ready capabilities. It requires attentive maintenance due to the high-stress nature of its performance output.

### 6.2L Supercharged V8 (Hellcat)

The supercharged 6.2-liter HEMI V8 produces up to 717 or more horsepower, providing extreme, tire-shredding performance for Hellcat models. This powertrain demands meticulous care and a careful pre-purchase inspection to verify that the drivetrain has not been abused.

### Fuel Filler Neck

Manages fuel delivery and filler components for the vehicle's fuel system.

**Fuel Leak and Odor**
- **Symptoms:** A strong gasoline smell inside and outside of the vehicle, accompanied by visible fuel leaking near the rear.
- **Solution:** Take the vehicle to a certified shop or dealership to inspect and replace the defective fuel filler neck and associated welds.

## Transmission

### TorqueFlite 8-Speed Automatic

The TorqueFlite 8-speed automatic transmission is paired with all engine options across rear-wheel and all-wheel-drive configurations, delivering quick shifts and responsive gearing. Overall, it is widely regarded as a robust unit, though occasional software or sensor anomalies can arise.

**Acceleration Surging and Shifting Concerns**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may exhibit unexpected surging during mild acceleration across various gears and speeds, or display warnings to service the shifter.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified technician or dealership inspect the transmission control module, update software, or check mechanical linkage components.

## Steering

### Steering Wheel Controls

Handles interior cabin electronics, instrumentation, and steering-wheel-mounted control switches.

**Steering Wheel Switch and Instrument Cluster Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Power and functionality to the steering wheel-mounted instrument controls and associated switches stop working intermittently or completely.
- **Solution:** Inspect the clockspring, wiring harness, and steering wheel control modules, and replace faulty components as necessary.

## Summary Verdict

The 2022 Dodge Charger is an appealing choice for used-car buyers seeking bold styling, spacious seating, and robust performance options ranging from everyday V6s to formidable supercharged V8s. While overall powertrain durability is solid, prospective buyers should remain vigilant regarding potential fuel system leaks, transmission surging, and electrical control glitches reported by some owners. Conducting a comprehensive pre-purchase inspection and verifying service records will help secure a reliable and enjoyable vehicle.
