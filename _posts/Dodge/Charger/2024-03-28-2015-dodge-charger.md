**Introduction:**

The 2015 Dodge Charger is a full-size sedan known for its powerful V8 engines and sleek design. It offers various engine and transmission options, including a 3.6L V6, 5.7L V8, and 6.4L V8 paired with an 8-speed automatic transmission or a 6-speed manual transmission for the 6.4L V8. The Charger is praised for its muscular styling, spacious interior, and impressive performance, but it also has its share of reported issues.

**Engine**

**3.6L V6**
- **Intake Manifold Gasket Leak:** Misfiring, rough idle, oil leaks
  - Solution: Replace intake manifold gasket
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800+
  - Recalls: None Found
- **Timing Chain Tensioner Failure:** Noises from the engine, rough idle, loss of power
  - Solution: Replace timing chain tensioner
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $1,500+
  - Recalls: None Found

**5.7L V8**

- **Valve Spring Failure:** Engine ticking or knocking, reduced power
  - Solution: Replace valve springs
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,500 - $3,000+
  - Recalls: None Found
- **Bearing Failure:** Noises from the engine, loss of power
  - Solution: Replace engine bearings
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $3,000 - $4,000+
  - Recalls: None Found

**6.4L V8**

- **Connecting Rod Failure:** Engine knocking, loss of power
  - Solution: Rebuild or replace engine
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $5,000 - $10,000+
  - Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**8-Speed Automatic**

- **Torque Converter Shudder:** Vibrations or shudders during acceleration
  - Solution: Replace torque converter
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,000+
  - Recalls: NHTSA ID Number: 18V639000
- **Shift Solenoid Failure:** Harsh or delayed shifts, no shifts
  - Solution: Replace shift solenoid
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800+
  - Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

- **Strut Mount Noise:** Squeaking or clunking sounds from the front suspension
  - Solution: Replace strut mounts
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+
  - Recalls: None Found
- **Rear Suspension Bushing Wear:** Loose or rattling rear end
  - Solution: Replace rear suspension bushings
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $600+
  - Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

- **Body Control Module Failure:** Various electrical issues, such as malfunctioning power windows or locks
  - Solution: Replace body control module
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $1,500+
  - Recalls: None Found
- **Alternator Failure:** Battery discharge, dim headlights
  - Solution: Replace alternator
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800+
  - Recalls: None Found

**Exterior (Exclude Paint Chips and Fading)**

- **Headlight Condensation:** Water buildup inside the headlights
  - Solution: Replace headlights
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
  - Recalls: None Found
- **Tailgate Latch Failure:** Tailgate opens unexpectedly or won't stay closed
  - Solution: Replace tailgate latch
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $300+
  - Recalls: None Found

**Interior (Exclude Leather Seat Cracking or Wear)**

- **Dashboard Rattle:** Loose or rattling dashboard components
  - Solution: Secure or replace dashboard components
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
  - Recalls: None Found
- **HVAC System Malfunction:** AC not blowing cold air, heater not working
  - Solution: Diagnose and repair HVAC system
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
  - Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

The 2015 Dodge Charger has several reported issues, particularly with the engine, transmission, and electrical systems. Some of the issues can be severe and expensive to fix, while others are more minor and less costly. Overall, the reliability of the vehicle is mixed, and it is advisable to be aware of these potential issues before purchasing a used 2015 Charger.
