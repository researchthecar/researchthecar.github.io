## Electrical

### Infotainment System
**Uconnect Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Occasional freezing, unresponsiveness, or Bluetooth connectivity issues.
- **Solution:** Software updates for the Uconnect system or a hard reset.
- **Cost:** $0 - $200


### Battery Drain
**Intermittent Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Unexplained battery drain leading to starting issues, often linked to parasitic draw from certain modules.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis to identify the source of the parasitic draw and repair or replace faulty components.
- **Cost:** $200 - $800

### Summary Verdict
The 2019 Dodge Charger demonstrates good overall reliability, particularly with its robust V6 and V8 engine options, which are generally trouble-free. The primary areas of concern tend to be minor electrical gremlins, such as occasional Uconnect system glitches or intermittent battery drain issues, which can be frustrating but are typically repairable. The 8-speed automatic transmission can sometimes exhibit minor hesitation or shuddering, often resolved with software updates or fluid services. Suspension, exterior, and interior components are largely reported as durable. With proactive maintenance and attention to potential electrical quirks, the 2019 Charger should provide a dependable and engaging driving experience.
