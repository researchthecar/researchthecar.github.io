# 2020 Dodge Charger Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2020 Dodge Charger continues to offer full-size performance, aggressive styling, and a wide variety of engine choices ranging from practical V6 power to supercharged V8 muscle. When shopping for a used Charger, buyers generally find a robust mechanical layout paired with modern comfort, though certain model years experience specific owner complaints regarding safety equipment and interior trim components. Prospective owners should review vehicle history reports closely and check for completed safety updates.

---

## Engine

### 3.6L Pentastar V6

The 3.6L Pentastar V6 engine produces around 292 to 300 horsepower and is standard on SXT and GT trims. It offers daily-driver reliability paired with respectable fuel economy.

### 5.7L HEMI V8

The 5.7L HEMI V8 pushes out 370 horsepower, powering the R/T trim with classic V8 performance and a robust soundtrack. It is generally regarded as a durable option with proper maintenance.

### 6.4L HEMI V8

Displacing 6.4 liters, this naturally aspirated V8 delivers 485 horsepower for the Scat Pack trim. It provides immense acceleration while keeping the mechanical architecture relatively straightforward.

### 6.2L Supercharged HEMI V8

The supercharged 6.2L HEMI V8 powers SRT Hellcat and Hellcat Redeye models, producing up to 717 or 797 horsepower. While incredibly powerful, these high-output variants demand diligent maintenance and thorough pre-purchase inspections.

**A/C Evaporator Core Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Air conditioning suddenly stops blowing cold air.
- **Solution:** Replace the faulty A/C evaporator core through a qualified repair shop or dealership.

## Transmission

### TorqueFlite 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

The ZF-sourced 8-speed automatic transmission delivers crisp, responsive shifts across all V6 and V8 trims. It is widely praised for its durability and refined operation in everyday driving.

## Exterior

### Door Panels

Cabin materials are generally driver-focused, but certain trim pieces can show wear or warping over time.

**Interior Door Panel Warping**
- **Symptoms:** Passenger interior door panel plastic expanding, warping, and shifting toward the window channel.
- **Solution:** Replace or secure the affected interior door panel components.

## Safety

### Air Bags

The supplemental restraint systems are designed to protect occupants during collisions, though owners have submitted complaints regarding unexpected deployments.

**Inadvertent Air Bag Deployment**
- **Symptoms:** Side curtain airbags or roof airbags deploying unexpectedly while parked or during normal driving conditions without impact.
- **Solution:** Consult a dealership to inspect vehicle airbag systems and ensure all safety recall campaigns are addressed.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V512000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Dodge&model=Charger&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 111, "Rearview Mirrors." The lack of a back-up camera display increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will update body control module software to disable stealth mode when the vehicle is shifted into "Reverse", free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V516000: Visibility:Windshield](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Dodge&model=Charger&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** During manufacturing, the windshield may not have been properly bonded to the vehicle, allowing it to detach in a crash. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 212, "Windshield Mounting." A windshield that separates from the vehicle during a crash can increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will remove and replace the front windshield urethane sealant, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V198000: Air Bags:Side/Window:Curtain:Inflator](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Dodge&model=Charger&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** An inflator rupture may result in sharp metal fragments striking occupants, resulting in injury or death.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace both side curtain air bags, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2020 Dodge Charger remains an appealing choice for used-car buyers seeking traditional rear-wheel-drive performance, spacious seating, and strong engine options. While major mechanical failures are uncommon, buyers should carefully verify that all open safety campaigns have been resolved and inspect interior trim and climate control functions during a test drive.
