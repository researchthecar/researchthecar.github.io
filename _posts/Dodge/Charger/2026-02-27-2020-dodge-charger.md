# 2020 Dodge Charger

### Overview
The 2020 Dodge Charger, a popular full-size sedan, offers a range of powerful engine options and a robust chassis. However, like any vehicle, there are specific areas where potential issues have been noted by owners and reported in automotive databases. This analysis focuses on documented technical reliability concerns beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into common problems and their potential resolutions for various configurations of the 2020 model year.

## Electrical

### Infotainment System
**Uconnect Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Occasional freezing, unresponsiveness, or Bluetooth connectivity issues.
- **Solution:** Software updates, hard reset of the system, or replacement of the head unit.
- **Cost:** $100 - $1200


### Battery Drain
**Premature Battery Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Battery dying unexpectedly, requiring jump starts or replacement sooner than expected.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis of parasitic draw, replacement of faulty battery, or checking alternator.
- **Cost:** $200 - $500

### Summary Verdict
The 2020 Dodge Charger generally demonstrates good mechanical reliability, particularly with its core engine and transmission components across all offered powertrains. The Pentastar V6 and various HEMI V8 engines are robust. The 8-speed automatic transmission also performs well without widespread reported issues. Areas that have seen some owner-reported concerns include the electrical system, specifically with the Uconnect infotainment system experiencing occasional glitches and a possibility of premature battery drain. Exterior and interior components are largely free from significant widespread defects. Overall, the Charger is a dependable choice, with most potential issues being relatively minor and addressable, especially when compared to its performance capabilities.
