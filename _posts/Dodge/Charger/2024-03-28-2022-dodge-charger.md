**Introduction:**
The 2022 Dodge Charger is a powerful muscle car that boasts sporty handling and a comfortable interior. It is available with various engine and transmission options, including:

* **Engine:**
    * 3.6L V6: 292 hp, 260 lb-ft
    * 5.7L V8: 370 hp, 410 lb-ft
    * 6.4L V8: 485 hp, 475 lb-ft
    * 6.2L Supercharged V8: 717 hp, 656 lb-ft
* **Transmission:**
    * 8-speed Automatic (all engines)
    * 6-speed Manual (SXT trim only)

Drivers appreciate the Charger's powerful engines, responsive handling, and spacious cabin. However, it has faced some issues, which we will explore in detail below.

**Engine**

* **3.6L V6**
    * **Intake Valve Coking:**
        * Description/Symptoms: Intake valves become clogged with carbon deposits, causing reduced engine power and fuel economy.
        * Solution: Cleaning or replacing the intake valves.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
        * Recalls: None Found
* **5.7L V8**
    * **Cam Phaser Failure:**
        * Description/Symptoms: Cam phasers malfunction, leading to reduced engine power and fuel efficiency.
        * Solution: Replacing the cam phasers.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500
        * Recalls: None Found
* **6.4L V8**
    * **Oil Pump Failure:**
        * Description/Symptoms: Oil pump fails, resulting in insufficient lubrication and engine damage.
        * Solution: Replacing the oil pump.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $1,500
        * Recalls: TSB 21-041-21
* **6.2L Supercharged V8**
    * **Belt Tensioner Failure:**
        * Description/Symptoms: Belt tensioner fails, causing the serpentine belt to slip or break.
        * Solution: Replacing the belt tensioner.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $750
        * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

* **8-speed Automatic**
    * **Torque Converter Shudder:**
        * Description/Symptoms: Transmission shudders during acceleration or cruising.
        * Solution: Replacing the torque converter.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,000
        * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **No Issues Found**

**Electrical**

* **Alternator Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Alternator malfunctions, causing battery drain and electrical system failure.
    * Solution: Replacing the alternator.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $750
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Battery Corrosion:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Battery terminals and cables corrode, leading to poor electrical connections.
    * Solution: Cleaning or replacing battery terminals and cables.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Rear Spoiler Looseness:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Rear spoiler becomes loose, potentially flying off while driving.
    * Solution: Retightening or replacing the spoiler mounting bolts.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $250
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Infotainment System Malfunction:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Infotainment system freezes, crashes, or displays error messages.
    * Solution: Updating the software or replacing the infotainment unit.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

While the 2022 Dodge Charger is a powerful and enjoyable vehicle, it has faced several issues ranging from minor electrical problems to serious engine and transmission concerns. The estimated cost to fix these issues can vary significantly, so it's essential for owners to be aware of potential expenses. The severity of the issues depends on the specific problem and the condition of the vehicle, which should be considered before making a purchase decision.
