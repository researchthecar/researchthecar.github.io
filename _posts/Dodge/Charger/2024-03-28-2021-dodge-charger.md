**Introduction**

The 2021 Dodge Charger is a powerful muscle car that offers a thrilling driving experience. It is available with a variety of engine and transmission options, including a 3.6L V6, 5.7L V8, 6.4L V8, and 6.2L supercharged V8. The Charger is also known for its spacious interior, comfortable seats, and impressive performance.

However, like any vehicle, the Charger has its share of issues. Here is a comprehensive list of the specific problems that people are experiencing with the 2021 Dodge Charger:

**Engine**

* **3.6L V6**
    * **Timing Chain Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: The timing chain may fail, causing the engine to lose power and stall.
        * Solution: Replace the timing chain and tensioners.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
        * Recalls: None Found
* **5.7L V8**
    * **Intake Manifold Gasket Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: The intake manifold gasket may fail, causing the engine to run rough and lose power.
        * Solution: Replace the intake manifold gasket.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
        * Recalls: None Found
* **6.4L V8**
    * **No Issues Found**
* **6.2L Supercharged V8**
    * **Supercharger Bearing Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: The supercharger bearing may fail, causing the supercharger to seize and the engine to lose power.
        * Solution: Replace the supercharger.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,500 - $4,000+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

* **8-Speed Automatic**
    * **Torque Converter Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: The torque converter may fail, causing the transmission to slip and the vehicle to lose power.
        * Solution: Replace the torque converter.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
        * Recalls: None Found
* **10-Speed Automatic**
    * **No Issues Found**

**Suspension**

* **Rear Suspension Lower Control Arm Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The rear suspension lower control arm may fail, causing the vehicle to pull to one side and the tires to wear unevenly.
    * Solution: Replace the rear suspension lower control arm.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Electrical System Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The electrical system may fail, causing the vehicle to lose power, the lights to turn off, and the engine to stall.
    * Solution: Diagnose and repair the electrical system.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,500+
    * Recalls: NHTSA Recall 21V-811, NHTSA Recall 21V-358

**Exterior**

* **Headlight Condensation**
    * Description/Symptoms: The headlights may accumulate condensation, impairing visibility.
    * Solution: Replace the headlights.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Touchscreen Display Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The touchscreen display may fail, causing the infotainment system to malfunction.
    * Solution: Replace the touchscreen display.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Heated and Ventilated Seat Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The heated and ventilated seats may fail, preventing them from providing heat or cooling.
    * Solution: Replace the heated and ventilated seat elements.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2021 Dodge Charger is a powerful muscle car with a variety of engine and transmission options. However, it is not without its issues. The most common problems reported by owners include timing chain failure, intake manifold gasket failure, supercharger bearing failure, torque converter failure, rear suspension lower control arm failure, electrical system failure, headlight condensation, touchscreen display failure, and heated and ventilated seat failure. The severity of these issues can range from minor inconveniences to major safety hazards. The cost to fix these issues can also be significant, ranging from a few hundred dollars to several thousand dollars. Overall, the 2021 Dodge Charger is a capable vehicle, but it is important to be aware of the potential problems that it may have before purchasing.
