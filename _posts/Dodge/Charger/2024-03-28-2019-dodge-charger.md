**Introduction to the 2019 Dodge Charger**

The 2019 Dodge Charger is a popular muscle car known for its powerful engine options and aggressive styling. It offers a range of engine and transmission configurations, including:

* **3.6L Pentastar V6** - 300 hp, 264 lb-ft torque
* **5.7L HEMI V8** - 370 hp, 395 lb-ft torque
* **6.4L HEMI V8** - 485 hp, 475 lb-ft torque
* **6.2L Supercharged HEMI Hellcat V8** - 707 hp, 650 lb-ft torque
* **8-speed automatic transmission**
* **10-speed automatic transmission**

Drivers appreciate the Charger's powerful engines, spacious interior, and bold exterior. However, some owners have reported specific issues with the vehicle.

**Engine**

**3.6L Pentastar V6**
- **Intake Manifold Runner Control Flaps:** Sticking or malfunctioning flaps can cause reduced engine performance and fuel economy. Solution: Replacement of the intake manifold. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
- **Oil Consumption:** Some vehicles have excessive oil consumption, requiring frequent top-ups. Solution: Engine rebuild or replacement of the piston rings. Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $5,000+
- **Timing Chain Tensioner Failure:** A failing tensioner can cause the timing chain to slip, leading to engine damage. Solution: Replacement of the timing chain tensioner. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+

**Transmission**

**8-speed automatic transmission**
- **Torque Converter Shudder:** Owners have experienced shuddering or vibration during acceleration. Solution: Replacement of the torque converter. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
- **Shifting Problems:** Erratic or delayed shifts can be caused by faulty sensors or software issues. Solution: Transmission programming update or component replacement. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+

**Suspension**

- **Rear Control Arm Bushings:** Premature wear of the bushings can lead to excessive noise, vibrations, and handling problems. Solution: Replacement of the bushings. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+

**Electrical**

- **Battery Drain:** Some vehicles experience rapid battery drain when parked. Solution: Investigation and repair of faulty components, such as the alternator or electrical accessories. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
- **Headlight Failure:** Malfunctioning headlights can reduce visibility and safety. Solution: Replacement of the headlights or related components. Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $600+

**Exterior**

- **Hood Scoop Fitment Issues:** The hood scoop on some vehicles may not fit properly, causing noise or water leaks. Solution: Readjustment or replacement of the hood scoop. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
- **Front Fascia Gaps:** There have been reports of gaps or misalignment in the front fascia. Solution: Adjustment or replacement of the fascia. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+

**Interior**

- **Steering Wheel Creaking:** Some drivers have experienced creaking noises from the steering wheel. Solution: Lubrication or replacement of the steering wheel components. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+
- **Heater Core Leakage:** A leaking heater core can cause the cabin to fog up and result in a coolant smell. Solution: Replacement of the heater core. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
- **Dashboard Rattling:** Owners have reported rattling noises coming from the dashboard. Solution: Tightening of loose components or replacement of the dashboard. Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $500+

**Summary**

Overall, while the 2019 Dodge Charger is a powerful and stylish muscle car, it has some known issues that can be costly to repair. The most significant issues involve the engine (oil consumption, timing chain tensioner failure), transmission (torque converter shudder, shifting problems), and suspension (rear control arm bushings). Some owners have also reported electrical, exterior, and interior problems, though these are less prevalent.
