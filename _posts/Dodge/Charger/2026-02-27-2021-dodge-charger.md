# 2021 Dodge Charger Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2021 Dodge Charger offers a compelling blend of classic American muscle, aggressive styling, and spacious sedan practicality. While it remains a popular choice for performance enthusiasts and daily drivers alike, used-car buyers should approach with a balanced perspective, keeping an eye on powertrain behavior and electrical accessories. Reviewing maintenance records and performing a thorough pre-purchase inspection can help ensure you find a reliable example of this iconic sedan.

---

## Engine

### 3.6L Pentastar V6

The 3.6-liter Pentastar V6 engine produces approximately 300 horsepower and 260 lb-ft of torque. It serves as the standard powerplant for base and all-wheel-drive models, offering reliable daily driving performance.

### 5.7L HEMI V8

The 5.7-liter HEMI V8 engine generates 370 horsepower and 395 lb-ft of torque. Found in R/T trims, it provides robust acceleration and a classic V8 exhaust note with generally solid mechanical durability.

### 6.4L HEMI V8

The 6.4-liter HEMI V8 engine pushes out 485 horsepower and 475 lb-ft of torque for the Scat Pack trim. It delivers exhilarating performance for enthusiasts, though owners should monitor routine maintenance closely.

### 6.2L Supercharged HEMI V8

The 6.2-liter supercharged HEMI V8 powers the SRT Hellcat models, producing 717 to 797 horsepower depending on the specific package. It is a high-strung performance engine built for maximum power output.

## Transmission

### TorqueFlite 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

The TorqueFlite 8-speed automatic transmission is paired across nearly the entire Charger lineup, known for crisp shifts and strong gear selection. While generally robust, some owners report occasional harsh shifting behaviors.

**Harsh Downshifting and Gear Warning Messages**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have reported aggressive downshifting into sixth gear accompanied by service transmission warnings or temporary gear inoperability.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified mechanic inspect the transmission control module and fluid condition for diagnostic trouble codes and software updates.

## Suspension

### Rear Control Arms

The suspension system delivers a firm, planted ride quality suited for performance driving, though certain components can experience heavy wear.

**Rear Control Arm Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Unusual popping noises from underneath the vehicle while driving, followed by compromised rear suspension stability.
- **Solution:** Inspect rear control arms and suspension linkages for bending or breakage, and replace damaged components immediately.

## Exterior

### Exterior Lighting Wiring

Electrical systems manage numerous comfort and safety features, but minor wiring or lighting glitches can occasionally surface.

**Headlight Intermittence**
- **Symptoms:** Low or high beam headlights temporarily turning off and back on during operation.
- **Solution:** Check headlight bulbs, connectors, and wiring harnesses for signs of overheating or loose connections.

## Safety

### Seat Belts

Safety equipment generally performs well, though occasional owner complaints highlight mechanical quirks in peripheral restraint hardware.

**Rear Seat Belt Unintended Loosening**
- **Symptoms:** Rear seat belts may loosen unexpectedly while attempting to secure child safety seats or passengers.
- **Solution:** Have the rear seat belt assembly inspected by a dealership or certified technician to ensure proper locking functionality.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V516000: Visibility:Windshield](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Dodge&model=Charger&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** During manufacturing, the windshield may not have been properly bonded to the vehicle, allowing it to detach in a crash. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 212, "Windshield Mounting." A windshield that separates from the vehicle during a crash can increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will remove and replace the front windshield urethane sealant, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V198000: Air Bags:Side/Window:Curtain:Inflator](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Dodge&model=Charger&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** An inflator rupture may result in sharp metal fragments striking occupants, resulting in injury or death.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace both side curtain air bags, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2021 Dodge Charger is an exciting and versatile option for buyers seeking full-size performance and comfort. While most mechanical systems prove dependable, shoppers should pay close attention during test drives to transmission shift quality and suspension noises. Conducting a comprehensive pre-purchase inspection will help catch any lingering electrical or mechanical issues before finalizing your purchase.
