# 2018 Dodge Charger Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2018 Dodge Charger blends aggressive muscle-car styling and spacious comfort with a wide range of potent engine and transmission configurations. While buyers praise its performance, road presence, and everyday usability, used-car shoppers should be mindful of a few recurring pain points. Common complaints involve interior door panel separation, infotainment screen delamination, and occasional electrical or brake concerns.

---

## Engine

### 3.6L V6 Pentastar

A 3.6-liter naturally aspirated V6 engine producing approximately 292 to 300 horsepower. It serves as the baseline powerplant for entry-level trims, offering a practical blend of daily efficiency and reasonable performance.

### 5.7L V8 HEMI

A 5.7-liter naturally aspirated V8 engine generating around 370 horsepower. It provides classic muscle-car power and sound while maintaining reasonable road manners for highway cruising.

### 6.4L V8 HEMI

A 6.4-liter naturally aspirated V8 engine outputting 485 horsepower found in Scat Pack trims. It delivers high-performance acceleration and track-capable capabilities.

### 6.2L Supercharged V8 HEMI

A supercharged 6.2-liter V8 engine pushing out 707 horsepower in SRT Hellcat models. It is an extreme performance option built for maximum straight-line speed.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

A ZF-sourced 8-speed automatic transmission paired across virtually all V6 and V8 engine options. It is widely praised for smooth and responsive gear shifts, contributing to both performance and fuel efficiency.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

Brake system feedback emphasizes the critical need for routine inspection of pads, rotors, and hydraulic components to ensure reliable stopping power.

**Brake System Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have reported unexpected increases in stopping distance or temporary brake unresponsiveness under heavy application.
- **Solution:** Inspect brake pads, rotors, master cylinder, and ABS module for wear or hydraulic failure.

## Electrical

### Infotainment and Uconnect System

Electrical complaints often highlight issues with the Uconnect infotainment display screen failing or behaving erratically due to adhesive degradation.

**Screen Delamination and Phantom Touches**
- **Symptoms:** The touchscreen begins to separate from its adhesive layer (delaminate), causing unresponsiveness, erratic 'phantom' touches, and unintended activation of climate or audio controls.
- **Solution:** Replace the touchscreen display assembly.

## Exterior

### Structure and Door Panels

Exterior structure complaints primarily center around interior door trim integrity and panel detachment over time.

**Door Panel Separation**
- **Symptoms:** The interior door panels and vinyl trim start to warp, detach, or peel away from the door frames, interfering with window and door controls.
- **Solution:** Replace or repair the affected door panels and securing clips.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V512000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Dodge&model=Charger&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 111, "Rearview Mirrors." The lack of a back-up camera display increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will update body control module software to disable stealth mode when the vehicle is shifted into "Reverse", free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V280000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Dodge&model=Charger&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** If the incorrect park lock rod is installed, the transmission may not shift into 'PARK' and keep the vehicle from moving, increasing the risk of unintended vehicle movement and the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will install the correct park lock rod, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V332000: Electrical System:Wiring](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Dodge&model=Charger&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** These vehicles are being recalled to address a defect that could prevent the cruise control system from disengaging. If, when using cruise control, there is a short circuit within the vehicle's wiring, the driver may not be able to shut off the cruise control either by depressing the brake pedal or manually turning the system off once it has been engaged.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the software, and perform a software flash on the engine or powertrain control module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V281000: Power Train:Driveline:Driveshaft](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Dodge&model=Charger&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** If the front driveshaft detaches from the vehicle it may become a road hazard, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will replace the front driveshaft, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V524000: Electrical System:Wiring:Front Underhood](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Dodge&model=Charger&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** A vehicle stall can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will replace the powertrain control module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V198000: Air Bags:Side/Window:Curtain:Inflator](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Dodge&model=Charger&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** An inflator rupture may result in sharp metal fragments striking occupants, resulting in injury or death.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace both side curtain air bags, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2018 Dodge Charger is an appealing option for used-car buyers looking for rear-wheel-drive performance, bold styling, and spacious seating. While the mechanical powertrains are generally robust when properly maintained, prospective owners should thoroughly inspect the condition of the interior door panels and verify that the touchscreen infotainment system operates without delamination or freezing issues.
