# 2012 Dodge Charger

### Overview
The 2012 Dodge Charger, a full-size sedan, offered a range of powertrain options and a rear-wheel-drive platform, appealing to those seeking performance and a distinctive style. This model year generally presented a solid reliability record, with most reported issues being relatively minor or specific to certain configurations. Owners often praised its comfortable ride, powerful engine choices, and spacious interior. However, like many vehicles of its era, some common areas for attention did emerge, particularly concerning the electrical system and specific powertrain components under certain conditions. This analysis focuses on documented technical issues beyond routine maintenance.

---


## Engine

### 3.6L Pentastar V6
**Intake Manifold Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Coolant leaks, engine overheating, rough idle.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the intake manifold assembly.
- **Cost:** $400 - $700

**Oil Cooler Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Coolant loss, oil in coolant, potential engine damage if ignored.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the oil cooler and seals.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600


### 5.7L HEMI V8
**Fuel Injector Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Misfires, rough idle, reduced fuel economy, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of faulty fuel injectors.
- **Cost:** $400 - $800

**Oil Pressure Sensor Failures**
- **Symptoms:** Inaccurate oil pressure readings, illuminated oil pressure warning light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the oil pressure sensor.
- **Cost:** $100 - $250

### Summary Verdict
The 2012 Dodge Charger is generally considered a reliable vehicle, especially when equipped with the more common 3.6L V6 or 5.7L V8 engines. The most frequently reported technical issues tend to be related to the intake manifold and oil cooler on the V6, and occasional fuel injector or oil pressure sensor problems on the V8. The 8-speed automatic transmission is notably reliable, while the older 5-speed can sometimes exhibit shifting issues. Electrical gremlins, particularly with the Uconnect system and occasional battery drain, are also noted but not universally problematic. Overall, with regular maintenance and attention to these specific potential issues, the 2012 Charger can provide dependable service.
