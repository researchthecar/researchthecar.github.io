**Introduction:**

The 1995 Dodge Caravan is a popular minivan known for its spacious interior, versatility, and affordability. It is available with various engine and transmission options, including the 2.4L 4-cylinder and 3.0L V6 engines, and a 3-speed automatic transmission. Despite its overall appeal, the Caravan has a number of issues that owners have reported.

**Engine Issues**

**2.4L 4-Cylinder Engine**

* **Blown Head Gasket:** Warped cylinder head or compromised head gasket causes coolant and oil leaks, engine overheating, and white smoke from the exhaust.
   - Solution: Replace the cylinder head and head gasket.
   - Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
   - Recalls: None Found

**3.0L V6 Engine**

* **Intake Manifold Gasket Leak:** Gasket between the intake manifold and cylinder head fails, leading to vacuum leaks, rough idle, and decreased engine performance.
   - Solution: Replace the intake manifold gasket.
   - Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+
   - Recalls: None Found

**Transmission Issues**

**3-Speed Automatic Transmission**

* **Slipping Gears:** Transmission slips out of gear while driving, causing loss of power.
   - Solution: Rebuild or replace the transmission.
   - Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,200 - $3,000+
   - Recalls: None Found

**Suspension Issues**

* **Rear Coil Springs Sagging:** Coils springs in the rear suspension lose tension, resulting in a lower ride height, reduced handling, and premature tire wear.
   - Solution: Replace the rear coil springs.
   - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
   - Recalls: None Found

**Electrical Issues**

* **Battery Drain:** Weak or faulty battery drains overnight or struggles to start the engine.
   - Solution: Replace the battery or diagnose and repair electrical system issues.
   - Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
   - Recalls: None Found

**Exterior Issues**

* **Sliding Door Handle Failure:** Plastic handle on the sliding door breaks, making it difficult to open or close the door.
   - Solution: Replace the sliding door handle assembly.
   - Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300+
   - Recalls: None Found

**Interior Issues**

* **Climate Control Fan Motor Noise:** Fan motor in the climate control system makes excessive noise or fails to blow air.
   - Solution: Replace the fan motor.
   - Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300+
   - Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

The 1995 Dodge Caravan has several issues that can be costly to repair, including blown head gaskets, transmission slipping, and suspension problems. The severity of these issues varies, but some, such as a blown head gasket, can lead to catastrophic engine failure. Owners should be aware of these potential issues and address them promptly to avoid further damage.
