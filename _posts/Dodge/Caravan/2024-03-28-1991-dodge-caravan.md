**Introduction:**

The 1991 Dodge Caravan was a popular minivan known for its spacious interior and versatile seating configurations. It came with a variety of engine and transmission options, including a 2.5L inline-4, 3.0L V6, and 3-speed automatic or 5-speed manual transmission. Drivers appreciated the Caravan's practicality and affordability, but it had some common issues that owners should be aware of.

**Engine**

**2.5L Inline-4**

* **Cylinder Head Gasket Failure:**
    * Oil or coolant leaks, overheating, loss of power
    * Replace cylinder head gasket, resurface cylinder head
    * $1000 - $1500+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Intake Manifold Gasket Leak:**
    * Vacuum leaks, rough idle, check engine light
    * Replace intake manifold gasket
    * $300 - $400+
    * Recalls: None Found

**3.0L V6**

* **Oil Pump Failure:**
    * Low oil pressure, engine noises, loss of power
    * Replace oil pump
    * $600 - $800+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Engine Timing Chain Tensioner Failure:**
    * Engine rattles on startup, loss of power
    * Replace timing chain tensioner
    * $300 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**3-Speed Automatic**

* **Transmission Slipping:**
    * Gears slip or fail to engage, loss of power
    * Rebuild or replace transmission
    * $1500 - $2500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**5-Speed Manual**

* **Clutch Master Cylinder Failure:**
    * Difficulty shifting gears, spongy clutch pedal
    * Replace clutch master cylinder
    * $200 - $300+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Strut Mounts Worn:**
    * Clunking noises over bumps, reduced handling
    * Replace strut mounts
    * $400 - $600+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Ball Joint Wear:**
    * Loose or clunking steering, uneven tire wear
    * Replace ball joints
    * $200 - $300+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Alternator Failure:**
    * Battery drains, dimming lights, electrical problems
    * Replace alternator
    * $400 - $600+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Starter Solenoid Failure:**
    * Difficulty starting the engine, clicking noises
    * Replace starter solenoid
    * $200 - $300+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Power Window Failure:**
    * Windows stuck or slow to operate
    * Replace window motors or switches
    * $200 - $400+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Tailgate Hinge Corrosion:**
    * Rusted or broken tailgate hinges
    * Replace tailgate hinges
    * $100 - $200+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Dashboard Crackling:**
    * Dashboard material cracks and deteriorates
    * Replacement dashboard cover or dash repair
    * $200 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Air Conditioning Compressor Failure:**
    * No cold air, warm airflow
    * Replace air conditioning compressor
    * $600 - $1000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

Overall, the 1991 Dodge Caravan has some common issues that can impact its reliability and functionality. Engine problems, such as cylinder head gasket failure and oil pump failure, can be costly to fix. Transmission issues, including slipping and clutch master cylinder failure, can also cause significant inconvenience. The Caravan's suspension and electrical systems are generally reliable, but issues like worn strut mounts and alternator failure can occur. Interior issues, such as dashboard crackling and air conditioning compressor failure, can affect comfort and convenience.

While the 1991 Dodge Caravan has its drawbacks, it remains a popular choice for families and budget-minded drivers due to its spacious and versatile design. Proper maintenance and timely repairs can help extend the life of the vehicle and minimize the impact of these common issues.
