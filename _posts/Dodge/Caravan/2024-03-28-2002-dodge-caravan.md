**Introduction:**

The 2002 Dodge Caravan is a popular minivan offering a spacious interior, versatile seating arrangements, and a comfortable ride. It is powered by a 3.3L V6 engine paired with a 4-speed automatic transmission, delivering 180 horsepower and 210 lb-ft of torque. Despite its practicality, the Caravan has faced several issues over the years.

**Engine**

* **3.3L V6**
    * **Intake Manifold Gasket Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: Coolant or oil leaks from the intake manifold area, engine overheating, decreased performance.
        * Solution: Replace intake manifold gasket.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $750
        * Recalls: None Found
    * **Water Pump Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: Leaking coolant, overheating, engine noise.
        * Solution: Replace water pump.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500
        * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

* **4-Speed Automatic**
    * **Shift Solenoid Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: Delayed or hard shifting, transmission slipping.
        * Solution: Replace shift solenoid.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
        * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Strut Mounting Bearing Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: Noises from the front suspension when turning or braking.
        * Solution: Replace strut mounting bearing.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $250
        * Recalls: None Found
* **Sway Bar Link Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: Noises from the front suspension when driving over bumps.
        * Solution: Replace sway bar link.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200
        * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Power Window Regulator Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: Inability to raise or lower power windows.
        * Solution: Replace power window regulator.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
        * Recalls: None Found
* **Ignition Switch Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: Difficulty starting the vehicle, engine stalling.
        * Solution: Replace ignition switch.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300
        * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Sliding Door Cable Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: Sliding doors become difficult to open or close.
        * Solution: Replace sliding door cable.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200
        * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Climate Control Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: Inability to adjust temperature or air flow.
        * Solution: Replace climate control module or associated components.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600
        * Recalls: None Found
* **Dashboard Cracking**
        * Description/Symptoms: Cracks or warping in the plastic dashboard.
        * Solution: Replace dashboard or apply a dashboard cover.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500
        * Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

The 2002 Dodge Caravan is a practical minivan but has faced several issues with its engine, transmission, suspension, electrical system, exterior, and interior components. While some repairs are minor and affordable, others can be more costly. The severity and frequency of these issues vary depending on the specific model and maintenance history.
