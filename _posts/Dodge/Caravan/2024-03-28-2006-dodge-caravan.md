**2006 Dodge Caravan**

The 2006 Dodge Caravan is a versatile minivan that offers both practicality and comfort. It is powered by a 3.3L V6 engine that produces 180 horsepower and 210 lb-ft of torque, paired with a 4-speed automatic transmission. The Caravan has a spacious interior with seating for up to seven passengers, while the exterior features sliding rear doors and a power liftgate. While the Caravan is popular for its roomy interior and affordable price point, it has also been plagued by a number of issues reported by owners.

**Engine**

* **3.3L V6**
    * **Intake Manifold Gasket Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaks, overheating, loss of performance
        * Solution: Replace intake manifold gasket
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800+
        * Recalls: None Found
    * **Head Gasket Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaks, overheating, white smoke from exhaust
        * Solution: Replace head gasket
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

* **4-Speed Automatic**
    * **TCC Solenoid Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: Slipping gears, erratic shifting, check engine light
        * Solution: Replace TCC solenoid
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
        * Recalls: None Found
    * **Transmission Fluid Leak**
        * Description/Symptoms: Transmission fluid leaks, slipping gears, rough shifting
        * Solution: Fix or replace leaking component
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Front Struts**
    * **Strut Mount Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: Clunking noises, poor handling, uneven tire wear
        * Solution: Replace strut mount
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
        * Recalls: None Found
* **Rear Sway Bar**
    * **Sway Bar Link Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: Clunking noises, poor handling, unstable ride
        * Solution: Replace sway bar link
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Battery**
    * **Battery Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: Vehicle won't start, dimming headlights, electrical system issues
        * Solution: Replace battery
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
        * Recalls: None Found
* **Alternator**
    * **Alternator Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: Dimming headlights, electrical system issues, battery won't charge
        * Solution: Replace alternator
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Body**
    * **Rust Corrosion**
        * Description/Symptoms: Rust formation on body panels, particularly around wheel arches and undercarriage
        * Solution: Bodywork repairs, rust proofing
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **HVAC**
    * **Blower Motor Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: Inability to heat or cool vehicle, poor airflow
        * Solution: Replace blower motor
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
        * Recalls: None Found
* **Door Locks**
    * **Power Door Lock Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: Inability to lock or unlock doors using power locks
        * Solution: Repair or replace door lock mechanism
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+
        * Recalls: 1C0474000

**Summary**

The 2006 Dodge Caravan has faced a range of issues, including engine gasket failures, transmission problems, suspension issues, electrical failures, and interior concerns. While many of the repairs are relatively minor and affordable, some issues like engine gasket failure and transmission replacement can be costly. The Caravan's rust corrosion issue is particularly notable and may require significant bodywork repairs. Despite its shortcomings, the Caravan remains a popular choice due to its spacious interior and affordable price point, but potential buyers should be aware of the potential issues they may encounter.
