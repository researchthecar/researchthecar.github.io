## 2001 Dodge Ram 1500

The 2001 Dodge Ram 1500 is a full-size pickup truck that offers a range of engine and transmission options. The standard engine is a 5.2L V8 with 230 horsepower and 325 lb-ft of torque. Other engine options include a 5.9L V8 with 245 horsepower and 350 lb-ft of torque, and a 4.7L V8 with 235 horsepower and 290 lb-ft of torque. Transmission options include a four-speed automatic, a five-speed manual, and a six-speed manual.

Drivers like the Ram 1500 for its powerful engines, comfortable interior, and spacious cabin. However, some owners have reported problems with the vehicle's reliability, including issues with the engine, transmission, suspension, and electrical system.

### Engine

**5.2L V8**

* **Intake manifold gasket failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaks, overheating, rough idle
    * Solution: Replace intake manifold gasket
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
    * Recalls: 01V223000
* **Exhaust manifold cracking**
    * Description/Symptoms: Exhaust leaks, loss of power
    * Solution: Replace exhaust manifold
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600
    * Recalls: None Found

**5.9L V8**

* **Head gasket failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaks, overheating, white smoke from exhaust
    * Solution: Replace head gasket
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,000
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Crankshaft position sensor failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Stalling, difficulty starting
    * Solution: Replace crankshaft position sensor
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
    * Recalls: None Found

**4.7L V8**

* **Timing chain failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Engine rattling, loss of power
    * Solution: Replace timing chain
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,200
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Ignition coil failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Misfires, reduced engine performance
    * Solution: Replace ignition coil
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200
    * Recalls: None Found
