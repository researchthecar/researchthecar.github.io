# 2021 Dodge Challenger Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2021 Dodge Challenger continues to offer muscle-car performance with everyday cruiser comfort, but prospective buyers should inspect specific mechanical and electrical systems before purchasing. Owner reports to federal safety regulators highlight occasional concerns ranging from powertrain behavior and steering functionality to interior seat failures. While these complaints represent isolated owner experiences rather than guaranteed defects, reviewing them helps used-car shoppers perform targeted pre-purchase inspections.

---

## Engine

### 3.6L Pentastar V6

The 3.6-liter Pentastar V6 produces around 300 horsepower and serves as the standard engine for entry-level trims paired with rear-wheel or all-wheel drive. It offers a balanced mix of daily driveability and efficiency, though isolated owner complaints note unusual electrical or thermal incidents.

**Unexpected Engine Fire**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle experienced an unexpected engine fire while at a standstill.
- **Solution:** Have the vehicle thoroughly inspected by a certified mechanic or fire investigator to identify the source of the thermal event.

### 5.7L HEMI V8

The 5.7-liter HEMI V8 pushes out roughly 375 horsepower, providing traditional muscle car performance with a satisfying exhaust note. It is a generally robust pushrod V8 engine popular among enthusiasts seeking classic V8 power.

### 6.4L HEMI V8

The 6.4-liter HEMI V8 generates an impressive 485 horsepower for R/T Scat Pack models, delivering strong acceleration and track-ready capabilities. This larger displacement engine is built for high performance but requires diligent maintenance.

### 6.2L Supercharged HEMI V8

The supercharged 6.2-liter HEMI V8 powers SRT Hellcat models, producing upwards of 707 to over 800 horsepower depending on the specific sub-trim. It is a high-output specialty engine designed for extreme power delivery.

## Transmission

### TorqueFlite 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

The TorqueFlite 8-speed automatic transmission delivers smooth and rapid gear changes across V6 and V8 configurations. While widely praised for responsiveness, a small number of owners have reported unusual shifting behavior or hesitation.

**Unexpected Shifting to Neutral**
- **Symptoms:** Car moves forward briefly and then unexpectedly shifts into neutral while driving.
- **Solution:** Inspect transmission control modules and fluid levels, and have a qualified technician diagnose internal mechanical components.

**Drivetrain Thumping and Lurching**
- **Symptoms:** The transmission or driveshaft produces a thumping noise and causes the car to lurch upon start-up.
- **Solution:** Check transmission mounts, driveshaft couplers, and software calibration for potential updates.

### TREMEC 6-Speed Manual Transmission

The TREMEC 6-speed manual transmission is paired exclusively with select V8 engine options for drivers seeking a traditional, engaging shift-it-yourself experience. It is a stout unit built to handle high torque output.

## Steering

### Electric Power Steering System

The electric power steering system provides adjustable steering effort and modern road feel. However, sudden steering loss reports indicate that buyers should verify steering assist responsiveness during a test drive.

**Sudden Steering Loss**
- **Symptoms:** The electric steering rack fails completely during normal driving, locking the steering wheel or rendering steering inoperative without warning.
- **Solution:** Inspect the electric power steering motor, wiring harness, and control modules, replacing faulty steering rack assemblies if necessary.

## Interior

### Power Driver Seat

The Challenger interior provides spacious seating for a coupe, but mechanical track or recliner failures can occasionally occur in power-adjustable seats.

**Driver Seat Reclines Unexpectedly**
- **Symptoms:** The back of the driver seat suddenly collapses backward into a flat position while traveling at highway speeds.
- **Solution:** Inspect the seat recline mechanism, gears, and power switch assembly for internal mechanical failure.

## Safety

### Seat Belts

Safety restraint systems are critical for occupant protection, and proper retractor operation should be confirmed on any used model.

**Seat Belt Retractor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The seat belt is slow to retract or jams entirely, eventually refusing to pull out from the retractor housing.
- **Solution:** Replace the faulty seat belt assembly and retractor mechanism.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V664000: Electrical System: Instrument Cluster/Panel](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Dodge&model=Challenger&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 101, "Control and Displays." Without visible safety system indicators, a driver may not receive information regarding vehicle safety, increasing the risk of injury or a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will re-program instrument clusters with the correct software version, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V516000: Visibility:Windshield](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Dodge&model=Challenger&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** During manufacturing, the windshield may not have been properly bonded to the vehicle, allowing it to detach in a crash. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 212, "Windshield Mounting." A windshield that separates from the vehicle during a crash can increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will remove and replace the front windshield urethane sealant, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2021 Dodge Challenger is an appealing choice for buyers wanting bold styling and potent engine options, but a comprehensive pre-purchase inspection is strongly recommended. Pay close attention to powertrain operation, steering responsiveness, and interior electronics to ensure a trouble-free ownership experience.
