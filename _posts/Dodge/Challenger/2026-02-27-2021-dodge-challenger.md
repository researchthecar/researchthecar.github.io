This analysis focuses on documented technical issues beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential problem areas for the various engine and transmission configurations available. Understanding these potential issues can help prospective buyers and current owners make informed decisions regarding maintenance and potential repairs.

### Summary Verdict
The 2021 Dodge Challenger demonstrates strong reliability across its powertrain options, with the 3.6L V6 and various HEMI V8 engines performing commendably. Both the automatic and manual transmissions are generally trouble-free. The primary areas of concern are minor electrical issues, specifically with the Uconnect infotainment system experiencing occasional glitches and a possibility of premature battery drain. These are not widespread critical failures but rather common electronic annoyances or potential maintenance items. The suspension, exterior, and interior components are largely free of significant reported problems. Overall, the Challenger is a reliable choice for a performance-oriented vehicle, with most potential issues being manageable and not indicative of systemic flaws.
