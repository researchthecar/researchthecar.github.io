# 2023 Dodge Challenger Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2023 Dodge Challenger serves as the final model year for this generation of the iconic American muscle car. Offering a blend of classic styling, spacious cabin room for its class, and a wide array of high-horsepower powertrain choices, it attracts enthusiasts seeking traditional performance. As a used buy, owner feedback indicates solid mechanical fundamentals, though buyers should watch for a few recurring cosmetic and body-related complaints, particularly surrounding glass durability.

---

## Engine

### 3.6L Pentastar V6

A 3.6-liter V6 producing approximately 303 horsepower, paired exclusively with rear-wheel drive or all-wheel drive on select trims. It provides a reliable, daily-driver friendly entry point into the Challenger lineup.

### 5.7L HEMI V8

A 5.7-liter V8 engine pushing around 372 to 375 horsepower, available on R/T trims. Known for classic muscle car rumble and robust performance with generally dependable long-term durability.

### 6.4L SRT HEMI V8

A 6.4-liter V8 delivering 485 horsepower found in Scat Pack models. Offers thrilling acceleration and track-ready capabilities while maintaining conventional naturally aspirated reliability.

### 6.2L Supercharged HEMI V8

A supercharged powerhouse generating from 717 horsepower up to over 800 horsepower in Hellcat and Jailbreak configurations. Requires diligent maintenance and premium fuel, appealing strictly to high-performance seekers.

## Transmission

### TorqueFlite 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

A widely praised ZF-sourced 8-speed automatic transmission offering smooth, fast shifts across V6 and V8 models. It is generally considered robust and trouble-free.

### TREMEC 6-Speed Manual Transmission

A driver-engaged 6-speed manual gearbox paired selectively with V8 trims like the R/T and Scat Pack. It provides an engaging shifting experience with a sturdy mechanical build.

## Electrical

### Infotainment and Backup Camera

Electrical system reports highlight minor touchscreen and backup camera reliability quirks that occasionally require software resets.

**Backup Camera Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** The backup camera display intermittently goes blank, displays error messages reading 'not available', or freezes during startup.
- **Solution:** Software updates or replacement of the camera module/radio synchronization components by a dealer may resolve the issue.

## Exterior

### Windshield

Exterior complaints center primarily on reports of unprovoked windshield stress cracks linked by owners to body flex or factory bonding.

**Spontaneous Windshield Cracking**
- **Symptoms:** Owners report stress cracks appearing on the passenger or driver sides near the A-pillar without any prior impact from road debris.
- **Solution:** Have the windshield professionally replaced and ensure the frame bonding and sealing are properly inspected.

### Seats

Interior owner feedback notes occasional mechanical frustrations with front passenger seat locking mechanisms and easy-entry returns in two-door models.

**Passenger Seat Latch and Return Failures**
- **Symptoms:** The passenger front seat fails to lock securely into place or does not return to its original home position after using the easy-entry handle.
- **Solution:** Inspect seat track mechanisms and latches, and have faulty mechanical assemblies replaced or adjusted.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V112000: Parking Brake](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Dodge&model=Challenger&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 135, "Light Vehicle Brake Systems." A vehicle roll away increases the risk of a crash or injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will properly adjust the parking brake, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V573000: Seats:Critical Fasteners](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Dodge&model=Challenger&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** The left rear seat back may not lock in the upright position. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 207, "Seating Systems." A seat back that is not locked into position can increase the risk of injury in a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and adjust the seat back latch striker as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2023 Dodge Challenger provides a muscular grand touring experience with a robust selection of engines and transmissions. While prospective buyers should thoroughly inspect the windshield for stress cracks and verify proper operation of the infotainment and seat latches, the vehicle generally proves mechanically dependable. A pre-purchase inspection focusing on the body, glass, and electronics will help ensure a confident used-car purchase.
