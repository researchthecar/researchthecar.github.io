# 2018 Dodge Challenger Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2018 Dodge Challenger remains a popular choice for muscle car enthusiasts, offering iconic styling, roomy interior packaging for its class, and a broad range of potent engine options. However, prospective buyers should inspect used models carefully. Owner reports submitted to federal safety regulators highlight recurring complaints regarding infotainment screen failures, electrical anomalies, and occasional steering or drivetrain concerns.

---

## Engine

### 3.6L Pentastar V6

This 3.6-liter naturally aspirated V6 engine produces approximately 305 horsepower and 268 lb-ft of torque. It provides dependable daily driveability and decent fuel economy, making it a common choice for base models.

### 5.7L Hemi V8

The 5.7-liter Hemi V8 pushes out around 372 to 375 horsepower and 400 lb-ft of torque. It offers classic V8 performance and a robust mechanical track record in standard R/T trims.

### 6.4L SRT Hemi V8

Displacing 6.4 liters, this heavy-hitting naturally aspirated V8 delivers 485 horsepower and 475 lb-ft of torque. It is tailored for high-performance driving enthusiasts seeking rapid acceleration.

### 6.2L Supercharged Hellcat V8

Equipped with a massive supercharger, this elite 6.2-liter engine cranks out 707 horsepower and 650 lb-ft of torque. It requires vigilant maintenance and respectful handling due to its extreme output.

## Transmission

### TorqueFlite 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

This ZF-sourced 8-speed automatic transmission is widely praised for smooth, rapid shifting and overall durability across various engine configurations.

### Tremec 6-Speed Manual Transmission

The Tremec 6-speed manual transmission pairs with select V8 engines for an engaging driving experience. While generally robust, a small number of owners have reported premature clutch failure.

**Clutch Failure and Gear Shifter Binding**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may experience the manual shifter getting stuck in gear, followed by a loud bang, chattering noises, and clutch components breaking apart.
- **Solution:** Requires replacement of the shattered clutch assembly and inspection of the manual transmission components.

## Steering

### Power Steering Assist

The electronic power steering system generally performs well, though isolated failures can lead to sudden loss of power assist.

**Power Steering Assist Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The instrument panel displays a warning message regarding power steering assist failure, making the steering wheel very heavy and difficult to turn.
- **Solution:** Diagnose the electronic power steering module and wiring harness, replacing faulty components as necessary.

## Electrical

### Infotainment and Display System

Electrical complaints for this model year frequently center on the infotainment display, where screen degradation can cause erratic, distracting behavior.

**Screen Delamination and Phantom Touches**
- **Symptoms:** The Uconnect touchscreen begins to delaminate, causing phantom touches that randomly activate audio controls, climate settings, and emergency dial features.
- **Solution:** Replacing the defective infotainment display unit.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18E053000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission:Control Module (Tcm/Pcm/Tecm)](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Dodge&model=Challenger&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** The PCMs are being recalled to address a defect that could prevent the cruise control system from disengaging. If, when using cruise control, there is a short circuit within the vehicle's wiring, the driver may not be able to shut off the cruise control either by depressing the brake pedal or manually turning the system off once it has been engaged.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will either perform a software update or replace the PCM, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V280000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Dodge&model=Challenger&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** If the incorrect park lock rod is installed, the transmission may not shift into 'PARK' and keep the vehicle from moving, increasing the risk of unintended vehicle movement and the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will install the correct park lock rod, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V332000: Electrical System:Wiring](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Dodge&model=Challenger&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** These vehicles are being recalled to address a defect that could prevent the cruise control system from disengaging. If, when using cruise control, there is a short circuit within the vehicle's wiring, the driver may not be able to shut off the cruise control either by depressing the brake pedal or manually turning the system off once it has been engaged.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the software, and perform a software flash on the engine or powertrain control module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V524000: Electrical System:Wiring:Front Underhood](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Dodge&model=Challenger&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** A vehicle stall can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will replace the powertrain control module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2018 Dodge Challenger is a compelling muscle car with strong powertrain options and comfortable cruising dynamics. While major mechanical failures are relatively infrequent, used car shoppers should thoroughly check the infotainment screen for delamination and test all electrical and manual clutch components during a pre-purchase inspection.
