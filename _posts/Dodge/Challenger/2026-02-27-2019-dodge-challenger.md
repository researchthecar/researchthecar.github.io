# 2019 Dodge Challenger Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2019 Dodge Challenger delivers classic muscle-car styling and impressive power options ranging from everyday V6 efficiency to supercharged V8 performance. While many owners enjoy its highway cruising capability and spacious interior for a coupe, prospective used car buyers should pay close attention to potential issues involving steering responsiveness, electrical accessories, and powertrain components. Reviewing service records and conducting a thorough pre-purchase inspection can help uncover any lingering electrical issues or mechanical wear before buying.

---

## Engine

### 3.6L Pentastar V6

The 3.6-liter Pentastar V6 produces 305 horsepower and 268 lb-ft of torque, serving as the base engine for SXT and GT trims. It offers a balanced mix of everyday drivability and manageable fuel economy, making it a solid choice for daily commuting.

### 5.7L HEMI V8

The 5.7-liter HEMI V8 powers R/T models, putting out up to 375 horsepower and 410 lb-ft of torque. It provides traditional muscle-car acceleration and a deep exhaust note while remaining relatively dependable under normal maintenance schedules.

### 6.4L SRT HEMI V8

The 6.4-liter SRT HEMI V8 delivers 485 horsepower and 475 lb-ft of torque for the Scat Pack trims. This high-output naturally aspirated engine offers thrilling straight-line performance but demands diligent fluid checks and attentive driving.

### 6.2L Supercharged HEMI V8

The supercharged 6.2-liter HEMI V8 found in Hellcat models produces immense horsepower figures starting around 717 and climbing higher. It is a high-strung performance motor meant for enthusiasts who are prepared for specialized maintenance and higher running costs.

## Transmission

### TorqueFlite 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

The widely used TorqueFlite 8-speed automatic transmission pairs smoothly with most engine options, offering crisp shifts and good highway efficiency. Software updates and routine fluid changes generally keep this gearbox running reliably.

**Transmission Engagement and Shifting Hesitation**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle experiences delayed acceleration response, gets stuck in gear when parked, or displays unexpected gear shift ratio errors while driving in traffic.
- **Solution:** Perform a powertrain diagnostic test to check for fault codes, update transmission control module software, or inspect internal hydraulic components.

### Tremac 6-Speed Manual Transmission

The 6-speed manual transmission is available exclusively on select V8 models like the R/T, offering an engaging, traditional shifting experience for driving purists.

**Manual Shifting Pop-Out**
- **Symptoms:** The manual shifter intermittently slips or pops out of first gear while attempting to accelerate from a stop.
- **Solution:** Inspect the shifter linkage, synchronizers, and transmission mount assembly; replace worn internal components or adjust linkage as needed.

## Steering

### Steering Rack and Column

The steering system generally provides responsive handling, though owner reports indicate occasional binding or loss of power assist that requires professional inspection.

**Power Steering Binding and Cut-Out**
- **Symptoms:** Steering wheel intermittently catches or sticks in a notch while cornering, or power assist cuts out entirely, making the steering heavy and difficult to control.
- **Solution:** Inspect the power steering rack, electronic assist motor, and intermediate steering shaft; replace defective steering rack assemblies if internal binding is found.

## Electrical

### Uconnect Infotainment and Electronics

Electrical accessories and infotainment features add modern convenience to the cabin, but software glitches and intermittent display failures can occasionally frustrate drivers.

**Infotainment Screen Freeze and Backup Camera Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** The Uconnect display screen goes black, becomes completely unresponsive, or remains stuck on the rearview camera view after shifting into drive.
- **Solution:** Perform a system software update, execute hard/soft resets, or replace the radio receiver unit if hardware failure persists.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V191000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Dodge&model=Challenger&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 111, "Rearview Mirrors." The lingering rearview image can distract the driver, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Chrysler has notified owners, and dealers will update the radio display software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V203000: Electrical System: Instrument Cluster/Panel](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Dodge&model=Challenger&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** The instrument cluster may not properly illuminate driver warnings. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 101, "Control and Displays." Failure of the instrument cluster to properly illuminate may prevent the driver from receiving warnings about the vehicle's systems, increasing.
- **What to do:** Chrysler has notified owners, and dealers will update the instrument cluster software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V758000: Service Brakes](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Dodge&model=Challenger&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** A damaged tire can suddenly lose air pressure and increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** FCA US will notify owners, and dealers will replace the front wheels and inspect and replace the front tires, as necessary. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2019 Dodge Challenger is an appealing choice for buyers seeking classic muscle car styling, comfortable cruising, and a wide selection of potent engines. While major mechanical failures are relatively uncommon, used car shoppers should thoroughly test the steering feel, verify proper operation of the Uconnect infotainment system, and check transmission shift quality during a test drive. Ensuring that all routine maintenance and recall services have been performed will help secure a more dependable ownership experience.
