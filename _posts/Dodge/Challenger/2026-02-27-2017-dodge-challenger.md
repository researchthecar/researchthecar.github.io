# 2017 Dodge Challenger Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2017 Dodge Challenger remains a favorite for muscle car enthusiasts, offering iconic styling, spacious comfort, and a wide array of powerful engine options. While its straight-line performance and cruiser credentials are strong, used-car buyers should be mindful of certain recurring complaints, particularly concerning infotainment system delamination and driveshaft or suspension wear. Thoroughly inspecting these specific areas before purchasing can help ensure a trouble-free ownership experience.

---

## Engine

### 3.6L Pentastar V6

The 3.6-liter Pentastar V6 engine produces approximately 305 horsepower and 268 lb-ft of torque, paired with an 8-speed automatic transmission. It serves as the efficient entry-level option, offering solid everyday reliability for daily drivers.

### 5.7L HEMI V8

The 5.7-liter HEMI V8 pushes out around 372 to 375 horsepower and 400 lb-ft of torque, delivering classic muscle car performance. It is generally robust, though buyers should watch out for cooling system issues.

**Coolant Leaks and Head Gasket Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may experience unexplained coolant loss, overheating, or a blown head gasket.
- **Solution:** Inspect the cooling system and engine block for leaks; replace damaged head gaskets or faulty components as necessary.

### 6.4L HEMI V8

The 6.4-liter SRT HEMI V8 generates 485 horsepower and 475 lb-ft of torque, providing high-performance thrills. It requires attentive maintenance regarding cooling and electrical support systems.

**Radiator Fan Seizure**
- **Symptoms:** The radiator fan motor can seize up, causing fan blades to break and potentially puncture surrounding components.
- **Solution:** Replace the seized radiator fan assembly and inspect the radiator and nearby lines for physical damage.

### 6.2L Supercharged HEMI V8

The supercharged Hellcat 6.2-liter V8 produces an immense 707 horsepower. It is built for extreme performance, requiring rigorous pre-purchase inspections to check for signs of abuse or excessive wear.

## Transmission

### TorqueFlite 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

The ZF-sourced 8-speed automatic transmission delivers smooth and rapid gear changes across V6 and V8 models. It is widely praised for durability, though sporadic cold-start shifting delays have been reported.

**Delayed Initial Shifts**
- **Symptoms:** When the vehicle is started after sitting for several hours, the first few gear shifts may hesitate or experience a noticeable delay.
- **Solution:** Check transmission fluid levels and update powertrain control modules if software bulletins apply.

### TREMEC 6-Speed Manual Transmission

The 6-speed manual transmission is paired exclusively with select V8 models for an engaging, old-school driving experience. It proves durable when driven responsibly, though clutch wear heavily depends on previous driver habits.

### Driveshaft and Carrier Bearing

The rear-wheel-drive layout relies on a central driveshaft and support bearing that can experience premature wear and vibration over time.

**Driveshaft Vibration and Carrier Bearing Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Unusual vibrations or rumbling noises emanate from underneath the vehicle, often occurring at moderate mileage.
- **Solution:** Replace the worn driveshaft assembly or faulty carrier bearing.
- **Cost:** $1,400

## Suspension

### Front Suspension and Ball Joints

The Challenger's heavy front end places stress on suspension joints and control arms, making pre-purchase inspections vital for identifying premature wear.

**Upper Ball Joint and Control Arm Failure**
- **Symptoms:** A sudden detachment of the wheel or upper ball joint can occur while driving at low speeds, accompanied by clunking noises.
- **Solution:** Inspect front suspension components for excessive play and replace worn control arms or ball joints promptly.

## Electrical

### Uconnect Infotainment Touchscreen

Electrical complaints on this model heavily center around the Uconnect infotainment system, where screen bubbling and touch controller failures are relatively common.

**Screen Delamination and Ghost Touches**
- **Symptoms:** The 8.4-inch touchscreen bubbles or delaminates, causing the system to register random inputs, change radio stations, or affect connected features independently.
- **Solution:** Replace the failing infotainment display unit with an updated or aftermarket screen assembly.

## Safety

### Airbag System

Owner complaints highlight sporadic dashboard warning lights and instances where airbags failed to deploy as expected during impacts.

**Airbag Deployment Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Airbags may fail to deploy during certain side or head-on collision impacts, or the airbag warning light remains illuminated.
- **Solution:** Diagnose the airbag control module and wiring harness, and replace faulty sensors or modules.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V332000: Electrical System:Wiring](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Dodge&model=Challenger&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** These vehicles are being recalled to address a defect that could prevent the cruise control system from disengaging. If, when using cruise control, there is a short circuit within the vehicle's wiring, the driver may not be able to shut off the cruise control either by depressing the brake pedal or manually turning the system off once it has been engaged.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the software, and perform a software flash on the engine or powertrain control module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V496000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Engine](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Dodge&model=Challenger&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** If an EOC line fails, oil may spray onto the windshield or contact a hot surface, impairing driver visibility and increasing the risk of a crash or fire.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will replace the EOC lines, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V431000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission:Control Module (Tcm/Pcm/Tecm)](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Dodge&model=Challenger&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** The transmission may not remain in the PARK position if that gear is selected, the engine is still running, and the driver exits the vehicle exited. If the vehicle is exited without the transmission remaining in the PARK position and without the parking brake set, the vehicle may roll, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will update the transmission control module software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2017 Dodge Challenger is a robust and immensely fun cruiser, but used-car buyers should perform targeted checks before finalizing a purchase. Pay close attention to the condition of the 8.4-inch infotainment screen for signs of bubbling or ghost touches, inspect the front suspension and driveshaft for wear or vibration, and verify that all cooling systems are functioning correctly. Selecting a well-maintained model with a clear service history minimizes the risk of unexpected repair bills.
