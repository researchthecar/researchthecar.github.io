# 2017 Dodge Challenger

### Overview
The 2017 Dodge Challenger, a popular American muscle car, generally offers a robust and engaging driving experience. Across its various trims and powertrain options, it has demonstrated a commendable level of reliability for its class. While performance-oriented vehicles can sometimes present unique challenges, the Challenger has largely avoided widespread, critical failures. Owners can expect a durable vehicle, particularly in its core mechanical components. However, as with any vehicle, specific issues can arise, often related to individual component performance or specific driving conditions. This analysis focuses on documented, non-routine issues that may require attention beyond standard maintenance.

## Electrical

### Infotainment System
**Uconnect System Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Screen freezing, unresponsiveness, Bluetooth connectivity issues, navigation errors.
- **Solution:** Software updates for the Uconnect system are often available. A hard reset may also resolve temporary glitches.
- **Cost:** $0 - $200 (for potential dealer diagnostic/update)


### Battery Drain
**Intermittent Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Battery dies unexpectedly, difficulty starting the vehicle.
- **Solution:** Diagnose parasitic draw from electrical components. May require replacing faulty modules or repairing wiring.
- **Cost:** $200 - $600

### Summary Verdict
The 2017 Dodge Challenger generally presents a strong reliability record, especially concerning its core powertrain options like the 3.6L Pentastar V6 and the various HEMI V8 engines, along with its automatic and manual transmissions. The chassis and suspension components are also typically robust. The most frequently cited non-routine issues tend to be related to the electrical system, specifically minor glitches within the Uconnect infotainment system and occasional instances of battery drain, which are often resolvable with software updates or parasitic draw diagnosis. The high-performance Hellcat model has a specific, though not uncommon for its type, concern with supercharger belt slippage. Overall, the Challenger is a reliable choice for a performance-oriented coupe, with most potential issues being manageable and not indicative of systemic design flaws.
