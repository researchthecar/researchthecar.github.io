Here's a breakdown of common issues and complaints reported by owners of the 2023 Dodge Challenger, organized by category and powertrain configuration. The 2023 Dodge Challenger is a popular muscle car known for its powerful engine options and retro styling. Available engines include a 3.6L Pentastar V6, a 5.7L Hemi V8, a 6.4L Hemi V8, and a supercharged 6.2L Hemi V8 (Hellcat). The base 3.6L V6 produces around 303 horsepower and 268 lb-ft of torque, making it a respectable performer. Enthusiasts often appreciate the Challenger's comfortable ride and spacious interior compared to some competitors, but some dislike its large size and weight, which can impact handling. Owners may report that the car consumes fuel faster than they anticipated or would like.

**Transmission**

**TorqueFlite 8-Speed Automatic Transmission (All Engines)**
* **Harsh Shifting/Hesitation**
    * Description/Symptoms: Some owners report experiencing harsh or jerky shifting, particularly during low-speed acceleration or deceleration. Hesitation when accelerating from a stop is also mentioned.
    * Solution: Diagnosis is recommended, this could include a software update, valve body replacement, or in some cases, a full transmission replacement.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $5,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **No Issues Found**

**Electrical**

* **Infotainment System Freezing/Crashing**
    * Description/Symptoms: The Uconnect infotainment system may occasionally freeze, become unresponsive, or crash, requiring a system reboot. This can affect navigation, audio, and climate control functions.
    * Solution: Software updates from the dealer may resolve the issue. In more severe cases, the infotainment unit may need replacement.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 (Software Update) - $1,200+ (Infotainment Unit Replacement)
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Backup Camera Malfunction**
    * Description/Symptoms: The backup camera may display a black screen or distorted image.
    * Solution: Check the camera connections. If the connections are good, the camera may need to be replaced.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

**Interior**

* **Seat Heater Malfunction**
    * Description/Symptoms: Seat heaters failing to activate, overheating, or intermittently shutting off.
    * Solution: Replacing the seat heater element or control module.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2023 Dodge Challenger, while praised for its powerful engine options and styling, can have a few potential issues. Electrical glitches, especially with the infotainment system and backup camera, are fairly common and can range from minor annoyances fixable with software updates to costly component replacements. The automatic transmission can present shifting problems, potentially leading to significant repair expenses. The seat heaters in the interior may also have issues requiring them to be replaced. While not every Challenger will experience these problems, being aware of them can help potential buyers make a more informed decision and budget accordingly.
