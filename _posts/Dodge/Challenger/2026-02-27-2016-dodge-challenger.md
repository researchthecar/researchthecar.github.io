# 2016 Dodge Challenger Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2016 Dodge Challenger blends old-school muscle car styling with modern daily-driving amenities. Buyers looking at the used market should weigh its powerful engine lineups and retro appeal against potential complaints regarding electrical issues, steering system glitches, and minor powertrain oil leaks. Prospective owners should perform a thorough pre-purchase inspection to catch any wear-and-tear items early.

---

## Engine

### 3.6L V6

This base Pentastar V6 engine produces approximately 305 horsepower and 268 lb-ft of torque, offering decent acceleration for daily driving while returning better fuel economy than the V8 options.

### 5.7L HEMI V8

The 5.7L HEMI V8 delivers roughly 372 to 375 horsepower, providing classic muscle car performance and exhaust tone, though owners should watch for common oil filter housing leaks and valvetrain ticking.

**Oil Filter Housing Leak**
- **Symptoms:** Oil pools under the vehicle or burning smells originating from the top of the engine block due to housing seal degradation.
- **Solution:** Replace the plastic oil filter housing assembly with an updated or upgraded aftermarket component.

### 6.4L HEMI V8

The larger 6.4L SRT HEMI V8 pumps out an impressive 485 horsepower and 475 lb-ft of torque, turning the Challenger into a high-performance straight-line cruiser.

### 6.2L Supercharged HEMI V8

Reserved for the Hellcat trim, this supercharged powerhouse churns out a massive 707 horsepower for unmatched track and drag performance.

## Transmission

### TorqueFlite 8-Speed Automatic

The ZF-derived 8-speed automatic transmission offers smooth, rapid gear changes across both V6 and V8 models, heavily contributing to the car's overall drivability.

**Shifter or Park Defect**
- **Symptoms:** Difficulty engaging gears properly or the vehicle failing to register when it is shifted into Park, potentially risking rollaway.
- **Solution:** Inspect the shifter linkage, update software modules, or replace failing shift assembly components.

### Tremec 6-Speed Manual

Available exclusively on select V8 models, the 6-speed manual transmission delivers an engaging, traditional enthusiast driving experience.

## Steering

### Electronic Power Steering

The electronic power steering setup provides adjustable steering feel, though a subset of owners have reported intermittent system lockups requiring a vehicle restart.

**Steering Wheel Lockup**
- **Symptoms:** The steering wheel suddenly becomes rigid or difficult to turn while driving, accompanied by an EPS or power steering warning light.
- **Solution:** Inspect electronic power steering components, check for software update requirements, or replace the faulty steering rack/motor.

## Exterior

### Lighting System

Electrical system complaints occasionally center around failing exterior lighting assemblies and fuse box wear.

**Headlight or Tail Light Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Headlights or center LED tail light strips flickering, burning out prematurely, or failing completely due to wiring or fuse issues.
- **Solution:** Inspect fuses, wiring harnesses, and replace burned-out bulb assemblies or LED units.

## Safety

### Air Bag and Restraints

Safety system grievances primarily involve seat belt sensor failures and owner reports regarding airbag functionality in specific collisions.

**Seat Belt and Airbag Deployment Concerns**
- **Symptoms:** Airbag warning lights illuminating, seat belt buckle sensors failing to register engaged belts, or airbags failing to deploy during front-end impacts.
- **Solution:** Inspect seat belt receivers, wiring connections, and replace faulty airbag sensor modules.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18E053000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission:Control Module (Tcm/Pcm/Tecm)](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Dodge&model=Challenger&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** The PCMs are being recalled to address a defect that could prevent the cruise control system from disengaging. If, when using cruise control, there is a short circuit within the vehicle's wiring, the driver may not be able to shut off the cruise control either by depressing the brake pedal or manually turning the system off once it has been engaged.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will either perform a software update or replace the PCM, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V332000: Electrical System:Wiring](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Dodge&model=Challenger&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** These vehicles are being recalled to address a defect that could prevent the cruise control system from disengaging. If, when using cruise control, there is a short circuit within the vehicle's wiring, the driver may not be able to shut off the cruise control either by depressing the brake pedal or manually turning the system off once it has been engaged.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the software, and perform a software flash on the engine or powertrain control module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2016 Dodge Challenger is a charismatic cruiser available with a wide array of potent engines, from the practical V6 to the ferocious Hellcat V8. While it offers timeless styling and a comfortable cabin, used car buyers should remain vigilant regarding potential electronic steering glitches, oil filter housing leaks on V8 models, and occasional exterior lighting failures. A comprehensive mechanical inspection before purchase will help ensure you find a reliable example.
