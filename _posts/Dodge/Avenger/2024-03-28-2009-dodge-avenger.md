**Introduction:** The 2009 Dodge Avenger is a mid-size sedan powered by a standard 2.4L 4-cylinder engine with 173 horsepower and 166 lb-ft of torque. A 3.5L V6 with 235 horsepower and 232 lb-ft of torque is available as an upgrade. Both engines are mated to a 4-speed automatic transmission. The Avenger is praised for its spacious interior, comfortable ride, and stylish exterior. However, it is criticized for its uninspired performance and poor fuel economy.

**Engine**

* **2.4L 4-Cylinder**

    * **Excessive Oil Consumption:** Consumes an excessive amount of oil, leading to low oil levels and potential engine damage.
        * Solution: Replace piston rings and valve guides.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $1,500+
        * Recalls: None Found
    * **Timing Chain Failure:** The timing chain can fail prematurely, causing catastrophic engine damage.
        * Solution: Replace timing chain and related components.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
        * Recalls: None Found
* **3.5L V6**

    * **Sludging:** Oil sludge can accumulate in the engine, leading to restricted oil flow and potential engine damage.
        * Solution: Regular oil changes and engine flush.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300
        * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

* **4-Speed Automatic**

    * **Shift Hesitation:** Transmission hesitates or shifts harshly when changing gears.
        * Solution: Replace transmission fluid and filter.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600
        * Recalls: None Found
    * **Slipping Gears:** Transmission slips gears while driving, resulting in loss of power.
        * Solution: Overhaul or rebuild transmission.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **No Issues Found**

**Electrical**

* **Alternator Failure:** Alternator may fail prematurely, leading to electrical issues and battery drain.
        * Solution: Replace alternator.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $700
        * Recalls: None Found
    * **Battery Discharge:** Battery can discharge prematurely due to parasitic drain.
        * Solution: Diagnose and repair electrical system.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
        * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Faulty Door Handles:** Exterior door handles may break or become difficult to operate.
        * Solution: Replace door handles.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $250
        * Recalls: None Found
    * **Window Regulator Failure:** Window regulators can fail, causing windows to become inoperable.
        * Solution: Replace window regulator.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600
        * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **HVAC System Malfunction:** HVAC system may not function properly, resulting in lack of heating or cooling.
        * Solution: Diagnose and repair HVAC system.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800
        * Recalls: None Found
    * **Radio Display Failure:** Radio display may become unresponsive or dim.
        * Solution: Replace radio display.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $600
        * Recalls: None Found

**Summary:** The 2009 Dodge Avenger has a mix of major and minor issues. Engine and transmission components, such as piston rings, valve guides, and timing chains, can fail prematurely. Electrical issues with the alternator and battery can also be a concern. While some suspension issues are not reported, exterior and interior components, like door handles, window regulators, and HVAC systems, can also experience problems. The severity and cost of the issues can vary widely, so it's crucial to research potential problems thoroughly before purchasing this vehicle.
