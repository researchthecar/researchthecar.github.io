**Introduction:**

The 1998 Dodge Avenger is a midsize sedan powered by a 2.5L V6 engine. It offers both an automatic and manual transmission option. The Avenger has been praised for its comfortable ride, spacious interior, and strong V6 engine. However, it also has some drawbacks, such as its uninspiring handling and lack of standard features.

**Engine**

2.5L V6

* **Intake Manifold Gasket Failure:**
Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaks, overheating engine, rough idle
Solution: Replace intake manifold gasket
Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
Recalls: None Found

* **Valve Cover Gasket Leaks:**
Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks from under valve cover
Solution: Replace valve cover gasket
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $350+
Recalls: None Found

* **Timing Belt Failure:**
Description/Symptoms: Catastrophic engine failure without warning
Solution: Preventative maintenance: timely replacement of timing belt per manufacturer guidelines
Estimated Cost to Fix: $600 - $1000+
Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

Automatic

* **Transmission Solenoid Failure:**
Description/Symptoms: Harsh shifting, slipping gears, no engagement
Solution: Replace transmission solenoid
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
Recalls: None Found

* **Torque Converter Failure:**
Description/Symptoms: Shuddering, slipping, no engagement
Solution: Replace torque converter
Estimated Cost to Fix: $1000 - $1500+
Recalls: None Found

Manual

* **Clutch Failure:**
Description/Symptoms: Slipping, difficulty shifting gears
Solution: Replace clutch
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800+
Recalls: None Found

* **Transmission Mount Failure:**
Description/Symptoms: Vibration, noise, difficulty shifting gears
Solution: Replace transmission mount
Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300+
Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Strut Mount Bearing Failure:**
Description/Symptoms: Clunking, knocking noise over bumps
Solution: Replace strut mount bearing
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
Recalls: None Found

* **Lower Control Arm Bushing Failure:**
Description/Symptoms: Vibration, excessive tire wear
Solution: Replace lower control arm bushing
Estimated Cost to Fix: $250 - $450+
Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Alternator Failure:**
Description/Symptoms: Battery not charging, electrical system failure
Solution: Replace alternator
Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $600+
Recalls: None Found

* **Starter Failure:**
Description/Symptoms: Engine not cranking
Solution: Replace starter
Estimated Cost to Fix: $250 - $400+
Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Headlight Switch Failure:**
Description/Symptoms: Headlights not turning on/off, intermittent failure
Solution: Replace headlight switch
Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $250+
Recalls: None Found

* **Tail Light Assembly Failure:**
Description/Symptoms: Tail lights not working, moisture buildup
Solution: Replace tail light assembly
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $350+
Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Dashboard Rattle:**
Description/Symptoms: Rattle or vibration coming from dashboard
Solution: Inspect and tighten loose components
Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the issue
Recalls: None Found

* **Seat Adjustment Motor Failure:**
Description/Symptoms: Difficulty adjusting seat position
Solution: Replace seat adjustment motor
Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+
Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

The 1998 Dodge Avenger is a reliable vehicle with some common issues to be aware of. The most severe issues are timing belt failure, torque converter failure, and strut mount bearing failure. These issues can be costly to fix and may require professional assistance. However, most of the other issues are minor and can be fixed at a reasonable cost.
