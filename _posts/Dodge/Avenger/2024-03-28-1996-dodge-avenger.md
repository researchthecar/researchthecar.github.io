**Introduction**: The 1996 Dodge Avenger was a midsize sedan that was offered with two engine options: a 2.0L inline-4 and a 2.5L V6. The 2.0L engine produced 132 horsepower and 160 lb-ft of torque, while the 2.5L engine produced 163 horsepower and 168 lb-ft of torque. Both engines were paired with a 4-speed automatic transmission. The Avenger was well-received for its spacious interior and comfortable ride, but it was criticized for its lack of power and poor fuel economy.

**Engine**
- **2.0L Inline-4**
  - **Valve Train Noise**: A common issue with the 2.0L engine is a rattling noise from the valve train. This noise is caused by worn valve lifters.
    - Solution: Replacing the valve lifters will resolve the noise.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $600+
    - Recalls: None Found
- **2.5L V6**
  - **Intake Manifold Gasket Failure**: The intake manifold gasket on the 2.5L engine can fail, causing coolant and oil leaks.
    - Solution: Replacing the intake manifold gasket will resolve the leak.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+
    - Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**
- **4-Speed Automatic Transmission**
  - **Shifting Issues**: Some owners have reported problems with the automatic transmission shifting gears. This issue can be caused by a faulty transmission control module or a worn transmission solenoid.
