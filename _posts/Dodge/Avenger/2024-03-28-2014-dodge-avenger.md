**Introduction:**

The 2014 Dodge Avenger is a midsize sedan offered with three engine options: a 2.4L I-4 (173 horsepower, 166 lb-ft torque), a 3.6L V6 (283 horsepower, 260 lb-ft torque), and a 3.6L V6 with E85 flex fuel capability (283 horsepower, 260 lb-ft torque). The 2.4L I-4 engine is paired with a 4-speed automatic transmission, while the 3.6L V6 engine is paired with a 6-speed automatic transmission.

The Avenger has been praised for its spacious interior, comfortable seats, and well-equipped trim levels. However, it has also been criticized for its unexciting driving dynamics and dated infotainment system.

**Engine**

**2.4L I-4**

* **Oil Leak from Timing Cover Gasket**
    * Description/Symptoms: A leak develops around the timing cover gasket, leading to oil drips and possible engine damage.
    * Solution: Replace the timing cover gasket.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $250 - $400
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Crankshaft Position Sensor Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The crankshaft position sensor fails, causing the engine to stall or run rough.
    * Solution: Replace the crankshaft position sensor.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $250
    * Recalls: None Found

**3.6L V6**

* **Timing Chain Tensioner Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The timing chain tensioner fails, resulting in a rattling noise from the engine and potential engine damage.
    * Solution: Replace the timing chain and tensioner.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $1,500
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Intake Manifold Gasket Leak**
    * Description/Symptoms: The intake manifold gasket fails, causing a vacuum leak and poor engine performance.
    * Solution: Replace the intake manifold gasket.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**4-Speed Automatic**

* **Shift Solenoid Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The shift solenoid fails, causing the transmission to slip or shift harshly.
    * Solution: Replace the shift solenoid pack.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Transmission Fluid Leak**
    * Description/Symptoms: A leak develops in the transmission, causing fluid loss and potential transmission failure.
    * Solution: Repair or replace the leaking component.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600
    * Recalls: None Found

**6-Speed Automatic**

* **Torque Converter Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The torque converter fails, causing the transmission to slip or fail completely.
    * Solution: Replace the torque converter.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $1,500
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Valve Body Malfunction**
    * Description/Symptoms: The valve body malfunctions, leading to erratic shifting patterns or transmission failure.
    * Solution: Replace or repair the valve body.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Struts and Shocks Wear Out Prematurely**
    * Description/Symptoms: The struts and shocks wear out quickly, resulting in poor handling and reduced ride comfort.
    * Solution: Replace the struts and shocks.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $600 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Control Arm Bushings Fail**
    * Description/Symptoms: The control arm bushings fail, causing clunking noises and reduced steering precision.
    * Solution: Replace the control arm bushings.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drains Quickly**
    * Description/Symptoms: The battery drains quickly, especially if the vehicle is left parked for extended periods.
    * Solution: Diagnose and fix the electrical fault causing the drain.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the fault
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Rear Wiper Motor Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The rear wiper motor fails, preventing the rear wiper from working.
    * Solution: Replace the rear wiper motor.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $250
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Hood Latch Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The hood latch fails, causing the hood to open unexpectedly.
    * Solution: Replace the hood latch.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200
    * Recalls: NHTSA Recall Number 14V342000

* **Headlight Bulbs Burn Out Prematurely**
    * Description/Symptoms: The headlight bulbs burn out frequently, reducing visibility at night.
    * Solution: Replace the headlight bulbs with higher-quality bulbs.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $150
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Climate Control System Malfunctions**
    * Description/Symptoms: The climate control system malfunctions, causing the air conditioner or heater to not work properly.
    * Solution: Diagnose and repair the faulty component within the climate control system.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the fault
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Door Lock Actuators Fail**
    * Description/Symptoms: The door lock actuators fail, causing the doors to lock or unlock unexpectedly.
    * Solution: Replace the faulty door lock actuators.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

The 2014 Dodge Avenger has several potential issues, ranging from minor annoyances like premature headlight bulb burnout to major mechanical failures like timing chain tensioner failure. While the severity of the issues varies, some repairs can be quite costly. Overall, the reliability of the Avenger is considered average, with some owners experiencing significant problems while others have a relatively trouble-free experience.
