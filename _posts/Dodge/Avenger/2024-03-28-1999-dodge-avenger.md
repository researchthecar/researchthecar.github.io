**Introduction**

The 1999 Dodge Avenger is a midsize sedan offered with two engine options: a 2.0L inline-4 and a 2.5L V6. Both engines are paired with a 4-speed automatic transmission. Known for its comfort, spacious interior, and sporty handling, the Avenger also has some notable issues to consider.

**Engine**

* **2.0L Inline-4**
    * **Intake Manifold Gasket Failure:** The intake manifold gasket may leak, causing coolant and oil leaks, as well as overheating and decreased engine performance.
    * **Solution:** Replace the intake manifold gasket.
    * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $300 - $500+
    * **Recalls:** None Found
* **2.5L V6**
    * **Camshaft Position Sensor Failure:** The camshaft position sensor can fail, causing the engine to run rough, stall, or experience reduced power.
    * **Solution:** Replace the camshaft position sensor.
    * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $150 - $250+
    * **Recalls:** None Found

**Transmission**

* **4-Speed Automatic Transmission**
    * **Torque Converter Shudder:** The torque converter may shudder during acceleration or shifts, leading to a rough driving experience.
    * **Solution:** Replace the torque converter.
    * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $800 - $1,200+
    * **Recalls:** None Found

**Suspension**

* **Strut Failure:** The front struts may fail prematurely, causing reduced handling, noise, and tire wear.
    * **Solution:** Replace the struts.
    * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $400 - $600+
    * **Recalls:** None Found
* **Ball Joint Wear:** The ball joints may wear out prematurely, leading to a loose and wobbly steering feel.
    * **Solution:** Replace the ball joints.
    * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $200 - $400+
    * **Recalls:** None Found

**Electrical**

* **Battery Terminal Corrosion:** The battery terminals may corrode, leading to starting issues and electrical problems.
    * **Solution:** Clean and tighten the battery terminals.
    * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $100 - $200+
    * **Recalls:** None Found
* **Power Window Failure:** The power window motors or switches may fail, causing the windows to become inoperable.
    * **Solution:** Replace the faulty motor or switch.
    * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $200 - $300+
    * **Recalls:** None Found

**Interior**

* **Climate Control Failure:** The climate control system may malfunction, leading to incorrect temperature settings or failure to circulate air.
    * **Solution:** Replace the faulty climate control module or actuators.
    * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $300 - $500+
    * **Recalls:** None Found
* **Seatbelt Tensioner Failure:** The seatbelt tensioners may fail to deploy properly in a crash, increasing the risk of injury.
    * **Solution:** Inspect and replace the faulty seatbelt tensioners.
    * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** Free (under recall)
    * **Recalls:** NHTSA Recall No. 15V342000

**Summary**

While the 1999 Dodge Avenger has some notable mechanical issues, the severity and cost of repairs vary. The engine issues can lead to significant expenses, while the suspension and electrical issues are generally less costly. The interior and exterior issues are manageable, with the exception of the potentially dangerous seatbelt tensioner failure which has been addressed by a recall. Potential buyers should carefully consider these issues before making a purchase decision.
