**Introduction:**

The 2001 Dodge Ramcharger is a full-size pickup truck that was only available with two engine and transmission options. The 5.2L V8 produced 230 horsepower and 325 lb-ft of torque, while the 5.9L V8 produced 245 horsepower and 390 lb-ft of torque. Both engines were paired with a 4-speed automatic transmission.

People who like the Ramcharger appreciate its powerful engines, spacious interior, and comfortable ride. However, some people have complained about its poor fuel economy and lack of modern features.

**Engine**

* **5.2L V8**
    * **Intake Manifold Gasket Failure:** Leaking coolant, loss of power, and rough idle. Solution: Replace the intake manifold gasket. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
    * **Timing Chain Tensioner Failure:** Noisy engine, loss of power, and eventually engine failure. Solution: Replace the timing chain and tensioner. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
* **5.9L V8**
    * **No Issues Found**

**Transmission**

* **4-Speed Automatic**
    * **Solenoid Failure:** Transmission slipping or not shifting properly. Solution: Replace the solenoids. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
    * **Torque Converter Failure:** Loss of power, vibration, and overheating transmission. Solution: Replace the torque converter. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+

**Suspension**

* **Front Control Arm Bushings Wear:** Squeaking or clunking noises when turning or braking. Solution: Replace the worn bushings. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
* **Rear Leaf Spring Breakage:** Broken leaf springs can cause the rear of the vehicle to sag or bounce. Solution: Replace the broken leaf springs. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+

**Electrical**

* **Alternator Failure:** Charging system failure, dim headlights, and difficulty starting the engine. Solution: Replace the alternator. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
* **Battery Drain:** Battery goes dead frequently due to parasitic draw. Solution: Diagnose and fix the parasitic draw. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+

**Exterior**

* **Rust:** Rust can occur on the body panels, frame, and suspension components. Solution: Treat and prevent rust by applying rust-resistant products. Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the severity of the rust

**Interior**

* **Air Conditioning Blower Motor Failure:** No air flow from the vents. Solution: Replace the blower motor. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
* **Power Window Switch Failure:** Windows do not operate properly. Solution: Replace the faulty switch. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+

**Summary:**

The 2001 Dodge Ramcharger has some reliability concerns to be aware of, particularly with the engine and transmission. These issues can be expensive to fix, so it is important to factor in the potential cost of repairs when considering purchasing this vehicle.
