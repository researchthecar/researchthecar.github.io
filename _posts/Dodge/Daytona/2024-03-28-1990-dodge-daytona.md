**1990 Dodge Daytona**

The 1990 Dodge Daytona was a sporty compact car offered with a variety of engine and transmission options. The base engine was a 2.2L inline-four producing 110 horsepower and 140 lb-ft of torque. A more powerful 2.5L V6 was also available, generating 150 horsepower and 170 lb-ft of torque. Transmission choices included a 5-speed manual and a 3-speed automatic.

The Daytona was praised for its stylish design, responsive handling, and comfortable interior. However, it was also criticized for its lack of power and relatively high fuel consumption.

**Engine**

**2.2L Inline-Four**

* **Head Gasket Failure:** Blown head gasket leading to coolant and oil leaks, white smoke from exhaust. **$500 - $1,000+**
    * Solution: Replace head gasket, resurface cylinder head
* **Intake Manifold Gasket Leak:** Leaking gasket between intake manifold and cylinder head, causing vacuum leaks and rough idle. **$200 - $400**
    * Solution: Replace intake manifold gasket

**2.5L V6**

* **Timing Belt Failure:** Timed engine, if timing belt snaps or jumps teeth, can cause catastrophic engine damage. **$500 - $800**
    * Solution: Replace timing belt according to recommended maintenance schedule
* **Water Pump Failure:** Leaking or seized water pump can lead to overheating and engine damage. **$300 - $500**
    * Solution: Replace water pump

**Transmission**

**5-Speed Manual**

* **Gear Shift Linkage Issues:** Worn or broken linkage can make shifting difficult or impossible. **$100 - $300**
    * Solution: Replace or repair gear shift linkage

**3-Speed Automatic**

* **Transmission Slippage:** Transmission slipping out of gear, especially during acceleration. **$500 - $1,500+**
    * Solution: Rebuild or replace transmission
* **Torque Converter Failure:** Worn or damaged torque converter can cause shuddering or loss of power. **$500 - $1,000+**
    * Solution: Replace torque converter

**Suspension**

* **Control Arm Bushings Worn:** Worn bushings can lead to excessive noise, vibration, and reduced handling. **$200 - $400+**
    * Solution: Replace control arm bushings
* **Strut Assembly Failure:** Leaking or worn strut assemblies can compromise ride quality and stability. **$300 - $600+**
    * Solution: Replace strut assemblies

**Electrical**

* **Alternator Failure:** Faulty alternator can lead to charging system issues, battery drain, and electrical problems. **$300 - $600**
    * Solution: Replace alternator
* **Battery Drain:** Parasitic draw from electrical components can discharge the battery. **$100 - $300**
    * Solution: Isolate and repair or replace faulty electrical components

**Exterior**

* **Rear Spoiler Vibration:** Loose or damaged rear spoiler can vibrate at high speeds. **$100 - $200**
    * Solution: Secure or replace rear spoiler
* **Door Lock Failure:** Broken or jammed door locks can prevent access to the vehicle. **$100 - $300**
    * Solution: Replace or repair door locks

**Interior**

* **Dashboard Cracks:** Dashboard material prone to cracking over time, especially in hot climates. **$200 - $500+**
    * Solution: Replace dashboard
* **HVAC System Malfunctions:** Blower motor failure, refrigerant leaks, or electrical issues can affect climate control performance. **$200 - $800+**
    * Solution: Repair or replace faulty HVAC components

**Summary**

The 1990 Dodge Daytona has a mix of common and severe issues, particularly in the engine, transmission, and electrical systems. While some repairs are relatively inexpensive, others can be costly. Owners of this vehicle should be prepared for potential maintenance expenses, especially if it has high mileage or has not been properly maintained.
