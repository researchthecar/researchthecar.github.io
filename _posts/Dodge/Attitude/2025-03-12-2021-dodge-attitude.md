Here's a breakdown of common issues and complaints for the 2021 Dodge Attitude, formatted for your website.

The 2021 Dodge Attitude, primarily sold in Mexico and other Latin American markets, is a subcompact sedan known for its fuel efficiency and affordability. The 2021 model typically comes equipped with a 1.2-liter inline-3 engine producing around 76 horsepower and 74 lb-ft of torque, paired with either a 5-speed manual or a continuously variable transmission (CVT). Some find the Attitude's affordability and fuel economy appealing, while others dislike its basic interior and limited performance capabilities.

**Engine**

**1.2L Inline-3 Engine**

*   **Excessive Engine Vibration**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners report experiencing more vibration than expected, especially at idle or low speeds. This can sometimes be attributed to engine mount issues.
    *   Solution: Inspect engine mounts for wear or damage. Replace worn or damaged mounts.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $400
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**CVT (Continuously Variable Transmission)**

*   **Hesitation/Jerky Acceleration**
    *   Description/Symptoms:  Some owners experience hesitation or jerky acceleration, particularly when trying to accelerate quickly from a stop or at low speeds. This can be a common characteristic of CVTs but may also indicate an issue.
    *   Solution: Start by performing a CVT fluid flush. If the issues persist, the CVT transmission might need to be replaced.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: CVT Fluid Flush: $150 - $300. CVT Replacement: $2000 - $4000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Freezing/Lagging**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The infotainment system occasionally freezes, becomes unresponsive, or experiences significant lag.
    *   Solution: Try resetting the system. If the problem persists, update the software (if available). In some cases, the unit may need to be replaced.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: Resetting - Free. Replacement: $300 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Battery Drain**
    *   Description/Symptoms:  Premature battery drain, especially if the vehicle sits for extended periods.
    *   Solution:  Have the battery and charging system tested. Check for parasitic draws. Replace the battery if it is faulty or address any parasitic drains.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: Battery Test - Free at most auto parts stores. Battery Replacement - $100 - $300. Parasitic Draw Diagnosis & Repair - $100 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Rattling/Squeaking Noises**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Owners have reported rattling and squeaking noises coming from various areas of the interior, such as the dashboard or door panels, particularly on rough roads.
    *   Solution:  Identify the source of the noise and tighten any loose trim pieces.  Add felt or foam padding to eliminate contact points causing the noise.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: DIY - $5 - $50 (for materials). Professional Diagnostic and Repair - $50 - $300+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

The 2021 Dodge Attitude, while offering affordability and fuel efficiency, appears to have some recurring issues. Common complaints include engine vibration, hesitation with the CVT transmission, and electrical issues with the infotainment system. Interior rattles are also a common nuisance. The severity of these issues varies, but potential repair costs could range from relatively inexpensive fixes like replacing engine mounts or tightening trim pieces to more substantial expenses such as CVT replacement. Potential buyers should be aware of these potential problems and carefully inspect the vehicle and its service history before purchasing.
