# 2012 Dodge Attitude

### Overview
The 2012 Dodge Attitude, a subcompact sedan primarily sold in Mexico, shares its platform with the Hyundai Accent. This relationship generally translates to a decent level of reliability, leveraging established Korean automotive engineering. While not a segment leader in all aspects, the Attitude offers a generally dependable ownership experience for its class. Owners can expect reasonable performance and fuel economy, with most reported issues being minor or related to typical usage rather than inherent design flaws. Maintenance is generally straightforward and parts availability is good due to its shared components with other popular vehicles.

**Battery Drain**

- **Solution:** Testing for parasitic draw and identifying faulty components (e.g., modules, aftermarket accessories).
- **Cost:** $100 - $300

### Summary Verdict
The 2012 Dodge Attitude presents itself as a generally reliable subcompact vehicle, benefiting from its shared platform with the Hyundai Accent. Major mechanical failures are uncommon, with most reported issues being minor electrical glitches such as power window problems or occasional battery drain. The engine and transmission options, both manual and automatic, are considered robust for the vehicle's class. Suspension and exterior components typically hold up well, with no widespread critical issues identified. Routine maintenance is key, but overall, the Attitude offers a dependable and cost-effective ownership experience for budget-conscious buyers seeking basic transportation.
