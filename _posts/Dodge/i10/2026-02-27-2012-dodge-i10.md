# 2012 Dodge i10

### Overview
The 2012 Dodge i10, a subcompact car, generally presents a mixed bag of reliability. While it offers an economical and practical solution for urban driving, certain areas have shown a propensity for issues that warrant attention. Owners have reported a few recurring problems, particularly concerning the engine and electrical systems, which can lead to unexpected repair costs. However, other aspects like the transmission and interior generally hold up well. This analysis focuses on documented technical failures, excluding routine maintenance and normal wear and tear, to provide a clear picture of potential reliability concerns for prospective buyers and current owners.

### 1.2L 4-cylinder Petrol
**Oil Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Visible oil spots under the vehicle, low oil level warnings, burning oil smell.
- **Solution:** Replacement of seals and gaskets.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600

**Engine Mount Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Increased vibration felt in the cabin, clunking noises during acceleration or braking.
- **Solution:** Replacement of worn engine mounts.
- **Cost:** $250 - $500

### Summary Verdict
The 2012 Dodge i10 is a budget-friendly city car that generally performs adequately. However, potential buyers should be aware of a few recurring issues. The 1.2L petrol engine can be prone to oil leaks and worn engine mounts, while the 4-speed automatic transmission may experience slipping. Electrical problems, such as alternator failure and power window issues, are also noted. The manual transmission, suspension, exterior, and interior components are typically reliable. Regular maintenance and prompt attention to early signs of these issues can help mitigate repair costs and ensure a more dependable ownership experience.
