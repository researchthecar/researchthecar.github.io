**Introduction**
The 1995 Dodge Ram 4000 is a full-size pickup truck that was available in a variety of configurations, including single-cab, extended-cab, and crew-cab models. It was powered by a choice of three engines: a 3.9-liter V6, a 5.2-liter V8, or a 5.9-liter V8. Transmission options included a 5-speed manual, a 4-speed automatic, and a 5-speed automatic.

Drivers of the Dodge Ram 4000 like its spacious cabin, powerful engines, and rugged construction. However, the truck has also been known for its reliability issues.

**Engine**

**3.9L V6**
* **Intake manifold gasket failure:** The intake manifold gasket can fail, causing a vacuum leak and engine performance problems. Symptoms: rough idle, loss of power, increased fuel consumption. Solution: Replace the intake manifold gasket. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
* **Timing chain failure:** The timing chain can fail, causing catastrophic engine damage. Symptoms: engine rattling, loss of power, no start. Solution: Replace the timing chain and related components. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
* **Head gasket failure:** The head gasket can fail, causing coolant and oil to leak. Symptoms: white smoke from the exhaust, coolant leaks, overheating. Solution: Replace the head gasket. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+

**5.2L V8**
* **Intake manifold gasket failure:** The intake manifold gasket can fail, causing a vacuum leak and engine performance problems. Symptoms: rough idle, loss of power, increased fuel consumption. Solution: Replace the intake manifold gasket. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
* **Throttle position sensor failure:** The throttle position sensor can fail, causing the engine to run poorly or stall. Symptoms: rough idle, surging, hesitation. Solution: Replace the throttle position sensor. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200
* **Fuel pump failure:** The fuel pump can fail, causing the engine to lose power or stall. Symptoms: loss of power, no start. Solution: Replace the fuel pump. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+

**Transmission**

**5-Speed Manual**
* **Clutch failure:** The clutch can wear out, causing difficulty shifting gears or slipping. Symptoms: difficulty shifting gears, slipping gears, shuddering. Solution: Replace the clutch. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
* **Transmission bearing failure:** The transmission bearings can fail, causing noise or grinding. Symptoms: whining noises, grinding noises, difficulty shifting gears. Solution: Replace the transmission bearings. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+

**4-Speed Automatic**
* **Transmission fluid leak:** The transmission can develop a leak, causing it to lose fluid and overheat. Symptoms: slipping gears, overheating, no forward or reverse. Solution: Replace the transmission seals or gaskets. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
* **Solenoid failure:** The transmission solenoids can fail, causing shifting problems. Symptoms: hard shifting, delayed shifting, no shifting. Solution: Replace the transmission solenoids. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
* **Torque converter failure:** The torque converter can fail, causing the transmission to slip or shudder. Symptoms: slipping gears, shuddering, overheating. Solution: Replace the torque converter. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
