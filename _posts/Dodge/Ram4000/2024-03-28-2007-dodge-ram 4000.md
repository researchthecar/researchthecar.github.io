**Introduction**

The 2007 Dodge Ram 4000 is a heavy-duty pickup truck that was offered with a variety of engine and transmission options. The most popular engine option was the 5.7L Hemi V8, which produced 345 horsepower and 375 lb-ft of torque. Other engine options included the 4.7L V8 and the 6.1L Hemi V8. The Ram 4000 was also offered with a choice of three transmission options: a six-speed manual, a four-speed automatic, and a five-speed automatic.

Owners of the 2007 Dodge Ram 4000 generally praise the truck's powerful engines, rugged construction, and spacious interior. However, there are some common issues that owners have reported with the truck.

**Engine**

**5.7L Hemi V8**

* **Valve Seat Failure:** The valve seats in the 5.7L Hemi V8 can fail prematurely, causing the engine to lose power and eventually fail. The fix involves replacing the valve seats and may require additional repairs to the cylinder heads. Estimated cost: $3,000 - $5,000+

* **Intake Manifold Gasket Failure:** The intake manifold gasket on the 5.7L Hemi V8 can fail, causing a vacuum leak and resulting in reduced power and fuel economy. The fix involves replacing the gasket and any associated seals. Estimated cost: $500 - $1,000

**4.7L V8**

* **Timing Chain Failure:** The timing chain on the 4.7L V8 can fail, causing the engine to lose power and eventually fail. The fix involves replacing the timing chain and tensioner. Estimated cost: $1,500 - $2,500

**6.1L Hemi V8**

* **No Issues Found**

**Transmission**

**Six-Speed Manual**

* **Clutch Failure:** The clutch on the six-speed manual transmission can fail, causing the transmission to slip or become difficult to shift. The fix involves replacing the clutch and pressure plate. Estimated cost: $500 - $1,000

**Four-Speed Automatic**

* **Torque Converter Failure:** The torque converter in the four-speed automatic transmission can fail, causing the transmission to slip and lose power. The fix involves replacing the torque converter and filter. Estimated cost: $1,000 - $1,500

**Five-Speed Automatic**

* **No Issues Found**

**Suspension**

* **Front Suspension Failure:** The front suspension on the Ram 4000 can fail, causing the truck to bounce or wobble when driving. The fix involves replacing the ball joints, tie rods, and other suspension components. Estimated cost: $500 - $1,500

**Electrical**

* **Battery Failure:** The battery on the Ram 4000 can fail prematurely, leaving the truck unable to start. The fix involves replacing the battery. Estimated cost: $150 - $250

* **Starter Failure:** The starter on the Ram 4000 can fail, preventing the truck from starting. The fix involves replacing the starter. Estimated cost: $250 - $500

* **Alternator Failure:** The alternator on the Ram 4000 can fail, preventing the truck from charging the battery. The fix involves replacing the alternator. Estimated cost: $300 - $500

**Exterior**

* **Rust:** The body of the Ram 4000 can rust prematurely, especially in areas where road salt is used. The fix involves repairing or replacing the rusted parts. Estimated cost: $500 - $2,000+

**Interior**

* **Dashboard Cracking:** The dashboard on the Ram 4000 can crack over time. The fix involves replacing the dashboard or having it repaired. Estimated cost: $300 - $500

**Summary**

The 2007 Dodge Ram 4000 has some common issues that owners should be aware of. These issues can be costly to repair, especially if they are not caught early. However, the Ram 4000 is still a popular choice for those who need a heavy-duty pickup truck. Overall, the severity of the issues is moderate while the cost of repairs tends to be higher than average.
