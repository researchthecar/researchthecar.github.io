The 2009 Dodge Ram 4000, a heavy-duty chassis cab aimed at commercial use, provides a robust platform for various applications. Engine options for this model year include the 5.7L HEMI V8, producing around 330 horsepower and 375 lb-ft of torque, the 6.7L Cummins Turbo Diesel Inline-6 which put out around 350 horsepower and 650 lb-ft of torque. While owners appreciate the Ram 4000 for its ruggedness and towing capacity, common complaints revolve around reliability issues, particularly with the electrical system and some drivetrain components. This guide aims to detail these specific problem areas and offer potential solutions, equipping potential buyers with essential knowledge.

**Engine**

**5.7L HEMI V8**

*   **HEMI Tick**
    *   Description/Symptoms: A distinct ticking or knocking sound emanating from the engine, particularly noticeable at idle or low speeds. This is often attributed to worn lifters or exhaust manifold bolts.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the affected lifters and/or exhaust manifold bolts and gaskets.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**6.7L Cummins Turbo Diesel Inline-6**

*   **Turbocharger Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, excessive black smoke from the exhaust, and a whistling or whining sound coming from the turbocharger.
    *   Solution: Replacement or rebuild of the turbocharger.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1500 - $3500+
    *   Recalls: None Found
*   **Fuel Injector Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rough idling, misfires, reduced fuel economy, and potential for white smoke from the exhaust.
    *   Solution: Replacement of faulty fuel injectors.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $2000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**All Transmission Options**

*   **Harsh Shifting**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rough or jerky shifts, particularly between gears, which can be caused by issues with the valve body or solenoids within the transmission.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis to determine if the issue is with the valve body, solenoids, or other internal components. Replacement or rebuild may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $3000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Worn Ball Joints**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Clunking or rattling noises from the front suspension, especially when going over bumps or turning.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the worn ball joints.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **TIPM (Totally Integrated Power Module) Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The TIPM controls various electrical functions. Common issues include stalling, fuel pump relay failure, and intermittent starting problems.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the TIPM.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1500+
    *   Recalls: None Found
*   **Wiring Harness Problems**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Corrosion or damage to the wiring harness, leading to various electrical malfunctions, including lighting issues, sensor failures, and communication problems.
    *   Solution: Repair or replacement of the affected wiring harness sections.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Rust**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rust forming on the lower panels and wheel wells of the vehicle
    *   Solution: Removing and repairing affected panels
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $10,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Dashboard Cracking**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Cracks appearing on the dashboard surface, particularly in areas exposed to sunlight.
    *   Solution: Replacement or repair of the dashboard (often with a dash cover).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

The 2009 Dodge Ram 4000, while a capable work truck, is prone to specific issues that potential buyers should be aware of. Electrical problems, particularly related to the TIPM and wiring harnesses, can lead to significant repair costs. Engine and transmission issues, such as the "HEMI tick," turbocharger failures, and harsh shifting, can also be costly to address. Rust issues on the exterior are a common problem in many areas. It is imperative to carefully inspect any prospective purchase for these issues and factor in potential repair costs when making a decision.
