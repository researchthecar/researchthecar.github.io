Alright, here is a comprehensive breakdown of common issues and complaints associated with the 2001 Dodge Ram 4000, formatted for your website.

The 2001 Dodge Ram 4000 is a robust and capable work truck known for its towing capacity and durability. It was available with a range of engines to suit different needs. Engine options included the 5.9L Cummins Turbo Diesel inline-six, producing around 235 horsepower and 460 lb-ft of torque, the 5.9L Magnum V8 with around 245 horsepower and 335 lb-ft of torque, and the 8.0L Magnum V10 delivering approximately 310 horsepower and 450 lb-ft of torque. The available transmissions included a 5-speed manual and a 4-speed automatic. Owners often praise the truck's ruggedness and towing capabilities, while common complaints include fuel economy, ride quality, and age-related maintenance issues.

**Engine**

**5.9L Cummins Turbo Diesel**

*   **VP44 Injection Pump Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Difficulty starting, loss of power, engine stalling, and black smoke. Can be intermittent at first, worsening over time.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the VP44 injection pump.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1200 - $2500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Lift Pump Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Low fuel pressure to the VP44 injection pump, leading to poor engine performance, stalling, or damage to the injection pump.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the lift pump. Upgrading to an aftermarket fuel system is often recommended for improved reliability.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Fuel Injector Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rough idle, misfires, excessive smoke, and poor fuel economy.
    *   Solution: Replacement of faulty fuel injectors.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300-$1000 (depending on how many injectors need to be replaced)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**5.9L Magnum V8**

*   **Intake Manifold Plenum Gasket Leak**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Oil consumption, pinging or knocking noises, and rough idle.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the intake manifold plenum gasket, often with an upgraded design.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **"Death Flash" PCM Issue**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Occasional stalling, rough idle, bucking while driving.
    *   Solution: PCM reflash or replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800
    *   Recalls: None Found

**8.0L Magnum V10**

*   **Exhaust Manifold Bolts Breaking**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Ticking noise, especially on cold start, exhaust leak.
    *   Solution: Removal of broken bolts and replacement with new, potentially upgraded bolts.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $1000+ (depending on the number of broken bolts and accessibility)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Overheating**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Engine temperature rising rapidly, coolant loss, potential damage to the engine.
    *   Solution: Inspection of cooling system components, including radiator, water pump, thermostat, and hoses. Replacement of faulty components.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200-$1500
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**4-Speed Automatic (46RE/47RE)**

*   **Overdrive Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Failure to shift into overdrive, slipping in overdrive, or erratic shifting.
    *   Solution: Transmission rebuild or replacement, focusing on overdrive clutches and related components.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1500 - $4000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Torque Converter Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Shuddering during acceleration, especially in lower gears.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the torque converter.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1500
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Harsh Shifting**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Hard shifts between gears, especially 1-2 and 2-3.
    *   Solution: Rebuilding transmission, replacing valve body, or adjusting bands.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200-$2500
    *   Recalls: None Found

**5-Speed Manual (NV4500)**

*   **5th Gear Nut Issue**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Loss of 5th gear.
    *   Solution: Disassembly of the transmission, replacement of the 5th gear nut with an updated design.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $2000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Hard Shifting/Grinding**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting into gear, grinding noises during gear changes.
    *   Solution: Inspection of clutch components, including clutch disc, pressure plate, and throwout bearing. Replacement of worn components.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500-$1500
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Ball Joint Wear**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Clunking noises from the front end, loose steering, uneven tire wear.
    *   Solution: Replacement of worn ball joints.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Steering Box Play**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Excessive play in the steering wheel, difficulty keeping the truck straight.
    *   Solution: Adjustment or replacement of the steering box.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Dashboard Cracking and Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Cracks in the dashboard, failure of instrument cluster gauges.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the dashboard or instrument cluster repair.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200-$1000 (Repair) $800-$2000 (Replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Window Motor Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Windows not working
    *   Solution: Replacement of window motor
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Rust**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rust on rocker panels, cab corners, wheel wells, and frame.
    *   Solution: Rust repair or panel replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $5000+ (depending on severity)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Headliner Sagging**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The headliner detaches from the roof and sags.
    *   Solution: Headliner replacement
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $400
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2001 Dodge Ram 4000, while a capable truck, is prone to age-related issues. Engine problems, particularly with the Cummins diesel's fuel system and the Magnum V8's plenum gasket, are common. Transmission issues, especially overdrive failure in the automatic, are also frequent concerns. Suspension and electrical problems add to the potential maintenance costs. The severity and cost of these issues can vary greatly, but prospective buyers should be aware of these common pitfalls and factor them into their purchase decision. Expect to encounter issues ranging from minor fixes costing a few hundred dollars to major repairs exceeding several thousand dollars.
