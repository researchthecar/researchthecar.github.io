**Introduction:**

The 2001 Dodge Stratus is a midsize sedan that offers a comfortable ride and plenty of interior space. It's available with a variety of engine and transmission options, including a 2.4L inline-four, a 2.7L V6, and a 2.0L turbocharged inline-four. The Stratus comes standard with front-wheel drive, but all-wheel drive is available as an option.

People like the Stratus for its spacious interior, comfortable ride, and available all-wheel drive. However, it has been criticized for its lackluster performance and poor fuel economy.

**Engine:**

**2.4L Inline-Four**

* **Intake Manifold Gasket Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Coolant or oil leaking from the intake manifold gasket.
    * Solution: Replace the intake manifold gasket.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $700+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Timing Belt Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Engine stalls or misfires, loss of power.
    * Solution: Replace the timing belt and related components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,200+
    * Recalls: None Found

**2.7L V6**

* **Cylinder Head Gasket Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaking externally or internally, overheating, white smoke from exhaust.
    * Solution: Replace the cylinder head gasket.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,000+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Intake Valve Sticking:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Engine misfires, loss of power.
    * Solution: Clean or replace intake valves.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**2.0L Turbocharged Inline-Four**

* **Turbocharger Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, whistling noise from turbo.
    * Solution: Replace the turbocharger.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,200 - $1,800+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission:**

**4-Speed Automatic Transmission (42LE)**

* **Shift Solenoid Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Transmission slipping, difficulty shifting, check engine light on.
    * Solution: Replace the shift solenoid.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Torque Converter Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Transmission shuddering, lack of acceleration.
    * Solution: Replace the torque converter.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $1,500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**5-Speed Manual Transmission (NV1500)**

* **Synchronizer Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears, grinding noises.
    * Solution: Replace the synchronizers.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $600+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Clutch Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Slipping clutch, difficulty engaging gears.
    * Solution: Replace the clutch assembly.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,200+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension:**

* **Front Lower Control Arm Bushing Wear:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Knocking or clunking noises when turning, uneven tire wear.
    * Solution: Replace the worn bushings.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical:**

* **Alternator Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Dimming lights, battery warning light on, electrical problems.
    * Solution: Replace the alternator.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Ignition Coil Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Engine misfires, loss of power.
    * Solution: Replace the ignition coil.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior:**

* **Tail Light Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Broken or cracked tail light lens.
    * Solution: Replace the tail light assembly.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior:**

* **Dashboard Cracking:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Cracks developing on the dashboard.
    * Solution: Replace the dashboard.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $700+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

The 2001 Dodge Stratus has a number of potential issues, including engine gasket failures, transmission problems, and electrical concerns. These issues can be costly to repair and can impact the overall reliability of the vehicle. Despite these issues, the Stratus offers a comfortable ride and spacious interior, making it a viable option for those seeking a midsize sedan.
