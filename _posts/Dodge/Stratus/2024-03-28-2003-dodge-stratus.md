**Introduction:**

The 2003 Dodge Stratus is a midsize sedan that offers a comfortable ride and a spacious interior. It is available with two engine options: a 2.4-liter four-cylinder and a 2.7-liter V6. Both engines are paired with a four-speed automatic transmission. Critics praise the Stratus for its roomy interior, smooth ride, and affordable price. However, it has received criticism for its lack of power and poor fuel economy.

**Engine**

**2.4-liter Four-Cylinder**

**Misfiring Engine**
* Description/Symptoms: Rough idling, poor acceleration, illuminated check engine light
* Solution: Inspect and replace faulty spark plugs or ignition coils
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
* Recalls: None Found

**2.7-liter V6**

**Timing Belt Failure**
* Description/Symptoms: Catastrophic engine failure, sudden loss of power, loud noises from the engine compartment
* Solution: Replace the timing belt and related components before it fails completely
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $600 - $1,000+
* Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**4-Speed Automatic**

**Shifting Problems**
* Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears, slipping gears, delayed engagement
* Solution: Perform a transmission fluid flush and replace worn or damaged transmission components
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,000+
* Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

**Strut Failure**
* Description/Symptoms: Bouncy ride, excessive body roll, uneven tire wear
* Solution: Replace the worn or damaged struts
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $600+
* Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

**Battery Drain**
* Description/Symptoms: Difficulty starting the vehicle, dim headlights, rapid battery discharge
* Solution: Inspect and test the electrical system to identify and fix the cause of the drain
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $200+
* Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

**Damaged Weatherstripping**
* Description/Symptoms: Water leaks into the interior, whistling noises while driving
* Solution: Replace the damaged weatherstripping seals around windows and doors
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+
* Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

**Cracked Dashboard**
* Description/Symptoms: Visible cracks on the dashboard
* Solution: Replace the damaged dashboard
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000+
* Recalls: None Found

**Malfunctioning Power Windows**
* Description/Symptoms: Windows do not operate properly, stuck or slow movement
* Solution: Repair or replace the faulty window motors or switches
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
* Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

The 2003 Dodge Stratus has a number of potential issues, including engine misfiring, timing belt failure, shifting problems, strut failure, battery drain, damaged weatherstripping, cracked dashboard, and malfunctioning power windows. The severity of these issues varies, but some can be costly to repair. Overall, the Stratus is a reliable vehicle, but it is important to be aware of these potential problems before purchasing one.
