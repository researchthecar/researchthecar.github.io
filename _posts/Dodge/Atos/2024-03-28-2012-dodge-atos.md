The 2012 Dodge Atos is a subcompact hatchback that was available with two engine options: a 1.1-liter I-4 and a 1.6-liter I-4. Both engines were paired with a 5-speed manual transmission or a 4-speed automatic transmission. The 1.1-liter engine produced 69 horsepower and 79 lb-ft of torque, while the 1.6-liter engine produced 110 horsepower and 109 lb-ft of torque. The Atos was praised for its fuel efficiency and affordability, but it was criticized for its lack of power and poor handling.

## **Engine**

### **1.1-liter I-4**
* **Timing Chain Failure**
Description/Symptoms: The timing chain can stretch or break, causing the engine to lose power or stall.
Solution: Replace the timing chain.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
Recalls: None Found

* **Head Gasket Failure**
Description/Symptoms: The head gasket can fail, causing coolant and oil to leak.
Solution: Replace the head gasket.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,200+
Recalls: None Found

### **1.6-liter I-4**
* **Piston Ring Failure**
Description/Symptoms: The piston rings can fail, causing the engine to burn excessive oil.
Solution: Replace the piston rings.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $1,500+
Recalls: None Found

## **Transmission**

### **5-speed Manual**
* **Clutch Failure**
Description/Symptoms: The clutch can wear out, causing difficulty shifting gears or the vehicle to stall.
Solution: Replace the clutch.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800+
Recalls: None Found

### **4-speed Automatic**
* **Transmission Fluid Leak**
Description/Symptoms: The transmission fluid can leak, causing the transmission to slip or shift erratically.
Solution: Replace the seals or gaskets that are leaking.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+
Recalls: None Found


## **Suspension**

* **Strut Mount Failure**
Description/Symptoms: The strut mounts can wear out, causing the suspension to make noise or the vehicle to handle poorly.
Solution: Replace the strut mounts.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
Recalls: None Found

## **Electrical**

* **Battery Drain**
Description/Symptoms: The battery can drain, causing the vehicle to not start.
Solution: Check for parasitic drains on the battery and repair or replace the affected components.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+
Recalls: None Found

* **Alternator Failure**
Description/Symptoms: The alternator can fail, causing the battery to not charge.
Solution: Replace the alternator.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+
Recalls: None Found

## **Exterior**

No Issues Found

## **Interior**

* **Seat Cushion Collapse**
Description/Symptoms: The seat cushions can collapse, causing the seats to be uncomfortable.
Solution: Replace the seat cushions.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
Recalls: None Found

## **Summary**

The 2012 Dodge Atos has several potential issues, including timing chain failure, head gasket failure, piston ring failure, clutch failure, transmission fluid leak, strut mount failure, battery drain, alternator failure, and seat cushion collapse. The severity of these issues varies, with some being relatively minor and inexpensive to fix, while others can be more serious and costly. Overall, the Atos is a relatively affordable and fuel-efficient vehicle, but its reliability and durability are questionable.
