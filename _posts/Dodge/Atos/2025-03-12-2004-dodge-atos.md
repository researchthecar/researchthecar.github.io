The 2004 Dodge Atos is a subcompact hatchback primarily sold in markets outside of North America. It was available with a 1.0-liter four-cylinder engine producing around 62 horsepower and 60 lb-ft of torque, paired with a 5-speed manual transmission. Some owners appreciate its fuel efficiency and maneuverability in urban environments. Common dislikes include its limited power, basic features, and small size.

**Engine**

**1.0L Inline-4**

*   **Rough Idling/Stalling**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Engine idles roughly or stalls, especially when cold. May be accompanied by a check engine light.
    *   Solution: Clean or replace the idle air control (IAC) valve. Check for vacuum leaks in the intake manifold and hoses.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $200+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Poor Fuel Economy**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Fuel economy is lower than expected.
    *   Solution: Check and replace the oxygen sensor. Also, inspect the fuel injectors for leaks. Replace the fuel filter if it's clogged.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $300+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**5-Speed Manual**

*   **Difficulty Shifting into Gear**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting into one or more gears, often accompanied by grinding noises.
    *   Solution: Check and adjust the clutch cable or replace the clutch kit (clutch disc, pressure plate, throwout bearing). Inspect and replace the transmission fluid.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Worn Suspension Components**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Clunking or rattling noises from the suspension, especially when going over bumps. Poor handling and uneven tire wear.
    *   Solution: Inspect and replace worn shocks/struts, ball joints, tie rod ends, and bushings.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $400+ per corner
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Failed Window Motor**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Window will not roll up or down.
    *   Solution: Replace window motor assembly.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $200+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Dim Headlights**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Headlights appear dim or flicker.
    *   Solution: Check and clean the headlight ground connections. Replace the headlight bulbs.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $10 - $50+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Rust**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rust on the underbody, wheel wells, and other areas prone to moisture.
    *   Solution: Remove rust and apply rust inhibitor. Repair or replace affected panels.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $500+ (depending on severity)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Broken Sun Visor**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Sun Visor breaks easily and droops down
    *   Solution: Replace the sun visor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $20 - $50+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2004 Dodge Atos, while known for its fuel efficiency, exhibits several common issues. Engine problems like rough idling and poor fuel economy, transmission issues such as difficulty shifting, and suspension wear can all lead to costly repairs. Electrical problems such as failed window motors can also occur. Rust and interior trim issues such as broken visors affect the vehicle's overall condition. While some repairs are relatively inexpensive, others can quickly add up, making it crucial to thoroughly inspect the vehicle before purchase and budget for potential maintenance.
