Here's a comprehensive breakdown of common issues and complaints for the 2007 Dodge Atos, formatted for your website.

The 2007 Dodge Atos, also known in some markets as the Hyundai Atos Prime, is a subcompact hatchback known for its fuel efficiency and affordability. For the 2007 model year, the primary engine option was a 1.1-liter SOHC four-cylinder engine, producing approximately 63 horsepower and 72 lb-ft of torque. The Atos was generally offered with a 5-speed manual transmission. Some owners appreciate its nimble handling in urban environments and its low running costs. Common dislikes include its limited cargo space, basic interior features, and modest performance.

**Engine**

**1.1L SOHC Four-Cylinder**

*   **Rough Idling/Stalling**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Engine idles roughly, stalls frequently, particularly when cold or under load.
    *   Solution: Clean or replace the Idle Air Control (IAC) valve, check for vacuum leaks, clean the throttle body, or replace the spark plugs/wires.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $250+ (depending on the component needing replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Check Engine Light (Various Codes)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The check engine light illuminates. Diagnostic codes often relate to the oxygen sensor, catalytic converter efficiency, or misfires.
    *   Solution: Diagnose the specific code with a scan tool. Replace the faulty sensor (oxygen, mass airflow, etc.), repair exhaust leaks, or address engine misfires.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $80 - $400+ (depending on the diagnosed issue)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Coolant Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Visible coolant leaks from hoses, radiator, or water pump area. Overheating may occur.
    *   Solution: Inspect hoses, radiator, water pump, and thermostat housing. Replace damaged components and properly bleed the cooling system.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $300+ (depending on the component needing replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**5-Speed Manual Transmission**

*   **Difficulty Shifting into Gears**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Difficulty engaging certain gears, grinding noises during shifting, or a "notchy" feel.
    *   Solution: Check and adjust the clutch cable, replace the clutch disc/pressure plate/throw-out bearing, or inspect/repair the shifter linkage.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $600+ (depending on the extent of the repair)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Clutch Slippage**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Engine revs higher than expected when accelerating, especially in higher gears.
    *   Solution: Replace the clutch disc/pressure plate.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Worn Suspension Bushings**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Clunking or rattling noises from the suspension, particularly when going over bumps. Poor handling and excessive tire wear may also occur.
    *   Solution: Replace worn-out suspension bushings (control arm bushings, sway bar bushings, etc.).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $400+ (depending on the number of bushings needing replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Worn Shock Absorbers/Struts**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Bouncy ride, excessive body roll in corners, and increased stopping distance.
    *   Solution: Replace worn shock absorbers or struts.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+ (for a pair of shocks/struts)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Battery Drain**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Battery drains quickly, especially overnight, leading to difficulty starting the car.
    *   Solution: Perform a parasitic draw test to identify the source of the drain. Common culprits include interior lights, aftermarket accessories, or faulty relays.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $200+ (depending on the source and solution)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Faulty Window Motor**
    * Description/Symptoms: Windows don't go up or down or are slow to do so.
    * Solution: Replace the window motor
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+
    * Recalls: None Found

*   **Alternator Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Battery light is on, car starts and immediately dies, or car sputters and shuts off.
    * Solution: Replace the alternator
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $400+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Rust (Especially in Coastal Regions)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rust formation on body panels, undercarriage, and around wheel wells.
    *   Solution: Repair or replace rusted panels. Apply rust inhibitor and paint. Prevention is key in high-risk areas.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $1000+ (depending on the extent of the rust damage)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Air Conditioning Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Weak or no cold air from the air conditioning system.
    *   Solution: Recharge the refrigerant, repair or replace the A/C compressor, condenser, or evaporator, or address any leaks in the system.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $80 - $600+ (depending on the component needing repair/replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

The 2007 Dodge Atos, while generally reliable, can experience some common issues related to its age and budget-oriented design. Engine problems like rough idling and coolant leaks, along with transmission issues such as difficulty shifting, are relatively common. Suspension wear is also typical. Electrical issues, and rust in certain climates, can also be problematic. Repair costs vary, with some issues being relatively inexpensive to fix while others can be more significant. Overall, potential buyers should be aware of these potential issues and factor them into their purchasing decision and maintenance budget.
