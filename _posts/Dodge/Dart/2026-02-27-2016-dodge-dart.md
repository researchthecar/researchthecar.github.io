# 2016 Dodge Dart Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2016 Dodge Dart offers distinct engine choices and an aggressive compact stance, but prospective buyers should approach with caution due to several widely reported mechanical and electrical vulnerabilities. Common pain points include unexpected engine stalling, manual clutch issues, and notable transmission shift cable defects that can compromise parking security. Reviewing maintenance records and confirming whether necessary recall repairs have been completed is critical before purchasing a used model.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Tigershark I4

The base 2.0-liter naturally aspirated four-cylinder engine produces 160 horsepower and 148 lb-ft of torque, providing standard daily drivability. Owners have reported intermittent stalling and unexpected motive power loss.

**Engine Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may unexpectedly lose power or stall while driving or attempting to start, creating safety risks on the road.
- **Solution:** Diagnose the fuel system, throttle body, and electronic control modules; replace faulty sensors or wiring harnesses as needed.

**Loss of Motive Power**
- **Symptoms:** The engine may abruptly shut down during operation without prior warning lights.
- **Solution:** Inspect powertrain control modules and electrical grounds for faults.

### 1.4L Turbocharged I4

This optional turbocharged four-cylinder engine generates 160 horsepower and 184 lb-ft of torque, offering stronger low-end pull and improved highway responsiveness.

### 2.4L Tigershark I4

The larger 2.4-liter naturally aspirated four-cylinder engine puts out 184 horsepower and 171 lb-ft of torque, delivering livelier acceleration for higher trims.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Manual

The 6-speed manual transmission pairs primarily with the turbo and base engines, though some owners experience hydraulic clutch failures.

**Clutch Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The clutch pedal may drop straight to the floorboard or fail to engage properly, preventing gear selection.
- **Solution:** Inspect and replace the clutch master cylinder, slave cylinder, or complete clutch assembly.

### 6-Speed Automatic

The 6-speed traditional automatic transmission delivers smooth gear shifts, but shifting linkage and cable failures represent a well-known point of failure.

**Shifter and Park Defect**
- **Symptoms:** The gear shifter may feel loose, fail to engage the correct gear, or allow the vehicle to roll despite the indicator showing Park.
- **Solution:** Replace the damaged transmission shift cable and worn bushings.

### 6-Speed Dual-Dry-Clutch Automatic

This dual-clutch automatic transmission is paired with the 1.4L turbo engine for quicker gear changes, though proper maintenance is vital for longevity.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

Braking components provide adequate stopping power under normal conditions, but soft pedal complaints and booster failures warrant thorough pre-purchase inspections.

**Brake Pedal Softness and Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The brake pedal may travel all the way to the floorboard during panic stops, requiring the driver to pump the pedal to achieve braking force.
- **Solution:** Inspect the brake booster, master cylinder, and vacuum lines; bleed or replace faulty hydraulic components.

## Electrical

### Electrical System

electrical issues involving infotainment resets, instrument cluster flickering, and parasitic drains are occasionally reported by owners.

**Battery Drain and Module Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle batteries may drain prematurely despite being new, accompanied by intermittent dashboard display blackouts or blown fuses.
- **Solution:** Diagnose parasitic battery draws, check multiplex wiring harnesses, and update software control modules.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V293000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Dodge&model=Dart&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** If the shifter cable detaches from the transmission, the vehicle may not perform the shifts performed by the driver. These scenarios may increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will replace the transmission side shifter cable bushing, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V824000: Equipment](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Dodge&model=Dart&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** Chrysler (FCA US LLC) is recalling various Dodge, Chrysler, and RAM vehicles equipped with Kidde Plastic-Handle or Push Button 'Pindicator' Fire Extinguishers. These extinguishers may become clogged, preventing the extinguisher from discharging as expected or requiring excessive force to activate the extinguisher.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 16V813000: Visibility:Windshield Wiper/Washer](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Dodge&model=Dart&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** Without functioning windshield wipers, the driver's visibility may be reduced, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will replace the windshield washer pumps, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V113000: Electrical System:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Dodge&model=Dart&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** Without a backup layer of software, the control module may fail to prevent an un-commanded torque event, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will update the Engine Control Module software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V674000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission:Lever And Linkage:Floor Shift](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Dodge&model=Dart&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** A loss of park function may allow the vehicle to rollaway, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2016 Dodge Dart appeals to buyers seeking sporty styling and multiple engine options, but it requires careful vetting due to documented complaints regarding engine stalling, manual clutch assembly wear, and transmission shift cable defects. Buyers should thoroughly test all electrical accessories, verify the condition of the braking system, and check vehicle history reports to ensure all safety recalls have been resolved.
