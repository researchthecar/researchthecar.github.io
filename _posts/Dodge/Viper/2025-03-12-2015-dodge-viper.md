The 2015 Dodge Viper is a high-performance sports car known for its powerful engine and aggressive styling. It came equipped with a single engine option, an 8.4-liter V10, producing a staggering 645 horsepower and 600 lb-ft of torque. Power was delivered through a Tremec TR6060 six-speed manual transmission. Owners often praise the Viper for its raw power, engaging driving experience, and iconic design. However, some common complaints include its harsh ride, limited practicality, and high running costs.

**Engine**

**8.4L V10**

*   **Valve Spring Failures**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported premature valve spring failure, leading to misfires, rough running, and potential engine damage. The issue seems to be more prevalent in heavily tracked or aggressively driven cars.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the valve springs with upgraded, more durable springs.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $3,000 - $5,000+

*   **Oil Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks, particularly around the valve covers and oil pan, have been reported.
    *   Solution: Replacing the valve cover gaskets or resealing the oil pan.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+

**Transmission**

**Tremec TR6060 6-Speed Manual**

*   **Shifting Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have experienced difficulty shifting into certain gears, particularly when cold. This can manifest as grinding or a feeling of resistance.
    *   Solution: Replacing the synchros, gear oil change with high performance oil, or a transmission rebuild.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $4,000+

**Suspension**

*   **No Issues Found**

**Electrical**

*   **Sensor Failures**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported issues with various sensors, such as the oxygen sensors or crankshaft position sensor. This can lead to check engine lights and performance issues.
    *   Solution: Diagnosing the faulty sensor and replacing it.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+

**Exterior**

*   **Delamination of Carbon Fiber Pieces**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some of the carbon fiber panels, especially those exposed to sunlight, may have delamination of the clear coat, white spotting, or turning yellow.
    *   Solution: Refinishing or replacing the carbon fiber panels.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $3,000+

**Interior**

*   **No Issues Found**

**Summary:** The 2015 Dodge Viper, while a performance icon, isn't without its issues. The most significant concerns revolve around the engine and transmission, with potential for valve spring failures, oil leaks, and shifting problems. Electrical sensor failures and carbon fiber delamination can also occur. The severity of these issues ranges from minor annoyances to potentially costly repairs. Prospective buyers should be aware of these potential problems and factor them into their purchasing decision.
