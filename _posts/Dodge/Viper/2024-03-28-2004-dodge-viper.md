**Introduction**

The 2004 Dodge Viper is a high-performance sports car known for its powerful engine and aggressive styling. It is powered by an 8.3-liter V10 engine that produces 500 horsepower and 525 lb-ft of torque. The Viper is available with either a 6-speed manual or 5-speed automatic transmission.

People like the Viper for its impressive performance and eye-catching design. However, it has also received criticism for its high price, poor fuel economy, and limited practicality.

**Engine**

**8.3L V10**

- **Camshaft Sensor Failure:** The camshaft sensor can fail, causing the engine to run poorly or stall.
  - Solution: Replace the camshaft sensor.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $300+
  - Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**6-Speed Manual**

- **Shift Linkage Malfunction:** The shift linkage can become loose, making it difficult to shift gears.
  - Solution: Tighten or replace the shift linkage.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
  - Recalls: None Found

**5-Speed Automatic**

- **Transmission Fluid Leak:** The transmission fluid pan gasket can leak, causing a loss of transmission fluid.
  - Solution: Replace the transmission fluid pan gasket.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $250+
  - Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

- **Front Control Arm Bushing Wear:** The front control arm bushings can wear out prematurely, causing the suspension to become loose and noisy.
  - Solution: Replace the front control arm bushings.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+
  - Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

- **Alternator Failure:** The alternator can fail, causing the battery to discharge and the vehicle to stall.
  - Solution: Replace the alternator.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $600+
  - Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

- **Headlight Condensation:** The headlights can develop condensation, which can impair visibility and damage the headlights.
  - Solution: Replace the headlight assemblies.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800+
  - Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

- **HVAC System Failure:** The HVAC system can fail, causing the heating, ventilation, and air conditioning to malfunction.
  - Solution: Repair or replace the HVAC system components.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1000+
  - Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2004 Dodge Viper is a high-performance sports car with some potential issues. The most common problems are related to the engine, transmission, and suspension. The severity of these issues can vary, and the cost to fix them can be significant. Overall, the Viper is a reliable car for its intended purpose, but potential owners should be aware of the possible issues before making a purchase.
