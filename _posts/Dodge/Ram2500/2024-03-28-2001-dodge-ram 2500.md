**2001 Dodge Ram 2500**

The 2001 Dodge Ram 2500 is a heavy-duty pickup truck that was available with a variety of engine and transmission options. The most popular engine was the 5.9L Cummins turbodiesel, which produced 245 horsepower and 505 lb-ft of torque. Other engine options included a 5.2L V8 (230 hp, 325 lb-ft), a 5.9L V8 (250 hp, 355 lb-ft), and a 8.0L V10 (310 hp, 450 lb-ft). The Ram 2500 was also available with a variety of transmission options, including a four-speed automatic, a five-speed manual, and a six-speed manual.

Owners of the 2001 Dodge Ram 2500 generally praise the truck's powerful engines, rugged construction, and towing and hauling capabilities. However, the truck has also been criticized for its poor fuel economy, uncomfortable ride, and cramped interior.

**Engine**

**5.9L Cummins Turbodiesel**

* **Turbocharger failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, excessive smoke, whistling noise from the turbocharger
    * Solution: Replace the turbocharger
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Injector failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Hard starting, rough running, decreased fuel economy
    * Solution: Replace the injectors
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $1,500+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Fuel pump failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Engine stalling, difficulty starting, loss of power
    * Solution: Replace the fuel pump
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**5.2L V8**

* **Intake manifold gasket failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Coolant leak, overheating, rough idle
    * Solution: Replace the intake manifold gaskets
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Spark plug failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Misfiring, rough running, decreased fuel economy
    * Solution: Replace the spark plugs
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
    * Recalls: None Found

**5.9L V8**

* **Head gasket failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Coolant leak, overheating, white smoke from the exhaust
    * Solution: Replace the head gasket
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**8.0L V10**

* **Ignition coil failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Misfiring, rough running, decreased fuel economy
    * Solution: Replace the ignition coils
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**Four-Speed Automatic**

* **Transmission slipping**
    * Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting, loss of power
    * Solution: Rebuild or replace the transmission
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Five-Speed Manual**

* **Clutch failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting, slipping, shuddering
    * Solution: Replace the clutch
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Six-Speed Manual**

* **No Issues Found**

**Summary**

The 2001 Dodge Ram 2500 is a capable heavy-duty pickup truck with a variety of engine and transmission options. The severity and cost of these issues can vary depending on the specific problem and the age and mileage of the truck.
