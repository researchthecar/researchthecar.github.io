**2005 Dodge Ram 2500**

The 2005 Dodge Ram 2500 heavy-duty pickup truck offers a powerful 5.7-liter V8 engine rated at 345 horsepower and 400 lb-ft of torque. Transmission options include a 6-speed manual or 5-speed automatic. The Ram 2500 is praised for its ruggedness and towing capabilities, but it has received some criticism for its interior quality and fuel economy.

**Engine**

* **5.7L V8**
    * **Intake Valve Seat Failure:** Valves can come loose or break off, causing loss of power and severe engine damage. Solution: Replace affected valves and valve seats. Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,000+ Recalls: 13V289, 14V584
    * **Head Gasket Failure:** Head gasket can leak coolant into the engine, causing overheating and engine damage. Solution: Replace failed head gasket and inspect cylinder head. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+ Recalls: 14V583
    * **EGR Cooler Failure:** EGR cooler can fail, allowing coolant to leak into the exhaust system. Solution: Replace failed EGR cooler. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $1,500+ Recalls: 14V263

**Transmission**

* **6-Speed Manual**
    * **Grinding or Difficulty Shifting:** Worn synchros or damaged shift forks can cause difficulty or grinding when shifting gears. Solution: Replace worn components. Estimated Cost to Fix: $600 - $1,200+ Recalls: None Found
* **5-Speed Automatic**
    * **Transmission Slippage:** Automatic transmission can slip or flare when changing gears, indicating worn clutch packs. Solution: Replace failed clutch packs and inspect transmission. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,800 - $2,800+ Recalls: 14V582
    * **Solenoid Failure:** Shift solenoids can fail, causing erratic shifting or transmission refusing to shift. Solution: Replace failed solenoid. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600+ Recalls: 15V172

**Suspension**

* **Leaf Spring Failure:** Leaf springs can break or sag over time, reducing suspension height and causing uneven wear on tires. Solution: Replace affected leaf springs. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+ Recalls: None Found
* **Control Arm Bushing Failure:** Bushing on control arms can wear out or tear, causing loose or excessive movement. Solution: Replace failed bushings. Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $600+ Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Alternator Failure:** Alternator can fail, causing battery not to charge properly. Solution: Replace failed alternator. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+ Recalls: None Found
* **Battery Drain:** Aftermarket accessories or faulty components can cause parasitic battery drain, resulting in a dead battery. Solution: Inspect and diagnose the source of the drain and repair as needed. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $600+ Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Tailgate Handle Failure:** Tailgate handle can break or come loose, preventing tailgate from latching securely. Solution: Replace failed tailgate handle. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+ Recalls: None Found
* **Door Handle Failure:** Door handles can break or come loose, making it difficult to enter or exit the vehicle. Solution: Replace affected door handles. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+ Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **HVAC Control Panel Failure:** Climate control panel can fail, causing malfunctioning of heating, air conditioning, or ventilation. Solution: Replace failed control panel. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $700+ Recalls: None Found
* **Power Window Failure:** Power window motors or switches can fail, causing power windows not to operate. Solution: Replace failed components. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600+ Recalls: None Found
* **Seat Belt Retractor Failure:** Seat belt retractor can malfunction, preventing seat belt from retracting properly. Solution: Replace failed seat belt retractor. Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800+ Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2005 Dodge Ram 2500 is a powerful pickup truck with impressive towing capabilities. However, it has some notable issues with its engine, transmission, and suspension systems. The cost of repairs can be significant, especially for major issues like engine failures or transmission replacements. Overall, the Ram 2500 is a reliable workhorse but requires regular maintenance and attention to potential problems.
