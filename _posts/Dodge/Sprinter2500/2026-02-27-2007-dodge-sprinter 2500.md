# 2007 Dodge Sprinter 2500

### Overview
The 2007 Dodge Sprinter 2500, a popular choice for commercial and recreational use, is generally known for its robust utility and cargo capacity. However, like any vehicle, it has specific areas that have seen more attention from owners regarding reliability. This analysis focuses on common reported issues beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential mechanical and electrical concerns. Understanding these potential weak points can help owners budget for repairs and prioritize preventative maintenance, ensuring the longevity and operational efficiency of their Sprinter.

---


## Engine

### 2.7L Mercedes-Benz OM647 Diesel
**Glow Plug Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Hard starting in cold weather, check engine light illumination.
- **Solution:** Replacement of faulty glow plugs and potentially the glow plug control module.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600

**Turbocharger Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of power, excessive smoke, whining noises.
- **Solution:** Inspection and potential rebuild or replacement of the turbocharger assembly.
- **Cost:** $1,200 - $2,500

**Injector Problems**
- **Symptoms:** Rough idling, poor fuel economy, engine misfires, black smoke.
- **Solution:** Cleaning or replacement of faulty fuel injectors.
- **Cost:** $600 - $1,200 per injector


## Transmission

### 5-Speed Automatic (NAG1)
**Torque Converter Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Slipping gears, shuddering, delayed engagement, fluid leaks.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of the torque converter and potentially transmission fluid flush.
- **Cost:** $1,500 - $3,000

**Transmission Control Module (TCM) Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Erratic shifting, 'limp mode' engagement, transmission warning light.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and replacement of the TCM.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1,500


## Suspension

### Standard Chassis
**Leaf Spring Breakage**
- **Symptoms:** Sagging rear end, clunking noises over bumps, uneven tire wear.
- **Solution:** Replacement of broken leaf springs.
- **Cost:** $400 - $800 per side

**Ball Joint Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Clunking or knocking sounds, steering looseness, uneven tire wear.
- **Solution:** Replacement of worn ball joints.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600 per side

### Summary Verdict
The 2007 Dodge Sprinter 2500, while a capable workhorse, exhibits some common reliability concerns primarily centered around its diesel engine components (glow plugs, turbo, injectors), automatic transmission (torque converter, TCM), and certain suspension parts (leaf springs, ball joints). Electrical gremlins, particularly with wiring harnesses and alternators, are also noted. Exterior and interior issues tend to be less critical but can impact convenience and comfort. Regular maintenance and proactive attention to these known problem areas can significantly mitigate potential downtime and repair costs for owners of this generation Sprinter.
