**2003 Dodge Sprinter 2500**

The 2003 Dodge Sprinter 2500 is a high-roof van that offers a spacious interior and powerful engine options. It comes with a standard 2.7L inline-5 engine producing 154 horsepower and 183 lb-ft of torque, or an optional 3.0L V6 engine with 184 horsepower and 229 lb-ft of torque. Both engines are paired with a standard 5-speed automatic transmission.

Owners praise the Sprinter's cavernous interior, comfortable seats, and reliable performance. However, they often criticize its poor fuel economy, limited cargo capacity, and excessive body roll during turns.

**Engine**

**2.7L Inline-5**

* **Head gasket failure:** Symptoms include excessive coolant consumption, white smoke from the exhaust, and overheating. Solution: Replace the head gasket. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,200 - $2,000+
* **Intake manifold gasket leak:** Symptoms include poor engine performance, rough idling, and vacuum leaks. Solution: Replace the intake manifold gasket. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+

**3.0L V6**

* **Timing chain failure:** Symptoms include rattling or ticking noises from the engine, reduced engine performance, and the check engine light illuminating. Solution: Replace the timing chain. Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,000+
* **Oil consumption:** Symptoms include low oil levels and excessive oil smoke from the exhaust. Solution: Replace the oil rings or the valve seals. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
* **Fuel injector failure:** Symptoms include rough idling, misfires, and poor fuel economy. Solution: Replace the faulty fuel injector. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+ per injector

**Transmission**

**5-Speed Automatic**

* **Shift solenoid failure:** Symptoms include harsh or delayed shifts, the transmission slipping out of gear, and the check engine light illuminating. Solution: Replace the shift solenoid. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
* **Torque converter failure:** Symptoms include shuddering or slipping during acceleration, overheating, and the check engine light illuminating. Solution: Replace the torque converter. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+

**Suspension**

* **Strut failure:** Symptoms include excessive bouncing, uneven tire wear, and poor handling. Solution: Replace the struts. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+ per strut
* **Coil spring breakage:** Symptoms include the vehicle sitting lower on one side, uneven tire wear, and a clunking noise when driving over bumps. Solution: Replace the broken coil spring. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+

**Electrical**

* **Alternator failure:** Symptoms include electrical system malfunctions, battery draining, and the check engine light illuminating. Solution: Replace the alternator. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
* **Battery drain:** Symptoms include a dead battery after the vehicle has been parked for a long period of time. Solution: Test the electrical system for parasitic drains and repair as necessary. Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the cause
* **Electrical wiring harness damage:** Symptoms include intermittent electrical problems, such as lights failing or gauges malfunctioning. Solution: Repair or replace the damaged wiring harness. Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the extent of the damage

**Exterior**

* **Rust:** Symptoms include peeling or flaking paint, bubbling or blistering metal, and holes in the body panels. Solution: Remove the rust and repair the damaged panels. Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the severity of the rust
* **Door handle failure:** Symptoms include the door handle breaking or becoming stuck. Solution: Replace the door handle. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+ per door handle

**Interior**

* **Climate control malfunction:** Symptoms include the air conditioner not blowing cold, the heater not working, or the fan not turning on. Solution: Diagnose and repair the faulty component. Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the component
* **Dashboard lights failure:** Symptoms include the dashboard lights not illuminating or flickering. Solution: Replace the faulty light bulbs or the lighting control module. Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $200+

**Summary**

The 2003 Dodge Sprinter 2500 is a reliable and spacious van, but it has a few common issues that can be expensive to repair. Engine problems, such as head gasket failure and timing chain failure, are the most severe and costly. Transmission issues can also be costly, especially torque converter failure. Suspension and electrical problems are less severe but can still be expensive to fix. Overall, the Sprinter 2500 is a solid vehicle but potential buyers should be aware of the potential issues before making a purchase.
