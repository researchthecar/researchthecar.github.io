**Introduction:**

The 2009 Dodge Ram 5500 is a heavy-duty pickup truck known for its impressive towing and hauling capabilities. Engine options include a 6.7-liter Cummins turbodiesel inline-6 and a 8.0-liter gasoline V10. Transmission options are a 6-speed automatic and a 6-speed manual. Owners praise the Ram 5500 for its strong performance, rugged exterior, and spacious cabin. However, some areas have faced criticism, including fuel efficiency and a lack of certain features.

**Engine**

**6.7L Cummins Turbodiesel I6**

* **EGR Cooler Failure:** The EGR cooler can crack and leak coolant, leading to overheating and engine damage.
    * Solution: Replace the EGR cooler.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Injector Failure:** The fuel injectors can become clogged or malfunction, causing engine misfires and reduced power.
    * Solution: Replace the faulty injector(s).
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**8.0L V10 Gasoline**

* **Intake Manifold Gasket Failure:** The intake manifold gasket can fail, causing a vacuum leak and reduced engine power.
    * Solution: Replace the intake manifold gasket.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**6-Speed Automatic**

* **Torque Converter Clutch Failure:** The torque converter clutch can fail, leading to transmission slipping and shuddering.
    * Solution: Replace the torque converter.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**6-Speed Manual**

* **No Issues Found**

**Suspension**

* **Rear Leaf Spring Failure:** The rear leaf springs can break or sag over time, affecting handling and load capacity.
    * Solution: Replace the leaf springs.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
    * Recalls: NHTSA Campaign Number: 22V324000

**Electrical**

* **Alternator Failure:** The alternator can fail, resulting in electrical system problems and battery drainage.
    * Solution: Replace the alternator.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Starter Failure:** The starter can wear out and fail to engage the flywheel, preventing engine start-up.
    * Solution: Replace the starter.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Headlight Lens Clouding:** The headlight lenses can become cloudy over time, reducing visibility.
    * Solution: Restore or replace the headlight lenses.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **HVAC Blower Motor Failure:** The HVAC blower motor can fail, causing a lack of air circulation and heating/cooling issues.
    * Solution: Replace the blower motor.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Dashboard Panel Cracking:** The dashboard panel can crack or warp over time, affecting aesthetics.
    * Solution: Replace the dashboard panel.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2009 Dodge Ram 5500 has several potential issues, mostly related to the engine, transmission, and electrical systems. While some repairs can be costly, the Ram 5500 remains a capable workhorse for heavy-duty tasks. Owners should be aware of potential problems and consider regular maintenance to mitigate repair expenses over time.
