# 2024 Fisker Ocean Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2024 Fisker Ocean is an all-electric SUV offering distinctive styling and dual-motor all-wheel-drive configurations. While it presents an intriguing option for EV enthusiasts, prospective buyers should approach with caution due to the limited manufacturer support following the company's financial restructuring and bankruptcy proceedings.

---

## Engine

### Single-Motor Electric Powertrain

Front-mounted single permanent magnet synchronous electric motor producing approximately 275 horsepower and 350 lb-ft of torque. Powers the entry-level front-wheel-drive trims with a focus on efficiency.

### Dual-Motor Electric Powertrain

Dual electric motor setup providing all-wheel drive, delivering up to 468 horsepower and 519 lb-ft of torque on higher trims. Offers brisk acceleration and robust performance, though long-term parts availability remains a concern.

## Transmission

### Single-Speed Direct-Drive Transmission

Single-speed direct-drive reduction gearbox paired with all powertrain configurations. Provides seamless power delivery without traditional gear shifts.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V466000: Structure:Body:Door:Handle:Exterior](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Fisker&model=Ocean&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** A door that fails to open can prevent occupants from exiting in an emergency, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Fisker will inspect and replace the door handles as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V499000: Electrical System:Propulsion System:Traction Battery:Management System/Energy Control Module (Bms/Becm)](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Fisker&model=Ocean&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** A sudden loss of drive power increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Fisker will replace the pumps, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V623000: Service Brakes, Electric:Software/Programming](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Fisker&model=Ocean&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** The brake module software may cause an unexpected reduction in regenerative braking, decreasing the braking performance. Decreased braking performance can extend the distance required to stop, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 24V865000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic; Autohold Brake System/Brake Hold:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Fisker&model=Ocean&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** The transmission may not shift into the selected gear, allowing the vehicle to roll away while in Drive or Reverse. A vehicle roll away increases the risk of a crash or injury.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2024 Fisker Ocean is a high-risk used vehicle purchase. Although it features modern electric powertrain options and compelling styling, the manufacturer's uncertain corporate future and lack of dealer support make sourcing specialized parts and servicing difficult for everyday owners.
