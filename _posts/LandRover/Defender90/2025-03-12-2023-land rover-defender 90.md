Here's a breakdown of common issues and complaints associated with the 2023 Land Rover Defender 90, organized by category and component, including information on symptoms, solutions, and estimated costs. The Defender 90 is a highly capable and stylish SUV, known for its off-road prowess and distinctive design. However, like any vehicle, it has its share of potential problems. The 2023 Defender 90 is available with a range of engines including a 2.0L turbocharged four-cylinder (296 hp, 295 lb-ft of torque), a 3.0L inline-six mild-hybrid (395 hp, 406 lb-ft of torque), and a 5.0L supercharged V8 (518 hp, 461 lb-ft of torque). All engine options are paired with an 8-speed automatic transmission. While owners often praise the Defender's ruggedness and luxurious interior, common complaints include electrical glitches, build quality issues, and the high cost of ownership.

**Engine**

**2.0L Turbocharged Four-Cylinder**

*   **Excessive Oil Consumption**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported higher than expected oil consumption, requiring more frequent oil top-offs between scheduled oil changes. This may manifest as low oil level warnings or the need to add oil every few thousand miles.
    *   Solution: The dealership might perform an oil consumption test to determine the severity. Solutions can range from replacing PCV valves to piston ring replacement, or in severe cases, a complete engine rebuild.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $8,000+ depending on the extent of the damage.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Coolant Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Owners have reported coolant leaks around the water pump or thermostat housing. This can lead to overheating if not addressed.
    *   Solution: Replacing the faulty water pump or thermostat housing.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**3.0L Inline-Six Mild-Hybrid**

*   **Mild Hybrid System Malfunctions**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Issues with the mild-hybrid system, often related to the 48V battery or integrated starter generator (ISG), may cause warning lights, reduced power, or even stalling.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis of the hybrid system components is required. Replacement of the ISG or 48V battery might be necessary. Software updates may resolve some issues.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $5,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Turbocharger Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported issues with the turbocharger, including whistling noises, loss of power, or turbocharger failure.
    *   Solution: Replacing the turbocharger.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,500 - $4,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**5.0L Supercharged V8**

*   **Supercharger Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Supercharger failure symptoms include a noticeable loss of power, unusual noises (grinding, whining), and potential diagnostic trouble codes (DTCs) related to supercharger performance.
    *   Solution: Supercharger replacement or rebuild.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $5,000 - $9,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Fuel Injector Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rough idling, misfires, reduced power, and fuel smell due to leaking injectors.
    *   Solution: Replace faulty fuel injectors.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,200 - $3,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**8-Speed Automatic (All Engines)**

*   **Harsh Shifting/Hesitation**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported experiencing harsh or jerky shifting, especially during low-speed acceleration or deceleration. Hesitation when shifting gears can also be present.
    *   Solution: A software update to the transmission control module (TCM) might resolve the issue. In more severe cases, valve body replacement or transmission rebuild might be needed.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 (software update) - $6,000+ (transmission rebuild)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Torque Converter Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Shuddering at low speeds, particularly when the torque converter is locking up.
    *   Solution: Torque converter replacement or transmission rebuild.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $6,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Air Suspension Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The air suspension system may fail, causing the vehicle to sag on one or more corners, or display warning lights on the dashboard. This can be due to leaks in the air struts or failures in the air compressor.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis and repair of the air suspension system. This may involve replacing air struts, the air compressor, or air lines.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $4,000+ per corner, depending on the component needing replacement.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Software issues with the Air Suspension**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The air suspension system may become disabled due to a software issue. This can cause a hard ride that is not the desired comfort level.
    *   Solution: Dealer needs to recalibrate the suspension system with a software update.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200-$500
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The Pivi Pro infotainment system can experience glitches such as freezing, crashing, slow response times, or failure to connect to smartphones.
    *   Solution: Software updates often resolve these issues, but sometimes a system reset or component replacement is necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 (software update) - $2,000+ (component replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Sensor Malfunctions**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Various sensors, such as parking sensors, blind-spot monitoring sensors, and adaptive cruise control sensors, may malfunction, leading to inaccurate readings or system failures.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis and replacement of the faulty sensor(s).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,000+ per sensor, depending on the type and location.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Battery Drain**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Premature battery drain leading to difficulty starting the vehicle or requiring frequent jump-starts.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis to find the parasitic drain. Potential solutions range from software updates, and module replacements.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $1,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Door Alignment Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported issues with door alignment, leading to wind noise or difficulty closing the doors properly.
    *   Solution: Adjusting door hinges and latches.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Sunroof Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Sunroof malfunctions, including sticking, failing to open or close completely, or water leaks.
    *   Solution: Repair or replacement of sunroof components, including motors, seals, and tracks.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Water Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Water leaks into the cabin, often around the sunroof, windshield, or door seals.
    *   Solution: Sealing the source of the leak, replacing damaged seals, or repairing body panels.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,500+ depending on the source and severity of the leak.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Seat Motor Failures**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Power seat motors failing, preventing seat adjustment.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the faulty seat motor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,200+ per seat.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2023 Land Rover Defender 90 is a capable and desirable vehicle, but potential buyers should be aware of the issues reported by owners. Electrical glitches and infotainment problems seem to be relatively common, though often resolvable with software updates. Engine and transmission issues, while less frequent, can be more costly to address. The severity of reported problems varies, but addressing these issues can range from a few hundred dollars for minor fixes to several thousands for major repairs. It is recommended to have a pre-purchase inspection performed by a qualified mechanic before purchasing a used Defender 90 to identify any potential problems.
