**Introduction:**

The 1995 Land Rover Defender 90 is a rugged and capable off-road vehicle. It is powered by a 3.9-liter V8 engine that produces 182 horsepower and 233 lb-ft of torque. The V8 engine is paired with a 4-speed manual transmission. The Defender 90 is known for its durability and off-road performance, but it also has some common issues.

**Engine**

**3.9-liter V8**

* **Blown head gasket:** Symptoms include overheating, white smoke from the exhaust, and loss of coolant. Solution: Replace the head gasket. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $1,500+
* **Intake manifold gasket failure:** Symptoms include rough idling, loss of power, and increased emissions. Solution: Replace the intake manifold gasket. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800+
* **Oil leaks:** Symptoms include oil puddles under the vehicle and low oil levels. Solution: Replace the leaking gaskets or seals. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
* **Timing chain failure:** Symptoms include rattling noises from the engine, loss of power, and difficulty starting. Solution: Replace the timing chain. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,000+

**Transmission**

**4-speed Manual**

* **Gearbox failure:** Symptoms include difficulty shifting gears, grinding noises, and loss of power. Solution: Replace the gearbox. Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,000+

**Suspension**

* **Leaf spring failure:** Symptoms include sagging rear end, loss of ride height, and increased noise. Solution: Replace the leaf springs. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
* **Shock absorber failure:** Symptoms include rough ride, loss of stability, and increased noise. Solution: Replace the shock absorbers. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+

**Electrical**

* **Alternator failure:** Symptoms include dim lights, flickering dashboard lights, and difficulty starting. Solution: Replace the alternator. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800+
* **Battery failure:** Symptoms include difficulty starting, dim lights, and electrical problems. Solution: Replace the battery. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
* **Wiring harness issues:** Symptoms include electrical problems, flickering lights, and loss of power. Solution: Repair or replace the wiring harness. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+

**Exterior**

* **Rust:** Symptoms include rust spots on the body, frame, and suspension components. Solution: Repair the rust or replace the affected components. Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the extent of the rust

**Interior**

* **Dashboard cracking:** Symptoms include cracks in the dashboard. Solution: Replace the dashboard. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+

**Summary:**

The 1995 Land Rover Defender 90 is a capable off-road vehicle, but it does have some common issues. The most severe and expensive issues include engine and transmission problems, which can cost over $1,000 to fix. However, many of the other issues are relatively minor and can be fixed for a few hundred dollars. Overall, the Defender 90 is a reliable vehicle that can provide years of off-road enjoyment.
