The 2022 Land Rover Defender 110 is a modern reimagining of the classic off-road icon. It is generally lauded for its rugged capability, distinctive styling, and relatively comfortable interior. Some common complaints include electrical gremlins, reliability concerns, and high maintenance costs. Engine options for the 2022 Defender 110 include a 2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4 producing 296 horsepower and 295 lb-ft of torque; a 3.0L Turbocharged Inline-6 with a mild hybrid system producing 395 horsepower and 406 lb-ft of torque; and a 5.0L Supercharged V8 producing 518 horsepower and 461 lb-ft of torque. All engine options are paired with an 8-speed automatic transmission.

**Engine**

**2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4**
* **Engine Misfires**
    * Description/Symptoms: Rough idling, noticeable shaking, check engine light illuminated.
    * Solution: Replacing faulty ignition coils.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**3.0L Turbocharged Inline-6 (Mild Hybrid)**
* **Mild Hybrid System Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: "Check Engine" light, reduced power, error message related to the 48V mild hybrid system.
    * Solution: Software update to the hybrid control module or replacement of a faulty component within the mild hybrid system.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2000+ depending on the component.
    * Recalls: None Found

**5.0L Supercharged V8**

* **Supercharger Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: Whining noise from the engine bay, reduced power, potential supercharger failure.
    * Solution: Replacing the supercharger unit.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $4000 - $8000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**8-Speed Automatic (All Engines)**

* **Harsh Shifting/Hesitation**
    * Description/Symptoms: Noticeable clunking or jerking during gear changes, hesitation when accelerating.
    * Solution: Software update to the transmission control module (TCM), transmission fluid flush, or replacement of valve body.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $3000+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Transmission Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Complete loss of drive, inability to shift gears.
    * Solution: Replacement of the transmission unit.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $7000 - $12000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Air Suspension Faults**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Vehicle sitting low on one or more corners, "Suspension Fault" warning light illuminated, inability to adjust ride height.
    *   Solution: Replacing air struts, air compressor, or ride height sensors.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $3000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Freezing/Crashing**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Touchscreen unresponsive, system restarting randomly, loss of functionality.
    *   Solution: Software update, replacement of the infotainment unit.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $2000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Sensor Malfunctions (Parking Sensors, Blind Spot Monitoring, etc.)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: False alarms, system not functioning correctly.
    *   Solution: Replacing faulty sensors or control modules.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Battery Drain**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Vehicle not starting after sitting for a short period.
    *   Solution: Replacing the battery, diagnosing and repairing parasitic drain.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Door Seal Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Water entering the cabin during rain or car washes.
    *   Solution: Replacing or resealing door seals.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Sunroof Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Sunroof not opening or closing properly, excessive noise.
    *   Solution: Repairing or replacing sunroof motor or components.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Rattles and Squeaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Annoying noises coming from various parts of the interior, especially while driving on rough roads.
    *   Solution: Identifying and addressing the source of the noise (e.g., loose trim pieces).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2022 Land Rover Defender 110, while admired for its off-road capabilities and design, presents a range of potential issues. Electrical problems, particularly with the infotainment system and various sensors, are relatively common and can be frustrating. Suspension issues, specifically with the air suspension system, can be costly to repair. Engine and transmission issues are also present, varying depending on the engine option. Overall, potential buyers should be aware of these potential issues and consider a thorough inspection before purchasing. Repair costs can range from a few hundred dollars for minor fixes to several thousand for major component replacements.
