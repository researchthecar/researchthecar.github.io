**Introduction:**

The 2020 Land Rover Defender 110 is a rugged and capable SUV that combines off-road prowess with modern convenience. Available engine options include the P300 (2.0L turbocharged inline-4) with 296 horsepower and 295 lb-ft of torque, the P400 (3.0L turbocharged inline-6) with 395 horsepower and 406 lb-ft of torque, and the P400e (3.0L turbocharged inline-6 with electric motor) with 398 horsepower and 472 lb-ft of torque. Transmission options include an 8-speed automatic transmission for all three engines.

Despite its impressive capabilities, the 2020 Land Rover Defender 110 has faced some issues that have been reported by owners. These issues range in severity from minor annoyances to more significant concerns that may require expensive repairs.

**Engine**
- **P300 2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4**
    - **Turbocharger Failure:**
        - Description/Symptoms: Reduced engine power, turbocharger noise, smoke from exhaust
        - Solution: Replace turbocharger and associated parts
        - Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,000+
        - Recalls: None Found
- **P400 3.0L Turbocharged Inline-6**
    - **Oil Consumption:**
        - Description/Symptoms: Excessive oil consumption, low oil level warning light on
        - Solution: Investigate source of oil leak, replace faulty parts as necessary
        - Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
        - Recalls: L552
- **P400e 3.0L Turbocharged Inline-6 with Electric Motor**
    - **Battery Issues:**
        - Description/Symptoms: Reduced electric range, battery warning lights on
        - Solution: Replace faulty battery or battery management system components
        - Estimated Cost to Fix: $5,000 - $8,000+
        - Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

- **8-Speed Automatic Transmission:**
    - **Shifting Problems:**
        - Description/Symptoms: Rough or delayed shifts, transmission slipping
        - Solution: Replace transmission fluid and filter, update transmission software
        - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
        - Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

- **No Issues Found**

**Electrical**

- **Electrical Faults:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Intermittent electrical issues, such as flickering lights, power window failures
    - Solution: Inspect wiring harness for damage, replace faulty components as necessary
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+
    - Recalls: L558

**Exterior**

- **Paint Defects:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Bubbling or peeling paint, especially on the hood and roof
    - Solution: Refinishing of affected panels
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+ per panel
    - Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

- **Instrument Panel Issues:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Faulty gauges, unresponsive touchscreen
    - Solution: Replace faulty instrument panel or components
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
    - Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

Overall, the 2020 Land Rover Defender 110 has received mostly positive reviews from owners. However, some issues have been reported, including turbocharger failures, oil consumption, shifting problems, and electrical faults. The severity and cost of these issues vary, but some can be significant. Potential buyers should be aware of these issues before making a purchase decision and consider extended warranty coverage.
