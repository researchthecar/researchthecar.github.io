# 2023 Land Rover Defender 110 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2023 Land Rover Defender 110 combines iconic styling with modern off-road capabilities and high-tech amenities. Buyers exploring the used market should weigh its impressive performance against notable owner-reported reliability concerns, particularly surrounding electrical systems and front windshield vulnerability.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4 (P300)

The 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine generates approximately 296 horsepower and 295 lb-ft of torque, serving as the entry-level powertrain option. It provides adequate acceleration for daily driving, though some buyers prefer the added refinement of the six-cylinder or V8 options.

### 3.0L Turbocharged Inline-6 MHEV (P400)

The 3.0-liter turbocharged inline-six mild-hybrid engine produces around 395 horsepower and 406 lb-ft of torque, offering a balanced mix of responsiveness and smooth power delivery. While generally robust, this mild-hybrid setup has seen owner complaints regarding electrical power converter failures.

**DC-DC Converter and Electrical Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Battery charging faults appear on the dashboard, followed by progressive shutdown of vehicle systems, loss of motive power, and engine stalling while driving.
- **Solution:** Have the vehicle inspected by a qualified technician or dealership to diagnose and replace the failed DC-DC converter component.

### 5.0L Supercharged V8 (P525 / V8)

The supercharged 5.0-liter V8 engine pushes output to 518 horsepower and 461 lb-ft of torque, delivering exceptionally rapid acceleration and robust towing capability. It is a high-performance choice that comes with increased fuel consumption and higher overall maintenance demands.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed ZF Automatic Transmission

The ZF 8-speed automatic transmission is paired across all engine configurations, delivering smooth and rapid gear changes both on-road and during technical off-roading. It is generally regarded as a durable unit in modern luxury applications, with few recurring mechanical defect reports.

## Exterior

### Exterior Lighting

The Defender's advanced electrical architecture powers extensive lighting, driver aids, and convenience features, though isolated component failures can occur.

**Daytime Running Light Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Daytime running lights or headlamp assemblies fail completely, sometimes occurring shortly outside of factory warranty coverage.
- **Solution:** Inspect and replace the failed headlamp or LED daytime running light assembly.

### Windshield and Glass

The upright stance and flat profile of the Defender's exterior glazing make the front windshield particularly susceptible to flying debris and temperature-induced stress cracking.

**Prone Windshield Cracking and Chipping**
- **Symptoms:** Windshields develop stress cracks spontaneously or splinter easily from minor road impacts and small rock strikes.
- **Solution:** Replace the damaged windshield and consider applying a protective film to minimize future stone-chip vulnerability.

## Summary Verdict

The 2023 Land Rover Defender 110 is a highly capable and desirable luxury SUV, but prospective used-car buyers should perform a thorough inspection. Pay close attention to vehicle service history regarding mild-hybrid electrical components, and check the front windshield closely for existing chips or cracks before finalizing a purchase.
