# 2019 Land Rover Range Rover Velar Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used luxury SUV, the Land Rover Range Rover Velar stands out for its striking design and premium interior features. However, prospective buyers should review owner-reported feedback carefully, as some drivers have encountered electrical glitches, lighting failures, and cooling system concerns. Keeping a close eye on service records and inspecting key components prior to purchase can help mitigate potential ownership surprises.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged 4-Cylinder (P250)

The 2.0-liter turbocharged 4-cylinder engine produces approximately 247 horsepower and is paired with an 8-speed automatic transmission. It offers a balance of efficiency and daily drivability, though buyers should verify cooling system maintenance.

**Cooling System and Engine Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Owners report coolant leaks originating from disintegrating plastic components such as cooling elbows and manifolds, potentially leading to overheating.
- **Solution:** Inspect coolant lines, elbows, and manifolds for signs of degradation, and replace faulty plastic fittings with updated parts.
- **Cost:** 1800.00

**Engine Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have experienced unexpected engine stalling or complete loss of power while driving at various speeds.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified mechanic diagnose electronic control modules, fuel delivery, and sensor connections to identify the root cause of power loss.

### 3.0L Supercharged V6 (P380)

The 3.0-liter supercharged V6 engine delivers around 380 horsepower for stronger acceleration and high-performance highway passing. Routine maintenance and inspection of cooling components are recommended for reliable long-term operation.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

Equipped across the lineup with an all-wheel-drive system, this 8-speed automatic provides smooth gear changes and optimized power delivery. No prominent recurring transmission failure patterns are highlighted in current owner feedback.

## Exterior

### Exterior Lighting

Exterior lighting systems rely heavily on integrated LEDs, which can experience premature individual component wear.

**Daytime Running Light (DRL) Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The integrated LED daytime running lights or headlight assemblies experience premature chip failure, causing them to flicker or stop working completely.
- **Solution:** Replace the affected headlight or LED module assembly as needed.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V040000: Engine](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Land+Rover&model=Range+Rover+Velar&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** Engine failure can increase the risk of crash.
- **What to do:** Land Rover has notified owners, and dealers will replace the crankshaft pulley center retaining bolt, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V117000: Exterior Lighting:Lighting Control Module:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Land+Rover&model=Range+Rover+Velar&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 108, "Lamps, Reflective Devices, and Associated Equipment." The driver may be unaware that the high beams are illuminated, reducing visibility for oncoming traffic, and increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Land Rover will notify owners, and dealers will update the vehicle software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The Land Rover Range Rover Velar offers luxury styling and dynamic performance, but buyers must perform thorough pre-purchase inspections. Pay particular attention to the cooling system, electrical connections, and exterior lighting functionality to avoid unexpected out-of-pocket repair expenses.
