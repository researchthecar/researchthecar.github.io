# 2023 Land Rover Range Rover Velar Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2023 Land Rover Range Rover Velar delivers striking modern styling and a luxurious cabin, but used car buyers should approach with careful consideration regarding overall reliability. While modern luxury vehicles feature complex electronics and advanced powertrains, owner reports indicate potential areas of concern that require thorough inspection before purchase.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4 (P250)

The P250 features a 2.0-liter turbocharged inline-4 engine producing approximately 247 horsepower and 269 lb-ft of torque. It provides adequate power for daily driving, though owners should carefully verify maintenance history.

**Oxygen Sensor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Multiple oxygen sensors can fail prematurely, leading to warning lights and requiring replacement that takes the vehicle out of service.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified technician diagnose the fault codes and replace faulty oxygen sensors with genuine or OEM-equivalent parts.

**Sudden Engine Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have reported catastrophic engine failure occurring suddenly at highway speeds without prior dashboard warnings or oil pressure alerts.
- **Solution:** Verify complete maintenance records and perform a thorough pre-purchase inspection by a specialist, checking for signs of internal engine wear or coolant/oil leaks.

### 3.0L Turbocharged Inline-6 MHEV (P340 / P400)

The P340 and P400 utilize a 3.0-liter turbocharged inline-6 mild-hybrid engine, generating roughly 335 to 395 horsepower depending on the tune. This powertrain offers strong performance and smoother power delivery, though its sophisticated hybrid components add complexity.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

Equipped with a standard 8-speed automatic transmission across all trims paired with all-wheel drive, this unit generally delivers smooth shifts and efficient highway cruising. Routine fluid checks are recommended to maintain long-term durability.

## Steering

### Lane Keep Assist

Advanced safety and driver-assistance technologies offer added peace of mind, though software dependency can occasionally lead to feature glitches.

**Loss of Active Steering Intervention**
- **Symptoms:** The lane keep assist system may lose its active steering intervention capability following software over-the-air updates.
- **Solution:** Visit a dealership to check for software patches, module resets, or calibration updates to restore full driver-assist functionality.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V044000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Engine:Oil/Lubrication](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Land+Rover&model=Range+Rover+Velar&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** An oil leak in the presence of an ignition source can increase the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the engine cam carrier and related components as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V248000: Electrical System:Propulsion System:Dc/Dc Converter](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Land+Rover&model=Range+Rover+Velar&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** A loss of drive power increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

When shopping for a used 2023 Land Rover Range Rover Velar, a comprehensive pre-purchase inspection by a knowledgeable technician is essential. Prospective buyers should verify that all software updates have been properly applied and look closely at powertrain health to ensure a trouble-free ownership experience.
