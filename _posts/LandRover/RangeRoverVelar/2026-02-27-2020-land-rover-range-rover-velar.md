# 2020 Land Rover Range Rover Velar Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2020 Land Rover Range Rover Velar, buyers are drawn to its striking design and luxurious interior. However, prospective owners should carefully evaluate mechanical and electrical reliability. Reviewing owner complaints reveals potential areas of concern, particularly regarding cooling system integrity, auxiliary electronics, and software behavior.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4 (P250)

The 2.0-liter turbocharged inline-4 engine produces approximately 247 horsepower and 269 lb-ft of torque. It provides adequate daily power, though buyers should watch for routine fluid maintenance and cooling system health.

### 3.0L Turbocharged/Supercharged Inline-6 / V6 (P340 / P400)

The larger 3.0-liter powertrain options offer robust performance ranging from 335 to 395 horsepower. While smooth and powerful, these engines require close inspection of cooling lines and auxiliary radiators for leaks.

**Cooling System and Coolant Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have reported sudden coolant low warnings on the dash, often stemming from leaks in components like the front lower coolant crossover pipes or auxiliary radiators.
- **Solution:** Inspect the engine valley, coolant hoses, and auxiliary radiators for signs of leakage, and replace compromised crossover pipes or radiators as needed.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

Equipped with an 8-speed ZF automatic transmission across all trims and standard all-wheel drive, this unit generally delivers smooth and responsive gear changes under normal operating conditions.

## Electrical

### Infotainment and Display System

Electrical architecture in modern luxury vehicles is complex, and the Velar is no exception. Infotainment and sensor glitches are among the more commonly reported electronic gripes.

**Infotainment Freezes and Screen Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** The InControl Touch Pro system and instrument screens can freeze, randomly change displays during driving, or require full vehicle restarts to function correctly.
- **Solution:** Check for dealership software updates that address known stability glitches within the infotainment architecture.

### Backup Camera

Exterior cameras and associated wiring harnesses should be tested during a pre-purchase inspection to ensure reliable rearview visibility.

**Backup Camera Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may encounter a camera fault error message on the display screen, potentially related to moisture or water intrusion.
- **Solution:** Inspect camera wiring connections and seals for water damage, and replace faulty camera units if necessary.

## Exterior

### Power Tailgate

Interior convenience features, including power liftgates and seating adjustments, should be thoroughly tested during an inspection.

**Unexpected Tailgate Closing**
- **Symptoms:** The power tailgate has been observed to close unexpectedly on its own while occupants are loading or near the cargo area.
- **Solution:** Inspect tailgate pinch sensors, electronic struts, and control modules for proper calibration or mechanical failure.

## Safety

### Driver Assistance and Remote Start

Advanced driver assistance and active safety features enhance protection but can occasionally be impacted by software conflicts.

**Remote Start Safety System Faults**
- **Symptoms:** Using the factory smartphone app for remote start has been reported to trigger repeated temporary failures in critical safety systems such as traction control, hill descent control, and automatic emergency braking.
- **Solution:** Consult a dealer or service specialist to check for software patches related to remote starting procedures and module communications.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V668000: Seat Belts:Front:Retractor](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Land+Rover&model=Range+Rover+Velar&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** An unsecured child restraint system can increase the risk of injury during a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the seat belt assemblies as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V117000: Exterior Lighting:Lighting Control Module:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Land+Rover&model=Range+Rover+Velar&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 108, "Lamps, Reflective Devices, and Associated Equipment." The driver may be unaware that the high beams are illuminated, reducing visibility for oncoming traffic, and increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Land Rover will notify owners, and dealers will update the vehicle software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2020 Land Rover Range Rover Velar offers striking road presence and a luxurious cabin, but used-car buyers should approach with a thorough inspection mindset. Pay close attention to cooling system components to catch potential leaks early, and verify that all software updates for the infotainment and driver assistance features have been applied. A comprehensive pre-purchase inspection by a knowledgeable specialist is strongly recommended.
