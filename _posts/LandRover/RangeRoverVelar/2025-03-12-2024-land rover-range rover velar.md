The 2024 Land Rover Range Rover Velar is a luxury SUV known for its sleek design, comfortable interior, and off-road capabilities. Engine options include a P250 2.0L turbocharged four-cylinder (247 hp, 269 lb-ft torque), a P340 3.0L inline-six mild-hybrid (335 hp, 354 lb-ft torque) and a P400 3.0L inline-six mild-hybrid (395 hp, 405 lb-ft torque). All engines are paired with an 8-speed automatic transmission. People generally appreciate the Velar's stylish exterior and well-appointed cabin. Common complaints include the infotainment system can be slow and glitchy and the reliability can be questionable.

**Transmission**

*   **8-Speed Automatic Transmission**

    *   **Hesitation and Jerking During Shifts**
        *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners report experiencing hesitation, rough shifts, or jerking motions, particularly when shifting at lower speeds or during light acceleration.
        *   Solution: In some cases, a software update to the transmission control module (TCM) can resolve the issue. If the problem persists, a more in-depth diagnostic check may be required, potentially leading to valve body replacement or, in severe cases, a transmission replacement.
        *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 (Software Update) - $2,000+ (Valve Body/Transmission Replacement)
        *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Air Suspension Failure (If Equipped)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The air suspension system may fail, causing the vehicle to sit unevenly or completely deflate. This can result in a rough ride and potentially render the vehicle undrivable.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis to identify the faulty component (air compressor, air struts, air lines, height sensors) and replacement of the defective part.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,500+ depending on the component and labor.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Freezing/Crashing**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The Pivi Pro infotainment system can be prone to freezing, crashing, or becoming unresponsive. This can affect navigation, audio, climate control, and other vehicle functions.
    *   Solution: Performing a hard reset or software update might resolve the issue. If it continues, a visit to the dealership for diagnostics and potential hardware replacement of the infotainment unit may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 (Software Update) - $1,500+ (Infotainment Unit Replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found
*   **Battery Drain Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The vehicle's battery may drain excessively, leading to difficulty starting or complete battery failure. This can be caused by various factors, including faulty modules, software glitches, or parasitic drains.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis to identify the source of the drain and repair or replace the affected component.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 (Battery Replacement) - $500+ (Identifying and Repairing Drain)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Malfunctioning Power Liftgate**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The power liftgate may fail to open or close properly, or may operate erratically.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis to identify the faulty component (liftgate motor, sensors, wiring) and replacement of the defective part.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **No Issues Found**

**Summary**

The 2024 Land Rover Range Rover Velar, while stylish and luxurious, can have issues primarily concerning the infotainment system, air suspension (if equipped), and potential transmission issues. Electrical glitches and battery drain can also be present. The severity of these issues can range from minor inconveniences that can be fixed with a software update to major mechanical failures that can require costly repairs. Potential buyers should be aware of these potential issues and factor in the potential cost of repairs when considering the vehicle.
