# 2020 Land Rover Range Rover Sport Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2020 Land Rover Range Rover Sport blends luxury, impressive off-road capability, and a striking design, but prospective buyers should approach with caution regarding long-term reliability. Owner complaints frequently highlight complex electrical glitches, cooling system leaks, and powertrain concerns that can lead to unexpected vehicle shutdowns. While it offers a refined driving experience and powerful engine options, maintaining a used model requires vigilance and a healthy repair budget.

---

## Engine

### 3.0L Turbocharged Inline-6 (P360 / P400)

The 3.0-liter turbocharged inline-6 utilizes mild-hybrid technology to produce roughly 355 to 395 horsepower, delivering brisk acceleration. It provides a smooth driving dynamic, though owners should routinely inspect for fluid leaks.

**Coolant Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Owners report strong sweet odors or visible puddles due to coolant leaking from engine components, potentially triggering overheating warnings.
- **Solution:** Inspect all coolant hoses, fittings, and the water pump for leaks; replace faulty lines or seals as needed.

**Fuel Supply Line Leak**
- **Symptoms:** Fuel leaks occurring near the top of the valve cover, creating a strong fuel odor and a potential fire hazard under the hood.
- **Solution:** Replace the leaking fuel supply line with an updated component when available.

### 5.0L Supercharged V8

The potent 5.0-liter supercharged V8 engine pumps out between 518 and 575 horsepower, offering rapid acceleration and robust towing capacity. It is mechanically robust compared to ancillary systems, but demands premium fuel and rigorous maintenance.

### 48V DC/DC Converter and Charging System

The electrical system relies heavily on complex software and a 48-volt architecture, which can occasionally experience total module failures and screen blackouts.

**Sudden Electrical Shutdown and High-Speed Stall**
- **Symptoms:** Illumination of charging system fault messages followed by a total loss of motive power and complete electrical system shutdown while driving.
- **Solution:** Have the 48V DC/DC converter and related electrical architecture inspected, and apply necessary manufacturer software updates or hardware replacements.

**Infotainment and Display Blackout**
- **Symptoms:** Upper and lower central touchscreens go completely black and become entirely inoperable while driving, disabling navigation and climate controls.
- **Solution:** Perform software resets or replace faulty display modules and wiring harnesses at a certified service center.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

This smooth-shifting 8-speed automatic transmission pairs with standard four-wheel drive to deliver refined gear changes across all trims. It generally operates reliably, though drivers should verify proper shift response during a test drive.

## Suspension

### Active Cornering Enhancement and Air Suspension

The adaptive air suspension delivers a plush ride and adjustable ground clearance, though the underbody lines require protection against road salt and moisture to prevent corrosion.

**ACE Hydraulic Line Corrosion**
- **Symptoms:** Underbody hydraulic lines and fittings for the Active Cornering Enhancement system suffer from severe corrosion due to poor shielding, leading to fluid loss and suspension failure.
- **Solution:** Inspect underbody hydraulic lines for rust, and replace compromised pumps, fittings, or lines.

## Brakes

### Brake Booster and Auxiliary Battery System

The braking system provides strong stopping power under normal conditions, but reliance on auxiliary electrical systems means a dead battery can severely compromise pedal feel.

**Auxiliary Battery Failure Leading to Hard Pedal**
- **Symptoms:** Auxiliary battery failure causes a severe loss of power brake assist, resulting in an extremely stiff brake pedal and significantly increased stopping distances.
- **Solution:** Replace the failed auxiliary battery and check the charging circuit to restore proper power assist to the braking system.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V117000: Exterior Lighting:Lighting Control Module:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Land+Rover&model=Range+Rover+Sport&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 108, "Lamps, Reflective Devices, and Associated Equipment." The driver may be unaware that the high beams are illuminated, reducing visibility for oncoming traffic, and increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Land Rover will notify owners, and dealers will update the vehicle software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V586000: Tires](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Land+Rover&model=Range+Rover+Sport&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 110, "Tire Selection and Rims." Failure to inflate tires to the correct pressure could lead to loss of vehicle stability and control, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Land Rover will notify owners, and dealers will apply the correct labels, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V585000: Electrical System:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Land+Rover&model=Range+Rover+Sport&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 138, "Tire Pressure Monitoring Systems." Drivers may unknowingly operate the vehicle with low tire pressure, possibly causing tire tread separation, thereby increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Land Rover will notify owners, and dealers will update the instrument cluster software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V044000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Engine:Oil/Lubrication](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Land+Rover&model=Range+Rover+Sport&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** An oil leak in the presence of an ignition source can increase the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the engine cam carrier and related components as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V023000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Land+Rover&model=Range+Rover+Sport&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 111, "Rear Visibility." A rearview camera image that does not display while in reverse decreases the driver's visibility, increasing the risk of injury or crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the rearview camera and add a new seal or replace the camera, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V364000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Land+Rover&model=Range+Rover+Sport&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 111, "Rear Visibility." A rearview camera image that does not display can decrease the driver's visibility, increasing the risk of injury or crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the rearview camera for damage. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V248000: Electrical System:Propulsion System:Dc/Dc Converter](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Land+Rover&model=Range+Rover+Sport&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** A loss of drive power increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2020 Land Rover Range Rover Sport is an undeniably luxurious and capable vehicle, but used buyers must weigh its appeal against a history of electrical and powertrain complaints. Key issues involving auxiliary battery failures, 48-volt converter shutdowns, and coolant leaks require careful pre-purchase inspections. If you purchase one, ensure a comprehensive service history is available and budget accordingly for potential maintenance.
