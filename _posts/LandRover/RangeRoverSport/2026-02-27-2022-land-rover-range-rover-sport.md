# 2022 Land Rover Range Rover Sport Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2022 Land Rover Range Rover Sport, prospective buyers should carefully review owner-reported complaints regarding early model-year electrical and engine cooling concerns. While this luxury SUV offers high-end performance, advanced technology, and distinctive styling, electrical charging faults and coolant leaks have surfaced in owner feedback. Prospective owners should verify maintenance records and ensure all necessary warranty or dealer servicing has been completed.

---

## Engine

### 3.0L Turbocharged Inline-6 MHEV

The 3.0-liter turbocharged mild-hybrid inline-6 engine serves as a core powerplant for the Range Rover Sport, balancing robust power delivery with modern efficiency. Buyers should monitor cooling system integrity and watch for sporadic gasket or starting issues.

**Coolant Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have reported coolant leaks pooling under the vehicle, potentially risking engine overheating if fluid levels drop significantly.
- **Solution:** Inspect cooling lines, gaskets, and connections for leaks; replace damaged hoses or seals as needed.

**Continuously Variable Valve Lift (CVVL) Gasket Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may suddenly fail to start without warning lights illuminating beforehand.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified technician diagnose the starting fault and replace the failed CVVL gasket.

### 5.0L Supercharged V8

The high-performance 5.0-liter supercharged V8 delivers strong acceleration and robust towing capability for enthusiasts. It requires diligent maintenance to ensure long-term reliability of its forced-induction and cooling systems.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

The ZF 8-speed automatic transmission provides smooth, prompt gear shifts matched to standard all-wheel drive. It generally proves dependable, though routine fluid checks are recommended during regular servicing.

## Safety

### Charging and Power System

The advanced electrical architecture powers numerous comfort and safety systems, but early-production glitches involving charging faults and display crashes require careful inspection.

**Electrical System and DC/DC Converter Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may experience sudden warnings such as 'Electrical Fault Detected' or 'Electrical Battery Charging Fault', potentially leading to total loss of power, a drained battery, or sudden vehicle shutdown while driving.
- **Solution:** Inspect the charging system, battery health, and DC/DC converter; replace failing electrical components as diagnosed.

**Infotainment and Backup Camera Screen Freezes**
- **Symptoms:** The central display screen may crash or turn completely black, temporarily disabling the rearview camera image when shifting into reverse.
- **Solution:** Perform software updates or reset the infotainment system at a dealership if display crashes persist.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V668000: Seat Belts:Front:Retractor](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Land+Rover&model=Range+Rover+Sport&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** An unsecured child restraint system can increase the risk of injury during a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the seat belt assemblies as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V523000: Seat Belts:Pretensioner](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Land+Rover&model=Range+Rover+Sport&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** Improperly restrained occupants have an increased risk of injury during a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the front seat belt assemblies, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V044000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Engine:Oil/Lubrication](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Land+Rover&model=Range+Rover+Sport&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** An oil leak in the presence of an ignition source can increase the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the engine cam carrier and related components as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V023000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Land+Rover&model=Range+Rover+Sport&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 111, "Rear Visibility." A rearview camera image that does not display while in reverse decreases the driver's visibility, increasing the risk of injury or crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the rearview camera and add a new seal or replace the camera, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V364000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Land+Rover&model=Range+Rover+Sport&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 111, "Rear Visibility." A rearview camera image that does not display can decrease the driver's visibility, increasing the risk of injury or crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the rearview camera for damage. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V248000: Electrical System:Propulsion System:Dc/Dc Converter](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Land+Rover&model=Range+Rover+Sport&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** A loss of drive power increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2022 Land Rover Range Rover Sport is an appealing luxury utility vehicle blending comfort with strong performance options. However, used buyers should exercise caution regarding potential electrical charging system faults and engine coolant leaks reported by owners. Prioritizing a pre-purchase inspection by a specialist and verifying that all software and mechanical updates have been applied will help mitigate unexpected ownership hurdles.
