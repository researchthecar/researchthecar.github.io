# 2018 Land Rover Range Rover Sport Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2018 Land Rover Range Rover Sport blends luxurious interior appointments with exceptional capability and a commanding presence. Used-car buyers looking at this model will find a range of sophisticated powertrain options, though luxury European utility vehicles of this era demand careful attention to maintenance history and electronic complexity.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4 PHEV (P400e)

A 2.0-liter turbocharged plug-in hybrid four-cylinder engine generating around 398 horsepower combined, offering electric efficiency paired with traditional utility performance. Reliability requires diligent maintenance of both the hybrid battery systems and forced-induction components.

### 3.0L Supercharged V6

A 3.0-liter supercharged V6 engine producing between 340 and 380 horsepower, delivering strong acceleration across all rev ranges. It is a widely used powerplant in the lineup, though routine checks for coolant leaks and supercharger snout wear are advised.

### 3.0L Turbo Diesel V6

A 3.0-liter turbocharged diesel V6 providing roughly 254 horsepower and robust low-end torque well-suited for towing. Buyers should ensure regular servicing of emissions equipment and fuel systems to maintain long-term dependability.

### 5.0L Supercharged V8

A high-performance 5.0-liter supercharged V8 engine pushing out 518 to 575 horsepower for dynamic, rapid acceleration. While thrilling, this powerplant requires meticulous service records and premium fuel to prevent timing chain and cooling complications.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed ZF Automatic Transmission

An 8-speed automatic transmission paired across all engine options, delivering smooth, responsive shifts and optimized fuel economy. This unit is generally robust, though periodic fluid changes support longevity in high-torque applications.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V625000: Forward Collision Avoidance: Automatic Emergency Braking](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Land+Rover&model=Range+Rover+Sport&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** The Autonomous Emergency Braking (AEB) system may be disabled without warning the driver that the system is not functional. If the AEB does not intervene when needed, without warning, there would be an increased risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Land Rover will notify owners, and dealers will update the vehicle software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V603000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Land+Rover&model=Range+Rover+Sport&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** Failure to display the rearview image will reduce the driver's visibility, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Land Rover will notify owners, and dealers will reprogram the infotainment system software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V117000: Exterior Lighting:Lighting Control Module:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Land+Rover&model=Range+Rover+Sport&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 108, "Lamps, Reflective Devices, and Associated Equipment." The driver may be unaware that the high beams are illuminated, reducing visibility for oncoming traffic, and increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Land Rover will notify owners, and dealers will update the vehicle software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V023000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Land+Rover&model=Range+Rover+Sport&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 111, "Rear Visibility." A rearview camera image that does not display while in reverse decreases the driver's visibility, increasing the risk of injury or crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the rearview camera and add a new seal or replace the camera, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V364000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Land+Rover&model=Range+Rover+Sport&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 111, "Rear Visibility." A rearview camera image that does not display can decrease the driver's visibility, increasing the risk of injury or crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the rearview camera for damage. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.
