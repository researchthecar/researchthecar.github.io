The 2014 Land Rover Range Rover Sport is a luxury SUV that offers a blend of performance, off-road capability, and opulent features. Engine options include a supercharged 3.0L V6 producing 340 horsepower and 332 lb-ft of torque, a supercharged 5.0L V8 available in two outputs (510 hp/461 lb-ft and later models 550hp/502 lb-ft), and a diesel 3.0L V6 (254 hp/443 lb-ft). All engines are paired with an 8-speed automatic transmission. Owners appreciate the Range Rover Sport for its refined interior, powerful engine options, and impressive off-road prowess. However, it's also criticized for its reliability issues, high maintenance costs, and complex electronics.

**Engine**

**Supercharged 3.0L V6**

*   **Timing Chain Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Whining noise from the engine, engine misfires, check engine light illuminated. Often occurs at higher mileage.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the timing chains, tensioners, and guides.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Water Pump Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaks, overheating, engine warning lights.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the water pump.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Supercharged 5.0L V8**

*   **Timing Chain Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Similar to the V6, whining noise, misfires, check engine light.  Can lead to significant engine damage if not addressed.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the timing chains, tensioners, and guides.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,500 - $5,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found
*   **Supercharger Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: A loud whine, loss of power, and potentially an illuminated check engine light. The supercharger snout bearing can fail, or the supercharger itself may require rebuilding.
    * Solution: Replacement of the supercharger snout or rebuilding the supercharger.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $4,000+
    * Recalls: None Found
*   **Coolant Leaks (Coolant Hoses and Pipes)**
    * Description/Symptoms: The vehicles 5.0L SC Engine can develop coolant leaks from the hoses and pipes, and cause the vehicle to overheat.
    * Solution: Replace leaking coolant hoses and pipes
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Diesel 3.0L V6**

* **Turbocharger Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, excessive black smoke from the exhaust, loud whining or screeching noises.
    * Solution: Turbocharger replacement.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,500 - $5,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**ZF 8HP70 8-Speed Automatic Transmission (All Engine Options)**

*   **Valve Body Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Harsh shifting, slipping between gears, delayed engagement, transmission fault warnings. Can also cause the transmission to go into limp mode.
    *   Solution: Replacement or rebuild of the valve body, fluid flush, and software update.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Torque Converter Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Shuddering or vibrations at low speeds, especially during acceleration. Can also cause stalling or difficulty shifting.
    *   Solution: Torque converter replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Air Suspension Compressor Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Vehicle leaning to one side, suspension warning lights, inability to adjust ride height, loud compressor noises.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the air suspension compressor and potentially the air struts.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $3,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found
*   **Air Strut Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Vehicle leaning to one side, hissing sounds from the suspension, rough ride.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the leaking air strut(s).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $2,000+ per strut
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Malfunctions**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Touchscreen freezing, unresponsive buttons, navigation errors, Bluetooth connectivity problems, system reboots.
    *   Solution: Software updates, system resets, or replacement of the infotainment unit.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Battery Drain Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Dead battery overnight, difficulty starting, electrical system malfunctions.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis of parasitic drain, replacement of faulty modules, or battery replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Tailgate Actuator Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Tailgate not opening or closing properly, unusual noises from the tailgate mechanism.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the tailgate actuator.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,200+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Sunroof Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: Sunroof not opening/closing properly, water leaks, or unusual noises when operating.
    * Solution: Repair or replacement of the sunroof motor, track, or seals.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Heated Seat Malfunctions**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Heated seats not working, overheating, or only working intermittently.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the heating element or control module.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+ per seat
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Navigation Screen Blacking Out**
    * Description/Symptoms: Navigation screen going completely black and losing all functionality while driving.
    * Solution: Replacement of the screen or the entire head unit.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $2000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

The 2014 Land Rover Range Rover Sport is a desirable vehicle with many appealing features, but it is plagued by several reliability issues that can lead to costly repairs. Engine issues, particularly timing chain problems on both the V6 and V8 engines, and valve body issues within the transmission are common. Air suspension problems also contribute to the overall cost of ownership. While some issues are relatively minor, others can lead to significant expenses, potentially making this vehicle a less reliable choice for those seeking a low-maintenance SUV.
