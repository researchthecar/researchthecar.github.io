**Introduction:**

The 2023 Land Rover Range Rover Sport is a luxurious SUV that boasts impressive off-road capabilities. It is powered by a range of engine options, including a 3.0-liter inline-six diesel, a 4.4-liter V8 gasoline, and a 3.0-liter inline-six hybrid. The vehicle is paired with either an eight-speed automatic or a nine-speed automatic transmission. Despite its rugged exterior and reputation for durability, the Range Rover Sport has faced some reported issues.

**Engine**

**3.0-Liter Inline-Six Diesel**

**Engine Oil Leak:**
Description/Symptoms: Leakage of engine oil is observed from various points in the engine. This may result in a drop in oil levels and potential engine damage if left unattended.
Solution: Repair or replacement of leaking components, such as gaskets, seals, or oil cooler lines.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+
Recalls: None Found

**4.4-Liter V8 Gasoline**

**Fuel Injector Failure:**
Description/Symptoms: Malfunctioning fuel injectors can cause engine misfires, reduced performance, and increased fuel consumption. Vehicle may enter limp mode in severe cases.
Solution: Replacement of faulty fuel injectors.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,500+
Recalls: NHTSA Campaign Number 22V-659

**3.0-Liter Inline-Six Hybrid**

**Battery Pack Degradation:**
Description/Symptoms: Prolonged use or extreme temperatures can result in reduced battery capacity, affecting hybrid functionality and overall performance.
Solution: Replacement or reconditioning of the battery pack.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $7,000 - $15,000+
Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**Eight-Speed Automatic**

**Transmission Slippage:**
Description/Symptoms: The transmission may fail to shift correctly, resulting in loss of power or jerky gear changes.
Solution: Repair or replacement of the transmission clutch packs, valve body, or solenoids.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $3,000 - $5,000+
Recalls: None Found

**Nine-Speed Automatic**

**Transmission Fluid Leak:**
Description/Symptoms: Leakage of transmission fluid can cause reduced fluid levels, leading to overheating and premature transmission wear.
Solution: Repair or replacement of leaking components, such as gaskets or seals.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

**Air Suspension Failure:**
Description/Symptoms: Malfunctioning air suspension system may result in uneven ride height, reduced handling, and potential tire damage.
Solution: Repair or replacement of air suspension components, such as air springs, compressors, or control modules.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+
Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

**Battery Drain:**
Description/Symptoms: Excessive battery drain is observed when the vehicle is parked and not in use. This can lead to a dead battery and difficulty starting the vehicle.
Solution: Diagnosis and repair of electrical system issues, such as parasitic drains or faulty components.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

**Body Panel Gaps:**
Description/Symptoms: Uneven or excessive gaps between body panels can occur during assembly.
Solution: Adjustment or replacement of affected panels.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

**Climate Control Malfunction:**
Description/Symptoms: Failure of the climate control system, leading to improper heating or cooling.
Solution: Repair or replacement of faulty components, such as the control module, sensors, or actuators.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+
Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

The 2023 Land Rover Range Rover Sport has faced some reported issues that vary in severity and repair cost. While some issues are relatively minor, others can be more expensive and affect the vehicle's performance or reliability. Prospective buyers should be aware of these potential issues and factor them into their decision-making process, considering the high cost of repairs for certain components.
