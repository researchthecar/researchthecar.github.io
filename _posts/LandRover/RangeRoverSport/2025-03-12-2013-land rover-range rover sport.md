The 2013 Land Rover Range Rover Sport is a luxury SUV known for its off-road capabilities and refined interior. It offered a blend of performance and comfort that appealed to buyers looking for a versatile vehicle. However, like many vehicles, it had its share of problems. Available engine options included a 5.0L naturally aspirated V8 producing 375 horsepower and 375 lb-ft of torque, and a supercharged 5.0L V8 producing 510 horsepower and 461 lb-ft of torque. Both engines were paired with a ZF 6HP28 6-speed automatic transmission. Owners often praised the vehicle's luxurious interior, off-road prowess, and powerful engine options. Common complaints included frequent maintenance needs, electrical issues, and relatively poor fuel economy.

**Engine**

**5.0L Naturally Aspirated V8**

*   **Timing Chain Tensioner Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rattling noise, especially on startup. Can lead to severe engine damage if not addressed.
    *   Solution: Replacement of timing chain tensioners, guides, and chains.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**5.0L Supercharged V8**

*   **Supercharger Snout Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Squealing or grinding noise from the supercharger. Loss of power.
    *   Solution: Supercharger rebuild or replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $5,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found
*   **Coolant Hose Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Coolant loss, overheating.
    *   Solution: Replacement of coolant hoses.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found
*   **Timing Chain Tensioner Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rattling noise, especially on startup. Can lead to severe engine damage if not addressed.
    *   Solution: Replacement of timing chain tensioners, guides, and chains.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**ZF 6HP28 6-Speed Automatic**

*   **Valve Body Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Harsh shifting, slipping, delayed engagement.
    *   Solution: Valve body replacement or rebuild.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found
*   **Torque Converter Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Shuddering, stalling, transmission slipping.
    *   Solution: Torque converter replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Air Suspension Compressor Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Vehicle sagging, especially overnight. "Suspension Fault" message on the dashboard.
    *   Solution: Replacement of air suspension compressor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found
*   **Air Strut Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Vehicle sagging, hissing sound from struts.
    *   Solution: Air strut replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $2,000+ per strut.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Battery Drain Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Dead battery, frequent jump starts.
    *   Solution: Diagnosing parasitic drain, replacing faulty components (e.g., infotainment system, modules).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+ depending on the source of the drain.
    *   Recalls: None Found
*   **Infotainment System Malfunctions**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Freezing, crashing, unresponsive touchscreen.
    *   Solution: Software update, replacement of infotainment unit.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $2,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Tail Light Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: LED failure or entire tail light failure.
    *   Solution: Tail light assembly replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+ per tail light.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Sunroof Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Sunroof failing to open or close properly, getting stuck, or leaking.
    *   Solution: Sunroof motor replacement, track repair, or replacement of the entire sunroof assembly.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $2,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2013 Land Rover Range Rover Sport, while luxurious and capable, is prone to several potentially expensive issues. Engine problems like timing chain tensioner and supercharger failures on the V8 engines can be costly. The 6-speed automatic transmission can suffer from valve body and torque converter issues. The air suspension system is known for compressor and strut failures. Electrical problems, especially related to battery drain and the infotainment system, are also common. Interior issues such as sunroof malfunctions can also occur. Overall, the severity of these issues ranges from moderate to severe, with repair costs potentially running into several thousands of dollars. Prospective buyers should be aware of these potential problems and factor them into their ownership considerations.
