The 2024 Land Rover Range Rover Sport is a luxury SUV known for its blend of off-road capability and on-road comfort. It offers a variety of engine options, including a 3.0L Inline-6 Mild Hybrid (355-395 hp and 369-406 lb-ft of torque), a 4.4L Twin-Turbo V8 (523 hp and 553 lb-ft of torque), and a Plug-in Hybrid (PHEV) option combining a 3.0L inline-6 with an electric motor (434 hp combined). All engines are paired with an 8-speed automatic transmission. Owners generally appreciate the Range Rover Sport's stylish design, luxurious interior, and impressive off-road prowess. However, reliability and electronic gremlins are frequent criticisms.

**Engine**

**3.0L Inline-6 Mild Hybrid**

*   **Engine Misfires**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Occasional engine misfires, rough idling, and a noticeable decrease in performance. The check engine light may illuminate.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis with an OBD-II scanner to pinpoint the affected cylinder. Replacement of faulty ignition coils or spark plugs. Inspection for vacuum leaks.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $600+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Coolant Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaks from various points in the cooling system, indicated by low coolant levels, overheating, and visible puddles of coolant.
    *   Solution: Replacement of leaking hoses, water pump, or expansion tank. Pressure testing to identify leak sources.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,200+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**4.4L Twin-Turbo V8**

*   **Oil Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks from the valve cover gaskets, rear main seal, or oil pan gasket. Can lead to low oil levels and potential engine damage if not addressed.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the leaking gaskets or seals. Thorough cleaning of the engine block to remove oil residue.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $2,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Turbocharger Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Whining or whistling noises from the turbochargers. Reduced engine power, especially during acceleration. Possible check engine light illumination.
    *   Solution: Inspection and replacement of faulty turbochargers. Checking for boost leaks or other related components.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,500 - $6,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**3.0L Inline-6 Plug-in Hybrid (PHEV)**

*   **Electric Motor Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: A complete loss of electric motor functionality.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the electric motor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $5,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**8-Speed Automatic Transmission**

*   **Rough Shifting**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Harsh or jerky shifts between gears, especially during acceleration or deceleration. Hesitation when shifting into drive or reverse.
    *   Solution: Transmission fluid flush and fill with manufacturer-recommended fluid. Inspection of valve body and solenoids. If the problem persists, a transmission rebuild or replacement may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $5,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Transmission Slipping**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The engine revs up without a corresponding increase in vehicle speed. The transmission may feel like it's briefly "slipping" out of gear.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis to determine the cause of the slipping. Replacement of worn clutches or other internal components. In severe cases, a transmission rebuild or replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $7,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Air Suspension Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The vehicle sits unevenly, or the air suspension fails to raise or lower. Warning lights illuminate on the dashboard.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis of the air suspension system, including the air compressor, air struts, and ride height sensors. Replacement of faulty components.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $3,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Knocking Noises**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Knocking or clunking noises coming from the suspension, especially when going over bumps or uneven surfaces.
    *   Solution: Inspection of suspension components, including ball joints, control arm bushings, and sway bar links. Replacement of worn or damaged parts.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The infotainment screen freezes, becomes unresponsive, or glitches frequently. Bluetooth connectivity problems. Navigation system errors.
    *   Solution: Software update for the infotainment system. Replacement of faulty touchscreen or other infotainment components.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 (Software update) - $2,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Sensor Malfunctions**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Faulty readings from various sensors, such as the blind-spot monitoring system, parking sensors, or tire pressure monitoring system (TPMS).
    *   Solution: Diagnosis of the affected sensor and replacement with a new unit. Calibration of the new sensor to ensure proper functionality.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Battery Drain**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The battery drains quickly, especially when the vehicle is not in use. Difficulty starting the engine.
    *   Solution: Test the battery and charging system. Look for parasitic drains and replace the battery.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Door Handle Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The door handles fail to extend or retract properly. Intermittent functionality.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the faulty door handle mechanism.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Sunroof Problems**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The sunroof fails to open or close smoothly. Leaks around the sunroof seal.
    *   Solution: Cleaning and lubrication of the sunroof tracks and seals. Replacement of damaged sunroof components.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $1,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Seat Motor Malfunctions**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The power seats fail to adjust properly. The motors make strange noises or stop working altogether.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the faulty seat motor or associated components.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2024 Land Rover Range Rover Sport, while offering a luxurious experience and impressive capabilities, appears to suffer from a range of potential issues. Engine problems, particularly oil leaks and turbocharger issues in the V8, can be expensive to repair. Electrical gremlins, especially with the infotainment system, and suspension problems, especially with the air suspension, are also common complaints. The severity of these issues ranges from minor annoyances to potentially major repairs, with costs spanning from a few hundred to several thousand dollars. Potential buyers should be aware of these potential problems and factor them into their purchasing decision and budget for future maintenance.
