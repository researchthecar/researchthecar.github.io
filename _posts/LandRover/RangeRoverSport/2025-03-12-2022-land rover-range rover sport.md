The 2022 Land Rover Range Rover Sport is a luxury SUV known for its off-road capability, refined interior, and powerful engine options. Engine choices range from a mild-hybrid inline-six to a potent supercharged V8 and even a plug-in hybrid option. For example, the P360 powertrain features a 3.0-liter inline-six mild-hybrid engine producing 355 horsepower and 369 lb-ft of torque. Owners appreciate the Range Rover Sport's luxurious appointments, comfortable ride, and iconic design. However, potential downsides include a reputation for reliability issues, complex electronics, and higher maintenance costs compared to some competitors.

**Engine**

**3.0L Inline-Six Mild-Hybrid (P360)**

*   **Engine Misfires**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rough idling, hesitation upon acceleration, check engine light illuminated. May be caused by faulty spark plugs, ignition coils, or fuel injectors.
    *   Solution: Diagnose the specific cause of the misfire, replace the faulty component (spark plugs, ignition coil, fuel injector), and clear the engine code.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+ depending on the affected component and labor rates.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**5.0L Supercharged V8 (P525/P575)**

*   **Coolant Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Low coolant level, overheating, coolant smell, visible leaks under the vehicle.  Common leak points include the water pump, radiator, and coolant hoses.
    *   Solution: Identify the source of the leak, replace the faulty component (water pump, radiator, hose), and refill the cooling system.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1500+ depending on the affected component and labor rates.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Supercharger Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Whining noise from the engine bay, reduced power, check engine light.  May indicate a failing supercharger bearing or other internal damage.
    *   Solution: Diagnose the supercharger and repair or replace it.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $2000 - $6000+ for supercharger replacement depending on parts and labor.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**ZF 8HP Automatic Transmission (All Engines)**

*   **Harsh Shifting/Hesitation**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Abrupt or jerky gear changes, hesitation when accelerating, especially at lower speeds.  Could be related to the valve body or software issues.
    *   Solution: Perform a transmission fluid flush and filter replacement. If the problem persists, further diagnostics may be needed, potentially requiring valve body repair or replacement, or software update.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $2500+ depending on the complexity of the repair.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Air Suspension Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Vehicle sagging on one or more corners, "Suspension Fault" warning light, inability to adjust ride height, compressor not working. This is a recurring issue across multiple Land Rover models.
    *   Solution: Diagnose the faulty component (air strut, compressor, valve block, air line) and replace it. It's also advisable to inspect the system for leaks.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $3000+ per air strut, $500 - $1500+ for compressor replacement, depending on parts and labor.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Freezing, lagging, screen blackouts, unresponsive touch controls, Bluetooth connectivity problems, navigation errors.
    *   Solution: Attempt a system reset. If the issue persists, a software update or hardware replacement may be required.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 for software update (if available), $500 - $2000+ for hardware replacement depending on parts and labor.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Battery Drain**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Dead battery after short periods of inactivity, difficulty starting the vehicle, multiple warning lights illuminated.  Can be caused by parasitic draw from various electronic modules.
    *   Solution: Diagnose the source of the parasitic draw, repair or replace the faulty component, and ensure the battery is in good condition.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+ depending on the cause and necessary repairs.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Sensor Malfunctions (Parking Sensors, Blind Spot Monitoring)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: False alerts, system not functioning correctly, warning messages on the dashboard.
    *   Solution: Inspect and clean the sensors. If the problem persists, the sensor may need to be replaced.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $500+ per sensor replacement depending on parts and labor.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Sunroof/Moonroof Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Sunroof not opening or closing properly, excessive noise during operation, leaks.
    *   Solution: Inspect the sunroof tracks for debris and lubricate them. If the problem persists, the motor or mechanism may need to be repaired or replaced.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1000+ depending on the complexity of the repair.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2022 Land Rover Range Rover Sport, while offering luxury and capability, can be prone to several issues. The air suspension and infotainment system problems are recurring themes, and the V8 engine can suffer from coolant leaks and supercharger issues. The severity of these issues ranges from minor inconveniences to potentially expensive repairs. Prospective buyers should be aware of these potential problems and factor them into their ownership considerations, budgeting for potentially higher maintenance costs.
