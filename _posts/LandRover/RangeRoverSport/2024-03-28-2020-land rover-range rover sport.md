**Introduction**

The 2020 Land Rover Range Rover Sport is a luxury SUV that combines off-road capability with on-road luxury. It is available with a variety of engine and transmission options, including a 3.0L turbocharged V6, a 5.0L supercharged V8, and a 4.4L twin-turbocharged V8. All models come standard with an eight-speed automatic transmission.

Owners of the 2020 Range Rover Sport generally praise the vehicle for its comfortable interior, powerful engines, and off-road capability. However, some owners have reported experiencing various issues with the vehicle.

**Engine**

* **3.0L Turbocharged V6**
    * **Intake Valve Carbon Buildup**
        * Description/Symptoms: Buildup of carbon deposits on the intake valves can reduce engine power and fuel economy. Symptoms may include hesitation or stumbling when accelerating, and a loss of power at high RPMs.
        * Solution: The intake valves must be cleaned to remove the carbon buildup.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
* **5.0L Supercharged V8**
    * **Supercharger Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: The supercharger may fail, causing a loss of power and a loud noise from the engine.
        * Solution: The supercharger must be replaced.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $3,000 - $5,000
* **4.4L Twin-Turbocharged V8**
    * **No Issues Found**

**Transmission**

* **Eight-Speed Automatic Transmission**
    * **Shifting Problems**
        * Description/Symptoms: The transmission may have difficulty shifting gears, or may slip or shudder during gear changes.
        * Solution: The transmission fluid may need to be flushed and replaced, or the transmission may need to be rebuilt.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $3,000

**Suspension**

* **Air Suspension Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The air suspension may fail, causing the vehicle to sag or ride unevenly.
    * Solution: The air suspension components must be inspected and replaced as necessary.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $3,000+
    * Recalls: NHTSA Campaign Number 20V480000

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain**
    * Description/Symptoms: The battery may drain quickly, even when the vehicle is not in use.
    * Solution: The electrical system must be inspected for a parasitic draw.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
* **Infotainment System Malfunctions**
    * Description/Symptoms: The infotainment system may experience crashes, freezes, or other malfunctions.
    * Solution: The infotainment system may need to be updated or replaced.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500

**Exterior**

* **Sunroof Leaks**
    * Description/Symptoms: The sunroof may leak water into the interior of the vehicle.
    * Solution: The sunroof seals must be inspected and replaced as necessary.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
    * Recalls: NHTSA Campaign Number 20V479000

**Interior**

* **Climate Control Malfunctions**
    * Description/Symptoms: The climate control system may not function properly, causing the vehicle to be too hot or too cold.
    * Solution: The climate control components must be inspected and replaced as necessary.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500
* **Navigation System Errors**
    * Description/Symptoms: The navigation system may provide incorrect directions or experience other errors.
    * Solution: The navigation system may need to be updated or replaced.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500

**Summary**

The 2020 Land Rover Range Rover Sport is a luxury SUV with a variety of engine and transmission options. While some owners are very satisfied with their vehicles, others have reported experiencing a number of issues. The severity and cost of the issues vary depending on the specific problem.
