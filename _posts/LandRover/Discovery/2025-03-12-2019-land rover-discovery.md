The 2019 Land Rover Discovery is a luxury SUV known for its off-road capabilities, spacious interior, and sophisticated styling. Engine options for the 2019 model year include a 3.0-liter Supercharged V6 gasoline engine producing 340 horsepower and 332 lb-ft of torque, and a 3.0-liter Turbocharged Diesel V6 engine producing 254 horsepower and 443 lb-ft of torque. Both engines are paired with an 8-speed automatic transmission. Owners often praise the Discovery for its comfortable ride, versatile cargo space, and advanced technology features. Common criticisms include its reliability record, higher running costs, and complex electronics that can be prone to glitches.

**Engine**

**3.0L Supercharged V6 Gasoline**

*   **Supercharger Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Whining noise from the engine, loss of power, check engine light.
    *   Solution: Replace the supercharger.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $3,000 - $5,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Coolant Leak**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Coolant level dropping, overheating, sweet smell from the engine bay, visible coolant leak. This is often associated with the plastic coolant pipes that can become brittle over time.
    *   Solution: Replacing the affected coolant hoses and potentially other cooling system components.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**3.0L Turbocharged Diesel V6**

*   **EGR Valve Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Check engine light, poor performance, excessive black smoke, rough idling.
    *   Solution: Replace the EGR valve.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Turbocharger Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Whining noise, loss of power, excessive smoke from the exhaust, check engine light.
    *   Solution: Inspect the turbocharger for damage, replace if necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,500 - $4,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**8-Speed Automatic (ZF 8HP70)**

*   **Rough Shifting/Hesitation**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Harsh or delayed shifts, particularly between certain gears.
    *   Solution: Software update of the transmission control module (TCM). In some cases, a transmission fluid flush and filter change can improve symptoms. In severe cases, valve body replacement or complete transmission rebuild may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 (Software Update) - $5,000+ (Transmission Rebuild)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Torque Converter Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Shuddering during acceleration, unusual noises from the transmission, difficulty shifting.
    *   Solution: Replace the torque converter.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Air Suspension Compressor Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Vehicle not leveling properly, suspension warning lights on the dashboard, vehicle sitting low or unevenly.
    *   Solution: Replace the air suspension compressor. Also, inspect and replace any leaking air springs.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,200 - $2,500+ (Compressor Replacement), $800-$1500 per corner (Air Spring Replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Air Spring Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Vehicle sitting low or unevenly, hissing sound from the suspension, suspension warning lights.
    *   Solution: Replace the leaking air spring.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: System freezing, unresponsive touchscreen, Bluetooth connectivity problems, navigation errors.
    *   Solution: Software update, system reset, or replacement of the infotainment unit.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 (Software Update) - $2,000+ (Unit Replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Battery Drain**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Car battery draining overnight or within a short period, difficulty starting the vehicle, various electrical systems malfunctioning.
    *   Solution: Diagnose the parasitic draw and repair the faulty component (e.g., faulty module, improperly shut off system). Replace the battery if necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $1,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Sensor Malfunctions**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Various warning lights on the dashboard (e.g., ABS, TPMS, parking sensors), inaccurate readings from sensors.
    *   Solution: Diagnose the faulty sensor and replace it.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+ per sensor, depending on location and type.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Tailgate Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Tailgate not opening or closing properly, malfunctioning sensors preventing full operation, hydraulic pump failure.
    *   Solution: Replace faulty sensors, repair or replace the hydraulic pump, adjust tailgate alignment.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Panoramic Roof Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Water leaks into the cabin, particularly around the edges of the panoramic sunroof.
    *   Solution: Clean and reseal the sunroof drains, repair or replace the sunroof seal.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Climate Control Problems**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Inconsistent temperature control, air not blowing hot or cold, blower motor malfunction.
    *   Solution: Repair or replace the climate control module, blower motor, or blend door actuator.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,200+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Power Window Malfunctions**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Windows not opening or closing properly, slow operation, clicking noises.
    *   Solution: Replace the window regulator or motor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $250 - $700+ per window
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2019 Land Rover Discovery, while offering a luxurious and capable driving experience, is prone to several issues. Engine problems, particularly with the supercharger and cooling system in the gasoline engine and the EGR valve and turbocharger in the diesel engine, can be costly to repair. Transmission issues, especially rough shifting, may require extensive repairs. Air suspension failures are also a significant concern. Electrical problems, including infotainment glitches and battery drain, are common and can be frustrating. While some issues can be resolved with software updates, others may require substantial mechanical work. Overall, the 2019 Discovery has the potential for high repair costs, making a thorough pre-purchase inspection essential.
