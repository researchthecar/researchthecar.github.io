Here's a comprehensive breakdown of common issues and complaints for the 2021 Land Rover Discovery, formatted for your website. The 2021 Land Rover Discovery is a luxury SUV known for its off-road capability and spacious interior. Engine options for the 2021 model year include a 296-horsepower 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder and a 355-horsepower 3.0-liter inline-six cylinder mild-hybrid. Both engines are paired with an 8-speed automatic transmission. Owners often praise the Discovery's refined ride, comfortable seats, and distinctive styling. However, common criticisms include the infotainment system's complexity and reliability concerns.

**Engine**

**2.0L Turbocharged Four-Cylinder**

*   **Turbocharger Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Whining noise from the engine, loss of power, check engine light illuminated.
    *   Solution: Turbocharger replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**3.0L Inline-Six Mild-Hybrid**

*   **Mild Hybrid System Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Erratic idle, failure to start, warning lights related to the 48V mild hybrid system.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis and repair/replacement of faulty mild-hybrid components (starter/generator, battery, control module).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $4,000+ (depending on component)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**8-Speed Automatic (ZF 8HP)**

*   **Rough Shifting/Hesitation**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Harsh shifts, especially between 2nd and 3rd gear, hesitation when accelerating, occasional clunking noises.
    *   Solution: Software update, transmission fluid flush, valve body replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 (software update) - $2,500+ (valve body)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Air Suspension Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Vehicle leaning to one side, suspension not raising or lowering, warning lights indicating suspension fault.
    *   Solution: Diagnose and replace faulty air struts, air compressor, or ride height sensors. Leaks in the air lines are common.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $3,000+ (depending on the failed component)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Malfunctions**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Freezing, slow response, screen going black, Bluetooth connectivity issues, navigation problems.
    *   Solution: Software update, replacement of the infotainment unit.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 (software update) - $1,500+ (unit replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found
*   **Battery Drain**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Vehicle failing to start after sitting for a short period, frequent jump starts required, warning messages related to low battery voltage.
    *   Solution: Diagnose and replace the faulty battery or parasitic draw. Parasitic draws may be caused by a faulty module not shutting down.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+ (depending on the cause)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Sunroof/Moonroof Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Failure to open or close properly, water leaks, unusual noises during operation.
    *   Solution: Repair or replacement of the sunroof motor, track assembly, or seals.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Dashboard Screen Delamination**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The protective film on the infotainment screen or instrument cluster begins to peel or bubble.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the screen.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found
*   **Climate Control Problems**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Inconsistent temperature regulation, failure to switch between modes (heat/AC/defrost), blower motor malfunction.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis and repair/replacement of the climate control module, blend door actuator, or blower motor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2021 Land Rover Discovery, while offering a blend of luxury and capability, does suffer from a range of potential issues. Electrical problems (particularly with the infotainment system) and suspension malfunctions are fairly common, potentially leading to moderate to high repair costs. Engine and transmission issues, while less frequent, can result in very expensive repairs. Potential buyers should carefully inspect the vehicle's service history, test all electronic features thoroughly, and consider obtaining an extended warranty for added peace of mind.
