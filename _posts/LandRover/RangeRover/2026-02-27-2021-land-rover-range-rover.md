# 2021 Land Rover Range Rover Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2021 Land Rover Range Rover delivers luxurious comfort, striking styling, and impressive capability, but prospective used-car buyers should approach with careful inspection. Owners have reported occasional issues relating to the engine cooling system, electrical stability, and suspension components. A thorough pre-purchase inspection by a knowledgeable specialist is strongly recommended before bringing one home.

---

## Engine

### 3.0L Turbocharged Inline-6

This mild-hybrid inline-6 engine features turbocharging to produce strong horsepower and torque for effortless highway passing. While powerful and smooth, the cooling and electrical plumbing require attentive maintenance to avoid overheating.

**Coolant Leaks and Overheating**
- **Symptoms:** Owners note a persistent burning coolant smell, disappearing coolant levels with no visible external puddles, and engine running warm.
- **Solution:** Inspect coolant hoses, water pump, and pressurized fittings for micro-fractures; replace faulty components and refill fluid.

### 5.0L Supercharged V8

The high-performance V8 delivers robust power and deep exhaust notes, making it a favorite for buyers seeking brisk acceleration. It demands strict adherence to service intervals and high-octane fuel.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

This smooth-shifting 8-speed automatic pairs seamlessly with both inline-6 and V8 powerplants, providing prompt gear selection and refined cruising. No widespread mechanical failure clusters have been reported for this gearbox.

## Suspension

### Air Suspension and Active Roll Control

Provides adjustable ride height and plush damping for ultimate ride quality, though pneumatic and hydraulic components can wear over time.

**Hydraulic Stabilization or Suspension Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Abnormal knocking sounds from the wheel wells or failure messages regarding dynamic response and roll control systems.
- **Solution:** Inspect suspension bushings, stabilizer hydraulic lines, and compressor components; replace worn or leaking assemblies.

## Electrical

### Charging and Battery System

Manages power distribution across complex onboard computer networks, accessories, and hybrid charging architecture.

**Battery Drain and DC/DC Converter Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Dashboard illumination of multiple warning lights, sudden battery discharge, and failure of the vehicle to start.
- **Solution:** Test charging system output, replace failed DC/DC converters, and install a fresh battery if damaged by power surges.

## Safety

### Seat Belts

Occupant protection systems designed to secure passengers during impact.

**Seat Belt Latch Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Difficulty latching or catching the seat belt buckle, leading to warning chimes or insecure restraint warnings.
- **Solution:** Inspect the buckle assembly for internal mechanical blockage or wear; replace the latch mechanism if required.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V668000: Seat Belts:Front:Retractor](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Land+Rover&model=Range+Rover&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** An unsecured child restraint system can increase the risk of injury during a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the seat belt assemblies as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V044000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Engine:Oil/Lubrication](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Land+Rover&model=Range+Rover&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** An oil leak in the presence of an ignition source can increase the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the engine cam carrier and related components as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V023000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Land+Rover&model=Range+Rover&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 111, "Rear Visibility." A rearview camera image that does not display while in reverse decreases the driver's visibility, increasing the risk of injury or crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the rearview camera and add a new seal or replace the camera, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V248000: Electrical System:Propulsion System:Dc/Dc Converter](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Land+Rover&model=Range+Rover&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** A loss of drive power increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2021 Land Rover Range Rover offers exceptional luxury and unmatched road presence, but its complex electronics and sophisticated powertrain require vigilance. Buyers should prioritize examples with detailed service history, and verify that all cooling system components and software updates are fully up to date.
