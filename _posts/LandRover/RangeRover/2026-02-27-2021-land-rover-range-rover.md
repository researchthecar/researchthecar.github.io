# 2021 Land Rover Range Rover Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2021 Land Rover Range Rover delivers flagship luxury, robust capability, and a commanding presence, but prospective buyers should approach its complex electronics and powertrains with careful inspection. While standard owner feedback for this model year is relatively sparse compared to older iterations, luxury European SUVs of this vintage demand rigorous maintenance histories. Ensuring all systems function properly during a pre-purchase inspection can save buyers from potentially expensive out-of-pocket repairs.

---

## Engine

### 3.0L Turbocharged Inline-6 MHEV

This mild-hybrid inline-6 engine produces approximately 355 to 395 horsepower, offering a smooth power delivery and modern efficiency. It serves as the base powerplant for standard models, blending traditional Land Rover performance with electrified assistance.

### 5.0L Supercharged V8

The potent 5.0-liter supercharged V8 outputs between 518 and 557 horsepower, delivering effortless acceleration and high-performance capability. While robust, it requires diligent maintenance and premium fuel to ensure long-term dependability.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

This ZF-sourced 8-speed automatic transmission provides seamless gear changes and pairs across all engine options with permanent four-wheel drive. It is generally regarded as a durable unit when serviced according to manufacturer intervals.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V668000: Seat Belts:Front:Retractor](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Land+Rover&model=Range+Rover&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** An unsecured child restraint system can increase the risk of injury during a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the seat belt assemblies as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V044000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Engine:Oil/Lubrication](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Land+Rover&model=Range+Rover&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** An oil leak in the presence of an ignition source can increase the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the engine cam carrier and related components as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V023000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Land+Rover&model=Range+Rover&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 111, "Rear Visibility." A rearview camera image that does not display while in reverse decreases the driver's visibility, increasing the risk of injury or crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the rearview camera and add a new seal or replace the camera, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V248000: Electrical System:Propulsion System:Dc/Dc Converter](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Land+Rover&model=Range+Rover&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** A loss of drive power increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2021 Land Rover Range Rover remains an opulent and capable choice for luxury SUV buyers, provided they prioritize a thorough pre-purchase inspection. Because widespread recurring mechanical complaints are minimal for this specific model year, the primary focus should be on verifying electronic functionality and complete service records. Buyers should budget accordingly for premium maintenance costs typical of high-end British luxury vehicles.
