**Introduction:** The 1996 Land Rover Range Rover, known for its rugged off-road capability and luxurious cabin, is powered by a 4.0L V8 engine mated to a 4-speed automatic transmission. Praised for its iconic design and comfortable interior, it faces some common issues reported by owners.

**Engine**

**4.0L V8**
* **Intake Manifold Gasket Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaks, overheating, rough idling
    * Solution: Replace the intake manifold gaskets
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500-$1,000
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Cracked Cylinder Heads:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Overheating, coolant loss, white exhaust smoke
    * Solution: Replace the cylinder heads
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000-$4,000
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**4-Speed Automatic**
* **Transmission Slipping:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears, loss of power
    * Solution: Overhaul or replace the transmission
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500-$3,000
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Rear Air Suspension System Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Sagging rear end, uneven ride height
    * Solution: Replace the air suspension components (e.g., airbags, compressor)
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000-$2,000
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Loose Suspension Components:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Rattling or clunking noises, unstable handling
    * Solution: Tighten or replace loose suspension components (e.g., bushings, ball joints)
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200-$500
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Failed Headlight Switch:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Headlights not working, intermittent lighting
    * Solution: Replace the headlight switch
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200-$400
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Electrical Short in Instrument Cluster:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Malfunctioning gauges, flickering lights
    * Solution: Repair or replace the instrument cluster
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300-$600
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Rust on Body Panels:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Reddish-brown spots, corrosion
    * Solution: Rust removal and paint repair
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500-$1,500
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Faulty Sunroof:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Leaking, malfunctioning drain tubes
    * Solution: Clean or replace sunroof drains, repair or replace sunroof mechanism
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200-$800
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Brittle Dashboard:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Cracks or warping, especially in hot weather
    * Solution: Replace or repair dashboard
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300-$700
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary:** The 1996 Land Rover Range Rover is a capable and luxurious SUV, but it is not without its issues. The most severe and expensive problems include intake manifold gasket failure, cracked cylinder heads, and transmission slipping. Suspension, electrical, exterior, and interior issues are generally less costly to repair. Overall, the Range Rover's reliability and repair costs should be considered before purchasing one.
