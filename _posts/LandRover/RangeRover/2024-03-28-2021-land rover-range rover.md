**Introduction:**

The 2021 Land Rover Range Rover is a full-size luxury SUV known for its opulent interior, unparalleled off-road capabilities, and powerful engines. It comes standard with a 3.0L V6 turbocharged engine producing 355 horsepower and 365 lb-ft of torque. Other engine options include a 4.4L V8 diesel engine and a 5.0L V8 supercharged engine. Transmission options include an 8-speed automatic and a 6-speed manual. Owners appreciate its spacious cabin, impressive performance, and rugged exterior, but some have encountered issues with its various components.

**Engine**

**3.0L V6 Turbocharged**

* **Fuel Injector Failure:** Fuel injectors can become clogged or malfunction, leading to rough idling, decreased fuel economy, and difficulty starting. Repair involves replacing the faulty injectors. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
* **Turbocharger Wastegate Failure:** The turbocharger wastegate can fail, causing loss of power and increased exhaust smoke. Replacement of the turbocharger or its wastegate assembly is necessary. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+

**4.4L V8 Diesel**

* **EGR Valve Issues:** The exhaust gas recirculation (EGR) valve can malfunction, leading to reduced engine performance and increased emissions. EGR valve replacement is typically required. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,200+

**5.0L V8 Supercharged**

* **Supercharger Overheating:** The supercharger can overheat under certain conditions, causing the engine to lose power. Supercharger cooling system repair or replacement may be necessary. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+

**Transmission**

**8-Speed Automatic**

* **Shifting Problems:** Some owners have reported issues with transmission shifting smoothly, resulting in hesitation, jerking, or slipping. Software updates or transmission parts replacement may be required. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+
* **Fluid Leaks:** Transmission fluid leaks can occur, causing slipping and decreased performance. Repair involves identifying and sealing the leak source. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+

**Suspension**

* **Air Suspension Failure:** The air suspension system can experience leaks or compressor malfunctions, causing the suspension to malfunction or collapse. Replacement of air springs, compressors, or other suspension components may be necessary. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $5,000+

**Electrical**

* **Infotainment System Issues:** Malfunctions in the infotainment system can include screen freezing, connectivity problems, or unresponsive controls. Software updates or hardware replacements may be required. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
* **Battery Drain:** Some owners have experienced rapid battery drainage, especially when the vehicle is not in use. Faulty modules or parasitic draws may be the issue. Repair involves diagnosing and resolving the cause of the drain. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+

**Exterior**

* **Headlight Moisture:** Moisture buildup can occur within the headlight assemblies, leading to condensation and reduced visibility. Replacement of headlight assemblies or resealing may be necessary. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+

**Interior**

* **Panoramic Sunroof Rattle:** Some owners have reported rattling noises coming from the panoramic sunroof, especially at higher speeds. Adjustment or replacement of sunroof components may be necessary. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
* **Creaking Dashboard:** Creaking noises can develop in the dashboard, potentially due to loose or misaligned components. Repair involves disassembling and reassembling the dashboard. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+

**Summary:**

While the 2021 Land Rover Range Rover offers many desirable features, it has also experienced a range of issues across its major components. Engine and transmission problems can be costly to fix, with repairs often exceeding $1,000. Suspension issues can also lead to expensive replacements, while electrical and interior issues can range from minor nuisances to more serious malfunctions. Owners should be aware of these potential problems and consider extended warranties to mitigate repair costs if necessary.
