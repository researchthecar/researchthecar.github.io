# 2017 Land Rover Range Rover

### Overview
The 2017 Land Rover Range Rover, a pinnacle of luxury and off-road capability, generally presents a strong reliability profile for its model year. While its sophisticated systems and powerful engines contribute to its premium experience, like any complex vehicle, certain areas have seen more attention than others. This analysis focuses on documented technical issues beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential concerns for owners and prospective buyers. The focus is on specific components and their reported problems, offering estimated repair costs and solutions where available. Understanding these potential issues can help in proactive maintenance and informed decision-making regarding this high-end SUV.

---


## Engine

### 3.0L Supercharged V6
**Coolant Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Visible coolant puddles under the vehicle, low coolant warning light, engine overheating.
- **Solution:** Replacement of leaking hoses, seals, or water pump.
- **Cost:** $300 - $700

**Supercharger Belt Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of engine power, unusual noises from the engine bay, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the supercharger drive belt.
- **Cost:** $200 - $450


### 5.0L Supercharged V8
**Timing Chain Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Rattling noise from the front of the engine, especially on startup; check engine light with timing codes.
- **Solution:** Replacement of timing chain, tensioners, and guides.
- **Cost:** $1500 - $3000

**Oil Leaks (Valve Covers/Gaskets)**
- **Symptoms:** Visible oil stains around the engine, burning oil smell, low oil level.
- **Solution:** Replacement of valve cover gaskets or other related seals.
- **Cost:** $400 - $900


## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic (ZF 8HP)
**Transmission Fluid Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Leaking fluid from the transmission pan or cooler lines, rough shifting, slipping gears.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of transmission pan gasket, seals, or cooler lines.
- **Cost:** $300 - $800

**Software Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Hesitation during shifts, unexpected gear changes, 'transmission fault' warning.
- **Solution:** Software update or reflash of the transmission control module.
- **Cost:** $150 - $300


## Suspension

### Air Suspension System
**Air Spring Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle sagging on one or more corners, compressor running excessively, 'suspension fault' warning.
- **Solution:** Replacement of leaking air springs or air lines.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1200 per corner

**Compressor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle not raising, compressor not engaging, 'suspension fault' warning.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the air suspension compressor.
- **Cost:** $700 - $1500


## Electrical

### Infotainment System
**Screen Freezing/Unresponsiveness**
- **Symptoms:** Touchscreen becomes unresponsive, system freezes, navigation issues.
- **Solution:** Software update or reset of the infotainment module.
- **Cost:** $100 - $300


### Battery Drain
**Parasitic Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Battery dies unexpectedly, car won't start, warning lights on dash.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and repair of faulty modules or circuits causing drain.
- **Cost:** $200 - $600

### Summary Verdict
The 2017 Land Rover Range Rover offers a generally reliable ownership experience, particularly concerning its exterior and interior components. The primary areas that may require attention are the sophisticated air suspension system, with potential for leaks or compressor issues, and the powerful V6 and V8 engines, which can experience coolant leaks or timing chain wear on the V8. Transmission issues are less common but can involve fluid leaks or software glitches. Electrical concerns, especially with the infotainment system and potential battery drain, are also noted. Proactive maintenance, particularly for the engine and suspension, is recommended to mitigate potential costs and ensure optimal performance of this luxury SUV.
