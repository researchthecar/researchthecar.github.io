The 2001 Land Rover Range Rover (P38A) is a luxury SUV known for its off-road capability and sophisticated design. The 2001 model came standard with a 4.6-liter V8 engine producing approximately 222 horsepower and 300 lb-ft of torque. A 4.0-liter V8 was also available in some markets. Both engines were paired with a 4-speed automatic transmission. Owners often appreciate the Range Rover's commanding presence, luxurious interior, and go-anywhere capability. Common complaints include frequent mechanical issues, high maintenance costs, and complex electrical systems.

**Engine**

**4.6L V8**

*   **Slipped Liner**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Overheating, coolant loss, white smoke from the exhaust, misfires. The engine block liners can slip, causing coolant to enter the cylinders.
    *   Solution: Engine rebuild or replacement with a block that has the liners pinned.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $5,000 - $10,000+

*   **Oil Pump Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Low oil pressure warning, engine knocking, potential for catastrophic engine damage.
    *   Solution: Replace the oil pump and inspect for further engine damage.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1500+

*   **Head Gasket Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Overheating, coolant loss, white smoke from the exhaust, coolant mixing with oil.
    *   Solution: Replace the head gaskets and check the cylinder heads for warping.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+

**4.0L V8**

*   **Slipped Liner**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Overheating, coolant loss, white smoke from the exhaust, misfires. The engine block liners can slip, causing coolant to enter the cylinders.
    *   Solution: Engine rebuild or replacement with a block that has the liners pinned.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $5,000 - $10,000+

*   **Oil Pump Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Low oil pressure warning, engine knocking, potential for catastrophic engine damage.
    *   Solution: Replace the oil pump and inspect for further engine damage.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1500+

*   **Head Gasket Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Overheating, coolant loss, white smoke from the exhaust, coolant mixing with oil.
    *   Solution: Replace the head gaskets and check the cylinder heads for warping.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+

**Transmission**

**4-Speed Automatic (ZF 4HP24)**

*   **Harsh Shifting/Slipping**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rough or delayed gear changes, transmission slipping out of gear, failure to engage gears.
    *   Solution: Transmission rebuild or replacement. Sometimes, a valve body replacement can resolve the issue.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,500 - $5,000+
*   **Torque Converter Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Shuddering or vibration at low speeds, stalling, poor acceleration.
    *   Solution: Replace the torque converter.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,500+

**Suspension**

*   **Air Suspension Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Sagging suspension, uneven ride height, "Suspension Inactive" warning light, compressor failure.
    *   Solution: Replace air springs (bags), compressor, or height sensors. Sometimes, leaks in the air lines are the cause. Many owners convert to coil springs for reliability.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: Air suspension repair: $500 - $3,000+. Coil spring conversion: $1,500 - $3,000+

**Electrical**

*   **Body Electrical Control Module (BECM) Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Intermittent electrical problems, including issues with windows, locks, lights, and alarm system. The BECM controls many of the vehicle's systems.
    *   Solution: Diagnose and repair or replace the BECM. This often requires specialized programming.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,500+
*   **Instrument Cluster Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Inaccurate readings, flickering displays, complete failure of the instrument cluster.
    *   Solution: Repair or replace the instrument cluster.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,500+

* **ABS Module Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: ABS warning light on, reduced braking power, hard braking.
    * Solution: Replacement of the ABS Module.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+

**Exterior**

*   **Sunroof Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Sunroof does not open or close properly, motor failure, jammed mechanism.
    *   Solution: Repair or replace the sunroof motor or mechanism.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,000+

**Interior**

*   **Heater Core Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Coolant smell inside the cabin, wet carpets, poor heater performance.
    *   Solution: Replace the heater core. This is a labor-intensive job.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $3,000+

*   **Blend Motor Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Inability to control temperature properly, one side blowing hot while the other blows cold.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the blend motors.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,000+

**Recalls:**

*   **01V089000** - POWER STEERING HOSE MAY BE DEFECTIVE
*   **00V378000** - FUEL TANK CHECK VALVE MAY LEAK

In summary, the 2001 Land Rover Range Rover is a vehicle plagued by a range of issues, from serious engine problems like slipped liners to electrical gremlins and air suspension failures. The severity of these problems can range from inconvenient to catastrophic, and the cost of repairs can quickly escalate due to the complexity of the vehicle and the specialized knowledge required to work on it. Potential buyers should be aware of these common issues and factor in significant maintenance costs when considering the purchase of a 2001 Range Rover.
