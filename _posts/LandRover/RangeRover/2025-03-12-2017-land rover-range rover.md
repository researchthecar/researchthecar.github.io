The 2017 Land Rover Range Rover is a luxury SUV known for its off-road capability and opulent interior. Engine options for 2017 include a 3.0L Supercharged V6 (340 hp and 332 lb-ft of torque), a 5.0L Supercharged V8 (510 hp and 461 lb-ft of torque), and a 3.0L Turbocharged Diesel V6 (254 hp and 443 lb-ft of torque). All engines are paired with an 8-speed automatic transmission. Owners often praise the Range Rover's luxurious interior, off-road prowess, and powerful engine options. Common dislikes include the high cost of maintenance, reliability concerns, and complex electronic systems.

**Engine**

**3.0L Supercharged V6**

*   **Coolant Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaks, often stemming from plastic coolant pipes or the water pump housing. Can lead to overheating if not addressed.
    *   Solution: Replace the leaking coolant pipes and/or water pump housing. It's generally recommended to replace related gaskets and seals at the same time.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,200+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Supercharger Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Whining noises from the supercharger, reduced engine power. Supercharger coupler can fail.
    *   Solution: Replace the supercharger coupler or, in more severe cases, rebuild or replace the supercharger.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $3,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**5.0L Supercharged V8**

*   **Timing Chain Tensioner Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rattling noise upon startup, potential for engine damage if the timing chain slips.
    *   Solution: Replace the timing chain, tensioners, guides, and related components.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $5,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Coolant Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaks, often stemming from coolant hoses or the water pump housing. Can lead to overheating if not addressed.
    *   Solution: Replace the leaking coolant pipes and/or water pump housing. It's generally recommended to replace related gaskets and seals at the same time.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,200+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**3.0L Turbocharged Diesel V6**

*   **Diesel Particulate Filter (DPF) Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Reduced engine performance, DPF warning light illuminated. DPF can become clogged, especially with frequent short trips.
    *   Solution: Forced DPF regeneration, cleaning, or replacement. Addressing underlying causes of excessive soot production is also critical.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $3,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **EGR Valve Problems**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rough idling, decreased fuel economy, engine stalling.
    *   Solution: Replacement of EGR Valve
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $1,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**ZF 8HP70 8-Speed Automatic (All Engine Options)**

*   **Rough Shifting/Hesitation**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Harsh or delayed shifts, particularly between certain gears. Hesitation when accelerating.
    *   Solution: Software update, transmission fluid flush, or valve body replacement. In severe cases, transmission rebuild or replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $6,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Torque Converter Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Shuddering or vibration at low speeds, especially during light acceleration.
    *   Solution: Torque converter replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Air Suspension Compressor Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Vehicle sagging or uneven ride height. "Suspension Fault" warning on the dashboard.
    *   Solution: Replace the air suspension compressor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $2,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Air Strut Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Vehicle sagging, particularly overnight. Hissing sound from the air struts.
    *   Solution: Replace the leaking air strut(s).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $3,000+ per strut
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Ride Height Sensor Malfunctions**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Incorrect ride height readings, leading to suspension malfunctions and error messages.
    *   Solution: Replace the faulty ride height sensor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Screen freezes, glitches, unresponsive touchscreen, navigation problems, Bluetooth connectivity issues.
    *   Solution: Software update, reset of the system, or replacement of the infotainment unit.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 (software update) - $3,000+ (unit replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Battery Drain**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Dead battery, requiring frequent jump starts.
    *   Solution: Diagnose and repair the source of the parasitic drain (e.g., faulty module, wiring issue).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $1,000+ (depending on the source)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Sensor Malfunctions (Various)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Various warning lights, inaccurate readings (e.g., tire pressure, parking sensors).
    *   Solution: Diagnose and replace the faulty sensor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+ per sensor
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Tailgate Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Tailgate not opening or closing properly, malfunctioning power liftgate mechanism.
    *   Solution: Repair or replace the tailgate motor, struts, or latch mechanism.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Panoramic Sunroof Problems**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Sunroof not opening or closing smoothly, leaks, or unusual noises.
    *   Solution: Lubricate the sunroof tracks, repair or replace the sunroof motor or seals.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $1,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Door Lock Actuator Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Door lock failing to lock or unlock, making it difficult to enter or exit the vehicle.
    *   Solution: Replace faulty door lock actuator.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Heated Seat Problems**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Heated seats not working, or only heating intermittently.
    *   Solution: Repair or replace the heating element or the control module.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2017 Land Rover Range Rover, while luxurious and capable, is known for its reliability issues. Common problems include coolant leaks, air suspension failures, and electrical glitches. Engine issues range from timing chain problems in the V8 to DPF issues in the diesel. Repair costs can be significant, especially for suspension and engine-related problems. Potential buyers should be aware of these issues and factor in the potential for costly repairs when considering this vehicle.
