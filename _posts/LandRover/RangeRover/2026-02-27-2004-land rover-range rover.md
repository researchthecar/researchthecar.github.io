# 2004 Land Rover Range Rover

### Overview
The 2004 Land Rover Range Rover, a luxury SUV known for its off-road prowess and opulent interior, presents a mixed bag in terms of long-term reliability. While its robust construction and powerful engines are commendable, certain model years and specific components are prone to issues that can impact ownership experience and costs. Owners often report a higher than average number of repairs, particularly concerning the advanced electronic systems and air suspension. However, for those who prioritize comfort, capability, and a commanding presence, the Range Rover offers a unique proposition, provided they are prepared for potential maintenance needs.

---


## Engine

### 4.4L V8 (BMW M62)
**Head Gasket Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Coolant loss, white smoke from exhaust, engine overheating, milky oil.
- **Solution:** Replacement of head gaskets and associated seals. Often recommended to replace timing chain guides and tensioners at the same time.
- **Cost:** $2,500 - $4,500

**Cooling System Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Visible coolant leaks, low coolant level, engine overheating.
- **Solution:** Replacement of radiator, hoses, water pump, and thermostat.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1,500

**Oil Leaks (Valve Cover Gaskets, Oil Pan Gasket)**
- **Symptoms:** Visible oil leaks, burning oil smell, low oil level.
- **Solution:** Replacement of leaking gaskets.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1,200


### 4.2L V8 Supercharged (Jaguar AJ34S)
**Supercharger Belt Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of power, whining noise from engine bay, battery warning light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the supercharger belt and tensioner.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600

**Cooling System Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Visible coolant leaks, low coolant level, engine overheating.
- **Solution:** Replacement of radiator, hoses, water pump, and thermostat.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1,500

**Oil Leaks (Valve Cover Gaskets, Oil Pan Gasket)**
- **Symptoms:** Visible oil leaks, burning oil smell, low oil level.
- **Solution:** Replacement of leaking gaskets.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1,200


## Transmission

### ZF 5HP24 (5-Speed Automatic)
**Harsh Shifting / Slipping**
- **Symptoms:** Jerky gear changes, delayed engagement, slipping between gears.
- **Solution:** Transmission fluid and filter change, valve body repair, or full transmission rebuild.
- **Cost:** $1,500 - $4,000

**Torque Converter Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Shuddering at low speeds, slipping, difficulty engaging gears.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the torque converter.
- **Cost:** $1,000 - $2,000


## Suspension

### Air Suspension (EAS)
**Air Spring Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle sagging on one or more corners, compressor running excessively, 'air suspension fault' warning.
- **Solution:** Replacement of leaking air springs or air lines.
- **Cost:** $600 - $1,200 per corner

**Compressor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle not raising, compressor not engaging, 'air suspension fault' warning.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the air compressor unit.
- **Cost:** $700 - $1,300

**Valve Block Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Uneven ride height, air leaks from the valve block, compressor running constantly.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of the EAS valve block.
- **Cost:** $400 - $800


## Electrical

### Body Control Module (BCM)
**Intermittent Electrical Faults**
- **Symptoms:** Malfunctioning lights, power windows, central locking, dashboard warnings, CAN bus errors.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and potential reprogramming or replacement of the BCM.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1,800


### ABS/DSC Module
**ABS/DSC Warning Lights**
- **Symptoms:** ABS and DSC warning lights illuminated on the dashboard, loss of ABS/traction control functionality.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of the ABS/DSC control module.
- **Cost:** $700 - $1,500


### Heater Control Panel
**Non-functional Climate Controls**
- **Symptoms:** Inability to adjust temperature, fan speed, or air direction; display issues.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of the heater control panel.
- **Cost:** $400 - $900


## Exterior

### Body Panels and Trim
**Rust on Body Panels**
- **Symptoms:** Corrosion visible on wheel arches, door bottoms, tailgate, and chassis.
- **Solution:** Rust removal and repair, repainting.
- **Cost:** $500 - $2,000+ (depending on severity)


### Sunroof
**Leaking Sunroof**
- **Symptoms:** Water ingress into the cabin, damp carpets, mold.
- **Solution:** Cleaning and clearing of sunroof drains, seal replacement.
- **Cost:** $200 - $500


## Interior

### Leather Upholstery
**Cracking and Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Deterioration of leather on seats, steering wheel, and armrests.
- **Solution:** Leather cleaning, conditioning, repair, or reupholstery.
- **Cost:** $300 - $1,500+


### Navigation System
**Outdated or Malfunctioning Unit**
- **Symptoms:** Inaccurate maps, unresponsiveness, screen issues.
- **Solution:** Software update, repair, or replacement with an aftermarket unit.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1,500


--- 
### Summary Verdict
The 2004 Land Rover Range Rover is a capable and luxurious SUV, but it is not without its reliability concerns. Owners should be prepared for potential issues, particularly with the air suspension system, electrical components like the BCM and ABS module, and the cooling system for the V8 engines. Regular and proactive maintenance, especially fluid changes and inspections for leaks, is crucial. While the V8 engines offer strong performance, they can be prone to significant repairs like head gasket failures. The ZF automatic transmission can also develop issues if not properly maintained. Despite these potential drawbacks, the Range Rover's off-road capability and comfort remain appealing to its target audience.
