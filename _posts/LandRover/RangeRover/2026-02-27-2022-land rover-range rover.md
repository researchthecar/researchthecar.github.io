# 2022 Land Rover Range Rover

### Overview
The 2022 Land Rover Range Rover, a pinnacle of luxury and off-road capability, generally presents a strong reliability profile for its model year. While sophisticated engineering often comes with complexity, this generation of Range Rover has seen fewer widespread critical failures compared to some predecessors. Owners can expect a premium driving experience, though as with any high-end vehicle, proactive maintenance and awareness of potential electronic nuances are key to long-term satisfaction. The vehicle's advanced systems, from its potent powertrains to its intricate electrical architecture, are designed for performance and comfort, but also represent areas where specialized attention might be required.

## Suspension

### Adaptive Air Suspension
**Air Spring Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle may sag on one corner, suspension warning light illuminated, uneven ride height.
- **Solution:** Replacement of leaking air spring or associated lines.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1500

**Compressor Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Inability to raise or lower the vehicle, constant running of compressor, suspension warning light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the air suspension compressor unit.
- **Cost:** $700 - $1800


## Electrical

### Infotainment System
**Software Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Screen freezing, unresponsive touch controls, intermittent connectivity issues (Bluetooth, Apple CarPlay/Android Auto).
- **Solution:** Software updates, system resets, or potential module replacement.
- **Cost:** $150 - $800


### Advanced Driver-Assistance Systems (ADAS)
**Sensor Calibration Issues**
- **Symptoms:** False warnings from safety systems (e.g., lane departure, adaptive cruise control), system deactivation.
- **Solution:** Recalibration of radar and camera sensors.
- **Cost:** $200 - $500

### Summary Verdict
The 2022 Land Rover Range Rover demonstrates a commendable level of reliability for a luxury SUV with extensive technological features. While the powertrain and drivetrain components are generally robust, potential owners should be aware of the complexities associated with the advanced air suspension system, which can occasionally develop leaks or compressor issues. Electrical gremlins, particularly within the sophisticated infotainment and ADAS, are not uncommon and typically require software updates or recalibration. Overall, the Range Rover offers a premium and capable experience, with most reported issues being manageable and not indicative of systemic failure across the model range. Regular, specialized maintenance is advised.
