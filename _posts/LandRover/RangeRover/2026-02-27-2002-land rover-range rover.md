# 2002 Land Rover Range Rover

### Overview
The 2002 Land Rover Range Rover, a luxury SUV known for its off-road prowess and opulent interior, presented a mixed bag in terms of long-term reliability. While its robust construction and capable powertrain were generally well-regarded, certain model years and specific components were prone to issues that could impact ownership experience and costs. Owners often reported a higher frequency of repairs compared to some competitors, particularly concerning the complex electronic systems and air suspension. Despite these potential drawbacks, the Range Rover remained a desirable vehicle for those prioritizing comfort, capability, and a commanding presence on the road. Regular and diligent maintenance was crucial for mitigating many of the common problems.

---


## Engine

### 4.4L V8 (BMW M62)
**Head Gasket Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Coolant loss, white smoke from exhaust, engine overheating, milky oil.
- **Solution:** Replacement of head gaskets and associated seals. Often recommended to replace head bolts as well.
- **Cost:** $2,500 - $4,500

**Cooling System Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Visible coolant leaks, low coolant level, engine overheating.
- **Solution:** Replacement of radiator, hoses, water pump, and thermostat.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1,200

**Oil Leaks (Valve Cover Gaskets, Oil Pan Gasket)**
- **Symptoms:** Visible oil leaks, burning oil smell, low oil level.
- **Solution:** Replacement of leaking gaskets.
- **Cost:** $300 - $800


## Transmission

### 5-Speed Automatic (ZF 5HP24)
**Harsh Shifting / Slipping**
- **Symptoms:** Delayed engagement, rough gear changes, slipping between gears, transmission warning light.
- **Solution:** Transmission fluid and filter change, solenoid replacement, or full transmission rebuild/replacement.
- **Cost:** $1,500 - $4,000

**Torque Converter Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Shuddering at low speeds, slipping, difficulty accelerating.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the torque converter.
- **Cost:** $1,000 - $2,500


## Suspension

### Air Suspension (EAS)
**Air Spring Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle sagging on one or more corners, compressor running excessively, 'EAS Fault' warning light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of leaking air springs or air lines.
- **Cost:** $400 - $1,000 per corner

**Compressor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle not raising, constant compressor noise, 'EAS Fault' warning light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the air suspension compressor.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1,000

**Height Sensor Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Incorrect vehicle height, erratic suspension behavior, 'EAS Fault' warning light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of faulty height sensors.
- **Cost:** $200 - $500


## Electrical

### Body Control Module (BCM)
**Intermittent Electrical Gremlins**
- **Symptoms:** Malfunctioning power windows, door locks, interior lights, dashboard warning lights illuminating randomly.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and potential reprogramming or replacement of the BCM.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1,500


### ABS/Traction Control Module
**ABS/Traction Control Warning Lights**
- **Symptoms:** ABS and traction control warning lights illuminated on the dashboard, loss of ABS functionality.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and potential replacement of the ABS control module.
- **Cost:** $700 - $1,200


### Heater Control Valve
**HVAC System Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** No heat or AC, inconsistent temperature control, clicking noises from dashboard.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the heater control valve.
- **Cost:** $200 - $400


## Exterior

### Body Panels & Seals
**Rust and Corrosion**
- **Symptoms:** Rust appearing on wheel arches, door bottoms, tailgate, and chassis.
- **Solution:** Rust treatment and repair, repainting, or replacement of affected panels.
- **Cost:** $500 - $2,000+

**Sunroof Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Water ingress into the cabin, damp carpets, headliner staining.
- **Solution:** Cleaning and clearing sunroof drains, replacing seals.
- **Cost:** $150 - $400


## Interior

### Leather Upholstery
**Wear and Cracking**
- **Symptoms:** Cracking, tearing, and excessive wear on leather seats, especially driver's seat.
- **Solution:** Leather repair and conditioning, or replacement of seat covers/sections.
- **Cost:** $300 - $1,000+


### Navigation System (if equipped)
**Outdated Maps / Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** Inaccurate navigation, system freezing or not powering on.
- **Solution:** Software updates (if available), or replacement of the head unit.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1,500


--- 
### Summary Verdict
The 2002 Land Rover Range Rover is a capable and luxurious SUV, but it is not without its reliability concerns. Owners should be prepared for potential issues, particularly with the air suspension system, electrical components like the BCM, and the aging BMW-sourced V8 engine, which can be prone to coolant and oil leaks. Transmission problems and rust can also be significant factors. While the interior and exterior offer premium comfort and style, maintenance and repair costs can be substantial. Prospective buyers should prioritize vehicles with a comprehensive service history and budget for ongoing maintenance and potential repairs to ensure a positive ownership experience.
