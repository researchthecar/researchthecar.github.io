Here is a comprehensive breakdown of common issues and complaints reported by owners of the 2017 Land Rover Discovery Sport, categorized by component and engine/transmission configuration, formatted as requested.

The 2017 Land Rover Discovery Sport is a compact luxury SUV known for its off-road capabilities, stylish design, and available third-row seating. It was offered with a 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine producing 240 horsepower and 250 lb-ft of torque, paired with a 9-speed automatic transmission. While owners often praise its refined interior and distinctive Land Rover styling, common complaints include reliability issues, particularly with the electrical systems and drivetrain, as well as higher-than-average maintenance costs.

**Engine**

**2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4 (Gasoline)**

*   **Timing Chain Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Engine rattling noise, especially during startup. Check engine light illumination. Potential for engine damage if not addressed.
    *   Solution: Replacement of timing chain, tensioners, and guides.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+

*   **Coolant Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Low coolant levels, overheating, visible coolant leaks around engine components (water pump, thermostat housing, hoses).
    *   Solution: Replacement of faulty coolant components (water pump, thermostat, hoses).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,200+

**Transmission**

**9-Speed Automatic (ZF 9HP48)**

*   **Rough Shifting/Hesitation**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Harsh or delayed shifts, particularly between lower gears. Hesitation upon acceleration.
    *   Solution: Software update to the transmission control module (TCM). If the problem persists, valve body replacement or complete transmission replacement may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 (software update) - $4,000+ (transmission replacement)

*   **Torque Converter Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Shuddering or vibration at low speeds, especially during light acceleration. Stall-like behavior when coming to a stop.
    *   Solution: Replacement of torque converter. In some cases, a complete transmission replacement may be recommended.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,500+

**Suspension**

*   **No Issues Found**

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Malfunctions**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Freezing, crashing, slow response, Bluetooth connectivity issues, navigation problems.
    *   Solution: Software updates, replacement of the infotainment unit.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200+ (software update) - $1,500+ (unit replacement)

*   **Battery Drain**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Dead battery after short periods of inactivity, difficulty starting, electrical system malfunctions.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis to identify the parasitic drain (e.g., faulty module, interior lights staying on). Replacement of the battery.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $500+

*   **Sensor Failures (Parking Sensors, Blind Spot Monitoring)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Warning lights, system malfunction messages, failure of sensors to function properly.
    *   Solution: Replacement of faulty sensors.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600+ per sensor

**Exterior**

*   **Panoramic Sunroof Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Cracking, difficulty opening or closing, leaks.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the sunroof assembly.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+

**Interior**

*   **Power Window Failures**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Window not working or slow moving.
    *   Solution: Replacement of faulty window motor or regulator.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200-$500+

**Recalls:**

*   **NHTSA Recall Number:** 18V157000
    *   Recall Description: The affected vehicles have a software error that can cause the passenger air bag to be disabled.

*   **NHTSA Recall Number:** 17V056000
    *   Recall Description: Vehicles do not conform to Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 114, "Theft Protection." The affected vehicles have a Body Control Module (BCM) software error that can cause the anti-theft alarm to be armed, but not triggered.

**Summary**

The 2017 Land Rover Discovery Sport presents a mixed bag. While stylish and capable, potential buyers should be aware of common issues related to the engine, transmission, electrical system, and sunroof. The most concerning are the engine timing chain issues and transmission problems, which can lead to significant repair costs. Electrical glitches and sensor failures, while less expensive, can be frustrating. Overall, prospective owners should have a pre-purchase inspection performed by a qualified mechanic familiar with Land Rover vehicles and be prepared for potentially higher-than-average maintenance expenses.
