The 2020 Land Rover Discovery Sport is a compact luxury SUV known for its off-road capabilities and stylish design. Engine options include a 2.0-liter turbocharged inline-4 Ingenium engine, producing 246 horsepower and 269 lb-ft of torque. Paired with this engine is a 9-speed automatic transmission. Owners often praise the Discovery Sport's comfortable ride, spacious interior (for its class), and distinctive Land Rover styling. However, common criticisms include its fuel economy, reliability concerns, and infotainment system usability. This information aims to provide a comprehensive overview of common issues reported by owners, separated by vehicle components for easier identification and troubleshooting.

**Engine**

**2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4 Ingenium**

*   **Excessive Oil Consumption**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported higher than normal oil consumption requiring frequent top-ups between scheduled oil changes. Symptoms include low oil level warnings and visible exhaust smoke.
    *   Solution: Dealers may perform an oil consumption test to determine the extent of the issue. Repair options range from replacing valve stem seals to piston ring replacement or engine replacement, depending on the severity.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $8,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Coolant Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaks may occur from various locations, including the water pump, thermostat housing, or radiator hoses. Symptoms include low coolant levels, overheating, and visible coolant puddles.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis involves pressure testing the cooling system to pinpoint the source of the leak. Repair typically involves replacing the leaking component.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Timing Chain Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported premature timing chain wear or stretching, leading to engine noise, rough running, and potential engine damage.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the timing chain, tensioners, and guides.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**9-Speed Automatic Transmission**

*   **Rough Shifting/Hesitation**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners report rough or jerky shifting between gears, particularly at lower speeds. Hesitation during acceleration is also reported.
    *   Solution: A transmission software update may resolve some instances of rough shifting. In more severe cases, valve body replacement or complete transmission replacement may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 (Software Update) - $6,000+ (Transmission Replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Transmission Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Complete transmission failure can occur, leaving the vehicle immobile. Symptoms include loud noises, inability to shift, and warning lights.
    *   Solution: Transmission replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $5,000 - $8,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Air Suspension Issues (If Equipped)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: If equipped with air suspension, problems can arise with the air compressor, air struts, or height sensors. Symptoms include the vehicle sitting unevenly, failing to raise or lower, or displaying suspension warning lights.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis involves checking for leaks in the air lines and testing the components. Replacement of the faulty component, such as the compressor or strut, is typically required.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+ per component
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Knocking or Clunking Noises**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Owners have reported knocking or clunking noises coming from the suspension, particularly when driving over bumps. This can be caused by worn sway bar links, ball joints, or control arm bushings.
    *   Solution: Inspection of the suspension components to identify worn parts. Replacement of the faulty components is necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Problems**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Freezing, lagging, and unresponsiveness of the infotainment system are common complaints. Bluetooth connectivity issues and navigation errors are also reported.
    *   Solution: Software updates can sometimes resolve infotainment system glitches. In some cases, replacement of the infotainment unit may be required.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 (Software Update) - $1,500+ (Unit Replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Battery Drain**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have experienced rapid battery drain, particularly when the vehicle is left unattended for extended periods. This can be caused by a parasitic draw from a faulty module or accessory.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis involves identifying the source of the parasitic draw using specialized testing equipment. Repair may involve replacing the faulty module or accessory.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Sensor Failures**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Failures of various sensors, such as the oxygen sensors, mass airflow sensor, or parking sensors, can lead to warning lights and performance issues.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis involves scanning the vehicle's computer for fault codes. Replacement of the faulty sensor is required.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $500+ per sensor
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Tail Light Malfunctions**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported premature failure of LED tail lights, often manifesting as flickering or complete failure of one or more lights.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the entire tail light assembly.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Sunroof Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Problems with the sunroof, including difficulty opening or closing, excessive noise, or leaks, have been reported.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis involves inspecting the sunroof mechanism and seals. Repair may involve lubricating the mechanism, replacing seals, or replacing the entire sunroof assembly.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $1,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **HVAC Malfunctions**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Issues with the heating, ventilation, and air conditioning (HVAC) system, such as inconsistent temperature, failure to blow cold air, or unusual noises, can occur.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis involves checking the refrigerant level, compressor, and blower motor. Repair may involve recharging the refrigerant, replacing the compressor, or replacing the blower motor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $1,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2020 Land Rover Discovery Sport, while stylish and capable, has potential reliability issues. Engine issues, particularly excessive oil consumption and coolant leaks, can be costly to repair. Transmission problems, including rough shifting and potential failures, are also a concern. Electrical gremlins and infotainment system issues are common. Suspension issues, can further add to the ownership costs. The severity of these issues ranges from minor inconveniences to major repairs, with costs ranging from a few hundred dollars to several thousand.
