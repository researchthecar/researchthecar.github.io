**Introduction**

The 2011 Land Rover Defender is a rugged and capable off-road SUV. It is powered by a 2.4-liter turbocharged diesel engine that produces 122 horsepower and 266 lb-ft of torque. The engine is mated to a six-speed manual transmission. The Defender is also available with a 3.0-liter V6 diesel engine that produces 240 horsepower and 443 lb-ft of torque. This engine is mated to a six-speed automatic transmission.

People like the Defender for its off-road capabilities, rugged construction, and unique styling. However, the Defender is also known for its reliability issues.

**Engine**

* **2.4-liter turbocharged diesel engine**
    * **Turbocharger failure:** The turbocharger can fail, causing a loss of power.
        * Solution: Replace the turbocharger.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,000+
        * Recalls: NHTSA Campaign Number: 14V607000
* **3.0-liter V6 diesel engine**
    * **Fuel injector failure:** The fuel injectors can fail, causing a loss of power.
        * Solution: Replace the fuel injectors.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800+
        * Recalls: NHTSA Campaign Number: 13V323000

**Transmission**

* **Six-speed manual transmission**
    * **Clutch failure:** The clutch can fail, making it difficult to shift gears.
        * Solution: Replace the clutch.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $1,500+
        * Recalls: None Found
* **Six-speed automatic transmission**
    * **Transmission slipping:** The transmission can slip, causing a loss of power.
        * Solution: Rebuild or replace the transmission.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,500 - $4,000+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Front suspension:** The front suspension can wear out prematurely, causing the vehicle to handle poorly.
    * Solution: Replace the suspension components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Rear suspension:** The rear suspension can also wear out prematurely, causing the vehicle to handle poorly.
    * Solution: Replace the suspension components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Battery:** The battery can fail, leaving the vehicle stranded.
    * Solution: Replace the battery.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Alternator:** The alternator can fail, causing the vehicle to lose power.
    * Solution: Replace the alternator.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $700+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Starter:** The starter can fail, making it difficult to start the vehicle.
    * Solution: Replace the starter.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Rust:** The Defender is prone to rust, especially on the body panels and undercarriage.
    * Solution: Preventative measures such as rustproofing or regular washing can help prevent rust.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the extent of the damage.
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Dashboard:** The dashboard can crack or warp over time.
    * Solution: Replace the dashboard.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Seats:** The seats can become worn or damaged over time.
    * Solution: Repair or replace the seats.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the extent of the damage.
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2011 Land Rover Defender is a capable off-road SUV, but it is also known for its reliability issues. The engine, transmission, suspension, electrical system, and interior can all have problems. The severity of the issues varies, but some can be costly to fix. If you are considering buying a Defender, be sure to factor in the potential cost of repairs.
