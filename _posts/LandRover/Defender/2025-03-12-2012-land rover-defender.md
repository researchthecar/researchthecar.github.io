Here's a breakdown of common issues and complaints associated with the 2012 Land Rover Defender, presented in the requested format.

The 2012 Land Rover Defender is a rugged and iconic off-road vehicle known for its durability and capability. While it offers a classic driving experience, it's important to be aware of common issues reported by owners. The 2012 Defender was primarily available with a 2.2-liter diesel engine (producing around 120 hp and 265 lb-ft of torque) mated to a 6-speed manual transmission. Its boxy design, off-road prowess, and simplicity are often praised. However, owners frequently cite concerns about reliability, rust, and the generally agricultural nature of the driving experience as downsides.

**Engine**

**2.2L Diesel Engine**

*   **Turbocharger Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Reduced engine power, excessive black smoke, whistling noises from the turbocharger, and potentially engine warning lights.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the turbocharger unit, including checking oil feed lines for blockages.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1500 - $3000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **EGR Valve Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rough idling, stalling, reduced power, increased emissions, and illumination of the engine management light.
    *   Solution: Cleaning or replacement of the EGR valve. Sometimes, the cooler also needs replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Injector Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Misfires, rough running, excessive black smoke, poor fuel economy, and potential engine knocking.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis to identify the faulty injector(s) and replacement with new or refurbished units.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $1200+ (depending on the number of injectors)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Cooling System Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Coolant loss, overheating, and visible leaks around the radiator, hoses, or water pump.
    *   Solution: Identify the source of the leak and replace the affected component (hose, radiator, water pump, etc.).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $600+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Timing Belt Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Loud noise, rough running, or engine will not start. Can result in complete engine failure.
    *   Solution: Replacement of timing belt and water pump at regular service intervals (typically every 72,000 miles or 6 years).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**6-Speed Manual Transmission**

*   **Clutch Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Clutch slipping, difficulty shifting gears, unusual noises during clutch operation, and a high clutch pedal biting point.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the clutch disc, pressure plate, and release bearing. It's often recommended to replace the flywheel as well.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Gearbox Oil Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Oil puddles under the vehicle, difficulty shifting gears, and low gearbox oil levels.
    *   Solution: Identify and repair the source of the leak (seals, gaskets).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Worn Suspension Bushings**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Clunking noises from the suspension, vague steering, and uneven tire wear.
    *   Solution: Replacement of worn bushings (radius arm, Panhard rod, trailing arm bushings).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Shock Absorber Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Bouncy ride, poor handling, and leaking shock absorbers.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the shock absorbers.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Wiring Harness Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Intermittent electrical faults, including lighting issues, starting problems, and sensor malfunctions.
    *   Solution: Inspection and repair of the wiring harness, including replacing corroded or damaged wires and connectors.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $1000+ (depending on the severity)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Alternator Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Dim headlights, warning lights on the dashboard, and difficulty starting the engine.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the alternator.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $700+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Rust**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rust on the chassis, body panels, door frames, and other areas, especially in areas exposed to salt.
    *   Solution: Rust removal, repair of affected areas, and application of rust-proofing treatments.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $5000+ (depending on the severity and extent of the rust)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Door Hinges**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Sagging doors, difficult to close, noisy hinges.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the door hinges, or repair and lubrication.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $400+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Water Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Water entering the cabin during rain, damp carpets, and musty odors.
    *   Solution: Identification and sealing of the source of the leak (door seals, window seals, bulkhead seals).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **HVAC Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Inconsistent heating or cooling, blower motor failure, and faulty temperature controls.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis and repair of the HVAC system, including replacing the blower motor, resistor, or control unit.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2012 Land Rover Defender, while a capable off-road vehicle, suffers from several reliability issues. Engine problems like turbocharger and EGR valve failures, along with potential rust issues and electrical gremlins, are common. Suspension and clutch wear are also frequently reported. The cost of repairs can range from a few hundred dollars for minor fixes to several thousand for major issues like turbocharger replacement or rust repair. Potential buyers should have a pre-purchase inspection done by a Land Rover specialist and be prepared for ongoing maintenance costs.
