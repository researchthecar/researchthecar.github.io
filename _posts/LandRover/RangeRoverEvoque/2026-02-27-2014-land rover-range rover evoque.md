# 2014 Land Rover Range Rover Evoque

### Overview
The 2014 Land Rover Range Rover Evoque, while celebrated for its distinctive styling and off-road prowess, presents a mixed reliability profile. Owners often praise its luxurious interior and capable performance, particularly in urban environments where its compact size is an advantage. However, like many vehicles in its class, it is not without its potential mechanical and electrical quirks. This analysis focuses on documented common issues beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into areas that may require attention for owners of this model year. Understanding these potential pitfalls can help in proactive maintenance and managing ownership costs.

---


## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged I4 (Si4)
**Turbocharger Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of power, whistling noises, increased oil consumption.
- **Solution:** Turbocharger replacement or repair.
- **Cost:** $1,500 - $3,000

**Coolant Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Overheating, visible coolant puddles, sweet smell.
- **Solution:** Repair or replace leaking hoses, water pump, or radiator.
- **Cost:** $300 - $1,000


## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic (Aisin AWF6F55)
**Hesitation or Jerking**
- **Symptoms:** Rough shifting, delayed engagement, occasional jerking during acceleration or deceleration.
- **Solution:** Transmission fluid flush and filter replacement, software update, or internal transmission repair.
- **Cost:** $400 - $2,500


## Suspension

### Standard Chassis
**Worn Bushings**
- **Symptoms:** Clunking noises over bumps, vague steering feel, uneven tire wear.
- **Solution:** Replacement of worn suspension bushings.
- **Cost:** $300 - $700


## Electrical

### Infotainment System
**Screen Freezing or Unresponsive**
- **Symptoms:** Touchscreen malfunctions, system reboots, loss of audio or navigation.
- **Solution:** Software update, system reset, or replacement of the infotainment module.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1,500


### Battery Drain
**Intermittent Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Dead battery, warning lights on the dashboard, electrical components not working.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis of parasitic draw, repair or replacement of faulty modules or wiring.
- **Cost:** $200 - $800


## Exterior

### Body Panels and Seals
**Water Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Water ingress into the cabin, damp carpets, fogged windows.
- **Solution:** Inspection and sealing of door seals, sunroof drains, or body seams.
- **Cost:** $150 - $500


## Interior

### Interior Trim and Electronics
**Stitching and Material Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Premature wear on leather seats, peeling trim pieces, loose interior panels.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of damaged trim or upholstery.
- **Cost:** $200 - $1,000


--- 
### Summary Verdict
The 2014 Range Rover Evoque is a stylish and capable compact SUV, but potential buyers should be aware of its reliability concerns. The 2.0L turbocharged engine can experience issues with the turbocharger and coolant leaks. The automatic transmission may exhibit hesitation or rough shifting. Electrical gremlins, particularly with the infotainment system and intermittent battery drain, are also noted. Suspension components like bushings can wear prematurely, leading to noises. Exterior water leaks and interior trim wear are less critical but still present. While not universally problematic, these issues suggest that proactive maintenance and a budget for potential repairs are advisable for owners of this model year.
