**Introduction**

The 2012 Land Rover Range Rover Evoque is a compact luxury SUV known for its sleek design and off-road capabilities. It is available with a variety of engine and transmission options, including a 2.0L turbocharged gasoline engine mated to a 6-speed automatic transmission or a 2.2L turbocharged diesel engine paired with a 6-speed manual or automatic transmission. The gasoline engine produces 240 horsepower and 251 lb-ft of torque, while the diesel generates 190 horsepower and 310 lb-ft of torque. Despite its positive attributes, the Evoque has encountered several issues, which are detailed below.

**Engine**

**2.0L Turbocharged Gasoline Engine**

* **Fuel Injector Failure:** Description/Symptoms: The fuel injectors can become clogged or faulty, leading to rough idling, loss of power, and increased fuel consumption. Solution: Replacement of the affected fuel injector(s). Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800+
* **Turbocharger Failure:** Description/Symptoms: The turbocharger can fail due to wear or a faulty wastegate, resulting in reduced engine power, excessive smoke, and strange noises. Solution: Replacement or repair of the turbocharger. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
* **Timing Chain Tensioner Failure:** Description/Symptoms: The timing chain tensioner can fail, causing the timing chain to skip or break, which can lead to catastrophic engine damage. Solution: Replacement of the timing chain tensioner and related components. Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,200+

**2.2L Turbocharged Diesel Engine**

* **Diesel Particulate Filter (DPF) Issues:** Description/Symptoms: The DPF can become clogged with soot, leading to reduced engine power and increased fuel consumption. Solution: Cleaning or replacement of the DPF. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,200+
* **Injector Seal Failure:** Description/Symptoms: The injector seals can fail, causing fuel leaks and poor engine performance. Solution: Replacement of the injector seals. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+
* **Turbocharger Valve Sticking:** Description/Symptoms: The turbocharger valve can stick, resulting in reduced engine power and poor fuel economy. Solution: Replacement or repair of the turbocharger valve. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+

**Transmission**

**6-Speed Automatic Transmission**

* **Torque Converter Shudder:** Description/Symptoms: The torque converter can develop a shudder or vibration upon acceleration or deceleration. Solution: Replacement of the torque converter. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $1,500+
* **Shift Solenoid Failure:** Description/Symptoms: The shift solenoid can fail, causing delayed or hard shifts, as well as slipping gears. Solution: Replacement of the affected shift solenoid(s). Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
* **Transmission Control Module (TCM) Failure:** Description/Symptoms: The TCM can fail, leading to erratic transmission behavior, shifting problems, and warning lights on the dashboard. Solution: Replacement of the TCM. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800+

**Suspension**

* **Shock Absorber Failure:** Description/Symptoms: The shock absorbers can wear out prematurely, resulting in a bumpy or unstable ride quality. Solution: Replacement of the affected shock absorber(s). Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+ per shock
* **Strut Mount Bearing Failure:** Description/Symptoms: The strut mount bearings can wear out, causing noise and vibration during steering. Solution: Replacement of the strut mount bearing(s). Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+ per mount

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain:** Description/Symptoms: The battery can drain prematurely, even when the vehicle is parked and turned off. Solution: Diagnosis and repair of the electrical system, often involving replacement of faulty components like the alternator or electrical wiring. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
* **Radio Malfunction:** Description/Symptoms: The radio may not turn on, experience intermittent reception, or have distorted sound. Solution: Replacement or repair of the radio unit. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
* **Instrument Cluster Failure:** Description/Symptoms: The instrument cluster may display inaccurate information or malfunction completely. Solution: Replacement of the instrument cluster. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+

**Exterior**

* **Sunroof Leaks:** Description/Symptoms: The sunroof may leak water into the cabin, especially during heavy rain. Solution: Adjustment or replacement of the sunroof seals. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
* **Door Handle Failure:** Description/Symptoms: The exterior door handles can break or become loose, making it difficult to open or close the doors. Solution: Replacement of the affected door handle(s). Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+ per handle
* **Trim Pieces Coming Loose:** Description/Symptoms: The exterior trim pieces, such as window molding or bumper trim, can become loose or fall off. Solution: Reattachment or replacement of the affected trim pieces. Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $200+

**Interior**

* **Climate Control Malfunction:** Description/Symptoms: The climate control system may malfunction, resulting in inconsistent temperatures or air flow issues. Solution: Diagnosis and repair of the climate control system, which may involve replacement of components like the blend door actuator or temperature sensors. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
* **Seat Recline Mechanism Failure:** Description/Symptoms: The seat recline mechanism may fail, making it difficult or impossible to recline or adjust the seats. Solution: Replacement or repair of the seat recline mechanism. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+ per seat
* **Infotainment System Glitches:** Description/Symptoms: The infotainment system may experience glitches or crashes, including touchscreen issues or software problems. Solution: Software updates or replacement of the infotainment unit. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+

**Summary**

Overall, the 2012 Land Rover Range Rover Evoque is a capable and stylish SUV that has received positive feedback for its design and off-road performance. However, it has also been plagued by a variety of issues affecting various components. The most common and expensive issues involve the engine, transmission, and suspension, with costs ranging from $500 to over $3,000 to repair. Electrical, exterior, and interior issues are less prevalent but still occur, resulting in repair costs between $100 and $800. Despite these issues, the Evoque remains a popular choice among those who value its unique blend of luxury and ruggedness.
