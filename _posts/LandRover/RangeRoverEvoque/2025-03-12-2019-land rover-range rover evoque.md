Here's a breakdown of common issues reported for the 2019 Land Rover Range Rover Evoque, organized by category and engine/transmission type, following your requested format.

The 2019 Land Rover Range Rover Evoque is a stylish compact SUV known for its luxurious interior and off-road capabilities. Engine options include a 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder Ingenium engine producing 237 horsepower and 251 lb-ft of torque, and a more powerful version of the same engine producing 296 horsepower and 295 lb-ft of torque. Both are paired with a 9-speed automatic transmission. People generally like the Evoque's distinctive design and comfortable ride, but some dislike its relatively small cargo space and reported reliability issues.

**Engine**

**2.0L Turbocharged Ingenium (237 hp & 296 hp)**

*   **Excessive Oil Consumption**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Owners report the engine consuming an excessive amount of oil, requiring frequent top-ups between scheduled oil changes.  This can lead to low oil levels and potential engine damage if not monitored.
    *   Solution: In some cases, replacement of the piston rings and/or the cylinder head has been required. Oil consumption tests should be performed to confirm the issue.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $3,000 - $8,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Timing Chain Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms:  The timing chain can stretch or fail prematurely.  Symptoms include a rattling noise upon startup, rough running, and potential engine stalling.  A check engine light may illuminate.
    *   Solution:  Replacement of the timing chain, tensioner, and guides.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

* **Coolant Leaks**
    * Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaks can occur from various sources, including the water pump, thermostat housing, and hoses. Low coolant levels can cause overheating.
    * Solution: Identify the source of the leak and replace the faulty component (water pump, thermostat, hose, etc.).
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**9-Speed Automatic (ZF 9HP48)**

*   **Harsh Shifting/Hesitation**
    *   Description/Symptoms:  Reports of rough or jerky shifting between gears, especially at lower speeds.  Also, some owners experience hesitation when accelerating from a stop.
    *   Solution: Software updates to the Transmission Control Module (TCM) may help in some cases.  In more severe cases, valve body replacement or complete transmission replacement may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 (software update) - $6,000+ (transmission replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Transmission Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The transmission fails completely, leading to the inability to shift gears or move the vehicle. This can be preceded by slipping, erratic shifting, or loud noises.
    *   Solution: Transmission replacement is typically required.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $5,000 - $8,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Air Suspension Compressor Failure (If Equipped)**
    *   Description/Symptoms:  The air suspension system may fail to maintain the correct ride height, resulting in the vehicle sitting low on one or more corners.  The compressor may be excessively noisy or fail to operate altogether.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the air suspension compressor.  It's often recommended to also replace the air struts if they are leaking.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $2,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Worn Suspension Bushings**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Knocking or creaking noises from the suspension, especially when going over bumps. Vague steering and reduced handling precision.
    *   Solution: Replacement of worn suspension bushings.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Malfunctions**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The infotainment system (Touch Pro Duo) can be prone to glitches, freezing, and unresponsiveness.  Navigation issues, Bluetooth connectivity problems, and screen blackouts are also reported.
    *   Solution: Software updates are often the first step.  In some cases, the infotainment module itself needs to be replaced.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 (software update) - $2,000+ (module replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Battery Drain**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The vehicle's battery drains quickly, especially when the car is not driven regularly.  This can lead to difficulty starting the engine or complete battery failure.
    *   Solution: Investigate parasitic draws.  Common culprits include faulty modules (infotainment, telematics) and improperly shut-down systems.  Battery replacement may also be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 (battery replacement) - $1,000+ (diagnosing and repairing parasitic draw)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Sensor Failures (Parking Sensors, Blind Spot Monitoring)**
    *   Description/Symptoms:  Parking sensors may give false alarms or fail to detect objects.  Blind spot monitoring systems may become inoperative.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the faulty sensor(s).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+ per sensor
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Water Leaks (Sunroof, Tail Lights)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Water leaks into the cabin, often from the sunroof seals or around the tail lights. This can lead to damp carpets and potential mold growth.
    *   Solution: Resealing the sunroof or tail lights. Repairing or replacing damaged seals.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $1,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Creaking/Rattling Noises**
    *   Description/Symptoms:  Owners report various creaking and rattling noises emanating from the dashboard, door panels, and seats.
    *   Solution:  This often requires identifying the source of the noise and applying felt tape or other sound-deadening materials.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+ (depending on the complexity of the repair)
    *   Recalls: None Found

* **Power Window Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: Power windows may fail to operate properly, sticking or not fully opening/closing. The window motor may be faulty.
    * Solution: Window motor replacement.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200-$500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2019 Land Rover Range Rover Evoque, while stylish and capable, has a history of reliability issues. The most concerning problems revolve around the engine (oil consumption, timing chain), transmission (harsh shifting, failures), and electrical system (infotainment glitches, battery drain). Suspension and interior issues, while less severe, can also be annoying. Repair costs can range from a few hundred dollars for minor fixes to several thousand dollars for major engine or transmission repairs, making it crucial to have a thorough inspection performed before purchasing a used 2019 Evoque.
