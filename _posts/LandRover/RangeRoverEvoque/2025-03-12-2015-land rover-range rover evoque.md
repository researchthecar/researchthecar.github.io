Here's a comprehensive breakdown of common issues and complaints for the 2015 Land Rover Range Rover Evoque, organized by category and powertrain configuration. The 2015 Range Rover Evoque is a compact luxury SUV known for its stylish design and off-road capability. The most common engine available was a 2.0-liter turbocharged inline-4 producing 240 horsepower and 250 lb-ft of torque. A 9-speed automatic transmission was the only transmission option. Owners often praise the Evoque's distinctive styling and luxurious interior, but some common complaints include the reliability and maintenance costs.

**Engine**

**2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4**

*   **Turbocharger Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Whining noise from the engine, loss of power, check engine light. Can be caused by oil starvation or internal component failure.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the turbocharger. Ensure proper oil changes are performed on the vehicle to prevent premature failures.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Timing Chain Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rattling noise on startup, especially when cold. This can progress to more severe engine damage if not addressed.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the timing chain, guides, and tensioner.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,200 - $2,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Coolant Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Low coolant warning, overheating, visible coolant leak under the vehicle. Sources may include the water pump, thermostat housing, or radiator.
    *   Solution: Identify and repair the source of the leak. This may involve replacing the affected component (water pump, thermostat, radiator, hoses).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,500+ depending on the source of the leak.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**9-Speed Automatic Transmission**

*   **Rough Shifting/Hesitation**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Harsh or jerky shifts between gears, hesitation when accelerating, especially at lower speeds.
    *   Solution: Transmission software update. If the problem persists, valve body replacement or a complete transmission rebuild/replacement may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300 for software update, $800 - $2,500+ for valve body or transmission work.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Transmission Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Complete loss of drive, inability to shift gears, loud noises from the transmission.
    *   Solution: Transmission replacement or full rebuild
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $3,000 - $7,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Worn Suspension Bushings**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Clunking or rattling noises from the suspension, especially when going over bumps. Imprecise handling.
    *   Solution: Replacement of worn bushings.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+ per corner depending on the bushing.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Faulty Wheel Bearings**
    * Description/Symptoms: Humming or grinding noise coming from the wheels that changes with vehicle speed
    *   Solution: Replacement of faulty wheel bearing.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300-$700 per wheel.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: System freezing, unresponsive touchscreen, Bluetooth connectivity problems, navigation malfunctions.
    *   Solution: Software update, replacement of the infotainment unit.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300 for software update, $800 - $2,000+ for unit replacement.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Battery Drain**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Dead battery, difficulty starting the vehicle, especially after sitting for a few days.
    *   Solution: Diagnose and repair the source of the parasitic draw. This may involve replacing a faulty module or component.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $1,000+ depending on the source of the drain.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Sensor Failures**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Check engine light, various warning lights, malfunctioning driver assistance systems (blind-spot monitoring, parking sensors).
    *   Solution: Identify and replace the faulty sensor(s).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $500+ per sensor depending on the location and type.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Power Tailgate Malfunctions**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Tailgate not opening or closing properly, making unusual noises, or failing to latch securely.
    *   Solution: Check for obstructions, lubricate hinges and latches, or replace the tailgate actuator motor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Panoramic Roof Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Water leaks into the cabin, especially during rain.
    *   Solution: Clean the sunroof drains. If damaged replace the panoramic roof seal.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $1,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Heater/AC Blower Motor Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Air only blowing on certain settings, or air not blowing at all. Whining noises from behind the dashboard.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the blower motor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Door Lock Actuator Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: One or more doors not locking or unlocking with the key fob or interior switch.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the faulty door lock actuator.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+ per door.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2015 Range Rover Evoque, while stylish and luxurious, is known to have a number of potential issues. Engine problems, particularly related to the turbocharger and timing chain, can be quite expensive to repair. Transmission issues with the 9-speed automatic are also common and can lead to significant costs. Electrical problems, especially with the infotainment system, can be frustrating. While not all Evoques will experience these issues, it's important for potential buyers to be aware of them and factor in potential maintenance costs. Overall, the issues can range from moderately inconvenient to severely impactful on the vehicle's reliability and cost of ownership.
