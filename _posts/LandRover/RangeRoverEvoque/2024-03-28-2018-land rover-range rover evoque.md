## Overview

The 2018 Land Rover Range Rover Evoque is a compact luxury SUV that combines style, performance, and off-road capability. It is powered by a range of turbocharged engines and comes with a standard 9-speed automatic transmission. The Evoque has received praise for its luxurious interior, responsive handling, and advanced technology features. However, like any vehicle, it has its share of reported issues.

**Engine**

**2.0L Ingenium Turbocharged Petrol**

**Engine Misfiring**
Description/Symptoms: The engine may experience misfires, causing rough idling, loss of power, and increased fuel consumption.
Solution: Replacing the faulty ignition coil and spark plugs is typically the solution.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+

**Coolant Leaks**
Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaks from the water pump or coolant hoses can lead to overheating and engine damage.
Solution: Replacing the faulty water pump or coolant hoses is necessary.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+

**2.0L Ingenium Turbocharged Diesel**

**Diesel Particulate Filter (DPF) Clogging**
Description/Symptoms: Frequent short-distance driving can clog the DPF, resulting in decreased engine performance and increased fuel consumption.
Solution: Forced regeneration of the DPF by taking the vehicle on a long drive or using a diagnostic tool to initiate the process.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+

**EGR Valve Failures**
Description/Symptoms: The EGR valve recirculates exhaust gases to reduce emissions, but failures can lead to engine performance issues and increased emissions.
Solution: Replacing the EGR valve is necessary.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $600 - $1,200+

**Transmission**

**9-Speed Automatic Transmission**

**Shift Hesitations**
Description/Symptoms: The transmission may hesitate or jerk when shifting gears.
Solution: Software updates or replacing the transmission control module may resolve the issue.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+

**Clutch Overheating**
Description/Symptoms: Extended use in off-road conditions can overheat the clutch, leading to slipping and loss of power.
Solution: Replacing the clutch assembly is typically required.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+

**Suspension**

**Air Suspension Leaks**
Description/Symptoms: Leaks in the air suspension system can cause the vehicle to lose height and affect ride quality.
Solution: Replacing faulty air suspension components or repairing leaks is necessary.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,500+

**Electrical**

**Battery Drain**
Description/Symptoms: The battery may discharge prematurely, especially after short trips or prolonged inactivity.
Solution: Identifying and disconnecting the source of the electrical drain is typically required.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+

**Exterior**

**Window Trim Peeling**
Description/Symptoms: The chrome or black trim around the windows may peel or fade.
Solution: Replacing the affected trim pieces is the best solution.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+

**Interior**

**Infotainment System Lag**
Description/Symptoms: The touchscreen infotainment system may experience lag or freezing issues.
Solution: Software updates or replacing the infotainment unit may resolve the issue.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+

**Summary**

The 2018 Land Rover Range Rover Evoque is a desirable luxury SUV with relatively few reported issues. While some of the engine and transmission problems can be severe and expensive to fix, most issues seem to be minor electrical or cosmetic concerns. Overall, the Evoque's reliability is comparable to other luxury vehicles in its class.
