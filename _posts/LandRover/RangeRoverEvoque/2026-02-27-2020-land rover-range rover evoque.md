# 2020 Land Rover Range Rover Evoque

### Overview
The 2020 Land Rover Range Rover Evoque, a compact luxury SUV, aims to blend premium comfort with off-road capability. It offers a refined interior, advanced technology, and a distinctive styling. For the 2020 model year, it introduced a new platform and updated powertrains, including mild-hybrid technology on some variants. While generally well-received for its design and features, like any vehicle, it has specific areas where owners have reported concerns. This analysis focuses on documented technical reliability issues, excluding routine maintenance and standard wear and tear, to provide a clear picture of potential long-term ownership considerations.

---


## Engine

### 2.0L Ingenium Turbocharged I4 (P250)
**Coolant Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Visible coolant puddles under the vehicle, low coolant warning light, engine overheating.
- **Solution:** Replacement of leaking hoses, seals, or potentially the water pump.
- **Cost:** $300 - $700


### 2.0L Ingenium Turbocharged I4 MHEV (P300)
**Mild Hybrid System Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** Reduced power, battery warning lights, issues with start-stop functionality.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and potential replacement of battery, inverter, or related sensors.
- **Cost:** $800 - $2500


## Transmission

### 9-Speed Automatic (9HP45/9HP75)
**Harsh Shifting / Jerking**
- **Symptoms:** Noticeable clunks or hesitations during gear changes, particularly at lower speeds.
- **Solution:** Software updates for transmission control module, or in severe cases, internal transmission repair or replacement.
- **Cost:** $500 - $3000

**Transmission Fluid Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Leaking fluid from the transmission pan or seals, potential for slipping gears.
- **Solution:** Replacement of seals, gaskets, or transmission pan.
- **Cost:** $400 - $900

**Electronic Damper Malfunctions**

## Electrical

### Infotainment System
**Touchscreen Glitches / Unresponsiveness**
- **Symptoms:** Screen freezing, becoming unresponsive to touch inputs, or displaying errors.
- **Solution:** Software updates, or in some cases, replacement of the infotainment unit.
- **Cost:** $500 - $2000


### Battery Drain
**Premature Battery Discharge**
- **Symptoms:** Difficulty starting the vehicle, battery warning lights, electrical components failing intermittently.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis of parasitic draw, potential replacement of battery or faulty modules.
- **Cost:** $300 - $800

### Summary Verdict
The 2020 Land Rover Range Rover Evoque presents a generally solid reliability record for its core mechanical components, with no widespread critical failures reported for the standard engine and transmission options. However, potential owners should be aware of specific issues that have been documented, particularly concerning the mild-hybrid system on the P300 variant, potential for coolant leaks from the Ingenium engines, and occasional transmission shifting anomalies. Electrical gremlins, especially with the infotainment system and battery drain, are also noted. While the suspension and exterior/interior components are largely reliable, optional adaptive suspension systems can introduce additional repair costs if they fail. Overall, the Evoque offers a luxurious experience, but proactive maintenance and awareness of these potential issues are recommended for long-term ownership satisfaction.
