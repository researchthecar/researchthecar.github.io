# 2019 Land Rover Range Rover Evoque Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2019 Land Rover Range Rover Evoque, buyers will find a stylish compact luxury SUV that delivers strong performance and upscale features. However, as with many European luxury vehicles, prospective owners should review owner-reported complaints carefully, particularly concerning electrical glitches, body sealing, and occasional powertrain responsiveness.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged I4 Engine

The 2.0-liter turbocharged inline-four engine produces approximately 246 to 296 horsepower depending on the tune, offering brisk acceleration for daily driving. While generally robust, routine maintenance and prompt attention to diagnostic trouble codes are essential for long-term reliability.

**Acceleration Delay**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may occasionally experience a delayed response when attempting to accelerate at highway speeds.
- **Solution:** Diagnostic checks by a qualified mechanic can verify throttle body and turbocharger operation.

## Transmission

### 9-Speed Automatic Transmission

The nine-speed automatic transmission comes paired with standard all-wheel drive, providing smooth gear changes across varying road conditions. Buyers should ensure regular fluid changes have been performed to maintain optimal shift quality.

## Electrical

### Backup Camera and Door Latches

electrical issues involving accessories and convenience features can occasionally pop up, requiring professional diagnostic work.

**Camera and Latch Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have reported inoperable backup cameras and faulty door latches that may prevent doors from closing securely.
- **Solution:** Inspect electrical connections and replace faulty door latch assemblies or camera units as needed.

## Exterior

### Windshield and Roof Sealing

Exterior weather stripping and windshield seals should be thoroughly inspected during a pre-purchase evaluation to prevent water leaks.

**Water Intrusion and Headliner Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Water may leak through the center of the windshield during heavy rain or snow, potentially damaging the center console or causing headliner detachment.
- **Solution:** Have the windshield professionally resealed and check the headliner attachment points.
