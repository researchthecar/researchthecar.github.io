**2002 Rolls-Royce Park Ward**

The 2002 Rolls-Royce Park Ward is a luxury sedan that is known for its elegant design, luxurious interior, and powerful engine. It is powered by a 6.75-liter V12 engine that produces 453 horsepower and 531 lb-ft of torque. The engine is mated to a four-speed automatic transmission.

**Engine**

**6.75-liter V12**

* **VVT Solenoid Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The vehicle may experience rough idling, stalling, and difficulty starting.
    * Solution: Replace the faulty VVT solenoid.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,200+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Intake Valve Sticking:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The engine may experience misfires, loss of power, and poor fuel economy.
    * Solution: Clean or replace the intake valves.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**Four-Speed Automatic**

* **Torque Converter Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The vehicle may experience shuddering, slipping, or delayed gear engagement.
    * Solution: Replace the torque converter.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,000+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Transmission Control Module Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The vehicle may experience erratic gear shifting, transmission slipping, or failure to engage gears.
    * Solution: Replace the transmission control module.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Air Suspension Compressor Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The vehicle may experience sagging or uneven suspension, and reduced ride height.
    * Solution: Replace the air suspension compressor.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,200 - $2,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The vehicle may have difficulty starting or experience electrical system malfunctions.
    * Solution: Inspect the electrical system for parasitic draw and replace any faulty components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Electrical Module Malfunction:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The vehicle may experience electrical system malfunctions, such as flickering lights, inoperable gauges, or communication errors.
    * Solution: Diagnose and replace the faulty electrical module.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **No Issues Found**

**Interior**

* **Sunroof Leak:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The vehicle may experience water leaking through the sunroof during rain or car washes.
    * Solution: Replace or reseal the sunroof gasket.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Climate Control Malfunction:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The vehicle may experience faulty heating, cooling, or ventilation systems.
    * Solution: Diagnose and repair or replace the faulty components in the climate control system.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2002 Rolls-Royce Park Ward is a luxury sedan that offers a combination of style, comfort, and performance. However, it is important to be aware of the potential issues that can affect the vehicle, including air suspension compressor failure, torque converter failure, and intake valve sticking. These issues can be expensive to repair, so it is important to factor in the cost of maintenance when considering purchasing this vehicle.
