**Introduction:**

The 2021 Rolls-Royce Wraith is a lavish coupe that combines unparalleled luxury with impressive performance. It features a 6.6-liter twin-turbocharged V12 engine that produces 624 horsepower and 642 lb-ft of torque, paired with an 8-speed automatic transmission. Despite its opulent nature, the Wraith has received mixed reviews, with some owners encountering various issues.

**Engine**

**6.6-liter Twin-Turbocharged V12**

* **Turbocharger Failure:** Premature failure of the turbochargers, resulting in reduced engine power and increased exhaust smoke.
Solution: Replace both turbochargers.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $10,000 - $15,000+
Recalls: N/A

* **Timing Chain Tensioner Failure:** A faulty timing chain tensioner can cause the timing chain to become loose, leading to engine damage.
Solution: Replace the timing chain tensioner.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,000+
Recalls: N/A

**Transmission**

**8-Speed Automatic**

* **Transmission Slipping:** Occasional slipping of the transmission when changing gears, accompanied by a loss of power.
Solution: Replace the faulty valve body.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,500 - $4,000+
Recalls: N/A

* **Transmission Pump Failure:** A defective transmission pump can result in insufficient fluid pressure, leading to transmission overheating and damage.
Solution: Replace the transmission pump.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $5,000 - $8,000+
Recalls: N/A

**Suspension**

* **Air Suspension Malfunction:** Failure of the air suspension components, causing the vehicle to ride at an improper height or experience uneven handling.
Solution: Replace the faulty air compressor, air bags, or height sensors.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $4,000+
Recalls: N/A

* **Rear Axle Bearing Failure:** Premature wear or failure of the rear axle bearings, resulting in excessive noise or vibration.
Solution: Replace the rear axle bearings.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
Recalls: N/A

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain:** Rapid battery discharge, even when the vehicle is not in use.
Solution: Diagnose and repair faulty electrical components or replace the battery.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+
Recalls: N/A

* **Infotainment System Crashing:** Intermittent or frequent crashes of the infotainment system, causing loss of functionality.
Solution: Software update or replacement of the infotainment module.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+
Recalls: N/A

**Exterior**

* **Trim Molding Detachment:** Loosening or detachment of the trim molding along the body panels, resulting in an unsightly appearance.
Solution: Reattach or replace the trim molding.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
Recalls: N/A

* **Headlamp Condensation:** Moisture accumulation inside the headlamps, affecting light output and aesthetics.
Solution: Replace or seal the affected headlamps.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
Recalls: N/A

**Interior**

* **Steering Wheel Squeaking:** A persistent squeaking noise coming from the steering wheel when turning.
Solution: Lubricate or replace the steering column components.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
Recalls: N/A

* **Wood Trim Cracking:** Premature cracking of the genuine wood trim panels, particularly around the center console area.
Solution: Replace the affected wood trim panels.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+
Recalls: N/A

**Summary:**

The 2021 Rolls-Royce Wraith has mixed reviews, with some owners encountering various issues. The most common problems relate to the engine, transmission, and suspension components. However, it's important to note that these issues may not be widespread and are often resolved under warranty. The cost to fix these issues can be substantial, reflecting the high-end nature of the vehicle.
