**Introduction**

The 2014 Rolls-Royce Wraith is a luxurious grand tourer that debuted in 2013. It is powered by a 6.6-liter twin-turbocharged V12 engine that produces 624 horsepower and 590 lb-ft of torque. The V12 is paired with an eight-speed automatic transmission. The Wraith has a curb weight of 5,340 pounds and can accelerate from 0 to 60 mph in 4.4 seconds. Top speed is electronically limited to 155 mph.

Owners praise the Wraith for its powerful engine, luxurious interior, and head-turning style. However, the Wraith is also known for its high price tag and expensive maintenance costs.

**Engine**

**6.6L Twin-Turbocharged V12**

* **Turbocharger Failure:**
    * **Description/Symptoms:** Loss of power, excessive smoke, unusual noises from the engine bay.
    * **Solution:** Replace the faulty turbocharger.
    * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $3,000 - $6,000+
    * **Recalls:** None Found
* **Oil Leaks:**
    * **Description/Symptoms:** Oil puddles under the vehicle, low oil levels on the dipstick.
    * **Solution:** Replace the leaking gaskets or seals.
    * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $500 - $2,000+
    * **Recalls:** None Found

**Transmission**

**Eight-Speed Automatic**

* **Transmission Slipping:**
    * **Description/Symptoms:** Difficulty shifting gears, delayed gear changes, loss of power.
    * **Solution:** Replace the faulty transmission.
    * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $5,000 - $10,000+
    * **Recalls:** None Found

**Suspension**

* **Air Suspension Compressor Failure:**
    * **Description/Symptoms:** Suspension becomes inoperable, vehicle sits low to the ground.
    * **Solution:** Replace the faulty compressor.
    * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $2,000 - $3,000+
    * **Recalls:** None Found

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain:**
    * **Description/Symptoms:** Vehicle battery discharges prematurely, difficulty starting the engine.
    * **Solution:** Replace the faulty battery or identify and fix the source of the drain.
    * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $200 - $500+
    * **Recalls:** None Found
* **Electrical System Malfunctions:**
    * **Description/Symptoms:** Various electrical components malfunctioning, such as lights, gauges, or infotainment system.
    * **Solution:** Diagnose and repair the faulty electrical components.
    * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $500 - $2,000+
    * **Recalls:** None Found

**Exterior**

* **Panel Gaps:**
    * **Description/Symptoms:** Uneven or large gaps between body panels, affecting the overall appearance.
    * **Solution:** Realign and adjust the panels.
    * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $500 - $1,500+
    * **Recalls:** None Found
* **Headlight Condensation:**
    * **Description/Symptoms:** Moisture accumulates inside the headlight housing.
    * **Solution:** Replace the faulty headlights.
    * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $1,000 - $2,000+
    * **Recalls:** None Found

**Interior**

* **Infotainment System Glitches:**
    * **Description/Symptoms:** Touchscreen unresponsive, system freezing or crashing.
    * **Solution:** Software update, hardware replacement.
    * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $500 - $1,500+
    * **Recalls:** None Found
* **Climate Control Malfunctions:**
    * **Description/Symptoms:** AC or heater not functioning properly, temperature fluctuations.
    * **Solution:** Diagnose and repair the faulty components.
    * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $500 - $2,000+
    * **Recalls:** None Found

**Summary**

Overall, the 2014 Rolls-Royce Wraith is a reliable and luxurious grand tourer. However, it is important to be aware of the potential issues that owners have reported. The most severe and expensive issues include transmission slipping, turbocharger failure, and electrical system malfunctions. Regular maintenance and timely repairs are crucial to ensure the longevity and performance of the Wraith. It is also worth noting that the cost of parts and labor for a Rolls-Royce can be significantly higher than for other vehicles.
