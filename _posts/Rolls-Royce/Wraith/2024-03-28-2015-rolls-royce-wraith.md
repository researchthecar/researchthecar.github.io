## Introduction
The 2015 Rolls-Royce Wraith is a hand-built, ultra-luxury coupe that epitomizes opulence and performance. It features a 6.6-liter twin-turbocharged V12 engine mated to an eight-speed automatic transmission, delivering 624 horsepower and 590 lb-ft of torque. The Wraith boasts a sleek and muscular exterior, adorned with iconic Rolls-Royce design cues. Its interior is a sanctuary of handcrafted leather, wood, and metal, offering an unparalleled level of comfort and refinement. While the Wraith is a masterpiece of engineering and craftsmanship, it is not without its share of reported issues.

## Engine
### 6.6L Twin-Turbocharged V12

- **Intake Valve Carbon Buildup**
Description/Symptoms: Over time, carbon deposits can accumulate on the intake valves, restricting airflow and reducing engine performance. Symptoms may include decreased power, rough idling, and increased fuel consumption.
Solution: The solution involves removing and cleaning the intake valves.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
Recalls: None Found

- **Turbocharger Wastegate Failure**
Description/Symptoms: Malfunctioning wastegates can cause overboosting or underboosting, leading to engine damage. Symptoms may include a loss of power, increased exhaust gas temperatures, or turbocharger noises.
Solution: Replacement of affected turbocharger assembly.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $3,000 - $5,000+
Recalls: None Found

## Transmission
### Eight-Speed Automatic

- **Transmission Fluid Leaks**
Description/Symptoms: Leaks can develop from seals or gaskets, leading to fluid loss and reduced transmission performance. Symptoms may include slipping gears, hard shifting, and transmission noises.
Solution: Repairing or replacing leaking components and replenishing transmission fluid.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
Recalls: None Found

- **Mechatronic Control Unit Failure**
Description/Symptoms: The Mechatronic Control Unit (MCU) regulates transmission operation. MCU failure can result in a variety of issues, including gear selection problems, shifting delays, or transmission failure.
Solution: Replacement of the Mechatronic Control Unit.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,000+
Recalls: None Found

## Suspension
### Front Suspension

- **Strut Mount Wear**
Description/Symptoms: Worn strut mounts can cause clunking noises, uneven tire wear, and reduced handling.
Solution: Replacement of worn strut mounts.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
Recalls: None Found

### Rear Suspension

- **Air Spring Leaks**
Description/Symptoms: Leaks in the air springs can lead to suspension sag, reduced ride height, and handling problems.
Solution: Repairing or replacing damaged air springs.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
Recalls: None Found

## Electrical
- **Infotainment System Malfunctions**
Description/Symptoms: The infotainment system may experience software glitches, display issues, or connectivity problems.
Solution: Software updates or hardware replacements as required.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
Recalls: None Found

- **Battery Drain**
Description/Symptoms: Excessive battery drain can lead to premature battery failure or difficulty starting the vehicle.
Solution: Identifying and resolving the source of the parasitic drain, which could be due to a malfunctioning module, faulty wiring, or improperly installed accessories.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+
Recalls: None Found

## Exterior
- **Chrome Trim Discoloration**
Description/Symptoms: The chrome trim on the exterior can develop discoloration or corrosion over time.
Solution: Replacement or repair of affected trim pieces.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
Recalls: None Found

- **Door Handle Mechanism Failure**
Description/Symptoms: The door handle mechanism can fail, making it difficult or impossible to open the door.
Solution: Repair or replacement of the door handle mechanism.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
Recalls: 15V-688

## Interior
- **Climate Control Malfunctions**
Description/Symptoms: Issues with the climate control system can lead to incorrect temperature settings, inadequate cooling or heating, or excessive noise.
Solution: Diagnosis and repair of the climate control system components, such as sensors, actuators, or the control unit.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+
Recalls: None Found

- **Seat Adjustment Problems**
Description/Symptoms: Problems with the seat adjustment mechanisms can prevent proper seat positioning or cause excessive noise.
Solution: Diagnosis and repair or replacement of the affected seat adjustment mechanisms.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
Recalls: None Found

## Summary
The 2015 Rolls-Royce Wraith is a stunning and powerful vehicle, but it is not immune to issues. The most notable concerns reported by owners include engine intake valve carbon buildup, turbocharger wastegate failure, suspension strut mount wear, and infotainment system malfunctions. While some of these issues are relatively minor and inexpensive to fix, others can be more severe and costly. It is important to note that the severity and cost of issues may vary depending on factors such as driving habits, maintenance history, and the specific vehicle in question.
