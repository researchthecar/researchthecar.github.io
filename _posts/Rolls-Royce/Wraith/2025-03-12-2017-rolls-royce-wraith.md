The 2017 Rolls-Royce Wraith is a luxury grand tourer coupe renowned for its opulent interior, powerful engine, and smooth ride. It's celebrated for its handcrafted details, coach doors, and "waftability." However, some owners have reported experiencing certain issues. The Wraith is powered by a 6.6-liter twin-turbocharged V12 engine producing 624 horsepower and 590 lb-ft of torque. Power is delivered through an 8-speed automatic transmission. Some common owner complaints include electrical glitches, infotainment system malfunctions, and occasional suspension issues. On the other hand, owners appreciate the car's exclusive feel, powerful acceleration, and comfortable ride.

**Engine**

**6.6L Twin-Turbo V12**

*   **Oil Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks can develop from various engine seals and gaskets, leading to oil consumption and potential engine damage if not addressed. Owners may notice oil spots under the car or a burning oil smell.
    *   Solution: Replacing the affected gaskets and seals, such as the valve cover gaskets, oil pan gasket, or rear main seal.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $3,000+ depending on the location and complexity of the leak.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Turbocharger Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The turbochargers can experience failures due to high heat and stress. Symptoms include reduced power, whistling noises, and potentially turbocharger failure.
    *   Solution: Replacing the faulty turbocharger(s).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $5,000 - $10,000+ for parts and labor to replace both turbochargers.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**8-Speed Automatic**

*   **Rough Shifting**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported experiencing rough or delayed shifting, particularly between certain gears. This can be caused by wear and tear on the transmission components, low fluid levels, or software glitches.
    *   Solution: Transmission fluid flush, software update, or, in more severe cases, replacement of the transmission valve body or the entire transmission.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300-$500 for fluid flush, $200-$500 for software update, $2,000 - $5,000+ for valve body replacement, $8,000 - $15,000+ for transmission replacement.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Air Suspension Malfunctions**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The air suspension system, responsible for the Wraith's smooth ride, can sometimes develop leaks or compressor failures. This can lead to the car sitting unevenly, a harsh ride, or warning lights on the dashboard.
    *   Solution: Replacing the faulty air struts, air compressor, or related components.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $5,000+ per corner for air strut replacement, $1,500 - $3,000+ for compressor replacement.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The infotainment system can experience glitches, freezing, or complete failures. This can affect navigation, audio, and other vehicle functions.
    *   Solution: Software updates, replacement of the head unit, or repair of faulty wiring.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500 for software updates, $2,000 - $5,000+ for head unit replacement.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Battery Drain**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported issues with excessive battery drain, especially when the car is left parked for extended periods. This can be caused by faulty modules, parasitic draws, or a failing battery.
    *   Solution: Identifying and addressing the source of the parasitic draw, replacing the battery, or updating software.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300 for battery replacement, $100 - $500+ for diagnosing and fixing parasitic draws.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Sensor Malfunctions**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Various sensors throughout the vehicle, such as those related to the parking system or driver aids, can malfunction, leading to warning lights and reduced functionality.
    *   Solution: Replacing the faulty sensor(s).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,000+ per sensor, depending on the sensor's location and function.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Door Handle Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The complex door handle mechanisms can sometimes fail, leading to difficulty opening or closing the doors.
    *   Solution: Repairing or replacing the door handle mechanism.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $3,000+ per door handle, depending on the extent of the damage.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Trim Delamination**
    * Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported issues with the wood or veneer trim delaminating, particularly in areas exposed to heat or sunlight.
    * Solution: Replacing the delaminated trim pieces.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500-$5000+ depending on which piece needs to be replaced.
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2017 Rolls-Royce Wraith, while a symbol of luxury and performance, can present owners with a range of potential issues. The most common reported problems involve the engine (oil leaks, turbocharger failures), transmission (rough shifting), suspension (air suspension malfunctions), and electrical systems (infotainment system glitches, battery drain). The severity of these issues can range from minor inconveniences to costly repairs. The estimated cost to fix these problems can vary widely, from a few hundred dollars for a software update or fluid flush to several thousand dollars for major component replacements like turbochargers or air struts. Owners should be aware of these potential issues and ensure they have access to a qualified Rolls-Royce service technician for diagnosis and repair.
