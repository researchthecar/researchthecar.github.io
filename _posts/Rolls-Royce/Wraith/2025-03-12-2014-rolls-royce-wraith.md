The 2014 Rolls-Royce Wraith is a luxury grand tourer coupe powered by a potent 6.6-liter twin-turbocharged V12 engine, producing 624 horsepower and 590 lb-ft of torque. A slightly more powerful Wraith was later released with 632 horsepower. The Wraith only comes with an 8-speed automatic transmission. Owners often praise the Wraith for its luxurious interior, smooth ride, and powerful engine. Common complaints include the high cost of maintenance and repairs and some concerns about the reliability of certain electronic components.

**Engine**

**6.6L Twin-Turbo V12**

*   **Oil Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks can occur from various seals and gaskets on the engine, leading to oil consumption and potential engine damage. Look for oil spots under the car or visible leaks on the engine itself.
    *   Solution: Replacing the affected seals and gaskets.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2000+, depending on the location and severity of the leak.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Turbocharger Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported issues with the turbochargers, including failure or reduced performance. Symptoms may include a loss of power, unusual noises from the engine, or the check engine light illuminating.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis and repair or replacement of the faulty turbocharger(s).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $3000 - $8000+ for turbocharger replacement.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**8-Speed Automatic (ZF 8HP)**

*   **Harsh Shifting/Hesitation**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have experienced rough or delayed shifting, particularly at lower speeds. This can manifest as a noticeable clunk or jerk during gear changes.
    *   Solution: Transmission software update, fluid flush, or valve body replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $3000+, depending on the cause.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Air Suspension Problems**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The Wraith utilizes an air suspension system, which can experience issues such as air leaks, compressor failure, or sensor malfunctions. This can result in uneven ride height, a harsh ride, or the suspension warning light illuminating.
    *   Solution: Repair or replacement of the air struts, compressor, or sensors.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1000 - $5000+, depending on the specific component needing repair.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Malfunctions**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The infotainment system can experience glitches, freezing, or complete failure.
    *   Solution: Software updates or replacement of the head unit.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $3000+, depending on the issue and repair needed.
    *   Recalls: None Found
*   **Battery Drain**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Excessive battery drain can occur, especially if the vehicle is not driven regularly. This can result in a dead battery and difficulty starting the car.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis of the parasitic drain and repair of the faulty component.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1000+, depending on the cause of the drain.
    *   Recalls: None Found
*   **Sensor Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Various sensors throughout the vehicle can malfunction, leading to warning lights and inaccurate readings.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis and replacement of the faulty sensor(s).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1000+ per sensor, depending on the location and type of sensor.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Automatic Door Closing Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The Wraith features automatic door closing, and this system can sometimes experience malfunctions, such as the doors not closing properly or making unusual noises.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis and repair of the door closing mechanism.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2000+.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **No Issues Found**

**Summary**

The 2014 Rolls-Royce Wraith, while offering exceptional luxury and performance, can be susceptible to various issues, particularly related to the engine, transmission, suspension, and electrical systems. The cost of repairs can be significant, reflecting the premium nature of the vehicle and its components. Potential buyers should be aware of these potential problems and factor in the cost of maintenance and repairs when considering purchasing a used Wraith.
