The 2022 Rolls-Royce Cullinan is a luxury SUV known for its opulent interior, powerful performance, and distinctive styling. All models are equipped with a 6.75-liter twin-turbocharged V12 engine producing 563 horsepower and 627 lb-ft of torque, paired with an 8-speed automatic transmission. The Black Badge variant boosts output to 592 horsepower. Owners generally praise the Cullinan's comfortable ride, bespoke customization options, and commanding presence. Criticisms often center on its high price, considerable size, and, occasionally, reliability concerns commensurate with its complex systems.

**Engine**

**6.75L Twin-Turbocharged V12**

*   **Engine Oil Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks can develop from various engine seals and gaskets. Common areas include the valve cover gaskets and the front crankshaft seal. Symptoms include visible oil leaks under the vehicle, a burning oil smell, and low engine oil levels.
    *   Solution: Replacing the leaking gaskets or seals.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $3,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**8-Speed Automatic**

*   **Rough Shifting/Hesitation**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported instances of rough or delayed shifting, particularly during low-speed acceleration or downshifts. The transmission may hesitate or jerk when changing gears.
    *   Solution: In some cases, a software update to the transmission control module (TCM) can resolve the issue. If the problem persists, valve body replacement or transmission rebuild may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $8,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Air Suspension Malfunctions**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The Cullinan uses an air suspension system for a smooth ride. Failures can occur in the air struts, compressor, or height sensors. Symptoms include the vehicle sitting unevenly, a harsh ride, or error messages on the dashboard.
    *   Solution: Replacing the faulty air strut, compressor, or height sensor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $6,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Glitches**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The infotainment system can experience various glitches, including screen freezes, slow response times, and connectivity problems with Bluetooth or Apple CarPlay.
    *   Solution: Software updates are often released to address these issues. In some cases, a complete system reset or hardware replacement may be required.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $3,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Battery Drain Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported issues with the vehicle's battery draining quickly, especially when the car is not driven regularly. This can lead to difficulty starting the engine or complete battery failure.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis to identify the source of the parasitic drain, which could be a faulty module, sensor, or wiring issue. Repair or replacement of the faulty component and battery replacement if necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $2,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Automatic Door Closing Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: The Cullinan features automatic doors that can close with the push of a button. Some owners have reported issues with these doors failing to close properly or becoming misaligned.
    * Solution: Adjusting or replacing the door actuators, sensors, or hinges.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $4,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Navigation System Errors**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The navigation system may exhibit errors in routing, display outdated maps, or fail to locate destinations accurately.
    *   Solution: Updating the navigation software or replacing the navigation unit.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $2,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2022 Rolls-Royce Cullinan, while a pinnacle of luxury, can experience issues typical of high-end vehicles with complex systems. Common problems revolve around the air suspension, electrical gremlins with the infotainment system, and engine oil leaks. The severity of these issues ranges from minor software glitches to major mechanical failures, resulting in repair costs spanning from a few hundred to several thousand dollars. While not every Cullinan will experience these issues, potential buyers should be aware of them and ensure a thorough pre-purchase inspection.
