# 2023 Rolls-Royce Cullinan Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2023 Rolls-Royce Cullinan represents the pinnacle of ultra-luxury sport utility vehicles, combining hand-crafted opulence with effortless performance. For used-car buyers looking at this tier of automotive engineering, understanding the mechanical baseline and potential ownership nuances is key. While official owner complaint data and recurring defect reports are extremely sparse for this model year, prospective buyers should still approach a pre-owned purchase with standard due diligence, ensuring comprehensive service histories and professional inspections.

---

## Engine

### 6.75-Liter Twin-Turbocharged V12

This twin-turbocharged 6.75-liter V12 produces approximately 563 horsepower and 627 lb-ft of torque, delivering exceptionally smooth and silent acceleration. It is a robust powerhouse designed to move the heavy chassis with effortless authority, though routine maintenance costs at authorized service centers are predictably substantial.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

The ZF-sourced 8-speed automatic transmission pairs seamlessly with the V12 engine, utilizing GPS data to pre-emptively select the correct gear for upcoming corners. It offers refined, nearly imperceptible shifts that complement the vehicle's ultra-luxury ride quality.

## Summary Verdict

The 2023 Rolls-Royce Cullinan remains an extraordinarily rare and exclusive pre-owned acquisition with virtually no widespread recurring mechanical complaints documented in early ownership data. Buyers should prioritize certified pre-owned options, verify complete dealer maintenance records, and keep in mind that ongoing upkeep and insurance for a vehicle of this caliber require a significant financial commitment.
