The 2023 Rolls-Royce Cullinan is the pinnacle of luxury SUVs, boasting a commanding presence and opulent interior. Powered by a 6.75-liter twin-turbo V12 engine producing 563 horsepower and 627 lb-ft of torque (or 592 hp in the Black Badge variant), it delivers effortless performance. All Cullinans utilize an 8-speed automatic transmission. Owners often praise its smooth ride, handcrafted interior, and customizable features. However, some criticisms include its considerable size, high price tag, and potential for complex electronic systems to malfunction.

**Engine**

**6.75L Twin-Turbo V12**
* **Oil Leaks**
    * Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks may develop from the valve cover gaskets, oil pan gasket, or turbocharger oil lines. Symptoms include visible oil drips, burning oil smell, and low oil level.
    * Solution: Replace the leaking gasket or oil lines. Ensure proper torque specifications are followed during reassembly.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $3,000+ depending on the source of the leak and the labor involved.
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Coolant Leaks**
    * Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaks, typically from hoses or the water pump, leading to overheating. Symptoms include low coolant level, warning lights, and visible leaks.
    * Solution: Replace the leaking hoses or water pump. Inspect the cooling system for further damage.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $2,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**8-Speed Automatic**
* **Harsh Shifting**
    * Description/Symptoms: Some owners report experiencing harsh or jerky shifting, particularly at lower speeds. This can stem from software issues or valve body problems within the transmission.
    * Solution: A software update to the transmission control module (TCM) may resolve the issue. If the problem persists, valve body replacement may be necessary.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800 for software update, $2,000 - $5,000+ for valve body replacement.
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Air Suspension Malfunctions**
    * Description/Symptoms: The Cullinan's air suspension system can sometimes experience malfunctions, leading to uneven ride height, error messages, or a complete loss of suspension functionality. This is most common in cold weather.
    * Solution: Diagnose the specific issue using diagnostic tools. Common fixes include replacing air struts, ride height sensors, or the air compressor.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $6,000+ depending on the component needing replacement.
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Infotainment System Glitches**
    * Description/Symptoms: The infotainment system can occasionally experience glitches, such as freezing, crashing, or unresponsive touch screen.
    * Solution: Try restarting the system. A software update from the dealer may also address the issue.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: Free (software update) - $1,000+ (if hardware replacement is needed).
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Sensor Failures**
    * Description/Symptoms: Issues related to parking sensors, blind spot monitoring, or adaptive cruise control systems.
    * Solution: Replacement of faulty sensors and recalibration of the affected systems.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+ depending on the system and sensor involved.
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Automatic Door Closing Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported problems with the automatic door closing mechanism failing to latch or close properly.
    * Solution: Adjust or replace the door latch mechanism or associated sensors.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+

**Interior**

* **Seat Adjustment Problems**
    * Description/Symptoms: The electric seat adjustments can occasionally fail to function correctly, or the seat may get stuck in one position.
    * Solution: Check the wiring and connections to the seat motors and control module. Replacing a faulty motor or module is often required.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2023 Rolls-Royce Cullinan, while a paragon of luxury, is not immune to issues. The most common complaints revolve around engine oil leaks and glitches in the advanced electronic systems. While some problems can be resolved with software updates, others require costly repairs. The severity of these issues ranges from minor inconveniences to significant mechanical failures, and repair costs can be substantial, reflecting the vehicle's premium status and the complexity of its engineering.
