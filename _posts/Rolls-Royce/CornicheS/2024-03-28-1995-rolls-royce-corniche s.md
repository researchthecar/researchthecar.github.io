**Introduction**

The 1995 Rolls-Royce Corniche S is a luxurious convertible that offers a combination of performance and sophistication. It is powered by a 6.75-liter V8 engine that produces 325 horsepower and 363 lb-ft of torque. The Corniche S is paired with a four-speed automatic transmission. Drivers appreciate the vehicle's opulent interior, smooth ride, and timeless design. However, like any vehicle, the Corniche S is not without its potential issues.

**Engine**

* **6.75L V8**
    * **Intake Manifold Gasket Failure:**
        * Description/Symptoms: The intake manifold gasket can fail, causing a vacuum leak that can result in rough idling, loss of power, and increased fuel consumption.
        * Solution: Replace the intake manifold gasket.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
        * Recalls: None Found
    * **Oil Leaks from Valve Cover Gaskets:**
        * Description/Symptoms: The valve cover gaskets can fail, causing oil to leak onto the engine. This can lead to visible oil stains, burning oil smell, and potential engine damage if not addressed.
        * Solution: Replace the valve cover gaskets.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

* **Four-Speed Automatic**
    * **Shifting Problems:**
        * Description/Symptoms: The transmission may experience issues with shifting gears, including slipping, hard shifting, or refusal to shift.
        * Solution: Diagnose the problem and repair or replace the faulty components, which could include the transmission control module, solenoids, or mechanical components within the transmission.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $4,000+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Air Suspension Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The air suspension system, responsible for adjusting the ride height, may fail due to leaks in the air lines, faulty compressor, or worn-out air bags. This can result in an uneven or low ride height, affecting handling and stability.
    * Solution: Diagnose and repair the failed component, which may involve replacing air lines, compressor, air bags, or other parts of the suspension system.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Electrical Wiring Harness Problems:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Electrical wiring harnesses may develop issues, causing various electrical problems such as malfunctioning lights, gauges, or even starting problems.
    * Solution: Diagnose the wiring harness and repair or replace damaged or faulty wires.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Alternator Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The alternator, responsible for charging the battery, may fail, leading to battery drain and potential electrical system malfunctions.
    * Solution: Replace the alternator.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Convertible Top Issues:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The convertible top may experience problems such as leaks, torn fabric, or malfunctioning motors.
    * Solution: Repair or replace the affected components, which could include the convertible top motor, hydraulic lines, or fabric.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Chrome Trim Corrosion:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Chrome trim pieces on the exterior of the vehicle may corrode over time, affecting the appearance of the car.
    * Solution: Remove the corroded trim and replace it or restore it professionally.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Wood Trim Cracking:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The wood trim on the interior may develop cracks or warping due to age, humidity, or excessive sunlight exposure.
    * Solution: Replace the damaged wood trim or repair it professionally.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 1995 Rolls-Royce Corniche S is a classic vehicle that combines luxury and performance. However, it can experience certain issues, particularly with its engine, suspension, electrical components, and exterior trim. The severity and cost of these issues vary, so it is essential to be aware of them and consider maintenance costs when purchasing and owning a Corniche S.
