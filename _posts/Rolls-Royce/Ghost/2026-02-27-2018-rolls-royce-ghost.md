# 2018 Rolls-Royce Ghost

### Overview
The 2018 Rolls-Royce Ghost represents the pinnacle of automotive luxury and engineering, and as such, its reliability is generally very high. Owners can expect a refined driving experience with meticulous attention to detail in its construction. While no vehicle is entirely immune to potential issues, the Ghost is designed with robust components and advanced systems to ensure long-term durability. This analysis focuses on documented technical reliability concerns beyond standard maintenance, aiming to provide a clear picture of potential areas that may require attention over the vehicle's lifespan. The focus is on specific components and their reported issues, rather than general wear and tear.

## Suspension

### Adaptive Air Suspension
**Air Spring Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle may sag on one corner, uneven ride height, warning lights on the dashboard.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the affected air spring or associated lines.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1500 per corner

**Compressor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Inability to maintain ride height, constant running of the compressor, warning lights.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the air suspension compressor unit.
- **Cost:** $1000 - $2000


## Electrical

### Infotainment System
**Software Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Screen freezing, unresponsiveness, navigation errors, connectivity issues.
- **Solution:** Software updates or system reset. In rare cases, module replacement.
- **Cost:** $200 - $1500


### Door Handle Actuators
**Malfunctioning Actuators**
- **Symptoms:** Power door handles not extending or retracting properly, difficulty opening doors.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the affected door handle actuator.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1000 per door

### Summary Verdict
The 2018 Rolls-Royce Ghost demonstrates a high level of technical reliability, consistent with its luxury positioning. The powertrain, comprising the 6.6L twin-turbo V12 engine and 8-speed automatic transmission, is exceptionally robust with no widespread reported issues. The primary areas where owners might encounter issues are within the sophisticated adaptive air suspension system, which can develop leaks or compressor failures, and certain electrical components like the infotainment system and door handle actuators, which may experience software glitches or mechanical failures. Interior and exterior components are generally well-built, though specific climate control actuators can occasionally fail. Overall, the Ghost offers a dependable ownership experience, with most potential issues being repairable at a premium cost reflective of the vehicle's exclusivity.
