# 2021 Rolls-Royce Ghost Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2021 Rolls-Royce Ghost represents an ultra-luxury sedan experience, combining immense power with supreme refinement. As a flagship vehicle built for discerning buyers, shopping for a pre-owned model requires careful attention to its advanced powertrain and high-end luxury electronics. While formal owner complaint data for this model year is extremely sparse, buyers should always verify vehicle history and electronic functionality given the sophisticated engineering involved.

---

## Engine

### 6.75-Liter Twin-Turbocharged V12

This 6.75-liter twin-turbocharged V12 engine produces approximately 563 horsepower and 627 lb-ft of torque, delivering effortless and exceptionally quiet acceleration. It is a robust power plant well-suited to the Ghost's grand touring character.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission with Satellite Aided Transmission

The 8-speed automatic transmission utilizes GPS data to pre-select the ideal gear for upcoming corners and terrain changes. It shifts seamlessly to maintain the brand's signature 'magic carpet ride' feel.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V554000: Seat Belts](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Rolls-Royce&model=Ghost&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** As such, these assemblies fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 208, "Occupant Crash Protection." During a crash, a seat belt retractor that does not function as intended increases the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the seat belt retractors (including the seat belts) and, if necessary, replace the retractors, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V645000: Electrical System: Instrument Cluster/Panel](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Rolls-Royce&model=Ghost&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The single-layer tempered glass protecting the instrument cluster may break in a crash. Broken glass can increase the risk of injury in a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will apply a protective coating on the instrument cluster's single-layer tempered glass, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.
