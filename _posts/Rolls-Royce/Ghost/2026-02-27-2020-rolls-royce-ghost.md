## Suspension

### Adaptive Air Suspension
**Air Spring Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle sagging on one side, loss of ride height, warning lights on the dashboard.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the affected air spring or associated lines.
- **Cost:** $1,500 - $3,000

**Compressor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Inability to maintain ride height, constant running of the compressor, suspension warning lights.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the air suspension compressor unit.
- **Cost:** $1,200 - $2,500


## Electrical

### Infotainment System
**Software Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Screen freezing, unresponsive touch controls, intermittent audio issues, navigation system errors.
- **Solution:** Software updates or a full system reset. In rare cases, module replacement.
- **Cost:** $300 - $1,500


### Battery Drain
**Parasitic Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Battery dying unexpectedly, difficulty starting the vehicle, frequent need for jump starts.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis to identify the source of the drain (e.g., faulty module, aftermarket accessory) and repair or replacement.
- **Cost:** $400 - $1,200

### Summary Verdict
The 2020 Rolls-Royce Ghost demonstrates a high level of reliability, particularly concerning its powertrain. The 6.6L V12 engine and 8-speed automatic transmission are robust and generally free from significant issues. The primary areas where owners might encounter technical concerns are within the sophisticated suspension system, specifically air spring leaks or compressor failures, and occasional electrical gremlins such as infotainment software glitches or parasitic battery drain. These issues, while not widespread, can be costly to repair due to the complexity and premium nature of the vehicle's components. Overall, the Ghost offers an exceptionally reliable ownership experience, with most potential problems being manageable and related to advanced comfort and technology features.
