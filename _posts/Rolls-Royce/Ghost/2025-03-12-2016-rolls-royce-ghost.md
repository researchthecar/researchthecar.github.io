The 2016 Rolls-Royce Ghost is a pinnacle of luxury, boasting a refined driving experience and opulent interior. Its standard engine is a 6.6-liter twin-turbocharged V12 engine producing 563 horsepower and 575 lb-ft of torque. Some owners praise the car's smooth ride, powerful engine, and bespoke customization options. Criticisms often revolve around the high cost of ownership, complex electronics, and potential for expensive repairs. The Ghost is paired with an 8-speed automatic transmission.

**Engine**

**6.6L Twin-Turbocharged V12**

*   **Oil Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks can develop from various engine seals and gaskets, leading to visible oil spots under the car and a burning smell.
    *   Solution: Replacing the faulty seals or gaskets.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2000+ (depending on the location and severity of the leak)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Coolant Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaks, often from hoses, water pump, or radiator. Can cause overheating and engine damage.
    *   Solution: Replacing the leaking components.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1500+ (depending on the source of the leak)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**8-Speed Automatic**

*   **Rough Shifting**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Harsh or jerky shifts between gears, especially at lower speeds.
    *   Solution: Transmission fluid flush and software update. In some cases, valve body replacement may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $3000+ (depending on the severity and required repair)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Air Suspension Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The air suspension system can experience leaks in the air struts or compressor malfunctions, leading to ride height issues, uneven suspension, and a warning light on the dashboard.
    *   Solution: Replacing the leaking air struts or the faulty compressor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $2000 - $6000+ per corner (for strut replacement), $1000 - $3000+ (for compressor replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Malfunctions**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The infotainment system can freeze, crash, or experience glitches, affecting navigation, audio, and other functions.
    *   Solution: Software updates or replacement of the infotainment system.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+ (for software updates), $3000 - $8000+ (for system replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Sensor Failures**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Various sensors, such as parking sensors or tire pressure sensors, can fail, triggering warning lights and affecting vehicle functionality.
    *   Solution: Replacing the faulty sensor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+ per sensor
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Battery Drain**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The car's battery can drain quickly, especially if left unused for extended periods.
    *   Solution: Diagnosing and addressing the source of the parasitic drain. This may involve replacing the battery, fixing faulty wiring, or replacing a module that isn't shutting down properly.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $2000+ (depending on the cause and complexity)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Automatic Door Closing Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The automatic door closing system may malfunction, causing doors to not close properly or at all.
    *   Solution: Diagnosing and repairing or replacing the door closing mechanism.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1000 - $4000+ per door
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Seat Motor Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The electric seat motors may fail, preventing the seats from adjusting properly.
    *   Solution: Replacing the faulty seat motor or repairing the wiring.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2000+ per seat
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2016 Rolls-Royce Ghost, while an exceptional vehicle, can present owners with a range of issues, many related to its complex systems. Electrical issues and air suspension problems are the most prevalent and potentially the most costly to address. Engine oil leaks and transmission issues are also fairly common. While these issues may not be extremely frequent, the high cost of parts and labor associated with a Rolls-Royce means repairs can be quite expensive, and a proactive approach to maintenance is advised.
