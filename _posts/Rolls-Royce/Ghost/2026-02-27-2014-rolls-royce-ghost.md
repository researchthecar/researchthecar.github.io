# 2014 Rolls-Royce Ghost

### Overview
The 2014 Rolls-Royce Ghost, a pinnacle of luxury and engineering, generally exhibits strong reliability, reflecting its premium build quality and meticulous manufacturing. While no vehicle is entirely immune to issues, the Ghost tends to avoid widespread, recurring problems. Owners typically report satisfaction with its robust powertrain and refined cabin. However, as with any high-end automobile, potential buyers should be aware of the specialized nature of its components and the associated maintenance costs. This analysis focuses on documented technical issues beyond routine maintenance, aiming to provide a clear picture of the Ghost's long-term dependability.

## Suspension

### Adaptive Air Suspension
**Air Spring Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle may sag on one corner, uneven ride height, warning lights on the dashboard.
- **Solution:** Replacement of leaking air spring or associated lines.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1500 per corner

**Compressor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Inability to raise or lower the vehicle, constant running of the compressor, suspension warning lights.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the air suspension compressor unit.
- **Cost:** $700 - $1200


## Electrical

### Infotainment System
**Software Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Screen freezing, unresponsive controls, navigation errors, Bluetooth connectivity issues.
- **Solution:** Software updates or full system reset. In rare cases, module replacement.
- **Cost:** $200 - $1000


### Door Handles (Gullwing Style)
**Actuator Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Door handles not extending or retracting properly, difficulty opening doors.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the door handle actuator mechanism.
- **Cost:** $400 - $800 per handle

### Summary Verdict
The 2014 Rolls-Royce Ghost demonstrates a high level of reliability, particularly concerning its core powertrain. The V12 engine and 8-speed automatic transmission are robust and rarely present significant issues. The primary areas where owners might encounter problems are the adaptive air suspension, which can develop leaks or compressor faults, and certain electrical components like the infotainment system or door handle actuators, which may experience software glitches or mechanical failures. These issues, while not endemic, are typical of complex luxury vehicles and can incur substantial repair costs. Overall, the Ghost offers a dependable ownership experience, provided regular, specialized maintenance is performed and potential issues are addressed promptly by qualified technicians.
