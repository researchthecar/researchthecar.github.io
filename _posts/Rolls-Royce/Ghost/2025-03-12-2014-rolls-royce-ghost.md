The 2014 Rolls-Royce Ghost is a luxury sedan known for its opulent interior, smooth ride, and powerful engine. The standard engine option is a 6.6-liter twin-turbocharged V12 engine producing 563 horsepower and 575 lb-ft of torque. Some owners appreciate the Ghost's comfortable cabin, prestigious brand image, and effortless acceleration. Common complaints include high maintenance costs, complex electronics, and potential for expensive repairs. It is paired with an 8-speed automatic transmission.

**Engine**

**6.6L Twin-Turbo V12**

*   **Oil Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks can develop from various points on the engine, including valve cover gaskets, oil pan gaskets, and turbocharger lines. These leaks can result in visible oil spots under the car, a burning oil smell, and low oil levels.
    *   Solution: Replacing the leaking gaskets or seals is the typical solution. In some cases, turbocharger line leaks may necessitate replacing the affected lines.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $3,000+ (depending on the location and severity of the leak)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Turbocharger Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The twin turbochargers on the 6.6L V12 can experience failures due to excessive heat and wear. Symptoms include reduced engine power, whistling noises from the engine bay, and potential fault codes related to turbocharger boost.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the faulty turbocharger(s) is usually required.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $4,000 - $8,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**8-Speed Automatic**

*   **Rough Shifting**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported experiencing rough or delayed shifts, particularly between certain gears. This can be accompanied by jerking or clunking noises.
    *   Solution: A transmission fluid flush may resolve minor cases. More severe cases may require valve body repair or replacement, or even a complete transmission rebuild or replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 (fluid flush) - $8,000+ (rebuild or replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Air Suspension Compressor Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The air suspension system is responsible for the Ghost's smooth ride. The compressor can fail, leading to the car sitting lower than normal, uneven ride height, and warning lights on the dashboard.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the air suspension compressor is usually necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Air Strut Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Air struts can develop leaks, resulting in similar symptoms to compressor failure â€“ low ride height, uneven ride height, and warning lights. A hissing sound may be audible near the affected strut.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the leaking air strut is required.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,500 - $5,000+ per strut
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Malfunctions**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The infotainment system, which controls navigation, audio, and other vehicle functions, can experience glitches, freezing, or complete failure.
    *   Solution: Software updates may resolve some issues. In other cases, replacement of the infotainment unit may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 (software update) - $5,000+ (unit replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Battery Drain**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The Ghost has a complex electrical system, which can lead to parasitic battery drain. This can result in a dead battery, particularly if the car is not driven frequently.
    *   Solution: Identifying and addressing the source of the drain is crucial. This may involve testing various components and modules, and replacing any faulty parts.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 (simple fix) - $1,000+ (complex electrical diagnosis and repair)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Automatic Door Closing System Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The automatic door closing system, which gently pulls the doors closed, can malfunction. This may result in the doors not closing properly, or the system making unusual noises.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis and repair of the door closing mechanism. This may involve replacing motors, sensors, or other components.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+ per door
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Electronic Component Failure (Seats, Windows, etc.)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The interior features a variety of electrically operated components such as seat adjustments, window controls, and sunshades. These components can fail, resulting in the inability to adjust seats, operate windows, or control sunshades.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis and replacement of the faulty component or wiring.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $2,000+ per component
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**
The 2014 Rolls-Royce Ghost offers a luxurious and powerful driving experience, but it is essential to be aware of the potential issues. Common problems include engine oil leaks and turbocharger failures, transmission problems, air suspension malfunctions, electrical glitches, and automatic door closing system issues. The severity and cost of these repairs can be significant, so prospective buyers should factor in potential maintenance costs when considering this vehicle. A thorough pre-purchase inspection by a qualified Rolls-Royce mechanic is highly recommended.
