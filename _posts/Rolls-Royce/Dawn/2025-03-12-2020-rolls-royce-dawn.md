The 2020 Rolls-Royce Dawn is a luxury convertible known for its opulent interior, smooth ride, and powerful performance. It's powered by a 6.6-liter twin-turbo V12 engine producing 563 horsepower and 605 lb-ft of torque, paired with an 8-speed automatic transmission. Some owners appreciate the Dawn for its handcrafted interior, iconic styling, and effortless power. Common complaints often revolve around the high cost of ownership, occasional electronic glitches, and the vehicle's large size making it difficult to maneuver in tight spaces.

**Engine**

**6.6L Twin-Turbo V12**

*   **Oil Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks can occur from various engine seals and gaskets. Symptoms include visible oil drips under the car, a burning oil smell, and low oil levels.
    *   Solution: Replacing the faulty seals and gaskets is the typical fix.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $5,000+ (depending on the location and severity of the leak)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Coolant Leaks**
    * Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaks can occur from hoses, the water pump, or the radiator. Symptoms include a sweet smell, low coolant levels, and overheating.
    * Solution: Replacing the faulty hose, water pump, or radiator is the typical fix.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $3,000+ (depending on the location and severity of the leak)
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**8-Speed Automatic (ZF 8HP)**

*   **Rough Shifting**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported occasional rough or jerky shifting, particularly at lower speeds.
    *   Solution: In some cases, a software update can resolve the issue. In other instances, a transmission flush and fluid change may be necessary. In severe cases, valve body replacement may be required.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 (fluid change) - $4,000+ (valve body replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Delayed Engagement**
    *   Description/Symptoms: A delay between shifting into drive or reverse and the transmission actually engaging.
    *   Solution: Valve body issues may need to be addressed, or in some cases, a complete transmission replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $4,000 - $10,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Air Suspension Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The Dawn utilizes an air suspension system. Potential problems include air leaks, compressor failure, and ride height sensor malfunctions. Symptoms can manifest as a saggy ride height, a rough ride, or error messages on the dashboard.
    *   Solution: Replacing the faulty air spring, compressor, or ride height sensor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $5,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Glitches**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The infotainment system can experience glitches such as freezing, crashing, or unresponsive touch screen.
    *   Solution: Software updates are the initial step. In more severe cases, replacement of the head unit may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 (software update) - $5,000+ (head unit replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Battery Drain**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Excessive battery drain, particularly when the car is not in use, leading to a dead battery.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis of parasitic draw and repair of the affected circuit or component.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Convertible Top Malfunctions**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The convertible top mechanism can sometimes experience issues with opening or closing, including getting stuck.
    *   Solution: Inspection and repair of the hydraulic system, sensors, or motors associated with the top mechanism.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $5,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Power Window Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Intermittent operation or complete failure of the power windows.
    *   Solution: Replacing the window motor or regulator.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2020 Rolls-Royce Dawn, while a symbol of luxury, can experience several issues ranging from relatively minor electrical glitches to more significant engine and suspension problems. The cost of repair can be substantial, reflecting the high cost of parts and labor associated with the brand. Potential buyers should be aware of these potential issues and factor them into their ownership considerations. Regular maintenance and thorough inspections are crucial to mitigating potential problems.
