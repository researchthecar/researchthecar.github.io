**Introduction**

The 2006 Rolls-Royce Phantom is a luxurious sedan powered by a 6.75-liter V12 engine that produces 453 horsepower and 531 lb-ft of torque. It comes standard with an eight-speed automatic transmission. The Phantom is known for its opulent interior, elegant exterior, and powerful performance. However, like all vehicles, it has some reported issues.

**Engine**

* **V12**
    * **Valve Stem Seal Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: Leaking oil, smoking exhaust, and reduced engine performance
        * Solution: Replace valve stem seals
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,500 - $4,000
        * Recalls: None Found
    * **Cylinder Head Bolt Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: Engine overheating, coolant leaks, and loss of power
        * Solution: Replace cylinder head bolts
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $3,500 - $5,000
        * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

* **8-Speed Automatic**
    * **Torque Converter Shudder**
        * Description/Symptoms: Vibration or shuddering during acceleration
        * Solution: Replace torque converter
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500
        * Recalls: None Found
    * **Transmission Slipping**
        * Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears, slipping gears, and loss of power
        * Solution: Overhaul or replace transmission
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $4,000 - $6,000
        * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Front Air Suspension Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Sagging front end, uneven ride height, and reduced handling
    * Solution: Replace air suspension struts
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,000
        * Recalls: None Found
* **Rear Air Suspension Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Sagging rear end, uneven ride height, and reduced handling
    * Solution: Replace air suspension struts
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,000
        * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain**
    * Description/Symptoms: Rapid battery discharge, leading to starting problems
    * Solution: Identify and repair electrical drain source
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500
        * Recalls: None Found
* **Faulty HVAC Control Module**
    * Description/Symptoms: Malfunctioning climate control, inconsistent temperature, and fan speed issues
    * Solution: Replace HVAC control module
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,200
        * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Weatherstripping Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Water leaks, wind noise, and drafts
    * Solution: Replace weatherstripping
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
        * Recalls: None Found
* **Chrome Trim Corrosion**
    * Description/Symptoms: Pitting, rusting, or peeling of chrome trim
    * Solution: Replace or refinish chrome trim
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $600 - $1,200
        * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Switchgear Malfunction**
    * Description/Symptoms: Non-functioning switches, intermittent controls, and electrical glitches
    * Solution: Replace or repair affected switchgear
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
        * Recalls: None Found
* **Wood Trim Warping**
    * Description/Symptoms: Cracking, warping, or discoloration of wood trim
    * Solution: Replace or refinish wood trim
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,500
        * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2006 Rolls-Royce Phantom is a luxurious vehicle with a powerful engine and an opulent interior. However, it has some potential issues, particularly with its engine, transmission, and electrical systems. These issues can be costly to repair, but they are not typically severe enough to prevent the vehicle from being driven.
