The 2019 Rolls-Royce Phantom is the pinnacle of luxury automobiles, offering a serene and opulent driving experience. It is powered by a 6.75-liter twin-turbocharged V12 engine producing 563 horsepower and 664 lb-ft of torque. This engine is paired exclusively with an 8-speed automatic transmission. Owners appreciate the Phantom's smooth ride, handcrafted interior, and distinctive styling. Common dislikes often revolve around its immense size, limited practicality, and high maintenance costs.

**Engine**

**6.75L Twin-Turbocharged V12**

* **Oil Leaks**
    * Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks are found around the valve cover gaskets and oil pan gasket. Symptoms include visible oil puddles under the car, a burning oil smell, and low oil levels.
    * Solution: Replacing the leaking gaskets.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Coolant Leaks**
    * Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaks originating from hoses, radiator connections, or the water pump. Symptoms include a sweet coolant smell, low coolant levels, and potential engine overheating.
    * Solution: Replacing the leaking hoses, connections, or water pump.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**8-Speed Automatic**

* **Harsh Shifting**
    * Description/Symptoms: Experiencing harsh or jerky shifts between gears, especially at lower speeds.
    * Solution: Software update and potential transmission rebuild.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300-$500+ for software update, $5,000-$10,000+ for rebuild
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Air Suspension Malfunctions**
    * Description/Symptoms: The air suspension system may experience issues leading to uneven ride height, a stiff or bouncy ride, or the vehicle sitting too low. This can be due to faulty air struts, compressors, or ride height sensors.
    * Solution: Replacing the faulty components of the air suspension system, followed by a recalibration.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $5,000+ per strut; $1,000 - $3,000+ for compressor
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Infotainment System Glitches**
    * Description/Symptoms: Freezing, crashing, or slow response times of the infotainment system. Connectivity issues with Bluetooth or smartphone integration can also occur.
    * Solution: Software updates can resolve some issues, but hardware replacement may be necessary in severe cases.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 - $500+ for software updates; $1,000 - $5,000+ for hardware replacement
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Battery Drain**
    * Description/Symptoms: The vehicle's battery can drain quickly, especially if left unused for extended periods. This can be due to a parasitic draw from the electrical system or a failing battery.
    * Solution: Replacing the battery or diagnosing and addressing the parasitic draw.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+ for battery replacement; $200 - $1,000+ for parasitic draw diagnosis and repair
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Sensor Failures**
    * Description/Symptoms: Failure of various sensors, such as parking sensors, tire pressure monitoring system (TPMS) sensors, or oxygen sensors. Symptoms include warning lights on the dashboard or inaccurate readings.
    * Solution: Replacing the faulty sensors.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+ per sensor, depending on the type
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Self-Closing Door Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: The self-closing doors may fail to latch properly or experience issues with the motor and mechanism.
    * Solution: Repair or replacement of the door closing mechanism.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $4,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Trim Separation/Loose Panels**
    * Description/Symptoms: Separation of trim pieces or loose panels within the cabin, especially on the dashboard or door panels.
    * Solution: Reattaching or replacing the trim pieces/panels.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+ depending on severity and location

    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2019 Rolls-Royce Phantom presents a mix of luxury-related and mechanical issues. While not widespread, issues like oil leaks and air suspension malfunctions can be costly to repair due to the complexity and high-end components. Electrical glitches and self-closing door problems are also areas to be aware of. The severity of these issues can range from minor inconveniences to significant expenses, highlighting the importance of thorough inspections and proactive maintenance.
