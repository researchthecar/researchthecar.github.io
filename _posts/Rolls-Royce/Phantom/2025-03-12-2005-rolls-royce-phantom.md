The 2005 Rolls-Royce Phantom is a luxury sedan known for its opulent interior, stately presence, and smooth ride. It came standard with a 6.75-liter V12 engine producing 453 horsepower and 531 lb-ft of torque, paired with a 6-speed automatic transmission. Owners often praise the Phantom for its unparalleled comfort, exquisite craftsmanship, and head-turning styling. However, some common complaints revolve around its high maintenance costs, complex electronics, and considerable size, making it challenging to maneuver in tight spaces.

**Engine**

**6.75L V12**

*   **Oil Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks may develop from various engine seals and gaskets, resulting in oil spots under the car, low oil levels, and potential engine damage if not addressed.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the leaking seals and gaskets is required.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Valve Stem Seal Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Excessive oil consumption, blue smoke from the exhaust, especially upon startup.
    *   Solution: Replacement of valve stem seals.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $3,000 - $6,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**6-Speed Automatic**

*   **Rough Shifting**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Harsh or delayed shifting between gears, particularly noticeable during acceleration or deceleration.
    *   Solution: Transmission fluid flush and filter replacement, or valve body repair/replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $2,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Transmission Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Complete loss of drive, loud noises from the transmission, or inability to shift gears.
    *   Solution: Transmission rebuild or replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $5,000 - $10,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Air Suspension Compressor Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Vehicle sagging or uneven ride height, warning lights on the dashboard. The compressor struggles to maintain the correct air pressure in the system.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the air suspension compressor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Air Strut Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Hissing sounds from the suspension, vehicle leaning to one side, rough ride quality.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the leaking air struts.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+ per strut
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Malfunctions**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Display screen freezing, navigation system errors, audio system cutting out, or complete system failure.
    *   Solution: Software updates, component repair, or complete system replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $5,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Battery Drain Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Vehicle battery draining quickly, requiring frequent jump starts or battery replacement. Often caused by a parasitic draw from a faulty module.
    *   Solution: Diagnose and repair the source of the parasitic draw, battery replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Central Locking System Problems**
    * Description/Symptoms: Doors not locking or unlocking properly, remote key fob malfunctions, alarm system issues.
    * Solution: Repair or replacement of door lock actuators, key fob reprogramming, or alarm system module replacement.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Self-Closing Door Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Doors failing to close completely on their own, requiring manual assistance.
    *   Solution: Repair or replacement of the self-closing door mechanism.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $3,000+ per door
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Hood Ornament (Spirit of Ecstasy) Malfunctions**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Hood ornament failing to retract or extend properly, becoming stuck in either position.
    *   Solution: Repair or replacement of the hood ornament mechanism.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $4,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Navigation Screen Delamination**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The screen has a visible bubble or separation forming between the layers of the glass.
    *   Solution: Replace the screen or unit.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $3,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

The 2005 Rolls-Royce Phantom, while a pinnacle of luxury, can present owners with a range of issues. Electrical problems and air suspension issues tend to be prevalent and expensive to repair. Engine and transmission issues, while less common, can also lead to substantial repair bills. Addressing these potential problems promptly is essential to maintaining the vehicle's performance and value, but be prepared for significant maintenance costs.
