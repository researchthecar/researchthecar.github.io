**Introduction:**
The 2021 Rolls-Royce Phantom is the latest iteration of the iconic luxury sedan. It features a 6.75-liter twin-turbocharged V12 engine that produces 563 horsepower and 664 lb-ft of torque, mated to an 8-speed automatic transmission. The Phantom is praised for its opulent interior, refined driving experience, and advanced technology. However, despite its high price point, it has not been immune to some issues.

**Engine**
- **6.75L Twin-Turbocharged V12**
  - **Intake Valve Carbon Buildup**
    Description/Symptoms: Excessive carbon buildup on intake valves can lead to reduced engine performance, misfires, and rough idling.
    Solution: Professional cleaning of intake valves and decarbonization of engine components.
    Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
  - **Turbocharger Failure**
    Description/Symptoms: Malfunctioning turbochargers can cause reduced boost pressure, decreased engine power, and excessive exhaust smoke.
    Solution: Replacement of faulty turbocharger(s).
    Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,500 - $5,000+
  - **Oil Leaks**
    Description/Symptoms: Leaks can occur from various areas, including rear main seal, valve cover gaskets, and oil pan. Symptoms include visible oil stains, low oil levels, and potential engine damage if left unchecked.
    Solution: Identification and replacement of faulty gaskets, seals, or components causing the leaks.
    Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,200+

**Transmission**
- **8-Speed Automatic**
  - **Slipping Gears**
    Description/Symptoms: Transmission may experience difficulty engaging or staying in gear, leading to erratic shifting, hesitation, and potential loss of power.
    Solution: Transmission fluid change, reprogramming of transmission control module, or replacement of worn or damaged components.
    Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,500+
  - **Shift Lock Solenoid Failure**
    Description/Symptoms: Faulty solenoid prevents the transmission from shifting out of park, resulting in the vehicle being unable to move.
    Solution: Replacement of shift lock solenoid.
    Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
  - **Transmission Cooler Line Leaks**
    Description/Symptoms: Leaks in transmission cooler lines can lead to fluid loss, overheating, and potential transmission damage.
    Solution: Inspection and replacement of leaking lines.
    Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600+

**Suspension**
  - **Air Suspension Malfunctions**
    Description/Symptoms: Air suspension may fail to provide proper ride height, resulting in uneven vehicle stance, compromised ride quality, and potential tire wear.
    Solution: Diagnosis and repair of faulty air suspension components, such as air springs, compressors, or sensors.
    Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,500+

**Electrical**
  - **Battery Drain**
    Description/Symptoms: Vehicle battery may drain prematurely, resulting in difficulty starting or reduced electrical functionality.
    Solution: Inspection and testing to identify electrical system parasitic drain, followed by repair or replacement of faulty components.
    Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
  - **Infotainment System Glitches**
    Description/Symptoms: Multimedia system may experience random reboots, frozen screens, or connectivity issues.
    Solution: Software updates, hardware module replacements, or repairs to faulty components within the infotainment system.
    Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+

**Exterior**
  - **Door Handle Sticking**
    Description/Symptoms: Door handles may become difficult to operate, requiring excessive force or multiple attempts to open doors.
    Solution: Adjustment, lubrication, or replacement of door handle assemblies.
    Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
  - **Windshield Washer Fluid Leak**
    Description/Symptoms: Leaks in windshield washer fluid system can result in fluid loss and reduced visibility.
    Solution: Identification and repair/replacement of leaking hoses, nozzles, or reservoir.
    Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+

**Interior**
  - **Rear Seat Entertainment System Issues**
    Description/Symptoms: Rear seat entertainment screens may experience flickering, freezing, or lack of functionality.
    Solution: Software updates, hardware repairs, or replacement of faulty components within the rear seat entertainment system.
    Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+
  - **Panoramic Sunroof Malfunctions**
    Description/Symptoms: Panoramic sunroof may fail to open, close, or tilt properly, causing leaks or wind noise.
    Solution: Diagnosis and repair of sunroof motor, gears, or control modules.
    Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,500+

**Summary:**
While the 2021 Rolls-Royce Phantom is a luxurious and impressive vehicle, it is not immune to a range of potential issues. Engine problems, transmission malfunctions, and electrical glitches can be costly to repair, and certain interior and exterior components have also been reported to have issues. Prospective buyers should carefully consider the potential costs associated with these issues before making a purchase decision.
