**Introduction:**

The 2007 Rolls-Royce Phantom is a flagship luxury sedan known for its opulent design, powerful engine, and refined cabin. The Phantom is powered by a 6.75-liter V12 engine mated to a 6-speed automatic transmission, producing 453 horsepower and 531 lb-ft of torque. It is praised for its unparalleled craftsmanship, smooth ride, and luxurious amenities, but it also has some notable issues that potential buyers should consider.

**Engine Issues:**

* **V12 Engine**
    * **Leaking Intake Manifold Gaskets:**
        * Description/Symptoms: Oil or coolant leaks from the intake manifold gaskets, causing a drop in engine performance and potential overheating.
        * Solution: Replace the faulty gaskets.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * **Throttle Position Sensor Malfunction:**
        * Description/Symptoms: Rough idling, stalling, and hesitation during acceleration caused by a faulty throttle position sensor providing incorrect readings to the engine computer.
        * Solution: Replace the throttle position sensor.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
**Transmission Issues**

* **6-Speed Automatic Transmission:**
    * **Transmission Slippage:**
        * Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears, delayed gear engagement, or sudden loss of power while driving due to worn or damaged clutches or torque converter.
        * Solution: Rebuild or replace the transmission.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,500 - $5,000+
    * **TCM Software Update:**
        * Description/Symptoms: Delayed gear shifts, harsh shifting, or transmission overheating due to outdated transmission control module (TCM) software.
        * Solution: Update the TCM software to the latest version.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300

**Suspension Issues**

* **Air Suspension:**
    * **Air Suspension Compressor Failure:**
        * Description/Symptoms: Vehicle sagging or uneven ride height due to a failing air suspension compressor that is unable to maintain the proper pressure in the air suspension system.
        * Solution: Replace the air suspension compressor.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,200 - $1,800+
    * **Air Suspension Strut Leaks:**
        * Description/Symptoms: Sagging or uneven ride height caused by leaks in the air suspension struts, allowing air to escape.
        * Solution: Replace the leaking struts.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,500+ per strut
**Electrical Issues**

* **Battery Drain:**
        * Description/Symptoms: Vehicle battery drains prematurely or fails to hold a charge, even when the vehicle is not in use.
        * Solution: Diagnose and repair electrical system components responsible for the battery drain, such as alternators, batteries, or parasitic electrical draws.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the root cause
* **Electrical Control Unit (ECU) Issues:**
        * Description/Symptoms: Various electrical system malfunctions, such as flickering lights, faulty interior controls, or engine performance problems caused by faulty ECUs.
        * Solution: Replace the affected ECU.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+ per ECU
**Exterior Issues**

* **Trunk Lid Release:**
        * Description/Symptoms: Inability to open the trunk due to a faulty trunk release mechanism or broken cables.
        * Solution: Replace the trunk release mechanism or cables.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600
**Interior Issues**

* **Interior Trim Panel Squeaks and Rattles:**
        * Description/Symptoms: Noises and vibrations coming from loose or poorly fitting interior trim panels.
        * Solution: Tighten or replace the trim panels.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $400
* **Sunroof Drainage Issues:**
        * Description/Symptoms: Water leaks into the cabin through the sunroof due to clogged drainage tubes or seals.
        * Solution: Clear the clogged drainage tubes or replace the seals.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600
**Recalls**

* 16V661 - Steering Column Shaft Bolt May Loosen
* 16V628 - Air Bag Control Unit Fault
* 17V317 - Air Bag Control Unit Connector Fault
* 17V323 - Sunroof Control Unit Electrical Fault
* 17V405 - Brake Pedal Deployment Lag
* 17V522 - Fuel Leakage During Fuel Fill

**Summary**

The 2007 Rolls-Royce Phantom is a luxurious and sophisticated vehicle, but it does have some reliability issues that potential buyers should be aware of. The engine, transmission, and suspension systems are prone to some common problems that can be expensive to repair. The electrical system and interior also have some issues, which can add to the overall cost of ownership. Overall, the severity and cost of the issues can vary depending on the specific problem and the extent of the repairs needed.
