The 2000 Rolls-Royce Corniche is a luxurious convertible, powered by a 6.75-liter V8 engine producing approximately 325 horsepower and 465 lb-ft of torque. It comes exclusively with a GM 4L80E four-speed automatic transmission. Owners often praise its opulent interior, smooth ride, and distinctive styling. Common criticisms include its high maintenance costs and susceptibility to electrical issues.

**Engine**

**6.75L V8**

*   **Oil Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks are common, often originating from valve cover gaskets, oil pan gasket, or rear main seal. Symptoms include visible oil drips, low oil level, and a burning oil smell.
    *   Solution: Replace the leaking gasket or seal.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,500+, depending on the location and complexity of the repair.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Cooling System Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Overheating can occur due to radiator leaks, thermostat failure, or water pump issues.
    *   Solution: Inspect and replace faulty components such as the radiator, thermostat, or water pump.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+, depending on the components that need to be replaced.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**GM 4L80E Four-Speed Automatic**

*   **Erratic Shifting/Slipping**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The 4L80E transmission can experience erratic shifting, slipping between gears, or delayed engagement.
    *   Solution: A transmission rebuild or replacement may be necessary. Inspect and replace worn solenoids.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,500 - $5,000+ for rebuild or replacement.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Torque Converter Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: Failure of the torque converter can cause stalling, vibrations, and poor performance.
    * Solution: Replace the torque converter.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Air Suspension Problems**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The air suspension system is prone to leaks and compressor failure, leading to a saggy ride height, uneven stance, and a rough ride.
    *   Solution: Replace faulty air struts, air springs, or the air suspension compressor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $6,000+, depending on the components replaced.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Electrical System Malfunctions**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Various electrical issues can arise, including malfunctioning power windows, door locks, and lights. Faulty wiring harnesses and corroded connections can be the source.
    *   Solution: Diagnose the specific issue and repair or replace faulty wiring or components.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $2,000+, depending on the complexity of the issue.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **ABS/Brake System Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: ABS sensor failures and brake accumulator problems can trigger warning lights and affect braking performance.
    *   Solution: Replace faulty ABS sensors or repair/replace the brake accumulator.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $2,000+, depending on the specific repair needed.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Convertible Top Malfunctions**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The convertible top mechanism can experience issues, including slow operation, failure to open or close completely, or leaks.
    *   Solution: Repair or replace hydraulic lines, motors, or sensors associated with the convertible top mechanism.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $4,000+, depending on the complexity of the repair.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Climate Control Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Malfunctioning air conditioning, heating, or ventilation systems due to compressor failure or faulty servo motors.
    *   Solution: Replace faulty components like the compressor, heater core, or servo motors.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,500+, depending on the components needing replacement.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2000 Rolls-Royce Corniche presents a blend of luxury and potential maintenance concerns. Owners should be prepared for potential issues with the air suspension, convertible top mechanism, and electrical systems. Engine and transmission issues are also common, with oil leaks and erratic shifting being notable concerns. The cost of repairs can be significant, reflecting the premium nature of the vehicle and its parts. Thorough inspection and preventative maintenance are crucial for minimizing potential problems and ensuring a pleasurable ownership experience.
