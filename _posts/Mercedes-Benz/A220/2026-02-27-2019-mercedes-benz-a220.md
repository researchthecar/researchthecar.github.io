# 2019 Mercedes-Benz A220 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used Mercedes-Benz A220, buyers are treated to a luxury compact sedan that packs advanced technology, modern styling, and an engaging drive into an entry-level package. While it delivers impressive cabin refinement and cutting-edge digital displays, potential owners should look closely at historical owner feedback. Reported areas of concern range from sporadic electrical and MBUX infotainment screen glitches to mechanical components like fuel system performance and cooling hardware. Conducting a thorough pre-purchase inspection can help uncover any lingering issues before finalizing your purchase.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4 (M260)

The 2.0-liter turbocharged inline-4 engine (M260) produces roughly 188 horsepower and 221 lb-ft of torque, providing brisk acceleration for daily driving. It balances everyday efficiency with turbocharged performance, though buyers should monitor cooling and fuel delivery components for longevity.

**Fuel System Stalling and Rough Idling**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have reported unexpected engine stalling, rough idling, and engine shaking or vibrating while operating the vehicle.
- **Solution:** Diagnose the fuel delivery components and replace faulty fuel pumps or pressure regulators as needed.

**Thermostat Failure and Overheating**
- **Symptoms:** The engine may overheat unexpectedly due to a malfunctioning thermostat during normal driving conditions.
- **Solution:** Inspect the cooling system and replace the failed thermostat and associated coolant components.
- **Cost:** Variable depending on parts and labor

## Transmission

### 7-Speed Dual-Clutch Automatic Transmission

The 7-speed dual-clutch automatic transmission delivers rapid, smooth gear changes paired with front-wheel drive or 4MATIC all-wheel drive configurations. It offers responsive shifting characteristics, though routine fluid maintenance is essential for sustained reliability.

## Electrical

### MBUX Infotainment and Driver Displays

The vehicle's electrical architecture centers around sophisticated dual digital displays and advanced cabin tech, which generally operate smoothly but can occasionally suffer from hardware blackouts.

**Black Screen and Infotainment Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** The digital instrument cluster or central MBUX screens can go completely black, leaving the driver unable to see speed, fuel levels, or navigation.
- **Solution:** Have a certified technician diagnose the MBUX hard disk drive or instrument display modules for potential software resets or hardware replacement.
- **Cost:** Up to $2000 for screen replacement

## Exterior

### Glass and Windshields

Exterior panels and glass provide sleek styling, though rare instances of structural glass stress have been documented by owners.

**Spontaneous Glass Fractures**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have experienced sudden cracking or shattering of the windshield or rear glass, sometimes accompanied by a loud pop when activating window or rear defrost features.
- **Solution:** Inspect affected glass for stress points and replace the windshield or rear glass panel.

## Interior

### Sunroof and Sunshade Mechanism

Interior appointments offer luxury aesthetics and premium materials, though mechanical components like the sunroof shade require careful handling.

**Stuck or Bunching Roller Shade**
- **Symptoms:** The fabric roller shade for the panoramic sunroof can get stuck midway, bunch up, or fail to retract properly due to fragile rolling hardware.
- **Solution:** Inspect the sunroof track mechanism and replace the sunshade assembly.
- **Cost:** $200 diagnostic fee plus repair costs

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V046000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=A220&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** In certain operating conditions, the rearview camera software may cause a delay in displaying the rearview camera image. As such, these vehicles fail to comply to Federal Motor Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 111, "Rearview Mirrors." A delayed rearview camera display can reduce the driver's view of what is behind the car, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** MBUSA will notify owners, and dealers will update the rearview camera software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V990000: Suspension:Front](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=A220&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** Front axle carrier failure may result in a loss of steering control, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the integral carrier, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V416000: Electrical System](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=A220&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** The air conditioning condensation drain hose may not be installed properly. Water entering electrical components, can result in malfunctions of the supplemental restraint system (SRS), failure to start, activation of the limp-home mode while driving, and an impairment of other features, increasing the risk of injury or crash.
- **What to do:** MBUSA will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the air conditioning drain hose installation, correcting it if necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V753000: Tires](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=A220&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** Certain labels containing important information such as tire pressure may not have been installed. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 110, "Tire Selection and Rims." If the tires are inflated to an incorrect tire pressure, the tires may be overloaded, and increase the risk of.
- **What to do:** MBUSA will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the vehicle for the labels and apply them, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V314000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Pedals And Linkages](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=A220&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** Impaired operation of the brake pedal may increase the risk of crash.
- **What to do:** MBUSA will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the welding of the brake pedal, replacing it as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V732000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Foundation Components:Disc:Pads](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=A220&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** The vehicles may not be equipped with an inspection gauge to check the wear of the rear brake pads. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 135, "Light Vehicle Brake Systems." Driving with worn brake pads can reduce braking ability, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will install an inspection gauge and provide supplemental owner's manual information, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The Mercedes-Benz A220 combines luxury appeal and advanced technology, making it an attractive pre-owned compact sedan. While most mechanical systems perform reliably, buyers should verify the proper operation of the MBUX digital displays, sunroof shade mechanisms, and cooling components during a test drive. Paying close attention to service history and ensuring a comprehensive pre-owned inspection will help secure a dependable and satisfying ownership experience.
