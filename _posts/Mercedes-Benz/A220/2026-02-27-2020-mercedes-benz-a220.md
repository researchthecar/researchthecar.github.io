# 2020 Mercedes-Benz A220 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2020 Mercedes-Benz A220, buyers will find a stylish and technologically advanced compact luxury sedan. However, owner reports and manufacturer campaigns indicate that prospective buyers should thoroughly review service histories and ensure all safety recalls have been properly completed before purchasing.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4

The 2.0-liter turbocharged inline-4 engine produces approximately 188 horsepower and 221 lb-ft of torque, offering lively performance for daily driving. While generally punchy and refined, buyers should watch for isolated reports of major mechanical failures and fuel system concerns.

**Fuel Injector Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Fuel leaking from the fuel injectors, potentially causing rough running and fire risks.
- **Solution:** Inspect fuel injectors and replace faulty components as necessary.

**Sudden Power Loss**
- **Symptoms:** Sudden loss of engine power while driving at highway speeds with no prior warning.
- **Solution:** Have the vehicle thoroughly diagnosed by a qualified technician; in severe cases, full engine replacement may be required.

## Transmission

### 7-Speed Dual-Clutch Automatic Transmission

The 7-speed dual-clutch automatic transmission delivers quick and seamless gear changes across front-wheel and 4MATIC all-wheel-drive configurations. Overall reliability is generally acceptable, though occasional electronic and software communication glitches have been reported by owners.

**Transmission Control Communication Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Digital instrument panel shutting off, warning messages displaying, and failure to start due to communication loss.
- **Solution:** Have the transmission control module and software diagnosed and updated or replaced by a dealership.

## Steering

### Steering Column and Mechanism

Steering complaints are relatively minor but worth noting during a pre-purchase test drive.

**Steering Noise and Roughness**
- **Symptoms:** Popping or clicking noises when turning the steering wheel, which can worsen in cold weather and contribute to rough driving dynamics.
- **Solution:** Inspect the steering column and intermediate shaft for wear or binding, and replace components as required.

## Electrical

### Infotainment and Remote Start Electronics

Electrical architecture is complex and heavily integrated into vehicle functions, requiring careful verification of proper operation.

**Remote Start Fire Hazard**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle catching fire shortly after utilizing the factory-installed remote start feature via the mobile application.
- **Solution:** Ensure all relevant software updates and safety campaigns pertaining to electrical systems are completed.

### Back-Up Camera

Driver assistance and safety features generally perform well, though backup camera reliability has been a recurring point of owner frustration.

**Rear View Camera Blackout**
- **Symptoms:** The backup camera display goes completely black when shifting into reverse, impeding visibility.
- **Solution:** Perform software updates or replace the camera hardware as specified by manufacturer service guidelines.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V990000: Suspension:Front](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=A220&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** Front axle carrier failure may result in a loss of steering control, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the integral carrier, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V732000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Foundation Components:Disc:Pads](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=A220&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** The vehicles may not be equipped with an inspection gauge to check the wear of the rear brake pads. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 135, "Light Vehicle Brake Systems." Driving with worn brake pads can reduce braking ability, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will install an inspection gauge and provide supplemental owner's manual information, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2020 Mercedes-Benz A220 provides an upscale cabin and engaging driving dynamics, but potential buyers must proceed with caution. Given reports concerning fuel system integrity, electrical anomalies, and rearview camera performance, a comprehensive pre-purchase inspection and verification of completed recall service are strongly recommended.
