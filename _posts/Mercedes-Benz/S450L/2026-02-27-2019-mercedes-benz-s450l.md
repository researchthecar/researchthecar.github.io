# 2019 Mercedes-Benz S450L Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2019 Mercedes-Benz S-Class represents the pinnacle of luxury, offering advanced technology, supreme comfort, and refined performance. As a high-end flagship sedan, prospective used-car buyers should consider that while major mechanical failures are relatively uncommon, the vehicle's complex electronics, advanced driver-assistance systems, and sophisticated powertrain components can occasionally lead to frustrating owner complaints. Thorough pre-purchase inspections and a careful review of service history are recommended when shopping for a used model.

---

## Engine

### 3.0L Twin-Turbo V6 (M276)

The 3.0-liter twin-turbocharged V6 engine produces approximately 362 horsepower and 369 lb-ft of torque, providing smooth and responsive power for the S-Class. Overall, this powerplant has a reputation for solid mechanical durability when properly maintained.

### 4.0L Twin-Turbo V8 (M175/M176)

The 4.0-liter biturbo V8 delivers robust performance with around 463 horsepower and 516 lb-ft of torque. It is generally robust, though regular high-grade fluid changes and attentive servicing are vital to ensure long-term reliability.

## Transmission

### 9G-TRONIC 9-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 9G-TRONIC 9-speed automatic transmission routes power seamlessly to the rear wheels or 4MATIC all-wheel-drive system. It offers refined gear shifts and contributes to highway fuel efficiency, though software updates are occasionally required to maintain optimal shift quality.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

The braking system provides strong stopping power for a heavy luxury sedan, but owners have reported isolated hydraulic and electronic actuator concerns.

**Rear Brake Actuator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The rear brake actuator may seize and become completely inoperable, preventing normal parking brake or braking functions.
- **Solution:** Have the vehicle towed to a qualified facility or dealership to inspect and replace the failed rear brake actuator assembly.

**Soft Brake Pedal Feel**
- **Symptoms:** Brakes may feel weak or unresponsive, occasionally requiring the driver to double-pump the pedal to bring the vehicle to a stop.
- **Solution:** Inspect the brake hydraulic system for air or leaks, bleed the brake lines, and replace worn master cylinders or pads if necessary.

## Steering

### Steering Column

Steering control remains sharp and responsive, though power adjustment mechanisms in the column can occasionally bind.

**Steering Column Adjustment Stickiness**
- **Symptoms:** The power-adjustable steering column regularly sticks in the full upward position and resists normal readjustment.
- **Solution:** Inspect the steering column adjustment motor and mechanisms, and lubricate or replace components as needed.

## Safety

### Seat Belts and Pre-tensioners

Advanced safety features generally offer high levels of occupant protection, though sensitive collision-preparation sensors can occasionally misfire.

**Inadvertent Seat Belt Tightening and Rear Movement**
- **Symptoms:** Seat belts may unexpectedly tighten as if preparing for an impact, while the rear of the vehicle may feel unstable or move side to side at highway speeds.
- **Solution:** Have the vehicle's electronic safety sensors, stability control modules, and seat belt pre-tensioner systems diagnosed by a professional.

## Summary Verdict

The 2019 Mercedes-Benz S-Class delivers an exceptional driving and ownership experience defined by supreme luxury and advanced engineering.
