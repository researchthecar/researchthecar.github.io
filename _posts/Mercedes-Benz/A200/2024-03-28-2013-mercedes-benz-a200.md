**Introduction**

The 2013 Mercedes-Benz A200 is a compact luxury hatchback powered by a 1.6L turbocharged inline-4 engine rated at 155 horsepower and 184 lb-ft of torque. It is available with either a 6-speed manual or 7-speed automated manual transmission. The A200 is known for its stylish design, premium interior, and agile handling, but it has also faced several issues.

**Engine**

**1.6L Turbocharged I-4**

**Fuel Pump Failure**
Description/Symptoms: Engine misfires, hesitates, or stalls during driving.
Solution: Replace fuel pump.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,200+
Recalls: None Found

**Ignition Coil Failure**
Description/Symptoms: Hard starting, misfires, loss of power.
Solution: Replace ignition coil.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**6-Speed Manual**

**Clutch Problems**
Description/Symptoms: Slipping, chattering, or difficulty shifting gears.
Solution: Replace clutch.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,500+
Recalls: None Found

**7-Speed Automated Manual**

**Transmission Control Module (TCM) Failure**
Description/Symptoms: Transmission jerks, hesitates, or shifts erratically.
Solution: Replace TCM.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $1,500+
Recalls: MB203152

**Electrical**

**Rear Sam Unit Failure**
Description/Symptoms: Electrical issues with taillights, brake lights, or trunk release.
Solution: Replace rear SAM unit.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800+
Recalls: MB203154

**Alternator Problems**
Description/Symptoms: Battery does not charge or discharges prematurely.
Solution: Replace alternator.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800+
Recalls: MB190013

**Suspension**

**Strut Mount Bearing Failure**
Description/Symptoms: Clunking or rattling noises from front suspension.
Solution: Replace strut mount bearings.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

**Sunroof Drainage Clogs**
Description/Symptoms: Water leaking into the cabin through the sunroof.
Solution: Clean or replace sunroof drains.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+
Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

**Radio Malfunction**
Description/Symptoms: Radio turning off or not responding to commands.
Solution: Software update or radio replacement.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2013 Mercedes-Benz A200 has faced a range of issues, including engine problems, transmission failures, and electrical glitches. While some issues are relatively minor and inexpensive to fix, others can be more severe and costly. Overall, the A200 has a moderate reliability rating, with issues that may affect its drivability, comfort, and convenience.
