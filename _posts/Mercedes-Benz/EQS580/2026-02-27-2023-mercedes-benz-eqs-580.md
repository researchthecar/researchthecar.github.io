# 2023 Mercedes-Benz EQS 580 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

Shopping for a used 2023 Mercedes-Benz EQS 580 offers buyers an entry into high-end luxury electric driving, featuring advanced technology and robust dual-motor performance. While early owner reports for this flagship EV remain relatively sparse, prospective buyers should carefully evaluate available models through comprehensive test drives and pre-purchase inspections to ensure all high-voltage and electronic systems operate as intended.

---

## Engine

### Dual Permanently Excited Synchronous Electric Motors

The EQS 580 features dual permanently excited synchronous electric motors producing an estimated 536 horsepower and 633 lb-ft of torque, providing standard 4MATIC all-wheel drive. This powertrain delivers effortless, silent acceleration paired with impressive long-term electric motor durability.

## Transmission

### Single-Speed Fixed Gear Transmission

Power is transferred to the wheels via a single-speed fixed-gear automatic transmission tailored specifically for high-output electric vehicle applications. This direct-drive setup operates seamlessly without traditional gear shifting, resulting in minimal mechanical complexity.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V115000: Electrical System:Wiring:Fuses And Circuit Breakers](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=EQS+580&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** An 80-Amp fuse that fails can result in a sudden loss of drive power or a loss of critical safety functions such as the restraint systems or instrument cluster, increasing the risk of a crash or injury. Additionally, there could be an increased risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the main fuse boxes, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.
