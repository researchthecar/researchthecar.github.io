# 2019 Mercedes-Benz CLS53 AMG S Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2019 Mercedes-Benz CLS53 AMG delivers striking styling and potent performance, but used-car shoppers should review owner feedback carefully. Reports point to a few recurring mechanical and electrical concerns that prospective buyers should inspect closely before finalizing a purchase.

---

## Engine

### 3.0L Turbocharged Inline-6 with EQ Boost

Equipped with a 3.0-liter turbocharged inline-6 featuring EQ Boost hybrid technology, producing around 429 horsepower. This sophisticated powerplant offers brisk acceleration but relies heavily on complex electronics.

**Wiring Harness and Electrical Failures**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle shaking, check engine lights, and unexpected electrical wiring harness degradation reported by owners.
- **Solution:** Inspect wiring harnesses and have a qualified technician diagnose any electrical shaking or fault codes.
- **Cost:** $6,000

## Transmission

### AMG Speedshift TCT 9-Speed Automatic

Paired with a 9-speed automatic transmission designed to deliver quick gear changes across all driving modes. It generally operates smoothly, though overall reliability depends on proper maintenance and software updates.

## Suspension

### Wheels and Tires

Suspension and wheel components provide sharp handling, but low-profile tires mounted on large alloy wheels are prone to impact damage.

**Rim Cracking and Tire Blowouts**
- **Symptoms:** Repeated tire blowouts, low tire pressure warnings, and cracked wheels after driving over minor road bumps and potholes.
- **Solution:** Inspect alloy wheels for structural cracks and check tire condition regularly, especially on low-profile setups.

## Electrical

### 48V Battery and Charging System

Electrical architecture handles advanced infotainment and mild-hybrid systems, though battery-related glitches have been noted by owners.

**48V Battery Malfunction and Starter Battery Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Dashboard warnings for a 48V battery malfunction, loss of motive power, and starter battery failure within early ownership periods.
- **Solution:** Have the 48V hybrid battery system and starter battery tested for proper charge retention and software updates.

When shopping for a used Mercedes-Benz CLS53 AMG, ensure a thorough pre-purchase inspection is performed with a focus on the 48V electrical system, wheels, and wiring harnesses.
