# 2023 Mercedes-Benz E53 AMG Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2023 Mercedes-Benz E53 AMG delivers thrilling performance and luxury, but prospective used-car buyers should review owner-reported issues. While most vehicles operate reliably, NHTSA complaints highlight potential concerns regarding sudden engine stalling, unexpected transmission behavior, and tire vulnerability on low-profile setups.

---

## Engine

### 3.0L Turbocharged Inline-6 with EQ Boost

The 3.0-liter turbocharged inline-6 engine features an integrated electric auxiliary compressor and EQ Boost mild-hybrid system, generating around 429 horsepower. It provides rapid acceleration and refined power delivery, though complex electrical and hybrid components require attentive maintenance.

**Engine Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have reported unexpected engine stalls while navigating turns or coming to a stop, occasionally accompanied by warning lights or a complete engine seizure.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified technician diagnose electronic throttle body, fuel delivery, or 48V mild-hybrid system faults.

## Transmission

### AMG Speedshift TCT 9-Speed Automatic

The AMG Speedshift TCT 9-speed automatic transmission delivers quick, responsive gear changes across various driving modes. It pairs smoothly with the high-output inline-6 engine, but software calibration is critical for long-term shift quality.

**Unintended Shifting to Neutral**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have experienced the vehicle unexpectedly shifting from drive into neutral while moving at moderate speeds without driver input.
- **Solution:** Inspect transmission control module software and gear selector linkages for electronic or mechanical glitches.

## Suspension

### Factory Tires and Wheels

Original factory wheel and tire combinations feature low-profile performance tires designed for sharp handling, though they remain susceptible to impact damage on rough pavement.

**Frequent Tire Blowouts from Potholes**
- **Symptoms:** Low-profile original factory tires prone to immediate sidewall blowouts and rapid air loss when driving over minor road irregularities and potholes.
- **Solution:** Inspect tires regularly for bulges or damage, and consider replacing factory run-flat tires with conventional tires if road conditions demand higher durability.

## Brakes

### Rear Brake Caliper Brackets

High-performance braking systems provide strong stopping power suited for AMG models, though routine inspection of pads, rotors, and calipers is essential to prevent uneven wear.

**Uneven Brake Pad Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Premature metal-on-metal brake wear observed on one side of the vehicle while opposite brake pads retain significant life.
- **Solution:** Inspect brake caliper sliding pins and mounting brackets for binding or manufacturing defects, and replace worn brake components as necessary.

## Electrical

### Driver Assistance Systems

Advanced safety and driver assistance electronics offer robust protection and convenience features, though camera and radar sensors can occasionally trigger false warnings or require recalibration.

**Active Lane Keeping and Emergency Braking Faults**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers experience sudden dashboard warnings indicating that active lane keeping or emergency braking systems are temporarily inoperable.
- **Solution:** Clean exterior sensors and camera lenses; have dealership technicians check for software updates or sensor calibration issues.

## Safety

### Power Trunk Lid

The power-operated trunk lid adds everyday convenience but relies on electronic sensors and motorized struts that can occasionally malfunction.

**Unexpected Trunk Closure**
- **Symptoms:** The power trunk lid can close unexpectedly without manual activation, posing a potential safety hazard while loading cargo.
- **Solution:** Inspect trunk latch sensors, struts, and control modules for proper operation at a service center.
