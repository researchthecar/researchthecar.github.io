# 2017 Mercedes-Benz GLE400 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a pre-owned 2017 Mercedes-Benz GLE400, buyers will find a luxury midsize SUV that blends refined road manners with robust powertrain options. However, as with many luxury European vehicles, prospective owners should be mindful of potential electrical issues, steering assist concerns, and occasional reports regarding exterior lighting or structural components like the panoramic sunroof.

---

## Engine

### 3.0L Twin-Turbo V6 (M276)

The 3.0-liter twin-turbocharged V6 engine produces approximately 329 horsepower and 354 lb-ft of torque, offering brisk and smooth acceleration. It is generally regarded as a robust powerplant, though routine maintenance and cooling system checks are vital as these vehicles age.

## Transmission

### 9-Speed Automatic Transmission (9G-TRONIC)

This 9-speed automatic transmission provides seamless gear changes and helps optimize highway fuel economy. Regular fluid maintenance is recommended to ensure long-term shift quality and reliability.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

Brake complaints highlight occasional irregular pedal feel or holding behavior requiring professional inspection.

**Brake System Hesitation and Holding Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have reported unexpected vehicle movement while the hold-brake function is engaged or minor pedal hesitation during application.
- **Solution:** Have the brake booster, master cylinder, and electronic parking/hold brake sensors inspected by a qualified technician.

## Steering

### Electronic Power Steering

Issues related to electronic power assist loss have been reported by owners, temporarily reducing steering responsiveness.

**Steering Assist Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may experience sudden stiffness in the steering wheel accompanied by a 'Power Steering Malfunction' message on the instrument cluster, making the vehicle difficult to maneuver.
- **Solution:** Requires diagnostic scanning of the power steering control module and potential replacement of failing electronic steering components.

## Exterior

### Exterior Lighting

Lighting complaints center mostly on halogen or low-beam adjustment and overall night-driving visibility.

**Headlight Performance and Adjustment Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Owners note inadequate illumination during nighttime or adverse weather driving conditions, along with low-beam headlights failing to adjust properly.
- **Solution:** Inspect headlight assemblies, bulbs, and leveling sensors; replace damaged components or upgraded bulbs where permitted.

## Safety

### Seat Belts

Seat belt sensor warnings can cause persistent dashboard alerts requiring sensor or buckle replacement.

**Seat Belt Warning Light and Sensor Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Illumination of the passenger seat belt warning light or chime activation despite the occupant being properly buckled.
- **Solution:** Inspect and replace faulty seat belt buckle sensors or wiring harnesses.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V058000: Electrical System:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=GLE400&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** The software design of the communication module may fail to communicate the correct vehicle location for the emergency call system (eCall) in the event of a crash. Failure of the eCall system may result in emergency responders being dispatched to the wrong location, increasing the risk of injury following a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 17V816000: Latches/Locks/Linkages:Doors:Latch](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=GLE400&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** The rear passenger door on the driver side may indicate that it is locked, when it may not be. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 206, "Door Locks and Door Retention Components." If the door appears to be locked, the door may be unintentionally opened, increasing the risk of.
- **What to do:** MBUSA will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the left rear door lock, replacing it as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V955000: Fuel System, Diesel:Delivery:Fuel Pump:Control/Drive Module](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=GLE400&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** Water might accumulate in the spare tire wheel well and damage the fuel pump control unit, which could cause an engine stall while driving. An engine stall while driving increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will install a water drain plug, inspect for water intrusion, and replace the fuel pump control unit as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2017 Mercedes-Benz GLE400 offers an appealing blend of luxury and V6 performance, making it an enjoyable daily driver. Prospective buyers should conduct a thorough pre-purchase inspection focusing on the electronic power steering, brake systems, and electrical features to ensure everything operates smoothly.
