# 2018 Mercedes-Benz GLE400 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When evaluating a used Mercedes-Benz, prospective buyers should review common owner-reported complaints regarding powertrain, electrical, and safety systems. While luxury and performance are hallmarks of the brand, monitoring specific mechanical and electronic subsystems helps ensure long-term reliability and safe operation.

---

## Engine

### 3.0L Twin-Turbo V6

A 3.0-liter twin-turbocharged V6 engine producing approximately 329 horsepower and 354 lb-ft of torque, offering strong acceleration and passing power. Routine maintenance is crucial to prevent auxiliary drive and fuel delivery complications.

**Fuel Pump Failure and Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may experience hesitation during acceleration or unexpected engine stalling while driving or turning, creating dangerous traffic situations.
- **Solution:** Inspect the fuel delivery system and replace the failing fuel pump.

## Transmission

### 9-Speed Automatic Transmission

A 9-speed automatic transmission designed to optimize highway fuel economy and smooth power delivery across various driving modes. Buyers should thoroughly test gear engagement during a pre-purchase inspection.

**Shifter and Gear Engagement Defects**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may experience difficulty moving the gear selector out of park, or the vehicle may unexpectedly slip from drive into neutral while operating.
- **Solution:** Diagnose the electronic shifter module or transmission control unit and replace faulty internal components or software as needed.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

Hydraulic braking hardware responsible for vehicle deceleration. Regular manual inspections help catch wear before rotor damage occurs.

**Premature Brake Pad Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Rear brake pads can wear down to metal-on-metal conditions prematurely without triggering adequate dashboard warning indicators.
- **Solution:** Perform a physical inspection of brake pads and rotors during routine maintenance services rather than relying solely on dashboard sensors.

## Electrical

### Backup Camera and Airbags

Safety systems designed to assist with driver awareness and collision protection. Camera and sensor functionality should be verified before purchase.

**Backup Camera Display Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The rearview camera display may fail to activate or show a blank black screen when shifting into reverse, reducing visibility.
- **Solution:** Inspect the camera wiring harness, display unit, or replace the backup camera assembly.

## Exterior

### Telematics and Lighting

Manages vehicle connectivity, lighting, and internal electronics. Electrical glitches can trigger persistent dashboard warnings.

**SOS Inoperative and Low Beam Visibility**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers frequently report constant 'SOS Inoperative' warning messages on the dash alongside inadequate low-beam headlight visibility during night driving.
- **Solution:** Inspect the telematics control unit and battery voltage, and check headlight alignment or bulb output.

---

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V123000: Tires](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=GLE400&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** Over cured tires may develop a break in the sidewall, resulting in sudden air loss or belt edge separation which could lead to tread/belt loss. Either condition can cause a loss of vehicle control, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** MBUSA will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the tires, and replace them as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

When shopping for a used luxury vehicle, conducting a thorough pre-purchase inspection that focuses on electrical accessories, transmission engagement, and brake wear will help mitigate unexpected repair expenses. Paying close attention to these common owner-reported areas ensures a more confident ownership experience.
