The 2016 Mercedes-Benz G550 is a luxury SUV known for its rugged off-road capabilities and iconic design. It's powered by a 4.0-liter twin-turbocharged V8 engine producing 416 horsepower and 450 lb-ft of torque. The G550 comes standard with a 7-speed automatic transmission. The G-Wagon's appeal lies in its unique combination of luxury and go-anywhere capability. Owners often praise its commanding presence, powerful engine, and robust build quality. Criticisms typically revolve around its poor fuel economy, outdated interior design (for its price point), and relatively cumbersome handling on the road.

**Engine**

**4.0L Twin-Turbocharged V8**

* **Oil Leaks from Valve Cover Gaskets**
    * Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks are found around the valve covers, leading to oil consumption and potential oil burning smell.
    * Solution: Replace the valve cover gaskets.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Turbocharger Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Reduced engine power, unusual noises from the engine bay (whining or screeching), check engine light illumination. Can be caused by oil starvation or component failure within the turbocharger.
    * Solution: Replacement of the turbocharger(s).
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $3,000 - $6,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Check Engine Light Due to Faulty Mass Air Flow (MAF) Sensor**
    * Description/Symptoms: Check engine light is illuminated. Vehicle might run rough.
    * Solution: Replace the MAF sensor.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**7G-Tronic 7-Speed Automatic Transmission**

* **Rough Shifting/Hesitation**
    * Description/Symptoms: Noticeable rough or delayed shifting between gears. Hesitation when accelerating. This can be caused by low transmission fluid or degradation of the fluid over time, or a worn out solenoid valve.
    * Solution: Perform a transmission fluid flush and filter change. If the problem persists, further diagnostics and potential solenoid replacement may be needed.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: Fluid flush and filter change: $300 - $600+. Solenoid replacement: $800 - $1,500+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Transmission Fluid Leaks**
    * Description/Symptoms: Leaks around the transmission pan gasket or seals.
    * Solution: Replace the leaking gasket or seals.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Air Suspension Compressor Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Vehicle sags on one or more corners, the air suspension takes a long time to raise the vehicle, or the air suspension warning light illuminates.
    * Solution: Replace the air suspension compressor.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,500+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Air Strut Leaks**
    * Description/Symptoms: Vehicle sits lower than normal or unevenly, accompanied by a hissing sound from the affected air strut.
    * Solution: Replace the leaking air strut.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+ per strut
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **COMAND System Malfunctions**
    * Description/Symptoms: The COMAND infotainment system freezes, glitches, or becomes unresponsive. Navigation issues are also reported.
    * Solution: Software update or replacement of the COMAND unit.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: Software update: $150 - $300. COMAND unit replacement: $1,500 - $3,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Battery Drain Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: The battery drains quickly, especially if the vehicle is not driven frequently.
    * Solution: Diagnose the source of the parasitic drain and repair or replace the faulty component.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies widely depending on the cause. $100 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Door Lock Actuator Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: One or more door locks fail to respond to the key fob or interior lock switch.
    * Solution: Replace the faulty door lock actuator.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+ per door
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Sunroof Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: Sunroof motor failure. Difficulty opening or closing. Sunroof track malfunctions.
    * Solution: Replace the sunroof motor or sunroof track.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400-$1000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2016 Mercedes-Benz G550, while a capable and luxurious SUV, can suffer from several notable issues. The most concerning are the potential for turbocharger failure and air suspension problems, which can be quite expensive to repair. Oil leaks and transmission issues, while less severe, can still lead to significant repair bills. Electrical problems with the COMAND system can be frustrating. Potential owners should be aware of these common issues and consider having a pre-purchase inspection performed by a qualified mechanic specializing in Mercedes-Benz vehicles.
