The 2021 Mercedes-Benz G550 is a luxury SUV known for its iconic design, off-road capabilities, and opulent interior. It is powered by a robust 4.0-liter twin-turbocharged V8 engine producing 416 horsepower and 450 lb-ft of torque, mated to a 9G-TRONIC nine-speed automatic transmission. This engine is also available in the AMG G63 trim, which produces 577 hp and 627 lb-ft of torque. People appreciate the G-Wagon for its distinctive styling, luxurious features, and go-anywhere attitude. Common complaints revolve around its high price tag, subpar fuel economy, and somewhat cumbersome handling in urban environments.

**Engine**

**4.0L Twin-Turbocharged V8**

*   **Oil Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks originating from the valve cover gaskets, oil filter housing, or oil cooler lines. Symptoms include visible oil drips under the vehicle, a burning oil smell, and low oil levels.
    *   Solution: Replace the leaking gaskets, housing, or lines.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+ depending on the source and severity of the leak.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Turbocharger Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Whining noises from the turbos, reduced power, and potential engine misfires. Can be caused by failing wastegate actuators or turbocharger bearing failure.
    *   Solution: Replacing faulty turbochargers or actuators.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $3,000 - $7,000+ depending on the extent of the damage.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**9G-TRONIC 9-Speed Automatic**

*   **Rough Shifting**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Harsh or jerky shifts, especially between lower gears. May be more pronounced when the transmission is cold.
    *   Solution: In some cases, a software update can improve shift quality. In more severe cases, valve body replacement or transmission replacement is required.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 for a software update, $1,500 - $4,000 for a valve body replacement, $7,000 - $10,000+ for transmission replacement.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Delayed Engagement**
    *   Description/Symptoms: A noticeable delay between shifting into drive or reverse and the vehicle actually moving.
    *   Solution: Could be related to low transmission fluid, valve body issues, or internal transmission damage. Requires diagnosis and repair by a qualified technician.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+ depending on the cause.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Air Suspension Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Vehicle sitting unevenly, ride height fluctuations, "Suspension Malfunction" warning light. Can be caused by leaks in the air struts, compressor failure, or issues with the control module.
    *   Solution: Replacing faulty air struts, compressor, or control module.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $4,000+ per strut, $1,000 - $2,500+ for the compressor.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Freezing, crashing, slow response times, and Bluetooth connectivity problems with the MBUX infotainment system.
    *   Solution: Software updates, system resets, or replacement of the head unit.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 for software updates, $500 - $2,000+ for head unit replacement.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Sensor Malfunctions**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Issues with parking sensors, blind-spot monitoring, or other driver-assistance systems. Warning lights illuminate on the dashboard.
    *   Solution: Replacing faulty sensors.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+ per sensor depending on location and complexity.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **No Issues Found**

**Interior**

*   **Sunroof Malfunction**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Sunroof gets stuck, makes excessive noise, or fails to open or close properly.
    *   Solution: Repairing or replacing the sunroof motor, tracks, or other components.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+ depending on the complexity of the repair.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Trim Peeling/Detaching**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Interior trim pieces, particularly around the dashboard or door panels, start to peel or detach.
    *   Solution: Replacing the affected trim pieces.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+ depending on the piece and labor involved.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary:** The 2021 Mercedes-Benz G550, while luxurious and capable, does have some common issues. The most concerning are potential engine oil leaks and turbocharger problems, which can be expensive to repair. Transmission issues and air suspension failures also present potential maintenance costs. Electrical problems, particularly with the infotainment system, can be frustrating. Overall, the severity of the potential issues ranges from minor inconveniences to costly repairs. Buyers should be aware of these potential problems and factor them into their ownership considerations, including the cost of maintenance and potential repairs.
