# 2019 Mercedes-Benz G550 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2019 Mercedes-Benz G550, buyers get an iconic, heavily redesigned luxury SUV featuring modern technology paired with traditional rugged capability. While owner reports to federal safety regulators are relatively limited, prospective buyers should review specific electrical and exterior concerns that have surfaced in real-world driving.

---

## Engine

### 4.0L Biturbo V8

The 2019 G550 is powered by a 4.0-liter twin-turbocharged V8 engine producing roughly 416 horsepower and 450 lb-ft of torque. This robust powerplant delivers swift acceleration and strong towing capability while maintaining refined road manners.

## Transmission

### 9-Speed Automatic Transmission

Power is routed through a 9-speed automatic transmission featuring standard four-wheel drive with triple locking differentials. This transmission offers smooth gear transitions and helps optimize highway efficiency for this heavy luxury SUV.

### Differential Lock System

Electrical anomalies primarily center around electronic stability controls and differential lock warnings reported by some owners.

**Lock Differential Error Message**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers experience persistent warning messages on the display screen indicating a front or rear differential lock system malfunction, accompanied by inactive ABS and ESP systems.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified technician or dealership inspect the differential sensors and control modules, as parts replacement may be required to clear the fault codes.

## Exterior

### Windshield

Exterior body components are generally solid, though the upright windshield design makes it vulnerable to cracking from road debris.

**Spontaneous Windshield Cracking**
- **Symptoms:** The windshield may develop long cracks running from top to bottom following minor impacts or, in some cases, entirely without motive cause or visible chip formation.
- **Solution:** Inspect the windshield for stress fractures and replace the glass through an insured auto glass specialist if structural cracks develop.

### Door Latches

Cabin appointments are exceptionally crafted, though isolated mechanical failures like door latch alignment issues can occasionally occur.

**Driver-Side Door Latch Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** The driver-side door fails to securely close or stay latched when shut firmly, creating a safety hazard while the vehicle is in motion.
- **Solution:** Have the door latch mechanism and locking linkage inspected and adjusted or replaced by a professional technician.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V090000: Power Train:Driveline:Differential Unit](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=G550&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** Deactivation of the vehicle stability control system or anti-lock brakes may increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** MBUSA will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the differential locking modules and replace them as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V761000: Electrical System:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=G550&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** The Central Powertrain Controller (CPC) control unit could reset while driving, possibly causing an engine stall. An engine stall can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** MBUSA will notify owners, and dealers will update the CPC control unit software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V820000: Electrical System:Wiring](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=G550&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** The damage to certain wires can impact safety features such as the side impact restraint system, the automatic unlocking of the door in the event of a crash, or the vehicle location in the event of an emergency call. These scenarios increases the risk of an injury in the event of a crash.
- **What to do:** MBUSA will notify owners, and dealers will inspect, modify, or replace the front door wiring harness in the event damage has occurred, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V247000: Latches/Locks/Linkages](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=G550&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** If an unrestrained child unexpectedly opens a door, they may fall from the vehicle increasing their risk of injury.
- **What to do:** MBUSA will notify owners, and dealers will install a label on the rear doors with the correct operating direction, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V564000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Storage:Tank Assembly](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=G550&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** The fuel tank may not have been welded properly during manufacturing, which could result in a fuel leak. A fuel leak in the presence of an ignition source can increase the risk of fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the fuel tank, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V097000: Electrical System:Wiring](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=G550&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** A loss of anti-lock brakes or electronic stability control can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and either install a protective sleeve or replace the front axle wiring harness, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2019 Mercedes-Benz G550 remains a highly desirable and capable luxury SUV, backed by a potent V8 and sophisticated drivetrain. While used-car buyers should keep an eye on potential electrical warnings concerning the differential locks and vulnerable flat glass panels, thorough pre-purchase inspections can help uncover these minor issues before buying.
