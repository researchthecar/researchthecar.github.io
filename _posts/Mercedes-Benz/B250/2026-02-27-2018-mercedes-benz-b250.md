# 2018 Mercedes-Benz B250

### Overview
The 2018 Mercedes-Benz B250, part of the B-Class lineup, is a compact luxury hatchback known for its practicality and premium feel. It typically features a turbocharged four-cylinder engine paired with a dual-clutch automatic transmission, offering a balance of performance and fuel efficiency. Reliability for the 2018 model year is generally considered good, with owners reporting fewer significant mechanical issues compared to some competitors. However, like any vehicle, there are specific areas that have seen occasional reports of concern, primarily related to certain electronic components and, in some cases, the powertrain under specific driving conditions. Regular maintenance is key to ensuring the longevity and optimal performance of this German-engineered vehicle.

---


## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4
**Turbocharger Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of power, whistling noises, increased oil consumption, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Inspection and potential replacement of the turbocharger unit and related seals.
- **Cost:** $1,500 - $3,000

**Coolant Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Overheating, visible coolant puddles under the vehicle, sweet smell, low coolant level.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and repair of leaking hoses, water pump, or radiator.
- **Cost:** $300 - $800


## Transmission

### 7-Speed Dual-Clutch Automatic (7G-DCT)
**Harsh Shifting / Jerking**
- **Symptoms:** Sudden jolts or rough gear changes, especially at low speeds or during initial acceleration.
- **Solution:** Software update for transmission control module, or potential internal component inspection and repair.
- **Cost:** $200 - $1,200

**Transmission Fluid Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Leaking fluid from the transmission casing or seals, potential for slipping gears.
- **Solution:** Replacement of faulty seals or gaskets, and topping up transmission fluid.
- **Cost:** $250 - $600

## Electrical

### Infotainment System
**Screen Freezing / Unresponsiveness**
- **Symptoms:** Infotainment screen becomes unresponsive, freezes, or restarts randomly.
- **Solution:** Software update or potential replacement of the head unit.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1,500


### Battery Drain
**Premature Battery Discharge**
- **Symptoms:** Difficulty starting the vehicle, battery warning light, electrical components malfunctioning.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis of parasitic draw, potential replacement of battery or faulty module.
- **Cost:** $200 - $600

### Summary Verdict
The 2018 Mercedes-Benz B250 demonstrates a solid reliability record, particularly in its core mechanical components like the engine and transmission, which are generally robust. The primary areas of concern tend to be within the electrical system, specifically related to the infotainment unit and potential battery drain issues, which can be costly to resolve. While the suspension and exterior/interior components are largely free from widespread problems, owners should be aware of the potential for turbocharger issues and coolant leaks in the engine, and occasional transmission shifting anomalies. Overall, with diligent maintenance and prompt attention to any electrical gremlins, the B250 should provide a dependable luxury compact car experience.
