# 2016 Mercedes-Benz B250

### Overview
The 2016 Mercedes-Benz B250, part of the B-Class lineup, is a compact luxury hatchback known for its practicality and premium feel. It typically features a turbocharged four-cylinder engine paired with a front-wheel-drive system and a continuously variable transmission (CVT). While generally considered a reliable vehicle within its segment, like any car, it can experience specific issues. This report focuses on documented technical reliability concerns, excluding routine maintenance and standard wear and tear, to provide a clear picture of potential mechanical and electrical vulnerabilities for owners and prospective buyers.

---


## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4 (M270)
**Turbocharger Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of power, whistling noises, increased oil consumption, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Turbocharger inspection, repair, or replacement.
- **Cost:** $1,200 - $2,500

**Coolant Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Overheating, visible coolant puddles under the vehicle, sweet smell.
- **Solution:** Inspection and repair/replacement of leaking hoses, water pump, or radiator.
- **Cost:** $300 - $800


## Transmission

### 7-Speed Dual-Clutch Transmission (7G-DCT)
**Transmission Shudder/Hesitation**
- **Symptoms:** Jerky shifts, hesitation during acceleration, rough engagement.
- **Solution:** Software updates, transmission fluid flush, or internal component repair/replacement.
- **Cost:** $500 - $2,000

### Summary Verdict
The 2016 Mercedes-Benz B250 generally demonstrates good reliability, with the powertrain being a strong point. The most commonly cited technical issues tend to revolve around the turbocharger and potential coolant leaks within the 2.0L engine, as well as occasional transmission shudder with the 7G-DCT. Electrical gremlins, particularly with the infotainment system, can also arise. While these issues are documented, they do not appear to be universal, and many B250 models perform without significant problems. Regular maintenance and prompt attention to early symptoms are key to ensuring long-term reliability for this luxury compact.
