**Introduction:**

The 2015 Mercedes-Benz B250 is a compact luxury hatchback renowned for its spacious interior, comfortable ride, and high-quality materials. It features a 2.0L turbocharged inline-4 engine producing 208 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque paired with a 7-speed dual-clutch automatic transmission (DCT).

**Engine**

* **2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4**

**No Issues Found**

**Transmission**

* **7-Speed Dual-Clutch Automatic (DCT)**

**Transmission Fluid Leak**
Description/Symptoms: A leak in the transmission fluid pan or cooler lines can cause transmission fluid to leak out. Symptoms include delayed or rough shifting, slipping gears, and a burning smell.
Solution: Replace the leaking component and replenish the transmission fluid.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
Recalls: None Found

**No Issues Found**

**Electrical**

* **Becker MAP Pilot Navigation System Failure**
Description/Symptoms: The Becker MAP Pilot navigation system may experience screen freezing, GPS signal loss, or incorrect navigation instructions.
Solution: Update the navigation system software or replace the Becker MAP Pilot unit.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Rearview Camera Distortion**
Description/Symptoms: The rearview camera may produce distorted or unclear images, making it difficult to see behind the vehicle.
Solution: Replace the rearview camera.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $700+
Recalls: NHTSA Campaign Number 20V298000

**Interior**

* **Sunroof Creaking Noise**
Description/Symptoms: A creaking noise may occur when opening or closing the panoramic sunroof.
Solution: Lubricate the sunroof tracks and hinges.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200
Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

The 2015 Mercedes-Benz B250 has a relatively low number of reported issues. The transmission fluid leak is the most common issue with the vehicle, but it is not a widespread problem. The other issues are minor and relatively inexpensive to fix. Overall, the B250 is a reliable and well-built vehicle.
