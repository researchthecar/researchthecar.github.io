# 2016 Mercedes-Benz GLC300 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used luxury compact SUV, understanding common owner-reported trouble spots can help you navigate potential pitfalls. While these vehicles offer a refined driving experience and upscale interior appointments, prospective buyers should inspect specific mechanical and trim systems carefully before purchasing.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4

The 2.0-liter turbocharged inline-4 engine produces approximately 241 horsepower and 273 lb-ft of torque, providing brisk acceleration and respectable efficiency. It serves as the standard powerplant for this model, though buyers should watch for occasional fuel system and injector issues.

**Fuel Leak and Odor**
- **Symptoms:** Owners report noticing a strong raw gasoline smell inside the cabin and around the exterior of the vehicle, particularly during cold weather starts.
- **Solution:** Inspect and replace failing fuel injectors and fuel rail components as necessary.

**Engine Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle may experience unexpected engine hesitation, rough running, or complete stalling during operation or startup.
- **Solution:** Diagnose fault codes with an OBD2 scanner and inspect the fuel and ignition management systems.

## Transmission

### 9-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 9-speed automatic transmission delivers smooth and efficient gear shifts across various driving conditions. It pairs with both rear-wheel drive and 4MATIC all-wheel drive configurations with generally solid long-term mechanical reliability.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

The braking system provides strong stopping power, but persistent noise complaints are common among owners.

**Brake Squeal and Noise**
- **Symptoms:** Loud screeching or squealing noises emanating from the brakes, particularly when backing up or during cold weather conditions.
- **Solution:** Replace brake pads and rotors, or consult updated service procedures to address persistent acoustic resonance.

## Steering

### Steering Column and Tilt Motor

Power adjustment mechanisms in the steering column can wear out prematurely over time.

**Steering Column Adjustment Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The steering wheel tilt motor becomes stuck in position, fails to adjust up and down, or locks unexpectedly.
- **Solution:** Replace the steering column tilt motor assembly.

## Exterior

### Sunroof and Glass Panels

Exterior trim and glass assemblies require periodic inspection to ensure weather stripping and mechanical tracks remain secure.

**Panoramic Sunroof Jamming or Detachment**
- **Symptoms:** Sunroof panels fail to open or close smoothly, make grinding noises, or in severe cases, detach while driving at highway speeds.
- **Solution:** Inspect sunroof tracks, lubricate mechanisms, or replace damaged glass panels and mounting hardware.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V683000: Structure:Body:Roof And Pillars](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=GLC300&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** If the rear seat passengers cannot fasten their seat belts, they have an increased risk of injury in the event of a crash.
- **What to do:** MBUSA will notify owners, and dealers will install felt strips between the C-pillar and rear side paneling, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V787000: Communication](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=GLC300&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** For a full list of the affected models visit: https://static.nhtsa.gov/odi/rcl/2019/RMISC-19V787-4620.pdf An inaccurate vehicle location may delay emergency responders, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** MBUSA will also notify owners, and dealers will check software for a successful OTA or update the communication module software as needed, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V197000: Structure:Body:Roof And Pillars](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=GLC300&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** The front roof panel located between the windshield and the panoramic sunroof may not have been bonded correctly to the vehicle during a prior repair. The adhesive bonding of the front panel might deteriorate, possibly causing the front panel to detach from the vehicle and become a road hazard, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Mercedes-Benz dealer will remove the bonded panel and will install a new front panel by following the detailed repair instructions and using the approved primer, cleaner and adhesive, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V058000: Electrical System:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=GLC300&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** The software design of the communication module may fail to communicate the correct vehicle location for the emergency call system (eCall) in the event of a crash. Failure of the eCall system may result in emergency responders being dispatched to the wrong location, increasing the risk of injury following a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 17V627000: Air Bags:Frontal](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=GLC300&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** The driver's air bag may unexpectedly deploy due to insufficient grounding of the steering components if an electrostatic discharge occurs and the air bag clockspring is broken. If the driver air bag inadvertently deploys, it can increase the risk of injury and a crash.
- **What to do:** MBUSA will notify owners, and dealers will add sufficient grounding to the steering components, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V114000: Electrical System:Starter Assembly](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=GLC300&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** If the starting current limiter overheats, the surrounding components can melt, increasing the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** MBUSA will notify owners, and dealers will install an additional fuse in the electrical line to the starter, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V177000: Electrical System:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=GLC300&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** Various control units on these vehicles may have been updated with incorrect software, potentially affecting the correct deployment of the air bags in the event of a crash. If the air bags do not deploy as intended in the event of a crash, there would be an increased risk of injury in the event of a crash.
- **What to do:** MBUSA will notify owners, and dealers will update software in the affected control units, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V603000: Electronic Stability Control (Esc)](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=GLC300&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** A damaged ESP control unit may inadvertently activate the stability control altering the vehicle's path. Additionally, the electronic parking brake may not function.
- **What to do:** MBUSA will notify owners, and dealers will replace the ESP Hydraulic control units, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

When evaluating a used model, perform a thorough inspection focusing on the fuel system, brakes, and power accessories like the sunroof and steering column. While these vehicles offer a compelling blend of luxury and performance, addressing potential owner-reported trouble spots proactively will ensure a more dependable ownership experience.
