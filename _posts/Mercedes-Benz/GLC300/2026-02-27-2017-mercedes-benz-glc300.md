# 2017 Mercedes-Benz GLC300 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2017 Mercedes-Benz GLC300 offers a blend of luxury, comfort, and refined performance that makes it a popular choice in the used compact luxury SUV market. However, prospective buyers should approach with a measure of caution, as owner reports highlight several recurring mechanical and electrical concerns. From crankcase ventilation issues to sunroof binding and transmission hiccups, understanding potential problem areas can help you inspect a used model more effectively.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4

Powered by a 2.0-liter turbocharged inline-4 engine producing around 241 horsepower and 273 lb-ft of torque, this option delivers brisk acceleration and respectable fuel economy. While generally smooth and capable, it is prone to specific accessory and emissions-related failures like PCV valve binding as the vehicle ages.

**Positive Crankcase Ventilation (PCV) Valve Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Triggers diagnostic trouble code P052E, illuminates the check engine light, and can cause the engine to idle rough, stall, or hesitate during acceleration.
- **Solution:** Replace the faulty PCV regulator valve assembly. Due to its buried location, labor can be extensive.
- **Cost:** $1,000+

**Fuel Odor or Leak**
- **Symptoms:** A strong smell of gasoline detected inside or outside of the vehicle, particularly around the engine bay or fuel components.
- **Solution:** Inspect fuel lines and related components for leaks and replace damaged parts or seals according to factory specifications.

## Transmission

### 9-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 9-speed automatic transmission pairs with the 2.0L engine to provide smooth gear changes and optimal highway cruising efficiency. Although generally refined, occasional internal valve body and control unit malfunctions have been reported by owners.

**Valve Body Control Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The transmission may get stuck in a single gear (such as 5th gear), refusing to shift higher, and can trigger a limp mode that makes highway driving hazardous.
- **Solution:** Diagnose the transmission control module and replace the valve body assembly if mechanical or electronic failure is confirmed.

## Steering

### Power Steering System

**Power Steering Assist Loss**
- **Symptoms:** Illuminates the power steering malfunction warning light and results in a sudden, heavy loss of power steering assist, making the vehicle difficult to maneuver.
- **Solution:** Inspect the electronic power steering rack, wiring connections, and control modules; replace failing steering components as necessary.

## Exterior

### Panoramic Sunroof and Glass

**Sunroof Track Binding or Explosion**
- **Symptoms:** Abnormal grinding noises when operating the sunroof, panels going off track and failing to close, or the glass shattering unexpectedly.
- **Solution:** Clear debris, lubricate tracks, readjust or replace sunroof tracks and cables, or replace shattered glass panels.

## Safety

### Seat Belts and Airbags

**Seat Belt Latch Sticking**
- **Symptoms:** Seat belts remain locked or fail to unlatch properly when pressed, requiring multiple attempts to release occupants.
- **Solution:** Inspect the seat belt buckle assembly and replace the latch mechanism if internal components are binding.

**Airbag and SRS Malfunction Warnings**
- **Symptoms:** Dashboard displays persistent warnings such as 'Driver's Air Bag Malfunction - Service Required' or disables collision prevention assist features.
- **Solution:** Scan the Supplemental Restraint System (SRS) module for fault codes and replace faulty sensors, wiring harnesses, or airbag igniters.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V683000: Structure:Body:Roof And Pillars](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=GLC300&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** If the rear seat passengers cannot fasten their seat belts, they have an increased risk of injury in the event of a crash.
- **What to do:** MBUSA will notify owners, and dealers will install felt strips between the C-pillar and rear side paneling, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V197000: Structure:Body:Roof And Pillars](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=GLC300&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** The front roof panel located between the windshield and the panoramic sunroof may not have been bonded correctly to the vehicle during a prior repair. The adhesive bonding of the front panel might deteriorate, possibly causing the front panel to detach from the vehicle and become a road hazard, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Mercedes-Benz dealer will remove the bonded panel and will install a new front panel by following the detailed repair instructions and using the approved primer, cleaner and adhesive, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V072000: Communication](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=GLC300&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** The communication module might have been inadvertently deactivated during a service visit, which could have disabled the Emergency Call (eCall) function. A disabled eCall system would prevent a vehicle occupant from contacting emergency services through the call center in an emergency, potentially delaying emergency responders and increasing the risk of.
- **What to do:** MBUSA will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the communication module and activate it, if necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V058000: Electrical System:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=GLC300&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** The software design of the communication module may fail to communicate the correct vehicle location for the emergency call system (eCall) in the event of a crash. Failure of the eCall system may result in emergency responders being dispatched to the wrong location, increasing the risk of injury following a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 17V715000: Visibility:Sun/Moon Roof Assembly](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=GLC300&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** If the sun roof panels detach from the vehicle, it can increase the risk of a crash or injury for following traffic.
- **What to do:** MBUSA will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the bonding on the sun roof panels, correcting it as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V627000: Air Bags:Frontal](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=GLC300&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** The driver's air bag may unexpectedly deploy due to insufficient grounding of the steering components if an electrostatic discharge occurs and the air bag clockspring is broken. If the driver air bag inadvertently deploys, it can increase the risk of injury and a crash.
- **What to do:** MBUSA will notify owners, and dealers will add sufficient grounding to the steering components, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V114000: Electrical System:Starter Assembly](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=GLC300&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** If the starting current limiter overheats, the surrounding components can melt, increasing the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** MBUSA will notify owners, and dealers will install an additional fuse in the electrical line to the starter, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V251000: Seat Belts:Pretensioner](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=GLC300&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** The affected vehicles have front seat seatbelt pretensioners that may not function properly in the event of a crash. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 208, "Occupant Crash Protection," and 209, "Seat Belt Assemblies." If the front seat seatbelt pretensioners do not function.
- **What to do:** MBUSA will notify owners, and dealers will replace the front seat belts, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2017 Mercedes-Benz GLC300 is an appealing entry into luxury SUV ownership, offering great driving dynamics and a premium cabin. However, buyers should perform a thorough pre-purchase inspection focusing on known trouble spots like the PCV valve, sunroof tracks, and transmission responsiveness. Budgeting for potential out-of-warranty luxury repair costs is strongly recommended to ensure a smooth ownership experience.
