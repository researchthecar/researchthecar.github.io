**Introduction**

The 2019 Mercedes-Benz GLC300 is a compact luxury SUV known for its stylish design, comfortable interior, and advanced safety features. It comes standard with a 2.0L turbocharged inline-4 engine that produces 255 horsepower and 273 lb-ft of torque. Other engine options include a 2.0L diesel engine and a 3.0L turbocharged V6 engine. The GLC300 is available with a 9-speed automatic transmission and either rear-wheel drive or all-wheel drive. While the GLC300 is generally well-received, it does have some reported issues.

**Engine**

* **2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4 Engine**

  * **Cracked cylinder head:** Cylinder head warping can cause coolant leaks and performance issues. Symptoms include overheating, loss of power, and rough idling. Requires replacement of the cylinder head. **Estimated Cost to Fix: $3,000 - $5,000**
  * **Intake manifold runner issues:** Faulty intake manifold runners can cause vacuum leaks, leading to rough idling and loss of power. Symptoms include stalling, hesitation, and reduced fuel efficiency. Requires replacement of the intake manifold. **Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500**

* **2.0L Diesel Engine**

  * **Diesel particulate filter (DPF) problems:** Clogged DPFs can cause reduced engine performance, increased fuel consumption, and difficulty starting. Requires cleaning or replacement of the DPF. **Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000**
  * **Exhaust gas recirculation (EGR) valve failure:** Faulty EGR valves can lead to reduced engine performance, rough idling, and increased emissions. Requires replacement of the EGR valve. **Estimated Cost to Fix: $600 - $1,200**

* **3.0L Turbocharged V6 Engine**

  * **No Issues Found**

**Transmission**

* **9-Speed Automatic Transmission**

  * **Shifting issues:** Erratic or delayed shifting, slipping gears, or hard shifting. May require software updates, transmission fluid replacement, or transmission repairs. **Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $3,000**
  * **Torque converter shudder:** Vibrations or shuddering during acceleration or shifting. Requires replacement of the torque converter. **Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,500**

**Suspension**

* **No Issues Found**

**Electrical**

* **Battery drain issues:** Premature battery discharge or battery failure. May be caused by faulty components, parasitic drains, or an alternator problem. Requires diagnosis and repair of the affected component. **Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000**
* **Infotainment system glitches:** Intermittent issues with the COMAND infotainment system, including crashes, freezing, or loss of functionality. May require software updates, hardware replacements, or reprogramming. **Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500**

**Exterior**

* **Sunroof problems:** Leaking, rattling, or sticking sunroofs. May require repairs or replacements to the sunroof seals, tracks, or motor. **Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000**
* **Headlight condensation:** Moisture buildup inside the headlight assemblies. May be caused by faulty seals or ventilation problems. Requires resealing or replacement of the headlights. **Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,000**

**Interior**

* **Panorama roof rattling:** Noises or vibrations from the panoramic sunroof while driving. May require adjustments to the sunroof seals or alignment. **Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500**
* **Door panel creaking:** Creaking or rattling noises from the door panels. May require tightening of loose panels or replacement of faulty clips. **Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300**

**Summary**

While the 2019 Mercedes-Benz GLC300 is a desirable luxury SUV, it has experienced some reported issues. Engine problems, transmission issues, and electrical glitches are the most common concerns. The severity and cost of these issues vary depending on the affected component and the extent of the damage. It's important to note that not all GLC300s may experience these issues, and some issues may be more common in certain model years or trim levels.
