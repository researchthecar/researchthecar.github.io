# 2020 Mercedes-Benz AMG GT 63 S

### Overview
The 2020 Mercedes-Benz AMG GT 63 S is a high-performance luxury sedan that blends exhilarating power with sophisticated comfort. As a top-tier model, it generally exhibits strong build quality and engineering. However, like any complex vehicle, particularly those with potent powertrains, certain areas may experience issues more frequently than others. This analysis focuses on documented technical reliability concerns, excluding routine maintenance and standard wear and tear, to provide a clear picture for owners and potential buyers. The focus is on specific components and their reported problems, offering insights into potential repair costs and solutions.

---


## Engine

### 4.0L Twin-Turbo V8
**Turbocharger Wastegate Actuator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Reduced engine power, 'check engine' light, whistling noises from the engine bay.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the faulty wastegate actuator or the complete turbocharger assembly.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1500

**Coolant Leaks (Intercooler System)**
- **Symptoms:** Overheating, visible coolant loss, sweet smell from the engine bay.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of leaking intercooler hoses or seals.
- **Cost:** $300 - $700


## Transmission

### 9-Speed AMG SPEEDSHIFT MCT
**Harsh Shifting / Jerking**
- **Symptoms:** Noticeable clunks or hesitations during gear changes, especially at lower speeds.
- **Solution:** Software update for transmission control module, or in severe cases, transmission fluid flush and filter replacement.
- **Cost:** $200 - $600


## Suspension

### AMG RIDE CONTROL+ Suspension
**Air Spring Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle sagging on one corner, uneven ride height, 'check suspension' warning light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the leaking air spring or associated air lines.
- **Cost:** $700 - $1200 per corner


## Electrical

### Infotainment System
**Screen Freezing / Unresponsiveness**
- **Symptoms:** Infotainment screen becomes unresponsive, glitches, or restarts randomly.
- **Solution:** Software update or reset of the infotainment module. In rare cases, module replacement.
- **Cost:** $150 - $800


### 12V Battery
**Premature Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Various electrical components malfunctioning, slow cranking, battery warning light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the 12V auxiliary battery.
- **Cost:** $250 - $500

### Summary Verdict
The 2020 Mercedes-Benz AMG GT 63 S demonstrates a generally high level of reliability, particularly in its exterior and interior components, where no significant widespread issues are reported. The powertrain, while powerful, can occasionally present issues such as turbocharger wastegate actuator failures and intercooler coolant leaks, with associated repair costs. The transmission may experience occasional harsh shifting, often resolvable with software updates. Suspension components, specifically air springs, can develop leaks. Electrical systems, particularly the infotainment screen and the 12V battery, have seen some reports of glitches or premature failure. Overall, the AMG GT 63 S is a robust vehicle, but owners should be aware of potential costs related to its high-performance systems.
