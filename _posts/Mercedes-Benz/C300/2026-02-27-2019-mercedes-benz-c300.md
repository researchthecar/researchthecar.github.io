# 2019 Mercedes-Benz C300 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used luxury compact car, the Mercedes-Benz C300 offers a compelling blend of premium styling, comfort, and performance. However, prospective buyers should carefully evaluate common mechanical and electrical concerns reported by owners, particularly regarding powertrain behavior, steering components, and interior safety features. Reviewing vehicle service records and arranging a thorough pre-purchase inspection can help mitigate potential ownership risks.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4

The 2.0-liter turbocharged inline-4 engine produces approximately 255 horsepower and 273 lb-ft of torque, providing brisk acceleration and respectable efficiency for daily driving. While generally robust, owners have occasionally noted electrical sensor and oil control valve issues that require prompt attention.

**Loss of Motive Power and Sensor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle suddenly shuts off or experiences a loss of motive power while driving, often accompanied by diagnostic trouble codes related to the throttle body or accelerator pedal position sensors.
- **Solution:** Inspect and replace faulty throttle body assemblies or accelerator pedal position sensors as indicated by stored diagnostic fault codes.

**Oil Control Valve Defect**
- **Symptoms:** Illuminated check engine light and potential engine lock-up while driving due to a defective oil control valve.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified technician diagnose the check engine light and replace the faulty oil control valve promptly.

## Transmission

### 9-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 9-speed automatic transmission delivers smooth, highly efficient gear shifts tailored to maximize both performance and highway fuel economy. Most units operate reliably, though rare instances of delayed gear engagement have been documented by owners.

**Intermittent Loss of Drive Engagement**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle temporarily fails to accelerate or move forward when shifting from a stop, despite depressing the accelerator pedal.
- **Solution:** Diagnose transmission control module software and internal hydraulic components; update software or repair transmission as necessary.

## Steering

### Steering Rack and Column

The steering system provides responsive handling characteristic of a luxury sport sedan. However, potential buyers should check for abnormal noises or binding during test drives due to rare steering lock complaints.

**Steering Wheel Lockup**
- **Symptoms:** Abnormal noises occur during low-speed turns, followed by the steering wheel locking up and restricting directional control.
- **Solution:** Inspect the steering column and electronic steering lock mechanisms, and replace defective steering components immediately.

## Safety

### Seat Belts

Cabin safety features generally perform well, though front seat belt retraction mechanisms require verification during a used car inspection.

**Seat Belt Retraction Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Front driver and passenger seat belts fail to fully retract into their stowed positions, leaving loose webbing that can impede rear exit or become a trip hazard.
- **Solution:** Inspect the seat belt assembly and replace faulty retractors or tensioner mechanisms.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V608000: Latches/Locks/Linkages](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=C300&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 201, "Occupant Protection In Interior Impact." In the event of a rear-end crash, the center console lid may open, increasing the risk of injury to occupants.
- **What to do:** MBUSA will notify owners, and dealers will check the center console storage compartment, and replace it, if necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V909000: Parking Brake](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=C300&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** Driving the vehicle with the ESP system deactivated could increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Mercedes has notified owners, and dealers will update the ESP system software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V761000: Electrical System:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=C300&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** The Central Powertrain Controller (CPC) control unit could reset while driving, possibly causing an engine stall. An engine stall can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** MBUSA will notify owners, and dealers will update the CPC control unit software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V839000: Seat Belts](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=C300&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** A correctly fastened seatbelt may be inaccurately detected as being unfastened, preventing the seat belt pre-tensioners from activating in the event of a crash. If the seat belt tensioning does not activate in the event of a crash, it can increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** MBUSA will notify owners, and dealers will replace the seat belt buckles, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2019 Mercedes-Benz C300 is a sophisticated and enjoyable luxury sedan, but used car buyers should approach with a healthy level of diligence. While its powertrain and comfort features deliver a premium driving experience, reported issues concerning electrical sensors, steering operation, and seat belt retraction warrant a careful pre-purchase inspection. Verifying service history and ensuring all software updates and recalls have been completed will significantly improve long-term ownership satisfaction.
