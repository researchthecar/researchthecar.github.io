# 2009 Mercedes-Benz C300

### Overview
The 2009 Mercedes-Benz C300, part of the W204 generation, is generally considered a solid vehicle with a good balance of luxury, performance, and technology for its era. While it offers a premium driving experience, like any vehicle, it has specific areas that have seen more attention from owners regarding reliability. This analysis focuses on documented common issues beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential concerns for prospective buyers or current owners. The C300 was primarily offered with a naturally aspirated V6 engine and a 7-speed automatic transmission, which are key components to examine for reliability trends.

---


## Engine

### 3.0L V6 (M272)
**Balance Shaft Gear Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Check engine light, rough idle, misfires, poor performance. This issue is related to the wear of a plastic gear on the balance shaft, affecting timing.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the balance shaft gear and associated components. This is a labor-intensive repair.
- **Cost:** $1,500 - $3,000

**Oil Leaks (Valve Cover Gaskets, Oil Cooler Seals)**
- **Symptoms:** Visible oil leaks under the hood, burning oil smell, low oil level.
- **Solution:** Replacement of leaking gaskets or seals.
- **Cost:** $300 - $700


## Transmission

### 7G-TRONIC (722.9) 7-Speed Automatic
**Transmission Control Module (TCM) Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Harsh shifting, slipping gears, inability to shift, transmission warning light, limp mode.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the Transmission Control Module.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1,500

**Torque Converter Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Shuddering or vibration at certain speeds, slipping, delayed engagement.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the torque converter.
- **Cost:** $1,000 - $2,000

## Electrical

### General Electrical System
**Window Regulator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Power windows not working, window stuck in position, grinding noise when operating window.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the window regulator assembly.
- **Cost:** $250 - $500

**Headlight Bulb Failure (HID)**
- **Symptoms:** One or both headlights not illuminating, flickering headlights.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the HID bulb and potentially the ballast.
- **Cost:** $150 - $400

### Summary Verdict
The 2009 Mercedes-Benz C300 (W204) presents a generally reliable luxury sedan, particularly regarding its core powertrain components when properly maintained. The most notable potential issues revolve around the M272 V6 engine's balance shaft gear wear and the 7G-TRONIC transmission's control module. Oil leaks from common gasket points are also a possibility. Electrical gremlins, such as window regulators and HID headlight issues, can occur. However, many owners report a positive ownership experience with these issues being manageable with timely repairs. Regular maintenance and addressing early warning signs are crucial for maximizing the longevity and reliability of this model.
