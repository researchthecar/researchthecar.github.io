# 2017 Mercedes-Benz C300 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2017 Mercedes-Benz C300 offers an appealing blend of luxury, technology, and refined performance, but prospective buyers should approach with a careful eye toward common mechanical and safety complaints. While it delivers a composed ride and upscale cabin, owner feedback highlights recurring trouble spots including emissions system codes, fuel odors, and seat belt retraction failures. Prioritizing a pre-purchase inspection that checks for these specific problem areas can help secure a much more reliable ownership experience.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4

The 2.0-liter turbocharged inline-4 engine produces approximately 241 horsepower and 273 lb-ft of torque, providing brisk acceleration and respectable fuel economy. It is generally smooth and responsive, though owners frequently report minor emissions and fuel system issues as the mileage climbs.

**Check Engine Light and P052E Code**
- **Symptoms:** The check engine light illuminates intermittently or stays on permanently, often storing diagnostic code P052E relating to the positive crankcase ventilation valve.
- **Solution:** Diagnose the crankcase ventilation system and replace faulty valves or associated breather components as required.

**Fuel Odor or Leak**
- **Symptoms:** A strong gasoline smell is noticeable inside the cabin or around the exterior of the vehicle while driving or after parking.
- **Solution:** Inspect fuel lines, high-pressure pumps, and connections for leaks or seepage, and replace compromised components.

**Engine Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** The engine may unexpectedly stall or fail to maintain proper propulsion, sometimes accompanied by warning lights on the dash.
- **Solution:** Inspect the fuel delivery system, electrical sensors, and throttle body for faults, and update software or replace failing modules.

## Transmission

### 9-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 9-speed automatic transmission routes power smoothly to the rear wheels or 4MATIC all-wheel-drive system. It is widely praised for seamless gear changes and highway cruising efficiency, showing very few systemic reliability complaints in owner logs.

## Steering

### Steering System

The electric power steering system provides light and accurate maneuverability under normal conditions, but sudden loss of assist has been reported by some drivers.

**Electric Power Steering Assist Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle displays a power steering malfunction warning on the dash, and the steering wheel becomes exceptionally heavy and difficult to turn.
- **Solution:** Scan the steering control module for fault codes, check electrical connections, and replace the steering rack or assist motor if defective.

## Exterior

### Sunroof and Structure

Exterior panels are generally durable, though the panoramic sunroof requires close inspection due to reports of spontaneous glass breakage or detachment.

**Sunroof Shattering or Detachment**
- **Symptoms:** The panoramic glass sunroof may explode spontaneously while driving, or trim panels and glass sections may loosen and detach at highway speeds.
- **Solution:** Inspect sunroof seals, glass integrity, and mounting hardware, and replace damaged roof assemblies.

## Safety

### Seat Belt

Safety equipment generally performs well in crash testing, but owners have logged notable complaints regarding malfunctioning seat belt retractors.

**Seat Belt Retraction Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The driver or passenger seat belt retracts fully and becomes stuck inside the B-pillar, rendering it completely inoperable.
- **Solution:** Inspect the seat belt assembly and mechanism, and replace the retractor unit if it fails to release.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V456000: Structure:Body](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=C300&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** If the beltline trim were to separate from the vehicle while driving, it could become a road hazard, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** MBUSA will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the rear beltline trim and replace the trim if necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V787000: Communication](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=C300&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** For a full list of the affected models visit: https://static.nhtsa.gov/odi/rcl/2019/RMISC-19V787-4620.pdf An inaccurate vehicle location may delay emergency responders, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** MBUSA will also notify owners, and dealers will check software for a successful OTA or update the communication module software as needed, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V850000: Steering:Rack And Pinion:Rack](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=C300&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** A car that cannot be properly steered has an increased risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** MBUSA will notify owners, and dealers will replace the steering rack, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V197000: Structure:Body:Roof And Pillars](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=C300&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** The front roof panel located between the windshield and the panoramic sunroof may not have been bonded correctly to the vehicle during a prior repair. The adhesive bonding of the front panel might deteriorate, possibly causing the front panel to detach from the vehicle and become a road hazard, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Mercedes-Benz dealer will remove the bonded panel and will install a new front panel by following the detailed repair instructions and using the approved primer, cleaner and adhesive, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V130000: Seats](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=C300&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** The seatbacks of the front seats may not properly lock on the right side of the seat. If the seatback does not properly lock, in the event of a crash, the seatbacks may fold forward, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Mercedes will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the locking of the seatback on the affected vehicles and replace the seatback lock, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V072000: Communication](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=C300&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** The communication module might have been inadvertently deactivated during a service visit, which could have disabled the Emergency Call (eCall) function. A disabled eCall system would prevent a vehicle occupant from contacting emergency services through the call center in an emergency, potentially delaying emergency responders and increasing the risk of.
- **What to do:** MBUSA will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the communication module and activate it, if necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V058000: Electrical System:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=C300&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** The software design of the communication module may fail to communicate the correct vehicle location for the emergency call system (eCall) in the event of a crash. Failure of the eCall system may result in emergency responders being dispatched to the wrong location, increasing the risk of injury following a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 21V032000: Seat Belts:Front:Retractor](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=C300&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 208, "Occupant Crash Protection." If the seat belt does not fully retract, the occupant may find the operation of the seat belt inconvenient and not wear it, increasing the risk of injury in the event of a crash.
- **What to do:** MBUSA will notify owners, and dealers will check the retraction function of the front seat belts and adjust the damping foam within the B-pillars, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2017 Mercedes-Benz C300 remains a compelling luxury sedan option, provided buyers verify its mechanical and structural condition before purchasing. Pay close attention to potential check engine codes like P052E, check for any fuel odors, and inspect the panoramic sunroof and seat belts for proper operation. Securing a comprehensive maintenance history and professional pre-purchase inspection will help mitigate unexpected repair expenses.
