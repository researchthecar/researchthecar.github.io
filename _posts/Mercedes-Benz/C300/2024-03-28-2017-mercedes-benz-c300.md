**Introduction**

The 2017 Mercedes-Benz C300 is a compact luxury sedan known for its sophisticated design, comfortable interior, and advanced technology. It comes with a range of engine and transmission options. The base model features a 2.0L inline-4 turbocharged engine producing 241 horsepower and 273 lb-ft of torque, paired with a 9-speed automatic transmission. Other engine options include a 3.0L V6 with 362 horsepower and 384 lb-ft of torque, mated to a 9-speed automatic transmission.

While the C300 is generally a reliable vehicle, it has experienced some issues in certain areas.

**Engine**

**2.0L Inline-4 Turbocharged Engine**

* **Engine Misfires**
    * Description/Symptoms: Rough engine idle, hesitation or stumbling during acceleration
    * Solution: Replacing spark plugs, ignition coils, or fuel injectors
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Oil Leaks**
    * Description/Symptoms: Oil pooling under the vehicle, low oil level warning light
    * Solution: Replacing gaskets or seals
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**3.0L V6 Engine**

* **No Issues Found**

**Transmission**

**9-Speed Automatic Transmission**

* **Jerky Shifting**
    * Description/Symptoms: Transmission hesitates or lurches during gear changes
    * Solution: Software updates or transmission fluid replacement
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Transmission Slippage**
    * Description/Symptoms: Vehicle fails to accelerate properly, transmission warning light
    * Solution: Replacing transmission components (e.g., clutch packs, solenoids)
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $5,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **No Issues Found**

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain**
    * Description/Symptoms: Vehicle struggles to start or battery dies prematurely
    * Solution: Identifying and fixing electrical draw (e.g., faulty alternator, parasitic drain)
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Electrical Malfunctions**
    * Description/Symptoms: Intermittent or non-functional electrical components (e.g., lights, windows)
    * Solution: Replacing electrical modules, wiring harnesses, or switches
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Headlight Condensation**
    * Description/Symptoms: Moisture accumulation inside headlight housings
    * Solution: Replacing headlight assemblies
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+ per headlight
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Sunroof Problems**
    * Description/Symptoms: Sunroof rattles, leaks, or fails to operate properly
    * Solution: Adjusting, repairing, or replacing sunroof mechanism
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Climate Control Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: AC not blowing cold, heating not working properly
    * Solution: Diagnosing and repairing AC or heating system (e.g., compressor, blower motor, blend door)
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

Overall, the 2017 Mercedes-Benz C300 is a reliable vehicle, but it has experienced some issues in certain areas, particularly with its engine and transmission. While the severity of these issues varies, the estimated cost to fix them can range from minor to significant. It is important for owners to be aware of potential problems and address them promptly to avoid further expenses or safety concerns.
