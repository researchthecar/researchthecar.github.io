# 2017 Mercedes-Benz GLS350d Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2017 Mercedes-Benz GLS-Class delivers high-end luxury, spacious three-row seating, and robust powertrain options. However, prospective buyers should carefully evaluate maintenance histories and common owner-reported trouble spots, particularly regarding advanced electronics, air suspension systems, and fuel components.

---

## Engine

### 3.0L V6 Turbo Diesel (GLS350d)

A 3.0-liter turbocharged V6 diesel engine offering strong torque and improved highway fuel efficiency, paired with standard luxury hauling capability. Routine maintenance and thorough inspections of fuel and emissions systems are recommended for used buyers.

**Fuel System and Rail Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Fuel odors around the vehicle and potential fuel leaks under the hood.
- **Solution:** Inspect fuel rails and lines for leaks; replace affected components as necessary.

### 3.0L V6 Twin-Turbo Gasoline (GLS450)

A 3.0-liter biturbo V6 gasoline engine producing around 362 horsepower, providing smooth and responsive acceleration. It serves as a balanced mainstream choice for daily driving, though proper battery and charging system upkeep is vital.

**Engine Compartment Smoke or Electrical Draw**
- **Symptoms:** Unusual smoke from the hood area or battery drain issues triggering check engine lights.
- **Solution:** Diagnose electrical resistance faults, replace weak batteries, or inspect engine wiring and components.

### 4.7L V8 Twin-Turbo Gasoline (GLS550)

A potent 4.7-liter twin-turbo V8 engine generating approximately 449 horsepower for robust towing and rapid acceleration. Buyers should prioritize service records verifying that high-pressure fuel components have been inspected.

**Fuel Rail Pressures and Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Strong fuel smells and sudden loss of engine power while driving.
- **Solution:** Check for recalled or compromised fuel rails and have professional technicians replace leaking hardware.

### 5.5L V8 Twin-Turbo Gasoline (AMG GLS 63)

A high-performance hand-built 5.5-liter twin-turbo V8 pumping out 577 horsepower for aggressive performance. It demands meticulous maintenance history and higher operating budgets.

## Transmission

### 9G-TRONIC 9-Speed Automatic

A 9-speed automatic transmission designed to optimize fuel economy and deliver smooth gear changes across standard models. Regular fluid changes help maintain longevity, though complex electronic shifters require thorough pre-purchase checks.

**Unpredictable Shifting and Park Engagement Failures**
- **Symptoms:** Transmission fails to properly engage Park, or shifts unexpectedly without driver input.
- **Solution:** Inspect valve body assembly, software calibrations, and shift linkage components.

### AMG SPEEDSHIFT PLUS 7G-TRONIC 7-Speed Automatic

A reinforced 7-speed automatic transmission tuned for high-output AMG performance models with quicker shift responses. Buyers should ensure smooth operation across all driving modes during a test drive.

## Suspension

### AIRMATIC Air Suspension

The adaptive air suspension provides a plush, adjustable ride quality typical of flagship luxury SUVs. Used buyers should inspect air springs and compressors carefully, as replacement costs can be substantial.

**Air Suspension Failure and Loss of Ride Height**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle drops, fails to inflate air springs, results in a very bumpy ride, and can cause temporary loss of vehicle control.
- **Solution:** Inspect air struts, compressors, and pneumatic lines; replace failed components.
- **Cost:** $3900

## Steering

### Power Steering System

The electronic power steering setup provides light maneuverability at low speeds and firm road feel on the highway. Verify that steering assist operates smoothly without warning lights.

**Electric Power Steering Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Power steering malfunction message displays on the dashboard, followed by a complete loss of power steering assistance while driving.
- **Solution:** Diagnose electronic steering module faults, wiring harnesses, or steering rack assemblies.

## Exterior

### Exterior Lighting

Exterior lighting systems provide automated high beams and dynamic illumination, though aging assemblies can experience diminished output over time.

**Headlight Intensity Reduction and Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Fading low beam headlights or humming noises from the headlamp housing leading to poor forward night visibility.
- **Solution:** Inspect headlamp assemblies, internal ballasts, and bulbs; replace failing units.

## Interior

### Seats and Adjustments

Cabin seating offers multi-way adjustments and heating features, though interior electrical controls can occasionally experience motor or heating element glitches.

**Seat Heater Malfunction and Power Adjustment Jams**
- **Symptoms:** Seat heaters become excessively hot and burn passengers, or power seat tracks fail to move forward and backward properly.
- **Solution:** Inspect seat heating elements, control modules, and under-seat adjustment motors; repair or replace as needed.

## Safety

### Driver Assistance and Collision Systems

Advanced safety suites enhance highway confidence and collision mitigation, though sensor calibration is critical for proper operation.

**Unexpected Lane Departure or Braking Behavior**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle abruptly swerves out of its lane while using lane keeping assist or fails to apply emergency brakes automatically.
- **Solution:** Check camera and radar sensor calibrations, clear lens obstructions, and update system software.

The 2017 Mercedes-Benz GLS-Class is a capable and ultra-luxurious family cruiser with strong engine choices and spacious accommodations.
