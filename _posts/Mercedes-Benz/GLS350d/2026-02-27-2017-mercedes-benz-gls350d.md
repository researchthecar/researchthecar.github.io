# 2017 Mercedes-Benz GLS350d

### Overview
The 2017 Mercedes-Benz GLS350d, a full-size luxury SUV, offers a blend of comfort, capability, and robust performance. Powered by a potent diesel engine, it's designed for long-distance cruising and towing. While generally well-regarded for its build quality and luxury appointments, like any vehicle, it has specific areas that have seen more attention from owners regarding reliability. This analysis focuses on documented technical issues beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential concerns for owners and prospective buyers of this model year.

---


## Engine

### 3.0L V6 Turbo Diesel (OM642)
**Diesel Particulate Filter (DPF) Regeneration Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Check engine light, reduced engine power, increased fuel consumption, exhaust smell.
- **Solution:** Forced regeneration, DPF cleaning, or replacement.
- **Cost:** $300 - $1500

**AdBlue/DEF System Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** AdBlue warning lights, reduced engine performance, potential inability to start.
- **Solution:** Refilling AdBlue tank, sensor replacement, or injector cleaning/replacement.
- **Cost:** $150 - $800

**Glow Plug Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Difficulty starting in cold weather, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of faulty glow plugs and potentially the control module.
- **Cost:** $400 - $900

## Suspension

### AIRMATIC Air Suspension
**Air Strut Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle sagging on one or more corners, uneven ride height, compressor running excessively.
- **Solution:** Replacement of leaking air struts or seals.
- **Cost:** $600 - $1200 per corner

**Air Compressor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Inability to raise or lower the vehicle, constant compressor running, warning lights.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the air suspension compressor.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1000

### Summary Verdict
The 2017 Mercedes-Benz GLS350d is a capable and luxurious SUV with a generally solid mechanical foundation. The primary areas of concern tend to revolve around the diesel engine's emissions system (DPF and AdBlue) and the AIRMATIC air suspension. These components, while advanced, can be costly to repair if they fail. Electrical issues like battery drain are also noted, though not excessively common. For prospective buyers, a thorough pre-purchase inspection, especially focusing on the aforementioned systems, is highly recommended. Regular maintenance adhering to Mercedes-Benz's schedule is crucial for mitigating potential issues and ensuring long-term reliability.
