**2010 Mercedes-Benz Torino**

The 2010 Mercedes-Benz Torino, also known as the W216, is a four-door full-size luxury sedan produced from 2007 to 2013. It was available with a range of engine and transmission options.

The Torino was praised for its luxurious interior, comfortable ride, and powerful engines. However, it was also criticized for its high price and complex electronics.

**Engine**

* **5.5L V8 (M273)**

   * **Camshaft Adjusters**
      * Description/Symptoms: The camshaft adjusters may fail, leading to reduced engine power and fuel economy.
      * Solution: Replace the camshaft adjusters.
      * Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,200+
      * Recalls: NHTSA Campaign Number: 11V323000

   * **Valve Stem Seals**
      * Description/Symptoms: The valve stem seals may leak, causing excessive oil consumption and emissions.
      * Solution: Replace the valve stem seals.
      * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,200 - $1,800+

* **6.3L V8 (M275)**

   * **Timing Chain Tensioner**
      * Description/Symptoms: The timing chain tensioner may fail, leading to a stretched timing chain and potential engine damage.
      * Solution: Replace the timing chain tensioner and timing chain.
      * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,000+
      * Recalls: NHTSA Campaign Number: 12V211000

**Transmission**

* **5-speed Automatic (5G-Tronic)**

   * **Torque Converter**
      * Description/Symptoms: The torque converter may lock up prematurely or fail, resulting in transmission slipping or shuddering.
      * Solution: Replace the torque converter.
      * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
      * Recalls: None Found

* **7-speed Automatic (7G-Tronic)**

   * **Transmission Control Module**
      * Description/Symptoms: The transmission control module may malfunction, causing shifting problems or transmission failure.
      * Solution: Replace the transmission control module.
      * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
      * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Air Suspension**

   * **Air Struts**
      * Description/Symptoms: The air struts may leak or fail, resulting in reduced ride height or excessive bouncing.
      * Solution: Replace the air struts.
      * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+ per strut
      * Recalls: None Found

* **Conventional Suspension**

   * **No Issues Found**

**Electrical**

* **Battery**

   * **Battery Drain**
      * Description/Symptoms: The battery may discharge prematurely, leaving the vehicle unable to start.
      * Solution: Check for parasitic drains and replace the battery if necessary.
      * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+

* **Electrical System**

   * **Wiring Harness**
      * Description/Symptoms: The wiring harness may develop shorts or faults, leading to electrical problems throughout the vehicle.
      * Solution: Diagnose and repair the faulty wiring.
      * Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the extent of the damage

**Exterior**

* **Headlights**

   * **Xenon Bulbs**
      * Description/Symptoms: The xenon bulbs may fail prematurely or malfunction.
      * Solution: Replace the xenon bulbs.
      * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+ per bulb

**Interior**

* **Seats**

   * **Seat Control Module**
      * Description/Symptoms: The seat control module may malfunction, causing seat adjustments to stop working.
      * Solution: Replace the seat control module.
      * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+

* **Audio System**

   * **COMAND System**
      * Description/Symptoms: The COMAND infotainment system may experience glitches, such as navigation errors or audio dropouts.
      * Solution: Update the software or replace the COMAND unit.
      * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+

* **Other**

   * **Interior Trim**
      * Description/Symptoms: The interior trim pieces may rattle or become loose.
      * Solution: Tighten or replace the loose trim pieces.
      * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+

**Summary**

The 2010 Mercedes-Benz Torino is a high-end luxury sedan that has been praised for its comfort and performance. However, it is not without its issues. The most common problems reported by owners include camshaft adjuster failure, valve stem seal leaks, torque converter problems, transmission control module failures, air strut leaks, battery drain, electrical wiring issues, xenon bulb failure, seat control module malfunctions, and COMAND system glitches. These issues can vary in severity and cost to repair, ranging from minor inconveniences to major expenses. Potential buyers should be aware of these potential problems before purchasing a used Torino.
