# 2006 Mercedes-Benz CLK350

### Overview
The 2006 Mercedes-Benz CLK350, part of the W209 generation, is a luxury coupe and convertible known for its blend of performance, comfort, and style. Powered by a robust 3.5-liter V6 engine, it offers a refined driving experience. While generally well-regarded for its build quality and luxury appointments, like any vehicle of this age, it can experience specific issues. This analysis focuses on common technical reliability concerns reported by owners and technicians, excluding routine maintenance. Understanding these potential problem areas can help prospective buyers and current owners manage upkeep and anticipate repair needs.

---


## Engine

### 3.5L V6 (M272)
**Intake Manifold Runner Flap Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Check Engine Light (CEL) illuminated, rough idle, loss of power, P2004/P2005/P2006 error codes.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the intake manifold assembly or repair kit.
- **Cost:** $600 - $1200

**Balance Shaft Gear Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Check Engine Light, rough idle, misfires, P0016/P0017 error codes (crankshaft/camshaft position correlation).
- **Solution:** Replacement of the balance shaft and associated gears, often requiring engine disassembly.
- **Cost:** $2000 - $4000+

**Oil Leaks (Valve Cover Gaskets, Oil Cooler Seals)**
- **Symptoms:** Visible oil leaks, burning oil smell, low oil level.
- **Solution:** Replacement of valve cover gaskets, oil cooler seals, and potentially other seals.
- **Cost:** $400 - $800


## Transmission

### 722.6 5-Speed Automatic
**Conductor Plate Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Harsh shifting, transmission slipping, inability to shift gears, 'F' or 'E' displayed on the gear selector, P0705/P0715 error codes.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the conductor plate and transmission fluid service.
- **Cost:** $700 - $1500

**Torque Converter Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Shuddering at low speeds, slipping, delayed engagement.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the torque converter.
- **Cost:** $1000 - $2000

## Electrical

### General Electrical System
**SAM (Signal Acquisition Module) Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Intermittent electrical problems, malfunctioning lights, power windows, wipers, or other accessories.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and potential repair or replacement of the SAM module.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1500

**Window Regulator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Power windows not operating, slow window movement, grinding noises.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the window regulator assembly.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600

## Interior

### Cabin Components
**Headliner Sagging**
- **Symptoms:** Fabric on the headliner detaches and sags.
- **Solution:** Re-gluing or replacement of the headliner.
- **Cost:** $300 - $700

**HVAC Blower Motor Resistor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** HVAC fan only works on the highest setting or not at all.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the blower motor resistor.
- **Cost:** $150 - $300

### Summary Verdict
The 2006 Mercedes-Benz CLK350 offers a compelling package of luxury and performance, but potential buyers and owners should be aware of specific reliability concerns. The 3.5L V6 engine (M272) is prone to issues with the intake manifold runner flaps and balance shaft gears, which can be costly to repair. The 722.6 5-speed automatic transmission can suffer from conductor plate failures and torque converter problems. Electrical gremlins, particularly related to the SAM module and window regulators, are also noted. While suspension and exterior components are generally robust, interior issues like sagging headliners and HVAC resistor failures can occur. Despite these potential drawbacks, with diligent maintenance and awareness of these common problems, the CLK350 can remain a reliable and enjoyable luxury vehicle.
