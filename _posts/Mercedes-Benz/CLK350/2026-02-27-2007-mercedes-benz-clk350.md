# 2007 Mercedes-Benz CLK350

### Overview
The 2007 Mercedes-Benz CLK350, part of the C209 generation, is generally regarded as a reliable luxury coupe and convertible. It offers a blend of performance, comfort, and Mercedes-Benz's signature build quality. While most owners report a positive ownership experience, like any vehicle, certain components can experience issues over time. This analysis focuses on documented, non-wear-and-tear related problems that may affect the CLK350's long-term dependability, particularly concerning its powertrain and electrical systems. Understanding these potential areas can help prospective buyers and current owners with maintenance planning.

---


## Engine

### 3.5L V6 (M272)
**Intake Manifold Runner Flap Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Check Engine Light (CEL) illuminated, rough idle, poor acceleration, reduced engine power.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the intake manifold assembly or repair kit.
- **Cost:** $600 - $1200

**Balance Shaft Gear Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Check Engine Light (CEL) with codes related to camshaft position sensors, rough idle, poor performance.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the balance shaft and associated gears/sprockets.
- **Cost:** $1500 - $3000


## Transmission

### 722.6 5-Speed Automatic
**Transmission Control Module (TCM) Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Erratic shifting, transmission not engaging gears, limp mode activation, warning lights on the dash.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the TCM.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1500

**Conductor Plate Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Harsh shifting, delayed engagement, transmission slipping, speedometer inaccuracies.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the conductor plate and associated sensors.
- **Cost:** $500 - $900

## Electrical

### General Electrical System
**Window Regulator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Power windows not operating, window getting stuck, unusual noises when operating windows.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the window regulator mechanism.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600

**Headlight Ballast Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Xenon headlights not turning on or flickering, intermittent operation.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the headlight ballast unit.
- **Cost:** $400 - $700

### Summary Verdict
The 2007 Mercedes-Benz CLK350 is a solid choice for a luxury coupe or convertible, with a generally good reliability record. The primary areas of concern revolve around the 3.5L V6 engine, specifically the intake manifold runner flaps and balance shaft gear wear, which can lead to significant repair costs if they fail. The 5-speed automatic transmission can also experience issues with its control module or conductor plate. Electrical components like window regulators and headlight ballasts are also noted potential failure points. While these issues exist, they are not universal, and many CLK350s provide dependable service. Regular maintenance and addressing early warning signs are crucial for mitigating these potential problems and ensuring a positive ownership experience.
