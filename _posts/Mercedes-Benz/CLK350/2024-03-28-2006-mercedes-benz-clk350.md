**Introduction**

The 2006 Mercedes-Benz CLK350 is a luxury coupe powered by a 3.5-liter V6 engine producing 268 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque. It comes with a standard 7-speed automatic transmission. The CLK350 is known for its stylish design, comfortable interior, and powerful engine. However, like any vehicle, it has some common issues that owners should be aware of.

**Engine**

* **3.5L V6**
    * **Oil Leak from Valve Cover Gasket:** Oil seeps from the valve cover gasket, causing a burning oil smell and potential engine damage.
        * Solution: Replace valve cover gasket.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000

**Transmission**

* **7-Speed Automatic**
    * **Hesitation or Jerking:** Transmission hesitates or jerks during gear changes.
        * Solution: Update transmission software or replace transmission solenoids.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    * **Slipping Gears:** Transmission slips out of gear while driving.
        * Solution: Replace transmission filter and fluid, or rebuild/replace transmission.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $3,000+

**Suspension**

* **Front Control Arm Bushings:** Bushings wear prematurely, leading to a rattling noise and unstable handling.
    * Solution: Replace control arm bushings.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain:** Battery discharges quickly due to a faulty trunk light switch.
    * Solution: Replace trunk light switch.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200
* **Central Locking Module Failure:** Central locking system fails to operate, leaving doors unlocked or locked.
    * Solution: Replace central locking module.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600

**Exterior**

* **Rear Window Seal Leak:** Water leaks into the trunk from a faulty rear window seal.
    * Solution: Replace rear window seal.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $300

**Interior**

* **Climate Control Malfunction:** Climate control system fails to regulate temperature properly.
    * Solution: Replace climate control module.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $600+
* **Sunroof Drain Plugs Clogging:** Sunroof drain plugs get clogged, causing water to leak into the cabin.
    * Solution: Clean or replace drain plugs.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200

**Summary**

Overall, the 2006 Mercedes-Benz CLK350 is a reliable vehicle, but it is not without its issues. The most common problems include oil leaks from the valve cover gasket, transmission hesitation or slipping gears, and electrical problems such as battery drain and central locking module failure. These issues can be costly to fix, especially if the transmission requires major repairs. However, if the vehicle is properly maintained and serviced, it can provide many years of enjoyable driving.
