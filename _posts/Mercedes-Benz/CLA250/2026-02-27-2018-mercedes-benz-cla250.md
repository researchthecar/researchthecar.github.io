# 2018 Mercedes-Benz CLA250 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2018 Mercedes-Benz CLA250 combines sleek styling with an accessible entry point into luxury ownership, but prospective buyers should carefully weigh potential repair histories. While it offers engaging driving dynamics, owner reports indicate specific problem areas ranging from electrical glitches to mechanical wear. Researching maintenance records and verifying past repairs will help ensure a smoother ownership experience.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4

The 2.0-liter turbocharged inline-4 engine produces 208 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque, providing brisk acceleration for daily driving. It generally delivers solid performance, though owners should monitor fuel and ignition systems for wear.

**Fuel System Corrosion**
- **Symptoms:** Sudden loss of engine power, multiple misfires, and a lack of acceleration during operation.
- **Solution:** Inspect the fuel system components and replace damaged parts or clean corrosion as necessary.

## Transmission

### 7-Speed Dual-Clutch Automatic

The 7-speed dual-clutch automatic transmission routes power smoothly in front-wheel-drive and 4MATIC all-wheel-drive configurations. Regular fluid service helps maintain shift quality over higher mileage.

## Suspension

### Wheels and Rims

Suspension and wheel components handle rough roads adequately, though low-profile tires and factory rims can be vulnerable to impacts.

**Cracked Factory Rims**
- **Symptoms:** Tires slowly lose air pressure and the low tire pressure warning light illuminates on the dash.
- **Solution:** Inspect alloy wheels for structural cracks and replace damaged rims.

## Steering

### Clock Spring and Steering Controls

Safety components generally perform well, but steering column electrical faults require attention.

**Clock Spring Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Steering wheel buttons stop working, the horn fails to operate, and an abnormal clicking sound occurs when turning.
- **Solution:** Replace the steering column clock spring assembly to restore electrical continuity.

## Electrical

### Instrument Cluster and Dashboard

Electrical complaints often center around dashboard displays and steering wheel controls.

**Cluster Failure and Flickering**
- **Symptoms:** Instrument cluster gauges display inaccurate information or the entire dashboard turns on and off intermittently.
- **Solution:** Diagnose the instrument cluster module and replace faulty wiring or the display unit if required.

## Exterior

### Roof and Sunroof Assembly

Exterior body seals and panoramic roof components warrant a thorough inspection for water intrusion.

**Sunroof Panel Detachment and Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Water enters the cabin through the overhead console during car washes, or exterior roof panels loosen and detach.
- **Solution:** Inspect sunroof seals, realign or re-secure loose exterior roof panels, and clear drain tubes.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V514000: Air Bags:Sensor:Occupant Classification](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=CLA250&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** The Occupant Classification System (OCS) may not be properly calibrated, resulting in the front passenger air bag not being deactivated if a child seat is in the front seat. If a child seat is in the front seat and the passenger air bag is not deactivated, in the event of a crash, it can increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** MBUSA will notify owners, and dealers will replace the front passenger seat cushion, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V892000: Air Bags:Sensor:Occupant Classification](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=CLA250&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** The Occupant Classification System (OCS) may have been improperly calibrated, which may prevent the proper deployment of the front passenger air bag. In the event of a crash, if the air bag does not deploy as designed, the front seat passenger has an increased risk of an injury.
- **What to do:** MBUSA will notify owners, and dealers will replace the passenger seat cushion, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V131000: Exterior Lighting:Tail Lights](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=CLA250&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** Reduced visibility increases the risk of crash.
- **What to do:** MBUSA will notify owners, and dealers will replace the affected light bulb sockets, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2018 Mercedes-Benz CLA250 offers sporty styling and luxury appointments, but buyers should approach with a mindset toward thorough pre-purchase inspections. Pay close attention to electrical functions, steering column components, and sunroof seals to avoid unexpected post-purchase repair bills. A well-maintained example can provide a rewarding ownership experience if potential trouble spots have already been addressed.
