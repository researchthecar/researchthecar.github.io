# 2013 Mercedes-Benz CLA250

### Overview
The 2013 Mercedes-Benz CLA250, while offering a stylish and sporty entry into the luxury compact sedan segment, has shown some areas of concern regarding its long-term reliability. As a first-generation model, some initial kinks were to be expected. Owners have reported a few recurring issues, particularly with certain powertrain components and the infotainment system. However, many of these problems are not universal, and a significant number of CLA250s have performed reliably with proper maintenance. It's crucial for potential buyers to be aware of these common complaints to make an informed decision and budget for potential repairs.

---


## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4 (M270)
**Turbocharger Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of power, whistling noises from the engine bay, increased oil consumption.
- **Solution:** Turbocharger replacement or repair.
- **Cost:** $1,200 - $2,500

**Oil Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Visible oil spots under the vehicle, burning oil smell, low oil level warnings.
- **Solution:** Replacement of seals, gaskets, or oil pan.
- **Cost:** $300 - $800


## Transmission

### 7-Speed Dual-Clutch Automatic (7G-DCT)
**Harsh Shifting / Jerking**
- **Symptoms:** Rough gear changes, hesitation during acceleration, jerky movements at low speeds.
- **Solution:** Transmission fluid flush and filter replacement, software update, or transmission control module (TCM) repair/replacement.
- **Cost:** $400 - $1,500

**Transmission Fluid Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Leaking fluid from the transmission housing, burning smell, difficulty shifting gears.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of seals, lines, or transmission pan.
- **Cost:** $300 - $900

### Summary Verdict
The 2013 Mercedes-Benz CLA250 presents a mixed reliability picture. While the core mechanical components like the engine and transmission are generally robust, owners have encountered issues with the turbocharger, oil leaks, and particularly the dual-clutch transmission's shifting behavior. Electrical gremlins, especially concerning the infotainment system and battery drain, are also notable. The suspension, exterior, and interior components appear to be more reliable. Prospective buyers should carefully inspect any used CLA250 for these specific issues and factor in potential repair costs for the transmission and electrical systems.
