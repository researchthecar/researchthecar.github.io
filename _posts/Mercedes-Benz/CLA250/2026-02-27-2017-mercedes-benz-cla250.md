# 2017 Mercedes-Benz CLA250 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used Mercedes-Benz CLA250, buyers will find a stylish and engaging entry-level luxury sedan powered by a turbocharged four-cylinder engine. While it offers a sharp driving dynamic and premium interior packaging, prospective owners should review owner-reported complaints regarding electrical anomalies, powertrain concerns, and exterior body hardware. Being mindful of these potential trouble spots helps ensure a more informed purchasing decision.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4

The 2.0-liter turbocharged inline-4 engine produces around 208 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque, delivering brisk acceleration for daily driving. It is generally responsive, though routine maintenance of ancillary cooling and crankcase ventilation parts is vital for long-term health.

**Crankcase Ventilation Valve Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Illuminated check engine light, unusual odors, and delayed throttle response.
- **Solution:** Replace the faulty partial-load ventilation valve and associated hoses.

## Transmission

### 7-Speed Dual-Clutch Automatic

The 7-speed dual-clutch automatic transmission delivers rapid gear shifts suited to the sporty character of the front-wheel-drive or 4MATIC all-wheel-drive CLA250. Buyers should test-drive thoroughly to ensure smooth engagement across all gears.

**Transmission Disengagement and Half-Shaft Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Sudden loss of drive power, inability to engage drive or reverse gears while maneuvering.
- **Solution:** Inspect and replace damaged half-shafts or repair internal transmission components as required.

## Suspension

### Wheels and Rims

Suspension components provide a firm, sporty ride quality, though low-profile tires and factory alloy wheels can be vulnerable to impact damage.

**Wheel Rim Cracking**
- **Symptoms:** Air loss from tires or visible fractures on alloy wheel rims during normal driving conditions.
- **Solution:** Inspect factory alloy wheels for structural cracks and replace damaged rims.

## Electrical

### Instrument Cluster and Electrical System

Complex vehicle electronics require a thorough diagnostic scan during pre-purchase inspections to catch latent electrical glitches.

**Instrument Cluster Display Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The dashboard instrument screen intermittently goes blank or displays incorrect driving information.
- **Solution:** Diagnose the instrument cluster module and update software or replace faulty wiring harnesses.

## Exterior

### Panoramic Sunroof

Exterior panels and trim are generally robust, but the panoramic sunroof requires careful inspection for structural security.

**Sunroof Glass Detachment or Shattering**
- **Symptoms:** Loud popping noises followed by the glass panel detaching, cracking, or completely flying off while driving at highway speeds.
- **Solution:** Inspect the sunroof assembly frame and replace damaged or compromised glass panels with properly bonded units.

## Safety

### Air Bag System

Safety equipment functions as expected in most conditions, though owners have reported rare component anomalies requiring dealer attention.

**Spontaneous Air Bag Deployment or Warning Delays**
- **Symptoms:** Air bags deploying unexpectedly without prior impact or vehicle collision.
- **Solution:** Inspect the supplemental restraint system wiring and sensors for faults or replacement parts availability.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V229000: Steering](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=CLA250&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** The steering shaft bore hole for the universal joint bearing may allow excessive movement that, over time, could result in the separation of the bearing and universal joint. A separated universal joint bearing can cause a loss of steering control and increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** MBUSA will notify owners, and dealers will inspect, and as necessary, replace the steering shaft, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V892000: Air Bags:Sensor:Occupant Classification](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=CLA250&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** The Occupant Classification System (OCS) may have been improperly calibrated, which may prevent the proper deployment of the front passenger air bag. In the event of a crash, if the air bag does not deploy as designed, the front seat passenger has an increased risk of an injury.
- **What to do:** MBUSA will notify owners, and dealers will replace the passenger seat cushion, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V197000: Structure:Body:Roof And Pillars](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=CLA250&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** The front roof panel located between the windshield and the panoramic sunroof may not have been bonded correctly to the vehicle during a prior repair. The adhesive bonding of the front panel might deteriorate, possibly causing the front panel to detach from the vehicle and become a road hazard, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Mercedes-Benz dealer will remove the bonded panel and will install a new front panel by following the detailed repair instructions and using the approved primer, cleaner and adhesive, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V072000: Communication](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=CLA250&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** The communication module might have been inadvertently deactivated during a service visit, which could have disabled the Emergency Call (eCall) function. A disabled eCall system would prevent a vehicle occupant from contacting emergency services through the call center in an emergency, potentially delaying emergency responders and increasing the risk of.
- **What to do:** MBUSA will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the communication module and activate it, if necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V058000: Electrical System:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=CLA250&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** The software design of the communication module may fail to communicate the correct vehicle location for the emergency call system (eCall) in the event of a crash. Failure of the eCall system may result in emergency responders being dispatched to the wrong location, increasing the risk of injury following a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 17V080000: Air Bags:Frontal:Passenger Side:Inflator Module](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=CLA250&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** The pelvis air bag or the front passenger air bag inflator initiator may fail to ignite in the event of a crash. If the pelvis airbag or the front passenger airbag does not deploy as intended in the event of a crash, the occupants have an increased risk of injury.
- **What to do:** MBUSA will notify owners, and dealers will replace the affected air bag modules, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V627000: Air Bags:Frontal](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=CLA250&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** The driver's air bag may unexpectedly deploy due to insufficient grounding of the steering components if an electrostatic discharge occurs and the air bag clockspring is broken. If the driver air bag inadvertently deploys, it can increase the risk of injury and a crash.
- **What to do:** MBUSA will notify owners, and dealers will add sufficient grounding to the steering components, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V114000: Electrical System:Starter Assembly](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=CLA250&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** If the starting current limiter overheats, the surrounding components can melt, increasing the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** MBUSA will notify owners, and dealers will install an additional fuse in the electrical line to the starter, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The Mercedes-Benz CLA250 combines expressive styling and turbocharged performance, making it an appealing choice in the used luxury market. Buyers should prioritize a comprehensive pre-purchase inspection that checks the panoramic sunroof bonding, verifies proper transmission and half-shaft operation, and scans for electrical faults. Keeping a close eye on maintenance records will help mitigate potential ownership surprises.
