# 2016 Mercedes-Benz CLA250 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2016 Mercedes-Benz CLA250 combines sleek, coupe-like styling and nimble handling, but prospective buyers should carefully review its reliability history. While it offers an attractive entry point into luxury car ownership, owner complaints highlight potential concerns across the electrical system, powertrain, and exterior glass. Keeping a close eye on maintenance records and previous repairs is essential when shopping for a used model.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4

The 2.0-liter turbocharged inline-4 engine produces approximately 208 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque, delivering spirited performance for daily driving. However, owners have occasionally reported sensor codes, cooling system failures, and check engine lights that require prompt professional attention.

**Engine Stalling and Thermostat Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Illumination of the check engine warning light, potential diagnostic trouble codes such as P052E, and unexpected engine performance issues.
- **Solution:** Take the vehicle to a qualified service center or dealership to diagnose check engine lights and replace failed cooling or emission components as needed.

## Transmission

### 7-Speed Dual-Clutch Automatic Transmission

The 7-speed dual-clutch automatic transmission routes power through front-wheel drive or optional 4MATIC all-wheel drive, offering quick shifts suited to the car's sporty character. A few drivers have experienced unexpected shifts into neutral while underway, requiring careful diagnosis.

**Unintended Neutral Shift**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of motive power while driving, accompanied by a dashboard warning message instructing the driver not to shift into gear and to visit a dealer.
- **Solution:** Have the transmission control module and software inspected by a specialist, as mechanical or electronic diagnostics may be required.

## Electrical

### Infotainment and Dashboard Electronics

Electrical architecture supports advanced infotainment and convenience features, though some owners note occasional issues with display screens and interior lighting power.

**Console and Display Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Dashboard screens, radio consoles, and lighting turning off completely or intermittent black screens during operation.
- **Solution:** Check battery health, fuses, and software firmware versions; software updates or module replacements may be required to resolve recurring blackouts.

## Exterior

### Sunroof and Glass

Exterior styling features sleek body lines and available panoramic glass roofs that enhance cabin light. Owners have occasionally reported rare instances of spontaneous glass fracture on highway drives.

**Spontaneous Sunroof Shattering**
- **Symptoms:** A loud explosion-like noise followed by the sunroof glass fracturing or shattering completely while driving at highway speeds.
- **Solution:** Inspect the panoramic roof for any existing micro-cracks or bonding issues, and replace damaged glass assemblies with updated factory components.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V787000: Communication](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=CLA250&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** For a full list of the affected models visit: https://static.nhtsa.gov/odi/rcl/2019/RMISC-19V787-4620.pdf An inaccurate vehicle location may delay emergency responders, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** MBUSA will also notify owners, and dealers will check software for a successful OTA or update the communication module software as needed, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V892000: Air Bags:Sensor:Occupant Classification](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=CLA250&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** The Occupant Classification System (OCS) may have been improperly calibrated, which may prevent the proper deployment of the front passenger air bag. In the event of a crash, if the air bag does not deploy as designed, the front seat passenger has an increased risk of an injury.
- **What to do:** MBUSA will notify owners, and dealers will replace the passenger seat cushion, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V197000: Structure:Body:Roof And Pillars](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=CLA250&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** The front roof panel located between the windshield and the panoramic sunroof may not have been bonded correctly to the vehicle during a prior repair. The adhesive bonding of the front panel might deteriorate, possibly causing the front panel to detach from the vehicle and become a road hazard, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Mercedes-Benz dealer will remove the bonded panel and will install a new front panel by following the detailed repair instructions and using the approved primer, cleaner and adhesive, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V058000: Electrical System:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=CLA250&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** The software design of the communication module may fail to communicate the correct vehicle location for the emergency call system (eCall) in the event of a crash. Failure of the eCall system may result in emergency responders being dispatched to the wrong location, increasing the risk of injury following a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 17V627000: Air Bags:Frontal](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=CLA250&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** The driver's air bag may unexpectedly deploy due to insufficient grounding of the steering components if an electrostatic discharge occurs and the air bag clockspring is broken. If the driver air bag inadvertently deploys, it can increase the risk of injury and a crash.
- **What to do:** MBUSA will notify owners, and dealers will add sufficient grounding to the steering components, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V114000: Electrical System:Starter Assembly](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=CLA250&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** If the starting current limiter overheats, the surrounding components can melt, increasing the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** MBUSA will notify owners, and dealers will install an additional fuse in the electrical line to the starter, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V177000: Electrical System:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=CLA250&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** Various control units on these vehicles may have been updated with incorrect software, potentially affecting the correct deployment of the air bags in the event of a crash. If the air bags do not deploy as intended in the event of a crash, there would be an increased risk of injury in the event of a crash.
- **What to do:** MBUSA will notify owners, and dealers will update software in the affected control units, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V854000: Structure:Body:Roof And Pillars](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=CLA250&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** The front roof panel located between the windshield and the panoramic sunroof may not have been bonded correctly to the vehicle during a prior repair, allowing the front panel to detach from the vehicle. A detached roof panel can become a road hazard, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the front panel, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2016 Mercedes-Benz CLA250 remains an appealing option for buyers seeking luxury style on a budget, but thorough pre-purchase inspections are strongly advised. Potential shoppers should verify the health of the dual-clutch transmission, inspect electrical accessories, and check for any history of cooling or sunroof issues. Selecting a well-documented example can help mitigate unexpected repair surprises.
