# 2018 Mercedes-Benz AMG GT C Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2018 Mercedes-Benz AMG GT C represents the pinnacle of high-performance grand touring, combining aggressive styling with a hand-built twin-turbo V8. As a low-volume, high-performance exotic sports car, prospective buyers should focus heavily on proper maintenance records and mechanical inspections rather than widespread consumer complaint trends. While everyday reliability data is scarce due to limited production numbers, ownership costs and mechanical upkeep for high-end AMG models require careful budgeting.

---

## Engine

### 4.0L Twin-Turbo V8

Equipped with a 4.0-liter biturbo V8 engine generating approximately 550 horsepower and 502 lb-ft of torque. This powerplant delivers blistering performance, though proper cooling and routine high-end maintenance are critical for long-term health.

## Transmission

### 7-Speed Dual-Clutch Automatic

Features a rear-mounted 7-speed dual-clutch transmission paired with a transaxle layout for optimal weight distribution. It shifts rapidly under hard driving, though urban low-speed manners can occasionally feel firm.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V724000: Electrical System:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=AMG+GT+C&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** Due to a software incompatibility, in the event of a low speed crash, both stages of the frontal air bags may deploy instead of only the first-stage deploying as intended. If the air bags do not deploy as intended, it can increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** MBUSA will notify owners, and dealers will update the air bag control unit software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V058000: Electrical System:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=AMG+GT+C&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** The software design of the communication module may fail to communicate the correct vehicle location for the emergency call system (eCall) in the event of a crash. Failure of the eCall system may result in emergency responders being dispatched to the wrong location, increasing the risk of injury following a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 18V208000: Air Bags:Frontal:Driver Side:Inflator Module](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=AMG+GT+C&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** In the event of an air bag deployment, the defective steel may cause the inflator to improperly inflate the driver-side air bag and potentially rupture. In the event of a crash involving driver air bag deployment, an improperly inflated air bag and/or an inflator rupture with sharp metal fragments might result in serious injury or death to the driver or to.
- **What to do:** MBUSA will notify owners, and dealers will replace the front driver air bag module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V826000: Seat Belts](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=AMG+GT+C&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** If the seat belt binds, slack may develop through occupant movement which may lead to increased personal injury in the event of a crash.
- **What to do:** MBUSA will notify owners, and dealers will reconfigure the seat belt guides, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.
