# 2024 Mercedes-Benz EQE 500 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2024 Mercedes-Benz EQE 500 represents the brand's modern luxury electric vehicle lineup, offering advanced technology, refined interior comfort, and dual-motor all-wheel drive performance. As a relatively new luxury EV offering in the pre-owned market, prospective buyers should evaluate both its high-tech cabin features and its complex electrical architecture to ensure a trouble-free ownership experience.

---

## Engine

### Dual Permanently Excited Synchronous Electric Motors

The EQE 500 utilizes dual permanently excited synchronous electric motors generating approximately 402 horsepower and 633 lb-ft of torque paired with all-wheel drive. These motors deliver swift, silent acceleration and robust long-term reliability compared to traditional internal combustion engines.

## Transmission

### Single-Speed Fixed Gear Transmission

Power is routed to the wheels via a single-speed fixed-gear automatic transmission engineered specifically for high-output electric powertrains. This direct-drive setup eliminates traditional gear shifting wear and provides seamless power delivery across all driving conditions.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V372000: Electrical System:Propulsion System:Traction Battery:Management System/Energy Control Module (Bms/Becm):Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=EQE+500&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** The battery management system software may cause the high-voltage battery to shut down, which can result in a sudden loss of drive power. A sudden loss of drive power increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the battery management system software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V115000: Electrical System:Wiring:Fuses And Circuit Breakers](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=EQE+500&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** An 80-Amp fuse that fails can result in a sudden loss of drive power or a loss of critical safety functions such as the restraint systems or instrument cluster, increasing the risk of a crash or injury. Additionally, there could be an increased risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the main fuse boxes, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2024 Mercedes-Benz EQE 500 offers a compelling blend of electric performance and luxury features for used-car shoppers. While powertrain reliability for its electric motors and single-speed transmission is inherently strong, buyers should thoroughly inspect the vehicle's extensive electrical and infotainment systems during a pre-purchase evaluation.
