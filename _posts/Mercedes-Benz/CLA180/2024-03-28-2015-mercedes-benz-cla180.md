**Introduction**

The 2015 Mercedes-Benz CLA180 is a compact executive sedan powered by a 1.6-liter turbocharged inline-four engine paired with a seven-speed dual-clutch transmission. It delivers 121 horsepower and 147 lb-ft of torque. The CLA180 is praised for its stylish design, premium interior, and car-like handling, while some criticism is directed toward its limited rear legroom and lack of advanced safety features.

**Engine**

**1.6L Turbocharged Inline-Four Engine**

**Fuel Injector Failure**
Description/Symptoms: Engine hesitation, stalling, poor fuel economy
Solution: Replace faulty fuel injectors
Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,200+
Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**Seven-Speed Dual-Clutch Transmission**

**Clutch Pack Wear**
Description/Symptoms: Slipping, shuddering, trouble shifting gears
Solution: Replace clutch pack
Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

**No Issues Found**

**Electrical**

**Battery Discharge**
Description/Symptoms: Battery drains quickly, difficulty starting
Solution: Replace defective battery or repair electrical system
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+
Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

**No Issues Found**

**Interior**

**Sunroof Rattle**
Description/Symptoms: Noises coming from sunroof when driving over rough surfaces
Solution: Adjust or replace sunroof seals
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2015 Mercedes-Benz CLA180 has some notable issues, mainly in the engine and transmission. The fuel injector failure can cause significant problems with engine performance and fuel efficiency. The clutch pack wear in the transmission can lead to costly repairs. The battery discharge issue can be frustrating and could result in unexpected breakdowns. The sunroof rattle, while less severe, can still be an annoyance. Overall, these issues can impact the reliability and ownership experience of the CLA180.
