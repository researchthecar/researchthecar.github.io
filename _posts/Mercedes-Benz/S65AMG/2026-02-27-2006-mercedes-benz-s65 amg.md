# 2006 Mercedes-Benz S65 AMG

### Overview
The 2006 Mercedes-Benz S65 AMG, a pinnacle of luxury and performance, is equipped with a formidable twin-turbocharged V12 engine. While offering exhilarating power, its complexity and high-performance nature mean that certain components are more prone to issues than in standard luxury sedans. Owners should be aware of potential challenges, particularly concerning the powertrain and associated systems, which can require specialized maintenance and potentially higher repair costs. Despite its exotic nature, many owners report a rewarding experience when these vehicles are properly maintained and any emerging issues are addressed promptly. This analysis focuses on documented common problems beyond routine maintenance.

---


## Engine

### 6.0L V12 Twin-Turbocharged (M275)
**Turbocharger Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of power, excessive smoke, whistling noises, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of turbochargers.
- **Cost:** $3,000 - $7,000 per turbocharger, plus labor.

**Coolant Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Overheating, visible coolant loss, sweet smell, white residue.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of hoses, seals, or radiator.
- **Cost:** $500 - $2,500 depending on the source of the leak.

**Ignition Coil Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Misfires, rough idling, reduced engine performance, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of faulty ignition coils.
- **Cost:** $200 - $500 per coil, plus labor.

**Oil Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Visible oil spots under the vehicle, burning oil smell, low oil level.
- **Solution:** Replacement of gaskets, seals, or oil pan.
- **Cost:** $400 - $1,200 depending on the location and severity.


## Transmission

### 5-Speed Automatic (AMG Speedshift)
**Torque Converter Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Slipping gears, shuddering during acceleration, delayed engagement.
- **Solution:** Rebuild or replacement of the torque converter.
- **Cost:** $1,500 - $3,500.

**Transmission Control Module (TCM) Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Erratic shifting, transmission warning lights, inability to shift gears.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the TCM.
- **Cost:** $800 - $2,000.


## Suspension

### Active Body Control (ABC) System
**Hydraulic Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle sagging, uneven ride height, suspension warning lights, fluid leaks under the car.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of hydraulic lines, struts, or pump.
- **Cost:** $1,000 - $4,000+ depending on the component.

**ABC Pump Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of hydraulic pressure, suspension warning lights, vehicle may not raise.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the ABC pump.
- **Cost:** $1,000 - $2,500.


## Electrical

### General Electrical System
**SAM Unit (Signal Acquisition Module) Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Various warning lights, malfunctioning lights, power window issues, infotainment glitches.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of the SAM unit.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1,500.

**Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Frequent dead batteries, electrical components malfunctioning intermittently.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and repair of parasitic draws, or battery replacement.
- **Cost:** $300 - $800.

## Interior

### General Interior Components
**Seat Control Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** Power seats not adjusting, memory functions failing.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of seat control modules or motors.
- **Cost:** $300 - $1,000.

**Infotainment System Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Screen freezing, navigation issues, audio problems.
- **Solution:** Software updates or replacement of head unit components.
- **Cost:** $500 - $2,000.

### Summary Verdict
The 2006 Mercedes-Benz S65 AMG is a high-performance luxury vehicle with a complex V12 twin-turbocharged engine and sophisticated systems like Active Body Control. While it offers unparalleled driving dynamics, these advanced features can lead to more frequent and costly repairs compared to less performance-oriented vehicles. Common issues revolve around the powertrain (turbochargers, ignition coils), the ABC suspension system (leaks, pump failures), and various electrical components, particularly the SAM unit. Regular, specialized maintenance is crucial to mitigate these potential problems. Owners should budget for higher-than-average maintenance and repair costs, especially for specialized components.
