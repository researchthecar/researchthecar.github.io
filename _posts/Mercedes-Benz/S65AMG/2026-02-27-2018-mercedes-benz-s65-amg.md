# 2018 Mercedes-Benz S65 AMG Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a luxury flagship sedan, the 2018 Mercedes-Benz S65 AMG delivers incredible handcrafted V12 performance and executive-level comfort. However, prospective buyers should approach this complex machine with awareness of potential mechanical and electrical issues reported by owners. While many high-end features offer unmatched refinement, proper maintenance history and independent pre-purchase inspections are vital due to the high cost of repairs.

---

## Engine

### 6.0L Biturbo V12 (AMG)

The 6.0-liter biturbo V12 engine produces 621 horsepower and a massive 738 lb-ft of torque, providing effortless acceleration for the heavy sedan. This handcrafted powerplant is exceptionally smooth when running properly, but its dense packaging and high output mean repairs can be extremely labor-intensive and costly.

**Engine Misfire and Loss of Power**
- **Symptoms:** Occasional engine misfires during acceleration, accompanied by shaking, a check engine light, and a temporary loss of engine power.
- **Solution:** Diagnose ignition coils, spark plugs, or fuel injectors, and replace faulty components as necessary.

**Oil and Coolant Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Unexplained fluid loss, oil pooling near the rear engine seals, or slow coolant drops despite relatively low mileage.
- **Solution:** Inspect seals and gaskets thoroughly; replace worn rear main seals or leaking coolant lines.

## Transmission

### 7-Speed AMG Speedshift Automatic

The 7-speed AMG Speedshift automatic transmission channels the V12's immense torque to the rear wheels with quick, authoritative gear changes. While generally robust under normal driving conditions, electronic control issues can occasionally surface and require professional diagnosis.

**Unintended Shifting to Neutral**
- **Symptoms:** The gear selector independently shifts into neutral while driving, requiring the driver to hold the shifter down for an extended period to reset.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified technician inspect the electronic shifter module, software calibration, and transmission linkage.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

Brake system complaints emphasize the critical importance of regular brake fluid flushes and component inspections on heavy, high-performance sedans.

**Brake Responsiveness Concerns**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have reported instances where depressing the brake pedal failed to decelerate the vehicle as expected or resulted in sudden loss of braking response.
- **Solution:** Inspect brake pads, rotors, hydraulic lines, and the brake booster system immediately if any braking inconsistency is felt.

## Steering

### Power Steering System

Steering assistance failures can catch drivers completely off guard, making timely diagnosis of electronic assist motors and sensors essential.

**Stiff Steering and Malfunction Warning**
- **Symptoms:** The steering wheel becomes extremely difficult to turn while driving, accompanied by a dashboard warning message to increase physical effort.
- **Solution:** Scan the steering control module for fault codes and replace malfunctioning electronic power steering components or sensors.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V607000: Lane Departure: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=S65+AMG&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** The software calibration for the Active Lane Keeping Assist may be incorrect, and as a result, the system may not intervene in the event of an unintended lane departure while traveling faster than 65 miles an hour. If the Active Lane Keeping Assist does not intervene as expected above 65 miles per hour, there would be an increased risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** MBUSA will notify owners, and dealers will update the multipurpose camera software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2018 Mercedes-Benz S65 AMG is an engineering masterpiece offering supreme luxury and awe-inspiring V12 performance, but it demands careful evaluation before purchase. Owners have reported intermittent engine misfires, electronic gear shifter glitches, and occasional steering or braking concerns. Because replacement parts and labor rates for an AMG V12 flagship are exceptionally high, a comprehensive pre-purchase inspection by a marque specialist is strongly advised.
