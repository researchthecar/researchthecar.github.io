**Introduction**

The 2009 Mercedes-Benz S65 AMG is a high-performance luxury sedan powered by a hand-crafted 6.0L V12 biturbo engine. It boasts a remarkable 604 horsepower and 738 lb-ft of torque, paired with a 5-speed automatic transmission. While the S65 AMG offers an unparalleled driving experience, it has also faced various issues over the years.

**Engine**

**6.0L V12 Biturbo**

* **Turbocharger Failure:** Premature failure of turbochargers, leading to reduced engine power and increased exhaust emissions.
    * Solution: Replace turbochargers.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $3,000 - $5,000+
* **Engine Oil Leaks:** Leaks from various gaskets and seals, causing oil contamination.
    * Solution: Replace faulty gaskets and seals.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500

**Transmission**

**5-Speed Automatic**

* **Slipping Gears:** Transmission failure to engage or stay in gear, resulting in delayed acceleration or loss of power.
    * Solution: Overhaul or replace transmission.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,500 - $5,000+

**Suspension**

* **Airmatic Suspension Compressor Failure:** Malfunctioning compressor leads to suspension failure, resulting in decreased ride height and impaired handling.
    * Solution: Replace airmatic suspension compressor.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
* **Control Arm Bushing Deterioration:** Premature wear of control arm bushings causes excessive vibration and noise during suspension movement.
    * Solution: Replace control arm bushings.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500 per side

**Electrical**

* **Alternator Failure:** Malfunctioning alternator fails to charge the battery, leading to potential electrical system failures.
    * Solution: Replace alternator.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
* **Xenon Headlight Ballast Failure:** Premature failure of headlight ballasts, causing erratic or inoperative lighting.
    * Solution: Replace headlight ballasts.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $700 per side
* **Battery Drain:** Excessive battery discharge when parked, potentially due to faulty electronics or parasitic draw.
    * Solution: Diagnose and repair electrical system components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies widely depending on the issue

**Exterior**

* **Rust on Door Panels:** Corrosion buildup on interior door panels, leading to paint bubbling and structural damage.
    * Solution: Replace affected door panels.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000 per panel

**Interior**

* **Panoramic Sunroof Malfunction:** Electrical or mechanical failure of panoramic sunroof, resulting in inoperative sunroof functionality.
    * Solution: Repair or replace sunroof components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,500+

**Summary**

While the 2009 Mercedes-Benz S65 AMG offers exceptional performance and luxury, its complexity and performance orientation make it susceptible to various issues. The cost of repairs can be significant, ranging from a few hundred dollars for minor issues to several thousand dollars for major component replacements. This should be considered by potential owners before purchasing this high-performance luxury sedan.
