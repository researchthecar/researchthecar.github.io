**Introduction:**

The 1995 Mercedes-Benz E420 is a luxury sedan powered by a 4.2L V8 engine paired with a 4-speed automatic transmission. With 275 horsepower and 295 lb-ft of torque, it offers impressive performance and a smooth ride. Owners appreciate its high-quality interior, advanced safety features, and timeless design. However, the vehicle is not without issues, particularly as it ages.

**Engine**

**4.2L V8**

* **Oil Leaks:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Engine components like gaskets and seals deteriorate over time, causing oil to leak. This can result in low oil levels, engine damage if left unchecked, and visible drips or stains under the vehicle.
    * Solution: Replace the leaking components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Variable Valve Timing (VVT) Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: A malfunction in the VVT system can lead to poor engine performance, decreased fuel efficiency, and check engine lights.
    * Solution: Replace the VVT solenoid or system components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**4-Speed Automatic**

* **Shifting Problems:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Transmission delays, harsh shifts, or slipping gears indicate potential issues with the transmission control module, solenoids, or internal components.
    * Solution: Diagnose the underlying cause and repair or replace faulty components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $3,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Air Suspension Compressor Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Compressor failure can cause the air suspension system to lose pressure, resulting in a lowered ride height or uneven suspension.
    * Solution: Replace the air suspension compressor.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Front Strut Mounts:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Worn strut mounts can create rattling noises over bumps and affect ride quality.
    * Solution: Replace the strut mounts.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Alternator Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: A faulty alternator can lead to electrical system issues, battery drain, or starting problems.
    * Solution: Replace the alternator.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Battery Drain:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Parasitic drain on the battery can cause it to discharge excessively, affecting starting capabilities.
    * Solution: Diagnose and repair the source of the drain, such as faulty modules or electrical components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Rust:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Rust can occur on various body panels and undercarriage components, especially in areas with high humidity or road salt exposure.
    * Solution: Rust repair or replacement of affected parts.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the extent of rust damage

**Interior**

* **Sunroof Operation Problems:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Sunroof may not open or close properly, or become misaligned.
    * Solution: Repair or replace sunroof motor, tracks, or other components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+

**Summary**

While the 1995 Mercedes-Benz E420 offers a luxurious and comfortable driving experience, it has some notable issues that owners should be aware of. The engine oil leaks and VVT failures can be costly repairs, while suspension problems and electrical issues can also impact drivability and safety. The cost of repairs can range from a few hundred dollars to over $3,000 for major components. Therefore, potential buyers should factor in the likelihood of these issues and the associated repair costs when considering this vehicle.
