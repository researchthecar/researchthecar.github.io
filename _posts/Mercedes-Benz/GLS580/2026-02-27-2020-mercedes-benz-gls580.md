# 2020 Mercedes-Benz GLS580 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2020 Mercedes-Benz GLS580 delivers high-end luxury, massive passenger space, and robust twin-turbo V8 performance. However, as a feature-dense German flagship, it demands thorough inspection. Buyers should carefully evaluate potential electrical issues, advanced driver-assist sensor behavior, and under-hood thermal management items before purchasing a pre-owned model.

---

## Engine

### 4.0L Biturbo V8 (M176)

The 4.0-liter biturbo V8 engine produces an effortless 483 horsepower and 516 lb-ft of torque, bolstered by a 48-volt mild-hybrid EQ Boost system. While immensely powerful and refined, the complex under-hood packaging means heat management and wiring durability require close attention.

**Engine Wiring Harness Heat Deterioration**
- **Symptoms:** Intermittent check engine lights, fault codes, or brittle wiring insulation near the ignition coils and turbocharger exhaust areas due to prolonged heat exposure.
- **Solution:** Inspect the main engine wiring harness and replace degraded connectors or sections of wiring before total failure occurs.

**Fuel Rail Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Fuel odors, visible fuel seepage around the passenger-side fuel rail, or related diagnostic trouble codes.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified technician inspect the fuel rail and associated plumbing, and replace leaking components promptly.

## Transmission

### 9G-TRONIC 9-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 9-speed automatic transmission routes power smoothly to the 4MATIC all-wheel-drive system. Most owners experience seamless operation, though occasional software-related shift hesitation has been reported.

**Delayed Downshift and Harsh Engagement**
- **Symptoms:** The transmission fails to downshift sequentially under certain driving conditions, causing sudden gear drops and hanging revs when accelerating.
- **Solution:** Check for available manufacturer software updates or have the transmission control module diagnosed by a specialist.

## Suspension

### Air Suspension and Dampers

The adaptive air suspension provides a cloud-like ride quality and adjustable ride height, though the complexity of the system means component wear can lead to costly repairs over time.

**Premature Shock Absorber Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Excessive bouncing, harsh ride quality, or fluid leaks from the shock absorbers.
- **Solution:** Inspect suspension struts and dampers for wear, and replace failed units as necessary.

## Electrical

### 48V Battery and Infotainment System

The electrical architecture is heavily integrated, driving everything from cabin displays to the mild-hybrid system. While technologically impressive, high-voltage battery components and central control modules can occasionally cause diagnostic headaches.

**48-Volt Battery Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle fails to start, accompanied by a 48V battery warning light on the instrument cluster.
- **Solution:** Replace the 48-volt battery assembly and ensure charging system software is up to date.
- **Cost:** $3000

**Intermittent MBUX Screen Blackout**
- **Symptoms:** The dashboard and infotainment screens randomly freeze or go blank during operation.
- **Solution:** Reboot or update the MBUX software system; inspect display modules if the condition persists.

## Safety

### Forward Collision Avoidance

Advanced safety and driver-assistance suites offer extensive protection but rely heavily on sensitive sensors and data networks that can occasionally misinterpret environmental inputs.

**Unexpected Automatic Emergency Braking**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle experiences sudden, unwarranted automatic emergency braking and tightening of seatbelts on clear roads.
- **Solution:** Clean radar and camera sensor surfaces, check for diagnostic trouble codes, and consult a dealer for calibration updates.

The 2020 Mercedes-Benz GLS580 is an ultra-luxurious, exceptionally capable family cruiser, but its high-tech complexity requires careful shopping.
