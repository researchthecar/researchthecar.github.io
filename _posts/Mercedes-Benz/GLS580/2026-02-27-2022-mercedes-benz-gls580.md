# 2022 Mercedes-Benz GLS580 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a luxury full-size SUV like the 2022 Mercedes-Benz GLS580, buyers expect top-tier performance, advanced technology, and high-end comfort. However, owner reports submitted to safety authorities highlight a few notable areas of concern, particularly involving advanced driver assistance systems, electrical stability, and powertrain glitches. Prospective buyers should carefully review service histories and ensure all software updates have been applied.

---

## Engine

### 4.0L Biturbo V8 with EQ Boost (362-volt mild hybrid system)

The 2022 Mercedes-Benz GLS580 is powered by a robust 4.0-liter biturbo V8 engine paired with a 48-volt mild-hybrid EQ Boost system, generating around 483 horsepower and 516 lb-ft of torque. This engine delivers effortless acceleration and refined highway cruising, though its complex electrical architecture requires vigilant maintenance.

**Electrical and Hybrid System Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have reported warning lights related to the 48-volt battery system, followed by sudden power loss and cooling system errors while driving.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis by a qualified technician is required to check hybrid charging components, software integration, and battery integrity.

## Transmission

### 9G-TRONIC 9-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 9G-TRONIC 9-speed automatic transmission comes paired with standard 4MATIC all-wheel drive, offering smooth and prompt gear changes tailored to the GLS580's heavy luxury footprint. While generally reliable, rare electronic control anomalies have been noted by owners.

**Unexpected Shifting to Neutral**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle unexpectedly shifts into neutral gear by itself while driving, resulting in a temporary loss of motive power.
- **Solution:** Inspect transmission control module software and mechanical linkage for potential faults or required updates.

## Exterior

### Panorama Sunroof

Exterior glass and body trim components are generally durable, though occasional structural stress incidents have been reported with large glass roofs.

**Sudden Glass Shattering**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have experienced the sunroof glass shattering unexpectedly while driving or parked, accompanied by a loud bang.
- **Solution:** Replace the damaged glass panel and inspect the frame for any structural stress or defects.

## Safety

### Forward Collision Avoidance and Lane Assistance

Advanced safety features provide reassuring protection but can occasionally trigger false warnings or unexpected interventions based on owner feedback.

**Phantom Braking and System Deactivation**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may suddenly apply the brakes without an obstruction ahead, or display error messages indicating that visibility and collision avoidance systems are inoperative.
- **Solution:** Have camera and radar sensors inspected, recalibrated, and checked for updated software modules.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V178000: Structure:Body](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=GLS580&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** Detached trim bars may cause a road hazard and increase the risk of a crash or injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and secure the rear window trim bars, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2022 Mercedes-Benz GLS580 is an impressively capable and powerful luxury cruiser, but its heavy reliance on sophisticated electronics means buyers should proceed with due diligence. Reviewing comprehensive vehicle service records for software updates, checking the operation of driver assistance features, and ensuring the mild-hybrid system is functioning properly will help secure a trouble-free ownership experience.
