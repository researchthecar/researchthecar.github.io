# 2021 Mercedes-Benz GLS580

### Overview
The 2021 Mercedes-Benz GLS580 is a luxury full-size SUV known for its opulent interior, advanced technology, and powerful performance. As a flagship model, it generally exhibits strong build quality and reliability, reflecting Mercedes-Benz's commitment to premium engineering. However, like any complex vehicle, specific components can occasionally experience issues. This report focuses on documented technical reliability data, excluding routine maintenance and common wear-and-tear items, to provide a clear picture of potential concerns for owners and prospective buyers.

## Suspension

### AIRMATIC Air Suspension
**Air Spring Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle may sag on one corner, uneven ride height, warning lights on the dashboard.
- **Solution:** Replacement of leaking air spring or associated seals.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1500 per corner

**Compressor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Inability to adjust ride height, constant running of compressor, warning lights.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the air suspension compressor.
- **Cost:** $700 - $1200


## Electrical

### Infotainment System (MBUX)
**Software Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Screen freezing, unresponsive touch controls, intermittent audio issues, navigation errors.
- **Solution:** Software updates or system resets. In some cases, module replacement may be required.
- **Cost:** $150 - $500 (for updates/diagnostics), $1000+ (for module replacement)


### 12V Battery
**Premature Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Various electrical systems malfunctioning, slow cranking, battery warning lights.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the 12V battery.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600

### Summary Verdict
The 2021 Mercedes-Benz GLS580 demonstrates a generally strong reliability record, particularly concerning its powertrain. The 4.0L twin-turbo V8 engine and 9G-TRONIC transmission are robust. The primary areas where owners might encounter issues are related to the AIRMATIC air suspension system, with potential for air spring leaks or compressor failures, and occasional electrical gremlins within the MBUX infotainment system, often resolved with software updates. Premature 12V battery failure has also been noted. Despite these potential concerns, the GLS580 offers a premium and largely dependable ownership experience for a luxury SUV of its complexity.
