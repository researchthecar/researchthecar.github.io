# 2021 Mercedes-Benz GLS580 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2021 Mercedes-Benz GLS580 represents the pinnacle of luxury, offering immense power, advanced technology, and spacious three-row comfort. However, like many modern luxury European flagships, its high-tech electrical architecture and complex systems require careful inspection by prospective buyers. While the vehicle provides a stellar driving experience, electrical issues and 48-volt mild-hybrid system concerns warrant attention.

---

## Engine

### 4.0L Biturbo V8 Mild-Hybrid (GLS580)

The GLS580 is powered by a robust 4.0-liter biturbo V8 paired with a 48-volt mild-hybrid system (EQ Boost), generating approximately 483 horsepower and 516 lb-ft of torque. This engine delivers effortless, smooth acceleration suited for a flagship luxury SUV, but the complexity of its electrified ancillaries demands diligent maintenance histories.

**Fuel Odor**
- **Symptoms:** An abnormally strong gasoline odor inside the passenger cabin without any illuminated warning lights.
- **Solution:** Have the fuel system inspected by a qualified technician to check for fuel line leaks or vapor purge valve issues.

## Transmission

### 9G-TRONIC 9-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 9G-TRONIC 9-speed automatic transmission routes power smoothly to the 4MATIC all-wheel-drive system. It generally offers seamless gear shifts and well-optimized gearing for highway cruising, with no widespread structural complaint patterns noted.

## Suspension

### Wheels and Rims

Heavy vehicle weight combined with large low-profile wheel packages can place stress on factory alloy rims, occasionally leading to structural cracking.

**Rear Wheel Cracking**
- **Symptoms:** Rear wheels develop stress cracks leading to progressive tire pressure loss without any record of impact.
- **Solution:** Inspect alloy wheels thoroughly for hairline fractures and replace compromised wheels.

## Electrical

### 48V Mild-Hybrid & Charging System

Electrical architecture complaints predominantly center on the 48-volt mild-hybrid battery system and digital displays, which can trigger limp-mode scenarios or loss of instrumentation while underway.

**48V Battery and Electrical Shutdown**
- **Symptoms:** Random vehicle shutdowns while driving, accompanied by 48-volt and 12-volt battery error messages, speed restrictions, and sudden multi-media screen blackouts.
- **Solution:** Dealer diagnosis of the 48-volt battery system, potential software updates, or component replacement under warranty bulletins.

**Instrument Cluster Blackout**
- **Symptoms:** The main cluster information display and head-up display intermittently go completely black, preventing the driver from viewing the speedometer.
- **Solution:** Have the software reset, updated, or have the instrument cluster module inspected and replaced if necessary.

## Exterior

### Sunroof and Glass

Exterior complaints highlight vulnerability in large panoramic glass components, which can occasionally experience spontaneous failure.

**Spontaneous Sunroof Shattering**
- **Symptoms:** A sudden loud pop followed by the panoramic sunroof glass shattering or exploding outward without impact.
- **Solution:** Replace the panoramic glass panel and inspect the frame for alignment or stress concentration points.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V354000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=GLS580&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The MBUX multimedia system software may cause the display to remain black, or reboot after startup. This could result in the rearview camera image not displaying properly.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 20V089000: Electrical System: Instrument Cluster/Panel](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=GLS580&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 101, "Control and Displays." If the interior switches are not illuminated, they may be more difficult to find and operate, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** MBUSA will notify owners, and dealers will update the instrument cluster software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V832000: Visibility:Glass, Side/Rear](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=GLS580&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The rear window may be insufficiently bonded to the tailgate, which could allow the rear glass to detach. Detached rear glass can become a road hazard, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the rear window, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V058000: Electrical System:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=GLS580&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The software design of the communication module may fail to communicate the correct vehicle location for the emergency call system (eCall) in the event of a crash. Failure of the eCall system may result in emergency responders being dispatched to the wrong location, increasing the risk of injury following a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 22V231000: Electrical System:Wiring:Interior/Under Dash](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=GLS580&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** A ground cable bolt for the 48V ground connection under the front passenger seat may not be secured properly, which can cause the electrical cable to overheat. An overheated electrical cable can increase the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and tighten the ground cable bolt, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V365000: Electrical System:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=GLS580&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** A disabled eCall system would prevent a vehicle occupant from contacting the emergency services call center in an emergency, potentially delaying emergency responders and increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 22V680000: Structure:Body](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=GLS580&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** Detached trim bars may cause a road hazard and increase the risk of a crash or injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and secure the rear window trim bars, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V732000: Seats:Mid/Rear Assembly](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=GLS580&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** Due to a manufacturing error, the locks on the third-row seatbacks may fail in the event of a crash. A seat with a seatback lock failure may not properly restrain occupants, increasing the risk of injury during a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the third-row seatback, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2021 Mercedes-Benz GLS580 is an impressively capable and luxurious cruiser, but prospective buyers should approach with a thorough pre-purchase inspection. Particular attention should be paid to the vehicle's complex 48-volt electrical system, infotainment displays, and alloy wheel integrity. Securing a warranty or verifying dealer service history can mitigate ownership risks associated with these high-tech luxury systems.
