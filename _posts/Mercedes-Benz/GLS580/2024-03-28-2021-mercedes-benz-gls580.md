**Introduction:**

The 2021 Mercedes-Benz GLS580 is a full-size luxury SUV renowned for its spacious interior, refined performance, and advanced technology. Under the hood, it boasts a 4.0L V8 engine that delivers 483 horsepower and 516 lb-ft of torque, paired with a 9-speed automatic transmission. Despite its impressive specs, the vehicle has faced some issues that owners have reported.

**Engine**

**4.0L V8**

* **Turbocharger Wastegate Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, reduced engine performance, exhaust leaks.
    * Solution: Replace the turbocharger wastegate.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,800 - $2,500+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Oil Leak from Valve Cover:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Oil leaking around the valve cover, engine rattling or ticking noises.
    * Solution: Replace the valve cover gasket.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,200+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**9-Speed Automatic**

* **Shift Delay or Hesitation:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Slow or delayed gear shifts, rough shifting, transmission slipping.
    * Solution: Update transmission software or replace transmission.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $4,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

**Air Suspension**

* **Air Compressor Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Suspension system lowers or does not rise, warning message on the dashboard.
    * Solution: Replace the air compressor.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,200+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Air Spring Leak or Rupture:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Suspension sags on one or more corners, uneven vehicle ride height.
    * Solution: Replace the leaking or ruptured air spring.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $900+ per air spring
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Battery drains quickly, difficulty starting the vehicle.
    * Solution: Identify and isolate the source of the electrical drain.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the source of the drain
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Infotainment System Malfunction:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Touchscreen unresponsive, software glitches, audio system not working.
    * Solution: Update software or replace infotainment system.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Headlight Condensation:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Moisture accumulation inside the headlight housings.
    * Solution: Replace the faulty headlights.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $600 - $1,400+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Sunroof Rattle:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Noise or rattling when the sunroof is closed.
    * Solution: Repair or replace the sunroof brackets to eliminate the noise.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Dashboard Panel Gaps:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Panel gaps or unevenness in the dashboard trim.
    * Solution: Adjust or replace the faulty dashboard panels.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $400+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

The 2021 Mercedes-Benz GLS580 is a well-crafted SUV that offers a comfortable and luxurious driving experience. However, it has faced some reliability issues, particularly with the engine, transmission, and suspension. The severity of these issues varies, with some being relatively minor and others requiring significant repairs. While some issues may be resolved through software updates or component replacements, others can be more costly to fix. It is essential to note that some issues reported by owners may not occur in all vehicles, and the frequency of these problems may vary depending on driving habits and maintenance practices.
