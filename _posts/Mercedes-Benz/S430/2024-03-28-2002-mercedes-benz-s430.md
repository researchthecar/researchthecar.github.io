**Introduction**

The 2002 Mercedes-Benz S430 is a luxury sedan powered by a 4.3-liter naturally aspirated V8 engine producing 275 horsepower and 302 lb-ft of torque. It is paired with a 5-speed automatic transmission. The S430 is known for its plush interior, advanced technology, and smooth ride. However, it has also been subject to several common issues.

**Engine**

* **V8 Engine**
    * **Oil leaks:** Oil leaks can occur from various areas of the engine, including the valve cover gaskets, oil pan gasket, and rear main seal. Symptoms include oil spots on the ground after parking and low oil levels.
    * **Solution:** Replace the leaking gaskets or seals.
    * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $150 - $500+
    * **Recalls:** None Found

**Transmission**

* **5-Speed Automatic Transmission**
    * **Shifting problems:** The transmission may hesitate or jerk when shifting gears. This can be caused by worn or damaged transmission components.
    * **Solution:** Repair or replace the faulty transmission components.
    * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $1,500 - $2,500+
    * **Recalls:** None Found

**Suspension**

* **Front Suspension**
    * **Strut mounts:** Worn or damaged strut mounts can cause clunking or knocking noises from the front of the vehicle.
    * **Solution:** Replace the strut mounts.
    * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $250 - $400
    * **Recalls:** None Found
* **Rear Suspension**
    * **Air suspension failure:** The air suspension system can fail due to leaks or faulty components, causing the vehicle to sag or ride unevenly.
    * **Solution:** Repair or replace the faulty air suspension components.
    * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $1,000 - $2,000+
    * **Recalls:** None Found

**Electrical**

* **Alternator failure:** The alternator can fail due to worn or damaged components, leading to electrical problems or a dead battery.
* **Solution:** Replace the alternator.
* **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $300 - $500+
* **Recalls:** None Found
* **Battery drain:** The battery can drain prematurely due to electrical parasitic draws or faulty components.
* **Solution:** Identify and repair the parasitic draw or faulty components.
* **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $100 - $300+
* **Recalls:** None Found

**Exterior**

* **Sunroof leaks:** The sunroof may leak water into the cabin when it rains or is washed.
* **Solution:** Inspect and repair any damaged or worn sunroof seals.
* **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $200 - $500+
* **Recalls:** None Found

**Interior**

* **Navigation system failure:** The navigation system may malfunction or become unresponsive.
* **Solution:** Update or replace the navigation system software or hardware.
* **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $200 - $1,000+
* **Recalls:** None Found
* **Climate control problems:** The climate control system may malfunction, leading to inconsistent or insufficient heating or cooling.
* **Solution:** Diagnose and repair the faulty climate control components.
* **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $200 - $1,000+
* **Recalls:** None Found

**Summary**

The 2002 Mercedes-Benz S430 is a luxurious and comfortable vehicle, but it has been known to experience a range of issues, particularly with its engine and transmission. While some repairs are relatively affordable, others can be costly. Potential owners should research and consider these potential issues before purchasing a used S430.
