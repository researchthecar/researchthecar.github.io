## Electrical

### Infotainment System
**COMAND System Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Occasional freezing, unresponsiveness, or display errors.
- **Solution:** Software update or system reset. In rare cases, module replacement may be necessary.
- **Cost:** $150 - $600


### Convertible Top Actuators
**Retractable Hardtop Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** Failure of the top to open or close smoothly, unusual noises, or error messages.
- **Solution:** Lubrication of mechanisms, adjustment of sensors, or replacement of faulty hydraulic pump or actuators.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1500

### Summary Verdict
The 2014 Mercedes-Benz SLK350 demonstrates strong overall reliability, particularly concerning its powertrain. The 3.5L V6 engine and 7-speed automatic transmission are robust and rarely present significant mechanical issues. The primary areas where owners might encounter problems are related to the complex retractable hardtop system, which can experience malfunctions with its actuators or hydraulic components, and occasional glitches within the COMAND infotainment system, often resolvable with software updates. These electrical and mechanical complexities are not uncommon in luxury convertibles of this era. With diligent maintenance and prompt attention to any emerging electrical or top mechanism issues, the SLK350 should provide a rewarding and dependable driving experience.
