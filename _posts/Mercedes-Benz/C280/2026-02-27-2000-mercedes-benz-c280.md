# 2000 Mercedes-Benz C280

### Overview
The 2000 Mercedes-Benz C280, part of the W202 generation, is generally considered a solid vehicle for its era, blending luxury with a degree of sportiness. While it benefits from Mercedes-Benz's reputation for build quality, like any vehicle of this age, it has specific areas that have shown more propensity for issues than others. Owners often praise its comfortable ride and refined interior. However, potential buyers and current owners should be aware of common electrical gremlins, potential suspension wear, and specific engine component concerns that can arise with mileage. Regular maintenance is key to longevity, but certain components may require attention sooner than others.

---


## Engine

### 2.8L M104 Inline-6
**Head Gasket Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Coolant loss, white smoke from exhaust, engine overheating, milky oil.
- **Solution:** Replacement of head gasket and associated seals. May require cylinder head resurfacing.
- **Cost:** $1200 - $2500

**Oil Leaks (Valve Cover Gasket, Oil Pan Gasket)**
- **Symptoms:** Visible oil drips under the engine, burning oil smell.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the leaking gasket.
- **Cost:** $300 - $700

**Mass Air Flow (MAF) Sensor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Rough idle, poor acceleration, check engine light, stalling.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the MAF sensor.
- **Cost:** $250 - $500


## Transmission

### 5-Speed Automatic (722.6)
**Transmission Control Module (TCM) Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Erratic shifting, harsh shifts, transmission not engaging gears, limp mode.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the TCM. Sometimes requires programming.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1500

**Conductor Plate Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Similar to TCM failure, including shifting problems and error codes.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the conductor plate and associated sensors.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1000


## Suspension

### Standard Independent Suspension
**Worn Control Arm Bushings**
- **Symptoms:** Clunking noises over bumps, vague steering feel, uneven tire wear.
- **Solution:** Replacement of control arm bushings or the entire control arm assembly.
- **Cost:** $400 - $800

**Sagging Springs/Worn Shocks**
- **Symptoms:** Poor ride quality, excessive body roll, bouncing, uneven ride height.
- **Solution:** Replacement of shock absorbers and/or coil springs.
- **Cost:** $600 - $1200


## Electrical

### General Electrical System
**Window Regulator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Power windows not operating, slow window movement, grinding noises.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the window regulator mechanism.
- **Cost:** $200 - $400

**Heater Control Valve Failure**
- **Symptoms:** No heat or inconsistent temperature control from HVAC system.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the heater control valve.
- **Cost:** $150 - $300

**Instrument Cluster Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Flickering gauges, dead pixels on display, non-functional indicators.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of the instrument cluster.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1000


## Exterior

### Body and Trim
**Rust (Wheel Arches, Rocker Panels)**
- **Symptoms:** Visible corrosion and bubbling paint on lower body panels.
- **Solution:** Rust removal and body panel repair/replacement.
- **Cost:** $500 - $2000+

**Headlight Lens Hazing**
- **Symptoms:** Cloudy or yellowed headlight lenses, reduced light output.
- **Solution:** Restoration of lenses or replacement.
- **Cost:** $50 - $200


## Interior

### Cabin Components
**HVAC Blower Motor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** No air circulation from HVAC system, or intermittent operation.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the blower motor and resistor.
- **Cost:** $200 - $400

**Seat Upholstery Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Cracking, tearing, or excessive wear on leather or fabric seats.
- **Solution:** Repair or reupholstering of seats.
- **Cost:** $300 - $1000+


--- 
### Summary Verdict
The 2000 Mercedes-Benz C280 offers a premium experience but requires diligent maintenance and awareness of common issues. The 2.8L inline-six engine is robust but prone to oil leaks and potential head gasket issues at higher mileage. The automatic transmission can develop problems with its control module or conductor plate. Electrical gremlins, particularly with window regulators and the instrument cluster, are not uncommon. Suspension components like bushings and shocks will likely need attention on older models. Exterior rust can be a concern in certain climates. Overall, while not without its potential pitfalls, addressing these known issues can lead to a reliable and enjoyable ownership experience for this classic Mercedes-Benz.
