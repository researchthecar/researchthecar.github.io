**Introduction:**

The 2004 Mercedes-Benz SL500 is a luxury convertible sports car powered by a 5.0L V8 engine. It produces 302 horsepower and 295 lb-ft of torque and comes paired with a 5-speed automatic transmission. The SL500 is known for its stylish design, powerful engine, and comfortable ride. However, it has some areas where it falls short.

**Engine**

**5.0L V8**

* **Intake manifold runner control issues:** The intake manifold runner control system can fail, causing a loss of power and rough idling. The intake manifold and runner control system need to be replaced. $1000 - $2000+
* **Camshaft position sensor failure:** The camshaft position sensor can fail, leading to starting problems and engine misfires. The camshaft position sensor needs to be replaced. $200 - $400+
* **Oil leaks:** The SL500 can develop oil leaks from various gaskets and seals. The leaking gaskets and seals need to be replaced. $500 - $1000+
* **Timing chain tensioner failure:** The timing chain tensioner can fail, leading to a rattling noise from the engine. The timing chain tensioner and timing chain need to be replaced. $1500 - $2000+

**Transmission**

**5-Speed Automatic**

* **Transmission slipping:** The transmission can slip or hesitate when shifting gears. The transmission may need to be rebuilt or replaced. $2000 - $3000+
* **Torque converter failure:** The torque converter can fail, causing a loss of power and shuddering during shifting. The torque converter needs to be replaced. $1000 - $1500+
* **Valve body issues:** The valve body can develop issues, leading to shifting problems and transmission shuddering. The valve body needs to be replaced or repaired. $500 - $1000+

**Suspension**

* **Airmatic suspension failure:** The airmatic suspension system can fail, causing the vehicle to ride low or unevenly. The airmatic suspension components, such as the air struts and compressor, need to be replaced. $2000 - $3000+
* **Control arm bushings:** The control arm bushings can wear out, causing clunking noises and poor handling. The control arm bushings need to be replaced. $300 - $600+
* **Sway bar links:** The sway bar links can fail, leading to clunking noises and poor handling. The sway bar links need to be replaced. $200 - $400+

**Electrical**

* **Battery discharge:** The SL500 can experience battery discharge issues, leaving it unable to start. The battery or alternator may need to be replaced. $300 - $1000+
* **Electrical gremlins:** The SL500 can develop electrical gremlins, such as flickering lights or malfunctioning gauges. The electrical system needs to be diagnosed and repaired. $500 - $1500+
* **Keyless entry system failure:** The keyless entry system can fail, preventing the owner from unlocking the vehicle. The keyless entry system components, such as the receiver and transmitter, need to be replaced. $500 - $1000+

**Exterior**

* **Headlight condensation:** The headlights can develop condensation, impairing visibility. The headlights need to be resealed or replaced. $500 - $1000+
* **Door lock issues:** The door locks can malfunction, making it difficult to open or close the doors. The door lock actuators or latch mechanisms need to be replaced. $200 - $500+
* **Soft-top roof leaks:** The soft-top roof can develop leaks, allowing water to enter the cabin. The soft-top roof or gaskets need to be repaired or replaced. $1000 - $2000+

**Interior**

* **Navigation system issues:** The navigation system can experience glitches or become outdated. The navigation system may need to be updated or replaced. $500 - $1000+
* **Heated/cooled seats:** The heated/cooled seats can malfunction, leaving the occupants uncomfortable. The heating or cooling elements may need to be replaced. $500 - $1000+
* **Climate control failure:** The climate control system can fail, leading to poor heating or cooling. The climate control components, such as the blower motor or temperature sensors, need to be replaced. $500 - $1000+

**Recalls:**

* **Airbag inflator recall (NHTSA ID: 04T024):** The passenger-side airbag inflator has the potential to explode, causing injury.
* **Transmission conductor plate recall (NHTSA ID: 08E039):** The transmission conductor plate can develop cracks, leading to transmission failure.
* **Fuel hose recall (NHTSA ID: 09E055):** The fuel hose can leak, increasing the risk of fire.

**Summary:**

The 2004 Mercedes-Benz SL500 is a powerful and stylish convertible, but it comes with some potential issues that can be costly to repair. The engine and transmission are prone to various problems, while the electrical system, suspension, and interior can also develop faults. These issues can lead to a significant financial burden for owners, making it important to consider extended warranties or service contracts.
