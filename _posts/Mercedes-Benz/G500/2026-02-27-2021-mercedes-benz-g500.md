# 2021 Mercedes-Benz G500 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2021 Mercedes-Benz G500 (marketed in North America primarily as the G550) represents the iconic, heavy-duty luxury SUV known for its hand-built craftsmanship and unmistakable presence. While high-end luxury vehicles of this caliber feature complex electronic architectures, used-car buyers should review owner-reported drivability and electrical concerns before purchasing.

---

## Engine

### 4.0L Biturbo V8

Equipped with a 4.0-liter twin-turbocharged V8 producing approximately 416 horsepower and 450 lb-ft of torque, providing immense power for both on-road and off-road driving. This engine offers robust performance, though owners have occasionally reported complex electrical interactions leading to unexpected stalls.

**Unexpected Engine Shutdown**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle may suddenly stall or shut down while idling at a red light or moving at low speeds, accompanied by a loss of throttle and an array of dashboard safety warnings.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified technician scan the vehicle computer for stored diagnostic trouble codes related to the electrical system or fuel management, and inspect battery connections and control modules.

## Transmission

### 9-Speed Automatic Transmission

Features a 9-speed automatic transmission paired with standard all-wheel drive, delivering smooth gear changes suited to the heavy chassis. No major recurring transmission-specific mechanical failures have been highlighted in initial owner feedback.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

Heavy-duty braking system designed to haul the substantial weight of the G-Class.

**Brake Lockup**
- **Symptoms:** Brakes may unexpectedly lock up during operation, creating hazardous driving conditions and triggering computer system warnings.
- **Solution:** Inspect the braking system hydraulics, anti-lock braking (ABS) modules, and wheel speed sensors for faults or software glitches.

## Summary Verdict

When shopping for a used 2021 Mercedes-Benz G500/G550, prospective buyers should prioritize a thorough pre-purchase inspection focusing on the vehicle's extensive electronic systems and powertrain controls. While rare, owner complaints highlight potential unexpected engine stalls and brake system anomalies that require professional diagnosis.
