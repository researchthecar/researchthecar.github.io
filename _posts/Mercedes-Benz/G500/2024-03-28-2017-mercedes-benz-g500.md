**Introduction:**

The 2017 Mercedes-Benz G500 is a luxurious and capable off-road SUV powered by a 4.0L V8 bi-turbo engine producing 416 horsepower and 450 lb-ft of torque. The G500 comes standard with a 7-speed automatic transmission and 4MATICÂ® four-wheel drive. It offers a rugged exterior, a spacious and well-equipped interior, and excellent off-road capabilities. While praised for its performance, durability, and prestige, the G500 has had some reported issues.

**Engine**

**4.0L V8 Bi-Turbo**

- **Intake Valve Sticking:** Intake valves may become stuck open or closed, causing reduced engine performance and misfiring.
  - Solution: Replace affected intake valves.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
- **Oil Pressure Sensor Failure:** The oil pressure sensor may fail, leading to inaccurate readings or a lack of oil pressure warning.
  - Solution: Replace faulty oil pressure sensor.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
- **Turbocharger Boost Leak:** The turbochargers may experience boost leaks, resulting in reduced power and increased fuel consumption.
  - Solution: Inspect and repair or replace affected turbocharger components.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+

**Transmission**

**7-Speed Automatic**

- **Shift Hesitations:** The transmission may hesitate or slip during gear changes, especially when shifting into higher gears.
  - Solution: Update transmission software or replace faulty components as needed.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+

**Suspension**

- **Front Axle Struts Failure:** Front axle struts may prematurely fail, causing knocking noises, reduced handling, and uneven tire wear.
  - Solution: Replace faulty struts.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $1,500+
- **Rear Shock Absorber Mount Corrosion:** Rear shock absorber mounts may corrode over time, leading to noises and reduced suspension performance.
  - Solution: Clean and lubricate mounts or replace if necessary.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+

**Electrical**

- **Battery Drain:** Vehicles may experience excessive battery drain, leading to difficulty starting or complete battery failure.
  - Solution: Diagnose and repair electrical system issues, such as faulty wiring or parasitic battery draw.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $1,000+
- **Electrical Module Failures:** Various electrical modules, including those for the headlights, windows, or navigation, may malfunction.
  - Solution: Diagnose and replace faulty electrical modules.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+

**Exterior**

- **Door Hinge Pin Wear:** Door hinge pins may wear prematurely, causing doors to sag or bind.
  - Solution: Replace faulty hinge pins.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500
- **Sunroof Drainage System Clogs:** Sunroof drainage system may clog, leading to water leaks inside the vehicle.
  - Solution: Clean or replace clogged drainage channels.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500

**Interior**

- **Dashboard Creaks and Rattles:** The dashboard and other interior components may develop creaks and rattles, especially over rough terrain.
  - Solution: Tighten loose components or apply sound-dampening material.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500

**Summary:**

The 2017 Mercedes-Benz G500 is a capable and luxurious SUV that has received positive reviews for its performance, durability, and off-road capabilities. However, it has been known to experience some issues, including engine intake valve sticking, turbocharger boost leaks, and transmission shift hesitations. The severity and cost of these issues can vary depending on the specific problem and the condition of the vehicle. It is recommended that owners research potential issues and consider extended warranty coverage to mitigate potential repair expenses.
