**Introduction**

The 2004 Mercedes-Benz G500 is a legendary off-road SUV known for its rugged construction, luxurious cabin, and powerful V8 engine. It is powered by a 5.0-liter V8 engine producing 292 horsepower and 336 lb-ft of torque paired with a 5-speed automatic transmission. Despite its off-road capabilities, the G500 also offers a comfortable and refined driving experience on the road. However, like any vehicle, the G500 has had its share of issues.

**Engine**

* **5.0L V8**
    * **Leaking Valve Cover Gaskets:**
        * Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks from the valve cover gaskets, causing oil consumption and a burning oil smell.
        * Solution: Replace the valve cover gaskets.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $600 - $1,000+
        * Recalls: None Found
    * **Crankshaft Position Sensor Failure:**
        * Description/Symptoms: The crankshaft position sensor fails, resulting in engine stalling or difficulty starting.
        * Solution: Replace the crankshaft position sensor.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

* **5-speed Automatic**
    * **Shift Solenoid Failure:**
        * Description/Symptoms: The shift solenoids fail, causing harsh or delayed shifting.
        * Solution: Replace the shift solenoids.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,200+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **No Issues Found**

**Electrical**

* **Battery Discharge Issue:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The battery discharges prematurely, leaving the vehicle stranded.
    * Solution: Diagnose and repair or replace any faulty electrical components, such as the alternator or battery.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Rust on Undercarriage:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Rust forms on the undercarriage, particularly in areas exposed to road salt.
    * Solution: Rust treatment and preventive measures.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Climate Control Malfunction:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The climate control system fails, resulting in poor heating or cooling.
    * Solution: Replace faulty climate control components, such as the blower motor or control module.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $1,500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2004 Mercedes-Benz G500 is a formidable SUV with off-road prowess and creature comforts. However, it has experienced some issues, particularly with its engine, transmission, and electrical system. The severity and cost of these issues can vary depending on the specific problem encountered. Overall, the G500 is a well-built vehicle that can provide years of reliable service with proper maintenance and repairs.
