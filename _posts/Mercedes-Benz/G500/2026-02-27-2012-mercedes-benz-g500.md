# 2012 Mercedes-Benz G500

### Overview
The 2012 Mercedes-Benz G500, a robust and iconic off-road vehicle, generally exhibits strong mechanical reliability, reflecting its premium build quality and durable engineering. While it's a highly capable machine, like any vehicle of its age and complexity, certain areas may require attention. This analysis focuses on documented common issues beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential areas of concern for owners and prospective buyers. The G500's legendary status is built on a foundation of resilience, but understanding specific potential failure points can aid in proactive maintenance and ownership experience.

---


## Engine

### 5.5L V8 (M273)
**Ignition Coil Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Misfires, rough idling, check engine light, reduced engine power.
- **Solution:** Replacement of faulty ignition coil(s).
- **Cost:** $150 - $300 per coil

**Oil Leaks (Valve Cover Gaskets)**
- **Symptoms:** Visible oil on engine components, burning oil smell, oil level dropping.
- **Solution:** Replacement of valve cover gaskets.
- **Cost:** $400 - $800


## Transmission

### 7G-TRONIC (7-Speed Automatic)
**Transmission Fluid Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Leaking fluid from transmission pan or lines, rough shifting, slipping gears.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of leaking seals, gaskets, or lines. Fluid flush and refill.
- **Cost:** $300 - $700

**Solenoid Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Harsh shifting, delayed engagement, transmission warning light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of faulty transmission solenoids.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1000


## Suspension

### Standard Chassis
**Worn Bushings**
- **Symptoms:** Clunking noises over bumps, vague steering feel, uneven tire wear.
- **Solution:** Replacement of worn suspension bushings.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600

**Leaking Shock Absorbers**
- **Symptoms:** Bouncy ride, reduced handling stability, visible fluid on shock bodies.
- **Solution:** Replacement of leaking shock absorbers.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1000


## Electrical

### General Electrical System
**Window Regulator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Power windows not operating, slow window movement, grinding noises.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the window regulator mechanism.
- **Cost:** $250 - $500 per window

**Headlight Bulb Failure (Xenon)**
- **Symptoms:** Dim or non-functional headlights, flickering.
- **Solution:** Replacement of Xenon bulbs and potentially ballasts.
- **Cost:** $200 - $500 per bulb/ballast


## Exterior

### Body and Trim
**Door Lock Actuator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Doors not locking or unlocking remotely or manually.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the faulty door lock actuator.
- **Cost:** $200 - $400 per actuator

**Sunroof Drain Clogs**
- **Symptoms:** Water leaks into the cabin, musty smell.
- **Solution:** Clearing of sunroof drain tubes.
- **Cost:** $100 - $250


## Interior

### Cabin Components
**HVAC Blower Motor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** No airflow from vents, or intermittent airflow, strange noises from dashboard.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the HVAC blower motor.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600

**Seat Heater Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Heated seats not working or working intermittently.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of seat heating elements or controls.
- **Cost:** $200 - $500 per seat


--- 
### Summary Verdict
The 2012 Mercedes-Benz G500 demonstrates a generally solid reliability record, particularly in its core powertrain components. The robust 5.5L V8 engine and the 7G-TRONIC transmission are known for their durability when properly maintained. However, as with many luxury vehicles of this era, potential issues can arise in the electrical system, such as window regulators and headlight components, and in suspension wear, like bushings and shocks. Interior features like HVAC and seat heaters can also be points of failure. Proactive maintenance, especially addressing fluid leaks and regular inspections of suspension and electrical components, is key to ensuring long-term reliability and enjoyment of this iconic SUV.
