**2003 Mercedes-Benz C230**

The 2003 Mercedes-Benz C230 is a compact luxury sedan powered by a 2.3-liter V6 engine that produces 189 horsepower and 184 lb-ft of torque. It comes standard with a 5-speed manual transmission, while a 5-speed automatic transmission is optional. The C230 is praised for its comfortable interior, stylish design, and zippy performance, but some owners have reported issues with various components.

**Engine**

* **2.3L V6 Engine**
    * **Intake manifold gasket failure:** This can cause coolant and oil leaks, overheating, and engine damage. The gasket needs to be replaced. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800+

**Transmission**

* **5-Speed Manual Transmission**
    * **Synchronizer failure:** This can make it difficult to shift gears, especially into second and third. The synchronizers need to be replaced. Estimated Cost to Fix: $700 - $1,200+
* **5-Speed Automatic Transmission**
    * **Torque converter failure:** This can cause shuddering, slipping, and overheating. The torque converter needs to be replaced. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+

**Suspension**

* **Front suspension struts:** These can wear out over time, leading to a rough ride and poor handling. The struts need to be replaced. Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,200+
* **Rear suspension shocks:** These can also wear out, causing similar symptoms as the front struts. The shocks need to be replaced. Estimated Cost to Fix: $600 - $1,000+

**Electrical**

* **Alternator failure:** This can cause the battery to discharge and the engine to stall. The alternator needs to be replaced. Estimated Cost to Fix: $600 - $900+
* **Starter failure:** This can prevent the engine from starting. The starter needs to be replaced. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800+

**Exterior**

* **Headlight condensation:** This is a common issue caused by moisture buildup inside the headlights. The headlights need to be resealed. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+

**Interior**

* **Climate control failure:** This can cause the air conditioning or heating system to malfunction. The climate control unit needs to be replaced. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800+
* **Power window failure:** This can prevent the windows from rolling up or down. The window motors or switches need to be replaced. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+

**Summary**

The 2003 Mercedes-Benz C230 is a generally reliable vehicle, but it can experience some common issues. While some of these issues can be costly to fix, most are relatively minor and can be resolved with routine maintenance. Overall, the C230 is a solid choice for those seeking a comfortable and stylish luxury sedan.
