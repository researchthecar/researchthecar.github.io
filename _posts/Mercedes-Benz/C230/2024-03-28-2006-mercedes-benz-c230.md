**Introduction**

The 2006 Mercedes-Benz C230 is a compact luxury sedan that offers a blend of performance and refinement. It is powered by a 2.5L V6 engine that delivers 201 horsepower and 186 lb-ft of torque. The C230 comes standard with a 6-speed manual transmission, while a 5-speed automatic transmission is available as an option.

Owners of the C230 praise its comfortable ride, luxurious interior, and responsive handling. However, some owners have reported encountering various issues with the vehicle.

**Engine**

* **2.5L V6**

**Sticky Intake Manifold Flaps**
Description/Symptoms: The intake manifold flaps become stuck or stiff, leading to reduced engine performance and fuel efficiency.
Solution: Replace the intake manifold.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $1,500+
Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

* **5-Speed Automatic**

**Delayed or Hard Shifting**
Description/Symptoms: The transmission shifts gears slowly or abruptly.
Solution: Replace the transmission control module or update the software.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

**No Issues Found**

**Electrical**

**Faulty Window Switches**
Description/Symptoms: The window switches fail to operate correctly or become unresponsive.
Solution: Replace the faulty window switches.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $350
Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

**Leaking Headlights**
Description/Symptoms: The headlight assemblies develop leaks, allowing moisture to enter and cause damage.
Solution: Replace the headlight assemblies.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $250 - $500
Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

**Sun Visor Mirror Covers**
Description/Symptoms: The mirror covers on the sun visors become loose or break off.
Solution: Replace the sun visors or repair the covers.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200
Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

Overall, the 2006 Mercedes-Benz C230 is a reliable and comfortable vehicle. However, some owners have encountered issues with the engine, transmission, and electrical systems. The severity and cost of these issues vary, and it is recommended to have the vehicle inspected by a qualified mechanic before purchasing.
