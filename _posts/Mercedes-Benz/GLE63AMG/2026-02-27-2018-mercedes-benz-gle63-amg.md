# 2018 Mercedes-Benz GLE63 AMG Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2018 Mercedes-Benz GLE 63 AMG delivers high-performance luxury with twin-turbo V8 power, but prospective buyers should carefully review owner-reported issues across safety and powertrain categories. While standard maintenance and routine upkeep apply to any high-end German performance vehicle, paying close attention to electronics, transmission shift behaviors, and potential accessory drive concerns during a pre-purchase inspection can save owners from unexpected headaches.

---

## Engine

### 5.5L Twin-Turbo V8 (M157)

The 5.5-liter twin-turbocharged V8 engine pushes out an impressive 550 to 577 horsepower, delivering blistering acceleration for a midsize luxury SUV. It requires meticulous maintenance history and premium fuel, as neglecting scheduled services can lead to costly performance-part failures.

**Accessory Drive Belt Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The serpentine or fan belt may snap unexpectedly while driving, causing immediate engine shutdown and potential engine seizure if the vehicle is not stopped quickly.
- **Solution:** Inspect the belt and pulleys regularly for wear or misalignment, and replace immediately at the first sign of cracking or fraying.

## Transmission

### AMG Speedshift Plus 7G-Tronic 7-Speed Automatic

The 7-speed automatic transmission is engineered to handle the massive torque output of the AMG V8 powerplant with rapid gear changes. Smooth operation is typical when fluid changes are performed on schedule, though owners should verify crisp engagement during a test drive.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

Performance braking systems offer immense stopping power suited for a heavy, fast SUV, but consumable components like pads and rotors can wear out quickly under aggressive driving.

**Premature Brake Pad Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Brake pads may wear down to metal-on-metal conditions rapidly without providing adequate dashboard warning indications.
- **Solution:** Manually inspect brake pad thickness and rotor condition regularly rather than relying solely on dashboard wear indicators.

## Electrical

### Backup Camera System

Safety equipment on this high-performance SUV includes advanced driver aids and comprehensive airbag arrays, though auxiliary electronics like backup cameras can occasionally glitch.

**Rear View Camera Display Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The infotainment screen fails to switch to the backup camera view when the vehicle is shifted into reverse, displaying a black screen instead.
- **Solution:** Check for software updates or consult a qualified technician to inspect the camera module and display wiring connections.

## Exterior

### Telematics and Lighting

Complex onboard electronics control everything from cabin comfort to safety telematics, making a healthy electrical system vital for trouble-free ownership.

**SOS Inoperative Message**
- **Symptoms:** Constant 'SOS Inoperative' warning messages appear on the dashboard, frequently following a weak or failing vehicle battery.
- **Solution:** Test and replace the main battery and auxiliary battery as needed, or have the Telematics Control Unit (TCU) diagnosed by a specialist.
