# 2022 Mercedes-Benz GLE63 AMG Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2022 Mercedes-Benz GLE-Class offers luxury, advanced technology, and high-performance options ranging from turbocharged four-cylinder and inline-six powerplants to hand-built AMG V8s. While it delivers an exceptionally refined driving experience and impressive feature content, prospective buyers should review known owner complaints concerning electrical issues, suspension and tire wear patterns, and sporadic engine driveability concerns.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4 (GLE 350)

The 2.0-liter turbocharged inline-4 engine produces 255 horsepower and 273 lb-ft of torque, serving as the entry-level powerplant for the GLE 350. It provides adequate daily acceleration and solid efficiency, though some owners have reported intermittent hesitation and stalling.

**Engine Hesitation and Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle may hesitate, fail to accelerate as intended when depressing the accelerator pedal, or experience sudden engine stalling accompanied by a check engine light.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified technician perform a full diagnostic scan to check for fault codes related to fuel delivery, sensors, or throttle control.

### 3.0L Turbocharged Inline-6 with EQ Boost (GLE 450)

The 3.0-liter turbocharged inline-6 features a 48-volt mild-hybrid system (EQ Boost), generating 362 horsepower and 369 lb-ft of torque. It delivers exceptionally smooth power delivery and seamless stop-start operation.

### 4.0L Twin-Turbocharged V8 with EQ Boost (GLE 580 & AMG GLE 63 S)

The hand-built 4.0-liter twin-turbo V8 produces massive performance outputs, ranging from 483 horsepower in the GLE 580 up to 603 horsepower in the AMG GLE 63 S. It provides thrilling acceleration backed by robust engineering, though maintenance demands are high.

## Transmission

### 9G-TRONIC 9-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 9-speed automatic transmission routes power smoothly across all rear-wheel-drive and 4MATIC all-wheel-drive variants. It is generally praised for seamless gear selection and highway efficiency.

## Suspension

### Tires and Wheels

The suspension balances ride comfort and athletic handling, particularly when equipped with air suspension or active ride control systems.

**Premature Tire Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Rear or front tires experience rapid tread wear, balding prematurely within the first 18,000 to 26,000 miles.
- **Solution:** Check wheel alignment frequently, inspect tire pressures, and budget for potential replacement sets depending on driving style and wheel size.
- **Cost:** $950

## Electrical

### Electrical System

The sophisticated electrical architecture drives the dual-screen MBUX infotainment system and advanced driver aids, but complex wiring harnesses require careful inspection for moisture or software glitches.

**Water Intrusion and Drainage Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Water leaks into the cabin or electrical components due to improperly secured air conditioning or sunroof drainage lines.
- **Solution:** Inspect drain tubes and seals during routine service intervals to ensure proper water evacuation away from interior electronics.

## Exterior

### Sunroof and Glass

Exterior styling features muscular proportions and expansive glass elements, though owners should remain vigilant regarding panoramic roof integrity.

**Panoramic Sunroof Shattering**
- **Symptoms:** Panoramic glass roof panels or windshield cowl glass may spontaneously shatter or explode outward while driving under normal conditions.
- **Solution:** Inspect glass panels for pre-existing stress cracks and verify warranty coverage if spontaneous breakage occurs.

## Summary Verdict

The 2022 Mercedes-Benz GLE-Class is a formidable luxury SUV that combines high-end cabin finishes with potent powertrain choices. While most owners enjoy a comfortable and sophisticated ride, used-car buyers should perform a thorough inspection checking for tire wear patterns, electrical or drainage vulnerabilities, and any history of engine driveability issues.
