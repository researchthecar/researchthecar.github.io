# 2020 Mercedes-Benz GLE63 AMG Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2020 Mercedes-Benz GLE-Class delivers luxury, cutting-edge technology, and robust performance, but buyers looking at the used market should approach with a healthy amount of caution. While owner reports highlight impressive capability, complaints filed with federal safety regulators point to recurring electronic glitches, suspension components, and occasional powertrain concerns. Reviewing maintenance histories and verifying whether previous fixes were performed can help shoppers avoid unexpected repair bills.

---

## Engine

### 4.0L Bi-Turbo V8

The potent 4.0-liter twin-turbocharged V8 engine provides immense horsepower and torque, delivering exhilarating performance suited to a high-end luxury performance SUV. However, complex engineering means routine maintenance is critical, and neglected service can lead to costly repairs.

**Engine Misfire & Internal Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have reported check engine lights, rough idling, cylinder misfires, and unusual metallic knocking or rattling sounds during acceleration.
- **Solution:** Requires professional diagnostic evaluation; may involve replacing faulty spark plugs, ignition coils, or addressing severe internal mechanical wear.

**Engine Stalling & Loss of Power**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle may unexpectedly lose motive power while driving or experience sudden engine stalling.
- **Solution:** Inspect fuel delivery, electrical sensors, and powertrain control modules for fault codes and underlying mechanical defects.

## Transmission

### 9-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 9-speed automatic transmission channels power smoothly under normal conditions, though electronic control failures and shifting anomalies have been noted by some owners.

**Shifting to Neutral & Control Module Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Transmissions may unexpectedly drop into neutral when shifted into drive or reverse, or experience valve body and control module failures.
- **Solution:** Reprogramming or replacing the transmission control module (TCM) and valve body assembly.
- **Cost:** $1000

## Suspension

### Rear Shock Absorbers

Suspension systems offer adaptable ride comfort, but premature shock absorber wear and fluid leaks have been documented at moderate mileages.

**Shock Absorber Leakage and Noise**
- **Symptoms:** Clunking or rattling noises when driving over bumps, accompanied by fluid leaks from the rear shock absorbers.
- **Solution:** Replace worn or leaking rear shock absorber assemblies.

## Electrical

### Infotainment and Electronics

Advanced dual-screen displays and electronics power the cabin experience, though software bugs and hardware glitches can occasionally impair functionality.

**Infotainment and Screen Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Main multimedia display screens freeze, fail completely, or cause intermittent backup camera and climate control access issues.
- **Solution:** Perform software updates, reset the infotainment system, or replace faulty display hardware and control modules.

## Safety

### Air Bag & Seat Belts

Safety restraint systems incorporate advanced sensors, though owners have experienced occasional electronic warnings and mechanical seat belt failures.

**Air Bag Warning & Faulty Seat Belts**
- **Symptoms:** Illuminated airbag warning lights due to passenger weight sensor issues, alongside jammed or non-retracting rear seat belts.
- **Solution:** Inspect occupant classification sensors, recalibrate or replace faulty seat belt assemblies.
- **Cost:** $1000

## Summary Verdict

The 2020 Mercedes-Benz GLE63 AMG is an extraordinarily fast and luxurious machine, but prospective buyers should proceed with care. Mechanical complexities mean that out-of-warranty repairs can quickly become expensive. Prioritize examples with comprehensive service records, and always invest in a thorough pre-purchase inspection by a qualified European car specialist before finalizing your purchase.
