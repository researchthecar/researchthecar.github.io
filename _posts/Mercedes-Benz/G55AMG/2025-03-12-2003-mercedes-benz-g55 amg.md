The 2003 Mercedes-Benz G55 AMG is a high-performance variant of the iconic G-Wagon, known for its rugged off-road capability combined with a luxurious interior and powerful engine. For the 2003 model year, the G55 AMG was only available with the 5.4L Supercharged V8 engine. This engine produces approximately 469 horsepower and 516 lb-ft of torque. The G55 AMG was only available with a 5-speed automatic transmission. Owners often appreciate the G55 AMG for its unique blend of luxury and off-road prowess, as well as its distinctive styling. However, potential downsides include relatively poor fuel economy, high maintenance costs, and potential for expensive repairs due to the complexity of the vehicle.

**Engine**

**5.4L Supercharged V8**

*   **Supercharger Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Whining noise from the engine, loss of power, and potential for the engine to go into limp mode.
    *   Solution: Replacement or rebuild of the supercharger.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $3,000 - $6,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Intercooler Pump Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Reduced engine performance, especially in hot weather, due to decreased cooling efficiency.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the intercooler pump.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Valve Cover Gasket Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks onto the exhaust manifolds, causing a burning smell. Can also lead to low oil levels.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the valve cover gaskets.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**5-Speed Automatic**

*   **Torque Converter Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Shuddering during acceleration, slipping between gears, or failure to shift properly.
    *   Solution: Replacement or rebuild of the torque converter.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Valve Body Problems**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Harsh shifting, delayed engagement, or failure to shift into certain gears.
    *   Solution: Replacement or rebuild of the valve body.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $2,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Air Suspension Compressor Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The vehicle sitting lower than normal, especially after being parked overnight. The air suspension warning light might illuminate on the dashboard.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the air suspension compressor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Air Strut Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Similar to compressor failure, the vehicle sits low or unevenly. Hissing sounds may be heard from the air struts.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the leaking air strut(s).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,500+ per strut
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Fuse Box Corrosion**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Intermittent electrical issues, such as lights flickering, accessories not working, or the vehicle not starting.
    *   Solution: Cleaning or replacement of the fuse box.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Window Regulator Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Windows failing to go up or down, or making grinding noises during operation.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the window regulator.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $700+ per window
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Rust on Body Panels**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rust forming on the lower body panels, around the wheel wells, and on the door frames.
    *   Solution: Rust repair and repainting of affected areas.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $3,000+ depending on severity
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Sunroof Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Sunroof failing to open or close, getting stuck, or leaking.
    *   Solution: Repair or replacement of the sunroof motor, tracks, or seals.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $1,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2003 Mercedes-Benz G55 AMG presents a unique ownership experience with its blend of performance and luxury. However, potential owners should be aware of the common issues that can arise, particularly those related to the supercharged engine, transmission, and air suspension system. Repairs can be costly, especially if major components like the supercharger or air struts need replacement. Electrical issues and rust can also contribute to the overall maintenance burden. Due to the age of the vehicle, it is highly recommended to have a pre-purchase inspection done by a qualified mechanic familiar with Mercedes-Benz vehicles. While some issues can be minor and inexpensive to fix, others can be quite severe and expensive, ranging from hundreds to thousands of dollars per repair.
