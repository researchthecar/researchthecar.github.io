# 2017 Mercedes-Benz CLS400 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2017 Mercedes-Benz CLS400, buyers are treated to a sleek four-door coupe style paired with refined luxury. While owner-reported complaints are relatively sparse, prospective purchasers should carefully inspect critical wear items and specialized equipment during a pre-purchase inspection to avoid unexpected repair bills.

---

## Engine

### 3.0L Biturbo V6 Engine

This 3.0-liter twin-turbocharged V6 engine produces approximately 329 horsepower and 354 lb-ft of torque, offering brisk acceleration. It is generally robust when properly maintained with timely oil changes.

## Transmission

### 9G-TRONIC 9-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 9-speed automatic transmission delivers smooth gear shifts and helps optimize highway fuel economy. Routine fluid service is recommended to ensure long-term shifting refinement.

## Suspension

### Wheels

Factory alloy wheels provide a striking look, though owners should verify their structural integrity.

**Wheel Rim Cracking and Tire Blowouts**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have reported unexpected wheel rim cracking and subsequent tire blowouts under normal driving conditions without notable impact from potholes.
- **Solution:** Inspect all alloy wheels closely for hairline fractures or structural damage, and replace any compromised rims immediately.

The 2017 Mercedes-Benz CLS400 offers compelling styling and strong powertrain performance. Buyers should prioritize a comprehensive mechanical inspection, paying special attention to wheel and tire condition.
