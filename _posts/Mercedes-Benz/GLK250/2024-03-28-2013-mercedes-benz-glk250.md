**Introduction:**

The 2013 Mercedes-Benz GLK250 is a compact luxury SUV known for its refined design, comfortable interior, and efficient performance. It features a 2.0L turbocharged 4-cylinder engine paired with a 7-speed automatic transmission, delivering 201 horsepower and 229 lb-ft of torque. The GLK250 is praised for its sleek exterior, premium amenities, and responsive handling, but some owners have reported certain issues.

**Engine**

* **2.0L Turbocharged 4-Cylinder Engine**

    * **Oil Leak from Oil Cooler:**
        * Description/Symptoms: Leaking oil visible around the oil cooler, leading to low oil levels and potential engine damage.
        * Solution: Replace the faulty oil cooler.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
        * Recalls: None Found

    * **Intake Manifold Failure:**
        * Description/Symptoms: Decreased engine performance, rough idling, and illuminated check engine light.
        * Solution: Replace the faulty intake manifold.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $1,500+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

* **7-Speed Automatic Transmission**

    * **Shifting Hesitations:**
        * Description/Symptoms: Transmission hesitates or delays when shifting gears, especially during acceleration.
        * Solution: Reflash the transmission control module or replace the transmission fluid.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **No Issues Found**

**Electrical**

* **No Issues Found**

**Exterior**

* **Rear Bumper Trim Separation:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Rear bumper trim pieces may detach from the body, especially in cold weather.
    * Solution: Reattach the trim or replace it if necessary.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Sun Visor Hinge Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Sun visor hinges break, causing the visors to droop or fall off.
    * Solution: Replace the broken hinges.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $150+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Panoramic Roof Leakage:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Water leaks into the cabin through the panoramic roof during heavy rain or car washes.
    * Solution: Seal or replace the faulty sunroof gaskets.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

Overall, the 2013 Mercedes-Benz GLK250 has received mixed reviews regarding reliability. While it offers a luxurious and comfortable driving experience, several issues have been reported, particularly with the engine, transmission, and interior components. The severity and cost of the repairs can vary depending on the specific issue and the extent of the damage. Potential buyers are advised to carefully consider the potential maintenance costs when purchasing this model.
