# 2022 Mercedes-Benz C43 AMG Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used Mercedes-Benz C43 AMG, buyers can expect thrilling performance backed by advanced engineering, but should also remain mindful of potential owner-reported quirks. While these luxury sport sedans offer engaging driving dynamics and modern technology, analyzing owner feedback helps highlight areas that may require closer inspection during a pre-purchase evaluation.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4 Hybrid (M139l)

The 2.0-liter turbocharged inline-4 hybrid engine utilizes an electric exhaust gas turbocharger derived from Formula 1 technology to produce around 402 horsepower. It delivers explosive acceleration and strong performance, though prospective buyers should carefully check maintenance records and fuel system health.

**Fuel Pump Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have reported unexpected fuel pump failures leading to vehicle stalling, loss of engine power, and safety risks in traffic.
- **Solution:** Have the fuel pump and related fuel delivery components inspected, and verify that all software updates or campaigns have been completed.

## Transmission

### AMG Speedshift MCT 9-Speed Automatic

The 9-speed multi-clutch automatic transmission delivers rapid, aggressive gear changes tailored to high-performance driving. It generally operates smoothly under normal conditions, though proper fluid maintenance is essential for long-term durability.

## Brakes

### Forward Collision / Active Brake Assist

Advanced safety systems provide comprehensive driver assistance, though sensor-based features should be thoroughly tested.

**Brake Assist Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have reported instances where active brake assist features failed to engage properly during critical frontal collision scenarios.
- **Solution:** Inspect the front-facing radar and camera sensors for proper calibration and ensure safety software is fully up to date.
