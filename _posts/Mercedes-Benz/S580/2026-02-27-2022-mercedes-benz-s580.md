## Suspension

### AIRMATIC Air Suspension
**Air Spring Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle sagging on one or more corners, uneven ride height, compressor running excessively.
- **Solution:** Replacement of leaking air springs or associated lines.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1500 per corner

**Compressor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Inability to adjust ride height, constant vehicle sag, warning lights on the dashboard.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the air suspension compressor.
- **Cost:** $700 - $1200


## Electrical

### Infotainment System (MBUX)
**Software Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Screen freezing, unresponsive touch controls, navigation system errors, connectivity issues (Bluetooth, Wi-Fi).
- **Solution:** Software updates, system resets, or potential module replacement.
- **Cost:** $150 - $500 (for updates/diagnostics), $1000+ (for module replacement)


### Driver Assistance Systems
**Sensor Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** False warnings from adaptive cruise control, lane keeping assist, or parking sensors; system deactivation.
- **Solution:** Cleaning, recalibration, or replacement of affected sensors (e.g., radar, cameras).
- **Cost:** $300 - $1000 per sensor

### Summary Verdict
The 2022 Mercedes-Benz S580 is a highly sophisticated luxury sedan. While its powertrain and core mechanicals demonstrate strong reliability, potential issues tend to arise from its advanced electronic systems and complex air suspension. Owners may encounter glitches with the MBUX infotainment system or driver assistance sensors, often requiring software updates or recalibration. The AIRMATIC suspension, while providing exceptional comfort, can be a source of repair costs if air springs or the compressor develop leaks or fail. These are not uncommon for vehicles with such advanced air suspension technology. Overall, the S580 offers a reliable ownership experience, provided owners are aware of and prepared for the potential costs associated with maintaining its cutting-edge features.
