# 2022 Mercedes-Benz S580 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2022 Mercedes-Benz S580 represents the pinnacle of luxury, advanced technology, and engineering for the flagship sedan lineup. Powered by a potent bi-turbo V8 engine paired with a sophisticated automatic transmission, it delivers vault-like quietness and effortless acceleration. However, as an early model year of a heavily digitized generation, prospective buyers should inspect electronics, suspension configurations, and powertrain responsiveness closely.

---

## Engine

### 4.0L Bi-Turbo V8 (M176)

The S580 features a 4.0-liter twin-turbocharged V8 engine producing roughly 496 horsepower and 516 lb-ft of torque, integrated with a 48-volt mild-hybrid EQ Boost system. It provides immense, seamless power but carries complex mechanical and electronic dependencies.

**Engine Stalling and Camshaft Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Illuminated check engine warning light and unexpected engine stalling while driving.
- **Solution:** Take the vehicle to a qualified dealer or specialist to inspect internal engine components and replace failed components such as the camshaft.

## Transmission

### 9G-Tronic 9-Speed Automatic

The 9G-Tronic 9-speed automatic transmission routes power smoothly to the 4MATIC all-wheel-drive system in standard driving conditions, though occasional shift harshness has been reported.

**Drivetrain Shifting Jolt**
- **Symptoms:** Severe and violent mechanical jolts or pushes during gear transitions, particularly when shifting at cruising speeds.
- **Solution:** Have transmission software adapted or inspected by a certified technician for internal hydraulic or electronic faults.

## Suspension

### Wheels and Low-Profile Tires

The S580's sophisticated air suspension typically offers a sublime ride, but optional 21-inch wheels wrapped in low-profile performance tires are prone to impact damage.

**Frequent Tire Blowouts and Rim Damage**
- **Symptoms:** Repeated tire blowouts, sidewall blisters, and bent rims during normal driving on factory 21-inch low-profile setups.
- **Solution:** Inspect tire pressures regularly or consider downsizing to smaller wheel diameters with higher sidewalls for rough road conditions.

## Electrical

### MBUX Infotainment and Charging Systems

Heavy reliance on cabin-wide screens and dual-battery electrical architecture makes thorough electronic diagnostic testing crucial before purchase.

**Display Blackout and High-Voltage Battery Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Dashboard display screens going completely black while driving, or high-voltage battery faults causing electrical burnout.
- **Solution:** Check software updates for the MBUX system and have the high-voltage and low-voltage charging circuits tested.

## Safety

### Advanced Driver Assistance Systems

The vehicle's suite of active safety and collision-avoidance features provides high-tech security but can occasionally trigger false warnings or unexpected interventions.

**Unexpected Braking and Safety System Deactivation**
- **Symptoms:** Ghost braking events or multiple advanced driver assistance warnings appearing inoperative simultaneously while driving.
- **Solution:** Clean exterior camera and radar sensors; consult a dealer to recalibrate safety modules and update software.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V194000: Suspension:Subframe/Mounting Bracket](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=S580&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** A detached thrust arm can cause a sudden loss of vehicle control, or cause the vehicle to pull to one side during emergency braking, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the sub frame weld on the rear axle carrier and replace the rear axle carrier as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V189000: Steering: Steering Wheel/Handle Bar](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=S580&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** Deactivated hands-off detection will prevent DISTRONIC system features from functioning, including warnings to grab the steering wheel and calling first responders as part of the Active Emergency Stop Assist, which can increase the risk of a crash or injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the hand detection control unit software for the DISTRONIC system, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2022 Mercedes-Benz S580 remains an engineering masterpiece delivering unmatched comfort and performance, but prospective buyers should approach with careful inspection habits. Pay close attention to the complex electrical architecture, the condition of large 21-inch wheels and low-profile tires, and ensure all powertrain software updates have been applied by a dealer or specialist.
