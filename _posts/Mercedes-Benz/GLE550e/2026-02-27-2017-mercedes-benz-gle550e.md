# 2017 Mercedes-Benz GLE550e Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2017 Mercedes-Benz GLE550e is a plug-in hybrid luxury SUV offering an upscale interior and robust performance. However, used car buyers should carefully review owner complaints regarding various electrical, steering, and body components before purchasing. While it delivers strong hybrid efficiency and comfort, prospective owners should inspect the vehicle thoroughly for any prior mechanical or electrical anomalies.

---

## Engine

### 3.0L V6 Turbo Hybrid Engine

Equipped with a 3.0-liter turbocharged V6 engine paired with an electric motor, producing around 436 combined horsepower. This powertrain provides brisk acceleration and efficient cruising, though proper maintenance of both the conventional and hybrid systems is essential for long-term reliability.

## Transmission

### 7-Speed Automatic Transmission

Features a 7-speed automatic transmission designed to integrate with the hybrid drive system. It generally delivers smooth gear changes, though proper fluid maintenance is critical to avoid shifting degradation.

## Steering

### Electronic Power Steering

Issues with electronic power assist can occasionally disable steering functionality without warning, requiring immediate driver attention.

**Power Steering Assist Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers experience a sudden loss of power steering accompanied by an instrument cluster warning message, making the steering wheel extremely stiff and difficult to maneuver.
- **Solution:** Requires diagnosis of the electronic steering system, which may involve replacing the steering gear assembly or related electronic control modules.

## Electrical

### Exterior Lighting and Infotainment

Electrical concerns generally center around auxiliary lighting performance and occasional digital display glitches.

**Headlight and Display Failures**
- **Symptoms:** Low beam headlights failing to illuminate adequately during night driving or center digital screens failing to operate while driving.
- **Solution:** Inspect headlight bulbs, alignment mechanisms, and electrical harnesses; software updates or module replacements may be required for digital screen failures.

## Exterior

### Sunroof and Glass

**Spontaneous Sunroof Shattering**
- **Symptoms:** The panoramic sunroof glass can fracture or shatter unexpectedly while driving at highway speeds, accompanied by a loud pop and falling glass.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the panoramic glass panel and inspection of the surrounding frame rails for wind force damage or alignment issues.

### Panoramic sunroof and glass components.

Exterior body reports highlight occasional structural vulnerabilities with panoramic glass panels.

## Safety

### Seat Belts

Safety equipment generally performs well, though sensor faults can trigger unwarranted warning lights.

**Seat Belt Warning Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** The front passenger seat belt warning light illuminates intermittently or stays on despite the occupant being properly buckled.
- **Solution:** Inspection and potential replacement of faulty seat belt buckle sensors or wiring harnesses.

## Recall Check

**[Recall 21V058000: Electrical System:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=GLE550e&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** The software design of the communication module may fail to communicate the correct vehicle location for the emergency call system (eCall) in the event of a crash. Failure of the eCall system may result in emergency responders being dispatched to the wrong location, increasing the risk of injury following a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2017 Mercedes-Benz GLE550e offers a sophisticated plug-in hybrid experience, but buyers should conduct a thorough pre-purchase inspection. Pay close attention to the electronic power steering functionality, electrical accessories, and the condition of the panoramic glass roof. Ensuring all service records are up to date will help mitigate potential ownership surprises.
