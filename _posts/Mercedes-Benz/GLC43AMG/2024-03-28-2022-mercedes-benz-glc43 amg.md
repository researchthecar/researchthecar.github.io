## 2022 Mercedes-Benz GLC43 AMG

The 2022 Mercedes-Benz GLC43 AMG is a performance-oriented SUV that offers a blend of luxury, capability, and driving excitement. It is powered by a turbocharged 3.0-liter V6 engine producing 385 horsepower and 384 lb-ft of torque, paired with a 9-speed automatic transmission. Other engine and transmission options for the GLC-Class include a 2.0-liter inline-4 engine with a 9-speed automatic transmission and a 3.0-liter inline-6 diesel engine with a 9-speed automatic transmission.

Despite its impressive performance, the GLC43 AMG has been plagued by several issues reported by owners. Here is a comprehensive list of these issues:

**Engine**

**3.0L Turbocharged V6**
* **Turbocharger failure:** The turbocharger can fail prematurely, resulting in a loss of power and elevated exhaust temperatures. The turbocharger and surrounding components need to be replaced. Estimated Cost to Fix: $4,000 - $6,000+
* **Oil leaks:** Oil leaks can occur from various gaskets and seals in the engine, resulting in oil consumption and potential environmental damage. The affected gaskets or seals need to be replaced. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+
Recalls: NHTSA Recall Number 22V-429, 22V-496

**Transmission**

**9-Speed Automatic**
* **Transmission slipping:** The transmission may slip gears or hesitate during shifts, causing a loss of power or rough gear changes. The transmission fluid and filter may need to be replaced, or the transmission may require a rebuild. Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $5,000+
* **Torque converter shudder:** The torque converter may shudder or vibrate during acceleration, leading to an uncomfortable driving experience. The torque converter may need to be replaced. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $3,000+
Recalls: NHTSA Recall Number 22V-429

**Suspension**

* **Air suspension failure:** The air suspension system may fail, resulting in a loss of ride height and handling issues. The air suspension components, such as the compressor, struts, or control module, may need to be replaced. Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $5,000+
Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Battery drain:** The battery may discharge rapidly, even when the vehicle is turned off. This can be caused by a parasitic draw from an electrical component. The affected component needs to be identified and repaired or replaced. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+
* **Electrical glitches:** Various electrical glitches, such as malfunctioning sensors, flickering lights, or intermittent component failures, have been reported. These issues may require software updates, component replacements, or electrical system repairs. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $2,000+
Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Rear bumper cracking:** The rear bumper may crack or split at the corners or edges. The bumper may need to be replaced. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Panoramic sunroof rattle:** The panoramic sunroof may rattle or creak while driving, creating an annoying noise inside the cabin. The sunroof track or alignment may need to be adjusted. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
* **Door lock malfunction:** The door locks may fail to operate properly, preventing entry or exit from the vehicle. The door lock actuators or wiring harness may need to be repaired or replaced. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+
Recalls: None Found

**Overall Summary**

The 2022 Mercedes-Benz GLC43 AMG is a powerful and luxurious SUV, but it has been marred by a number of issues that can compromise its performance, reliability, and overall driving experience. Some of the engine and transmission issues can be costly to repair, while other problems affect the vehicle's comfort and convenience features. Overall, the severity and cost of these issues should be carefully considered before purchasing this model.
