# 2017 Mercedes-Benz AMG GT S

### Overview
The 2017 Mercedes-Benz AMG GT S is a high-performance grand tourer known for its exhilarating driving dynamics and potent V8 engine. As a halo model from Mercedes-AMG, it generally exhibits strong build quality and engineering. However, like any complex performance vehicle, specific areas can experience issues. This report focuses on documented technical reliability concerns beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential problem areas for owners and enthusiasts. The data aims to offer a balanced perspective on the vehicle's long-term dependability, highlighting common component failures and their associated repair considerations.

---


## Engine

### 4.0L Twin-Turbocharged V8 (M178)
**Turbocharger Wastegate Actuator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Check engine light, reduced engine power, whistling noises from the engine bay.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the faulty wastegate actuator or the complete turbocharger assembly.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1500

**Oil Cooler Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Oil smell in the engine bay, visible oil residue around the oil cooler lines, potential oil level drops.
- **Solution:** Replacement of leaking oil cooler lines or the oil cooler itself.
- **Cost:** $500 - $900


## Transmission

### 7-Speed AMG SPEEDSHIFT DCT
**Mechatronics Unit Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Harsh shifting, transmission warning lights, inability to select gears, limp mode engagement.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of the transmission mechatronics unit.
- **Cost:** $2500 - $4000

**Clutch Pack Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Slipping gears, rough engagement, delayed acceleration, especially under load.
- **Solution:** Replacement of worn clutch packs.
- **Cost:** $3000 - $5000

## Electrical

### Infotainment System
**Software Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Screen freezing, unresponsive controls, Bluetooth connectivity issues, navigation system errors.
- **Solution:** Software updates, system resets, or potential replacement of the infotainment module.
- **Cost:** $300 - $1200


### Battery Drain
**Parasitic Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Dead battery after short periods of inactivity, electrical components malfunctioning intermittently.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and repair of the parasitic drain, which could involve faulty modules, wiring, or aftermarket accessories.
- **Cost:** $200 - $800

### Summary Verdict
The 2017 Mercedes-Benz AMG GT S demonstrates a generally strong reliability profile, particularly for its powertrain, which is a testament to AMG's engineering prowess. However, owners should be aware of potential issues with the twin-turbo V8 engine, specifically related to wastegate actuators and oil cooler lines, which can lead to performance degradation and require costly repairs. The 7-speed dual-clutch transmission, while offering rapid shifts, can be susceptible to mechatronics unit failures and clutch pack wear, representing significant repair expenses. Electrical gremlins, such as infotainment system glitches and parasitic battery drain, are also noted concerns that may require professional diagnosis. Despite these potential drawbacks, with diligent maintenance and prompt attention to warning signs, the AMG GT S can remain a thrilling and relatively dependable performance machine.
