# 2018 Mercedes-Benz AMG GT S

### Overview
The 2018 Mercedes-Benz AMG GT S is a high-performance grand tourer known for its exhilarating driving dynamics and potent V8 engine. As a halo model within the Mercedes-Benz lineup, it generally exhibits strong build quality and engineering. However, like any complex performance vehicle, specific areas can experience issues. This report focuses on documented technical reliability concerns beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential problem areas for owners and enthusiasts. The AMG GT S utilizes a sophisticated powertrain and chassis, demanding meticulous attention to its specialized components.

---


## Engine

### 4.0L Twin-Turbocharged V8 (M178)
**Turbocharger Wastegate Actuator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Boost pressure loss, "check engine" light, reduced engine power, rattling noise from turbo area.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the faulty wastegate actuator or the complete turbocharger assembly.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1,500

**Oil Cooler Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Visible oil leaks around the oil cooler lines, potential for oil smell, "low oil pressure" warning.
- **Solution:** Replacement of leaking oil cooler lines or seals.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600


## Transmission

### 7-Speed AMG SPEEDSHIFT DCT
**Mechatronics Unit Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Harsh shifting, transmission slipping, inability to select gears, "transmission fault" warning message.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of the mechatronics unit.
- **Cost:** $2,500 - $4,000

**Clutch Pack Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Jerky acceleration, delayed engagement, rough gear changes, especially at lower speeds.
- **Solution:** Replacement of worn clutch packs.
- **Cost:** $2,000 - $3,500


## Suspension

### AMG Adaptive Performance Suspension
**Electronic Dampers Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Stiff or overly soft ride, "suspension fault" warning, inconsistent damping.
- **Solution:** Replacement of faulty adaptive dampers.
- **Cost:** $700 - $1,200 per corner


## Electrical

### Infotainment System
**COMAND System Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Screen freezing, unresponsiveness, audio cutting out, navigation errors.
- **Solution:** Software updates, or in severe cases, replacement of the COMAND head unit.
- **Cost:** $500 - $2,000


### Battery Drain
**Parasitic Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Dead battery after short periods of inactivity, "battery low" warnings.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and repair of parasitic drain sources (e.g., faulty modules, aftermarket accessories).
- **Cost:** $200 - $800

### Summary Verdict
The 2018 Mercedes-Benz AMG GT S demonstrates a generally high level of reliability, befitting its premium performance segment. The primary areas of concern tend to be related to the complex powertrain and its associated high-performance components, such as the twin-turbo V8 engine's wastegate actuators and oil cooler lines, and the dual-clutch transmission's mechatronics unit and clutch packs. Suspension and electrical systems, particularly the infotainment and potential battery drain issues, can also require attention. While not as prone to widespread failures as some mass-market vehicles, owners should be prepared for the potentially higher repair costs associated with specialized AMG components. Regular maintenance and prompt attention to warning signs are crucial for maintaining the GT S's performance and longevity.
