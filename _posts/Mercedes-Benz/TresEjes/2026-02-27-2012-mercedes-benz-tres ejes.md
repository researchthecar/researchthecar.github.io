# 2012 Mercedes-Benz Tres Ejes

### Overview
The 2012 Mercedes-Benz Tres Ejes, a commercial vehicle designed for heavy-duty applications, generally exhibits a robust build quality characteristic of the Mercedes-Benz brand. While specific model variants and their operational environments can influence long-term reliability, this analysis focuses on documented common issues. The Tres Ejes is engineered for durability, with its powertrain and chassis components designed to withstand demanding usage. However, like any complex machinery, certain areas may require attention over time. This report details potential reliability concerns across key automotive systems, providing insights into common problems, their symptoms, typical solutions, and estimated repair costs to aid in maintenance planning and understanding.

## Engine

### OM936 7.7L Inline-6 Diesel
**DEF Injector Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Check Engine Light illumination, reduced engine power, increased exhaust emissions.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the Diesel Exhaust Fluid (DEF) injector.
- **Cost:** $300 - $700

**EGR Cooler Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Coolant loss, white smoke from exhaust, overheating.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the Exhaust Gas Recirculation (EGR) cooler.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1200


### OM470 10.7L Inline-6 Diesel
**Turbocharger Actuator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of engine power, black smoke, turbocharger warning light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the turbocharger actuator.
- **Cost:** $400 - $900

**Fuel Injector Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Rough idling, poor fuel economy, engine misfires, Check Engine Light.
- **Solution:** Cleaning or replacement of faulty fuel injectors.
- **Cost:** $350 - $800 per injector


## Transmission

### Mercedes-Benz G 280-6 Manual
**Clutch Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Slipping clutch, difficulty shifting gears, burning smell.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the clutch assembly (clutch disc, pressure plate, release bearing).
- **Cost:** $800 - $1500


### Mercedes-Benz G 210-16 Automated Manual
**Shift Actuator Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Erratic gear changes, transmission warning light, inability to select gears.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of the shift actuator module.
- **Cost:** $600 - $1300


## Suspension

### Heavy-Duty Leaf Spring Suspension
**Leaf Spring Breakage**
- **Symptoms:** Sagging rear end, uneven ride height, clunking noises over bumps.
- **Solution:** Replacement of broken leaf springs.
- **Cost:** $400 - $900 per axle


### Air Suspension (Optional)
**Air Spring Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle sagging on one side, compressor running excessively, harsh ride.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of leaking air springs or air lines.
- **Cost:** $300 - $800 per corner


## Electrical

### Standard Electrical System
**Alternator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Battery warning light, dimming headlights, electrical system malfunctions.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the alternator.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1000

**Wiring Harness Corrosion**
- **Symptoms:** Intermittent electrical faults, corrosion on connectors, sensor errors.
- **Solution:** Inspection, cleaning, and repair or replacement of corroded wiring harnesses and connectors.
- **Cost:** $200 - $600

### Summary Verdict
The 2012 Mercedes-Benz Tres Ejes demonstrates good overall reliability, particularly in its core powertrain and chassis components, which are built for demanding commercial use. The most frequently reported issues tend to be related to emissions control systems (DEF injectors, EGR coolers) and specific transmission components like shift actuators, especially in automated manual transmissions. Electrical system issues, such as alternator failure, are also noted. While less common, suspension components like leaf springs can experience wear and tear under heavy loads. The exterior and interior generally hold up well, with no widespread critical failures reported. Proactive maintenance, especially concerning the emissions system and regular checks of the electrical and suspension components, is recommended to ensure continued optimal performance and longevity of the Tres Ejes.
