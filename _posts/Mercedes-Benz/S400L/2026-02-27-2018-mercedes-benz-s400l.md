# 2018 Mercedes-Benz S400L Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2018 Mercedes-Benz S-Class represents the pinnacle of luxury, advanced technology, and engineering sophistication. While generally built to a high standard, used-car buyers should pay close attention to complex electronic systems, high-performance powertrains, and routine wear-and-tear items. Reviewing service history and performing a thorough pre-purchase inspection can help mitigate potential ownership surprises.

---

## Engine

### 3.0L Twin-Turbo V6 (S450)

The 3.0-liter twin-turbocharged V6 engine delivers smooth and efficient performance for entry S-Class trims, though buyers should monitor for occasional ignition or misfire concerns.

**Engine Misfire and Loss of Power**
- **Symptoms:** The engine may occasionally misfire upon acceleration, causing the vehicle to shake, lose power, and trigger the check engine light.
- **Solution:** Diagnose ignition components, fuel injectors, or compression levels and replace faulty parts as needed.

### 4.0L Twin-Turbo V8 (S560)

The 4.0-liter bi-turbo V8 offers effortless, high-output acceleration characteristic of flagship luxury sedans, requiring diligent maintenance to keep seals and cooling systems sound.

**Oil and Coolant Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have reported fluid leaks, such as engine oil seepage from rear seals or slow coolant loss, even at relatively low mileages.
- **Solution:** Inspect engine gaskets and seals during routine services and replace failing seals promptly to prevent major leaks.

## Transmission

### 9-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 9-speed automatic transmission provides seamless gear changes and optimizes highway efficiency across the model lineup.

**Unexpected Shifting to Neutral**
- **Symptoms:** The gear shifter may intermittently shift into neutral independently while driving, requiring the driver to hold the selector to reset the system.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified technician inspect the electronic shifter module and transmission control unit for software updates or hardware faults.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

Brake components provide strong stopping power for a heavy luxury vehicle, but any reports of reduced responsiveness require immediate professional evaluation.

**Brake Responsiveness Concerns**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have experienced unexpected instances where depressing the brake pedal failed to decelerate the vehicle adequately or resulted in delayed response.
- **Solution:** Inspect the master cylinder, brake booster, and hydraulic lines immediately upon any sign of pedal travel irregularities.

## Steering

### Power Steering System

The electronic steering system offers light maneuvering at low speeds and firm feedback on the highway.

**Stiff Steering and Malfunction Warnings**
- **Symptoms:** The steering wheel may suddenly become extremely difficult to turn accompanied by a dashboard warning message to increase physical effort.
- **Solution:** Check the electronic power steering module, wiring harnesses, and related software calibration.

## Summary Verdict

The 2018 Mercedes-Benz S-Class delivers an unmatched blend of comfort and advanced technology, but its complex systems demand thorough inspection prior to purchase. While powertrain options are generally robust, potential buyers should carefully verify the condition of the electronics, brakes, and engine seals to ensure a trouble-free ownership experience.
