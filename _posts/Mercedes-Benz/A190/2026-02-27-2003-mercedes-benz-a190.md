# 2003 Mercedes-Benz A190

### Overview
The 2003 Mercedes-Benz A190, part of the W168 generation, was known for its innovative 'sandwich' floor design, maximizing interior space within a compact footprint. While generally considered a reliable vehicle for its era, specific components and systems can exhibit common issues as they age. This analysis focuses on documented technical reliability concerns beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential problem areas for owners and prospective buyers. Understanding these potential issues can help in proactive maintenance and budgeting for repairs.

---


## Engine

### 1.9L Inline-4 (M166 E19)
**Ignition Coil Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Misfiring, rough idling, check engine light, reduced power.
- **Solution:** Replacement of faulty ignition coil(s).
- **Cost:** $150 - $300 per coil

**Mass Airflow Sensor (MAF) Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Hesitation during acceleration, poor fuel economy, stalling, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Cleaning or replacement of the MAF sensor.
- **Cost:** $100 - $250


## Transmission

### Autotronic (Continuously Variable Transmission - CVT)
**Solenoid Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Jerky shifting, slipping gears, inability to engage drive or reverse.
- **Solution:** Replacement of faulty transmission solenoids.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1200

**CVT Fluid Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Low transmission fluid level, difficulty shifting, potential transmission damage.
- **Solution:** Repair of leaks and fluid flush/replacement.
- **Cost:** $300 - $700


## Suspension

### Standard Chassis
**Worn Control Arm Bushings**
- **Symptoms:** Clunking noises over bumps, vague steering feel, uneven tire wear.
- **Solution:** Replacement of worn control arm bushings.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600

**Strut Mount Bearing Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Popping or creaking noise when turning the steering wheel, especially at low speeds.
- **Solution:** Replacement of strut mount bearings.
- **Cost:** $200 - $400


## Electrical

### Body Control Module (BCM)
**Intermittent Electrical Faults**
- **Symptoms:** Malfunctioning power windows, central locking issues, dashboard warning lights, interior lights not working.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and potential reprogramming or replacement of the BCM.
- **Cost:** $600 - $1500


### Window Regulator Failure
**Power Window Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Power windows not operating, slow operation, or falling into the door.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the window regulator assembly.
- **Cost:** $200 - $400 per window


## Exterior

### Body Panels & Seals
**No Major Issues**
- **Symptoms:** No widespread issues reported.
- **Solution:** No widespread issues reported.
- **Cost:** No widespread issues reported.


## Interior

### HVAC System
**Blower Motor Resistor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** HVAC fan only works on the highest setting or not at all.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the blower motor resistor.
- **Cost:** $100 - $250


### Seat Adjustment Mechanism
**Manual Seat Adjustment Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Difficulty adjusting seat position, stuck levers.
- **Solution:** Lubrication or replacement of seat adjustment components.
- **Cost:** $150 - $350


--- 
### Summary Verdict
The 2003 Mercedes-Benz A190, while offering practical space and Mercedes-Benz refinement, is susceptible to age-related issues common in vehicles of this vintage. The Autotronic CVT transmission can be a source of costly repairs if solenoids fail or leaks develop. Electrical gremlins, particularly related to the Body Control Module and window regulators, are also noted concerns. Engine components like ignition coils and MAF sensors may require attention. Suspension wear, especially bushings and strut mounts, is typical. Owners should be prepared for potential repairs in these areas, and regular maintenance, particularly of the transmission fluid, is crucial for longevity.
