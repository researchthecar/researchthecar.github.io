# 2017 Mercedes-Benz SLC300 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2017 Mercedes-Benz SLC300 offers a stylish hardtop convertible experience with agile handling and a refined turbocharged powertrain. Prospective buyers should review owner-reported mechanical and body concerns closely, as isolated severe failures have been documented.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4

The 2.0-liter turbocharged inline-4 engine produces approximately 241 horsepower and 273 lb-ft of torque, providing brisk acceleration for the roadster. While generally responsive, owners have reported isolated catastrophic internal failures that require careful pre-purchase inspection.

**Internal Engine Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Complete engine failure accompanied by broken internal components such as a piston around 76,000 miles.
- **Solution:** Requires extensive engine repair or a complete engine replacement.

## Transmission

### 9-Speed Automatic Transmission

Equipped with a 9-speed automatic transmission driving the rear wheels, the SLC300 delivers smooth and efficient gear shifts. No widespread recurring transmission defects have been highlighted by owner complaints.

## Exterior

### Retractable Hardtop Roof

Issues related to the retractable hardtop mechanism can involve wind noise and rare structural panel detachment.

**Roof Panel Detachment and Wind Noise**
- **Symptoms:** Loud whistling sounds coming from the upper roof panel during driving, or in severe cases, complete detachment of the front roof panel at highway speeds.
- **Solution:** Inspect roof seals, panel alignment, and latching mechanisms; replace damaged roof assemblies as necessary.

---

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V058000: Electrical System:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=SLC300&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** The software design of the communication module may fail to communicate the correct vehicle location for the emergency call system (eCall) in the event of a crash. Failure of the eCall system may result in emergency responders being dispatched to the wrong location, increasing the risk of injury following a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 17V308000: Electronic Stability Control (Esc)](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=SLC300&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** If brake pads are constantly applied over long periods of driving, the brake components may heat up, affecting braking performance and increasing the risk of a crash. Additionally, the dragging brakes may overheat, increasing the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** MBUSA will notify owners, and dealers will update the software for the ESC system, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2017 Mercedes-Benz SLC300 is an engaging luxury roadster, but buyers should exercise caution. Be sure to verify the condition of the retractable hardtop and request a thorough mechanical inspection to check for internal engine health before purchasing.
