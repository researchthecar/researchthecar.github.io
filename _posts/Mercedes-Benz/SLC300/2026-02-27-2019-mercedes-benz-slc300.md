# 2019 Mercedes-Benz SLC300 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a 2019 Mercedes-Benz SLC300, buyers will find a stylish and engaging retractable hardtop roadster powered by a punchy turbocharged four-cylinder engine. While overall owner complaint volume is very low, prospective buyers should always perform a thorough pre-purchase inspection to check for early electronic or powertrain sensor glitches that owner reports occasionally highlight.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4

This 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine produces around 241 horsepower and 273 lb-ft of torque, offering brisk acceleration for the roadster. It generally provides dependable daily operation, though routine maintenance and prompt attention to warning lights are essential.

**Check Engine Light and Exhaust Smoke**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle may exhibit a check engine light accompanied by noticeable exhaust smoke and hesitation while driving.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified mechanic or dealership diagnose fault codes (such as P052E71) and inspect the emissions and crankcase ventilation systems.

## Transmission

### 9-Speed Automatic Transmission

Paired with rear-wheel drive, this 9-speed automatic transmission delivers smooth, prompt gear shifts and helps optimize highway fuel economy. It has a solid reputation for refinement when properly maintained.

## Summary Verdict

The 2019 Mercedes-Benz SLC300 is an appealing luxury roadster offering a refined blend of performance and open-air cruising comfort. With minimal reported owner complaints, it stands out as a relatively trouble-free pre-owned choice, provided buyers verify service history and ensure all powertrain electronics are operating correctly.
