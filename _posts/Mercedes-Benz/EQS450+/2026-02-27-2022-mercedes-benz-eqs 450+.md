# 2022 Mercedes-Benz EQS 450+

### Overview
The 2022 Mercedes-Benz EQS 450+ represents a significant leap in electric luxury vehicle technology. As the first all-electric sedan from Mercedes-Benz's EQ line, it boasts advanced aerodynamics, a futuristic interior, and a powerful electric drivetrain. Reliability data for this model is still emerging, given its recent introduction. However, initial reports and the inherent complexity of its systems suggest a focus on sophisticated electronics and battery management. While Mercedes-Benz has a strong reputation for build quality, the cutting-edge nature of the EQS means that any potential issues are likely to be related to its advanced software and integrated systems rather than traditional mechanical components. Owners should expect a premium ownership experience with a focus on proactive maintenance and software updates.

## Electrical

### High-Voltage Battery System
**Battery Management System (BMS) Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Intermittent range inaccuracies, charging interruptions, or dashboard warnings related to battery performance.
- **Solution:** Software updates for the BMS, potential battery module diagnostics or replacement under warranty.
- **Cost:** $0 - $5,000 (if out of warranty, highly dependent on specific issue)


### Infotainment and MBUX System
**Software Freezes and Lag**
- **Symptoms:** Infotainment screen becomes unresponsive, slow to react to touch inputs, or requires a system reboot.
- **Solution:** Software updates, system resets, or potential replacement of the MBUX control unit.
- **Cost:** $500 - $2,500


### 12V Auxiliary Battery
**Premature Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Various electrical systems malfunctioning, difficulty starting the vehicle (though less critical in EVs), or warning lights related to the electrical system.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the 12V auxiliary battery.
- **Cost:** $300 - $700

### Summary Verdict
The 2022 Mercedes-Benz EQS 450+ demonstrates strong initial reliability, particularly in its core electric powertrain and chassis components. The primary areas of concern, as with many advanced electric vehicles, lie within the sophisticated electrical and software systems. Issues related to the Battery Management System and the MBUX infotainment system are the most frequently cited, though often resolvable with software updates. The 12V auxiliary battery, while a more traditional component, can also present issues. Overall, the EQS 450+ offers a reliable luxury electric experience, with potential issues concentrated in its cutting-edge technology rather than mechanical wear.
