## Engine

### 2.2L CDI Diesel
**Glow Plug Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Difficulty starting in cold weather, rough idling, check engine light illumination.
- **Solution:** Replacement of faulty glow plugs and potentially the glow plug control module.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600

**EGR Valve Clogging**
- **Symptoms:** Reduced engine power, increased fuel consumption, black smoke from exhaust, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Cleaning or replacement of the Exhaust Gas Recirculation (EGR) valve.
- **Cost:** $400 - $800


## Transmission

### 5-Speed Automatic (722.6)
**Torque Converter Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Slipping gears, shuddering during acceleration, delayed engagement, fluid leaks.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of the torque converter and potentially transmission fluid flush.
- **Cost:** $1200 - $2500

**Transmission Control Module (TCM) Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Erratic shifting, 'limp mode' engagement, transmission warning lights.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and replacement of the TCM.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1500

## Electrical

### General Electrical System
**Alternator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Dimming headlights, battery warning light, electrical components malfunctioning, dead battery.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the alternator.
- **Cost:** $500 - $900

**Wiring Harness Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Intermittent electrical faults, warning lights appearing and disappearing, non-functional accessories.
- **Solution:** Inspection and repair or replacement of damaged wiring sections.
- **Cost:** $300 - $700

### Summary Verdict
The 2011 Mercedes-Benz Gran Viale LE, while a specialized vehicle, demonstrates a generally reliable mechanical foundation. The 2.2L CDI diesel engine, though susceptible to common diesel issues like DPF clogging and EGR valve problems, is generally robust. Transmission concerns, particularly with the 5-speed automatic, can arise, with torque converter and TCM issues being the most notable. Electrical systems may require attention, with alternators and wiring harnesses being potential points of failure. Suspension, exterior, and interior components are less prone to widespread issues, suggesting good build quality in these areas. Regular maintenance and prompt attention to early symptoms are key to ensuring long-term reliability for this model.
