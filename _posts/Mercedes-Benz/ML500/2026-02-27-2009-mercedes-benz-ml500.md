# 2009 Mercedes-Benz ML500

### Overview
The 2009 Mercedes-Benz ML500, part of the W164 generation, is a luxury mid-size SUV known for its comfortable ride and powerful performance. Equipped with a robust 5.0L V8 engine, it offers strong acceleration and towing capabilities. While generally well-regarded for its build quality and features, like any vehicle of this age, it can experience specific issues. This analysis focuses on common mechanical and electrical concerns reported by owners, excluding routine maintenance. Understanding these potential problem areas can help owners budget for repairs and ensure the longevity of their ML500.

---


## Engine

### 5.0L V8 (M113)
**Oil Leaks (Valve Cover Gaskets, Oil Cooler Seals)**
- **Symptoms:** Visible oil drips under the vehicle, burning oil smell, low oil level.
- **Solution:** Replacement of valve cover gaskets and/or oil cooler seals.
- **Cost:** $400 - $800

**Coolant Leaks (Reservoir, Hoses)**
- **Symptoms:** Low coolant level, sweet smell, visible coolant puddles, engine overheating.
- **Solution:** Replacement of coolant reservoir, hoses, or radiator.
- **Cost:** $300 - $700


## Transmission

### 7G-TRONIC (722.9)
**Transmission Control Module (TCM) Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Harsh shifting, slipping gears, limp mode, check engine light, failure to engage gears.
- **Solution:** Replacement or repair of the Transmission Control Module.
- **Cost:** $1000 - $2500

**Torque Converter Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Shuddering at low speeds, rough engagement, slipping.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the torque converter.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1500


## Suspension

### Air Suspension (Optional)
**Air Spring Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle sagging on one or more corners, compressor running excessively, uneven ride height.
- **Solution:** Replacement of leaking air springs or seals.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1000 per corner

**Air Compressor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle does not raise, compressor makes noise but no air is delivered, suspension remains low.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the air suspension compressor.
- **Cost:** $600 - $1200

## Electrical

### General Electrical System
**Window Regulator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Power windows not operating, slow operation, grinding noises.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the window regulator mechanism.
- **Cost:** $250 - $500 per window

**Sensor Failures (e.g., ABS, Crankshaft)**
- **Symptoms:** Warning lights on the dashboard (ABS, ESP, Check Engine), erratic performance.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and replacement of faulty sensors.
- **Cost:** $200 - $600

## Interior

### Cabin Electronics and Features
**Heated Seat Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** One or both heated seats not working, intermittent heating.
- **Solution:** Replacement of heating elements or control modules.
- **Cost:** $300 - $700

**Navigation System Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Screen freezing, system reboots, inaccurate GPS.
- **Solution:** Software update or replacement of the COMAND unit.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1500

### Summary Verdict
The 2009 Mercedes-Benz ML500 offers a compelling blend of luxury and performance, but potential buyers and current owners should be aware of common issues. The 5.0L V8 engine is generally robust, but oil and coolant leaks can occur. The 7G-TRONIC transmission, while advanced, is prone to TCM and torque converter problems. Optional air suspension systems can develop leaks or compressor failures. Electrical gremlins, such as window regulators and various sensors, are also noted. Interior electronics like heated seats and the navigation system can also require attention. Despite these potential drawbacks, with diligent maintenance and awareness of these specific repair areas, the ML500 can remain a reliable and enjoyable vehicle.
