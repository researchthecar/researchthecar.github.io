# 2002 Mercedes-Benz ML500

### Overview
The 2002 Mercedes-Benz ML500, part of the W163 generation, offered a blend of luxury and utility. While generally well-regarded for its comfortable ride and powerful engine options, like any vehicle of its age, it has specific areas that have shown a propensity for issues. Owners often report on the performance and features, but long-term reliability can be a concern, particularly with certain electronic components and the aging drivetrain. Regular maintenance is crucial for this model to mitigate potential problems and ensure continued performance. This analysis focuses on documented common failures beyond standard wear and tear.

---


## Engine

### 5.0L V8 (M113)
**Ignition Coil Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Misfires, rough idling, check engine light, reduced power.
- **Solution:** Replacement of faulty ignition coil(s).
- **Cost:** $300 - $600

**Mass Air Flow (MAF) Sensor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Poor acceleration, stalling, rough idle, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Cleaning or replacement of the MAF sensor.
- **Cost:** $200 - $500

**Oil Cooler Seals Leak**
- **Symptoms:** Coolant loss, oil in coolant, overheating.
- **Solution:** Replacement of oil cooler seals.
- **Cost:** $500 - $900


## Transmission

### 5G-Tronic Automatic (722.6)
**Transmission Control Module (TCM) Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Harsh shifting, slipping gears, failure to shift, limp mode.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of the TCM.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1500

**Conductor Plate Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Erratic shifting, transmission warning light, no communication with TCM.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the conductor plate.
- **Cost:** $600 - $1200


## Suspension

### Standard Chassis
**Air Suspension Compressor Failure (if equipped)**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle sagging, uneven ride height, compressor not engaging.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the air suspension compressor.
- **Cost:** $700 - $1300

**Control Arm Bushing Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Clunking noises over bumps, vague steering, uneven tire wear.
- **Solution:** Replacement of worn control arm bushings.
- **Cost:** $400 - $800


## Electrical

### General Electrical System
**Window Regulator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Power windows not operating, slow window movement, grinding noise.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the window regulator mechanism.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600

**Instrument Cluster Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Flickering gauges, dead pixels, intermittent power loss to cluster.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of the instrument cluster.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1000

**Keyless Entry/Remote Fob Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Remote not working, intermittent locking/unlocking.
- **Solution:** Reprogramming or replacement of the key fob, or repair of receiver module.
- **Cost:** $150 - $400


## Exterior

### Body and Trim
**Rust on Body Panels**
- **Symptoms:** Visible rust, particularly around wheel arches, rocker panels, and tailgate.
- **Solution:** Rust repair and repainting.
- **Cost:** $500 - $2000+

**Headlight Lens Hazing/Yellowing**
- **Symptoms:** Reduced light output, poor visibility at night.
- **Solution:** Restoration or replacement of headlight assemblies.
- **Cost:** $200 - $600


## Interior

### Cabin Components
**Headliner Sagging**
- **Symptoms:** Fabric detaching from the headliner board.
- **Solution:** Re-gluing or replacement of the headliner.
- **Cost:** $300 - $700

**HVAC Blower Motor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** No airflow from vents, or intermittent airflow.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the blower motor or resistor.
- **Cost:** $250 - $500


--- 
### Summary Verdict
The 2002 Mercedes-Benz ML500, while offering a premium experience, is prone to several common issues typical of German luxury vehicles of this era. The engine, particularly the 5.0L V8, can experience ignition and sensor problems. The 5G-Tronic transmission is known for its complex electronic components like the TCM and conductor plate, which can lead to costly repairs. Electrical gremlins, such as window regulators and instrument clusters, are also frequently reported. Suspension components, especially if equipped with air suspension, can require attention. Exterior rust and interior wear, like sagging headliners, are also considerations. Prospective buyers should be prepared for potential maintenance costs beyond routine servicing.
