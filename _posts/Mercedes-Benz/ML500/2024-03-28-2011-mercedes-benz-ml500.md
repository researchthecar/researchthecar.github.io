**Introduction:**

The 2011 Mercedes-Benz ML500 is a midsize luxury SUV powered by a 5.5L V8 engine producing 382 horsepower and 391 lb-ft of torque. It is paired with a 7-speed automatic transmission and comes standard with all-wheel drive. The ML500 is known for its spacious interior, comfortable ride, and impressive performance. However, like any vehicle, it has its share of issues.

**Engine**

* **5.5L V8**

  * **Valve Stem Seals Leak:** Symptoms include excessive oil consumption and blue smoke from the exhaust. Solution involves replacing the valve stem seals. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $1,200 - $2,000+
  * **Intake Manifold Runner Problems:** Issues with the intake manifold runners can cause loss of power, hesitation, and poor fuel economy. Solution involves replacing the intake manifold. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $1,000 - $1,500+
  * **Timing Chain Tensioner Failure:** A faulty timing chain tensioner can lead to excessive noise and potential engine damage. Solution involves replacing the tensioner and timing chain. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $2,000 - $3,000+
  * **Recalls:** NHTSA Campaign ID: 14V414000, 14V413000, 11V406000, 10V390000

**Transmission**

* **7-Speed Automatic**

  * **Transmission Fluid Leak:** Leaks can occur from the transmission pan, lines, or seals. Symptoms include low transmission fluid levels and slipping gears. Solution involves repairing or replacing the leaking components. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $500 - $1,500+
  * **Shifting Problems:** Issues with the transmission control module or solenoids can cause rough shifting or delayed shifts. Solution involves diagnosing and replacing the faulty components. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $1,000 - $2,000+
  * **Torque Converter Failure:** Early torque converters had issues with premature lockup and increased wear. Solution involves replacing the torque converter. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $1,500 - $2,500+
  * **Recalls:** None Found

**Suspension**

* **Air Suspension Malfunction:** Air leaking from the suspension system can lead to uneven ride height, poor handling, and reduced comfort. Solution involves repairing or replacing the faulty air springs, lines, or compressor. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $1,000 - $2,000+
* **Control Arm Bushings Wear:** Worn control arm bushings can cause excessive vibration and noise, especially over bumps. Solution involves replacing the bushings. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $300 - $600+
* **Recalls:** None Found

**Electrical**

* **Battery Discharge:** A faulty voltage regulator or electrical drain can cause the battery to discharge prematurely. Symptoms include difficulty starting and reduced electrical power. Solution involves diagnosing and fixing the underlying electrical issue. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $500 - $1,000+
* **Electrical Wiring Harness Issues:** Faulty wiring can lead to various electrical problems, such as malfunctioning lights, gauges, or audio systems. Solution involves repairing or replacing the affected wiring. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $200 - $1,000+
* **Recalls:** NHTSA Campaign ID: 14V371000

**Exterior**

* **Roof Rack Detachment:** Early models had issues with the roof rack detaching from the vehicle. Solution involves replacing the roof rack and reinforcing the mounting points. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $800 - $1,500+
* **Recalls:** NHTSA Campaign ID: 11V398000

**Interior**

* **Heater Core Leak:** A leaking heater core can lead to coolant smell inside the vehicle and reduced heating efficiency. Solution involves replacing the heater core. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $1,000 - $1,500+
* **Navigation System Malfunction:** The navigation system can experience software glitches or hardware issues, causing incorrect navigation data or display problems. Solution involves updating the software or replacing the affected components. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $500 - $1,200+
* **Recalls:** NHTSA Campaign ID: 14V222000

**Summary:**

The 2011 Mercedes-Benz ML500 is a well-regarded luxury SUV, but it has its share of issues. The most significant problems include engine valve stem seal leaks, intake manifold runner problems, and timing chain tensioner failure. The suspension can experience air suspension malfunctions and control arm bushing wear. Electrical issues like battery discharge and wiring harness problems are also reported. Interior issues include heater core leaks and navigation system malfunctions. While some issues are minor and relatively inexpensive to fix, others can be costly and impact the overall reliability of the vehicle.
