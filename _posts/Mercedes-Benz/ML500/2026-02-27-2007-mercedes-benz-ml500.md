# 2007 Mercedes-Benz ML500

### Overview
The 2007 Mercedes-Benz ML500, part of the W164 generation, is a luxury mid-size SUV known for its comfortable ride and powerful performance. It typically came equipped with a robust 5.0L V8 engine, offering strong acceleration and towing capability. While generally well-regarded for its build quality and features, like any vehicle of this age, it can experience specific issues. This analysis focuses on documented common problems beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential areas of concern for owners and prospective buyers. Understanding these potential reliability points can help in proactive maintenance and budgeting for repairs.

---


## Engine

### 5.0L V8 (M113)
**Oil Leaks (Valve Covers, Oil Pan)**
- **Symptoms:** Visible oil stains under the vehicle, burning oil smell, low oil level.
- **Solution:** Replacement of gaskets and seals.
- **Cost:** $300 - $700

**Coolant Leaks (Hoses, Radiator)**
- **Symptoms:** Overheating, visible coolant puddles, sweet smell.
- **Solution:** Replacement of hoses, radiator, or water pump.
- **Cost:** $400 - $900


## Transmission

### 722.6 (5-Speed Automatic)
**Transmission Fluid Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Leaking fluid from transmission pan or cooler lines, rough shifting, slipping gears.
- **Solution:** Replacement of seals, pan gasket, or cooler lines.
- **Cost:** $250 - $600

**Electronic Conductor Plate Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Harsh shifting, transmission warning light, failure to engage gears.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the conductor plate.
- **Cost:** $600 - $1200


## Suspension

### Air Suspension (Optional)
**Air Spring Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle sagging on one or more corners, compressor running excessively, uneven ride height.
- **Solution:** Replacement of air springs or air lines.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1500 per corner

**Air Compressor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Inability to raise or lower the vehicle, constant compressor running, suspension warning light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the air compressor unit.
- **Cost:** $700 - $1400

## Electrical

### General Electrical System
**Window Regulator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Power windows not operating, slow window movement, grinding noises.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the window regulator mechanism.
- **Cost:** $250 - $500 per window

**Headlight Ballast Failure**
- **Symptoms:** One or both HID headlights not illuminating or flickering.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the headlight ballast.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600 per headlight

## Interior

### Cabin Components
**Heated Seat Element Failure**
- **Symptoms:** One or both heated seats not working.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the seat heating element or switch.
- **Cost:** $300 - $700

**Sunroof Motor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Sunroof not opening or closing properly, slow operation.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the sunroof motor.

### Summary Verdict
The 2007 Mercedes-Benz ML500 offers a compelling blend of luxury and performance, but like many premium vehicles of its era, it is not immune to specific mechanical and electrical issues. Common concerns revolve around potential oil and coolant leaks from the robust V8 engine, and transmission issues such as fluid leaks or conductor plate failures in the 5-speed automatic. Optional air suspension systems can be a source of costly repairs due to leaks or compressor failures. Electrical gremlins like window regulators and headlight ballasts are also noted. While the exterior and standard suspension tend to be reliable, owners should be prepared for potential maintenance costs associated with the powertrain and optional features. Regular servicing and prompt attention to early signs of trouble are crucial for maintaining the ML500's reliability and value.
