**Introduction:**

The 2006 Mercedes-Benz ML500 is a mid-size luxury SUV powered by a 5.0L V8 engine that produces 302 horsepower and 339 lb-ft of torque. It is paired with a 7-speed automatic transmission. The ML500 is known for its spacious interior, comfortable ride, and high-end features. However, there are some common issues that have been reported by owners.

**Engine**

**5.0L V8**

* **Intake Manifold Leaks:** Description: Cracks can develop in the plastic intake manifolds, causing vacuum leaks and rough idling. Solution: Replace the intake manifold. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
* **Cam Shaft Adjuster Failure:** Description: The camshaft adjusters can fail, leading to reduced engine performance and check engine lights. Solution: Replace the camshaft adjusters. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+

**Transmission**

**7-Speed Automatic**

* **Shifting Issues:** Description: The transmission may experience rough or delayed shifts, especially when cold. Solution: Replace the valve body or transmission fluid. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $3,000+
* **Torque Converter Failure:** Description: The torque converter may fail, causing shuddering or slipping during gear changes. Solution: Replace the torque converter. Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,500+

**Suspension**

* **Air Suspension Compressor Failure:** Description: The air suspension compressor may fail, causing the vehicle to sag or become unlevel. Solution: Replace the air suspension compressor. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
* **Lower Control Arm Bushings Failure:** Description: The lower control arm bushings can wear out, causing clunking noises and poor handling. Solution: Replace the lower control arm bushings. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+

**Electrical**

* **Battery Discharge:** Description: The battery may discharge prematurely due to a parasitic drain. Solution: Identify and repair the source of the drain. Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $500+
* **Headlamp Leveling System Failure:** Description: The headlamp leveling system may fail, causing the headlights to point incorrectly. Solution: Replace the headlamp leveling sensor or control module. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+

**Exterior**

* **Sunroof Drainage Issues:** Description: The sunroof drainage system can become clogged, causing water leaks into the cabin. Solution: Clean the drainage system or replace clogged hoses. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+

**Interior**

* **Seat Memory Module Failure:** Description: The seat memory module may fail, causing the seats to lose their programmed positions. Solution: Replace the seat memory module. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
* **HVAC Panel Failure:** Description: The HVAC panel may malfunction, causing issues with climate control. Solution: Replace the HVAC panel or control module. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+

**Summary:**

The 2006 Mercedes-Benz ML500 has some common issues, including intake manifold leaks, camshaft adjuster failures, shifting issues, torque converter failures, and suspension problems. The electrical system can also experience battery discharge and headlamp leveling system failures. Interior issues include seat memory module failures and HVAC panel malfunctions. The severity of these issues varies, and the cost to fix them can range from a few hundred dollars to several thousand dollars.
