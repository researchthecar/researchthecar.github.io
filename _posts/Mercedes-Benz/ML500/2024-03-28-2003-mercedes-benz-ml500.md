**Introduction:**

The 2003 Mercedes-Benz ML500 is a luxury SUV powered by a 5.0-liter V8 engine producing 302 horsepower and 339 lb-ft of torque. It came standard with a 5-speed automatic transmission. The ML500 is known for its spacious cabin, comfortable ride, and powerful V8 engine. However, it has also been plagued by several issues over the years.

**Engine**

* **5.0-liter V8**
    * **Intake manifold gasket failure:** Symptoms include coolant leaks, overheating, and poor performance. Requires replacement of the intake manifold gaskets. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $1,000 - $2,000+
    * **Timing chain tensioner failure:** Symptoms include rattling noises from the engine, reduced power, and possible engine damage. Requires replacement of the timing chain tensioner. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $2,000 - $3,000+
* **No Issues Found for Other Engine Options**

**Transmission**

* **5-speed Automatic**
    * **Transmission slipping:** Symptoms include delayed or rough shifting, lack of power, and check engine light illumination. Requires repair or replacement of the transmission. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $3,000 - $5,000+
    * **Torque converter failure:** Symptoms include shuddering or slipping when shifting, overheating, and loss of power. Requires replacement of the torque converter. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $1,500 - $2,500+
* **No Issues Found for Other Transmission Options**

**Suspension**

* **Air suspension leak:** Symptoms include sagging or uneven suspension, rough ride, and warning lights on the dashboard. Requires repair or replacement of the air suspension system. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $1,500 - $3,000+
* **No Issues Found for Other Suspension Components**

**Electrical**

* **BCM failure:** Symptoms include electrical malfunctions, such as inoperative lights, windows, or locks. Requires replacement of the Body Control Module (BCM). **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $500 - $1,000+
* **Alternator failure:** Symptoms include dim headlights, difficulty starting the engine, and battery warning light illumination. Requires replacement of the alternator. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $500 - $1,000+
* **No Issues Found for Other Electrical Components**

**Exterior**

* **Rust on rear quarter panels:** Requires repair or replacement of the affected body panels. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $1,000 - $2,000+
* **No Issues Found for Other Exterior Components**

**Interior**

* **Sunroof drain tube clogging:** Symptoms include water leaking into the interior of the vehicle. Requires cleaning or replacement of the sunroof drain tubes. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $200 - $500+
* **Navigation system failure:** Symptoms include blank or unresponsive screen, lost GPS signal, or system crashes. Requires repair or replacement of the navigation system. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $500 - $1,500+
* **No Issues Found for Other Interior Components**

**Summary:**

The 2003 Mercedes-Benz ML500 is a luxury SUV with several known issues, particularly with the engine, transmission, suspension, and electrical systems. These issues can be costly to repair, ranging from a few hundred dollars to several thousand dollars. However, the ML500 remains a popular choice for drivers who value its spacious cabin, comfortable ride, and powerful V8 engine.
