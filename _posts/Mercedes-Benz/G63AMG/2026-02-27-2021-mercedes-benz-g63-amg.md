# 2021 Mercedes-Benz G63 AMG Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

Shopping for a 2021 Mercedes-Benz G63 AMG means investing in an iconic, high-performance luxury SUV known for its hand-built biturbo V8 and unmistakable road presence. While owner reports to federal safety regulators are relatively limited for this model year, prospective buyers should still carefully evaluate complex electronics, powertrain behavior, and braking systems during a pre-purchase inspection.

---

## Engine

### 4.0L Biturbo V8 Engine

This hand-built 4.0-liter twin-turbocharged V8 produces 577 horsepower and 627 lb-ft of torque, delivering blistering acceleration for a heavy luxury SUV. It is generally robust, though proper maintenance and premium fuel are essential for long-term health.

## Transmission

### AMG Speedshift TCT 9-Speed Automatic

The 9-speed automatic transmission handles high torque outputs with rapid gear changes and smooth power delivery across various driving modes. Regular fluid changes will help ensure dependable performance over time.

### Engine Electrical and Powertrain Management

The complex electrical architecture manages numerous systems, though occasional owner reports point to unexpected powertrain shutdowns.

**Unexpected Engine Shutdown**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle may suddenly stall or shut down while idling at a traffic light or pulling away at low speeds, accompanied by a loss of throttle and dashboard safety warnings.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified technician scan the vehicle computer for stored diagnostic trouble codes and check electrical connections, battery health, and software calibration.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

The heavy-duty braking system provides strong stopping power, but electronic control issues warrant professional verification.

**Brake Lockup**
- **Symptoms:** Brakes may unexpectedly lock up during operation, potentially creating hazardous driving conditions.
- **Solution:** Inspect the electronic brake control module, brake actuators, and hydraulic lines for faults or software glitches.
