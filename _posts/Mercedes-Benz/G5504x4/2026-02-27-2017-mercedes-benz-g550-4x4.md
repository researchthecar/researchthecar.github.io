# 2017 Mercedes-Benz G550 4x4 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a 2017 Mercedes-Benz G550 4x4², buyers are looking at an ultra-capable, heavy-duty luxury icon built for serious off-road duty. Because owner-reported data for this low-volume, specialized variant is relatively sparse, prospective purchasers should carefully inspect electronic stability systems, sensor arrays, and brake modules during pre-purchase evaluations.

---

## Engine

### 4.0L Biturbo V8

This 4.0-liter twin-turbocharged V8 engine produces approximately 416 horsepower and 450 lb-ft of torque. It provides robust acceleration for this heavy machine, though routine maintenance costs remain high.

## Transmission

### 7-Speed Automatic Transmission

Paired with a dedicated four-wheel-drive system and portal axles, this 7-speed automatic gearbox handles heavy torque loads. Regular fluid changes are critical for maintaining long-term reliability.

## Brakes

### Anti-lock Brake and Stability System

Heavy-duty braking systems require close inspection of electronic modules and sensors to ensure proper dynamic stability.

**ABS and Electronic Stability Control Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers report unexpected warnings or complete failures involving the anti-lock braking and electronic stability control systems.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified technician scan the vehicle for diagnostic trouble codes and inspect the ABS control modules and wheel speed sensors.

## Electrical

### Backup Camera and Parking Sensors

Advanced driver assistance and safety electronics should be fully tested during a pre-purchase inspection.

**Intermittent Rear Camera Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The backup camera and parking sensors may experience intermittent operational failures, potentially leading to visibility issues while reversing.
- **Solution:** Inspect the camera wiring harness, connectors, and software configuration, replacing faulty sensor units if necessary.

### Traction Control and Stability Modules

Complex vehicle electronics govern stability and drivetrain behavior, making module health vital for safe operation.

**Traction Control Software Errors**
- **Symptoms:** Software or module errors can trigger dashboard warning lights and disable core traction management systems.
- **Solution:** Consult an authorized service center to perform necessary software updates or replace defective control modules.
