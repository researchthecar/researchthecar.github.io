# 2005 Mercedes-Benz O400

### Overview
The 2005 Mercedes-Benz O400, a robust platform often utilized for bus and coach applications, generally exhibits strong reliability, reflecting Mercedes-Benz's engineering standards. While specific engine and transmission variants exist, the overall design prioritizes durability for commercial use. Common areas of attention for vehicles of this age and type include the powertrain, suspension, and electrical systems. However, widespread critical failures are not a common complaint, with most issues stemming from the demands of high mileage and operational stress rather than inherent design flaws. Regular maintenance is crucial for optimal performance and longevity.

---


## Engine

### OM 457 LA (12.0L Inline-6 Diesel)
**Turbocharger Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of power, increased exhaust smoke, audible whistling noise.
- **Solution:** Turbocharger rebuild or replacement.
- **Cost:** $1,500 - $3,000

**Coolant Leaks (Hoses/Gaskets)**
- **Symptoms:** Visible coolant puddles, engine overheating, sweet smell.
- **Solution:** Replacement of damaged hoses, gaskets, or seals.
- **Cost:** $300 - $700


### OM 906 LA (6.4L Inline-6 Diesel)
**Injector Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Rough idling, reduced fuel economy, engine misfires, black smoke.
- **Solution:** Cleaning or replacement of fuel injectors.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1,600

**EGR System Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** Check engine light, reduced engine power, increased emissions.
- **Solution:** Cleaning or replacement of EGR valve and associated components.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1,200


## Transmission

### ZF Ecolife (Automatic)
**Torque Converter Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Slipping gears, shuddering during acceleration, delayed engagement.
- **Solution:** Torque converter replacement.
- **Cost:** $1,200 - $2,500


### Mercedes-Benz GO 190 (Manual)
**Clutch Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Slipping clutch, difficulty shifting gears, burning smell.
- **Solution:** Clutch kit replacement (clutch disc, pressure plate, release bearing).
- **Cost:** $800 - $1,500


## Suspension

### Air Suspension System
**Air Spring Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle sagging on one side, uneven ride height, compressor running excessively.
- **Solution:** Replacement of leaking air springs or seals.
- **Cost:** $400 - $900 per spring

**Compressor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Inability to raise or lower the vehicle, constant compressor running.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the air compressor unit.
- **Cost:** $600 - $1,200


## Electrical

### Wiring Harness Degradation
**Corrosion and Shorts**
- **Symptoms:** Intermittent electrical faults, non-functioning lights or accessories, warning lights.
- **Solution:** Inspection, repair, or replacement of damaged wiring sections.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1,500+


### Alternator Issues
**Reduced Output/Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Battery warning light, dimming lights, electrical system malfunctions.
- **Solution:** Alternator testing and replacement if necessary.
- **Cost:** $400 - $800


## Exterior

### Body Panels and Seals
**Corrosion**
- **Symptoms:** Rust formation on body panels, especially around wheel arches and lower sections.
- **Solution:** Rust treatment and panel repair/replacement.
- **Cost:** $300 - $1,000+

**Door Seal Deterioration**
- **Symptoms:** Water ingress, drafts, increased cabin noise.
- **Solution:** Replacement of door seals.
- **Cost:** $100 - $300 per door


## Interior

### HVAC System
**Blower Motor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** No airflow from vents, or intermittent airflow.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the blower motor.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600

**Refrigerant Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Poor or no cooling from the air conditioning system.
- **Solution:** Leak detection and repair, followed by refrigerant recharge.
- **Cost:** $400 - $900


### Seating and Upholstery
**Wear and Tear**
- **Symptoms:** Torn upholstery, worn seat padding, broken adjustment mechanisms.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of seat components or upholstery.
- **Cost:** $200 - $700 per seat


--- 
### Summary Verdict
The 2005 Mercedes-Benz O400 is a generally reliable vehicle, particularly for its intended commercial applications. The most common issues tend to be related to high mileage wear on components like turbochargers, injectors, and suspension parts, especially air springs and compressors. Electrical system integrity, particularly wiring harnesses, can degrade over time and require attention. Interior components like HVAC and seating will show signs of wear consistent with heavy use. Overall, with diligent maintenance and timely repairs, the O400 can remain a dependable asset.
