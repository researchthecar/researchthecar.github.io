# 2020 Mercedes-Benz GLE580 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2020 Mercedes-Benz GLE580 delivers high-end luxury and robust performance, but potential buyers should approach used models with eyes wide open regarding complex electrical and powertrain systems. While owners praise its impressive power and comfort, a pattern of owner-reported issues highlights potential weak points in engine reliability, electrical accessories, and suspension components that can lead to expensive repair bills.

---

## Engine

### 4.0L Biturbo V8 Engine

The 4.0L Biturbo V8 engine generates roughly 483 horsepower and 516 lb-ft of torque, offering exceptionally swift acceleration and effortless highway passing. However, owners have reported severe internal engine failures and misfires that require careful pre-purchase inspection.

**Engine Misfire and Loss of Motive Power**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers experience sudden engine misfires, loud metallic knocking sounds during acceleration, and potential catastrophic internal engine failure without prior warning lights.
- **Solution:** Requires professional diagnostic evaluation of the cylinder heads and internal components, potentially leading to major mechanical repairs or engine replacement.

**Engine Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle unexpectedly stalls while driving, posing safety risks on roadways and highways.
- **Solution:** Inspect fuel, electrical, and coolant systems to diagnose the root cause of the stall.

## Transmission

### 9G-TRONIC 9-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 9-speed automatic transmission comes paired with the standard all-wheel-drive (4MATIC) system, delivering smooth gear changes during normal driving. While generally refined, specific reports indicate potential electronic or valve body glitches.

**Shifting to Neutral / Control Module Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The transmission unexpectedly shifts into neutral when placed into drive or reverse, or the transmission control module fails entirely.
- **Solution:** Diagnostic scanning of the transmission control module and valve body; replacement of faulty modules or internal components may be necessary.

## Suspension

### Rear Shock Absorbers

The advanced suspension systems provide a plush ride quality, though individual owner reports note premature wear and fluid leaks in the rear shock absorbers.

**Suspension Rattle and Fluid Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Clunking or rattling noises when driving over road bumps, accompanied by visible fluid leaks from the rear shocks.
- **Solution:** Replace the failed rear shock absorbers and inspect related sealing components.

## Electrical

### Infotainment and Lighting Electronics

The vehicle relies heavily on complex digital displays and electronic control modules, which can occasionally experience glitches or complete system failures.

**Infotainment and Headlamp Module Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of primary multimedia screens, backup camera failure, climate control lockout, and exterior headlamp/turn signal module errors.
- **Solution:** Update or replace faulty control modules and headlamp assemblies.

## Safety

### Air Bag and Seat Belt Sensors

Safety occupant detection and restraint systems generally protect passengers well, but sensor malfunctions and physical belt mechanism failures have been documented by owners.

**Air Bag Warning and Faulty Seat Belts**
- **Symptoms:** Illuminated airbag warning lights due to passenger weight sensor issues, or rear seat belts locking up and failing to retract properly.
- **Solution:** Inspect, recalibrate, or replace passenger seat weight sensors and malfunctioning seat belt assemblies.
- **Cost:** $1,000 out-of-pocket reported for seat belt repair

---

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V671000: Seat Belts: Rear/Other:Buckle Assembly](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=GLE580&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** While buckling the seat belt, the middle seat belt buckle could be pushed down into the gap below the seat cushion. An unrestrained occupant has an increased risk of injury in the event of a crash.
- **What to do:** MBUSA will notify owners, and dealers will secure the middle seat belt buckle, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

Purchasing a used 2020 Mercedes-Benz GLE580 requires caution due to potential high-cost repair risks involving the V8 engine, transmission control modules, and sophisticated onboard electronics. Buyers should insist on a comprehensive pre-purchase inspection by a qualified mechanic, verify complete service records, and strongly consider an extended warranty to mitigate unexpected repair expenses.
