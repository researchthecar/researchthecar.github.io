# 2021 Mercedes-Benz A45 AMG S Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a 2021 Mercedes-Benz A45 AMG S, buyers are looking at a high-performance luxury hatchback known for its potent turbocharged engine and engaging driving dynamics. While owner-reported complaints for this specific performance trim are relatively scarce in broader filing databases, used car shoppers should carefully evaluate the vehicle's complex electronics, powertrain components, and advanced safety features to ensure a trouble-free ownership experience.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4 (M139)

The 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine (M139) pushes an impressive 416 horsepower and 369 lb-ft of torque, making it one of the most powerful production four-cylinder engines available. Buyers should expect exhilarating performance paired with the maintenance demands typical of a highly stressed, high-boost luxury turbo engine.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Dual-Clutch Automatic (AMG SPEEDSHIFT DCT)

The 8-speed dual-clutch automatic transmission delivers rapid-fire gear changes and distributes power efficiently through the performance-tuned all-wheel-drive system. Routine fluid changes and software health are vital for maintaining smooth operation during spirited driving.

## Electrical

### Electrical System and Backup Camera

The A45 AMG S relies heavily on advanced electronics, infotainment architectures, and driver-assistance modules that can occasionally present software-related glitches.

**Intermittent Rearview Camera Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may put the vehicle in reverse only to find the backup camera screen is completely blank or displays an inoperative message without warning.
- **Solution:** Dealers may need to update software or inspect camera module connections, though owners note that specific software remedy availability can vary.

## Safety

### Driver Assistance and Restraints

Advanced safety systems and active driver aids offer high-tech protection but can occasionally exhibit calibration sensitivity or hardware anomalies.

**Phantom Braking Events**
- **Symptoms:** While using cruise control or driving assistance features, the vehicle may suddenly and unexpectedly decelerate when passing offramps or alongside specific roadway signs.
- **Solution:** Have a dealer check for advanced driver-assistance system calibration updates or sensor cleanliness.

**Seatbelt Latch Engagement Failure**
- **Symptoms:** In sudden stop or collision scenarios, the driver's seatbelt may fail to properly secure, potentially increasing the risk of occupant movement.
- **Solution:** Inspect seatbelt buckle assemblies for physical wear or mechanical binding, and replace components if latching is compromised.
