**Introduction**

The 1995 Mercedes-Benz S320 is a luxury sedan powered by a 3.2-liter V6 engine that produces 228 horsepower and 232 lb-ft of torque. It is paired with a standard 4-speed automatic transmission. The S320 is praised for its comfortable ride, luxurious interior, and powerful engine. However, it is criticized for its high price and some reliability issues.

**Engine**

* **M104 3.2L V6 Engine**
    * **Intake Manifold Vacuum Leak**
        * Description/Symptoms: Rough idle, loss of power, increased fuel consumption
        * Solution: Replace intake manifold gaskets
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
        * Recalls: None Found
    * **Crankshaft Position Sensor Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: Engine stalling, no-start condition
        * Solution: Replace crankshaft position sensor
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

* **722.4 4-Speed Automatic Transmission**
    * **Shift Solenoid Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: Transmission slipping, hard shifting, no shifting
        * Solution: Replace shift solenoid
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Front Control Arm Bushing Wear**
    * Description/Symptoms: Noises, vibrations from front suspension, uneven tire wear
    * Solution: Replace control arm bushings
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Rear Shock Absorber Leakage**
    * Description/Symptoms: Decreased ride quality, excessive bouncing
    * Solution: Replace shock absorbers
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $300+ per shock
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Alternator Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Dimming lights, electrical components not working properly
    * Solution: Replace alternator
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $600+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Starter Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Engine not cranking
    * Solution: Replace starter
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Sunroof Drain Plugs Clogging**
    * Description/Symptoms: Water leaking into interior
    * Solution: Clear clogged drain plugs
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Radio Display Flickering**
    * Description/Symptoms: Radio display becomes unreadable
    * Solution: Replace radio display unit
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Climate Control Malfunction**
    * Description/Symptoms: Heating or cooling not working properly
    * Solution: Repair or replace climate control module
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 1995 Mercedes-Benz S320 is a reliable luxury sedan, but it is not without its issues. The most severe and expensive issues are related to the alternator, starter, and transmission. The average cost to fix these issues can range from $200 to $1,000+, depending on the specific part and labor costs. Other issues, such as electrical gremlins and interior problems, are less severe and typically less expensive to fix. Overall, the 1995 Mercedes-Benz S320 is a solid choice for those looking for a comfortable and well-built luxury vehicle, but they should be aware of the potential issues that may arise.
