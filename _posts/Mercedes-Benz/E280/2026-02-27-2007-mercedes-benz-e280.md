# 2007 Mercedes-Benz E280

### Overview
The 2007 Mercedes-Benz E280, part of the W211 generation, is generally considered a reliable luxury sedan, offering a blend of comfort, performance, and advanced features for its time. While it shares some common Mercedes-Benz characteristics, specific model year and engine/transmission combinations can influence its long-term dependability. This analysis focuses on documented technical reliability concerns beyond routine maintenance, aiming to provide a clear picture for potential owners or those maintaining the vehicle. Owners can expect a solid ownership experience, provided regular servicing is adhered to and any emerging issues are addressed promptly.

---


## Engine

### 3.0L V6 (M272)
**Intake Manifold Runner Flap Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Check engine light, rough idle, loss of power, rattling noise from intake.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the intake manifold assembly or repair kit.
- **Cost:** $600 - $1200

**Balance Shaft Gear Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Check engine light with specific codes (e.g., P0016, P0017), timing issues.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the balance shaft and associated timing components.
- **Cost:** $1500 - $3000


## Transmission

### 7G-TRONIC (722.9)
**Valve Body Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Harsh shifting, delayed engagement, transmission warning light, limp mode.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of the valve body assembly.
- **Cost:** $1000 - $2500

**Torque Converter Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Shuddering at low speeds, slipping, unusual noises.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the torque converter.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1800


## Suspension

### Standard Chassis
**Air Suspension Leaks (if equipped)**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle sagging on one side, compressor running excessively, uneven ride height.
- **Solution:** Replacement of air springs, lines, or compressor.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1500 per corner

### Summary Verdict
The 2007 Mercedes-Benz E280 (W211) presents a generally good reliability record, with the most notable concerns centering around the 3.0L V6 engine's intake manifold and balance shaft, and the 7G-TRONIC transmission's valve body. Electrical issues, particularly with the SAM and EIS modules, can also arise. While suspension components are generally robust, air suspension systems (if equipped) can be a source of costly repairs. Owners should be prepared for potential expenses related to these specific components, but otherwise, the E280 offers a dependable luxury driving experience when properly maintained.
