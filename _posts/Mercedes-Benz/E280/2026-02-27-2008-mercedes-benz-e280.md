# 2008 Mercedes-Benz E280

### Overview
The 2008 Mercedes-Benz E280, part of the W211 generation, is generally regarded as a solid luxury sedan with a focus on comfort and performance. While Mercedes-Benz vehicles often come with a reputation for sophisticated engineering, they can also present specific reliability concerns as they age. This analysis focuses on common technical issues reported by owners and technicians, excluding routine maintenance and normal wear and tear. Understanding these potential problem areas can help owners budget for repairs and ensure the longevity of their E280.

---


## Engine

### 3.0L V6 (M272)
**Balance Shaft Gear Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Check engine light, rough idle, misfires, reduced engine power.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the balance shaft gear and associated components. This is a labor-intensive repair.
- **Cost:** $2,500 - $4,500

**Intake Manifold Flap Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Check engine light, poor engine performance, increased fuel consumption.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the intake manifold assembly.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1,500


### 3.0L V6 Diesel (OM642)
**Glow Plug Module Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Difficulty starting in cold weather, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the glow plug control module.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600

**Turbocharger Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of power, excessive smoke, whistling noises.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of the turbocharger unit.
- **Cost:** $1,500 - $3,000


## Transmission

### 722.6 (5-Speed Automatic)
**Conductor Plate Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Harsh shifting, transmission slipping, 'limp mode', speedometer issues.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the transmission conductor plate (also known as the electronic shifter module).
- **Cost:** $700 - $1,200

**Torque Converter Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Shuddering at low speeds, slipping, delayed engagement.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the torque converter.
- **Cost:** $1,000 - $2,000


## Suspension

### Standard Chassis
**Air Suspension (Optional)**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle sagging, uneven ride height, compressor failure, air leaks.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of air springs, compressor, or lines.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1,500 per corner/component

**Control Arm Bushings**
- **Symptoms:** Clunking noises over bumps, vague steering, uneven tire wear.
- **Solution:** Replacement of worn control arm bushings or the entire control arm assembly.
- **Cost:** $400 - $800


## Electrical

### General Electrical System
**SAM Unit (Signal Acquisition Module) Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Various warning lights, malfunctioning lights (headlights, taillights), power window problems, central locking issues.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and repair or replacement of the SAM unit. Can be complex and costly.
- **Cost:** $800 - $2,000

**Window Regulator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Power windows not working, window getting stuck.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the window regulator mechanism.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600 per window

### Summary Verdict
The 2008 Mercedes-Benz E280 offers a blend of luxury and performance, but like many German luxury vehicles of this era, it can be susceptible to specific, often costly, repairs as it ages. Key areas to monitor include the engine's balance shaft gear and intake manifold (M272 engine), transmission conductor plate, and potential electrical gremlins related to the SAM unit. Optional air suspension systems can also be a source of expensive repairs. While the exterior and interior generally hold up well, proactive maintenance and awareness of these common issues are crucial for maintaining reliability and managing ownership costs for this E-Class model.
