# 2019 Mercedes-Benz GLS550 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2019 Mercedes-Benz GLS550 offers a luxurious and powerful driving experience, blending high-end features with a spacious cabin. While owners generally praise its performance and comfort, prospective used-car buyers should review available owner feedback regarding occasional powertrain responsiveness and electrical or suspension warnings. Keeping up with routine maintenance and pre-purchase inspections can help ensure a smooth ownership experience.

---

## Engine

### 4.7L Twin-Turbo V8 Engine

The 4.7-liter twin-turbocharged V8 engine delivers approximately 449 horsepower and 516 lb-ft of torque, offering robust acceleration for this large luxury SUV. While sophisticated and powerful, prospective buyers should ensure meticulous maintenance history to avoid potential oil leak or solenoid issues.

## Transmission

### 9G-TRONIC 9-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 9-speed automatic transmission provides smooth gear changes and optimizes highway efficiency across all driving conditions. Overall reliability is solid, though proper fluid maintenance is key to long-term durability.

### Gear Selector and Shift Control

Electrical accessories and comfort features are extensive, making a thorough battery and diagnostic check essential during a pre-purchase inspection.

**Failure to Shift from Park**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may occasionally fail to respond when attempting to shift out of park into drive, reverse, or neutral.
- **Solution:** Inspect the electronic shifter module, brake pedal position switch, and related control software for faults.

## Electrical

### Hood Latch Sensor

Exterior body panels and glass require careful examination, particularly for sunroof and latch sensors reported by some drivers.

**False Hood Open Warnings**
- **Symptoms:** The dashboard may repeatedly display a warning message indicating that the front hood is open despite being securely locked.
- **Solution:** Inspect and replace the hood latch sensor or switch assembly as needed.

## Interior

### Active Curve System

Steering and active stabilization components generally offer responsive handling, but electronic and hydraulic warnings require prompt professional evaluation.

**Active Curve System Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have reported dashboard warnings and potential fluid leaks related to the Active Curve System shortly after operation or service.
- **Solution:** Have the hydraulic lines, fluid levels, and system sensors inspected and repaired by a qualified technician.

## Summary Verdict

The 2019 Mercedes-Benz GLS550 remains a commanding and luxurious choice for buyers seeking high performance and ample space. While most owners enjoy trouble-free luxury motoring, a comprehensive pre-purchase inspection focusing on the complex electronics, hydraulic suspension systems, and powertrain operation is strongly advised.
