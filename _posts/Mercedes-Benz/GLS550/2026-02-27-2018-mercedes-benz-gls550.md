# 2018 Mercedes-Benz GLS550 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2018 Mercedes-Benz GLS550 delivers luxurious hauling capability and robust twin-turbo V8 performance, but used-car buyers should approach this model with realistic expectations regarding its complex systems. While owner reports reflect praise for its comfort and power, a number of recurring complaints highlight potential trouble spots—particularly with air suspension components and braking hardware. As with many high-end luxury European SUVs, thorough pre-purchase inspections and a healthy maintenance budget are strongly recommended.

---

## Engine

### 4.7L Biturbo V8 Engine

Powered by a 4.7-liter twin-turbocharged V8 engine producing approximately 449 horsepower and 516 lb-ft of torque, this powerhouse provides swift acceleration and impressive towing capability. Overall, the engine core itself is generally durable, though proper maintenance is critical to avoid costly upkeep.

## Transmission

### 9-Speed Automatic Transmission

Equipped with a 9-speed automatic transmission paired with standard 4MATIC all-wheel drive, it delivers smooth and seamless power delivery across highway speeds. Routine fluid changes are essential to maintain shift quality and longevity over high mileage.

## Suspension

### AIRMATIC Air Suspension

The adaptive air suspension provides an ultra-smooth ride, but air springs and compressors are known wear items that can require expensive repairs as the vehicle ages.

**Air Suspension Failure and Compressor Overheat**
- **Symptoms:** Owners report compressor cooling warnings, malfunction messages, and sudden sagging, leading to an extremely bumpy and unsafe ride quality.
- **Solution:** Requires diagnosis of the air struts, valve block, or replacement of the air compressor.

**Leaking Rear Shock Absorbers**
- **Symptoms:** A squeaking or abnormal noise from the rear suspension accompanied by fluid leaks from the shock absorbers.
- **Solution:** Replace the leaking shock absorbers and inspect related bushings.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

Braking performance is strong when functioning properly, but owners have reported premature wear, pedal feel concerns, and occasional caliper binding.

**Brake Pedal Sponginess and Sticking Calipers**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have noted spongy brake pedal feel, abnormal noises when applying the brakes, and instances of front brake calipers sticking or seizing.
- **Solution:** Inspect brake pads, rotors, and calipers; bleed the brake system or replace stuck calipers.

## Electrical

### Third-Row Seating

The cabin is spacious and appointment-rich, though motorized third-row seat mechanisms can occasionally develop locking and sensor glitches.

**Third-Row Seat Latch Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** The third-row seats fail to lock securely into position, accompanied by persistent warning messages on the instrument cluster.
- **Solution:** Inspect and repair or replace the seat latch mechanism sensors and locking hardware.

## Exterior

### Panoramic Sunroof

The exterior styling is timeless and robust, though the large panoramic glass roof has been cited by some owners for rare spontaneous breakage.

**Spontaneous Sunroof Shattering**
- **Symptoms:** The stationary glass panel of the panoramic sunroof can crack or shatter spontaneously while driving or parked, producing a loud pop resembling a gunshot.
- **Solution:** Replace the damaged glass panel and clear out any remaining glass shards from the mechanism.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V539000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=GLS550&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** The rear brake caliper pistons on these vehicles may not have been correctly coated, causing gas to become temporarily released into the rear brake hydraulic circuit, reducing braking performance. Reduced braking performance can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** MBUSA will notify owners, and dealers will bleed the rear axle brake system, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V816000: Latches/Locks/Linkages:Doors:Latch](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=GLS550&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** The rear passenger door on the driver side may indicate that it is locked, when it may not be. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 206, "Door Locks and Door Retention Components." If the door appears to be locked, the door may be unintentionally opened, increasing the risk of.
- **What to do:** MBUSA will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the left rear door lock, replacing it as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.
