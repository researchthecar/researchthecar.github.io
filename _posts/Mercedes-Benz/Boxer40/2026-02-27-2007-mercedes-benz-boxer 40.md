# 2007 Mercedes-Benz Boxer 40

### Overview
The 2007 Mercedes-Benz Boxer 40, a robust commercial vehicle, generally exhibits strong reliability, a hallmark of its German engineering. Designed for demanding vocational use, it prioritizes durability and longevity. While specific model years can have unique quirks, the 2007 iteration benefits from Mercedes-Benz's extensive experience in the light commercial vehicle sector. Owners can expect a dependable workhorse, provided regular maintenance is adhered to. This analysis focuses on documented common issues beyond routine wear and tear, offering insights into potential areas of concern for prospective buyers or current owners.

---


## Engine

### 2.2L CDI Diesel
**Turbocharger Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of power, whistling noises, black smoke from exhaust.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the turbocharger unit.
- **Cost:** $1200 - $2500

**EGR Valve Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Check engine light, rough idling, reduced engine performance, increased emissions.
- **Solution:** Cleaning or replacement of the EGR valve.
- **Cost:** $300 - $700


### 2.7L CDI Diesel
**Glow Plug Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Difficulty starting in cold weather, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of faulty glow plugs.
- **Cost:** $200 - $500


## Transmission

### 5-Speed Manual
**Clutch Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Slipping clutch, difficulty shifting gears, burning smell.
- **Solution:** Replacement of clutch kit (clutch disc, pressure plate, release bearing).
- **Cost:** $800 - $1500


### 5-Speed Automatic
**Transmission Fluid Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Leaking fluid, rough shifting, overheating.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of seals and gaskets, fluid flush.
- **Cost:** $400 - $900


## Suspension

### Standard Chassis
**Worn Ball Joints**
- **Symptoms:** Clunking noises over bumps, uneven tire wear, steering wander.
- **Solution:** Replacement of worn ball joints.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600

**Leaf Spring Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Sagging rear end, excessive bouncing, cracking noises.
- **Solution:** Replacement of damaged or fatigued leaf springs.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1000


## Electrical

### Wiring Harness
**Corrosion**
- **Symptoms:** Intermittent electrical faults, non-functioning lights or accessories, warning lights.
- **Solution:** Cleaning and sealing of corroded connections, repair or replacement of damaged wiring.
- **Cost:** $200 - $600


### Alternator
**Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Battery warning light, dimming lights, dead battery.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the alternator.
- **Cost:** $500 - $900


## Exterior

### Body Panels
**Rust**
- **Symptoms:** Visible rust on wheel arches, door sills, and underbody.
- **Solution:** Rust treatment and repair, panel replacement if severe.
- **Cost:** $300 - $1500+


### Door Latches
**Sticking/Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Doors difficult to open or close, central locking issues.
- **Solution:** Lubrication or replacement of door latch mechanisms.
- **Cost:** $150 - $400


## Interior

### HVAC System
**Blower Motor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** No airflow from vents, or intermittent airflow.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the blower motor resistor or motor.
- **Cost:** $200 - $500


### Seat Upholstery
**Wear and Tear**
- **Symptoms:** Rips, tears, and thinning of fabric or vinyl, especially on driver's seat.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of upholstery.
- **Cost:** $100 - $400


--- 
### Summary Verdict
The 2007 Mercedes-Benz Boxer 40 is a generally reliable commercial vehicle, particularly for its intended purpose. The diesel engines, while robust, can experience issues like turbocharger problems or EGR valve malfunctions, which are not uncommon for this type of powertrain. Transmission reliability is decent, with clutch wear being the primary concern for manual units and fluid leaks for automatics. Suspension components, especially ball joints and leaf springs, may require attention due to the vehicle's load-carrying nature. Electrical gremlins, often related to wiring harness corrosion, and exterior rust are potential areas to inspect. Interior wear is typical for a work vehicle. Overall, with diligent maintenance and addressing specific known issues, the Boxer 40 can remain a dependable asset.
