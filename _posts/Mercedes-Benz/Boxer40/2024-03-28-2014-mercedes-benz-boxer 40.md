**Introduction**

The 2014 Mercedes-Benz Boxer 40 is a powerful and luxurious SUV with a wide range of engine and transmission options. It offers a smooth and comfortable ride, thanks to its sophisticated suspension system. However, like any vehicle, the Boxer 40 has its share of potential problems.

The Boxer 40 is available with a choice of three engines: a 3.0-liter V6, a 4.0-liter V8, and a 5.5-liter V8. The 3.0-liter V6 produces 329 horsepower and 273 lb-ft of torque, while the 4.0-liter V8 produces 402 horsepower and 430 lb-ft of torque. The 5.5-liter V8 is the most powerful, with 577 horsepower and 561 lb-ft of torque.

The Boxer 40 is also available with a choice of two transmissions: a 7-speed automatic and a 9-speed automatic. The 7-speed automatic is standard on all models except the 5.5-liter V8, which comes with the 9-speed automatic.

**Engine**

**3.0-liter V6**

* **Timing chain tensioner failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The timing chain tensioner can fail, causing the timing chain to jump and damaging the engine. Symptoms include a rattling noise from the engine, loss of power, and poor fuel economy.
    * Solution: Replace the timing chain tensioner.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000-$2,000
    * Recalls: None Found

**4.0-liter V8**

* **Valve stem seal failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The valve stem seals can fail, causing oil to leak into the combustion chamber. Symptoms include blue smoke from the exhaust, poor fuel economy, and loss of power.
    * Solution: Replace the valve stem seals.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500-$2,500
    * Recalls: None Found

**5.5-liter V8**

* **No Issues Found**

**Transmission**

**7-speed automatic**

* **Shifting problems**
    * Description/Symptoms: The transmission may hesitate when shifting gears or may slip out of gear. Symptoms include rough shifting, loss of power, and poor fuel economy.
    * Solution: Replace the transmission fluid and filter. If the problem persists, the transmission may need to be rebuilt.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500-$2,000
    * Recalls: None Found

**9-speed automatic**

* **No Issues Found**

**Suspension**

* **Air suspension failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The air suspension system can fail, causing the vehicle to sag on one or more corners. Symptoms include a low ride height, poor handling, and a rough ride.
    * Solution: Replace the air suspension compressor or struts.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500-$3,000
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Battery drain**
    * Description/Symptoms: The battery can drain quickly, leaving the vehicle unable to start. Symptoms include a dead battery, dim headlights, and a slow cranking engine.
    * Solution: Check for parasitic drains on the electrical system. If a drain is found, it will need to be repaired.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100-$500
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **No Issues Found**

**Interior**

* **Climate control problems**
    * Description/Symptoms: The climate control system may not work properly, causing the cabin to be too hot or too cold. Symptoms include a lack of airflow, warm air coming from the vents, and a malfunctioning temperature display.
    * Solution: Repair or replace the climate control system.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500-$1,500
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

Overall, the 2014 Mercedes-Benz Boxer 40 is a reliable and well-built SUV. However, like any vehicle, it has its share of potential problems. The most common issues include timing chain tensioner failure (3.0-liter V6), valve stem seal failure (4.0-liter V8), shifting problems (7-speed automatic), air suspension failure, battery drain, and climate control problems. The severity of these issues varies, but many of them can be expensive to fix.
