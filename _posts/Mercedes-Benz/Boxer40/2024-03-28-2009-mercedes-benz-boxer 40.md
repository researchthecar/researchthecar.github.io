**Suspension**

* **Rear Air Suspension Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The rear air suspension system may fail, causing the rear of the vehicle to sag. This issue can be caused by a faulty air compressor or a leak in the air lines.
    * Solution: Replace the faulty air compressor or repair the leak in the air lines.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Electrical System Malfunctions**
    * Description/Symptoms: The boxer 40's electrical system may experience malfunctions, such as power window failures, power seat failures, and dashboard warning lights illuminating.
    * Solution: Diagnose and repair the specific electrical component that is causing the issue.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Rust on Undercarriage**
    * Description/Symptoms: The undercarriage of the boxer 40 may develop rust due to exposure to water and salt. This can lead to premature failure of suspension components.
    * Solution: Apply rust-proofing treatment to the undercarriage.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Air Conditioning Blower Motor Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The climate control system's air conditioning blower motor may fail, resulting in no air being blown into the cabin.
    * Solution: Replace the faulty air conditioning blower motor.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

While the 2009 Mercedes-Benz Boxer 40 is a capable and luxurious SUV, it has not been without its fair share of issues. The most severe problems include the rear air suspension failure, electrical system malfunctions, and rust on the undercarriage. The estimated cost to fix these issues can range from a few hundred dollars to over $2,000, depending on the severity of the problem.
