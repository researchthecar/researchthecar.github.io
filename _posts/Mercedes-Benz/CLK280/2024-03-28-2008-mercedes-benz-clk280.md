**Introduction**

The 2008 Mercedes-Benz CLK280 is a luxury coupe powered by a 3.0-liter V6 engine that produces 230 horsepower and 229 lb-ft of torque. It is paired with a standard 7-speed automatic transmission. The CLK280 is known for its stylish design, comfortable interior, and responsive handling. However, some owners have reported various issues with the vehicle, which are detailed below:

**Engine**

* **3.0L V6**
    * **Valve stem seal failure:** Oil leaks and excessive oil consumption, especially at high mileage.
    * **Solution:** Replace the valve stem seals.
    * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $1,500 - $2,500
    * **Recalls:** None Found

**Transmission**

* **7-Speed Automatic**
    * **Shifting problems:** Rough or delayed shifts, transmission slipping or refusing to engage gears.
    * **Solution:** Repair or replace the transmission components, such as solenoids, sensors, or clutches.
    * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $2,000 - $5,000+
    * **Recalls:** None Found

**Suspension**

* **Front suspension components:** Worn or damaged ball joints, control arms, or bushings.
    * **Description/Symptoms:** Unusual noises, vibrations, or steering instability while driving.
    * **Solution:** Replace the affected components.
    * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $500 - $1,500
    * **Recalls:** None Found

**Electrical**

* **Alternator failure:** Loss of electrical power, battery discharge, and warning lights.
    * **Solution:** Replace the alternator.
    * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $500 - $1,000
    * **Recalls:** None Found

**Exterior**

* **Sunroof leaks:** Water entering the cabin through the sunroof seals.
    * **Solution:** Replace or repair the sunroof seals.
    * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $200 - $500

**Interior**

* **Climate control system failure:** Malfunctioning temperature control, air leaks, or blower motor issues.
    * **Solution:** Repair or replace the affected components, such as the compressor, heater core, or blower motor.
    * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $500 - $1,500

**Overall Summary**

The 2008 Mercedes-Benz CLK280 is a reliable vehicle overall, but it has experienced some issues that owners should be aware of. The most significant and costly issues tend to be with the transmission and engine. It is important to have the vehicle regularly serviced and maintained to minimize the risk of experiencing these issues.
