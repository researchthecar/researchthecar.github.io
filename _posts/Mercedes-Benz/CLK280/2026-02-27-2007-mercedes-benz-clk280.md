# 2007 Mercedes-Benz CLK280

### Overview
The 2007 Mercedes-Benz CLK280, part of the W209 generation, is generally considered a reliable luxury coupe, offering a blend of performance and comfort. It is equipped with a capable V6 engine and a smooth automatic transmission. While most owners report a positive ownership experience, like any vehicle of this age, certain components may require attention. This analysis focuses on documented common issues beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential areas of concern for prospective buyers or current owners.

---


## Engine

### 3.0L M272 V6
**Variable Valve Timing (VVT) Actuator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Check engine light, rough idle, reduced engine power, rattling noise from the engine.
- **Solution:** Replacement of VVT actuators and potentially camshafts.
- **Cost:** $1,500 - $3,000

**Intake Manifold Flap Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Check engine light (P2004, P2005, P2006 codes), poor engine performance, rough idle.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the intake manifold.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1,500

## Electrical

### General Electrical System
**Window Regulator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Power windows not operating, slow window movement, grinding noise.
- **Solution:** Replacement of window regulator assembly.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600

**Headlight Ballast Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Xenon headlights not turning on or flickering.
- **Solution:** Replacement of headlight ballast.
- **Cost:** $400 - $800

### Summary Verdict
The 2007 Mercedes-Benz CLK280 is a solid choice for a luxury coupe, with its primary reliability concerns centering around the M272 V6 engine's variable valve timing system and intake manifold. These are known issues for Mercedes-Benz vehicles of this era and can be costly to repair. The transmission, suspension, and exterior/interior components generally hold up well, with only isolated incidents of component failure like window regulators or headlight ballasts. Regular maintenance and addressing engine warning lights promptly are key to ensuring long-term reliability.
