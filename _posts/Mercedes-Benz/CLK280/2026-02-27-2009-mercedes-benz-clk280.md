# 2009 Mercedes-Benz CLK280

### Overview
The 2009 Mercedes-Benz CLK280, part of the W209 generation, is generally considered a reliable luxury coupe, blending performance with comfort. It typically features a robust V6 engine and a smooth automatic transmission. While Mercedes-Benz vehicles are known for their sophisticated engineering, like any car, they can encounter specific issues over time. This analysis focuses on documented common problems beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential areas of concern for owners and prospective buyers of the CLK280. The focus is on components that have shown a tendency for premature failure or widespread complaints.

---


## Engine

### 3.0L M272 V6
**Intake Manifold Runner Flap Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Check Engine Light, rough idle, loss of power, P2004/P2005/P2006 codes.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the intake manifold assembly or repair kit.
- **Cost:** $600 - $1200

**Balance Shaft Gear Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Check Engine Light, P0016/P0017 codes (crankshaft/camshaft position correlation), rough running.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the balance shaft and associated timing components.
- **Cost:** $1500 - $3000+


## Transmission

### 722.6 5-Speed Automatic
**Conductor Plate Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Harsh shifting, transmission slipping, failure to shift gears, 'limp mode', ABS/ESP warning lights.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the conductor plate and transmission fluid service.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1000

**Torque Converter Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Shuddering at low speeds, slipping, delayed engagement.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the torque converter.
- **Cost:** $1000 - $2000

## Electrical

### General Electrical System
**SAM Unit (Signal Acquisition Module) Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** Intermittent electrical issues, non-functioning lights, power windows, or other accessories, CAN bus errors.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of the affected SAM module.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1500

**Window Regulator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Power windows not working, slow operation, or falling into the door.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the window regulator assembly.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600

### Summary Verdict
The 2009 Mercedes-Benz CLK280, equipped with the 3.0L M272 V6 engine and the 722.6 5-speed automatic transmission, presents a generally solid reliability record. However, potential owners should be aware of specific issues common to this powertrain, particularly concerning the intake manifold runner flaps and balance shaft gear wear on the engine, and conductor plate failures or torque converter problems on the transmission. Electrical gremlins, often related to the SAM unit or window regulators, can also arise. While suspension, exterior, and interior components are typically robust, proactive maintenance and addressing these known issues can ensure a more dependable ownership experience for this stylish German coupe.
