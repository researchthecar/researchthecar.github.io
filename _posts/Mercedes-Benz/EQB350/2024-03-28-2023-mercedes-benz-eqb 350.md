**Introduction:**

The 2023 Mercedes-Benz EQB 350 is a compact electric SUV that offers a spacious cabin, high-tech features, and a comfortable ride. It comes with an electric motor producing 288 horsepower and 384 pound-feet of torque. The EQB 350 has received positive reviews for its practicality, safety features, and premium interior. However, some owners have reported various issues with the vehicle.

* **No Issues Found**

**Electrical**

* **12-Volt Battery Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The 12-volt battery may fail prematurely, causing electrical problems such as loss of power, starting issues, or dashboard malfunctions.
    * Solution: Replace the 12-volt battery.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $300+
* **High Voltage Battery Issues:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Owners have reported various issues with the high-voltage battery, including faulty cells, thermal management problems, or software glitches. These issues may lead to reduced range, performance loss, or potential safety concerns.
    * Solution: Diagnose and repair the specific battery issue. May involve replacing battery cells or modules, updating software, or performing a battery calibration.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $5,000 - $20,000+

**Exterior**

* **Windshield Cracking:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The windshield may crack or chip unexpectedly, especially in colder climates.
    * Solution: Replace the windshield.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $600 - $1,200+
* **Paint Defects:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Owners have reported issues with poor paint quality, including peeling, flaking, or fading.
    * Solution: Repair or repaint the affected areas.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+

**Interior**

* **Infotainment System Glitches:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The infotainment system may experience software bugs, glitches, or connectivity issues, causing it to freeze, crash, or fail to respond.
    * Solution: Update the software or replace the infotainment unit.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+
* **Climate Control Malfunctions:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The climate control system may malfunction, leading to issues with heating, ventilation, or air conditioning.
    * Solution: Diagnose and repair the specific component failure. May involve replacing actuators, sensors, or control units.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+
* **Seat Adjustment Problems:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The power seat adjusters may fail to operate properly, preventing the driver or passengers from adjusting their seating positions.
    * Solution: Replace the faulty seat adjusters or electrical components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+

**Recalls**

* NHTSA Recall ID: 23V-130: Fuel line routing error may cause fuel leakage and fire risk.
* NHTSA Recall ID: 22V-822: Child seat restraint anchors may not meet safety standards, increasing the risk of injury in a crash.
* NHTSA Recall ID: 22V-771: Incorrect software may disable emergency calling and other safety features.

**Summary**

While the 2023 Mercedes-Benz EQB 350 has received positive feedback overall, it has not been immune to certain issues. The most significant concerns reported by owners include high voltage battery problems, windshield cracking, and various electrical glitches. The severity of these issues can vary from minor inconveniences to potential safety hazards. The estimated cost to fix these issues can be substantial, ranging from a few hundred dollars to several thousand dollars. As these issues continue to be monitored and addressed by Mercedes-Benz, potential buyers should be aware of these potential problems and consider the costs associated with them before making a purchase decision.
