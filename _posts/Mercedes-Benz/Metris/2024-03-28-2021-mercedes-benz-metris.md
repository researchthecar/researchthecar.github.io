**Introduction:**

The 2021 Mercedes-Benz Metris is a versatile cargo van that offers a spacious interior and impressive fuel efficiency. It's powered by a standard 2.0L turbocharged inline-4 engine paired with a 7-speed automatic transmission. The Metris is known for its comfort, reliability, and safety features but has received some reports of issues in certain areas.

**Engine**

* **2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4**
    * **Intake Manifold Leakage:** Symptoms include unusual engine noises, decreased performance, and increased oil consumption. Solution: Replacement of the intake manifold. Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1200+
    * **Turbocharger Failure:** Symptoms include reduced engine power, black smoke from exhaust, and excessive noise. Solution: Replacement of the turbocharger. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1500 - $2500+

**Transmission**

* **7-Speed Automatic Transmission**
    * **Shifting Delays and Hesitations:** Symptoms include slow or delayed gear changes, especially when shifting into higher gears. Solution: Software update or replacement of transmission components. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600+
    * **Fluid Leaks:** Symptoms include reduced fluid levels and visible fluid around the transmission housing. Solution: Inspection and repair of the transmission seal or gasket. Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $400+

**Suspension**

* **Front Suspension:**
    * **Strut Mount Failure:** Symptoms include excessive noise, vibration, and steering instability. Solution: Replacement of the strut mounts. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
* **Rear Suspension:**
    * **Leaf Spring Breakage:** Symptoms include a noticeable change in ride height, uneven tire wear, and possible scraping noises. Solution: Replacement of the leaf springs. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800+

**Electrical**

* **Battery Failure:** Symptoms include difficulty starting the vehicle, flickering lights, or a dead battery. Solution: Battery replacement. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $300+
* **Alternator Failure:** Symptoms include charging system issues, flickering lights, or a dead battery. Solution: Replacement of the alternator. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800+

**Exterior**

* **Tailgate Hinge Corrosion:** Symptoms include rust or corrosion on the tailgate hinges, making it difficult to open or close the tailgate. Solution: Replacement of the tailgate hinges. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
* **Rear Bumper Damage:** Symptoms include denting, scratches, or cracks on the rear bumper. Solution: Repair or replacement of the rear bumper. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1000+

**Interior**

* **Climate Control Issues:** Symptoms include malfunctioning climate control systems, such as failure to regulate temperature or adjust fan speed. Solution: Repair or replacement of climate control components. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600+
* **Navigation System Problems:** Symptoms include navigation system errors, map glitches, or failure to display information accurately. Solution: Software update or replacement of navigation components. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+

**Summary**

While the 2021 Mercedes-Benz Metris is generally well-regarded, it has received reports of some common issues. These issues range in severity from minor annoyances to more serious mechanical problems. Repair costs for these issues can vary from relatively inexpensive to more substantial. Overall, the majority of Metris owners report satisfaction with their vehicles and appreciate its spaciousness, fuel efficiency, and safety features.
