**2017 Mercedes-Benz Metris**

The 2017 Mercedes-Benz Metris is a versatile cargo van that offers impressive performance and reliability. It is powered by a 208-horsepower, 2.0-liter inline-four turbocharged engine paired with a seven-speed automatic transmission. While the Metris has received generally positive feedback for its comfort, safety, and cargo capacity, it has also been subject to a few reported issues.

**Engine**

**2.0-liter Inline-Four Turbocharged Engine**

* **Turbocharger Failure:** The turbocharger may fail prematurely, leading to reduced engine power and increased oil consumption.
  * Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, excessive smoke from exhaust, rattling noises
  * Solution: Replacement of the turbocharger
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,000+
* **Fuel Injector Malfunction:** Fuel injectors may become clogged or faulty, resulting in poor fuel delivery and engine performance.
  * Description/Symptoms: Rough idling, stalling, difficulty starting
  * Solution: Cleaning or replacing the fuel injectors
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000

**Transmission**

**Seven-Speed Automatic Transmission**

* **Shifting Issues:** The transmission may have difficulty shifting gears, causing hesitation or rough shifting.
  * Description/Symptoms: Jerking or slipping during gear changes, delayed response
  * Solution: Reconditioning or replacing the transmission
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,500 - $5,000+
* **Torque Converter Failure:** The torque converter may malfunction, leading to loss of power and overheating.
  * Description/Symptoms: Shuddering during acceleration, transmission slipping or overheating
  * Solution: Replacement of the torque converter
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500

**Suspension**

**Front Suspension**

* **Strut Failure:** The front struts may fail prematurely, causing noise, vibration, and impaired handling.
  * Description/Symptoms: Clunking noises, excessive bouncing, uneven tire wear
  * Solution: Replacement of the struts
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,200

**Rear Suspension**

* **Rear Spring Breakage:** The rear springs may break prematurely, leading to a sagging suspension and reduced stability.
  * Description/Symptoms: Sagging rear end, difficulty handling loads, uneven tire wear
  * Solution: Replacement of the rear springs
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600

**Electrical**

* **Battery Draining:** The battery may discharge prematurely, leading to difficulty starting or electrical system failure.
  * Description/Symptoms: Frequent need to jump-start, dimming lights, dead battery
  * Solution: Inspection and replacement of the alternator or battery
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500
* **Electrical Control Unit Malfunction:** The electrical control unit (ECU) may fail, causing various electrical system malfunctions.
  * Description/Symptoms: Electrical system faults, warning lights, engine performance issues
  * Solution: Diagnosis and replacement of the ECU
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+

**Exterior**

* **Taillight Failure:** The taillights may fail prematurely, leading to reduced visibility and safety.
  * Description/Symptoms: Dim or inoperative taillights
  * Solution: Replacement of the taillights
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
* **Side Mirror Vibration:** The side mirrors may vibrate excessively at highway speeds, causing poor visibility.
  * Description/Symptoms: Shaking or blurry vision from side mirrors
  * Solution: Adjustment or replacement of the side mirrors
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300

**Interior**

* **HVAC System Malfunction:** The heating, ventilation, and air conditioning (HVAC) system may fail, causing discomfort for occupants.
  * Description/Symptoms: Insufficient heating or cooling, airflow problems
  * Solution: Diagnosis and repair or replacement of HVAC components
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
* **Infotainment System Issues:** The infotainment system may experience glitches, freezing, or connectivity problems.
  * Description/Symptoms: Touchscreen unresponsiveness, software bugs, Bluetooth issues
  * Solution: Software updates, system resets, or component replacements
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+

**Summary**

While the 2017 Mercedes-Benz Metris offers a reliable and versatile option for cargo transportation, it has been subject to a few issues, particularly with its engine, transmission, and electrical systems. The severity of these issues varies, with some being relatively minor and inexpensive to fix, while others can be more costly and time-consuming. It is important for owners to be aware of these potential problems and to seek professional maintenance and repairs promptly to minimize the risk of costly failures and ensure the safety and reliability of their Metris.
