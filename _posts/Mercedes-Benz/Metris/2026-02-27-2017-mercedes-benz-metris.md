# 2017 Mercedes-Benz Metris

### Overview
The 2017 Mercedes-Benz Metris is a versatile commercial van known for its practicality and Mercedes-Benz engineering. As a relatively new entrant in some markets, its long-term reliability is still being established, but initial data suggests a solid performance for its class. The Metris is designed for demanding commercial use, balancing cargo capacity with maneuverability. Its powertrain, while robust, can experience specific issues as detailed below. Overall, the Metris aims to provide a dependable solution for businesses requiring a reliable work vehicle, with a focus on minimizing downtime through well-engineered components and accessible service.

---


## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4 (M274)
**Turbocharger Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of power, whistling noises, increased oil consumption.
- **Solution:** Turbocharger replacement or repair.
- **Cost:** $1,500 - $3,000

**Coolant Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Overheating, visible coolant puddles, sweet smell.
- **Solution:** Replacement of leaking hoses, seals, or water pump.
- **Cost:** $300 - $800


## Transmission

### 7G-TRONIC 7-Speed Automatic
**Transmission Fluid Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Shifting problems, slipping gears, warning lights.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of seals, transmission pan, or cooler lines.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1,200

**Solenoid Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Harsh shifting, failure to engage gears.
- **Solution:** Replacement of transmission solenoids.
- **Cost:** $400 - $900

### Summary Verdict
The 2017 Mercedes-Benz Metris demonstrates good reliability for a commercial van, with the powertrain being the primary area for potential issues. The 2.0L turbocharged engine and 7-speed automatic transmission, while generally robust, can experience problems like turbocharger wear, coolant leaks, and transmission fluid leaks or solenoid failures. These issues are not necessarily widespread but are documented concerns. The suspension, electrical, exterior, and interior components show a higher degree of reliability with no significant widespread problems reported. Regular maintenance and prompt attention to early symptoms are key to ensuring the Metris remains a dependable workhorse.
