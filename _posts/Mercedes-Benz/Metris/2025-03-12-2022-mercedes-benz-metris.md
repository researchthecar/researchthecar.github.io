The 2022 Mercedes-Benz Metris is a versatile mid-size van appreciated for its maneuverability, fuel efficiency compared to larger vans, and cargo capacity. It's powered by a 2.0L turbocharged inline-4 gasoline engine producing 208 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque, paired exclusively with a 9G-TRONIC 9-speed automatic transmission. Owners often praise its practicality for both commercial and personal use, while common complaints revolve around its somewhat basic interior, limited technology features compared to passenger vehicles, and potential for certain mechanical issues.

**Engine**

**2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4 Gasoline Engine**

*   **Failed High-Pressure Fuel Pump**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Engine may stall, run rough, or fail to start. A check engine light is usually illuminated.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the high-pressure fuel pump.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Turbocharger Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, unusual noises (whining or whistling) from the engine, excessive smoke from the exhaust, check engine light.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the turbocharger.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $2000 - $3500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Faulty Camshaft Adjusters**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rough idling, engine misfires, loss of power, check engine light.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the camshaft adjuster and potentially related components.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1500 - $3000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**9G-TRONIC 9-Speed Automatic Transmission**

*   **Rough or Delayed Shifting**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Harsh or jerky shifts between gears, noticeable delay when shifting, especially from park or reverse to drive.
    *   Solution: Software update of the transmission control module (TCM), or in severe cases, valve body replacement or complete transmission replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+ for software, $1500 - $4000+ for valve body/transmission.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Transmission Slipping**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Engine revs high without corresponding acceleration, loss of power, difficulty maintaining speed on inclines.
    *   Solution: Typically requires a transmission rebuild or replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $4000 - $7000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Premature Wear of Front Suspension Components**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Clunking or rattling noises from the front suspension, uneven tire wear, poor handling.
    *   Solution: Replacement of worn components such as ball joints, control arms, and struts/shocks.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Malfunctioning Sliding Door Actuator**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Sliding door may fail to open or close properly, or may make excessive noise during operation.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the sliding door actuator.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1200+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Faulty Electrical Wiring Harnesses**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Intermittent electrical issues, such as malfunctioning lights, sensors, or infotainment system.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis and repair or replacement of damaged wiring harnesses.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1000+, depending on the severity and location of the damage.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Sensor Failures (e.g., Oxygen Sensors, Mass Airflow Sensor)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Check engine light, poor fuel economy, rough idling, or stalling.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the faulty sensor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+ per sensor.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Rusting Underneath Vehicle**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Excessive Rusting on the undercarriage of the vehicle, with minor rusting on the exterior of the body.
    *   Solution: Rust repair on undercarriage or exterior body to prevent further damage.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1000 - $5000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Rattling Dashboard**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Annoying rattling noises emanating from the dashboard area while driving.
    *   Solution: Tightening loose fasteners, adding padding to reduce vibration, or replacing damaged components.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Infotainment System Freezing/Crashing**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The infotainment screen freezes, becomes unresponsive, or crashes unexpectedly.
    *   Solution: Software updates, system reset, or replacement of the head unit.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 (if software update) to $1000+ for head unit replacement
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2022 Mercedes-Benz Metris, while a practical van, can present owners with a range of issues. Engine-related problems like fuel pump and turbocharger failures can be costly. Transmission issues can also be expensive to resolve. Electrical issues can be varied and difficult to diagnose. Overall, while some issues are relatively minor, others can result in significant repair bills. Careful inspection and preventative maintenance are advised for potential and current owners.
