# 2012 Mercedes-Benz CL65 AMG

### Overview
The 2012 Mercedes-Benz CL65 AMG, a pinnacle of luxury grand touring, is equipped with a formidable twin-turbocharged V12 engine. While offering exhilarating performance, its complexity and high-output nature mean that certain components require diligent maintenance and can be prone to specific issues. This analysis focuses on documented reliability concerns beyond standard wear and tear, providing insights into potential areas of attention for owners and prospective buyers. The CL65 AMG represents a significant investment, and understanding its reliability profile is crucial for long-term ownership satisfaction.

---


## Engine

### 6.0L Twin-Turbo V12 (M275)
**Turbocharger Wastegate Actuator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of boost, reduced engine power, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of faulty wastegate actuator(s).
- **Cost:** $800 - $1500 per actuator

**Ignition Coil Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Misfires, rough idling, reduced engine performance, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of faulty ignition coil(s).
- **Cost:** $200 - $400 per coil

**Coolant Leak (Intercooler System)**
- **Symptoms:** Overheating, loss of coolant, visible leaks around intercooler pipes.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of leaking intercooler hoses and seals.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1200


## Transmission

### 5-Speed Automatic (722.6)
**Torque Converter Seal Leak**
- **Symptoms:** Transmission fluid leaks, potential for slipping or delayed engagement.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the torque converter seal.
- **Cost:** $1000 - $2000

**Transmission Control Module (TCM) Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Erratic shifting, 'limp mode' engagement, transmission warning lights.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the Transmission Control Module.
- **Cost:** $1500 - $2500


## Suspension

### Active Body Control (ABC)
**Hydraulic Fluid Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle sagging, suspension warning lights, fluid leaks under the car.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of leaking ABC struts, lines, or pump.
- **Cost:** $1500 - $4000 per component

**ABC Pump Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of hydraulic pressure, vehicle unable to maintain ride height, suspension warning.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the ABC hydraulic pump.
- **Cost:** $1200 - $2000


## Electrical

### Infotainment System
**COMAND System Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Screen freezing, unresponsive controls, audio issues.
- **Solution:** Software update or replacement of COMAND head unit.
- **Cost:** $500 - $2000


### Keyless Go System
**Door Handle Sensor Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Keyless entry not working on specific doors.
- **Solution:** Replacement of faulty door handle sensor.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600 per handle

### Summary Verdict
The 2012 Mercedes-Benz CL65 AMG is a high-performance luxury coupe with a complex V12 engine and advanced systems like Active Body Control. While generally well-built, owners should be aware of potential issues related to the turbocharger wastegate actuators, ignition coils, and ABC system hydraulic components, which can be costly to repair. Transmission issues, particularly with the torque converter seal and TCM, are also noted. Electrical gremlins in the infotainment system can occur. Despite these potential concerns, with proper maintenance and prompt attention to any developing issues, the CL65 AMG can provide a rewarding ownership experience, though maintenance costs will remain high due to the specialized nature of its components.
