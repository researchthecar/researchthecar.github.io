# 2009 Mercedes-Benz CL65 AMG

### Overview
The 2009 Mercedes-Benz CL65 AMG, a pinnacle of luxury grand touring, is equipped with a formidable twin-turbocharged V12 engine. While offering exhilarating performance, its complexity and high-strung nature can lead to specific reliability concerns. Owners should be aware of potential issues related to the advanced powertrain, sophisticated electronics, and the specialized nature of AMG components. Maintenance and repair costs are typically high due to the exclusive parts and specialized labor required. Despite these considerations, for those who prioritize performance and exclusivity, the CL65 AMG remains a desirable, albeit demanding, vehicle.

---


## Engine

### 6.0L Twin-Turbo V12 (M275)
**Intercooler System Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Reduced engine power, overheating, coolant smell.
- **Solution:** Replacement of leaking intercooler hoses or core.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1,500

**Turbocharger Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of boost, whistling noises, smoke from exhaust.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of turbochargers and associated lines.
- **Cost:** $3,000 - $6,000+

**Ignition Coil Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Misfires, rough idle, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of faulty ignition coils.
- **Cost:** $400 - $800


## Transmission

### 5-Speed Automatic (722.6)
**Torque Converter Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Slipping, shuddering, delayed engagement.
- **Solution:** Rebuild or replacement of the torque converter.
- **Cost:** $1,500 - $3,000

**Transmission Control Module (TCM) Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Erratic shifting, transmission warning lights, limp mode.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the TCM.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1,400


## Suspension

### Active Body Control (ABC)
**Hydraulic Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle sagging, suspension warning lights, fluid leaks.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of leaking hoses, struts, or pump.
- **Cost:** $1,000 - $4,000+

**ABC Pump Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of hydraulic pressure, suspension malfunction.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the ABC hydraulic pump.
- **Cost:** $1,200 - $2,000


## Electrical

### Infotainment System
**COMAND System Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Screen freezing, audio issues, navigation problems.
- **Solution:** Software updates or replacement of head unit.
- **Cost:** $500 - $2,000


### Sensors and Modules
**Various Sensor Failures (e.g., ABS, Parking Assist)**
- **Symptoms:** Warning lights, system malfunctions.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and replacement of faulty sensors or control modules.
- **Cost:** $300 - $1,000 per component

### Summary Verdict
The 2009 Mercedes-Benz CL65 AMG is a high-performance luxury coupe with a complex and powerful V12 engine. While it offers an exceptional driving experience, its reliability is marked by potential issues common to high-end, performance-oriented vehicles. Key areas of concern include the intricate Active Body Control suspension system, potential turbocharger and intercooler problems, and the sophisticated electronic systems. The 5-speed automatic transmission, while robust, can also experience issues with the torque converter or control module. Owners should budget for higher maintenance and repair costs, as specialized parts and labor are often required. Despite these potential drawbacks, with diligent maintenance and prompt attention to issues, the CL65 AMG can remain a rewarding ownership experience for enthusiasts.
