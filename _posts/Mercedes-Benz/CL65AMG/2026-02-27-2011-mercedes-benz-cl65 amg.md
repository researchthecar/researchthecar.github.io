# 2011 Mercedes-Benz CL65 AMG

### Overview
The 2011 Mercedes-Benz CL65 AMG, a pinnacle of luxury grand touring, is equipped with a formidable twin-turbocharged V12 engine. While offering exhilarating performance, its complexity and high-strung nature can lead to specific reliability concerns. Owners should be aware of potential issues related to the advanced powertrain and sophisticated electronic systems. Maintenance is critical for longevity, and repairs can be costly due to specialized parts and labor. Despite its performance pedigree, the CL65 AMG requires diligent attention to ensure continued dependability and enjoyment of its exceptional driving experience.

---


## Engine

### 6.0L Twin-Turbocharged V12 (M275)
**Turbocharger Actuator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of boost, reduced engine power, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of faulty turbocharger wastegate actuators.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1500

**Ignition Coil Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Misfires, rough idling, reduced engine performance, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of individual or sets of ignition coils.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600 per coil

**Coolant Leak (Intercooler System)**
- **Symptoms:** Coolant loss, overheating, potential engine damage.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of intercooler hoses and connections.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1000


## Transmission

### 5-Speed Automatic (722.6)
**Torque Converter Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Slipping, shuddering, delayed engagement, harsh shifting.
- **Solution:** Rebuild or replacement of the torque converter.
- **Cost:** $1500 - $3000

**Transmission Control Module (TCM) Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Erratic shifting, transmission warning lights, limp mode.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the TCM.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1500


## Suspension

### Active Body Control (ABC)
**Hydraulic Fluid Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle sagging, suspension warning lights, stiff ride.
- **Solution:** Replacement of leaking hoses, struts, or pump.
- **Cost:** $1000 - $3000+

**ABC Pump Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of hydraulic pressure, vehicle sagging, suspension warning lights.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the ABC hydraulic pump.
- **Cost:** $1200 - $2000


## Electrical

### Infotainment System
**COMAND System Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Screen freezing, audio issues, navigation malfunctions.
- **Solution:** Software updates or replacement of the COMAND unit.
- **Cost:** $500 - $2000


### Sensors (Parking, Rain, etc.)
**Malfunctioning Sensors**
- **Symptoms:** False alerts, non-functional systems (e.g., wipers not activating).
- **Solution:** Replacement of faulty sensors.
- **Cost:** $200 - $500 per sensor

### Summary Verdict
The 2011 Mercedes-Benz CL65 AMG is a high-performance luxury coupe with a complex powertrain and advanced systems. While generally well-built, potential owners should anticipate higher maintenance and repair costs compared to less performance-oriented vehicles. Key areas of concern include the twin-turbo V12 engine's ancillary components, the robust but complex 5-speed automatic transmission, and the sophisticated Active Body Control (ABC) suspension system, which can be prone to hydraulic leaks and pump failures. Electrical gremlins, particularly with the infotainment system, can also arise. Despite these potential issues, with diligent maintenance and a willingness to invest in repairs, the CL65 AMG can remain a reliable and exhilarating machine.
