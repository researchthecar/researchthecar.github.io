# 2006 Mercedes-Benz CL65 AMG

### Overview
The 2006 Mercedes-Benz CL65 AMG, a pinnacle of luxury and performance, is equipped with a formidable twin-turbocharged V12 engine. While offering exhilarating power, its complexity and high-performance nature can lead to specific reliability concerns. Owners should be aware of potential issues related to the advanced powertrain, sophisticated electronics, and the specialized nature of AMG components. Regular and meticulous maintenance by qualified technicians is paramount to ensuring the longevity and optimal performance of this exotic grand tourer. Despite its performance pedigree, some areas require vigilant attention to avoid costly repairs.

---


## Engine

### 6.0L Twin-Turbo V12 (M275)
**Turbocharger Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of power, whistling noises, black smoke from exhaust, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of turbochargers. Often requires replacement of associated oil lines and intercooler components.
- **Cost:** $4,000 - $8,000+

**Intercooler System Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Reduced engine performance, overheating, coolant loss, engine misfires.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of intercooler hoses, seals, or the intercooler unit itself.
- **Cost:** $800 - $2,500

**Ignition Coil Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Engine misfires, rough idling, reduced power, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of faulty ignition coils. It's often recommended to replace them in pairs or sets.
- **Cost:** $300 - $700

**Oil Cooler Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Oil leaks, potential for oil in coolant, overheating.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the oil cooler and associated seals.
- **Cost:** $700 - $1,500


## Transmission

### 5-Speed Automatic (722.6)
**Torque Converter Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Slipping gears, shuddering during acceleration, delayed engagement, fluid leaks.
- **Solution:** Rebuild or replacement of the torque converter. Transmission fluid and filter service is also crucial.
- **Cost:** $1,500 - $3,500

**Valve Body Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** Harsh shifting, delayed gear changes, transmission warning lights.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of the transmission valve body. Requires specialized knowledge and fluid.
- **Cost:** $1,000 - $2,500


## Suspension

### Active Body Control (ABC)
**Hydraulic Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle sagging, suspension warning lights, fluid leaks under the car, stiff or bouncy ride.
- **Solution:** Replacement of leaking hoses, struts, or pumps. System flush and fluid replacement.
- **Cost:** $1,000 - $4,000+

**ABC Pump Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of hydraulic pressure, vehicle sagging, suspension warning lights, loud whining noise from pump.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the ABC hydraulic pump.
- **Cost:** $1,200 - $2,500


## Electrical

### General Electrical System
**SAM Unit (Signal Acquisition Module) Failures**
- **Symptoms:** Various warning lights, malfunctioning lights (headlights, taillights), power window issues, central locking problems.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and potential replacement or reprogramming of the SAM unit. Can be complex and costly.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1,500

**Infotainment System Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Screen freezing, audio issues, navigation problems, unresponsive buttons.
- **Solution:** Software updates, component replacement (e.g., head unit, amplifier), or module resets.
- **Cost:** $300 - $1,200

**Seat Control Module Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Power seats not working, memory seat functions failing, seat heating/cooling issues.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and replacement of faulty seat control modules or wiring.
- **Cost:** $400 - $900

### Summary Verdict
The 2006 Mercedes-Benz CL65 AMG is a high-performance luxury coupe with a complex V12 engine and advanced systems. While the core mechanicals are robust, owners should anticipate potential issues with the twin-turbocharged engine's components, particularly the turbochargers and intercooler system. The Active Body Control suspension is prone to hydraulic leaks and pump failures, which can be expensive to repair. Electrical gremlins, often related to the SAM unit or infotainment system, are also a possibility. Transmission issues, while less common, can involve the torque converter or valve body. Due to the specialized nature and cost of parts and labor, meticulous maintenance and a thorough pre-purchase inspection are highly recommended for this exotic vehicle.
