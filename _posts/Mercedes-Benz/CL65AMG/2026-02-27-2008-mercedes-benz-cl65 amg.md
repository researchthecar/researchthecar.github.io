# 2008 Mercedes-Benz CL65 AMG

### Overview
The 2008 Mercedes-Benz CL65 AMG, a pinnacle of luxury grand touring, is equipped with a formidable twin-turbocharged V12 engine. While offering exhilarating performance, its complexity and high-strung nature can lead to specific reliability concerns. Owners should be aware of potential issues related to the advanced powertrain, sophisticated electronics, and the specialized nature of AMG components. Maintenance is critical for this high-performance vehicle, and repairs can be costly due to the specialized parts and labor required. Despite its performance pedigree, the CL65 AMG demands diligent attention to ensure continued operational excellence and to mitigate potential reliability drawbacks.

---


## Engine

### 6.0L Twin-Turbo V12 (M275)
**Turbocharger Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of power, excessive smoke, whistling noises, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of turbochargers.
- **Cost:** $3,000 - $7,000 per turbocharger, plus labor.

**Intercooler Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Reduced engine performance, overheating, coolant loss.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of intercooler system.
- **Cost:** $800 - $2,500.

**Ignition Coil Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Misfires, rough idling, reduced engine power, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of faulty ignition coils.
- **Cost:** $150 - $300 per coil, plus labor.

**Oil Cooler Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Oil leaks, potential for coolant contamination, overheating.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the oil cooler and seals.
- **Cost:** $700 - $1,500.


## Transmission

### 5-Speed Automatic (AMG Speedshift)
**Torque Converter Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Slipping gears, shuddering, delayed engagement, fluid leaks.
- **Solution:** Rebuild or replacement of the torque converter.
- **Cost:** $1,500 - $3,500.

**Transmission Control Module (TCM) Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Erratic shifting, transmission warning lights, limp mode.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the TCM.
- **Cost:** $800 - $2,000.


## Suspension

### Active Body Control (ABC)
**Hydraulic Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle sagging, suspension warning lights, stiff ride, fluid leaks under the car.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of leaking hoses, struts, or pump.
- **Cost:** $1,000 - $4,000+ depending on component.

**ABC Pump Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of hydraulic pressure, vehicle sagging, suspension warning lights.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the ABC hydraulic pump.
- **Cost:** $1,200 - $2,500.


## Electrical

### Infotainment System
**COMAND System Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Screen freezing, unresponsive buttons, navigation issues, audio problems.
- **Solution:** Software updates or replacement of head unit.
- **Cost:** $500 - $2,500.


### Sensors (Parking, Rain, etc.)
**Malfunctioning Sensors**
- **Symptoms:** False alerts, non-functional systems (e.g., wipers not activating), warning lights.
- **Solution:** Replacement of faulty sensors.
- **Cost:** $200 - $600 per sensor.


### Battery Drain
**Parasitic Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Dead battery after short periods of inactivity, electrical components malfunctioning.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and repair of faulty modules or wiring causing the drain.
- **Cost:** $300 - $1,000+ depending on the cause.

## Interior

### Climate Control System
**HVAC Blower Motor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** No air conditioning or heat, inconsistent fan speed.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the blower motor.
- **Cost:** $400 - $800.


### Seat Actuators
**Malfunctioning Seat Adjustments**
- **Symptoms:** Power seats not moving, memory functions failing.
- **Solution:** Replacement of seat motors or control modules.
- **Cost:** $300 - $900 per seat.

### Summary Verdict
The 2008 Mercedes-Benz CL65 AMG is a high-performance luxury coupe with a complex V12 engine and advanced systems. While it offers an exceptional driving experience, potential buyers should be prepared for higher maintenance and repair costs compared to less performance-oriented vehicles. Key areas to monitor include the twin-turbo V12 engine's components (turbos, intercoolers), the sophisticated Active Body Control (ABC) suspension for leaks, and the intricate electrical systems, particularly the COMAND infotainment and potential battery drain issues. Transmission reliability is generally good, but torque converter and TCM issues can arise. Diligent maintenance and a thorough pre-purchase inspection are crucial for ownership satisfaction.
