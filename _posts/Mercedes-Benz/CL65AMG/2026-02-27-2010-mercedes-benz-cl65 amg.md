# 2010 Mercedes-Benz CL65 AMG

### Overview
The 2010 Mercedes-Benz CL65 AMG, a pinnacle of luxury and performance, is equipped with a formidable twin-turbocharged V12 engine. While offering exhilarating power, its complexity and high-performance nature can lead to specific reliability concerns that differ from more mainstream vehicles. Owners should be aware of potential issues related to the advanced powertrain and sophisticated electronic systems. Regular and meticulous maintenance by qualified technicians is paramount to preserving the longevity and performance of this exotic grand tourer. Despite its performance pedigree, the CL65 AMG requires a proactive approach to ownership to mitigate potential costly repairs.

---


## Engine

### 6.0L Twin-Turbo V12 (M275)
**Turbocharger Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of power, whistling noises, black smoke from exhaust, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of turbochargers.
- **Cost:** $4,000 - $8,000

**Ignition Coil Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Misfires, rough idling, reduced engine power, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of faulty ignition coils.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1,200

**Coolant Leaks (Intercooler/Radiator)**
- **Symptoms:** Overheating, visible coolant leaks, sweet smell from engine bay.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of intercooler or radiator system components.
- **Cost:** $1,000 - $3,000


## Transmission

### 5-Speed Automatic (722.6)
**Torque Converter Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Slipping gears, shuddering during acceleration, delayed engagement.
- **Solution:** Rebuild or replacement of the torque converter.
- **Cost:** $1,500 - $3,500

**Transmission Control Module (TCM) Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Erratic shifting, transmission warning lights, inability to shift gears.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the TCM.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1,800


## Suspension

### Active Body Control (ABC)
**Hydraulic Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle sagging, suspension warning lights, stiff ride, fluid leaks under the car.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of hydraulic lines, struts, or pump.
- **Cost:** $2,000 - $6,000+

**ABC Pump Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of hydraulic pressure, vehicle sagging, suspension warning lights.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the ABC hydraulic pump.
- **Cost:** $1,500 - $3,000


## Electrical

### Infotainment System
**COMAND System Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Screen freezing, audio issues, navigation malfunctions, unresponsive controls.
- **Solution:** Software updates or replacement of COMAND unit.
- **Cost:** $1,000 - $3,000


### Sensors (Parking, Rain, etc.)
**Malfunctioning Sensors**
- **Symptoms:** False alerts from parking sensors, wipers not activating automatically, dashboard warning lights.
- **Solution:** Replacement of faulty sensors.
- **Cost:** $300 - $800 per sensor

### Summary Verdict
The 2010 Mercedes-Benz CL65 AMG is a high-performance luxury coupe with a complex V12 engine and advanced systems. While generally well-built, its performance-oriented nature means potential owners should anticipate higher maintenance and repair costs compared to standard vehicles. Key areas of concern include the twin-turbo V12 engine's components like turbochargers and ignition coils, the robust but complex 5-speed automatic transmission, and the sophisticated Active Body Control (ABC) suspension system, which can be prone to hydraulic leaks. Electrical gremlins, particularly with the COMAND infotainment system and various sensors, can also arise. Despite these potential issues, with diligent maintenance and prompt attention to any developing problems, the CL65 AMG can remain a rewarding and reliable exotic car.
