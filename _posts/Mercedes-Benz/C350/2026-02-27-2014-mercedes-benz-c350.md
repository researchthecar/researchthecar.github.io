# 2014 Mercedes-Benz C350

### Overview
The 2014 Mercedes-Benz C350, part of the W204 generation, is generally regarded as a solid performer with a good balance of luxury and sportiness. It was offered with a potent 3.5L V6 engine, known for its smooth power delivery. Reliability for this model year is typically strong, reflecting Mercedes-Benz's engineering standards. However, like any vehicle, specific components can experience issues over time. This analysis focuses on documented, non-wear-and-tear related problems reported by owners and technicians, providing insights into potential areas of concern and their associated repair costs to help prospective buyers and current owners make informed decisions.

---


## Engine

### 3.5L V6 (M272/M276)
**Variable Valve Timing (VVT) Actuator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Check engine light, rough idle, reduced engine power, rattling noise from the engine.
- **Solution:** Replacement of VVT actuators and potentially camshaft adjusters.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1500

**Oil Leaks from Valve Covers or Oil Cooler**
- **Symptoms:** Visible oil leaks, burning oil smell, low oil level.
- **Solution:** Replacement of valve cover gaskets or oil cooler seals.
- **Cost:** $400 - $900


## Transmission

### 7G-TRONIC (722.9) 7-Speed Automatic
**Transmission Control Module (TCM) Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Harsh shifting, slipping gears, transmission warning light, inability to select gears.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the Transmission Control Module.
- **Cost:** $1000 - $2000

**Torque Converter Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Shuddering during acceleration, slipping, delayed engagement.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the torque converter.
- **Cost:** $1200 - $2500

## Electrical

### General Electrical System
**Window Regulator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Power windows not operating, window getting stuck.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the window regulator mechanism.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600

**Headlight Ballast Failure**
- **Symptoms:** One or both headlights not working, flickering headlights.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the headlight ballast unit.
- **Cost:** $200 - $500

### Summary Verdict
The 2014 Mercedes-Benz C350 (W204) demonstrates good overall reliability, with its 3.5L V6 engine and 7-speed automatic transmission being robust for the most part. The primary areas that may require attention are the Variable Valve Timing actuators and potential oil leaks from the engine, along with the Transmission Control Module and torque converter in the transmission. Electrical components like window regulators and headlight ballasts are also noted as occasional failure points. However, these issues are not universally reported and often occur after significant mileage. With proper maintenance, the C350 can be a dependable luxury sedan.
