# 2012 Mercedes-Benz C350

### Overview
The 2012 Mercedes-Benz C350, part of the W204 generation, is generally considered a reliable luxury sedan, offering a blend of performance, comfort, and advanced features for its era. While it shares some common Mercedes-Benz traits, specific model year investigations reveal areas of particular strength and occasional weaknesses. Owners often praise its refined driving dynamics and upscale interior. However, like any vehicle, it is not immune to potential issues, particularly as it ages. This analysis focuses on documented technical reliability concerns beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into common failure points and their estimated repair costs for various components and powertrain configurations.

---


## Engine

### 3.5L V6 (M272)
**Intake Manifold Runner Flap Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Check engine light, rough idle, loss of power, P2004/P2005/P2006 codes.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the intake manifold assembly or repair kit.
- **Cost:** $600 - $1200

**Timing Chain Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Rattling noise from the front of the engine, especially on startup; check engine light with timing-related codes.
- **Solution:** Replacement of timing chain, tensioners, and guides.
- **Cost:** $1500 - $3000


## Transmission

### 7G-TRONIC (722.9)
**Valve Body Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Harsh shifting, delayed engagement, transmission warning light, limp mode.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of the valve body assembly.
- **Cost:** $1000 - $2500

**Torque Converter Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Shuddering at low speeds, slipping, unusual noises.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the torque converter.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1800

## Electrical

### General Electrical System
**SAM Unit (Signal Acquisition Module) Failures**
- **Symptoms:** Intermittent electrical problems, malfunctioning lights, power window issues, CAN bus errors.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and potential replacement or repair of the SAM unit.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1500

**Headlight Ballast Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Xenon headlights not turning on or flickering.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the headlight ballast.
- **Cost:** $200 - $500

## Interior

### Cabin Electronics and Features
**Seat Heater Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** One or both seat heaters not working.
- **Solution:** Replacement of seat heating elements or control modules.
- **Cost:** $300 - $700

**COMAND System Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Infotainment system freezing, unresponsiveness, or audio issues.
- **Solution:** Software updates or potential replacement of the COMAND unit.
- **Cost:** $400 - $1500

### Summary Verdict
The 2012 Mercedes-Benz C350 (W204) presents a generally solid reliability record, especially concerning its chassis and exterior components. The primary areas requiring attention tend to be the powertrain and certain electrical modules as the vehicle ages. Engine issues like intake manifold flap failures and timing chain wear, along with transmission problems such as valve body malfunctions, are the most frequently cited concerns, demanding significant repair costs. Electrical gremlins, particularly with the SAM unit and headlight ballasts, can also arise. While not a disproportionate number of issues for a luxury vehicle of this age, potential buyers should be aware of these common failure points and factor in potential maintenance expenses. Regular servicing and prompt attention to warning signs are crucial for maintaining the C350's long-term reliability.
