**Introduction to the 2012 Mercedes-Benz C350**

The 2012 Mercedes-Benz C350 is a luxury sedan that offers a refined driving experience and a spacious interior. It comes equipped with a powerful 3.5-liter V6 engine that produces 302 horsepower and 273 lb-ft of torque, paired with a smooth-shifting seven-speed automatic transmission. The C350 also features a well-equipped cabin with features such as a sunroof, leather seats, and a premium audio system. It is praised for its comfortable ride, spirited performance, and impressive fuel efficiency for its class. However, some owners have reported issues with various components of the vehicle.

**Engine**

* **3.5L V6 Engine**
    * **Oil leak from oil filter housing:* Oil leaks around the oil filter housing, causing oil to accumulate on the engine and undercarriage.
    Description/Symptoms: Oil leak, oily residue, burning oil smell
    Solution: Replace oil filter housing gasket
    Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
    Recalls: None Found
    
    * **Timing chain rattle:** A rattling noise is heard from the engine, especially during startup or acceleration.
    Description/Symptoms: Rattling noise, reduced engine power
    Solution: Replace timing chain and related components
    Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,000+
    Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

* **7-Speed Automatic Transmission**

    * **Hesitation or jerking during shifts:** The transmission may hesitate or jerk during gear changes, causing uncomfortable ride and potential damage.
    Description/Symptoms: Hesitation, jerking, delayed shifting
    Solution: Software update, transmission fluid change/flush, or replacement of transmission components
    Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,500+
    Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **No Issues Found**

**Electrical**

* **Alternator failure:** The alternator may fail prematurely, leading to battery drainage and potential electrical problems.
Description/Symptoms: Electrical system malfunction, battery warning light, difficulty starting
Solution: Replace alternator
Estimated Cost to Fix: $700 - $1,000
Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Headlight condensation:** Moisture may accumulate inside the headlights, causing condensation and impaired visibility.
Description/Symptoms: Condensation inside headlights, reduced lighting output
Solution: Replace headlight seals or entire headlight assembly
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Sunroof rattle:** A rattling noise may be heard from the panoramic sunroof, especially during rough road conditions.
Description/Symptoms: Rattling noise, vibration, wind noise
Solution: Adjust sunroof alignment, replace sunroof seals
Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2012 Mercedes-Benz C350 offers a comfortable and refined driving experience, but it has been subject to some issues with its engine, transmission, electrical system, and exterior components. The timing chain rattle and transmission hesitation can be particularly costly to repair. Overall, the severity and cost of the reported issues should be considered when evaluating this vehicle.
