# 2013 Mercedes-Benz C350

### Overview
The 2013 Mercedes-Benz C350, part of the W204 generation, is generally considered a reliable luxury sedan, offering a blend of performance, comfort, and advanced features for its time. It was available with a potent V6 engine and a smooth automatic transmission. While most owners report a positive ownership experience, like any vehicle, certain components can experience issues over time. This analysis focuses on documented, non-wear-and-tear related problems that may require attention, providing insights into potential repair costs and solutions for common concerns.

---


## Engine

### 3.5L V6 (M272)
**Intake Manifold Runner Flap Actuator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Check engine light, rough idle, loss of power, P2004/P2005/P2006 codes.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the intake manifold runner flap assembly or actuator.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1200

**Oil Leaks (Valve Cover Gaskets, Oil Cooler Seals)**
- **Symptoms:** Visible oil stains under the engine, burning oil smell, low oil level.
- **Solution:** Replacement of valve cover gaskets and/or oil cooler seals.
- **Cost:** $400 - $900


## Transmission

### 7G-TRONIC (722.9) Automatic Transmission
**Transmission Control Module (TCM) Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Harsh shifting, slipping gears, inability to shift, 'F1' or 'F2' error on the dashboard.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the Transmission Control Module.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1500

**Torque Converter Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Shuddering at low speeds, slipping, delayed engagement.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the torque converter.
- **Cost:** $1000 - $2000

## Electrical

### General Electrical System
**Window Regulator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Power windows not working, window stuck in position, grinding noise when operating.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the window regulator and motor assembly.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600

**Headlight Ballast Failure**
- **Symptoms:** One or both headlights not turning on, flickering headlights.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the headlight ballast.
- **Cost:** $200 - $500

### Summary Verdict
The 2013 Mercedes-Benz C350 (W204) demonstrates good overall reliability, particularly for its powertrain. The 3.5L V6 engine is robust, though common issues like intake manifold runner flap actuator failure and minor oil leaks can occur. The 7G-TRONIC transmission is generally dependable, but potential failures of the TCM or torque converter are the most significant concerns. Electrical components like window regulators and headlight ballasts are the most frequently reported non-wear-and-tear issues. Suspension, exterior, and interior components are largely free from widespread problems. Regular maintenance and addressing early signs of issues can significantly contribute to the long-term reliability of this luxury sedan.
