## Suspension

### Air Suspension System
**Air Spring Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle sagging on one side, uneven ride height, audible hissing from suspension components.
- **Solution:** Replacement of faulty air spring or associated air lines.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1500 per corner

**Compressor Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Failure to maintain ride height, persistent compressor running, warning lights on the dashboard.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of the air suspension compressor.
- **Cost:** $800 - $2000


## Electrical

### Body Control Module (BCM)
**Intermittent Faults**
- **Symptoms:** Malfunctioning lights, door locks, wipers, or other auxiliary systems; error codes related to BCM.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and reprogramming or replacement of the BCM.
- **Cost:** $1000 - $3000


### Wiring Harness Issues
**Corrosion/Abrasion**
- **Symptoms:** Intermittent electrical failures, short circuits, warning lights, particularly in high-moisture or high-vibration areas.
- **Solution:** Inspection, repair, or replacement of damaged sections of the wiring harness.
- **Cost:** $500 - $2500+

### Summary Verdict
The 2018 Mercedes-Benz Gran Viale Articulado demonstrates a commendable level of reliability, particularly in its powertrain and core structural components. While the air suspension system and certain electrical modules, such as the BCM and wiring harnesses, present potential areas for concern and require diligent maintenance, these are not indicative of systemic failure. The interior and exterior components generally hold up well under typical operational stress. Fleet operators can expect a durable and dependable vehicle, provided that proactive maintenance is performed, especially on the suspension and electrical systems, to mitigate the risk of costly repairs and ensure continuous service.
