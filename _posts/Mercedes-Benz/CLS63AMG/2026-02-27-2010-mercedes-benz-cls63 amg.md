# 2010 Mercedes-Benz CLS63 AMG

### Overview
The 2010 Mercedes-Benz CLS63 AMG, a high-performance luxury coupe, is renowned for its potent V8 engine and engaging driving dynamics. While generally well-regarded for its performance and premium features, like any complex vehicle, it has specific areas that have seen more attention from owners regarding reliability. This analysis focuses on documented issues beyond routine maintenance, highlighting common concerns within its powertrain, suspension, and electrical systems. Owners should be aware of potential issues related to the engine's twin-turbo setup and the sophisticated transmission, as well as the advanced electronic systems that contribute to its luxury experience. Understanding these potential weak points can help in proactive maintenance and managing ownership costs for this performance-oriented model.

---


## Engine

### 5.5L Twin-Turbo V8 (M156/M159)
**Coolant Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Visible coolant puddles under the vehicle, low coolant level warning, engine overheating.
- **Solution:** Replacement of leaking hoses, seals, or radiator.
- **Cost:** $300 - $800

**Turbocharger Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Reduced engine power, whistling noises from the engine bay, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of turbocharger assemblies and associated lines.
- **Cost:** $2,500 - $5,000+

**Camshaft Adjuster Solenoids**
- **Symptoms:** Rough idle, check engine light with camshaft position codes, rattling noise on startup.
- **Solution:** Replacement of faulty camshaft adjuster solenoids.
- **Cost:** $600 - $1,200


## Transmission

### 7-Speed AMG Speedshift MCT
**Torque Converter Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Slipping gears, harsh shifting, shuddering during acceleration, transmission warning light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the torque converter.
- **Cost:** $1,500 - $3,000

**Mechatronics Unit Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Erratic shifting, inability to select gears, transmission warning light.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of the transmission mechatronics unit.
- **Cost:** $2,000 - $4,000


## Suspension

### AMG Sport Suspension
**Air Suspension Leaks (if equipped)**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle sagging on one side, uneven ride height, compressor running excessively.
- **Solution:** Replacement of leaking air springs or air compressor.
- **Cost:** $800 - $2,000 per corner/component

**Control Arm Bushing Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Clunking noises over bumps, vague steering feel, uneven tire wear.
- **Solution:** Replacement of worn control arm bushings or entire control arms.
- **Cost:** $400 - $900


## Electrical

### Infotainment System
**COMAND System Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Screen freezing, audio cutting out, navigation system errors, unresponsive buttons.
- **Solution:** Software updates, or replacement of the COMAND head unit.
- **Cost:** $500 - $2,500


### Sensors
**Oxygen Sensor Failures**
- **Symptoms:** Check engine light, poor fuel economy, rough running.
- **Solution:** Replacement of faulty oxygen sensors.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600

## Interior

### Upholstery & Electronics
**Seat Heater Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** One or both seat heaters not working.
- **Solution:** Replacement of seat heating elements or control modules.
- **Cost:** $300 - $700

**Sunroof Mechanism Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Sunroof not opening/closing properly, unusual noises.
- **Solution:** Lubrication, adjustment, or replacement of sunroof motor/mechanism.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1,200

### Summary Verdict
The 2010 Mercedes-Benz CLS63 AMG offers exhilarating performance but comes with potential reliability concerns typical of high-performance luxury vehicles. The 5.5L twin-turbo V8, while powerful, can experience issues like coolant leaks and turbocharger problems, requiring significant investment. The AMG Speedshift MCT transmission, though robust, may develop issues with its torque converter or mechatronics unit. Suspension components, particularly air springs if equipped, and electrical systems like the COMAND infotainment can also be sources of expense. While exterior and interior components are generally durable, specific electrical features like seat heaters can fail. Prospective buyers should budget for potential repairs, especially concerning the powertrain and advanced electronics, and ensure thorough pre-purchase inspections.
