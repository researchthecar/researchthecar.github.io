# 2017 Mercedes-Benz E43 AMG Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2017 Mercedes-Benz E43 AMG offers a blend of luxury and performance, but prospective buyers should carefully review potential mechanical and electrical concerns. Owner feedback submitted to federal safety regulators highlights several recurring problem areas ranging from powertrain control to fuel system and steering complaints. While luxury German performance cars demand meticulous maintenance, being aware of these reported trouble spots can help you evaluate a used model more effectively.

---

## Engine

### 3.0L Bi-Turbo V6

The 3.0-liter bi-turbo V6 engine generates substantial horsepower and torque to deliver dynamic AMG performance. While generally robust, owners have reported occasional fuel system odors and crankcase ventilation issues that require professional diagnosis.

**Fuel Odor and Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers note a strong smell of gasoline around or inside the cabin during startup or normal operation.
- **Solution:** Inspect fuel lines, high-pressure pumps, and connections for cracks or leaks, and replace damaged components.

**Crankcase Ventilation Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Illuminated check engine lights, error codes such as P052E, or rough engine performance.
- **Solution:** Replace the failed crankcase ventilation assembly.
- **Cost:** $1,700

## Transmission

### 9-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 9-speed automatic transmission channels power smoothly to the 4MATIC all-wheel-drive system under normal conditions. However, electronic shift module glitches and rough engagement have been noted in driver complaints.

**Gear Shifter and Neutral Shift Issues**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may inadvertently shift into neutral while coming to a stop, or fail to engage drive or reverse properly.
- **Solution:** Inspect the electronic steering column shift module and replace or update components as necessary.

**Transmission Slipping and Rough Shifting**
- **Symptoms:** Unintended downshifting, jerking when transitioning between gears, or delayed engagement.
- **Solution:** Reprogram or replace the transmission control module and inspect internal hydraulic components.

## Steering

### Power Steering System

The power steering system provides responsive handling characteristic of an AMG sports sedan. Intermittent assist failures can occur, making prompt inspection essential if warning lights appear.

**Steering Assist Loss**
- **Symptoms:** Intermittent loss of power steering functionality accompanied by dashboard warnings requiring increased physical effort to turn the wheel.
- **Solution:** Diagnose the electronic power steering control module and replace faulty electrical harnesses or steering racks.

## Electrical

### Electrical and Infotainment

Sophisticated electronics govern everything from driver displays to cabin convenience features. Occasional screen blackouts and parasitic battery drains are important items to verify during a pre-purchase inspection.

**Instrument Panel Blackout**
- **Symptoms:** The digital instrument cluster and speed display suddenly go blank while starting or driving the vehicle.
- **Solution:** Check software versions, fuses, and instrument display modules for internal failures.

## Exterior

### Sunroof and Body Glass

Exterior trim and glass features complete the vehicle's premium appearance. Spontaneous panoramic sunroof glass breakage has been reported by a subset of owners.

**Panoramic Sunroof Shattering**
- **Symptoms:** The panoramic glass roof panel unexpectedly shatters while driving without external impact.
- **Solution:** Replace the shattered glass panel and clear out any residual glass fragments from the track and interior cabin.

## Safety

### Air Bag System

Advanced safety restraint systems protect occupants but rely on delicate wiring and sensors. Intermittent squib and weight-sensing faults can trigger dashboard warnings.

**Airbag Warning and Seat Squib Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Airbag warning lights illuminate on the instrument cluster, often pointing to open circuits in the seat cushion or steering wheel clock spring.
- **Solution:** Inspect wiring harnesses, clock springs, and seat weight sensors, and replace malfunctioning occupant detection modules.
