# 2017 Mercedes-Benz E500 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2017 Mercedes-Benz E500 combines luxury performance with sophisticated engineering. Used car buyers considering this model should weigh its sophisticated features against potential maintenance considerations, particularly regarding electrical systems and powertrain components.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged 4-Cylinder

A turbocharged 2.0-liter four-cylinder engine delivering around 241 horsepower and 273 lb-ft of torque, providing responsive acceleration for daily driving.

## Transmission

### 9-Speed Automatic Transmission

A smooth-shifting 9-speed automatic transmission designed to optimize both fuel efficiency and highway cruising comfort.

## Steering

### Power Steering System

Issues related to power steering assist loss have been reported by vehicle owners.

**Steering Assist Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers experience an unexpected loss of power steering functionality, making the steering wheel significantly harder to turn, especially at lower speeds.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified mechanic inspect the steering control module and power steering components for electrical faults or mechanical wear.

## Electrical

### Electrical System

Electrical and cabin sealing reports highlight potential moisture issues.

**Water Intrusion and Drainage**
- **Symptoms:** Water leaks into the cabin space, leading to damp floorboards and potential electrical battery drain.
- **Solution:** Clear clogged sunroof drains and inspect weatherstripping seals around windows and doors.

## Exterior

### Sunroof

Reports of spontaneous panoramic sunroof glass failure.

**Panoramic Sunroof Shattering**
- **Symptoms:** The panoramic glass roof panel spontaneously fractures or shatters while driving without external impact.
- **Solution:** Replace the shattered glass panel and inspect the frame for alignment issues or stress concentrations.

## Summary Verdict

The 2017 Mercedes-Benz E500 offers a high-end driving experience filled with advanced luxury appointments. Prospective buyers should thoroughly inspect vehicle history records, test all electrical functions, and check for any signs of water intrusion or previous mechanical repairs.
