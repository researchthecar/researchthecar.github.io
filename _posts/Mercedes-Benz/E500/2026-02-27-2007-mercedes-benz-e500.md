# 2007 Mercedes-Benz E500

### Overview
The 2007 Mercedes-Benz E500, part of the W211 generation, is a luxury sedan known for its potent performance and refined driving experience. Powered by a robust V8 engine, it offers strong acceleration and a comfortable ride. While generally well-regarded for its build quality and advanced features for its time, like any vehicle, it has specific areas that have seen more attention from owners regarding reliability. This analysis focuses on documented common issues beyond standard maintenance, providing insights into potential concerns for prospective buyers or current owners.

---


## Engine

### 5.0L V8 (M113)
**Oil Cooler Seals Leak**
- **Symptoms:** Oil in coolant reservoir, coolant in oil, overheating.
- **Solution:** Replacement of oil cooler seals.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600

**Intake Manifold Gasket Leak**
- **Symptoms:** Vacuum leaks, rough idle, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of intake manifold gaskets.
- **Cost:** $400 - $700


## Transmission

### 5-Speed Automatic (722.6)
**Conductor Plate Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Harsh shifting, transmission slipping, failure to engage gears, 'limp mode'.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the transmission conductor plate.
- **Cost:** $600 - $1200

**Transmission Fluid Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Leaking fluid from transmission pan or cooler lines.
- **Solution:** Tightening pan bolts, replacing pan gasket, or repairing/replacing cooler lines.
- **Cost:** $200 - $500


## Suspension

### AirMatic Suspension
**Air Strut Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle sagging on one or more corners, uneven ride height, compressor running excessively.
- **Solution:** Replacement of leaking air struts or seals.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1500 per strut

**Air Compressor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Inability to raise or lower the vehicle, constant compressor operation.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the AirMatic compressor unit.
- **Cost:** $700 - $1300

## Electrical

### General Electrical System
**SAM Unit (Signal Acquisition Module) Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Intermittent electrical faults, malfunctioning lights, power window issues, battery drain.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of the SAM unit, often requiring programming.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1500

**Seat Control Module Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Malfunctioning power seats, seat memory issues.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the affected seat control module.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600

### Summary Verdict
The 2007 Mercedes-Benz E500 offers a compelling blend of luxury and performance. While its V8 engine and comfortable ride are highlights, potential owners should be aware of common issues. The transmission's conductor plate and the AirMatic suspension's air struts and compressor are known weak points that can lead to significant repair costs. Electrical gremlins, particularly related to the SAM unit, can also arise. Despite these potential concerns, many E500s have proven reliable with diligent maintenance and timely repairs. Addressing these specific issues proactively can help maintain the vehicle's luxury experience and overall dependability.
