**Introduction:**

The 2012 Mercedes-Benz E500 is a luxury sedan powered by a 4.7-liter V8 engine mated to a 7-speed automatic transmission. The E500 boasts 402 horsepower and 443 lb-ft of torque. Praised for its stylish design, spacious interior, and powerful performance, the E500 is also known for certain issues affecting various components.

**Engine**

**4.7L V8 Engine**

* **Intake Manifold Cracking:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Rough idling, power loss, check engine light.
    * Solution: Replace intake manifold.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
* **Timing Chain Rattle:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Metallic rattling noise during engine startup, especially in cold weather.
    * Solution: Replace timing chain and tensioner.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
* **Oil Leak from Rear Main Seal:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Oil dripping from the rear of the engine, visible on the ground.
    * Solution: Replace rear main seal.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+

**Transmission**

**7-Speed Automatic Transmission (7G-Tronic)**

* **Shifting Hesitation:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Transmission hesitates or slips when shifting gears.
    * Solution: Replace or reprogram transmission control module (TCM).
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
* **Torque Converter Shudder:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Vibrations or shuddering during acceleration or deceleration.
    * Solution: Replace torque converter.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
* **Transmission Fluid Leak:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Transmission fluid leaking from the transmission pan or seals.
    * Solution: Replace leaking seals or gaskets.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+

**Suspension**

* **Air Suspension Compressor Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Suspension system fails to raise or lower, check engine light.
    * Solution: Replace air suspension compressor.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
* **Control Arm Bushing Wear:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Noises from the front suspension when driving over bumps, uneven tire wear.
    * Solution: Replace worn bushings.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+

**Electrical**

* **Headlight Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: One or both headlights may fail to illuminate.
    * Solution: Replace headlight assembly or wiring harness.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,000+
* **Electrical Wiring Harness Faults:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Various electrical issues, such as flickering lights, malfunctioning gauges, or power windows not working.
    * Solution: Diagnose and repair or replace faulty wiring harness.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+

**Exterior**

* **Door Handle Malfunction:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Door handle fails to open or close the door.
    * Solution: Replace door handle assembly.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
* **Sunroof Seal Leak:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Water leaking into the cabin from the sunroof.
    * Solution: Replace or reseal sunroof seal.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+

**Interior**

* **Navigation System Malfunction:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Navigation system freezes, does not respond, or provides incorrect directions.
    * Solution: Replace or update navigation system module.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
* **Ventilated Seat Fan Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Ventilated seats do not blow air.
    * Solution: Replace fan motor or control module.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+

**Summary:**

The 2012 Mercedes-Benz E500 is a luxurious and powerful sedan, but it is not without its issues. While some problems, such as electrical wiring harness faults and door handle malfunctions, are relatively minor and inexpensive to fix, others like engine intake manifold cracking and transmission shift hesitation can be more costly and time-consuming to repair. Potential buyers should carefully consider these issues when evaluating an E500, as some of them could lead to significant expenses in the future.
