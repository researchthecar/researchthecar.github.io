**Introduction:**
The 2015 Mercedes-Benz E500 is a luxury sedan known for its advanced engineering, refined interior, and powerful performance. It features a 4.7L twin-turbocharged V8 engine that produces 402 horsepower and 443 lb-ft of torque. The E500 is available with a 7-speed automatic transmission and comes standard with rear-wheel drive. It offers a comfortable ride, excellent handling, and a spacious cabin. However, like any vehicle, it has its share of issues.

**Engine**

**4.7L Twin-Turbocharged V8**
* **Oil Filter Housing Leak:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Leaking oil from the oil filter housing or oil cooler. Can cause reduced oil levels and potential engine damage.
    * Solution: Replace oil filter housing or oil cooler.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Air Suspension Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Suspension system failures, resulting in a loss of ride height or stability.
    * Solution: Replace air suspension components, including air bladders, compressors, or sensors.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**7-Speed Automatic Transmission**
* **Transmission Slippage or Hesitation:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears, slipping, or hesitation during acceleration.
    * Solution: Replace or repair transmission components, including the valve body or clutch packs.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,500 - $4,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Active Body Control System Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Malfunctioning active body control system, affecting ride quality and handling.
    * Solution: Replace or repair active body control components, including sensors, valves, or hydraulic pumps.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Battery Discharge:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Battery drains prematurely, leaving the vehicle unable to start.
    * Solution: Diagnose and repair electrical system, replace battery if necessary.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Panel Gaps:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Uneven panel gaps, especially around the hood, trunk, or doors.
    * Solution: Adjust or replace body panels.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Climate Control Malfunction:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Inconsistent or malfunctioning climate control system, affecting temperature or airflow.
    * Solution: Repair or replace climate control components, such as the thermostat, sensors, or actuators.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2015 Mercedes-Benz E500 is a reliable and capable vehicle, but it has its share of potential issues that can be costly to repair. The most common engine and transmission problems center around leaks and slippage, while the suspension and electrical systems can experience failures. Interior issues are relatively minor, while some exterior issues can be cosmetic. Overall, the severity of the issues is moderate, and the majority of them can be resolved with repairs costing between $500 and $3,000+. However, some issues, such as transmission slippage or active suspension failures, can require more extensive and expensive repairs.
