# 2014 Mercedes-Benz E500

### Overview
The 2014 Mercedes-Benz E500, part of the W212 generation, is generally recognized for its robust engineering and luxury appointments. This model year benefits from Mercedes-Benz's extensive experience in producing executive sedans, aiming for a balance of performance, comfort, and long-term durability. While individual component failures can occur in any vehicle, the E500 from this era typically exhibits strong reliability, especially in its core powertrain and structural elements. Owners can expect a refined driving experience, though like any premium vehicle, maintenance and potential repairs can be more costly than average. This analysis focuses on documented common issues beyond standard wear and tear.

### AIRMATIC Air Suspension
**Air Strut Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle may sag on one or more corners, uneven ride height, warning lights on the dashboard.
- **Solution:** Replacement of leaking air strut(s).
- **Cost:** $800 - $1500 per corner

**Air Compressor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Inability to raise or lower the vehicle, constant running of the compressor, warning lights.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the air compressor unit.
- **Cost:** $600 - $1200

### Summary Verdict
The 2014 Mercedes-Benz E500 is a highly reliable luxury sedan, particularly in its powertrain and core electrical systems. The primary areas where owners might encounter issues are related to the optional AIRMATIC air suspension, with potential for leaks in air struts or compressor failure. These are not universal but are documented concerns for vehicles equipped with this system. Beyond these specific components, the E500 generally holds up well, offering a dependable ownership experience for a vehicle in its class. Regular maintenance is key to preserving its performance and longevity.
