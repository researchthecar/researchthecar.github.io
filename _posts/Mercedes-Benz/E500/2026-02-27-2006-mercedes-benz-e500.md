# 2006 Mercedes-Benz E500

### Overview
The 2006 Mercedes-Benz E500, part of the W211 generation, is a luxury sedan known for its potent V8 engine and refined driving experience. While generally well-regarded for its performance and comfort, like any vehicle of its age and complexity, it has specific areas that have seen more attention from owners regarding reliability. This analysis focuses on documented common issues beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential concerns for prospective buyers or current owners. The E500's advanced systems, particularly in its electronics and powertrain, are key areas to consider when evaluating its long-term dependability.

---


## Engine

### 5.0L V8 (M113)
**Oil Cooler Seals Leak**
- **Symptoms:** Oil in coolant reservoir, coolant in oil, overheating.
- **Solution:** Replacement of oil cooler seals.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600

**Intake Manifold Gasket Leak**
- **Symptoms:** Vacuum leaks, rough idle, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of intake manifold gaskets.
- **Cost:** $400 - $800


## Transmission

### 5-Speed Automatic (722.6)
**Conductor Plate Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Harsh shifting, transmission slipping, failure to engage gears, 'limp mode'.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the transmission conductor plate and fluid service.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1500

**Transmission Fluid Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Leaking fluid from transmission pan or cooler lines, low fluid level.
- **Solution:** Tightening pan bolts, replacing pan gasket, or replacing leaking cooler lines.
- **Cost:** $200 - $500


## Suspension

### AirMatic Air Suspension
**Air Strut Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle sagging on one or more corners, 'vehicle too high' or 'vehicle too low' warning messages.
- **Solution:** Replacement of leaking air struts.
- **Cost:** $700 - $1200 per strut

**Air Compressor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Inability to raise or lower the vehicle, compressor not engaging.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the air suspension compressor.
- **Cost:** $500 - $900


### Standard Coil Spring Suspension
**No Major Issues**
- **Symptoms:** No widespread issues reported.
- **Solution:** No widespread issues reported.
- **Cost:** No widespread issues reported.


## Electrical

### General Electrical System
**SAM (Signal Acquisition Module) Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Intermittent electrical faults, malfunctioning lights, power window issues, dashboard warning lights.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of the SAM module, often requiring programming.
- **Cost:** $600 - $1200

**Headlight Ballast Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Xenon headlights not turning on or flickering.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the headlight ballast.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600 per ballast

## Interior

### Cabin Components
**Seat Control Module Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Power seats not functioning, memory seat issues.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the seat control module.
- **Cost:** $400 - $700

**Sunroof Mechanism Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Sunroof not opening or closing properly, unusual noises.
- **Solution:** Lubrication, adjustment, or replacement of sunroof motor/mechanism.
- **Cost:** $300 - $700

### Summary Verdict
The 2006 Mercedes-Benz E500 offers a compelling blend of luxury and performance, but potential buyers should be aware of its specific reliability considerations. The V8 engine is generally robust, though common leaks from oil cooler seals and intake manifold gaskets can occur. Transmission issues, particularly with the conductor plate on the 5-speed automatic, are a notable concern and can be costly to repair. The optional AirMatic suspension is a complex system prone to leaks and compressor failures, making the standard suspension a more reliable choice if available. Electrical gremlins, often stemming from the SAM module, and specific component failures like headlight ballasts are also documented. While the exterior and interior are generally well-built, some interior electrical components like seat modules can fail. Regular maintenance and proactive inspection of these known weak points are crucial for maintaining the E500's dependability.
