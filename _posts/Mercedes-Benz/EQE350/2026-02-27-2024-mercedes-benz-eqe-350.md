# 2024 Mercedes-Benz EQE 350+ Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2024 Mercedes-Benz EQE 350+ represents an advanced entry into the luxury electric vehicle segment, offering a sleek aerodynamic design and refined cabin technology. As a relatively new luxury EV model, prospective used buyers should weigh its cutting-edge powertrain technology against the typical complexities associated with modern software-driven luxury cars.

---

## Engine

### Permanent Magnet Synchronous Motor (Rear-Wheel Drive)

This rear-mounted permanent magnet synchronous motor produces approximately 288 horsepower and 417 lb-ft of instant torque. It delivers smooth, silent acceleration while serving as the primary propulsion and regenerative braking unit for the EQE 350+.

## Transmission

### Single-Speed Fixed Gear Transmission

A single-speed direct-drive transmission efficiently transfers power from the electric motor to the rear wheels. The system operates seamlessly without traditional gear shifts, contributing to a refined and maintenance-free powertrain experience.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V372000: Electrical System:Propulsion System:Traction Battery:Management System/Energy Control Module (Bms/Becm):Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=EQE+350%2B&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** The battery management system software may cause the high-voltage battery to shut down, which can result in a sudden loss of drive power. A sudden loss of drive power increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the battery management system software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V366000: Back Over Prevention:Warnings:External/Pedestrian Alert](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=EQE+350%2B&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** Software in the Acoustic Vehicle Alerting System (AVAS) may cause an incorrect external pedestrian warning sound while reversing. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 141, "Minimum Sound Requirements for Hybrid Electric Vehicles."
