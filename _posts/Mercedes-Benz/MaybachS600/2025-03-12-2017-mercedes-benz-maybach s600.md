The 2017 Mercedes-Benz Maybach S600 represents the pinnacle of luxury and refinement. Powered by a robust 6.0L Twin-Turbo V12 engine producing 523 horsepower and 612 lb-ft of torque, this vehicle delivers effortless performance. Although the V12 is the standard engine, other S-Class models offer a range of engine options, including V8s and even hybrid powertrains. Owners often praise the Maybach S600 for its opulent interior, smooth ride, and advanced technology. Common dislikes often revolve around the high maintenance costs and the complexity of the vehicle's systems, which can lead to expensive repairs.

**Engine**

**6.0L Twin-Turbo V12**
*   **Oil Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks may develop from various seals and gaskets on the engine, leading to a burning oil smell or visible oil spots under the vehicle.
    *   Solution: Replacing the affected seals and gaskets.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+ depending on the location of the leak and the labor involved.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Ignition Coil Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Misfires, rough idling, and a check engine light can indicate a failing ignition coil.
    *   Solution: Replacing the faulty ignition coil(s).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+ per coil.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Turbocharger Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Whining noises from the engine, reduced power, and a check engine light could indicate issues with the turbochargers.
    *   Solution: Repair or replacement of the turbocharger(s).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $3,000 - $7,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**7G-TRONIC Automatic Transmission**

*   **Transmission Slipping/Harsh Shifting**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The 7G-TRONIC transmission, may experience slipping between gears or harsh shifting, especially when cold. This can be caused by worn valve body components or low transmission fluid.
    *   Solution: Valve body replacement, transmission service, or complete transmission rebuild.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $6,000+ depending on severity.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Torque Converter Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Shuddering or vibrations at low speeds, particularly during acceleration.
    *   Solution: Torque converter replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Airmatic Suspension Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The Airmatic suspension can experience leaks or compressor failures, resulting in a sagging vehicle, uneven ride height, or a suspension malfunction warning light.
    *   Solution: Replacing leaking air struts, repairing or replacing the air compressor, or addressing any air line leaks.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $4,000+ per strut, or $500 - $1,500+ for the compressor.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **COMAND System Malfunctions**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The COMAND infotainment system can experience glitches, screen freezes, or complete failures.
    *   Solution: Software updates or replacement of the COMAND unit.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $3,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Battery Drain Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Premature battery drain, often caused by a parasitic draw from one or more of the vehicle's electronic systems.
    *   Solution: Identifying and repairing the source of the parasitic draw, potentially replacing faulty modules.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,500+ depending on the source of the drain.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Sensor Failures**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Failures of various sensors such as oxygen, ABS, or crankshaft sensors can lead to performance issues and warning lights.
    *   Solution: Replacing the faulty sensor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+ per sensor.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Panoramic Sunroof Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The panoramic sunroof may experience motor failures or track issues, leading to difficulties opening or closing.
    *   Solution: Repairing or replacing the sunroof motor or track components.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Seat Control Module Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Malfunctioning seat adjustments, heating, or massage functions.
    *   Solution: Replacing the seat control module.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Ambient Lighting Malfunctions**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Sections of the ambient lighting system failing to illuminate or displaying incorrect colors.
    *   Solution: Diagnosing and replacing faulty LED modules or wiring.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Rear Entertainment System Problems**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Freezing, screen issues, or connectivity problems with the rear entertainment system.
    *   Solution: Software updates or component replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $2,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2017 Mercedes-Benz Maybach S600, while offering unmatched luxury, is prone to a range of issues that can result in significant repair costs. Engine oil leaks and ignition coil failures can be costly to address. The Airmatic suspension and electrical system, including the COMAND unit, also present potential problems. Owners should be prepared for potentially expensive repairs, especially concerning the air suspension and complex electronic components. The severity of these issues ranges from minor inconveniences to major mechanical failures, potentially requiring thousands of dollars to rectify.
