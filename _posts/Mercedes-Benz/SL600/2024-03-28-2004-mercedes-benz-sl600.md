**Introduction**

The 2004 Mercedes-Benz SL600 is a high-performance luxury roadster that offers a powerful V12 engine and a comfortable and stylish cabin. It is available with a 5-speed automatic transmission and rear-wheel drive. The SL600 produces 493 horsepower and 590 lb-ft of torque, allowing it to accelerate from 0 to 60 mph in 4.6 seconds. Despite its impressive performance, the SL600 has also been plagued by numerous issues.

**Engine**

* **V12 Engine**
    * **Intake Manifold Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: The intake manifold can crack, causing a loss of power and a rough idle.
        * Solution: Replace the intake manifold.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,000+
        * Recalls: None Found
    * **Valvetrain Noise**
        * Description/Symptoms: The valvetrain can develop excessive noise due to worn or broken components.
        * Solution: Inspect and replace the affected valvetrain components.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000
        * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

* **5-Speed Automatic Transmission**
    * **Transmission Slipping**
        * Description/Symptoms: The transmission may slip or hesitate when shifting gears.
        * Solution: Replace or repair the affected transmission components.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,500 - $5,000+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **No Issues Found**

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain**
    * Description/Symptoms: The battery may drain prematurely, causing the car to lose power.
    * Solution: Diagnose and repair the source of the drain, such as a faulty alternator or parasitic battery drain.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Electrical Glitches**
    * Description/Symptoms: The car may experience intermittent electrical issues, such as flickering lights or malfunctioning dashboard components.
    * Solution: Diagnose and repair the affected electrical components or wiring.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **No Issues Found**

**Interior**

* **Power Seat Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The power seats may malfunction, failing to move or adjust properly.
    * Solution: Replace or repair the affected seat motor or switch.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2004 Mercedes-Benz SL600 is a luxurious and powerful roadster, but it is also prone to significant issues. The engine, transmission, electrical system, and interior have all been reported to have problems. The severity and cost of these issues can vary widely, but they can be significant in some cases. Overall, the SL600 is a high-maintenance vehicle that may not be suitable for all buyers.
