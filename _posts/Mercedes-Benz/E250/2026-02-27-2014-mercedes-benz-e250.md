# 2014 Mercedes-Benz E250

### Overview
The 2014 Mercedes-Benz E250, part of the W212 generation, is generally regarded as a solid luxury sedan with a focus on comfort and refinement. It typically features a robust powertrain and a well-built interior. While Mercedes-Benz vehicles are known for their advanced technology and premium feel, like any complex machine, they can encounter specific issues over time. This analysis focuses on documented, non-wear-and-tear related problems reported by owners and technicians, excluding routine maintenance. The E250, particularly with its diesel engine options, offers good fuel economy for its class, but potential buyers should be aware of specific component vulnerabilities that may arise with age and mileage.

---


## Engine

### 2.1L OM651 Turbocharged Diesel
**Diesel Particulate Filter (DPF) Regeneration Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Check engine light, reduced engine power, increased fuel consumption, potential stalling.
- **Solution:** Forced regeneration, DPF cleaning, or replacement.
- **Cost:** $300 - $1500

**EGR Valve Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Rough idling, poor acceleration, increased emissions, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Cleaning or replacement of the EGR valve.
- **Cost:** $400 - $800

**Turbocharger Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of power, whistling noises, black smoke from exhaust.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of the turbocharger assembly.
- **Cost:** $1000 - $2500


## Transmission

### 7G-TRONIC PLUS (7-Speed Automatic)
**Transmission Control Module (TCM) Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Harsh shifting, transmission slipping, inability to select gears, warning lights.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the TCM.
- **Cost:** $600 - $1200

**Torque Converter Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Shuddering at low speeds, slipping, delayed engagement.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the torque converter.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1800

## Electrical

### General Electrical System
**Sensor Failures (e.g., ABS, Crankshaft Position)**
- **Symptoms:** Warning lights on the dashboard (ABS, ESP, Check Engine), erratic performance.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and replacement of faulty sensors.
- **Cost:** $200 - $600

**Battery Drain Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Difficulty starting, battery warning light, electrical components malfunctioning.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis to find parasitic draw, replacement of faulty modules or battery.
- **Cost:** $300 - $900

## Interior

### Cabin Electronics and Comfort
**Infotainment System Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Screen freezing, unresponsive buttons, audio issues.
- **Solution:** Software updates or replacement of the head unit.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1500

**Climate Control Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** AC not blowing cold, heater not working, inconsistent temperature.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and repair/replacement of blower motor, blend door actuators, or AC components.
- **Cost:** $300 - $1000

### Summary Verdict
The 2014 Mercedes-Benz E250 is a well-engineered vehicle with a generally good reliability record, especially concerning its core mechanical components like the engine and transmission when properly maintained. However, like many luxury vehicles of this era, it is susceptible to electrical gremlins and issues with complex systems such as the DPF and EGR on the diesel engine, and potential glitches within the infotainment and climate control systems. Owners should budget for potential repairs related to these areas, particularly as the vehicle ages. Regular servicing and prompt attention to warning lights are crucial for maintaining its long-term reliability and avoiding more costly repairs.
