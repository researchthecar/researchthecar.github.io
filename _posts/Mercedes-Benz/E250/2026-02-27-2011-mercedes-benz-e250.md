# 2011 Mercedes-Benz E250

### Overview
The 2011 Mercedes-Benz E250, part of the W212 generation, is generally regarded as a solid vehicle with a focus on comfort and refinement. While Mercedes-Benz vehicles often come with a premium price tag for maintenance, the E250 from this era offers a good balance of luxury and performance. Reliability is typically strong, but like any complex German automobile, specific components can occasionally present issues. This analysis focuses on documented common problems beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential areas of concern for owners and prospective buyers. Understanding these potential issues can help in proactive maintenance and budgeting for repairs.

---


## Engine

### 2.1L OM651 Diesel
**Turbocharger Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of power, whistling noises, black smoke from exhaust.
- **Solution:** Turbocharger replacement or repair.
- **Cost:** $1,500 - $3,000

**Diesel Particulate Filter (DPF) Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Check engine light, reduced engine performance, increased fuel consumption.
- **Solution:** DPF regeneration or replacement.
- **Cost:** $1,000 - $2,500

**Glow Plug Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Difficulty starting, especially in cold weather, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of faulty glow plugs and control module.
- **Cost:** $400 - $800


## Transmission

### 7G-Tronic (722.9) Automatic
**Transmission Control Module (TCM) Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Harsh shifting, slipping gears, inability to select gears, warning lights.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the TCM.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1,500

**Torque Converter Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Shuddering at low speeds, slipping, delayed engagement.
- **Solution:** Torque converter replacement.
- **Cost:** $1,200 - $2,500

## Electrical

### General Electrical System
**Sensor Failures (e.g., ABS, Crankshaft)**
- **Symptoms:** Illuminated warning lights (ABS, ESP, Check Engine), erratic behavior of systems.
- **Solution:** Replacement of faulty sensors.
- **Cost:** $200 - $600 per sensor

**Headlight Control Module Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Headlights not working, flickering, or turning off unexpectedly.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the headlight control module.
- **Cost:** $300 - $700

## Interior

### Cabin Electronics and Comfort
**Seat Adjustment Motor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Power seats not moving or adjusting properly.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the seat adjustment motor.

**Infotainment System Glitches**

### Summary Verdict
The 2011 Mercedes-Benz E250 (W212) demonstrates good overall reliability, particularly in its chassis and exterior components. The primary areas that may require attention are the diesel engine's emissions system (DPF) and turbocharger, as well as potential issues with the 7G-Tronic transmission's control module and torque converter. Electrical components, such as sensors and headlight modules, can also be a source of concern. While not indicative of widespread failure, these are the most commonly reported non-wear-and-tear issues. Proactive maintenance and addressing early symptoms can mitigate costs and ensure the longevity of this luxury sedan.
