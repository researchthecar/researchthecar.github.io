# 2016 Mercedes-Benz E250 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2016 Mercedes-Benz E-Class brings luxury, advanced safety features, and refined driving dynamics to the midsize luxury segment. Prospective buyers should inspect used models carefully for potential electrical glitches, steering assist failures, and body integrity concerns like windshield seal leaks.

---

## Engine

### 2.1L Turbo Diesel 4-Cylinder

A 2.1-liter twin-turbocharged diesel 4-cylinder engine producing approximately 195 horsepower and 369 lb-ft of torque. It offers exceptional fuel economy and strong low-end torque for highway cruising.

### 3.0L V6 Turbocharged

A 3.0-liter twin-turbocharged V6 gas engine delivering around 329 horsepower and 354 lb-ft of torque. It provides smooth, robust acceleration suited to executive-class travel.

**Fuel Odor and Leak**
- **Symptoms:** A strong fuel odor is noticeable while the vehicle is idling, indicating a potential leak in the fuel delivery system.
- **Solution:** Inspect fuel lines, pumps, and injectors for leaks and replace compromised seals or assembly components.

## Transmission

### 7-Speed Automatic Transmission

A 7-speed automatic transmission paired with rear-wheel drive or 4MATIC all-wheel drive, known for smooth gear changes and reliable daily performance.

## Brakes

### Parking Brake System

Standard braking components provide solid stopping power, though electronic parking brake actuators require proper maintenance.

**Electric Parking Brake Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Failure or fault codes within the electronic parking brake mechanism requiring cable or actuator replacement.
- **Solution:** Inspect parking brake cables, motors, and control modules, replacing worn or failed mechanical components.

## Steering

### Power Steering System

Electronic power steering systems generally offer precise handling, though occasional assist failures require immediate diagnostic attention.

**Steering Assist Failure**
- **Symptoms:** A warning message appears on the instrument cluster and the vehicle suddenly loses power steering assist, making the steering wheel very heavy and difficult to turn.
- **Solution:** Have the steering assembly, control module, and electrical connections diagnosed and serviced or replaced by a qualified technician.

## Exterior

### Windshield and Seals

Exterior trim and glass installations require intact seals to prevent cabin water leaks and subsequent interior damage.

**Windshield Water Intrusion**
- **Symptoms:** Water droplets form on the interior headliner or passenger side floor after rain, and the windshield glass may exhibit looseness when pushed outward.
- **Solution:** Remove and professionally re-seal or replace the windshield to restore proper structural bonding and weatherproofing.

## Safety

### Electronic Safety & Control Systems

The electrical architecture controls numerous sophisticated driver-assistance features, but complex wiring can occasionally trigger false warnings or sensor faults.

**Brake and Assist Warning Lights**
- **Symptoms:** Unusual knocking sounds upon starting accompanied by illuminated check engine, automatic brake, and adaptive cruise control warning lights.
- **Solution:** Scan the vehicle control modules for stored diagnostic trouble codes and inspect faulty sensors or wiring harnesses.

## Summary Verdict

The 2016 model offers a compelling mix of luxury, advanced technology, and powertrain variety. While owners have reported issues ranging from electronic power steering assist failures to windshield seal leaks, a thorough pre-purchase inspection can catch these potential concerns before finalizing a deal.
