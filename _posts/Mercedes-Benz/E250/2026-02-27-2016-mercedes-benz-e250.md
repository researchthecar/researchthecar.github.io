# 2016 Mercedes-Benz E250

### Overview
The 2016 Mercedes-Benz E250, part of the W212 generation's facelift, is generally regarded as a reliable luxury sedan. It offers a blend of comfort, performance, and advanced technology. This model year benefits from Mercedes-Benz's engineering prowess, with most owners reporting a positive ownership experience. However, like any vehicle, certain components can experience issues over time. This analysis focuses on documented technical reliability concerns beyond standard maintenance, providing insights into potential problem areas and their associated costs for different configurations.

---


## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4 (Gasoline)
**Turbocharger Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of power, whistling noises, increased oil consumption, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Turbocharger replacement or repair.
- **Cost:** $1,500 - $3,000

**PCV Valve Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Rough idle, engine misfires, oil leaks, potential for increased oil consumption.
- **Solution:** PCV valve replacement.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600


### 2.1L Turbocharged Inline-4 Diesel (OM651)
**Diesel Particulate Filter (DPF) Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Reduced engine power, increased fuel consumption, check engine light, potential for engine stalling.
- **Solution:** DPF regeneration (forced or passive), DPF cleaning, or replacement.
- **Cost:** $1,000 - $2,500

**EGR Valve Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Rough idling, poor acceleration, increased emissions, check engine light.
- **Solution:** EGR valve cleaning or replacement.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1,000

**Timing Chain Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Rattling noise from the engine, especially on startup, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Timing chain and tensioner replacement.
- **Cost:** $1,200 - $2,500


## Transmission

### 7G-TRONIC PLUS (7-Speed Automatic)
**Transmission Fluid Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Leaking fluid, rough shifting, slipping gears, warning lights.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of seals, lines, or transmission pan.
- **Cost:** $400 - $1,200

**Torque Converter Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Shuddering during acceleration, slipping, delayed engagement.
- **Solution:** Torque converter replacement.
- **Cost:** $1,000 - $2,500

### Summary Verdict
The 2016 Mercedes-Benz E250 is a well-built vehicle with a generally strong reliability record. The most common areas for potential concern lie within the powertrain, particularly with the diesel engine's emissions system (DPF, EGR) and potential turbocharger issues on the gasoline variant. Transmission issues are less frequent but can be costly if they arise. Electrical gremlins, such as infotainment glitches, are also noted. However, many owners experience trouble-free operation, especially with regular maintenance. Addressing potential issues proactively can help maintain the E250's luxury driving experience and longevity.
