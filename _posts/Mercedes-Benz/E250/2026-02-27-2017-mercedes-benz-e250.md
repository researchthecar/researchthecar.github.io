# 2017 Mercedes-Benz E250 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used Mercedes-Benz, understanding its mechanical layout and common trouble spots helps set realistic maintenance expectations. Luxury European vehicles require diligent upkeep, and reviewing owner feedback highlights key areas to inspect before finalizing a purchase.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4

This 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine generates approximately 241 horsepower and 273 lb-ft of torque, providing brisk acceleration for daily driving. It balances efficiency with luxury performance, though routine maintenance and prompt attention to minor leaks are essential for long-term reliability.

## Transmission

### 9-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 9-speed automatic transmission delivers smooth gear changes and optimizes highway fuel economy across rear-wheel and all-wheel-drive configurations. Regular fluid services are vital to maintain shift quality and prevent internal wear over high mileage.

## Steering

### Steering Assist & Electronic Control

Issues related to electronic power assist can occasionally reduce steering feedback and increase effort.

**Steering Assist Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may experience a sudden loss of power steering assist, accompanied by increased physical steering effort or warning messages on the instrument cluster.
- **Solution:** Have the electronic steering control module and wiring inspected by a qualified technician to determine if component replacement or software updates are required.

## Interior

### Sunroof & Cabin Sealing

Cabin electronics and weatherstripping require careful observation to prevent moisture intrusion.

**Panoramic Sunroof Glass Fracture and Water Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** The panoramic sunroof glass may shatter unexpectedly while driving, potentially allowing water to enter the cabin and drain toward electrical components.
- **Solution:** Inspect the sunroof assembly for stress cracks, clear drainage channels regularly, and replace damaged glass panels or seals promptly.

## Safety

### Airbag & Restraint Systems

Restraint system sensors and warning indicators should be verified during a pre-purchase inspection.

**Airbag Warning Light & Seat Sensor Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Illumination of the airbag warning light accompanied by diagnostic trouble codes indicating open circuits in seat cushion sensors or squibs.
- **Solution:** Scan the supplemental restraint system computer, inspect seat wiring harnesses, and replace faulty weight-sensing control units or clock springs.

Overall, evaluating a used luxury vehicle involves careful attention to both powertrain health and electronic accessories.
