# 2017 Mercedes-Benz E250

### Overview
The 2017 Mercedes-Benz E250, part of the W213 generation, is generally regarded as a reliable luxury sedan. It offers a blend of comfort, technology, and performance, powered by a refined turbocharged four-cylinder engine. While Mercedes-Benz vehicles are known for their sophisticated engineering, like any complex machine, certain components can experience issues over time. This analysis focuses on documented technical reliability concerns beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential problem areas for owners and prospective buyers. The E250 aims to deliver a premium driving experience with a strong emphasis on build quality and advanced features.

---


## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4 (M274)
**Oil Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Visible oil stains under the vehicle, burning oil smell, low oil level warnings.
- **Solution:** Replacement of seals and gaskets, such as valve cover gasket or oil pan gasket.
- **Cost:** $300 - $700

**Turbocharger Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of power, whistling noises from the engine bay, increased exhaust smoke.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of the turbocharger unit and associated plumbing.
- **Cost:** $1500 - $3000


## Transmission

### 9G-TRONIC 9-Speed Automatic
**Transmission Fluid Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Leaking fluid from the transmission pan or cooler lines, rough shifting, slipping gears.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of seals, gaskets, or transmission lines.
- **Cost:** $400 - $1000

**Software Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Erratic shifting patterns, delayed engagement, 'limp mode' activation.
- **Solution:** Software update or reprogramming of the transmission control module (TCM).
- **Cost:** $150 - $300


## Suspension

### Standard Air Body Control (Optional)
**Air Suspension Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle sagging on one or more corners, 'air suspension malfunction' warning light, uneven ride height.
- **Solution:** Replacement of leaking air springs, lines, or compressor.
- **Cost:** $800 - $2500

### Summary Verdict
The 2017 Mercedes-Benz E250 demonstrates good overall reliability, with the powertrain generally performing well. The most commonly reported technical issues tend to be related to the optional Air Body Control suspension, potential oil leaks from the 2.0L turbo engine, and occasional electrical glitches within the infotainment system. Transmission issues are infrequent but can involve fluid leaks or software anomalies. Owners should be aware of these potential areas and ensure regular maintenance. Despite these points, the E250 remains a solid choice in its segment, offering a luxurious and technologically advanced driving experience with a reasonable expectation of dependability when properly cared for.
