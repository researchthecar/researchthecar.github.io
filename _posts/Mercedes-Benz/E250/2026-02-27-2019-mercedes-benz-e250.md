# 2019 Mercedes-Benz E250 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2019 Mercedes-Benz E-Class delivers sophisticated luxury, advanced technology, and a refined ride. Prospective buyers should review owner-reported issues and safety system alerts carefully when evaluating a used model.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbo Inline-4

A turbocharged 2.0-liter four-cylinder engine producing around 241 horsepower, offering a balance of efficiency and daily drivability for base models.

### 3.0L Turbo V6 / Inline-6

A smooth and powerful six-cylinder engine option delivering stronger acceleration and highway passing power for higher-tier trims.

## Transmission

### 9-Speed Automatic Transmission

A 9-speed automatic transmission paired with rear-wheel drive or 4MATIC all-wheel drive, designed for seamless gear shifts and optimized fuel economy.

## Electrical

### Back Over Prevention

Visibility aids and rearview camera systems occasionally experience display interruptions.

**Backup Camera Display Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The rearview camera image distorts, cuts out, or fails to display entirely while the vehicle is in reverse.
- **Solution:** Inspect camera wiring harnesses and software modules; replace faulty rearview camera components as needed.

## Safety

### Seat Belt

Owner reports highlight occasional concerns regarding seat belt sensors and latch operation.

**Seat Belt Fastening and Warning Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers or passengers experience warnings or mechanical complaints regarding seat belt latching and detection.
- **Solution:** Have the seat belt buckle assembly and sensors inspected and replaced by a qualified technician if faulty.
