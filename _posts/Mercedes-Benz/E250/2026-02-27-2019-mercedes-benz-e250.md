# 2019 Mercedes-Benz E250

### Overview
The 2019 Mercedes-Benz E250, part of the W213 generation, is generally regarded as a reliable luxury sedan, offering a blend of comfort, technology, and performance. It typically comes equipped with a potent yet efficient turbocharged four-cylinder engine paired with a smooth automatic transmission. While Mercedes-Benz vehicles are known for their sophisticated engineering, like any complex machine, some specific areas may experience issues over time. This report focuses on documented technical reliability concerns beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential problem areas for owners and prospective buyers.

---


## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4 (M274/M264)
**Oil Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Visible oil stains under the vehicle, burning oil smell, low oil level warnings.
- **Solution:** Replacement of seals, gaskets, or oil pan.
- **Cost:** $300 - $700

**PCV Valve Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Rough idle, increased oil consumption, potential engine stalling, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the Positive Crankcase Ventilation (PCV) valve.
- **Cost:** $200 - $500


## Transmission

### 9G-TRONIC 9-Speed Automatic
**Harsh Shifting / Hesitation**
- **Symptoms:** Jerky gear changes, delayed engagement, or hesitation during acceleration.
- **Solution:** Software update, transmission fluid flush and filter replacement, or internal component repair.
- **Cost:** $400 - $1500


## Suspension

### Standard/Air Body Control
**Air Suspension Leaks (if equipped)**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle sagging on one side, 'Air suspension temporarily unavailable' warning message.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of air springs, lines, or compressor.
- **Cost:** $800 - $2500


## Electrical

### Infotainment System
**Screen Glitches/Freezing**
- **Symptoms:** Infotainment screen becomes unresponsive, displays errors, or freezes.
- **Solution:** Software update or replacement of the head unit.
- **Cost:** $500 - $2000

### Summary Verdict
The 2019 Mercedes-Benz E250 demonstrates good overall reliability, with most reported issues being relatively minor and addressable. The engine and transmission are generally robust, though potential for oil leaks and occasional transmission shifting anomalies exists. Electrical gremlins, particularly with the infotainment system and battery drain, are the most common concerns. Suspension issues are infrequent, with air suspension components being the primary area of concern if equipped. Owners should be aware of these potential points and ensure regular maintenance to maximize longevity and minimize unexpected repair costs.
