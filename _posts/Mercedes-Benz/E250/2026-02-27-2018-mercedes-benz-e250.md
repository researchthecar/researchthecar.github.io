# 2018 Mercedes-Benz E250 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2018 Mercedes-Benz E-Class brings luxury, advanced technology, and refined performance to the executive car segment. Prospective used buyers should weigh its premium driving dynamics and comfort against potential mechanical and electrical complexities as vehicles age. Reviewing service records and checking for completed recall work are strongly recommended before purchase.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4

A 2.0-liter turbocharged inline-4 engine producing approximately 241 horsepower and 273 lb-ft of torque, offering a balance of efficiency and punchy daily performance. Routine maintenance is crucial to ensure long-term reliability and prevent premature wear.

### 3.0L Biturbo V6

A potent 3.0-liter biturbo V6 engine delivering around 329 to 362 horsepower depending on the tune, providing smooth and authoritative acceleration. Buyers should look for well-maintained models with documented oil change histories to avoid performance-robbing buildup.

## Transmission

### 9-Speed Automatic Transmission

A 9-speed automatic transmission paired with rear-wheel or 4MATIC all-wheel-drive configurations, designed to maximize highway efficiency and smoothness. While generally robust, periodic fluid service is vital to prevent internal control module and valve body wear.

**Transmission Shifting and Limp Mode**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle may abruptly shift into neutral, experience delayed or harsh engagement between drive and reverse, or enter limp mode due to electronic valve body failure.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified technician diagnose the electronic valve body or internal transmission components; replacement or specialized electronic repair may be required.

## Suspension

### Wheels and Tires

Suspension and wheel assemblies are tuned for responsive handling and ride comfort but can be sensitive to rough road conditions, especially on models equipped with low-profile performance tires.

**Wheel Cracking and Tire Blistering**
- **Symptoms:** Factory alloy wheels may develop structural cracks under normal driving conditions, or tires can exhibit tread blistering and premature wear.
- **Solution:** Inspect wheels for hidden fractures and replace damaged rims or compromised tires immediately.

## Electrical

### Infotainment and Digital Dash

Manages the vehicle's extensive suite of screens, driver aids, and connectivity features. electrical issues can occasionally surface, requiring professional diagnostic checks.

**Backup Camera and Display Blanking**
- **Symptoms:** The digital dashboard or central infotainment screen may intermittently go completely black, freeze, or display a camera error icon when engaging reverse.
- **Solution:** Inspect software calibration, wiring harnesses, and camera modules; dealership software updates or component replacement may be necessary.

## Safety

### Seat Belts

Comprehensive safety restraint systems designed to protect occupants, though occasional electromechanical retractor failures have been reported by owners.

**Seat Belt Retractor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Driver or passenger seat belts fail to fully retract, leaving dangerous slack across the occupant's chest and lap.
- **Solution:** Have an authorized dealer inspect the seat belt assembly and replace the faulty seat belt motor or retractor mechanism.

## Summary Verdict

The 2018 Mercedes-Benz E-Class offers a luxurious, feature-packed driving experience, but used buyers should remain vigilant regarding potential transmission electronic issues, electrical screen glitches, and wheel durability. Prioritize vehicles with thorough maintenance records, and ensure a pre-purchase inspection is performed by a qualified European car specialist to catch any hidden concerns.
