# 2007 Mercedes-Benz CLK550

### Overview
The 2007 Mercedes-Benz CLK550, part of the C209 generation, is a luxury grand tourer known for its potent V8 engine and refined driving experience. While generally well-regarded for its performance and comfort, like any vehicle of its age, it can experience specific issues. This analysis focuses on documented technical reliability concerns beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential problem areas for owners and prospective buyers. The CLK550 was offered with a single, robust powertrain configuration, simplifying the assessment of engine and transmission reliability.

---


## Engine

### 5.5L M273 V8
**Intake Manifold Runner Flap Actuator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Check Engine Light illumination, rough idle, reduced engine power, potential for intake manifold damage if flap breaks off.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the intake manifold or repair of the actuator mechanism.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1200

**Oil Cooler Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Visible oil leaks around the oil cooler housing, potential for coolant contamination in oil or vice-versa.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the oil cooler and associated seals.
- **Cost:** $400 - $800


## Transmission

### 7G-TRONIC 7-Speed Automatic (722.9)
**Transmission Control Module (TCM) Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Harsh shifting, inability to select gears, limp mode activation, transmission warning lights.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the TCM, often requiring programming.
- **Cost:** $1000 - $2500

**Valve Body Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Erratic shifting, delayed engagement, slipping gears.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of the valve body assembly.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1800

## Electrical

### General Electrical System
**SAM Unit (Signal Acquisition Module) Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** Intermittent electrical gremlins, non-functional lights, power window issues, dashboard warning lights.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and potential repair or replacement of the SAM unit.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1500

**Seat Control Module Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Malfunctioning power seats, memory seat issues.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the affected seat control module.
- **Cost:** $300 - $700

## Interior

### Cabin Components
**Headliner Sagging**
- **Symptoms:** Fabric on the headliner detaches and sags.
- **Solution:** Re-gluing or replacement of the headliner.
- **Cost:** $300 - $700

**Climate Control Actuator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Inability to control temperature or airflow direction, unusual noises from the dashboard.
- **Solution:** Replacement of faulty HVAC blend door actuators.
- **Cost:** $200 - $500

### Summary Verdict
The 2007 Mercedes-Benz CLK550 is a capable luxury coupe with a generally solid mechanical foundation, particularly its potent V8 engine. However, as with many German luxury vehicles of this era, potential buyers should be aware of specific electrical and transmission-related issues. The 7G-TRONIC transmission can be a source of costly repairs, with TCM and valve body problems being the most common. Electrical gremlins, often stemming from the SAM unit, are also a recurring concern. While the engine itself is robust, the intake manifold runner flap actuator is a known weak point. Routine maintenance and proactive inspection of these areas are crucial for maintaining the CLK550's reliability and value.
