# 2016 Mercedes-Benz GLA250 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used Mercedes-Benz GLA250, buyers will find a stylish and agile luxury crossover that combines hatchback-like driving dynamics with raised ride height. However, prospective owners should review owner-reported reliability trends carefully, particularly regarding powertrain behavior and onboard electronics. While many drivers enjoy its fuel economy and punchy turbocharged performance, attention to maintenance history is essential to avoid unexpected repair bills.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4

Powered by a 2.0-liter turbocharged inline-4 engine making roughly 208 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque, this powerplant offers brisk acceleration and solid efficiency. It generally runs strong when properly maintained, though auxiliary emissions and crankcase components warrant close inspection.

**Crankcase Ventilation Valve Failure (P052E)**
- **Symptoms:** Illuminated check engine light and stored fault code P052E indicating a regulator valve performance issue.
- **Solution:** Inspect the positive crankcase ventilation (PCV) system and replace the faulty regulator valve assembly.

**Engine Stalling at Stops**
- **Symptoms:** The engine cuts off unexpectedly when coming to a stop or at traffic lights, sometimes accompanied by a warning message to not change gears.
- **Solution:** Diagnose fuel delivery and engine management software, and inspect throttle or crankcase components.

## Transmission

### 7-Speed Dual-Clutch Automatic Transmission

The 7-speed dual-clutch automatic transmission routes power smoothly to either the front wheels or the 4MATIC all-wheel-drive system. While shift quality is normally crisp, owners have occasionally reported unexpected gear disengagements that require professional diagnosis.

**Unintended Neutral Shift**
- **Symptoms:** The transmission unexpectedly shifts into neutral while driving or while idling at a stoplight, resulting in a temporary loss of propulsion and warning messages.
- **Solution:** Perform a diagnostic scan of the transmission control module and update software or inspect internal mechanical actuators.

## Electrical

### Telematics and SOS System

**Obsolete 3G SOS Inoperable Warning**
- **Symptoms:** Continuous dashboard prompts regarding an inoperable SOS system due to sunsetted 3G cellular networks, requiring constant driver acknowledgment.
- **Solution:** Consult a dealership regarding potential software updates or module retrofits to clear the persistent warning message.

## Exterior

### Panoramic Sunroof

**Sunroof Track Binding and Jamming**
- **Symptoms:** The panoramic glass panel fails to open or close evenly due to plastic track failure, causing the glass to tilt, bind, or jam.
- **Solution:** Inspect the sunroof mechanism and replace damaged track components or drive motors.

## Summary Verdict

The GLA250 offers European luxury and sporty driving dynamics, but potential buyers should perform a thorough mechanical and electronic inspection before purchase. Pay close attention to transmission shift behavior, verify that crankcase ventilation parts are functioning properly, and check for any lingering dashboard warning alerts caused by outdated telematics or module glitches. Securing a detailed service history greatly reduces the risk of encountering expensive out-of-pocket repairs.
