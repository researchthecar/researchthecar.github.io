# 2018 Mercedes-Benz GLA250 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2018 Mercedes-Benz GLA250 combines nimble handling with punchy turbocharged performance, making it an appealing entry point into luxury compact crossovers. However, used car buyers should approach with care, as owner complaints highlight a few recurring mechanical and electrical issues. Reviewing specific trouble spots like transmission behavior and engine ventilation components will help ensure a reliable ownership experience.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4

The 2.0-liter turbocharged inline-4 engine produces 208 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque, offering brisk acceleration for daily driving. It is generally robust, though minor emissions and crankcase ventilation issues can arise over time.

**PCV Regulator Valve Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Illuminated check engine light and stored diagnostic trouble code P052E related to the positive crankcase ventilation regulator valve.
- **Solution:** Replace the faulty crankcase ventilation valve assembly.

## Transmission

### 7-Speed Dual-Clutch Automatic Transmission

The 7-speed dual-clutch automatic transmission delivers quick, responsive shifts across front-wheel-drive and 4MATIC all-wheel-drive configurations. While generally smooth, some owners have reported intermittent electronic or mechanical shifting anomalies.

**Unintended Neutral Shift**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle unexpectedly shifts into neutral while driving, accompanied by warning messages such as 'Transmission Error' or loss of drive power.
- **Solution:** Have the vehicle inspected by a qualified technician or dealer to diagnose potential internal transmission control module or mechanical linkage faults.

## Steering

### Steering Column Clockspring

**Air Bag Warning Light**
- **Symptoms:** Air bag warning light illuminates on the instrument cluster due to a fractured or inoperable clockspring in the steering column.
- **Solution:** Replace the steering column clockspring assembly.

## Electrical

### Infotainment and Instrument Cluster

**Intermittent Screen and Cluster Failures**
- **Symptoms:** Backup camera displays a black screen, instrument gauges fluctuate or drop to zero temporarily, and infotainment systems fail to boot promptly upon startup.
- **Solution:** Check software versions, wiring harnesses, and display modules for secure connections and potential software updates.

## Exterior

### Panoramic Sunroof

**Sunroof Glass Shattering or Bonding Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Panoramic sunroof glass shatters unexpectedly while driving, or structural panels lift due to adhesive breakdown.
- **Solution:** Inspect sunroof seals, bonding agents, and glass panels for alignment or structural integrity issues.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V405000: Child Seat:Tether: Connector/Clip](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=GLA250&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** The child seat top tether anchorage point on the center position of the rear seat may not have been installed. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 225, "Child Restraint Anchorage Systems." If the top tether anchorage point is missing, a child seat will not be able to be.
- **What to do:** MBUSA will notify owners, and dealers will replace the rear seat backrest, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V585000: Seat Belts:Rear/Other](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=GLA250&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** The seatbelt for a passenger seated in the rear middle seating position might not be properly secured, which could increase the risk of an injury in the event of a crash.
- **What to do:** MBUSA will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the left anchor plate of the rear middle seatbelt, correcting its installation as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V132000: Seats](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=GLA250&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** An incorrect rear seat back covering carpet could have been installed on the split-rear seat back. As such, these vehicles fail to comply to Federal Motor Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 225, "Child Restraint Anchorage Systems." If the upper anchorage point is covered, the child seat will not be able to be secured as intended in the center position of the.
- **What to do:** Mercedes will notify owners, and dealers will check the rear seat back covering carpet on the affected vehicles and replace it if necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V912000: Visibility:Sun/Moon Roof Assembly](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=GLA250&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** Water leaking into the vehicle from around the sunroof may affect the electrical contacts of the window air bag, potentially causing the window air bag to not deploy as intended, increasing the risk of injury in the event of a crash.
- **What to do:** Mercedes has notified owners, and dealers will inspect the bonding of the panorama sliding sunroof and the electrical contacts of the window air bag on the affected vehicles, replacing the components as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V229000: Steering](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=GLA250&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** The steering shaft bore hole for the universal joint bearing may allow excessive movement that, over time, could result in the separation of the bearing and universal joint. A separated universal joint bearing can cause a loss of steering control and increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** MBUSA will notify owners, and dealers will inspect, and as necessary, replace the steering shaft, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V653000: Exterior Lighting:Headlights](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=GLA250&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 108, "Lamps, Reflective Devices, and Associated Equipment." Reduced headlight range can affect visibility and increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the headlight adjustment, correcting it as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2018 Mercedes-Benz GLA250 is an engaging luxury crossover, but prospective buyers should perform a thorough pre-purchase inspection focusing on the transmission, electrical accessories, and crankcase ventilation system. Checking service history for unresolved recalls or prior electrical repairs will help mitigate potential ownership headaches.
