# 2023 Mercedes-Benz GLA250 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used Mercedes-Benz GLA250, buyers will find a compact luxury crossover that combines modern styling with energetic turbocharged performance. While overall owner satisfaction tends to be high, reviewing reported owner complaints highlights a few powertrain and electrical quirks to keep on your radar during a pre-purchase inspection.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4 (M260)

The GLA250 is powered by a 2.0-liter turbocharged inline-4 engine (M260) producing approximately 221 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque. It offers brisk acceleration and solid fuel economy, though routine maintenance is vital for long-term health.

**Engine Stalling and Misfires**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have reported unexpected cylinder misfires, rough unstable idling, jerking during acceleration, and sudden loss of power or engine stalling while driving.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified mechanic inspect the ignition components, fuel system, and engine computer for diagnostic trouble codes to pinpoint faulty parts.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Dual-Clutch Automatic Transmission

An 8-speed dual-clutch automatic transmission channels power to the front wheels or available 4MATIC all-wheel-drive system. It delivers crisp gear shifts, though proper electronic diagnostics are recommended before purchasing.

**Transmission Malfunction Warning and Power Loss**
- **Symptoms:** A transmission malfunction warning light may illuminate on the dashboard while driving at highway speeds, accompanied by an immediate loss of acceleration ability.
- **Solution:** Take the vehicle to an authorized service center or specialist to run diagnostic scans on the transmission control module and hydraulic actuators.

## Safety

### Seat Belts

Standard safety equipment includes advanced restraint systems and active driver assists designed to protect occupants during unexpected road incidents.

**Seat Belt Buckle Obstruction**
- **Symptoms:** Passengers may find that the seat belt buckle cannot be inserted into the receiver due to small debris or foreign objects lodged inside the mechanism.
- **Solution:** Inspect and clean out the receiver opening or replace the buckle assembly if the internal latch mechanism is obstructed or damaged.
