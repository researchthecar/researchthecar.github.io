## 2018 Mercedes-Benz GLA250

The 2018 Mercedes-Benz GLA250 is a compact luxury SUV that offers a comfortable and stylish ride. It comes standard with a 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine that produces 208 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque. The GLA250 is available with either front-wheel drive or all-wheel drive, and it comes standard with a seven-speed dual-clutch automatic transmission.

People who like the GLA250 appreciate its spacious interior, comfortable seats, and user-friendly infotainment system. However, some people have complained about the GLA250's lack of power, especially when accelerating from a stop.

### Engine

**2.0-liter Turbocharged Four-Cylinder**

**Power Loss at Higher Altitudes**
Description/Symptoms: The engine may lose power at higher altitudes, causing the vehicle to accelerate slowly or stall.
Solution: Reprogramming the engine control unit (ECU)
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
Recalls: None Found

### Transmission

**Seven-Speed Dual-Clutch Automatic**

**Delayed Gear Shifts**
Description/Symptoms: The transmission may hesitate or delay when shifting gears, causing rough or jerky shifts.
Solution: Updating the transmission software
Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
Recalls: None Found

### Suspension

**Front and Rear Suspension**

**No Issues Found**

### Electrical

**Electrical System**

**Battery Drain**
Description/Symptoms: The battery may drain quickly, even when the vehicle is not in use.
Solution: Replacing the faulty battery
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
Recalls: None Found

### Exterior

**Body Panels**

**No Issues Found**

### Interior

**Infotainment System**

**Software Issues**
Description/Symptoms: The infotainment system may experience software glitches, such as freezing or crashing.
Solution: Updating the software
Estimated Cost to Fix: Free
Recalls: None Found

### Summary

The 2018 Mercedes-Benz GLA250 is a reliable vehicle with a comfortable interior and a user-friendly infotainment system. However, some owners have experienced issues with the engine losing power at higher altitudes, the transmission delaying gear shifts, and the battery draining quickly. While these issues are not widespread, they can be costly to fix.
