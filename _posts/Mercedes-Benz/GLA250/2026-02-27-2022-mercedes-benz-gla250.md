# 2022 Mercedes-Benz GLA250 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

Shopping for a 2022 Mercedes-Benz GLA250 offers a compact luxury crossover experience loaded with modern tech and refined styling. While driver reports for this model year are relatively sparse, early owners have highlighted a few isolated glitches concerning emissions equipment and driver-assistance sensors. Reviewing how these specific components perform can help ensure a smooth ownership experience.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4 Engine

The 2.0-liter turbocharged inline-4 engine generates approximately 221 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque, providing brisk acceleration for daily driving. This powerplant balances responsive performance with respectable efficiency, though routine maintenance is critical for long-term health.

### Fuel System and Charcoal Canister

Owner reports suggest that the evaporative emissions system requires attention if warning lights appear after refueling.

**Charcoal Canister Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may notice the check engine light or malfunction indicator light activating unexpectedly after fueling up.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified technician inspect the EVAP system and replace the charcoal canister if internal saturation or failure is confirmed.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Dual-Clutch Automatic Transmission

The 8-speed dual-clutch automatic transmission delivers rapid gear shifts and power to either the front wheels or the 4MATIC all-wheel-drive system. It generally offers smooth operation in varied driving conditions when properly maintained.

## Safety

### Forward Collision Avoidance

Advanced safety systems provide peace of mind but should be checked for proper calibration if false activations occur.

**Phantom Emergency Braking**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle's emergency braking system unexpectedly engages while driving at highway speeds.
- **Solution:** Report sensor calibration issues to a dealer or specialist to check for software updates or radar alignment adjustments.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V662000: Exterior Lighting:Headlights](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=GLA250&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 108, "Lamps, Reflective Devices, and Associated Equipment." Incorrectly adjusted headlights can reduce the visibility of oncoming traffic, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and correct the headlight adjustment, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2022 Mercedes-Benz GLA250 brings luxury, efficient turbocharged power, and advanced features to the subcompact SUV segment. While isolated owner complaints point to potential emissions component quirks and sensor calibration sensitivities, widespread systemic failures remain uncommon. Prospective buyers should perform a thorough test drive, verify vehicle history, and check that all safety systems and software are fully updated.
