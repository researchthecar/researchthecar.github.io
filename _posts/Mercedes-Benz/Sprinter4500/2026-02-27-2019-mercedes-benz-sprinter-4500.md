# 2019 Mercedes-Benz Sprinter 4500 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2019 Mercedes-Benz Sprinter 4500, buyers will find a robust heavy-duty commercial van designed for heavy payloads and upfits. While these vehicles offer substantial capability and refined powertrain options, prospective owners should review owner-reported feedback regarding advanced driver assistance features, electrical modules, and steering components. Conducting a thorough pre-purchase inspection helps ensure all systems function as intended.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged I4 Diesel

This entry-level diesel engine provides efficient power for lighter commercial duties. It pairs with an automatic transmission designed for smooth urban delivery cycles.

### 3.0L Turbo V6 Diesel

Featuring a displacement of 3.0 liters in a V6 configuration, this diesel engine delivers strong torque output for heavy-duty towing and hauling. It is widely utilized across commercial fleets for its long-haul durability.

## Transmission

### 7-Speed Automatic Transmission

This traditional automatic transmission offers well-spaced gear ratios tailored to diesel torque bands. Proper fluid maintenance is essential for longevity in commercial fleets.

### 9-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 9-speed automatic transmission optimizes highway cruising efficiency and low-end acceleration. Regular service intervals help maintain shift quality under heavy loads.

## Brakes

### Driver Assistance Systems

Advanced safety suites manage collision prevention, lane monitoring, and adaptive cruise control features.

**Active Distance and Brake Assist Inoperative**
- **Symptoms:** Cruise control unexpectedly disengages while displaying error messages indicating active distance and brake assist functions are limited.
- **Solution:** Inspect radar sensors and camera modules for obstruction, and have diagnostic trouble codes cleared or software updated by a professional.

**Lane and Crosswind Assist Behavior**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers report that automated stability interventions during crosswinds or passing maneuvers can feel abrupt or overly aggressive.
- **Solution:** Verify sensor calibration and consult a dealer regarding available software optimizations for driver-assist parameters.

## Steering

### Power Steering System

Steering mechanisms provide directional control and rely on power assist units to manage heavy vehicle weight.

**Power Steering Assist Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Steering wheel vibrations at highway speeds may precede a complete loss of power steering assistance, requiring significant physical effort to control.
- **Solution:** Inspect the steering rack, electrical connections, and assist motor for faults, replacing worn components as necessary.

## Electrical

### Electronic Ignition System

Electrical architecture supports advanced convenience features, ignition systems, and onboard accessories.

**Ignition Module Connector Exposure**
- **Symptoms:** The electronic ignition system module location under the dashboard near the A-pillar can leave electrical connectors exposed to potential moisture or interference.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified technician inspect the ignition module wiring and mounting position for signs of vulnerability or corrosion.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V908000: Equipment](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=Sprinter+4500&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** The lashing rails on the side walls may not have been properly installed, possibly resulting in the lashing rails detaching. If the lashing rails disconnect, the cargo could become unsecured, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** DVUSA will notify owners, and dealers will correctly affix the lashing rails to the side walls, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V798000: Fuel System, Diesel](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=Sprinter+4500&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** The bayonet lock between the fuel pump and the fuel tank may not have been closed and locked during manufacturing. As a result, a leak between the fuel pump and the fuel tank could occur.
- **What to do:** DVUSA will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the bayonet lock on the fuel pump, locking it as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V800000: Seats](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=Sprinter+4500&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** In the event of a crash, the weld seam may break and cause the seat cushion to shift forward, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** DVUSA will notify owners, and dealers will replace the tilt adjuster for the front seats, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V693000: Unknown Or Other](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=Sprinter+4500&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** Failure to identify loose safety related parts can increase the risk of crash and injury.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 19V695000: Air Bags: Air Bag/Restraint Control Module](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=Sprinter+4500&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** The airbag systems may not have been activated after vehicle production. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 208, "Occupant Crash Protection." An inactive airbag system can increase the risk of injury in the event of a crash.
- **What to do:** DVUSA will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the vehicles to determine if the airbag systems have been activated, correcting them as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V507000: Visibility:Glass, Side/Rear](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=Sprinter+4500&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** In the event of a side impact crash or vehicle rollover, the fitted side windows may not adequately protect the rear occupants from being ejected, increasing their risk of injury.
- **What to do:** DVUSA will notify owners, and Mercedes-Benz or Freightliner dealers will replace the single-pane safety glass with laminated safety glass., free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V078000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Exhaust System:Emission Control:Gas Recirculation Valve (Egr Valve)](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=Sprinter+4500&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** The connecting screws on the exhaust gas recirculation (EGR) pipe may not have been correctly tightened. If the connecting screws are not properly tightened, hot exhaust gases may leak from the EGR pipe, increasing the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** DVUSA will notify owners, and Mercedes-Benz or Freightliner Sprinter dealers will renew the screw connection and the flange seals on the exhaust gas recirculation pipe, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V591000: Exterior Lighting:Tail Lights:Switch](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=Sprinter+4500&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** If the manual turn-switch that activates the vehicle lights is turned quickly from one lighting position to another, the brightness of the taillights can be significantly reduced or could switch off completely. Reduced taillight visibility increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** MBUSA will notify owners, and Mercedes-Benz or Freightliner Sprinter dealers will update the body control unit software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2019 Mercedes-Benz Sprinter 4500 is a highly capable commercial platform, but buyers should carefully inspect electronic safety aids, steering components, and electrical connections before purchasing. Verifying service records and ensuring all past recall remedies have been completed will help secure a dependable work vehicle.
