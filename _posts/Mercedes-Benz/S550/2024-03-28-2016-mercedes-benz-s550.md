**Introduction:**

The 2016 Mercedes-Benz S550 is a luxurious sedan known for its elegant design, comfortable interior, and advanced technology. It is powered by a 4.7-liter V8 engine that generates 449 horsepower and 516 lb-ft of torque, paired with a 7-speed automatic transmission. The S550 also offers a range of engine and transmission options, including a 5.5-liter V8 with 577 horsepower and 664 lb-ft of torque, and a 3.0-liter V6 with 329 horsepower and 354 lb-ft of torque.

Despite its high-end status, the 2016 Mercedes-Benz S550 has encountered some issues that have affected its owners.

**Engine**

**4.7-liter V8**

- **Leaking oil pan gasket:** The oil pan gasket can develop leaks, causing oil to leak out of the engine. Replacing the gasket can cost around $200-$400.
- **Intake manifold failure:** The intake manifold can crack or develop leaks, leading to reduced engine power and performance. Replacing the intake manifold can cost around $1,500-$2,500.

- **Valve cover gasket leak:** A leak can develop in the valve cover gasket, causing oil to leak out. The repair involves replacing the gasket and resealing the valve cover, costing around $500-$1,000.

**Transmission**

**7-speed automatic transmission**

- **Rough shifting:** The transmission may shift gears roughly or hesitate when shifting. This can be caused by faulty solenoids or a malfunctioning transmission control module (TCM). Replacing the solenoids can cost around $200-$500, while a new TCM can cost $1,000-$2,000.
- **Delayed engagement:** The transmission may hesitate or delay when engaging from park or neutral. This can be caused by a faulty torque converter or a problem with the transmission control system. Replacing the torque converter can cost around $1,500-$2,500.

**Suspension**

**No Issues Found**

**Electrical**

- **Battery discharge:** The battery may discharge prematurely, leaving the vehicle unable to start. This can be caused by a faulty alternator, a parasitic drain, or a battery that has reached the end of its lifespan. Replacing the alternator can cost around $500-$1,000, while diagnosing and repairing a parasitic drain can range from $100-$500. Battery replacement typically costs around $200-$400.
- **Faulty Comand system:** The Comand system, which controls the vehicle's infotainment and navigation, may experience glitches or stop working entirely. Updating the software or replacing the Comand unit can cost around $1,000-$2,000.

**Exterior**

- **Fading or peeling clear coat:** The clear coat on the exterior paint may fade or peel over time, affecting the vehicle's appearance. Touching up or repainting the affected areas can cost around $500-$1,500.

**Interior**

- **Faulty sunroof:** The sunroof may fail to open or close properly, or it may make unusual noises when operating. Replacing the sunroof motor or repairing the sunroof mechanism can cost around $1,000-$2,000.
- **Sticky buttons:** The buttons on the dashboard or steering wheel may become sticky or unresponsive. Replacing the affected buttons can cost around $100-$200.

**Summary:**

The 2016 Mercedes-Benz S550 is a luxurious vehicle with a powerful engine and advanced technology. However, it has experienced some issues that can affect its performance and reliability. The electrical system and interior components have been particularly problematic. While some repairs can be relatively inexpensive, others can be costly, especially for major components like the transmission or Comand system. Potential buyers should be aware of these issues and factor in the potential cost of repairs when considering purchasing this vehicle.
