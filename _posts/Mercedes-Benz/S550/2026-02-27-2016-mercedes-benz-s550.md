# 2016 Mercedes-Benz S550 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2016 Mercedes-Benz S550 delivers flagship luxury, advanced technology, and effortless performance, but prospective buyers should approach a used model with careful awareness of potential repair costs. While major powertrain failures are relatively uncommon, owner reports highlight recurring concerns with exterior glass, suspension components like wheels and low-profile tires, and occasional electrical or steering glitches. Prioritizing a comprehensive pre-purchase inspection by a qualified European car specialist is strongly recommended before finalizing a purchase.

---

## Engine

### 4.7L Twin-Turbocharged V8

The 4.7-liter twin-turbocharged V8 engine produces approximately 449 horsepower and 516 lb-ft of torque, offering exceptionally smooth and rapid acceleration. This powerplant is generally robust, though standard luxury-car maintenance costs and occasional emissions equipment wear apply.

**Catalytic Converter Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Illuminated check engine lights and reduced exhaust efficiency performance.
- **Solution:** Inspect exhaust components and replace faulty catalytic converters.

## Transmission

### 7-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 7-speed automatic transmission delivers seamless gear changes tailored to luxury cruising. It generally proves dependable when routine fluid services are maintained.

## Suspension

### Wheels and Tires

The S550's sophisticated suspension setup provides a supremely plush ride quality, but large factory wheels and low-profile run-flat tires are vulnerable to road impacts.

**Wheel Cracks and Tire Blowouts**
- **Symptoms:** Sudden tire deflation, visible sidewall blowouts, or cracked alloy wheel rims discovered during routine service.
- **Solution:** Replace damaged low-profile run-flat tires and have alloy wheels inspected or replaced as needed.

## Steering

### Power Steering System

Steering components generally offer precise handling and assist, though rare electronic assist glitches have been noted by owners.

**Intermittent Steering Lockout**
- **Symptoms:** Power steering failure warning lights illuminate accompanied by temporary heavy steering or complete lockout.
- **Solution:** Diagnose electronic steering control modules and replace failing steering components.

## Exterior

### Panoramic Glass and Windshield

Exterior glass and body trim require careful inspection, as rare instances of environmental stress cracking on panoramic roofs have been reported.

**Spontaneous Glass Cracking or Shattering**
- **Symptoms:** Loud banging noises heard while driving followed by sudden cracks or shattering of the panoramic sunroof or windshield.
- **Solution:** Replace damaged glass panels and ensure proper frame alignment or adhesive application.

## Safety

### Seat Belts

Cabin safety restraint systems generally perform reliably, but individual mechanical failures in buckles and retractors can occur.

**Seat Belt Latch and Retractor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Front passenger seat belt fails to securely latch into the buckle, or rear seat belts remain stuck and fail to retract.
- **Solution:** Inspect and replace faulty seat belt buckles or retractor assemblies.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V787000: Communication](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=S550&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** For a full list of the affected models visit: https://static.nhtsa.gov/odi/rcl/2019/RMISC-19V787-4620.pdf An inaccurate vehicle location may delay emergency responders, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** MBUSA will also notify owners, and dealers will check software for a successful OTA or update the communication module software as needed, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V197000: Structure:Body:Roof And Pillars](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=S550&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** The front roof panel located between the windshield and the panoramic sunroof may not have been bonded correctly to the vehicle during a prior repair. The adhesive bonding of the front panel might deteriorate, possibly causing the front panel to detach from the vehicle and become a road hazard, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Mercedes-Benz dealer will remove the bonded panel and will install a new front panel by following the detailed repair instructions and using the approved primer, cleaner and adhesive, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V829000: Seat Belts](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=S550&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** The seat belt extenders on the affected vehicles may not retract as intended and may potentially break the event of a crash. In the event of a crash, if the seat belt extender does not retract and/or the extender breaks, the seat occupant may not be properly restrained, increasing their risk of injury.
- **What to do:** MBUSA will notify owners, and dealers will update the control unit software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V760000: Exterior Lighting:Headlights](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=S550&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** The low beam headlights on the affected vehicles may not be properly adjusted. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 108, "Lamps, Reflective Devices, and Associated Equipment." If the low-beam headlights are out of adjustment, the driver's visibility may be reduced, increasing.
- **What to do:** MBUSA will notify owners, and dealers will inspect and correct the headlight adjustment, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V854000: Structure:Body:Roof And Pillars](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=S550&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** The front roof panel located between the windshield and the panoramic sunroof may not have been bonded correctly to the vehicle during a prior repair, allowing the front panel to detach from the vehicle. A detached roof panel can become a road hazard, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the front panel, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

Purchasing a used 2016 Mercedes-Benz S550 offers incredible value relative to its original sticker price, but buyers must budget for higher-than-average maintenance and potential repair expenses. Key areas to inspect include the condition of the wheels and tires, the integrity of the panoramic glass roof, and all electronic safety and steering systems. A thorough pre-purchase inspection from an independent mechanic specializing in German luxury vehicles is essential to uncovering hidden issues before purchase.
