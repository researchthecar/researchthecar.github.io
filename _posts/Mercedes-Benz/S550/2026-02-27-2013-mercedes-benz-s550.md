# 2013 Mercedes-Benz S550

### Overview
The 2013 Mercedes-Benz S550, representing the W221 generation's final model year, is a luxury sedan known for its opulent interior, advanced technology for its time, and powerful performance. It typically features a robust 4.7L V8 biturbo engine paired with a 7-speed automatic transmission. While generally well-regarded for its build quality and comfort, like any complex luxury vehicle, it can experience specific issues as it ages. This analysis focuses on documented common problems beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential areas of concern for owners and prospective buyers.

---


## Engine

### 4.7L V8 Biturbo (M278)
**Timing Chain Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Rattling noise from the front of the engine, especially on startup; check engine light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of timing chain, tensioners, and guides.
- **Cost:** $1,500 - $3,000

**Oil Leaks (Valve Cover Gaskets/Oil Cooler Seals)**
- **Symptoms:** Visible oil on the engine block, burning oil smell, low oil level.
- **Solution:** Replacement of valve cover gaskets and/or oil cooler seals.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1,200


## Transmission

### 7G-Tronic (722.9) Automatic
**Transmission Control Module (TCM) Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Harsh shifting, slipping gears, inability to shift, transmission warning light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the TCM.
- **Cost:** $1,000 - $2,500

**Torque Converter Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Shuddering at low speeds, slipping, delayed engagement.
- **Solution:** Rebuild or replacement of the torque converter.
- **Cost:** $1,200 - $2,800


## Suspension

### Active Body Control (ABC) System
**Hydraulic Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle sagging, 'ABC system faulty' warning message, fluid leaks under the car.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of leaking hoses, struts, or pump.
- **Cost:** $1,000 - $4,000+

**Pump Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of hydraulic pressure, vehicle sagging, loud whining noise from the pump.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the ABC hydraulic pump.
- **Cost:** $1,500 - $3,000


### Standard Air Suspension (if equipped)
**Air Strut Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle sagging on one corner, uneven ride height, 'ABC system faulty' warning.
- **Solution:** Replacement of leaking air struts.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1,500 per corner


## Electrical

### Command Navigation System
**Screen Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** Flickering screen, dead pixels, unresponsive touch controls (if applicable).
- **Solution:** Software update or replacement of the display unit.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1,500


### Keyless Go System
**Door Handle Sensors**
- **Symptoms:** Keyless entry not working, intermittent operation.
- **Solution:** Replacement of faulty door handle sensors.
- **Cost:** $300 - $700


### Battery Drain
_No major issues reported for this configuration._

## Exterior

### Headlight Ballasts
**Xenon Headlight Failure**
- **Symptoms:** One or both headlights not turning on, flickering.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the headlight ballast.
- **Cost:** $400 - $800 per side


### Door Seals
**Degradation and Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Water ingress into the cabin, wind noise at higher speeds.
- **Solution:** Replacement of door seals.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600


## Interior

### Seat Actuators
**Power Seat Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Power seats not moving or moving erratically.
- **Solution:** Replacement of faulty seat motors or control modules.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1,000


### Headliner Fabric
**Sagging Headliner**
- **Symptoms:** Fabric detaching from the headliner board, especially in hot climates.
- **Solution:** Re-gluing or replacement of the headliner.
- **Cost:** $400 - $900

### Summary Verdict
The 2013 Mercedes-Benz S550 is a sophisticated luxury vehicle that, while offering a high level of comfort and performance, can present specific repair needs as it ages. Key areas to monitor include the Active Body Control (ABC) suspension for hydraulic leaks and pump issues, and the 7-speed transmission for potential control module failures. Engine-wise, timing chain wear and oil leaks are the most commonly cited concerns. Electrical gremlins, particularly with the COMAND system and battery drain, can also arise. While not overwhelmingly problematic, owners should budget for potential repairs, especially concerning the complex suspension and powertrain systems. Regular maintenance and prompt attention to warning signs are crucial for maintaining this flagship sedan's reliability.
