# 2017 Mercedes-Benz G65 AMG Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2017 Mercedes-Benz G65 AMG is an ultra-exclusive, hand-built luxury SUV powered by a massive twin-turbo V12 engine. While highly coveted for its commanding road presence and brute force, prospective buyers should approach this low-volume exotic with careful mechanical and electronic scrutiny.

---

## Engine

### 6.0L Biturbo V12 (M275/M279)

The G65 AMG features a hand-built 6.0-liter twin-turbocharged V12 engine producing roughly 621 horsepower and 738 lb-ft of torque. This powerhouse delivers staggering straight-line performance, though routine maintenance and upkeep costs are exceptionally high.

## Transmission

### 7G-Tronic Plus 7-Speed Automatic

Power is routed through a reinforced 7-speed automatic transmission designed to handle the immense torque output of the V12 engine. It offers smooth highway cruising and responsive gear selection when properly maintained.

## Brakes

### ABS and Electronic Stability Control

Heavy braking components require regular monitoring due to the sheer mass and performance capability of the vehicle.

**ABS and ESC System Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have reported total failures of the anti-lock braking and electronic stability control systems, compromising vehicle handling and dynamic stability.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified specialist inspect the hydraulic control unit, wheel speed sensors, and associated ABS/ESP control modules for faults.

## Electrical

### Backup Camera and Parking Sensors

Advanced driver assistance features add convenience but can present intermittent electronic issues.

**Intermittent Rear Camera Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The backup camera and rear parking sensors may experience intermittent operational failures, potentially leading to visibility hazards.
- **Solution:** Diagnose the camera wiring harness, display unit, and individual bumper sensors for loose connections or internal faults.

### Traction Control and Vehicle Modules

Complex vehicle electronics and software integration require specialized diagnostic equipment to troubleshoot effectively.

**Traction Control Software Error**
- **Symptoms:** Software errors can trigger persistent error codes and disable crucial traction control systems, rendering the vehicle temporarily undrivable.
- **Solution:** Consult a dealership or specialized facility to perform necessary software reflashes or replace defective electronic control modules.
