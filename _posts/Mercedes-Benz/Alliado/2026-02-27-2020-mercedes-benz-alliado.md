# 2020 Mercedes-Benz Alliado

### Overview
The 2020 Mercedes-Benz Metris (often referred to by its internal designation, W447, and sometimes colloquially as the 'Alliado' in certain markets, though 'Metris' is the official North American name) is a versatile commercial van known for its practicality and Mercedes-Benz build quality. Reliability data for this model year generally indicates a robust vehicle, particularly in its core mechanical components. Owners and industry reports suggest that while no vehicle is entirely immune to issues, the Metris demonstrates strong performance in key areas. Common areas of attention, as with many modern vehicles, tend to be related to electronics and specific emissions control systems, rather than fundamental powertrain failures. Overall, the 2020 Metris is considered a dependable workhorse for businesses and individuals requiring cargo or passenger transport.

### Infotainment System
**Infotainment Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Occasional screen freezes, unresponsiveness, or connectivity issues with Apple CarPlay/Android Auto.
- **Solution:** Software updates, system resets, or potential replacement of the head unit.
- **Cost:** $100 - $1500


### Battery Drain
**Intermittent Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Premature battery depletion, requiring jump starts or battery replacement sooner than expected.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis of parasitic draw from electrical components, potential module reprogramming or component replacement.
- **Cost:** $200 - $800

### Summary Verdict
The 2020 Mercedes-Benz Metris (Alliado) presents itself as a reliable commercial vehicle with a strong mechanical foundation. The powertrain, including the 2.0L turbocharged engine and 7-speed automatic transmission, shows minimal reported issues, indicating good durability for demanding use. Suspension components are also generally robust. The primary areas where minor concerns have been noted are within the electrical system, specifically concerning infotainment glitches and occasional battery drain issues, which are not uncommon in modern vehicles with complex electronics. Interior components and the exterior bodywork have also proven to be dependable. Overall, the Metris is a solid choice for those seeking a dependable van, with most potential issues being manageable and not indicative of systemic failures.
