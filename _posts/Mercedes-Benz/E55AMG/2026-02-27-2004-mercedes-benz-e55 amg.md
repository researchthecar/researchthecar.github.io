# 2004 Mercedes-Benz E55 AMG

### Overview
The 2004 Mercedes-Benz E55 AMG, part of the W211 generation, is renowned for its potent supercharged V8 engine and luxurious appointments. While generally a robust performance sedan, like any high-performance vehicle of its era, it has specific areas that owners and technicians commonly report issues with. This data focuses on documented, non-wear-and-tear related problems that may require repair beyond routine maintenance. Understanding these potential pitfalls can help owners budget for and proactively address common concerns, ensuring the continued enjoyment and reliability of this iconic AMG model.

---


## Engine

### 5.4L Supercharged V8 (M113K)
**Supercharger Belt Tensioner Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Squealing noise from the engine bay, loss of power steering, battery charging issues, potential belt breakage.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the supercharger belt tensioner assembly.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600

**Intercooler Pump Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Reduced engine power, especially under load; engine may run hotter than normal.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the intercooler pump.
- **Cost:** $400 - $700

**Oil Cooler Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Visible oil leaks around the oil cooler lines or connections, potential for oil smell.
- **Solution:** Replacement of leaking oil cooler lines or seals.
- **Cost:** $500 - $900


## Transmission

### 5-Speed Automatic (722.6)
**Conductor Plate Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Harsh shifting, transmission slipping, check engine light, inability to select gears.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the transmission conductor plate and fluid service.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1500

**Transmission Control Module (TCM) Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Erratic shifting behavior, limp mode engagement, transmission warning lights.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and potential replacement or repair of the TCM.
- **Cost:** $700 - $1200


## Suspension

### ABC (Active Body Control) Suspension
**Hydraulic Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle sagging on one or more corners, 'ABC Malfunction' warning on the dashboard, fluid leaks under the car.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of leaking hydraulic lines, struts, or pump.
- **Cost:** $1000 - $3000+

**ABC Pump Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of hydraulic pressure, vehicle sagging, loud whining noise from the pump.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the ABC hydraulic pump.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1500


## Electrical

### General Electrical System
**SAM (Signal Acquisition Module) Corrosion/Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Various electrical gremlins, including non-functional lights, wipers, power windows, or dashboard warning lights.
- **Solution:** Cleaning or replacement of the affected SAM module, often due to water ingress.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1000

**Seat Control Module Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Malfunctioning power seats, mirrors, or memory functions.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and replacement of faulty seat control modules.
- **Cost:** $300 - $700

### Summary Verdict
The 2004 Mercedes-Benz E55 AMG (W211) offers exhilarating performance but comes with potential reliability concerns, particularly in its advanced systems. The supercharged V8 engine, while powerful, can experience issues with the supercharger belt tensioner and intercooler pump. The sophisticated 5-speed automatic transmission may suffer from conductor plate or TCM failures. The Active Body Control (ABC) suspension is a common source of expensive repairs due to hydraulic leaks and pump failures. Electrical gremlins, often linked to the SAM module, can also manifest. While the exterior and interior are generally well-built, these specific mechanical and electrical components require attention and potential budget allocation for repairs.
