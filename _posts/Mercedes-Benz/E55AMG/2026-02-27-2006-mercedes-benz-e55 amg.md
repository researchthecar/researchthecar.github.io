# 2006 Mercedes-Benz E55 AMG

### Overview
The 2006 Mercedes-Benz E55 AMG, a high-performance variant of the W211 E-Class, is renowned for its potent supercharged V8 engine and luxurious appointments. While generally well-built, like any performance vehicle of its era, it is susceptible to certain age-related and performance-specific issues. Owners often report a robust powertrain, but attention to the complex electronic systems and the specialized AMG components is crucial for maintaining its reliability and performance. Regular and diligent maintenance, particularly with specialized fluids and filters, is paramount to mitigating potential problems and ensuring the longevity of this sophisticated German sedan.

---


## Engine

### 5.4L Supercharged V8 (M113)
**Supercharger Belt Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of power, squealing noise from the engine bay.
- **Solution:** Replace the supercharger drive belt and tensioner.
- **Cost:** $150 - $300

**Coolant Leaks (Intercooler/Hoses)**
- **Symptoms:** Overheating, visible coolant leaks, sweet smell from engine.
- **Solution:** Inspect and replace leaking intercooler seals, hoses, or the intercooler itself.
- **Cost:** $300 - $800

**Oil Leaks (Valve Covers/Oil Cooler)**
- **Symptoms:** Visible oil stains, burning oil smell, low oil level.
- **Solution:** Replace valve cover gaskets or the oil cooler seals.
- **Cost:** $400 - $900


## Transmission

### 5-Speed Automatic (722.6)
**Transmission Control Module (TCM) Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Erratic shifting, 'limp mode', transmission warning lights.
- **Solution:** Replace or repair the Transmission Control Module.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1500

**Conductor Plate Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Harsh shifting, delayed engagement, no gear selection.
- **Solution:** Replace the transmission conductor plate.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1000


## Suspension

### Active Body Control (ABC)
**Hydraulic Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle sagging, suspension warning lights, fluid leaks under the car.
- **Solution:** Repair or replace leaking ABC struts, hoses, or pump.
- **Cost:** $1000 - $3000+

**ABC Pump Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of hydraulic pressure, suspension warning lights, noisy pump.
- **Solution:** Replace the ABC hydraulic pump.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1500


## Electrical

### SAM Unit (Signal Acquisition Module)
**Water Intrusion/Corrosion**
- **Symptoms:** Various electrical gremlins, non-functional lights, power windows, or other accessories.
- **Solution:** Repair or replace the affected SAM unit and address the source of water ingress.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1200


### Window Regulators
**Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Power windows not operating, window dropping into the door.
- **Solution:** Replace the window regulator mechanism.
- **Cost:** $200 - $400

### Summary Verdict
The 2006 Mercedes-Benz E55 AMG offers exhilarating performance but requires diligent maintenance and awareness of its specific potential issues. The supercharged V8 is generally robust, but components like the supercharger belt and cooling system can require attention. The 5-speed automatic transmission, while strong, can experience issues with its control module and conductor plate. The Active Body Control (ABC) suspension is a complex system prone to hydraulic leaks and pump failures, representing a significant potential cost. Electrical issues, particularly with the SAM unit due to water intrusion, are also noted. Despite these potential challenges, with proper care and timely repairs, the E55 AMG remains a desirable and capable performance sedan.
