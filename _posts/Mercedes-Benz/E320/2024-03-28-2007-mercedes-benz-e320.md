**Introduction:**

The 2007 Mercedes-Benz E320 is a midsize luxury sedan powered by a 3.2L V6 engine that produces 221 horsepower and 232 lb-ft of torque. It is available with a 7-speed automatic transmission. The E320 is praised for its refined performance, luxurious interior, and smooth ride. However, like any vehicle, it has some reported issues.

**Engine**

**3.2L V6 Engine**

* **Intake Manifold Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaks, overheating, loss of power
    * Solution: Replace the intake manifold
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**7-Speed Automatic Transmission**

* **Shift Delay or Hard Shifting**
    * Description/Symptoms: Delay or hesitation during gear changes, rough or clunky shifts
    * Solution: Transmission fluid and filter change, software update, or transmission rebuild
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $3,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Front Suspension Bushing Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Thunking or clunking noises, vibration, uneven tire wear
    * Solution: Replace the failed bushings
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Rear Suspension Spring Sag**
    * Description/Symptoms: Drooping rear suspension, reduced ride height, potential handling issues
    * Solution: Replace the rear springs
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $600+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Battery Discharge**
    * Description/Symptoms: Battery drains overnight, difficulty starting the vehicle, warning lights
    * Solution: Check and replace the battery or alternator
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Electrical Module Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Malfunctioning sensors, warning lights, intermittent electrical issues
    * Solution: Diagnose and replace the faulty electrical module
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Headlight Condensation**
    * Description/Symptoms: Moisture inside the headlight assembly, reduced visibility
    * Solution: Seal or replace the headlight assembly
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600+
    * Recalls: MB21143

**Interior**

* **Sunroof Motor Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Sunroof does not open or close properly, grinding noises
    * Solution: Replace the sunroof motor
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,200+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

The 2007 Mercedes-Benz E320 has some reported issues, but most are not overly severe or expensive to fix. Owners of this vehicle should be aware of potential intake manifold failure, transmission issues, and suspension bushing problems. Regular maintenance and promptly addressing any issues can help prevent major repairs down the road.
