**Introduction:**

The 1999 Mercedes-Benz E320 is a luxury sedan powered by a 3.2L V6 engine paired with a 5-speed automatic transmission. It produces 221 horsepower and 232 lb-ft of torque. The E320 is praised for its refined interior, comfortable ride, and impressive performance, but it does come with some potential issues to consider.

**Engine**

- **M112 3.2L V6 Engine:**
 **Intake Manifold Gasket Failure:**
  Description/Symptoms: Coolant or oil leaks, rough idling, engine overheating
  Solution: Replace intake manifold gaskets
  Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
  Recalls: None Found

 **Cam Position Sensor Failure:**
  Description/Symptoms: Engine stalling, rough idling, reduced power
  Solution: Replace cam position sensor
  Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500
  Recalls: None Found

- **M113 3.2L V6 Engine:**
 **None Found**

**Transmission**

- **5-Speed Automatic Transmission:**
 **Torque Converter Lock-Up Clutch Wear:**
  Description/Symptoms: Transmission slipping, shuddering, or delayed engagement
  Solution: Replace torque converter
  Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500
  Recalls: None Found

- **4-Speed Automatic Transmission:**
 **None Found**

**Suspension**

 **Front Lower Control Arm Bushings Wear:**
  Description/Symptoms: Noises from suspension, uneven tire wear
  Solution: Replace control arm bushings
  Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $600
  Recalls: None Found

 **Rear Shock Absorber Mount Wear:**
  Description/Symptoms: Bouncing or rattling suspension, reduced handling
  Solution: Replace shock absorber mounts
  Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
  Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

 **Battery Drain:**
  Description/Symptoms: Battery goes dead after short periods of time
  Solution: Identify and fix electrical component causing drain
  Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the cause
  Recalls: None Found

 **Alternator Failure:**
  Description/Symptoms: Dimming lights, electrical system problems
  Solution: Replace alternator
  Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
  Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

 **Sunroof Drain Plugs Clogged:**
  Description/Symptoms: Water leaking into interior through sunroof
  Solution: Unclog or replace drain plugs
  Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200
  Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

 **Climate Control Malfunction:**
  Description/Symptoms: AC or heater not working properly
  Solution: Diagnose and repair climate control system
  Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the issue
  Recalls: None Found

 **Power Window Regulator Failure:**
  Description/Symptoms: Windows not operating properly
  Solution: Replace power window regulator
  Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400 per window
  Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 1999 Mercedes-Benz E320 is generally a reliable vehicle, but it does have some potential issues that buyers should be aware of. While the engine and transmission are generally sturdy, there are some known problems with intake manifold gaskets, cam position sensors, and torque converter lock-up clutches. The suspension can also experience wear in the control arm bushings and shock absorber mounts. Electrical issues such as battery drain and alternator failure have also been reported. In the interior, climate control malfunction and power window regulator failure are common. The severity of these issues varies, and the cost to fix them can be significant. However, many of these issues can be addressed with regular maintenance and prompt repairs.
