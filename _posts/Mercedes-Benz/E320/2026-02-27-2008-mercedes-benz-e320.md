# 2008 Mercedes-Benz E320

### Overview
The 2008 Mercedes-Benz E320, part of the W211 generation, is generally regarded as a well-built luxury sedan with a strong emphasis on comfort and performance. It offers a refined driving experience, particularly with its potent diesel engine options. While Mercedes-Benz vehicles are known for their advanced engineering, like any complex machine, they can encounter specific issues over time. This report focuses on documented technical reliability concerns beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential problem areas for owners and prospective buyers of the 2008 E320. Understanding these common issues can help in proactive maintenance and budgeting for repairs.

---


## Engine

### 3.2L V6 Diesel (OM642)
**Intake Manifold Runner Flap Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Check engine light, rough idle, loss of power, rattling noise from intake area.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the intake manifold assembly or repair of the flap mechanism.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1500

**Turbocharger Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of boost, whistling noises, black smoke from exhaust, reduced engine power.
- **Solution:** Turbocharger inspection, repair, or replacement.
- **Cost:** $1500 - $3000

**Glow Plug Module Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Difficulty starting in cold weather, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the glow plug control module.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600


## Transmission

### 5-Speed Automatic (722.6)
**Transmission Control Module (TCM) Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Erratic shifting, harsh shifts, transmission warning light, limp mode.
- **Solution:** Replacement or reprogramming of the TCM.
- **Cost:** $700 - $1200

**Conductor Plate Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Shifting problems, no communication with TCM, error codes related to speed sensors.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the conductor plate.
- **Cost:** $500 - $900


## Suspension

### Standard Chassis
**Air Suspension Leaks (if equipped)**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle sagging on one side, uneven ride height, compressor running excessively.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of air springs, lines, or compressor.
- **Cost:** $600 - $1500 per corner

**Control Arm Bushing Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Clunking noises over bumps, vague steering, uneven tire wear.
- **Solution:** Replacement of worn control arm bushings or the entire control arm assembly.
- **Cost:** $400 - $800


## Electrical

### General Electrical System
**SAM Unit (Signal Acquisition Module) Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Malfunctioning lights (headlights, taillights), power window problems, erratic dashboard warnings.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of the SAM unit.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1000

**O2 Sensor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Check engine light, poor fuel economy, rough idle.
- **Solution:** Replacement of faulty oxygen sensors.
- **Cost:** $200 - $500


## Exterior

### Body and Trim
**Sunroof Drain Clogs**
- **Symptoms:** Water leaks into the cabin, musty smell, potential damage to electronics.
- **Solution:** Clearing of sunroof drain tubes.
- **Cost:** $100 - $300


## Interior

### Cabin Components
**HVAC Blower Motor Resistor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Fan only works on highest setting or not at all.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the blower motor resistor.
- **Cost:** $100 - $250

**Seat Control Module Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Power seats not working, memory functions failing.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and potential replacement of seat control modules.
- **Cost:** $400 - $800

### Summary Verdict
The 2008 Mercedes-Benz E320 (W211) is a robust vehicle, but like many luxury cars of its era, it has specific areas that may require attention. The diesel engine, while powerful and efficient, can be prone to issues with the intake manifold runner flaps and turbocharger. Transmission problems, particularly with the TCM and conductor plate, are also noted. Electrical gremlins, often centered around the SAM unit, can manifest in various ways. Suspension components, especially if equipped with air suspension, and interior electronics like HVAC controls can also present issues. Despite these potential concerns, many owners report high satisfaction when these issues are addressed promptly. Regular maintenance and addressing known problem areas proactively are key to maintaining the E320's reliability and luxury experience.
