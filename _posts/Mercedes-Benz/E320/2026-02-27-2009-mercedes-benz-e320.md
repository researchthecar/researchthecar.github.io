# 2009 Mercedes-Benz E320

### Overview
The 2009 Mercedes-Benz E320, part of the W211 generation, is generally considered a robust and well-engineered vehicle. It offers a blend of luxury, performance, and comfort, powered by a refined diesel engine. While Mercedes-Benz vehicles are known for their sophisticated engineering, like any car, they can experience specific issues over time. This analysis focuses on documented, non-wear-and-tear related problems that owners have reported, providing insights into potential areas of concern and their associated repair costs. The E320's strong build quality contributes to its overall reliability, but understanding common failure points can help prospective buyers and current owners.

---


## Engine

### 3.0L V6 Turbo Diesel (OM642)
**Intake Manifold Runner Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Check engine light, rough idle, loss of power, increased emissions.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the intake manifold or repair of the actuator arms.
- **Cost:** $600 - $1200

**EGR Valve Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Check engine light, poor performance, black smoke from exhaust, rough idling.
- **Solution:** Cleaning or replacement of the EGR valve.
- **Cost:** $400 - $800

**Turbocharger Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of power, whistling noises, excessive smoke, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Turbocharger repair or replacement.
- **Cost:** $1500 - $3000

### 7-Speed Automatic (722.9)
**Valve Body Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Harsh shifting, delayed engagement, slipping gears, transmission warning light.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of the transmission valve body.
- **Cost:** $1000 - $2500

**Torque Converter Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Shuddering at low speeds, slipping, rough engagement.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the torque converter.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1800

## Electrical

### General Electrical System
**SAM (Signal Acquisition Module) Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Various electrical gremlins, malfunctioning lights, power windows, or other accessories.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of the SAM module.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1500

**Window Regulator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Power windows not working, slow operation, or falling into the door.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the window regulator assembly.
- **Cost:** $200 - $500

### Summary Verdict
The 2009 Mercedes-Benz E320 (W211) is a reliable luxury sedan, particularly known for its strong diesel engine. While generally well-built, common issues tend to revolve around the diesel engine's emissions system (DPF, EGR) and intake manifold, as well as potential transmission valve body or torque converter problems, especially with the 7-speed automatic. Electrical gremlins, such as SAM module issues or window regulators, can also occur. Despite these potential points of failure, many owners report a positive ownership experience with proper maintenance. The estimated repair costs for these specific issues can range from moderate to significant, underscoring the importance of pre-purchase inspections and a maintenance budget for this class of vehicle.
