**Introduction:**

The 2009 Mercedes-Benz E320 is a mid-size luxury sedan powered by a 3.5L V6 engine paired with a 5-speed automatic transmission. It produces 250 horsepower and 247 lb-ft of torque. The E320 is known for its comfortable ride, refined interior, and advanced safety features. However, like any vehicle, it has some reported issues.

**Engine**

**3.5L V6**

* **Oil Leak from Timing Cover:** Oil may leak from the timing cover gaskets, causing a drop in oil levels and potential engine damage. Solution: Replace the timing cover gaskets. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1000+
* **Intake Manifold Swirl Flaps Failure:** The intake manifold swirl flaps may fail, causing reduced engine power and increased fuel consumption. Solution: Replace the intake manifold. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1500 - $2500+
* **Cam Adjuster Solenoid Failure:** The camshaft adjusters may fail, causing a loss of engine power and reduced fuel efficiency. Solution: Replace the cam adjusters. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1000+
* **Crankshaft Pulley Failure:** The crankshaft pulley may fail, causing a squealing noise, belt damage, and potential engine damage. Solution: Replace the crankshaft pulley. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800+

**Transmission**

**5-Speed Automatic**

* **Transmission Fluid Leak:** The transmission may develop a leak in the fluid lines or seals, causing a drop in fluid levels and potential transmission damage. Solution: Replace the leaking components. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1500+
* **Transmission Slipping:** The transmission may slip or hesitate when shifting, indicating worn or damaged internal components. Solution: Rebuild or replace the transmission. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1500 - $3000+
* **Shift Solenoid Failure:** The shift solenoids may fail, causing shifting problems, delayed response, or a lack of gear engagement. Solution: Replace the shift solenoids. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1000+

**Suspension**

* **Front Control Arm Bushing Failure:** The bushings in the front control arms may fail, causing excessive noise, vibrations, and reduced steering precision. Solution: Replace the control arm bushings. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800+
* **Rear Airmatic Suspension Failure:** The rear air suspension may fail due to leaks or compressor issues, causing the vehicle to drop or ride unevenly. Solution: Replace the air suspension components. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1500 - $3000+

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain:** The vehicle may experience a rapid battery drain, which can be caused by faulty modules, parasitic draw, or a weak battery. Solution: Diagnose the source of the drain and replace or repair the affected components. Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the source of the drain
* **Window Switch Failure:** The window switches may fail, causing windows to become inoperative or move erratically. Solution: Replace the faulty switches. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400 per switch
* **Interior Lights Malfunction:** The interior lights may malfunction, flicker, or fail to turn on, indicating a faulty wiring harness or light module. Solution: Repair or replace the affected components. Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the extent of the issue

**Exterior**

* **Headlight Condensation:** The headlights may collect condensation inside, reducing visibility and potentially damaging the lens. Solution: Replace the headlights or have them resealed. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1000+ per headlight

**Interior**

* **Navigation System Malfunction:** The navigation system may malfunction, freeze, or display incorrect information. Solution: Update the software or replace the navigation module. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1000+
* **Climate Control Failure:** The climate control system may fail, causing temperature fluctuations, lack of airflow, or uneven cooling/heating. Solution: Replace the faulty climate control module or other components. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1500+

**Summary:**

The 2009 Mercedes-Benz E320 has some reported issues that can affect different vehicle components. While some issues are relatively minor and inexpensive to fix, others can be more severe and costly. It's important to note that not all vehicles will experience these issues, and the frequency and severity can vary depending on individual driving habits and maintenance practices.
