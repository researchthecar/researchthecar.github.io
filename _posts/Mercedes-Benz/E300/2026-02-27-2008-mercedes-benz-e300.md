# 2008 Mercedes-Benz E300

### Overview
The 2008 Mercedes-Benz E300, part of the W211 generation, is generally regarded as a solid vehicle with a reputation for comfort and build quality. However, like any complex machine, it has specific areas that have seen more attention from owners regarding reliability. This analysis focuses on documented common issues beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential problem areas for the E300, particularly concerning its powertrain and electrical systems. Understanding these potential pitfalls can help owners budget for and address issues proactively, ensuring the continued longevity and enjoyment of their E-Class.

---


## Engine

### 3.0L V6 Diesel (OM642)
**Intake Manifold Runner Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Check engine light, rough idle, loss of power, rattling noise from the intake area.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the intake manifold or repair of the runner flaps.
- **Cost:** $600 - $1200

**Glow Plug Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Difficulty starting, especially in cold weather, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of faulty glow plugs and potentially the glow plug module.
- **Cost:** $400 - $800

**Turbocharger Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of power, whistling noise, black smoke from exhaust.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of the turbocharger unit.
- **Cost:** $1500 - $3000


## Transmission

### 5-Speed Automatic (722.6)
**Transmission Control Module (TCM) Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Erratic shifting, transmission not engaging gears, 'limp mode'.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the TCM.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1500

**Conductor Plate Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Harsh shifting, delayed engagement, transmission fluid leaks.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the conductor plate and associated sensors.
- **Cost:** $500 - $900


## Suspension

### Standard Chassis
**Air Suspension Leaks (if equipped)**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle sagging on one side, compressor running excessively, warning lights.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of air springs, lines, or compressor.
- **Cost:** $700 - $1500 per corner

**Control Arm Bushing Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Clunking noises over bumps, vague steering feel, uneven tire wear.
- **Solution:** Replacement of worn control arm bushings or the entire control arm assembly.
- **Cost:** $400 - $700


## Electrical

### General Electrical System
**SAM (Signal Acquisition Module) Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Various electrical gremlins, non-functioning lights, power windows, or other accessories.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and potential repair or replacement of the SAM unit.
- **Cost:** $600 - $1200

**Window Regulator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Power windows not operating, window getting stuck.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the window regulator mechanism.
- **Cost:** $300 - $500 per window

**Headlight Ballast Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Xenon headlights not turning on or flickering.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the headlight ballast.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600

### Summary Verdict
The 2008 Mercedes-Benz E300 (W211) offers a generally reliable ownership experience, particularly for its diesel variants. The most common areas requiring attention tend to be related to the diesel engine's emissions system (DPF, glow plugs) and the robust but complex 5-speed automatic transmission, with potential issues in the TCM or conductor plate. Electrical gremlins, often stemming from the SAM module, can also arise. While suspension components can wear, especially if equipped with air suspension, these are often manageable. Overall, proactive maintenance and addressing specific known issues can ensure the E300 remains a dependable luxury sedan.
