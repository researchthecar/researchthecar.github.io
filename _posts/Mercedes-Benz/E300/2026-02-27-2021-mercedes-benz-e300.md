# 2021 Mercedes-Benz E300 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a luxury executive car, understanding common owner-reported issues can help you make a confident decision. This reliability summary explores potential trouble spots reported by owners, ranging from engine valve wear and electrical quirks to transmission concerns. Reviewing these owner experiences allows prospective buyers to ask the right questions during pre-purchase inspections.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4 (E350)

The 2.0-liter turbocharged inline-4 engine produces approximately 255 horsepower and 273 lb-ft of torque, providing brisk acceleration and efficient cruising. While generally refined, some owners have reported cylinder misfires linked to valve wear.

**Cylinder Misfire and Valve Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Illuminated check engine light, engine misfires, and sudden entry into limp mode resulting in a loss of motive power.
- **Solution:** Requires professional diagnosis and potential replacement of worn exhaust valves or cylinder components.

### Fuel Pump and EVAP System

Fuel delivery and emissions management systems maintain strict environmental standards while ensuring steady fuel supply. Intermittent electrical warnings or sensor faults can occasionally trigger diagnostic trouble codes.

**Evaporative Emission Control Faults**
- **Symptoms:** Diagnostic trouble codes indicating electrical faults or short circuits within the EVAP system.
- **Solution:** Inspect wiring harnesses, control modules, and related emission components for faults.

## Transmission

### 9-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 9-speed automatic transmission delivers smooth and rapid gear changes tailored for both comfort and highway efficiency. While rare, isolated owner reports point to severe mechanical failures requiring comprehensive drivetrain service.

**Premature Transmission Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Catastrophic transmission failure while driving at highway speeds, leaving the vehicle completely disabled and unable to shift.
- **Solution:** Professional mechanical inspection and potential transmission overhaul or replacement.

## Brakes

### Parking Brake and Service Brakes

The braking system provides confident stopping power aided by electronic parking brake features. Intermittent locking or failure to release properly after sitting can create frustrating mobility hurdles.

**Emergency Brake Seizure**
- **Symptoms:** The electronic emergency brake or service brakes unexpectedly lock up or seize, preventing the vehicle from moving forward or backward.
- **Solution:** Inspect electronic brake actuators, sensors, and calipers for proper release and operation.

## Electrical

### 48V Mild-Hybrid Electrical System

The 48-volt electrical architecture powers advanced hybrid features, onboard electronics, and accessory systems. Battery and charging module malfunctions have been cited by some owners as a cause for sudden roadside immobilization.

**48-Volt Battery Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Sudden electrical system failure, instrument panel warnings, and total vehicle inoperability requiring a tow.
- **Solution:** Test the 48V battery and associated charging components, replacing failed modules as necessary.

## Summary Verdict

This luxury model offers impressive performance, advanced technology, and high-end comfort, but prospective buyers should remain thorough during their evaluation. Pay close attention to service history regarding electrical systems, battery health, and powertrain operation.
