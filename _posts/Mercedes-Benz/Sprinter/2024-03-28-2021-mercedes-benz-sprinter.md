**Introduction:**

The 2021 Mercedes-Benz Sprinter is a versatile full-size van that combines powerful performance with a spacious and well-equipped interior. It offers a wide range of engine and transmission options to meet varying needs and applications.

The 2.0L 4-cylinder turbocharged diesel engine produces 188 horsepower and 325 lb-ft of torque, while the 3.0L V6 turbocharged diesel engine delivers 190 horsepower and 332 lb-ft of torque. Both engines are paired with a 7-speed automatic transmission.

Users have praised the Sprinter for its impressive power, advanced safety features, and customizable interior. However, like any vehicle, it has also been reported to have certain issues.

**Engine**

**2.0L 4-Cylinder Turbocharged Diesel**

* **Fuel Injector Failure:** Symptoms include rough idling, poor fuel economy, and decreased power. Solution involves replacing the faulty fuel injectors. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
* **Turbocharger Failure:** Symptoms include excessive smoke, loss of power, and increased oil consumption. Solution involves replacing the faulty turbocharger. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
* **Diesel Particulate Filter (DPF) Clogging:** Symptoms include reduced engine performance, increased fuel consumption, and warning lights on the dashboard. Solution involves cleaning or replacing the clogged DPF. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+

**3.0L V6 Turbocharged Diesel**

* **EGR Valve Failure:** Symptoms include poor engine performance, reduced fuel economy, and increased emissions. Solution involves replacing the faulty EGR valve. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+
* **Intake Manifold Failure:** Symptoms include rough idling, poor acceleration, and increased engine noise. Solution involves replacing the faulty intake manifold. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+

**Transmission**

**7-Speed Automatic**

* **Shifting Issues:** Symptoms include difficulty shifting gears, delayed shifting, or slipping gears. Solution involves reprogramming the transmission or replacing faulty components. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+
* **Torque Converter Failure:** Symptoms include shuddering or slipping during acceleration, as well as transmission overheating. Solution involves replacing the faulty torque converter. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+

**Suspension**

* **No Issues Found**

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain:** Symptoms include difficulty starting the vehicle or dead battery after sitting for extended periods. Solution involves diagnosing and repairing the electrical system components causing the drain. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,000+
* **Electrical Faults:** Symptoms include malfunctioning electrical components, such as lights, wipers, or gauges. Solution involves diagnosing and repairing the faulty electrical components. Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the specific component

**Exterior**

* **Windshield Cracking:** Symptoms include large cracks forming on the windshield, often due to road debris. Solution involves replacing the windshield. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+
* **Body Panel Rust:** Symptoms include visible rust on the body panels, typically on older models or vehicles exposed to harsh weather conditions. Solution involves repairing or replacing the affected body panels. Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the extent of rust damage

**Interior**

* **Seat Adjustment Issues:** Symptoms include difficulty adjusting the driver's seat, including power seat malfunctions or broken seat tracks. Solution involves repairing or replacing the faulty seat components. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+
* **Dashboard Cracking:** Symptoms include cracks or splits forming on the dashboard, often due to sun exposure or extreme temperature changes. Solution involves replacing the dashboard. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+

**Summary:**

The 2021 Mercedes-Benz Sprinter is a reliable and versatile van with a number of impressive features. However, it is not without its issues, particularly in the engine and transmission departments. Some of these issues can be costly to repair, but overall, the Sprinter has a relatively low number of reported problems for a vehicle of its size and complexity.
