# 2014 Mercedes-Benz CLS63 AMG S

### Overview
The 2014 Mercedes-Benz CLS63 AMG S is a high-performance luxury sedan known for its potent powertrain and sophisticated design. As a flagship model, it generally exhibits strong build quality and reliability, particularly in its core mechanical components. However, like any complex performance vehicle, it can be susceptible to specific issues, often related to its advanced systems and high-output engine. Owners should be aware of potential concerns in areas such as the powertrain, electronics, and specific performance-oriented components. Regular maintenance and prompt attention to any developing problems are crucial for maintaining the CLS63 AMG S's exceptional performance and longevity.

---


## Engine

### 5.5L Twin-Turbo V8 (M157)
**Turbocharger Wastegate Actuator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Reduced engine power, boost leaks, check engine light illumination, rattling noise from turbo area.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the wastegate actuator or the complete turbocharger assembly.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1500

**Oil Cooler Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Coolant loss, oil in coolant, milky oil, overheating.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the oil cooler and potentially flushing the cooling system.
- **Cost:** $600 - $1200


## Transmission

### 7-Speed AMG SPEEDSHIFT MCT
**Torque Converter Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Slipping gears, harsh shifting, shuddering during acceleration, transmission warning light.
- **Solution:** Rebuilding or replacement of the torque converter.
- **Cost:** $1500 - $3000

**Mechatronics Unit Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Erratic shifting, inability to select gears, transmission fault messages.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of the mechatronics unit.
- **Cost:** $2000 - $4000


## Suspension

### AMG Sport Suspension
**Air Suspension Compressor Failure (Rear)**
- **Symptoms:** Rear end sagging, uneven ride height, 'Visit Workshop' message related to suspension.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the air suspension compressor.
- **Cost:** $700 - $1300

**Active Body Control (ABC) Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle leaning to one side, suspension fluid leaks, harsh ride.
- **Solution:** Repair of hydraulic lines, seals, or replacement of ABC pump/struts.
- **Cost:** $1000 - $3000+


## Electrical

### Infotainment System (COMAND)
**Screen Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** Flickering screen, unresponsive touch controls, system reboots.
- **Solution:** Software update or replacement of the COMAND unit.
- **Cost:** $500 - $2000


### Parking Sensors
**Sensor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** False alarms, system deactivation, 'Parking Assist Unavailable' message.
- **Solution:** Replacement of faulty sensor(s).
- **Cost:** $200 - $500

### Summary Verdict
The 2014 Mercedes-Benz CLS63 AMG S is a powerful and luxurious vehicle that generally offers a high level of reliability for its class. The primary areas of concern tend to be related to its high-performance AMG-specific components, such as the twin-turbo V8 engine's wastegate actuators and oil coolers, and the specialized AMG SPEEDSHIFT transmission's torque converter and mechatronics unit. The air suspension system can also present issues, particularly the compressor. Electrical gremlins, especially with the COMAND infotainment system, are not uncommon. While not plagued by widespread critical failures, potential buyers should budget for maintenance and potential repairs of these performance-oriented systems, as costs can be significant due to the specialized nature of AMG parts and labor.
