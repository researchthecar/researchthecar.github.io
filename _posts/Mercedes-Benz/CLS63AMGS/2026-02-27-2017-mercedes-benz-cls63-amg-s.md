# 2017 Mercedes-Benz CLS63 AMG S Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2017 Mercedes-Benz CLS63 AMG S, buyers are looking at a high-performance luxury four-door coupe that delivers blistering power and striking road presence. While owner complaints in public logs are relatively sparse, prospective buyers should carefully inspect high-wear components like wheels and suspension, as heavy performance usage can lead to costly maintenance items. A thorough pre-purchase inspection by an experienced Mercedes-AMG specialist is strongly recommended before finalizing a purchase.

---

## Engine

### 5.5L Twin-Turbo V8

The 5.5-liter twin-turbocharged V8 engine produces approximately 577 horsepower and 590 lb-ft of torque, offering immense performance. It is generally robust when properly maintained, but owners must strictly adhere to premium fluid and service intervals to avoid costly turbo or valvetrain wear.

## Transmission

### 7-Speed AMG Speedshift MCT Automatic

The 7-speed AMG Speedshift multi-clutch automatic transmission delivers rapid, aggressive gear changes suited to the vehicle's high-output character. While generally durable, smooth operation depends heavily on timely fluid changes, and repairs can be expensive if neglect occurs.

## Suspension

### Wheels

The suspension and wheel setup is tuned for aggressive handling and high-speed stability, though large low-profile tires and performance wheels make the setup sensitive to harsh road impacts.

**Factory Wheel Cracking and Blowouts**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have reported unexpected cracking on OEM factory wheels without notable impacts from potholes, leading to sudden tire pressure loss and repeated blowouts.
- **Solution:** Inspect all wheels closely for hairline fractures during a pre-purchase inspection and replace damaged rims with quality aftermarket or OEM replacements if structural compromises are found.

## Summary Verdict

The 2017 Mercedes-Benz CLS63 AMG S is an exhilarating performance machine that rewards enthusiastic drivers, provided they are prepared for high-end luxury maintenance costs. Prospective buyers should prioritize a comprehensive mechanical and structural inspection—paying close attention to wheels, tires, and powertrain health—to ensure the vehicle has been well-maintained.
