# 2021 Mercedes-Benz CLS53 AMG Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2021 Mercedes-Benz CLS53 AMG blends striking fastback styling with high-performance luxury, anchored by an electrified inline-six engine and sophisticated suspension and transmission systems. When shopping for a used luxury performance vehicle of this caliber, buyers should review service history carefully and watch for rare isolated component failures, such as wheel rim integrity.

---

## Engine

### 3.0L Turbocharged Inline-6 with EQ Boost (429 hp)

This 3.0-liter turbocharged inline-six engine features Mercedes-Benz's EQ Boost 48-volt mild-hybrid system, producing approximately 429 horsepower and 384 lb-ft of torque. It delivers rapid acceleration paired with smooth power delivery, though maintenance costs out of warranty can be substantial.

## Transmission

### AMG SPEEDSHIFT TCT 9-Speed Automatic

The 9-speed automatic transmission features quick shift response times tailored for sporty driving dynamics while maintaining highway cruising comfort. Routine fluid changes are vital to ensure long-term mechanical durability.

## Suspension

### Wheels and Rims

Suspension and wheel components are designed to handle high loads and dynamic cornering forces, though low-profile tires and large alloy wheels remain susceptible to structural impacts from road hazards.

**Rear Rim Cracking**
- **Symptoms:** A through-crack can develop on the shoulder of the wheel rim, resulting in a sudden loss of tire air pressure.
- **Solution:** Inspect wheels regularly for structural damage and replace any cracked or compromised wheel rims immediately.
