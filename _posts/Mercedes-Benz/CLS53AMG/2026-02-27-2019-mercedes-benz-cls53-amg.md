# 2019 Mercedes-Benz CLS53 AMG Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2019 Mercedes-Benz CLS-Class delivers striking styling, advanced technology, and high-performance luxury. Prospective buyers should review owner-reported feedback regarding wheel, tire, and electrical system concerns before purchasing a pre-owned model.

---

## Engine

### 3.0L Turbocharged Inline-6 (CLS 450)

The 3.0-liter turbocharged inline-6 engine paired with a 48-volt mild-hybrid system produces approximately 362 horsepower and 369 lb-ft of torque. It offers smooth power delivery and respectable efficiency for a luxury grand tourer, though owners should monitor the complex hybrid electronics.

### 3.0L Turbocharged and Supercharged Inline-6 (AMG CLS 53)

The AMG-enhanced 3.0-liter turbocharged and electric-auxiliary-supercharged inline-6 features EQ Boost and generates roughly 429 horsepower and 384 lb-ft of torque. It delivers exhilarating performance, though buyers should watch for occasional reports regarding engine wiring and diagnostic alerts.

**Electrical Wiring Harness**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle shaking and abnormal engine behavior due to wiring harness faults.
- **Solution:** Inspect and replace affected electrical wiring harnesses.
- **Cost:** $6,000

**Engine Clicking and Check Engine Light**
- **Symptoms:** Abnormal clicking noises from the engine bay accompanied by an illuminated check engine warning light.
- **Solution:** Have the vehicle diagnosed by a qualified technician to identify the source of mechanical noise.

## Transmission

### 9-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 9-speed automatic transmission channels power efficiently across rear-wheel and 4MATIC all-wheel-drive configurations. It generally provides smooth gear changes, but buyers should ensure proper diagnostic checks during a pre-purchase inspection.

**Motive Power Loss**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle inadvertently loses motive power while driving.
- **Solution:** Have the powertrain and electrical control modules inspected by a professional service center.

## Suspension

### Wheels and Tires

Low-profile tires and large alloy wheels enhance handling and appearance but leave the vehicle vulnerable to pothole damage and rim cracking on rough roadways.

**Rim Cracks and Tire Blowouts**
- **Symptoms:** Repeated tire blowouts, low tire pressure warnings, and cracked wheels after driving over minor road bumps.
- **Solution:** Inspect low-profile wheels for structural damage and replace compromised rims or tires.

## Electrical

### 48V Battery and Charging System

The electrical architecture relies heavily on advanced 48-volt systems to support mild-hybrid functions and cabin electronics. Owners have reported intermittent battery malfunctions that require professional diagnosis and component replacement.

**48V Battery Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Dashboard warnings indicating a 48V battery or electrical system failure, which can lead to sudden loss of vehicle operation.
- **Solution:** Test and replace failing 48-volt batteries or related charging components.

### Backup Camera

Advanced driver assistance features support daily safety, though minor electronic glitches such as intermittent rearview display failures have been noted by owners.

**Blank Rearview Camera Display**
- **Symptoms:** The rearview camera image appears completely black when shifting into reverse.
- **Solution:** Have the camera wiring and display module inspected by a dealer or specialist.

## Summary Verdict

The 2019 Mercedes-Benz CLS-Class offers impressive luxury, powerful engine options, and dynamic handling. While mechanical systems are generally robust, prospective buyers should thoroughly inspect the wheels, tires, and 48-volt electrical components for any signs of prior issues or wear.
