# 2023 Mercedes-Benz CLS53 AMG Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a luxury four-door coupe like the 2023 Mercedes-Benz CLS 53 AMG, buyers can expect exceptional performance, sleek styling, and advanced technology. However, prospective owners should carefully inspect areas like the factory wheel and tire assemblies, as owner reports highlight vulnerability to bending and cracking under normal driving conditions.

---

## Engine

### 3.0L Turbocharged Inline-6 with EQ Boost

This 3.0-liter turbocharged inline-six engine features an integrated electric auxiliary compressor and EQ Boost 48-volt mild-hybrid system, producing approximately 429 horsepower and 384 lb-ft of torque. It delivers exceptionally smooth power delivery and robust performance across the rev range. Routine maintenance and timely servicing are key to keeping this complex powertrain running reliably.

## Transmission

### AMG Speedshift TCT 9-Speed Automatic

The AMG Speedshift TCT 9-speed automatic transmission routes power through an all-wheel-drive system, offering rapid gear changes and refined cruising capability. It is well-matched to the high-output inline-six engine, though buyers should verify smooth operation during a test drive.

## Suspension

### Wheels and Tires

The suspension and wheel setup provides an athletic, responsive ride quality characteristic of AMG models, though low-profile tires and large alloy wheels can leave the vehicle susceptible to road hazard damage.

**Factory Alloy Wheel Failure and Tire Damage**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have reported chronic structural bending and cracking of factory alloy wheels when driving on normal roads at standard speeds, frequently resulting in sidewall tire bubbles and frequent tire replacements.
- **Solution:** Inspect all wheels closely for bends, cracks, or structural fatigue, and check tire sidewalls for bubbles or bulges. Replacement of damaged wheels and low-profile tires may be necessary.

## Summary Verdict

The 2023 Mercedes-Benz CLS 53 AMG offers thrilling performance and high-end luxury, but used-car buyers should thoroughly inspect the condition of the alloy wheels and low-profile tires for signs of bending or cracking. Aside from localized wheel concerns reported by owners, standard pre-purchase inspections remain recommended to ensure overall mechanical health.
