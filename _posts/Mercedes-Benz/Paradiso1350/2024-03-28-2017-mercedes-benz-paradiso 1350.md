**Introduction:**

The 2017 Mercedes-Benz Paradiso 1350 is a luxury coach bus that offers a comfortable and spacious interior for long-distance journeys. It comes with a powerful engine and a reliable transmission, making it a popular choice for tour operators and travelers alike. The Paradiso 1350 is available with two engine and transmission combinations:

* **OM 471 Inline-6 Diesel Engine** (394 hp / 1,400 lb-ft torque) with **Mercedes PowerShift G 281 6-Speed Automated Manual Transmission**
* **OM 473 V8 Diesel Engine** (510 hp / 1,850 lb-ft torque) with **Mercedes PowerShift G 330 8-Speed Automated Manual Transmission**

People generally praise the Paradiso 1350 for its comfort, handling, and fuel efficiency. However, like any vehicle, it has its own set of issues that owners have reported.

**Engine**
* **OM 471 Inline-6 Diesel Engine**
    * **EGR Valve Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: The exhaust gas recirculation (EGR) valve can malfunction, causing reduced engine performance, increased emissions, and a check engine light illumination.
        * Solution: Replace the EGR valve.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
        * Recalls: None Found
* **OM 473 V8 Diesel Engine**
    * **Fuel Injector Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: The fuel injectors can become clogged or malfunction, leading to rough idling, reduced power, and black smoke from the exhaust.
        * Solution: Replace the faulty fuel injectors.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600 per injector
        * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**
* **Mercedes PowerShift G 281 6-Speed Automated Manual Transmission**
    * **Clutch Wear**
        * Description/Symptoms: Excessive clutch wear can cause slipping, shuddering, and difficulty shifting gears.
        * Solution: Replace the clutch.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,000
        * Recalls: None Found
* **Mercedes PowerShift G 330 8-Speed Automated Manual Transmission**
    * **Mechatronic Unit Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: The mechatronic unit, which controls the transmission's electronic and hydraulic functions, can fail, resulting in erratic shifting, loss of gears, and a check transmission light illumination.
        * Solution: Replace the mechatronic unit.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $4,000 - $6,000
        * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**
* **No Issues Found**

**Electrical**
* **Battery Discharge**
        * Description/Symptoms: The battery can discharge prematurely, leading to difficulty starting the vehicle or complete electrical failure.
        * Solution: Inspect and test the battery and charging system. Replace the battery if necessary.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
        * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**
* **Door Hinge Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: The door hinges can wear out or break, causing the doors to sag or become misaligned.
        * Solution: Replace the faulty door hinges.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500 per hinge
        * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**
* **Dashboard Panel Cracks**
        * Description/Symptoms: The dashboard panel can develop cracks over time, especially in hot climates.
        * Solution: Replace the damaged dashboard panel.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
        * Recalls: None Found
* **HVAC System Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: The heating, ventilation, and air conditioning (HVAC) system can malfunction, leading to issues with temperature control, airflow, and defrosting.
        * Solution: Diagnose the issue and repair or replace the faulty components.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**
Overall, the 2017 Mercedes-Benz Paradiso 1350 is a reliable and comfortable coach bus with ample power and fuel efficiency. However, like any vehicle, it has its share of issues, ranging from minor inconveniences to more significant mechanical problems. The most common issues reported by owners are EGR valve failure (OM 471 engine), fuel injector failure (OM 473 engine), clutch wear (G 281 transmission), mechatronic unit failure (G 330 transmission), and battery discharge. While these issues can be expensive to repair, they are generally not widespread and can be effectively addressed by qualified mechanics.
