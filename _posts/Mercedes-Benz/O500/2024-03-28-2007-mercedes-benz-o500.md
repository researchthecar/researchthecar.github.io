**Introduction:**

The 2007 Mercedes-Benz O500 is a coach bus powered by a single engine option and transmission option. It offers a spacious interior, ample storage, and a comfortable ride for passengers. However, owners have reported a number of issues with the vehicle.

**Engine**

**OM906LA (408 hp | 1,450 lb-ft)**

* **EGR Valve Failure:** The EGR valve can fail, causing a loss of power and increased fuel consumption. **Solution:** Replace the EGR valve. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $500 - $800+
* **Turbocharger Failure:** The turbocharger can fail, leading to reduced boost pressure and a loss of power. **Solution:** Replace the turbocharger. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $1,500 - $2,500+

**Transmission**

**G 856 (Manual)**

* **Synchros Worn:** The synchros in the transmission can wear out, causing difficulty shifting gears. **Solution:** Rebuild the transmission. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $2,000 - $3,000+

**Suspension**

* **Air Suspension Leaks:** The air suspension can develop leaks, causing the bus to sag or bounce. **Solution:** Find and seal the leak. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $200 - $500+
* **Shock Absorber Failure:** The shock absorbers can fail, resulting in a rough ride. **Solution:** Replace the shock absorbers. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $300 - $600+

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain:** The battery can drain prematurely, causing difficulty starting the vehicle. **Solution:** Check for parasitic battery drains and replace the battery if necessary. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $100 - $500+
* **Alternator Failure:** The alternator can fail, preventing the battery from charging. **Solution:** Replace the alternator. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $500 - $800+

**Exterior**

* **Rust on Undercarriage:** The undercarriage of the bus can develop rust, especially in areas exposed to salt or moisture. **Solution:** Inspect the undercarriage for rust and apply anti-rust treatment if necessary. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $500 - $1,000+

**Interior**

* **Roof Leak:** The roof can develop a leak, allowing water to enter the bus. **Solution:** Locate and seal the leak. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $200 - $500+
* **Dashboard Cracks:** The dashboard can develop cracks over time. **Solution:** Replace the dashboard or cover it with a dashboard cover. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $500 - $1,000+

**Summary:**

The 2007 Mercedes-Benz O500 has some known issues that range in severity and cost to fix. Common problems include EGR valve failure, turbocharger failure, suspension leaks, and battery drain. While some issues can be minor and inexpensive to repair, others can be more serious and costly. Overall, it is important for owners to be aware of these potential issues before purchasing or operating this vehicle.
