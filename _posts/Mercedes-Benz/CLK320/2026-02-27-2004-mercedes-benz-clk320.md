# 2004 Mercedes-Benz CLK320

### Overview
The 2004 Mercedes-Benz CLK320, part of the W209 generation, is generally considered a reliable luxury coupe and convertible. It offers a blend of performance, comfort, and Mercedes-Benz's signature build quality. While most owners report a positive ownership experience, like any vehicle of this age, certain components can be prone to issues. This analysis focuses on common, documented reliability concerns beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential problem areas and their associated costs for the specific engine and transmission configurations available for this model year. Understanding these potential issues can help prospective buyers and current owners make informed decisions regarding maintenance and repairs.

---


## Engine

### 3.2L V6 (M112)
**Intake Manifold Gasket Leak**
- **Symptoms:** Coolant leaks, sweet smell of coolant, engine misfires, overheating.
- **Solution:** Replacement of intake manifold gaskets.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600

**Oil Cooler Seal Leak**
- **Symptoms:** Coolant in oil, oil in coolant, milky residue in coolant reservoir or on oil cap.
- **Solution:** Replacement of oil cooler seals.
- **Cost:** $400 - $700

**Camshaft Position Sensor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Check engine light, rough idling, stalling, difficulty starting.
- **Solution:** Replacement of camshaft position sensor(s).
- **Cost:** $200 - $400


## Transmission

### 5-Speed Automatic (722.6)
**Transmission Control Module (TCM) Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Harsh shifting, slipping gears, 'limp mode', transmission warning light.
- **Solution:** Replacement or repair of the TCM.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1500

**Conductor Plate Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Erratic shifting, no gear engagement, transmission warning light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the conductor plate.
- **Cost:** $500 - $900

## Electrical

### General Electrical System
**Window Regulator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Power windows not operating, slow window movement, grinding noise.
- **Solution:** Replacement of window regulator assembly.
- **Cost:** $250 - $500 per window

**Headlight Switch Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Headlights not turning on/off, dimming issues, dashboard lights flickering.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the headlight switch.
- **Cost:** $200 - $400

**Keyless Entry/Remote Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Remote not working, intermittent locking/unlocking, battery drain.
- **Solution:** Battery replacement in key fob, reprogramming, or replacement of receiver module.
- **Cost:** $100 - $500

## Interior

### Cabin Components
**Pixelated LCD Display (Instrument Cluster/Radio)**
- **Symptoms:** Missing segments or lines on the digital displays.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of the LCD display unit.
- **Cost:** $300 - $800

**HVAC Blower Motor Resistor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Fan only works on highest setting, or not at all.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the blower motor resistor.
- **Cost:** $150 - $300

### Summary Verdict
The 2004 Mercedes-Benz CLK320 (W209) with the 3.2L V6 engine and 5-speed automatic transmission is a generally dependable luxury vehicle. The most common areas for potential concern revolve around the engine's intake manifold gaskets and oil cooler seals, as well as transmission issues like TCM or conductor plate failures, which can be costly to repair. Electrical gremlins, particularly with window regulators and headlight switches, are also noted. Interior electronic displays can suffer from pixel degradation. However, many of these issues are age-related and can be mitigated with diligent maintenance. Overall, the CLK320 offers a solid ownership experience if potential buyers are aware of and prepared for these specific reliability points.
