**Introduction:**

The 1998 Mercedes-Benz CLK320 is a luxurious grand tourer that offers a refined driving experience. It is equipped with a 3.2L V6 engine producing 215 horsepower and 229 lb-ft of torque, paired with a 5-speed automatic transmission. The CLK320 is praised for its elegant design, comfortable interior, and responsive handling. However, like any vehicle, it has its share of reported issues.

**Engine**

**3.2L V6 Engine**

* **Intake manifold gasket failure**
    * **Description/Symptoms:** Leaking coolant, rough idle, engine overheating
    * **Solution:** Replace intake manifold gasket
    * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $500 - $1000
    * **Recalls:** None Found
* **Crankshaft position sensor failure**
    * **Description/Symptoms:** Engine stalling, difficulty starting, erratic idle
    * **Solution:** Replace crankshaft position sensor
    * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $200 - $400
    * **Recalls:** None Found

**Transmission**

**5-Speed Automatic Transmission**

* **Slipping gears**
    * **Description/Symptoms:** Transmission slipping out of gear while driving, difficulty shifting gears
    * **Solution:** Replace or rebuild transmission
    * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $2000 - $3500+
    * **Recalls:** None Found
* **Torque converter failure**
    * **Description/Symptoms:** Transmission shuddering, overheating, loss of power
    * **Solution:** Replace torque converter
    * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $1000 - $1500+
    * **Recalls:** None Found

**Suspension**

* **Rear suspension arm bushing failure**
    * **Description/Symptoms:** Clunking noise over bumps, excessive suspension travel, uneven tire wear
    * **Solution:** Replace rear suspension arm bushings
    * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $250 - $500
    * **Recalls:** None Found

**Electrical**

* **Headlight switch failure**
    * **Description/Symptoms:** Headlights failing to turn on or flickering
    * **Solution:** Replace headlight switch
    * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $200 - $400
    * **Recalls:** None Found
* **Battery drain**
    * **Description/Symptoms:** Battery discharging overnight, difficulty starting the car
    * **Solution:** Identify and repair electrical drain, replace battery
    * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $50 - $200+
    * **Recalls:** None Found

**Exterior**

* **Sunroof leak**
    * **Description/Symptoms:** Water leaking into the passenger compartment through the sunroof
    * **Solution:** Replace or repair sunroof seals
    * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $500 - $1000
    * **Recalls:** None Found
* **Door lock malfunction**
    * **Description/Symptoms:** Doors failing to lock or unlock, keyless entry not working
    * **Solution:** Replace door lock actuators
    * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $200 - $400 per door
    * **Recalls:** None Found

**Interior**

* **Air conditioning evaporator core failure**
    * **Description/Symptoms:** Air conditioning blowing warm air, strange odor from vents
    * **Solution:** Replace evaporator core
    * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $1000 - $1500
    * **Recalls:** None Found
* **SRS airbag module malfunction**
    * **Description/Symptoms:** Airbag warning light illuminated, airbags not deploying in a crash
    * **Solution:** Replace SRS airbag module
    * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $500 - $1000
    * **Recalls:** None Found

**Summary:**

The 1998 Mercedes-Benz CLK320 has a range of issues that can affect its performance, comfort, and safety. While some issues are relatively minor and inexpensive to fix, others can be more severe and costly. Potential owners should be aware of these issues before purchasing a CLK320 and factor in the potential repair costs when making their decision.
