## 2004 Mercedes-Benz CLK500

The 2004 Mercedes-Benz CLK500 is a luxurious coupe powered by a high-performance V8 engine. It offers a comfortable and stylish driving experience with a spacious interior and sleek exterior. However, like any vehicle, it is not without its potential issues.

Other engine and transmission options for the 2004 Mercedes-Benz CLK-Class include:

- CLK320: 3.2L V6, 215 hp, 229 lb-ft of torque
- CLK430: 4.3L V8, 275 hp, 295 lb-ft of torque
- CLK55 AMG: 5.4L V8, 362 hp, 376 lb-ft of torque

People like the 2004 Mercedes-Benz CLK500 for its stylish design, powerful engine, and luxurious interior. However, some common complaints include its high maintenance costs, lack of rear seat space, and outdated infotainment system.

**Engine**
- **3.5L V6**
    - **Intake Manifold Failure:**
        - Description/Symptoms: The plastic intake manifold can crack, causing coolant and oil leaks, reduced engine power, and potential overheating.
        - Solution: Replacement of the intake manifold.
        - Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000
        - Recalls: None Found
    - **Crankshaft Position Sensor Failure:**
        - Description/Symptoms: The crankshaft position sensor may fail, causing the engine to stall or have difficulty starting. Engine light will come on.
        - Solution: Replacement of the crankshaft position sensor.
        - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
        - Recalls: None Found

- **5.0L V8**
    - **Valve Stem Seal Leakage:**
        - Description/Symptoms: Worn valve stem seals can cause excessive oil consumption and blue smoke from the exhaust.
        - Solution: Replacement of the valve stem seals. May require cylinder head removal.
        - Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000
        - Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**
- **5-Speed Automatic Transmission**
    - **Torque Converter Lock-up Clutch Failure:**
        - Description/Symptoms: The torque converter lock-up clutch can fail, leading to shuddering or slipping during gear changes.
        - Solution: Rebuild or replacement of the torque converter.
        - Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500
        - Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**
- **No Issues Found**

**Electrical**
- **Battery Discharge:**
    - Description/Symptoms: The battery may discharge prematurely, causing difficulty starting the vehicle.
    - Solution: Testing and replacement of the battery as needed.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200
    - Recalls: None Found
- **Electrical Harness Issues:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Electrical harnesses can experience corrosion or damage, leading to various electrical problems such as malfunctioning lights, gauges, or components.
    - Solution: Repair or replacement of the affected electrical harness.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500
    - Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**
- **Door Lock Actuator Failure:**
    - Description/Symptoms: The door lock actuators may fail, preventing the doors from locking or unlocking.
    - Solution: Replacement of the door lock actuators.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500
    - Recalls: None Found

**Interior**
- **Navigation System Failure:**
    - Description/Symptoms: The navigation system may experience software glitches or hardware failures, resulting in incorrect navigation information or a blank screen.
    - Solution: Software updates or replacement of the navigation unit.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
    - Recalls: None Found
- **Climate Control Unit Failure:**
    - Description/Symptoms: The climate control unit may malfunction, causing problems with temperature control, air flow, or other climate functions.
    - Solution: Replacement of the climate control unit.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800
    - Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

Overall, the 2004 Mercedes-Benz CLK500 is a capable and luxurious vehicle. However, potential issues can arise, particularly with the engine, transmission, and electrical components. It is important to consider the potential costs of repairs before purchasing this vehicle. The severity and frequency of these issues can vary, so it is recommended to consider maintenance history and have the vehicle inspected by a qualified mechanic before purchasing.
