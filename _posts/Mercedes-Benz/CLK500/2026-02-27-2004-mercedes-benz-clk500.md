# 2004 Mercedes-Benz CLK500

### Overview
The 2004 Mercedes-Benz CLK500, part of the W209 generation, is a luxury grand tourer known for its blend of performance and comfort. Powered by a robust 5.0-liter V8 engine, it offers brisk acceleration and a refined driving experience. While generally well-regarded for its build quality and sophisticated design, like any vehicle of its age, it can exhibit specific reliability concerns. Owners often report on the performance of the powertrain, the electronic systems, and the wear on interior and exterior components. This analysis focuses on documented common issues beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential repair costs and solutions for prospective or current owners.

---


## Engine

### 5.0L M113 V8
**Ignition Coil Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Misfires, rough idling, check engine light, reduced power.
- **Solution:** Replace faulty ignition coil(s).
- **Cost:** $300 - $600 per coil

**Oil Cooler Seals Leak**
- **Symptoms:** Oil leaks around the engine block, potential coolant contamination.
- **Solution:** Replace leaking oil cooler seals.
- **Cost:** $500 - $900

**Mass Air Flow (MAF) Sensor Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Hesitation during acceleration, rough idle, poor fuel economy, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Clean or replace the MAF sensor.
- **Cost:** $200 - $500


## Transmission

### 5G-Tronic (722.6) Automatic
**Transmission Control Module (TCM) Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Erratic shifting, limp mode, transmission not engaging gears.
- **Solution:** Replace or repair the TCM.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1500

**Conductor Plate Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Harsh shifting, delayed engagement, transmission errors.
- **Solution:** Replace the conductor plate assembly.
- **Cost:** $600 - $1000


## Suspension

### Standard Chassis
**ABC (Active Body Control) System Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle sagging, suspension warning lights, fluid leaks.
- **Solution:** Repair or replace leaking hydraulic lines, struts, or pump.
- **Cost:** $1000 - $3000+

**Control Arm Bushing Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Clunking noises over bumps, vague steering feel, uneven tire wear.
- **Solution:** Replace worn control arm bushings.
- **Cost:** $400 - $800


## Electrical

### General Electrical System
**Window Regulator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Power windows not operating, slow or stuck windows.
- **Solution:** Replace the window regulator mechanism.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600 per window

**Headlight Ballast Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Xenon headlights not turning on or flickering.
- **Solution:** Replace the headlight ballast unit.
- **Cost:** $300 - $700 per headlight

**SAM Unit (Signal Acquisition Module) Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Various electrical gremlins, non-functioning lights, wipers, or other accessories.
- **Solution:** Repair or replace the SAM unit.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1200

## Interior

### Cabin Components
**Headliner Sagging**
- **Symptoms:** Fabric on the headliner detaches and sags.
- **Solution:** Re-glue or replace the headliner.
- **Cost:** $300 - $700

**Pixelated Instrument Cluster Display**
- **Symptoms:** Missing or distorted pixels on the odometer or other displays.
- **Solution:** Repair or replace the instrument cluster.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1000

### Summary Verdict
The 2004 Mercedes-Benz CLK500 offers a compelling package for those seeking a sporty and luxurious coupe. Its V8 engine and refined chassis provide an engaging driving experience. However, potential buyers should be aware of common issues, particularly with the Active Body Control suspension, electrical components like the SAM unit and window regulators, and the 5G-Tronic transmission's conductor plate and TCM. Engine-related concerns, such as ignition coil failures and oil cooler seal leaks, are also documented. While interior issues like sagging headliners and pixelated displays can occur, they are generally less critical. Addressing these potential problems proactively can help maintain the CLK500's performance and longevity.
