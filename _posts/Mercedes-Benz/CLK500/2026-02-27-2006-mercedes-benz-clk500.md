# 2006 Mercedes-Benz CLK500

### Overview
The 2006 Mercedes-Benz CLK500, part of the W209 generation, is a luxury grand tourer known for its blend of performance and comfort. Powered by a robust 5.0-liter V8 engine, it offers strong acceleration and a refined driving experience. While generally well-regarded for its build quality and luxury appointments, like any vehicle of this age and complexity, it can experience specific issues. Owners often report on the reliability of its powertrain, the sophisticated electronics, and the wear on interior and exterior components. This analysis focuses on documented common problems beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential repair costs and solutions for this model.

---


## Engine

### 5.0L M113 V8
**Oil Cooler Seals Leak**
- **Symptoms:** Oil in coolant reservoir, coolant in oil, overheating.
- **Solution:** Replace oil cooler seals.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600

**Camshaft Adjuster Solenoids**
- **Symptoms:** Check engine light, rough idle, reduced engine power.
- **Solution:** Replace faulty camshaft adjuster solenoids.
- **Cost:** $400 - $800


## Transmission

### 5G-Tronic (722.6) 5-Speed Automatic
**Transmission Control Module (TCM) Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Harsh shifting, slipping gears, 'limp mode', transmission warning light.
- **Solution:** Replace or repair the TCM.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1500

**Conductor Plate Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Erratic shifting, no gear engagement, transmission warning light.
- **Solution:** Replace the conductor plate and associated sensors.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1000

## Electrical

### General Electrical System
**Window Regulator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Power windows not operating, window stuck in position.
- **Solution:** Replace the window regulator mechanism.
- **Cost:** $300 - $500

**Headlight Ballast Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Xenon headlights not turning on or flickering.
- **Solution:** Replace the headlight ballast unit.
- **Cost:** $400 - $700

### Cabin Components
**Seat Heater Malfunction**

### Summary Verdict
The 2006 Mercedes-Benz CLK500 (W209) generally presents good reliability for its class and age, particularly with its potent V8 engine. However, potential owners should be aware of common issues such as leaks from the oil cooler seals and solenoid failures in the engine. The 5-speed automatic transmission, while robust, can experience problems with its control module or conductor plate, leading to shifting issues. Electrical gremlins, like failing window regulators and headlight ballasts, are also noted. Interior components, such as seat heaters and the instrument cluster display, can also develop faults. Addressing these potential issues proactively can help maintain the CLK500's luxury driving experience.
