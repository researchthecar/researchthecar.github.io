# 2008 Mercedes-Benz CLK500

### Overview
The 2008 Mercedes-Benz CLK500, part of the W209 generation, is a luxury grand tourer known for its blend of performance and comfort. Powered by a robust V8 engine, it offers a refined driving experience. While generally well-regarded for its build quality and engineering, like any vehicle of this age and complexity, certain areas may require attention. This analysis focuses on documented technical reliability concerns beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential issues that owners might encounter. The CLK500 aims for a balance between sporty dynamics and long-distance cruising capability, making its reliability crucial for owner satisfaction.

---


## Engine

### 5.0L M113 V8
**Oil Cooler Seals**
- **Symptoms:** Oil leaks detected around the oil cooler housing, potentially leading to low oil levels.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the oil cooler seals.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600

**Intake Manifold Gasket Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Vacuum leaks, rough idle, check engine light, potential misfires.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the intake manifold gaskets.
- **Cost:** $400 - $800


## Transmission

### 5G-Tronic (722.6) Automatic
**Transmission Control Module (TCM) Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Erratic shifting, limp mode engagement, failure to shift gears.
- **Solution:** Replacement or repair of the TCM.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1500

**Conductor Plate Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Harsh shifting, delayed engagement, transmission warning light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the conductor plate.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1000

## Electrical

### General Electrical System
**Window Regulator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Power windows not operating, slow or stuck window operation.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the window regulator mechanism.
- **Cost:** $300 - $500

**Headlight Ballast Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Xenon headlights not turning on or flickering.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the headlight ballast unit.
- **Cost:** $400 - $700

### Summary Verdict
The 2008 Mercedes-Benz CLK500 is a capable luxury coupe with a generally solid mechanical foundation. The primary areas of concern tend to be related to the aging of specific components within the powertrain and electrical systems, such as oil cooler seals, intake manifold gaskets, and transmission control modules. These issues, while not necessarily universal, are documented enough to warrant consideration. Routine maintenance and prompt attention to early symptoms are key to maintaining the CLK500's reliability. Overall, with proper care and addressing potential age-related issues, the CLK500 can remain a dependable and enjoyable vehicle.
