**Introduction:**

The 2021 Mercedes-Benz Boxer 50 is a high-powered sports car powered by an 8-cylinder engine. It offers exceptional performance and handling. The Boxer 50 is popular for its sleek design, luxurious interior, and advanced technology. However, like all vehicles, it has some reported issues.

**Engine**

**4.0 Liter Twin-Turbocharged V8**

* **Excessive Oil Consumption:** Excessive oil consumption leading to low oil levels and potential engine damage. Symptoms include illuminated oil warning light and frequent oil top-ups.
* **Solution:** Engine rebuild or replacement of piston rings, valve guides, or oil pump.
* **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $5,000 - $10,000+

**Transmission**

* **7-Speed Automatic Transmission**

* **Jerky Shifting:** Hesitation or rough shifting during gear changes. Symptoms include delayed or sudden shifts.
* **Solution:** Transmission fluid flush, software update, or transmission replacement.
* **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $500 - $3,000+

**Suspension**

* **No Issues Found**

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain:** Premature battery discharge, leading to difficulty starting the vehicle. Symptoms include frequent battery replacement.
* **Solution:** Diagnostics to identify faulty components, such as parasitic draws or alternator issues. Battery replacement may also be necessary.
* **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $100 - $1,000+
* **Recall:** NHTSA Campaign Number: 22V371000, Issue: Battery Discharge

**Exterior**

* **Clearcoat Peeling:** Peeling or blistering of the clearcoat on the exterior paint. Symptoms include discoloration or dullness of the paint.
* **Solution:** Professional paint repair or repainting.
* **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $500 - $2,000+

**Interior**

* **Infotainment System Glitches:** Software malfunctions causing freezing, lagging, or connectivity issues with the infotainment system.
* **Solution:** Software updates or replacement of the infotainment unit.
* **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $500 - $2,000+

**Summary:**

The 2021 Mercedes-Benz Boxer 50 is a reliable and enjoyable sports car with some reported issues. The engine and transmission problems can be severe and costly to fix, while other issues like electrical glitches and clearcoat peeling are less common and less expensive. Overall, the Boxer 50 remains a desirable vehicle, but potential buyers should be aware of these potential issues before making a purchase.
