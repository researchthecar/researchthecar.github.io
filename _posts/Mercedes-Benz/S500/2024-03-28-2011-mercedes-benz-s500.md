**Introduction:**

The 2011 Mercedes-Benz S500 is a luxury sedan powered by a 4.7L V8 engine generating 435 horsepower and 516 lb-ft of torque. Paired with a 7-speed automatic transmission, this model delivers exceptional power and efficiency. While it boasts advanced features and luxurious amenities, the S500 has also faced some common issues.

**Engine**

- **4.7L V8:**
    - **Oil Leak from Camshaft Adjusters:** Oil seepage can occur due to faulty camshaft adjusters, resulting in oil consumption and potential engine damage.
        - Solution: Replace camshaft adjusters.
        - Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500
        - Recalls: None Found
- **Fuel Injectors Sticking:** Stuck fuel injectors can cause engine misfires, rough idling, and decreased fuel efficiency.
        - Solution: Clean or replace fuel injectors.
        - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
        - Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

- **7-Speed Automatic:**
    - **Shifting Delay or Jerking:** Hesitation or rough shifting may indicate transmission fluid contamination or solenoid issues.
        - Solution: Flush transmission fluid and clean or replace solenoids.
        - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500
        - Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

- **Air Suspension Compressor Failure:** The compressor that provides air pressure for the suspension system can malfunction, leading to a sagging ride height or suspension failure.
        - Solution: Replace air suspension compressor.
        - Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500
        - Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

- **Battery Drain:** Premature battery discharge can be caused by faulty electrical components, such as the navigation system or trunk latch.
        - Solution: Diagnose and repair faulty component.
        - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800
        - Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

- **Sunroof Rattle:** A loose or misaligned sunroof mechanism can cause rattling noises while driving.
        - Solution: Adjust or replace sunroof tracks.
        - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
        - Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

- **Steering Wheel Vibration:** A vibrating steering wheel at certain speeds can indicate worn or damaged steering components.
        - Solution: Replace steering components.
        - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,200
        - Recalls: None Found
- **Climate Control Malfunction:** The climate control system may fail to regulate temperature or airflow due to faulty sensors or actuators.
        - Solution: Diagnose and repair faulty component.
        - Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,000
        - Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

The 2011 Mercedes-Benz S500 is a luxurious and powerful sedan, but it has faced some common issues. While some problems are minor and inexpensive to fix, others can be more severe and costly. Potential owners should consider these issues before purchasing and factor in the potential cost of repairs.
