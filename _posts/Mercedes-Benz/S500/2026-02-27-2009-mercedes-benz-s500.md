# 2009 Mercedes-Benz S500

### Overview
The 2009 Mercedes-Benz S500, representing the W221 generation, is a luxury sedan known for its advanced technology and comfort. While generally well-regarded for its performance and sophisticated features, like any complex vehicle, it can experience specific reliability concerns. This analysis focuses on documented technical issues beyond routine maintenance, highlighting areas that may require attention for owners of this model year. The S500 typically came with a powerful V8 engine, and its advanced systems, while offering a premium experience, can also be sources of potential repair needs.

---


## Engine

### 5.5L V8 (M273)
**Timing Chain Tensioner Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Rattling noise from the front of the engine, especially on startup; check engine light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of timing chain tensioners, guides, and potentially the chain itself.
- **Cost:** $1,500 - $3,000

**Oil Leaks from Valve Covers/Gaskets**
- **Symptoms:** Visible oil leaks around the top of the engine; burning oil smell.
- **Solution:** Replacement of valve cover gaskets and potentially spark plug well seals.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1,000


## Transmission

### 7G-TRONIC (722.9)
**Valve Body Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** Harsh shifting, delayed engagement, slipping gears, transmission warning light.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of the transmission valve body; transmission fluid and filter service.
- **Cost:** $1,000 - $3,500

**Torque Converter Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Shuddering at low speeds, rough idle, potential for stalling.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the torque converter.
- **Cost:** $1,200 - $2,500


## Suspension

### ABC (Active Body Control)
**Hydraulic Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle sagging on one side, 'ABC system temporarily unavailable' warning message, fluid leaks under the car.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of leaking suspension struts, hoses, or pump; fluid flush.
- **Cost:** $1,000 - $4,000+

**Pump Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of suspension height control, loud whining noise from the pump.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the ABC hydraulic pump.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1,500


## Electrical

### Infotainment System (COMAND)
**Screen Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** Flickering screen, unresponsive touch controls, dead pixels.
- **Solution:** Software update or replacement of the COMAND unit/screen.
- **Cost:** $500 - $2,000


### SAM (Signal Acquisition Module) Issues
**Corrosion/Water Intrusion**
- **Symptoms:** Various electrical gremlins, non-functional lights, power windows, or other accessories.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of the affected SAM module, addressing the source of water intrusion.
- **Cost:** $600 - $1,500

### Summary Verdict
The 2009 Mercedes-Benz S500 offers a luxurious driving experience but comes with potential reliability concerns, particularly in its advanced systems. The engine can suffer from timing chain tensioner wear and oil leaks. The 7-speed automatic transmission may develop issues with the valve body or torque converter. The sophisticated Active Body Control (ABC) suspension is prone to hydraulic leaks and pump failures, which can be costly to repair. Electrical gremlins, especially related to the COMAND infotainment system and SAM modules, are also noted. While the exterior and interior are generally robust, owners should be prepared for potential high repair costs associated with the complex engineering of this flagship sedan.
