The 2022 Mercedes-Benz S500 represents a pinnacle of luxury and technology in the automotive world. The S500 comes standard with a 3.0-liter turbocharged inline-6 engine producing 429 horsepower and 384 lb-ft of torque, paired with a 9G-TRONIC nine-speed automatic transmission. There is also an electric motor that puts out an additional 21 hp and 184 lb-ft of torque. Some owners praise the S500 for its opulent interior, smooth ride quality, and advanced driver-assistance systems. Conversely, complaints often revolve around the complexity of the infotainment system, the high cost of maintenance, and occasional glitches in the electronic features. Other engine options for the 2022 S-Class include the S580 with a 4.0-liter twin-turbo V8 engine or the S580e plug-in hybrid.

**Electrical**

*   **MBUX Infotainment System Freezing/Crashing**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The Mercedes-Benz User Experience (MBUX) system occasionally freezes, crashes, or becomes unresponsive. This can affect navigation, climate control, and other vehicle functions controlled through the system.
    *   Solution: Software updates from the dealership, sometimes requiring a full system reset.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: Covered under warranty, otherwise $150+ for diagnostics and software updates.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **360-Degree Camera Malfunctions**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The 360-degree camera system can display distorted images, fail to activate, or show error messages. This impacts parking and maneuvering in tight spaces.
    *   Solution: Recalibration of the camera system or replacement of faulty camera modules.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+ depending on the repair needed.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Digital Light System Errors**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The advanced "Digital Light" system, if equipped, may display error messages or malfunction, causing the headlights to not function optimally.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis of the headlight control module and potential replacement of the faulty unit.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2000+ depending on the repair needed.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Malfunctioning Power Seats**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Power seats may become unresponsive, move erratically, or fail to retain memory settings.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis of the seat control module or motor replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1000+ depending on the repair needed.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Air Conditioning System Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Issues with the AC system are common and range from not cooling properly, to not blowing cold air at all.
    *   Solution: Check for the source of the freon leak and replace parts if necessary, perform a AC system flush, and then refill with Freon.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1500+ depending on the repair needed.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

No Issues Found

**Engine**

**3.0-Liter Turbocharged Inline-6 (M256)**

*   **Engine Misfires**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Engine misfires can occur due to faulty spark plugs, coil packs, or fuel injectors. Symptoms include rough idling, reduced power, and a check engine light.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis of the cause of the misfire and replacement of the faulty component.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $800+ depending on the repair needed.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Oil Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks can occur from various engine seals and gaskets, leading to oil consumption and potential engine damage.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis of the source of the leak and replacement of the affected seals or gaskets.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1000+ depending on the repair needed.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**9G-TRONIC Nine-Speed Automatic**

*   **Harsh Shifting**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The 9G-TRONIC transmission may exhibit harsh or jerky shifting, especially during low-speed maneuvers or when shifting between certain gears.
    *   Solution: Transmission software update or valve body replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $2000+ depending on the repair needed.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Air Suspension Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The air suspension system may fail, resulting in a loss of ride height, uneven suspension, or error messages on the dashboard.
    *   Solution: Replacement of faulty air struts, air compressor, or suspension control module.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $3000+ depending on the repair needed.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

The 2022 Mercedes-Benz S500 can present some electrical and suspension issues. Many electrical issues are due to the complexity of the infotainment system and can be resolved with software updates. Suspension issues tend to be expensive due to the expensive parts. Engine and transmission issues are less common but can be costly if they occur. Potential buyers should be aware of these potential problems and budget accordingly for maintenance and repairs.
