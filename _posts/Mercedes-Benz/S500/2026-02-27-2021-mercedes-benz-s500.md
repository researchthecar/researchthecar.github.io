# 2021 Mercedes-Benz S500 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2021 Mercedes-Benz S500 delivers world-class luxury, advanced technology, and refined performance. However, as an early model year of a heavily redesigned generation, prospective buyers should review owner-reported complaints carefully. While luxury flagships are marvels of engineering when functioning properly, electronic complexity and high-end component wear require attention.

---

## Engine

### 3.0L Turbocharged Inline-6 (M256) with EQ Boost

The S500 features a 3.0-liter turbocharged inline-6 mild-hybrid engine producing approximately 429 horsepower and 384 lb-ft of torque. It provides smooth, effortless power delivery paired with high-tech efficiency, though complex hybrid and valve mechanisms can occasionally lead to expensive out-of-warranty repairs.

**Camtronic Actuator and Powertrain Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may experience a loss of acceleration accompanied by an illuminated check engine light, indicating a failure within the valve lift system.
- **Solution:** Requires diagnosis and replacement of the failed actuator or related engine components by a qualified technician.
- **Cost:** $9000

**Engine Stalling at Low Speeds**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may unexpectedly stall while driving at low speeds, such as 10 MPH, until switched into a different driving mode like sport mode.
- **Solution:** Have the powertrain control module and engine management systems inspected for software updates or faulty sensors.

## Transmission

### 9-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 9G-Tronic 9-speed automatic transmission routes power smoothly to the rear wheels or 4MATIC all-wheel-drive system. It generally operates seamlessly with minimal mechanical complaints reported by owners.

## Suspension

### Wheels and Tires (AMG 21-inch)

The standard and optional adaptive air suspension systems provide an isolating, cloud-like ride quality. However, vehicles equipped with large 21-inch low-profile wheel packages face heightened vulnerability to pothole damage and rim cracking.

**Cracked Wheels and Excessive Tire Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Large 21-inch alloy wheels are prone to cracking along the rims, resulting in sudden tire deflation, persistent air loss, and sidewall bubbling.
- **Solution:** Inspect wheels for structural cracks during a pre-purchase evaluation and consider replacing fragile low-profile tire setups if driving on rough roads.

## Exterior

### Exterior Lighting and Cameras

A complex network of sensors, digital lighting, and driver assistance cameras powers the cabin's high-tech environment. Electrical glitches and sensor obstruction warnings represent some of the more common owner frustrations.

**Adaptive Headlight and Camera System Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may experience erratic high-beam operation from the digital light system or recurring instrument panel warnings stating 'Camera System Unavailable' or 'Camera Dirty.'
- **Solution:** Check camera lenses for obstructions and have dealership technicians inspect camera calibration and software modules.

## Safety

### Forward Collision Avoidance

Advanced safety suites offer cutting-edge collision mitigation and driver aids. However, owner reports indicate that sensor misfires or obscured forward lenses can cause erratic behavior in active safety systems.

**Inadvertent Emergency Braking**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may falsely detect obstacles while cruising at highway or moderate speeds, unexpectedly triggering harsh anti-lock braking events.
- **Solution:** Ensure driver assistance sensors and windshield camera housings are clean; consult a dealer for potential software recalibrations.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V789000: Air Bags:Sensor:Occupant Classification:Front Passenger](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=S500&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** A deactivated passenger air bag increases the risk of injury during a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the air bag control unit software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V167000: Exterior Lighting:Back Up Lights](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=S500&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** Disabled rear turn signals, hazard lights, and reverse lights can reduce the vehicle's visibility to other driver's, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the rear signal software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V214000: Steering:Column](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=S500&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The electrical connector to the steering column module may not be secured properly, which can disable gear shifts and/or safety features including the electronic stability program, the driver's air bag, and turn signal indicators. Disabled safety features or the inability to shift gears can increase the risk of a crash or injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and reinstall the cover for the electrical connector, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V493000: Back Over Prevention:Display Function](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=S500&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The rearview camera may not display the rearview image due to a software error in the MBUX multimedia system. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 111, "Rear Visibility." An inoperative rearview camera display reduces the driver's rear view, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the MBUX multimedia system software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2021 Mercedes-Benz S500 remains an pinnacle of modern automotive luxury, but used-car buyers should approach with diligence. Pay close attention to potential high-cost powertrain repairs, active safety sensor warnings, and the vulnerability of large 21-inch wheels to road damage. A comprehensive pre-purchase inspection by a knowledgeable specialist is strongly recommended.
