# 2021 Mercedes-Benz S500

### Overview
The 2021 Mercedes-Benz S500 represents the pinnacle of luxury and technology, and for the most part, it delivers on its promise of robust engineering. As a flagship model, it incorporates advanced systems and materials, which generally contribute to a high-quality ownership experience. However, like any complex vehicle, particularly one with cutting-edge features, there are specific areas where owners have reported minor concerns. This analysis focuses on documented technical reliability, excluding routine maintenance and standard wear and tear, to provide a clear picture of potential issues beyond expected operational factors. The focus is on the primary powertrain and common areas of concern in high-end sedans.

## Suspension

### AIRMATIC Air Suspension
**Air Spring Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle may sag on one corner, uneven ride height, warning lights on the dashboard.
- **Solution:** Replacement of faulty air spring or associated seals.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1500 per corner

**Compressor Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Inability to raise or lower the vehicle, constant running of the compressor, warning lights.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the air suspension compressor.
- **Cost:** $700 - $1200


## Electrical

### Infotainment System (MBUX)
**Software Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Screen freezing, unresponsive touch controls, occasional system reboots, connectivity issues.
- **Solution:** Software updates, system resets, or in rare cases, replacement of control modules.
- **Cost:** $100 - $500 (for software updates/diagnostics)


### 12V Battery Drain
**Intermittent Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Difficulty starting the vehicle, battery warning lights, electrical components malfunctioning.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis to identify parasitic draw from modules or accessories, potential battery replacement.
- **Cost:** $300 - $700

### Summary Verdict
The 2021 Mercedes-Benz S500 (W223 generation) generally exhibits strong reliability, particularly in its core powertrain components. The 3.0L turbocharged inline-six engine and the 9-speed automatic transmission are robust and have not shown significant widespread issues. However, as with many vehicles equipped with advanced air suspension systems, owners may encounter issues related to air spring leaks or compressor malfunctions, which can be costly to repair. The sophisticated electrical systems, especially the MBUX infotainment, can sometimes suffer from software glitches requiring updates or resets. Minor concerns also include potential intermittent 12V battery drain and issues with interior seat actuators. Overall, the S500 is a reliable luxury sedan, but potential buyers should be aware of the costs associated with maintaining its complex suspension and electronic features.
