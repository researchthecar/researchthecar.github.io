# 2024 Mercedes-Benz S500 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2024 Mercedes-Benz S500, buyers are looking at a flagship luxury sedan defined by advanced technology, supreme comfort, and sophisticated engineering. While early-ownership owner reports for this model year are limited, some drivers have noted initial concerns touching upon powertrain behavior, digital display stability, and suspension or wheel components. Prospective buyers should review vehicle history and ensure thorough pre-purchase inspections.

---

## Engine

### 3.0L Turbocharged Inline-6 (M256) with EQ Boost

This 3.0-liter turbocharged inline-six engine features a 48-volt mild-hybrid system (EQ Boost), producing approximately 429 horsepower and 384 lb-ft of torque. It delivers exceptionally smooth power delivery and strong efficiency for a large luxury sedan, though its complex electronics require attentive maintenance.

## Transmission

### 9G-Tronic 9-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 9G-Tronic 9-speed automatic transmission routes power smoothly through standard 4MATIC all-wheel drive. While generally refined, early owner feedback highlights occasional shift harshness and driveline anomalies that warrant careful evaluation.

**Harsh Shifting or Power Delivery Interruption**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have reported abnormal or harsh shifting behavior between lower gears, as well as occasional unexpected loss of power response at highway speeds.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified technician or dealership inspect transmission software calibrations and fluid conditions to diagnose potential mechanical or electronic faults.

## Suspension

### Tires and Wheels

The advanced suspension and large wheel setups provide a composed ride, though low-profile tires are susceptible to road hazard damage.

**Tire Bulges and Rapid Air Loss**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have experienced unexpected bumps forming on low-profile tires, leading to sudden deflation or flat tires.
- **Solution:** Inspect tires and wheels regularly for structural damage, and replace compromised low-profile tires promptly.

## Electrical

### Digital Instrument Cluster and Control Units

The heavy reliance on complex digital architecture means software stability is critical for seamless operation of cabin displays and driver assistance features.

**Instrument Cluster Blanking and System Resets**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle control units may trigger fallback resets, causing the digital instrument cluster display to temporarily cut out or go completely blank.
- **Solution:** Check for manufacturer software updates or have the central control modules inspected by a specialist.
