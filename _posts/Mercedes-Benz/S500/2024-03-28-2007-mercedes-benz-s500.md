**Introduction:**

The 2007 Mercedes-Benz S500 is a luxurious full-size sedan that offers a refined ride, advanced technology, and a powerful engine. The S500 is powered by a 5.5-liter V8 engine delivering 382 horsepower and 391 lb-ft of torque, paired with a 7-speed automatic transmission. It's known for its comfortable cabin, spacious interior, and impressive handling dynamics. However, like any vehicle, the S500 has encountered specific issues over time.

**Engine**

**5.5L V8**

* **Valve Stem Seal Failure:**
Description/Symptoms: Oil consumption, blue smoke from the exhaust during acceleration, low oil pressure warning light
Solution: Replacing valve stem seals
Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+
Recalls: None Found

* **Intake Manifold Air Flap Failure:**
Description/Symptoms: Rough idling, reduced engine power, engine stalling
Solution: Replacing intake manifold or air flaps
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500
Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**7-Speed Automatic**

* **Shift Solenoid Malfunction:**
Description/Symptoms: Transmission slipping, delayed shifts, erratic shifting
Solution: Replacing shift solenoids
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500
Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Air Suspension Compressor Failure:**
Description/Symptoms: Vehicle lowering to the ground, difficulty raising and lowering suspension
Solution: Replacing air suspension compressor or relay
Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,500
Recalls: None Found

* **Rear Shock Absorber Leak:**
Description/Symptoms: Excessive noise, loss of dampening
Solution: Replacing rear shock absorbers
Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $1,000
Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Battery Draining Issues:**
Description/Symptoms: Battery discharging overnight, warning lights on dash
Solution: Diagnosing and repairing electrical system faults, replacing battery
Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $1,000+
Recalls: None Found

* **Command System Failure:**
Description/Symptoms: Navigation, audio, and climate control issues
Solution: Updating or replacing software, replacing Command system
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $2,000+
Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Headlight Condensation:**
Description/Symptoms: Moisture accumulation inside headlight housings
Solution: Replacing headlight housings or gaskets
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500
Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Instrument Cluster Flickering:**
Description/Symptoms: Speedometer, tachometer, or other gauges flickering
Solution: Replacing instrument cluster or updating software
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,200
Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

The 2007 Mercedes-Benz S500 offers a luxurious and sophisticated driving experience, but it's not immune to specific issues. While the severity and cost of these issues vary, they range from minor annoyances like flickering gauges to more significant problems like engine and transmission malfunctions. Potential buyers and owners should be aware of these potential concerns to make informed decisions about maintenance and repairs.
