**Introduction:**

The 2016 Mercedes-Benz GL550 is a full-size luxury SUV powered by a 4.7-liter V8 engine producing 429 horsepower and 516 lb-ft of torque. It comes with a 9-speed automatic transmission. The GL550 is known for its powerful engine, spacious interior, and comfortable ride. However, it has also been plagued by some common issues.

**Engine**

* **No Issues Found**

**Transmission**

* **9-Speed Automatic Transmission**
    * **Hesitation or Jerking During Gear Shifts:**
        * Description/Symptoms: When shifting gears, the transmission may hesitate or jerk, causing a rough or inconsistent ride.
        * Solution: Update the transmission software, replace the transmission control module, or replace the entire transmission.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $5,000+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **No Issues Found**

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The battery may drain quickly, especially when the vehicle is parked for an extended period.
    * Solution: Replace the battery, check for parasitic drains, or update the software.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
* **Malfunctioning Rear Backup Camera:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The rear backup camera may display a distorted image, freeze, or stop working completely.
    * Solution: Replace the rear backup camera module.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+

**Exterior**

* **Headlight Condensation:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Moisture can build up inside the headlight assemblies, causing condensation to form.
    * Solution: Replace the headlight assemblies.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+

**Interior**

* **Sunroof Leaks:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Water can leak into the cabin through the sunroof, causing water damage to the interior.
    * Solution: Replace sunroof seals or adjust sunroof alignment.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
* **Seat Heater Malfunction:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The seat heaters may not work properly or may overheat.
    * Solution: Replace the seat heater elements or control module.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+

**Summary:**

While the 2016 Mercedes-Benz GL550 has some great features, it has also experienced a number of common issues. The transmission problems are the most severe and expensive to fix. The electrical issues can also be costly to repair. Overall, the GL550 has a mixed reliability record.
