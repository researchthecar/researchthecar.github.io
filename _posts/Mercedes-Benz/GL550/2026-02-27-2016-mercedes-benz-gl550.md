# 2016 Mercedes-Benz GL550 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used luxury full-size SUV, understanding common owner feedback helps set realistic expectations for upkeep and potential repairs. While these vehicles offer robust performance and comfort, prospective buyers should review historical complaints regarding electrical accessories, body glass, and interior features to ensure a thorough pre-purchase inspection.

---

## Engine

### 3.0L Turbo Diesel V6 (GL350 BlueTEC)

A 3.0-liter turbocharged diesel V6 engine producing around 240 horsepower and 455 lb-ft of torque, offering strong low-end torque and high fuel efficiency for highway cruising, though complex emissions equipment requires attentive maintenance.

### 3.0L Twin-Turbo V6 (GL450)

A 3.0-liter twin-turbocharged V6 engine making approximately 362 horsepower and 384 lb-ft of torque, balancing lively acceleration with modern direct-injection efficiency.

### 4.7L Twin-Turbo V8 (GL550)

A robust 4.7-liter twin-turbocharged V8 engine delivering roughly 429 horsepower and 516 lb-ft of torque, providing effortless acceleration and towing capability alongside demanding thermal management requirements.

**Cooling System Hose Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Coolant leaks or sudden loss of engine coolant resulting from a blown reservoir hose.
- **Solution:** Inspect coolant hoses regularly and replace any worn or bulging lines promptly.

### 5.5L Twin-Turbo V8 (GL63 AMG)

A hand-built 5.5-liter twin-turbocharged V8 engine pumping out 550 horsepower and 560 lb-ft of torque, offering incredible high-performance capability paired with specialized maintenance needs.

## Transmission

### 7-Speed Automatic Transmission

A 7-speed automatic transmission paired across various trims and drivetrains, offering smooth shifts when properly maintained with timely fluid changes.

**Unexpected Gear Engagement / Rollaway**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may unexpectedly disengage from park, potentially allowing it to roll if the parking brake is not engaged.
- **Solution:** Have the shifter linkage, selector module, and software checked by a qualified technician.

## Electrical

### Electrical System

Electrical components generally perform well, though owners should watch out for rare moisture intrusion issues that can affect rear-mounted electronics.

**Water Intrusion in Rear Compartment**
- **Symptoms:** Water accumulation in the spare tire and battery wells, which can compromise the starter and battery systems.
- **Solution:** Inspect and clear sunroof drains and rear hatch seals to prevent moisture leaks.

## Exterior

### Sunroof and Glass

Exterior styling and lighting are sophisticated, but owners have reported isolated incidents of stress fractures in exterior glass components.

**Spontaneous Sunroof Shattering**
- **Symptoms:** The panoramic glass roof may shatter unexpectedly while driving or parked.
- **Solution:** Inspect the glass panel for pre-existing stress cracks and consider comprehensive glass insurance coverage.

## Interior

### Seat Headrests

Cabin appointments are upscale and durable, though certain rear seat mechanisms and headrest designs draw ergonomic complaints from shorter passengers.

**Active Headrest Positioning**
- **Symptoms:** Second-row headrests may feel too rigid or push occupants' heads forward uncomfortably depending on occupant height.
- **Solution:** Adjust seat configurations and verify proper mechanical operation during a test drive.

## Summary Verdict

Overall, this luxury SUV provides exceptional power, space, and refinement, but prospective buyers must budget for higher-than-average maintenance costs. A comprehensive pre-purchase inspection focusing on the cooling system, electrical compartments, and glass panels is strongly recommended to catch minor issues before they become expensive problems.
