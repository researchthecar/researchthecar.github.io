# 2016 Mercedes-Benz C250 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2016 Mercedes-Benz C-Class (commonly represented by popular variants like the C300) delivers luxury, impressive styling, and refined driving dynamics, but used-car buyers should approach with caution regarding potential mechanical and electrical hiccups. While it offers a comfortable ride and upscale interior, owner reports indicate that certain areas—such as fuel system components, sunroof assemblies, and steering racks—require close inspection before purchasing.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4

A 2.0-liter turbocharged inline-4 engine generating around 241 horsepower and 273 lb-ft of torque, providing a responsive and punchy drive. While generally energetic, buyers should monitor for fuel line leaks and crankcase ventilation code alerts.

**Fuel Odor and Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** A strong smell of gasoline around the vehicle or visible fuel weeping near engine lines and fuel rails.
- **Solution:** Inspect fuel lines, high-pressure fuel pumps, and injectors; replace degraded seals or leaking lines immediately.

**Crankcase Ventilation / P052E Fault**
- **Symptoms:** Check engine light illuminates with diagnostic trouble codes like P052e related to the positive crankcase ventilation valve.
- **Solution:** Replace the faulty crankcase ventilation valve assembly or associated PCV components.

## Transmission

### Transmission: 7-Speed Automatic

A smooth-shifting 7-speed automatic transmission designed to balance performance and efficiency. Routine fluid maintenance is critical to ensuring long-term shifting refinement.

## Brakes

### Brake System

Delivers confident stopping power, though owners have occasionally flagged uneven pad wear, brake pull, or vacuum booster noise.

**Brake Pull and Hissing Noises**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle pulls to one side when applying the brakes, accompanied by a hissing sound near the brake pedal indicating possible booster wear.
- **Solution:** Inspect brake calipers, slide pins, pads, and the brake booster assembly; service or replace worn components.

## Steering

### Electric Power Steering

Provides light, speed-sensitive maneuvering, though complete electronic rack failures can be an expensive repair out of warranty.

**Steering Rack Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Sudden loss of power steering assistance, heavy steering effort, or complete electronic steering rack failure while driving.
- **Solution:** Inspect the electronic steering assembly and replace the steering rack and pinion unit if internal mechanical or electrical failure occurs.
- **Cost:** $5,840.14

## Exterior

### Panoramic Sunroof

Offers an open-air cabin feel, but mechanical track binding and rare spontaneous glass fracturing require careful pre-purchase verification.

**Sunroof Binding, Jamming, or Glass Shattering**
- **Symptoms:** Panoramic sunroof binds on tracks, fails to close evenly, makes grinding noises, or spontaneously shatters while driving.
- **Solution:** Clean and lubricate tracks regularly; replace damaged guide rails, motors, or shatter-prone glass panels if structural binding occurs.
