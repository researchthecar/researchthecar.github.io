The 2017 Mercedes-Benz CLA200 is a stylish compact sedan that offers a blend of luxury and performance. The CLA200, in particular, features a 1.6-liter turbocharged inline-4 engine, producing around 156 horsepower and 184 lb-ft of torque. Other engine options available on the 2017 CLA range include the CLA250 with a more powerful 2.0-liter turbo engine and the AMG CLA45 with an even more potent 2.0-liter engine. All engine options are typically paired with a 7-speed dual-clutch automatic transmission. Owners often praise the CLA200 for its sleek design and comfortable ride, but some common complaints include cramped rear seating and occasional electronic glitches.

**Engine**

**1.6L Turbocharged Inline-4 (CLA200)**
*   **Oil Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks can occur from various locations, including the valve cover gasket, oil filter housing, or rear main seal. Symptoms include visible oil drips under the car, a burning oil smell, and low oil levels.
    *   Solution: Replacing the leaking gasket or seal.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $1,000+ depending on the location of the leak and complexity of the repair.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Turbocharger Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The turbocharger might experience failures due to oil starvation, wear, or other factors. Symptoms include reduced engine power, unusual noises from the turbocharger, and potential engine misfires.
    *   Solution: Replacing the turbocharger.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**7-Speed Dual-Clutch Automatic**
*   **Hesitation and Jerky Shifting**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The transmission may exhibit hesitation during acceleration or experience jerky shifts, particularly at low speeds. This can be due to issues with the valve body or clutch packs.
    *   Solution: Possible solutions include a transmission control module (TCM) reflash, valve body replacement, or clutch replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $4,000+ depending on the repair needed.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Transmission Mount Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Worn or failed transmission mount. This may cause excessive vibration, noises and harsh or delayed shifting.
    *   Solution: Replace the transmission mount.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Noises**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Owners report clunking or squeaking noises when driving over bumps or uneven surfaces. This can be due to worn sway bar links, bushings, or ball joints.
    *   Solution: Replacing the worn suspension components.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+ per component depending on the repair needed.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Battery Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The battery might drain prematurely or fail to hold a charge. This can be due to a parasitic draw or a faulty battery.
    *   Solution: Replacing the battery or diagnosing and repairing the parasitic draw.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+ depending on the cause.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Infotainment System Glitches**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The infotainment system may freeze, reboot unexpectedly, or experience other software-related issues.
    *   Solution: Performing a software update or replacing the infotainment system.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $1,500+ depending on the severity.
    *   Recalls: None Found

* **Door Lock Actuators Failing**
    * Description/Symptoms: The door lock actuators might fail causing the doors to become difficult to lock or unlock.
    * Solution: Replacing the door lock actuator.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **No Issues Found**

**Interior**

*   **Sunroof Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The sunroof may become stuck or fail to open or close properly. This can be due to a faulty motor or damaged tracks.
    *   Solution: Repairing or replacing the sunroof motor or tracks.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,000+ depending on the issue.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2017 Mercedes-Benz CLA200, while a stylish and enjoyable vehicle, can experience several issues. Engine oil leaks and turbocharger problems are among the more significant concerns, potentially leading to costly repairs. Transmission issues, particularly jerky shifting, are also commonly reported. Electrical glitches and suspension noises are other areas to be mindful of. Overall, while the CLA200 offers a luxurious driving experience, potential buyers should be aware of these potential problems and factor in the possibility of moderate to high repair costs.
