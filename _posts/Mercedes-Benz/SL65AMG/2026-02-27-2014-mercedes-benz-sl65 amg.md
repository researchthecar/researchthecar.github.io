# 2014 Mercedes-Benz SL65 AMG

### Overview
The 2014 Mercedes-Benz SL65 AMG, a high-performance luxury roadster, is generally known for its robust engineering and powerful V12 engine. As a flagship model, it benefits from extensive development and premium materials. While specific common failures are rare due to its exclusive nature and targeted owner base, like any complex vehicle, certain areas can experience issues. This analysis focuses on documented, non-wear-and-tear related problems that may arise, providing insights into potential repair costs and solutions for owners.

---


## Engine

### 6.0L Twin-Turbo V12 (M275)
**Turbocharger Wastegate Actuator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Reduced engine power, check engine light, audible whistling or hissing sounds from the engine bay.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the faulty wastegate actuator. In some cases, turbocharger assembly replacement may be necessary.
- **Cost:** $1,500 - $3,000

**Ignition Coil Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Misfires, rough idling, reduced engine performance, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the faulty ignition coil(s). It is often recommended to replace all coils if one fails.
- **Cost:** $300 - $700 per coil


## Transmission

### 5-Speed Automatic (722.6)
**Torque Converter Seal Leak**
- **Symptoms:** Transmission fluid leaks, slipping gears, delayed engagement, potential overheating.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the torque converter seal. This often requires transmission removal.
- **Cost:** $1,000 - $2,500

**Transmission Control Module (TCM) Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Erratic shifting, inability to shift gears, limp mode activation, transmission warning lights.
- **Solution:** Replacement or reprogramming of the Transmission Control Module.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1,800


## Suspension

### Active Body Control (ABC) System
**Hydraulic Fluid Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle sagging on one or more corners, suspension warning lights, noticeable fluid under the vehicle.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of leaking hydraulic lines, struts, or pumps. Fluid flush and refill.
- **Cost:** $1,000 - $4,000+

**ABC Pump Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of hydraulic pressure, vehicle suspension dropping, loud whining noise from the pump.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the ABC hydraulic pump.
- **Cost:** $1,200 - $2,500


## Electrical

### Infotainment System (COMAND)
**Screen Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** Flickering display, unresponsive touch screen, freezing, or complete black screen.
- **Solution:** Software update, or replacement of the COMAND head unit or display.
- **Cost:** $500 - $2,000


### Convertible Top Hydraulics/Actuators
**Actuator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Convertible top not opening or closing properly, slow operation, unusual noises.
- **Solution:** Replacement of faulty hydraulic actuators or pumps for the convertible top mechanism.
- **Cost:** $800 - $2,000

### Summary Verdict
The 2014 Mercedes-Benz SL65 AMG is a highly capable and generally reliable performance vehicle, particularly in its powertrain. The V12 engine and its associated transmission are robust, though specific components like wastegate actuators and ignition coils can require attention. The Active Body Control (ABC) suspension system, while advanced, is a known area where hydraulic leaks and pump failures can occur, representing a significant potential repair cost. Electrical issues, primarily with the COMAND infotainment system and convertible top hydraulics, are also noted. Overall, for a vehicle of its complexity and performance level, the SL65 AMG offers good reliability, but owners should be prepared for the specialized maintenance and potential costs associated with its high-performance systems.
