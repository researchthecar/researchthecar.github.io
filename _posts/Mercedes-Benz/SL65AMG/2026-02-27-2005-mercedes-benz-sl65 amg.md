# 2005 Mercedes-Benz SL65 AMG

### Overview
The 2005 Mercedes-Benz SL65 AMG, a high-performance grand tourer, is renowned for its potent V12 twin-turbocharged engine and luxurious appointments. While offering exhilarating performance, like any complex, high-performance vehicle of its era, it presents certain reliability considerations. Owners should be aware of potential issues primarily related to its advanced powertrain and sophisticated electronic systems. Regular and meticulous maintenance is crucial for preserving the performance and longevity of this exotic machine. The SL65 AMG, with its hand-built engine and advanced technology, demands specialized attention and can incur significant repair costs when issues arise, making proactive maintenance a wise investment.

---


## Engine

### 6.0L V12 Twin-Turbo (M275)
**Turbocharger Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of power, excessive smoke, whistling noises from the engine bay.
- **Solution:** Replacement of turbochargers. Regular oil changes with high-quality synthetic oil are critical.
- **Cost:** $3,000 - $7,000 per turbocharger

**Intercooler Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Reduced engine performance, overheating, coolant smell.
- **Solution:** Replacement of intercooler hoses or the intercooler unit itself.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1,500

**Ignition Coil Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Misfires, rough idling, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of faulty ignition coils.
- **Cost:** $150 - $300 per coil

**Oil Cooler Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Oil leaks around the engine block, potential for oil in coolant.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the oil cooler and seals.
- **Cost:** $800 - $2,000


## Transmission

### 5-Speed Automatic (AMG Speedshift)
**Torque Converter Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Slipping gears, shuddering during acceleration, delayed engagement.
- **Solution:** Rebuilding or replacement of the torque converter.
- **Cost:** $1,500 - $3,500

**Transmission Control Module (TCM) Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Erratic shifting, transmission warning lights, inability to select gears.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the TCM.
- **Cost:** $800 - $2,000


## Suspension

### Active Body Control (ABC)
**Hydraulic Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle sagging, suspension warning lights, fluid leaks under the car.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of leaking hoses, struts, or pumps. System flush and fluid replacement.
- **Cost:** $1,000 - $4,000+

**ABC Pump Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of hydraulic pressure, vehicle sagging, loud whining noise from the pump.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the ABC hydraulic pump.
- **Cost:** $1,200 - $2,500


## Electrical

### Infotainment System
**COMAND System Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Screen freezing, navigation issues, audio problems, unresponsive buttons.
- **Solution:** Software updates, or replacement of the COMAND head unit.
- **Cost:** $500 - $2,500


### Sensors (e.g., ABS, Parking)
**Sensor Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** Illuminated warning lights (ABS, ESP), incorrect readings.
- **Solution:** Replacement of faulty sensors.
- **Cost:** $200 - $600 per sensor


### Battery Drain
**Parasitic Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Dead battery after short periods of inactivity, electrical components malfunctioning.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis to find the source of the drain (e.g., faulty module, light), repair or replacement.
- **Cost:** $300 - $1,000+


## Exterior

### Retractable Hardtop Mechanism
**Hydraulic Pump/Cylinder Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Slow or failed operation of the roof, fluid leaks near the roof or trunk.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of hydraulic lines, pumps, or cylinders.
- **Cost:** $1,000 - $3,000

**Sensor Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Roof not closing/opening completely, warning lights related to the roof.
- **Solution:** Replacement of faulty roof sensors.
- **Cost:** $300 - $800


## Interior

### Climate Control System
**Blower Motor Resistor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Fan only works on highest setting or not at all.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the blower motor resistor.
- **Cost:** $150 - $400


### Seat Adjustment Motors
**Malfunctioning Seat Motors**
- **Symptoms:** Power seats not moving in one or more directions.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the seat adjustment motor or control module.
- **Cost:** $400 - $1,000

### Summary Verdict
The 2005 Mercedes-Benz SL65 AMG is a formidable performance car with a complex V12 twin-turbo engine and advanced systems. Reliability is a significant consideration, with potential issues concentrated in the powertrain (turbochargers, intercoolers), the sophisticated Active Body Control suspension, and the retractable hardtop mechanism. Electrical gremlins, particularly with the COMAND system and parasitic battery drain, are also noted. While not prone to widespread, catastrophic failures if well-maintained, repairs can be expensive due to the specialized nature of the components and the AMG performance tuning. Owners should budget for potential maintenance and repairs, prioritizing specialized technicians for optimal upkeep.
