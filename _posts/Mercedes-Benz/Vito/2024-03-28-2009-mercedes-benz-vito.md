**Introduction:**

The 2009 Mercedes-Benz Vito is a premium commercial van that offers a diverse range of engine and transmission options. Its 110 CDI engine, paired with a Dual Mass Flywheel (DMF) system, delivers 109 horsepower and 280 lb-ft of torque. Other available engine options include the 111 CDI (136 hp, 258 lb-ft) and 113 CDI (163 hp, 280 lb-ft). Transmission choices include a 5-speed manual, 6-speed manual, and 5-speed automatic. The Vito is praised for its spacious interior, comfortable ride quality, and advanced safety features. However, certain issues have been reported with some components.

**Engine**

**110 CDI**

* **Turbocharger Failure:**
  * Description/Symptoms: Excessive exhaust smoke, reduced engine power, whistling sounds
  * Solution: Replacement of turbocharger
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,200 - $1,800+
  * Recalls: None Found

* **DPF (Diesel Particulate Filter) Issues:**
  * Description/Symptoms: Increased fuel consumption, reduced engine power, warning lights on dashboard
  * Solution: Filter cleaning or replacement
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,200+
  * Recalls: None Found

**111 CDI**

* **Injector Failure:**
  * Description/Symptoms: Engine misfiring, reduced power, rough idle
  * Solution: Replacement of faulty injectors
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $600 per injector
  * Recalls: None Found

**113 CDI**

* **No Issues Found**

**Transmission**

**5-Speed Manual**

* **Shift Linkage Problems:**
  * Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears, grinding noises
  * Solution: Repair or replacement of shift linkage
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
  * Recalls: None Found

**6-Speed Manual**

* **No Issues Found**

**5-Speed Automatic**

* **Transmission Fluid Leakage:**
  * Description/Symptoms: Fluid leaking from transmission pan or seals
  * Solution: Replacement of seals or transmission pan
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600
  * Recalls: None Found

* **Torque Converter Failure:**
  * Description/Symptoms: Slipping gears, shuddering, overheating
  * Solution: Replacement of torque converter
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500
  * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Rear Axle Wear:**
  * Description/Symptoms: Squeaking or grinding noises from rear axle, uneven tire wear
  * Solution: Replacement of rear axle bearings or components
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $600 - $1,200+
  * Recalls: None Found

* **Front Strut Failure:**
  * Description/Symptoms: Knocking or banging noises over bumps, loss of handling stability
  * Solution: Replacement of struts
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800+
  * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Battery Discharge:**
  * Description/Symptoms: Battery drains prematurely, difficulty starting
  * Solution: Testing electrical system for parasitic draw, replacement of battery or alternator
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+
  * Recalls: None Found

* **Faulty Wiring:**
  * Description/Symptoms: Intermittent electrical issues, warning lights on dashboard
  * Solution: Inspection and repair of wiring harness or components
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+
  * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Rust on Body Panels:**
  * Description/Symptoms: Corrosion on panels, especially near wheel arches and lower trim
  * Solution: Rust treatment, panel replacement
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+
  * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Climate Control Malfunctions:**
  * Description/Symptoms: Air conditioning or heating not working properly
  * Solution: Repair or replacement of climate control components
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,200+
  * Recalls: None Found

* **Dashboard Panel Cracks:**
  * Description/Symptoms: Cracks or warping of dashboard panel
  * Solution: Replacement of dashboard panel
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
  * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

Overall, the severity of the issues with the 2009 Mercedes-Benz Vito vary from minor to major, with most being repairable within a reasonable cost range. However, some issues such as turbocharger failure and transmission problems can be more expensive to address. It's important to note that these issues may not affect all Vito models and that regular maintenance and addressing issues promptly can help extend the vehicle's lifespan.
