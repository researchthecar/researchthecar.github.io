## Suspension

### Air Suspension System
**Air Spring Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle sagging on one side, loss of ride height, audible hissing.
- **Solution:** Replacement of faulty air spring or associated lines.
- **Cost:** $300 - $800 per corner

**Compressor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Inability to raise or lower the vehicle, constant running of compressor, warning lights.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the air compressor unit.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1200


## Electrical

### Body Control Module (BCM)
**Intermittent Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** Erratic behavior of lights, wipers, door locks, or dashboard indicators.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and reprogramming or replacement of the BCM.
- **Cost:** $600 - $1500


### Alternator
**Overheating/Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Battery warning light illumination, dimming lights, starting issues.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the alternator.
- **Cost:** $400 - $900

### Summary Verdict
The 2011 Mercedes-Benz OC 500 demonstrates a high level of reliability, particularly in its powertrain. The diesel engines and automatic transmissions are generally robust and designed for long service life. Potential areas for attention are primarily related to the air suspension system, where leaks or compressor issues can arise over time, and certain electrical components like the Body Control Module, which may experience intermittent faults. The HVAC blower motor is another component that can occasionally fail. Overall, the OC 500 is a dependable platform, and with diligent maintenance, especially concerning the suspension and electrical systems, it offers excellent long-term operational value.
