**Introduction:**

The 2005 Mercedes-Benz FS65 is a high-performance sedan powered by a naturally aspirated 6.0-liter V12 engine. It produces 493 horsepower and 465 lb-ft of torque, paired with a 5-speed automatic transmission. The FS65 is known for its aggressive styling, luxurious interior, and impressive power, but it is also susceptible to a variety of issues.

**Engine**

**6.0L V12**
 * **Valve cover gaskets leak:** Oil leaks from the valve covers, causing a burning smell and smoke from the engine bay. Replacement of the valve cover gaskets is required.
 * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1000 - $1500+
 * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**5-Speed Automatic**
 * **Early shifting:** The transmission shifts into higher gears prematurely, resulting in reduced acceleration and fuel economy. Reprogramming the transmission control module (TCM) may resolve the issue.
 * Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 - $500
 * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

**Airmatic system:**
 * **Air leaks:** Air leaks in the airmatic suspension system can cause the vehicle to sag or ride rough. Replacement of the faulty air springs or lines is necessary.
 * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2000+
 * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

 * **Electrical system failures:** Various electrical components, such as the radio, climate control, or power windows, may malfunction intermittently or fail completely. Diagnosis and repair of the affected components is required.
 * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1000+
 * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

 * **Tail light condensation:** Moisture can accumulate inside the tail lights, causing condensation or fogging. Replacement of the tail lights is necessary.
 * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1000+
 * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

 * **Navigation system glitches:** The navigation system may experience software glitches or freeze, requiring a software update or hardware replacement.
 * Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 - $1000+
 * Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

The 2005 Mercedes-Benz FS65 faces a range of issues, from engine leaks to suspension problems and electrical failures. While some fixes are relatively minor and inexpensive, others can be more severe and costly to repair. Overall, owners should be prepared for potential maintenance and repair expenses to maintain their FS65 in optimal condition.
