# 2006 Mercedes-Benz ML350

### Overview
The 2006 Mercedes-Benz ML350, part of the W164 generation, aimed to blend luxury, performance, and utility. It offered a range of powertrain options designed for comfort and capability. While generally well-regarded for its upscale interior and driving dynamics, like any vehicle of its age, it has specific areas where owners have reported issues. This analysis focuses on common mechanical and electrical problems that go beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential long-term reliability for this model year. Understanding these potential pitfalls can help prospective buyers and current owners make informed decisions regarding maintenance and repairs.

---


## Engine

### 3.5L V6 (M272)
**Intake Manifold Runner Flap Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Check engine light, rough idle, loss of power, P2004/P2005/P2006 error codes.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the intake manifold assembly or repair kit.
- **Cost:** $600 - $1200

**Balance Shaft Failure (Timing Chain Wear)**
- **Symptoms:** Rattling noise from the front of the engine, especially on startup, check engine light, potential timing issues.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the balance shaft, timing chain, tensioners, and guides.
- **Cost:** $2500 - $4500


## Transmission

### 7G-TRONIC (722.9)
**Valve Body Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Harsh shifting, delayed engagement, slipping gears, transmission warning light.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of the transmission valve body.
- **Cost:** $1500 - $3000

**Torque Converter Problems**
- **Symptoms:** Shuddering at low speeds, slipping, unusual noises.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the torque converter.
- **Cost:** $1000 - $2000


## Suspension

### Standard Air Suspension (Optional)
**Air Spring Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle sagging on one corner, compressor running excessively, 'Visit Workshop' message.
- **Solution:** Replacement of leaking air springs or air compressor.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1500 per corner (spring), $800 - $1500 (compressor)

## Electrical

### General Electrical System
**SAM Module Failures**
- **Symptoms:** Intermittent electrical faults, malfunctioning lights, wipers, or power windows.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of the Signal Acquisition Module (SAM).
- **Cost:** $500 - $1000

**EIS (Electronic Ignition Switch) Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Key not recognized, difficulty starting the vehicle.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of the Electronic Ignition Switch.
- **Cost:** $400 - $800

## Interior

### Cabin Electronics and Features
**Sunroof Drain Clogs**
- **Symptoms:** Water leaks into the cabin, especially after rain or washing.
- **Solution:** Clearing of sunroof drain tubes.
- **Cost:** $150 - $300

**Seat Control Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** Power seats not moving or adjusting correctly.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of seat control modules or motors.
- **Cost:** $300 - $700

### Summary Verdict
The 2006 Mercedes-Benz ML350 (W164) presents a mixed reliability profile. The 3.5L V6 engine is susceptible to costly issues like intake manifold runner flap failure and balance shaft wear, which can significantly impact performance and longevity. The 7G-TRONIC transmission can also develop problems with its valve body and torque converter, leading to expensive repairs. While the standard suspension is generally robust, the optional air suspension can suffer from leaks. Electrical gremlins, particularly with the SAM module and EIS, are not uncommon. Interior issues like clogged sunroof drains are more of a nuisance but can lead to water damage if ignored. Overall, while offering luxury and capability, potential buyers should be aware of these common failure points and budget for potential repairs, especially concerning the engine and transmission.
