# 2010 Mercedes-Benz ML350

### Overview
The 2010 Mercedes-Benz ML350, part of the W164 generation, is generally considered a capable and luxurious SUV. It offers a comfortable ride and a premium interior, appealing to those seeking a blend of utility and refinement. While many owners report a positive ownership experience, like any vehicle of this age and complexity, certain areas have shown a propensity for specific issues. This analysis focuses on documented technical reliability concerns beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential problem areas for prospective buyers or current owners looking to maintain their vehicle effectively. Understanding these common issues can help in proactive maintenance and budgeting for repairs.

---


## Engine

### 3.5L V6 (M272)
**Timing Chain Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Check engine light, rough idle, rattling noise from the front of the engine.
- **Solution:** Replacement of timing chain, tensioners, guides, and sprockets.
- **Cost:** $1,500 - $3,000

**Intake Manifold Flap Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Check engine light (P2004, P2005, P2006 codes), reduced engine power, rough idle.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the intake manifold assembly.
- **Cost:** $600 - $1,200


## Transmission

### 7G-TRONIC (722.9) Automatic Transmission
**Valve Body Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Harsh shifting, delayed engagement, slipping gears, transmission warning light.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of the valve body, often requiring transmission fluid flush.
- **Cost:** $1,000 - $2,500

**Torque Converter Problems**
- **Symptoms:** Shuddering at low speeds, slipping, rough engagement.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the torque converter.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1,800


## Suspension

### Standard Air Suspension (Optional)
**Air Spring Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle sagging on one or more corners, compressor running excessively, 'Service Suspension' warning.
- **Solution:** Replacement of leaking air springs or air compressor.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1,500 per corner (springs), $400 - $800 (compressor)


### Standard Coil Spring Suspension
**No Major Issues**
- **Symptoms:** No widespread issues reported.
- **Solution:** No widespread issues reported.
- **Cost:** No widespread issues reported.


## Electrical

### General Electrical System
**SAM (Signal Acquisition Module) Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Various electrical gremlins, including malfunctioning lights, wipers, power windows, and dashboard warnings.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of the SAM module.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1,000

**Keyless Go System Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** Difficulty starting the vehicle, door handle sensors not working.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and repair/replacement of related sensors or modules.
- **Cost:** $300 - $800

**No Major Issues**

## Interior

### Interior Components
**Headliner Sagging**
- **Symptoms:** Fabric detaching from the headliner board, especially in hot climates.
- **Solution:** Re-gluing or replacement of the headliner.
- **Cost:** $300 - $700

**Seat Heater Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** One or both seat heaters not working.
- **Solution:** Replacement of heating elements or related wiring.
- **Cost:** $200 - $500

### Summary Verdict
The 2010 Mercedes-Benz ML350 (W164) presents a generally solid ownership proposition, but potential buyers should be aware of specific common issues. The 3.5L V6 engine can experience timing chain and intake manifold problems, while the 7G-TRONIC transmission may develop valve body or torque converter issues. Optional air suspension systems are prone to leaks. Electrical gremlins, particularly related to the SAM module, can also arise. Interior issues like sagging headliners are not uncommon. While many ML350s are reliable, proactive maintenance and budgeting for these potential repairs are advisable for long-term satisfaction.
