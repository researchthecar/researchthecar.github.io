**Introduction:**

The 2005 Mercedes-Benz ML350 is a mid-size luxury SUV that offers a blend of performance, comfort, and off-road capability. It comes equipped with a 3.5-liter V6 engine rated at 273 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque, paired with a 5-speed automatic transmission. The ML350 is highly regarded for its luxurious cabin, powerful engine, and impressive safety features. However, like any vehicle, it has its share of reported issues.

**Engine**

* **3.5-liter V6 (M272)**
    * **Intake manifold swirl flaps failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: The plastic swirl flaps in the intake manifold can break and become lodged in the engine, potentially causing severe damage.
        * Solution: Replacement of the intake manifold and swirl flap assembly.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
        * Recalls: None Found
    * **Balance shaft gear failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: The balance shaft gears can develop excessive wear, leading to a rattling or knocking noise from the engine.
        * Solution: Replacement of the balance shaft module.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $3,000 - $5,000+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

* **5-speed automatic (722.6)**
    * **Leaking torque converter**
        * Description/Symptoms: The torque converter may develop a leak, causing the transmission fluid to escape and the transmission to slip or shudder.
        * Solution: Replacement of the torque converter.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,200+
        * Recalls: None Found
    * **Valve body failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: The transmission valve body may malfunction, causing the transmission to shift erratically or fail to engage gears.
        * Solution: Replacement or repair of the valve body.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **No Issues Found**

**Electrical**

* **Battery drain**
    * Description/Symptoms: The battery may drain prematurely due to a parasitic draw on the electrical system.
    * Solution: Diagnosis and repair of the faulty component causing the draw.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600+
        * Recalls: None Found
* **Alternator failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The alternator may fail to charge the battery, leading to electrical system problems and potential stalling.
    * Solution: Replacement of the alternator.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,200+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Door lock actuator failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The door lock actuators may fail, causing the doors to become inoperable or difficult to open.
    * Solution: Replacement of the faulty door lock actuator(s).
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+ per door
        * Recalls: None Found
* **Tailgate latch failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The tailgate latch may fail to secure the tailgate, allowing it to open unexpectedly.
    * Solution: Replacement of the tailgate latch.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Sunroof motor failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The sunroof motor may fail, preventing the sunroof from opening or closing properly.
    * Solution: Replacement of the sunroof motor.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $600 - $1,000+
        * Recalls: None Found
* **Instrument cluster failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The instrument cluster may experience intermittent failures, causing gauges or warning lights to malfunction.
    * Solution: Replacement of the instrument cluster.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

The 2005 Mercedes-Benz ML350 is a generally reliable vehicle, but like any car, it has its share of potential issues. The most significant concerns are related to the engine, particularly the intake manifold swirl flaps and balance shaft gear failures. Other common issues include transmission leaks, suspension problems, and various electrical and interior component failures. The severity of these issues can vary from minor annoyances to major mechanical failures, and the cost of repairs can range from a few hundred dollars to several thousand. It's important to note that not all ML350s will experience these problems, and the frequency of occurrences may vary depending on factors such as maintenance, driving habits, and environmental conditions.
