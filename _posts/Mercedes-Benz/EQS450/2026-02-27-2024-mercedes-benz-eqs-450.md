# 2024 Mercedes-Benz EQS 450+ Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2024 Mercedes-Benz EQS 450+ represents the brand's flagship luxury electric vehicle experience, offering impressive range and advanced technology. As a relatively new luxury EV model, prospective used buyers will want to evaluate its advanced powertrain and onboard systems carefully, keeping in mind that ownership experiences often rely heavily on software stability and dealer support.

---

## Engine

### Permanent Magnet Synchronous Electric Motor (Rear-Wheel Drive)

This rear-mounted permanent magnet synchronous electric motor produces approximately 355 horsepower and 417 lb-ft of torque. It delivers smooth, silent acceleration and strong efficiency, serving as the core propulsion unit for the rear-wheel-drive EQS 450+ variant.

## Transmission

### Single-Speed Fixed-Ratio Direct Drive

A single-speed fixed-ratio direct-drive transmission manages power delivery from the electric motor to the rear wheels. It provides seamless, gearless acceleration suited to the luxury EV driving dynamic.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V372000: Electrical System:Propulsion System:Traction Battery:Management System/Energy Control Module (Bms/Becm):Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=EQS+450%2B&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** The battery management system software may cause the high-voltage battery to shut down, which can result in a sudden loss of drive power. A sudden loss of drive power increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the battery management system software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V366000: Back Over Prevention:Warnings:External/Pedestrian Alert](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=EQS+450%2B&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** Software in the Acoustic Vehicle Alerting System (AVAS) may cause an incorrect external pedestrian warning sound while reversing. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 141, "Minimum Sound Requirements for Hybrid Electric Vehicles."
