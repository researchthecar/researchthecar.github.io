**Introduction:**

The 2012 Mercedes-Benz E350 is a mid-size luxury sedan renowned for its refined performance, opulent interior, and advanced technology. It is powered by a 3.5-liter V6 engine producing 302 horsepower and 273 lb-ft of torque, paired with a seven-speed automatic transmission. Other engine and transmission options include:

* 2.1-liter inline-4 diesel engine (204 horsepower, 369 lb-ft of torque) with a seven-speed automatic transmission
* 3.5-liter V6 engine (349 horsepower, 310 lb-ft of torque) with a seven-speed automatic transmission

While the E350 offers impressive capabilities and a comfortable driving experience, it has faced various issues reported by owners.

**Engine**

* **M276 3.5-liter V6 Engine (302 horsepower)**

**Intake Manifold Stalling**
Description/Symptoms: Engine stalling, reduced power, check engine light
Solution: Replace intake manifold
Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

* **Seven-Speed Automatic Transmission**

**Shifting Issues**
Description/Symptoms: Hard shifts, delayed shifts, slipping gears
Solution: Transmission fluid flush, software update, transmission rebuild
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $3,500+
Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Airmatic Air Suspension**

**Air Suspension Failure**
Description/Symptoms: Vehicle leaning to one side, rough ride, check suspension light
Solution: Replace air suspension struts, compressor, or control module
Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $5,000+
Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain**
Description/Symptoms: Dead battery after short periods of inactivity
Solution: Replace battery, check for parasitic drain
Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500
Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Sunroof Gasket Leak**
Description/Symptoms: Water leaks into the interior through the sunroof gasket
Solution: Replace sunroof gasket
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Navigation System Malfunctions**
Description/Symptoms: Navigation system crashing, freezing, or providing inaccurate directions
Solution: Software update, replace navigation system
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000
Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

While the 2012 Mercedes-Benz E350 offers a luxurious and technologically advanced experience, it has faced several issues that can be expensive to repair. The most severe and costly problems include the intake manifold stalling, transmission shifting issues, and air suspension failure. Other issues, such as electrical and navigation system malfunctions, pose less significant concerns but can still impact the overall ownership experience. It is advisable for potential owners to consider these issues and research reliable maintenance and repair options before purchasing this vehicle.
