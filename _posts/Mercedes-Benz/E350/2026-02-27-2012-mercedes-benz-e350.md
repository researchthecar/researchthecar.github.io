# 2012 Mercedes-Benz E350

### Overview
The 2012 Mercedes-Benz E350, part of the W212 generation, is generally considered a reliable luxury sedan, offering a blend of comfort, performance, and advanced technology for its era. It was available with a potent V6 engine and a smooth automatic transmission, contributing to a refined driving experience. While most owners report satisfaction, like any vehicle, certain components can experience issues over time. This analysis focuses on documented, non-wear-and-tear related problems that may require attention, providing insights into potential repair costs and solutions for common concerns.

---


## Engine

### 3.5L V6 (M272/M276)
**Intake Manifold Runner Flap Actuator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Check Engine Light, rough idle, reduced engine power, P2004/P2005/P2006 codes.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the intake manifold or repair kit for the actuator.
- **Cost:** $400 - $800

**Oil Leaks (Valve Cover Gaskets/Oil Cooler Seals)**
- **Symptoms:** Visible oil on engine components, burning oil smell, low oil level.
- **Solution:** Replacement of valve cover gaskets and/or oil cooler seals.
- **Cost:** $300 - $700


## Transmission

### 7G-TRONIC (722.9) 7-Speed Automatic
**Transmission Control Module (TCM) Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Harsh shifting, slipping gears, inability to shift, limp mode, warning lights.
- **Solution:** Replacement or repair of the TCM.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1500

**Torque Converter Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Shuddering at low speeds, slipping, delayed engagement.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the torque converter.
- **Cost:** $1000 - $2000


## Suspension

### Standard Chassis
**Air Suspension Leaks (if equipped)**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle sagging on one side, uneven ride height, compressor running excessively.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of air springs, lines, or compressor.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1500


## Electrical

### General Electrical System
**Window Regulator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Power windows not operating, slow operation, unusual noises.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the window regulator mechanism.
- **Cost:** $200 - $400

**Headlight Ballast Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Xenon headlights not turning on or flickering.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the headlight ballast.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600

## Interior

### Cabin Electronics and Comfort
**COMAND System Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Infotainment system freezing, unresponsiveness, audio issues.
- **Solution:** Software update or replacement of the COMAND unit.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1500

### Summary Verdict
The 2012 Mercedes-Benz E350 is a solid luxury vehicle with a generally good reliability record. The most common non-wear-and-tear issues tend to be related to the intake manifold runner flaps on the V6 engine, potential oil leaks from gaskets, and occasional electronic glitches within the transmission control module or COMAND infotainment system. Air suspension components can also be a point of concern if equipped. While these issues can be costly to repair, they are not universally experienced by all vehicles. Regular maintenance and addressing minor issues promptly can help mitigate more significant problems.
