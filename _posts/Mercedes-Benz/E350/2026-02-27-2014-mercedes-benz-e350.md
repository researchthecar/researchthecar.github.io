# 2014 Mercedes-Benz E350

### Overview
The 2014 Mercedes-Benz E350, part of the W212 generation, is generally regarded as a solid luxury sedan with a reputation for comfort and performance. It was offered with a potent V6 engine and a smooth automatic transmission, aiming to provide a refined driving experience. While Mercedes-Benz vehicles typically come with a premium price tag, the E350 from this era balances luxury appointments with robust engineering. Owners often praise its sophisticated interior, advanced safety features for its time, and the overall quality of materials. However, like any complex vehicle, certain components can experience issues over time, particularly as mileage increases. This analysis focuses on documented, non-wear-and-tear related reliability concerns.

---


## Engine

### 3.5L V6 (M276)
**Coolant Leaks (Intake Manifold)**
- **Symptoms:** Coolant loss, potential overheating, visible coolant residue around the intake manifold.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the intake manifold.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1000

**Oil Leaks (Valve Cover Gaskets)**
- **Symptoms:** Visible oil leaks around the valve covers, burning oil smell, potential for oil to drip onto exhaust components.
- **Solution:** Replacement of valve cover gaskets.
- **Cost:** $400 - $800


## Transmission

### 7G-TRONIC PLUS (7-Speed Automatic)
**Transmission Control Module (TCM) Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Erratic shifting, 'limp mode' engagement, warning lights on the dashboard, failure to engage gears.
- **Solution:** Replacement or repair of the Transmission Control Module.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1500

**Torque Converter Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Shuddering during acceleration, slipping gears, delayed engagement.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the torque converter.
- **Cost:** $1200 - $2000


## Suspension

### Standard Chassis
**Air Suspension Leaks (if equipped)**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle sagging on one side, uneven ride height, compressor running excessively.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of leaking air springs or lines.
- **Cost:** $600 - $1500 per corner


## Electrical

### General Electrical System
**Sensor Failures (e.g., ABS, Crankshaft Position)**
- **Symptoms:** Illuminated warning lights (ABS, Check Engine), performance issues, starting problems.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and replacement of faulty sensor.
- **Cost:** $200 - $600 per sensor

**Headlight Control Module Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Headlights not working, flickering, or intermittent operation.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the headlight control module.
- **Cost:** $300 - $700

## Interior

### Infotainment and Controls
**COMAND System Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Screen freezing, unresponsive buttons, audio issues, navigation problems.
- **Solution:** Software updates or potential replacement of the COMAND unit.
- **Cost:** $500 - $2000


### Seat Actuators
**Power Seat Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** Power seats failing to move forward, backward, or adjust height.
- **Solution:** Replacement of faulty seat motor or control module.
- **Cost:** $400 - $800

### Summary Verdict
The 2014 Mercedes-Benz E350 offers a generally reliable ownership experience, especially concerning its core powertrain components. The 3.5L V6 engine and 7-speed automatic transmission are robust, though common issues like coolant leaks from the intake manifold and occasional transmission control module failures can arise. Electrical gremlins, particularly sensor issues and infotainment glitches, are also noted. While not as prone to major failures as some competitors, proactive maintenance and addressing minor issues promptly are key to maintaining the E350's luxury and performance. Owners should be aware of potential costs associated with repairing complex electronic systems and specialized components like air suspension if equipped.
