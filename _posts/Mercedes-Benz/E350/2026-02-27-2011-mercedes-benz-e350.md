# 2011 Mercedes-Benz E350

### Overview
The 2011 Mercedes-Benz E350, part of the W212 generation, is generally considered a reliable luxury sedan, offering a blend of comfort, performance, and advanced technology for its era. It was available with a potent V6 engine and a smooth-shifting automatic transmission. While most owners report a positive ownership experience, like any vehicle, some common areas have seen more frequent attention than others. This analysis focuses on documented technical issues beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential concerns for prospective buyers or current owners.

---


## Engine

### 3.5L V6 (M272/M276)
**Intake Manifold Runner Flap Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Check engine light, rough idle, loss of power, rattling noise from the intake.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the intake manifold assembly or repair kit.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1200

**Oil Leaks (Valve Cover Gaskets/Oil Cooler Seals)**
- **Symptoms:** Visible oil on the engine block, burning oil smell, low oil level.
- **Solution:** Replacement of valve cover gaskets and/or oil cooler seals.
- **Cost:** $400 - $900


## Transmission

### 7G-TRONIC (722.9) Automatic
**Transmission Control Module (TCM) Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Harsh shifting, slipping gears, limp mode, transmission warning light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the Transmission Control Module.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1500

**Torque Converter Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Shuddering during acceleration, slipping, delayed engagement.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the torque converter.
- **Cost:** $1000 - $2000


## Suspension

### Standard Chassis
**Air Suspension Leaks (if equipped)**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle sagging on one side, uneven ride height, compressor running excessively.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of air springs, lines, or compressor.
- **Cost:** $600 - $1500+

**Control Arm Bushing Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Clunking noises over bumps, vague steering feel, uneven tire wear.
- **Solution:** Replacement of worn control arm bushings or complete control arms.
- **Cost:** $400 - $800


## Electrical

### General Electrical System
**Window Regulator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Power windows not operating, window stuck in position, grinding noise.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the window regulator mechanism.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600

**Headlight Ballast Failure**
- **Symptoms:** One or both HID headlights not turning on or flickering.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the headlight ballast unit.
- **Cost:** $200 - $500

### Summary Verdict
The 2011 Mercedes-Benz E350 is a solid luxury vehicle with a generally good reliability record. The most common concerns tend to revolve around the engine's intake manifold runner flaps and potential oil leaks, as well as the transmission's control module and torque converter. Suspension components, particularly on models with air suspension, can also require attention. Electrical issues like window regulators and headlight ballasts are not uncommon for a vehicle of this age. Overall, with diligent maintenance and addressing these specific potential issues, the E350 can provide a comfortable and dependable driving experience.
