# 2021 Mercedes-Benz E350 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2021 Mercedes-Benz E350 delivers sophisticated luxury, advanced technology, and refined everyday performance. However, prospective buyers should review driver-reported feedback carefully, as some owners have experienced mechanical and electrical hiccups ranging from powertrain faults to electronic glitches. Staying informed about these specific areas will help you navigate your pre-purchase inspection and secure a well-maintained luxury sedan.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged I4

This 2.0-liter turbocharged inline-four engine produces around 255 horsepower and 273 lb-ft of torque, providing brisk acceleration and competitive fuel efficiency for daily driving. While generally smooth and responsive, buyers should watch for occasional reports of cylinder misfires and valvetrain concerns during pre-purchase inspections.

**Cylinder Misfire and Valve Seat Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Illuminated check engine light, engine misfires, and sudden entry into limp mode resulting in a loss of motive power on the highway.
- **Solution:** Dealer diagnosis and repair of affected valves, which may be related to known technical service bulletins regarding valve seat wear.

## Transmission

### 9-Speed Automatic

The 9-speed automatic transmission delivers seamless gear changes tailored to balance efficiency and performance across rear-wheel and all-wheel-drive configurations. While most units operate reliably, isolated owner reports highlight severe mechanical failures requiring thorough service history checks before purchase.

**Premature Transmission Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Sudden loss of drive capability, severe mechanical failure while driving, and leaving the vehicle completely stranded.
- **Solution:** Requires professional diagnosis, which may involve complete transmission overhaul or replacement depending on the severity of internal component damage.

## Brakes

### Parking Brake

The braking system provides confident stopping power under normal conditions. However, electronic parking brake glitches have occasionally caused unexpected locking after periods of sitting.

**Independent Parking Brake Seizure**
- **Symptoms:** Emergency brake locking up unexpectedly during parking maneuvers, abruptly stopping the vehicle and preventing forward or reverse movement.
- **Solution:** Inspection of the electronic parking brake actuators, software modules, and rear brake components.

## Electrical

### 48V Mild Hybrid System

The electrical architecture incorporates a 48-volt mild-hybrid system to support accessories and powertrain smoothness. Electrical complaints primarily focus on early battery system failures that can render the car temporarily immobile.

**48V Battery Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Sudden electrical failure, instrument panel error messages, and total vehicle inoperability requiring a tow.
- **Solution:** Diagnostic testing of the charging system and replacement of the 48-volt battery or associated electronic modules.

### Backup Camera and Sensors

Advanced driver assistance features support everyday safety and parking convenience. Some drivers report intermittent camera and sensor blackouts that reduce visibility when maneuvering.

**Intermittent Camera Inoperability**
- **Symptoms:** Rearview and 360-degree camera displays failing to activate when shifting into reverse, accompanied by parking sensor warnings.
- **Solution:** Software updates or replacement of faulty camera modules and multimedia system components.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V483000: Seat Belts:Rear/Other:Retractor](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=E350&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 208, "Occupant Crash Protection." An unsecured child restraint system can increase the risk of injury during a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect, and replace as necessary, the rear seat belt assemblies, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V228000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=E350&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** An inoperative rearview camera display and/or parking sensors can reduce the driver's view and awareness of what is behind the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** MBUSA will replace the parking control unit, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V216000: Seats](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=E350&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** A seat too close to the air bag increases the risk of injury in the event of a crash necessitating the deployment of the frontal passenger air bag.
- **What to do:** MBUSA will notify owners, and dealers will recalibrate the passenger seat limiter, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V230000: Seats](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=E350&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** A faulty electrical contact within the seat position switch may prevent the driver and passenger air bags and seat belt force limiters from properly activating during a crash. Air bags and seat belts that do not perform properly in a crash can increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** MBUSA will notify owners, and dealers will replace the seat position switches, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V673000: Seats](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=E350&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** If the front passenger seat is adjusted beyond the acceptable range in relation to the air bag, there could be an increased risk of injury in the event of a crash with air bag deployment.
- **What to do:** MBUSA will notify owners, and dealers will inspect for the mechanical seat adjustment range limitation for the front passenger seat and install it if necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V189000: Steering: Steering Wheel/Handle Bar](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=E350&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** Deactivated hands-off detection will prevent DISTRONIC system features from functioning, including warnings to grab the steering wheel and calling first responders as part of the Active Emergency Stop Assist, which can increase the risk of a crash or injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the hand detection control unit software for the DISTRONIC system, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2021 Mercedes-Benz E350 offers a luxurious cabin, cutting-edge tech, and a refined driving experience, but used-car buyers should proceed with diligence. Key areas to inspect include the 48-volt electrical system, electronic parking brake functionality, and the service history of the engine and transmission. A comprehensive pre-purchase inspection by a knowledgeable specialist is strongly recommended to catch any lingering mechanical or electronic issues before buying.
