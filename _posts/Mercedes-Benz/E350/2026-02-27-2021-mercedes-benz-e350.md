# 2021 Mercedes-Benz E350

### Overview
The 2021 Mercedes-Benz E350, part of the W213 generation, is generally regarded as a reliable luxury sedan. While Mercedes-Benz vehicles are known for their sophisticated engineering, like any complex machine, some areas may experience issues over time. This analysis focuses on documented technical reliability concerns beyond routine maintenance, examining common problem areas reported by owners and technicians. The aim is to provide a clear picture of potential technical vulnerabilities for the 2021 E350, specifically addressing its engine, transmission, suspension, electrical systems, exterior, and interior components.

### AIR BODY CONTROL (Optional Air Suspension)
**Air Strut Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle sagging on one corner, "Visit workshop" warning message, uneven ride height.
- **Solution:** Replacement of leaking air strut or air spring assembly.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1500 per corner


## Electrical

### Infotainment System (MBUX)
**Software Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Screen freezing, unresponsive touch controls, intermittent audio issues, navigation system errors.
- **Solution:** Software updates, system resets, or potential module replacement.
- **Cost:** $150 - $500


### 12V Battery Drain
**Premature Battery Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Difficulty starting, electrical components malfunctioning or not working, "Auxiliary battery malfunction" warning.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the 12V auxiliary battery.
- **Cost:** $200 - $400

### Summary Verdict
The 2021 Mercedes-Benz E350 demonstrates strong overall reliability, with its core powertrain (2.0L turbo engine and 9-speed automatic transmission) showing no significant widespread issues. The primary areas of concern are typically related to optional features and complex electronics. The optional AIR BODY CONTROL suspension can be prone to air strut leaks, a common issue with air suspension systems across various brands, with repair costs varying by component. Electrically, occasional software glitches within the MBUX infotainment system and premature 12V battery failure have been noted, though these are not universally reported. Exterior and interior components generally hold up well, reflecting Mercedes-Benz's build quality. For owners, proactive maintenance and addressing warning lights promptly are key to maintaining the E350's reliability.
