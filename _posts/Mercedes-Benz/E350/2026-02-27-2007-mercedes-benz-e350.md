# 2007 Mercedes-Benz E350

### Overview
The 2007 Mercedes-Benz E350, part of the W211 generation, is generally considered a solid vehicle with a reputation for comfort and performance. While it offers a luxurious driving experience, like any vehicle of its age, it is susceptible to certain common issues. This analysis focuses on documented technical reliability concerns beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential problem areas for owners and prospective buyers. Understanding these specific points can help in proactive maintenance and informed repair decisions, ensuring the continued enjoyment of this executive sedan.

---


## Engine

### 3.5L V6 (M272)
**Intake Manifold Runner Flap Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Check engine light, rough idle, loss of power, P2004/P2005/P2006 codes.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the intake manifold assembly or repair kit.
- **Cost:** $600 - $1200

**Balance Shaft Gear Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Check engine light, P0016/P0017 codes (crankshaft/camshaft position correlation), rough running.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the balance shaft and associated gears, often requiring engine disassembly.
- **Cost:** $2000 - $4000+

**Oil Leaks (Valve Cover Gaskets, Oil Cooler Seals)**
- **Symptoms:** Visible oil leaks, burning oil smell, low oil level.
- **Solution:** Replacement of valve cover gaskets and/or oil cooler seals.
- **Cost:** $400 - $800


## Transmission

### 5-Speed Automatic (722.6)
**Conductor Plate Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Harsh shifting, transmission slipping, 'limp mode', speedometer issues, P0705/P0715 codes.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the conductor plate and transmission fluid service.
- **Cost:** $700 - $1500

**Torque Converter Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Shuddering at low speeds, slipping, delayed engagement.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the torque converter.
- **Cost:** $1000 - $2000


### 7-Speed Automatic (722.9 - '7G-Tronic')
**Valve Body Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Harsh shifting, delayed engagement, transmission warning light.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of the valve body, often requires software update.
- **Cost:** $1500 - $3000+

**Transmission Control Module (TCM) Failure**
- **Symptoms:** No communication with transmission, various transmission fault codes, complete loss of function.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the TCM.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1500


## Suspension

### Standard Chassis
**Air Suspension (if equipped)**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle sagging, air leaks, compressor failure, warning lights.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of air springs, compressor, or lines.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1500 per corner/component

**Control Arm Bushings**
- **Symptoms:** Clunking noises over bumps, vague steering, uneven tire wear.
- **Solution:** Replacement of worn control arm bushings or complete control arms.
- **Cost:** $400 - $800


## Electrical

### General Electrical System
**SAM (Signal Acquisition Module) Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Intermittent electrical gremlins, malfunctioning lights, wipers, or other accessories.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and potential repair or replacement of the SAM unit.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1200

**Window Regulator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Power windows not operating, slow operation, or falling into the door.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the window regulator mechanism.
- **Cost:** $200 - $400

**Headlight Ballast Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Xenon headlights not turning on or flickering.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the headlight ballast.
- **Cost:** $200 - $500

## Interior

### Cabin Systems
**Climate Control Actuator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Air not blowing from desired vents, temperature control issues.
- **Solution:** Replacement of faulty HVAC actuators.
- **Cost:** $200 - $500

**Seat Control Module Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Power seats not functioning correctly or intermittently.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and potential replacement of the seat control module.
- **Cost:** $300 - $700

### Summary Verdict
The 2007 Mercedes-Benz E350 (W211) offers a compelling blend of luxury and performance, but potential buyers should be aware of specific reliability concerns. The 3.5L V6 engine (M272) is prone to issues with the intake manifold runner flaps and balance shaft gear wear, which can be costly to repair. Transmission reliability varies between the 5-speed and 7-speed automatics, with conductor plate and valve body issues being notable. Electrical gremlins, particularly related to the SAM module and window regulators, are also common. While the exterior is generally robust, interior components like climate control actuators can fail. Despite these potential issues, with diligent maintenance and awareness of these common problems, the E350 can remain a dependable and enjoyable vehicle.
