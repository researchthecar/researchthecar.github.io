# 2020 Mercedes-Benz E350 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2020 Mercedes-Benz E350 delivers sophisticated luxury, advanced technology, and refined performance that appeal to used luxury car buyers. However, owner reports submitted to federal safety regulators highlight a few mechanical and electrical areas that deserve closer inspection during a pre-purchase evaluation.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4

The 2.0-liter turbocharged inline-4 engine produces approximately 255 horsepower and 273 lb-ft of torque, offering an efficient blend of power and everyday driveability. Buyers should remain diligent about monitoring for minor oil leaks that could impact surrounding engine electronics.

**Oil Leak and Harness Corrosion**
- **Symptoms:** Oil can leak from the camshaft sensor and wick throughout the electrical harness, potentially causing severe corrosion and expensive repairs without generating early dashboard warnings.
- **Solution:** Inspect the camshaft sensor and wiring harness thoroughly during routine maintenance and replace any compromised electrical components.
- **Cost:** $4,000+

**Inoperative Oil Pump Valve**
- **Symptoms:** Illuminated engine warning messages and associated fault codes indicating an inoperative oil pump valve.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified technician diagnose the fault code and replace the faulty oil pump control valve as needed.

### 3.0L Turbocharged Inline-6 (E450)

The 3.0-liter turbocharged inline-6 engine generates around 362 horsepower and 369 lb-ft of torque, providing robust and exceptionally smooth acceleration. This option generally maintains a solid reputation among owners, provided standard maintenance schedules are strictly followed.

## Transmission

### 9G-TRONIC 9-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 9-speed automatic transmission delivers seamless gear changes across rear-wheel and 4MATIC all-wheel-drive configurations. It is generally robust, though occasional electronic hesitation tied to accessory features has been noted by some drivers.

**Auto Start-Stop Hesitation**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may occasionally fail to respond promptly or resume motion immediately after the gasoline-saving start-stop system shuts off the engine at a traffic signal.
- **Solution:** Check for available powertrain control module software updates at a dealership or specialized service center.

## Electrical

### Backup Camera

Owner reports indicate intermittent software and hardware glitches associated with the rearview camera system.

**Rearview Camera Freeze or Black Screen**
- **Symptoms:** When shifting into reverse, the infotainment display may show a frozen image, display an inoperable warning message, or go completely black.
- **Solution:** Inspect software firmware versions and replace malfunctioning camera modules or display wiring if issues persist.

## Exterior

### Convertible Top and Sunroof

Exterior glass and roof components require careful inspection for premature wear or environmental stress.

**Convertible Soft Top Window Delamination**
- **Symptoms:** The rear glass window integrated into the convertible soft top may prematurely separate or delaminate from the surrounding assembly.
- **Solution:** Have an upholstery specialist inspect the soft top bonding and replace the top assembly if structural separation occurs.

## Summary Verdict

The 2020 Mercedes-Benz E350 offers high-end luxury and impressive performance, but prospective buyers should conduct a thorough mechanical inspection. Pay close attention to potential oil leaks around the engine sensors, electrical wiring conditions, and the functionality of onboard electronics like the rearview camera and convertible top before finalizing a purchase.
