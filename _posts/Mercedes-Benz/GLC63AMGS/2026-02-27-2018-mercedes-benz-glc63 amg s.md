# 2018 Mercedes-Benz GLC63 AMG S

### Overview
The 2018 Mercedes-Benz GLC63 AMG S is a high-performance luxury SUV that combines potent AMG engineering with the practicality of the GLC platform. While generally well-regarded for its exhilarating driving dynamics and premium interior, like any performance vehicle, it can present specific reliability considerations. This analysis focuses on documented technical issues beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential areas of concern for owners and prospective buyers. The focus is on the specific AMG-tuned powertrain and associated systems that contribute to its unique driving experience.

---


## Engine

### 4.0L Twin-Turbo V8 (M177)
**Turbocharger Wastegate Actuator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Check engine light, reduced engine power, boost pressure issues, rattling noise from turbo area.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the faulty wastegate actuator or the complete turbocharger assembly.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1500

**Oil Cooler Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Oil smell in the engine bay, visible oil residue around the oil cooler, potential for oil level drops.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the oil cooler and associated seals.
- **Cost:** $500 - $900


## Transmission

### AMG SPEEDSHIFT MCT 9-Speed Automatic
**Harsh Shifting / Jerking**
- **Symptoms:** Noticeable clunks or jerks during gear changes, particularly at lower speeds or during aggressive acceleration.
- **Solution:** Software update for transmission control module, or in more severe cases, internal transmission repairs or replacement.
- **Cost:** $300 - $800 (Software Update), $3000+ (Repairs/Replacement)

**Transmission Fluid Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Leaking fluid from the transmission pan or seals, potential for slipping gears or warning lights.
- **Solution:** Tightening or replacing the transmission pan gasket, or repairing/replacing seals.
- **Cost:** $400 - $700


## Suspension

### AMG RIDE CONTROL+ Air Suspension
**Air Spring Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle sagging on one corner, uneven ride height, compressor running excessively, warning lights related to suspension.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the leaking air spring or air strut assembly.
- **Cost:** $1000 - $2500 per corner

**Air Suspension Compressor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Inability to adjust ride height, constant vehicle sag, suspension warning lights.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the air suspension compressor unit.
- **Cost:** $700 - $1200


## Electrical

### Infotainment System
**COMAND System Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Screen freezing, unresponsiveness, Bluetooth connectivity issues, navigation system errors.
- **Solution:** Software updates, hard resets, or replacement of the COMAND unit.
- **Cost:** $200 - $500 (Software/Reset), $1500+ (Replacement)


### Battery Drain
**Parasitic Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Battery dying prematurely, difficulty starting the vehicle, electrical components malfunctioning.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis to identify the parasitic draw (e.g., faulty module, aftermarket accessory) and repair or replacement of the offending component.
- **Cost:** $300 - $800

### Summary Verdict
The 2018 Mercedes-Benz GLC63 AMG S, while a formidable performance SUV, exhibits some specific reliability concerns primarily related to its high-output AMG powertrain and advanced air suspension system. Potential owners should be aware of possible issues with turbocharger wastegate actuators, oil cooler leaks, and the complexity of the AMG SPEEDSHIFT transmission, which can sometimes exhibit harsh shifting. The air suspension, while offering excellent ride quality, can be prone to leaks or compressor failures, leading to significant repair costs. Electrical gremlins, particularly with the COMAND infotainment system and occasional battery drain, are also noted. Despite these points, many components, including the exterior and interior elements, are generally robust. Regular maintenance and prompt attention to any warning signs are crucial for maintaining this performance-oriented vehicle.
