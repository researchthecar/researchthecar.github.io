# 2019 Mercedes-Benz GLC63 AMG S Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2019 Mercedes-Benz GLC 63 AMG S offers high-performance luxury with twin-turbo V8 power, but prospective used-car buyers should review owner-reported complaints carefully. While high-performance luxury vehicles bring thrilling driving dynamics, they also demand rigorous maintenance and can involve expensive out-of-pocket repair bills if complex electronic or mechanical systems fail. Thoroughly inspecting vehicle history records and securing a pre-purchase inspection by a qualified technician can help mitigate potential ownership risks.

---

## Engine

### 4.0L Biturbo V8 Engine

The hand-built 4.0-liter biturbo V8 engine produces an impressive 503 horsepower and 516 lb-ft of torque, delivering blistering performance. Routine maintenance is critical, and complex engine systems can be costly to service outside of warranty.

**Engine Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers report unexpected engine stalling while operating the vehicle, accompanied by warning lights and diagnostic trouble codes.
- **Solution:** Diagnostic scanning by a qualified service center to pinpoint faulty sensors, fuel delivery components, or software issues.

## Transmission

### AMG Speedshift MCT 9-Speed Automatic Transmission

The AMG Speedshift MCT 9-speed automatic transmission features a wet clutch pack in place of a traditional torque converter for rapid shifts. It handles high torque loads effectively, though proper fluid maintenance is essential for longevity.

**Unintended Neutral Shift**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may unexpectedly shift or slip into neutral while driving, leading to a temporary loss of drive power.
- **Solution:** Inspect transmission control modules and mechanical shift linkage; update software or replace failing internal components as needed.

## Steering

### Steering and Suspension Geometry

The steering system provides sharp, direct feedback suited for a high-performance SUV, though wide performance tires can introduce low-speed tracking quirks.

**Tire Skipping and Binding ('Crabbing')**
- **Symptoms:** Low-speed tight turns can cause the front wheels to judder, hop, or skip across the pavement.
- **Solution:** Consult a dealer or specialist regarding tire compounds and alignment adjustments designed to minimize cold-weather scrubbing.

## Electrical

### Electrical and Infotainment Systems

Advanced electrical architecture powers numerous convenience and safety features, but complex wiring harnesses require attentive troubleshooting if electrical glitches arise.

**Motive Power Loss and Key Fob Detection Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have reported dashboard warnings indicating key fobs are not detected, occasionally coinciding with sudden losses of motive power.
- **Solution:** Check key fob batteries, antenna modules, and main vehicle electrical grounds; perform software updates as recommended.

## Exterior

### Sunroof

Exterior panels and trim elements are solidly constructed, though panoramic glass components require careful inspection for stress cracks.

**Panoramic Sunroof Shattering**
- **Symptoms:** The glass panel on the panoramic sunroof may crack or shatter spontaneously while driving at highway speeds without prior impact.
- **Solution:** Replace the damaged glass panel and inspect the sunroof frame for alignment issues or stress points.
