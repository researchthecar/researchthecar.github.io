# 2019 Mercedes-Benz GLE500e Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When evaluating a used luxury hybrid SUV, checking historical maintenance records and common trouble areas is essential. This reliability report breaks down potential pain points gathered from owner experiences, helping prospective buyers make an informed choice on the pre-owned market.

---

## Engine

### 3.0L Turbo V6 Plug-in Hybrid

The 3.0-liter turbocharged V6 plug-in hybrid engine pairs forced induction with electric assistance to deliver robust power. It generally provides strong performance, though owners should monitor routine electronic and sensor codes.

**Oil Pump Solenoid Code**
- **Symptoms:** The check engine warning light may illuminate due to an active P06DA00 diagnostic trouble code.
- **Solution:** Inspect the oil pump solenoid and associated wiring harness for faults, replacing damaged wiring or components as necessary.

## Transmission

### 7-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 7-speed automatic transmission distributes power smoothly across the hybrid drivetrain. Routine fluid maintenance is critical to preserving long-term shift quality.

## Exterior

### Panoramic Sunroof and Trim

Exterior body structures and glass assemblies generally hold up well, though owners have reported rare issues with roof bonding agents.

**Sunroof Panel Detachment**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may hear loud banging or whistling noises at highway speeds, potentially followed by exterior roof trim or glass panels detaching.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified technician inspect the sunroof bonding adhesive and rail system, replacing failing seals or securing loose trim components.

## Safety

### Seat Belts

Safety systems generally offer robust protection, though electronic sensors and pretensioners require occasional diagnostic checks.

**Seat Belt Pretensioner Faults**
- **Symptoms:** The seat belt warning light illuminates on the dashboard even when occupants are securely buckled, logging diagnostic hard fault codes.
- **Solution:** Inspect the seat belt wiring and powered pretensioner modules, and replace faulty assemblies according to diagnostic protocols.

A thorough pre-purchase inspection by a qualified specialist will help uncover any lingering electrical or mechanical concerns.
