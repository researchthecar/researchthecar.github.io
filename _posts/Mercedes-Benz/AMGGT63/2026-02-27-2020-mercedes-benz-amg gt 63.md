# 2020 Mercedes-Benz AMG GT 63

### Overview
The 2020 Mercedes-Benz AMG GT 63 is a high-performance luxury grand tourer that blends potent power with sophisticated design. As a top-tier AMG model, it generally exhibits strong build quality and engineering. However, like any complex vehicle, particularly those with high-output powertrains, certain areas may experience issues over time. This analysis focuses on documented technical reliability concerns beyond standard maintenance, providing insights into potential problem areas for owners and enthusiasts.

---


## Engine

### 4.0L Twin-Turbo V8 (M177)
**Turbocharger Wastegate Actuator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Check engine light, reduced engine power, whistling noise from engine bay.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the faulty wastegate actuator or the complete turbocharger assembly.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1500

**Oil Leaks (Valve Covers/Oil Pan)**
- **Symptoms:** Visible oil under the vehicle, burning oil smell, low oil level.
- **Solution:** Replacement of gaskets or seals.
- **Cost:** $300 - $700


## Transmission

### AMG SPEEDSHIFT MCT 9-Speed Transmission
**Harsh Shifting / Jerking**
- **Symptoms:** Noticeable clunks or hesitations during gear changes, especially at lower speeds.
- **Solution:** Software update for transmission control module, or in some cases, internal transmission service/repair.
- **Cost:** $200 - $1000


## Suspension

### AMG RIDE CONTROL+ Suspension
**Air Suspension Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle sagging on one corner, uneven ride height, 'airmatic' warning light.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of air springs, lines, or compressor.
- **Cost:** $700 - $2000


## Electrical

### Infotainment System
**Screen Freezing/Unresponsiveness**
- **Symptoms:** Infotainment screen becomes unresponsive or freezes, requiring a system reset.
- **Solution:** Software update or potential replacement of the head unit.
- **Cost:** $150 - $800


### 12V Battery Drain
**Premature Battery Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Difficulty starting the vehicle, electrical components malfunctioning intermittently, battery warning light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the 12V auxiliary battery.
- **Cost:** $200 - $400

### Summary Verdict
The 2020 Mercedes-Benz AMG GT 63 demonstrates good overall reliability for a high-performance vehicle. The primary areas of concern tend to be related to the complex powertrain and advanced suspension systems. Issues with turbocharger wastegates, occasional oil leaks, and transmission shifting anomalies have been noted. The advanced air suspension can also be a source of repair costs if leaks develop. Electrical gremlins, particularly with the infotainment system and auxiliary battery, are not uncommon in modern Mercedes-Benz vehicles. Despite these potential issues, the AMG GT 63 remains a robust and capable machine, with most problems being repairable and not indicative of systemic design flaws.
