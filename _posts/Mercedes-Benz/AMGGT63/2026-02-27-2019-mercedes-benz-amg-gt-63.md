# 2019 Mercedes-Benz AMG GT 63 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2019 Mercedes-Benz AMG GT 63 delivers breathtaking performance and luxury, pairing a potent twin-turbo V8 with a sophisticated all-wheel-drive system. While formal owner complaint filings are sparse for this exclusive high-performance model, prospective buyers should still approach a used luxury sports sedan with standard mechanical diligence and thorough inspection habits.

---

## Engine

### 4.0L Twin-Turbo V8 (M177)

This hand-built 4.0-liter biturbo V8 produces around 577 horsepower and 553 lb-ft of torque, offering blistering acceleration alongside complex plumbing and cooling requirements. Proper maintenance and timely servicing are vital to ensure long-term durability in high-stress performance environments.

## Transmission

### AMG Speedshift MCT 9-Speed Automatic

The 9-speed multi-clutch transmission pairs quick, aggressive shifting characteristics with daily-drivable manners when cruising. It is engineered to handle heavy torque loads, though prospective buyers should test all drive modes for smooth operation.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V047000: Electrical System:Wiring](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=AMG+GT+63&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** A wiring harness for the automatic transmission may not be properly secured, allowing it to chafe against the drive shaft and possibly cause an engine stall. An engine stall can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** MBUSA will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the mounting clips and wiring harness, and repair or replace them, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V048000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Engine:Gasoline:Turbo/Supercharger](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=AMG+GT+63&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** An oil leak in the presence of an ignition source such as hot engine or exhaust components can increase the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** MBUSA will notify owners, and dealers will replace the oil feed line to the turbocharger, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V788000: Seat Belts](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=AMG+GT+63&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** Although correctly fastened, the front seat belts may be inaccurately detected as being unfastened, which may cause the electric PRE-SAFE®-function and the pretensioner to be deactivated. The electric PRE-SAFE®-function and the seat belt pretensioners may not deploy during a crash, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** MBUSA will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the front seat belt buckles, replacing them as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V938000: Structure:Critical Fasteners](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=AMG+GT+63&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** In certain types of crash events, the center console may unlatch, allowing the storage compartment to open. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 201, "Occupant Protection in Interior Impact." A center console that opens during a crash can allow unsecured cargo to eject into the cabin.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the front center console storage compartment, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.
