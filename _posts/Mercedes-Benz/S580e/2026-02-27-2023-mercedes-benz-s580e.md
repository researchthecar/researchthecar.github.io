# 2023 Mercedes-Benz S580e Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2023 Mercedes-Benz S580e, buyers are looking at a flagship plug-in hybrid luxury sedan that blends high-end comfort with advanced powertrain technology. While official owner complaint volume is relatively modest, early reports highlight occasional concerns regarding advanced driver assistance features, tire durability on large low-profile setups, and unique body or glass incidents. Prospective buyers should thoroughly inspect vehicle systems and maintenance history during a pre-purchase evaluation.

---

## Engine

### 3.0L Turbocharged Inline-6 Plug-In Hybrid

The 3.0-liter turbocharged inline-6 plug-in hybrid powertrain pairs forced induction with an electric motor to deliver robust output and smooth acceleration. It provides a sophisticated driving experience tailored for high-end luxury buyers, though proper diagnostic oversight is essential.

**Check Engine Warning Light**
- **Symptoms:** The check engine warning light unexpectedly illuminates while driving at highway speeds, accompanied by temporary dashboard warnings.
- **Solution:** Take the vehicle to a qualified dealer or specialist to scan diagnostic trouble codes and inspect engine management or emissions systems.

## Transmission

### 9-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 9-speed automatic transmission works seamlessly with the hybrid system to route power to the rear wheels or 4MATIC system. It generally operates smoothly, though regular fluid maintenance is recommended to ensure long-term durability.

## Suspension

### Tires and Wheels

Suspension components are generally robust and tuned for supreme ride comfort, though large low-profile tire setups are prone to impact damage on rough road surfaces.

**Sidewall Bubbles and Tire Blowouts**
- **Symptoms:** Low-profile tires develop unexpected bubbles on the sidewall or suffer sudden blowouts after minor road impacts.
- **Solution:** Replace damaged tires and consider inspecting wheel alignment and tire pressures regularly; some owners switch to alternative tire options.

## Electrical

### Driver Assistance and Cruise Control

Advanced electrical architectures govern numerous convenience and safety systems, though software sensitivity in driver aids can occasionally cause erratic behavior.

**Abrupt Speed Control and Deceleration**
- **Symptoms:** Adaptive cruise control and speed limit recognition systems cause sudden, jarring deceleration or acceleration without direct driver input.
- **Solution:** Check for software updates at a dealership service center to ensure driver assistance modules operate with the latest calibrations.

## Exterior

### Body Structure and Sunroof

Exterior panels and glass provide a sleek aesthetic, but low ground clearance and large glass roof surfaces require careful handling.

**Sudden Sunroof Shattering**
- **Symptoms:** The glass sunroof panel shatters unexpectedly while driving without any apparent external impact.
- **Solution:** Have the glass assembly inspected and replaced under comprehensive insurance or applicable warranty coverage.

**Front Bumper Clearance**
- **Symptoms:** The front bumper sits low to the ground and can catch on parking lot stops or curbs, leading to partial disconnection.
- **Solution:** Exercise caution when parking facing curbs and have bumper mounting clips inspected or resecured if dislodged.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V309000: Electrical System:Propulsion System:Traction Battery Thermal:Management:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=S580e&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** The high-voltage battery monitoring system software may not alert the driver of a battery malfunction. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 305, "Electric-powered vehicles: Electrolyte Spillage and Electrical Shock Protection." A high-voltage battery monitoring system that does not.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the battery management system software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V360000: Electronic Stability Control (Esc):Control Module:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=S580e&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** The electronic stability program (ESP) software may malfunction, which can result in disabled vehicle dynamics control systems, including the anti-lock brake system, acceleration slip regulation, electronic stability program, and electronic brake force distribution (ABS, ASR, ESP, and EBD), as well as a vehicle speed display failure. Disabled vehicle.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the ESP control unit software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2023 Mercedes-Benz S580e is a technologically advanced luxury sedan that offers refined hybrid performance and exceptional comfort. While owner complaints are few, buyers should pay close attention to tire condition, advanced driver assistance software calibrations, and general electrical health. Conducting a thorough pre-purchase inspection will help ensure that all complex electronic and mechanical systems are functioning correctly.
