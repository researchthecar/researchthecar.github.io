# 2012 Mercedes-Benz GLK350

### Overview
The 2012 Mercedes-Benz GLK350 is a compact luxury SUV known for its distinctive styling and comfortable ride. It generally offers a solid ownership experience, with many owners reporting good reliability. However, like any vehicle, it has specific areas that have seen more attention from repair shops. This analysis focuses on common mechanical and electrical issues reported by owners and technicians, excluding routine maintenance and expected wear and tear. Understanding these potential problem areas can help prospective buyers and current owners budget for and address any necessary repairs.

---


## Engine

### 3.5L V6 (M272)
**Intake Manifold Runner Flap Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Check engine light, rough idle, loss of power, rattling noise from engine.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the intake manifold assembly or repair kit.
- **Cost:** $600 - $1200

**Oil Leaks (Valve Cover Gaskets, Oil Cooler Seals)**
- **Symptoms:** Visible oil stains under the vehicle, burning oil smell, low oil level.
- **Solution:** Replacement of valve cover gaskets and/or oil cooler seals.
- **Cost:** $400 - $800


## Transmission

### 7G-TRONIC 7-Speed Automatic
**Transmission Fluid Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Rough shifting, slipping gears, warning lights, low transmission fluid level.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of transmission seals and cooler lines.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1500

**Valve Body Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Erratic shifting, delayed engagement, harsh shifts.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of the transmission valve body.
- **Cost:** $1000 - $2500

## Electrical

### General Electrical System
**Window Regulator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Power windows not operating, slow window movement, grinding noise.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the window regulator and motor assembly.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600

**Sensor Failures (e.g., ABS, Crankshaft Position)**
- **Symptoms:** Illuminated warning lights (ABS, Check Engine), poor engine performance, traction control issues.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the faulty sensor.
- **Cost:** $200 - $500

### Summary Verdict
The 2012 Mercedes-Benz GLK350 is a generally reliable luxury SUV, particularly for its powertrain components when properly maintained. The most common reported issues tend to be related to the intake manifold runner flaps in the 3.5L V6 engine and potential oil leaks from gaskets and seals. Transmission issues, while not overwhelmingly common, can arise and may involve leaks or valve body problems. Electrical gremlins, such as failing window regulators and sensors, are also noted. Suspension, exterior, and interior components are typically robust. With proactive maintenance and attention to potential leak points and sensor health, the GLK350 can provide a dependable ownership experience.
