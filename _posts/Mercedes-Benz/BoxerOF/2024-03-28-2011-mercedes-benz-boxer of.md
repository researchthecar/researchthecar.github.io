## 2011 Mercedes-Benz Boxer OF

The 2011 Mercedes-Benz Boxer OF is a light-duty commercial vehicle that is available in various configurations, including a box van, chassis cab, and passenger van. It is powered by a 3.0-liter V6 turbo diesel engine that produces 188 horsepower and 354 lb-ft of torque. The OF is equipped with a 6-speed manual transmission as standard, with a 5-speed automatic transmission available as an option.

Owners of the 2011 Mercedes-Benz Boxer OF generally like its powerful engine, spacious interior, and comfortable ride. However, some have reported issues with the vehicle's reliability, particularly with the engine and transmission.

## Engine

**3.0L V6 Turbo Diesel**

* **Turbocharger Failure:** The turbocharger can fail prematurely, resulting in a loss of power and increased smoke from the exhaust. Replacement of the turbocharger is typically required. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $2,000 - $3,000+
* **Fuel Injector Failure:** Faulty fuel injectors can cause rough idling, hesitation during acceleration, and poor fuel economy. Replacement of the fuel injectors is usually necessary. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $500 - $1,000+

## Transmission

**6-Speed Manual**

* **Clutch Failure:** The clutch can wear out prematurely, leading to difficulty shifting gears or slipping. Replacement of the clutch is required. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $1,200 - $1,800+

**5-Speed Automatic**

* **Transmission Fluid Leak:** The transmission can develop a fluid leak, which can lead to slipping or complete failure of the transmission. Replacing the leaking seal or component is necessary. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $500 - $1,500+
* **Shift Solenoid Failure:** Faulty shift solenoids can cause shifting problems, such as delayed shifts or harsh engagements. Replacement of the shift solenoids is typically required. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $300 - $700+

## Suspension

* **Front Suspension Noise:** The front suspension can develop a rattling or clunking noise, indicating worn suspension components. Replacing the affected components, such as the control arms or ball joints, is necessary. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $400 - $1,000+

## Electrical

* **Battery Drain:** The battery can drain prematurely, especially when the vehicle is parked. A faulty alternator, starter, or electrical component may be causing the issue. Diagnosis and repair of the underlying cause is required. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $500 - $1,500+

## Exterior

* **Door Lock Failure:** The door locks can malfunction, making it difficult to open or close the doors. Replacement of the door lock actuators is typically necessary. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $200 - $500+
* **Headlight Failure:** The headlights can experience premature failure. Replacement of the headlight bulbs or the headlight assembly may be required. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $150 - $500+

## Interior

* **Climate Control Malfunction:** The climate control system can malfunction, leading to issues with heating or cooling the cabin. Repair or replacement of the faulty component, such as the blower motor or temperature sensor, is necessary. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $200 - $800+
* **Dashboard Rattle:** The dashboard can develop a rattle or vibration, indicating loose or damaged components. Tightening or replacing the affected components is necessary. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $100 - $300+

## Summary

The 2011 Mercedes-Benz Boxer OF has received mixed reviews regarding reliability. While it offers a powerful engine and comfortable ride, some owners have experienced significant issues with the engine, transmission, and other components. The severity and cost of repairs for these issues can vary depending on the specific problem and the extent of the damage. Regular maintenance and timely repairs are essential to ensure the vehicle's longevity and minimize the risk of major breakdowns.
