# 2018 Mercedes-Benz C180 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2018 Mercedes-Benz C-Class offers a blend of luxury, advanced technology, and refined performance that attracts many used-car buyers. However, prospective owners should review common owner-reported complaints and known issues regarding powertrain components, electrical systems, and body hardware before purchasing.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4 (C300)

A 2.0-liter turbocharged inline-4 engine delivering approximately 241 horsepower and 273 lb-ft of torque, providing strong acceleration balanced with daily efficiency. While generally robust, buyers should monitor for occasional crankcase ventilation faults and check engine warnings.

**Check Engine Light and P052E Code**
- **Symptoms:** The check engine light illuminates on the dashboard, accompanied by diagnostic code P052E relating to the crankcase ventilation system.
- **Solution:** Requires replacement of the defective crankcase ventilation component with an updated manufacturer-revised part.
- **Cost:** $16,500.00

## Transmission

### 9G-TRONIC 9-Speed Automatic Transmission

A 9-speed automatic transmission designed to deliver smooth gear shifts and optimized fuel economy across rear-wheel and all-wheel-drive layouts. It operates reliably under routine maintenance conditions with few widespread mechanical complaints.

## Steering

### Steering Rack and Power Steering

Steering components provide responsive handling, but owners have noted occasional power steering assist failures and related mechanical inspections.

**Power Steering Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may experience sudden loss of power steering assistance accompanied by a dashboard warning message to check the owners manual.
- **Solution:** Inspect steering rack assembly and electrical connections; replace faulty steering components as necessary.

## Safety

### Seat Belts

Safety equipment generally performs well, though front seat belt retraction mechanisms require inspection for proper operation.

**Failure to Retract**
- **Symptoms:** Front driver or passenger seat belts fail to retract or return smoothly to their stowed positions after unbuckling.
- **Solution:** Inspect seat belt retractors and replace faulty seat belt assembly units.
