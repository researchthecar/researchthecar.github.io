# 2004 Mercedes-Benz CLK55 AMG

### Overview
The 2004 Mercedes-Benz CLK55 AMG, part of the W209 generation, is a high-performance grand tourer known for its potent V8 engine and luxurious appointments. While generally well-regarded for its driving dynamics and build quality, like any performance vehicle of its era, it can present specific reliability concerns. This analysis focuses on documented common issues beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential areas of concern for owners and prospective buyers. The CLK55 AMG was equipped with a robust powertrain designed for spirited driving, but certain components may require attention over time due to the demands placed upon them.

---


## Engine

### 5.4L M113 V8
**Coolant Leaks (Hose/Reservoir)**
- **Symptoms:** Visible coolant puddles under the vehicle, low coolant level warning, engine overheating.
- **Solution:** Replacement of leaking hoses or the coolant reservoir. Ensure proper bleeding of the cooling system.
- **Cost:** $200 - $500

**Oil Leaks (Valve Cover Gaskets)**
- **Symptoms:** Oil smell, visible oil on the engine block, potential for oil to drip onto exhaust components.
- **Solution:** Replacement of valve cover gaskets.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600

**Crankshaft Position Sensor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Engine cranks but won't start, intermittent stalling, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the crankshaft position sensor.
- **Cost:** $250 - $450


## Transmission

### 5G-Tronic (722.6) Automatic
**Transmission Control Module (TCM) Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Erratic shifting, harsh shifts, transmission not engaging gears, 'limp mode'.
- **Solution:** Replacement or repair of the TCM. Software updates may also be necessary.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1500

**Conductor Plate Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Similar to TCM failure: shifting problems, no gear engagement, check engine light with transmission codes.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the conductor plate, often done in conjunction with a transmission fluid and filter service.
- **Cost:** $600 - $1000


## Suspension

### Standard AMG Sport Suspension
**Front Control Arm Bushings Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Clunking noises over bumps, vague steering feel, uneven tire wear.
- **Solution:** Replacement of worn control arm bushings or the entire control arm assembly.
- **Cost:** $400 - $700

**Sway Bar Linkage Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Rattling or clunking noises, especially when turning or going over uneven surfaces.
- **Solution:** Replacement of worn sway bar links.
- **Cost:** $150 - $300


## Electrical

### General Electrical System
**Window Regulator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Power windows not operating, window getting stuck in the down or up position.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the window regulator mechanism.
- **Cost:** $250 - $450

**Headlight Ballast Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Xenon headlights not turning on or flickering.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the faulty headlight ballast.
- **Cost:** $300 - $500

**Keyless Go System Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** Difficulty starting the car, door handles not recognizing the key fob.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and potential replacement of keyless go antennas, door handle sensors, or the control module.
- **Cost:** $400 - $800


## Exterior

### Body and Trim
**Sunroof Drain Clogs**
- **Symptoms:** Water leaks into the cabin, particularly around the headliner and A-pillars, musty smell.
- **Solution:** Clearing of the sunroof drain tubes. Regular maintenance is recommended.
- **Cost:** $100 - $250

**Headlight Lens Hazing/Yellowing**
- **Symptoms:** Reduced light output, unsightly appearance of headlights.
- **Solution:** Restoration of headlights using a polishing kit or replacement of headlight units.
- **Cost:** $100 - $400 (restoration) / $800+ (replacement)


## Interior

### Cabin Electronics and Upholstery
**Pixelated Instrument Cluster Display**
- **Symptoms:** Missing or garbled segments on the LCD display (e.g., odometer, warning messages).
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of the instrument cluster.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1000

**HVAC Blower Motor Resistor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Fan only works on certain speeds or not at all.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the blower motor resistor.
- **Cost:** $150 - $300

### Summary Verdict
The 2004 Mercedes-Benz CLK55 AMG (W209) offers a compelling blend of performance and luxury. While its V8 engine and robust chassis are generally reliable, owners should be aware of potential issues common to high-performance vehicles of this era. Key areas to monitor include the cooling system for leaks, the automatic transmission for electronic control module and conductor plate problems, and suspension components like control arm bushings. Electrical gremlins, such as window regulators and headlight ballasts, can also arise. Proactive maintenance, especially fluid changes for the engine and transmission, and regular checks of common leak points will significantly contribute to the long-term reliability and enjoyment of this AMG model.
