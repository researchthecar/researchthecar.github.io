# 2021 Mercedes-Benz C63 AMG S Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2021 Mercedes-Benz C63 AMG S, buyers are looking at a high-performance luxury machine powered by a handcrafted biturbo V8. While owner reports to federal safety regulators are relatively scarce for this specific high-performance trim, prospective buyers should still inspect potential powertrain performance, cooling components, and electronic accessories carefully during a pre-purchase inspection.

---

## Engine

### 4.0L Biturbo V8 (M177)

The C63 AMG S features a handcrafted 4.0-liter twin-turbocharged V8 engine producing around 503 horsepower and 516 lb-ft of torque. It delivers thrilling acceleration and track-capable performance, though buyers should verify regular maintenance intervals and cooling system integrity.

**Engine Stalling and Power Loss**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have reported instances where the vehicle experienced sudden loss of power, reduced speed limitations, or engine stalling accompanied by an illuminated check engine light.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified technician diagnose fault codes, inspect the fuel injection system and sensors, and update software modules as required.

### Cooling System

The engine bay manages high thermal loads due to twin turbochargers, making routine inspections of hoses, fluid levels, and cooling efficiency vital for long-term reliability.

**Coolant Hose Leak**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have experienced unexpected coolant leaks resulting from hose degradation or failures.
- **Solution:** Inspect engine hoses and clamps for signs of wear or leaks, and replace damaged lines immediately to prevent overheating.

## Transmission

### AMG Speedshift MCT 9-Speed Automatic

The wet-clutch 9-speed multi-clutch transmission routes power to the rear wheels with lightning-fast gear changes. Regular fluid maintenance is essential to ensure smooth operation under aggressive driving.

## Electrical

### Panoramic Sunroof

Advanced cabin electronics and comfort accessories require thorough testing during a test drive to ensure all convenience features operate smoothly.

**Sunroof Mechanism Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** The panoramic sunroof may fail to open or close properly, sometimes emitting grinding or popping noises during operation.
- **Solution:** Inspect the track mechanism, motor, and seals, and replace faulty components or reset the control module as needed.
