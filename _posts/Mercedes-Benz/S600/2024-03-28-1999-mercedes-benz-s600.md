**1999 Mercedes-Benz S600**

The 1999 Mercedes-Benz S600 is a luxury sedan that offers a powerful V12 engine and a smooth ride. It comes standard with a 5-speed automatic transmission and has an available 4MATIC all-wheel drive system. The S600 is known for its spacious interior, comfortable seats, and advanced technology.

**Engine**

**5.8L V12**

* **Oil Leaks:** Leaks can occur from the camshaft covers, valve covers, and oil pan. Symptoms include low oil levels, oil spots under the vehicle, and a burning oil smell. Fix: Replace the leaking gaskets or seals. Cost: $500 - $1,500+
* **Timing Chain Tensioner Failure:** The timing chain tensioner can fail, causing the timing chain to skip and damage the engine. Symptoms include a rattling noise from the engine, reduced power, and engine failure. Fix: Replace the timing chain tensioner and timing chain. Cost: $2,500 - $5,000+
* **Intake Manifold Gasket Failure:** The intake manifold gasket can fail, causing an air leak. Symptoms include rough idle, poor acceleration, and a check engine light. Fix: Replace the intake manifold gasket. Cost: $300 - $600+

**Transmission**

**5-Speed Automatic**

* **Shifting Problems:** The transmission may shift hard, slip out of gear, or refuse to shift. Symptoms include difficulty shifting, delayed shifting, and a check engine light. Fix: Repair or replace the transmission. Cost: $2,500 - $5,000+
* **Torque Converter Failure:** The torque converter can fail, causing the transmission to slip or shudder. Symptoms include loss of power, transmission noise, and a check engine light. Fix: Replace the torque converter. Cost: $1,000 - $2,500+

**Suspension**

* **Air Suspension Failure:** The air suspension system can fail, causing the vehicle to ride low or unevenly. Symptoms include a rough ride, difficulty steering, and a check engine light. Fix: Repair or replace the air suspension components. Cost: $1,500 - $3,000+
* **Control Arm Bushings:** The control arm bushings can wear out, causing a clunking noise when driving over bumps. Symptoms include a clunking sound, poor handling, and uneven tire wear. Fix: Replace the control arm bushings. Cost: $500 - $1,000+

**Electrical**

* **Battery Failure:** The battery can fail, causing the vehicle to lose power. Symptoms include difficulty starting, dim headlights, and a check engine light. Fix: Replace the battery. Cost: $200 - $400+
* **Alternator Failure:** The alternator can fail, causing the battery to discharge and the vehicle to lose power. Symptoms include dim headlights, flickering lights, and a check engine light. Fix: Replace the alternator. Cost: $500 - $1,000+
* **Power Window Failure:** The power window motors can fail, causing the windows to become inoperable. Symptoms include difficulty opening or closing the windows, and a burning smell. Fix: Replace the power window motors. Cost: $200 - $500+ per window

**Exterior**

* **Headlight Switch Failure:** The headlight switch can fail, causing the headlights to not work. Symptoms include dim headlights, flickering lights, and a check engine light. Fix: Replace the headlight switch. Cost: $200 - $400+
* **Sunroof Leak:** The sunroof can leak, causing water to enter the vehicle. Symptoms include water stains on the headliner, wet carpets, and a musty smell. Fix: Seal the sunroof or replace the sunroof seal. Cost: $200 - $500+

**Interior**

* **Navigation System Failure:** The navigation system can fail, causing the map screen to become blank or unresponsive. Symptoms include a blank map screen, incorrect directions, and a check engine light. Fix: Replace the navigation system. Cost: $1,000 - $2,500+
* **Climate Control Failure:** The climate control system can fail, causing the air conditioning or heater to not work. Symptoms include warm air blowing from the vents, cold air blowing from the vents, and a check engine light. Fix: Repair or replace the climate control components. Cost: $500 - $1,500+
* **Instrument Cluster Failure:** The instrument cluster can fail, causing the gauges to become inoperable. Symptoms include no gauges working, flickering gauges, and a check engine light. Fix: Replace the instrument cluster. Cost: $500 - $1,000+

**Summary**

The 1999 Mercedes-Benz S600 is a reliable and luxurious vehicle, but it can experience some common issues. The most severe issues are engine failures, transmission failures, and suspension failures, which can be expensive to repair. Minor issues, such as electrical problems and interior component failures, are less common and less costly to fix. Overall, the S600 is a solid choice for those seeking a comfortable and luxurious ride.
