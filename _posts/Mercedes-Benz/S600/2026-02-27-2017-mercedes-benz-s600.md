# 2017 Mercedes-Benz S600 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2017 Mercedes-Benz S600 represents the pinnacle of luxury, performance, and sophisticated engineering. As a flagship sedan, it offers immense power and a serene cabin experience, making it a highly desirable option on the pre-owned market. Prospective buyers should still approach with standard luxury-car due diligence, keeping in mind that sophisticated electronics and specialized mechanical components can require attentive maintenance.

---

## Engine

### 6.0L Biturbo V12

The 2017 Mercedes-Benz S600 is powered by a 6.0-liter twin-turbocharged V12 engine producing approximately 523 horsepower and 612 lb-ft of torque. This engine delivers exceptionally smooth, effortless acceleration suited to a luxury flagship, though routine maintenance costs are characteristically high due to its complex layout and component density.

## Transmission

### 7G-TRONIC Plus 7-Speed Automatic

Power is routed through a 7-speed automatic transmission designed to deliver seamless gear changes matching the refined nature of the V12 engine. While generally robust, electronic shifter logic requires familiarization to prevent unintended gear selection when powering down.

**Automatic Shift to Neutral Upon Shutdown**
- **Symptoms:** When pushing the ignition button to turn off the car while the transmission is in Drive, the vehicle may shift into Neutral rather than Park, leading to potential rollaway risks if the door is not opened.
- **Solution:** Ensure proper software calibration or adhere strictly to opening the door or manually shifting into Park before exiting.

## Exterior

### Windshield and Sunroof

Exterior glass and large panoramic roof assemblies provide an open cabin feel but require careful inspection for vulnerability to chips and mechanical binding.

**Sunroof and Windshield Cracking**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have reported cracking sounds and operational failures when attempting to close the panoramic sunroof, alongside frequent windshield cracking under normal driving conditions.
- **Solution:** Inspect glass panels and sunroof tracks carefully for stress fractures or alignment issues, and replace damaged components as necessary.

## Safety

### Dashboard Trim and Airbag Area

Safety equipment is comprehensive, though cosmetic material degradation around sensitive interior areas like airbag covers has been noted by some owners.

**Dashboard Leather Separation**
- **Symptoms:** The leather covering on the dashboard begins to pull away or warp around the passenger and driver side airbag deployment zones, potentially exposing internal components.
- **Solution:** Requires dashboard trim replacement or specialized upholstery repair, which can involve significant out-of-pocket expenses.
- **Cost:** $3,000 to $6,000

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V058000: Electrical System:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=S600&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** The software design of the communication module may fail to communicate the correct vehicle location for the emergency call system (eCall) in the event of a crash. Failure of the eCall system may result in emergency responders being dispatched to the wrong location, increasing the risk of injury following a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2017 Mercedes-Benz S600 remains an extraordinary machine that delivers supreme comfort and commanding V12 performance. While major mechanical failures are rare, used buyers should thoroughly inspect interior trim elements like the dashboard leather, test all glass and sunroof operations, and verify software functions. Budgeting for specialized maintenance will ensure a rewarding ownership experience.
