# 2005 Mercedes-Benz S600

### Overview
The 2005 Mercedes-Benz S600, a flagship luxury sedan, is renowned for its potent V12 engine and advanced features. While offering a supremely comfortable and powerful driving experience, like many high-performance, complex vehicles of its era, it can present specific reliability challenges. Owners should be aware of potential issues, particularly concerning the sophisticated powertrain and intricate electronic systems. Regular and specialized maintenance is crucial for mitigating these concerns and preserving the vehicle's performance and longevity. This analysis focuses on common reported technical issues beyond standard maintenance, providing insights into potential repair areas and associated costs.

---


## Engine

### 5.5L V12 Bi-Turbo
**Ignition Coil Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Misfires, rough idling, check engine light, reduced engine power.
- **Solution:** Replacement of faulty ignition coils.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600 per coil

**Turbocharger Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of power, whistling noises from the engine bay, increased oil consumption.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of turbochargers.
- **Cost:** $2,000 - $5,000 per turbocharger

**Coolant Leaks (ABC System)**
- **Symptoms:** Low coolant level, visible leaks, engine overheating, warning lights.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of hoses, seals, or the ABC pump.
- **Cost:** $500 - $2,500


## Transmission

### 5-Speed Automatic (722.6)
**Transmission Control Module (TCM) Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Erratic shifting, transmission slipping, 'limp mode', failure to engage gears.
- **Solution:** Replacement or reprogramming of the TCM.
- **Cost:** $800 - $2,000

**Torque Converter Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Shuddering at low speeds, slipping, delayed engagement.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the torque converter.
- **Cost:** $1,500 - $3,000


## Suspension

### Active Body Control (ABC)
**Hydraulic Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle sagging on one or more corners, 'ABC Malfunction' warning on the dashboard, stiff ride.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of hydraulic lines, struts, or the ABC pump.
- **Cost:** $1,000 - $4,000+

**ABC Pump Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of hydraulic pressure, vehicle sagging, loud whining noise from the pump.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the ABC hydraulic pump.
- **Cost:** $1,500 - $3,000


## Electrical

### Infotainment System
**COMAND System Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Screen freezing, unresponsive buttons, audio issues, navigation problems.
- **Solution:** Software updates, component replacement (head unit, screen).
- **Cost:** $500 - $2,500


### Sensors
**Oxygen Sensor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Check engine light, poor fuel economy, rough idling.
- **Solution:** Replacement of faulty oxygen sensors.
- **Cost:** $200 - $500 per sensor


### Headlight Ballast Failure
_No major issues reported for this configuration._

## Exterior

### Door Lock Actuators
**Malfunctioning Door Locks**
- **Symptoms:** Doors not locking or unlocking remotely or manually, individual door lock failure.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the door lock actuator module.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600 per actuator


### Sunroof Mechanism
**Sunroof Motor/Mechanism Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Sunroof not opening or closing, unusual noises during operation.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of the sunroof motor or associated mechanisms.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1,200


## Interior

### Seat Control Modules
**Power Seat Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** Power seats not moving, memory functions failing, seat heating/cooling issues.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and replacement of seat control modules or motors.
- **Cost:** $400 - $1,000


### Climate Control System
**HVAC Blower Motor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** No air coming from vents, inconsistent fan speed, unusual noises from the dashboard.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the HVAC blower motor or resistor.
- **Cost:** $300 - $700

### Summary Verdict
The 2005 Mercedes-Benz S600 is a complex luxury vehicle with a powerful V12 engine and advanced systems. While it offers unparalleled comfort and performance, potential buyers and owners should anticipate higher maintenance and repair costs compared to less complex vehicles. Key areas of concern include the sophisticated Active Body Control (ABC) suspension, the intricate electrical system, and the twin-turbocharged V12 engine's ignition and turbo components. Transmission issues, particularly with the electronic control module, can also arise. Despite these potential challenges, with diligent maintenance and prompt attention to emerging issues, the S600 can remain a rewarding ownership experience. Budgeting for specialized repairs is highly recommended.
