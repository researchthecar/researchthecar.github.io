The 2002 Mercedes-Benz S600 represents the pinnacle of luxury sedans from that era. It boasts a powerful V12 engine and a host of advanced features for its time. The S-Class was also available with V6 and V8 engine options, offering a broader range of performance and price points. The S600's V12 engine delivers impressive horsepower and torque, resulting in smooth and effortless acceleration. Owners often praise the car's comfortable ride, luxurious interior, and sophisticated styling. However, the complexity of the S600's systems can lead to higher maintenance costs and potential reliability issues. Common complaints often revolve around the car's intricate electronics, air suspension, and specialized components. The vehicle is equipped with a 5-speed automatic transmission.

**Engine**

**5.8L M137 V12**

*   **Coil Pack Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rough running engine, misfires, check engine light. Often affects one or more of the individual coil packs.
    *   Solution: Replace faulty coil pack(s).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300+ per coil pack
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Oil Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Oil spots under the car, burning oil smell, low oil level. Common leak areas include valve cover gaskets, oil pan gasket, and rear main seal.
    *   Solution: Replace the leaking gaskets or seals.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1500+, depending on the location of the leak.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Cooling System Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Overheating, coolant leaks, coolant loss. Common issues include radiator leaks, thermostat failure, and water pump failure.
    *   Solution: Replace faulty components.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**5-Speed Automatic (722.6)**

*   **Torque Converter Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Shuddering during acceleration, especially at lower speeds. Delayed or erratic shifting.
    *   Solution: Replace the torque converter.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1500 - $3000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Valve Body Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Harsh or delayed shifting, slipping between gears, failure to shift.
    *   Solution: Rebuild or replace the valve body.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $2000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Fluid Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Reddish fluid under the car, difficulty shifting, transmission slipping. Often leaks from the conductor plate.
    *   Solution: Replace the conductor plate and reseal the transmission.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Air Suspension Failure (Airmatic)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Car sagging on one or more corners, suspension warning light, harsh ride. Common issues include leaking air struts, compressor failure, and valve block issues.
    *   Solution: Replace faulty air struts, compressor, or valve block.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $2000+ per strut, $500 - $1500+ for compressor, $300 - $800+ for valve block.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **ABC Suspension Failure (if equipped)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Car sinking to the ground when parked, suspension warning lights, ride height issues, leaking hydraulic fluid.
    *   Solution: Repair or replace faulty accumulators, pulsation dampener, or tandem pump.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1000 - $2000+ depending on the failed part
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Battery Drain**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Dead battery, car not starting, electrical components malfunctioning. Can be caused by a variety of issues including faulty modules, parasitic draws, or a failing alternator.
    *   Solution: Diagnose and repair the source of the battery drain.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $1000+, depending on the source.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Instrument Cluster Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Display malfunctions, flickering lights, incorrect readings.
    *   Solution: Repair or replace the instrument cluster.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Window Regulator Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Window not going up or down, grinding noises.
    *   Solution: Replace the window regulator.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $700+ per window
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Rust**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rust bubbling or corrosion on body panels, particularly around wheel wells, rocker panels, and door edges.
    *   Solution: Repair or replace affected panels, treat rust.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $2000+, depending on the severity and location.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Headlight Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Cloudy or yellowed headlight lenses, malfunctioning headlight bulbs, or faulty leveling systems.
    *   Solution: Polish or replace headlight lenses, replace bulbs, or repair the leveling system.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $1000+, depending on the issue.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Sunroof Problems**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Sunroof not opening or closing properly, getting stuck, or leaking.
    *   Solution: Repair or replace the sunroof motor, tracks, or seals.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Audio System Malfunctions**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Speakers not working, head unit not functioning, amplifier issues.
    *   Solution: Diagnose and repair or replace faulty components.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $1000+, depending on the issue.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2002 Mercedes-Benz S600, while offering a luxurious and powerful driving experience, is prone to a number of potentially expensive issues. The air suspension and complex electrical systems are common sources of problems, as are the V12 engine and its associated components. Transmission issues can also arise. While some repairs may be relatively minor, others can be quite costly, potentially exceeding several thousand dollars. Due to the age of the vehicle, preventative maintenance is essential to prolong its life and minimize major repairs.
