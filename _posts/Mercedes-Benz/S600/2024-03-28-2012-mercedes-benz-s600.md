**Introduction**

The 2012 Mercedes-Benz S600 is a luxury sedan that is known for its comfort, performance, and technology. It is powered by a 5.5-liter twin-turbocharged V8 engine that produces 510 horsepower and 612 lb-ft of torque. The engine is mated to a seven-speed automatic transmission. The S600 is available in rear-wheel drive or all-wheel drive.

Some of the things that people like about the S600 include its spacious and luxurious interior, its powerful engine, and its advanced technology features. However, some people have reported issues with the vehicle's engine, transmission, suspension, electrical system, exterior, and interior.

**Engine**

**5.5L Twin-Turbocharged V8**

* **Oil leaks:** Oil leaks can occur from the valve covers, oil pan, or rear main seal. The symptoms of an oil leak include a burning smell, visible oil under the vehicle, or low oil levels. The solution is to replace the leaking part. The estimated cost to fix an oil leak is $200 - $1,000+ depending on the severity of the leak.
* **Turbocharger failure:** The turbochargers can fail due to a number of reasons, including oil starvation, excessive heat, or mechanical failure. The symptoms of turbocharger failure include a loss of power, black smoke from the exhaust, or a whining noise. The solution is to replace the turbocharger. The estimated cost to fix a turbocharger failure is $2,000 - $4,000+.
* **Engine misfire:** Engine misfires can occur due to a number of reasons, including faulty spark plugs, ignition coils, or fuel injectors. The symptoms of an engine misfire include a rough idle, loss of power, or increased emissions. The solution is to diagnose and replace the faulty part. The estimated cost to fix an engine misfire is $100 - $500+.

**Transmission**

**7-Speed Automatic**

* **Transmission slipping:** The transmission may slip when shifting gears. The symptoms of transmission slipping include a loss of power, jerking, or hesitation. The solution is to rebuild or replace the transmission. The estimated cost to fix transmission slipping is $2,000 - $4,000+.
* **Transmission fluid leaks:** Transmission fluid leaks can occur from the transmission pan, lines, or seals. The symptoms of a transmission fluid leak include low fluid levels, a burning smell, or slipping gears. The solution is to replace the leaking part. The estimated cost to fix a transmission fluid leak is $200 - $1,000+ depending on the severity of the leak.

**Suspension**

* **Air suspension failure:** The air suspension system can fail due to a number of reasons, including leaks, electrical problems, or mechanical failure. The symptoms of air suspension failure include a rough ride, uneven ride height, or a loss of control. The solution is to diagnose and repair the failed component. The estimated cost to fix air suspension failure is $500 - $2,000+.
* **Suspension noise:** The suspension may make noise over bumps or rough roads. The symptoms of suspension noise include a clunking, rattling, or squeaking sound. The solution is to diagnose and replace the worn or damaged part. The estimated cost to fix suspension noise is $200 - $1,000+.

**Electrical**

* **Battery drain:** The battery may drain prematurely due to a number of reasons, including a faulty alternator, starter, or electrical component. The symptoms of a battery drain include difficulty starting the vehicle, dim headlights, or a dead battery. The solution is to diagnose and replace the faulty component. The estimated cost to fix a battery drain is $200 - $1,000+.
* **Electrical system failure:** The electrical system may fail due to a number of reasons, including a faulty battery, alternator, starter, or wiring harness. The symptoms of an electrical system failure include a loss of power, no lights, or no start. The solution is to diagnose and replace the faulty component. The estimated cost to fix an electrical system failure is $500 - $2,000+.

**Exterior**

* **Door lock failure:** The door locks may fail to lock or unlock due to a number of reasons, including a faulty actuator, switch, or wiring harness. The symptoms of a door lock failure include difficulty locking or unlocking the doors, or the doors locking or unlocking on their own. The solution is to diagnose and replace the faulty component. The estimated cost to fix a door lock failure is $200 - $1,000+.
* **Window regulator failure:** The window regulators may fail due to a number of reasons, including a faulty motor, switch, or wiring harness. The symptoms of a window regulator failure include difficulty raising or lowering the windows, or the windows opening or closing on their own. The solution is to diagnose and replace the faulty component. The estimated cost to fix a window regulator failure is $300 - $1,000+.

**Interior**

* **Navigation system failure:** The navigation system may fail due to a number of reasons, including a faulty software, GPS module, or wiring harness. The symptoms of a navigation system failure include a blank screen, incorrect directions, or no sound. The solution is to diagnose and replace the faulty component. The estimated cost to fix a navigation system failure is $500 - $2,000+.
* **Climate control failure:** The climate control system may fail due to a number of reasons, including a faulty compressor, blower motor, or wiring harness. The symptoms of a climate control failure include no heat or air conditioning, or the system not blowing air. The solution is to diagnose and replace the faulty component. The estimated cost to fix a climate control failure is $500 - $2,000+.

**Summary**

The 2012 Mercedes-Benz S600 is a luxurious and powerful sedan that has a number of potential issues. The most common issues are with the engine, transmission, suspension, electrical system, exterior, and interior. The severity of these issues can range from minor to major, and the cost to fix them can range from a few hundred dollars to several thousand dollars. Overall, the S600 is a reliable vehicle, but it is important to be aware of the potential issues that can occur.
