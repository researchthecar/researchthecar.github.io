**2001 Mercedes-Benz S600**

The 2001 Mercedes-Benz S600 is a high-performance luxury sedan powered by a 5.8-liter V12 engine. It also offers a 5-speed automatic transmission with either rear-wheel or all-wheel drive. The S600 produces 493 horsepower and 516 lb-ft of torque, giving it exceptional acceleration and power. 

Despite its high praise for comfort, luxury, and performance, the 2001 Mercedes-Benz S600 has been reported to have some recurring issues. These issues range from minor inconveniences to more serious mechanical problems.

**Engine**

**5.8-liter V12**

* **Intake Manifold Runner Valve Malfunction**
    * Description/Symptoms: Rough idling, decreased engine power, illuminated check engine light
    * Solution: Replace intake manifold runner valve assembly
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,200+
* **Camshaft Timing Adjustment Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Difficulty starting, engine misfires, reduced fuel efficiency
    * Solution: Replace camshaft timing adjuster assembly
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,200 - $1,800+
    * Recalls: N/A

**Transmission**

**5-Speed Automatic**

* **Slipping Gears**
    * Description/Symptoms: Transmission fails to engage gears smoothly, hesitation or slipping during acceleration
    * Solution: Repair or replace transmission
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,500 - $4,000+
    * Recalls: N/A

**Suspension**

* **Air Suspension Compressor Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Suspension system malfunctions, vehicle may sag or lean, illuminated suspension warning light
    * Solution: Replace air suspension compressor
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $1,500+
* **Struts and Shocks Deterioration**
    * Description/Symptoms: Reduced handling and stability, excessive noise or vibration from suspension
    * Solution: Replace worn struts or shocks
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $600 - $1,200+

**Electrical**

* **Alternator Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Difficulty starting, dimming headlights, illuminated battery warning light
    * Solution: Replace alternator
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
* **Battery Discharge**
    * Description/Symptoms: Battery drains quickly, vehicle struggles to start, illuminated battery warning light
    * Solution: Replace battery, check for electrical system parasitic drain
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600+

**Exterior**

**Headlight Gasket Failure**
Description/Symptoms: Water leakage into headlights, condensation fogging, illuminated headlight warning light
Solution: Replace headlight gaskets
Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+

**Interior**

**Dashboard Display Malfunction**
Description/Symptoms: Dimmed or flickering display, loss of functionality, distorted graphics
Solution: Replace or repair dashboard display
Estimated Cost to Fix: $700 - $1,200+
Recalls: N/A

**Summary**

While the 2001 Mercedes-Benz S600 is a luxurious and powerful vehicle, it has been reported to have some significant issues, particularly with its engine, transmission, and suspension. These issues can be costly to repair, ranging from hundreds to thousands of dollars. It is important for owners to be aware of these potential problems and to have their vehicles regularly inspected and maintained to avoid unexpected repairs.
