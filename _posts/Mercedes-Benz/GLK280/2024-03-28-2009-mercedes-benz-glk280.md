**2009 Mercedes-Benz GLK280**

The 2009 Mercedes-Benz GLK280 is a compact luxury SUV that offers a comfortable ride and a well-equipped interior. It is powered by a 3.0L V6 engine that produces 228 horsepower and 221 lb-ft of torque. The GLK280 comes standard with a 7-speed automatic transmission.

People who like the GLK280 appreciate its luxurious interior, comfortable ride, and powerful engine. However, some have complained about its poor fuel economy and high cost of repairs.

**Engine**

**3.0L V6**

* **Intake Manifold Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The intake manifold can crack, causing coolant and oil to leak. This can lead to engine overheating and catastrophic failure.
    * Solution: Replace the intake manifold.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Timing Chain Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The timing chain can stretch or break, causing the engine to lose power or stall.
    * Solution: Replace the timing chain, tensioners, and guides.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**7-Speed Automatic**

* **Shift Solenoid Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The shift solenoids can fail, causing the transmission to shift erratically or not shift at all.
    * Solution: Replace the shift solenoids.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Valve Body Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The valve body can fail, causing the transmission to slip or shudder.
    * Solution: Replace the valve body.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Front Control Arm Bushing Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The front control arm bushings can wear out, causing the vehicle to pull to one side or the other.
    * Solution: Replace the control arm bushings.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Rear Axle Bushings Worn**
    * Description/Symptoms: The rear axle bushings can wear out, causing the vehicle to sway or bounce excessively when driving over uneven surfaces.
    * Solution: Replace the rear axle bushings.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain**
    * Description/Symptoms: The battery can drain quickly, even when the vehicle is not in use.
    * Solution: Diagnose and repair the electrical system to stop the battery drain.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Alternator Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The alternator can fail, causing the battery to discharge and the vehicle to lose power.
    * Solution: Replace the alternator.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Door Lock Mechanism Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The door lock mechanism can fail, making it difficult to open or close the doors.
    * Solution: Repair or replace the door lock mechanism.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Sunroof Problems**
    * Description/Symptoms: The sunroof can leak or become stuck.
    * Solution: Repair or replace the sunroof components as needed.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Instrument Cluster Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The instrument cluster can fail, causing the gauges to become inaccurate or inoperable.
    * Solution: Replace the instrument cluster.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Climate Control Malfunction**
    * Description/Symptoms: The climate control system can malfunction, causing the air conditioning or heater to not work properly.
    * Solution: Diagnose and repair the climate control system as needed.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2009 Mercedes-Benz GLK280 has some notable issues that can be expensive to repair. The engine and transmission are particularly prone to problems, which can cost thousands of dollars to fix. The suspension and electrical systems also have their share of issues. However, the GLK280 is still a popular choice for luxury SUV buyers due to its comfortable ride and well-equipped interior.
