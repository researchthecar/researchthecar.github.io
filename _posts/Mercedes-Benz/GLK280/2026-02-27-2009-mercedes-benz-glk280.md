# 2009 Mercedes-Benz GLK280

### Overview
The 2009 Mercedes-Benz GLK280, a compact luxury SUV, generally presents a solid reliability record for its model year. While no vehicle is entirely free from potential issues, the GLK280 tends to avoid widespread, recurring problems that plague many of its competitors. Owners often praise its robust build quality and the refined driving experience it offers. However, like any vehicle of this age, specific components may require attention over time. This analysis focuses on documented common issues beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential areas of concern for prospective buyers or current owners.

---


## Engine

### 3.0L V6 (M272)
**Intake Manifold Runner Flap Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Check engine light, rough idle, loss of power, rattling noise from engine.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the intake manifold assembly or repair kit.
- **Cost:** $600 - $1200

**Balance Shaft Gear Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Check engine light (P0016, P0017 codes), rough idle, poor performance.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the balance shaft and timing components.
- **Cost:** $1500 - $3000

### General Electrical System
**Sensor Failures (e.g., ABS/ESP sensors)**

### Summary Verdict
The 2009 Mercedes-Benz GLK280 is a generally reliable luxury compact SUV. The powertrain, particularly the 7-speed automatic transmission, is robust. The primary areas to be aware of are potential engine issues related to the intake manifold runner flaps and balance shaft wear, which are known concerns for the M272 engine. Electrical gremlins, such as sensor failures or headlight module issues, can also occur. However, these are often isolated incidents rather than widespread design flaws. With diligent maintenance and prompt attention to any warning signs, the GLK280 can offer a dependable ownership experience.
