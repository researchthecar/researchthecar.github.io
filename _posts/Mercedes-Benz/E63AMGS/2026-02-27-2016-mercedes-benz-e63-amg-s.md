# 2016 Mercedes-Benz E63 AMG S Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2016 Mercedes-Benz E63 AMG S delivers exhilarating performance wrapped in executive luxury, but prospective used-car buyers should approach with a clear understanding of its complex engineering. While high-performance powertrains offer thrilling dynamics, owners have reported various electrical, steering, and structural issues that require vigilant maintenance. Careful inspection of service history and a pre-purchase inspection by a knowledgeable specialist are strongly recommended before finalizing a purchase.

---

## Engine

### 5.8L V8 Biturbo (E63 AMG S)

The 2016 E63 AMG S features a handcrafted 5.5-liter twin-turbocharged V8 engine pushing out 577 horsepower and 590 lb-ft of torque. It provides breathtaking acceleration and a muscular exhaust note, though its high-strung nature demands meticulous maintenance and premium fuel.

**Fuel Odor or Leak**
- **Symptoms:** A strong fuel odor is noticeable while idling, sometimes pointing toward fuel injector or pump assembly concerns.
- **Solution:** Inspect fuel lines, seals, and pump assemblies for leaks, and replace damaged components.

## Transmission

### AMG Speedshift MCT 7-Speed Automatic

The 7-speed AMG Speedshift multi-clutch automatic transmission delivers rapid, aggressive gear changes suited for high-performance driving. Routine fluid changes are essential to maintain shift quality and longevity.

## Brakes

### Brake System

High-performance braking setups offer immense stopping power, though auxiliary components like electronic parking brakes require regular inspection.

**Electronic Parking Brake Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Malfunctions in the electronic parking brake system can prevent proper engagement or require extensive cable and component replacement.
- **Solution:** Inspect the parking brake cables, actuators, and control modules, replacing worn or damaged parts.

## Steering

### Power Steering System

The power steering system provides responsive handling, though occasional electrical or mechanical faults can lead to a sudden loss of power assist.

**Power Steering Assist Failure**
- **Symptoms:** A power steering malfunction message appears on the dashboard, resulting in a sudden loss of power steering assist and heavy steering feel.
- **Solution:** Inspect the steering assembly and electronic modules, and replace faulty components or wiring as necessary.

## Electrical

### Electrical & Advanced Driver Assistance

Sophisticated electronics govern everything from powertrain management to luxury cabin features, making proper battery health and wiring integrity vital.

**Warning Light and Knocking Sounds**
- **Symptoms:** Starting the vehicle may trigger a knocking sound accompanied by illuminated check engine, automatic brake, and adaptive cruise control warning lights.
- **Solution:** Run diagnostic scans to identify fault codes in the engine or driver assistance modules and replace failing sensors or wiring.

## Safety

### Air Bag System

Comprehensive occupant safety systems are standard, but sensitive seat sensors and wiring connections can occasionally trigger diagnostic trouble codes.

**Air Bag Warning and Sensor Faults**
- **Symptoms:** Air bag warning lights illuminate on the instrument cluster, or passenger seat capacity sensors fail to activate the passenger-side air bag.
- **Solution:** Diagnose the SRS module and seat occupancy sensors using an OBD scanner, and replace faulty sensor pads or wiring harnesses.

## Summary Verdict

Owning a used 2016 Mercedes-Benz E63 AMG S is an extraordinary driving experience, but it comes with the financial responsibility typical of high-end German performance cars. Buyers should prioritize finding an example with a documented dealer service history and budget for potential expenses related to electrical, steering, and brake system upkeep. A thorough pre-purchase inspection by an experienced technician is essential to catch hidden issues before buying.
