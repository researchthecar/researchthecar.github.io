# 2021 Mercedes-Benz E63 AMG S Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a 2021 Mercedes-Benz E63 AMG S, buyers are looking at a high-performance luxury machine powered by a handcrafted twin-turbo V8. While owners praise its incredible power and prestige, owner feedback submitted to federal safety regulators highlights potential problem areas ranging from powertrain hiccups and electrical anomalies to camera and brake concerns. Reviewing maintenance records and ensuring all software or mechanical updates have been performed can help mitigate these risks.

---

## Engine

### 4.0L Biturbo V8

The hand-built 4.0-liter twin-turbocharged V8 engine delivers immense horsepower and torque, offering breathtaking acceleration. However, complex engine management systems require diligent maintenance, and isolated owner reports note potential cylinder misfire concerns.

**Cylinder Misfire and Valve Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Illuminated check engine light, engine misfires, and potential sudden entry into limp mode resulting in a loss of motive power.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis by a qualified technician; affected components such as exhaust valves may require professional repair or replacement.

### Fuel Pump and EVAP System

The fuel delivery setup is designed to feed high-output demands reliably, though electronic control monitoring can occasionally throw diagnostic fault codes related to emissions.

**Evaporative Emission Control Faults**
- **Symptoms:** Diagnostic trouble codes indicating electrical faults or positive short circuits within the EVAP system, triggering warning lights.
- **Solution:** Inspect fuel system control modules, wiring harnesses, and related vapor emission components for faults.

## Transmission

### AMG Speedshift MCT 9-Speed Automatic

The 9-speed multi-clutch automatic transmission is engineered for lightning-fast gearchanges suited to track-capable performance. While generally robust, isolated owner reports point to severe mechanical failures requiring comprehensive service.

**Premature Transmission Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Sudden loss of drive capability, severe mechanical failure, or unexpected immobilization while driving at highway speeds.
- **Solution:** Requires professional mechanical evaluation and potentially a complete transmission overhaul or replacement.

## Brakes

### Electronic Parking Brake

High-performance braking hardware provides exceptional stopping power, though electronic parking brake components can occasionally experience unexpected locking behavior.

**Brake Locking and Seizing**
- **Symptoms:** Emergency or parking brake engages independently or locks up after sitting for several days, preventing vehicle movement.
- **Solution:** Inspect electronic brake actuators, calipers, and control software for anomalies.

## Electrical

### 48V Mild-Hybrid Electrical System

The advanced electrical architecture supports modern luxury features and powertrain integration. Some owners have experienced premature failures within the 48-volt system leading to sudden power loss.

**Battery Malfunction and Power Loss**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle becomes totally inoperable, instrument cluster displays erroneous crash or low-voltage messages, and the car requires towing.
- **Solution:** Diagnostic inspection of the 48-volt battery and associated charging modules, often requiring component replacement.

### Backup Camera and Sensors

Driver assistance and parking aids rely heavily on exterior cameras and sensors, which have occasionally suffered from intermittent display failures.

**Camera System Inoperative**
- **Symptoms:** Rearview and 360-degree camera feeds fail to display when shifting into reverse, accompanied by parking sensor malfunctions.
- **Solution:** Check multimedia software versions, camera wiring harnesses, and display modules for faults.
