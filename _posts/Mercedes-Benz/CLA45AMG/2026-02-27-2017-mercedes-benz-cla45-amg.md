# 2017 Mercedes-Benz CLA45 AMG Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used Mercedes-Benz CLA45 AMG, buyers can expect thrilling performance from its high-output turbocharged engine paired with an engaging dual-clutch transmission. However, prospective owners should thoroughly evaluate mechanical and body integrity, as owner reports highlight specific problem areas ranging from powertrain components to exterior trim. Reviewing service history and performing a pre-purchase inspection remain vital steps before finalizing a purchase.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4 (AMG Enhanced)

This hand-built 2.0-liter turbocharged inline-4 pushes out an impressive output of around 375 horsepower, offering intense acceleration. While potent, high-performance engines require meticulous maintenance and are sensitive to cooling and crankcase ventilation wear.

**Crankcase Ventilation Valve Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Unusual odors, illumination of the check engine light, or sluggish throttle response due to pressure regulation issues.
- **Solution:** Inspect and replace the faulty partial-load ventilation valve and associated crankcase hoses.

## Transmission

### 7-Speed AMG Speedshift DCT Dual-Clutch Automatic

Equipped with a quick-shifting 7-speed dual-clutch automatic transmission and all-wheel drive, this setup delivers rapid gear changes suited for sporty driving. Buyers should test all gear selections thoroughly for smooth operation and signs of driveline wear.

**Half Shaft and Transmission Engagement Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Sudden loss of drive capability, inability to engage drive or reverse gears, or complete transmission disengagement while driving.
- **Solution:** Inspect half shafts, CV joints, and transmission internal modules; replace damaged axle components or service the transmission assembly.

## Steering

### Steering Assembly

The responsive steering system provides sharp handling characteristics expected from an AMG sport sedan. Prospective buyers should check for smooth, binding-free rotation from lock to lock.

**Steering Shaft Defects and Binding**
- **Symptoms:** Unusual noises from the steering column or stiffness/locking sensations while attempting to steer.
- **Solution:** Have an authorized dealer inspect the steering shaft and address any outstanding mechanical recalls or component wear.

## Electrical

### Instrument Cluster and Electrical System

Modern electronics govern nearly every function of the vehicle, from infotainment to vital driver readouts. Ensuring stable battery voltage and undamaged wiring harnesses is critical for trouble-free operation.

**Instrument Cluster Display Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The dashboard display intermittently shows incorrect information or goes completely blank while the vehicle is in motion.
- **Solution:** Inspect electrical harnesses, module connections, and software versions; replace faulty cluster units if software resets fail.

## Exterior

### Panoramic Sunroof

The exterior features sleek body lines and an available panoramic glass roof, which adds an open-air feel to the cabin. However, buyers should closely inspect the sunroof assembly for proper sealing and structural retention.

**Sunroof Detachment or Shattering**
- **Symptoms:** Loud popping noises followed by the glass roof panel detaching completely or shattering while driving at highway speeds.
- **Solution:** Inspect sunroof bonding, seals, and frame integrity; replace glass panels using updated manufacturer specifications if necessary.

## Safety

### Air Bag System

Standard safety equipment includes a comprehensive airbag suite designed to protect occupants in a collision. Confirming that all safety systems and sensors are fully operational is essential.

**Airbag Component Unavailability and Faults**
- **Symptoms:** Warning lights on the dashboard or prolonged delays in sourcing replacement airbag components during repairs.
- **Solution:** Verify the vehicle's airbag readiness status and check VIN history for unresolved safety campaigns.
