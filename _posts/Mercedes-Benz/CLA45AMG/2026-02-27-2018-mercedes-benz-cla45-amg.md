# 2018 Mercedes-Benz CLA45 AMG Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2018 Mercedes-Benz CLA45 AMG offers high-performance luxury in a compact four-door coupe style, driven by a potent hand-built turbocharged engine and an advanced all-wheel-drive system. While offering thrilling dynamics, prospective buyers should review ownership records carefully. Common owner-reported concerns center around minor electrical quirks, interior water leaks, and steering-related noises.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4

A hand-built 2.0-liter turbocharged inline-4 engine producing roughly 375 horsepower and 350 lb-ft of torque. It delivers explosive acceleration for its size, though premium maintenance and attentive upkeep are vital for long-term health.

**Check Engine Light / Emissions Component Fault**
- **Symptoms:** Illumination of the check engine light, often related to specific emission control sensors or PCV system valves.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified mechanic pull diagnostic trouble codes and replace the faulty valve or sensor as needed.

## Transmission

### 7-Speed Dual-Clutch Automatic

A 7-speed dual-clutch automatic transmission paired with 4MATIC all-wheel drive, delivering lightning-fast gear changes suited to high-performance driving.

## Steering

### Steering Column and Clock Spring

Manages steering feel and houses critical electronic controls via the clock spring. Failures can disable steering-mounted switches.

**Clock Spring Failure and Steering Noises**
- **Symptoms:** Abnormal clicking or ticking sounds when turning the steering wheel, accompanied by inoperative steering wheel control buttons and potential airbag warning lights.
- **Solution:** Replace the steering column clock spring assembly to restore electrical connectivity and eliminate noise.

## Electrical

### Instrument Cluster and Dashboard

Handles displays, cabin electronics, and driver assistance modules. Owners have occasionally reported intermittent gauge cluster failures.

**Inaccurate Gauge Readings and Dashboard Flickering**
- **Symptoms:** Gauges provide erratic information, and the entire dashboard display may intermittently turn on and off while driving.
- **Solution:** Inspect instrument cluster wiring and software modules; software flashes or hardware replacement may be necessary.

## Exterior

### Roof Panel and Sunroof

Encompasses exterior glass, panels, and sunroof mechanisms. Weatherstrip and roof panel bonding require inspection to prevent leaks.

**Roof Panel Separation and Water Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Water enters the cabin near the overhead console during car washes or rain, and in severe cases, exterior roof trim panels can loosen or detach.
- **Solution:** Inspect roof panel seals and fasteners, and re-secure or replace damaged adhesive and exterior panels.

## Interior

### Cabin Carpeting and Seating

Covers cabin upholstery, seat latches, and floor integrity. Owners have noted rare instances of water accumulation inside the passenger compartment.

**Water Intrusion and Floor Accumulation**
- **Symptoms:** Standing water pooling on rear footwells and front floorboards due to compromised exterior seals or drainage channels.
- **Solution:** Clear blocked sunroof or body drain tubes and inspect door and window weatherstripping for proper sealing.

## Summary Verdict

The 2018 Mercedes-Benz CLA45 AMG is an exciting and capable sport sedan that rewards enthusiastic drivers, but it requires diligent pre-purchase inspection. Buyers should thoroughly check for interior water leaks around the roof panels, verify that all steering wheel controls and dashboard gauges operate properly, and confirm a solid maintenance history.
