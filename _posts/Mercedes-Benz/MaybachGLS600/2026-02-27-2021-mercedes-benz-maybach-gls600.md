# 2021 Mercedes-Benz Maybach GLS600 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2021 Mercedes-Benz Maybach GLS600 represents the pinnacle of ultra-luxury motoring, blending imposing presence with effortless performance. For used-car buyers entering the ultra-luxury SUV market, understanding its sophisticated powertrain and complex electronic architecture is key. While owner-reported complaints for this ultra-exclusive model are minimal, careful inspection of its advanced systems is always recommended.

---

## Engine

### 4.0L Biturbo V8 Mild Hybrid (M179)

Equipped with a potent 4.0-liter twin-turbocharged V8 engine featuring EQ Boost mild-hybrid technology, producing approximately 550 horsepower and 538 lb-ft of torque. This powerhouse delivers ultra-smooth, authoritative acceleration fitting for an ultra-luxury flagship, though prospective buyers should maintain rigorous adherence to scheduled maintenance intervals.

## Transmission

### 9G-TRONIC 9-Speed Automatic Transmission

Utilizes a 9-speed automatic transmission paired with standard 4MATIC all-wheel drive, engineered for seamless gear shifts and refined cruising. It complements the V8 powerplant by maintaining quiet, low-rpm highway cruising while remaining instantly responsive when power is demanded.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V354000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=Maybach+GLS600&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The MBUX multimedia system software may cause the display to remain black, or reboot after startup. This could result in the rearview camera image not displaying properly.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 20V089000: Electrical System: Instrument Cluster/Panel](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=Maybach+GLS600&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 101, "Control and Displays." If the interior switches are not illuminated, they may be more difficult to find and operate, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** MBUSA will notify owners, and dealers will update the instrument cluster software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V231000: Electrical System:Wiring:Interior/Under Dash](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=Maybach+GLS600&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** A ground cable bolt for the 48V ground connection under the front passenger seat may not be secured properly, which can cause the electrical cable to overheat. An overheated electrical cable can increase the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and tighten the ground cable bolt, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V680000: Structure:Body](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=Maybach+GLS600&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** Detached trim bars may cause a road hazard and increase the risk of a crash or injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and secure the rear window trim bars, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V177000: Visibility:Defroster/Defogger/Hvac System](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=Maybach+GLS600&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** Water intrusion into the foot wells may cause corrosion and/or short circuits to electrical components, increasing the risk of fire. In addition, the emergency call (eCall) feature may be disabled and the doors may not unlock, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and reinstall the air conditioner drain hoses, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.
