# 2020 Mercedes-Benz CLS450 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2020 Mercedes-Benz CLS450 blends striking four-door coupe styling with luxurious comfort and sophisticated powertrain technology. While owners generally praise its refined road manners and upscale interior, shopping for a used model requires careful attention to emerging owner-reported complaints regarding complex electronics and vehicle systems. As with any modern luxury vehicle, checking service history and verifying that open campaigns have been completed remains essential.

---

## Engine

### 3.0L Turbocharged Inline-6 (M256) with EQ Boost

The 2020 CLS450 features a 3.0-liter turbocharged inline-six engine producing approximately 362 horsepower and 369 lb-ft of torque, integrated with a 48-volt mild-hybrid system. This powerplant delivers smooth, effortless acceleration and improved efficiency, though its complex electrical architecture requires attentive maintenance.

**48V Electrical System Shut Down**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers report that the vehicle's 48-volt mild-hybrid system can cause the engine to shut down unexpectedly, particularly in high-heat environments.
- **Solution:** Have a certified technician inspect the 48V battery and related electrical components for software updates or hardware replacements.

## Transmission

### 9G-TRONIC 9-Speed Automatic Transmission

Power is sent to the rear wheels or 4MATIC all-wheel-drive system via a 9-speed automatic transmission known for smooth and prompt gear changes. It generally operates reliably when routine fluid service intervals are maintained.

## Suspension

### Wheels and Tires

The suspension balances sporty handling with luxury comfort, though large wheels and low-profile tires are susceptible to road hazard damage.

**Premature Tire Wear and Sidewall Bubbles**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have experienced premature tread wear, bald spots, and sidewall bubbles on factory tire setups during normal driving conditions.
- **Solution:** Replace worn tires with quality replacements and regularly check tire pressures and wheel alignment.

## Electrical

### Backup Camera System

Advanced driver assistance features provide good peace of mind, though motorized exterior sensors and cameras should be tested during a pre-purchase inspection.

**Sticking Rearview Camera**
- **Symptoms:** The motorized rearview camera housing can stick in the open position, resulting in a blank or black screen on the infotainment display.
- **Solution:** Inspect the camera mechanism and replace faulty actuator components or wiring as needed.

## Summary Verdict

The 2020 Mercedes-Benz CLS450 is a sophisticated luxury cruiser offering strong performance and standout design. Prospective buyers should thoroughly inspect the vehicle's electrical systems, mild-hybrid components, and tires before purchase.
