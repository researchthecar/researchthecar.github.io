# 2019 Mercedes-Benz CLS450 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used luxury fastback like the 2019 Mercedes-Benz CLS-Class, prospective buyers will find a striking design paired with sophisticated powertrain options. However, owner reports submitted to federal safety regulators highlight a few recurring trouble spots, particularly concerning wheel durability, tire wear, and complex electrical systems. Reviewing these common owner complaints can help you perform a more targeted pre-purchase inspection.

---

## Engine

### 3.0L Turbo Inline-6 (CLS 450)

The 3.0-liter turbocharged inline-6 engine powers the CLS 450 with approximately 362 horsepower and 369 lb-ft of torque, integrating a 48-volt mild-hybrid system. It delivers smooth, responsive performance, though buyers should watch out for occasional electrical and battery-related complexities.

**48V Battery Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have reported receiving a yellow or red "48V Battery malfunction" warning on the dashboard, which can precede a total loss of motive power or electrical failure.
- **Solution:** Have the 48-volt electrical system and hybrid starter-generator inspected by a qualified technician; software updates or battery replacement may be required.

**Electrical Wiring Harness Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have experienced sudden engine shaking, abnormal noises, and check engine lights due to compromised wiring harnesses.
- **Solution:** Inspect engine bay wiring harnesses for wear, damage, or chafing, and replace damaged harnesses if necessary.

### 3.0L Turbocharged Inline-6 AMG (CLS 53)

The high-output 3.0-liter turbocharged inline-6 engine powers the AMG CLS 53 with roughly 429 horsepower and 384 lb-ft of torque. It provides exhilarating acceleration and robust performance, though it shares the advanced 48-volt electrical architecture of its stablemates.

## Transmission

### 9-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 9-speed automatic transmission routes power smoothly across rear-wheel and 4MATIC all-wheel-drive configurations, generally operating without widespread mechanical complaints.

## Suspension

### Wheels and Rims

The suspension and wheel setup provides responsive handling and a planted ride, but low-profile tire packages on large alloy wheels make the vehicle vulnerable to rim damage on rough roads.

**Cracked Wheels and Repeated Tire Blowouts**
- **Symptoms:** Owners frequently report low tire pressure warning lights, visible rim cracks, and sudden tire blowouts after driving over minor road bumps or potholes.
- **Solution:** Inspect all alloy wheels closely for structural cracks, especially on vehicles equipped with low-profile tires, and replace damaged rims.

## Steering

### Steering and Front Suspension Linkage

Steering is precise and weighted well for a luxury sedan, though worn front suspension components can degrade tracking stability.

**Loose Steering and Side-to-Side Movement**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers note a disturbing side-to-side movement or a sensation that the wheels and lug nuts are loose when driving over uneven road surfaces.
- **Solution:** Inspect front-end suspension components, control arms, and steering linkages for excessive wear or loose fasteners.

## Electrical

### Starter Battery and Electronics

Extensive electronics manage everything from cabin comfort to powertrain controls, making a healthy electrical charging system essential for trouble-free operation.

**Premature Starter Battery Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle dashboards and companion mobile applications display battery warnings, and original starter batteries may fail unexpectedly shortly after purchase.
- **Solution:** Test the auxiliary and starter batteries during a pre-purchase inspection and replace weak batteries with factory-spec units.

### Backup Camera and Display

Advanced safety features and driver-assistance electronics offer strong protection when fully operational, but software glitches can occasionally affect visibility displays.

**Black Rearview Camera Display**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers placing the vehicle in reverse observe a blank black screen instead of the rearview camera image.
- **Solution:** Have the infotainment software and camera wiring harness checked by a dealer or specialist to identify software glitches or hardware failure.

## Summary Verdict

The 2019 Mercedes-Benz CLS-Class offers sleek styling and refined performance, but used-car buyers should perform a thorough inspection prior to purchase. Pay close attention to the condition of the alloy wheels and low-profile tires, test all electrical features including the 48-volt hybrid system, and ensure suspension components are tight. Addressing these areas ahead of time will help secure a more reliable luxury ownership experience.
