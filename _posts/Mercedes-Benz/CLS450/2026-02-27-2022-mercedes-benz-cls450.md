# 2022 Mercedes-Benz CLS450 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2022 Mercedes-Benz CLS450 blends striking coupe-like styling with refined luxury and mild-hybrid performance. For used car buyers, exploring this sophisticated luxury vehicle means paying close attention to early owner reports concerning electrical systems, wheel durability, and safety restraint functionality.

---

## Engine

### 3.0L Turbocharged Inline-6 (EQ Boost)

This 3.0-liter turbocharged inline-6 engine utilizes a 48-volt mild-hybrid EQ Boost system, generating approximately 362 horsepower and 369 lb-ft of torque. It delivers exceptionally smooth power delivery and strong efficiency, though shoppers should verify the health of the complex 48-volt electrical architecture.

### 48-Volt Charging System

The advanced 48-volt electrical grid supports the mild-hybrid system and heavy electrical accessories, but isolated owner reports indicate potential charging faults.

**48V Charging System Failure**
- **Symptoms:** A dashboard warning appears regarding the 48-volt charging system, potentially causing climate control failure and reduced engine power.
- **Solution:** Have a certified technician diagnose the 48-volt battery, starter-generator, and associated software modules.

## Transmission

### 9-Speed Automatic Transmission

Paired exclusively with 4MATIC all-wheel drive, the 9-speed automatic transmission offers seamless gear changes and optimizes highway efficiency. It generally proves dependable, provided regular fluid maintenance schedules have been followed by previous owners.

## Suspension

### Wheels and Rims

Low-profile tires mounted on large alloy wheels enhance handling and appearance but leave the rims vulnerable to impact damage from severe road hazards.

**Cracked Wheel Rim**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may experience sudden tire pressure loss or a tire blowout while driving due to a fractured wheel rim.
- **Solution:** Inspect alloy wheels thoroughly for structural damage or hairline cracks, and replace any compromised rims.

## Safety

### Air Bag Deployment

Advanced restraint systems are standard, though isolated consumer complaints suggest rare instances of sensor or deployment failures during impacts.

**Non-Deployment in Frontal Impact**
- **Symptoms:** Frontal airbags may fail to deploy during a collision, increasing the risk of occupant injury.
- **Solution:** Verify that all airbag sensors, wiring harnesses, and restraint control modules are fully operational via professional inspection.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V483000: Seat Belts:Rear/Other:Retractor](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=CLS450&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 208, "Occupant Crash Protection." An unsecured child restraint system can increase the risk of injury during a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect, and replace as necessary, the rear seat belt assemblies, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V189000: Steering: Steering Wheel/Handle Bar](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=CLS450&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** Deactivated hands-off detection will prevent DISTRONIC system features from functioning, including warnings to grab the steering wheel and calling first responders as part of the Active Emergency Stop Assist, which can increase the risk of a crash or injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the hand detection control unit software for the DISTRONIC system, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V733000: Electrical System:12V/24V/48V Battery](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=CLS450&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** The 12-Volt battery in the trunk may not be properly secured, which, in the event of a crash, can cause electrical connection failure, disabling various safety features including, emergency call (eCall) system, electric seat adjustment, hazard warning lights, and automatic door locks. The inability to operate safety features (eCall system, hazard lights.
- **What to do:** Dealers will secure the 12V battery, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V100000: Electrical System:Wiring](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=CLS450&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** The 48-volt ground cable connection located in the engine compartment may not be tightened properly and may result in the ground cable overheating. An overheated ground cable increases the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the 48-volt ground cable connection and repair it as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2022 Mercedes-Benz CLS450 offers a compelling mix of performance, technology, and luxury styling. While powertrain performance is generally praised, prospective buyers should conduct a thorough pre-purchase inspection focusing on the 48-volt electrical system, alloy wheel condition, and overall vehicle history.
