The 2005 Mercedes-Benz CLS500 is a mid-size luxury coupe that was produced from 2004 to 2010. It is the first generation of the CLS-Class and is available with rear- or all-wheel drive. Its engine is a naturally aspirated 5.0-liter V8 that produces 302 horsepower and 339 lb-ft of torque. It is paired to a 7-speed automatic transmission. 

The CLS500 has received praise for its sleek design, comfortable interior, and powerful engine. However, it has also been criticized for its high price and lack of practicality. 

**Engine**

* **5.0L V8**
    * **Intake Manifold**
        * Description/Symptoms: The intake manifold can develop cracks, causing vacuum leaks. This can lead to a loss of power, rough idling, and increased fuel consumption.
        * Solution: The intake manifold must be replaced.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $1,500+
    * **Camshaft Adjusters**
        * Description/Symptoms: The camshaft adjusters can fail, causing the camshaft timing to be off. This can lead to a loss of power, rough idling, and increased fuel consumption.
        * Solution: The camshaft adjusters must be replaced.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix:$500 - $1,000+
    * **Valve Stem Seals**
        * * Description/Symptoms:** The valve stem seals weaken and allow oil to leak into the combustion chamber. Oil consumption will increase and the engine may smoke.
        * Solution:* Replace the valve stem seals.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix:* $1,000 - $1,500+

**Transmission**

* **7-Speed Automatic**
    * **Valve Body**
        * Description/Symptoms: The valve body can fail, causing the transmission to shift erratically or not at all.
        * Solution: The valve body must be replaced.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,000
    * **Torque Converter**
        * Description/Symptoms: The torque converter can fail, causing the transmission to shudder or slip.
        * Solution: The torque converter must be replaced.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $1,500+
    * **Transmission Control Module**
        * Description/Symptoms: The transmission control module can fail, causing the transmission to shift erratically or not at all.
        * Solution: The transmission control module must be replaced.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000

**Suspension**

* **Front Suspension**
    * **Control Arms**
        * Description/Symptoms: The control arms can wear out, causing the suspension to become loose and the car to handle poorly.
        * Solution: The control arms must be replaced.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
* **Rear Suspension**
    * **Springs**
        * Description/Symptoms: The springs can sag over time, causing the car to ride lower and handle poorly.
        * Solution: The springs must be replaced.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * **Shock Absorbers**
        * Description/Symptoms: The shock absorbers can wear out, causing the suspension to become bouncy and the car to handle poorly.
        * Solution: The shock absorbers must be replaced.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+

**Electrical**

* **Battery**
    * **Premature Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: The battery can fail prematurely, leaving the driver stranded.
        * Solution: The battery must be replaced.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
* **Alternator**
    * **Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: The alternator can fail, causing the battery to not charge and the car to stall.
        * Solution: The alternator must be replaced.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
* **Starter**
    * **Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: The starter can fail, preventing the engine from starting.
        * Solution: The starter must be replaced.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+

**Exterior**

* **Headlights**
    * ** Condensation**
        * Description/Symptoms: Condensation can build up inside the headlights, causing them to fog up and become less effective.
        * Solution: The headlights must be sealed or replaced.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+

**Interior**

* **Navigation System**
    * **Malfunction**
        * Description/Symptoms: The navigation system can malfunction, causing it to give inaccurate directions or not work at all.
        * Solution: The navigation system must be repaired or replaced.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
* **Heater/AC**
    * **Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: The heater or AC can fail, causing the car to become uncomfortable.
        * Solution: The heater or AC system must be repaired or replaced.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+


**Summary**

The 2005 Mercedes-Benz CLS500 is a stylish and powerful car that can be expensive to maintain. The most common issues are with the engine, transmission, and suspension. The cost of repairs can vary depending on the severity of the issue. Overall, the CLS500 is a reliable car, but it is important to be aware of the potential issues before purchasing one.
