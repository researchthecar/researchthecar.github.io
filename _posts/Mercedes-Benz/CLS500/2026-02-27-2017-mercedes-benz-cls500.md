# 2017 Mercedes-Benz CLS500 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used Mercedes-Benz CLS500, prospective buyers will find a stylish and capable luxury cruiser with robust powertrain engineering. While owner feedback highlights a couple of notable suspension-related concerns, reviewing known mechanical options and performing a thorough pre-purchase inspection can help ensure a confident buying experience.

---

## Engine

### 4.7L Twin-Turbocharged V8

This 4.7-liter twin-turbocharged V8 engine produces approximately 402 horsepower and 443 lb-ft of torque, delivering swift acceleration. Regular maintenance and timely oil changes are vital for long-term reliability in this high-performance luxury application.

## Transmission

### 9-Speed Automatic Transmission

Equipped with a 9-speed automatic transmission paired with rear-wheel drive or 4MATIC all-wheel drive, this gearbox provides smooth and efficient shifting. Routine fluid services help maintain optimal performance and longevity.

## Suspension

### Wheels

The wheel assemblies require close inspection, as owner reports point to potential durability concerns with certain factory rims.

**Cracked Wheels and Tire Blowouts**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have reported unexpected wheel rim cracking without noticeable impacts, leading to repeated tire blowouts and potential driving hazards.
- **Solution:** Inspect all wheels thoroughly for hairline cracks or structural damage, and replace any compromised rims immediately.

The Mercedes-Benz CLS500 offers impressive V8 performance and a luxurious cabin, making it an appealing used luxury vehicle. Buyers should pay close attention to wheel and tire condition during pre-purchase inspections to avoid unexpected maintenance issues.
