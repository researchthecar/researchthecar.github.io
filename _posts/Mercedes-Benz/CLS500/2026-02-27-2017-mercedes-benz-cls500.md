# 2017 Mercedes-Benz CLS500

### Overview
The 2017 Mercedes-Benz CLS500, part of the C218 generation, is a luxury grand tourer known for its potent performance and sophisticated design. While generally well-regarded for its build quality and driving dynamics, like any vehicle, it has specific areas where owners have reported issues. This analysis focuses on common technical reliability concerns beyond routine maintenance, examining the powertrain, suspension, electrical systems, and other key areas. Understanding these potential weak points can help prospective buyers and current owners make informed decisions regarding maintenance and potential repairs.

---


## Engine

### 4.7L Twin-Turbo V8 (M278)
**Timing Chain Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Rattling noise from the front of the engine, especially on startup; check engine light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of timing chain, tensioners, and guides.
- **Cost:** $1,500 - $3,000

**Oil Leaks (Valve Covers/Oil Pan)**
- **Symptoms:** Visible oil residue around the engine; burning oil smell.
- **Solution:** Replacement of valve cover gaskets or oil pan gasket.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1,200


## Transmission

### 7G-TRONIC PLUS (7-Speed Automatic)
**Transmission Fluid Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Rough shifting, slipping gears, warning lights, low transmission fluid level.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of seals, transmission cooler lines, or valve body.
- **Cost:** $800 - $2,500

**Solenoid Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** Harsh shifting, delayed engagement, transmission fault codes.
- **Solution:** Replacement of faulty solenoids within the valve body.
- **Cost:** $600 - $1,500


## Suspension

### AIRMATIC Air Suspension
**Air Strut Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle sagging on one or more corners, 'air suspension malfunction' warning message.
- **Solution:** Replacement of leaking air struts or air springs.
- **Cost:** $700 - $1,500 per corner

**Air Compressor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Inability to raise or lower the vehicle, constant running of the compressor, warning messages.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the air suspension compressor.
- **Cost:** $600 - $1,200


## Electrical

### Infotainment System (COMAND)
**Screen Freezing/Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** Infotainment screen becomes unresponsive, freezes, or displays errors.
- **Solution:** Software updates or replacement of the COMAND unit.
- **Cost:** $500 - $2,000


### Battery Drain
**Parasitic Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Battery dies unexpectedly, car won't start, electrical components malfunction.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and repair of faulty modules or circuits causing excessive drain.
- **Cost:** $300 - $1,000

### Summary Verdict
The 2017 Mercedes-Benz CLS500 offers a compelling blend of performance and luxury. While its core powertrain is robust, potential owners should be aware of common issues such as timing chain wear and oil leaks on the M278 engine. The AIRMATIC suspension, while providing excellent comfort, can be a source of costly repairs due to leaks or compressor failures. Electrical gremlins, particularly with the COMAND infotainment system and parasitic battery drain, are also noted. Transmission issues, though less frequent, can involve leaks or solenoid problems. Overall, the CLS500 is a reliable luxury vehicle, but proactive maintenance and awareness of these specific potential issues are recommended for long-term ownership satisfaction.
