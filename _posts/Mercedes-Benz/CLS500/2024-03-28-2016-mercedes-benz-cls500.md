**Introduction:**
The 2016 Mercedes-Benz CLS500 is a luxury sedan powered by a 4.7L V8 engine, producing 402 horsepower and 443 lb-ft of torque. It comes with a 7-speed automatic transmission, offering a smooth and responsive driving experience. The CLS500 boasts a spacious cabin, advanced safety features, and an elegant exterior design. While it is a popular choice for those seeking a luxurious and sporty sedan, there have been some issues reported by owners.

**Engine**
- **4.7 L V8**
	- **Intake Valve Sticking (2016-2017)**
		- Description/Symptoms: Engine hesitation, loss of power, check engine light
		- Solution: Replace intake valves and related components
		- Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,000+
		- Recalls: None Found
- **Crankshaft Position Sensor Failure**
		- Description/Symptoms: Engine stalling, difficulty starting, check engine light
		- Solution: Replace crankshaft position sensor
		- Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+
		- Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**
- **7-Speed Automatic**
	- **Valve Body Failure**
		- Description/Symptoms: Harsh shifting, slipping, loss of gears, check engine light
		- Solution: Replace valve body
		- Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
		- Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**
- **Airmatic Suspension Leak**
		- Description/Symptoms: Sagging suspension, reduced ride height, air compressor running excessively
		- Solution: Replace air bags or seals, repair air lines
		- Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
		- Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**
- **Battery Discharge**
		- Description/Symptoms: Battery drains overnight, difficulty starting
		- Solution: Test and replace battery, check for parasitic drain
		- Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
		- Recalls: None Found
- **Headlight Malfunction**
		- Description/Symptoms: Headlights flicker, dim, or turn off prematurely
		- Solution: Replace headlight bulbs or ballast
		- Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+
		- Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**
- **Sunroof Drain Clogs**
		- Description/Symptoms: Water leaking into cabin from sunroof, mold growth
		- Solution: Clean clogged drains
		- Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
		- Recalls: None Found

**Interior**
- **Dashboard Rattling**
		- Description/Symptoms: Rattling noise from dashboard, especially on uneven surfaces
		- Solution: Tighten loose components, apply sound dampening material
		- Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
		- Recalls: None Found
- **Climate Control Malfunction**
		- Description/Symptoms: A/C not blowing cold air, defroster not working, temperature fluctuations
		- Solution: Repair or replace faulty climate control components
		- Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,200+
		- Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**
The 2016 Mercedes-Benz CLS500 offers a blend of luxury and performance but has some reported issues that can impact reliability and ownership experience. While some issues are relatively minor and inexpensive to fix, others can be more severe and costly. It is advisable to consider these potential issues before purchasing a used CLS500.
