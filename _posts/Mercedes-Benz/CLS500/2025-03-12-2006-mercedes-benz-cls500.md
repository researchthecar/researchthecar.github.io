The 2006 Mercedes-Benz CLS500 is a luxury four-door coupe that blends sleek styling with a comfortable ride. The CLS500 comes equipped with a 5.0L V8 engine producing 302 horsepower and 339 lb-ft of torque. This engine is paired with a 7-speed automatic transmission (7G-Tronic). Some owners appreciate its distinctive design and powerful engine, while others dislike its high maintenance costs and occasional electrical issues. Other engine options for the 2006 CLS include the CLS55 AMG (5.5L Supercharged V8).

**Engine**

**5.0L V8 (M113)**

*   **Oil Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks are common, often stemming from the valve cover gaskets, oil filter housing gasket, or the front crankshaft seal. Symptoms include visible oil leaks on the engine, oil spots on the ground, and a burning oil smell.
    *   Solution: Replacing the affected gaskets or seals.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+ depending on the location and severity of the leak.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Coolant Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaks can occur from various points including the radiator, hoses, or water pump. Symptoms may include low coolant levels, overheating, and visible coolant puddles.
    *   Solution: Replacing the leaking component such as hoses, water pump, or radiator.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $1000+ depending on the source of the leak.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**7-Speed Automatic (7G-Tronic) (722.9)**

*   **Valve Body Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The 722.9 transmission can suffer from valve body issues, leading to harsh or erratic shifting, delayed engagement, or getting stuck in gear.
    *   Solution: Replacing or rebuilding the valve body.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $2000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Torque Converter Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Torque converter failure can manifest as shuddering at low speeds, transmission slipping, or complete loss of drive.
    *   Solution: Replacing the torque converter.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1500 - $3000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Airmatic Suspension Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The Airmatic air suspension system is prone to failures, including air leaks from the struts or air lines, compressor failure, and ride height control issues. Symptoms include a sagging vehicle, ride height malfunctions, and a noisy or non-functioning air compressor.
    *   Solution: Replacing the leaking struts, air lines, or air compressor. Sometimes, the entire system needs an overhaul.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2000+ per strut; Compressor replacement $500 - $1500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **EIS/ESL (Electronic Ignition Switch/Electronic Steering Lock) Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The EIS/ESL can fail, preventing the car from starting or unlocking the steering wheel. This is a common issue in this era of Mercedes-Benz vehicles.
    *   Solution: Replacing or repairing the EIS/ESL module. Programming is often required.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $2000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **SAM (Signal Acquisition Module) Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: SAM modules control various electrical functions and can fail, leading to issues with lighting, wipers, or other electronic systems.
    *   Solution: Replacing the faulty SAM module. Programming is often required.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Rust**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rust can develop on the fenders, door panels, and around the wheel wells, particularly in areas with harsh winters or salty road conditions.
    *   Solution: Repairing the affected areas through bodywork and repainting.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $3000+ depending on the severity and location.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Sunroof Malfunctions**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The sunroof mechanism can fail, causing the sunroof to become stuck, noisy, or leak.
    *   Solution: Repairing or replacing the sunroof mechanism.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2006 Mercedes-Benz CLS500 is a stylish vehicle that can be prone to certain issues, particularly related to its Airmatic suspension, electrical system, and transmission. Engine oil leaks are common but often manageable. The Airmatic suspension and electrical issues can be costly to repair, making it important to inspect these systems carefully before purchasing. Overall, potential buyers should be aware of the potential maintenance costs associated with owning this vehicle.
