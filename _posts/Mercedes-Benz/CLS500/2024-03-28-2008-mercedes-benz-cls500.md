**Introduction:**

The 2008 Mercedes-Benz CLS500 is a mid-size luxury sedan known for its sleek design, comfortable interior, and powerful performance. It's powered by a 5.5-liter V8 engine producing 382 horsepower and 391 lb-ft of torque, paired with a 7-speed automatic transmission. Other engine and transmission options available include a 3.5-liter V6 with a 5-speed automatic and a 6.2-liter V8 with a 5-speed automatic.

Owners often praise the CLS500's refined ride, stylish exterior, and high-quality interior. However, some have encountered various issues with the vehicle over the years.

**Engine**

* **3.5L V6**
  * **Intake Manifold Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, rough idle, illuminated check engine light
    * Solution: Replace intake manifold
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500
    * Recalls: None Found
* **5.5L V8**
  * **Timing Chain Tensioner Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Noisy engine, loss of power, illuminated check engine light
    * Solution: Replace timing chain tensioner and related components
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,500
    * Recalls: None Found
* **6.2L V8**
  * **Valve Stem Seal Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Excessive oil consumption, blue smoke from exhaust
    * Solution: Replace valve stem seals
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $5,000 - $7,500
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

* **7-Speed Automatic**
  * **Shift Solenoid Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Hard shifting, delayed engagement, slipping gears
    * Solution: Replace shift solenoid
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
    * Recalls: None Found
* **5-Speed Automatic**
  * **Transmission Slipping:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Inability to engage gears, loss of power under acceleration
    * Solution: Rebuild or replace transmission
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $3,000 - $5,000
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Airmatic Suspension Failure:**
  * Description/Symptoms: Uneven ride height, spongy handling, illuminated suspension warning light
  * Solution: Replace air struts or compressor
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500
  * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **SAM Module Failure:**
  * Description/Symptoms: Intermittent electrical issues, loss of power, illuminated warning lights
  * Solution: Replace SAM module
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
  * Recalls: None Found
* **Central Command Module Failure:**
  * Description/Symptoms: Loss of functionality for various electronic systems, including climate control and infotainment
  * Solution: Replace central command module
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $1,500
  * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Navigation System Malfunction:**
  * Description/Symptoms: Inability to navigate, frozen screen, blackouts
  * Solution: Update or replace navigation system
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500
  * Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

The 2008 Mercedes-Benz CLS500 has a range of issues, some of which can be costly to repair. Engine problems, particularly with the V6 and V8 options, are the most common and expensive to resolve. Transmission issues, suspension problems, and electrical failures are also present and can impact the vehicle's reliability and performance. The severity of these issues can vary, with some being minor annoyances and others requiring major repairs. Owners should be aware of these potential issues before purchasing or owning a CLS500 and budget accordingly.
