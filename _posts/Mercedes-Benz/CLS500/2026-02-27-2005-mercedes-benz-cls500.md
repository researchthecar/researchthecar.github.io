# 2005 Mercedes-Benz CLS500

### Overview
The 2005 Mercedes-Benz CLS500, part of the C219 generation, is a luxury grand tourer known for its distinctive styling and powerful performance. Powered by a robust 5.0-liter V8 engine, it offers a blend of comfort and dynamic driving. While generally well-regarded for its build quality and luxury appointments, like any vehicle of this age and complexity, it can experience specific issues. Owners should be aware of potential concerns related to the air suspension system, electronic components, and the aging of certain interior and exterior elements. Regular maintenance is crucial to mitigate these potential problems and ensure the longevity of this sophisticated German automobile.

---


## Engine

### 5.0L V8 (M113)
**Oil Cooler Seals Leak**
- **Symptoms:** Oil in coolant reservoir, coolant loss, potential overheating.
- **Solution:** Replacement of oil cooler seals.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600

**Intake Manifold Gasket Leak**
- **Symptoms:** Vacuum leaks, rough idle, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of intake manifold gaskets.
- **Cost:** $400 - $700


## Transmission

### 5-Speed Automatic (722.6)
**Conductor Plate Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Harsh shifting, transmission slipping, failure to engage gears, 'limp mode'.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the transmission conductor plate and fluid service.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1500

**Transmission Fluid Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Leaking fluid from the transmission pan or cooler lines, low fluid level.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of leaking seals, pan gasket, or cooler lines.
- **Cost:** $300 - $700


## Suspension

### Air Suspension (ABC)
**Hydraulic Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle sagging, 'ABC system fault' warning on the dashboard, bouncy ride.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of leaking hoses, struts, or pump.
- **Cost:** $1000 - $3000+

**Pump Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of hydraulic pressure, vehicle sagging, loud pump noise.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the Active Body Control (ABC) pump.
- **Cost:** $700 - $1200


## Electrical

### General Electrical System
**SAM Unit Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Various warning lights, malfunctioning lights (headlights, taillights), power window issues.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of the Signal Acquisition Module (SAM).
- **Cost:** $500 - $1000

**Window Regulator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Power windows not working, window stuck in position.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the window regulator mechanism.
- **Cost:** $300 - $500


## Exterior

### Body and Trim
**Headlight Condensation**
- **Symptoms:** Moisture or fogging inside headlight assemblies.
- **Solution:** Sealing or replacement of headlight units.
- **Cost:** $200 - $600


## Interior

### Interior Components
**Headliner Sagging**
- **Symptoms:** Fabric on the headliner detaches and sags.
- **Solution:** Re-gluing or replacement of the headliner.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600

**Seat Control Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Power seat adjustments not working, memory seat issues.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of seat control modules or motors.
- **Cost:** $400 - $800


--- 
### Summary Verdict
The 2005 Mercedes-Benz CLS500 is a luxurious and powerful vehicle, but its advanced systems, particularly the air suspension and electronics, can be sources of costly repairs as the car ages. Engine and transmission reliability are generally good, but specific components like the conductor plate and oil cooler seals are known weak points. Prospective buyers should be prepared for potential expenses related to these areas, as well as the possibility of interior and exterior trim issues common in vehicles of this vintage. Thorough pre-purchase inspection and a budget for maintenance are highly recommended.
