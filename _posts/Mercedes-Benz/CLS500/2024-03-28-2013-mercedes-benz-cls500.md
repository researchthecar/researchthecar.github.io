**Introduction:**

The 2013 Mercedes-Benz CLS500 is a luxurious and powerful four-door coupe powered by a 4.7-liter V8 engine that produces 402 horsepower and 443 lb-ft of torque. It features a seven-speed automatic transmission and is available in both rear-wheel drive and all-wheel drive configurations. The CLS500 is praised for its stylish design, comfortable interior, and impressive performance, but it has faced some reported issues.

**Engine**

* **4.7L V8 Engine**
    * **Intake Manifold Leaks:**
        * Description/Symptoms: Coolant or oil leaks from the intake manifold, leading to overheating and engine performance issues.
        * Solution: Replace the intake manifold.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $1,500+
        * Recalls: NHTSA Campaign 16V355000

**Transmission**

* **7-Speed Automatic Transmission (7G-TRONIC PLUS)**
    * **Torque Converter Failure:**
        * Description/Symptoms: Shuddering, slipping, or delayed engagement of gears, eventually leading to transmission failure.
        * Solution: Replace torque converter and related components.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,500 - $3,500+
        * Recalls: NHTSA Campaign 16V361000

**Suspension**

* **No Issues Found**

**Electrical**

* **Battery Issues:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Premature battery failure, electrical system malfunctions, and starting problems.
    * Solution: Replace battery or repair charging system components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+

**Exterior**

* **Door Lock Actuator Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Doors fail to lock or unlock, or may open spontaneously while driving.
    * Solution: Replace door lock actuators.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: NHTSA Campaign 17V636000

**Interior**

* **Sunroof Control Module Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Sunroof fails to open or close, or operates erratically.
    * Solution: Replace sunroof control module.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+

**Other**

* **Fuel Pump Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Engine stalls or hesitates under load, especially when driving uphill.
    * Solution: Replace fuel pump.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,200 - $1,800+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

Overall, the 2013 Mercedes-Benz CLS500 is a well-regarded vehicle, but it has faced some potential issues. The most significant issues relate to the engine, transmission, and electrical systems, which can be costly to repair. However, it's important to note that these issues may not affect all CLS500s, and many owners have had positive experiences with the vehicle.
