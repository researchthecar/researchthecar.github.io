**Introduction:**

The 2014 Mercedes-Benz CLS500 is a high-performance luxury sedan powered by a 4.7L twin-turbocharged V8 engine paired to a 7-speed automatic transmission. It produces 402 horsepower and 443 lb-ft of torque. This vehicle is praised for its elegant styling, high-quality interior, and impressive driving dynamics. However, like any vehicle, the CLS500 has faced some common issues reported by owners.

**Engine**

**4.7L Twin-Turbocharged V8**

* **Timing Chain Tensioner Failure:** Over time, the timing chain tensioner may fail, leading to a loss of engine power, rattling noises, and potentially catastrophic engine damage.
   * Solution: Replacement of the timing chain tensioner and associated components.
   * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,500 - $4,000+
   * Recalls: SB-2015-12, SB-2018-10

**Transmission**

**7-Speed Automatic Transmission**

* **Harsh Gear Shifts:** Some owners have reported experiencing harsh gear shifts, especially when shifting from Park to Drive or Reverse.
   * Solution: Software updates or transmission fluid replacement may resolve the issue.
   * Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $500

**Suspension**

* **Air Suspension System Failure:** The air suspension system can malfunction due to faulty sensors, leaks, or compressor issues, resulting in a loss of ride height and handling problems.
   * Solution: Repair or replacement of the affected components.
   * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $3,000+

**Electrical**

* **Battery Discharge:** Premature battery discharge has been reported in some CLS500s due to parasitic drain from various electrical components.
   * Solution: Tracing and resolving the source of the parasitic drain.
   * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500

* **Malfunctioning Command System:** The Command system, which controls the infotainment and navigation functions, can experience glitches or freezing.
   * Solution: Software updates or hardware replacement.
   * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500

**Exterior**

* **Sunroof Leaks:** Water leaks have been reported around the edges of the panoramic sunroof.
   * Solution: Resealing or replacement of the sunroof components.
   * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000

**Interior**

* **Cracked or Brittle Plastic Trim:** Some interior plastic trim pieces may become cracked or brittle over time, especially in areas exposed to sunlight.
   * Solution: Replacement of the affected trim pieces.
   * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500

* **Malfunctioning Sun Visor:** The sun visor may not fully extend or retract due to a faulty mechanism.
   * Solution: Replacement of the sun visor.
   * Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300

**Summary:**

The 2014 Mercedes-Benz CLS500 is a luxurious and powerful sedan, but it has faced some issues that owners should be aware of. The most severe and expensive issues include timing chain tensioner failure and air suspension system problems. However, most other reported issues are relatively minor and can be resolved at a reasonable cost.
