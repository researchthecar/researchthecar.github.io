**Introduction:**

The 2012 Mercedes-Benz SLK200 is a compact luxury roadster that offers sleek styling and a comfortable ride. It features a 1.8L turbocharged 4-cylinder engine that produces 204 horsepower and 221 lb-ft of torque. The engine is paired with a 7-speed automatic transmission. The SLK200 is known for its handling and acceleration, but it has also been reported to have some issues.

**Engine**

* **None Found**

**Transmission**

* **7-Speed Automatic Transmission**
    * **Issue:** Premature transmission failure
    * **Description/Symptoms:** Transmission slipping, grinding, or jerking
    * **Solution:** Replace transmission
    * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $5,000 - $8,000+
    * **Recalls:** None Found

**Suspension**

* **None Found**

**Electrical**

* **Alternator Failure**
    * **Description/Symptoms:** Electrical system malfunctions, battery not charging, warning lights illuminated
    * **Solution:** Replace alternator
    * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $500 - $1,000
    * **Recalls:** None Found

**Exterior**

* **Hood Prop Rod Failure**
    * **Description/Symptoms:** Hood will not stay open
    * **Solution:** Replace hood prop rod
    * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $100 - $200
    * **Recalls:** None Found

**Interior**

* **Automatic Climate Control Malfunction**
    * **Description/Symptoms:** Climate control system not functioning properly, temperature fluctuations, fan not working
    * **Solution:** Replace climate control module
    * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $500 - $1,000
    * **Recalls:** None Found

**Summary:**

The 2012 Mercedes-Benz SLK200 has been reported to have some issues with its transmission and electrical systems. While these issues can be costly to fix, they are relatively rare. Overall, the SLK200 is a reliable and enjoyable vehicle to drive.
