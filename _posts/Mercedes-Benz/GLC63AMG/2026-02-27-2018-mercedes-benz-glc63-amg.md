# 2018 Mercedes-Benz GLC63 AMG Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2018 Mercedes-Benz GLC63 AMG delivers high-performance luxury, anchored by robust AMG-tuned engine and transmission options. While its powertrains offer exhilarating power, used-car buyers should review owner-reported complaints carefully. Common areas of concern include occasional electrical glitches, minor accessory issues, and localized safety feedback regarding seat belts and airbags.

---

## Engine

### 4.0L Biturbo V8 (M177)

The hand-built 4.0-liter twin-turbocharged V8 engine generates substantial horsepower and torque, delivering supercar-rivaling acceleration for the high-performance AMG trim. Routine maintenance is critical for long-term reliability given the complex turbocharging and ventilation plumbing.

**Crankcase Ventilation Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Illuminated check engine lights and stored diagnostic codes such as P052E.
- **Solution:** Inspect and replace faulty crankcase ventilation components or pressure control valves as necessary.

## Transmission

### AMG Speedshift MCT 9-Speed Automatic

The wet-clutch 9-speed automatic transmission is engineered to handle massive torque output while providing rapid shifts. Buyers should verify smooth operation across all driving modes during a test drive.

**Unintended Neutral Shift**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may unexpectedly shift into neutral while driving or fail to engage gear properly from a standstill.
- **Solution:** Perform a comprehensive transmission diagnostic check, inspect selector linkages, and update software modules if required.

## Steering

### Power Steering System

Electric power-assist steering provides responsive handling, though occasional software glitches can trigger temporary assist warnings.

**Steering Assist Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Heavy steering feel accompanied by diagnostic trouble codes indicating power assist module or software communication failures.
- **Solution:** Perform a software reset or update, and inspect power steering control modules for internal faults.

## Safety

### Seat Belts

Occupant safety systems generally perform well, though minor hardware complaints exist regarding rear seat belt latches.

**Rear Latch and Plate Concerns**
- **Symptoms:** Difficulty latching rear seat belts or loose-fitting buckle assemblies reported by occupants.
- **Solution:** Inspect seat belt buckles and latch plates for physical obstruction or internal latch wear, replacing hardware if needed.

### Airbags

Airbag and steering column electronics require proper maintenance to ensure warning lights and integrated wheel controls function seamlessly.

**Airbag Warning and Steering Clockspring Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Illuminated airbag warning lights on the dashboard alongside an inoperable horn or steering wheel control faults.
- **Solution:** Diagnose steering column electronic modules and replace faulty clockspring components or wiring harnesses.
