# 2022 Mercedes-Benz E450 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used Mercedes-Benz E450, buyers will find a luxury vehicle renowned for its high-tech cabin, smooth performance, and solid engineering. While owners praise its refined ride quality and advanced features, reviewing common owner-reported complaints can help prospective buyers anticipate minor electronic and driver-assistance quirks.

---

## Engine

### 3.0L Turbocharged Inline-6 (M256)

The 3.0-liter turbocharged inline-6 engine utilizes a 48-volt mild-hybrid system to produce approximately 362 horsepower, offering brisk acceleration and refined operation. Overall, this powertrain is sophisticated and generally smooth, though shoppers should pay close attention to the mild-hybrid electrical components during a pre-purchase inspection.

**48V Battery Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** A dashboard warning appears for a 48V battery malfunction, followed shortly by a red warning light and sudden power loss or deceleration.
- **Solution:** Have the 48-volt electrical system, battery, and related converter modules inspected and replaced or reset by a qualified technician.

## Transmission

### 9-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 9-speed automatic transmission delivers seamless gear changes across rear-wheel-drive and 4MATIC all-wheel-drive configurations. It generally operates reliably under normal driving conditions without widespread mechanical complaints.

## Steering

### Steering and Front Suspension

The steering system offers precise handling, though proper alignment and tire selection are crucial to prevent uneven wear.

**Steering Pull and Low-Speed Tire Contact**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have reported unexpected steering pulls or humming noises, along with low-profile tires contacting under-bumper components or rubbing during tight turns.
- **Solution:** Inspect front wheel alignment, suspension components, and tire wear patterns to ensure proper clearance and tracking.

## Electrical

### Infotainment and Camera System

In-car electronics are feature-rich and advanced, though occasional software glitches can affect display screens.

**Blank Backup Camera Display**
- **Symptoms:** The backup camera screen remains completely blank or fails to engage when shifting the vehicle into reverse.
- **Solution:** Reset the infotainment system or have the camera module and wiring harness inspected for faults.

## Exterior

### Sunroof and Underbody Shields

Exterior panels and glass are generally well-fitted, but rare reports of spontaneous glass breakage warrant a thorough check of the panoramic roof.

**Panoramic Sunroof Fracture**
- **Symptoms:** The panoramic glass roof may shatter or explode suddenly without any external impact or warning.
- **Solution:** Have the glass inspected for stress fractures and replace the sunroof assembly if damage is found.

## Safety

### Forward Collision Avoidance

Advanced safety features provide excellent protection, but sensitive driver-assistance sensors can occasionally report false readings.

**Phantom Braking and False Speed Limits**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may incorrectly read local speed limit signs or unexpectedly activate the emergency brakes in traffic without any obstruction ahead.
- **Solution:** Check for software updates at a dealership or service center to recalibrate the driver-assistance camera and radar sensors.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V189000: Steering: Steering Wheel/Handle Bar](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=E450&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** Deactivated hands-off detection will prevent DISTRONIC system features from functioning, including warnings to grab the steering wheel and calling first responders as part of the Active Emergency Stop Assist, which can increase the risk of a crash or injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the hand detection control unit software for the DISTRONIC system, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V733000: Electrical System:12V/24V/48V Battery](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=E450&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** The 12-Volt battery in the trunk may not be properly secured, which, in the event of a crash, can cause electrical connection failure, disabling various safety features including, emergency call (eCall) system, electric seat adjustment, hazard warning lights, and automatic door locks. The inability to operate safety features (eCall system, hazard lights.
- **What to do:** Dealers will secure the 12V battery, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V100000: Electrical System:Wiring](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=E450&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** The 48-volt ground cable connection located in the engine compartment may not be tightened properly and may result in the ground cable overheating. An overheated ground cable increases the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the 48-volt ground cable connection and repair it as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The Mercedes-Benz E450 provides a luxurious driving experience backed by a strong inline-six engine and comfortable interior. While owner reports highlight a few potential electrical quirks, mild-hybrid battery warnings, and driver-assistance calibration sensitivities, a thorough pre-purchase inspection can help ensure a quality ownership experience.
