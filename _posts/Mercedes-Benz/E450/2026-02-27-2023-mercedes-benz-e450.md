# 2023 Mercedes-Benz E450 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2023 Mercedes-Benz E450 delivers a sophisticated blend of luxury and performance, underpinned by advanced technology and refined engineering. As prospective buyers evaluate this model on the used market, reviewing owner-reported feedback can offer helpful context regarding potential mechanical, electrical, and equipment nuances.

---

## Engine

### 3.0L Turbocharged Inline-6 (M256)

The 3.0-liter turbocharged inline-6 engine utilizes a 48-volt mild-hybrid EQ Boost system, producing approximately 362 horsepower and 369 lb-ft of torque. It offers smooth, robust power delivery, though buyers should verify proper hybrid system and engine diagnostics during a pre-purchase inspection.

**Engine Stalling and Seizure Concerns**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have reported unexpected engine stalling while making turns or driving at various speeds, accompanied by warning lights and, in rare instances, engine seizure.
- **Solution:** Have a certified technician diagnose any powertrain error codes, check the electrical system, and inspect internal components immediately if stalling or warnings occur.

## Transmission

### 9G-TRONIC 9-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 9G-TRONIC 9-speed automatic transmission comes paired with rear-wheel drive or 4MATIC all-wheel drive, delivering seamless gear changes. Regular fluid maintenance and software checks ensure continued smooth operation.

**Inadvertent Shifting to Neutral**
- **Symptoms:** Some owners have experienced the vehicle unexpectedly shifting from drive into neutral while moving at moderate speeds without driver input.
- **Solution:** Consult a dealership or qualified specialist to check for transmission control module software updates or mechanical linkage faults.

## Suspension

### Factory Tires and Wheels

The factory suspension setup prioritizes ride comfort, but low-profile tires mounted on large alloy wheels remain susceptible to impact damage on rough road surfaces.

**Factory Low-Profile Tire Blowouts**
- **Symptoms:** Owners equipped with factory low-profile tires have reported sudden sidewall blowouts and flat tires after hitting minor road potholes or bumps.
- **Solution:** Inspect tire condition and tread depth carefully; some owners opt for replacement tires with stronger sidewalls or carry an alternative repair kit.

## Brakes

### Rear Brake Caliper Bracket

The braking system provides confident stopping power under normal driving conditions, though periodic physical inspection of pads, rotors, and calipers is recommended.

**Uneven Brake Pad Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Premature metal-on-metal brake wear has been observed on one side while opposite brake pads retain significant life, potentially due to a sticking caliper or bracket defect.
- **Solution:** Have a brake specialist inspect brake calipers, slide pins, and brackets during routine service to ensure even pad wear and proper clamping force.

## Safety

### Advanced Driver Assistance Systems

Advanced electrical architecture powers numerous convenience and safety features, which can occasionally display sensitivity to sensor obstructions or software glitches.

**Active Safety Sensor Inoperable Warnings**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have noted unexpected yellow warning messages regarding lane-keeping assistance followed by emergency braking prompts while cruise control is active.
- **Solution:** Clean exterior camera and radar sensors, and have a dealer check sensor calibration and software modules if warnings persist.

### Power Trunk Lid

Exterior panels and power accessories offer a premium feel, though motorized convenience features require proper sensor maintenance.

**Unexpected Trunk Lid Closure**
- **Symptoms:** The motorized trunk lid has occasionally closed unexpectedly without triggering standard automatic closure sensors or obstruction safety stops.
- **Solution:** Inspect trunk strut mechanisms and have the electronic trunk control module diagnosed if erratic closing behavior occurs.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V100000: Electrical System:Wiring](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=E450&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** The 48-volt ground cable connection located in the engine compartment may not be tightened properly and may result in the ground cable overheating. An overheated ground cable increases the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the 48-volt ground cable connection and repair it as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2023 Mercedes-Benz E450 is a high-tech luxury vehicle offering impressive performance and refinement. While most owners enjoy a trouble-free experience, used-car buyers should prioritize a comprehensive pre-purchase inspection checking the powertrain electronics, brake components, and tire condition to ensure peace of mind.
