# 2021 Mercedes-Benz E450 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2021 Mercedes-Benz E450 delivers luxury, cutting-edge technology, and robust performance, but potential buyers should approach the used market with a clear understanding of its documented quirks. While owner feedback highlights sophisticated cabin features and strong powertrain dynamics, specific concerns surrounding electrical systems, powertrain components, and auxiliary equipment have surfaced in owner reports. Careful pre-purchase inspections and service history checks remain essential before finalizing a purchase.

---

## Engine

### 3.0L Turbo Inline-6 (M256) with EQ Boost

The 3.0-liter turbocharged inline-six engine features mild-hybrid EQ Boost technology, producing approximately 362 horsepower and 369 lb-ft of torque. It offers brisk, smooth acceleration and refined highway cruising, though owners should monitor for potential misfire or valve-related issues.

**Engine Misfire and Loss of Power**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may experience unexpected engine misfires, illuminating the check engine light and potentially forcing the vehicle into a reduced-power 'limp mode'.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis by a qualified technician is required to check for underlying valve wear or related technical service bulletins, potentially requiring component replacement.

## Transmission

### 9G-TRONIC 9-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 9-speed automatic transmission delivers seamless gear changes across rear-wheel-drive and 4MATIC all-wheel-drive configurations. While generally smooth and efficient, isolated reports of severe mechanical failure mean prospective buyers should thoroughly test-shift the vehicle.

**Premature Transmission Failure**
- **Symptoms:** A small number of owners have reported sudden transmission failure resulting in a complete loss of vehicle mobility while driving.
- **Solution:** Requires professional inspection, diagnostic troubleshooting, and potentially major transmission overhaul or replacement by a dealership or specialist.

## Brakes

### Parking Brake and Service Brakes

The braking system provides confident stopping power under normal conditions, though electronic parking brake engagement glitches have been flagged in rare instances.

**Unexpected Brake Locking**
- **Symptoms:** The emergency or parking brake may independently seize or lock up after the vehicle has been parked for multiple days.
- **Solution:** Inspect brake actuators, electronic parking brake modules, and calipers for proper release and operation.

## Electrical

### 48V Electrical System & Battery

The advanced electrical architecture supports luxury cabin accessories and the mild-hybrid system, but battery and charging anomalies have been documented by early owners.

**48-Volt Battery Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Failure of the 48V battery system can render the vehicle entirely inoperable, frequently triggering dashboard warnings and requiring a tow.
- **Solution:** Requires diagnostic testing of the electrical system and replacement of faulty battery components or charging modules.

### Backup Camera and Sensor Systems

Driver-assistance and visibility electronics provide helpful maneuvering aids, though software bugs can cause intermittent display dropouts.

**Intermittent Camera Inoperation**
- **Symptoms:** The rearview camera display and parking sensors may intermittently fail to activate when shifting into reverse, showing error messages.
- **Solution:** Software updates or replacement of faulty camera modules and wiring harnesses can resolve display failures.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V483000: Seat Belts:Rear/Other:Retractor](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=E450&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 208, "Occupant Crash Protection." An unsecured child restraint system can increase the risk of injury during a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect, and replace as necessary, the rear seat belt assemblies, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V406000: Seats:Mid/Rear Assembly:Head Restraint](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=E450&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** Incorrectly mounted headrests could move or detach from the seat during a crash, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the rear seatback guide bushings and replace them if necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V228000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=E450&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** An inoperative rearview camera display and/or parking sensors can reduce the driver's view and awareness of what is behind the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** MBUSA will replace the parking control unit, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V216000: Seats](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=E450&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** A seat too close to the air bag increases the risk of injury in the event of a crash necessitating the deployment of the frontal passenger air bag.
- **What to do:** MBUSA will notify owners, and dealers will recalibrate the passenger seat limiter, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V230000: Seats](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=E450&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** A faulty electrical contact within the seat position switch may prevent the driver and passenger air bags and seat belt force limiters from properly activating during a crash. Air bags and seat belts that do not perform properly in a crash can increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** MBUSA will notify owners, and dealers will replace the seat position switches, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V673000: Seats](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=E450&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** If the front passenger seat is adjusted beyond the acceptable range in relation to the air bag, there could be an increased risk of injury in the event of a crash with air bag deployment.
- **What to do:** MBUSA will notify owners, and dealers will inspect for the mechanical seat adjustment range limitation for the front passenger seat and install it if necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V189000: Steering: Steering Wheel/Handle Bar](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=E450&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** Deactivated hands-off detection will prevent DISTRONIC system features from functioning, including warnings to grab the steering wheel and calling first responders as part of the Active Emergency Stop Assist, which can increase the risk of a crash or injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the hand detection control unit software for the DISTRONIC system, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V733000: Electrical System:12V/24V/48V Battery](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=E450&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The 12-Volt battery in the trunk may not be properly secured, which, in the event of a crash, can cause electrical connection failure, disabling various safety features including, emergency call (eCall) system, electric seat adjustment, hazard warning lights, and automatic door locks. - **What to do:** Dealers will secure the 12V battery, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2021 Mercedes-Benz E450 is a sophisticated and powerful luxury cruiser, but used-car shoppers should proceed with diligence. Reviewing service records for any past electrical work, battery updates, or powertrain repairs is highly recommended. Buyers should ensure all recall items have been properly addressed by a certified dealer prior to purchase.
