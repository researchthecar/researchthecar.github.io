# 2020 Mercedes-Benz E450 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2020 Mercedes-Benz E450, buyers can generally expect a luxurious driving experience backed by robust powertrain options. However, owner reports submitted to safety agencies highlight a few recurring electronic and mechanical areas worth inspecting during a pre-purchase evaluation.

---

## Engine

### 3.0L Turbo V6 (M276)

The 3.0-liter twin-turbo V6 engine produces around 362 horsepower and 369 lb-ft of torque, delivering smooth and responsive performance. Overall, this powerplant is generally considered reliable, though routine inspection of oil-related sensors is advised.

**Oil Pump Control Valve Fault**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may notice an intermittent engine warning message on the dashboard, with diagnostic scans revealing oil pump valve fault codes.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified mechanic inspect the oil pump valve and replace the faulty component as necessary.

### 3.0L Turbo Inline-6 Mild Hybrid (M256)

The 3.0-liter turbocharged inline-6 features EQ Boost mild-hybrid technology, generating roughly 362 horsepower alongside instant electric torque assistance. It offers refined power delivery, though buyers should verify the engine harness is free from oil migration.

**Camshaft Sensor Oil Leak and Harness Corrosion**
- **Symptoms:** Engine oil leaks past the camshaft sensor and wicks into the main electrical wiring harness, potentially causing extensive corrosion without early dashboard warnings.
- **Solution:** Inspect camshaft sensors regularly for oil seepage, and replace leaking sensors and affected wiring harnesses promptly.
- **Cost:** $4,000+

## Transmission

### 9G-TRONIC 9-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 9-speed automatic transmission routes power smoothly to the rear wheels or 4MATIC all-wheel-drive system. It is generally praised for refined gear selection and cruising efficiency.

## Electrical

### Backup Camera System

Safety equipment generally performs well, though rearview camera glitches have been noted by some owners.

**Intermittent Rearview Camera Failure**
- **Symptoms:** When shifting into reverse, the infotainment display may show a frozen image, display a black screen, or indicate that the rearview camera is inoperable.
- **Solution:** Inspect the camera wiring and software modules; dealerships may need to apply software updates or replace failing camera components.

## Exterior

### Convertible Soft Top

Exterior styling is sleek and sophisticated, though soft top models require careful inspection of weatherstripping and window seals.

**Rear Window Delamination**
- **Symptoms:** The integrated glass window on the convertible soft top may begin to prematurely separate or delaminate from the surrounding fabric assembly.
- **Solution:** Inspect the soft top assembly closely for signs of adhesive failure; a professional top replacement or resealing may be required.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V228000: Seat Belts:Front:Webbing](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=E450&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** A damaged seatbelt may not properly restrain an occupant, increasing the risk of injury in the event of a crash.
- **What to do:** MBUSA will notify owners, and dealers will inspect and reroute the front left seatbelt as necessary, or replace it if the webbing is damaged, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2020 Mercedes-Benz E450 is a sophisticated luxury vehicle offering strong engine options and refined handling. While most owners enjoy trouble-free driving, prospective buyers should carefully check for camshaft sensor oil leaks, verify proper operation of the backup camera, and inspect convertible top seals if applicable.
