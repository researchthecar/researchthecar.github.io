# 2012 Mercedes-Benz C200

### Overview
The 2012 Mercedes-Benz C200, part of the W204 generation, is generally considered a reliable vehicle, offering a blend of luxury, performance, and comfort. It was available with a range of efficient diesel and petrol engines, paired with either a manual or automatic transmission. While most owners report a positive ownership experience, like any vehicle, certain components can experience issues over time. This analysis focuses on documented, non-wear-and-tear related problems reported by owners and technicians, excluding routine maintenance. The C200's build quality is typically high, contributing to its overall dependability, but specific areas warrant attention for potential buyers or current owners.

---


## Engine

### 1.8L M271 CGI Turbocharged Petrol
**Timing Chain Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Rattling noise from the front of the engine, especially on startup; check engine light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the timing chain, tensioners, and guides.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1500

**Turbocharger Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of power, whistling noises, increased oil consumption.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of the turbocharger unit.
- **Cost:** $1200 - $2500


### 2.1L OM651 DE22LA Turbocharged Diesel
**Diesel Particulate Filter (DPF) Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Reduced engine power, increased fuel consumption, warning lights on the dashboard.
- **Solution:** Forced regeneration or DPF cleaning/replacement.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1800

**Injector Problems**
- **Symptoms:** Rough idling, poor acceleration, increased smoke, engine misfires.
- **Solution:** Cleaning or replacement of diesel injectors.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600 per injector


## Transmission

### 7G-TRONIC PLUS Automatic Transmission
**Mechatronic Unit Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Harsh shifting, transmission slipping, inability to select gears, warning lights.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of the mechatronic unit.
- **Cost:** $1500 - $3000

**Torque Converter Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Shuddering at low speeds, slipping, delayed engagement.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the torque converter.
- **Cost:** $1000 - $2000

## Electrical

### General Electrical System
**SAM Unit (Signal Acquisition Module) Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Intermittent electrical faults, malfunctioning lights, power window issues, dashboard warning lights.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and potential reprogramming or replacement of the SAM unit.
- **Cost:** $600 - $1200

**Sensor Failures (e.g., ABS/ESP)**
- **Symptoms:** ABS or ESP warning lights illuminated, loss of traction control.
- **Solution:** Replacement of faulty sensor(s).
- **Cost:** $200 - $500

### Summary Verdict
The 2012 Mercedes-Benz C200 is a solid choice for a premium compact sedan, with a generally good reliability record. The most common concerns tend to revolve around the petrol engine's timing chain and turbocharger, and potential issues with the diesel engine's DPF and injectors. The automatic transmission's mechatronic unit and torque converter can also be sources of repair costs. Electrical gremlins, particularly related to the SAM unit, are not uncommon. However, many of these issues are age-related and can be mitigated with diligent maintenance and prompt attention to early warning signs. Overall, it offers a refined driving experience with reasonable long-term prospects if well-maintained.
