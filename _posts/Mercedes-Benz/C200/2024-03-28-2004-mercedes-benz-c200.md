**Introduction:**
The 2004 Mercedes-Benz C200 is a compact luxury sedan that offers a comfortable and refined driving experience. It comes with a 1.8L inline-four engine producing 161 horsepower and 155 lb-ft of torque, paired with a 5-speed automatic transmission. The C200 is known for its stylish design, responsive handling, and luxurious interior. However, it has also been reported to have some potential issues.

**Engine**
**1.8L Inline-Four**

* **Intake Manifold Cracking:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Cracks in the intake manifold can lead to vacuum leaks, causing engine performance issues such as rough idling, power loss, and increased emissions.
    * Solution: Replace the intake manifold.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**
**5-Speed Automatic**

* **Shifting Issues:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The transmission may hesitate or slip when shifting gears, resulting in rough shifting and increased fuel consumption.
    * Solution: Replace or repair the transmission control module (TCM) or transmission solenoid.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**
* **Front Suspension Strut Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Failure of the front suspension struts can lead to uneven tire wear, poor handling, and a rough ride.
    * Solution: Replace the front suspension struts.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**
* **Battery Drain:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The vehicle's battery may drain prematurely, causing starting issues.
    * Solution: Identify and replace the faulty component causing the battery drain, such as a faulty alternator or parasitic draw.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**
* **Sunroof Issues:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The sunroof may fail to open or close properly, or develop leaks.
    * Solution: Repair or replace the sunroof motor, cables, or gaskets.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**
* **Instrument Cluster Malfunction:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The instrument cluster may malfunction, displaying incorrect information or failing to work entirely.
    * Solution: Replace or repair the instrument cluster.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**
The 2004 Mercedes-Benz C200 offers a comfortable and luxurious driving experience, but it has been reported to have some potential issues. While these issues vary in severity, some can be costly to repair. If you are considering purchasing a C200, it is advisable to be aware of these issues and factor in the potential cost of repairs into your decision.
