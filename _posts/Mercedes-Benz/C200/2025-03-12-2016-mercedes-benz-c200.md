The 2016 Mercedes-Benz C200 is a popular compact executive sedan known for its stylish design, comfortable ride, and refined interior. The C200 variant typically features a 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine producing around 181 horsepower and 221 lb-ft of torque. Other engine options available for the 2016 C-Class range include a more powerful 2.0-liter turbo-four, a 3.0-liter V6, and even a hybrid variant. The standard transmission is a 7-speed automatic (7G-TRONIC PLUS). Owners often praise the C200 for its fuel efficiency, smooth handling, and luxurious feel. However, some common complaints revolve around the reliability of certain electronic components and occasional issues with the powertrain.

**Engine**

**2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4 (M274)**

* **Oil Leaks from Timing Chain Cover**
    * Description/Symptoms: Oil leaking from the front of the engine, near the timing chain cover. This can lead to low oil levels, potential engine damage, and visible oil stains.
    * Solution: Replacement of the timing chain cover gasket or resealing the cover.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1200+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **High Pressure Fuel Pump Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Extended cranking, difficulty starting, rough idle, or a loss of power. Diagnostic trouble codes (DTCs) related to fuel pressure or fuel pump performance may be present.
    * Solution: Replacement of the high-pressure fuel pump.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**7G-TRONIC PLUS (722.9)**

* **Rough Shifting/Delayed Engagement**
    * Description/Symptoms: Harsh or jerky shifts between gears, noticeable delays when shifting from park to drive or reverse, or slipping between gears.
    * Solution: In some cases, a software update can resolve the issue. More severe cases may require valve body replacement or a transmission rebuild.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 (software update) - $4000+ (transmission rebuild)
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Torque Converter Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: Shuddering or vibrations at low speeds, especially when accelerating. Can also cause abnormal noise from the transmission.
    * Solution: Replacement of the torque converter.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1500 - $3000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **No Issues Found**

**Electrical**

* **COMAND System Malfunctions**
    * Description/Symptoms: The central infotainment system (COMAND) may experience freezing, crashing, or intermittent failures. Functions such as navigation, audio, and Bluetooth connectivity can be affected.
    * Solution: Software updates may resolve some issues. More severe problems may require replacement of the COMAND unit.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 (software update) - $2000+ (COMAND unit replacement)
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Faulty Steering Column Lock**
    * Description/Symptoms: The vehicle may not start due to a failure of the electronic steering column lock. A warning message may appear on the dashboard.
    * Solution: Replacement of the steering column lock module.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Malfunctioning Power Folding Mirrors**
    * Description/Symptoms: Power folding mirrors not extending or retracting when the door is unlocked or locked
    * Solution: Replacement of the power folding motor
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Sunroof Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: Sunroof may get stuck, refuse to open or close fully, or make excessive noise during operation.
    * Solution: Repair or replacement of the sunroof motor, tracks, or related components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2016 Mercedes-Benz C200, while offering a luxurious experience, can be prone to certain issues. Engine oil leaks and high-pressure fuel pump failures appear to be the most common engine-related concerns. The 7G-TRONIC PLUS transmission can suffer from rough shifting or torque converter problems. Electrically, malfunctions of the COMAND system and steering column lock failures are reported. While the severity and cost of these issues can vary, addressing them promptly is crucial to maintaining the vehicle's reliability and performance.
