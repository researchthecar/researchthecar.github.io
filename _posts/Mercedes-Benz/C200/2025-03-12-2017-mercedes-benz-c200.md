The 2017 Mercedes-Benz C200 is a popular luxury sedan known for its stylish design, comfortable ride, and advanced technology. In addition to the C200, the 2017 C-Class also offered a range of engine options including the C300, C350e, C43 AMG, C63 AMG, and C63 S AMG. The C200 is powered by a 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine producing 184 horsepower and 221 lb-ft of torque. Owners generally appreciate the car's fuel efficiency, refined interior, and smooth handling. However, some common complaints include issues with the infotainment system, electrical glitches, and certain engine components.

**Engine**

**2.0L Turbocharged Four-Cylinder (M274)**

*   **High-Pressure Fuel Pump Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Engine misfires, rough idling, loss of power, and difficulty starting. The check engine light may illuminate.
    *   Solution: Replace the high-pressure fuel pump with an updated version.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1200+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Camshaft Adjuster Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Engine rattle on startup, loss of power, rough idling, and check engine light. Often related to the camshaft adjuster solenoid or the adjuster itself.
    *   Solution: Replace faulty camshaft adjuster solenoid or the camshaft adjuster.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1000 - $2500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Coolant Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Coolant level dropping, overheating, and visible coolant leaks around the engine. Often occurs at the water pump or thermostat housing.
    *   Solution: Replace the leaking water pump, thermostat housing, or associated seals and gaskets.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**7G-TRONIC PLUS (722.9) 7-Speed Automatic**

*   **Torque Converter Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Shuddering or vibration during low-speed acceleration, particularly in lower gears. Can worsen over time.
    *   Solution: Replace the torque converter. In some cases, a transmission flush and fluid replacement with updated fluid can temporarily alleviate the symptoms.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $2000 - $4000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Valve Body Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Harsh or delayed shifting, slipping between gears, and potential for getting stuck in gear.
    *   Solution: Replace or rebuild the valve body. Replacing the conductor plate may also resolve the issue in some cases.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1500 - $3500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Front Suspension Lower Control Arm Bushings**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Clunking or rattling noise from the front suspension, especially when going over bumps. Can also lead to imprecise steering.
    *   Solution: Replace the worn lower control arm bushings. Complete control arm replacement may be recommended in some cases.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800+ per side.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Freezing/Crashing**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The COMAND system freezes, reboots unexpectedly, or becomes unresponsive. Can affect navigation, audio, and other functions.
    *   Solution: Software update, replacing the head unit may be required.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Battery Drain**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The car battery drains quickly, especially if left unused for a few days. Can be caused by a parasitic drain from various electrical components.
    *   Solution: Identify and repair the source of the parasitic drain. Common culprits include faulty modules, lights, or aftermarket accessories.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+ depending on the source
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Sunroof Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Sunroof getting stuck, making excessive noise, or failing to close properly. Can be caused by a faulty motor, damaged tracks, or debris accumulation.
    *   Solution: Repair or replace the sunroof motor, tracks, or associated components. Cleaning and lubrication of the tracks may resolve minor issues.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **No Issues Found**

**Summary:** The 2017 Mercedes-Benz C200 can present owners with several potential issues. The most concerning are engine-related problems like high-pressure fuel pump or camshaft adjuster failures which can be relatively expensive to repair. Transmission issues, particularly those related to the torque converter or valve body, are also prevalent and costly. Electrical glitches with the infotainment system and battery drain are common annoyances. While the C200 offers a luxurious experience, potential buyers should be aware of these potential repair costs and ensure a thorough inspection before purchasing.
