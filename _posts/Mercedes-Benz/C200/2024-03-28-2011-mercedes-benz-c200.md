**Introduction:**

The 2011 Mercedes-Benz C200 is a compact luxury sedan that offers a refined driving experience. It is equipped with a 1.8L inline-four engine that produces 181 horsepower and 192 lb-ft of torque. The C200 is available with a six-speed manual or seven-speed automatic transmission. Notable features include a comfortable interior, advanced safety features, and a responsive suspension system. However, some owners have reported specific issues with their vehicles.

**Engine**

- **1.8L Inline-Four Engine**
    - **Intake Manifold Gasket Failure**
       - Description/Symptoms: Coolant or oil leaks, engine overheating, rough idling
       - Solution: Replacing the intake manifold gasket
       - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800+
       - Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

- **Six-Speed Manual Transmission**
    - **No Issues Found**
- **Seven-Speed Automatic Transmission**
    - **Torque Converter Clutch shudder**
       - Description/Symptoms: Vibrations or shuddering during acceleration or gear changes
       - Solution: Replacing the torque converter
       - Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,200 - $1,800+
       - Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

- **Front Suspension**
    - **Strut Mount Bearing Failure**
       - Description/Symptoms: Noises or vibrations while driving over bumps, uneven tire wear
       - Solution: Replacing the strut mount bearing
       - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
       - Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

- **Battery Issues**
       - Description/Symptoms: Premature battery discharge, difficulty starting the vehicle
       - Solution: Replacing the battery
       - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $300+
       - Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

- **Sunroof Drain Plugs**
       - Description/Symptoms: Water leaks into the cabin during heavy rain
       - Solution: Cleaning or replacing the sunroof drain plugs
       - Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200
       - Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

- **Steering Wheel Buttons**
       - Description/Symptoms: Buttons become unresponsive or malfunction
       - Solution: Replacing the steering wheel buttons
       - Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+
       - Recalls: None Found
- **A/C Compressor Clutch**
       - Description/Symptoms: A/C system fails to cool effectively, compressor noise
       - Solution: Replacing the compressor clutch
       - Estimated Cost to Fix: $600 - $1,000+
       - Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

Overall, the 2011 Mercedes-Benz C200 is well-regarded for its comfort and handling. However, it has experienced some common issues that primarily affect the engine, transmission, suspension, electrical, and interior components. While repairs can be costly in some cases, most issues are relatively minor and do not significantly impact the overall reliability of the vehicle.
