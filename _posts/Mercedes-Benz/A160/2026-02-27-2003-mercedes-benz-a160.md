# 2003 Mercedes-Benz A160

### Overview
The 2003 Mercedes-Benz A160, part of the W168 generation, is a compact hatchback known for its innovative 'sandwich' floor construction, which allows for a spacious interior and a low seating position. While generally considered a practical city car, like any vehicle of its age, it can experience specific reliability concerns. This analysis focuses on documented technical issues beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential problem areas for owners and prospective buyers. The A160 was offered with a range of petrol and diesel engines, and manual or automatic transmissions, each with its own set of potential reliability considerations.

---


## Engine

### 1.6L M166 Petrol
**Ignition Coil Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Misfiring, rough idling, check engine light illumination, poor acceleration.
- **Solution:** Replacement of faulty ignition coil(s).
- **Cost:** $150 - $300 per coil

**Oil Leaks (Valve Cover Gasket)**
- **Symptoms:** Visible oil around the top of the engine, burning oil smell, potential for oil level drop.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the valve cover gasket.
- **Cost:** $250 - $500


## Transmission

### 5-Speed Manual
**Clutch Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Slipping clutch, difficulty engaging gears, grinding noises, stiff clutch pedal.
- **Solution:** Clutch kit replacement (clutch disc, pressure plate, release bearing).
- **Cost:** $600 - $1000


### Autotronic (CVT)
**Transmission Fluid Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Leaking fluid under the vehicle, potential for slipping gears, rough shifting, warning lights.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of seals and gaskets, fluid flush and refill.
- **Cost:** $300 - $700

**Torque Converter Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Shuddering at low speeds, hesitation during acceleration, unusual noises.
- **Solution:** Rebuild or replacement of the torque converter.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1500


## Suspension

### Standard Chassis
**Worn Control Arm Bushings**
- **Symptoms:** Clunking noises over bumps, vague steering feel, uneven tire wear.
- **Solution:** Replacement of control arm bushings or the entire control arm assembly.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600

**Rear Shock Absorber Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Bouncy ride, poor handling, visible leaks from shock absorbers.
- **Solution:** Replacement of rear shock absorbers.
- **Cost:** $200 - $400


## Electrical

### General Electrical System
**Window Regulator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Power windows not operating, slow window movement, grinding noises from door panel.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the window regulator mechanism.
- **Cost:** $200 - $400 per window

**Central Locking Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** Doors not locking/unlocking consistently, individual door lock actuators failing.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and repair/replacement of faulty actuators or control modules.
- **Cost:** $150 - $500


## Exterior

### Body Panels and Trim
**Rust on Wheel Arches and Sills**
- **Symptoms:** Visible corrosion, bubbling paintwork, particularly in areas prone to road salt.
- **Solution:** Rust treatment and repair, repainting.
- **Cost:** $300 - $800+


## Interior

### Cabin Components
**HVAC Blower Motor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** No airflow from vents, inconsistent fan speed, unusual noises from the dashboard.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the HVAC blower motor.
- **Cost:** $200 - $400

**Dashboard Squeaks and Rattles**
- **Symptoms:** Annoying noises from the dashboard, especially over uneven surfaces.
- **Solution:** Identification and insulation of loose trim pieces or components.
- **Cost:** $100 - $300


--- 
### Summary Verdict
The 2003 Mercedes-Benz A160 is a practical compact car with some common age-related issues. Engine reliability is generally good, but ignition coils and valve cover gaskets can be points of failure. The Autotronic CVT transmission, while innovative, can be prone to fluid leaks and torque converter problems, making regular maintenance crucial. Suspension components like control arm bushings and shock absorbers are typical wear items that may require attention. Electrical gremlins, such as window regulators and central locking, are also noted. Exterior rust can be a concern, especially in harsher climates. Interior issues often involve the HVAC system and minor rattles. Overall, while not immune to problems, many issues are repairable and not prohibitively expensive, especially if caught early.
