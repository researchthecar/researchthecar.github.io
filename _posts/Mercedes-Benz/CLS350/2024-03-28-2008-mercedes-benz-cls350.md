**Introduction:**

The 2008 Mercedes-Benz CLS350 is a mid-size luxury sedan powered by a 3.5-liter V6 engine producing 268 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque. It comes with a standard seven-speed automatic transmission. The CLS350 is praised for its stylish design, comfortable interior, and potent engine. However, it has faced some issues over the years.

**Engine**

* **3.5L V6**

  * **Camshaft Adjuster Failure:** Camshaft adjusters can fail, resulting in reduced engine power, rough idling, and engine knocking. Solution: Replacing the camshaft adjusters. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $1,500+.
  * **Intake Manifold Runner Failure:** Intake manifold runners may break or become loose, leading to air leaks and engine performance issues. Solution: Replacing the intake manifold. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800+.
  * **Timing Chain Tensioner Failure:** The timing chain tensioner can fail, causing the timing chain to become loose and skip, resulting in engine damage. Solution: Replacing the timing chain and tensioner. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,000+.
  * **Oil Leaks:** Various engine components, such as valve covers and oil pans, may develop leaks. Solution: Replacing the leaking components or seals. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600+ depending on the leak source.

**Transmission**

* **7-Speed Automatic**

  * **Shift Solenoid Malfunction:** Shift solenoids can malfunction, causing delayed or harsh shifting, slipping gears, or preventing the transmission from shifting. Solution: Replacing the faulty solenoid(s). Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800+.
  * **Transmission Fluid Leak:** The transmission fluid pan or other components may develop leaks, causing fluid loss and transmission damage. Solution: Replacing the leaking components or seals. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+.

**Suspension**

* **Airmatic Suspension Failure:** The airmatic suspension system can fail due to faulty air struts, compressors, or control modules. Solution: Replacing the faulty components. Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+ depending on the component.
* **Strut Mount Bearing Failure:** Strut mount bearings can wear out, causing noise, vibration, and reduced handling. Solution: Replacing the strut mounts. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $900+.

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain:** The battery can drain prematurely due to faulty battery modules in the AGM battery or parasitic draws from other electrical components. Solution: Testing and replacing the faulty battery modules or diagnosing and repairing the parasitic draw. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+.
* **Control Module Failure:** Various control modules, such as the SAM module or gateway module, can fail, causing various electrical issues throughout the vehicle. Solution: Replacing the faulty module. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+.
* **Xenon Headlight Ballast Failure:** Xenon headlight ballasts can fail, causing the headlights to dim or fail. Solution: Replacing the faulty ballast(s). Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+.

**Exterior**

* **Door Lock Actuator Failure:** Door lock actuators can fail, preventing the doors from locking or unlocking. Solution: Replacing the faulty actuator(s). Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+.
* **Windshield Washer Pump Failure:** The windshield washer pump can fail, preventing the windshield from being cleaned. Solution: Replacing the washer pump. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+.

**Interior**

* **Sunroof Drain Clogs:** Sunroof drains can become clogged, causing water leaks into the cabin. Solution: Cleaning out the clogged drains. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+.

**Summary:**

The 2008 Mercedes-Benz CLS350 has a number of potential issues, ranging from engine and transmission malfunctions to electrical and suspension problems. While some of these issues are minor and inexpensive to fix, others can be severe and costly. Owners should be aware of these potential issues and ensure they factor in the cost of potential repairs when considering purchasing or owning a CLS350.
