The 2022 Mercedes-Benz CLS350 is a sleek, four-door coupe known for its stylish design and comfortable ride. In some markets, this model features a 2.0-liter turbocharged inline-4 engine, producing around 295 horsepower and 295 lb-ft of torque. The CLS is often praised for its elegant interior and advanced technology features, but some owners have expressed concerns about the complexity of the infotainment system and the relatively limited rear headroom due to the sloping roofline. Other available engines include a 3.0-liter inline-6 turbo with mild hybrid drive. All engines are paired with a 9G-TRONIC 9-speed automatic transmission.

**Suspension**

*   **Air Suspension Malfunctions**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The air suspension system may experience leaks, causing the vehicle to sag or not maintain the correct ride height. Error messages related to the air suspension system may appear on the dashboard.
    *   Solution: Replacing the faulty air strut(s) or compressor, or repairing leaks in the air lines. Recalibrating the system after repair.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $3,000+ per strut, depending on the shop and parts used.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **MBUX Infotainment System Glitches**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The Mercedes-Benz User Experience (MBUX) system can experience freezing, lagging, or random reboots. Navigation, voice control, or smartphone integration may become unresponsive.
    *   Solution: Software updates from the dealership can often resolve these glitches. In some cases, a complete system reset or replacement of the head unit may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: Free (software update) to $1,500+ (head unit replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Sensor Malfunctions (Parking Sensors, Blind Spot Monitoring)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Parking sensors may give false alarms or fail to detect obstacles. Blind spot monitoring may become erratic or non-functional.
    *   Solution: Diagnosing and replacing the faulty sensor. Calibration of the system after sensor replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+ per sensor, depending on location and complexity.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2022 Mercedes-Benz CLS350 generally exhibits issues typical of modern luxury vehicles. The most common complaints revolve around the MBUX infotainment system's glitches and potential air suspension problems. While some electrical issues with sensors can occur, they are not universally reported. The severity of these issues ranges from minor inconveniences to more costly repairs, especially regarding the air suspension. Overall, the CLS350, when properly maintained, is a reliable and luxurious vehicle but potential buyers should be aware of the potential for electronic and suspension-related issues.
