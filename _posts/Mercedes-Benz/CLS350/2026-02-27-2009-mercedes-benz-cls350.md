# 2009 Mercedes-Benz CLS350

### Overview
The 2009 Mercedes-Benz CLS350, part of the C219 generation, is a luxury grand tourer known for its distinctive styling and comfortable ride. Powered by a robust 3.5L V6 engine, it offers a blend of performance and refinement. While generally well-regarded for its build quality and driving experience, like any vehicle of its age, certain components may exhibit wear or specific issues. This analysis focuses on documented technical reliability concerns beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential areas of attention for owners and prospective buyers of this model year.

---


## Engine

### 3.5L V6 (M272)
**Intake Manifold Runner Flap Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Check engine light, rough idle, loss of power, P2004/P2005/P2006 error codes.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the intake manifold assembly or repair kit.
- **Cost:** $600 - $1200

**Balance Shaft Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Check engine light, rough idle, P0016/P0017 codes (crankshaft/camshaft position correlation).
- **Solution:** Replacement of the balance shaft and associated timing components.
- **Cost:** $2000 - $4000+

**Oil Leaks (Valve Cover Gaskets, Oil Cooler Seals)**
- **Symptoms:** Visible oil leaks, burning oil smell, low oil level.
- **Solution:** Replacement of leaking gaskets and seals.
- **Cost:** $400 - $800


## Transmission

### 7G-TRONIC (722.9) Automatic
**Valve Body Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Harsh shifting, delayed engagement, slipping gears, transmission warning light.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of the valve body.
- **Cost:** $1500 - $3000

**Torque Converter Problems**
- **Symptoms:** Shuddering at low speeds, slipping, rough engagement.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the torque converter.
- **Cost:** $1200 - $2500


## Suspension

### Standard Chassis
**Air Suspension Leaks (if equipped)**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle sagging on one corner, uneven ride height, compressor running excessively.
- **Solution:** Replacement of leaking air springs, lines, or compressor.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1500 per corner

**Control Arm Bushing Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Clunking noises over bumps, vague steering, uneven tire wear.
- **Solution:** Replacement of worn control arm bushings or complete control arms.
- **Cost:** $400 - $800


## Electrical

### General Electrical System
**SAM (Signal Acquisition Module) Failures**
- **Symptoms:** Intermittent electrical faults, non-functioning lights, power windows, or other accessories.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and potential replacement or reprogramming of the SAM unit.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1000

**Headlight Ballast Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Xenon headlights not turning on or flickering.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the headlight ballast.
- **Cost:** $200 - $500

## Interior

### Cabin Components
**Seat Actuator Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** Power seats not moving or moving erratically.
- **Solution:** Replacement of faulty seat actuators.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600

**Climate Control Actuator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Air not directing correctly, temperature issues, unusual noises from dashboard.
- **Solution:** Replacement of climate control actuators.
- **Cost:** $200 - $400

### Summary Verdict
The 2009 Mercedes-Benz CLS350 is a stylish and comfortable luxury coupe. While it offers a premium driving experience, potential owners should be aware of common issues, particularly concerning the M272 engine's intake manifold and balance shaft, as well as the 7G-TRONIC transmission's valve body. Electrical gremlins, especially related to the SAM module, and potential air suspension leaks (if equipped) are also areas to monitor. Despite these potential concerns, with diligent maintenance and addressing issues proactively, the CLS350 can remain a rewarding vehicle.
