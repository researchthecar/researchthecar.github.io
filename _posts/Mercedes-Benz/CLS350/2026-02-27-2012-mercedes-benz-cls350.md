# 2012 Mercedes-Benz CLS350

### Overview
The 2012 Mercedes-Benz CLS350, part of the C218 generation, is a luxury grand tourer known for its striking design and potent performance. It typically features a 3.5L V6 engine paired with a 7-speed automatic transmission. While generally well-regarded for its build quality and driving dynamics, like any vehicle, it can experience specific issues as it ages. This analysis focuses on documented technical reliability concerns beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential problem areas for owners and prospective buyers. Understanding these common issues can help in proactive maintenance and budgeting for repairs.

---


## Engine

### 3.5L V6 (M276)
**Timing Chain Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Rattling noise from the front of the engine, especially on startup; check engine light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of timing chain, tensioners, and guides.
- **Cost:** $1,500 - $3,000

**Oil Leaks (Valve Cover Gaskets/Oil Cooler Seals)**
- **Symptoms:** Visible oil on the engine or under the vehicle; burning oil smell.
- **Solution:** Replacement of valve cover gaskets or oil cooler seals.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1,200


## Transmission

### 7G-TRONIC PLUS (722.9)
**Torque Converter Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Slipping gears, rough shifting, shuddering, or a whining noise.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of the torque converter.
- **Cost:** $1,000 - $2,500

**Transmission Control Module (TCM) Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Erratic shifting, transmission warning lights, inability to shift gears.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the TCM.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1,800


## Suspension

### Standard Chassis
**Air Suspension Leaks (if equipped)**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle sagging on one or more corners, especially after sitting; compressor running excessively.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of air springs, lines, or compressor.
- **Cost:** $700 - $2,000


## Electrical

### General Electrical System
**Sensor Failures (e.g., ABS, Crankshaft Position)**
- **Symptoms:** Illuminated warning lights (ABS, Check Engine), erratic engine performance, loss of power.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and replacement of faulty sensor.
- **Cost:** $300 - $800

**Infotainment System Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Screen freezing, unresponsiveness, audio issues, navigation problems.
- **Solution:** Software updates or replacement of head unit components.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1,500

### Summary Verdict
The 2012 Mercedes-Benz CLS350 (C218) generally offers a reliable luxury experience, with its core powertrain components being robust. However, as with many luxury vehicles of this era, potential issues tend to arise in areas such as the engine's timing system and seals, the 7-speed automatic transmission (particularly torque converter and TCM), and the optional air suspension. Electrical gremlins, including sensor failures and infotainment glitches, are also noted. While not indicative of widespread failure, these are the most commonly reported technical concerns that owners should be aware of for maintenance planning. Regular servicing and addressing early symptoms can mitigate the impact of these potential issues.
