# 2018 Mercedes-Benz Sprinter 3500 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2018 Mercedes-Benz Sprinter 3500 is a heavy-duty commercial van and chassis platform popular for fleets, conversions, and motorhomes. While known for robust capability and utility, prospective used buyers should review owner-reported complaints regarding emissions systems, sensors, and foundational equipment before purchasing.

---

## Engine

### 2.1L 4-Cylinder Turbo Diesel (OM651)

This 2.1-liter twin-turbo diesel engine produces approximately 161 horsepower and 266 lb-ft of torque, paired with a 7-speed automatic transmission. It offers solid fuel economy for heavy commercial applications, though buyers should watch for emissions and sensor maintenance history.

### 3.0L V6 Turbo Diesel (OM642)

The 3.0-liter V6 turbo diesel generates around 188 horsepower and 325 lb-ft of torque, mated to a 5-speed automatic transmission. It provides strong pulling power for heavy loads, but complex emissions equipment requires attentive upkeep.

**Diesel Exhaust Fluid (DEF) Warnings**
- **Symptoms:** Illuminated DEF warning lights can appear on the instrument cluster, sometimes requiring professional diagnosis and resetting at a dealership.
- **Solution:** Have the emissions control system and fluid injectors inspected by a qualified technician.

### Sensors and Exhaust Odors

Electrical and sensor networks govern modern commercial chassis operations, meaning intermittent faults can occasionally trigger dashboard alerts.

**Wheel Speed Sensor Failures**
- **Symptoms:** Individual wheel speed sensors can fail prematurely, potentially triggering related stability control or braking warnings.
- **Solution:** Test and replace faulty wheel speed sensors individually as needed.

**Abnormal Engine Odors**
- **Symptoms:** An unusual sulfur-like odor may occasionally be detected inside or outside the vehicle during low-speed driving.
- **Solution:** Check the exhaust system, catalytic converter, and emissions components for proper function.

## Transmission

### 5-Speed Automatic Transmission

This traditional planetary-gear automatic pairs with the V6 diesel engine to handle heavy-duty hauling workloads. It generally delivers durable service when routine fluid and filter changes are maintained.

### 7-Speed Automatic Transmission (7G-Tronic)

The 7-speed automatic transmission works alongside the 4-cylinder diesel engine to balance highway efficiency with commercial capability. Smooth operation depends on timely transmission fluid services.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

Braking components must manage substantial vehicle weight, making regular physical inspections of pads, rotors, and hydraulics critical for safety.

**Brake Pedal Travel and Pressure Loss**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have reported instances where the brake pedal traveled further down toward the floorboard than usual, requiring extra effort or pumping to stop effectively.
- **Solution:** Inspect the master cylinder, brake lines, and hydraulic fluid for leaks or pressure degradation immediately.

## Exterior

### Body Trim and Lighting

Exterior body fittings and large lens assemblies experience heavy vibration on commercial routes and should be checked periodically.

**Taillight Assembly Separation**
- **Symptoms:** The outer rear taillight housing can loosen and detach unexpectedly while driving at highway speeds.
- **Solution:** Inspect rear light assembly mounting fasteners and secure or replace broken clips.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V693000: Unknown Or Other](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=Sprinter+3500&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** Failure to identify loose safety related parts can increase the risk of crash and injury.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 19V695000: Air Bags: Air Bag/Restraint Control Module](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=Sprinter+3500&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** The airbag systems may not have been activated after vehicle production. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 208, "Occupant Crash Protection." An inactive airbag system can increase the risk of injury in the event of a crash.
- **What to do:** DVUSA will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the vehicles to determine if the airbag systems have been activated, correcting them as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V507000: Visibility:Glass, Side/Rear](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=Sprinter+3500&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** In the event of a side impact crash or vehicle rollover, the fitted side windows may not adequately protect the rear occupants from being ejected, increasing their risk of injury.
- **What to do:** DVUSA will notify owners, and Mercedes-Benz or Freightliner dealers will replace the single-pane safety glass with laminated safety glass., free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V042000: Electronic Stability Control (Esc):Control Module](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=Sprinter+3500&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** The Electronic Stability Control (ESC) unit may become disabled on these vehicles. If ESC becomes disabled, vehicle driving stability may be impacted under certain driving conditions, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** DVUSA will notify owners, and dealers will replace the rear-wheel speed sensor with a more robust sensor, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V155000: Equipment:Other:Labels](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=Sprinter+3500&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** With only the metric speed listed on the spare tire label, the driver could misinterpret the kph as mph and operate the vehicle with the spare tire installed at higher speeds than the tire can withstand, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** DVUSA will notify owners, and Mercedes-Benz or Freightliner Sprinter dealers will replace it with the label with the correct maximum speed information indicated in miles per hour, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V156000: Equipment:Other:Labels](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=Sprinter+3500&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** Without the label, an operator may be unaware of the maximum speed limit of the spare tire and operate the vehicle at a excessive speed, increasing the risk of tire failure and a crash.
- **What to do:** DVUSA will notify owners, and Mercedes-Benz or Freightliner Sprinter dealers will attach the speed limitation label on the spare wheel, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V222000: Unknown Or Other](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=Sprinter+3500&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** Failure to identify loose safety related parts can increase the risk of crash and injury.
- **What to do:** DVUSA will notify owners, and dealers will complete the final quality inspection, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V743000: Latches/Locks/Linkages:Doors:Latch](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=Sprinter+3500&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** As a result, when the doors are electronically unlocked, the right-side doors may not be able to be opened from inside or outside of the vehicle. In the event of a crash, if the right side doors cannot be opened, it could increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** DVUSA will notify owners, and dealers will inspect and replace the locks if necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2018 Mercedes-Benz Sprinter 3500 remains a capable workhorse for heavy hauling and RV conversions, but used buyers should perform a thorough pre-purchase inspection. Pay close attention to brake hydraulic integrity, emissions and exhaust sensor readiness, and body fittings to ensure a reliable ownership experience.
