**2015 Mercedes-Benz Sprinter 3500**

The 2015 Mercedes-Benz Sprinter 3500 is a full-size van that offers a spacious cargo area and a comfortable interior. It is available with two engine options: a 3.0L V6 turbodiesel or a 4.0L V6 gasoline engine. Both engines are mated to a 7-speed automatic transmission. The Sprinter 3500 has a maximum payload capacity of 4,920 lbs and a maximum towing capacity of 7,500 lbs.

People who like the Sprinter 3500 appreciate its spacious cargo area, comfortable interior, and powerful engine options. However, some people have reported issues with the vehicle's engine, transmission, suspension, and electrical system.

**Engine**

**3.0L V6 Turbodiesel**

* **Fuel injector failure:** The fuel injectors can fail, causing the engine to run rough or stall. The fuel injectors will need to be replaced. Estimated cost to fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
* **Turbocharger failure:** The turbocharger can fail, causing a loss of power. The turbocharger will need to be replaced. Estimated cost to fix: $2,000 - $3,000+
* **EGR valve failure:** The EGR valve can fail, causing the engine to run poorly. The EGR valve will need to be replaced. Estimated cost to fix: $500 - $1,000+

**4.0L V6 Gasoline**

* **Intake manifold gasket failure:** The intake manifold gasket can fail, causing a vacuum leak. The intake manifold gasket will need to be replaced. Estimated cost to fix: $500 - $1,000+
* **Spark plug failure:** The spark plugs can fail, causing the engine to misfire. The spark plugs will need to be replaced. Estimated cost to fix: $200 - $400+

**Transmission**

**7-Speed Automatic**

* **Shift solenoid failure:** The shift solenoid can fail, causing the transmission to shift erratically. The shift solenoid will need to be replaced. Estimated cost to fix: $500 - $1,000+
* **Torque converter failure:** The torque converter can fail, causing the transmission to slip or shudder. The torque converter will need to be replaced. Estimated cost to fix: $2,000 - $3,000+

**Suspension**

* **Front suspension strut failure:** The front suspension struts can fail, causing the vehicle to ride rough. The front suspension struts will need to be replaced. Estimated cost to fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
* **Rear suspension spring failure:** The rear suspension springs can fail, causing the vehicle to sag. The rear suspension springs will need to be replaced. Estimated cost to fix: $500 - $1,000+

**Electrical**

* **Battery failure:** The battery can fail, causing the vehicle to not start. The battery will need to be replaced. Estimated cost to fix: $200 - $400+
* **Alternator failure:** The alternator can fail, causing the battery to not charge. The alternator will need to be replaced. Estimated cost to fix: $500 - $1,000+
* **Starter failure:** The starter can fail, causing the vehicle to not start. The starter will need to be replaced. Estimated cost to fix: $500 - $1,000+

**Exterior**

* **Door handle failure:** The door handles can fail, causing the doors to not open. The door handles will need to be replaced. Estimated cost to fix: $200 - $400+
* **Tailgate lock failure:** The tailgate lock can fail, causing the tailgate to not open. The tailgate lock will need to be replaced. Estimated cost to fix: $200 - $400+

**Interior**

* **Climate control failure:** The climate control system can fail, causing the vehicle to not heat or cool properly. The climate control system will need to be repaired or replaced. Estimated cost to fix: $500 - $1,000+
* **Instrument cluster failure:** The instrument cluster can fail, causing the gauges to not work properly. The instrument cluster will need to be replaced. Estimated cost to fix: $500 - $1,000+

**Summary**

The 2015 Mercedes-Benz Sprinter 3500 is a spacious and comfortable van that has some reported issues with its engine, transmission, suspension, and electrical system. The severity of the issues can range from minor to major, and the cost to fix them can be high.
