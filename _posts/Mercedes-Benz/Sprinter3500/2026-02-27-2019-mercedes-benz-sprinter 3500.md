# 2019 Mercedes-Benz Sprinter 3500

### Overview
The 2019 Mercedes-Benz Sprinter 3500 is a robust and capable commercial van, known for its versatility and cargo capacity. As a workhorse vehicle, its reliability is paramount for businesses. While generally well-regarded, like any complex machine, it can experience specific issues. This analysis focuses on documented technical reliability concerns beyond routine maintenance, examining common problems reported by owners and technicians across its various configurations. Understanding these potential pitfalls can help owners budget for repairs and ensure the longevity of their Sprinter 3500.

---


## Engine

### 2.1L 4-Cylinder Diesel (OM651)
**Diesel Particulate Filter (DPF) Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Check engine light, reduced engine power, rough idling, increased fuel consumption.
- **Solution:** Forced regeneration, DPF cleaning, or replacement.
- **Cost:** $500 - $2000

**AdBlue System Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** AdBlue warning lights, reduced engine performance, potential inability to start.
- **Solution:** Refilling AdBlue tank, sensor replacement, or injector cleaning/replacement.
- **Cost:** $300 - $1500


### 3.0L V6 Diesel (OM642)
**Glow Plug Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Hard starting, especially in cold weather, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of faulty glow plugs and potentially the control module.
- **Cost:** $400 - $1000

**Turbocharger Problems**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of power, whistling noises, black smoke from exhaust.
- **Solution:** Turbocharger inspection, repair, or replacement.
- **Cost:** $1500 - $3000


## Transmission

### 7G-TRONIC PLUS Automatic Transmission
**Transmission Fluid Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Leaking fluid under the vehicle, shifting problems, potential overheating.
- **Solution:** Inspection and repair of seals, lines, or transmission pan.
- **Cost:** $300 - $800

**Torque Converter Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Shuddering during acceleration, slipping gears, delayed engagement.
- **Solution:** Torque converter replacement.
- **Cost:** $1500 - $3000

## Electrical

### General Electrical System
**Sensor Failures**
- **Symptoms:** Various warning lights on the dashboard (e.g., ABS, ESP, engine), erratic behavior of systems.
- **Solution:** Diagnostic scan to identify faulty sensor and replacement.
- **Cost:** $200 - $700

**Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Difficulty starting, dead battery, electrical components not functioning.
- **Solution:** Diagnostic testing for parasitic draw, battery replacement, or alternator check.
- **Cost:** $300 - $900

### Summary Verdict
The 2019 Mercedes-Benz Sprinter 3500 demonstrates good overall reliability, particularly in its chassis and body components. The primary areas of concern tend to be related to its advanced diesel powertrains, specifically the Diesel Particulate Filter (DPF) and AdBlue systems for the 4-cylinder engine, and glow plug or turbocharger issues for the V6. Transmission issues, while not rampant, can involve leaks or torque converter problems. Electrical gremlins, often manifesting as sensor failures or battery drain, are also noted. For owners, proactive maintenance and awareness of these potential issues can mitigate costs and ensure the Sprinter remains a dependable work vehicle.
