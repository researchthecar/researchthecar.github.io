# 2022 Mercedes-Benz Sprinter 3500 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2022 Mercedes-Benz Sprinter 3500, buyers are typically looking at heavy-duty workhorses or foundation platforms for luxury camper vans and RV conversions. Known for robust hauling capability and advanced driver-assist features, this commercial chassis also introduces complex electrical and stability control systems. While owner feedback is generally limited for this model year, reports submitted to safety regulators highlight a few electronic and powertrain quirks that potential buyers should inspect closely.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbo Diesel I4

This 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder diesel engine offers efficient power delivery for lighter commercial applications. It pairs with advanced emissions equipment to balance performance with fuel economy.

### 3.0L Turbo Diesel V6

The 3.0-liter turbocharged V6 diesel engine produces ample torque well-suited for heavy hauling and large upfits. It is a common powerplant found across heavy-duty utility and RV configurations.

**No-Start Condition**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers note that the vehicle dashboard lights up normally, but the engine fails to crank or start, requiring a tow to the dealer.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified technician diagnose the starting system, battery connections, or starter motor.

## Transmission

### 9-Speed Automatic Transmission

This modern nine-speed automatic transmission delivers smooth gear selection and optimizes highway efficiency. It handles heavy loads well, though proper maintenance is essential for longevity.

### 7-Speed Automatic Transmission

The seven-speed automatic transmission is paired with specific diesel configurations to deliver dependable power to the rear wheels. It generally provides smooth operation under varying cargo loads.

**Reverse Gear Engagement Failure**
- **Symptoms:** When attempting to reverse up an incline, the transmission fails to engage properly while the engine revs up, leaving the vehicle stationary.
- **Solution:** Consult a transmission specialist to check fluid levels, clutch packs, or control modules.

## Suspension

### Wheels and Tires

The chassis suspension and dual-wheel setup are engineered to manage substantial weight capacity safely.

**Incorrect Valve Stem Configuration**
- **Symptoms:** Servicing mechanics may discover improper rubber valve stems installed on dual rear inner wheels during routine maintenance.
- **Solution:** Replace improper valve stems with high-pressure metal valve stems suited for commercial dual-wheel setups.

## Brakes

### Parking Brake

The braking system provides strong stopping power necessary for a heavy vehicle platform, though electronic parking brake components require attention if they fail to release.

**Parking Brake Stuck Engaged**
- **Symptoms:** The emergency parking brake fails to disengage, rendering the vehicle completely immobile until serviced.
- **Solution:** Inspect the electronic parking brake actuator and release mechanisms for faults.

## Safety

### Crosswind Stabilization and Driver Assists

Advanced safety systems enhance highway stability in large vans, but electronic sensors can occasionally misinterpret environmental conditions.

**Inadvertent Braking and Sensor Sensitivity**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers report that heavy rain can temporarily disable adaptive cruise control and collision avoidance systems, while crosswind stabilization may abruptly apply brakes and alter vehicle steering path.
- **Solution:** Keep radar and camera sensors clean, and consult a dealer for potential software updates regarding assist system calibration.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V796000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission:Internal:Park Pawl](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=Sprinter+3500&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** Vehicle rollaway increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the software of the electronic stability, transmission control, direct select module, and electronic ignition switch control units, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V101000: Air Bags:Side/Window](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=Sprinter+3500&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** The wiring harnesses for the swivel-seats may have been incorrectly installed and may become damaged, which can cause the front side air bags not to deploy as intended. An air bag that does not deploy as intended increases the risk of injury in a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and reattach the wiring harnesses, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V204000: Electrical System: Instrument Cluster/Panel](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=Sprinter+3500&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** A blank instrument panel will not show critical information, such as vehicle speed, fuel gauge, warning messages, and warning lights, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the instrument cluster software on the affected vehicles, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V806000: Structure:Body](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=Sprinter+3500&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** The topcoat paint formula may cause the windows and interior lashing rails to be insufficiently bonded to the vehicle. An insufficiently bonded window can detach during a crash, and objects secured to an insufficiently bonded interior lashing rail can detach, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the paint adhesion and repair it as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V876000: Structure:Critical Fasteners](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=Sprinter+3500&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** The partition wall may be missing bolts, allowing the wall to detach during a crash. A detached partition wall can increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and secure the partition wall as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V930000: Electrical System: Instrument Cluster/Panel](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=Sprinter+3500&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** The instrument cluster illumination may not be adjustable and appear too bright in certain lighting conditions. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 101, "Controls and Displays." A display that is too bright can make it difficult for the driver to see critical safety.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the instrument cluster software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2022 Mercedes-Benz Sprinter 3500 is a capable and heavy-duty platform widely trusted for commercial fleets and custom builds. While most owners experience reliable service, buyers should thoroughly test electronic safety aids, check parking brake functionality, and verify that dual-wheel tire configurations have been properly maintained. A comprehensive pre-purchase inspection by a knowledgeable technician is strongly recommended before finalizing a purchase.
