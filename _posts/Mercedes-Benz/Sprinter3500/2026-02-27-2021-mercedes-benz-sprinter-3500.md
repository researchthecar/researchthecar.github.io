# 2021 Mercedes-Benz Sprinter 3500 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2021 Mercedes-Benz Sprinter 3500, buyers will find a heavy-duty commercial van built for intensive hauling and conversion work. While these vans offer robust utility and advanced features, prospective owners should review available vehicle history and address any pending manufacturer campaigns before purchase.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged I4

A 2.0-liter turbocharged gasoline engine providing standard power for lighter-duty commercial applications. It offers adequate around-town responsiveness but is less common for heavy RV upfits compared to the diesel alternatives.

### 3.0L Turbo Diesel V6

A 3.0-liter turbo diesel V6 producing around 188 horsepower and 325 lb-ft of torque, paired commonly with a 7-speed automatic transmission. It is a workhorse powertrain well-suited for heavy hauling, though occasional electronic throttle or fuel delivery quirks have been reported by owners.

**Periodic Acceleration Hesitation**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers experience a sudden lack of acceleration or a dead pedal sensation when trying to advance from a stopped position, with the van barely crawling forward.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified technician inspect the throttle body, accelerator pedal sensor, and engine management software for faults.

## Transmission

### 7-Speed Automatic

A multi-speed automatic transmission designed to optimize highway cruising and load management with the diesel engine. Regular fluid maintenance is key to long-term operational health.

## Suspension

### Front Suspension

The front suspension handles the substantial gross vehicle weight rating of the 3500 platform, though specific assembly components have been subject to quality recall oversight.

**Torsion Bar Assembly Concerns**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have received notices regarding incorrect assembly of the torsion bar, requiring dealer inspection and remedy parts.
- **Solution:** Verify with a dealer that all suspension-related manufacturer campaigns have been properly performed.

## Safety

### Air Bags

Occupant protection systems feature advanced multi-stage airbags, though sensor or software faults can occasionally trigger warning lights.

**Driver's Side Air Bag Warning**
- **Symptoms:** A dashboard warning message indicating that the driver's side air bag has been deactivated.
- **Solution:** Take the vehicle to an authorized dealer to diagnose the SRS sensor or wiring harness fault.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V513000: Seat Belts:Rear/Other:Retractor](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=Sprinter+3500&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** As such, these assemblies fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 208, "Occupant Crash Protection." An unsecured child restraint system can increase the risk of injury during a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the seat belt assemblies, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V779000: Electrical System:Wiring](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=Sprinter+3500&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** This may cause the starter-generator wiring harness to chafe against the transmission carrier, which could result in an electrical short-circuit. An electrical short can increase the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the engagement hooks on the affected cable duct with expanding rivets, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V411000: Electrical System:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=Sprinter+3500&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The MBUX multimedia system software may cause the display to remain black, or reboot after startup. This could result in the rearview camera image not displaying properly.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 21V410000: Electrical System](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=Sprinter+3500&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The wiring harnesses for the front seats may have been routed incorrectly and could become jammed and damaged in the swivel seat frames. Damage to the wiring harness can prevent the side air bag within the seat to deploy inadvertently or not deploy as designed, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will attach the mounting clip to the correct position, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V934000: Structure](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=Sprinter+3500&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** Loose bolt fasteners may allow the rear step to separate from the vehicle, becoming a road hazard and increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will tighten the bolt fasteners and replace the console and attaching hardware, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V933000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Foundation Components:Hoses, Lines/Piping, And Fittings](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=Sprinter+3500&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** A damaged brake hose may leak brake fluid, extending the distance needed to stop the vehicle and increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the condition and clearance of the brake hose, modify the fender liner trim, and replace the brake hose as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V073000: Equipment:Other:Labels](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=Sprinter+3500&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 120, "Wheels and Rims-Other Than Passenger Cars." The operator may overload the vehicle, resulting in excessive tire wear and increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace, as necessary, the tire and loading information label. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V131000: Suspension:Front:Springs:Torsion Bar](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=Sprinter+3500&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** An incorrectly assembled torsion bar may fail at the connection between the torsion bar and linkage, resulting in a loss of vehicle stability, and increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will correct the position of the shim and replace the torsion bar linkages, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2021 Mercedes-Benz Sprinter 3500 is a highly capable commercial platform, but buyers should perform a thorough inspection and verify that all open safety campaigns have been resolved. Paying close attention to powertrain responsiveness and electrical warnings during a test drive will help ensure a reliable ownership experience.
