# 2012 Mercedes-Benz Sprinter 3500

### Overview
The 2012 Mercedes-Benz Sprinter 3500 is a robust commercial van known for its utility and cargo capacity. Reliability for this model year is generally considered good, particularly for its intended heavy-duty use. However, like any vehicle, specific components can experience issues. This analysis focuses on documented technical reliability concerns beyond routine maintenance, examining common problems reported by owners and technicians across various systems. Understanding these potential weak points can help in proactive maintenance and cost management for fleet operators and individual owners.

---


## Engine

### 2.1L OM651 Diesel
**Diesel Particulate Filter (DPF) Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Check engine light, reduced engine power, increased fuel consumption, exhaust smoke.
- **Solution:** Forced regeneration, DPF cleaning, or replacement.
- **Cost:** $500 - $2500

**AdBlue System Failures**
- **Symptoms:** AdBlue warning lights, reduced engine performance, potential inability to start.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of AdBlue pump, sensors, or injector.
- **Cost:** $300 - $1200


### 3.0L OM642 Diesel
**Glow Plug Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Hard starting in cold weather, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of faulty glow plugs and relay.
- **Cost:** $200 - $600

**Turbocharger Problems**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of power, whistling noises, black smoke from exhaust.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of the turbocharger assembly.
- **Cost:** $1000 - $2500


## Transmission

### 5G-Tronic (722.6) Automatic
**Transmission Control Module (TCM) Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Harsh shifting, slipping gears, failure to engage gears, warning lights.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the TCM.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1500

**Torque Converter Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Shuddering at low speeds, slipping, delayed engagement.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the torque converter.
- **Cost:** $700 - $1400


### 6-Speed Manual
**Clutch Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Slipping clutch, difficulty shifting gears, burning smell.
- **Solution:** Replacement of clutch kit (disc, pressure plate, release bearing).
- **Cost:** $600 - $1200


## Suspension

### Standard Chassis
**Leaf Spring Breakage**
- **Symptoms:** Sagging rear end, clunking noises, uneven ride height.
- **Solution:** Replacement of broken leaf springs.
- **Cost:** $400 - $800

**Shock Absorber Leaks/Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Bouncy ride, poor handling, visible fluid leaks on shocks.
- **Solution:** Replacement of worn or leaking shock absorbers.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600


## Electrical

### General Electrical System
**Alternator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Battery warning light, dimming lights, starting problems, dead battery.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the alternator.
- **Cost:** $400 - $800

**Wiring Harness Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Intermittent electrical faults, non-functioning accessories, warning lights.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and repair/replacement of damaged wiring.
- **Cost:** $200 - $700


## Exterior

### Body Panels and Doors
**Door Hinge Rust/Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Sticking doors, difficulty opening/closing, sagging doors.
- **Solution:** Lubrication, adjustment, or replacement of hinges.
- **Cost:** $100 - $300

**Rear Door Latch Problems**
- **Symptoms:** Rear doors not latching securely, difficulty opening.
- **Solution:** Adjustment or replacement of rear door latches.
- **Cost:** $150 - $400


## Interior

### HVAC System
**Blower Motor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** No airflow from vents, inconsistent fan speed.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the blower motor or resistor.
- **Cost:** $200 - $500


### Dashboard and Controls
**Instrument Cluster Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** Erratic gauge readings, dead pixels, warning lights staying on.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of the instrument cluster.
- **Cost:** $400 - $900


--- 
### Summary Verdict
The 2012 Mercedes-Benz Sprinter 3500 demonstrates solid reliability for its class, with most reported issues stemming from the diesel engines' emissions systems (DPF, AdBlue) and common wear items on transmissions and suspension components. Electrical and interior systems can also present occasional faults, such as alternator issues or HVAC blower motor failures. While not immune to problems, proactive maintenance, especially concerning the diesel exhaust treatment systems, can mitigate many potential costly repairs. Overall, it remains a dependable workhorse when properly cared for.
