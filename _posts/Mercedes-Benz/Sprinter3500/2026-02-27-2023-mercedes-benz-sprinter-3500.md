# 2023 Mercedes-Benz Sprinter 3500 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2023 Mercedes-Benz Sprinter 3500, buyers should review potential owner-reported concerns regarding powertrain behavior and auxiliary systems. While heavy-duty commercial vans offer exceptional utility, prospective owners should pay attention to how these vehicles handle electronics, engine management, and safety features during a pre-purchase inspection.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged I4 Diesel

Equipped with a robust diesel powerplant designed for heavy hauling, this engine balances commercial capability with modern emissions equipment. Buyers should verify proper maintenance history and check for any past powertrain fault codes.

**Unintended Neutral Shift and Limp Mode**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have reported that the vehicle unexpectedly enters limp mode while driving at highway speeds, accompanied by abnormal noises or requiring a shift to neutral and engine restart to restore operation.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis by a qualified technician is required to check for fault codes, software updates, or potential sensor and transmission control module issues.

## Transmission

### 9-Speed Automatic Transmission

This multi-speed automatic transmission is engineered to optimize fuel efficiency and power delivery under heavy loads. Smooth operation across all gears is vital during a test drive.

## Electrical

### Charging and Solar Electrical System

The electrical architecture supports complex vehicle systems and auxiliary equipment. Careful inspection of aftermarket or upfitted wiring is strongly recommended for used buyers.

**Melted Fuses**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have experienced melted fuses near solar control panels, presenting a potential electrical hazard.
- **Solution:** Inspect the wiring harness, fuses, and solar charging components for signs of thermal damage or overloading, and replace damaged fuses with correctly rated components.

## Safety

### Forward Collision Avoidance

Advanced safety features enhance protection on the road but rely heavily on properly calibrated sensors and clear windshield visibility.

**Unintended Emergency Braking**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may occasionally apply hard braking unexpectedly without an apparent obstacle or collision risk in the path of travel.
- **Solution:** Have dealership technicians inspect and recalibrate the radar and camera sensors responsible for advanced driver assistance systems.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V806000: Structure:Body](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=Sprinter+3500&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** The topcoat paint formula may cause the windows and interior lashing rails to be insufficiently bonded to the vehicle. An insufficiently bonded window can detach during a crash, and objects secured to an insufficiently bonded interior lashing rail can detach, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the paint adhesion and repair it as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V849000: Suspension:Front](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=Sprinter+3500&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** The front left suspension strut bolt may not be secured properly, which can result in a loss of steering control. A loss of steering control can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the front left suspension strut bolt connection and repair the body contact surface as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V876000: Structure:Critical Fasteners](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=Sprinter+3500&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** The partition wall may be missing bolts, allowing the wall to detach during a crash. A detached partition wall can increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and secure the partition wall as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V166000: Electrical System:Wiring](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=Sprinter+3500&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** The main wiring harness may chafe against the drive shaft and short circuit, which can result in a loss of drive power, power steering, and brake system control. A short circuit increases the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the routing of the main wiring harness and correct it, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V225000: Electrical System:Wiring](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=Sprinter+3500&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** A 12-Volt ground connection may not be secured properly, which can result in the ground connection overheating and a loss of drive power. An overheated ground connection can increase the risk of a fire, and a loss of drive power can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and repair the 12-Volt ground connection, as necessary free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V517000: Suspension:Rear:Springs:Leaf Spring Assembly:U-Bolt, Leaf Spring To Axle](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=Sprinter+3500&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** An axle that moves out of position can cause a loss of vehicle control, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the spring U-bolts, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2023 Mercedes-Benz Sprinter 3500 is a capable and spacious heavy-duty platform, but used buyers should exercise due diligence. A thorough pre-purchase inspection focusing on the engine electronics, auxiliary electrical upfits, and advanced safety sensor calibration will help ensure a reliable ownership experience.
