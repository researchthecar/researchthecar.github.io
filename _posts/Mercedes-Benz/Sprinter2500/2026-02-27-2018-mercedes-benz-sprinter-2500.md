# 2018 Mercedes-Benz Sprinter 2500 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2018 Mercedes-Benz Sprinter 2500, buyers should look closely at how the van has been maintained, especially given its common commercial duty cycle. Owner reports submitted to safety regulators highlight potential concerns regarding powertrain performance, steering fluid leaks, and interior safety hardware. While the vehicle offers strong utility for conversion or delivery work, inspecting these specific trouble spots before purchase can save you from unexpected repair bills.

---

## Engine

### 2.1L 4-Cylinder Diesel Engine

The 2.1-liter twin-turbocharged 4-cylinder diesel engine produces approximately 161 horsepower and 295 lb-ft of torque, paired with a 7-speed automatic transmission. It offers solid fuel economy for fleet duties, but buyers should monitor for turbo and fueling sensor longevity.

**Motive Power Loss and Acceleration Hesitation**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have reported unexpected engine hesitation while pressing the accelerator, along with sudden losses of motive power while driving at highway speeds.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified technician diagnose the fuel delivery system, turbocharger operation, and engine management sensors.

### 3.0L V6 Diesel Engine

The 3.0-liter turbocharged V6 diesel engine generates around 188 horsepower and 325 lb-ft of torque, mated to a 5-speed automatic transmission. It provides robust towing capacity and highway performance, though complex emissions equipment requires careful inspection.

## Transmission

### 7-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 7-speed automatic transmission routes power smoothly in lighter-duty 4-cylinder models, but electronic glitches can occasionally trigger protective limp mode.

**Intermittent Limp Mode Activation**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may unexpectedly shift into limp mode while driving at various speeds without prior warning, severely limiting engine RPM and speed.
- **Solution:** Scan transmission and powertrain control modules for stored fault codes and update software or service transmission components as needed.

### 5-Speed Automatic Transmission

The traditional 5-speed automatic transmission is paired with the V6 diesel engine and is generally regarded as mechanically durable under heavy commercial loads.

## Steering

### Power Steering Hose and Clamps

The power steering system relies on high-pressure lines that require careful inspection for secure connections and fluid leaks.

**Power Steering Hose Disconnection and Fluid Leak**
- **Symptoms:** Pressurized power steering lines can loosen or disconnect at the cooler unit due to inadequate clamping or hose length, resulting in a sudden loss of steering assist.
- **Solution:** Inspect power steering lines and clamps for secure fitment and replace worn hoses or upgraded clamps immediately.

## Safety

### Seat Belts

Cabin safety restraints should be thoroughly inspected to ensure proper latching and smooth belt retraction before purchasing.

**Slow Retraction and Internal Cable Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Driver-side seat belts may become slow to retract or fail to retract fully. Additionally, internal wire strands inside the buckle receiver can fatigue and break over time.
- **Solution:** Inspect seat belt mechanisms for proper retraction and replace worn seat belt assemblies or buckle receivers if structural damage is found.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V695000: Air Bags: Air Bag/Restraint Control Module](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=Sprinter+2500&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** The airbag systems may not have been activated after vehicle production. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 208, "Occupant Crash Protection." An inactive airbag system can increase the risk of injury in the event of a crash.
- **What to do:** DVUSA will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the vehicles to determine if the airbag systems have been activated, correcting them as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V693000: Unknown Or Other](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=Sprinter+2500&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** Failure to identify loose safety related parts can increase the risk of crash and injury.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 19V507000: Visibility:Glass, Side/Rear](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=Sprinter+2500&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** In the event of a side impact crash or vehicle rollover, the fitted side windows may not adequately protect the rear occupants from being ejected, increasing their risk of injury.
- **What to do:** DVUSA will notify owners, and Mercedes-Benz or Freightliner dealers will replace the single-pane safety glass with laminated safety glass., free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V155000: Equipment:Other:Labels](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=Sprinter+2500&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** With only the metric speed listed on the spare tire label, the driver could misinterpret the kph as mph and operate the vehicle with the spare tire installed at higher speeds than the tire can withstand, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** DVUSA will notify owners, and Mercedes-Benz or Freightliner Sprinter dealers will replace it with the label with the correct maximum speed information indicated in miles per hour, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V156000: Equipment:Other:Labels](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=Sprinter+2500&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** Without the label, an operator may be unaware of the maximum speed limit of the spare tire and operate the vehicle at a excessive speed, increasing the risk of tire failure and a crash.
- **What to do:** DVUSA will notify owners, and Mercedes-Benz or Freightliner Sprinter dealers will attach the speed limitation label on the spare wheel, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V222000: Unknown Or Other](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=Sprinter+2500&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** Failure to identify loose safety related parts can increase the risk of crash and injury.
- **What to do:** DVUSA will notify owners, and dealers will complete the final quality inspection, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V743000: Latches/Locks/Linkages:Doors:Latch](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=Sprinter+2500&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** As a result, when the doors are electronically unlocked, the right-side doors may not be able to be opened from inside or outside of the vehicle. In the event of a crash, if the right side doors cannot be opened, it could increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** DVUSA will notify owners, and dealers will inspect and replace the locks if necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V665000: Latches/Locks/Linkages:Hood:Latch](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=Sprinter+2500&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** As a result, factors such as the vehicle's speed, wind resistance and road conditions may cause the hood to open while the vehicle is being driven. A hood that opens while driving can reduce the driver's visibility, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** DVUSA will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the screw connection, tightening it as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2018 Mercedes-Benz Sprinter 2500 is a versatile and capable platform for commercial fleets and camper conversions, but it demands diligent pre-purchase inspection. Buyers should pay close attention to potential diesel powertrain hesitations, transmission electronic faults, and power steering line security. Ensuring that all past maintenance has been completed will significantly reduce the risk of unexpected roadside breakdowns.
