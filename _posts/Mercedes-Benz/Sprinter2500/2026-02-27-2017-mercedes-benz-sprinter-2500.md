# 2017 Mercedes-Benz Sprinter 2500 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2017 Mercedes-Benz Sprinter 2500, buyers should look closely at how the van has been maintained, as these heavy-duty commercial workhorses often see hard miles as delivery vans or camper conversions. While general reliability heavily depends on prior service history, owner reports highlight a few specific mechanical quirks—ranging from steering hose concerns to emission sensor alerts. Keeping up with preventative maintenance is crucial to avoiding unexpected downtime.

---

## Engine

### 2.1L 4-Cylinder Turbo Diesel

The 2.1-liter four-cylinder turbodiesel engine produces approximately 161 horsepower and 295 lb-ft of torque, paired with a 7-speed automatic transmission. It offers solid fuel economy for fleet and conversion duty, though modern emissions equipment requires attentive upkeep.

**Check Engine Light / NOx Sensor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Illuminated check engine warning light, sometimes accompanied by diagnostic trouble codes related to emissions components or reduced engine efficiency.
- **Solution:** Have the diagnostic codes scanned by a qualified technician and replace faulty NOx sensors or emissions control components as needed.

### 3.0L V6 Turbo Diesel

The 3.0-liter V6 turbodiesel delivers roughly 188 horsepower and 325 lb-ft of torque, mated to a 5-speed automatic transmission. Known for robust pulling power, it is a popular choice for heavier camper builds and commercial hauling.

## Transmission

### 7-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 7-speed automatic transmission pairs with the 4-cylinder diesel engine to provide smooth highway cruising and efficient power delivery. Regular fluid and filter changes are vital for long-term durability.

### 5-Speed Automatic Transmission

The traditional 5-speed automatic transmission is paired with the V6 diesel engine, offering proven mechanical design and dependable towing capability.

**Unexpected Shifting into Neutral**
- **Symptoms:** The transmission may unexpectedly slip into neutral while driving at low speeds, resulting in a temporary loss of motive power until the driver shifts gears.
- **Solution:** Have a transmission specialist inspect the electronic valve body, control modules, and fluid condition for potential wear or software faults.

## Steering

### Power Steering Hose

The steering system provides responsive handling for a large van, but owners should regularly inspect lines and fluid levels for early signs of wear.

**Power Steering Hose Detachment**
- **Symptoms:** Sudden loss of power steering assist and fluid leaks resulting from the lower power steering hose detaching from the cooler.
- **Solution:** Inspect the power steering hose and secure it with a heavy-duty or upgraded clamp to prevent recurrence.

## Exterior

### Sliding Door Latches

Cabin and cargo area components withstand heavy commercial use well, though sliding door mechanisms benefit from periodic cleaning and lubrication.

**Cargo Sliding Door Binding**
- **Symptoms:** The side cargo sliding door may fail to open or latch properly from either the inside or the outside handle.
- **Solution:** Lubricate the track mechanism, adjust the door alignment, or replace worn latch cables and locking actuators.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V004000: Air Bags:Frontal:Passenger Side:Inflator Module](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=Sprinter+2500&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** In the event of a crash necessitating deployment of the passenger frontal air bag, these inflators may explode due to propellant degradation occurring after long-term exposure to absolute humidity and temperature cycling. An inflator explosion may result in sharp metal fragments striking the driver or other occupants resulting in serious injury or death.
- **What to do:** DVUSA will notify owners, and dealers will replace the front passenger air bag module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V003000: Air Bags:Frontal:Driver Side:Inflator Module](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=Sprinter+2500&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** In the event of a crash necessitating deployment of the driver frontal air bag, these inflators may explode due to propellant degradation occurring after long-term exposure to absolute humidity and temperature cycling. An inflator explosion may result in sharp metal fragments striking the driver or other occupants resulting in serious injury or death.
- **What to do:** DVUSA will notify owners, and dealers will replace the front driver air bag module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V288000: Structure:Body](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=Sprinter+2500&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** These vehicles may be missing spot welds on the front side wall, connecting the door portal to the hinge reinforcements in the A-pillar area, possibly affecting vehicle strength in a crash. If the missing welds affect body strength, there may be an increased risk of injury in the event of a crash.
- **What to do:** DVUSA will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the vehicles and add any missing spot welds, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V663000: Steering:Hydraulic Power Assist:Hose, Piping, And Connections](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=Sprinter+2500&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** The sudden loss of power steering assist and hydraulic steering fluid leaking onto the roadway may increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** DVUSA will notify owners, and dealers will check the return line connections of the power steering system, correcting it as needed, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V113000: Equipment:Other:Labels](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=Sprinter+2500&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** A vehicle that exceeds its maximum weight has an increased risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** DVUSA will notify owners, and dealers will replace the UVW label and inspect the vehicle to make sure that it is not overloaded, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V786000: Equipment](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=Sprinter+2500&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** These vehicles are equipped with Kidde Plastic-Handle or Push Button 'Pindicator' fire extinguishers that may become clogged, preventing the extinguisher from discharging as expected or excessive force may be needed to activate the extinguisher. Additionally, in certain models, the nozzle may detach from the valve assembly with enough force that it could.
- **What to do:** Midwest will notify owners, and dealers will replace the fire extinguisher with a different brand, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V742000: Tires:Temporary/Emergency Spare Tire](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=Sprinter+2500&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** The spare tire holder under the vehicle may not be properly secured. If the spare tire holder is not properly secured, the spare tire may separate from the vehicle and become a road hazard, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** DVUSA will notify owners and dealers will lift the spare tire holder completely and tighten the securing screws as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2017 Mercedes-Benz Sprinter 2500 is a highly capable and versatile platform, particularly favored for custom camper conversions and commercial transport. Prospective buyers should thoroughly check the vehicle's maintenance history, verify the health of the emissions and steering systems, and ensure all doors and mechanical accessories operate smoothly before purchasing.
