# 2016 Mercedes-Benz Sprinter 2500 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2016 Mercedes-Benz Sprinter 2500 is a versatile full-size van popular for commercial use and camper conversions, offering robust capability and flexible configurations. However, prospective buyers should review owner complaints regarding powertrain accessories, electrical components, and body seals carefully before purchasing.

---

## Engine

### 2.1L 4-Cylinder Diesel Engine

This 2.1-liter twin-turbocharged 4-cylinder diesel engine produces approximately 161 horsepower and 265 lb-ft of torque, paired with a 7-speed automatic transmission. It offers strong fuel efficiency for highway cruising, though owners report occasional auxiliary emissions and EGR component issues.

**EGR Valve Failure and Power Loss**
- **Symptoms:** Failing EGR valve causing intermittent power loss, hesitation, and acceleration issues at relatively low mileage.
- **Solution:** Inspect and replace the faulty EGR valve as needed.

### 3.0L V6 Diesel Engine

The 3.0-liter turbocharged V6 diesel engine generates roughly 188 horsepower and 325 lb-ft of torque, mated to a 5-speed automatic transmission. It provides robust towing and pulling power for heavier loads, though buyers should monitor for boost leaks and turbo-related wear.

**Boost Leaks and Acceleration Loss**
- **Symptoms:** Check engine light illuminates accompanied by a hissing noise during acceleration and noticeable loss of power or speed on the highway.
- **Solution:** Inspect intake plumbing, turbo hoses, and clamps for leaks or cracks, and replace damaged components.

## Transmission

### 7-Speed Automatic Transmission

Paired with the 4-cylinder diesel engine, this 7-speed automatic transmission provides smooth gear changes and optimized highway efficiency. Maintenance history and regular fluid changes are key to long-term reliability.

### 5-Speed Automatic Transmission

Matched to the V6 diesel engine, this 5-speed automatic transmission is a proven, heavy-duty unit capable of handling demanding commercial duty cycles. Routine inspection of fluid condition and cooler lines is recommended.

## Brakes

### Wheel Speed Sensors and Brake Control

Braking performance is generally strong, but intermittent sensor failures can trigger warning lights and affect stability control features.

**Wheel Speed Sensor Faults**
- **Symptoms:** Warning lights on the dashboard illuminate and stability or brake control systems may trigger unexpectedly.
- **Solution:** Diagnose the fault codes, clean or replace faulty wheel speed sensors and associated wiring.

## Steering

### Power Steering Pump and Lines

Hydraulic steering components provide responsive control for a large vehicle, but high-mileage examples should be inspected for fluid leaks and pump wear.

**Power Steering Fluid Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of power steering fluid, pump cavitation noise, and increased steering effort which could lead to loss of control if left unaddressed.
- **Solution:** Inspect steering hoses, fittings, and the pump for leaks, then replace worn lines and refill or replace the power steering pump.

## Interior

### Roof Seals and AC Units

Exterior body seals generally hold up well, though rooftop equipment installations require periodic inspection to prevent water intrusion.

**Roof-Mounted Air Conditioner Seal Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Water leaks into the cabin through the ceiling and roof during heavy rainstorms, causing interior water damage and dampness.
- **Solution:** Inspect and reseal or replace the rooftop air conditioning unit gasket.

## Safety

### Air Bag and Seat Belt Systems

Standard safety equipment is comprehensive, but owners have reported isolated incidents concerning airbag system faults and recall campaigns.

**Inadvertent Airbag Deployment**
- **Symptoms:** Passenger-side airbag deploys unexpectedly during normal low-speed driving without any impact or collision.
- **Solution:** Have the supplemental restraint system (SRS) thoroughly inspected by a qualified dealer or technician to replace deployed modules and faulty sensors.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V003000: Air Bags:Frontal:Driver Side:Inflator Module](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=Sprinter+2500&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** In the event of a crash necessitating deployment of the driver frontal air bag, these inflators may explode due to propellant degradation occurring after long-term exposure to absolute humidity and temperature cycling. An inflator explosion may result in sharp metal fragments striking the driver or other occupants resulting in serious injury or death.
- **What to do:** DVUSA will notify owners, and dealers will replace the front driver air bag module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V004000: Air Bags:Frontal:Passenger Side:Inflator Module](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=Sprinter+2500&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** In the event of a crash necessitating deployment of the passenger frontal air bag, these inflators may explode due to propellant degradation occurring after long-term exposure to absolute humidity and temperature cycling. An inflator explosion may result in sharp metal fragments striking the driver or other occupants resulting in serious injury or death.
- **What to do:** DVUSA will notify owners, and dealers will replace the front passenger air bag module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V663000: Steering:Hydraulic Power Assist:Hose, Piping, And Connections](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=Sprinter+2500&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** The sudden loss of power steering assist and hydraulic steering fluid leaking onto the roadway may increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** DVUSA will notify owners, and dealers will check the return line connections of the power steering system, correcting it as needed, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V592000: Power Train:Driveline:Driveshaft](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=Sprinter+2500&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** A loose driveshaft may strike and puncture the gas tank, resulting in a fuel leak, increasing the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** DVUSA will notify owners, and dealers will install the missing catch strap for the driveshaft, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V093000: Air Bags](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=Sprinter+2500&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** As a result, the air bag control unit may not remain functional after the initial impact, possibly preventing additional air bags from deploying in response to a secondary impact. If additional air bags do not deploy as intended, the occupants are at an increased risk of injury.
- **What to do:** DVUSA will notify owners, and dealers will install a reinforcement plate and protective bracket around the air bag control unit, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V670000: Seat Belts:Rear/Other](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=Sprinter+2500&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** The bolts connecting the lower seat belt connection to the rear bench seat may not have been properly tightened. If the bolts were not properly tightened, the seat belt may fail to secure the seat occupant in the event of a crash.
- **What to do:** DVUSA will notify owners, and Sprinter dealers will inspect the connecting bolts, tightening them as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V113000: Equipment:Other:Labels](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=Sprinter+2500&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** A vehicle that exceeds its maximum weight has an increased risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** DVUSA will notify owners, and dealers will replace the UVW label and inspect the vehicle to make sure that it is not overloaded, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V351000: Structure:Body:Roof And Pillars](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=Sprinter+2500&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** The affected vehicles are configured to have super-high roofs, but may not be equipped with the required crash pads. If the required crash pads are missing, it can increase the risk of injury to a front passenger seat occupant if they are unbelted.
- **What to do:** DVUSA will notify owners, and dealers will install the required crash-pads, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2016 Mercedes-Benz Sprinter 2500 remains a capable choice for heavy-duty hauling and camper builds, provided it has been meticulously maintained. Buyers should thoroughly inspect the vehicle for potential EGR system performance, power steering leaks, roof seal condition, and outstanding safety recalls before finalizing a purchase.
