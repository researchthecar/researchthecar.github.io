The 2022 Mercedes-Benz Sprinter 2500 is a versatile van popular for commercial use and camper conversions. The standard engine is a 2.0L inline-4 turbo diesel producing 188 hp and 325 lb-ft of torque. A gasoline 2.0L inline-4 turbo engine is also available, producing 188 hp and 258 lb-ft of torque. Both engines are paired with a 9G-TRONIC 9-speed automatic transmission. Owners often praise the Sprinter's spacious cargo area, customizable configurations, and comfortable ride. Common complaints include the high purchase price, expensive maintenance, and occasional electrical glitches.

**Engine**

**2.0L Inline-4 Turbo Diesel**

* **Diesel Exhaust Fluid (DEF) System Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: "Check Engine" light illuminated, accompanied by diagnostic trouble codes (DTCs) related to the DEF system (e.g., insufficient DEF quality, faulty DEF injector, malfunctioning DEF pump). Could lead to limp mode.
    * Solution: Diagnosis of the specific DEF system component failure. Replacement of faulty DEF injector, pump, sensor, or DEF tank heater. Ensure proper DEF fluid quality.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,500+ depending on the component.
    * Recalls: None Found

* **EGR Valve Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: Rough idling, poor acceleration, black smoke from exhaust, "Check Engine" light. Diagnostic codes related to EGR valve malfunction.
    * Solution: EGR valve replacement.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800+
    * Recalls: None Found

**2.0L Inline-4 Turbo Gasoline**

* **No Issues Found**

**Transmission**

**9G-TRONIC 9-Speed Automatic Transmission**

* **Rough or Delayed Shifting**
    * Description/Symptoms: Hesitation or jerking during gear changes, particularly between lower gears. Can also include difficulty downshifting when slowing.
    * Solution: Software updates and transmission adaptation resets may temporarily alleviate the issue. Replacement of valve body or complete transmission replacement might be required in severe cases.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 (software update) to $5,000+ (transmission replacement).
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Torque Converter Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: Shuddering at low speeds, especially when accelerating lightly, can indicate torque converter issues.
    * Solution: Replace Torque Converter
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Rear Suspension Sagging**
    * Description/Symptoms: Rear of the vehicle sits noticeably lower than the front, especially when loaded. Can cause poor handling and premature tire wear.
    * Solution: Replacement of rear leaf springs or installation of helper springs or air suspension system.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+ depending on the solution.
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **MBUX Infotainment System Glitches**
    * Description/Symptoms: System freezing, unresponsive touchscreen, Bluetooth connectivity issues, navigation problems, and random reboots.
    * Solution: Software updates. In severe cases, replacement of the MBUX head unit.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 (software update) to $2,000+ (head unit replacement).
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Sensor Failures**
    * Description/Symptoms: Malfunction of various sensors (e.g., oxygen sensors, parking sensors, blind spot monitoring sensors) leading to warning lights and system malfunctions.
    * Solution: Diagnosis of the faulty sensor and replacement.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $500+ per sensor.
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Door Lock Actuator Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Door will not lock or unlock electronically.
    * Solution: Replace Door Lock Actuator
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $400
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Sliding Door Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: Difficulty opening or closing the sliding door, binding, sticking, or excessive noise.
    * Solution: Adjustment or lubrication of the door mechanism. Replacement of worn rollers or hinges.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Rattling and Squeaking**
    * Description/Symptoms: Various interior panels and trim pieces rattling or squeaking, especially when driving on rough roads.
    * Solution: Tightening or replacing loose fasteners. Applying sound-deadening material to reduce vibrations.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $300+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

The 2022 Mercedes-Benz Sprinter 2500 has some known issues, particularly in the electrical and diesel engine components. While the gasoline engine seems to have fewer complaints, the diesel variant can suffer from DEF system and EGR valve problems, leading to potentially high repair costs. The 9-speed transmission can also exhibit shifting issues. Interior rattles and sliding door problems are common annoyances. Overall, the Sprinter can be reliable with proper maintenance, but potential buyers should be aware of these common issues and factor them into their budget. The cost to repair these issues range from $50 to $5000.
