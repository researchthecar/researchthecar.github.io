**Introduction:**

The 2022 Mercedes-Benz GLS63 AMG is a high-performance luxury SUV powered by a 4.0L twin-turbocharged V8 engine producing 603 horsepower and 627 lb-ft of torque. It comes standard with a 9-speed automatic transmission and 4MATIC all-wheel drive. The GLS63 AMG is praised for its powerful engine, luxurious interior, and outstanding driving dynamics, but it is also known for its high price and potential reliability issues.

**Engine**

**4.0L Twin-Turbocharged V8**

- **Turbocharger Failure:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, excessive smoke, oil leaks
    - Solution: Replace turbocharger
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+
    - Recalls: None Found
- **Oil Leaks:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks from various engine components
    - Solution: Inspect and repair or replace leaking components
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+
    - Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**9-Speed Automatic**

- **Shifting Issues:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Hard shifting, delayed shifts, loss of gears
    - Solution: Update transmission software, replace transmission control module
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+
    - Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

- **Adaptive Air Suspension Malfunction:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Vehicle sagging, uneven ride height, reduced handling
    - Solution: Replace air struts, repair or replace air compressor
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
    - Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

- **Battery Drain:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Battery goes dead quickly, especially after sitting overnight
    - Solution: Diagnose and repair or replace faulty electrical components
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
    - Recalls: None Found
- **Infotainment System Malfunctions:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Screen freezes, buttons unresponsive, navigation errors
    - Solution: Update software, replace infotainment module
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
    - Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

- **Door Lock Actuator Failure:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Door locks not working, unable to open or close doors
    - Solution: Replace door lock actuators
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    - Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

- **Panoramic Sunroof Failure:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Sunroof stuck open or closed, water leaks into cabin
    - Solution: Replace sunroof motor or mechanism
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
    - Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

The 2022 Mercedes-Benz GLS63 AMG faces several potential issues that can be costly to repair. These include turbocharger failures, oil leaks, and transmission shifting issues. However, it is important to note that these problems are not universal and may vary based on individual vehicles and maintenance history. Despite these issues, the GLS63 AMG remains a desirable high-performance SUV that offers exceptional driving dynamics and luxury.
