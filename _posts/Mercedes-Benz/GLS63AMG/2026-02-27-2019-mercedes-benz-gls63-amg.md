# 2019 Mercedes-Benz GLS63 AMG Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2019 Mercedes-Benz GLS63 AMG is a high-performance luxury SUV that combines immense power with opulent three-row comfort. Prospective buyers looking at a used model should note that while owner reports are relatively sparse, maintaining such an advanced performance vehicle requires diligence and a healthy budget for potential mechanical and electrical complexities.

---

## Engine

### 3.0L V6 Biturbo (GLS450 equivalent/context)

A baseline twin-turbo V6 engine offering smooth power delivery and everyday drivability, though it represents a lower performance tier compared to the AMG variants.

### 4.7L V8 Biturbo (GLS550 equivalent/context)

A robust twin-turbo V8 engine delivering strong mid-range torque and confident towing capabilities, balancing luxury cruising with substantial performance.

### 5.5L Handcrafted AMG V8 Biturbo

A potent handcrafted 5.5-liter twin-turbo V8 producing over 570 horsepower, designed for blistering performance but demanding meticulous maintenance and premium upkeep.

**Oil Pump Solenoid Valve Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Low oil pressure warnings and illuminated check engine lights appear on the dashboard.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified mechanic inspect the oil pump solenoid and replace faulty components, which may require removing major engine accessories.

## Transmission

### 9-Speed Automatic Transmission

A multi-speed automatic transmission designed to optimize fuel efficiency and smooth gear transitions across various driving conditions, though periodic fluid maintenance is critical.

### AMG Speedshift Plus 7-Speed Automatic Transmission

A high-performance 7-speed automatic tailored to handle the massive torque output of the AMG V8 engine with rapid, aggressive gear changes.

**Shift Response Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle fails to respond when attempting to shift from park into drive, reverse, or neutral, requiring multiple restarts.
- **Solution:** Consult a certified technician to diagnose electronic shifter modules or transmission control unit software.

## Suspension

### Active Curve System and Air Suspension

Advanced adaptive suspension components designed to reduce body roll and maintain a plush ride quality, though hydraulic and electronic complexities can arise as the vehicle ages.

**Active Curve System Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Dashboard warning messages indicate an active curve system malfunction, which can occasionally be accompanied by fluid leaks.
- **Solution:** Have the hydraulic lines, fluid levels, and system sensors thoroughly inspected and repaired by a specialist.

## Brakes

### Parking Brake and Sensors

Comprehensive electronic architecture managing convenience and safety features, prone to occasional sensor or actuator glitches.

**Electronic Parking Brake Assist Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** The parking brake assist warning repeatedly appears upon startup or engages unexpectedly during operation.
- **Solution:** Inspect the electronic parking brake actuators, switches, and associated control modules for faults.

## Exterior

### Sunroof and Body Glass

Exterior glass and body fixtures that generally provide a premium aesthetic but require careful inspection for stress fractures or seal degradation.

**Spontaneous Glass Shattering**
- **Symptoms:** The panoramic sunroof or windshield may suddenly crack or shatter while driving under normal conditions.
- **Solution:** Replace the damaged glass panel and inspect the frame for any structural stress points or prior impacts.

## Summary Verdict

The 2019 Mercedes-Benz GLS63 AMG delivers thrilling performance and supreme luxury, but prospective used buyers should approach with an awareness of potential high-end maintenance costs. While major mechanical failures are not overwhelmingly common in owner reports, complexities surrounding the AMG powertrain, advanced suspension systems, and electronics mean that securing a thorough pre-purchase inspection by an AMG specialist is strongly advised.
