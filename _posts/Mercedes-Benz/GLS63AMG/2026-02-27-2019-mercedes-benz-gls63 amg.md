# 2019 Mercedes-Benz GLS63 AMG

### Overview
The 2019 Mercedes-Benz GLS63 AMG, a high-performance luxury SUV, generally exhibits strong reliability, reflecting its premium engineering and robust construction. While its powerful AMG-tuned engine and sophisticated drivetrain are designed for demanding use, like any complex vehicle, certain areas can experience issues. This analysis focuses on documented, non-wear-and-tear related problems reported by owners and technicians, providing insights into potential areas of concern and their typical resolutions. The GLS63 AMG aims to balance exhilarating performance with the practicality expected of a full-size SUV, and its reliability record largely supports this ambition.

---


## Engine

### 5.5L Twin-Turbo V8 (M157)
**Turbocharger Wastegate Actuator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Reduced engine power, check engine light, whistling or grinding noises from the turbo area.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the faulty wastegate actuator or the complete turbocharger assembly.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1500

**Oil Cooler Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Visible oil leaks around the engine, oil smell, potential for oil in coolant.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the oil cooler and associated seals.
- **Cost:** $600 - $1100


## Transmission

### 7-Speed AMG SPEEDSHIFT PLUS 7G-TRONIC
**Transmission Control Module (TCM) Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Erratic shifting, failure to engage gears, transmission warning lights.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and reprogramming or replacement of the TCM.
- **Cost:** $700 - $1300

**Torque Converter Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Shuddering during acceleration, slipping gears, delayed engagement.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the torque converter.
- **Cost:** $1200 - $2000


## Suspension

### Active Curve System (Optional)
**Hydraulic Pump Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Increased body roll, warning lights related to suspension system, stiff steering.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the hydraulic pump and fluid flush.
- **Cost:** $1500 - $2500

**Active Stabilizer Bar Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Fluid leaks from suspension components, reduced effectiveness of active sway bars, clunking noises.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of leaking active stabilizer bar components.
- **Cost:** $1000 - $1800


### AIRMATIC Air Suspension
**Air Spring Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle sagging on one or more corners, compressor running excessively, harsh ride quality.
- **Solution:** Replacement of leaking air springs or air struts.
- **Cost:** $700 - $1400 per corner


## Electrical

### Infotainment System
**COMAND System Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Screen freezing, unresponsive touch controls, audio cutting out, navigation errors.
- **Solution:** Software updates, or replacement of the COMAND head unit.
- **Cost:** $500 - $2000


### Battery Drain
**Parasitic Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Dead battery after short periods of inactivity, electrical components malfunctioning intermittently.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis to find the source of the drain (e.g., faulty module, aftermarket accessory) and repair.
- **Cost:** $300 - $800

### Summary Verdict
The 2019 Mercedes-Benz GLS63 AMG offers a compelling blend of luxury and performance, with a generally solid reliability record. The primary areas that may require attention are related to its high-performance components, such as the twin-turbo V8 engine's turbochargers and oil cooler, and the sophisticated AMG transmission. The optional Active Curve System and standard AIRMATIC suspension can also present unique repair needs, particularly concerning hydraulic components and air springs. Electrical issues, mainly with the COMAND infotainment system and occasional battery drain, are also noted. While not immune to problems, most issues are repairable, though costs can be significant due to the vehicle's premium nature and complexity. Regular maintenance and prompt attention to warning signs are crucial for maintaining the GLS63 AMG's performance and longevity.
