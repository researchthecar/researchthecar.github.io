# 2017 Mercedes-Benz S63 AMG Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2017 Mercedes-Benz S63 AMG represents the pinnacle of luxury paired with high-performance engineering, offering buyers immense power and sophisticated styling. However, prospective buyers of this flagship sedan should carefully evaluate its complex systems and potentially high maintenance costs. While owner complaints are relatively scarce given the volume of advanced electronics and mechanical parts, reviewing specific recurring pain points can help ensure a more confident pre-owned purchase.

---

## Engine

### 4.0L Twin-Turbo V8

The 4.0-liter twin-turbocharged V8 engine delivers robust horsepower and deep low-end torque for effortless acceleration. Regular preventative maintenance and timely servicing are vital to maintaining its high-performance longevity.

### 5.5L Twin-Turbo V8

The hand-built 5.5-liter bi-turbo V8 produces immense output, providing staggering straight-line performance for the luxury sedan. Proper thermal management and rigorous oil change intervals are essential for trouble-free operation.

## Transmission

### AMG Speedshift MCT 7-Speed Automatic

The 7-speed multi-clutch automatic transmission offers lightning-fast gear changes tailored for aggressive driving as well as smooth cruising. Routine fluid changes help maintain crisp shift quality and mechanical reliability.

### AMG Speedshift MCT 9-Speed Automatic

The 9-speed multi-clutch automatic transmission optimizes power delivery and highway efficiency across diverse driving modes. It provides seamless gear selection, though software updates may occasionally be required to address shift behavior.

## Suspension

### Wheels

Large alloy wheels contribute to the vehicle's aggressive stance and handling precision, but low-profile tires make them susceptible to damage from road imperfections.

**Cracking AMG Wheels**
- **Symptoms:** Factory AMG rear alloy wheels develop structural cracks under normal driving conditions, leading to slow air leaks or pressure loss.
- **Solution:** Replace the damaged factory rims with structural replacements or aftermarket wheels better suited to rough road surfaces.

## Steering

### Steering Column

Power-adjustable steering columns provide effortless customization of the driving position, though electronic motors can occasionally fail.

**Power Steering Column Tilt Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The steering wheel adjusts telescopically in and out, but the up-and-down tilt function becomes stuck in the raised position, impairing driver visibility and comfort.
- **Solution:** Inspect and replace the steering column adjustment motor or associated switch assembly.

## Interior

### Dashboard Trim

The cabin features handcrafted leather and premium materials that generally age well if treated properly, though specific trim elements require monitoring.

**Dashboard Leather Separation**
- **Symptoms:** The leather on the dashboard begins to pull away or warp around the passenger and driver side airbag deployment zones, potentially exposing internal components.
- **Solution:** Requires professional adhesive repair or complete dashboard replacement, which can involve significant out-of-pocket expenses.
- **Cost:** $3,000 to $6,000

## Summary Verdict

The 2017 Mercedes-Benz S63 AMG is a masterpiece of luxury and high-performance engineering, but prospective buyers should approach with careful inspection. While major powertrain failures are uncommon in owner reports, buyers should inspect the condition of the interior leather dashboard, structural wheel integrity, and electronic steering adjustments. Securing a comprehensive pre-purchase inspection from an independent specialist can mitigate unexpected repair expenses.
