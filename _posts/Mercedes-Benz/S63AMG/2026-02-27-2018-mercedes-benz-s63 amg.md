## Suspension

### AIRMATIC Air Suspension
**Air Strut Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle sagging on one corner, loss of ride height, 'Service Air Suspension' warning light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of leaking air strut or air spring assembly.
- **Cost:** $1,200 - $2,500 per corner

**Air Compressor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Inability to raise or lower the vehicle, constant running of the compressor, 'Service Air Suspension' warning light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the air suspension compressor unit.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1,500


## Electrical

### Infotainment System
**Software Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Screen freezing, unresponsive touch controls, intermittent audio issues, navigation system errors.
- **Solution:** Software updates, system resets, or potential module replacement.
- **Cost:** $150 - $800


### Battery Drain
**Parasitic Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Battery dying prematurely, difficulty starting the vehicle, electrical components malfunctioning.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis to identify the source of the drain (e.g., faulty module, aftermarket accessory) and repair or replacement.
- **Cost:** $300 - $1,000+

### Summary Verdict
The 2018 Mercedes-Benz S63 AMG demonstrates a commendable level of reliability for a high-performance luxury vehicle. The powertrain, including the 4.0L Biturbo V8 engine and the 9-speed automatic transmission, is generally robust with no widespread reported issues. The primary areas where owners might encounter technical challenges are related to the sophisticated AIRMATIC air suspension system, which can experience leaks or compressor failures, and occasional electrical gremlins such as infotainment software glitches or parasitic battery drain. These issues, while potentially costly to repair, are not indicative of systemic failures across the vehicle. Overall, the S63 AMG offers a reliable and exhilarating ownership experience, provided potential buyers are aware of and prepared for the maintenance needs of its advanced systems.
