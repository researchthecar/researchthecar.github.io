The 2011 Mercedes-Benz S63 AMG is a high-performance luxury sedan known for its powerful engine, opulent interior, and advanced technology. While it offers a thrilling driving experience, potential owners should be aware of certain common issues. The S63 AMG was primarily offered with a 5.5-liter twin-turbo V8 engine, which produces 536 horsepower and 590 lb-ft of torque. Some enjoy the car's immense power and luxurious features, while others dislike the high running costs and potential maintenance issues.

**Engine**

**5.5L Twin-Turbo V8 (M157)**

*   **Valve Cover Gasket Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks from the valve cover gaskets, leading to a burning smell and potential oil loss.
    *   Solution: Replace the valve cover gaskets.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $600 - $1200+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Turbocharger Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Whining noises, reduced power, and potential turbo failure.
    *   Solution: Replace or rebuild the turbochargers.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $3000 - $6000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Direct Injector Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rough idling, misfires, and check engine light.
    *   Solution: Replace the faulty direct fuel injectors.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800+ per injector
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Oil Leaks (Various Locations)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks originating from the oil filter housing, oil cooler lines, or other areas of the engine.
    *   Solution: Identify the source of the leak and replace the corresponding seals or components.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1500+ (depending on location and complexity)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**AMG Speedshift MCT 7-Speed**

*   **Torque Converter Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Shuddering during acceleration, harsh shifts, and potential transmission slipping.
    *   Solution: Replace the torque converter.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $2000 - $4000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Valve Body Malfunctions**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Erratic shifting, delayed engagement, and transmission warning lights.
    *   Solution: Replace or rebuild the valve body.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1500 - $3000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Airmatic System Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Vehicle sagging, uneven ride height, and compressor failure.
    *   Solution: Replace the faulty air struts, compressor, or air lines.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $2500+ per strut; $500 - $1500+ for compressor
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Battery Drain**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Frequent battery drain, especially when the vehicle is not in use.
    *   Solution: Diagnose and repair parasitic draws from faulty modules or components.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $1000+ (depending on the source of the drain)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **COMAND System Malfunctions**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Freezing, lagging, or complete failure of the COMAND infotainment system.
    *   Solution: Software updates or replacement of the COMAND unit.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $2000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Headlight Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Adaptive headlight malfunctions or LED failures.
    *   Solution: Replace the faulty headlight unit or individual components.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1000 - $3000+ per headlight
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Sunroof Malfunctions**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Sunroof getting stuck, making unusual noises, or failing to open or close properly.
    *   Solution: Diagnose and repair or replace the sunroof motor, tracks, or associated components.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2011 Mercedes-Benz S63 AMG, while a powerful and luxurious vehicle, has some common issues that prospective buyers should be aware of. Engine issues, especially those related to the turbochargers and valve cover gaskets, can be costly to repair. Airmatic suspension and transmission problems can also lead to significant expenses. Electrical issues such as battery drain are also problems to be aware of. Overall, the S63 AMG has a reputation for requiring regular maintenance and can be costly to keep in top condition.
