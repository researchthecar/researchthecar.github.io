# 2017 Mercedes-Benz B250e

### Overview
The 2017 Mercedes-Benz B250e, a compact electric hatchback, aimed to blend practicality with premium features. As an electric vehicle (EV), its powertrain differs significantly from traditional internal combustion engine (ICE) vehicles, focusing on battery and electric motor performance. Reliability data for EVs often centers on battery health, charging systems, and the longevity of electric motor components. While generally considered robust, specific issues can arise, particularly with early EV technology. This analysis focuses on documented technical reliability concerns beyond standard maintenance, providing insights into potential problem areas for owners and prospective buyers.

## Engine

### Electric Powertrain
**Battery Degradation**
- **Symptoms:** Reduced range, slower charging times, loss of overall power.
- **Solution:** Battery replacement (expensive) or software updates to manage performance.
- **Cost:** $5,000 - $15,000+

**Electric Motor Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Whining noises, loss of power, intermittent operation.

## Electrical

### Charging System
**Charging Port Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Inability to charge, intermittent charging connection.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of charging port assembly.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1,500

**Onboard Charger Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle not charging, error messages related to charging.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the onboard charger unit.
- **Cost:** $1,000 - $3,000

### Summary Verdict
The 2017 Mercedes-Benz B250e, as an early electric vehicle from a luxury brand, exhibits a reliability profile distinct from its gasoline counterparts. The primary concerns revolve around the electric powertrain, specifically battery degradation over time and potential electric motor issues, which can represent significant repair costs. The charging system, including the port and onboard charger, has also seen some reported malfunctions. While the transmission, suspension, exterior, and interior components generally appear robust with no widespread critical failures, owners should be particularly mindful of battery health and charging infrastructure compatibility. Regular diagnostics and adherence to manufacturer recommendations for battery care are advised.
