# 2017 Mercedes-Benz B250e Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2017 Mercedes-Benz B250e is an all-electric hatchback that offers a unique entry into the luxury EV market, sharing its powertrain roots with Tesla's early partnership era. Prospective used car buyers will find that while the electric drivetrain provides smooth and quiet daily commuting, researching overall vehicle history and inspecting secondary systems is still essential before purchase.

---

## Engine

### Electric Drive Motor

The B250e utilizes a front-mounted electric motor producing approximately 177 horsepower and 251 lb-ft of torque, powered by a lithium-ion battery pack. This electric drive unit delivers brisk acceleration and strong reliability, though battery capacity degradation should be evaluated on used models.

## Transmission

### Single-Speed Direct Drive Transmission

Power is sent to the front wheels via a single-speed fixed-ratio direct-drive transmission. This setup lacks traditional gears, resulting in seamless power delivery and very few mechanical complications.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V229000: Steering](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=B250e&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** The steering shaft bore hole for the universal joint bearing may allow excessive movement that, over time, could result in the separation of the bearing and universal joint. A separated universal joint bearing can cause a loss of steering control and increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** MBUSA will notify owners, and dealers will inspect, and as necessary, replace the steering shaft, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V058000: Electrical System:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=B250e&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** The software design of the communication module may fail to communicate the correct vehicle location for the emergency call system (eCall) in the event of a crash. Failure of the eCall system may result in emergency responders being dispatched to the wrong location, increasing the risk of injury following a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.
