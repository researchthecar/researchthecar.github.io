**2001 Mercedes-Benz CL600**

Other engine options: None
Other transmission options: None

The Mercedes-Benz CL600 is a high-performance luxury coupe known for its powerful V12 engine and advanced features. It produces 362 horsepower and 530 lb-ft of torque, providing impressive acceleration and handling. Its luxurious interior and sophisticated design are also highly praised. However, like any vehicle, the CL600 has some known issues that owners should be aware of.

**Engine**

**M137/M275 5.8L V12**

* **Head gasket failure:** Premature failure of the head gasket, leading to coolant and oil leaks. Symptoms include overheating, white smoke from the exhaust, and a loss of coolant. Requires replacement of the head gaskets and related components. Estimated cost to fix: $2,500 - $4,000+
* **Timing chain tensioner failure:** A faulty timing chain tensioner can lead to excessive chain noise and, if not addressed, catastrophic engine failure. Symptoms include a rattling noise from the engine and decreased performance. Requires replacement of the timing chain tensioner and related components. Estimated cost to fix: $2,000 - $3,000+
* **Valve stem seal failure:** Worn or damaged valve stem seals can cause excessive oil consumption and blue smoke from the exhaust. Requires replacement of the valve stem seals and related components. Estimated cost to fix: $1,000 - $2,000+

**Transmission**

* **No Issues Found**

**Suspension**

* **Airmatic suspension failure:** The airmatic suspension system can develop leaks or other faults, leading to uneven ride height and impaired handling. Requires replacement of air bladders, sensors, or other components. Estimated cost to fix: $1,500 - $3,000+

**Electrical**

* **Battery drain:** Excessive battery drain can occur due to faulty electronic components or a failing alternator. Symptoms include difficulty starting the vehicle, dim lights, and other electrical malfunctions. Requires diagnosis and repair or replacement of the affected components. Estimated cost to fix: $500 - $1,500+
* **Control unit failure:** The various electronic control units in the CL600 can malfunction, leading to various issues such as engine management problems, transmission issues, and other electrical malfunctions. Requires diagnosis and replacement of the faulty control unit. Estimated cost to fix: $1,000 - $2,500+

**Exterior**

* **Door lock actuator failure:** The door lock actuators can fail, preventing the doors from locking or unlocking properly. Symptoms include difficulty locking or unlocking the doors, and a warning light on the dashboard. Requires replacement of the affected actuator. Estimated cost to fix: $200 - $500+
* **Headlight condensation:** Condensation can accumulate inside the headlight assemblies, causing fogging or impaired lighting performance. Requires replacement of the headlight assemblies. Estimated cost to fix: $500 - $1,000+

**Interior**

* **Navigation system failure:** The navigation system can malfunction, resulting in incorrect directions or a loss of functionality. Requires software updates or replacement of the navigation unit. Estimated cost to fix: $500 - $1,500+
* **Climate control system failure:** The climate control system can experience various issues, including inoperative fans, temperature malfunctions, or air conditioning compressor failure. Requires diagnosis and repair or replacement of the affected components. Estimated cost to fix: $500 - $2,000+

**Summary**

Overall, the 2001 Mercedes-Benz CL600 is a powerful and luxurious vehicle, but it is not without its known issues. The engine and electrical components are known to have several potential problems, which can be costly to repair. The suspension and exterior components can also experience faults, but the transmission has a good reliability record. Potential buyers should be aware of these potential issues and factor the potential repair costs into their decision-making process.
