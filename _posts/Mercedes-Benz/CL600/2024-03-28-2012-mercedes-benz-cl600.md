**Introduction**

The 2012 Mercedes-Benz CL600 is a high-performance luxury coupe powered by a twin-turbocharged 5.5-liter V8 engine. It boasts 518 horsepower and 612 lb-ft of torque, and comes equipped with a 7-speed automatic transmission. The CL600 is praised for its powerful engine, luxurious cabin, and advanced technology, but some potential issues have been reported.

**Engine**

* **5.5L Twin-Turbocharged V8**

**Intake Manifold Runner Malfunction**
* Description/Symptoms: Malfunctioning intake manifold runners can cause reduced engine power, rough idling, and increased emissions.
* Solution: Replacing the intake manifold runners
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
* Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

* **7-Speed Automatic**

**Torque Converter Shudder**
* Description/Symptoms: A shuddering sensation when accelerating from a stop or low speeds indicates a faulty torque converter.
* Solution: Replacing the torque converter
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+
* Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

**No Issues Found**

**Electrical**

* **Battery Discharge Issue**
* Description/Symptoms: The battery discharges prematurely, leading to difficulty starting the vehicle or electrical system failures.
* Solution: Replacing the battery or alternator
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+
* Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Sunroof Mechanism Failure**
* Description/Symptoms: The sunroof may fail to open or close properly, or get stuck in an open position.
* Solution: Replacing the sunroof motor or mechanism
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
* Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Center Console Trim Cracking**
* Description/Symptoms: The plastic trim on the center console may crack or break over time.
* Solution: Replacing the center console trim
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
* Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

While the 2012 Mercedes-Benz CL600 is a powerful and luxurious vehicle, it has some potential issues that can affect its performance, reliability, and comfort. Some of these issues, such as the intake manifold runner malfunction and torque converter shudder, can be costly to repair. However, most issues are relatively minor and can be addressed if detected early on.
