The 2006 Mercedes-Benz CL600 is a luxurious grand tourer known for its powerful engine, sophisticated features, and comfortable ride. The CL600, in particular, features a twin-turbocharged 5.5L V12 engine producing 493 horsepower and 590 lb-ft of torque, paired with a 5-speed automatic transmission. Other engine options available for the 2006 CL-Class include a 5.0L V8 in the CL500 and a supercharged 5.4L V8 in the CL55 AMG. Owners often praise the CL600 for its smooth ride, impressive performance, and luxurious interior, but some common complaints include high maintenance costs and the complexity of the vehicle's systems, which can lead to expensive repairs.

**Engine**

**5.5L Twin-Turbo V12 (M275)**

*   **Coil Pack Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Misfires, rough idling, engine hesitation, and check engine light illumination.
    *   Solution: Replace faulty coil pack(s).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+ per coil pack.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Valve Cover Gasket Leak**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks onto the exhaust manifolds, causing a burning smell.
    *   Solution: Replace valve cover gaskets.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Turbocharger Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Whining or whistling sound from the turbochargers, loss of power, and possible oil leaks.
    *   Solution: Repair or replace the turbochargers.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $3000 - $6000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **ABC (Active Body Control) Pump Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: ABC warning light illumination, suspension sagging, and poor ride quality. The ABC pump is crucial for the hydraulic suspension system.
    *   Solution: Replace the ABC pump.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1500 - $3000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **ABC (Active Body Control) Strut Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: ABC warning light illumination, suspension sagging, poor ride quality, hydraulic fluid leaks.
    *   Solution: Replace the ABC strut(s).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1500 - $3000+ per strut
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**5-Speed Automatic (722.6)**

*   **Conductor Plate Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Erratic shifting, transmission stuck in gear, or failure to shift.
    *   Solution: Replace the conductor plate inside the transmission.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Transmission Fluid Leak**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Reddish fluid leaking from the transmission, difficulty shifting, and slipping gears.
    *   Solution: Replace the transmission pan gasket or the transmission fluid cooler lines.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **ABC (Active Body Control) System Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: ABC warning light illumination, suspension sagging, and poor ride quality. The ABC system is prone to leaks and failures.
    *   Solution: Diagnose and repair specific components, such as the pump, struts, or valve blocks.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies greatly depending on the component. $500 - $3000+ per component.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Battery Drain**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Car battery drains quickly, especially when the car is not driven frequently.
    *   Solution: Identify and address the source of the parasitic drain. Common causes include faulty modules or aftermarket accessories.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Instrument Cluster Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Pixels missing on the instrument cluster display, or complete failure of the cluster.
    *   Solution: Repair or replace the instrument cluster.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **SAM (Signal Acquisition Module) Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Various electrical malfunctions, such as lighting issues, windshield wipers not working, or problems with the central locking system.
    *   Solution: Replace the faulty SAM unit.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Sunroof Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Sunroof failing to open or close properly, getting stuck, or making excessive noise.
    *   Solution: Repair or replace the sunroof mechanism or motor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Door Lock Actuator Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Door fails to lock or unlock properly.
    *   Solution: Replace the door lock actuator.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $700+ per door.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Seat Motor Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Seats failing to adjust properly or making grinding noises.
    *   Solution: Repair or replace the seat motor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+ per seat.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Navigation System Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Navigation system malfunctioning or displaying outdated maps.
    *   Solution: Update the navigation system software or replace the navigation unit.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2006 Mercedes-Benz CL600, while offering impressive performance and luxury, is known for its high maintenance costs and complex systems. The engine and suspension, particularly the ABC system, are prone to issues that can lead to expensive repairs. Electrical gremlins are also common. Prospective buyers should be prepared for potentially high repair bills and consider a thorough pre-purchase inspection to identify any existing or potential problems. The severity of these issues is generally moderate to high, and the cost to fix them can range from a few hundred dollars for minor repairs to several thousand dollars for major component replacements.
