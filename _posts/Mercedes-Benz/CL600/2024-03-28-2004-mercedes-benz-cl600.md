**Introduction:**

The 2004 Mercedes-Benz CL600 is a luxurious grand tourer with outstanding performance. It features a 5.5L V12 engine that produces 493 horsepower and 590 lb-ft of torque, paired with a 5-speed automatic transmission. The CL600 is highly praised for its opulent interior, exceptional handling, and powerful engine. However, it is not without potential issues.

**Engine:**

**5.5L V12**

* **Engine Valve Stem Seal Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Excessive oil consumption and blue smoke from the exhaust during acceleration.
    * Solution: Replace valve stem seals.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,000+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Intake Manifold Tuning Flap Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Rattling noise from the engine compartment, reduced engine power.
    * Solution: Replace intake manifold tuning flaps.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $1,500
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission:**

**5-speed Automatic**

* **Torque Converter Lock-Up Clutch Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Shuddering or vibration during acceleration or gear changes.
    * Solution: Replace torque converter lock-up clutch.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,200
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension:**

* **Air Suspension Compressor Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Vehicle sags or fails to raise to proper height, excessive noise from suspension system.
    * Solution: Replace air suspension compressor.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,000
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Rear Control Arm Bushing Wear:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Loose or rattling rear suspension, uneven tire wear.
    * Solution: Replace worn control arm bushings.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500 per bushing

**Electrical:**

* **Battery Discharge:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Battery drains quickly, difficulty starting the vehicle.
    * Solution: Replace battery or diagnose and repair electrical system.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Central Locking System Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Doors fail to lock or unlock using the remote or door handle.
    * Solution: Repair or replace central locking system components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior:**

* **Headlight Condensation:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Moisture accumulation inside headlight assembly.
    * Solution: Replace headlight assembly or reseal existing assembly.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000 per headlight
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior:**

* **Navigation System Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Navigation system screen goes black or displays errors.
    * Solution: Update software or replace navigation system components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $1,500
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

The 2004 Mercedes-Benz CL600 is a highly capable vehicle, but it can experience some significant issues. Engine valve stem seal failure and transmission torque converter lock-up clutch failure are among the most common and expensive repairs. Suspension issues, such as air suspension compressor failure and control arm bushing wear, are also prevalent. Electrical problems, including battery discharge and central locking system failure, can be frustrating and costly to resolve. Interior concerns, like navigation system failure, are generally less severe but still require attention and expense. Overall, the CL600 is a refined and powerful vehicle, but potential repairs can be costly and time-consuming.
