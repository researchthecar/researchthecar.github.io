### AIR BODY CONTROL (Optional)
**Air Spring Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle sagging on one corner, loss of ride height, compressor running excessively.
- **Solution:** Replacement of leaking air spring or associated seals. Compressor may need service or replacement if overworked.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1500 per corner


## Electrical

### Infotainment System (MBUX)
**Software Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Screen freezing, unresponsive touch controls, intermittent connectivity issues (Bluetooth, Apple CarPlay/Android Auto).
- **Solution:** Software updates, system resets. In rare cases, module replacement.
- **Cost:** $100 - $500


### 12V Battery
**Premature Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Difficulty starting, dashboard warning lights, electrical system malfunctions.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the 12V auxiliary battery.
- **Cost:** $200 - $400

### LED Headlights
**Condensation**
- **Symptoms:** Moisture buildup inside headlight housing.
- **Solution:** Inspection for seal integrity, potential resealing or replacement of headlight assembly if severe.
- **Cost:** $100 - $300 (for resealing/minor repair), $1000+ (for full assembly replacement)

### Climate Control System
**Actuator Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** Air not directing correctly (e.g., only blowing from defroster), inconsistent temperature.
- **Solution:** Replacement of faulty HVAC blend door actuators.
- **Cost:** $200 - $500

### Summary Verdict
The 2022 Mercedes-Benz E200 demonstrates strong technical reliability, with the standard powertrain and core components showing minimal widespread issues. Owners should be aware of potential, though not common, problems with optional air suspension systems, infotainment software glitches, and the longevity of the 12V auxiliary battery. Minor concerns like headlight condensation and climate control actuator issues are also noted but are generally repairable without excessive cost. Overall, the E200 is a dependable luxury sedan, with most reported issues being isolated incidents or related to specific optional features.
