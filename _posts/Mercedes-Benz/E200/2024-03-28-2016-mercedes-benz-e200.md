**Introduction:**

The 2016 Mercedes-Benz E200 is a luxury sedan powered by a 2.0L turbocharged inline-4 engine mated to a 9-speed automatic transmission. With 184 horsepower and 221 lb-ft of torque, it offers a balanced blend of performance and efficiency. The E200 boasts a sophisticated exterior with LED headlights, a panoramic sunroof, and stylish alloy wheels. However, it has drawn some criticism for its cramped rear seat and unintuitive infotainment system.

**Engine:**

* **2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4**

**Transmission:**

* **9-Speed Automatic**

**Suspension:**

* **No Issues Found**

**Electrical:**

* **Battery Drain:** The battery drains prematurely, especially after the vehicle has been parked for an extended period.
    * Solution: Replacing the affected battery.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300+
* **Alternator Failure:** The alternator may fail, resulting in electrical system malfunctions and a dead battery.
    * Solution: Replacing the alternator.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
* **Engine Control Unit (ECU) Software Issues:** The ECU may experience software glitches, leading to engine performance problems.
    * Solution: Updating the ECU software.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: Free (if covered under warranty) - $500+
* **Keyless Entry System Malfunction:** The keyless entry system may fail to unlock the doors or start the vehicle.
    * Solution: Replacing the keyless entry module or key fob.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+

**Exterior:**

* **Door Lock Issues:** The door locks may malfunction, making it difficult to open or close the doors.
    * Solution: Lubricating or replacing the door lock mechanisms.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $200+

**Interior:**

* **Climate Control Malfunction:** The climate control system may malfunction, leading to issues with heating or cooling.
    * Solution: Replacing the climate control module or actuators.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
* **Infotainment Screen Issues:** The infotainment screen may experience glitches, such as freezing or unresponsive touch input.
    * Solution: Software update or replacing the infotainment unit.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: Free (if covered under warranty) - $1,000+

**Summary:**

Overall, the 2016 Mercedes-Benz E200 has a moderate number of issues across various components. The most common problems include battery drain, alternator failures, ECU software glitches, and electrical system malfunctions. The severity of these issues can range from minor inconveniences to more significant repairs that may affect drivability. Repair costs can be expensive, especially for electrical and electronic components. It is advisable to research potential issues thoroughly and consider extended warranty coverage to mitigate the financial burden of repairs.
