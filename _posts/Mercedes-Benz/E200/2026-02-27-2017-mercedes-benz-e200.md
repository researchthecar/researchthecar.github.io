# 2017 Mercedes-Benz E200 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2017 Mercedes-Benz E-Class brings luxury, advanced technology, and refined performance to the midsize executive sedan market. While it offers a sophisticated driving experience, used-car buyers should carefully evaluate powertrain reliability, electrical systems, and known owner-reported issues when shopping for a pre-owned model.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4

The 2.0-liter turbocharged inline-4 engine produces approximately 241 horsepower and 273 lb-ft of torque, providing responsive acceleration and efficiency for daily driving. It balances luxury performance with everyday practicality, though routine inspection of fuel and ventilation lines is recommended.

**Fuel Leak or Odor**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers report noticing a strong smell of gasoline around the vehicle or inside the cabin during startup or operation.
- **Solution:** Inspect fuel lines, pumps, and high-pressure connections for leaks or cracks, and replace damaged components as necessary.

**Crankcase Ventilation Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Illuminated check engine lights and diagnostic trouble codes such as P052E can occur, sometimes accompanied by rough running or reduced engine performance.
- **Solution:** Replace the failed crankcase ventilation components and clear associated diagnostic trouble codes.
- **Cost:** $1,700

## Transmission

### 9G-TRONIC 9-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 9G-TRONIC 9-speed automatic transmission delivers smooth gear transitions and optimizes fuel economy across rear-wheel-drive and 4MATIC configurations. While generally robust, prospective buyers should test all gear selections and check for smooth engagement.

**Unintended Neutral Shift and Shifting Delays**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may inadvertently shift into neutral while coming to a stop, or fail to engage drive or reverse properly, displaying service messages.
- **Solution:** Inspect the electronic gear selector module and software calibration; replace faulty shift modules where required.

**Transmission Slipping and Rough Shifting**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers experience harsh jerking motions when shifting between gears or hesitation and slipping during acceleration.
- **Solution:** Perform a transmission diagnostic check to evaluate control module software and internal clutch pack condition.

## Steering

### Power Steering System

The electronic power steering system provides light and accurate handling feedback at various speeds, though electrical assist issues have been noted by some owners.

**Steering Assist Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers experience intermittent loss of power steering functionality, requiring increased physical effort to turn the steering wheel, accompanied by dashboard warning messages.
- **Solution:** Inspect the electronic power steering control module, wiring harness, and assist motor for faults.

## Electrical

### Electrical and Sunroof Systems

Electrical architecture supports numerous cabin amenities and driver displays, but rare reports of environmental leaks and glass panel stress warrant thorough inspection.

**Panoramic Sunroof Shattering**
- **Symptoms:** The panoramic glass roof panel can spontaneously fracture or shatter while driving without external impact, allowing water to enter the cabin.
- **Solution:** Replace the damaged glass panel and inspect interior electronics or headliners for water intrusion damage.

## Safety

### Air Bag and Restraints

Advanced safety restraint systems help protect occupants, though sensor or wiring faults can occasionally trigger warning lights requiring professional diagnosis.

**Air Bag Warning Light**
- **Symptoms:** The airbag warning light illuminates on the instrument cluster due to squib circuit malfunctions, open wiring, or passenger seat weight-sensing control unit faults.
- **Solution:** Scan diagnostic codes to identify faulty clock springs, seat sensors, or wiring harnesses and replace defective parts.

## Summary Verdict

The 2017 Mercedes-Benz E-Class offers an exceptional blend of luxury, technology, and refined driving dynamics, making it an appealing used luxury sedan option. However, buyers should pay close attention to potential transmission shifting quirks, steering assist operation, and fuel or crankcase ventilation maintenance history. A thorough pre-purchase inspection by a qualified European car specialist is strongly recommended to ensure all electronic modules and mechanical systems are operating correctly before purchase.
