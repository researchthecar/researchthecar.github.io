# 2019 Mercedes-Benz E200

### Overview
The 2019 Mercedes-Benz E200, part of the W213 generation, is generally regarded as a reliable executive sedan, blending luxury with advanced technology. It typically features a 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine paired with a 9-speed automatic transmission. While Mercedes-Benz vehicles are known for their sophisticated engineering, like any complex machine, some specific areas may experience issues over time. This report focuses on documented technical reliability concerns beyond routine maintenance, aiming to provide a clear picture for owners and potential buyers.

---


## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4 (M274/M264)
**Oil Leaks (Valve Cover Gasket)**
- **Symptoms:** Visible oil residue around the top of the engine, potential oil smell, low oil level warnings.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the valve cover gasket.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600

**PCV System Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Rough idle, increased oil consumption, potential whistling noises from the engine bay.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the Positive Crankcase Ventilation (PCV) valve or system components.
- **Cost:** $200 - $500

## Electrical

### Infotainment System
**Software Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Screen freezing, unresponsiveness, intermittent audio issues, navigation system errors.
- **Solution:** Software updates, system resets, or potential module replacement.
- **Cost:** $150 - $700


### Battery Drain
**Parasitic Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Battery dying prematurely, difficulty starting the vehicle, warning lights related to battery or electrical system.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis to identify the source of the drain (e.g., faulty module, aftermarket accessory) and repair or replacement.
- **Cost:** $200 - $800

### Summary Verdict
The 2019 Mercedes-Benz E200 demonstrates strong overall reliability, with its powertrain components generally performing well. The most commonly reported technical issues tend to be related to the electrical system, particularly infotainment software glitches and occasional battery drain, which can be resolved with diagnostics and software updates. Minor engine oil leaks from the valve cover gasket and PCV system issues are also noted but are not typically indicative of catastrophic failure. Owners should be aware of these potential points and ensure regular servicing. Beyond these specific items, the E200 is a robust vehicle with few widespread mechanical complaints.
