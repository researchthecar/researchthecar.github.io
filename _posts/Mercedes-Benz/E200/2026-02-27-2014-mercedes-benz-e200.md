# 2014 Mercedes-Benz E200

### Overview
The 2014 Mercedes-Benz E200, part of the W212 facelift generation, is generally regarded as a reliable executive sedan. It offers a blend of comfort, performance, and luxury, with a focus on build quality. While no vehicle is entirely immune to issues, the E200 typically demonstrates strong mechanical and electrical integrity when properly maintained. Owners often praise its refined driving experience and durable construction. However, like many modern vehicles, specific components can occasionally present challenges, particularly as the vehicle ages. This analysis focuses on documented, non-wear-and-tear related reliability concerns.

---


## Engine

### 2.0L M274 Turbocharged Inline-4
**Turbocharger Actuator Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of power, check engine light illumination, whistling noises from the engine bay.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the turbocharger wastegate actuator or the entire turbocharger assembly.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1500

**PCV Valve Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Rough idle, oil leaks, increased oil consumption, potential for engine oil seal damage.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the Positive Crankcase Ventilation (PCV) valve.
- **Cost:** $200 - $400


## Transmission

### 7G-TRONIC PLUS (7-Speed Automatic)
**Transmission Control Module (TCM) Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** Erratic shifting, delayed engagement, transmission warning lights, limp mode activation.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and potential reprogramming or replacement of the TCM.
- **Cost:** $700 - $1200

**Torque Converter Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Shuddering during acceleration, slipping gears, unusual noises during operation.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the torque converter.
- **Cost:** $1000 - $2000

## Electrical

### General Electrical System
**Infotainment System Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Screen freezing, unresponsive buttons, Bluetooth connectivity issues, audio problems.
- **Solution:** Software updates, reset procedures, or replacement of the head unit.
- **Cost:** $300 - $1000

**Sensor Failures (e.g., ABS, Parking Assist)**
- **Symptoms:** Warning lights on the dashboard, malfunctioning driver assistance systems.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and replacement of faulty sensors.
- **Cost:** $250 - $600 per sensor

### Summary Verdict
The 2014 Mercedes-Benz E200 is a solid choice for those seeking a dependable luxury sedan. Its powertrain, particularly the 2.0L turbocharged engine and 7-speed automatic transmission, are generally robust but can occasionally experience issues with the turbocharger actuator, PCV valve, or transmission control module. Electrical systems, while complex, are mostly reliable, though infotainment glitches and sensor failures can occur. The suspension, exterior, and interior components are typically well-built and hold up well over time. Regular maintenance is key to mitigating potential problems and ensuring the longevity of this well-engineered vehicle.
