# 2016 Mercedes-Benz E200

### Overview
The 2016 Mercedes-Benz E200, part of the W212 generation, is generally regarded as a solid and reliable executive sedan. It offers a blend of luxury, performance, and advanced technology. While most owners report a positive ownership experience, like any vehicle, there are specific areas that have seen more attention than others. This analysis focuses on documented technical reliability concerns beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential issues for the 2.0L turbocharged gasoline engine and the 7G-TRONIC PLUS automatic transmission, which were the primary configurations for this model. Understanding these potential points of concern can help prospective buyers and current owners.

---


## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged Gasoline (M274)
**Oil Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Visible oil stains under the vehicle, burning oil smell, low oil level warnings.
- **Solution:** Replacement of seals, gaskets, or oil pan.
- **Cost:** $300 - $700

**PCV Valve Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Rough idle, increased oil consumption, whistling noises from the engine bay.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the Positive Crankcase Ventilation (PCV) valve.
- **Cost:** $200 - $450

## Electrical

### General Electrical System
**Infotainment System Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Screen freezing, unresponsive controls, Bluetooth connectivity issues.
- **Solution:** Software updates, module reset, or replacement of the head unit.
- **Cost:** $400 - $1200

**Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Difficulty starting, electrical components malfunctioning intermittently, battery warning light.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis of parasitic draw, replacement of faulty modules or battery.
- **Cost:** $300 - $800

### Summary Verdict
The 2016 Mercedes-Benz E200 demonstrates good overall reliability, particularly with its powertrain. The 2.0L turbocharged engine and 7-speed automatic transmission are robust, though minor oil leaks and PCV valve issues have been noted. Electrical gremlins, such as infotainment glitches and occasional battery drain, are the most frequently reported non-wear-related concerns. Suspension, exterior, and interior components generally hold up well, with no widespread critical failures documented. Regular maintenance and addressing minor electrical issues promptly are key to ensuring a trouble-free ownership experience with this luxury sedan.
