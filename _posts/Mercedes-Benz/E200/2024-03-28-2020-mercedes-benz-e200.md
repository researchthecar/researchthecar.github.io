**Introduction**

The 2020 Mercedes-Benz E200 is a luxury sedan that offers a refined driving experience and a host of advanced features. It comes with a 2.0-liter turbocharged inline-four engine that produces 197 horsepower and 221 lb-ft of torque, paired with a 9-speed automatic transmission. The E200 is known for its comfortable ride, spacious interior, and impressive fuel economy. However, it has also been reported to have several issues.

**Engine**

**2.0L Turbocharged Inline-Four**

**Leaking Vacuum Pump**
Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, rough idle, increased fuel consumption
Solution: Replace vacuum pump
Estimated Cost to Fix: $600 - $800+
Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**9-Speed Automatic**

**Shifting Hesitation**
Description/Symptoms: Transmission hesitates or jerks when shifting gears
Solution: Update transmission software
Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

**No Issues Found**

**Electrical**

**Battery Drain**
Description/Symptoms: Battery drains prematurely
Solution: Replace or reprogram battery management module
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800+
Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

**No Issues Found**

**Interior**

**Sunroof Rattling**
Description/Symptoms: Sunroof rattles when closed
Solution: Replace sunroof seal or adjust sunroof alignment
Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+
Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2020 Mercedes-Benz E200 has several reported issues, including a leaking vacuum pump, shifting hesitation, battery drain, and sunroof rattling. While the severity of these issues varies, some can be costly to repair. It's important to note that not all E200s will experience these problems, but it's advisable to be aware of them before making a purchase.
