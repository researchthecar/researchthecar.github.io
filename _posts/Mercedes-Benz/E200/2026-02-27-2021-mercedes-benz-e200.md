# 2021 Mercedes-Benz E200 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2021 Mercedes-Benz E-Class, buyers are drawn to its luxurious interior, advanced technology, and refined driving dynamics. However, owner reports submitted to safety authorities indicate certain recurring mechanical and electrical issues that prospective buyers should keep on their radar. Reviewing specific powertrain setups, common trouble spots, and electrical concerns can help you make an informed purchasing decision.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4 (M264)

The 2.0-liter turbocharged inline-4 engine produces approximately 255 horsepower and 273 lb-ft of torque, offering a strong balance of efficiency and daily drivability. While generally smooth and responsive, some owners have experienced cylinder misfires and valve seat wear that require attention.

**Cylinder Misfire and Valve Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Illuminated check engine light, engine misfires, and sudden entry into limp mode resulting in a loss of motive power on the highway.
- **Solution:** Requires professional diagnosis and potential cylinder head or exhaust valve replacement as outlined by manufacturer service bulletins.

## Transmission

### 9G-TRONIC 9-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 9-speed automatic transmission channels power smoothly through rear-wheel drive or 4MATIC all-wheel-drive configurations. While most units shift seamlessly, a small number of severe failures have been reported at relatively low mileages.

**Premature Transmission Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Catastrophic loss of drive function while operating on the highway, leaving the vehicle entirely immobilized.
- **Solution:** Requires professional inspection and potential transmission overhaul or complete unit replacement.

## Brakes

### Electronic Parking Brake

The braking system delivers confident stopping power under normal conditions, though electronic parking brake engagement glitches have frustrated some owners.

**Independent Brake Seizing**
- **Symptoms:** The emergency brake unexpectedly engages or locks up while parking or after sitting for multiple days, making the vehicle difficult to move.
- **Solution:** Inspect the electronic parking brake actuators, calipers, and software calibration.

## Electrical

### 48V Mild-Hybrid Electrical System

The 48-volt electrical architecture supports advanced mild-hybrid features and accessories. Issues with battery reliability can occasionally disable the vehicle entirely.

**48V Battery and System Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Sudden loss of motive power, instrument panel error messages indicating a crash, and complete vehicle inoperability requiring a tow.
- **Solution:** Have the 48-volt battery and associated control modules tested or replaced by a qualified service center.

### Backup and Surround-View Cameras

Advanced driver assistance and camera systems enhance parking and visibility, but software glitches can cause temporary feed losses.

**Intermittent Camera Inoperability**
- **Symptoms:** The rearview or 360-degree camera display fails to activate when shifting into reverse, displaying an 'inoperative' message.
- **Solution:** Check software versions, wiring harnesses, and camera modules for faults.

## Summary Verdict

The 2021 Mercedes-Benz E-Class delivers impressive luxury, comfort, and performance, but prospective buyers should approach with careful inspection. Pay close attention to service history regarding engine misfires, 48-volt electrical health, and transmission operation. Securing a pre-purchase inspection from an independent specialist can help mitigate unexpected repair expenses.
