**Introduction**

The 2014 Mercedes-Benz E200 is a mid-size luxury sedan with a 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine. It offers 184 horsepower and 221 pound-feet of torque. The engine is paired with a seven-speed automatic transmission. The E200 is known for its refined driving experience, comfortable interior, and stylish exterior. However, there have been some reported issues with the vehicle.

**Engine**

**2.0-liter Turbocharged Four-Cylinder**

- **Intake Manifold Cracking:**
    - **Description/Symptoms:** Vacuum leaks, rough idling, decreased engine performance
    - **Solution:** Replace intake manifold
    - **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $1,000 - $2,000+

**Transmission**

**Seven-Speed Automatic**

- **Shifting Problems:**
    - **Description/Symptoms:** Hard shifting, delayed shifts, slipping gears
    - **Solution:** Transmission software update or rebuild
    - **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $500 - $2,500+

**Suspension**

- **Air Suspension Compressor Failure:**
    - **Description/Symptoms:** Vehicle sagging, loss of ride height
    - **Solution:** Replace air suspension compressor
    - **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $1,500 - $2,500

**Electrical**

- **Battery Discharge:**
    - **Description/Symptoms:** Difficulty starting, electrical system malfunctions
    - **Solution:** Replace battery
    - **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $200 - $500

- **Headlight Bulb Failure:**
    - **Description/Symptoms:** Dim or non-functioning headlights
    - **Solution:** Replace headlight bulbs
    - **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $100 - $200

**Exterior**

- **Front Bumper Detachment:**
    - **Description/Symptoms:** Bumpers detaching from the vehicle
    - **Solution:** Reattach bumper
    - **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $500 - $1,000

**Interior**

- **Power Window Failure:**
    - **Description/Symptoms:** Inoperable power windows
    - **Solution:** Replace window motor or switch
    - **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $300 - $800
- **Radio Display Malfunction:**
   - **Description/Symptoms:** Black or unresponsive screen, glitches
   - **Solution:** Replace or repair radio display
   - **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $500-$2,000+

**Summary**

Overall, the 2014 Mercedes-Benz E200 has a number of reported issues. These issues range in severity and cost from minor electrical issues to major engine problems. It is important to be aware of these potential issues before purchasing this vehicle.
