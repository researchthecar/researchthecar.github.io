The 2016 Mercedes-Benz E200 is a popular luxury sedan known for its refined ride, comfortable interior, and sophisticated styling. The E200, specifically, features a 2.0-liter turbocharged inline-4 engine producing around 181 horsepower and 221 lb-ft of torque. Other engine options available for the 2016 E-Class range include other gasoline engines and diesel engines, with varying horsepower and torque figures. Transmission options typically included a 7-speed automatic. While owners appreciate the E200's fuel efficiency and elegant design, common complaints often revolve around electronic glitches and the high cost of maintenance and repairs.

**Engine**

**2.0-liter Turbocharged Inline-4**

* **Oil Leaks**
    * Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks may develop from various points, including the valve cover gasket, oil filter housing, or rear main seal. Symptoms include visible oil spots under the car, a burning oil smell, and low oil levels.
    * Solution: Replacing the leaking gasket or seal. In some cases, resealing the affected area.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1000+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Check Engine Light Due to Sensor Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: The check engine light may illuminate due to faulty sensors like the oxygen sensor or mass airflow sensor. Rough running engine.
    * Solution: Diagnose the fault codes and replace the faulty sensor.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $400+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**7-Speed Automatic (7G-Tronic)**

* **Rough Shifting/Hesitation**
    * Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported rough or delayed shifting, particularly between certain gears. This can manifest as a noticeable jerk or a hesitation when accelerating.
    * Solution: In some cases, a transmission fluid flush and software update can resolve the issue. More severe cases may require valve body replacement or a complete transmission rebuild.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 (flush) - $4000+ (rebuild)
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Torque Converter Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: The torque converter may exhibit shuddering or vibration, especially at lower speeds or when under load.
    * Solution: Replacing the torque converter.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1500 - $3000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Air Suspension Issues (if equipped)**
    * Description/Symptoms: The E200 can be equipped with air suspension. Common issues include air leaks, compressor failure, or malfunctioning sensors. This can result in the car sitting unevenly or failing to maintain the proper ride height.
    * Solution: Repair or replace the leaking air spring, compressor, or affected sensor.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2000+ per component
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain**
    * Description/Symptoms: Excessive battery drain can lead to difficulty starting the car, or a completely dead battery. This can be caused by a parasitic draw from a faulty module or accessory.
    * Solution: Identify and repair the source of the parasitic draw. This may involve testing and replacing various electrical components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+ (diagnosis) + cost of parts/labor
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Malfunctioning Sensors**
    * Description/Symptoms: Various sensors throughout the vehicle can fail, leading to issues such as inaccurate readings, warning lights, or system malfunctions.
    * Solution: Diagnose and replace the faulty sensor.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $400+ per sensor
    * Recalls: None Found

* **COMAND System Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: The COMAND infotainment system can experience glitches, freezing, or display issues.
    * Solution: Software updates, or replacement of the COMAND unit in more severe cases.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 (Software Update) - $1500+ (replacement)
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Headlight Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: LED headlights may fail, flicker, or exhibit condensation.
    * Solution: Replacement of the headlight assembly.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $2000+ per headlight
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Sunroof Problems**
    * Description/Symptoms: Sunroofs can experience issues such as getting stuck, failing to open or close properly, or leaking.
    * Solution: Repair or replacement of the sunroof motor, tracks, or seals.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1000+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Seat Adjustment Motor Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Electric seat adjustment motors can fail, leading to difficulty adjusting the seat position.
    * Solution: Replacing the faulty seat motor.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+ per motor
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2016 Mercedes-Benz E200, while a luxurious and comfortable vehicle, is not without its potential issues. Engine oil leaks and sensor malfunctions are common concerns, along with transmission problems. Air suspension issues, electrical problems, and COMAND system glitches are also frequently reported. The cost of repairs can range from a few hundred dollars for minor fixes to several thousand dollars for major component replacements, making it essential for potential buyers to thoroughly inspect the vehicle and be prepared for potentially high maintenance costs.
