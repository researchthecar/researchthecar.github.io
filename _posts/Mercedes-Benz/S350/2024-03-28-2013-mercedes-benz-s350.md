**Introduction to the 2013 Mercedes-Benz S350**

The 2013 Mercedes-Benz S350 is a luxury sedan that offers a combination of performance, style, and comfort. It is powered by a 3.5-liter V6 engine producing 302 horsepower and 273 lb-ft of torque, paired with a 7-speed automatic transmission. Other available engine options include a 4.6-liter V8 and a 3.0-liter V6 diesel engine. The S350 features a spacious and well-appointed interior, along with a wide range of advanced safety and technology features. While it is generally praised for its driving dynamics, fuel efficiency, and luxurious amenities, some owners have reported various issues.

**Engine**

**3.5L V6**

* **Inconsistent Fuel Consumption:** Some owners have reported experiencing inconsistent fuel consumption, particularly in urban driving conditions.
    * Solution: Engine software updates or fuel injector replacements may be necessary.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
* **Intake Manifold Leak:** A faulty intake manifold gasket can cause a vacuum leak, leading to rough idling and reduced engine performance.
    * Solution: Replacement of the intake manifold gasket.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+

**4.6L V8**

* **Timing Chain Rattle:** In some cases, the timing chain can develop a rattle at higher mileage.
    * Solution: Replacement of the timing chain and related components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
* **Balance Shaft Gear Failure:** The balance shaft gear can fail, causing a grinding noise and loss of engine power.
    * Solution: Replacement of the balance shaft gear and related components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $3,000 - $5,000+
* **Valve Stem Seal Wear:** Worn valve stem seals can lead to excessive oil consumption and blue smoke from the exhaust.
    * Solution: Replacement of the valve stem seals.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+

**Transmission**

**7-Speed Automatic**

* **Shift Hesitation:** Some owners have experienced occasional shift hesitation, especially when accelerating or downshifting.
    * Solution: Transmission fluid change or software updates may be necessary.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
* **Torque Converter Shudder:** A faulty torque converter can cause the transmission to shudder during gear changes.
    * Solution: Replacement of the torque converter.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+

**Suspension**

* **Air Suspension Leaks:** The air suspension system can develop leaks over time, causing the vehicle to sag on one or more corners.
    * Solution: Replacement of the air suspension bags or lines.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
* **Front Control Arm Bushings:** Premature wear of the front control arm bushings can lead to suspension noise and reduced handling.
    * Solution: Replacement of the control arm bushings.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
* **Rear Trailing Arm Bushings:** Similarly, premature wear of the rear trailing arm bushings can cause suspension noise and decreased stability.
    * Solution: Replacement of the trailing arm bushings.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800+

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain:** Some owners have reported experiencing battery drain, particularly when the vehicle is left parked for extended periods.
    * Solution: Diagnosis and repair of electrical system components, such as alternator or parasitic drain.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
* **Electrical Gremlins:** Various electrical issues have been reported, such as malfunctioning sensors, faulty wiring, or intermittent power loss.
    * Solution: Troubleshooting and repair of specific electrical components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the issue

**Exterior**

* **Door Hinge Rust:** The door hinges can develop rust over time, particularly in areas with high humidity.
    * Solution: Cleaning and lubrication of the hinges, or replacement if necessary.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
* **Water Leakage:** Some owners have reported experiencing water leakage into the interior of the vehicle, especially during heavy rain or car washes.
    * Solution: Inspection and repair of seals or weatherstripping.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $700+

**Interior**

* **Seat Switch Malfunction:** The seat switch can malfunction, preventing the seat from adjusting properly.
    * Solution: Replacement of the seat switch.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
* **Infotainment System Glitches:** The infotainment system can experience occasional glitches, such as freezing or loss of connectivity.
    * Solution: Software updates or component replacements may be necessary.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+

**Summary**

The 2013 Mercedes-Benz S350 offers a luxurious and comfortable driving experience, but it has been reported to have some issues that owners should be aware of. While some of the identified issues are relatively minor and inexpensive to fix, others, such as engine or transmission problems, can be more severe and costly. Overall, it is essential to consider the potential repair costs and reliability concerns before purchasing this model.
