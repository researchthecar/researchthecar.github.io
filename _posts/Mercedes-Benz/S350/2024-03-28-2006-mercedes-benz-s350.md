**Introduction**

The 2006 Mercedes-Benz S350 is a luxury sedan that offers a comfortable and refined driving experience. It is powered by a 3.5-liter V6 engine that produces 268 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque. The S350 comes standard with a seven-speed automatic transmission. Other engine and transmission options include:

* 5.5-liter V8 engine with 382 horsepower and 391 lb-ft of torque (optional)
* 5.5-liter V12 engine with 510 horsepower and 612 lb-ft of torque (optional)
* 3.0-liter V6 diesel engine with 215 horsepower and 400 lb-ft of torque (optional)

People like the S350 for its luxurious interior, comfortable ride, and powerful engine. However, some people have reported problems with the vehicle's engine, transmission, and other components.

**Engine**

**3.5-liter V6 Engine**

* **Intake manifold runner control failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The intake manifold runner control valve may fail, causing the engine to run rough and lose power.
    * Solution: Replace the intake manifold runner control valve.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Timing chain failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The timing chain may fail, causing the engine to lose power and make a rattling noise.
    * Solution: Replace the timing chain.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**5.5-liter V8 Engine**

* **Cylinder head bolt failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The cylinder head bolts may fail, causing the engine to lose coolant and overheat.
    * Solution: Replace the cylinder head bolts.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**Seven-Speed Automatic Transmission**

* **Torque converter failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The torque converter may fail, causing the transmission to slip and hesitate.
    * Solution: Replace the torque converter.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Valve body failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The valve body may fail, causing the transmission to shift erratically.
    * Solution: Replace the valve body.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Airmatic suspension failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The airmatic suspension system may fail, causing the vehicle to ride low and handle poorly.
    * Solution: Replace the airmatic suspension components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Battery drain**
    * Description/Symptoms: The battery may drain prematurely, leaving the vehicle unable to start.
    * Solution: Find and fix the source of the battery drain.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Alternator failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The alternator may fail, causing the battery to not charge and the vehicle to lose power.
    * Solution: Replace the alternator.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Rust on undercarriage**
    * Description/Symptoms: The undercarriage of the vehicle may rust, causing cosmetic damage and potential structural issues.
    * Solution: Clean and treat the rust.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Navigation system failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The navigation system may fail, causing the screen to go blank or display incorrect information.
    * Solution: Replace the navigation system.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Climate control failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The climate control system may fail, causing the vehicle to become too hot or too cold.
    * Solution: Diagnose and repair the climate control system.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2006 Mercedes-Benz S350 is a luxurious and powerful sedan that offers a comfortable and refined driving experience. However, some people have reported problems with the vehicle's engine, transmission, and other components. These issues can be expensive to repair, so it is important to factor in the cost of potential repairs when considering purchasing a used S350. Overall, the severity and cost of the issues reported for the 2006 S350 can vary depending on the specific problem and the extent of the damage.
