**Introduction:**

The 1995 Mercedes-Benz S350 is a luxury sedan powered by a 3.5-liter V8 engine producing 232 horsepower and 255 lb-ft of torque. It comes standard with a 5-speed automatic transmission. The S350 is praised for its comfortable ride, refined interior, and powerful engine. However, like any vehicle, it is not without its issues.

**Engine**

* **M119 3.5L V8**
    * **Valve Seat Recession:**
        * Description/Symptoms: The valve seats in the cylinder head can become recessed over time, leading to loss of compression and increased oil consumption.
        * Solution: The valve seats need to be machined and replaced.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,500 - $4,000+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

* **5-Speed Automatic (722.6)**
    * **Torque Converter Failure:**
        * Description/Symptoms: The torque converter may fail, causing shuddering, loss of power, and overheating.
        * Solution: The torque converter needs to be replaced.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500
        * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Front Control Arm Bushings:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The front control arm bushings can wear out and cause clunking or rattling noises.
    * Solution: The bushings need to be replaced.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $600
        * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Alternator Regulator:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The alternator regulator may fail, causing the battery to not charge properly.
    * Solution: The alternator regulator needs to be replaced.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800
        * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Headlight Bulbs:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The headlight bulbs may burn out prematurely.
    * Solution: The bulbs need to be replaced.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200
        * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Climate Control Panel:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The climate control panel may malfunction, causing the heating and cooling system to not work properly.
    * Solution: The panel needs to be replaced.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
        * Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

Overall, the 1995 Mercedes-Benz S350 is a reliable vehicle with a comfortable ride and powerful engine. However, it is not without its issues, such as valve seat recession, transmission problems, and electrical malfunctions. While some repairs can be costly, the S350 generally provides a solid ownership experience for those who are willing to invest in its upkeep.
