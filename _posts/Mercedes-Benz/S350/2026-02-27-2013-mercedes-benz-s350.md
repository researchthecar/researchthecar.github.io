# 2013 Mercedes-Benz S350

### Overview
The 2013 Mercedes-Benz S350, a flagship luxury sedan, is generally known for its sophisticated engineering and comfortable ride. While it offers a premium experience, like any complex vehicle, it can encounter specific issues. This analysis focuses on documented technical reliability concerns beyond routine maintenance. The S350 in this model year is equipped with a potent diesel engine and a refined automatic transmission, contributing to its performance and fuel efficiency. Owners should be aware of potential areas that may require attention over time, particularly concerning the powertrain and electronic systems, which are extensive in a vehicle of this caliber.

---


## Engine

### 3.0L V6 Turbo Diesel (OM642)
**Diesel Particulate Filter (DPF) Regeneration Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Check engine light, reduced engine power, increased fuel consumption.
- **Solution:** Forced regeneration, DPF cleaning, or replacement.
- **Cost:** $500 - $2000

**Glow Plug Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Hard starting, rough idle, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of faulty glow plugs and potentially the control module.
- **Cost:** $300 - $800

**EGR Valve Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Rough idle, poor acceleration, increased emissions, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Cleaning or replacement of the EGR valve.
- **Cost:** $400 - $900


## Transmission

### 7G-Tronic (722.9) 7-Speed Automatic
**Torque Converter Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Shuddering, slipping, delayed engagement, unusual noises.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the torque converter and potentially transmission fluid service.
- **Cost:** $1500 - $3000

**Valve Body Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Harsh shifting, transmission fault messages, inability to select gears.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of the valve body, often requires transmission fluid flush.
- **Cost:** $1000 - $2500


## Suspension

### Active Body Control (ABC) / AirMatic
**Hydraulic Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle sagging, bouncy ride, warning lights on the dashboard.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of leaking hoses, struts, or pump.
- **Cost:** $800 - $3000+

**Pump Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of suspension height adjustment, vehicle sitting low.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the hydraulic pump.
- **Cost:** $700 - $1500


## Electrical

### Command System (Infotainment)
**Screen Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** Flickering screen, unresponsive touch, dead pixels.
- **Solution:** Software update, repair, or replacement of the head unit.
- **Cost:** $500 - $2000


### Sensors (Parking, Rain, etc.)
**Sensor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** False warnings, non-functional features (e.g., wipers not activating).
- **Solution:** Replacement of faulty sensors.
- **Cost:** $200 - $600


### Battery Drain
**Parasitic Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Dead battery after short periods, electrical gremlins.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis to find the source of the drain (e.g., faulty module, light), repair or replacement.
- **Cost:** $300 - $1000+

### Summary Verdict
The 2013 Mercedes-Benz S350 offers a luxurious and technologically advanced experience. While generally reliable for its class, potential owners should be aware of specific issues that can arise, particularly with the diesel engine's emissions system (DPF, EGR), the sophisticated 7-speed transmission (torque converter, valve body), and the complex air suspension system. Electrical components, especially infotainment and sensors, can also be a source of concern. Addressing these potential issues proactively through diligent maintenance and prompt repairs can help maintain the S350's performance and longevity.
