# 2009 Mercedes-Benz OMC 1400

### Overview
The 2009 Mercedes-Benz OMC 1400, a commercial vehicle, generally exhibits a solid reliability record, particularly in its core mechanical components. Owners and fleet operators often praise its robust construction and dependable performance under demanding conditions. While no vehicle is entirely immune to issues, the OMC 1400 tends to avoid widespread, recurring problems that significantly impact its operational uptime. Routine maintenance is key to longevity, but significant design flaws or systemic component failures are uncommon. This data focuses on documented technical issues beyond standard wear and tear, providing insights for potential buyers and current owners.

---


## Engine

### 2.2L CDI Diesel
**Glow Plug Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Difficulty starting in cold weather, rough idling, engine warning light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of faulty glow plugs.
- **Cost:** $150 - $300

**Turbocharger Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of power, whistling noises, black smoke from exhaust.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of the turbocharger unit.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1500


### 2.7L CDI Diesel
**Injector Problems**
- **Symptoms:** Rough idling, poor fuel economy, engine misfires, black smoke.
- **Solution:** Cleaning or replacement of fuel injectors.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600 per injector


## Transmission

### 5-Speed Manual
**Clutch Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Slipping clutch, difficulty shifting gears, burning smell.
- **Solution:** Clutch kit replacement (clutch disc, pressure plate, release bearing).
- **Cost:** $600 - $1000


### 5-Speed Automatic
**Torque Converter Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Shuddering, slipping gears, delayed engagement.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of the torque converter.
- **Cost:** $700 - $1200


## Suspension

### Standard Chassis
**Leaf Spring Fatigue**
- **Symptoms:** Sagging rear end, increased road noise, uneven tire wear.
- **Solution:** Replacement of worn or broken leaf springs.
- **Cost:** $400 - $800 per axle


## Electrical

### General Electrical System
**Alternator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Dimming lights, battery warning light, electrical system malfunctions.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the alternator.
- **Cost:** $400 - $700

### Summary Verdict
The 2009 Mercedes-Benz OMC 1400 is a generally reliable commercial vehicle. Its diesel engines, while susceptible to common issues like glow plug or injector problems, are robust. Transmission reliability is good, with manual clutches and automatic torque converters being the most common wear items. Suspension components, particularly leaf springs, can show fatigue over time due to heavy use. Electrical systems are mostly sound, with alternator failure being a notable, albeit manageable, concern. Exterior and interior components are typically durable. Overall, with proper maintenance, the OMC 1400 offers dependable service, making it a sound choice for commercial applications where longevity and operational consistency are paramount.
