# 2018 Mercedes-Benz E400 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a pre-owned 2018 Mercedes-Benz E400, prospective buyers will find a luxury vehicle defined by advanced technology, smooth performance, and sophisticated engineering. However, like many modern luxury European cars, it requires careful inspection of its complex electronic architecture, powertrain components, and chassis hardware. While owner feedback highlights several high-tech amenities and strong performance, reports also point to potential problem areas ranging from transmission shifting quirks to electrical glitches and wheel durability concerns.

---

## Engine

### 3.0L V6 Biturbo Engine

The 2018 E400 is typically powered by a 3.0-liter twin-turbocharged V6 engine producing approximately 329 horsepower and 354 lb-ft of torque. This powerplant delivers brisk acceleration and refined cruising capabilities, though routine maintenance is critical to avoid long-term mechanical snags.

**Engine Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may unexpectedly lose power or stall out during operation without prior warning indicators.
- **Solution:** Diagnostic testing by a qualified technician to inspect fuel delivery, ignition systems, and electronic control modules.

## Transmission

### 9-Speed Automatic Transmission

Equipped with a 9-speed automatic transmission paired with rear-wheel drive or 4MATIC all-wheel drive, this gearbox is designed for seamless power delivery and optimized fuel efficiency. Proper fluid services at recommended intervals are essential for sustained longevity.

**Shifting Delays and Unexpected Neutral Shifts**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may hesitate or independently shift into neutral when attempting to select drive, or experience rough engagement between drive and reverse.
- **Solution:** Inspect transmission software calibration, valve body operation, and fluid condition; replace failing electronic valve components if necessary.

## Suspension

### Wheels and Tires

The suspension and wheel setup provides a dynamic, composed ride quality, though low-profile tire and large alloy configurations are susceptible to road hazard damage and cracking.

**Wheel Cracking and Tire Blistering**
- **Symptoms:** Low-profile alloy wheels may develop structural cracks under normal driving conditions, and tires can exhibit tread blistering.
- **Solution:** Inspect wheels for structural integrity during routine maintenance and replace damaged rims or compromised tires immediately.

## Electrical

### Infotainment and Digital Display

The electrical architecture supports extensive digital displays and driver-assist features, but screen blanking and camera connectivity faults have been noted by owners.

**Backup Camera and Dashboard Screen Failures**
- **Symptoms:** The digital instrument cluster or the backup camera display may intermittently go blank, freeze, or display error graphics.
- **Solution:** Check software versions, wiring harnesses, and camera modules, or replace failing display units as diagnosed by a specialist.

## Safety

### Seat Belts

Safety equipment generally performs to high standards, though motorized seat belt retractors require verification to ensure proper tensioning.

**Seat Belt Retractor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The driver-side seat belt may fail to fully retract, leaving dangerous slack across the occupant's chest and lap.
- **Solution:** Have an authorized dealer inspect the seat belt motor and replace the retractor assembly.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V676000: Structure:Body](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=E400&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** If the spoiler detaches while driving, it can become a road hazard, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** MBUSA will notify owners, and dealers will inspect and correct the mounting of the spoiler, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V515000: Air Bags:Sensor:Occupant Classification](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=E400&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** The Occupant Classification System (OCS) may not be properly calibrated, resulting in the front passenger air bag not being deactivated if a child seat is in the front seat. If a child seat is in the front seat and the passenger air bag is not deactivated, in the event of a crash, it can increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** MBUSA will notify owners, and dealers will replace the front passenger seat cushion, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V513000: Child Seat:Latch Strap](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=E400&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** The child seat anchoring system (ISOFIX) in these vehicles may not have been properly installed. As a result, in the event of a crash, the ISOFIX console may partially detach from the car body.
- **What to do:** MBUSA will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the ISOFIX console, and replace it if necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V449000: Electrical System:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=E400&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** The software in the replaced instrument cluster may cause the seat belt warning system to not operate properly, only displaying a solid warning light if either the driver or passenger seat belt is unfastened, instead of a blinking light with an audible tone. ## Summary Verdict

The 2018 Mercedes-Benz E400 remains an appealing option for used car buyers seeking high-end luxury, advanced technology, and robust performance. However, because it relies heavily on complex electrical systems and sophisticated powertrain components, potential owners should prioritize a comprehensive pre-purchase inspection. Particular attention should be paid to checking transmission shift quality, verifying the operation of all digital screens and cameras, and inspecting the wheels and tires for signs of wear or structural damage.
