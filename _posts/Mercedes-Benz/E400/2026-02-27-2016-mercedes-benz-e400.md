# 2016 Mercedes-Benz E400 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2016 Mercedes-Benz E400 offers a compelling blend of luxury, comfort, and performance, anchored by refined powertrain options. While the vehicle delivers a sophisticated driving experience, used car buyers should carefully inspect certain areas for recurring owner complaints. Common trouble spots involve electrical glitches, steering assist failures, and exterior seal integrity, alongside notable safety system concerns.

---

## Engine

### 3.0L V6 Twin-Turbo

The 3.0-liter V6 twin-turbo engine produces approximately 329 horsepower and 354 lb-ft of torque, delivering brisk and smooth acceleration. It is generally robust, though owners should monitor for minor fuel system odors and accessory electrical integration issues.

**Fuel Odor**
- **Symptoms:** A noticeable fuel odor is apparent while the vehicle is idling, raising concerns about potential leaks in the fuel system components.
- **Solution:** Inspect fuel lines, rails, and pump assemblies for leaks, and replace damaged seals or faulty components.

## Transmission

### 7-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 7-speed automatic transmission provides smooth, seamless gear shifts suited to the luxury character of the E400. No widespread or severe mechanical failures are frequently reported for this unit.

## Steering

### Power Steering System

The power steering system provides responsive handling under normal conditions, but owners have experienced sudden electronic assist failures.

**Steering Assist Failure**
- **Symptoms:** A power steering malfunction message appears on the dashboard, resulting in a sudden loss of power steering assist and making the vehicle very difficult to steer.
- **Solution:** Diagnose the electronic power steering assembly and replace faulty sensors, wiring, or steering components as necessary.

## Exterior

### Windshield Sealing

Exterior body panels and glass are generally well-fitted, but compromised windshield seals can lead to frustrating cabin water leaks.

**Water Intrusion**
- **Symptoms:** Water drops form on the interior upper edge of the windshield after rain, and pushing on the glass can reveal looseness in the adhesive seal.
- **Solution:** Remove and reinstall the front windshield with a fresh, proper adhesive seal to eliminate water leaks.

## Safety

### Electrical and Collision Systems

The electrical architecture supports numerous advanced safety and convenience features, though occasional sensor ghosting and warning lights can occur.

**Spurious Collision Warning and Braking**
- **Symptoms:** The collision prevention system unexpectedly engages and slams on the brakes without an actual obstruction in front of the vehicle.
- **Solution:** Have the radar and camera sensors recalibrated or inspected by a qualified technician.

### Air Bag System

The comprehensive airbag and restraint suite provides high levels of occupant protection, though sensor faults can occasionally disable specific bags.

**Occupant Sensor Fault**
- **Symptoms:** Passenger seat capacity sensor malfunctions, causing the passenger side air bag to remain deactivated.
- **Solution:** Diagnose the seat sensor via OBD scanner and replace the faulty passenger seat sensor mat or module.

## Summary Verdict

The 2016 Mercedes-Benz E400 is a sophisticated luxury sedan that rewards prospective buyers with strong performance and executive comfort. While major mechanical catastrophes are uncommon, shoppers should thoroughly check for potential power steering warnings, windshield seal degradation, and passenger seat sensor faults during a pre-purchase inspection. Addressing these specific areas ensures a more dependable and satisfying ownership experience.
