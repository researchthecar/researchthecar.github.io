# 2017 Mercedes-Benz E400 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used luxury vehicle, understanding potential mechanical and electrical hiccups is essential. This reliability overview highlights common owner-reported concerns for the vehicle, giving you a clear picture of what to watch out for during your pre-purchase inspection.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4

The base turbocharged inline-4 engine delivers responsive power for daily commuting with respectable efficiency, though owners should monitor for fuel system wear.

**Fuel Leak or Odor**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers notice a strong smell of gasoline inside the cabin or around the vehicle, occasionally accompanied by starting difficulties.
- **Solution:** Inspect fuel lines, high-pressure pumps, and connections for cracks or leaks, and replace damaged components.

### 3.0L Twin-Turbo V6

The available twin-turbo V6 provides smooth, effortless acceleration and robust power delivery across the rev range, representing a refined choice for highway cruising.

## Transmission

### 9-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 9-speed automatic transmission pairs with available rear-wheel and all-wheel drive systems to provide smooth gear changes, though proper electronic upkeep is vital.

**Unintended Neutral Shift**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may inadvertently shift into neutral while coming to a stop or driving at various speeds.
- **Solution:** Inspect the electronic gear shifter module and update software or replace faulty shift components.

## Steering

### Steering Assist

Power steering systems generally offer precise road feedback, but electronic assist failures can occasionally occur.

**Steering Assist Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers experience a sudden loss of power steering functionality, resulting in heavy steering effort especially when turning at lower speeds.
- **Solution:** Inspect the electric power steering control module and wiring harness, and replace failing components as necessary.

## Safety

### Air Bag

Advanced safety systems provide comprehensive protection, though interior sensors require careful inspection.

**Air Bag Warning Light**
- **Symptoms:** The dashboard airbag warning light illuminates, often indicating a fault in the seat cushion weight sensor or front squib circuit.
- **Solution:** Diagnose fault codes and replace faulty seat cushion sensors, wiring harnesses, or clockspring assemblies.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V010000: Air Bags:Frontal:Passenger Side:Inflator Module](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=E400&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** An inflator explosion may result in sharp metal fragments striking the passenger or other occupants resulting in serious injury or death.
- **What to do:** MBUSA will notify owners, and dealers will replace the passenger frontal air bag module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V676000: Structure:Body](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=E400&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** If the spoiler detaches while driving, it can become a road hazard, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** MBUSA will notify owners, and dealers will inspect and correct the mounting of the spoiler, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V271000: Exterior Lighting:Headlights](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=E400&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** The headlight horizontal adjustment screws on these vehicles may not be properly sealed behind a non-removable sealing cap to keep headlights from being readjusted. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 108, "Lamps, Reflective Devices, and Associated Equipment." If the.
- **What to do:** MBUSA will notify owners, and dealers will retrofit the sealing caps for the headlights, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V058000: Electrical System:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=E400&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** The software design of the communication module may fail to communicate the correct vehicle location for the emergency call system (eCall) in the event of a crash. Failure of the eCall system may result in emergency responders being dispatched to the wrong location, increasing the risk of injury following a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 17V080000: Air Bags:Frontal:Passenger Side:Inflator Module](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=E400&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** The pelvis air bag or the front passenger air bag inflator initiator may fail to ignite in the event of a crash. If the pelvis airbag or the front passenger airbag does not deploy as intended in the event of a crash, the occupants have an increased risk of injury.
- **What to do:** MBUSA will notify owners, and dealers will replace the affected air bag modules, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V627000: Air Bags:Frontal](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=E400&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** The driver's air bag may unexpectedly deploy due to insufficient grounding of the steering components if an electrostatic discharge occurs and the air bag clockspring is broken. If the driver air bag inadvertently deploys, it can increase the risk of injury and a crash.
- **What to do:** MBUSA will notify owners, and dealers will add sufficient grounding to the steering components, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V114000: Electrical System:Starter Assembly](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=E400&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** If the starting current limiter overheats, the surrounding components can melt, increasing the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** MBUSA will notify owners, and dealers will install an additional fuse in the electrical line to the starter, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V831000: Structure:Frame And Members:Underbody Shields](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=E400&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** The insufficient clearance may cause the panel to melt, increasing the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** MBUSA will notify owners, and dealers will inspect and replace the underbody panel, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.
