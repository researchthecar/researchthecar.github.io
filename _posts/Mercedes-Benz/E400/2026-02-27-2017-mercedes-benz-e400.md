# 2017 Mercedes-Benz E400

### Overview
The 2017 Mercedes-Benz E400, part of the W213 generation, is generally regarded as a reliable luxury sedan, blending sophisticated technology with robust engineering. It typically features a potent V6 engine paired with a smooth automatic transmission. While Mercedes-Benz vehicles are known for their premium features and performance, like any complex machine, some areas may experience specific issues over time. This analysis focuses on documented technical reliability concerns beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential problem areas and their estimated repair costs for owners and prospective buyers.

---


## Engine

### 3.0L Twin-Turbo V6 (M276)
**Turbocharger Wastegate Actuator**
- **Symptoms:** Check engine light, reduced engine power, whistling noise from engine bay.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the faulty wastegate actuator or turbocharger assembly.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1500

**Oil Leaks (Valve Cover Gaskets)**
- **Symptoms:** Visible oil on engine components, burning oil smell, low oil level.
- **Solution:** Replacement of valve cover gaskets.
- **Cost:** $400 - $700


## Transmission

### 9G-TRONIC (9-Speed Automatic)
**Transmission Control Module (TCM) Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Harsh shifting, delayed engagement, transmission warning light.
- **Solution:** Software update or replacement of the TCM.
- **Cost:** $600 - $1200


## Suspension

### Air Body Control (Optional)
**Air Spring Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle sagging on one corner, uneven ride height, compressor running excessively.
- **Solution:** Replacement of leaking air springs or air struts.
- **Cost:** $700 - $1500 per corner

### Summary Verdict
The 2017 Mercedes-Benz E400 offers a compelling package of luxury and performance. While generally reliable, potential owners should be aware of specific issues such as turbocharger wastegate actuators, oil leaks from valve cover gaskets, and potential glitches with the 9G-TRONIC transmission's control module. Optional Air Body Control suspension can be prone to air spring leaks, and electrical gremlins, particularly with the infotainment system and battery drain, are not uncommon. Exterior and interior components are largely robust, with minor issues like headlight condensation and ambient lighting faults being the most frequently cited. Proactive maintenance and addressing these potential issues early can ensure a positive ownership experience.
