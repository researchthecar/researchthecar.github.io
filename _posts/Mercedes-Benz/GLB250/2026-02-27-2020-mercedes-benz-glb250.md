# 2020 Mercedes-Benz GLB250 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a pre-owned 2020 Mercedes-Benz GLB250, buyers will find a versatile compact luxury SUV that packs advanced technology and distinctive styling. However, prospective owners should review owner-reported reliability trends carefully, as early model-year examples have experienced notable electrical, airbag, and sunroof concerns. Keeping a close eye on service histories and verifying that all safety campaigns have been completed can help ensure a smoother ownership experience.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4

The 2.0-liter turbocharged inline-4 engine produces approximately 221 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque, offering lively acceleration for daily driving. It is generally robust, though sporadic owner complaints have touched upon fuel system components and diagnostics.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Dual-Clutch Automatic

The 8-speed dual-clutch automatic transmission delivers quick, seamless gear changes across front-wheel and 4MATIC all-wheel-drive configurations. While generally smooth, rare owner reports indicate electronic or mechanical shifts to neutral.

**Unexpected Shifting to Neutral**
- **Symptoms:** The transmission may unexpectedly shift into neutral while driving at highway speeds, accompanied by a dashboard warning message.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified technician or dealership inspect the transmission control module and diagnostic trouble codes for software glitches or internal component wear.

## Electrical

### Instrument Display and Control Modules

The vehicle relies heavily on complex digital architecture and multiple onboard computers to operate climate control, displays, and convenience features.

**Display Failure After Moisture Exposure**
- **Symptoms:** The entire digital instrument display and infotainment system may shut down, potentially preventing the vehicle from starting after washing or exposure to water.
- **Solution:** Inspect electrical harnesses and control modules for moisture intrusion and replace damaged components or seals as necessary.

## Exterior

### Panoramic Sunroof Glass

Exterior trim and glass assemblies add to the vehicle's upscale aesthetic, but select owners have reported vulnerable panoramic glass panels.

**Spontaneous Sunroof Shattering**
- **Symptoms:** The glass panel of the panoramic sunroof may crack or explode outward while driving at highway speeds without any visible impact from external objects.
- **Solution:** Replace the damaged glass panel and inspect the surrounding frame alignment and weatherstripping to relieve structural tension.

## Safety

### Air Bag System

Safety systems provide comprehensive protection, though recurring owner reports note electronic warnings and occasional sensor malfunctions in the restraint network.

**Air Bag Warning Light**
- **Symptoms:** An unexpected air bag or passenger restraint malfunction warning message illuminates on the instrument cluster.
- **Solution:** Have the dealer or a specialist scan the Supplemental Restraint System (SRS) for faulty seat sensors, wiring harnesses, or module communication errors.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V480000: Structure:Body](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=GLB250&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** The rear spoiler above the tailgate may not have been welded correctly. This could result in parts of the spoiler detaching from the vehicle.
- **What to do:** MBUSA will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the rear spoiler and replace it, if necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V628000: Air Bags:Side/Window](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=GLB250&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** The window air bag in the A-pillar area may not be mounted correctly, affecting its performance. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 226, "Ejection Mitigation." In the event of a crash that deploys the window air bag, the catch strap could disconnect and increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** MBUSA will notify owners, and dealers will check the mounting of the window air bag and correct it as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V403000: Structure](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=GLB250&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** This may result in the instrument cluster going blank, an engine stall while driving, or the vehicle may not start. Either a blank instrument cluster display or an engine stall can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will seal the vehicle body in the wheel well area and inspect the front foot wells for water intrusion, repairing as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V405000: Air Bags:Passenger Side Frontal:Cushion](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=GLB250&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** The front passenger air bag inflator and cushion may be improperly attached inside the air bag module, which could result in the air bag cushion tearing upon deployment. An air bag that does not deploy properly during a crash can increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the front passenger air bag module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V404000: Air Bags:Side/Window:Curtain](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=GLB250&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** The side curtain air bags may not deploy properly during a crash. Air bags that do not deploy as intended can increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the side curtain air bags, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V231000: Structure:Body:Tailgate](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=GLB250&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** Detached spoilers can create a road hazard, increasing the risk of a crash or injury.
- **What to do:** MBUSA will notify owners, and dealers will inspect and, if necessary, replace the side spoilers, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V232000: Structure:Body](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=GLB250&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** Detached wheel arch covers can create a road hazard, increasing the risk of a crash or injury.
- **What to do:** MBUSA will notify owners, and dealers will check the installation of the front wheel arch covers and correct them as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V246000: Structure](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=GLB250&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** Either a blank instrument cluster display or an engine stall can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** MBUSA will notify owners, and dealers will seal the vehicle body in the wheel well area and inspect the front foot wells for water intrusion, repairing as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2020 Mercedes-Benz GLB250 is an appealing pre-owned choice for buyers seeking luxury packaging and flexible interior space. However, because early production models exhibit higher rates of electrical quirks, sunroof issues, and airbag warning lights, a thorough pre-purchase inspection is highly recommended. Buyers should confirm that all past recall campaigns have been resolved and look for a comprehensive service record.
