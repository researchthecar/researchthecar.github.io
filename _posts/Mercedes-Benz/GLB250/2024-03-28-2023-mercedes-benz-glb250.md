**Introduction**

The 2023 Mercedes-Benz GLB250 is a compact luxury SUV that combines versatility, style, and off-road capability. It features a 2.0-liter turbocharged inline-4 engine that produces 221 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque, paired with an 8-speed dual-clutch automatic transmission. The GLB250 has received praise for its spacious cabin, comfortable ride quality, and responsive handling. However, some owners have reported issues with various components of the vehicle.

**Engine**

* **2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4**
    * **Turbocharger Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, increased oil consumption, unusual noises from the engine.
    * Solution: Replacement of the turbocharger.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
    * Recalls: 2022 Mercedes-Benz GLB - Engine:Fuel and Fuel System (NHTSA Campaign Number: 22V574000)

**Transmission**

* **8-Speed Dual-Clutch Automatic**
    * **Transmission Fluid Leak**
    * Description/Symptoms: Visible fluid leaking from the transmission, slipping or hard shifting, delayed engagement.
    * Solution: Repair or replacement of the transmission fluid pan gasket or other leaking components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+

**Suspension**

* **No Issues Found**

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain**
    * Description/Symptoms: Rapid battery discharge, difficulty starting the vehicle.
    * Solution: Diagnosis and repair of the electrical system to identify the source of the drain.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+

**Exterior**

* **Windshield Wiper Motor Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Inoperative windshield wipers, intermittent or complete failure.
    * Solution: Replacement of the wiper motor.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $250 - $500+
    * Recalls: 2022 Mercedes-Benz GLB - Visibility:Wiper and Washer Systems (NHTSA Campaign Number: 22V372000)

**Interior**

* **Center Console Rattles**
    * Description/Symptoms: Persistent rattling noises coming from the center console area.
    * Solution: Removal and tightening of loose components within the console.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300+

**Overall Summary**

The 2023 Mercedes-Benz GLB250 is a reliable vehicle overall, but some owners have encountered issues with specific components. The most severe and costly issues involve turbocharger failure and electrical system drain, which can both lead to significant expenses. However, many of the other reported issues are relatively minor and can be resolved at a moderate cost. It is important to note that these issues may not be common to all GLB250s and that the vast majority of owners have reported positive experiences with their vehicles.
