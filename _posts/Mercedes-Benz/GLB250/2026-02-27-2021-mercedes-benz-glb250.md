# 2021 Mercedes-Benz GLB250 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

Shopping for a used 2021 Mercedes-Benz GLB250 gives buyers access to a stylish and practical compact luxury SUV with flexible seating configurations. Powertrain reliability for this model year is generally solid, though prospective owners should always verify proper maintenance and mechanical condition before purchasing.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4 (221 hp, 258 lb-ft)

This 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine produces 221 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque, providing responsive acceleration for daily driving. It is widely utilized across Mercedes-Benz compact offerings and generally exhibits dependable operation when properly maintained.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Dual-Clutch Automatic

The 8-speed dual-clutch automatic transmission delivers quick, efficient gear changes across front-wheel and 4MATIC all-wheel-drive variants. While mostly trouble-free, individual reports of sudden warning lights indicate that a thorough test drive is recommended.

**Transmission Malfunction / Limp Mode**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may experience a red transmission malfunction warning light illuminating while driving at highway speeds, accompanied by the vehicle entering limp mode.
- **Solution:** Have the vehicle diagnosed by a qualified service center or dealership to inspect transmission control modules and fluid condition.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V860000: Air Bags:Side/Window](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=GLB250&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The driver-side window air bag may not have been mounted correctly to the lower area of the A-pillar. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 226, "Ejection Mitigation." The driver-side window air bag may not function properly during a crash, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the mounting of the driver-side window air bag and repair it as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V232000: Structure:Body](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=GLB250&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** Detached wheel arch covers can create a road hazard, increasing the risk of a crash or injury.
- **What to do:** MBUSA will notify owners, and dealers will check the installation of the front wheel arch covers and correct them as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V231000: Structure:Body:Tailgate](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=GLB250&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** Detached spoilers can create a road hazard, increasing the risk of a crash or injury.
- **What to do:** MBUSA will notify owners, and dealers will inspect and, if necessary, replace the side spoilers, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V078000: Air Bags:Frontal](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=GLB250&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** An incorrect air bag module may not properly restrain an occupant during a crash. In addition, the air bag module could detach from the steering wheel during deployment.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the front driver's air bag, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V314000: Suspension:Rear](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=GLB250&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The level sensor linkage on the rear axle might not have been installed properly, which could affect the headlight leveling function. Improperly leveled headlights may project a glare, which can impact other drivers and increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and repair the level sensor linkage, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V679000: Electronic Stability Control (Esc):Control Module](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=GLB250&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The Electronic Stability Program (ESP) unit may be damaged, which can result in disabled anti-lock brakes (ABS), disabled electronic stability control (ESC), and an electrical short circuit. Disabled anti-lock brakes and electronic stability control can increase the risk of a crash or injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the ESP unit, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2021 Mercedes-Benz GLB250 is a versatile and well-appointed luxury crossover that appeals to families and commuters alike. While overall powertrain performance is strong, used car buyers should perform a detailed inspection and test drive to ensure the transmission and electronics operate seamlessly.
