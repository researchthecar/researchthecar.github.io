# 2005 Mercedes-Benz S 417

### Overview
The 2005 Mercedes-Benz S 417, a luxury sedan known for its comfort and advanced features for its time, generally presents a solid reliability record. As with any high-end vehicle of this vintage, potential owners should be aware of specific areas that may require attention. While the core powertrain components are robust, the complexity of its electronic systems and the age of the vehicle can lead to certain electrical gremlins. Suspension systems, while designed for a smooth ride, can also be a point of concern due to the sophistication of the technology employed. Overall, the S 417 offers a luxurious experience, but proactive maintenance and awareness of common issues are key to ensuring long-term satisfaction.

## Suspension

### ABC (Active Body Control)
**Hydraulic Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle leaning to one side, 'ABC Malfunction' warning on the dashboard, stiff ride.
- **Solution:** Repair or replace leaking hoses, struts, or pump. Fluid flush and replacement.
- **Cost:** $1,500 - $4,000+

**Pump Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of hydraulic pressure, vehicle sagging, loud whining noise from the pump.
- **Solution:** Replace the ABC hydraulic pump.
- **Cost:** $1,000 - $2,500


### Standard Air Suspension
**Air Spring Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle sagging, especially overnight; compressor running frequently.
- **Solution:** Replace leaking air springs or seals.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1,200 per corner


## Electrical

### Infotainment System (COMAND)
**Screen Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** Flickering screen, dead pixels, failure to power on.
- **Solution:** Repair or replace the COMAND unit or screen.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1,500


### SAM (Signal Acquisition Module)
**Corrosion/Water Intrusion**
- **Symptoms:** Various electrical components failing intermittently or completely (lights, wipers, windows), CAN bus errors.
- **Solution:** Inspect and clean SAM module, repair water ingress points, replace SAM if damaged.
- **Cost:** $400 - $1,000


### Keyless Go System
**Door Handle Sensors**
- **Symptoms:** Keyless entry/start not functioning, intermittent operation.
- **Solution:** Replace faulty door handle sensors.
- **Cost:** $200 - $500 per handle

### Summary Verdict
The 2005 Mercedes-Benz S 417 is a luxurious vehicle that, while generally well-built, requires careful consideration regarding its advanced systems. The most common areas for potential concern are the Active Body Control (ABC) suspension and various electrical components, particularly the SAM modules and infotainment system, which can be susceptible to water intrusion or age-related failures. While the engines and transmissions are typically robust, owners should budget for potential repairs related to the complex suspension and electronics. Regular maintenance and prompt attention to warning lights are crucial for maintaining the S 417's comfort and reliability.
