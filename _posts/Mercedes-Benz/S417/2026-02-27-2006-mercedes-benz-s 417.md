# 2006 Mercedes-Benz S 417

### Overview
The 2006 Mercedes-Benz S 417, a flagship luxury sedan, is generally known for its robust engineering and high-quality materials. However, like any complex vehicle, it has specific areas that have seen more attention from owners regarding reliability. This analysis focuses on documented issues beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential concerns for owners and prospective buyers. The S 417 was offered with sophisticated engine and transmission options, contributing to its performance but also presenting unique reliability considerations. Understanding these potential weak points can help in proactive maintenance and informed decision-making.

---


## Engine

### 5.5L V8 (M273)
**Timing Chain Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Rattling noise from the front of the engine, especially on startup; check engine light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of timing chain, tensioners, and guides.
- **Cost:** $1,500 - $3,000

**Oil Leaks (Valve Covers/Oil Pan)**
- **Symptoms:** Visible oil stains under the vehicle, burning oil smell.
- **Solution:** Replacement of valve cover gaskets or oil pan gasket.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1,200


### 6.0L V12 (M275)
**Ignition Coil Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Misfires, rough idling, reduced engine power, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of faulty ignition coils.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600 per coil

**Coolant Leaks (Turbocharged Models)**
- **Symptoms:** Low coolant level, visible coolant leaks, overheating.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of coolant hoses, intercooler, or radiator.
- **Cost:** $800 - $2,000


## Transmission

### 7G-TRONIC (7-Speed Automatic)
**Transmission Control Module (TCM) Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Erratic shifting, transmission warning lights, inability to select gears.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the Transmission Control Module.
- **Cost:** $1,000 - $2,500

**Torque Converter Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Shuddering during acceleration, slipping gears, delayed engagement.
- **Solution:** Rebuild or replacement of the torque converter.
- **Cost:** $1,200 - $2,800


## Suspension

### ABC (Active Body Control)
**Hydraulic Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle sagging, suspension warning lights, fluid leaks under the vehicle.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of hydraulic lines, struts, or pump.
- **Cost:** $1,500 - $5,000+

**Pump Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of hydraulic pressure, vehicle suspension dropping, loud whining noise from pump.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the ABC hydraulic pump.
- **Cost:** $1,000 - $2,500


### Standard Air Suspension
**Air Spring Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle sagging on one or more corners, compressor running excessively.
- **Solution:** Replacement of leaking air springs.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1,000 per corner


## Electrical

### General Electrical System
**SAM Unit (Signal Acquisition Module) Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Various warning lights, malfunctioning interior/exterior lights, power window issues.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of the SAM unit.
- **Cost:** $800 - $2,000

**EIS (Electronic Ignition Switch) Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Key not recognized, inability to start the vehicle.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of the EIS module.
- **Cost:** $600 - $1,500


## Exterior

### Body Panels and Trim
**No Major Issues**
- **Symptoms:** No widespread issues reported.
- **Solution:** No widespread issues reported.
- **Cost:** No widespread issues reported.


## Interior

### Infotainment and Controls
**COMAND System Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Screen freezing, navigation issues, audio problems.
- **Solution:** Software updates or replacement of the COMAND unit.
- **Cost:** $500 - $2,000


### Seat Actuators
**Malfunctioning Power Seats**
- **Symptoms:** Seats not moving, stuck in position, unusual noises.
- **Solution:** Replacement of faulty seat motors or control modules.
- **Cost:** $300 - $800 per seat


--- 
### Summary Verdict
The 2006 Mercedes-Benz S 417 is a luxurious and capable vehicle, but its advanced technology and complex systems mean potential owners should be aware of specific reliability concerns. The ABC suspension system, while providing an exceptional ride, is a common source of expensive repairs due to hydraulic leaks and component failures. Engine-wise, the V8 and V12 engines can experience timing chain wear and oil leaks, respectively, requiring significant investment. Electrical gremlins, particularly with the SAM and EIS modules, are also noted. Transmission issues with the 7G-TRONIC, such as TCM failures, can also arise. While the exterior is generally robust, interior electronics like the COMAND system and seat actuators can develop faults. Regular, specialized maintenance is crucial for mitigating these potential issues and ensuring the longevity of this high-end sedan.
