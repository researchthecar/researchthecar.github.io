# 2022 Mercedes-Benz GLS450

### Overview
The 2022 Mercedes-Benz GLS450 is a luxury full-size SUV known for its opulent interior, advanced technology, and comfortable ride. As a flagship model, it generally exhibits strong build quality and reliability, reflecting Mercedes-Benz's commitment to premium engineering. While no vehicle is entirely immune to issues, the GLS450 for this model year has demonstrated a commendable track record. Owners can expect a sophisticated driving experience with a focus on passenger comfort and safety. This analysis focuses on documented technical reliability concerns beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential areas of attention for prospective and current owners.

## Suspension

### AIRMATIC Air Suspension
**Air Spring Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle may sag on one corner, uneven ride height, warning lights on the dashboard.
- **Solution:** Replacement of leaking air springs or associated lines.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1500 per corner

### Summary Verdict
The 2022 Mercedes-Benz GLS450 demonstrates strong overall reliability, particularly in its powertrain. The 3.0L inline-six engine and 9-speed automatic transmission are robust and have not shown widespread issues. The primary areas of concern, though not universally reported, are related to the AIRMATIC air suspension system, where air spring leaks can occur, and occasional software glitches within the MBUX infotainment system. Premature 12V battery failure has also been noted by some owners. These issues are generally repairable, with costs varying depending on the component and labor rates. For a luxury SUV of this caliber, the GLS450 presents a generally reliable ownership experience, with most potential issues being manageable.
