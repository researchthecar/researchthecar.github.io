# 2021 Mercedes-Benz GLS450 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2021 Mercedes-Benz GLS450 offers a luxurious, spacious ride packed with cutting-edge technology and a refined powertrain. However, prospective buyers should carefully review owner-reported reliability trends, particularly concerning its complex 48-volt electrical system and certain body and trim components. While it delivers exceptional comfort and prestige, upkeep and diagnostic troubleshooting for its advanced tech suite can require patience and specialized service.

---

## Engine

### 3.0L Turbocharged Inline-6 Mild Hybrid

The GLS450 features a 3.0-liter turbocharged inline-6 engine paired with a 48-volt mild-hybrid system (EQ Boost), producing approximately 362 horsepower and 369 lb-ft of torque. This powertrain is generally praised for its smooth power delivery and seamless stop-start capability, though the integrated electrical architecture demands attentive maintenance.

## Transmission

### 9-Speed Automatic Transmission

Power is routed through a 9-speed automatic transmission paired with 4MATIC all-wheel drive. It offers crisp, smooth gear changes tailored to maximize highway efficiency and low-end responsiveness.

## Suspension

### Wheels and Rims

The heavy curb weight places significant stress on suspension and wheel components, making periodic inspections of rims and tires wise for used-car buyers.

**Rear Wheel Cracks**
- **Symptoms:** Rear wheels develop stress cracks leading to gradual tire pressure loss without any reported impact or road hazard.
- **Solution:** Inspect wheels for structural fractures during routine maintenance and replace any compromised rims.

## Electrical

### 48V Battery and Charging System

The electrical architecture relies heavily on advanced modules and a 48-volt secondary system. While robust when functioning properly, occasional battery failures and display glitches have been documented by owners.

**48-Volt Battery Failure and Power Loss**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers experience sudden dashboard warnings for the 48V battery, loss of acceleration (limp mode restricting speed), and unexpected engine shut-offs while driving.
- **Solution:** Take the vehicle to an authorized dealer or specialist to diagnose the 48V system and replace faulty battery modules or related control units under warranty where applicable.

**Instrument Cluster Blackout**
- **Symptoms:** The digital cluster and head-up display intermittently go completely black, leaving the driver without speedometer or vehicle status information.
- **Solution:** Software updates or module replacements performed by a qualified service center may be required to resolve persistent display glitches.

## Exterior

### Sunroof

Exterior glass and trim components generally hold up well, though isolated reports highlight issues with large glass sunroof panels.

**Panoptic Sunroof Shattering**
- **Symptoms:** The glass sunroof panel spontaneously shatters or pops while driving, dropping glass fragments into the cabin.
- **Solution:** Inspect the sunroof assembly for alignment or factory stress points, and replace the glass panel if damaged.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V354000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=GLS450&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The MBUX multimedia system software may cause the display to remain black, or reboot after startup. This could result in the rearview camera image not displaying properly.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 20V089000: Electrical System: Instrument Cluster/Panel](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=GLS450&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 101, "Control and Displays." If the interior switches are not illuminated, they may be more difficult to find and operate, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** MBUSA will notify owners, and dealers will update the instrument cluster software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V832000: Visibility:Glass, Side/Rear](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=GLS450&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The rear window may be insufficiently bonded to the tailgate, which could allow the rear glass to detach. Detached rear glass can become a road hazard, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the rear window, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V058000: Electrical System:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=GLS450&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The software design of the communication module may fail to communicate the correct vehicle location for the emergency call system (eCall) in the event of a crash. Failure of the eCall system may result in emergency responders being dispatched to the wrong location, increasing the risk of injury following a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 22V231000: Electrical System:Wiring:Interior/Under Dash](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=GLS450&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** A ground cable bolt for the 48V ground connection under the front passenger seat may not be secured properly, which can cause the electrical cable to overheat. An overheated electrical cable can increase the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and tighten the ground cable bolt, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V365000: Electrical System:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=GLS450&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** A disabled eCall system would prevent a vehicle occupant from contacting the emergency services call center in an emergency, potentially delaying emergency responders and increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 22V680000: Structure:Body](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=GLS450&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** Detached trim bars may cause a road hazard and increase the risk of a crash or injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and secure the rear window trim bars, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V732000: Seats:Mid/Rear Assembly](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=GLS450&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** Due to a manufacturing error, the locks on the third-row seatbacks may fail in the event of a crash. A seat with a seatback lock failure may not properly restrain occupants, increasing the risk of injury during a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the third-row seatback, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2021 Mercedes-Benz GLS450 is a premier luxury cruiser offering supreme comfort and advanced technology. Used-car shoppers should pay close attention to the health of the 48-volt electrical system and verify that all software updates and recalls have been completed by a certified service center prior to purchase.
