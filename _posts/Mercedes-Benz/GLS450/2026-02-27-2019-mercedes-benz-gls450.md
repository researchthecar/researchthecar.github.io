# 2019 Mercedes-Benz GLS450 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

Shopping for a used 2019 Mercedes-Benz GLS450 offers a blend of luxury, spaciousness, and commanding performance. However, prospective buyers should review owner-reported concerns regarding powertrain responsiveness, suspension features, and occasional electrical or body glitches to ensure a trouble-free ownership experience.

---

## Engine

### 3.0L Twin-Turbo V6

The 3.0-liter twin-turbocharged V6 engine produces around 362 horsepower and 369 lb-ft of torque, delivering robust acceleration for a large luxury SUV. Overall, it is generally dependable, though owners should monitor for occasional accessory or oil control component leaks.

**Oil Pump Solenoid Valve Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may experience low oil pressure warnings and illuminated check engine lights on the dashboard.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified technician inspect the oil pump solenoid and replace faulty components as necessary.

## Transmission

### 9G-TRONIC 9-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 9-speed automatic transmission pairs with standard all-wheel drive to deliver smooth, efficient shifting. It is mostly robust, but electronic shift communication issues can occasionally arise.

**Shift Interlock / Failure to Respond**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may occasionally fail to respond when attempting to shift out of park into drive, reverse, or neutral.
- **Solution:** Have the shift module, brake pedal switch, and transmission control unit diagnosed by a specialist.

## Suspension

### Active Curve / Air Suspension

The air suspension and stability systems provide a plush ride quality typical of flagship German SUVs, but they can become costly to repair if hydraulic components develop leaks.

**Active Curve System Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers report dashboard warnings regarding the Active Curve system, sometimes accompanied by hydraulic fluid leaks.
- **Solution:** Inspect hydraulic lines, fluid levels, and system sensors, replacing leaking seals or faulty pumps.

## Brakes

### Parking Brake System

The braking system provides strong stopping power for this heavy luxury vehicle, though electronic parking brake actuators require careful inspection.

**Automatic Parking Brake Engagement Glitch**
- **Symptoms:** The electronic parking brake assist may engage unexpectedly or display persistent warning messages upon startup.
- **Solution:** Inspect the parking brake actuator, software calibration, and related switches.

## Exterior

### Body and Seat Electronics

Advanced onboard electronics govern numerous convenience and safety features, making thorough pre-purchase testing of all cabin modules essential.

**Seat and Hatch Malfunction Warnings**
- **Symptoms:** The dashboard may display erroneous error messages such as third-row seat faults or hood-open warnings while securely latched.
- **Solution:** Check seat wiring harnesses, latch microswitches, and clear any stored diagnostic trouble codes.

### Glass and Roof Components

Safety equipment is comprehensive, featuring advanced driver aids and robust occupant protection systems, though rare glass integrity issues have been noted by owners.

**Spontaneous Sunroof Glass Shattering**
- **Symptoms:** The panoramic sunroof glass may unexpectedly fracture or shatter outward while driving at highway speeds.
- **Solution:** Replace the damaged glass panel and inspect the frame for alignment issues or stress points.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V058000: Electrical System:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=GLS450&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** The software design of the communication module may fail to communicate the correct vehicle location for the emergency call system (eCall) in the event of a crash. Failure of the eCall system may result in emergency responders being dispatched to the wrong location, increasing the risk of injury following a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 22V365000: Electrical System:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=GLS450&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** A disabled eCall system would prevent a vehicle occupant from contacting the emergency services call center in an emergency, potentially delaying emergency responders and increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2019 Mercedes-Benz GLS450 remains a highly capable, spacious luxury hauler with strong performance credentials. While major mechanical failures are infrequent, buyers should carefully evaluate the complex air suspension, electronics, and transmission shift responsiveness during a pre-purchase inspection.
