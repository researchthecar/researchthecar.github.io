# 2017 Mercedes-Benz GLS450 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

Shopping for a pre-owned 2017 Mercedes-Benz GLS450 offers a blend of luxury, three-row spaciousness, and robust performance. However, prospective buyers should carefully review historical owner feedback regarding potential mechanical and electrical hiccups. While many owners enjoy a smooth ride and refined capability, keeping a close eye on known trouble spots can help prevent unexpected repair bills.

---

## Engine

### 3.0L Twin-Turbo V6

The 3.0-liter twin-turbocharged V6 engine delivers approximately 362 horsepower and 369 lb-ft of torque, providing brisk acceleration for this large SUV. It is generally praised for its refined power delivery, though proper maintenance is critical to avoid long-term accessory and fuel system concerns.

**Fuel System and Electrical Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may experience unexpected check engine lights, battery drain, or fuel odors.
- **Solution:** Inspect fuel lines and battery terminals; replace degraded components or damaged batteries as needed.
- **Cost:** Variable depending on diagnosed electrical component or fuel rail service.

## Transmission

### 9-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 9-speed automatic transmission pairs with the standard 4MATIC all-wheel-drive system to offer smooth gear changes and optimized highway efficiency. While generally robust, owners should verify proper shift response during a pre-purchase test drive.

**Erratic Shifting and Park Engagement**
- **Symptoms:** The transmission may fail to properly engage Park or experience unpredictable shifting behavior while driving.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified technician inspect the valve body, software calibration, and shifting mechanism.

## Suspension

### Airmatic Suspension

The airmatic suspension system provides a plush, adjustable ride quality typical of luxury German engineering, but air springs and compressors can wear out over time and require expensive upkeep.

**Air Suspension Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may drop, fail to inflate air pressure, result in a bumpy ride, or cause a temporary loss of vehicle control.
- **Solution:** Replace failing air struts, compressors, or leaking air lines.
- **Cost:** $3,900

## Steering

### Power Steering System

Steering feel is generally well-regarded for a vehicle of this size, though electronic assist failures warrant attention if warning lights appear.

**Power Steering Loss**
- **Symptoms:** A power steering malfunction message may appear upon startup, followed by a complete loss of power steering assistance while driving.
- **Solution:** Inspect power steering electrical connections, fluid levels, or electronic assist modules.

## Exterior

### Exterior Lighting

The exterior lighting system provides advanced illumination features, though long-term degradation of headlight assemblies has been noted by several owners.

**Headlight Dimming and Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Owners report fading low beam headlights and poor forward visibility during night driving.
- **Solution:** Inspect projector assemblies, bulbs, and ballasts; replace worn lighting components.

## Interior

### Seating

Interior cabin amenities offer premium comfort features, though complex motorized adjustments can occasionally experience mechanical resistance.

**Seat Heater Malfunction and Track Binding**
- **Symptoms:** Seats may become excessively hot or fail to move forward and backward properly on their tracks.
- **Solution:** Inspect seat adjustment motors, wiring harnesses, and climate elements underneath the seat.

## Summary Verdict

The 2017 Mercedes-Benz GLS450 is a capable and luxurious family cruiser, but prospective buyers should approach with an awareness of potential maintenance costs. Pay close attention to the air suspension, transmission behavior, and electrical systems during a pre-purchase inspection. Securing a comprehensive service history can significantly reduce the risk of unexpected repair expenses.
