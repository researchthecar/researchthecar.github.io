# 2021 Mercedes-Benz S560 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a luxury flagship sedan, understanding owner-reported experiences can help you navigate potential ownership costs and maintenance items. The vehicle brings advanced technology, sophisticated engineering, and high-end comfort to the market, though prospective buyers should review recurring owner concerns regarding wheel and tire durability, camera and driver assistance systems, and powertrain components. Evaluating these areas can help ensure a smoother ownership experience.

---

## Engine

### 4.0L Twin-Turbo V8 Engine

Equipped with a powerful 4.0-liter twin-turbocharged V8 engine delivering substantial horsepower and torque, providing effortless acceleration. Buyers should inspect service history closely for any historical engine management or actuator repairs.

**Camtronic Actuator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle loses acceleration capability and illuminates the check engine light.
- **Solution:** Have the vehicle diagnosed by a qualified specialist or dealer to replace the failed actuator component.
- **Cost:** $9000

**Low-Speed Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may experience unexpected engine stalling when traveling at low speeds.
- **Solution:** Inspect the engine control modules and fuel delivery systems; driving in alternate modes has been reported by owners as a temporary workaround.

### 3.0L Turbocharged Inline-6 Engine (Hybrid)

Features a 3.0-liter turbocharged inline-6 engine paired with a hybrid system, balancing refined power delivery with enhanced efficiency. This option offers smooth everyday performance suited for executive commuting.

## Transmission

### 9-Speed Automatic Transmission

Features a 9-speed automatic transmission paired with rear-wheel drive or all-wheel drive configurations, delivering seamless gear changes and optimized highway cruising. The transmission generally operates smoothly, though regular fluid maintenance is recommended.

## Suspension

### Wheels and Tires

Factory large-diameter wheels and low-profile tires add visual appeal but can be vulnerable to road impacts.

**Rim Cracks and Tire Failures**
- **Symptoms:** Large AMG alloy wheels are prone to developing structural cracks, leading to rapid air loss, tire sidewall bubbling, and frequent tire replacements.
- **Solution:** Inspect wheels thoroughly for hidden structural cracks and consider wheel replacement or tire protection packages if driving on rough roads.

## Electrical

### Camera and Lighting Systems

Extensive electronics and driver aids require precise sensor calibration to maintain reliable functionality.

**Camera System Availability Warnings**
- **Symptoms:** The instrument cluster displays sudden warning messages indicating that camera systems are unavailable or dirty.
- **Solution:** Clean sensor lens areas and have dealership software calibrations or hardware connections inspected.

**Erratic High Beam Operation**
- **Symptoms:** Adaptive high beam assist functions fail to detect ambient lighting accurately, causing headlights to switch unpredictably.
- **Solution:** Check headlight sensor calibrations and software updates at an authorized service center.

## Safety

### Forward Collision Avoidance

Advanced safety features provide vital protection but rely heavily on clear sensor visibility and software stability.

**Inadvertent Emergency Braking**
- **Symptoms:** The forward collision avoidance system activates unexpectedly while driving, causing the vehicle to brake harshly.
- **Solution:** Have dealer technicians inspect windshield sensor housings, camera alignment, and software campaign updates.

Purchasing a pre-owned luxury flagship requires careful inspection due to complex electronics, advanced driver aids, and high-performance wheel setups. While the vehicle offers exceptional comfort and performance, buyers should verify that all open campaigns are completed and thoroughly inspect the wheels, tires, and camera systems prior to purchase.
