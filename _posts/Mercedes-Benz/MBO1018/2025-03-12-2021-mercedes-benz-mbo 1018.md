The 2021 Mercedes-Benz Metris Van (MBO 1018) is a versatile cargo and passenger van known for its maneuverability and fuel efficiency compared to larger vans. It is powered by a 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine producing 208 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque. This engine is paired with a 9G-TRONIC 9-speed automatic transmission. People often appreciate the Metris for its compact size, making it easier to navigate city streets and park, as well as its comfortable ride and Mercedes-Benz build quality. Common complaints often center around the vehicle's smaller size compared to full-size vans, limited payload capacity, and some reliability concerns.

**Engine**

**2.0L Turbocharged Four-Cylinder**

*   **High-Pressure Fuel Pump Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Engine stalling, difficulty starting, fuel smell, check engine light.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the high-pressure fuel pump.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Camshaft Adjuster Solenoid Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rough idle, decreased fuel economy, check engine light, engine noise.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the camshaft adjuster solenoid.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $700+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Turbocharger Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Whining noises, lack of power, check engine light, oil leaks.
    *   Solution: Inspection of turbocharger for shaft play or damage, potential replacement of the turbocharger.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1500 - $3500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**9G-TRONIC 9-Speed Automatic**

*   **Rough or Delayed Shifting**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Harsh shifting between gears, noticeable delays when shifting, particularly when cold.
    *   Solution: Software update, valve body replacement, or complete transmission replacement in severe cases.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 (software) - $6000+ (transmission replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Torque Converter Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Shuddering at low speeds, especially during light acceleration; possible stalling.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the torque converter.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1500 - $3000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Rear Suspension Sagging/Weak Leaf Springs**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rear of the van sagging, especially when loaded; reduced ride height; rough ride.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the leaf springs.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Malfunctioning Sliding Door**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Sliding door not opening or closing properly, getting stuck, error messages on the dash.
    *   Solution: Inspection of the door latch, wiring harness, and motor. Possible replacement of faulty components.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Sensor Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The tire pressure monitor system gives inaccurate readings, or the sensors fail to work all together.
    *   Solution: Replacement of sensors.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Infotainment System Glitches**
    *   Description/Symptoms: System freezing, unresponsive touchscreen, Bluetooth connectivity issues, navigation errors.
    *   Solution: Software updates, system reset, or replacement of the head unit.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 (software update) - $2000+ (head unit replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Sliding Door Track Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Difficulty opening or closing the sliding door, grinding noises, visible wear or damage to the track.
    *   Solution: Cleaning, lubrication, or replacement of the sliding door track components.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Rattling/Squeaking Noises**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Various interior panels rattling or squeaking, especially on rough roads.
    *   Solution: Tightening loose panels, adding sound-deadening material, or replacing worn clips and fasteners.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2021 Mercedes-Benz Metris can have some frustrating issues. Engine issues such as high-pressure fuel pump failures and turbocharger problems can be costly to repair. Transmission issues, particularly rough shifting, can also require significant expense to remedy. Suspension, electrical, exterior, and interior issues can be a nuisance as well. Overall, owners should be prepared for potential repairs that could range from a few hundred to several thousand dollars, depending on the severity.
