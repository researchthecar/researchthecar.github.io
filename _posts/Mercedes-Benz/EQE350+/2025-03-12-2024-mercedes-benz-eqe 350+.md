The 2024 Mercedes-Benz EQE 350+ is an all-electric luxury sedan offering a blend of comfort, technology, and performance. The EQE 350+ configuration features a single electric motor producing 288 horsepower and 417 lb-ft of torque, powering the rear wheels. Other EQE configurations include dual-motor all-wheel-drive options with increased power output. People generally like the smooth ride, luxurious interior, and advanced technology features of the EQE. Common dislikes often revolve around the infotainment system complexity, range limitations compared to some competitors, and the high price point.

**Electric Motor**

**Single Motor RWD (EQE 350+)**

*   **Potential for Reduced Range in Cold Weather**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported a noticeable decrease in range during cold weather conditions due to increased energy consumption for heating the cabin and battery pack. This is a common characteristic of electric vehicles.
    *   Solution: Preconditioning the cabin and battery while plugged in can help mitigate range loss. Using seat heaters and steering wheel heaters instead of the full climate control system can also conserve energy.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: N/A - inherent characteristic
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **MBUX Infotainment System Glitches**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported occasional glitches with the MBUX infotainment system, including slow response times, screen freezes, and difficulty connecting to smartphones.
    *   Solution: Performing a system reset (often involving holding down the power button for an extended period) may temporarily resolve the issue. A software update from Mercedes-Benz may be necessary to permanently fix the problem.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 (software updates) - $100+ (diagnosis if hardware issue)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **12V Battery Drain**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have experienced issues with the 12V auxiliary battery draining prematurely, leading to starting problems or system malfunctions. This can happen if the vehicle sits unused for extended periods.
    *   Solution: Ensuring the vehicle is charged regularly, even if not driven frequently, can help maintain the 12V battery. In some cases, the battery may need to be replaced.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300+ (battery replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Creaking Noises from Trim Pieces**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported creaking noises emanating from various trim pieces within the cabin, particularly on uneven road surfaces.
    *   Solution: Tightening or lubricating the affected trim pieces may resolve the issue. In some cases, replacement of the trim may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 (DIY fix) - $100+ (dealer repair)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Malfunctioning Door Handles**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The flush-mounted door handles may occasionally fail to extend or retract properly, requiring multiple attempts to open or close the door.
    *   Solution: A software update may address the issue. In some cases, the door handle mechanism may need to be replaced.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 (software update) - $300+ (door handle mechanism replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

No Issues Found

**Summary**

The 2024 Mercedes-Benz EQE 350+ presents a mix of minor electrical and interior issues along with potential range reduction in cold weather, which is inherent to EVs. The most prevalent issues are related to the MBUX infotainment system and trim noises. While some issues can be resolved with software updates or minor adjustments, others, like door handle malfunctions, could require more costly repairs. Overall, the severity of reported issues is generally moderate, with potential repair costs ranging from minimal to a few hundred dollars.
