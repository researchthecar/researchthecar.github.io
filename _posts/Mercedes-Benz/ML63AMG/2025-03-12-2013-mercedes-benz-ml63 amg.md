The 2013 Mercedes-Benz ML63 AMG is a high-performance SUV that blends luxury with impressive power. It was exclusively offered with a 5.5-liter twin-turbo V8 engine producing 518 horsepower and 516 lb-ft of torque (or 550 hp/560 lb-ft with the optional AMG Performance Package) paired with a 7-speed AMG Speedshift Plus 7G-Tronic automatic transmission. Owners often praise its exhilarating acceleration, comfortable ride, and luxurious interior. Common complaints revolve around its high running costs (fuel, maintenance, repairs), complex electronics that can be prone to issues, and the potential for expensive repairs typical of high-performance German vehicles.

**Engine**

**5.5L Twin-Turbo V8 (M157)**

*   **Timing Chain Tensioner Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rattling noise on cold start, potential for severe engine damage if the chain jumps.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the timing chain tensioner and potentially the timing chain itself.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $4,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Turbocharger Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, whining noise from the engine, potential oil leaks.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the faulty turbocharger(s).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $3,000 - $8,000+ (per turbo)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Fuel Injector Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Misfires, rough idle, poor fuel economy, check engine light.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the faulty fuel injector(s).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+ (depending on number of injectors replaced)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Oil Leaks (Valve Cover Gaskets, Oil Filter Housing, Rear Main Seal)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Visible oil leaks, burning smell, low oil level.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the leaking gaskets or seals.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,500+ (depending on the location and severity of the leak)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**7-Speed AMG Speedshift Plus 7G-Tronic (722.9)**

*   **Valve Body Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Harsh or delayed shifting, slipping between gears, erratic shifting behavior.
    *   Solution: Valve body replacement or rebuild.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Torque Converter Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Shuddering at low speeds, stalling, abnormal noises.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the torque converter.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,200 - $3,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Airmatic Suspension Issues (Air Leaks, Compressor Failure, Strut Failure)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Vehicle sagging, uneven ride height, "Suspension Malfunction" warning light, loud compressor noise.
    *   Solution: Repairing or replacing the leaking air springs, replacing the air compressor, or replacing the struts.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $4,000+ (per strut or compressor)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Electronic Control Unit (ECU) or Module Failures**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Various warning lights, erratic behavior of vehicle systems, starting issues.
    *   Solution: ECU/module replacement or reprogramming.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $3,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Battery Drain Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Dead battery, difficulty starting.
    *   Solution: Diagnosing and repairing the source of the parasitic drain (often related to faulty modules or aftermarket accessories).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $1,000+ (depending on the source of the drain)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **No Issues Found**

**Interior**

*   **Command System Malfunctions**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Frozen screen, unresponsive buttons, Navigation System failures, Bluetooth connectivity problems.
    *   Solution: Software update, replacement of the Command Unit.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2013 Mercedes-Benz ML63 AMG is known for its power and luxury but comes with a risk of potentially expensive repairs. Engine issues, particularly related to the timing chain tensioner and turbochargers, can be quite costly. The airmatic suspension and electrical systems are also prone to problems, adding to the potential for high maintenance costs. Potential buyers should be aware of these issues and factor them into their ownership budget. While some issues are minor and inexpensive, others can be quite severe and lead to thousands of dollars in repairs.
