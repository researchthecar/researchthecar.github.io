**Introduction to the 2001 Mercedes-Benz C240**

The 2001 Mercedes-Benz C240 is a popular luxury compact sedan that provides a blend of performance and comfort. Equipped with a 2.6-liter V6 engine rated at 168 horsepower and 181 lb-ft of torque, it comes with a 5-speed automatic transmission. The vehicle boasts a sporty and elegant appearance, with a stylish exterior and a well-appointed interior.

While the C240 is known for its reliability and durability, it has experienced some common issues that owners may encounter. Below is a comprehensive list of these issues, categorized by the major components:

**Engine**

**Engine:** 2.6-liter V6

**Issue:** Excessive Oil Consumption
Description/Symptoms: Burning excessive amounts of oil, leading to low oil levels and potential engine damage.
Solution: Replacing valve stem seals or piston rings.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**Transmission Option:** 5-Speed Automatic

**Issue:** Delayed Shifting
Description/Symptoms: Transmission hesitation or delay in shifting gears, especially during acceleration or gear changes.
Solution: Updating transmission control module (TCM) software or replacing solenoids.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,200+
Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

**Issue:** Worn Control Arms
Description/Symptoms: Front-end clunking, vibration during braking or acceleration, and uneven tire wear.
Solution: Replacing worn control arms.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800+
Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

**Issue:** Faulty Window Regulator
Description/Symptoms: Windows failing to operate properly, getting stuck, or falling off the track.
Solution: Replacing window regulator motor or assembly.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

**Issue:** Sunroof Drainage Problems
Description/Symptoms: Water leaking into the cabin through the sunroof, causing water damage and musty odors.
Solution: Cleaning or replacing clogged sunroof drains.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

**Issue:** Fogged Headlights
Description/Symptoms: Headlights becoming clouded or fogged, reducing visibility.
Solution: Replacing headlight assemblies.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,200+
Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2001 Mercedes-Benz C240 is generally a reliable vehicle, but it has experienced some issues that can be costly to repair. The most severe and expensive issues include excessive oil consumption and fogged headlights, while others like delayed shifting and sunroof drainage problems are less costly but still warrant attention. Owners should be aware of these potential issues and factor in the potential repair costs when considering purchasing or maintaining this vehicle.
