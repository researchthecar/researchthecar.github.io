# 2005 Mercedes-Benz C240

### Overview
The 2005 Mercedes-Benz C240, part of the W203 generation, is generally considered a reliable luxury sedan, offering a blend of comfort, performance, and Mercedes-Benz build quality. While it doesn't suffer from widespread catastrophic failures, like any vehicle of its age, certain components are more prone to issues than others. Owners often report satisfaction with the overall driving experience and the longevity of the core powertrain. However, potential buyers should be aware of common electrical gremlins and some specific wear items that may require attention. Regular maintenance is key to ensuring the continued reliability of this model.

---


## Engine

### 2.6L V6 (M112 E26)
**Oil Leaks (Valve Cover Gaskets, Oil Cooler Seals)**
- **Symptoms:** Visible oil on engine components, burning oil smell, low oil level.
- **Solution:** Replacement of valve cover gaskets and/or oil cooler seals.
- **Cost:** $300 - $700

**Coolant Leaks (Reservoir, Hoses)**
- **Symptoms:** Low coolant level, visible coolant puddles, overheating.
- **Solution:** Replacement of coolant reservoir, hoses, or associated clamps.
- **Cost:** $200 - $500


## Transmission

### 5G-Tronic Automatic (722.6)
**Transmission Control Module (TCM) Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Harsh shifting, slipping gears, 'limp mode', check engine light.
- **Solution:** Replacement or repair of the TCM.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1500

**Conductor Plate Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Erratic shifting, no gear engagement, transmission warning light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the conductor plate and associated sensors.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1000


## Suspension

### Standard Chassis
**Worn Control Arm Bushings**
- **Symptoms:** Clunking noises over bumps, vague steering feel, uneven tire wear.
- **Solution:** Replacement of front control arm bushings or the entire control arm assembly.
- **Cost:** $400 - $800

**Sagging Springs**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle sits lower than normal, uneven ride height, poor handling.
- **Solution:** Replacement of front or rear coil springs.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600


## Electrical

### General Electrical System
**Window Regulator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Power windows not operating, grinding noise when operating.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the window regulator mechanism.
- **Cost:** $250 - $500 per window

**Instrument Cluster Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** Dim or flickering displays, non-functional gauges, warning lights staying on.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of the instrument cluster.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1200

**ABS/ESP Sensor Issues**
- **Symptoms:** ABS and ESP warning lights illuminated on the dashboard.
- **Solution:** Replacement of faulty ABS wheel speed sensors or ESP module.
- **Cost:** $300 - $700

## Interior

### Cabin Components
**HVAC Blower Motor Resistor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Fan only works on highest setting or not at all.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the blower motor resistor.
- **Cost:** $100 - $250

**Sunroof Drain Clogs**
- **Symptoms:** Water leaks into the cabin, musty smell, potential headliner damage.
- **Solution:** Clearing of sunroof drain tubes.
- **Cost:** $100 - $300

### Summary Verdict
The 2005 Mercedes-Benz C240 is a competent luxury sedan with a generally good reliability record for its age. The V6 engine and automatic transmission are robust, though specific components like the conductor plate or TCM can be costly to repair if they fail. Common issues tend to be electrical in nature, such as window regulators and instrument cluster problems, and some suspension wear items like control arm bushings are expected. Proactive maintenance, especially fluid changes for the transmission and addressing any oil or coolant leaks promptly, will significantly contribute to the longevity and enjoyment of this vehicle. Overall, it represents a solid choice for a used luxury sedan if well-maintained.
