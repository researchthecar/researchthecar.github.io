# 2020 Mercedes-Benz GLE350 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2020 Mercedes-Benz GLE350 combines luxury, advanced technology, and practical utility, making it an attractive option on the used market. However, buyers should approach with a measure of caution. Owner reports submitted to federal safety regulators highlight recurring trouble spots ranging from powertrain glitches to electrical and suspension concerns.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4 (GLE350)

The standard 2.0-liter turbocharged inline-4 engine produces approximately 255 horsepower and 273 lb-ft of torque, offering adequate acceleration for daily driving. While generally smooth, used buyers should thoroughly inspect the powertrain for historical misfires and intermittent stalling complaints.

**Engine Misfire and Loss of Power**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers experience sudden engine misfires, rough idling, metallic knocking sounds, and unexpected loss of motive power while driving.
- **Solution:** Requires diagnostic testing to identify failing ignition components, spark plugs, or internal cylinder issues, often needing part replacements.

**Engine Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may unexpectedly stall during operation, posing significant safety hazards in traffic.
- **Solution:** Professional diagnosis of fuel, electrical, or cooling system interlocks to locate the root cause of the stall.

## Transmission

### 9G-TRONIC 9-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 9-speed automatic transmission delivers power smoothly across various driving conditions, though some owners have reported control module and shifting glitches.

**Transmission Control Module and Shifting Faults**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may unexpectedly slip into neutral when shifted into drive or reverse, or experience erratic shifting behavior linked to valve body failures.
- **Solution:** Reprogramming, software updates, or replacement of the transmission control module or valve body assembly.
- **Cost:** $1,000

## Suspension

### Rear Suspension Shocks

Suspension components provide a plush, balanced ride, but rear shock absorbers have been noted by owners to wear out prematurely.

**Rear Shock Absorber Leakage and Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Rattling noises coming from the rear end of the vehicle when traveling over bumps, often caused by leaking shock absorbers.
- **Solution:** Inspect rear shocks for fluid leaks and replace damaged shock absorber assemblies.

## Electrical

### Infotainment and Lighting Electronics

The vehicle's cabin relies heavily on complex digital displays and advanced electronic control units, which can occasionally experience software or hardware glitches.

**Infotainment Screen and Headlamp Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** Main multimedia screens freezing or failing entirely, loss of backup camera functionality, and error messages related to headlamp control modules.
- **Solution:** Software resets, module replacements, or updating vehicle firmware at a qualified service center.

## Safety

### Seat Belts and Airbag Sensors

Safety equipment generally performs well in crash scenarios, but individual component sensors and rear seat belt retractors have generated owner complaints.

**Faulty Seat Belts and Passenger Weight Sensors**
- **Symptoms:** Rear seat belts jamming or failing to retract properly, alongside illuminated airbag warning lights due to seat sensor issues.
- **Solution:** Inspection and replacement of faulty seat belt mechanisms or recalibration of passenger occupancy sensors.
- **Cost:** $1,000

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V627000: Power Train:Driveline:Driveshaft](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=GLE350&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** If the transfer case disconnects from the rear axle differential the vehicle could stall, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the drive shaft, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V671000: Seat Belts: Rear/Other:Buckle Assembly](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=GLE350&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** While buckling the seat belt, the middle seat belt buckle could be pushed down into the gap below the seat cushion. An unrestrained occupant has an increased risk of injury in the event of a crash.
- **What to do:** MBUSA will notify owners, and dealers will secure the middle seat belt buckle, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V648000: Equipment:Other:Owners/Service/Other Manual](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=GLE350&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 202a, "Head Restraints." Improperly adjusted head restraints increase the risk of injury to the third row seat passengers in the event of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 19V459000: Structure](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=GLE350&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** Water in the passenger compartment may affect the vehicle's electrical systems, increasing the risk of a crash or injury.
- **What to do:** MBUSA will notify owners, and dealers will seal the affected area, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V458000: Air Bags:Frontal:Passenger Side:Inflator Module](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=GLE350&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** In the event of a crash while the temperature in the passenger cabin is very high, the passenger frontal air bag deployment may be delayed or may not completely deploy. An air bag that does not deploy as intended can increase the risk of injury in the event of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the passenger air bag module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V226000: Electrical System:Wiring](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=GLE350&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** The wiring harness under the seat may become pinched during seat adjustment, possibly causing damage to the wires. Damaged wires may cause the rear side air bags to not deploy as intended.
- **What to do:** MBUSA will notify owners, and dealers will inspect and reroute the wiring harness as necessary, and repair any wire damage, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V056000: Exterior Lighting:Back Up Lights](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=GLE350&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 108, "Lamps, Reflective Devices, and Associated Equipment." If the reverse light remains illuminated in a gear other than reverse, it may cause confusion about the driver's intention to other motorists, and increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** MBUSA will notify owners, and dealers will replace the lighting control unit, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V572000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Storage:Tank Assembly:Filler Pipe And Cap](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=GLE350&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** A fuel leak in the presence of an ignition source increases the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** MBUSA will notify owners, and dealers will replace the fuel tank, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

When shopping for a pre-owned 2020 model, buyers should prioritize obtaining a comprehensive vehicle history report and conducting a thorough pre-purchase inspection. Pay close attention to transmission shift quality, electrical accessory operation, and suspension condition to avoid costly out-of-pocket repairs.
