The 2021 Mercedes-Benz GLE350, a mid-size luxury SUV, generally presents a strong reliability profile, reflecting Mercedes-Benz's commitment to quality engineering. ### Optional AIRMATIC Air Suspension
**Air Spring Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle sagging on one corner, loss of ride height, "check suspension" warning light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of leaking air spring or associated lines.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1500 per corner


## Electrical

### Infotainment System (MBUX)
**Software Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Screen freezing, unresponsive touch controls, connectivity issues (Bluetooth, Apple CarPlay/Android Auto).
- **Solution:** Software updates, system resets, or module replacement.
- **Cost:** $150 - $500


### 12V Battery
**Premature Battery Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Difficulty starting, electrical components malfunctioning intermittently, "battery protection" warnings.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the 12V battery.
- **Cost:** $250 - $450

### Summary Verdict
The 2021 Mercedes-Benz GLE350 is a generally reliable luxury SUV. The powertrain (engine and transmission) has shown good durability with no significant widespread issues reported. The primary areas where owners might encounter problems are related to the optional AIRMATIC air suspension, potential software glitches within the MBUX infotainment system, and occasional premature failure of the 12V battery. Exterior and interior components are largely robust, though condensation in headlights and HVAC blower motor issues have been noted. Overall, the GLE350 offers a dependable ownership experience, with most potential issues being manageable and not indicative of systemic flaws.
