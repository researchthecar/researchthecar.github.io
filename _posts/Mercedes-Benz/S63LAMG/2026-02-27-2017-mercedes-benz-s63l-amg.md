# 2017 Mercedes-Benz S63L AMG Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2017 Mercedes-Benz S63L AMG delivers blistering performance and supreme luxury, but prospective buyers should approach with careful inspection. As a high-end flagship sedan, maintenance and unexpected mechanical or cosmetic repairs can carry steep luxury-brand pricing. Reviewing owner feedback reveals a handful of recurring complaints ranging from interior trim separation to unique electrical and body concerns.

---

## Engine

### 5.5L Biturbo V8

The 5.5-liter hand-built biturbo V8 engine produces around 577 horsepower and 664 lb-ft of torque, offering phenomenal acceleration for the large sedan. This powerhouse is generally robust when properly maintained, though regular high-performance service intervals are non-negotiable.

## Transmission

### AMG Speedshift MCT 7-Speed Automatic

The 7-speed AMG Speedshift Multi-Clutch Transmission utilizes a wet clutch pack instead of a traditional torque converter for lightning-fast gear changes. It provides rapid response in manual mode and smooth operation in comfort settings, though routine fluid changes are vital.

**Automatic Shift to Neutral**
- **Symptoms:** When attempting to turn off the vehicle while it is in drive with the foot on the brake, the transmission may shift into neutral instead of park, posing a rollaway risk.
- **Solution:** Have the gear selector module and software inspected by a qualified technician for potential updates or component replacement.

## Suspension

### Wheels and Rims

The performance-tuned suspension offers an adaptable ride that balances sharp handling with supreme comfort, though low-profile tires and large alloy wheels remain vulnerable to harsh impacts.

**AMG Wheel Cracking**
- **Symptoms:** Large AMG alloy wheels can crack under normal driving conditions when encountering minor road imperfections and potholes.
- **Solution:** Repair cracked wheels or replace them with stronger aftermarket or OEM wheel alternatives.

## Exterior

### Sunroof and Glass

Exterior panels and glass components give the vehicle its commanding presence, but complex panoramic roof mechanisms require vigilant maintenance to prevent binding.

**Panoramic Sunroof Binding**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have reported hearing cracking noises and experiencing panoramic sunroof panels getting stuck or failing to close properly.
- **Solution:** Inspect the sunroof tracks and motor assembly; clear debris or replace binding mechanical guide components.

## Interior

### Dashboard Trim

The cabin showcases exquisite materials and advanced technology, but leather shrinking and trim separation can plague upper dashboard areas under intense sun exposure.

**Leather Dashboard Separation**
- **Symptoms:** The leather wrapping on the dashboard begins to pull away or shrink near the passenger and driver side airbag deployment zones, potentially exposing internal components.
- **Solution:** Professional upholstery repair or dashboard replacement, which can involve significant out-of-pocket costs.
- **Cost:** $3,000 to $6,000

## Summary Verdict

The 2017 Mercedes-Benz S63L AMG is an engineering marvel that blends ultra-luxury with supercar performance, but used-car buyers should proceed with eyes wide open. While the hand-built V8 engine is stout, potential shoppers must inspect for high-dollar luxury wear items, including dashboard leather separation, sunroof mechanism failures, and vulnerable low-profile alloy wheels. Setting aside a robust maintenance fund is strongly advised to keep this flagship sedan performing at its highest potential.
