# 2016 Mercedes-Benz E550 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2016 Mercedes-Benz E-Class represents a refined generation of luxury engineering, offering high-end interior comfort and sophisticated driving dynamics. While generally celebrated for its robust build quality, prospective buyers should review known owner complaints regarding specific electrical, steering, and structural components. Thoroughly inspecting a used model for water intrusion, steering assist function, and subframe condition will help ensure a reliable ownership experience.

---

## Engine

### 3.0L V6 Biturbo

This 3.0-liter twin-turbocharged V6 engine delivers strong power and smooth acceleration suited for executive touring. It provides ample performance while maintaining respectable highway efficiency for its class.

**Fuel Odor**
- **Symptoms:** A noticeable fuel smell while the vehicle is idling, potentially pointing toward fuel system component leaks.
- **Solution:** Inspect fuel lines, rails, and pump seals for leaks, and replace damaged or compromised components.

### 4.7L V8 Biturbo

The 4.7-liter twin-turbo V8 produces robust horsepower and torque for rapid, effortless passing power. It is generally robust when properly maintained with regular fluid changes.

## Transmission

### 7-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 7-speed automatic transmission delivers seamless gear changes that complement the vehicle's smooth ride quality. Routine fluid maintenance is key to preserving long-term shift quality.

## Suspension

### Rear Subframe

The suspension provides a plush, composed ride quality characteristic of luxury grand tourers, though structural components should be checked for corrosion in salt-belt regions.

**Subframe Corrosion**
- **Symptoms:** Premature rust and corrosion on the rear subframe, which can compromise suspension and brake mounting points.
- **Solution:** Have the rear subframe inspected for rust perforation; consult a dealer regarding extended warranty coverage options for subframe replacement.

## Brakes

### Parking Brake

Brake components offer strong stopping power, but secondary systems like electronic parking brakes require regular inspection.

**Electronic Parking Brake Binding**
- **Symptoms:** Issues or failure within the electronic parking brake system, often involving worn cables or mechanical linkages.
- **Solution:** Inspect parking brake cables and actuators, replacing any worn or damaged components.

## Steering

### Power Steering Assist

Issues related to electronic power steering assist failures have been reported by some owners, requiring increased steering effort.

**Steering Assist Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Sudden loss of power steering functionality accompanied by a dashboard warning message, making the steering wheel very heavy and difficult to turn.
- **Solution:** Have the steering assembly, wiring, and power assist module diagnosed and repaired or replaced by a qualified technician.

## Exterior

### Windshield Seal

Exterior body seals generally hold up well, though windshield adhesive degradation can occasionally lead to cabin leaks.

**Windshield Water Intrusion**
- **Symptoms:** Water drops forming on the interior headliner or front passenger area after rain, with noticeable looseness when pushing outward on the windshield glass.
- **Solution:** Remove and reseal or replace the windshield with a fresh adhesive bead to restore cabin water integrity.

## Summary Verdict

The 2016 model offers a compelling mix of performance and luxury, but prospective buyers should perform a detailed pre-purchase inspection. Pay close attention to the condition of the windshield seals for water leaks, verify proper operation of the power steering system, and inspect the rear subframe for corrosion. Addressing these potential items before purchase will help ensure a satisfying and trouble-free ownership experience.
