# 2017 Mercedes-Benz E550 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2017 Mercedes-Benz E-Class brings luxury, advanced technology, and refined performance to the executive sedan market. While overall ownership offers a comfortable and prestigious experience, prospective buyers should review historical owner complaints and potential mechanical issues carefully before purchasing a used model. Paying close attention to powertrain options, electrical accessories, and known recurring issues will help ensure a more dependable ownership journey.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4 (E300)

This 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine produces approximately 241 horsepower and 273 lb-ft of torque. It provides adequate acceleration and respectable fuel efficiency for daily driving, though buyers should monitor for fuel system and minor electrical quirks.

**Fuel Leak or Odor**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers report noticing a strong smell of gasoline inside the cabin and around the exterior of the vehicle upon starting.
- **Solution:** Inspect fuel lines, high-pressure fuel pumps, and connections for cracks or leaks, and replace damaged components.

**Engine Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may experience unexpected engine stalling or a loss of motive power while driving.
- **Solution:** Diagnose the fuel delivery and engine management systems for fault codes and replace failing components.

### 3.0L Twin-Turbo V6 (E400 / AMG E43)

The 3.0-liter twin-turbocharged V6 generates roughly 329 to 396 horsepower depending on the specific tuning. It offers robust performance and smooth power delivery, representing a strong balance of executive comfort and sporty capability.

### 4.0L Twin-Turbo V8 (AMG E63)

The high-performance 4.0-liter twin-turbo V8 delivers immense horsepower and torque for aggressive acceleration. While exhilarating, maintenance and repair costs tend to be significantly higher than standard V6 or four-cylinder trims.

## Transmission

### 9-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 9-speed automatic transmission is paired across most mainstream trims, designed to maximize highway efficiency and keep engine RPMs low. Overall shifting is generally smooth, though electronic control module glitches have been reported.

**Unintended Neutral Shift**
- **Symptoms:** The gear shifter may inadvertently slip into neutral while coming to a stop or driving, leading to a temporary loss of drive power.
- **Solution:** Inspect the electronic shifter module and update software or replace failing mechanical selectors.

**Transmission Slipping and Harsh Shifting**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers note harsh jerking motions when shifting between gears, delayed engagement, or slipping during acceleration.
- **Solution:** Reprogram or replace the transmission control module and check fluid levels.

## Steering

### Power Steering System

The electric power steering system provides light, adjustable feedback suited for effortless parking and stable highway cruising.

**Steering Assist Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers experience sudden loss of power steering functionality accompanied by dashboard warnings requiring increased physical effort to turn the steering wheel.
- **Solution:** Inspect the electronic power steering rack and control modules, and replace faulty components as necessary.

## Safety

### Air Bag System

Comprehensive passive safety equipment is standard, though occasional sensor or wiring faults can trigger warning lights.

**Air Bag Warning Light**
- **Symptoms:** The SRS airbag warning light illuminates on the instrument cluster, often indicating a fault in the seat cushion sensor, squib circuit, or clock spring.
- **Solution:** Scan diagnostic codes and replace faulty occupant classification units, seat wiring harnesses, or clock springs.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V010000: Air Bags:Frontal:Passenger Side:Inflator Module](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=E550&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** An inflator explosion may result in sharp metal fragments striking the passenger or other occupants resulting in serious injury or death.
- **What to do:** MBUSA will notify owners, and dealers will replace the passenger frontal air bag module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V058000: Electrical System:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=E550&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** The software design of the communication module may fail to communicate the correct vehicle location for the emergency call system (eCall) in the event of a crash. Failure of the eCall system may result in emergency responders being dispatched to the wrong location, increasing the risk of injury following a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 17V080000: Air Bags:Frontal:Passenger Side:Inflator Module](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=E550&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** The pelvis air bag or the front passenger air bag inflator initiator may fail to ignite in the event of a crash. If the pelvis airbag or the front passenger airbag does not deploy as intended in the event of a crash, the occupants have an increased risk of injury.
- **What to do:** MBUSA will notify owners, and dealers will replace the affected air bag modules, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V627000: Air Bags:Frontal](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=E550&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** The driver's air bag may unexpectedly deploy due to insufficient grounding of the steering components if an electrostatic discharge occurs and the air bag clockspring is broken. If the driver air bag inadvertently deploys, it can increase the risk of injury and a crash.
- **What to do:** MBUSA will notify owners, and dealers will add sufficient grounding to the steering components, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V831000: Structure:Frame And Members:Underbody Shields](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=E550&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** The insufficient clearance may cause the panel to melt, increasing the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** MBUSA will notify owners, and dealers will inspect and replace the underbody panel, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2017 Mercedes-Benz lineup offers high-end luxury and sophisticated features, but used-car buyers should perform a thorough mechanical inspection before committing. Pay special attention to fuel system components, steering assist operation, and transmission shifting behavior. Finding a well-maintained example with a documented service history remains the best way to enjoy this vehicle while minimizing unexpected repair bills.
