**Introduction:**

The 2009 Mercedes-Benz E550 is a luxury sedan powered by a naturally-aspirated 5.5-liter V8 engine, paired with a 7-speed automatic transmission. The V8 engine produces 382 horsepower and 391 lb-ft of torque. The E550 is praised for its smooth ride, luxurious interior, and powerful engine. However, there have been reports of various issues affecting specific components.

**Engine**

**5.5L V8 Engine**

* **Intake Manifold Runner Control Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Engine misfires, loss of power, check engine light illuminates
    * Solution: Replace intake manifold runner control assembly
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
* **Timing Chain Tensioner Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Engine rattling noise, difficulty starting, reduced engine power
    * Solution: Replace timing chain tensioner and related components
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,500+
* **Oil Cooler Housing Leak:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Oil leak from the engine oil cooler housing
    * Solution: Replace oil cooler housing gasket or entire housing assembly
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+

**Transmission**

**7-Speed Automatic Transmission**

* **Torque Converter Shudder:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Vibration or shuddering felt during gear changes, especially in lower gears
    * Solution: Replace torque converter and related components
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+
* **Transmission Slippage:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Difficulty engaging or shifting gears, loss of power
    * Solution: Rebuild or replace transmission
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $3,000 - $6,000+

**Suspension**

* **Airmatic Suspension Air Spring Leak:**
    * Description/Symptoms: One or more air springs lose pressure, resulting in a sagging suspension
    * Solution: Replace leaking air spring and related components
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
* **Front Control Arm Bushing Wear:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Squealing or clunking noises from the front suspension, reduced stability
    * Solution: Replace worn control arm bushings
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+

**Electrical**

* **Battery Discharge:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Battery drains prematurely, leaving the vehicle unable to start
    * Solution: Replace discharged battery and identify any electrical drains
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
* **Alternator Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Dim headlights, loss of electrical power, check engine light illuminates
    * Solution: Replace alternator
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+

**Exterior**

* **Headlight Condensation:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Moisture accumulates inside headlight assembly
    * Solution: Replace headlight assembly
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+

**Interior**

* **Sunroof Rattle:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Rattles or squeaks coming from the sunroof area
    * Solution: Inspect and tighten sunroof frame and components
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
* **Navigation System Malfunction:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Navigation system fails to load maps, no audio output, GPS signal lost
    * Solution: Update or replace navigation system unit
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+

**Summary**

The 2009 Mercedes-Benz E550 has been reported to have various issues across different components. While some issues, such as battery discharge and headlight condensation, are relatively minor, others, such as timing chain tensioner failure and transmission slippage, can be quite severe and expensive to fix. Owners should be aware of these potential issues and budget accordingly for maintenance and repairs.
