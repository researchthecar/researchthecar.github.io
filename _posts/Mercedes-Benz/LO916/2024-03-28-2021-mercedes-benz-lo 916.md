**Introduction:**

The 2021 Mercedes-Benz LO 916 is a versatile and powerful low-entry truck designed for various commercial applications. It offers a choice of engine and transmission configurations, providing customization options for different hauling and performance needs. The vehicle combines advanced engineering, safety features, and efficiency, but like any automobile, it has encountered certain issues reported by its users.

**Engine**

**OM936 7.7L Inline-6 Diesel**

* **Exhaust Gas Recirculation (EGR) Valve Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Increased emissions, decreased engine performance, rough idle
    * Solution: Replace EGR valve
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Turbocharger Boost Pressure Sensor Malfunction:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Engine power loss, smoke emission, check engine light
    * Solution: Replace boost pressure sensor
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**G 210-12 6-Speed Manual**

* **Synchronizer Wear:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears, grinding noises
    * Solution: Replace synchronizer assembly
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $600 - $1,200+
    * Recalls: None Found

**G 131-12 9-Speed Automated Manual**

* **Transmission Slipping:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, inconsistent gear changes
    * Solution: Rebuild or replace transmission
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $5,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Leaf Spring Breakage:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Drooping suspension, uneven ride, handling issues
    * Solution: Replace leaf spring assembly
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+ per spring
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Shock Absorber Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Bouncy or harsh ride, reduced stability
    * Solution: Replace shock absorbers
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+ per shock
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Difficulty starting, electrical system malfunction
    * Solution: Replace battery or diagnose electrical system
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Alternator Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Dim headlights, flickering electronics, battery not charging
    * Solution: Replace alternator
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Headlight Condensation:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Moisture inside headlight assembly
    * Solution: Replace headlight assembly or use moisture-absorbing devices
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+ per headlight
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Rust Development:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Appearance of rust on body panels or undercarriage
    * Solution: Repair or replace rusted parts, apply corrosion protection
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on severity
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Dashboard Button Failures:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Buttons not working or responding intermittently
    * Solution: Replace faulty buttons or repair electrical connections
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Infotainment System Freezing:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Touchscreen not responding, system not booting up
    * Solution: Reset or update infotainment system, replace if necessary
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

The 2021 Mercedes-Benz LO 916 is a generally reliable truck, but it does have some reported issues. The engine and transmission are relatively robust and have minor issues. The suspension and electrical systems are prone to wear and tear, leading to occasional repairs. The exterior is durable, but some owners have encountered headlight condensation and rust concerns. The interior has a few electronic glitches, but these are usually straightforward to fix. While the issues can be inconvenient, they are typically not severe or costly to resolve. However, it's important to note that the frequency and severity of these issues can vary between vehicles and depend on factors such as driving conditions, maintenance habits, and individual vehicle history.
