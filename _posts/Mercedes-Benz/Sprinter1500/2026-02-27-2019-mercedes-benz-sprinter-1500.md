# 2019 Mercedes-Benz Sprinter 1500 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2019 Mercedes-Benz Sprinter 1500 offers versatile commercial and recreational utility with advanced technology and robust powertrain choices. While it provides strong utility and modern driving dynamics, prospective buyers should review available owner feedback regarding intermittent electrical, steering, and parking system behaviors to ensure a thorough pre-purchase evaluation.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged 4-Cylinder Gasoline Engine

This 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder gasoline engine produces approximately 188 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque, providing responsive performance for light-duty commercial applications. It offers a smooth power delivery suited for stop-and-go driving, though buyers should verify routine maintenance history.

### 3.0L Turbocharged V6 Diesel Engine

This 3.0-liter turbocharged V6 diesel engine generates around 188 horsepower and 325 lb-ft of torque, delivering strong low-end pulling power ideal for heavy loads or upfitted camper configurations. It is a well-established diesel option, though proper emissions and fuel system upkeep are essential for long-term reliability.

## Transmission

### 7G-TRONIC 7-Speed Automatic Transmission

Paired with the gasoline engine, this 7-speed automatic transmission delivers refined gear changes tailored for efficiency and urban delivery duty. Regular fluid services help maintain smooth operation over high mileage.

### 9G-TRONIC 9-Speed Automatic Transmission

Coupled primarily with the diesel powerplant, this 9-speed automatic transmission optimizes highway cruising speeds and fuel economy. It provides seamless power delivery across diverse driving conditions when properly maintained.

### Parking Gear Engagement

Exterior body and operational hardware remain sturdy, though parking engagement habits and surrounding console layouts warrant careful driver familiarity.

**Unexpected Vehicle Movement in Park**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have reported instances where the vehicle moved after being placed in park, or where gear shift positioning was easily bumped near the ignition area.
- **Solution:** Ensure the transmission fully engages park before exiting the vehicle, and have the shifter linkage inspected if any engagement ambiguity is observed.

## Steering

### Power Steering Hose and Assist

Steering components generally offer responsive control, though owners have noted occasional hydraulic line security concerns in specific operating environments.

**Power Steering Hose Disconnection**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have reported that the power steering hose can disconnect or slip from its clamp, leading to a sudden loss of fluid and heavy steering effort.
- **Solution:** Inspect power steering lines and clamps for secure positioning, replacing worn hoses or upgrading clamps as necessary.

## Electrical

### Defrost and Climate Blower

Electrical accessories manage cabin comfort and advanced safety features, though occasional climate control glitches have been documented by vehicle operators.

**Climate Blower and Defrost Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Some drivers experience sudden failure of the air blower and defrost system during heavy precipitation, rapidly reducing cabin and window visibility.
- **Solution:** Check blower motors, fuses, and relay switches, and replace faulty electrical components to restore reliable defrost operation.

## Safety

### SRS and Seat Framework

Comprehensive safety equipment protects occupants, though periodic inspection of seat structures and driver-assistance calibrations is recommended.

**Driver Seat Frame Wear and SRS Warning**
- **Symptoms:** An illuminated SRS warning light can appear on the dashboard due to structural tearing or stress on the metal driver-side seat frame.
- **Solution:** Inspect the seat frame for structural fatigue or damage, and repair or replace affected seat components to ensure proper airbag system communication.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V800000: Seats](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=Sprinter+1500&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** In the event of a crash, the weld seam may break and cause the seat cushion to shift forward, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** DVUSA will notify owners, and dealers will replace the tilt adjuster for the front seats, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V908000: Equipment](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=Sprinter+1500&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** The lashing rails on the side walls may not have been properly installed, possibly resulting in the lashing rails detaching. If the lashing rails disconnect, the cargo could become unsecured, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** DVUSA will notify owners, and dealers will correctly affix the lashing rails to the side walls, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V798000: Fuel System, Diesel](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=Sprinter+1500&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** The bayonet lock between the fuel pump and the fuel tank may not have been closed and locked during manufacturing. As a result, a leak between the fuel pump and the fuel tank could occur.
- **What to do:** DVUSA will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the bayonet lock on the fuel pump, locking it as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V695000: Air Bags: Air Bag/Restraint Control Module](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=Sprinter+1500&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** The airbag systems may not have been activated after vehicle production. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 208, "Occupant Crash Protection." An inactive airbag system can increase the risk of injury in the event of a crash.
- **What to do:** DVUSA will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the vehicles to determine if the airbag systems have been activated, correcting them as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V693000: Unknown Or Other](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=Sprinter+1500&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** Failure to identify loose safety related parts can increase the risk of crash and injury.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 19V507000: Visibility:Glass, Side/Rear](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=Sprinter+1500&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** In the event of a side impact crash or vehicle rollover, the fitted side windows may not adequately protect the rear occupants from being ejected, increasing their risk of injury.
- **What to do:** DVUSA will notify owners, and Mercedes-Benz or Freightliner dealers will replace the single-pane safety glass with laminated safety glass., free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V078000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Exhaust System:Emission Control:Gas Recirculation Valve (Egr Valve)](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=Sprinter+1500&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** The connecting screws on the exhaust gas recirculation (EGR) pipe may not have been correctly tightened. If the connecting screws are not properly tightened, hot exhaust gases may leak from the EGR pipe, increasing the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** DVUSA will notify owners, and Mercedes-Benz or Freightliner Sprinter dealers will renew the screw connection and the flange seals on the exhaust gas recirculation pipe, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V591000: Exterior Lighting:Tail Lights:Switch](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=Sprinter+1500&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** If the manual turn-switch that activates the vehicle lights is turned quickly from one lighting position to another, the brightness of the taillights can be significantly reduced or could switch off completely. Reduced taillight visibility increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** MBUSA will notify owners, and Mercedes-Benz or Freightliner Sprinter dealers will update the body control unit software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2019 Mercedes-Benz Sprinter 1500 is a capable choice for buyers needing commercial-grade space or custom conversion potential. While major mechanical components are generally robust, used-car shoppers should thoroughly inspect steering fluid lines, electrical climate controls, and seat structures during a pre-purchase inspection.
