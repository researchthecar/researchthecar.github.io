# 2018 Mercedes-Benz GLE63 AMG S Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a pre-owned 2018 Mercedes-Benz GLE63 AMG S, buyers are looking at a high-performance luxury SUV combining immense power with upscale comfort. While these German performance flagships offer thrilling driving dynamics, prospective owners should carefully evaluate mechanical upkeep and historical maintenance records. Owner feedback highlights a few electrical and powertrain concerns worth keeping on your radar during a pre-purchase inspection.

---

## Engine

### 5.5L Twin-Turbo V8 Engine

The 5.5-liter twin-turbocharged V8 engine delivers a formidable 577 horsepower and 561 lb-ft of torque, providing blistering acceleration for a midsize SUV. Regular and meticulous maintenance is critical, as complex forced-induction plumbing and high-output components can experience wear over time.

**Fuel Pump Failure and Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** Hesitation during acceleration, unexpected engine stalling while driving or turning, which can leave the vehicle stranded in traffic.
- **Solution:** Inspect and replace the failing fuel pump or associated fuel delivery components.

## Transmission

### AMG Speedshift Plus 7G-Tronic 7-Speed Automatic

The AMG Speedshift Plus 7-speed automatic transmission handles high torque outputs and routes power to the 4MATIC all-wheel-drive system. It generally shifts briskly under spirited driving, though routine fluid changes are vital for long-term durability.

## Electrical

### Backup Camera Display

Safety equipment is robust overall, though electronic peripheral assists require functional testing during a used vehicle inspection.

**Intermittent Rearview Camera Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The infotainment display shows a black screen or fails to switch to the backup camera view when placing the vehicle in reverse.
- **Solution:** Diagnose the camera unit and wiring harness, or replace the faulty display module as needed.

### Telematics and Control Units

Advanced electronics govern nearly every function of this high-performance SUV, making a healthy battery and trouble-free wiring harness essential.

**SOS Inoperative and Electrical Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Persistent dashboard messages regarding telematics failure or electrical system malfunctions following battery drainage.
- **Solution:** Reset or replace the Telematics Control Unit (TCU) and verify proper system voltage.
