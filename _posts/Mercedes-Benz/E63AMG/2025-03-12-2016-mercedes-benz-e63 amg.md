The 2016 Mercedes-Benz E63 AMG is a high-performance luxury sedan known for its powerful engine and engaging driving experience. It boasts a handcrafted 5.5-liter twin-turbocharged V8 engine producing 577 horsepower and 590 lb-ft of torque. While owners generally praise the car's performance, luxurious interior, and distinctive styling, some common complaints include the high running costs, occasional electrical glitches, and the firm ride quality. There were no other engine and transmission options available for the 2016 E63 AMG.

**Engine**

**5.5L Twin-Turbo V8 (M157)**

*   **Oil Leaks from Valve Cover Gaskets and Camshaft Adjusters**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks may be observed on the engine. Burning oil smell may be present.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the valve cover gaskets and camshaft adjuster seals.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1200+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Turbocharger Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, whining noises from the engine bay, and potential check engine lights.
    * Solution: Replacement or rebuild of the turbocharger(s).
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $3,000 - $6,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**AMG Speedshift MCT 7-Speed Automatic (722.9)**

*   **Harsh Shifting/Jerking**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Abrupt or jerky shifts, especially during low-speed maneuvers or when shifting between gears.
    *   Solution: Software update to the transmission control module (TCM). If that doesn't fix it, replacement of the conductor plate or valve body.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500 (Software Update), $1500 - $3000+ (Conductor Plate/Valve Body)
    * Recalls: None Found

*   **Torque Converter Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Shuddering or vibrations at certain speeds, especially during light acceleration.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the torque converter.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1800 - $3500+
     * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Air Suspension Failure (Airmatic)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Vehicle sagging on one or more corners, compressor failing to inflate the system, warning lights on the dashboard.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis of the leak or faulty component (air strut, compressor, valve block) and replacement of the affected parts.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $2500+ per strut, $500 - $1500+ for compressor.
     * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Battery Drain Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Vehicle battery draining quickly, especially when the car is not driven frequently.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis of parasitic drain (often caused by a faulty module not shutting down properly) and repair or replacement of the affected component.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $1000+ (depending on the source of the drain)
    * Recalls: None Found

*   **COMAND System Malfunctions**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Screen freezing, navigation issues, Bluetooth connectivity problems, and general sluggishness of the infotainment system.
    *   Solution: Software update, replacement of the COMAND unit, or repair of faulty wiring.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500 (Software Update), $800 - $2000+ (Unit Replacement)
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Headlight failure**
     *   Description/Symptoms: Headlights will not work at all
     *   Solution: Replacement of the headlight assembly or ballast.
     *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $2500+
     * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Sunroof Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Sunroof not opening or closing properly, unusual noises during operation, water leaks.
    *   Solution: Cleaning and lubricating the sunroof tracks, repairing or replacing the motor or regulator, or sealing the sunroof assembly.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1000+
     * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Seat Heater Malfunction**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Seat heaters failing to heat up or overheating.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the heating element or the control module.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+ per seat
     * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2016 Mercedes-Benz E63 AMG is a performance-oriented vehicle, and its common issues reflect this. Engine issues such as oil leaks and potential turbocharger failures can be costly. Transmission issues like harsh shifting can also be problematic. The air suspension is prone to failure, potentially leading to expensive repairs. Electrical gremlins, including battery drain and COMAND system malfunctions, are also relatively common. Overall, while the E63 AMG offers a thrilling driving experience, potential buyers should be aware of these potential issues and factor in higher maintenance costs.
