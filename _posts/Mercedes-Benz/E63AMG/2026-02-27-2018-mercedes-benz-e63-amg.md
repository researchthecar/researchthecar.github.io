# 2018 Mercedes-Benz E63 AMG Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2018 Mercedes-Benz E-Class delivers luxury, performance, and advanced technology, but used car buyers should approach with careful inspection. While owner reports reflect solid luxury appointments, various electrical, powertrain, and safety complaints indicate potential maintenance and repair costs common to high-end European luxury vehicles.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4

The 2.0-liter turbocharged inline-4 engine produces around 241 horsepower and 273 lb-ft of torque, offering efficient daily driving performance for entry-level trims. Buyers should ensure regular maintenance intervals have been strictly followed.

**Engine Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle may unexpectedly stall or fail to maintain smooth idle, presenting driving hazards.
- **Solution:** Perform professional diagnostic testing to isolate fuel delivery, sensor, or electronic control module faults.

### 3.0L Twin-Turbo V6

The 3.0-liter twin-turbo V6 engine generates approximately 329 to 362 horsepower, delivering robust acceleration and smooth power delivery. Regular servicing of cooling and turbo systems is crucial for long-term reliability.

### 4.0L Twin-Turbo V8

The hand-built 4.0-liter twin-turbo V8 powering high-performance AMG variants outputs an impressive 603 horsepower and 627 lb-ft of torque. While exceptionally potent, it requires meticulous maintenance and can incur high ownership costs.

## Transmission

### 9-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 9-speed automatic transmission provides quick gear changes and optimized highway fuel economy across standard models. Prompt attention to fluid changes and software updates helps maintain proper shift quality.

**Shifting Delays and Neutral Shift**
- **Symptoms:** Transmission may hesitate when shifting between drive and reverse or spontaneously slip into neutral.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified technician inspect transmission software, fluid condition, and electronic valve body components.

### 9-Speed AMG Speedshift MCT Automatic Transmission

The AMG-tuned 9-speed wet-clutch transmission delivers lightning-fast gear changes tailored for high-performance driving dynamics. Regular fluid services are vital to ensure long-term durability under aggressive driving.

## Suspension

### Wheels and Tires

Suspension and wheel components on performance-oriented models are susceptible to damage from road imperfections due to low-profile tire setups.

**Wheel Cracking and Tire Blistering**
- **Symptoms:** Low-profile alloy wheels may develop structural cracks, or tires may exhibit premature sidewall blistering.
- **Solution:** Inspect wheels and low-profile tires regularly for structural damage, and replace compromised rims or tires.

## Electrical

### Backup Camera and Infotainment

Electrical system issues in these models frequently center around advanced digital displays, cameras, and connectivity modules that may require software re-flashes or hardware replacements.

**Intermittent Camera Blackout**
- **Symptoms:** The backup camera screen may display a blank image, error symbol, or fail to open when shifting into reverse.
- **Solution:** Inspect camera wiring harnesses, trunk lid pass-through wires, and infotainment software modules for faults.

## Safety

### Seat Belts

Safety restraint systems are critical, and owners have reported mechanical failures within motorized seat belt retractors requiring component replacement.

**Seat Belt Retractor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The seat belt fails to retract properly, leaving dangerous slack across the driver's chest and lap.
- **Solution:** Replace the malfunctioning seat belt motor and retractor assembly through an authorized service center.
