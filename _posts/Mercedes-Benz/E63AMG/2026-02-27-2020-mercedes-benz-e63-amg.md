# 2020 Mercedes-Benz E63 AMG Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2020 Mercedes-Benz E63 AMG, buyers gain access to an extraordinarily powerful luxury performance vehicle that delivers thrilling acceleration and sophisticated styling. While owner reports to safety regulators are relatively limited for this specific high-performance variant, prospective buyers should still carefully evaluate complex powertrain electronics, advanced driver aids, and body components during a pre-purchase inspection.

---

## Engine

### 4.0L Twin-Turbocharged V8 Engine

This hand-built 4.0-liter biturbo V8 produces immense horsepower and torque, delivering supercar-rivaling performance while requiring meticulous maintenance schedules. Overall reliability is typical for high-output German performance engines, making service history verification essential for buyers.

### Wiring Harness & Sensors

Advanced electrical architectures provide immense convenience and performance controls, though complex wiring layouts demand careful inspection as vehicles age.

**Camshaft Sensor Oil Leak and Harness Corrosion**
- **Symptoms:** Oil wicks through the camshaft sensor into the main engine electrical harness, causing widespread corrosion and potential electrical failures without early dashboard warnings.
- **Solution:** Inspect engine sensors and wiring connectors during routine service, replacing leaking sensors and damaged harnesses as necessary.
- **Cost:** $4,000+

## Transmission

### 9-Speed AMG Speedshift MCT Automatic Transmission

The multi-clutch 9-speed automatic transmission channels massive torque to the all-wheel-drive system with rapid shifts and responsive gear selection. It generally proves robust under normal driving conditions when fluid changes are maintained on schedule.

## Electrical

### Back Over Prevention Camera

Comprehensive safety and driver-assistance systems support daily driving, though software glitches can occasionally affect peripheral camera functions.

**Intermittent Backup Camera Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The rearview camera display freezes, goes completely black, or displays an inoperable message when shifting into reverse.
- **Solution:** Have the dealership inspect the infotainment software, camera module, and related wiring connections for faults.

## Exterior

### Glass and Roof Panels

Exterior panels and specialized glass elements require close examination to ensure proper sealing, structural integrity, and unimpeded visibility.

**Panoramic Sunroof Glass Shattering**
- **Symptoms:** The panoramic sunroof glass may spontaneously fracture or shatter while driving at highway speeds, accompanied by a loud acoustic report.
- **Solution:** Inspect the sunroof glass for stress points or prior damage, and consult a dealer if replacement is required.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V216000: Seats](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=E63+AMG&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** A seat too close to the air bag increases the risk of injury in the event of a crash necessitating the deployment of the frontal passenger air bag.
- **What to do:** MBUSA will notify owners, and dealers will recalibrate the passenger seat limiter, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V673000: Seats](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=E63+AMG&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** If the front passenger seat is adjusted beyond the acceptable range in relation to the air bag, there could be an increased risk of injury in the event of a crash with air bag deployment.
- **What to do:** MBUSA will notify owners, and dealers will inspect for the mechanical seat adjustment range limitation for the front passenger seat and install it if necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.
