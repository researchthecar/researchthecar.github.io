# 2012 Mercedes-Benz E63 AMG

### Overview
The 2012 Mercedes-Benz E63 AMG, a high-performance luxury sedan, is generally regarded as a robust vehicle, especially considering its potent powertrain. However, like any complex performance car, it has specific areas that have seen more attention from owners regarding reliability. The twin-turbocharged V8 engine, while powerful, can be a source of concern for certain components. Similarly, the advanced transmission systems, while smooth and responsive, can require specialized attention. Owners should be aware of potential issues related to the sophisticated electronics and the demanding nature of the performance-oriented suspension and braking systems. Regular and meticulous maintenance is crucial for preserving the longevity and performance of this AMG model.

---


## Engine

### 5.5L Twin-Turbo V8 (M157)
**Turbocharger Wastegate Actuator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Reduced engine power, boost leaks, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the wastegate actuator or the entire turbocharger assembly.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1500 per actuator

**Coolant Leaks (Intercooler Hoses)**
- **Symptoms:** Coolant smell, visible coolant on engine components, potential overheating.
- **Solution:** Replacement of cracked or degraded intercooler hoses.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600

**Carbon Buildup on Intake Valves**
- **Symptoms:** Rough idle, reduced performance, misfires.
- **Solution:** Intake manifold removal and walnut blasting or chemical cleaning.
- **Cost:** $700 - $1200


## Transmission

### 7-Speed AMG Speedshift MCT
**Torque Converter Seal Leak**
- **Symptoms:** Transmission fluid leaks, potential for transmission slippage, warning lights.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the torque converter seal.
- **Cost:** $1000 - $2000

**Mechatronic Unit Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Harsh shifting, delayed engagement, transmission fault codes.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of the mechatronic unit.
- **Cost:** $2000 - $4000


## Suspension

### AMG Sport Suspension
**Air Suspension Compressor Failure (if equipped)**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle sagging on one side, loss of adjustable ride height.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the air suspension compressor.
- **Cost:** $700 - $1500

**Control Arm Bushing Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Clunking noises over bumps, vague steering feel, uneven tire wear.
- **Solution:** Replacement of worn control arm bushings or the entire control arm assembly.
- **Cost:** $500 - $900 per axle


## Electrical

### General Electrical System
**Seat Control Module Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** Power seats not working, memory seat issues, seat belt extender problems.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and replacement of faulty seat control modules.
- **Cost:** $400 - $800

**Infotainment System Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Screen freezing, audio issues, navigation problems.
- **Solution:** Software updates or replacement of head unit components.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1500

### Summary Verdict
The 2012 Mercedes-Benz E63 AMG offers a compelling blend of luxury and extreme performance. While its core mechanical components, particularly the engine and transmission, are generally robust for their power output, owners should be prepared for potential costs associated with specialized repairs. Issues like turbocharger wastegate actuators, torque converter seals, and mechatronic unit problems are not uncommon in high-performance vehicles of this era. The sophisticated electrical systems and performance-oriented suspension can also present unique repair needs. Despite these potential concerns, with diligent maintenance and prompt attention to any emerging issues, the E63 AMG can remain a reliable and exhilarating machine.
