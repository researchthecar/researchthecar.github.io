# 2006 Mercedes-Benz S55 AMG

### Overview
The 2006 Mercedes-Benz S55 AMG, a high-performance luxury sedan, offers a potent blend of power and refinement. Powered by a formidable 5.4L supercharged V8 engine, it delivers exhilarating acceleration and a commanding presence on the road. While generally well-engineered, like any complex performance vehicle of its era, it can present specific reliability concerns that owners should be aware of. This data focuses on documented, non-wear-and-tear related issues that may require attention beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential repair costs and common problem areas for this sophisticated German machine.

---


## Engine

### 5.4L Supercharged V8 (M113K)
**Supercharger Belt Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of boost, reduced engine power, whining noise from the front of the engine.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the supercharger drive belt and potentially the idler pulley.
- **Cost:** $200 - $500

**Coolant Leaks (Intercooler System)**
- **Symptoms:** Low coolant level, engine overheating, visible coolant residue around intercooler hoses and connections.
- **Solution:** Inspection and replacement of leaking hoses, clamps, or intercooler seals.
- **Cost:** $300 - $700

**Oil Leaks (Valve Cover Gaskets)**
- **Symptoms:** Visible oil on the engine exterior, burning oil smell, potential for oil to drip onto exhaust manifolds.
- **Solution:** Replacement of valve cover gaskets.
- **Cost:** $400 - $800


## Transmission

### 5-Speed Automatic (722.6)
**Transmission Control Module (TCM) Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Erratic shifting, 'limp mode' engagement, transmission warning lights on the dashboard.
- **Solution:** Replacement or repair of the Transmission Control Module.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1500

**Conductor Plate Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Harsh shifting, delayed engagement, transmission fault codes related to speed sensors.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the conductor plate assembly.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1000


## Suspension

### Active Body Control (ABC) System
**Hydraulic Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle sagging on one or more corners, 'ABC System Malfunction' warning on the dashboard, low hydraulic fluid level.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of leaking hydraulic lines, struts, or pump.
- **Cost:** $1000 - $3000+

**ABC Pump Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of suspension height control, loud whining noise from the pump, 'ABC System Malfunction' warning.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the ABC hydraulic pump.
- **Cost:** $700 - $1200

### Summary Verdict
The 2006 Mercedes-Benz S55 AMG is a powerful and luxurious vehicle, but its complexity, particularly the Active Body Control (ABC) suspension and the supercharged V8 engine, can lead to significant repair costs if issues arise. Common problems include hydraulic leaks within the ABC system, potential issues with the transmission control module and conductor plate, and electrical gremlins often stemming from the SAM modules. While the core engine and transmission are robust, specific components like the supercharger belt and intercooler hoses can require attention. Owners should budget for potential maintenance and repairs, especially concerning the advanced suspension and electrical systems, to ensure continued reliability and enjoyment of this high-performance flagship sedan.
