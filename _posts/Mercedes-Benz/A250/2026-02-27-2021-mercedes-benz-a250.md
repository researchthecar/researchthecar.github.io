# 2021 Mercedes-Benz A250 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2021 Mercedes-Benz A250, buyers will find a compact luxury vehicle offering modern tech and engaging driving dynamics. However, owner-reported feedback highlights a few potential areas of concern, particularly involving electronics, rear visibility systems, and occasional powertrain management glitches.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4

This 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine produces approximately 221 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque, providing brisk acceleration for the compact luxury sedan. Overall, it is generally robust, though occasional owner reports note unexpected electronic engine shutdowns while in motion.

## Transmission

### 7-Speed Dual-Clutch Automatic Transmission

The 7-speed dual-clutch automatic transmission delivers quick and seamless gear changes, paired with either front-wheel drive or 4MATIC all-wheel drive. It operates smoothly under normal driving conditions, though like many modern dual-clutch units, prospective buyers should test shift quality thoroughly.

## Electrical

### Backup Camera

Driver assistance features generally enhance safety, but rearview camera reliability has been questioned in owner reports.

**Intermittent Blank Screen**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have reported that putting the vehicle in reverse results in a completely blank or inoperative rearview camera display without warning.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis by a qualified technician is required to check for software updates or faulty camera hardware modules.

## Summary Verdict

The 2021 Mercedes-Benz A250 combines luxury and performance in an accessible package, but used-car buyers should thoroughly inspect the electrical systems and rearview camera functionality during a test drive. While serious mechanical failures are uncommon, paying close attention to software and sensor operation can help avoid frustrating post-purchase issues.
