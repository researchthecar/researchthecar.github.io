# 2013 Mercedes-Benz A250

### Overview
The 2013 Mercedes-Benz A250, part of the W176 generation, is a premium compact hatchback known for its sporty driving dynamics and upscale interior. It typically features a 2.0-liter turbocharged gasoline engine paired with a 7-speed dual-clutch automatic transmission. While generally well-regarded for its performance and technology, like any vehicle, it has specific areas that have seen more attention from owners regarding reliability. This analysis focuses on documented common issues beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential concerns for prospective buyers or current owners.

---


## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged Gasoline (M270 DE20 AL)
**Turbocharger Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of power, whistling noises, increased oil consumption.
- **Solution:** Turbocharger replacement or repair.
- **Cost:** $1,200 - $2,500

**Coolant Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Overheating, visible coolant loss, sweet smell.
- **Solution:** Replacement of leaking hoses, water pump, or radiator.
- **Cost:** $300 - $800


## Transmission

### 7-Speed Dual-Clutch Automatic (7G-DCT)
**Mechatronics Unit Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Harsh shifting, transmission warning lights, inability to select gears.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of the mechatronics unit.
- **Cost:** $1,500 - $3,000

**Solenoid Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Jerky shifts, delayed engagement, transmission fault codes.
- **Solution:** Replacement of faulty solenoids.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1,200

## Electrical

### General Electrical System
**Infotainment System Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Screen freezing, unresponsive controls, Bluetooth connectivity issues.

- **Cost:** $200 - $1,000

**Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Difficulty starting, dead battery, electrical components malfunctioning.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and repair of parasitic draw or battery replacement.
- **Cost:** $200 - $500

### Summary Verdict
The 2013 Mercedes-Benz A250 offers a compelling blend of performance and luxury. While its core mechanical components are generally robust, potential owners should be aware of specific issues that have been reported. The 7-speed dual-clutch transmission, particularly its mechatronics unit and solenoids, can be a source of concern and costly repairs. Engine-related issues, such as turbocharger problems and coolant leaks, are also noted. Electrical gremlins, especially with the infotainment system and occasional battery drain, can also arise. Despite these potential drawbacks, with diligent maintenance and addressing issues promptly, the A250 can remain a reliable and enjoyable vehicle.
