# 2017 Mercedes-Benz A250

### Overview
The 2017 Mercedes-Benz A250, part of the W176 generation, is generally considered a reliable compact premium hatchback. It offers a blend of sporty performance, upscale interior, and advanced technology. While most owners report a positive ownership experience, like any vehicle, there are specific areas that have seen more attention from repair shops. This analysis focuses on documented technical issues beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential weak points and their associated repair costs for different powertrain configurations.

---


## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4 (M270/M274)
**Turbocharger Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Reduced engine power, whistling noises, increased oil consumption.
- **Solution:** Turbocharger replacement or repair.
- **Cost:** $1,200 - $2,500

**Coolant Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Overheating, visible coolant loss, sweet smell from engine bay.
- **Solution:** Replacement of leaking hoses, seals, or water pump.
- **Cost:** $300 - $800


## Transmission

### 7-Speed Dual-Clutch Automatic (7G-DCT)
**Mechatronics Unit Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Harsh shifting, transmission warning lights, inability to select gears.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of the mechatronics unit.
- **Cost:** $1,500 - $3,000

**Clutch Pack Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Slipping gears, rough engagement, delayed acceleration.
- **Solution:** Replacement of clutch packs.
- **Cost:** $1,000 - $2,000


## Suspension

### Standard & Sport Suspension
**Worn Control Arm Bushings**
- **Symptoms:** Clunking noises over bumps, vague steering feel, uneven tire wear.
- **Solution:** Replacement of control arm bushings or entire control arms.
- **Cost:** $400 - $700


## Electrical

### Infotainment System
**Screen Freezing/Unresponsiveness**
- **Symptoms:** Infotainment screen becomes unresponsive or freezes.
- **Solution:** Software update or replacement of head unit.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1,500


### Battery Drain
**Parasitic Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Battery dies frequently, car won't start.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and repair of faulty modules or circuits causing drain.
- **Cost:** $200 - $600

### Summary Verdict
The 2017 Mercedes-Benz A250 demonstrates good overall reliability, particularly in its powertrain and core mechanical components. The most frequently reported technical concerns revolve around the dual-clutch transmission's mechatronics unit and clutch packs, as well as potential turbocharger issues and coolant leaks in the 2.0L engine. Electrical gremlins, such as infotainment system glitches and parasitic battery drain, are also noted. While these issues can be costly to repair, they are not universally present across all vehicles. Regular maintenance and prompt attention to early symptoms can help mitigate these potential problems, making the A250 a solid choice for those seeking a premium compact car with a generally dependable track record.
