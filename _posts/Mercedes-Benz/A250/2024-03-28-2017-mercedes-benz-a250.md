**Introduction:**

The 2017 Mercedes-Benz A250 is a compact luxury sedan powered by a 2.0L turbocharged inline-4 engine producing 211 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque. It comes standard with a 7-speed dual-clutch transmission (DCT). The A250 is known for its stylish design, comfortable interior, and responsive handling. However, owners have reported several issues with the vehicle, particularly with its engine and transmission.

**Engine**

* **2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4**

    * **Intake Manifold Cracking:**
        * Description/Symptoms: Air leaking from the intake manifold can cause rough idling, reduced engine power, and engine stalling.
        * Solution: Replacement of the intake manifold.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $1,500
        * Recalls: 2017009
    * **Turbocharger Failure:**
        * Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, smoke from the engine compartment, and a rattling noise from the turbocharger.
        * Solution: Replacement of the turbocharger.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,000
        * Recalls: None Found
    * **Fuel Injector Failure:**
        * Description/Symptoms: Engine misfires, rough idling, and difficulty starting.
        * Solution: Replacement of the fuel injectors.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800
        * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

* **7-Speed Dual-Clutch Transmission (DCT)**

    * **Clutch Pack Failure:**
        * Description/Symptoms: Slipping gears, jerking during shifts, and transmission overheating.
        * Solution: Replacement of the clutch pack.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,500 - $3,500
        * Recalls: 2018012
    * **Transmission Control Module Failure:**
        * Description/Symptoms: Transmission not shifting properly, stuck in gear, and transmission warning lights.
        * Solution: Replacement of the transmission control module.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $1,500
        * Recalls: None Found
    * **Mechatronic Unit Failure:**
        * Description/Symptoms: Loss of gear selection, transmission slipping, and transmission shuddering.
        * Solution: Replacement of the mechatronic unit.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $2,500
        * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **No Issues Found**

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Vehicle battery drains quickly when parked.
    * Solution: Diagnosis and repair of the issue causing the battery drain.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Infotainment System Malfunction:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Screen freezing, unresponsive buttons, or loss of audio.
    * Solution: Software updates or replacement of the infotainment system.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Windshield Cracking:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Formation of cracks on the windshield due to temperature fluctuations or impact.
    * Solution: Replacement of the windshield.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Trim Panel Squeaks and Rattles:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Noises coming from interior trim panels due to loose connections or misalignments.
    * Solution: Tightening or replacement of trim panels.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Door Lock Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Inability to lock or unlock doors due to faulty door lock actuators.
    * Solution: Replacement of door lock actuators.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

The 2017 Mercedes-Benz A250 has several potential issues, primarily with its engine and transmission. These issues can range from minor inconveniences to major repairs requiring significant expense. Buyers considering this vehicle should be aware of these potential problems and factor in the potential costs of repairs.
