# 2017 Mercedes-Benz S550e Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2017 Mercedes-Benz S550e combines plug-in hybrid efficiency with flagship luxury, making it an appealing option for used car buyers seeking high-end comfort. However, as a sophisticated luxury vehicle, maintenance and unexpected mechanical or electrical repairs can be substantial. Prospective buyers should carefully review service histories and conduct thorough pre-purchase inspections to identify any looming issues.

---

## Engine

### 3.0L Twin-Turbo V6 Plug-In Hybrid

The 2017 S550e features a 3.0-liter twin-turbocharged V6 paired with an electric motor, generating robust hybrid power while delivering smooth efficiency. Overall powertrain reliability requires attentive maintenance due to the complexity of the hybrid and forced-induction systems.

**Fuel System and Leak Concerns**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may notice unexpected check engine lights or experience fuel system odors and leaks.
- **Solution:** Have the fuel tank, lines, and related components professionally inspected and repaired or replaced as needed.

## Transmission

### 7-Speed Automatic Transmission

Equipped with a 7-speed automatic transmission tailored for hybrid applications, this unit provides seamless power delivery. Routine fluid changes and software updates are recommended to maintain shift quality.

**Shift Logic and Gear Engagement Behavior**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may fail to engage the expected gear when shutting down or exhibit unexpected neutral shifts.
- **Solution:** Inspect transmission software settings and shifter module functionality at a qualified service center.

## Suspension

### Wheels and Rims

**Wheel Rim Cracking**
- **Symptoms:** Factory alloy or AMG wheels may develop fractures under normal driving conditions over time.
- **Solution:** Replace damaged wheel rims with durable aftermarket or OEM replacements.

## Steering

### Power Steering Column

**Power Adjustment Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The steering wheel may adjust telescopically in and out but fail to move up and down, remaining stuck in an elevated position.
- **Solution:** Inspect and replace the steering column adjustment motor or switch mechanism.

## Exterior

### Sunroof and Windshield

**Sunroof Jamming and Glass Vulnerability**
- **Symptoms:** Panoramic sunroof panels may stick, crack, or fail to close properly, and windshields may be prone to frequent stone chips and cracks.
- **Solution:** Lubricate and service sunroof tracks or replace damaged glass components as necessary.

## Safety

### Dashboard Trim and Airbag Area

**Dashboard Leather Separation**
- **Symptoms:** The leather covering on the dashboard begins to pull away or warp around the passenger-side airbag deployment zone.
- **Solution:** Replacement or professional reupholstering of the dashboard assembly, which can involve significant out-of-pocket costs.
- **Cost:** $3,000 to $6,000

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V058000: Electrical System:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=S550e&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** The software design of the communication module may fail to communicate the correct vehicle location for the emergency call system (eCall) in the event of a crash. Failure of the eCall system may result in emergency responders being dispatched to the wrong location, increasing the risk of injury following a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 17V714000: Steering:Electric Power Assist System](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=S550e&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** A transistor within the power steering control unit may overheat resulting in the power steering system being disabled. If the power steering assist is unexpectedly disabled, it may increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** MBUSA will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the steering rack, replace it as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V816000: Latches/Locks/Linkages:Doors:Latch](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mercedes-Benz&model=S550e&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** The rear passenger door on the driver side may indicate that it is locked, when it may not be. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 206, "Door Locks and Door Retention Components." If the door appears to be locked, the door may be unintentionally opened, increasing the risk of.
- **What to do:** MBUSA will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the left rear door lock, replacing it as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2017 Mercedes-Benz S550e offers exceptional luxury and plug-in hybrid technology, but potential buyers should budget for higher-than-average maintenance expenses. Pay close attention to interior trim condition, electronic adjustments, and service records before making a purchase.
