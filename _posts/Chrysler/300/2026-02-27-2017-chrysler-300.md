# 2017 Chrysler 300 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2017 Chrysler 300 offers a compelling blend of bold styling, spacious comfort, and strong performance options. However, prospective buyers should review common owner-reported complaints regarding interior electronics, powertrain components, and trim durability. Being aware of these potential trouble spots helps ensure a thorough pre-purchase inspection.

---

## Engine

### 3.6L V6 24V VVT Engine

The 3.6-liter Pentastar V6 engine produces approximately 292 horsepower and 260 lb-ft of torque, offering responsive everyday performance. It is generally robust, though owners frequently report issues with the plastic oil filter housing leaking over time.

**Oil Filter Housing Leak**
- **Symptoms:** Oil pooling around the filter housing or leaking down the back of the engine due to thermal expansion cracking the plastic housing.
- **Solution:** Replace the plastic oil filter housing assembly with an updated or aftermarket aluminum replacement unit.

### 5.7L V8 HEMI MDS VVT Engine

The 5.7-liter HEMI V8 generates roughly 363 horsepower and 394 lb-ft of torque, delivering classic muscle-car acceleration and strong towing capability. It pairs with a multi-displacement system for cylinder deactivation on the highway.

## Transmission

### TorqueFlite 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

The TorqueFlite 8-speed automatic transmission provides smooth gear changes and optimizes fuel efficiency across both rear-wheel and all-wheel-drive configurations. While generally durable, occasional shifting clunks have been reported by owners.

**Shifting Clunk or Delay**
- **Symptoms:** Unusual clunking noises or delayed engagement when shifting the rotary dial between drive and reverse.
- **Solution:** Check transmission fluid levels, inspect mounts, and update powertrain control modules if software-related.

## Electrical

### Infotainment Touchscreen

The electrical system manages a wide array of comfort and safety features, but owner reports frequently center on infotainment display failures.

**Screen Delamination and Ghost Touching**
- **Symptoms:** Bubbling or separating screen layers cause the display to register random inputs, freeze, or disable climate and audio controls.
- **Solution:** Replace the touchscreen display assembly.

## Exterior

### Door Panels

Exterior and interior trim structures are generally solid, though specific cosmetic components like door panels show recurring durability complaints.

**Warping and Delamination**
- **Symptoms:** Interior door panels warp, unbuckle, or detach near the door frame and lock pins, occasionally causing door locking failures.
- **Solution:** Replace the warped interior door panels.

## Safety

### Seat Belts and Airbags

Safety systems protect occupants effectively in collisions, but harness and sensor faults can trigger warning lights requiring component replacement.

**Airbag Warning Light and Seatbelt Harness Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The airbag warning light illuminates on the dashboard due to faults in the driver seatbelt harness or buckle assembly.
- **Solution:** Inspect and replace the faulty seatbelt harness or buckle component.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V332000: Electrical System:Wiring](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chrysler&model=300&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** These vehicles are being recalled to address a defect that could prevent the cruise control system from disengaging. If, when using cruise control, there is a short circuit within the vehicle's wiring, the driver may not be able to shut off the cruise control either by depressing the brake pedal or manually turning the system off once it has been engaged.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the software, and perform a software flash on the engine or powertrain control module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V097000: Power Train:Axle Assembly:Axle Shaft](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chrysler&model=300&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** A loss of motive power can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will replace all eight front driveshaft bolts, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2017 Chrysler 300 remains an appealing choice for buyers seeking a large, comfortable sedan with strong engine choices. Prospective owners should carefully test all infotainment, climate, and door functions during a pre-purchase inspection to avoid unexpected repair costs.
