# 2021 Chrysler 300 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2021 Chrysler 300, buyers will find a classic, full-size American sedan offering a spacious cabin, comfortable ride, and solid performance options. While generally well-regarded for long-distance cruising, owner reports submitted to safety authorities highlight a few specific areas to watch out for, including intermittent engine misfires and occasional occupant restraint concerns. Thorough pre-purchase inspections and verifying that all manufacturer safety campaigns have been completed will help ensure a reliable ownership experience.

---

## Engine

### 3.6L V6 Pentastar

The 3.6-liter Pentastar V6 engine produces approximately 292 horsepower and 260 lb-ft of torque, providing smooth everyday acceleration. It is a widely used powerplant across the brand's lineup, though used buyers should monitor for occasional reports of rough idling and stalling.

**Engine Misfire and Rough Idle**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have reported rough idling, unexpected engine stalling, and decreased fuel efficiency, sometimes without triggering an immediate check engine light.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified technician perform a full diagnostic scan, inspect ignition components, and check for fuel delivery or timing concerns.

### 5.7L V8 HEMI

The 5.7-liter HEMI V8 generates 363 horsepower and 394 lb-ft of torque, offering robust performance and classic muscle-car character. This option pairs exclusively with rear-wheel drive and is generally durable, though routine maintenance is essential.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

The TorqueFlite 8-speed automatic transmission delivers smooth, responsive gear changes and contributes to highway fuel efficiency. No widespread, recurring complaint patterns are currently dominating owner feedback for this model year.

## Safety

### Seat Belts

Restraint systems generally perform as expected, but physical wear on seat belt straps should be manually verified.

**Seat Belt Separation**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have experienced seat belt webbing unexpectedly tearing or unraveling at the seams during normal use.
- **Solution:** Inspect all seat belt webbing and anchor points closely during a test drive; replace any damaged assemblies immediately.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V516000: Visibility:Windshield](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chrysler&model=300&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** During manufacturing, the windshield may not have been properly bonded to the vehicle, allowing it to detach in a crash. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 212, "Windshield Mounting." A windshield that separates from the vehicle during a crash can increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will remove and replace the front windshield urethane sealant, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V198000: Air Bags:Side/Window:Curtain:Inflator](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chrysler&model=300&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** An inflator rupture may result in sharp metal fragments striking occupants, resulting in injury or death.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace both side curtain air bags, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2021 Chrysler 300 remains an appealing option for buyers seeking traditional rear-wheel-drive sedan comfort and styling. While most mechanical systems prove dependable, prospective owners should carefully check for smooth engine operation, inspect the condition of all seat belts, and confirm that all relevant dealership safety repairs have been successfully addressed.
