# 2019 Chrysler 300 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2019 Chrysler 300 offers a classic large-sedan feel, roomy cabin, and powerful engine options. While overall mechanically straightforward, used-car buyers should review electronics, lighting, and powertrain performance carefully during a test drive to ensure everything operates smoothly.

---

## Engine

### 3.6L V6 Engine

The standard 3.6-liter Pentastar V6 produces around 292 horsepower and is paired with an 8-speed automatic transmission, offering smooth daily drivability and respectable fuel economy for a full-size sedan.

### 5.7L HEMI V8 Engine

The optional 5.7-liter HEMI V8 churns out 363 horsepower, delivering robust acceleration and traditional muscle-car performance when mated to its multi-speed automatic transmission.

## Transmission

### TorqueFlite 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 8-speed automatic transmission shifts smoothly under normal conditions, though some owners have reported rough shifting and engagement concerns.

**Shifting Irregularities and Gear Engagement**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may experience hard shifting, hesitation, jerking, vibration, or unexpected auto-park engagement accompanied by diagnostic trouble codes.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified mechanic inspect transmission control modules, fluid conditions, and software calibrations for potential updates or component repair.
- **Cost:** Varies by diagnostic findings and required repairs

## Electrical

### Infotainment and Lighting Electrical System

Electrical complaints typically revolve around intermittent Uconnect software errors, lighting fuse issues, and sensor glitches.

**Uconnect System and Headlight Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** The infotainment display may show errors or freeze, and turning on the radio can occasionally cause headlight failure or flashing interior dome lights due to door sensor errors.
- **Solution:** Check fuses, inspect wiring harnesses, and perform applicable Uconnect software updates at a service center.
- **Cost:** $90 for fuse replacement based on dealer service reports

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V203000: Electrical System: Instrument Cluster/Panel](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chrysler&model=300&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** The instrument cluster may not properly illuminate driver warnings. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 101, "Control and Displays." Failure of the instrument cluster to properly illuminate may prevent the driver from receiving warnings about the vehicle's systems, increasing.
- **What to do:** Chrysler has notified owners, and dealers will update the instrument cluster software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V198000: Air Bags:Side/Window:Curtain:Inflator](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chrysler&model=300&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** An inflator rupture may result in sharp metal fragments striking occupants, resulting in injury or death.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace both side curtain air bags, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2019 Chrysler 300 remains an appealing option for buyers seeking a spacious, comfortable cruiser with strong engine choices. Prospective owners should thoroughly check the electronics, infotainment system, and transmission shifting behavior during a pre-purchase inspection to avoid lingering issues.
