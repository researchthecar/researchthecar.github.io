# 2020 Chrysler 300 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2020 Chrysler 300 delivers classic full-size sedan styling, a spacious cabin, and a choice of robust powertrains ranging from an efficient V6 to a powerful HEMI V8. Used car buyers looking at this model should review the available owner-reported issues concerning interior technology, electrical components, and minor structural items. While NHTSA complaints are owner reports rather than definitive proof of widespread defects, keeping an eye on these specific areas during a pre-purchase inspection can help ensure a smoother ownership experience.

---

## Engine

### 3.6L Pentastar V6

The 3.6-liter Pentastar V6 produces around 292 to 300 horsepower and 260 lb-ft of torque, offering a smooth blend of everyday performance and efficiency. It is widely used across the manufacturer's lineup and generally proves dependable with standard maintenance.

### 5.7L HEMI V8

The 5.7-liter HEMI V8 pushes out 363 horsepower and 394 lb-ft of torque, delivering strong acceleration and traditional muscle-car character. This engine option is well-regarded for durability, though it demands higher fuel consumption.

## Transmission

### TorqueFlite 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

The TorqueFlite 8-speed automatic transmission pairs with both engine options, delivering responsive shifting and reliable highway cruising. Overall, it is considered a robust unit with few widespread mechanical complaints.

**Rotary Shifter Vibration and Grinding**
- **Symptoms:** The center console rotary shifter knob may emit a grinding noise or vibrate noticeably while navigating curves.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified technician inspect the shifter assembly linkage and transmission range sensor for proper operation.

## Electrical

### Infotainment Touchscreen

Electrical and infotainment systems generally operate without issue, though occasional screen degradation has been reported by owners.

**Screen Delamination and Unresponsive Controls**
- **Symptoms:** The infotainment LCD screen develops bubbling or delamination, causing random beeping, temperature changes, and loss of radio or climate control functionality.
- **Solution:** Replace the touchscreen display assembly or head unit to restore normal system operation.

## Exterior

### Sunroof

Exterior panels and trim hold up well, though panoramic glass components warrant a close visual check during an inspection.

**Panoramic Sunroof Glass Fracture**
- **Symptoms:** The panoramic sunroof glass may shatter unexpectedly while driving.
- **Solution:** Replace the damaged glass panel and inspect the frame for alignment issues or stress points.

## Safety

### Air Bag System

Safety systems standard features function reliably, though isolated complaints regarding unexpected air bag deployments have been documented.

**Inadvertent Curtain Air Bag Deployment**
- **Symptoms:** Side curtain air bags may deploy unexpectedly without an impact or visible collision damage.
- **Solution:** Have the air bag control module and related wiring inspected by a certified dealer or technician.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V516000: Visibility:Windshield](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chrysler&model=300&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** During manufacturing, the windshield may not have been properly bonded to the vehicle, allowing it to detach in a crash. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 212, "Windshield Mounting." A windshield that separates from the vehicle during a crash can increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will remove and replace the front windshield urethane sealant, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V198000: Air Bags:Side/Window:Curtain:Inflator](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chrysler&model=300&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** An inflator rupture may result in sharp metal fragments striking occupants, resulting in injury or death.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace both side curtain air bags, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2020 Chrysler 300 remains a compelling choice for buyers seeking a comfortable, traditional American sedan with strong engine choices and a smooth automatic transmission. While most owners experience trouble-free driving, used car shoppers should thoroughly check the infotainment screen, panoramic glass, and steering/shifter operation prior to purchase. Conducting a comprehensive pre-purchase inspection will help catch any lingering owner-reported quirks before finalizing the sale.
