# 2022 Chrysler 300 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2022 Chrysler 300, buyers will find a stately full-size sedan known for its commanding road presence, spacious interior, and classic American cruiser feel. While owner reports to safety authorities highlight a few isolated areas of concern, prospective owners should evaluate the vehicle's powertrain choices and general condition thoroughly during a test drive.

---

## Engine

### 3.6L Pentastar V6

The 3.6-liter Pentastar V6 produces approximately 300 horsepower and 260 lb-ft of torque, offering smooth everyday acceleration. It is paired with rear-wheel drive or all-wheel drive, providing a dependable and widely used baseline for this full-size sedan.

### 5.7L HEMI V8

The 5.7-liter HEMI V8 pushes out around 363 horsepower and 394 lb-ft of torque for robust performance. Available on higher trims with rear-wheel drive, it delivers strong acceleration and a classic V8 exhaust note.

### Alternator and Charging System

Electrical system concerns center around potential alternator diode failures affecting power supply.

**Alternator Diode Failure**
- **Symptoms:** A faulty alternator diode can cause the vehicle to unexpectedly lose electrical power and stall while driving.
- **Solution:** Inspect the charging system and replace the alternator if diode failure is detected.

## Transmission

### TorqueFlite 8-Speed Automatic

The TorqueFlite 8-speed automatic transmission delivers crisp and efficient shifting across all trim levels and drivetrain configurations. It is generally praised for its refined operation and durability in daily driving conditions.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

Brake system complaints focus on intermittent performance and diagnostic challenges.

**Brake System Performance Concerns**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have reported unusual brake system behavior or perceived failures that can prove difficult to diagnose and resolve.
- **Solution:** Conduct a thorough mechanical inspection of pads, rotors, lines, and the master cylinder.

## Safety

### Air Bags

Owner reports note rare instances of air bags failing to deploy during collisions.

**Frontal Impact Air Bag Non-Deployment**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have reported instances where frontal collisions occurred at moderate speeds and the air bags failed to deploy.
- **Solution:** Have the Supplemental Restraint System (SRS) inspected by a qualified technician to ensure sensors and modules are functioning correctly.
