# 2023 Chrysler 300 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2023 Chrysler 300 represents the final model year for this iconic American full-size sedan, bringing rear-wheel drive comfort and muscular styling to its swan song. While it offers a spacious cabin and strong powertrain options, prospective buyers should review available owner reports covering minor trim fitment and high-performance brake wear. As an older vehicle architecture, checking service history and performing a thorough pre-purchase inspection remains essential.

---

## Engine

### 3.6L Pentastar V6

The 3.6-liter Pentastar V6 produces approximately 292 to 300 horsepower and 260 lb-ft of torque, serving as the standard engine for base and touring trims. It is a widely used powerplant known for balanced daily performance and straightforward maintenance.

### 5.7L Hemi V8

The 5.7-liter Hemi V8 generates 363 horsepower and 394 lb-ft of torque, delivering classic muscle car acceleration for mid-tier trims. It provides robust performance with a proven track record, though fuel economy is notably lower than the V6.

### 6.4L Hemi V8

Exclusive to the limited-edition 300C, the 6.4-liter Hemi V8 pushes out 485 horsepower and 475 lb-ft of torque for high-performance driving enthusiasts. This engine transforms the sedan into a serious straight-line performer but requires careful attention to supporting drivetrain components.

## Transmission

### TorqueFlite 8-Speed Automatic

Paired across all engine options, the ZF-derived TorqueFlite 8-speed automatic transmission delivers smooth and responsive gear shifts. It is generally regarded as a durable and refined unit in modern Chrysler applications.

## Brakes

### Brembo Brake Rotors

High-performance variants feature heavy-duty braking systems that offer strong stopping power but may be prone to premature wear under aggressive driving conditions.

**Premature Rotor Warping**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have reported brake pedal pulsation and vibration during normal stops, sometimes occurring at very low mileage due to heat buildup.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified mechanic inspect the brake rotors and pads, and replace or resurface the rotors if warping is confirmed.

## Exterior

### Fender Alignment

Exterior panels generally maintain solid construction, though occasional fitment inconsistencies have been reported by early owners.

**Improperly Installed Body Panels**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have noted that exterior body panels, such as passenger-side fenders, may pop out of place or require manual adjustment.
- **Solution:** Inspect body panel gaps and clips during a test drive, and have a body shop secure or replace faulty mounting hardware.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V112000: Parking Brake](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chrysler&model=300&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 135, "Light Vehicle Brake Systems." A vehicle roll away increases the risk of a crash or injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will properly adjust the parking brake, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2023 Chrysler 300 remains a compelling choice for buyers seeking traditional rear-wheel-drive sedan comfort and strong V6 or V8 performance. While isolated owner complaints highlight potential concerns with high-performance brake rotor wear and minor exterior panel fitment, the vehicle shows no widespread systemic mechanical failures. Buyers should verify maintenance records and conduct a comprehensive inspection, especially on performance trims equipped with Brembo brakes.
