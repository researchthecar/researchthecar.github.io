# 2016 Chrysler 300 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2016 Chrysler 300 offers a classic full-size sedan layout with confident rear-wheel or all-wheel-drive capability and spacious comfort. However, used car buyers should inspect powertrain components and interior trim integrity closely, as owner reports point to a few recurring mechanical and electrical pain points.

---

## Engine

### 3.6L V6

The 3.6-liter Pentastar V6 produces approximately 292 horsepower and 260 lb-ft of torque, serving as the standard reliable powerplant for everyday driving.

### 5.7L V8 HEMI

The 5.7-liter HEMI V8 generates 363 horsepower and 394 lb-ft of torque, delivering robust acceleration and traditional muscle-car performance.

**Oil Filter Housing Leak**
- **Symptoms:** Oil leaks beneath the upper intake plenum due to a failing plastic oil filter housing or sender unit.
- **Solution:** Replace the oil filter housing assembly, often upgrading to an improved aftermarket or updated OEM unit.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic

The ZF-sourced 8-speed automatic transmission provides smooth power delivery across trims, though some owners report shifting irregularities requiring software or mechanical attention.

**Rough Shifting and Engagement Delay**
- **Symptoms:** Hard shifting between first and second gears, delayed engagement upon takeoff, and harsh downshifts when slowing down.
- **Solution:** Reprogram the transmission control module or inspect internal valve body and software updates.

## Steering

### Electronic Power Steering

The electronic power steering system offers light maneuvering at low speeds, but sudden assist failures have been noted in owner complaints.

**Power Steering Loss**
- **Symptoms:** Inadvertent loss of power steering assistance while driving at highway speeds, accompanied by steering stiffness or locking.
- **Solution:** Inspect power steering electrical connections, wiring harnesses, and replace faulty steering gear assemblies if necessary.

## Exterior

### Door Panels and Trim

Exterior and body trim elements generally hold up well, though cabin door panel degradation is a recognized cosmetic and structural nuisance.

**Interior Door Panel Separation**
- **Symptoms:** Interior door panels begin separating from the door frame, exposing padding and causing vibration or interference with door locks.
- **Solution:** Re-secure or replace the affected interior door panels and retaining clips.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18E053000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission:Control Module (Tcm/Pcm/Tecm)](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chrysler&model=300&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** The PCMs are being recalled to address a defect that could prevent the cruise control system from disengaging. If, when using cruise control, there is a short circuit within the vehicle's wiring, the driver may not be able to shut off the cruise control either by depressing the brake pedal or manually turning the system off once it has been engaged.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will either perform a software update or replace the PCM, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V332000: Electrical System:Wiring](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chrysler&model=300&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** These vehicles are being recalled to address a defect that could prevent the cruise control system from disengaging. If, when using cruise control, there is a short circuit within the vehicle's wiring, the driver may not be able to shut off the cruise control either by depressing the brake pedal or manually turning the system off once it has been engaged.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the software, and perform a software flash on the engine or powertrain control module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V097000: Power Train:Axle Assembly:Axle Shaft](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chrysler&model=300&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** A loss of motive power can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will replace all eight front driveshaft bolts, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

When shopping for a used 2016 Chrysler 300, verify proper transmission shift quality and test drive the vehicle to ensure smooth steering operation. Pay close attention during a pre-purchase inspection for oil leaks around the engine valley and interior trim wear.
