The 2005 Chrysler Town & Country is a popular minivan known for its spaciousness and versatility, making it a favorite for families. Engine options include a 3.3L V6 producing 180 horsepower and 210 lb-ft of torque, a 3.8L V6 with 215 horsepower and 245 lb-ft of torque, and a 2.4L Inline-4 with 150 horsepower and 165 lb-ft of torque. The 3.3L and 3.8L are typically paired with a 4-speed automatic transmission. Owners appreciate the comfortable ride and ample storage. However, common complaints revolve around reliability issues, particularly with electrical systems and transmission problems.

**Engine**

**2.4L Inline-4**

*   **Engine Misfires**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rough idling, engine hesitates during acceleration, check engine light illuminates. Often caused by faulty ignition coils, spark plugs, or fuel injectors.
    *   Solution: Replace faulty ignition coils, spark plugs, or fuel injectors. Diagnose and repair vacuum leaks.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $400+ depending on the number of parts replaced and labor costs.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**3.3L V6**

*   **EGR Valve Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Check engine light, poor acceleration, rough idle.
    *   Solution: Replace the EGR valve.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Coolant Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Loss of coolant, overheating, visible leaks under the vehicle. Often originates from the water pump, radiator, or hoses.
    *   Solution: Replace leaking water pump, radiator, or hoses.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $600+ depending on the source of the leak.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**3.8L V6**

*   **Oil Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Oil spots under the vehicle, burning oil smell, low oil level. Often caused by worn valve cover gaskets or oil pan gaskets.
    *   Solution: Replace valve cover gaskets or oil pan gaskets.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $400+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Crankshaft Position Sensor Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Engine stalls, difficulty starting, check engine light.
    *   Solution: Replace the crankshaft position sensor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $80 - $200+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**4-Speed Automatic (All Engines)**

*   **Transmission Slipping/Harsh Shifting**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Delayed engagement, slipping between gears, hard shifts. Can be caused by low transmission fluid, worn clutches, or a faulty solenoid pack.
    *   Solution: Check and refill transmission fluid. If the problem persists, a transmission rebuild or replacement might be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 (fluid change) - $3000+ (rebuild/replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Torque Converter Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Shuddering or vibration at low speeds, particularly when accelerating.
    *   Solution: Replace the torque converter.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Worn Struts/Shocks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Bouncy ride, excessive body roll, poor handling.
    *   Solution: Replace worn struts and/or shocks.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600+ per axle
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Worn Sway Bar Links/Bushings**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Clunking or rattling noises, especially when going over bumps.
    *   Solution: Replace worn sway bar links and/or bushings.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+ per side
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Power Door Lock Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Power door locks failing to work, intermittently working, or only working on some doors. Often due to a faulty door lock actuator.
    *   Solution: Replace the faulty door lock actuator.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+ per door
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Power Window Motor Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Power windows failing to operate, moving slowly, or only working intermittently. Often due to a faulty window motor.
    *   Solution: Replace the faulty power window motor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $400+ per window
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Sliding Door Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Sliding doors failing to open or close properly, making unusual noises. Can be related to worn rollers, faulty door latches, or issues with the power door mechanism.
    *   Solution: Diagnose and repair or replace worn rollers, latches, or the power door mechanism.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Instrument Cluster Problems**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Gauges not working, dim display, intermittent power loss to the cluster.
    *   Solution: Repair or replace the instrument cluster.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Rust**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rust forming on the bottom of the doors, the wheel wells, and the side panels.
    *   Solution: Cut out the rust, weld in new metal, and repaint the affected area.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $1000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Headlight clouding**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Headlight lens becomes yellowed or cloudy reducing light output
    *   Solution: Polish or replace headlight lens
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $400+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Power Window Switch Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Windows failing to respond to the switch
    *   Solution: Replace the switch
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $150+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2005 Chrysler Town & Country, while offering practicality and space, is prone to a range of issues. Engine problems vary based on the engine type, with the 3.3L and 3.8L V6s experiencing leaks and sensor failures. Transmission issues are a significant concern across all engine options, often involving slipping or harsh shifting, potentially leading to costly repairs. Electrical problems such as door lock and window motor failures are also frequently reported. Suspension components wear and require replacement. Overall, potential buyers should be aware of these common issues and factor in the possibility of repairs. The severity ranges from minor fixes to major component replacements, with costs spanning from a few hundred to several thousand dollars, particularly for transmission-related problems.
