**Introduction**
The 1995 Chrysler LHS is a luxurious sedan with performance-oriented features. It is powered by a 3.5L V6 engine that produces 214 horsepower and 225 lb-ft of torque. The engine is paired with a 4-speed automatic transmission. Other engine options include a 3.3L V6 and a 3.8L V6. The 3.3L V6 has a horsepower of 162 and torque of 188 lb-ft, while the 3.8L V6 produces 253 horsepower and 255 lb-ft of torque. The 3.8L V6 comes with a 5-speed automatic transmission, while the other two engines are paired with a 4-speed automatic transmission.

Owners appreciate the LHS's comfortable ride and elegant styling. However, they also note its lack of agility and poor fuel economy.

**Engine**
* **3.5L V6**
    * **Intake manifold gasket failure:** Symptoms include coolant leaks, overheating, and a drop in engine performance. Solution: Replace the intake manifold gasket. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1000
    * **Exhaust manifold gasket failure:** Symptoms include exhaust leaks, a drop in engine performance, and a rough idle. Solution: Replace the exhaust manifold gasket. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600
    * **Oil leaks:** Symptoms include oil spots under the vehicle and a drop in oil level. Solution: Replace the leaking seals or gaskets. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
    * **Timing chain failure:** Symptoms include a rattling noise from the engine, a drop in engine performance, and a no-start condition. Solution: Replace the timing chain and related components. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1500 - $2500
* **3.3L V6**
    * **No Issues Found**
* **3.8L V6**
    * **Intake valve failure:** Symptoms include a loss of compression in one or more cylinders, a drop in engine performance, and a rough idle. Solution: Replace the affected intake valves. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1000 - $1500
    * **Exhaust valve failure:** Symptoms include a loss of compression in one or more cylinders, a drop in engine performance, and a rough idle. Solution: Replace the affected exhaust valves. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1000 - $1500

**Transmission**
* **4-Speed Automatic**
    * **Torque converter failure:** Symptoms include shuddering, slipping, and a loss of power. Solution: Replace the torque converter. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1200 - $1800
    * **Transmission fluid leaks:** Symptoms include transmission fluid spots under the vehicle and a drop in transmission fluid level. Solution: Replace the leaking seals or gaskets. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
    * **Solenoid failure:** Symptoms include harsh shifting, slipping, and a loss of power. Solution: Replace the faulty solenoid. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600
* **5-Speed Automatic**
    * **No Issues Found**

**Suspension**
* **Strut mount failure:** Symptoms include a clunking noise from the front end, a decrease in ride quality, and uneven tire wear. Solution: Replace the strut mounts. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600
* **Sway bar link failure:** Symptoms include a clunking noise from the front or rear of the vehicle, a decrease in handling, and uneven tire wear. Solution: Replace the sway bar links. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
* **Control arm bushing failure:** Symptoms include a clunking noise from the front or rear of the vehicle, a decrease in handling, and uneven tire wear. Solution: Replace the control arm bushings. Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800

**Electrical**
* **Battery failure:** Symptoms include difficulty starting the vehicle, a dim or flickering dashboard, and a loss of electrical power. Solution: Replace the battery. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200
* **Alternator failure:** Symptoms include a dim or flickering dashboard, a drop in battery voltage, and a loss of electrical power. Solution: Replace the alternator. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800
* **Starter failure:** Symptoms include a clicking noise when trying to start the vehicle, a no-start condition, and a loss of electrical power. Solution: Replace the starter. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500

**Exterior**
* **Headlight failure:** Symptoms include a dim or flickering headlight, a complete loss of headlight, and a warning light on the dashboard. Solution: Replace the headlight bulb or ballast. Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $200
* **Taillight failure:** Symptoms include a dim or flickering taillight, a complete loss of taillight, and a warning light on the dashboard. Solution: Replace the taillight bulb or assembly. Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $200

**Interior**
* **Heater core failure:** Symptoms include a lack of heat from the heater, a sweet smell in the cabin, and a fogging windshield. Solution: Replace the heater core. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800
* **Air conditioning failure:** Symptoms include a lack of cool air from the air conditioner, a weak or intermittent airflow, and a musty smell in the cabin. Solution: Repair or replace the air conditioning system components. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1500

**Summary**
The 1995 Chrysler LHS has some common issues that can be costly to repair. The severity of the issues varies, with some being minor inconveniences while others can affect the safety and reliability of the vehicle. It is important to stay up-to-date on maintenance and repairs to help prevent these issues from occurring or worsening.
