**Introduction to the 1995 Chrysler Intrepid**

The 1995 Chrysler Intrepid is a mid-size sedan produced by Chrysler Corporation. It was the first Chrysler vehicle to be built on the company's LH platform, which shared with the Dodge Intrepid and Eagle Vision. The Intrepid was offered with three engine options: a 3.3L V6 with 160 horsepower and 210 lb-ft of torque, a 3.5L V6 with 214 horsepower and 229 lb-ft of torque, and a 3.8L V6 with 225 horsepower and 235 lb-ft of torque. All engines were paired with a 4-speed automatic transmission.

**Engine**

**3.3L V6**

* **Intake Manifold Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Intake manifold gasket failure can cause vacuum leaks, resulting in reduced engine performance, rough idle, and increased emissions.
    * Solution: Replace the intake manifold gasket.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
    * Recalls: None Found

**3.5L V6**

* **Cylinder Head Cracking:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Cracking in the cylinder head can lead to coolant leaks, overheating, and engine damage.
    * Solution: Replace the cylinder head.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**3.8L V6**

* **Timing Chain Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Worn or stretched timing chain can cause engine misfiring, rough running, and decreased power.
    * Solution: Replace the timing chain and related components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,200+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**4-Speed Automatic**

* **Torque Converter Shudder:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Shuddering during acceleration or transmission engagement may indicate a faulty torque converter.
    * Solution: Replace the torque converter.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $600 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Front Lower Control Arm Bushings Wear:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Worn lower control arm bushings can cause excessive noise, vibration, and reduced handling.
    * Solution: Replace the lower control arm bushings.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Alternator Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: A faulty alternator can lead to battery discharge, electrical system malfunctions, and no-start conditions.
    * Solution: Replace the alternator.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Ignition Coil Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Failed ignition coils can cause engine misfiring, reduced power, and difficulty starting.
    * Solution: Replace the ignition coils.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Door Lock Actuator Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Malfunctioning door lock actuators can prevent doors from locking or unlocking properly.
    * Solution: Replace the door lock actuator.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Dashboard Cracks:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Cracks may appear on the dashboard due to exposure to sunlight and heat.
    * Solution: Replace the dashboard.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 1995 Chrysler Intrepid has various issues that range in severity and cost. Some of the more serious issues include cylinder head cracking and timing chain failure. While these issues can be expensive to repair, the Intrepid is generally a reliable vehicle with a comfortable ride and spacious interior.
