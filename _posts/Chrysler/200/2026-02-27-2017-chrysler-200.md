# 2017 Chrysler 200 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2017 Chrysler 200, buyers will find a stylish midsize sedan that offers a comfortable ride and modern cabin appointments. However, prospective owners should approach this vehicle with careful inspection, as owner reports highlight potential reliability concerns regarding engine stalling and electrical system glitches. Knowing what to look for during a test drive can help you spot these issues early.

---

## Engine

### 2.4L Tigershark 4-Cylinder

The 2.4-liter Tigershark four-cylinder engine produces 184 horsepower and 173 lb-ft of torque, serving as the standard powerplant for the model. It provides adequate fuel economy for daily commuting, though routine oil level monitoring is recommended.

### 3.6L Pentastar V6

The 3.6-liter Pentastar V6 engine delivers a robust 295 horsepower and 262 lb-ft of torque, offering brisk acceleration and smooth highway performance. It is a widely used FCA engine, but buyers should verify proper maintenance history.

**Engine Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may unexpectedly lose power or stall while driving, occasionally displaying a message prompting the driver to shift into park to restart.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified mechanic diagnose potential powertrain control module or electronic throttle body faults.

## Transmission

### 9-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 9-speed automatic transmission pairs with both engine options to optimize highway efficiency. Thoroughly test shift quality during a test drive to ensure smooth operation.

**Delayed or Harsh Shifting**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may experience delayed engagement when shifting into drive or reverse, accompanied by occasional clunking or random harsh downshifting.
- **Solution:** Check for available transmission software updates or consult a specialist for diagnostic evaluation.

## Electrical

### Electrical and Ignition System

The electrical architecture controls keyless entry, lighting, and dashboard displays, making a thorough battery and alternator test essential prior to purchase.

**Key Fob Recognition and Power Loss**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may display warnings that it cannot identify the key fob, or experience sudden loss of instrument panel and lighting functions while underway.
- **Solution:** Test the key fob battery and have the ignition node and body control module inspected by a technician.

## Safety

### Air Bag System

The supplemental restraint system provides standard airbag protection, though cabin wiring connections require periodic checks.

**Airbag Warning Light**
- **Symptoms:** The SRS airbag warning light illuminates or flashes intermittently on the instrument cluster, sometimes pointing to seat wiring harness issues.
- **Solution:** Inspect the under-seat wiring harnesses and connectors; replacement or installation of a jumper harness may be required.
- **Cost:** $1179.29

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V332000: Electrical System:Wiring](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chrysler&model=200&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** These vehicles are being recalled to address a defect that could prevent the cruise control system from disengaging. If, when using cruise control, there is a short circuit within the vehicle's wiring, the driver may not be able to shut off the cruise control either by depressing the brake pedal or manually turning the system off once it has been engaged.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the software, and perform a software flash on the engine or powertrain control module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2017 Chrysler 200 appeals to used-car buyers looking for contemporary styling and comfortable road manners at an accessible price point. However, because owner reports flag potential issues with engine stalling and transmission shift quality, buyers should prioritize a comprehensive pre-purchase inspection and an extended test drive to ensure all systems operate reliably.
