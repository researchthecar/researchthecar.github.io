# 2016 Chrysler 200 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2016 Chrysler 200 offers a sleek exterior design, a comfortable cabin, and a smooth highway ride. However, prospective buyers should approach the model with caution due to a notable history of owner-reported electrical and powertrain concerns. While it presents an attractive price point on the used market, verifying service history and checking for completed recalls is strongly recommended.

---

## Engine

### 2.4L Tigershark 4-Cylinder

The base 2.4-liter four-cylinder engine produces approximately 184 horsepower and 173 lb-ft of torque. It provides adequate daily efficiency, though buyers should regularly monitor oil levels.

### 3.6L Pentastar V6

The optional 3.6-liter V6 delivers a robust 295 horsepower and 262 lb-ft of torque, offering significantly stronger acceleration and passing power. It is generally regarded as a strong performer within Chrysler's lineup.

## Transmission

### 9-Speed Automatic Transmission

The nine-speed automatic transmission routes power to the front wheels or available all-wheel drive. It has been associated with owner complaints regarding rough shifting and unexpected neutral drops.

**Unintended Neutral Shift and Power Loss**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may unexpectedly shift into neutral or lose motive power while driving at highway speeds, accompanied by transmission warning lights.
- **Solution:** Software updates or powertrain control module reprogramming performed by a dealership may resolve some shifting anomalies.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

Brake system performance is vital, though a small number of owners have reported unexpected pedal firmness and reduced stopping responsiveness.

**Stiff Pedal and Reduced Stopping Power**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may experience an unusually hard brake pedal and significantly increased stopping distances when attempting to slow down.
- **Solution:** Inspect the brake booster, vacuum lines, and brake fluid condition; bleed or replace components as necessary.

## Steering

### Power Steering

Electronic power steering provides light effort at low speeds, but potential assist failures and tracking anomalies have been noted in owner complaints.

**Power Steering Seizure**
- **Symptoms:** The power steering warning light may illuminate, followed by a sudden loss of power steering assist or unexpected steering wheel movement.
- **Solution:** Inspect the electronic power steering rack, wiring connections, and related control modules.

## Electrical

### Electrical System

The electrical architecture manages critical vehicle functions but can be prone to intermittent glitches, sensor failures, and sudden shutdowns.

**Engine Stalling and Total Electrical Shutdown**
- **Symptoms:** The instrument cluster may flicker, and the vehicle can unexpectedly stall or lose complete electrical power without prior warning.
- **Solution:** Inspect the powertrain control module, battery connections, and ensure all relevant manufacturer software updates have been applied.

## Safety

### Air Bag

Restraint system warnings can compromise occupant safety confidence and require careful diagnostic inspection of seat sensors and wiring.

**Service Airbag Warning Light**
- **Symptoms:** The service airbag warning light illuminates on the dashboard, often triggered when a passenger occupies the front seat or places items near the seat wiring harness.
- **Solution:** Inspect under-seat wiring harnesses, connectors, and sensors for loose connections or faults.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V332000: Electrical System:Wiring](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chrysler&model=200&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** These vehicles are being recalled to address a defect that could prevent the cruise control system from disengaging. If, when using cruise control, there is a short circuit within the vehicle's wiring, the driver may not be able to shut off the cruise control either by depressing the brake pedal or manually turning the system off once it has been engaged.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the software, and perform a software flash on the engine or powertrain control module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V589000: Steering](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chrysler&model=200&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** Multiple chassis fastener joints may not have been tightened to the proper torque specification. If the chassis fastener joints are improperly torqued they may loosen and fail or fall out, reducing steering and handling capability and increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Chrysler has notified owners, and dealers will inspect the joints and tighten or replace the fasteners, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2016 Chrysler 200 appeals to buyers looking for modern styling and available V6 performance at an affordable price. However, prospective owners should thoroughly inspect the vehicle for electrical stability, transmission shifting smoothness, and airbag warning lights. Prioritize models with a documented service history and confirmed completion of safety recalls.
