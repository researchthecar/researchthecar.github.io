# 2020 Chrysler Pacifica Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2020 Chrysler Pacifica offers exceptional family utility, flexible Stow 'n Go seating, and a choice between a traditional gasoline V6 and an efficient plug-in hybrid powertrain. However, prospective buyers should review ownership history closely, as owner complaints point to recurring electrical issues, powertrain anomalies, and occasional engine cooling or head gasket concerns.

---

## Engine

### 3.6L Pentastar V6

The 3.6-liter Pentastar V6 produces 287 horsepower and 262 lb-ft of torque, providing smooth and capable performance for daily driving. While generally durable, some owners have reported cooling system leaks and head gasket failures requiring professional intervention.

**Coolant Leak and Head Gasket Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Engine overheating, coolant consumption, white smoke, and cylinder misfires due to a compromised head gasket.
- **Solution:** Inspect cylinder head and gasket integrity; replace failed head gaskets or cylinder heads as necessary.

### 3.6L V6 Plug-In Hybrid (PHEV)

The plug-in hybrid variant pairs the 3.6L V6 with dual electric motors for an EPA-estimated 32 miles of all-electric range and a combined 260 horsepower. Buyers should verify that all hybrid-specific software updates and wiring inspections have been completed.

**Loss of Motive Power and Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** Sudden loss of propulsion or unexpected engine shutdown while driving at highway speeds.
- **Solution:** Diagnostic scan for powertrain fault codes and adherence to relevant manufacturer recalls or software updates.

## Transmission

### 9-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 9-speed automatic transmission delivers power for the gas-powered models, though some owners note erratic shifting behavior or unexpected park engagement.

**Unintended Shifting into Park**
- **Symptoms:** The gear shifter or vehicle independently shifts into park or engages the emergency brake while slowing down or coming to a stop.
- **Solution:** Inspect electronic shifter modules, software calibrations, and wiring harnesses for faults.

### Si-EVT Electrically Variable Transmission (Hybrid)

The proprietary eFlite electrically variable transmission manages power distribution for the hybrid Pacifica, though internal wiring connector issues can occasionally trigger propulsion warnings.

**Internal Wiring and Propulsion Faults**
- **Symptoms:** Illuminated warning lights, sudden loss of motive power, or vehicle shutdown during operation.
- **Solution:** Check internal transmission wiring connectors and perform necessary harness replacements.

## Steering

### Electric Power Steering

Electric power steering provides light effort at low speeds, though intermittent assist failures have been reported by a subset of owners.

**Power Steering Assist Loss**
- **Symptoms:** Sudden heavy steering effort or complete loss of power steering assistance while maneuvering or driving at speed.
- **Solution:** Inspect power steering control modules, wiring connections, and steering rack assemblies.

## Electrical

### Battery and Charging System

The Pacifica relies heavily on complex electrical architectures, making auxiliary battery health and sensor stability critical for avoiding unexpected warnings.

**Auxiliary Battery and Sensor Failures**
- **Symptoms:** Frequent battery depletion, intermittent electrical system malfunctions, and disabled stop-start features due to faulty internal battery sensors (IBS).
- **Solution:** Replace failing auxiliary or main batteries and update internal battery sensor modules.

## Safety

### Air Bag System

Standard airbag systems are designed to protect occupants during impacts, though isolated owner reports highlight deployment failures during major collisions.

**Airbag Non-Deployment**
- **Symptoms:** Failure of front or side airbags to deploy during significant frontal collisions.
- **Solution:** Inspect SRS control modules, impact sensors, and wiring harnesses following diagnostic protocols.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V191000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chrysler&model=Pacifica&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 111, "Rearview Mirrors." The lingering rearview image can distract the driver, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Chrysler has notified owners, and dealers will update the radio display software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V010000: Electrical System:Wiring:Connectors/Plugs/Receptacles](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chrysler&model=Pacifica&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** An internal transmission wiring connector may short circuit, resulting in an unexpected engine shutdown. An unexpected engine shutdown can cause a loss of drive power, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the Power Inverter Module (PIM) software and, if necessary, update the instrument panel cluster (IPC) and the electronic control module (ECM) software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V793000: Air Bags:Side/Window:Curtain](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chrysler&model=Pacifica&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** An improperly secured connector for the side curtain air bags may prevent the air bags from deploying. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard numbers 214, "Side Impact Protection" and 226, "Ejection Mitigation." An air bag that does not deploy during a crash increases the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will secure the connector, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V876000: Equipment Adaptive/Mobility:Wheelchair Restraints/Securement:Latch/Anchor:](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chrysler&model=Pacifica&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** The retractors may not lock, preventing the wheelchair from being properly secured. An unsecured wheelchair can move during transit, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 26V362000: Electrical System:Propulsion System:Traction Battery](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chrysler&model=Pacifica&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** The battery pack may cause a vehicle fire, even when the vehicle is parked with the ignition in the "Off" position. A vehicle fire increases the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the high voltage battery pack control module software. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2020 Chrysler Pacifica remains a compelling choice for families seeking unmatched interior versatility and efficient powertrain options. Buyers should prioritize models with thorough service records, verify that all applicable recalls and software updates have been completed, and test drive the vehicle to check for any electrical or powertrain anomalies.
