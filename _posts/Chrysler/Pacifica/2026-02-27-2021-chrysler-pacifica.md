# 2021 Chrysler Pacifica Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2021 Chrysler Pacifica offers exceptional family-friendly versatility, blending advanced tech, comfortable seating, and available all-wheel drive or plug-in hybrid efficiency. However, prospective buyers should look closely at its reliability record, as owner reports highlight recurring concerns with electrical glitches, hybrid powertrain behaviors, and specific mechanical components. Understanding these common pain points can help you evaluate potential ownership costs and inspect a used model more thoroughly.

---

## Engine

### 3.6L V6 (Pentastar)

The standard 3.6-liter Pentastar V6 produces 287 horsepower and 262 lb-ft of torque, providing smooth and capable performance for everyday driving. While generally durable, it is known for occasional coolant and oil leaks around plastic housing components that require proactive maintenance.

**Oil Filter Housing Leak**
- **Symptoms:** Oil pooling in the engine valley or dripping underneath the vehicle, often accompanied by burning smells.
- **Solution:** Replace the plastic oil filter housing assembly with an updated unit, ensuring proper torque specifications during installation.

### 3.6L V6 Plug-In Hybrid (PHEV)

The 3.6-liter Plug-In Hybrid pairs the V6 engine with an electrically variable transmission and a high-voltage battery pack to deliver an EPA-estimated 32 miles of pure electric range. While highly efficient, owners report complex electrical and propulsion integration bugs that can cause unexpected drivability hiccups.

**Loss of Motive Power and Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** Sudden dashboard warnings, loss of throttle response, unexpected engine shutdown, or complete stalling while driving.
- **Solution:** Software updates, diagnostic evaluation of powertrain control modules, or replacement of failing high-voltage battery components as required.

### Coolant Heaters and Battery Systems

The Pacifica relies on sophisticated electronics to manage cabin climate, accessory power, and hybrid powertrain thermal loops. electrical issues and sensor faults are among the more frequently cited owner complaints.

**Electric Coolant Heater Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of cabin heat, inability to defrost windows, and diagnostic error codes like P0E15 disabling hybrid operation.
- **Solution:** Replace the failed electric coolant heater unit to restore thermal management to the high-voltage battery system.

## Transmission

### 9-Speed Automatic Transmission

Paired with the standard gasoline V6, the 9-speed automatic transmission helps maximize highway fuel economy. Some owners have experienced premature mechanical wear or shifting anomalies necessitating major repairs.

**Transmission Failure and Power Loss**
- **Symptoms:** Harsh shifting, illumination of warning lights, sudden loss of motive power, or complete failure to engage gears.
- **Solution:** Perform a diagnostic scan for fault codes; severely damaged units may require professional transmission rebuild or replacement.

### Electrically Variable Transmission (PHEV)

The eFlite electrically variable transmission seamlessly blends electric motor power with the gasoline V6 in hybrid trims. Maintenance relies heavily on software calibration and healthy high-voltage electrical systems.

**Charging and Powertrain Errors**
- **Symptoms:** Dashboard warnings stating 'Charging Error, vehicle will shut down' followed by a loss of propulsion and power steering assistance.
- **Solution:** Inspect hybrid wiring harnesses, apply manufacturer software updates, or replace affected internal electrical modules.

## Steering

### Steering Gear Assembly

The electric power steering system provides light, maneuverable handling suitable for a large family vehicle. Review any steering noises carefully during a pre-purchase test drive.

**Steering Noise and Power Assistance Loss**
- **Symptoms:** Clicking noises when turning the wheel while stationary or driving over bumps, alongside intermittent power steering dropouts.
- **Solution:** Inspect the steering column and rack components; replace the steering gear assembly if internal wear or electronic failure is confirmed.

## Exterior

### Stow 'n Go Seating

The innovative Stow 'n Go seating system allows second and third-row seats to fold completely flat into floor compartments. While immensely practical, mechanical wear in the folding hinges and headrest latches can cause operational frustrations.

**Headrest and Seat Latch Defects**
- **Symptoms:** Second-row seat headrests failing to lock upright after being raised from the floor, or seats failing to latch securely into position.
- **Solution:** Inspect the internal seat-folding mechanisms and replace damaged headrest latch assemblies or seat hardware.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V181000: Seats:Mid/Rear Assembly](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chrysler&model=Pacifica&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 225, "Child Restraint Anchorage Systems." An unsecured seat increases the risk of injury in a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and, if necessary, replace the second-row seat cushion frame, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V010000: Electrical System:Wiring:Connectors/Plugs/Receptacles](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chrysler&model=Pacifica&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** An internal transmission wiring connector may short circuit, resulting in an unexpected engine shutdown. An unexpected engine shutdown can cause a loss of drive power, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the Power Inverter Module (PIM) software and, if necessary, update the instrument panel cluster (IPC) and the electronic control module (ECM) software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V436000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chrysler&model=Pacifica&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 111, "Rear Visibility." A rearview camera image that does not display decreases the driver's visibility, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the radio software or the software will be OTA, "Over-The-Air," free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V793000: Air Bags:Side/Window:Curtain](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chrysler&model=Pacifica&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** An improperly secured connector for the side curtain air bags may prevent the air bags from deploying. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard numbers 214, "Side Impact Protection" and 226, "Ejection Mitigation." An air bag that does not deploy during a crash increases the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will secure the connector, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V876000: Equipment Adaptive/Mobility:Wheelchair Restraints/Securement:Latch/Anchor:](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chrysler&model=Pacifica&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The retractors may not lock, preventing the wheelchair from being properly secured. An unsecured wheelchair can move during transit, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 25V877000: Equipment Adaptive/Mobility:Wheelchair Restraints/Securement:Latch/Anchor:](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chrysler&model=Pacifica&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The retractors may not lock, preventing the wheelchair from being properly secured. An unsecured wheelchair can move during transit, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the retractors, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V362000: Electrical System:Propulsion System:Traction Battery](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chrysler&model=Pacifica&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The battery pack may cause a vehicle fire, even when the vehicle is parked with the ignition in the "Off" position. A vehicle fire increases the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the high voltage battery pack control module software. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2021 Chrysler Pacifica is a feature-rich and comfortable choice for families, but its reliability profile requires careful inspection before purchase. Buyers should pay close attention to transmission shift quality, hybrid charging systems, and electrical accessory operation. Taking the vehicle for a comprehensive test drive and verifying that all open recalls and software updates have been performed by a qualified dealer will help ensure a more dependable ownership experience.
