# 2018 Chrysler Pacifica Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2018 Chrysler Pacifica offers unmatched family utility, a clever Stow 'n Go seating system, and smooth ride dynamics. However, used car buyers should approach this model year with caution due to a notable volume of owner complaints regarding mechanical and electrical reliability. Prospective buyers need to carefully inspect service records and verify whether major powertrain and steering repairs have already been performed.

---

## Engine

### 3.6L V6 Engine

The standard 3.6-liter Pentastar V6 engine produces 287 horsepower and 262 lb-ft of torque, providing robust acceleration for hauling family and cargo. While generally smooth and widely used across the Chrysler lineup, this model year has accumulated recurring complaints concerning head gasket durability and coolant leaks.

**Head Gasket Failure and Coolant Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Engine overheating, sweet-smelling coolant odors, white exhaust smoke, or dashboard warning lights accompanied by engine misfires and loss of power.
- **Solution:** Requires professional diagnosis and potential head gasket replacement or cylinder head service.

**Engine Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** Unexpected engine shut-off while driving or difficulty restarting the vehicle after being parked.
- **Solution:** Inspection of electronic throttle bodies, sensors, or powertrain control modules by a qualified technician.

## Transmission

### 9-Speed Automatic Transmission

Equipped with a 9-speed automatic transmission paired to front-wheel drive, the standard powertrain aims to balance highway efficiency and responsiveness. Buyers should monitor for early signs of fluid leaks or irregular shift quality during test drives.

**Fluid Leaks and Powertrain Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** Transmission fluid pooling under the vehicle, harsh shifting, or unexpected warning messages related to powertrain performance.
- **Solution:** Inspect transmission seals and gaskets for wear; replace leaking components and top off fluid levels.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

The braking system must manage a heavy family van, but owner complaints highlight intermittent booster stiffness and inconsistent pedal feedback during hard stops.

**Stiff Brake Pedal and Braking Hesitation**
- **Symptoms:** The brake pedal suddenly becomes extremely hard to depress, pushes back against the foot, or requires excessive stopping distance.
- **Solution:** Inspect brake booster components and regenerative braking handoff systems for proper hydraulic pressure.

## Steering

### Electric Power Steering System

The electric power steering setup is central to low-effort maneuverability in parking lots and weighted feel at highway speeds. Owners have reported intermittent sticking and loss of power assist, making a thorough steering check essential before purchasing.

**Steering Wheel Sticking and Loss of Assist**
- **Symptoms:** The steering wheel intermittently sticks or hangs in position during turns, requiring extra physical effort to maneuver around highway curves.
- **Solution:** Have the electric power steering assembly and software inspected by a dealer or specialist.

## Electrical

### Battery and Charging System

The electrical architecture supports advanced cabin tech, sliding doors, and convenience features, but parasitic draws and starting issues remain a frequent point of frustration among owners.

**Parasitic Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Battery drains completely between starts, requiring frequent jumps or leaving dashboard screens blank upon startup.
- **Solution:** Diagnose electrical draws and replace faulty battery cells, modules, or software as needed.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V395000: Electrical System:Ignition:Anti-Theft:Control Module](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chrysler&model=Pacifica&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 114, "Theft Protection." If the MPR is engaged unintentionally, the vehicle could roll away striking and injuring a bystander or cause a crash.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will replace the MPR plug, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V492000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chrysler&model=Pacifica&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** A component in the transmission may not have been welded properly, possibly causing the transmission to not transmit engine power to the wheels. If the transmission weld fails, the vehicle will stop moving, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will replace the transmission, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V493000: Power Train:Axle Assembly:Axle Shaft](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chrysler&model=Pacifica&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** Either condition may increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will replace the left or right halfshaft assembly, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V332000: Electrical System:Wiring](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chrysler&model=Pacifica&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** These vehicles are being recalled to address a defect that could prevent the cruise control system from disengaging. If, when using cruise control, there is a short circuit within the vehicle's wiring, the driver may not be able to shut off the cruise control either by depressing the brake pedal or manually turning the system off once it has been engaged.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the software, and perform a software flash on the engine or powertrain control module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V143000: Suspension:Front:Control Arm:Lower Arm](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chrysler&model=Pacifica&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** A control arm separation could increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the right front knuckle and lower control arm for damage, replacing the components as necessary. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V348000: Electrical System:12V/24V/48V Battery](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chrysler&model=Pacifica&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** A loss of power steering assist or a stall can increase the risk of crash.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the battery ground surfaces, clean the contact surfaces as needed, and properly reassemble the ground connection, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V524000: Electrical System:Wiring:Front Underhood](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chrysler&model=Pacifica&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** A vehicle stall can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will replace the powertrain control module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V824000: Equipment](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chrysler&model=Pacifica&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** Chrysler (FCA US LLC) is recalling various Dodge, Chrysler, and RAM vehicles equipped with Kidde Plastic-Handle or Push Button 'Pindicator' Fire Extinguishers. These extinguishers may become clogged, preventing the extinguisher from discharging as expected or requiring excessive force to activate the extinguisher.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2018 Chrysler Pacifica remains a practical and versatile choice for families, but its above-average volume of engine and steering complaints warrants caution. Buyers should prioritize models with documented maintenance histories, verify the health of the engine head gaskets, and test the steering thoroughly. Securing an extended warranty can also provide valuable peace of mind against potential powertrain or electrical repairs.
