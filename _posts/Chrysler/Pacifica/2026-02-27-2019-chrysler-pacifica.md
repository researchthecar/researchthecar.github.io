# 2019 Chrysler Pacifica Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2019 Chrysler Pacifica is a popular family hauler praised for its comfortable interior, Stow 'n Go seating versatility, and available plug-in hybrid efficiency. However, used car buyers should approach this model year with caution due to a significant cluster of owner complaints. Key areas of concern involve engine issues, steering failures, and brake system anomalies that require careful inspection before purchasing.

---

## Engine

### 3.6L V6 (Pentastar)

The standard 3.6-liter Pentastar V6 engine produces around 287 horsepower and 262 lb-ft of torque, paired with a smooth-shifting automatic transmission. While it offers strong performance and decent fuel economy for a minivan, potential buyers should watch out for reported cooling system leaks and head gasket issues.

**Head Gasket Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers report engine misfires, white exhaust smoke, and coolant loss, often resulting from blown head gaskets at relatively moderate mileage.
- **Solution:** Replace the head gaskets and inspect the cylinder heads for warping or damage.

**Engine Stalling and Loss of Power**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicles may experience unexpected engine stalls while driving or display warnings such as "Stop Safely – Vehicle Will Shut Off Soon."
- **Solution:** Diagnose electrical codes, auxiliary battery health, and powertrain control modules to identify the root cause of the shutdown.

### 3.6L V6 Plug-In Hybrid (PHEV)

The 3.6-liter plug-in hybrid powertrain combines the V6 engine with dual electric motors to deliver 260 total horsepower and electric-only driving range. It provides excellent efficiency for city driving, though its complex electrical architecture requires thorough diagnostic checks.

**Hybrid Propulsion and Auxiliary Battery Faults**
- **Symptoms:** Owners note sudden auxiliary battery drain, charging system failures, and unexpected electrical warnings related to the hybrid powertrain.
- **Solution:** Check battery health, software update status, and charging connectors for faults.

## Transmission

### 9-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 9-speed automatic transmission is paired with the standard V6 engine, aiming to optimize highway efficiency. While generally adequate when functioning properly, some owners have reported rough shifting and control module glitches.

**Harsh Shifting and Unexpected Park Engagement**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers experience abrupt gear transitions, transmission jerking, and rare instances where the vehicle unexpectedly shifts into park while stopped.
- **Solution:** Reprogram the transmission control module or inspect internal electronic shifter assemblies for failure.

## Brakes

### Hydraulic Brake System

The standard anti-lock disc brakes provide adequate stopping power for daily driving duties. Buyers should test the brake pedal feel thoroughly to ensure the brake booster is operating without resistance or hissing.

**Brake Booster Failure and Soft Pedal**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers note a hissing sound when depressing the brake pedal, increased stopping distances, and a firm pedal that pushes back against the driver.
- **Solution:** Replace the faulty brake booster and inspect hydraulic lines for proper pressure.

## Steering

### Electric Power Steering

The electric power steering system provides light and adjustable feedback suited for parking and maneuvering a large minivan. However, complaints regarding sticking sensations and sudden assist failures warrant a careful pre-purchase test drive.

**Steering Assist Failure and Sticking**
- **Symptoms:** The steering wheel may intermittently stick or catch when navigating curves, requiring extra effort and causing over-correction, sometimes accompanied by a complete loss of power steering assist.
- **Solution:** Inspect the electric power steering assembly and steering gear for mechanical binding or electrical faults.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V348000: Electrical System:12V/24V/48V Battery](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chrysler&model=Pacifica&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** A loss of power steering assist or a stall can increase the risk of crash.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the battery ground surfaces, clean the contact surfaces as needed, and properly reassemble the ground connection, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V524000: Electrical System:Wiring:Front Underhood](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chrysler&model=Pacifica&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** A vehicle stall can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will replace the powertrain control module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V191000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chrysler&model=Pacifica&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 111, "Rearview Mirrors." The lingering rearview image can distract the driver, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Chrysler has notified owners, and dealers will update the radio display software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V886000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chrysler&model=Pacifica&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 111, "Rearview Mirrors." The lingering rearview image can distract the driver, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will update the radio software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V010000: Electrical System:Wiring:Connectors/Plugs/Receptacles](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chrysler&model=Pacifica&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** An internal transmission wiring connector may short circuit, resulting in an unexpected engine shutdown. An unexpected engine shutdown can cause a loss of drive power, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the Power Inverter Module (PIM) software and, if necessary, update the instrument panel cluster (IPC) and the electronic control module (ECM) software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V876000: Equipment Adaptive/Mobility:Wheelchair Restraints/Securement:Latch/Anchor:](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chrysler&model=Pacifica&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** The retractors may not lock, preventing the wheelchair from being properly secured. An unsecured wheelchair can move during transit, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 26V327000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chrysler&model=Pacifica&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** Contamination in the central vision processing module or improper programming may cause the rearview camera image not to display. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 111, "Rear Visibility." A rearview image that does not display reduces the driver's view behind the vehicle.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the central vision processing module software or replace the module, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2019 Chrysler Pacifica remains an appealing choice for families seeking unmatched interior flexibility and comfort. However, its reliability record is marred by notable complaints concerning head gasket leaks, steering assist binding, and brake booster malfunctions. Prospective buyers should prioritize models with documented service histories, verify that all safety recalls have been addressed, and conduct a thorough mechanical inspection before purchasing.
