# 2024 Chrysler Pacifica Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2024 Chrysler Pacifica continues to offer family-friendly versatility, advanced seating configurations, and the choice of standard V6 power or a plug-in hybrid setup. However, owner reports highlight some recurring concerns regarding sudden electrical shutdowns, transmission failures, and steering behavior. Prospective buyers should review service history carefully and ensure all applicable software updates have been applied.

---

## Engine

### 3.6L V6 Engine

The 3.6-liter Pentastar V6 produces 287 horsepower and 262 lb-ft of torque, providing smooth and capable acceleration for daily family hauling. While generally established mechanically, early-production electrical and software integrations can occasionally lead to unexpected stall events.

**Sudden Loss of Power and Engine Shutdown**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may completely shut down while driving at low or high speeds, killing the engine and all electrical systems simultaneously.
- **Solution:** Have a certified technician diagnose electrical modules, battery connections, and powertrain control software for faults.

### 3.6L V6 Plug-In Hybrid (PHEV)

The Plug-In Hybrid variant pairs the 3.6-liter V6 with an electrically variable transmission and dual electric motors, generating a total of 260 horsepower while offering an EPA-estimated all-electric range. Buyers should verify battery health and hybrid system software updates during inspection.

## Transmission

### 9-Speed Automatic Transmission

The conventional 9-speed automatic transmission routes power smoothly in front-wheel or all-wheel-drive configurations, though some owners have experienced abrupt shifting behavior or premature mechanical failure.

**Transmission Failure and Limp Mode**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may enter 'turtle mode' or limp mode unexpectedly, or suffer complete internal transmission failure requiring replacement.
- **Solution:** Take the vehicle to a dealer for diagnostic trouble code scans, software reflashes, or transmission replacement under warranty if necessary.

## Steering

### Steering System

The electric power steering system provides light and maneuverable low-speed handling suitable for parking lots, but highway tracking concerns have been noted by some owners.

**Highway Steering Vagueness**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may experience unusual steering sensations, pulling, or jerking behavior while maintaining highway speeds.
- **Solution:** Have the steering linkage, wheel alignment, and electronic power steering software inspected by a qualified professional.

## Electrical

### Body Electrical and Doors

The vehicle relies heavily on complex electronic modules to manage convenience features, sliding doors, and driver assistance systems, which can occasionally trigger false warning messages.

**Sliding Door Warning Errors**
- **Symptoms:** The driver information display may falsely indicate that a rear sliding door is open while the vehicle is actively being driven.
- **Solution:** Inspect sliding door contact switches, wiring harnesses, and body control module software for proper operation.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V199000: Air Bags:Frontal:Driver Clockspring/Spiral Cassette](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chrysler&model=Pacifica&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** The steering column control module may not be welded properly, which can prevent the driver's air bag from deploying in a crash. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 208, "Occupant Crash Protection." An air bag that does not deploy in a crash can increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the steering column control module, as necessary free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V238000: Visibility:Windshield:Critical Fasteners](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chrysler&model=Pacifica&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** The windshield may not have been properly bonded to the vehicle, allowing it to detach during a crash. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 212, "Windshield Mounting." A windshield that detaches from the vehicle during a crash can increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the windshield, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V240000: Traction Control System](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chrysler&model=Pacifica&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** An inactive traction control system can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the TCS software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V888000: Seats:Barriers](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chrysler&model=Pacifica&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** The seat limiter may not have been installed, allowing the seat to move too far forward. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 208, "Occupant Crash Protection." A seat positioned too close to the air bag can increase the risk of injury during a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and install a seat limiter as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V302000: Air Bags:Side/Window:Curtain](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chrysler&model=Pacifica&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 226, "Ejection Mitigation." An air bag with insufficient pressure retention increases the risk of ejection and injury during a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the side curtain air bags, as necessary free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V471000: Equipment:Appliance:Tv/Radio/Speakers](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chrysler&model=Pacifica&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 111, "Rear Visibility." A rearview image that does not display reduces the driver's view of what is behind the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 25V876000: Equipment Adaptive/Mobility:Wheelchair Restraints/Securement:Latch/Anchor:](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chrysler&model=Pacifica&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** The retractors may not lock, preventing the wheelchair from being properly secured. An unsecured wheelchair can move during transit, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 25V877000: Equipment Adaptive/Mobility:Wheelchair Restraints/Securement:Latch/Anchor:](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chrysler&model=Pacifica&modelYear=2024)**

## Summary Verdict

The 2024 Chrysler Pacifica remains a feature-packed and comfortable choice for families, but prospective used-car buyers should proceed with careful inspection. Pay close attention to powertrain performance, transmission shifting smoothness, and electrical system stability during a test drive. Verifying remaining factory warranty coverage can provide valuable peace of mind.
