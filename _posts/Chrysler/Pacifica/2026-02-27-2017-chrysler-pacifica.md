# 2017 Chrysler Pacifica Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2017 Chrysler Pacifica introduced a sleek modern design, highly praised Stow 'n Go seating, and respectable family-friendly utility. However, as an introductory model year, it has accumulated a significant volume of owner complaints regarding powertrain stability, electrical anomalies, and steering concerns. Used-car buyers should approach this model year with caution, ensuring thorough pre-purchase inspections and verified service history.

---

## Engine

### 3.6L V6 (Pentastar)

The 3.6-liter Pentastar V6 engine produces roughly 287 horsepower and 262 lb-ft of torque, providing smooth acceleration for highway cruising. While generally powerful, this introductory model year suffers from notable reports of coolant leaks, head gasket issues, and unexpected stalling.

**Engine Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may unexpectedly stall while driving, resulting in a loss of power and illuminated warning lights.
- **Solution:** Diagnostic testing of the electrical system, throttle body, or powertrain control module is typically required to isolate and replace failing sensors or components.

**Head Gasket Failure and Coolant Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Low coolant levels, overheating engine warnings, white smoke from the exhaust, or coolant mixing with engine oil.
- **Solution:** Requires professional mechanical teardown to replace damaged head gaskets, warped cylinder heads, or leaking cooling components.

### 3.6L V6 Plug-In Hybrid (PHEV)

The hybrid powertrain pairs the 3.6-liter V6 with dual electric motors and an internal battery pack to deliver efficient fuel economy alongside family hauling capability. Buyers should verify that all hybrid-specific recall updates and diagnostic checks have been successfully completed.

**Loss of Motive Power and Limp Mode**
- **Symptoms:** Sudden entry into limp mode accompanied by a red turtle warning light and loss of electric or gas propulsion while driving.
- **Solution:** Inspection and potential software updates, module replacements, or hybrid system repairs performed by a certified dealer technician.

## Transmission

### 9-Speed Automatic Transmission

This 9-speed automatic transmission routes power smoothly in optimal conditions, but early production units frequently draw owner complaints regarding rough engagement, erratic gear selection, and unexpected shifts.

**Harsh Shifting and Gear Select Faults**
- **Symptoms:** Transmission slams hard into gear, hesitates during acceleration, or randomly shifts into neutral or park unexpectedly.
- **Solution:** Perform transmission control module software updates, check fluid condition, or replace worn internal transmission components and shift actuators.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

The braking system offers solid stopping power under normal conditions, but mechanical wear in vacuum boosters can lead to sudden loss of power assist.

**Brake Booster Failure**
- **Symptoms:** A constant hissing sound from the brake pedal accompanied by a hard pedal feel and significant loss of braking assist.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the failed brake booster and inspection of the vacuum system components.

## Steering

### Steering Rack and Column

The electronic power steering system is designed to provide light, effortless maneuverability in tight parking lots. However, a recurring defect involving a sticky steering feel at highway speeds poses serious safety and handling concerns.

**Steering Wheel Sticking**
- **Symptoms:** The steering wheel catches or sticks at highway speeds around the 10, 11, 1, or 2 o'clock positions, requiring forceful correction.
- **Solution:** Inspection of the steering rack and pinion assembly, often requiring full replacement of the steering gear rack.

## Electrical

### Infotainment and Backup Camera

Electrical architecture supports comfort features and driver aids, though glitches in cabin displays and backup cameras are frequently reported by owners.

**Backup Camera and Display Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The infotainment display shows a blank blue, black, or purple screen, freezes, or fails to activate when shifting into reverse.
- **Solution:** Software flashing, wiring harness repair, or replacement of the rearview camera unit and display module.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V395000: Electrical System:Ignition:Anti-Theft:Control Module](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chrysler&model=Pacifica&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 114, "Theft Protection." If the MPR is engaged unintentionally, the vehicle could roll away striking and injuring a bystander or cause a crash.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will replace the MPR plug, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V332000: Electrical System:Wiring](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chrysler&model=Pacifica&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** These vehicles are being recalled to address a defect that could prevent the cruise control system from disengaging. If, when using cruise control, there is a short circuit within the vehicle's wiring, the driver may not be able to shut off the cruise control either by depressing the brake pedal or manually turning the system off once it has been engaged.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the software, and perform a software flash on the engine or powertrain control module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V348000: Electrical System:12V/24V/48V Battery](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chrysler&model=Pacifica&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** A loss of power steering assist or a stall can increase the risk of crash.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the battery ground surfaces, clean the contact surfaces as needed, and properly reassemble the ground connection, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V490000: Seat Belts:Rear/Other](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chrysler&model=Pacifica&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** If passengers occupying the third-row and/or second-row center seats cannot use a seat belt, there is an increased risk of injury in the event of a crash.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will replace the third-row and, if necessary, second-row center overhead seat belt stow bezel, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V482000: Power Train:Driveline:Differential Unit](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chrysler&model=Pacifica&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** A wheel wobble may cause a possible loss of control, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** VMI will notify owners, and dealers will replace the pinion brackets, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V342000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Foundation Components:Hoses, Lines/Piping, And Fittings](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chrysler&model=Pacifica&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 135, "Light Vehicle Brake Systems." The crossed rear brake lines will cause the rear anti-lock brake system to be ineffective, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Vantage Mobility will notify owners, and dealers will correct the rear brake line attachments, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V049000: Electrical System:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chrysler&model=Pacifica&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** The engine control software may become out of sync with the crankshaft position sensor, possibly resulting in an engine stall. An engine stall can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will update the engine control software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V542000: Seat Belts:Rear/Other](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chrysler&model=Pacifica&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** In certain passenger seating and vehicle situations, the second-row center seating position seat belt buckle could cause the left outboard seat belt to become unlatched. Inadvertent unlatching of the left outboard seat belt can increase the risk of injury in the event of a crash.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will install a shorter second-row seat belt buckle, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2017 Chrysler Pacifica is an attractive option for budget-conscious families looking for unrivaled interior flexibility, but its first-year status brings notable reliability risks. Prospective buyers should prioritize vehicles with documented dealer service records, paying close attention to transmission shift quality, steering rack behavior, and completed recall campaigns.
