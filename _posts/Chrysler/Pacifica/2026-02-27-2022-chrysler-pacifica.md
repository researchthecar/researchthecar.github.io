# 2022 Chrysler Pacifica Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2022 Chrysler Pacifica offers high-end family versatility, distinctive styling, and the flexibility of both standard gasoline and plug-in hybrid powertrains. However, used car buyers should approach this model year with caution due to several prominent owner-reported complaints concerning powertrain stability, electrical glitches, and interior seat hardware failures.

---

## Engine

### 3.6L Pentastar V6

The standard 3.6-liter Pentastar V6 engine produces 287 horsepower and 262 lb-ft of torque, providing smooth acceleration for daily family hauling. While mechanically robust, it is occasionally susceptible to electrical and sensor-related drivability hiccups.

**Engine Stalling and Loss of Motive Power**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers report sudden dashboard warnings such as "Stop Safely. Vehicle Will Shut Off Soon" followed by an unexpected loss of acceleration or complete engine stalling while driving.
- **Solution:** Have a certified mechanic or dealership inspect powertrain software updates, fuel systems, and electrical connectors to rule out known recall or wiring faults.

### 3.6L Pentastar V6 Plug-In Hybrid (PHEV)

The plug-in hybrid variant pairs the 3.6L V6 with dual electric motors and an internal battery pack, delivering an EPA-rated all-electric range and strong efficiency. Buyers should inspect hybrid service records carefully before purchasing.

**Hybrid Electrical and Battery Faults**
- **Symptoms:** Owners note sudden warnings, reduced propulsion mode (turtle mode), or charging restrictions tied to high-voltage battery concerns.
- **Solution:** Verify that all manufacturer hybrid system and high-voltage battery recalls have been fully performed by an authorized dealership.

## Transmission

### 9-Speed Automatic (Gasoline) / eFlite Electrically Variable (Hybrid)

The standard gasoline model utilizes a 9-speed automatic transmission, while the hybrid employs an eFlite electrically variable transmission. Proper shifting behavior is essential, as some owners have experienced abrupt limp-mode activations.

**Unexpected Limp Mode / Power Interruption**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicles may unexpectedly enter limp mode or drop speed on highways, frequently accompanied by transmission control module warnings.
- **Solution:** Check service history for transmission control module updates or wiring harness repairs; persistent issues may require professional diagnostic evaluation.

## Steering

### Rack and Pinion Steering

The electronic power steering system provides light, maneuverable handling suitable for a large minivan, though early component wear can lead to mechanical noises.

**Knocking Noises and Power Steering Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers experience an abnormal knocking sound from the front end when turning or notice sudden power steering failure messages leaving the wheel difficult to turn.
- **Solution:** Inspect the steering gear rack and electronic power steering components; worn assemblies may require replacement under warranty or service guidelines.

## Electrical

### Backup Camera and Display System

Advanced safety and driver-assistance features enhance daily parking and highway driving, though software glitches can occasionally disrupt rearview camera visibility.

**Intermittent Backup Camera Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The touchscreen displays a blue screen, distorts images, or shows a "camera unavailable" warning when shifting into reverse.
- **Solution:** Apply applicable radio and infotainment software updates or replace failing camera modules as specified by technical guidance.

## Interior

### Stow 'n Go Seats and Active Headrests

The Pacifica's interior is celebrated for its innovative second- and third-row Stow 'n Go folding seats, though the complex mechanical hinges and active headrest components are prone to occasional durability issues.

**Headrest and Seat Mechanism Breakage**
- **Symptoms:** Second-row seat headrests fail to return to an upright position or seatbacks lose their fixed locking position after utilizing the Stow 'n Go fold-flat feature.
- **Solution:** Inspect all seat latching mechanisms and headrest brackets during a test drive; broken plastic housings and internal springs often require component replacement.
- **Cost:** $1,100+

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V181000: Seats:Mid/Rear Assembly](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chrysler&model=Pacifica&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 225, "Child Restraint Anchorage Systems." An unsecured seat increases the risk of injury in a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and, if necessary, replace the second-row seat cushion frame, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V619000: Visibility:Windshield Wiper/Washer:Linkages](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chrysler&model=Pacifica&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** Improperly functioning wipers can reduce the driver's visibility in certain weather conditions, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will tighten the wiper arm nuts, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V010000: Electrical System:Wiring:Connectors/Plugs/Receptacles](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chrysler&model=Pacifica&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** An internal transmission wiring connector may short circuit, resulting in an unexpected engine shutdown. An unexpected engine shutdown can cause a loss of drive power, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the Power Inverter Module (PIM) software and, if necessary, update the instrument panel cluster (IPC) and the electronic control module (ECM) software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V436000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chrysler&model=Pacifica&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 111, "Rear Visibility." A rearview camera image that does not display decreases the driver's visibility, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the radio software or the software will be OTA, "Over-The-Air," free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V793000: Air Bags:Side/Window:Curtain](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chrysler&model=Pacifica&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** An improperly secured connector for the side curtain air bags may prevent the air bags from deploying. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard numbers 214, "Side Impact Protection" and 226, "Ejection Mitigation." An air bag that does not deploy during a crash increases the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will secure the connector, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V302000: Air Bags:Side/Window:Curtain](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chrysler&model=Pacifica&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 226, "Ejection Mitigation." An air bag with insufficient pressure retention increases the risk of ejection and injury during a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the side curtain air bags, as necessary free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V876000: Equipment Adaptive/Mobility:Wheelchair Restraints/Securement:Latch/Anchor:](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chrysler&model=Pacifica&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** The retractors may not lock, preventing the wheelchair from being properly secured. An unsecured wheelchair can move during transit, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 25V877000: Equipment Adaptive/Mobility:Wheelchair Restraints/Securement:Latch/Anchor:](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chrysler&model=Pacifica&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** The retractors may not lock, preventing the wheelchair from being properly secured. An unsecured wheelchair can move during transit, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the retractors, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2022 Chrysler Pacifica remains a feature-rich and comfortable choice for families, but prospective buyers should perform a thorough pre-purchase inspection. Pay close attention to powertrain operation, ensure all safety recalls have been addressed by a dealer, and test all seating mechanisms and electronics before finalizing your purchase.
