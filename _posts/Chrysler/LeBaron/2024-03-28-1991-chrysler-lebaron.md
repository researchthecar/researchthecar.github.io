**1991 Chrysler LeBaron Introduction**

The 1991 Chrysler LeBaron was a popular mid-size sedan that offered a comfortable ride and stylish exterior. It was available with a variety of engine and transmission options, including a 2.5L V6 with a 3-speed automatic transmission, a 3.0L V6 with a 4-speed automatic transmission, and a 3.3L V6 with a 4-speed automatic transmission. The LeBaron received praise for its spacious interior, smooth handling, and powerful engines. However, it also had several issues that could affect its reliability and overall performance.

**Engine**

* **2.5L V6**
    * **Valve cover gasket leak**
        * Description/Symptoms: Oil leaking from the top of the engine, visible as a brownish or blackish fluid on the valve covers or engine block
        * Solution: Replace the valve cover gaskets
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300+
        * Recalls: None Found
* **3.0L V6**
    * **Lower intake manifold gasket failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: Coolant or oil leaking from the front of the engine, poor engine performance, overheating
        * Solution: Replace the lower intake manifold gasket
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800+
        * Recalls: None Found
* **3.3L V6**
    * **Timing chain tensioner failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: Engine rattling or knocking noise, poor engine performance, timing chain jumping
        * Solution: Replace the timing chain tensioner
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

* **3-speed automatic transmission**
    * **Slipping gears**
        * Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears, delayed engagement, loss of power
        * Solution: Rebuild or replace the transmission
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
        * Recalls: None Found
* **4-speed automatic transmission**
    * **Overheating**
        * Description/Symptoms: Transmission slipping or shuddering, burnt transmission fluid smell, difficulty shifting
        * Solution: Flush the transmission fluid and replace the cooler lines
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Front struts**
    * **Leaking fluid**
        * Description/Symptoms: Squealing noise when turning, uneven tire wear, reduced shock absorption
        * Solution: Replace the front struts
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Alternator failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Battery dying prematurely, dim headlamps, electrical system problems
    * Solution: Replace the alternator
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Power window motors**
    * Description/Symptoms: Windows not rolling up or down, intermittent operation, burning smell
    * Solution: Replace the power window motors
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+ per window
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Dashboard cracking**
    * Description/Symptoms: Cracks appearing on the dashboard, especially around the defroster vents
    * Solution: Replace or repair the dashboard
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Carpet fading**
    * Description/Symptoms: Carpet losing color or becoming discolored
    * Solution: Dye or replace the carpet
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+ depending on the carpeting material and installation

**Summary**

The 1991 Chrysler LeBaron had a range of issues that could affect its reliability and overall performance, particularly with the engine, transmission, and electrical systems. The severity of these issues could vary depending on the specific engine and transmission option, ranging from minor cosmetic problems to major mechanical failures. The estimated cost to fix these issues can be significant, so it's important to consider the potential repair costs before buying a used LeBaron.
