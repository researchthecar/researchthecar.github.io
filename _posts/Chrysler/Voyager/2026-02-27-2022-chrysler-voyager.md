# 2022 Chrysler Voyager Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

Shopping for a modern family minivan can be daunting, but the 2022 Chrysler Voyager offers a straightforward and budget-friendly passenger-hauling option. Built as a more affordable, fleet-and-retail-oriented sibling to the Pacifica, it serves everyday driving needs with a practical layout. While overall owner feedback is relatively light, used car buyers should still carefully evaluate specific mechanical and electronic systems prior to purchase.

---

## Engine

### 3.6L V6 Engine

This 3.6-liter Pentastar V6 engine produces approximately 287 horsepower and 262 lb-ft of torque, providing confident acceleration for highway merging and daily commuting. It is generally regarded as a robust and dependable powerplant for Chrysler's modern minivan lineup.

## Transmission

### 9-Speed Automatic Transmission

Paired exclusively with front-wheel drive, this 9-speed automatic transmission is designed to optimize fuel economy on long highway stretches. Buyers should test drive the vehicle thoroughly to ensure smooth shifting behavior across all gears.

## Electrical

### Liftgate and Convenience Electronics

Electrical accessories govern comfort and convenience features throughout the cabin, requiring proper functioning of body control modules.

**Power Liftgate Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have reported electrical glitches affecting convenience features like the rear power liftgate mechanism, requiring multiple dealership visits.
- **Solution:** Have a certified technician diagnose the power liftgate module, wiring harness, and actuators to determine if a component replacement or software reset is necessary.

## Exterior

### Door Latches and Linkages

Interior mechanisms include door latches, power windows, and seating adjustments that ensure daily passenger accessibility and comfort.

**Passenger Door Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Occupants may experience doors failing to open properly from the inside or outside, occasionally trapping passengers inside the vehicle.
- **Solution:** Inspect door latches, electronic release mechanisms, and internal door linkages for binding or mechanical failure, replacing faulty assemblies as needed.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V619000: Visibility:Windshield Wiper/Washer:Linkages](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chrysler&model=Voyager&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** Improperly functioning wipers can reduce the driver's visibility in certain weather conditions, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will tighten the wiper arm nuts, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V436000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chrysler&model=Voyager&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 111, "Rear Visibility." A rearview camera image that does not display decreases the driver's visibility, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the radio software or the software will be OTA, "Over-The-Air," free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V302000: Air Bags:Side/Window:Curtain](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chrysler&model=Voyager&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 226, "Ejection Mitigation." An air bag with insufficient pressure retention increases the risk of ejection and injury during a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the side curtain air bags, as necessary free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V876000: Equipment Adaptive/Mobility:Wheelchair Restraints/Securement:Latch/Anchor:](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chrysler&model=Voyager&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** The retractors may not lock, preventing the wheelchair from being properly secured. An unsecured wheelchair can move during transit, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 26V189000: Air Bags:Side/Window:Curtain](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chrysler&model=Voyager&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 226, "Ejection Mitigation." Air bags with insufficient pressure retention increase the risk of ejection and injury during a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the side curtain air bags, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2022 Chrysler Voyager remains a practical and functional choice for budget-conscious families looking for modern minivan utility. While isolated owner reports point to minor electrical and door latch concerns, the core powertrain remains proven. Prospective buyers should perform a comprehensive pre-purchase inspection focusing on electronics and convenience features to ensure a trouble-free ownership experience.
