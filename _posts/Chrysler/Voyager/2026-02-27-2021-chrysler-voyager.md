# 2021 Chrysler Voyager Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2021 Chrysler Voyager, buyers will find a practical and budget-friendly minivan designed primarily for family transport. While it shares much of its platform and utility with the Pacifica, prospective owners should review common owner-reported concerns regarding steering feel, electrical accessories, and occasional powertrain hiccups. Keeping these potential problem areas in mind during a test drive can help ensure a reliable purchase.

---

## Engine

### 3.6L V6 Engine

Equipped with a 3.6-liter Pentastar V6 producing approximately 287 horsepower and 262 lb-ft of torque, this engine provides capable daily performance for a minivan. Owner reports indicate standard operation overall, though isolated complaints mention rough idling or throttle control warnings.

## Transmission

### 9-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 9-speed automatic transmission routes power smoothly to the front wheels under normal driving conditions. It pairs decently with the V6 powerplant, though buyers should thoroughly test-shift the vehicle across various speeds.

## Steering

### Steering Rack and Mechanism

**Steering Wheel Sticking and Stiffness**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers note the steering wheel sticking at certain angles (such as 11 and 1 o'clock) when exiting turns or becoming unusually stiff and difficult to turn, requiring significant physical effort.
- **Solution:** Inspection of tie rods, the steering intermediate shaft, or replacement of the steering rack and pinion assembly may be required.

## Electrical

### Electrical and Infotainment System

Electrical complaints center mostly around accessory display screens, camera feeds, and integrated stop-start functions.

**Backup Camera and Infotainment Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** The backup camera display may intermittently fail, turn solid pink, or display frozen historical images, while smartphone integration like Apple CarPlay can occasionally fail to connect.
- **Solution:** Software updates, camera module replacement, or wiring harness inspections are typically needed to restore proper operation.

**Auto-Stop and Acceleration Interruption**
- **Symptoms:** Following rapid acceleration, drivers may experience electrical behaviors that prevent proper acceleration and cause the automatic start-stop system to fail.
- **Solution:** Diagnose battery health, charging system output, and powertrain control module software for faults.

## Exterior

### Stow 'n Go Seating

Cabin features are generally user-friendly, though mechanical latches on folding seats deserve a close inspection during a used vehicle walkaround.

**Second-Row Headrest Locking Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Second-row seat headrests may fail to fold upright and lock securely back into a fixed position after being stowed, rendering the seat unsafe or unusable for passengers.
- **Solution:** Inspect the internal seat latch mechanisms and replace damaged headrest or hinge hardware.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V181000: Seats:Mid/Rear Assembly](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chrysler&model=Voyager&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 225, "Child Restraint Anchorage Systems." An unsecured seat increases the risk of injury in a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and, if necessary, replace the second-row seat cushion frame, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V793000: Air Bags:Side/Window:Curtain](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chrysler&model=Voyager&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** An improperly secured connector for the side curtain air bags may prevent the air bags from deploying. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard numbers 214, "Side Impact Protection" and 226, "Ejection Mitigation." An air bag that does not deploy during a crash increases the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will secure the connector, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2021 Chrysler Voyager offers strong utility and a comfortable ride for families shopping on a budget, but buyers should perform a thorough inspection prior to purchase. Pay close attention to steering response during test drives to check for any binding or stiffness, and verify that all interior seats, backup cameras, and infotainment features function correctly.
