# 2020 Chrysler Voyager Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2020 Chrysler Voyager, buyers will find a budget-friendly minivan designed primarily for practical family hauling. While it shares much of its DNA and capability with the Pacifica, the Voyager acts as the more value-oriented trim tier in the lineup. As with any modern vehicle packed with electronics and start-stop systems, reviewing potential owner-reported trouble spots can help you perform a more thorough pre-purchase inspection.

---

## Engine

### 3.6L V6 287-hp Engine

The standard 3.6-liter Pentastar V6 produces 287 horsepower and 262 lb-ft of torque, offering smooth and capable power for highway merging and city driving. Overall, this engine is widely used across the manufacturer's lineup and is generally considered robust, though routine maintenance is essential.

**Engine Stalling and Rough Running**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have reported instances where the engine begins running very roughly, attempts to stall, illuminates the check engine and stop/start warning lights, and struggles to accelerate.
- **Solution:** Diagnostic scanning is required to check for fault codes related to the electronic throttle body, fuel delivery, or ignition systems.

## Transmission

### 9-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 9-speed automatic transmission pairs with the V6 engine to optimize highway fuel efficiency and power delivery. It operates smoothly under normal conditions, though prospective buyers should pay attention to shift quality during a test drive.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

Braking performance is critical for a family vehicle; ensure the brake system undergoes a professional inspection before purchase.

**Brake Pedal Responsiveness**
- **Symptoms:** An owner noted an inability to slow the vehicle effectively despite fully depressing the brake pedal during an emergency situation.
- **Solution:** Thoroughly inspect the hydraulic brake system, brake booster, pads, and rotors for any underlying mechanical or hydraulic failures.

## Steering

### Power Steering System

Issues related to electric power steering assist and mechanical steering feel have been highlighted by some owners.

**Intermittent Power Steering Loss**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers experience a sudden loss of power steering assist while driving or turning, which can make the vehicle very difficult to control. Restarting the engine sometimes temporarily restores function.
- **Solution:** Inspect the electric power steering system, charging system, and auxiliary battery health, as low voltage can trigger electrical component dropouts.

**Steering Wheel Sticking**
- **Symptoms:** The steering wheel may temporarily stick or feel like it locks when executing slow turns or gradual curves to the right, requiring extra effort to return to center.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified mechanic inspect the steering column, intermediate shaft, and rack-and-pinion assembly for binding or mechanical wear.

## Exterior

### Seating Mechanisms

Interior convenience features generally hold up well, but high-use mechanical components like seat adjusters warrant careful checking.

**Second-Row Seat Latch Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The second-row bucket seat may fail to hold its recline position or refuse to latch securely into the floor base, causing the seat to slide freely.
- **Solution:** Inspect and replace broken seat-back recline mechanisms or floor latch components as necessary.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V793000: Air Bags:Side/Window:Curtain](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chrysler&model=Voyager&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** An improperly secured connector for the side curtain air bags may prevent the air bags from deploying. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard numbers 214, "Side Impact Protection" and 226, "Ejection Mitigation." An air bag that does not deploy during a crash increases the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will secure the connector, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2020 Chrysler Voyager offers practical family transportation with ample power from its V6 engine. While most owners enjoy trouble-free driving, used-car shoppers should pay close attention during their test drive to steering feel, brake responsiveness, and the operation of interior seating mechanisms. Conducting a pre-purchase inspection remains the best way to catch any lingering electrical or mechanical quirks before finalizing your purchase.
