**Introduction**

The 1992 Chrysler Daytona was a sporty coupe that offered a blend of performance and comfort. It was available with three engine options: a 2.5L V6, a 3.0L V6, and a 3.5L V6. The 2.5L V6 produced 160 horsepower and 170 lb-ft of torque, while the 3.0L V6 generated 193 horsepower and 205 lb-ft of torque. The top-of-the-line 3.5L V6 delivered 214 horsepower and 221 lb-ft of torque. Transmission options included a 5-speed manual, a 4-speed automatic, and a 3-speed automatic.

Despite its popularity, the 1992 Chrysler Daytona had several issues that plagued owners. Here's a comprehensive list of the most common problems:

**Engine**

**2.5L V6**

* **Intake manifold gasket failure:** Symptoms include coolant leaks, overheating, and rough idling. Solution: Replace the intake manifold gasket. Estimated cost: $200 - $500+

**3.0L V6**

* **No issues found**

**3.5L V6**

* **Head gasket failure:** Symptoms include coolant leaks, overheating, and white smoke from the exhaust. Solution: Replace the head gasket. Estimated cost: $1,000 - $1,500+
* **Timing belt failure:** Symptoms include loss of engine power and a rattling noise from the engine. Solution: Replace the timing belt. Estimated cost: $500 - $700+

**Transmission**

**5-Speed Manual**

* **No issues found**

**4-Speed Automatic**

* **Overdrive slipping:** Symptoms include delayed or no engagement into overdrive gear. Solution: Replace the overdrive solenoid. Estimated cost: $200 - $400+

**3-Speed Automatic**

* **No issues found**

**Suspension**

* **Rear suspension noise:** Symptoms include clunking or squeaking noises when driving over bumps. Solution: Lubricate or replace the rear suspension bushings. Estimated cost: $100 - $200+

**Electrical**

* **Alternator failure:** Symptoms include flickering lights, dimming instruments, and battery discharge. Solution: Replace the alternator. Estimated cost: $300 - $500+
* **Starter failure:** Symptoms include difficulty starting the vehicle or a grinding noise when trying to start. Solution: Replace the starter. Estimated cost: $200 - $300+

**Exterior**

* **Door lock malfunctions:** Symptoms include difficulty locking or unlocking the doors. Solution: Replace the door lock actuators. Estimated cost: $100 - $300+ per door

**Interior**

* **Power window failure:** Symptoms include windows that do not work or operate slowly. Solution: Replace the power window motor or switch. Estimated cost: $200 - $400+ per window

**Recalls**

* **None found**

**Summary**

The 1992 Chrysler Daytona experienced a range of issues that could be both costly and frustrating for owners. The engine and transmission problems were the most severe, with potential repairs costing over $1,000. While the suspension, electrical, exterior, and interior issues were generally less expensive to fix, they still added to the overall inconvenience and cost of owning this vehicle.
