# 2002 Chrysler Concorde

### Overview
The 2002 Chrysler Concorde, a full-size sedan, offered a comfortable ride and a spacious interior, appealing to buyers seeking American-style cruising. It was available with a choice of V6 engines, paired with an automatic transmission. While generally considered a decent value for its segment, like many vehicles of its era, it has specific areas where owners have reported issues. This analysis focuses on common mechanical and electrical problems that go beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential reliability concerns for prospective owners or those maintaining the vehicle.

---


## Engine

### 2.7L V6
**Oil Sludge Buildup**
- **Symptoms:** Engine knocking, overheating, loss of power, eventual engine seizure.
- **Solution:** Regular oil changes with high-quality synthetic oil, engine flushes, and timely replacement of PCV valve.
- **Cost:** $500 - $2000 (depending on severity and if engine rebuild is needed)

**Timing Chain Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Rattling noise from the front of the engine, especially on startup; check engine light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of timing chain, tensioners, and guides.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1500


### 3.2L V6
**Oil Leaks (Valve Covers, Oil Pan)**
- **Symptoms:** Visible oil leaks under the vehicle, burning oil smell, low oil level.
- **Solution:** Replacement of valve cover gaskets and/or oil pan gasket.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600


### 3.5L V6
**No Major Issues**
- **Symptoms:** No widespread issues reported.
- **Solution:** No widespread issues reported.
- **Cost:** No widespread issues reported.


## Transmission

### 42LE Automatic Transmission
**Harsh Shifting / Slipping**
- **Symptoms:** Jerky gear changes, delayed engagement, slipping between gears.
- **Solution:** Transmission fluid and filter change, potential internal repairs or rebuild.
- **Cost:** $400 - $2500 (depending on repair scope)

**Overheating**
- **Symptoms:** Transmission fluid smells burnt, delayed shifting, especially under load.
- **Solution:** Transmission cooler flush and potentially an auxiliary cooler installation.
- **Cost:** $300 - $700


## Suspension

### Standard Chassis
**Worn Ball Joints and Tie Rod Ends**
- **Symptoms:** Clunking noises over bumps, vague steering, uneven tire wear.
- **Solution:** Replacement of worn ball joints and tie rod ends.
- **Cost:** $400 - $800

**Sagging Springs**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle sits lower than normal, poor handling, uneven tire wear.
- **Solution:** Replacement of front or rear springs.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600


## Electrical

### Body Control Module (BCM)
**Intermittent Electrical Failures**
- **Symptoms:** Power windows, locks, interior lights, or dashboard lights malfunctioning intermittently.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and potential replacement or reprogramming of the BCM.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1000


### Alternator
**Premature Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Dimming headlights, battery warning light, difficulty starting.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the alternator.
- **Cost:** $400 - $700


## Exterior

### Body Panels and Paint
**Clear Coat Peeling**
- **Symptoms:** Fading paint, peeling clear coat, especially on horizontal surfaces.
- **Solution:** Repainting affected areas.
- **Cost:** $500 - $2000+ (depending on extent)


### Headlight Lenses
**Clouding / Hazing**
- **Symptoms:** Yellowed or foggy headlight lenses, reduced light output.
- **Solution:** Headlight restoration kit or replacement of headlight assemblies.
- **Cost:** $50 - $300


## Interior

### HVAC System
**Blend Door Actuator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Inability to control temperature (stuck on hot or cold), air only coming from certain vents.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the blend door actuator(s).
- **Cost:** $200 - $500


### Power Window Motors
**Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Power windows not operating.
- **Solution:** Replacement of individual power window motors.
- **Cost:** $200 - $400 per window


--- 
### Summary Verdict
The 2002 Chrysler Concorde can be a comfortable and affordable large sedan, but potential buyers should be aware of common issues. The 2.7L V6 engine is particularly prone to oil sludge and timing chain problems, making diligent maintenance crucial. Transmission issues like harsh shifting and overheating are also reported. On the electrical side, the Body Control Module can cause various gremlins, and alternators may fail prematurely. Exterior paint and interior components like HVAC actuators and power window motors are also areas that may require attention. While not a universally problematic vehicle, addressing these known weak points proactively can significantly improve the ownership experience.
