# 2023 Rivian R1T Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2023 Rivian R1T is an innovative, high-performance electric truck packed with advanced technology and impressive capability. As a relatively new entry from an emerging EV manufacturer, buyers should approach used models with a balanced perspective, keeping an eye on early-production electronic glitches, HVAC performance, and critical fastener inspections.

---

## Engine

### Dual-Motor Electric Powertrain

The standard dual-motor electric setup delivers robust horsepower and instantaneous torque to all four wheels. Buyers generally experience strong performance, though early production software and high-voltage electrical management systems require careful monitoring.

## Transmission

### Quad-Motor Electric Powertrain

The flagship quad-motor configuration utilizes an independent electric motor for each wheel, generating over 800 horsepower for extraordinary acceleration and off-road control. While fiercely capable, its complex integrated electronics can occasionally trigger severe propulsion warnings.

**High-Voltage System and Propulsion Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may experience sudden critical battery warnings, loss of regenerative braking, or unexpected entry into limited performance 'turtle mode' resulting in a loss of motive power.
- **Solution:** Have the vehicle's high-voltage battery management software and powertrain modules inspected and updated by a certified Rivian service center.

### Single-Speed Reduction Gearbox

Equipped with direct-drive single-speed gearboxes for each motor unit, eliminating traditional multi-speed shifting. This layout is mechanically straightforward and robust, though dependent on secure axle and hub fasteners.

## Suspension

### Axles and Hubs

The sophisticated independent air suspension and drive axles provide incredible ride adjustment and articulation. However, owners have reported concerns regarding improperly torqued hub and CV axle fasteners.

**Loose CV Axle or Hub Bolts**
- **Symptoms:** Unusual metal-on-metal scraping noises or loose axle components discovered during routine maintenance or tire rotations.
- **Solution:** Inspect and retorque axle and hub bolts to factory specifications; replace damaged hardware if necessary.

## Electrical

### Infotainment and Climate Controls

Heavy reliance on central touchscreens and integrated software governs nearly all vehicle functions. While feature-rich, OTA updates and HVAC climate actuators can occasionally introduce operational glitches.

**Defrost System Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** The climate control system blows cool air instead of warm air onto the windshield, causing ice buildup and reduced visibility while driving.
- **Solution:** Perform software resets or service center diagnostics to resolve HVAC valve and climate software logic errors.

## Safety

### Occupant Detection Sensor

Advanced safety restraints and airbag systems protect occupants, though sensitive weight-classification sensors in the front passenger seat can occasionally misidentify adult passengers.

**Passenger Airbag Deactivation**
- **Symptoms:** The 'Passenger Airbag Off' warning light illuminates intermittently while an adult passenger is properly seated, disabling the airbag incorrectly.
- **Solution:** Have the seat weight sensor calibration and occupant detection module evaluated and recalibrated by a technician.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V783000: Visibility:Defroster/Defogger/Hvac System](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Rivian&model=R1T&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** The inability to operate the windshield defrosting and defogging system can decrease the driver's visibility, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 24V367000: Exterior Lighting:Headlights](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Rivian&model=R1T&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 108, "Lamp, Reflective Devices and Associated Equipment." Improperly aimed headlights can result in insufficient illumination of the road, decreasing the driver's visibility and increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Rivian will inspect and adjust the headlight alignment as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V408000: Air Bags:Critical Fasteners](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Rivian&model=R1T&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** Damaged pillar trim panel clips can result in improper side curtain air bag deployment, increasing the risk of injury during a crash.
- **What to do:** Rivian will replace the pillar trim panels, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V458000: Equipment:Other:Labels](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Rivian&model=R1T&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 110, "Tire Selection and Rims." A vehicle with an incorrect maximum capacity weight value may be overloaded, which can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 25V370000: Seat Belts:Front:Anchorage](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Rivian&model=R1T&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** The D-ring bolts for one or both front seat belt anchorage assemblies may not have been properly installed. An improperly secured seat belt may not restrain an occupant as intended, increasing the risk of injury during a crash.
- **What to do:** Rivian will inspect and properly secure the seat belt anchorage assemblies as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V003000: Suspension:Critical Fasteners](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Rivian&model=R1T&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** The toe link joint may separate, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Rivian will replace the rear toe link bolts, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V009000: Seat Belts:Critical Fasteners](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Rivian&model=R1T&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** An improperly secured seat belt retractor may not adequately restrain the seat occupant, increasing the risk of injury during a crash.
- **What to do:** Rivian will inspect and tighten the bolts as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2023 Rivian R1T offers groundbreaking electric performance, utility, and capability that few trucks can match. Prospective buyers should thoroughly review service history, verify that all software updates and fastener recall campaigns have been completed, and test all climate, battery, and safety systems prior to purchase.
