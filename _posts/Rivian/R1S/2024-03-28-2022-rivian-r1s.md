**2022 Rivian R1S**

The Rivian R1S is an all-electric SUV with a quad-motor setup that produces up to 835 horsepower and 908 lb-ft of torque. It has a range of up to 314 miles and can tow up to 7,700 pounds. The R1S is praised for its off-road capability, spacious interior, and advanced technology. However, it has also been criticized for its high price, limited availability, and some quality issues.

**Electrical**

**12-Volt Battery Failure**
Description/Symptoms: The 12-volt battery may fail, causing the vehicle to lose power and become inoperable. The issue is caused by a defective battery module.
Solution: The battery module must be replaced.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $700+
Recalls: None Found

**High-Voltage Battery Degradation**
Description/Symptoms: The high-voltage battery may experience premature degradation, reducing the vehicle's range and performance. The issue is caused by a faulty battery management system.
Solution: The battery management system must be updated or replaced.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $5,000 - $10,000+
Recalls: None Found

**No Issues Found**

**Interior**

**Door Panel Squeaks and Rattles**
Description/Symptoms: The door panels may squeak and rattle when the vehicle is moving. The issue is caused by loose or misaligned components.
Solution: The door panels must be adjusted or replaced.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
Recalls: None Found

**Center Console Wobble**
Description/Symptoms: The center console may wobble when the vehicle is in motion. The issue is caused by a faulty mounting bracket.
Solution: The mounting bracket must be replaced.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $700+
Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2022 Rivian R1S has some electrical and interior quality issues that can be frustrating for owners. However, the vehicle's overall reliability is still good, and most issues are relatively inexpensive to fix.
