# 2023 Rivian R1S Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2023 Rivian R1S is an advanced, fully electric adventure SUV offering exceptional performance and utility. As an early-production model from a newer automaker, buyers should be aware of several reported electrical, suspension, and driver-assistance quirks documented by owners.

---

## Engine

### Dual-Motor Electric Powertrain

The standard dual-motor setup delivers robust all-wheel-drive performance with high horsepower and instantaneous torque. As a modern electric drive unit, it generally requires very little routine maintenance compared to internal combustion engines.

### Quad-Motor Electric Powertrain

The quad-motor configuration provides independent power to each wheel for supreme traction and extreme performance capabilities. This sophisticated system is powerful and swift, though it introduces complex electronic management systems.

## Transmission

### Single-Speed Direct-Drive Transmission

Equipped with fixed-ratio gearboxes for each motor unit, delivering seamless power delivery without traditional shifting. These direct-drive units are mechanically simple and robust.

## Suspension

### Suspension and Toe Links

The R1S utilizes an advanced independent air suspension system designed for adjustable ride height and comfort. Certain owners have experienced hardware stresses in the rear suspension linkage.

**Rear Toe Link or Suspension Component Separation**
- **Symptoms:** Clunking or grinding noises from the rear, unexpected vehicle pulling, or wheel misalignment caused by component fatigue or fastener failure.
- **Solution:** Inspect rear suspension components and ensure all recalls or service campaigns regarding toe links and hardware are addressed.

## Brakes

### Regenerative and Service Brakes

The braking system blends regenerative deceleration with traditional friction brakes for stopping power. Changes in battery parameters can occasionally alter regenerative braking feel.

**Regenerative Braking Reduction**
- **Symptoms:** Regenerative braking temporarily reduces or cuts out, often indicated by a greyed-out regen bar, causing the vehicle to coast unexpectedly.
- **Solution:** Be prepared to apply the mechanical brake pedal manually if regenerative braking availability changes due to battery temperature or state of charge.

## Steering

### Steering Assembly and Lane Assist

Electric power steering provides adjustable feedback, though automated driver-assistance features can occasionally introduce erratic steering corrections.

**Lane Keep Assist High-Speed Lurches**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may uncommandedly pull or steer abruptly toward lane markers or off-center while lane-keeping aids are active at highway speeds.
- **Solution:** Have the steering calibration, software versions, and mechanical rack fasteners thoroughly inspected by a service professional.

## Electrical

### Electrical System and OTA Updates

The vehicle relies heavily on integrated digital screens and frequent software updates to manage most comfort and driving controls.

**Electrical System Faults and Screen Blackouts**
- **Symptoms:** Warning messages regarding electrical faults or battery status appear on the dash, and over-the-air software updates can occasionally cause center display blackouts.
- **Solution:** Perform a system reboot or visit a service center to clear persistent high-voltage or low-voltage electrical fault codes.

## Safety

### Air Bag and Restraints

The occupant classification system manages front passenger airbag deployment based on weight sensing. Owners have reported intermittent detection issues with adult passengers.

**Passenger Airbag Sensor Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** The passenger airbag indicator incorrectly displays 'airbag off' or fails to detect adult passengers properly seated in the front passenger seat.
- **Solution:** Have the seat weight sensors and occupant detection module inspected by a qualified service center.

### Power Seats

Power-adjustable interior seating provides comfort and convenience features, though motorized adjustments lack obstruction sensors in certain configurations.

**Seat Memory Adjustment Obstruction**
- **Symptoms:** Power-adjustable seats move automatically into preset positions without detecting child safety seats or physical objects placed behind them.
- **Solution:** Exercise caution when utilizing seat memory presets if rear-facing child seats or bulky cargo are installed immediately behind the front seats.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V159000: Air Bags:Side/Window:Curtain](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Rivian&model=R1S&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** Improperly secured side curtain air bags may not perform as intended, increasing the risk of injury during a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will secure or replace the side curtain air bags as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V233000: Exterior Lighting:Back Up Lights](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Rivian&model=R1S&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** The reverse light beam may not be visible from certain angles. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 108, "Lamps, Reflective Devices, and Associated Equipment." Reduced visibility of the reverse lights increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Rivian will repair or replace the reverse lights, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V783000: Visibility:Defroster/Defogger/Hvac System](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Rivian&model=R1S&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** The inability to operate the windshield defrosting and defogging system can decrease the driver's visibility, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 24V367000: Exterior Lighting:Headlights](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Rivian&model=R1S&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 108, "Lamp, Reflective Devices and Associated Equipment." Improperly aimed headlights can result in insufficient illumination of the road, decreasing the driver's visibility and increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Rivian will inspect and adjust the headlight alignment as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V408000: Air Bags:Critical Fasteners](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Rivian&model=R1S&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** Damaged pillar trim panel clips can result in improper side curtain air bag deployment, increasing the risk of injury during a crash.
- **What to do:** Rivian will replace the pillar trim panels, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V458000: Equipment:Other:Labels](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Rivian&model=R1S&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 110, "Tire Selection and Rims." A vehicle with an incorrect maximum capacity weight value may be overloaded, which can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 25V370000: Seat Belts:Front:Anchorage](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Rivian&model=R1S&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** The D-ring bolts for one or both front seat belt anchorage assemblies may not have been properly installed. An improperly secured seat belt may not restrain an occupant as intended, increasing the risk of injury during a crash.
- **What to do:** Rivian will inspect and properly secure the seat belt anchorage assemblies as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V003000: Suspension:Critical Fasteners](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Rivian&model=R1S&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** The toe link joint may separate, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Rivian will replace the rear toe link bolts, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2023 Rivian R1S is an innovative and capable electric SUV, but prospective used-car buyers should inspect potential examples thoroughly. Pay close attention to vehicle service history regarding suspension recalls, passenger sensor operation, and electrical updates to ensure a smooth ownership experience.
