# 2022 Rivian R1S Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a pre-owned Rivian R1S, understanding its unique architecture and owner-reported tendencies can help guide your purchase. As an all-electric adventure SUV, it offers high-performance dual- or quad-motor setups paired with a single-speed direct-drive transmission. While owner complaints in early production models highlight occasional cabin and safety sensor quirks, evaluating each vehicle's service history remains an important step during your search.

---

## Engine

### Dual-Motor Electric Powertrain

This setup features dual electric motors delivering robust horsepower and instant torque for all-wheel-drive capability. It provides strong everyday performance with fewer moving parts than a traditional internal combustion engine.

### Quad-Motor Electric Powertrain

This high-performance quad-motor configuration places an independent motor at each wheel, generating massive horsepower and exceptional torque vectoring. It offers incredible off-road agility and brisk acceleration for enthusiasts.

## Transmission

### Single-Speed Direct-Drive Transmission

Utilizing a fixed-gear, single-speed reduction unit for each motor layout, this system eliminates traditional gear shifting entirely. It delivers seamless power delivery and high reliability with minimal required maintenance.

## Interior

### Third-Row Seat Crossmember

The third-row seating structure and mounting hardware provide folding capability and passenger accommodation in the rear cabin.

**Crossmember Bolt Disconnection**
- **Symptoms:** On early production vehicles, third-row seat crossmember bolts may have been mis-installed during factory assembly, causing threaded inserts to disconnect.
- **Solution:** Inspect the third-row seating frame anchors and have service technicians properly re-secure or replace disconnected fasteners.

## Safety

### Passenger Seat Occupant Sensor

The occupant detection system manages front passenger airbag deployment based on weight and seating posture.

**Passenger Airbag Deactivation**
- **Symptoms:** Owners report that the front passenger seat occasionally fails to detect seated occupants of sufficient weight, incorrectly setting the passenger airbag status to off.
- **Solution:** Have a certified service center inspect the seat sensor calibration, wiring harness, or weight-sensing mat.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V641000: Seat Belts:Front:Anchorage](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Rivian&model=R1S&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** An improperly secured seat belt anchor may not adequately restrain the seat occupant during a crash, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Rivian will inspect the front seat belt assemblies and, as necessary, properly secure the bolts, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V744000: Suspension:Front:Control Arm:Upper Arm](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Rivian&model=R1S&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** A loose steering knuckle fastener could separate, causing a loss of vehicle control and increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and, as necessary, tighten the steering knuckle fastener, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V109000: Seat Belts:Front:Retractor](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Rivian&model=R1S&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** A faulty automatic locking retractor (ALR) sensor in the front passenger seat belt system may incorrectly report as ON when it is actually OFF, causing the suppression or improper deployment of the air bag. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 208, "Occupant Crash Protection." Failure.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the front passenger seat belt system components as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V159000: Air Bags:Side/Window:Curtain](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Rivian&model=R1S&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** Improperly secured side curtain air bags may not perform as intended, increasing the risk of injury during a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will secure or replace the side curtain air bags as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V233000: Exterior Lighting:Back Up Lights](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Rivian&model=R1S&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** The reverse light beam may not be visible from certain angles. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 108, "Lamps, Reflective Devices, and Associated Equipment." Reduced visibility of the reverse lights increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Rivian will repair or replace the reverse lights, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V783000: Visibility:Defroster/Defogger/Hvac System](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Rivian&model=R1S&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** The inability to operate the windshield defrosting and defogging system can decrease the driver's visibility, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 23V883000: Vehicle Speed Control:Accelerator Pedal](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Rivian&model=R1S&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** The accelerator pedal may not detect when the driver releases the pedal, failing to activate "auto-hold" or "park" as intended. Failure to activate "auto-hold" or "park" can result in unintended vehicle movement, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 24V408000: Air Bags:Critical Fasteners](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Rivian&model=R1S&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** Damaged pillar trim panel clips can result in improper side curtain air bag deployment, increasing the risk of injury during a crash.
- **What to do:** Rivian will replace the pillar trim panels, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.
