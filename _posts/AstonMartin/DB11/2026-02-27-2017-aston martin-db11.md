# 2017 Aston Martin DB11

### Overview
The 2017 Aston Martin DB11, a grand tourer known for its potent performance and luxurious design, generally presents a strong reliability record for its class. While exotic vehicles inherently require more specialized maintenance and can incur higher repair costs than mainstream cars, the DB11 demonstrates a commendable level of engineering robustness. Owners can expect a thrilling driving experience, but should be prepared for the associated costs of ownership, particularly concerning specialized components and potential electronic intricacies. This analysis focuses on documented, non-wear-and-tear related issues.

### Summary Verdict
The 2017 Aston Martin DB11 exhibits a generally good reliability profile for a high-performance luxury vehicle. The core mechanical components, including the V12 and V8 engines and the ZF automatic transmission, are robust and have not shown widespread failure patterns. The primary areas where minor issues have been noted are within the electrical system, specifically concerning the infotainment interface and occasional sensor malfunctions, which are not uncommon in complex modern vehicles. Exterior and interior components, as well as the suspension, are typically reliable. Owners should factor in the higher cost of specialized maintenance and parts inherent to exotic car ownership, but major mechanical concerns are not a prevalent issue for this model year.
