Here's a breakdown of common issues and complaints related to the 2023 Aston Martin DBX707, categorized for clarity and accuracy.

The 2023 Aston Martin DBX707 is a high-performance SUV boasting a handcrafted 4.0-liter twin-turbocharged V8 engine producing 697 horsepower and 663 lb-ft of torque, paired exclusively with a 9-speed automatic transmission. The DBX707 is celebrated for its stunning design, luxurious interior, and exhilarating performance, making it a standout in the high-end SUV market. However, like any vehicle, it's not without its potential drawbacks. Common praises include the powerful engine, sharp handling for an SUV, and the lavish interior. Common dislikes revolve around the high price tag, potential reliability concerns, and limited rear passenger space compared to some competitors.

**Engine**

**4.0L Twin-Turbocharged V8**
*   **Turbocharger Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Whining noise from the engine bay, loss of power, check engine light illuminated. Potential causes include oil starvation to the turbocharger bearings or a manufacturing defect.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the faulty turbocharger(s).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $5,000 - $10,000+ (includes parts and labor for replacing one or both turbos)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Oil Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Oil spots under the vehicle, burning oil smell, low oil level warning light. Common leak points include valve cover gaskets, oil pan gasket, and turbocharger oil lines.
    *   Solution: Replacing the leaking gaskets or seals.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,500+ (depending on the location and complexity of the leak repair)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**9-Speed Automatic**

*   **Harsh Shifting**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rough or jerky shifts between gears, especially during low-speed acceleration or deceleration. Could be caused by low transmission fluid levels, or issues with the mechatronic unit.
    *   Solution: Transmission software update to be performed by a certified technician. In extreme cases, replacement of the transmission.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150-$300 for software update. $8,000+ for a replacement transmission.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Delayed Engagement**
    *   Description/Symptoms: A noticeable delay between shifting into drive or reverse and the vehicle actually moving.
    *   Solution: Software update or transmission replacement
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150-$300 for software update. $8,000+ for a replacement transmission.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Air Suspension Malfunction**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Vehicle sitting unevenly, suspension warning light illuminated, ride height adjustment not working. Possible causes include a faulty air compressor, leaking air struts, or a malfunctioning ride height sensor.
    *   Solution: Diagnose the specific component failure and replace the faulty parts.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $5,000+ (depending on the specific component needing replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Freezing, crashing, slow response times, Bluetooth connectivity problems, navigation errors.
    *   Solution: Software update or replacement of the infotainment unit.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150-$300 for software update. $2,000+ for replacement
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Sensor Malfunctions**
    *   Description/Symptoms: False warnings or errors related to various systems such as parking sensors, blind-spot monitoring, or tire pressure monitoring.
    *   Solution: Diagnose the specific sensor that is malfunctioning and replace it.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+ (depending on the sensor and location)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Sensor Malfunctions**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Issues with the powered tailgate getting stuck, not opening, or not closing properly.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the tailgate struts.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Trim Detachment**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Interior trim pieces (e.g., dashboard trim, door panel trim) coming loose or detaching.
    *   Solution: Re-attach trim pieces or replace if damaged
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500

**Summary**

The 2023 Aston Martin DBX707, while offering exceptional performance and luxury, appears to have some reliability concerns. The most significant issues relate to the engine (turbocharger failure and oil leaks), transmission (harsh shifting), suspension (air suspension malfunctions), and electrical systems (infotainment glitches and sensor malfunctions). The cost to rectify these issues can be considerable, ranging from a few hundred dollars for minor fixes to several thousand for major component replacements. While no specific recalls were found for the common complaints listed, prospective buyers should be aware of these potential issues and carefully inspect the vehicle's service history and consider a pre-purchase inspection by a qualified mechanic.
