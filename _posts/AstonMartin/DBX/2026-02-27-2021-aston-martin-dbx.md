# 2021 Aston Martin DBX Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2021 Aston Martin DBX marks the luxury brand's entry into the high-performance SUV segment, blending exotic styling with everyday utility. Because it is a low-volume exotic vehicle backed by limited owner-submitted complaint data in this early production phase, prospective used buyers should inspect service history closely and verify proper operation of all onboard electronics and mechanical systems.

---

## Engine

### 4.0L Twin-Turbocharged V8

The 2021 Aston Martin DBX features a Mercedes-AMG-sourced 4.0-liter twin-turbocharged V8 engine producing roughly 542 horsepower and 516 lb-ft of torque. This powerplant delivers brisk acceleration and a characteristic exhaust note, though ongoing maintenance requires specialized technician support and can be costly.

## Transmission

### 9-Speed Automatic

Power is routed through a 9-speed traditional automatic transmission paired with an all-wheel-drive system designed to handle high torque outputs. It offers smooth cruising and responsive manual shifting via paddle shifters, with no widespread systemic mechanical failures reported by owners.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V784000: Structure:Interior Panels:Ceiling](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Aston+Martin&model=DBX&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 201, "Occupant Protection in Interior Impact." The missing foam blocks can increase the risk of injury to unbelted occupants in the event of a crash.
- **What to do:** Aston Martin will notify owners, and dealers will install the headliner foam blocks, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V248000: Power Train:Driveline:Driveshaft](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Aston+Martin&model=DBX&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** A loss of front wheel drive or an engine stall can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the fasteners, and tighten them if necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V255000: Electrical System:Wiring:Fuses And Circuit Breakers](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Aston+Martin&model=DBX&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** An engine stall or loss of headlights and/or power steering can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the fuses and cables for damage and replace them as necessary. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V200000: Suspension:Rear](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Aston+Martin&model=DBX&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** Failure of the rear lower suspension arm can cause a loss of vehicle control, damage the brake line or other suspension components, and increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Aston Martin will inspect the lower rear suspension arms for cracks, and as necessary, replace the lower rear arms, and replace the bolts for the torque reaction links, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2021 Aston Martin DBX offers grand-touring performance and luxury in an SUV package, though its exotic nature demands careful pre-purchase inspection and a healthy maintenance budget. With minimal recurring complaint data available, buyers are encouraged to prioritize models with documented dealer service records.
