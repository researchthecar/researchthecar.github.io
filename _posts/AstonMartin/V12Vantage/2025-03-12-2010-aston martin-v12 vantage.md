The 2010 Aston Martin V12 Vantage is a high-performance sports car known for its stunning design, luxurious interior, and exhilarating driving experience. The most desirable engine option is the 6.0L V12 engine, producing 510 horsepower and 420 lb-ft of torque. This engine was paired with a 6-speed manual transmission. While owners praise its potent engine, responsive handling, and distinctive exhaust note, some common complaints include the high cost of maintenance, limited practicality for everyday use, and some issues with the carâ€™s electronic systems.

**Engine**

**6.0L V12**

*   **Oil Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks can develop from various locations on the engine, including valve cover gaskets, oil pan gasket, and front timing cover. Symptoms include visible oil drips under the car, a burning oil smell, and low oil levels.
    *   Solution: Identify the source of the leak and replace the affected gasket or seal. For more serious leaks, resealing the engine might be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $2,000+ (depending on the location and complexity of the repair)
    * Recalls: None Found

*   **Coil Pack Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Misfires can occur due to failing coil packs. Symptoms include rough idling, reduced power, and the check engine light illuminating.
    *   Solution: Diagnose the failing coil pack using a diagnostic tool and replace the faulty unit.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $500 (depending on the number of coil packs needing replacement and labor rates)
    * Recalls: None Found

*   **Vacuum Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Vacuum leaks can cause rough idling, poor performance, and difficulty starting.
    *   Solution: Identify and repair or replace the damaged vacuum lines or components. Smoke testing is a popular method to find leaks.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+ (depending on the location and complexity of the repair)
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**6-Speed Manual**

*   **Clutch Wear**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Premature clutch wear can occur, especially with aggressive driving. Symptoms include slipping clutch, difficulty shifting, and a high engagement point.
    *   Solution: Replace the clutch disc, pressure plate, and throw-out bearing. Consider resurfacing or replacing the flywheel.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+ (depending on parts and labor)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Adaptive Damper Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The adaptive dampers (if equipped) can fail, leading to a rough ride, poor handling, and warning lights on the dashboard.
    *   Solution: Replace the faulty damper.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+ per damper (depending on the source of the parts and labor rates)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Battery Drain**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Excessive battery drain can occur, particularly if the car is not driven frequently. This can lead to a dead battery and difficulty starting the car.
    *   Solution: Investigate and eliminate any parasitic draws on the battery. Potential sources include faulty modules, aftermarket accessories, or a failing battery. Consider using a battery tender when the car is not in use.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+ (depending on the source of the drain)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Sensor Failures**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Various sensors, such as oxygen sensors, mass airflow sensors, and crankshaft position sensors, can fail, leading to performance issues and warning lights.
    *   Solution: Diagnose the faulty sensor and replace it with a new one.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+ per sensor (depending on the type of sensor and labor rates)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Parking Sensor Malfunction**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The parking sensors can malfunction, leading to false alarms or failure to detect obstacles.
    *   Solution: Diagnose and replace the faulty parking sensor. Check the wiring harness for damage.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $400+ per sensor (depending on the number of sensors needing replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Navigation System Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The factory navigation system can experience glitches, become outdated, or fail altogether.
    *   Solution: Attempt to update the system software. If the issue persists, consider replacing the unit with an aftermarket system.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $2,000+ (depending on the solution)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2010 Aston Martin V12 Vantage is a desirable sports car, but potential buyers should be aware of some common issues. Engine oil leaks, coil pack failures, and vacuum leaks are prevalent. Clutch wear is a concern for the manual transmission. Electrical problems like battery drain and sensor failures can also occur. Although a stunning vehicle, these issues can become costly as repairs can be expensive. Careful inspection and a pre-purchase inspection by a qualified technician are highly recommended.
