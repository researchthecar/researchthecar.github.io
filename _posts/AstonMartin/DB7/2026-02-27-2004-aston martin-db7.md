# 2004 Aston Martin DB7

### Overview
The 2004 Aston Martin DB7 represents a pinnacle of British luxury grand touring, blending potent performance with exquisite craftsmanship. While generally considered a reliable vehicle for its class and era, like any high-performance exotic, it can experience specific issues that owners should be aware of. This data focuses on documented, non-wear-and-tear related problems that may arise, particularly concerning its advanced powertrain and complex electrical systems. Regular maintenance is crucial for longevity, but this analysis highlights potential areas of concern that might require specialized attention and can impact ownership costs.

---


## Engine

### 5.9L V12
**Ignition Coil Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Misfires, rough idling, check engine light, reduced power.
- **Solution:** Replacement of faulty ignition coil(s).
- **Cost:** $300 - $600 per coil

**Coolant Leaks (Hoses/Radiator)**
- **Symptoms:** Visible coolant loss, overheating, sweet smell, puddles under the car.
- **Solution:** Replacement of damaged hoses or radiator.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1500

**Timing Chain Tensioner Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Rattling noise from the front of the engine, especially on startup.
- **Solution:** Replacement of timing chain tensioners and guides.
- **Cost:** $2000 - $4000


## Transmission

### 5-Speed Automatic (ZF 5HP30)
**Torque Converter Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Slipping gears, shuddering, delayed engagement, unusual noises.
- **Solution:** Rebuild or replacement of the torque converter.
- **Cost:** $1500 - $3000

**Valve Body Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Erratic shifting, harsh shifts, transmission warning light.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of the transmission valve body.
- **Cost:** $1000 - $2500


### 6-Speed Manual
**Clutch Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Slipping clutch, difficulty shifting gears, burning smell.
- **Solution:** Replacement of clutch kit (clutch disc, pressure plate, release bearing).
- **Cost:** $1500 - $3000


## Suspension

### Standard Chassis
**Shock Absorber Leaks/Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Bouncy ride, poor handling, visible fluid leaks from shocks.
- **Solution:** Replacement of worn shock absorbers.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1500

**Bushings Deterioration**
- **Symptoms:** Clunking noises over bumps, vague steering feel, uneven tire wear.
- **Solution:** Replacement of worn suspension bushings.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1000


## Electrical

### General Electrical System
**Window Regulator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Power windows not operating, slow operation, grinding noises.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the window regulator mechanism.
- **Cost:** $400 - $800

**Central Locking Actuator Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Doors not locking/unlocking consistently, individual door lock failure.
- **Solution:** Replacement of faulty door lock actuators.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600 per actuator

**ECU (Engine Control Unit) Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Intermittent engine performance issues, warning lights, starting problems.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and potential reprogramming or replacement of the ECU.
- **Cost:** $1000 - $3000

## Interior

### Cabin Electronics and Trim
**Heater Control Valve Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Inconsistent cabin temperature, lack of heat or AC.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the heater control valve.
- **Cost:** $300 - $700

**Infotainment System (if equipped) Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Screen freezing, audio issues, navigation problems.
- **Solution:** Software updates or component repair/replacement.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1500

### Summary Verdict
The 2004 Aston Martin DB7, particularly the V12 models, offers a compelling blend of performance and luxury. While its core mechanicals are robust, potential owners should be prepared for the possibility of issues related to its complex V12 engine (ignition, cooling, timing), automatic transmission, and sophisticated electrical systems. These are not typically minor repairs and often require specialized knowledge and parts, contributing to higher maintenance costs. However, with diligent maintenance and prompt attention to any developing issues, the DB7 can remain a rewarding and reliable grand tourer.
