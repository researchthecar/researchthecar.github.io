The 2022 Aston Martin Vantage is a luxury sports car known for its striking design, powerful performance, and handcrafted interior. The standard engine is a Mercedes-AMG sourced 4.0-liter twin-turbocharged V8 producing 503 horsepower and 505 lb-ft of torque. The Vantage is available with either an 8-speed automatic or a 7-speed manual transmission. While praised for its sharp handling, luxurious appointments, and exhilarating driving experience, some owners have voiced concerns about reliability, build quality, and the infotainment system.

**Engine**

**4.0L Twin-Turbo V8**

*   **Oil Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks can occur from various locations, including the valve cover gaskets, oil pan gasket, or turbocharger oil lines. Symptoms include visible oil leaks, a burning oil smell, and low oil levels.
    *   Solution: Identifying and replacing the leaking gasket or seal.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1500+ depending on the leak location and extent of the repair.
    *   Recalls: None Found

* **Coolant Leaks**
    * Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaks may occur from hoses, the water pump, or the radiator. Symptoms include low coolant levels, overheating, and visible leaks.
    * Solution: Replace the leaking component.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1000+, depending on the location and component that is leaking.
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**8-Speed Automatic (ZF 8HP)**

*   **Rough Shifting/Hesitation**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported experiencing rough or delayed shifts, especially during cold starts or when transitioning between gears. This can manifest as a noticeable jerk or hesitation.
    *   Solution: This issue is often resolved with a transmission software update or a transmission fluid flush. In more severe cases, valve body replacement may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 for a fluid flush, $150 - $300 for a software update, $1500 - $3000 for valve body replacement.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**7-Speed Manual**

*No Issues Found*

**Suspension**

*   **Adaptive Damper Malfunctions**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Error messages on the dashboard indicating a problem with the adaptive damping system. Noticeable changes in ride quality, such as increased stiffness or bounciness.
    *   Solution: Diagnosing the faulty damper(s) and replacing them.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1000 - $3000+ per damper, including labor.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The Mercedes-sourced infotainment system can experience glitches, freezing, and connectivity problems. Navigation may be inaccurate, and the screen may occasionally go blank.
    *   Solution: Software updates from the dealer, resetting the system. In rare cases, the entire unit needs replacing.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 - $100 for software updates, $500 - $3000+ for unit replacement.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Sensor Malfunctions**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Faulty sensors, such as those for the tire pressure monitoring system (TPMS) or parking sensors, can trigger warning lights and inaccurate readings.
    *   Solution: Replacing the malfunctioning sensor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+ per sensor, including labor.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Panel Alignment Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Gaps between body panels may be inconsistent or uneven, particularly around the doors, hood, and trunk.
    *   Solution: Adjusting the panel alignment at a body shop.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1000+, depending on the severity.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Trim Loosening/Rattling**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Interior trim pieces, such as those on the dashboard or door panels, may become loose or start to rattle over time, particularly on rough roads.
    *   Solution: Re-securing the trim pieces or replacing damaged clips.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+ depending on the location and complexity.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

The 2022 Aston Martin Vantage, while a desirable sports car, can suffer from a variety of issues ranging from minor trim rattles to more significant mechanical problems. Engine oil and coolant leaks, transmission issues, and infotainment system glitches are some of the most common complaints. The severity and cost of these issues vary greatly, with some repairs being relatively inexpensive while others can require significant investment. Prospective buyers should be aware of these potential problems and thoroughly inspect any Vantage they are considering purchasing.
