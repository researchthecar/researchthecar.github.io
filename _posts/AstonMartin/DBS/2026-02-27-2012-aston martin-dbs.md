# 2012 Aston Martin DBS

### Overview
The 2012 Aston Martin DBS, a grand tourer known for its potent V12 engine and luxurious appointments, generally exhibits strong mechanical reliability. However, as with any high-performance, low-volume exotic car, potential owners should be aware of specific areas that may require attention. While the core powertrain is robust, certain electronic systems and specialized components can present challenges. Maintenance costs are inherently high due to the specialized nature of the vehicle and the cost of parts and labor. This analysis focuses on documented, non-wear-and-tear related issues that have been reported by owners and technicians, providing a realistic outlook for prospective buyers.

---


## Engine

### 5.9L V12
**Ignition Coil Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Misfires, rough idling, check engine light illumination.
- **Solution:** Replacement of faulty ignition coil(s).
- **Cost:** $300 - $600 per coil

**Oil Cooler Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Visible oil leaks around the cooler, potential for oil in coolant.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the oil cooler and associated seals.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1500


## Transmission

### Touchtronic 2 (6-Speed Automatic)
**Torque Converter Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Slipping gears, harsh shifting, shuddering during acceleration.
- **Solution:** Rebuild or replacement of the torque converter.
- **Cost:** $2000 - $4000


## Suspension

### Adaptive Damping System
**Damper Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Harsh ride, clunking noises, warning lights on the dashboard related to suspension.
- **Solution:** Replacement of faulty adaptive dampers.
- **Cost:** $1000 - $2500 per damper


## Electrical

### Infotainment System
**Navigation System Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Freezing, unresponsiveness, inaccurate GPS.
- **Solution:** Software update or replacement of the infotainment module.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1500


### Central Locking System
**Actuator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Doors not locking or unlocking consistently, individual door issues.
- **Solution:** Replacement of faulty door lock actuators.
- **Cost:** $300 - $700 per actuator

### Summary Verdict
The 2012 Aston Martin DBS is a remarkable vehicle with a generally solid mechanical foundation, particularly its V12 engine. However, its exotic nature means that certain specialized components, especially within the electrical and adaptive suspension systems, can be prone to failure and are costly to repair. Owners should budget for potential issues such as ignition coil problems, torque converter wear, and electronic module glitches. While the exterior and core interior materials are durable, specific electronic controls within the cabin can be a source of concern. Regular, specialized maintenance is crucial for mitigating these risks and ensuring the long-term reliability of this exclusive grand tourer.
