**Introduction**

The 2010 Aston Martin DBS is a grand tourer that was produced from 2007 to 2012. It is powered by a 6.0-liter V12 engine that produces 510 horsepower and 420 lb-ft of torque. The DBS is paired with a 6-speed manual or 6-speed automatic transmission.

People like the Aston Martin DBS for its sleek design, powerful engine, and luxurious interior. However, it has also been criticized for its high price tag, poor fuel economy, and lack of practicality.

**Engine**

**6.0-liter V12**

**Carbon buildup on intake valves**
Description/Symptoms: This is a common issue on Aston Martin V12 engines. Carbon buildup can cause the intake valves to stick, which can lead to misfires, rough idling, and a loss of power.
Solution: The carbon buildup can be removed by walnut blasting or by using a chemical cleaner.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1000

**Timing chain failure**
Description/Symptoms: This is a rare but serious issue that can occur on Aston Martin V12 engines. The timing chain can fail, which can cause catastrophic engine damage.
Solution: The timing chain must be replaced if it fails.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $5000 - $10000

**Transmission**

**6-Speed Automatic**

**Faulty torque converter**
Description/Symptoms: The torque converter can fail, which can cause the transmission to shudder or slip.
Solution: The torque converter must be replaced.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $2000 - $3000

**Suspension**

**No Issues Found**

**Electrical**

**Battery drain**
Description/Symptoms: The battery can drain quickly, even when the car is not in use.
Solution: The cause of the battery drain must be diagnosed and repaired.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+

**Exterior**

**No Issues Found**

**Interior**

**Malfunctioning infotainment system**
Description/Symptoms: The infotainment system can malfunction, which can cause the screen to go blank or to display error messages.
Solution: The infotainment system must be repaired or replaced.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $1000 - $2000

**Summary**

The 2010 Aston Martin DBS is a high-performance grand tourer that has a number of potential issues. The most serious issues are the timing chain failure and the battery drain. However, most of the other issues are relatively minor and can be fixed for a reasonable cost. Overall, the DBS is a reliable car that is well-suited for long-distance driving.
