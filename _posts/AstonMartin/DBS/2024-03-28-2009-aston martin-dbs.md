The 2009 Aston Martin DBS is a grand tourer produced by Aston Martin from 2007 to 2012. It was powered by a 6.0-liter V12 engine mated to a 6-speed manual or 6-speed Touchtronic 2 automatic transmission. The DBS was praised for its stunning design, powerful engine, and luxurious interior, but it was also criticized for its high price and stiff ride.

**Engine**

**6.0-liter V12**

* **Oil Leaks:** Oil leaks can occur from the camshaft covers, oil pan, or rear main seal. Symptoms include oil spots on the ground or under the car. The fix involves replacing the faulty seals or gaskets. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $500 - $1,500+
* **Coolant Leaks:** Coolant leaks can occur from the radiator, hoses, or water pump. Symptoms include overheating, low coolant levels, or puddles of coolant under the car. The fix involves replacing the faulty component. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $300 - $1,000+
* **Timing Chain Problems:** The timing chain can stretch or break, causing the engine to lose power or fail. Symptoms include a rattling noise from the engine, reduced performance, or a check engine light. The fix involves replacing the timing chain and components. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $2,000 - $4,000+
* **Intake Manifold Issues:** The intake manifold can develop cracks or leaks, causing the engine to run rough or lose power. Symptoms include a loss of power, a rough idle, or a check engine light. The fix involves replacing the intake manifold. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $1,000 - $2,000+

**Transmission**

**6-Speed Manual**

* **Clutch Problems:** The clutch can wear out over time, causing difficulty shifting gears or slipping. Symptoms include a spongy clutch pedal, grinding noises when shifting, or a loss of power. The fix involves replacing the clutch and pressure plate. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $1,500 - $2,500+
* **Synchronizer Issues:** The synchronizers can wear out, making it difficult to shift into certain gears. Symptoms include grinding noises when shifting or difficulty shifting. The fix involves replacing the faulty synchronizers. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $500 - $1,000+

**6-Speed Touchtronic 2**

* **Transmission Control Unit (TCU) Failures:** The TCU can fail, causing the transmission to shift erratically or not shift at all. Symptoms include harsh shifting, delayed shifts, or a check engine light. The fix involves replacing the TCU. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $1,000 - $2,000+
* **Valve Body Issues:** The valve body can malfunction, causing the transmission to shift improperly or slip. Symptoms include rough shifting, delayed shifts, or a loss of power. The fix involves replacing the valve body. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $1,500 - $2,500+

**Suspension**

* **Front Suspension Knocking:** The front suspension can develop a knocking sound when driving over bumps or rough roads. The fix involves replacing the faulty suspension components. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $500 - $1,000+
* **Rear Suspension Sagging:** The rear suspension can sag or collapse over time, causing the car to ride low in the rear. The fix involves replacing the worn or damaged suspension components. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $1,000 - $2,000+

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain:** The battery can drain quickly, even when the car is not in use. This can be caused by a faulty alternator, starter, or electrical component. Symptoms include difficulty starting the car, dim headlights, or a dead battery. The fix involves diagnosing and replacing the faulty component. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $200 - $1,000+
* **Electrical Gremlins:** Various electrical issues can occur, such as malfunctioning lights, power window problems, or radio issues. The fix involves diagnosing and repairing the faulty electrical components. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $100 - $500+

**Exterior**

* **Headlight Condensation:** Condensation can form inside the headlights, causing them to become foggy or cloudy. The fix involves replacing the headlight assemblies. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $500 - $1,000+

**Interior**

* **Navigation System Malfunctions:** The navigation system can experience glitches, freezing, or becoming unresponsive. The fix involves updating the software or replacing the navigation system. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $500 - $1,500+
* **Climate Control Issues:** The climate control system can malfunction, causing the temperature to not regulate properly or the air conditioning to not work. The fix involves diagnosing and repairing the faulty components. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $300 - $1,000+

**Recalls**

* NHTSA Recall 14V454000: Airbag sensor failure
* NHTSA Recall 15V272000: Brake pedal nut recall
* NHTSA Recall 16V001000: Fuel pump hose recall
* NHTSA Recall 16V808000: Brake light switch recall
* NHTSA Recall 18V341000: Seatbelt malfunction recall

**Summary**

The 2009 Aston Martin DBS is a beautiful and powerful grand tourer, but it is not without its issues. The most common problems include oil leaks, coolant leaks, timing chain problems, clutch problems, TCU failures, and suspension knocking. These issues can be expensive to fix, so it is important to factor in the potential cost of repairs when considering purchasing this vehicle.
