# 2011 Aston Martin DBS

### Overview
The 2011 Aston Martin DBS, a grand tourer known for its potent V12 engine and luxurious design, generally exhibits strong mechanical reliability for its class. However, as with any high-performance, low-volume exotic vehicle, potential owners should be aware of specific areas that may require attention. While the core powertrain is robust, certain electronic systems and specialized components can present challenges. Maintenance and repair costs are inherently high due to the specialized nature of the vehicle and the limited number of qualified technicians. Early detection of issues and adherence to a rigorous maintenance schedule are crucial for preserving the DBS's performance and value.

---


## Engine

### 5.9L V12
**Ignition Coil Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Misfires, rough idling, check engine light, reduced power.
- **Solution:** Replacement of faulty ignition coil(s).
- **Cost:** $300 - $600 per coil

**Coolant Leaks (Hoses/Pipes)**
- **Symptoms:** Visible coolant loss, overheating, sweet smell, white smoke from exhaust.
- **Solution:** Replacement of damaged coolant hoses or pipes.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1200


## Transmission

### Touchtronic 2 (6-Speed Automatic)
**Transmission Fluid Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Leaking fluid under the vehicle, shifting problems, slipping gears.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of seals, lines, or transmission cooler.
- **Cost:** $800 - $2500

**Torque Converter Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Shuddering during acceleration, delayed engagement, whining noises.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the torque converter.
- **Cost:** $2000 - $4000


## Suspension

### Adaptive Damping System
**Damper Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Harsh ride, clunking noises over bumps, uneven tire wear, warning lights.
- **Solution:** Replacement of faulty adaptive dampers.
- **Cost:** $1000 - $2500 per corner


## Electrical

### Infotainment System
**Screen Glitches/Freezing**
- **Symptoms:** Display issues, unresponsive touch screen, system reboots.
- **Solution:** Software update or replacement of infotainment module.
- **Cost:** $1500 - $3000


### Central Locking System
**Actuator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Doors not locking/unlocking, intermittent operation.
- **Solution:** Replacement of faulty door lock actuators.
- **Cost:** $300 - $700 per door


## Exterior

### Standard Chassis
**No widespread issues reported.**
- **Symptoms:** N/A
- **Solution:** N/A
- **Cost:** Varies


## Interior

### Leather Upholstery
**Excessive Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Cracking or tearing of leather on high-contact areas (seats, steering wheel).
- **Solution:** Professional reconditioning or replacement of leather panels.
- **Cost:** $500 - $2000+

### Summary Verdict
The 2011 Aston Martin DBS is a remarkable vehicle with a generally solid mechanical foundation. The V12 engine is a highlight, though ignition and cooling system components can require attention. The automatic transmission is robust but can suffer from leaks or torque converter issues. Electrical gremlins, particularly with the infotainment and locking systems, are not uncommon in exotic cars of this era. Suspension components, especially the adaptive dampers, can be costly to repair. Interior wear is typical for a car of its age and use. Overall, the DBS demands significant financial commitment for maintenance and repairs, but its reliability is acceptable for a hand-built supercar when properly cared for.
