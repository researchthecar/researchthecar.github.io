Here's a comprehensive breakdown of common issues and complaints associated with the 2010 Aston Martin DBS, organized by category and component.

The 2010 Aston Martin DBS is a grand tourer known for its elegant design, powerful performance, and luxurious interior. It was offered with a naturally aspirated 6.0-liter V12 engine, producing 510 horsepower and 420 lb-ft of torque. The available transmissions were a 6-speed manual or a 6-speed automatic. Owners often praise the car's stunning aesthetics, exhilarating driving experience, and the distinctive Aston Martin sound. Conversely, some criticisms include the car's relatively high maintenance costs, potential reliability issues, and the dated infotainment system.

**Engine**

**6.0L V12**

*   **Oil Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks can occur from various points, including valve cover gaskets, timing chain cover, and rear main seal. Symptoms include visible oil drips, oil consumption, and burning oil smell.
    *   Solution: Replace the leaking gasket or seal.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+ (depending on location and severity)
    * Recalls: None Found

*   **Coil Pack Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Individual coil packs can fail, leading to misfires, rough running, and a check engine light.
    *   Solution: Replace the faulty coil pack.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500 (per coil pack)
    * Recalls: None Found

*   **Coolant Leaks**
    * Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaks around coolant hoses.
    * Solution: Replace the faulty hoses.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $1000 (depending on severity)
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**6-Speed Manual**

*   **Clutch Wear**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Premature clutch wear can occur due to the high performance nature of the car. Symptoms include slipping clutch, difficulty shifting, and unusual noises.
    *   Solution: Replace the clutch assembly.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**6-Speed Automatic**

*   **Valve Body Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The valve body can experience issues, leading to harsh shifting, delayed engagement, or complete failure.
    *   Solution: Repair or replace the valve body.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Adaptive Damper Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The adaptive dampers can fail, leading to a loss of ride control, a stiffer ride, and error messages on the dashboard.
    *   Solution: Replace the faulty damper(s).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,500+ (per damper)
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Battery Drain**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Excessive battery drain can occur, leading to difficulty starting or a dead battery. This can be caused by a parasitic drain from various electrical components.
    *   Solution: Diagnose and repair the source of the parasitic drain.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+ (depending on the source)
    * Recalls: None Found
*   **Infotainment System Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The infotainment system can experience issues such as freezing, slow response times, or complete failure.
    *   Solution: Update or replace the infotainment system.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+
    * Recalls: None Found
*   **Sensor Failures**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Various sensors can fail, such as oxygen sensors, mass airflow sensors, and crankshaft position sensors. This can lead to a check engine light and performance issues.
    *   Solution: Replace the faulty sensor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+ (per sensor)
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Door Handle Actuator Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The door handle actuators can fail, making it difficult to open the doors.
    *   Solution: Replace the faulty actuator.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+ (per door)
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Navigation Screen Delamination**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The navigation screen can develop a delamination issue, causing discoloration and making it hard to read.
    *   Solution: Replace the navigation screen.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $3,000+
    * Recalls: None Found
*   **Stitching Coming Loose**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The stitching on the dashboard or seats can come loose.
    *   Solution: Repair the stitching.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found
*   **Dashboard Shrinkage**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The leather dashboard may shrink and cause the dashboard to crack.
    *   Solution: Replace the dashboard.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $5,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2010 Aston Martin DBS presents owners with a mix of performance-related and age-related issues. Engine oil leaks and coil pack failures, as well as clutch wear for manual transmissions, represent common maintenance concerns with moderate to high repair costs. Electrical issues, particularly with the battery drain and infotainment system, can also be problematic. While the DBS offers a luxurious driving experience, potential buyers should be aware of these potential maintenance costs and challenges.
