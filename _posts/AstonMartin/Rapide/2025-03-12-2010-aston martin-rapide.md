The 2010 Aston Martin Rapide is a four-door grand tourer known for its sleek design, luxurious interior, and powerful performance. It features a naturally aspirated 6.0-liter V12 engine producing 470 horsepower and 443 lb-ft of torque, paired with a six-speed Touchtronic 2 automatic transmission. Owners appreciate the Rapide for its exclusivity, elegant styling, and engaging driving experience. Common dislikes include its tight rear seating, dated infotainment system, and relatively high running costs.

**Engine**

**6.0L V12**

*   **Oil Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks can occur from various areas of the engine, including valve cover gaskets, oil pan gaskets, and front crankshaft seal. Symptoms include visible oil puddles under the car, a burning oil smell, and low oil levels.
    *   Solution: Replacing the leaking gaskets or seals.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+ depending on the location and extent of the leak.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Coil Pack Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Misfires, rough idling, and a check engine light. Often caused by degraded coil packs due to age and heat.
    *   Solution: Replacing the faulty coil pack(s).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600+ per coil pack depending on labor.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**Touchtronic 2 (6-Speed Automatic)**

*   **Harsh Shifting**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Reports of rough or jerky gear changes, particularly when shifting at lower speeds or under light throttle.
    *   Solution: Performing a transmission fluid flush and software update. In some cases, valve body replacement may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $3,000+ depending on the severity and required repair.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Adaptive Damper Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Warning lights on the dashboard, ride quality degradation, and uneven handling.
    *   Solution: Replacing the faulty damper(s).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+ per damper.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Battery Drain**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The battery drains quickly, especially when the car is not driven for a few days.
    *   Solution: Replacing the battery, or investigating the parasitic draw to isolate the source of the drain and repairing the affected component.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,000+ depending on the cause.
    *   Recalls: None Found
*   **Infotainment System Malfunctions**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The infotainment system freezes, becomes unresponsive, or exhibits glitches.
    *   Solution: Software update, or replacing the head unit.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $2,000+ depending on the severity and required repair.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Door Handle Actuator Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Failure of the door handle actuators, making it difficult to open doors from the exterior.
    *   Solution: Replacing the faulty actuator.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+ per door.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Navigation System Problems**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Outdated navigation system maps. Navigation system failure.
    *   Solution: Updating the maps or navigation system.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2010 Aston Martin Rapide presents a mixed bag of potential issues. While the engine is generally robust, oil leaks and coil pack failures can be expected. The transmission can suffer from harsh shifting, which can lead to costly repairs. Electrical issues, particularly battery drain and infotainment malfunctions, are relatively common. Suspension issues and door handle problems add to the potential maintenance costs. The severity of these issues ranges from minor inconveniences to substantial repairs, with costs varying accordingly. Prospective buyers should be aware of these potential issues and factor them into their ownership budget.
