The 2016 Aston Martin Vanquish is a grand tourer known for its stunning design, powerful performance, and luxurious interior. It features a naturally aspirated 6.0-liter V12 engine producing 568 horsepower and 465 lb-ft of torque. The engine is paired with a Touchtronic III eight-speed automatic transmission. Owners typically praise the Vanquish for its exhilarating driving experience, exquisite craftsmanship, and distinctive styling. However, some common complaints include higher than average maintenance costs, some electrical glitches, and dated infotainment system.

**Engine**

**6.0L V12**

* **Oil Leaks**
    * Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks, often observed around the valve cover gaskets or rear main seal. May lead to low oil levels and potential engine damage if left unchecked.
    * Solution: Replacement of the leaking gaskets or seals. Thorough cleaning of the engine bay to remove residual oil.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Coil Pack Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Engine misfires, rough idling, and a noticeable loss of power. OBD code indicating a misfire on a specific cylinder.
    * Solution: Replacement of the faulty coil pack. Replacement of spark plugs if needed.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**Touchtronic III 8-Speed Automatic**

* **Rough Shifting/Hesitation**
    * Description/Symptoms: Noticeable delays or harshness during gear changes. Hesitation when accelerating from a stop or during overtaking maneuvers.
    * Solution: Transmission software reflash or update. In more severe cases, valve body replacement or transmission rebuild.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $5,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Adaptive Damper Malfunction**
    * Description/Symptoms: Stiff or uneven ride quality. Warning light on the dashboard indicating a problem with the adaptive damping system.
    * Solution: Replacement of the faulty damper. Diagnostic check of the suspension control module.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $3,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Infotainment System Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: Infotainment system freezing, crashing, or becoming unresponsive. Navigation system errors. Bluetooth connectivity problems.
    * Solution: Software update of the infotainment system. Replacement of the infotainment system control module in severe cases.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $2,000+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Battery Drain**
    * Description/Symptoms: Battery draining prematurely, especially when the car is not used for extended periods. Difficult starting or complete failure to start.
    * Solution: Diagnostic check for parasitic draws. Replacement of the battery. Alternator testing and replacement if needed.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Parking Sensor Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Parking sensors failing to detect obstacles. False alarms or no audible warning when approaching objects.
    * Solution: Replacement of the faulty parking sensor. Cleaning of the sensor to remove dirt or debris.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Navigation/Infotainment Screen Delamination**
    * Description/Symptoms: Screen delaminates, bubbling of the protective layer on the screen.
    * Solution: Replacement of the screen.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $3,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2016 Aston Martin Vanquish, while offering a remarkable driving experience and luxurious feel, has a handful of common issues that potential buyers should be aware of. The engine can suffer from oil leaks and coil pack failures, while the transmission may exhibit rough shifting. Electrical gremlins, particularly with the infotainment system and battery drain, are also frequently reported. Suspension issues are also present in a good amount of these cars. Most repairs tend to be costly, reflecting the exotic nature of the vehicle.
