# 2016 Aston Martin Vanquish Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2016 Aston Martin Vanquish is an exotic grand tourer known for its striking design and formidable V12 performance. As a high-end hand-built British supercar, shopping for a used model requires careful attention to maintenance history, electronic complexity, and specialized servicing costs. While official complaint data for this specific model year is sparse, prospective buyers should always perform a thorough pre-purchase inspection to ensure all mechanical and electrical systems operate as intended.

---

## Engine

### 6.0L V12

Powered by a naturally aspirated 6.0-liter V12 engine producing approximately 568 horsepower and 457 lb-ft of torque. This hand-built powerplant offers thrilling acceleration and a sonorous exhaust note, though routine servicing demands specialized technician expertise.

## Transmission

### Touchtronic III 8-speed Automatic

Equipped with a ZF-sourced 8-speed torque-converter automatic transmission paired with a rear-mounted transaxle layout. It delivers smooth, rapid gear changes and improved efficiency compared to older single-clutch automated manuals.

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

- **Issue:** The affected vehicles are equipped with interior door unlocking mechanisms that may not function when the vehicle has been locked from the outside. As such, these vehicles fail to conform to Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 206, "Door Locks and Door Retention Components." ## Summary Verdict

The 2016 Aston Martin Vanquish remains a breathtaking and exclusive choice for luxury GT enthusiasts. Because owner complaints for this model year are virtually non-existent in public registries, buyers should rely heavily on professional PPIs, service records, and verification of any remaining specialist warranties to protect against the high cost of exotic car ownership.
