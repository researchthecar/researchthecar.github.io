# 2004 Aston Martin Vanquish

### Overview
The 2004 Aston Martin Vanquish, a grand tourer known for its striking design and potent V12 engine, represents a pinnacle of automotive engineering for its era. While generally built to a high standard, as expected from a luxury supercar, certain components and systems can exhibit issues over time due to age and the demanding nature of its performance. This analysis focuses on documented reliability concerns beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential areas of attention for owners and enthusiasts. The Vanquish's complex systems, particularly its powertrain and electronics, are areas where specific attention may be warranted.

---


## Engine

### 5.9L V12
**Ignition Coil Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Misfires, rough idling, check engine light, reduced engine power.
- **Solution:** Replacement of faulty ignition coil(s).
- **Cost:** $300 - $600 per coil

**Coolant Leaks (Hoses/Radiator)**
- **Symptoms:** Visible coolant puddles, overheating, sweet smell from engine bay.
- **Solution:** Replacement of leaking hoses or radiator.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1500

**Timing Chain Tensioner Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Rattling noise from the front of the engine, especially on startup.
- **Solution:** Replacement of timing chain tensioners and guides.
- **Cost:** $2500 - $4000


## Transmission

### 6-Speed 'Touchtronic' Automatic
**Torque Converter Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Slipping gears, shuddering during acceleration, delayed engagement.
- **Solution:** Rebuild or replacement of the torque converter.
- **Cost:** $1500 - $3000

**Hydraulic Control Unit Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Erratic shifting, failure to shift into certain gears, transmission warning light.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of the hydraulic control unit.
- **Cost:** $2000 - $3500


## Suspension

### Adaptive Dampers
**Damper Seal Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Bouncy ride, visible fluid leaks from shock absorbers, warning light on dashboard.
- **Solution:** Replacement of leaking adaptive dampers.
- **Cost:** $1000 - $2000 per corner

## Electrical

### Central Electronics Module (CEM)
**CEM Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** Intermittent electrical faults, non-functioning accessories, warning lights.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and potential reprogramming or replacement of the CEM.
- **Cost:** $1000 - $2500


### Infotainment System
**Navigation System Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Screen freezing, inaccurate GPS, system reboots.
- **Solution:** Software update or replacement of the head unit.

### Summary Verdict
The 2004 Aston Martin Vanquish, while a desirable supercar, exhibits some common reliability concerns associated with high-performance vehicles of its age. The V12 engine, though powerful, can experience issues with ignition coils and cooling system components. The 'Touchtronic' transmission, while advanced for its time, may require attention to its torque converter or hydraulic control unit. Electrical gremlins, particularly with the CEM and infotainment system, are also noted. Suspension components, especially adaptive dampers, can develop leaks. Owners should be prepared for potentially significant repair costs, especially for powertrain and electrical issues. Regular maintenance and prompt attention to early symptoms are crucial for preserving the Vanquish's performance and value.
