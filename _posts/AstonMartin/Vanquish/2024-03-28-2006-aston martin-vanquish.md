**Introduction**

The 2006 Aston Martin Vanquish is a high-performance sports car known for its sleek design and powerful V12 engine. It is available with a 6-speed manual transmission or a 6-speed automatic transmission. The 6.0-liter V12 engine produces 460 horsepower and 417 lb-ft of torque. The Vanquish has received praise for its stunning appearance, luxurious interior, and exhilarating driving experience. However, like any vehicle, it has also faced some reported issues.

**Engine**

* **6.0-Liter V12**

**Intake Manifold Gasket Failure**
Description/Symptoms: Failure of the intake manifold gasket can cause a vacuum leak, leading to rough idling, loss of power, and increased fuel consumption.
Solution: Replace the intake manifold gasket.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1500+
Recalls: None Found

**Timing Chain Tensioners Failure**
Description/Symptoms: Failure of the timing chain tensioners can lead to a rattling noise from the engine, decreased engine performance, and potential catastrophic engine failure.
Solution: Replace the timing chain tensioners and any damaged components.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $3000 - $5000+
Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

* **6-Speed Manual**

**Transmission Gear Oil Leak**
Description/Symptoms: A transmission gear oil leak can manifest as a puddle of fluid under the vehicle or difficulty shifting gears.
Solution: Replace the leaking seal or gasket.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1000+
Recalls: None Found

* **6-Speed Automatic**

**Valve Body Failure**
Description/Symptoms: Failure of the valve body can cause erratic shifting, slipping gears, or complete transmission failure.
Solution: Replace the valve body.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $2000 - $3000+
Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

**Front Upper Control Arm Bushing Failure**
Description/Symptoms: Worn or failed front upper control arm bushings can lead to excessive movement and noise in the suspension, as well as decreased handling and stability.
Solution: Replace the failed bushings.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800+
Recalls: None Found

**Rear Shock Absorber Mount Failure**
Description/Symptoms: Failure of the rear shock absorber mount can cause a clunking noise from the suspension when driving over bumps or rough terrain.
Solution: Replace the failed mount.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

**Battery Drain**
Description/Symptoms: A parasitic drain on the battery can lead to a dead battery and inability to start the vehicle.
Solution: Diagnose the source of the battery drain and repair or replace the affected component.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

**Headlight Condensation**
Description/Symptoms: Condensation inside the headlight assembly can cause fogging or reduced visibility at night.
Solution: Replace the headlight assembly or reseal the existing one.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1000+
Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

**Navigation System Failure**
Description/Symptoms: Failure of the navigation system can result in incorrect or lost directions, as well as other display or functionality issues.
Solution: Update or replace the navigation system.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1500+
Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2006 Aston Martin Vanquish is generally considered a reliable sports car, but it is not immune to some common issues. While the potential engine and transmission problems can be costly to repair, the other reported issues are typically less severe and expensive. Overall, the severity and cost of the reported issues for the Vanquish are comparable to other high-performance vehicles of its era.
