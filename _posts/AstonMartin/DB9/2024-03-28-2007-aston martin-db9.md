**Introduction:**

The 2007 Aston Martin DB9 is a grand tourer powered by a 6.0L V12 engine paired with a 6-speed ZF 6HP26 automatic transmission. With 450 horsepower and 420 lb-ft of torque, the DB9 delivers an exhilarating driving experience. Its sleek design, luxurious interior, and impressive performance have made it a popular choice among enthusiasts. However, like any other vehicle, it has its share of issues.

**Engine**

**6.0L V12**

* **Spark Plug Failure:** Premature spark plug failure can cause engine misfiring, rough idling, and reduced power. Replacing the spark plugs typically resolves the issue.
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
* **Recalls:** None Found

**Transmission**

**6-speed ZF 6HP26 Transmission**

* **Transmission Slippage:** In some cases, the transmission may experience slippage, causing difficulty in shifting gears. Rebuilding or replacing the transmission usually fixes the problem.
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,500 - $5,000+
* **Recalls:** None Found

**Suspension**

* **Front Suspension Noise:** The front suspension may develop a clunking or rattling sound, particularly during turns or over bumps. Replacing worn suspension components, such as control arms or ball joints, typically eliminates the noise.
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
* **Rear Suspension Sagging:** Over time, the rear suspension may sag or settle, affecting handling and ride quality. Replacing the rear springs or shock absorbers usually resolves the issue.
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+

**Electrical**

* **Battery Discharge:** The battery may drain prematurely, leading to difficulty starting the vehicle. Replacing the battery or investigating electrical system issues, such as parasitic draws, is necessary.
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
* **Recalls:** None Found

**Exterior**

* **Door Handle Failure:** The exterior door handles may become loose or unresponsive, making it difficult to open the doors. Replacing the door handles usually resolves the issue.
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
* **Convertible Top Leakage:** Convertibles may experience leaks around the top, especially during rainfall. Replacing weatherstripping or seals typically fixes the issue.
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+

**Interior**

* **Navigation System Malfunction:** The navigation system may freeze, display incorrect information, or fail to operate. Updating the software or replacing the navigation unit usually resolves the issue.
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
* **Sunroof Motor Failure:** The sunroof motor may become inoperable, preventing the sunroof from opening or closing. Replacing the motor usually fixes the issue.
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+

**Summary:**

Overall, the 2007 Aston Martin DB9 is a reliable and enjoyable vehicle. However, it is not immune to issues. Common problems include transmission slippage, suspension noise, and electrical issues. While some of these issues can be minor and inexpensive to fix, others can be more severe and costly to resolve. It's important to have the vehicle regularly serviced and inspected by a qualified mechanic to address any potential problems promptly.
