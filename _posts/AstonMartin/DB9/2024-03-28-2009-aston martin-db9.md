**Introduction**

The 2009 Aston Martin DB9 is a grand tourer powered by a 6.0-liter V12 engine producing 470 horsepower and 443 lb-ft of torque. It comes with a 6-speed automatic transmission. Customers appreciate the DB9's elegant styling, luxurious interior, and powerful engine. However, it has some drawbacks, including its high price, limited practicality, and potential reliability issues.

**Engine**

- **6.0L V12**

- **Intake Manifold Gasket Failure**
    - Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaks, overheating, performance issues
    - Solution: Replace intake manifold gasket
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
    - Recalls: N/A

- **Valve Seat Recession**
    - Description/Symptoms: Loss of compression, rough idle, reduced power
    - Solution: Repair or replace cylinder heads
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $5,000+
    - Recalls: N/A

**Transmission**

- **6-Speed Automatic**

- **Torque Converter Failure**
    - Description/Symptoms: Shuddering, slipping, loss of power
    - Solution: Replace torque converter
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,000+
    - Recalls: N/A

**Suspension**

- **Front Control Arm Bushing Wear**
    - Description/Symptoms: Clunking or knocking noises from front suspension, poor handling
    - Solution: Replace control arm bushings
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    - Recalls: N/A

**Electrical**

- **Battery Drain**
    - Description/Symptoms: Dead battery, electrical system failures
    - Solution: Diagnose and repair electrical system fault causing drain
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    - Recalls: N/A

- **Electrical Switch Failures**
    - Description/Symptoms: Non-functioning windows, mirrors, or lights
    - Solution: Replace defective switch
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    - Recalls: N/A

**Exterior**

- **Door Lock Actuator Malfunction**
    - Description/Symptoms: Difficulty locking or unlocking doors
    - Solution: Replace door lock actuator
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
    - Recalls: N/A

**Interior**

- **Navigation System Malfunction**
    - Description/Symptoms: Inaccurate or non-functioning navigation system
    - Solution: Update or replace navigation software
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    - Recalls: N/A

**Summary**

While the 2009 Aston Martin DB9 is a desirable grand tourer, it is not without its potential issues. The engine and transmission components can be expensive to repair, and electrical and suspension issues can also arise. However, many owners find that the DB9's performance and style make it worth the potential risks.
