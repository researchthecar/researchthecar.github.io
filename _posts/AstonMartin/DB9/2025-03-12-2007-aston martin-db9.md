Alright, here's the breakdown of common issues and complaints for the 2007 Aston Martin DB9, formatted for your website and tailored to the specific engine and transmission options available for that model year.

The 2007 Aston Martin DB9 is a grand tourer celebrated for its elegant design, luxurious interior, and engaging driving experience. It is powered by a 5.9-liter V12 engine that produced 450 horsepower and 420 lb-ft of torque. Most DB9s came equipped with a six-speed automatic transmission. A six-speed manual transmission was also available, but far less common. Owners generally love the DB9's styling, the sound of its V12 engine, and the overall prestige of the brand. Common complaints often center around higher-than-average maintenance costs, the age of some of the technology, and some electrical quirks.

**Engine**

**5.9L V12**

*   **Oil Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks can occur from various locations, including the valve cover gaskets, oil pan gasket, and rear main seal. Symptoms include visible oil drips, oil consumption, and a burning oil smell.
    *   Solution: Replacing the affected gaskets or seals.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,500+ (depending on the location and severity of the leak).
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Coil Pack Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Misfires, rough idling, and a loss of power. Often accompanied by a check engine light.
    *   Solution: Replacing the faulty coil pack(s).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600+ (depending on how many need to be replaced, plus labor).
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Coolant Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Coolant loss, overheating, and a sweet smell emanating from the engine bay. Common leak points include hoses, the water pump, and the radiator.
    *   Solution: Replacing the leaking component (hose, water pump, radiator, etc.)
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+ (depending on the part and labor)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**6-Speed Automatic (Touchtronic 2)**

*   **Valve Body Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Harsh or delayed shifting, slipping between gears, or failure to shift at all. Can trigger a transmission fault warning light.
    *   Solution: Replacing or rebuilding the valve body.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $4,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Torque Converter Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Shuddering or vibrations during acceleration, particularly at lower speeds. Can also cause poor fuel economy.
    *   Solution: Replacing the torque converter.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $3,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**6-Speed Manual**

*   No Issues Found

**Suspension**

*   **Adaptive Damper Issues (if equipped)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Uneven ride quality, clunking noises from the suspension, or a warning light indicating a problem with the adaptive dampers.
    *   Solution: Replacing the faulty damper(s).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $3,000+ (per damper)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Suspension Bushing Wear**
    * Description/Symptoms: Clunking or squeaking noises, especially when going over bumps. Vague steering feel.
    * Solution: Replacing worn bushings (control arm, sway bar, etc.).
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,500+ (depending on which bushings need replacing and the extent of the labor).
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Battery Drain**
    *   Description/Symptoms: A dead battery, especially if the car sits for extended periods. Can be caused by a parasitic draw from a faulty module or accessory.
    *   Solution: Identifying and repairing the source of the parasitic draw.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $1,000+ (diagnosis can be time-consuming).
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Central Locking System Malfunctions**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Doors not locking or unlocking properly, alarm system issues.
    *   Solution: Repairing or replacing faulty door lock actuators, central locking module, or related wiring.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Infotainment System Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Screen malfunctions, navigation problems, or audio system failures. The infotainment system is dated and prone to glitches.
    *   Solution: Repairing or replacing the faulty component. Aftermarket upgrades are also a possibility.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $2,000+ (depending on the issue and whether an aftermarket solution is chosen).
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Door Handle Actuator Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Difficulty opening the doors, requiring multiple attempts or the use of the key.
    *   Solution: Replacing the door handle actuator.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+ (per door)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Navigation System Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: System freezing, glitching, inaccurate information.
    *   Solution: Updating the software, which may not be possible due to the age of the system, or replacing the system.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $2000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Dashboard Leather Shrinkage**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Leather pulling away from the dashboard
    *   Solution: Replacement of the dashboard
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $2000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2007 Aston Martin DB9 presents a mix of potential issues, mostly stemming from its age and the complexity of its systems. Engine oil leaks and electrical gremlins can be annoying and potentially expensive. The automatic transmission can be a source of significant cost if the valve body or torque converter fails. Suspension issues, especially with the adaptive dampers, can also be pricey to address. Overall, prospective buyers should budget for higher-than-average maintenance costs and be prepared to address some of these common issues as they arise. A pre-purchase inspection by a qualified Aston Martin mechanic is highly recommended.
