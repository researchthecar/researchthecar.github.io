The 2014 Aston Martin DB9 is a grand tourer known for its elegant styling, luxurious interior, and exhilarating performance. The 2014 DB9 is powered by a 5.9-liter naturally aspirated V12 engine, which produces 510 horsepower and 457 lb-ft of torque. It is paired with a six-speed Touchtronic 2 automatic transmission. Some owners are drawn to the DB9 for its beautiful design, powerful engine, and the prestige associated with the Aston Martin brand. Conversely, some dislike the relatively outdated infotainment system, higher running costs, and potential for expensive repairs.

**Engine**

**5.9L V12**
*   **Oil Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks can develop from various locations, including valve cover gaskets, oil pan gasket, and rear main seal. Symptoms include visible oil leaks on the ground, a burning oil smell, and low oil levels.
    *   Solution: Replacing the affected gaskets or seals.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+ depending on the location and severity of the leak.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Coil Pack Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Misfires, rough running, reduced power, and a check engine light. This issue may stem from worn or damaged coil packs.
    *   Solution: Replacing the faulty coil pack(s).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+ per coil pack (including labor).
    *   Recalls: None Found

* **Coolant Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaks can occur from hoses, the water pump, or the radiator. Symptoms include a low coolant level, overheating, and visible coolant leaks.
    *   Solution: Replacing the affected hoses, water pump, or radiator.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,500+ depending on the component and labor.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**Touchtronic 2 6-speed Automatic Transmission**

*   **Harsh Shifting**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The Touchtronic 2 transmission can sometimes exhibit harsh or jerky shifting, particularly during downshifts.
    *   Solution: Transmission fluid flush, software update, or valve body repair/replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $3,000+ depending on the complexity of the repair.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Delayed Engagement**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Delayed engagement when shifting from park to drive or reverse, or between gears while driving.
    *   Solution: Transmission fluid flush, software update, or valve body repair/replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $3,000+ depending on the complexity of the repair.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Adaptive Damper Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Failure of the adaptive dampers can lead to a poor ride quality, uneven handling, and suspension warning lights on the dashboard.
    *   Solution: Replacing the faulty damper(s).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $2,000+ per damper (including labor).
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Suspension Bushing Wear**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Worn suspension bushings can cause clunking noises, vague steering, and uneven tire wear.
    *   Solution: Replacing the worn suspension bushings.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+ depending on the number and location of bushings replaced.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Battery Drain**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Excessive battery drain can lead to a dead battery, difficulty starting the car, and electrical malfunctions. This can be due to a faulty module not going to sleep, or an old battery.
    *   Solution: Identifying and addressing the source of the drain, replacing the battery.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+ depending on the complexity of the issue.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Infotainment System Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The infotainment system can suffer from software glitches, freezing, and navigation malfunctions.
    *   Solution: Software updates or replacement of the head unit.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $2,000+ depending on the nature of the issue.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Door Handle Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The door handles, particularly the electronic release mechanisms, can fail, making it difficult to open the doors.
    *   Solution: Replacing the faulty door handle mechanism.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+ per door handle.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Navigation Screen Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The navigation screen can fail or display distorted images.
    *   Solution: Replacing the navigation screen.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $2,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Air Conditioning Malfunctions**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Problems with the climate control system, such as failure to blow cold air, uneven temperature distribution, or blower motor issues.
    *   Solution: Repair or replacement of faulty components (compressor, condenser, evaporator, blower motor).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $2,000+ depending on the component and labor.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2014 Aston Martin DB9 presents a mix of potential issues, ranging from relatively minor electrical glitches to more significant concerns involving the engine, transmission, and suspension. Repair costs can vary widely depending on the specific issue and the extent of the damage. While some issues are relatively common, the DB9's overall reliability can be improved with regular maintenance and proactive attention to potential problems. The severity of issues ranges from minor inconveniences to costly repairs, making pre-purchase inspections and diligent maintenance crucial for prospective owners.
