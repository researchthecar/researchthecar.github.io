The 2014 Aston Martin DB9 is a high-performance luxury sports car known for its elegant styling, powerful engine, and responsive handling. It is powered by a 6.0-liter V12 engine that produces 510 horsepower and 457 lb-ft of torque. The DB9 is available with a 6-speed manual or a 6-speed automatic transmission. While the DB9 is generally a reliable vehicle, there are some common issues that owners have reported.

**Engine**

* **6.0L V12**
    * **Intake manifold resonance**
        * Description/Symptoms: A loud rattling noise at low RPMs
        * Solution: Replace the intake manifold
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,000+
        * Recalls: None Found
    * **Lower intercooler hose failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: Coolant leak, engine overheating
        * Solution: Replace the lower intercooler hose
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

* **6-Speed Manual**
    * **Gearbox whine**
        * Description/Symptoms: A whining noise when shifting gears
        * Solution: Replace the gearbox bearings
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
        * Recalls: None Found
* **6-Speed Automatic**
    * **Transmission shudder**
        * Description/Symptoms: A shudder or vibration when shifting gears
        * Solution: Update the transmission software or replace the transmission valve
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Front suspension creaking**
    * Description/Symptoms: A creaking noise from the front suspension when driving over bumps
    * Solution: Replace the front suspension bushings
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Rear suspension sagging**
    * Description/Symptoms: The rear of the car sits lower than the front
    * Solution: Replace the rear suspension springs or shocks
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Battery drain**
    * Description/Symptoms: The battery drains quickly, even when the car is not in use
    * Solution: Replace the faulty battery or alternator
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Electrical system glitches**
    * Description/Symptoms: Intermittent electrical issues, such as flickering lights or power window problems
    * Solution: Diagnose and repair the faulty electrical component
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Headlight condensation**
    * Description/Symptoms: Moisture buildup inside the headlights
    * Solution: Replace the headlight seals
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Door handle sticking**
    * Description/Symptoms: The door handles stick or become difficult to open
    * Solution: Lubricate or replace the door handle mechanism
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Infotainment system malfunction**
    * Description/Symptoms: The infotainment system freezes, crashes, or has other issues
    * Solution: Update the software or replace the infotainment system
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Heater or air conditioning failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The heater or air conditioning does not work properly
    * Solution: Diagnose and repair the faulty component
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

Overall, the 2014 Aston Martin DB9 is a reliable vehicle, but it is not without its issues. The most common problems reported by owners include intake manifold resonance, lower intercooler hose failure, gearbox whine, and transmission shudder. While some of these issues can be relatively inexpensive to fix, others can be more costly. It is important to have any issues diagnosed and repaired by a qualified mechanic to ensure the safety and reliability of the vehicle.
