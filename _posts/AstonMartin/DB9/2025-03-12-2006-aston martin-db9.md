Here's a comprehensive breakdown of common issues and complaints associated with the 2006 Aston Martin DB9, formatted for your website.

The 2006 Aston Martin DB9 is a grand tourer renowned for its elegant design, luxurious interior, and exhilarating performance. Powered by a naturally aspirated 5.9L V12 engine producing 450 horsepower and 420 lb-ft of torque, the DB9 provides a thrilling driving experience. Transmission options included a 6-speed manual and a 6-speed automatic. While owners often praise the DB9's beautiful styling and potent engine, common complaints revolve around its maintenance costs, electrical quirks, and the age-related issues that often plague high-end vehicles of this era.

**Engine**

**5.9L V12**

*   **Oil Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks can develop from various locations, including valve cover gaskets, rear main seal, and timing chain cover. Symptoms include visible oil drips, a burning oil smell, and low oil levels.
    *   Solution: Replacing the affected gaskets and seals is the typical solution. In severe cases, the timing chain cover may need to be resealed.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $2,000+ depending on the location and severity of the leak.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Coil Pack Failures**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Ignition coil failure, leading to misfires, rough running, and a check engine light.
    *   Solution: Replacing the faulty coil pack(s).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $400+ per coil pack (including labor).
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Cooling System Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Overheating due to failing thermostats, water pumps, or radiator leaks.
    *   Solution: Replacing the faulty component and flushing the cooling system.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,500+ depending on the component.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**6-Speed Automatic (Touchtronic 2)**

*   **Transmission Slippage/Harsh Shifting**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Slipping between gears, particularly during upshifts, and harsh or jerky shifts. This can be caused by worn clutch packs or valve body issues.
    *   Solution: In some cases, a transmission fluid flush and filter change may alleviate the issue. However, a valve body rebuild or complete transmission overhaul is often required.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 (fluid flush) - $5,000+ (overhaul).
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Torque Converter Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Shuddering or vibration at low speeds, especially when accelerating from a stop. Can also cause stalling.
    *   Solution: Replacing the torque converter.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Adaptive Damper Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The DB9 features an adaptive damping system.  Malfunctions can lead to a harsh or bouncy ride, uneven handling, and warning lights on the dashboard.
    *   Solution: Diagnosing the specific issue (faulty sensor, damaged damper) and replacing the affected component.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+ per damper.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Bushings and Ball Joint Wear**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Clunking noises, vague steering, and uneven tire wear. Due to the car's age, rubber components in the suspension wear out.
    *   Solution: Replacing worn bushings and ball joints.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,500+ depending on the extent of the work.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Battery Drain**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The DB9 is known for electrical gremlins that can cause battery drain, especially when the car is not driven regularly.
    *   Solution: Identifying and addressing the parasitic drain. This may involve checking the alternator, starter, and other electrical components.  Sometimes, aftermarket accessories are the culprit.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+ depending on the source of the drain.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Central Locking System Problems**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Failure of the central locking system, making it difficult to lock or unlock the doors.
    *   Solution: Replacing the faulty door lock actuator or repairing wiring issues.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600+ per door.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Window Regulator Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Windows failing to operate properly, often getting stuck or moving slowly.
    *   Solution: Replacing the faulty window regulator.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $700+ per window.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Door Handle Mechanism Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Door handles becoming difficult or impossible to use, requiring excessive force to open the doors. This is a common issue due to the complex design of the handles.
    *   Solution: Repairing or replacing the door handle mechanism.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $1,000+ per door.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Navigation System/Infotainment Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The original infotainment system is dated and prone to malfunctions. The navigation system may be inaccurate or unresponsive.
    *   Solution: Replacing the unit with an aftermarket system or attempting to repair the original (often unreliable).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 (repair) - $2,000+ (replacement).
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Sticky Buttons**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Many of the interior buttons and switches have a rubberized coating that deteriorates over time, becoming sticky and unpleasant to touch.
    *   Solution: Cleaning the buttons with a specialized cleaner or replacing the affected components.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 (cleaning supplies) - $500+ (parts replacement).
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2006 Aston Martin DB9 presents a mix of potential issues, ranging from relatively minor electrical quirks to more significant mechanical problems. Engine oil leaks, transmission issues, and suspension wear are common concerns due to the age and complexity of the vehicle. Repair costs can be substantial, particularly for engine and transmission work. Prospective buyers should factor in these potential expenses when considering a DB9 and prioritize a thorough pre-purchase inspection by a qualified Aston Martin mechanic. Regular maintenance and proactive repairs are crucial to preserving the value and reliability of this iconic grand tourer.
