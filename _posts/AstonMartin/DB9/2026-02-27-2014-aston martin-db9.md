# 2014 Aston Martin DB9

### Overview
The 2014 Aston Martin DB9 represents a pinnacle of British luxury grand touring, blending potent performance with exquisite craftsmanship. As a high-performance exotic, its reliability is generally considered good for its class, though specific components can be prone to issues typical of complex, low-volume vehicles. Owners should expect a higher maintenance cost compared to mainstream vehicles. This analysis focuses on documented, non-wear-and-tear related issues that may affect the vehicle's core functionality and ownership experience. While the DB9 offers a thrilling driving experience, understanding potential reliability concerns is crucial for prospective and current owners.

---


## Engine

### 5.9L V12
**Ignition Coil Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Misfires, rough idling, check engine light, reduced engine power.
- **Solution:** Replacement of faulty ignition coil(s).
- **Cost:** $300 - $600 per coil

**Coolant Leaks (Hoses/Pipes)**
- **Symptoms:** Visible coolant puddles under the car, overheating, low coolant level warning.
- **Solution:** Replacement of damaged coolant hoses or pipes.
- **Cost:** $400 - $800

**Timing Chain Tensioner Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Rattling noise from the engine, especially on startup; potential for severe engine damage if ignored.
- **Solution:** Replacement of timing chain tensioners and potentially the timing chain itself.
- **Cost:** $2,500 - $5,000+


## Transmission

### Touchtronic 2 (6-speed Automatic)
**Torque Converter Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Slipping gears, shuddering during acceleration, delayed engagement.
- **Solution:** Rebuild or replacement of the torque converter.
- **Cost:** $1,500 - $3,000

**Transmission Fluid Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Leaking fluid, potential for shifting problems, overheating.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of seals and gaskets.
- **Cost:** $300 - $700


## Suspension

### Adaptive Damping System
**Shock Absorber Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Harsh ride, excessive bouncing, clunking noises over bumps, warning lights on the dashboard.
- **Solution:** Replacement of faulty adaptive shock absorbers.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1,500 per corner


## Electrical

### Infotainment System
**Screen Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** Flickering screen, unresponsive touch controls, system freezes.
- **Solution:** Software updates or replacement of the infotainment unit.
- **Cost:** $1,000 - $2,500


### Central Locking System
**Actuator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Doors not locking or unlocking consistently, individual door issues.
- **Solution:** Replacement of faulty door lock actuators.
- **Cost:** $200 - $500 per door

### Summary Verdict
The 2014 Aston Martin DB9, while a magnificent grand tourer, exhibits some reliability concerns typical of exotic sports cars. The 5.9L V12 engine can experience issues like ignition coil failures and, more significantly, timing chain tensioner problems which require costly repairs. The automatic transmission, while generally robust, can suffer from torque converter issues. Electrical gremlins, particularly with the infotainment system and central locking, are also noted. The convertible roof mechanism on Volante models is a potential source of expensive hydraulic leaks. Owners should budget for higher maintenance and repair costs, and regular inspections are recommended to catch potential issues early.
