**2011 Aston Martin DB9**

The 2011 Aston Martin DB9 is a luxury grand tourer powered by a naturally aspirated 6.0-liter V12 engine that produces 470 horsepower and 443 lb-ft of torque. It is paired with a 6-speed manual or 6-speed automatic transmission and features a rear-wheel-drive system.

The DB9 is known for its sleek and timeless design, powerful engine, and luxurious interior. However, it also has its share of common problems that owners should be aware of.

**Engine**

* **AM11 V12**

**Spark Plug Failure**
Description/Symptoms: Misfires, rough idling, loss of power
Solution: Replace spark plugs
Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
Recalls: None Found

**Intake Manifold Gaskets Leak**
Description/Symptoms: Oil or coolant leaks, rough idling, loss of power
Solution: Replace intake manifold gaskets
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1000+
Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

* **6-Speed Manual**

**Clutch Wear**
Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears, slipping, shuddering
Solution: Replace clutch assembly
Estimated Cost to Fix: $1500 - $2500+
Recalls: None Found

* **6-Speed Automatic**

**Torque Converter Failure**
Description/Symptoms: Shuddering, slipping, loss of power
Solution: Replace torque converter
Estimated Cost to Fix: $2500 - $3500+
Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

**Front Suspension Strut Mount Failure**
Description/Symptoms: Clunking noises, vibration, poor handling
Solution: Replace strut mounts
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $700+
Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

**Battery Discharge**
Description/Symptoms: Car won't start, electrical system malfunctions
Solution: Replace battery
Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300+
Recalls: None Found

**ECU Failure**
Description/Symptoms: Engine misfires, loss of power, check engine light
Solution: Replace ECU
Estimated Cost to Fix: $1000 - $2000+
Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*Brake Light Switch Failure*
Description/Symptoms: Brake lights do not illuminate, cruise control not functional
Solution: Replace brake light switch
Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Infotainment System Malfunction**
Description/Symptoms: Touchscreen unresponsive, Bluetooth connectivity issues, audio problems
Solution: Update software, replace infotainment module
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1500+
Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2011 Aston Martin DB9 is a desirable luxury car, but it is not without its problems. The engine and transmission can experience serious and expensive issues, while the suspension, electrical, and exterior components are also prone to failures. The severity and cost of these issues should be considered before purchasing a used DB9.
