# 2021 Mazda CX-5 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2021 Mazda CX-5 is widely praised for its upscale interior, engaging handling, and sleek exterior styling, making it a popular choice in the compact SUV segment. While the majority of owners enjoy a dependable ownership experience, certain recurring mechanical and electrical concerns have been reported to federal safety authorities. Prospective buyers should carefully review service records and inspect powertrain components, electronics, and standard mechanical systems prior to purchase.

---

## Engine

### 2.5L SkyActiv-G 4-Cylinder

This naturally aspirated 2.5-liter four-cylinder engine produces approximately 187 horsepower and 186 lb-ft of torque, offering adequate daily acceleration and solid fuel economy. It is generally robust, though buyers should monitor fluid levels closely for signs of oil consumption.

**Excessive Oil Consumption**
- **Symptoms:** Low oil warning lights illuminating unexpectedly and a noticeable drop in oil levels between scheduled service intervals, sometimes accompanied by a burning smell.
- **Solution:** Monitor oil levels frequently between changes and consult a qualified technician for diagnostic testing if rapid oil loss occurs.

**Engine Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** Unexpected engine hesitation, power loss, or complete stalling while operating the vehicle.
- **Solution:** Perform a comprehensive diagnostic scan of the fuel, ignition, and sensor systems to identify underlying electrical or mechanical faults.

### 2.5L SkyActiv-G Turbo 4-Cylinder

The turbocharged variant boosts output up to 250 horsepower and 320 lb-ft of torque (on premium fuel), delivering significantly punchier performance across the power band. It shares the same core architecture as the naturally aspirated version and requires attentive maintenance.

## Transmission

### SkyActiv-Drive 6-Speed Automatic

This traditional six-speed automatic transmission pairs with both engine options and is widely regarded for its smooth, crisp shifts and responsive nature. It avoids the reliability pitfalls of many contemporary CVTs, contributing positively to the vehicle's long-term outlook.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

The braking system provides confident stopping power under normal conditions, but pad and rotor longevity can vary based on regional driving environments.

**Premature Brake Wear and Rotor Rust**
- **Symptoms:** Early squealing from brake pads or heavy surface rust accumulation on brake rotors at relatively low mileage.
- **Solution:** Have a certified mechanic measure remaining pad thickness and rotor condition during a pre-purchase inspection.

## Electrical

### Infotainment System

The cabin electronics manage infotainment, driver aids, and vehicle connectivity. While feature-rich, software glitches can occasionally disrupt functionality.

**Screen Freezing or Blanking**
- **Symptoms:** The central infotainment display goes completely black or freezes during operation, temporarily disabling rearview camera feeds and audio controls.
- **Solution:** Perform a system reboot or software update; persistent hardware failure may require module replacement.

## Exterior

### Door Locks and Mirrors

Exterior body accessories and power mechanisms generally function well, though occasional mechanical or electronic binding has been noted.

**Door Lock and Mirror Actuator Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Power door locks fail to respond to key fob inputs, or power-folding exterior mirrors fail to unfold upon unlocking the vehicle.
- **Solution:** Inspect door lock actuators and mirror motors for binding, electrical failure, or weather-related freezing, and replace components as necessary.

## Summary Verdict

The 2021 Mazda CX-5 stands out as a refined, engaging, and generally dependable compact SUV, though buyers should approach with normal due diligence. Pay close attention to engine oil levels, test all infotainment and electronic features thoroughly, and ensure a qualified mechanic inspects the brakes and powertrain during a pre-purchase evaluation.
