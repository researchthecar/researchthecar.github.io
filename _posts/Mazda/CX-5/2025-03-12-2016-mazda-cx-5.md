Here is a comprehensive list of common issues and complaints for the 2016 Mazda CX-5, categorized by component, engine/transmission options, symptoms, solutions, estimated costs, and recalls.

The 2016 Mazda CX-5 is a compact SUV known for its sporty handling, fuel efficiency, and upscale interior. It came with two engine options: a 2.0-liter four-cylinder engine producing 155 horsepower and 150 lb-ft of torque, and a more powerful 2.5-liter four-cylinder engine generating 184 horsepower and 185 lb-ft of torque. Both engines were available with either a six-speed manual transmission (only on the 2.0L FWD) or a six-speed automatic transmission. People generally liked the CX-5 for its responsive steering, comfortable ride, and stylish design. Common complaints included road noise and a somewhat small cargo area compared to competitors.

**Engine**

**2.0L Four-Cylinder**

*   **No Issues Found**

**2.5L Four-Cylinder**

*   **Excessive Oil Consumption**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners reported higher than expected oil consumption, requiring frequent top-offs between oil changes. Symptoms include low oil level warning lights and potential engine damage if left unaddressed.
    *   Solution: Mazda issued a TSB (Technical Service Bulletin) addressing this issue, but it did not specify the solution. The solution usually involves replacing the valve stem seals or piston rings. In some severe cases, a complete engine replacement might be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $5000+ (depending on the severity and repair needed)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**Six-Speed Automatic Transmission**

*   **Harsh Shifting/Hesitation**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners reported experiencing harsh or jerky shifting, particularly between lower gears. Others have noted hesitation or delayed engagement when accelerating from a stop.
    *   Solution: In some cases, a software update to the transmission control module (TCM) can resolve the issue. In other cases, replacement of the transmission is required.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $5000+ (depending on the fix)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Rear Shock Absorber Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Premature failure and leaking of the rear shock absorbers. This can cause a bouncy or unstable ride, especially over bumps.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the rear shock absorbers.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Freezing/Rebooting**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The Mazda Connect infotainment system may freeze, become unresponsive, or randomly reboot. This can affect navigation, audio, and other vehicle functions.
    *   Solution: Sometimes a software update can resolve the issue. In other cases, the infotainment unit may need to be replaced.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 - $1000
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Backup Camera Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The backup camera may intermittently fail to display an image or show a distorted view.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the backup camera.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **No Issues Found**

**Interior**

*   **Rattling/Squeaking Noises**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Reports of various rattling or squeaking noises emanating from the dashboard, door panels, or rear cargo area.
    *   Solution: Identifying and securing the source of the noise, which may involve tightening screws, applying felt tape, or replacing worn clips.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $300+

*   **Sunroof Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported issues with the sunroof, including difficulty opening or closing, leaking, or unusual noises.
    *   Solution: Repairing or replacing the sunroof motor, seals, or track.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2016 Mazda CX-5 generally enjoys a good reputation, but owners have reported issues ranging from minor interior noises to more significant concerns such as excessive oil consumption and transmission problems. The severity and cost of these issues vary, but potential buyers should be aware of these common complaints and factor them into their purchasing decision. Addressing these issues promptly can prevent more extensive and costly repairs down the road.
