# 2020 Mazda CX-5 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2020 Mazda CX-5 is widely praised for its upscale interior, engaging driving dynamics, and sleek styling, making it a popular choice in the compact SUV segment. However, prospective buyers should review common owner complaints regarding electrical glitches, safety system behavior, and occasional powertrain concerns. While overall mechanical reliability is generally strong, checking service history and verifying whether infotainment or brake updates have been performed is recommended.

---

## Engine

### 2.5L 4-Cylinder (Naturally Aspirated)

The base 2.5-liter naturally aspirated four-cylinder engine produces approximately 187 horsepower and 186 lb-ft of torque, offering adequate daily acceleration and good fuel economy. It is generally robust, though rare reports indicate potential upper engine component issues.

**Cylinder Head Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Engine performance issues and potential internal damage reported around moderate mileage.
- **Solution:** Inspection by a qualified mechanic; may require cylinder head replacement or repair.

### 2.5L Turbo 4-Cylinder

The available 2.5-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine generates up to 250 horsepower and 320 lb-ft of torque on premium fuel, providing much punchier acceleration and passing power. It adds strong performance appeal, though buyers should verify regular oil change maintenance.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

Equipped with a traditional 6-speed automatic transmission paired with front-wheel or all-wheel drive, the CX-5 avoids the sluggishness of many CVTs. Most owners experience smooth operation, though isolated complaints cite rough shifting or unusual noises.

**Transmission Hesitation and Chirp**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle experiences abnormal shifting, jerking during acceleration, or a distinct one-time chirp noise.
- **Solution:** Have the transmission control module updated or inspected by a dealership for internal wear.

## Brakes

### Smart Brake Support (SBS) and Collision Avoidance

Advanced driver assistance features enhance safety but can occasionally trigger false warnings or unintended emergency braking events.

**Unexpected Automatic Braking**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle's forward collision avoidance system unexpectedly applies harsh braking without an actual obstacle present.
- **Solution:** Check for dealer sensor recalibrations or software updates addressing forward-sensing camera parameters.

## Electrical

### Infotainment System

The Connect infotainment system offers user-friendly controls, but the display screen is prone to hardware glitches and erratic touch behavior over time.

**Ghost Touching and Screen Freezes**
- **Symptoms:** The touchscreen registers un-commanded inputs, opens menus randomly, makes unintended phone calls, or freezes completely.
- **Solution:** Software updates or replacement of the touchscreen display unit by a dealer.

## Summary Verdict

The 2020 Mazda CX-5 remains a highly attractive used compact SUV choice thanks to its upscale cabin and engaging driving dynamics. Buyers should thoroughly test the infotainment touchscreen for ghost inputs and ensure all safety system recalls or updates have been completed. Overall, with a proper pre-purchase inspection, it serves as a stylish and dependable daily driver.
