**Introduction:**

The 2021 Mazda CX-5 is a compact SUV known for its premium interior, sharp handling, and excellent safety ratings. It comes with a choice of two engine options: a 2.5L four-cylinder with 187 horsepower and 186 lb-ft of torque, and a 2.5L turbocharged four-cylinder with 250 horsepower and 320 lb-ft of torque. Transmission options include a six-speed automatic and a six-speed manual for the non-turbo engine, and a six-speed automatic for the turbo engine.

**Engine**

**2.5L Naturally Aspirated Four-Cylinder**

* **Excessive Oil Consumption:** Some owners have experienced excessive oil consumption, requiring frequent oil top-offs. The issue is attributed to worn piston rings.
    * Solution: Engine rebuild or replacement
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $5,000+
    * Recalls: NHTSA ID 22V-119

**2.5L Turbocharged Four-Cylinder**

* **Turbocharger Failure:** Premature turbocharger failure has been reported, leading to reduced engine power and increased oil consumption. The issue affects early production models.
    * Solution: Turbocharger replacement
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**Six-Speed Manual Transmission**

* **Gear Grinding:** Difficulty shifting gears, especially from 1st to 2nd, is reported in some vehicles equipped with the manual transmission.
    * Solution: Transmission synchronization repair
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Six-Speed Automatic Transmission**

* **Slow or No Response:** Delays in gear shifting or complete lack of response have been experienced. The issue is often related to the transmission control module (TCM).
    * Solution: TCM reprogramming or replacement
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Front Suspension Noise:** Some owners have reported a clunking or rattling noise coming from the front suspension. The issue is attributed to worn suspension bushings.
    * Solution: Bushings replacement
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain:** Excessive battery drain has been reported in some vehicles, even when the vehicle is turned off. The issue is attributed to a defective parasitic drain.
    * Solution: Battery replacement and parasitic drain repair
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Cruise Control Failure:** In some cases, the cruise control system may fail to engage or disengage properly. The issue is related to a faulty cruise control switch.
    * Solution: Cruise control switch replacement
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Wind Noise:** Some owners have reported excessive wind noise coming from around the windshield and side mirrors. The issue may be due to poor sealing.
    * Solution: Windshield or mirror adjustment
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $150+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Dashboard Rattling:** Some owners have experienced a rattling noise coming from the dashboard area. The issue is often related to loose interior panels.
    * Solution: Dashboard panel tightening
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Infotainment System Lag:** Occasional lag or freezing of the infotainment system has been reported. The issue may be due to software glitches.
    * Solution: Software update
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 - $200+ (if not under warranty)
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

While the 2021 Mazda CX-5 is a generally reliable and well-rated vehicle, it is not without its issues. The most common problems reported by owners include excessive oil consumption, turbocharger failure, slow or no transmission response, and excessive wind noise. The severity and cost of these issues vary, with some being relatively minor and affordable to fix, while others can be more expensive and require major repairs.
