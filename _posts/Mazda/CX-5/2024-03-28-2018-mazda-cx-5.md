**Introduction:**

The 2018 Mazda CX-5 is a compact SUV known for its stylish design, comfortable interior, and sporty driving dynamics. It is available with two engine options: a 2.5L four-cylinder engine producing 187 horsepower and 186 lb-ft of torque, and a 2.5L turbocharged four-cylinder engine producing 250 horsepower and 310 lb-ft of torque. Both engines can be paired with either a six-speed manual or six-speed automatic transmission.

While many owners have been satisfied with the CX-5, there have been some reported issues with the vehicle.

**Engine**

* **2.5L Four-Cylinder Engine**
    * **Excessive Oil Consumption:** Some owners have reported excessive oil consumption, leading to low oil levels and potential engine damage. The issue may be caused by a faulty piston ring design or excessive blow-by.
        * **Solution:** Engine rebuild or replacement.
        * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $3,000 - $8,000+
        * **Recalls:** None Found
* **2.5L Turbocharged Four-Cylinder Engine**
    * **Turbocharger Failure:** Some owners have experienced premature turbocharger failures, resulting in loss of power and potential engine damage. The issue may be caused by faulty materials or excessive heat.
        * **Solution:** Turbocharger replacement.
        * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $1,500 - $3,000+
        * **Recalls:** None Found

**Transmission**

* **Six-Speed Manual Transmission**
    * **Grinding Noises When Shifting:** Some owners have reported grinding noises when shifting gears, particularly when the transmission is cold. The issue may be caused by a faulty synchronizer or other internal component.
        * **Solution:** Transmission rebuild or replacement.
        * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $1,000 - $2,500+
        * **Recalls:** None Found
* **Six-Speed Automatic Transmission**
    * **Delayed or Hesitant Shifts:** Some owners have experienced delayed or hesitant shifts, especially during acceleration. The issue may be caused by a faulty valve body, solenoid, or other electronic component.
        * **Solution:** Transmission fluid flush, software update, or transmission rebuild.
        * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $200 - $1,000+
        * **Recalls:** None Found

**Suspension**

* **Rear Suspension:** Some owners have reported excessive wear and premature failure of the rear suspension components, such as the control arms and bushings. The issue may be caused by poor design or inadequate materials.
        * **Solution:** Replacement of affected components.
        * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $500 - $1,500+
        * **Recalls:** None Found

**Electrical**

* **Electrical Gremlins:** Some owners have experienced various electrical issues, such as flickering lights, malfunctioning sensors, and intermittent power loss. The issue may be caused by faulty wiring harnesses or electronic components.
        * **Solution:** Diagnosis and repair of affected components.
        * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $100 - $1,000+
        * **Recalls:** None Found

**Exterior**

* **Panel Gap Issues:** Some owners have reported inconsistent panel gaps between the body panels, such as the hood and fenders. The issue may be caused by poor manufacturing tolerances or assembly errors.
        * **Solution:** Panel adjustment or replacement.
        * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $200 - $1,000+
        * **Recalls:** None Found

**Interior**

* **Climate Control Malfunction:** Some owners have experienced problems with the climate control system, such as inconsistent temperature control or malfunctioning buttons. The issue may be caused by a faulty climate control module or temperature sensor.
        * **Solution:** Climate control module replacement or sensor recalibration.
        * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $300 - $900+
        * **Recalls:** None Found

**Summary**

While the 2018 Mazda CX-5 offers a compelling combination of style, performance, and practicality, it has not been without its issues. The most significant concerns include excessive oil consumption, premature turbocharger failures, and electrical gremlins. However, it is important to note that not all CX-5s will experience these problems, and the severity and cost of these issues can vary widely. Overall, the CX-5 remains a solid choice for those seeking a compact SUV with sporty handling and a premium interior, but prospective buyers should be aware of the potential issues before making a purchasing decision.
