# 2018 Mazda CX-5 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2018 Mazda CX-5 is widely praised for its premium interior, engaging driving dynamics, and stylish design. However, used car buyers should carefully evaluate powertrain durability and electronic quirks before purchasing. While most owners enjoy a refined and reliable crossover, certain model-year specific mechanical issues require close inspection.

---

## Engine

### 2.5L 4-Cylinder (SkyActiv-G)

The standard 2.5-liter naturally aspirated four-cylinder engine produces 187 horsepower and 186 lb-ft of torque, offering adequate daily performance and commendable fuel economy. While generally smooth and responsive, used buyers should thoroughly inspect the engine bay for signs of coolant leaks or cylinder head issues.

**Cylinder Head Cracking and Coolant Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** White smoke coming from the engine compartment, unexplained coolant loss, and a burning smell entering the cabin once the engine reaches normal operating temperature.
- **Solution:** Requires professional inspection and, in severe cases, replacement of the cylinder head assembly.

**Head Gasket Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Overheating, sweet-smelling exhaust smoke, coolant mixing with engine oil, and illuminated warning lights.
- **Solution:** Diagnostic testing for combustion leaks; requires head gasket replacement or engine rebuilding if internal damage has occurred.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

The traditional 6-speed automatic transmission pairs well with the SkyActiv engine lineup, avoiding the rubber-band feel of contemporary CVTs. It is generally robust, though occasional hesitation or shifting glitches have been reported by owners.

**Delayed Shifting and Acceleration Hesitation**
- **Symptoms:** Engine RPMs flare or rev during acceleration without a corresponding increase in vehicle speed, leading to sluggish response.
- **Solution:** Check transmission fluid levels and condition; update powertrain control modules or have a certified transmission specialist inspect internal components.

## Electrical

### Infotainment and Touch Screen

The interior electronics provide modern connectivity features like smartphone integration, but early Mazda Connect screens are prone to touchscreen digitizer failures and software glitches.

**Ghost Touch and Screen Delamination**
- **Symptoms:** The infotainment display registers phantom touch inputs, randomly changing radio stations, adjusting volume to maximum, or altering navigation settings independently.
- **Solution:** Inspect the display screen laminate for degradation and replace the touchscreen digitizer or infotainment unit if necessary.

## Exterior

### Sunroof and Body Trim

Exterior styling remains a major selling point for this generation, though minor build-quality flaws regarding exterior trim pieces and sunroof durability warrant a careful pre-purchase inspection.

**Spontaneous Sunroof Shattering**
- **Symptoms:** The glass sunroof panel unexpectedly shatters or blows out while driving at highway speeds without prior impact.
- **Solution:** Replace the damaged glass assembly and inspect the surrounding frame for alignment or pressure issues.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V426000: Air Bags:Side/Window](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mazda&model=CX-5&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** In the event of a side impact or rollover crash, the side curtain air bags may not properly unfold during deployment. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 226, "Ejection Mitigation." If the air bags do not deploy properly in a crash, it can increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Mazda will notify owners, and dealers will replace the curtain air bags, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V497000: Engine](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mazda&model=CX-5&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** A software error in the powertrain control module (PCM) may cause the engine to stall. An unexpected stall increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Mazda will notify owners, and dealers will reprogram the PCM software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V875000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mazda&model=CX-5&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** Fuel pump failure may cause an engine stall, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the fuel pumps, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

When shopping for a used 2018 Mazda CX-5, prioritize vehicles with a documented service history and verify that all recall and technical updates have been completed. Ensure a trusted mechanic thoroughly checks the cooling system and cylinder head area for leaks, and test all infotainment and driver-assistance features during your test drive to confirm proper operation.
