**Introduction to the 2022 Mazda CX-5**

The 2022 Mazda CX-5 is a compact crossover SUV that has garnered praise for its stylish design, efficient performance, and comfortable interior. It is available with a choice of two gasoline engines: a 2.5-liter naturally aspirated engine and a turbocharged 2.5-liter engine. Both engines can be paired with a 6-speed automatic transmission or a 6-speed manual transmission. The naturally aspirated engine produces 187 horsepower and 186 lb-ft of torque, while the turbocharged engine produces 227 horsepower and 310 lb-ft of torque.

**Interior**

**Infotainment System**

**Issue:** Occasional freezing or glitches
**Description/Symptoms:** The infotainment system may freeze or experience glitches, causing it to become unresponsive or display incorrect information.
**Solution:** Software updates or system resets may resolve the issue.
**Estimated Cost to Fix:** $0 - $200+
**Recalls:** None Found

**Steering Wheel Audio Controls**

**Issue:** Buttons may become unresponsive or sticky
**Description/Symptoms:** The steering wheel audio controls may fail to respond to inputs or become sticky, making it difficult to adjust volume or change tracks.
**Solution:** Replacement of the affected buttons or the entire steering wheel control unit.
**Estimated Cost to Fix:** $200 - $500+
**Recalls:** None Found

**Climate Control System**

**Issue:** Malfunctioning sensors or actuators
**Description/Symptoms:** The climate control system may fail to regulate temperature or airflow correctly due to faulty sensors or actuators.
**Solution:** Replacement of the affected sensors or actuators.
**Estimated Cost to Fix:** $150 - $300+
**Recalls:** None Found

**Summary**

Overall, the 2022 Mazda CX-5 has a good reliability record with relatively few reported issues. The most common issues reported are related to the infotainment system, steering wheel audio controls, and climate control system. These issues are typically not severe and can be resolved at a reasonable cost.
