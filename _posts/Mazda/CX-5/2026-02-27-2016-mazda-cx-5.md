# 2016 Mazda CX-5 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2016 Mazda CX-5 is widely praised for its engaging driving dynamics, upscale cabin feel, and strong fuel economy. However, prospective used-car buyers should review common owner complaints concerning electrical glitches, exterior lighting failures, and occasional powertrain hesitations. While generally reliable when properly maintained, checking for completed safety campaigns and functional electronic accessories is strongly recommended before purchase.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L SkyActiv-G 4-Cylinder

The base 2.0-liter naturally aspirated four-cylinder engine produces approximately 155 horsepower and 150 lb-ft of torque. It offers solid fuel efficiency and reliable everyday operation, though performance is leisurely when the vehicle is heavily loaded.

### 2.5L SkyActiv-G 4-Cylinder

The larger 2.5-liter naturally aspirated four-cylinder engine delivers 184 horsepower and 185 lb-ft of torque. It provides more confident acceleration for highway passing and is generally durable, though occasional emissions and sensor issues have been reported.

**Catalytic Converter and Sensor Failures**
- **Symptoms:** Illuminated check engine light accompanied by oxygen sensor faults or catalytic converter degradation.
- **Solution:** Diagnose fault codes, replace failed oxygen sensors, or install a replacement catalytic converter as necessary.
- **Cost:** Variable depending on parts and labor

## Transmission

### SkyActiv-Drive 6-Speed Automatic

The six-speed traditional automatic transmission pairs well with both engine options, delivering crisp and responsive gear changes. While mechanically robust, some owners report hesitation or unusual noise requiring professional inspection.

**Delayed Engagement and Harsh Shifting**
- **Symptoms:** Significant lag or hesitation when shifting from park or reverse into drive, sometimes resulting in a sudden lurch or roll-back.
- **Solution:** Inspect transmission fluid condition, check for updated powertrain control module software, or service internal valve body components.
- **Cost:** Varies by repair complexity

**Unintended Neutral Shift or Whining Noise**
- **Symptoms:** Audible whining noises from the transaxle during acceleration or cruising, alongside occasional RPM flares or unexpected drops into neutral.
- **Solution:** Inspect the transaxle for mechanical wear, check fluid levels, and consult a professional transmission specialist.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

The braking system provides reliable stopping power, though rear brake components can experience premature wear or caliper sticking if not properly maintained.

**Uneven Rear Brake Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Accelerated or uneven wear on rear brake pads and rotors, accompanied by potential overheating evidence.
- **Solution:** Inspect and service brake calipers, slide pins, and parking brake actuators when performing pad replacements.

## Electrical

### Infotainment System

Cabin electronics provide modern connectivity features, though early versions of the infotainment software are prone to freezing and erratic screen behavior.

**Mazda Connect Reboot and Ghost Touch**
- **Symptoms:** The touchscreen display freezes, repeatedly reboots to the logo screen, or registers phantom touches that alter navigation and audio settings.
- **Solution:** Update the infotainment firmware version, replace corrupted navigation SD cards, or replace the digitizer screen.
- **Cost:** Free to $150 depending on SD card or software updates

## Exterior

### Exterior Lighting

Exterior lighting systems generally perform well, but multiple owner reports highlight premature failure of LED daytime running light assemblies and headlights.

**Daytime Running Light and Headlight Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Failure of exterior daytime running lights or headlights, often accompanied by warning indicators on the instrument cluster.
- **Solution:** Inspect lighting modules, wiring harnesses, and replace burned-out assemblies or control units.

## Interior

### Seats and Upholstery

Interior comfort is generally high with supportive seating materials, but malfunctioning seat heating elements present a notable safety and fire concern in isolated cases.

**Seat Heater Overheating**
- **Symptoms:** Heated seats become excessively hot, potentially burning the seat upholstery, creating smoke, or causing localized discomfort.
- **Solution:** Inspect seat heater elements and wiring, and replace damaged heating pads or control modules.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V644000: Structure:Body:Hatchback/Liftgate](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mazda&model=CX-5&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** On the affected vehicles, the lift gate support struts may corrode, possibly causing the struts to break and/or the lift gate to drop unexpectedly. If the lift gate falls unexpectedly, it may hit the user, increasing their risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Mazda dealers will replace the both lift gate supports, free of charge, however parts are not currently available. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V203000: Steering](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mazda&model=CX-5&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** If the steering knuckle separates from the strut assembly, it can cause a loss of steering control, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Mazda will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the lower mounting nuts and bolts that secure the front strut assembly to the steering knuckle. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V064000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Storage:Tank Assembly:Filler Pipe And Cap](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mazda&model=CX-5&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** The affected vehicles have a fuel filler pipe that may rupture in the event of a crash. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 301, "Fuel System Integrity." A rupture of the fuel filler pipe may result in a fuel leak, increasing the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Mazda will notify owners, and dealers will alter the attachment between the fuel filler pipe and the left rear side of the bracket of the fuel filler pipe, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16E002000: Visibility:Windshield](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mazda&model=CX-5&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** If the bracket loosens or detaches from the windshield, the safety features of the Smart City Brake Support system may be disabled and therefore increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 20V063000: Exterior Lighting:Headlights](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mazda&model=CX-5&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** If the DRL fails, the vehicle's visibility may be reduced to other drivers on the road, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Mazda will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the headlights, and replace the sealing gasket or headlight assemblies, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2016 Mazda CX-5 remains a compelling choice for used-car shoppers seeking sporty handling and good fuel economy. Buyers should thoroughly test all electronics, including the infotainment screen and heated seats, while listening for any unusual transmission lag or drivetrain noise during a test drive. Verifying service history and confirming that all safety recalls have been addressed will help ensure a dependable ownership experience.
