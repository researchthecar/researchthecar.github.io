**Introduction**

The 2016 Mazda CX-5 is a compact crossover SUV that offers a blend of style, performance, and practicality. It is available with two engine options: a 2.0-liter Skyactiv-G I-4 and a 2.5-liter Skyactiv-G I-4. Both engines are paired with a six-speed automatic transmission.

Critics and owners alike have praised the CX-5's sharp handling, spacious interior, and impressive fuel economy. However, like all vehicles, the CX-5 has its share of reported issues.

**Engine**

**2.0-Liter Skyactiv-G I-4**

* **Excessive Oil Consumption:** Some owners have reported that their 2.5-liter engines consume excessive amounts of oil, leading to low oil levels and potential engine damage.
  * Solution: Mazda has issued a technical service bulletin (TSB) outlining a fix for this issue, which involves replacing the piston rings and valve seals.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,000+
  * Recalls: NHTSA Campaign ID 17V538000

**2.5-Liter Skyactiv-G I-4**

* **Timing Chain Tensioner Failure:** A small number of owners have experienced timing chain tensioner failures, which can lead to catastrophic engine damage.
  * Solution: Mazda has issued a recall (NHTSA Campaign ID 18V393000) to address this issue. Affected vehicles will receive a new timing chain tensioner.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: None (covered under warranty)
  * Recalls: NHTSA Campaign ID 18V393000

**Transmission**

**Six-Speed Automatic**

* **Hesitation or Jerking:** Some owners have reported hesitation or jerking during gear changes, particularly when shifting from first to second gear.
  * Solution: Mazda has released a software update to address this issue.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: None (covered under warranty)
  * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Rear Suspension Noise:** Some owners have reported a clunking or rattling noise from the rear suspension, particularly over rough roads.
  * Solution: Mazda has issued a TSB outlining a fix for this issue, which involves replacing the rear shock absorbers.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $700+
  * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain:** Some owners have reported experiencing rapid battery drain, particularly if the vehicle is left unused for extended periods.
  * Solution: Mazda has released a software update to address this issue.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: None (covered under warranty)
  * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Roof Rack Crossbar Corrosion:** Some owners have reported that the roof rack crossbars can corrode over time, leading to a loss of structural integrity.
  * Solution: Mazda has issued a TSB outlining a fix for this issue, which involves replacing the crossbars.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+
  * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Instrument Cluster Dimming:** Some owners have reported that the instrument cluster can dim or malfunction, particularly in low-light conditions.
  * Solution: Mazda has issued a TSB outlining a fix for this issue, which involves replacing the instrument cluster.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $1,500+
  * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

While the 2016 Mazda CX-5 is generally a reliable vehicle, it has experienced some reported issues. The severity and cost of these issues vary, with some being relatively minor and inexpensive to fix, while others can be more serious and costly. Owners should be aware of these potential issues and consider taking precautionary measures to avoid or mitigate them.
