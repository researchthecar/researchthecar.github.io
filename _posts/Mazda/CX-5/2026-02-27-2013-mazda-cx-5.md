# 2013 Mazda CX-5

### Overview
The 2013 Mazda CX-5 is generally considered a reliable compact SUV, praised for its engaging driving dynamics and fuel efficiency, particularly with its Skyactiv technology. While most owners report a positive ownership experience with minimal unexpected repairs, like any vehicle, there are a few areas that have seen more attention than others. This analysis focuses on documented technical issues beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential concerns for owners and prospective buyers. The CX-5's engineering prioritizes a balance of performance and economy, which has largely translated into a dependable vehicle for daily use.

---


## Engine

### 2.0L Skyactiv-G (PE-VPS)
**Oil Consumption**
- **Symptoms:** Increased oil level drops between changes, potentially leading to low oil warnings.
- **Solution:** Monitor oil levels regularly and top up as needed. In some cases, piston ring replacement may be considered, but this is not a widespread fix.
- **Cost:** $50 - $150 (for oil top-up), significantly higher if internal repairs are needed.

**Carbon Buildup**
- **Symptoms:** Reduced engine performance, rough idling, decreased fuel efficiency.
- **Solution:** Regular fuel system cleaning and intake manifold cleaning. Use of quality fuel can help mitigate this.
- **Cost:** $100 - $300

## Electrical

### Infotainment System
**Bluetooth Connectivity Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Intermittent connection drops, failure to pair with devices.
- **Solution:** Software updates for the infotainment system, or device compatibility checks.
- **Cost:** $0 - $150 (depending on software update needs)


### Body Control Module (BCM)
**Intermittent Power Failures**
- **Symptoms:** Random failures of interior lights, power windows, or other accessories.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and potential reprogramming or replacement of the BCM.
- **Cost:** $300 - $800

**Condensation**
- **Symptoms:** Moisture buildup inside headlight housings.
- **Solution:** Ensure seals are intact. In some cases, drilling small weep holes or replacing seals may be necessary.

**Blower Motor Resistor Failure**

- **Solution:** Replacement of the blower motor resistor.

### Summary Verdict
The 2013 Mazda CX-5 is a solid choice for a reliable compact SUV. While not entirely immune to issues, the problems reported are generally minor and not indicative of systemic failures. The most commonly cited concerns revolve around potential oil consumption in the 2.0L Skyactiv-G engine and occasional electrical glitches with the infotainment system or body control module. Transmission and suspension components have proven robust. Owners should be mindful of regular oil level checks and potential carbon buildup. Overall, the CX-5 offers good value with a strong reliability record for its class and model year.
