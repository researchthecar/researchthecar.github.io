## Electrical

### Standard Electrical System
**Infotainment System Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Occasional freezing or unresponsiveness of the infotainment screen, Bluetooth connectivity issues.
- **Solution:** Software updates, system resets, or potential replacement of the head unit.
- **Cost:** $100 - $800

**Keyless Entry/Start Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** Intermittent issues with the push-button start or keyless entry system not recognizing the fob.
- **Solution:** Battery replacement in the key fob, reprogramming of the fob, or diagnosis of the receiver module.
- **Cost:** $50 - $300


## Exterior

### Body Panels and Trim
**Rust on Undercarriage/Wheel Wells**
- **Symptoms:** Visible rust formation, particularly in areas exposed to salt and moisture, leading to potential structural compromise over time.
- **Solution:** Rustproofing treatments, rust removal and repair, or replacement of affected panels.
- **Cost:** $150 - $1000+


## Interior

### Cabin Materials
**Driver's Seat Bolster Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Premature wear and tear on the fabric or leather of the driver's seat bolster due to frequent ingress and egress.
- **Solution:** Seat cover installation, professional upholstery repair, or replacement of the seat cover.
- **Cost:** $100 - $500

### Summary Verdict
The 2013 Mazda 3 stands out as a reliable choice in the compact car segment. Its Skyactiv engine and transmission technologies have proven robust, with no widespread mechanical failures reported. The primary areas where owners might encounter issues are within the electrical system, specifically infotainment glitches and keyless entry problems, and some exterior concerns like rust in harsh climates. Interior wear on the driver's seat is also a common observation. Overall, the 2013 Mazda 3 offers good long-term reliability, with most potential issues being relatively minor and manageable, making it a sensible used car purchase.
