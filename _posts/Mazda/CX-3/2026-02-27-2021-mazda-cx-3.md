The 2021 Mazda CX-3 is generally considered a reliable compact SUV, benefiting from Mazda's reputation for building well-engineered vehicles. ### Summary Verdict
The 2021 Mazda CX-3 demonstrates strong reliability across its core mechanical components, including the engine and transmission. The powertrain is generally robust, with no significant widespread issues reported. The suspension and exterior components also tend to hold up well. The primary areas where minor concerns have been noted are within the electrical system, specifically occasional infotainment glitches and, in some instances, premature battery failure. These issues are not pervasive but are worth being aware of. Overall, the CX-3 is a dependable choice for a compact SUV, with most potential problems being manageable and not indicative of systemic flaws.
