# 2017 Mazda CX-3

### Overview
The 2017 Mazda CX-3 is a subcompact crossover known for its stylish design, engaging driving dynamics, and upscale interior for its class. Reliability data for this model year generally indicates a positive ownership experience, with most owners reporting satisfaction. While no vehicle is entirely free from potential issues, the CX-3 tends to perform well in terms of mechanical and electrical dependability. Common areas of concern, though not widespread, often relate to minor electronic glitches or specific component wear that can occur with mileage. Overall, the CX-3 is considered a solid choice for those seeking a practical yet fun-to-drive small SUV.

### Summary Verdict
The 2017 Mazda CX-3 demonstrates good overall reliability, particularly in its powertrain components. The 2.0L Skyactiv-G engine and its associated automatic and manual transmissions are generally robust. The primary areas where minor issues have been noted are within the electrical system, specifically concerning the infotainment unit and, in some cases, premature battery failure. These are not typically indicative of systemic design flaws but rather common electronic or component wear points. Exterior and interior components are largely free from widespread complaints. With proper maintenance, the CX-3 should provide a dependable ownership experience.
