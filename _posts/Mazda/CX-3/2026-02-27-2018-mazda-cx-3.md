# 2018 Mazda CX-3 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2018 Mazda CX-3, buyers generally find a stylish and agile subcompact crossover that shares its mechanical foundation with the Mazda2. While overall owner satisfaction tends to be high regarding handling and interior finish, prospective buyers should review known owner complaints concerning powertrain components and electronic safety systems. A careful pre-purchase inspection can help ensure any underlying mechanical or electrical concerns are addressed before purchase.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L SkyActiv-G 4-Cylinder

The 2018 Mazda CX-3 comes equipped with a 2.0-liter SkyActiv-G four-cylinder engine producing 146 horsepower and 146 lb-ft of torque. It generally offers spirited performance and good fuel economy, though occasional owner reports highlight component failures like fuel injectors.

**Fuel Injector Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Sudden engine failure accompanied by fuel leaking into the engine, which can ruin spark plugs and damage the catalytic converter.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified mechanic or dealership diagnose the fueling system, replace failed fuel injectors, and inspect related components like spark plugs and the catalytic converter.

## Transmission

### SkyActiv-Drive 6-Speed Automatic

The standard transmission is a 6-speed automatic paired with either front-wheel drive or i-Activ all-wheel drive, known for responsive shifting and solid long-term durability.

## Exterior

### Headlamps

Electrical complaints for this model year are minimal, though isolated issues with lighting components have been reported by owners.

**Headlamp Condensation**
- **Symptoms:** Heavy moisture and condensation buildup inside the headlamp housing, which may cause visibility issues or lead to inspection failures.
- **Solution:** Inspect the headlamp housing seals for leaks and replace the headlight assembly if moisture infiltration persists.

## Safety

### Forward Collision Avoidance

Advanced safety features enhance protection but can occasionally experience software or sensor false positives reported as unexpected braking events.

**Unintended Emergency Braking**
- **Symptoms:** Activation of warning alarms and sudden unexpected application of the emergency braking system while driving with no obstructions ahead.
- **Solution:** Have the vehicle's collision sensors and software calibration checked by a certified dealer or service technician.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V487000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mazda&model=CX-3&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** The rearview camera image may not display properly by showing a flickering or distorted image. A rearview camera that does not display properly can reduce the driver's rear view, increasing the risk of a crash or injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the rearview camera, as necessary. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2018 Mazda CX-3 is a compelling option for buyers seeking an engaging subcompact crossover with nimble handling. While major mechanical failures are relatively infrequent, potential buyers should thoroughly check the service history, test drive all safety and electrical systems, and verify that any open recalls have been completed by a certified dealership.
