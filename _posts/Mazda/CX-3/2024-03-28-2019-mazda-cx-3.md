**Introduction:**

The 2019 Mazda CX-3 is a subcompact crossover SUV known for its sporty handling and stylish design. It offers two engine and two transmission options: a 148-horsepower 2.0-liter four-cylinder engine with a six-speed automatic transmission and a 165-horsepower turbocharged 2.5-liter four-cylinder engine with a six-speed automatic or six-speed manual transmission. What people like about the CX-3 includes its fun-to-drive nature, comfortable interior, and sleek exterior. However, some complaints focus on its limited rear-seat space, noisy cabin at higher speeds, and relatively high price tag.

**Engine**

**2.0-Liter Four-Cylinder Engine**

* **Intake Valve Carbon Buildup:** 
Description/Symptoms: Engine hesitation, reduced performance, and poor fuel economy caused by carbon buildup on intake valves. 
Solution: Remove and clean intake valves, including decarbonization treatments. 
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+

**2.5-Liter Turbocharged Four-Cylinder Engine:**

**No Issues Found**

**Transmission**

**Six-Speed Automatic Transmission**

* **Shifting Issues:** 
Description/Symptoms: Rough or delayed gear changes, especially from 1st to 2nd and 2nd to 3rd gears. 
Solution: Software updates or transmission fluid exchange. 
Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+
**Recalls:** NHTSA number 20V077000

**Six-Speed Manual Transmission**

**No Issues Found**

**Suspension**

* **Rear Suspension Bushing Failure:** 
Description/Symptoms: Clunking or rubbing noise from rear suspension when driving over uneven surfaces. 
Solution: Replace rear suspension bushings. 
Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain:** 
Description/Symptoms: Dead battery after leaving vehicle unattended for extended periods. 
Solution: Diagnose and resolve electrical system draw, which may include replacing faulty components like the infotainment system or alternator. 
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+

* **Headlight Condensation:** 
Description/Symptoms: Moisture accumulation inside headlight housing. 
Solution: Replace or repair headlight housing with updated version to prevent condensation. 
Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600

**Exterior**

* **Roof Rail Peeling:** 
Description/Symptoms: Paint peeling or flaking on roof rails. 
Solution: Repaint or replace roof rails. 
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400

**Interior**

* **Dashboard Cracking:** 
Description/Symptoms: Cracks appearing on the dashboard, especially near the passenger airbag. 
Solution: Replace damaged dashboard. 
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+

* **Seat Belt Sensor Malfunction:** 
Description/Symptoms: Seat belt warning light staying on even when seat belt is properly buckled. 
Solution: Replace seat belt sensor. 
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $300

* **Wiper Motor Failure:** 
Description/Symptoms: Windshield wipers not working or functioning intermittently. 
Solution: Replace wiper motor. 
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+

**Summary:**

While the 2019 Mazda CX-3 has its strengths, several issues can affect different components of the vehicle. These issues range in severity and cost, with some being relatively minor and others requiring more significant repairs. Engine issues, such as intake valve carbon buildup, can impact performance and fuel economy, while transmission problems can affect drivability. Suspension issues may result in noise or handling problems, while electrical issues can lead to battery drain or potential electrical failures. Interior and exterior issues can affect aesthetics or functionality. Overall, the CX-3's reliability has received mixed reviews, with some owners reporting few problems while others have encountered more significant issues. It's essential to carefully research and consider these potential issues, especially if purchasing a used CX-3.
