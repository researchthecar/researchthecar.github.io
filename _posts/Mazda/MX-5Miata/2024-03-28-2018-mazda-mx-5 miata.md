## 2018 Mazda MX-5 Miata

The 2018 Mazda MX-5 Miata is a compact sports car that is known for its handling, performance, and style. It is available with two engine options: a 1.5-liter four-cylinder engine with 131 horsepower and 150 lb-ft of torque, and a 2.0-liter four-cylinder engine with 181 horsepower and 151 lb-ft of torque. Both engines are available with either a six-speed manual or a six-speed automatic transmission.

People who like the Mazda MX-5 Miata praise its handling, performance, and style. However, some people find the interior cramped and the ride too harsh.

### Engine
- **1.5-liter four-cylinder engine**
    - **Excessive oil consumption**
    - Description/Symptoms: The engine may consume excessive amounts of oil, leading to low oil levels and potential engine damage.
    - Solution: Replace the piston rings and valve stem seals.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
    - Recalls: None Found
- **2.0-liter four-cylinder engine**
    - **No Issues Found**

### Transmission
- **Six-speed manual transmission**
    - **Difficulty shifting into gear**
    - Description/Symptoms: The transmission may be difficult to shift into gear, especially when the engine is cold.
    - Solution: Replace the shift linkage or transmission fluid.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
    - Recalls: None Found
- **Six-speed automatic transmission**
    - **Shifting problems**
    - Description/Symptoms: The transmission may shift gears erratically or may not shift gears at all.
    - Solution: Replace the transmission control module or transmission.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+
    - Recalls: None Found

### Suspension
- **No Issues Found**

### Electrical
- **Battery drain**
    - Description/Symptoms: The battery may drain prematurely, even when the car is not in use.
    - Solution: Replace the battery or alternator.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
    - Recalls: None Found
- **Electrical shorts**
    - Description/Symptoms: The car may experience electrical shorts, causing various electrical components to malfunction.
    - Solution: Repair or replace the affected wiring or components.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,000+
    - Recalls: None Found

### Exterior
- **Paint peeling**
    - Description/Symptoms: The paint on the car may peel or chip prematurely.
    - Solution: Repaint the affected areas.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+
    - Recalls: None Found
- **Rust**
    - Description/Symptoms: The car may develop rust on various body panels, especially in areas exposed to moisture.
    - Solution: Repair or replace the affected body panels.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+
    - Recalls: None Found

### Interior
- **Steering wheel vibration**
    - Description/Symptoms: The steering wheel may vibrate when driving at certain speeds.
    - Solution: Balance the wheels or tires.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200
    - Recalls: None Found
- **Seat belt problems**
    - Description/Symptoms: The seat belts may not retract properly or may lock up unexpectedly.
    - Solution: Replace the seat belt retractors or seat belts.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
    - Recalls: None Found

### Summary
The 2018 Mazda MX-5 Miata is a reliable and fun-to-drive sports car. However, it does have some potential issues, including excessive oil consumption, transmission problems, and electrical shorts. The severity and cost of these issues vary depending on the specific issue and the extent of the damage. Overall, the MX-5 Miata is a good choice for those who are looking for a sporty and affordable convertible.
