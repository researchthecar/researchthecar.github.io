Here's a breakdown of common issues and complaints for the 2019 Mazda MX-5 Miata, formatted for your website.

The 2019 Mazda MX-5 Miata is a popular roadster known for its engaging driving dynamics, lightweight design, and affordability. It comes equipped with a 2.0-liter Skyactiv-G inline-4 engine producing 181 horsepower and 151 lb-ft of torque. Transmission options include a 6-speed manual and a 6-speed automatic. Owners praise the Miata's nimble handling, responsive engine, and open-air experience. Common criticisms include limited interior space, a small trunk, and road noise.

**Engine**

**2.0L Skyactiv-G Inline-4**

*   **Oil Consumption**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners report excessive oil consumption, requiring more frequent oil top-offs between scheduled oil changes. The specific amount varies, but some report needing to add a quart every 1,000-3,000 miles.
    *   Solution: Monitoring oil levels regularly is key. If consumption is excessive, a dealer inspection and possible replacement of valve stem seals or piston rings may be needed.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $3,000+ (depending on the severity and required repairs)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**6-Speed Manual**

*   **Grinding into 2nd Gear**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners report a grinding noise or difficulty shifting into second gear, especially when the transmission is cold.
    *   Solution: Try using higher quality transmission fluid, it is worth the change. If the problem continues, transmission replacement may be needed.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $3,500+ (depending on the solution)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**6-Speed Automatic**

*   **Harsh Shifting**
    * Description/Symptoms: Some owners report harsh or jerky shifting, particularly when downshifting.
    * Solution: Try a transmission fluid flush or inspect the valve body. If the problem continues, transmission replacement may be needed.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $4,000+ (depending on the solution)
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

No Issues Found

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Freezing/Crashing**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The Mazda Connect infotainment system can occasionally freeze, crash, or become unresponsive. This can affect navigation, audio, and other functions.
    *   Solution: Restarting the system often resolves the issue temporarily. Updating the software to the latest version is recommended. In some cases, a hardware replacement may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 (software update) - $500+ (hardware replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Convertible Top Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Water leaks around the convertible top seals, particularly in heavy rain or when going through a car wash.
    *   Solution: Inspect and adjust the top seals. Applying a silicone-based lubricant to the seals can help. In severe cases, the seals or the entire top may need to be replaced.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $20 (lubricant) - $1,000+ (top replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Seat Discomfort**
    * Description/Symptoms: Some drivers find the seats uncomfortable on long drives due to limited adjustability and firm cushioning.
    * Solution: Aftermarket seat cushions or professional seat modifications may be needed to improve comfort.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $20-$500+ (depending on solution)
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2019 Mazda MX-5 Miata generally experiences relatively minor issues. The most reported issues for the 2019 MX-5 are: oil consumption, grinding of 2nd gear on the manual transmission, and the convertible top leaking. Overall, the 2019 MX-5 Miata is a fun and engaging sports car, and the most reported issues that owners complain about should be taken into consideration when purchasing the vehicle.
