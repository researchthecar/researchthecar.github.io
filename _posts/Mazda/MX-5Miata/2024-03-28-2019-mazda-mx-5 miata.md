**Introduction:**

The 2019 Mazda MX-5 Miata is a highly acclaimed sports car known for its agile handling, peppy engine, and stylish design. It comes with a single engine and transmission option: a 2.0-liter naturally aspirated four-cylinder engine paired with a six-speed manual transmission. This engine produces 181 horsepower and 151 pound-feet of torque. Owners generally praise the Miata for its fun driving experience, but some have reported minor issues.

**Engine**

**2.0L Naturally Aspirated Four-Cylinder**

* **VVT Solenoid Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: The variable valve timing (VVT) solenoid may fail, causing poor engine performance, stalling, and difficulty starting.
    * Solution: Replace the VVT solenoid.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**Six-Speed Manual**

* **No Issues Found**

**Suspension**

* **No Issues Found**

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain**
    * Description/Symptoms: The battery may discharge prematurely, resulting in difficulty starting the vehicle.
    * Solution: Replace the faulty battery or alternator.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Cracked Windshields**
    * Description/Symptoms: Some vehicles have experienced cracked windshields without any apparent impact.
    * Solution: Replace the windshield.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Creaky Interior Panels**
    * Description/Symptoms: Some interior panels may develop squeaks or rattles.
    * Solution: Lubricate the affected panels.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: DIY Fix

**Summary:**

Overall, the 2019 Mazda MX-5 Miata has a good reliability record with minor issues reported. The most common problems involve the VVT solenoid and battery drain, which can be relatively inexpensive to repair. Other issues, such as cracked windshields and creaky interior panels, are less severe and can be addressed with minimal cost.
