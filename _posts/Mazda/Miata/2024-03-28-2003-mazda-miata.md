Introduced in 2003, the fourth-generation Mazda Miata (MX-5) is a compact, two-seat roadster available with a naturally aspirated 1.6L inline-four or 1.8L inline-four engine mated to either a 5-speed manual or 6-speed automatic transmission. The Miata is renowned for its nimble handling, affordability, and lively driving dynamics. However, like any vehicle, it has its share of potential issues.

**Engine**
**1.6L Inline-Four**
* **Excessive Oil Consumption:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Burning excessive amounts of oil, leading to low oil levels and potential engine damage.
    * Solution: Replacement of piston rings or oil control valves.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,000+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Intake Manifold Flap Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Malfunction of the intake manifold flaps, causing rough idling, poor acceleration, and increased emissions.
    * Solution: Replacement of the intake manifold.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**1.8L Inline-Four**
* **No Issues Found**

**Transmission**
**5-Speed Manual**
* **Grinding Gears:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Difficulty engaging or shifting gears, accompanied by a grinding noise.
    * Solution: Replacement of worn or damaged gears, synchros, or clutch.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**6-Speed Automatic**
* **Delayed Shifting:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Transmission shifts gears slowly or hesitatingly, leading to poor acceleration and reduced performance.
    * Solution: Software update or replacement of faulty transmission components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**
* **Front Strut Mount Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Worn or damaged strut mounts, resulting in clunking noises, poor handling, and premature tire wear.
    * Solution: Replacement of the strut mounts.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**
* **Alternator Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Malfunction of the alternator, leading to a lack of charging for the battery and electrical system.
    * Solution: Replacement of the alternator.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**
* **Rear Diffuser Detachment:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The rear diffuser may detach from the vehicle, potentially causing damage to the exhaust system.
    * Solution: Reattachment or replacement of the diffuser.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**
* **Center Console Lid Malfunction:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The center console lid may not open or close properly, causing inconvenience and possible damage to the console.
    * Solution: Replacement of the console lid latch mechanism.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $250+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2003 Mazda Miata is a fun and reliable roadster that offers excellent handling and performance for its price. However, it is not without its potential issues. The most significant problems owners face are related to excessive oil consumption, transmission problems, and suspension issues. These issues can be costly to fix, but they are generally not severe and can be addressed with proper maintenance and repairs. Overall, the Miata is a worthy choice for enthusiasts seeking a budget-friendly and engaging driving experience.
