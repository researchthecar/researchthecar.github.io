# 2013 Mazda CX-9

### Overview
The 2013 Mazda CX-9 is a mid-size SUV known for its practicality and family-friendly features. From a technical reliability standpoint, it generally performs well, particularly concerning its powertrain. The CX-9 typically came equipped with a robust V6 engine and a smooth-shifting automatic transmission, which are the primary focus for many owners. While most owners report a positive ownership experience with minimal unexpected repairs, like any vehicle, there are a few areas that have seen occasional reports of issues. This analysis focuses on documented, non-wear-and-tear related problems to provide a clear picture of potential concerns for prospective buyers or current owners.

---


## Engine

### 3.7L V6
**Intake Manifold Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Coolant loss, engine overheating, sweet smell from engine bay.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the intake manifold assembly.
- **Cost:** $400 - $800

**Variable Valve Timing (VVT) System Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Rough idle, check engine light, rattling noise from the engine, reduced engine power.
- **Solution:** Replacement of VVT solenoids or phasers.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1200

### Summary Verdict
The 2013 Mazda CX-9 demonstrates solid reliability, with its V6 engine and automatic transmission being generally robust. The most commonly cited issues, such as intake manifold leaks and VVT system problems, are not universal but warrant attention. Electrical gremlins, particularly with the infotainment system, have also been noted. However, these are often manageable and do not typically indicate a fundamental flaw in the vehicle's design. Overall, the CX-9 is a dependable choice for a family SUV, provided potential buyers are aware of and prepared for these specific, albeit infrequent, repair possibilities.
