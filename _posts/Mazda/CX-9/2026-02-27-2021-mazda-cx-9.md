# 2021 Mazda CX-9 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2021 Mazda CX-9 combines upscale styling, engaging handling, and a refined interior that punches well above its price class. Powered by a standard turbocharged four-cylinder engine paired with a smooth automatic transmission, it offers a compelling alternative to more mainstream three-row crossovers. However, used car shoppers should review common owner-reported grievances regarding powertrain components and auxiliary systems before making a purchase.

---

## Engine

### 2.5L Turbocharged Inline-4

The 2.5-liter turbocharged inline-4 produces up to 250 horsepower and 320 lb-ft of torque on premium fuel, providing strong low-end acceleration for the heavy crossover. While generally robust, this powerplant has been subject to owner scrutiny regarding valve stem seal durability and occasional misfire diagnostics.

**Valve Stem Seal Wear and Oil Consumption**
- **Symptoms:** Owners note concerns regarding valve stem seals, which have been tied to class action settlement campaigns involving oil consumption and smoke.
- **Solution:** Consult a certified dealer or mechanic regarding applicable warranty extensions, settlements, or seal replacements.

**Engine Misfire and Diagnostic Codes**
- **Symptoms:** Illuminated check engine light accompanied by diagnostic trouble codes such as P0303, persisting even after component swaps like spark plugs or ignition coils.
- **Solution:** Perform thorough diagnostic testing of fuel delivery, compression, and ignition circuits to identify root electrical or mechanical faults.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

The traditional 6-speed automatic transmission delivers crisp, responsive shifts and pairs well with both front-wheel drive and predictive i-ACTIV all-wheel-drive configurations. It generally maintains a solid reputation for long-term mechanical reliability among owners.

## Suspension

### Front Suspension

Suspension complaints are minimal, though isolated front-end noises can develop over time.

**Front-End Popping Noises**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may hear a distinct popping or clicking sound originating from the front end while turning at various speeds or driving over road bumps.
- **Solution:** Inspect front suspension bushings, struts, ball joints, and control arms for wear or binding.

## Exterior

### Door Locks and Keyless Entry

Electrical systems are generally modern and trouble-free, though occasional quirks with convenience features are reported.

**Unintended Auto-Locking**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle doors may automatically lock with personal items and keys left inside shortly after closing passenger doors.
- **Solution:** Check key fob proximity sensors and software settings, and avoid leaving keys inside the cabin unattended.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V750000: Seats:Mid/Rear Assembly](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mazda&model=CX-9&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The left-side second-row seat may unexpectedly slide forward during a crash. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 210, "Seat Belt Assembly Anchorages." A seat that slides forward during a crash can transfer excessive force onto a belted occupant, increasing their risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the internal parts of the affected second-row seats, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2021 Mazda CX-9 remains a stylish and enjoyable choice for families seeking a three-row crossover with dynamic driving manners. While major mechanical failures are relatively infrequent, prospective buyers should thoroughly inspect the engine for any signs of oil consumption or valve stem seal wear, and listen closely for suspension noises during a test drive. Checking service histories against manufacturer warranty campaigns is strongly recommended.
