# 2020 Mazda CX-9 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used Mazda CX-9, buyers generally find a stylish and engaging three-row SUV with a well-appointed interior. While owner reports to federal regulators indicate some areas to watch, the vehicle offers a solid overall driving experience. Reviewing specific powertrain options and known owner-reported quirks helps ensure a confident purchase.

---

## Engine

### 2.5L Turbocharged Inline-4

This 2.5-liter turbocharged inline-4 engine produces approximately 250 horsepower and 310 lb-ft of torque on premium fuel, providing strong low-end acceleration. It serves as the standard and sole powerplant across all trims, pairing responsive daily performance with generally dependable operation when properly maintained.

**Coolant Leak and Overheating**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have reported coolant leaks leading to engine overheating and potential cylinder head issues.
- **Solution:** Inspect the cooling system and cylinder head area for signs of leakage or cracks; repair or replace faulty components as necessary.

**Fuel System and Stalling Concerns**
- **Symptoms:** Some drivers experience rough idling, sputtering, or unexpected hesitations and engine stalls while driving.
- **Solution:** Diagnose the fuel pump and fuel injectors, replacing failing parts to restore proper fuel delivery and engine smoothness.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 6-speed automatic transmission delivers smooth and predictable shifts across front-wheel and all-wheel-drive configurations. It is generally robust and free from widespread, systemic mechanical failures in owner reports.

## Brakes

### Advanced Driver Assistance Systems

Safety systems offer comprehensive protection, but sensor-based driver aids should be thoroughly tested for intermittent warning lights or calibration glitches.

**False Collision Warnings and Camera Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers experience intermittent warning messages for the smart brake system and forward collision sensors, sometimes accompanied by temporary steering stiffness.
- **Solution:** Have a dealer or specialist inspect the front radar sensors, camera modules, and related software calibration.

## Electrical

### Infotainment Screen

Electrical components generally perform well, though cabin electronics and infotainment displays warrant inspection during a pre-purchase test drive.

**Screen Cracking and Opacity**
- **Symptoms:** The infotainment display develops spider cracks across the surface and turns opaque, making the information unreadable.
- **Solution:** Replace the infotainment display unit if internal delamination or screen cracking occurs.
- **Cost:** $1000 - $3000

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V750000: Seats:Mid/Rear Assembly](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mazda&model=CX-9&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** The left-side second-row seat may unexpectedly slide forward during a crash. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 210, "Seat Belt Assembly Anchorages." A seat that slides forward during a crash can transfer excessive force onto a belted occupant, increasing their risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the internal parts of the affected second-row seats, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The Mazda CX-9 remains a compelling choice for used SUV buyers seeking utility and driving refinement. While prospective owners should carefully check for potential cooling system concerns and infotainment screen wear, a thorough pre-purchase inspection can help secure a reliable and enjoyable vehicle.
