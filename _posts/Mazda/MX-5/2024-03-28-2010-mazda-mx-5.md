**Introduction**
The 2010 Mazda MX-5 is a lightweight, rear-wheel-drive roadster available in both coupe and convertible models. It is powered by a 2.0-liter four-cylinder engine that produces 167 horsepower and 140 lb-ft of torque. The transmission options are a five-speed manual or a six-speed automatic. People like the MX-5 for its handling, performance, and affordability. However, it is not without its issues.

**Engine**
*2.0-liter Four-Cylinder*
**Premature Spark Plug Failure**
Description/Symptoms: Spark plugs can foul prematurely, causing misfires, rough idling, and poor performance.
Solution: Replace the spark plugs with new ones from Mazda.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**
*Five-Speed Manual*
**Shifting Difficulty**
Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears, especially into first and second.
Solution: Replace the transmission synchronizers.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000
Recalls: None Found

*Six-Speed Automatic*
**No Issues Found**

**Suspension**
**Front Sway Bar End Links**
Description/Symptoms: Worn end links can cause clunking noises when driving over bumps or turning.
Solution: Replace the end links with new ones from Mazda.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**
**Alternator**
Description/Symptoms: Alternator failure can cause the battery to discharge and the vehicle to stall.
Solution: Replace the alternator with a new one from Mazda.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $700
Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**
**Convertible Top Fabric Fading**
Description/Symptoms: The fabric on the convertible top can fade over time, especially in hot, sunny climates.
Solution: Replace the convertible top with a new one from Mazda.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000
Recalls: None Found

**Interior**
**Power Window Switches**
Description/Symptoms: Power window switches can fail, causing the windows to stop working.
Solution: Replace the failed switches with new ones from Mazda.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
Recalls: None Found

**Summary**
The 2010 Mazda MX-5 is a fun-to-drive car with a few potential issues. The most serious issues are the premature spark plug failure and the shifting difficulty with the five-speed manual transmission. However, these issues are relatively inexpensive to fix. Overall, the MX-5 is a reliable and affordable sports car that is worth considering if you are looking for a fun and exciting driving experience.
