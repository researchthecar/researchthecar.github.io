# 2021 Mazda MX-5 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2021 Mazda MX-5 continues to deliver an engaging, lightweight sports car experience beloved by driving enthusiasts. While generally celebrated for its nimble handling and lively powertrain, prospective buyers should always review owner feedback and inspect individual vehicles carefully for any unexpected mechanical hiccups.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Skyactiv-G 4-Cylinder

The 2.0-liter naturally aspirated four-cylinder engine produces 181 horsepower and 151 lb-ft of torque. It is known for its energetic high-revving character and solid overall reliability in daily driving conditions.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Manual Transmission

The traditional 6-speed manual gearbox features short, crisp throws that complement the sports car layout. It is widely praised by enthusiasts for its direct mechanical feel and robust durability.

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 6-speed automatic transmission with paddle shifters offers relaxed cruising while retaining prompt shift responses. It provides a dependable alternative for drivers preferring two pedals.

## Safety

### Power Train Assembly

Owner reports for drivetrain components indicate rare instances of unexpected power loss.

**Motive Power Loss and Burning Odor**
- **Symptoms:** While driving, the vehicle may suddenly lose motive power accompanied by a burning odor, creating a safety hazard.
- **Solution:** Have the vehicle inspected by a qualified technician or dealer to diagnose the underlying mechanical or electrical failure.

---

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V695000: Air Bags: Air Bag/Restraint Control Module:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mazda&model=MX-5&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** A software error in the air bag control module may cause the front air bags to deploy with excessive force during a crash. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 208, "Occupant Crash Protection." An air bag that deploys with excessive force can increase the risk of injury during a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the Sophisticated Air Bag Sensor (SAS) control module software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2021 Mazda MX-5 remains a compelling choice for sports car shoppers, balancing joyful driving dynamics with a generally solid reputation. Buyers should perform a thorough pre-purchase inspection to ensure all mechanical and powertrain systems are functioning properly.
