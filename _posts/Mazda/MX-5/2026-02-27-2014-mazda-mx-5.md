# 2014 Mazda MX-5

### Overview
The 2014 Mazda MX-5 Miata, a beloved roadster, is generally known for its engaging driving dynamics and robust engineering. This generation, the NC, continued to offer a pure, lightweight sports car experience. Reliability is a strong suit for the MX-5, with owners often praising its mechanical soundness and relatively low cost of ownership. While no car is entirely immune to issues, the MX-5 typically avoids major, widespread problems, making it a dependable choice for enthusiasts. ### Soft Top
**Leaking Seals**
- **Symptoms:** Water ingress into the cabin, especially during heavy rain or car washes.
- **Solution:** Replacement or adjustment of rubber seals, or application of seal conditioners.
- **Cost:** $100 - $300


### Paint

- **Solution:** Professional repainting or touch-up work.

### Summary Verdict
The 2014 Mazda MX-5 Miata stands out as a highly reliable sports car. The powertrain, including the 2.0L engine and both manual and automatic transmissions, is exceptionally robust with no significant widespread mechanical failures reported. Suspension and the core electrical systems also demonstrate strong reliability. Minor concerns are primarily cosmetic or related to the soft top's seals, which can degrade over time leading to leaks. Paint issues like clear coat peeling are not uncommon for vehicles of this age and exposure. Overall, the MX-5 is a dependable choice for driving enthusiasts, with most potential issues being minor and manageable.
