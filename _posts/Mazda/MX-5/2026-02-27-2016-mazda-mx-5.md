# 2016 Mazda MX-5 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2016 Mazda MX-5 is celebrated for its agile handling, engaging driving dynamics, and lightweight design. However, used car buyers should pay careful attention to a few recurring mechanical and electrical concerns documented by owners, particularly regarding early manual transmission durability, steering feel, and interior accessory wear.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Skyactiv-G 4-Cylinder

The 2.0-liter naturally aspirated four-cylinder engine produces approximately 155 horsepower and 148 lb-ft of torque. It is generally regarded as robust and reliable, providing lively performance with strong fuel economy.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Manual Transmission

The 6-speed manual transmission is praised for its short, crisp throws, but early model years have experienced notable durability complaints regarding third-gear failures.

**Transmission Failure and Gear Shifting Difficulties**
- **Symptoms:** Owners report difficulty engaging first or third gear, loud whining noises under throttle, and sudden mechanical failure during acceleration or highway driving.
- **Solution:** Requires professional inspection, potential component rebuild, or complete transmission replacement depending on internal damage.

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

The traditional 6-speed automatic transmission offers smooth shifts and pairs well with daily commuting, showing fewer reported mechanical failure complaints than its manual counterpart.

## Steering

### Electric Power Steering

The electric power steering system provides quick responses, though some owners experience intermittent binding sensations.

**Sticky or Binding Steering at Highway Speeds**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers note that the steering can feel stuck, heavy, or bound up during sustained high-speed driving or in warmer weather, requiring extra force that can lead to overcorrection.
- **Solution:** Have the steering rack and electronic assist module inspected by a qualified technician.

## Electrical

### Infotainment and Sensors

Cabin electronics generally function well, but cosmetic screen defects and sensor intermittency are common minor grievances.

**Infotainment Screen Delamination and Blind Spot Sensor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The center display screen can develop spider cracking or delamination, and blind spot monitoring sensors may intermittently fail to alert the driver.
- **Solution:** Replace the infotainment display unit or service the blind spot monitoring radar modules as needed.

## Interior

### Sun Visors and Convertible Top Trim

Interior trim and convenience features experience typical wear issues, with soft top and sun visor components being the most frequently cited.

**Sun Visor Collapse and Convertible Liner Sag**
- **Symptoms:** Sun visors fail internally and drop down blocking the driver's view, while the convertible top inner liner can sag and obstruct part of the rear window.
- **Solution:** Replace the damaged sun visors and adjust or secure the convertible top headliner assembly.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V072000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission:Control Module (Tcm/Pcm/Tecm)](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mazda&model=MX-5&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** Due to incorrect programming of the Transmission Control Module (TCM), certain conditions may cause the vehicle to unexpectedly downshift and abruptly decelerate. If the vehicle abruptly downshifts, the driver may lose control of the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Mazda will notify owners, and dealers will reprogram the TCM with corrected software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V496000: Structure:Frame And Members:Underbody Shields](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mazda&model=MX-5&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** Parts that separate from the vehicle may become a road hazard, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Mazda will notify owners, and dealers will replace the skid plate, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V695000: Air Bags: Air Bag/Restraint Control Module:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mazda&model=MX-5&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** A software error in the air bag control module may cause the front air bags to deploy with excessive force during a crash. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 208, "Occupant Crash Protection." An air bag that deploys with excessive force can increase the risk of injury during a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the Sophisticated Air Bag Sensor (SAS) control module software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2016 Mazda MX-5 remains a highly rewarding sports car to own and drive, offering unmatched handling agility for the price. Prospective buyers looking at manual transmission models should thoroughly check for shifting resistance or gear whine during a test drive, and verify that all interior electronics and safety recalls have been properly addressed.
