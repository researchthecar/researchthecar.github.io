# 2007 Mazda MX-5

### Overview
The 2007 Mazda MX-5 Miata, also known as the NC generation, is generally regarded as a reliable sports car. It offers a pure driving experience with a focus on handling and driver engagement. While most owners report a positive ownership experience with minimal unexpected repairs, like any vehicle, certain components can experience issues over time. This analysis focuses on documented, non-wear-and-tear related problems that have been reported by owners and technicians, providing insights into potential areas of concern for the 2.0L engine and its associated transmissions.

---


## Engine

### 2.0L MZR (LF-VE)
**Oil Consumption**
- **Symptoms:** Excessive oil burning between changes, potentially leading to low oil levels and engine damage if not monitored.
- **Solution:** Regular oil level checks and top-ups. In some cases, piston ring replacement may be necessary.
- **Cost:** $1,500 - $3,000

**Variable Valve Timing (VVT) Actuator**
- **Symptoms:** Rattling noise from the engine on startup, check engine light illumination.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the VVT actuator and potentially the camshaft phaser.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1,000


## Transmission

### 6-Speed Manual
**Shifter Bushing Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Vague or sloppy shifter feel, difficulty engaging certain gears.
- **Solution:** Replacement of worn shifter bushings.
- **Cost:** $100 - $250

### Standard Electrical System
**Window Regulator Failure**

### Summary Verdict
The 2007 Mazda MX-5 Miata is a mechanically sound vehicle with a strong reputation for reliability. The most commonly cited issues revolve around the 2.0L engine's tendency for oil consumption and potential VVT actuator problems, though these are not universal. Transmission issues are relatively rare, with manual shifter bushings being the most noted minor concern. Electrical components like window regulators can fail, but this is a common wear item across many vehicles. Overall, the MX-5 offers a dependable platform for spirited driving, provided regular maintenance and attention to oil levels are maintained.
