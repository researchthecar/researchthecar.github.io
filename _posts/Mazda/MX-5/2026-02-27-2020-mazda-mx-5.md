# 2020 Mazda MX-5 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

Shopping for a used 2020 Mazda MX-5 offers a compelling blend of engaging rear-wheel-drive handling, nimble dynamics, and modern styling. While owner reports to federal safety regulators generally focus on relatively minor or isolated mechanical and electrical concerns, prospective buyers should review vehicle history records carefully. As with any sporty convertible or fastback, verifying proper maintenance and checking for open manufacturer campaigns is a vital step before purchase.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Skyactiv-G 4-Cylinder

The 2.0-liter Skyactiv-G four-cylinder engine produces approximately 181 horsepower and 151 lb-ft of torque, providing lively performance. It is generally regarded as a reliable powerplant, though isolated owner complaints highlight unexpected driveability concerns.

**Sudden Power Loss**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle experienced a sudden loss of engine power while driving in traffic and failed to restart, requiring a tow.
- **Solution:** Diagnose the underlying mechanical or fuel system failure and replace faulty components as necessary.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Manual Transmission

The crisp 6-speed manual transmission is a favorite among driving enthusiasts, offering short throws and precise clutch engagement.

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 6-speed automatic transmission features steering-wheel-mounted paddle shifters and delivers smooth, responsive gear changes for everyday commuting.

## Safety

### Air Bags

Safety equipment generally performs as expected, though owners have experienced frustrating delays when attempting to complete open manufacturer safety campaigns.

**Recall Remedy Delays**
- **Symptoms:** Owners receiving recall notices for safety campaigns report that dealerships are sometimes unable to perform repairs immediately due to a lack of required tools or parts.
- **Solution:** Contact a local authorized dealership using your VIN to confirm parts availability before scheduling safety recall service.

---

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V695000: Air Bags: Air Bag/Restraint Control Module:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mazda&model=MX-5&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** A software error in the air bag control module may cause the front air bags to deploy with excessive force during a crash. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 208, "Occupant Crash Protection." An air bag that deploys with excessive force can increase the risk of injury during a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the Sophisticated Air Bag Sensor (SAS) control module software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2020 Mazda MX-5 remains an exhilarating choice for used-car buyers seeking a lightweight sports car with sharp handling. While isolated owner reports note potential powertrain and safety recall scheduling hurdles, the vehicle's overall mechanical track record is solid. Buyers should perform a comprehensive pre-purchase inspection and verify that all open safety campaigns have been successfully addressed.
