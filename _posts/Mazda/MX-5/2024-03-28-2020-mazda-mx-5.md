**Introduction**

The 2020 Mazda MX-5 is a two-seater roadster known for its nimble handling, sporty performance, and sleek design. It offers a 2.0-liter Skyactiv-G naturally aspirated engine paired with a 6-speed manual transmission or a 6-speed automatic transmission.

The MX-5 has received praise for its engaging driving experience, comfortable interior, and affordable price point. However, like any vehicle, it has also had its share of reported issues.

**Engine**

* **Skyactiv-G 2.0L with 6-speed Manual Transmission**

    * **Excessive oil consumption**
    Description/Symptoms: Some vehicles have experienced excessive oil consumption, leading to low oil levels and potentially engine damage.
    Solution: The issue can be fixed by replacing the piston rings and oil seals.
    Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
    Recalls: None Found

* **Skyactiv-G 2.0L with 6-speed Automatic Transmission**

    * **No major issues reported**

**Transmission**

* **6-speed Manual Transmission**

    * **Grinding gears**
    Description/Symptoms: Drivers may experience grinding or difficulty shifting gears, especially when the transmission is cold.
    Solution: The issue can be resolved by replacing the clutch kit or synchronizers.
    Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,500+
    Recalls: None Found

* **6-speed Automatic Transmission**

    * **Hesitation or slipping**
    Description/Symptoms: Some vehicles have experienced hesitation or slipping when shifting gears.
    Solution: The issue can be fixed by reprogramming the transmission control module or replacing the transmission fluid.
    Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+
    Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **No major issues reported**

**Electrical**

* **Battery drain**
Description/Symptoms: Some vehicles have experienced rapid battery drain, leading to difficulty starting the engine.
Solution: The issue can be caused by a faulty alternator or a parasitic power drain.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+
Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Convertible top issues**
Description/Symptoms: Some vehicles have experienced issues with the convertible top, such as water leakage or difficulty opening or closing.
Solution: The issue can be fixed by replacing the convertible top seals or adjusting the top mechanism.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
Recalls: NHTSA Campaign Number 21V744

**Interior**

* **Door lock malfunction**
Description/Symptoms: Some vehicles have experienced issues with the door locks, such as difficulty locking or unlocking the doors.
Solution: The issue can be fixed by replacing the door lock actuators.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

Overall, the 2020 Mazda MX-5 is a reliable vehicle with generally minor issues. The most common problems reported involve excessive oil consumption, grinding gears, battery drain, and convertible top issues. While these issues can be inconvenient and costly to fix, they do not appear to be widespread or severe enough to significantly impact the overall reliability of the vehicle.
