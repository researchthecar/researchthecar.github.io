The 2017 Mazda MX-5 Miata is a popular roadster known for its agile handling, engaging driving experience, and classic design. It comes equipped with a 2.0-liter inline-4 engine producing 155 horsepower and 148 lb-ft of torque. It is available with either a 6-speed manual or a 6-speed automatic transmission. Owners typically praise the car's fun-to-drive nature, responsive steering, and relatively good fuel economy. However, some common complaints include limited cargo space, a small interior, and a sometimes rough ride due to the sporty suspension. The infotainment system has also been a point of contention for some owners.

**Engine**

**2.0L Inline-4**

*   **Oil Consumption**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported excessive oil consumption, requiring more frequent oil top-offs between scheduled oil changes. This can manifest as the low oil pressure light illuminating or discovering significantly less oil during routine checks.
    *   Solution: Monitoring oil levels regularly and addressing any leaks or underlying engine issues. In severe cases, engine repair or replacement may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies greatly depending on the cause and repair needed. Could range from a few hundred dollars to several thousand for an engine rebuild or replacement.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**6-Speed Manual**

*   **Grinding when shifting into 2nd gear**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Owners have reported grinding when shifting into second gear, especially when cold.
    *   Solution: Replacing or rebuilding the transmission. Some owners also report that changing the transmission fluid can help alleviate the issue.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**6-Speed Automatic**

*   No Issues Found

**Suspension**

*   **Rear suspension noise**
    * Description/Symptoms: Some owners report clunking or squeaking noises coming from the rear suspension, especially when going over bumps.
    * Solution: Inspect and replace worn suspension components such as sway bar links, bushings, and shocks.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+ depending on the worn components.
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Freezing/Rebooting**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The Mazda Connect infotainment system can occasionally freeze, become unresponsive, or spontaneously reboot while driving.
    *   Solution: Updating the infotainment system software to the latest version is often the first step. In some cases, replacing the infotainment unit may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 (for software updates) to $500 - $1000+ (for unit replacement).
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Battery Drain**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners report issues with the battery draining quickly, especially if the car is not driven frequently.
    *   Solution: Ensuring all accessories are turned off when the car is off. Testing the battery and alternator to ensure they are functioning properly. Replacing the battery if necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Convertible Top Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Reports of the soft top leaking, especially around the windows or along the seams.
    *   Solution: Adjusting the top's latches and seals, applying sealant, or replacing the soft top.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $150 (for sealant/adjustments) to $1000+ (for replacement).
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Seat Creaking/Squeaking**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners experience creaking or squeaking noises coming from the seats, especially during turns or when shifting weight.
    *   Solution: Tightening seat bolts, lubricating the seat tracks, or replacing worn seat components.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 (for tightening bolts) to $100 - $300+ (for parts and labor).
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2017 Mazda MX-5 Miata generally exhibits good reliability, with most reported issues being relatively minor and not widespread. The most concerning issues involve the excessive oil consumption in the engine, the 2nd gear grind in the manual transmission, and the infotainment system malfunctions. The cost to repair these issues can vary significantly, ranging from affordable fixes to more expensive repairs depending on the severity and cause of the problem. Potential buyers should be aware of these potential issues and thoroughly inspect the vehicle before purchasing.
