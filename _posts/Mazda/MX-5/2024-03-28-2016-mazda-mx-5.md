**Introduction:**

The 2016 Mazda MX-5 is a compact sports car available with two engine options: a 1.5-liter SKYACTIV-G inline-four and a 2.0-liter SKYACTIV-G inline-four. Both engines are paired with a standard six-speed manual transmission or an optional six-speed automatic transmission. The 1.5-liter engine produces 155 horsepower and 150 lb-ft of torque, while the 2.0-liter engine produces 181 horsepower and 151 lb-ft of torque. The MX-5 is praised for its handling, performance, and affordability, but it has received some criticism for its lack of cargo space and rear-seat room.

**Engine**

**1.5-liter SKYACTIV-G Engine**

**Excessive Oil Consumption**
Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported excessive oil consumption, leading to low oil levels and potential engine damage.
Solution: Mazda has issued a Technical Service Bulletin (TSB 01-001/16) to address the issue. The solution involves replacing the piston rings and oil pump.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
Recalls: None Found

**2.0-liter SKYACTIV-G Engine**

**No Issues Found**

**Transmission**

**Six-Speed Manual Transmission**

**Grinding or Difficulty Shifting Gears**
Description/Symptoms: Some owners have experienced grinding or difficulty shifting gears, particularly into third gear.
Solution: The issue can usually be resolved by replacing the shift linkage or adjusting the transmission.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600
Recalls: None Found

**Six-Speed Automatic Transmission**

**Shifting Delays or Hesitations**
Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported shifting delays or hesitations, especially during acceleration or deceleration.
Solution: The issue may be caused by software glitches or electrical problems. Mazda has issued a software update to address the issue.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 - $200
Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

**No Issues Found**

**Electrical**

**Battery Drain**
Description/Symptoms: Some owners have experienced battery drain, leading to dead batteries or difficulty starting the vehicle.
Solution: Mazda has issued a TSB (01-002/16) to address the issue. The solution involves updating the software and replacing the battery.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 - $200
Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

**Windshield Cracking**
Description/Symptoms: Some owners have experienced windshield cracking, especially at the base of the windshield.
Solution: Replacing the windshield is the only solution.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

** rattling door panels**
Description/Symptoms: Some owners have experienced rattling door panels caused by loose or misaligned clips.
Solution: Realigning or replacing the clips can fix the issue.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $200
Recalls: None Found

**Erratic gauge readings**
Description/Symptoms: Some owners have encountered erratic gauge readings, especially on the fuel and speedometer.
Solution: Replacing the affected gauge cluster can resolve the issue.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $300-$500
Recalls: None Found

**Overall Vehicle Summary**

The 2016 Mazda MX-5 generally has good reliability but has experienced some issues, particularly with the 1.5-liter engine and the manual transmission. However, these issues are relatively minor and can be fixed relatively inexpensively. Overall, the MX-5 remains a reliable and enjoyable sports car.
