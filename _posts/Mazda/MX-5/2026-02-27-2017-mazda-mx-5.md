# 2017 Mazda MX-5 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2017 Mazda MX-5, buyers can generally expect an engaging driving experience backed by solid overall engineering. However, reviewing owner-submitted complaints highlights a few specific areas—ranging from glass durability and intermittent electronic steering warnings to manual transmission failures—that deserve a close inspection during your pre-purchase evaluation.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L SKYACTIV-G 4-Cylinder

The 2.0-liter naturally aspirated four-cylinder engine produces around 155 horsepower and 148 lb-ft of torque. It is generally regarded as a reliable and efficient powerplant for everyday driving and spirited weekend trips.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Manual Transmission

The responsive 6-speed manual transmission is a favorite among enthusiasts, though early iterations of this generation have experienced isolated internal component failures reported by owners.

**Manual Transmission Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may experience sudden internal transmission failure without prior warning while driving, resulting in a loss of drive function.
- **Solution:** Inspection by a qualified technician is necessary, which often requires transmission overhaul or complete unit replacement.

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

The available 6-speed automatic transmission offers smooth shifts and reliable operation for drivers who prefer two-pedal commuting.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

Braking performance is sharp and confident, especially with upgraded equipment, though occasional overnight caliper sticking has been documented.

**Brake Sticking**
- **Symptoms:** Performance brake packages can experience calipers sticking after the vehicle has been parked overnight, releasing with a loud pop when moving forward.
- **Solution:** Inspect brake calipers, slide pins, and pads for corrosion or binding, and service or replace components as needed.

## Steering

### Electronic Power Steering

The electronic power steering system provides light and agile handling, but occasional sensor or electrical glitches can trigger warning lights.

**Power Steering Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** A power steering warning light may illuminate on the dashboard, temporarily causing heavy steering effort or loss of power assist.
- **Solution:** Check for diagnostic trouble codes in the power steering control module and inspect electrical connections or steering sensors.

## Exterior

### Windshield and Glass

Exterior glass components are generally standard, though owner reports note susceptibility to surface pitting and stress fractures.

**Windshield Cracking and Rear Glass Shattering**
- **Symptoms:** Windshields may develop unexpected stress cracks or prove unusually susceptible to chip damage from road debris, while rear glass can occasionally shatter spontaneously.
- **Solution:** Replace the damaged windshield or rear glass through an automotive glass specialist.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V072000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission:Control Module (Tcm/Pcm/Tecm)](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mazda&model=MX-5&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** Due to incorrect programming of the Transmission Control Module (TCM), certain conditions may cause the vehicle to unexpectedly downshift and abruptly decelerate. If the vehicle abruptly downshifts, the driver may lose control of the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Mazda will notify owners, and dealers will reprogram the TCM with corrected software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V695000: Air Bags: Air Bag/Restraint Control Module:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mazda&model=MX-5&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** A software error in the air bag control module may cause the front air bags to deploy with excessive force during a crash. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 208, "Occupant Crash Protection." An air bag that deploys with excessive force can increase the risk of injury during a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the Sophisticated Air Bag Sensor (SAS) control module software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2017 Mazda MX-5 remains a highly rewarding used sports car purchase, offering agile handling and reliable core mechanicals. While potential buyers should check for signs of manual transmission wear, steering assist glitches, and windshield damage during a pre-purchase inspection, these reported concerns do not overshadow the vehicle's overall strong reputation.
