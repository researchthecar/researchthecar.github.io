**Introduction**

The 1992 Mazda B2200 is a compact pickup truck available with either a 2.2L or 2.6L gasoline engine, paired with a 5-speed manual or 4-speed automatic transmission. It offers a comfortable cabin, good handling, and a reliable powertrain. However, it falls short in terms of power and towing capacity compared to its competitors.

**Engine**

**2.2L Gasoline Engine**

**Intake Manifold Gasket Failure**
- Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaks, overheating, rough idle, reduced engine power
- Solution: Replace intake manifold gasket
- Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
- Recalls: None Found

**Head Gasket Failure**
- Description/Symptoms: Coolant and oil leaks, overheating, white smoke from exhaust, rough idle
- Solution: Replace head gasket
- Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
- Recalls: None Found

**2.6L Gasoline Engine**

**Timing Chain Failure**
- Description/Symptoms: Rattling or ticking noise from engine, reduced engine power, check engine light
- Solution: Replace timing chain and related components
- Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
- Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**5-Speed Manual Transmission**

**Shifter Bushing Wear**
- Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears, grinding noises
- Solution: Replace shifter bushing
- Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
- Recalls: None Found

**4-Speed Automatic Transmission**

**Transmission Slippage**
- Description/Symptoms: Failure to engage gears, delayed shifting, loss of power
- Solution: Rebuild or replace transmission
- Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
- Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

**Front Suspension**

**Lower Ball Joint Wear**
- Description/Symptoms: Noises when turning or braking, uneven tire wear
- Solution: Replace ball joints
- Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
- Recalls: None Found

**Leaf Spring Failure**
- Description/Symptoms: Sagging rear end, reduced handling and stability
- Solution: Replace leaf springs
- Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
- Recalls: None Found

**Rear Suspension**

**Shock Absorber Failure**
- Description/Symptoms: Bouncy ride, reduced handling and stability
- Solution: Replace shock absorbers
- Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
- Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

**Ignition System Faults**
- Description/Symptoms: Difficulty starting, engine misfires, check engine light
- Solution: Replace ignition coil, spark plugs, or ignition module
- Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+
- Recalls: None Found

**Alternator Failure**
- Description/Symptoms: Battery not charging, lights dimming, electrical problems
- Solution: Replace alternator
- Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
- Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

**Tailgate Corrosion**
- Description/Symptoms: Rust and deterioration of tailgate
- Solution: Replace tailgate or repair corrosion
- Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies based on severity
- Recalls: None Found

**Door Lock Failure**
- Description/Symptoms: Doors not locking or unlocking properly, broken door lock cylinders
- Solution: Replace faulty lock cylinders, lubricate locks
- Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
- Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

**Window Regulator Failure**
- Description/Symptoms: Power windows not operating properly, window getting stuck
- Solution: Replace window regulator motor or switch
- Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
- Recalls: None Found

**Climate Control Malfunction**
- Description/Symptoms: Air conditioning or heater not working, blower motor failure, control panel issues
- Solution: Repair or replace faulty components
- Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies based on issue
- Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 1992 Mazda B2200 is a reliable truck with some known issues. The severity of the issues varies, but some can be expensive to repair, such as timing chain failure or head gasket failure. The truck's strengths lie in its comfortable cabin, good handling, and decent fuel economy. However, potential buyers should be aware of these potential issues before purchasing.
