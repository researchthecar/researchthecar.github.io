**Introduction**

The 2009 Mazda CX-7 is a compact SUV that offers a comfortable and sporty driving experience. It's powered by a standard 2.5L inline-four engine that produces 161 horsepower and 161 lb-ft of torque, paired with a six-speed automatic transmission. A 2.3L turbocharged inline-four engine that produces 244 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque is also available, mated to a six-speed manual or automatic transmission. Drivers appreciate the CX-7's responsive handling, spacious interior, and available all-wheel drive system. However, some owners have reported issues with various components of the vehicle.

**Engine**

**2.5L Inline-Four**

* **Engine Stalling:** The engine may stall intermittently, especially when idling or under acceleration.
    * Solution: Replace the throttle position sensor.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $300+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Oil Consumption:** Excessive oil consumption has been reported, requiring frequent top-offs.
    * Solution: Replace the piston rings or valve stem seals.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Timing Chain Rattle:** A rattling noise may be heard from the engine, indicating a stretched or worn timing chain.
    * Solution: Replace the timing chain and tensioner.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800+
    * Recalls: None Found

**2.3L Turbocharged Inline-Four**

* **Turbocharger Failure:** The turbocharger may fail prematurely, resulting in a loss of power or performance.
    * Solution: Replace the turbocharger.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,000+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **High-Pressure Fuel Pump Failure:** The high-pressure fuel pump may fail, causing difficulty starting the engine or poor performance.
    * Solution: Replace the high-pressure fuel pump.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $1,500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**Six-Speed Automatic**

* **Shifting Issues:** The transmission may exhibit rough shifting, delayed shifts, or slipping gears.
    * Solution: Replace the transmission fluid and filter, or rebuild the transmission.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $2,500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Six-Speed Manual**

* **Clutch Issues:** The clutch may slip or chatter, making it difficult to engage gears.
    * Solution: Replace the clutch assembly.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Strut Mount Failure:** The strut mounts may wear or break, causing clunking or rattling noises.
    * Solution: Replace the strut mounts.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Electrical System Malfunction:** Various electrical issues have been reported, including problems with the battery, alternator, or wiring harness.
    * Solution: Diagnose and repair the specific electrical problem.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the issue
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Sunroof Seal Failure:** The sunroof seal may fail, allowing water to leak into the cabin.
    * Solution: Replace the sunroof seal.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Climate Control Malfunction:** The climate control system may fail to regulate temperature or blow air effectively.
    * Solution: Repair or replace the faulty component.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Seat Heater Malfunction:** The seat heaters may not work properly or overheat.
    * Solution: Replace the seat heater element or relay.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2009 Mazda CX-7 has received mixed reviews from owners. While it offers a comfortable and sporty driving experience, certain issues with the engine, transmission, and other components have been reported. The severity and cost of these issues can vary, but some can be significant. Potential buyers should be aware of these issues and factor them into their decision-making process.
