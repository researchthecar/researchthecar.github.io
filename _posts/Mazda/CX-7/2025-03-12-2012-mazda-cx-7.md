The 2012 Mazda CX-7 is a compact crossover SUV that aimed to blend sporty handling with everyday practicality. Engine options included a naturally aspirated 2.5L inline-4 producing 161 horsepower and 161 lb-ft of torque, and a turbocharged 2.3L inline-4 generating 244 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque. The 2.5L engine was paired with a 5-speed automatic transmission, while the 2.3L turbo came with a 6-speed automatic. People generally liked the CX-7's stylish exterior, responsive handling (especially with the turbo engine), and comfortable front seats. Dislikes often cited include fuel economy, particularly with the turbo engine, a somewhat cramped rear seat, and reported reliability issues.

**Engine**

**2.3L Turbocharged Inline-4**

*   **Turbocharger Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Whining noise from the turbocharger, loss of power, check engine light.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the turbocharger.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Timing Chain Stretch/Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rattling noise upon startup, especially when cold, check engine light, potential for engine damage if the chain breaks.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the timing chain, guides, and tensioner.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Excessive Oil Consumption**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Low oil level warnings, blue smoke from the exhaust, needing to add oil frequently.
    *   Solution: Depending on the cause, solutions can range from replacing valve stem seals to piston rings, or even a complete engine rebuild.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $4,000+ (depending on the severity and chosen repair)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**2.5L Naturally Aspirated Inline-4**

*   **Oil Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Visible oil leaks, especially around the valve cover gasket or oil pan gasket, burning oil smell.
    *   Solution: Replacing the leaking gasket(s).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**6-Speed Automatic (Paired with 2.3L Turbo)**

*   **Harsh Shifting**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rough or jerky shifts, especially between certain gears, transmission slipping.
    *   Solution: Transmission fluid flush and fill, valve body replacement, or transmission rebuild/replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $3,500+ (depending on the severity and chosen repair)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**5-Speed Automatic (Paired with 2.5L Naturally Aspirated)**

*No Issues Found*

**Suspension**

*   **Strut/Shock Absorber Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Bouncy ride, poor handling, clunking noises over bumps, visible leaks from the struts/shocks.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the struts/shocks.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $1,000+ (for all four corners)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Wheel Bearing Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Grinding or humming noise that increases with speed, especially when turning.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the wheel bearing.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500 per wheel
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Battery Drain**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Dead battery, needing frequent jump starts, slow engine cranking.
    *   Solution: Diagnosing and repairing the source of the parasitic drain (e.g., faulty sensor, relay, or module).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+ (depending on the source of the drain)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Power Window/Door Lock Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Windows not operating correctly, door locks failing to lock or unlock.
    *   Solution: Replacing faulty window motors, door lock actuators, or related wiring.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $400 per window/door
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Rust (Especially in areas with road salt)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rust forming around wheel wells, rocker panels, and other areas.
    *   Solution: Rust repair, panel replacement, or rustproofing treatments.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $2,000+ (depending on the severity)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Dashboard Cracking**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Cracks forming in the dashboard, particularly in hot climates.
    *   Solution: Dashboard replacement or repair (e.g., using a dashboard cover).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $1,000+ (depending on the repair method)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Sunroof Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Sunroof not opening or closing properly, leaking, or making excessive noise.
    *   Solution: Repair or replacement of the sunroof motor, track, or seals.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2012 Mazda CX-7, especially with the turbocharged engine, can present owners with a range of potential issues. Engine-related problems, such as turbocharger failure, timing chain issues, and oil consumption, can be costly to repair. Other common complaints involve the transmission, suspension, and electrical systems. While the 2.5L engine is generally more reliable, oil leaks can still be a concern. Overall, potential buyers should be aware of these issues and factor in potential repair costs when considering purchasing a 2012 Mazda CX-7. The severity and cost of repairs can vary greatly depending on the specific issue and the chosen repair method.
