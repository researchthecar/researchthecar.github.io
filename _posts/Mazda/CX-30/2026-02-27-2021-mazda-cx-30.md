# 2021 Mazda CX-30 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2021 Mazda CX-30 delivers upscale styling and confident handling, making it a popular choice in the subcompact crossover segment. While owners generally praise its interior comfort and driving dynamics, public safety and reliability reports highlight a few recurring mechanical and electrical concerns. Buyers should evaluate potential issues ranging from battery drains to driver-assistance sensor behavior before making a purchase.

---

## Engine

### 2.5L 4-Cylinder Engine (Naturally Aspirated)

The base 2.5-liter naturally aspirated four-cylinder engine produces 186 horsepower and 186 lb-ft of torque, offering reliable daily performance for most drivers.

**Excessive Oil Consumption**
- **Symptoms:** Low oil warning light illuminates between scheduled oil changes, with dipstick checks confirming low fluid levels.
- **Solution:** Monitor oil levels regularly between changes and consult a mechanic if consumption persists.

### 2.5L 4-Cylinder Engine (Turbocharged)

The available 2.5-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine increases output to up to 250 horsepower (on premium fuel), providing brisk acceleration and robust performance.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

A traditional 6-speed automatic transmission pairs with both engine options, routing power to front-wheel drive or i-Activ AWD systems with smooth and predictable shifts.

## Brakes

### Rear Brake Pads and Rotors

The service brake system provides adequate stopping power, though some owners report shorter-than-expected service life for rear brake components.

**Rapid Rear Brake Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Rear brake pads and rotors become excessively worn within a short timeframe and mileage.
- **Solution:** Inspect rear brake pads and rotors during routine service and replace worn friction materials.

## Electrical

### Battery and Charging System

Electrical system concerns center primarily around unexpected battery discharge and starting difficulties.

**Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle fails to start after sitting overnight or loses charge completely if doors are left unlocked.
- **Solution:** Test battery health and inspect vehicle body control modules for parasitic power draws.

## Safety

### Advanced Driver Assistance Systems

Active safety systems enhance driver awareness but can occasionally trigger warning lights or deactivate due to environmental factors or sensor issues.

**Camera and Sensor Disabling**
- **Symptoms:** i-Activsense safety cameras and driver support features temporarily disable themselves while driving, disabling cruise control and lane keeping assistance.
- **Solution:** Have dealership technicians inspect camera modules, calibration settings, and windshield alignment.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V086000: Structure:Body:Hatchback/Liftgate](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mazda&model=CX-30&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** When fully opened, the power liftgate may partially lower unexpectedly when the vehicle is parked facing uphill on a slope. If the power liftgate unexpectedly lowers while a person is accessing the rear storage area of the vehicle, it can increase their risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Mazda will notify owners, and dealers will update the power liftgate control module software and inspect the serial number of the power liftgate drive unit, replacing it if necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V101000: Tires:Valve](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mazda&model=CX-30&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** A sudden air leak from the tire air valve may cause a loss of vehicle control while driving, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Mazda will notify owners, and dealers will replace all four tire air valves with new parts, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2021 Mazda CX-30 is a well-rounded subcompact SUV that combines engaging driving dynamics with a refined cabin. Prospective buyers should thoroughly test electrical accessories, verify battery health, and inspect brake wear during a pre-purchase inspection to ensure trouble-free ownership.
