# 2017 Mazda 6

### Overview
The 2017 Mazda 6 is generally regarded as a reliable vehicle, benefiting from Mazda's 'Kodo' design philosophy and 'Skyactiv' technology. This generation of the Mazda 6 is known for its engaging driving dynamics, upscale interior for its class, and good fuel efficiency. Reliability is a strong suit, with owners reporting fewer significant mechanical issues compared to many competitors. Common areas of concern, while infrequent, tend to be minor and often related to specific component failures rather than widespread design flaws. Mazda's commitment to quality engineering is evident in the overall durability of the powertrain and chassis.

## Electrical

### Infotainment System
**Occasional Glitches/Freezing**
- **Symptoms:** Screen may become unresponsive, freeze, or reboot unexpectedly.
- **Solution:** Software update or system reset. In rare cases, head unit replacement.
- **Cost:** $100 - $1500 (Software update is usually free, replacement can be costly)


### Battery Drain
**Intermittent Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Battery may discharge prematurely, leading to starting issues. Can be related to parasitic draw from modules.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis of parasitic draw and identification of faulty module or accessory. Replacement of faulty component.
- **Cost:** $200 - $600

### Summary Verdict
The 2017 Mazda 6 stands out as a highly reliable mid-size sedan. Its Skyactiv powertrains (2.5L engine and 6-speed transmissions) are robust and generally free from significant mechanical complaints. While minor electrical gremlins like infotainment glitches or occasional battery drain can occur, they are not pervasive and often resolvable with software updates or component diagnosis. The chassis, suspension, exterior, and interior components have also demonstrated good durability. Overall, the 2017 Mazda 6 is a solid choice for buyers prioritizing dependability and a refined driving experience with minimal expected long-term repair costs beyond routine maintenance.
