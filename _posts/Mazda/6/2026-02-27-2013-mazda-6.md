# 2013 Mazda 6

### Overview
The 2013 Mazda 6, often praised for its engaging driving dynamics and stylish design, generally presents a solid reliability record. Across its various engine and transmission configurations, owners have reported a good level of dependability. While no vehicle is entirely free from potential issues, the 2013 Mazda 6 tends to avoid widespread, recurring problems that significantly impact its overall ownership experience. Common areas of concern, when they arise, are typically manageable and do not represent systemic flaws. This analysis focuses on documented technical issues beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential areas that may require attention.

### Summary Verdict
The 2013 Mazda 6 generally offers good reliability, particularly with its Skyactiv gasoline engines and transmissions, which are known for their durability. The diesel variant, while offering efficiency, may occasionally encounter carbon buildup issues common to direct-injection diesel engines, requiring proactive maintenance. Electrical gremlins, primarily related to the infotainment system, are the most frequently cited non-routine issues, usually resolvable with software updates. Suspension and exterior/interior components have proven robust. Overall, the 2013 Mazda 6 is a dependable choice, with potential issues being relatively minor and manageable, contributing to a positive ownership experience.
