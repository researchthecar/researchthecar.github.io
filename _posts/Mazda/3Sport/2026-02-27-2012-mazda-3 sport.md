# 2012 Mazda 3 Sport

### Overview
The 2012 Mazda 3 Sport is generally considered a reliable vehicle, offering a good balance of performance and practicality. While most owners report a positive experience, like any vehicle, it has a few areas that have seen more attention than others. This analysis focuses on documented technical issues beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential concerns for owners and prospective buyers. We will examine the engine and transmission options, suspension, electrical systems, and interior/exterior components to give a comprehensive reliability picture.

## Electrical

### Standard System
**Power Window Actuator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Power windows not operating, slow operation, or unusual noises from the door panel.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the power window motor/regulator assembly.
- **Cost:** $200 - $500 per window

**Infotainment System Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Screen freezing, unresponsiveness, Bluetooth connectivity issues.
- **Solution:** Software updates or potential replacement of the infotainment unit.
- **Cost:** $300 - $1000+

### Summary Verdict
The 2012 Mazda 3 Sport is a generally dependable compact car. The Skyactiv engines and transmissions have proven to be robust with minimal reported issues. The primary concerns tend to be minor electrical gremlins like power window failures and occasional infotainment glitches, which are not uncommon in vehicles of this age. The 2.5L engine, while offering more power, has a documented tendency for increased oil consumption that requires diligent monitoring. Suspension and exterior/interior components are largely free of widespread problems. Overall, with regular maintenance and attention to potential oil consumption on the 2.5L, the 2012 Mazda 3 Sport remains a solid choice for reliability.
