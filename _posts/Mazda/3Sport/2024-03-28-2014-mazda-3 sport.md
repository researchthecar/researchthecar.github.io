**Introduction:**

The 2014 Mazda 3 Sport boasts a sleek design and sporty performance. It is available with two different engine and transmission options: a 2.0L Skyactiv-G engine paired with a 6-speed manual transmission or 6-speed automatic transmission, and a 2.5L Skyactiv-G engine paired with a 6-speed automatic transmission only. With the 2.0L engine, it delivers 155 horsepower and 150 lb-ft of torque. The 2.5L engine produces 184 horsepower and 185 lb-ft of torque. Praised for its agile handling and responsive steering, the Mazda 3 Sport offers a fun-to-drive experience. However, it has been reported to have certain issues in different components.

**Engine**

**2.0L Skyactiv-G Engine**

* **Piston Ring Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Excessive oil consumption, blue smoke from exhaust, engine knocking
    * Solution: Piston ring replacement
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Valve Cover Gasket Leak:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks from the valve cover area, visible smoke from the engine bay
    * Solution: Valve cover gasket replacement
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
    * Recalls: None Found

**2.5L Skyactiv-G Engine**

* **Intake Manifold Leak:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Rough idling, loss of power, check engine light
    * Solution: Intake manifold replacement
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**6-Speed Manual Transmission**

* **Shifting Difficulty:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Hard or notchy shifting, especially when cold
    * Solution: Transmission fluid replacement, clutch adjustment
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500
    * Recalls: None Found

**6-Speed Automatic Transmission**

* **Delayed Shift Engagement:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Transmission slow to shift gears, especially from 1st to 2nd
    * Solution: Transmission software update, transmission fluid replacement
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Front Strut Noise:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Knocking noise from front suspension, especially over bumps
    * Solution: Strut replacement
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Alternator Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Battery not charging, electrical system failure
    * Solution: Alternator replacement
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Headlight Malfunction:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Headlights flickering, not turning on
    * Solution: Headlight bulb replacement, wiring harness repair
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Door Lock Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Doors not locking or unlocking properly
    * Solution: Door lock actuator replacement
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Infotainment System Malfunction:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Screen freezing, touch sensitivity issues, audio problems
    * Solution: System reset, software update, hardware replacement
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000
    * Recalls: None Found

**Overall Summary:**

The 2014 Mazda 3 Sport has received positive feedback for its performance and handling. However, it has been reported to experience certain issues, including engine problems, transmission issues, and electrical malfunctions. The severity and cost of these issues vary, with some being minor and inexpensive to fix, while others can be costly and require extensive repairs. It is advisable for owners to be aware of these potential issues and consult a qualified mechanic if any symptoms arise.
