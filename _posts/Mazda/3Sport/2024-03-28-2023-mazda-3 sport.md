**Introduction**

The 2023 Mazda 3 Sport is a compact hatchback that offers a stylish design, a spacious interior, and a responsive driving experience. It is available with a choice of three engine options: a 155-horsepower 2.0-liter inline-four, a 186-horsepower 2.5-liter inline-four, and a 250-horsepower 2.5-liter turbocharged inline-four. The 2.0-liter engine is paired with a six-speed automatic transmission, while the 2.5-liter engines are paired with a six-speed or eight-speed automatic transmission.

People who love the Mazda 3 Sport praise its handling, fuel economy, and overall value. However, some have complained about its lack of power from the base engine, its firm suspension, and its cramped rear seat.

**Engine**

**2.0-liter Inline-Four**

* **Issue:** Low oil consumption
  * Description/Symptoms: The engine may consume excessive amounts of oil, requiring frequent top-ups.
  * Solution: Replace piston rings or valve stem seals.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
  * Recalls: None Found

* **Issue:** Timing chain noise
  * Description/Symptoms: A rattling or ticking noise may be heard from the engine, especially during cold starts.
  * Solution: Replace timing chain and related components.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $1,500+
  * Recalls: None Found

**2.5-liter Inline-Four**

* **No Issues Found**

**2.5-liter Turbocharged Inline-Four**

* **No Issues Found**

**Transmission**

**Six-Speed Automatic**

* **No Issues Found**

**Eight-Speed Automatic**

* **Issue:** Transmission slipping or jerking
  * Description/Symptoms: The transmission may hesitate to shift gears or experience sudden shifts, especially under load or during acceleration.
  * Solution: Replace or repair transmission components.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+
  * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Issue:** Excessive road noise
  * Description/Symptoms: The cabin may experience excessive road noise, especially over rough or uneven surfaces.
  * Solution: Inspect and replace worn or damaged suspension components.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
  * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **No Issues Found**

**Exterior**

* **Issue:** Door lock malfunctions
  * Description/Symptoms: The door locks may not engage or disengage properly, leading to difficulty entering or exiting the vehicle.
  * Solution: Replace faulty door lock actuator or related components.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600+
  * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Issue:** Windshield wiper malfunction
  * Description/Symptoms: The windshield wipers may fail to operate correctly, especially during heavy rain or snowfall.
  * Solution: Replace wiper motor or related electrical components.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
  * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2023 Mazda 3 Sport is a well-rounded compact hatchback with a range of engine and transmission options. However, some owners have reported issues related to oil consumption, timing chain noise, transmission slipping, excessive road noise, door lock malfunctions, and windshield wiper failures. The severity and cost of these issues vary, with some being relatively minor and inexpensive to fix. Overall, the Mazda 3 Sport is still a reliable and enjoyable vehicle for most owners.
