## Electrical

### Standard Electrical System
**Infotainment System Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Occasional freezing, unresponsiveness, or failure to connect Bluetooth devices.
- **Solution:** Software updates or a system reset. In rare cases, replacement of the head unit may be necessary.
- **Cost:** $100 - $800

**Keyless Entry Fobs**
- **Symptoms:** Intermittent issues with remote keyless entry system not unlocking or locking doors.
- **Solution:** Battery replacement in the fob, reprogramming the fob, or potential replacement of the fob.
- **Cost:** $50 - $250

### Summary Verdict
The 2013 Mazda 3 Sport is a commendable choice for those seeking a reliable compact car. Its powertrain options, the 2.0L and 2.5L Skyactiv-G engines paired with either the 6-speed manual or automatic transmission, have demonstrated strong durability with no significant widespread mechanical failures reported. The suspension and exterior components are also generally robust. The primary areas where minor issues have been noted are within the electrical system, specifically concerning the infotainment unit and keyless entry fobs. These are typically resolved with software updates, battery replacements, or reprogramming, and are not indicative of systemic failures. Overall, the 2013 Mazda 3 Sport offers a solid ownership experience with a low probability of major mechanical problems.
