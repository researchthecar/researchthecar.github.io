**2022 Mazda 3 Sport**

The 2022 Mazda 3 Sport is a compact car that is offered with a choice of two engines: a 2.0-liter four-cylinder that produces 155 horsepower and 150 lb-ft of torque, or a 2.5-liter four-cylinder that produces 186 horsepower and 186 lb-ft of torque. Both engines are mated to a six-speed automatic transmission.

The Mazda 3 Sport has received generally positive reviews from critics, who praise its stylish design, comfortable interior, and responsive handling. However, some owners have reported a number of issues with the vehicle, including:

**Engine**

**2.0-liter Four-Cylinder**

* **Intake valve carbon buildup**
    * Description/Symptoms: Reduced engine power, rough idle, and increased fuel consumption.
    * Solution: Intake valve cleaning.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
* **Timing chain tensioner failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Rattling noise from the engine, especially on startup.
    * Solution: Timing chain tensioner replacement.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,200+

**2.5-liter Four-Cylinder**

* **No issues found**

**Transmission**

**Six-Speed Automatic**

* **Harsh shifting**
    * Description/Symptoms: Transmission shifts abruptly or hesitates.
    * Solution: Transmission fluid flush and filter replacement.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
* **Premature transmission failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Complete transmission failure with no warning.
    * Solution: Transmission replacement.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $3,000 - $5,000+

**Suspension**

* **Rear suspension noise**
    * Description/Symptoms: Thumping or clunking noise from the rear of the vehicle.
    * Solution: Replacement of rear suspension components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
* **Front strut mount failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Noise and vibration from the front of the vehicle.
    * Solution: Replacement of front strut mounts.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600

**Electrical**

* **Battery drain**
    * Description/Symptoms: Battery drains quickly, even after the vehicle has been turned off.
    * Solution: Identification and replacement of faulty components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+
* **Infotainment system issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: Infotainment screen freezes, locks up, or does not respond to input.
    * Solution: Software updates or replacement of the infotainment unit.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
    * Recalls: NHTSA Recall Number: 22V-277

**Exterior**

* **Rear bumper paint peeling**
    * Description/Symptoms: Paint on the rear bumper peels or chips easily.
    * Solution: Replacement or refinishing of the rear bumper.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000

**Interior**

* **Wind noise from windows**
    * Description/Symptoms: Excessive wind noise from around the windows.
    * Solution: Adjustment or replacement of window seals.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
* **Loose dashboard panels**
    * Description/Symptoms: Dashboard panels rattle or come loose.
    * Solution: Tightening or replacement of dashboard panels.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300

**Summary**
