**Introduction:**

The 2019 Mazda 3 Sport is a compact hatchback that offers a refined driving experience and a well-appointed interior. It is available with a choice of two engines: a 2.0-liter naturally aspirated engine delivering 155 horsepower and 150 lb-ft of torque, or a 2.5-liter naturally aspirated engine producing 186 horsepower and 186 lb-ft of torque. Transmission options include a six-speed manual or a six-speed automatic.

Drivers appreciate the Mazda 3 Sport's sporty handling, responsive steering, and comfortable interior. However, some common issues have been reported.

**Engine**

**2.0-liter Engine**

* **Carbon Buildup on Intake Valves:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Excessive carbon buildup on intake valves can lead to reduced engine power, rough idling, and poor fuel economy.
    * Solution: Manual cleaning of the intake valves by a mechanic.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**2.5-liter Engine**

* No Issues Found

**Transmission**

**Six-Speed Manual**

* **Gear Selector Issues:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears, grinding noises when shifting, or popping out of gear.
    * Solution: Replacement of the shift linkage or transmission rebuild.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $3,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Six-Speed Automatic**

* No Issues Found

**Suspension**

* **Front Suspension Noise:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Thumping or clunking noises from the front suspension when driving over bumps or turning.
    * Solution: Replacement of worn or damaged suspension components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Alternator Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Loss of electrical power, dim headlights, or a warning light on the dashboard indicating a charging system issue.
    * Solution: Replacement of the alternator.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Headlight Condensation:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Moisture accumulation inside the headlights, causing fogging or dimming of the lights.
    * Solution: Replacement of the headlights or repair of the seals.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Dashboard Squeaks and Rattles:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Annoying noises coming from the dashboard, especially when driving over rough roads.
    * Solution: Tightening of loose screws or replacing faulty components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

While the 2019 Mazda 3 Sport offers a pleasurable driving experience, it has some common issues that should be considered. The severity and cost of these issues vary, with some being relatively minor and inexpensive to fix, while others can result in more significant repairs. Potential buyers should be aware of these issues and factor them into their decision-making process.
