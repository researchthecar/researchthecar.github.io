**2015 Mazda 3 Sport Overview**

The 2015 Mazda 3 Sport is a compact hatchback that offers a stylish exterior, a comfortable interior, and a spirited driving experience. It is powered by a 2.0L 4-cylinder engine that produces 155 horsepower and 150 lb-ft of torque, paired with either a 6-speed manual transmission or a 6-speed automatic transmission.

Mazda 3 drivers praise the vehicle's handling, performance, and fuel efficiency. However, some drivers have reported issues with certain components.

**Engine**

* **2.0L 4-cylinder engine**
    * **Excessive oil consumption:** Symptoms include low oil levels on the dipstick, blue smoke from the exhaust, and engine knocking. Solution: Replace the piston rings and/or valve seals. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
    * **Timing chain noise:** Symptoms include a rattling or ticking noise from the engine, especially during cold starts. Solution: Replace the timing chain and tensioner. Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,200

**Transmission**

* **6-speed manual transmission**
    * **Synchronizer issues:** Symptoms include difficulty shifting into gear, especially from 2nd to 3rd gear. Solution: Replace the synchronizers. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800
* **6-speed automatic transmission**
    * **Hesitation or slipping:** Symptoms include delayed or inconsistent gear changes. Solution: Update the transmission control module software or replace the transmission. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,500+

**Suspension**

* **Front strut mounts:** Symptoms include clunking or rattling noises over bumps. Solution: Replace the strut mounts. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400

**Electrical**

* **CEL illumination due to faulty oxygen sensor:** Symptoms include the Check Engine Light (CEL) being illuminated and the engine running rough. Solution: Replace the oxygen sensor. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $300
* **Dim headlights:** Symptoms include reduced visibility at night. Solution: Replace the headlight bulbs or clean the headlight connections. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
* **Battery drain:** Symptoms include the battery going dead even after a short period of time. Solution: Inspect the electrical system for possible parasitic drains. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+

**Exterior**

* **Premature rust on wheel wells:** Symptoms include corrosion on the inner surfaces of the wheel wells. Solution: Apply an anti-corrosion treatment or replace the affected panels. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+

**Interior**

* **Loose door panel clips:** Symptoms include rattling or squeaking noises from the door panels. Solution: Reattach or replace the loose clips. Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $150
* **Sticky dashboard materials:** Symptoms include the dashboard material becoming tacky or sticky to the touch. Solution: Replace the dashboard. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+

**Summary**

Overall, the 2015 Mazda 3 Sport is a reliable and enjoyable vehicle. However, some drivers have experienced issues with the engine, transmission, suspension, electrical system, and interior components. The severity and cost of these issues vary depending on the specific problem and the extent of the repairs required.
