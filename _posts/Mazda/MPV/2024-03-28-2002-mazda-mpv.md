**2002 Mazda MPV**

The 2002 Mazda MPV was a minivan that was available with a variety of engine and transmission options. It was praised for its spacious interior, comfortable ride, and affordable price. However, it was also criticized for its lack of power, poor fuel economy, and dated design.

**Engine**

* **2.5L V6**
    * **Timing belt failure:** The timing belt can fail, causing catastrophic engine damage. **Symptoms:** Engine will stall or run poorly. **Solution:** Replace the timing belt and tensioner. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $500 - $1,000+
    * **Valve cover gasket leak:** The valve cover gasket can leak, causing oil to leak onto the engine. **Symptoms:** Oil leak from the top of the engine. **Solution:** Replace the valve cover gasket. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $200 - $500
    * **Intake manifold gasket leak:** The intake manifold gasket can leak, causing coolant and oil to leak. **Symptoms:** Coolant leak from the front of the engine, oil leak from the top of the engine. **Solution:** Replace the intake manifold gasket. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $500 - $1,000
    * **EGR valve failure:** The EGR valve can fail, causing the engine to run poorly and produce excessive emissions. **Symptoms:** Engine runs poorly, high emissions. **Solution:** Replace the EGR valve. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $200 - $500

**Transmission**

* **4-speed automatic**
    * **Slipping:** The transmission may slip, causing the vehicle to lose power. **Symptoms:** Transmission slips while shifting gears. **Solution:** Replace or rebuild the transmission. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $1,500 - $3,000+
    * **Hard shifting:** The transmission may shift hard, causing the vehicle to jerk. **Symptoms:** Transmission shifts hard between gears. **Solution:** Replace or rebuild the transmission. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $1,500 - $3,000+
    * **No Issues Found**
* **5-speed manual**
    * **No Issues Found**

**Suspension**

* **Front struts:** The front struts can wear out, causing the vehicle to ride rough and handle poorly. **Symptoms:** Rough ride, poor handling. **Solution:** Replace the front struts. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $500 - $1,000
* **Rear shocks:** The rear shocks can wear out, causing the vehicle to ride rough and handle poorly. **Symptoms:** Rough ride, poor handling. **Solution:** Replace the rear shocks. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $300 - $600

**Electrical**

* **Battery drain:** The battery can drain prematurely, causing the vehicle to fail to start. **Symptoms:** Battery drains quickly, vehicle may not start. **Solution:** Test the battery and charging system, replace the battery or alternator as needed. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $100 - $500
* **Alternator failure:** The alternator can fail, causing the battery to drain and the vehicle to lose power. **Symptoms:** Battery light illuminates on the dash, vehicle may lose power. **Solution:** Replace the alternator. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $500 - $1,000

**Exterior**

* **Door handles breaking:** The door handles can break, making it difficult to open the doors. **Symptoms:** Door handle breaks off. **Solution:** Replace the door handle. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $200 - $500
* **Power window failure:** The power windows can fail, preventing them from being opened or closed. **Symptoms:** Power windows do not work. **Solution:** Replace the power window motor or switch. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $200 - $500

**Interior**

* **Dashboard cracking:** The dashboard can crack, causing the interior to look unsightly. **Symptoms:** Cracks in the dashboard. **Solution:** Replace the dashboard. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $500 - $1,000
* **Seat fabric tearing:** The seat fabric can tear, causing the interior to look unsightly. **Symptoms:** Tears in the seat fabric. **Solution:** Repair or replace the seat fabric. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $200 - $500

**Summary**

The 2002 Mazda MPV has a number of potential issues, including timing belt failure, valve cover gasket leaks, intake manifold gasket leaks, EGR valve failure, transmission slipping, hard shifting, worn suspension components, battery drain, alternator failure, breaking door handles, power window failure, dashboard cracking, and seat fabric tearing. The severity of these issues can vary, and the cost to fix them can be significant. Overall, the 2002 Mazda MPV is a reliable vehicle, but it is important to be aware of its potential problems before purchasing one.
