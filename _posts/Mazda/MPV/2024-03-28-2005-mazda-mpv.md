**Introduction:**

The 2005 Mazda MPV is a versatile minivan known for its spacious interior, comfortable seating, and reliable performance. It offers a choice of two engine options: a 3.0L V6 engine with 200 horsepower and 200 lb-ft of torque, and a 2.3L I4 engine with 155 horsepower and 154 lb-ft of torque. Both engines are paired with a 5-speed automatic transmission.

**Engine**

**3.0L V6**

* **Intake manifold gasket failure:** Causes a coolant leak and engine overheating. Symptoms include low coolant levels, white smoke from the exhaust, and a decrease in engine power. Solution involves replacing the intake manifold gasket. Estimated cost: $500 - $700+
* **Ignition coil failure:** Results in a misfire and poor engine performance. Symptoms include rough idling, loss of power, and increased fuel consumption. Solution requires replacing the ignition coils. Estimated cost: $200 - $400+
* **Timing chain tensioner failure:** Can lead to a timing chain jump and catastrophic engine damage. Symptoms include a rattling noise from the engine and a loss of power. Solution involves replacing the timing chain tensioner and timing chain. Estimated cost: $1,500 - $2,000+

**2.3L I4**

* **No Issues Found**

**Transmission**

**5-speed Automatic**

* **Slipping transmission:** Occurs when the transmission does not fully engage a gear, resulting in a loss of power and poor acceleration. Symptoms include delayed or slipping shifts and a jerking motion during gear changes. Solution involves rebuilding or replacing the transmission. Estimated cost: $1,500 - $3,000+
* **Torque converter failure:** Causes a shuddering or vibration during acceleration. Symptoms include loss of power, noise from the transmission, and a delayed engagement of gears. Solution requires replacing the torque converter. Estimated cost: $500 - $1,000+

**Suspension**

* **Strut mounts worn or damaged:** Results in a clunking or rattling noise when driving over bumps. Symptoms include poor handling, excessive tire wear, and a decrease in ride quality. Solution involves replacing the strut mounts. Estimated cost: $200 - $400+
* **Sway bar links worn or loose:** Causes a clunking noise when turning or driving over bumps. Symptoms include poor handling and a decrease in ride stability. Solution requires replacing the sway bar links. Estimated cost: $100 - $200+

**Electrical**

* **Alternator failure:** Prevents the battery from charging, leading to a loss of power. Symptoms include dim headlights, difficulty starting the engine, and a check engine light. Solution involves replacing the alternator. Estimated cost: $300 - $500+
* **Battery drain:** Causes the vehicle to not start or lose power. Symptoms include a rapid discharge of the battery and a clicking sound when attempting to start the engine. Solution involves identifying and fixing the cause of the battery drain, such as a faulty component or parasitic draw. Estimated cost: Varies depending on the cause

**Exterior**

* **Tailgate handle failure:** Causes difficulty opening the tailgate. Symptoms include a broken handle or a handle that is stuck in place. Solution involves replacing the tailgate handle. Estimated cost: $100 - $200+
* **Rear bumper fascia cracking:** Occurs due to impact or stress. Symptoms include cracks or damage to the bumper fascia. Solution involves repairing or replacing the bumper fascia. Estimated cost: $300 - $500+

**Interior**

* **HVAC blower motor failure:** Results in a lack of airflow from the heating or air conditioning system. Symptoms include no air blowing from the vents and poor climate control. Solution involves replacing the blower motor. Estimated cost: $200 - $400+
* **Dashboard cracking:** Occurs due to heat or UV exposure. Symptoms include cracks or splits in the dashboard surface. Solution involves repairing or replacing the dashboard. Estimated cost: $500 - $1,000+

**Recalls:**

* 05V342000: Faulty seat belt buckle that may not properly engage
* 05V508000: Inoperative daytime running lights
* 05V443000: Incorrect tire pressure label

**Summary:**

The 2005 Mazda MPV is a generally reliable vehicle with a comfortable and spacious interior. However, it does have some potential issues to be aware of, particularly in the areas of engine, transmission, and electrical components. The severity and cost of these issues vary, but it is important to address them promptly to avoid further problems or safety concerns. Regular maintenance and timely repairs can help to extend the life and reliability of this popular minivan.
