**Introduction:**

The 1996 Mazda MPV is a versatile minivan that offers ample space and practicality. It is powered by a 3.0L V6 engine, which produces 170 horsepower and 190 lb-ft of torque. The MPV is available with either a 4-speed automatic or 5-speed manual transmission. While it has a spacious interior and a smooth ride, the MPV is not without its drawbacks.

**Engine**

**3.0L V6**

* **Oil Leaks:** Oil leaks can occur from the valve cover gaskets, oil pan gasket, or rear main seal. Symptoms may include spotting oil on the ground or a burning smell. The repair involves replacing the leaking gaskets or seals. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
* **Intake Manifold Gasket Failure:** The intake manifold gasket can fail, causing coolant and oil to leak. Symptoms may include overheating, coolant loss, and poor engine performance. The repair involves replacing the intake manifold gasket. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
* **Head Gasket Failure:** Head gasket failure can occur, which can cause coolant and oil to mix. Symptoms may include overheating, coolant loss, and white smoke from the exhaust. The repair is complex and requires removal of the cylinder head. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+

**Transmission**

**4-Speed Automatic**

* **Shifting Problems:** The automatic transmission may experience shifting problems, such as delayed shifting or slipping gears. Symptoms may include difficulty shifting, erratic vehicle speed, or shuddering. The repair may involve replacing the transmission control module, transmission fluid filter, or rebuilding the transmission. Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $2,000+

**5-Speed Manual**

* **Clutch Problems:** The clutch may wear out or fail, causing difficulty shifting gears or slipping. Symptoms may include a burning smell, grinding noises when shifting, or a spongy clutch pedal. The repair involves replacing the clutch assembly. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+

**Suspension**

* **Strut Failure:** The struts can wear out, causing a bumpy or unstable ride. Symptoms may include bouncing, clunking noises, or poor handling. The repair involves replacing the struts. Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800+
* **Ball Joint Failure:** The ball joints can wear out, causing looseness in the suspension. Symptoms may include clunking noises, loose steering, or uneven tire wear. The repair involves replacing the ball joints. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
* **Sway Bar Bushing Deterioration:** The sway bar bushings can deteriorate, causing noise and reduced handling. Symptoms may include clunking noises over bumps or corners. The repair involves replacing the sway bar bushings. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+

**Electrical**

* **Electrical Panel Malfunction:** The electrical panel may malfunction, which can affect various electrical components. Symptoms may include inoperative windows, door locks, or lights. The repair may involve diagnosing and replacing faulty electrical components. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600+
* **Alternator Failure:** The alternator may fail, which can lead to battery drain and electrical system problems. Symptoms may include dimming lights, slow cranking, or a warning light on the dashboard. The repair involves replacing the alternator. Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800+

**Exterior**

* **Door Handle Failure:** The door handles can fail, which can make it difficult to open the doors. Symptoms may include loose or broken door handles. The repair involves replacing the door handles. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+

**Interior**

* **Center Console Latch Malfunction:** The center console latch may malfunction, which can prevent the console from locking or opening. Symptoms may include a loose or broken latch. The repair involves replacing the latch. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
* **Dash Panel Cracking:** The dash panel may crack over time. Symptoms may include cracks in the dash panel. The repair involves replacing the dash panel or using a dash cover. Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800+

**Summary**

The 1996 Mazda MPV has several common issues, including engine leaks, transmission problems, suspension issues, electrical malfunctions, door handle failures, and interior design flaws. The severity of the issues varies, but some can be costly to repair. Overall, the MPV is a practical minivan, but it is important to be aware of its potential issues before making a purchase.
