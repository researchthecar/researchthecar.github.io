**Introduction**

The 2001 Mazda MPV is a 7-passenger minivan that was offered with two engine options: a 2.5L V6 and a 3.0L V6. The 2.5L V6 produced 170 horsepower and 160 lb-ft of torque, while the 3.0L V6 produced 200 horsepower and 200 lb-ft of torque. Both engines were paired with a 4-speed automatic transmission.

The MPV was praised for its spacious interior, comfortable ride, and available all-wheel drive system. However, it was also criticized for its lack of power, poor fuel economy, and reliability issues.

**Engine**

**2.5L V6**

* **Head gasket failure:** Description/Symptoms: The head gasket can fail, causing coolant and oil to leak. This can lead to overheating and engine damage if not repaired promptly. Solution: The head gasket must be replaced. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
* **Timing belt replacement:** Description/Symptoms: The timing belt needs to be replaced every 60,000 miles. If it fails, it can cause catastrophic engine damage. Solution: The timing belt and related components should be replaced as part of the scheduled maintenance. Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800

**3.0L V6**

* **No major issues found**

**Transmission**

**4-Speed Automatic**

* **Transmission slipping:** Description/Symptoms: The transmission may slip or shudder when shifting gears. This can be caused by worn clutch packs or other internal components. Solution: The transmission may need to be rebuilt or replaced. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+

**Suspension**

* **Strut mount failure:** Description/Symptoms: The strut mounts can fail, causing the struts to make noise or become unstable. Solution: The strut mounts should be replaced. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
* **Sway bar link failure:** Description/Symptoms: The sway bar links can fail, causing the vehicle to sway excessively when cornering. Solution: The sway bar links should be replaced. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200

**Electrical**

* **Alternator failure:** Description/Symptoms: The alternator can fail, causing the battery to discharge and the vehicle to stall. Solution: The alternator should be replaced. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
* **Starter failure:** Description/Symptoms: The starter can fail, preventing the engine from starting. Solution: The starter should be replaced. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500

**Exterior**

* **Rust:** Description/Symptoms: The body of the MPV can rust, especially in areas where salt is used on the roads. Solution: Rust can be prevented or repaired by applying rust-resistant coatings or replacing rusted panels. Estimated Cost to Fix: Dependent on severity of rust
* **Power window failure:** Description/Symptoms: The power windows may fail to operate due to electrical issues or mechanical failure. Solution: The window motor or switch may need to be replaced. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500

**Interior**

* **Dashboard rattle:** Description/Symptoms: The dashboard may rattle or squeak due to loose components. Solution: The loose components should be tightened or replaced. Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $150
* **Door panel issues:** Description/Symptoms: The door panels may rattle or become loose. Solution: The door panels should be tightened or the clips replaced. Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $150

**Summary**

The 2001 Mazda MPV has a number of potential issues, many of which are related to the engine, transmission, and electrical system. While the MPV is a spacious and comfortable vehicle, its reliability issues can be a major concern for potential buyers. The severity and cost of the repairs can vary depending on the specific issue and the condition of the vehicle.
