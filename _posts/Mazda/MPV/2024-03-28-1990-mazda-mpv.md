Introduced in 1990, the Mazda MPV was a revolutionary minivan that offered a unique combination of space, comfort, and performance. It was available with two engine options: a 2.6-liter V6 and a 3.0-liter V6, both mated to a 4-speed automatic transmission. Despite its popularity, the 1990 Mazda MPV has been plagued by a number of issues over the years.

**Engine**

* **2.6-liter V6**
    * **Intake manifold gasket failure:** Causes coolant and oil leaks, leading to overheating and engine damage. Solution: Replace intake manifold gaskets. Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $600+
    * **Timing belt failure:** If the timing belt fails, it can cause catastrophic engine damage. Solution: Replace timing belt and tensioner according to maintenance schedule. Estimated Cost to Fix: $600 - $900+
    * **Valve cover gasket leaks:** Leaks oil, causing a burning smell and smoke. Solution: Replace valve cover gaskets. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
* **3.0-liter V6**
    * **Head gasket failure:** Blown head gaskets can cause coolant leaks, overheating, and engine damage. Solution: Replace head gaskets. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,000+

**Transmission**

* **4-speed automatic transmission**
    * **Transmission slipping:** Gears can slip or fail to engage, causing difficulty driving. Solution: Rebuild or replace transmission. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $3,000+
    * **Shift solenoid failure:** Causes harsh or delayed shifting. Solution: Replace shift solenoid. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+

**Suspension**

* **Rear suspension sag:** Worn or broken rear suspension components can cause the vehicle to sag or handle poorly. Solution: Replace worn components. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
* **Struts and shocks:** Worn or damaged struts and shocks can affect ride quality and handling. Solution: Replace struts and shocks. Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800+

**Electrical**

* **Alternator failure:** A faulty alternator can lead to battery drain and electrical problems. Solution: Replace alternator. Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $600+
* **Starter failure:** A worn or damaged starter can prevent the vehicle from starting. Solution: Replace starter. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+
* **Electrical wiring issues:** Faulty wiring can cause a variety of electrical problems, such as flickering lights or inoperative components. Solution: Diagnose and repair faulty wiring. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+

**Exterior**

* **Door lock failure:** Door locks can fail to operate properly, making it difficult to enter or exit the vehicle. Solution: Replace door lock actuators. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
* **Rust:** The 1990 Mazda MPV is prone to rust, especially in areas exposed to road salt. Solution: Repair or prevent rust with rustproofing treatments. Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on severity of rust

**Interior**

* **Dashboard cracking:** The dashboard can crack or warp over time. Solution: Replace dashboard. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
* **Seat upholstery tearing:** The seat upholstery can tear or wear over time. Solution: Repair or replace seat covers. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+

**Summary**

The 1990 Mazda MPV was a popular minivan that offered a lot of value for the money. However, it has been plagued by a number of issues over the years, including engine problems, transmission issues, and electrical problems. These issues can be expensive to fix, so it is important to be aware of them before purchasing a used 1990 Mazda MPV.
