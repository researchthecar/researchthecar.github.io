# 2002 Mazda 626

### Overview
The 2002 Mazda 626, a mid-size sedan known for its comfortable ride and practical design, generally presented a respectable reliability record for its era. While not without its quirks, many owners found it to be a dependable vehicle. Common areas of concern tended to be related to aging electrical components and occasional issues with specific engine or transmission variants, particularly as mileage increased. However, for its time, the 626 offered a good balance of features and value, making it a popular choice in the competitive sedan market. Regular maintenance was key to ensuring its longevity.

---


## Engine

### 2.0L FS-DE (Naturally Aspirated)
**Oil Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Visible oil under the vehicle, burning oil smell, low oil level.
- **Solution:** Replacement of seals and gaskets (e.g., valve cover gasket, oil pan gasket).
- **Cost:** $150 - $350

**Coolant Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Overheating, visible coolant loss, sweet smell from engine bay.
- **Solution:** Replacement of hoses, radiator, or water pump.
- **Cost:** $200 - $600


### 2.5L Ford Duratec V6 (Optional)
**Intake Manifold Gasket Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Vacuum leaks, rough idle, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of intake manifold gaskets.
- **Cost:** $300 - $700


## Transmission

### 5-Speed Manual
**Clutch Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Slipping clutch, difficulty shifting gears, grinding noises.
- **Solution:** Clutch replacement (clutch kit).
- **Cost:** $600 - $1200


### 4-Speed Automatic (GF4A-EL)
**Solenoid Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Harsh shifting, delayed engagement, transmission warning light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of transmission solenoids.
- **Cost:** $300 - $700

**Torque Converter Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Shuddering, slipping, poor acceleration.
- **Solution:** Torque converter replacement.
- **Cost:** $700 - $1500


## Suspension

### Standard Chassis
**Worn Ball Joints and Bushings**
- **Symptoms:** Clunking noises over bumps, poor handling, uneven tire wear.
- **Solution:** Replacement of worn ball joints and suspension bushings.
- **Cost:** $300 - $800

**Rear Wheel Bearing Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Humming or grinding noise from the rear that increases with speed.
- **Solution:** Replacement of rear wheel bearings.
- **Cost:** $250 - $500


## Electrical

### General Electrical System
**Window Regulator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Power windows not operating, slow window movement.
- **Solution:** Replacement of window regulator mechanism.
- **Cost:** $150 - $300 per window

**Alternator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Dimming lights, battery warning light, engine stalling.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the alternator.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600

**HVAC Blower Motor Resistor**
- **Symptoms:** Fan only works on highest setting, or not at all.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the blower motor resistor.
- **Cost:** $75 - $150


## Exterior

### Body and Trim
**Rust on Wheel Arches and Rocker Panels**
- **Symptoms:** Visible rust or bubbling paint on lower body panels.
- **Solution:** Rust repair and repainting.
- **Cost:** $300 - $1000+

**Headlight Haze/Oxidation**
- **Symptoms:** Yellowed or cloudy headlights, reduced visibility.
- **Solution:** Headlight restoration or replacement.
- **Cost:** $50 - $200


## Interior

### Cabin Components
**HVAC Control Panel Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Buttons not responding, inconsistent fan speed control.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of the HVAC control unit.
- **Cost:** $200 - $500

**Door Lock Actuator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Power locks not working on one or more doors.
- **Solution:** Replacement of door lock actuators.
- **Cost:** $100 - $250 per door


--- 
### Summary Verdict
The 2002 Mazda 626 is a generally reliable vehicle, especially with the 2.0L engine and manual transmission. Common issues tend to be age-related, such as oil and coolant leaks, worn suspension components, and electrical gremlins like window regulators and HVAC resistors. The automatic transmission can be prone to solenoid or torque converter issues. Exterior rust can be a concern in certain climates. Overall, with diligent maintenance and addressing minor issues promptly, the 626 can remain a dependable daily driver, offering good value for its age.
