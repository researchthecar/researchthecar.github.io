# 2001 Mazda 626

### Overview
The 2001 Mazda 626, a mid-size sedan known for its sporty handling and practical design, generally presented a respectable reliability record for its era. While not without its quirks, many owners found it to be a dependable vehicle when properly maintained. Common areas of concern tended to be related to specific powertrain components and some electrical systems, though widespread catastrophic failures were not the norm. The vehicle offered a comfortable ride and a well-appointed interior for its class, contributing to its appeal. Overall, the 2001 626 can be considered a solid choice, with potential buyers advised to be aware of a few recurring issues.

---


## Engine

### 2.0L FS-DE (Non-VVT)
**Oil Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Visible oil spots under the vehicle, burning oil smell, low oil level.
- **Solution:** Replacement of seals and gaskets (e.g., valve cover gasket, oil pan gasket).
- **Cost:** $150 - $350

**Coolant Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Overheating, visible coolant puddles, sweet smell, white smoke from exhaust.
- **Solution:** Replacement of hoses, radiator, or water pump.
- **Cost:** $200 - $600


### 2.0L FS-ZE (VVT)
**Variable Valve Timing (VVT) Actuator Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Rough idle, poor performance, check engine light, rattling noise on startup.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the VVT actuator and potentially related solenoids.
- **Cost:** $300 - $700


## Transmission

### 5-Speed Manual
**Clutch Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Slipping clutch, difficulty shifting gears, burning smell.
- **Solution:** Clutch kit replacement (clutch disc, pressure plate, release bearing).
- **Cost:** $500 - $1000


### 4-Speed Automatic (GF4A-EL)
**Solenoid Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Harsh shifting, delayed engagement, transmission warning light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of transmission solenoids.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600

**Torque Converter Problems**
- **Symptoms:** Shuddering at low speeds, slipping, poor acceleration.
- **Solution:** Rebuild or replacement of the torque converter.
- **Cost:** $600 - $1200


## Suspension

### Standard Chassis
**Worn Control Arm Bushings**
- **Symptoms:** Clunking noises over bumps, vague steering, uneven tire wear.
- **Solution:** Replacement of control arm bushings or entire control arms.
- **Cost:** $300 - $700

**Strut Mount Bearing Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Popping or creaking noise when turning the steering wheel.
- **Solution:** Replacement of strut mount bearings.
- **Cost:** $150 - $300


## Electrical

### General Electrical System
**Window Regulator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Power windows not operating, slow window movement, grinding noise.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the window regulator mechanism.
- **Cost:** $200 - $400

**Alternator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Dimming headlights, battery warning light, difficulty starting.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the alternator.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600


## Exterior

### Body Panels and Trim
**Rust on Wheel Arches and Sills**
- **Symptoms:** Visible corrosion and bubbling paint on lower body panels.
- **Solution:** Rust repair and repainting, or replacement of affected panels.
- **Cost:** $400 - $1500+


## Interior

### Interior Components
**HVAC Blower Motor Resistor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Fan only works on highest setting, or not at all.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the blower motor resistor.
- **Cost:** $100 - $250


--- 
### Summary Verdict
The 2001 Mazda 626 offers a generally reliable ownership experience, particularly with the 2.0L FS-DE engine. Potential buyers should be aware of common issues such as oil and coolant leaks, which are often manageable with timely repairs. The automatic transmission can sometimes develop solenoid or torque converter problems, while manual transmissions may require clutch replacements over time. Suspension components like control arm bushings and strut mounts are also known wear items. Electrical gremlins, such as failing window regulators and alternators, can occur. Exterior rust, especially in the wheel arches and sills, is a concern in certain climates. Overall, the 626 is a decent car, but thorough inspection and awareness of these potential issues are recommended.
