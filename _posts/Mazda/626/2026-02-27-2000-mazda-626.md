# 2000 Mazda 626

### Overview
The 2000 Mazda 626, a mid-size sedan known for its sporty handling and practical design, generally offered a good balance of performance and reliability for its era. While not without its quirks, many owners reported a positive ownership experience. Common areas of concern tended to be related to age and mileage, particularly with certain powertrain components and electrical systems. However, for its time, the 626 was a competitive offering in its segment, with many units providing dependable service when properly maintained. This analysis focuses on documented common issues beyond routine maintenance.

---


## Engine

### 2.0L FS-DE (Naturally Aspirated)
**Intake Manifold Gasket Leak**
- **Symptoms:** Vacuum leaks, rough idle, potential loss of power.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the intake manifold gasket.
- **Cost:** $150 - $300

**Oil Leaks (Valve Cover Gasket)**
- **Symptoms:** Visible oil on the engine block, burning oil smell, low oil level.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the valve cover gasket.
- **Cost:** $100 - $250


### 2.5L Ford Duratec V6 (Naturally Aspirated)
**Timing Chain Tensioner Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Rattling noise from the front of the engine, especially on startup.
- **Solution:** Replacement of timing chain tensioners and guides.
- **Cost:** $600 - $1200

**Coolant Leaks (Intake Manifold)**
- **Symptoms:** Sweet smell of coolant, visible coolant residue, overheating.
- **Solution:** Replacement of intake manifold gaskets and potentially the manifold itself.
- **Cost:** $400 - $800


## Transmission

### 5-Speed Manual
**Clutch Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Slipping clutch, difficulty shifting gears, burning smell.
- **Solution:** Clutch kit replacement (clutch disc, pressure plate, release bearing).
- **Cost:** $500 - $900


### 4-Speed Automatic (F4A-EL)
**Harsh Shifting / Delayed Engagement**
- **Symptoms:** Jerky shifts between gears, delayed engagement when shifting into Drive or Reverse.
- **Solution:** Transmission fluid and filter change, solenoid replacement, or transmission rebuild.
- **Cost:** $200 - $2500+

**Torque Converter Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Shuddering at low speeds, slipping, whining noise.
- **Solution:** Torque converter replacement.
- **Cost:** $700 - $1500


## Suspension

### Standard Chassis
**Worn Control Arm Bushings**
- **Symptoms:** Clunking noises over bumps, vague steering feel, uneven tire wear.
- **Solution:** Replacement of control arm bushings or entire control arms.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600

**Rear Shock Absorber Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Bouncy ride, visible fluid leaks on shock bodies.
- **Solution:** Replacement of rear shock absorbers.
- **Cost:** $200 - $400


## Electrical

### General Electrical System
**Window Regulator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Power windows not operating, slow window movement, grinding noise.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the window regulator mechanism.
- **Cost:** $150 - $300 per window

**Instrument Cluster Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** Dim or non-functional gauges, flickering lights, intermittent power loss to cluster.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of the instrument cluster.
- **Cost:** $300 - $700

**Alternator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Battery warning light illuminated, dimming headlights, starting issues.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the alternator.
- **Cost:** $300 - $500


## Exterior

### Body Panels & Trim
**Rust (Wheel Arches, Rocker Panels)**
- **Symptoms:** Visible corrosion and bubbling paint on lower body sections.
- **Solution:** Rust repair and repainting, or panel replacement.
- **Cost:** $200 - $1000+ per panel

**Headlight Haze/Clouding**
- **Symptoms:** Reduced headlight visibility, yellowed or foggy lenses.
- **Solution:** Headlight restoration kit or replacement of headlight assemblies.
- **Cost:** $50 - $300


## Interior

### Cabin Components
**HVAC Blower Motor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** No airflow from vents, or intermittent airflow.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the blower motor and resistor.
- **Cost:** $150 - $300

**Seat Fabric Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Torn or worn seat upholstery, especially on driver's side bolster.
- **Solution:** Seat cover replacement or professional upholstery repair.
- **Cost:** $100 - $500


--- 
### Summary Verdict
The 2000 Mazda 626 presents a generally reliable profile for its age, with most reported issues stemming from typical wear and tear on components like gaskets, suspension parts, and electrical accessories. The engines, particularly the 2.0L, are known for their durability, though the 2.5L V6 can have timing chain related concerns. Automatic transmissions may require attention for shifting issues as mileage increases. Electrical components, such as window regulators and instrument clusters, are common failure points on many vehicles of this vintage. Exterior rust can be a concern in certain climates. Overall, the 626 is a solid choice if well-maintained, but potential buyers should be aware of these common repair areas.
