## Electrical

### Standard Electrical System
**Power Window Actuator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Power windows may become slow, inoperative, or make grinding noises.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the power window motor and regulator assembly.
- **Cost:** $250 - $500

**Keyless Entry Fob Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Remote key fob may stop working intermittently or entirely.
- **Solution:** Reprogramming or replacement of the key fob battery or the fob itself.
- **Cost:** $50 - $200


## Exterior

### Body Panels and Trim
**Paint Fading/Peeling**
- **Symptoms:** Paint on certain panels, particularly the roof or hood, may exhibit fading or peeling over time, especially in areas with high sun exposure.
- **Solution:** Repainting affected panels.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1500+


## Interior

### Cabin Components
**HVAC Blower Motor Resistor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The fan speed may only work on certain settings (often high) or not at all.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the blower motor resistor.
- **Cost:** $100 - $250

**Seat Fabric Wear**
- **Symptoms:** The fabric on the driver's seat, particularly the bolsters, may show premature wear or pilling.
- **Solution:** Seat cover installation or professional upholstery repair/replacement.
- **Cost:** $150 - $400


--- 
### Summary Verdict
The 2012 Mazda 2 is a generally dependable vehicle with a good reputation for reliability. The powertrain (engine and transmission) is robust and rarely presents significant issues beyond normal wear. The most commonly reported problems tend to be in the electrical system, such as power window failures and key fob issues, and some minor interior and exterior wear concerns like paint fading or seat fabric wear. These are typically not critical failures and are manageable with maintenance. Overall, the 2012 Mazda 2 remains a solid choice for budget-conscious buyers seeking a reliable small car.
