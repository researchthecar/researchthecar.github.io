The 2019 Mazda 2 is a subcompact car known for its stylish design, fuel efficiency, and engaging driving dynamics. For the 2019 model year, the Mazda 2 was primarily offered with a 1.5-liter Skyactiv-G inline-4 engine, producing around 108 horsepower and 103 lb-ft of torque. This engine was typically paired with either a 6-speed manual or a 6-speed automatic transmission depending on the market. Owners often praise the Mazda 2 for its nimble handling, comfortable interior, and user-friendly infotainment system. Common dislikes include the somewhat limited rear passenger space and cargo capacity.

**Engine**

**1.5L Skyactiv-G Inline-4**

* **Excessive Oil Consumption**
    * Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported higher-than-normal oil consumption, requiring more frequent oil top-ups between scheduled services. Symptoms include the low oil level warning light illuminating and visible exhaust smoke in severe cases.
    * Solution: In some cases, replacing the piston rings or the entire engine may be necessary. A compression test can help diagnose the severity of the issue.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $5,000+ (depending on the extent of the repairs)
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Engine Misfires**
    * Description/Symptoms: A small number of owners have noted random engine misfires, often accompanied by a check engine light. The issue can be intermittent.
    * Solution: Diagnosis may involve checking the spark plugs, ignition coils, and fuel injectors. Replacing faulty components is the usual fix.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+ (depending on the affected components)
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**6-Speed Automatic**

* **Rough Shifting**
    * Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported experiencing rough or jerky shifting, particularly between certain gears. This can be more noticeable at lower speeds.
    * Solution: A transmission fluid flush and software update might resolve the issue. In more severe cases, valve body replacement or a transmission rebuild may be required.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $3,000+ (depending on the severity and required repair)
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **No Issues Found**

**Electrical**

* **Infotainment System Freezing/Crashing**
    * Description/Symptoms: The infotainment system occasionally freezes or crashes, requiring a restart to restore functionality. This can affect navigation, audio, and Bluetooth connectivity.
    * Solution: A software update might resolve the issue. In some cases, the head unit may need to be replaced.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 (software update) - $800+ (head unit replacement)
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Battery Drain Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: Some owners have experienced premature battery drain, particularly if the car is not driven frequently.
    * Solution: Check for parasitic drains caused by aftermarket accessories or faulty electrical components. A battery replacement or addressing the underlying drain may be necessary.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **No Issues Found**

**Interior**

* **Rattling Noises**
    * Description/Symptoms: Some owners report rattling noises emanating from the dashboard or door panels, especially on rough roads.
    * Solution: Identifying the source of the rattle and securing the loose components. This may involve adding foam padding or tightening screws.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 (DIY) - $200+ (professional repair)
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2019 Mazda 2 is generally considered a reliable vehicle. The most commonly reported issues relate to excessive oil consumption in the engine and rough shifting in the automatic transmission. While the engine issues can be more costly to address, other complaints are typically less severe and easier to resolve. Overall, potential buyers should be aware of these issues and thoroughly inspect the vehicle before purchasing, paying particular attention to the engine's oil level and the transmission's shifting behavior. The infotainment issues are annoying but generally less severe.
