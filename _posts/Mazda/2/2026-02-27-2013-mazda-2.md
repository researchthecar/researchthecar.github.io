# 2013 Mazda 2

### Overview
The 2013 Mazda 2 is generally considered a reliable subcompact car, known for its agile handling and fuel efficiency. It was offered with a single engine and transmission option in most markets, simplifying its mechanical complexity. While it doesn't typically suffer from major widespread mechanical failures, like any vehicle, some owners have reported isolated incidents. This analysis focuses on documented, non-wear-and-tear related issues that might impact reliability. The Mazda 2's straightforward design contributes to its overall positive reputation for dependability, making it a sensible choice for budget-conscious buyers seeking a dependable daily driver.

## Electrical

### Standard Electrical System
**Power Window Motor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Power windows may become slow, inoperative, or make grinding noises.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the power window motor and regulator assembly.
- **Cost:** $250 - $500

**Faulty Keyless Entry Fob Battery**
- **Symptoms:** Remote keyless entry system may not function intermittently or at all.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the key fob battery.
- **Cost:** $5 - $15

### Summary Verdict
The 2013 Mazda 2 is a solid choice for reliability, with its powertrain and chassis demonstrating good durability. The most commonly cited non-wear-and-tear issues tend to be minor electrical components like power window motors and key fob battery performance. These are generally inexpensive and straightforward to address. Overall, the Mazda 2 offers a dependable ownership experience with minimal risk of major mechanical surprises, making it a practical and economical option in the subcompact segment.
