Here's a breakdown of common issues and complaints for the 2020 Mazda 2, formatted as requested. This information is based on available data, owner reports, and common automotive problem resources. The Mazda 2 is a subcompact hatchback praised for its sporty handling, fuel efficiency, and stylish design. While not sold in North America in 2020, it remains a popular model in other markets. Common engine options include a 1.5L inline-4 gasoline engine, producing around 75-115 horsepower depending on the specific tune and market. Transmission options typically include a 6-speed manual and a 6-speed automatic. Some owners find the rear seats cramped and the infotainment system basic compared to competitors, while others appreciate its affordability and nimble driving dynamics.

**Engine**

**1.5L Inline-4 Gasoline Engine**

*   **Engine Misfires**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rough idling, loss of power, engine light illumination. Often caused by faulty ignition coils or spark plugs.
    *   Solution: Replace faulty ignition coils and/or spark plugs. Diagnose any underlying issues causing the misfires (e.g., vacuum leaks, fuel injector problems).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $400+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Fuel Injector Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Poor fuel economy, rough running, difficulty starting, fuel smell. Can be caused by clogged or leaking fuel injectors.
    *   Solution: Clean or replace faulty fuel injectors.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $600+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**6-Speed Automatic Transmission**

*   **Harsh Shifting**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Abrupt or jerky gear changes, especially noticeable during acceleration or deceleration.
    *   Solution: Start with a transmission fluid flush and filter change. If the problem persists, further diagnosis may be needed to check valve body or solenoids.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $1,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Delayed Engagement**
    * Description/Symptoms: A noticeable delay between selecting a gear (Drive or Reverse) and the transmission engaging.
    * Solution: Check transmission fluid level and condition. If low or contaminated, a fluid flush is recommended. If issue persists, valve body or internal damage is possible.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $2,500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Worn Suspension Bushings**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Clunking or rattling noises from the suspension, particularly when driving over bumps. Reduced handling precision.
    *   Solution: Inspect and replace worn suspension bushings (e.g., control arm bushings, sway bar bushings).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $500+ per corner
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Freezing/Crashing**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The infotainment screen becomes unresponsive, freezes, or restarts intermittently.
    *   Solution: Try a system reset. Check for software updates and install if available. If the problem persists, the infotainment unit may need replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 (reset/update) - $800+ (replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Battery Drain**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The battery repeatedly dies, especially if the car is left unused for a few days.
    *   Solution: Test the battery and charging system. Check for parasitic draws (excessive current draw when the car is off) caused by faulty accessories or wiring.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Rattling Noises**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Various rattling or squeaking noises coming from the dashboard, door panels, or rear hatch area while driving.
    *   Solution: Identify the source of the noise and secure loose panels or components. Applying felt tape or foam padding can help dampen vibrations.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 - $200+ (depending on severity and if professional help is required).
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Headlight Condensation**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Moisture build-up inside the headlight housings.
    *   Solution: Remove the headlight assembly and dry it out. Check for cracks or damaged seals and repair or replace the headlight housing as needed.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 - $500+ (depending on severity and if professional help is required)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2020 Mazda 2 generally enjoys a reputation for reliability, but like any vehicle, it has potential issues. Engine and transmission issues, while not widespread, can be costly to repair. Electrical glitches with the infotainment system are also reported, while suspension noises and interior rattles are relatively common but less severe. Overall, the potential for major issues is moderate, and the cost of repairs can range from minor fixes to substantial expenses depending on the specific problem. Pre-purchase inspections are always recommended.
