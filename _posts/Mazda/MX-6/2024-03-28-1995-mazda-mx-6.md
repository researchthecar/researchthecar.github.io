**Introduction**

The 1995 Mazda MX-6 is a stylish and sporty coupe that offers a comfortable ride and responsive handling. It is available with a variety of engine and transmission options, including a 1.8-liter inline-four paired with a five-speed manual or four-speed automatic, and a 2.5-liter V6 paired with a five-speed manual or four-speed automatic. The MX-6 is known for its sleek design, good performance, and well-equipped interior. However, like all vehicles, it has some common issues that owners should be aware of.

**Engine**

**1.8-Liter Inline-Four**

* **Intake Manifold Gasket Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Leaking coolant, overheating, rough idling
    * Solution: Replace intake manifold gasket
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
    * Recalls: NHTSA Campaign ID: 02V123000

**2.5-Liter V6**

* **Valve Cover Gasket Leaks:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks, engine noise
    * Solution: Replace valve cover gaskets
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $300
* **Timing Belt Tensioner Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Squealing noise, loss of power
    * Solution: Replace timing belt tensioner
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $600
    * Recalls: NHTSA Campaign ID: 02V177000

**Transmission**

**Five-Speed Manual**

* **Clutch Slave Cylinder Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears, clutch pedal goes to the floor
    * Solution: Replace clutch slave cylinder
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400

**Four-Speed Automatic**

* **Torque Converter Shudder:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Vibration when shifting gears, loss of power
    * Solution: Replace torque converter
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,200 - $1,800
* **Transmission Control Module (TCM) Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Erratic shifting, no gears
    * Solution: Replace TCM
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800

**Suspension**

* **Front Strut Mount Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Clunking noise when turning or braking, poor ride quality
    * Solution: Replace front strut mounts
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500

**Electrical**

* **Ignition Coil Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Hard starting, misfiring, loss of power
    * Solution: Replace ignition coil
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200
* **Alternator Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Dimming lights, battery discharge
    * Solution: Replace alternator
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $700

**Exterior**

* **Plastic Trim Fading:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Discoloration, fading of exterior plastic trim
    * Solution: Color restoration, replacement
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500

**Interior**

* **Door Panel Rattle:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Loose door panels causing noise
    * Solution: Adjust or replace door panel clips
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $150

**Summary**

Overall, the 1995 Mazda MX-6 is a reliable and enjoyable car. However, it does have some common issues that owners should be aware of. The most severe issues include engine gasket failures, transmission problems, and electrical issues. The cost of repairs for these issues can range from minor to expensive, but most can be addressed with proper maintenance and care.
