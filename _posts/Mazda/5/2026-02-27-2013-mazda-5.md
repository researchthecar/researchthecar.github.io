# 2013 Mazda 5

### Overview
The 2013 Mazda 5, a compact multi-purpose vehicle (MPV), is generally considered a reliable option within its segment. It offers a practical and versatile interior, making it a good choice for families. While most owners report a positive ownership experience with minimal unexpected repairs, like any vehicle, there are a few areas that have seen occasional reports of issues. This analysis focuses on documented technical reliability concerns beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential problem areas and their typical resolutions.

---


## Engine

### 2.5L I4
**Oil Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Visible oil spots under the vehicle, low oil level warnings, burning oil smell.
- **Solution:** Replacement of seals and gaskets (e.g., valve cover gasket, oil pan gasket).
- **Cost:** $300 - $700

**Coolant Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Overheating, visible coolant puddles, sweet smell from engine bay, low coolant level.
- **Solution:** Replacement of hoses, radiator, or water pump.
- **Cost:** $400 - $900


## Transmission

### 5-Speed Automatic
**Hesitation/Jerking**
- **Symptoms:** Noticeable delay when shifting gears, rough or jerky shifts, especially at lower speeds.
- **Solution:** Transmission fluid flush and filter replacement, or potential internal transmission repair.
- **Cost:** $300 - $1500


## Suspension

### Standard Chassis
**Strut Mount Noise**
- **Symptoms:** Clunking or popping noises when turning or going over bumps.
- **Solution:** Replacement of front strut mounts.
- **Cost:** $200 - $500


## Electrical

### Standard System
**Power Window Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Windows not rolling up or down, slow operation, or stopping mid-travel.
- **Solution:** Replacement of window regulator or motor.
- **Cost:** $250 - $600

**Infotainment System Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Screen freezing, unresponsiveness, audio cutting out.
- **Solution:** Software update or replacement of the infotainment unit.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1200


## Exterior

### Body Panels & Trim
**Rust on Rear Hatch**
- **Symptoms:** Visible rust spots or bubbling paint, particularly around the license plate area and lower edges of the rear hatch.
- **Solution:** Rust repair and repainting.
- **Cost:** $300 - $800


## Interior

### Cabin Components
**Sliding Door Mechanism Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Difficulty opening or closing sliding doors, sticking, or not latching properly.
- **Solution:** Lubrication of tracks, adjustment of cables, or replacement of door components.
- **Cost:** $150 - $400


--- 
### Summary Verdict
The 2013 Mazda 5 demonstrates a commendable level of reliability for a vehicle in its class. The powertrain, particularly the 2.5L engine and 5-speed automatic transmission, is generally robust, though occasional reports of oil leaks, coolant leaks, and transmission hesitation suggest vigilance with fluid maintenance and prompt attention to any unusual shifting behavior. Suspension components like strut mounts can develop noise over time, and electrical issues such as power window malfunctions and infotainment glitches are not uncommon. Exterior rust, especially on the rear hatch, is a concern in certain climates. Interiorly, the sliding door mechanisms are the most frequently cited area for potential issues. Overall, the Mazda 5 is a practical and dependable choice, with most reported problems being manageable and repairable.
