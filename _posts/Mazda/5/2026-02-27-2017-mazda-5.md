# 2017 Mazda 5

### Overview
The 2017 Mazda 5, a compact multi-purpose vehicle (MPV), is generally recognized for its practicality and family-friendly features. From a technical reliability standpoint, it offers a solid performance, benefiting from Mazda's engineering focus. While no vehicle is entirely immune to issues, the 2017 Mazda 5 demonstrates a commendable track record, with most owners reporting satisfaction with its durability. Common areas of concern, though infrequent, tend to be related to specific powertrain components or minor electrical glitches. However, widespread systemic failures are not a significant characteristic of this model year, making it a dependable choice for many.

### Summary Verdict
The 2017 Mazda 5 presents itself as a reliable option within the compact MPV segment. Its powertrain, consisting of the 2.5L Skyactiv-G engine and a 6-speed automatic transmission, shows no significant widespread issues, indicating robust engineering. Suspension and exterior components are also generally durable. The primary areas where minor concerns have been noted are within the electrical system, specifically occasional infotainment glitches and intermittent battery drain issues, which are not uncommon in vehicles of this age and technology level. Overall, the 2017 Mazda 5 is a dependable vehicle with a good reliability record, provided potential electrical quirks are addressed.
