# 2014 Mazda 5

### Overview
The 2014 Mazda 5, a compact multi-purpose vehicle (MPV), is generally considered a reliable option within its segment. It offers a practical and versatile interior, making it a suitable choice for families. While it doesn't typically suffer from widespread, critical mechanical failures, like any vehicle, it can experience specific component issues over time. This analysis focuses on documented technical reliability concerns, excluding routine maintenance and normal wear and tear, to provide a clear picture of potential problem areas for owners and prospective buyers.

## Suspension

### Standard Independent Suspension
**Worn Control Arm Bushings**
- **Symptoms:** Clunking noises over bumps, vague steering feel, uneven tire wear.
- **Solution:** Replacement of worn control arm bushings or the entire control arm assembly.
- **Cost:** $300 - $700


## Electrical

### Body Control Module (BCM)
**Intermittent Power Failures**
- **Symptoms:** Randomly failing interior lights, power windows, or door locks; warning lights may illuminate erratically.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and potential reprogramming or replacement of the BCM.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1200


### Infotainment System
**Screen Freezing/Unresponsiveness**
- **Symptoms:** Touchscreen becomes unresponsive, system freezes, audio cuts out.
- **Solution:** Software update or replacement of the infotainment unit.
- **Cost:** $400 - $1000


## Exterior

### Body Panels
**Rusting (Rear Wheel Arches)**
- **Symptoms:** Visible rust formation and bubbling paint on the rear wheel arches, especially in areas with harsh winters and road salt.
- **Solution:** Rust treatment and repair, repainting.
- **Cost:** $200 - $600


## Interior

### Sliding Door Mechanisms
**Sticking or Difficult Sliding Doors**
- **Symptoms:** Rear sliding doors become difficult to open or close, may stick or require excessive force.
- **Solution:** Lubrication of tracks, cleaning of debris, or replacement of worn components within the mechanism.
- **Cost:** $150 - $400


--- 
### Summary Verdict
The 2014 Mazda 5 demonstrates a good overall reliability record, particularly with its powertrain. The primary areas of concern tend to be related to specific electrical components like the Body Control Module and infotainment system, which can exhibit intermittent issues. Some owners have also reported minor exterior rust formation on the rear wheel arches, a common issue for vehicles exposed to corrosive elements. Interiorly, the sliding door mechanisms can occasionally become stiff. However, these issues are generally manageable and do not indicate systemic failures. With regular maintenance and attention to these potential problem areas, the 2014 Mazda 5 should continue to provide dependable service.
