**Introduction:**

The 1991 Plymouth Acclaim was a mid-size sedan available with three engine options: a 2.5L V6 with 142 horsepower and 165 lb-ft of torque, a 3.0L V6 with 141 horsepower and 185 lb-ft of torque, and a 3.3L V6 with 150 horsepower and 185 lb-ft of torque. Transmission options included a 5-speed manual, a 3-speed automatic, and a 4-speed automatic. The Acclaim was praised for its roomy interior, smooth ride, and affordable price, but criticized for its lackluster performance and poor fuel economy.

**Engine**

- **2.5L V6**
  - **Intake Manifold Gasket Failure**
    - **Description/Symptoms:** Coolant leaks, engine overheating, rough idling
    - **Solution:** Replacement of the intake manifold gasket
    - **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $150 - $300+
  - **Valve Cover Gasket Leaks**
    - **Description/Symptoms:** Oil leaks, visible oil residue around the valve covers
    - **Solution:** Replacement of the valve cover gaskets
    - **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $100 - $200+
- **3.0L V6**
  - **Intake Manifold Gasket Failure**
    - **Description/Symptoms:** Coolant leaks, engine overheating, rough idling
    - **Solution:** Replacement of the intake manifold gasket
    - **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $200 - $400+
- **3.3L V6**
  - **Exhaust Manifold Leak**
    - **Description/Symptoms:** Ticking or rattling noises, poor engine performance
    - **Solution:** Replacement of the exhaust manifold gasket
    - **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $250 - $450+

**Transmission**

- **5-Speed Manual**
  - **Difficulty Shifting Gears**
    - **Description/Symptoms:** Hard to shift into or out of gears, grinding noises
    - **Solution:** Replacement of worn or damaged clutch components
    - **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $400 - $700+
- **3-Speed Automatic**
  - **Slipping Gears**
    - **Description/Symptoms:** Delayed or no engagement of gears, loss of power
    - **Solution:** Overhaul of the transmission
    - **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $1,000 - $2,000
- **4-Speed Automatic**
  - **Overheating**
    - **Description/Symptoms:** Transmission fluid leaks, burnt smell, difficulty shifting
    - **Solution:** Replacement of the transmission cooler and/or overhaul of the transmission
    - **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $500 - $1,500+

**Suspension**

- **Strut Mount Failure**
  - **Description/Symptoms:** Clunking or rattling noises from the front of the vehicle, excessive vibration
  - **Solution:** Replacement of the strut mounts
  - **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $200 - $400+
- **Sway Bar Link Failure**
  - **Description/Symptoms:** Rattling noises while turning or driving over bumps
  - **Solution:** Replacement of the sway bar links
  - **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $100 - $200+

**Electrical**

- **Ignition Switch Failure**
  - **Description/Symptoms:** Difficulty starting the vehicle, no power to accessories
  - **Solution:** Replacement of the ignition switch
  - **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $200 - $350+
  - **Recalls:** NHTSA Campaign ID Number: 98V013000
- **Alternator Failure**
  - **Description/Symptoms:** Dimming headlights, electrical system not functioning properly, battery not charging
  - **Solution:** Replacement of the alternator
  - **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $300 - $500+
  - **Recalls:** None Found

**Exterior**

- **Windshield Wiper Motor Failure**
  - **Description/Symptoms:** Windshield wipers not working, intermittent operation
  - **Solution:** Replacement of the windshield wiper motor
  - **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $150 - $250+
  - **Recalls:** None Found

**Interior**

- **Climate Control Issues**
  - **Description/Symptoms:** Malfunctioning AC or heater, temperature control not working properly
  - **Solution:** Repair or replacement of the climate control module
  - **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $150 - $300+
- **Instrument Cluster Failure**
  - **Description/Symptoms:** Gauges not working properly, lights not illuminating
  - **Solution:** Repair or replacement of the instrument cluster
  - **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $200 - $400+

**Summary**

Overall, the 1991 Plymouth Acclaim is a generally reliable vehicle with some minor issues that are common to cars of its age and mileage. Engine and transmission problems can be costly to repair, but the other issues are typically less expensive to fix. The severity of these issues can vary depending on the year, make, and model of the vehicle, as well as the driving habits and maintenance history of the individual owner.
