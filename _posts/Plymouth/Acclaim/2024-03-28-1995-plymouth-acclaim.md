**Introduction**

The 1995 Plymouth Acclaim was a mid-size sedan produced by Plymouth. It was powered by a choice of two engines: a 2.5L V6 (169 hp, 174 lb-ft torque) and a 3.3L V6 (162 hp, 210 lb-ft torque), paired with either a 3-speed automatic or a 5-speed manual transmission. The Acclaim was praised for its spacious interior and comfortable ride, but criticized for its lackluster performance and poor fuel economy.

**Engine**

**2.5L V6**

* **Head Gasket Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Symptoms include coolant loss, overheating, and a blown head gasket.
    * Solution: Requires the replacement of the head gasket.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $1,500
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Intake Manifold Gasket Leak:**
    * Description/Symptoms: May cause coolant or oil leaks and engine misfires.
    * Solution: Replace the intake manifold gasket.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $700
    * Recalls: None Found

**3.3L V6**

* **Timing Belt Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: A broken timing belt can cause catastrophic engine damage.
    * Solution: Replace the timing belt according to the recommended maintenance schedule.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**3-Speed Automatic**

* **Slipping Transmission:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Transmission may slip or hesitate upon shifting.
    * Solution: May require rebuilding or replacement of the transmission.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,800 - $2,500+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Transmission Fluid Leak:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Fluid may leak from the transmission pan or lines.
    * Solution: Identify and repair the source of the leak.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $400
    * Recalls: None Found

**5-Speed Manual**

* **Grinding Gears:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Difficulty or grinding noise when shifting gears.
    * Solution: May require replacement of the clutch or other transmission components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,200
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Worn Struts:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Reduced stability and handling, excessive bouncing.
    * Solution: Replace the worn struts.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Leaking Shocks:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Fluid leakage from the shock absorbers.
    * Solution: Replace the leaking shocks.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Battery Issue:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Premature battery failure or difficulty starting the vehicle.
    * Solution: Replace the battery or test charging system.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Alternator Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: May cause battery failure or lack of charging while driving.
    * Solution: Replace the alternator.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Rust:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Holes or corrosion in the body, especially in areas prone to moisture.
    * Solution: Repair or replace rusted panels.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies widely depending on severity

**Interior**

* **Dashboard Dry Rot:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Cracking or splitting dashboard material.
    * Solution: Replace or repair the dashboard.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 1995 Plymouth Acclaim has a range of potential issues that can vary in severity and cost. Some of the more common and costly issues include head gasket failure, transmission problems, and rust. While many of the issues can be addressed with proper maintenance and repairs, it's important to note that the Acclaim may require more frequent attention than some other vehicles.
