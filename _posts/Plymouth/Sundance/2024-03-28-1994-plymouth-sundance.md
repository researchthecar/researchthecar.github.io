**Introduction:**

The 1994 Plymouth Sundance is a compact hatchback powered by a 2.2L inline-four engine paired with a 5-speed manual or 3-speed automatic transmission. It offers a modest 110 horsepower and 129 lb-ft of torque. Known for its affordability and practicality, it has received mixed reviews from owners. Some appreciate its fuel efficiency and affordability, while others complain about its underwhelming performance and lack of features.

**Engine**

**2.2L Inline-Four (Manual Transmission)**

* **Excessive Oil Consumption:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The engine burns an excessive amount of oil, resulting in low oil levels and potential engine damage.
    * Solution: Replace worn piston rings or valve seals.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Valve Cover Gasket Leak:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks from the valve cover gasket, causing an oil smell and potential fire hazard.
    * Solution: Replace the valve cover gasket.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300
    * Recalls: None Found

**2.2L Inline-Four (Automatic Transmission)**

* **Transmission Fluid Leak:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The transmission fluid leaks from the transmission pan or lines, resulting in low fluid levels and potential transmission damage.
    * Solution: Replace the faulty gasket or part.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**5-Speed Manual Transmission:**

* **Difficult Shifting:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Gears are difficult to shift, especially when cold or after prolonged use.
    * Solution: Replace or adjust shift linkages or lubricate transmission components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600
    * Recalls: None Found

**3-Speed Automatic Transmission:**

* **Harsh Shifting:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The transmission shifts roughly between gears, causing a jolt or shudder.
    * Solution: Replace or adjust transmission solenoids or valves.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,000
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Strut Mount Bearing Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The strut mount bearing fails, causing excessive noise, vibration, and poor handling.
    * Solution: Replace the strut mount bearings.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The battery drains prematurely, leaving the vehicle unable to start.
    * Solution: Test and replace the faulty electrical component (e.g., alternator, starter, battery).
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Door Handle Breakage:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The door handles become brittle and break easily, making it difficult to open the doors.
    * Solution: Replace the door handles.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Dashboard Panel Cracking:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The dashboard panel cracks and fades over time, affecting the vehicle's aesthetics.
    * Solution: Replace the dashboard panel.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

The 1994 Plymouth Sundance is a budget-friendly and practical vehicle, but it is not without its fair share of issues. The most notable engine problems involve excessive oil consumption and valve cover gasket leaks, while the transmission can suffer from fluid leaks and shifting issues. Suspension components, such as strut mount bearings, may also fail prematurely. Electrical issues include battery drain, while exterior and interior issues include door handle breakage and dashboard cracking. The severity of these issues varies, and the cost to fix them ranges from minor to moderate.
