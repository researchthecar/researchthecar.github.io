## 1999 Plymouth Voyager

The 1999 Plymouth Voyager is a minivan offered with a 3.0L V6 engine and a 4-speed automatic transmission. It offers a spacious interior, comfortable seating, and a variety of features. However, owners have reported several issues with the vehicle.

**Engine**

**3.0L V6**

* **Intake manifold gasket failure:** The intake manifold gasket can fail, causing coolant and oil leaks. Symptoms include coolant and oil puddles under the vehicle, engine overheating, and white smoke from the exhaust. The solution is to replace the intake manifold gasket. Estimated cost to fix: $300 - $600+

* **Head gasket failure:** The head gasket can fail, causing coolant and oil to mix. Symptoms include white smoke from the exhaust, coolant and oil puddles under the vehicle, and engine overheating. The solution is to replace the head gasket. Estimated cost to fix: $1,000 - $2,000+

* **Timing belt failure:** The timing belt can fail, causing catastrophic engine damage. Symptoms include a rattling noise from the engine, engine misfiring, and a sudden loss of power. The solution is to replace the timing belt. Estimated cost to fix: $200 - $400+

**Transmission**

**4-speed automatic**

* **Transmission slipping:** The transmission may slip, causing a loss of power and acceleration. Symptoms include difficulty shifting gears, a delayed response when shifting, and a shuddering sensation while driving. The solution is to repair or replace the transmission. Estimated cost to fix: $1,500 - $3,000+

* **Transmission fluid leaks:** The transmission may develop fluid leaks, causing a drop in transmission fluid levels. Symptoms include slipping gears, difficulty shifting, and a burning smell. The solution is to fix the leak and replace the transmission fluid. Estimated cost to fix: $200 - $400+

**Suspension**

* **Strut failure:** The struts can fail, causing a loss of stability and handling. Symptoms include a rough ride, excessive body roll, and difficulty controlling the vehicle. The solution is to replace the struts. Estimated cost to fix: $400 - $800+

* **Sway bar link failure:** The sway bar links can fail, causing a clunking or rattling sound when driving over bumps. The solution is to replace the sway bar links. Estimated cost to fix: $100 - $200+

**Electrical**

* **Power window failure:** The power windows may fail to operate, either intermittently or completely. Symptoms include difficulty or inability to raise or lower the windows. The solution is to repair or replace the window motor, switch, or wiring. Estimated cost to fix: $100 - $300+

* **Battery drain:** The battery may drain prematurely, causing difficulty starting the vehicle. Symptoms include a dead battery, flickering lights, and a loss of power. The solution is to identify and fix the source of the drain, such as a faulty alternator, parasitic draw, or faulty wiring. Estimated cost to fix: $100 - $500+

**Exterior**

* **Rust:** The body panels may develop rust, especially in areas exposed to road salt and moisture. Symptoms include bubbling paint, rust spots, and holes in the metal. The solution is to repair or replace the affected panels. Estimated cost to fix: $500 - $2,000+


**Interior**

* **Dashboard cracks:** The dashboard may develop cracks, especially in areas exposed to sunlight. Symptoms include visible cracks and fading of the dashboard material. The solution is to replace the dashboard or cover it with a dash cover. Estimated cost to fix: $200 - $500+

* **Seat fabric tearing:** The seat fabric may tear or fray, especially in areas subject to wear and tear. Symptoms include holes or tears in the seat fabric. The solution is to repair or replace the seat covers. Estimated cost to fix: $100 - $300+

## Summary

The 1999 Plymouth Voyager has been reported to have several issues affecting the engine, transmission, suspension, electrical, exterior, and interior components. The severity and cost of these issues can vary depending on the specific problem and the extent of the damage. It is important to address these issues promptly to prevent further damage and ensure the safety and reliability of the vehicle.
