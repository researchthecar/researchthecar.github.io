The 2016 Volvo B290R is a popular choice for coach and city bus applications, known for its reliability and fuel efficiency in heavy-duty use. While specific horsepower and torque figures can vary based on configuration and market, it's generally recognized for providing ample power for its intended purpose. Common complaints revolve around the cost of replacement parts and specialized service requirements due to its commercial nature.

**Engine**

**Volvo D8K Engine**

*   **Fuel Injector Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rough idling, decreased fuel economy, black smoke from exhaust, engine misfires, and potential engine stalling.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the faulty fuel injector(s).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1500+ per injector (depending on labor and if multiple injectors are replaced)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **EGR Valve Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Reduced engine power, poor fuel economy, engine light illuminated, rough idling, and potential engine stalling. Could also cause excessive soot and carbon buildup within the intake.
    *   Solution: Cleaning or replacement of the EGR valve.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**ZF Ecolife 6AP1400B Automatic Transmission**

*   **Transmission Slipping**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Delay in gear engagement, erratic shifting, loss of power, and unusual noises during gear changes.
    *   Solution: Can range from a software update to valve body replacement to a full transmission rebuild or replacement, depending on the severity and cause of the slippage.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $8,000+ depending on the repair needed.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Torque Converter Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Shuddering at low speeds, stalling, overheating.
    * Solution: Torque Converter replacement
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $4,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Air Suspension Compressor Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The bus leans to one side, suspension doesn't raise or lower, error messages on the dashboard.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the air suspension compressor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $2,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Air Spring Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Hissing sound from the suspension, sagging of the vehicle, and a rougher ride.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the leaking air spring.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $1,200+ per air spring
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Multiplex Wiring Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Intermittent issues with lights, sensors, and other electrical components. Can be difficult to diagnose due to the complexity of the system.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis and repair of the wiring harness and replacement of faulty components.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $2,000+ (depending on the severity of the issue and the time required for diagnostics).
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **CAN Bus Communication Problems**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Various warning lights, disabled systems, and communication errors between different control units.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis of the CAN bus network and replacement of faulty modules or wiring.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $3,000+ (depending on the affected modules and the complexity of the repair).
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Door Mechanism Malfunctions**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Doors sticking, failing to open or close properly, unusual noises during operation. This can include issues with the pneumatic or electric door operating systems.
    *   Solution: Repair or replacement of the door mechanism components.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,500+ per door, depending on the issue and parts needed.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **HVAC System Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Inadequate heating or cooling, strange noises from the HVAC unit, and air flow problems.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis and repair of the HVAC system, including components like the compressor, blower motor, and control modules.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $2,000+ depending on the component that is having the issue.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2016 Volvo B290R, while a reliable workhorse, can suffer from issues common to heavy-duty vehicles. The most pressing concerns involve engine and transmission problems, which can lead to significant downtime and expense. Electrical and suspension issues are also relatively common. Due to the B290R being a commercial vehicle, the cost of repairs can be expensive. Overall, while potentially expensive, these issues are not necessarily more severe than issues that you might find in other similar commercial vehicles.
