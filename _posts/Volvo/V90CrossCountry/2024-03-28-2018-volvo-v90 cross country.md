**Introduction:**

The 2018 Volvo V90 Cross Country is a versatile station wagon that combines luxury, practicality, and ruggedness. It offers a 2.0L turbocharged four-cylinder engine with 250 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque, mated to an 8-speed automatic transmission.

## **Engine:**

### **T5 (2.0L Turbocharged Four-Cylinder)**

**Internal Oil Leak**
Description/Symptoms: Oil leaking from the engine, primarily from the valve cover gasket or oil pan gasket.
Solution: Replace the affected gasket.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
Recalls: None Found

## **Transmission:**

### **Aisin TG-81SC (8-Speed Automatic)**

**No Issues Found**

## **Suspension:**

**Rear Suspension Bushing Wear**
Description/Symptoms: Premature wear of the rear suspension bushings, leading to knocking noises and reduced ride comfort.
Solution: Replace the worn bushings.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $250 - $500+
Recalls: None Found

## **Electrical:**

**Electrical Sensor Failures**
Description/Symptoms: Malfunctioning electrical sensors, such as the crankshaft position sensor or camshaft position sensor, causing starting or performance issues.
Solution: Replace the faulty sensor.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
Recalls: None Found

## **Exterior:**

**No Issues Found**

## **Interior:**

**Infotainment System Lag**
Description/Symptoms: Infotainment system experiencing occasional lag or freezing, affecting navigation, audio, and other functions.
Solution: Software update or hardware replacement.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
Recalls: None Found

## **Summary:**

The 2018 Volvo V90 Cross Country has generally received positive reviews for its comfort, versatility, and technology. However, there are some reported issues with the engine oil leak and rear suspension bushing wear. The severity of these issues is not considered major, but the cost of repairs can vary depending on the extent of the problem. Overall, the V90 Cross Country is still a reliable and well-rounded vehicle.
