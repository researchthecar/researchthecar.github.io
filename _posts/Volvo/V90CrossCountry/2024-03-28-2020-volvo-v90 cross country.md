**Introduction:**

The 2020 Volvo V90 Cross Country is a premium midsize station wagon that combines comfort, luxury, and utility. It features a spacious interior, a versatile cargo area, and a capable all-wheel-drive system. Engine options include a 2.0-liter turbocharged inline-4 (T5) and a 2.0-liter supercharged and turbocharged inline-4 (T6), paired with an 8-speed automatic transmission. While it is generally well-regarded, the V90 Cross Country has had some reported issues.

**Engine**

**T5 2.0L Inline-4**

* **Intake Manifold Issues:** Description/Symptoms: Malfunctioning intake manifold can lead to loss of power, rough idling, and increased fuel consumption. Solution: Replacement of intake manifold. Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,200+ Recalls: NHTSA 20V-684

* **Throttle Body Problems:** Description/Symptoms: Faulty throttle body can cause hesitation during acceleration, stalling, and poor fuel economy. Solution: Replacement of throttle body. Estimated Cost to Fix: $600 - $800+ Recalls: None Found

**T6 2.0L Inline-4**

* **Supercharger Issues:** Description/Symptoms: Failed supercharger can result in reduced power, increased fuel consumption, and a whining noise from the engine. Solution: Replacement of supercharger. Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,000+ Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**8-Speed Automatic**

* **Shifting Problems:** Description/Symptoms: Transmission may hesitate or refuse to shift gears, causing jerky acceleration, slipping, or loss of power. Solution: Software update, transmission fluid change, or replacement of transmission. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $4,000+ Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Front Suspension Noise:** Description/Symptoms: Squeaking, rattling, or knocking noises coming from the front suspension. Solution: Replacement of worn or damaged suspension components, such as ball joints, bushings, or struts. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+ Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain:** Description/Symptoms: Battery discharges quickly, preventing the vehicle from starting. Solution: Diagnosis and repair of faulty electrical components, such as alternator, starter, or battery. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+ Recalls: None Found

* **Electrical System Malfunctions:** Description/Symptoms: Intermittent electrical issues, such as flickering lights, malfunctioning electronics, or power loss. Solution: Diagnosis and repair of faulty electrical components, wiring, or software. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+ Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Roof Rack Issues:** Description/Symptoms: Loose or rattling roof rack. Solution: Tightening or replacement of roof rack components. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+ Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Infotainment System Lag:** Description/Symptoms: Slow or unresponsive infotainment system. Solution: Software update or hardware replacement. Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 - $600+ Recalls: None Found

* **Climate Control Malfunctions:** Description/Symptoms: Inconsistent temperature control, failure to heat or cool, or excessive noise from ventilation system. Solution: Diagnosis and repair of faulty climate control components, such as sensors, actuators, or compressor. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+ Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

While the 2020 Volvo V90 Cross Country offers a luxurious and comfortable driving experience, it has experienced some issues. Engine problems, particularly with the T5 and T6 engines, can be costly to repair. Transmission issues can also be a concern, but electrical and exterior issues are generally less severe. Overall, the reported issues are not considered widespread, but they can befrustrating and expensive for affected owners.
