# 2018 Volvo V90 Cross Country

### Overview
The 2018 Volvo V90 Cross Country is a premium wagon known for its blend of luxury, safety, and all-weather capability. Built on Volvo's Scalable Product Architecture (SPA) platform, it shares many components with other modern Volvos. Reliability for this model year is generally strong, reflecting Volvo's commitment to quality and durability. The V90 Cross Country typically features a robust powertrain and a well-built interior, designed for long-term ownership. While no vehicle is entirely immune to issues, the V90 Cross Country tends to perform well in reliability assessments, with most reported problems being minor or related to specific component failures rather than widespread systemic flaws. Owners often praise its comfortable ride and advanced technology.

### Summary Verdict
The 2018 Volvo V90 Cross Country demonstrates a commendable level of reliability, particularly in its core mechanical components like the engine and transmission. The primary areas where minor issues have been noted are within the electrical system, specifically concerning the Sensus infotainment system's occasional software glitches and rare instances of battery drain. These are not typically indicative of major systemic failures but rather require software updates or targeted diagnostics. Overall, the V90 Cross Country is a solid choice for those seeking a dependable and luxurious all-wheel-drive wagon, with most owners reporting a positive ownership experience and few significant mechanical concerns.
