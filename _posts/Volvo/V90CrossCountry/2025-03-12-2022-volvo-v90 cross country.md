The 2022 Volvo V90 Cross Country is a luxury wagon known for its comfortable ride, spacious interior, and stylish design. Available with a B6 2.0-liter turbocharged and supercharged four-cylinder engine paired with a 48-volt mild-hybrid system, producing 295 horsepower and 310 lb-ft of torque. Common praise points include its comfortable seating, advanced safety features, and overall refined driving experience. Criticisms often focus on the infotainment system's complexity and potentially higher maintenance costs.

**Engine**

**B6 2.0-Liter Turbocharged and Supercharged Engine (Mild Hybrid)**

* **Oil Consumption**
    * Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported higher-than-expected oil consumption, requiring more frequent oil top-offs between scheduled services. This can manifest as low oil level warnings or the need to add oil every few thousand miles.
    * Solution: Monitoring oil levels regularly and addressing any leaks or underlying issues contributing to oil consumption. A possible fix is to replace the PCV valve or piston rings.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $100 for PCV valve. $3,000-$5,000+ for piston ring replacement, depending on labor.
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Infotainment System Glitches**
    * Description/Symptoms: The Sensus Connect infotainment system can experience occasional glitches, including freezing, slow response times, Bluetooth connectivity issues, and navigation malfunctions.
    * Solution: Software updates from Volvo can often resolve these issues. A system reset may also be necessary in some cases.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: Free software update (if available).
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Sensor Malfunctions (Parking Sensors, Blind Spot Monitoring)**
    * Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported issues with the functionality of parking sensors and blind spot monitoring systems, leading to false alarms or failure to detect obstacles or vehicles.
    * Solution: Diagnosis and replacement of faulty sensors. Calibration of the system after sensor replacement.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500 per sensor, plus labor for diagnosis and replacement.
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Sunroof Rattling or Squeaking**
    * Description/Symptoms: Rattling or squeaking noises coming from the sunroof area, especially when driving on uneven surfaces.
    * Solution: Tightening or lubricating the sunroof mechanism. In some cases, replacement of worn seals or components may be required.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $200 for lubrication/tightening. $300 - $1000+ for parts and labor.
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2022 Volvo V90 Cross Country appears to have relatively few widespread and severe issues. The most commonly reported problems relate to the infotainment system and minor electrical sensor glitches. Engine-related issues like high oil consumption are less frequent but can be more expensive to address. Overall, while potential buyers should be aware of these potential issues, the V90 Cross Country generally enjoys a reputation for reliability and comfort.
