The 2021 Volvo V90 Cross Country is a luxury wagon known for its Scandinavian design, comfortable ride, and standard all-wheel drive. The T6 engine option is a 2.0-liter four-cylinder that is both turbocharged and supercharged. This engine produces 316 horsepower and 295 lb-ft of torque. The engine is paired with an Aisin eight-speed automatic transmission. Owners generally appreciate the V90 Cross Country for its elegant interior, spacious cargo area, and safety features. Common complaints include the somewhat complex infotainment system and the higher price point compared to some competitors.

**Engine**

**2.0L Turbocharged and Supercharged Inline-4 (T6)**

*   **Oil Consumption**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported higher than expected oil consumption, requiring more frequent oil top-offs between scheduled services. Symptoms include low oil level warnings and the need to add oil every few thousand miles.
    *   Solution: Monitoring oil levels frequently and addressing any leaks or potential engine issues promptly. A software update may help, but in some cases, piston ring replacement might be necessary for severe cases.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: Diagnosis and software update $150-$300; Piston ring replacement $2,000 - $5,000+ depending on the shop and the extent of the work needed.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Coolant Leak**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners reported coolant leaks around the water pump or thermostat housing.
    *   Solution: Replace the water pump or thermostat housing.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**Aisin AW TG-81SC 8-Speed Automatic**

*   **Rough Shifting/Hesitation**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported experiencing rough or delayed shifting, particularly between lower gears. Hesitation when accelerating from a stop has also been noted.
    *   Solution: In some cases, a software update can improve shift quality. If the problem persists, a transmission fluid flush or valve body replacement may be required.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: Software update $150 - $300; Transmission fluid flush $200 - $400; Valve body replacement $1,500 - $3,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

No Issues Found

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Freezing/Crashing**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The Sensus infotainment system can sometimes freeze, become unresponsive, or crash, requiring a restart. This can affect navigation, audio, and climate control functions.
    *   Solution: Performing a software update to the latest version of the Sensus system. If the issue persists, a full system reset or replacement of the infotainment unit may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: Software update $150 - $300; Infotainment unit replacement $1,000 - $3,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Sensor Malfunctions (Parking Sensors, Blind Spot Monitoring)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Malfunctions with parking sensors and blind spot monitoring systems. Erroneous warnings or complete failure of the system to operate.
    *   Solution: Diagnosing and replacing faulty sensors. Checking wiring and connections for corrosion or damage.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: Sensor replacement $200 - $500 per sensor; Wiring repair $100 - $400+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Sunroof Issues (Leakage/Malfunction)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Sunroof may leak during heavy rain or malfunction when opening/closing.
    *   Solution: Clean sunroof drains, replace seals, or repair/replace the sunroof motor or mechanism.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: Drain cleaning $100 - $200; Seal replacement $200 - $500; Motor/mechanism repair $500 - $1500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Seat Heater Malfunction**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Seat heaters failing to work or overheating.
    *   Solution: Diagnose and replace faulty heating element or control module.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2021 Volvo V90 Cross Country generally presents with issues of moderate severity. Most problems are electrical, and powertrain related. The most expensive repairs generally involve the engine, transmission, or infotainment system. While the frequency of these issues does not appear to be overwhelming, prospective buyers should be aware of these potential problems.
