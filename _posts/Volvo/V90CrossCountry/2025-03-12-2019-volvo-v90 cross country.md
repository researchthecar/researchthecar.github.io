The 2019 Volvo V90 Cross Country is a luxury wagon known for its comfortable ride, spacious interior, and sophisticated Scandinavian design. Engine options for this model year included a 2.0-liter turbocharged and supercharged inline-4 (T6) producing 316 horsepower and 295 lb-ft of torque, and a 2.0-liter turbocharged inline-4 (T5) producing 250 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque. Both engines were paired with an 8-speed automatic transmission. Owners generally appreciate the V90 Cross Country's elegant styling, comfortable seats, and ample cargo space. Common complaints include the complicated infotainment system and some reports of electronic glitches.

**Engine**

**2.0L Turbocharged and Supercharged Inline-4 (T6)**

*   **Excessive Oil Consumption**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported higher-than-normal oil consumption, requiring frequent top-offs between oil changes. This can manifest as a low oil warning light illuminating on the dashboard, or visible blue smoke from the exhaust.
    *   Solution: A software update and oil consumption test performed by the dealer can help determine if the engine is consuming oil at an excessive rate. If the oil consumption is found to be too high, piston ring replacement is the common fix.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $3,000 - $5,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4 (T5)**

No Issues Found

**Transmission**

**Aisin Warner 8-Speed Automatic (All Engines)**

*   **Rough or Delayed Shifting**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported experiencing rough or delayed shifting, particularly when shifting between lower gears, or when accelerating from a stop.
    *   Solution: Performing a software update on the transmission control module. In some cases, the transmission needs to be rebuilt or replaced.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+ for software, $3,000 - $6,000+ for replacement.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Rear Air Suspension Leaks (if equipped)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Vehicles equipped with the optional rear air suspension may experience leaks in the air springs. This can lead to the vehicle sagging in the rear, a noisy air compressor, and a warning light on the dashboard.
    *   Solution: Replacing the leaking air spring(s).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1500+ per side.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Freezing/Crashing**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The Sensus infotainment system can be prone to freezing, crashing, or becoming unresponsive. This can affect navigation, audio, climate control, and other vehicle functions.
    *   Solution: Performing a software update or resetting the system. In some cases, the infotainment unit may need to be replaced.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+ for software, $1,000 - $2,000+ for replacement.
    *   Recalls: None Found
*   **Battery Drain**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have experienced issues with the car's battery draining quickly, especially when the vehicle is not driven for extended periods.
    *   Solution: Testing the battery and charging system, and replacing the battery if necessary. It is also important to identify and address any parasitic drains that may be present.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+ for battery replacement, varies for parasitic drain diagnosis and repair.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Sunroof Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Problems with the sunroof are reported in some vehicles, including the sunroof failing to close correctly or unusual noises during operation.
    *   Solution: Inspecting and lubricating the sunroof tracks. Repairing or replacing the sunroof motor or other damaged components may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+ for minor repairs, $500 - $1,500+ for motor replacement.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **No Issues Found**

**Summary**

The 2019 Volvo V90 Cross Country generally offers a reliable and comfortable driving experience. However, potential buyers should be aware of issues reported with the infotainment system, and excessive oil consumption on the T6 engine. Suspension problems and sunroof problems may also arise. While some issues, like software glitches, can be addressed relatively inexpensively, others such as engine repairs or air suspension issues can be costly. Pre-purchase inspection by a qualified mechanic is recommended.
