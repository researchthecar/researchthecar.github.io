# 2021 Volvo V90 Cross Country

### Overview
The 2021 Volvo V90 Cross Country is a premium wagon that blends Scandinavian design with robust capability. Known for its safety features and comfortable ride, it generally offers a reliable ownership experience. This model year benefits from Volvo's commitment to quality and advanced engineering. While most owners report satisfaction, like any vehicle, there are specific areas that have seen occasional reports of issues. This analysis focuses on documented technical reliability concerns beyond standard maintenance, providing insights into potential problem areas and their typical resolutions for the available powertrain configurations.

---


## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged & Supercharged (T6)
**Coolant Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Visible coolant puddles under the vehicle, low coolant level warning light, engine overheating.
- **Solution:** Inspection and replacement of leaking hoses, seals, or water pump.
- **Cost:** $300 - $700

**Turbocharger Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of power, whistling noises from the engine bay, increased exhaust smoke.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and potential replacement or repair of the turbocharger unit and associated plumbing.
- **Cost:** $1200 - $2500


### 2.0L Turbocharged (B5 AWD)
**Minor Oil Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Slight oil residue around the engine block, occasional oil smell.
- **Solution:** Tightening of seals or replacement of gaskets as needed.
- **Cost:** $200 - $500


## Transmission

### 8-Speed Geartronic Automatic
**Hesitation or Jerking**
- **Symptoms:** Slight delay or rough shifting between gears, particularly at lower speeds.
- **Solution:** Software updates for the transmission control module or fluid flush and replacement.
- **Cost:** $250 - $600

## Electrical

### Infotainment System
**Screen Freezing or Lag**
- **Symptoms:** Infotainment screen becomes unresponsive, slow to react to touch inputs, or temporarily freezes.
- **Solution:** Software updates or a system reset. In rare cases, replacement of the unit.
- **Cost:** $100 - $1500


### Battery Drain
**Intermittent Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Battery dies unexpectedly, requiring jump starts or replacement.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis to identify parasitic draw from modules or accessories, followed by repair or replacement of faulty components.
- **Cost:** $300 - $800

### Summary Verdict
The 2021 Volvo V90 Cross Country demonstrates strong overall reliability, consistent with Volvo's reputation. The primary areas that have seen occasional technical concerns relate to the engine's turbocharger system and potential coolant leaks, particularly with the more powerful T6 engine. Electrical issues, such as infotainment system glitches and intermittent battery drain, are also noted but often resolvable with software updates or targeted diagnostics. The transmission and exterior/interior components generally perform without widespread issues. Owners should be aware of these potential, though not universal, problems and ensure regular maintenance to mitigate risks. Overall, it remains a dependable choice for those seeking a luxurious and capable wagon.
