## Suspension

### Air Suspension System
**Air Spring Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle sagging on one side, uneven ride height, audible hissing from suspension components.
- **Solution:** Replacement of faulty air springs or associated air lines.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1500

**ECAS Control Module Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Inconsistent ride height adjustment, suspension warning lights, loss of automatic leveling.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and potential replacement of the Electronic Control for Air Suspension (ECAS) module.
- **Cost:** $800 - $2000

### Summary Verdict
The 2020 Volvo 9700 demonstrates a high level of reliability, consistent with Volvo's reputation in the commercial vehicle sector. The powertrain options, including the D11K and D13K engines paired with the I-Shift transmission, are robust and generally free from widespread issues. Areas that may require attention over time include the air suspension system, particularly air spring leaks and ECAS module functionality, and the infotainment system, which can experience software-related glitches. Exterior door seals and interior HVAC blower motors are also potential points of failure, though typically manageable. Overall, the 9700 is a dependable coach with a solid track record, provided routine maintenance is performed and potential issues are addressed promptly.
