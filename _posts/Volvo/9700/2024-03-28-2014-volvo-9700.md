**Introduction:**

The 2014 Volvo 9700 is a coach bus manufactured by Volvo Buses. It is available with two engine options: the D11C and the D13C, and two transmission options: the I-Shift 12-speed automated manual transmission and the Voith DIWA 4-speed automatic transmission. Other features include a comfortable passenger cabin, a spacious cargo area, and advanced safety features.

**Electrical:**

* **Battery Drain**
    * Description/Symptoms: The battery drains quickly, even when the vehicle is turned off.
    * Solution: Replace the battery or alternator.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

    * Description/Symptoms: Electrical components, such as lights, gauges, or power windows, may fail intermittently.

    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior:**

* **Rear Bumper Damage**
    * Description/Symptoms: The rear bumper may be damaged or broken when backing up or parking.
    * Solution: Replace or repair the bumper.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior:**

* **Dashboard Panel Fitment**
    * Description/Symptoms: The dashboard panels may not fit properly, causing rattles or gaps.
    * Solution: Reinstall or replace the dashboard panels.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Air Conditioning Fan Noise**
    * Description/Symptoms: The air conditioning fan may make a loud noise when turned on.
    * Solution: Replace the fan motor or blower assembly.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

Overall, the 2014 Volvo 9700 seems to be a reliable and well-built bus with minimal reported issues. The electrical and interior issues can be inconvenient but are typically inexpensive to fix. The exterior issue of rear bumper damage can be avoided with careful driving and parking maneuvers.
