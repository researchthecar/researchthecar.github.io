The 2013 Volvo 9700 is a popular coach bus known for its comfort, reliability, and safety features. While specific owner complaints are limited due to the nature of commercial vehicle maintenance often being handled by fleet managers, some common issues can be identified. The 2013 Volvo 9700 was typically offered with Volvo's D13 engine, a 12.8-liter inline-six diesel engine. Horsepower ranges varied, generally between 425 and 500 hp, with torque figures between 1650 and 1850 lb-ft. Transmission options usually include Volvo's I-Shift automated manual transmission. Owners generally appreciate the 9700's comfortable ride, spacious interior, and fuel efficiency. Some common dislikes include the higher initial cost compared to other bus models, and the complexity of some electronic systems.

**Engine**

**Volvo D13 (12.8L Inline-Six Diesel)**

*   **Diesel Particulate Filter (DPF) Clogging**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Reduced engine power, increased fuel consumption, frequent regeneration cycles, and the DPF warning light illuminating on the dashboard.
    *   Solution: Forced regeneration of the DPF, cleaning the DPF, or replacing the DPF if it is severely damaged or clogged beyond repair.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $3,000+ depending on the severity and whether cleaning or replacement is required.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Exhaust Gas Recirculation (EGR) Valve Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rough idling, decreased engine performance, black smoke from the exhaust, and the check engine light activating.
    *   Solution: Replacing the faulty EGR valve.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Fuel Injector Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Poor fuel economy, rough running engine, misfires, and difficulty starting.
    *   Solution: Replacing the faulty fuel injector(s).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+ per injector, plus labor.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**Volvo I-Shift Automated Manual Transmission**

*   **Shifting Problems (Hard Shifts, Slipping)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears, harsh or jerky shifts, slipping during acceleration, and the transmission warning light illuminating.
    *   Solution: Could be caused by a variety of issues including low transmission fluid, worn clutch, faulty solenoids, or issues with the transmission control module (TCM). Requires diagnosis and repair or replacement of the faulty component.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $6,000+ depending on the severity and the parts that need to be replaced.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Transmission Control Module (TCM) Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Erratic shifting, failure to shift, or complete loss of transmission function.
    *   Solution: Replacing the faulty TCM.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $3,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Air Suspension System Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Bus leaning to one side, uneven ride height, hissing sound from air leaks, and ride quality issues.
    *   Solution: Identifying and repairing air leaks in the system, replacing faulty air bags, or repairing/replacing the air compressor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $2,000+ depending on the source and severity of the leak.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Shock Absorber Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Bouncy or unstable ride, excessive body roll when cornering, and longer stopping distances.
    *   Solution: Replacing the worn or damaged shock absorbers.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,200+ per axle, depending on the type and quality of the shocks.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Multiplexing System Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Malfunctions in various electrical systems, such as lighting, HVAC, or door controls.
    *   Solution: Requires diagnostics to identify the source of the problem, which could involve faulty modules, wiring issues, or software glitches. Repair may involve replacing modules, repairing wiring, or reflashing the software.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: Highly variable, ranging from $100 to several thousand dollars depending on the complexity of the issue.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Battery Problems**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Difficulty starting the engine, dead battery, and electrical system malfunctions.
    *   Solution: Testing and replacing the battery if necessary. Also, checking the charging system for proper operation.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+ for battery replacement, plus additional costs for diagnosing and repairing charging system issues.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Lighting Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Headlights, taillights, or marker lights failing.
    *   Solution: Replacing bulbs, checking wiring connections, or replacing faulty lighting fixtures.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $500+ depending on the extent of the repairs.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **HVAC System Malfunctions**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Inadequate heating or cooling, blower motor failure, or strange noises from the HVAC system.
    *   Solution: Diagnosing the cause of the malfunction, which could involve a faulty compressor, blower motor, refrigerant leak, or control module issue. Repair may involve replacing components, repairing leaks, or recharging the system.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $2,000+ depending on the severity of the problem.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2013 Volvo 9700, while generally reliable, can experience some common issues, particularly relating to its emissions systems (DPF, EGR), transmission, air suspension, and electrical systems. The severity and cost of these issues can vary widely. DPF and EGR problems can lead to decreased engine performance and higher fuel consumption and the repairs can be fairly expensive. Transmission issues can range from minor shifting problems to major failures, with repair costs varying accordingly. Proper maintenance and timely repairs are crucial to keeping the bus in good working condition and minimizing downtime.
