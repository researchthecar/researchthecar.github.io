The 2020 Volvo 9700 is a premium coach bus, primarily used for long-distance passenger transport. While specific engine options can vary based on market and configuration, a common engine choice is the Volvo D13 engine, a 12.8-liter inline-six diesel. This engine typically produces around 425-500 horsepower and 1650-1850 lb-ft of torque. The Volvo 9700 is known for its comfortable ride, advanced safety features, and fuel efficiency. Some criticisms include the high initial cost and the complexity of the vehicle's systems, which can make maintenance more challenging.

**Engine**

**Volvo D13**

*   **EGR Cooler Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The EGR cooler can crack, leading to coolant leaks and potential overheating. White smoke may be seen from the exhaust.
    *   Solution: Replace the EGR cooler.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Fuel Injector Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rough idling, misfires, decreased fuel economy, and black smoke from the exhaust can all be symptoms.
    *   Solution: Replacing the fuel injectors. Requires recalibration.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $2,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**Volvo I-Shift Automated Manual Transmission**

*   **Shift Hesitation/Rough Shifting**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The transmission may hesitate when shifting between gears, or shifts can be abrupt and jerky. This can be more pronounced in certain gears or under heavy loads.
    *   Solution: Software updates or a replacement of the valve body.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $3,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Clutch Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Clutch slippage, difficulty engaging gears, and unusual noises during shifting.
    *   Solution: Clutch replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $5,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Air Suspension Component Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Leaks in the air lines, malfunctioning air bags, or a faulty air compressor can cause the vehicle to sag, ride unevenly, or have difficulty maintaining the correct ride height.
    *   Solution: Replacing the affected components.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+ (per component)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Multiplexing System Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The multiplexing system controls various electrical functions, and problems can manifest as intermittent failures of lights, HVAC, or other accessories.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis and repair or replacement of the affected modules or wiring.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $3,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Sensor Failures**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Engine sensors (e.g., NOx, O2, temperature) can fail, triggering warning lights and affecting engine performance.
    *   Solution: Replacing the faulty sensor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+ (per sensor)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Lighting System Malfunctions**
    *   Description/Symptoms: LED lights may fail, flicker, or have inconsistent brightness.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the affected light assembly.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,500+ (per light)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **HVAC System Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Problems with the climate control system, such as inconsistent heating or cooling, or a malfunctioning compressor.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis and repair or replacement of the affected components.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $2,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

The 2020 Volvo 9700, while a premium vehicle, experiences several common issues. Engine problems like EGR cooler and fuel injector failures can lead to significant repair costs. The I-Shift transmission may suffer from shifting problems and clutch failures, also resulting in high expenses. Suspension and electrical issues contribute further to potential maintenance costs. While the issues are not necessarily widespread, they can be severe and costly to fix. Potential buyers should be aware of these potential problems and ensure a thorough inspection before purchasing.
