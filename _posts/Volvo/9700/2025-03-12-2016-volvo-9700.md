The 2016 Volvo 9700 is a coach bus known for its comfortable ride, fuel efficiency, and safety features. While specific horsepower and torque figures may vary slightly depending on market specifications and configurations, typical engine options include Volvo's D11 and D13 series engines, offering a range of power outputs, often around 450-500 horsepower. These engines are typically paired with Volvo's I-Shift automated manual transmission. Owners often appreciate the bus's spacious interior, smooth handling, and advanced safety systems. Common dislikes can include the higher cost of maintenance and specific parts availability in certain regions, as well as the complexity of the electronics systems.

**Engine**

**Volvo D13 Engine**
* **Turbocharger Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, excessive black smoke, unusual turbocharger noise. The turbocharger can fail due to oil contamination or wear.
    * Solution: Replace the turbocharger. Ensure the oil supply lines are clean and clear.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $3,000 - $5,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **EGR Valve Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: Rough idling, reduced fuel economy, check engine light. The EGR valve can become clogged with carbon deposits.
    * Solution: Clean or replace the EGR valve.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
    * Recalls: None Found

No Issues Found

**Transmission**

**Volvo I-Shift Automated Manual Transmission**

* **Shift Hesitation/Rough Shifting**
    * Description/Symptoms: Delay in shifting, jerky gear changes, difficulty engaging gears. This can be caused by worn clutch components, solenoid issues, or software problems.
    * Solution: Perform a transmission adaptation reset. Replace worn clutch components, diagnose and replace faulty solenoids, or update transmission software.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $5,000+ depending on the severity and parts needing replacement.
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Transmission Slipping**
    * Description/Symptoms: Engine revving without corresponding acceleration, loss of power. Slipping can indicate internal wear or damage to the clutch packs.
    * Solution: Inspect and potentially rebuild the transmission, replacing worn clutch packs and other damaged components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $4,000 - $8,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Air Suspension Leaks**
    * Description/Symptoms: Bus leaning to one side, ride height issues, hissing sound from air leaks. Air springs, leveling valves, or air lines can develop leaks over time.
    * Solution: Locate and repair the air leak. Replace faulty air springs, leveling valves, or air lines.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $2,000+ depending on the component and severity.
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Multiplexing System Problems**
    * Description/Symptoms: Intermittent issues with various electrical components (lights, HVAC, etc.), error messages on the dashboard. The multiplexing system can be sensitive to voltage fluctuations and wiring issues.
    * Solution: Diagnose and repair wiring issues. Replace faulty control modules or sensors.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $3,000+ depending on the complexity of the issue.
    * Recalls: None Found

* **HVAC System Malfunctions**
    * Description/Symptoms: Inconsistent cooling or heating, blower motor issues, error codes related to the HVAC system.
    * Solution: Diagnose and repair or replace faulty HVAC components such as the compressor, blower motor, or control module.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $2,500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Door Mechanism Problems**
    * Description/Symptoms: Doors failing to open or close properly, sticking, or making unusual noises.
    * Solution: Lubricate and adjust the door mechanism. Replace worn or damaged parts such as actuators, hinges, or seals.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+ depending on the complexity of the issue.
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Seat Actuator Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: Seats failing to adjust properly, getting stuck in one position, or making grinding noises.
    * Solution: Replace the seat actuator motor or gears.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+ per seat.
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2016 Volvo 9700 experiences issues ranging from moderate to severe. Engine issues such as turbocharger and EGR valve problems can lead to significant repair costs. Transmission problems, particularly with the I-Shift, can also be expensive to address. Suspension and electrical issues, while potentially less frequent, can still result in considerable expenses. Interior and exterior issues are generally less critical but should be addressed for passenger comfort and safety. Overall, potential buyers should be aware of the higher maintenance costs associated with this vehicle, especially regarding the engine, transmission, and complex electrical systems.
