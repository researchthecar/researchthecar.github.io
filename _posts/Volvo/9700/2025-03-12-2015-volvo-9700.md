The 2015 Volvo 9700 is a premium coach bus known for its comfort, safety, and reliability. Engine options included the Volvo D13 engine. The D13 offers around 425-500 horsepower and around 1650-1770 lb-ft of torque. Owners generally appreciate the smooth ride, comfortable seating, and robust build quality of the 9700. Common complaints often revolve around the cost of maintenance and parts, as well as the complexity of some of the electronic systems.

**Engine**

**Volvo D13**

*   **EGR Valve Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Reduced engine power, increased emissions, check engine light illuminated. Rough idling and stalling are also common symptoms.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the EGR valve. Thorough cleaning of the EGR cooler may also be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Diesel Particulate Filter (DPF) Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Frequent regeneration cycles, reduced fuel economy, black smoke from the exhaust, and eventually engine derating or shutdown. Clogged DPF can also trigger a check engine light.
    *   Solution: Forced regeneration, cleaning the DPF, or replacement of the DPF if cleaning is not effective.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 (cleaning) - $3000+ (replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Fuel Injector Problems**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Misfires, rough idling, poor fuel economy, and reduced power. Can lead to damage of the engine if left unresolved.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis to determine the faulty injector(s) and replacement with new injectors.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1200+ per injector (including labor)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**Volvo I-Shift (Automated Manual Transmission)**

*   **Shifting Problems**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Harsh or delayed shifting, inability to shift into certain gears, error codes related to the transmission control module (TCM).
    *   Solution: Recalibration of the transmission, replacement of solenoids within the transmission, or replacement of the TCM if faulty.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 (recalibration) - $4000+ (TCM replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Clutch Wear and Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Slipping clutch, difficulty engaging gears, unusual noises when shifting. Clutch wear is dependent on operating conditions.
    *   Solution: Clutch replacement. Includes inspection of the flywheel and possible resurfacing or replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $2000 - $4000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Air Suspension Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Bus leaning to one side, uneven ride height, hissing sound from air leaks, failure to maintain proper ride height.
    *   Solution: Identify and repair air leaks (usually at fittings, air bags, or air lines). Replace worn or damaged air bags.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1000+ per air bag
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Shock Absorber Wear**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Bouncy or unstable ride, excessive body roll, reduced handling performance.
    *   Solution: Replacement of worn shock absorbers.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $1200+ per axle (including labor)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Multiplexing System Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Intermittent failures of various electrical components (lights, wipers, HVAC), error messages on the dashboard display, communication problems between different electronic modules.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis of the multiplexing system to identify faulty modules or wiring. Repair or replacement of affected components. This often requires specialized diagnostic equipment.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $2000+ depending on the complexity of the issue
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Wiring Harness Problems**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Corrosion or damage to wiring harnesses can cause a variety of electrical problems, including intermittent failures, short circuits, and open circuits.
    *   Solution: Repair or replacement of damaged wiring harnesses. Proper routing and securing of wiring to prevent future damage.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1500+ depending on the extent of the damage
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Door Mechanism Failures**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Difficulty opening or closing doors, doors not sealing properly, unusual noises from the door mechanism.
    *   Solution: Lubrication of the door mechanism, adjustment of door alignment, or replacement of worn components (actuators, hinges, etc.).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+ per door
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **HVAC System Problems**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Inadequate heating or cooling, inconsistent temperature control, unusual noises from the HVAC unit, refrigerant leaks.
    *   Solution: Recharge the refrigerant, repair leaks, replace faulty components (compressor, condenser, evaporator, blower motor).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

The 2015 Volvo 9700, while generally a reliable coach bus, can experience issues typical of commercial vehicles. The most common issues relate to the engine, transmission, suspension, and electrical systems, which can be costly to repair due to the specialized nature of the components. The severity of these issues ranges from minor inconveniences to major mechanical failures that can affect the vehicle's performance and safety. Regular maintenance and prompt attention to warning signs can help mitigate these problems and extend the lifespan of the vehicle.
