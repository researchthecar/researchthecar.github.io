The 2018 Volvo VNR 640 is a popular regional haul tractor known for its driver comfort and fuel efficiency. Engine options typically include the Volvo D11, D13, and Cummins X15. The Volvo D13 engine, a common choice, produces around 405-500 horsepower and 1450-1850 lb-ft of torque. The Volvo I-Shift automated manual transmission is the most common transmission. Drivers appreciate the VNR's maneuverability and visibility, while some common complaints include the complexity of the electrical system and the cost of replacement parts.

**Engine**

**Volvo D13**

*   **EGR Valve Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Reduced engine power, increased fuel consumption, fault codes related to the EGR system, black smoke from exhaust.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the EGR valve.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Diesel Particulate Filter (DPF) Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Frequent DPF regenerations, reduced engine power, fault codes related to DPF pressure or efficiency.
    *   Solution: DPF cleaning or replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $4000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Cummins X15**

*   **Fuel Injector Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rough running engine, misfires, reduced power, fuel knock, fault codes related to individual injectors.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the faulty fuel injector(s).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1000+ per injector
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Turbocharger Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, excessive black smoke, whistling noise from the turbo, fault codes related to turbocharger boost pressure.
    *   Solution: Turbocharger replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $2000 - $5000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**Volvo I-Shift (Automated Manual Transmission)**

*   **Shift Actuator Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears, erratic shifting, transmission getting stuck in gear, fault codes related to the shift actuators.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the faulty shift actuator.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1500 - $4000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Clutch Problems**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Slipping clutch, difficulty engaging gears, clutch chatter, unusual noises when using the clutch.
    *   Solution: Clutch adjustment or replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1500 - $3500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Air Suspension Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Uneven ride height, air leaks, sagging suspension, failure of air bags, compressor failure.
    *   Solution: Air bag replacement, compressor replacement, or repair of air lines.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1500+ per air bag, $500 - $2000+ for compressor.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Sensor Failures (various sensors throughout the truck)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Intermittent warning lights, inaccurate readings, engine performance issues (if engine sensor), transmission issues (if transmission sensor), ABS issues (if ABS sensor).
    *   Solution: Replacement of the faulty sensor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+ per sensor
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Wiring Harness Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Intermittent electrical problems, short circuits, corroded connectors, difficulty diagnosing electrical issues.
    *   Solution: Repair or replacement of damaged wiring harness sections.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $2000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Headlight and Taillight Condensation**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Moisture buildup inside the headlight or taillight housings, reducing visibility.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the headlight or taillight assembly.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+ per light
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **HVAC System Problems**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Inconsistent heating or cooling, blower motor failure, failure of blend door actuators, refrigerant leaks.
    *   Solution: Repair or replacement of the faulty HVAC component.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Instrument Cluster Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Intermittent display problems, inaccurate gauge readings, complete failure of the instrument cluster.
    *   Solution: Instrument cluster repair or replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2018 Volvo VNR 640 can have a range of issues, from relatively minor sensor failures to more significant engine or transmission problems. The severity of these issues varies, and the cost to repair them can range from a few hundred to several thousand dollars. Electrical issues are a common complaint, and can be difficult to diagnose. Be sure to inspect the vehicle thoroughly, especially the electrical system, before buying.
