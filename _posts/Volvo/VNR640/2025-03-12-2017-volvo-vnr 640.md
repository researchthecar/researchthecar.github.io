The 2017 Volvo VNR 640 is a popular semi-truck known for its fuel efficiency, comfortable cab, and overall reliability. Engine options for this model year typically included the Volvo D11, D13, and Cummins X15. The D13 engine, a common choice, produces around 405-500 horsepower and 1450-1850 lb-ft of torque. Transmission options are the Volvo I-Shift automated manual transmission. Drivers often praise the VNR 640's smooth ride and ergonomic design, while some common complaints revolve around the complexity of the electrical systems and the cost of replacement parts.

**Engine**

**Volvo D13**

*   **EGR Cooler Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaks, white smoke from the exhaust, engine overheating, and a noticeable loss of power.
    *   Solution: Replace the EGR cooler.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Diesel Particulate Filter (DPF) Clogging**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Reduced engine power, frequent regeneration cycles, DPF light illumination on the dashboard.
    *   Solution: Force regeneration, cleaning the DPF, or replacing the DPF if cleaning is ineffective.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $4,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Cummins X15**
*   **Fuel Injector Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rough idling, misfires, reduced power, and black smoke from the exhaust.
    *   Solution: Replace faulty fuel injector(s).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+ per injector
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**Volvo I-Shift**

*   **Gear Slippage/Harsh Shifting**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Transmission slipping between gears, especially during upshifts or downshifts, accompanied by harsh or jerky shifting.
    *   Solution: Recalibration of the transmission, valve body replacement, or complete transmission replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $8,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Clutch Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Difficulty engaging gears, clutch slippage, unusual noises coming from the transmission, and eventual loss of power.
    *   Solution: Replacing the clutch assembly.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $5,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Air Suspension Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Vehicle leaning to one side, compressor running continuously, and a noticeable hissing sound indicating an air leak.
    *   Solution: Locate and repair the air leak in the air bags, air lines, or leveling valves. Replacement of worn components may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Sensor Malfunctions**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Various warning lights on the dashboard, including ABS, engine, and transmission lights, leading to inaccurate readings and potential system failures.
    *   Solution: Diagnose the specific sensor that is malfunctioning and replace it.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+ per sensor
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Wiring Harness Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Intermittent electrical problems, such as lights flickering, gauges not working correctly, and communication errors between different modules.
    *   Solution: Inspect the wiring harness for damage or corrosion and repair or replace as necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $2,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Headlight Condensation**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Moisture buildup inside the headlight housing, reducing visibility and potentially damaging the bulb and electrical components.
    *   Solution: Seal the headlight housing or replace the headlight assembly.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+ per headlight
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **HVAC System Malfunctions**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Inconsistent heating or cooling, blower motor failure, and strange noises coming from the HVAC unit.
    *   Solution: Diagnose and repair the HVAC system, including replacing faulty components such as the blower motor, compressor, or control module.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Dashboard Display Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Dim or flickering display, pixelation, or complete failure of the dashboard display.
    *   Solution: Replace the dashboard display unit.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2017 Volvo VNR 640 has some common issues, most notably with the engine, transmission, and electrical systems. EGR cooler failure, DPF clogging, and fuel injector issues are notable engine problems, while the I-Shift transmission can experience gear slippage, harsh shifting, and clutch failure. Electrical issues such as sensor malfunctions and wiring harness problems can also occur. The severity and cost of these issues can vary widely, with repairs ranging from a few hundred dollars to several thousand dollars depending on the specific problem and required parts and labor. While the VNR 640 is known for its reliability, potential buyers should be aware of these common problems and thoroughly inspect any used vehicle before purchase.
