The 2019 Volvo VNR 640 is a popular Class 8 semi-truck often used for regional and vocational hauling. It was offered with a Volvo D11, D13, or a Cummins X15 engine, paired with either a Volvo I-Shift automated manual transmission or an Eaton Fuller manual transmission. The Volvo D13 engine, a common choice, produces between 405-500 horsepower and 1450-1850 lb-ft of torque depending on the specific tuning. Owners often praise the VNR for its comfortable cab, fuel efficiency, and smooth ride. Common complaints include the cost of parts and repairs, and some electronic system glitches.

**Engine**

**Volvo D13**
*   **EGR Cooler Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaks, white smoke from the exhaust, engine overheating, and reduced engine performance.
    *   Solution: Replace the EGR cooler.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+

*   **Fuel Injector Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rough idling, misfires, reduced fuel economy, and black smoke from the exhaust.
    *   Solution: Replace faulty fuel injectors.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+ per injector

**Cummins X15**
*   **DEF System Problems**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Check engine light illuminated, reduced engine power, frequent DEF refills, and potential issues with the DEF injector or pump.
    *   Solution: Diagnose and repair the DEF system, which may involve replacing the DEF injector, pump, or sensors.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+

**Transmission**

**Volvo I-Shift**
*   **Shift Hesitation/Erratic Shifting**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Delays during gear changes, jerky shifts, and occasional failure to shift.
    *   Solution: Software update, clutch adjustment, or replacement of the transmission control module (TCM).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $2,500+

*   **Clutch Actuator Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting, grinding gears, and eventually complete failure to shift.
    *   Solution: Replace the clutch actuator.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+

**Eaton Fuller Manual Transmission**
* **Shift Linkage Problems**
    * Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting, loose shifter, inability to engage certain gears.
    * Solution: Adjust or replace shift linkage components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+

**Suspension**

*   **Air Suspension Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Leaking air bags, uneven ride height, and compressor failures.
    *   Solution: Replace leaking air bags, repair or replace the air compressor, and inspect air lines for leaks.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,500+ per air bag.

**Electrical**

*   **Sensor Failures**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Various warning lights on the dashboard, inaccurate readings from sensors (e.g., temperature, pressure), and potential engine performance issues.
    *   Solution: Diagnose and replace faulty sensors.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+ per sensor

*   **Wiring Harness Problems**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Intermittent electrical issues, short circuits, and potential engine performance problems.
    *   Solution: Repair or replace damaged wiring harnesses.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+ depending on the extent of the damage.

*   **Lighting Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: Headlights, tail lights, or marker lights not working; flickering lights; blown fuses related to lighting circuits.
    * Solution: Replace bulbs, repair wiring connections, replace faulty light fixtures, or address any short circuits.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $500+ depending on the complexity of the issue.

**Exterior**

* **Headlight Condensation**
    * Description/Symptoms: Moisture buildup inside headlight lenses, reducing visibility.
    * Solution: Replace headlight assembly or attempt to reseal the lens.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+

**Interior**

*   **HVAC System Malfunctions**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Inconsistent heating or cooling, blower motor failure, and issues with the temperature controls.
    *   Solution: Diagnose and repair the HVAC system, which may involve replacing the blower motor, control module, or other components.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+

**Recalls:**

*   **Recall Number:** 20V744000
        *   Summary: Volvo Trucks North America (Volvo Trucks) is recalling certain 2019-2021 VAH, VHD, VNL, and VNR vehicles. The steering shaft universal joint may not have been properly tightened.
        *   Remedy: Volvo Trucks will inspect and tighten the universal joint fasteners, as necessary, free of charge. Owners are expected to be notified December 1, 2020. Owners may contact Volvo Trucks customer service at 1-800-528-6586. Volvo Trucks' number for this recall is RVXX2004.
*   **Recall Number:** 20V573000
        *   Summary: Volvo Trucks North America (Volvo Trucks) is recalling certain 2019-2021 VAH, VHD, VNL, and VNR vehicles equipped with Hendrickson Primaxx air suspension. The clamp that attaches the suspension torque rod to the axle may be improperly torqued, possibly resulting in the clamp loosening and the torque rod detaching.
        *   Remedy: Volvo Trucks will inspect and tighten the torque rod clamp fasteners, as necessary, free of charge. Owners are expected to be notified October 26, 2020. Owners may contact Volvo Trucks customer service at 1-800-528-6586. Volvo Trucks' number for this recall is RVXX2003.

**Summary:** The 2019 Volvo VNR 640, while generally reliable, can experience issues across various systems. The most common problems involve the engine (particularly related to emissions systems), transmission (especially I-Shift models), and electrical components. Repairs can range from a few hundred dollars for sensor replacements to several thousand for major engine or transmission work. Potential buyers should carefully inspect these areas and review the vehicle's maintenance history. Addressing potential problems early can help mitigate costly repairs down the road. The recalls related to the steering shaft and suspension should also be checked to ensure they have been addressed.
