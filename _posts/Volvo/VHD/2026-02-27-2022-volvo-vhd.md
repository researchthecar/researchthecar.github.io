# 2022 Volvo VHD Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2022 Volvo VHD is a heavy-duty vocational truck engineered for demanding applications like construction, dump operations, and heavy hauling. Because this model is relatively new to the secondary market and primarily serves commercial fleets, individual owner complaint volume in public databases is extremely sparse. Prospective buyers evaluating a used 2022 model should focus heavily on standard commercial pre-purchase inspections, maintenance history, and verifying that all heavy-duty powertrain components align with their specific operational needs.

---

## Engine

### Volvo D11 11-liter Turbo Diesel

The Volvo D11 is an 11-liter inline-six diesel engine typically producing between 325 and 425 horsepower, designed for vocational applications requiring optimized weight and fuel efficiency. It generally offers robust performance for regional dump and mixer configurations, though commercial buyers should closely monitor emissions equipment and fluid maintenance.

### Volvo D13 13-liter Turbo Diesel

The Volvo D13 is a 13-liter inline-six heavy-duty diesel engine outputting from 405 to 500 horsepower, built for severe-service hauling and heavy vocational tasks. It provides strong low-end torque and proven commercial durability, making it a staple option for demanding job sites.

## Transmission

### Volvo I-Shift Automated Manual Transmission

The Volvo I-Shift is an advanced automated manual transmission available in standard and crawler configurations tailored for vocational use. It optimizes shifting points for heavy loads and rough terrain, contributing to reduced drivetrain wear when properly maintained.

### Allison Vocational Automatic Transmission

The Allison fully automatic transmission option provides seamless power delivery and exceptional reliability in stop-and-go vocational settings. It is frequently selected by buyers prioritizing ease of operation and frequent PTO utilization on tough job sites.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V417000: Wheels:Hub](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volvo&model=VHD&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** A separated hubcap can create a road hazard, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will remove the hubcap, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V403000: Air Bags:Side/Window:Curtain](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volvo&model=VHD&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** A missing circuit will prevent the side air bag from deploying during a crash. An air bag that does not deploy, increases the risk of an injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will connect a jumper harness between the seat and main cab harness, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V511000: Visibility:Windshield Wiper/Washer:Motor](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volvo&model=VHD&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** Inoperative windshield wipers can reduce visibility, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the windshield wiper motor assembly, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V769000: Seats:Critical Fasteners](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volvo&model=VHD&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard numbers 207, "Seating Systems" and 210, "Seatbelt Assembly Anchorage." The attachment points may fail, allowing the bench seat to shift and increasing the risk of injury during a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the weld nut with two nuts and a washer, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V790000: Electrical System:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volvo&model=VHD&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** Electrical noise and low signal to the power line carrier may cause the ECU to incorrectly process commands or stop working. Safety systems that depend on the ECU (Automatic Traction Control, ABS, Electronic Stability Control, Active Cruise Control, and Collison Mitigation System) may have diminished or lost functionality, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will reprogram the ECU software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2022 Volvo VHD represents a capable and modern vocational platform with robust engine and transmission configurations suited for heavy commercial use. Due to the lack of widespread consumer complaints for this model year, buyers should prioritize professional fleet inspection reports, service records, and a thorough check of the emissions systems and chassis wear before purchasing.
