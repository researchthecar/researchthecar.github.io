The 2010 Volvo VHD is a heavy-duty truck designed for vocational applications like construction, logging, and refuse hauling. Engine options included the Volvo D11, D13, and D16. The D13 engine, being a popular choice, produced around 375-500 horsepower and 1450-1850 lb-ft of torque. Owners generally appreciate the VHD for its robust build, comfortable cab, and Volvo's reputation for safety. Common dislikes include the higher initial cost compared to some competitors and potential for more expensive repairs due to specialized parts and service requirements.

**Engine**

**Volvo D11**

*   **Fuel Injector Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rough running engine, decreased fuel economy, black smoke, and possible Diagnostic Trouble Codes (DTCs) related to fuel injector performance.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the faulty fuel injector(s).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+ (depending on the number of injectors needing replacement and labor costs)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Volvo D13**

*   **EGR Valve Malfunction**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Reduced engine power, poor fuel economy, black smoke, and possible check engine light illumination with EGR-related DTCs.
    *   Solution: Cleaning or replacement of the EGR valve.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Turbocharger Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, excessive black smoke, unusual noises from the turbocharger area, and possible check engine light.
    *   Solution: Turbocharger replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Volvo D16**

*   **Coolant Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Loss of coolant, overheating, and visible coolant leaks around the engine. Often found around the water pump, hoses, or radiator.
    *   Solution: Repair or replace the leaking components (water pump, hoses, radiator).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $1,500+ (depending on the location and extent of the leak)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

*   No Transmission Option Specified - Generic Issues Listed Below
*   **Clutch Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Slipping clutch, difficulty shifting gears, unusual noises when engaging the clutch.
    *   Solution: Clutch replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Shift Linkage Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears, loose or sloppy shifter feel.
    *   Solution: Adjustment or replacement of the shift linkage components.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Air Suspension Problems**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Sagging suspension, uneven ride height, air leaks, compressor not running.
    *   Solution: Repair or replacement of air bags, air lines, leveling valves, or the air compressor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $2,000+ (depending on the component needing repair or replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Leaf Spring Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Broken or cracked leaf springs, sagging suspension, poor ride quality.
    *   Solution: Leaf spring replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+ per spring
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Wiring Harness Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Intermittent electrical problems, sensor failures, lights not working, and difficulty starting. Corrosion can affect any part of the wiring harness.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis and repair of the damaged wiring harness sections, cleaning or replacing corroded connectors.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+ (depending on the extent of the damage and labor)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Sensor Failures**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Check engine light, poor engine performance, and various system malfunctions.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis and replacement of the faulty sensor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+ per sensor (depending on the sensor type and location)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Corrosion**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rust formation, especially in areas prone to moisture and salt exposure (frame, undercarriage, body panels).
    *   Solution: Rust removal, repair of affected areas, and application of rust protection coatings.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $5,000+ (depending on the extent of the corrosion)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **HVAC Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Inconsistent heating or cooling, blower motor failure, and malfunctioning controls.
    *   Solution: Repair or replacement of the faulty HVAC components (blower motor, compressor, control module).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2010 Volvo VHD, while a robust and capable truck, can experience issues common to heavy-duty vehicles. Engine issues such as fuel injector, EGR valve, and turbocharger problems, especially within the D13 variant, can lead to significant repair costs. Suspension and electrical issues add to the potential maintenance burden. The severity and cost of these issues can range from a few hundred dollars for sensor replacements to several thousand dollars for engine or transmission overhauls, making thorough inspection and maintenance crucial for long-term reliability.
