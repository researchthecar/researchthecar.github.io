The 2017 Volvo VHD is a heavy-duty truck designed for vocational applications like construction, logging, and refuse hauling. It offers a robust platform and a comfortable, functional cab. Engine options include the Volvo D11, D13, and D16. The D13 engine is popular for its balance of power and efficiency, producing up to 500 horsepower and 1850 lb-ft of torque. Drivers often praise the VHD for its comfortable ride, quiet cab, and Volvo's I-Shift automated manual transmission. Some common complaints include the high cost of parts and repairs and the complexity of the electronic systems.

**Engine**

**Volvo D13**
*   **Leaking Diesel Particulate Filter (DPF) Gasket**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Diesel smell in the cab. Black soot accumulating around the DPF. Reduced engine performance.
    *   Solution: Replace the DPF gasket. Inspect the DPF for damage and clean or replace if necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**Volvo I-Shift**
*   **Shifting Problems/ Erratic Shifting**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Transmission slipping, harsh shifting, failure to shift, or getting stuck in gear. Error codes related to the transmission are likely present.
    *   Solution: Software update. If that doesn't fix the issue then replacing the transmission control module (TCM) or valve body, requires professional diagnosis and repair.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $4,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Air Suspension Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Vehicle leaning to one side, ride height issues, air compressor running constantly. Hissing sound coming from the suspension.
    *   Solution: Locate and repair the air leak by replacing the leaking air bag, air line, or height control valve.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Sensor Failures**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Check engine light illuminated. Various warning lights on the dashboard. Reduced engine performance or other system malfunctions.
    *   Solution: Diagnose the faulty sensor using diagnostic tools and replace the sensor. Common culprits include NOx sensors, coolant temperature sensors, and pressure sensors.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+ per sensor
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Body Control Module (BCM) Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Intermittent electrical problems, such as lights not working, wipers malfunctioning, or issues with power windows and door locks.
    *   Solution: Diagnose the BCM for faults and reprogram or replace the BCM as needed.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Headlight Condensation**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Moisture buildup inside the headlight housing, reducing visibility.
    *   Solution: Remove the headlight assembly, dry it out, and reseal it. Replace the headlight if damage is present or the condensation is severe.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **HVAC System Malfunctions**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Inconsistent heating or cooling, blower motor not working, or issues with temperature control.
    *   Solution: Diagnose and repair the HVAC system, which may involve replacing the blower motor, blend door actuator, or other components.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2017 Volvo VHD, while generally a reliable heavy-duty truck, can experience issues that owners should be aware of. The most common problems seem to revolve around the Volvo I-Shift transmission, air suspension, electrical sensors and HVAC systems. The severity of these issues can range from minor inconveniences to significant downtime, with repair costs varying accordingly. Addressing these issues promptly with proper diagnostics and quality repairs is key to maintaining the VHD's reliability and performance.
