## Suspension

### Volvo Air Suspension (Various Axle Configurations)
**Air Leakage in Air Springs**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle sagging on one side, loss of ride height, audible hissing.
- **Solution:** Replacement of damaged air springs or associated air lines.
- **Cost:** $300 - $800 per spring

**Shock Absorber Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Excessive bouncing, poor handling, uneven tire wear.
- **Solution:** Replacement of worn shock absorbers.
- **Cost:** $400 - $1000 per axle set

### Summary Verdict
The 2020 Volvo VHD demonstrates strong reliability, particularly in its core powertrain components (engines and transmissions), where no widespread issues are reported. The chassis and body components also appear robust for its intended heavy-duty applications. Potential areas for attention include the suspension system, specifically air springs and shock absorbers, which can experience wear and leaks under heavy use, and electrical systems where connector corrosion can lead to intermittent faults. The interior and exterior generally hold up well, with only minor electrical system glitches noted. Overall, the VHD is a dependable choice for commercial operations, with most reported issues being manageable and not indicative of systemic design flaws.
