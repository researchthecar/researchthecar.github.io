# 2020 Volvo VHD Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2020 Volvo VHD is a heavy-duty vocational truck engineered for demanding work environments like construction, dumping, and heavy hauling. Buyers considering a used model should carefully inspect the specific powertrain configuration and overall service history to ensure it matches their operational requirements.

---

## Engine

### Volvo D11 10.8L Turbo-Diesel

The Volvo D11 is an 10.8-liter inline-six diesel engine typically producing between 325 and 425 horsepower, designed for vocational applications requiring a balance of payload capacity and fuel efficiency. It is generally regarded as a durable workhorse, though proper maintenance of emissions and fuel systems is critical for long-term reliability.

### Volvo D13 12.8L Turbo-Diesel

The Volvo D13 is a 12.8-liter inline-six diesel engine offering ratings from 405 to 500 horsepower, built for heavy-duty hauling and demanding vocational tasks. It provides robust low-end torque and is a popular choice for heavy VHD configurations.

## Transmission

### Volvo I-Shift Automated Manual Transmission

The Volvo I-Shift is an automated manual transmission known for smart shifting logic and fuel efficiency in vocational settings. Routine clutch calibration and software updates are recommended to maintain optimal shift quality.

### Allison Automatic Transmission

Allison traditional automatic transmissions are frequently paired with Volvo VHD vocational setups for frequent stop-and-start operations and heavy PTO use. They offer dependable torque conversion and simplified operation in severe-service environments.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V510000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volvo&model=VHD&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** The deformation of the air tank strap t-bolts may cause the t-bolt to disconnect from the strap allowing the tank to drop to the ground and become a road hazard, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Volvo Trucks will notify owners, and dealers will inspect and replace the air tank strap, if necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V509000: Electrical System:Wiring:Fuses And Circuit Breakers](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volvo&model=VHD&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** If the fuse blows the transmission may not shift, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Volvo Trucks will notify owners, and dealers will inspect and replace the fuse, if necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V536000: Service Brakes, Air](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volvo&model=VHD&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 121, "Air Brake Systems." A dislodged brake pedal would be inoperable, increasing a risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Volvo Trucks will notify owners, and dealers will inspect and replace the pedal plates if required, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V769000: Seats:Critical Fasteners](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volvo&model=VHD&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard numbers 207, "Seating Systems" and 210, "Seatbelt Assembly Anchorage." The attachment points may fail, allowing the bench seat to shift and increasing the risk of injury during a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the weld nut with two nuts and a washer, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V790000: Electrical System:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volvo&model=VHD&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** Electrical noise and low signal to the power line carrier may cause the ECU to incorrectly process commands or stop working. Safety systems that depend on the ECU (Automatic Traction Control, ABS, Electronic Stability Control, Active Cruise Control, and Collison Mitigation System) may have diminished or lost functionality, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will reprogram the ECU software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.
