The 2018 Volvo VHD is a heavy-duty vocational truck designed for demanding applications such as construction, refuse hauling, and heavy equipment transport. Engine options include the Volvo D11, D13, and D16. The D13, a popular choice, produces between 405-500 horsepower and 1450-1850 lb-ft of torque, depending on the specific configuration. The Volvo VHD is appreciated for its robust build quality, comfortable cab, and advanced safety features. However, some owners have reported issues related to electrical systems, emissions controls, and specific engine components. Some dislike the cost of replacement parts and repairs.

**Engine**

**Volvo D13**

* **EGR Cooler Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Engine overheating, coolant loss, white smoke from the exhaust, potential for engine damage.
    * Solution: Replacement of the EGR cooler.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Diesel Particulate Filter (DPF) Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: Frequent DPF regeneration cycles, reduced engine power, DPF clogging, dashboard warning lights.
    * Solution: Forced DPF regeneration, cleaning, or replacement.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $3,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Volvo D11**

* **Fuel Injector Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Rough idling, misfires, reduced power, black smoke from the exhaust.
    * Solution: Replacement of the faulty fuel injector(s).
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Volvo D16**

* **Turbocharger Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, excessive black smoke, unusual turbocharger noises, engine warning light.
    * Solution: Turbocharger replacement.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $3,000 - $6,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**Volvo I-Shift**

* **Shift Controller Malfunction**
    * Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears, erratic shifting behavior, transmission fault codes.
    * Solution: Replacement of the shift controller.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Clutch Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Slipping clutch, difficulty engaging gears, transmission noise.
    * Solution: Clutch replacement.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Air Suspension Leaks**
    * Description/Symptoms: Vehicle leaning to one side, ride height issues, air compressor running constantly.
    * Solution: Repair or replacement of leaking air bags, lines, or valves.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Sensor Failures (Various)**
    * Description/Symptoms: Engine warning lights, ABS lights, transmission issues, incorrect readings on gauges.
    * Solution: Diagnosis and replacement of the faulty sensor. This could be wheel speed sensors, coolant temperature sensors, etc.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+ per sensor
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Wiring Harness Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: Intermittent electrical problems, short circuits, corrosion.
    * Solution: Inspection, repair, or replacement of affected wiring harness sections.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Headlight/Taillight Condensation**
    * Description/Symptoms: Moisture buildup inside headlight or taillight housings, reducing visibility.
    * Solution: Replacement of headlight/taillight assembly or resealing of the housing.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+ per light
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **HVAC System Problems**
    * Description/Symptoms: Inconsistent heating or cooling, blower motor failure, refrigerant leaks.
    * Solution: Diagnosis and repair of the HVAC system, including component replacement (blower motor, compressor, etc.).
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2018 Volvo VHD, while a capable and robust vocational truck, can experience a range of issues, particularly related to the engine (EGR coolers, DPF), transmission (I-Shift controller), and electrical systems. While some issues are relatively minor and inexpensive to fix (sensor replacements), others can be quite costly, such as turbocharger replacement or major transmission repairs. The severity of these issues can range from minor inconveniences to potential downtime and significant repair expenses, making thorough inspection and maintenance crucial for owners.
