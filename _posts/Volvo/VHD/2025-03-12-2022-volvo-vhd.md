The 2022 Volvo VHD is a heavy-duty vocational truck designed for demanding applications like construction, logging, and refuse hauling. Engine options include the Volvo D11, D13, and D16. The D13 engine is a popular choice, known for its balance of power and fuel efficiency. The D13 offers horsepower ranging from 405-500 and torque from 1450-1850 lb-ft. Transmissions are typically Volvo's I-Shift automated manual transmission. Owners often appreciate the VHD's robust build quality, comfortable cab, and advanced safety features. Common dislikes include the relatively high price point and the complexity of some of the electronic systems.

**Engine**

**Volvo D13**

*   **Leaking Injector Seals**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Diesel fuel smell, reduced fuel economy, visible fuel leak around the injectors.
    *   Solution: Replace the injector seals.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **EGR Valve Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Check engine light, reduced power, rough idling, increased emissions.
    *   Solution: Replace the EGR valve.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1200
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Volvo D16**

*   **Turbocharger Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, black smoke from exhaust, whining noise from turbocharger.
    *   Solution: Replace the turbocharger.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $2500 - $5000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**Volvo I-Shift**

*   **Shifting Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Harsh shifting, delayed shifting, skipping gears, transmission slipping.
    *   Solution: Valve body replacement, software update.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $3000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Clutch Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Difficulty engaging gears, clutch slippage, unusual noises when pressing the clutch pedal.
    *   Solution: Replace the clutch assembly.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $2000 - $4000
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Air Suspension Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Vehicle leaning to one side, air compressor running frequently, hissing sound from air suspension components.
    *   Solution: Replace leaking air springs or fittings.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1500+ (depending on the component)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Sensor Malfunctions**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Various warning lights, inaccurate readings on gauges, engine performance issues.
    *   Solution: Replace faulty sensors (e.g., ABS sensor, speed sensor, temperature sensor).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500 per sensor
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Wiring Harness Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Intermittent electrical problems, lights flickering, random system failures.
    *   Solution: Repair or replace damaged wiring harness sections.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $2000+ (depending on the severity)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Headlight Condensation**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Moisture buildup inside headlight lenses.
    *   Solution: Replace headlight assembly or attempt to reseal the existing unit.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1000+ per headlight
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **HVAC Blower Motor Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: No air blowing from vents, weak airflow, unusual noises from the blower motor.
    *   Solution: Replace the blower motor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2022 Volvo VHD, while a robust and capable truck, experiences common issues related to its diesel engines, automated manual transmission, and complex electrical systems. While not every VHD will experience all these problems, potential buyers and owners should be aware of these potential issues. The severity and cost of repairs can vary widely depending on the specific component affected, with engine and transmission repairs potentially costing several thousand dollars. Addressing these issues promptly is crucial to maintaining the VHD's reliability and minimizing downtime.
