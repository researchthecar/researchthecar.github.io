The 2016 Volvo VHD is a heavy-duty truck designed for vocational applications like construction, logging, and refuse hauling. It's known for its robust build, driver comfort, and Volvo's focus on safety. Engine options typically include the Volvo D11, D13, and D16, providing a range of horsepower and torque outputs. The D13, for example, delivers up to 500 horsepower and 1850 lb-ft of torque. Transmission options commonly include Volvo's I-Shift automated manual transmission, as well as manual transmissions from Eaton. Drivers often appreciate the VHD's comfortable cabin and maneuverability, while some may find the Volvo-specific parts and maintenance can be pricier than some competitors.

**Engine**

**Volvo D13**

*   **EGR Cooler Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaks, white smoke from the exhaust, engine overheating, reduced engine performance.
    *   Solution: Replace the EGR cooler.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Fuel Injector Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rough idling, misfires, reduced power, poor fuel economy, black smoke from the exhaust.
    *   Solution: Replace faulty fuel injectors.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Volvo D16**

*   **Oil Leaks from Valve Cover Gaskets**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Visible oil leaks around the valve covers, burning oil smell, low oil level.
    *   Solution: Replace the valve cover gaskets.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**Volvo I-Shift**

*   **Shifting Problems/ Erratic Shifting**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears, slipping between gears, harsh or jerky shifts, error codes related to the transmission control module (TCM).
    *   Solution: This can involve TCM reprogramming, solenoid replacement, or clutch adjustment/replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $5,000+ (depending on the severity and required repair)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Clutch Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Slipping clutch, difficulty engaging gears, unusual noises during shifting.
    *   Solution: Clutch replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,500 - $5,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Air Suspension Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Vehicle leaning to one side, air compressor running constantly, hissing sounds from air leaks, rough ride.
    *   Solution: Repair or replace leaking air bags, air lines, or leveling valves.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,500+ (depending on the component needing replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Worn Shock Absorbers**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Bouncy or unstable ride, excessive body roll during turns, uneven tire wear.
    *   Solution: Replace worn shock absorbers.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Sensor Failures**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Various warning lights on the dashboard, fault codes related to engine, transmission, or other systems, reduced engine performance.
    *   Solution: Diagnose and replace faulty sensors (e.g., wheel speed sensors, coolant temperature sensors, pressure sensors).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+ per sensor
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Wiring Harness Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Intermittent electrical problems, short circuits, corrosion, difficulty starting, malfunctioning lights or accessories.
    *   Solution: Repair or replace damaged wiring harnesses.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $2,000+ (depending on the extent of the damage)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Headlight Condensation**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Moisture or condensation inside the headlight housings, reduced headlight visibility.
    *   Solution: Replace or reseal the headlight housings.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **HVAC System Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Inconsistent heating or cooling, weak airflow, unusual noises from the HVAC system.
    *   Solution: Diagnose and repair or replace faulty components such as the blower motor, blend door actuator, or compressor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2016 Volvo VHD can experience a range of issues, from engine and transmission problems to suspension and electrical malfunctions. Engine issues such as EGR cooler failures and fuel injector issues for the D13 can be costly to repair. Transmission problems with the I-Shift, including shifting problems and clutch failure, also represent potentially expensive repairs. Other common issues include air suspension leaks and sensor failures. While the VHD is known for its durability, owners should be aware of these potential issues and budget accordingly for maintenance and repairs. The severity of the reported issues vary from minor to major.
