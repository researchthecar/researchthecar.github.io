# 2017 Volvo VHD Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2017 Volvo VHD is a heavy-duty vocational truck built for demanding work environments like construction and utility hauling. Powered by robust commercial diesel engines paired with heavy-duty manual or automated manual transmissions, it is designed for heavy workloads. Prospective used buyers should inspect powertrain components thoroughly and review maintenance histories to ensure optimal performance in commercial applications.

---

## Engine

### Volvo D11 10.8L Turbo-Diesel I6

The Volvo D11 is an 10.8-liter inline-six turbo-diesel engine typically outputting between 325 and 425 horsepower. It offers a balanced blend of power and fuel efficiency suited for vocational straight truck applications.

### Volvo D13 12.8L Turbo-Diesel I6

The Volvo D13 is a heavy-duty 12.8-liter inline-six turbo-diesel engine producing roughly 375 to 500 horsepower. Known for strong low-end torque, it is the primary engine choice for heavy hauling and demanding job-site duties.

## Transmission

### Volvo I-Shift 12-Speed Automated Manual Transmission

The Volvo I-Shift is an advanced 12-speed automated manual transmission designed to optimize shift points and improve fuel economy. It is widely favored in vocational fleets for its durability and ease of operation.

### Eaton Fuller 10/13/18-Speed Manual Transmission

Traditional Eaton Fuller manual transmissions offer robust mechanical reliability and direct driver control for severe-service applications. They remain a popular choice for operators preferring classic gear selection.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V766000: Exterior Lighting](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volvo&model=VHD&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 108, "Lamps, Reflective Devices, and Associated Equipment." The driver may operate the vehicle with the snow plow headlights on but the marker and tail lamps off, reducing the vehicle's visibility and increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Volvo will notify owners, and dealers will update the software related to the plow lights, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V258000: Equipment:Other:Labels](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volvo&model=VHD&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of 49 CFR Part 567, "Certification." If the GAWR information is incorrect, the operator may overload the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 16V927000: Suspension:Front:Hub](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volvo&model=VHD&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** Because of this, the driver may lose control of the vehicle or parts may be dropped on the road, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Volvo will notify owners, and dealers will replace the hub assemblies, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2017 Volvo VHD is a capable and durable vocational platform equipped with proven heavy-duty commercial powertrains. While commercial trucks demand rigorous inspection of their engines, transmissions, and chassis components prior to purchase, a well-maintained model can serve reliably in demanding work environments.
