The 2007 Volvo C30 is a sporty and stylish compact hatchback known for its unique design and comfortable ride. Engine options include a 2.4L inline-5 producing 168 horsepower and 170 lb-ft of torque and a turbocharged 2.5L inline-5 producing 218 horsepower and 236 lb-ft of torque. Transmission options consist of a 5-speed manual, 6-speed manual, and a 5-speed automatic. Owners often praise the C30 for its distinctive look, comfortable front seats, and overall driving experience. However, common complaints include potential electrical issues, relatively small rear seats, and some reported reliability concerns related to specific components.

**Engine**

**2.4L Inline-5**
*   **Rough Idling/Misfires**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rough idling, engine misfires, and the check engine light may illuminate. Often caused by faulty ignition coils or spark plugs.
    *   Solution: Replace faulty ignition coils and spark plugs.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Evaporative Emission (EVAP) System Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Check engine light illuminated, often with EVAP system related codes. Potential causes include a faulty gas cap, purge valve, or other EVAP components.
    *   Solution: Diagnose and replace the leaking EVAP system component.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**2.5L Turbocharged Inline-5 (T5)**

*   **Turbocharger Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Reduced power, whining noise from the turbocharger, or the check engine light illuminating. Potential issues include wastegate failure or damaged turbocharger bearings.
    *   Solution: Inspect and repair or replace the turbocharger.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $2000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Boost Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, hissing sound during acceleration, and the check engine light may illuminate. Often caused by damaged or loose vacuum hoses or intercooler piping.
    *   Solution: Replace damaged hoses or tighten loose connections.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**5-Speed Automatic**

*   **Harsh or Delayed Shifting**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Harsh or delayed shifts, slipping between gears, or erratic shifting behavior. Could be caused by worn valve body components or contaminated transmission fluid.
    *   Solution: Perform a transmission fluid flush and filter change. If the issue persists, further diagnosis and repair of the valve body may be required.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $1500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Worn Suspension Components**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Clunking noises from the suspension, poor handling, and uneven tire wear. Common culprits include worn struts, shocks, sway bar links, and control arm bushings.
    *   Solution: Replace worn suspension components.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Central Electronic Module (CEM) Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Intermittent electrical problems, such as malfunctioning windows, door locks, or lights. Can be caused by water intrusion or internal CEM failure.
    *   Solution: Diagnose and repair or replace the CEM.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Driver Information Module (DIM) Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Intermittent flickering of the instrument cluster.
    *   Solution: Requires DIM replacement or professional soldering repair.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Tail Light Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Malfunctioning tail lights are a common problem reported, due to corroded bulb sockets or wiring issues.
    *   Solution: Replace bulb socket, repair wiring, or replace the tail light assembly.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $300+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Rear Hatch Handle Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The rear hatch handle can become brittle and break, making it difficult to open the rear hatch.
    *   Solution: Replace the rear hatch handle assembly.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Sunroof Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Sunroof not opening/closing smoothly, sunroof getting stuck, or water leaks.
    *   Solution: Repair the sunroof mechanism or replace seals to stop leaking.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

The 2007 Volvo C30, while praised for its style and driving experience, can be susceptible to certain issues. Electrical problems stemming from the CEM and DIM are notable concerns and potentially costly. Engine issues such as turbocharger problems on the T5 and rough idling on the 2.4L engine are also worth noting. The severity of these issues can vary, with costs ranging from relatively minor fixes like replacing a gas cap to more substantial repairs like replacing a turbocharger or CEM.
