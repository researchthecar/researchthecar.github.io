The 2017 Volvo VNR 300 is a regional day cab tractor designed for vocational and local distribution applications. Engine options included the Volvo D11, D13, and Cummins ISX12 (later known as the X12). The most common transmission choice was the Volvo I-Shift automated manual transmission. Drivers often praised the VNR 300 for its maneuverability, comfortable cab, and fuel efficiency. Common complaints centered around electronic issues, specific engine component failures, and the cost of replacement parts and repairs. Horsepower and torque figures vary depending on the specific engine and calibration, but the Volvo D13 for example, offers a range of horsepower typically between 405-500 hp and torque between 1450-1850 lb-ft.

**Engine**

**Volvo D13**

*   **EGR Cooler Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaks, white smoke from exhaust, engine overheating, loss of power.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the EGR cooler.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Fuel Injector Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rough idling, misfires, reduced fuel economy, black smoke.
    *   Solution: Replacement of faulty fuel injectors.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $2,500+ (depending on the number of injectors)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Turbocharger Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, excessive black smoke, unusual noises from the turbo.
    *   Solution: Turbocharger replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Cummins ISX12/X12**

*   **Diesel Particulate Filter (DPF) Problems**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Frequent regeneration cycles, reduced engine power, DPF light illumination.
    *   Solution: Forced DPF regeneration, cleaning, or replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $4,000+ (depending on severity and if replacement is needed)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **EGR Valve Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rough idling, poor performance, check engine light.
    *   Solution: EGR valve replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Volvo D11**

*   **Oil Leaks (Various Locations)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Visible oil leaks, low oil level, potential engine damage if ignored.
    *   Solution: Identify and repair the source of the leak (valve cover gaskets, oil pan gasket, etc.).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+ (depending on location and complexity)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**Volvo I-Shift (Automated Manual)**

*   **Shift Hesitation/Rough Shifting**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Delay when shifting gears, jerky or harsh shifts.
    *   Solution: Software updates, clutch adjustment, or potentially valve body replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $3,000+ (depending on the cause)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Clutch Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Slipping clutch, difficulty engaging gears, complete loss of drive.
    *   Solution: Clutch replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,500 - $5,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Air Suspension Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Vehicle leaning to one side, compressor running constantly, ride height issues.
    *   Solution: Repair or replacement of leaking air bags, air lines, or leveling valves.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,500+ (depending on the component)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Worn Shock Absorbers**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Bouncy ride, poor handling, excessive body roll.
    *   Solution: Shock absorber replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,000+ (depending on number of shocks needing replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Sensor Failures (Various)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Check engine light, ABS light, or other warning lights illuminated. Faulty data being reported.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis to identify the failing sensor and replacement of the sensor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+ per sensor (depending on sensor type and location)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Wiring Harness Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Intermittent electrical problems, shorts, open circuits, difficulty starting.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis and repair or replacement of damaged wiring.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $2,000+ (depending on the extent of the damage)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **ECU Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Fault codes, improper operation, drivability issues, no start.
    *   Solution: Reprogram ECU, or replace ECU.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500-$3000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Headlight/Taillight Failures**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Lights not working properly, dimmed lights
    *   Solution: Replace headlight or taillight bulbs or assembly if damaged.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50-$300+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **HVAC System Malfunctions**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Inconsistent heating or cooling, blower motor failures, blend door actuator issues.
    *   Solution: Repair or replacement of faulty components.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Instrument Cluster Problems**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Incorrect readings, flickering lights, complete failure.
    *   Solution: Repair or replacement of the instrument cluster.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2017 Volvo VNR 300, while generally a reliable work truck, can experience issues related to its engine, transmission, electrical system, and other components. Engine problems, especially with the D13, often involve the EGR system and fuel injectors. The I-Shift transmission can have shifting issues and clutch failures. Electrical problems and air suspension leaks are also common. The severity of these issues varies, but repairs can be costly, ranging from a few hundred dollars for sensor replacements to several thousand for engine or transmission overhauls. Prospective buyers should thoroughly inspect the truck and review its maintenance history to identify and address potential problems before purchase.
