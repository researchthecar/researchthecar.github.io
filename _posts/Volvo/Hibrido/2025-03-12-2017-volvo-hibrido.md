The 2017 Volvo XC60 T8 Twin Engine is a plug-in hybrid SUV that combines a 2.0-liter supercharged and turbocharged four-cylinder engine with an electric motor. This combination produces a total output of 400 horsepower and 472 lb-ft of torque. Other engine options available for the 2017 XC60 included a 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder (T5) and a 2.0-liter turbocharged and supercharged four-cylinder (T6). The XC60 is generally praised for its comfortable ride, stylish interior, and safety features, but some owners have reported issues with the infotainment system, hybrid system reliability, and certain electrical components.

**Electric Motor**

**T8 Twin Engine**

*   **Electric Motor Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported complete failure of the electric motor, resulting in a significant loss of power and rendering the hybrid system inoperable. The vehicle may display error messages related to the hybrid system.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the electric motor is typically required.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $5,000 - $8,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Battery**

**T8 Twin Engine**

*   **Reduced Battery Capacity**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners report a noticeable reduction in the battery's ability to hold a charge over time, leading to shorter electric-only driving ranges.
    *   Solution: Battery rebalancing or replacement of individual battery cells may be necessary. In severe cases, a complete battery pack replacement may be required.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $7,000+ depending on the extent of the damage
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**T8 Twin Engine (8-Speed Automatic)**

*   **Rough Shifting**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Harsh or jerky gear changes, especially at lower speeds. Hesitation or delayed engagement when accelerating from a stop.
    *   Solution: Software updates, transmission fluid flush, or valve body replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $2,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Sensus Infotainment System Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Freezing, slow response times, Bluetooth connectivity problems, navigation errors, and system reboots.
    *   Solution: Software updates are often the first step. In some cases, replacement of the infotainment unit may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 (software update) - $1,500+ (unit replacement)
    * Recalls: None Found
*   **Sensor Malfunctions**
    * Description/Symptoms: Faulty readings from sensors such as the park assist sensors, blind spot monitoring sensors, or lane keep assist sensors.
    * Solution: Diagnosis and replacement of the faulty sensor.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500 per sensor.
    * Recalls: None Found

**HVAC**

*   **Climate Control Inconsistent Performance**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported inconsistent climate control performance, with the system struggling to maintain a consistent temperature or experiencing issues with air distribution.
    *   Solution: Check refrigerant levels, diagnose and repair any leaks in the system, and inspect/replace the blend door actuator.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $800
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

The 2017 Volvo XC60 T8 Twin Engine presents a mix of potential issues. Electrical issues with the infotainment system and sensor malfunctions appear to be relatively common, potentially causing frustration. Hybrid system components such as the electric motor and battery can present more significant problems that lead to costly repairs. Transmission issues can also be a cause for concern. Overall, potential buyers should be aware of these potential issues and consider a thorough inspection before purchasing. The severity and cost of repairs can vary widely, but addressing hybrid system issues can be particularly expensive.
