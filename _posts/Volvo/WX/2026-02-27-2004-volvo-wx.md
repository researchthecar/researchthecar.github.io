## Engine

### D12 (12.1L Diesel)
**EGR Cooler Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Coolant loss, white smoke from exhaust, engine overheating.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the EGR cooler assembly.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1500

**Injector Sleeve Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Coolant entering cylinders, rough running, misfires.
- **Solution:** Replacement of injector sleeves and potentially injectors.
- **Cost:** $600 - $1200

## Suspension

### Air Suspension System
**Air Bag Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle sagging on one side, uneven ride height, compressor running excessively.
- **Solution:** Replacement of leaking air springs or air lines.
- **Cost:** $400 - $900

**Height Control Valve Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Inability to maintain ride height, erratic suspension behavior.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of the height control valve.
- **Cost:** $300 - $700

### Summary Verdict
The 2004 Volvo WX, particularly with its D12 engine option, demonstrates a generally strong reliability record for a commercial vehicle. The most commonly cited issues revolve around the EGR cooler and injector sleeves on the D12, which, while requiring attention, are repairable. The I-Shift transmission can experience actuator issues, and the air suspension system may develop leaks over time. Electrical system integrity, especially wiring harnesses, is crucial for long-term performance. Overall, with diligent maintenance and addressing specific known issues promptly, the 2004 Volvo WX is considered a dependable workhorse, with its robust construction contributing significantly to its longevity in demanding vocational applications.
