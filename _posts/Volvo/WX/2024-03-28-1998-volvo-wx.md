**Introduction to the 1998 Volvo WX**

The 1998 Volvo WX is a midsize luxury sedan that combines safety, comfort, and performance. It is available with a choice of two engines: a 2.4-liter inline-5 and a 2.9-liter inline-6. Both engines are mated to a 4-speed automatic transmission. The WX is praised for its spacious interior, comfortable seats, and impressive safety features. However, it has also been plagued by several common issues across its major components.

**Engine**

**2.4-liter Inline-5**

* **Intake Manifold Gasket Failure:** Causes coolant and oil leaks, rough idle, and loss of power.
* Solution: Replace intake manifold gasket.
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $600 - $800
* Recalls: 13V-023

**2.9-liter Inline-6**

* **Timing Belt Failure:** Can lead to catastrophic engine damage if not replaced at regular intervals.
* Solution: Replace timing belt and water pump.
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $1,200
* Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**4-speed Automatic Transmission**

* **Transmission Slippage:** May cause delayed or sluggish shifting, especially in higher gears.
* Solution: Overhaul or replace transmission.
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500
* Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Strut Mount Failure:** Causes excessive noise, vibration, and poor handling.
* Solution: Replace strut mounts.
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $600
* Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Alternator Failure:** Causes battery drain and potential electrical system malfunctions.
* Solution: Replace alternator.
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $600 - $800
* Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Headlight Switch Failure:** Can lead to intermittent or complete headlight failure.
* Solution: Replace headlight switch.
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $300
* Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Power Window Failure:** May cause difficulty in raising or lowering windows.
* Solution: Replace window motor or switch.
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $250 - $500
* Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 1998 Volvo WX has a range of issues, with the most common problems affecting the engine, transmission, and suspension. While the car is known for its safety and comfort, it is important to be aware of these potential concerns before making a purchase decision. The severity of the issues and the cost of repairs can vary, so it is recommended to have the vehicle inspected by a qualified mechanic before purchasing.
