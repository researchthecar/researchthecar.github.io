The 2004 Volvo XC90 marked Volvo's entry into the SUV market. It was generally praised for its safety, comfortable ride, and Scandinavian design. However, some common complaints included electrical gremlins, particularly with the early models, and some concerns about reliability with the transmission. Engine options for the 2004 XC90 included a 2.5L Turbocharged Inline-5 producing 208 hp and 236 lb-ft of torque, and a 2.9L Turbocharged Inline-6 generating 268 hp and 280 lb-ft of torque. The inline-5 was paired with an Aisin Warner 5-speed automatic transmission while the inline-6 was paired with an Aisin Warner 4-speed automatic transmission.

**Engine**

**2.5L Turbocharged Inline-5**
*   **Turbocharger Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Whining noise from the turbo, loss of power, check engine light.
    *   Solution: Turbocharger replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1200 - $2000+

*   **PCV System Clogging**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rough idle, oil leaks from various seals, whistling noise from the engine.
    *   Solution: PCV system cleaning or replacement (oil trap, hoses, etc.).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+

**2.9L Turbocharged Inline-6**

*   **Coil Pack Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Misfires, rough running engine, check engine light.
    *   Solution: Coil pack replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+ (per coil pack)

*   **Throttle Body Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Erratic idle, hesitation upon acceleration, check engine light.
    *   Solution: Throttle body cleaning or replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+

**Transmission**

**Aisin Warner 5-speed Automatic (paired with 2.5L Turbo)**
*   **Valve Body Problems**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Harsh or delayed shifting, slipping between gears, transmission fault message.
    *   Solution: Valve body replacement or rebuild.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $2000+

**Aisin Warner 4-speed Automatic (paired with 2.9L Turbo)**
*   **Slipping**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Transmission slips in gear, hesitates to engage, lacks power, "check engine light"
    *   Solution: Full transmission replacement
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $3000+

**Suspension**

*   **Front Lower Control Arm Bushing Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Clunking noise from the front suspension, especially over bumps, uneven tire wear, vague steering.
    *   Solution: Front lower control arm replacement or bushing replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+ (per side)

*   **Rear Suspension Nivomat Leveling System Failure (if equipped)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Sagging rear suspension, poor ride quality, bottoming out.
    *   Solution: Replacement of Nivomat shocks with new Nivomats, or conversion to standard shocks.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $2000+ (for Nivomat replacement)
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $1000+ (conversion to standard)

**Electrical**

*   **Driver Information Module (DIM) Issues (Instrument Cluster)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Intermittent or complete failure of the instrument cluster display, flickering lights, warning messages.
    *   Solution: DIM repair or replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1000+

*   **Electronic Throttle Module (ETM) Problems**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Reduced engine power message, limp mode, stalling. This was particularly common on earlier Volvos and can persist in the 2004 model year.
    *   Solution: ETM replacement or software update.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Central Electronic Module (CEM) Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Various electrical malfunctions, including issues with power windows, door locks, lights, and alarm system.
    *   Solution: CEM repair or replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1500+

**Exterior**

*   **Tailgate Wire Harness Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Malfunctioning tailgate lights, license plate lights, or rear wiper.
    *   Solution: Wire harness repair or replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $400+

**Interior**

*   **Sunroof Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Sunroof sticking, failing to open or close properly, excessive noise during operation.
    *   Solution: Sunroof motor replacement, track repair, or linkage repair.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+

**Summary**

The 2004 Volvo XC90 can be a comfortable and safe SUV, but potential buyers should be aware of the common issues, particularly with the electrical system, transmission, and suspension components. Engine issues are typically related to turbocharger maintenance and proper PCV system function. Repair costs can vary, but some issues, like transmission or Nivomat suspension repairs, can be quite expensive. Careful inspection and a thorough pre-purchase inspection are highly recommended.
