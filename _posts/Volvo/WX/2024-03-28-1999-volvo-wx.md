The 1999 Volvo WX is a compact executive car available with a variety of engine and transmission options. The naturally aspirated 2.4-liter inline-5 engine produces 168 horsepower and 170 lb-ft of torque, while the turbocharged 2.5-liter inline-5 engine generates 225 horsepower and 236 lb-ft of torque. Both engines are paired with a 5-speed manual transmission or a 4-speed automatic transmission.

The 1999 Volvo WX is praised for its comfortable interior, responsive handling, and high safety ratings. However, it has received some criticism for its lack of standard features and dated exterior design.

**Engine**

**2.4-liter Inline-5**

* **Oil Leaks:** Symptoms include visible oil under the vehicle, burning oil smell, and low oil level. Solution: Replace leaking gaskets, seals, or lines. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,000
* **Vacuum Leaks:** Symptoms include rough idling, poor fuel economy, and engine stalling. Solution: Inspect and replace any damaged or leaking vacuum hoses. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $250
* **Intake Manifold Gasket Failure:** Symptoms include coolant leaks, overheating, and reduced engine performance. Solution: Replace the intake manifold gasket. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,200
* **Coolant Thermostat Failure:** Symptoms include engine overheating or underheating, poor heater performance. Solution: Replace the coolant thermostat. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500

**2.5-liter Inline-5 Turbo**

* **Wastegate Issues:** Symptoms include overboosting, decreased engine performance, and potential engine damage. Solution: Replace or adjust the wastegate actuator or solenoid. Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800
* **Oil Leaks:** Symptoms include visible oil under the vehicle, burning oil smell, and low oil level. Solution: Replace leaking gaskets, seals, or lines. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,000
* **Coolant Leaks:** Symptoms include visible coolant leaks, overheating, and low coolant level. Solution: Inspect and replace any damaged or leaking hoses, gaskets, or seals. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800

**Transmission**

**5-Speed Manual**

* **Clutch Issues:** Symptoms include difficulty shifting gears, slipping clutch, or chatter. Solution: Replace the clutch disc, pressure plate, and throw out bearing. Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,200

**4-Speed Automatic**

* **Transmission Slipping:** Symptoms include delayed or hesitant shifting, loss of power, and rough shifting. Solution: Replace or rebuild the transmission. Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+
* **Shift Solenoid Malfunction:** Symptoms include shifting problems, delayed or no engagement of certain gears, or limp mode. Solution: Replace the faulty shift solenoid. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800
* **Torque Converter Failure:** Symptoms include shuddering, slipping, or overheating during gear changes. Solution: Replace the torque converter. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+

**Suspension**

* **Strut Mount Bearing Wear:** Symptoms include clunking or rattling noises over bumps, reduced handling and stability. Solution: Replace the strut mount bearing. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
* **Control Arm Bushing Failure:** Symptoms include excessive vibration, poor handling and stability, and uneven tire wear. Solution: Replace the control arm bushings. Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300 per arm

**Electrical**

* **Alternator Failure:** Symptoms include dim headlights, flickering interior lights, and difficulty starting the vehicle. Solution: Replace the alternator. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
* **Starter Motor Failure:** Symptoms include difficulty starting the vehicle or intermittent starting issues. Solution: Replace the starter motor. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600
* **Electrical Wiring Issues:** Symptoms include intermittent power loss, malfunctioning electrical components, and electrical shorts. Solution: Diagnose and repair the faulty wiring. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,000+

**Exterior**

* **Headlight Moisture:** Symptoms include water or condensation inside the headlight assembly. Solution: Inspect and replace any damaged seals or gaskets. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $250
* **Tail Light Circuit Malfunction:** Symptoms include intermittent or non-functional tail lights. Solution: Inspect and replace any damaged bulbs, sockets, or wiring. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200

**Interior**

* **Power Window Switch Failure:** Symptoms include non-functional power windows. Solution: Replace the defective window switch. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
* **Door Lock Actuator Malfunction:** Symptoms include difficulty locking or unlocking the doors. Solution: Replace the defective door lock actuator. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400

**Summary**

The 1999 Volvo WX offers a reliable and comfortable driving experience with a proven track record of safety and handling. However, it does have some potential issues that owners may encounter, particularly with the transmission and electrical system. The cost of these issues can vary greatly depending on the severity of the problem and the labor rates in your area.
