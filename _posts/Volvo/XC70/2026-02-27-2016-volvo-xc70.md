# 2016 Volvo XC70 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used Volvo XC70, buyers will find a comfortable, rugged wagon known for its distinct styling and practical cargo space. Reviewing owner experiences highlights a few recurring electrical, exterior, and safety concerns worth keeping in mind during a pre-purchase inspection.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged 4-Cylinder

This 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine generates approximately 240 horsepower and provides a balance of efficiency and power for daily driving. It is generally robust, though owners should ensure regular maintenance intervals have been followed.

### 2.5L Turbocharged 5-Cylinder

This iconic 2.5-liter turbocharged five-cylinder engine produces around 250 horsepower, offering a signature exhaust note and strong mid-range torque. It is widely regarded as durable, though routine upkeep remains essential.

**Engine Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle may experience unexpected stalling while driving or stopping at intersections, sometimes accompanied by electrical power issues.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified mechanic diagnose the charging system, battery health, and electronic control modules.

### 3.0L Turbocharged 6-Cylinder

This 3.0-liter turbocharged inline-six pushes out roughly 300 horsepower, delivering smooth and rapid acceleration across the power band. It is a solid performer for buyers seeking extra passing power.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

This 8-speed automatic transmission pairs with front-wheel drive configurations to optimize fuel economy and gear selection. Routine fluid checks help ensure long-term shifting smoothness.

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

This 6-speed automatic transmission works alongside all-wheel drive models to deliver power reliably to all four wheels. It generally offers dependable operation when properly maintained.

**Gear Shifter Binding**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may experience difficulty moving the gear selector out of park when the vehicle is cold, or receive false warnings that the car is not in park.
- **Solution:** Inspect the shifter assembly and associated linkage sensors for mechanical wear or electronic fault codes.

## Electrical

### Instrument Panel and Lighting Controls

Electrical system complaints indicate occasional intermittent failures within the cabin display modules and auxiliary trailer lighting controls.

**Instrument Cluster Blackout**
- **Symptoms:** The driver's information module or instrument panel may experience intermittent blackouts, alongside potential faults in accessory lighting controls.
- **Solution:** Have a technician inspect the vehicle's central electronic modules and wiring harnesses for loose connections or software glitches.

### Sunroof and Visibility Equipment

Exterior body and visibility components generally function as intended, though specific design quirks and rare glass durability reports warrant careful inspection.

**Sunroof Shattering and Backup Camera Obstruction**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have reported rare instances of the sunroof glass shattering unexpectedly while driving, as well as backup camera view fields being partially blocked by the vehicle body design.
- **Solution:** Inspect the panoramic sunroof for stress fractures or damage, and verify camera alignment during a test drive.

## Safety

### Seat Belts

Safety equipment operates reliably under normal conditions, but any reports of latching or unbuckling difficulties should be addressed immediately.

**Seat Belt Buckle Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Front driver or passenger seatbelts may occasionally fail to release properly when attempting to unbuckle.
- **Solution:** Have the affected seat belt buckle assemblies inspected and replaced if mechanical latching mechanisms stick.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V378000: Seat Belts:Front](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volvo&model=XC70&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** The flexible steel cable that connects the seat belt to the front outboard seating positions may fatigue over time, causing the seat belt to not secure the occupant in the event of a crash. An unsecured occupant has an increased risk of injury in the event of a crash.
- **What to do:** Volvo will notify owners, and dealers will replace the seat belt anchor cable for both front seats. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 15V555000: Electrical System:Wiring:Fuses And Circuit Breakers](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volvo&model=XC70&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** If the vehicle is unexpectedly unable to be started in the middle of traffic, such as when stopped at an intersection, there is an increased risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Volvo will notify owners, and dealers will replace the existing starter motor fuse with a higher amperage fuse, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2016 Volvo XC70 remains a practical and versatile option for used car buyers seeking luxury, utility, and safety. While owner reports point to occasional electrical glitches, shifter binding, and rare seat belt or sunroof concerns, a thorough pre-purchase inspection can help verify that all systems are in proper working order.
