The 2015 Volvo XC70 is a versatile wagon known for its comfort, safety, and all-weather capability. Engine options included the 2.0L turbocharged inline-4 (T5 Drive-E), the 2.5L turbocharged inline-5 (T5), and the 3.0L turbocharged inline-6 (T6). The T5 Drive-E produced 240 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque. What people love about the XC70 is its comfortable ride, spacious interior, and reputation for longevity. Common dislikes include its somewhat dated infotainment system and occasionally higher repair costs compared to some competitors.

**Engine**

**2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4 (T5 Drive-E)**

*   **Oil Consumption**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported excessive oil consumption, requiring more frequent oil top-offs between scheduled changes. Visible exhaust smoke may also be present.
    *   Solution: Potential solutions include replacing piston rings or addressing PCV system issues.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $4,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**2.5L Turbocharged Inline-5 (T5)**

*   **PCV System Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Clogged or failing PCV system can cause vacuum leaks, rough idling, oil leaks, and potential engine damage.
    *   Solution: Replacing the PCV valve and related components.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**3.0L Turbocharged Inline-6 (T6)**

*   **Turbocharger Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported premature turbocharger failure, leading to reduced power, unusual noises (whining or screeching), and potential engine damage.
    *   Solution: Replacing the turbocharger.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**Aisin Warner AW55-51SN 5-Speed Automatic (paired with 2.5L Turbocharged Inline-5)**

*   **Valve Body Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Hard shifting, slipping between gears, or failure to shift properly.
    *   Solution: Replacing or rebuilding the valve body. Fluid changes may temporarily alleviate the problem.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $2,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Aisin Warner TF-80SC 6-Speed Automatic (paired with 2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4 and 3.0L Turbocharged Inline-6)**

*   **Solenoid Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Erratic shifting, harsh engagement, or getting stuck in a gear.
    *   Solution: Replacing the affected solenoids within the transmission.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Rear Air Suspension Failure (if equipped)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Vehicle sagging in the rear, compressor running constantly, suspension fault warnings.
    *   Solution: Replacing the air springs, compressor, or related components.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Sunroof Drain Clogs**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Water leaking into the cabin, particularly around the headliner or footwells.
    *   Solution: Clearing the sunroof drains.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Central Electronic Module (CEM) Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Intermittent electrical issues, such as malfunctioning windows, locks, lights, or other electronic systems.
    *   Solution: Replacing or reprogramming the CEM.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Headlight Condensation**
    * Description/Symptoms: Moisture buildup inside the headlight lenses.
    * Solution: Replacing the headlight housing or resealing the existing one.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300-$800+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Navigation Screen Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The navigation screen may become unresponsive, glitchy, or completely fail.
    *   Solution: Replacing the navigation screen unit.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2015 Volvo XC70 is generally a reliable vehicle, but owners should be aware of potential issues with the PCV system, turbochargers (on T6 models), transmissions (specifically valve body/solenoid issues), and the rear air suspension (if equipped). Electrical issues related to the CEM and sunroof drains can also occur. Repair costs can range from a few hundred dollars for minor fixes to several thousand for major engine or transmission work. While the XC70 is known for its longevity, preventative maintenance and addressing issues promptly can help minimize unexpected repairs.
