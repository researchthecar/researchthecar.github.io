The 2014 Volvo XC70 is a versatile and comfortable wagon known for its safety, reliability, and spacious interior. Engine options include a 3.2L inline-6 producing 240 horsepower and 236 lb-ft of torque, a 3.0L turbocharged inline-6 (T6) producing 300 horsepower and 325 lb-ft of torque, and a 2.0L turbocharged inline-4 producing 240 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque. All engines were paired with a 6-speed automatic transmission. Owners often praise the XC70 for its comfortable ride, ample cargo space, and all-weather capability. Common dislikes include somewhat dated infotainment and lower fuel economy than some competitors.

**Engine**

**3.2L Inline-6**

* **Oil Leaks**
    * Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks, often from the valve cover gasket or rear main seal. Can result in oil consumption, low oil pressure warning, and visible oil spots under the vehicle.
    * Solution: Replace the leaking gasket or seal.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+ depending on the source of the leak and the mechanic.
    * Recalls: None Found

**3.0L Turbocharged Inline-6 (T6)**

* **Oil Leaks**
    * Description/Symptoms: Similar to the 3.2L, oil leaks can occur from the valve cover gasket, turbocharger lines, or rear main seal. May cause oil consumption, low oil pressure, and visible oil leaks.
    * Solution: Replace the leaking gasket or seal. Address any turbocharger-related oil leaks with appropriate repair or replacement.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1200+ depending on the source of the leak and if turbocharger work is needed.
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Turbocharger Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: Whining noises from the turbocharger, loss of power, and potential boost leaks.
    * Solution: Diagnose the turbocharger for leaks or damage. Repair or replace as necessary.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2000+ depending on the extent of the damage.
    * Recalls: None Found

**2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4**

* **Oil Consumption**
    * Description/Symptoms: Excessive oil consumption that requires the driver to add oil between services.
    * Solution: Diagnose the cause. Possible issues include worn piston rings, valve stem seals, or PCV system issues. Repair or replace components as needed.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $2000+ depending on the source of the leak and the mechanic.
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**All Engines - 6-Speed Automatic (Aisin Warner TF-80SC)**

* **Harsh Shifting/Slipping**
    * Description/Symptoms: Rough or delayed shifts, transmission slipping, or failure to shift into certain gears. Can be more pronounced when the transmission is cold.
    * Solution: Often a fluid change is recommended first. If the issue persists, valve body repair or replacement, or a complete transmission rebuild or replacement may be necessary.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 (fluid change) - $4000+ (transmission replacement).
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Worn Suspension Components**
    * Description/Symptoms: Clunking or rattling noises from the suspension, particularly over bumps. Poor handling, uneven tire wear, and bouncy ride.
    * Solution: Inspect and replace worn shocks, struts, control arms, ball joints, and bushings.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1500+ depending on which components need replacing.
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Failed Power Tailgate Struts**
    * Description/Symptoms: The power tailgate fails to open or close properly, or makes unusual noises during operation.
    * Solution: Replace the power tailgate struts.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800+ depending on parts and labor.
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Central Electronic Module (CEM) Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: Intermittent electrical issues, including problems with lights, wipers, power windows, or starting. Can be caused by water intrusion.
    * Solution: Diagnose the CEM for corrosion or damage. Clean, repair, or replace the CEM. Relocate or protect the CEM from water intrusion.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1500+ depending on the severity of the issue and whether the CEM can be repaired or needs to be replaced.
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Navigation System Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: The navigation system freezes, reboots, or displays incorrect information.
    * Solution: Try updating the software. If that doesn't work, the navigation module may need to be replaced.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **No Issues Found**

**Interior**

* **Sunroof Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: Sunroof sticking, failing to open or close properly, or making excessive noise.
    * Solution: Clean and lubricate the sunroof tracks. If the problem persists, the sunroof motor or mechanism may need to be repaired or replaced.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $800+ depending on the complexity of the repair.
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2014 Volvo XC70 has some common issues that potential buyers should be aware of. Oil leaks are fairly prevalent across all engine options. The 6-speed automatic transmission can suffer from harsh shifting or slipping issues, which can be costly to address. Suspension wear is typical for a vehicle of this age. Electrical issues, particularly with the CEM, can also occur. While some of these repairs can be relatively inexpensive, others can be quite costly. Overall, a thorough inspection is recommended before purchasing a 2014 Volvo XC70, and regular maintenance is crucial to prevent and mitigate these common problems.
