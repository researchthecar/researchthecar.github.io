The 2008 Volvo XC70 is a versatile wagon known for its safety, comfort, and all-weather capability. It came standard with a 3.2-liter inline-6 engine producing 235 horsepower and 236 lb-ft of torque. All models were equipped with a six-speed automatic transmission. Owners appreciate its comfortable ride, spacious interior, and robust build quality. Common complaints include higher maintenance costs compared to domestic vehicles and some reported electrical gremlins.

**Engine**

**3.2L Inline-6**

* **Excessive Oil Consumption**
    * Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported higher-than-normal oil consumption, requiring frequent top-offs between oil changes. Symptoms include low oil level warnings, blue exhaust smoke (in severe cases), and the need to add oil more frequently than expected.
    * Solution: Monitor oil levels regularly. The consumption can sometimes be mitigated with a switch to a higher-viscosity oil or by addressing any leaks. In more severe cases, piston ring replacement may be necessary.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1500 - $4000+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Thermostat Housing Cracking/Leaking**
    * Description/Symptoms: The thermostat housing can crack over time, leading to coolant leaks. Symptoms include coolant loss, overheating, and visible coolant pooling near the thermostat housing.
    * Solution: Replace the thermostat housing and gasket.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**Aisin Warner AWF21 / AW36 (6-Speed Automatic)**

* **Valve Body Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported harsh or delayed shifting, slipping between gears, or complete transmission failure. This is often attributed to issues with the valve body within the transmission.
    * Solution: Replacing or rebuilding the valve body. In severe cases, a complete transmission replacement may be necessary.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $4000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Rear Self-Leveling Suspension (Nivomat) Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The Nivomat self-leveling shocks can fail, leading to a sagging rear end, a bouncy ride, and compromised handling, especially when carrying loads or towing.
    * Solution: Replacing the Nivomat shocks with new units or converting to a conventional shock absorber setup.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1500+ (for Nivomat replacement)
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Front Lower Control Arm Bushing Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Premature wear and tear of the front lower control arm bushings, especially the rear bushing, can cause a clunking noise when going over bumps. Symptoms include loose steering, and vibrations.
    * Solution: Replacement of the lower control arms
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300-$700
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Sunroof Drain Clogs**
    * Description/Symptoms: The sunroof drains can become clogged with debris, leading to water leaks into the cabin. This can cause damp carpets, mildew smells, and electrical issues.
    * Solution: Clearing the sunroof drains with compressed air or a flexible wire. Regular maintenance is recommended.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $200 (depending on severity and whether professional cleaning is required)
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Central Electronic Module (CEM) Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: The CEM can experience failures leading to various electrical malfunctions, including issues with power windows, door locks, lighting, and starting.
    * Solution: Diagnose and repair the CEM or replace it. Programming is required after replacement.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1500+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Tail Light Bulb Socket Corrosion**
    * Description/Symptoms: Corrosion in the tail light bulb sockets can cause intermittent lighting issues or complete failure of the tail lights.
    * Solution: Cleaning or replacing the corroded sockets and bulbs.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $200
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Power Tailgate Strut Failure (if equipped)**
    * Description/Symptoms: The power tailgate struts can weaken over time, causing the tailgate to open slowly, not open fully, or fail to stay open.
    * Solution: Replacing the power tailgate struts.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Driver Information Module (DIM) / Instrument Cluster Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: The instrument cluster can experience pixelation, flickering, or complete failure, resulting in loss of important information like speedometer readings.
    * Solution: Repair or replacement of the instrument cluster.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2008 Volvo XC70 presents a mix of potential issues, with the most concerning being related to the transmission, electrical system (CEM), and suspension. Engine-related problems like oil consumption and thermostat housing leaks can also occur. The severity and cost of repairs vary widely. Valve body and CEM issues are the most expensive. Suspension repairs like Nivomat shocks can be costly, but alternatives exist. Regular maintenance, particularly addressing potential leaks, clogged sunroof drains, and electrical connector corrosion, can help prevent some of these problems. While some repairs can be costly, overall, the 2008 XC70 can be a reliable vehicle if properly maintained and potential issues are addressed promptly.
