The 2007 Volvo XC70 is a wagon known for its safety, comfort, and all-weather capability. It offers a blend of practicality and a touch of luxury. The base engine is a 2.5L turbocharged inline-5 producing 208 horsepower and 236 lb-ft of torque. This engine is paired with a 5-speed automatic transmission. While owners appreciate the XC70's reliability and spaciousness, common complaints include issues with the electronic throttle module and some suspension components.

**Engine**

**2.5L Turbocharged Inline-5**

*   **Electronic Throttle Module (ETM) Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Erratic idling, loss of power, stalling, "Reduced Engine Performance" message displayed.
    *   Solution: Replace the ETM with an updated unit, or send the module for repair and calibration.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $1000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **PCV System Clogging**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Whistling noise from the engine, rough running, oil leaks from seals (rear main seal, camshaft seals), excessive crankcase pressure.
    *   Solution: Replace the PCV system components, including the oil trap, hoses, and intake manifold gasket.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $700+
    *   Recalls: None Found

* **Turbocharger Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, whistling sound during acceleration, excessive oil consumption, blue smoke from the exhaust.
    * Solution: Replace the turbocharger unit.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1200 - $2500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**Aisin AW55-50/51SN 5-Speed Automatic**

*   **Valve Body Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Harsh or delayed shifting, slipping between gears, erratic shifting behavior.
    *   Solution: Replace or rebuild the valve body.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Torque Converter Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Shuddering at low speeds, especially during acceleration, stalling, poor fuel economy.
    *   Solution: Replace the torque converter.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $700 - $1500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Rear Self-Leveling Suspension (Nivomat) Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Sagging rear suspension, poor ride quality, bottoming out over bumps.
    *   Solution: Replace the Nivomat shocks. Can be expensive.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800-$2000+ per pair
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Front Lower Control Arm Bushing Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Clunking noise from the front suspension, especially over bumps, vague steering.
    *   Solution: Replace the front lower control arms or just the bushings.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Driver's Side Door Module (DDM) Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Windows not working, door locks not working, mirror adjustments not working.
    *   Solution: Replace the DDM.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Sunroof Drain Clogs**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Water leaks into the cabin, wet carpets.
    *   Solution: Clean out the sunroof drains.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+ if professionally cleaned, or DIY.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Rear Wiper Motor Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rear wiper not working.
    *   Solution: Replace the rear wiper motor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $350+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Climate Control Fan Motor Resistor Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Blower fan only works on certain speeds or not at all.
    *   Solution: Replace the blower fan motor resistor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100-$300+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**
The 2007 Volvo XC70 has several known issues. Engine issues, particularly with the ETM and PCV system, can be relatively common and costly to address. Transmission valve body issues are also a potential concern. Suspension problems, particularly with the Nivomat system, can also be expensive. Electrical issues, such as DDM failure, are also fairly common. Overall, while the XC70 is a generally reliable vehicle, potential buyers should be aware of these common problems and factor in the potential repair costs.
