The 2004 Volvo VNM is a heavy-duty truck primarily used for commercial transportation. Engine options included the Volvo D12 and Cummins ISX. The Volvo D12 offered around 465 horsepower and 1650 lb-ft of torque. Owners appreciate the VNM for its durability and spacious cab, while common complaints revolve around maintenance costs and some specific component failures due to the vehicle's age and heavy usage.

**Engine**

**Volvo D12**
* **EGR Cooler Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Overheating, coolant loss, white smoke from exhaust, reduced engine power.
    * Solution: Replacing the EGR cooler.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Fuel Injector Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: Rough idling, misfires, decreased fuel economy, black smoke from exhaust.
    * Solution: Replacing faulty fuel injectors.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+ per injector depending on location.
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Turbocharger Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, excessive black smoke, whining or screeching noises from the turbo.
    * Solution: Replacing or rebuilding the turbocharger.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Cummins ISX**

* **Injector Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Poor fuel economy, rough running.
    * Solution: Replacing the fuel injectors
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+ per injector depending on location.
    * Recalls: None Found

* **EGR Valve Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: Engine light illuminating, rough idle, decreased power, increased emissions.
    * Solution: Replacing the EGR valve.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,200+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**Eaton Fuller Transmissions (Specific Models Vary)**

* **Shifter Problems**
    * Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting, grinding gears, popping out of gear.
    * Solution: Replacing or rebuilding the shifter assembly, checking linkage.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Clutch Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Slipping clutch, difficulty engaging gears, clutch pedal issues.
    * Solution: Replacing the clutch disc, pressure plate, and throwout bearing.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,200 - $2,500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Air Suspension Problems**
    * Description/Symptoms: Sagging suspension, uneven ride height, air leaks, compressor not working.
    * Solution: Repairing or replacing air bags, ride height sensors, air lines, or the air compressor.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $2,000+ per component.
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Worn Shocks and Struts**
    * Description/Symptoms: Bouncy ride, poor handling, excessive tire wear.
    * Solution: Replacing shocks and struts.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+ per axle.
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Wiring Harness Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: Intermittent electrical problems, lights flickering, gauge malfunctions, starting problems.
    * Solution: Inspecting and repairing damaged wiring, replacing sections of the harness.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+, depending on severity
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Sensor Failures**
    * Description/Symptoms: Engine lights on, performance issues.
    * Solution: Replacing faulty sensors (ABS, wheel speed, etc.)
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+ per sensor.
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Headlight Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: Headlight becoming foggy, yellow, or not producing proper beam.
    * Solution: Replacing headlights.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $600+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **HVAC Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: Inconsistent heating or cooling, blower motor failure.
    * Solution: Repairing or replacing the blower motor, blend door actuator, or other HVAC components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2004 Volvo VNM, while generally reliable for its intended purpose, can experience issues common to heavy-duty trucks, especially given its age. Engine and transmission issues can be costly, ranging from a few hundred to several thousand dollars depending on the specific problem and the severity of the damage. Electrical and suspension problems are also fairly common. Potential buyers should carefully inspect the vehicle's maintenance history and condition, paying close attention to the engine, transmission, and suspension components.
