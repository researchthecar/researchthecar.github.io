The 2015 Volvo VNM is a heavy-duty truck primarily used for long-haul transportation. Engine options include the Volvo D11, D13, and D16, offering a range of horsepower and torque depending on the specific configuration. The Volvo D13 engine, a popular choice, typically produces around 425-500 horsepower and 1550-1850 lb-ft of torque. Drivers often appreciate the VNM for its comfortable cab, fuel efficiency, and smooth ride. Common complaints often revolve around the complexity of the engine aftertreatment systems and the cost of replacement parts and repairs.

**Engine**

**Volvo D13**

*   **EGR Cooler Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaks, white smoke from the exhaust, and potential engine overheating.
    *   Solution: Replace the EGR cooler.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1500 - $3000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Diesel Particulate Filter (DPF) Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Frequent regeneration cycles, reduced engine power, and illuminated dashboard warning lights. Can lead to engine damage if not addressed.
    *   Solution: Cleaning or replacement of the DPF filter. Addressing underlying causes like faulty sensors or injectors.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $4000+ (depending on cleaning vs. replacement and diagnosing the underlying cause)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Fuel Injector Problems**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rough idling, misfires, decreased fuel efficiency, and black smoke from the exhaust.
    *   Solution: Replacing faulty fuel injectors.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800+ per injector (parts and labor).
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Volvo D11**

*   **Turbocharger Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, excessive black smoke, and unusual noises from the turbocharger.
    *   Solution: Turbocharger replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $2000 - $4000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Volvo D16**

*   **High Oil Consumption**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Needing to add oil frequently, blue smoke from the exhaust.
    *   Solution: Could indicate wear on piston rings or valve seals; engine rebuild might be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $5000 - $15,000+ (depending on the severity and repair needed)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**Volvo I-Shift Automated Manual Transmission**

*   **Shifting Problems**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Harsh shifting, failure to shift, transmission slipping, and error codes.
    *   Solution: Calibration or repair/replacement of the transmission control module (TCM), solenoids, or clutch.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $5000+ depending on the issue
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Air Suspension Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Sagging suspension, uneven ride height, air leaks, and failure of the air compressor.
    *   Solution: Replacing air bags, leveling valves, or the air compressor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1500+ per component
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Sensor Failures**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Various warning lights illuminated, including check engine light, ABS light, etc.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis of the specific sensor and replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+ per sensor (parts and labor)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Wiring Harness Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Intermittent electrical problems, such as lights flickering, sensors malfunctioning, and starting issues.
    *   Solution: Repairing or replacing damaged sections of the wiring harness.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1000+ depending on the complexity
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Headlight/Taillight Failures**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Bulbs burning out frequently, or complete failure of the headlight/taillight assembly.
    *   Solution: Bulb replacement or complete assembly replacement
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $500
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **HVAC Problems**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Inconsistent heating or cooling, failure of the blower motor, or issues with the climate control system.
    *   Solution: Repair or replacement of the blower motor, blend door actuator, or HVAC control module.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2015 Volvo VNM can suffer from a variety of issues, with engine and transmission problems being the most costly to address. EGR cooler failures, DPF clogging, and fuel injector problems are common on the D13, while the D11 and D16 have their own sets of problems. Transmission issues related to the I-Shift automated manual transmission can also be expensive. Suspension and electrical issues contribute to the overall maintenance burden. While some issues may be relatively minor and inexpensive to fix, others can be quite severe and require extensive repairs, potentially costing thousands of dollars. Prospective buyers should thoroughly inspect the vehicle's maintenance history and consider a pre-purchase inspection by a qualified mechanic.
