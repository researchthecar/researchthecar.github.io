The 2012 Volvo VNM is a versatile Class 8 truck popular with owner-operators and fleets alike, known for its comfortable cab, relatively fuel-efficient engines, and robust build. Engine options for the 2012 VNM primarily include the Volvo D11, D13, and Cummins ISX15. The Volvo D13, a popular choice, produces between 405 and 500 horsepower and 1450-1850 lb-ft of torque. Transmission choices generally include Volvo's I-Shift automated manual transmission and Eaton Fuller manual transmissions. Some common praises for the VNM include its comfortable ride and driver-friendly features. Common criticisms include its complexity of the electrical system and the cost of replacement parts.

**Engine**

**Volvo D13**

*   **EGR Cooler Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaks, white smoke from the exhaust, engine overheating, loss of power. Can lead to severe engine damage if not addressed.
    *   Solution: Replace the EGR cooler. Inspect and clean EGR valve.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Injector Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rough running engine, misfires, decreased fuel economy, excessive smoke, difficulty starting.
    *   Solution: Replace faulty injectors. Recalibrate the ECM.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $2,500+ (depending on the number of injectors replaced)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Cummins ISX15**

*   **EGR Cooler Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaks, white smoke from the exhaust, engine overheating, loss of power. Can lead to severe engine damage if not addressed.
    *   Solution: Replace the EGR cooler. Inspect and clean EGR valve.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Turbocharger Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, excessive black smoke, unusual noises from the turbo, increased oil consumption.
    *   Solution: Replace the turbocharger. Check oil supply lines.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**Volvo I-Shift (Automated Manual)**

*   **Shift Actuator Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears, erratic shifting, transmission getting stuck in gear, error codes displayed on the dash.
    *   Solution: Replace the faulty shift actuator. Recalibrate the transmission control module (TCM).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,800 - $3,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Clutch Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Slipping clutch, difficulty engaging gears, shuddering during takeoff, unusual noises from the transmission.
    *   Solution: Replace the clutch assembly. Inspect and potentially replace the flywheel.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Eaton Fuller Manual Transmission**

*   **Shift Fork Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting into certain gears, grinding noises during shifting, gear lockout.
    *   Solution: Replace the worn or broken shift fork. Inspect related components (sliders, synchronizers).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $2,000+ (depending on the extent of the damage)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Air Spring Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Sagging suspension, uneven ride height, rough ride, air leaks, hissing sounds from the suspension.
    *   Solution: Replace the leaking or damaged air spring. Check air lines and fittings for leaks.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+ per air spring
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Shock Absorber Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Bouncing, excessive body roll, poor handling, uneven tire wear.
    *   Solution: Replace worn shock absorbers.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600+ per shock
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Wiring Harness Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Intermittent electrical problems, dashboard warning lights, sensor failures, difficulty starting, communication issues between modules. Often caused by chafing or corrosion.
    *   Solution: Repair or replace damaged wiring harness sections. Properly route and secure wiring.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+ (depending on the severity and location of the damage)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Sensor Failures (EGR, DPF, NOx)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Check engine light, reduced engine performance, increased emissions, error codes related to specific sensors.
    *   Solution: Replace faulty sensors. Check wiring and connections.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600+ per sensor
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Hood Latch Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Hood does not securely latch, difficulty opening or closing the hood, hood rattling.
    *   Solution: Replace the hood latch assembly. Adjust hood alignment.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **HVAC Blower Motor Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: No air flow from the vents, weak air flow, unusual noises from the blower motor area.
    *   Solution: Replace the blower motor. Check the blower motor resistor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Seat Suspension Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Air seat deflates, seat not holding position, uncomfortable ride, seat rocking or squeaking.
    *   Solution: Replace the seat suspension components, repair leaks in the air lines, replace seat bladder.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2012 Volvo VNM, while generally a reliable truck, experiences its share of common issues. Engine problems, particularly with the EGR system and fuel injectors, can be costly. Transmission issues, especially concerning the I-Shift's shift actuators, are another area of concern. Electrical problems, often stemming from wiring harness issues, can be frustrating to diagnose and repair. Suspension and interior components also have their common failure points. Overall, potential buyers should be aware of these potential issues and factor in the cost of maintenance and repairs when considering this model. The severity and cost of these issues vary, but addressing them promptly is crucial to maintaining the truck's reliability and longevity.
