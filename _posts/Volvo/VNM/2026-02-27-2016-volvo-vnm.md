# 2016 Volvo VNM Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2016 Volvo VNM is a versatile medium-to-heavy-duty commercial truck engineered primarily for regional haul and vocational applications. Buyers considering a used model should evaluate the condition of its heavy-duty diesel engines and commercial transmissions, as well as auxiliary cab and chassis systems. While commercial vehicles experience heavy operational demands, reviewing specific powertrain configurations helps ensure long-term dependability on the road.

---

## Engine

### Volvo D11 Diesel Engine

The Volvo D11 is an 11-liter inline-6 diesel engine offering strong torque ratings suited for regional hauling. It provides a balanced combination of fuel efficiency and heavy-duty reliability for commercial fleets.

### Volvo D13 Diesel Engine

The Volvo D13 is a 13-liter inline-6 diesel engine delivering higher horsepower and torque outputs for demanding workloads. It is widely utilized in heavy-duty commercial applications with robust long-term performance.

## Transmission

### Volvo I-Shift Automated Manual Transmission

The Volvo I-Shift is an advanced automated manual transmission designed to optimize fuel economy and shift smoothness. It is a popular choice for reducing driver fatigue in commercial regional operations.

### Eaton Fuller Manual Transmission

Eaton Fuller manual transmissions offer traditional gear selection control and proven mechanical durability for heavy-duty vocational uses. They remain a dependable option for operators preferring direct mechanical engagement.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V536000: Steering](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volvo&model=VNM&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** The castellated nut on the steer axles may not be properly torqued, allowing the tie rod to loosen. If the tie rod loosens, it may disconnect from the steering knuckle, causing a complete loss of steering, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Volvo will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the torque of the castellated nut and tie rod, tightening it as necessary. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V928000: Service Brakes, Air](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volvo&model=VNM&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** If the brake system becomes contaminated, braking performance could be affected, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Volvo will notify owners, and dealers will reprogram the vehicle control module and replace the air dryer filter, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 15V274000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Engine](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volvo&model=VNM&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** Excessive engine crankcase pressures may cause the 90-degree elbow of the vent tube assembly to detach from the crankcase ventilation breather, possibly allowing engine oil to come into contact with hot surfaces. If engine oil comes into contact with a hot surface, there could be an increased risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Volvo will notify owners, and dealers will secure the ends of the 90-degree elbow. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V095000: Suspension:Front:Control Arm:Upper Ball Joint](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volvo&model=VNM&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** Failure of the ball joint may cause separation of the socket and a complete loss of steering, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Volvo will notify owners, and dealers will replace the drag links, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V097000: Steering](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volvo&model=VNM&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** Also, the bolt connecting the upper steering shaft to the lower steering shaft may not have been properly tightened. Separation of the steering shaft will result in complete loss of steering which may lead to a vehicle crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.
