**Introduction:**

The 2018 Volvo VNM is a versatile heavy-duty truck known for its durability and efficiency. It's offered with a variety of engine and transmission options, each providing a balance of power and fuel economy. Owners appreciate the VNM's comfortable cab, advanced safety features, and reliable performance.

**Engine**

**D13 425HP (EPA10)**
- **Low Power Output:**
   - Description/Symptoms: The engine lacks power, resulting in slow acceleration and reduced hauling capacity.
   - Solution: Replacement of fuel injectors; software updates
   - Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
   - Recalls: None Found

**D13 455HP (EPA10)**
- **No Issues Found**

**Transmission**

**I-Shift 12-Speed**
- **Transmission Failure:**
   - Description/Symptoms: The transmission becomes unresponsive, causing the vehicle to stall or lose power.
   - Solution: Replacement of transmission control unit
   - Estimated Cost to Fix: $5,000 - $7,000+
   - Recalls: NHTSA Campaign Number: 19V168000

**Suspension**

- **Front Axle Wear:**
   - Description/Symptoms: Excessive wear on the front axle components, leading to decreased handling and premature tire wear.
   - Solution: Replacement of worn parts (bushings, bearings, ball joints)
   - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+ per axle
   - Recalls: None Found

- **Rear Axle Seal Leaks:**
   - Description/Symptoms: Leaking rear axle seals allow gear oil to escape, reducing performance and potentially causing damage.
   - Solution: Replacement of faulty seals
   - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500 per axle
   - Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

- **Battery Discharge:**
   - Description/Symptoms: The vehicle's battery drains quickly, even when parked.
   - Solution: Troubleshooting of electrical system; replacement of faulty components (alternator, starter)
   - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+
   - Recalls: None Found

- **Headlight Malfunction:**
   - Description/Symptoms: Headlights fail to illuminate or dim prematurely.
   - Solution: Replacement of faulty wiring harness or headlight assembly
   - Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500 per headlight
   - Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

- **Windshield Cracking:**
   - Description/Symptoms: Cracks develop in the windshield, reducing visibility and compromising structural integrity.
   - Solution: Replacement of windshield
   - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
   - Recalls: None Found

- **Door Lock Failure:**
   - Description/Symptoms: Door locks become difficult to operate or fail to function entirely.
   - Solution: Replacement of door lock actuators
   - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400 per door
   - Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

- **Dashboard Lighting Issues:**
   - Description/Symptoms: Dimming or flickering of dashboard lights, affecting visibility of vital information.
   - Solution: Replacement of faulty light bulbs
   - Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300
   - Recalls: None Found

- **HVAC Malfunction:**
   - Description/Symptoms: Heating or air conditioning systems fail to function properly, affecting comfort and visibility.
   - Solution: Troubleshooting of electrical system; replacement of faulty components (blower motor, compressor)
   - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+
   - Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

The 2018 Volvo VNM is a reliable and capable truck, but it's not without its issues. The most severe issues reported include transmission failure and low engine power output, which can be costly to repair. Other issues, such as battery discharge and axle wear, are relatively minor and less expensive to fix. Overall, the VNM's issues are manageable and do not detract significantly from its overall value and performance.
