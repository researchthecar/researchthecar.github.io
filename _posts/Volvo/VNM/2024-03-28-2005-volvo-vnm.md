**Introduction**

The 2005 Volvo VNM is a heavy-duty truck designed for long-haul transportation. It is powered by a Volvo D12 diesel engine with 425 horsepower and 1,550 lb-ft of torque. The VNM comes standard with a 10-speed manual transmission, but an optional 12-speed automated manual transmission is also available.

People like the VNM for its powerful engine, spacious cab, and comfortable ride. However, some owners have reported issues with the engine, transmission, suspension, electrical system, exterior, and interior.

**Engine**

**D12 425hp 1550lb-ft**

* **Blown head gasket**
    * Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaking from the head gasket, engine overheating, white smoke from the exhaust
    * Solution: Replace the head gasket
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Turbocharger failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, black smoke from the exhaust, loud whining noise from the turbocharger
    * Solution: Replace the turbocharger
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**10-Speed Manual**

* **Transmission grinding**
    * Description/Symptoms: Grinding noise when shifting gears, difficulty shifting gears
    * Solution: Replace the transmission gears or synchronizers
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Transmission fluid leak**
    * Description/Symptoms: Transmission fluid leaking from the transmission pan or seals, low transmission fluid level
    * Solution: Replace the leaking seals or transmission pan
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**12-Speed Automated Manual**

* **Shifting problems**
    * Description/Symptoms: Transmission not shifting smoothly, delayed shifts, hard shifts
    * Solution: Update the transmission software or replace the transmission control module
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Rear suspension springs broken**
    * Description/Symptoms: Sagging rear end of the truck, rough ride, uneven tire wear
    * Solution: Replace the rear suspension springs
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Front suspension bushings worn**
    * Description/Symptoms: Squeaking or rattling noise from the front suspension, uneven tire wear
    * Solution: Replace the worn suspension bushings
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Alternator failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Battery not charging, dim headlights, electrical components not working
    * Solution: Replace the alternator
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Starter motor failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Engine not starting, clicking noise when trying to start the engine
    * Solution: Replace the starter motor
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $700+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Rust on the frame**
    * Description/Symptoms: Rust spots on the frame, especially in areas with salt or moisture
    * Solution: Sandblast and repaint the frame, or replace the frame if necessary
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $5,000+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Headlight failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: One or both headlights not working
    * Solution: Replace the headlight bulb or assembly
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Dashboard lights not working**
    * Description/Symptoms: One or more dashboard lights not working, making it difficult to see important information
    * Solution: Replace the faulty bulbs or repair the wiring
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Climate control system not working**
    * Description/Symptoms: Heater or air conditioner not blowing air, temperature not regulating properly
    * Solution: Repair or replace the climate control system components, such as the blower motor, compressor, or control module
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2005 Volvo VNM is a generally reliable truck, but it is not without its issues. The most common problems reported by owners include blown head gaskets, turbocharger failures, transmission grinding, and suspension problems. These issues can be costly to fix, and some may require extensive repairs. However, with proper maintenance and care, the VNM can be a dependable workhorse for many years.
