The 2007 Volvo VNM is a Class 8 semi-truck designed for regional and vocational hauling. Engine options for the 2007 VNM typically included Volvo's D11, D13, and Cummins ISX series engines, paired with manual or automated manual transmissions. The Volvo D13 engine, for example, produced around 400-500 horsepower and 1450-1850 lb-ft of torque depending on the specific configuration. Owners often appreciate the VNM for its comfortable cab, maneuverability, and fuel efficiency. Common complaints include concerns about the reliability of certain components and the cost of repairs, particularly for Volvo-specific parts.

**Engine**

**Volvo D13**

*   **EGR Cooler Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Overheating, coolant loss, white smoke from exhaust, reduced engine power.
    *   Solution: Replace the EGR cooler.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Fuel Injector Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rough idling, misfires, decreased fuel economy, black smoke from exhaust.
    *   Solution: Replace faulty fuel injectors.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+ per injector (depending on whether replacing individually or as a set)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Cummins ISX**

*   **Camshaft Wear**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, increased fuel consumption, unusual engine noise.
    *   Solution: Replace the camshaft and related components (lifters, bearings).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $3,000 - $7,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Turbocharger Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, excessive black smoke, whistling noise from turbo, increased oil consumption.
    *   Solution: Replace or rebuild the turbocharger.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $5,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**Volvo I-Shift (Automated Manual)**

*   **Shift Actuator Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears, transmission stuck in gear, error codes on the dashboard.
    *   Solution: Replace the shift actuator.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $4,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Clutch Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Slipping clutch, difficulty engaging gears, shuddering during take-off.
    *   Solution: Replace the clutch.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $5,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Manual Transmission (Eaton Fuller)**

*   **Gear Slippage**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Transmission pops out of gear, especially under load, grinding noises.
    *   Solution: Rebuild or replace the transmission, focusing on worn synchronizers and gears.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $3,000 - $8,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Air Spring Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Sagging suspension, uneven ride height, air leaks.
    *   Solution: Replace the air springs.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+ per air spring (depending on axle)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Shock Absorber Wear**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Bouncy ride, poor handling, excessive body roll.
    *   Solution: Replace the shock absorbers.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+ per shock absorber (depending on axle)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Wiring Harness Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Intermittent electrical problems, lights malfunctioning, sensor failures, engine stalling.
    *   Solution: Repair or replace damaged wiring harnesses.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $2,000+ (depending on the extent of the damage)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Sensor Failures (ABS, Wheel Speed, etc.)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Warning lights on the dashboard, ABS malfunctioning, inaccurate speedometer.
    *   Solution: Replace faulty sensors.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+ per sensor
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Hood Latch Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Hood not latching securely, hood flying open while driving.
    *   Solution: Replace the hood latch mechanism.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Mirror Motor Malfunction**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Mirrors not adjusting properly, motor noise without movement.
    *   Solution: Replace the mirror motor or entire mirror assembly.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $500+ per mirror
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **HVAC Blower Motor Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: No air blowing from vents, weak airflow, noisy blower motor.
    *   Solution: Replace the blower motor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Gauge Cluster Problems**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Inaccurate readings, flickering lights, complete failure of the gauge cluster.
    *   Solution: Repair or replace the gauge cluster.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**
The 2007 Volvo VNM, while appreciated for its driver comfort and maneuverability, can present several issues that owners should be aware of. Engine and transmission problems can be costly, particularly with the Volvo I-Shift automated manual transmission. Suspension and electrical issues are also common and can lead to significant downtime and repair expenses. The severity of these issues ranges from moderate to severe, with repair costs varying widely based on the specific problem and the availability of aftermarket parts. Potential buyers should carefully inspect the vehicle's maintenance history and consider a pre-purchase inspection to identify any existing or potential problems.
