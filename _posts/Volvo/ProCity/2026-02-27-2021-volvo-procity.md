Owners often praise its robust build, comfortable ride, and advanced safety features. While most components perform as expected, like any vehicle, there are specific areas where minor concerns have been noted by users and technicians. This analysis focuses on documented technical issues beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential problem areas and their associated costs for the 2021 model year.

## Electrical

### Infotainment System
**Occasional Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Screen freezing, unresponsiveness, or temporary loss of connectivity.
- **Solution:** Software updates, system resets, or potential module replacement.
- **Cost:** $150 - $500


### Battery Drain
**Intermittent Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Premature battery depletion, especially when the vehicle is parked for extended periods. May be linked to parasitic draw from certain modules.
- **Solution:** Diagnostic check for parasitic draw, identification of faulty module, and replacement.
- **Cost:** $200 - $700

### Summary Verdict
The 2021 Volvo V60 ProCity demonstrates a high level of reliability, with its powertrain and core mechanical systems performing commendably. The most frequently reported technical concerns are minor electrical issues, primarily related to the infotainment system's occasional glitches and, less commonly, intermittent battery drain. These issues are generally resolvable through software updates or targeted component diagnostics. For owners, proactive maintenance and addressing any early signs of electrical anomalies can ensure continued dependable performance. Overall, the V60 ProCity is a solid choice for those seeking a reliable and well-built vehicle.
