# 2015 Volvo ProCity

The 2015 Volvo ProCity is a compact SUV that offers a unique blend of affordability, style, and practicality. It comes standard with a 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine producing 240 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque. The ProCity is available with either a six-speed automatic or an eight-speed manual transmission.

Despite its impressive specs, the 2015 Volvo ProCity has been plagued by a number of issues, ranging from minor annoyances to more serious mechanical problems. Owners have reported issues with the engine, transmission, suspension, electrical system, exterior, and interior.

## **Engine**

- **2.0L Turbocharged Four-Cylinder Engine**

   - **Intake Manifold Gasket Failure**
     - Description/Symptoms: The intake manifold gasket can fail, causing coolant and oil leaks. This can lead to overheating and engine damage.
     - Solution: Replace the intake manifold gasket.
     - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
     - Recalls: None Found

## **Transmission**

- **Six-Speed Automatic Transmission**

   - **Torque Converter Failure**
     - Description/Symptoms: The torque converter can fail, causing a loss of power and shuddering during gear changes.
     - Solution: Replace the torque converter.
     - Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500
     - Recalls: None Found

- **Eight-Speed Manual Transmission**

   - **Clutch Plate Failure**
     - Description/Symptoms: The clutch plate can wear out prematurely, causing difficulty shifting gears and a loss of power.
     - Solution: Replace the clutch plate.
     - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
     - Recalls: None Found

## **Suspension**

   - **Strut Failure**
     - Description/Symptoms: The struts can fail, causing a loss of ride quality and handling. This can also lead to uneven tire wear.
     - Solution: Replace the struts.
     - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
     - Recalls: None Found

## **Electrical**

   - **Battery Drain**
     - Description/Symptoms: The battery can drain prematurely, causing difficulty starting the vehicle.
     - Solution: Replace the battery or check for a parasitic draw.
     - Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200
     - Recalls: None Found

## **Exterior**

   - **Door Lock Actuator Failure**
     - Description/Symptoms: The door lock actuators can fail, causing the doors to lock or unlock randomly.
     - Solution: Replace the door lock actuators.
     - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
     - Recalls: None Found

## **Interior**

   - **Infotainment System Glitches**
     - Description/Symptoms: The infotainment system can experience glitches, such as freezing or losing functionality.
     - Solution: Reset the infotainment system or update the software.
     - Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 - $200
     - Recalls: None Found

## **Summary**

The 2015 Volvo ProCity has a number of issues that can be costly to fix. Some of the more serious problems, such as engine and transmission failures, can lead to dangerous situations on the road. While the ProCity offers a lot of value for the price, buyers should be aware of the potential problems before making a purchase.
