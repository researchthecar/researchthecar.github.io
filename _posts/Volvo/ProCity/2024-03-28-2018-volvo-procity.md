**Introduction:**

The 2018 Volvo ProCity is a small, premium SUV offered with two engine options: a 2.0-liter turbocharged inline-4 and a 2.0-liter turbocharged inline-4 hybrid. Both engines are paired with an 8-speed automatic transmission. The ProCity is praised for its stylish design, comfortable interior, and advanced safety features. However, some owners have reported various issues with the vehicle.

**Engine**

**2.0-liter Turbocharged Inline-4**

**Leaking Coolant Hose**
Description/Symptoms: A crack or leak develops in the coolant hose, causing coolant to leak out. The leak may be visible as a puddle under the vehicle or as a decrease in coolant levels.
Solution: Replace the coolant hose.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
Recalls: None Found

**2.0-liter Turbocharged Inline-4 Hybrid**

**No Issues Found**

**Transmission**

**8-Speed Automatic**

**Shifting Hesitation**
Description/Symptoms: The transmission hesitates or delays when shifting gears.
Solution: Software update or transmission fluid flush and filter replacement.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+
Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

**Front Suspension Knock**
Description/Symptoms: A knocking or clunking noise is heard from the front suspension when driving over uneven surfaces.
Solution: Replace the front lower control arms.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $600 - $800+
Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

**Battery Drain**
Description/Symptoms: The battery drains quickly, even when the vehicle is not in use.
Solution: Replace the battery or diagnose and fix the electrical system.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

**Panoramic Sunroof Leaking**
Description/Symptoms: Water leaks into the cabin through the panoramic sunroof during heavy rain.
Solution: Replace or repair the sunroof seals.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

**Infotainment System Glitches**
Description/Symptoms: The infotainment system experiences freezes, glitches, or unresponsive touchscreens.
Solution: Software update or replacement of the infotainment unit.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

While the 2018 Volvo ProCity offers a comfortable and feature-packed experience, owners have reported a range of issues with the vehicle. The most prevalent issues include coolant hose leaks, transmission shifting hesitations, and sunroof leaks. The severity and cost of these issues can vary, but some require significant repairs that may be expensive.
