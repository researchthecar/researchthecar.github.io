The 2016 Volvo S60 Cross Country is a unique vehicle, blending sedan comfort with added ground clearance and all-wheel drive for light off-road capability. It was offered with the T5 2.5-liter turbocharged inline-5 engine, producing 250 horsepower and 266 lb-ft of torque, paired exclusively with a 6-speed automatic transmission. Drivers often appreciate its comfortable ride, distinctive styling, and standard safety features. However, some common criticisms include its limited rear-seat space and the infotainment system's complexity.

**Engine**

**2.5L Turbocharged Inline-5 (T5)**
* **Oil Leaks from Rear Main Seal**
    * Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks observed near the bell housing where the engine and transmission meet. May also notice burning oil smell.
    * Solution: Replacement of the rear main seal.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **PCV System Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Whistling noise coming from the engine, rough idle, and potential oil leaks due to increased crankcase pressure. Check engine light may illuminate.
    * Solution: Replacement of the PCV valve or the entire PCV system.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**6-Speed Automatic**
* **Rough Shifting**
    * Description/Symptoms: Harsh or jerky shifts between gears, especially at lower speeds. May also experience slipping or delayed engagement.
    * Solution: Fluid flush and inspection, or in some cases replacement of the valve body.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200+ for fluid flush and inspection, $800 - $1500+ for valve body replacement.
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Torque Converter Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Shuddering or vibration at low speeds, especially when accelerating. Check engine light may illuminate.
    * Solution: Torque converter replacement.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Rear Shock Absorber Leaks**
    * Description/Symptoms: Leaking fluid observed on the rear shocks. Bouncy or unstable ride, especially over bumps.
    * Solution: Replacement of the rear shock absorbers.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+ (for both shocks)
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Infotainment System Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: Screen freezing, slow response times, Bluetooth connectivity problems, and navigation errors.
    * Solution: Software updates (if available) or replacement of the infotainment unit.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: Software updates may be free or low cost, replacement unit $500 - $1500+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Battery Drain**
    * Description/Symptoms: Car is difficult to start or won't start, especially after sitting for a few days. The battery may repeatedly die, requiring jump starts.
    * Solution: Test the charging system and parasitic draw. Common causes are a faulty alternator, a failing battery, or an electronic module that's not shutting off properly.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: Battery replacement $150 - $300+, Alternator Replacement $400 - $800+, Diagnosing parasitic drain $100-$300+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Headlight Condensation**
    * Description/Symptoms: Moisture buildup inside the headlight housings, reducing visibility.
    * Solution: Removal of headlights, drying, and resealing. Replacement if damage is severe.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+ for resealing, $500+ per headlight for replacement.
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Sunroof Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: Sunroof failing to open or close properly, getting stuck, or making excessive noise.
    * Solution: Lubrication of the tracks, repair or replacement of the sunroof motor, or repair/replacement of the sunroof assembly.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $200+ for lubrication, $300 - $800+ for motor replacement, $500 - $2000+ for assembly replacement.
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2016 Volvo S60 Cross Country has a mix of potential issues. Engine and transmission problems, while not universally reported, can be costly to address. Electrical and interior issues can be frustrating, while suspension issues are relatively minor. Overall, prospective buyers should have a pre-purchase inspection performed by a qualified mechanic to assess the vehicle's condition and address any potential problems before purchase.
