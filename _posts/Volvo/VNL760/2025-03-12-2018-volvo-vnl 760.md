The 2018 Volvo VNL 760 is a popular long-haul semi-truck known for its fuel efficiency, comfortable cabin, and safety features. Engine options for the 2018 VNL 760 include the Volvo D11, D13, and Cummins X15. The Volvo D13 engine produces between 405 and 500 horsepower and up to 1850 lb-ft of torque, depending on the specific configuration. Transmission options are primarily Volvo's I-Shift automated manual transmission. Drivers often praise the VNL 760 for its spacious sleeper cab, smooth ride, and advanced safety systems. Common complaints include the complexity of the emissions systems, electrical issues, and the cost of replacement parts.

**Engine**

**Volvo D13**

*   **DPF (Diesel Particulate Filter) Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Frequent DPF regeneration cycles, reduced engine power, illuminated check engine light. Can also result in the engine derating.
    *   Solution: DPF cleaning or replacement. Addressing underlying issues causing excessive soot buildup, such as faulty injectors or turbocharger problems.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $5,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **EGR (Exhaust Gas Recirculation) Valve Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rough idling, poor fuel economy, black smoke from exhaust, check engine light.
    *   Solution: EGR valve replacement. Cleaning EGR cooler may also be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $2,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Fuel Injector Problems**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Misfires, rough running, poor fuel economy, white smoke from exhaust, hard starts.
    *   Solution: Fuel injector replacement, often requiring specialized tools and calibration.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+ per injector
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Cummins X15**

*   **DEF (Diesel Exhaust Fluid) System Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Check engine light, DEF level sensor malfunction, DEF pump failure, crystallization in the DEF tank.
    *   Solution: Replace faulty DEF components (sensor, pump, injector). Clean DEF tank.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Turbocharger Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, excessive black smoke, unusual turbocharger noise (whining or screeching).
    *   Solution: Turbocharger replacement. Inspect and address any oil supply issues to the turbo.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**Volvo I-Shift**

*   **Shift Hesitation/Rough Shifting**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Delayed or jerky shifts, particularly between certain gears.
    *   Solution: Software update to the transmission control module (TCM). Clutch adjustment or replacement may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $3,000+ (depending on whether clutch replacement is needed)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Clutch Actuator Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting, inability to engage gears, transmission fault codes.
    *   Solution: Clutch actuator replacement. Inspection of clutch components for wear.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Transmission Control Module (TCM) Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Erratic shifting, transmission fault codes, inability to shift.
    *   Solution: TCM replacement or reprogramming.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $3,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Air Suspension Problems**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Leaking air bags, ride height issues, compressor failure, uneven ride height.
    *   Solution: Air bag replacement, compressor replacement, repair of air lines and fittings.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,500+ per air bag, $500 - $1,000+ for compressor
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Ride Height Sensor Failures**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Incorrect ride height adjustment, suspension malfunction lights.
    *   Solution: Replacement of ride height sensors.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+ per sensor
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Wiring Harness Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Intermittent electrical problems, short circuits, sensor failures, communication errors on the CAN bus. Often caused by chafing or corrosion.
    *   Solution: Inspection and repair of wiring harnesses. Replacement of damaged connectors.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $2,000+ (depending on severity and location of the issue)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Sensor Failures**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Various engine, transmission, and chassis system malfunctions indicated by fault codes. Examples: wheel speed sensors, temperature sensors, pressure sensors.
    *   Solution: Replacement of faulty sensors.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+ per sensor
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Lighting Problems**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Headlight, taillight, or marker light failures. Can be caused by bulb failures, wiring issues, or faulty modules.
    *   Solution: Bulb replacement, wiring repair, module replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $500+ (depending on the component)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Mirror Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Motorized mirror failures, broken mirror glass, loose mounting hardware.
    *   Solution: Mirror assembly replacement, mirror glass replacement, tightening or replacing mounting hardware.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Hood Latch Problems**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Difficulty opening or closing the hood, loose or broken hood latch mechanism.
    *   Solution: Hood latch adjustment or replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **HVAC System Problems**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Inadequate heating or cooling, blower motor failure, refrigerant leaks, blend door actuator issues.
    *   Solution: Refrigerant recharge, blower motor replacement, blend door actuator replacement, leak repair.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $1,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Infotainment System Glitches**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Freezing, slow response, Bluetooth connectivity issues, navigation problems.
    *   Solution: Software update, system reset, module replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $1,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2018 Volvo VNL 760 can experience a range of issues, with engine and transmission problems potentially being the most costly. Electrical issues, while not always expensive to fix individually, can be frequent and time-consuming to diagnose. Suspension and interior problems are also common and can affect driver comfort and safety. Overall, the VNL 760 is a solid truck but requires diligent maintenance to prevent significant downtime. The severity of issues can range from minor inconveniences to major mechanical failures, with repair costs varying accordingly. Regular inspections and preventative maintenance are crucial for minimizing potential problems.
