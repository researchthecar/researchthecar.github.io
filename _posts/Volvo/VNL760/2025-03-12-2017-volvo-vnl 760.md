The 2017 Volvo VNL 760 is a popular semi-truck known for its fuel efficiency, driver comfort, and safety features. Engine options for this year included the Volvo D11, D13, and Cummins ISX15. The Volvo D13 engine, a common choice, produces between 405-500 horsepower and 1450-1850 lb-ft of torque depending on the specific configuration. Transmission options include the Volvo I-Shift automated manual transmission and Eaton Fuller manual transmissions. Drivers often praise the VNL 760 for its spacious cab, smooth ride, and advanced technology, while common complaints revolve around the complexity of the emissions systems and the cost of repairs.

**Engine**

**Volvo D13**

* **EGR Cooler Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The EGR cooler can crack, leading to coolant leaks, overheating, and reduced engine performance. White smoke may be visible from the exhaust.
    * Solution: Replacement of the EGR cooler.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+

* **Diesel Particulate Filter (DPF) Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: The DPF can become clogged with soot, leading to reduced engine power, increased fuel consumption, and frequent regeneration cycles.
    * Solution: DPF cleaning or replacement.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $3,000+

* **Fuel Injector Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Rough idling, misfires, reduced power, and poor fuel economy.
    * Solution: Replacement of faulty fuel injectors.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,500+

**Cummins ISX15**

* **EGR Valve Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The EGR valve can become clogged or fail, leading to reduced engine performance, increased emissions, and potentially triggering the check engine light.
    * Solution: Cleaning or replacement of the EGR valve.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $1,200+

* **Turbocharger Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, excessive smoke from the exhaust, and unusual noises from the turbocharger.
    * Solution: Turbocharger repair or replacement.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+

**Transmission**

**Volvo I-Shift**

* **Shift Hesitation/Erratic Shifting**
    * Description/Symptoms: The transmission may hesitate during shifts, shift erratically, or fail to shift into the correct gear.
    * Solution: Software update or replacement of faulty sensors.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,500+

* **Clutch Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Slipping clutch, difficulty shifting, and abnormal noises from the transmission.
    * Solution: Clutch replacement.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+

**Eaton Fuller Manual Transmission**

* **Difficulty Shifting**
    * Description/Symptoms: Hard to shift into gear, grinding noises during shifting.
    * Solution: Replacement of worn synchronizers or clutch components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $3,000+

**Suspension**

* **Air Suspension Leaks**
    * Description/Symptoms: The air suspension system may develop leaks, causing the vehicle to sag, ride poorly, and potentially damage other components.
    * Solution: Replacement of faulty air bags or air lines.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+

**Electrical**

* **Sensor Failures**
    * Description/Symptoms: Various sensors can fail, leading to warning lights, reduced engine performance, and other issues. Common culprits include wheel speed sensors, coolant temperature sensors, and oil pressure sensors.
    * Solution: Replacement of faulty sensors.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+ per sensor

* **Wiring Harness Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: Corrosion or damage to the wiring harness can cause electrical shorts, intermittent failures, and communication problems between various systems.
    * Solution: Repair or replacement of the affected wiring harness.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $2,000+

**Exterior**

* **Headlight Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: Headlights may have condensation buildup within the lens or flickering lights.
    * Solution: Replacement of headlight assembly or bulbs.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $800+

**Interior**

* **HVAC Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: Faulty climate controls, weak airflow, or failure of the air conditioning system.
    * Solution: Repair or replacement of the HVAC components, such as the compressor, blower motor, or control module.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,500+

* **Gauge Cluster Problems**
    * Description/Symptoms: The gauge cluster may experience intermittent failures, display incorrect readings, or stop working altogether.
    * Solution: Repair or replacement of the gauge cluster.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,200+

**Recalls:**

* **NHTSA Recall Number: 19V-496:** Volvo Trucks North America (Volvo Trucks) is recalling certain 2017-2020 VNL, VNX, and VAH vehicles. The steering gear input shaft may have been improperly heat treated, potentially resulting in the shaft fracturing.
* **NHTSA Recall Number: 20V-231:** Volvo Trucks North America (Volvo Trucks) is recalling certain 2017-2020 VNL, VNX, and VAH vehicles. The steering gear input shaft may have been improperly heat treated, potentially resulting in the shaft fracturing.
* **NHTSA Recall Number: 17V-524:** Volvo Trucks North America (Volvo Trucks) is recalling certain 2017-2018 VNL and VNM vehicles. The bolts that attach the vehicle speed sensor (VSS) to the transmission may have been improperly tightened.

**Summary**

The 2017 Volvo VNL 760, while generally reliable, does have some common issues. Engine and transmission problems, particularly concerning the emissions system and I-Shift transmission, can be costly to repair. Electrical gremlins and suspension issues also contribute to the overall maintenance burden. While some issues are relatively minor, others can lead to significant downtime and expense. It's important to inspect these key areas thoroughly before purchasing a used 2017 VNL 760. The severity of the issues ranges from minor sensor failures to major engine and transmission overhauls, with repair costs ranging from a few hundred dollars to several thousand dollars.
