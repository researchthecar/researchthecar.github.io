# 2019 Volvo VNL 760

### Overview
The 2019 Volvo VNL 760 is a premium Class 8 truck designed for long-haul applications, emphasizing driver comfort, fuel efficiency, and robust performance. Volvo's commitment to quality is generally reflected in the VNL 760's reliability, with many owners reporting satisfaction with its durability and operational uptime. However, like any complex vehicle, specific components can experience issues. This analysis focuses on documented technical reliability concerns beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential problem areas and their associated repair considerations for various powertrain configurations.

---


## Engine

### Volvo D11 (9.4L Diesel)
**EGR Cooler Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Coolant loss, white smoke from exhaust, overheating.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the EGR cooler assembly.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1500

**Turbocharger Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of power, excessive smoke, whistling noises, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Turbocharger replacement.
- **Cost:** $1500 - $3000


### Volvo D13 (12.8L Diesel)
**Fuel Injector Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Rough idling, poor fuel economy, engine misfires, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of faulty fuel injectors.
- **Cost:** $600 - $1200 per injector

**DPF (Diesel Particulate Filter) Clogging**
- **Symptoms:** Reduced engine power, increased fuel consumption, check engine light, regeneration failures.
- **Solution:** Forced regeneration or DPF cleaning/replacement.
- **Cost:** $500 - $2500

## Transmission

### Volvo I-Shift (Automated Manual)
**Clutch Actuator Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Difficulty shifting gears, jerky engagement, transmission fault codes.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the clutch actuator or related sensors.
- **Cost:** $1000 - $2500

**Transmission Control Module (TCM) Errors**
- **Symptoms:** Erratic shifting, inability to select gears, dashboard warnings.
- **Solution:** Software update or TCM replacement.
- **Cost:** $800 - $2000

### Air Suspension (Volvo Active Air Suspension)

### Summary Verdict
The 2019 Volvo VNL 760 demonstrates strong overall reliability, particularly in its core powertrain components when properly maintained. The most frequently cited technical issues tend to revolve around emission control systems (EGR coolers, DPFs) and specific transmission components like the I-Shift's actuator or TCM, especially with higher mileage. Electrical system connections and occasional HVAC blower motor failures are also noted. However, these are generally manageable and repairable, with many owners experiencing minimal downtime. The D16 engine and manual transmission options appear to be exceptionally robust. Regular preventative maintenance and prompt attention to warning indicators are crucial for maximizing the VNL 760's long-term reliability and operational efficiency.
