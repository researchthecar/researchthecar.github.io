The 2019 Volvo VNL 760 is a popular semi-truck known for its fuel efficiency, comfortable cab, and advanced safety features. Engine options for this model year include the Volvo D11, D13, and Cummins X15. The Volvo D13 engine is a common choice, producing between 405 and 500 horsepower and up to 1850 lb-ft of torque. Transmission options include Volvo's I-Shift automated manual transmission and Eaton Fuller manual transmissions. Drivers appreciate the VNL 760 for its smooth ride and ergonomic design, while some common complaints include electrical issues and the cost of replacement parts.

**Engine**

**Volvo D13**
*   **Coolant Leaks from EGR Cooler**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaks originating from the EGR cooler, leading to a loss of coolant and potential overheating. This can be identified by visual inspection of the EGR cooler and coolant level checks.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the EGR cooler.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Diesel Particulate Filter (DPF) Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Frequent regeneration cycles, premature clogging, and potential engine derate due to high backpressure. Can be diagnosed through diagnostic codes related to DPF performance.
    *   Solution: DPF cleaning or replacement, depending on the severity of the issue. Ensure proper engine maintenance and fuel quality to prevent recurrence.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $3000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Cummins X15**
*   **Fuel Injector Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rough idling, misfires, reduced power, and poor fuel economy. Diagnosed through diagnostic codes and injector testing.
    *   Solution: Replacement of faulty fuel injector(s).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1000+ per injector.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Turbocharger Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, excessive smoke, and unusual noises from the turbocharger.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the turbocharger.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $2000 - $4000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**Volvo I-Shift**
*   **Shift Hesitation or Slipping**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Delay in shifting gears, transmission slipping, or rough shifts. Can be diagnosed with diagnostic codes.
    *   Solution: Software updates, clutch adjustment, or valve body replacement. In severe cases, a complete transmission rebuild or replacement may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $7,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Clutch Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Difficulty engaging gears, clutch slippage, or complete loss of clutch function.
    *   Solution: Clutch replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1500 - $3000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Air Suspension Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Truck leaning to one side, rough ride, air compressor running constantly. Usually caused by leaks in the air bags or airlines.
    *   Solution: Replace leaking air bags or repair/replace airlines.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+ per air bag or line.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Sensor Failures**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Various engine or transmission performance issues, depending on the sensor that fails. Diagnostic codes will usually be present.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the faulty sensor. Common culprits include NOx sensors, temperature sensors, and pressure sensors.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+ per sensor.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Wiring Harness Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Intermittent electrical problems, such as lights flickering, gauges not working, or engine malfunctions. Corrosion or damage to the wiring harness can cause these issues.
    *   Solution: Repair or replacement of the damaged wiring harness.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1000+ depending on the extent of the damage.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Headlight Condensation**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Moisture buildup inside the headlight housing, reducing visibility.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the headlight assembly or resealing the existing one.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+ per headlight.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **HVAC System Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Air conditioning not blowing cold air, heater not producing heat, or inconsistent airflow.
    *   Solution: Repair or replacement of components such as the compressor, condenser, evaporator, or blower motor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Instrument Cluster Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Gauges not working, warning lights illuminated, or complete failure of the instrument cluster.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the instrument cluster.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2019 Volvo VNL 760 has some common issues across the engine, transmission, suspension, electrical, exterior, and interior categories. Engine issues such as EGR cooler leaks and DPF problems can be costly, while transmission issues with the I-Shift can range from minor software adjustments to complete replacements. Electrical problems, particularly sensor failures and wiring harness issues, are also frequent. Interior HVAC and instrument cluster failures add to the list of potential problems. While some repairs are relatively inexpensive, others can be quite costly, making thorough inspections and preventative maintenance crucial for owners of this vehicle.
