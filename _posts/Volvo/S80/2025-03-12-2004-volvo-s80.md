The 2004 Volvo S80 is a mid-size luxury sedan known for its comfort and safety features. Engine options for this model year include a 2.9L inline-6 engine producing 197 hp and 207 lb-ft of torque, a 2.5L turbocharged inline-5 engine producing 208 hp and 236 lb-ft of torque, and a 2.9L twin-turbocharged inline-6 engine producing 268 hp and 280 lb-ft of torque. All engines were paired with a 4-speed automatic or 5-speed automatic transmission, depending on the engine option. Owners often praise the S80 for its comfortable ride and spacious interior, but common complaints include electrical issues and relatively high repair costs.

**Engine**

**2.5T 2.5L Turbocharged Inline-5**

* **Failed Mass Air Flow (MAF) Sensor**
    * Description/Symptoms: Engine hesitates or stumbles during acceleration, poor fuel economy, check engine light illuminated.
    * Solution: Replace the MAF sensor.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Faulty Ignition Coils**
    * Description/Symptoms: Misfires, rough idling, reduced power, check engine light.
    * Solution: Replace the faulty ignition coil(s).
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+ (depending on how many coils need to be replaced)
    * Recalls: None Found

**2.9 2.9L Inline-6**

* **Oil Leaks**
    * Description/Symptoms: Oil spots under the car, burning oil smell, low oil level. Often from the rear main seal, valve cover gasket, or oil pan gasket.
    * Solution: Replace the leaking gasket or seal.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $700+ (depending on the location of the leak)
    * Recalls: None Found

**T6 2.9L Twin-Turbocharged Inline-6**

* **Turbocharger Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, whistling or whining noise from the engine, check engine light. Can be related to turbo failure, vacuum leaks, or boost control issues.
    * Solution: Diagnose and repair or replace the faulty turbocharger component.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2000+ (depending on the severity and which parts are needed)
    * Recalls: None Found

* **PCV System Clogging**
    * Description/Symptoms: Rough idle, oil leaks, whistling sound from the engine.
    * Solution: Replace the PCV valve and clean out the PCV system.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $400
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**AW50/51 5-Speed Automatic**

* **Transmission Slipping or Harsh Shifting**
    * Description/Symptoms: Delayed engagement, slipping between gears, harsh shifts.
    * Solution: Perform a transmission fluid flush. If the problem persists, the transmission may need to be rebuilt or replaced.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 (fluid flush) - $3000+ (rebuild or replace)
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Torque Converter Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Shuddering at low speeds, stalling, poor acceleration.
    * Solution: Replace the torque converter.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1500
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Worn Control Arm Bushings**
    * Description/Symptoms: Clunking noises when driving over bumps, poor handling, uneven tire wear.
    * Solution: Replace the control arm bushings or the entire control arm.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600+ (depending on whether bushings or entire arms are replaced)
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Failed Strut Mounts**
    * Description/Symptoms: Noises when turning the steering wheel, excessive bouncing.
    * Solution: Replace the strut mounts.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Electronic Throttle Module (ETM) Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Reduced engine power, stalling, check engine light.
    * Solution: Replace the ETM.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1000
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Climate Control Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: Inconsistent temperature, failure of the blower motor, display malfunctions.
    * Solution: Diagnose and repair or replace the faulty component (blower motor, resistor, control module).
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $800+ (depending on the issue)
    * Recalls: None Found

* **ABS Module Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: ABS light on, traction control light on, brake problems.
    * Solution: Replace the ABS module.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1200
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Headlight Lens Clouding/Yellowing**
    * Description/Symptoms: Reduced headlight brightness, yellowed or cloudy lenses.
    * Solution: Polish the headlight lenses or replace the headlight assemblies.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $300+ (depending on the method used)
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Sunroof Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: Sunroof not opening or closing properly, leaking.
    * Solution: Repair or replace the sunroof motor or track.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2004 Volvo S80 can present owners with a range of issues, with electrical problems and engine-related concerns being the most prevalent. While some issues, such as headlight clouding, are relatively inexpensive to address, others, like turbocharger failure or transmission rebuilds, can be quite costly. Suspension and electrical repairs also contribute to the overall cost of ownership. The severity of these issues varies, but the potential for expensive repairs should be considered when evaluating a used 2004 Volvo S80.
