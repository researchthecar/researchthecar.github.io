The 2013 Volvo S80 is a mid-size luxury sedan known for its comfort, safety features, and understated elegance. Available engine options include a 3.2L inline-6 producing 240 horsepower and 236 lb-ft of torque, a 3.0L turbocharged inline-6 producing 300 horsepower and 325 lb-ft of torque, and a 2.0L turbocharged inline-4 producing 240 horsepower and 236 lb-ft of torque. All engines were paired with a 6-speed automatic transmission. Owners often praise the S80 for its comfortable ride and spacious interior. Criticisms often include relatively low fuel economy and somewhat dated technology compared to newer competitors.

**Engine**

**3.2L Inline-6**

*   **Oil Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks can occur from various seals and gaskets, including the valve cover gasket and rear main seal. Symptoms include visible oil leaks, a burning oil smell, and low oil levels.
    *   Solution: Replace the leaking gaskets or seals.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1000+ depending on the location and complexity of the leak.

*   **Thermostat Housing Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The thermostat housing, often made of plastic, can crack and leak coolant. Symptoms include overheating, coolant leaks, and low coolant levels.
    *   Solution: Replace the thermostat housing.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+

**3.0L Turbocharged Inline-6**

*   **Turbocharger Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Turbochargers can fail due to oil starvation or general wear and tear. Symptoms include a loss of power, whining noises from the turbo, and potentially check engine lights.
    *   Solution: Replace or rebuild the turbocharger.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1500 - $3000+

*   **Oil Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Similar to the 3.2L, oil leaks from seals and gaskets are common. Specifically, the turbocharger oil lines can also develop leaks.
    *   Solution: Replace the leaking gaskets, seals, or oil lines.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1000+ depending on the location and complexity of the leak.

**2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4**

*   **PCV Valve Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The Positive Crankcase Ventilation (PCV) valve can fail, leading to vacuum leaks, rough idling, and potentially oil leaks.
    *   Solution: Replace the PCV valve.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+

**Transmission**

**6-Speed Automatic (All Engines)**

*   **Valve Body Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: Erratic shifting, harsh shifts, slipping, or failure to shift can occur.
    * Solution: Replace or rebuild the valve body.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $800-$2500+

*   **Torque Converter Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Shuddering during acceleration, unusual noises, or complete transmission failure.
    * Solution: Replace the torque converter. In some cases, a transmission rebuild may be necessary.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $800-$3500+

**Suspension**

*   **Strut/Shock Absorber Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Bouncy ride, poor handling, and excessive body roll.
    *   Solution: Replace worn struts or shock absorbers.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+ per axle

*   **Control Arm Bushing Wear**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Clunking noises when going over bumps, vague steering feel, and uneven tire wear.
    *   Solution: Replace worn control arm bushings or the entire control arm.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600+ per control arm

**Electrical**

*   **Central Electronic Module (CEM) Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Various electrical malfunctions, including issues with lighting, power windows, and the immobilizer system.
    *   Solution: Diagnose and potentially reprogram or replace the CEM.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1500+

*   **Battery Drain**
    * Description/Symptoms: Battery drains quickly and the car is difficult to start.
    * Solution: Check all lights, modules, and connections to find the cause. Repair or replace the problem part.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100-$1,000+

**Exterior**

*   **Door Lock Actuator Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Door locks failing to lock or unlock properly.
    *   Solution: Replace the faulty door lock actuator.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+ per door

**Interior**

*   **Sunroof Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Sunroof may get stuck, make excessive noise, or fail to open or close properly.
    *   Solution: Repair or replace the sunroof motor, tracks, or other components.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+

*   **Navigation System/Infotainment Screen Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The navigation screen may freeze, glitch, or become unresponsive.
    *   Solution: Requires diagnosis of the head unit or navigation module.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1200+

**Recalls**

*   None Found

**Summary**

The 2013 Volvo S80, while a comfortable and safe vehicle, can suffer from a range of issues. Engine issues, particularly oil leaks and turbocharger problems in the 3.0L, can be costly to address. Transmission issues, while not universal, are a potential concern. Electrical gremlins, especially those related to the CEM, can also be frustrating and expensive. Suspension issues are relatively typical for a vehicle of this age. Overall, while many issues are minor and can be prevented with good maintenance, some can be significant, requiring careful inspection and potentially higher repair costs.
