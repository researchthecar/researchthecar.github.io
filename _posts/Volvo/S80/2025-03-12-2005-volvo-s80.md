The 2005 Volvo S80 is a mid-size luxury sedan known for its safety, comfort, and understated elegance. Engine options for the 2005 S80 included a 2.5L turbocharged inline-5 cylinder producing 208 horsepower and 236 lb-ft of torque, a 2.9L inline-6 cylinder producing 201 horsepower and 221 lb-ft of torque, and a 2.9L twin-turbocharged inline-6 cylinder producing 268 horsepower and 280 lb-ft of torque. All engines were paired with a 5-speed automatic transmission. Owners often appreciate the comfortable ride and spacious interior, but some find the fuel economy and repair costs to be drawbacks.

**Engine**

**2.5L Turbocharged Inline-5**

*   **Oil Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks often occur from the turbocharger oil lines, valve cover gasket, or rear main seal. Symptoms include visible oil leaks, burning oil smell, and low oil levels.
    *   Solution: Replace the leaking seals or gaskets. If the turbocharger oil lines are leaking, replacement of the lines is necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+ depending on the location of the leak and the parts needing to be replaced.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Faulty ETM (Electronic Throttle Module)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Hesitation, surging, rough idling, and eventual limp-home mode. A common code thrown is ECM-914F.
    *   Solution: Replacing the ETM.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $700-$1200+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**2.9L Inline-6 (Naturally Aspirated and Turbocharged)**

*   **Coil Pack Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Misfires, rough running engine, check engine light.
    *   Solution: Replace the faulty coil pack. Sometimes all 6 are replaced at once to prevent future failures
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50-$200 per coil pack, $300-$1200+ for all 6.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Vacuum Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rough idle, poor performance, and check engine light.
    *   Solution: Identify and replace the leaking vacuum lines or intake manifold gaskets.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $400+ depending on the location and complexity.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**All Transmissions (5-Speed Automatic)**

*   **Rough or Delayed Shifting**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Harsh or delayed shifts, particularly between 2nd and 3rd gear, can be caused by worn valve body components, low transmission fluid, or failing solenoids.
    *   Solution: Perform a transmission fluid flush and filter replacement. If the problem persists, the valve body may need to be rebuilt or replaced. Solenoids may also need replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300 for fluid and filter, $800 - $2000+ for valve body or solenoid replacement.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Worn Suspension Bushings**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Clunking noises, especially when going over bumps, can indicate worn control arm bushings or sway bar bushings. This can also cause imprecise steering.
    *   Solution: Replace the worn bushings. Sometimes, it's more cost-effective to replace the entire control arm.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600+ per corner depending on the component and labor.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Strut/Shock Absorber Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Bouncy ride, poor handling, and visible leaks from the struts/shocks.
    *   Solution: Replace the failed struts/shocks.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300-$800+ per pair depending on parts and labor
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **DIM (Driver Information Module) Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Display fading, pixel loss, or complete failure of the instrument cluster.
    *   Solution: Repair or replace the DIM. Some independent shops specialize in repairing these units.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300 for repair, $400 - $800+ for replacement.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **SRS (Supplemental Restraint System) Warning Light**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The SRS light illuminates due to a fault in the airbag system, often related to the seatbelt pretensioners or the SRS module itself.
    *   Solution: Diagnose the fault code using a Volvo-specific scan tool and repair or replace the faulty component.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+ depending on the faulty component.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Door Lock Actuator Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Door not locking or unlocking with the remote or interior switch.
    *   Solution: Replace the faulty door lock actuator.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $400 per door.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Sunroof Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Sunroof malfunctioning, sticking, or failing to open/close properly.
    *   Solution: Repair or replace the sunroof motor or tracks.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+ depending on the severity and the required parts.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**
The 2005 Volvo S80 can be a comfortable and reliable car, but potential buyers should be aware of the common issues. Engine issues such as oil leaks and coil pack failures can be costly to repair. Transmission issues are also a concern. Electrical problems with the DIM and SRS system are fairly common. Suspension components wear out and need to be replaced at a regular maintenance interval. Overall, while the S80 offers a luxurious experience, potential owners should factor in the potential for maintenance and repair costs when considering purchasing one.
