The 2001 Volvo S80 is a luxury sedan known for its comfort, safety features, and understated styling. Engine options include a 2.9L inline-6 producing 197 hp and 207 lb-ft of torque, a turbocharged 2.8L inline-6 producing 272 hp and 284 lb-ft of torque (T6), and a 2.4L inline-5 producing 168 hp and 170 lb-ft of torque. All engines are paired with a 4-speed automatic transmission or a 5-speed automatic transmission, depending on the model. Owners often praise the S80 for its comfortable ride and spacious interior, but common complaints include electrical issues, suspension problems, and relatively high maintenance costs.

**Engine**

**2.9L Inline-6**
*   **Coil Pack Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Misfires, rough idling, check engine light.
    *   Solution: Replace faulty coil pack(s).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+ (depending on how many coil packs need replacing).
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Electronic Throttle Module (ETM) Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Erratic idling, stalling, reduced engine power, check engine light.
    *   Solution: Replacement of ETM.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**2.8L Turbocharged Inline-6 (T6)**

*   **Turbocharger Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, whistling or grinding noises, excessive oil consumption, check engine light.
    *   Solution: Replace or rebuild the turbocharger.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1500 - $3000+
    *   Recalls: None Found
*   **Electronic Throttle Module (ETM) Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Erratic idling, stalling, reduced engine power, check engine light.
    *   Solution: Replacement of ETM.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found
*   **Coil Pack Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Misfires, rough idling, check engine light.
    *   Solution: Replace faulty coil pack(s).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+ (depending on how many coil packs need replacing).
    *   Recalls: None Found

**2.4L Inline-5**

*   **Electronic Throttle Module (ETM) Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Erratic idling, stalling, reduced engine power, check engine light.
    *   Solution: Replacement of ETM.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found
*   **Coil Pack Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Misfires, rough idling, check engine light.
    *   Solution: Replace faulty coil pack(s).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+ (depending on how many coil packs need replacing).
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**AW50-42LE (4-speed automatic)**
*   **Slipping or Harsh Shifting**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Delayed engagement, erratic shifting, slipping between gears.
    *   Solution: Transmission rebuild or replacement. In some cases, a valve body replacement might resolve the issue.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $2000 - $4000+ for rebuild/replacement. $500-1000+ for valve body.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**AW55-50/51SN (5-speed automatic)**

*   **Valve Body Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Harsh shifting, slipping, delayed engagement, "flare" between shifts.
    *   Solution: Valve body replacement or rebuild.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Front Control Arm Bushing Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Clunking noises from the front end, especially over bumps, vague steering.
    *   Solution: Replace control arm bushings or entire control arm assembly.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+ per side.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Strut Mount Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Creaking or popping noises when turning, loose steering feel.
    *   Solution: Replace strut mounts.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+ per pair
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Central Electronic Module (CEM) Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Intermittent electrical problems such as malfunctioning lights, windows, or wipers.
    *   Solution: CEM repair or replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1500+
    *   Recalls: None Found
*   **ABS Module Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: ABS light illumination, loss of ABS function, brake assist issues.
    *   Solution: ABS module repair or replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Headlight Condensation**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Moisture buildup inside headlight housings, reducing visibility.
    *   Solution: Remove headlight assembly and dry it out, reseal the housing. May require replacement of headlight assembly.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $600+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Sunroof Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Sunroof failing to open or close properly, binding or making unusual noises.
    *   Solution: Lubricate tracks, repair or replace motor or related components.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2001 Volvo S80, while comfortable and safe, is prone to several issues. The most common and potentially expensive problems relate to the electronic throttle module (ETM) which can fail on all engine options, as well as the transmissions which are prone to slipping or harsh shifts, and the CEM which can lead to a variety of electrical problems. Suspension components like control arm bushings and strut mounts are also known to fail. Repair costs can range from a few hundred dollars for minor fixes to several thousand for major repairs such as turbocharger or transmission replacements.
