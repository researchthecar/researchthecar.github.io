The 2006 Volvo S80 is a mid-size luxury sedan known for its safety, comfort, and understated elegance. Engine options include a 2.5L Turbocharged Inline-5 producing 208 hp and 236 lb-ft of torque, a 2.9L Inline-6 producing 201 hp and 221 lb-ft of torque, and a 4.4L V8 producing 311 hp and 325 lb-ft of torque. All engine options are paired with an Aisin Warner 6-speed automatic transmission. Owners generally appreciate the S80's comfortable ride, spacious interior, and safety features. Common dislikes include the somewhat dated infotainment system, occasional electrical quirks, and relatively high maintenance costs.

**Engine**

**2.5L Turbocharged Inline-5**
* **Coil Pack Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Misfires, rough running, check engine light.
    * Solution: Replace faulty coil pack(s).
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Turbocharger Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, whistling noises, excessive smoke, check engine light.
    * Solution: Diagnose turbocharger (leaks, damage, etc.) and replace or repair as needed.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $2500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**2.9L Inline-6**

No Issues Found

**4.4L V8**
* **Timing Chain Tensioner Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Rattling noise on startup, especially when cold, check engine light. If left unresolved, could result in catastrophic engine failure.
    * Solution: Replace the timing chain, tensioner, and guides. This is a labor-intensive job.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1500 - $4000+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Alternator Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Battery light illuminated, dimming headlights, difficulty starting, battery draining.
    * Solution: Replace the alternator.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**Aisin Warner 6-Speed Automatic (All Engines)**
* **Valve Body Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: Harsh shifting, slipping between gears, delayed engagement, transmission fault codes.
    * Solution: Replace or rebuild the valve body. Some have had success with valve body cleaning.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $2000+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Torque Converter Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Shuddering or vibration at low speeds, especially when accelerating. Stalling. Transmission fault codes.
    * Solution: Replace the torque converter.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $600 - $1800+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**
* **Front Lower Control Arm Bushing Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Clunking noises from the front suspension, especially over bumps. Wandering steering.
    * Solution: Replace the front lower control arms.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+ per side
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Rear Suspension Self-Leveling System Malfunctions (if equipped)**
    * Description/Symptoms: Rear end sagging, especially when loaded. Compressor failing to engage or running constantly.
    * Solution: Diagnose the system for leaks or component failures (air springs, compressor, sensors). Replace faulty parts.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**
* **Climate Control Module (CCM) Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: Intermittent or complete loss of climate control functions, display malfunctions.
    * Solution: Replacement of the CCM.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Central Electronic Module (CEM) Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: Various electrical malfunctions, including issues with lights, windows, door locks, and starting. Can be caused by water intrusion.
    * Solution: Diagnose the CEM and potentially replace or repair it. Requires reprogramming.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**
* **Sunroof Drain Clogs**
    * Description/Symptoms: Water leaks into the cabin, especially during or after rain. Wet carpets.
    * Solution: Clean the sunroof drains.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $200+ (depending on extent of damage and whether professional cleaning is needed).
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**
* **Driver Information Module (DIM) Pixel Loss**
    * Description/Symptoms: Pixels on the instrument cluster display fade or disappear, making it difficult to read information.
    * Solution: Replacement of the DIM.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $1000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary:** The 2006 Volvo S80, while generally a reliable and comfortable vehicle, has several common issues to be aware of. Engine problems, particularly with the 2.5L Turbo and the 4.4L V8, can be costly. Transmission issues are also prevalent, regardless of the engine. Electrical problems, stemming from the CCM and CEM, can be frustrating and require specialized diagnostics. While some issues are relatively minor, others can be quite expensive to repair. It's crucial to have any potential S80 thoroughly inspected before purchase to identify and address any existing or potential problems.
