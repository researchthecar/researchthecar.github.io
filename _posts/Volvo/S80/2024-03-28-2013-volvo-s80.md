**Introduction:**

The 2013 Volvo S80 is a midsize luxury sedan known for its stylish design, comfortable interior, and advanced safety features. It comes with a wide range of engine and transmission options, including a 2.5L turbocharged inline-5, a 3.2L naturally aspirated inline-6, a 3.0L turbocharged inline-6, and a 2.0L turbocharged inline-4. The transmission options include a 6-speed automatic, an 8-speed automatic, and a 6-speed manual. Despite its overall reliability, certain issues have been reported by owners.

**Engine**

**2.5L Turbocharged Inline-5**

* **Timing Belt Tensioner Failure:** The timing belt tensioner can fail, causing the belt to slip or break, which can lead to engine damage. The symptoms include a rattling noise from the engine, reduced power, and difficulty starting. The solution involves replacing the faulty tensioner and timing belt. Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,200+
* **Intake Manifold Runner Valve Issues:** The intake manifold runner valves can become stuck or malfunction, causing reduced engine performance and fuel economy. The solution involves cleaning or replacing the faulty valves. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600
* **Turbocharger Oil Leak:** The turbocharger can develop an oil leak, which can lead to reduced turbo boost and engine damage. The solution involves replacing the faulty turbocharger. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500

**3.2L Naturally Aspirated Inline-6**

* **No Issues Found**

**3.0L Turbocharged Inline-6**

* **Engine Coolant Leak:** The engine may develop a coolant leak from the water pump or radiator, leading to overheating and possible engine damage. The solution involves replacing the faulty component. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
* **Inlet Valve Carbon Buildup:** Carbon buildup on the inlet valves can cause reduced engine performance and fuel economy. The solution involves removing the carbon buildup using a chemical cleaning or walnut blasting. Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800

**Transmission**

**6-Speed Automatic**

* **Transmission Fluid Leak:** The transmission may develop a fluid leak from the pan gasket or seals, which can lead to transmission damage. The solution involves replacing the faulty gasket or seals. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
* **Shifting Problems:** The transmission may experience rough shifting or slipping gears, indicating a problem with the transmission control module or hydraulic system. The solution involves diagnosing and repairing or replacing the faulty components. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+

**8-Speed Automatic**

* **No Issues Found**

**6-Speed Manual**

* **Clutch Pedal Sticking:** The clutch pedal may stick or become difficult to operate, making it difficult to shift gears. The solution involves lubricating the pedal linkage or replacing the clutch master or slave cylinder. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600

**Suspension**

* **Front Control Arm Bushing Wear:** The bushings on the front control arms can wear out, leading to clunking or rattling noises and reduced steering precision. The solution involves replacing the faulty bushings. Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800

**Electrical**

* **Headlight Switch Failure:** The headlight switch can fail, causing the headlights or other electrical components to malfunction. The solution involves replacing the faulty headlight switch. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
* **Heated Seat Element Failure:** The heated seat elements in the front or rear seats may fail, preventing the seats from warming up. The solution involves replacing the faulty heating element or seat cover. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600

**Exterior**

* **No Issues Found**

**Interior**

* **Door Lock Switch Failure:** The door lock switches on the driver's side or other doors may fail, making it difficult to lock or unlock the doors. The solution involves replacing the faulty door lock switch. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300
* **Sunroof Drain Clogging:** The sunroof drains can become clogged with debris, which can lead to water leakage into the cabin. The solution involves cleaning out the clogged drains. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200

**Overview:**

While the 2013 Volvo S80 is generally a reliable vehicle, it has been reported to have some specific issues related to the engine, transmission, suspension, electrical, and interior components. These issues range in severity from minor annoyances to potential engine damage. The estimated cost to fix these issues can vary depending on the specific problem and the location. Regular maintenance and repairs can help mitigate the risk of experiencing these issues.
