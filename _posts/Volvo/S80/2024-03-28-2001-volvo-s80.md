**The 2001 Volvo S80** was a luxury sedan that offered a comfortable and refined driving experience. It was available with several engine and transmission options including a 2.9L inline-6 producing 204 horsepower and 236 lb-ft of torque. Transmission options included a 5-speed manual, 5-speed automatic, and 4-speed automatic. The S80 was praised for its safety features, spacious interior, and comfortable ride. However, like any vehicle, it had some common issues reported by owners.

**Engine**

**2.9L Inline-6**
* **Intake manifold gasket failure:** The intake manifold gasket can fail, causing coolant and oil leaks. Symptoms include overheating, rough idle, and decreased engine performance. The solution is to replace the gasket. The estimated cost to fix is $500-$1,000+.
* **Transmission failure:** The transmission can experience slipping, shuddering, or failure. Symptoms include difficulty shifting, delayed engagement, and loss of power. The solution is to rebuild or replace the transmission. The estimated cost to fix is $2,000-$4,000+.

**Transmission**

**5-speed Manual**
* **Clutch wear:** The clutch can become worn, causing slipping and difficulty engaging gears. Symptoms include a burning smell, difficulty shifting, and loss of power. The solution is to replace the clutch. The estimated cost to fix is $500-$1,000.

**5-speed Automatic**
* **Transmission fluid leaks:** The transmission fluid can leak from the pan, lines, or cooler. Symptoms include low transmission fluid levels, slipping gears, and difficulty shifting. The solution is to locate and repair the leak. The estimated cost to fix is $200-$500+.

**Suspension**
* **Strut failure:** The struts can fail, causing a rough ride, excessive body roll, and decreased handling. Symptoms include a knocking or clunking noise, uneven tire wear, and decreased ride height. The solution is to replace the struts. The estimated cost to fix is $500-$1,000 per axle.

**Electrical**
* **Battery drain:** The battery can drain quickly, causing the vehicle to fail to start. Symptoms include a weak or dead battery, flickering lights, and electrical system malfunctions. The solution is to diagnose the source of the drain and repair or replace the faulty component. The estimated cost to fix varies depending on the cause of the drain.
* **Alternator failure:** The alternator can fail, causing the battery to not charge. Symptoms include a dim or flickering dashboard, difficulty starting the vehicle, and a loss of electrical power. The solution is to replace the alternator. The estimated cost to fix is $500-$1,000.

**Exterior**
* **Headlight condensation:** Condensation can build up inside the headlights, causing poor visibility at night. Symptoms include fogging or water droplets inside the headlights. The solution is to seal the headlights or replace the headlight assembly. The estimated cost to fix is $200-$500 per headlight.

**Interior**
* **Climate control failure:** The climate control system can fail, causing a loss of heating or cooling. Symptoms include a malfunctioning HVAC system, inconsistent temperature control, and a lack of airflow. The solution is to diagnose the fault and repair or replace the faulty component. The estimated cost to fix varies depending on the cause of the failure.

**Summary**

The 2001 Volvo S80 offers a comfortable and refined driving experience, but it has some common issues reported by owners. The most severe and costly issue is transmission failure, which can cost $2,000-$4,000+ to repair. Other common issues include intake manifold gasket failure, clutch wear, and suspension strut failure. While these issues can be costly to repair, they are relatively rare compared to other vehicles in its class.
