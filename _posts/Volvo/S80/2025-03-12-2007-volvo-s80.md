The 2007 Volvo S80 is a luxury sedan known for its comfort, safety, and understated elegance. Engine options included a 3.2L Inline-6 producing 235 hp and 236 lb-ft of torque, a 4.4L V8 producing 311 hp and 325 lb-ft of torque, and a 2.5L Turbo Inline-5 producing 208 hp and 236 lb-ft of torque. The transmissions were either a 6-speed automatic or a 5-speed automatic (depending on the engine). Owners often praise the S80 for its comfortable ride, spacious interior, and safety features. Common complaints include electrical gremlins, expensive repairs, and some issues with the automatic transmission.

**Engine**

**3.2L Inline-6**

*   **Coil Pack Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Misfires, rough running engine, check engine light.
    *   Solution: Replace the faulty coil pack.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Oil Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Oil spots under the car, burning oil smell, low oil level. Often originates from valve cover gasket or rear main seal.
    *   Solution: Replace the leaking gasket or seal.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**4.4L V8**

*   **Timing Chain Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rattling noise from the engine, especially on startup. Can lead to serious engine damage if not addressed.
    *   Solution: Replace the timing chain and related components.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Alternator Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Battery light on, dimming lights, difficulty starting.
    *   Solution: Replace the alternator.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**2.5L Turbo Inline-5**

*   **Turbocharger Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, whistling noise from the turbo, check engine light.
    *   Solution: Repair or replace the turbocharger.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Vacuum Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Poor idling, rough running, check engine light.
    *   Solution: Locate and repair the vacuum leak.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**Aisin Warner AWTF-80 SC 6-Speed Automatic (Most common transmission for all engine options)**

*   **Valve Body Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Harsh shifting, slipping between gears, delayed engagement, transmission fault codes.
    *   Solution: Replace or rebuild the valve body.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $2,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Torque Converter Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Shuddering at low speeds, especially during lock-up, transmission fault codes.
    *   Solution: Replace the torque converter.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Front Control Arm Bushing Wear**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Clunking noise from the front suspension, especially when going over bumps.
    *   Solution: Replace the control arm bushings or the entire control arm.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Rear Shock Absorber Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Bouncy ride, poor handling, excessive rear suspension movement.
    *   Solution: Replace the rear shock absorbers.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Central Electronic Module (CEM) Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Intermittent electrical problems, including issues with lights, wipers, door locks, and starting.
    *   Solution: Repair or replace the CEM. This often requires reprogramming.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Power Window Regulator Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Window moves slowly or not at all, grinding noise from the door panel.
    *   Solution: Replace the window regulator.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Headlight Condensation**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Moisture buildup inside the headlight housing.
    *   Solution: Remove and reseal the headlight housing or replace the headlight assembly.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Door Lock Actuator Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Door will not lock or unlock with the key fob or interior switch.
    *   Solution: Replace the faulty door lock actuator.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Sunroof Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Sunroof gets stuck, makes grinding noises, or leaks.
    *   Solution: Repair or replace the sunroof motor, track, or seals.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Climate Control Fan Motor Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Blower motor not working, only works on certain speeds, or makes noise.
    *   Solution: Replace the blower motor or resistor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2007 Volvo S80 can be a comfortable and luxurious vehicle, but potential buyers should be aware of the common issues. Engine issues vary depending on the engine choice, with timing chain concerns in the V8 being a notable point. Transmission issues with the Aisin Warner 6-speed automatic are fairly common across all engine options. Electrical problems and suspension wear are also typical areas of concern. Repair costs can range from relatively minor to quite expensive, especially for engine or transmission work. Overall, a pre-purchase inspection is highly recommended, and budgeting for potential repairs is advisable.
