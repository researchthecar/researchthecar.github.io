The 2010 Volvo S80 is a mid-size luxury sedan known for its safety features, comfortable ride, and understated elegance. Engine options for the 2010 S80 included a 3.2L inline-6 producing 235 horsepower and 236 lb-ft of torque, a 3.0L turbocharged inline-6 producing 281 horsepower and 295 lb-ft of torque and a 4.4L V8 producing 311 horsepower and 325 lb-ft of torque. All engine options were paired with a 6-speed automatic transmission. Owners often appreciate the S80's comfortable and spacious interior, as well as its reputation for safety. Some common criticisms include lackluster fuel economy, some reliability concerns, and less sporty handling compared to some competitors.

**Engine**

**3.2L Inline-6**

*   **Oil Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks can develop from various seals and gaskets, including the valve cover gasket, oil pan gasket, and rear main seal. Symptoms include visible oil leaks, a burning oil smell, and low oil levels.
    *   Solution: Replacing the leaking gasket or seal.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $800+ depending on the location of the leak.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**3.0L Turbocharged Inline-6**

*   **Turbocharger Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Turbocharger failure can occur, resulting in reduced engine power, whistling noises from the turbo, and potential engine damage.
    *   Solution: Replacing the turbocharger.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Oil Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks can develop from various seals and gaskets, including the valve cover gasket, oil pan gasket, and rear main seal. Symptoms include visible oil leaks, a burning oil smell, and low oil levels.
    *   Solution: Replacing the leaking gasket or seal.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $800+ depending on the location of the leak.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**4.4L V8**

*   **Oil Consumption**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The 4.4L V8 engine may exhibit excessive oil consumption.
    *   Solution: Possible solutions include piston ring replacement or valve stem seal replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $5,000+ depending on the repair needed.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Oil Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks can develop from various seals and gaskets, including the valve cover gasket, oil pan gasket, and rear main seal. Symptoms include visible oil leaks, a burning oil smell, and low oil levels.
    *   Solution: Replacing the leaking gasket or seal.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $800+ depending on the location of the leak.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**6-Speed Automatic (All Engines)**

*   **Valve Body Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Harsh or delayed shifting can be caused by a faulty valve body.
    *   Solution: Valve body replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Front Strut Mounts**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Premature wear or failure of the front strut mounts can cause clunking noises, especially when turning.
    *   Solution: Replacing the strut mounts.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+ for both sides.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Rear Suspension Bushings**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Worn rear suspension bushings can lead to a loose or wandering feeling in the rear end, as well as clunking noises.
    *   Solution: Replacing the rear suspension bushings.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800+ depending on which bushings need replacement.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Central Electronic Module (CEM) Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The CEM can experience failures leading to various electrical problems such as intermittent starting issues, malfunctioning lights, and issues with the climate control system.
    *   Solution: CEM replacement or reprogramming.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Power Window Motor Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The power windows may occasionally fail to function properly, requiring replacement of the window motor.
    * Solution: Replacing the power window motor
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $250 - $400+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Headlight Condensation**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Moisture buildup inside the headlight housings, causing reduced visibility and potential damage to the headlight components.
    *   Solution: Replacing the headlight assembly or resealing the existing one.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1000+ depending on if you reseal or replace.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Sunroof Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The sunroof mechanism can fail, causing it to jam, leak, or not open/close properly.
    *   Solution: Sunroof repair or replacement of the sunroof motor or mechanism.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,000+ depending on the extent of the damage.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

The 2010 Volvo S80, while generally a comfortable and safe vehicle, has some notable issues that prospective buyers should be aware of. Engine oil leaks and consumption problems appear across engine options. Turbocharger failures and valve body issues in the transmission can be costly repairs. Electrical issues with the CEM are also relatively common and can cause a variety of problems. Suspension and sunroof issues can also arise. Overall, while some issues are relatively minor, others can be significant, potentially leading to repair costs ranging from a few hundred to several thousand dollars.
