The 2016 Volvo S80 is a luxury sedan known for its comfortable ride, spacious interior, and focus on safety. Engine options for the 2016 model year include the T5 Drive-E, a 2.0L turbocharged inline-4 producing 240 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque, and the T6 Drive-E, a 2.0L turbocharged and supercharged inline-4 producing 302 horsepower and 295 lb-ft of torque. The T5 engine is paired with an 8-speed automatic transmission, while the T6 engine is also paired with an 8-speed automatic transmission. Owners often praise the S80 for its refined driving experience and comfortable seating. Common dislikes include the somewhat dated infotainment system and the limited availability of advanced driver-assistance features compared to some competitors.

**Engine**

**2.0L T5 Drive-E (Turbocharged Inline-4)**

*   **Excessive Oil Consumption**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported higher than normal oil consumption, requiring more frequent oil top-offs between scheduled oil changes. The oil consumption can gradually get worse over time as the piston rings wear down.
    *   Solution: Monitor oil levels regularly and top off as needed. In more severe cases, piston ring replacement may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $5,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**2.0L T6 Drive-E (Turbocharged and Supercharged Inline-4)**

*   **Coolant Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaks can occur at the water pump or thermostat housing, leading to low coolant levels and potential overheating. White smoke from exhaust may also be present.
    *   Solution: Inspect and replace the leaking water pump or thermostat housing.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**Aisin Warner TG-81SC 8-Speed Automatic**

*   **Rough Shifting/Hesitation**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners report experiencing rough or delayed shifting, particularly between lower gears. This can manifest as a noticeable "clunk" or hesitation during acceleration.
    *   Solution: Transmission fluid flush and fill may help in some cases. If the problem persists, valve body replacement or transmission rebuild may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 (fluid flush), $1,500 - $4,000+ (valve body/rebuild)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

No Issues Found

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Freezing/Crashing**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The infotainment system can occasionally freeze, crash, or become unresponsive, requiring a restart. This affects navigation, audio, and climate control functions.
    *   Solution: Software updates from the Volvo dealer may resolve some of these issues. In more severe cases, replacement of the infotainment system module may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 (software update), $500 - $1,500+ (module replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Battery Drain**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The car battery drains quickly, especially if the vehicle sits for a few days without being driven. This can lead to difficulty starting the car.
    *   Solution: Check for parasitic draws and repair any faulty components. A new battery may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+ (parasitic draw diagnosis/repair), $200 - $400 (battery replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Sunroof Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: Sunroof fails to fully close, or does not open when prompted. May hear a grinding noise when attempting to operate the sunroof.
    * Solution: The sunroof motor or track may need to be replaced.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Air Conditioning Malfunctions**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The air conditioning system may blow warm air, even when set to the coldest setting. This can be due to a refrigerant leak, a faulty compressor, or a malfunctioning blend door actuator.
    *   Solution: Diagnose the specific cause of the AC malfunction and repair or replace the faulty components.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+ (refrigerant recharge/leak repair), $500 - $1,500+ (compressor replacement), $200 - $500+ (blend door actuator)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2016 Volvo S80, while a comfortable and safe vehicle, does have a few common issues that potential buyers should be aware of. Engine issues such as excessive oil consumption and coolant leaks can be costly to repair. Transmission problems like rough shifting may also require significant investment. Electrical issues, such as infotainment system malfunctions and battery drain, are less severe but can be frustrating. Overall, while the severity and cost of these issues vary, careful inspection and a pre-purchase inspection are highly recommended before buying a used 2016 S80.
