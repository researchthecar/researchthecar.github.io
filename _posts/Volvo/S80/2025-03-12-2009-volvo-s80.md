The 2009 Volvo S80 is a mid-size luxury sedan known for its safety, comfort, and understated elegance. Engine options include a 3.2L inline-6 producing 235 hp and 236 lb-ft of torque, a 4.4L V8 producing 311 hp and 325 lb-ft of torque, and a 3.0L turbocharged inline-6 producing 281 hp and 295 lb-ft of torque. Transmission options primarily consist of a 6-speed automatic. Owners generally appreciate the S80's comfortable ride, spacious interior, and safety features. Common dislikes include higher repair costs and occasional electrical quirks.

**Engine**

**3.2L Inline-6**

*   **Oil Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks can occur from various locations, including the valve cover gasket, oil filter housing, and rear main seal. Symptoms include visible oil leaks, burning oil smell, and low oil levels.
    *   Solution: Replacing the leaking gasket or seal.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+ depending on location and mechanic rates.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Faulty Thermostat**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The thermostat may fail, causing the engine to overheat or run too cold. Symptoms include the temperature gauge fluctuating wildly, the engine taking longer than usual to warm up, or the heater not working properly.
    *   Solution: Replacing the thermostat.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300
    *   Recalls: None Found

**4.4L V8**

*   **Timing Chain Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: The timing chain can stretch or fail, leading to engine misfires, rough running, and potential engine damage if not addressed. Symptoms include a rattling noise from the engine, especially on startup, and the engine running poorly.
    * Solution: Replacing the timing chain, guides, and tensioners.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

*   **Valve Cover Gasket Leaks**
    * Description/Symptoms: Leaks from the valve cover gaskets cause oil to drip onto hot engine components, resulting in a burning oil smell. Visible oil around the valve covers.
    * Solution: Replacing the valve cover gaskets.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600
    * Recalls: None Found

**3.0L Turbocharged Inline-6**
*   **Turbocharger Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The turbocharger can fail, resulting in a loss of power and increased engine noise.
    * Solution: Replacing the turbocharger.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**Aisin Warner AW55-50/51SN 6-Speed Automatic (All Engines)**
* **Valve Body Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: The valve body in the transmission can wear out, causing hard shifting, delayed engagement, and slipping between gears.
    * Solution: Replacing or rebuilding the valve body.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1500+
    * Recalls: None Found

*   **Torque Converter Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The torque converter may fail, leading to shuddering, poor acceleration, and transmission slipping.
    *   Solution: Replacing the torque converter.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Worn Suspension Bushings**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Worn bushings in the control arms, sway bar links, and struts can cause clunking noises, poor handling, and uneven tire wear.
    *   Solution: Replacing the worn bushings or the entire suspension component.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+ per corner depending on the component.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Strut and Shock Absorber Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Leaking or worn struts and shock absorbers can result in a bouncy ride, poor handling, and increased stopping distances.
    *   Solution: Replacing the struts and shock absorbers.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1000+ per axle.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Central Electronic Module (CEM) Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The CEM can fail, causing a variety of electrical problems, including issues with the windows, locks, lights, and starting.
    *   Solution: Replacing or reprogramming the CEM.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Power Window Regulator Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The power window regulator can fail, causing the window to not operate properly or to get stuck.
    *   Solution: Replacing the power window regulator.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Sunroof Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The sunroof may leak, get stuck, or fail to open/close properly.
    *   Solution: Repairing or replacing the sunroof motor, seals, or tracks.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Climate Control Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Problems with the climate control system, such as failure to blow hot or cold air, or inconsistent temperature.
    *   Solution: Repairing or replacing the blend door actuator, heater core, or AC compressor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1200+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2009 Volvo S80 can present owners with a range of issues, from relatively minor concerns like oil leaks and worn suspension components to more significant problems like timing chain failures, CEM failures, and transmission issues. While some issues are relatively inexpensive to address, others can result in considerable repair costs. The severity of these issues can vary greatly depending on the vehicle's maintenance history and driving conditions. Potential buyers should carefully inspect any used S80 and consider a pre-purchase inspection by a qualified mechanic to identify any existing or potential problems.
