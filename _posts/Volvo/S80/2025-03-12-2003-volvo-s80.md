The 2003 Volvo S80 is a mid-size luxury sedan known for its safety, comfort, and understated elegance. Available engine options include a 2.9L inline-6 producing 197 horsepower and 207 lb-ft of torque, a turbocharged 2.9L inline-6 producing 268 horsepower and 280 lb-ft of torque, and a 2.5L turbocharged inline-5 producing 208 horsepower and 236 lb-ft of torque. All engines were paired with a 4-speed automatic transmission or a 5-speed automatic transmission depending on the trim. Owners often appreciate the S80's comfortable ride, spacious interior, and safety features. Common complaints include the car's complex electrical system, potential for expensive repairs, and somewhat dated styling compared to newer vehicles.

**Engine**

**2.9L Inline-6**

*   **Electronic Throttle Module (ETM) Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Reduced engine power, rough idling, stalling, and a warning light on the dashboard.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the ETM.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $600 - $1200+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Camshaft Position Sensor Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Engine misfires, rough idling, difficulty starting, stalling. Check engine light will illuminate.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the faulty camshaft position sensor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**2.5L Turbocharged Inline-5**

*   **Oil Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks from various seals and gaskets, especially around the turbocharger and valve cover. Noticeable oil consumption.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the leaking seals and gaskets.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+ (depending on the location and severity of the leak)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**4-Speed Automatic (Aisin Warner AW50-42LE)**

*   **Harsh Shifting/Slipping**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Abrupt or delayed shifts between gears, especially when cold. Transmission slipping under load.
    *   Solution: Fluid change, valve body repair/replacement, or complete transmission rebuild/replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 (fluid change) - $3000+ (rebuild/replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**5-Speed Automatic (Aisin Warner AW55-50/51SN)**

*   **Valve Body Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Erratic shifting, delayed engagement, hard shifts, or failure to shift at all.
    *   Solution: Valve body replacement or rebuild, software update.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $2000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Worn Suspension Bushings**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Clunking or rattling noises from the suspension, especially when going over bumps. Vague steering.
    *   Solution: Replacement of worn bushings (control arm bushings, sway bar bushings, etc.).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+ (depending on which bushings need replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Strut/Shock Absorber Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Bouncy or unstable ride, excessive body roll in corners, leaking fluid from the struts/shocks.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the faulty struts/shocks.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1000+ (for a pair)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **ABS Module Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: ABS warning light on, loss of ABS functionality, potential for brake lockup.
    *   Solution: ABS module repair or replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Dimming or Flickering Headlights**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Headlights dimming or flickering intermittently.
    *   Solution: Clean electrical connections, inspect the alternator and battery, or replace the headlight switch.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50-$500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Door Lock Actuator Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Door locks failing to lock or unlock, requiring manual operation.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the faulty door lock actuator.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+ per door
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Sunroof Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Sunroof failing to open or close properly, getting stuck, or leaking.
    *   Solution: Lubricate tracks, repair or replace the sunroof motor or regulator.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $600+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2003 Volvo S80 is generally a reliable vehicle, but owners should be aware of potential issues with the ETM, transmissions, suspension components, ABS module, and door lock actuators. While some repairs can be relatively inexpensive, others, such as transmission overhauls or ABS module replacements, can be costly. It's crucial to have a pre-purchase inspection performed by a qualified mechanic to identify any existing or potential problems. Addressing these issues promptly can help extend the life of the vehicle and prevent more significant damage down the road.
