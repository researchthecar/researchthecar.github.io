The 2000 Volvo S80 was a luxury sedan known for its comfort, safety features, and distinctive styling. Engine options included a 2.9L inline-6 producing around 200 hp and 207 lb-ft of torque, and a 2.8L twin-turbocharged inline-6 generating approximately 272 hp and 284 lb-ft of torque. The vehicle came standard with a 4-speed automatic transmission. Owners often praised its comfortable ride, spacious interior, and advanced safety technology for its time. Common complaints included electrical gremlins, issues with the electronic throttle module (ETM), and some reliability concerns related to the automatic transmission.

**Engine**

**2.9L Inline-6**

*   **Faulty Mass Air Flow (MAF) Sensor**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Engine hesitates, rough idle, poor fuel economy, check engine light illuminated.
    *   Solution: Replace the MAF sensor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300
    * Recalls: None Found

*   **Failed Fuel Injectors**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rough idle, misfires, poor fuel economy, check engine light illuminated.
    *   Solution: Replace faulty fuel injectors.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**2.8L Twin-Turbo Inline-6**

*   **Turbocharger Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, whining sound from engine, excessive smoke, check engine light illuminated.
    *   Solution: Replace or rebuild the turbocharger.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1500 - $3500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Oil Leaks from Turbo Lines**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Burning oil smell, visible oil leaks, low oil level.
    *   Solution: Replace oil lines and seals.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

* **Electronic Throttle Module (ETM) Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: Reduced engine power, erratic idling, stalling.
    * Solution: Replace or repair the ETM.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**4-Speed Automatic**

*   **Slipping or Harsh Shifting**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Transmission slips between gears, harsh or delayed shifting.
    *   Solution: Fluid flush, valve body repair, or transmission rebuild/replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300 (fluid flush), $500 - $1500+ (valve body), $2500 - $4500+ (rebuild/replace).
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Torque Converter Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Shuddering at low speeds, stalling, poor acceleration.
    *   Solution: Replace the torque converter.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Worn Suspension Bushings**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Clunking noises, vague steering, uneven tire wear.
    *   Solution: Replace worn bushings (control arm bushings, sway bar bushings, etc.).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Failed Struts/Shocks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Bouncy ride, poor handling, excessive body roll.
    *   Solution: Replace struts and/or shocks.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Central Electronic Module (CEM) Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Intermittent electrical problems, such as malfunctioning lights, wipers, or power windows.
    *   Solution: Diagnose and repair/replace the CEM. This might require reprogramming.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1200+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **ABS Module Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: ABS light illuminated, ABS system not functioning, brake pedal feels different.
    *   Solution: Repair or replace the ABS module.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Power Window Regulator Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Power windows don't work or only work intermittently.
    *   Solution: Replace power window regulator.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+ per window
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Headlight Housing Cracking/Clouding**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Headlights have yellowed or cracked plastic, reduced visibility.
    *   Solution: Replace headlight assemblies or refinish the plastic.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Sunroof Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Sunroof won't open or close properly, makes grinding noises.
    *   Solution: Repair or replace the sunroof motor or mechanism.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

* **Climate Control Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: Air conditioning not blowing cold, heater blowing cold or hot air
    * Solution: Check or replace climate control module
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2000 Volvo S80 presents a mix of potential issues, ranging from relatively minor electrical problems to more significant mechanical concerns with the engine and transmission. The twin-turbocharged models may be more prone to expensive repairs due to turbocharger-related issues. Electrical gremlins and suspension wear are common problems as well. While some repairs are moderately priced, others, such as turbocharger replacement or transmission rebuilds, can be quite costly. Addressing issues promptly can prevent further damage and maintain the vehicle's reliability.
