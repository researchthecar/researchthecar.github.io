# 2016 Volvo S80 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a 2016 Volvo S80, buyers are looking at the final model year for this luxury sedan's generation. While it offers the refined comfort, safety focus, and classic Scandinavian styling characteristic of the brand, prospective owners should review available owner feedback regarding powertrain options and isolated mechanical or electrical complaints. Taking the time to thoroughly inspect service records and conduct a comprehensive pre-purchase inspection can help ensure a dependable ownership experience.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged 4-Cylinder

This 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine utilizes both supercharging and turbocharging in some Drive-E configurations, typically producing around 302 horsepower. It provides spirited acceleration alongside respectable efficiency for a luxury sedan. Buyers should monitor regular maintenance, particularly fluid changes and timing components, to maintain long-term reliability.

### 3.0L Turbocharged V6

The 3.0-liter turbocharged inline-six engine generates approximately 300 horsepower and smooth torque delivery paired with all-wheel drive. It is generally robust but requires vigilant monitoring of fluid levels and regular servicing. Buyers should check for any history of oil consumption or sensor faults.

**Sudden Engine Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have reported unexpected engine stalls while driving, resulting in a sudden loss of acceleration and power.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified mechanic diagnose the fuel delivery system, electronic throttle body, and engine management sensors.

**Low Oil Pressure Warning**
- **Symptoms:** A sudden low oil pressure warning light illuminates on the dashboard, potentially indicating critically low oil levels without prior alerts.
- **Solution:** Check oil levels frequently between changes and inspect the engine for leaks or excessive consumption.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 8-speed automatic transmission works alongside Drive-E engines to deliver smooth, responsive gear changes and optimize fuel economy. Routine fluid checks and software updates help ensure longevity in everyday driving conditions.

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 6-speed automatic transmission is paired with inline-six engine configurations and all-wheel-drive systems, offering traditional gear-shifting behavior. Regular fluid maintenance is recommended to prevent premature wear and maintain shift quality.

## Suspension

### Wheels and Rims

Suspension components are generally durable, though low-profile factory wheels may be susceptible to damage from severe road impacts.

**Alloy Rim Cracking**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have experienced repeated cracking of factory alloy rims, leading to gradual tire pressure loss and potential air leaks.
- **Solution:** Inspect wheels for structural cracks during routine tire rotations and replace damaged rims as necessary.

## Brakes

### Start/Stop System

Electrical systems on this model support a wide array of luxury and convenience features, though occasional sensor or software glitches can arise.

**Start/Stop Delay**
- **Symptoms:** The engine auto start/stop function may exhibit delayed responsiveness when releasing the brake pedal, causing a hesitation before restart.
- **Solution:** Have the battery, starter components, and software calibration checked by a Volvo technician.

## Exterior

### Sunroof

Exterior body panels and glass components are typically well-assembled, though isolated incidents involving glass stress have been documented.

**Sunroof Glass Shattering**
- **Symptoms:** The glass panel on the sunroof may fracture or shatter unexpectedly while driving.
- **Solution:** Inspect the sunroof glass for any pre-existing stress marks or chips, and ensure seals are properly aligned.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 15V555000: Electrical System:Wiring:Fuses And Circuit Breakers](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volvo&model=S80&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** If the vehicle is unexpectedly unable to be started in the middle of traffic, such as when stopped at an intersection, there is an increased risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Volvo will notify owners, and dealers will replace the existing starter motor fuse with a higher amperage fuse, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V378000: Seat Belts:Front](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volvo&model=S80&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** The flexible steel cable that connects the seat belt to the front outboard seating positions may fatigue over time, causing the seat belt to not secure the occupant in the event of a crash. An unsecured occupant has an increased risk of injury in the event of a crash.
- **What to do:** Volvo will notify owners, and dealers will replace the seat belt anchor cable for both front seats. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2016 Volvo S80 remains a comfortable and feature-rich used luxury sedan choice, provided it has been well-maintained by previous owners. Buyers should prioritize a detailed pre-purchase inspection focusing on the engine, electrical systems, and wheel condition. Reviewing service history will help minimize unexpected repair expenses and ensure a satisfying ownership experience.
