### 2015 Volvo S80

The 2015 Volvo S80 is a luxury sedan known for its sleek exterior, spacious and comfortable interior, and a range of advanced safety features. It's available with two engine options: a 2.0-liter turbocharged inline-4 and a 3.0-liter turbocharged inline-6. Transmission options include a 6-speed automatic and an 8-speed automatic. The S80 has received positive reviews for its smooth ride, responsive handling, and well-appointed cabin. However, it has also been criticized for its limited rear legroom and uninspiring engine performance.

### Engine

#### 2.0-liter Turbocharged Inline-4

**Oil Leaks**
Description/Symptoms: Oil leaking from the rear crankshaft seal or oil pan gasket can cause oil loss and a burning oil smell.
Solution: Replace the leaking seal or gasket.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
Recalls: None Found

**Fuel Injector Failure**
Description/Symptoms: Faulty fuel injectors can cause rough idling, poor acceleration, and increased emissions.
Solution: Replace the faulty fuel injectors.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800+
Recalls: None Found

#### 3.0-liter Turbocharged Inline-6

**Timing Chain Tensioner Failure**
Description/Symptoms: A failing timing chain tensioner can cause a rattling noise from the engine, loss of power, and eventually engine damage.
Solution: Replace the timing chain tensioner and related components.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
Recalls: NHTSA Campaign Number: 22V700

**Intake Valve Deposits**
Description/Symptoms: Carbon buildup on the intake valves can lead to reduced engine performance, misfires, and increased emissions.
Solution: Remove and clean the intake valves.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800+
Recalls: None Found

### Transmission

#### 6-speed Automatic

**Shift Solenoid Failure**
Description/Symptoms: Malfunctioning shift solenoids can cause rough shifting, delayed shifts, or the transmission to get stuck in a particular gear.
Solution: Replace the faulty shift solenoid.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800+
Recalls: None Found

#### 8-speed Automatic

**Mechatronic Unit Failure**
Description/Symptoms: A faulty mechatronic unit can lead to various transmission problems, including rough shifting, slipping, or loss of gears.
Solution: Replace the mechatronic unit.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+
Recalls: None Found

### Electrical

**Battery Drain**
Description/Symptoms: A parasitic drain on the battery can lead to a dead battery and starting problems.
Solution: Identify and fix the source of the electrical drain.
Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the source of the drain
Recalls: None Found

**Headlight Condensation**
Description/Symptoms: Moisture buildup inside the headlight assemblies can cause condensation and impair visibility.
Solution: Replace the affected headlight assemblies.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
Recalls: NHTSA Campaign Number: 21V401

**No Issues Found**

### Interior

**Climate Control Malfunction**
Description/Symptoms: The climate control system may malfunction, causing problems with heating, cooling, or air distribution.
Solution: Diagnose and repair the faulty component in the climate control system.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
Recalls: None Found

**Cruise Control Inoperative**
Description/Symptoms: The cruise control system may become inoperable, preventing the vehicle from maintaining a set speed.
Solution: Replace the defective component in the cruise control system.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
Recalls: None Found

### Summary

The 2015 Volvo S80 is a generally reliable vehicle, but it has been reported to have some potential issues, particularly with its engine and transmission. The severity of these issues can vary, and the cost to fix them can range from minor to major. However, it's important to note that these issues do not affect all S80 models and may not be widespread. If you are considering purchasing a used 2015 Volvo S80, it is recommended to have it inspected by a qualified mechanic to determine any potential problems.
