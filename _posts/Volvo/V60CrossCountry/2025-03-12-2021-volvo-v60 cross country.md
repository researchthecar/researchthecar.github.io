The 2021 Volvo V60 Cross Country is a stylish and capable wagon known for its comfortable ride, luxurious interior, and standard all-wheel drive. It's powered by a 2.0-liter turbocharged and supercharged four-cylinder engine producing 250 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque. This engine is paired with an 8-speed automatic transmission. People generally appreciate the V60 Cross Country for its distinctive design, comfortable seats, and practicality. Some common complaints include the somewhat complex infotainment system and limited rear-seat legroom compared to some competitors.

**Engine**

**2.0L Turbocharged and Supercharged Four-Cylinder**

*   **Oil Consumption**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported higher than expected oil consumption, requiring them to add oil between scheduled oil changes.
    *   Solution: Monitoring oil levels regularly and topping off as needed. In some cases, a software update or engine component replacement (such as piston rings or valve stem seals) may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $5000+ depending on the cause
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Coolant Leak**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners reported coolant leaking from the coolant reservoir.
    *   Solution: Replacing the coolant reservoir.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**8-Speed Automatic**

*   **Rough Shifting**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported occasional rough or jerky shifting, especially during low-speed maneuvers.
    *   Solution: A software update to the transmission control module (TCM) may resolve the issue. In some cases, a transmission fluid flush or valve body replacement might be required.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $2000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

No Issues Found

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Freezing/Lagging**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The Sensus infotainment system can sometimes freeze, lag, or become unresponsive.
    *   Solution: Performing a system reset or software update. In some cases, the infotainment unit may need to be replaced.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 (software update) - $1500+ (unit replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Sensor Malfunctions**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Various sensors (e.g., parking sensors, blind spot monitoring) may occasionally malfunction, displaying error messages or failing to operate correctly.
    *   Solution: Diagnosing the faulty sensor and replacing it.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+ per sensor
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Sunroof Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Sunroof may not open/close properly.
    *   Solution: Replacing the sunroof motor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Creaking/Rattling Noises**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported creaking or rattling noises emanating from the dashboard, door panels, or seats.
    *   Solution: Identifying and addressing the source of the noise. This may involve tightening loose fasteners, applying lubricant, or replacing worn components.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $500+ depending on complexity

**Summary**

The 2021 Volvo V60 Cross Country appears to have a mix of minor and potentially more significant issues. The electrical gremlins within the infotainment system and sensor malfunctions are the most prevalent issues. Oil consumption and rough shifting, while less common, can be more costly to address. Overall, while not severely plagued by issues, prospective buyers should be aware of these potential problem areas and factor them into their purchasing decision.
