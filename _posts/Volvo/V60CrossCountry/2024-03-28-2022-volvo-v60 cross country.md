**Introduction:**
The 2022 Volvo V60 Cross Country is a compact luxury crossover with a rugged appearance and all-wheel drive capability. It features a 2.0L turbocharged inline-four engine that produces 250 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque. The engine is paired with an eight-speed automatic transmission. The V60 Cross Country is praised for its comfortable ride, spacious interior, and advanced safety features. However, it has received some complaints regarding certain issues.

**Engine**

**2.0L Turbocharged Inline-Four Engine**

* **Premature Engine Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Engine failure occurring prematurely, often before reaching 100,000 miles. Symptoms may include excessive oil consumption, knocking sounds, and loss of power.
    * Solution: Engine replacement
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $6,000 - $10,000+
    * Recalls: Recalls NHTSA 22V-287 and NHTSA 22V-288 are related to engine issues.

**Transmission**

**Eight-Speed Automatic Transmission**

* **Transmission Shuddering**
    * Description/Symptoms: Transmission shuddering or jerking during gear shifts, especially during low-speed maneuvers or climbing hills.
    * Solution: Transmission fluid flush or replacement
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $500
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Premature Suspension Wear**
    * Description/Symptoms: Excessive wear and tear on suspension components, leading to creaking and rattling noises, instability while driving, and difficulty maintaining alignment.
    * Solution: Suspension component replacement (e.g., struts, springs, control arms)
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+ per component
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain**
    * Description/Symptoms: Battery draining prematurely, even when the vehicle is not in use.
    * Solution: Repairing or replacing faulty electrical components, such as the alternator or battery
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Wind Noise**
    * Description/Symptoms: Excessive wind noise entering the cabin from around the windows or doors.
    * Solution: Replacing or adjusting window seals or door weatherstripping
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Infotainment System Lag**
    * Description/Symptoms: Slow and sluggish response from the infotainment system, including delayed inputs and difficulty navigating menus.
    * Solution: Updating software or replacing the infotainment unit
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**
The 2022 Volvo V60 Cross Country has received some complaints regarding engine failure, premature suspension wear, and occasional electrical issues. While some of these issues can be costly to fix, the overall reliability of the vehicle is generally considered good. It is recommended to perform regular maintenance and address any issues promptly to avoid further damage and additional expenses.
