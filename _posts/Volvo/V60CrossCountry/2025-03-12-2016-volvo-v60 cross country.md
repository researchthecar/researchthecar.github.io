The 2016 Volvo V60 Cross Country is a versatile and stylish wagon known for its comfortable ride, ample cargo space, and standard all-wheel drive. The primary engine option for this model year is the turbocharged 2.0-liter four-cylinder engine, producing around 240 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque, paired with an eight-speed automatic transmission. Some owners appreciate its unique blend of practicality and luxury, while others find the infotainment system a bit dated and the fuel economy not as impressive as some competitors.

**Engine**

**2.0L Turbocharged Four-Cylinder (T5)**

*   **Excessive Oil Consumption**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported higher than expected oil consumption, requiring frequent top-offs between oil changes. Symptoms include low oil level warnings and the need to add oil every few thousand miles.
    *   Solution: In some cases, replacing the piston rings has resolved the issue. In other cases, the PCV valve may be faulty and need replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $5,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

* **Faulty Oil Trap**
    * Description/Symptoms: A faulty oil trap, part of the PCV system, can cause a vacuum leak, rough idling, and potentially lead to oil leaks around engine seals.
    * Solution: Replacing the oil trap.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**Aisin Warner 8-Speed Automatic**

*   **Harsh Shifting/Hesitation**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have experienced harsh or jerky shifting, particularly between lower gears. Hesitation during acceleration has also been reported.
    *   Solution: In some cases, a software update may resolve the issue. In other cases, replacing the valve body or complete transmission replacement may be needed.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 (software update) - $4,000+ (transmission replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Torque Converter Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The torque converter can sometimes exhibit shuddering or vibrations, particularly at low speeds or during light acceleration.
    *   Solution: Replacing the torque converter or flushing the transmission fluid.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $2,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Rear Suspension Noisy**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rear suspension components, such as the shocks or bushings, may develop noise (squeaks, clunks) over time, especially when driving over bumps.
    *   Solution: Replacing the worn shocks, struts, or bushings.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+ per corner
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Glitches**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The infotainment system (Sensus) can experience glitches, freezes, or slow response times.
    *   Solution: Software updates may resolve some issues. In severe cases, the infotainment unit may need to be replaced.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 (software update) - $1,000+ (unit replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Sensor Failures (Parking Sensors, Blind Spot Monitoring)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Parking sensors or blind spot monitoring systems can sometimes fail or provide inaccurate readings.
    *   Solution: Diagnosing and replacing the faulty sensor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+ per sensor
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Battery Drain**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Premature battery drain issues reported due to modules not properly going into sleep mode.
    *   Solution: Identify the parasitic drain and repair the source or replace the module causing the parasitic drain.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200-$1,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Sunroof Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Sunroof may leak or become stuck.
    *   Solution: Clean sunroof drain or replace sunroof motor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Cracked Dashboard (Limited Instances)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: In rare cases, owners in very hot climates have reported cracking of the dashboard material.
    *   Solution: Replacement of dashboard
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Power Seat Malfunctions**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Power seats may not adjust properly, or the motor might fail, leaving the seat stuck in one position.
    *   Solution: Replacing the seat motor or the seat control module.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2016 Volvo V60 Cross Country, while a generally reliable vehicle, has some common issues that prospective buyers should be aware of. The most concerning issues revolve around the engine's oil consumption and potential transmission problems. Electrical glitches and suspension noises are also fairly common. Repair costs can range from minor software updates to major engine or transmission overhauls, potentially reaching several thousand dollars. Checking service history and performing a thorough inspection before purchase is highly recommended.
