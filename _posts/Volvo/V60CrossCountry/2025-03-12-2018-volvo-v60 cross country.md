The 2018 Volvo V60 Cross Country is a stylish and capable wagon known for its comfortable ride, Scandinavian design, and focus on safety. It's offered with a 2.0-liter turbocharged and supercharged four-cylinder engine (T6) producing 302 horsepower and 295 lb-ft of torque, paired with an 8-speed automatic transmission. While owners appreciate the car's distinctive look and comfortable interior, some have reported issues across various components. Potential buyers should be aware of these common complaints.

**Engine**

**2.0L Turbocharged and Supercharged (T6)**

* **Oil Consumption**
    * Description/Symptoms: Excessive oil consumption requiring frequent top-offs between oil changes. Owners may notice a low oil level warning light.
    * Solution: Updated piston ring design and software update to address oil consumption issues.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $5,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Coolant Leaks**
    * Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaks originating from the water pump or coolant hoses, leading to low coolant levels and potential overheating.
    * Solution: Replacement of the faulty water pump or coolant hoses.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**8-Speed Automatic**

* **Rough Shifting/Hesitation**
    * Description/Symptoms: Harsh or jerky shifting between gears, especially during low-speed acceleration. Hesitation or delays when shifting from park to drive or reverse.
    * Solution: Software updates to the transmission control module (TCM) or replacement of valve body. Fluid change may also resolve the issues.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $2,500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Rear Air Suspension Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: The rear air suspension can experience failures causing sagging, uneven ride height, or complete deflation. This often leads to a rough ride and impacts vehicle handling.
    * Solution: Replacement of the air springs, air compressor, or ride height sensors.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $2,500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Sensus Infotainment System Problems**
    * Description/Symptoms: The Sensus infotainment system can experience freezing, lagging, reboots, and connectivity issues. This may affect navigation, audio, climate control, and other vehicle functions.
    * Solution: Software updates, system resets, or replacement of the infotainment unit.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 - $1,500+ (depending on warranty and troubleshooting)
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Battery Drain**
    * Description/Symptoms: Premature battery drain, leading to difficulty starting the vehicle, especially after short periods of inactivity.
    * Solution: Diagnostic testing to identify parasitic drains, followed by replacement of faulty components such as relays, modules, or the battery itself.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **No Issues Found**

**Interior**

* **Sunroof/Moonroof Malfunction**
    * Description/Symptoms: Sunroof or moonroof failing to open or close properly, getting stuck, or exhibiting excessive noise during operation.
    * Solution: Lubrication of the tracks, adjustment of the mechanism, or replacement of the sunroof motor or related components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2018 Volvo V60 Cross Country, while generally reliable, has some common issues that prospective buyers should be aware of. Engine-related problems, specifically oil consumption and coolant leaks, can be costly to repair. Transmission issues like rough shifting might require software updates or more significant repairs. Electrical glitches with the infotainment system and battery drain are also common. The severity and cost of these issues vary, with some being relatively minor and others potentially leading to substantial repair bills. Overall, pre-purchase inspections and a thorough review of service records are recommended.
