**2021 Volvo V60 Cross Country**

The 2021 Volvo V60 Cross Country is a luxury station wagon that combines rugged off-road capabilities with a premium interior. It offers a variety of engine and transmission options, including a 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine with 250 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque, an optional 2.0-liter turbocharged and supercharged four-cylinder engine with 316 horsepower and 295 lb-ft of torque, and a plug-in hybrid option that combines a 2.0-liter turbocharged and supercharged four-cylinder engine with an electric motor for a total of 400 horsepower and 472 lb-ft of torque. All engines are paired with an eight-speed automatic transmission.

**Engine**

**2.0-Liter Turbocharged Four-Cylinder**
* **Intake Valve Carbon Buildup**
    * Description/Symptoms: Reduced engine power, rough idling, decreased fuel efficiency
    * Solution: Intake valve cleaning
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1000
    * Recalls: None Found

**2.0-Liter Turbocharged and Supercharged Four-Cylinder**
* **High-Pressure Fuel Pump Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Engine stalling, difficulty starting, reduced engine power
    * Solution: Replace high-pressure fuel pump
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1200 - $1800+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**Eight-Speed Automatic Transmission**
* **Shift Solenoid Malfunction**
    * Description/Symptoms: Harsh shifting, delayed shifts, transmission slipping
    * Solution: Replace shift solenoid
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $600 - $1200+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Front Strut Mount Bearing Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Knocking or clunking noises from the front suspension, especially over bumps
    * Solution: Replace strut mount bearing
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Battery Discharge**
    * Description/Symptoms: Dead battery after periods of inactivity
    * Solution: Replace battery or diagnose parasitic draw
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Sunroof Wind Noise**
    * Description/Symptoms: Excessive wind noise from the sunroof at high speeds
    * Solution: Adjust or replace sunroof seal
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Climate Control Malfunction**
    * Description/Symptoms: Climate control does not adjust temperature properly, air flow is weak or non-existent
    * Solution: Replace climate control module or actuator
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1200+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

Overall, the 2021 Volvo V60 Cross Country has a mixed record in terms of reliability. While some issues, such as carbon buildup and transmission problems, are relatively common, others, such as exterior and interior issues, are less frequent. The cost to fix these issues can vary significantly depending on the severity of the problem and the location of the repair.
