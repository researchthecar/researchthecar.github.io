The 2023 Volvo V60 Cross Country is a stylish and capable wagon known for its comfortable ride, spacious interior, and standard all-wheel drive. It is available with a B5 mild-hybrid 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine producing 247 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque, paired with an 8-speed automatic transmission. Owners often praise the V60 Cross Country for its elegant design, comfortable seats, and ample cargo space. Criticisms tend to center around the infotainment system's complexity and some reported reliability concerns.

**Engine**

**B5 2.0L Turbocharged I4 Mild-Hybrid**

*   **Oil Consumption**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported higher than expected oil consumption, requiring more frequent oil top-offs between scheduled services. The amount of oil loss will vary between vehicles
    *   Solution: Check oil levels regularly and top off as needed. If consumption is excessive, consult with a Volvo technician to check for leaks or internal engine issues.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies greatly depending on the cause.

*   **Coolant Leak**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Owners reporting slow coolant leaks from various points in the cooling system. Coolant may be visible on the ground under the engine, or a coolant warning light may appear on the dashboard.
    *   Solution: A thorough inspection by a qualified technician is needed to locate and repair the leak. This may involve replacing hoses, clamps, or other components.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $1000+ depending on leak location.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**8-Speed Automatic**

*   **Rough Shifting**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported harsh or jerky shifting, particularly during low-speed acceleration or deceleration.
    *   Solution: Some improvements may come from a transmission software update, but more significant issues might require valve body replacement or transmission repair.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $400 for software; $800+ for valve body or other repairs.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **No Issues Found**

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Freezing/Lagging**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The Sensus Connect infotainment system can be slow to respond, freeze intermittently, or experience glitches with the navigation or audio functions.
    *   Solution: Software updates from Volvo can sometimes address these issues. In more severe cases, the infotainment unit may need to be replaced.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 for software updates; $1000+ for unit replacement.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Sensor Malfunction**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Multiple sensors related to safety systems such as blind spot monitoring, adaptive cruise control, or parking assist can malfunction and trigger false alerts or system failures.
    *   Solution: A dealer technician should diagnose the malfunctioning sensor using diagnostic equipment and replace the faulty sensor as needed.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+ depending on the sensor and labor.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **No Issues Found**

**Interior**

*   **Sunroof/Moonroof Rattling or Squeaking**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Owners report rattling or squeaking noises coming from the sunroof area, particularly when driving over uneven surfaces.
    *   Solution: Lubricating the sunroof tracks and seals may resolve minor noises. More significant issues might require adjusting or replacing the sunroof components.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $500+ depending on the issue.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2023 Volvo V60 Cross Country generally receives positive reviews, but some owners have encountered issues with the infotainment system, transmission, sensors and minor coolant leaks. The severity and cost of these issues vary, with infotainment problems potentially requiring expensive unit replacements.
