## 2017 Volvo V60 Cross Country

The 2017 Volvo V60 Cross Country is a compact luxury station wagon that offers a blend of practicality and performance. It is available with a turbocharged 2.0-liter four-cylinder engine producing 240 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque or a turbocharged and supercharged 2.0-liter four-cylinder engine producing 316 horsepower and 295 lb-ft of torque. Both engines are paired with an eight-speed automatic transmission.

The V60 Cross Country is known for its comfortable interior, spacious cargo area, and advanced safety features. However, it has also been plagued by a number of issues, including engine and transmission problems.

**Engine**

**2.0-liter Turbocharged Four-Cylinder**

* **Oil Consumption**
    * Description/Symptoms: Excessive oil consumption, leading to low oil levels and potential engine damage.
    * Solution: Engine rebuild or replacement.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $5,000 - $10,000+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Timing Chain Tensioner Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Failure of the timing chain tensioner, leading to rattling noises, engine performance issues, and potential catastrophic engine failure.
    * Solution: Replacement of the timing chain tensioner and related components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**2.0-liter Turbocharged and Supercharged Four-Cylinder**

* **Turbocharger Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Failure of the turbocharger, leading to decreased engine performance, excessive smoke, and potential engine damage.
    * Solution: Replacement of the turbocharger.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,500 - $4,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**Eight-Speed Automatic**

* **Shifting Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: Erratic shifting, delayed shifts, or failure to shift gears properly.
    * Solution: Repair or replacement of transmission components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Rear Suspension Bushing Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Failure of the rear suspension bushings, leading to excessive noise, poor handling, and premature tire wear.
    * Solution: Replacement of the rear suspension bushings.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain**
    * Description/Symptoms: Excessive battery drain, leading to dead batteries or difficulty starting the vehicle.
    * Solution: Diagnosis and repair of electrical components, software updates, or battery replacement.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Headlight Condensation**
    * Description/Symptoms: Moisture buildup inside the headlights, leading to poor visibility and potential damage to the headlight assembly.
    * Solution: Replacement of the headlight assembly.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Climate Control Malfunction**
    * Description/Symptoms: Malfunctioning climate control system, leading to improper heating or cooling.
    * Solution: Diagnosis and repair or replacement of the climate control components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2017 Volvo V60 Cross Country has a number of potential issues that can affect its reliability and performance. The most severe and costly problems include oil consumption, timing chain tensioner failure, and turbocharger failure. While some issues can be resolved through recalls or software updates, others may require significant repairs or even replacement of major components.
