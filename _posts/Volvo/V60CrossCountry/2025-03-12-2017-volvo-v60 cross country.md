The 2017 Volvo V60 Cross Country is a versatile wagon known for its blend of comfort, safety, and all-weather capability. It offers a raised ride height and standard all-wheel drive, making it suitable for various road conditions. The most common engine option is the 2.0-liter turbocharged and supercharged four-cylinder engine, producing 240 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque. A six-speed automatic transmission is paired with the 2.0L engine. While owners appreciate its distinctive styling, comfortable interior, and safety features, some common complaints include electrical glitches and occasional issues with the infotainment system.

**Engine**

**2.0L Turbocharged and Supercharged Four-Cylinder**

*   **Oil Consumption**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported excessive oil consumption, requiring more frequent oil top-ups between scheduled oil changes. Symptoms include low oil level warnings and potentially engine knocking or damage if not addressed promptly.
    *   Solution: Regular monitoring of oil levels and addressing any leaks or underlying issues such as piston ring problems or PCV valve failure.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: varies greatly depending on the cause and extent of damage, ranging from PCV valve replacement ($150-$300) to piston ring repair or engine replacement ($3,000-$7,000+).
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Coolant Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaks can occur from various points in the cooling system, including the water pump, radiator, or hoses. Signs include a sweet smell, visible leaks under the vehicle, and overheating.
    *   Solution: Locating and repairing the source of the leak, which may involve replacing hoses, the water pump, or the radiator.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $1000+ depending on the leak

**Transmission**

**Aisin Warner Six-Speed Automatic**

*   **Rough Shifting**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have experienced rough or jerky shifting, particularly when the transmission is cold or between certain gears.
    *   Solution: In some cases, a software update can improve shifting performance. If the issue persists, a transmission fluid flush or, in more severe cases, valve body replacement may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: Software update ($100-$200), fluid flush ($200-$400), valve body replacement ($1,000-$2,000+).
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

No Issues Found

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Glitches**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The Sensus infotainment system can experience occasional glitches, including freezing, slow response times, or connectivity issues with Bluetooth or Apple CarPlay/Android Auto.
    *   Solution: Software updates are the most common solution, but in some cases, a system reset or hardware replacement may be required.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: Software update (free or $100-$200 at a dealership), hardware replacement ($500-$1500+)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Sensor Malfunctions**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Various sensors, such as those related to the parking assist system or blind spot monitoring, may malfunction, leading to warning lights and non-functional features.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis to identify the faulty sensor and replacement of the affected sensor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200-$600 per sensor, depending on location and type.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Sunroof Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Sunroofs may leak or have issues with opening and closing.
    *   Solution: Cleaning and lubricating the sunroof tracks and seals, replacing damaged seals, or repairing/replacing the sunroof motor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $800+ depending on the repair.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Navigation System Errors**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Navigation system freezing, incorrect directions, or failure to locate addresses.
    *   Solution: Updating the navigation software or replacing the navigation system module.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: Software update ($100-$200), Module replacement ($800-$1,500+).
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2017 Volvo V60 Cross Country generally exhibits a mix of minor and moderate issues. Electrical glitches, particularly with the infotainment system, are relatively common but often resolved with software updates. More concerning are potential engine issues such as excessive oil consumption or coolant leaks, which can lead to significant repair costs if left unaddressed. Transmission issues, such as rough shifting, can also occur. Overall, while the V60 Cross Country is a desirable vehicle, potential buyers should be aware of these common problems and factor in potential repair costs when considering a purchase.
