# 2017 Volvo V60 Cross Country

### Overview
The 2017 Volvo V60 Cross Country is a premium compact wagon known for its blend of Scandinavian design, safety, and all-wheel-drive capability. Built on Volvo's robust platform, it offers a comfortable and refined driving experience, particularly suited for those seeking a versatile vehicle that can handle light off-road conditions and inclement weather. Reliability for the 2017 model year is generally strong, reflecting Volvo's commitment to quality engineering. While most owners report a positive ownership experience with minimal unexpected repairs, like any vehicle, certain components can occasionally present issues. This analysis focuses on documented, non-wear-and-tear related concerns across key automotive systems.

**Occasional Glitches/Freezing**

**Intermittent Battery Drain**

- **Solution:** Diagnosis to identify parasitic draw from specific modules or accessories. May require software updates or component replacement.

### Summary Verdict
The 2017 Volvo V60 Cross Country demonstrates a commendable level of reliability, with the powertrain and chassis components generally performing without significant issues. The most commonly cited concerns tend to be related to the electronic systems, specifically the infotainment unit experiencing occasional glitches and a less frequent issue of intermittent battery drain. These electrical gremlins, while not pervasive, are the primary areas where owners might encounter unexpected repair needs. Overall, the V60 Cross Country is a solid choice for those prioritizing safety and capability, with a strong likelihood of dependable service when basic maintenance is performed.
