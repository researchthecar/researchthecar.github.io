# 2019 Volvo V60 Cross Country

### Overview
The 2019 Volvo V60 Cross Country is a premium wagon that blends Scandinavian design with robust capability. Known for its safety features and comfortable ride, it aims to offer a refined driving experience with the added benefit of all-wheel drive and increased ground clearance for light off-road excursions. Reliability for this model year is generally strong, reflecting Volvo's commitment to quality and durability. Owners often praise its sophisticated interior and advanced technology. While most systems perform well, like any vehicle, there are specific areas where minor concerns have been noted, primarily related to electronics and some powertrain components under certain conditions. Overall, the V60 Cross Country presents a compelling option for those seeking a versatile and safe luxury vehicle.

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged I4 (T5)
**Coolant Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Visible coolant puddles under the vehicle, low coolant level warning light, engine overheating.
- **Solution:** Inspection and replacement of leaking hoses, seals, or the water pump.
- **Cost:** $300 - $700

**Turbocharger Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of power, whistling noises from the engine bay, increased exhaust smoke.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and potential repair or replacement of the turbocharger assembly.
- **Cost:** $1,200 - $2,500


### 2.0L Turbocharged & Supercharged I4 (T6 - if applicable in market)
**Carbon Buildup on Intake Valves**
- **Symptoms:** Rough idle, reduced engine performance, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Intake manifold cleaning or walnut blasting service.
- **Cost:** $400 - $800


## Transmission

### 8-Speed Geartronic Automatic
**Hesitation or Jerking During Shifts**
- **Symptoms:** Noticeable delays or rough transitions between gears, especially at lower speeds.
- **Solution:** Software update for the transmission control module or fluid flush and filter replacement.
- **Cost:** $200 - $600

### Summary Verdict
The 2019 Volvo V60 Cross Country demonstrates good overall reliability, with its powertrain and chassis components generally performing as expected. The most frequently reported issues tend to be minor and often related to the advanced electrical systems, particularly the infotainment unit, and occasional engine-related concerns like coolant leaks or turbocharger performance under specific conditions. Transmission shifts can sometimes be less smooth than ideal, but this is often rectifiable with software updates or maintenance. For owners, proactive maintenance and addressing any early warning signs, especially with the electrical systems, will contribute to a consistently positive ownership experience. The V60 Cross Country remains a solid choice for those prioritizing safety, comfort, and versatility.
