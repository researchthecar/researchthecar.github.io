The 2019 Volvo V60 Cross Country is a stylish and capable wagon known for its comfortable ride, spacious interior, and standard all-wheel drive. One popular engine option for this model year is the T5 Drive-E engine, a 2.0-liter turbocharged inline-4 producing 250 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque. This engine is paired with an 8-speed automatic transmission. Owners appreciate the car's elegant design, comfortable seats, and advanced safety features. Some common dislikes include the somewhat complicated infotainment system and the relatively high price point.

**Engine**

**2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4 (T5 Drive-E)**

* **Oil Consumption**
    * Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported excessive oil consumption, requiring frequent top-offs between oil changes. The exact cause can vary, but possibilities include worn piston rings or valve seals. Symptoms include needing to add oil more frequently than expected, blue smoke from the exhaust, and a low oil level warning light.
    * Solution: The solution can range from using a higher viscosity oil to replacing the piston rings or valve seals, depending on the severity. A leakdown test can help determine the source of the oil consumption.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $5,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Coolant Leak**
    * Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported coolant leaks in the system, resulting in the low coolant level warning.
    * Solution: Replace the leaking components such as the water pump or coolant reservoir.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**Aisin Warner 8-Speed Automatic (AW TG-81SC)**

* **Rough Shifting/Hesitation**
    * Description/Symptoms: Some owners have experienced rough or delayed shifting, particularly between certain gears. This can manifest as a noticeable "clunk" or a hesitation when accelerating.
    * Solution: In some cases, a software update or transmission fluid flush can resolve the issue. In more severe cases, valve body replacement or transmission overhaul may be necessary.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $4,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **No Issues Found**

**Electrical**

* **Infotainment System Freezing/Crashing**
    * Description/Symptoms: The Sensus infotainment system can sometimes freeze, crash, or become unresponsive. This can affect navigation, audio, and climate control functions.
    * Solution: A software update can sometimes resolve the issue. In other cases, the infotainment unit may need to be replaced.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 (software update) - $1,500+ (unit replacement)
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Battery Drain**
    * Description/Symptoms: Premature battery drain, leading to difficulty starting the vehicle, especially after periods of inactivity.
    * Solution: Identify the source of the parasitic draw (e.g., a faulty module or accessory) and repair or replace it. Could be as simple as replacing the battery.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Sunroof Issues (Water Leaks/Malfunctions)**
    * Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported water leaks around the sunroof or issues with the sunroof not opening or closing properly.
    * Solution: Cleaning the sunroof drains, resealing the sunroof, or replacing the sunroof motor or mechanism.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Software Problems**
    * Description/Symptoms: Connectivity issues with Apple CarPlay or Android Auto. Intermittent issues with the driver-assist systems.
    * Solution: Software update and module reset if the problem persists.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 - $500
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

The 2019 Volvo V60 Cross Country, while generally a reliable and well-regarded vehicle, can be prone to issues affecting the engine, transmission, electrical system, and sunroof. Engine issues, especially excessive oil consumption, could potentially lead to costly repairs. Infotainment system glitches and premature battery drain are other areas of concern. While most issues are not inherently severe, the cost to repair them can be substantial, especially for complex electronic systems and engine-related problems.
