The 2007 Volvo C70 is a stylish and versatile hardtop convertible known for its safety, comfort, and refined driving experience. It offers a blend of open-air enjoyment and coupe practicality. The 2007 C70 came with a single engine option: a 2.5L turbocharged inline-5 cylinder engine producing 218 horsepower and 236 lb-ft of torque. This engine was paired with either a 5-speed automatic or a 6-speed manual transmission. Owners often appreciate the C70's elegant design, comfortable interior, and solid build quality. Common dislikes include some reliability concerns related to electrical components and certain engine parts, as well as relatively high maintenance costs.

**Engine**

**2.5L Turbocharged Inline-5 (B5254T7)**

* **Oil Leaks**
    * Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks from various locations, including the rear main seal, valve cover gasket, and turbocharger lines. Visible oil drips under the car, burning oil smell, and low oil level.
    * Solution: Replace the faulty seals and gaskets. Address any leaks from the turbocharger lines by replacing the lines or tightening the fittings.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $1,000+ depending on location and severity.
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Turbocharger Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: Whining or screeching noises from the turbocharger, loss of power, and potentially a check engine light. Can be caused by wastegate malfunction, bearing failure, or oil starvation.
    * Solution: Diagnose the specific cause of the turbocharger issue. Repair or replace the turbocharger as needed. Replace any damaged oil lines.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **PCV System Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Rough idle, oil leaks, and whistling noises from the engine. A failed PCV (Positive Crankcase Ventilation) system can cause increased pressure within the engine, leading to gasket failure and oil leaks.
    * Solution: Replace the PCV valve and any related components, such as hoses and the oil trap.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**Aisin AW55-50/51SN 5-Speed Automatic**

* **Valve Body Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: Harsh or delayed shifting, slipping between gears, and a general lack of smooth operation.
    * Solution: Replace the valve body with a new or remanufactured unit. Flushing the transmission fluid regularly may help prevent this.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $2,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Transmission Fluid Leaks**
    * Description/Symptoms: Reddish fluid leaking from the transmission, difficulty shifting, and potential damage to internal components.
    * Solution: Identify the source of the leak (seals, lines, or the transmission pan) and replace the damaged components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Strut Mount Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Clunking noises from the front suspension, especially when going over bumps. Can also cause vague steering and uneven tire wear.
    * Solution: Replace the strut mounts. Replacing the struts at the same time is recommended.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+ per axle.
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Control Arm Bushing Wear**
    * Description/Symptoms: Loose steering, vibrations, and clunking noises, especially when braking or accelerating.
    * Solution: Replace the worn control arm bushings or the entire control arm assembly.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600+ per control arm.
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Convertible Top Malfunctions**
    * Description/Symptoms: The convertible top failing to open or close properly. Can be caused by issues with the hydraulic pump, sensors, or wiring.
    * Solution: Diagnose the root cause of the issue using a diagnostic scan tool. Repair or replace the faulty components, such as the hydraulic pump, sensors, or wiring harness.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $2,000+ depending on the complexity of the repair.
    * Recalls: None Found

* **DIM (Driver Information Module) Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Display problems, such as flickering or complete loss of information, and potential warning lights illuminating incorrectly.
    * Solution: Have the DIM diagnosed and repaired, or replaced if necessary. Some independent shops specialize in repairing Volvo DIMs.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Power Window Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: Windows failing to go up or down, or operating erratically.
    * Solution: Diagnosing the root of the issue includes checking the window motor, the window regulator, and the window switch.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $500
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Convertible Top Seal Leaks**
    * Description/Symptoms: Water leaks into the cabin, especially during rain or car washes. Can lead to mold and mildew growth.
    * Solution: Replace the worn or damaged convertible top seals. Adjust the top alignment for a tighter seal.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $800+ depending on the seals that need replacing and the labor involved.
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Sun Visor Sagging**
    * Description/Symptoms: Sun visors that do not stay in the up position and constantly fall down, obstructing the driver's view.
    * Solution: Replace the sun visors.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $200+ per visor.
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2007 Volvo C70, while stylish and enjoyable, has a few common problem areas. Engine issues, particularly oil leaks and turbocharger problems, can be costly to address. Electrical problems, especially those related to the convertible top mechanism and DIM, are also frequently reported. While not every C70 will experience all of these issues, prospective buyers should be aware of these potential problems and factor them into their ownership costs. Overall, the cost of ownership can be moderate to high depending on the condition and maintenance history of the vehicle.
