**Introduction:**

The 2012 Volvo C70 is a stylish and sporty convertible that offers a comfortable and refined driving experience. It is available with a variety of engine and transmission options, including a 2.5-liter inline-5 engine with 227 horsepower and 236 lb-ft of torque, a 3.0-liter inline-6 engine with 300 horsepower and 271 lb-ft of torque, and a 2.5-liter turbocharged inline-5 engine with 227 horsepower and 236 lb-ft of torque. Transmission options include a 6-speed manual transmission, a 5-speed automatic transmission, and a 6-speed automatic transmission.

People who like the 2012 Volvo C70 praise its stylish design, comfortable interior, and responsive handling. However, some people have experienced issues with the vehicle's engine, transmission, suspension, electrical system, exterior, and interior.

**Engine**

**2.5-liter Inline-5 Engine**

* **Intake manifold gasket failure:** Causes coolant and oil leaks, engine overheating, and loss of power.
    * Solution: Replace the intake manifold gasket.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**3.0-liter Inline-6 Engine**

* **Valve cover gasket leak:** Causes oil leaks and a burning oil smell.
    * Solution: Replace the valve cover gasket.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500
    * Recalls: None Found

**2.5-liter Turbocharged Inline-5 Engine**

* **Turbocharger failure:** Causes a loss of power, engine knocking, and excessive smoke from the exhaust.
    * Solution: Replace the turbocharger.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**6-speed Manual Transmission**

* **Clutch failure:** Causes difficulty shifting gears, slipping gears, and a grinding noise when shifting.
    * Solution: Replace the clutch.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
    * Recalls: None Found

**5-speed Automatic Transmission**

* **Transmission slipping:** Causes the transmission to hesitate or slip when shifting gears.
    * Solution: Rebuild or replace the transmission.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**6-speed Automatic Transmission**

* **Torque converter failure:** Causes shuddering or vibration during acceleration.
    * Solution: Replace the torque converter.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $1,500
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Front strut mount failure:** Causes a clunking noise when driving over bumps.
    * Solution: Replace the front strut mounts.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Rear shock absorber failure:** Causes a rough ride and difficulty controlling the vehicle over bumps.
    * Solution: Replace the rear shock absorbers.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $600
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Battery drain:** Causes the battery to drain quickly.
    * Solution: Replace the battery and inspect the electrical system for any parasitic drains.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Electrical short:** Causes electrical problems such as blown fuses, flickering lights, and inoperative components.
    * Solution: Repair the electrical short.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Convertible top failure:** Causes the convertible top to not open or close properly.
    * Solution: Replace or repair the convertible top mechanism.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Door latch failure:** Causes the door to not open or close properly.
    * Solution: Replace the door latch.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Climate control failure:** Causes the climate control system to not function properly.
    * Solution: Replace the climate control module or repair the wiring harness.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Instrument cluster failure:** Causes the instrument cluster to malfunction or display incorrect information.
    * Solution: Replace the instrument cluster.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2012 Volvo C70 is a stylish and sporty convertible with a comfortable interior and responsive handling. However, some people have experienced issues with the vehicle's engine, transmission, suspension, electrical system, exterior, and interior. The severity and cost of the issues can vary depending on the specific problem and the part that needs to be replaced or repaired.
