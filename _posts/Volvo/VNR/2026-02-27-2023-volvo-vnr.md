# 2023 Volvo VNR Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

Shopping for a used 2023 Volvo VNR means looking at a modern regional-haul commercial truck designed for efficiency and driver comfort. While commercial fleets and owner-operators generally value its advanced powertrain technology and maneuverability, buyers should carefully evaluate individual maintenance histories and test drive thoroughly to ensure proper operation.

---

## Engine

### Volvo D11 11-Liter Turbo Diesel

The Volvo D11 is an 11-liter inline-six diesel engine typically ranging from 325 to 425 horsepower, optimized for regional distribution and vocational applications. It offers a solid balance of fuel economy and responsive low-end torque for daily commercial duties.

### Volvo D13 13-Liter Turbo Diesel

The larger Volvo D13 is a 13-liter inline-six diesel engine delivering between 405 and 500 horsepower for heavier hauling needs. It is a widely used heavy-duty powerplant known for strong pulling power and broad service support.

## Transmission

### Volvo I-Shift Automated Manual Transmission

The Volvo I-Shift is an automated manual transmission engineered to maximize fuel efficiency and ease driver fatigue in heavy commercial trucks. Buyers should verify proper shifting behavior under stop-and-go driving conditions during a pre-purchase test.

**Unexpected Shifting to Neutral**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle shifts into neutral unexpectedly when slowing down for a turn or stop, failing to re-engage gear until the vehicle comes to a complete stop and is manually shifted.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified commercial truck technician inspect the transmission control module, software calibration, and electronic shift actuators.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V255000: Equipment Adaptive/Mobility:Vehicle Controls:Braking](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volvo&model=VNR&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** Overly aggressive braking can result in the rear axle skidding, the brakes locking up, and/or a loss of vehicle control, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will secure wiring and remove radar antennas and brackets from the vehicle. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V280000: Electrical System:Propulsion System:Traction Battery:Thermal Management:Fluid/Coolant](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volvo&model=VNR&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** The coolant line in the high voltage battery pack may not be fully seated and locked, which can result in the loss of coolant and an electrical short circuit. A battery short circuit increases the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 23V511000: Visibility:Windshield Wiper/Washer:Motor](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volvo&model=VNR&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** Inoperative windshield wipers can reduce visibility, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the windshield wiper motor assembly, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2023 Volvo VNR is a capable regional-haul commercial vehicle, but prospective buyers should inspect powertrain electronics and transmission shifting behavior closely. Conducting a thorough pre-purchase inspection and test drive will help ensure that potential electronic or shifting glitches are identified before purchase.
