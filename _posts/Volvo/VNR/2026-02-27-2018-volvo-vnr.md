# 2018 Volvo VNR Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2018 Volvo VNR is a regional-haul commercial truck designed for maneuverability, efficiency, and driver comfort. When shopping for a used model, buyers should carefully evaluate powertrain performance and overall mechanical condition. Because available public complaint data for this commercial chassis is minimal, a thorough pre-purchase inspection remains essential to ensure the vehicle has been well-maintained in commercial service.

---

## Engine

### Volvo D11

The Volvo D11 is an 11-liter inline-six diesel engine typically rated between 325 and 425 horsepower, optimized for regional distribution and fuel efficiency. It provides a lighter-weight alternative to larger displacement heavy-duty engines while maintaining strong low-end torque.

### Volvo D13

The Volvo D13 is a 12.8-liter inline-six diesel engine offering horsepower ratings ranging from 405 to 500 hp, built for heavier regional applications. It is widely utilized for its robust power delivery and strong integration with proprietary automated manual transmissions.

## Transmission

### Volvo I-Shift 12-Speed Automated Manual

The Volvo I-Shift is a 12-speed automated manual transmission engineered to maximize fuel economy and reduce driver fatigue. It features intelligent shift programming, though buyers should verify proper clutch actuation and shifting smoothness during a test drive.

### Allison Automatic

Certain configurations feature an Allison fully automatic transmission, providing dependable torque conversion and smooth shifting in stop-and-go regional operations. These units are generally known for durability, but fluid maintenance history should be verified.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V509000: Electrical System:Wiring:Fuses And Circuit Breakers](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volvo&model=VNR&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** If the fuse blows the transmission may not shift, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Volvo Trucks will notify owners, and dealers will inspect and replace the fuse, if necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V384000: Equipment:Other:Labels](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volvo&model=VNR&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of 49 CFR Part 567, "Certification." If the rear axles are overloaded, over time the wheel hubs may fail prematurely, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2018 Volvo VNR offers a capable and driver-focused platform for regional hauling operations. While public owner complaints are sparse, commercial vehicles endure heavy duty cycles, making a comprehensive inspection by a qualified diesel technician crucial before finalizing any purchase.
