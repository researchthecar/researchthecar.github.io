# 2020 Volvo VNR Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2020 Volvo VNR is a versatile regional-haul semi-truck designed for maneuverability and efficiency. When shopping for a used model, buyers should inspect the powertrain performance, electrical systems, and routine wear components to ensure maximum on-road reliability.

---

## Engine

### Volvo D11

The Volvo D11 is an 11-liter inline-6 diesel engine offering around 325 to 425 horsepower, providing a balance of fuel efficiency and power for regional routes. It generally offers solid dependability for commercial buyers when properly maintained.

### Volvo D13

The Volvo D13 is a 13-liter inline-6 diesel engine delivering up to 500 horsepower and heavy torque output for demanding regional applications. It is a widely utilized commercial powerplant known for long-haul durability.

## Transmission

### Volvo I-Shift Automated Manual Transmission

The Volvo I-Shift is an automated manual transmission engineered to optimize fuel economy and smooth shifting in commercial operations. It is widely praised for its reliability and driver-friendly operation in regional haul fleets.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V509000: Electrical System:Wiring:Fuses And Circuit Breakers](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volvo&model=VNR&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** If the fuse blows the transmission may not shift, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Volvo Trucks will notify owners, and dealers will inspect and replace the fuse, if necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V510000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volvo&model=VNR&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** The deformation of the air tank strap t-bolts may cause the t-bolt to disconnect from the strap allowing the tank to drop to the ground and become a road hazard, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Volvo Trucks will notify owners, and dealers will inspect and replace the air tank strap, if necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V241000: Steering](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volvo&model=VNR&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** The loose fasteners may result in a loss of vehicle control, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Volvo Trucks will notify owners, and dealers will tighten the steering gear fasteners to specification, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V756000: Steering](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volvo&model=VNR&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** A loose steering gear can cause a loss of vehicle control, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Volvo Trucks will notify owners, and dealers will inspect and re-torque the steering gear to a higher value. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V536000: Service Brakes, Air](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volvo&model=VNR&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 121, "Air Brake Systems." A dislodged brake pedal would be inoperable, increasing a risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Volvo Trucks will notify owners, and dealers will inspect and replace the pedal plates if required, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V384000: Equipment:Other:Labels](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volvo&model=VNR&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of 49 CFR Part 567, "Certification." If the rear axles are overloaded, over time the wheel hubs may fail prematurely, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 23V255000: Equipment Adaptive/Mobility:Vehicle Controls:Braking](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volvo&model=VNR&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** Overly aggressive braking can result in the rear axle skidding, the brakes locking up, and/or a loss of vehicle control, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will secure wiring and remove radar antennas and brackets from the vehicle. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V280000: Electrical System:Propulsion System:Traction Battery:Thermal Management:Fluid/Coolant](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volvo&model=VNR&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** The coolant line in the high voltage battery pack may not be fully seated and locked, which can result in the loss of coolant and an electrical short circuit. A battery short circuit increases the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2020 Volvo VNR is a capable and efficient regional-haul commercial vehicle backed by robust engine and transmission options. Prospective buyers should perform a thorough mechanical and electrical inspection before purchasing to confirm long-term fleet readiness.
