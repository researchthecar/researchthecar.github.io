The 2022 Volvo VAH is a versatile Class 8 truck designed for a variety of heavy-duty applications, including long-haul transport, regional distribution, and vocational tasks. Engine options include the Volvo D11, D13, and D16. The D13 produces up to 500 horsepower and 1850 lb-ft of torque. Drivers appreciate the VAH for its comfortable cabin, fuel efficiency, and advanced safety features. Some common criticisms involve the complexity of the electronics and the higher cost of Volvo-specific parts and service.

**Engine**

**Volvo D13**

*   **Crankshaft Position Sensor Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Engine stalls or fails to start, check engine light illuminates. Diagnostic codes related to crankshaft position sensor are present.
    *   Solution: Replace the crankshaft position sensor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **EGR Valve Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Reduced engine power, increased fuel consumption, black smoke from exhaust, check engine light illuminates.
    *   Solution: Replace the EGR valve.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1500
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Volvo D16**

* **Fuel Injector Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Rough idling, decreased fuel efficiency, misfires, and black smoke.
    * Solution: Replace the failed fuel injector(s).
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1000+ per injector (depending on labor and diagnostics)
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**Volvo I-Shift Automated Manual Transmission**

*   **Shift Actuator Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears, transmission stuck in gear, error codes related to shift actuators.
    *   Solution: Replace or repair the faulty shift actuator(s).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Clutch Slippage**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Engine RPM increases without a corresponding increase in vehicle speed, particularly under load.
    *   Solution: Replace the clutch assembly.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Air Spring Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Vehicle leaning to one side, ride height inconsistent, hissing sound from air leaks.
    *   Solution: Replace the leaking air spring(s).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800 per air spring
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Sensor Failures (Various)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: A wide range of issues can arise, including erratic gauge readings, warning lights, and system malfunctions. Failures can be related to temperature sensors, pressure sensors, or speed sensors.
    *   Solution: Diagnose and replace the faulty sensor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $500+ per sensor (depending on location and complexity)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Multiplexing Module Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Intermittent electrical problems, communication errors between systems, lights flickering, or components not functioning correctly.
    *   Solution: Diagnose and reprogram or replace the multiplexing module.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $2,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Headlight Condensation**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Moisture buildup inside the headlight lenses, reducing visibility.
    *   Solution: Replace the headlight assembly or attempt to reseal it (temporary fix).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800 per headlight assembly
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **HVAC Blend Door Actuator Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Inability to control air temperature, air only blowing hot or cold, clicking noise from the dashboard.
    *   Solution: Replace the faulty blend door actuator.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $700
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

The 2022 Volvo VAH, while generally a reliable truck, experiences common issues mostly in the engine, transmission, electrical, suspension, exterior and interior areas. Common issues include sensor failures, shift actuator failures, and air suspension leaks. The severity of these issues ranges from minor inconveniences to major repairs, with costs ranging from a few hundred to several thousand dollars depending on the problem and the repair needed.
