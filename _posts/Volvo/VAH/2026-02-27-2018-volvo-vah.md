# 2018 Volvo VAH Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2018 Volvo VAH is a specialized auto-hauler commercial tractor designed for efficient vehicle transport. Buyers evaluating a used model should carefully inspect the powertrain options and overall vehicle condition. Because owner-reported data for commercial trucks can vary widely depending on fleet maintenance history, a thorough pre-purchase inspection remains essential.

---

## Engine

### Volvo D11

The Volvo D11 is an 11-liter inline-6 diesel engine offering strong torque and fuel efficiency suited for regional transport and auto hauling. This powerplant is generally well-regarded in commercial fleets when subjected to regular preventative maintenance.

### Volvo D13

The Volvo D13 is a 13-liter inline-6 diesel engine providing higher horsepower and torque ratings for heavy-duty hauling demands. It is a widely utilized heavy-duty commercial engine known for long-term service life under proper care.

## Transmission

### Volvo I-Shift Automated Manual Transmission

The Volvo I-Shift is an automated manual transmission designed to optimize fuel economy and reduce driver fatigue in commercial applications. It is widely praised for smooth shifting and reliability, though software calibrations and clutch wear should be verified on high-mileage used units.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V509000: Electrical System:Wiring:Fuses And Circuit Breakers](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volvo&model=VAH&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** If the fuse blows the transmission may not shift, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Volvo Trucks will notify owners, and dealers will inspect and replace the fuse, if necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2018 Volvo VAH offers capable powertrain choices suited for dedicated auto-transport operations. Prospective buyers should prioritize a comprehensive inspection of both engine and transmission systems, along with service records, to ensure reliable long-term performance.
