The 2019 Volvo VAH series is a line of heavy-duty trucks primarily used for regional haul and vocational applications. These trucks are known for their safety features, driver comfort, and fuel efficiency. Engine options for the 2019 VAH typically include Volvo's D11, D13, and D16 series engines, paired with Volvo I-Shift automated manual transmissions. The D13 engine, for instance, is a popular choice, delivering horsepower ratings from 405 to 500 and torque ranging from 1450 to 1850 lb-ft. Drivers often appreciate the VAH's comfortable cab, smooth ride, and advanced technology, while some common complaints revolve around the complexity of the electronics and potential for costly repairs.

**Engine**

**Volvo D13 Engine**

*   **EGR Cooler Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaks, white smoke from the exhaust, engine overheating, and potential engine damage. The EGR cooler is prone to cracking and leaking internally.
    *   Solution: Replace the EGR cooler. It is also recommended to check the EGR valve for proper function.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Fuel Injector Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rough running engine, poor fuel economy, and potential engine misfires. Can be caused by contaminated fuel or injector wear.
    *   Solution: Diagnose the specific injector(s) causing the issue and replace them. It is recommended to replace all injectors at the same time.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+ per injector
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Volvo D11 Engine**

*   **Turbocharger Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, excessive smoke from the exhaust, and unusual noises from the turbocharger.
    *   Solution: Replace the turbocharger and inspect the oil supply line for any blockages.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,500 - $4,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Volvo D16 Engine**

*   **Engine Oil Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks from various locations on the engine, such as the valve cover gaskets, oil pan gasket, and front/rear main seals.
    *   Solution: Replace the leaking gaskets and seals.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+ depending on the location of the leak.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**Volvo I-Shift Automated Manual Transmission**

*   **Shift Actuator Malfunction**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears, transmission getting stuck in a gear, and error codes related to the shift actuators.
    *   Solution: Replace the faulty shift actuator(s).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Clutch Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Clutch slippage, difficulty engaging gears, and unusual noises coming from the transmission.
    *   Solution: Replace the clutch assembly.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Air Suspension Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The vehicle leaning to one side, suspension not maintaining the correct ride height, and hissing sounds from the air suspension components.
    *   Solution: Locate and repair the air leaks. This may involve replacing air springs, air lines, or leveling valves.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,500+ per component
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Sensor Failures**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Engine not starting, stalling, ABS issues, and other malfunctions can be caused by faulty sensors such as crankshaft position sensors, camshaft position sensors, and ABS sensors.
    *   Solution: Diagnose and replace the faulty sensor(s).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+ per sensor
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Wiring Harness Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Intermittent electrical problems, such as lights not working, gauges not functioning correctly, and error codes related to various systems.
    *   Solution: Inspect and repair or replace the affected wiring harness.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $2,000+ depending on the extent of the damage
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Headlight Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Headlights becoming dim or failing prematurely.
    *   Solution: Replace the headlight bulbs or assemblies.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+ per headlight
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **HVAC System Malfunctions**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Inconsistent heating or cooling, blower motor failure, and issues with the temperature control settings.
    *   Solution: Diagnose and repair or replace the faulty components, such as the blower motor, actuator, or control module.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2019 Volvo VAH, while appreciated for its comfort and technology, exhibits several common issues. Engine problems, particularly with the EGR cooler and fuel injectors, can be costly to repair. The I-Shift transmission may experience problems with its shift actuators. Electrical issues and air suspension leaks are also fairly common. Overall, the severity and cost of repairs can range from a few hundred dollars for sensor replacements to several thousand for major engine or transmission work.
