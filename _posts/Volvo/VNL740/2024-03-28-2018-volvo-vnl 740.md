**Introduction**

The 2018 Volvo VNL 740 is a heavy-duty truck powered by a Volvo D13 engine with 500 horsepower and 1,850 lb-ft of torque. It is also available with D13TC engines producing 425 and 460 horsepower. The VNL 740 comes with a choice of manual or automated manual transmissions in 10-speed, 12-speed, and 13-speed configurations. The truck is praised for its comfort, fuel efficiency, and safety features, but criticisms include a lack of storage space and a complex dashboard.

**Engine**

**D13-500**
- **Turbocharger Failure:** The turbocharger may fail, causing a loss of power and increased fuel consumption. This is fixed by replacing the turbocharger. The estimated cost to fix is $3,000 - $5,000.
- **Coolant Leak:** A coolant leak may occur due to a faulty gasket or cracked hose. This is fixed by replacing the leaking component. The estimated cost to fix is $200 - $500+.
- **Recalls:** NHTSA Campaign Number: 20V297000

**D13-460**
- **Engine Valve Problem:** The engine valves may experience premature wear, leading to reduced engine power and increased fuel consumption. This is fixed by replacing the worn valves. The estimated cost to fix is $2,000 - $4,000.
- **Exhaust Gas Recirculation (EGR) Valve Failure:** The EGR valve may fail, causing increased emissions and performance issues. This is fixed by replacing the EGR valve. The estimated cost to fix is $1,000 - $2,000.

**D13-425**
- **No Issues Found**

**Transmission**

**10-speed Manual**
- **Clutch Failure:** The clutch may slip or fail, causing difficulty shifting gears and transmitting power. This is fixed by replacing the clutch. The estimated cost to fix is $1,500 - $2,500.

**12-speed Automated Manual**
- **Transmission Control Unit (TCU) Failure:** The TCU may fail, causing transmission shifting problems and vehicle stalling. This is fixed by replacing the TCU. The estimated cost to fix is $2,000 - $3,000.
- **Recalls:** NHTSA Campaign Number: 20V313000

**13-speed Manual**
- **No Issues Found**

**Suspension**

- **Rear Airbag Leak:** The rear airbags may develop leaks, causing the truck to sag and affect ride quality. This is fixed by replacing the leaking airbags. The estimated cost to fix is $500 - $1,000.

**Electrical**

- **Battery Discharge:** The battery may discharge prematurely due to electrical drain or a faulty charging system. This is fixed by identifying and repairing the electrical drain or replacing the charging system components. The estimated cost to fix is $200 - $600.

**Exterior**

- **Headlight Condensation:** Moisture may accumulate inside the headlights, affecting their performance. This is fixed by resealing the headlights. The estimated cost to fix is $200 - $400.

**Interior**

- **Dashboard Switch Failure:** Some dashboard switches may fail, causing them to become inoperable. This is fixed by replacing the faulty switches. The estimated cost to fix is $100 - $300.
- **Seat Cushion Wear:** Seat cushions may experience premature wear, especially in high-use areas. This is fixed by replacing the seat cushions. The estimated cost to fix is $400 - $800.

**Summary**

The 2018 Volvo VNL 740 is a reliable truck with some common issues that can be costly to repair. The most severe issues include turbocharger failure, engine valve problems, and transmission control unit failure. However, these issues are not widespread and the cost of maintenance is generally manageable.
