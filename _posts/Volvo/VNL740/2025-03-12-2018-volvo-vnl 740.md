The 2018 Volvo VNL 740 is a popular long-haul semi-truck known for its fuel efficiency, comfortable cabin, and overall reliability. Engine options typically include the Volvo D11, D13, and Cummins X15 engines, while the Volvo I-Shift automated manual transmission is a common choice. Drivers often praise the VNL 740 for its smooth ride, driver-friendly features, and aerodynamic design. However, some common complaints involve electrical issues, sensor failures, and the cost of replacement parts. Specific to the 2018 model year, some owners have reported issues with the aftertreatment system and certain engine components.

**Engine**

**Volvo D13**
* **EGR Valve Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Reduced engine power, increased fuel consumption, black smoke from the exhaust, and a check engine light. Diagnostic codes related to EGR flow or valve position may be present.
    * Solution: Replacement of the EGR valve.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,500+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Diesel Particulate Filter (DPF) Clogging**
    * Description/Symptoms: Frequent regeneration cycles, reduced engine power, increased fuel consumption, and a check engine light. Diagnostic codes related to DPF pressure or soot accumulation may be present.
    * Solution: Forced regeneration or replacement of the DPF.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 (forced regeneration) - $3,000+ (DPF replacement)
    * Recalls: None Found

**Cummins X15**
* **Injector Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Rough idling, misfires, reduced power, increased fuel consumption, and white smoke from the exhaust. Diagnostic codes related to injector performance may be present.
    * Solution: Replacement of the faulty injector(s).
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $600 - $1,200+ (per injector, including labor)
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Turbocharger Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, excessive black smoke, unusual turbocharger noises (whining or screeching), and a check engine light. Diagnostic codes related to turbocharger boost pressure or performance may be present.
    * Solution: Replacement of the turbocharger.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,500 - $5,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**Volvo I-Shift**
* **Shift Hesitation/Rough Shifting**
    * Description/Symptoms: Delays in shifting, jerky or harsh shifts, and difficulty engaging certain gears.
    * Solution: Software update or replacement of the shift solenoids or valve body. In severe cases, transmission replacement may be required.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500 (software update); $800 - $2,000+ (solenoids/valve body); $7,000 - $12,000+ (transmission replacement)
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Clutch Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: Slipping clutch, difficulty engaging gears, and unusual noises during shifting.
    * Solution: Clutch adjustment or replacement.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Air Suspension Leaks**
    * Description/Symptoms: Truck leaning to one side, difficulty maintaining ride height, and hissing sounds from the air suspension components.
    * Solution: Repair or replacement of the leaking air bags, air lines, or leveling valves.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+ (per air bag); $100 - $300+ (per leveling valve)
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Worn Shock Absorbers**
    * Description/Symptoms: Bouncy ride, excessive body roll, and poor handling.
    * Solution: Replacement of the shock absorbers.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+ (per axle)
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Sensor Failures (EGT, NOx, Temperature)**
    * Description/Symptoms: Check engine light, reduced engine performance, and various diagnostic codes related to sensor readings.
    * Solution: Replacement of the faulty sensor.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+ (per sensor)
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Wiring Harness Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: Intermittent electrical problems, such as lights flickering, gauges not working, and error messages on the dashboard.
    * Solution: Repair or replacement of the damaged wiring harness.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Headlight/Taillight Condensation**
    * Description/Symptoms: Moisture buildup inside the headlight or taillight housings, reducing visibility.
    * Solution: Replacement of the affected headlight or taillight assembly.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,000+ (per light)
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **HVAC System Malfunctions**
    * Description/Symptoms: Inconsistent temperature control, weak airflow, and unusual noises from the HVAC system.
    * Solution: Repair or replacement of the blower motor, blend door actuator, or other HVAC components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+
    * Recalls: None Found

In summary, the 2018 Volvo VNL 740 is a generally reliable truck, but owners should be aware of potential issues with the EGR system, DPF, injectors, turbocharger, I-Shift transmission, air suspension, sensors, and electrical wiring. The severity and cost of these issues can vary significantly, with some repairs being relatively minor and others requiring major component replacements. The DPF and transmission issues are probably the most severe and costly to fix.
