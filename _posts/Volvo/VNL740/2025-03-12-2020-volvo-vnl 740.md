The 2020 Volvo VNL 740 is a popular long-haul truck known for its fuel efficiency, comfortable cab, and safety features. Engine options for the 2020 VNL 740 typically include the Volvo D11, D13, and Cummins X15. The Volvo D13 engine, a common choice, produces around 405-500 horsepower and 1450-1850 lb-ft of torque. Transmission options usually consist of Volvo's I-Shift automated manual transmission. Many drivers appreciate the VNL 740's smooth ride and ergonomic design. Some common complaints about the 2020 Volvo VNL 740 include electrical glitches and issues with the aftertreatment system.

**Engine**

**Volvo D13**

* **EGR Cooler Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Coolant leak, white smoke from the exhaust, engine overheating, loss of power, and increased coolant consumption.
    * Solution: Replacement of the EGR cooler.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Diesel Particulate Filter (DPF) Clogging/Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Reduced engine power, frequent regeneration cycles, DPF warning light illuminated, and potential engine shutdown.
    * Solution: DPF cleaning or replacement.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $4,000+ depending on cleaning vs replacement.
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Fuel Injector Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: Rough idling, misfires, reduced fuel economy, and black smoke from the exhaust.
    * Solution: Fuel injector replacement.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+ per injector.
    * Recalls: None Found

**Cummins X15**
* **Turbocharger Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, excessive black smoke, whistling noises from the engine, and increased oil consumption.
    * Solution: Turbocharger replacement.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,500 - $5,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Cylinder Head Cracking**
    * Description/Symptoms: Coolant loss, overheating, white smoke from exhaust, and potential engine misfires.
    * Solution: Cylinder head replacement or repair.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $3,000 - $8,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**Volvo I-Shift**
* **Shift Hesitation/Rough Shifting**
    * Description/Symptoms: Delays in gear changes, jerky or clunky shifts, and difficulty engaging certain gears.
    * Solution: Software update, clutch adjustment, or valve body repair/replacement.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $3,000+ depending on the cause.
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Clutch Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Slipping clutch, difficulty engaging gears, and complete loss of power transfer.
    * Solution: Clutch replacement.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Transmission Control Module (TCM) Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Erratic shifting, inability to shift gears, and transmission warning lights.
    * Solution: TCM replacement and reprogramming.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Air Suspension Leaks**
    * Description/Symptoms: Truck leaning to one side, ride height issues, air compressor running constantly, and hissing sounds.
    * Solution: Repair or replace leaking air bags, airlines, or leveling valves.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,500+ per air bag or component.
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Worn Shocks/Struts**
    * Description/Symptoms: Bouncy ride, poor handling, excessive body roll, and increased tire wear.
    * Solution: Shock absorber or strut replacement.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+ per shock/strut.
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Sensor Failures (EGT, NOx, etc.)**
    * Description/Symptoms: Check engine light, reduced engine performance, and inaccurate readings on gauges.
    * Solution: Sensor replacement.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+ per sensor.
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Wiring Harness Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: Intermittent electrical problems, lights flickering, and difficulty starting the engine.
    * Solution: Wiring harness repair or replacement.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $2,000+ depending on the severity.
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Battery Drain**
    * Description/Symptoms: Difficulty starting the engine, dead battery, and electrical components not functioning properly.
    * Solution: Diagnose and repair the source of the drain (alternator, parasitic draw, etc.) or replace the battery.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+ depending on the cause.
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Headlight/Taillight Condensation**
    * Description/Symptoms: Moisture buildup inside the headlight or taillight housing, reducing visibility.
    * Solution: Resealing or replacing the headlight/taillight assembly.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+ per light.
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Mirror Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: Power mirrors not adjusting properly, heated mirrors not working, or mirror housing damage.
    * Solution: Mirror repair or replacement.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+ per mirror.
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **HVAC System Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: Weak airflow, inconsistent temperature, and malfunctioning controls.
    * Solution: Repair or replace the blower motor, actuator, or control module.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,500+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Seat Issues (Air Ride or Adjustment)**
    * Description/Symptoms: Seat not adjusting properly, air ride not functioning, or uncomfortable seating position.
    * Solution: Repair or replace the seat components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2020 Volvo VNL 740 can have several potential issues ranging from moderate to severe. Engine problems, particularly with the D13 and Cummins X15 (such as EGR cooler failures, DPF clogging, and turbocharger issues), can lead to significant downtime and repair costs. Transmission issues with the I-Shift, like shift hesitation and clutch failure, are also common concerns. Electrical issues and suspension problems contribute to overall maintenance costs. While some issues are minor and easily resolved, others can be expensive to fix, potentially costing thousands of dollars. Thorough inspection and preventative maintenance are crucial for mitigating these risks.
