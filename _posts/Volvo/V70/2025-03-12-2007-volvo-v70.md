The 2007 Volvo V70 is a practical and comfortable wagon, appreciated for its safety, spacious interior, and comfortable ride. Engine options for this model year include a 2.4L inline-5 producing 168 horsepower and 170 lb-ft of torque, and a 2.5L turbocharged inline-5 producing 208 horsepower and 236 lb-ft of torque. The V70 is generally liked for its reliability and practicality, but some owners have reported issues with various components as the vehicles age. Common complaints revolve around the electronic throttle module, suspension wear, and some electrical gremlins.

**Engine**

**2.4L Inline-5**

*   **Electronic Throttle Module (ETM) Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rough idling, stalling, loss of power, and the check engine light illuminating. A common Diagnostic Trouble Code (DTC) is P0121.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the ETM.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**2.5L Turbocharged Inline-5**

*   **Turbocharger Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Whining or whistling noise from the engine bay, loss of power, increased oil consumption, and blue smoke from the exhaust.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the turbocharger.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1500 - $2500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **PCV System Clogging**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rough idle, oil leaks from seals (rear main seal, camshaft seals), increased crankcase pressure.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the PCV valve/oil trap and cleaning or replacing related hoses.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**AW55-50/51SN 5-Speed Automatic Transmission**

*   **Valve Body Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Harsh or delayed shifting, slipping between gears, erratic shifting, and the transmission entering "limp mode."
    *   Solution: Valve body rebuild or replacement. Fluid changes might alleviate the symptoms temporarily.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $2000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Front Control Arm Bushing Wear**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Clunking noise from the front suspension, especially when going over bumps. Poor handling and steering feel.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the front control arms or just the bushings.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+ per side.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Rear Shock Absorber Wear**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Bouncy ride, poor handling, and excessive rear-end sway.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the rear shock absorbers.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Central Electronic Module (CEM) Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Intermittent electrical problems, such as malfunctioning power windows, locks, lights, or wipers. Can also cause issues with starting the car.
    *   Solution: Replacement or reprogramming of the CEM.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Tail Light Assembly Corrosion**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Intermittent tail light function, brake light failure, or turn signal issues. Corrosion within the tail light assembly is often the cause.
    *   Solution: Cleaning the contacts, replacing the bulb holder, or replacing the entire tail light assembly.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $300+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Sunroof Drain Clogs**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Water leaks into the cabin, particularly in the headliner or footwells, due to clogged sunroof drains.
    *   Solution: Cleaning the sunroof drain tubes.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $200+ (depending on labor)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Climate Control Fan Motor Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The blower motor stops working, resulting in no air coming from the vents. The fan may work intermittently before failing completely.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the blower motor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**
The 2007 Volvo V70, while generally reliable, does suffer from several common issues as it ages. Electrical gremlins and suspension wear are prevalent, while engine-specific problems like ETM failure and turbocharger issues should be carefully inspected. The transmission can also be a source of trouble. The severity of these issues ranges from minor inconveniences to potentially costly repairs, making a pre-purchase inspection highly recommended. Repair costs can vary significantly depending on the location and the shop performing the work.
