The 2004 Volvo V70 is a mid-size wagon known for its safety, practicality, and comfortable ride. Engine options include a 2.4L inline-5 producing 168 hp and 170 lb-ft of torque, a 2.5L turbocharged inline-5 producing 197 hp and 210 lb-ft of torque, and a high-performance 2.3L turbocharged inline-5 in the V70 T5 producing 247 hp and 243 lb-ft of torque. A 5-speed automatic transmission and 5-speed manual transmission are available. Owners often appreciate the V70's spacious interior and cargo capacity, as well as its reputation for longevity. Common dislikes include the car's somewhat dated styling and occasionally complex electrical systems.

**Engine**

**2.4L Inline-5**

*   **Oil Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks often develop from the rear main seal, valve cover gasket, or oil pan gasket. Symptoms include visible oil leaks under the car, burning oil smell, and low oil levels.
    *   Solution: Replace the leaking gasket or seal.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $800+
    * Recalls: None Found

*   **Electronic Throttle Module (ETM) Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The ETM can fail, causing rough idling, stalling, and reduced engine power. A check engine light will usually illuminate.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the ETM. The original Magneti Marelli ETM is prone to failure and aftermarket replacements are available.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800+
    * Recalls: None Found

**2.5L Turbocharged Inline-5**

*   **Turbocharger Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The turbocharger can fail due to age and wear, resulting in reduced power, increased oil consumption, and potentially a whistling or whining noise from the turbo.
    *   Solution: Turbocharger replacement or rebuild.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $2,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Oil Leaks (Turbo-specific)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks can occur at the turbocharger oil feed and drain lines.
    *   Solution: Replace the oil lines and seals.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **PCV System Clogging**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The positive crankcase ventilation (PCV) system can become clogged, leading to increased crankcase pressure, oil leaks, and potential damage to seals.
    *   Solution: Clean or replace the PCV system components, including the oil trap/separator and hoses.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**2.3L Turbocharged Inline-5 (T5)**

*   **Turbocharger Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Similar to the 2.5L turbo, the turbocharger can fail due to age, resulting in reduced power, oil consumption, and unusual noises.
    *   Solution: Turbocharger replacement or rebuild.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $2,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Boost Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Leaks in the turbocharger's boost system can cause a loss of power and decreased fuel efficiency.
    *   Solution: Inspect and replace leaking hoses or intercooler.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**AW55-50/51SN 5-Speed Automatic**

*   **Valve Body Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Harsh or delayed shifting, slipping between gears, and failure to shift are common symptoms.
    *   Solution: Replacement or rebuild of the valve body.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Solenoid Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Erratic shifting or failure to shift.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the faulty solenoids.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**M56 5-Speed Manual**

*   **Clutch Wear**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Clutch slippage, difficulty shifting, and a high engagement point.
    *   Solution: Clutch replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,200+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Front Suspension Components Wear**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Worn ball joints, tie rod ends, and control arm bushings can cause clunking noises, poor handling, and uneven tire wear.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the worn suspension components.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Rear Suspension Nivomat Shocks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The self-leveling Nivomat shocks can fail, resulting in a sagging rear end and a bouncy ride.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the Nivomat shocks (expensive) or conversion to standard shocks.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,500+ for Nivomats; $400 - $800+ for standard shocks
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Central Electronic Module (CEM) Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The CEM can fail, causing a variety of electrical problems, including issues with the lights, wipers, power windows, and immobilizer.
    *   Solution: Replacement or repair of the CEM.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Dimming Instrument Cluster Lights**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Dim or flickering instrument cluster lights.
    *   Solution: Repair or replacement of the instrument cluster.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Rust**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rust can develop on the rocker panels, wheel wells, and around the windshield.
    *   Solution: Rust repair and repainting.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $2,000+ depending on the severity
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Climate Control Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Failure of the climate control system, including issues with the blower motor, blend door actuators, and temperature sensors.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis and replacement of the faulty component.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2004 Volvo V70 can be a reliable and practical vehicle, but owners should be aware of some common issues. The most significant concerns are related to the electronic throttle module (ETM), automatic transmission valve body, CEM, turbocharger failure (on turbo models), and suspension components. The severity of these issues can range from minor inconveniences to major repairs, with costs varying accordingly. Potential buyers should have a pre-purchase inspection performed by a qualified mechanic to assess the condition of these components and budget for potential repairs.
