The 2009 Volvo V70 is a practical and comfortable wagon known for its safety and spacious interior. Engine options included a 3.2L inline-6 producing 235 horsepower and 236 lb-ft of torque, a turbocharged 2.5L inline-5, and a turbocharged 3.0L inline-6 with more power. It was offered with a six-speed automatic transmission. People often appreciate the V70 for its comfortable ride, ample cargo space, and reputation for safety. Some common dislikes include a somewhat dated infotainment system and relatively high running costs.

**Engine**

**3.2L Inline-6**

*   **Oil Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks can develop from various seals and gaskets, including the valve cover gasket and rear main seal. Symptoms include visible oil leaks on the engine or ground, burning oil smell, and low oil levels.
    *   Solution: Replacing the affected gaskets and seals.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $500+ depending on the location and severity of the leak.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Variable Valve Timing (VVT) Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Problems with the VVT system, often involving the VVT solenoid or actuator, can lead to rough idling, poor performance, and a check engine light.
    *   Solution: Replacing the faulty VVT solenoid or actuator.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Turbocharged 2.5L Inline-5**

* **Turbocharger Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Reduced power, whistling noises from the engine, and a check engine light.
    * Solution: Replacing the turbocharger.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Turbocharged 3.0L Inline-6**

*   **Oil Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks can develop from various seals and gaskets, including the valve cover gasket and rear main seal. Symptoms include visible oil leaks on the engine or ground, burning oil smell, and low oil levels.
    *   Solution: Replacing the affected gaskets and seals.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $500+ depending on the location and severity of the leak.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Variable Valve Timing (VVT) Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Problems with the VVT system, often involving the VVT solenoid or actuator, can lead to rough idling, poor performance, and a check engine light.
    *   Solution: Replacing the faulty VVT solenoid or actuator.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**6-Speed Automatic (Aisin Warner)**

*   **Valve Body Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Harsh or delayed shifting, slipping between gears, and general transmission malfunction.
    *   Solution: Replacing or rebuilding the valve body.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Torque Converter Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Shuddering at low speeds, especially during acceleration, and potential stalling.
    *   Solution: Replacing the torque converter.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Rear Suspension Bushings**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Worn rear suspension bushings can cause clunking noises, especially when going over bumps, and can negatively affect handling.
    *   Solution: Replacing the worn bushings.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Strut Mounts**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Squeaking, creaking, or knocking noises from the front suspension, especially when turning the steering wheel or going over bumps.
    *   Solution: Replacing the strut mounts.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Central Electronic Module (CEM) Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Intermittent electrical problems, such as issues with the windows, lights, or starting the car.
    *   Solution: Diagnosing and potentially replacing the CEM.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Power Window Motor Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Window not operating, slow operation, or unusual noises coming from the door while operating the windows.
    *   Solution: Replacing the power window motor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Sunroof Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Sunroof may leak, malfunction, or fail to open or close properly.
    *   Solution: Repairing or replacing the sunroof motor, seals, or entire assembly.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Climate Control Fan Motor**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The blower motor for the climate control system can fail, leading to reduced or no airflow. Sometimes only some speeds work.
    *   Solution: Replacing the blower motor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2009 Volvo V70 can have a number of issues. The most common issues are oil leaks, transmission issues, and electrical problems. While some fixes are relatively inexpensive, others, like CEM replacement or turbocharger repair, can be quite costly. Overall, the V70 is generally reliable. However, keeping an eye out for potential electrical and transmission problems is important.
