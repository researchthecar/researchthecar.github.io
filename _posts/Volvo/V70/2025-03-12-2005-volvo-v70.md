The 2005 Volvo V70 is a mid-size wagon known for its safety, practicality, and comfortable ride. Engine options include a 2.4L inline-5 (168 hp, 170 lb-ft of torque), a 2.5L turbocharged inline-5 (208 hp, 236 lb-ft of torque), and a high-pressure turbocharged 2.3L inline-5 (247 hp, 243 lb-ft of torque) found in the T5 model. Transmission options include a 5-speed manual (less common) and a 5-speed automatic. People generally appreciate the V70's cargo capacity, comfortable seats, and reputation for longevity. Common dislikes include repair costs and some electronic gremlins.

**Engine**

**2.4L Inline-5**

*   **Failed Electronic Throttle Module (ETM)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Erratic idling, hesitation, loss of power, check engine light. Often accompanied by codes related to the throttle position sensor. Early versions of the ETM (Magneti Marelli) are particularly prone to failure.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the ETM with a Bosch unit or a refurbished ETM from a reputable vendor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800+
    * Recalls: None Found

*   **PCV System Clogging**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rough idle, oil leaks (rear main seal, camshaft seals), increased crankcase pressure, whistling noise from the engine.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the PCV breather box, hoses, and oil trap.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
    * Recalls: None Found

**2.5L Turbocharged Inline-5**

*   **Turbocharger Failure**

    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800+

*   **PCV System Clogging**

    *   Solution: Replacement of the PCV breather box, hoses, and oil trap.

    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**5-Speed Automatic (AW55-50SN)**

*   **Valve Body Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Harsh shifting, slipping between gears, delayed engagement, transmission fault codes.
    *   Solution: Replacement or rebuild of the valve body. Sometimes a software update can help, but often the issue is mechanical.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $2000+
    * Recalls: None Found

*   **Torque Converter Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Shuddering or vibration at low speeds, especially during lockup, transmission fault codes.
    *   Solution: Torque converter replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1500+
    * Recalls: None Found

*   **Solenoid Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Erratic shifting, transmission fault codes.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the faulty solenoid(s).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Front Lower Control Arm Bushing Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Clunking noise from the front suspension, especially when going over bumps, vague steering.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the front lower control arms or just the bushings (if possible).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $700+
    * Recalls: None Found

*   **Rear Shock Absorber Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Bouncy ride, poor handling, leaking shock absorbers.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the rear shock absorbers.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **DIM (Driver Information Module) Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Intermittent or complete loss of instrument cluster functionality (speedometer, tachometer, fuel gauge, etc.), warning lights illuminating incorrectly.
    *   Solution: Replacement or repair of the DIM. Refurbished units are often available.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+
    * Recalls: None Found

*   **SRS (Supplemental Restraint System) Module Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: SRS warning light illuminated, possible issues with airbag deployment.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis of the SRS system to determine the cause (clock spring, seat belt pretensioner, etc.) and repair/replacement of the faulty component.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1000+ (depending on the component)
    * Recalls: None Found

*   **Central Electronic Module (CEM) Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Various electrical malfunctions, including issues with headlights, wipers, power windows, and remote locking.
    *   Solution: Replacement or reprogramming of the CEM. This often requires specialized equipment and expertise.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Headlight Lens Clouding/Yellowing**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Reduced headlight output, yellowed or cloudy headlight lenses.
    *   Solution: Headlight lens restoration (polishing) or headlight assembly replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $200+ (restoration), $200 - $600+ (replacement)
    * Recalls: None Found

*   **Rear Wiper Motor Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rear wiper not functioning.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the rear wiper motor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Sunroof Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Sunroof not opening or closing properly, binding, motor failure.
    *   Solution: Repair or replacement of the sunroof motor, tracks, or cables.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+
    * Recalls: None Found

*   **Heater Core Clogging**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Reduced heat output, especially on one side of the car, sweet smell inside the cabin (coolant leak).
    *   Solution: Replacement of the heater core. This is a labor-intensive job.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2005 Volvo V70 is generally a reliable vehicle, but like any car of its age, it's prone to certain issues. Engine problems, particularly with the ETM and PCV system, are common. Transmission issues with the AW55-50SN automatic can be expensive to address. Electrical gremlins are also a concern. The severity of these issues ranges from minor annoyances to costly repairs. Prospective buyers should carefully inspect the vehicle for these common problems and factor potential repair costs into their purchase decision.
