The 2016 Volvo V60 is a stylish and practical wagon offering a blend of Scandinavian design, comfort, and safety. Engine options include a range of turbocharged four-cylinder engines. For example, the T5 Drive-E engine produces around 240 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque, paired with an 8-speed automatic transmission. Common praises for the V60 include its comfortable seats, refined ride, and ample cargo space. Criticisms often mention the somewhat dated infotainment system and occasionally complex controls.

**Engine**

**2.0L Turbocharged I4 (T5 Drive-E)**

* **Oil Consumption**
    * Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported excessive oil consumption, requiring frequent top-ups between oil changes. Blue smoke from the exhaust may be observed in some instances.
    * Solution: The exact cause can vary. Some cases may be addressed with updated PCV (positive crankcase ventilation) system components. In more severe cases, piston ring replacement may be necessary.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $3000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**2.0L Turbocharged and Supercharged I4 (T6 Drive-E)**

* **No Issues Found**

**Engine (General)**

* **Coolant Leaks**
    * Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaks can occur from various points in the cooling system, including the water pump, hoses, and radiator. Obvious leaks will be spotted.
    * Solution: Replace the leaking component. Pressure testing the system can help pinpoint the source.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $800+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**Aisin Warner 8-Speed Automatic (AW TG-81SC)**

* **Harsh Shifting/Hesitation**
    * Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported harsh or jerky shifting, particularly between lower gears. Hesitation during acceleration or when downshifting is also reported.
    * Solution: A software update may resolve some cases. Others may require valve body replacement or, in more severe cases, full transmission rebuild or replacement.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $5000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Rear Suspension Noises**
    * Description/Symptoms: Some owners report noises coming from the rear suspension, such as clunking or squeaking, particularly when going over bumps.
    * Solution: Inspect and replace worn suspension components such as bushings, sway bar links, or shock absorbers.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Infotainment System Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: The Sensus Connect infotainment system can experience glitches, freezing, or slow response times. Bluetooth connectivity issues are also reported.
    * Solution: Software updates may resolve some issues. In some cases, replacement of the infotainment unit may be necessary.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 (Software Update) - $1000+ (Unit Replacement)
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Battery Drain**
    * Description/Symptoms: Premature battery drain, leading to difficulty starting the vehicle.
    * Solution: Identify the parasitic drain by testing current draw when the car is off. Common culprits can be faulty modules or improperly shut off electrical components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **No Issues Found**

**Interior**

* **Sunroof Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: Sunroof may get stuck, fail to close or open properly, or create excessive noise.
    * Solution: Inspect and lubricate the sunroof tracks. If the motor is faulty, replace it.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $800+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Door Lock Actuator Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Door lock may fail to lock or unlock properly.
    * Solution: Replace the faulty door lock actuator.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $400+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2016 Volvo V60 can experience a range of issues, with engine oil consumption and transmission harsh shifting being among the more significant concerns. Electrical issues with the infotainment system can also be frustrating. While some problems may be resolved with relatively inexpensive fixes like software updates, others, such as engine or transmission repairs, can be costly. Potential buyers should be aware of these potential issues and carefully inspect the vehicle's service history and current condition before purchasing.
