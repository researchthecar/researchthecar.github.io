**Introduction**

The 2019 Volvo V60 is a luxury compact wagon known for its sleek design, safety features, and comfortable interior. It comes standard with a 2.0L turbocharged 4-cylinder engine mated to an 8-speed automatic transmission. Optional powertrain options include a plug-in hybrid and a 2.0L turbocharged and supercharged 4-cylinder engine with a 48-volt mild hybrid system. Despite its appeal, the V60 has been plagued by several issues since its release.

**Engine**

**2.0L Turbocharged 4-Cylinder**

* **Intake Manifold Failure:** The intake manifold can crack, causing coolant and oil leaks, rough idling, and reduced power. **Solution:** Replacement of the intake manifold. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $1,000 - $2,000+

* **Turbocharger Failure:** The turbocharger can fail prematurely, leading to a loss of power, excessive smoke, and whistling noises. **Solution:** Replacement of the turbocharger. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $2,000 - $4,000+

* **Fuel Injector Failure:** The fuel injectors can become clogged or faulty, causing rough idling, hesitation during acceleration, and reduced fuel economy. **Solution:** Replacement of the fuel injectors. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $500 - $800+

**Transmission**

**8-Speed Automatic**

* **Transmission Fluid Leaks:** The transmission can develop leaks from the seals or lines, resulting in fluid loss and potential transmission failure. **Solution:** Repair or replacement of the leaking components. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $500 - $1,500+

**Suspension**

**No Issues Found**

**Electrical**

* **Battery Discharge:** The battery can discharge prematurely, even when the vehicle is parked. **Solution:** Diagnosis and repair or replacement of the affected electrical components (battery, alternator, etc.). **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $500 - $1,000+

* **Infotainment System Glitches:** The infotainment system can experience software bugs, display errors, and connectivity issues. **Solution:** Software updates or replacement of the infotainment module. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $500 - $1,500+

* **Headlight Malfunctions:** The headlights can experience flickering, dimming, or complete failure. **Solution:** Replacement of the bulbs or headlight assembly. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $200 - $1,000+

**Exterior**

* **Paint Peeling:** The paint on the exterior can peel or chip prematurely. **Solution:** Refinishing or repainting of the affected areas. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $500 - $2,000+

* **Door Handle Failure:** The door handles can become stuck or loose, making it difficult to open the doors. **Solution:** Replacement of the door handles. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $200 - $500+

**Interior**

* **Seat Adjustment Issues:** The power seat adjustments can become intermittent or stop working altogether. **Solution:** Replacement of the seat motors or control module. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $500 - $1,500+

* **Dashboard Rattles:** The dashboard can develop rattles or squeaks, especially when the vehicle is driven on rough roads. **Solution:** Tightening of the fasteners or replacement of the dashboard components. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $200 - $600+

**Summary**

The 2019 Volvo V60 is a vehicle with several drawbacks, including engine, transmission, electrical, and interior issues. The severity of these problems can range from minor inconveniences to major repairs with significant costs. It is advisable to thoroughly research and consider these potential problems before purchasing a V60 or factor in the potential expenses for repairs and maintenance.
