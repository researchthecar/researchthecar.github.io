**2021 Volvo V60**

The 2021 Volvo V60 is a luxurious and sporty wagon that offers a range of engine and transmission options. It is praised for its comfortable interior, advanced safety features, and refined driving experience. However, there have been some reported issues with the vehicle.

**Engine**

* **B4** 2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4 (250 hp, 258 lb-ft)
    * **Valve Stem Seal Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: Excessive oil consumption, blue smoke from the exhaust
        * Solution: Replace valve stem seals
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
        * Recalls: None Found
    * **Timing Chain Tensioner Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: Noisy engine, reduced engine power
        * Solution: Replace timing chain tensioner
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,200+
        * Recalls: None Found

* **B5** 2.0L Supercharged and Turbocharged Inline-4 (316 hp, 295 lb-ft)
    * No Issues Found

* **B6** 2.0L Supercharged and Turbocharged Inline-4 (400 hp, 352 lb-ft)
    * No Issues Found

**Transmission**

* **8-Speed Geartronic Automatic (B4)**
    * **Shift Hesitation**
        * Description/Symptoms: Delayed or rough shifting between gears
        * Solution: Update transmission software, replace solenoid
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
        * Recalls: None Found

* **8-Speed Geartronic Automatic (B5, B6)**
    * No Issues Found

**Suspension**

* **Strut Mount Bearing Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Clunking or popping noise from the front suspension when turning
    * Solution: Replace strut mount bearing
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $600+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Headlight Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: One or both headlights malfunctioning or flickering
    * Solution: Replace headlight module
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Infotainment System Freeze**
    * Description/Symptoms: Infotainment screen freezing or becoming unresponsive
    * Solution: Update software, replace infotainment module
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Weatherstripping Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Loose or torn weatherstripping causing water leaks or wind noise
    * Solution: Replace weatherstripping
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Climate Control Malfunction**
    * Description/Symptoms: AC or heater not working properly
    * Solution: Repair or replace climate control module
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Power Seat Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Power seats not adjusting properly
    * Solution: Repair or replace power seat module
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $700+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Overall Summary**

The 2021 Volvo V60 has a number of reported issues, with the engine and transmission being the most affected components. While many of the issues are relatively minor and inexpensive to fix, some, such as the valve stem seal failure and timing chain tensioner failure, can be more serious and costly. Overall, potential buyers should be aware of these potential problems and factor them into their decision-making process.
