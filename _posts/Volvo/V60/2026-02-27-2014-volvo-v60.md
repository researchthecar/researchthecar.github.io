# 2014 Volvo V60

### Overview
The 2014 Volvo V60, a compact luxury wagon, is generally recognized for its strong safety features and comfortable ride. It offers a range of powertrain options, including efficient turbocharged gasoline engines and a potent diesel variant in some markets. While Volvo's reputation for build quality is a strong point, like any vehicle, the V60 can experience specific issues as it ages. This report focuses on documented technical reliability concerns beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential problem areas for owners and prospective buyers of the 2014 model year.

---


## Engine

### 2.0L GTDi (T5)
**Turbocharger Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of power, whistling noises, increased oil consumption.
- **Solution:** Turbocharger inspection, repair, or replacement.
- **Cost:** $1,200 - $2,500


### 3.0L T6 (T6)
**Coolant Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Overheating, visible coolant puddles, sweet smell.
- **Solution:** Inspection and replacement of leaking hoses, water pump, or radiator.
- **Cost:** $400 - $1,000


### 2.4L D5 (Diesel)
**Diesel Particulate Filter (DPF) Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Check engine light, reduced engine performance, increased fuel consumption.
- **Solution:** DPF regeneration cycle, cleaning, or replacement.
- **Cost:** $800 - $2,000


## Transmission

### 6-Speed Geartronic Automatic
**Hesitation or Jerking**
- **Symptoms:** Rough shifting, delayed engagement, occasional jerking during gear changes.
- **Solution:** Transmission fluid flush and filter replacement, software update, or internal transmission repair.
- **Cost:** $300 - $1,500

## Suspension

### Standard Chassis
**Worn Control Arm Bushings**
- **Symptoms:** Clunking noises over bumps, vague steering feel, uneven tire wear.
- **Solution:** Replacement of control arm bushings or the entire control arm assembly.
- **Cost:** $300 - $700


## Electrical

### General Electrical System
**Infotainment System Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Screen freezing, unresponsiveness, audio issues, Bluetooth connectivity problems.
- **Solution:** Software updates, system reset, or potential module replacement.
- **Cost:** $100 - $800

**Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Difficulty starting, dead battery, electrical components malfunctioning.
- **Solution:** Testing and replacement of battery, alternator, or parasitic drain investigation.
- **Cost:** $200 - $600

### Summary Verdict
The 2014 Volvo V60 is a generally reliable vehicle, particularly praised for its safety and comfort. The most commonly reported technical issues tend to revolve around the turbocharger on gasoline engines, potential coolant leaks on the T6 models, and DPF concerns for diesel variants. Transmission issues, while not pervasive, can manifest as rough shifting. Electrical gremlins, especially with the infotainment system, are also noted. Suspension components like control arm bushings may require attention over time. Overall, with proactive maintenance and addressing specific known issues, the V60 can offer dependable service.
