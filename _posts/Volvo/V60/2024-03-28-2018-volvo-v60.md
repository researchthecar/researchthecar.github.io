**2018 Volvo V60**

The 2018 Volvo V60 is a luxury station wagon that offers a comfortable and stylish driving experience. It is available with a variety of engine and transmission options, including a 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine with 250 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque, a 2.0-liter turbocharged and supercharged four-cylinder engine with 316 horsepower and 295 lb-ft of torque, and a 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder diesel engine with 190 horsepower and 317 lb-ft of torque. Transmission options include an eight-speed automatic transmission and a six-speed manual transmission.

People like the Volvo V60 for its spacious interior, comfortable seats, and high-quality materials. They also appreciate its safety features, which include automatic emergency braking, lane keeping assist, and blind spot monitoring. However, some people find the V60 to be expensive, and they complain about its lack of cargo space compared to other station wagons.

**Engine**

**2.0-liter Turbocharged Four-Cylinder Engine**

* **Turbocharger failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, decreased fuel economy, excessive smoke from the exhaust
    * Solution: Replace the turbocharger
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Timing chain failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Engine rattling, metal shavings in the oil, loss of power
    * Solution: Replace the timing chain and related components
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**2.0-liter Turbocharged and Supercharged Four-Cylinder Engine**

* **No Issues Found**

**2.0-liter Turbocharged Four-Cylinder Diesel Engine**

* **Fuel injector failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Hard starting, rough idling, decreased fuel economy, black smoke from the exhaust
    * Solution: Replace the fuel injectors
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,200+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**Eight-Speed Automatic Transmission**

* **Transmission fluid leak**
    * Description/Symptoms: Transmission slipping, difficulty shifting gears, grinding noises
    * Solution: Replace or repair the transmission seals
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Torque converter failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Shuddering or vibration during acceleration, loss of power, overheating
    * Solution: Replace the torque converter
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $1,500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Six-Speed Manual Transmission**

* **Clutch failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears, grinding noises, slipping
    * Solution: Replace the clutch
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Front suspension strut failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Clunking or rattling noises, uneven tire wear, poor handling
    * Solution: Replace the front suspension struts
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $600 - $1,200+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Rear suspension spring failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Sagging rear end, excessive body roll, poor handling
    * Solution: Replace the rear suspension springs
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Battery failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Difficulty starting the engine, loss of electrical power, dimming headlights
    * Solution: Replace the battery
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Alternator failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Dimming headlights, loss of electrical power, difficulty starting the engine
    * Solution: Replace the alternator
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Headlight condensation**
    * Description/Symptoms: Water droplets or fog inside the headlight lenses
    * Solution: Replace the headlight lenses
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Paint peeling**
    * Description/Symptoms: Clear coat peeling or flaking off the paint
    * Solution: Repaint the affected areas
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Climate control system failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Difficulty controlling temperature, blowing hot or cold air, excessive noise
    * Solution: Repair or replace the climate control system components
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Infotainment system failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Freezing or crashing, difficulty connecting to devices, no sound
    * Solution: Update or replace the infotainment system software
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2018 Volvo V60 is a well-built and reliable vehicle. However, it is not without its problems. Some of the most common issues reported by owners include turbocharger failure, timing chain failure, transmission fluid leaks, suspension strut failure, and electrical problems. The severity of these issues can vary, and the cost to fix them can be significant. Overall, the V60 is a good choice for those looking for a luxury station wagon, but it is important to be aware of the potential problems that can occur.
