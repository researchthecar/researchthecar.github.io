**Introduction**

The 2023 Volvo V60 is a premium compact station wagon that offers a blend of style, safety, and practicality. It is available with three engine options: a base 2.0-liter turbocharged inline-4, a 2.0-liter turbocharged and supercharged inline-4, and a 2.0-liter turbocharged and supercharged inline-4 paired with an electric motor (plug-in hybrid). The base engine produces 250 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque, while the upgraded engines produce 316 horsepower and 295 lb-ft of torque (gasoline-only) and 400 horsepower and 472 lb-ft of torque (plug-in hybrid). Transmission options include an 8-speed automatic and an 8-speed dual-clutch automatic.

Owners generally praise the V60's sleek design, spacious and well-equipped interior, and advanced safety features. However, some have encountered various issues with the vehicle.

**Engine**

**2.0-liter Turbocharged Inline-4**

* **Oil Consumption Issue**
Description/Symptoms: Excessive oil consumption, leading to low oil levels and potential engine damage.
Solution: Replacement of piston rings and valve stem seals.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $3,000 - $5,000+
Recalls: TSB 2049527

**2.0-liter Turbocharged and Supercharged Inline-4**

* **Fuel Injector Failure**
Description/Symptoms: Engine stalling, rough idle, and poor fuel economy.
Solution: Replacement of faulty fuel injectors.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**8-speed Automatic**

* **Shifting Issues**
Description/Symptoms: Delayed or harsh shifting, slipping gears, and loss of power.
Solution: Software updates or transmission replacement.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+
Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Rear Shock Absorber Noise**
Description/Symptoms: Clunking or rattling noise from the rear suspension, especially over rough roads.
Solution: Replacement of faulty shock absorbers.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain**
Description/Symptoms: Rapid battery discharge, leading to difficulty starting the vehicle.
Solution: Software updates or replacement of faulty electrical components.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Headlight Condensation**
Description/Symptoms: Moisture accumulation inside the headlight assemblies, affecting visibility.
Solution: Replacement of affected headlights or resealing.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
Recalls: None Found

* **Windshield Wiper Motor Failure**
Description/Symptoms: Inoperative windshield wipers.
Solution: Replacement of faulty wiper motor.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Infotainment System Lag**
Description/Symptoms: Slow response or freezing of the infotainment touchscreen.
Solution: Software updates or replacement of the infotainment unit.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
Recalls: None Found

* **Climate Control Malfunction**
Description/Symptoms: Inconsistent temperature control, fan noise, or failure of the climate control system.
Solution: Software updates or replacement of faulty components.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

While the 2023 Volvo V60 is generally well-received, it has experienced some issues with its engine, transmission, and various other components. The severity of these issues varies, with some being relatively minor and others potentially more costly to repair. It is recommended to research potential problems before purchasing a V60 and to consider extended warranty coverage to mitigate potential repair expenses.
