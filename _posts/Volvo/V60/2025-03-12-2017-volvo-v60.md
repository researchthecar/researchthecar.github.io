The 2017 Volvo V60 is a stylish and practical wagon offering a blend of comfort, safety, and performance. For 2017, engine options included a 2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4 (T5), a 2.0L Turbocharged and Supercharged Inline-4 (T6), and the Polestar variant with a more powerful version of the T6. Transmission options were primarily an 8-speed automatic. The T5 produces around 240 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque. The T6 variant produces around 302 horsepower and 295 lb-ft of torque. Owners often praise the V60 for its comfortable ride, distinctive Scandinavian design, and comprehensive safety features. Common dislikes include the infotainment system's complexity, and some report reliability concerns.

**Engine**

**2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4 (T5)**

*   **Excessive Oil Consumption**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported excessive oil consumption, requiring more frequent oil top-ups between scheduled services. This can manifest as low oil level warnings or the need to add oil every few thousand miles.
    *   Solution: Repair may involve replacing piston rings and/or valve stem seals.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $5,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**2.0L Turbocharged and Supercharged Inline-4 (T6)**

*   **Coolant Leakage**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaking from the water pump or other coolant lines. This can cause overheating if not addressed.
    *   Solution: Replace the water pump and/or coolant lines.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Supercharger Clutch Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The supercharger clutch may fail. Symptoms include loss of power, unusual noises from the engine, and potential error codes.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the supercharger clutch.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**8-Speed Automatic (All Engines)**

*   **Harsh Shifting/Hesitation**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported harsh or delayed shifting, particularly between lower gears. The transmission may also hesitate when accelerating.
    *   Solution: Software updates may resolve the issue in some cases. More severe cases may require valve body replacement or complete transmission replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 (Software Update) - $6,000+ (Transmission Replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Rear Shock Absorber Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Premature wear or leaks from the rear shock absorbers, leading to a bouncy or unstable ride, particularly over bumps.
    *   Solution: Replace the rear shock absorbers.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The Sensus infotainment system can experience glitches, freezing, slow response times, or complete failure.
    *   Solution: Software updates can sometimes resolve the issue. More significant problems may require replacement of the infotainment unit.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 (Software Update) - $1,500+ (Unit Replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Battery Drain**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Premature battery drain, especially if the vehicle is not driven regularly. This can lead to difficulty starting the car.
    *   Solution: Identify and address any parasitic draws in the electrical system. Replace the battery if necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **No Issues Found**

**Interior**

*   **Sunroof Rattling/Creaking**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rattling or creaking noises emanating from the sunroof area, especially when driving over uneven surfaces.
    *   Solution: Adjusting or lubricating the sunroof mechanism may alleviate the noise. In some cases, replacement of sunroof components may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2017 Volvo V60 can present a range of issues. Engine problems such as oil consumption and coolant leaks, along with transmission concerns like harsh shifting, are among the more costly and potentially severe issues. Electrical problems, especially with the infotainment system, can be frustrating. While some issues can be resolved with software updates or minor repairs, others may require more extensive and expensive work. Potential buyers should thoroughly inspect the vehicle and be aware of these common issues.
