The 2014 Volvo V60 is a stylish and practical wagon known for its safety features and comfortable ride. Engine options include the T5 2.0L turbocharged inline-5, the T6 3.0L turbocharged inline-6, and the T6 R-Design 3.0L turbocharged inline-6. The T5 engine produces around 240 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque. Drivers often praise the V60 for its comfortable seats, elegant interior, and cargo space. Common complaints revolve around the complicated infotainment system and some reported reliability issues.

**Engine**

**2.0L Turbocharged Inline-5 (T5)**

*   **Oil Consumption**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Excessive oil consumption is reported in some of the 2.0L turbocharged inline-5 engines. Owners may notice the low oil warning light illuminating frequently or have to add oil between scheduled changes.
    *   Solution: Address any leaks or replace the piston rings.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+

**3.0L Turbocharged Inline-6 (T6/R-Design)**

*   **Coolant Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaks are sometimes reported, potentially stemming from the water pump or hoses. Symptoms may include the low coolant warning light or visible coolant pooling under the car.
    *   Solution: Replacing the leaking component, such as the water pump or a hose.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+

**Transmission**

**AW55-51SN 5-Speed Automatic (T5)**

*   **Valve Body Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Harsh or erratic shifting, slipping between gears, or a complete failure to shift may occur.
    *   Solution: Replacing or rebuilding the valve body.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**TF-80SC 6-Speed Automatic (T6/R-Design)**

*   **Torque Converter Problems**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Shuddering or vibrations at specific speeds, especially during light acceleration. Can sometimes be diagnosed as a torque converter clutch (TCC) issue.
    *   Solution: Replacing the torque converter or, in some cases, the entire transmission.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,200 - $4,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Rear Shock Absorber Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Excessive bouncing or poor ride quality from the rear suspension. Fluid leaks may be visible on the shock absorbers.
    *   Solution: Replacing the rear shock absorbers.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $700+

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System (Sensus) Malfunctions**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Freezing, slow response times, or complete failure of the infotainment system. Issues with Bluetooth connectivity and navigation are sometimes reported.
    *   Solution: Software updates or, in severe cases, replacement of the infotainment unit.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $1,200+ depending on the fix

*   **Power Window Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Power windows may stop working or operate erratically, often due to a faulty window regulator or motor.
    *   Solution: Replacing the window regulator or motor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+

**Exterior**

*No Issues Found*

**Interior**

*   **Sunroof Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The sunroof may become stuck open or closed, or leak water into the cabin. This can be caused by a faulty motor or damaged seals.
    *   Solution: Replacing the sunroof motor, repairing the seals, or replacing the entire sunroof assembly.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+

**Summary**

The 2014 Volvo V60 can present some reliability concerns, primarily related to the engine, transmission, electrical systems, and suspension. The severity of these issues ranges from minor inconveniences to more substantial repairs. The cost to fix these issues can vary significantly, from a few hundred dollars for a simple repair to several thousand dollars for major engine or transmission work. While not every V60 will experience all of these problems, prospective buyers should be aware of these potential issues and consider having a pre-purchase inspection performed by a qualified mechanic.
