The 2015 Volvo V60 is a stylish and practical wagon known for its safety features and comfortable ride. Engine options include the T5 Drive-E 2.0L turbocharged inline-4 producing 240 hp and 258 lb-ft of torque, the T5 AWD 2.5L turbocharged inline-5 producing 250 hp and 266 lb-ft of torque, and the T6 AWD 3.0L turbocharged inline-6 producing 302 hp and 325 lb-ft of torque. The T5 Drive-E is paired with an 8-speed automatic transmission, while the T5 AWD and T6 AWD are paired with a 6-speed automatic transmission. Owners often praise the V60's comfortable seats, distinctive styling, and cargo capacity. Common dislikes include the sometimes-clunky infotainment system and relatively high running costs.

**Engine**

**T5 Drive-E 2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4**

*   **Oil Consumption**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners report excessive oil consumption, requiring frequent top-offs between scheduled oil changes. Symptoms include low oil level warnings and potentially engine knocking if the oil level drops too low.
    *   Solution: Regular monitoring of oil levels and topping off as needed. In severe cases, piston ring replacement may be required.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+

**T5 AWD 2.5L Turbocharged Inline-5**

*   **PCV System Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Failure of the Positive Crankcase Ventilation (PCV) system can lead to oil leaks, rough idle, and potentially engine damage. Symptoms include a whistling noise from the engine, oil leaks around seals, and difficulty starting.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the PCV valve and potentially other components of the PCV system.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500

**T6 AWD 3.0L Turbocharged Inline-6**

*   **Coolant Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaks are sometimes reported, often originating from the water pump or coolant hoses. Symptoms include low coolant levels, overheating, and visible coolant leaks.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the leaking water pump and/or coolant hoses.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800

**Transmission**

**Aisin Warner 8-Speed Automatic (T5 Drive-E)**

*   **Rough Shifting**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported rough or delayed shifting, particularly between lower gears. This can manifest as a clunking noise or a noticeable jerk when shifting.
    *   Solution: Transmission fluid flush and refill with updated fluid specification. In some cases, valve body replacement or transmission rebuild may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $3,000+

**Aisin Warner 6-Speed Automatic (T5 AWD and T6 AWD)**

*   **No Issues Found**

**Suspension**

*   **Rear Suspension Noises**
    * Description/Symptoms: Clunking or rattling noises from the rear suspension, especially when going over bumps.
    * Solution: Replacement of worn sway bar links, bushings, or shock absorbers.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The Sensus infotainment system can be prone to glitches, freezing, and slow response times. Issues may include problems with navigation, audio, and Bluetooth connectivity.
    *   Solution: Software updates may resolve some issues. In some cases, replacement of the infotainment unit may be required.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 (Software Update) - $1,000+

*   **Battery Drain**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Premature battery drain can occur, especially in colder climates. This can lead to difficulty starting the vehicle or requiring frequent jump starts.
    *   Solution: Testing the battery and charging system to identify any faulty components. Replacement of the battery or alternator may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $500

**Exterior**

*   **No Issues Found**

**Interior**

*   **Sunroof Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Sunroof malfunctions, including difficulty opening or closing, or leaking.
    *   Solution: Repair or replacement of sunroof motor or seals.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+

**Recalls**

*   Recall Number: R01293
    *   Description: Driver Front Air Bag may not deploy as designed.

*   Recall Number: R01241
    *   Description: Fuel Tank may Crack

*   Recall Number: R00077
    *   Description: Fuel rail pressure sensor may loosen, possibly resulting in fuel leakage.

**Summary**

The 2015 Volvo V60 presents a range of potential issues, with engine-related problems (oil consumption, PCV system, and coolant leaks) and electrical glitches being the most prominent. Transmission issues, especially with the 8-speed automatic, can also occur. The severity of these issues ranges from minor inconveniences to potentially costly repairs. While some issues are relatively inexpensive to fix, others, such as engine repairs or infotainment system replacements, can be quite costly. Owners should be aware of these potential problems and perform regular maintenance to mitigate the risk of major failures.
