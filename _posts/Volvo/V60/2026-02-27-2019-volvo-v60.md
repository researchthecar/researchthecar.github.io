# 2019 Volvo V60

### Overview
The 2019 Volvo V60 is a premium compact wagon known for its safety, comfort, and Scandinavian design. As a first-year model for the second generation, it introduced updated powertrains and technology. Reliability data for this model year generally indicates a solid performance, with most reported issues being minor or related to specific component failures rather than widespread systemic problems. Owners often praise its refined driving experience and advanced safety features. However, as with any vehicle, certain components may experience issues over time, particularly those related to the advanced electronic systems and powertrain options.

---


## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged-Supercharged (T6 Twin Engine)
**Coolant Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Visible coolant puddles under the vehicle, low coolant warning light, engine overheating.
- **Solution:** Inspection and replacement of leaking hoses, seals, or water pump.
- **Cost:** $300 - $700

**Turbocharger Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of power, whistling noises from the engine bay, increased exhaust smoke.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and potential replacement of the turbocharger unit or related plumbing.
- **Cost:** $1,200 - $2,500


### 2.0L Turbocharged (T5)
**Oil Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Visible oil stains under the vehicle, burning oil smell, low oil level.
- **Solution:** Identification and repair of leaking gaskets or seals, such as valve cover or oil pan gaskets.
- **Cost:** $200 - $500


### 2.0L Turbocharged Diesel (D4)
**Diesel Particulate Filter (DPF) Regeneration Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Check engine light, reduced engine power, increased fuel consumption.
- **Solution:** Forced DPF regeneration or cleaning; in severe cases, replacement of the DPF.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1,500


## Transmission

### 8-Speed Geartronic Automatic
**Hesitation or Jerking During Shifting**
- **Symptoms:** Noticeable delays or rough shifts between gears, especially at lower speeds.
- **Solution:** Software update for the transmission control module (TCM) or fluid flush and filter replacement.
- **Cost:** $300 - $800

## Interior

### Climate Control System
**AC Performance Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Reduced cooling efficiency, unusual noises from the HVAC system.
- **Solution:** Recharge refrigerant, inspect for leaks, or repair/replace blower motor or compressor.
- **Cost:** $300 - $1,000


### Seat Adjustment Motors
**Malfunctioning Seat Adjustments**
- **Symptoms:** Power seats fail to move forward, backward, or adjust height.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and replacement of faulty seat adjustment motors or control modules.
- **Cost:** $400 - $900

### Summary Verdict
The 2019 Volvo V60 demonstrates good overall reliability, particularly for a first-year model of its generation. While some owners have reported issues with the infotainment system, minor coolant leaks on turbocharged engines, and occasional transmission hesitation, these are not considered widespread critical failures. The diesel variants may encounter DPF-related issues, common for this technology. Most reported problems are repairable with moderate costs. Regular maintenance and addressing early signs of trouble are recommended to ensure long-term dependability.
