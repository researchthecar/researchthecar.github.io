# 2019 Volvo V60 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2019 Volvo V60 brings sleek Scandinavian styling, a refined interior, and practical wagon utility to the luxury market. As buyers shop for this model used, understanding its powertrain options and common owner-reported quirks helps ensure a smooth ownership experience. While the vehicle generally offers a composed ride and strong safety credentials, reviewing potential trouble spots across its systems is a smart step before making a purchase.

---

## Engine

### T5 2.0L Turbocharged 4-Cylinder

The T5 features a 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine producing around 250 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque, paired with front-wheel drive. It offers a balanced blend of efficiency and everyday power for daily commuting.

### T6 2.0L Supercharged & Turbocharged 4-Cylinder

The T6 utilizes a 2.0-liter engine that is both supercharged and turbocharged, generating approximately 316 horsepower and 295 lb-ft of torque with standard all-wheel drive. This setup provides brisk acceleration and robust performance across various driving conditions.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Geartronic Automatic Transmission

An 8-speed automatic transmission manages power delivery smoothly across all trims, complementing the V60's refined character with responsive gear shifts.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V800000: Electrical System:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volvo&model=V60&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** If the GPS location is not provided to emergency personnel it can inhibit emergency response in the event of a crash or injury.
- **What to do:** Volvo will notify owners, and dealers will correct the software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V144000: Forward Collision Avoidance: Automatic Emergency Braking](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volvo&model=V60&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** Due to a software/hardware incompatibility, the Automatic Emergency Brake (AEB) system may not detect obstacles and engage as intended. If the automatic emergency braking system does not engage as intended, it can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Volvo will notify owners, and dealers will update the Active Safety Domain Master (ASDM) software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V414000: Electrical System:Wiring:Fuses And Circuit Breakers](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volvo&model=V60&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** Failure of the low-pressure fuel pump can cause a stall, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the fuse, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.
