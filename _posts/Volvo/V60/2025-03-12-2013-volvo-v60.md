The 2013 Volvo V60 is a stylish and practical wagon that offers a blend of comfort, safety, and performance. Engine options include a 2.0L turbocharged inline-4, a 2.5L turbocharged inline-5, and a 3.0L turbocharged inline-6. The 2.0L engine produces around 240 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque, while the 2.5L engine generates approximately 250 horsepower and 266 lb-ft of torque. The top-of-the-line 3.0L T6 engine delivers around 300 horsepower and 325 lb-ft of torque. Most V60s are equipped with a 6-speed automatic transmission, although some models may have a manual option. Owners often praise the V60 for its comfortable ride, spacious interior, and distinctive design. Common complaints include relatively high maintenance costs, some electrical issues, and occasionally, issues with the infotainment system.

**Engine**

**2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4 (T5)**

*   **Oil Consumption**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Excessive oil consumption, requiring frequent top-offs. May be accompanied by blue exhaust smoke.
    *   Solution: Replacing the piston rings or potentially the entire engine in severe cases.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $5,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**2.5L Turbocharged Inline-5 (T5)**

*   **PCV System Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Clogged or failing Positive Crankcase Ventilation (PCV) system leading to increased crankcase pressure, oil leaks, and rough running.
    *   Solution: Replacing the PCV valve, hoses, and potentially the oil trap/separator.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Vacuum Pump Leak**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Loss of vacuum due to a leaky vacuum pump. This can cause reduced braking performance, particularly with power-assisted braking.
    *   Solution: Replace the vacuum pump
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500
    *   Recalls: None Found

**3.0L Turbocharged Inline-6 (T6)**

*   **Oil Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks from various engine seals and gaskets, including the valve cover gasket, rear main seal, and oil cooler lines.
    *   Solution: Replacing the leaking seals and gaskets.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $1,000+ (depending on the location and severity of the leak)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**6-Speed Automatic (All Engine Options)**

*   **Valve Body Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rough or delayed shifting, slipping between gears, or complete transmission failure. Often caused by worn or malfunctioning valves within the valve body.
    *   Solution: Replacing or rebuilding the valve body. In some cases, a complete transmission replacement may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $4,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Torque Converter Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Shuddering or vibrations during acceleration, particularly at lower speeds. Can also cause stalling or difficulty shifting.
    *   Solution: Replacing the torque converter.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,500
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Strut Mount Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Clunking or rattling noises from the front suspension, particularly when going over bumps. Can also cause excessive tire wear.
    *   Solution: Replacing the strut mounts.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500 per strut
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Rear Spring Breakage**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Vehicle sagging in the rear, uneven tire wear, and potentially a broken spring visible upon inspection.
    *   Solution: Replacing the broken rear spring(s).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600 (for both rear springs)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Software Glitches**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Malfunctions in the infotainment system, warning lights appearing for no apparent reason, or issues with electronic safety systems.
    *   Solution: Software updates or reprogramming of the affected module(s).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+ (depending on the complexity of the issue)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Central Electronic Module (CEM) Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Intermittent electrical problems affecting various vehicle systems, such as lighting, wipers, or door locks. Can be caused by corrosion or failure within the CEM.
    *   Solution: Repairing or replacing the CEM.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Sunroof Drain Clogs**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Water leaks into the cabin, particularly in the headliner or footwells, due to clogged sunroof drain tubes.
    *   Solution: Clearing the drain tubes.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $200 (depending on whether it can be DIY or requires professional help)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Navigation Screen Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Touch screen is unresponsive, screen is cracked, screen is black, or screen has lines.
    * Solution: Replace the navigation screen.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,500
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2013 Volvo V60, while praised for its safety, comfort, and design, does have some potential issues that owners should be aware of. Engine issues vary depending on the chosen engine, but oil consumption and leaks are potential concerns. Transmission issues, particularly with the valve body and torque converter, can be costly to repair. Electrical glitches and suspension wear are also common areas of concern. Overall, while the severity of these issues ranges from minor to significant, it is essential for prospective buyers to have a pre-purchase inspection performed by a qualified mechanic to identify any potential problems. Repairs can range from a few hundred dollars for minor issues to several thousand dollars for major engine or transmission repairs.
