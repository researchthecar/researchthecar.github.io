The 2023 Volvo V60 is a stylish and practical wagon, known for its comfortable ride, luxurious interior, and advanced safety features. Engine options for the 2023 model year include the B5 mild-hybrid powertrain, which combines a turbocharged 2.0-liter four-cylinder engine with a 48-volt mild-hybrid system, producing 247 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque. All engine options are paired with an 8-speed automatic transmission. Common praises include its refined interior, comfortable seats, and smooth driving experience. Criticisms often involve the sometimes-complicated infotainment system and the limited cargo space compared to some competitors.

**Engine**

**B5 Mild-Hybrid 2.0L Turbocharged I4**
*   **Coolant Leak**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaking from the water pump. May notice a decrease in coolant levels, overheating, or white smoke coming from the engine bay.
    *   Solution: Replace the water pump and associated seals.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**8-Speed Automatic**
* **Rough Shifting**
    * Description/Symptoms: Harsh or jerky shifting between gears, especially at lower speeds. May be accompanied by a clunking noise.
    * Solution: Software update to transmission control module (TCM). In some cases, valve body replacement might be required.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 for software update, $1000 - $2000+ for valve body replacement.
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Freezing/Lagging**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The Sensus infotainment system freezes, lags, or becomes unresponsive. May require a hard reset to function.
    *   Solution: Software update to the infotainment system.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 (covered under warranty or as a software update), $150+ (if needing shop time to diagnose)
    * Recalls: None Found
*   **Sensor Malfunctions**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Various sensor malfunctions related to driver-assistance features (e.g., blind-spot monitoring, lane-keeping assist) may occur.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis and replacement of the faulty sensor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+ per sensor.
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Sunroof Rattling**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rattling or squeaking noises coming from the sunroof area, especially when driving on uneven roads.
    *   Solution: Tightening of sunroof components or replacement of worn seals and gaskets.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $400+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2023 Volvo V60 generally offers a refined driving experience, but some owners have reported issues with the electrical system, particularly the infotainment system and sensors. Engine cooling and transmission issues, such as coolant leaks and rough shifting, can also occur. Interior noises, like sunroof rattling, are a less severe but common concern. The severity of these issues ranges from minor inconveniences to more costly repairs, potentially affecting the overall ownership experience. Repair costs can range from a few hundred dollars for minor fixes to several thousand for more extensive engine or transmission work.
