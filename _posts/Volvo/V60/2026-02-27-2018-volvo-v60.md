# 2018 Volvo V60 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2018 Volvo V60, buyers will find a stylish and practical wagon that blends Scandinavian luxury with robust powertrain options. While the available owner feedback on this model year is very light, reviewing standard maintenance histories and testing electronic features thoroughly during a pre-purchase inspection remains a smart approach.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged 4-Cylinder (Drive-E)

This 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine produces around 240 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque, offering a responsive daily driving experience. It balances respectable fuel efficiency with punchy mid-range acceleration, though routine maintenance of its dual-charging or turbo systems is key to long-term health.

### 2.0L Supercharged and Turbocharged 4-Cylinder (Drive-E)

Featuring both a supercharger and a turbocharger, this high-output 2.0-liter engine pushes approximately 302 horsepower for spirited performance. It delivers strong power across the entire rev range, but prospective buyers should ensure meticulous service records are present given its complex forced-induction setup.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 8-speed automatic transmission provides smooth and quick gear changes that complement Volvo's Drive-E engine lineup. It generally offers reliable operation for daily commuting, provided the transmission fluid has been inspected and maintained according to schedule.

## Electrical

### Rearview Mirrors

Exterior mirrors and associated adjustment electronics generally hold up well, though minor convenience features can occasionally exhibit memory recall quirks.

**Mirror Position Drift**
- **Symptoms:** The rearview mirrors fail to return to their original saved positions after shifting from reverse back into drive, causing the mirror angle to progressively shift lower over time.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified technician inspect the mirror memory module and actuator mechanism for software glitches or mechanical wear.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V800000: Electrical System:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volvo&model=V60&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** If the GPS location is not provided to emergency personnel it can inhibit emergency response in the event of a crash or injury.
- **What to do:** Volvo will notify owners, and dealers will correct the software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V378000: Seat Belts:Front](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volvo&model=V60&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** The flexible steel cable that connects the seat belt to the front outboard seating positions may fatigue over time, causing the seat belt to not secure the occupant in the event of a crash. An unsecured occupant has an increased risk of injury in the event of a crash.
- **What to do:** Volvo will notify owners, and dealers will replace the seat belt anchor cable for both front seats. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2018 Volvo V60 presents itself as an appealing used luxury wagon option with solid powertrain choices and limited reported owner complaints. Buyers should still conduct a thorough test drive, paying special attention to cabin electronics, mirror functions, and overall mechanical health before finalizing a purchase.
