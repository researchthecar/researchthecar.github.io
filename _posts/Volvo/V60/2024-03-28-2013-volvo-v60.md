**Introduction**

The 2013 Volvo V60 is a compact luxury wagon that offers a blend of style, performance, and practicality. It is available with a range of engine and transmission options, including a turbocharged 2.5L inline-five (T5) and a 3.0L turbocharged inline-six (T6). Both engines are paired with a six-speed automatic transmission. The V60 is known for its comfortable interior, spacious cargo area, and responsive handling. However, it has received some criticism for its lack of certain advanced features and its relatively high price.

**Engine**

**T5**

* **Intake manifold crack:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Symptoms include coolant leaks, reduced engine power, and a check engine light.
    * Solution: Replace the intake manifold.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Timing chain tensioner failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Symptoms include a rattling noise from the engine, reduced engine power, and a check engine light.
    * Solution: Replace the timing chain tensioner and timing chain.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000
    * Recalls: None Found

**T6**

* **Turbocharger failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Symptoms include a loss of boost pressure, reduced engine power, and a check engine light.
    * Solution: Replace the turbocharger.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Fuel injector failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Symptoms include rough idle, reduced engine power, and a check engine light.
    * Solution: Replace the fuel injector(s).
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $600
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**Six-Speed Automatic**

* **Shift solenoid failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Symptoms include delayed or hard shifting, and a check engine light.
    * Solution: Replace the shift solenoid(s).
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Torque converter failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Symptoms include slipping gears, shuddering during acceleration, and a check engine light.
    * Solution: Replace the torque converter.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Strut mount bearing failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Symptoms include clunking noises over bumps, vibration in the steering wheel, and reduced handling.
    * Solution: Replace the strut mount bearing(s).
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Coil spring failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Symptoms include a sagging ride height, reduced handling, and a bouncy ride.
    * Solution: Replace the coil spring(s).
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Alternator failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Symptoms include dim headlights, difficulty starting the car, and a battery warning light.
    * Solution: Replace the alternator.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Battery drain:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Symptoms include a dead battery after sitting for a period of time.
    * Solution: Test and replace the faulty component causing the battery drain.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Windshield wiper motor failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Symptoms include inoperative windshield wipers.
    * Solution: Replace the windshield wiper motor.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Infotainment system freezing:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Symptoms include the infotainment system becoming unresponsive or freezing.
    * Solution: Update the software or replace the infotainment system.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Seat heater failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Symptoms include the seat heater not activating or providing sufficient heat.
    * Solution: Test and repair or replace the faulty heating element or wiring.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

Overall, the 2013 Volvo V60 is a reliable and well-built vehicle. However, it has some potential issues that owners should be aware of. The most common problems relate to the engine, transmission, and suspension. These issues can range in severity and cost, but many can be resolved relatively inexpensively. It is important to note that these issues are not necessarily common to all V60 models, and many vehicles may experience no problems whatsoever.
