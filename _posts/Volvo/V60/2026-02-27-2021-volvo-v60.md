# 2021 Volvo V60 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2021 Volvo V60 brings sleek Scandinavian styling, luxurious interior accommodations, and advanced safety features to the compact wagon segment. As buyers consider this stylish vehicle on the used market, understanding its powertrain options and typical owner-reported experiences helps ensure a confident purchase.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged 4-Cylinder (T5)

The 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine produces approximately 250 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque, providing responsive daily power. It generally offers solid performance, though buyers should verify regular maintenance history.

### 2.0L Turbocharged and Supercharged 4-Cylinder (T6)

Combining a turbocharger and a supercharger, this engine generates around 316 horsepower for brisk acceleration. It delivers strong performance but features a more complex forced-induction system.

### 2.0L Turbocharged Plug-In Hybrid (T8 Recharge)

The T8 plug-in hybrid powertrain pairs forced induction with an electric motor to output roughly 400+ total system horsepower. It offers robust performance and electric-only driving range, though electric component health should be inspected.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

An 8-speed automatic transmission routes power smoothly across front-wheel and all-wheel-drive configurations. Regular fluid checks help maintain shift quality and long-term durability.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V682000: Seat Belts:Front:Retractor](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volvo&model=V60&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 208, "Occupant Crash Protection." An unsecured child restraint system can increase the risk of injury during a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the seat belt assemblies, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V001000: Air Bags](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volvo&model=V60&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The Supplementary Restraint System (SRS) control unit may not have been properly attached to the car body and may detach during a crash. A detached SRS control unit can decrease the performance of occupant protection systems such as air bags, seat belts, and high voltage battery disconnect, increasing the risk of injury during a crash.
- **What to do:** Volvo Car will notify owners, and dealers will inspect and, as necessary, tighten the SRS control unit mounting screws, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V179000: Electrical System:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volvo&model=V60&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The high-voltage battery may experience a short circuit within the battery module when the battery is fully charged and the vehicle is parked. A short circuit in a high-voltage battery increases the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the high-voltage battery module as necessary. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2021 Volvo V60 remains a compelling choice for used luxury wagon buyers seeking distinctive design and comprehensive safety tech. Prospective owners should perform a standard pre-purchase inspection to verify mechanical and electronic health before finalizing a deal.
