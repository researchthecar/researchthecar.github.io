# 2021 Volvo V60

### Overview
The 2021 Volvo V60 is a premium compact wagon known for its sophisticated design, advanced safety features, and comfortable interior. It offers a range of powertrain options, including efficient turbocharged and supercharged gasoline engines, often paired with an 8-speed automatic transmission. Reliability for the V60 is generally considered strong, reflecting Volvo's commitment to quality and durability. Owners typically report satisfaction with the vehicle's performance and build quality. While no vehicle is entirely immune to issues, the V60 tends to avoid widespread, recurring problems, with most reported concerns being isolated incidents or related to specific component failures rather than systemic design flaws. Regular maintenance is key to ensuring its long-term dependability.

### Summary Verdict
The 2021 Volvo V60 demonstrates a commendable level of reliability, aligning with Volvo's reputation for building durable and safe vehicles. While minor electrical glitches, particularly with the infotainment system, and occasional battery issues have been noted, these are not indicative of widespread systemic failures. The powertrain options, including the hybrid variants, are generally robust, though the hybrid battery cooling system can present an issue. The chassis and interior components are well-regarded for their longevity. Overall, the V60 is a solid choice for those seeking a reliable and luxurious wagon, with most potential issues being manageable and not prohibitively expensive to repair.
