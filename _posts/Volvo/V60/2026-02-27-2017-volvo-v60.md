# 2017 Volvo V60

### Overview
The 2017 Volvo V60 is a compact luxury wagon known for its safety features, comfortable ride, and practical interior. It offers a range of powertrain options, including efficient four-cylinder and robust five-cylinder engines, paired with automatic transmissions. Reliability for the V60 is generally considered good, with Volvo's reputation for solid engineering shining through. However, like any vehicle, specific components can experience issues over time. This report details common technical reliability concerns reported by owners and technicians for the 2017 model year, focusing on specific engine and transmission variants, as well as other key vehicle systems.

---


## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged (T5)
**Turbocharger Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of power, whistling noises from the engine bay, increased oil consumption.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the turbocharger unit.
- **Cost:** $1,200 - $2,500

**Coolant Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Visible coolant puddles under the vehicle, engine overheating, sweet smell from the engine bay.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of leaking hoses, seals, or the water pump.
- **Cost:** $300 - $800


### 2.0L Turbocharged & Supercharged (T6 - Drive-E)
**Timing Belt Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Engine misfires, rough idling, potential catastrophic engine damage if it breaks.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the timing belt and tensioners.
- **Cost:** $600 - $1,000

**Oil Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Visible oil stains under the vehicle, low oil level warnings, burning oil smell.
- **Solution:** Replacement of seals and gaskets (e.g., valve cover gasket, oil pan gasket).
- **Cost:** $400 - $900

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Geartronic Automatic
**Transmission Fluid Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Leaking fluid from the transmission pan or lines, rough shifting, slipping gears.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of seals, gaskets, or transmission lines.
- **Cost:** $300 - $700

**Hesitation or Jerking Shifts**
- **Symptoms:** Delayed engagement, jerky movements when shifting between gears.
- **Solution:** Software update for transmission control module or internal transmission repair.
- **Cost:** $200 - $1,500

## Suspension

### Standard Chassis
**Worn Control Arm Bushings**
- **Symptoms:** Clunking noises over bumps, vague steering feel, uneven tire wear.
- **Solution:** Replacement of control arm bushings or the entire control arm assembly.
- **Cost:** $400 - $800

**Strut Mount Bearing Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Popping or creaking noises when turning the steering wheel, especially at low speeds.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the strut mount bearings.
- **Cost:** $250 - $500

## Electrical

### General Electrical System
**Infotainment System Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Screen freezing, unresponsiveness, Bluetooth connectivity issues, audio problems.
- **Solution:** Software updates, hard reset, or replacement of the infotainment module.
- **Cost:** $300 - $1,200

**Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Difficulty starting the vehicle, warning lights on the dashboard, dead battery.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis of parasitic draw, replacement of faulty modules or battery.
- **Cost:** $200 - $600

### Summary Verdict
The 2017 Volvo V60 generally exhibits good reliability, aligning with Volvo's reputation for building durable vehicles. The most frequently reported issues tend to be related to the powertrain, particularly with the turbocharged engines experiencing turbocharger or coolant system problems, and automatic transmissions showing occasional leaks or shifting anomalies. Electrical gremlins, such as infotainment system glitches and battery drain, are also noted. While suspension components like control arm bushings can wear over time, these are often considered normal wear and tear for a vehicle of this age and mileage. Overall, the V60 remains a solid choice, but potential buyers should be aware of these specific areas and ensure thorough pre-purchase inspections.
