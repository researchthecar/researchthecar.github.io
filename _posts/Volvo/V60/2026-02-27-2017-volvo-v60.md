# 2017 Volvo V60 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2017 Volvo V60, buyers will find a stylish and practical wagon that blends Scandinavian luxury with everyday utility. While owner feedback is relatively limited, prospective buyers should review specific mechanical and structural areas reported by drivers to ensure a thorough pre-purchase inspection.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged 4-Cylinder (Drive-E)

The 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine produces approximately 240 horsepower, offering a responsive and efficient daily driving experience. Regular maintenance is key to keeping this modern powerplant running smoothly.

### 2.0L Supercharged and Turbocharged 4-Cylinder

This dual-charged 2.0-liter engine pushes out around 302 horsepower, providing brisk acceleration across the rev range. Buyers should verify proper service history on forced-induction components during inspection.

### 3.0L Turbocharged Inline-6

The 3.0-liter turbocharged inline-six engine delivers roughly 325 horsepower for a smooth and powerful performance feel. It represents a robust traditional option for enthusiasts seeking a six-cylinder configuration.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Geartronic Automatic

The 8-speed automatic transmission pairs with Drive-E powertrains to optimize fuel economy and power delivery. Overall reliability is generally sound, though software calibration can influence shift smoothness.

**Power Cut and Shifting Hesitation**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have reported a noticeable power interruption or jerky shift behavior when transitioning between 1st and 2nd gear, particularly when driving uphill.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified technician check for transmission software updates or adaptation resets that may improve shift quality.

### 6-Speed Geartronic Automatic

The 6-speed automatic transmission works alongside inline-six engine configurations to provide reliable, traditional gear changes. Fluid changes at recommended intervals help ensure long-term durability.

## Exterior

### Sunroof

Exterior components generally hold up well, though isolated glass incidents warrant a careful visual check.

**Glass Shattering**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have experienced the sunroof suddenly shattering while driving, accompanied by a loud bang and falling glass.
- **Solution:** Inspect the sunroof glass and surrounding frame for any pre-existing stress cracks or alignment issues before purchasing.

## Interior

### Front Seats

Cabin materials are durable and comfortable, but buyers should test front seat stability during a test drive.

**Seat Rocking or Wobbling**
- **Symptoms:** The front seats may develop a subtle rocking or shifting movement, most noticeably during low-speed braking or directional changes.
- **Solution:** Check seat track mounting bolts and anchor points to ensure they are properly torqued.

## Safety

### Collision Avoidance System

Volvo's advanced safety suite offers comprehensive protection features, though electronic sensors require unobstructed surfaces to operate correctly.

**Slow-Speed Avoidance Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have noted unexpected behavior or perceived defects in low-speed collision prevention functionality.
- **Solution:** Ensure sensors and camera lenses are clean and have a dealer verify system calibration if warnings persist.

- **What to do:** Volvo will notify owners, and dealers will correct the software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

- **What to do:** Volvo will notify owners, and dealers will replace all four door latches, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2017 Volvo V60 remains a compelling choice for used luxury wagon shoppers, offering distinct powertrain choices and high safety standards. While minor complaints regarding low-speed shifting feel, seat stability, and isolated sunroof issues have been reported by some owners, a thorough pre-purchase inspection will help secure a dependable vehicle.
