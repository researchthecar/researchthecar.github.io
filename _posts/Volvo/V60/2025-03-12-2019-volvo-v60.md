The 2019 Volvo V60 is a stylish and practical wagon that offers a blend of Scandinavian design, comfort, and safety. Available engine options included the T5, a 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine producing 250 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque; the T6, a 2.0-liter turbocharged and supercharged four-cylinder engine producing 316 horsepower and 295 lb-ft of torque; and the T8 eAWD plug-in hybrid, combining a 2.0-liter turbocharged and supercharged engine with an electric motor for a combined output of 400 horsepower and 472 lb-ft of torque. All engines are paired with an Aisin-Warner eight-speed automatic transmission. Owners often praise the V60 for its comfortable ride, elegant interior, and advanced safety features. Common dislikes include the complicated infotainment system and some reported reliability concerns.

**Engine**

**2.0L Turbocharged T5**

*   **Oil Consumption**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners report excessive oil consumption, requiring frequent top-offs between oil changes. Symptoms include low oil level warnings and visible exhaust smoke.
    *   Solution: Monitor oil levels frequently and address any leaks or issues identified during inspections. Consider a piston ring replacement if oil consumption is severe.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $5,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**2.0L Turbocharged and Supercharged T6**

*   **Supercharger Clutch Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Premature failure of the supercharger clutch, leading to reduced performance and a whining noise from the engine.
    *   Solution: Replace the supercharger clutch assembly.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000
    *   Recalls: None Found

**2.0L Turbocharged and Supercharged T8 eAWD Plug-In Hybrid**

*   **Battery Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Reduced electric range, failure to charge, or error messages related to the hybrid battery system.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis of the battery management system and replacement of faulty battery modules or the entire battery pack, if necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $5,000 - $15,000+ (depending on the extent of the damage and if a whole battery replacement is needed)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**Aisin-Warner Eight-Speed Automatic (All Engine Options)**

*   **Rough Shifting/Hesitation**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported rough or jerky shifting, particularly between lower gears. Hesitation during acceleration has also been noted.
    *   Solution: Transmission fluid flush and software update may resolve minor issues. Valve body replacement or complete transmission rebuild/replacement may be necessary for more severe cases.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 (fluid flush) - $5,000+ (transmission replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Rear Air Suspension Leaks (If Equipped)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: If equipped with rear air suspension, some owners have reported air leaks, leading to a sagging rear end and a rough ride.
    *   Solution: Locate and repair the air leak, or replace the faulty air spring or compressor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+ per air spring or compressor.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Freezing/Crashing**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The Sensus infotainment system can be prone to freezing, crashing, and slow response times.
    *   Solution: Software updates can sometimes resolve the issue. In some cases, the entire infotainment unit may need to be replaced.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 (software update) - $1,500+ (unit replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found
*   **Sensor Malfunctions (e.g., Parking Sensors, Blind Spot Monitoring)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Malfunctioning parking sensors or blind spot monitoring systems, providing false warnings or failing to detect obstacles.
    *   Solution: Diagnose and replace the faulty sensor or module.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+ per sensor.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Panoramic Sunroof Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported issues with the panoramic sunroof, including leaks, difficulty opening or closing, and unusual noises.
    *   Solution: Clean and lubricate the sunroof tracks. Repair or replace damaged seals or components.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $1,000+ (depending on the severity).
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Dashboard Creaking/Rattling**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported creaking or rattling noises coming from the dashboard area, particularly in cold weather.
    *   Solution: Identify the source of the noise and apply felt pads or other sound-deadening materials to eliminate the vibration.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $300 (depending on the complexity of the repair).
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2019 Volvo V60 has some common issues, ranging from minor infotainment glitches and interior rattles to more significant engine and transmission problems. While some issues can be resolved with software updates or minor repairs, others, such as engine oil consumption or transmission failures, can be costly to fix. The severity of these issues varies among owners, but potential buyers should be aware of these potential problems and thoroughly inspect the vehicle before purchasing. Owners should be prepared for potential repair costs ranging from a few hundred dollars to several thousand dollars, depending on the issue.
