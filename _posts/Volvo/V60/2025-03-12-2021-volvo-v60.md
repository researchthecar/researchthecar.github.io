The 2021 Volvo V60 is a stylish and practical wagon known for its comfortable ride, elegant interior, and advanced safety features. Engine options include the T5 (2.0L turbocharged inline-4 producing 250 hp and 258 lb-ft of torque), the T6 (2.0L turbocharged and supercharged inline-4 producing 316 hp and 295 lb-ft of torque), and the T8 eAWD Plug-In Hybrid (2.0L turbocharged and supercharged inline-4 with electric motor producing 415 hp and 494 lb-ft of torque). All engines are paired with an 8-speed automatic transmission. People generally like the V60's sophisticated design, comfortable seats, and ample cargo space. Common complaints revolve around the complexity of the infotainment system and some reported reliability issues.

**Transmission**

**8-Speed Automatic (All Engines)**
*   **Harsh Shifting/Hesitation**
    * Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported experiencing rough or delayed shifting, particularly when accelerating from a stop or during gear changes. The transmission may hesitate or jerk during these maneuvers.
    * Solution: A software update to the transmission control module (TCM) can often resolve this issue. In some cases, a transmission flush or replacement of the valve body may be necessary.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 (software update) - $1,500+ (valve body/transmission replacement)
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: The Sensus infotainment system can be prone to glitches, freezing, slow response times, and connectivity issues. This includes problems with the touchscreen, navigation, Bluetooth, and Apple CarPlay/Android Auto integration.
    * Solution: Performing a system reset, software updates, or, in more severe cases, replacement of the infotainment unit.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 (reset/update) - $1,500+ (unit replacement)
    * Recalls: None Found

*   **Sensor Malfunctions**
    * Description/Symptoms: Issues with various sensors, including those for the blind-spot monitoring system, parking sensors, and collision avoidance systems, have been reported. These can result in false alarms, system failures, or inaccurate readings.
    * Solution: Diagnosing the specific sensor causing the issue and replacing it.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Sunroof/Moonroof Problems**
    * Description/Symptoms: Some owners have experienced issues with the sunroof or moonroof, including difficulty opening or closing, unusual noises, or leaks.
    * Solution: Inspection and repair or replacement of the sunroof motor, seals, or entire assembly.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,200+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**
*   **No Issues Found**

**Summary:**

The 2021 Volvo V60, while appreciated for its design and comfort, has some reported issues. The most prevalent seem to be related to the infotainment system and potential transmission issues. Electrical sensor malfunctions and sunroof issues are also possibilities. The severity and cost of repairs vary, but owners should be aware of the potential for glitches in the infotainment system, as well as potential sensor problems, and be observant of any unusual transmission behavior.
