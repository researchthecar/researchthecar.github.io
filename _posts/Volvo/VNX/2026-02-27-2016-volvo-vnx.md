# 2016 Volvo VNX Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2016 Volvo VNX is a heavy-duty vocational tractor designed for demanding heavy-haul applications. Buyers considering a used model should carefully inspect the powertrain and auxiliary systems, as heavy-duty commercial trucks often undergo demanding service cycles. While commercial reliability heavily depends on prior maintenance history, understanding the available engine and transmission configurations helps prospective buyers select the right specification for their operational needs.

---

## Engine

### Volvo D16 16.1L Turbo-Diesel Inline-6

The Volvo D16 is a robust 16.1-liter turbocharged diesel inline-6 engine, delivering robust horsepower and high torque outputs tailored for heavy-haul operations. It offers strong pulling power for vocational applications, though maintenance costs can be higher than smaller displacement alternatives.

## Transmission

### Volvo I-Shift Automated Manual Transmission

The Volvo I-Shift is an automated manual transmission designed to optimize fuel efficiency and ease of operation in heavy-duty vocational fleets. It provides smooth shifting characteristics when properly maintained.

### Eaton Fuller Manual Transmission

Certain VNX configurations may feature heavy-duty Eaton Fuller manual transmissions, offering traditional gear selection and proven mechanical durability for severe-service applications.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V097000: Steering](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volvo&model=VNX&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** Also, the bolt connecting the upper steering shaft to the lower steering shaft may not have been properly tightened. Separation of the steering shaft will result in complete loss of steering which may lead to a vehicle crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 15V026000: Parking Brake:Driveline:Hydraulic:Actuator](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volvo&model=VNX&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** This air pressure in turn, if not vented, may prevent full release of the actuator unit, and may induce brake drag and may result in overheated wheelend service brakes, increasing the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Volvo will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the brake actuator and replace, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2016 Volvo VNX is a capable heavy-haul vocational truck built for demanding workloads. Prospective buyers should prioritize a comprehensive mechanical inspection focusing on the engine, transmission, and frame condition before purchase.
