The 2015 Volvo VNX is a heavy-duty truck designed for vocational applications like heavy hauling, construction, and logging. Engine options include the Volvo D11, D13, and D16. The D13 produces around 500 horsepower and 1850 lb-ft of torque depending on the specific tuning. Drivers often praise the VNX for its robust build, comfortable cab, and Volvo's I-Shift automated manual transmission. Common complaints can include the high cost of parts and maintenance, and some report issues with the emissions systems and electrical components.

**Engine**

**Volvo D13**

*   **EGR Cooler Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Coolant loss, white smoke from the exhaust, engine overheating, and potential engine damage if not addressed promptly.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the EGR cooler.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Diesel Particulate Filter (DPF) Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Frequent regeneration cycles, reduced engine power, and eventually, a clogged DPF. Can also trigger fault codes.
    *   Solution: DPF cleaning or replacement. Issues can sometimes be resolved with forced regeneration.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 for cleaning, $2,000 - $5,000+ for replacement.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Fuel Injector Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rough idling, misfires, reduced fuel economy, and black smoke from the exhaust.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the faulty fuel injector(s).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+ per injector, depending on labor and diagnostic time.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**Volvo I-Shift (Automated Manual)**

*   **Shift Hesitation/Rough Shifting**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Delays in shifting, jerky or harsh shifts, and difficulty finding the correct gear. This can be caused by a worn clutch, faulty solenoids, or software issues.
    *   Solution: Could require clutch replacement, solenoid replacement, or a software update.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: Clutch replacement can range from $2,000 - $4,000+, solenoid replacement $500-$1,500+, software update $100-$300.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Loss of Power/Engagement**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Transmission slips or completely disengages, resulting in a loss of power. This can be due to internal damage, low fluid levels, or a faulty transmission control module (TCM).
    *   Solution: Could require a transmission rebuild or replacement, fluid top-off, or TCM replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: Transmission rebuild $3,000-$7,000+, replacement $5,000-$10,000+, TCM replacement $1,000-$2,500+.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Air Suspension Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Truck leaning to one side, ride height problems, air compressor running constantly, hissing sounds. Most commonly found in air bags, airline connections, and leveling valves.
    *   Solution: Identify and repair the leak. This may involve replacing air bags, airlines, fittings, or leveling valves.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200-$1,000+ depending on the component.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Sensor Failures (Various)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Various warning lights, engine performance issues, transmission problems, and ABS faults. These can be caused by faulty sensors such as ABS sensors, coolant temperature sensors, and oil pressure sensors.
    *   Solution: Diagnose the specific sensor failure and replace the faulty sensor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+ per sensor, depending on the sensor type and location.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Wiring Harness Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Intermittent electrical problems, short circuits, and corrosion in wiring connectors. Especially common in areas exposed to harsh weather.
    *   Solution: Repair or replace damaged sections of the wiring harness. Clean and protect connectors.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200-$1,500+ depending on the severity and location of the damage.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Headlight Condensation**
    * Description/Symptoms: Moisture building up inside the headlight housings. Reduces the effectiveness of the headlights, and can damage the bulbs and electrical components.
    * Solution: Remove the headlights and dry the housings, reseal the headlights.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+, depending on if you repair yourself or have a shop do it.
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **HVAC Blower Motor Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The blower motor stops working, resulting in no air blowing from the vents, or only blowing at certain speeds.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the blower motor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600+.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2015 Volvo VNX is a capable vocational truck, but owners may experience issues with the EGR system, DPF, and I-Shift transmission. Electrical problems can also arise. The severity and cost of these repairs can vary greatly, but addressing them promptly is crucial to maintaining the truck's reliability.
