**Introduction to the 2017 Volvo VNX**

The 2017 Volvo VNX is a heavy-duty truck produced by Volvo Trucks. It is available with a variety of engine and transmission options, including:

* Engine Options:
    * D13 455 (455 horsepower, 1,550 lb-ft torque)
    * D13 475 (475 horsepower, 1,650 lb-ft torque)
    * D13 500 (500 horsepower, 1,850 lb-ft torque)
* Transmission Options:
    * Volvo I-Shift 6-speed automated manual transmission
    * Volvo I-Shift 10-speed automated manual transmission

The VNX is a popular choice for heavy-duty hauling applications, with owners praising its fuel efficiency, durability, and comfort. However, like all vehicles, it has some potential issues that owners should be aware of.

**Engine**

**D13 455**

* **Turbocharger Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, excessive smoke, and a whistling noise.
    * Solution: Replace the turbocharger.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**D13 475**

* **Injector Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Hard starting, rough running, and increased fuel consumption.
    * Solution: Replace the faulty injector(s).
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000 per injector
    * Recalls: None Found

**D13 500**

* **Oil Leak from Rear Main Seal:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Oil leak at the rear of the engine.
    * Solution: Replace the rear main seal.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**I-Shift 6-speed**

* **Shifting Issues:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears, or gears slipping out of place.
    * Solution: Repair or replace the transmission control unit (TCU).
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000
    * Recalls: None Found

**I-Shift 10-speed**

* **No Issues Found**

**Suspension**

* **Front Axle Wear:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Excessive wear on the front axle components, leading to poor handling and alignment issues.
    * Solution: Replace worn parts, such as bushings, ball joints, and tie rods.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Alternator Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Charging system issues, leading to weak or dead batteries.
    * Solution: Replace the alternator.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **No Issues Found**

**Interior**

* **Dashboard Panel Cracking:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Cracks in the plastic dashboard panel, especially around the air vents.
    * Solution: Replace the dashboard panel.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2017 Volvo VNX is a capable and reliable heavy-duty truck, but it has some potential issues that owners should be aware of. The severity and cost of these issues vary, but overall they are not major concerns. Potential buyers should thoroughly research the specific issues related to their desired engine and transmission configuration before making a decision.
