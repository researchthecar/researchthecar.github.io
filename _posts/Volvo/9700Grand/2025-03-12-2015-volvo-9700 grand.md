The 2015 Volvo 9700 Grand is a coach bus known for its comfort, safety features, and fuel efficiency. While specific engine and transmission options can vary depending on the market and original configuration, a common engine choice is the Volvo D13 engine, a 12.8-liter inline-6 diesel. This engine typically produces around 425-500 horsepower and 1650-1770 lb-ft of torque. Owners often praise the 9700 Grand for its smooth ride, spacious interior, and reliability on long journeys. Common dislikes include the cost of maintenance and specialized repair needs.

**Engine**

**Volvo D13**

*   **Turbo Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Reduced power, black smoke from exhaust, unusual noises from the engine bay.
    *   Solution: Replace the turbocharger.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,500 - $5,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **EGR Valve Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Poor engine performance, increased fuel consumption, check engine light.
    *   Solution: Clean or replace the EGR valve.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Fuel Injector Problems**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Misfiring, rough idling, reduced fuel economy.
    *   Solution: Replace faulty fuel injectors.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $2,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**Volvo I-Shift (Automated Manual Transmission)**

*   **Clutch Slippage**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Jerky acceleration, failure to shift properly, RPM flare during gear changes.
    *   Solution: Replace the clutch assembly.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Shift Actuator Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears, transmission getting stuck in a gear, error messages on the dashboard.
    *   Solution: Replace or repair the shift actuator.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Software Glitches**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Erratic shifting behavior, transmission entering limp mode.
    *   Solution: Re-flash or update the transmission control module (TCM) software.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Air Suspension Compressor Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Bus not leveling properly, air leaks, compressor running constantly or not at all.
    *   Solution: Replace the air suspension compressor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $2,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Air Spring Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Noticeable hissing sound, the bus leaning to one side, uneven ride height.
    *   Solution: Replace leaking air springs.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+ per air spring.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Multiplexing System Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Intermittent failures of lights, door controls, or other electrical components.
    *   Solution: Diagnose and repair wiring harness or replace faulty multiplexing modules.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $3,000+ depending on complexity.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Battery Drain**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Battery draining overnight, difficulty starting the bus.
    *   Solution: Identify and repair the parasitic drain (e.g., faulty relay, aftermarket accessory).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $1,000+ depending on the source of the drain.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Door Mechanism Malfunctions**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Doors failing to open or close properly, sticking, or making excessive noise.
    *   Solution: Repair or replace the door actuators, hinges, or other components of the door mechanism.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,500+ per door.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **HVAC System Problems**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Inadequate heating or cooling, unusual noises from the HVAC system, failure of the blower motor.
    *   Solution: Repair or replace the HVAC components, such as the compressor, blower motor, or control module.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $2,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2015 Volvo 9700 Grand, while generally reliable, can experience issues typical of large vehicles with complex systems. Engine and transmission issues, while potentially expensive, are not overwhelmingly common but should be considered. Suspension and electrical problems are more frequently reported. Interior issues, particularly with the HVAC system, can also occur. Overall, potential buyers should be aware of these potential problems and factor in the cost of possible repairs and maintenance when considering a purchase. Repair costs can range from a few hundred dollars for minor fixes to several thousand dollars for major component replacements.
