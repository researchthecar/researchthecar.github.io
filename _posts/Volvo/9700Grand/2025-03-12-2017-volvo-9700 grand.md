The 2017 Volvo 9700 Grand is a popular choice for tour and charter bus operators, known for its comfortable ride, spacious interior, and overall reliability. This coach is typically powered by a Volvo D13 engine, known for its robust performance, offering around 425-500 horsepower and up to 1750 lb-ft of torque, coupled with a Volvo I-Shift automated manual transmission. While generally well-regarded, some owners have reported specific issues across different components. Owners appreciate the comfortable seating, smooth ride, and large luggage capacity, but some dislike the turning radius and occasional electronic glitches.

**Engine**

**Volvo D13**

*   **EGR Cooler Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Coolant loss, white smoke from the exhaust, engine overheating, and potential engine damage if not addressed promptly. This can be due to corrosion and cracks in the EGR cooler.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the EGR cooler.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Fuel Injector Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rough idling, decreased fuel economy, lack of power, and potential engine misfires. Could be due to a clogged or failing injector.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis and replacement of faulty fuel injectors.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+ per injector
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**Volvo I-Shift**

*   **Shifting Problems**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Erratic shifting, delayed engagement, slipping between gears, and occasional difficulty engaging reverse. This can be caused by worn clutch components or problems with the transmission control module (TCM).
    *   Solution: Clutch replacement, TCM reprogramming or replacement, or transmission overhaul.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,500 - $8,000+ (depending on the severity and required repair)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Transmission Oil Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Visible oil leaks underneath the vehicle, low transmission fluid levels, and potential transmission damage due to lack of lubrication. Often originates from seals or gaskets.
    *   Solution: Replacing the leaking seals and gaskets.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Air Suspension Compressor Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The bus may lean to one side, ride lower than normal, or the air suspension system may fail to inflate properly. This is typically due to a failing compressor.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the air suspension compressor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $2,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Air Bag Leaks**
    * Description/Symptoms: Hissing sound, leaning, the bus may ride lower than normal, or the air suspension system may fail to inflate properly
    * Solution: Repair or replacement of leaking air bag
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Multiplexing System Glitches**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Intermittent issues with lighting, HVAC, or other electronically controlled systems. This can be caused by faulty wiring or a malfunctioning control module.
    *   Solution: Diagnostic troubleshooting to identify the source of the problem, followed by wiring repair or module replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $2,000+ (highly variable depending on the specific issue)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Battery Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Difficulty starting, frequent battery drain, and the need for jump starts.
    *   Solution: Replacing the battery.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+ per battery
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Door Actuator Failures**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Doors failing to open or close properly, requiring manual operation. This is often caused by a faulty door actuator.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the door actuator.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+ per door
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **HVAC System Malfunctions**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Inconsistent cooling or heating, uneven temperature distribution throughout the bus, and blower motor failures.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis and repair of the HVAC system, including component replacement as needed.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $3,000+ (depending on the specific issue)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2017 Volvo 9700 Grand, while generally reliable, can experience issues related to its engine, transmission, suspension, electrical systems, exterior components, and interior features. The severity and cost of these issues vary greatly. Engine and transmission problems tend to be the most expensive to repair, while electrical glitches and HVAC malfunctions can range in cost. Overall, prospective buyers should be aware of these potential issues and consider a thorough inspection before purchasing a used vehicle.
