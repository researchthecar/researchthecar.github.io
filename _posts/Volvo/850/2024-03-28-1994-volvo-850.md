**Introduction:**

The 1994 Volvo 850 is a popular Swedish front-wheel-drive sedan renowned for its safety, comfort, and performance. The 850 was offered with a variety of engine and transmission options, including a 2.4L inline-5 and a 2.3L turbocharged inline-5 engine, paired with a 4-speed automatic or a 5-speed manual transmission. Despite its overall reliability, the 850 has several common issues that owners should be aware of.

**Engine**

**2.4L inline-5**

- **Intake Manifold Gasket Failure:** A faulty intake manifold gasket can cause coolant and oil leaks, leading to overheating. Symptoms include white smoke from the exhaust, low coolant levels, and oil in the coolant. Solution: Replacing the intake manifold gasket. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
- **Ignition Coil Failure:** Worn-out ignition coils can cause misfiring, rough idling, and starting problems. Symptoms include decreased engine performance, engine hesitation, and an illuminated check engine light. Solution: Replacing the ignition coils. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
- **Timing Belt Failure:** Neglecting to change the timing belt at the recommended intervals (70,000 miles) can result in a snapped belt, leading to catastrophic engine damage. Symptoms include a lack of engine power, engine overheating, and a grinding noise from the engine. Solution: Replacing the timing belt. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+

**2.3L turbocharged inline-5**

- **Turbocharger Failure:** A faulty turbocharger can lead to a loss of power, increased oil consumption, and a burning smell. Symptoms include a loud whistling sound from the engine bay, blue smoke from the exhaust, and decreased engine performance. Solution: Replacing the turbocharger. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,000+
- **Intercooler Leakage:** A leaking intercooler can result in a loss of turbocharger boost, reduced engine power, and poor fuel efficiency. Symptoms include a drop in engine performance and a decrease in boost pressure. Solution: Replacing the intercooler or repairing any leaks. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
- **Vacuum Hoses Deterioration:** Deteriorated vacuum hoses can cause various engine performance issues, such as idle problems, stalling, and poor fuel economy. Symptoms include a rough idle, vacuum leaks, and a drop in engine power. Solution: Replacing the faulty vacuum hoses. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+

**Transmission**

**4-speed automatic**

- **Transmission Slipping:** Worn-out transmission components can lead to transmission slipping, resulting in a lack of acceleration and difficulty shifting gears. Symptoms include delayed gear shifts, slipping out of gear, and increased transmission noise. Solution: Rebuilding or replacing the transmission. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+

**5-speed manual**

- **Clutch Wear:** Engaging and disengaging the clutch frequently can lead to clutch wear, resulting in difficulty shifting gears or a slipping clutch. Symptoms include a chattering noise when engaging the clutch, difficulty changing gears, and a burning smell. Solution: Replacing the clutch. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
- **Shift Linkage Malfunction:** A malfunctioning shift linkage can make it difficult or impossible to engage gears. Symptoms include difficulty shifting gears, grinding noises when shifting, and a loose or sloppy gear lever. Solution: Replacing the shift linkage. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+

**Suspension**

- **Strut Mounting Bearing Failure:** Worn-out strut mounting bearings can cause a clunking noise when hitting bumps or turning. Symptoms include a loud knocking or banging sound from the suspension, especially over rough roads. Solution: Replacing the strut mounting bearings. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600+
- **Ball Joint Failure:** Loose or damaged ball joints can cause a clunking noise and a decrease in steering precision. Symptoms include a knocking sound when turning or hitting bumps, excessive play in the steering wheel, and uneven or premature tire wear. Solution: Replacing the ball joints. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+

**Electrical**

- **Electrical Harness Problems:** Faulty wiring or loose connections in the electrical harness can lead to various electrical issues, such as problems with lighting, gauges, or other electrical systems. Symptoms include intermittent or non-functioning electrical components, dimming or flickering lights, and electrical shorts. Solution: Inspecting and repairing the electrical harness. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,000+
- **Alternator Failure:** A failing alternator can result in reduced charging performance and a loss of electrical power. Symptoms include a dimly lit dashboard, drained battery, and an illuminated battery warning light. Solution: Replacing the alternator. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+

**Exterior**

- **Rusty Fuel Filler Neck:** The fuel filler neck can develop rust over time, leading to fuel leaks or difficulty refueling. Symptoms include a fuel leak around the filler neck, a strong fuel odor, and an illuminated check engine light. Solution: Replacing the fuel filler neck. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+

**Interior**

- **Climate Control Malfunctions:** Malfunctioning climate control systems can result in problems with heating, cooling, or ventilation. Symptoms include a lack of airflow, inadequate heating or cooling, and a malfunctioning air conditioner. Solution: Repairing or replacing the faulty components in the climate control system. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+
- **Power Window Failure:** Worn-out window motors or switches can lead to power window failures. Symptoms include difficulty rolling up or down windows, intermittent window operation, or complete window failure. Solution: Replacing the faulty window motor or switch. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $700+

**Recalls:**

- 94V108000: A fault in the braking system
- 94V175000: A fault in the seat belts
- 94V237000: A fault in the airbags

**Summary:**

The 1994 Volvo 850 is a generally reliable car, but it does have some common issues that owners should be aware of. Engine and transmission problems are the most severe and expensive to fix, while electrical and interior issues are less common and less costly. Most issues can be fixed for under $1,000, with the exception of major engine or transmission repairs. Regular maintenance and proper care can help prevent many of these issues from occurring.
