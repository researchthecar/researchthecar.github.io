**Introduction**

The 1991 Volvo 780 was a luxurious grand tourer that offered a smooth and comfortable ride. It was powered by a range of inline-six engines and came with a 4-speed automatic transmission. The 780 was praised for its elegant design, spacious interior, and safety features, but it also had some drawbacks, including limited performance and an aging interior.

**Engine**

* **B230F Inline-Six (141 hp, 159 lb-ft torque)**

    * **Timing Belt Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: The timing belt can snap, causing catastrophic engine damage. The symptoms include a sudden loss of power, a loud bang, and smoke from the engine.
        * Solution: Replace the timing belt according to the manufacturer's recommended schedule.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,200+
        * Recalls: None Found

    * **Oil Leaks**
        * Description/Symptoms: The engine may develop oil leaks from various seals and gaskets, causing a decrease in oil levels and visible leaks.
        * Solution: Replace the faulty seals or gaskets.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600+

* **B230FT Inline-Six (162 hp, 189 lb-ft torque)**

    * **Turbocharger Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: The turbocharger can fail, resulting in reduced engine power, black smoke from the exhaust, and a whistling sound.
        * Solution: Replace the turbocharger.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+

* **B280F Inline-Six (177 hp, 192 lb-ft torque)**

    * **Head Gasket Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: The head gasket can fail, causing coolant and oil to mix, overheating, and white smoke from the exhaust.
        * Solution: Replace the head gasket.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+

**Transmission**

* **AW30-40 4-Speed Automatic**

    * **Slipping Gears**
        * Description/Symptoms: The transmission may slip out of gears, causing a loss of power and acceleration.
        * Solution: Rebuild or replace the transmission.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **No Issues Found**

**Electrical**

* **Alternator Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The alternator can fail, leading to a loss of battery power, dimming headlights, and a flickering dashboard.
    * Solution: Replace the alternator.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Sunroof Seal Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The sunroof seal can deteriorate, allowing water to leak into the cabin.
    * Solution: Replace the sunroof seal.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+

**Interior**

* **Dashboard Cracks**
    * Description/Symptoms: The dashboard can develop cracks due to aging or exposure to sunlight.
    * Solution: There is no permanent fix, but cracks can be filled with epoxy or vinyl repair kits.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500

Regular maintenance and timely repairs can help extend the life of the vehicle and prevent major breakdowns.
