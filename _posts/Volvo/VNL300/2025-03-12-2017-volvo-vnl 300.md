The 2017 Volvo VNL 300 is a popular semi-truck known for its comfort and fuel efficiency. It is often favored for regional and long-haul routes. Engine options include the Volvo D11, D13, and Cummins X15. The Volvo D13 engine in its various configurations can produce between 405 and 500 horsepower, and torque figures ranging from 1450 to 1850 lb-ft. Transmission choices generally consist of Volvo's I-Shift automated manual transmission. Drivers appreciate the VNL 300's spacious cab and smooth ride, but some report concerns about the complexity of its electronic systems and potential maintenance costs.

**Engine**

**Volvo D13**

*   **EGR Cooler Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Engine overheating, loss of coolant, white smoke from exhaust, and a noticeable coolant smell.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the EGR cooler.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Diesel Particulate Filter (DPF) Clogging/Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Reduced engine power, frequent regeneration cycles, dashboard warning lights, and potential engine damage.
    *   Solution: Cleaning or replacement of the DPF. In some cases, related sensors (differential pressure sensor) may also need replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $4,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Cummins X15**

*   **Injector Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rough running engine, misfires, reduced fuel economy, excessive smoke, and potential engine damage.
    *   Solution: Replacing the faulty fuel injector(s).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $2,500+ (depending on the number of injectors replaced)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Water Pump Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Overheating engine, coolant leaks, and potential engine damage.
    *   Solution: Replacing the water pump.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $700 - $1,800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**Volvo I-Shift Automated Manual Transmission**

*   **Shift Actuator Malfunction**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears, erratic shifting, transmission getting stuck in gear, and dashboard warning lights.
    *   Solution: Replacement or recalibration of the shift actuator.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $3,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Clutch Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Slipping clutch, difficulty engaging gears, and grinding noises.
    *   Solution: Clutch replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,500 - $5,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Air Spring Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Uneven ride height, sagging suspension, and reduced ride comfort.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the faulty air spring(s).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+ (per air spring)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Sensor Failures (Various)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: A wide range of issues depending on the sensor involved. Common ones include ABS sensor failures (ABS light, brake performance issues), coolant temperature sensor (incorrect temperature readings, overheating), and wheel speed sensors (ABS/traction control problems).
    *   Solution: Diagnosis to identify the faulty sensor and replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+ (per sensor, including diagnosis)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Wiring Harness Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Intermittent electrical problems, dashboard warning lights, and malfunctioning components.
    *   Solution: Repair or replacement of damaged wiring harnesses.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $2,000+ (depending on the severity and location of the damage)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Headlight Assembly Condensation**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Moisture build-up inside the headlight housing, reducing visibility.
    *   Solution: Replacing the headlight assembly or attempting to seal the existing assembly.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,000+ (per headlight assembly)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **HVAC System Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Inconsistent heating or cooling, weak airflow, or failure of the system to operate.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis and repair or replacement of faulty components (e.g., blower motor, compressor, blend door actuator).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

The 2017 Volvo VNL 300, while generally a reliable truck, can suffer from various issues primarily concerning its engine (especially emissions-related components like the DPF and EGR cooler), transmission (I-Shift actuator and clutch), and electrical system (sensor failures and wiring issues). The severity ranges from minor inconveniences to potentially costly repairs, with estimated costs varying widely depending on the specific problem and the extent of the damage. Addressing these issues promptly is crucial to maintaining the truck's performance and longevity.
