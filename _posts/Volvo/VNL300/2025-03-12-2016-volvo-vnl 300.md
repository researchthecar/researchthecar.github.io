The 2016 Volvo VNL 300 is a popular semi-truck known for its fuel efficiency and comfortable cab. Engine options for the 2016 VNL 300 include the Volvo D11, D13, and Cummins ISX15. The Volvo D13 engine, a common choice, produces around 405-500 horsepower and 1450-1850 lb-ft of torque depending on the specific tuning. Transmission options include the Volvo I-Shift automated manual transmission and manual transmissions like the Eaton Fuller. Owners appreciate the VNL 300 for its comfortable ride and spacious sleeper, while some dislike the complexity of the Volvo's electrical systems and potential for sensor-related issues.

**Engine**

**Volvo D13**

*   **EGR Cooler Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Engine overheating, coolant loss, white smoke from the exhaust.
    *   Solution: Replace the EGR cooler.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Diesel Particulate Filter (DPF) Clogging**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Reduced engine power, frequent regenerations, DPF light illumination.
    *   Solution: Clean or replace the DPF.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $4,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Fuel Injector Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rough idling, misfires, decreased fuel economy, black smoke from the exhaust.
    *   Solution: Replace faulty fuel injectors.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Cummins ISX15**

*   **EGR Valve Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Reduced engine power, poor fuel economy, check engine light.
    *   Solution: Replace the EGR valve.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Turbocharger Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, excessive smoke, unusual noises from the turbocharger.
    *   Solution: Replace or rebuild the turbocharger.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $5,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Camshaft Wear**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Engine knocking, reduced performance, metal shavings in the oil.
    *   Solution: Replace the camshaft and related components.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $3,000 - $7,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Volvo D11**
*   **Fuel Injector Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Poor fuel economy, loss of power, rough idling, misfires.
    *   Solution: Replace faulty fuel injectors.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**Volvo I-Shift Automated Manual Transmission**

*   **Shift Actuator Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears, error codes related to shift actuators, transmission stuck in gear.
    *   Solution: Replace the faulty shift actuator.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $3,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Clutch Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Slipping clutch, difficulty engaging gears, clutch pedal vibrations.
    *   Solution: Replace the clutch assembly.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Transmission Control Module (TCM) Problems**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Erratic shifting, error codes related to the TCM, communication issues.
    *   Solution: Reprogram or replace the TCM.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $2,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Eaton Fuller Manual Transmission**

*   **Shifter Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears, stiff or loose shifter.
    *   Solution: Replace shifter assembly
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Clutch Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Slipping clutch, difficulty engaging gears, clutch pedal vibrations.
    *   Solution: Replace the clutch assembly.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Air Suspension Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Truck leaning to one side, air compressor running constantly, hissing sound from air leaks.
    *   Solution: Repair or replace leaking air bags, airlines, or leveling valves.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+ per component.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Shock Absorber Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Bouncy ride, poor handling, excessive tire wear.
    *   Solution: Replace worn shock absorbers.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+ per axle.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Sensor Failures**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Check engine light, various warning lights, performance issues, incorrect readings on gauges.
    *   Solution: Diagnose and replace faulty sensors (e.g., coolant temperature sensor, oil pressure sensor, wheel speed sensor).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+ per sensor.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Wiring Harness Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Intermittent electrical problems, short circuits, corrosion in wiring connectors.
    *   Solution: Repair or replace damaged wiring harnesses.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,500+ depending on the severity.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Lighting Problems**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Headlights, taillights, or marker lights not working.
    *   Solution: Replace bulbs, repair wiring, or replace faulty lighting modules.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $500+ per light.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Hood Latch Failures**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Hood not latching properly or opening unexpectedly.
    *   Solution: Replace the hood latch assembly.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Mirror Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Broken or loose mirrors, non-functional power mirrors.
    *   Solution: Replace the mirror assembly or repair the power mirror mechanism.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+ per mirror.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **HVAC System Problems**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Inadequate heating or cooling, blower motor not working, refrigerant leaks.
    *   Solution: Repair or replace faulty HVAC components such as the compressor, blower motor, or condenser.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $2,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Gauge Cluster Malfunctions**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Gauges not displaying accurate readings or failing to function.
    *   Solution: Repair or replace the gauge cluster.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Seat Problems**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Air ride seat not inflating or deflating properly, broken seat adjustments.
    *   Solution: Repair or replace seat components, such as the air bladder or adjustment mechanisms.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2016 Volvo VNL 300 can experience a range of issues, from engine and transmission problems to suspension, electrical, exterior, and interior concerns. The severity and cost of repairs vary widely. Engine-related issues like EGR cooler failures and DPF clogging can be costly, as can transmission problems like shift actuator failures in the I-Shift. Electrical issues, particularly sensor failures and wiring harness problems, are also common. While some issues are relatively minor, others can lead to significant downtime and expense. Potential buyers should be aware of these common problems and thoroughly inspect any used VNL 300 before purchase.
