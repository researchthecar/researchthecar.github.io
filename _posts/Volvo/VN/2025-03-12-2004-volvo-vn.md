The 2004 Volvo VN series is a heavy-duty truck primarily used for long-haul transportation. Engine options included various Volvo diesel engines, such as the D12 and D16, with horsepower ranging from 400 to over 600 depending on the specific configuration. Transmission options included manual and automated manual transmissions. Owners often appreciated the VN for its comfortable cab, fuel efficiency, and reliability, but some common complaints included electrical issues and maintenance costs associated with heavy-duty trucks.

**Engine**

**Volvo D12 Engine**
* **Injector Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Rough running engine, loss of power, excessive smoke, and potential for engine damage.
    * Solution: Replace faulty injectors.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1500+ per injector.
    * Recalls: None Found

* **EGR Valve Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: Reduced engine power, poor fuel economy, black smoke, and potential for engine overheating.
    * Solution: Replace or clean the EGR valve.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Volvo D16 Engine**
* **Cylinder Head Cracking**
    * Description/Symptoms: Coolant loss, overheating, white smoke from exhaust, and potential engine damage.
    * Solution: Replace the cylinder head.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $3000 - $8000+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Turbocharger Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, excessive black smoke, whining noise from the turbocharger.
    * Solution: Replace the turbocharger.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1500 - $4000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**Volvo Automated Manual Transmission (I-Shift)**
* **Shifting Problems**
    * Description/Symptoms: Erratic shifting, delayed engagement, difficulty shifting gears, and potential for transmission failure.
    * Solution: Recalibration of the transmission control module (TCM), repair or replacement of solenoids, or complete transmission overhaul.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $5000+ depending on the severity.
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Clutch Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Slipping clutch, difficulty engaging gears, and complete loss of power transfer.
    * Solution: Replace the clutch.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1500 - $4000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Air Suspension Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: Sagging suspension, uneven ride height, air leaks, and failure of air bags.
    * Solution: Replace faulty air bags, repair air lines, or replace the air compressor.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1500+ per air bag.
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Shock Absorber Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Bouncy ride, poor handling, and excessive tire wear.
    * Solution: Replace worn shock absorbers.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+ per shock absorber.
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Wiring Harness Problems**
    * Description/Symptoms: Intermittent electrical issues, short circuits, and malfunctions of various components.
    * Solution: Repair or replace damaged wiring harnesses.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $2000+ depending on the extent of the damage.
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Sensor Failures**
    * Description/Symptoms: Malfunctioning engine sensors, transmission sensors, and ABS sensors leading to inaccurate readings and system failures.
    * Solution: Replace faulty sensors.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $300+ per sensor.
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Alternator Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Battery light illuminated on the dashboard, dimming headlights, and difficulty starting the engine.
    * Solution: Replace the alternator.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Lighting Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: Headlights, taillights, and marker lights malfunctioning due to corrosion or bulb failure.
    * Solution: Replace bulbs, clean corroded connectors, or replace entire light assemblies.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $20 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Mirror Damage**
    * Description/Symptoms: Broken or damaged side mirrors due to impact or wear.
    * Solution: Replace the side mirror assembly.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+ per mirror.
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **HVAC Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: Inadequate heating or cooling, malfunctioning blower motor, and faulty temperature controls.
    * Solution: Repair or replace the blower motor, replace the HVAC control module, or recharge the refrigerant.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $1000+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Gauge Cluster Problems**
    * Description/Symptoms: Inaccurate readings, flickering gauges, and complete failure of the instrument cluster.
    * Solution: Repair or replace the instrument cluster.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2004 Volvo VN is a reliable truck, but has some common issues related to its engine, transmission, suspension, electrical system, exterior and interior. Engine issues, such as injector failure and cylinder head cracking, can be costly to repair. Transmission problems, particularly with the I-Shift, can also be expensive. Electrical gremlins, including wiring harness issues and sensor failures, are also prevalent. Overall, while the VN offers a comfortable and efficient ride, potential buyers should be aware of these common issues and factor in the cost of potential repairs.
