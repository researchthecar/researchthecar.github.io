The 2009 Volvo S40 is a compact sedan known for its safety, comfort, and understated style. It was available with several engine options, including a 2.4L inline-5 engine producing 168 hp and 170 lb-ft of torque, and a turbocharged 2.5L inline-5 engine generating 227 hp and 236 lb-ft of torque. The naturally aspirated engine was typically paired with a 5-speed automatic transmission, while the turbo engine was often paired with a 5-speed automatic, or a 6-speed manual. Owners often praise its comfortable seats, solid build quality, and safety features. Common criticisms include a somewhat cramped rear seat, and potential reliability issues related to some of its electronic systems.

**Engine**

**2.4L Inline-5**

*   **Coil Pack Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Engine misfires, rough idle, check engine light.
    *   Solution: Replace faulty coil pack(s).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **PCV System Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rough idle, vacuum leaks, oil leaks, whistling noise from the engine. Clogged PCV can cause excessive pressure and damage seals.
    *   Solution: Replace the PCV valve, breather box, and hoses as necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**2.5L Turbocharged Inline-5**

*   **Turbocharger Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, whistling or whining noise from the turbo, excessive smoke from the exhaust, check engine light.
    *   Solution: Replace the turbocharger.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1500 - $3000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Vacuum Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rough idle, poor performance, check engine light.
    *   Solution: Identify and repair vacuum leaks by replacing cracked or damaged hoses.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $400+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**5-Speed Automatic (for both engine options)**

*   **Valve Body Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Harsh or erratic shifting, slipping between gears, failure to shift, check engine light.
    *   Solution: Replacement or rebuild of the valve body. In some cases, a transmission rebuild or replacement is necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $3000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Front Control Arm Bushings**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Clunking noise from the front suspension, especially when going over bumps, vague steering.
    *   Solution: Replace the control arm bushings or the entire control arm assembly.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600+ per side
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Strut Mounts**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Squeaking or groaning noise from the suspension, particularly when turning the steering wheel, loose steering feel.
    *   Solution: Replace strut mounts.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+ per side
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Central Electronic Module (CEM) Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Intermittent electrical problems such as lights not working, issues with the instrument cluster, problems with power windows, door locks not working, or the car not starting.
    *   Solution: Repair or replacement of the CEM. This often requires reprogramming by a Volvo dealer or specialist.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Dimming Interior Lights**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Interior lights slowly dim over time and will not return to full brightness.
    *   Solution: Replace interior light bulbs
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $20 - $50
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Side Mirror Motor Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Side mirrors fail to adjust or fold properly.
    *   Solution: Replace the side mirror assembly or the motor within.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+ per mirror.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Sunroof Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Sunroof not opening or closing properly, getting stuck, or making unusual noises.
    *   Solution: Repair or replace the sunroof motor, track, or related components.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2009 Volvo S40, while praised for its safety and comfort, does have some common issues that potential buyers should be aware of. Engine problems, especially with the turbo models, can be costly, as can electrical issues related to the CEM. Suspension components also tend to wear and need replacement. Overall, the severity of these issues ranges from moderate to significant, and repair costs can vary widely depending on the specific problem. Pre-purchase inspection and a thorough maintenance history review are recommended.
