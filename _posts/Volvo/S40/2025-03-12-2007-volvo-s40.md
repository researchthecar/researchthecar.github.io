The 2007 Volvo S40 is a compact sedan known for its safety, comfort, and understated Swedish design. Engine options include a 2.4L naturally aspirated inline-5 producing 168 hp and 170 lb-ft of torque, and a turbocharged 2.5L inline-5 (T5) producing 220 hp and 236 lb-ft of torque. Transmission options include a 5-speed manual, a 5-speed automatic (with the 2.4L engine), and a 6-speed manual and a 5-speed automatic (with the T5 engine). Owners often praise the S40 for its comfortable ride and solid build quality. Common complaints revolve around electrical gremlins and age-related wear on components.

**Engine**

**2.4L Inline-5**

*   **Coil Pack Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Misfires, rough running engine, check engine light.
    *   Solution: Replace faulty coil pack(s).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**2.5L Inline-5 (T5)**

*   **Turbocharger Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Whining noise from the turbo, loss of power, check engine light.
    *   Solution: Inspect turbocharger for damage or wear. Replace turbocharger if necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Vacuum Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rough idle, poor fuel economy, check engine light.
    *   Solution: Smoke test to find vacuum leaks. Replace cracked or damaged vacuum lines.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $400+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**5-Speed Automatic (Aisin Warner AW50-55)**

*   **Valve Body Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Harsh shifting, slipping between gears, delayed engagement.
    *   Solution: Replace or rebuild the valve body. Transmission flush.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Strut Mount Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Clunking noise from the front suspension, especially over bumps.
    *   Solution: Replace strut mounts.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Control Arm Bushing Wear**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Vague steering, wandering, clunking noises.
    *   Solution: Replace control arm bushings or entire control arms.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $700+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Central Electronic Module (CEM) Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Intermittent electrical problems, including issues with windows, locks, lights, and starting.
    *   Solution: Diagnose the CEM and repair or replace it if necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $1200+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **DIM (Driver Information Module) Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The instrument cluster (speedometer, tachometer, etc.) may flicker, dim, or completely fail.
    *   Solution: Repair or replace the DIM.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Side Mirror Motor Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The side mirror adjustment motor may fail, making it impossible to adjust the mirrors.
    *   Solution: Replace the side mirror assembly.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Sunroof Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Sunroof may get stuck, make excessive noise, or leak.
    *   Solution: Clean and lubricate the sunroof tracks. If the motor is failing, replace it.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

The 2007 Volvo S40 is a generally reliable vehicle but can be prone to certain issues as it ages. Electrical gremlins, particularly those related to the CEM, can be frustrating and costly. Suspension and transmission issues are also common areas of concern. Addressing these issues proactively can help keep the S40 running smoothly. The cost of repairs can vary widely depending on the severity of the problem and the mechanic's labor rates.
