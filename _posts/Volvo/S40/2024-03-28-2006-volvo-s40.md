**Introduction**

The 2006 Volvo S40 is a compact executive car available with various engine and transmission configurations. The 2.4-liter inline-5 engine produces 168 horsepower and 170 lb-ft of torque, while the T5 version boasts a turbocharged 2.5-liter inline-5 engine that generates 218 horsepower and 236 lb-ft of torque. Transmission options include a 5-speed manual or a 5-speed automatic gearbox. The S40 offers a comfortable ride, spacious interior, and high safety ratings but has received criticism for its lackluster handling and expensive maintenance costs.

**Engine**

* **2.4-liter inline-5**

  * **Intake manifold gasket failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaks, overheating, performance loss
    * Solution: Replace intake manifold gasket
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
    * Recalls: None Found
  * **Oil leaks from valve cover gasket:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks visible under the valve cover
    * Solution: Replace valve cover gasket
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **T5 2.5-liter turbocharged inline-5**

  * **Turbocharger failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Loss of boost pressure, reduced power, excessive smoke from exhaust
    * Solution: Replace turbocharger
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
    * Recalls: None Found
  * **Fuel injector failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Hard starting, rough idle, decreased fuel efficiency
    * Solution: Replace failed fuel injector
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+ per injector
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

* **5-speed manual**

  * **Gear linkage failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears, grinding noises
    * Solution: Replace gear linkage
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **5-speed automatic**

  * **Transmission slipping:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Inability to engage gears, delayed shifting
    * Solution: Transmission overhaul or replacement
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Front struts:**
  * **Strut mounts failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Clunking or rattling noises, poor handling
    * Solution: Replace strut mounts
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+ per strut
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Battery failure:**
  * Description/Symptoms: Inability to start the vehicle, dim headlights
  * Solution: Replace battery
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
  * Recalls: None Found
* **Starter failure:**
  * Description/Symptoms: Difficulty starting the vehicle, clicking noises
  * Solution: Replace starter motor
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
  * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Headlight assembly condensation:**
  * Description/Symptoms: Moisture build-up inside headlight housings
  * Solution: Replace headlight assembly or reseal it
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+ per headlight
  * Recalls: None Found
* **Fog light failure:**
  * Description/Symptoms: Fog lights not illuminating
  * Solution: Replace fog light assembly or fix wiring issues
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+ per fog light
  * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Climate control system malfunctions:**
  * Description/Symptoms: Failure to adjust temperature, fan issues
  * Solution: Repair or replace climate control module
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
  * Recalls: None Found
* **Power window switch failure:**
  * Description/Symptoms: Unable to operate power windows
  * Solution: Replace power window switch or fix wiring issues
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+ per window
  * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2006 Volvo S40 faces several common issues that can be costly to repair. Issues like turbocharger failure, transmission slipping, and electrical malfunctions can result in significant expenses. While the vehicle offers a comfortable ride and high safety ratings, potential owners should be aware of the potential repair costs before purchasing.
