The 2008 Volvo S40 is a compact sedan known for its safety, comfort, and Scandinavian design. Engine options include a 2.4-liter naturally aspirated inline-5 producing 168 horsepower and 170 lb-ft of torque, and a 2.5-liter turbocharged inline-5 producing 227 horsepower and 236 lb-ft of torque. Transmission options include a 5-speed manual, 5-speed automatic, and a 6-speed manual. Owners appreciate the S40's comfortable ride, stylish interior, and safety features. Common complaints include higher maintenance costs compared to other vehicles in its class and some electrical quirks.

**Engine**

**2.4L Inline-5**

*   **Coil Pack Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Misfires, rough running engine, check engine light illumination.
    *   Solution: Replace faulty coil pack(s).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+ depending on how many coils are replaced.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Electronic Throttle Module (ETM) Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Erratic idle, hesitation during acceleration, limp mode activation.
    *   Solution: Replace or repair the ETM.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**2.5L Turbo Inline-5 (T5)**

*   **Turbocharger Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, whistling or whining noise from the engine bay, check engine light illumination.
    *   Solution: Replace the turbocharger.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1500 - $3000+
    *   Recalls: None Found
*   **Vacuum Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rough idle, poor fuel economy, check engine light.
    *   Solution: Identify and repair or replace cracked or damaged vacuum lines.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $400+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**5-Speed Automatic (Both Engines)**

*   **Valve Body Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Harsh shifting, slipping between gears, delayed engagement.
    *   Solution: Replace or rebuild the valve body.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $2000+
    *   Recalls: None Found
*   **Torque Converter Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Shuddering during acceleration, stalling, transmission noise.
    *   Solution: Replace the torque converter.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1000 - $2500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Front Control Arm Bushing Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Clunking noise from the front suspension, uneven tire wear, vague steering.
    *   Solution: Replace the control arm bushings.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+ per side.
    *   Recalls: None Found
*   **Strut Mount Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Squeaking or creaking noise from the front suspension, especially when turning.
    *   Solution: Replace the strut mounts.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+ per side.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Central Electronic Module (CEM) Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Intermittent electrical problems such as malfunctioning windows, lights, or central locking system.
    *   Solution: Replace or reprogram the CEM.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1500+
    *   Recalls: None Found
*   **Door Lock Actuator Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Door not locking or unlocking, clicking noise from the door.
    *   Solution: Replace the door lock actuator.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+ per door.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Side Mirror Motor Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Side mirror will not adjust electrically.
    *   Solution: Replace the side mirror assembly.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+ per side.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Sunroof Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Sunroof getting stuck, making grinding noises, or leaking water.
    *   Solution: Repair or replace the sunroof motor or mechanism.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2008 Volvo S40 presents a mix of common issues across engine, transmission, suspension, and electrical systems. Engine issues, particularly with the turbocharger in the T5 models and ETM for the 2.4L, can be expensive. Transmission issues, primarily affecting the 5-speed automatic, can also lead to significant repair bills. Suspension components like control arm bushings and strut mounts are typical wear items. Electrical quirks, especially related to the CEM and door lock actuators, can be frustrating. Overall, while the S40 is a comfortable and safe vehicle, owners should be prepared for potentially higher maintenance costs and the possibility of encountering some of these common issues.
