The 2006 Volvo S40 is a compact sedan known for its safety, comfort, and distinctive Scandinavian design. Engine options included a 2.4L inline-5 producing 168 hp and 170 lb-ft of torque, and a turbocharged 2.5L inline-5 (T5) delivering 218 hp and 236 lb-ft of torque. Transmission options were a 5-speed manual or a 5-speed automatic for the 2.4i, and a 6-speed manual or 5-speed automatic for the T5. Owners often praised the S40 for its comfortable seats, solid build quality, and overall driving experience. Common criticisms included repair costs, some electrical gremlins, and occasional issues with specific engine components.

**Engine**

**2.4i 2.4L Inline-5**

*   **Faulty Mass Airflow (MAF) Sensor**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Engine hesitation, poor fuel economy, rough idling, and the check engine light illuminating.
    *   Solution: Replacing the MAF sensor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Failed Ignition Coils**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Misfires, rough running engine, check engine light. Can happen with age or excessive use.
    *   Solution: Replacing the faulty ignition coil(s).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $80 - $200+ per coil (including labor)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**T5 2.5L Turbo Inline-5**

*   **Turbocharger Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, whining noise from the turbo, excessive oil consumption, and potentially the check engine light coming on.
    *   Solution: Replacing the turbocharger, and sometimes associated oil lines and seals.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1200 - $2500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Vacuum Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rough idle, boost issues, poor fuel economy, and a hissing sound from the engine bay.
    *   Solution: Identifying and replacing damaged vacuum lines.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $400+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**5-Speed Automatic (Both 2.4i and T5)**

*   **Slipping or Harsh Shifting**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Delayed engagement, slipping between gears, or harsh shifts, especially between 2nd and 3rd gear.
    *   Solution: Fluid flush and software update *may* alleviate the problem. If the problem persists, transmission replacement or rebuild is often required.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300 for fluid flush and software; $2500 - $4500+ for replacement or rebuild.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Worn Control Arm Bushings**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Clunking noises from the front suspension, especially over bumps, vague steering feel.
    *   Solution: Replacing the worn control arm bushings or the entire control arm assembly.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600+ per side
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Failed Strut Mounts**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Knocking or creaking sounds from the front suspension, especially when turning the steering wheel.
    *   Solution: Replacing the strut mounts.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $700+ per pair
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Central Electronic Module (CEM) Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Intermittent electrical problems, such as malfunctioning lights, wipers, or power windows. Can also cause starting issues.
    *   Solution: Replacing or repairing the CEM. Often requires programming by a Volvo dealer or specialist.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Dimming Instrument Cluster**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The instrument cluster lights dimming or flickering intermittently.
    *   Solution: Repairing the instrument cluster or replacing it with a new or refurbished unit.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Fog Light Housing Cracking**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Cracks in the fog light lenses, allowing moisture to enter and damage the bulbs.
    *   Solution: Replacing the fog light assembly.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+ per side
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Climate Control Fan Motor Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The blower motor for the climate control system not working, working only on certain speeds, or making excessive noise.
    *   Solution: Replacing the blower motor resistor or the entire blower motor assembly.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2006 Volvo S40 can be a comfortable and stylish vehicle, but potential buyers should be aware of common issues, particularly those related to the turbocharger (on T5 models), transmission, suspension, and electrical systems. Repairs can be relatively expensive, especially those involving the CEM or a transmission rebuild. Addressing these issues proactively can help maintain the S40's reliability and driving enjoyment.
