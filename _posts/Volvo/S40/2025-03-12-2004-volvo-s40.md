The 2004 Volvo S40 is a compact sedan known for its safety, comfort, and understated styling. Engine options for the 2004 model year include a 2.4L inline-5 producing 168 horsepower and 170 lb-ft of torque, and a turbocharged 2.0L inline-4. Transmission options include a 5-speed manual and a 5-speed automatic. Owners often praise the S40 for its comfortable seats, solid build quality, and responsive handling. Common complaints revolve around the cost of maintenance and repairs, some electrical quirks, and occasionally, issues with specific engine components.

**Engine**

**2.4L Inline-5**

*   **Faulty Mass Air Flow (MAF) Sensor**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rough idling, poor acceleration, decreased fuel economy, and the check engine light may illuminate.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the MAF sensor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Failed Throttle Position Sensor (TPS)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Erratic shifting, hesitation during acceleration, and the check engine light illuminating.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the TPS.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4**

*   **Turbocharger Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Whining noise from the engine, loss of power, excessive oil consumption, and the check engine light may illuminate.
    *   Solution: Replacement or rebuild of the turbocharger.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Boost Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, hissing sound during acceleration, and decreased fuel economy. Often caused by cracked or loose hoses.
    *   Solution: Inspect and replace damaged hoses or tighten connections.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $200+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**5-Speed Automatic**

*   **Slipping or Harsh Shifting**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Delayed engagement, jerky shifts, and overall rough operation of the transmission.
    *   Solution: Transmission fluid flush and fill. If problems persist, further diagnostics may be needed.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+ (fluid change); $1500 - $3000+ (rebuild or replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Valve Body Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Hard shifts, failure to shift, transmission getting stuck in a gear
    *   Solution: Replace the valve body with an upgraded version.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $750 - $1,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Worn Control Arm Bushings**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Clunking noise from the front suspension, especially when going over bumps. Imprecise steering.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the control arm bushings or the entire control arm assembly.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+ per side
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Failed Strut Mounts**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Knocking noise from the suspension, particularly during turns or over uneven surfaces. Reduced ride comfort.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the strut mounts.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+ per side
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Central Electronic Module (CEM) Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Intermittent electrical problems, such as malfunctioning lights, wipers, or power windows. Difficulty starting the vehicle.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis and potential reprogramming or replacement of the CEM.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1200+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Faulty Window Regulator**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Window does not go up or down, or goes up and down very slowly
    *   Solution: Replacing the window regulator assembly
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Side Mirror Motors Failing**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Side mirror adjustment failing or no longer functioning
    *   Solution: Side mirror replacement
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+ per mirror
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Sunroof Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Sunroof not opening or closing properly, getting stuck, or making excessive noise.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis and repair of the sunroof mechanism, which may involve cleaning, lubrication, or replacement of components.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Broken cup holder**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Cupholder no longer works or cannot be used.
    *   Solution: Replacing the cupholder assembly
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $150+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2004 Volvo S40, while generally reliable, can experience a range of issues, particularly as it ages. Engine problems such as MAF sensor failure and turbocharger issues on the turbocharged models can lead to moderate to high repair costs. Transmission problems, especially with the automatic transmission, can be expensive to address. Suspension issues like worn control arm bushings are common and can impact ride quality. Electrical problems, particularly those related to the CEM, can be complex and costly to diagnose and repair. While some issues are relatively minor, others can be more severe and require significant investment to fix, so potential buyers should carefully inspect the vehicle and review its maintenance history.
