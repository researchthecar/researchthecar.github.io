The 2009 Volvo V50 is a compact wagon known for its safety, Scandinavian design, and practicality. Engine options include the 2.4i inline-5 producing 168 horsepower and 170 lb-ft of torque, the T5 turbocharged inline-5 producing 227 horsepower and 236 lb-ft of torque, and the T5 AWD, which is the same engine as the T5, paired with all-wheel drive. Transmission options include a 5-speed manual, a 6-speed manual (T5 only), and a 5-speed automatic. Owners often praise the V50 for its comfortable ride, spacious cargo area, and distinctive styling. Common complaints include relatively high running costs and some reported electrical issues.

**Engine**

**2.4i Inline-5**
*   **Rough Idle/Stalling**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The engine may exhibit a rough idle, especially when cold, and can stall unexpectedly. This can be more severe at idle with the AC on.
    *   Solution: Replacing the mass airflow sensor, cleaning the throttle body, or addressing vacuum leaks may resolve the issue.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $400+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**T5/T5 AWD Turbocharged Inline-5**

*   **Turbocharger Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, whining noises from the engine bay, and/or a check engine light indicating a turbocharger malfunction.
    *   Solution: Turbocharger replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1200 - $2500+
    *   Recalls: None Found
*   **Oil Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks can occur from the turbocharger oil lines, valve cover gasket, or rear main seal.
    *   Solution: Replace the leaking components.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $1000+ depending on the location and severity of the leak.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**5-Speed Automatic**

*   **Harsh Shifting**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Abrupt or jerky shifts between gears, especially noticeable during acceleration or deceleration.
    *   Solution: Performing a transmission fluid flush, or replacing the valve body.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $1200+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Worn Suspension Bushings**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Clunking or rattling noises from the front or rear suspension, especially when driving over bumps.
    *   Solution: Replace worn control arm bushings, sway bar links, or strut mounts.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Central Electronic Module (CEM) Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Intermittent electrical malfunctions, such as issues with the headlights, turn signals, wipers, or remote keyless entry. In some cases, the car may not start.
    *   Solution: Reflashing or replacing the CEM.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Power Window Problems**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Power windows failing to operate, or operating intermittently.
    *   Solution: Replacing the window regulator or motor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $400+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Headlight Condensation**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Moisture buildup inside the headlight housings.
    *   Solution: Removing the headlight assembly, drying out the moisture, and resealing the unit. Replacing the headlight assembly if resealing is not effective.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Climate Control Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Malfunctioning climate control system, including issues with the blower motor, blend door actuator, or temperature regulation.
    *   Solution: Replacing the faulty components.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $600+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2009 Volvo V50, while appreciated for its design and practicality, exhibits several common issues. Engine problems, particularly turbocharger issues and oil leaks on the T5 models, can be expensive to repair. Electrical gremlins stemming from the CEM can also be frustrating and costly. Suspension wear and interior component failures, while less severe, contribute to the overall ownership experience. Before purchasing a 2009 V50, a thorough inspection by a qualified mechanic is highly recommended to identify and address potential problems.
