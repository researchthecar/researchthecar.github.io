The 2017 Volvo VNL 400 is a popular Class 8 semi-truck known for its fuel efficiency, comfortable cab, and overall reliability. The 2017 VNL 400 could be equipped with a Volvo D13 engine, offering horsepower ranging from 405 to 500 and torque from 1450 to 1850 lb-ft. Other engine options included the Cummins ISX15 and the Volvo D11. Transmission options included the Volvo I-Shift automated manual transmission, Eaton Fuller manual transmissions, and Allison automatic transmissions. Owners generally appreciate the VNL 400's fuel economy and driver comfort but occasionally report issues with the aftertreatment system, electrical glitches, and some transmission problems.

**Engine**

**Volvo D13**

*   **Diesel Particulate Filter (DPF) Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Frequent DPF regeneration cycles, reduced engine power, DPF warning light illumination. May be caused by faulty sensors or excessive soot buildup.
    *   Solution: Forced DPF regeneration, cleaning the DPF, or replacing the DPF. Inspect and replace sensors as needed.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $3,000+ depending on whether cleaning or replacement is necessary.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Exhaust Gas Recirculation (EGR) Valve Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rough idling, decreased fuel efficiency, increased emissions, check engine light.
    *   Solution: Replacing the EGR valve and cleaning the EGR cooler.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Turbocharger Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, excessive black smoke, unusual noises from the turbocharger.
    *   Solution: Turbocharger replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Cummins ISX15**

*   **Camshaft Wear/Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Engine ticking or knocking noises, reduced engine performance, difficulty starting.
    *   Solution: Camshaft replacement and inspection of related components (lifters, followers).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $3,000 - $6,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Fuel Injector Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rough idling, misfires, reduced fuel economy, white or black smoke.
    *   Solution: Fuel injector replacement and proper programming.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000 per injector, plus labor.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Volvo D11**

*   **Injector Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Rough idling, poor engine performance, and/or a check engine light.
    * Solution: Replace the fuel injectors.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $4,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**Volvo I-Shift**

*   **Shift Actuator Malfunction**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears, erratic shifting behavior, transmission warning light.
    *   Solution: Replacing or recalibrating the shift actuator.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Clutch Problems**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Clutch slippage, difficulty engaging gears, shuddering during acceleration.
    *   Solution: Clutch replacement and inspection of related components (throwout bearing, pilot bearing).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Eaton Fuller Manual Transmission**

*   **Shift Fork Wear/Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting into specific gears, grinding noises during shifting.
    *   Solution: Replacing the worn or broken shift fork.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+ depending on the extent of damage and labor involved.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Bearing Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Whining or rumbling noises from the transmission, especially at certain speeds.
    *   Solution: Replacing the worn bearings and inspecting related components for damage.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $3,000+ depending on the number of bearings needing replacement and labor.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Allison Automatic Transmission**

*   **Torque Converter Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Slipping, shuddering, or complete failure to transfer power from the engine to the transmission.
    *   Solution: Torque converter replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,500 - $5,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Valve Body Problems**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Erratic shifting, harsh shifting, or failure to shift gears.
    *   Solution: Valve body replacement or rebuild.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Air Spring Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Sagging of the suspension, uneven ride height, air leaks, poor ride quality.
    *   Solution: Replacing the faulty air spring(s).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800 per air spring, plus labor.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Shock Absorber Wear/Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Excessive bouncing, poor handling, reduced ride comfort.
    *   Solution: Replacing the worn shock absorbers.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300 per shock absorber, plus labor.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Sensor Failures (Various)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Check engine light, various warning lights, inaccurate readings from gauges. Common sensors include: coolant temperature sensor, oil pressure sensor, and wheel speed sensors.
    *   Solution: Replacing the faulty sensor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+ depending on the sensor and labor.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Wiring Harness Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Intermittent electrical problems, short circuits, difficulty starting, warning lights.
    *   Solution: Repairing or replacing the damaged section of the wiring harness.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+ depending on the complexity of the repair.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Headlight/Taillight Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Dim or non-functioning lights, condensation inside the lens.
    *   Solution: Bulb replacement, lens replacement, or complete headlight/taillight assembly replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $500+ depending on the type of light and repair needed.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Mirror Damage**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Cracks in the mirror glass, broken mirror housings.
    *   Solution: Replacing the mirror glass or the entire mirror assembly.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **HVAC System Problems**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Inadequate heating or cooling, strange noises from the HVAC system.
    *   Solution: Repairing or replacing the faulty components (compressor, condenser, blower motor).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Seat Issues (Air Ride)**
    * Description/Symptoms: The air ride suspension for the driver or passenger seat is not functioning properly. The seat might not hold air or adjust properly.
    * Solution: Replacing the seat suspension system.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2017 Volvo VNL 400 generally receives positive reviews, but some common issues exist. Engine-related issues, particularly concerning the aftertreatment system (DPF, EGR), can be costly to repair. Transmission problems, especially with the I-Shift and Allison automatic transmissions, can also lead to significant expenses. Electrical glitches and suspension problems are less severe but still require attention. Overall, while the VNL 400 is a reliable truck, potential buyers should be aware of these common issues and factor in potential repair costs.
