The 2015 Volvo XC90 marked a significant redesign for the Swedish automaker's flagship SUV. Available engine options included the T5 2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4 (240 hp, 258 lb-ft torque), the T6 2.0L Turbocharged and Supercharged Inline-4 (316 hp, 295 lb-ft torque), and the D5 2.0L Turbocharged Diesel Inline-4 (225 hp, 347 lb-ft torque, not available in the US). All engines were paired with an Aisin 8-speed automatic transmission. Owners often praise the XC90's luxurious interior, comfortable ride, and advanced safety features. Common complaints revolve around the complex infotainment system, some reliability concerns with early production models, and higher repair costs.

**Electric Motor**

No Issues Found

**Transmission**

**Aisin 8-Speed Automatic**
* **Harsh Shifting/Hesitation**
    * Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported instances of harsh shifting, particularly between lower gears, or hesitation upon acceleration. This can be intermittent.
    * Solution: Software updates to the transmission control module (TCM) can sometimes resolve the issue. If the problem persists, valve body replacement or transmission rebuild may be necessary.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+ for software updates; $1,500 - $4,000+ for valve body/transmission work.
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Air Suspension Failure (If Equipped)**
    * Description/Symptoms: The optional air suspension can experience failures, leading to the vehicle sagging or failing to maintain proper ride height. Common causes include leaks in the air struts, compressor failure, or issues with the air suspension control module.
    * Solution: Replacing the faulty air strut(s), compressor, or control module.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $2,000+ per strut; $500 - $1,500+ for compressor or control module.
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Sensus Infotainment System Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: The Sensus infotainment system is known for being complex and can exhibit various issues, including slow response times, freezing, black screens, Bluetooth connectivity problems, and navigation glitches.
    * Solution: Software updates are often the first step. If the problem persists, the Sensus unit may need replacement.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 - $300+ for software updates; $1,000 - $2,500+ for Sensus unit replacement.
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Battery Drain**
    * Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported issues with the battery draining overnight or within a few days of non-use. This can be caused by a parasitic drain from various electrical components.
    * Solution: Identifying and addressing the source of the parasitic drain, which could involve replacing a faulty module or fixing a wiring issue.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+ depending on the source of the drain.
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Panoramic Sunroof Leaks**
    * Description/Symptoms: Water leaks from the panoramic sunroof, often due to clogged drain tubes.
    * Solution: Clearing the drain tubes and resealing the sunroof if necessary.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Power Tailgate Malfunction**
    * Description/Symptoms: The power tailgate may fail to open or close properly, or may make unusual noises.
    * Solution: Replacing the tailgate struts or the motor.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2015 Volvo XC90, while praised for its luxury and safety, can present owners with several potential issues. The most common concerns involve the infotainment system and the optional air suspension, which can be costly to repair. While not all XC90s will experience these problems, it's important for potential buyers to be aware of them and factor them into their purchasing decision. The harsh shifting issues seem to be the most common transmission issue, which can often be solved with a software update but can also be a more expensive fix if it involves a valve body or transmission rebuild.
