## Suspension

### Air Suspension System
**Air Spring Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle sagging on one side, uneven ride height, compressor running excessively.
- **Solution:** Replacement of faulty air spring or associated air lines.
- **Cost:** $300 - $700 per corner

**Ride Height Sensor Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Inconsistent ride height, dashboard warning lights, poor handling.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and replacement of faulty ride height sensor.
- **Cost:** $150 - $350


## Electrical

### Body Control Module (BCM)
**Intermittent Electrical Faults**
- **Symptoms:** Random malfunctions of lights, wipers, door locks, or dashboard indicators.
- **Solution:** Software update or replacement of the BCM.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1200


### Alternator
**Premature Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Battery warning light illuminated, dimming headlights, starting issues.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the alternator.
- **Cost:** $400 - $800

### Summary Verdict
The 2015 Volvo 7900 demonstrates a commendable level of reliability, particularly in its powertrain and structural components. The most frequently cited issues tend to be related to the air suspension system, specifically air spring leaks and ride height sensor malfunctions, which are common in vehicles utilizing this technology. Electrical gremlins, such as Body Control Module faults and occasional alternator failures, are also noted but do not appear to be systemic. Interior components like the HVAC blower motor can require replacement over time. Overall, the 7900 is a dependable platform, with most potential issues being manageable and repairable, ensuring continued operational service with proper maintenance.
