# 2019 Volvo XC60 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2019 Volvo XC60 brings Scandinavian luxury, distinctive styling, and advanced safety features to the compact luxury SUV segment. However, used car buyers should approach with a balanced perspective, as owner reports highlight various electrical and powertrain nuances to consider during a pre-purchase inspection.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4 (T5)

The T5 features a 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine producing around 250 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque. It offers a balanced blend of efficiency and daily drivability for standard commuting.

### 2.0L Turbocharged and Supercharged Inline-4 (T6)

The T6 adds a supercharger to the 2.0-liter turbo engine, boosting output to approximately 316 horsepower and 295 lb-ft of torque. This configuration provides brisk acceleration but introduces additional mechanical complexity.

### 2.0L Turbocharged and Supercharged Plug-in Hybrid (T8)

The T8 combines the T6 engine setup with an electric motor to deliver around 400 total system horsepower. Buyers should verify the health of both the internal combustion components and the hybrid battery system.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

An 8-speed automatic transmission is paired across all standard and hybrid powertrains, routing power smoothly through front-wheel drive or all-wheel drive. Routine fluid maintenance is key to preserving long-term shift quality.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V589000: Equipment:Electrical](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volvo&model=XC60&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** Capacitors within these charging systems may fail, possibly resulting in a shock hazard or a fire. An electrical shock or a fire can increase the risk of injury or death.
- **What to do:** Volvo will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the charge cord, replacing it as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V144000: Forward Collision Avoidance: Automatic Emergency Braking](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volvo&model=XC60&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** Due to a software/hardware incompatibility, the Automatic Emergency Brake (AEB) system may not detect obstacles and engage as intended. If the automatic emergency braking system does not engage as intended, it can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Volvo will notify owners, and dealers will update the Active Safety Domain Master (ASDM) software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V220000: Seats](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volvo&model=XC60&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** The driver and passenger front seat rail rear flange nuts may not have been installed during assembly. If the nuts are missing, in the event of a crash, the front seat structure and position may be affected, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Volvo will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the front seat rails for missing flange nuts, installing them as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V414000: Electrical System:Wiring:Fuses And Circuit Breakers](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volvo&model=XC60&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** Failure of the low-pressure fuel pump can cause a stall, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the fuse, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V046000: Structure:Body:Tailgate](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volvo&model=XC60&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** Under certain conditions and cold temperatures, the tailgate lifting arms may freeze which may cause the lifting arms to separate from the vehicle. If the tailgate lifting arms separate, the arms could project rearward with significant force, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Volvo has notified owners, and dealers will replace the tailgate lift arms with an improved designed, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V800000: Electrical System:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volvo&model=XC60&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** If the GPS location is not provided to emergency personnel it can inhibit emergency response in the event of a crash or injury.
- **What to do:** Volvo will notify owners, and dealers will correct the software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V503000: Visibility:Windshield Wiper/Washer:Linkages](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volvo&model=XC60&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** The nuts that secure the front wipers may not have been sufficiently tightened, possibly causing the wipers to not function properly. The risk of the front wiper arms nut fixation to loosen and not holding the arm in correct position, the wiper function could be reduced and vision could be impaired, thus increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Volvo Car will notify owners, and dealers will tighten the nuts holding the front windshield wiper arms in position, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V001000: Steering](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volvo&model=XC60&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** A screw inside the gearbox housing can cause the steering wheel to lock up and increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the steering gear, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

When shopping for a used 2019 Volvo XC60, prioritize a comprehensive pre-purchase inspection that checks the complex electrical systems and drivetrain components. While it offers refined comfort and strong safety credentials, verifying service history helps mitigate potential ownership surprises.
