The 2015 Volvo XC60 is a compact luxury SUV known for its safety features, comfortable ride, and stylish Scandinavian design. Available engine options include a 2.0L turbocharged and supercharged four-cylinder (T6), a 2.0L turbocharged four-cylinder (T5), and a 3.0L turbocharged inline-six (T6). The 2.0L T6 produces around 302 horsepower and 295 lb-ft of torque. The 2.0L T5 produces around 240 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque. The 3.0L T6 produces around 300 horsepower and 325 lb-ft of torque. A common dislike is the sometimes-complex infotainment system, while owners typically appreciate the comfortable seats and overall build quality.

**Engine**

**2.0L Turbocharged and Supercharged Four-Cylinder (T6)**

*   **Oil Consumption**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Excessive oil consumption requiring frequent top-offs between oil changes. Some owners report needing to add a quart every 1,000-2,000 miles. Blue smoke from the exhaust may also be present.
    *   Solution: Replacement of piston rings or, in severe cases, the entire engine.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $7,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Water Pump Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Overheating, coolant leaks, and warning lights on the dashboard. The water pump may also make unusual noises.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the water pump and potentially other cooling system components.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**2.0L Turbocharged Four-Cylinder (T5)**

*   **Oil Consumption**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Excessive oil consumption requiring frequent top-offs between oil changes. Blue smoke from the exhaust may also be present.
    *   Solution: Replacement of piston rings or, in severe cases, the entire engine.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $7,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**3.0L Turbocharged Inline-Six (T6)**

*   **Cracked Cylinder Head**
    * Description/Symptoms: Coolant loss, overheating, white smoke from the exhaust, rough running engine.
    * Solution: Replacement of the cylinder head and possibly the head gasket.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $3,000-$6,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**Aisin Warner TF-80SC (All Engine Options)**
*   **Torque Converter Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: Shuddering or vibrating during acceleration, especially at lower speeds.
    * Solution: Torque converter replacement or transmission rebuild.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,500 - $5,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Rear Air Suspension Failure (If Equipped)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The rear of the vehicle sagging, the suspension compressor running constantly or not at all, "Suspension Service Required" message appearing on the dash.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the air struts, air compressor, or ride height sensors.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $2,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Issues (Sensus)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Freezing, slow response times, blank screen, Bluetooth connectivity problems, navigation malfunctions.
    *   Solution: Software updates, replacement of the infotainment unit.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found
*   **Central Electronic Module (CEM) Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Intermittent electrical issues, including problems with starting, lights, windows, and other electronic systems.
    *   Solution: Replacement or reprogramming of the CEM.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Sunroof Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Water leaks into the cabin, especially during heavy rain.
    *   Solution: Cleaning and resealing the sunroof drains, replacing the sunroof seal.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Dashboard Cracking**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Cracks appearing on the dashboard surface, especially in warmer climates.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the dashboard (expensive) or installation of a dashboard cover.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $2,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2015 Volvo XC60 has some common issues, with engine oil consumption and infotainment system glitches being among the most frequent. Transmission issues, particularly with the torque converter, and electrical problems can also be a concern. While some repairs are relatively inexpensive, others, like engine or transmission work, can be costly. Prospective buyers should be aware of these potential issues and have the vehicle inspected by a qualified mechanic before purchase. Overall, most of the potential issues are of medium severity, but some could be very high depending on the extent of the damage done. The cost of these issues vary but can be high depending on the specific problem.
