**Introduction:**

The 2014 Volvo XC60 is a compact luxury crossover SUV known for its safety, fuel efficiency, and sophisticated design. It is available with three engine options: a 240-horsepower, 2.5-liter turbocharged inline-5, a 302-horsepower, 3.0-liter turbocharged inline-6, and a 325-horsepower, 3.2-liter inline-6. Transmission options include a 6-speed Geartronic automatic and a 6-speed Powershift automatic.

Owners appreciate the XC60's comfortable interior, spacious cargo area, and responsive handling. However, certain issues have been reported.

**Engine**

**2.5L Turbocharged Inline-5**

- **Turbocharger Failure:**
  - Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, increased engine noise, and reduced fuel efficiency.
  - Solution: Replace the turbocharger.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,000+
  - Recalls: None Found

**3.0L Turbocharged Inline-6**

- **Intake Manifold Failure:**
  - Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, rough idling, and check engine light.
  - Solution: Replace the intake manifold.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500
  - Recalls: None Found

**3.2L Inline-6**

- **Timing Chain Tensioner Failure:**
  - Description/Symptoms: Rattle or ticking noise from the engine at startup, reduced power, and check engine light.
  - Solution: Replace the timing chain tensioner.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
  - Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**6-Speed Geartronic Automatic**

- **Transmission Fluid Leak:**
  - Description/Symptoms: Leaking transmission fluid, transmission slipping, and difficulty shifting gears.
  - Solution: Repair or replace the leaking seal.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500

**6-Speed Powershift Automatic**

- **Clutch Pack Failure:**
  - Description/Symptoms: Shuddering or slipping when shifting gears, loss of power, and check engine light.
  - Solution: Replace the clutch pack.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,500+

**Suspension**

- **Front Suspension Noise:**
  - Description/Symptoms: Rattling or creaking noise from the front suspension over uneven roads.
  - Solution: Replace the sway bar end links.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400

**Electrical**

- **Electrical System Issues:**
  - Description/Symptoms: Intermittent electrical issues, such as flickering lights, dead battery, and electrical component failure.
  - Solution: Diagnose and repair the specific electrical fault.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+

**Exterior**

- **Sunroof Panoramic Glass Leaking:**
  - Description/Symptoms: Water leaking into the cabin through the panoramic sunroof.
  - Solution: Replace or repair the sunroof seals.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
  - Recalls: NHTSA 17V-039 (Sunroof Bonding)

**Interior**

- **Dashboard Rattle:**
  - Description/Symptoms: Rattling or buzzing noise coming from the dashboard area.
  - Solution: Tighten or replace the dashboard components.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300

**Summary**

While the 2014 Volvo XC60 is a well-rounded SUV, it has faced several issues, particularly with the engine, transmission, and electrical system. The severity of these issues can vary, and the cost of repairs can be substantial in some cases. Potential owners should be aware of these issues and consider their impact on ownership costs before making a purchasing decision.
