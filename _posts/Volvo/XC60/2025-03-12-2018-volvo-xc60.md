The 2018 Volvo XC60 is a popular compact luxury SUV known for its stylish design, comfortable interior, and emphasis on safety. It was offered with several engine options including a 2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4 (T5), a 2.0L Turbocharged and Supercharged Inline-4 (T6), and a 2.0L Turbocharged and Supercharged Inline-4 with electric motor (T8 Plug-in Hybrid). The T5 produces 250 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque, the T6 produces 316 horsepower and 295 lb-ft of torque, and the T8 produces 400 horsepower and 472 lb-ft of torque. All engine options were paired with an 8-speed automatic transmission. People generally like the XC60 for its comfortable ride, upscale interior, and advanced safety features. Common dislikes include the sometimes-complicated infotainment system and the higher price point compared to some competitors.

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Issues (Sensus Connect)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The Sensus Connect infotainment system can experience freezing, slow response times, and Bluetooth connectivity problems. The navigation system may also glitch.
    *   Solution: Software updates are the most common fix. In some cases, replacing the entire infotainment unit may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 (Software Updates) - $1200+ (Unit Replacement)
    * Recalls: None Found

*   **Battery Drain**
    * Description/Symptoms: The battery can drain prematurely, especially if the vehicle is not driven regularly. This can lead to difficulty starting the car.
    * Solution: Check for parasitic draws. Software updates, or replacing the battery may be necessary.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Sunroof Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Water leaks from the sunroof, often due to clogged drain tubes. This can cause water damage to the headliner and interior components.
    *   Solution: Clean and unclog the sunroof drain tubes. In severe cases, the sunroof seal may need to be replaced.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**2.0L Turbocharged and Supercharged (T6)**

* **Oil Consumption**

    * Solution: Regular monitoring of oil levels. Address any leaks.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+ (for leak detection and repair)

**Summary**

The 2018 Volvo XC60 generally has a good reputation, but owners have reported some electrical and interior issues. The infotainment system glitches and sunroof leaks appear to be the most common and potentially costly problems. Engine issues with the T6 variant, while not widespread, should be monitored. The cost of repairs can vary widely depending on the specific issue and the repair shop, but addressing these potential problems proactively can help maintain the vehicle's reliability and value.
