The 2021 Volvo XC60 is a popular luxury compact SUV known for its elegant design, comfortable ride, and focus on safety. It offered several engine options, including the T5, T6, and T8 plug-in hybrid. The T5 engine is a 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder producing 250 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque. The T6 adds a supercharger to the turbocharger, boosting output to 316 horsepower and 295 lb-ft of torque. All engines are paired with an 8-speed automatic transmission. Owners often praise the XC60's stylish interior, comfortable seats, and advanced safety features. Some common complaints include the sometimes-complex infotainment system and the higher price point compared to some competitors.

**Engine**

**T5 2.0L Turbocharged Engine**

*   **Oil Consumption**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported higher-than-expected oil consumption, requiring more frequent oil top-offs between scheduled services. Symptoms include the low oil pressure light illuminating or noticing a burning oil smell.
    *   Solution: Some cases have been resolved with updated PCV systems or software updates to reduce blow-by. In more severe cases, piston ring replacement might be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $3,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**T6 2.0L Turbocharged and Supercharged Engine**

*   **Supercharger Clutch Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The supercharger clutch, responsible for engaging and disengaging the supercharger, can fail prematurely. This can result in a loss of power, particularly at lower RPMs, and potential warning lights on the dashboard.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the supercharger clutch or the entire supercharger assembly.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $2,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**T8 2.0L Turbocharged and Supercharged Plug-In Hybrid Engine**
*   **Engine Management Software Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The complex engine management system of the T8 can sometimes experience glitches or software errors, leading to erratic engine behavior, warning lights, or reduced performance.
    *   Solution: Software update or re-flash the ECU.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $400+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**Aisin Warner AW TG-81SC 8-Speed Automatic Transmission (All Engine Options)**

*   **Harsh Shifting/Hesitation**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported harsh shifting, particularly between lower gears, or hesitation when accelerating.
    *   Solution: A software update to the transmission control module (TCM) or a transmission fluid flush can sometimes resolve the issue. In more severe cases, valve body replacement may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $3,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Air Suspension Issues (If Equipped)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: If equipped with the optional air suspension, some owners have experienced issues with the air compressor, air struts, or air lines, leading to ride height problems, error messages, or a noisy compressor.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the faulty air suspension component (compressor, strut, or line).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Freezing/Crashing**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The Sensus infotainment system can be prone to freezing, crashing, or exhibiting other software glitches, such as slow response times or Bluetooth connectivity problems.
    *   Solution: Software update to the infotainment system. In some cases, a complete system reset or replacement of the infotainment unit may be required.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 (Software Update) - $1,500+ (Replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found
*   **Sensor Malfunctions (Parking Sensors, Blind Spot Monitoring, etc.)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported malfunctions with various sensors, such as parking sensors, blind spot monitoring, or lane departure warning systems. These malfunctions can result in false warnings or complete system failure.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis of the faulty sensor and replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Sunroof Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported issues with the sunroof, including difficulty opening or closing, excessive noise, or leaks.
    *   Solution: Lubrication of the sunroof tracks, adjustment of the sunroof mechanism, or replacement of seals. In some cases, the entire sunroof assembly may need to be replaced.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $1,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Dashboard Cracking/Delamination**
    *   Description/Symptoms: In some climates with high heat and sun exposure, the dashboard can be prone to cracking or delamination, particularly around the edges.
    *   Solution: Dashboard replacement
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2021 Volvo XC60, while generally a reliable and well-regarded vehicle, does have some common issues. The infotainment system and potential sensor malfunctions are the most frequently reported. Engine issues related to oil consumption and supercharger components, plus the transmission's shifting issues, represent moderate concerns from a repair perspective. Air suspension problems (if equipped) can lead to significant costs. The dashboard cracking and sunroof problems are somewhat less common. The severity of these issues ranges from minor inconveniences to potentially costly repairs, so a thorough pre-purchase inspection is recommended.
