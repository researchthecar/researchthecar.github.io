The 2022 Volvo XC60 is a popular compact luxury SUV known for its Scandinavian design, comfortable interior, and emphasis on safety. Engine options include the B5 mild-hybrid powertrain and the T8 Recharge plug-in hybrid. The B5 powertrain consists of a 2.0-liter turbocharged and supercharged four-cylinder engine paired with a 48-volt mild-hybrid system, producing 247 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque. All engine options are paired with an 8-speed automatic transmission. Owners often praise the XC60 for its stylish appearance and comfortable ride, while some criticisms include the infotainment system's complexity and occasional software glitches.

**Engine**

**B5 Mild-Hybrid (2.0L Turbocharged and Supercharged Four-Cylinder)**

*   **Oil Consumption**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported excessive oil consumption, requiring more frequent oil top-ups between scheduled oil changes. The exact cause is often difficult to pinpoint, but can potentially be attributed to issues with piston rings or valve stem seals.
    *   Solution: A dealer inspection is necessary to determine the root cause. Depending on the findings, the solution could range from using a different type of oil to replacing the piston rings or valve stem seals.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $5,000+ (depending on the extent of repairs needed)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**8-Speed Automatic (All Engine Options)**

*   **Harsh or Jerky Shifting**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported experiencing harsh or jerky shifting, particularly during low-speed acceleration or deceleration. This may be accompanied by a noticeable clunking sound.
    *   Solution: A transmission software update may resolve the issue. If the problem persists, further inspection may be needed to assess the condition of the valve body or other internal components.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 (software update) - $3,000+ (valve body replacement or rebuild)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Freezing/Crashing**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The Sensus infotainment system, controlling navigation, audio, and climate functions, has been reported to freeze, crash, or become unresponsive.
    *   Solution: Performing a system reset, updating the software to the latest version, or, in more severe cases, replacing the infotainment unit.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 (reset) - $150 (software update) - $1,000+ (replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found
*   **Battery Drain**
    * Description/Symptoms: Premature battery drain, resulting in difficulty starting the vehicle or requiring frequent jump-starts, has been reported.
    * Solution: Diagnose the cause of the parasitic draw, which may involve testing the alternator and checking for faulty electrical components that are not shutting off properly. Replace the battery if it is found to be defective.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300 (battery replacement) - $100 - $500+ (diagnostics and repair of electrical components)
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Rattling/Squeaking Noises**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported experiencing rattling or squeaking noises emanating from the dashboard, door panels, or rear cargo area.
    *   Solution: Identifying the source of the noise and tightening loose components or applying felt tape to prevent friction.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 (DIY) - $100 - $500+ (dealer service)
    *   Recalls: None Found
*   **Sunroof Malfunction**
    * Description/Symptoms: Issues such as the sunroof failing to open or close, getting stuck, or making excessive noise during operation.
    * Solution: Inspection and possible replacement of the sunroof motor, regulator, or other related components. Lubricating the sunroof tracks may resolve minor issues.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $1000+ (depending on the parts and labor involved)
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Sensor Malfunctions (Parking Sensors, Blind Spot Monitoring)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Malfunctions of parking sensors or the blind spot monitoring system, leading to false alerts or failure to detect obstacles.
    *   Solution: Clean the sensors, check wiring connections, and replace faulty sensors if necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+ (depending on the sensor and labor costs)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2022 Volvo XC60, while generally a reliable vehicle, can experience some common issues. Electrical glitches, particularly with the infotainment system, and interior noises seem to be the most frequently reported problems. Engine oil consumption issues, while not widespread, can be costly if they require internal repairs. Transmission problems, like harsh shifting, are also concerns. The severity of these issues ranges from minor inconveniences to more substantial repairs, and the associated costs can vary widely. Overall, potential buyers should be aware of these potential issues and consider a thorough inspection before purchasing a used model.
