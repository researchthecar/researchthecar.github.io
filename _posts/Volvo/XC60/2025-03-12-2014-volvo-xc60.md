The 2014 Volvo XC60 is a compact luxury SUV known for its safety features, comfortable ride, and stylish design. Engine options for the 2014 XC60 vary depending on the trim and drivetrain (FWD or AWD). One popular engine option is the T6 3.0L Inline-6 Turbocharged engine producing 300 horsepower and 325 lb-ft of torque, paired with a 6-speed automatic transmission. Owners appreciate the XC60's comfortable seats, upscale interior, and responsive handling. Common dislikes include the sometimes-confusing infotainment system and higher maintenance costs compared to non-luxury vehicles.

**Engine**

**T6 3.0L Inline-6 Turbocharged**

*   **Oil Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks may develop from the valve cover gasket or rear main seal over time. Signs include visible oil drips under the vehicle, burning oil smell, and low oil level.
    *   Solution: Replacing the valve cover gasket or rear main seal.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Turbocharger Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The turbocharger may experience failures such as excessive noise, reduced boost, or complete failure.
    *   Solution: Replacing or rebuilding the turbocharger.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**Aisin Warner TF-80SC 6-speed Automatic**

*   **Torque Converter Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The torque converter can experience shuddering or vibrations, especially at lower speeds or during light acceleration.
    *   Solution: Replacing or rebuilding the torque converter.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800-$2,000
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Valve Body Problems**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Harsh or delayed shifting can occur due to valve body issues.
    *   Solution: Replacing or rebuilding the valve body.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $700 - $1,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Front Strut Mounts**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Premature wear or cracking of the front strut mounts, leading to clunking noises over bumps.
    *   Solution: Replacing the front strut mounts.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+ per side
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Software Glitches**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Intermittent issues with the infotainment system, warning lights, or other electronic features.
    *   Solution: Software updates or module replacements.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+ depending on the issue
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Battery Drain**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The battery may drain quickly, especially if the vehicle is not driven frequently. This can be caused by a parasitic draw.
    *   Solution: Identifying and fixing the source of the parasitic draw or replacing the battery.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $400+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Power Tailgate Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The power tailgate may stop working or become difficult to open and close, sometimes related to strut or motor issues.
    *   Solution: Replacing the tailgate struts or motor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Sunroof Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The sunroof may leak, become stuck, or make excessive noise when opening or closing.
    *   Solution: Repairing or replacing the sunroof motor, seals, or tracks.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**
The 2014 Volvo XC60 is generally a reliable vehicle, but owners should be aware of potential issues, particularly with the engine, transmission, and electrical systems. Oil leaks, turbocharger problems, and torque converter issues can be costly to repair. Addressing these issues promptly can help maintain the vehicle's reliability and longevity.
