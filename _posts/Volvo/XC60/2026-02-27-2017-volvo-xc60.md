# 2017 Volvo XC60 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2017 Volvo XC60, buyers will find a stylish and comfortable luxury crossover with a solid reputation for safety. While most owners enjoy a refined driving experience, owner reports filed with safety authorities point to a few occasional electrical quirks and automated safety system sensitivities to keep an eye on during a pre-purchase inspection.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged 4-Cylinder

This 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine produces approximately 240 horsepower and provides a balance of daily efficiency and punchy power. It is generally reliable, though owners should keep an eye on auxiliary battery and start-stop systems as the vehicle ages.

### 2.0L Supercharged and Turbocharged 4-Cylinder

Featuring both a supercharger and a turbocharger, this engine pushes output to around 302 horsepower for brisk acceleration. Routine maintenance is key to keeping the complex dual-induction setup running smoothly.

### 3.0L Turbocharged 6-Cylinder

This smooth 3.0-liter turbocharged inline-six generates roughly 325 horsepower in sportier trims for robust performance. It offers solid long-term mechanical durability, though occasional electrical and stalling complaints have been noted by owners.

**Engine Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle may unexpectedly stall during sudden braking maneuvers in heavy traffic, accompanied by dashboard lights going out.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified mechanic inspect the electrical charging system, throttle body, and engine control modules for fault codes.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

This 8-speed automatic transmission delivers seamless gear changes paired with front-wheel or all-wheel drive. It generally proves dependable when fluid changes are maintained on schedule.

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 6-speed automatic transmission is paired with specific powertrain configurations to deliver smooth power delivery. It remains a durable unit for everyday driving.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

Brake components generally perform well, though sudden pedal softness has been reported by isolated drivers.

**Mushy Brake Pedal Feel**
- **Symptoms:** Brake pedal suddenly loses resistance and travels all the way to the floor, significantly reducing stopping confidence.
- **Solution:** Inspect the master cylinder, brake lines, and fluid condition immediately for leaks or internal failure.

## Steering

### Steering Assembly

Steering feel is precise and comfortable, though rare mechanical or electronic binding has required professional intervention.

**Steering Wheel Lockup**
- **Symptoms:** The steering wheel may intermittently fail to turn properly, requiring dealer diagnosis and component replacement.
- **Solution:** Have the steering column and electronic steering components inspected by a qualified technician.

## Safety

### Forward Collision Avoidance

Advanced safety features offer great peace of mind but rely heavily on clean sensors to prevent occasional false warnings.

**Phantom Auto-Braking**
- **Symptoms:** The City Safety system unexpectedly triggers a full emergency stop while driving straight through clear intersections.
- **Solution:** Ensure windshield sensors and camera mounts are clean and properly calibrated; consult a dealer if false activations persist.

### Wiring Harness and Start-Stop

Electrical accessories and wiring harnesses can develop minor connection faults or auxiliary battery warnings over time.

**SRS Airbag Warning Light**
- **Symptoms:** Airbag warning light illuminates intermittently on the instrument cluster.
- **Solution:** Inspect door wiring harnesses for loose pin tension and clear stored diagnostic trouble codes.

## Summary Verdict

The 2017 Volvo XC60 remains an appealing used luxury SUV choice, blending comfort, strong safety credentials, and versatile engine options. Buyers should perform a thorough test drive to verify proper brake feel, smooth electrical operation, and flawless functioning of advanced driver aids. Scheduling a pre-purchase inspection with a marque specialist is always recommended to catch any hidden wear.
