The 2023 Volvo XC60 is a popular luxury compact SUV known for its stylish design, comfortable interior, and focus on safety. Engine options include the B5 mild-hybrid 2.0L turbocharged and supercharged four-cylinder engine producing 247 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque and the T8 Recharge plug-in hybrid, a 2.0L turbocharged and supercharged four-cylinder paired with an electric motor for a combined output of 455 horsepower and 523 lb-ft of torque. All XC60 models utilize an 8-speed automatic transmission. Owners generally appreciate the XC60's elegant interior, comfortable ride, and advanced safety features, but some have voiced concerns about the infotainment system's complexity and occasional software glitches.

**Electrical**

* **Infotainment System Freezing/Crashing**
    * Description/Symptoms: The Sensus infotainment system can become unresponsive, freeze, or unexpectedly reboot. This can affect navigation, climate control, and audio functions.
    * Solution: Software updates from Volvo can often resolve these issues. In some cases, a system reset or replacement of the infotainment module may be necessary.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: Software update: Free, Infotainment module replacement: $1,200 - $1,800+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **12V Battery Drain**
    * Description/Symptoms: The 12V battery can drain prematurely, especially if the vehicle is not driven frequently or if accessories are left on. This can cause starting problems and other electrical issues.
    * Solution: Identify and address any parasitic draws. Replacing the battery with a high-quality AGM battery or updating software to better manage battery drain can help.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: Battery replacement: $300 - $500+, Diagnostic and parasitic draw repair: $150 - $400+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Sunroof Rattling**
    * Description/Symptoms: Owners have reported rattling noises emanating from the sunroof area, particularly when driving on uneven surfaces.
    * Solution: Inspection and tightening of sunroof components, lubrication of moving parts, or replacement of worn seals or guides.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Sensor Malfunctions (Parking Sensors, BLIS)**
    * Description/Symptoms: Parking sensors or the Blind Spot Information System (BLIS) may intermittently fail or provide false alerts.
    * Solution: Cleaning the sensors, checking wiring connections, or replacing faulty sensors. Calibration of the system may also be required.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: Sensor replacement: $200 - $600+ per sensor, Calibration: $100 - $300+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Engine**

**B5 2.0L Mild-Hybrid**

* **No Issues Found**

**T8 Recharge 2.0L Plug-In Hybrid**

* **High Voltage Coolant Heater Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: A faulty High Voltage Coolant Heater (HVCH) can prevent the vehicle from properly heating the cabin, especially in cold weather. It can also affect the efficiency of the hybrid system.
    * Solution: Replacement of the HVCH unit.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electric Motor**

**T8 Recharge 2.0L Plug-In Hybrid**

* **No Issues Found**

**Transmission**

* **8-Speed Automatic (All Models)**

    * **Rough or Delayed Shifting**
        * Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported instances of rough or delayed shifting, particularly between certain gears. This can be more pronounced at lower speeds.
        * Solution: Software updates to the transmission control module (TCM) may resolve the issue. In some cases, a transmission fluid flush or valve body replacement may be necessary.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: Software update: Free, Transmission fluid flush: $200 - $400+, Valve body replacement: $1,000 - $2,000+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Air Suspension Issues (If Equipped)**
    * Description/Symptoms: Air suspension systems, if equipped, may experience leaks, compressor failures, or ride height sensor malfunctions. This can result in uneven ride height, a harsh ride, or warning lights.
    * Solution: Replacing leaking air struts or air lines, replacing the air suspension compressor, or replacing faulty ride height sensors.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: Air strut replacement: $800 - $1500+ per strut, Compressor replacement: $700 - $1200+, Ride height sensor replacement: $300 - $600+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2023 Volvo XC60 generally presents with issues primarily in the electrical and interior areas, with infotainment glitches and interior rattling being the most commonly reported. Engine and transmission problems are less prevalent, but the plug-in hybrid T8 is known for HVCH failures. Suspension issues are also possible, especially on vehicles equipped with air suspension. The severity and cost of these issues range from minor software updates to more significant repairs like infotainment module or HVCH replacement, making it important for prospective buyers to be aware of these potential problems and to thoroughly inspect and test drive the vehicle before purchase.
