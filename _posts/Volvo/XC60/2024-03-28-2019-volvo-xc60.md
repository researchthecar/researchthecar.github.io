**Introduction to the 2019 Volvo XC60**

The 2019 Volvo XC60 is a compact luxury SUV known for its Scandinavian design, safety features, and comfortable interior. It offers a choice of three engine options and two transmission options. The base engine is a turbocharged 2.0-liter inline-four producing 250 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque, paired with an 8-speed automatic transmission. The mid-range engine is a supercharged and turbocharged 2.0-liter inline-four producing 316 horsepower and 295 lb-ft of torque, paired with an 8-speed automatic transmission. The top-of-the-line engine is a turbocharged and supercharged 2.0-liter inline-four producing 400 horsepower and 472 lb-ft of torque, paired with an 8-speed automatic transmission.

Owners generally praise the XC60 for its stylish exterior, spacious interior, and advanced safety features. However, some common issues have been reported.

**Engine**

**Turbocharged 2.0-liter Inline-Four**

* **Oil Consumption:** Excessive oil consumption, leading to low oil levels and potential engine damage.
    * Solution: Oil consumption monitoring and top-ups, or engine replacement in severe cases.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 - $10,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Supercharged and Turbocharged 2.0-liter Inline-Four**

* **Timing Belt Failure:** Premature failure of the timing belt, potentially leading to catastrophic engine damage.
    * Solution: Timing belt replacement at recommended intervals (e.g., 100,000 miles).
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Turbocharged and Supercharged 2.0-liter Inline-Four**

* **No Issues Found**

**Transmission**

**8-Speed Automatic Transmission**

* **Hesitation and Jerking:** Transmission hesitation or jerking during gear shifts.
    * Solution: Transmission software updates or valve body replacement.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**No Issues Found**

**Electrical**

**Battery Discharge:** Rapid battery discharge, leading to starting issues or electrical system failure.
    * Solution: Battery replacement or electrical system diagnosis and repair.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

**Windshield Cracking:** Premature cracking or chipping of the windshield.
    * Solution: Windshield replacement.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

**Infotainment System Freezing:** Infotainment system freezing or unresponsive.
    * Solution: Software updates or system reset.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

Overall, the 2019 Volvo XC60 has some reported issues, primarily related to oil consumption, timing belt failure, and electrical problems. While the severity of these issues can vary, some potential costs for repairs can be significant. However, it's important to note that these issues may not be present in every vehicle and that the XC60 generally receives positive reviews for its safety, comfort, and style.
