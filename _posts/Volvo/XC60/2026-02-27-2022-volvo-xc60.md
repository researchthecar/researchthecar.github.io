# 2022 Volvo XC60 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used luxury compact SUV, understanding common owner-reported vulnerabilities can help guide your purchase decision. This review highlights key areas where drivers have experienced issues, providing practical insights to help you inspect potential vehicles with confidence.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged I4

This turbocharged four-cylinder engine provides a balance of efficiency and responsive power for everyday driving. Reliability is generally solid, though routine maintenance is essential to avoid potential mechanical complications.

Issues affecting the core mechanical systems are infrequent, but owners should monitor for unusual engine noises or performance inconsistencies during test drives.

**Engine Stalling and Timing Noise**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicles may develop a metallic ticking noise in the timing area at idle, potentially relating to camshaft seal concerns.
- **Solution:** Inspect the timing area and consult technical service information to determine if seal replacement or related repairs are necessary.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic

The eight-speed automatic transmission delivers smooth gear shifts across various driving conditions. Regular fluid inspections help maintain long-term durability and shift quality.

## Electrical

### Electrical System

Electrical and software-driven features require careful verification during a pre-purchase inspection to ensure all cabin controls and displays operate reliably.

**Infotainment System Freezing and Reboots**
- **Symptoms:** The central touchscreen may intermittently crash, display error screens, or reboot while driving, temporarily disabling audio and climate controls.
- **Solution:** Ensure the vehicle's software is updated to the latest manufacturer release, as many bugs are addressed via software patches.

## Safety

### Backup Camera and Forward Collision

Advanced driver assistance and safety systems generally perform well, though occasional camera and sensor glitches have been reported by owners.

**Backup Camera Display Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The rearview camera may display a black screen or fail to activate when shifting into reverse, intermittently impacting visibility.
- **Solution:** Check for pending software updates or have the camera module and wiring inspected for faults.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V682000: Seat Belts:Front:Retractor](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volvo&model=XC60&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 208, "Occupant Crash Protection." An unsecured child restraint system can increase the risk of injury during a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the seat belt assemblies, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V793000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Engine:Engine Control Module (Ecu/Ecm)](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volvo&model=XC60&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** The vehicle may lose drive power as the high voltage battery is depleted, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the ECM software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V001000: Steering](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volvo&model=XC60&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** A screw inside the gearbox housing can cause the steering wheel to lock up and increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the steering gear, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V179000: Electrical System:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volvo&model=XC60&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** The high-voltage battery may experience a short circuit within the battery module when the battery is fully charged and the vehicle is parked. A short circuit in a high-voltage battery increases the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the high-voltage battery module as necessary. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V282000: Back Over Prevention:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volvo&model=XC60&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** The rearview camera image may not display when the vehicle is placed in reverse. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 111, "Rear Visibility." A rearview camera image that does not display reduces the driver's view of what is behind the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 25V908000: Back Over Prevention:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volvo&model=XC60&modelYear=2022)**

Overall, this vehicle offers a comfortable ride, refined styling, and robust safety credentials. Prospective buyers should thoroughly test all infotainment features, climate controls, and driver-assistance cameras to ensure past software or hardware glitches have been fully resolved.
