# 2021 Volvo XC60

### Overview
The 2021 Volvo XC60 is a luxury compact SUV known for its sophisticated design, advanced safety features, and comfortable interior. It offers a range of powertrains, including mild-hybrid options, aiming for a balance of performance and efficiency. Reliability data for the 2021 model year generally indicates a strong performance, with owners reporting satisfaction with its build quality and technological integration. While no vehicle is entirely without potential issues, the XC60 tends to perform well in long-term durability assessments, particularly in areas like its powertrain and core electrical systems. Common areas of concern, though infrequent, often relate to specific electronic modules or minor trim pieces, rather than widespread mechanical failures.

### Summary Verdict
The 2021 Volvo XC60 demonstrates good overall reliability, with its powertrains and core systems performing commendably. The most frequently cited, though still relatively uncommon, issues tend to be related to the infotainment system experiencing occasional glitches and, in some instances, premature 12V battery failure. The optional air suspension system has also seen reports of compressor issues. However, these are not indicative of widespread systemic problems. For the most part, owners can expect a dependable luxury SUV experience, with most maintenance falling into the realm of routine servicing rather than unexpected repairs.
